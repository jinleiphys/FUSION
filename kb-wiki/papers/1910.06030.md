# Calculated fission-fragment mass yields and average total kinetic energies of heavy and superheavy nuclei

> Fission-fragment mass and total-kinetic-energy (TKE) distributions following fission of even-even nuclides in the region $74 \leq Z \leq 126$ and $92 \leq N \leq 230$, comprising 896 nuclides have been calculated using the Brownian shape-motion method. The emphasis is the region of superheavy nuclei. To show compatibility with earlier results the calculations are extended to include earlier studied regions. An island of asymmetric fission is obtained in the superheavy region, $106\leq Z\leq114$ and $162\leq N\leq 176$, where the heavy fragment is found to be close to $^{208}$Pb and the light fragment adjusts accordingly. Most experimentally observed $α$-decay chains of superheavy nuclei with $Z > 113 $ terminate by spontaneous fission in our predicted region of asymmetric fission. In these cases, the pronounced large asymmetry is accompanied by a low TKE value compatible with measurements.

## Key claim

The Brownian shape-motion method predicts a new island of highly asymmetric fission in the superheavy region (Z=106–114, N=162–176) where the heavy fragment is close to 208Pb, but the authors note that the exact transition points from asymmetric to symmetric fission in this region differ slightly from those obtained using a scission-point model by Carjan et al. (2015) and Pasca et al. (2018).

## Method

The authors calculated fission-fragment mass yields and average total kinetic energies for 896 even-even nuclides (Z=74–126, N=92–230) using the Brownian shape-motion method on five-dimensional potential-energy surfaces. The potential-energy surfaces were computed with a macroscopic-microscopic model using a larger Strutinsky smoothing range (γ = 1.5 × 41 MeV/A^(1/3) × B_S) than in previous work, and single-particle levels were calculated for four "center" nuclei (214Rn, 288Pu, 258Fm, 270Hs) and reused for neighboring nuclei. Each mass distribution was based on 10000 random walks started at the second minimum (or ground-state minimum) with a bias potential of 60 MeV, and fragment separation was assumed at a critical neck radius of 1.5 fm.

## Key numbers

- The study covers 896 even-even nuclides in the region 74 ≤ Z ≤ 126 and 92 ≤ N ≤ 230
- The island of asymmetric fission in the superheavy region spans 106 ≤ Z ≤ 114 and 162 ≤ N ≤ 176
- The critical neck radius for fragment separation is c_0 = 1.5 fm
- The largest value of the dimensionless elongation parameter q_2 was extended from roughly 16 to 20
- Each fission-fragment mass distribution is based on 10000 random walks
- The bias potential used in the walks is 60 MeV
- The Viola TKE systematics used for comparison is E_TKE^(Viola) = 0.1189 Z^2/A^(1/3) + 7.3 MeV
- The transition from symmetric to asymmetric fission in the lighter actinide region was previously suggested at A ≈ 226 by Schmidt et al. (2000)

## Context

This work extends the Brownian shape-method calculations previously applied to nuclides from Z=74 to Z=94 by Moller et al. (2015) up to Z=126, providing the first BSM-based predictions for the heavier actinide and superheavy regions. The results show a new region of asymmetry near Z=114 corresponding to 208Pb-like heavy fragments, which is compatible with recent density functional theory calculations by Warda et al. (2018) and Matheson et al. (2019), but differs from the prescission point model of Carjan et al. (2019) that predicts 132Sn-like light fragments. The calculations enable interpretation of alpha-decay chains of superheavy nuclei with Z > 113 that terminate by spontaneous fission, and predict that neutron-rich nuclei near the neutron drip line also fission asymmetrically.
