# Color Superconductivity in Compact Stars

> After a brief review of the phenomena expected in cold dense quark matter, color superconductivity and color-flavor locking, we sketch some implications of recent developments in our understanding of cold dense quark matter for the physics of compact stars. We give a more detailed summary of our recent work on crystalline color superconductivity and the consequent realization that (some) pulsar glitches may originate in quark matter.

## Key claim

Crystalline color superconductivity (the LOFF state) may occur in quark matter within compact stars when the Fermi momenta of different quark flavors differ sufficiently, and this state could provide a mechanism for pulsar glitches via pinning of rotational vortices in a crystalline gap region, though the authors note that the LOFF state is only favored over BCS pairing in a narrow window of chemical potential differences.

## Method

The authors analyze a toy model of quark matter with a four-fermion interaction having the quantum numbers of single-gluon exchange, considering pairing between up and down quarks with differing chemical potentials. They solve the gap equation for a plane-wave LOFF ansatz, vary the Cooper pair momentum to find the lowest free energy state at each chemical potential difference, and compare the free energy of the LOFF state to that of the BCS state. The calculation is simplified by assuming a single plane-wave condensate rather than determining the full crystal structure.

## Key numbers

- The LOFF state is favored for chemical potential differences satisfying δμ₁ < δμ < δμ₂, with δμ₁/Δ₀ = 0.707 and δμ₂/Δ₀ = 0.754 in the weak coupling limit where Δ₀ ≪ μ
- The LOFF gap parameter at δμ = δμ₁ is 0.23 Δ₀, decreasing to zero at δμ = δμ₂
- For a BCS gap Δ₀ = 40 MeV at zero splitting, the LOFF state has lower free energy than BCS above δμ₁ (vertical dashed line in Fig. 1a)
- The average quark chemical potential is set to 0.4 GeV, corresponding to a baryon density of about 4 to 5 times nuclear matter density
- At this density, the estimated δμ between quark flavors is 15-30 MeV depending on the effective strange quark mass
- In the LOFF state, each Cooper pair carries momentum 2q with |q| ≈ 1.2 δμ, and the condensate varies in space with wavelength π/|q|

## Context

This work extends the LOFF mechanism, first explored by Larkin and Ovchinnikov (1964) and Fulde and Ferrell (1964) in electron superconductivity with magnetic impurities, to the QCD context of color superconductivity. The authors note that the LOFF state may occur wherever the difference between Fermi momenta of any two quark flavors is near an unpairing transition, including near the unlocking transition between the 2SC and CFL phases studied by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1999) and by Schäfer and Wilczek (1999). The results enable future work on the crystal structure of the QCD LOFF phase and quantitative modeling of glitch phenomena in compact stars with quark matter cores.
