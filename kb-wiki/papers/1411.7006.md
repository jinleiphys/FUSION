# QCD symmetries and the eta and etaprime in nuclei

> We discuss the role of QCD symmetries in understanding the eta and etaprime mesons in nuclear media. Recent results on the etaprime mass in nuclei from the CBELSA/TAPS collaboration are very similar to the prediction of the Quark Meson Coupling model.

## Key claim

The CBELSA/TAPS collaboration's measurement of the $\eta'$ mass shift in nuclei is very similar to the prediction of the Quark Meson Coupling model, but this agreement depends on the assumed $\eta$-$\eta'$ mixing angle and the treatment of gluonic contributions from the QCD axial anomaly.

## Method

The paper reviews the role of QCD symmetries, particularly spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking and axial U(1) dynamics, in understanding the $\eta$ and $\eta'$ mesons in nuclear media. It discusses the Quark Meson Coupling model, where medium modifications are calculated at the quark level through coupling of light quarks in the hadron to scalar isoscalar $\sigma$ and vector $\omega$ and $\rho$ mean fields in the nucleus, working in mean-field approximation. The in-medium mass shift is obtained by solving the meson propagator equation at zero three-momentum, with the self-energy expressed in terms of the meson-nucleon scattering length and nuclear density including the Ericson-Ericson-Lorentz-Lorenz double scattering correction.

## Key numbers

- $\eta'$ mass shift at nuclear matter density from CBELSA/TAPS: $-37 \pm 10$ (stat.) $\pm 10$ (syst.) MeV, with imaginary part of optical potential $-10 \pm 2.5$ MeV
- QMC prediction for $\eta'$ mass in medium at nuclear matter density: 921 MeV, corresponding to a mass shift of $-37$ MeV (with $\eta$-$\eta'$ mixing angle of $-20^\circ$)
- $\eta'$ width in nuclei: $20 \pm 5.0$ MeV at nuclear matter density
- $\eta'$-nucleon scattering length from COSY-11: Re($a_{\eta' p}$) = $0 \pm 0.43$ fm, Im($a_{\eta' p}$) = $0.37^{+0.40}_{-0.16}$ fm
- QMC prediction for $\eta$-nucleon scattering length with mixing angle $-20^\circ$: Re($a_{\eta}$) = 0.85 fm
- QMC prediction for $\eta_8$ (pure octet) mass in medium: 500.0 MeV, with scattering length 0.43 fm
- QMC prediction for $\eta'$ with mixing angle $-10^\circ$: mass 899.2 MeV, scattering length 0.74 fm
- QMC prediction for $\eta_0$ (pure singlet) mass in medium: 878.6 MeV, scattering length 0.99 fm
- Effective proton mass in QMC at nuclear matter density: about 755 MeV
- $S_{11}$ excitation energy in QMC: about 1544 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Quark Meson Coupling model calculations of Bass and Thomas (2013) for $\eta$ and $\eta'$ in nuclei, incorporating $\eta$-$\eta'$ mixing and the flavour-singlet component from the QCD axial anomaly. The results contrast with Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model calculations by Hirenzaki and collaborators that predict larger $\eta'$ mass shifts of 80-150 MeV, and with linear sigma model calculations by Jido and collaborators that suggest a rising $\eta$ effective mass at finite density. The paper enables future searches for $\eta'$ bound states in carbon using the (p,d) reaction at GSI and photoproduction at ELSA, as well as ongoing COSY searches for $\eta$ bound states in $^3$He and $^4$He.
