# The Coulomb Interaction between Pion-Wavepackets: The piplus-piminus Puzzle

> The time dependent Schrödinger equation for $π^+$--$π^-$ pairs, which are emitted from the interaction zone in relativistic nuclear collisions, is solved using wavepacket states. It is shown that the Coulomb enhancement in the momentum correlation function of such pairs is smaller than obtained in earlier calculations based on Coulomb distorted plane waves. These results suggest that the experimentally observed positive correlation signal cannot be caused by the Coulomb interaction between pions emitted from the interaction zone. But other processes which involve long-lived resonances and the related extended source dimensions could provide a possible explanation for the observed signal.

# The Coulomb Interaction between Pion-Wavepackets: The π⁺–π⁻ Puzzle

## Key claim

The Coulomb enhancement in the momentum correlation function of π⁺–π⁻ pairs is smaller than obtained in earlier calculations based on Coulomb distorted plane waves, suggesting that the experimentally observed positive correlation signal cannot be caused by the Coulomb interaction between pions emitted from the interaction zone, but other processes involving long-lived resonances and extended source dimensions could provide a possible explanation.

## Method

The authors solved the time-dependent Schrödinger equation for π⁺–π⁻ pairs using Gaussian wavepacket states as initial conditions, with the Hamiltonian including the Coulomb potential. The relative motion wavefunction was expanded in a multiconfigurational basis of time-dependent harmonic oscillator states (s, p, d, and f states), and the equations of motion for the expansion coefficients were integrated numerically. A Monte-Carlo procedure sampled the two-particle probability density in momentum space, and event-mixing was used to construct the correlation function C₂(q) of the relative momentum q = |p₁ − p₂|.

## Key numbers

- The de Broglie wavelength of a typical pion from Δ(1232)-decay with momentum p₀ = 150 MeV/c is λ₀ ≈ 8 fm, and the coherence length is Λ ≈ 6 fm (derived from ΔE ≈ 120 MeV for the Δ(1232) resonance).
- The zero-point energy for a wavepacket width σ₀ = 1 fm is E₀(σ₀ = 1 fm) ≈ 100 MeV.
- The relative Coulomb strength ξ = E_coul^max / E₀ equals 1.4 × 10⁻² for σ₀ = 1.8 fm, 3.1 × 10⁻² for σ₀ = 4 fm, and 0.16 for σ₀ = 20 fm.
- Reducing ℏ to 1/10 of its real value gives ξ = 1.4 for σ₀ = 1.8 fm, bringing the system into the classical domain.
- The momentum uncertainty for σ₀ = 4 fm is σ_p = ℏ/(2σ₀) = 25 MeV/c.
- The pion mass used is m = 140 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the wavepacket approach previously applied to identical pions by Merlitz (1997) to the non-identical π⁺–π⁻ system, where the wavefunction does not require symmetrization. It contradicts classical trajectory and plane-wave scattering calculations (e.g., references 7–10 in the paper) that predicted strong Coulomb enhancement in π⁺–π⁻ correlations. The results challenge the interpretation of experimental positive correlations observed in proton-induced reactions above 10 AGeV (Afanasiev, 1997) and nucleus-induced reactions (Barz, 1997), and suggest that long-lived resonance decays could provide an alternative explanation. The work enables future low-energy experiments that exclude long-lived resonance contributions to help resolve the puzzle.
