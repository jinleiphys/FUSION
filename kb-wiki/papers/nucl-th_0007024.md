# Inclusive quasielastic electron scattering on $^6$He: a probe of the halo structure

> We investigate inclusive electron scattering reactions on two-neutron halo nuclei in the quasielastic region. Expressions for the cross section and structure functions are given assuming that the halo nucleus can be described as a three-body system (${core}+n+n$). The method is applied to $^6$He. We compute cross sections and structure functions, and investigate the kinematic conditions for which the observables are determined either by $α$-knockout or by halo neutron-knockout. The optimal kinematical domain to disantangle the momentum distributions of the various components of the three--body system ($q \lesssim 200$ MeV/c and $ω< q^2/2M_N + 20$ MeV) are explored.

# Inclusive quasielastic electron scattering on \(^6\)He: a probe of the halo structure

## Key claim

Inclusive quasielastic electron scattering on \(^6\)He can separately probe the momentum distributions of the \(\alpha\)-core and the halo neutrons, provided the kinematics are restricted to low momentum transfer (\(q \lesssim 200\) MeV/c) and low energy transfer (\(\omega < q^2/2M_N + 20\) MeV) where core-breakup contributions are minimal. The authors explicitly note that technical difficulties from short nuclear lifetimes and low production rates are not addressed, and that such experiments remain only in the project phase at RIKEN, GSI, and Mainz.

## Method

The halo nucleus \(^6\)He is treated as a three-body system (\(\alpha + n + n\)) within the Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (PWIA). The inclusive cross section and structure functions \(W_L\) and \(W_T\) are computed as sums of contributions from \(\alpha\)-knockout and halo-neutron knockout, using a three-body wave function obtained from Faddeev equations in coordinate space. The final-state interaction between the two spectator particles is included via continuum wave functions solved with \(n\)-\(n\) and \(n\)-\(\alpha\) potentials fitted to low-energy scattering data and phase shifts up to 20 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The optimal kinematical domain to disentangle momentum distributions is \(q \lesssim 200\) MeV/c and \(\omega < q^2/2M_N + 20\) MeV, where \(M_N\) is the nucleon mass and the nucleon separation energy in the \(\alpha\)-particle is \(S_N \approx 20\) MeV.
- At \(q = 90\), 150, and 200 MeV/c, the longitudinal structure function \(W_L\) is dominated by the \(\alpha\)-peak centered at \(\omega \approx q^2/2m_\alpha\), while the transverse structure function \(W_T\) shows halo-neutron peaks centered at \(\omega \approx q^2/2m_n\).
- For \(q > 250\) MeV/c and \(\omega > 25\) MeV, core-breakup contributions become sizeable; for \(^4\)He(\(e,e^\prime\)) at \(q \sim 360\)–380 MeV/c and \(\omega \sim 70\)–80 MeV, maximum values are \(W_L \sim 1.5\) fm and \(W_T \sim 2\) fm.
- For an incoming electron energy \(E_0 = 100\) MeV and scattering angles from \(10^\circ\) to \(170^\circ\), the inclusive cross section peaks in the low-\((\omega,q)\) region free from core breakup; at \(\theta_e \gtrsim 160^\circ\) with \(50 \lesssim E_0 \lesssim 100\) MeV and \(\omega < 40\) MeV, the cross section is dominated by the halo-neutron peak.

## Context

This work extends the authors’ prior formalism for elastic and exclusive \((e,e^\prime \alpha)\) and \((e,e^\prime n)\) scattering on \(^6\)He (Garrido and Moya de Guerra, 1999) to the inclusive \((e,e^\prime)\) case, providing the first theoretical predictions for the kinematic conditions under which \(\alpha\)-knockout and halo-neutron knockout dominate. It enables experimental design for future electron-beam experiments at RIKEN, GSI, and Mainz, and the method is stated to be extendable to other halo nuclei. The calculations use the CC1 prescription for the nucleon current (Forest and Walecka, 1983) and experimental \(\alpha\)-particle charge densities (Vries et al., 1987), with three-body wave functions from Faddeev solutions (Fedorov et al., 1995).
