# Hadron-hadron potentials coupled to quark degrees of freedom for exotic hadrons

> We study the properties of the hadron-hadron potentials and quark-antiquark potentials from the viewpoint of the channel coupling. We demonstrate that, for finite quark masses, the coupling to the two-hadron continuum induces the imaginary part of the quark-antiquark potential, in contrast to the string-breaking phenomena in the static limit. It is also shown that the elimination of the different degrees of freedom induces the nonlocality and energy dependence of the effective potentials. For the obtained nonlocal potentials, we apply two methods of the local approximation proposed previously, the formal derivative expansion and the derivative expansion in the HAL QCD method, by carefully examining the energy dependence of the potential. As an application, we construct a coupled-channel model of $c\bar{c}$ and $D^{0}\bar{D}^{*0}$ to describe $X(3872)$, and discuss the property of the effective $D^{0}\bar{D}^{*0}$ potentials. We confirm that the local approximation by the HAL QCD method works better than the formal derivative expansion also for the energy-dependent potential. At the same time, we show that, in the HAL QCD method, the resulting phase shift is sensitive to the choice of the wavefunction to construct the local potential when the system has a shallow bound state such as $X(3872)$.

## Key claim
For finite quark masses, the coupling to the two-hadron continuum induces an imaginary part in the quark-antiquark potential, unlike the string-breaking phenomena in the static limit where no imaginary part appears. The local approximation by the HAL QCD method works better than the formal derivative expansion for energy-dependent potentials, but the resulting phase shift is sensitive to the choice of wavefunction used to construct the local potential when the system has a shallow bound state such as X(3872).

## Method
The authors formulate a coupled-channel Hamiltonian in non-relativistic quantum mechanics using Feshbach's method, coupling a quark-antiquark channel with a confinement potential to a two-hadron channel with a scattering potential via a transition potential. They derive effective nonlocal, energy-dependent potentials by eliminating one channel, then apply two local approximation methods (formal derivative expansion and the HAL QCD derivative expansion) to the separable nonlocal potentials with Yukawa-type form factors. As an application, they construct a coupled-channel model of c-cbar and D0-D*0bar to describe X(3872) and numerically evaluate the effective D0-D*0bar potentials.

## Key numbers
- The scattering length a0 for the Yukawa-type nonlocal potential is given by a0 = +8π m ω(E=0) / [μ(4π m ω(E=0) + μ^3)], where μ is the cutoff constant and ω(E) is the energy-dependent strength.
- The local approximated potential by the formal derivative expansion at lowest order is V_formal(r,E) = ω(E) (4π/μ^2) (e^{-μr}/r).
- The k0 → 0 limit of the HAL QCD local potential is V_HAL(r;k0=0) = [a0 μ^2 e^{-μr}] / [2m (r - a0 + a0 e^{-μr})].
- The wavefunction in the k0 → 0 limit is ψ_{k0}(r) = (r - a0 + a0 e^{-μr})/r.

## Context
This work extends the study of quark-antiquark potentials from the static limit (Bali 2005) to finite quark masses, where the coupling to the two-hadron continuum produces an imaginary part in the potential. It builds on the HAL QCD method (Aoki 2021) for local approximation of nonlocal potentials and applies the framework to the exotic hadron X(3872), whose structure is considered a mixture of a c-cbar core state and a D-D* molecular component (Takizawa 2012, Hosaka 2016). The results enable improved modeling of hadron-hadron interactions with coupled quark degrees of freedom, particularly for near-threshold exotic states.
