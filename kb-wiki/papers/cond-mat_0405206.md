# Universal Properties of Two-Dimensional Boson Droplets

> We consider a system of N nonrelativistic bosons in two dimensions, interacting weakly via a short-range attractive potential. We show that for N large, but below some critical value, the properties of the N-boson bound state are universal. In particular, the ratio of the binding energies of (N+1)- and N-boson systems, B_{N+1}/B_N, approaches a finite limit, approximately 8.567, at large N. We also confirm previous results that the three-body system has exactly two bound states. We find for the ground state B_3^(0) = 16.522688(1) B_2 and for the excited state B_3^(1) = 1.2704091(1) B_2.

## Key claim

For a system of \(N\) nonrelativistic bosons in two dimensions with weak short-range attractive interactions, the ratio of binding energies \(B_{N+1}/B_N\) approaches a finite limit of approximately 8.567 at large \(N\), but this universal behavior holds only for \(1 \ll N \ll N_{\text{crit}} \approx 0.931 \ln(R_2/\ell)\), where \(\ell\) is the next largest length scale in the problem (such as the van der Waals length or the range of the potential).

## Method

The authors used a variational mean-field approach with a scale-dependent coupling constant, incorporating the renormalization group running of the coupling to account for asymptotic freedom in two dimensions. They minimized the energy functional with respect to the droplet size and shape, solving the resulting Hartree-like differential equation numerically via the shooting method. For the three-body system, they employed an effective field theory with a dimeron auxiliary field and solved the homogeneous integral equation for the three-body bound state amplitude projected onto the S-wave.

## Key numbers

- Ratio of binding energies for large \(N\): \(B_{N+1}/B_N \approx 8.567\) (asymptotic limit)
- Ratio of droplet sizes for large \(N\): \(R_{N+1}/R_N \approx 0.3417\)
- Three-body ground state binding energy: \(B_3^{(0)} = 16.522688(1) B_2\)
- Three-body excited state binding energy: \(B_3^{(1)} = 1.2704091(1) B_2\)
- Critical particle number for universality breakdown: \(N_{\text{crit}} \approx 0.931 \ln(R_2/\ell) + \mathcal{O}(1)\)
- Optimal shape parameters from numerical solution: \(A = \frac12 B = C \approx 1.862\), giving \(B/(AC) \approx 1.074\)

## Context

This work extends the study of universal properties of few-body systems with short-range interactions from three dimensions (where the Efimov effect is known) to two-dimensional boson droplets with attractive interactions. It confirms and improves upon earlier three-body calculations by Bruch and Tjon and by Nielsen et al., and provides a framework for understanding the exponential growth of binding energy with particle number. The results enable future calculations of \(N\)-body ground state energies for \(N>4\) and suggest possible experimental realization near Feshbach resonances, while raising open questions about extension to three-dimensional systems with large scattering length as studied by Bulgac.
