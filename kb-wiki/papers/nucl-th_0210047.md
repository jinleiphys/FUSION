# Energy Density Functional Approach to Superfluid Nuclei

> We show that within the framework of a simple local nuclear energy density functional (EDF), one can describe accurately the one-- and two--nucleon separation energies of semi--magic nuclei. While for the normal part of the EDF we use previously suggested parameterizations, for the superfluid part of the EDF we use the simplest possible local form compatible with known nuclear symmetries.

## Key claim

The authors show that a simple local nuclear energy density functional (EDF) can accurately describe one- and two-nucleon separation energies of semi-magic nuclei, but only for the specific combination of coupling constants \(g = g_0 + g_1\) that can be tested in these systems; the other combination \(g' = g_0 - g_1\) remains untested.

## Method

The authors construct a superfluid EDF with the simplest local form compatible with nuclear symmetries, using a zero-range pairing interaction renormalized with an explicit energy cutoff \(E_c\) (approximately 70 MeV for SLy4 and 55 MeV for FaNDF\(^0\)). They solve the Bogoliubov–de Gennes equations in coordinate representation for spherical nuclei, treating even and odd particle numbers within the same framework, and evaluate densities via contour integration of Gorkov propagators in the complex energy plane. For the normal part of the EDF they use either the SLy4 Skyrme parameterization or Fayans' FaNDF\(^0\) functional.

## Key numbers

*   For tin and lead isotopes with constant pairing, the bare coupling constant is \(g(\mathbf{r}) = -250\ \mathrm{MeV\ fm}^3\) for SLy4 and \(g(\mathbf{r}) = -200\ \mathrm{MeV\ fm}^3\) for FaNDF\(^0\).
*   For the half volume–half surface pairing model with SLy4, the parameters are \(V_0 = -370\ \mathrm{MeV\ fm}^3\) and \(\rho_c = 0.32\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\).
*   The rms deviation of two-neutron separation energies \(S_{2N}\) for the Z=50 (tin) chain is 0.29 MeV, and for the Z=82 (lead) chain it is 0.23 MeV, using the present approach.
*   The rms deviation of one-neutron separation energies \(S_N\) for the Z=50 chain is 0.21 MeV, and for the Z=82 chain it is 0.37 MeV.
*   For the N=126 isotone chain, the rms deviation of \(S_{2N}\) is 0.42 MeV and of \(S_N\) is 0.23 MeV.
*   The energy cutoff \(E_c\) is chosen as approximately 70 MeV for SLy4 and 55 MeV for FaNDF\(^0\), ensuring convergence of the pairing field \(\Delta(\mathbf{r})\) with a relative error of about \(10^{-5}\) for density-independent bare couplings.

## Context

This work extends the local EDF approach to superfluid nuclei, building on the Hohenberg–Kohn theorem (1964) and the authors' own renormalization framework for zero-range pairing interactions (Yu, Bulgac 2002; Bulgac, Yu 2002). It contrasts with approaches using finite-range pairing interactions (Gogny 1975) or explicit energy cutoffs (Goriely, Pearson 2000), arguing that those are equivalent and lack physical content. The results enable a simpler, parameter-economical description of pairing in semi-magic nuclei, using only one or two parameters for the pairing channel, and suggest that the density dependence of the pairing coupling is not strongly constrained by separation energies alone.
