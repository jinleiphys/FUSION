# Event multiplicity, transverse momentum and energy dependence of charged particle production, and system thermodynamics in $pp$ collisions at the Large Hadron Collider

> In the present work, we study the recent collision energy and multiplicity dependence of the charged particle transverse momentum spectra as measured by the ALICE collaboration in $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 5.02 and 13 TeV using the non-extensive Tsallis distribution and the Boltzmann-Gibbs Blast Wave (BGBW) model. A thermodynamically consistent form of the Tsallis distribution is used to extract the kinetic freeze-out parameters from the transverse momentum spectra of charged particles at mid-rapidity. In addition, a comprehensive study of fitting range dependence of transverse momentum spectra on the freeze-out parameters is done using Tsallis statistics. The applicability of BGBW model is verified by fitting the transverse momentum spectra of the bulk part ($\sim 2.5~ {\rm GeV}/c$)for both 5.02 and 13 TeV energies and also in different multiplicity classes. The radial flow, $<β>$ is almost independent of collision energy and multiplicity whereas the behavior of kinetic freeze-out temperature significantly depends on multiplicity classes. It is found that the Tsallis distribution generally leads to a better description for the complete transverse momentum spectra whereas the BGBW model explains the bulk part of the system.

## Key claim

The Tsallis non-extensive distribution provides a better description of the complete transverse momentum spectra of charged particles in pp collisions at LHC energies, while the Boltzmann-Gibbs Blast Wave (BGBW) model explains only the bulk part (up to about 2.5 GeV/c); however, the Tsallis fit quality degrades for the highest multiplicity class (V0M1) at high pT, and the extracted parameters show a strong dependence on the fitting range.

## Method

The authors fit charged particle transverse momentum spectra measured by ALICE in pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.02 and 13 TeV, for ten V0M event multiplicity classes at mid-rapidity, using a thermodynamically consistent form of the Tsallis non-extensive distribution (with weight factors 0.8, 0.12, and 0.08 for pions, kaons, and protons, respectively). They also fit the bulk part of the spectra (up to about 2.5 GeV/c) using the BGBW model with a linear flow profile (n=1) and the same particle weight factors. A comprehensive study of the fitting range dependence is performed by varying the maximum pT from 1.2 to 20 GeV/c for the highest (V0M1) and lowest (V0M10) multiplicity classes.

## Key numbers

- Charged particle multiplicity densities for V0M1 (highest multiplicity) class: 19.2 ± 0.9 at sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV and 26.6 ± 1.1 at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV.
- Charged particle multiplicity densities for V0M10 (lowest multiplicity) class: 2.27 ± 0.27 at sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV and 2.54 ± 0.26 at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV.
- The Tsallis fits are performed up to pT = 20 GeV/c; the BGBW fits cover the bulk part up to about 2.5 GeV/c.
- The non-extensive parameter q increases with multiplicity and remains almost constant above dNch/deta about 15 for both energies.
- The radial flow velocity from BGBW is almost independent of collision energy and multiplicity; the kinetic freeze-out temperature Tkin increases with multiplicity.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of Tsallis statistics in pp collisions at RHIC and LHC (Cleymans, Wilk, and others) and the BGBW model used in heavy-ion collisions (Schnedermann 1993). It directly analyzes the ALICE data on charged particle spectra at 5.02 and 13 TeV (Acharya 2019). The results enable a systematic comparison of freeze-out parameters across multiplicity classes and collision energies, and the observed threshold at dNch/deta about 15 and pT about 8 GeV/c motivates further event-shape-dependent studies to understand the transition from equilibrium-like to jet-dominated behavior in high-multiplicity pp collisions.
