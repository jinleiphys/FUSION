# NN and NDelta Form Factors viewed from ChPT

> I discuss recent work on nucleon form factors, magnetic strangeness in the nucleon and the isovector nucleon-delta transition based on the broken chiral symmetry of QCD, utilizing recent theoretical developments in ChPT.

## Key claim

Chiral Perturbation Theory (ChPT) can provide model-independent predictions for the low-momentum-transfer evolution of baryon form factors, but the overall normalization at zero momentum transfer (such as the strange magnetic moment) often requires additional information from unknown counterterms, limiting the predictive power to the shape rather than the absolute value.

## Method

The author applies Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (HBChPT) and its extension, the "small scale expansion" (SSE), which includes the Delta(1232) resonance as an explicit degree of freedom, to calculate nucleon form factors, the strange magnetic form factor, and the isovector nucleon-delta transition form factors. Calculations are performed to third order in the chiral expansion, with the momentum dependence of the strange magnetic form factor derived from kaon-loop contributions and the isoscalar form factor. For the NDelta transition, the matrix element is expanded in inverse powers of the nucleon mass to match the non-relativistic SSE calculation with the general relativistic amplitude.

## Key numbers

- The leading-order HBChPT result for the isovector Pauli form factor radius squared is \((r_2^v)^2 = 0.52\ \mathrm{fm}^2\); inclusion of explicit delta components adds a \(+0.09\ \mathrm{fm}^2\) correction, compared to the empirical value of \(0.80\ \mathrm{fm}^2\).
- The strange magnetic form factor \(G_M^{(s)}(Q^2)\) is predicted to \({\cal O}(p^3)\) in SU(3) HBChPT, with the momentum dependence given by a function \(f(Q^2)\) that rises almost linearly with \(Q^2\) for small and moderate \(Q^2\); the analysis uses \(F_\pi \equiv (F_\pi + F_K)/2 \simeq 102\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and the kaon mass \(m_K\).
- The ratio of E2/M1 for the photoexcitation of the Delta(1232) is observed to be about -1% to -3% from pion photoproduction data, indicating non-zero quadrupole strength.

## Context

This work extends earlier one-loop relativistic and non-relativistic HBChPT analyses of nucleon form factors (Gasser et al., 1988; Bernard et al., 1992) by incorporating the Delta(1232) resonance via the SSE formalism (Hemmert et al., 1997). The predictions for the strange magnetic form factor provide a low-energy benchmark for the SAMPLE experiment at Bates and the upcoming G0 experiment at Jefferson Lab. The analysis of the NDelta transition form factors aims to resolve discrepancies in the treatment of 1/M corrections and counterterms that have affected previous ChPT calculations of the E2/M1 ratio (Butler et al., 1993).
