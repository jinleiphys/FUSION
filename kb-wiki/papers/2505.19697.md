# Study of $p_\mathrm{T}$-differential radial flow in blast-wave model

> The transverse momentum-differential radial flow observable $v_0(p_\mathrm{T})$, recently proposed and measured by the ATLAS and ALICE collaborations, provides a novel tool to probe radial expansion dynamics in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. In this work, we conduct a detailed study of $v_0(p_\mathrm{T})$ using a blast-wave model that incorporates hydrodynamic-like expansion and thermal emission. We introduce event-by-event fluctuations in the transverse expansion velocity and kinetic freeze-out temperature using Gaussian probability distributions. Our results show that increasing the mean expansion velocity leads to a clear mass ordering in $v_0(p_\mathrm{T})$, while fluctuations in both expansion velocity and freeze-out temperature significantly enhance the magnitude of $v_0(p_\mathrm{T})$, particularly at higher $p_\mathrm{T}$. We fit blast-wave model calculations for identified hadrons ($π$, K, and p) to recent ALICE data from Pb--Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV using a Bayesian parameter estimation framework. The extracted mean transverse expansion velocity decreases, while the kinetic freeze-out temperature increases, from central to peripheral collisions. Additionally, the freeze-out temperatures inferred from $v_0(p_\mathrm{T})$ are systematically higher than those obtained from conventional $p_\mathrm{T}$-spectra fits, likely due to the reduced sensitivity of $v_0(p_\mathrm{T})$ to resonance decay contributions.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the blast-wave model, when augmented with Gaussian event-by-event fluctuations in transverse expansion velocity and kinetic freeze-out temperature, can reproduce the key features of the recently measured $p_\mathrm{T}$-differential radial flow observable $v_0(p_\mathrm{T})$ for identified hadrons, but the extracted freeze-out temperatures are systematically higher than those from conventional $p_\mathrm{T}$-spectra fits, likely due to the reduced sensitivity of $v_0(p_\mathrm{T})$ to resonance decay contributions.

## Method
The authors used a Boltzmann-Gibbs blast-wave model with a parametrized radial expansion velocity profile to generate event-by-event $p_\mathrm{T}$ spectra for pions, kaons, and protons. They introduced Gaussian fluctuations in the surface transverse expansion velocity ($\beta_s$) and kinetic freeze-out temperature ($T_\mathrm{kin}$), systematically varying the mean and width of each parameter to study their effects on $v_0(p_\mathrm{T})$. The model predictions were then fit to ALICE data from Pb--Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV using a Bayesian parameter estimation framework with Markov Chain Monte Carlo sampling to extract posterior distributions for the blast-wave parameters and their fluctuation widths.

## Key numbers
- For the 10--20% centrality class, the extracted surface velocity is $\beta_s = 0.8959^{+0.0063}_{-0.0073}$, with fluctuation width $\sigma(\beta_s) = 0.0115^{+0.0007}_{-0.0007}$.
- For the 10--20% centrality class, the extracted kinetic freeze-out temperature is $T_\mathrm{kin} = 0.1357^{+0.0082}_{-0.0073}$ GeV, with fluctuation width $\sigma(T_\mathrm{kin}) = 0.0022^{+0.0010}_{-0.0011}$ GeV.
- For the 60--70% peripheral centrality class, $\beta_s$ decreases to $0.7005^{+0.0379}_{-0.0424}$, while $T_\mathrm{kin}$ increases to $0.2346^{+0.0185}_{-0.0183}$ GeV.
- The fluctuation widths increase from central to peripheral collisions: $\sigma(\beta_s)$ rises from 0.0115 to 0.0455, and $\sigma(T_\mathrm{kin})$ rises from 0.0022 GeV to 0.0201 GeV.
- The $p_\mathrm{T}$ intervals used for fitting were 0.5--1 GeV/$c$ for pions, 0.2--1.5 GeV/$c$ for kaons, and 0.3--3 GeV/$c$ for protons.

## Context
This work extends the study of the $v_0(p_\mathrm{T})$ observable, recently proposed by Schenke (2020) and measured by the ALICE (2025) and ATLAS (2025) collaborations, by providing a simplified blast-wave model interpretation. It complements full (3+1)D hydrodynamic simulations using the MUSIC framework (Schenke 2020, Parida 2024) that showed mass ordering in $v_0(p_\mathrm{T})$ and sensitivity to bulk viscosity and the QCD equation of state. The results enable a direct comparison between parameters extracted from $v_0(p_\mathrm{T})$ and those from traditional blast-wave fits to $p_\mathrm{T}$ spectra (ALICE 2019), revealing that $v_0(p_\mathrm{T})$ provides a probe of freeze-out conditions with reduced sensitivity to resonance decay contributions.
