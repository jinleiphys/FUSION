# Beam energy dependence of Elliptic and Triangular flow with the AMPT model

> A beam energy scan has been carried out at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider at Brookhaven National Laboratory to search for the onset of deconfinement and a possible critical point where the transition from a Quark Gluon Plasma to a hadronic phase changes from a rapid cross-over to a first order phase transition. Anisotropy in the azimuthal distribution of produced particles such as the second and third harmonics $v_2$ and $v_3$ are expected to be sensitive to the existence of a Quark Gluon Plasma phase and the Equation of State of the system. For this reason, they are of great experimental interests. In this Letter we report on calculations of $v_2$ and $v_3$ from the AMPT model in the Default(Def.) and String Melting(SM) mode to provide a reference for the energy dependence of $v_2$ and $v_3$ for $\sqrt{s_{_{NN}}}$ from 7.7 GeV to 2.76 TeV. We expect that in the case that collisions cease to produce QGP at lower colliding energies, data will deviate from the AMPT String Melting calculations and come in better agreement with the Default calculations.

## Key claim

The AMPT model in both String Melting (SM) and Default modes can describe the beam energy dependence of elliptic flow ($v_2$) and triangular flow ($v_3$) from $\sqrt{s_{_{NN}}}=7.7$ GeV to 2.76 TeV, providing a baseline for the RHIC Beam Energy Scan. The authors expect that if collisions cease to produce a Quark Gluon Plasma at lower energies, experimental data will deviate from the AMPT String Melting calculations and agree better with the Default calculations.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model in two modes: Default mode (string and minijets without a QGP phase, using HIJING, parton cascade ZPC, Lund string fragmentation, and ART hadronic transport) and String Melting mode (strings decomposed into partons, parton cascade, hadronization via coalescence, then ART). They fixed model parameters (a=2.2, b=0.5 GeV$^{-2}$, $\alpha_s$=0.47, $\mu$=1.8 fm$^{-1}$) with initial and final state radiation in HIJING turned off, and calculated $v_2$ and $v_3$ for Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions across the full energy range without adjusting parameters per energy.

## Key numbers

- Charged particle multiplicity density scaled by $N_{\mathrm{part}}/2$ for 0-5% central collisions from AMPT SM and Default agrees with experimental data across $\sqrt{s_{_{NN}}}=7.7$ GeV to 2.76 TeV.
- For $p_T<1$ GeV, $v_2\{\mathrm{RP}\}$ in AMPT SM varies by about 5% from 7.7 GeV up to 200 GeV; going from 200 GeV to 2.76 TeV, $v_2\{\mathrm{RP}\}$ increases by 20%, independent of $p_T$.
- At $p_T=1.5$ GeV, $v_2\{\mathrm{RP}\}$ for 7.7 GeV is 20% larger than for 200 GeV in AMPT SM.
- The slope $d\langle v_3\rangle/d\varepsilon_3$ increases linearly with $\sqrt{N_{\mathrm{part}}}$ for all energies studied (7.7, 39, 200 GeV, and 2.76 TeV).
- At 7.7 GeV, $v_3\{2\}$ and $v_3\{\mathrm{PP}\}$ from AMPT SM are equivalent, indicating negligible non-flow from minijets.
- For parameter set A ($\mu=1.8$ fm$^{-1}$, $\alpha_s=0.47$), the estimated $\eta_s/s$ at 378 MeV is 0.088, close to the ADS/CFT lower bound of $1/4\pi$.

## Context

This work extends prior AMPT studies by Xu et al. (2011) that described $v_2$ and $v_3$ at 200 GeV and 2.76 TeV, and by Alver and Roland (2010) who showed $v_3$ proportional to initial $\varepsilon_{3,\mathrm{part}}$ within AMPT. It provides a reference for the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program searching for the onset of deconfinement and a critical point, enabling comparison of experimental $v_2$ and $v_3$ data across $\sqrt{s_{_{NN}}}=7.7$ GeV to 2.76 TeV to distinguish between scenarios with and without a QGP phase. The study also demonstrates that AMPT can describe data with a wide range of $\eta_s/s$ values, highlighting the need to understand initial entropy production to extract the correct viscosity.
