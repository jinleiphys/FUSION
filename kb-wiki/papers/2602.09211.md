# Reply to "Comment on 'QCD factorization with multihadron fragmentation functions'"

> Reply to arXiv:2502.15817v2. 6 pages. This is a version of a reply to appear in Physical Review D. Equation numbering here references the arXiv version of Ref.~\cite{Pitonyak:2025lin}

# Wiki Page: Reply to "Comment on 'QCD factorization with multihadron fragmentation functions'"

## Key claim

The authors defend the standard operator definition of multihadron fragmentation functions against the claim by Pitonyak et al. (2025) that the standard definition lacks a number density interpretation and must be modified with external-state-dependent prefactors. They argue that the proposed alternative definition breaks factorization by introducing hadron-number-dependent factors into the hard part, and that the sum rule used to motivate the modification is invalid.

## Method

The authors analyze the factorization theorem for semi-inclusive annihilation (SIA) with multihadron final states, comparing the standard fragmentation function definition (based on quark expectation values of hadron number density operators) with the modified definition proposed by Pitonyak et al. (2023, 2025). They demonstrate that the standard definition yields a parton-model expression where the fragmentation function is a density in physical hadronic phase space, with no need for reduced fragmentation functions. They show that the alternative definition requires shifting external-state-dependent factors between the fragmentation function and the hard part, which violates the principle that hard parts must be independent of nonperturbative external states.

## Key numbers

- The standard parton model expression is given as (1/σ₀) dσ/(dz dY') = d(z, {p_h}), where d(z, {p_h}) is the standard fragmentation function for a multihadron system with small invariant mass.
- The phase space differential dY' can be expressed as dY' = dM_h dζ dφ_{R,H} M_h / 32π³ (from Eq. (106) of Rogers et al. (2024)).
- The alternative definition from Pitonyak et al. (2025) replaces the standard quark normalization factor H = (2π)³ 2ξ with an external-state-dependent factor H → (2π)³ 2ξ₁ × ... × (2π)³ 2ξ_n ∝ ξ^n, introducing dependence on the number of hadrons n.

## Context

This reply extends the debate initiated by Rogers et al. (2024) defending standard QCD factorization for multihadron fragmentation functions against criticisms by Pitonyak et al. (2023, 2025). The authors invoke the textbook treatment by Collins (2011) and earlier work by Collins (1994) establishing that changing the identity of the observed final state requires no changes to the factorization theorem beyond replacing the hadronic state. The reply also references Collins (2023) and Rogers (2024) for earlier explanations of why the sum rule in Pitonyak et al. (2023) is invalid. The work enables continued use of standard fragmentation function definitions in nonperturbative QCD studies, including recent lattice QCD calculations by Galvez-Viruet et al. (2025), Qian et al. (2024), Grieninger et al. (2024), Araz et al. (2024), von Kuk et al. (2025), Lee et al. (2025), and Kang et al. (2025).
