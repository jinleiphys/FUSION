# Superheavy Nuclei: Relativistic Mean Field Outlook

> The analysis of quasiparticle spectra in heaviest $A\sim 250$ nuclei with spectroscopic data provides an additional constraint for the choice of effective interaction for the description of superheavy nuclei. It strongly suggest that only the parametrizations of the relativistic mean field Lagrangian which predict Z=120 and N=172 as shell closures are reliable for superheavy nuclei. The influence of the central depression in the density distribution of spherical superheavy nuclei on the shell structure is studied. Large central depression produces large shell gaps at Z=120 and N=172. The shell gaps at Z=126 and N=184 are favored by a flat density distribution in the central part of nucleus. It is shown that approximate particle number projection (PNP) by means of the Lipkin-Nogami method removes pairing collapse seen at these gaps in the calculations without PNP.

# Superheavy Nuclei: Relativistic Mean Field Outlook

## Key claim

The analysis of quasiparticle spectra in heaviest A~250 nuclei strongly suggests that only parametrizations of the relativistic mean field Lagrangian which predict Z=120 and N=172 as shell closures are reliable for superheavy nuclei, but the accuracy of the description of the ν4s1/2 state is unknown, so the existence of the N=184 gap cannot be excluded.

## Method

The study used cranked relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (CRHB) theory with and without approximate particle number projection via the Lipkin-Nogami method to calculate quasiparticle states in odd-mass deformed nuclei of the A~250 region (249,251Cf and 249Bk). The calculated single-particle energies were compared with experimental spectroscopic data to determine "empirical shifts" for spherical subshells, which were then used to constrain the predictions of shell closures in spherical superheavy nuclei. The influence of central depression in nuclear density distribution on shell structure was studied in spherical RMF calculations without pairing using the NL3 parametrization.

## Key numbers

- The effective mass of nucleons at the Fermi surface in RMF theory is m*(k_F)/m ≈ 0.66, which leads to a stretching of the energy scale compared to experiment
- In the NL1 and NL3 parametrizations, the energies of spherical subshells are described with accuracy better than 0.5 MeV for most subshells, with discrepancies in the range of 0.6-1.0 MeV for some subshells (π1h9/2, ν1i11/2, ν1j15/2, ν2g9/2)
- The scaling factor for the Gogny D1S force in the pairing channel was f=1.0 in calculations without Lipkin-Nogami and f=0.864 in calculations with Lipkin-Nogami
- The energy of the 1i13/2 state in the NL1 parametrization was increased by 0.78 MeV to align with NL3 for comparison

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Afanasjev (2005) on quasiparticle spectra in heavy nuclei and provides constraints that rule out the NLSH and NL-RA1 parametrizations, which predict Z=114 as a magic proton number (as reported by Lahanas et al. 1996 and the NL-RA1 collaboration). The study also compares with density-dependent parametrizations DD-ME1 and DD-ME2 (developed by Niksic et al. 2002 and 2005), finding they do not remove problems with the π1h9/2 state description. The results enable future work on exact particle number projection for pairing properties in superheavy nuclei, which the author identifies as necessary given that the Lipkin-Nogami method may be less reliable in the regime of weak pairing typical for large shell gaps.
