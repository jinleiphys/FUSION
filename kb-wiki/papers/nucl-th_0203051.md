# Predictive Ability of QCD Sum Rules for Excited Baryons

> The masses of octet baryons are calculated by the method of QCD sum rules. Using generalized interpolating fields, three independent sets of QCD sum rules are derived which allow the extraction of low-lying N* states with spin-parity 1/2+, 1/2- and 3/2- in both the non-strange and strange channels. The predictive ability of the sum rules is examined by a Monte-Carlo based analysis procedure in which the three phenomenological parameters (mass, coupling, threshold) are treated as free parameters simultaneously. Realistic uncertainties in these parameters are obtained by simultaneously exploring all uncertainties in the QCD input parameters. Those sum rules with good predictive power are identified and their predictions are compared with experiment where available.

## Key claim

The QCD sum rule method can predict masses of low-lying excited baryons (N* states with spin-parity 1/2+, 1/2-, and 3/2- in both non-strange and strange channels), but the predictive power is sum-rule dependent and must be assessed by a Monte-Carlo based analysis that treats the three phenomenological parameters (mass, coupling, threshold) as free parameters simultaneously, while exploring all uncertainties in the QCD input parameters.

## Method

The authors derived three independent sets of QCD sum rules using generalized interpolating fields for octet baryons (N, Sigma, Xi, Lambda) and for spin-3/2 currents, including operators up to dimension 8, first-order strange quark mass corrections, flavor symmetry breaking of strange quark condensates, anomalous dimension corrections, possible factorization violation of the four-quark condensate, and a pole-plus-continuum model. They applied a Monte-Carlo based analysis procedure that simultaneously treats the mass, coupling, and continuum threshold as free parameters and maps uncertainties in QCD input parameters into uncertainties in the phenomenological parameters, with careful regard to OPE convergence and ground-state dominance.

## Key numbers

- The Ioffe formula gives M_N approximately equal to (-8 pi^2 <q-bar q>)^(1/3) = 0.97 GeV, which is an oversimplified result keeping only leading-order contributions in the nucleon QCD sum rules.
- The renormalization scale mu is set to 500 MeV for the anomalous dimension corrections.
- The four-quark condensate is parameterized by kappa_v via the factorization approximation <u-bar u u-bar u> = kappa_v <u-bar u>^2.
- The chiral-even sum rule for the nucleon from the mixed correlator has zero continuum contribution, while for strange baryons the continuum contribution is small, making it difficult to select a valid Borel region independently.

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rules approach to excited baryons (N* physics) building on the foundational method of Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1979) and early baryon studies by Ioffe (1981), Belyaev and Ioffe (1982, 1983), and Chung et al. (1984). It follows a similar Monte-Carlo based analysis procedure introduced by Leinweber (1996) for decuplet baryons, and aims to provide a complete, updated set of sum rules for octet baryons under one roof, enabling future applications to three-point functions for matrix elements such as magnetic moments, transition moments, axial charges, and tensor charges.
