# Giant and pigmy dipole resonances in 4He, 16,22O, and 40Ca from chiral nucleon-nucleon interactions

> We combine the coupled-cluster method and the Lorentz integral transform for the computation of inelastic reactions into the continuum. We show that the bound-state-like equation characterizing the Lorentz integral transform method can be reformulated based on extensions of the coupled-cluster equation-of-motion method, and we discuss strategies for viable numerical solutions. Starting from a chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order, we compute the giant dipole resonances of 4He, 16,22O and 40Ca, truncating the coupled-cluster equation-of-motion method at the two-particle-two-hole excitation level. Within this scheme, we find a low-lying E1 strength in the neutron-rich 22O nucleus, which compares fairly well with data from [Leistenschneider et al. Phys. Rev. Lett. 86, 5442 (2001)]. We also compute the electric dipole polariziability in 40Ca. Deficiencies of the employed Hamiltonian lead to overbinding, too small charge radii and a too small electric dipole polarizability in 40Ca.

## Key claim

The authors combine the coupled-cluster method with the Lorentz integral transform to compute dipole response functions in medium-mass nuclei, finding that within the two-particle-two-hole truncation the low-lying E1 strength in neutron-rich 22O compares fairly well with experimental data, although deficiencies in the employed chiral Hamiltonian lead to overbinding, too small charge radii, and a too small electric dipole polarizability in 40Ca.

## Method

The authors reformulate the Lorentz integral transform method using the equation-of-motion coupled-cluster framework, solving the resulting linear equations for the right and left Lorentz states at the singles-and-doubles excitation level (LIT-CCSD). They employ a Lanczos algorithm adapted for non-Hermitian complex symmetric matrices to compute the Lorentz integral transform efficiently across all energy parameters without repeated matrix inversions. The method is applied to 4He, 16O, 22O, and 40Ca using a chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) from Entem and Machleidt (2003), with the dipole operator written in translationally invariant form.

## Key numbers

- 4He binding energy from CCSD: 23.97 MeV; 3H binding energy from CCSD: 7.37 MeV, giving a threshold omega_th = 16.60 MeV
- 4He binding energy from EIHH: 25.39 MeV; 3H binding energy from EIHH: 7.85 MeV, giving a threshold omega_th = 17.54 MeV
- LIT convergence achieved with 60 Lanczos steps giving numerical precision below one percent, and about 90 Lanczos vectors sufficient for full convergence
- LIT-CCSD calculations for 4He used model spaces up to N_max = 18 with harmonic oscillator frequency hbar Omega = 20 MeV
- The spurious center-of-mass state in the dipole response appears below 1 MeV of excitation energy in model spaces of about 10 oscillator shells

## Context

This work extends the Lorentz integral transform method, previously applied to few-body systems (A <= 7) using Faddeev, correlated hyperspherical-harmonics, EIHH, and no-core shell model approaches, to medium-mass nuclei by formulating it within coupled-cluster theory. The authors build on their earlier Letter reporting first calculations of the giant dipole resonance in 16O (Bacca et al., 2013) and provide comprehensive results including new calculations for the neutron-rich nucleus 22O and the heavier nucleus 40Ca. The method enables first-principles calculations of continuum response functions in nuclei beyond the few-body regime, though the authors note that the employed Hamiltonian's deficiencies require improved chiral three-nucleon forces for quantitative predictions.
