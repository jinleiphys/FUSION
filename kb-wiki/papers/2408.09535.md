# Dissecting a strongly coupled scalar nucleon

> We continue our investigation of the stress within a strongly coupled scalar nucleon, and now dissect the gravitational form factors into contributions from its constituents, the (mock) nucleon and the (mock) pion. The computation is based on a non-perturbative solution of the scalar Yukawa model in the light-front Hamiltonian formalism with a Fock sector expansion including up to one nucleon and two pions. By employing the ``good currents" $T^{++}_i$, $T^{+-}_i$ and $T^{12}_i$, we extract the full set of gravitational form factors $A_i$, $D_i$, $\bar c_i$ without the contamination of the spurious form factors, and free of uncanceled UV divergences. With these results, we decompose the mass of the system into its constituents and compute the matter and mechanical radii, gaining insights into the strongly coupled system.

## Key claim

The paper extracts the individual gravitational form factors (GFFs) for the nucleon and pion degrees of freedom in a strongly coupled scalar Yukawa model, showing that the nucleon degree of freedom contributes 52% of the total mass at coupling α = 1.0 and UV resolution μ_PV = 15 GeV, but this decomposition is renormalization scheme and scale dependent, and the total GFF c̄(Q²) is non-vanishing at finite Q² due to Fock space truncation breaking Poincaré symmetry.

## Method

The authors solve the (3+1)D scalar Yukawa model non-perturbatively using light-front Hamiltonian formalism with a Fock sector expansion truncated at one nucleon and two pions (three-body truncation). They compute hadronic matrix elements of the "good currents" T^{++}_i, T^{+-}_i, and T^{12}_i in the Drell-Yan-Breit frame, which are free of spurious form factors and uncanceled UV divergences, to extract the full set of GFFs A_i, D_i, and c̄_i for the nucleon (i=N) and pion (i=π) constituents. Pauli-Villars regularization with mass μ_PV = 15 GeV is employed.

## Key numbers

- At coupling α = 1.0, the nucleon matter radius r_{A,N} = 0.086 fm, pion matter radius r_{A,π} = 0.51 fm, total matter radius r_A = 0.18 fm
- At α = 1.0, the nucleon mechanical radius r_{D,N} = 0.15 fm, pion mechanical radius r_{D,π} = 1.1 fm, total mechanical radius r_D = 0.86 fm
- At α = 1.0, forward GFFs: A_N(0) = 0.90, A_π(0) = 0.10, D_N(0) = -1.3, D_π(0) = -2.0, D_total(0) = -3.2, c̄_N(0) = -0.38, c̄_π(0) = 0.38, c̄_total(0) = 0.0
- At α = 1.0, the nucleon degree of freedom contributes 52% of the total mass (A_N(0) + c̄_N(0) = 0.52) and the pion degree of freedom contributes 48% (A_π(0) + c̄_π(0) = 0.48)
- At α = 2.0, forward GFFs: A_N(0) = 0.81, A_π(0) = 0.19, D_N(0) = -1.6, D_π(0) = -4.1, D_total(0) = -5.8, c̄_N(0) = -0.87, c̄_π(0) = 0.87
- At α = 0.5 (non-perturbative), r_{A,N} = 0.061 fm, r_{A,π} = 0.50 fm, r_{D,N} = 0.11 fm, r_{D,π} = 1.1 fm

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous investigation of the total GFFs in the scalar Yukawa model (Cao 2023) by now dissecting the GFFs into individual nucleon and pion contributions using the "good currents" formalism developed in their covariant analysis (Cao 2024). The results enable a coupled multi-fluid picture of hadron structure, where the mass, matter radius, and mechanical radius can be decomposed into constituent contributions, analogous to lattice QCD estimates that attribute 43% of the proton mass to quarks and 57% to gluons at 2 GeV (Metz 2020). The approach provides a microscopic wave-function representation that can be extended to QCD bound states as high-quality light-front wave functions become available.
