# Evaluation of particle--anti-particle scaled correlation within effective models

> Correlations and fluctuations of physical quantities are known to play an important role in phase transitions and critical phenomena. In recent years some experimental attempts were made in the scope of the Beam Energy Scan program to locate a possible critical point in the QCD phase diagram. In this work we use the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model to investigate the off-diagonal quark susceptibility, which is related to the quark--anti-quark scaled correlation at the mean field level. We show that this correlation has a significant peak near the critical point and, therefore, may be a useful quantity to measure in experiment. We further study the effects of a repulsive vector coupling, which reduces the strength of the scaled correlation near the critical point.

# Evaluation of particle–anti-particle scaled correlation within effective models

## Key claim

The off-diagonal quark susceptibility, related to the quark–anti-quark scaled correlation at the mean field level, exhibits a significant peak near the critical point in the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model and may therefore be a useful experimental observable; however, a repulsive vector coupling reduces the strength of this scaled correlation near the critical point.

## Method

The authors use the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with two quark flavors (up and down) at finite temperature and chemical potential within the mean field approximation. They compute the off-diagonal quark–anti-quark susceptibility as a second derivative of the thermodynamic potential with respect to quark and anti-quark chemical potentials, then relate this to the proton–anti-proton scaled correlation using isospin symmetry and baryon number conservation factors. The model parameters (cutoff Λ = 590 MeV, coupling GΛ² = 2.435, current quark mass m = 6 MeV) are fixed to reproduce the pion mass, pion decay constant, and quark condensate.

## Key numbers

- The chiral pseudocritical temperature at zero chemical potential is T_pc^(0) = 188 MeV.
- The critical chemical potential in the NJL model is μ_c ≈ 328 MeV.
- The model parameters reproduce f_π = 92.6 MeV, m_π = 140.2 MeV, and ⟨ψ̄ψ⟩^(1/3) = −241.5 MeV.
- The proton–anti-proton scaled correlation R_{p, p̄} extracted from STAR data ranges from 0.0064 ± 0.0433 at √s_NN = 7.7 GeV to 0.00895 ± 0.00113 at √s_NN = 200 GeV.
- At μ = 0, the scaled correlation peaks at the crossover pseudocritical temperature T_pc^(0) = 188 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical exploration of fluctuations and correlations near the QCD critical point, building on the suggestion by Stephanov (2004) that the most singular contribution to two-particle correlators near the critical point comes from sigma meson exchange. The authors use the NJL model, following earlier work by Nambu and Jona-Lasinio (1961) and subsequent developments by Buballa (2003) and Klevansky (1992), to compute a quantity that can be compared with STAR collaboration data from the Beam Energy Scan program (Adamczyk 2013). The results enable future comparisons with more complete approaches such as Dyson-Schwinger or functional renormalization group methods, and motivate improved experimental statistics at low beam energies to detect the predicted critical enhancement.
