# $Λ$(1520) as a probe of resonance-driven deuteron formation at the LHC

> Light nuclei such as deuterons are produced abundantly in high-energy proton-proton and nuclear collisions despite their tiny binding energies. Their production mechanism remains unresolved, as both nucleon coalescence and statistical thermal models reproduce inclusive LHC yields. We propose a direct invariant-mass observable that discriminates between these scenarios using the long-lived $Λ(1520) \to {\rm pK}$ resonance. If decay protons coalesce into deuterons, the produced nuclei remain correlated with the kaon, allowing the resonance peak to be reconstructed experimentally through proxy masses, $M_{\rm (d/2)K}$, formed from kaons and half the deuteron four-momentum. Using Thermal-FIST and PYTHIA with a deuteron coalescence afterburner, we show that a $M_{\rm (d/2)K}$ peak emerges only in the coalescence scenario. This observable provides a direct experimental probe of resonance-fed deuteron production and of late-stage coalescence dynamics in high-energy collisions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a resonance peak in the proxy invariant mass \(M_{\rm (d/2)K}\), constructed from kaons and half the deuteron four-momentum, can discriminate between coalescence and statistical thermal models of deuteron production, but only if the coalescence process does not introduce too large smearing and if the deuteron sample contains a contribution from \(\Lambda(1520)\) decay protons.

## Method

The authors compare two contrasting model baselines: Thermal-FIST, a statistical hadronization model with a Blast-Wave parameterization tuned to ALICE pp data at \(\sqrt{s}=13\) TeV, and PYTHIA 8.3 with a deuteron coalescence afterburner using the Argonne \(v_{18}\) wave function. They generate \(\Lambda(1520)\to {\rm pK}\) and \(\Lambda(1520)\to {\rm (d/2)K}\) invariant mass spectra in the kinematic range \(0.3 < p_{\rm T}^{\rm cand} < 4.0\) GeV/\(c\) and \(|\eta| < 0.8\), using like-sign subtraction to isolate the resonance signal.

## Key numbers

*   The \(\Lambda(1520)\) resonance has an average lifetime of approximately 13 fm/\(c\).
*   The proxy yield extracted from PYTHIA with coalescence in the window \(1.47 < M_{\rm (d/2)K} < 1.57\) GeV/\(c^2\) (corresponding to \(m_{\Lambda(1520)} \pm 3\Gamma\)) is \(S = 1.88 \times 10^4\) over a scaled like-sign background of \(B = 3.28 \times 10^5\), giving a statistical significance \(S/\sqrt{S+B} \simeq 32\).
*   The simulated PYTHIA sample of nearly 1.5 billion minimum bias events corresponds to only about 0.01% of the total events recorded by ALICE in Run 3.

## Context

This work extends the recent ALICE femtoscopic analysis (ALICE 2025) which provided first evidence for resonance-fed deuteron formation using \(\Delta(1232)\) baryons, but where the short resonance lifetime (of order the system size) complicates interpretation. The authors instead use the long-lived \(\Lambda(1520)\) resonance, which decays far from the collision point, to provide a cleaner invariant-mass probe. The observable enables direct experimental tests of late-stage coalescence dynamics and can constrain the space-time picture of light-nucleus production, complementing femtoscopic measurements.
