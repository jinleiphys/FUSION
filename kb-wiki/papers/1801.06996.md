# Investigation of complete and incomplete fusion in $^{7}$Li+$^{124}$Sn reaction around Coulomb barrier energies

> The complete and incomplete fusion cross sections for $^{7}$Li+$^{124}$Sn reaction were measured using online and offline characteristic $γ$-ray detection techniques. The complete fusion (CF) cross sections at energies above the Coulomb barrier were found to be suppressed by $\sim$ 26 \% compared to the coupled channel calculations. This suppression observed in complete fusion cross sections is found to be commensurate with the measured total incomplete fusion (ICF) cross sections. There is a distinct feature observed in the ICF cross sections, i.e., $\textit{t}$-capture is found to be dominant than $α$-capture at all the measured energies. A simultaneous explanation of complete, incomplete and total fusion (TF) data was also obtained from the calculations based on Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel method with short range imaginary potentials. The cross section ratios of CF/TF and ICF/TF obtained from the data as well as the calculations showed the dominance of ICF at below barrier energies and CF at above barrier energies.

## Key claim
The complete fusion (CF) cross sections for the $^{7}$Li+$^{124}$Sn reaction at energies above the Coulomb barrier are suppressed by approximately 26% compared to coupled-channel calculations, and this suppression is commensurate with the measured total incomplete fusion (ICF) cross sections. The authors note that the suppression factor of 26 ± 4% is derived from normalizing the coupled calculations by a factor of 0.74, with the uncertainty estimated from uncertainties in the barrier height and CF cross sections.

## Method
Online and offline characteristic γ-ray detection techniques were used to measure evaporation residues from complete fusion (CF) and incomplete fusion (ICF) for the $^{7}$Li+$^{124}$Sn reaction at beam energies from 17 to 39 MeV. Online measurements employed two Compton-suppressed clover detectors at 125° and 90°, while offline measurements used a HPGe detector on irradiated target-catcher assemblies at six selected energies. Coupled-channel calculations using CCFULL and CDCC calculations using FRESCO 2.9 with short-range imaginary potentials were performed to simultaneously describe CF, ICF, and total fusion (TF) cross sections.

## Key numbers
- CF suppression factor: 26 ± 4% at energies above the Coulomb barrier, obtained by normalizing coupled-channel calculations by a factor of 0.74.
- Coulomb barrier parameters for the system: V_B = 19.7 MeV, R_B = 10.3 fm, ħω = 4.13 MeV.
- Measured CF cross sections range from 0.64 ± 0.22 mb at E_cm = 15.5 MeV to 1056 ± 14 mb at E_cm = 36.6 MeV.
- Total ICF cross sections (t-capture + α-capture) are dominated by t-capture at all measured energies; for example, at E_lab = 35.6 MeV, t-capture residues include $^{126}$Sb$^m$ (19.9 ± 1.5 mb), $^{126}$Sb$^g$ (16.7 ± 1.5 mb), $^{125}$Sb (316 ± 22 mb), and $^{124}$Sb$^g$ (46.9 ± 2.6 mb), while α-capture residues $^{127}$Te and $^{126}$Te are 28.3 ± 1.9 mb and 26.3 ± 4.3 mb, respectively.
- At E_lab = 38.7 MeV, α-capture cross sections are $^{127}$Te = 28.3 ± 2.1 mb and $^{126}$Te = 29.2 ± 4.2 mb.

## Context
This work extends systematic studies of CF suppression for weakly bound projectiles ($^{6,7}$Li, $^{9}$Be) on medium and heavy targets, supporting the observation by Wang (2014) and Kundu (2016) that the suppression factor is independent of target mass. The simultaneous measurement of CF and ICF cross sections over a wide energy range enables comparison with CDCC-based calculations, following the approach of Parkar (2016) for $^{6,7}$Li+$^{209}$Bi and $^{6,7}$Li+$^{198}$Pt reactions. The results enable future tests of breakup and transfer mechanisms in fusion reactions with weakly bound nuclei, relevant for understanding super-heavy element synthesis.
