# Existence of Decreasing Nambu Solutions to the Rainbow Ladder Gap Equation of QCD by Cone Compression

> Studying Nambu solutions of the rainbow-ladder gap equation in QCD at zero temperature and chemical potential, we prove that the mass function emerges continuously from zero as the interaction strength is increased past the critical point for all positive, asymptotically perturbative kernels almost everywhere continuous in $L^1$ using the Krasnosel'skii-Guo Cone Compression Theorem. We prove that the coupled system of equations must have a positive, continuous Nambu solution with decreasing mass function for all current quark masses for a class of models which includes the physical point of a popular model of QCD by using a hybrid Krasnosel'skii-Schauder Fixed Point Theorem.

# Existence of Decreasing Nambu Solutions to the Rainbow Ladder Gap Equation of QCD by Cone Compression

## Key claim

The paper proves that for all positive, asymptotically perturbative kernels almost everywhere continuous in L^1, a positive, continuous Nambu solution with decreasing mass function exists for every current quark mass m ≥ 0, but only when the largest eigenvalue of the operator T_c exceeds unity; below this critical point, no non-trivial Nambu solution exists. The proof relies on a specific choice of weighting function r(p) and the assumption that Z(p) is known and fixed, with extension to the coupled system requiring additional conditions on A_-(p).

## Method

The author studies the rainbow-ladder gap equation of QCD at zero temperature and chemical potential after taking the renormalization scale to infinity, which eliminates Wigner solutions and isolates Nambu solutions. The trace of the gap equation is transformed into a nonlinear integral equation for u(p) = r(p) B(p), where B(p) = M(p)/Z(p) and r(p) is a carefully chosen positive weighting function with logarithmic asymptotic behavior. Existence is proven using the Krasnosel'skii-Guo Cone Compression Theorem on a Banach space of decreasing functions with a norm that includes both supremum and a logarithmic scaling term, establishing compressive boundary conditions at small and large norm. The coupled system for M(p) and Z(p) is then treated using a hybrid Krasnosel'skii-Schauder Fixed Point Theorem, requiring that a lower bound A_-(p) can be found satisfying certain positivity conditions.

## Key numbers

- For the simplest extension kernel, the critical anomalous dimension is γ_m^crit ≥ 0.66 / max(Z^2(p)) from the non-existence bound, achieved at p ~ 1.4 μ
- For the simplest extension kernel, the upper bound on the critical point is γ_m^crit ≤ 1.0 / min(Z^2(p)) with a ~ 0, b = 4 μ
- For the simplest extension, dynamical chiral symmetry breaking occurs at γ_m ≈ 0.74 for Z_c(p) = 1
- For the model of Qin (2011) with Z(p) = 1, the largest eigenvalue of T_c equals one at D/ω^2 ≈ 0.7
- For the model of Qin (2011), the critical point for the coupled system is D/ω^2 = 0.89
- Solutions are guaranteed at the physical point D/ω^2 = 2.4 and for 1.75 < D/ω^2 < 3.6
- The ratio (1 - δ/γ_m)^(γ_m/δ - 1) lies between 1/e and 1 for 0 < δ < γ_m

## Context

This work extends existence proofs for the QCD gap equation beyond the numerical studies of Wang (2012) and the finite-temperature analysis of Aoki (2006), providing rigorous mathematical foundations using fixed-point theory. The approach builds on the cone compression method of Krasnosel'skii and the Krein-Rutman theorem as applied to integral equations by Kwong (2008), and adapts the coupled-system fixed-point framework of Infante (2015) and Infante (2024) to the rainbow-ladder truncation of QCD. The results enable rigorous study of the chiral symmetry restoration transition and guarantee the existence of Nambu solutions for the popular model of Qin (2011), while identifying the Ball-Chui vertex as a significant challenge for future work due to the loss of positive kernel properties.
