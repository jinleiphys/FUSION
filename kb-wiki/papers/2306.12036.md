# Exploring the chiral and deconfinement phase transitions in a self-consistent PNJL model

> In this work, we study the chiral and deconfinement phase transitions in a two-flavor Polyakov loop extended Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model. And note that the self-consistent mean field approximation is employed by introducing an arbitrary parameter $α$ to measure the weights of the Fierz-transformed interaction channels. By making use of this model, we systematically investigate the chiral and deconfinement phase transition lines (as well as the chiral ones in the NJL model for comparison) under different values of $α$. It is found that, the increasing of $α$ helps to enhance the chiral (pseudo)critical temperature at fixed chemical potential, and also to enhance the chiral (pseudo)critical chemical potential at fixed temperature. And the critical end point (CEP) vanishes when $α$ becomes large enough. Besides, we find that the incorporation of Polyakov loop increases $T_{CEP}$ but does not change $μ_{CEP}$ for small values of $α$.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the weighting parameter α, which measures the relative contribution of Fierz-transformed interaction channels in a self-consistent PNJL model, can enhance the chiral (pseudo)critical temperature at fixed chemical potential and the chiral (pseudo)critical chemical potential at fixed temperature, and that the critical end point (CEP) vanishes when α becomes large enough; the incorporation of the Polyakov loop increases T_CEP but does not change μ_CEP for small values of α.

## Method

The authors construct a two-flavor PNJL model Lagrangian by linearly combining the original NJL interaction with its Fierz-transformed counterpart using a weighting parameter α, then add a Polyakov-loop effective potential. They derive the thermodynamic potential and solve the coupled gap equations for the constituent quark mass M, the Polyakov loop fields Φ and Φ̄, and the shifted chemical potential μ̃, using a three-momentum cutoff regularization. The model parameters (current quark mass m = 5.5 MeV, coupling constant g = 5.074 × 10⁻⁶ MeV⁻², cutoff Λ = 631 MeV) are fixed by fitting the pion mass m_π = 138 MeV, pion decay constant f_π = 93 MeV, and quark condensate ⟨φ̄φ⟩ = -(250 MeV)³.

## Key numbers

- For α = 0, the CEP in the PNJL model is at (μ_CEP, T_CEP) = (327, 115) MeV; in the NJL model it is at (328, 47) MeV.
- For α = 0.5, the CEP in the PNJL model is at (μ_CEP, T_CEP) = (347, 78) MeV; in the NJL model it is at (348, 29) MeV.
- The critical value α_c beyond which no CEP exists is approximately 0.71 in both models.
- At zero chemical potential, the chiral critical temperature T_c ≈ 234 MeV in the PNJL model, compared to T_c = 178 MeV in the NJL model.
- The deconfinement critical temperature at zero chemical potential T_d ≈ T_c ≈ 234 MeV for all α values studied.
- The change in the deconfinement critical temperature with chemical potential up to 700 MeV is less than 10% even for α = 0.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent mean field approach previously applied to the NJL model by Wang (2019) and Yu (2020) to the PNJL model, which includes gluonic degrees of freedom via the Polyakov loop. The conventional NJL model (α = 0) and the choice α = 1/2 suggested by Klevansky (1992) are special cases of this generalized framework. The results enable future studies of neutron star equations of state and meson properties in heavy-ion collision experiments, where the weighting parameter α could be constrained by observations.
