# Exact solution of the XXZ Gaudin model with generic open boundaries

> The XXZ Gaudin model with {\it generic} integerable boundaries specified by generic {\it non-diagonal} K-matrices is studied. The commuting families of Gaudin operators are diagonalized by the algebraic Bethe ansatz method. The eigenvalues and the corresponding Bethe ansatz equations are obtained.

## Key claim

The paper provides an exact diagonalization of the XXZ Gaudin model with generic non-diagonal boundary K-matrices, where the boundary parameters are fully generic rather than restricted to diagonal forms as in prior work. The key technical condition is that the face-vertex transformation renders the face-type K-matrices diagonal, enabling the algebraic Bethe ansatz to succeed despite the non-diagonal vertex form.

## Method

The authors construct generalized Gaudin operators from the quasi-classical expansion of the double-row transfer matrix of the inhomogeneous spin-1/2 XXZ open chain with generic non-diagonal K-matrices satisfying the reflection equation and its dual. They apply a face-vertex transformation to map the non-diagonal vertex-type K-matrices to diagonal face-type K-matrices, then use the algebraic Bethe ansatz method for SOS-type models to diagonalize the transfer matrix. The eigenvalues and Bethe ansatz equations for the Gaudin operators are extracted from the expansion of the transfer matrix eigenvalues around the quasi-classical limit eta approaching zero.

## Key numbers

- The Gaudin operators H_j depend on three free boundary parameters {lambda_1, lambda_2, xi} and N = 2M inhomogeneous parameters {z_j}, generalizing previous two-parameter and one-parameter models.
- The Bethe ansatz equations for the Gaudin model are: sum_{j=1}^2 1/[sin(lambda_j+xi-v_a) sin(lambda_j+xi+v_a)] + sum_{k=1}^{2M} 1/[sin(v_a+z_k) sin(v_a-z_k)] = 2 sum_{k != a}^M 1/[sin(v_a+v_k) sin(v_a-v_k)], for a = 1,...,M.
- The eigenvalue E_j of the Gaudin operator H_j is: cot(2z_j) + sum_{j=1}^2 cot(lambda_j+xi-z_j) + sum_{k=1}^M sin(2z_j)/[sin(v_k-z_j) sin(v_k+z_j)].

## Context

This work extends the Gaudin model solutions of Hikami (1995) and Loradan (2002), which treated diagonal K-matrices, and of Amico et al. (2001) which considered specific boundary conditions, to the fully generic non-diagonal case with three free boundary parameters. The results enable the study of Gaudin magnets with the most general integrable open boundary conditions, relevant to BCS theory of small metallic grains, nuclear physics, and QCD theory, and provide a testing ground for the functional Bethe ansatz and separation of variables methods.
