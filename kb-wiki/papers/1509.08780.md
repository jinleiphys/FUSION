# How strange is pion electroproduction?

> We consider pion production in parity-violating electron scattering (PVES) in the presence of nucleon strangeness in the framework of partial wave analysis with unitarity. Using the experimental bounds on the strange form factors obtained in elastic PVES, we study the sensitivity of the parity-violating asymmetry to strange nucleon form factors. For forward kinematics and electron energies above 1 GeV, we observe that this sensitivity may reach about 20\% in the threshold region. With parity-violating asymmetries being as large as tens p.p.m., this study suggests that threshold pion production in PVES can be used as a promising way to better constrain strangeness contributions. Using this model for the neutral current pion production, we update the estimate for the dispersive $γZ$-box correction to the weak charge of the proton. In the kinematics of the Qweak experiment, our new prediction reads Re$\,\Box_{γZ}^V(E=1.165\,{\rm GeV}) = (5.58\pm1.41)\times10^{-3}$, an improvement over the previous uncertainty estimate of $\pm2.0\times10^{-3}$. Our new prediction in the kinematics of the upcoming MESA/P2 experiment reads Re$\,\Box_{γZ}^V(E=0.155\,{\rm GeV}) = (1.1\pm0.2) \times 10^{-3}$.

## Key claim

The parity-violating asymmetry in threshold pion electroproduction can be sensitive to nucleon strangeness contributions at the level of about 20% for forward kinematics and electron energies above 1 GeV, but this sensitivity is model-dependent and relies on the specific parametrization of strange form factors from elastic PVES data.

## Method

The authors constructed weak neutral-current pion production amplitudes from electromagnetic ones using the MAID isobar model with partial wave analysis and unitarity, explicitly incorporating strangeness contributions via the strange magnetic moment extracted from elastic PVES data. They used the experimental bounds on strange form factors from a global analysis by Armstrong (2012) and applied the isospin decomposition to relate electromagnetic and weak neutral-current multipoles, including strangeness through modifications to the Born multipoles. The resulting model was then used to compute parity-violating asymmetries in inclusive pion electroproduction and to update the dispersive gamma-Z box correction to the proton's weak charge.

## Key numbers

- Strange magnetic moment from elastic PVES: mu_s = 0.38 +/- 0.27, derived from G_M^s(0.1 GeV^2) = 0.29 +/- 0.21
- Strange electric form factor at Q^2 = 0.1 GeV^2: G_E^s = -0.008 +/- 0.016
- Updated gamma-Z box correction for Qweak kinematics (E = 1.165 GeV): Re Box_V = (5.58 +/- 1.41) x 10^{-3}, an improvement over the previous uncertainty estimate of +/- 2.0 x 10^{-3}
- Updated gamma-Z box correction for MESA/P2 kinematics (E = 0.155 GeV): Re Box_V = (1.1 +/- 0.2) x 10^{-3}
- Sensitivity of parity-violating asymmetry to strangeness reaches about 20% in the threshold region for forward kinematics and electron energies above 1 GeV
- Parity-violating asymmetries are as large as tens of parts per million (p.p.m.)

## Context

This work extends the approach of Mukhopadhyay (1998) and Sato (2003) by explicitly including strangeness contributions that were previously neglected in weak pion production models. It builds on the MAID partial wave analysis framework of Drechsel (1998, 2007) and uses experimental constraints on strange form factors from the global analysis by Armstrong (2012). The updated gamma-Z box correction improves upon earlier estimates by Gorchtein (2008, 2011), Sibirtsev (2010), Rislow (2010), and Hall (2013), reducing the uncertainty from +/- 2.0 x 10^{-3} to +/- 1.41 x 10^{-3} for Qweak kinematics. This work enables a new method for extracting strange form factors from inelastic PVES data, complementary to traditional elastic PVES measurements, and provides a more controlled input for dispersion relation calculations of electroweak radiative corrections.
