# The mass and spectral function of scalar and pseudoscalar mesons in a hot and chirally imbalanced medium using the two-flavor NJL model

> We explore the properties of neutral mesons within the context of a chirally imbalanced medium, employing the two-flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. The temperature dependence of the constituent quark mass at finite values of the chiral chemical potential (CCP) demonstrates the well-established phenomena of chiral catalysis at lower temperatures and inverse chiral catalysis at higher temperatures. The polarization functions in both the scalar ($σ$) and pseudo-scalar ($π^0$) channels have been evaluated using real time formalism of thermal field theory. These have been used to determine the masses and spectral functions of $ σ$ and $ π$ mesons. Detailed investigation of the analytic structure of the imaginary part of the polarization function for $σ$ and $π$ mesons results in the emergence of non-trivial Landau cut contributions due to the presence of chiral imbalance. The multiple solutions for the mass of the $π$ meson for specific values of CCP have been analysed on the basis of their residue at the pole. Furthermore, we have observed abrupt changes in the masses of both scalar and pseudo-scalar mesons at finite CCP values, particularly at higher temperatures. A decreasing trend in the Mott transition temperature is seen with the increase in CCP.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in a hot and chirally imbalanced medium, the chiral chemical potential (CCP) induces chiral catalysis at low temperatures and inverse chiral catalysis at high temperatures, and that the Landau cut contributions to the meson polarization functions emerge only at finite CCP, but these contributions have sub-leading magnitudes compared to the Unitary cuts and negligible impact on mesonic spectral functions and masses.

## Method

The authors employed the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the Hartree mean field approximation to compute the constituent quark mass via the gap equation at finite temperature and chiral chemical potential. The polarization functions for scalar (sigma) and pseudoscalar (pion) mesons were evaluated using the real time formalism of thermal field theory, and the meson propagators were obtained within the Random Phase Approximation (RPA). Meson masses were determined either by solving the pole equation of the propagator or, when no real solution existed, by locating the global maximum of the spectral function at vanishing three-momentum.

## Key numbers

* Constituent quark mass in vacuum: M approximately 343.6 MeV, obtained with NJL coupling G = 5.742 GeV^-2, current quark mass m = 5.6 MeV, and smooth three-momentum cutoff Lambda = 568.69 MeV.
* Pion decay constant in vacuum: f_pi approximately 92 MeV, and pion mass m_pi approximately 140 MeV, reproduced by the chosen model parameters.
* Quark condensate in vacuum: <bar u u> = <bar d d> approximately -(245)^3 MeV^3.
* Pseudo-chiral phase transition temperature T_c decreases monotonically with increasing CCP, as shown in the T-mu_5 phase diagram.
* At T = 140 MeV (chiral symmetry broken phase), the Unitary cut threshold (q^0 = 2M) moves toward higher q^0 by a few tens of MeV as CCP increases from 0 to 250 MeV, due to the increase in M from chiral catalysis.
* The Landau cut contributions to the imaginary part of the polarization function lie in the region |q^0| <= 2 mu_5 and are sub-leading compared to Unitary cut contributions.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of meson properties using the NJL model at finite temperature and density (Hatsuda 1985, Ayala 2002, Hansen 2006, Inagaki 2007) by incorporating a chiral chemical potential to model chirally imbalanced matter relevant to heavy-ion collisions and the chiral magnetic effect. The authors note that no prior references have investigated spectral properties of mesons in the presence of chiral imbalance. The results enable future studies of dissipative phenomena and transport coefficients in strongly interacting matter with chiral imbalance, and the authors plan to report numerical results including both temperature and quark chemical potential elsewhere.
