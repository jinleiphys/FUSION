> 5 pages, 1 figure, contributed to "QCD@Work 2018 - International Workshop on Quantum Chromodynamics: Theory and Experiment" (25 - 28 June 2018, Matera, Italy)

# Simplified Bethe-Salpeter Description of Basic Pseudoscalar-Meson Features

## Key claim
The paper assesses whether a simplified Bethe-Salpeter formalism, situated between the fully relativistic Bethe-Salpeter description and its instantaneous Salpeter limit, can produce reliable predictions for light pseudoscalar mesons; the key test is compatibility with a generalized Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation, and the conclusion is that the formalism is "still alive" but not definitively confirmed.

## Method
The author uses a previously proposed bound-state equation (Lucha, various years) that reduces the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation to a coupled set of radial equations for two Lorentz-scalar Salpeter amplitude components, assuming identical quark and antiquark propagator functions and a spherically symmetric effective potential. The effective interquark potential \(V(r)\) is extracted by inverting these equations using a Salpeter amplitude derived from a chiral-quark propagator model (Munczek and Nemirovsky, 1983; Praschifka et al., 1987) via a Ward–Takahashi identity. The formalism is then solved numerically for chiral, \(u/d\), and \(s\) quarks to obtain meson masses, decay constants, and in-hadron condensates, which are checked against the generalized Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation.

## Key numbers
- Pion average interquark distance: \(\langle r \rangle = 0.478\ \text{fm}\)
- Pion root-mean-square radius: \(\sqrt{\langle r^2 \rangle} = 0.529\ \text{fm}\) (compared to experimental electromagnetic charge radius \(\sqrt{\langle r_\pi^2 \rangle} = (0.672 \pm 0.008)\ \text{fm}\) from Particle Data Group)
- For chiral quarks: mass \(\widehat M_B = 6.8\ \text{MeV}\), decay constant \(f_B = 151\ \text{MeV}\), in-meson condensate \(\mathbb C_B = 0.585\ \text{GeV}^2\), resulting quark mass \(m = 0.0059\ \text{MeV}\)
- For \(u/d\) quarks: mass \(\widehat M_B = 148.6\ \text{MeV}\), decay constant \(f_B = 155\ \text{MeV}\), in-meson condensate \(\mathbb C_B = 0.598\ \text{GeV}^2\), resulting quark mass \(m = 2.85\ \text{MeV}\) (compared to \(\overline{m}(2\ \text{GeV}) = 3.5^{+0.7}_{-0.3}\ \text{MeV}\) from PDG)
- For \(s\) quarks: mass \(\widehat M_B = 620.7\ \text{MeV}\), decay constant \(f_B = 211\ \text{MeV}\), in-meson condensate \(\mathbb C_B = 0.799\ \text{GeV}^2\), resulting quark mass \(m = 51.0\ \text{MeV}\) (compared to \(\overline{m}(2\ \text{GeV}) = 96^{+8}_{-4}\ \text{MeV}\) from PDG)

## Context
This work extends the author's own series of papers (Lucha, various years) developing a Bethe-Salpeter-inspired bound-state formalism that avoids the full complexity of the relativistic Bethe-Salpeter equation (Salpeter and Bethe, 1951) while going beyond the instantaneous Salpeter approximation (Salpeter, 1952). The key test uses a generalized Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation (Maris, Roberts, and Tandy, 1998) that relates pseudoscalar meson mass, decay constant, and in-hadron condensate to quark masses. The results enable future work to assess whether this intermediate formalism can reliably predict hadronic observables beyond the chiral limit, though the author notes that the pion radius agreement is not a strong test since the chiral-quark input already determined the potential.
