# Double, Triple and Hidden Charm Production in the Statistical Coalescence Model

> The production of particles with double, triple and hidden charm in heavy ion collisions is studied in the framework of the statistical coalescence model. According to the postulates of the model, the charm quark-antiquark pairs are created at the initial stage of a heavy ion reaction in hard parton collisions. The amount of charm is assumed to be unchanged at later stages. The charm (anti)quarks are distributed among different hadron species at hadronization according to the laws of statistical physics. Several approaches to the statistical treatment of charm hadronization are considered. The grand canonical approach is appropriate for systems containing large number of charm (anti)quarks. The exact charm conservation and Poissonian fluctuations of the number of charm quark-antiquark pairs should be taken into account, if the average number of these pairs is of oder of unity or smaller. The charm hadronization in a subsystem of a larger system is discussed. It is explained why the canonical approach is not appropriate for the description of charm hadronization. The obtained formulas can be used to calculate the production of charm in heavy ion collisions in a wide energy range.

## Key claim

The statistical coalescence model (SCM) can describe the production of double, triple, and hidden charm hadrons in heavy ion collisions, but the appropriate statistical treatment depends on the average number of charm quark-antiquark pairs: the grand canonical approach works for large numbers, while exact charm conservation and Poissonian fluctuations must be accounted for when the average number is of order unity or smaller.

## Method

The author derives partition functions for charm hadronization in the statistical coalescence model under three different statistical treatments: grand canonical (for systems with many charm pairs), fixed charm number (canonical with respect to charm), and Poissonian fluctuations of charm pair number. The yields of hadrons with single, double, triple, and hidden charm are calculated in each approach using the Boltzmann approximation for the hadron gas, with the thermal parameters (temperature, volume, chemical potentials) taken from fits to light-flavored hadron data. The case of charm production in a subsystem (e.g., limited rapidity interval) is also treated using binomial distributions.

## Key numbers

- The leading-order grand canonical expression for hidden charm yield is ⟨N_H⟩_{GCE}^{(0)} = ⟨N_c⟩ ⟨N_anti-c⟩ (Ñ_H)/(Ñ_1 Ñ_anti-1), where Ñ_k are the particle numbers at zero charm chemical potential and γ_c=1.
- The leading-order grand canonical expression for double charm yield is ⟨N_2⟩_{GCE}^{(0)} = ⟨N_c⟩^2 (Ñ_2)/(Ñ_1^2).
- The leading-order grand canonical expression for triple charm yield is ⟨N_3⟩_{GCE}^{(0)} = ⟨N_c⟩^3 (Ñ_3)/(Ñ_1^3).
- For a system with Poissonian fluctuations of charm pairs, the hidden charm yield is ⟨N_H⟩_P^{(0)} = ⟨N_c anti-c⟩ (⟨N_c anti-c⟩ + 1) (Ñ_H)/(Ñ_1 Ñ_anti-1), which differs from the grand canonical result when ⟨N_c anti-c⟩ is small.
- For a subsystem with probability ξ to find a charm quark, the double charm yield is ⟨N_2⟩_sub^{(0)} = ξ^2 ⟨N_c anti-c⟩^2 (Ñ_2)/(Ñ_1^2) and the triple charm yield is ⟨N_3⟩_sub^{(0)} = ξ^3 ⟨N_c anti-c⟩^3 (Ñ_3)/(Ñ_1^3).

## Context

This work extends the statistical coalescence model, previously applied to J/ψ and ψ' production at SPS energies by Kostyuk (2002) and others, to predict yields of doubly and triply charmed hadrons that have been predicted by the quark model but mostly not yet observed experimentally. The paper clarifies why the canonical ensemble approach, used for strangeness production by Cleymans (1991) and for baryonic charge, is not appropriate for charm coalescence because the Poissonian fluctuation pattern of charm pairs differs from the canonical distribution. The derived formulas enable calculations of charm hadron production in heavy ion collisions across a wide energy range, from SPS through RHIC to LHC.
