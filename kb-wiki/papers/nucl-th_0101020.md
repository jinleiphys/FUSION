# Boundary and expansion effects on two-pion correlation functions in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We examine the effects that a confining boundary together with hydrodynamical expansion play on two-pion distributions in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. We show that the effects arise from the introduction of further correlations due both to collective motion and the system's finite size. As is well known, the former leads to a reduction in the apparent source radius with increasing average pair momentum K. However, for small K, the presence of the boundary leads to a decrease of the apparent source radius with decreasing K. These two competing effects produce a maximum for the effective source radius as a function of K.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the effective source radius extracted from two-pion correlation functions in relativistic heavy-ion collisions exhibits a maximum as a function of the average pair momentum K, arising from the competing effects of a confining boundary (dominant at small K) and hydrodynamical expansion (dominant at large K), with the maximum occurring at K approximately equal to eta T, where eta = T / (gamma(R) m beta).

## Method

The authors model the pion system at freeze-out as a confined, spherically symmetric volume with a sharp boundary, incorporating hydrodynamical expansion via a velocity field that scales linearly with distance from the fireball center. They solve the wave equation for pions in a spherical harmonic well with rigid boundaries, using a Taylor expansion of the Lorentz factor valid for moderate surface velocities, and compute the two-pion correlation function from the discrete set of eigenfunctions under the assumption of a completely chaotic (thermal) pion source.

## Key numbers

* For a system radius R = 6 fm, temperature T = 120 MeV, surface expansion velocity beta = 0.5 c, and chemical potential mu = 0, the width of the correlation function C2(q) decreases as R is increased from 6 fm to 10 fm at fixed K = 500 MeV.
* For R = 6 fm, T = 120 MeV, beta = 0.5 c, and mu = 0, the width of C2(q) increases as K is increased from 300 MeV to 500 MeV to 700 MeV, but decreases as K is increased from 50 MeV to 100 MeV to 200 MeV.
* When the ground state population fraction Ng/N reaches 0.37 (mu = 315 MeV), the correlation function intercept lambda becomes approximately 0.62; at Ng/N = 0.77 (mu = 317.5 MeV), lambda becomes approximately 0.23.
* The effective radius Reff reaches a maximum at K approximately equal to eta T, where eta = T / (gamma(R) m beta).

## Context

This work extends the "expanding pion liquid" model developed by Ayala, Barreiro, and Montano (1999) and Ayala and Smerzi (1997) from single-pion spectra to two-pion correlations, incorporating both the confining boundary effects studied by Zhang and Padula (2000) and the hydrodynamical expansion effects described by Pratt (1984). The results enable experimental extraction of the freeze-out temperature and flow velocity from the position of the maximum in Reff versus K, and provide a framework for distinguishing between boundary-dominated and expansion-dominated regimes in HBT measurements at AGS and SPS energies.
