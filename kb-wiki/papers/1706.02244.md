# Electron-deuteron deep-inelastic scattering with spectator nucleon tagging and final-state interactions at intermediate x

> We consider electron-deuteron deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) with detection of a proton in the nuclear fragmentation region ("spectator tagging") as a method for extracting the free neutron structure functions and studying their nuclear modifications. Such measurements could be performed at a future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) with suitable forward detectors. The measured proton recoil momentum (~< 100 MeV in the deuteron rest frame) specifies the deuteron configuration during the high-energy process and permits a controlled theoretical treatment of nuclear effects. Nuclear and nucleonic structure are separated using methods of light-front quantum mechanics. The impulse approximation (IA) to the tagged DIS cross section contains the free neutron pole, which can be reached by on-shell extrapolation in the recoil momentum. Final-state interactions (FSI) distort the recoil momentum distribution away from the pole. In the intermediate-x region 0.1 < x < 0.5 FSI arise predominantly from interactions of the spectator proton with slow hadrons produced in the DIS process on the neutron (rest frame momenta ~< 1 GeV, target fragmentation region). We construct a schematic model describing this effect, using final-state hadron distributions measured in nucleon DIS experiments and low-energy hadron scattering amplitudes. We investigate the magnitude of FSI, their dependence on the recoil momentum (angular dependence, forward/backward regions), their analytic properties, and their effect on the on-shell extrapolation. We comment on the prospects for neutron structure extraction in tagged DIS with EIC. We discuss possible extensions of the FSI model to other kinematic regions (large/small x). In tagged DIS at x << 0.1 FSI resulting from diffractive scattering on the nucleons become important and require separate treatment.

## Key claim

The paper argues that final-state interactions (FSI) in electron-deuteron deep-inelastic scattering with spectator proton tagging at intermediate \(x\) (0.1 < \(x\) < 0.5) arise predominantly from interactions of the spectator proton with slow hadrons (rest frame momenta \(\lesssim 1\) GeV) produced in the target fragmentation region of the DIS process on the neutron, not from fast hadrons; this physical picture enables a controlled theoretical treatment of FSI effects on the on-shell extrapolation needed to extract free neutron structure functions.

## Method

The authors construct a schematic model of FSI in tagged DIS on the deuteron using light-front quantum mechanics to separate nuclear and nucleonic structure. They describe the slow hadron component of the nucleon DIS final state using empirical hadron distributions measured in nucleon DIS experiments and treat the rescattering of these slow hadrons with the spectator proton as on-shell scattering with an effective cross section, using low-energy hadron scattering amplitudes. The model is applied to compute the distorted spectral function and the recoil momentum dependence of the tagged cross section in the intermediate-\(x\) region, with the deuteron treated in an S-wave approximation for recoil momenta below 200 MeV.

## Key numbers

*   The on-shell point for the neutron pole corresponds to \(t - M_N^2 = -2 |\bm{p}_p|^2 + t'_{\rm min}\), where \(t'_{\rm min} = -M_d \epsilon_d = -0.0041 \, \textrm{GeV}^2\) and \(\epsilon_d = 2.2\) MeV is the deuteron binding energy.
*   The spectator proton recoil momentum in the deuteron rest frame is \(\lesssim 100\) MeV for the controlled configuration of interest.
*   The intermediate-\(x\) region is defined as \(0.1 < x < 0.5\), where FSI arise from slow hadrons with rest frame momenta \(\lesssim 1\) GeV.
*   The median of the nonrelativistic deuteron momentum distribution is approximately 70 MeV.
*   The JLab EIC detector is designed to provide momentum resolution corresponding to \(|\bm{p}_p| \sim 20\) MeV in the deuteron rest frame for spectator protons.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for neutron structure extraction via spectator tagging in deuteron DIS, building on the on-shell extrapolation method proposed by Sargsian (2005) and the BONuS experiment at JLab (Tkachenko 2014, Griffioen 2015). It provides a quantitative estimate of FSI effects specific to the intermediate-\(x\) region accessible at a future Electron-Ion Collider, complementing earlier microscopic FSI studies by Ciofi degli Atti (2002, 2003) and others. The model enables assessment of the feasibility of model-independent neutron structure extraction and can be extended to other kinematic regions (large \(x\), small \(x\) with diffractive scattering) in future work.
