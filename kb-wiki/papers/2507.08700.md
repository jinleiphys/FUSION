# The breakdown scale of pionless effective field theory in the three-nucleon sector

> We make order-by-order predictions of neutron-deuteron total cross sections up to next-to-next-to-leading order in pionless effective field theory. Using Bayesian methods, we infer a posterior distribution for the breakdown scale. The result shows a mode near 100 MeV, and a combined analysis with neutron-proton scattering further sharpens the inference, placing the mode close to the pion mass scale, consistent with the expected range of pionless effective field theory.

## Key claim

The breakdown scale of pionless effective field theory inferred from neutron-deuteron scattering is approximately 100 MeV, which is somewhat lower than the roughly 190 MeV scale inferred from neutron-proton scattering, suggesting that the inferred breakdown scale can depend on the process under consideration and may be sensitive to off-shell two-body dynamics probed in three-body systems.

## Method

The authors calculated neutron-deuteron total cross sections at leading order (LO), next-to-leading order (NLO), and next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO) in pionless effective field theory using three-body integral equations solved on a complex contour with a sharp momentum cutoff. They applied Bayesian inference with a log-uniform prior on the breakdown scale, assuming the EFT expansion coefficients follow a normal prior with conjugate hyperprior, and evaluated the cross sections at three relative momenta (9, 61, and 132 MeV) to extract the posterior distribution for the breakdown scale.

## Key numbers

- The N2LO posterior for the breakdown scale from nd scattering alone has a mode near 100 MeV, with 68% DoB interval [63,117] MeV and 95% DoB interval [45,157] MeV
- The NLO posterior from nd scattering gives 68% DoB interval [24,87] MeV and 95% DoB interval [9,150] MeV, with median 49 MeV
- The combined analysis using both nd and np cross sections yields N2LO 68% DoB interval [108,166] MeV and NLO 68% DoB interval [113,192] MeV, with medians of 135 MeV and 149 MeV respectively
- The overlap coefficient between nd and np posteriors is 0.41 at NLO and 0.23 at N2LO
- The triton binding energy used to fix three-nucleon interaction coefficients is 8.48 MeV
- The nd scattering length used for the N2LO fit is 0.65 +/- 0.04 fm

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian inference of the pionless EFT breakdown scale from the two-nucleon sector (Ekstrom 2024) to the three-nucleon sector, where off-shell two-body dynamics and three-nucleon interactions play a significant role. The lower breakdown scale inferred from nd scattering compared to np scattering suggests sensitivity to the pion cut at approximately 69 MeV (half the pion mass) in three-body systems, and the combined analysis places the breakdown scale close to the pion mass scale of 138 MeV. This enables future studies of spin-dependent observables and correlated truncation errors using Gaussian processes.
