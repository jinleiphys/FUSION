# Excited states of the Wick-Cutkosky model with the Nakanishi representation in the Light-Front framework

> The Wick-Cutkosky model is investigated using the framework of the Nakanishi Perturbative Integral Representation projected in the Light-Front hyperplane for an s-wave amplitude. We developed a new technique based on sucessive integration by parts of the Nakanishi Representation which enabled us to transform one of the integrations into a sum. With a set of boundary conditions it was possible to guess the format of the solution, which was in fact a distribution. The eigenequations obtained were the same as the originals of Cutkosky \cite{CutPR54}. Finally, a numerical check confirmed that the final equation reproduce the same eigenvalues as the initial Bethe-Salpeter equation.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Nakanishi weight function for the Wick-Cutkosky model in the massless ladder exchange limit is a distribution (a sum of derivatives of Dirac delta functions) rather than a regular function, and that this distributional form can be derived systematically through integration by parts rather than assumed as an Ansatz.

## Method

The authors start from the Bethe-Salpeter equation for scalar particles exchanging a massless scalar, expressed using the Nakanishi integral representation projected onto the Light-Front hyperplane. They develop a technique of successive integration by parts in the gamma-prime variable to transform the integral over the Nakanishi weight function into an infinite sum of terms involving boundary values of antiderivatives of the weight function. By matching powers of the denominator function d_0(gamma,z) in the resulting series, they obtain a triangular system of equations for the boundary-value functions b_i(z), whose highest-index equation reduces exactly to the original Wick-Cutkosky equation. They then verify numerically that the eigenvalues from this derived equation match those from the original Bethe-Salpeter equation.

## Key numbers

* Ground state coupling alpha_1 at B/m = 0.1: 1.11 (from Bethe-Salpeter equation) and 1.12 (from Wick-Cutkosky equation)
* Ground state coupling alpha_1 at B/m = 0.5: 3.29 (Bethe-Salpeter) and 3.30 (Wick-Cutkosky)
* First excited state coupling alpha_2 at B/m = 0.1: 2.90 (Bethe-Salpeter) and 2.93 (Wick-Cutkosky)
* First excited state coupling alpha_2 at B/m = 0.5: 9.44 (Bethe-Salpeter) and 9.42 (Wick-Cutkosky)
* Relative error between the two methods for all computed eigenvalues: approximately 1 percent
* Numerical calculations used 5 Laguerre basis functions in gamma and gamma-prime and 5 Legendre polynomials in z and z-prime

## Context

This work extends the original Wick-Cutkosky model (Wick 1954, Cutkosky 1954) by rederiving its equations within the modern Light-Front projected Nakanishi representation framework developed by Karmanov and Carbonell (2006) and later extended by Frederico, Salme and Viviani (2012-2014). The method provides a systematic derivation showing that the previously assumed Dirac delta Ansatz for the weight function is actually a consequence of the massless limit, and it enables the reconstruction of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude and Light-Front wave function for excited states. The integration-by-parts technique may be applicable to the small-mass case where basis expansions perform poorly.
