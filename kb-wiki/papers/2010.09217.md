# On the Possibility of Dibaryon Formation near the N*(1440)N threshold -- the Isoscalar Single-Pion Production Revisited

> The isoscalar single-pion production exhibits a broad bump in the energy dependence of the total cross section, which does not correspond to the usual opening of the $N^*(1440)$ production channel with subsequent pion decay. In arxiv:2102.05575 it was interpreted as a narrow Breit-Wigner structure, which leads in a sequential single-pion production process to a possible explanation of the $d^*(2380)$ resonance. We demonstrate that such an attempt fails already, when confronted with the data base for isoscalar single-pion production. We investigate whether the observed bump structure rather points to the formation of dibaryon states with $I(J^P) = 0(1^+)$ and $0(1^-)$ near the $N^*(1440)N$ threshold. This situation would be similar to the situation at the $Δ(1232)N$ threshold, where the signature of a number of dibaryonic resonances has been found.

## Key claim

The broad bump in the isoscalar single-pion production cross section, previously interpreted as a narrow Breit-Wigner structure with width 70 MeV that could explain the d*(2380) resonance via sequential single-pion production, is instead consistent with a much broader structure (width 150 MeV) that may indicate dibaryon formation with quantum numbers I(J^P) = 0(1+) and 0(1-) near the N*(1440)N threshold.

## Method

The authors reanalyzed WASA-at-COSY data for pp -> ppπ0 and pn -> ppπ- reactions by recalibrating the detector and applying tighter cuts on the π0 peak. They extracted the isoscalar single-pion production cross section using the isospin relation σ_pn(I=0) = (3/2)(2σ_pn->ppπ- - σ_pp->ppπ0), and compared their renormalized results with partial-wave analysis results from earlier bubble-chamber measurements. They also examined Dalitz plots and invariant mass distributions to identify the dominant partial waves in the exit channel.

## Key numbers

- The isoscalar cross section bump is described by a Breit-Wigner shape with width 150(20) MeV and peak at 2.31(1) GeV, in agreement with earlier results from Refs. [NNpi, NstarN]
- A 1% change in normalization of σ_pn->ppπ- leads to a shift of σ_pn(I=0) by approximately 0.08 mb for sqrt(s) > 2.25 GeV
- The isoscalar Nπ-invariant mass spectrum peaks at approximately 1370 MeV with a width of approximately 150 MeV
- The isoscalar pp invariant mass spectrum shows essentially no strength above M_pp > 2050 MeV = 2m_p + 170 MeV
- The isovector dibaryon excitations in pp -> ppπ0 are represented by a Lorentzian centered at sqrt(s) = 2200 MeV with width 90 MeV and height about 1 mb

## Context

This work extends the investigation of dibaryonic resonances near baryon excitation thresholds, following the observation of the d*(2380) resonance and multiple dibaryon states near the Δ(1232)N threshold (including states with I(J^P) = 1(2+), 2(1+), 1(0-), and 1(2-)). The authors argue against the interpretation of Platonova and Kukulin (2021, arxiv:2102.05575) that a narrow 70 MeV Breit-Wigner structure in isoscalar single-pion production could explain d*(2380) via sequential single-pion production. Instead, the broader structure supports the possibility of dibaryon states with I(J^P) = 0(1+) and 0(1-) near the N*(1440)N threshold, similar to the dibaryonic resonances found at the Δ(1232)N threshold. The dominant pp partial waves in the exit channel are identified as 1S0 and 3P1, not D-waves as assumed in the sequential production model.
