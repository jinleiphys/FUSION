# Pair momentum dependence of tilted source in heavy ion collisions

> In non-central heavy-ion collisions, the particle-emitting source can be tilted away from the beam direction, an effect that becomes particularly significant at collision energies of a few GeV and lower. This phenomenon, manifest itself in many observables such as directed flow, polarization, and vorticity, is therefore important to investigate. In this paper, we study the consistency between the tilt extracted directly from the freeze-out distribution of pions and the tilt parameter obtained using the azimuthally sensitive femtoscopy (asHBT) method. Using the UrQMD model, we demonstrate a strong dependence of the tilt parameter extracted with asHBT on the momentum of the particle pair. Considering the experimental challenges in accessing low particle momenta - where the tilt parameter extracted with asHBT closely matches the tilt of the freeze-out distribution of pions - we propose an exponential extrapolation method to obtain the tilt of the entire freeze-out distribution. This approach aims to enhance the accuracy of experimental measurements of tilt in non-central heavy-ion collisions.

# Pair momentum dependence of tilted source in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim
Using the UrQMD model, the tilt parameter extracted via azimuthally sensitive femtoscopy (asHBT) depends strongly on the transverse momentum of the pion pair, ranging from approximately 5 degrees at the lowest considered pair momentum to about 40 degrees at the highest; the tilt extracted at low pair momenta closely matches the tilt of the full freeze-out distribution, but experimental access to these low momenta is challenging, motivating an exponential extrapolation to zero pair momentum.

## Method
The authors used the UrQMD 3.4 model in cascade mode to simulate Au+Au collisions at 7.7 GeV and 27 GeV, selecting pions with transverse momentum below 0.8 GeV/c and pseudorapidity |eta| < 1. They constructed two-particle correlation functions for identical pions, binned by centrality, transverse momentum of the pair, and azimuthal angle relative to the reaction plane, then fitted these with a three-dimensional Gaussian to extract femtoscopic radii and their oscillations. The tilt angle of the homogeneity region was calculated from the Fourier coefficients of the radius oscillations using two independent formulas (theta_out-long and theta_side-long), and the dependence of this tilt on pair transverse momentum was fitted with an exponential function to extrapolate to zero momentum.

## Key numbers
- Tilt angle extracted via asHBT ranges from approximately 5 degrees at the lowest considered pair transverse momentum to approximately 40 degrees at the highest pair transverse momentum studied (for Au+Au at 7.7 GeV, 30-50% centrality).
- Two collision energies were studied: 7.7 GeV and 27 GeV.
- Pions were selected with transverse momentum less than 0.8 GeV/c and pseudorapidity |eta| < 1.
- Correlation functions were divided into eight ranges of azimuthal angle of the pion pair relative to the reaction plane.
- The effectively reconstructed momentum threshold in the STAR TPC starts at approximately 0.2 GeV/c.
- The tilt values extracted from the out-long and side-long formulas coincide within statistical uncertainties at the smallest considered pair momentum.

## Context
This work extends the azimuthally sensitive femtoscopy (asHBT) method proposed by Wiedemann (1997) and Lisa (2000), and the tilted Gaussian source description of Mount (2010), by demonstrating that the tilt parameter extracted via asHBT depends strongly on pair momentum, not just on centrality and collision energy as previously studied. The results confirm earlier findings by Graef (2013) that the tilt may depend on the scale over which the source is probed. The proposed exponential extrapolation method aims to enable experimental measurements of the full freeze-out source tilt despite detector acceptance limitations at low momenta, which is relevant for understanding directed flow and the three-dimensional initial state in models that go beyond boost-invariant approaches such as MC-Glauber, MC-KLN, EKRT, TRENTO, and IP-Glasma.
