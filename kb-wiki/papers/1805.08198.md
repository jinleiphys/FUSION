# Thermo-magnetic evolution of the QCD strong coupling

> We study the one-loop gluon polarization tensor at zero and finite temperature in the presence of a magnetic field, to extract the thermo-magnetic evolution of the strong coupling $α_s$. We analyze four distinct regimes, to wit, the small and large field cases, both at zero and at high temperature. From a renormalization group analysis we show that at zero temperature, either for small or large magnetic fields, and for a fixed transferred momentum $Q^2$, $α_s$ grows with the field strength with respect to its vacuum value. However, at high temperature and also for a fixed value of $Q^2$ we find two different cases: When the magnetic field is even larger than the squared temperature, $α_s$ also grows with the field strength. On the contrary, when the squared temperature is larger than the magnetic field, a turnover behavior occurs and $α_s$ decreases with the field strength. This thermo-magnetic behavior of $α_s$ can help explain the inverse magnetic catalysis phenomenon found by lattice QCD calculations.

## Key claim

The strong coupling α_s grows with magnetic field strength at zero temperature and at high temperature when the magnetic field is the largest energy scale, but decreases with field strength when the squared temperature exceeds the magnetic field, providing a potential explanation for the inverse magnetic catalysis phenomenon observed in lattice QCD.

## Method

The authors computed the one-loop gluon polarization tensor in the presence of a constant magnetic field at zero and finite temperature, working in the weak and strong magnetic field limits. They applied the renormalization group equation (RGE) to extract the thermo-magnetic running of the strong coupling α_s, using the method of characteristics to relate the coupling at different energy scales. The analysis considered four distinct regimes: small and large magnetic fields at zero temperature, and the same two cases at high temperature, with the reference scale taken as the fixed transferred momentum Q^2 plus the smaller of T^2 or |eB|.

## Key numbers

- At T=0, for both weak and strong magnetic fields, α_s(Q^2+|eB|) = α_s(Q^2) / [1 + b_1 α_s(Q^2) ln(Q^2/(Q^2+|eB|))], showing the coupling increases with field strength.
- For the case |eB| > T^2 at high temperature, α_s(|eB|) = α_s(Q^2+~T^2) / [1 + b_1 α_s(Q^2+~T^2) ln((Q^2+T^2)/(Q^2+T^2+|eB|))], with the coupling growing with field strength.
- For the case T^2 > |eB| at high temperature, α_s(|eB|) = α_s(Q^2+~|eB|) / [1 + b_1 α_s(Q^2+~|eB|) ln((Q^2+|eB|)/(Q^2+|eB|+T^2))], with the coupling decreasing with field strength.
- The one-loop beta function coefficient is b_1 = (1/12π)(11N_c - 2N_f) with N_c=3 colors and N_f=3 active flavors.
- The numerical example used Q^2 = 1 GeV^2, with T = 1.1 GeV for the |eB| > T^2 case and T = 1.5 GeV for the |eB| < T^2 case.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of inverse magnetic catalysis (IMC) observed in lattice QCD simulations (Bali et al.), where the pseudo-critical temperature and quark condensate decrease with magnetic field strength above the transition temperature. The authors build on earlier effective model approaches (Farias, Ferreira, Ayala et al.) and Schwinger-Dyson analyses (Mueller) that suggested thermo-magnetic corrections to the strong coupling could explain IMC. The RGE analysis provides a first-principles QCD derivation of the coupling's thermo-magnetic behavior, complementing earlier work on temperature-dependent renormalization constants (Steffens) and gauge-gravity correspondence studies (Fito). The results enable a quantitative framework for understanding how the competition between thermal and magnetic scales drives the coupling's behavior, which can be incorporated into effective models of the QCD phase diagram.
