# U_A(1) Symmetry Restoration from an In-Medium eta' Mass Reduction in sqrt(s(NN)) = 200 GeV Au+Au Collisions

> A reduction of the mass of the eta'(958) meson may signal restoration of the U_A(1) symmetry in a hot and dense hadronic matter, corresponding to the return of the 9th, "prodigal" Goldstone boson. We report on an analysis of a combined PHENIX and STAR data set on the intercept parameter of the two-pion Bose-Einstein correlation functions, as measuremed in sqrt(s(NN)) = 200 GeV Au + Au collisions at RHIC. To describe this combined PHENIX and STAR dataset, an in-medium eta' mass reduction of at least 200 MeV is needed, at the 99.9 % confidence level in a broad model class of resonance abundances.

## Key claim
An in-medium reduction of the eta'(958) meson mass by at least 200 MeV is needed to describe the combined PHENIX and STAR two-pion Bose-Einstein correlation data at the 99.9% confidence level, but only within a broad class of resonance abundance models; the best-fit value of the in-medium eta' mass is 340 MeV with large systematic uncertainties dominated by the choice of resonance model.

## Method
The authors performed Monte-Carlo simulations of the transverse mass dependence of long-lived resonance multiplicities, including enhanced eta' production from a partial in-medium U_A(1) restoration. They computed the intercept parameter lambda_* of two-pion Bose-Einstein correlations from simulated resonance decays using JETSET 7.4, and fitted the model parameters to combined PHENIX and STAR data from sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV Au+Au collisions. The analysis used five different resonance production models (ALCOR, Kaneta, Letessier, Stachel, UrQMD) and varied the expansion dynamics exponent alpha, condensation temperature T_cond, freeze-out temperature T_fo, and mean transverse flow velocity <u_T> to map systematic uncertainties.

## Key numbers
- In-medium eta' mass reduction of at least 200 MeV is required at the 99.9% confidence level (more than 5-sigma effect) to describe the combined PHENIX and STAR data.
- Best-fit in-medium eta' mass: 340 +50/-60 (stat) +280/-140 (model) +/- 45 (systematic) MeV, compared to the vacuum mass of 958 MeV.
- For the Kaneta resonance model, best-fit in-medium eta' mass = 530 +/- 50 MeV with B_inv = 55 MeV and chi^2/NDF = 22.8/11 (CL 4.12%).
- For the Letessier resonance model, best-fit in-medium eta' mass = 340 +50/-60 MeV with B_inv = 86 MeV and chi^2/NDF = 18.9/11 (CL 6.35%).
- The 5-sigma upper limits on in-medium eta' mass range from 570 MeV (Letessier, Stachel models) to 730 MeV (Kaneta model).
- Enhancement factors f_eta' range from 25.6 (Kaneta model) to 67.6 (Letessier, Stachel models).
- The systematic error from PHENIX and STAR centrality ranges, particle identification, and acceptance cuts was estimated not to exceed 9.8%, 7%, and 3% respectively.

## Context
This work extends earlier simulations by Vance (1999) and Csorgo (1999) that predicted a hole in the intercept parameter at low transverse mass from enhanced eta' production, and provides the first quantitative fit to combined PHENIX and STAR data. The analysis uses resonance models from Kaneta (2005), Stachel (2004), Rafelski (2005), ALCOR, FRITIOF, and UrQMD, and finds that FRITIOF cannot describe the data even with arbitrary eta' mass modification. The results contradict the null observation in NA44 S+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 19.4 GeV (Beker, 1994), and suggest that the eta' mass reduction effect is nearly maximal at 200 GeV Au+Au collisions, decreasing with centrality, energy, and system size. The observed low-mass dilepton enhancement in PHENIX minimum bias Au+Au collisions (Adare, 2009) may be explained by the enhanced eta' and eta production reported here.
