# Meson-exchange currents and quasielastic predictions for charged-current neutrino-12C scattering in the superscaling approach

> We evaluate and discuss the impact of meson-exchange currents (MECs) on charged-current quasielastic neutrino cross sections. We consider the nuclear transverse response arising from two-particle two-hole states excited by the action of electromagnetic, purely isovector meson-exchange currents in a fully relativistic framework based on the work by the Torino Collaboration [A. D. Pace, M. Nardi, W. M. Alberico, T. W. Donnelly, and A. Molinari, Nucl. Phys. A726, 303 (2003)]. An accurate parametrization of this MEC response as a function of the momentum and energy transfers involved is presented. Results of neutrino-nucleus cross sections using this MEC parametrization together with a recent scaling approach for the one-particle one-hole contributions (named SuSAv2) are compared with experimental data (MiniBooNE, MINERvA, NOMAD and T2K Collaborations).

## Key claim

The paper claims that including a new parametrization of vector-vector transverse meson-exchange currents (MEC) in the SuSAv2 superscaling model improves agreement with charged-current quasielastic neutrino-12C cross section data from MiniBooNE, NOMAD, and MINERvA, but only for certain kinematic regions; the missing axial-axial and vector-axial MEC responses likely explain remaining discrepancies, especially for neutrinos versus antineutrinos.

## Method

The authors computed the purely isovector vector-vector transverse 2p-2h MEC response for 12C using a fully relativistic Fermi gas model based on the Torino collaboration framework (De Pace 2003), including pion-in-flight, seagull, and delta-resonance currents. They produced a smooth parametrization of this response as a function of the scaling variable Psi' and momentum transfer q, then combined it with the SuSAv2 one-particle one-hole scaling model to predict charged-current neutrino cross sections, comparing with experimental data from MiniBooNE, NOMAD, MINERvA, and T2K.

## Key numbers

- The MEC contribution to the cross section is of the order of 10% (average value within the RFG model)
- For 12C, a binding energy shift of E_s = 20 MeV is used in the scaling variable
- The MEC response peaks for q >= 400 MeV/c and becomes negligible for q < 300 MeV/c and for q > 2000 MeV/c
- The main contribution to the total cross section comes from omega < 1000 MeV and q less than or approximately 1000 MeV/c
- The region omega < 50 MeV and q < 250 MeV/c contributes less than 10% to the total cross section and less than 2% to the MEC cross section
- At NOMAD kinematics (neutrino energies between 5 and 100 GeV), the SuSAv2+MEC results show good agreement, partly because the VA response becomes very small for E_nu > 5 GeV

## Context

This work extends the superscaling approach (SuSAv2) developed by the authors (Caballero 2005, 2006, 2007; Amaro 2005) by adding a new parametrization of 2p-2h MEC contributions that improves upon an earlier parametrization used in Amaro (2011, 2012). The MEC contributions help resolve discrepancies between simple relativistic Fermi gas models and experimental data from MiniBooNE (2010, 2013), NOMAD (2009), and MINERvA (2013, 2014), similar to findings by Martini (2009) and Nieves (2011). The work enables more accurate modeling of neutrino-nucleus scattering for oscillation experiments, though the axial-axial and vector-axial MEC responses remain to be computed.
