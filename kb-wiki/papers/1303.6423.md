# Strong and Electromagnetic Forces in Heavy Ion Collisions

> The interplay between the strong and electromagnetic force in high energy nucleus-nucleus collisions was studied experimentally and theoretically in our earlier works. This effect appeared to result in very large distortions in spectra of charged pions produced in the collision. It was also found to bring new, independent information on the space-time evolution of the non-perturbative process of particle production. In this paper, we present our new results on the influence of the spectator-induced electromagnetic force on spectra of charged particles produced in two different Pb-induced reactions. For the first time, we also address the topic of p+A collisions in view of obtaining information about their centrality and nuclear break-up, both subjects being of importance in the context of the new p+A data collected at the LHC.

# Strong and Electromagnetic Forces in Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim
The spectator-induced electromagnetic force, not the strong interaction, is responsible for the large distortions observed in charged pion spectra in peripheral Pb+Pb and Pb+gas collisions at SPS energies, with the effect providing a new tool to probe the space-time evolution of pion production, but the model shows disagreement at specific kinematic regions (xF ≈ 0.2, low pT) that reveals additional information about spectator fragmentation.

## Method
The authors analyzed experimental data from the NA49 experiment at SPS for peripheral Pb+Pb collisions (150-300 charged particles, mean impact parameter 10.9±0.5 fm) and Pb+gas collisions (mostly Pb+N, Pb+O) at √sNN = 17.3 GeV. They constructed a simplified model treating spectator systems as Lorentz-contracted, uniformly charged spheres, with pions emitted from a single point at a variable emission time tE (a free parameter), then numerically propagated in the electromagnetic field including relativistic retardation effects. Initial pion spectra were constructed from NA49 p+p data with isospin symmetry and the Pb nucleus proton/neutron ratio (40%/60%) taken into account.

## Key numbers
- Beam energy: 158 GeV/nucleon, √sNN = 17.3 GeV
- Peripheral Pb+Pb sample: average 54±11 participating nucleons, mean impact parameter 10.9±0.5 fm
- The π+/π− ratio reaches a minimum below 0.2 at low transverse momenta
- The minimum position occurs at xF ≈ 0.15 = mπ/mN, corresponding to pions moving longitudinally with spectator velocity
- Model simulation uses a mixed set of initial conditions: 50% of events with tE = 0.5 fm/c and 50% with tE = 1 fm/c
- The electromagnetic distortion extends to transverse momenta up to and above pT = 1 GeV/c at high xF

## Context
This work extends earlier studies by the same group (Klusek-Gawenda, Rybicki, Szczurek, and collaborators) on the interplay between strong and electromagnetic forces in nucleus-nucleus collisions, previously reported in publications such as "pos", "hab", and "twospec". The paper presents new results on Pb+gas collisions and, for the first time, addresses p+A collisions in the context of obtaining information about centrality and nuclear break-up, which is directly relevant to new p+A data collected at the LHC. The electromagnetic effect is also being investigated for kaon spectra, azimuthal anisotropies (directed flow), and ultra-peripheral Pb+Pb collisions (Szczurek and collaborators).
