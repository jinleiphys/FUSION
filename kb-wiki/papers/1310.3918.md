# Nuclear charge-exchange excitations in localized covariant density functional theory

> The recent progress in the studies of nuclear charge-exchange excitations with localized covariant density functional theory is briefly presented, by taking the fine structure of spin-dipole excitations in 16O as an example. It is shown that the constraints introduced by the Fock terms of the relativistic Hartree-Fock scheme into the particle-hole residual interactions are straightforward and robust.

## Key claim

The localized covariant density functional derived from the relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) scheme via zero-range reduction and Fierz transformation reproduces the fine structure of spin-dipole excitations in 16O almost identically to the full RHF+RPA approach, confirming that the constraints from Fock terms on particle-hole residual interactions are straightforward and robust.

## Method

The authors started from the RHF parametrization PKO2, which includes only sigma, omega, and rho mesons, and applied a zero-range reduction to the finite-range Yukawa propagators followed by Fierz transformation to express the Fock terms as local Hartree terms. This produced a Hartree-Fock equivalent parametrization called PKO2-H, which was then used in random phase approximation (RPA) calculations of spin-dipole excitations in 16O for the (p,n) channel. The resulting strength distributions for each spin-parity component (J^pi = 0^-, 1^-, and 2^-) were compared with experimental data from polarized proton beam measurements.

## Key numbers

- The peak at E_x approximately 0 MeV in 16O is composed of the 0_1^-, 1_1^-, 2_1^- triplets dominated by the J^pi = 2^- component.
- The main spin-dipole resonance is located at E_x approximately 7.5 MeV and is a J^pi = 2^- state.
- A "shoulder" structure at E_x = 5.86 MeV is identified as a J^pi = 2^- state.
- Broad spin-dipole resonances at E_x approximately 9.5 MeV and 12 MeV are formed by mixtures of J^pi = 1^- and 2^- states, with the former dominated by 2^- and the latter by 1^-.
- The localized RHF equivalent RPA calculations produce results nearly identical to the full RHF+RPA calculations for all three spin-parity components.

## Context

This work extends the fully self-consistent description of charge-exchange excitations in covariant density functional theory (CDFT) achieved by Liang (2008) using RPA based on density-dependent RHF theory. It provides a local alternative to the non-local RHF approach, maintaining the advantages of existing relativistic Hartree functionals while solving problems in the isovector channel. The approach enables future extensions to deformed systems via the finite amplitude method, as demonstrated by Liang (2013), and opens perspectives for developing nuclear local covariant density functionals with proper isoscalar and isovector properties.
