# Excitation Functions of Stopping Power and Flow in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Using a relativistic transport (ART) model, we study the stopping power, the formation of superdense hadronic matter as well as the strength of transverse and radial flow in central Au+Au collisions at beam momentum from 2 to 12 GeV/c per nucleon. We find that complete stopping is achieved in the whole beam momentum range. In particular, the proton rapidity distribution scaled by the beam rapidity is independent of the beam momentum, and this is in agreement with the experimental findings. Also, a large volume of superdense hadronic matter with a local energy density exceeding that expected for the transition of a hadronic matter to the quark-gluon plasma is formed in collisions at beam momenta greater than 8 GeV/c per nucleon. Furthermore, it is found that the transverse flow in these collisions is sensitive to the nuclear equation of state and decreases with increasing beam momentum. On the other hand, the radial flow is insensitive to the equation of state, and its strength increases with beam momentum.

# Excitation Functions of Stopping Power and Flow in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

## Key claim
Complete stopping is achieved in central Au+Au collisions across the entire beam momentum range from 2 to 12 GeV/c per nucleon, and the scaled proton rapidity distribution is independent of beam momentum. However, the results are based on purely hadronic dynamics without including quark-gluon plasma or chiral restored phases, so deviations of experimental observations from these predictions might signal new phenomena related to QGP formation.

## Method
The authors used the A Relativistic Transport (ART) model to simulate central Au+Au collisions at an impact parameter of 2 fm, with beam momenta from 2 to 12 GeV/c per nucleon. They analyzed stopping power via proton rapidity distributions, superdense matter formation by tracking volumes exceeding a local energy density of 2.5 GeV/fm³, and collective flow using the Danielewicz-Odyniec method for transverse flow and a radial velocity definition for radial flow. Both a cascade mode (no mean field) and a transport mode with a soft equation of state (compressibility 210 MeV) were compared.

## Key numbers
- Complete stopping is achieved across the whole beam momentum range from 2 to 12 GeV/c per nucleon for central Au+Au collisions.
- A large volume of superdense hadronic matter with local energy density exceeding 2.5 GeV/fm³ is formed at beam momenta greater than 8 GeV/c per nucleon.
- The maximum baryon density from the transport model agrees well with relativistic hydrodynamics predictions at beam momenta below 9 GeV/c per nucleon.
- The radial flow velocity reaches a constant value of about 0.78 c at higher beam momenta.
- The transverse flow parameter F from the cascade mode is about a factor of two smaller than that using the soft equation of state at all energies studied.

## Context
This work extends earlier hydrodynamical predictions by Gyulassy (1984) that the hadronic-to-QGP phase transition might occur between 2 and 10 GeV per nucleon, and by Rischke (1995) showing that QGP formation could cause abrupt changes in transverse and radial flows. The study provides baseline hadronic predictions for experiments at Brookhaven's AGS (E895, E866, E917) searching for QGP formation and chiral symmetry restoration. The scaling of proton rapidity distributions agrees with the experimental analysis by Harris (1991) covering incident energies from 0.25 to 160 GeV per nucleon, and the maximum baryon density results are compared to the Rankine-Hugoniot shock equation solutions from Taub (1948) and Nix (1980).
