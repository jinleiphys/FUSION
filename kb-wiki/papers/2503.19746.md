# The role of chiral symmetry and the non-ordinary $κ/K^*_0(700)$ nature in $π^\pm K_S$ femtoscopic correlations

> We show that the use of realistic $πK$ interactions, obtained from a dispersive analysis of scattering data, as well as relativistic corrections, are essential to describe recently observed $π^\pm K_S$ femtoscopic correlations. We demonstrate that the spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking dynamics and the non-ordinary features of the $κ/K^*_0(700)$ resonance, together with large cancellations between isospin channels, produce a large suppression of $π^\pm K_S$ femtoscopic correlations compared to widely used models. Within an improved version of the standard on-shell factorization formalism, we illustrate that compensating for this interaction suppression leads to source radii smaller than 1 fm, contrary to usual expectations, as well as larger correlation strengths. The relation between these two parameters cannot be accommodated within naive models describing the nature of the resonances. This may raise concerns about the applicability of popular but too simple approaches for systems with light mesons. However, the correlation-suppression effects we demonstrate here will be relevant in any formalism, and substantial corrections may be expected for other femtoscopic systems involving light mesons.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that realistic πK interactions from dispersive analyses and relativistic corrections are essential to describe ALICE π±KS femtoscopic correlations, and that the spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking dynamics and non-ordinary nature of the κ/K*0(700) resonance produce a large suppression of correlations compared to widely used models, with the caveat that these effects are demonstrated within an improved on-shell factorization formalism but are argued to be relevant in any formalism.

## Method

The authors used an improved version of the Lednický–Lyuboshitz (LL) factorization formalism, replacing the non-relativistic integration measure and propagators with relativistic counterparts, and employed realistic πK S-wave scattering amplitudes from a dispersively constrained fit to data (CFD) for both isospin I=1/2 and I=3/2 channels. They fitted the resulting correlation function C_S(k*) to three ALICE datasets (different multiplicity classes and kT cuts) using three free parameters: source radius R, correlation strength λ, and a normalization factor N, while keeping the πK interactions fixed across all datasets.

## Key numbers

- Source radii from fits: R = 0.36(3)(3) fm (dataset I, 0-100% multiplicity class, kT>0), R = 0.41(3)(3) fm (dataset II, 0-100% multiplicity class, kT<0.5 GeV), R = 0.68(5)(3) fm (dataset III, 0-5% multiplicity class, kT<0.5 GeV)
- Correlation strengths: λ = 0.19(2)(4) (dataset I), λ = 0.29(4)(5) (dataset II), λ = 0.80(14)(13) (dataset III)
- χ²/d.o.f. values: 0.86 (dataset I), 1.01 (dataset II), 1.33 (dataset III)
- ChPT two-loop result for I=1/2 scattering length: mπ a0(1/2) = 0.224; CFD value: mπ a0(1/2) = 0.225(8)
- Dispersive value for I=1/2 effective range: mπ d0(1/2) = -3.8(4); CFD I=3/2 effective range: mπ d0(3/2) = 30(5)
- Relativistic correction at k*=0 is approximately 20% suppression compared to non-relativistic LL model

## Context

This work extends the ALICE (2023) analysis of π±KS femtoscopic correlations, which used a non-relativistic elastic Breit-Wigner model for the I=1/2 channel only and neglected the I=3/2 component. The authors show that the ALICE toy model overestimates correlations because it ignores chiral symmetry threshold suppression (Weinberg, 1966) and the non-ordinary nature of the κ/K*0(700) resonance, as well as large cancellations between isospin channels first noted by Venugopalan (1992). The results raise concerns about the applicability of simple factorization approaches for systems with light mesons and suggest that similar corrections will be needed for other femtoscopic systems involving pions, kaons, and etas, including ππ correlations with the σ/f0(500) resonance (Peláez, 2021).
