# New features of the triaxial nuclei described with a coherent state model

> Supplementing the Liquid Drop Model (LDM) Hamiltonian, written in the intrinsic reference frame, with a sextic oscillator plus a centrifugal term in the variable $β$ and a potential in $γ$ with a minimum in $\fracπ{6}$, the Schödinger equation is separated for the two variables which results in having a new description for the triaxial nuclei, called Sextic and Mathieu Approach (SMA). SMA is applied for two non-axial nuclei, $^{180}$Hf and $^{182}$W and results are compared with those yielded by the Coherent State Model (CSM). As the main result of this paper we derive analytically the equations characterizing SMA from a semi-classical treatment of the CSM Hamiltonian. In this manner the potentials in $β$ and $γ$ variables respectively, show up in a quite natural way which contrasts their ad-hoc choice when SMA emerges from LDM.

## Key claim

The Sextic and Mathieu Approach (SMA), previously introduced by supplementing the Liquid Drop Model Hamiltonian with ad-hoc potentials, can be analytically derived from a semi-classical treatment of the Coherent State Model (CSM) Hamiltonian, provided one neglects non-quadratic momentum terms and coordinate-momentum couplings in the classical limit.

## Method

The authors first quantize the classical Hamilton function obtained by averaging the CSM boson Hamiltonian with a coherent state, restricting to the subspace where the two non-zero quadrupole boson amplitudes are equal. After applying polar coordinate transformations and approximating the potential as separable in the radial variable r (proportional to the deformation β) and the angular variable γ, the Schrödinger equation separates into a sextic oscillator plus centrifugal term for β and a Mathieu equation for γ. The resulting SMA formalism is then applied to calculate excitation energies and B(E2) transition probabilities for the two triaxial nuclei ¹⁸⁰Hf and ¹⁸²W, with parameters fitted to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- For ¹⁸⁰Hf, the CSM parameters used in the classical potential analysis are d=3.5, A₁=10.65 keV, A₂=15.146 keV, and A₃=150 keV; the minimum of the approximated potential U(r,γ) occurs at (r₀,γ₀)=(2.6,π/6) with U_min=-3305.25 keV.
- For ¹⁸²W, the CSM parameters are d=3.22, A₁=12.95 keV, A₂=7.9 keV, and A₃=240 keV; the minimum of the approximated potential U(r,γ) occurs at (r₀,γ₀)=(2.4,π/6) with U_min=-3276.43 keV.
- The exact potential V(r,γ) for ¹⁸⁰Hf has minimum V_min=-3280.31 keV at (r₀,γ₀)=(2.7,π/6), with equipotential curves separated by 50 keV.
- The exact potential V(r,γ) for ¹⁸²W has minimum V_min=-3250.92 keV at (r₀,γ₀)=(2.5,π/6), with equipotential curves separated by 50 keV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous SMA formalism (Raduta and Buganu, 2011) which was applied to five triaxial nuclei (¹⁸⁸Os, ¹⁹⁰Os, ¹⁹²Os, ²²⁸Th, ²³⁰Th) by providing a theoretical derivation from the Coherent State Model (CSM) of Raduta (various works). The derivation explains why SMA and CSM yield similar-quality agreement with experimental data, and it contrasts with other approaches for γ-soft nuclei around γ₀=π/6 that use different β-γ potentials, such as the Kratzer potential in β with an oscillator in γ (Fortunato et al., various years). The work enables a unified understanding of triaxial nuclear descriptions and provides a semiclassical foundation for the separable β-γ equations used in critical-point symmetries like Y(5) and Z(5) (Iachello, 2003; Bonatsos et al., various years).
