# From a complex scalar field to the two-fluid picture of superfluidity

> The hydrodynamic description of a superfluid is usually based on a two-fluid picture. We compute the basic properties of the relativistic two-fluid system from the underlying microscopic physics of a relativistic φ^4 complex scalar field theory. We work at nonzero but small temperature and weak coupling, and we neglect dissipation. We clarify the relationship between different formulations of the two-fluid model, and how they are parameterized in terms of partly redundant current and momentum 4-vectors. As an application, we compute the velocities of first and second sound at small temperatures and in the presence of a superflow. While our results are of a very general nature, we also comment on their interpretation as a step towards the hydrodynamics of the color-flavor locked state of quark matter, which, in particular in the presence of kaon condensation, appears to be a complicated multi-component fluid.

## Key claim

The paper derives the relativistic two-fluid hydrodynamic description of a superfluid from the microscopic physics of a complex scalar field theory with a quartic interaction, working at nonzero but small temperature and weak coupling while neglecting dissipation, and clarifies the relationship between different formulations of the two-fluid model.

## Method

The authors start from a relativistic φ⁴ complex scalar field theory with spontaneous U(1) symmetry breaking and compute the one-loop effective action at nonzero temperature in the presence of a homogeneous, static superflow. They evaluate the stress-energy tensor and conserved current from the microscopic theory, then translate these into the two-fluid hydrodynamic formalism by identifying the generalized pressure and energy density as Legendre transforms of each other. The sound velocities are computed from the dispersion relations of the Goldstone mode obtained from the tree-level propagator.

## Key numbers

* First sound velocity at zero temperature and zero superflow, in the massless limit (m=0): 1/√3, the well-known relativistic value.
* Second sound velocity: computed at small temperatures and in the presence of a superflow (explicit numerical value not given in the truncated text).
* Goldstone mode dispersion at small momenta: ε₁,ₖ = √[(σ²−m²)/(3σ²−m²)] ζ(û)|k| + O(|k|³), where ζ(û) is an angular-dependent function given by Eq. (13) in the paper.
* Mass of the kaon condensate Goldstone mode in CFL-K⁰ quark matter: approximately 50 keV (cited from Son (2001)).
* Critical temperature for kaon condensation in CFL-K⁰: estimated to be of order tens of MeV (cited from Alford (2007)).

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature hydrodynamic interpretation of field-theoretic effective actions by Son (2002) to nonzero temperatures, and connects the two-fluid formalism of Lebedev and Khalatnikov (1982) and Carter (1989) with a microscopic field theory. The results are intended as a step toward understanding the hydrodynamics of the color-flavor locked phase of quark matter, particularly the kaon-condensed CFL-K⁰ phase where multiple spontaneously broken U(1) symmetries may create a multi-component superfluid.
