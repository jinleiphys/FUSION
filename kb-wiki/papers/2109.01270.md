# Dynamics of light hypernuclei in collisions of $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au at GeV energies

> The dynamics of light hypernuclei and nuclear clusters produced in $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au collisions has been investigated thoroughly with a microscopic transport model. All possible channels of hyperon production and transportation of hyperons in nuclear medium are implemented into the model. The light complex fragments are recognized with the Wigner density approach at the stage of freeze out in nuclear collisions. The isospin diffusion in the collisions is responsible for the neutron-rich cluster formation. The collective flows of nuclear clusters are consistent with the experimental data from FOPI collaboration. It is found that the influence of the hyperon-nucleon potential on the free hyperons is negligible, but available for the light hypernuclide formation. The directed and elliptic flows of $^{3}_Λ$H and $^{4}_Λ$H at incident energies of 2, 2.5, 3, 3.5 and 4 GeV/nucleon are investigated thoroughly and manifest the same structure with the nuclear clusters. The hypernuclear yields are produced in a wide rapidity and momentum regime with increasing the beam energy.

# Dynamics of light hypernuclei in collisions of $^{197}$Au+$197$Au at GeV energies

## Key claim

The hyperon-nucleon potential has a negligible influence on free hyperon production but is favorable for the formation of bound light hypernuclei ($^{3}_{\Lambda}$H and $^{4}_{\Lambda}$H); the directed and elliptic flows of these hypernuclei manifest the same structure as nuclear clusters, and hypernuclear yields saturate above 3 GeV/nucleon beam energy.

## Method

The Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics (LQMD) transport model was extended to include all possible channels of hyperon production (Λ, Σ, Ξ) and transportation in nuclear medium, with hyperon-nucleon optical potentials derived from relativistic mean-field theory (NL3 parameter set). Light complex fragments (Z ≤ 2) and hypernuclei were recognized at freeze-out using the Wigner phase-space density approach, and collective flows were computed from the Fourier expansion of azimuthal distributions. The model was applied to central and semi-central $^{197}$Au+$197$Au collisions at incident energies of 2, 2.5, 3, 3.5, and 4 GeV/nucleon.

## Key numbers

- Directed and elliptic flows of $^{3}_{\Lambda}$H and $^{4}_{\Lambda}$H were calculated at incident energies of 2, 2.5, 3, 3.5, and 4 GeV/nucleon.
- The optical potential of Λ at saturation density is -32 MeV; for Ξ it is -16 MeV; for Σ it is +12 MeV (weakly repulsive).
- The compression modulus of isospin symmetric nuclear matter is K = 230 MeV at saturation density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³.
- The symmetry energy parameter C_sym = 38 MeV, with linear symmetry energy (γ_s = 1) used; the symmetry energy at saturation density is 31.5 MeV.
- The root-mean-square radii used for Wigner density are 1.61 fm for triton and 1.74 fm for $^{3}$He.
- Hyperfragment yields in $^{20}$Ne+$^{12}$C collisions at 4.25 GeV/nucleon are calculated to be several tens μb.
- The model reproduces FOPI collaboration data for directed and elliptic flows of protons, deuterons, and α particles at 1.5 GeV/nucleon (centrality 3.35 fm < b < 6 fm).

## Context

This work extends the LQMD transport model (Feng, 2011; 2018) to hypernuclear dynamics in heavy-ion collisions, building on prior statistical multifragmentation models (Botvina, 2007; 2012), thermal source approaches (Andronic, 2011), and other microscopic transport models (Botvina, 2015; Gaitanos, 2016; Le, 2019). The results are consistent with experimental data from the FOPI collaboration (Reisdorf, 2012) and the HypHI experiment at GSI (Rappold, 2013; 2015). The work enables predictions for future experiments at HIAF (China), FAIR (Germany), and NICA (Russia), particularly for producing neutron-rich hypernuclei and multiple-strangeness hypernuclei. The author notes that direct 3-body or 4-body collisions and preequilibrium hyperfragment formation are not yet implemented, and modifications are in progress following the parton-hadron quantum molecular dynamics (PHQMD) approach (Aichelin, 2020).
