# $K^{*}$ vector meson resonances dynamics in heavy-ion collisions

> We study the strange vector meson ($K^*, \bar K^*$) dynamics in relativistic heavy-ion collisions based on the microscopic Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach which incorporates partonic and hadronic degrees-of-freedom, a phase transition from hadronic to partonic matter - Quark-Gluon-Plasma (QGP) - and a dynamical hadronization of quarks and antiquarks as well as final hadronic interactions. We investigate the role of in-medium effects on the $K^*, \bar K^*$ meson dynamics by employing Breit-Wigner spectral functions for the $K^*$'s with self-energies obtained from a self-consistent coupled-channel G-matrix approach. Furthermore, we confront the PHSD calculations with experimental data for p+p, Cu+Cu and Au+Au collisions at energies up to $\sqrt{{s}_{NN}} = 200$~GeV. Our analysis shows that at relativistic energies most of the final $K^*$s (observed experimentally) are produced during the late hadronic phase, dominantly by the $K+ π\to K^*$ channel, such that the fraction of the $K^*$s from the QGP is small and can hardly be reconstructed from the final observables. The influence of the in-medium effects on the $K^*$ dynamics at RHIC energies is rather modest due to their dominant production at low baryon densities (but high meson densities), however, it increases with decreasing beam energy. Moreover, we find that the additional cut on the invariant mass region of the $K^*$ further influences the shape and the height of the final spectra. This imposes severe constraints on the interpretation of the experimental results.

## Key claim

The authors claim that at relativistic energies (RHIC top energy of √sNN = 200 GeV), most final K* mesons observed experimentally are produced during the late hadronic phase dominantly by the K + π → K* channel, such that the fraction of K*s originating from the Quark-Gluon Plasma is small and can hardly be reconstructed from final observables, with the caveat that this conclusion depends on the specific collision energy and that in-medium effects become more important at lower beam energies.

## Method

The authors employed the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, which incorporates partonic and hadronic degrees of freedom, a phase transition to Quark-Gluon Plasma, dynamical hadronization, and final hadronic interactions. They implemented in-medium effects for K* and anti-K* mesons using relativistic Breit-Wigner spectral functions with self-energies obtained from a self-consistent coupled-channel G-matrix approach, and compared PHSD calculations with experimental data for p+p, Cu+Cu, and Au+Au collisions at energies up to √sNN = 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum decay width of K* and anti-K* is Γ⁰ = 42 MeV with nominal resonance mass μ₀ = 892 MeV (from PDG).
- The K* experiences a repulsive mass shift of δM_K* ≈ 50 MeV at normal nuclear matter density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³ using tree-level amplitudes, reduced to δM_K*(ρ₀) ≈ 30 MeV when using unitarized amplitudes.
- The K* production cross section from Kπ scattering reaches a maximum value of 160-170 mb, while the anti-K* cross section reaches about 100 mb at nuclear matter density.
- The threshold energy for K* creation is shifted to M_th ≈ 0.633 GeV + ΔM(ρ), with ΔM(ρ) ≈ 0.06 M_K at normal matter density.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of strange vector meson dynamics using transport models such as UrQMD for Pb+Pb at √sNN = 17.3 GeV (Bleicher 2002, Vogel 2010) and EPOS3 with UrQMD afterburner for Pb+Pb at √sNN = 2.76 TeV (Knospe 2015). The authors build on their earlier evaluation of K* properties as a function of nuclear baryon density (Ilner 2013) and incorporate those in-medium effects into the PHSD framework, which has been successfully applied to p+p, p+A, and A+A reactions from about √sNN = 8 GeV to 2.76 TeV. The findings impose constraints on interpreting experimental K* signals as direct probes of the QGP, and suggest that lower beam energies (e.g., √sNN = 10 GeV) offer better prospects for observing in-medium modifications.
