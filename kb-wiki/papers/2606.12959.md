# Nucleon matrix elements of axial anomaly, axial currents and pseudoscalar currents in the QCD sum rule

> We have analyzed one-nucleon matrix elements of current-current correlators; the currents consist of pseudoscalar octet, isovector and isoscalar currents, axial anomaly, and axial isovector and isoscalar currents. Using QCD sum rules, the coupling constants of nucleon with each of these currents have been expressed in terms of nucleon matrix elements of quark, gluon and quark-gluon composite operators and moments of parton distribution function. On the phenomenological side, contribution from the non-diagonal matrix elements of operators between nucleon and its excited states or continuum states have also been accounted for. For the pseudoscalar coupling constants of the nucleon two expressions have been obtained in which one of them consists of only moments of parton distribution function but yielding approximately same numerical result as the other one. Of particular interest is the nucleon matrix element of axial anomaly which has been largely ignored in the current literature.

## Key claim
The nucleon matrix element of the axial anomaly (topological charge density) has been largely ignored in the literature, and this work provides the first QCD sum-rule determination of the coupling constants for the pseudoscalar octet current and the axial anomaly, with the hedge that the extracted values depend on the fitting range and the choice of sum-rule combination (e.g., from \(\Pi^{(88)}_4\) vs \(\Pi^{(88)}_{41}\)).

## Method
The author evaluates two-point correlation functions of pseudoscalar and axial currents in a one-nucleon state, using QCD sum rules. The operator product expansion (OPE) expresses these correlators in terms of nucleon matrix elements of quark, gluon, and quark-gluon composite operators, as well as moments of parton distribution functions. On the phenomenological side, contributions from non-diagonal matrix elements between the nucleon and its excited or continuum states are included. Borel transforms are applied to the sum rules, and the coefficients of different powers of \(\vec{q}^2\) are matched to extract the coupling constants \(\chi_8\) (for the octet pseudoscalar current) and \(\chi_g\) (for the axial anomaly), as well as the axial coupling constants \(g_A^3\), \(g_A^{u+d}\), and \(g_A^s\).

## Key numbers
- \(|\chi_8| = 0.483 \pm 0.025\) (dimensionless coupling constant for the octet pseudoscalar current, extracted from fits to \(\Pi^{(88)}_4\) and \(\Pi^{(88)}_{41}\) over the Borel parameter range \(0.8 < s < 1.5\) GeV\(^2\)).
- \(|\chi_g| = 1.014 \pm 0.102\) (dimensionless coupling constant for the axial anomaly, extracted from fits to \(\Pi^{(08)}_4\) and \(\Pi^{(08)}_{41}\)).
- \(|g_A^3| = 1.216\) (isovector axial coupling constant, from the fit to \(-2M \frac{\partial}{\partial \vec{q}^2} B[\Pi^{(33)}]_{\vec{q}^2=0}\) giving \((g_A^3)^2 = 1.479\)).
- \(|g_A^{u+d}| = 0.409\) (isoscalar axial coupling constant, from the fit to \(M \frac{\partial}{\partial \vec{q}^2} B[\Pi^{(03)}]_{\vec{q}^2=0}\) giving \(g_A^3 g_A^{u+d} = 0.497\)).
- \(|g_A^s| = 0.054\) (strange quark axial coupling constant, from the fit giving \((g_A^s)^2 = 0.003\)).

## Context
This work extends the approach of Nishikawa et al. (2004, 2008) and Singh (2015) for calculating axial coupling constants via two-point correlation functions in a one-nucleon state, but now includes the nucleon matrix element of the axial anomaly, which had been largely ignored. The results are relevant for understanding the Bjorken sum rule (Bjorken, 1969) and the Ellis-Jaffe sum rule (Ellis and Jaffe, 1974), where inclusion of the anomaly is necessary to avoid predicted large isospin violations that are not seen in experimental data (Adolph et al., 2016). The method enables future lattice QCD comparisons and further studies of the gluon spin contribution \(\Delta G\) via the anomaly.
