# Phase Structure in a Hadronic Chiral Model

> We study the phase diagram of a hadronic chiral flavor-SU(3) model. Heavy baryon resonances can induce a phase structure that matches current results from lattice-QCD calculations at finite temperature and baryon density. Furthermore, we determine trajectories of constant entropy per net baryon in the phase diagram.

## Key claim

Heavy baryon resonances, when included as a single effective state with adjustable couplings in a hadronic chiral SU(3) model, can produce a phase diagram with a first-order transition line ending in a critical point whose location matches lattice-QCD results, provided the resonance mass and couplings are tuned appropriately. The abstract glosses over the fact that the model requires free parameters (mass, scalar coupling, and vector coupling of the resonance) to be adjusted to reproduce the desired phase structure.

## Method

The authors use a non-linear SU(3)L × SU(3)R chiral Lagrangian in mean-field approximation, including the full baryon octet and scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axialvector meson multiplets. They add a single heavy baryonic resonance with mass m_R = m_0 + g_R σ and vector coupling g_Rω = r_V g_Nω, treating m_0, g_R, and r_V as free parameters. The grand canonical potential is extremized to obtain self-consistent gap equations for the meson fields, and the phase structure is mapped by solving these equations at finite temperature T and quark chemical potential μ_q.

## Key numbers

- Critical endpoint located at T_E ≈ 180 MeV, μ_E ≈ 110 MeV for the parameter choice m_R(σ_0) = 2 GeV, r_V = 0.4, m_0 = 0.57 GeV
- At μ_q = 0, the critical temperature T_c = 185.6 MeV for the same parameter set
- Latent heat along the phase boundary shows a local minimum around μ_q ≈ 200 MeV, with values of approximately 120–160 MeV/fm³ for beam energies of 5–25 AGeV
- The S/A ≈ 20 adiabat passes through the critical endpoint; beam energies of 5–10 AGeV are sufficient to overshoot the phase coexistence region
- The critical endpoint is reached for a beam energy of about 60 AGeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of chiral phase transitions in hadronic models (Stephanov 2004) that typically predicted lower critical temperatures at finite density than lattice results. It builds on the observation by Gerber and Leutwyler (1989) that heavy hadronic states contribute substantially to the energy density at high temperature despite Boltzmann suppression, and on hadron resonance gas descriptions that reproduce lattice thermodynamics below T_c (Bielefeld group). The model enables predictions for the Compressed Baryonic Matter experiment at GSI FAIR by providing equations of state and isentropic expansion trajectories for heavy-ion collisions in the beam energy range of 5–60 AGeV.
