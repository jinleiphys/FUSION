# HFB theory for nuclei near the drip-lines: continuum coupling

> We have developed a new HFB code that specifically addresses nuclear structure physics near the driplines. The HFB equations are solved on a two-dimensional lattice for axially symmetric even-even nuclei using B-Spline techniques. The quasiparticle energy spectrum is obtained by direct diagonalization of the HFB lattice Hamiltonian with LAPACK. The energy spectrum extends high into the continuum, up to several thousand MeV. Calculations with Skyrme forces and (density-dependent) delta pairing interactions are now underway.

## Key claim

The authors have developed a new Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) code that solves the equations on a two-dimensional lattice for axially symmetric even-even nuclei, specifically targeting the challenges of nuclear structure near the drip lines where large pairing correlations and strong coupling to the particle continuum are expected.

## Method

The HFB equations are solved on a two-dimensional lattice for axially symmetric even-even nuclei using B-Spline techniques, with the quasiparticle energy spectrum obtained by direct diagonalization of the HFB lattice Hamiltonian using LAPACK. The code uses the Skyrme M* interaction in the particle-hole channel and either a pure delta force or a density-dependent delta interaction (DDDI) for pairing. The energy spectrum extends high into the continuum, up to several thousand MeV, with a cutoff at an equivalent single-particle energy of about 60 MeV for calculating observables.

## Key numbers

- Quasiparticle energy spectrum extends up to about 2000 MeV for the test case of 22Ne
- Cutoff for calculating observables is set at an equivalent single-particle energy of about 60 MeV
- Pure delta pairing interaction strength used: V0 = -173 MeV fm3 (taken from Dobaczewski et al. 1995)
- B-Splines of order M=7 used, with a lattice size of 8 fm in radial direction and lattice spacing of about 1 fm
- Convergence tests performed with lattice spacings of Δx = 1.33, 1.14, and 1.00 fm for 22Ne

## Context

This work extends the 1-D calculations for spherical nuclei by Dobaczewski et al. (1996) to a full 2-D lattice treatment for axially symmetric deformed nuclei. The code enables calculations of binding energies, separation energies, pairing gaps, densities, rms radii, and electromagnetic moments for nuclei near the drip lines, with planned extensions to investigate neutron halos, neutron skins, and proton radioactivity. The authors plan to compare results with Dobaczewski's 1-D HFB code for spherical nuclei and to add constraints for quadrupole and octupole moments to calculate potential energy surfaces for nuclei far from stability.
