# Dihadron fragmentation functions and high Pt hadron-hadron correlations

> We propose the formulation of a dihadron fragmentation function in terms of parton matrix elements. Under the collinear factorization approximation and facilitated by the cut-vertex technique, the two hadron inclusive cross section at leading order (LO) in e+ e- annihilation is shown to factorize into a short distance parton cross section and the long distance dihadron fragmentation function. We also derive the DGLAP evolution equation of this function at leading log. The evolution equation for the non-singlet quark fragmentation function is solved numerically with a simple ansatz for the initial condition and results are presented for cases of physical interest.

# Dihadron fragmentation functions and high Pt hadron-hadron correlations

## Key claim

The dihadron fragmentation function can be formulated in terms of parton matrix elements and factorized from the hard parton cross section at leading order in e+e- annihilation, but the numerical demonstration of its DGLAP evolution is restricted to the non-singlet quark case with a simple ansatz for the initial condition at Q^2 = 2 GeV^2, and the results show little change in the ratio D_q^{h1h2}/D_q^{h1} as Q^2 is varied, which depends strongly on the ratio of the momentum fractions of the two hadrons.

## Method

The author defines the dihadron fragmentation function D_q^{h1,h2}(z1,z2,Q^2) using a cut-vertex technique in the collinear factorization approximation at leading twist, integrating over all transverse momentum q_perp up to a scale mu_perp. The DGLAP evolution equation at leading log is derived for the non-singlet quark fragmentation function D_NS^{h1,h2} = D_q^{h1,h2} - D_qbar^{h1,h2}, incorporating both soft gluon radiation and perturbative production of hadron pairs from independent single fragmentation of a quark and a gluon. The evolution equation is solved numerically with an initial condition D_NS^{h1h2}(z1,z2) = D^{h1}(z1) D^{h2}(z2) at Q^2 = 2 GeV^2.

## Key numbers

- Initial condition for evolution set at Q^2 = 2 GeV^2; results presented up to log(Q^2) = 4.693, corresponding to Q^2 = 109 GeV^2.
- Numerical results shown for the ratio D_q^{h1h2}(z1,z2,Q^2) / D_q^{h1}(z1,Q^2) at fixed ratios z1 = 2 z2 and z1 = 3 z2, over the Q^2 range from 2 GeV^2 to 109 GeV^2.
- The ratio of the dihadron to single hadron fragmentation functions shows little change as Q^2 is varied over a wide range, but the evolution is strongly dependent on the ratio r = z1/z2.

## Context

This work extends the standard DGLAP evolution formalism for single fragmentation functions (Gribov-Lipatov 1972) to the double inclusive fragmentation function D_q^{h1,h2}(z1,z2,Q^2), motivated by experimental observations from the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) by Adler et al. (2003) that same-side correlations of two high pT hadrons in central Au+Au collisions remain approximately unchanged compared to p+p and d+Au collisions. The author assumes that medium modification due to multiple scattering and induced gluon radiation closely resembles radiative corrections due to evolution in vacuum, following the approach of Guo and Wang. The results enable future study of the more complicated singlet fragmentation functions and the influence of medium modifications on dihadron correlations in heavy-ion collisions.
