# Beyond the neutron-drip line: two-neutron decay of unbound nuclei

> We discuss a decay of unbound nuclei beyond the neutron drip-line using a three-body model with a core nucleus and two valence neutrons. We particularly discuss the role of dineutron correlation between the valence neutrons in the two-neutron emission from the ground state of $^{26}$O and $^{10}$He nuclei. Our calculations clearly indicate that the emission of the two neutrons in the back-to-back direction is enhanced due to the dineutron correlation.

# Beyond the neutron-drip line: two-neutron decay of unbound nuclei

## Key claim
The authors argue that the enhancement of back-to-back emission of two neutrons from unbound nuclei such as ²⁶O and ¹⁰He provides a direct experimental signature of dineutron correlation, but note that for ¹⁰He the agreement with experimental decay energy spectra depends sensitively on the assumed s-wave scattering length for n-⁸He scattering, which remains poorly constrained.

## Method
The authors employ a three-body model consisting of an inert core nucleus plus two valence neutrons, using a density-dependent contact interaction for the neutron-neutron force. For ²⁶O, the initial state is constructed from the ²⁷F ground state wave function, and a sudden proton removal approximation is used to generate a wave packet that evolves in time; the decay energy spectrum is obtained via the two-particle Green's function in coordinate space. For ¹⁰He, a similar approach is used with a parity-dependent Woods-Saxon potential for the neutron-core interaction, and the s-wave scattering length is varied to study its effect on the decay spectrum and angular correlation.

## Key numbers
- The decay energy of ²⁶O was measured at RIKEN to be 18 ± 3 (stat) ± 4 (syst) keV (Kondo 2015).
- The uncorrelated (1d₃/₂)² configuration in ²⁶O produces a peak at 1.498 MeV, corresponding to twice the 1d₃/₂ resonance energy in ²⁵O (749 keV).
- The three-body model calculation yields the 2⁺ state in ²⁶O at 1.282 MeV with a width Γ = 0.12 MeV, in agreement with the experimental secondary peak at 1.28⁺⁰·¹¹₋₀.₀₈ MeV.
- The ¹⁰He decay energy spectrum shows a prominent peak at 1.54(11) MeV with a width Γ = 1.91(41) MeV (Johansson 2010).
- The ¹/₂⁻ resonance in ⁹He is reported at 1.33(8) MeV with width Γ = 0.10(6) MeV (Bohlen 1999), but other measurements give 2.0 ± 0.2 MeV with width ~2 MeV (Golovkov 2007) and 1.235 ± 0.115 MeV with width 130⁺¹⁷⁰₋₁₃₀ keV (Al Kalanee 2013).
- The s-wave scattering length for n-⁸He scattering is reported as a_s = -3.17(66) fm (Johansson 2010), a_s > -20 fm (Golovkov 2007), a_s = -12 ± 3 fm (Al Kalanee 2013), and a_s < -10 fm (Chen 2001).

## Context
This work extends previous three-body model studies of ²⁶O by Hagino and Sagawa (2014, 2016) and complements Gamow shell model calculations by Wang (2017) and another three-body model by Grigorenko et al. (2013). The study is motivated by the "oxygen anomaly", the observation that the neutron drip-line extends from ²⁴O (N=16) to at least ³¹F (N=22), explained by Otsuka et al. (2005, 2010) using three-body and tensor interactions. The angular correlation analysis provides a more direct probe of dineutron correlation than previous Coulomb dissociation studies of Borromean nuclei (Nakamura 2006, Hagino and Sagawa 2007, Baur and Hencken 2007), which could only access the mean opening angle. The work also parallels two-proton radioactivity studies (Pfützner et al. 2012) but benefits from the absence of long-range Coulomb interactions. Future directions include extending to five-body models for four-neutron emission decays such as ²⁸O, for which experimental data are under analysis at RIKEN (Kondo 2018).
