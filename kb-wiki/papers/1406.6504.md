# Decomposition of sensitivity of the symmetry energy observables

> To exactly answer which density region that some frequently used symmetry-energy-sensitive observables probe, for the first time, we make a study of decomposition of the sensitivity of some symmetry-energy-sensitive observables. It is found that for the Au+Au reaction at incident beam energies of 200 and 400 MeV/nucleon, frequently used symmetry-energy-sensitive observables mainly probe the density-dependent symmetry energy around 1.25$ρ_{0}$ (for pionic observables) or 1.5$ρ_{0}$ (for nucleonic observables). Effects of the symmetry energy in the low-density region is in general small but observable. The fact that the symmetry-energy-sensitive observables are not sensitive to the symmetry energy in the maximal baryon-density region increases the difficulty of studying nuclear symmetry energy at super-density.

# Decomposition of sensitivity of the symmetry energy observables

## Key claim
For Au+Au reactions at 200 and 400 MeV/nucleon, the frequently used symmetry-energy-sensitive observables do not probe the symmetry energy at the maximal baryon density reached (about 2.0–2.5ρ₀), but instead show maximal sensitivity around 1.25ρ₀ for pionic observables and around 1.5ρ₀ for nucleonic observables. The abstract omits the qualifier that this decomposition result depends on the specific isospin-dependent transport model (IBUU) and the particular form of the symmetry potential used.

## Method
The authors used the isospin-dependent IBUU (Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck) transport model to simulate central Au+Au collisions at 200 and 400 MeV/nucleon with impact parameter b=1 fm. They decomposed the sensitivity of each observable by turning off the symmetry energy in one of five density bins (ρ≤0.5ρ₀, 0.5–1.0ρ₀, 1.0–1.5ρ₀, 1.5–2.0ρ₀, >2.0ρ₀) while keeping it active in all other regions, then computing the relative sensitivity RS = |R₀−Rᵢ|/R₀ × 100% compared to the standard calculation with full symmetry energy.

## Key numbers
- Central maximal baryon density reached: about 2.5ρ₀ for 400 MeV/nucleon beam energy and about 2.0ρ₀ for 200 MeV/nucleon beam energy.
- Maximal sensitivity of π⁻/π⁺ ratio lies in the density region around 1.25ρ₀ at both 200 and 400 MeV/nucleon beam energies.
- Maximal sensitivity of pre-equilibrium free neutron-to-proton ratio n/p lies in the density region around 1.5ρ₀ at both 200 and 400 MeV/nucleon beam energies.
- Sensitivity of π⁻/π⁺ to the symmetry energy at 200 MeV/nucleon is about two times larger than that at 400 MeV/nucleon beam energy.

## Context
This work extends the approach of Liu et al. (2005) by applying sensitivity decomposition to multiple symmetry-energy observables in heavy-ion collisions. It contradicts the common assumption that observables like the π⁻/π⁺ ratio (first proposed by Li in 2002) probe the highest densities reached, showing instead that maximal sensitivity occurs at moderate supra-saturation densities (1.25–1.5ρ₀). The finding that collision effects at higher densities mask the symmetry energy signal explains why experiments at lower beam energies (e.g., planned at RIKEN, as noted by Lynch in 2014) may be more effective for constraining the high-density symmetry energy.
