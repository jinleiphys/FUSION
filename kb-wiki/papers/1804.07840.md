# Towers of baryons of the ${\cal N}=2$ quark model band in the large $N_c$ limit

> We performed a complete analysis of the spectrum of all the states in the ${\cal N}=2$ quark model harmonic oscillator band with $N_f=3$ in the large $N_c$ limit including the often disregarded antisymmetric multiplet $[{\bf 20},1^+]$. We included configuration mixing effects. We found that the states in the $[{\bf 56},L^+]$ and $[{\bf 70},L^+]$ with $L=0,2$ fall into nine towers of degenerate states. We found that nonstrange antisymmetric states fall into three towers and respond to the same structure as the states in the $[{\bf 70},1^-]$ multiplet. We also showed explicitly the compatibility of these results and the scattering resonance picture.

## Key claim

The paper claims that all states in the ${\cal N}=2$ quark model harmonic oscillator band with $N_f=3$ in the large $N_c$ limit, including the antisymmetric multiplet $[{\bf 20},1^+]$, organize into a small number of degenerate towers, but this result holds only in the $SU(3)$ flavor symmetry limit and when configuration mixing effects are included at leading order.

## Method

The authors constructed effective mass operators up to order $O(N_c^0)$ using a generalization of the quark operator method that allows for configuration mixing between different $SU(6)\times O(3)$ multiplets with different orbital angular momentum $L$. They computed mass matrix elements for all 146 isospin-degenerate states in the $[{\bf 56},0^+]$, $[{\bf 70},0^+]$, $[{\bf 56},2^+]$, $[{\bf 70},2^+]$, and $[{\bf 20},1^+]$ multiplets, diagonalized the resulting 24 mass matrices, and analyzed the tower structure in terms of the quantum number $K$ that labels irreducible representations of the contracted $SU(2N_f)_c$ symmetry. They also checked compatibility with the resonance picture from meson-nucleon scattering.

## Key numbers

- The ${\cal N}=2$ band contains five multiplets: $[{\bf 56},0^+]$, $[{\bf 70},0^+]$, $[{\bf 56},2^+]$, $[{\bf 70},2^+]$, and $[{\bf 20},1^+]$.
- There are 40 spin-flavor multiplets containing 146 isospin-degenerate states to be considered.
- All symmetric and mixed-symmetric states of the ${\cal N}=2$ band have only nine distinct masses, expressed in terms of 11 coefficients: ${\bar c}_1^{S_0}$, ${\bar c}_1^{MS_0}$, ${\bar c}_1^{S_2}$, ${\bar c}_1^{MS_2}$, ${\bar c}_2^{MS_2}$, $c_3^{MS_2}$, $c_4^{MS_2}$, $c_5^{MS_0}$, $c_5^{MS_2}$, ${\bar c}_2^{S_2,MS_2}$, $c_3^{MS_0,MS_2}$.
- The nine towers are labeled by $K$ values: $0$, $1^\pm$, $2^\pm$, $3$, $1/2$, $3/2$, $5/2$.
- Nonstrange antisymmetric states fall into three towers with the same structure as the $[{\bf 70},1^-]$ multiplet.
- The mixing parameters are $\mu_1 = -\sqrt{2}\,c_3^{MS_0,MS_2}$ and $\mu_2 = -\sqrt{2}\,{\bar c}_2^{S_2,MS_2}$.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the ${\cal N}=2$ band from the nonstrange case studied in Willemyns (2017) to $N_f=3$ flavors, including the antisymmetric $[{\bf 20},1^+]$ multiplet that was often disregarded in prior studies. Previous works by Carlson (2000), Goity (2003), and Matagne (2005, 2006) studied individual multiplets of the ${\cal N}=2$ band but did not include configuration mixing effects in the large $N_c$ limit. The results are compatible with the resonance picture of Cohen (2005) and show that the tower structure for nonstrange antisymmetric states responds to the same structure as the $[{\bf 70},1^-]$ multiplet studied by Pirjol (2003) and Goity (2002). This enables future fits to experimental baryon masses using the 11 undetermined coefficients to predict the mass scale of the towers.
