# Hypernuclear stars from relativistic Hartree-Fock density functional theory

> The hypernuclear matter is studied within the relativistic Hartree-Fock theory employing several parametrizations of the hypernuclear density functional with density-dependent couplings. The equations of state and compositions of hypernuclear matter are determined for each parametrization and compact stars are constructed by solving their structure equations in spherical symmetry. We quantify the softening effect of Fock terms on the equation of state, as well as discuss the impact of tensor interactions, which are absent in the Hartree theories. Starting from models of density functionals which are fixed in the nuclear sector to the nuclear phenomenology, we vary the couplings in the hyperonic sector around the central values which are fitted to the hyperon potentials in nuclear matter. We use the SU(6) spin-flavor and SU(3) flavor symmetric quark models to relate the hyperonic couplings to the nucleonic ones. We find, consistent with previous Hartree studies, that for the SU(6) model the maximal masses of compact stars are below the two-solar mass limit. In the SU(3) model we find sufficiently massive compact stars with cores composed predominantly of $Λ$ and $Ξ$ hyperons and a low fraction of leptons (mostly electrons). The parameter space of the SU(3) model is identified where simultaneously hypernuclear compact stars obey the astrophysical limits on pulsar masses and the empirical hypernuclear potentials in nuclear matter are reproduced.

# Hypernuclear stars from relativistic Hartree-Fock density functional theory

## Key claim

The authors show that within relativistic Hartree-Fock theory, the SU(3) flavor symmetric quark model can produce hypernuclear compact stars with maximum masses above the two-solar mass limit, while the SU(6) spin-flavor model yields maximum masses below this threshold, consistent with previous Hartree studies. The viability of the SU(3) model depends on simultaneously satisfying both the astrophysical pulsar mass constraints and the empirical hyperon potentials in nuclear matter.

## Method

The authors extend relativistic density functional theory from the Hartree to the Hartree-Fock level for the full baryon octet, using density-dependent meson-baryon couplings with four RHF parametrizations (PKO1-3, PKA1) and two RH parametrizations (DD-ME2, GM1). They compute equations of state and compositions of hypernuclear matter in beta equilibrium with charge neutrality, then construct compact star models by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations, using SU(6) and SU(3) symmetric quark models to relate hyperonic couplings to nucleonic ones.

## Key numbers

- Maximum masses for purely nucleonic stars range from 2.36 M_sun (GM1) to 2.49 M_sun (PKO3), leaving room for softening from hyperonization
- For the SU(6) model, maximal masses of hypernuclear compact stars are below the two-solar mass limit (consistent with previous Hartree studies)
- The SU(3) model produces sufficiently massive compact stars with cores composed predominantly of Lambda and Xi hyperons and a low fraction of leptons (mostly electrons)
- Nuclear saturation properties for RHF parametrizations: saturation density 0.151-0.160 fm^-3, binding energy -15.83 to -16.04 MeV, compression modulus 229.96-262.44 MeV, symmetry energy 32.49-36.02 MeV with slope 75.92-103.50 MeV
- Dirac masses for RHF DFs range from 0.55 to 0.60 M (within the realistic description of spin-orbit splitting requiring 0.55-0.60 M)
- Nonrelativistic effective masses for RHF DFs range from 0.68 to 0.76 M (within the giant resonance constraint of 0.70-0.90 M)

## Context

This work extends previous Hartree-approximation studies of hypernuclear matter (Colucci 2013, van Dalen 2014, Banik 2014, Oertel 2015, Fortin 2017) to the relativistic Hartree-Fock level, which explicitly includes Fock terms and tensor interactions from pion exchanges that are absent in Hartree theories. The only prior treatment including the Lambda hyperon at the Fock level was Long (2012). The results enable future comparisons with gravitational-wave constraints from neutron star mergers (such as GW170817) and with upcoming NICER radius measurements, while the identified parameter space of the SU(3) model provides a framework for resolving the "hyperon puzzle" where hyperon formation typically softens the EoS below the two-solar mass limit.
