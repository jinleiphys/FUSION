# Properties of 12Be and 11Be in terms of single-particle motion in deformed potential

> Inspired by the recent measurement of the B(E2;$0_{2}^{+} \to 2_{1}^+$) and B(E0;$0_{2}^{+} \to 0_{1}^+$) values in $^{12}$Be, we give an interpretation of available spectroscopic data on both $^{12}$Be and $^{11}$Be, using a simple model which contains the essential feature of these two nuclei; the presence of weakly-bound neutron(s) in deformed potentials. The agreement of the calculated results with observed data is surprisingly good, including well-known strong E1 transitions in both nuclei.

# Properties of 12Be and 11Be in terms of single-particle motion in deformed potential

## Key claim
The observed spectroscopic data on 12Be and 11Be can be understood using a simple Bohr-Mottelson model with weakly-bound neutron(s) in deformed Woods-Saxon potentials, provided the wave functions are computed with correct asymptotic conditions rather than harmonic-oscillator approximations. The agreement is surprisingly good for E1, E2, and E0 transitions, but the authors caution that spectroscopic factors extracted from data may contain ambiguity due to the one-particle radial wave-functions used in analysis.

## Method
The authors apply a deformed Woods-Saxon potential with a Y20 deformation, solving coupled-channel equations in coordinate space with correct asymptotic boundary conditions for r → ∞, without confining the system in a finite box. For 11Be, the seventh neutron occupies either the [220,1/2] or [101,1/2] Nilsson level; for 12Be, the two 0+ states are expressed as equal-amplitude linear combinations of two two-neutron configurations from these same Nilsson levels, with the mixing interpreted as resulting from pairing interaction. The rotational perturbation of intrinsic structure is neglected, and only states close to band heads are studied.

## Key numbers
- B(E1;1/2− → 1/2+) in 11Be: (0.115 ± 0.01) e²fm² (observed), reproduced using potential [b] with diffuseness a=1.00 fm
- Magnetic moment of 11Be ground state: calculated as −1.72 μN using potential [b] with gR=Z/A and gs^eff=0.9 gs^free, compared to observed μ(11Be) = −1.6816(8) μN
- B(E2;0₂⁺ → 2₁⁺) in 12Be: measured (7.0 ± 0.6) e²fm⁴; calculated as 141 (e_eff^n(E2))² = 5.6–12.7 e²fm⁴ using e_eff^n(E2) = (0.2–0.3)e
- |⟨0₂⁺|e r²|0₁⁺⟩| in 12Be: measured (0.87 ± 0.03) e fm²; neutron matrix element calculated as 11.4 fm², yielding e_eff^n(E0)/e = 0.076
- Deformation β for 11Be: β = 0.73 (potential [a], a=0.65 fm) or β = 0.82 (potential [b], a=1.00 fm)
- Deformation β for 12Be: β = 0.66 (potential [a]) or β = 0.72 (potential [b])
- Rotational constant for 11Be: ħ²/2ℑ = 0.403 MeV; for 12Be: ħ²/2ℑ = 0.349 MeV (scaled as A^(5/3))
- Excitation energy of 2₁⁺ in 12Be: calculated 2.09 MeV, observed 2.11 MeV
- Neutron separation energy S(n) in 11Be: 0.504 MeV
- One-particle energies assumed: ε([220,1/2]) = −0.5 MeV and ε([101,1/2]) = −0.2 MeV for 11Be; both levels at −1.1 MeV for 12Be

## Context
This work extends the Bohr-Mottelson collective model (Bohr and Mottelson 1975) to very light neutron-rich nuclei where N=8 is no longer a magic number, following earlier studies of 11Be using deformed Woods-Saxon potentials by Esbensen, Brown, and Sagawa (1995) and by Aumann and Moro (2001). The authors avoid using the observed properties of the 10Be core, unlike those prior works, because adding one neutron may considerably change the nuclear shape in such light nuclei. The results enable future measurements of the E1 transition between the 1− state at 2.70 MeV and the 0₂⁺ state at 2.25 MeV in 12Be, and suggest that measuring charge radii of 12Be relative to 11Be or 10Be would help determine the neutron effective charge for E0 transitions.
