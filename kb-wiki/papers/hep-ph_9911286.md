# Model for Polarized and Unpolarized Parton Density Functions in the Nucleon

> We present a physical model for polarized and unpolarized structure functions and parton density functions (PDFs) of the proton and the neutron. It reproduces the data on F_2^p(x,Q^2) for 0.00001<x<1 and 2.5<Q^2<5000 GeV^2, F_2^p(x)-F_2^n(x), F_2^n(x)/F_2^p(x), xg(x), dbar(x)-ubar(x), d(x)/u(x), the Gottfried sum, the fractional momentum of charged partons and the polarized structure functions g_1^{p,n}(x), at various Q^2. We present for the first time, proton and neutron PDFs which do not assume charge symmetry. Contrary to the common practice, we explain polarized and unpolarized data with a single model.

## Key claim

The authors present a single statistical model that reproduces both polarized and unpolarized parton density functions (PDFs) and structure functions for the proton and neutron, without assuming charge symmetry between the two nucleons. The model's parameters are determined by fitting unpolarized structure function data at only one value of Q², after which it predicts all other observables without further adjustment.

## Method

The model uses a statistical mechanics ansatz where input-scale parton densities are derived from Fermi-Dirac and Bose-Einstein distributions, incorporating finite-size effects through volume, surface, and curvature terms in the density of states. The authors solve seven coupled nonlinear equations for the temperature and six chemical potentials (distinguishing spin-up and spin-down for u, d, and s quarks) under number, spin, and momentum constraints, then evolve the PDFs to other Q² values using next-to-leading order Dokshitzer-Gribov-Lipatov-Altarelli-Parisi equations with Q₀² = M², N_f = 4, and α_s(m_z²) = 0.117.

## Key numbers

- F₂ᵖ data reproduced over 0.00001 < x < 1 and 2.5 < Q² < 5000 GeV²
- Gottfried sum at Q² = 4 GeV² calculated as 0.215 over 0.004 < x < 0.8, compared to experimental value 0.221 ± 0.019(syst) ± 0.008(stat)
- d(x)/u(x) ratio in the x → 1 limit found to be 0.22, in agreement with the QCD prediction of 0.2
- Momentum fraction carried by quarks and antiquarks at Q² = 15 GeV² is 0.58
- Temperature T = 62 MeV for proton, T = 59 MeV for neutron
- Parameters (a,b) = (-0.376, 0.504) for proton and (-0.300, 0.504) for neutron
- Δu = 0.83 ± 0.03, Δd = -0.43 ± 0.03, Δs = -0.10 ± 0.03 for proton; Δu = -0.40 ± 0.04, Δd = 0.86 ± 0.04, Δs = -0.06 ± 0.04 for neutron

## Context

This work extends the statistical model approach of Bhalerao (1996) by introducing separate chemical potentials for spin-up and spin-down partons, enabling the simultaneous description of polarized and unpolarized data that previous versions could not achieve. The model provides the first set of proton and neutron PDFs that do not assume charge symmetry, addressing a topic discussed extensively by Boros, Londergan, and Thomas (1998) and others. The approach enables quantitative predictions for input PDFs and can be systematically extended by incorporating finite charm quark mass, nuclear corrections in the deuteron, and higher-twist effects.
