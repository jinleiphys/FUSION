# Operator evolution for ab initio electric dipole transitions of 4He

> A goal of nuclear theory is to make quantitative predictions of low-energy nuclear observables starting from accurate microscopic internucleon forces. A major element of such an effort is applying unitary transformations to soften the nuclear Hamiltonian and hence accelerate the convergence of ab initio calculations as a function of the model space size. The consistent simultaneous transformation of external operators, however, has been overlooked in applications of the theory, particularly for nonscalar transitions. We study the evolution of the electric dipole operator in the framework of the similarity renormalization group method and apply the renormalized matrix elements to the calculation of the 4He total photoabsorption cross section and electric dipole polarizability. All observables are calculated within the ab initio no-core shell model. We find that, although seemingly small, the effects of evolved operators on the photoabsorption cross section are comparable in magnitude to the correction produced by including the chiral three-nucleon force and cannot be neglected.

## Key claim

The effects of SRG-evolving the electric dipole operator on the \(^4\)He photoabsorption cross section are comparable in magnitude to the correction from including the chiral three-nucleon force, and therefore cannot be neglected, even though the operator-evolution effects appear small.

## Method

The authors performed SRG evolution of the electric dipole operator in the two-nucleon space using a harmonic-oscillator basis, then embedded the evolved two-body matrix elements into the \(^4\)He no-core shell model (NCSM) calculation. The total photoabsorption cross section was obtained via the Lorentz integral transform (LIT) method combined with the Lanczos algorithm, and the electric dipole polarizability was computed using Podolsky’s technique. The Hamiltonian included a chiral N\(^3\)LO nucleon-nucleon force and a N\(^2\)LO three-nucleon force, with induced three-body forces retained up to the three-body level.

## Key numbers

* Total dipole strength \(M_0\) of \(^4\)He: convergence studied as a function of \(N_{\rm max}\) at \(\hbar\Omega = 22, 28, 34, 40\) MeV using the bare operator and evolved wavefunctions from the \(NN+3N\) Hamiltonian with \(\lambda = 2.5\) fm\(^{-1}\).
* Total dipole strength \(M_0\) of \(^4\)He: convergence as a function of \(N_{\rm max}\) at \(\hbar\Omega = 28\) MeV using the bare operator and the 2B evolved \(\hat D\) operator with wavefunctions from the \(NN+3N\) Hamiltonian at \(\lambda = 1.8, 2.2, 2.5, 3.0\) fm\(^{-1}\).
* SRG evolution parameter \(\lambda\): values of 1.5, 1.8, 2.2, 2.5, and 3.0 fm\(^{-1}\) are used; \(\lambda = \infty\) corresponds to the bare (unevolved) operator.
* Model-space truncation: \(N_{\rm A2max} = 300\) for the two-body system, \(N_{\rm A3max} = 40\) for the three-body system.

## Context

This work extends the SRG operator-evolution studies of Schuster et al. (2014), which treated only scalar operators, to the nonscalar electric dipole operator. It builds on prior NCSM calculations of the \(^4\)He photoabsorption cross section by Quaglioni et al. (2007) and electric polarizability by Stetcu et al. (2009), which used Lee-Suzuki effective interactions without operator renormalization. The results enable future investigations of continuum observables with consistently evolved operators, and the authors note that evolution of nonscalar operators in the three-body space remains for future work.
