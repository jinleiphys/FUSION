# Multiple pion production from an oriented chiral condensate

> We consider an ``oriented'' chiral condensate produced in the squeezed states of the effective field theory with time- and space-dependent pion mass parameter. We discuss the general properties of the solution, identifying condensate modes and determining the resulting pion distributions. The implementation of the dynamics in the form of sudden perturbation allows us to look for exact solutions. In the region of condensation, the dramatic increase in pion production and charge fluctuations are demonstrated.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that an "oriented" chiral condensate, described by a quadratic pion field term (rather than a linear current-type term), can produce exact solutions for pion multiplicity distributions when the effective mass parameter undergoes a sudden perturbation; the resulting distributions recover the standard DCC probability formula \( \mathcal{P}(f) = 1/(2\sqrt{f}) \) only in the single-mode limit, while multiple condensate modes yield Gaussian distributions.

## Method

The authors solve the linear sigma model with a time- and space-dependent effective pion mass \( m_{\rm eff}^2(\mathbf{x},t) \) that enters the field equation \( \partial^2 \vec{\pi}/\partial t^2 - \nabla^2 \vec{\pi} + m_{\rm eff}^2(\mathbf{x},t) \vec{\pi} = 0 \). They reduce the problem to independent harmonic oscillators per momentum mode when the mass is spatially uniform, and use Bogoliubov transformations to relate "in" and "out" creation/annihilation operators. For the general case of coupled modes, they derive transition amplitudes between coherent states and compute moments of multiplicity distributions from the matrix \( v v^\dagger \).

## Key numbers

- The average number of particles produced from vacuum in a single mode is \( \overline{n} = \rho/(1-\rho) \), where \( \rho = |v/u|^2 \) and \( \rho \in [0,1) \).
- For a single condensate mode with \( \rho = 0.999 \), the probability distribution for neutral pions is \( P^0(2n) = (2n)!/(2^{2n} (n!)^2) (1-\rho)^{1/2} \rho^n \).
- For charged pions from a single mode, \( P^{\rm charged}(2n) = (1-\rho)\rho^n \).
- For total pions from a single mode, \( P^{\rm tot}(2n) = (2n+1)!/(2^{2n} (n!)^2) (1-\rho)^{3/2} \rho^n \), with maximum at \( n_{\rm max} = (3\rho-2)/(1-\rho) \).
- The second moment of the total particle number is \( \overline{n^2}_{\rm total} = \overline{n}_{\rm total}^2 + 2\overline{n}_{\rm total} + 2\,{\rm Tr}\left((v v^\dagger)^2\right) \).
- The probability for a fraction \( f = n/n_t \) of neutral pions in the single-mode large-\( n \) limit is \( \mathcal{P}(f) = 1/((2n_t+1)\sqrt{f}) \).

## Context

This work extends the DCC literature by replacing the linear current-type coupling (used in coherent state approaches such as Horn (1971) and Camelia (1997)) with a quadratic term that preserves chiral symmetry, following the linear sigma model framework of Koch (1997). The exact solutions for sudden perturbations allow the authors to identify when the standard DCC probability distribution of Anselm (1994) emerges (single-mode limit) versus when Gaussian distributions appear (multiple modes). The results enable future studies of how the number and strength of condensate modes affect observable pion charge ratios in relativistic heavy ion collisions.
