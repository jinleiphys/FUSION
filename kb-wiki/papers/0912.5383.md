# Collective modes and instabilities in anisotropically expanding ultarelativistic plasmas

> The time evolution of collective modes in an expanding ultarelativistic and (effectively) Abelian plasma is studied in the hard-loop approximation semi-analytically by means of integro-differential equations. A previous treatment is generalized to arbitrary orientation of wave vectors with respect to the direction of anisotropy and thus to a fully 3+1 dimensional situation. Moreover, initial fluctuations are allowed in both gauge fields and currents, which is necessary in the case of (stable) longitudinal modes. For unstable (Weibel) modes, this generalization of initial conditions reduces drastically the lower bound on the delay in the onset of growth that was found previously by considering only collective gauge fields as seeds. This makes it appear much more likely that non-Abelian plasma instabilities seeded by small initial rapidity fluctuations could play an important role in the early stage of heavy-ion collisions at LHC energies.

## Key claim

The authors generalize previous semi-analytical treatments of collective modes in expanding ultarelativistic plasmas to fully 3+1 dimensions and to arbitrary initial fluctuations in both gauge fields and currents, finding that this generalization drastically reduces the lower bound on the delay in the onset of growth for Weibel instabilities, making it much more likely that non-Abelian plasma instabilities seeded by small initial rapidity fluctuations could play an important role in the early stage of heavy-ion collisions at LHC energies.

## Method

The time evolution of collective modes in an expanding ultarelativistic and effectively Abelian plasma is studied in the hard-loop approximation by solving integro-differential equations derived from the gauge-covariant Boltzmann-Vlasov equations. The authors generalize previous work by considering arbitrary orientation of wave vectors with respect to the direction of anisotropy (allowing fully 3+1-dimensional dynamics) and by allowing initial fluctuations in both gauge fields and the auxiliary W fields that correspond to colored fluctuations in the hard particle distribution. The resulting memory integrals for the induced current are expressed in terms of Bessel functions after Fourier decomposition in the spatial coordinates and spacetime rapidity.

## Key numbers

- The anisotropy parameter is given by ξ(τ) = (τ/τ_iso)^2 - 1, where τ_iso is the proper time at which the distribution is locally isotropic
- The Debye mass squared m_D^2 is defined as m_D^2 = -g^2 t_R ∫ dp p^2/(2π)^2 f'_iso(p), equaling the Debye mass at time τ_iso
- For the case k=0 (effectively 1+1-dimensional), the functional dependences in j^1[A^1] and j^2[A^2] become the same
- The angle between the wave vector and the anisotropy direction is given by ϑ = arctan(τk/ν), which is time-dependent when both k≠0 and ν≠0

## Context

This work extends the semi-analytical treatment of Romatschke and Rebhan (2006) from effectively 1+1-dimensional dynamics to fully 3+1-dimensional situations with arbitrary wave vector orientations. It also generalizes the initial conditions beyond the collective gauge field seeds considered in Romatschke and Rebhan (2006) and Rebhan and Steineder (2008). The results enable future fully 3+1-dimensional real-time lattice studies of non-Abelian plasma instabilities in expanding plasmas, and suggest that at LHC energies the situation is much more favorable for plasma instabilities to play an important role in early thermalization than previously thought based on the work of Romatschke and Rebhan (2006) and Rebhan and Steineder (2008).
