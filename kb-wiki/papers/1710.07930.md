# Pure spin-3/2 propagator for use in particle and nuclear physics

> We propose the use of pure spin-3/2 propagator in the $(3/2,0) \oplus (0,3/2)$ representation in particle and nuclear physics. To formulate the propagator in a covariant form we use the antisymmetric tensor spinor representation and we consider the $Δ$ resonance contribution to the elastic $πN$ scattering as an example. We find that the use of conventional gauge invariant interaction Lagrangian leads to a problem; the obtained scattering amplitude does not exhibit the resonance behavior. To overcome this problem we modify the interaction by adding a momentum dependence. As in the case of Rarita-Schwinger we find that a perfect resonance description could be obtained in the pure spin-3/2 formulation only if hadronic form factors were considered in the interactions.

## Key claim

The pure spin-3/2 propagator in the $(3/2,0) \oplus (0,3/2)$ representation, formulated using the antisymmetric tensor spinor representation, can describe resonance behavior in elastic $\pi N$ scattering only if the interaction Lagrangian is modified to include momentum dependence and if hadronic form factors are included in the interactions.

## Method

The authors constructed a pure spin-3/2 propagator using the antisymmetric tensor spinor representation, which embeds an 8-dimensional spinor into a totally antisymmetric tensor of second rank, and projected out the $(3/2,0) \oplus (0,3/2)$ representation using a Casimir operator projection. They applied this propagator to the $\Delta(1232)$ resonance contribution in elastic $\pi N$ scattering, comparing the resulting total cross section with that obtained from the conventional Rarita-Schwinger propagator with gauge-invariant interaction. The interaction Lagrangian was modified by replacing gamma matrices with partial derivatives to avoid vanishing contraction with the pure spin-3/2 field tensor.

## Key numbers

- The pure spin-3/2 field in the antisymmetric tensor spinor representation has 8 degrees of freedom, obtained from $24-16=8$ after applying the constraint $\gamma_\mu w^{\mu \nu}=0$.
- The resonance peak from the pure spin-3/2 model appears centered around $W\approx 1.25$ GeV, shifted from the original $\Delta(1232)$ position at 1.232 GeV.
- A dipole hadronic form factor with cutoff $\Lambda=0.5$ GeV was used to suppress background contributions and produce a perfect resonance structure.
- The pure spin-3/2 propagator numerator contains a term proportional to $(q^2 - m_\Delta^2)/2m_\Delta^4$ that vanishes at the resonance pole $q^2 = m_\Delta^2$, leaving only the spin-3/2 projection operator contribution.

## Context

This work extends the pure spin-3/2 representation formalism of Delgado Acosta, Banda Guzmán, and Kirchbach (2015) by constructing a covariant propagator and applying it to a physical scattering process. It addresses the long-standing problem of unphysical spin-1/2 degrees of freedom in the Rarita-Schwinger propagator, which has been known since Rarita and Schwinger (1941) and discussed by Williams (1985), Benmerrouche, Davidson, and Mukhopadhyay (1989), and Pascalutsa and Timmermans (1999). The results enable the use of a spin-3/2 propagator free from lower-spin background in isobar and coupled-channels models for meson-nucleon scattering, though the authors note that consistent gauge-invariant interactions for the pure spin-3/2 field remain an open question for future work.
