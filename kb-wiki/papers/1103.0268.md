# Spin ordered phase transitions in neutron matter under the presence of a strong magnetic field

> In dense neutron matter under the presence of a strong magnetic field, considered in the model with the Skyrme effective interaction, there are possible two types of spin ordered states. In one of them the majority of neutron spins are aligned opposite to magnetic field (thermodynamically preferable state), and in other one the majority of spins are aligned along the field (metastable state). The equation of state, incompressibility modulus and velocity of sound are determined in each case with the aim to find the peculiarities allowing to distinguish between two spin ordered phases.

## Key claim

In dense neutron matter under a strong magnetic field, modeled with Skyrme effective interactions, two spin-ordered states are possible: a thermodynamically stable state with the majority of neutron spins aligned opposite to the magnetic field (negative spin polarization) and a metastable state with spins aligned along the field (positive spin polarization). The metastable state is only mechanically stable (positive incompressibility modulus) above a critical density, which for the SLy7 force at \(H=10^{18}\) G is \(\varrho_c \approx 4.85\varrho_0\), despite the self-consistent equations having solutions at lower threshold densities.

## Method

The authors used a Fermi-liquid approach with Skyrme effective interactions (SLy4 and SLy7 parameter sets) to solve self-consistent equations for the single-particle energy components and distribution functions of neutrons at zero temperature. They computed the equation of state, incompressibility modulus, and sound velocity for both the stable (negative spin polarization) and metastable (positive spin polarization) branches of solutions, imposing the mechanical stability condition \(K>0\) and the causality condition \(v_s < c\). The magnetic field contribution to the total pressure was omitted as it remains small compared to matter pressure in the high-density region of interest.

## Key numbers

- Threshold density for the metastable branch \(\Pi_3\) at \(H=10^{18}\) G: \(\varrho_{th} \approx 3.92\varrho_0\) (where solutions first appear).
- Critical density for mechanical stability of the metastable branch \(\Pi_3\) at \(H=10^{18}\) G: \(\varrho_c \approx 4.85\varrho_0\) (where incompressibility modulus becomes positive).
- Transition density for the ferromagnetic phase transition in the stable branch \(\Pi_1\) at \(H=10^{18}\) G: \(\varrho_{tr} \approx 3.16\varrho_0\) (where spin polarization begins to increase rapidly).
- Magnetic field strengths considered: up to \(H_{max} \sim 10^{18}\) G, with the impact on the EoS remaining small up to about \(10^{17}\) G.

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Isayev and Yang (2009), which identified the metastable branch of positive spin polarization in neutron matter with Skyrme forces, by calculating the EoS, incompressibility modulus, and sound velocity for both spin-ordered states. It contrasts with studies using realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions (e.g., by Pethick, Gnedin, and collaborators) which found no ferromagnetic transition at relevant densities, and with earlier Skyrme-force studies (e.g., by Rikovska Stone and collaborators) that did not identify the metastable branch. The results enable distinguishing between the two spin-ordered phases in magnetar interiors and provide constraints for the density range where the metastable state could exist, with the next step being the inclusion of finite temperature effects.
