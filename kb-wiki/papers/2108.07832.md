# Nonuniqueness of scattering amplitudes at special points

> We point out little discussed phenomenon in elementary quantum mechanics. In one-dimensional potential scattering problems, the scattering amplitudes are not uniquely determined at special points in parameter space. We examine a few explicit examples. We also discuss the relation with the pole-skipping phenomena recently found in holographic duality. In the holographic pole-skipping, the retarded Green's functions are not uniquely determined at imaginary Matsubara frequencies. It turns out that this universality comes from the fact that the corresponding potential scattering problem has the angular momentum potential.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in one-dimensional potential scattering problems, the S-matrix is not uniquely determined at special points in parameter space because it takes the form 0/0, and this nonuniqueness depends on the slope with which one approaches the special point; the authors argue this phenomenon is universal and underlies the holographic pole-skipping found in strongly-coupled quantum field theories.

## Method

The authors analyze the Schrödinger equation for one-dimensional potential scattering with a regular singularity at x=0, using power-series expansions around x=0 and x=∞ to identify points where the recursion relation breaks down and the wave function becomes non-unique. They then examine three analytically solvable examples: the Coulomb potential, the Pöschl-Teller potential with angular momentum term, and a potential regular at x=0 but with a regular singularity at x=∞, computing the Jost functions and S-matrices to locate pole-skipping points where poles and zeros coincide.

## Key numbers

* For potentials with angular momentum ν = l + 1/2, pole-skipping occurs at ν = -n/2 for n = 1, 2, ... (e.g., ν = -1/2, -1, -3/2, ...)
* For the Coulomb potential, the first pole-skipping point is at ν = -1/2 with κ = 0, and the second at ν = -1 with κ = ±i/2
* For the Pöschl-Teller potential, pole-skipping occurs at ν = -1 with 2k = i, and at ν = -2 with 2k = i, 3i
* For potentials with exponential decay at infinity, nonuniqueness occurs at ik = n/2 for n = 1, 2, ...
* In holographic duality, pole-skipping points are located at ω = -(2πT)ni for n = 1, 2, ... where T is temperature

## Context

This work extends the little-discussed phenomenon of S-matrix nonuniqueness briefly mentioned in Newton (1960) and connects it to the holographic pole-skipping phenomenon recently found by Grozdanov et al. (2019), Blake et al. (2019), and Natsuume and Okamura (2019). The authors show that the holographic pole-skipping problem can be mapped to a quantum mechanical problem with angular momentum, explaining the universality of pole-skipping points at Matsubara frequencies. This enables a deeper understanding of why retarded Green's functions become non-unique at special points in complex momentum space in strongly-coupled quantum field theories.
