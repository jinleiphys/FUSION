# Studying the Landau mass parameter of the extended $σ$-$ω$ model for neutron star matter

> We present a Bayesian analysis of the Landau mass within the extended $σ$-$ω$ model for neutron star matter. To this purpose, we consider the mass measurement of the object PSR 0740+6620, the tidal deformability estimation from the GW170817 and the mass-radius estimate of PSR J0030+0451 by NICER. Using Landau mass as free parameter of the theory, we rely on the prediction power of the Bayesian method to find the best value for this nuclear quantity.

## Key claim
The paper uses a Bayesian analysis to determine the best value of the Landau mass parameter in the extended sigma-omega model for neutron star matter, finding that the posterior probabilities peak at approximately 750 ± 15 MeV when combining constraints from three astrophysical observations. The abstract glosses over the fact that the result is conditional on the specific choice of observational constraints and that the choice between two different NICER analyses of PSR J0030+0451 does not significantly affect the outcome.

## Method
The authors construct an equation of state for neutron star matter using the extended sigma-omega model in the mean field approximation at zero temperature, with the Landau mass as the single free parameter. They compute mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities for sequences of compact stars by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations and its perturbation equations. A Bayesian inference is then performed using three observational constraints: the mass measurement of PSR 0740+6620, the tidal deformability estimation from GW170817, and the mass-radius estimate of PSR J0030+0451 from NICER.

## Key numbers
- Landau mass posterior probability peaks at m_L = 750 ± 15 MeV from the full Bayesian analysis combining all three measurements
- Nuclear saturation data used to fit the model: binding energy B = -16.3 MeV, saturation density n_0 = 0.156 fm^-3, nucleon effective mass m* = 0.6 m_N, compressibility K = 240 MeV, asymmetry energy a_sym = 32.5 MeV
- The most massive pulsar PSR 0740+6620 has a mass centered at 2.14 solar masses (green horizontal band in Figure 1)
- The Landau mass reference value used in the model fitting is m_L = 0.83 m_N

## Context
This work extends previous Bayesian analyses of neutron star properties (Alvarez-Castillo 2016, Raithel 2017, Ayriyan 2018, Miller 2019, Raaijmakers 2019) by focusing specifically on the Landau mass parameter and using the most recent observational constraints. The authors note that earlier works often used constraints with higher uncertainties, such as X-ray burst analyses from compact star surfaces (Steiner 2010). The results enable future studies incorporating additional constraints like the GW190425 merger event and allow for extension to multi-parameter variations of the model to better satisfy both laboratory empirical values and compact star observations.
