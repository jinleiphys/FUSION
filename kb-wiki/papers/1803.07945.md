# Mean free path and shear viscosity in central $^{129}$Xe+$^{119}$Sn collisions below 100 MeV/nucleon

> Thermal and transport properties of hot nuclear matter formed in central $^{129}$Xe + $^{119}$Sn collisions at the Fermi energy are investigated using the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamical (IQMD) model. Temperature ($T$), average density ($ρ$), chemical potential ($μ$), mean momentum ($P$), shear viscosity ($η$) and entropy density ($s$) are obtained from the phase-space information. The mean free path ($λ_{nn}$) and the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section ($σ_{nn}$) in the highest compressible stage at different incident energies are deduced and compared with the experimental results from Phys. Rev. C $\bf{90}$ (2014) 064602. The result shows that $λ_{nn}$ and $σ_{nn}$ have the same trend and similar values as the experimental results when the beam energy is greater than 40 MeV/u at maximum compressed state. Furthermore, the derived shear viscosity over entropy density ($η/s$) shows a decreasing behaviour to a saturated value around $\frac{3}{4π}$ as a function of incident energy.

## Key claim

The IQMD model reproduces the experimental mean free path and in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section from Lopez et al. (2014) for central Xe+Sn collisions only when the beam energy exceeds 40 MeV/u at the maximum compressed state; below that energy the agreement is not claimed.

## Method

Central collisions of 129Xe + 119Sn at beam energies from 15 to 100 MeV/u were simulated with the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model using a soft momentum-dependent interaction. Temperature was extracted from momentum fluctuations of nucleons using a Fermi-Dirac distribution, and shear viscosity was obtained from a parametrization as a function of density and temperature. The mean free path and in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section were then derived from kinetic theory relations using the calculated shear viscosity, mean momentum, and density.

## Key numbers

- Maximum average density reaches 1.3 times the saturation density (rho_0 = 0.168 fm^-3) at 100 MeV/u beam energy.
- Mean free path at maximum compression: approximately 6 fm at 100 MeV/u, consistent with the experimental value of 4.5 +/- 1 fm from Lopez et al. (2014).
- In-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section is less than 10 mb for beam energies below 100 MeV/u at the central region during compression.
- Shear viscosity over entropy density (eta/s) at 80-100 MeV/u reaches values around 2-4 in units of 1/(4 pi), saturating near 3/(4 pi).
- The in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section from the IQMD output and from the formula both lie in a range of a few mb.

## Context

This work extends the experimental study of nuclear stopping and mean free path by the INDRA Collaboration (Lopez et al., 2014) for the same 129Xe + 119Sn system, using the IQMD model to extract thermodynamic and transport properties that are difficult to measure directly. The derived shear viscosity over entropy density shows a decreasing trend with beam energy that is consistent with previous IQMD and BUU model calculations (Deng et al., 2016; Zhang et al., 2013; Zhang et al., 2014) and with giant dipole resonance data (Gao et al., 2017), suggesting the hot nucleonic matter behaves as a near-perfect fluid at higher energies. The asymptotic eta/s value around 3/(4 pi) is comparable to the KSS bound for quark-gluon plasma, despite the different microscopic scale.
