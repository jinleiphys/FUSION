# Generation of strong magnetic fields in dense quark matter driven by the electroweak interaction of quarks

> We study the generation of strong large scale magnetic fields in dense quark matter. The magnetic field growth is owing to the magnetic field instability driven by the electroweak interaction of quarks. We discuss the situation when the chiral symmetry is unbroken in the degenerate quark matter. In this case we predict the amplification of the seed magnetic field $10^{12}\,\text{G}$ to the strengths $(10^{14}-10^{15})\,\text{G}$. In our analysis we use the typical parameters of the quark matter in the core of a hybrid star or in a quark star. We also discuss the application of the obtained results to describe the magnetic fields generation in magnetars.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that seed magnetic fields of $10^{12}\,\text{G}$ in dense quark matter can be amplified to strengths of $(10^{14}-10^{15})\,\text{G}$ through a magnetic field instability driven by the electroweak interaction of quarks, but only under the condition that chiral symmetry remains unbroken in the degenerate quark matter, which is expected in hybrid stars or quark stars rather than in ordinary neutron stars.

## Method

The author derived a system of kinetic equations for the spectra of magnetic helicity density and magnetic energy density, coupled with equations for the chiral imbalances of $u$ and $d$ quarks, based on the induced anomalous electric current proportional to the sum of chiral chemical potentials and electroweak interaction potentials. The equations were solved numerically using initial conditions corresponding to a compact star with a Kolmogorov spectrum for the magnetic energy density, seed field $B_0=10^{12}\,\text{G}$, and initial temperatures $T_0=10^8\,\text{K}$ or $10^9\,\text{K}$, with the electric conductivity of degenerate quark matter taken from Heiselberg and Pethick (1993) and helicity flip rates computed from quark scattering via plasmon exchange.

## Key numbers

- Seed magnetic field amplified from $10^{12}\,\text{G}$ to saturation strength $B_{\text{sat}} \approx 1.1\times10^{14}\,\text{G}$ for initial temperature $T_0=10^8\,\text{K}$
- Seed magnetic field amplified to $B_{\text{sat}} \approx 1.1\times10^{15}\,\text{G}$ for initial temperature $T_0=10^9\,\text{K}$
- Electric conductivity of quark matter: $\sigma_{\text{cond}} = 4.64\times10^{20}\,(\alpha_s T/T_0)^{-5/3}(\mu_0/300\,\text{MeV})^{8/3}\,\text{s}^{-1}$, with $\sigma_0 = 3.15\times10^{22}\,\text{s}^{-1}$ at reference temperature
- Helicity flip rates: $\Gamma_u = 1.59\times10^{14}\,\text{s}^{-1}$ and $\Gamma_d = 3.13\times10^{12}\,\text{s}^{-1}$
- Mean chemical potentials: $\mu_u = 0.69\mu_0$ and $\mu_d = 0.87\mu_0$, where $\mu_0 = 346\,\text{MeV}$
- Electroweak potentials: $V_{5u} = 4.5\,\text{eV}$ and $V_{5d} = 2.9\,\text{eV}$ for number density $n_0 = 1.8\times10^{38}\,\text{cm}^{-3}$
- Saturation time $t_{\text{sat}} \lesssim 10\,\text{hours}$ for $T_0=10^8\,\text{K}$ and $\lesssim 10^2\,\text{minutes}$ for $T_0=10^9\,\text{K}$

## Context

This work extends the magnetic field generation mechanism previously applied to neutron stars by Dvornikov and Semikoz (2015a, 2015b, 2015c) and Dvornikov (2016), which relied on the electroweak interaction of electrons with nucleons, to the case of dense quark matter where chiral symmetry is restored. The key improvement is that the earlier electron-based mechanism is problematic because electrons have nonzero mass, which destroys the chiral magnetic effect as shown by Vilenkin (1980) and confirmed by Dvornikov (2016b); in contrast, lattice simulations by Brandt (2016) indicate chiral symmetry restoration in high-density quark matter, making the quark-based scenario more realistic. The results enable a possible explanation for magnetar magnetic fields through rapid magnetic cooling of hybrid stars or quark stars, with saturation times much shorter than those in the electron-nucleon case.
