# Heat capacity and pairing transition in nuclei

> A simple model based on the canonical-ensemble theory is outlined for hot nuclei. The properties of the model are discussed with respect to the Fermi gas model and the breaking of Cooper pairs. The model describes well the experimental level density of deformed nuclei in various mass regions. The origin of the so-called S-shape of the heat capacity curve Cv(T) is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a simple canonical-ensemble model with equidistant single-particle levels and a pairing gap parameter can reproduce the experimental level densities and the S-shaped heat capacity of deformed nuclei, but the model's critical temperature for the pairing transition in the iron region (T_c ~ 1.2 MeV) is significantly higher than the value of ~0.7 MeV obtained from Shell Model Monte Carlo simulations, indicating that further theoretical and experimental work is needed.

## Method

The authors construct a canonical-ensemble partition function as a product of particle, rotational, and vibrational contributions, where particle excitations are described by fermions occupying an infinite set of equally spaced single-particle levels with spacing epsilon, and Cooper pair breaking is governed by an empirical pairing gap Delta = 12 A^{-1/2} MeV. Thermodynamic quantities (free energy, entropy, average excitation energy, heat capacity) are derived from the partition function, and the level density is obtained via the saddle-point approximation for comparison with experimental anchor points from discrete level counting and neutron-resonance spacing data.

## Key numbers

- Pairing gap parameter: Delta = 12 A^{-1/2} MeV (empirical formula from Bohr and Mottelson)
- For 162Dy: Delta = 0.94 MeV, level spacing epsilon = 0.13 MeV, rotational parameter A_rig = 7.6 keV, vibrational energy hbar omega_vib = 0.9 MeV
- For 58Fe: Delta = 1.58 MeV, epsilon = 0.80 MeV, A_rig = 42.0 keV, hbar omega_vib = 2.0 MeV
- For 106Pd: Delta = 1.17 MeV, epsilon = 0.21 MeV, A_rig = 15.4 keV, hbar omega_vib = 1.4 MeV
- For 234U: Delta = 0.78 MeV, epsilon = 0.08 MeV, A_rig = 4.1 keV, hbar omega_vib = 0.8 MeV
- Critical temperature for pairing transition in 161,162Dy: T_c ~ 0.5 MeV (from the local maximum of the S-shaped heat capacity)
- Critical temperature for 58,59Fe from the model: T_c ~ 1.2 MeV (compared to ~0.7 MeV from SMMC simulations)
- Level density parameter for 162Dy: a = 25 MeV^{-1} (derived from epsilon = 0.13 MeV)
- As T approaches infinity, C_V approaches 20, 21, and 22 for even-even, odd, and odd-odd systems respectively (equal to the total number of nucleons in the system)

## Context

This work extends the experimental findings of Schiller et al. (2001) and Melby et al. (2001), who reported the S-shaped heat capacity and critical temperature for pairing transitions in 161,162Dy and 171,172Yb isotopes, and the 166,167Er nuclei. The model provides a simple theoretical framework that reproduces these features and the level densities of deformed nuclei across mass regions A ~ 58, 106, 162, and 234. The results enable comparison with more sophisticated approaches such as finite-temperature Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations by Egido et al. (2000), relativistic Hartree-Fock-BCS calculations by Agrawal et al. (2000), and Shell Model Monte Carlo simulations by Rombouts et al. (1998) and Liu and Alhassid (2000), revealing a discrepancy in the iron region that requires further investigation.
