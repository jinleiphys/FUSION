# Nucleus-nucleus potential from identical-particle interference

> Based on the quantum interference between two-identical-nucleus scattering at energies around the Coulomb barrier, the barrier positions for $^{58}$Ni+$^{58}$Ni and $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O are extracted from Mott oscillations in the angular distributions around 90$^{\circ}$ for the first time. The angle separation of pairs of Mott scattering valleys around 90$^{\circ}$ has a direct relationship with the closest distance between two nuclei in elastic scattering. Together with the barrier height from fusion excitation function, the extracted barrier position provides a sensitive probe to constrain the model predictions for the nucleus-nucleus potential barrier.

# Nucleus-nucleus potential from identical-particle interference

## Key claim
The barrier positions for $^{58}$Ni+$^{58}$Ni and $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O can be extracted from the angular separation of Mott oscillations around 90° in elastic scattering angular distributions, using a modified analytical formula that relates the closest distance between two nuclei to the oscillation separation. This extraction is model-independent but requires the barrier height to be known beforehand from fusion excitation functions.

## Method
The authors analyzed measured elastic and quasi-elastic scattering angular distributions for $^{58}$Ni+$^{58}$Ni and $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O at energies around the Coulomb barrier. They extracted the angular separation $\Delta \theta$ of pairs of Mott scattering valleys around 90° using Gaussian fits, then applied a modified expression $d \approx 2\lambda / [\sin(\Delta \theta) + \tan(\Delta \theta)]$ to obtain the closest distance of approach, which corresponds to the barrier position. The barrier height was taken from previously measured fusion excitation functions.

## Key numbers
- For $^{58}$Ni+$^{58}$Ni at $E_{c.m.}=80$ MeV (sub-barrier): $\Delta \theta = 2.42^\circ \pm 0.03^\circ$, closest distance $d = 14.12 \pm 0.17$ fm, consistent with the expected value of 14.11 fm from pure Coulomb scattering.
- For $^{58}$Ni+$^{58}$Ni at $E_{c.m.}=100$ MeV (around the barrier): $\Delta \theta = 2.91^\circ \pm 0.03^\circ$, closest distance $d = 10.49 \pm 0.11$ fm.
- For $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O at $E_{c.m.}=10.91$ MeV: $\Delta \theta = 21.04^\circ \pm 0.80^\circ$, closest distance $d = 8.63 \pm 0.32$ fm.
- The extracted mean barrier height for $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O from fusion excitation functions is $10.1 \pm 0.5$ MeV.
- For $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O, the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory predicts $V_B = 10.12$ MeV and $R_B = 8.52$ fm, in good agreement with the extracted values.

## Context
This work extends the use of quantum interference effects in nuclear scattering, specifically Mott oscillations, to directly extract the Coulomb barrier position, complementing earlier methods that extracted barrier heights from fusion cross sections (e.g., Birkelund 1978) or back-angle quasi-elastic scattering (e.g., Timmers 1997). The conventional method for extracting barrier position from fusion cross sections using the classical formula $\sigma_{\rm fus} = \pi R_B^2 (1 - V_B/E_{c.m.})$ is model-dependent and yields large uncertainties (e.g., $R_B = 8.7 \pm 0.5$ fm versus $9.8 \pm 0.6$ fm for $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O depending on the data set used). The proposed method reduces this uncertainty and provides a sensitive probe to constrain empirical potentials such as the Bass (1980), Broglia-Winther (1991), and extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) potentials, as well as TDHF predictions. This enables more precise tests of theoretical nucleus-nucleus potentials and may be extended to other identical-nucleus systems.
