# General formulation of transverse hydrodynamics

> General formulation of hydrodynamics describing transversally thermalized matter created at the early stages of ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions is presented. Similarities and differences with the standard three-dimensionally thermalized relativistic hydrodynamics are discussed. The role of the conservation laws as well as the thermodynamic consistency of two-dimensional thermodynamic variables characterizing transversally thermalized matter is emphasized.

# General formulation of transverse hydrodynamics

## Key claim
The paper presents a general, thermodynamically consistent formulation of transverse hydrodynamics for matter that is thermalized only in the transverse plane, with longitudinal free-streaming imposed by the condition that particle rapidity equals spacetime rapidity (y = η). This formalism extends earlier work by Bialas et al. (2007) and Chojnacki et al. (2007) by removing the restrictions to massless particles and classical Boltzmann statistics, and by deriving the hydrodynamic equations directly from the energy-momentum tensor using thermodynamic identities alone.

## Method
The authors construct an energy-momentum tensor for transversally thermalized matter that includes a term proportional to V^μ V^ν, which enforces zero longitudinal pressure. They derive the hydrodynamic equations (entropy conservation and Euler-type equations) from the conservation laws ∂_μ T^{μν} = 0 using only thermodynamic identities, without assuming a specific equation of state. The underlying phase-space distribution function is factorized into a longitudinal part containing a delta function δ(p·V) that enforces y = η and a transverse part g(p·U) describing local thermal equilibrium in the transverse plane. The formalism allows for massive particles, bosons or fermions, and a non-constant cluster density n_0(η) that breaks boost invariance.

## Key numbers
- For massless fermions: n₂ = ν_g π T² / 24, ε₂ = 3 ν_g ζ(3) T³ / (4π)
- For massless bosons: n₂ = ν_g π T² / 12, ε₂ = ν_g ζ(3) T³ / π
- For both massless fermions and bosons: P₂ = (1/2) ε₂, c_s² = 1/2
- For classical (Boltzmann) statistics with finite mass m: n₂ = (ν_g T / (2π)) (m + T) e^{-m/T}, P₂ = (ν_g T² / (2π)) (m + T) e^{-m/T}
- In the classical limit: lim_{m→0} c_s² = 1/2, lim_{T→∞} c_s² = 1/2
- For gluon-dominated systems: ν_g = 16

## Context
This work extends the concept of purely transversally thermalized matter originally formulated by Heinz and Wong (2002) and later implemented by Bialas et al. (2007) and Chojnacki et al. (2007), who showed consistency with RHIC data on pion transverse-momentum spectra and elliptic flow v₂. The formalism provides a thermodynamically consistent framework that can be matched to standard 3D hydrodynamics via Landau matching conditions, similar to the approach used by Broniowski et al. (2008) for 3D free-streaming followed by boost-invariant hydrodynamics. The possible 2D to 3D transition has been addressed in dissipative hydrodynamics by Bozek (2007). This work enables systematic treatment of the early stages of ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where gluon-dominated matter may be only transversally thermalized.
