# Euclidean resonance and a new type of nuclear reactions

> The extremely small probability of quantum tunneling through an almost classical potential barrier may become not small under the action of the specially adapted nonstationary field. The tunneling rate has a sharp peak as a function of the particle energy when it is close to the certain resonant value defined by the nonstationary field (Euclidean resonance). Alpha decay of nuclei has a small probability since the alpha particle should tunnel through a very nontransparent Coulomb barrier. The incident proton, due to the Coulomb interaction with the tunneling alpha particle, plays the role of a nonstationary field which may result in Euclidean resonance in tunneling of the alpha particle. At the resonant proton energy, which is of the order of 0.2 Mev, the alpha particle escapes the nucleus and goes to infinity with no influence of the nuclear Coulomb barrier. The process is inelastic since the alpha particle releases energy and the proton gains it. This stimulation of alpha decay by a proton constitutes a new type of nuclear reactions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the extremely small probability of alpha decay through a Coulomb barrier can become not small when an incident proton of a specific resonant energy (approximately 0.2 MeV) interacts with the tunneling alpha particle via Coulomb forces, causing the alpha particle to escape with no influence of the nuclear Coulomb barrier. The hedge is that this Euclidean resonance effect requires the nonstationary field (the proton) to be not small and very precisely tuned, and the formal applicability of the relations requires the tunneling probability to remain much less than 1, though the peak can grow dramatically from, for example, 10⁻³⁷ to 10⁻².

## Method

The author applies a semiclassical (WKB) approach generalized to nonstationary conditions, using classical trajectories in imaginary time (Euclidean action) to describe tunneling through a potential barrier under the influence of a time-dependent field. For the nuclear case, the incident proton's Coulomb interaction with the tunneling alpha particle is treated as the nonstationary field, and the condition for Euclidean resonance is derived from the extremization of the classical action with respect to energy transfer, leading to a sharp peak in tunneling probability at a specific resonant proton energy. The analysis uses the specific example of uranium-235 alpha decay and a proton incident on the nucleus, with the Coulomb barrier and centrifugal terms included.

## Key numbers

* The resonant proton energy is of the order of 0.2 MeV.
* For uranium-235 alpha decay (²³⁵U → ²³¹Th + α), the alpha particle energy is 4.678 MeV.
* The nuclear radius parameter x₀ is approximately 0.92 × 10⁻¹² cm, calculated as 1.2[(231)^(1/3) + 4^(1/3)] × 10⁻¹³ cm.
* The characteristic nuclear time is 10⁻²¹ s, and a proton of energy 1 MeV sweeps the tunneling region in approximately 10⁻²¹ s.
* The WKB tunneling rate for alpha decay is given by exp[-A_M(E, L_M)], where the action A_M is expressed in terms of the Coulomb constant α_M = 2(z₀ − 2)e², with z₀ = 92.
* The imaginary time of motion under the barrier for alpha decay is τ_M(E) ≈ (πα_M)/(2ℏE) √(M/(2E)), and for the proton it is τ_m(ε) ≈ (πα_m)/(2ℏε) √(m/(2ε)), where α_m = z₀e².

## Context

This work extends the concept of Euclidean resonance, previously introduced by the same author (Ivlev, 2002) for tunneling in nonstationary fields, to nuclear reactions involving alpha decay. It contrasts with conventional photon-assisted tunneling, where absorption of quanta enhances tunneling, by showing that emission processes can also enhance tunneling non-perturbatively. The proposed mechanism enables a new type of nuclear reaction where alpha decay is stimulated by an incident proton at a specific resonant energy, relying solely on Coulomb effects rather than strong interaction or compound nucleus formation, which differs from typical nuclear resonances described in standard texts (Weisskopf, Bohr).
