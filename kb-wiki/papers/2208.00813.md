# The role of initial state radiation in quenched jets

> Jet quenching in heavy ion collisions and in particular the sub-structure of quenched jets are promising tools for investigating the microscopic processes underlying jet quenching and the background medium's response to energy and momentum depositions. A quantitative understanding of the data can, however, be complicated by the presence of initial state radiation in reconstructed jets. Using an extended version of JEWEL the effect of initial state radiation on different jet observables is studied in proton-proton and heavy ion collisions. It is shown that, depending on the observable and the jet radius, the initial state contributions can be sizable. Some general insights into when sizable effects can be expected also emerges.

## Key claim
Initial state radiation (ISR) can produce sizable contributions to reconstructed jets in both proton-proton and heavy ion collisions, but the effect is observable-dependent and generally increases with jet radius and rapidity; the paper shows that ISR can mimic signatures of medium response and deflected jets, requiring careful inclusion in theoretical modeling for precision comparisons.

## Method
The study uses an extended version of the JEWEL event generator (version 2.4.0) where initial state radiation undergoes the same medium-modified evolution as final state radiation, unlike previous versions. Di-jet samples at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV were generated for central Pb+Pb collisions (0-10% centrality) using EPPS16NLO nuclear PDFs and for p+p collisions using CT14NLO PDFs, with jets reconstructed using the anti-k⊥ algorithm at parton level via Rivet and FastJet.

## Key numbers
- Jets with p⊥(jet) > 100 GeV and |η(jet)| < 3 are considered; sub-leading jets in di-jet selection require p⊥(jet) > 60 GeV
- Average ISR contribution to jet p⊥ is at the level of a few percent, reaching 10% at |y(jet)| ≈ 2.5
- In p+p collisions, ISR contribution to the jet profile exceeds 10% for R=1 jets at large r; in Pb+Pb collisions it reaches up to 25%
- Fraction of SoftDrop tagged jets with at least one IS sub-jet is in the range of up to 10%
- ISR contributions to the √d12-distribution and di-jet acoplanarity can exceed 40%
- Reducing rapidity coverage to |η(jet)| < 2 reduces the jet cross section by 10%

## Context
This work extends previous JEWEL studies (Zapp 2012, Zapp 2013) by enabling ISR to interact in the medium, and addresses concerns raised in experimental measurements of large-radius jets in heavy ion collisions (CMS 2021, ATLAS 2019). The results show that ISR can fake signatures of medium response and Molière scattering (D'Eramo 2018), and that parton shower-based jet quenching models (Armesto 2008, Schenke 2009, Casalderrey-Solana 2014) typically include ISR automatically while analytical approaches require manual addition. The study enables better interpretation of jet substructure observables and highlights the need for matrix element corrections in heavy ion collisions.
