# Shell Effects in Nuclei with Vector Self-Coupling of Omega Meson in Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov Theory

> Shell effects in nuclei about the stability line are investigated within the framework of the Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) theory with self-consistent finite-range pairing. Using 2-neutron separation energies of Ni and Sn isotopes, the role of $σ$- and $ω$-meson couplings on the shell effects in nuclei is examined. It is observed that the existing successful nuclear forces (Lagrangian parameter sets) based upon the nonlinear scalar coupling of $σ$-meson exhibit shell effects which are stronger than suggested by the experimental data. We have introduced nonlinear vector self-coupling of $ω$-meson in the RHB theory. It is shown that the inclusion of the vector self-coupling of $ω$-meson in addition to the nonlinear scalar coupling of $σ$-meson provides a good agreement with the experimental data on shell effects in nuclei about the stability line. A comparison of the shell effects in the RHB theory is made with the Hartree-Fock Bogoliubov approach using the Skyrme force SkP. It is shown that the oft-discussed shell quenching with SkP is not consistent with the available experimental data.

# Shell Effects in Nuclei with Vector Self-Coupling of Omega Meson in Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov Theory

## Key claim

Existing successful nuclear forces based on nonlinear scalar coupling of the sigma meson produce shell effects that are stronger than experimental data indicate; including nonlinear vector self-coupling of the omega meson in addition to the scalar coupling brings the calculated shell effects into good agreement with experimental data for nuclei near the stability line.

## Method

The authors performed Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations with self-consistent finite-range Gogny pairing (parameter set D1S) for spherical Ni and Sn isotopes, using an oscillator basis with 20 shells. They compared results from forces with only nonlinear scalar sigma coupling (NL-SH, NL3) against forces with both scalar and vector self-coupling (NL-SV1, NL-SV2, TM1), and also compared RHB results with RMF+BCS calculations using experimental pairing gaps and with Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations using the Skyrme force SkP.

## Key numbers

- The experimental 2-neutron separation energy S2n for 56Ni is approximately 30.8 MeV, dropping dramatically to about 22.5 MeV for 58Ni, giving an experimental shell gap of about 8.4 MeV at N=28.
- With the scalar-only force NL-SH, the difference in S2n between 56Ni and 58Ni is 11.1 MeV, while with the scalar-vector force NL-SV2 it is 9.2 MeV, compared to the experimental difference of about 8.4 MeV.
- For Ni isotopes around A=62, the RMF+BCS approach with scalar-only forces underestimates binding energy by about 3-4 MeV (approximately 0.5%).
- The force TM1 underestimates binding energies by about 4 MeV for several Ni nuclei in the region A=54-62.

## Context

This work extends the RHB framework developed by Gonzalez-Llarena et al. (1996) and the RMF theory with nonlinear scalar coupling established by Boguta and Bodmer (1977). It directly addresses the debate between strong shell effects predicted by the RMF theory with scalar coupling (Sharma et al., 1994) and the shell quenching predicted by the HFB approach with Skyrme force SkP (Dobaczewski et al., 1994). The authors show that SkP produces shell effects at N=28 that are quenched contrary to experimental observation, while the new NL-SV2 force with vector self-coupling reproduces the experimental shell gap. The vector self-coupling of the omega meson was first introduced by Bodmer (1991) and applied to finite nuclei by Sugahara and Toki (1994), with the NL-SV1 and NL-SV2 parameter sets developed by Sharma and Farhan (1999). This work enables improved predictions for shell effects at the neutron drip line, particularly for nuclei such as 80Ni where experimental masses are not yet available.
