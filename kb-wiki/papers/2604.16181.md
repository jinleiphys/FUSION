# Sensitivity of the $^{3,4}$He($K^-$, $Ï€^0$) production ratio to the $Î›$ binding energy of $^3_Î›$H

> We study the production of $^3_Î›$H and $^4_Î›$H in the $^{3,4}$He($K^-$,$Ï€^0$) reactions at $p_{K^-}=1.0$~GeV/$c$ within the distorted-wave impulse approximation, using the optimal Fermi-averaged $K^-p\toÏ€^0Î›$ amplitude. Because the $^3_Î›$H ground state is extremely weakly bound, the $d$--$Î›$ wave function becomes spatially extended. We calculate the integrated cross sections $Ïƒ_{\rm lab}$ and their ratio $R_{34}=Ïƒ_{\rm lab}(^3_Î›{\rm H})/Ïƒ_{\rm lab}(^4_Î›{\rm H})$ for forward angles $Î¸_{\rm lab}=0^\circ$--$20^\circ$. The production strength of $^3_Î›$H and the ratio $R_{34}$ are strongly sensitive to the $Î›$ binding energy $B_Î›$, which is constrained to be approximately 0.05--0.15~MeV by comparison with experimental data from the J-PARC E73 experiment. This indicates that the $^3$He($K^-$,$Ï€^0$) reaction provides a sensitive probe of the weak binding of $^3_Î›$H.

## Key claim

The ratio of integrated cross sections for producing $^3_\Lambda$H and $^4_\Lambda$H in the $^{3,4}$He($K^-$,$\pi^0$) reactions at $p_{K^-}=1.0$ GeV/$c$ is strongly sensitive to the $\Lambda$ binding energy $B_\Lambda$ of $^3_\Lambda$H, and comparison with J-PARC E73 experimental data constrains $B_\Lambda$ to approximately 0.05--0.15 MeV within the distorted-wave impulse approximation framework, supporting an extremely weakly bound hypertriton.

## Method

The authors performed distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) calculations for the $^{3,4}$He($K^-$,$\pi^0$) reactions at $p_{K^-}=1.0$ GeV/$c$ using optimal Fermi-averaged $K^-p\to\pi^0\Lambda$ elementary amplitudes from Gopal et al. (1977). They computed integrated laboratory cross sections $\sigma_{\rm lab}$ over forward angles $\theta_{\rm lab}=0^\circ$--$20^\circ$ for both $^3_\Lambda$H and $^4_\Lambda$H, employing a $2N$--$\Lambda$ model for $^3_\Lambda$H and a $3N$--$\Lambda$ model for $^4_\Lambda$H, and varied the $\Lambda$--$d$ interaction strength factor $f_s$ to explore the $B_\Lambda$ dependence of the cross-section ratio $R_{34}=\sigma_{\rm lab}(^3_\Lambda{\rm H})/\sigma_{\rm lab}(^4_\Lambda{\rm H})$.

## Key numbers

- For $^4_\Lambda$H ($0^+_{\rm g.s.}$) at $p_{K^-}=1.0$ GeV/$c$, $\sigma_{\rm lab}=63.09$ $\mu$b in DWIA and 150.44 $\mu$b in PWIA for $\theta_{\rm lab}=0^\circ$--$20^\circ$.
- For $^3_\Lambda$H with $B_\Lambda=0.130$ MeV (the nominal value at $f_s=1.000$), $\sigma_{\rm lab}=25.85$ $\mu$b in DWIA and 43.62 $\mu$b in PWIA, with r.m.s. $d$--$\Lambda$ distance $\langle r^2_{d\mbox{-}\Lambda} \rangle^{1/2}=11.31$ fm.
- The experimental ratio from J-PARC E73 is $R_{34}^{\rm exp}=0.300 \pm 0.054\,(\mathrm{stat.})\,^{+0.047}_{-0.051}\,(\mathrm{syst.})$, derived from $\sigma^{\rm exp}_{\rm lab}(^3_\Lambda{\rm H})=15.0 \pm 2.6 \,(\mathrm{stat.})\,^{+2.4}_{-2.8}\,(\mathrm{syst.})~\mu$b and $\sigma^{\rm exp}_{\rm lab}(^4_\Lambda{\rm H})=49.9 \pm 2.1 \,(\mathrm{stat.})\,^{+7.8}_{-8.0}\,(\mathrm{syst.})~\mu$b.
- The DWIA calculation yields $R_{34}=0.410$ at $B_\Lambda=0.130$ MeV ($f_s=1.000$), and the experimental $R_{34}^{\rm exp}\approx 0.30$ intersects the DWIA curve in the region $B_\Lambda\approx 0.05$--$0.15$ MeV.
- Varying the $^4_\Lambda$H binding energy from 2.16 MeV to 2.25 MeV changes $\sigma_{\rm lab}(^4_\Lambda{\rm H})$ from 63.1 $\mu$b to 63.9 $\mu$b (a 1.3% increase), reducing $R_{34}$ by a factor of 0.987.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous DWIA studies of hypernuclear production (Harada and Hirabayashi, 2021; 2019) by explicitly quantifying the $B_\Lambda$ dependence of both the absolute cross section and the ratio $R_{34}$, rather than using empirical binding energies. The results support weak-binding values of $B_\Lambda$ from reaction analyses (Kasagi et al., 2025; Akaishi et al., 2026) but are in tension with larger values reported by the STAR Collaboration (2022) and from decay-pion spectroscopy at MAMI (Kino et al., 2025). The ratio $R_{34}$ is designed to reduce common normalization uncertainties and provides a robust observable for future experiments and improved few-body calculations to sharpen constraints on the hypertriton binding energy.
