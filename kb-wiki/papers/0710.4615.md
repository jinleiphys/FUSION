# Nucleon Form Factors and Hidden Symmetry in Holographic QCD

> The vector dominance of the electromagnetic form factors both for mesons and baryons arises naturally in holographic QCD, where both the number of colors and the 't Hooft coupling are taken to be very large, offering a bona-fide derivation of the notion of vector dominance. The crucial ingredient for this is the infinite tower of vector mesons in the approximations made which share features that are characteristic of the quenched approximation in lattice QCD. We approximate the infinite sum by contributions from the lowest four vector mesons of the tower which turn out to saturate the charge and magnetic moment sum rules within a few percent and compute them totally free of unknown parameters for momentum transfers Q^2 less than 1GeV^2. We identify certain observables that can be reliably computed within the approximations and others that are not, and discuss how the improvement of the latter can enable one to bring holographic QCD closer to QCD proper.

## Key claim

The paper claims that holographic QCD (hQCD) in the Sakai-Sugimoto model, taken in the large \(N_c\) and large 't Hooft coupling limits, provides a parameter-free derivation of vector meson dominance for nucleon electromagnetic form factors, but only when the infinite tower of vector mesons is included; the results are reliable only for momentum transfers \(Q^2 \lesssim 1\ \text{GeV}^2\) and are subject to corrections from \(1/N_c\) and \(1/\lambda\) effects that are not yet calculable.

## Method

The authors use the Sakai-Sugimoto holographic dual of QCD, where a five-dimensional bulk baryon field is introduced to represent nucleons, and the external electromagnetic current is dual to a bulk photon field. The nucleon Dirac and Pauli form factors are computed from overlap integrals of the 5D baryon wave functions with the non-normalizable photon field, which is expanded in terms of normalizable vector meson modes. The infinite tower of vector mesons is truncated to the lowest four states, which saturate the charge and magnetic moment sum rules within a few percent, and all parameters are fixed from the meson sector with no free parameters.

## Key numbers

- The sum rule for the proton charge at \(Q^2=0\) is saturated by the lowest four vector mesons to a value of 1.04 (compared to the exact value 1).
- The sum rule for the proton Pauli form factor at \(Q^2=0\) (giving \(\mu_p-1\)) is saturated to a value of 1.66.
- The predicted proton electric charge radius is \(r_{pe} = 0.796\ \text{fm}\), compared to the experimental value \(0.895 \pm 0.018\ \text{fm}\).
- The predicted proton magnetic radius is \(r_{pm} = 0.744\ \text{fm}\), compared to the experimental value \(0.855 \pm 0.035\ \text{fm}\).
- The ultraviolet cutoff of the model is \(M_{KK} \simeq 0.94\ \text{GeV}\), which also sets the scale for the momentum transfer limit \(Q^2 \lesssim 1\ \text{GeV}^2\).

## Context

This work extends the holographic QCD approach of Sakai and Sugimoto (2004) and the instanton-based baryon construction of Hong, Rho, Yee, and Yi (2007) to compute nucleon electromagnetic form factors. It contrasts with earlier two-component models (Iachello, Jackson, and Lande; the chiral bag model) that required an ad-hoc "intrinsic core" to fit nucleon data, showing that the infinite tower of vector mesons in hQCD naturally accounts for this core. The results enable future studies of vector meson dominance violation in hot and dense matter, linking to the hidden local symmetry theory of Harada and Yamawaki and its vector manifestation fixed point.
