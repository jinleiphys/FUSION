# Binding of hypernuclei, and phtoproduction of $Λ$-hypernuclei in the latest quark-meson coupling model

> We study the binding of hypernuclei based on the latest version of quark-meson coupling model, and estimate the phtoproduction cross sections for the $^{12}$C($γ,K^+$)$^{12}_Λ$B reaction using the bound $Λ$ spinors obtained in the model.

## Key claim

The latest version of the quark-meson coupling model, which includes the medium effect on the color-hyperfine interaction, predicts no bound middle or heavy mass Sigma hypernuclei, while it yields good agreement with experimental Lambda hypernuclear single-particle energies and predicts bound Xi hypernuclei.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic shell model with bound hyperon spinors obtained from the latest quark-meson coupling model, which incorporates the effect of the nuclear medium on the color-hyperfine interaction between quarks. For the photoproduction reaction, they employed a fully covariant model to calculate differential cross sections for the 12C(gamma,K+)12LambdaB reaction, using plane waves for the outgoing kaon and including only the s-channel process for the elementary gamma p to K+ Lambda reaction.

## Key numbers

- The 1s1/2 level of 208LambdaPb is calculated to be -26.9 MeV, compared to the experimental value of -26.3 +/- 0.8 MeV
- For 17LambdaO, the 1s1/2 level is -16.2 MeV (experimental: -12.42 +/- 0.05 +/- 0.36 MeV)
- For 41LambdaCa, the 1s1/2 level is -20.6 MeV (experimental: -18.7 +/- 1.1 MeV)
- For 209Xi0Pb, the 1s1/2 binding energy is -15.0 MeV
- For 17Xi0O, the 1s1/2 binding energy is -5.3 MeV
- The threshold for kaon photoproduction on 12C is about 695 MeV
- The momentum transfer at 10 degree kaon angle varies between approximately 2 fm-1 to 1.4 fm-1 in the photon energy range of 0.7 GeV to 1.2 GeV

## Context

This work extends the quark-meson coupling model of Guichon (1987) and Rikovska-Stone (2006) to hypernuclei, providing a quark-level description that contrasts with phenomenological models used in earlier studies such as Bennhold (1989) and Shyam (2008). The results enable future experimental searches for Xi hypernuclei at J-PARC and GSI-FAIR, and demonstrate that kaon photoproduction reactions can probe the quark degrees of freedom in hypernuclear production for the first time.
