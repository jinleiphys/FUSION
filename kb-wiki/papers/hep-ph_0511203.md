# Numerical Studies of QGP Instabilities and Implications

> Because the initial shape of the QGP in a heavy ion collision is anisotropic, the momentum distribution becomes anisotropic after a short time. This leads to plasma instabilities, which may help explain how the plasma isotropizes. We explain the physics of instabilities and give the latest results of numerical simulations into their evolution. Nonabelian interactions cut off the size to which the soft unstable fields grow, and energy in the soft fields subsequently cascades towards more ultraviolet scales. We present first results for the power spectrum of this cascade.

## Key claim

Plasma instabilities in an anisotropic quark-gluon plasma transfer energy from hard excitations to soft nonabelian magnetic fields, but nonabelian interactions cut off the growth of the unstable soft fields and cause the energy to cascade toward ultraviolet scales, with the caveat that these results are obtained in the weak coupling limit (\(\alpha_s \ll 1\)) and for SU(2) rather than SU(3) gauge theory.

## Method

The authors solve the Vlasov-Yang-Mills equations numerically on a lattice in real (Minkowski) time, treating soft modes as classical fields and hard modes as particles. The \(W^a(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{v})\) fields, which represent the net color of particles moving in direction \(\mathbf{v}\), are discretized by expanding in spherical harmonics and truncating at a finite \(\ell_{\text{max}}\). The simulations are performed for SU(2) gauge theory in a local patch of plasma that is statistically spatially uniform, with an anisotropic particle distribution \(\Omega(\mathbf{v})\).

## Key numbers

- Instability growth rate: \(\gamma \sim gT\), compared to the large-angle scattering rate in equilibrium \(\Gamma \sim g^4 T\), indicating the instability grows faster than any scattering time scale.
- The screening scale parameter \(m_\infty^2 = g^2 C_f \int d^3 p f(p)/p\) equals \(m_{\rm D}^2/2\) in equilibrium and sets the exponentiation time \(\gamma \sim m_\infty\) for \(O(1)\) anisotropy.
- The power spectrum of the cascade follows \(f(k) \propto k^{-2}\) (spectral index \(\alpha \simeq 2\)), observed in the Coulomb gauge spectrum from electric and magnetic fields.

## Context

This work extends the analytic perturbative treatment of Weibel instabilities in anisotropic QGP, previously developed by Mrowczynski (1990s) and Arnold, Lenaghan, and Moore (2003), into the fully nonlinear regime via numerical simulation. The results contradict the assumption that standard \(2\leftrightarrow 2\) scattering with equilibrium screening (Gelis, 1990s) suffices to describe isotropization, since instabilities grow on a faster time scale. The cascade phenomenon and power-law spectrum provide a starting point for understanding how energy flows from soft to ultraviolet scales, with implications for thermalization models in heavy ion collisions; however, the authors note that the implications for realistic couplings at current experiments remain unclear.
