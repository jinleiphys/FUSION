# Nonaxial octupole deformations in light N=Z nuclei at high spins

> High spin states of ^{32}S$ and ^{56}Ni are investigated by means of the cranking Hartree-Fock method with the Gogny interaction without imposing a restriction on the axial reflection symmetry. It was found that a non-axial octupole deformation of the Y_{31} type becomes important in the yrast states of ^{32}S. A similar effect is predicted for the nucleus ^{56}Ni.

## Key claim

Using the cranking Hartree-Fock method with the Gogny interaction and without imposing axial reflection symmetry, the authors find that a non-axial octupole deformation of the Y31 type becomes important in the yrast states of 32S, and predict a similar effect for 56Ni; however, the octupole minima are shallow and the effect is conditional on specific rotational frequencies where pseudo level-crossings occur between single-particle orbits with opposite parities.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent cranking Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations (with pairing found negligible) using the Gogny D1S effective interaction, without imposing axial or signature symmetry a priori, but retaining z-simplex and S^T_y symmetries. Single-particle wave functions were expanded in a three-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis up to principal quantum number N=8 for 32S and N=10 for 56Ni, with the y-axis as the quantization axis for deformation. The cranking HFB equations were solved iteratively with a convergence condition of 0.1 keV on quasi-particle energies, and deformation parameters β2, γ, β31, and β33 were extracted from the calculated density distributions.

## Key numbers

- For 32S: the non-axial octupole deformation |β31| reaches a finite value of 0.13 at ω ≈ 2.2 MeV/ħ, where the pseudo level-crossing occurs between N=2 and N=3 orbits.
- For 32S: the superdeformed band becomes yrast for I ≥ 12 ħ, with excitation energy of the SD minimum relative to ground state about 10 MeV.
- For 32S: the ground state quadrupole deformation is β = 0.16 and the SD minimum has β = 0.7 (consistent with SLy4-HF calculations).
- For 56Ni: the maximal Y31 deformation is β31 = 0.09 at ω ≈ 1.4 MeV/ħ, corresponding to I ≈ 10 ħ.
- For 56Ni: the ground state is spherical; the ND shape isomer lies ~5 MeV above ground state and the SD isomer lies ~18 MeV above ground state at ω = 0.
- For 56Ni: the SD minimum has quadrupole deformation β = 0.6 and is formed by a 4p-4h configuration relative to the ground state.
- For 56Ni: the ND minimum has prolate deformation β = 0.35 at ω = 0.
- The energy gaps between quasi-particle energies near the Fermi surface in 32S are about 4 MeV in both proton and neutron fields.

## Context

This work extends prior self-consistent cranking HF+Skyrme calculations by Yam98 that predicted Y31 deformation in 32S, and is guided by a harmonic oscillator model study by He99 that suggested octupole instability at particle numbers N=16 and 28. The authors use the Gogny interaction (which includes pairing correlations more completely than Skyrme forces) to study non-axial octupole deformations in light N=Z nuclei at high spins, complementing macroscopic-microscopic method calculations by Ch that predicted banana-type Y31 deformation for highly deformed nuclei. The results enable experimental tests via high-resolution gamma spectroscopy and may help understand the link between strongly deformed nuclear shapes and cluster structures, as well as the interplay between classical chaos and quantum spectra in finite Fermi systems.
