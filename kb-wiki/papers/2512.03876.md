# Generalized Beth--Uhlenbeck entropy formula from the $Φ-$derivable approach

> We derive a generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck formula for the entropy of a dense fermion system with strong two-particle correlations, including scattering states and bound states. We work within the $Φ-$derivable approach to the thermodynamic potential. The formula takes the form of an energy-momentum integral over a statistical distribution function times a unique spectral density. In the near mass-shell limit, the spectral density reduces, contrary to naïve expectations, not to a Lorentzian but rather to a "squared Lorentzian" shape. The relation of the Beth-Uhlenbeck formula to the $Φ$-derivable approach is exact at the two-loop level for $Φ$. The formalism we develop, which extends the Beth-Uhlenbeck approach beyond the low-density limit, includes Mott dissociation of bound states, in accordance with Levinson's theorem, and the self-consistent back reaction of correlations in the fermion propagation. We discuss applications to further systems, such as quark matter and nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The paper derives a generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck formula for the entropy of a dense fermion system with strong two-particle correlations, showing that the relation between this formula and the Phi-derivable approach is exact at the two-loop level for Phi, but only under the condition that the cancellation term S' vanishes, which holds specifically for the two-loop approximation.

## Method

The authors start from the Phi-derivable formulation of the thermodynamic potential for quantum electrodynamics, following the approach of Vanderheyden and Baym (1998). They evaluate the entropy as the temperature derivative of the thermodynamic potential, using spectral representations for the Green's functions and applying the stationarity condition to ignore derivatives of spectral densities. By rewriting the bosonic entropy contribution using a derivative optical theorem and expressing the propagators in polar representation with a phase function delta, they transform the entropy formula into a form involving sin^2(delta) times the frequency derivative of the phase shift.

## Key numbers

- The entropy spectral function B_{s,l} near plasmon poles takes the form of a squared Lorentzian: 4(Z_l L_l/2)^3 / [(omega - omega_l(q))^2 + (Z_l L_l/2)^2]^2, where Z_l is the residue and L_l is the imaginary part of the photon self-energy.
- For a Breit-Wigner resonance shape with phase shift delta(omega) = -arctan[Gamma/(omega - omega_R)], the derivative of the phase shift is d(delta)/d(omega) = -Gamma / [(omega - omega_R)^2 + Gamma^2].
- The resulting spectral weight in the generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck formula is sin^2(delta) d(delta)/d(omega) = Gamma^3 / [(omega - omega_R)^2 + Gamma^2]^2.

## Context

This work extends the Beth-Uhlenbeck approach beyond the low-density limit by incorporating quasiparticle effects and self-consistent mean-field shifts, building on the earlier generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck formula of Schmidt et al. (1990) and the Phi-derivable framework of Baym (1962). The formalism enables consistent treatment of Mott dissociation of bound states in dense fermion systems, with applications to nuclear matter, quark matter, and plasmas, and has been used in recent work by Blaschke et al. (2023) to describe the transition from hadron resonance gas to quark-gluon plasma in agreement with lattice QCD thermodynamics.
