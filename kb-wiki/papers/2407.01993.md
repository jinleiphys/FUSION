# Reveal short range interactions between $u/d$ quarks in the $NN$, $D_{03}$, and $D_{30}$ systems

> The dynamic mechanism of short range interactions between $u/d$ quarks remains an open and challenging problem. In order to reveal this quark dynamics, we perform a systematic analysis of $NN$, $D_{03}$, and $D_{30}$ systems in the (extended) chiral SU(3) constituent quark models. By comparing results calculated with different models and different parameter sets, the effects of one gluon exchange and vector meson exchange terms are carefully examined. The results indicate that the vector meson exchange interactions dominate the short range interactions between $u/d$ quarks, whereas the small residual one gluon exchange coupling strength is also allowed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that vector meson exchange (VME) interactions dominate the short-range interactions between u/d quarks in the NN, D03, and D30 systems, while a small residual one-gluon exchange (OGE) coupling strength is still allowed; this conclusion is conditional on the specific parameter sets and model variants (chiral SU(3) vs. extended chiral SU(3) constituent quark models) used, and the authors note that eliminating OGE entirely makes the binding energy of D03 sensitive to the oscillator width parameter b_u.

## Method

The authors performed a systematic analysis of NN, D03, and D30 six-quark systems using the chiral SU(3) constituent quark model and its extended version that includes vector meson exchange (VME) interactions. They adjusted model parameters (including the harmonic-oscillator width b_u = 0.43, 0.45, 0.47 fm) to fit the ground-state masses of light baryons, the deuteron binding energy, and NN phase shifts, then calculated the properties of D03 and D30 dibaryons using the resonating group method (RGM). By comparing results from models with OGE only, VME only, and combinations with different tensor-to-vector coupling ratios (f/g = 0 and f/g = 2/3), they isolated the contributions of OGE and VME to short-range interactions.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: 2.10–2.30 MeV across all models and parameter sets (Sets I–III).
- D03 (d* (2380)) binding energy: 18.54–32.35 MeV in the chiral SU(3) model; 72.84–87.01 MeV in the extended SU(3) model with f/g = 0; 58.27–75.02 MeV with f/g = 2/3; 73.39–110.95 MeV in the no-OGE extended model (Sets I–III).
- D30 binding energy: 10.92–13.38 MeV in the chiral SU(3) model; 4.96–6.71 MeV in the extended SU(3) model with f/g = 0; 10.71–12.49 MeV with f/g = 2/3; 5.00–5.54 MeV in the no-OGE extended model (Sets I–III).
- RMS radius R_{ΔΔ/CC} for D03: approximately 0.73 fm in the extended SU(3) models (Sets I–III), compared to 0.95–1.16 fm in the chiral SU(3) model.
- OGE coupling constant g_u: 0.672–0.788 in the chiral SU(3) model; reduced to 0.052–0.394 in the extended SU(3) models; set to zero in the no-OGE case (Sets I–III).
- Vector meson coupling constant g_chv: 2.351 (f/g = 0), 1.972 (f/g = 2/3), and 2.358–2.715 (no OGE) across Sets I–III.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of short-range quark interactions using constituent quark models, particularly the chiral SU(3) and extended chiral SU(3) models developed by Zhang (1997), Dai (2003), and Huang (2015). It contradicts the view of Oka and collaborators (1981) that OGE is the sole mechanism for short-range interactions in nonstrange dibaryons. The results enable future studies to refine the balance between OGE and VME in compact dibaryon systems like D03 and D30, and to test the model predictions against experimental data on d* (2380) from WASA-at-COSY and other collaborations.
