# Hadron Spectrum in a Two-Colour Baryon-Rich Medium

> The hadron spectrum of SU(2) lattice gauge theory with two flavours of Wilson quark is studied on an 8^3x16 lattice using all-to-all propagators, with particular emphasis on the dependence on quark chemical potential mu. As mu is increased from zero the diquark states with non-zero baryon number B respond as expected, while states with B=0 remain unaffected until the onset of non-zero baryon density at mu=m_pi/2. Post onset the pi-meson mass increases in accordance with chiral perturbation theory while the rho becomes lighter. In the diquark sector a Goldstone state associated with a superfluid ground state can be identified. A further consequence of superfluidity is an approximate degeneracy between mesons and baryons with the same spacetime and isospin quantum numbers. Finally we find tentative evidence for the binding of states with kaon quantum numbers within the baryonic medium.

## Key claim

The hadron spectrum of SU(2) lattice gauge theory with two flavours of Wilson quark shows that beyond the onset of non-zero baryon density at μ = mπ/2, the pion mass increases in qualitative agreement with chiral perturbation theory while the rho meson becomes significantly lighter, and a Goldstone state associated with a superfluid ground state can be identified in the diquark sector; however, these results are obtained on a modest 8³ × 16 lattice with a diquark source term that must be extrapolated to zero, and the statistical noise increases substantially at higher chemical potentials.

## Method

The study used Hybrid Monte Carlo simulations of SU(2) lattice gauge theory with two flavours of Wilson quark on an 8³ × 16 lattice at β = 1.7 and κ = 0.178, with a diquark source term j to regulate infrared fluctuations in the superfluid phase. All-to-all propagators with time, spin and flavour dilution were employed to construct meson and diquark correlation functions, and masses were extracted by fitting to cosh functions or sums of two exponentials for states with non-zero baryon number. For kaon states, partial quenching was used by calculating strange propagators with μ = 0 on the μ ≠ 0 gauge configurations.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing a = 0.26(1) fm, determined from string tension measurements on a 12³ × 24 lattice.
- Pion mass at μ = 0: Mπ a = 0.800(3), with Mπ/Mρ = 0.80(1) at μ = 0.
- Onset chemical potential for the superfluid transition: μo a ≈ 0.4, corresponding to μo = Mπ/2.
- Diquark source strengths studied: ja = 0.02, 0.04, 0.06, with extrapolation to j → 0 performed at μa = 0.3, 0.5, and 0.7.
- Post-onset, the Goldstone diquark mass scales as MGold ∝ √j, with χ²/Ndf values of 1.08, 2.69, and 0.24 for the three extrapolations at μa = 0.3, 0.5, and 0.7 respectively.
- The kaon mass shows a minimum near μ ≈ μo, lying below its vacuum value for μo < μ ≲ MK, supporting the possibility of bound kaon-nuclear states.

## Context

This work extends the pioneering lattice studies of the Hiroshima group (Muroya et al., 2002) on smaller lattices, who found that the rho meson becomes lighter beyond onset but observed no significant μ-dependence in baryon channels. It also builds on simulations with staggered fermions (Kogut et al., 2001) that identified the Goldstone mode associated with superfluidity in QC₂D. The results enable future studies of meson and diquark wavefunctions, extended operators for the I=0 0⁺ and I=1 1⁺ mesons, and enhanced statistics for disconnected diagrams, which may clarify the nature of the suspected deconfining phase transition at μd a ≈ 0.65.
