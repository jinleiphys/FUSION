# The QCD deconfinement transition for heavy quarks and all baryon chemical potentials

> Using combined strong coupling and hopping parameter expansions, we derive an effective three-dimensional theory from thermal lattice QCD with heavy Wilson quarks. The theory depends on traced Polyakov loops only and correctly reflects the centre symmetry of the pure gauge sector as well as its breaking by finite mass quarks. It is valid up to certain orders in the lattice gauge coupling and hopping parameter, which can be systematically improved. To its current order it is controlled for lattices up to N_τ\sim 6 at finite temperature. For nonzero quark chemical potentials, the effective theory has a fermionic sign problem which is mild enough to carry out simulations up to large chemical potentials. Moreover, by going to a flux representation of the partition function, the sign problem can be solved. As an application, we determine the deconfinement transition and its critical end point as a function of quark mass and all chemical potentials.

## Key claim

Using combined strong coupling and hopping parameter expansions, the authors derive a three-dimensional effective theory from thermal lattice QCD with heavy Wilson quarks that is controlled for lattices up to \(N_\tau \sim 6\) at finite temperature, and they show that the fermionic sign problem at nonzero chemical potential is mild enough to simulate up to large chemical potentials or can be solved entirely by a flux representation.

## Method

The authors start from the 4d Euclidean lattice QCD partition function with Wilson gauge action and heavy Wilson fermions, then integrate out the spatial link variables using a combined strong coupling expansion (in the character expansion coefficient \(u(\beta)\)) and hopping parameter expansion (in \(\kappa\)). The resulting effective 3d theory depends only on traced Polyakov loops \(L(\vec{x})\) and contains a nearest-neighbour interaction term with coupling \(\lambda_1(u,\kappa,N_\tau)\) and a symmetry-breaking term with coupling \(h_1(u,\kappa,\mu,N_\tau)\). The effective theory is simulated using both a standard Metropolis algorithm with reweighting (for the mild sign problem) and a worm algorithm in a flux representation that completely eliminates the sign problem.

## Key numbers

- The critical coupling for the pure gauge deconfinement transition in the effective theory is \(\lambda_{1,c} = 0.18805(2) \equiv \lambda_0\).
- The continuum extrapolation of the pure gauge deconfinement temperature gives \(T_c = 250(14)(50)\) MeV, where the second systematic error is the difference between \(\mathcal{O}(u^{10})\) and \(\mathcal{O}(u^9)\) orders.
- The effective theory is controlled for lattices up to \(N_\tau \sim 6\) at finite temperature.
- The leading-order hopping parameter coupling is \(h_1 = (2\kappa e^{a\mu})^{N_\tau}\), with corrections up to \(\mathcal{O}(u^n\kappa^m)\) where \(n+m=7\).
- The pion mass to leading orders is \(aM_\pi = -2\ln(2\kappa) - 6\kappa^2 - 54\kappa^4 -24\kappa^2 \frac{u}{1-u} + \mathcal{O}(\kappa^6, \kappa^2u^5)\).
- The average sign remains fully controlled for system sizes up to \(N_s=24\) and chemical potentials up to \(\mu/T \sim 3\).

## Context

This work extends the systematic strong-coupling derivation of a 3d centre-symmetric effective theory for finite-temperature \(SU(N_c)\) Yang-Mills (Langelage 2010) to include heavy dynamical fermions via a hopping parameter expansion. A similar approach to the fermionic sector was taken by DePietri (2007), but that work left the gauge sector in the original 4d form; here the authors extend the effective fermionic action to order \(\kappa^6\) and integrate out all spatial gauge links. The effective theory enables exploration of the full range of baryon chemical potentials with numerical ease, making contact with the region of asymptotically large chemical potentials, and as a first application maps out the entire deconfinement critical surface in the \((M_{u,d}, M_s, \mu)\) parameter space.
