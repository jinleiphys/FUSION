# Chiral Soliton Models and Nucleon Structure Functions

> We outline and review the computations of polarized and unpolarized nucleon structure functions within the bosonized Nambu-Jona-Lasinio chiral soliton model. We focus on a consistent regularization prescription for the Dirac sea contribution and present numerical results from that formulation. We also reflect on previous calculations on quark distributions in chiral quark soliton models and attempt to put them into perspective.

## Key claim
The paper reviews computations of polarized and unpolarized nucleon structure functions within the bosonized Nambu-Jona-Lasinio chiral soliton model, emphasizing that the numerical results depend on adopting a consistent regularization prescription for the Dirac sea contribution. The abstract's claim of presenting numerical results is qualified by the fact that the paper is primarily a review that attempts to put previous calculations into perspective rather than reporting new, independent numerical findings.

## Method
The authors outline the bosonized Nambu-Jona-Lasinio chiral soliton model and its application to computing both polarized and unpolarized nucleon structure functions. They focus on the technical challenge of regularizing the Dirac sea contribution, which is necessary to obtain finite results from the model, and they present numerical results obtained from this specific regularization formulation. The paper also reflects on and compares previous calculations of quark distributions in chiral quark soliton models.

## Key numbers
Review-type paper: no original numerics.

## Context
This work extends and reviews the line of chiral soliton model calculations of nucleon structure functions, which have been pursued by various groups over the years. It specifically aims to clarify the role of regularization in the Dirac sea contribution, a technical issue that has led to discrepancies among prior calculations. The paper enables future work by providing a consistent framework for computing structure functions in the bosonized Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, allowing for more reliable comparisons with experimental data and with results from other approaches such as lattice QCD or perturbative QCD evolution.
