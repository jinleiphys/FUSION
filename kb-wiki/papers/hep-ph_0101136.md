# Radial and elliptic flow at RHIC: further predictions

> Using a hydrodynamic model, we predict the transverse momentum dependence of the spectra and the elliptic flow for different hadrons in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 130 A GeV. The dependence of the differential and p_t-integrated elliptic flow on the hadron mass, equation of state and freeze-out temperature is studied both numerically and analytically.

## Key claim

Using a hydrodynamic model, the authors predict that the transverse momentum dependence of elliptic flow and single-particle spectra for various hadron species in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 130 A GeV can distinguish between different equations of state and freeze-out temperatures, but these predictions may fail at large impact parameters (b > 7 fm) and high transverse momenta (p_t > 1.5 GeV/c) where deviations from hydrodynamic behavior have already been observed for charged particles.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic hydrodynamic model with three parameter sets (EOS Q at freeze-out temperatures of 120 and 140 MeV, and EOS H at 140 MeV) to compute transverse momentum spectra and elliptic flow coefficients v_2 for various hadron species in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 130 A GeV. They initialized the hydrodynamic evolution with conditions extrapolated from earlier SPS analyses and implemented freeze-out along a surface of constant energy density. An analytical blast-wave model with azimuthal flow modulation and a simplified four-fireball source model were used to understand the mass and freeze-out temperature dependence of v_2(p_t).

## Key numbers

- For minimum bias Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 130 A GeV, the p_t-integrated elliptic flow v_2 for pions ranges from 3.6% (EOS Q, T_f ~ 140 MeV) to 4.4% (EOS Q, T_f ~ 120 MeV)
- For protons, v_2 ranges from 5.3% (EOS Q, T_f ~ 140 MeV) to 6.6% (EOS H, T_f ~ 140 MeV)
- For Omega baryons with simultaneous freeze-out, v_2 ranges from 6.2% (EOS Q, T_f ~ 140 MeV) to 7.7% (EOS H, T_f ~ 140 MeV)
- For Omega baryons freezing out at T_f = 164 MeV, v_2 is 4.4% for both EOS Q parameter sets
- The latent heat for the first-order phase transition in EOS Q is 1.15 GeV/fm^3 at T_c = 164 MeV
- The sound velocity in EOS H is c_s ~ sqrt(0.15); in EOS Q it is c_s = sqrt(1/3) in the quark-gluon phase

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier hydrodynamic analysis (Kolb et al., 2000) of charged particle elliptic flow at RHIC to predictions for identified hadrons, following the first RHIC elliptic flow measurements by STAR (2000). The paper contrasts its hydrodynamic approach with the hadronic cascade code UrQMD (Bleicher, 1999), which produces steeper spectra and less radial flow, and with the RQMD-based interpretation of Teaney et al. (2000) suggesting that deviations from hydrodynamics at SPS energies arise from a transition to a late hadronic kinetic stage. The predictions enable experimental tests of thermalization and freeze-out mechanisms by comparing v_2(p_t) for different hadron species across collision centralities.
