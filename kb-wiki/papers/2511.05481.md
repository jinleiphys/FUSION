# Radiative corrections to superallowed beta decays at $\mathcal O(α^2 Z)$

> We compute $\mathcal O(α^2 Z)$ radiative corrections to superallowed $β$ decays with a heavy-particle effective field theory that systematically describes the interactions of low-energy ultrasoft photons with nuclei. We calculate two-loop virtual and one-loop real-virtual amplitudes by reducing the Feynman integrals to a set of master integrals, which we solve analytically using a variety of techniques. These techniques can be applied to other phenomenologically interesting observables. The ultrasoft corrections can then be combined with contributions arising from the exchange of potential photons to obtain the complete $\mathcal O(α^2 Z)$ correction to the decay rate, with resummation of large logarithms of the electron energy times the nuclear radius. We find that $\mathcal O(α^2 Z)$ ultrasoft loops induce a relative correction to the decay rate that ranges from $0.7 \cdot 10^{-3}$ in the decay of $^{10}$C to $3.6 \cdot 10^{-3}$ in the decay of $^{54}$Co, and will thus impact the extraction of $V_{ud}$ at the permille level. We show that the inclusion of these corrections reduces the residual renormalization scale dependence of the decay rate to a negligible level, making missing ultrasoft perturbative corrections a subdominant source of theoretical error.

## Key claim

The paper computes the complete $\mathcal O(\alpha^2 Z)$ radiative corrections to superallowed beta decays using a heavy-particle effective field theory, finding that these corrections induce a relative correction to the decay rate ranging from $0.7 \cdot 10^{-3}$ in $^{10}$C to $3.6 \cdot 10^{-3}$ in $^{54}$Co, which will impact the extraction of $V_{ud}$ at the permille level; the abstract glosses over the fact that the calculation is limited to ultrasoft photon contributions and must be combined with potential photon exchange contributions and nuclear structure corrections for the complete result.

## Method

The authors constructed a heavy-particle effective field theory Lagrangian with nuclear degrees of freedom minimally coupled to photons, describing $0^+ \rightarrow 0^+$ transitions for both electron and positron emitters. They calculated two-loop virtual and one-loop real-virtual Feynman diagrams, reducing the integrals to a set of master integrals solved analytically using differential equations and various integration techniques. The ultrasoft corrections were then combined with contributions from potential photon exchange to obtain the complete $\mathcal O(\alpha^2 Z)$ correction to the decay rate, with resummation of large logarithms of the electron energy times the nuclear radius.

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory framework for superallowed beta decays developed by Hill et al. (2023), Cirigliano et al. (2023, 2024), and Borah et al. (2024), which identified large logarithms in $\mathcal O(\alpha^2 Z)$ corrections arising from the separation between nuclear physics scales and the scale of the Q-value. The calculation resolves the residual renormalization scale uncertainty that was estimated by Cirigliano et al. (2024) to be about $\delta V_{ud}|_\mu \approx 2\cdot 10^{-4}$ for $^{10}$C and $^{14}$O, and which became comparable to nuclear structure uncertainties for heavier systems like $^{46}$V and $^{54}$Co. The results disagree with the finite parts of the calculation by Sirlin (1986) in the relativistic limit, but agree with the very recent calculation by Plestid (to appear), suggesting the discrepancy arises from different ultraviolet regulators. The techniques developed can be applied to other phenomenologically interesting observables and extended to subleading terms in the heavy-particle Lagrangian for sub-permille accuracy in $V_{ud}$ determinations.
