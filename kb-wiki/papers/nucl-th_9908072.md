# Chiral model for dense, hot and strange hadronic matter

> An extended chiral SU(3) model is applied to the description of dense, hot and strange hadronic matter. The degrees of freedom are the baryon octet and decuplet and the spin-0 and spin-1 meson multiplets. The parameters of the model are fitted to the hadron masses in vacumm, infinite nuclear matter properties and soft pion theorems. At high densities the appearance of density isomers cannot be ruled out and extrapolation to finite temperature exhibits a first order phase transition at $T \approx 150 MeV$. The predicted dropping baryon masses lead to drastically changed particle ratios compared to ideal gas calculations.

# Chiral Model for Dense, Hot and Strange Hadronic Matter

## Key claim

The extended chiral SU(3) model predicts a first-order phase transition at approximately T ≈ 150 MeV and cannot rule out the appearance of density isomers at high densities, but the equation of state at high density cannot be predicted unambiguously because the vector coupling ratio of baryon resonances (specifically the Δ–nucleon vector coupling ratio r_v) cannot be fixed from first principles.

## Method

The authors construct a relativistic mean-field model based on chiral SU(3)×SU(3) symmetry, including the baryon octet and decuplet as well as spin-0 and spin-1 meson multiplets. Parameters are fitted to hadron vacuum masses, infinite nuclear matter ground state properties, and soft pion theorems. The grand canonical thermodynamic potential is minimized in the mean-field approximation to obtain the meson field expectation values and the equation of state at finite temperature and density.

## Key numbers

- First-order phase transition temperature: T ≈ 150 MeV (at vanishing chemical potential, when baryon resonances are included)
- For the parameter set C₁ (nucleon and Δ couple to the strange condensate ζ), the minimal vector coupling ratio r_v = g_{Δω}/g_{Nω} is ≥ 0.68 to avoid absolutely stable density isomers
- For the parameter set C₂ (pure σ-dependence of nonstrange baryon masses), the minimal vector coupling ratio r_v is ≥ 0.91
- Particle ratios are calculated using freeze-out parameters T = 160 MeV, μ_q = 57 MeV, and μ_s = 20 MeV, as obtained from ideal gas fits to S+Au collisions at 200 AGeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous chiral SU(3) σ–ω model (Zschiesche et al., 1999; Papazoglou et al., 1998) which described nuclear matter ground states and finite nuclei, by applying it to finite temperature and including the baryon decuplet. The results challenge the common assumption of vacuum masses or a universal linear drop of masses with density used in ideal gas models (Braun-Munzinger et al., 1996), showing that the chiral model's strongly nonlinear density dependence of baryon masses leads to drastically altered particle ratios. The ambiguity in the vector coupling of resonances prevents a unique prediction of the high-density equation of state, leaving open the question of whether a supersoft equation of state or density isomers occur.
