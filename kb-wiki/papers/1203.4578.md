# Identifying the Charge Carriers of the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Charge correlations in lattice gauge calculations suggest that up, down and strange charges move independently in the QGP (quark-gluon plasma), and that the density of such charges is similar to what is expected from simple thermal arguments. Here, we show how specific elements of the charge-charge correlation matrix in the QGP survive hadronization and become manifest in final-state charge-charge correlation measurements.

# Identifying the Charge Carriers of the Quark-Gluon Plasma

## Key claim
Specific elements of the charge-charge correlation matrix in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) survive hadronization and become manifest in final-state charge-charge correlation measurements, but the connection requires accounting for a second wave of quark-antiquark pair creation at hadronization and the mapping from spatial rapidity to measured rapidity.

## Method
The author derives an expression for the correlation function between hadronic species, $G_{\alpha\beta}(\eta)$, from the QGP charge-charge correlation matrix $\chi_{ab}^{\rm(QGP)}$ by assuming correlations are distributed thermally among species via a Lagrange multiplier. The spatial rapidity correlations are then mapped to regular rapidity using a blast-wave model with a Gaussian transverse flow profile, and decays of hyperons and neutral kaons are simulated to produce final-state correlations.

## Key numbers
- The default calculation assumes the density of all quarks during the QGP is 0.85 times the final-state density of hadrons, and the density of strange quarks is 92% that of up or down quarks.
- The blast-wave model uses a Gaussian distribution of transverse flow velocities with a variance of 0.6c and a fixed kinetic breakup temperature of 120 MeV.
- The chemical freeze-out temperature for the hadron gas is assumed to be 165 MeV, combined with a suppression factor for baryons of two-thirds to match proton yields from PHENIX.
- For $\pi^+\pi^-$ correlations, roughly two-thirds of the correlation comes from the hadronization component and approximately one-third from the QGP component.
- The default value of $\sigma_{\rm QGP}$ is 1.0, with alternative values of 0.5 and 1.5 shown for comparison; $\sigma_{\rm HAD}$ is set to 0.2.
- The STAR detector at RHIC and the ALICE detector at the LHC cover ranges of relative rapidity close to 2 units; CMS and ATLAS cover over 3 units of rapidity.

## Context
This work extends lattice QCD calculations of charge-charge correlations (Borsanyi 2011, Bazavov 2012) by showing how the QGP correlation matrix $\chi_{ab}$ can be extracted from experimental measurements of hadronic correlations. The method builds on prior work by Pratt (2011) on charge-balance functions, which have been studied by Bass (2000), Cheng (2004), Schlichting (2010), and Bozek (2004), and measured for a few species by the STAR collaboration (Aggarwal 2010, Adams 2003, Westfall 2004). The results enable experimental determination of the four unknown elements of $\chi_{ab}^{\rm(QGP)}$ (using isospin symmetry) and the diffusive width $\sigma_{\rm QGP}$ from analysis of correlations among pions, kaons, and protons, which encompass six independent distributions.
