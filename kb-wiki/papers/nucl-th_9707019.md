# Radiative Muon Capture by a Proton in Chiral Perturbation Theory

> The first measurement of the radiative muon capture (RMC) rate on a proton was recently carried out at TRIUMF. The TRIUMF group analyzed the RMC rate in terms of the theoretical formula of Beder and Fearing, and found the surprising result that the pseudoscalar coupling g_P is 1.5 times the value expected from PCAC. To assess the reliability of the theoretical framework used by the TRIUMF group to relate the RMC rate to the pseudoscalar form factor, we calculate the RMC rate in chiral perturbation theory, which provides a systematic framework to describe all the vertices involved in RMC, fulfilling gauge-invariance and chiral-symmetry requirements in a transparent manner. As a first step we present a chiral perturbation calculation at tree level which includes sub-leading order terms.

## Key claim

The authors present a tree-level chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) calculation of the radiative muon capture (RMC) rate on a proton, up to next-to-leading order (NLO) in the chiral expansion, as a first step toward assessing the reliability of the phenomenological framework used by the TRIUMF group to extract the pseudoscalar coupling g_P from their measured RMC rate. The calculation does not yet directly address the "g_P problem" because it does not treat capture from separate hyperfine states or from the pμp molecular state.

## Method

The authors employ heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory, using the effective Lagrangian of Bernard et al. (1995) expanded to chiral orders ν=0 and ν=1, which includes nucleon recoil corrections of order 1/M. They evaluate all tree-level Feynman diagrams up to chiral order ν=1 for the process μ⁻ + p → n + ν + γ, with the muon and proton assumed at rest, and compute the spin-averaged total capture rate and photon spectrum for RMC from the μp atomic state with statistical spin distributions.

## Key numbers

- Total RMC capture rate from the full NLO calculation: Γ_RMC = 0.075 s⁻¹
- Leading-order (O((1/M)⁰)) contribution to Γ_RMC: 0.061 s⁻¹
- Next-to-leading-order (O(1/M)) contribution to Γ_RMC: 0.014 s⁻¹
- Γ_RMC with all pion-pole diagrams omitted: 0.053 s⁻¹
- The 1/M recoil corrections account for about 20% of the leading-order contribution
- About 30% of the total Γ_RMC comes from pion-pole exchange diagrams
- The ChPT calculation gives a photon spectrum that is slightly harder (by about 10% for E_γ > 60 MeV) than the result of Beder and Fearing (1987) and Fearing (1980) using the Goldberger-Treiman value g_P = 6.6 g_A

## Context

This work extends the application of chiral perturbation theory, previously used for ordinary muon capture (OMC) by Bernard et al. (1994) and Fearing et al. (1997), to the more sensitive probe of radiative muon capture. The calculation provides a systematic alternative to the phenomenological minimal-substitution approach of Beder and Fearing (1987) used by the TRIUMF group (Jon et al., 1996), and enables future comparisons with experiment once hyperfine-state decomposition and pμp molecular capture are included, as well as next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) calculations.
