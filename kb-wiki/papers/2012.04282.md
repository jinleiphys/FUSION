# Electric dipole response of low-lying excitations in the two-neutron halo nucleus $\boldsymbol{^{29}}$F

> The neutron-rich $^{28,29}$F isotopes have been recently studied via knockout and interaction cross-section measurements. The $2n$ halo in $^{29}$F has been linked to the occupancy of $pf$ intruder configurations. We investigate bound and continuum states in $^{29}$F, focusing on the $E1$ response of low-lying excitations and the effect of dipole couplings on nuclear reactions. $^{29}\text{F}$ ($^{27}\text{F}+n+n$) wave functions are built within the hyperspherical harmonics formalism, and reaction cross sections are calculated using the Glauber theory. Continuum states and $B(E1)$ transition probabilities are described in a pseudostate approach using the analytical THO basis. The corresponding structure form factors are used in CDCC calculations to describe low-energy scattering. Parity inversion in $^{28}$F leads to a $^{29}$F ground state characterized by 57.5% of $(p_{3/2})^2$ intruder components, a strong dineutron configuration, and an increase of the matter radius with respect to the core radius of $ΔR=0.20$ fm. Glauber-model calculations for a carbon target at 240 MeV/nucleon provide a total reaction cross section of 1370 mb, in agreement with recent data. The model produces also a barely bound excited state corresponding to a quadrupole excitation. $B(E1)$ calculations into the continuum yield a total strength of 1.59 e$^2$fm$^2$ up to 6 MeV, and the $E1$ distribution exhibits a resonance at $\approx$ 0.85 MeV. Results using a standard shell-model order for $^{28}$F lead to a considerable reduction of the $B(E1)$ distribution. The four-body CDCC calculations for $^{29}\text{F}+^{120}\text{Sn}$ around the Coulomb barrier are dominated by dipole couplings, which totally cancel the Fresnel peak in the elastic cross section. These results are consistent with a two-neutron halo and may guide future experimental campaigns.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the two-neutron halo in \(^{29}\)F is characterized by a dominant \((p_{3/2})^2\) intruder configuration (57.5% of the ground-state wave function), but this result is conditional on the assumed two-neutron separation energy \(S_{2n}=1.44\) MeV; using the lower limit of the experimental \(S_{2n}\) (approximately 1 MeV) increases the \((p_{3/2})^2\) weight by 5% and yields a matter radius increase \(\Delta R = 0.25\) fm, closer to the experimental value of 0.35(8) fm.

## Method

The \(^{29}\)F nucleus is described as a three-body system (\(^{27}\)F + \(n\) + \(n\)) using the hyperspherical harmonics expansion formalism, with the hyperradial functions expanded in an analytical transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) basis to obtain bound and pseudostate continuum wave functions. The binary potentials include a Woods-Saxon \(^{27}\)F-\(n\) interaction adjusted to reproduce the measured \(^{28}\)F resonances (a \(2p_{3/2}\) ground-state resonance at 0.199 MeV and a \(1d_{3/2}\) state at 0.966 MeV) and the Gogny-Pires-Tourreil (GPT) potential for the \(nn\) interaction; a phenomenological three-body force is tuned to match the experimental two-neutron separation energy \(S_{2n}=1.44\) MeV. Total reaction cross sections are computed with Glauber theory, and the \(E1\) response is obtained from \(B(E1)\) transition probabilities into the continuum, with the resulting structure form factors used in continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) calculations for low-energy scattering on a heavy target.

## Key numbers

*   Ground-state intruder \((p_{3/2})^2\) component: 57.5% of the wave function, with 29.0% \((d_{3/2})^2\) and 6.1% \((f_{7/2})^2\).
*   Increase of the matter radius relative to the core: \(\Delta R = 0.20\) fm (for \(S_{2n}=1.44\) MeV); \(\Delta R = 0.25\) fm (for \(S_{2n}\approx 1\) MeV).
*   Total reaction cross section on a carbon target at 240 MeV/nucleon: 1370 mb (for \(S_{2n}=1.44\) MeV) and 1390 mb (for \(S_{2n}\approx 1\) MeV), compared to the experimental value of \(1396 \pm 28\) mb.
*   Total \(B(E1)\) strength into the continuum up to 6 MeV: 1.59 e\(^2\)fm\(^2\).
*   \(E1\) distribution resonance energy: approximately 0.85 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous three-body calculations for \(^{29}\)F (Singh 2020) by incorporating the latest experimental data on \(^{28}\)F resonances from knockout measurements (Revel 2020) and interaction cross sections (Bagchi 2020). The predictions of a low-lying \(E1\) resonance and strong dipole couplings in CDCC calculations for \(^{29}\)F + \(^{120}\)Sn around the Coulomb barrier, which cancel the Fresnel peak in elastic scattering, are analogous to features observed in well-established two-neutron halo nuclei such as \(^{6}\)He (MRoGa 2008) and \(^{11}\)Li (Cubero 2012). These results provide a direct link between the halo structure and reaction mechanisms, and may guide future Coulomb dissociation experiments aimed at confirming the two-neutron halo in \(^{29}\)F.
