# Dilepton Production in Elementary Processes on the Nucleon

> We study elementary processes for production of dileptons from nucleons, either through NN bremsstrahlung or through photon-induced reactions. We emphasize the dependence of the expected cross-sections on the electromagnetic formfactor of the nucleon in the time-like regime and point out that the mentioned reactions can provide important information on the validity of vector meson dominance for the nucleon. We also study the off-shell dependence of the formfactors.

## Key claim

Dilepton production in elementary nucleon-nucleon and photon-nucleon reactions can probe the electromagnetic formfactor of the nucleon in the time-like regime, but existing data on proton-induced dilepton production on light nuclei appear to disagree with predictions based on naive vector meson dominance (VMD), and this discrepancy may be due to off-shell effects on the formfactors that are not captured by the simple VMD picture.

## Method

The authors studied dilepton production in nucleon-nucleon collisions using an effective T matrix based on a One-Boson-Exchange (OBE) model fitted to elastic scattering data, treating dilepton emission from nucleon and Delta lines coherently. They also developed a microscopic model combining a loop expansion for pi, N, and Delta with VMD for the pion to study the off-shell dependence of the nucleon electromagnetic formfactor. For photon-induced reactions, they generalized a semi-empirical pole-fit description of Compton scattering to the case of outgoing massive time-like photons, enforcing current conservation and gauge invariance.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of dilepton invariant mass spectra for pp to pd was calculated at bombarding energies from 1.03 to 4.90 GeV, showing interference effects between nucleon and Delta contributions at higher invariant masses.
- For p + Be-9 at 2.1 GeV, the calculated dilepton invariant mass spectrum using a VMD formfactor showed a strong vector meson signal at the rho meson mass, but the DLS data showed no special effect in that mass region.
- The microscopic model reproduces the on-shell electromagnetic formfactor of the nucleon in the space-like region and shows that the time-like electric formfactor in the particle-particle channel is dominated by VMD and is remarkably stable as a function of off-shellness; however, the magnetic formfactor is strongly off-shell dependent.
- For gamma + p at 1.2 GeV, the dilepton invariant mass spectrum at forward emission angles is dominated by the Bethe-Heitler contribution over a wide range, while at backward emission angles the overall cross section is smaller but the resonance structure is more pronounced.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions by Wolf and Ko, where the eta Dalitz decay and pi+ pi- annihilation dominate the spectra, by focusing on the nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung and Delta decay components that become dominant at higher invariant masses in proton-nucleus reactions. The calculations use a more refined model than the commonly employed long-wavelength approximation, and the results challenge the universal applicability of VMD for the nucleon formfactor, as previously assumed by Sakurai and others. The proposed photon-induced dilepton production experiment offers a cleaner probe of the time-like formfactor than nucleon-nucleon collisions, where strong and electromagnetic vertex effects are entangled.
