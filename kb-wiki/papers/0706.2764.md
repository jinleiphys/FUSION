# Self-consistent calculations within the Extended Theory of Finite Fermi Systems

> The Extended Theory of Finite Fermi Systems(ETFFS) describes nuclear excitations considering phonons and pairing degrees of freedom, using experimental single particle energies and the effective Landau-Migdal interaction. Here we use the Skyrme interactions in order to extend the range of applicability of the ETFFS to experimentally not yet investigated short-lived isotopes. We find that Skyrme interactions which reproduce at the mean field level both ground state properties and nuclear excitations are able to describe the spreading widths of the giant resonances in the new approach, but produce shifts of the centroid energies. A renormalization of the Skyrme interactions is required for approaches going beyond the mean field level.

## Key claim

Skyrme interactions that reproduce ground-state properties and RPA-level excitations can describe the spreading widths of giant resonances within the Extended Theory of Finite Fermi Systems (ETFFS), but they produce centroid energies that are too low by about 1.5-2 MeV, implying that a renormalization of the Skyrme parameterization is required for approaches going beyond the mean field level.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the code HFBRAD to obtain the mean field, then applied the Quasiparticle Time Blocking Approximation (QTBA) version of the ETFFS to compute excited states. The residual particle-hole interaction was derived as the second derivative of the Skyrme energy functional, with the spin-orbit term dropped and velocity-dependent terms approximated by their Landau-Migdal limit. The single-particle continuum was discretized with a quasiparticle energy cutoff of 100 MeV, and 14-16 low-lying phonons with angular momentum L=2-6 and natural parity were included.

## Key numbers

- For $^{120}$Sn GDR: ETFFS gives E0 = 12.3 MeV, Gamma = 5.2 MeV; RPA gives E0 = 15.1 MeV, Gamma = 3.6 MeV; experiment gives E0 = 15.4 MeV, Gamma = 4.9 MeV (SLy4 interaction)
- For $^{132}$Sn GDR: ETFFS gives E0 = 12.5 MeV, Gamma = 5.2 MeV; RPA gives E0 = 14.3 MeV, Gamma = 3.8 MeV; experiment gives E0 = 16.1 MeV, Gamma = 4.7 MeV (SLy4 interaction)
- For $^{132}$Sn GDR with BSk5: ETFFS gives E0 = 12.6 MeV, Gamma = 4.4 MeV; RPA gives E0 = 14.0 MeV, Gamma = 2.9 MeV
- For $^{132}$Sn GDR with SKM*: ETFFS gives E0 = 13.1 MeV, Gamma = 3.4 MeV; RPA gives E0 = 14.0 MeV, Gamma = 3.3 MeV
- Low-lying $2^+$ state in $^{120}$Sn: without phonons 1.60 MeV, experiment 1.17 MeV; with phonons the state is shifted below ground state
- Low-lying $3^-$ state in $^{120}$Sn: without phonons 2.60 MeV, with phonons 1.3 MeV, experiment 2.40 MeV
- Low-lying $2^+$ state in $^{132}$Sn: without phonons 4.46 MeV, with phonons 2.10 MeV, experiment 4.04 MeV
- Low-lying $3^-$ state in $^{132}$Sn: without phonons 3.00 MeV, with phonons 1.50 MeV, experiment 4.35 MeV
- Nuclear matter properties of Skyrme forces used: BSk5 (m*/m = 0.92, kappa = 237 MeV, a_s = 28.7 MeV); SLy4 (m*/m = 0.70, kappa = 230 MeV, a_s = 32.0 MeV); SKM* (m*/m = 0.79, kappa = 218 MeV, a_s = 30 MeV); SIII (m*/m = 0.76, kappa = 356 MeV, a_s = 28.2 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the ETFFS, previously limited by its reliance on experimental single-particle spectra, to short-lived isotopes by using self-consistent Skyrme mean fields. It contrasts with the relativistic mean field approach of Litvinova, Ring, and collaborators (2006-2007), whose phonon calculations differed little from RPA due to a subtraction procedure the present authors consider ad hoc. The results are qualitatively similar to calculations by Sarchi, Bortignon, and Colo (2004) using the SIII parametrization, which gave higher RPA centroid energies. The paper establishes that a consistent description of both even-even and odd nuclei requires renormalizing Skyrme interactions: reducing the isoscalar particle-hole strength and increasing the isovector strength to compensate for phonon-induced compression of the single-particle spectrum.
