# Cross sections of $α$-induced reactions for targets with masses $A \approx 20-50$ at low energies

> A simple reduction scheme using so-called reduced energies $E_{\rm{red}}$ and reduced cross sections $σ_{\rm{red}}$ allows the comparison of heavy-ion induced reaction cross sections for a broad range of masses of projectile and target and over a wide energy range. A global behavior has been found for strongly bound projectiles whereas much larger reduced cross sections have been observed for weakly bound and halo projectiles. It has been shown that this simple reduction scheme works also well for $α$-particle induced reactions on heavy target nuclei, but very recently significant deviations have been seen for $α$+$^{33}$S and $α$+$^{23}$Na. Motivated by these unexpected discrepancies, the present study analyses $α$-induced reaction cross sections for targets with masses $A \approx 20-50$. The study shows that the experimental data for $α$-induced reactions on nuclei with $A \approx 20-50$ deviate slightly from the global behavior of reduced cross sections. However, in general the deviations evolve smoothly towards lower masses. The only significant outliers are the recent data for $^{33}$S and $^{23}$Na which are far above the general systematics, and some very old data may indicate that $^{36}$Ar and $^{40}$Ar are below the general trend. As expected, also the doubly-magic $^{40}$Ca nucleus lies slightly below the results for its neighboring nuclei. Overall, the experimental data are nicely reproduced by a statistical model calculation utilizing the simple $α$-nucleus potential by McFadden and Satchler. Simultaneously with the deviation of reduced cross sections $σ_{\rm{red}}$ from the general behavior, the outliers $^{23}$Na, $^{33}$S, $^{36}$Ar, and $^{40}$Ar also show significant disagreement between experiment and statistical model calculation.

## Key claim

The experimental data for alpha-induced reactions on nuclei with masses A ≈ 20–50 deviate slightly from the global behavior of reduced cross sections, but these deviations evolve smoothly toward lower masses; the only significant outliers are the recent data for 33S and 23Na which are far above the general systematics, and some very old data may indicate that 36Ar and 40Ar are below the general trend.

## Method

The study analyzes all available experimental data from the EXFOR database for alpha-induced reactions on targets with masses A ≈ 20–50, comparing measured total reaction cross sections to statistical model calculations using the simple alpha-nucleus potential by McFadden and Satchler (1966). Reduced cross sections σred and reduced energies Ered are computed following the scheme of Gomes et al. (2005), which scales cross sections by the geometrical size of the projectile-plus-target system and energies by the Coulomb barrier height. For each nucleus, available data from activation experiments, direct neutron and proton measurements, and elastic scattering angular distributions are reviewed and compared to the statistical model predictions.

## Key numbers

* For 23Na and 33S, reduced cross sections σred are found to be "far above the general systematics" (no specific numerical deviation given in the text).
* The Gamow window for alpha-induced reactions at T9 = 1 (temperature in Giga-Kelvin) is found at Ered ≈ 0.29 MeV, with variation between 0.293 MeV for 21Ne and 0.279 MeV for 51V.
* T9 = 2 corresponds to Ered ≈ 0.45 MeV, and T9 = 3 corresponds to Ered ≈ 0.59 MeV for all nuclei under study.
* The McFadden/Satchler potential gives "excellent predictions" in the whole mass range A ≈ 20–50, with reduced cross sections σred for lighter targets being "close above the general systematics" with increasing differences toward lower energies and lower masses.

## Context

This work extends the reduction scheme of Gomes et al. (2005) and the earlier findings of Mohr (2010, 2013) for heavy nuclei (A ≈ 90–150) to the lighter mass range A ≈ 20–50, motivated by recent unexpected discrepancies seen for alpha+33S and alpha+23Na. The study contradicts the expectation that the McFadden/Satchler potential overestimates experimental cross sections at low energies (as found for heavy nuclei like 64Zn by Gyürky et al. (2012) and 58Ni by Quinn et al. (2014)), instead finding that this potential reproduces the data well for most nuclei in this mass range. The results enable improved predictions for astrophysical reaction rates relevant to the s-process, rp-process, and nucleosynthesis in core-collapse supernovae, particularly for reactions such as 22Ne(α,n)25Mg, 23Na(α,p)26Mg, and 40Ca(α,γ)44Ti.
