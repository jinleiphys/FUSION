# Quark mass density- and temperature- dependent model for bulk strange quark matter

> It is shown that the quark mass density-dependent model can not be used to explain the process of the quark deconfinement phase transition because the quark confinement is permanent in this model. A quark mass density- and temperature-dependent model in which the quark confinement is impermanent has been suggested. We argue that the vacuum energy density B is a function of temperature. The dynamical and thermodynamical properties of bulk strange quark matter for quark mass density- and temperature-dependent model are discussed.

# Quark mass density- and temperature- dependent model for bulk strange quark matter

## Key claim

The quark mass density-dependent (QMDD) model cannot describe quark deconfinement phase transitions because its quark masses diverge at zero baryon density, leading to infinite temperature; the authors propose a quark mass density- and temperature-dependent (QMDTD) model where the vacuum energy density B depends on temperature as B = B0[1 − (T/Tc)^2], making confinement impermanent and allowing the model to qualitatively mimic QCD phase transitions.

## Method

The authors replace the constant vacuum energy density B in the QMDD model with a temperature-dependent function B(T) = B0[1 − (T/Tc)^2] for 0 ≤ T ≤ Tc, and B = 0 for T > Tc, where Tc is the critical deconfinement temperature. They adopt the thermodynamic treatment of Peng et al. (2000) which includes an extra term in the pressure due to density-dependent quark masses, and solve the thermodynamic potential under constraints of baryon number conservation, chemical equilibrium from weak processes, and charge neutrality. Numerical calculations use parameters B0 = 170 MeV fm⁻³, m_s0 = 150 MeV, and Tc = 170 MeV.

## Key numbers

- At fixed pressure P = 400 MeV fm⁻³, the QMDTD model gives T → Tc = 170 MeV as n_B → 0, whereas QMDD model temperatures diverge to infinity in the same limit.
- Saturation point for QMDTD model: n_B0 = 0.45 fm⁻³ and (ε/n_B)0 = 1006 MeV fm⁻³ at T = 50 MeV.
- For comparison, QMDD model saturation values: first treatment n_B0 = 0.46 fm⁻³, (ε/n_B)0 = 1023 MeV fm⁻³; second treatment n_B0 = 0.55 fm⁻³, (ε/n_B)0 = 1083 MeV fm⁻³; third treatment n_B0 = 0.36 fm⁻³, (ε/n_B)0 = 1120 MeV fm⁻³.
- Stability window for QMDTD model at zero temperature: 168.7 MeV fm⁻³ ≤ B0 ≤ 273.3 MeV fm⁻³, compared to 69.05 MeV fm⁻³ ≤ B0 ≤ 111.6 MeV fm⁻³ for the second treatment of QMDD model.

## Context

This work extends the quark mass density-dependent model of Fowler, Raha and Weiner (1981) by incorporating temperature dependence inspired by the Friedberg-Lee soliton bag model (Lee, 1981), where the vacuum energy density vanishes at a critical temperature due to restoration of spontaneously broken symmetry. The paper addresses inconsistencies in prior thermodynamic treatments of the QMDD model by Chakrabarty (1991), Benvenuto and Lugones (1995), and Peng et al. (2000), showing that none of these treatments can avoid the permanent confinement problem at finite temperature. The QMDTD model enables qualitative description of quark deconfinement phase transitions and provides a wider stability window for strange quark matter parameters compared to the MIT bag model and QMDD model approaches.
