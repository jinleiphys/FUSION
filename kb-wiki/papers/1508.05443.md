# Roles of Antinucleon Degrees of Freedom in the Relativistic Random Phase Approximation

> Roles of antinucleon degrees of freedom in the relativistic random phase approximation(RPA) are investigated. The energy-weighted sum of the RPA transition strengths is expressed in terms of the double commutator between the excitation operator and the Hamiltonian, as in nonrelativistic models. The commutator, however, should not be calculated with a usual way in the local field theory, because, otherwise, the sum vanishes. The sum value obtained correctly from the commutator is infinite, owing to the Dirac sea. Most of the previous calculations takes into account only a part of the nucleon-antinucleon states, in order to avoid the divergence problems. As a result, RPA states with negative excitation energy appear, which make the sum value vanish. Moreover, disregarding the divergence changes the sign of nuclear interactions in the RPA equation which describes the coupling of the nucleon particle-hole states with the nucleon-antinucleon states. Indeed, excitation energies of the spurious state and giant monopole states in the no-sea approximation are dominated by those unphysical changes. The baryon current conservation can be described without touching the divergence problems. A schematic model with separable interactions is presented, which makes the structure of the relativistic RPA transparent.

## Key claim

The energy-weighted sum of relativistic RPA transition strengths can be expressed formally in terms of the double commutator between the excitation operator and the Hamiltonian, as in nonrelativistic models, but the commutator must not be calculated using the usual anticommutation relation of local field theory (which would give a vanishing sum); instead, it must be evaluated in a finite momentum space first, yielding an infinite sum value due to the Dirac sea.

## Method

The authors formulate the relativistic random phase approximation (RPA) within the σ-ω model, starting from the Hartree polarization function and deriving the RPA equation in terms of nucleon particle-hole (p-h) and nucleon-antinucleon (N-$\nbar$) excitations. They compare four treatments of the N-$\nbar$ space: the full space, the no-sea approximation (NSA), the no free term approximation (NFA), and the no-antinucleon approximation (No$\nbar$). The energy-weighted sum rule is derived by expressing the sum of RPA transition strengths in terms of the double commutator, and the continuity equation for the baryon current is examined in each approximation.

## Key numbers

* In the full space, the Hartree contribution to the energy-weighted sum, $S_{\mathrm{H}}$, is positive and divergent, proportional to $P_\infty^2$ (the cutoff momentum squared), in contrast to $S_{\mathrm{H}}=0$ in NSA and NFA.
* In NSA and NFA, the second term of $S_{\mathrm{H}}$ from antinucleon contributions is negative because $E_{\bar{n}h} = -E_{h\bar{n}} < 0$, exactly canceling the particle-excitation term in nuclear matter to yield $S_{\mathrm{H}}=0$.
* The sign of nuclear interactions in the RPA equation for the remaining N-$\nbar$ states is changed in NSA and NFA: attractive forces work as repulsive and repulsive as attractive in the coupling of nucleon particle-hole states with N-$\nbar$ states.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous resolution of the long-standing paradox (Kurasawa and Suzuki, 2015) that the relativistic energy-weighted sum rule could not be expressed with the double commutator. It contradicts the common use of the no-sea approximation (NSA) and no free term approximation (NFA) in earlier relativistic RPA calculations (Chin, 1977; Kurasawa and Suzuki, 1987; Dawson and Furnstahl, 1990), showing that these approximations produce unphysical negative excitation energies and sign-flipped interactions that nonetheless dominated previous descriptions of spurious states and giant monopole states. The analysis enables a proper understanding of how antinucleon degrees of freedom must be treated in relativistic RPA, and provides a schematic model with separable interactions to clarify the structure.
