# Octupole Correlations in Excited Bands of Superdeformed Dy-152

> RPA calculations, based on the cranked shell model, are performed for superdeformed \dy\ in which five excited bands have been found recently. We show that characteristic features of the observed dynamical moments of inertia are well accounted for by explicitly taking the octupole correlations into account. Importance of the interplay between rotation and octupole vibrations is stressed, and it is suggested that one of the observed excited bands might be a collective octupole vibration built on the superdeformed yrast band.

# Octupole Correlations in Excited Bands of Superdeformed Dy-152

## Key claim
The observed dynamical moments of inertia for five excited superdeformed bands in Dy-152 are well accounted for by explicitly including octupole correlations in RPA calculations based on the cranked shell model, and one of the observed excited bands (Band 6) is suggested to be a collective octupole vibration built on the superdeformed yrast band. The authors propose this scenario as compatible with the experimental discussion by Dagnall et al. (1994) but with octupole correlations explicitly considered.

## Method
RPA calculations were performed using a cranked single-particle Nilsson Hamiltonian with doubly-stretched octupole and dipole operators, in a configuration space of nine major shells for both protons and neutrons. The equilibrium quadrupole deformations were determined by the shell correction method, and pairing gaps were assumed to be zero for rotational frequencies above 0.3 MeV/hbar where experimental data are available. The isoscalar octupole coupling strengths were set to 1.05 times the self-consistent harmonic oscillator values, guided by systematic RPA calculations for low-lying 3- states in medium-heavy nuclei.

## Key numbers
- B(E3; 3- → 0+) values calculated at ω_rot = 0 are approximately 35 Weisskopf units for the collective octupole vibrational band.
- The alignment of the lowest α=0 1p-1h excitation (proton N=6 → N=7) is i_p - i_h ≈ 4.5 hbar.
- The alignment of the collective octupole phonon (α=1 band) is less than 3 hbar.
- The crossing frequency between Bands 2 and 3 is ω_rot^(c) ≈ 0.5 MeV/hbar.
- The excitation energy of the lowest α=1 band (Band 6) is approximately 1 MeV near the yrast band.
- The dynamical moment of inertia of the yrast SD band is approximated by a Harris expansion with α = 88.5 hbar^2 MeV^{-1} and β = -11.9 hbar^4 MeV^{-3}.
- Sums of squared backward RPA amplitudes at ω_rot = 0.3 MeV/hbar are 0.13, 0.11, and 0.48 for Bands 2, 3, and 6, respectively.

## Context
This work extends earlier RPA calculations based on the cranked shell model by Matsuyanagi et al. (1990) and Nakatsukasa et al. (1992), and builds on theoretical suggestions of strong octupole correlations in superdeformed states from multiple groups (e.g., Dudek et al. 1990, Aberg 1990, Heenen and Ackermann 1990, Cottle 1990, Bonche et al. 1991, Leander et al. 1991, Nazarewicz and Dobaczewski 1992, Skalski 1992, 1993). The calculations directly address the five excited superdeformed bands in Dy-152 observed in a Eurogam experiment by Dagnall et al. (1994). The results enable interpretation of excited superdeformed bands in terms of octupole vibrational modes, and the authors note that a similar interpretation of an excited band in superdeformed Hg-190 has recently been proposed by Crouch et al. (1994).
