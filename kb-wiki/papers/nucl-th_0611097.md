# Relativistic BCS-BEC crossover in a boson-fermion model

> We investigate the crossover from Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) pairing to a Bose-Einstein condensate (BEC) in a relativistic superfluid within a boson-fermion model. The model includes, besides the fermions, separate bosonic degrees of freedom, accounting for the bosonic nature of the Cooper pairs. The crossover is realized by tuning the difference between the boson mass and boson chemical potential as a free parameter. The model yields populations of condensed and uncondensed bosons as well as gapped and ungapped fermions throughout the crossover region for arbitrary temperatures. Moreover, we observe the appearance of antiparticles for sufficiently large values of the crossover parameter. As an application, we study pairing of fermions with imbalanced populations. The model can potentially be applied to color superconductivity in dense quark matter at strong couplings.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates a relativistic BCS-BEC crossover in a boson-fermion model where the crossover parameter \(x = -(m_{b,r}^2 - \mu_b^2)/(4g^2)\) is tuned, but the authors note that the model is "too simple to describe a realistic scenario of color-superconducting quark matter since it contains only a single fermion species" and therefore cannot account for different pairing patterns of quarks.

## Method

The authors construct a relativistic Lagrangian with fermions and a complex scalar boson field coupled via a Yukawa interaction, with chemical equilibrium enforced by \(\mu_b = 2\mu\). They apply a mean-field approximation by replacing the zero-mode of the boson field with its expectation value \(\phi\) and neglecting interactions with non-zero boson modes. The thermodynamic potential is computed via the path integral, and the coupled density and gap equations are solved numerically at fixed effective Fermi momentum \(p_F\), fermion mass \(m\), and boson-fermion coupling \(g\) to determine the gap \(\Delta\) and chemical potential \(\mu\) as functions of the crossover parameter \(x\) and temperature \(T\).

## Key numbers

- For the chosen parameters \(p_F/\Lambda = 0.3\), \(m/\Lambda = 0.2\), and \(g=4\), the fermion chemical potential at zero temperature in the weak-coupling regime is \(\mu_0 = \epsilon_F\) with \(\epsilon_F/\Lambda \simeq 0.36\).
- In the unitary limit (\(x=0\)) at zero temperature, the ratio \(\mu/\epsilon_F \simeq 0.37\).
- At zero temperature, the fermion fraction \(n_F\) and condensate fraction \(n_0\) are approximately equal (\(n_F \simeq n_0\)) at \(x/x_0 \simeq 0\).
- At the critical temperature, the fermion fraction and thermal boson fraction are equal (\(n_F = n_B\)) at \(x/x_0 \simeq 0.3\).

## Context

This work extends the boson-fermion model of superconductivity from Friedberg (1989) to the relativistic regime, building on nonrelativistic studies of the BCS-BEC crossover in cold fermionic atoms (Ketterle, imbalanced spin case) and earlier relativistic crossover studies (Son 2000, Nishida 2005, Abuki 2006). The model can potentially be applied to color superconductivity in dense quark matter at strong couplings, and the authors note that the appearance of antiparticles for large crossover parameter values has been termed "relativistic BEC (RBEC)" in prior work (Nishida 2005, Abuki 2006).
