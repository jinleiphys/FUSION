# Short Range Correlations and the EMC Effect in Effective Field Theory

> We show that the empirical linear relation between the magnitude of the EMC effect in deep inelastic scattering on nuclei and the short range correlation scaling factor $a_2$ extracted from high-energy quasi-elastic scattering at $x\ge 1$ is a natural consequence of scale separation and derive the relationship using effective field theory. While the scaling factor $a_2$ is a ratio of nuclear matrix elements that individually depend on the calculational scheme, we show that the ratio is independent of this choice. We perform Green's function Monte Carlo calculations with both chiral and Argonne-Urbana potentials to verify this and determine the scaling factors for light nuclei. The resulting values for $^3$He and $^4$He are in good agreement with experimental values. We also present results for $^9$Be and $^{12}$C extracted from variational Monte Carlo calculations.

## Key claim
The empirical linear relation between the magnitude of the EMC effect in deep inelastic scattering on nuclei and the short range correlation scaling factor a2 extracted from high-energy quasi-elastic scattering at x≥1 is a natural consequence of scale separation, derived using effective field theory. The relation holds up to higher-order corrections, and the ratio a2 is scheme- and scale-independent even though the individual nuclear matrix elements that compose it are not.

## Method
The authors used chiral effective field theory to match QCD operators for parton distribution functions to hadronic operators at a scale Λ, then derived the factorization of the nuclear structure function into a free-nucleon part and a nuclear modification term proportional to g2(A,Λ), the scaled two-body contact density. They computed the SRC scaling factor a2 for 3He and 4He using Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) calculations with both chiral N2LO interactions (at cutoffs R0=1.0 fm and 1.2 fm) and the phenomenological Argonne v18 plus Urbana IX (AV18+UIX) potentials, and for 9Be and 12C using variational Monte Carlo (VMC) calculations with AV18+UX potentials.

## Key numbers
- For 3He, the GFMC-calculated a2 values are 2.1(2)–2.1(3) (chiral N2LO), 2.0(4) (AV18+UIX), compared to the experimental value 2.13(4) from Hen (2012).
- For 4He, the GFMC-calculated a2 values are 3.8(7)–4.2(8) (chiral N2LO), 3.4(3) (AV18+UIX), compared to the experimental value 3.60(10) from Hen (2012).
- For 3H, the GFMC-calculated a2 values are 2.1(2)–2.3(3) (chiral N2LO) and 2.0(4) (AV18+UIX).
- The chiral expansion parameter ε ∼ P/Λ ∼ 0.2–0.3, where P ∼ mπ is a typical momentum inside the nucleus and Λ ∼ 0.5 GeV is the EFT validity range.
- The SU(4) non-singlet two-body operator is suppressed by a factor O(1/Nc2) ∼ 0.1 relative to the SU(4) singlet operator.

## Context
This work extends the EFT description of the EMC effect from Chen (2004), which explained the factorization of x and A dependence in REMC−1, by showing that the linear EMC-SRC relation discovered empirically by Weinstein (2010) and Hen (2012) follows naturally from scale separation. The calculations confirm that a2 is scheme- and scale-independent, resolving the issue raised by Furnstahl (2013) that individual one- and two-body distribution functions are scale- and scheme-dependent. The results enable future GFMC calculations with chiral interactions for 9Be, 12C, and other light nuclei to test whether a2 is determined by local rather than global nuclear density, as suggested by Seely (2009), and open the door to computing the C(x) coefficient in the EMC-SRC relation from lattice QCD calculations of the deuteron.
