# Primordial Galactic Magnetic Fields from the QCD Phase Transition

> In this paper, we propose a mechanism to generate large-scale magnetic fields with correlations lengths of 100 kpc. Domain walls with QCD scale internal structure form and coalesce obtaining Hubble size correlations and align nucleon spins. Due to strong CP violation, nucleons in these walls have anomalous electric and magnetic dipole moments and thus the walls are ferromagnetic. This induces electromagnetic fields with Hubble size correlations. The same CP violation also induces maximal helicity (Chern-Simons) correlated through the Hubble volume which supports an "inverse cascade" allowing the initial correlations to grow to 100 kpc today. We estimate the generated electromagnetic fields in terms of the QCD parameters and discuss the effects of the resulting fields.

# Primordial Galactic Magnetic Fields from the QCD Phase Transition

## Key claim
The authors propose that domain walls formed at the QCD phase transition, possessing internal structure at the QCD scale (Λ_QCD ≈ 0.2 GeV), can generate magnetic fields with correlation lengths of 100 kpc today, provided that the fields were initially of Hubble size correlation and that a galactic dynamo amplifies them to microgauss strength. The mechanism requires strong CP violation to induce anomalous nucleon dipole moments, making the domain walls ferromagnetic rather than diamagnetic.

## Method
The authors model axion/eta' (a_η') domain walls using an effective Lagrangian that includes the axion, pion, and eta' fields, with a potential derived from QCD chiral and gluon vacuum condensates. They reduce the four-dimensional Dirac equation for nucleons interacting with the domain wall background to two coupled two-dimensional Dirac equations, then apply bosonization and the Goldstone-Wilczek adiabatic approximation to compute expectation values of spin and dipole operators. The induced electromagnetic fields are estimated from these expectation values using dimensional arguments, accounting for the domain wall curvature through an effective correlation length ξ*.

## Key numbers
- QCD phase transition temperature: T_QCD ≈ 0.2 GeV (1 GeV also cited as approximate scale)
- Hubble size at QCD transition: l(T_QCD) ≈ 30 km
- Present-day magnetic field estimate: B_rms ∼ 10^(-9) G / (ξ* Λ_QCD), with correlation length l ∼ 100 kpc
- Initial magnetic field strength estimate: B_rms(T_i) ≈ e Λ_QCD^2 / (ξ* Λ_QCD) ≈ (10^17 G) / (ξ* Λ_QCD)
- Fermi momentum estimate: p_F ≈ Λ_QCD ≈ 150 MeV
- Nucleon mass: m_N (used as dimensional parameter)
- Plasma frequency near QCD transition: ω_p ≈ Λ_QCD
- Conductivity estimate: σ ∼ cT/e^2 ∼ Λ_QCD, with c ≈ 0.07 at T = 100 MeV
- Temperature at e+e- annihilation: T_0 ≈ 100 eV
- Present-day temperature: T_now ≈ 2 × 10^(-4) eV
- Degeneracy factor: g = 4 (2 for spin × 2 for isospin)

## Context
This work extends the "inverse cascade" mechanism for magnetic field evolution discussed by Cornwall (1997), Son (1999), and Field and Carroll (1998), which requires maximal helicity and turbulence to transfer energy from small to large scale modes. The authors argue that earlier proposals, such as those by Iwazaki, fail to generate sufficiently large correlation lengths, and they specifically address Voloshin's argument that domain walls would be diamagnetic rather than ferromagnetic. The mechanism enables a possible explanation for ultra-high energy cosmic rays past the GZK cutoff and apparent anisotropy in Faraday rotation measurements, potentially connecting to the Chern-Simons term discussed by Carroll, Field, and Jackiw (1990).
