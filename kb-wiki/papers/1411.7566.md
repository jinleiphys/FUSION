# Double parton distributions in Light-Front constituent quark models

> Double parton distribution functions (dPDF), accessible in high energy proton-proton and proton nucleus collisions, encode information on how partons inside a proton are correlated among each other and could represent a tool to explore the 3D proton structure. In recent papers, double parton correlations have been studied in the valence quark region, by means of constituent quark models. This framework allows to understand clearly the dynamical origin of the correlations and to establish which, among the features of the results, are model independent. Recent relevant results, obtained in a relativistic light-front scheme, able to overcome some drawbacks of previous calculations, such as the poor support, will be presented. Peculiar transverse momentum correlations, generated by the correct treatment of the boosts, are obtained. The role of spin correlations will be also shown. In this covariant approach, the symmetries of the dPDFs are unambiguously reproduced. The study of the QCD evolution of the model results has been performed in the valence sector, showing that, in some cases, the effect of evolution does not cancel that of correlations.

# Double parton distributions in Light-Front constituent quark models

## Key claim

Double parton distribution functions (dPDFs) calculated in a fully Poincaré-covariant Light-Front constituent quark model show that the commonly used factorized approximation for dPDFs is violated in the valence quark region, but after perturbative QCD evolution to higher energy scales, unpolarized dynamical correlations are suppressed at small x while double spin correlations remain significant.

## Method

The authors compute dPDFs using a Light-Front (LF) approach with a hyper-central constituent quark model, starting from the canonical proton wave function and applying Melosh rotations to transform from instant-form to light-front spin states. The dPDFs are evaluated at the hadronic scale and then evolved to higher energy scales using leading-order perturbative QCD evolution equations (generalized DGLAP equations) for the non-singlet valence sector, solving via Mellin transformations.

## Key numbers

- The ratios r1 and r2 (defined at x2 = 0.4 for five values of k⊥) show k⊥-dependence, demonstrating that a factorized form of the dPDF in the (x1,x2)-k⊥ dependence is not supported by this approach
- At the hadronic scale, the ratio r3 = 2 uu(x1,x2,k⊥=0)/[u(x1)u(x2)] deviates from 1 in the valence region, indicating violation of the factorized approximation
- After evolution to Q² = 10 GeV², r3 approaches ~1 for small x values, indicating suppression of unpolarized dynamical correlations
- At Q² = 10 GeV², the ratio r4 (for double spin correlations) remains significantly different from 1, showing that spin correlations survive evolution

## Context

This work extends previous non-relativistic constituent quark model calculations of dPDFs by Chang et al. and the authors' own earlier work, overcoming the poor support problem (dPDFs vanishing in the forbidden region x1+x2>1) through the Light-Front covariant approach. The results contradict the commonly used factorized approximation of Eq. (1) used in LHC analyses, and enable more reliable model-guided interpretations of double parton scattering data at high energy scales. Further work including non-perturbative sea quarks, gluons, and singlet sector evolution is in progress.
