# Hypermatter in chiral field theory

> We investigate the properties of hadronic matter and nuclei be means of a generalized $SU(3)\times SU(3)$ $σ$ model with broken scale invariance. In mean-field approximation, vector and scalar interactions yield a saturating nuclear equation of state. Finite nuclei can be reasonably described, too. The condensates and the effective baryon masses at finite baryon density and temperature are discussed.

# Hypermatter in chiral field theory

## Key claim

The paper claims that a generalized SU(3)L × SU(3)R σ-model incorporating broken scale invariance via a dilaton field can simultaneously reproduce the vacuum hadron mass spectrum, saturating nuclear matter, and finite nuclei properties, but only if the σ-meson mass is taken around 500 MeV, which the authors acknowledge may actually be generated by correlated two-pion exchange rather than a fundamental scalar field.

## Method

The authors construct a chiral Lagrangian with baryon octet, spin-0 and spin-1 nonets, and a dilaton field χ representing the gluon condensate, using a nonlinear realization of chiral symmetry. They apply the mean-field approximation to infinite nuclear matter and a Hartree formalism for finite nuclei, fitting parameters to vacuum meson and baryon masses, the pion and kaon decay constants, and nuclear matter saturation properties. The thermodynamical potential is minimized in the grand canonical ensemble to study condensates and effective masses at finite baryon density and temperature.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter compressibility K = 278.7 MeV, with effective nucleon mass ratio m*N/mN = 0.62 at saturation
- Pion decay constant fπ = 93.0 MeV, kaon decay constant fK = 122.0 MeV
- Fitted baryon masses: mN = 939.0 MeV, mΛ = 1117.8 MeV, mΣ = 1193.1 MeV, mΞ = 1334.5 MeV (experimental values in parentheses: 939, 1115, 1193, 1315)
- Fitted vector meson masses: mρ = 783.0 MeV, mω = 783.0 MeV, mK* = 857.7 MeV, mφ = 1019.5 MeV (experimental: 770, 783, 892, 1020)
- Asymmetry energy a4 = 39.3 MeV
- For 208Pb: predicted binding energy E/A = 7.65 MeV (experimental 7.86), charge radius rch = 5.48 fm (experimental 5.50), diffraction radius R = 6.81 fm (experimental 6.81)
- At T = 200 MeV, the nonstrange condensate σ drops to 40% of its vacuum expectation value, while the strange condensate ζ drops to 80%

## Context

This work extends earlier chiral σ-model approaches by Gell-Mann and Levy, which suffered from bifurcations and unphysical equations of state, by incorporating broken scale invariance following Schechter (1980) and the logarithmic dilaton potential. It provides a unified SU(3) framework that contrasts with the Walecka model, where all parameters are adjusted to nuclear matter rather than vacuum masses. The results enable future development of a chiral transport theory for heavy-ion collisions with dynamical hadron masses, potentially revealing signatures of a chiral phase transition.
