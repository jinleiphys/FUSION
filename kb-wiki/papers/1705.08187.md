# Multiplicity Derivative: A new signature of first order phase transition in intermediate energy heavy ion collision

> Measurement of M, the total multiplicity, for central collision between comparable mass heavy ions can provide a signature for first order phase transition. The derivative of M with respect to E*/A where E* is the excitation energy in the centre of mass and A the total mass of the dissociating system is expected to go through maximum as a function of E*. Theoretical modelling shows that this is the energy where the specific heat Cv maximizes which typically happens at first order phase transition. The measurement of total M is probably feasible in more than one laboratory.

# Multiplicity Derivative: A new signature of first order phase transition in intermediate energy heavy ion collision

## Key claim

The derivative of total multiplicity M with respect to excitation energy per nucleon E*/A is expected to exhibit a maximum as a function of E*/A, and this maximum coincides with the maximum of specific heat Cv, which is a signature of first order phase transition, even when Coulomb effects are strong enough to destroy the bimodality signal that would otherwise appear in finite systems.

## Method

The authors used the canonical thermodynamic model (CTM) to simulate multifragmentation of heavy ion systems with varying Coulomb interaction strength (controlled by a multiplicative factor xc from 0 to 1). They computed total multiplicity M and its derivative dM/dT (and dM/d(E*/A)) as functions of temperature and excitation energy, comparing these to the specific heat Cv and entropy S for systems of different sizes (Z=82, N=126 and Z=28, N=30) both with and without Coulomb interactions. They also examined the effect of sequential secondary decay on the multiplicity derivative and tested the behavior of intermediate mass fragment (IMF) multiplicity.

## Key numbers

- For the system with Z=82 and N=126 (A=208), the peak in dM/dT coincides with the maximum of Cv; Cv is normalized by a factor of 1/50 to plot on the same scale as dM/dT.
- For the hypothetical one-species system with A=208 and no Coulomb interaction, the rise and peak in dM/dT are much sharper than for the real nuclear system with Coulomb.
- For the smaller system with Z=28 and N=30 (A=58), the features in dM/dT are less sharp than for the larger Z=82, N=126 system.
- For IMF multiplicity M_IMF (composites with charge 3 ≤ z ≤ 20) in the Z=82, N=126 system, the peak in dM_IMF/dT does not exactly coincide with the Cv peak; Cv is normalized by a factor of 1/100 to plot on the same scale.
- Sequential secondary decay makes the peak in dM/dT sharper (shown for the Z=28, N=30 system).

## Context

This work extends the study of phase transition signatures in heavy ion collisions beyond the bimodality signal proposed by Gulminelli and Chomaz, which is destroyed by Coulomb effects at full strength. The authors previously showed bimodality in BUU transport calculations (Mallik 2014) and in quantum molecular dynamics (Lefevre), as well as in the canonical thermodynamic model (Chaudhuri, Mallik 2013). The proposed multiplicity derivative method avoids ambiguities of earlier approaches such as fitting individual fragment yields to power laws (Elliot, Scharenberg, Fisher, Stauffer) which gave equally acceptable fits from very different models. The method enables experimental tests using 4π detectors in multiple laboratories, and the authors suggest it would be interesting to apply the same analysis to the lattice gas model (Pan).
