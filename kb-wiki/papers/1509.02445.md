# A model of charmed baryon-nucleon potential and 2- and 3-body bound states with charmed baryon

> A potential model for the interaction between a charmed baryon ($Λ_c$, $Σ_c$, and $Σ_c^*$) and the nucleon ($N$) is constructed. The model contains a long-range meson ($π$ and $σ$) exchange part and a short-distance quark exchange part. The quark cluster model is used to evaluate the short-range repulsion and a monopole type form factor is introduced to the long-range potential to reflect the extended structure of hadrons. We determine the cutoff parameters in the form factors by fitting the $NN$ scattering data with the same approach and we obtain four sets of parameters (a -- d). The most attractive potential (d) leads to bound $Λ_c N$ states with $J^π= 0^+$ and $1^+$ once the channel couplings among $Λ_c$, $Σ_c$ and $Σ_c^*$ are taken into account. One can also investigate many-body problems with the model. Here, we construct an effective $Λ_c N$ one-channel potential with the parameter set (d) and apply it to the 3-body $Λ_c NN$ system. The bound states with $J=1/2$ and $3/2$ are predicted.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a potential model for charmed baryon-nucleon interactions that, when the most attractive parameter set (d) is used and channel couplings among \(\Lambda_c\), \(\Sigma_c\), and \(\Sigma_c^*\) are included, predicts bound \(\Lambda_c N\) states with \(J^\pi = 0^+\) and \(1^+\) and also bound three-body \(\Lambda_c NN\) states with \(J = 1/2\) and \(3/2\); however, the existence of these bound states depends on the specific choice of cutoff parameters and the inclusion of the short-range quark exchange repulsion.

## Method

The authors constructed a hybrid potential model for the \(Y_c N\) (\(Y_c = \Lambda_c, \Sigma_c, \Sigma_c^*\)) interaction combining a long-range one-pion and one-sigma exchange part with a short-range repulsive part derived from the quark cluster model (QCM), using a Gaussian form for the QCM potential. They determined the cutoff parameters \(\Lambda_\pi\), \(\Lambda_\sigma\) and the sigma coupling constant \(C_\sigma\) by fitting the NN scattering length (\(^1S_0\)) and deuteron binding energy (\(^3S_1\)) with the same model, yielding four parameter sets (a–d). The coupled-channel Schrödinger equation was solved using the Gaussian expansion method (GEM) for the two-body \(Y_c N\) systems, and an effective one-channel \(\Lambda_c N\) potential was constructed from the CTNN-d set and applied to the three-body \(\Lambda_c NN\) system using the Minnesota potential for the NN interaction.

## Key numbers

- NN scattering length (\(^1S_0\)) fitted to \(-23.7\) fm and deuteron binding energy fitted to \(2.22\) MeV.
- For the CTNN-d potential (\(b = 0.5\) fm, \(C_\sigma = -70.68\)), the \(\Lambda_c N\) (\(J^\pi = 0^+\)) binding energy is \(1.37\) MeV (without Coulomb) and \(0.56\) MeV (with Coulomb); for \(J^\pi = 1^+\), the binding energy is \(1.56\) MeV (without Coulomb) and \(0.72\) MeV (with Coulomb).
- For the \(\Lambda_c np\) system (\(I=0\), without Coulomb), the binding energy is \(21.78\) MeV for \(J^\pi = 1/2^+\) and \(22.46\) MeV for \(J^\pi = 3/2^+\); the root mean square distance between nucleons \(r\) is \(1.91\) fm and between \(\Lambda_c\) and the NN center \(R\) is \(1.34\) fm (for \(J^\pi = 3/2^+\)).
- For the \(\Lambda_c nn\) system (\(I=1\), \(J^\pi = 1/2^+\)), the binding energy is \(10.26\) MeV, with \(r = 2.62\) fm and \(R = 1.64\) fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of charmed hypernuclei (e.g., by the same group in a 2015 paper using OBE potentials alone, which found results very sensitive to cutoff parameters) by incorporating a quark cluster model for short-range repulsion to avoid overly deep bound states. The model enables the prediction of bound \(\Lambda_c N\) and \(\Lambda_c NN\) systems, analogous to hypernuclei but with stronger attraction, and provides a framework for future many-body calculations of charmed baryon-nucleus systems. The results are consistent with heavy quark spin symmetry and with a recent three-body calculation by the same authors that also favors the \(3/2^+\) \(\Lambda_c NN\) as the lowest state.
