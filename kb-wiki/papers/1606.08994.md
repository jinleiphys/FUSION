# Tetrahedral shapes of neutron-rich Zr isotopes from multidimensionally-constrained relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model

> We develop a multidimensionally constrained relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (MDC-RHB) model in which the pairing correlations are taken into account by making the Bogoliubov transformation. In this model, the nuclear shape is assumed to be invariant under the reversion of $x$ and $y$ axes; i.e., the intrinsic symmetry group is $V_4$ and all shape degrees of freedom $β_{λμ}$ with even $μ$ are included self-consistently. The RHB equation is solved in an axially deformed harmonic oscillator basis. A separable pairing force of finite range is adopted in the MDC-RHB model. The potential energy curves of neutron-rich even-even Zr isotopes are calculated with relativistic functionals DD-PC1 and PC-PK1 and possible tetrahedral shapes in the ground and isomeric states are investigated. The ground state shape of $^{110}$Zr is predicted to be tetrahedral with both functionals and so is that of $^{112}$Zr with the functional DD-PC1. The tetrahedral ground states are caused by large energy gaps around $Z=40$ and $N=70$ when $β_{32}$ deformation is included. Although the inclusion of the $β_{30}$ deformation can also reduce the energy around $β_{20}=0$ and lead to minima with pear-like shapes for nuclei around $^{110}$Zr, these minima are unstable due to their shallowness.

## Key claim
The paper predicts that the ground state of \(^{110}\)Zr has a tetrahedral shape with both relativistic functionals used (DD-PC1 and PC-PK1), and that of \(^{112}\)Zr is tetrahedral with DD-PC1; these tetrahedral minima arise from large energy gaps at Z=40 and N=70 when \(\beta_{32}\) deformation is included, but the authors note that pear-like minima from \(\beta_{30}\) deformation also appear near \(^{110}\)Zr, though they are unstable due to shallowness.

## Method
The authors developed a multidimensionally constrained relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (MDC-RHB) model with \(V_4\) symmetry (invariance under reversal of x and y axes), including all shape degrees of freedom \(\beta_{\lambda\mu}\) with even \(\mu\) self-consistently. The RHB equation was solved in an axially deformed harmonic oscillator basis, and a separable pairing force of finite range was adopted. Potential energy curves of neutron-rich even-even Zr isotopes (A=100 to 114) were calculated using the relativistic functionals DD-PC1 and PC-PK1 to investigate possible tetrahedral shapes in ground and isomeric states.

## Key numbers
- The ground state shape of \(^{110}\)Zr is predicted to be tetrahedral with both functionals DD-PC1 and PC-PK1.
- The ground state shape of \(^{112}\)Zr is predicted to be tetrahedral with the functional DD-PC1.
- Large energy gaps around Z=40 and N=70 are identified as the cause of tetrahedral ground states when \(\beta_{32}\) deformation is included.
- The pairing strength parameter used is \(G = G_0 = 728\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^3\) with effective range \(a = 0.644\) fm, fine-tuned to reproduce pairing gaps of Zr isotopes.
- The basis truncation parameter \(N_f\) is used with \(N_g = N_f + 1\) major shells for the small component to avoid spurious states.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of tetrahedral shapes in neutron-rich Zr isotopes that used macroscopic-microscopic models (Dudek 2002) and Skyrme Hartree-Fock or Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov models (Yamagami 2001, Olbratowski 2006, Zberecki 2006) by applying a fully self-consistent covariant density functional theory approach. The MDC-RHB model builds on the earlier multidimensionally constrained relativistic mean field (MDC-RMF) model (Lu 2014) by incorporating pairing correlations via the Bogoliubov transformation, enabling more reliable predictions for ground state shapes of neutron-rich nuclei. The results enable experimental searches for tetrahedral shapes in \(^{110}\)Zr and \(^{112}\)Zr, which are accessible at current radioactive beam facilities, and provide a microscopic foundation for understanding shape coexistence in the A\(\sim\)110 region relevant to the r-process path.
