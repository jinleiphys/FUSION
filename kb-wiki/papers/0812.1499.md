# Pion-cloud effects in the BSE description of mesons

> We investigate the effect of including hadronic resonance contributions in the description of light quarks and mesons. To this end we take into account the back-coupling of the pion onto the quark propagator within the non-perturbative continuum framework of Schwinger-Dyson equations (SDE) and Bethe-Salpeter equations (BSE), in essence describing the so-called \emph{pion-cloud}. As a result of our study we find that an unquenching of this form provides for considerable effects in the spectrum of light mesons.

## Key claim

Including pion-cloud effects via the back-coupling of the pion onto the quark propagator within the Schwinger-Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter framework produces considerable shifts in the light meson spectrum, but the magnitude and sign of the shift depend on the specific model interaction used and the observable considered; the pion mass itself shifts only slightly (by a few MeV) while the leptonic decay constant shifts by about 10%.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled system of quark Schwinger-Dyson equation and meson Bethe-Salpeter equation, adding a pion-exchange diagram (the "pion-cloud") to the usual rainbow-ladder gluon exchange. The quark-pion vertex is approximated by the leading chiral-limit amplitude, proportional to the scalar dressing function of the quark propagator divided by the pion decay constant. Two model interactions are used: a "soft-divergent" model fitted to gluon SDE solutions and a Maris-Tandy phenomenological model; both are tuned to reproduce the pion mass and decay constant when the pion back-reaction is included.

## Key numbers

* Quark mass in the MSbar scheme at 2 GeV: 3.4 MeV for the soft-divergent model, 4.4 MeV for the Maris-Tandy model.
* Pion mass with pion-cloud: 138 MeV (Maris-Tandy) and 138 MeV (soft-divergent); without pion-cloud: 140 MeV (Maris-Tandy) and 125 MeV (soft-divergent).
* Pion decay constant with pion-cloud: 93.2 MeV (Maris-Tandy) and 93.8 MeV (soft-divergent); without pion-cloud: 104 MeV (Maris-Tandy) and 102 MeV (soft-divergent); experimental value: 92.4 MeV.
* Rho meson mass with pion-cloud: 720 MeV (Maris-Tandy) and 703 MeV (soft-divergent); without pion-cloud: 821 MeV (Maris-Tandy) and 795 MeV (soft-divergent); experimental value: 776 MeV.
* Sigma meson mass with pion-cloud: 598 MeV (Maris-Tandy) and 485 MeV (soft-divergent); without pion-cloud: 746 MeV (Maris-Tandy) and 638 MeV (soft-divergent).
* a1 meson mass with pion-cloud: 913 MeV (Maris-Tandy) and 873 MeV (soft-divergent); without pion-cloud: 979 MeV (Maris-Tandy) and 941 MeV (soft-divergent); experimental value: 1230 MeV.
* b1 meson mass with pion-cloud: 750 MeV (Maris-Tandy) and 806 MeV (soft-divergent); without pion-cloud: 820 MeV (Maris-Tandy) and 879 MeV (soft-divergent); experimental value: 1230 MeV.
* Eta and eta-prime masses (soft-divergent model only) with pion-cloud: 497 MeV and 963 MeV; without pion-cloud: 493 MeV and 949 MeV; experimental: 548 MeV and 948 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the rainbow-ladder truncation of Schwinger-Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter equations (used extensively by Maris and Tandy (1999)) by adding hadronic resonance contributions in the form of a pion-cloud, following the formalism of Fischer and collaborators (2007, 2008). The results show that unquenching via mesonic degrees of freedom produces negative mass shifts of 100-200 MeV for heavier mesons, bringing the rho mass closer to lattice results (Leinweber, 2004) and to experiment, but the a1 and b1 masses remain far below experiment, indicating that spin-dependent contributions from the Yang-Mills part of the quark-gluon vertex are still needed.
