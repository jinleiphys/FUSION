# Density dependence of 2p-2h meson-exchange currents

> We analyze the density dependence of the contribution of meson-exchange currents to the lepton-nucleus inclusive cross section in the two-particle two-hole channel. The model is based on the Relativistic Fermi Gas, where each nucleus is characterized by its Fermi momentum $k_F$. We find that the 2p-2h nuclear response functions at their peaks scale as $A k_F^2$ for Fermi momentum going from 200 to 300 MeV/c and momentum transfer $q$ from $2k_F$ to 2 GeV/c. This behavior is different from what is found for the quasielastic response, which scales as $A/k_F$. Additionally, the deep scaling region is also discussed and there the usual scaling behavior is found to be preferable.

## Key claim
The 2p-2h meson-exchange current nuclear response functions at their peaks scale as \(A k_F^2\) for Fermi momenta between 200 and 300 MeV/c and momentum transfers \(q\) from \(2k_F\) to 2 GeV/c, but this scaling breaks down at lower momentum transfers and in the deep scaling region, where the usual \(1/k_F\) scaling is more appropriate.

## Method
The authors used the Relativistic Fermi Gas model, where each nucleus is characterized by its Fermi momentum \(k_F\), to compute the 2p-2h nuclear response functions induced by meson-exchange currents. They evaluated the electromagnetic transverse response \(R^T_{\rm MEC}\) from a fully relativistic Lagrangian including nucleons, pions, and \(\Delta\) degrees of freedom, calculating direct and exchange Goldstone diagrams via 7-dimensional integrals. The density dependence was studied by varying \(k_F\) from 200 to 300 MeV/c and momentum transfer \(q\) from 50 to 2000 MeV/c, with the response scaled by \(\eta_F^2 = (k_F/m_N)^2\) and analyzed using both a MEC-specific scaling variable \(\psi^\prime_{\rm MEC}\) and the standard quasielastic scaling variable \(\psi^\prime_{\rm QE}\).

## Key numbers
- The 2p-2h MEC response per nucleon scales as \(k_F^2\) for Fermi momenta from 200 to 300 MeV/c and momentum transfers \(q\) from \(2k_F\) to 2 GeV/c.
- The quasielastic 1-body response scales as \(A/k_F\), so the relative importance of the 2p-2h contribution grows as \(k_F^3\).
- The MEC scaling variable \(\psi^\prime_{\rm MEC}\) uses parameters for \(^{12}\)C including \(m_* = 1170\) MeV/c\(^2\), \(\alpha = 1.3345\), \(\beta = 30.73\), \(\gamma = 0.85\), and energy shift parameters \(E_0 = 42.718\) MeV, \(E_1 = -70.0\) MeV, \(E_2 = 37.0\) MeV.
- For \(^{2}\)H with \(k_F = 55\) MeV/c, the MEC responses were found to be completely anomalous and too small to show in the figure.

## Context
This work extends the 2p-2h MEC model of DePace (2003) by systematically studying its density dependence, complementing earlier superscaling analyses by Donnelly (1999) that showed the quasielastic response scales as \(A/k_F\). The results enable extrapolation of 2p-2h contributions from \(^{12}\)C to heavier nuclei like \(^{16}\)O, \(^{40}\)Ar, \(^{56}\)Fe, and \(^{208}\)Pb used in ongoing and future neutrino oscillation experiments, and should facilitate implementation of 2p-2h effects in Monte Carlo generators. The deep scaling region was previously investigated by DePace (2004) for \(^{12}\)C and \(^{197}\)Au, where the usual scaling was found compatible with JLab data at electron energy 4.045 GeV and scattering angles 23\(^\circ\) and 30\(^\circ\).
