# Nuclear Effects in Charm Production

## Key claim
Charm production on nuclear targets is consistent with leading twist (hard process) expectations for open charm and Drell-Yan, but charmonium production shows significant nuclear suppression that is a higher twist effect, with the suppression decreasing for heavier quarks (bottom vs. charm) and varying strongly with Feynman x.

## Method
The paper reviews experimental data on the atomic number A dependence of charm, charmonium, and bottomonium production in proton-nucleus collisions, parametrized as σ ∝ A^α. It compares the measured α values across different kinematic regions (x_F) and different quarkonium states (J/ψ, ψ', Υ) to distinguish leading twist from higher twist contributions, and discusses theoretical mechanisms including elastic scattering of heavy quarks, gluon radiation energy loss, coherent scattering from light quarks, and comover interactions.

## Key numbers
- Charm quark mass m_c ≈ 1.5 GeV = (1/7 fm)^(-1); QCD confinement scale Λ_QCD ≈ 0.2 GeV = 1 fm^(-1)
- J/ψ total width: 86 keV
- Open charm (D meson) hadroproduction at 800 GeV: α = 1.00 ± 0.05 ± 0.02 for 0 < x_F(D) < 0.8 (E769); α = 1.02 ± 0.03 ± 0.02 for average ⟨x_F⟩ = 0.031 (E789)
- Light hadrons (π, K, p) in same kinematic region: α = 0.7 to 0.8
- Inclusive J/ψ and ψ' production at 0.1 ≲ x_F ≲ 0.3: α ≈ 0.92 ± 0.01
- Υ production at same x_F: α ≈ 0.97
- J/ψ at x_F ≈ 0.6: α ≈ 0.8
- Inelastic J/ψ photoproduction: α = 0.99 ± 0.04 for p_⊥² > 1 GeV² (E691); α = 1.05 ± 0.03 for x_F ≤ 0.85, p_⊥² > 0.4 GeV² (NMC)
- At 800 GeV beam energy, a J/ψ with x_F ≈ 0 has energy ≈ 60 GeV in nuclear rest frame; comover energy ≈ 6 GeV

## Context
This review extends the understanding of nuclear target dependence in heavy quark production, contrasting open charm (which follows leading twist additivity as seen by E769 and E789) with charmonium (which shows suppression). The work builds on earlier measurements of nuclear suppression in charmonium production (Cha) and Drell-Yan (DY), and discusses theoretical proposals including energy loss from gluon radiation (Gunion and Mueller), limits from the uncertainty principle (Brodsky and Hoyer), hard secondary scattering (Jain and Ralston), coherent scattering from light quarks (Brodsky, Hoyer, Mueller, Tang), and comover interactions (com). The results inform the experimental program at the proposed ELFE facility, where charmonium formation inside the nucleus could be studied.
