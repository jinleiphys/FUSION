# The Magnus expansion and the in-medium similarity renormalization group

> We present an improved variant of the in-medium similarity renormalization group (IM-SRG) based on the Magnus expansion. In the new formulation, one solves flow equations for the anti-hermitian operator that, upon exponentiation, yields the unitary transformation of the IM-SRG. The resulting flow equations can be solved using a first-order Euler method without any loss of accuracy, resulting in substantial memory savings and modest computational speedups. Since one obtains the unitary transformation directly, the transformation of additional operators beyond the Hamiltonian can be accomplished with little additional cost, in sharp contrast to the standard formulation of the IM-SRG. Ground state calculations of the homogeneous electron gas (HEG) and $^{16}$O nucleus are used as test beds to illustrate the efficacy of the Magnus expansion.

## Key claim

The paper presents an improved variant of the in-medium similarity renormalization group (IM-SRG) based on the Magnus expansion, claiming that the new formulation allows the use of a first-order Euler method for solving the flow equations without any loss of accuracy, provided the Magnus(2) truncation is employed and the series for the derivative and the Baker-Campbell-Hausdorff formula are truncated based on convergence criteria.

## Method

The authors reformulate the IM-SRG by solving flow equations for the anti-hermitian operator Omega(s), where the unitary transformation is U(s) = exp(Omega(s)). The derivative dOmega/ds is computed using the Magnus expansion involving nested commutators with Bernoulli numbers, and the transformed Hamiltonian is obtained via the Baker-Campbell-Hausdorff formula. Both the derivative and the BCH series are truncated to the normal-ordered two-body (NO2B) level, with numerical truncation criteria based on the magnitude of successive terms. The method is tested on the homogeneous electron gas (HEG) and the oxygen-16 nucleus using the White generator with Moller-Plesset energy denominators.

## Key numbers

* For the 2x2 matrix model, the Magnus expansion with a first-order Euler method converges to the exact ground-state energy to within machine precision regardless of step size, while direct integration of the SRG flow equation shows strong dependence on the Euler step size (e.g., plateaus at large s differ visibly for different delta s values).
* The truncation criteria for the Magnus derivative series uses the condition |(B_k/k!) * ||ad_Omega^k(eta)|| / ||Omega||| < epsilon_deriv, and for the BCH series uses |{ad^k_Omega(H)}_{0b} / k!| < epsilon_BCH, with the paper stating that final results are insensitive to large variations in these thresholds.
* The paper reports that the Magnus formulation provides "substantial memory savings and modest computational speedups" compared to standard IM-SRG, which requires storing 15-20 copies of the solution vector for high-order ODE solvers.

## Context

This work extends the in-medium similarity renormalization group (IM-SRG) approach previously developed by Bogner (2009), Tsukiyama (2010), and Hergert (2014) for ab-initio nuclear structure calculations. The Magnus expansion formulation addresses three specific difficulties of the standard IM-SRG: stiffness of the coupled ODEs, memory requirements of high-order solvers (e.g., the predictor-corrector solver of Shampine and Gordon), and the cost of evolving additional observables. The method enables more efficient computation of multiple observables beyond the ground-state energy, and the authors note that the connection to Moller-Plesset denominators will be used in future work to develop approximations beyond IM-SRG(2).
