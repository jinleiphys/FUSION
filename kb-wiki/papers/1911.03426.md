# Binomial acceptance corrections for particle number distributions in high-energy reactions

> The binomial acceptance correction procedure is studied for particle number distributions detected in high energy reactions in finite regions of the momentum space. We present acceptance correction formulas for scaled variance, skewness, and kurtosis. Our considerations include various specific types of particles - positively or negatively charged, baryons and antibaryons - as well as conserved charges, namely, the net baryon number and electric charge. A simple model with effects of exact charge conservation, namely the Bessel distribution, is studied in some detail where effects of multi-particle correlations are present. The accuracy of the binomial filter is studied with UrQMD model simulations of inelastic proton-proton reactions. Binomial acceptance correction procedure works well when used inside a small region of phase space as well as for certain efficiency corrections, in particular for constructing net proton fluctuation from net baryon ones. Its performance is less accurate when applied to obtain UrQMD fluctuations in a finite rapidity window from fluctuations in the full $4π$ space.

## Key claim

The binomial acceptance correction (BAC) procedure, which assumes each particle is detected with a fixed probability independent of event properties, works well for correcting fluctuations of particle numbers and conserved charges when applied inside a small region of phase space or for certain efficiency corrections (e.g., constructing net proton fluctuations from net baryon ones), but its performance is less accurate when applied to obtain fluctuations in a finite rapidity window from fluctuations in the full 4π space, as tested with UrQMD model simulations.

## Method

The authors derived analytic formulas for the binomial acceptance correction of scaled variance, skewness, and kurtosis for particle number distributions and conserved charge fluctuations (net baryon number and electric charge) using the method of characteristic functions. They applied these formulas to the Bessel distribution, which models exact charge conservation with multi-particle correlations, and tested the accuracy of the binomial filter by comparing its predictions to UrQMD transport model simulations of inelastic proton-proton collisions at √s = 6.3 GeV and √s = 62.4 GeV, using acceptance cuts in rapidity windows.

## Key numbers

- For a fixed number of particles in full phase space (N₀), the binomial acceptance gives ω_x[n] = 1 - x, Sσ_x[n] = 1 - 2x, and κσ²_x[n] = 1 - 6x(1 - x) for the accepted distribution.
- For net charge fluctuations with equal acceptance parameters x₊ = x₋ ≡ x, the scaled variance is ω_x[q] = (⟨N_ch⟩/Q)(1 - x), the skewness is Sσ_x[q] = (Q/⟨N_ch⟩)(1 - 2x), and the kurtosis is κσ²_x[q] = 1 + 3x(1 - x)(ω[N_ch] - 2).
- The skewness Sσ_x[q] equals zero at x = 0.5 for arbitrary values of Q and N_ch.
- The kurtosis κσ²_x[q] equals unity at x = 0 and x = 1, with the vertex of the parabolic dependence located at x = 1/2.
- UrQMD simulations were performed at √s = 6.3 GeV (SPS energy) and √s = 62.4 GeV (RHIC energy) for inelastic p+p collisions with Q = B = 2.

## Context

This work extends prior binomial acceptance correction results from Bzdak (2012), Kitazawa (2011), and Kitazawa (2016) by providing explicit formulas for scaled variance, skewness, and kurtosis for both single particle species and conserved charges, and by testing the BAC against UrQMD transport model simulations. The Bessel distribution analysis extends earlier canonical ensemble studies by Begun (2004) and Braun-Munzinger (2018) to higher-order cumulants. The results enable experimental comparisons of fluctuation measures (such as those measured by the NA61/SHINE and STAR collaborations) with theoretical predictions, while clarifying the limitations of the binomial filter for large acceptance regions in momentum space.
