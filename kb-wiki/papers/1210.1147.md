# Further microscopic studies of the fission barriers of heavy nuclei

> Two systematic sources of error in most current microscopic evaluations of fission-barrier heights are studied. They are concerned with an approximate treatment of the Coulomb exchange terms (known as the Slater approximation) in the self-consistent mean fields and the projection on good parity states (e.g., of positive parity for the spontaneous fission of an even-even nucleus) of left-right reflection asymmetric intrinsic solutions (e.g., around the second barrier). Approximate or unprojected solutions are shown to lead each to an underestimation of the barrier heights by a few hundred keV.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that two common approximations in microscopic fission-barrier calculations, the Slater approximation for Coulomb exchange and the neglect of parity projection for reflection-asymmetric intrinsic states, each systematically underestimate fission-barrier heights by a few hundred keV, contrary to the abstract's implication that these are merely "sources of error" without quantifying their systematic direction.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Hartree-Fock and Hartree-Fock-plus-BCS calculations using the SIII Skyrme interaction, comparing exact Coulomb exchange treatment with the Slater approximation across spherical and deformed nuclei, including fission paths for 238U and 70Se. For parity projection, they used the Highly Truncated Diagonalization Approach (HTDA) with the SkM* Skyrme interaction to construct correlated wave functions for 240Pu, then projected these onto positive parity states to evaluate the second fission barrier.

## Key numbers

- For 238U, correcting the Slater approximation adds about 310 keV to the first fission-barrier height and about 280 keV to the fission isomeric energy.
- For 240Pu, parity projection on positive parity enhances the second fission-barrier height by about 350 keV.
- The relative error in Coulomb exchange energy for deformed ground-state solutions is always larger than for spherically constrained solutions of the same nucleus, as shown for 24Mg, 48Cr, 106Mo, 178Hf, and 238U.
- For 90Zr and 298114, including BCS pairing reduces the Coulomb exchange error by much more than half compared to the closed-shell Hartree-Fock solutions.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the Slater approximation by Tondeur et al. (1984) and Skalski (2001) from spherical ground states to deformed nuclei and fission barriers, and it provides the first parity-projection calculations for fission barriers using HTDA correlated wave functions. The findings enable more accurate predictions of spontaneous fission half-lives, where a 1 MeV error in barrier height corresponds to roughly four orders of magnitude change in half-life, and they suggest that fits of energy density functionals (like the SkM* interaction of Bartel et al., 1982) that rely on fission-barrier data may need revision.
