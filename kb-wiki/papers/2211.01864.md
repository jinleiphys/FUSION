# Mirror beta transitions

> Beta decays of mirror nuclei differ in Q-value, but will otherwise proceed with transitions of similar strength. The current status is reviewed: Fermi transitions are all very similar, whereas Gamow-Teller transitions can differ in strength by more than a factor two. The main cause of the asymmetries appears to be binding energy differences between the mirror systems.

## Key claim

The paper reviews mirror beta transitions in nuclei with isospin \(T \geq 1\) and finds that while Fermi transitions are very similar across mirror pairs, Gamow-Teller transitions can differ in strength by more than a factor of two, with the main cause of the asymmetries appearing to be binding energy differences between the mirror systems rather than fundamental symmetry-breaking in the weak interaction.

## Method

The author compiles experimental log(ft) values for mirror beta decays from the Evaluated Nuclear Structure Data File (ENSDF) and the Experimental Unevaluated Nuclear Data List (XUNDL) databases, focusing on systems with isospin \(T = 1\) and \(T = 3/2\). The asymmetry between mirror decays is quantified using the parameter \(\Delta = \mathrm{log(ft)}_+ - \mathrm{log(ft)}_-\), where positive values indicate stronger \(\beta^-\) decay. The paper discusses the role of Coulomb effects, binding energy differences, and isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections, and examines specific cases including superallowed Fermi transitions and the strongly suppressed \(^{14}\mathrm{C}\) decay.

## Key numbers

- For \(T=1\) triplets, the asymmetry \(\Delta\) for \(0^+ \rightarrow 0^+\) Fermi transitions ranges from 0.0001(12) for \(A=34\) to 0.016(15) for \(A=54\), with all values consistent with zero within uncertainties.
- The largest asymmetry in the \(T=1\) non-Fermi transitions is for \(A=14\): \(\Delta = -1.756(9)\) (log(ft) for \(^{14}\mathrm{O}\) is 7.280(8) and for \(^{14}\mathrm{C}\) is 9.040(3)).
- For \(A=32\) (non-Fermi), \(\Delta = -1.16(18)\) (log(ft) for \(^{32}\mathrm{Cl}\) is 6.74(18) and for \(^{32}\mathrm{P}\) is 7.9002(2)).
- The experimental ratio of weak coupling constants is \(g_A/g_V = -1.2754(13)\) (PDG 2020).
- The experimental value for \(\mathcal{T}\) from superallowed Fermi decays is 6144(4) s (Hardy and Towner 2020).
- The isospin-symmetry-breaking correction \(\delta_C\) for superallowed Fermi decays ranges from 0.2% to 1.7%, increasing with mass number.

## Context

This review extends the long-standing study of isospin symmetry in nuclear beta decay, building on the foundational work edited by Wilkinson (1969) and more recent reviews of proton-rich nuclei by Blank (2008), Pfutzner (2012, 2022), and of Gamow-Teller transitions by Fujita (2011). The paper systematically compares mirror decays for systems with \(T \geq 1\), complementing the precision studies of superallowed Fermi transitions (Hardy and Towner 2020) and the use of \(T=1/2\) mirror decays for weak interaction tests (Naviliat-Cuncic and Severijns 2022). The results enable future theoretical work to disentangle Coulomb and charge-symmetry-breaking effects from structural differences due to binding energy, and highlight the need for more experimental data on proton-rich decays, especially for heavier nuclei.
