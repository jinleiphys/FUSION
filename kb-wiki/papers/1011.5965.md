# Qualitative and Quantitative Energy Loss?

> Despite the 300% systematic theoretical uncertainty associated with extracting qhat using current energy loss models due to the collinear approximation, and despite the uncertainties in the initial geometry and corrections due to the fluctuations in the initial overlap of a heavy ion collision, a simultaneous description of high-pT pi^0 RAA and v2 is not possible with current pQCD-based energy loss models. However a good description of out-of-plane RAA as a function of centrality is possible. Alternatively, energy loss models based on AdS/CFT give a better qualitative description of pi^0 and non-photonic electron RAA as well as high-pT ratios of antiprotons to pi^- production than those based on pQCD.

## Key claim
Despite the 300% systematic theoretical uncertainty in extracting qhat from collinear-approximation energy loss models, and despite uncertainties in initial geometry and fluctuations, a simultaneous description of high-pT pi0 RAA and v2 is not possible with current pQCD-based energy loss models; however, a good description of out-of-plane RAA as a function of centrality is possible, and AdS/CFT-based models give better qualitative descriptions of pi0 and non-photonic electron RAA as well as high-pT antiproton-to-pion ratios.

## Method
The author compares pQCD-based energy loss calculations (using the WHDG model, which includes both radiative and elastic energy loss) with AdS/CFT-based energy loss models (using a simple theta-function model where a particle escapes if its pathlength L is shorter than the thermalization length L_therm proportional to (C_R E/T)^(1/3)/T). The study examines the uncertainty in extracted qhat from the collinear approximation by varying the maximum gluon emission angle from 45 degrees to 90 degrees, and explores geometric uncertainties by comparing Glauber and KLN (Color Glass Condensate) initial conditions, as well as reaction-plane versus participant-plane calculations.

## Key numbers
- The systematic theoretical uncertainty in extracting qhat from current energy loss models due to the collinear approximation is 300%.
- Pion v2 measurements now extend out to approximately 14 GeV, where the discrepancy between pQCD-based theoretical calculations and data is large (approximately a factor of 2).
- The AdS/CFT thermalization length scales as L_therm proportional to (C_R E/T)^(1/3)/T, with the color factor coming to the power 1/3 (compared to power 1 in pQCD-based models).

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Horowitz and Cole (2009) on uncertainties in pQCD energy loss models and addresses the high-pT v2 puzzle raised by PHENIX measurements (Adare 2010). It contrasts pQCD-based approaches (such as the WHDG model of Gyulassy, Levai, and Vitev) with AdS/CFT-based models (following Gubser 2008 and Chesler 2008). The paper suggests that future LHC measurements of the double ratio of charm to bottom RAA (Horowitz 2007) could help determine whether the quark-gluon plasma is a strongly coupled liquid or a weakly coupled plasma, independently from hydrodynamic calculations.
