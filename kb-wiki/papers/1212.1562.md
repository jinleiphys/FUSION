# Inelastic cross sections and continuum transitions illustrated by $^{8}$Be results

> We use the two-alpha cluster model to describe the properties of $^{8}$Be. The $E2$-transitions in a two-body continuum can be described as bremsstrahlung in an inelastic scattering process. We compute cross sections as functions of initial energy for the possible $E2$-transitions from initial angular momenta $(0^+,2^+,4^+,6^+,8^+)$. The dependence on the exact shape of potentials is very small when the low-energy scattering phase-shifts are the same. We relate to practical observables where energies of the emerging alpha-particles are restricted in various ways. The unphysical infrared contribution is removed. We find pronounced peaking for photon energies matching resonance positions. Contributions from intra-band transitions are rather small although substantial (and even dominating) for initial energies between resonances. Structure information are derived but both ${\cal B}^{(E2)}$ values and electromagnetic transition rates are ambiguous in the continuum.

## Key claim

The authors compute inelastic E2-transition cross sections for the two-alpha cluster model of $^{8}$Be, showing that while the cross sections depend very little on the exact shape of the potential when low-energy phase shifts are identical, the extracted ${\cal B}^{(E2)}$ values and electromagnetic transition rates remain ambiguous in the continuum because they require well-defined initial and final continuum states that are not a priori available.

## Method

The authors use the two-body bremsstrahlung formalism for E2 radiation in $\alpha-\alpha$ scattering, solving the radial Schrodinger equation with two different gaussian potentials (Buck and Ali-Bodmer-d) that reproduce the same low-energy phase shifts. They compute cross sections via Eq. (1) for transitions between even angular momenta $(0^+,2^+,4^+,6^+,8^+)$, regularizing the divergent continuum wave functions with the Zel'dovich prescription and removing the unphysical infrared (soft-photon) contribution by excluding the sharp peak at very small photon energies.

## Key numbers

- The $0^+$ resonance in $^{8}$Be has an experimental energy of 0.0918 MeV and width $(5.57\pm0.25)\times 10^{-6}$ MeV; the Buck potential gives 0.091 MeV and $3.6\times10^{-5}$ MeV, while Ali-Bodmer-d gives 0.092 MeV and $3.1\times10^{-6}$ MeV.
- The $2^+$ resonance has experimental energy $2.94\pm0.01$ MeV and width $1.51\pm0.02$ MeV; Buck gives 2.88 MeV and 1.24 MeV, Ali-Bodmer-d gives 2.90 MeV and 1.27 MeV.
- The $4^+$ resonance has experimental energy $11.35\pm0.15$ MeV and width $\sim 3.5$ MeV; Buck gives 11.78 MeV and 3.57 MeV, Ali-Bodmer-d gives 11.70 MeV and 3.07 MeV.
- The $6^+$ and $8^+$ resonances are computed only theoretically: Buck gives $E_r=33.55$ MeV ($\Gamma=37.38$ MeV) and $E_r=51.56$ MeV ($\Gamma=92.38$ MeV); Ali-Bodmer-d gives $E_r=34.38$ MeV ($\Gamma=37.19$ MeV) and $E_r=53.65$ MeV ($\Gamma=93.74$ MeV).
- For the $2^+ \rightarrow 0^+$ transition with a 0.5 keV final energy window, the maximum cross section is about 14 nb; reducing the window to 5 eV decreases the maximum to less than 10 nb.
- The Zel'dovich parameter used is $\eta=0.02$ fm$^{-1}$; values up to twenty times smaller produce indistinguishable cross sections.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of inelastic $\alpha-\alpha$ scattering cross sections around the $2^+$ and $4^+$ resonances in $^{8}$Be by Langanke (1986), Kroupa (1987), and others, which assumed Breit-Wigner shapes for the resonances and neglected continuum background contributions. The authors provide cross sections covering all resonances up to $8^+$, systematically investigate the dependence on the chosen potential and final energy window, and demonstrate that the soft-photon (infrared) contribution must be removed to obtain physically meaningful results. This enables more precise comparison with future experiments probing continuum properties of $^{8}$Be and clarifies the inherent ambiguity in extracting ${\cal B}^{(E2)}$ values from continuum transitions.
