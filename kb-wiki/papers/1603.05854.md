# Formation of deuterons by coalescence: Consequences on the deuteron number fluctuations

> Two scenarios for cluster production have since long been discussed in the literature: i) direct emission of the clusters from a (grand canonical) thermal source or ii) subsequent formation of the clusters by coalescence of single nucleons. While both approaches have been successfully applied in the past it has not yet been clarified which of the two mechanisms dominates the cluster production. We propose to use recently developed event-by-event techniques to study particle multiplicity fluctuations on nuclear clusters and employ this analysis to the deuteron number fluctuations to disentangle the two production mechanisms. We argue that for a grand canonical cluster formation, the cluster fluctuations will follow Poisson distribution, while for the coalescence scenario, the fluctuations will strongly deviate from the Poisson expectation. We estimate the effect to be 10% for the variance and up to a factor of 5 for the kurtosis of the deuteron number multiplicity distribution. Our prediction can be tested in the beam energy scan program at RHIC as well as experiments at the FAIR and NICA facilities.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that measuring event-by-event fluctuations of deuteron multiplicity can distinguish between two production mechanisms: direct thermal emission from a grand-canonical source (which yields Poissonian fluctuations) versus coalescence of nucleons after kinetic freeze-out (which yields strongly non-Poissonian fluctuations). The authors estimate the effect to be a 10% deviation for the variance and up to a factor of 5 for the kurtosis, but note that the exact magnitude depends on whether proton and neutron fluctuations are correlated or independent.

## Method

The authors compare three scenarios: (i) direct grand-canonical deuteron production with Poissonian fluctuations; (ii) coalescence Model A, where deuteron formation probability is proportional to the squared proton number (assuming correlated proton and neutron fluctuations); and (iii) coalescence Model B, where deuteron formation probability is proportional to the product of independent Poisson-distributed proton and neutron numbers. For each scenario, they compute the scaled moments (variance/mean, skewness times sigma, kurtosis times sigma-squared) of the deuteron multiplicity distribution as functions of collision energy, fixing the coalescence parameter B to reproduce the mean deuteron-to-proton ratio parametrized from thermal model fits.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au collisions at 2.6 GeV beam energy in Model A: sigma-squared/mean = 1.609, S sigma = 2.218, kappa sigma-squared = 6.915
- For Au+Au collisions at 2.6 GeV beam energy in Model B: sigma-squared/mean = 1.308, S sigma = 1.616, kappa sigma-squared = 3.422
- The deuteron-to-proton ratio is parametrized as d/p = 0.8 * [sqrt(s_NN)/1 GeV]^{-1.55} + 0.0036
- The deviation from Poisson expectation is predicted to be 10% for the variance and up to a factor of 5 for the kurtosis
- The effect is predicted to be observable for energies up to about 5 GeV in all moments, and up to higher energies in kurtosis only

## Context

This work extends the longstanding debate between thermal statistical models (Andronic 2010) and coalescence models (Nagle 1996) for light cluster production in heavy-ion collisions. The authors build on recent event-by-event fluctuation techniques developed for the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program (Aggarwal 2010) and their own prior work on proton number fluctuations (Feckova 2015). The predictions are testable at RHIC-BES, FAIR, and NICA facilities, and the authors propose proton-deuteron multiplicity correlations as a complementary observable to further constrain the production mechanism.
