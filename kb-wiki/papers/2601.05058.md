# Benchmarking projected generator coordinate method for nuclear Gamow-Teller transitions

> In this work, we aim to achieve a minimal extension of the quantum-number projected generator coordinate method (PGCM) to describe Gamow-Teller (GT) transition strengths in even-even nuclei and to compute the NME of $2νββ$ decay. Within the PGCM framework, the wave functions of odd-odd nuclei are constructed as superpositions of neutron and proton quasiparticle configurations built on quasiparticle vacua constrained to have, on average, odd neutron and odd proton particle numbers. The angular momentum and particle numbers associated with the underlying mean-field states are restored through projection techniques. Using a shell-model Hamiltonian defined in the $fp$ shell, we assess the validity of this approach by benchmarking GT transitions in calcium and titanium isotopes, as well as the $2νββ$ decay of $^{48}$Ca to $^{48}$Ti, against exact solutions. For comparison, we also confront our results with those obtained from configuration-interaction calculations employing different particle-hole truncation schemes, both with and without in-medium similarity renormalization group (IMSRG) evolution.

# Benchmarking projected generator coordinate method for nuclear Gamow-Teller transitions

## Key claim
The projected generator coordinate method (PGCM) can be minimally extended to describe Gamow-Teller (GT) transition strengths and two-neutrino double-beta decay matrix elements in even-even nuclei, but the agreement with exact solutions deteriorates as the number of valence nucleons increases, and the PGCM overestimates the $2\nu\beta\beta$ nuclear matrix element of $^{48}$Ca by about 57%, primarily due to overestimation of the GT transition strength to the first excited state of $^{48}$Sc.

## Method
The wave functions of even-even nuclei are constructed as superpositions of quantum-number projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) states with different intrinsic quadrupole deformations, while odd-odd nuclei wave functions are approximated as superpositions of quantum-number projected two-quasiparticle configurations built on a HFB reference state constrained to have odd neutron and odd proton numbers on average. Angular momentum and particle numbers are restored through projection techniques, and the Hill-Wheeler-Griffin equation is solved to determine mixing weights. The method is benchmarked against exact shell-model solutions using the GXPF1A Hamiltonian defined in the $fp$ shell for calcium and titanium isotopes, and compared with configuration-interaction calculations employing different particle-hole truncation schemes.

## Key numbers
- The PGCM overestimates the $2\nu\beta\beta$ nuclear matrix element $M^{2\nu}$ for $^{48}$Ca decay to $^{48}$Ti by approximately 57%, with the cumulated NME showing an almost constant offset of approximately -0.053 relative to the shell-model value of about 0.090 MeV$^{-1}$.
- The reduced matrix elements from PGCM are $\langle {}^{48}\mathrm{Ti}(0^+_1)\Vert \sigma\tau^- \Vert {}^{48}\mathrm{Sc}(1^+_1)\rangle = 0.345$ and $\langle {}^{48}\mathrm{Sc}(1^+_1)\Vert \sigma\tau^- \Vert {}^{48}\mathrm{Ca}(0^+_1)\rangle = 1.568$, compared with shell-model values of 0.241 and 1.496 respectively.
- The experimental $Q_{\beta\beta}$ value for $^{48}$Ca is 4.2682 MeV, and the energy difference between the $^{48}$Sc ground state and $^{48}$Ca ground state is $E(6^+_1)-E(0^+_i)=0.503$ MeV.
- The effective NME derived from the experimental half-life $T^{2\nu}_{1/2}=5.3^{+1.2}_{-0.8}\times 10^{19}$ yr is $M^{2\nu}_{\rm eff}=0.035$, corresponding to $M^{2\nu}\simeq 0.0685$ MeV$^{-1}$ with a quenching factor $q\simeq 0.78$.
- The quasiparticle configuration truncation cutoff is set at $E_{\rm cut}=48$ MeV, which gives convergent solutions for excited states of odd-odd nuclei in the energy region of interest.

## Context
This work extends the PGCM framework, previously combined with in-medium similarity renormalization group (IMSRG) for $0\nu\beta\beta$ decay calculations by Yao (2018) and Belley (2020, 2024), to describe GT transitions and $2\nu\beta\beta$ decay. It benchmarks against exact shell-model solutions using the GXPF1A Hamiltonian from Honma (2004), and compares with configuration-interaction calculations at different truncation levels. Related work includes angular-momentum projected Hartree-Fock benchmarking by Lauber (2021), antisymmetrized molecular dynamics applications by Kanada-Enyo (2014), and isospin projection combined with PGCM by Konieczka (2016) and Morita (2018). The results establish PGCM as viable for beta-decay observables near closed shells and point toward systematic improvements through extending the generator-coordinate space and incorporating IMSRG evolution.
