# Background of the su(2)-Algebraic Many-Fermion Models in the Boson Realization

> The su(2)-algebraic many-fermion model is formulated so as to be able to get the unified understanding of the structures of three simple models: the single-level pairing, the isoscalar proton-neutron pairing and the two-level Lipkin model. Basic idea is to introduce an auxiliary su(2)-algebra, any generator of which commutes with any generator of the starting su(2)-algebra. With the aid of this algebra, the minimum weight states are completely determined in a simple forms. Further, concerning the two algebras, boson realization is presented. Through this formulation, the behavior of the total fermion in the Lipkin model is notably different of those in the other two models. As supplementary problem, the boson-fermion realization and the Lipkin model in the isovector pairing model are investigated.

## Key claim

The paper provides a unified formulation of three su(2)-algebraic many-fermion models (single-level pairing, isoscalar proton-neutron pairing, and the two-level Lipkin model) by introducing an auxiliary su(2)-algebra whose generators commute with the original su(2)-algebra, enabling a complete determination of minimum weight states; however, the behavior of the total fermion number in the Lipkin model is notably different from the other two models because in the Lipkin model the S-spin generator S̃₀ is not a linear function of the total fermion number operator Ñ, unlike in the pairing and isoscalar pairing models.

## Method

The authors start with a many-fermion system confined to 4Ω₀ single-particle states, divided into two sets P and \bar{P}. They construct the su(2)-generators S̃±,₀ (the "S-spin") and an auxiliary su(2)-algebra R̃±,₀ (the "R-spin") from bilinear fermion operators, with the condition that every generator of the R-spin commutes with every generator of the S-spin. The minimum weight states are determined by requiring both S̃₋|m₀⟩ = 0, S̃₀|m₀⟩ = −s|m₀⟩ and R̃₋|m₀⟩ = 0, R̃₀|m₀⟩ = −r|m₀⟩, with the relation s + r = Ω₀. The authors then apply the Schwinger boson representation and the Holstein–Primakoff representation to both algebras, constructing the physical boson space under the constraint r + s = Ω₀, and apply the general scheme to the three specific models.

## Key numbers

* The total number of single-particle states is 4Ω₀, where Ω₀ is an integer or half-integer.
* The eigenvalues of R̃₀ and S̃₀ on the minimum weight state satisfy r + s = Ω₀.
* For the pairing model, the degeneracy of the single-particle level is 2j + 1 = 2Ω, and the relation Ω → 2Ω₀ is used; the seniority number 2r ranges from 0 to N (for 0 ≤ N ≤ Ω) or from 0 to 2Ω − N (for Ω ≤ N ≤ 2Ω), with explicit even/odd rules given in equations (3.13a) and (3.13b).
* For the pairing model, the total fermion number N is related to the boson quantum numbers by N = 2(r + σ) for 0 ≤ N ≤ Ω, and N = 2(Ω − (r + σ)) for Ω ≤ N ≤ 2Ω.
* For the isoscalar pairing model, the degeneracy of each of the two single-particle levels is 2Ω = 2j + 1.

## Context

This work extends the long history of boson expansion theory for nuclear many-fermion systems, which began with the quasi-particle random phase approximation proposed by Marumori, Arvieu and Veneroni, and Baranger (1960) and was further developed by Belyaev and Zelevinsky, Marumori, Yamamura and Tokunaga, J. da Providência and Weneser, and Marshalek, as reviewed by Klein and Marshalek. The paper provides a unified framework for three well-known su(2)-algebraic models: the single-level pairing model, the isoscalar proton-neutron pairing model, and the two-level Lipkin model. The explicit use of the auxiliary R-spin algebra to determine minimum weight states is new, and the formulation enables future investigation of the boson-fermion realization and the Lipkin model within the isovector pairing model based on the so(5)-algebra.
