# Cold Bose gases with large scattering lengths

> We calculate the energy and condensate fraction for a dense system of bosons interacting through an attractive short range interaction with positive s-wave scattering length $a$. At high densities, $n>>a^{-3}$, the energy per particle, chemical potential, and square of the sound speed are independent of the scattering length and proportional to $n^{2/3}$, as in Fermi systems.

# Cold Bose gases with large scattering lengths

## Key claim
For a dense Bose gas with attractive short-range interactions and positive s-wave scattering length, at high densities where the gaseousness parameter \(n a^3 \gg 1\), the energy per particle, chemical potential, and sound speed become independent of the scattering length and scale as \(n^{2/3}\), analogous to a Fermi system, but only within the LOCV approximation, which assumes the Jastrow wave function remains a good description despite the gas not being in its ground state.

## Method
The authors apply the lowest order constrained variational (LOCV) method, using a Jastrow wave function \(\Psi_J = \prod_{i<j} f(\mathbf{r}_i - \mathbf{r}_j)\) with a pair correlation function \(f(r)\) that satisfies the two-body Schrödinger equation for \(r < d\) (the healing distance) and obeys the boundary condition \(f'(r=d)=0\). The interaction is replaced by the boundary condition \((rf)'/rf = -1/a\) at \(r=0\), and the healing distance \(d\) is determined self-consistently from the condition that the average number of correlated pairs equals one. The energy per particle is then \(E/N = \lambda/2\), where \(\lambda\) is the pair energy eigenvalue.

## Key numbers
- In the dense limit (\(a/r_0\) large), the LOCV solution gives \(kd = 2.798386\) for the lowest node, leading to energy per particle \(E/N = 13.33 \, \hbar^2 n^{2/3} / m\).
- The chemical potential in the dense limit is \(\mu = 22.22 \, \hbar^2 n^{2/3} / m\).
- The square of the sound speed in the dense limit is \(c_s^2 = 14.81 \, \hbar^2 n^{2/3} / m^2\).
- The condensate fraction is quenched when \(a \gtrsim r_0\), i.e., when \(n a^3 \simeq 1\).
- The three-body cluster probability in the dense limit is \(P_3(R) \sim 1\), independent of density; in the dilute limit it scales as \((4\pi n a^3 / 3)^2\).

## Context
This work extends the study of Bose gases from the well-known dilute regime (Lenz 1937; Huang, Lee, and Yang 1957; Wu 1959) into the dense regime where \(n a^3 \gtrsim 1\), using the LOCV method previously applied to hard-sphere gases and liquid helium. The results parallel earlier work on Fermi gases in the large-scattering-length limit by Heiselberg (2001). The predictions for density-independent scaling at high densities could be tested experimentally by tuning scattering lengths via Feshbach resonances, though the authors note that three-body molecule formation will limit the lifetime of the monoatomic gas.
