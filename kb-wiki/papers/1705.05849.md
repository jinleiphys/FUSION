# Sea Quarks Contribution to the Nucleon Magnetic Moment and Charge Radius at the Physical Point

> We report a comprehensive analysis of the light and strange disconnected-sea quarks contribution to the nucleon magnetic moment, charge radius, and the electric and magnetic form factors. The lattice QCD calculation includes ensembles across several lattice volumes and lattice spacings with one of the ensembles at the physical pion mass. We adopt a model-independent extrapolation of the nucleon magnetic moment and the charge radius. We have performed a simultaneous chiral, infinite volume, and continuum extrapolation in a global fit to calculate results in the continuum limit. We find that the combined light and strange disconnected-sea quarks contribution to the nucleon magnetic moment is $μ_M\,(\text{DI})=-0.022(11)(09)\,μ_N$ and to the nucleon mean square charge radius is $\langle r^2\rangle_E\,\text{(DI)}=-0.019(05)(05)$ fm$^2$ which is about $1/3$ of the difference between the $\langle r_p^2\rangle_E$ of electron-proton scattering and that of muonic atom and so cannot be ignored in obtaining the proton charge radius in the lattice QCD calculation. The most important outcome of this lattice QCD calculation is that while the combined light-sea and strange quarks contribution to the nucleon magnetic moment is small at about $1\%$, a negative $2.5(9)\%$ contribution to the proton mean square charge radius and a relatively larger positive $16.3(6.1)\%$ contribution to the neutron mean square charge radius come from the sea quarks in the nucleon. For the first time, by performing global fits, we also give predictions of the light and strange disconnected-sea quarks contributions to the nucleon electric and magnetic form factors at the physical point and in the continuum and infinite volume limits in the momentum transfer range of $0\leq Q^2\leq 0.5$ GeV$^2$.

## Key claim

The combined light and strange disconnected-sea quarks contribute a small but non-negligible amount to the nucleon magnetic moment (about 1%) and a significant negative contribution to the proton mean square charge radius (2.5(9)%) and a larger positive contribution to the neutron mean square charge radius (16.3(6.1)%), with the total disconnected-sea contribution to the nucleon mean square charge radius being about one-third of the difference between the electron-proton scattering and muonic atom values.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations using valence overlap fermions on four ensembles of (2+1) flavor domain-wall fermion gauge configurations from the RBC/UKQCD collaboration, with lattice volumes ranging from 24^3 x 64 to 48^3 x 96 and lattice spacings from 0.0828 fm to 0.1431 fm. They used 24 valence quark masses corresponding to pion masses from 0.135 GeV to 0.403 GeV, including one ensemble at the physical pion mass (139 MeV). Nucleon matrix elements were extracted using a hybrid joint two-state correlated fit of the standard three-point to two-point correlation function ratio and the summed ratio, with low-mode substitution and eigenmode deflation techniques to improve statistics. The disconnected-sea quark contributions to the electromagnetic form factors were obtained from self-contracted quark loops correlated with the valence quarks through the fluctuating gauge fields, and the magnetic moment and charge radius were extracted using a model-independent z-expansion fit to the form factor data in the momentum transfer range 0.051 to 1.31 GeV^2, followed by simultaneous chiral, infinite volume, and continuum extrapolation in a global fit.

## Key numbers

* Combined light and strange disconnected-sea quarks contribution to the nucleon magnetic moment: mu_M(DI) = -0.022(11)(09) mu_N
* Combined light and strange disconnected-sea quarks contribution to the nucleon mean square charge radius: <r^2>_E(DI) = -0.019(05)(05) fm^2
* Negative 2.5(9)% contribution to the proton mean square charge radius from sea quarks
* Positive 16.3(6.1)% contribution to the neutron mean square charge radius from sea quarks
* Light disconnected-sea quarks contribution to the electric form factor G^light-sea_E(Q^2) is 6 to 10 times larger than the strange quark contribution G^s_E(Q^2)
* Momentum transfer range for form factor predictions: 0 <= Q^2 <= 0.5 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations of disconnected-sea quark contributions to nucleon electromagnetic form factors by Abdel-Rehim (2013) at pion mass 370 MeV (which found results consistent with zero) and Green (2015) at pion mass 317 MeV (which found nonzero contributions). The present calculation is the first to include an ensemble at the physical pion mass (139 MeV) and to perform simultaneous chiral, infinite volume, and continuum extrapolations. The results demonstrate that disconnected-sea quark contributions cannot be ignored in lattice QCD calculations of the proton charge radius, as they account for about one-third of the discrepancy between the electron-proton scattering value and the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift measurement. This work enables future complete first-principles lattice QCD calculations including both connected and disconnected insertions to address the proton charge radius puzzle.
