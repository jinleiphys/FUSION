# Stability of Strutinsky Shell Correction Energy in Relativistic Mean Field Theory

> The single-particle spectrum obtained from the relativistic mean field (RMF) theory is used to extract the shell correction energy with the Strutinsky method. Considering the delicate balance between the plateau condition in the Strutinsky smoothing procedure and the convergence for the total binding energy, the proper space sizes used in solving the RMF equations are investigated in detail by taking 208Pb as an example. With the proper space sizes, almost the same shell correction energies are obtained by solving the RMF equations either on basis space or in coordinate space.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that, by carefully balancing the Strutinsky plateau condition with convergence of the total binding energy, a specific space size (N_f=12 in basis space or R_box=13 fm in coordinate space) yields stable and nearly identical neutron shell correction energies for 208Pb, but only within a limited range of smoothing parameters.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic mean field (RMF) equations for 208Pb using the PK1 parameter set, obtaining single-particle spectra either by diagonalization in a harmonic-oscillator basis (with variable fermion shell number N_f and fixed boson shell number N_b=20) or by solving coupled differential equations in a finite coordinate-space box (with variable box size R_box). The Strutinsky shell correction energy was then extracted from these spectra, and the stability of the plateau condition was tested as a function of the smoothing range gamma and the order M of the generalized Laguerre polynomial.

## Key numbers

- Neutron shell correction energy for 208Pb in basis space (N_f=12, N_b=20): -11.92 to -11.74 MeV within smoothing range 1.2 ≤ γ ≤ 1.8 (in units of ℏω₀ = 41A⁻¹/³(1 + ⅓(N-Z)/A) MeV)
- Neutron shell correction energy for 208Pb in coordinate space (R_box=13 fm): -12.00 to -11.80 MeV within smoothing range 1.2 ≤ γ ≤ 1.8
- Accuracy of total binding energy with N_f=12 in basis space: 0.04%
- Accuracy of total binding energy with R_box=13 fm in coordinate space: 0.01%
- Recommended fermion shell number: N_f=12 (with N_b=20 fixed)
- Recommended box size: R_box=13 fm
- Optimal order of generalized Laguerre polynomial: M=3

## Context

This work extends the Strutinsky shell correction method, previously applied with the phenomenological folded Yukawa potential (Bolsterli 1972, Moller 1995), to the self-consistent relativistic mean field framework. It resolves the ambiguity in choosing the basis space size or coordinate-space box size when extracting shell corrections from RMF single-particle spectra, enabling reliable calculations of shell correction energies for superheavy nuclei and their fission barriers, as previously investigated by Zhang and Wei (2003). The recommended space sizes (N_f=12, R_box=13 fm) match those found suitable for the folded Yukawa potential, suggesting a common optimal scale for the Strutinsky procedure across different mean-field models.
