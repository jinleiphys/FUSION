# Strongly intensive observable between multiplicities in two acceptance windows in a string model

> The strongly intensive observable between multiplicities in two acceptance windows separated in rapidity and azimuth is calculated in the model with quark-gluon strings acting as sources. The dependence of this observable on the two-particle correlation function of a string, the width of observation windows and the rapidity gap between them is analyzed. In the case with independent identical strings the model calculation confirms the strongly intensive character of this observable: it is independent of both the mean number of string and its fluctuation. For this case the peculiarities of its behaviour for particles with different electric charges are also analyzed. In the case when the string fusion processes are taken into account and a formation of strings of a few different types takes place in a collision, this observable is proved to be equal to a weighted average of its values for different string types. Unfortunately, in this case through the weight factors this observable becomes dependent on collision conditions and, strictly speaking, can not be considered any more as strongly intensive variable. For a comparison the results of the calculation of considered observable with the PYTHIA event generator are also presented.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the strongly intensive observable between multiplicities in two acceptance windows is truly strongly intensive only for independent identical strings, where it depends solely on individual string characteristics and not on the mean number of strings or its fluctuation; when string fusion processes are included, the observable becomes a weighted average of values for different string types and loses its strongly intensive character, becoming dependent on collision conditions.

## Method

The authors calculate the strongly intensive observable Sigma(n_F, n_B) using a model where quark-gluon strings act as particle-emitting sources, with multiplicities n_F and n_B measured in two acceptance windows separated in rapidity and azimuth. They analyze the dependence on the string two-particle correlation function, window widths, and rapidity gap, first for independent identical strings and then for cases with string fusion leading to multiple string types. The results are compared with calculations from the PYTHIA event generator.

## Key numbers

- For small observation windows (width delta_eta much less than correlation length eta_c), the observable Sigma = 1 + mu_0 * delta_eta * [Lambda(0) - Lambda(Delta_eta)], where mu_0 is the particle density from a single string and Lambda is the string two-particle correlation function
- The string correlation length eta_c decreases with collision energy: 1.52 at 0.9 TeV, 1.43 at 2.76 TeV, and 1.33 at 7.0 TeV
- The product mu_0 * Lambda_0 increases with energy: 0.73 at 0.9 TeV, 0.83 at 2.76 TeV, and 0.93 at 7.0 TeV
- For windows separated in both rapidity and azimuth with widths delta_eta = 0.2 and delta_phi = pi/4, the observable is calculated as a function of separation distances at three energies (0.9, 2.76, and 7.0 TeV)
- The scaled variance of the number of strings (mu_0 * omega_N) is 0.7 at 0.9 TeV, 1.4 at 2.76 TeV, and 2.1 at 7.0 TeV

## Context

This work extends the study of forward-backward correlations in high-energy hadronic interactions, following earlier work on string fusion and percolation phenomena by Biro (1984), Bialas (1986), and Braun and Pajares (1992, 1993). The strongly intensive observable was introduced by Gorenstein and Gazdzicki (2011) to suppress trivial "volume" fluctuations from fluctuations in the number of initial sources. The string model parameters are fitted using experimental pp ALICE data on forward-backward correlations between multiplicities at three initial energies (ALICE, 2015). The results enable future experimental tests of whether the observable retains its strongly intensive character in heavy-ion collisions where string fusion is expected to be significant.
