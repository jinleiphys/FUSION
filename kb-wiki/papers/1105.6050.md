# The $α-α$ fishbone potential revisited

> The fishbone potential of composite particles simulates the Pauli effect by nonlocal terms. We determine the $α-α$ fishbone potential by simultaneously fitting to two-$α$ resonance energies, experimental phase shifts and three-$α$ binding energies. We found that essentially a simple gaussian can provide a good description of two-$α$ and three-$α$ experimental data without invoking three-body potentials.

## Key claim

The authors claim that a simple Gaussian fishbone potential with only three fitting parameters can simultaneously describe both two-α and three-α experimental data without invoking any three-body potential, contradicting earlier findings that a strong three-body force was necessary.

## Method

The authors solved the Lippmann-Schwinger integral equation for two-body problems and Faddeev integral equations for three-body problems, approximating the short-range parts of the fishbone potential in a Coulomb-Sturmian basis. They performed a simultaneous fit to two-α resonance energies, experimental phase shifts in l=0, l=2, and l=4 partial waves, and three-α binding energies for the L=0 ground state, L=0 excited state, and L=2 bound state, using a local potential of Gaussian form plus a Coulomb term.

## Key numbers

- Two-α l=0 resonance state: E = 0.09158 − 0.000003 i MeV (from simultaneous fit to two-body and three-body data)
- Three-α L=0 ground state from simultaneous fit: E = −7.01 MeV (experimental value: −7.275 MeV)
- Three-α L=0 excited state from simultaneous fit: E = 0.51 MeV (experimental value: 0.375 MeV)
- Three-α L=2 bound state from simultaneous fit: E = −4.5 MeV (experimental value: −2.836 MeV)
- Best-fit parameters: a = 0.6266 fm⁻², v₀ = −101.78 MeV, β = 0.1881 fm⁻²
- For comparison, fit to two-body data only gave three-α L=0 ground state at E = −13.6 MeV, showing overbinding without three-body data

## Context

This work extends the fishbone model of Schmid (1981, 1983) for composite-particle interactions, which incorporates partially Pauli-forbidden states. It contradicts the earlier parametrization by Kircher and Schmid (1985) that gave E³α ≈ −15.5 MeV, and the conclusion of Oryu and Kamada (1994) that a huge three-body potential was needed. The new parametrization shows that a simple Gaussian can describe both two-α and three-α data without three-body forces, suggesting that cluster-model-based potentials deserve further consideration, potentially for nucleon-nucleon interactions.
