# New insights into the neutron electric dipole moment

> We analyze the CP-violating electric dipole form factor of the nucleon in the framework of covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory. We give a new upper bound on the vacuum angle, |θ_0| \lesssim 2.5 \cdot 10^{-10}. The quark mass dependence of the electric dipole moment is discussed and compared to lattice QCD data. We also perform the matching between its representations in the three- and two-flavor theories.

# New insights into the neutron electric dipole moment

## Key claim

The paper provides a new upper bound on the QCD vacuum angle, |θ₀| ≲ 2.5 × 10⁻¹⁰, but this bound is highly sensitive to cancellations between tree-level and loop contributions and is based on large-Nc arguments, so it should be considered with caution.

## Method

The authors analyze the CP-violating electric dipole form factor of the nucleon using covariant U(3)L × U(3)R baryon chiral perturbation theory, extending earlier calculations by one order in the chiral expansion (to third order, O(δ³)). They evaluate loop diagrams using infrared regularization, expand contributions up to and including O(δ³), and perform matching between the three-flavor and two-flavor representations of the theory.

## Key numbers

- Upper bound on the vacuum angle: |θ₀| ≲ 2.5 × 10⁻¹⁰ (derived from experimental limit on neutron EDM)
- Tree-level contribution to neutron EDM: |dₙᵗʳᵉᵉ| ≃ (2.9 ± 1.1) × 10⁻¹⁶ θ₀ e cm
- Loop contribution to neutron EDM: dₙˡᵒᵒᵖ = −3.0₋₀.₈⁺¹·¹ × 10⁻¹⁶ θ₀ e cm (with renormalization scale μ varied between Mρ and mΞ)
- Lower bound on theoretical estimate: |dₙᵗʰᵉᵒ| ≳ 1.1 × 10⁻¹⁶ θ₀ e cm
- Electric dipole radius of the neutron: ⟨r²ₑd⟩ₙ = −20.4 [1 − 0.67 + O(δ²)] θ₀ e fm²
- Matching relation for CP-violating pion-nucleon coupling: ḡπNN = −0.03 [−0.05] θ₀ (numbers in brackets from alternative values of bD, bF)

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Borasoy (2000) in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory and by Hockings (2005) in chiral SU(2) to higher order using covariant U(3)L × U(3)R baryon chiral perturbation theory. The results are compared to lattice QCD data from Shintani (2008) and show that third-order corrections reproduce the trend of lattice data even without the unknown tree contribution. The matching relations provide explicit representations for low-energy constants appearing in the two-flavor framework of Hockings (2005) and confirm the dimensional estimate for the CP-violating pion-nucleon coupling originally obtained by Crewther (1979).
