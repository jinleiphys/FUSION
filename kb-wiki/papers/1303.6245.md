# Contact and Momentum Distribution of the Unitary Fermi Gas

> A key quantity in strongly-interacting resonant Fermi gases is the contact $\mathcal{C}$, which characterizes numerous properties such as the momentum distribution at large momenta or the pair correlation function at short distances. The temperature dependence of $\mathcal{C}$ was measured at unitarity, where existing theoretical predictions differ substantially even at the qualitative level. We report accurate data for the contact and the momentum distribution of the unitary gas in the normal phase, obtained by Bold Diagrammatic Monte Carlo and Borel resummation. Our results agree with experimental data within error bars and provide crucial benchmarks for the development of advanced theoretical treatments and precision measurements.

## Key claim

The contact of the unitary Fermi gas in the normal phase is a slowly decreasing function of temperature at fixed density, with existing theoretical predictions contradicting each other even at the qualitative level; the authors report accurate data that agree with experimental results within error bars and discriminate between earlier contradictory predictions.

## Method

The authors employed Bold Diagrammatic Monte Carlo (BDMC), in which all skeleton Feynman diagrams for single-particle and pair propagators are sampled stochastically up to a maximal order N_max = 9, with convergence to the exact infinite-order limit obtained by conformal-Borel resummation of the divergent diagrammatic series. The contact was extracted directly from the fully dressed pair propagator Gamma via the relation C = -Gamma(r=0, tau=0^-), and the momentum distribution was computed with the large-momentum C/k^4 tail built in analytically by evaluating the lowest-order self-energy diagram numerically rather than by Monte Carlo sampling.

## Key numbers

* Contact density C/k_F^4 at T/T_F ≈ 0.19 (about 10% above the superfluid transition temperature T_c/T_F ≈ 0.17): the slope |d(C/k_F^4)/d(T/T_F)| is much smaller than unity in the region 0.19 ≲ T/T_F ≲ 1, then quickly increases to values ≳ 1 on approach to T_c.
* At the superfluid transition temperature T_c/T_F ≈ 0.17, the contact from Ref. [GoulkoProceeding2010] is C/k_F^4 = 0.110(1) by linear extrapolation or 0.105(5) by quadratic extrapolation.
* The virial expansion coefficients: c_2 = 1/pi (from the 2-body problem) and c_3 = -0.1399(1) (from the 3-body problem).
* At beta*mu = 0, the relative difference between the bold scheme and the ladder scheme for C*lambda^4 is smaller than 10^-4.
* The C/k^4 tail contains 10 to 15 percent of the particles.

## Context

This work extends the BDMC approach previously used to compute the equation of state of the unitary Fermi gas (Van Houcke et al., 2012) and provides controlled benchmarks that discriminate between earlier contradictory theoretical predictions. The results contradict the non-self-consistent T-matrix approximation (Strinati, 2011) and the Nozieres-Schmitt-Rink approach (Hu et al., 2008), which predicted a pronounced enhancement of the contact at low temperature interpreted as pseudogap physics, and instead agree remarkably with the self-consistent T-matrix approximation (Haussmann et al., 2007). The data also disagree with lattice Auxiliary-Field Quantum Monte Carlo results (Drut, 2012) and Determinantal Diagrammatic Monte Carlo results (Goulko et al., 2016), likely due to space-discretization errors in those methods. The results enable precision tests of theoretical treatments and reveal non-Fermi-liquid behavior in the momentum distribution, consistent with deviations from Fermi liquid theory seen in the equation of state (Ku et al., 2012).
