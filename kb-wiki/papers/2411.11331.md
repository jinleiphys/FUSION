# Quadrupole-hexadecapole correlations in neutron-rich samarium and gadolinium isotopes

> We present an extensive study of quadrupole-hexadecapole correlation effects in even-even Sm and Gd isotopes with neutron number $N=88-106$. The calculations are performed in the framework of the Gogny energy density functional (EDF) with the D1S parametrization and the $sdg$ interacting boson model (IBM). The quadrupole-hexadecapole constrained self-consistent mean-field potential energy surface is mapped onto the expectation value of the $sdg$-boson Hamiltonian. This procedure determines the parameters of the $sdg$-IBM Hamiltonian microscopically. Calculated excitation energies and transition strengths are compared to the ones obtained with a simpler $sd$-IBM, as well as with the experimental data. The Gogny-EDF mapped $sdg$-IBM reproduces spectroscopic properties of the studied nuclei as reasonably as in the case of the previous $sdg$-boson mapping calculations that were based on the relativistic EDF, indicating that the axial quadrupole-hexadecapole method is sound regardless of whether relativistic or nonrelativistic EDF is employed. The mapped $sdg$-IBM improves some of the results in lighter Sm and Gd isotopes compared to the mapped $sd$-IBM, implying the existence of significant hexadecapole correlations in those nuclei. For those nuclei with $N \geq 94$, hexadecapole effects are minor, and the only significant difference between the two boson models can be found in the description of $E0$ monopole transitions.

## Key claim
The Gogny-D1S energy density functional mapped onto the sdg interacting boson model reproduces spectroscopic properties of neutron-rich Sm and Gd isotopes as reasonably as previous calculations based on a relativistic EDF, indicating that the axial quadrupole-hexadecapole mapping method is sound regardless of whether relativistic or nonrelativistic EDF is employed. The improvement over the simpler sd-IBM is significant only in lighter isotopes (N ≤ 92) where hexadecapole correlations are substantial; for N ≥ 94, the main difference appears in E0 monopole transitions.

## Method
The authors performed axially symmetric quadrupole-hexadecapole constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the Gogny-D1S energy density functional to generate potential energy surfaces (PESs) for even-even Sm and Gd isotopes with neutron numbers N=88-106. These fermionic PESs were then mapped onto the expectation value of an sdg-boson Hamiltonian via a coherent-state formalism, determining the seven parameters of the sdg-IBM microscopically. The mapped sdg-IBM Hamiltonian was diagonalized using the ARBMODEL computer program to compute excitation energies and E2, E4, and E0 transition strengths, which were compared to both experimental data and results from a simpler 1D sd-IBM mapping along the β₄=0 line.

## Key numbers
- The HFB ground state quadrupole deformation β₂ᵐⁱⁿ starts at 0.2 and increases up to N=94, then decreases to β₂ᵐⁱⁿ=0.32 for N=106 isotopes.
- The HFB ground state hexadecapole deformation β₄ᵐⁱⁿ starts at 0.08, increases until N=94, then drops to β₄ᵐⁱⁿ=0.04 at N=106.
- The g-boson energy ε_g takes values between 1.1 MeV and 1.3 MeV across the isotopic chains.
- The parameter σ, accounting for quadrupole-hexadecapole coupling in the sdg-IBM, drops from σ=2.8 to σ=1.0 with increasing neutron number.
- For N=90 and 92 isotones, the sdg-IBM predicts larger B(E2) transition strengths for yrast states with J^π ≥ 6^+ compared to the sd-IBM, with σ=2.8 for ¹⁵²Sm, ¹⁵⁴Sm, and ¹⁵⁴Gd, and σ=2.1 for ¹⁵⁶Gd.
- The sdg-IBM predicts strong B(E4; 4₃⁺ → 0₁⁺) transitions in ¹⁵⁰Sm and ¹⁵²Gd, and for the 4₄⁺ state in ¹⁵⁴Gd.

## Context
This work extends previous sdg-IBM mapping studies by Lotina et al. (2023, 2024) that used the relativistic DD-PC1 functional, now employing the nonrelativistic Gogny-D1S EDF to test the EDF-independence of the quadrupole-hexadecapole mapping method. The results confirm that hexadecapole correlations are significant in lighter Sm and Gd isotopes (N ≤ 92) but minor for N ≥ 94, consistent with earlier Gogny HFB calculations by Kumar and Robledo (2023). The method enables future applications to odd-mass nuclei, heavier rare-earth nuclei up to W and Os, actinide regions, and extension to proton-neutron degrees of freedom (sdg-IBM-2) for mixed-symmetry states and scissor modes.
