# Phenomenology of pp->pp eta reaction close to threshold

> The recent high statistics measurement of the pp -> pp eta reaction at an excess energy Q=15.5 MeV has been analysed by means of partial wave decomposition of the cross section. Guided by the dominance of the final state 1S0 pp interaction (FSI), we keep only terms involving the FSI enhancement factor. The measured p-p and p-eta effective mass spectra can be well reproduced by lifting the standard on-shell approximation in the enhancement factor and by allowing for a linear energy dependence in the leading 3P0->1S0,s partial wave amplitude. Higher partial waves seem to play only a marginal role.

# Phenomenology of pp->pp eta reaction close to threshold

## Key claim

The measured pp and eta-p effective mass spectra at Q=15.5 MeV can be well reproduced by lifting the standard on-shell approximation in the proton-proton final-state interaction enhancement factor and by allowing for a linear energy dependence in the leading 3P0 -> 1S0,s partial wave amplitude, but only if the off-shell correction to the enhancement factor is included rather than relying on the simpler Watson-Migdal on-shell formula.

## Method

The author performed a partial wave decomposition of the pp -> pp eta cross section, retaining only terms involving the final-state interaction enhancement factor due to the dominance of the 1S0 pp interaction. The enhancement factor was calculated using the full off-shell expression derived from the Reid soft-core potential rather than the on-shell Watson-Migdal approximation, and the leading 3P0 -> 1S0,s partial wave amplitude was allowed to have a linear energy dependence parameterized by a coefficient x. The model was fitted to the experimental effective mass distributions at excess energy Q=15.5 MeV from Moskal et al.

## Key numbers

- Excess energy of the analyzed data: Q = 15.5 MeV
- Best fit value for the linear energy dependence parameter in the dominant amplitude: x = -0.514
- Best fit value for the d-wave admixture parameter in the eta-p distribution: y = 3.38
- At the maximal momentum k_max = 120 MeV, the on-shell enhancement factor |C_WM(k_max)|^2 calculated from the effective range formula is smaller by a factor of 2 compared to |C(k_max)|^2 obtained from the full off-shell expression, when both are normalized to the same peak height
- Experimental pp scattering length used in the effective range approximation: a = -7.83 fm
- Experimental pp effective range used: b = 2.8 fm

## Context

This work extends the analysis of near-threshold eta meson production in proton-proton collisions, following experimental measurements by the CELSIUS/WASA collaboration (Calen et al.) and Moskal et al. The paper improves upon the standard Watson-Migdal on-shell approximation (Watson, 1952; Migdal, 1955) by using the full off-shell enhancement factor, which had been shown to be necessary because the on-shell approximation gives a too rapid fall-off of the relative momentum distribution. The approach enables a quantitative description of both pp and eta-p effective mass distributions at the lowest available excitation energy, and provides a framework that could be extended to higher excitation energies where p-wave contributions may become important.
