# The Role of Nucleons in Electromagnetic Emission Rates

> Electromagnetic emission rates from a thermalized hadronic gas are important for the interpretation of dilepton signals from heavy-ion collisions. Although there is a consensus in the literature about rates for a pure meson gas, qualitative differences appear with a finite baryon density. We show this to be essentially due to the way in which the pi-N background is treated in regards to the nucleon resonances. Using a background constrained by unitarity and broken chiral symmetry, it is emphasized that the thermalized hadronic gas can be considered dilute.

## Key claim

The discrepancy between theoretical dilepton emission rates in a baryon-rich hadronic gas arises chiefly from how the πN background is treated relative to nucleon resonances; using a background constrained by unitarity and broken chiral symmetry, the thermalized hadronic gas can be considered dilute, and the larger background (rather than the smaller one used in some prior work) is uniquely specified by perturbative unitarity and broken chiral symmetry.

## Method

The authors expand the thermal expectation value of the electromagnetic current-current correlation function to first order in nucleon and pion densities, valid for temperatures T ≲ mπ and nucleon densities ρN ≲ 3ρ0. They compute the nucleon-density term from the spin-averaged forward Compton amplitude on the nucleon, using a one-loop πN background constrained by perturbative unitarity and broken chiral symmetry, with the Δ and N*(1520) resonances added as unitarized tree terms. The resulting Compton amplitude is fitted to experimental data on nucleon polarizabilities and Compton scattering, and then used to calculate dilepton and photon emission rates at T = 150 MeV and baryon chemical potential μ = 540 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon polarizabilities from the fit: (ᾱ + β̄) = 13 × 10⁻⁴ fm³, β̄ = 3.6 × 10⁻⁴ fm³, using couplings Q = 1.8/m and Q* = 2.2/m for the Δ and N*(1520) respectively.
- Radiative widths from the resonant part: Δ gives 0.27 MeV, N*(1520) gives 0.19 MeV.
- At T = 150 MeV and μ = 540 MeV (nucleon density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³), the dilepton rate from a smaller πN background (consistent with Rapp et al.) is about two times larger than from the larger background (consistent with the authors' prior work) in the intermediate mass range.
- The smaller background gives twice as many photons as the larger one at the same T and μ.
- The expansion parameter κ for each particle is less than 1/3 for T ≲ mπ and ρN ≲ 3ρ₀.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior analysis (Steele and Zahed, 1998) of dilepton rates in a baryon-free meson gas to the baryon-rich case, and directly addresses the discrepancy between their rates and those of Rapp et al. (1997, 1998) which used a smaller πN background and included medium modifications. The paper shows that both choices of background can fit the Compton scattering data on nucleons and nuclei, but that the larger background is uniquely specified by perturbative unitarity and broken chiral symmetry. The results enable future work using the framework of Bernard et al. (1996) to systematically include higher-order contributions from pion photo-production and pion knock-out while obeying unitarity and broken chiral symmetry.
