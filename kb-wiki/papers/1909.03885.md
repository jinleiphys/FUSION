# A new Monte Carlo-based fitting method

> We present a new fitting technique based on the parametric bootstrap method, which relies on the idea to produce artificial measurements using the estimated probability distribution of the experimental data. In order to investigate the main properties of this technique, we develop a toy model and we analyze several fitting conditions with a comparison of our results to the ones obtained using both the standard $χ^2$ minimization procedure and a Bayesian approach. Furthermore, we investigate the effect of the data systematic uncertainties both on the probability distribution of the fit parameters and on the shape of the expected goodness-of-fit distribution. Our conclusion is that, when systematic uncertainties are included in the analysis, only the bootstrap procedure is able to provide reliable confidence intervals and p-values, thus improving the results given by the standard $χ^2$ minimization approach. Our technique is then applied to an actual physics process, the real Compton scattering off the proton, thus confirming both the portability and the validity of the bootstrap-based fit method.

## Key claim

The paper presents a parametric bootstrap-based fitting method that, when systematic uncertainties are included in the analysis, is the only approach among those tested that provides reliable confidence intervals and p-values, improving upon standard chi-squared minimization and Bayesian approaches.

## Method

The method generates artificial bootstrap measurements by sampling each experimental data point from its estimated probability distribution (Gaussian for statistical uncertainties, uniform for systematic scale uncertainties), then performs standard chi-squared minimization on each bootstrap sample. This cycle is repeated a large number of times to reconstruct the probability distributions of the fit parameters and the expected goodness-of-fit distribution, from which p-values are computed. The method is tested on a Breit-Wigner toy model with simulated data and applied to real Compton scattering off the proton.

## Key numbers

- Toy model uses Breit-Wigner parameters: mu_0 = 0, Gamma_0 = 1, I_0 = 250
- 30000 simulated events generated, divided into 3 subsets with 100-bin histograms over x in [-4, 4]
- Systematic scale uncertainties for the three subsets: Delta_1 = 0.04, Delta_2 = 0.06, Delta_3 = 0.03
- Sampled parameter mu uncertainty tested at sigma_mu0 = k/100 with k = 3 and k = 20

## Context

This work extends the parametric bootstrap technique (Davidson and Hinkley, 1997) to fitting problems in nuclear physics, addressing limitations of standard chi-squared minimization when systematic uncertainties are present. The method is compared to the Hierarchical Bayesian Model approach described in a 2015 reference, and is applied to real Compton scattering off the proton, building on results previously published by Pasquini et al. (2019). The technique enables reliable confidence interval and p-value estimation in fits with complex systematic uncertainty structures where standard chi-squared tests fail.
