# Possibility of a superfluid in high multiplicity p-p and p-Pb collisions

> We consider the case where the saturated and unequilibriated initial stage of a high multiplicity p-p and p-Pb collision, may, due to high occupation number, display superfluid properties. The special case of an unequilibrated plasma of SU(2) gluons formed in the collision of two nuclei with deeply saturated gluon wave functions is considered. Color gauge symmetry is broken by the presence of a net non-vanishing, space-time dependent color expectation value. A two component scenario is envisioned, containing momentum states that are densely occupied below the saturation scale and a dilute normal fluid of excitations within this state. The spectrum of these excitations is shown to have an energy gap and thus it is hard to excite quanta out of the condensed state. Consequences for the stress energy tensor are outlined, in particular the possibility of a near invisid flow is discussed.

# Possibility of a superfluid in high multiplicity p-p and p-Pb collisions

## Key claim

In very high multiplicity p-p and p-Pb collisions at LHC energies, the initial glasma state may display superfluid properties due to high occupation number of gluons below the saturation scale, without requiring thermalization or Bose-Einstein condensation. The analysis is restricted to the small rapidity region at times 1/Λ_QCD ≫ τ ≫ 1/Q_S, and applies only to p-p and p-Pb collisions (not heavy-ion collisions).

## Method

The author considers an SU(2) gluon plasma formed from deeply saturated gluon wave functions, separating the gauge field into a classical condensate (soft modes below the saturation scale Q_S) and small-amplitude fluctuation fields (hard modes above Q_S). An effective Lagrangian is expanded to second order in the fluctuation field, and an event average over the product of background gauge fields is taken. The classical equations of motion for the background field are solved in axial gauge using an ansatz that decomposes the vector field into amplitude and phase components, yielding a diagonal interaction term that behaves as an effective mass term for the fluctuation field.

## Key numbers

- High multiplicity events are defined as total detected particles N ≳ 100, following experimental terminology.
- The saturation scale is given by Q_S ∼ α_S N_c x G(x)/(π R²), where x ∼ 10⁻⁴ for mid-rapidity collisions at √s ∼ 5 TeV.
- The time regime considered is 1/Λ_QCD ≫ τ ≫ 1/Q_S, requiring Q_S ≫ Λ_QCD.
- The superfluid phase may exist for τ > 1/Q_S at small rapidities.

## Context

This work extends the color glass condensate and glasma framework (McLerran 1993, Lappi 2006) to propose a microscopic mechanism for the near-inviscid fluid behavior observed in high multiplicity p-p and p-Pb collisions at the LHC. It differs from the proposal of gluonic Bose-Einstein condensation during thermalization (Blaizot 2011) by not requiring thermalization or BEC to demonstrate superfluidity. The mechanism provides a possible explanation for the short thermalization time (τ ∼ 0.6 fm/c) and small viscosity-to-entropy ratio used in hydrodynamic descriptions of these small systems (Bozek 2011, Shuryak 2013, Qin 2013). Future numerical simulations are needed to determine how long the superfluid phase persists and whether a normal fluid phase emerges.
