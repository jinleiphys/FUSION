# Non linearities in the harmonic spectrum of heavy ion collisions with ideal and viscous hydrodynamics

> We determine the non-linear hydrodynamic response to geometrical fluctuations in heavy ion collisions using ideal and viscous hydrodynamics. This response is characterized with a set of non-linear response coefficients that determine, for example, the $v_5$ that is produced by an $ε_2$ and an $ε_3$. We analyze how viscosity damps both the linear and non-linear response coefficients, and provide an analytical estimate that qualitatively explains most of the trends observed in more complete simulations. Subsequently, we use these nonlinear response coefficients to determine the linear and non-linear contributions to $v_1$, $v_4$ and $v_5$. For viscous hydrodynamics the nonlinear contribution is dominant for $v_4$, $v_5$ and higher harmonics. For $v_1$, the nonlinear response constitutes an important $\sim 25%$ correction in mid-central collisions. The nonlinear response is also analyzed as a function of transverse momentum for $v_1$, $v_4$ and $v_5$. Finally, recent measurements of correlations between event-planes of different harmonic orders are discussed in the context of non-linear response.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that higher harmonic flow coefficients $v_4$ and $v_5$ in heavy ion collisions arise predominantly from non-linear hydrodynamic mode-mixing (e.g., $v_5$ from $\epsilon_2$ and $\epsilon_3$ interactions) rather than from linear response to the corresponding initial spatial deformations, with the non-linear contribution dominating for viscous hydrodynamics.

## Method

The authors performed ideal and viscous (conformal second-order) hydrodynamic simulations of heavy ion collisions, initializing Gaussian entropy distributions deformed by specific cumulants (e.g., $\epsilon_2$, $\epsilon_3$, $\c_4$, $\c_5$) to isolate linear and non-linear response coefficients. They extracted the response coefficients $w_n/\epsilon_n$ (linear) and $w_{n(mk)}/(\epsilon_m\epsilon_k)$ (non-linear) as functions of transverse momentum, centrality, and shear viscosity, then combined these with Monte Carlo Glauber model correlations to predict the full $v_1$, $v_4$, and $v_5$ spectra.

## Key numbers

- For $v_1$, the non-linear response constitutes an important $\sim 25\%$ correction in mid-central collisions.
- The viscous damping of linear response coefficients scales approximately as $-\Delta w_n/w_n^{\rm id} \propto n^2 \, (\eta/s)$, with ratios $4:4:9:16:25$ for $v_1:v_2:v_3:v_4:v_5$.
- The non-linear response coefficient $w_{5(23)}$ is damped with a rate $\Gamma_{5(23)} \sim \Gamma_{2,2} + \Gamma_{3,3}$, corresponding to a scaling of $-\Delta w_{5(23)}/w_{5(23)}^{\rm id} \propto 13 \, (\eta/s)$.
- The freeze-out temperature used in simulations is $T_{\rm fo} = 150\,{\rm MeV}$.
- For the non-linear response determination, the initial deformations were set to $\epsilon_2=\epsilon_3=0.02$.

## Context

This work extends the findings of Gardim et al. (2011) who first quantified non-linear contributions to higher harmonics in ideal hydrodynamics, and complements event-by-event simulations by Heinz and Qiu (2011) which showed decorrelation of $\Psi_4$ and $\Psi_5$ from initial spatial deformations. The paper provides a systematic framework of non-linear response coefficients that enables disentangling linear and non-linear contributions to flow harmonics, and enables quantitative predictions for how viscosity affects each harmonic differently, which is essential for extracting $\eta/s$ from global flow analyses.
