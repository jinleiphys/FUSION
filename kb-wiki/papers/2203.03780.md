# Norm operator method for boson expansions

> We propose a new boson expansion theory that does not premise the closed-algebra approximation, indispensable for formulation until now, as an extension of the conventional practical boson expansion methods that have tried to elucidate nuclear collective motion, which is a method that allows the closed-algebra approximation not to be used or to be used appropriately, enables us to obtain the boson expansion easier, and reproduces the fermion subspace onto the boson subspace more faithfully than the conventional practical methods. The two-phonon norm matrix composed of all the phonon excitation modes is investigated in detail, which reveals the mechanism, essential for the boson expansion methods, of how we should construct the fermion subspace to be mapped from the whole fermion space. The conventional practical boson expansion methods have applied the closed-algebra approximation improperly to strengthen the effect of the Pauli principle inappropriately, which should be replaced by those that do not use that approximation or use it properly.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new boson expansion theory that does not require the closed-algebra approximation as a prerequisite, but can either avoid it or use it appropriately, thereby reproducing the fermion subspace onto the boson subspace more faithfully than conventional practical methods.

## Method

The author constructs a mapping operator that limits both the number of phonon excitations and the sorts of phonon excitation modes, expressed as the product of a power of the norm operator and a common mapping part. The two-phonon norm matrix composed of all phonon excitation modes is investigated in detail to reveal how the fermion subspace should be constructed from the whole fermion space. The method yields both Hermitian and non-Hermitian types of boson expansions and provides higher-order terms not previously obtained by mapping methods.

## Key numbers

- The maximum phonon excitation number is denoted \(N_{max}\), and the mapping operator is defined for \(0 \leq N \leq N_{max}\).
- The norm matrix eigenvalues \(z_a(N)\) become positive or zero; eigenvectors with zero eigenvalue (\(a_0\)) correspond to null state vectors.
- For the case where the maximum phonon excitation number is 1, the mapping operator becomes \(U_\xi = \bar U = \vert 0)\langle 0\vert + \sum_\mu \vert\mu)\langle \mu\vert\), and the boson expansions yield \((X_\mu)_B = b_\mu\) and \((X_\mu^\dagger)_B = b_\mu^\dagger\).

## Context

This work extends the conventional practical boson expansion methods such as the normal-ordered linked-cluster expansion (NOLCEXP) by Kishimoto and Tamura (1983) and the Dyson boson expansion theory (DBET) by Taniguchi (2001), which have relied on the closed-algebra approximation. The new method enables incorporation of non-collective excitation modes into the coefficients of boson expansions consisting only of collective bosons, avoiding the convergence problems that plagued earlier approaches. It also connects to the boson-fermion expansion theory of Taniguchi and Matsuyanagi (1990, 1991), allowing comparison between the two frameworks.
