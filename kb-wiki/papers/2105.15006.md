# The N=50 and Z=28 shell closure revisited

> Recent experiments performed in neutron-rich copper isotopes have revealed a crossing in the nucleus $^{75}$Cu between the $3/2^-$ and $5/2^-$ levels, which correspond to the ground-state and the first excited state in isotopes with mass number below $A =75$. Due to the strong single-particle character of these states, this scenario can be investigated through the analysis of the proton spectrum provided by mean-field models in nickel isotopes with neutron numbers between $N$=40 and $N$=50. In this work we show that the aforementioned crossing is mainly driven by the mean-field provided by the effective nucleon-nucleon and spin-orbit interactions. We also analyze the impact of the tensor interaction, and find that in some mean-field models it is essential to reproduce the crossing of the 2$p_{3/2}$ and 1$f_{5/2}$ proton single-particle levels, as in the case of the SAMi-T Skyrme force and the D1M Gogny interaction, whereas in other cases, as for example the SLy5 Skyrme force, a reasonable tensor force appears to be unable to modify the mean-field enough to reproduce this level crossing. Finally, in the calculations performed with the so-called simple effective interaction (SEI), it is shown that the experimental data in nickel and copper isotopes considered in this work can be explained satisfactorily without any explicit consideration of the tensor interaction.

# The N=50 and Z=28 shell closure revisited

## Key claim
The crossing between the 2p3/2 and 1f5/2 proton single-particle levels in neutron-rich nickel isotopes, which underlies the ground-state spin inversion observed in copper isotopes beyond A=73, can be reproduced by the central and spin-orbit components of the mean field alone without explicit tensor interaction, as demonstrated by the simple effective interaction (SEI) model, contrary to claims that a tensor force is essential.

## Method
The authors performed quasilocal density functional theory (QLDFT) calculations of proton and neutron single-particle energies in nickel isotopes from A=68 to A=78 using the SEI, the D1M Gogny force (with and without tensor terms), and several Skyrme forces (SAMi-T, SLy5 with tensor parametrizations). For copper isotopes from 69Cu to 79Cu, they carried out mean-field calculations with blocking in the uniform filling approximation, neglecting deformation effects, to extract ground-state spins and first-excited-state energies. The QLDFT results were benchmarked against full Hartree-Fock calculations in coordinate space for the Gogny forces.

## Key numbers
- The SEI model predicts the 2p3/2 and 1f5/2 proton level crossing in nickel isotopes at neutron number N=46 (nucleus 74Ni).
- The splitting between the 1f5/2 and 1f7/2 proton levels in nickel decreases by 0.75 MeV from N=40 to N=50 in the SEI calculation.
- The 2p3/2-2p1/2 proton gap in nickel is reduced by 0.26 MeV from 68Ni to 78Ni in the SEI model.
- For 79Cu, the SEI predicts the 3/2- first excited state at 525 keV above the 5/2- ground state, compared to the experimental value of 660 keV from Olivier (2017).
- For 75Cu, the SEI predicts the 3/2- ground state with the 5/2- first excited state at 72 keV, compared to the experimental value of 62 keV from Flanagan (2009).
- For 77Cu, the SEI predicts the 5/2- ground state with the 3/2- first excited state at 246 keV, compared to the experimental value of 295 keV from Sahin (2017).
- The D1M Gogny force without tensor terms predicts the 2p3/2 and 1f5/2 proton levels almost coincide at 78Ni but does not produce the crossing.
- The D1MTd Gogny force (with tensor terms) predicts the crossing at 74Ni.
- The SAMi-T Skyrme force predicts the crossing at 72Ni.
- The SLy5 Skyrme force with tensor terms from Colo (2007) or Grasso (2013) fails to reproduce the crossing.

## Context
This work extends the discussion of shell evolution near the doubly magic nucleus 78Ni, following experimental measurements of ground-state spins in neutron-rich copper isotopes by Flanagan (2009), Stefanescu (2008), and Olivier (2017). It directly addresses the claim by Brink and Stancu (2018) that a tensor term is unavoidable in Skyrme forces to reproduce the proton level crossing in nickel isotopes. The paper shows that the SEI model, previously applied to binding energies of 620 even-even nuclei by Behera (2013, 2016), can reproduce the crossing without tensor terms, suggesting that the monopole component of the central interaction can mask tensor effects. This enables future mean-field studies of exotic nuclei near N=50 and Z=28 without requiring explicit tensor parametrizations, and provides a benchmark for comparing central versus tensor contributions to shell evolution.
