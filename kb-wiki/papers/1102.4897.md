# Model dependence of single-energy fits to pion photoproduction data

> Model dependence of multipole analysis has been explored through energy-dependent and single-energy fits to pion photoproduction data. The MAID energy-dependent solution has been used as input for an event generator producing realistic pseudo data. These were fitted using the SAID parametrization approach to determine single-energy and energy-dependent solutions over a range of lab photon energies from 200 to 1200 MeV. The resulting solutions were found to be consistent with the input amplitudes from MAID. Fits with a $χ$-squared per datum of unity or less were generally achieved. We discuss energy regions where consistent results are expected, and explore the sensitivity of fits to the number of included single- and double-polarization observables. The influence of Watson's theorem is examined in detail.

## Key claim

The model dependence of multipole analyses in pion photoproduction can be systematically studied by generating pseudo data from a known energy-dependent solution (MAID MD07) and then refitting it with a different parametrization (SAID), but the resulting single-energy solutions are only consistent with the input amplitudes when sufficient polarization observables are included, and the degree of model independence varies significantly across different energy regions.

## Method

The authors generated pseudo data for single and double polarization observables of the γp → π⁰p and γp → π⁺n reactions using a Monte Carlo event generator based on the MAID MD07 energy-dependent solution, with statistical uncertainties comparable to those expected at modern electromagnetic facilities. These pseudo data were then fitted using the SAID parametrization approach to extract both energy-dependent and single-energy multipole solutions over lab photon energies from 200 to 1200 MeV. The single-energy fits were performed in narrow energy bins (5-20 MeV) with constraints from the energy-dependent solution, and the influence of Watson's theorem was examined by comparing fits where multipole phases were either fixed to πN elastic scattering phases or allowed to vary.

## Key numbers

- Lab photon energy range covered: 200 to 1200 MeV (total CM energy W from 1120 to 1770 MeV)
- At Eγ = 340 MeV (Δ(1232) resonance region), the SE4 fit to MD07 pseudo data gave M1+^(3/2) = 51.91(0.06) millifermi, compared to MD07 value of 52.0 millifermi and SP09 value of 55.25 millifermi
- At Eγ = 340 MeV, the E0+^(1/2) multipole from SE4 fit was 9.40(0.08) millifermi, compared to MD07 value of 9.36 millifermi
- At 600 MeV, the M1-^(1/2) phase from SE4p fit (4 observables) was 68.2(2.4) degrees, while SE8p fit (8 observables) gave 73.4(1.6) degrees, compared to MD07 value of 72.7 degrees
- At 600 MeV, the M1-^(3/2) phase from SE4p fit was 176.8(6.5) degrees, while SE8p gave 167.5(1.8) degrees, compared to MD07 value of 163.9 degrees
- Pseudo data generated with beam polarization PT = 60% for linearly polarized photons and P⊙ = 70% for circularly polarized photons
- Target polarization assumed to be P = 80%; recoil nucleon analyzing power assumed to be A = 20%
- Approximately 5×10⁶ events generated per observable over the full energy range

## Context

This work extends the long-standing multipole analysis programs of the MAID group (Drechsel et al., 2007) and SAID group (Arndt et al., 2002) by systematically comparing their methodologies using controlled pseudo data, thereby isolating model dependence from experimental systematic uncertainties. The study enables future analyses to identify energy regions where single-energy fits can be considered relatively model-independent, and demonstrates that including double-polarization observables (such as beam-target observables E, F, G, H) significantly improves the reliability of extracted multipoles compared to fits using only the unpolarized cross section and single-spin observables (dσ/dΩ, Σ, T, P).
