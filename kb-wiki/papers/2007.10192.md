# Near-threshold $K^*(892)^+$ meson production in the interaction of $π^-$ mesons with nuclei

> We study the inclusive strange vector meson $K^*(892)^+$ production in ${π^-}A$ reactions at near-threshold laboratory incident pion momenta of 1.4--2.0 GeV/c within a nuclear spectral function approach. The approach accounts for incoherent primary $π^-$ meson--proton ${π^-}p \to {K^*(892)^+}Σ^-$ production processes as well as the influence of the scalar $K^*(892)^+$--nucleus potential (or the $K^*(892)^+$ in-medium mass shift) on these processes. We calculate the absolute differential and total cross sections for the production of $K^*(892)^+$ mesons off carbon and tungsten nuclei at laboratory angles of 0$^{\circ}$--45$^{\circ}$ and at these momenta within five scenarios for the above shift. We show that the $K^*(892)^+$ momentum distributions and their excitation functions (absolute and relative) possess a high sensitivity to changes in the in-medium $K^*(892)^+$ mass shift in the low-momentum region of 0.1--0.6 GeV/c. Therefore, the measurement of such observables in a dedicated experiment at the GSI pion beam facility in the near-threshold momentum domain will allow to get valuable information on the $K^*(892)^+$ in-medium properties.

# Near-threshold $K^*(892)^+$ meson production in the interaction of $\pi^-$ mesons with nuclei

## Key claim

The momentum distributions and excitation functions of $K^*(892)^+$ mesons produced in $\pi^- A$ reactions show high sensitivity to the in-medium mass shift of the $K^*(892)^+$ meson, but only in the low-momentum region of 0.1--0.6 GeV/c; the sensitivity is strongest at subthreshold beam momenta (1.4--1.7 GeV/c) where absolute cross sections are very small, while at near-threshold momenta (1.7--2.0 GeV/c) the cross sections are measurable but the sensitivity to the mass shift is somewhat reduced.

## Method

The study uses a nuclear spectral function approach based on a first-collision model with eikonal approximation, previously developed for $\phi$, $\eta'$, and $\Lambda(1520)$ production. The model accounts for incoherent primary $\pi^- p \to K^*(892)^+ \Sigma^-$ production processes and incorporates the $K^*(892)^+$ in-medium mass shift via an average effective mass, with five scenarios for the scalar potential $V_0$ at normal nuclear density $\rho_0$: -40, -20, 0, +20, and +40 MeV. The calculations include distortion of the incident pion, attenuation of $K^*(892)^+$ flux due to decays (with vacuum decay width $\Gamma_{K^*}=50$ MeV), and use a parametrization of scarce experimental data for the elementary $\pi^- p \to K^*(892)^+ \Sigma^-$ cross section.

## Key numbers

- Incident pion momenta studied: 1.4, 1.7, and 2.0 GeV/c, with the free production threshold at 1.84 GeV/c.
- For $\pi^-$ momentum 2.0 GeV/c and $K^*(892)^+$ momentum 0.3 GeV/c on $^{12}$C, the yield enhancement factor from $V_0=+20$ MeV to $V_0=+40$ MeV is about 4.1; from $V_0=+20$ to $V_0=0$ MeV about 2.8; from $V_0=0$ to $V_0=-20$ MeV about 2.2; from $V_0=-20$ to $V_0=-40$ MeV about 1.8.
- For the same kinematics on $^{184}$W, the corresponding enhancement factors are about 14.0, 3.5, 2.1, and 1.7.
- At beam momentum 1.4 GeV/c and $K^*(892)^+$ momentum 0.3 GeV/c, enhancement factors on $^{12}$C are about 5.0, 3.3, 2.6, and 2.5; on $^{184}$W about 6.0, 3.3, 2.4, and 2.0.
- Ratio of total cross sections in the low-momentum region (0.1--0.6 GeV/c) for $V_0=-40$ MeV versus $V_0=+40$ MeV: at 1.4 GeV/c, about 21.0 for $^{12}$C and 14.0 for $^{184}$W; at 1.7 GeV/c, about 5.0 for $^{12}$C and 10.0 for $^{184}$W; at 2.0 GeV/c, about 3.0 for $^{12}$C and 4.0 for $^{184}$W.
- The on-shell $\pi^- p \to K^*(892)^+ \Sigma^-$ cross section at 2.0 GeV/c with free proton at rest is approximately 31 $\mu$b.
- The $K^*(892)^+$ decay mean free path is 4 fm for typical values $p'_{K^*} \approx m^*_{K^*}$ and $\Gamma_{K^*}=50$ MeV.
- Total $K^*(892)^+$ production cross sections in the near-threshold region (1.7--2.0 GeV/c) have measurable strength of about 0.1--5 $\mu$b.

## Context

This work extends the spectral function approach previously applied to $\phi$ meson production (Paryev, 2019) and $\eta'$ meson production to the strange vector meson $K^*(892)^+$, motivated by theoretical predictions of its in-medium mass shift from chiral effective field theory (Kolomeitsev and Lutz, 2004; Tolos et al., 2004), the quark-meson coupling model (Tsushima et al., 1998), and the three-flavor extended linear sigma model (Kumar and Jido, 2019). The calculations are designed as guidance for future experiments at the GSI pion beam facility using the HADES spectrometer, which has previously measured $\phi$ meson production on $^{12}$C and $^{184}$W targets (HADES Collaboration, 2019). The results enable extraction of the $K^*(892)^+$ in-medium mass shift from measurements of low-momentum $K^*(892)^+$ yields and excitation functions, complementing studies of chiral symmetry restoration via the $K^*(892)$-$K_1(1270)$ mass difference proposed for J-PARC (Kim et al., 2014).
