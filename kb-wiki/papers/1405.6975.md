# Fluctuations of pion flow harmonics and HBT correlation functions in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions

> We investigate the fluctuations of pion elliptic flow, triangular flow, and Hanbury-Brown-Twiss (HBT) correlation functions for the hydrodynamic sources with fluctuating initial conditions in the heavy ion collisions of the Au-Au at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV and the Pb-Pb at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} =2.76$ TeV. A method based on event-subcollection analysis is used to detect these fluctuations in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions. We introduce a granularity length to describe the granular inhomogeneity of the initial sources, and investigate its relationships with the fluctuations of the flow harmonics and HBT correlation functions. Our investigations indicate that the fluctuations of the triangular flow of event subcollections are sensitive to the granularity length of the initial source. This dependence may provide a way to study the granular inhomogeneity of the initial source through the analyses of the fluctuations of triangular flow in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The fluctuations of triangular flow in event subcollections are sensitive to the granularity length of the initial source, providing a potential way to study the granular inhomogeneity of the initial source, but this relationship is not monotonic when comparing collisions at greatly different energies (RHIC and LHC).

## Method

The authors used HIJING to generate fluctuating initial conditions for Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV and Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV, then evolved the systems using (2+1)-dimensional ideal relativistic hydrodynamics with Bjorken longitudinal boost invariance and the s95p-PCE equation of state. They introduced a granularity length L_xi to quantify the granular inhomogeneity of the initial sources and analyzed fluctuations of elliptic flow, triangular flow, and HBT correlation functions using an event-subcollection method with fluctuation distributions dN/df.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of fluctuating initial conditions in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, building on the IP-Glasma FIC approach of Schenke (2012) and the hydrodynamic modeling of Gale (2013) which reproduced v_2 through v_5. The authors also draw on prior work on single-event HBT correlation function fluctuations for granular sources by Wong and Zhang (2004) and Ren and Zhang (2008). The results suggest that triangular flow fluctuations could serve as a probe of initial-state granular inhomogeneity, though the granularity length parameter L_xi has limitations when comparing collisions at very different energies.
