# Bremsstrahlung radiation by a tunneling particle

> We study the bremsstrahlung radiation of a tunneling charged particle in a time-dependent picture. In particular, we treat the case of bremsstrahlung during alpha-decay, which has been suggested as a promissing tool to investigate the problem of tunneling times. We show deviations of the numerical results from the semiclassical estimates. A standard assumption of a preformed particle inside the well leads to sharp high-frequency lines in the bremsstrahlung emission. These lines correspond to "quantum beats" of the internal part of the wavefunction during tunneling arising from the interference of the neighboring resonances in the well.

# Bremsstrahlung radiation by a tunneling particle

## Key claim
The bremsstrahlung spectrum from a tunneling charged particle (specifically an alpha-particle in nuclear alpha-decay) shows large deviations from classical and semiclassical estimates, and the standard assumption of a preformed particle inside the well leads to sharp high-frequency lines corresponding to "quantum beats" from interference of neighboring resonances, but the authors do not attempt to compare with experimental data because for realistic cases like 210Po the required numerical time steps (about 10^30) are computationally impossible.

## Method
The authors solve the time-dependent Schrödinger equation for an alpha-particle in a potential well plus Coulomb barrier, using a tri-diagonal matrix inversion method with space discretization of 0.05 fm and time steps of Δt = mc(Δx)²/ħ. They calculate the radial momentum expectation value from the time-dependent wavefunction and obtain the bremsstrahlung spectrum via its Fourier transform. For a simplified square-well-plus-barrier system, they also use an analytic expansion of the time-dependent wavefunction in terms of continuum states.

## Key numbers
- For Eα = 23.1 MeV, the width of the peak in the bremsstrahlung spectrum at Eγ ≈ 8.5 MeV is ΔE = 0.2 MeV
- For Eα = 20 MeV, the width of the peak is ΔE = 0.02 MeV
- The barrier height used is 27.6 MeV, with nuclear radius R0 = 8.75 fm
- For the square-well-plus-barrier system with Eα = 24.2 MeV, peaks appear at Eγ = 9.1 MeV and 11.1 MeV, corresponding to energy differences of (24.2 − 15.7 = 8.5) MeV and (35.4 − 24.2 = 11.2) MeV between resonances
- The space discretization step is Δx = 0.05 fm
- The semiclassical bremsstrahlung spectrum for a tunneling particle varies as Eγ⁻¹ for low photon energies and as [exp(−2Eγd/ħv₁)]/Eγ for high photon energies

## Context
This work extends the theoretical discussion of bremsstrahlung during alpha-decay, which was triggered by experiments by Kasagi et al. (1997) and previously treated semiclassically by Dyakonov and Gornyi (1996) and quantum-mechanically by Papenbrock and Bertsch (1998) and Takigawa et al. (1998). The authors show that time-dependent effects, neglected in perturbation theory approaches, produce quantum beats between neighboring resonances that generate sharp peaks in the bremsstrahlung spectrum. This enables future experimental investigation of alpha-particle preformation states inside nuclei and the dynamics of tunneling times, though the authors note that the coupled particle-photon equations they formulate (following Pauli and Fierz, 1938) remain to be solved for a full treatment.
