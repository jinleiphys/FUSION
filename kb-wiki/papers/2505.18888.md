# Constraints on maximum neutron star mass from proto-neutron star evolution

> A proto-neutron star (PNS) gets formed after a successful supernova when the stellar remnant decouples from the ejecta. In this study, we explore a relativistic framework for the finite-temperature $β$-equilibrium limit of equation of state (EOS), constrained via a Bayesian inference methodology. The EOS is constrained by minimal approximations on a few nuclear saturation properties, low-density pure neutron matter constraints from chiral effective field theory, and a neutron star (NS) maximum mass greater than 2.0 $M_{\odot}$. Two sets of EOS derived from the relativistic mean field model for nucleonic and hyperonic matter constrained by a Bayesian inference calculation at the zero temperature limit are used. The thermal adiabatic index ($Γ_{\rm Th}$) is calculated as a function of the baryonic density across several temperatures for both the sets. Our results suggest that the maximum NS mass is of the order of 2.15 $M_\odot$ if hyperons are present. In addition, the present study suggests that an observation of NS with mass larger than $2.2\ M_{\odot}$ can indirectly indicates the absence of hyperons in its core. The deleptonization of hyperonic PNS reduces the stellar maximum mass rendering the PNS exceeding the zero temperature maximum stellar (baryonic) mass limit becomes metastable which is prone to collapse into a black hole while PNS below such a mass threshold evolves to a stable NS.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that the maximum neutron star mass is approximately 2.15 solar masses if hyperons are present in the core, and that an observation of a neutron star with mass larger than 2.2 solar masses would indirectly indicate the absence of hyperons. The authors qualify that hyperonic proto-neutron stars with baryonic masses exceeding the zero-temperature maximum baryonic mass limit become metastable and prone to collapse into a black hole during deleptonization.

## Method

The authors employed a relativistic mean field (RMF) model with nonlinear mesonic interactions to generate two sets of 18,000 equations of state (EOS) each, one for nucleonic matter and one including hyperons (Lambda, Sigma, Xi). The EOS sets were derived from a Bayesian inference calculation at zero temperature, constrained by nuclear saturation properties (binding energy, incompressibility, symmetry energy), low-density pure neutron matter from chiral effective field theory at N3LO, and the astrophysical observation of a neutron star mass exceeding 2.0 solar masses. The finite-temperature EOS was then computed for fixed temperatures (0, 10, 30, 50 MeV), fixed entropies per baryon (0, 1, 2), and fixed lepton fractions (0.4 for trapped neutrinos, zero for neutrino-free matter), and the thermal adiabatic index was calculated as a function of baryonic density.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass is approximately 2.15 solar masses if hyperons are present.
- A neutron star mass larger than 2.2 solar masses would indicate the absence of hyperons in its core.
- The EOS was constrained by the requirement that the neutron star maximum mass exceeds 2.0 solar masses.
- Temperatures considered for the EOS calculations: 0, 10, 30, and 50 MeV.
- Entropies per baryon considered: 0, 1, and 2.
- Lepton fraction for trapped neutrino scenario: Y_lep = 0.4.
- The thermal adiabatic index parametrization for nucleonic matter is valid for baryon density n_B in the range 0.08 to 1.2 fm^-3 and temperature T in the range 5 to 100 MeV, with fitted coefficients a=1.3665, b=11.9638, c=0.0022, d=0.0867.
- Hyperon onset density at T=0 is approximately 0.4 fm^-3.

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian-inference-constrained RMF models of Kumar (2025) to finite temperatures relevant for proto-neutron star evolution, building on earlier studies of PNS cooling by Prakash (1996) and Pons (1998). The results enable more accurate modeling of the Kelvin-Helmholtz cooling phase and the potential delayed collapse of hyperonic PNS into black holes, and provide a parametric expression for the thermal adiabatic index useful for binary neutron star merger simulations. The study also complements the finite-temperature EOS analyses in the CompOSE database evaluated by Raduta (2021) and the thermal pressure framework of Raithel (2019).
