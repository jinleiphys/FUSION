# Mixed Symmetry Nuclear Shell Model

> A mixed-symmetry nuclear shell-model scheme for carrying out calculations in regimes where there is a competition between two or more modes is proposed. A one-dimensional toy model is used to demonstrate the concept. The theory is then applied to $^{24}Mg$ and $^{44}Ti$. For lower pf-shell nuclei such as $^{44-48}Ti$ and $^{48}Cr$ there is strong SU(3) symmetry breaking due to the spin-orbit interaction. However, the quadrupole collectivity as measured by B(E2) transition strengths in the yrast band remain high even though SU(3) appears to be broken. Some results for the so-called X(5) symmetry that falls along the U(5) $\leftrightarrow$ SU(3) leg of the Interacting Boson Model are also considered. The results show that the mixed-symmetry concept is effective, even when strong symmetry breaking occurs.

# Mixed Symmetry Nuclear Shell Model

## Key claim
The paper proposes a mixed-symmetry (oblique-basis) nuclear shell-model scheme that combines two competing basis sets, spherical shell-model states and collective SU(3) configurations, to efficiently describe nuclei where single-particle and collective quadrupole modes compete, and demonstrates that this approach yields accurate results even when strong SU(3) symmetry breaking occurs due to the spin-orbit interaction, particularly in lower pf-shell nuclei.

## Method
The authors introduce a one-dimensional harmonic oscillator in a box as a toy model to illustrate the mixed-mode concept, then apply the oblique-basis approach to fermionic systems using realistic interactions: Wildenthal's interaction for 24Mg in the sd-shell and the Kuo-Brown-3 (KB3) interaction for 44Ti and other pf-shell nuclei. For bosonic systems, they diagonalize a schematic U(5)↔SU(3) transitional Hamiltonian in the interacting boson model (IBM) using SU(3) basis vectors, varying the phase parameter x to study shape phase transitions and critical point behavior.

## Key numbers
- For 24Mg, a SM(2)+(8,4)&(9,2) model space reproduces the binding energy within 2% of the full-space result, with eigenstates having greater than 90% overlap with full-space results; this space adds only 0.5% to the dimensionality compared to SM(2), while SM(4) requires a 54% increase in dimensionality.
- Adding SU(3) to SM(2) for 44Ti increases the model space from 47.7% to 49.8% of the full pf-shell space, yielding results comparable to SM(3) which uses 84% of the full space.
- For 44Ti, SM(2)-type calculations yield 80% overlap with exact states, while SM(3) shows overlaps greater than 97%; SM(2)+(12,0)&(10,1) calculations in about 50% of the full space give results as good as SM(3).
- In the IBM transitional study with N=10 bosons and f(N)=4N, the critical point occurs at x_c ~ 0.46, with energy ratios R_02 dropping from 25.338 (deformed limit) to ~2 (spherical limit) and R_42 dropping from 10/3 to 2.
- For f(N)=1, the critical point is x_c = 0.974; for f(N)=4N with N=10, x_c ~ 0.46.
- B(E2, 2+_3 → 0+_g) and B(E2, 2+_3 → 2+_1) show a small peak around x ~ 0.44; B(E2, 2+_3 → 0+_2) has a saddle point around x ~ 0.38.

## Context
This work extends the Elliott SU(3) model and its pseudo-SU(3) extension by introducing a mixed-mode framework that handles competition between single-particle and collective degrees of freedom, building on earlier oblique-basis calculations for 24Mg by Gueorguiev and collaborators. The fermionic applications use the realistic interactions of Wildenthal (for sd-shell) and Kuo-Brown-3 (for pf-shell), while the bosonic analysis follows the U(5)↔SU(3) transitional studies of Iachello and collaborators, including the X(5) symmetry discovered by Iachello (2001). The results enable future multi-mode shell-model calculations incorporating pairing interactions via Sp(4) algebraic approaches, Hamiltonian-driven basis sets, or extensions to the three exact limits of the interacting boson model.
