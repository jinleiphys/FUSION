# Quasinormal modes of charged magnetic black branes & chiral magnetic transport

> We compute quasinormal modes (QNMs) of the metric and gauge field perturbations about black branes electrically and magnetically charged in the Einstein-Maxwell-Chern-Simons theory. By the gauge/gravity correspondence, this theory is dual to a particular class of field theories with a chiral anomaly, in a thermal charged plasma state subjected to a constant external magnetic field, $B$. The QNMs are dual to the poles of the two-point functions of the energy-momentum and axial current operators, and they encode information about the dissipation and transport of charges in the plasma. Complementary to the gravity calculation, we work out the hydrodynamic description of the dual field theory in the presence of a chiral anomaly, and a constant external $B$. We find good agreement with the weak field hydrodynamics, which can extend beyond the weak $B$ regime into intermediate regimes. Furthermore, we provide results that can be tested against thermodynamics and hydrodynamics in the strong $B$ regime. We find QNMs exhibiting Landau level behavior, which become long-lived at large $B$ if the anomaly coefficient exceeds a critical magnitude. Chiral transport is analyzed beyond the hydrodynamic approximation for the five (formerly) hydrodynamic modes, including a chiral magnetic wave.

## Key claim

The authors compute quasinormal modes (QNMs) of metric and gauge field perturbations about charged magnetic black branes in Einstein-Maxwell-Chern-Simons theory, finding that the hydrodynamic description derived from weak-field assumptions can extend beyond the weak magnetic field regime into intermediate regimes, but only for propagation parallel to the magnetic field.

## Method

The authors solve the fully backreacted Einstein-Maxwell-Chern-Simons equations numerically using spectral methods to obtain charged magnetic black brane backgrounds, then linearize the fluctuation equations around these backgrounds. They classify fluctuations into helicity-2, helicity-1, and helicity-0 sectors under rotations about the magnetic field direction, and solve the resulting generalized eigenvalue problem for QNM frequencies subject to incoming boundary conditions at the horizon and vanishing non-normalizable modes at the AdS boundary. Complementarily, they perform a systematic hydrodynamic calculation of the five hydrodynamic modes in the dual field theory with a chiral anomaly and constant external magnetic field.

## Key numbers

- Temperature is given by \(T = |\bar{u}_1|/(4\pi)\) where \(\bar{u}_1\) is the near-horizon expansion coefficient of the metric function \(u(z)\)
- Entropy density is \(s = 4\pi \bar{v}_0^2 \bar{w}_0\) where \(\bar{v}_0\) and \(\bar{w}_0\) are near-horizon values of metric functions
- The chiral anomaly coefficient on the field theory side is related to the Chern-Simons coupling by \(C = \gamma/6\)
- The chiral magnetic transport coefficients are \(\xi_V = -3C\mu^2 + \tilde{C}T^2\) and \(\xi_B = -6C\mu\) in the thermodynamic hydrodynamic frame
- The trace of the energy-momentum tensor is \(\langle T_\mu{}^\mu \rangle = -B^2/2\) from the holographic calculation

## Context

This work extends previous studies of QNMs about charged black branes (Janiszewski 2015) by including both a Chern-Simons term and a magnetic field, closing gaps in gravitational calculations of fluctuations with nonzero momentum along the magnetic field. It also generalizes hydrodynamic analyses of chiral transport in weak magnetic fields (Abbasi 2015, Kalaydzhyan 2016) to include nonzero chemical potential and charge density, and provides results testable against thermodynamics and hydrodynamics in the strong magnetic field regime (Kovtun 2016). The findings enable future studies of non-equilibrium dynamics in strongly coupled chiral plasmas, relevant for understanding the quark-gluon plasma in heavy ion collisions and Weyl/Dirac semimetals.
