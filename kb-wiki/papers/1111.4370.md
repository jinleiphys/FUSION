# Variational calculation of 4He tetramer ground and excited states using a realistic pair potential

> We calculated the 4He trimer and tetramer ground and excited states with the LM2M2 potential using our Gaussian expansion method (GEM) for ab initio variational calculations of few-body systems. The method has extensively been used for a variety of three-, four- and five-body systems in nuclear physics and exotic atomic/molecular physics. The trimer (tetramer) wave function is expanded in terms of symmetric three-(four-)body Gaussian basis functions, ranging from very compact to very diffuse, without assuming any pair correlation function. Calculated results of the trimer ground and excited states are in excellent agreement with the literature. Binding energies of the tetramer ground and excited states are obtained to be 558.98 mK and 127.33 mK (0.93 mK below the trimer ground state), respectively. Precisely the same shape of the short-range correlation (r_ij < 4 Å) in the dimer appear in the ground and excited states of the trimer and tetramer. Analyzing the asymptotic wave functions (accurate up to 1000 Å) of those excited states, we propose a model which predicts the binding energy of the first excited state of 4He_N measured from the 4He_{N-1} ground state to be N/2(N-1)xB_2 using dimer binding energy B_2 only; fit in N=3 and 4 is excellent.

# Variational calculation of 4He tetramer ground and excited states using a realistic pair potential

## Key claim

The authors propose a "dimerlike-pair" model predicting that the binding energy of the first excited state of 4He_N measured from the 4He_{N-1} ground state is approximately N/(2(N-1)) times the dimer binding energy B_2, with excellent fit for N=3 and 4; this model is based on the observation that the asymptotic wave functions of the trimer and tetramer excited states are almost proportional to the dimer wave function up to approximately 1000 Angstrom.

## Method

The authors employed the Gaussian expansion method (GEM) for ab initio variational calculations of the 4He trimer and tetramer bound states using the LM2M2 realistic pair potential. The trimer (tetramer) wave function was expanded in terms of symmetric three-(four-)body Gaussian basis functions ranging from very compact to very diffuse, constructed on all sets of Jacobi coordinates without assuming any pair correlation function. The basis functions used complex-range Gaussians for the short-range coordinate space to handle the strong short-range repulsive potential, and the Rayleigh-Ritz variational principle was applied to solve the Schrodinger equation.

## Key numbers

- Dimer binding energy B_2 = 1.30348 mK, with root-mean-square interparticle distance sqrt(<x^2>) = 70.93 Angstrom and mean interparticle distance <x> = 52.00 Angstrom
- Trimer ground state binding energy B_3^(0) = 126.40 mK, with mean interparticle distance <r_ij> = 9.616 Angstrom
- Trimer excited state binding energy B_3^(1) = 2.2706 mK, with mean interparticle distance <r_ij> = 84.51 Angstrom
- Tetramer ground state binding energy = 558.98 mK
- Tetramer excited state binding energy = 127.33 mK (0.93 mK below the trimer ground state)
- Asymptotic normalization coefficient for trimer ground state C_3^(0) = 0.562 Angstrom^(-1/2) and for trimer excited state C_3^(1) = 0.179 Angstrom^(-1/2)
- The dimer wave function's exact asymptotic form is 0.1498 exp(-kappa_2 x) Angstrom^(-1/2) with kappa_2 = 0.0104 Angstrom^(-1)

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of 4He trimers using realistic potentials by Carbonell, Kievsky (2001), and Roudnev, which had well-converged results, while tetramer calculations had remained limited with significant disagreement on the excited state binding energy. The authors' GEM approach, previously applied to various three-, four-, and five-body systems in nuclear physics and exotic atomic/molecular physics as reviewed by Hiyama (2003, 2009, 2010), provides accurate results for both tetramer ground and excited states. The finding that the same short-range correlation shape appears in dimer, trimer, and tetramer states provides a foundation for using Jastrow-type pair correlation functions in few-body calculations, and the proposed dimerlike-pair model enables prediction of binding energies for larger 4He_N clusters using only the dimer binding energy.
