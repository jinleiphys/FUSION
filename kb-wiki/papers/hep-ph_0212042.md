# Meson resonances from unitarized meson scattering at one loop in Chiral Perturbation Theory

> We show the results for the scattering poles associated to the rho, f0, a0, K*, sigma and kappa resonances in meson-meson scattering. Our amplitudes are obtained from the complete one-loop meson-meson scattering amplitudes from Chiral Perturbation Theory. Once unitarized with the Inverse Amplitude Method, they describe remarkably well the data simultaneously in the low energy and resonance regions up to 1.2 GeV, using low energy parameters compatible with present determinations.

# Meson resonances from unitarized meson scattering at one loop in Chiral Perturbation Theory

## Key claim
The poles associated to the rho, f0, a0, K*, sigma and kappa resonances in meson-meson scattering are robust results of the Inverse Amplitude Method when applied to complete one-loop Chiral Perturbation Theory amplitudes, provided the low-energy parameters are compatible with previous determinations. However, the a0 pole position is very sensitive to whether one truncates the series in terms of a single f_pi or also in terms of f_K and f_eta.

## Method
The authors used the complete one-loop meson-meson scattering amplitudes from Chiral Perturbation Theory, regularized in the standard MS-1 scheme, and unitarized them with the Inverse Amplitude Method (IAM). They performed fits to the whole meson-meson scattering data up to 1.2 GeV, using two different sets of amplitudes (IAM I using only f_pi, and IAM II using f_pi, f_K, and f_eta). The amplitudes were then continued to the second Riemann sheet of the complex s-plane to extract pole positions.

## Key numbers
- Rho pole: 760 - i 82 MeV (IAM I) with errors ±52 ± i 25 MeV; 754 - i 74 MeV (IAM II) with errors ±18 ± i 10 MeV
- K* pole: 886 - i 21 MeV (IAM I) with errors ±50 ± i 8 MeV; 889 - i 24 MeV (IAM II) with errors ±13 ± i 4 MeV
- Sigma pole: 443 - i 217 MeV (IAM I) with errors ±17 ± i 12 MeV; 440 - i 212 MeV (IAM II) with errors ±8 ± i 15 MeV
- f0 pole: 988 - i 4 MeV (IAM I) with errors ±19 ± i 3 MeV; 973 - i 11 MeV (IAM II) with errors +39/-127 +i 189/-i 11 MeV
- Kappa pole: 750 - i 226 MeV (IAM I) with errors ±18 ± i 11 MeV; 753 - i 235 MeV (IAM II) with errors ±52 ± i 33 MeV
- a0 pole: 1117 - i 12 MeV (IAM II) with errors +24/-320 +i 43/-i 12 MeV; IAM I gave a cusp-like behavior rather than a clear pole

## Context
This work extends earlier single-channel IAM calculations by Dobado (1996) that found the rho, sigma, and K* poles in elastic pi-pi and pi-K scattering, and the approximate coupled-channel approach by Oller (1999) that used only leading-order ChPT plus dominant s-channel loops with a cutoff regulator. The present calculation uses the complete one-loop meson-meson amplitudes recently completed by Gomez Nicola (2001), including the new K-eta, eta-eta, and K-eta-to-K-pi amplitudes, and recalculating all other independent amplitudes consistently. This enables testing compatibility of the chiral parameters with values from other low-energy processes, which was not possible with the cutoff-regularized approach. The results enable future studies of the controversial scalar states (sigma, kappa, f0, a0) with controlled uncertainties and proper chiral symmetry constraints.
