# Shell-model calculations of stellar weak interaction rates: II. Weak rates for nuclei in the mass range A=45-65 in supernovae environment

> Based on large-scale shell model calculations we have determined the electron capture, positron capture and beta-decay rates on more than 100 nuclei in the mass range A=45-65. The rates are given for densities ρY_e =10^7-10^{10} mol/cm^3 and temperatures T=10^9-10^{10} K and hence are relevant for both types of supernovae (Type Ia and Type II). The shell model electron capture rates are significantly smaller than currently assumed. For proton-to-baryon ratios Y_e=0.42-0.46 mol/g, the beta-decay rates are faster than the electron capture rates during the core collapse of a massive star.

## Key claim

The shell-model electron capture rates for nuclei in the mass range A=45-65 are significantly smaller than the previously standard FFN (Fuller, Fowler, Newman) rates, but the paper qualifies that the comparison depends on temperature and density conditions, with the largest deviations occurring at lower temperatures and densities where the FFN parametrization of the Gamow-Teller resonance as a single state is least accurate.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell-model diagonalization calculations in the pf shell using a modified KB3 residual interaction, computing Gamow-Teller (GT) strength distributions for more than 100 nuclei. For each nucleus pair, they explicitly included GT distributions for the lowest few states in the parent nucleus (4-12 states depending on nuclear type), supplemented these with back-resonance contributions derived from the inverse process, and applied the Brink hypothesis for the remaining GT strength at higher excitation energies. Experimental data for excitation energies and GT transition strengths replaced shell-model results whenever available.

## Key numbers

- Electron capture rates were calculated for densities ρY_e = 10^7 to 10^10 mol/cm^3 and temperatures T = 10^9 to 10^10 K
- The effective ratio of axial to vector coupling constants used was (g_A/g_V)_eff = 0.74 × (g_A/g_V)_bare, with (g_A/g_V)_bare = -1.2599(25)
- The constant K for the rate formula was taken as K = 6146 ± 6 s from superallowed Fermi transitions
- For the 63Ni electron capture rate at T_9=3 and log(ρY_e)=7, the full rate was log λ^ec = -8.46, compared to -9.62 when using only the ground state
- For the 63Co beta-decay rate at T_9=3 and log(ρY_e)=7, the full rate was log λ^β- = -1.60, compared to -1.64 when using only the ground state
- The Q_β value for the 63Co-63Ni pair is 3.672(20) MeV
- For the 56Fe-56Co pair, the Q_β value is negative, making electron capture the dominant laboratory decay mode

## Context

This work extends the shell-model approach validated in Caurier (1999) to produce a comprehensive set of weak interaction rates for supernova modeling, directly replacing the seminal FFN rates from Fuller, Fowler, and Newman (1980-1985) that had been the standard input for supernova simulations. The paper enables more accurate simulations of both Type Ia and Type II supernovae by providing systematically calculated rates that show the FFN parametrization overestimates electron capture rates, particularly at lower temperatures and densities where the single-state approximation for the GT resonance fails. The rates are available in electronic format for incorporation into supernova models.
