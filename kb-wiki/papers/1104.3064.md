# Categorizing resonances X(1835), X(2120) and X(2370) in the pseudoscalar meson family

> Inspired by the newly observed three resonances X(1835), X(2120) and X(2370), in this work we systematically study the two-body strong decays and double pion decays of $η(1295)/η(1475)$, $η(1760)/X(1835)$ and $X(2120)/X(2370)$ by categorizing $η(1295)/η(1475)$, $η(1760)/X(1835)$, X(2120) and X(2370) as the radial excitations of $η(548)/η^\prime(958)$. Our numerical results indicate the followings: (1) The obtained theoretical strong decay widths of three pseudoscalar states $η(1295)$, $η(1475)$ and $η(1760)$ are consistent with the experimental measurements; (2) X(1835) could be the second radial excitation of $η^\prime(958)$; (3) X(2120) and X(2370) can be explained as the third and fourth radial excitations of $η(548)/η^\prime(958)$, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the three resonances X(1835), X(2120), and X(2370) can be categorized as conventional radial excitations of the η(548)/η′(958) pseudoscalar meson family, with the hedge that X(1835) "could be" the second radial excitation of η′(958) and X(2120)/X(2370 "can be explained as" the third and fourth radial excitations, respectively, rather than being definitively established.

## Method

The authors employed the quark pair creation (QPC) model, also known as the ³P₀ model, to systematically calculate the two-body strong decay widths and double pion decay widths of the pseudoscalar states η(1295)/η(1475), η(1760)/X(1835), X(2120), and X(2370). They used simple harmonic oscillator (SHO) wave functions to describe the meson spatial wave functions and introduced mixing angles between the η_q(nS) and η_s(nS) flavor basis states for each radial excitation level. The double pion decays were assumed to proceed through intermediate scalar mesons σ(600), f₀(980), and a₀(980).

## Context

This work extends the conventional quark model classification of pseudoscalar mesons proposed by Huang and Zhu (2005) and Li et al. (2008), who treated X(1835) as a radial excitation of η′(958), and contrasts with exotic interpretations of X(1835) as a pseudoscalar glueball (Kochelev 2005), a baryonium state (Ding and Yan 2005), or a mixture with glueball (Liu 2010). The authors differ from the assignment in Liu (2010), where X(2120) and X(2370) were assumed as third radial excitations of η(548) and η′(958) respectively; here X(2370) is instead assigned as the fourth radial excitation of η(548). The predicted decay patterns enable future experimental tests at BES-III to distinguish between conventional meson assignments and exotic interpretations for these resonances.
