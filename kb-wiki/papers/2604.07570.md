# Systematics of characteristics of pygmy dipole resonances in medium-heavy and heavy atomic nuclei with neutron excess

> The systematics of energies and the contribution of pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) to the energy-weighted sum rule of dipole gamma transitions in medium-heavy and heavy nuclei with an excess of neutrons are considered. The modified macroscopic model of Isacker-Nagarajan-Warner was used for calculating PDR energies with the number of surface neutrons proportional to the thickness of the neutron skin according to the Pethick- Ravenhall expression (PR INW approach). Such modification of the macroscopic approach by Isacker-Nagarajan-Warner enables to take into account microscopic evidence of direct relationship between skin thickness and low-energy dipole response. The results are compared with the microscopic calculations for the chains of Ni, Sn and Pb isotopes. It was demonstrated that the dependence of the magnitudes of the energies within the PR INW approach on neutron excess is in rather good agreement with experimental data and microscopic calculations if the absolute value of the strength of the neutron-proton interaction is nearly three times as large as that obtained by Isacker-Nagarajan-Warner by the volume integral of the nucleon-nucleon interaction. While the macroscopic INW PR model can describe the main features of the PDR, above mentioned discrepancy of the strength values doesn't not provide reason enough for the conclusion that PDR is pure collective state. The analytical expressions for the PDR fraction of the energy-weighted sum rule for electric dipole transitions (E1 EWSR) are used. They are based on the "molecular" energy-weighted E1 sum rule considering the number of surface neutrons as a function of the neutron thickness (PR MSR approach). Systematics for the PDR fraction of E1 EWSR are proposed with parameters obtained by fitting the experimental data and microscopic calculations.

# Systematics of characteristics of pygmy dipole resonances in medium-heavy and heavy atomic nuclei with neutron excess

## Key claim

The modified macroscopic model of Isacker-Nagarajan-Warner with the Pethick-Ravenhall expression for surface neutrons (PR INW approach) can describe the main features of the pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) energies and their dependence on neutron excess, but only if the absolute value of the neutron-proton interaction strength is taken nearly three times larger than the value obtained from the volume integral of the nucleon-nucleon interaction; this discrepancy means the results do not provide sufficient reason to conclude that the PDR is a pure collective state.

## Method

The authors used the macroscopic Isacker-Nagarajan-Warner (INW) model modified by the Pethick-Ravenhall (PR) expression, which relates the number of surface neutrons linearly to the neutron skin thickness, to calculate PDR energies for chains of Ni, Sn, and Pb isotopes. The neutron skin thickness was approximated as a linear function of the neutron-proton asymmetry parameter I = (N-Z)/A with mass-dependent coefficients. For the PDR fraction of the energy-weighted sum rule for electric dipole transitions (E1 EWSR), analytical expressions based on the "molecular" energy-weighted E1 sum rule (PR MSR approach) were used, with parameters obtained by fitting experimental data and microscopic calculations from multiple methods including relativistic RPA, relativistic quasiparticle RPA, quasiparticle-phonon model, and others.

## Key numbers

- The strength of effective neutron-proton interaction used in the PR INW model was taken as the constant component of the Skyrme force MSk7: κ_np = -1828.23 MeV·fm³, yielding K_p = 57.3 MeV
- Fitting the PR INW model to microscopic data gave κ_np = -1930 ± 78 MeV·fm³, with a reduced chi-square ratio of 0.98 relative to the MSk7 value
- The best systematics for PDR energies used parameters e₁ = 27.3, e₂ = -0.03 (Eq. 6) with reduced chi-square ratio 0.38, and d₁ = 27.1, d₂ = 0.01, d₃ = 31.1 (Eq. 7) with reduced chi-square ratio 0.37
- For the ratio of PDR to GDR fractions in E1 EWSR, systematics parameters from fitting microscopic calculations were δ = 0.37, γ = 0.55 (relative chi-square 0.45), and from fitting experimental data δ = 0.37, γ = 0.78 (relative chi-square 0.37)
- The PDR fraction of E1 EWSR does not exceed approximately 5% in neutron-rich nuclei with A ≥ approximately 100
- The ratio of PDR to GDR sum rules according to the PR MSR expression does not exceed approximately 6-8% in neutron-rich nuclei with A ≥ approximately 100

## Context

This work extends the macroscopic INW model of Isacker (1992) by incorporating the Pethick-Ravenhall expression (1996) for surface neutrons, enabling a direct relationship between neutron skin thickness and low-energy dipole response as suggested by microscopic calculations from Paar (2005) and Tsoneva (2008). The results are compared with microscopic calculations using RRPA (Vretenar 2001), RQRPA (Paar 2005), MF RRPA (Piekarewicz 2006), QPM (Tsoneva 2008), RQTBA (Litvinova 2009), and HF CRPA (Co 2013). The proposed systematics enable estimation of PDR energies and E1 EWSR fractions for medium-heavy and heavy nuclei where microscopic calculations or experimental data are unavailable, which is relevant for improving r-process nucleosynthesis calculations as noted by Goriely (1998, 2023) and Arnould (2007).
