# Parton Distribution Functions from a Light Front Hamiltonian and QCD Evolution for Light Mesons

> We obtain the pion and the kaon parton distribution functions from the eigenstates of a light front effective Hamiltonian in the constituent quark-antiquark representation suitable for low-momentum scale applications. By taking these scales as the only free parameters, the valence quark distribution functions of the pion, after QCD evolution, are consistent with the data from the FNAL-E615 experiment. The ratio of the up quark distribution of the kaon to that of the pion also agrees with the CERN-NA3 experiment. Supplemented by known parton distribution functions for the nucleons, we further obtain the cross section consistent with experimental data for the ${π^-\rm{nucleus}}\rightarrow{μ^+μ^-X}$ Drell-Yan process.

## Key claim

The authors obtain pion and kaon parton distribution functions from a light front effective Hamiltonian with constituent quark-antiquark degrees of freedom, and by taking the initial low-momentum scales as the only two free parameters, the evolved valence PDFs agree with experimental data from FNAL-E615 and CERN-NA3. The claim is hedged by the fact that the model cannot reach the chiral limit and uses large valence quark masses to model the NJL self-energy, and the initial scales are determined by fitting to the same data they are compared against.

## Method

The authors solve a light front effective Hamiltonian (BLFQ-NJL model) that includes holographic QCD confinement, longitudinal confinement, and Nambu-Jona-Lasinio interactions in the valence quark-antiquark Fock space for the pion and kaon. The resulting light front wave functions are used to compute the initial valence PDFs at low model scales, which are then evolved to higher experimental scales using next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) DGLAP equations via the Higher Order Perturbative Parton Evolution toolkit. The two initial scales (one for the pion, one for the kaon) are the only free parameters and are determined by simultaneously fitting the evolved pion PDF to FNAL-E615 data and the ratio \(u^K_v/u^\pi_v\) to CERN-NA3 data.

## Key numbers

- Pion initial scale: \(\mu_{0\pi}^2 = 0.240 \pm 0.024\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\)
- Kaon initial scale: \(\mu_{0K}^2 = 0.246 \pm 0.024\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\)
- Pion valence PDF at large \(x\) falls off as \((1-x)^{1.44}\) at the experimental scale of \(16\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\)
- Kaon \(u\) quark PDF at large \(x\) falls off as \((1-x)^{1.60}\) at \(\mu^2 = 20\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\); kaon \(\overline{s}\) quark PDF falls off as \((1-x)^{1.32}\) at the same scale
- Pion valence PDF fitting parameters (extrapolated to infinite basis): \(a = b = 0.5961\)
- Kaon valence PDF fitting parameters: \(a = 0.6337\), \(b = 0.8546\)
- \(\chi^2\) per degree of freedom for pion PDF fit: 3.64; for the ratio \(u^K_v/u^\pi_v\): 0.50
- At the model scale, valence quarks carry 57% of the pion's momentum; gluons carry 35%
- Best-fit initial scales increase by 17% when advancing from NLO to NNLO DGLAP evolution

## Context

This work extends the BLFQ-NJL model previously developed by Jia et al. (2018) for light meson masses, charge radii, distribution amplitudes, and electromagnetic form factors to the calculation of parton distribution functions and Drell-Yan cross sections. It addresses the long-standing disagreement on the large-\(x\) behavior of the pion valence PDF, where the authors find a \((1-x)^{1.44}\) falloff that is softer than the perturbative QCD expectation of \((1-x)^2\) favored by Aicher et al. (2010) and Chen et al. (2016), but comparable to the \((1-x)^{1.51}\) falloff from light front holographic QCD by de Teramond et al. (2018). The results enable future application of analogous effective Hamiltonians to baryons with subsequent QCD scale evolution.
