# REGULAR SUPPRESSION OF P,T-VIOLATING NUCLEAR MATRIX ELEMENTS

> In heavy nuclei there is a parametrical suppression, $\;\sim A^{-1/3}\;$, of T-odd, P-odd matrix elements as compared to T-even, P-odd ones.

# Regular Suppression of P,T-Violating Nuclear Matrix Elements

## Key claim
In heavy nuclei, T-odd, P-odd (TOPO) nuclear matrix elements are parametrically suppressed by a factor of approximately A^{-1/3} compared to T-even, P-odd (TEPO) matrix elements, where A is the atomic mass number. This suppression arises because the T-odd commutator operator has no classical limit, while the T-even anticommutator operator does, making the suppression a regular feature of heavy nuclei rather than a case-specific numerical accident.

## Method
The author constructs effective contact potentials for both T-even, P-odd and T-odd, P-odd interactions of a valence nucleon with a nuclear core, following the Landau-Migdal approach. The T-even potential is written as an anticommutator of spin-momentum and density operators, while the T-odd potential is written as a commutator (equivalently, a gradient of density). Matrix elements are estimated semiclassically for a heavy nucleus with a step-like density profile, using the Fermi momentum and nuclear radius scaling with A^{1/3}.

## Key numbers
- The Fermi weak interaction constant is G = 1.027 × 10^{-5} m^{-2}, where m is the proton mass.
- The dimensionless strength constant g for the T-even, P-odd nuclear interaction is of order g ∼ 1.
- The upper limit on the electric dipole moment of ^{199}Hg is d(^{199}Hg)/e < 9.1 × 10^{-28} cm, which bounds the T-odd, P-odd dimensionless constant ξ < 1.7 × 10^{-3}.
- The nuclear radius parameter is r_0 = 1.2 fm, giving R = r_0 A^{1/3}.
- The T-even matrix element estimate is ⟨{p, ρ(r)}⟩ ∼ 3 ρ_0 r_0^{-1}, where ρ_0 is nuclear density.
- The T-odd matrix element estimate is ⟨∇ρ(r)⟩ ∼ 1.5 ρ_0 r_0^{-1} A^{-1/3}, using the boundary condition ℜ^2 R^3 ≈ 1.5.
- The parametric suppression factor of T-odd relative to T-even matrix elements is A^{-1/3}.

## Context
This work provides a simple intuitive explanation for the regular suppression of T-odd, P-odd nuclear matrix elements that had been observed in detailed numerical studies by other groups (Ginges and Volotka; Thomas; see also Herczeg). The result is relevant for experimental searches for T-odd, P-odd effects in nuclei, which were being planned by many groups at the time of publication. The suppression mechanism enables experimentalists to estimate typical TOPO mixing matrix elements in heavy nuclei and to set realistic sensitivity requirements for experiments searching for time-reversal violation beyond the Standard Model.
