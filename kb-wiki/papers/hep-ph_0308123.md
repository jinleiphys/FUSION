# Progress in the physics of massive neutrinos

> The current status of the physics of massive neutrinos is reviewed with a forward-looking emphasis. The article begins with the general phenomenology of neutrino oscillations in vacuum and matter and documents the experimental evidence for oscillations of solar, reactor, atmospheric and accelerator neutrinos. Both active and sterile oscillation possibilities are considered. The impact of cosmology (BBN, CMB, leptogenesis) and astrophysics (supernovae, highest energy cosmic rays) on neutrino observables and vice versa, is evaluated. The predictions of grand unified, radiative and other models of neutrino mass are discussed. Ways of determining the unknown parameters of three-neutrino oscillations are assessed, taking into account eight-fold degeneracies in parameters that yield the same oscillation probabilities, as well as ways to determine the absolute neutrino mass scale (from beta-decay, neutrinoless double-beta decay, large scale structure and Z-bursts). Critical unknowns at present are the amplitude of ν_μto ν_e oscillations and the hierarchy of the neutrino mass spectrum; the detection of CP violation in the neutrino sector depends on these and on an unknown phase. The estimated neutrino parameter sensitivities at future facilities (reactors, superbeams, neutrino factories) are given. The overall agenda of a future neutrino physics program to construct a bottom-up understanding of the lepton sector is presented.

# Progress in the physics of massive neutrinos

## Key claim

The review asserts that neutrino oscillations have been definitively established as the cause of solar and atmospheric neutrino deficits, but the absolute neutrino mass scale, the hierarchy of the mass spectrum, the value of the mixing angle θ_x (θ_13), and the possibility of CP violation in the neutrino sector all remain unknown and require future experimental programs to resolve.

## Method

The paper provides a comprehensive review of neutrino oscillation phenomenology in vacuum and matter, including the MSW effect for solar neutrinos and matter effects for long-baseline propagation through the Earth. It documents experimental evidence from solar (Homestake, SAGE, GALLEX, GNO, Super-Kamiokande, SNO), reactor (KamLAND, CHOOZ, Palo Verde), atmospheric (Super-Kamiokande), and accelerator (LSND, K2K) experiments, and evaluates constraints from cosmology (BBN, CMB from WMAP) and astrophysics (supernovae, cosmic rays). The review also assesses model predictions from grand unified theories, radiative models, and other frameworks for neutrino mass generation.

## Key numbers

- Atmospheric neutrino oscillations: δm²_a ~ 2.0 × 10⁻³ eV² with near maximal mixing from Super-Kamiokande data
- Solar neutrino LMA solution: δm²_s ~ 6 × 10⁻⁵ eV² and tan²θ_s ~ 0.4 from combined solar and KamLAND data
- CHOOZ/Palo Verde upper bound: sin²2θ_x ≲ 0.2 (for δm²_a = 2.0 × 10⁻³ eV²) at 95% C.L.
- Tritium beta decay upper limit on neutrino mass: about 2 eV from Troitsk and Mainz experiments
- WMAP upper limit on sum of neutrino masses: Σ m_ν about 1 eV from CMB and large-scale structure data
- Z-boson invisible width: N_ν = 2.984 ± 0.008 from LEP measurements at the Z-resonance pole
- WMAP analysis (with HST prior and age constraint t₀ ≥ 11 Gyr): best-fit ΔN_ν = -0.25, with 2σ range N_ν = 0.9 to 8.3
- BBN analysis (deuterium and helium abundances): best-fit ΔN_ν = -0.7, with N_ν = 3 allowed at 2σ
- Combined WMAP + BBN: N_ν = 1.7 to 3.0 at 2σ
- Solar neutrino survival probability less than 1/2 for energies in range 2 MeV ≲ E_ν ≲ 20 GeV for best-fit LMA parameters (δm²_s = 7 × 10⁻⁵ eV², sin²2θ_s = 0.83)
- Matter effect parameter A = 1.54 × 10⁻⁴ eV² × Y_e × ρ(g/cm³) × E_ν(GeV)

## Context

This review extends the earlier work summarized in references such as the reviews by Barr and Dorsner, Altarelli and Feruglio, Mohapatra, and McKeown and Chen, and provides a comprehensive status update following the definitive KamLAND confirmation of the LMA solar solution and the WMAP first-year results. The paper synthesizes results from the Super-Kamiokande atmospheric neutrino discovery (1998), the SNO neutral-current measurements confirming solar oscillations independent of solar models, and the KamLAND reactor antineutrino results that uniquely selected the LMA solution at more than 3σ significance. It enables a roadmap for future neutrino physics, identifying the measurement of θ_x (θ_13), the neutrino mass hierarchy, and the CP-violating phase δ as the critical next steps, to be pursued with off-axis beams, superbeams, and neutrino factories.
