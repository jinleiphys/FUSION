# Quark Delocalization, Color Screening and Dibaryons

> The quark delocalization and color screening model, a quark potential model, is used for a systematic search of dibaryon candidates in the $u,d$ and $s$ three flavor world. Color screening which appears in unquenched lattice gauge calculations and quark delocalization (which is similar to electron delocalization in molecular physics) are both included. Flavor symmetry breaking and channel coupling effects are studied. The model is constrained not only by baryon ground state properties but also by the $N$-$N$ scattering phase shifts. The deuteron and zero energy di-nucleon resonance are both reproduced qualitatively. The model predicts two extreme types of dibaryonic systems: ``molecular'' like the deuteron, and highly delocalized six-quark systems among which only a few narrow dibaryon resonances occur in the $u,d$ and $s$ three flavor world. Possible high spin dibaryon resonances are emphasized.

## Key claim

The Quark Delocalization and Color Screening Model (QDCSM) predicts that only a few narrow dibaryon resonances exist in the u,d,s three-flavor world, but the quantitative predictions of dibaryon masses are uncertain by approximately 10 MeV for nonstrange states and even larger for strange states, so the claim of stability is sensitive to model details.

## Method

The authors extended a quark potential model by incorporating color screening (motivated by unquenched lattice gauge calculations) and quark delocalization (analogous to electron delocalization in molecular physics) into the six-quark Hamiltonian. They performed a systematic search over all possible strangeness, isospin, and spin quantum numbers within the u,d,s three-flavor world, using an adiabatic approximation with channel coupling and flavor symmetry breaking effects, with model parameters fixed by baryon ground state properties and N-N scattering phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron channel (S=0, IJ=01): predicted mass M = 1880 (±14) MeV, with delocalization ε = 0.2
- Di-nucleon resonance (S=0, IJ=10): predicted mass M = 1889 (±4) MeV, with delocalization ε = 0.1
- Di-Δ state (S=0, IJ=03): predicted mass M = 2110 (±36) MeV, binding energy 320-390 MeV, with delocalization ε = 1.0
- H particle: predicted mass M = 2199 (±24) MeV, which is 32 MeV below the ΛΛ threshold, with delocalization ε = 1.0
- State (S=-3, I=1/2, J=2): predicted mass M = 2529 (±25) MeV, which is 45 MeV below the ΛΞπ three-body decay threshold
- Model parameters: m_u,d = 313 MeV, m_s = 634 MeV, b = 0.603 fm, α_s = 1.54, a = 25.13 MeV·fm⁻²
- Color screening parameters: μ = 1.0 fm⁻² (form 6A), ν = 0.40 fm⁻² (form 6B)

## Context

This work extends the quark potential model approach to dibaryons beyond earlier calculations by Jaffe (1977) and Silvestre-Brac et al., who used the chromomagnetic model which could only give N-N repulsion but not attraction. The QDCSM improves upon both the fully deconfined models (where six quarks merge into a single confinement region) and fully confined models (where quarks remain in separate baryons) by allowing the system to choose its configuration variationally. The results enable experimental searches to focus on a few promising dibaryon candidates, particularly high-spin resonances, and motivate further theoretical work including dynamical channel coupling calculations and relativistic corrections.
