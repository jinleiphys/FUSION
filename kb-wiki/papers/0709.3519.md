# Designing Gapped Soft Functions for Jet Production

> Distributions in jet production often depend on a soft function, S, which describes hadronic radiation between the jets. Near kinematic thresholds S encodes nonperturbative information, while far from thresholds S can be computed with an operator product expansion (OPE). We design soft functions for jets that serve this dual purpose, reducing to the perturbative result in the OPE region and to a consistent model in the nonperturbative region. We use the MSbar scheme, and in both regions S displays the appropriate renormalization group scale dependence. We point out that viable soft function models should have a gap associated with the minimum hadronic energy deposit. This gap is connected to the leading O(Lambda_QCD) renormalon ambiguity in jet event shapes. By defining the gap in a suitable scheme we demonstrate that the leading renormalon can be eliminated. This improves the convergence of perturbative results, and also the stability by which non-perturbative parameters encode the underlying soft physics.

## Key claim

The paper argues that viable soft function models for jet production must include a gap parameter associated with the minimum hadronic energy deposit, and that by defining this gap in a suitable scheme the leading O(Lambda_QCD) renormalon ambiguity can be eliminated, improving the convergence of perturbative results and the stability of non-perturbative parameters.

## Method

The authors construct soft functions for jets using a convolution of a partonic perturbative soft function S_part with a nonperturbative model function S_mod, where S_mod includes a gap parameter Delta representing the minimum hadronic energy deposit in each hemisphere. They demonstrate that the leading O(Lambda_QCD) renormalon ambiguity in the soft function can be removed by splitting the gap into a renormalon-free model parameter and a perturbative expansion that cancels the ambiguity order-by-order in perturbation theory. The construction is carried out explicitly for hemisphere mass definitions in top-quark jet production at e+e- colliders, using the MSbar scheme with renormalization group evolution.

## Key numbers

- The size of the nonperturbative "peak region" is set by the hadronic scale Lambda_QCD
- In the tail region, the soft function is probed at perturbative scales of ell^pm approximately 3-30 GeV or larger, depending on the center-of-mass energy Q
- The one-loop partonic soft function at NLO contains a term (C_F alpha_s(mu)/pi) * (pi^2/24) delta(ell) plus plus-function terms with coefficient -2/mu * [theta(ell) ln(ell/mu)/(ell/mu)]_+
- The leading renormalon ambiguity corresponds to a Borel pole at u=1/2 with residue (8 C_F e^{-5/6})/(pi beta_0 (u-1/2)) * mu * (delta'(ell^+)delta(ell^-) + delta(ell^+)delta'(ell^-))
- The cusp anomalous dimension coefficients are Gamma_0 = 4 C_F and Gamma_1^cusp = 4 C_F * [(67/9 - pi^2/3)C_A - 10 n_f/9]

## Context

This work extends the approach of Korchemsky (2000) who implemented a hard IR cutoff on event shape variables, and improves upon the gluing method of Bosch (2004) for B-meson decays by constructing soft functions that simultaneously exhibit proper renormalization group scale dependence in the MSbar scheme and incorporate a gap parameter. The paper enables systematic treatment of both peak and tail regions in jet event shape distributions, and connects to the renormalon analysis of Gardi (2000) for event-shape distributions in full QCD.
