# Dilepton radiation by vector mesons and off-shell partons in the hot and dense medium

> According to the dynamical quasiparticle model (DQPM) - matched to reproduce lattice QCD results in thermodynamic limit, - the constituents of the strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) are massive and off-shell quasi-particles (quarks and gluons) with broad spectral functions. In order to address the electromagnetic radiation of the sQGP, we derive off-shell cross sections of q+qbar->gamma*, q+qbar->gamma*+glue and q+glue->gamma*+q(qbar+glue->gamma*qbar) reactions taking into account the effective propagators for quarks and gluons from the DQPM. Dilepton production in In+In collisions at 158 AGeV is studied by implementing these processes into the parton-hadron-string dynamics (PHSD) transport approach. The microscopic PHSD transport approach describes the full evolution of the heavy-ion collision: from the dynamics of quasi-particles in the sQGP phase (when the local energy density is above ~1 GeV/fm^3) through hadronization and to the following hadron interactions and off-shell propagation after the hadronization. A comparison to the data of the NA60 Collaboration shows that the low mass dilepton spectra are well described by including a collisional broadening of vector mesons, while the spectra in the intermediate mass range are dominated by off-shell quark-antiquark annihilation, quark Bremsstrahlung and gluon-Compton scattering in the nonperturbative QGP. In particular, the observed softening of the mT spectra at intermediate masses (1 GeV < M < 3 GeV) is approximately reproduced.

## Key claim
The low-mass dilepton spectra from In+In collisions at 158 AGeV are well described by including a collisional broadening of vector mesons, while the intermediate-mass spectra (1 GeV < M < 3 GeV) are dominated by off-shell quark-antiquark annihilation, quark Bremsstrahlung, and gluon-Compton scattering in the nonperturbative QGP; the observed softening of the mT spectra at intermediate masses is approximately reproduced, but the slope parameter Teff is still underestimated.

## Method
The authors derived off-shell cross sections for q+qbar->gamma*, q+qbar->gamma*+glue, and q+glue->gamma*+q (and the corresponding antiquark processes) using effective propagators for quarks and gluons from the Dynamical Quasiparticle Model (DQPM), which reproduces lattice QCD results in the thermodynamic limit. These processes were implemented into the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, which describes the full evolution of heavy-ion collisions from the sQGP phase (local energy density above ~1 GeV/fm^3) through hadronization to hadronic interactions. The calculations were compared to NA60 data for In+In collisions at 158 AGeV.

## Key numbers
- In+In collisions at 158 AGeV: the low-mass dilepton spectrum (below 1 GeV) is reproduced by the rho meson yield with collisional broadening.
- Intermediate mass range 1 GeV < M < 3 GeV: partonic sources dominate the dilepton spectrum.
- The effective temperature (Teff) of dileptons in the intermediate mass range is lower than the Teff from the hadronic phase, but the calculated Teff still underestimates the NA60 data.
- For Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV, the HSD results underestimate the measured dilepton spectra in the invariant mass range 0.2 to 0.6 GeV by an average factor of 3.

## Context
This work extends the earlier HSD studies (Bratkovskaya 2008) that showed the NA60 data favored the 'melting rho' scenario for low-mass dileptons, but could not account for the M>1 GeV spectrum with hadronic sources alone. The results confirm the findings of van Hees (2008) for the low-mass region and address the NA60 observation (Arnaldi 2008) of a softening of the mT distribution at intermediate masses. The approach enables quantitative testing of partonic contributions to dilepton production at both SPS and RHIC energies, with the PHENIX low-mass enhancement (0.2-0.6 GeV) remaining unexplained and attributed to non-hadronic sources for future investigation.
