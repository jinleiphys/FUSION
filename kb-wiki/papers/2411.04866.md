# Modification of the Jet Energy-Energy Correlator in Cold Nuclear Matter

> We compute medium corrections to the energy-energy correlator (EEC) for jets in electron-nucleus collisions at leading order in the QCD coupling and the interaction of the jet with the medium. We derive an analytical expression for the modification of the EEC as a function of the opening angle and show that the modification is strongest at large angles within the jet cone. We obtain explicit results for the dependence of the modification on the jet energy, the scattering power of cold nuclear matter, and the path length within the medium. We extend our calculations to gluon jets in proton-nucleus collisions and compare our results with recent preliminary data for proton-lead collisions at the LHC. We also discuss the role of comovers on the EEC in p+Pb collisions.

# Modification of the Jet Energy-Energy Correlator in Cold Nuclear Matter

## Key claim
The paper presents the first analytical calculation of medium modifications to the jet energy-energy correlator (EEC) in cold nuclear matter using the higher-twist formalism at leading order, showing that initial-state effects cause a uniform suppression while final-state interactions produce an enhancement at large angles within the jet cone. The authors qualify that the perturbative calculation is only valid for angles where Qθ > 2.4 GeV, with the region below dominated by nonperturbative confinement physics.

## Method
The authors compute the energy-weighted differential cross section for jets in electron-nucleus deep-inelastic scattering at leading order in the QCD coupling, using collinear factorization and the higher-twist formalism to treat single and double scattering of the jet with cold nuclear matter. The nuclear modification is separated into initial-state effects encoded in nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDFs) and final-state effects parametrized by the jet transport coefficient q̂ and the path length L of the jet inside the nucleus. The calculation is extended to gluon jets in proton-nucleus collisions by replacing the quark-gluon splitting function with the gluon-gluon splitting function and including a comover contribution.

## Key numbers
- The jet transport coefficient for cold nuclear matter is taken as q̂_CNM ≈ 0.02 GeV²/fm, approximately two orders of magnitude smaller than its value in a hot medium.
- For e+Pb collisions, the EEC ratio is evaluated at Q = 30 GeV with path length L = 6 fm and varying xB.
- The nonperturbative region is defined by Qθ < 2.4 GeV; for Q = 30 GeV this corresponds to θ ≲ 0.08.
- For p+Pb collisions at √s = 5.02 TeV, the calculation uses a 30 GeV/c gluon jet at midrapidity.
- The comover region length is estimated as L_co ≈ 1 fm, with q̂_co ≈ 0.9 GeV²/fm derived from scaling the charged multiplicity ratio of p+Pb to Pb+Pb collisions.
- The nuclear shadowing effect from nPDFs alone gives a suppression factor R_pA/pp ≈ 0.89 when hypothetically extended to θ ≈ 0.01.

## Context
This work extends the study of jet energy-energy correlators from vacuum and hot nuclear matter (studied in heavy-ion collisions) to cold nuclear matter accessible in electron-nucleus and proton-nucleus collisions. It provides the first analytical calculation using the higher-twist formalism, complementing a prior phenomenological study by Devereaux (2023) based on the eHIJING event generator. The results are compared with preliminary ALICE data for proton-lead collisions at 5.02 TeV. The work enables future measurements at the Electron-Ion Collider to constrain the jet transport coefficient q̂ for cold nuclear matter, which has only been extracted with limited precision by Ru (2019) from transverse momentum broadening data, and to complement existing nPDF extractions by Eskola (2021) and Kovarik (2015).
