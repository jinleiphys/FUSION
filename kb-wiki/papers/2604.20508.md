# Charged-Current Neutrino-Induced Single-Pion Production in the Superscaling Approach and Relativistic Distorted-Wave Impulse Approximation

> In this work, we present a detailed comparison of the SuSAv2 (SuperScaling Approach version 2) and RDWIA (Relativistic Distorted-Wave Impulse Approximation) models with measurements of charged-current neutrino-induced single-pion production from different experiments (T2K, MINERvA and MiniBooNE), studying the differences between the two theoretical descriptions. The neutrino energy range in these experiments spans from hundreds of MeV to roughly 20 GeV, and the nuclear targets are mainly composed of $^{12}$C. The SuSAv2 model uses the single-nucleon inelastic structure functions from the ANL-Osaka DCC model, which allows for a separation of pion production channels, distinguishing between the $π^+$, $π^-$ and $π^0$ final states. In the RDWIA approach, the Hybrid model developed by the Ghent group is used for the description of the boson-pion-nucleon vertex.

## Key claim

The SuSAv2 and RDWIA models both describe charged-current neutrino-induced single-pion production on carbon targets across the energy range of hundreds of MeV to roughly 20 GeV, but they differ in their treatment of the nuclear medium and the elementary pion-production vertex; the comparison reveals that neither model consistently reproduces all experimental datasets, with the choice of nuclear model and single-nucleon input affecting agreement with T2K, MINERvA, and MiniBooNE data in channel-dependent ways.

## Method

The SuSAv2-inelastic model computes inclusive nuclear responses by integrating the single-nucleon inelastic hadronic tensor (using structure functions from the ANL-Osaka Dynamical Coupled-Channels model) over the invariant mass of the final hadronic system, weighted by an inelastic scaling function derived from relativistic mean-field theory. The RDWIA approach computes semi-inclusive cross sections for single-pion production by evaluating the hadronic current as the Fourier transform of a current density involving bound-state and scattering nucleon wavefunctions (from an energy-dependent relativistic mean-field potential) and a plane-wave pion, using the Hybrid model for the boson-pion-nucleon vertex. Both models are compared with experimental CC1π data from MiniBooNE, MINERvA, and T2K.

## Key numbers

- Neutrino energy range of the experiments spans from hundreds of MeV to roughly 20 GeV.
- Nuclear targets are mainly composed of ¹²C.
- The DCC model used in SuSAv2 has a validity range up to an invariant mass of 2.1 GeV and Q² below 3 GeV².
- The SuSAv2 inelastic nuclear response integral limits are defined as μ_X^{min} = 1 + m_π/m_N and μ_X^{max} = 1 + ω/m_N − E_S, where m_π is the pion mass and E_S the separation energy.
- The RDWIA cross section expression includes a factor f_rec = (p_N/E_N)(1 + (E_N/E_{A-1})|1 + (p_N·(k_π − q))/p_N²|).

## Context

This work extends the SuSAv2 model, previously developed for quasielastic scattering, into the full inelastic regime for neutrino-induced single-pion production, building on the earlier SuSAv2-QE approach of Gonzalez-Jimenez (2014) and the DCC model of Nakamura (2015). It also compares with the RDWIA approach using the Hybrid model of the Ghent group (PhysRevD.95.113007, PhysRevD.97.013004), which has been previously compared with MiniBooNE, MINERvA, and T2K data by Nikolakopoulos (2023). The results enable validation of nuclear models for use in neutrino oscillation experiments such as T2K, MINERvA, and future experiments like DUNE and Hyper-Kamiokande, where pion production is a significant background.
