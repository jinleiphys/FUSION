# A Universal Four-Fermion Formation Framework and Odd-Even Staggering in $α$ Decay

> Clustering phenomena are common in many physical systems across multiple scales. The nuclear $α$ decay is one of the earliest observed evidences of clustering in quantum systems, yet its formation process remains poorly understood even today. In this letter, we propose a novel global odd-even staggering (OES) feature in $α$ decay, which emerges during the clustering process. To unveil its origin, we develop a Universal Four-Fermion Formation Framework (U4F), which describes the formation of any four-nucleon cluster, such as $α$ particle, from a general microscopic wave function, without assuming the preexistence of clustering or pairing. By combining U4F with the large-scale configuration-interaction approach, we demonstrate that the OES effect in $α$ decay arises from the suppression of clustering correlations due to unpaired nucleons. These findings significantly advance our understanding of cluster formation in nuclei and have important implications for the production of new elements and nuclear synthesis in the universe.

## Key claim

The paper claims that odd-even staggering (OES) in alpha decay is a global feature arising solely from the alpha formation process, not from tunneling or overall decay. This claim is hedged by the fact that the global OES is demonstrated for the formation probability extracted via the universal decay law, while the direct observables Q_alpha and T_alpha do not show a clear global OES pattern.

## Method

The authors develop a Universal Four-Fermion Formation Framework (U4F) that extracts quartet amplitudes from large-scale configuration-interaction shell-model wave functions, then maps these onto the intrinsic frame of the alpha particle using Talmi-Moshinsky transformations. They apply this framework to Po and At isotopes using the KSHELL code within the h9fpi13 model space with two different Hamiltonians (Int.1 and Int.2), calculating theoretical alpha formation probabilities and comparing them with values extracted from experimental half-lives via the universal decay law.

## Key numbers

- About 90% of nuclides with even N and even Z exhibit a positive Delta_n(p) F_alpha, while the majority of odd-N (Z) emitters display negative values, demonstrating the global OES feature in alpha formation
- The theoretical alpha formation probabilities for isotopes 202-210Po and 205-211At calculated via U4F agree with experimental values extracted from half-lives
- The alpha formation probability decreases toward the N=126 shell closure, reflecting reduction of pairing correlations near the closed shell
- The quartet energy E_QE and pairing energy contribution extracted via U4F exhibit the same shell feature and OES feature as the alpha formation probability

## Context

This work extends previous studies that showed local OES in alpha decay energy, half-life, and formation probability in specific isotopic chains (Yang 2022, Xu 2005, Delion 2016, Sun 2017, Deng 2021). It contradicts the assumption that OES in alpha decay might be a residual effect of quantum tunneling rather than formation. The U4F framework generalizes earlier configuration-interaction approaches (Harada 1961, Mang 1962, Tonozuka 1979, Lovas 1998) that were limited by symmetry constraints, particularly the restriction to J=0 proton-proton and neutron-neutron pairs. The framework enables future studies of heavier clustering, multi-nucleon transfer, cluster capture reactions, and alpha-capture reactions in stellar nucleosynthesis.
