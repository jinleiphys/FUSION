# Effective degrees of freedom, trace anomaly and c-theorem like condition in the hadron resonance gas model

> The relation between the effective degrees of freedom (EDOF) and the trace anomaly is studied in the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model. If we regard the thermodynamical relation as the evolution equation and define the EDOF as P/T^4, where P and T are the pressure and the temperature, respectively, we obtain the equation which relates to the trace anomaly. The structure of the equation resembles that of the so-called c-theorem, which asserts that the EDOF should not increase as the energy scale parameter decreases, in the two dimensional conformal field theory. There is a stationary point where the trace anomaly (modified trace anomaly) vanishes, and the scale symmetry is restored. To investigate the limiting temperature of the HRG model with the excluded volume effects, we consider two types of the c-theorem like conditions for the EDOF. The first condition requires that the EDOF should not decrease when T increases. This condition is equivalent to the condition that the trace anomaly (modified trace anomaly) should not be negative. The second condition requires that the EDOF should be convex downwards as a function of T. It is found that the first condition gives the limiting temperature of the HRG model with the excluded volume effect which is much higher than the crossover transition temperature obtained by the lattice QCD calculation and, at zero baryon number density, is close to the transition temperature in the pure gluonic theory, while the second one gives the limiting temperature which almost coincides with the one obtained by using the normalized baryon number fluctuation in the previous study and is consistent with the critical point predicted by the lattice QCD calculation.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the limiting temperature of the hadron resonance gas model with excluded volume effects can be determined by applying two types of c-theorem-like conditions on the effective degrees of freedom, defined as pressure divided by T^4. The first condition (EDOF should not decrease as T increases) gives a limiting temperature much higher than the lattice QCD crossover temperature and close to the pure gluonic transition temperature, while the second condition (EDOF should be convex downward as a function of T) gives a limiting temperature consistent with the critical point predicted by lattice QCD.

## Method

The authors study the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model with excluded volume effects (EVE), assuming all baryons and antibaryons have the same volume v_B = (4π/3)r_B^3 with r_B = 0.8 fm, using a Boltzmann distribution approximation for baryons and an ideal Bose gas approximation for mesons. They define the effective degrees of freedom (EDOF) as g_μ(T) = P/T^4 and derive its relation to the trace anomaly Δ = ε - 3P, then impose two c-theorem-like conditions: (1) ∂g_μ/∂T ≥ 0 (equivalent to Δ - μn_B ≥ 0) and (2) ∂²g_μ/∂T² ≥ 0 (convex downward EDOF). The limiting temperatures from these conditions are extracted numerically using hadron resonance data from the Particle Data Group (2024).

## Key numbers

- The first condition (∂g_μ/∂T ≥ 0) gives a limiting temperature T = 0.274 GeV at μ = 0, where the baryon contribution Δ_B vanishes; this is close to the pure gluonic transition temperature T_d = 0.285 GeV
- The second condition (convex downward EDOF) gives a limiting temperature T = 0.197 GeV at μ = 0, where Δ_B/T^5 has its maximum, close to T_χ,max = 0.195 GeV from baryon number fluctuation analysis
- At μ = 0.6 GeV, the first condition gives a limiting temperature T = 0.169 GeV where Δ_B - μn_B vanishes, while the second condition gives T = 0.111 GeV where (Δ_B - μn_B)/T^5 has its maximum
- The baryon volume is set to v_B = (4π/3)r_B^3 with r_B = 0.8 fm

## Context

This work extends the analysis of limiting temperatures in the HRG model with excluded volume effects, previously studied using baryon number fluctuations by Oshima (2026) and the R=0 criterion in thermodynamic geometry by Castorina (2018, 2019, 2020) and Zhang (2019). The c-theorem-like conditions are inspired by the two-dimensional conformal field theory c-theorem of Zamolodchikov (1986) and Casini (2006). The results enable connecting the limiting temperature from the convexity condition to the lattice QCD predicted critical point, while the weaker condition relates to the pure gluonic deconfinement temperature from Borsanyi (2022) and Giusti (2025).
