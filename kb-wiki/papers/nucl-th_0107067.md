# (e,e'N) and (e,e'NN) experiments at NIKHEF, Mainz and JLab and open problems in one- and two-nucleon emission

> A critical review is presented of recent data on direct one- and two-nucleon emission obtained in electron scattering at NIKHEF, Mainz and JLab. In the case of (e,e$'$p) reactions attention is focussed on extracting spectroscopic factors, looking at medium effects on the bound nucleon form factors, and investigating nuclear transparency. The possibility of obtaining information on nucleon-nucleon correlations in (e,e$'$pp) and (e,e$'$pn) reactions is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the apparent \(Q^2\) dependence of spectroscopic factors extracted from (e,e'p) data, jumping from values around 0.6–0.7 at low \(Q^2\) to near unity at \(Q^2 \ge 1\) GeV\(^2\), indicates that something is missing in the theoretical treatment of the reaction mechanism, rather than reflecting a genuine change in nuclear structure.

## Method

The author reviews experimental data from NIKHEF, Mainz, and JLab on (e,e'p) and (e,e'NN) reactions, comparing nonrelativistic and relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) calculations. For (e,e'p), the focus is on extracting spectroscopic factors, studying nuclear transparency via the ratio of experimental to plane-wave cross sections, and measuring polarization observables (induced polarization \(P^N\) and polarization transfer \(P'^S/P'^L\)). For (e,e'NN), the review covers triple-coincidence measurements of two-nucleon knockout, where the cross section is analyzed in terms of one-body currents (sensitive to correlations) and two-body currents (meson-exchange and isobar configurations), with emphasis on superparallel kinematics to separate longitudinal and transverse structure functions.

## Key numbers

- Spectroscopic factors for \(^{16}\)O p-shell holes from low-\(Q^2\) data: \(S_{p_{1/2}} = 0.644\) and \(S_{p_{3/2}} = 0.537\) (experiment), compared to Green function calculations giving \(S_{p_{1/2}} = 0.890\) and \(S_{p_{3/2}} = 0.914\).
- Reanalysis of \(^{12}\)C(e,e'p) data at \(Q^2 \le 0.3\) GeV\(^2\) found a reduction of s- and p-shell strength by factor \(0.57 \pm 0.02\).
- At \(Q^2 = 0.8\) GeV\(^2\) and \(\omega = 439\) MeV, extracted spectroscopic factors for \(^{16}\)O are \(S_{p_{1/2}} = 0.73\) and \(S_{p_{3/2}} = 0.71\) (Udias99) or \(S_{p_{1/2}} = 0.72\) and \(S_{p_{3/2}} = 0.67\) (Kelly99).
- The ratio \(G_E^p/G_M^p\) for a free proton decreases linearly with \(Q^2\) starting around 0.8 GeV\(^2\), measured up to 6 GeV\(^2\) at JLab.
- For \(^{16}\)O(e,e'p) at \(Q^2 = 0.8\) GeV\(^2\), the ratio \(P'^S/P'^L\) agrees with free-proton form factors within an experimental uncertainty of about 18%.
- For (e,e'pn) in superparallel kinematics at Mainz with \((\omega, q) = (215, 316)\) MeV, predicted cross sections are about one order of magnitude larger than for (e,e'pp).

## Context

This review extends the analysis of (e,e'p) data from low-\(Q^2\) experiments at NIKHEF (e.g., Lapikas reanalysis of \(^{12}\)C) to higher \(Q^2\) at JLab (e.g., Gao 1999, Malov 2000), highlighting the unresolved \(Q^2\) dependence of spectroscopic factors. It also builds on earlier theoretical work on two-nucleon emission (Gottfried 1958) and recent pioneering measurements at NIKHEF (Onderwater 1997, 1998) and Mainz (Rosner 2001). The paper enables future experiments to measure exclusive cross sections directly and to use polarization observables to disentangle final-state interactions, medium-modified form factors, and nucleon-nucleon correlations, with specific proposals at JLab (E-93-049, E-00-102) and Mainz (A1-1-97, A1-5-98).
