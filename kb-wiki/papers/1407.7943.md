# Second random-phase approximation, Thouless' theorem and the stability condition reexamined and clarified

> It has been revealed through numerical calculations that the Second Random Phase Approximation (SRPA) with the Hartree-Fock solution as its reference state results in 1) spurious states at genuinely finite energy, contrary to common expectation, and 2) unstable solutions, which within the first-order Random Phase Approximation correspond to real low-energy collective vibrations. In the present work, these shortcomings of SRPA are shown to not contradict Thouless' theorem about the energy-weighted sum rule, and their origin is traced to the violation of the stability condition. A more general theorem is proven. Formal arguments are elucidated through numerical examples. Implications for the validity of SRPA are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Second Random-Phase Approximation (SRPA) using the Hartree-Fock solution as its reference state produces spurious states at finite energy and unstable solutions, which does not contradict Thouless' theorem about the energy-weighted sum rule, but originates from the violation of the stability condition that is inherent in the SRPA formalism.

## Method

The author analyzes the formal properties of the SRPA eigenvalue problem in the particle-hole plus two-particle-two-hole space, examining the positive-definiteness of the A±B submatrices and the stability matrix. Numerical calculations are performed for the monopole and dipole response of 16O using the Brink-Boeker potential and the VUCOM potential within a harmonic-oscillator basis, solving the full SRPA eigenvalue problem for various two-particle-two-hole energy cutoffs up to exhaustion of the model space.

## Key numbers

- For the monopole response of 16O with the Brink-Boeker potential in nine shells, the total number of configurations is M_ph=20 and M_2p2h=9620, with the highest-energy two-particle-two-hole configuration at 277 MeV
- The energy-weighted sums are identical to several digits: 9.073636×10^3 e^2 fm^4 for the isoscalar operator and 1.504700×10^4 e^2 fm^4 for the isovector operator
- For the dipole channel of 16O in seven shells, M_ph=38 and M_2p2h=11144
- The RPA spurious state is obtained at i49 keV (imaginary), while upon exhaustion of the two-particle-two-hole space the spurious state is found at -2 MeV
- For two-particle-two-hole energy cutoff larger than approximately 60 MeV, the A-B matrix acquires a negative eigenvalue

## Context

This work extends and clarifies the findings of Papakonstantinou (2009, 2010) which first demonstrated that fully self-consistent HF-SRPA calculations produce spurious states at finite energy and unstable solutions, contrary to expectations from the formal derivations of Yannouleas (1987). The paper reconciles these numerical observations with Thouless' theorem (1961) by showing that the stability condition proven for RPA does not extend to SRPA, and proves a generalized version of the energy-weighted sum rule that accommodates antinormal solutions. The results imply that SRPA with a Hartree-Fock reference state is poorly formulated and that future work must address the stability condition, potentially through subtraction procedures as discussed by Tse (2013) or by incorporating higher-order correlations.
