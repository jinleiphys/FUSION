# Neutron star properties with careful parameterization in the (axial)vector meson extended linear sigma model

> The existence of quark matter inside the cores of heavy neutron stars is a possibility which can be probed with modern astrophysical observations. We use an (axial)vector meson extended quark-meson model to describe quark matter in the core of neutron stars. We discover that an additional parameter constraint is necessary in the quark model to ensure chiral restoration at high densities. By investigating hybrid star sequences with various parameter sets we show that low sigma meson masses are needed to fulfill the upper radius constraints, and that the maximum mass of stable hybrid stars is only slightly dependent on the parameters of the crossover-type phase transition. Using this observation and results from recent astrophysical measurements a constraint of 2.6 < g_V < 4.3 is set for the constituent quark - vector meson coupling. The effect of a nonzero bag constant is also investigated and we observe that its effect is small for values adopted in previous works.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that an additional asymptotic constraint (3h₁ + 2h₂ + 2h₃ < 0) is necessary in the (axial)vector meson extended linear sigma model to prevent the unphysical recurrence of chiral symmetry breaking at high quark chemical potentials, and that with this constraint the maximum mass of stable hybrid stars depends only weakly on the parameters of the crossover-type phase transition.

## Method

The authors use a three-flavor (axial)vector meson extended linear sigma model with constituent quarks at zero temperature and finite baryon chemical potential, solving the coupled field equations for scalar and vector condensates under beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions. They construct hybrid star equations of state by interpolating between two hadronic relativistic mean-field models (SFHo and DD2) and the quark matter EoS using either a fifth-order polynomial in baryon chemical potential or a hyperbolic tangent interpolation in baryon number density. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are then solved to obtain mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities for hybrid star sequences.

## Key numbers

- The asymptotic condition requires 3h₁ + 2h₂ + 2h₃ < 0, with the fitted parameters giving h₁ = 5.8467, h₂ = -12.3456, h₃ = 3.5755 for the mσ = 290 MeV parameter set
- The vector coupling is constrained to 2.6 < g_V < 4.3 from combining the model results with recent astrophysical measurements
- The sigma meson mass is set to mσ = 290 MeV in the primary parameter set, with the experimental f₀(500) resonance ranging from 400 to 800 MeV
- The SFHo hadronic model gives a maximum neutron star mass of 2.06 M⊙ and a radius of 11.97 km for a 1.4 M⊙ star; the DD2 model gives 2.42 M⊙ and 13.26 km respectively
- The GW170817 analysis inferred Λ < 800 for 1.4 M⊙ neutron stars, corresponding to an upper radius limit of 13.6 km

## Context

This work extends previous investigations of the quark-meson model for compact stars by Zacchi et al. (2015, 2016) and the finite-temperature studies of Kovacs et al. (2016), adding the crucial asymptotic constraint to ensure proper chiral restoration at high densities. The model reproduces the meson spectrum and decay widths at zero temperature from Parganlija et al. (2012) and agrees with lattice results at finite temperature. The results enable more reliable predictions for hybrid star properties that can be tested against upcoming astrophysical observations from NICER, gravitational-wave detectors, and future facilities like CBM and NICA.
