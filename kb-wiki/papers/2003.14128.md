# Valence-Quark Distribution of the Kaon and Pion from Lattice QCD

> We present the first lattice-QCD calculation of the kaon valence-quark distribution functions using the large-momentum effective theory (LaMET) approach. The calculation is performed with multiple pion masses with the lightest one around 220 MeV, 2 lattice spacings $a=0.06$ and 0.12 fm, $(M_π)_\text{min} L \approx 5.5$, and high statistics ranging from 11,600 to 61,312 measurements. We also calculate the valence-quark distribution of pion and find it to be consistent with the FNAL E615 experimental results, and our ratio of the $u$ quark PDF in the kaon to that in the pion agrees with the CERN NA3 experiment. We also make predictions of the strange-quark distribution of the kaon.

## Key claim

This paper presents the first lattice-QCD calculation of the kaon valence-quark distribution functions using the large-momentum effective theory (LaMET) approach, with the caveat that the results are obtained at a single lattice spacing for the chiral-continuum extrapolation and rely on a parametrization-and-fit procedure to extract the PDFs from the renormalized matrix elements.

## Method

The authors use clover valence fermions on MILC ensembles with N_f=2+1+1 flavors of highly improved staggered quarks (HISQ) at two lattice spacings (a=0.06 and 0.12 fm) and three pion masses (approximately 690, 310, and 220 MeV). They calculate quasi-PDF matrix elements in coordinate space, renormalize them nonperturbatively in the RI/MOM scheme at mu_R=2.4 GeV, perform a chiral-continuum extrapolation to physical pion mass, and then convert the renormalized quasi-PDFs to lightcone PDFs in the MSbar scheme using a perturbative matching kernel with meson-mass corrections.

## Key numbers

* Lightest pion mass used: 220 MeV (a12m220L ensemble) and 217 MeV (valence pion mass)
* Lattice spacings: a=0.06 fm and a=0.12 fm
* Minimum M_pi L approximately 5.5 for the a12m220L ensemble
* Statistics range from 11,600 to 61,312 measurements per ensemble
* RI/MOM renormalization scale: mu_R=2.4 GeV
* Pion valence-quark distribution moments at 27 GeV^2: <x>_v = 0.225(18)_stat(10)_syst, <x^2>_v = 0.100(13)_stat(5)_syst, <x^3>_v = 0.056(10)_stat(2)_syst
* Kaon light-quark valence distribution moments at 27 GeV^2: <x>_v = 0.192(8)_stat(6)_syst, <x^2>_v = 0.080(7)_stat(6)_syst, <x^3>_v = 0.041(6)_stat(4)_syst
* Kaon strange-quark valence distribution moments at 27 GeV^2: <x>_v = 0.261(8)_stat(8)_syst, <x^2>_v = 0.120(7)_stat(9)_syst, <x^3>_v = 0.069(6)_stat(8)_syst
* Pion distribution approaches large-x as (1-x)^1.01
* Ratio u_v^K/u_v^pi approaches 0.4 as x goes to 1

## Context

This work extends the LaMET methodology previously applied to nucleon PDFs (Lin 2018, Chen 2018) and the pion PDF (Chen 2018, Chen 2019) to the kaon for the first time. The pion valence-quark distribution is consistent with the FNAL E615 experimental results (Conway 1989) and with an independent lattice study using the lattice cross sections approach (Sufian 2020), while the ratio u_v^K/u_v^pi agrees with the CERN NA3 experiment (Badier 1983). The predictions for the strange-quark distribution of the kaon enable reduced systematic uncertainties in future Drell-Yan experiments proposed by the COMPASS++/AMBER collaboration and in tagged deep inelastic scattering experiments at JLab and the future Electron-Ion Collider.
