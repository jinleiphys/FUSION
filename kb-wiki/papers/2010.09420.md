# Hidden spin-isospin exchange symmetry

> The strong interactions among nucleons have an approximate spin-isospin exchange symmetry that arises from the properties of quantum chromodynamics in the limit of many colors, $N_c$. However this large-$N_c$ symmetry is well hidden and reveals itself only when averaging over intrinsic spin orientations. Furthermore, the symmetry is obscured unless the momentum resolution scale is close to an optimal scale that we call $Λ_{{\rm large-}N_c}$. We show that the large-$N_c$ derivation requires a momentum resolution scale of $Λ_{{\rm large-}N_c} \sim 500$ MeV. We derive a set of spin-isospin exchange sum rules and discuss implications for the spectrum of $^{30}$P and applications to nuclear forces, nuclear structure calculations, and three-nucleon interactions.

## Key claim

The large-\(N_c\) derivation of spin-isospin exchange symmetry in nucleon-nucleon interactions is valid with relative error \(1/N_c^2\) only when the momentum resolution scale is near an optimal scale \(\Lambda_{\text{large-}N_c} \sim 500\) MeV; at other resolution scales the symmetry is obscured and the predicted hierarchy fails.

## Method

The authors derived the leading large-\(N_c\) nucleon-nucleon interaction from a non-relativistic quark model with a Hartree product state for \(N_c = 2k+1\) quarks, partitioning the quarks into two groups with maximal spin and isospin. They then applied a multipole expansion to effective nucleon-nucleon interactions built from the leading quark operators \(\bar{q}q\) and \(\bar{q}\sigma^a \tau^b q\). The resulting local interactions were tested by computing two-body matrix elements in the 1s-0d shell using the AV18 potential and the chiral N\(^3\)LO interaction, evolved via \(V_{\text{low }k}\) renormalization group flow at momentum scales from 2.0 to 3.5 fm\(^{-1}\), and comparing spin-singlet isospin-triplet \((S=0,T=1)\) and spin-triplet isospin-singlet \((S=1,T=0)\) channels.

## Key numbers

- The optimal momentum resolution scale for the large-\(N_c\) symmetry is \(\Lambda_{\text{large-}N_c} \sim 500\) MeV, corresponding to \(\Lambda = 2.5\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- For the \(^{30}\)P spectrum, the \(1^+\) ground state (spin-triplet, isospin-singlet) lies 0.677 MeV below the \(0^+\) state (spin-singlet, isospin-triplet), showing approximate degeneracy.
- Using AV18 at \(\Lambda = 2.5\) fm\(^{-1}\), the two-body matrix elements for the \(1s_{1/2}\) orbitals are \(-2.54\) MeV for the \(1^+\) state and \(-2.37\) MeV for the \(0^+\) state.
- Using chiral N\(^3\)LO at \(\Lambda = 2.5\) fm\(^{-1}\), the same matrix elements are \(-2.48\) MeV for the \(1^+\) state and \(-2.36\) MeV for the \(0^+\) state.
- At \(\Lambda = 6.0\) fm\(^{-1}\) with AV18, the matrix elements become 1.58 MeV for the \(1^+\) state and 0.19 MeV for the \(0^+\) state, violating the expected \(1/N_c^2\) error.
- Lattice chiral effective field theory at lattice spacing \(a = 1.32\) fm (momentum cutoff \(\pi/a = 471\) MeV) gives S-wave short-range couplings: \(^1\text{S}_0, Q^0 = 1.45(5)\) l.u. and \(^3\text{S}_1, Q^0 = 1.56(3)\) l.u., showing good agreement between channels.

## Context

This work extends the large-\(N_c\) analysis of nucleon-nucleon interactions by Kaplan, Savage, and Manohar (1996) and the renormalization group studies of Timoteo, Arriola, and Ruiz Arriola (2011, 2013, 2016), who noted a momentum scale where large-\(N_c\) constraints are satisfied. The authors provide a theoretical explanation for this optimal scale and demonstrate that spin-isospin exchange symmetry is a practical tool for constraining nuclear forces, three-nucleon interactions, and shell-model calculations. The results enable more reliable many-body calculations by imposing the symmetry on three-nucleon contact interactions at the appropriate cutoff scale, and they suggest that chiral effective field theory is most effective near \(\Lambda_{\text{large-}N_c}\).
