# $\mathbfα$-induced reaction cross sections in the mass range $\mathbf{A \approx 20 - 50}$: a critical review

> In a recent review it was shown that the cross sections of $α$-induced reactions in the $A \approx 20 - 50$ mass range follow a general and smooth trend in most cases. For comparison of cross sections of different targets at various energies the method of reduced cross sections $σ_{\rm{red}}$ and reduced energies $E_{\rm{red}}$ was used. Four outliers were identified: $^{36}$Ar and $^{40}$Ar with unusally small cross sections and $^{23}$Na and $^{33}$S with unusually large cross sections. New data for $^{23}$Na were presented at this NPA-7 conference; contrary to the previous data, these new data fit into the general systematics. In addition, a relation between the most effective energy $E_0$ for astrophysical reaction rates (the so-called Gamow window) and the reduced energy $E_{\rm{red}}$ is presented.

## Key claim

The cross sections of alpha-induced reactions on targets in the mass range A ≈ 20–50 mostly follow a smooth systematic trend when plotted as reduced cross sections versus reduced energy, but four outliers were previously identified; new data for ²³Na presented at the NPA-7 conference supersede earlier anomalous results and bring ²³Na into agreement with the general systematics, leaving only ³⁶Ar, ⁴⁰Ar, and ³³S as potential outliers pending further measurements.

## Method

The author uses the reduced cross section σ_red and reduced energy E_red formalism (Gomes 2005) to compare alpha-induced reaction cross sections across different target nuclei and energies, where E_red scales the center-of-mass energy by the Coulomb barrier height and σ_red scales the total reaction cross section by the geometric size of the projectile-target system. For ²³Na, two new experimental datasets from the Aarhus accelerator (forward kinematics with a NaCl target) and the ISAC facility at TRIUMF (inverse kinematics with a helium-filled gas cell) are compared to previous data and to statistical model predictions from Mohr (2015). A relation between the reduced energy and the Gamow window energy E_0 for astrophysical reaction rates is derived analytically.

## Key numbers

- The reduced Gamow energy for alpha-induced reactions in the A ≈ 20–50 mass range is approximately E_red,0 ≈ 0.284 MeV × T_9^(2/3), with explicit values: 0.284 MeV at T_9 = 1, 0.451 MeV at T_9 = 2, and 0.591 MeV at T_9 = 3.
- The function f(Z_P, Z_T, A_P, A_T) varies only by a few percent across the mass range, from f(²⁰Ne) = 2.367 to f(⁵¹V) = 2.288.
- Absolute uncertainties for the new ²³Na(p,γ)²⁶Mg data are 15–25% (Aarhus experiment) and slightly below 20% at higher energies with 30–40% at lowest energies (TRIUMF experiment).
- The target thickness was approximately 80–100 keV in the Aarhus experiment and approximately 140 keV in the TRIUMF experiment (center-of-mass system).
- The previous ²³Na data by Almarez-Calderon et al. (2014) were found to be more than one order of magnitude higher than the new data; an erratum later scaled those data down by a factor of 0.01.

## Context

This work extends the systematic review of alpha-induced reaction cross sections by Mohr (2015) for the A ≈ 20–50 mass range, which identified four outliers: ³⁶Ar and ⁴⁰Ar with unusually small cross sections (based on a single old experiment by Schwartz 1956) and ²³Na and ³³S with unusually large cross sections. The new ²³Na data from Howard et al. (2015) and Tomlinson et al. (2015) resolve the ²³Na discrepancy, and the derived relation between reduced energy and the Gamow window enables direct translation of laboratory systematics to astrophysical reaction rate calculations. New measurements for ³⁶Ar, ⁴⁰Ar, ³⁸Ar, and ³³S are needed to confirm or reject their status as outliers.
