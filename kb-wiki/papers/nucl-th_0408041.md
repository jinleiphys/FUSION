# Photo-emission of two protons from nuclei

> The photo-emission of two protons from the $^{12}$C, $^{16}$O and $^{40}$Ca nuclei is investigated. Aim of the work is the study of the possibilities offered by this probe to obtain information about the characteristics of the short-range correlations. We have also evaluated the effects of the two-body $Δ$-currents which, in this processes, compete with those produced by the short-range correlations. Our results show that ($γ$,pp) processes could be more useful than (e,e'pp) for the study of the short-range correlations.

## Key claim

The authors argue that ($\gamma$,pp) reactions could be more useful than (e,e'pp) for studying short-range correlations, but this claim is qualified by the finding that two-body $\Delta$-current contributions are small at photon energies of 100 MeV (far from the $\Delta$ resonance) and become significant only at higher energies or in specific kinematics such as symmetric kinematics.

## Method

The authors calculate ($\gamma$,pp) cross sections for $^{12}$C, $^{16}$O, and $^{40}$Ca using a nuclear model where correlated nuclear states are constructed from Slater determinants of Woods-Saxon single-particle wave functions multiplied by a Jastrow correlation function, expanded to first order in the correlation function $h_{ij}=f^2(r_{ij})-1$. The continuum single-particle wave functions for emitted protons are described using complex optical potentials (Schwandt et al. 1982) to account for final-state interactions, and the two-body $\Delta$-current contributions are evaluated from diagrams involving chargeless pion exchange, with the convection current included (unlike their prior (e,e'pp) work).

## Key numbers

- The commonly accepted value of the pion-nucleon coupling constant is $f^2_{\pi NN}/4\pi = 0.079$.
- For $^{16}$O($\gamma$,pp)$^{14}$C at $\omega=100$ MeV, $\epsilon_2=40$ MeV, and $\theta_2=0^\circ$, the uncertainty from different optical potentials produces cross section variations of about 10%.
- The ratio of integrated cross sections $S(C)/S(C\Delta)$ for $^{16}$O at $\omega=100$ MeV is close to unity for most final states and $\theta_2$ values, indicating small $\Delta$-current effects, while at $\omega=215$ MeV the ratios are smaller (larger $\Delta$ effects), with some states showing ratios below 0.8.
- In superparallel back-to-back kinematics for $^{16}$O at $\omega=100$ MeV, the ratio of cross sections without and with $\Delta$ currents for the $0^+_1$ state is approximately 2.06 when using real Woods-Saxon versus Schwandt optical potential, and 1.09 for Nadasen versus Schwandt.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior studies of short-range correlation effects in inclusive electron scattering (Co' 1998, Mok 2000, Co' 2001), (e,e'p) (Mok 2001), ($\gamma$,p) (Anguiano 2002), and (e,e'pp) (Anguiano 2003) to the ($\gamma$,pp) case. The approach is similar to that of the Pavia group (Giusti 1992, Schiavilla 2004) which uses Woods-Saxon wave functions and optical potentials, but differs in the treatment of short-range correlations (expansion in the correlation function versus two-nucleon spectral functions). The results suggest that ($\gamma$,pp) at photon energies around 100 MeV may provide a cleaner probe of short-range correlations than (e,e'pp), since the $\Delta$-current contributions are smaller for real photons than for electrons at comparable momentum transfers, though the authors note that direct comparison with experimental data (Kan 1987) requires further work accounting for detector acceptances.
