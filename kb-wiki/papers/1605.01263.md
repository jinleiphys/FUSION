# Direct Bethe-Salpeter solutions in Minkowski space

> We review a method to directly solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation in Minkowski space, both for bound and scattering states. It is based on a proper treatment of the many singularities which appear in the kernel and propagators. The off-mass shell scattering amplitude for spinless particles interacting by a one boson exchange was computed for the first time. Using our Minkowski space solutions for the initial (bound) and final (scattering) states, we calculate elastic and transition (bound to scattering state) electromagnetic form factors. The conservation of the transition electromagnetic current J.q=0, verified numerically, confirms the validity of our solutions.

## Key claim

The authors claim to have directly solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation in Minkowski space for both bound and scattering states, including the first computation of the off-mass shell scattering amplitude for spinless particles interacting by one boson exchange; however, the method is currently demonstrated only for spinless particles and the numerical verification of current conservation is presented as the primary validation of the solutions.

## Method

The Bethe-Salpeter equation is solved directly in Minkowski space by treating the singularities in the kernel and propagators. The off-shell partial wave amplitude is factorized so that the strongest singularities are included in a known analytical prefactor, and the remaining regular part is expanded in a spline basis. The on-shell amplitude is obtained from the off-shell solution by imposing the mass-shell condition \(k_0=0, k=p_s\), and the phase shifts are extracted from the on-shell amplitude. Elastic and transition electromagnetic form factors are then calculated using the Minkowski space solutions for the initial bound state and final scattering state.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies obtained from the direct method coincide within four-digit accuracy with previous calculations from the authors' prior work (Carbonell and Karmanov, 2014).
- The Bethe-Salpeter scattering length \(a_0\) as a function of coupling constant \(\alpha\) is computed for \(\mu=0.50\) (in units of mass \(m\)), with the first bound state appearing at \(\alpha_0=1.02\) for the Bethe-Salpeter treatment and \(\alpha_0=0.840\) for the non-relativistic treatment.
- The transition electromagnetic form factor is computed for an initial bound state with binding energy \(B=0.01\,m\) (initial state mass \(M=1.99\,m\)) and a final scattering state with relative momentum \(p_s=0.1\,m\) (final state mass \(M'=2.00998\,m\)).
- The imaginary part of the phase shift appears starting from the first inelastic meson-production threshold \(p^{(1)}_s=0.75\) and displays quadratic behavior \(\text{Im}[\delta_0]\sim (p_s-p^{(1)}_s)^2\) near threshold.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous bound-state Bethe-Salpeter solutions in Minkowski space (Carbonell and Karmanov, 2014) to include scattering states, and provides the first direct computation of the off-shell scattering amplitude. It reproduces phase shifts found in earlier work by Tjon (1975) and scattering lengths found by Frederico, Salme, and Viviani (2012) using a different method. The method is applicable to any energy domain and can be generalized to fermions, opening the way for studying the nucleon-nucleon system in the Bethe-Salpeter framework.
