# Two-loop analysis of the pion-mass dependence of the $ρ$ meson

> Analyzing the pion-mass dependence of $ππ$ scattering phase shifts beyond the low-energy region requires the unitarization of the amplitudes from chiral perturbation theory. In the two-flavor theory, unitarization via the inverse-amplitude method (IAM) can be justified from dispersion relations, which is therefore expected to provide reliable predictions for the pion-mass dependence of results from lattice QCD calculations. In this work, we provide compact analytic expression for the two-loop partial-wave amplitudes for $J=0,1,2$ required for the IAM at subleading order. To analyze the pion-mass dependence of recent lattice QCD results for the $P$-wave, we develop a fit strategy that for the first time allows us to perform stable two-loop IAM fits and assess the chiral convergence of the IAM approach. While the comparison of subsequent orders suggests a breakdown scale not much below the $ρ$ mass, a detailed understanding of the systematic uncertainties of lattice QCD data is critical to obtain acceptable fits, especially at larger pion masses.

## Key claim
The two-loop inverse-amplitude method (IAM) fits to lattice QCD data for the $\rho$ meson improve the fit quality compared to one-loop fits, but the comparison of successive chiral orders suggests that the breakdown scale of the chiral expansion is not much below the $\rho$ mass; a detailed understanding of lattice systematic uncertainties is critical for acceptable fits, especially at larger pion masses.

## Method
The authors derived compact analytic expressions for the two-loop (NNLO) partial-wave amplitudes for $\pi\pi$ scattering with angular momentum $J=0,1,2$ in two-flavor chiral perturbation theory (ChPT). They unitarized these amplitudes using the inverse-amplitude method (IAM) at both NLO and NNLO, then performed global fits to lattice QCD energy levels from the CLS collaboration (six ensembles at pion masses 200–284 MeV) and the HadSpec collaboration (ensembles at 236 and 391 MeV). The fits minimized the $\chi^2$ between the IAM-predicted and lattice-computed energy levels via Lüscher's quantization condition, using differential evolution followed by Powell's method, with the pion decay constant fitted simultaneously.

## Key numbers
- NLO fit to CLS data: $\chi^2/\text{dof} = 216 / (122 - 9) = 1.91$; NNLO fit: $\chi^2/\text{dof} = 165 / (123 - 15) = 1.53$
- $\rho$ mass at physical point from NLO fit: $M_\rho = 761.4(5.1)(0.3)(24.7)\ \text{MeV}$ (statistical, lattice spacing, truncation errors); from NNLO fit: $M_\rho = 750(12)(1)(1)\ \text{MeV}$
- $\rho$ width at physical point from NLO fit: $\Gamma_\rho = 150.9(4.4)(0.1)(4.9)\ \text{MeV}$; from NNLO fit: $\Gamma_\rho = 129(12)(1)(1)\ \text{MeV}$
- Pion decay constant in chiral limit from NLO fit: $F = 88.27(0.23)(0.04)(2.86)\ \text{MeV}$; from NNLO fit: $F = 93.7(2.3)(0.1)(0.2)\ \text{MeV}$
- Bayesian information criterion (BIC) improves from 259 (NLO) to 237 (NNLO), with $\Delta \text{BIC} = 22$ providing very strong evidence for the NNLO over NLO IAM

## Context
This work extends previous one-loop IAM analyses of the $\rho$ meson's pion-mass dependence (Hanhart 2008, Nebreda 2010, Pelaez 2010, Nebreda 2011) to two-loop order, providing compact analytic expressions for the two-loop partial-wave amplitudes that were previously unavailable. It enables systematic assessment of chiral convergence in unitarized ChPT fits to lattice QCD data, and provides a framework for future high-precision analyses of other partial waves (including the $f_0(500)$) as lattice calculations improve. The results are compared to the dispersive Roy-equation analysis of Garcia-Martin (2011) for the $\rho$ parameters at the physical point.
