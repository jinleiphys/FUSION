# Effective Group Structure in the Interacting Boson Model

> Spectrum generating algebras are used in various fields of physics as models to determine quantum structure, including energy levels and transition strengths. The advantage of such models is that their group structure allows an extensive understanding of the system being studied. In addition they possess a simple classical limit, at least for bosonic systems. In this paper we discuss an algebraic model of nuclear structure, the Interacting Boson Model (IBM), and show that in one limit its group structure is particularly simple. For zero angular momentum there is an effective lower dimensional group structure which describes the system both classically and quantum mechanically.

# Effective Group Structure in the Interacting Boson Model

## Key claim

For zero angular momentum states in the Interacting Boson Model (IBM), the full U(6) group structure reduces to an effective U(3) group structure that describes the system both classically and quantum mechanically, but this effective model constitutes a semiclassical approximation to the original U(6) problem rather than an exact equivalence, with discrepancies appearing at next-to-leading order in 1/N.

## Method

The author first obtains the classical limit of the IBM quantum model using coherent states and a time-dependent variational approximation valid for large boson number. Imposing zero angular momentum suppresses three of the six classical degrees of freedom, leaving three variables (α_s, α_0, α_p) whose nine bilinear products generate a U(3) algebra. The author then identifies the effective group chains U(3) ⊃ U(2) ⊃ SO(2), U(3) ⊃ U(1) × U(1), and U(3) ⊃ SO(3) ⊃ SO(2) as the zero-angular-momentum analogues of the standard IBM dynamical symmetries. Finally, the reduced classical model is requantised with the same operator ordering as the original Hamiltonian to obtain a new quantum model.

## Key numbers

- The U(3) representation is labelled by N, the same boson number as in the original U(6) model.
- For the U(2) chain, the U(2) representation label n takes all integer values from 0 to N, and SO(2) representations are μ = -n, -n+2, ..., n.
- For N=6, the effective U(3) model yields exactly seven states in each group chain, matching the original U(6) model's J=0 state count.
- The SO(2) quantum number μ is restricted to values non-negative and divisible by three (μ = 0, 3, 6 for N=6).
- For the U(1) × U(1) chain with N=6, the allowed (a,b) pairs are seven in number, identical to the (λ,μ) labels of the original SU(3) chain.
- The eigenenergies at the U(5) dynamical symmetry limit are E_i = N n_{di}, agreeing exactly with the original U(6) result.
- The Hamiltonian at the U(1) × U(1) limit (χ̄=1, η=0) yields oscillation frequencies Ω_± = 6N - 12J_± - 6J_∓, which for small oscillations are approximately degenerate near 6N.

## Context

This work extends the algebraic approach to nuclear structure known as the Interacting Boson Model (IBM), originally developed by Arima and Iachello, by showing that the J=0 subspace possesses a simpler U(3) effective group structure. The dimensional reduction for zero angular momentum was previously noticed in a different context by Paar (1990) and in the author's thesis. The result enables detailed studies of chaos in collective nuclei using two-dimensional classical phase space, amenable to Poincaré sections and periodic orbit analysis, following approaches by Heller (1984) and others. The philosophy of finding the classical limit and requantising is similar to that of Hatch and Levit (1985).
