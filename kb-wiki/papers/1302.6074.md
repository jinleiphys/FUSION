# Structure of the hadron-quark mixed phase in protoneutron stars

> We study the hadron-quark phase transition in the interior of hot protoneutron stars, combining the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach for hadronic matter with the MIT bag model or the Dyson-Schwinger model for quark matter. We examine the structure of the mixed phase constructed according to different prescriptions for the phase transition, and the resulting consequences for stellar properties. We find important effects for the internal composition, but only very small influence on the global stellar properties.

## Key claim

The internal composition of hybrid protoneutron stars is strongly affected by the choice between global charge neutral (GCN) and local charge neutral (LCN) constructions for the hadron-quark mixed phase, but the impact on global stellar properties such as mass and equation of state is very small, making these global observables unlikely to reveal the type of phase transition or internal structure.

## Method

The authors combined a Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach for hadronic matter (using Argonne V18 nucleon-nucleon and Nijmegen NSC89 nucleon-hyperon potentials with Urbana UIX three-body forces) with two quark matter models: an extended MIT bag model with a density-dependent bag constant and a Dyson-Schwinger model (DSM, specifically model DS4). They constructed the hadron-quark mixed phase using two limiting prescriptions: the GCN (Bulk Gibbs/Glendenning) construction, which assumes a small Wigner-Seitz cell with constant electron density, and the LCN (Maxwell-like) construction, which assumes local charge neutrality in each phase. Calculations were performed for trapped matter with lepton fraction Y_e = 0.4 under isentropic (S/A = 2) and isothermal (T = 40 MeV) conditions, as well as for untrapped cold matter.

## Key numbers

- For the MIT bag model, the hadron-quark phase transition starts at fairly low baryon density and a pure quark phase is reached at not too large density; with the DSM, the onset occurs at higher density and the system remains in the mixed phase even at very large density.
- For the MIT model with the hadron-quark phase transition, the maximum mass of hybrid stars is about 1.5 solar masses in all configurations (trapped and untrapped).
- For the DSM with hyperons excluded, the maximum mass for protoneutron stars is about 1.75 solar masses, while for cold neutron stars it is slightly smaller.
- The trapping condition shifts the onset of the mixed phase to slightly higher density in all cases.
- The low-density matching to the Shen EOS occurs below baryon density n_B less than about 0.07 fm^{-3}.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by the same group on static properties of protoneutron stars using finite-temperature hadronic equations of state including hyperons (Chen et al., previous papers) and earlier treatments of the hadron-quark phase transition with the MIT bag model and Dyson-Schwinger model. The paper compares two idealized phase transition constructions (GCN and LCN) that represent opposite limiting cases of the full microscopic treatment of finite-size effects, as discussed by Yasutake et al. (2012) and Maruyama et al. (2005-2008). The results show that while internal composition differs substantially between constructions, global observables are insensitive, motivating future detailed microscopic investigations of finite-size effects for stellar microphysics such as cooling, transport, and oscillations.
