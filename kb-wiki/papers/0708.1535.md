# Three-Nucleon Scattering at Intermediate Energies

> By means of a technique, which does not employ partial wave (PW) decompositions, the nucleon-deuteron break-up process is evaluated in the Faddeev scheme, where only the leading order term of the amplitude is considered. This technique is then applied to calculate the semi-exclusive proton-deuteron break-up reaction d(p,n)pp for proton laboratory energies Elab of a few hundred MeV. A comparison with PW calculations is performed at 197 MeV projectile energy. At the same energy rescattering processes, which are not included in the 3D calculations yet, are shown to be still important in the full Faddeev PW calculations, especially for the cross section and the analyzing power Ay. Next, kinematical relativistic effects are investigated for projectile energies up to about 500 MeV. At the higher energies, those relativistic effects start not to be negligible, especially in the peak of the cross section.

# Three-Nucleon Scattering at Intermediate Energies

## Key claim

The leading-order term of the Faddeev multiple scattering series, evaluated without partial wave decompositions using a 3D momentum-space technique, may sufficiently describe the nucleon-deuteron break-up process at intermediate energies of a few hundred MeV, but rescattering processes not yet included in the 3D calculations remain important at 197 MeV for the cross section and analyzing power, and relativistic kinematic effects become non-negligible above about 200 MeV, especially for the quasi-free peak position.

## Method

The authors evaluated the nucleon-deuteron break-up amplitude using only the leading term \(TP\) of the Faddeev transition operator, working directly with momentum vector states rather than partial wave decompositions. They applied this 3D technique to the semi-exclusive proton-deuteron break-up reaction \(d(p,n)pp\) for proton laboratory energies up to about 500 MeV, using the AV18 and Bonn-B nucleon-nucleon potentials. Relativistic kinematics were introduced by re-evaluating Jacobi momenta, applying Lorentz transformations to two- and three-particle center-of-mass subsystems, and using relativistic energy-momentum relations and a relativistic cross section description.

## Key numbers

- At \(E_{lab} = 197\) MeV, the 3D and partial wave calculations (with NN angular momenta up to \(j = 5\) and \(7\), and 3N total angular momenta up to \(J = 31/2\)) show deviations in the cross section peak, indicating the PW calculation has not sufficiently converged.
- At \(E_{lab} = 197\) MeV and \(\theta = 37^\circ\), full Faddeev PW calculations including rescattering lower the cross section and are essential for describing the analyzing power \(A_y\), especially for small outgoing neutron energies.
- Relativistic kinematics shift the quasi-free peak position and height; at \(E_{lab} = 495\) MeV and \(\theta = 18^\circ\), the relativistic calculation places the peak at the correct position relative to data, while the nonrelativistic calculation does not.
- For energies below 200 MeV, the 3D calculations show perfect agreement with PW calculations.

## Context

This work extends the 3D momentum-space approach previously applied to three-boson scattering (Refs. [3bosons3d]) and to nucleon-nucleon scattering and the deuteron (Fachruddin, Elster, Gloeckle, earlier papers) by applying it to three-nucleon break-up with spin and isospin. It provides a viable alternative to the traditional partial wave technique (Faddeev, 1960s) which becomes increasingly difficult at higher energies due to the proliferation of angular momentum states. The calculations enable future inclusion of rescattering terms in the 3D framework and systematic study of relativistic effects in three-nucleon scattering up to 500 MeV, with experimental data available from Refs. [495] and [197] for comparison.
