# Transverse momentum balance of dijets in Xe+Xe collisions at the LHC

> We present a theoretical study of the medium modifications of the $p_T$ balance ($x_J$) of dijets in Xe+Xe collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.44$ TeV. The initial production of dijets was carried out using the POWHEG+PYTHIA8 prescription, which matches the next-to-leading-order (NLO) QCD matrix elements with the parton shower (PS) effect. The SHELL model described the in-medium evolution of nucleus-nucleus collisions using a transport approach. The theoretical results of the dijet $x_J$ in the Xe+Xe collisions exhibit more imbalanced distributions than those in the p+p collisions, consistent with recently reported ATLAS data. By utilizing the Interleaved Flavor Neutralisation, an infrared-and-collinear-safe jet flavor algorithm, to identify the flavor of the reconstructed jets, we classify dijets processes into three categories: gluon-gluon ($gg$), quark-gluon ($qg$), and quark-quark ($qq$), and investigated the respective medium modification patterns and fraction changes of the $gg$, $qg$, and $qq$ components of the dijet sample in Xe+Xe collisions. It is shown that the increased fraction of $qg$ component at a small $x_J$ contributes to the imbalance of the dijet; in particular, the $q_1g_2$ (quark-jet-leading) dijets experience more significant asymmetric energy loss than the $g_1q_2$ (gluon-jet-leading) dijets traversing the QGP. By comparing the $Δ\langle x_J\rangle = \langle x_J \rangle_{pp} - \langle x_J \rangle_{AA}$ of inclusive, $c\bar{c}$ and $b\bar{b}$ dijets in Xe+Xe collisions, we observe $Δ\langle x_J \rangle_{incl.}>Δ\langle x_J \rangle_{c\bar{c}}>Δ\langle x_J \rangle_{b\bar{b}}$. Moreover, $ρ_{Xe, Pb}$, the ratios of the nuclear modification factors of dijets in Xe+Xe to those in Pb+Pb, were calculated, which indicates that the yield suppression of dijets in Pb+Pb is more pronounced than that in Xe+Xe owing to the larger radius of the lead nucleus.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first theoretical study of dijet transverse momentum balance in Xe+Xe collisions, showing that the SHELL transport model combined with POWHEG+PYTHIA8 initial-state generation reproduces the recently reported ATLAS data, with the caveat that the calculation overestimates the balanced (x_J near 1) region in central 0%-10% collisions for the highest p_T,1 interval (158-199 GeV/c).

## Method

The initial dijet production was generated using the POWHEG-BOX-V2 framework matched with PYTHIA 8.309 parton shower at NLO QCD, with jets reconstructed using the anti-k_T algorithm (R=0.4) in FastJet. The in-medium evolution of both light- and heavy-flavor dijets was simulated using the SHELL transport model, which incorporates elastic energy loss via modified Langevin equations for heavy quarks and Hard-Thermal Loop pQCD for massless partons, plus inelastic energy loss via the higher-twist formalism for medium-induced gluon radiation. The Interleaved Flavor Neutralisation (IFN) algorithm was used to classify dijets into gg, qg, and qq flavor categories at the parton level.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: sqrt(s_NN) = 5.44 TeV for Xe+Xe, compared to sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV for Pb+Pb
- Leading jet p_T,1 > 100 GeV/c, subleading jet p_T,2 > 32 GeV/c; azimuthal separation Delta_phi >= 7pi/8; rapidity |y| < 2.1
- Six p_T,1 intervals for p+p baseline: [100, 112], [112, 126], [126, 141], [141, 158], [158, 171], and [171, 200] GeV/c
- Four centrality bins for Xe+Xe: 0%-10%, 10%-20%, 20%-40%, and 40%-80%, with average impact parameters 3.02, 5.24, 7.41, and 10.49 fm respectively
- The hierarchy of average x_J shift between p+p and A+A collisions: Delta<x_J>_incl. > Delta<x_J>_c-cbar > Delta<x_J>_b-bbar for the same centrality bin
- The ratio rho_Xe,Pb of nuclear modification factors is greater than one for all centrality bins, indicating weaker dijet yield suppression in Xe+Xe than in Pb+Pb

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dijet momentum imbalance in Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV and 5.02 TeV by ATLAS (2017, 2022) and CMS (2011, 2012, 2018) to the smaller xenon collision system, enabling investigation of system-size dependence of jet quenching. The flavor-dependent analysis using the IFN algorithm builds on prior theoretical work on partonic energy loss (Gyulassy 2003, Wang 1998) and the dead-cone effect for heavy quarks (Dokshitzer 2001, Zhang 2003), and the mass hierarchy results Delta<x_J>_incl. > Delta<x_J>_c-cbar > Delta<x_J>_b-bbar provide a testbed for future measurements of heavy-flavor dijets.
