# The complete tree-level result up to order $1/Q$ for polarized deep-inelastic leptoproduction

> We present the results of the tree-level calculation of deep-inelastic leptoproduction, including polarization of target hadron and produced hadron. We also discuss the dependence on transverse momenta of the quarks, which leads to azimuthal asymmetries for the produced hadrons.

## Key claim

The paper presents the complete tree-level calculation of semi-inclusive deep-inelastic leptoproduction up to subleading order \(1/Q\), including polarization of both the target hadron and the produced hadron, and shows that transverse momentum dependence of quarks leads to azimuthal asymmetries in the produced hadrons; the authors note that while factorization theorems exist for transverse-momentum-integrated functions, they do not exist for the unintegrated distributions, so the tree-level result represents an important step but QCD corrections such as Sudakov effects may affect the asymmetries.

## Method

The authors perform a tree-level diagrammatic expansion of the hadronic tensor for semi-inclusive lepton-hadron scattering, expressing the cross section in terms of quark-quark correlation functions for the target hadron and quark fragmentation functions for the produced hadron. They decompose these correlation functions into twist-two and twist-three distribution and fragmentation functions using Dirac projections, working in a collinear frame where the two hadrons are collinear and keeping terms up to order \(1/Q\). The analysis includes the full dependence on transverse momenta of quarks and uses the QCD equations of motion to relate twist-three functions to twist-two functions plus interaction-dependent quark-gluon correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- The cross section is given by \(\frac{d\sigma}{d\xbj\,dy\,dz_h\,d^2\bqt} = \frac{\pi\,\alpha^2}{2Q^4}\,y\,z_h\,L_{\mu \nu}\, 2M{\cal W}^{\mu \nu}\), with \(Q^2 = -q^2\) and invariants \(\xbj \equiv Q^2/2P\cdot q\), \(y \equiv P\cdot q/P\cdot l\), and \(z_h \equiv P\cdot P_h/P\cdot q\)
- Six twist-two quark distribution functions are identified: \(f_1(x,\bpt^2)\), \(g_{1L}(x,\bpt^2)\), \(g_{1T}(x,\bpt^2)\), \(h_{1T}(x,\bpt^2)\), \(h_{1L}^\perp(x,\bpt^2)\), and \(h_{1T}^\perp(x,\bpt^2)\)
- Eight twist-three distribution functions appear at subleading order: \(e(x,\bpt^2)\), \(f^\perp(x,\bpt^2)\), \(g_T^\prime(x,\bpt^2)\), \(g_L^\perp(x,\bpt^2)\), \(g_T^\perp(x,\bpt^2)\), \(h_T^\perp(x,\bpt^2)\), \(h_L(x,\bpt^2)\), and \(h_T(x,\bpt^2)\)
- After \(\bpt\)-integration, only three twist-two functions (\(f_1\), \(g_1 = g_{1L}\), \(h_1\)) and three twist-three functions (\(e\), \(g_T\), \(h_L\)) remain nonvanishing
- The leading-order structure functions for inclusive scattering are \(F_2(\xbj,Q^2)/\xbj = 2F_1(\xbj,Q^2) = \sum_a e_a^2\,(f_1^{a}(\xbj) + \bar f_1^{a}(\xbj))\) and \(2g_1(\xbj,Q^2) = \sum_a e_a^2\,(g_1^{a}(\xbj) + \bar g_1^{a}(\xbj))\)

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Ralston and Soper (1979) and the authors' own previous work (Tangerman and Mulders, 1995) by providing the complete tree-level result up to subleading order \(1/Q\) for semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering, including all polarization degrees of freedom. The paper builds on the operator product expansion framework of Jaffe and Ji (1991, 1992) for twist-two and twist-three quark distributions and extends it to include transverse-momentum-dependent distribution and fragmentation functions. The results enable future experimental searches for azimuthal asymmetries at DESY, CERN, or Fermilab, and provide the theoretical basis for extracting transverse-momentum-dependent parton distributions from semi-inclusive measurements, though the authors caution that factorization theorems for unintegrated distributions do not yet exist (Collins, Soper, and Sterman, 1983; Collins, 1993).
