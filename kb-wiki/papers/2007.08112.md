# Hybrid stars from a three-flavor NJL model with two kinds of tensor condensates

> To obtain the equation of state of quark matter and construct hybrid stars, we calculate the thermodynamic potential in the three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model including the tensor-type four-point interaction and the Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft interaction. To construct the hybrid stars, it is necessary to impose the beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions on the system. It is shown that tensor condensed phases appear at large chemical potential. Under the possibility of the existence of the tensor condensates, the relationship between the radius and mass of hybrid stars is estimated.

## Key claim

The paper shows that tensor condensates can appear in three-flavor quark matter under beta equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions at large chemical potential, but only when the tensor coupling strength \(G_T\) exceeds a threshold relative to the scalar coupling \(G_s\); the resulting hybrid star mass-radius relation depends on whether one or both of the two possible tensor condensates (\(F_3\) and \(F_8\)) are present.

## Method

The authors calculate the thermodynamic potential at zero temperature within the three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model including a tensor-type four-point interaction and the Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft (determinant) interaction, using a mean-field approximation that retains only diagonal flavor components. They impose beta equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions, solve the gap equations for the chiral condensates and the two tensor condensates \(F_3\) and \(F_8\), and then compute the equation of state to construct hybrid stars by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations.

## Key numbers

* The three-momentum cutoff is \(\Lambda = 0.6314\) GeV; the scalar coupling constant is \(G_s = 3.666/\Lambda^2 \approx 9.2\) GeV\(^{-2}\); the current quark masses are \(m_u = m_d = 0.0055\) GeV and \(m_s = 0.1375\) GeV; the Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft coupling is \(G_D = 9.288/\Lambda^5\).
* For model GT2.0, the tensor condensate \(F_8\) appears at \(\mu_q = \mu_{cr_1} \approx 0.488\) GeV; for model GT2.2 at \(\mu_{cr_1} \approx 0.475\) GeV; for model GT2.4 at \(\mu_{cr_1} \approx 0.460\) GeV.
* For model GT2.6, both tensor condensates \(F_3\) and \(F_8\) appear simultaneously at \(\mu_q = \mu_{cr_{1'}} \approx 0.446\) GeV.
* In models GT2.0, GT2.2, and GT2.4, the second tensor condensate \(F_3\) appears gradually at \(\mu_q = \mu_{cr_2} \approx 0.54\), 0.52, and 0.51 GeV, respectively.
* The baryon number density jumps at \(\mu_q \approx 0.34\) GeV for all models, corresponding to chiral restoration for light quarks.
* The electron chemical potential takes its maximum value near \(\mu_q \approx 0.44\) GeV in model GT0.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of spin polarization via tensor condensates in the three-flavor NJL model (Morimoto et al., 2019; Kagawa et al., 2019) by imposing the beta equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions necessary for compact star construction. It also builds on earlier two-flavor NJL investigations of hybrid stars with tensor interaction (Morimoto et al., 2019). The results enable future calculations of hybrid star properties such as mass and radius that account for the possibility of tensor-condensed quark matter in the inner core.
