# Orbital momentum effects due to a liquid nature of transient state

> It is argued that directed flow $v_1$, the observable introduced for description of nucleus collisions, can be used for the detection of the nature of state of the matter in the transient state of hadron and nuclei collisions. We consider a possible origin of the directed flow in hadronic reactions as a result of rotation of the transient matter and trace analogy with nucleus collisions. Our proposal it that the presence of directed flow can serve as a signal that transient matter is in a liquid state.

# Orbital momentum effects due to a liquid nature of transient state

## Key claim

The authors propose that the directed flow observable $v_1$, originally introduced for nucleus collisions, can serve as a signal that the transient state of matter in hadronic and nuclear collisions is in a liquid state, provided that the observed directed flow originates from coherent rotation of a strongly interacting quark-pion liquid in the overlap region.

## Method

The authors extend a previously developed model of elastic hadron scattering, based on spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking in QCD and a shock-wave mechanism for condensate cloud interaction, to particle production. They assume that valence constituent quarks excite parts of a rotating quark-pion liquid in the overlap region, and that the resulting hadronization of these droplets produces directed flow. The orbital angular momentum of the transient state is estimated from the collision geometry and the fraction of energy carried by condensate clouds, and the directed flow is argued to arise from the coherent rotation of the liquid with a single angular velocity.

## Key numbers

- The number of generated massive virtual quarks in the overlap region scales as $\tilde{N}(s,b) \propto (1-\langle k_Q\rangle)\sqrt{s}/m_Q$, where $m_Q$ is the constituent quark mass and $\langle k_Q\rangle$ is the average fraction of hadron energy carried by valence quarks.
- The interaction radius of a valence quark is given by $R_Q = \kappa/m_Q$, where $m_Q$ is the constituent quark mass.
- The total orbital angular momentum of the transient state is estimated as $L(s,b) \simeq \alpha b (\sqrt{s}/2) D_C(b)$, where $\alpha$ is related to the fraction of initial energy going into rotation and $D_C(b)$ describes the overlap region geometry.
- The model predicts asymptotic energy dependencies: $\sigma_{tot,el} \sim \ln^2 s$, $\sigma_{inel} \sim \ln s$, and $\bar{n} \sim s^\delta$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier model of elastic scattering based on spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking and the instanton mechanism of QCD (Troshin and Tyurin, prior work) to multiparticle production and directed flow. It draws an analogy with the directed flow observed in non-central nucleus collisions at RHIC, as reported by the J. M. P. E. collaboration (2005), and proposes that the same underlying dynamics of a rotating liquid transient state applies to both hadron and nucleus collisions. The mechanism enables a qualitative explanation of the incident-energy scaling of $v_1$ observed at RHIC and suggests that directed flow measurements in hadronic reactions could serve as a diagnostic for the liquid nature of the transient state.
