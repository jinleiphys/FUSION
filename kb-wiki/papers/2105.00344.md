# Scaling of factorial moments in cumulative variables

> A search for power-law fluctuations within the framework of the intermittency method is ongoing to locate the critical point of the strongly interacting matter. In particular, experimental data on proton and pion production in heavy-ion collisions are analyzed in transverse-momentum, $p_T$, space. In this regard, we have studied the dependence of the second scaled factorial moment $F_2$ of particle multiplicity distribution on the number of subdivisions of transverse momentum-interval used in the analysis. The study is performed using a simple model with a power-law two-particle correlation function in $p_T$. We observe that $F_2$ values depend on the size and position of the $p_T$ interval. However, when we convert the non-uniform transverse-momentum distribution to uniform one using cumulative transformation, $F_2$ calculated in subdivisions of the cumulative $p_T$ becomes independent of the cumulative-$p_T$ interval. The scaling behaviour of $F_2$ for the cumulative variable is observed. Moreover, $F_2$ follows a power law with the number of subdivisions of the cumulative-$p_T$ interval with the intermittency index close to the correlation function's exponent.

## Key claim

Using a simple model with a power-law two-particle correlation function in transverse momentum, the second scaled factorial moment \(F_2\) depends on the size and position of the \(p_T\) interval when calculated in subdivisions of \(p_T\), but becomes independent of the interval when calculated in subdivisions of the cumulative \(p_T\) variable, with the intermittency index extracted from the cumulative variable being close to the correlation function's exponent.

## Method

The authors constructed a one-dimensional model with a two-particle distribution function \(\rho(X_1,X_2) = \rho(X_1)\rho(X_2) / (|X_1-X_2|^{\phi_2} + \epsilon)\), where \(X\) is dimensionless transverse momentum (\(p_T\) in GeV/\(c\)) following a non-uniform distribution \(\rho(X) = C X e^{-6X}\) over the range 0–1. They generated 180,000 events with one correlated pair per event, using \(\phi_2 = 0.8\) and \(\epsilon = 10^{-6}\), and computed the second scaled factorial moment \(F_2(M)\) for various \(X\) intervals and numbers of subdivisions \(M\). They then applied a cumulative transformation \(Q_X = \int_a^X \rho(X) dX / \int_a^b \rho(X) dX\) to convert the non-uniform \(X\) distribution to a uniform distribution and repeated the \(F_2(M)\) analysis in subdivisions of \(Q_X\).

## Key numbers

* The correlation function exponent used in the model was \(\phi_2 = 0.8\), as suggested by a critical point model for protons (Antoniou, 2006).
* At \(M = 1\), \(F_2\) was 0.5 for each \(X\) interval, since each event contained exactly one correlated pair.
* For all tested \(X\) intervals, the deviation of \(F_2(M)\) from the reference interval (0, 1) exceeded 5% for \(M > 1\) when using subdivisions of \(X\).
* The power-law fit of \(F_2(M)\) versus \(M\) (for \(M\) from 2 to 2000, 17 points) for the cumulative variable in the \(X\) interval (0, 1) yielded an intermittency index \(\varphi_2 \approx 0.77\), with \(\chi^2/\text{ndf} \approx 0.6\).

## Context

This work extends the intermittency method proposed by Bialas (1985, 1988) and Satz (1989) for locating the critical point of strongly interacting matter in heavy-ion collisions, specifically addressing the issue that \(F_2\) values depend on the chosen \(p_T\) interval when the \(p_T\) distribution is non-uniform. The cumulative transformation, introduced by Bialas (1990), is shown to remove this dependence, enabling a more robust extraction of the intermittency index. The results enable future studies of detector efficiency effects on \(F_2\), as noted by the authors, and are relevant to ongoing critical point searches at CERN SPS, BNL RHIC, NICA, and FAIR.
