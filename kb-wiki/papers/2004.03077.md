# Model Independent Extraction of the Proton Charge Radius from PRad data

> The proton radius puzzle has motivated several new experiments that aim to extract the proton charge radius and resolve the puzzle. Recently PRad, a new electron-proton scattering experiment at Jefferson Lab, reported a proton charge radius of $0.831\pm 0.007_\textnormal{statistical}\pm 0.012_\textnormal{systematic}$. The value was obtained by using a rational function model for the proton electric form factor. We perform a model-independent extraction using $z$-expansion of the proton charge radius from PRad data. We find that the model-independent statistical error is more than 50\% larger compared to the statistical error reported by PRad.

# Model Independent Extraction of the Proton Charge Radius from PRad data

## Key claim
Using a model-independent z-expansion fit to the published PRad data, the statistical uncertainty on the proton charge radius is more than 50% larger than the statistical error reported by PRad using their default Rational (1,1) fit, even though the central values are nearly identical.

## Method
The author performed a z-expansion fit to the published PRad data for the proton electric form factor G_E^p, using the values of G_E^p reported by PRad with only statistical errors. The fit included a bound on the expansion coefficients (default bound B=5) implemented by adding a penalty term to the chi-squared function, following the approach of Hill and Paz (2010). The fit was performed to the entire PRad data set (both 1.1 GeV and 2.2 GeV beam data) as well as to subsets, and the proton charge radius was extracted from the slope of G_E^p at q^2=0 via the relation between the radius and the first z-expansion coefficient a_1.

## Key numbers
- Proton charge radius from model-independent z-expansion fit to entire PRad data: r_E^p = 0.828^{+0.011}_{-0.012} fm (statistical only, bound B=5)
- PRad default Rational (1,1) fit result: r_E^p = 0.831 ± 0.007 fm (statistical only)
- Second order polynomial in z fit to PRad data: r_E^p = 0.830 ± 0.008 fm (statistical)
- Third order polynomial in z fit to PRad data: r_E^p = 0.825 ± 0.015 fm (statistical)
- Rational (1,1) fit to 1.1 GeV data only: r_E^p = 0.845^{+0.041}_{-0.039} fm (statistical)
- Rational (1,1) fit to 2.2 GeV data only: r_E^p = 0.829^{+0.008}_{-0.008} fm (statistical)
- z-expansion fit to 2.2 GeV data only: r_E^p = 0.826^{+0.012}_{-0.013} fm (B=5, statistical)
- Extracted z-expansion coefficients from full PRad data: a_1 = -0.921 ± 0.026, a_2 = -1.2 ± 0.6, a_3 = 2.2 ± 5.7 (B=5)

## Context
This work extends the model-independent z-expansion methodology developed by Hill and Paz (2010) for baryon form factors, applying it specifically to the PRad data from Xiong et al. (2019). The PRad experiment reached the lowest Q^2 in electron-proton scattering (2.1×10^{-4} GeV^2), an order of magnitude lower than previously achieved at A1 Mainz by Bernauer et al. (2010, 2013). The analysis demonstrates that the Rational (1,1) fit used by PRad underestimates the statistical uncertainty, consistent with the warning in Hill and Paz (2010) that fits with a small number of parameters can underestimate errors. This result enables future model-independent extractions that should also incorporate systematic errors, and it complements recent analyses by Horbatsch (2019) using rational functions and Alarcon et al. (2020) using the DIchiEFT model.
