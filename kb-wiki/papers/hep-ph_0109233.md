# High-p_T pion and kaon production in relativistic nuclear collisions

> High-p_T pion and kaon production is studied in relativistic proton-proton, proton-nucleus, and nucleus-nucleus collisions in a wide energy range. Cross sections are calculated based on perturbative QCD, augmented by a phenomenological transverse momentum distribution of partons (``intrinsic k_T''). An energy dependent width of the transverse momentum distribution is extracted from pion and charged hadron production data in proton-proton/proton-antiproton collisions. Effects of multiscattering and shadowing in the strongly interacting medium are taken into account. Enhancement of the transverse momentum width is introduced and parameterized to explain the Cronin effect. In collisions between heavy nuclei, the model over-predicts central pion production cross sections (more significantly at higher energies), hinting at the presence of jet quenching. Predictions are made for proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC energies.

## Key claim

The authors claim that hard pion and kaon production in relativistic nuclear collisions can be described by leading-order perturbative QCD augmented with an energy-dependent Gaussian intrinsic transverse momentum distribution of partons, but the model over-predicts central pion production in heavy nucleus-nucleus collisions (more significantly at higher energies), which hints at the presence of jet quenching as an additional mechanism not included in the calculation.

## Method

The authors used leading-order perturbative QCD with GRV parton distribution functions and KKP fragmentation functions, incorporating a phenomenological Gaussian intrinsic transverse momentum distribution for partons. They extracted the energy-dependent width of this distribution by fitting pion and charged hadron production data from proton-proton and proton-antiproton collisions, then applied the same framework to proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions by adding an enhanced transverse momentum width parameterized with a coefficient C and an effective number of semi-hard collisions nu_m.

## Key numbers

- The extracted width of the transverse momentum distribution, <k_T^2>, shows an increasing trend from approximately 1.6 GeV^2 at sqrt(s) ~ 20 GeV to 1.7 GeV^2 at sqrt(s) ~ 60 GeV for pion production in pp collisions
- The best fit coefficient C in the width enhancement formula <k_T^2>_pA = <k_T^2>_pp + C * h_pA(b) is C = 0.40 +/- 0.05 GeV^2, obtained with nu_m = 4 effective semi-hard collisions
- The model over-predicts central Pb+Pb pion production cross sections at SPS energies (sqrt(s) ~ 20 AGeV) by up to 40% in the transverse momentum window above 2 GeV

## Context

This work extends the pQCD-improved parton model approach of Owens (1987) and the intrinsic transverse momentum framework of Wang (1998) and Wong (1998) to systematically describe pion and kaon production across pp, pA, and AA collisions. The saturation prescription for the Cronin effect proposed earlier by the authors in a Rapid Communication (2000) is here examined against the alternative of all nucleon-nucleon collisions contributing, with the finding that nu_m=4 (approximately 3 effective semi-hard collisions) provides the most consistent description, overlapping with results from nuclear stopping experiments by Cole (2001) and collaborators at lower energies. The over-prediction in heavy nucleus collisions enables future studies of jet quenching as proposed by Baier (1998) and Gyulassy (2000), and the authors make predictions for proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC energies.
