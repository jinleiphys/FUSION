# Universality of isolated $N$-body resonances at large scattering length

> Non Efimovian $N$-body resonances are investigated in the regime of a large two-body s wave scattering length. In view of a universal description of low-energy bound and quasi-bound states, a contact model is introduced. The modeling requires two parameters in addition to the scattering length. Using a modified scalar product, the contact model provides a normalization of bound states, possibly not square-integrable, that coincides with that of the corresponding finite range model.

## Key claim

Non-Efimovian N-body resonances at large two-body s-wave scattering length can be described universally using a contact model with three parameters (scattering length, effective radius, and detuning), but only when a modified scalar product resolves the "normalization catastrophe" that occurs for bound states with exponent s >= 1, which are not square-integrable in the usual sense.

## Method

The author constructs a contact model where N-body interactions are replaced by two-body and N-body contact conditions, with the wave function satisfying the free Schrodinger equation except at particle contacts. The hyper-radial Schrodinger equation at unitarity reduces to a 2D problem with an inverse-square potential, and the N-body contact condition is imposed via a log-derivative condition at an effective radius R, parameterized by a detuning parameter epsilon. A modified scalar product is introduced, integrating only over hyper-radii greater than R, which provides a self-adjoint extension of the kinetic operator and yields norms that match those of the finite-range reference model.

## Key numbers

- For small positive s (0 < s << 1) and negative detuning, the binding energy is approximately E = -4 E_R [ -epsilon s sin(pi s) Gamma(s)^2 / ((2s - epsilon) pi) ]^(1/s)
- For large s (s >> 1) and small detuning, the binding energy is approximately E = 2(s - 1) epsilon E_R
- At the threshold s = 1 with epsilon -> 0, the quasi-bound state energy is E_r = -2 epsilon E_R / W_{-1}(-x) where x = e^(2 gamma_E) epsilon/2, and Gamma/(2E_r) = -pi/(1 + ln x)
- For large s, the quasi-bound state has Gamma/E_r = 4^(1-s) pi (s-1) / [Gamma(s)]^2 (E_r/E_R)^(s-1)
- The effective radius R satisfies R < R_sep, where R_sep is of order the potential range R_p
- For s = 0.72 and qR = 10^(-2), the ratio of modified norm to usual norm is r = 0.9

## Context

This work extends the universal description of N-body resonances beyond the Efimov regime (where s is imaginary) to the non-Efimovian regime (where s is real), where resonances are isolated and require fine-tuning of finite-range interactions. It builds on prior studies of three-body resonances in Fermi mixtures by Nishida (2008), Safronov (2013), and Kartavtsev (2016), and on the resolution of the normalization catastrophe for high partial wave resonances in two-body systems by Pricoupenko (2006). The results enable the description of tunable isolated N-body resonances in heterogeneous two-component Fermi mixtures using combined optical p-wave and magnetic s-wave Feshbach resonances.
