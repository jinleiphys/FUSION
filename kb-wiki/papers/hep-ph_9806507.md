# Faddeev Null Plane Model of Proton

> The proton is formulated as a relativistic system of three constituent quarks interacting via a zero-range two-body force in the null-plane. The covariance of the null-plane Faddeev-like equation under kinematical front-form boosts is discussed. A simplified three-boson model of the nucleon wave-function is obtained numerically. The proton electric form-factor reproduces the experimental data for low momentum transfers and qualitatively describes the asymptotic region.

# Faddeev Null Plane Model of the Proton

## Key claim

The proton electric form-factor calculated from a three-boson Faddeev wave-function with zero-range two-body force and no explicit confinement reproduces experimental data at low momentum transfers and qualitatively describes the asymptotic region, but the non-confining nature of the wave-function causes the product q^4 G_E(q^2) to remain flat at high q^2 rather than decreasing as in confinement models.

## Method

The authors formulated the proton as a relativistic system of three constituent quarks interacting via a zero-range (contact) two-body force in the null-plane, using a simplified three-boson model to obtain the totally symmetric spatial part of the wave-function numerically from the Faddeev equation. The proton electric form-factor G_E(q^2) was computed in the Breit-frame using the boost transformation properties of the Faddeev vertex under kinematical front-form boosts, with the initial and final wave-functions related by the boost transformation of the vertex.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass was fixed to 938 MeV as a constraint on the input parameters (constituent quark mass M and meson mass μ).
- The ratio μ/M was set to 1.8 and 1.95 for the calculations.
- The product q^4 G_E(q^2) was computed for squared momentum transfers below 6 GeV^2 and compared with experimental data from Hohler et al. (1976).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Faddeev null-plane approach (de Araujo, de Melo, and Frederico, 1995) by testing the totally symmetric spatial part of the proton wave-function with minimal ingredients: relativistic constituent quarks and a contact interaction. The model contrasts with confinement models such as Dziembowski (1988) and Frederico (1992), which yield form-factors that decrease more quickly in the asymptotic region or require a small constituent quark mass to match asymptotic data. The results suggest that explicit confinement is not essential for a good fit of the electric form-factor at low momentum transfers, but the non-confining nature limits the description at high q^2.
