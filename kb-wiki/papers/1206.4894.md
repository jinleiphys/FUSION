# Three-nucleon contact interaction at the next-to-leading order

> A satisfactory description of bound and scattering states of the three-nucleon (3N) system is still lacking, contrary to what happens in the two-nucleon case. In the framework of chiral effective theory, it is possible that a realistic 3N interaction will require the inclusion of subleading contact terms, which are unconstrained by chiral symmetry. We construct the minimal 3N contact Lagrangian imposing all constraints from the discrete symmetries, Fierz identities and Poincaré covariance, and show that it consists of 10 independent operators. By integrating out the pions from the effective theory we derive the pion-induced contributions to the corresponding low-energy constants (LECs), and find no clear signal of convergence of the pionful theory between N2LO and N3LO.

# Three-nucleon contact interaction at the next-to-leading order

## Key claim

The minimal three-nucleon contact Lagrangian with two spatial derivatives consists of 10 independent operators after imposing discrete symmetries, Fierz identities, and Poincaré covariance; however, when the pion-induced contributions to the corresponding low-energy constants are derived by integrating out pions, there is no clear signal of convergence of the pionful theory between N2LO and N3LO, suggesting that subleading contact terms (which would only appear at N4LO in the chiral expansion) may be phenomenologically relevant at very low energies.

## Method

The authors constructed the most general three-nucleon contact Lagrangian with two covariant derivatives by starting from an initial list of 146 operators, then imposing constraints from parity, time-reversal invariance, Fierz identities (arising from antisymmetry of the nuclear wave function), and Poincaré covariance order by order in the low-energy expansion. They reduced the list to 10 independent operators and expressed the resulting three-nucleon contact potential in both momentum space and coordinate space. They then matched the pionful theory to the contact theory by Taylor-expanding the N2LO and N3LO chiral three-nucleon potentials in the low-energy limit, deriving the pion-induced contributions to the 10 low-energy constants as functions of the lowest-order LECs.

## Key numbers

- The initial list of 146 isospin-invariant three-nucleon contact operators with two spatial derivatives is reduced to 14 after Fierz constraints, then further reduced to **10 independent operators** after imposing Poincaré covariance.
- The numerical values of the low-energy constants $E_1$ through $E_{10}$ (in units of $F_\pi^4 M_\pi^3$) from pion-induced contributions are approximately: $E_1 \sim 0.10$, $E_2 \sim 0.06$, $E_3 \sim 0.00$, $E_4 \sim 0.00$, $E_5 \sim 0.02$, $E_6 \sim 0.04$, $E_7 \sim -0.08$, $E_8 \sim -0.02$, $E_9 \sim 0.14$, $E_{10} \sim 0.09$.
- Only $E_{10}$ receives contributions from both N2LO ($\sim -0.05$) and N3LO ($\sim 0.15$), showing no sign of convergence between these orders.
- For the leading isospin-breaking contact operators (no derivatives), the initial seven operators are reduced to **one independent operator** after applying Fierz identities from all three nucleon pair exchanges.

## Context

This work extends the classification of three-nucleon contact interactions by Girlanda (2011) to subleading order (two derivatives), and builds upon the chiral effective field theory framework for nuclear forces developed by Bernard, Epelbaum, Krebs, and others (2002-2012). Currently available three-nucleon potentials fail to simultaneously describe the neutron-deuteron doublet scattering length and low-energy observables such as the $A_y$ puzzle (Kievsky 2010). The 10 independent operators identified here, particularly the spin-orbit terms proportional to $E_7$ and $E_8$, have the properties suggested by Kievsky (1999) to potentially resolve the $A_y$ puzzle. The results enable future fitting of these low-energy constants to three-nucleon scattering data, combined with a realistic two-nucleon interaction, and subsequent testing in four-body scattering states using the single four-nucleon contact term (Girlanda 2011).
