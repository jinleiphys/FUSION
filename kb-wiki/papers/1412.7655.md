# From asymmetric nuclear matter to neutron stars: a functional renormalization group study

> A previous study of nuclear matter in a chiral nucleon-meson model is extended to isospin-asymmetric matter. Fluctuations beyond mean-field approximation are treated in the framework of the functional renormalization group. The nuclear liquid-gas phase transition is investigated in detail as a function of the proton fraction in asymmetric matter. The equations of state at zero temperature of both symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter are found to be in good agreement with realistic many-body computations. We also study the density dependence of the pion mass in the medium. The question of chiral symmetry restoration in neutron matter is addressed; we find a stabilization of the phase with spontaneously broken chiral symmetry once fluctuations are included. Finally, neutron star matter including beta equilibrium is discussed. The model satisfies the constraints imposed by the existence of two-solar-mass neutron stars.

# From asymmetric nuclear matter to neutron stars: a functional renormalization group study

## Key claim
The chiral nucleon-meson model with functional renormalization group fluctuations satisfies the constraint of supporting two-solar-mass neutron stars, but only when the vector-isoscalar and vector-isovector couplings are readjusted from their mean-field values to account for fluctuation effects, and when the model is restricted to conventional nucleon and meson degrees of freedom without exotic compositions such as hyperons or quark matter.

## Method
The authors extended a chiral SU(2)L × SU(2)R nucleon-meson model to isospin-asymmetric matter, treating fluctuations of pions and sigma fields non-perturbatively via the functional renormalization group (FRG) with the optimized Litim cutoff, while omega and rho meson fields were kept as non-fluctuating mean fields. The flow equations for the effective potential were solved self-consistently using a grid method, with parameters fitted to reproduce nuclear saturation properties (binding energy, compression modulus, surface tension) and the symmetry energy at saturation density. The grand canonical potential was then evaluated at the absolute minimum to compute thermodynamic quantities and the equation of state for varying proton fractions, including beta-equilibrated neutron star matter.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear saturation density: n0 = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Binding energy at saturation: E/A = -16 MeV
- Compression modulus from mean-field fit: K = 293 MeV (empirical: K = 240 ± 30 MeV)
- Surface tension from mean-field fit: Σ = 1.1 MeV/fm² (empirical: Σ = 1.1 MeV/fm²)
- Symmetry energy at saturation: E_sym = 32 MeV (fitted value, within empirical range 29–33 MeV)
- Vector-isoscalar coupling (with fluctuations): G_ω = 4.04 fm²
- Vector-isovector coupling (with fluctuations): G_ρ = 1.12 fm²
- Sigma mass (with fluctuations): m_σ = 770 MeV
- Ultraviolet cutoff: Λ = 1.4 GeV
- Critical proton fraction below which nuclear matter is no longer self-bound: x = 0.045
- Proton fraction at which energy per particle vanishes at the minimum: x ≃ 0.11
- Landau mass at mean-field level: m_L ≃ 0.8 M_N
- Fermi momentum at saturation: p_F = 265 MeV
- Critical chemical potential for liquid-gas transition: μ_c = 923 MeV (M_N - B with B = 16 MeV)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous FRG studies of symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter (Drews 2013, 2014) to asymmetric nuclear matter with varying proton fractions. The results agree well with realistic many-body computations such as the Akmal-Pandharipande-Ravenhall equation of state (1998) and Quantum Monte Carlo calculations (Armani 2011). The model enables systematic investigation of the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition as a function of asymmetry, the density dependence of the in-medium pion mass, and the stability of chiral symmetry breaking in neutron matter, while satisfying the observational constraint from two-solar-mass neutron stars (Demorest 2010, Antoniadis 2013).
