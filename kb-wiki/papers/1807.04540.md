# The nuclear configurational entropy approach to dynamical QCD effects

> This paper scrutinizes the dynamical QCD effects influence on mesons, namely, the mean-square root radius of a pion in the holographic light-front wave function setup, in the context of the AdS/QCD. The nuclear configurational entropy, associated to mesonic holographic light-front wave functions, is shown to have a critical point that optimizes the two parameters of the spin-improved light-front wave function. The mean-square root pion radius and its cross-section, computed upon these derived values, match the exact existing experimental data to the precision of 0.14\%, below the experimental error at the PDG.

## Key claim

The nuclear configurational entropy (CE) of the spin-improved holographic light-front wave function for the pion exhibits a critical point that determines the optimal values of the two free parameters A and B, yielding a predicted pion root mean-square radius that matches experimental data to 0.14% precision, which is below the experimental error quoted by the Particle Data Group (PDG).

## Method

The author constructs the nuclear configurational entropy from the spatial Fourier transform of the pion cross section, derived from the spin-improved holographic light-front wave function in the AdS/QCD framework. The two free parameters A and B in the helicity-dependent wave function are varied, and the critical point of the nuclear CE is identified as the minimum of the entropy function. Using the critical-point values A=0.989 and B=1.010, the pion root mean-square radius is computed via the standard light-front wave function expression involving the impact parameter b.

## Key numbers

* Critical point of the nuclear configurational entropy occurs at A = 0.989 and B = 1.010, deviating at most 1.1% from the assumption A = 1 = B.
* Predicted pion root mean-square radius: sqrt(<r_pi^2>) = 0.673 fm.
* Experimental PDG value: sqrt(<r_pi^2>) = 0.672 +/- 0.008 fm.
* Agreement between predicted and experimental radius is within 0.14% precision.
* AdS/QCD energy scale used: kappa = 523 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the application of configurational entropy, previously used to study meson production (Bernardini 2016, Barbosa-Cendejas 2018), glueball dominance (Bernardini 2016), and quarkonium states (Braga 2017), to the inner structure of the pion. The results corroborate the spin-improved holographic wave function approach of Ahmady (2014), which used A=1=B based on fitting to HERA data, and demonstrate that the nuclear CE can independently determine the optimal parameters without fitting. This enables a first-principles method for extracting hadronic observables from the information-theoretic content of the wave function.
