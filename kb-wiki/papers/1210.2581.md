# Wilson lines and orbital angular momentum

> We present an explicit realization of the Chen et al. approach to the proton spin decomposition in terms of Wilson lines, generalizing the light-front gauge-invariant extensions discussed recently by Hatta. Particular attention is drawn to the residual gauge freedom by further separating the pure-gauge term into contour and residual terms. We show that the kinetic orbital angular momentum operator can be expressed in terms of the Wigner operator only when the momentum variable is integrated over. Finally, we confirm from twist-2 arguments that the advanced, retarded and antisymmetric light-front canonical orbital angular momenta are the same.

## Key claim

The paper provides an explicit realization of the Chen et al. approach to proton spin decomposition using Wilson lines, showing that the decomposition is not unique due to Stueckelberg symmetry, the freedom in defining what is meant by "pure-gauge" and "physical" contributions depends on the choice of contour and reference point.

## Method

The author constructs the pure-gauge and physical parts of the gauge field by parallel transport from a reference point using Wilson lines along standard contours, then separates the pure-gauge term further into contour and residual terms. The Wigner operator is analyzed to relate it to canonical and kinetic orbital angular momentum operators, and light-front gauge-invariant extensions are examined using twist-2 arguments.

## Key numbers

- The kinetic orbital angular momentum operator can be expressed in terms of the Wigner operator only when the momentum variable is integrated over, as shown by the relation \( \int d^4k\,k^\rho\,\widehat W^{[\gamma^\mu]q}(x,k) = \frac{i}{2}\,\bar\psi(x) \gamma^\mu \stackrel{\leftrightarrow}{D}^\rho_\text{pure}(x)\psi(x) \)
- The advanced, retarded and antisymmetric light-front canonical orbital angular momenta are confirmed to be the same from twist-2 arguments, consistent with previous twist-3 level approaches
- The derivative of the direct straight Wilson line with respect to \(z\) gives \( \frac{\partial}{\partial z^\mu}\mathcal W_{\mathcal C}(x+z,x)=ig\,A_\mu^\text{pure,$x$-FS}(x+z)\,\mathcal W_{\mathcal C}(x+z,x) \), where \(A_\mu^\text{pure,$x$-FS}\) is the pure-gauge field in the \(x\)-based Fock-Schwinger gauge-invariant extension

## Context

This work extends the Chen et al. approach (Chen 2008, Chen 2011) to proton spin decomposition by providing an explicit Wilson-line construction, generalizing the light-front gauge-invariant extensions discussed by Hatta (2011). It clarifies the residual gauge freedom and the relationship between Wigner operators and orbital angular momentum, building on prior work by Ji (1996) on kinetic orbital angular momentum and by Jaffe and Manohar (1989) on canonical orbital angular momentum. The results enable a clearer understanding of how different definitions of canonical orbital angular momentum are related and how they can be accessed experimentally through Wigner distributions.
