# Energy loss baseline for light hadrons in oxygen-oxygen collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=5.36\,\text{TeV}$

> I present predictions for inclusive charged hadron spectra in minimum-bias proton-proton and oxygen-oxygen collisions at a centre-of-mass energy of $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 5.36\,\text{TeV}$, assuming no final-state interactions. Using next-to-leading order perturbative QCD matrix elements, along with state-of-the-art (nuclear) parton distribution and fragmentation functions, I establish a baseline for the nuclear modification factor $R^h_\text{AA}$ in oxygen-oxygen collisions in the absence of quenching. Theoretical uncertainties in this baseline are found to be substantial for transverse momenta below $20\,\text{GeV}$. In the intermediate range $20\,\text{GeV} \lesssim p_T^h \lesssim 70\,\text{GeV}$, these uncertainties are significantly reduced to approximately 5\%. At higher momenta ($p_T^h \gtrsim 70\,\text{GeV}$), however, predictions exhibit a marked spread due to differences between fragmentation functions, reflecting varying assumptions about isospin symmetry. Finally, I show that considering neon-neon collisions in the initial state or neutral pions in the final state does not appreciably change the nuclear modification factor.

# Energy loss baseline for light hadrons in oxygen-oxygen collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=5.36\,\text{TeV}$

## Key claim

The paper establishes a perturbative QCD baseline for the nuclear modification factor $R^h_\mathrm{AA}$ of inclusive charged hadrons in minimum-bias oxygen-oxygen collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 5.36\,\text{TeV}$ assuming no final-state interactions (no jet quenching), with the caveat that theoretical uncertainties in this baseline are substantial for transverse momenta below $20\,\text{GeV}$ and above $70\,\text{GeV}$, limiting the ability to unambiguously detect medium-induced energy loss outside the intermediate momentum window.

## Method

The author computed single-inclusive hadron cross sections using next-to-leading order (NLO) perturbative QCD matrix elements via the INCNLO program, convoluted with state-of-the-art nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDFs: EPPS21, nNNPDF3.0, TUJU21) and fragmentation functions (FFs: BKK, NNFF1.1h, NPC23). Theoretical uncertainties were estimated through 15-point scale variations, 68% confidence intervals from nPDF and FF error sets, and comparisons between different nPDF and FF choices, with the ratio of oxygen-oxygen to proton-proton cross sections scaled by $A^2$ defining the nuclear modification factor $R_\mathrm{AA}$.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear modification factor baseline $R^h_\mathrm{AA}$ can be controlled at approximately 5% uncertainty in the intermediate momentum range $20\,\text{GeV} \lesssim p_T^h \lesssim 70\,\text{GeV}$.
- At transverse momenta below $20\,\text{GeV}$, theoretical uncertainties become substantial, with EPPS21 nPDFs giving $R^h_\mathrm{AA} < 1$ for $p_T \lesssim 10\,\text{GeV}$ while TUJU21 and nNNPDF3.0 remain near unity.
- For $p_T \gtrsim 70\,\text{GeV}$, NNFF1.1h fragmentation functions produce $R^h_\mathrm{AA}$ values well below unity due to isospin symmetry breaking, while NPC23 and BKK FFs remain near unity.
- The ratio of differential hadron cross sections between neon-neon and oxygen-oxygen collisions agrees with unity to within 1% over most of the momentum range.
- NLO scale uncertainties in the absolute cross section reduce to approximately 15% at high momenta for proton-proton collisions.

## Context

This work updates the previous baseline estimate of Huss (2020) with the actual LHC centre-of-mass energy for oxygen-oxygen collisions and the latest nPDFs and FFs, extending prior baseline computations for RHIC energies by Belmont (2023) and for inclusive jet observables by Gebhard (2024). The predictions provide a necessary reference for upcoming experimental measurements from the LHC oxygen-oxygen run conducted in July 2025, enabling searches for jet quenching in small collision systems with approximately ten participating nucleons, where dedicated model predictions have been developed by Huss (2020), Liu (2021), Zakharov (2021), Ke (2022), Behera (2023), Xie (2022), van der Schee (2025), and Pablos (2025).
