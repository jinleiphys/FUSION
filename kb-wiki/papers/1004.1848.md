# Empirically Charting Dynamical Chiral Symmetry Breaking

> We provide a snapshot of recent progress in hadron physics made using QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations, reviewing the generation of a quark anomalous chromomagnetic moment, which may explain the longstanding puzzle of the $a_1$-$ρ$ mass splitting, and the form of the pion and kaon valence-quark parton distribution functions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a quark anomalous chromomagnetic moment, generated by dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB) and incorporated into the dressed quark-gluon vertex, can resolve the long-standing puzzle of the \(a_1\)-\(\rho\) mass splitting, which has been underestimated by a factor of about four in all previous symmetry-preserving calculations.

## Method

The authors use QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) with a symmetry-preserving truncation scheme that extends the rainbow-ladder approximation. They solve the coupled gap and Bethe-Salpeter equations for vector and axial-vector mesons, employing the Ball-Chiu vertex ansatz augmented by a term representing the quark anomalous chromomagnetic moment, with its strength set by the DCSB-driven \(\Delta_B\) function. The resulting masses for the \(\rho\) and \(a_1\) mesons are computed and compared with experimental values and with results from the rainbow-ladder truncation alone.

## Key numbers

*   \(a_1\) meson mass: 1230 MeV (experiment); 759 MeV (rainbow-ladder); 1066 MeV (Ball-Chiu consistent); 1230 MeV (Ball-Chiu plus anomalous chromomagnetic moment).
*   \(\rho\) meson mass: 775 MeV (experiment); 644 MeV (rainbow-ladder); 924 MeV (Ball-Chiu consistent); 745 MeV (Ball-Chiu plus anomalous chromomagnetic moment).
*   \(a_1\)-\(\rho\) mass splitting: 455 MeV (experiment); 115 MeV (rainbow-ladder); 142 MeV (Ball-Chiu consistent); 485 MeV (Ball-Chiu plus anomalous chromomagnetic moment).
*   For the valence-quark parton distribution functions at large Bjorken-\(x\), the evolved distributions at \(Q^2=25\) GeV\(^2\) behave as \((1-x)^a\) with \(a=2.7\).
*   The ratio \(u_{\rm v}^K/u_{\rm v}^\pi\) at \(x=1\) is 0.13, invariant under QCD evolution.

## Context

This work extends the DSE-based approach to hadron physics by addressing a known failure of the rainbow-ladder truncation, which produces an \(a_1\)-\(\rho\) mass splitting only about one-quarter of the experimental value, as seen in studies by Watson (2004), Maris (2006), and Fischer (2009). The new method, introduced by Chang (2009), enables the inclusion of nonperturbative DCSB effects in the Bethe-Salpeter kernel that are impossible to capture in any finite sum of perturbative contributions. The results enable future unified calculations of the hadron spectrum, elastic and transition form factors, and parton distribution functions within a single framework.
