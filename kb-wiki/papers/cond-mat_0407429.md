# Exactly-Solvable Models Derived from a Generalized Gaudin Algebra

> We introduce a generalized Gaudin Lie algebra and a complete set of mutually commuting quantum invariants allowing the derivation of several families of exactly solvable Hamiltonians. Different Hamiltonians correspond to different representations of the generators of the algebra. The derived exactly-solvable generalized Gaudin models include the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer, Suhl-Matthias-Walker, the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick, generalized Dicke, the Nuclear Interacting Boson Model, a new exactly-solvable Kondo-like impurity model, and many more that have not been exploited in the physics literature yet.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a generalized Gaudin Lie algebra (GGA) whose quantum invariants can be exactly diagonalized, unifying the exact solvability of many well-known many-body models (including BCS, Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick, and the Nuclear Interacting Boson Model) under a single algebraic framework; the authors claim that different Hamiltonians correspond to different representations of the same algebra, and that new exactly-solvable models can be constructed by choosing appropriate representations.

## Method

The authors define a generalized Gaudin algebra with generators satisfying commutation relations involving antisymmetric complex functions X, Y, Z of two variables, and derive the Gaudin equations from Jacobi identities. They then focus on the XXZ case (X=Y), parametrize the solutions into rational, trigonometric, and hyperbolic families, and diagonalize the Gaudin field operators using a Bethe ansatz. Different physical models are obtained by realizing the algebra generators in terms of su(2) or su(1,1) generators, which are then represented using canonical fermions or bosons.

## Key numbers

* The rational parametrization uses X(eta_l, eta_n) = Z(eta_l, eta_n) = g / (eta_l - eta_n) (Eq. Rat).
* The trigonometric parametrization uses X(eta_l, eta_n) = g / sin(eta_l - eta_n) and Z(eta_l, eta_n) = g cot(eta_l - eta_n) (Eq. Trig).
* The hyperbolic parametrization uses X(eta_l, eta_n) = g / sinh(eta_l - eta_n) and Z(eta_l, eta_n) = g coth(eta_l - eta_n) (Eq. Hyp).
* The constant Gamma = X_{mn}^2 - Z_{mn}^2 takes values Gamma = 0 (rational), Gamma > 0 (trigonometric), or Gamma < 0 (hyperbolic), with Gamma = s g^2 where s = 0, +1, or -1.
* The limiting behavior of the structure functions is given by f(x) = g(x) = h(x) = g(1 + s x^2), which reduces to f(x) = g for the three standard parametrizations.

## Context

This work extends the original Gaudin algebra (Gaudin, 1976) and unifies the exact solution of the BCS pairing model (Richardson, 1960s), the Suhl-Matthias-Walker model, the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model, the generalized Dicke model, and the Nuclear Interacting Boson Model (IBM) within a single algebraic framework. The paper enables the construction of new exactly-solvable models, including SU(N) spin models, spin-fermion, spin-boson, and fermion-boson Hamiltonians, and establishes that the LMG model, used for decades to study phase transitions in finite nuclei, is exactly solvable.
