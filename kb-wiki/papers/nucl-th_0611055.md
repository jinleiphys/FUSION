# Overview of Non-Relativistic QCD

> Plenary talk at the 4th International Conference on Quarks and Nuclear Physics (QNP06), 5-10 June 2006, Madrid, Spain; 6 pages, 1 figure, EPJ style. References added. Journal version

## Key claim

Non-Relativistic QCD (NRQCD) provides a systematic effective field theory framework for heavy quarkonium systems, but the formalism for production is "more controversial" than for spectroscopy and decay, and the standard NRQCD velocity counting "has no guarantee that it holds for all the states."

## Method

The paper reviews the construction of NRQCD by integrating out the heavy quark mass scale \(m_Q\) from QCD, leaving two-component Pauli spinor fields for quarks and antiquarks, and then discusses a further effective theory called Potential NRQCD (pNRQCD) which integrates out the scale \(m_Q v\) (typical momentum) to leave only the binding energy scale \(m_Q v^2\). The matching between NRQCD and pNRQCD can be carried out either in perturbation theory (weak coupling regime, when \(\Lambda_{QCD} \lesssim m_Q v^2\)) or non-perturbatively (strong coupling regime, when \(\Lambda_{QCD} \gg m_Q v^2\)). For certain kinematic endpoints in semi-inclusive decays and production, NRQCD is supplemented with collinear degrees of freedom from Soft-Collinear Effective Theory (SCET).

## Key numbers

* The static potential terms in the pNRQCD hamiltonian are known at two loops (Peter 1997, Schroder 1998, Kniehl 2004), with logarithmic contributions at three loops and (for the singlet) at four loops also known (Brambilla 1999, 2006).
* The \(1/m_Q\) and \(1/m_Q^2\) terms in the singlet hamiltonian are known at two and one loop respectively (Kniehl 2002).
* An almost complete NNNLO expression for the spectrum is available (Penin 2002), assuming \(\Lambda_{QCD} \ll m_Q \alpha_s^2\).
* The \(\eta_b\) mass and its electromagnetic decay width have been predicted at NNLL and NLL respectively (Kniehl 2003, Penin 2004).
* For bottomonium, current data on radiative decay photon spectra disfavor the \(n=1\) state being in the strong coupling regime and are compatible with \(n=2,3\) in it (Garcia i Tormo 2005).
* The cross-section for electromagnetic threshold production is known at NNLO (Hoang 2000) with log resummation at NLL (Pineda 2001, Hoang 2002); partial NNNLO and NNLL results exist.
* The NRQCD factorization formula for inclusive production has been proved correct at NNLO in \(\alpha_s(m_Q)\) (Nayak 2005, 2006).

## Context

This plenary talk reviews the status of NRQCD as of 2006, extending the earlier review by Brambilla (2004) and building on the foundational work of Caswell and Lepage (1985) and Bodwin, Braaten, and Lepage (1994). It contrasts the well-understood NRQCD formalism for spectroscopy and decay with the more controversial production formalism, and describes how pNRQCD (Pineda 1997, Brambilla 1999) and vNRQCD (Luke 1999) aim to resolve the velocity counting ambiguities in NRQCD. The combination of NRQCD with SCET (Bauer 2000) has enabled progress on endpoint regions of semi-inclusive decays and production, and the strong coupling regime of pNRQCD allows new model-independent predictions such as ratios of hadronic decay widths of P-wave states from charmonium data (Brambilla 2001).
