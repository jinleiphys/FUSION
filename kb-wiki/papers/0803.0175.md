# Is gluonic color-spin locked phase stable?

> We study the gluonic color-spin locked (GCSL) phase in dense two-flavor quark matter. In this phase, the color and spatial rotational symmetries are spontaneously broken down to SO(2)_{diag} with the generator being an appropriate linear combination of the color and rotational ones. The Meissner masses of gluons and the mass of the radial mode of the diquark field in the GCSL phase are calculated and it is shown that this phase is free from the chromomagnetic and Sarma instabilities in the whole parameter region where it exists. The GCSL phase describes an anisotropic color and electromagnetic superconducting medium. Because most of the initial symmetries in this phase are spontaneously broken, its dynamics is very rich.

## Key claim

The gluonic color-spin locked (GCSL) phase resolves both the chromomagnetic and Sarma instabilities in the whole parameter region where it exists, unlike the minimal cylindrical gluonic phase II which only partially resolves the chromomagnetic instability. The claim is conditional on the phase existing within a specific window of the 2SC gap parameter \(\Delta_0\) (76 MeV to 161 MeV for \(\alpha_s=1\)) and for realistic values of the strong coupling constant (\(0.75 < \alpha_s < 1.25\)) and chemical potential (300 MeV to 500 MeV).

## Method

The author uses a gauged Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with two light quarks, neglecting current quark masses and the \((\bar{\psi}\psi)^2\)-interaction channel. The Meissner screening masses for gluons and the mass of the diquark Higgs field (radial mode) at zero momentum are calculated using a renormalized effective potential with a subtraction scheme that removes artificial gluon mass terms. The analysis is performed in the fermion one-loop approximation, with the effective potential minimized under neutrality conditions to find dynamical solutions for the gluon condensates \(\langle A_x^4 \rangle\) and \(\langle A_z^6 \rangle\).

## Key numbers

- The GCSL phase exists for the 2SC gap parameter \(\Delta_0\) in the range 76 MeV \(< \Delta_0 <\) 161 MeV, for \(\alpha_s=1\), \(\mu=400\) MeV, and \(\Lambda=653.3\) MeV.
- When varying \(\mu\) from 300 MeV to 400 MeV and from 400 MeV to 500 MeV, the window where the GCSL phase is energetically stabler than the minimal cylindrical gluonic phase II widens by about 15% in each step.
- When the cutoff \(\Lambda\) is increased from 653.3 MeV to 1 GeV, the window widens by about 30%.
- The diquark Higgs mass and all eigenvalues of the Meissner masses are positive in the whole region where the GCSL phase exists, with masses given in units of \(M_{\Delta_0}^2 \equiv 4\mu^2/\pi^2\) and \(M_g^2 \equiv 4\alpha_s \mu^2/(3\pi)\).
- Six vanishing Meissner masses are found within numerical precision, three of which correspond to gauge fixing fields and three possibly to Nambu-Goldstone bosons.

## Context

This work extends the study of gluonic phases in dense two-flavor quark matter, following the discovery of chromomagnetic instabilities in the gapped 2SC and gapless g2SC phases by Huang (2004) and the proposal of gluon condensates (gluonic phase) by Gorbar (2005, 2007). The GCSL phase is compared to the minimal cylindrical gluonic phase II studied by Hashimoto (2007), which only partially resolved the chromomagnetic instability. The paper shows that the GCSL phase is energetically more favorable than the normal, 2SC/g2SC, and single plane wave LOFF phases in a wide parameter region, and it enables future work on whether the GCSL phase is the global vacuum in dense two-flavor quark matter and on the nature of its Nambu-Goldstone bosons.
