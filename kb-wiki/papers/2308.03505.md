# Sensitivity study of mirror energy differences in positive parity bands of T=3/2 A=45 nuclei

> Symmetry conserving density functional theory (DFT) based no-core-configuration-interaction framework (DFT-NCCI) is an excellent tool for precision calculation of diverse (pseudo-)observables related to isospin symmetry breaking from elusive isospin impurities trough isospin corrections to superallowed beta decays to mirror- and triplet-displacement energies and mirror energy differences (MED) along rotational bands. In our recent work [Phys. Rev. C {\bf 106}, 024327 (2022)] we performed axial DFT-NCCI calculations and failed to reproduce a sign of MED in positive-parity bands of 45Sc/45Cr T=3/2 mirror pair what casts a shadow on credibility of the model. In this work we aim to perform a thorough analysis of this case with the focus on sensitivity of our predictions with respect to: (i) low-energy constants (LECs) of our effective contact charge symmetry breaking (CSB) force and (ii) nuclear shape. We demonstrate, among the other, that inclusion of triaxial positive-parity ground-state - which is actually the global positive-parity minimum in our unconstrained mean-field calculation - in the DFT-NCCI calculations instead of the axial one used before leads to MED which are consistent with experimental data concerning both their sign as well as magnitude without any need for fine-tuning of the model's LECs.

## Key claim

Including triaxial positive-parity ground-state configurations in DFT-NCCI calculations, rather than the axial ones used previously, yields mirror energy differences (MED) for the 45Sc/45Cr T=3/2 mirror pair that are consistent with experimental data in both sign and magnitude, without requiring fine-tuning of the model's low-energy constants (LECs).

## Method

The authors performed symmetry-conserving density functional theory based no-core-configuration-interaction (DFT-NCCI) calculations using the SVISB T; NLO Skyrme pseudo-potential with class III charge symmetry breaking (CSB) terms up to next-to-leading order. They constructed a configuration space of 26 low-lying multiparticle-multihole self-consistent Hartree-Fock solutions for the positive-parity bands of 45Sc and 45Cr, then applied angular-momentum projection and K-mixing followed by Hill-Wheeler configuration mixing. Unlike prior work, the authors allowed the mean-field solutions to be triaxial rather than imposing axial symmetry, and they tested six variants of the NLO CSB force with different LEC values to study sensitivity of the MED predictions.

## Key numbers

- The experimental mirror displacement energy (MDE) for the J=3/2+ band-heads is 24 MeV; the original NLO CSB force with LECs t0III = 11±2 MeV fm3, t1III = -14±4 MeV fm5, and t2III = -7.8±0.8 MeV fm5 reproduces this value.
- Variant A (t0III = -6.0 MeV fm3, t1III = 14.0 MeV fm5, t2III = -7.8 MeV fm5) gives MDE = 24.126 MeV; Variant B (t0III = 1.0 MeV fm3, t1III = 2.5 MeV fm5, t2III = -7.8 MeV fm5) gives MDE = 24.104 MeV.
- Variant C (t0III = 11.0 MeV fm3, t1III = -14.0 MeV fm5, t2III = 0.0 MeV fm5) gives MDE = 21.705 MeV; Variant D (t0III = 11.0 MeV fm3, t1III = -14.0 MeV fm5, t2III = 7.8 MeV fm5) gives MDE = 19.333 MeV.
- The cut-off parameter for K-mixing was set to zeta = 0.01, and the integration over Euler angles used n_alpha = n_beta = n_gamma = 40 quadrature knots.

## Context

This work extends the DFT-NCCI method previously applied to T=1/2 mirrors in the lower fp-shell by Baczyk et al. (2021) and to T=3/2 47Ti/47Mn mirrors by Uthayakumaar et al. (2022). It directly addresses a failure reported in that prior work (Uthayakumaar, 2022) where axial DFT-NCCI calculations could not reproduce the sign of MED for positive-parity bands in 45Sc/45Cr. The results demonstrate that triaxial degrees of freedom are essential for correct MED predictions in these nuclei, and they enable future applications of the DFT-NCCI framework to other mirror pairs where axial approximations may be insufficient.
