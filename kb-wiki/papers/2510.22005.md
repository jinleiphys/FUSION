# Interactions of Neutrino Wave Packets

> The low energy effective field theory of interacting neutrinos derived from the Standard Model may be framed as a pointlike interaction and thereby modeled on a lattice of neutrino momenta. We identify a path to take a continuum limit of this lattice problem in the center of momentum frame. In this limit, the weak interaction is found to become trivial between incoming plane waves describing ultrarelativistic particles, unless finite neutrino wave packet sizes are taken into consideration. We follow up with an analytic treatment of interacting neutrino wave packets, demonstrating the importance of the wave packet size for characterizing neutrino-neutrino scattering in dense environments.

## Key claim

The weak interaction between ultrarelativistic neutrinos described by plane waves becomes trivial in the continuum limit of the lattice problem in the center of momentum frame, unless finite neutrino wave packet sizes are taken into consideration; the physical importance of neutrino-neutrino interactions is essentially dependent upon both nonforward scattering and wave packet size effects.

## Method

The author discretizes the allowed momentum states of interacting neutrinos on a lattice in the center of momentum (CoM) frame, restricting to elastic scattering of a pair of neutrinos with initial momenta p0 and -p0. The Hamiltonian in this frame becomes isotropic and proportional to a projection operator onto a uniform superposition of antipodal momentum pairs. The author then takes a continuum limit by increasing the number of lattice points M to infinity while holding the momentum-space volume fixed, and analytically treats the evolution of interacting neutrino wave packets of generic size using the formalism of Kiers, Nussinov, and Weiss (1996).

## Key numbers

- The Fermi coupling constant is given as G_F ~ 10^{-11} MeV^{-2}
- Neutrino masses are taken as m_nu ~ 10^{-2} eV and energies as E ~ 1-100 MeV for environments producing copious neutrinos
- The interaction timescale for neutrino pairs is estimated as ~ (G_F E^3)^{-1}, which is much shorter than the length scale of vacuum oscillations
- For M allowed outgoing momentum states on the lattice, the first trough in occupancy oscillations occurs at dimensionless time G' t_min = pi/M, with survival probability 1 - 4/M + 4/M^2
- In numerical simulations, a dimensionless time step of G' delta = 0.002 was used

## Context

This work extends the lattice treatment of nonforward neutrino-neutrino scattering introduced by Cirigliano, Sen, and Yamauchi (2024), which considered general momentum transfers between neutrino pairs beyond the forward-scattering approximation. The analysis also builds on quantum many-body calculations of collective neutrino oscillations (Pantaleone, 1992; Friedland and Lunardini, 2003; Cervia et al., 2019-2022) and recent reassessments of neutrino correlations with short wave packet sizes (Shalgar and Tamborra, 2023; Goimil-Garcia, 2024; Laraib and Sherwood, 2025). The results enable a more complete understanding of when neutrino-neutrino interactions are physically important in dense environments such as supernovae, showing that both the continuum limit and finite wave packet size must be considered together.
