# The ^{144}Sm-αoptical potential at astrophysically relevant energies derived from ^{144}Sm(α,α)^{144}Sm elastic scattering

> For the determination of the $^{144}Sm-α$ optical potential we measured the angular distribution of $^{144}Sm(α,α)^{144}Sm$ scattering at the energy $E_{lab} = 20 MeV$ with high accuracy. Using the known systematics of $α$-nucleus optical potentials we are able to derive the $^{144}Sm-α$ optical potential at the astrophysically relevant energy $E_{c.m.} = 9.5 MeV$ with very limited uncertainties.

## Key claim

By measuring the elastic scattering angular distribution at 20 MeV and applying known systematics of alpha-nucleus optical potentials, the authors derive the 144Sm-alpha optical potential at the astrophysically relevant energy of 9.5 MeV with "very limited uncertainties," though the extrapolation relies on the threshold anomaly and a parametrization of the imaginary part's energy dependence that is not strongly constrained by only two data points.

## Method

The authors measured the angular distribution of 144Sm(alpha,alpha)144Sm elastic scattering at Elab = 20 MeV using four silicon surface-barrier detectors in a scattering chamber at ATOMKI, Debrecen, covering angles from 15 to 172 degrees. The real part of the optical potential was calculated via a double-folding procedure using the DDM3Y effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, while the imaginary part was parametrized with Woods-Saxon or Fourier-Bessel forms and fitted to the data. Using the systematics of alpha-nucleus potentials from Atzrott (1996) and the Brown-Rho parametrization for the energy dependence of the imaginary volume integral, the potential was extrapolated from 20 MeV down to the astrophysically relevant energy Ec.m. = 9.5 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The elastic scattering angular distribution was measured at Elab = 20 MeV with experimental accuracy better than 2% over more than 4 orders of magnitude in cross section.
- The volume integral of the real part at Elab = 20 MeV (family 4) is JR = 349 MeV fm3; the volume integral of the imaginary part at the same energy is JI = 52.5 MeV fm3.
- At the astrophysically relevant energy Ec.m. = 9.5 MeV, the extrapolated real volume integral is JR = 334 +/- 6 MeV fm3, and the imaginary volume integral is JI = 22.5 (+3.0, -1.5) MeV fm3.
- The 144Sm(alpha,gamma)148Gd reaction rate derived from the new potential is reduced by a factor of about 1.5 compared to a previous folding potential calculation by Rauscher (1995) and Mohr (1994).
- The resulting 146Sm/144Sm production ratio is about 0.3, which lies between the experimentally derived limits of 0.1 to 0.7 from Prinzhofer (1989).

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of alpha-nucleus optical potentials by Atzrott (1996) to the specific case of 144Sm at low energies relevant to the gamma-process in Type II supernovae, as proposed by Woosley and Howard (1978). Previous calculations of the 144Sm(alpha,gamma)148Gd capture cross section using equivalent square well potentials by Woosley and Howard (1990) differed by a factor of 10 depending on the radius parameter, while Woods-Saxon and folding potentials from Mann (1978), Mohr (1994), and Rauscher (1995) gave intermediate results. The new potential reduces the uncertainty in the capture cross section and enables more reliable calculations of the 146Sm/144Sm production ratio, which is important for interpreting meteoritic abundance anomalies.
