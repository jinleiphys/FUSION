# Incoherent photoproduction of $ϕ$-meson from deuteron at low energies

> The LEPS and CLAS data of the incoherent photoproduction of $ϕ$ meson from deuteron at low energies are studied with a model for $ϕ$ meson photoproduction from nucleon consisting of Pomeron, $π$, and $η$ meson exchanges in the t-channel, and a postulated resonance, with parameters fitted to recent LEPS data on $ϕ$ production from proton near threshold. The resonance was introduced to explain an observed bump in the forward differential cross section. Within impulse approximation, we find that the Fermi motion, final state interaction, and the resonance excitation all give important contributions to improve the agreement with data. However, discrepancies remain. Contributions from $ϕ$ production via spectator nucleon by other mesons like $π, ρ,$ and $ϕ$ produced from the first nucleon need to be calculated in order to gain insight on the medium effects as well as the existence of the postulated nucleon resonance.

## Key claim

The authors find that Fermi motion, final-state interaction, and resonance excitation all give important contributions to improve agreement with LEPS and CLAS data for incoherent photoproduction of phi meson from deuteron, but discrepancies remain, particularly at forward angles; the overall agreement ranges from poor to reasonable.

## Method

The authors model phi meson photoproduction from nucleon using Pomeron, pi, and eta meson exchanges in the t-channel plus a postulated N*(3/2-) resonance with mass 2.10 GeV and width 0.465 GeV, with parameters fitted to LEPS data on phi production from proton near threshold. They extend this model to the deuteron target within the impulse approximation, summing contributions from struck proton and struck neutron, using the Bonn potential for the deuteron wavefunction and including final-state interactions between the outgoing proton and neutron via the NN scattering amplitude. For neutron production, they assume Pomeron isoscalarity and use the relativistic quark model of Capstick (1992) to determine the resonance photocoupling from neutron.

## Key numbers

- Postulated resonance mass M = 2.10 ± 0.03 GeV and width Γ = 0.465 ± 0.141 GeV, from fits to LEPS proton data
- Differential cross sections for γ d → φ p n calculated at four energy bins between 1.97 and 2.37 GeV as a function of momentum transfer t_φ
- CLAS data compared for photon energies 1.65 ≤ E_γ ≤ 1.75 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier model (Kiswandhi 2010) for phi photoproduction on protons, which introduced a N*(3/2-) resonance to explain a near-threshold bump observed by LEPS, to the deuteron target where LEPS (Chang 2010, Chang 2010_DCS) and CLAS (Gao 2011) data have recently become available. The study aims to test whether the postulated resonance manifests in deuteron data, learn about phi production from neutrons, and investigate medium effects in the simplest nuclear target. The authors note that further improvements require calculating contributions from phi production via spectator nucleon by intermediate mesons (pi, rho, phi) produced from the first nucleon, and medium effects on meson propagation.
