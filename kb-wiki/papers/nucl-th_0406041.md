# Comment on "Unmasking the nuclear equation of state"

> Referring to a recently published paper [J. Piekarewicz, Phys. Rev. C 69, 041301 (2004)], we point out that Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations of the symmetry energy predict values of the neutron skin of heavy nuclei which are remarkably consistent with those from a recent ``best-fit'' analysis.

## Key claim

The Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) calculations of the symmetry energy predict a neutron skin thickness for 208Pb and a nuclear matter compression modulus that are remarkably close to the values Piekarewicz (2004) suggested as most realistic, even though the DBHF approach is microscopic and parameter-free rather than based on effective forces.

## Method

The author applies the equation of state for isospin-asymmetric matter from Alonso and Sammarruca (2003), derived from a microscopic relativistic DBHF calculation using the Bonn-B free-space two-body force with no free parameters, to predict neutron radii and neutron skins of heavy nuclei. The resulting predictions for the neutron skin of 208Pb and the compression modulus of symmetric nuclear matter are then compared to the values obtained by Piekarewicz (2004) from continuum RPA calculations using calibrated relativistic mean-field models.

## Key numbers

- Compression modulus of symmetric nuclear matter predicted by DBHF with Bonn-B: 233 MeV.
- Neutron skin of 208Pb predicted by DBHF with Bonn-B: 0.188 fm.
- Neutron skin of 208Pb suggested by Piekarewicz (2004) as most realistic: 0.17 fm.
- Compression modulus of symmetric nuclear matter suggested by Piekarewicz (2004): 248 ± 8 MeV.
- Upper bound on neutron skin of 208Pb from Piekarewicz (2004) analysis: 0.22 fm.

## Context

This comment extends the discussion in Piekarewicz (2004), who used calibrated relativistic mean-field models and continuum RPA to constrain the neutron skin of 208Pb and the compression modulus, concluding that the results favored non-relativistic predictions over relativistic ones. The author points out that microscopic relativistic DBHF calculations, which start from realistic free-space two-body forces rather than effective forces, produce values consistent with Piekarewicz's "best-fit" analysis, contradicting the implication that only non-relativistic models are supported. This work enables further comparison between parameter-free microscopic approaches and phenomenologically calibrated models for the nuclear equation of state.
