# Universality and the Coulomb interaction

> We study the relationship between universal effects and the Coulomb interaction. Here, we present our approach and a first illustrative example. Understanding the relationship of universality and the Coulomb interaction is important for weakly bound nuclear few-body systems. The example nucleus we study is the excited state of $^{17}$F which we model as a proton and an $^{16}$O core. We use a Gaussian potential to represent short-range forces together with a Coulomb potential. Our calculation uses the Gaussian Expansion Method (GEM), which is a variational method well-suited to our problem. We find that we need to choose the range of the Gaussian potential in addition to the potential strength that reproduces the right scattering length. We propose to fit the range of the Gaussian potential such that the effective range is reproduced. We show that this approach leads to consistent results for $^{17}$F.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for a weakly bound nuclear system with Coulomb interaction, the range of the short-range Gaussian potential must be fitted to reproduce the Coulomb-modified effective range, not just the scattering length, to obtain a unique prediction for the binding energy.

## Method

The author models the first excited state of ¹⁷F as a proton and an ¹⁶O core using a Hamiltonian with a Gaussian short-range potential plus the Coulomb potential. The system is solved variationally using the Gaussian Expansion Method (GEM) with geometrically spaced Gaussian basis functions. The range of the Gaussian potential is determined by fitting the Coulomb-modified effective range, while the potential strength is set to reproduce the Coulomb-modified scattering length.

## Key numbers

- The ¹⁶O-p Coulomb-modified scattering length is a_C = 4475.96 fm.
- The Coulomb-modified effective range for ¹⁶O-p is r_eff^C = 1.18737 fm.
- The binding momentum of the ¹⁷F excited state is gamma = 13.6 MeV.
- The asymptotic normalization coefficient for ¹⁷F is A approximately 80 fm^{-1/2}.
- The reduced mass of the ¹⁶O-p system is approximately 873 MeV.
- The range of the Gaussian potential that reproduces the correct effective range is r_0 = 2.75 fm.

## Context

This work extends studies of the Efimov effect in nuclear physics, which were complicated by the long-range Coulomb interaction, as discussed by Efimov (1991). The approach builds on the Coulomb-modified effective range expansion developed by Bethe (1949) and van Haeringen and Kok (1982), and uses the Gaussian Expansion Method of Kamimura and colleagues (2003). The method enables future calculations of trimers and tetramers of identical charged bosons, which will be presented in an upcoming publication.
