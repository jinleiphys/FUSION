# Revisting Lepton Pairs at the SPS

> We confirm the importance of standard medium effects (hadronic rescattering) in heavy-ion collisions by using a pQCD-based model to investigate the dilepton spectra from Pb+Au collisions at 158 GeV/nucleon.

## Key claim

The authors confirm that standard hadronic rescattering effects (specifically prompt ПҖ ПҒ вҶ’ ПҖ eвҒә eвҒ») can explain a portion of the dilepton excess observed by the CERES experiment in Pb+Au collisions at 158 GeV/nucleon, but not the entire effect.

## Method

The authors use a pQCD-based model similar to HIJING, employing PYTHIA and JETSET event generators with Glauber-type geometry to simulate nucleon-nucleon collisions and subsequent hadronic rescattering. Secondary scattering processes include ПҖвҒә ПҖвҒ» вҶ’ ПҒвҒ° вҶ’ eвҒә eвҒ», ПҖвҒ° ПҒВұ вҶ’ aвӮҒВұ вҶ’ ПҖВұ eвҒә eвҒ», and non-resonant ПҖвҒ° ПҒВұ вҶ’ ПҖВұ eвҒә eвҒ», with double counting between ПҖвҒә ПҖвҒ» annihilation and ПҖ ПҒ scattering accounted for. The model also includes О· production from intermediate-stage scattering of pions with previously unscattered projectile nucleons.

## Key numbers

- The model reproduces the total number of produced pions to better than a factor of two compared to CERN experimental data
- On average, only about 20 projectile nucleons rescatter with pions in Pb-induced collisions, and about 5 in S-induced collisions
- The dilepton excess from secondary scattering appears between an invariant mass of 200 and 500 MeV
- The calculations are much closer to experiment for the heavier Pb+Au system than for S+Au

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of S+Au dilepton spectra (Murray, Bauer, and Haglin, 1998) by applying the same rescattering model to Pb+Au collisions at the CERN SPS. The results are consistent with earlier studies by Haglin (1996) and others showing that conventional rescattering effects explain only part of the CERES dilepton excess, leaving room for alternative explanations such as in-medium vector meson mass shifts proposed by Li, Ko, and Brown (1995) or modifications to the ПҖвҒә ПҖвҒ» вҶ’ eвҒә eвҒ» reaction studied by Rapp, Chanfray, and Wambach (1996). The work enables more precise separation of conventional hadronic effects from possible signatures of quark-gluon plasma formation in future heavy-ion experiments.
