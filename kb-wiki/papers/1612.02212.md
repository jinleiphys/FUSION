# The effect of magnetization and electric polarization on the anomalous transport coefficients of a chiral fluid

> The effects of finite magnetization and electric polarization on dissipative and non-dissipative (anomalous) transport coefficients of a chiral fluid are studied. First, using the second law of thermodynamics as well as Onsager's time reversal symmetry principle, the complete set of dissipative transport coefficients of this medium is derived. It is shown that the properties of the resulting shear and bulk viscosities are mainly affected by the anisotropy induced by external electric and magnetic fields. Then, using the fact that the anomaly induced currents do not contribute to entropy production, the corresponding algebro-differential equations to non-dissipative anomalous transport coefficients are derived in a certain derivative expansion. The solutions of these equations show that, within this approximation, anomalous transport coefficients are, in particular, given in terms of the electric susceptibility of the medium.

## Key claim

The paper extends the standard derivation of anomalous transport coefficients (such as those for the chiral magnetic effect and chiral separation effect) to a chiral fluid with finite magnetization and electric polarization, showing that within a second-order derivative expansion these coefficients become functions of the electric susceptibility of the medium.

## Method

The authors first derive the complete set of dissipative transport coefficients for an anomalous electro-magnetohydrodynamic (EMHD) fluid by applying the second law of thermodynamics and Onsager's time-reversal symmetry principle to the entropy current, including the effects of both electric and magnetic fields. They then use the fact that anomaly-induced currents do not contribute to entropy production to derive algebro-differential equations for the non-dissipative anomalous transport coefficients, solving these equations analytically for media with vanishing and non-vanishing electric and magnetic susceptibilities.

## Key numbers

* The anomaly coefficient for the axial current is given by \(C = e^2/(4\pi^2)\), appearing in the anomalous conservation equation \(\partial_\mu n_a^\mu = -C E\cdot B\).
* The electric and magnetic susceptibilities, \(\chi_e\) and \(\chi_m\), are introduced via the linearized relations \(\mathbf{P} = \chi_e \mathbf{E}\) and \(\mathbf{M} = \chi_m \mathbf{B}\).
* The relation \(\partial_\mu E^\mu = u\cdot\mathcal{D}E - \frac{2(1-\chi_m)}{(1+\chi_e)}B\cdot\omega + \frac{n_e}{1+\chi_e}\) is derived for the divergence of the electric field four-vector in the presence of finite susceptibilities.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic hydrodynamical approach of Son and Surowka (2009) and Sadofyev, Isachenkov, and Andreev (2010), who derived anomalous transport coefficients for a chiral fluid without in-medium effects, to include finite magnetization and electric polarization. It also generalizes the dissipative EMHD framework of Rischke and collaborators (2010, 2011), which neglected electric field effects, by incorporating both electric and magnetic fields and their interplay. The results are relevant for quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions, which is known to have finite magnetic and electric susceptibilities as studied by Bali et al. (2012) and Fukushima (2010).
