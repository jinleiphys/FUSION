# Bound to unbound states transitions of heavy quarkonia in the cooling phase of QGP

> Emphasizing the possibility of moderate suppression of heavy quarkonium states, we invite some attention towards the issue of real time evolution of quarkonia during the cooling phase of quark gluon plasma(QGP). In this context, we have used time dependent perturbation theory to show that $Υ(1S)$, $Υ(2S)$, $J/Ψ$ can further be dissociated in the medium at a temperature below their dissociation thresholds even though they survive the Debye screening. We have presented and compared the dissociation probabilities and dissociation rates of these states in real and complex valued potential in this article. We realise that our method is an approximate way to analyse the short time behaviour of quarkonium in real valued potential and for long time behaviour one must adopt a non perturbative technique for solving schrödinger equation of quarkonium bound states in evolving QGP. On the other hand the perturbation technique seems to be valid enough to deal with the same for a complex valued quark anti-quark potential.

# Bound to unbound states transitions of heavy quarkonia in the cooling phase of QGP

## Key claim

Heavy quarkonium states (Υ(1S), Υ(2S), J/Ψ) that survive Debye screening at high temperature can still undergo dissociation at temperatures below their dissociation thresholds during the cooling phase of quark-gluon plasma, due to non-adiabatic transitions driven by the time-dependent potential. The authors acknowledge that their perturbative method is only approximate for short-time behavior in a real-valued potential, but argue it is valid for the entire cooling phase when a complex-valued potential is used.

## Method

The authors model heavy quarkonia in a time-dependent potential where the Debye mass varies with temperature according to a Bjorken cooling law T(t) = T₀(τ₀/(τ₀+t))^(1/3), with τ₀ = 0.5 fm/c. They apply first-order time-dependent perturbation theory to calculate transition amplitudes from bound states (Υ(1S), Υ(2S), J/Ψ) to unbound plane-wave continuum states, using both a real-valued heavy quark potential and a complex-valued potential that includes an imaginary part from Landau damping. The bound state wave functions and binding energies are obtained via the Numerov method, and the total dissociation probability is obtained by integrating over all continuum momenta.

## Key numbers

- Υ(1S) dissociation probability increases approximately linearly with time in the real-valued potential, obeying the Fermi golden rule, and the calculation is reliable for times up to a few fm/c in that case
- Υ(2S) and J/Ψ dissociation probabilities in the real potential are higher than that of Υ(1S), and the perturbative results are reliable up to approximately 5-6 fm/c for the real potential
- The complex-valued potential calculation yields a dissociation rate for Υ(1S) that decays faster than for the other two states, because Υ(1S) spends longer time in the medium
- The medium cools to approximately 230 MeV after about 2 fm; J/Ψ can be formed when the temperature drops to 1.7 T_c after about 1.24 fm
- Charm quark mass taken as 1.3 GeV; bottom quark mass taken as 4.66 GeV
- Initial temperature of thermalized deconfined medium at t=0 taken as approximately 400 MeV
- Pseudo-critical temperature T_c taken as 155 MeV
- Strong coupling α ≈ 0.47; string tension σ ≈ 0.2

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of heavy quarkonium real-time dynamics in QGP, which either modeled quarkonia as open quantum systems (Akamatsu 2015, Rothkopf 2014, Brambilla 2017) or solved the Schrodinger equation in a potential model (Borghini 2011, Akamatsu 2011). Those prior works addressed bound-state to bound-state transitions in static QGP using master equations or stochastic Schrodinger equations, but did not calculate dissociation probabilities to unbound states. The present paper addresses bound-to-unbound transitions in an evolving (cooling) medium, which was previously untreated. The results enable more complete modeling of sequential quarkonium suppression in heavy-ion collisions, where dissociation through real-time evolution must be considered alongside Debye screening and gluodissociation. An earlier attempt by Bagchi (2014) used the sudden approximation for dissociation during thermalization.
