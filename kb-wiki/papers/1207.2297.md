# Spherical to deformed shape transition in the nucleon-pair shell model

## Key claim
The nucleon-pair approximation (NPA) of the shell model can produce a shape transition from spherical to axially deformed nuclei in the even Ce isotopes, but only when the dominant collective pairs are determined using a Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) framework appropriate to deformed nuclei rather than the traditional broken-pair approximation. However, the resulting transition is too rapid, with nuclei that should be transitional (such as 144Ce) appearing fairly well deformed, suggesting the need to include several pairs with each angular momentum.

## Method
The authors applied the nucleon-pair approximation (NPA) truncated to S (spin-zero), D (spin-two), and G (spin-four) collective pairs for both neutrons and protons, using a semi-realistic Hamiltonian with monopole and quadrupole pairing in the like-nucleon channels and a separable quadrupole-quadrupole interaction in the neutron-proton channel. For the deformed region, they derived the structure of collective pairs from axially-symmetric HFB calculations in the canonical basis, projecting pairs of definite angular momentum from the intrinsic HFB wave function. They compared results using traditional broken-pair approximation (BPA) pairs, HFB-derived pairs, and a combined basis (COM) that included both sets of pairs with nonorthogonality taken into account, for the even-mass Ce isotopes 142Ce through 148Ce with a 132Sn doubly-magic core.

## Key numbers
- Experimental R4/2 ratios for the Ce chain: 142Ce = 1.90, 144Ce = 2.36, 146Ce = 2.58, 148Ce = 2.87 (SU(3) rotational limit = 3.33)
- Experimental R6/2 ratios: 142Ce = 2.72, 144Ce = 4.15, 146Ce = 4.52, 148Ce = 5.32 (SU(3) limit = 7.00)
- HFB deformation energy for 148Ce: 1.27 MeV
- Monopole pairing strength for protons: Gπ0 = -0.18 MeV; for neutrons: Gν0 = -0.13 MeV
- Quadrupole pairing strength for neutrons: Gν2 = -0.012 MeV
- Proton-neutron quadrupole interaction strength: κ = -0.20 MeV
- Additional binding from HFB pairs relative to BPA pairs in 142Ce: roughly 0.1 MeV

## Context
This work extends earlier model studies by Draayer and collaborators that showed the NPA truncated to S and D pairs could qualitatively reproduce IBM phase transitions, but only in schematic calculations not connected to real nuclei. The authors build on the HFB-based prescription for collective pairs first discussed by Pittel and Dukelsky (2000) in the context of microscopically deriving the IBM for deformed nuclei. The results highlight a persistent difficulty: the NPA, like early microscopic IBM derivations, struggles to describe transitional nuclei and produces moments of inertia that are too small for well-deformed systems. The authors suggest that including multiple pairs per angular momentum (as in their COM calculations) may improve the description of transitional regions, analogous to how Otsuka and collaborators (2005) recently succeeded in mapping density functionals directly onto IBM collective surfaces.
