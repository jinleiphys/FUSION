# Nucleon sum rules in symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter

> We calculate the nucleon parameters in isospin symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter using the QCD sum rules. The higher moments of the nucleon structure functions are included. The complete set of the nucleon expectation values of the four-quark operators is employed. We analyze the role of the lowest order radiative corrections beyond the logarithmic approximation.

# Nucleon sum rules in symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter

## Key claim
The authors calculate nucleon self-energies and effective masses in symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter using QCD sum rules, including higher moments of nucleon structure functions and the complete set of four-quark operators, but the analysis is restricted to the gas approximation (linear density dependence) and the radiative corrections are evaluated only for the vacuum case, not yet extended to finite density.

## Method
The authors apply Borel-transformed QCD sum rules to the in-medium nucleon propagator, separating contributions from local condensates (vector, scalar, gluon), nonlocal condensates (higher moments and twists of nucleon structure functions), and four-quark condensates (evaluated using the perturbative chiral quark model). The sum rules are solved for the vector self-energy Σ_v, Dirac effective mass m*, and single-particle potential energy U as functions of total density ρ and asymmetry parameter β = (ρ_n − ρ_p)/(ρ_p + ρ_n), using the gas approximation and subtracting vacuum contributions.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³
- Vacuum nucleon mass without α_s corrections: m = 0.930 GeV, residue λ² = 1.79 GeV⁶, continuum threshold W² = 2.00 GeV²
- Vacuum nucleon mass with α_s corrections (α_s = 0.35): m = 0.94 GeV, residue λ² = 2.00 GeV⁶, continuum threshold W² = 1.90 GeV²
- Vector condensate normalization: v_N = 3, v_N^(−) = −1
- Scalar condensate linear coefficient: κ_N = 8
- Asymmetric scalar condensate coefficient: ζ_N = 0.54
- Four-quark condensate coefficients for symmetric matter: A^q_4q = −0.11, A^p_4q = −0.57, A^I_4q = 1.90
- Four-quark condensate β-dependence: A^q_4q = −0.11 − 0.21β, A^p_4q = −0.57 + 0.09β, A^I_4q = 1.90 − 0.92β
- Gluon condensate density coefficient: g_N = −(8/9)m (model-independent)
- Borel mass range used: 0.8 ≤ M² ≤ 1.4 GeV²

## Context
This work extends the QCD sum rule approach for nucleons in nuclear matter, originally developed for symmetric matter by Cohen, Furnstahl, and Griegel (1991–1992), to include isospin asymmetry and a complete set of four-quark condensates from the perturbative chiral quark model of Thomas et al. (2000). The inclusion of higher moments of nucleon structure functions (using parton distributions from Gluck, Reya, and Vogt) goes beyond earlier treatments that considered only local condensates. The radiative corrections analysis follows the work of Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1979) and the more recent calculation by Colangelo and Khodjamirian (2000) which found a large coefficient (~6) for the lowest-order α_s correction. The results enable future extension of radiative corrections to finite density and provide predictions for neutron-rich matter relevant to neutron star physics.
