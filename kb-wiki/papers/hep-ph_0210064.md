# The nucleon's strange electromagnetic and scalar matrix elements

> Quenched lattice QCD simulations and quenched chiral perturbation theory are used together for this study of strangeness in the nucleon. Dependences of the matrix elements on strange quark mass, valence quark mass and momentum transfer are discussed in both the lattice and chiral frameworks. The combined results of this study are in good agreement with existing experimental data and predictions are made for upcoming experiments. Possible future refinements of the theoretical method are suggested.

# The nucleon's strange electromagnetic and scalar matrix elements

## Key claim
The strangeness electric and magnetic form factors of the nucleon are very small and consistent with zero within uncertainties, both in raw lattice QCD results and after chiral perturbation theory extrapolation to physical quark masses; the results agree with existing experimental data, but the quenched approximation and limited statistics prevent a definitive nonzero determination.

## Method
Quenched lattice QCD simulations were performed on 2000 gauge configurations (20³×32 lattices, β=6, Wilson gauge action) using Wilson fermions with three valence quark masses (κ_v=0.152, 0.153, 0.154) and two strange quark loop masses (κ_l=0.152, 0.154). Strange quark loop contributions to nucleon three-point functions were computed using stochastic Z₂ noise estimators with perturbative subtraction, and matrix elements were extracted via a differential method. Quenched SU(3) chiral perturbation theory expressions for the scalar density, magnetic form factor, and electric form factor were derived and fitted to the lattice data to enable extrapolation to physical quark masses.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.1011(7) fm (from string tension √K = 427 MeV)
- Pion masses for valence quarks: am_π = 0.4772(9), 0.4237(8), and 0.364(1)
- Vector meson masses for strange loop quarks: 912(8) MeV (κ_l=0.152) and 1066(4) MeV (κ_l=0.154), bracketing m_φ = 1019 MeV
- At -q² = 0.1 GeV²: G_M^(s) = 0.05 ± 0.06 (this work) vs. 0.14 ± 0.29 ± 0.31 (SAMPLE experiment)
- At -q² = 0.477 GeV²: G_E^(s) + 0.39 G_M^(s) = 0.07 ± 0.05 (this work) vs. 0.025 ± 0.020 ± 0.014 (HAPPEX experiment)
- Fractional strange quark contribution to nucleon mass: (m_s/m_N)⟨N|s̄s|N⟩(0) = 0.21(11) at β=6.0

## Context
This work extends earlier quenched lattice studies of nucleon strangeness (Mathur et al. 2000, Dong et al. 1996) by using higher statistics (2000 configurations) and combining with quenched SU(3) chiral perturbation theory to handle extrapolations. The results contradict the nonzero G_M^(s) interpretation of Mathur et al. (2000), who used only 100 configurations. The study enables comparison with ongoing experiments (A4, G0, HAPPEx-II) and suggests that future partially quenched simulations and lighter valence quarks could improve precision, while noting that isospin violation effects (Lewis and Mobed 2001) are comparable in magnitude to the strange quark effects studied here.
