# Coexistence of BCS and BEC-like pair structures in halo nuclei

> We investigate the spatial structure of the two-neutron wave function in the Borromean nucleus $^{11}$Li, using a three-body model of $^9$Li $+n+n$, which includes many-body correlations stemming from the Pauli principle. The behavior of the neutron pair at different densities is simulated by calculating the two-neutron wave function at several distances between the core nucleus $^9$Li and the center of mass of the two neutrons. With this representation, a strong concentration of the neutron pair on the nuclear surface is for the first time quantitatively established for neutron-rich nuclei. That is, the neutron pair wave function in $^{11}$Li has an oscillatory behavior at normal density, while it becomes a well localized single peak in the dilute density region around the nuclear surface. We point out that these features qualitatively correspond to the BCS and BEC-like structures of the pair wave function found in infinite nuclear matter.

# Coexistence of BCS and BEC-like pair structures in halo nuclei

## Key claim

The two-neutron pair wave function in the Borromean nucleus $^{11}$Li exhibits a qualitative transition from BCS-like oscillatory behavior at normal nuclear density to a BEC-like compact single-peak structure in the dilute density region around the nuclear surface, demonstrating that both pairing regimes coexist within a single finite nucleus. This is the first quantitative establishment of a strong concentration of the neutron pair on the nuclear surface for neutron-rich nuclei.

## Method

The authors solved a three-body Hamiltonian for $^{9}$Li $+ n + n$ using a density-dependent delta pairing interaction, with the core density described by a Fermi function. The two-neutron wave function was expanded in eigenfunctions of the single-particle Hamiltonian, excluding states occupied by core nucleons to enforce the Pauli principle, and then transformed to relative ($r$) and center-of-mass ($R$) coordinates using the Bayman-Kallio method. The $L=0$ component of the $S=0$ wave function was analyzed at several fixed distances $R$ between the core and the two-neutron center of mass to simulate the pair behavior at different densities.

## Key numbers

- The $L=0$ component probability in the $S=0$ wave function is $P_L = 0.578$, with $L=2$ at 0.020 and $L=4$ at 0.0045; the total $S=0$ probability is 0.606.
- The two-peaked structure in the $L=0$ wave function shows a di-neutron peak at $(r,R) = (2.2, 3.4)$ fm and a cigar-like peak at $(r,R) = (4.4, 1.8)$ fm.
- The root mean square distance $r_{\rm rms}$ of the neutron pair in $^{11}$Li reaches a minimum of 2.6 fm at $R = 3.2$ fm (density $\rho \sim 0.4\rho_0$), compared to the asymptotic $nn$ virtual state extension of approximately 12 fm.
- For $^{16}$C, the rms distance minimum occurs at $\rho \sim 0.2\rho_0$ ($R \sim 4.2$ fm).

## Context

This work extends the BCS-BEC crossover concept from infinite nuclear matter, as studied by Matsuo (2006), to finite neutron-rich nuclei, and provides a unified picture of the di-neutron and cigar-like configurations previously discussed in Borromean nuclei by Zhukov (1993) and by Barranco (2001). The results enable future studies of surface pairing condensation in other neutron-rich systems, including $^{6}$He, $^{16}$C, and $^{24}$O, and connect to earlier work on spatial correlations in stable heavy nuclei by Catara (1984) and by Terasaki (1998).
