# Open charm and bottom tetraquarks in an extended relativized quark model

> In present work, we systematically calculate the mass spectra of open charm and bottom tetraquarks $q q\bar q \bar Q$ within an extended relativized quark model. The four-body relativized Hamiltonians including the Coulomb potential, confining potential, spin-spin interactions, and relativistic corrections are solved by using the variational method. It is found that the predicted masses of four $0^+$ $ud\bar s \bar c$ states are 2765, 3065, 3152, and 3396 MeV, which disfavor the assignment of the newly observed $X_0(2900)$ as a compact tetraquark. Moreover, the whole mass spectra of the open charm and bottom tetraquarks show quite similar patterns, which preserve the light flavor SU(3) symmetry and heavy quark symmetry well. In addition, our results suggest that the flavor exotic states $nn\bar s \bar c$, $nn\bar s \bar b$, $ss\bar n \bar c$, and $ss\bar n \bar b$ and their antiparticles can be searched in the heavy-light meson plus kaon final states by future experiments. More theoretical and experimental efforts are needed to investigate these singly heavy tetraquarks.

## Key claim
The predicted masses of four \(0^+\) \(ud\bar s \bar c\) states (2765, 3065, 3152, and 3396 MeV) disfavor the assignment of the newly observed \(X_0(2900)\) as a compact tetraquark, though the authors note that other interpretations (molecules, kinematic effects) remain possible and that the \(X_1(2900)\) is also excluded as a compact tetraquark due to its negative parity.

## Method
The authors solved a four-body relativized Hamiltonian (including Coulomb, confining, spin-spin, and relativistic corrections) using the variational method with Gaussian basis functions. They constructed total wave functions from color, flavor, spin, and spatial parts, enforcing antisymmetry for identical quarks and antiquarks, and diagonalized the mass matrix for configurations with the same \(I(J^P)\).

## Key numbers
- Four \(0^+\) \(ud\bar s \bar c\) states predicted at 2765, 3065, 3152, and 3396 MeV, which disfavor the \(X_0(2900)\) (mass \(2866.3 \pm 6.5 \pm 2.0\) MeV) as a compact tetraquark.
- Lowest \(0^+\) \(nn\bar n \bar c\) state mass: 2570 MeV; lowest \(0^+\) \(nn\bar n \bar b\) state mass: 5977 MeV.
- Lowest \(0^+\) \(nn\bar s \bar c\) state (isospin 0): 2765 MeV; lowest \(0^+\) \(nn\bar s \bar c\) state (isospin 1): 3065 MeV.
- Lowest \(0^+\) \(nn\bar s \bar b\) state (isospin 0): 6194 MeV; lowest \(0^+\) \(nn\bar s \bar b\) state (isospin 1): 6430 MeV.
- \(X_1(2900)\) mass: \(2904.1 \pm 4.8 \pm 1.3\) MeV; its negative parity excludes assignment as an \(S\)-wave compact tetraquark.

## Context
This work extends the relativized quark model (Godfrey and Isgur, 1985) to open charm and bottom tetraquarks, following the authors' prior studies on doubly and fully heavy tetraquarks (Lu et al., 2020). It contradicts the simple quark model interpretation of \(X_0(2900)\) as a compact tetraquark (Karliner and Rosner, 2020) and the color-magnetic interaction model (Cheng et al., 2020), which gave a higher \(0^+\) state near 2850 MeV. The results enable future experimental searches for flavor-exotic states \(nn\bar s \bar c\), \(nn\bar s \bar b\), \(ss\bar n \bar c\), and \(ss\bar n \bar b\) in heavy-light meson plus kaon final states.
