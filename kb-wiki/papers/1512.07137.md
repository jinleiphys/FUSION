# Long wavelength perfect fluidity from short distance jet transport in quark-gluon plasmas

> We build a new phenomenological framework that bridges the long wavelength bulk viscous transport properties of the strongly-coupled quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) and short distance hard jet transport properties in the QGP. The full nonperturbative chromo-electric (E) and chromo-magnetic (M) structure of the near "perfect fluid" like sQGP in the critical transition region are integrated into a semi-Quark-Gluon-Monopole Plasma (sQGMP) model lattice-compatibly and implemented into the new CUJET3.0 jet quenching framework. All observables computed from CUJET3.0 are found to be consistent with available data at RHIC and LHC simultaneously. A quantitative connection between the shear viscosity and jet transport parameter is rigorously established within this framework. We deduce the $T=160-600$ MeV dependence of the QGP's $η/s$: its near vanishing value in the near $T_c$ regime is determined by the composition of E and M charges, it increases as $T$ rises, and its high $T$ limit is fixed by color screening scales.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a quantitative connection between the long wavelength shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (η/s) and the short distance jet transport parameter (q̂) in the quark-gluon plasma, showing that the near-perfect fluidity (η/s ≈ 1/4π) near Tc arises from the nonperturbative chromo-electric and chromo-magnetic structure of the medium, with the exact minimum value depending on the chosen deconfinement scheme (χT^L or χT^u).

## Method

The authors constructed the CUJET3.0 framework by integrating a semi-Quark-Gluon-Monopole Plasma (sQGMP) model, which includes both chromo-electric quasi-particles (EQPs) and chromo-magnetic monopoles (MQPs), into the DGLV energy loss kernel. The model parameters (αc, cm) were constrained by lattice QCD data for the Polyakov loop, screening masses μE,M, and equation of state, as well as by the reference datum at LHC for RAA of charged hadrons at pT = 12.5 GeV. Jet quenching observables (RAA and v2) for light hadrons and open heavy flavors were computed using 2+1D viscous hydrodynamical backgrounds from VISHNU simulations and compared with RHIC and LHC data.

## Key numbers

- Tc = 160 MeV, with the temperature range studied being T = 160-600 MeV
- The shear viscosity minimum appears at T ∼ 1.3-1.4 Tc, coinciding with the peak of scaled monopole number density ρM/T^3
- The (η/s)min approaches the KSS quantum bound η/s = 1/4π
- Reference datum: LHC 20-30% Pb+Pb √sNN = 2.76 TeV RAAh±(pT = 12.5 GeV) ≈ 0.3
- Three parameter schemes studied: (i) αc = 0.95, cm = 0.3, χT^L; (ii) αc = 0.95, cm = 0.4, χT^u; (iii) αc = 1.33, cm = 0.3, χT^u
- Jet transport parameter q̂F/T^3 shows a maximum at T ∼ 1.3-1.4 Tc
- High pT observables computed for pT > 8 GeV

## Context

This work extends the CUJET2.0 framework (which used HTL QGP) by incorporating the nonperturbative chromo-electric and chromo-magnetic structure of the sQGP near Tc, building on the EM seesaw scenario proposed by Liao and Shuryak and the semi-QGP model. It bridges the gap between soft hydrodynamic descriptions of perfect fluidity and hard perturbative QCD jet quenching models, enabling quantitative constraints on confinement physics from heavy-ion data at RHIC and LHC. The framework allows future work to use open heavy flavor RAA and v2 data as probes of the detailed composition of electric and magnetic degrees of freedom near Tc.
