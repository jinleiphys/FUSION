# Scalarful double beta decay

> We revisit scalar emissions in double beta decays of nuclei, often discussed in the context of Majoron models, in light of the latest developments on the study of neutrinoless double beta decay amplitudes from an effective field theory approach. The sensitivity of double beta decay experiments to this process is assessed through an analysis of spectral shapes, and the study is extended to massive scalars, scalars coupling to sterile neutrinos, and exotic right-handed effective couplings.

## Key claim
The paper revisits scalar emissions in double beta decay (often called Majoron models) using an effective field theory approach to neutrinoless double beta decay amplitudes, finding that the sensitivity of experiments to these processes depends critically on the spectral shape of the emitted electrons and the mass of the scalar, with the caveat that for certain intermediate scalar masses the angular correlation becomes indistinguishable from the standard two-neutrino double beta decay background.

## Method
The authors compute the decay rate for neutrinoless double beta decay with scalar emission ($0\nu\beta\beta\phi$) by deriving the neutrino potential from effective scalar-neutrino couplings, including contributions from active neutrinos, sterile neutrinos, and mixed couplings. They map the nuclear matrix elements to those of standard $0\nu\beta\beta$ decay using interpolation formulae for neutrino masses below and above 2 GeV, and compute phase space factors and angular correlations for the emitted electrons. A frequentist statistical analysis is employed to set limits on the effective scalar-neutrino coupling $g_{ee}$ by comparing the predicted summed electron energy, single electron energy, and angular distributions to the standard $2\nu\beta\beta$ background.

## Key numbers
- Phase space factor for massless scalar emission in $^{136}$Xe: $\Omega^\phi/g_A^4 \sim 2.7 \times 10^{-16}~\text{yr}^{-1}$ (compared to $4.1 \times 10^{-16}~\text{yr}^{-1}$ and $4.3 \times 10^{-16}~\text{yr}^{-1}$ in prior work using exact Dirac wavefunctions)
- Central values of $\xi_{31}$ parameter for $2\nu\beta\beta$: 0.16 for $^{136}$Xe, 0.12 for $^{76}$Ge, 0.13 for $^{82}$Se, 0.56 for $^{100}$Mo
- Q-values used: 2.458 MeV ($^{136}$Xe), 2.039 MeV ($^{76}$Ge), 2.995 MeV ($^{82}$Se), 3.034 MeV ($^{100}$Mo)
- Gamow-Teller matrix element $M_{GT}^{(-1)}$: 0.013 ($^{136}$Xe), 0.058 ($^{76}$Ge), 0.050 ($^{82}$Se), 0.11 ($^{100}$Mo)
- Low-energy constants for short-distance contributions: $g_1^{NN} = (1+3g_A^2)/4$, $g_1^{\pi N}=0$, $g_1^{\pi\pi} = 0.36$

## Context
This work extends the seminal earlier papers on Majoron-emitting double beta decay by Doi (1987), Brune (2018), and Blum (2018), incorporating the latest effective field theory framework for $0\nu\beta\beta$ amplitudes developed by Cirigliano (2017, 2018) and Dekens (2024). It enables improved experimental sensitivity projections for upcoming large-scale experiments such as nEXO, LEGEND, and CUPID by providing more accurate spectral shape predictions and quantifying the impact of nuclear matrix element uncertainties, particularly the $\xi_{31}$ parameter range $[0.12, 0.20]$ which is identified as the dominant source of uncertainty in setting limits on scalar couplings.
