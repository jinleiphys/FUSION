# Comment on "Comparison of optical potential for nucleons and Δ resonances" by Arie Bodek and Tejin Cai

## Key claim
Bodek and Cai (2020) claim that the Δ resonance potential in nuclei is considerably more attractive than the nucleon potential (e.g., for ¹²C, U^Δ ≈ 1.5 U^nucleon), but this result is at variance with a multitude of earlier analyses of (e,A) and (γ,A) reactions performed about 30–40 years ago, which consistently found the opposite relation.

## Method
The comment reviews prior theoretical analyses of (γ,A) and (e,A) reactions from the 1980s that extracted Δ potentials by carefully evaluating non-resonant background terms. It compares these earlier results, obtained from studies of the Δ self-energy in nuclear matter and from phenomenological fits to inclusive scattering data, with the potentials reported by Bodek and Cai (2020).

## Key numbers
- For ¹²C, Bodek and Cai find U^Δ ≈ 1.5 U^nucleon, implying the Δ potential is more attractive than the nucleon potential.
- Hirata et al. (1978) obtained a Δ potential depth of -30 MeV.
- Freedman et al. (1981) also obtained a Δ potential of -30 MeV.
- Garcia-Recio et al. (1989) obtained V_Δ = -33 ρ/ρ₀ MeV (ρ-dependent, at zero momentum).
- Oset et al. (1987) obtained V_Δ = -23 ± 5 ρ/ρ₀ MeV (at zero momentum).
- Ericson and Weise (1988) give a Δ potential depth of -30 MeV in ¹²C and ¹⁶O.
- Earlier nucleon potentials are about 40–50 MeV depth, establishing an approximate relation U^Δ ≈ 2/3 U^nucleon.

## Context
This comment directly contradicts the finding of Bodek and Cai (2020) that the Δ potential is more attractive than the nucleon potential. It instead aligns with a body of work from the 1970s–1980s, including Hirata et al. (1978), Freedman et al. (1981), Garcia-Recio et al. (1989), Oset et al. (1987), and the book by Ericson and Weise (1988), which consistently found the Δ potential to be less attractive (about 2/3 the depth of the nucleon potential). The comment highlights that proper treatment of non-resonant background is essential for extracting reliable Δ potentials, and that the Bodek and Cai result, if incorrect, would affect event generators for electron and neutrino interactions.
