# Modeling of $^6_Λ$He hypernucleus within configuration space Faddeev approach

> The cluster $^4\rm He+Λ+\rm n$ model is applied to describe the $^6_Λ$He hypernucleus. The consideration is based on the configuration space Faddeev equations for a system of non-identical particles. A set of the pair potentials includes the OBE simulating (NSC97f) model for the $Λ\rm n$ interaction and the phenomenological potentials for the $αΛ$ and $α\rm n$ interactions. We calculated energies of spin (1$^-$,2$^-$) doublet. For the 2$^-$ excitation energy, the obtained value is 0.18 MeV. The hyperon binding energy of the bound 1$^-$ state is less than the experimental value, which may be an evidence for violation of the exact three-body cluster structure.

## Key claim

The cluster $^4\textrm{He}+\Lambda+\textrm{n}$ model, solved via configuration-space Faddeev equations, yields a hyperon binding energy for the $1^-$ ground state of $^6_\Lambda\textrm{He}$ that is *less* than the experimental value, which the authors interpret as possible evidence for violation of the exact three-body cluster structure.

## Method

The authors solved the configuration-space Faddeev equations for three non-identical particles ($\alpha$, $\Lambda$, neutron) using an $LS$-coupling partial-wave expansion. The $\Lambda\textrm{n}$ interaction was represented by a three-range Gaussian potential simulating the NSC97f model of baryon-baryon interaction, while the $\alpha\Lambda$ and $\alpha\textrm{n}$ interactions were taken from phenomenological potentials. The $2^-$ resonance energy was extracted via the method of analytical continuation in a coupling constant, using a Pade approximant.

## Key numbers

* Hyperon binding energy of the $1^-$ ground state of $^6_\Lambda\textrm{He}$: 0.65 MeV (calculated without three-body force).
* Excitation energy of the $2^-$ state relative to the $1^-$ state: 0.18 MeV.
* Resonance energy of the $2^-$ state (without three-body force): about 0.11 MeV.
* Experimental hyperon binding energy of the $^6_\Lambda\textrm{He}$ ground state: $0.17 \pm 0.10$ MeV (with respect to the $^5_\Lambda\textrm{He}+\textrm{n}$ threshold).
* Experimental neutron separation energy $E_n$ for $^6_\Lambda\textrm{He}$: $0.17 \pm 0.10$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior three-body cluster $\alpha\Lambda\textrm{n}$ calculations by Motoba et al. and Hiyama et al. (1996), which either underestimated or overestimated the binding energy of $^6_\Lambda\textrm{He}$. The present results disagree with the binding energy from Hiyama et al. (2014) but yield a similar $2^-$ resonance energy. The authors note that a three-body force is required to reproduce the experimental data within this cluster model, and that the cluster structure may be violated at small interparticle distances.
