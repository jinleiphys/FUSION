# $d^*(2380)$ and its partners in a diquark model

> The purpose of the present study was to explore the possibility of accommodating the $d^*(2380)$ and its flavor SU(3) partners in a diquark model. Proposing that $d^*(2380)$ is composed of three vector diquarks, its mass is calculated by use of an effective Hamiltonian approach and its decay width is estimated by considering the effects of quark tunneling from one diquark to the others and the decays of the subsequent two-baryon bound state. Both the obtained mass and decay width of $d^*(2380)$ are in agreement with the experimental data, with the unexpected narrow decay width being naturally explained by the large tunneling suppression of a quark between a pair of diquarks. The masses and decay widths of the flavor SU(3) partners of $d^*(2380)$ are also predicated within the same diquark scenario.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the hexaquark state \(d^*(2380)\) can be described as a bound state of three vector diquarks, and that this diquark model naturally explains the unexpectedly narrow decay width of \(d^*(2380)\) through a quark tunneling mechanism, with the caveat that the decay width estimate is a rough approximation rather than a precise calculation.

## Method

The authors construct the \(d^*(2380)\) as a system of three color-antitriplet vector diquarks, each with spin 1 and isospin 1, coupled to total quantum numbers \(I(J^P) = 0(3^+)\). The mass is calculated using an effective Hamiltonian with QCD-inspired interactions, where model parameters are fixed by fitting known masses of heavy baryons (\(\Lambda_c\), \(\Sigma_c\), \(\Sigma_c^*\), \(\Xi_c\), \(\Xi'_c\), \(\Xi_c^*\), \(\Omega_c^0\)) and heavy mesons (\(D_s\), \(D_s^*\)). The decay width is estimated via a two-step mechanism: first a quark tunnels from one diquark to another to form a bound \(\Delta\Delta\) state, then that bound state decays, with the tunneling probability computed using a semiclassical approximation.

## Key numbers

- Mass of \(d^*(2380)\) calculated as 2383 MeV, compared to experimental value of 2380 MeV
- Calculated mass of \(d^*(2380)\) is 81 MeV below the \(\Delta\Delta\) threshold of 2464 MeV
- Predicted masses for flavor SU(3) partners: \(d^*_s\) at 2541 MeV (76 MeV below \(\Delta\Sigma^*\) threshold), \(d^*_{2s}\) at 2689 MeV (73 MeV below \(\Delta\Xi^*\) threshold), \(d^*_{3s}\) at 2797 MeV (107 MeV below \(\Delta\Omega\) threshold)
- At a diquark-diquark distance \(r_0 \sim 1.3\) fm, the estimated decay width of \(d^*(2380)\) is 71.4 MeV, close to the experimental value of about 70 MeV
- Predicted decay widths at \(r_0 = 1.3\) fm: \(d^*_s\) about 47 MeV, \(d^*_{2s}\) about 39 MeV, \(d^*_{3s}\) about 25 MeV

## Context

This work extends the diquark model approach, originally proposed by Gell-Mann (1964) and later applied to multiquark states by Maiani (2004) and others, to the hexaquark sector. It contrasts with the hidden-color hexaquark picture of Huang (2014, 2016) and the \(\Delta\Delta\) bound state interpretation, offering an alternative explanation for the narrow width of \(d^*(2380)\) observed by the WASA-at-COSY Collaboration (2011-2015). The predictions for the flavor SU(3) partners \(d^*_s\), \(d^*_{2s}\), and \(d^*_{3s}\) provide targets for future experimental searches.
