# Quantifying a Possibly Reduced Jet-Medium Coupling of the sQGP at the LHC

> The nuclear modification factor R_{AA} measured in Pb+Pb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) suggests that the jet-medium coupling in a Quark-Gluon Plasma at LHC energies is reduced as compared to energies reached at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). We estimate the reduction factor using a simple generic energy-loss model and find that the jet-medium coupling at the LHC is approximately 10% smaller than at RHIC. Moreover, we examine different jet-energy loss prescriptions with $dE/dx\sim E^a$ and show that the measured momentum dependence of the nuclear modification factor at the LHC rules out any model with $E^{a>1/3}$.

## Key claim

The jet-medium coupling in the Quark-Gluon Plasma at LHC energies is approximately 10% smaller than at RHIC, based on a simple generic energy-loss model; this reduction is a moderate effect, and the measured momentum dependence of the nuclear modification factor rules out any energy-loss model with a power-law exponent \(a > 1/3\) in \(dE/dx \sim E^a\).

## Method

The authors used a generic jet-energy loss model where the energy loss per unit time is proportional to a dimensionless coupling \(\kappa\), the energy dependence \(P^a\), a path-length dependence \(\tau^z\), and a local temperature dependence \(T^c\). They assumed boost-invariant Bjorken hydrodynamics with an initialization time \(\tau_0 = 1\) fm and a freeze-out temperature \(T_f \sim 100\) MeV, and compared Glauber participant and binary initial geometries with CGC-like deformed geometries (dcgc1.2). The coupling \(\kappa\) was fixed to reproduce the most central \(R_{AA}\) data at RHIC, then extrapolated to LHC energies to quantify the reduction.

## Key numbers

- The jet-medium coupling at the LHC is approximately 10% smaller than at RHIC.
- For a pQCD radiative energy loss, \(\kappa \propto \alpha^3\); using \(\alpha_{\rm RHIC} \sim 0.3\), the extracted coupling at the LHC is \(\kappa_{\rm LHC} \sim 0.24 - 0.28\).
- For a falling-string scenario, \(\kappa \propto \sqrt{\lambda}\), the reduction of the t'Hooft coupling yields \(\lambda_{\rm LHC}\) up to a factor of four smaller than \(\lambda_{\rm RHIC} = 20\).
- The measured momentum dependence of \(R_{AA}\) at the LHC rules out any model with \(dE/dx \sim E^{a>1/3}\).
- The CGC-like initial conditions were modeled with a deformation factor \(f = 1.2 \pm 0.1\) to reproduce numerical MC-KLN tables.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of jet quenching from RHIC to LHC energies, addressing the overquenching problem observed when models constrained to RHIC data (e.g., Horowitz) are extrapolated to the LHC. The authors compare their generic model with specific prior approaches, including the Shuryak-Liao (SL) model with a temperature-dependent coupling and the CUJET code by Buzzatti and Gyulassy based on the Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev (GLV) approach. The results enable future work to constrain the energy-loss mechanism and the running of the coupling in the strongly-coupled Quark-Gluon Plasma, though none of the models reproduce the plateau-like structure of \(R_{AA}\) for \(p_T > 100\) GeV indicated by CMS and ALICE data.
