# Aspects of Neutrino Interactions (Scattering at small $Q^2$- Region)

> The article begins with a description of chiral symmetry and its application to neutrino induced reactions. For small $Q^2$ (forward direction) the process is dominated by the amplitute with helicity zero where the pion pole disappears when multiplied with the polarization vector. The remaining part of the amplitude is determined by PCAC. For $E_ν> 2$ GeV the computed cross sections are in good agreement with data. In coherent pion production we expect equal yields for neutrinos and antineutrinos a relation which for $E_ν> 2$ GeV is fulfilled. We discuss specific features of the data and suggest methods for improving them by presenting new estimates for the incoherent background.

## Key claim

The PCAC-based approach for neutrino-induced coherent pion production, which predicts equal yields for neutrinos and antineutrinos for neutrino energies above 2 GeV, is validated by experimental data; however, the agreement requires careful treatment of the lower kinematic limit on the momentum transfer variable |t| to avoid overestimating the cross section by nearly a factor of two.

## Method

The authors apply chiral symmetry and the partially conserved axial current (PCAC) hypothesis to compute cross sections for coherent pion production on nuclei and Delta resonance production at small four-momentum transfer squared Q^2. They expand the leptonic current in polarization vectors, isolate the helicity-zero and scalar contributions, and relate the hadronic amplitude to elastic pion-nucleus scattering data via the pion-pole cancellation. The coherent cross section is integrated over the kinematic boundaries, using a parametrization of elastic pion-Carbon scattering data with an exponential form dσ/dt = a exp[-b|t|], and the incoherent background from Delta resonance production is computed separately.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay coupling constant f_π = 0.093 GeV.
- For the elastic pion-Carbon cross section parametrization at energies above 1.046 GeV, the constant values used are a = 3.53 barn/GeV^2 and b = 53.49 1/GeV^2.
- The Delta resonance cross section maximum σ_max = 199 × 10^{-27} cm^2.
- The coherent signal peaks at pion angles θ_π ≈ 10°–15° and vanishes for θ_π ≈ 30°, while the incoherent background from Delta production peaks at θ_π ≈ 40° for a 12C target with E_π = 0.4 GeV.
- For neutrino energies E_ν > 2 GeV, the computed cross sections agree well with data; for E_ν < 4 GeV, curves with Q^2_max below 0.5 GeV^2 are favored, while for E_ν > 4 GeV, Q^2_max closer to 1.0 GeV^2 is needed.

## Context

This work extends the PCAC-based approach to coherent pion production developed by Adler (1964), Rein and Sehgal (1983), and Kartavtsev, Gounaris, and Paschos (2006), and uses the cross section calculations from Paschos and Schalla (2009) and Berger and Sehgal (2009) as reference curves. The paper compares these theoretical predictions with experimental data from K2K (Hasegawa et al., 2005), SciBooNE (Hiraide et al., 2008), SKAT (Grabosch et al., 1986), MINERvA (Higuera et al., 2014), and ArgoNeuT (Acciari et al., 2014). The results enable improved background subtraction methods for future coherent scattering measurements by providing new estimates of the incoherent Delta resonance contribution and suggesting kinematic cuts (pion angle θ_π < 15°) to isolate the coherent signal.
