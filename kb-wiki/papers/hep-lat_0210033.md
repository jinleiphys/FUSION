# Perturbative Wilson loops with massive sea quarks on the lattice

> We present O(g^4) calculations of both planar and non-planar Wilson loops for various actions in the presence of sea quarks. In particular, the plaquette, the static potential and the static self energy are calculated to this order for massive Wilson, Sheikholeslami-Wohlert and Kogut-Susskind fermions, including the mass and n_f dependence. The results can be used to obtain alpha_{MS} and m_b(m_b) from lattice simulations. We compare our perturbative calculations to simulation data of the static potential and report excellent qualitative agreement with boosted perturbation theory predictions for distances r<1/GeV. We are also able to resolve differences in the running of the coupling between n_f=2 and n_f=0 static potentials. We compute perturbative estimates of the ``beta-shifts'' of QCD with sea quarks, relative to the quenched theory, which we find to agree within 10 % with non-perturbative simulations. This is done by matching the respective static potentials at large distances. The prospects of determining the QCD running coupling from low energy hadron phenomenology in the near future are assessed. We obtain the result $Λ^{(2)}_{\bar{MS}}r_0=0.69(15)$ for the two flavour QCD Lambda-parameter from presently available lattice data where $r_0^{-1}\approx 400$ MeV and estimate $α_{\bar{MS}}^{(5)}(m_Z)=0.1133(59)$.

## Key claim

The paper presents O(g⁴) perturbative calculations of Wilson loops, the static potential, and the static self energy for lattice QCD with massive sea quarks, finding that boosted perturbation theory predictions agree excellently with simulation data for distances r < 1/GeV, and that perturbative estimates of the "beta-shifts" for QCD with sea quarks agree within 10% with non-perturbative simulations.

## Method

The authors compute the O(g⁴) expansion of planar and non-planar Wilson loops for SU(N) lattice gauge theory using the Wilson gauge action combined with massive Wilson, Sheikholeslami-Wohlert (SW), and Kogut-Susskind (KS) fermionic actions. They evaluate the tree-level (O(g²)) and one-loop (O(g⁴)) contributions, including vacuum polarization insertions, spider diagrams, and non-Abelian double gluon exchange, by generating phase factors for arbitrary curves and performing numerical momentum integrations using the VEGAS algorithm. The static potential is obtained by taking the T→∞ limit of rectangular Wilson loops, and the static self energy is extracted from the R→∞ limit of the potential.

## Context

This work extends the O(g⁴) calculations of Wilson loops by Weisz, Wohlert and Wetzel (1984) and Curci, Paffuti and Tripiccione (1984) from pure gauge theory to include massive sea quarks with Wilson, SW, and KS actions, and presents the first calculation of non-planar Wilson loops. The results enable the determination of α_MS and the bottom quark mass m_b(m_b) from non-perturbative lattice simulations, and allow comparison with simulation data from the CP-PACS collaboration and others to test boosted perturbation theory predictions and resolve differences in the running coupling between n_f=2 and n_f=0 theories.
