# Quark correlations and gluon propagators in elastic vector meson production

> We study the behavior of the differential cross section for vector meson photoproduction at large momentum transfer in the two-gluon exchange model. We focus on the treatment of two-quark correlation function in the proton and on gluon propagators with a dynamically generated mass. We find that only the large t region is sensitive to the particular details of these inputs.

# Quark correlations and gluon propagators in elastic vector meson production

## Key claim
The differential cross section for vector meson photoproduction at large momentum transfer is sensitive to the specific treatment of two-quark correlations in the proton and the choice of gluon propagator, but only in the large t region; at low and moderate t, the isoscalar Dirac form factor dominates and the details of these inputs are not important.

## Method
The authors computed the differential cross section for vector meson photoproduction using a two-gluon exchange model, comparing two treatments of the two-quark correlation function: an approximation using the isoscalar Dirac form factor versus an explicit calculation using a simplified nucleon wave function with a fitted parameter a_N = 1.25 GeV^{-1}. They also compared two gluon propagator choices: a Gaussian form with λ_0^2 = 2.7 GeV^2 and a dynamically massive propagator from Cornwall (1982) with m(0) = 396 MeV, both normalized to the pomeron exchange amplitude at t=0.

## Key numbers
- The free parameter a_N in the nucleon wave function was fitted to a_N = 1.25 GeV^{-1}, giving an average transverse momentum ⟨k_i^2⟩^{1/2} = 0.325 GeV.
- The Gaussian gluon propagator parameter λ_0^2 = 2.7 GeV^2 was fixed to reproduce the total cross section for ρ electroproduction.
- The dynamically generated gluon mass at zero momentum was m(0) = 396 MeV for Λ = 300 MeV, within the range of Cornwall's estimates of m(0) = 500 ± 200 MeV.
- The two-gluon exchange model previously accounted for ρ photoproduction data up to -t ≲ 6 GeV^2 and φ photoproduction up to -t ≲ 3 GeV^2.

## Context
This work extends the two-gluon exchange model calculations of Laget (2000) and Anciant (2000), which successfully described existing ρ and φ photoproduction data, to test the stability of predictions against changes in model inputs. The authors find that the explicit calculation of the two-quark correlation function using a nucleon wave function from Bolz (1996) and the use of a massive gluon propagator from Cornwall (1982) produce significant differences only at large momentum transfer. Preliminary data from the CLAS Collaboration at JLab for φ photoproduction at E_γ = 4.5 GeV support the depletion obtained with the massive gluon propagator and explicit G_2 calculation, enabling more precise tests of non-perturbative QCD dynamics in the large t region.
