# Semi-microscopic folding model for the description of two-body halo nuclei

> One-neutron halo nuclei, composed by a weakly-bound particle coupled to a core nucleus, are studied within a particle-plus-core model. A semi-microscopic method to generate the two-body Hamiltonian of such a system, including core excitation, is proposed. The method consists in generating the spin-independent part of the valence-core interaction using a single-folding procedure, convoluting a realistic nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction with the core transition densities. The latter are calculated with the Antisymetrized Molecular Dynamics (AMD) method. The prescription is applied to the well known halo nucleus, $^{11}$Be, as a test case. The results show an important predictive power that opens a door to the understanding of other lesser known halo nuclei. In order to show the potential usefulness of the method, it is applied to analyze the structure of $^{19}$C.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a semi-microscopic folding method to generate the valence-core interaction for two-body halo nuclei, using microscopic transition densities from AMD calculations rather than phenomenological potentials, and demonstrates that this approach has predictive power for lesser-known halo nuclei without requiring prior knowledge of the composite system's properties.

## Method

The authors construct a particle-plus-core Hamiltonian for one-neutron halo nuclei, where the spin-independent part of the valence-core interaction is generated by single-folding a realistic nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction (the JLM interaction) with core transition densities calculated from the Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (AMD) method. The Hamiltonian is diagonalized using a Transformed Harmonic Oscillator (THO) pseudo-state basis, and the method is applied to ¹¹Be as a test case and then to ¹⁹C to demonstrate predictive capability.

## Key numbers

- For ¹¹Be, the method reproduces the energies of the bound states and low-lying resonances 3/2⁺₁, 5/2⁺₁, and 3/2⁻₁, which were previously described by the particle-rotor model of Nunes et al. (1996) (model Be12-b).
- The JLM interaction parameters have typical values between 0.8 and 1.2 for λ_v (normalization factor) and between 1.2 and 1.4 for t_v (effective range of the Gaussian form factor).
- The AMD calculation for ¹⁰Be reproduces E2 transition probabilities between different energy levels with substantial improvement over shell model calculations.

## Context

This work extends the particle-rotor model (PRM) of Nunes et al. (1996) and particle-vibrator model (PVM) of Vincke and Malfliet (1995) and Gori et al. (2004) by replacing phenomenological potentials with a semi-microscopic folding approach. The method enables the study of halo nuclei whose cores are not well described by simple rotor or vibrator models, and the AMD transition densities used here had previously been shown to reproduce p+¹⁰Be inelastic cross sections at intermediate energies within the DWBA framework by Takashina et al. (2008).
