# Low-energy dipole excitations towards the proton drip-line: doubly magic 48Ni

> The properties of the low-energy dipole response are investigated for the proton-rich doubly magic nucleus $^{48}$Ni, in a comparative study of two microscopic models: fully self-consistent Relativistic Random-Phase Approximation(RRPA) based on the novel density-dependent meson-exchange interactions, and Continuum Random-Phase Approximation(CRPA) using Skyrme-type interactions with the continuum properly included. Both models predict the existence of the low-energy soft mode, i.e. the proton pygmy dipole resonance (PDR), for which the transition densities and RPA amplitudes indicate the dynamics of loosely bound protons vibrating against the rest of the nucleons. The CRPA analysis indicates that the escape width for the proton PDR is rather large, as a result of the coupling to the continuum.

## Key claim

Both the fully self-consistent Relativistic Random-Phase Approximation (RRPA) and the Continuum Random-Phase Approximation (CRPA) predict a low-energy proton pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) in the proton-rich doubly magic nucleus 48Ni, but the CRPA analysis shows that the escape width for this mode is rather large due to coupling to the continuum, and the two models agree only fairly well on the global properties, with the best agreement found between the relativistic DD-ME1 interaction and the Skyrme MSk7 interaction despite their quite different properties.

## Method

The authors performed a comparative study using two microscopic models: fully self-consistent Relativistic Random-Phase Approximation (RRPA) based on density-dependent meson-exchange interactions (DD-ME1 and DD-ME2), and Continuum Random-Phase Approximation (CRPA) using Skyrme-type interactions (BSk8, BSk2, MSk7, SkM*) with the particle continuum fully taken into account. Both models were applied to the doubly magic nucleus 48Ni and the stable isotope 56Ni for comparison, with transition densities and RPA amplitudes analyzed to characterize the nature of the low-energy dipole states.

## Key numbers

- The low-lying proton PDR peak in 48Ni appears at 7.72 MeV in the RRPA model with DD-ME1 interaction, while the IVGDR peak is at 18.71 MeV
- The centroid energies of the proton PDR for 48Ni are around 8 MeV across both models and various interactions
- The low-lying strength exhausts from approximately 0.9% (BSk8) to approximately 1.6% (DD-ME1, MSk7) of the classical Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn sum rule (TRK = 14.9 * (NZ/A) e^2 fm^2 MeV)
- The particle threshold energy for 48Ni is 3.3 MeV (BSk8), 4.2 MeV (SkM*), 3.0 MeV (BSk2), and 3.0 MeV (MSk7)
- The energy of the least bound proton 1f7/2 state in 48Ni is 0.11 MeV (RRPA with DD-ME1), -0.88 MeV (SkM*), -0.03 MeV (BSk8), 0.26 MeV (BSk2), and 0.35 MeV (MSk7)
- The width of the PDR from CRPA is approximately 1.4 MeV, while the RRPA fragmentation width is 0.77 MeV

## Context

This work extends the first microscopic theoretical prediction of the proton pygmy dipole resonance in the framework of RQRPA based on the Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model by Paar (2005), and provides a comparative study between relativistic and nonrelativistic approaches to ensure the proton PDR is inherent to different models. The study is motivated by previous experimental evidence for low-lying dipole excitations in neutron-rich nuclei from electromagnetic excitations in heavy-ion collisions by Leistenschneider (2001) and nuclear resonance fluorescence experiments on Sn, Ba, Pb, and Ca isotopes by various groups (1998-2004), and the results are relevant for describing the rapid proton capture process of nucleosynthesis and for future experimental studies towards the proton drip-line.
