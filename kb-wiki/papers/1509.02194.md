# Towards an improved understanding of $η\to γ^* γ^*$

> We argue that high-quality data on the reaction $e^+e^-\to π^+π^-η$ will allow one to determine the doubly-virtual form factor $η\to γ^*γ^*$ in a model-independent way with controlled accuracy. This is an important step towards a reliable evaluation of the hadronic light-by-light scattering contribution to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon. When analyzing the existing data for $e^+e^-\toπ^+π^-η$ for total energies squared $k^2>1\text{GeV}^2$, we demonstrate that the effect of the $a_2$ meson provides a natural breaking mechanism for the commonly employed factorization ansatz in the doubly-virtual form factor $F_{ηγ^*γ^*}(q^2,k^2)$. However, better data are needed to draw firm conclusions.

## Key claim

High-quality data on the reaction \(e^+e^-\to \pi^+\pi^-\eta\) will allow a model-independent determination of the doubly-virtual form factor \(\eta\to \gamma^*\gamma^*\) with controlled accuracy, but the existing data are insufficient to draw firm conclusions because the integration over the initial energy \(k^2\) in current measurements limits the extraction accuracy.

## Method

The authors generalize a dispersive representation for the \(\eta\to\pi^+\pi^-\gamma\) amplitude to the doubly-virtual case \(\eta\to\pi^+\pi^-\gamma^*\), using a dispersion relation for the isovector part of the \(\eta\) transition form factor dominated by the two-pion intermediate state. They incorporate the \(a_2(1320)\) meson exchange as a left-hand-cut contribution and parameterize the high-energy behavior of \(\tilde{F}_{\eta\gamma\gamma^*}(k^2)\) for \(k^2>1\ \text{GeV}^2\) using a sum of Breit-Wigner functions for the \(\rho(770)\), \(\rho(1450)\), and \(\rho(1700)\) resonances, fitting to total cross section and \(\pi\pi\) spectrum data from BaBar, SND, and CMD-3.

## Key numbers

- The slope parameter \(\alpha_\Omega\) for the linear polynomial approximation of \(P(t,0)\) is \(1.52(6)\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\) (statistical uncertainty only), extracted from \(\eta\to\pi^+\pi^-\gamma\) data.
- With the \(a_2\) contribution included, the slope parameter shifts to \(\alpha_\Omega[a_2] = 1.42(6)\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\).
- The \(a_2\) left-hand-cut contribution is approximated by a quadratic polynomial with coefficients \(\alpha_{a_2}=0.28\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\) and \(\beta_{a_2}=-0.66\ \text{GeV}^{-4}\), accurate to better than 1% in the range \(4M_\pi^2 \leq t \leq 1\ \text{GeV}^2\).
- The cutoff parameter \(s_c\) for the high-energy continuation is varied among \(3\), \(4\), and \(5\ \text{GeV}^2\) to test independence of the choice.

## Context

This work extends the dispersive approach of Hanhart (2013) for the singly-virtual \(\eta\) transition form factor to the doubly-virtual case, analogous to how Hoferichter (2014, 2018) treated the \(\pi^0\) transition form factor via \(e^+e^-\to 3\pi\). It enables a test of the factorization ansatz \(\bar F_{\eta\gamma^*\gamma^*}(q^2,k^2)=\bar F_{\eta\gamma^*\gamma}(q^2)\bar F_{\eta\gamma\gamma^*}(k^2)\) in the kinematic regime \(q^2\ll 1\ \text{GeV}^2\) and \(k^2>1\ \text{GeV}^2\), which is important for reducing the theoretical uncertainty in the hadronic light-by-light scattering contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment.
