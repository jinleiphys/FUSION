# Can one measure timelike Compton scattering at LHC ?

> Exclusive photoproduction of dileptons, gamma N->e+e-N, is and will be measured in ultraperipheral collisions at hadron colliders, such as the Tevatron, RHIC and the LHC . We demonstrate that the timelike deeply virtual Compton scattering (TCS) mechanism gamma q -> e+e- q where the lepton pair comes from the subprocess gamma q -> gamma* q dominates in some accessible kinematical regions, thus opening a new way to study generalized parton distributions (GPD) in the nucleon. High energy kinematics enables to probe parton distributions at small skewedness. This subprocess interferes at the amplitude level with the pure QED subprocess gamma gamma* -> e+e- where the virtual photon is radiated from the nucleon.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that timelike Compton scattering (TCS) can be measured in ultraperipheral collisions at the LHC, provided one selects kinematics where the TCS amplitude and the Bethe-Heitler amplitude are of comparable magnitude, and uses the interference term (odd under lepton charge exchange) to extract the TCS signal. The claim is conditional on the choice of kinematic cuts (e.g., lepton polar angle between π/4 and 3π/4) and on using a specific angular projection to isolate the interference term.

## Method

The authors calculate the cross sections for the Bethe-Heitler (BH) process, the timelike Compton scattering (TCS) process, and their interference in the reaction γ p → ℓ⁺ℓ⁻ p, using leading-twist factorization and leading-order in α_S. The TCS amplitude is expressed in terms of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) via handbag diagrams, with a double-distribution ansatz for the GPDs and NLO GRVGJR 2008 parton distributions as input. The BH amplitude is computed from QED using nucleon form factors. The cross sections are then folded with the equivalent photon flux for ultraperipheral pp collisions at √s_pp = 14 TeV to estimate event rates at the LHC.

## Key numbers

- BH cross section integrated over θ ∈ [π/4, 3π/4], φ ∈ [0, 2π], Q'² ∈ [4.5, 5.5] GeV², |t| ∈ [0.05, 0.25] GeV², as a function of s, is constant at large s and equals 28.4 pb.
- The pure Bethe-Heitler contribution to σ_pp, integrated over θ = [π/4, 3π/4], φ = [0, 2π], t = [-0.05, -0.25] GeV², Q'² = [4.5, 5.5] GeV², and photon energies k = [20, 900] GeV, gives σ_pp^BH = 2.9 pb.
- The Compton contribution (with NLO GRVGJR2008 PDFs and μ_F² = 5 GeV²) gives σ_pp^TCS = 1.9 pb.
- The estimated event rate is of order 10⁵ events/year at the LHC with nominal luminosity (10³⁴ cm⁻² s⁻¹).

## Context

This work extends the study of deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) to the timelike regime, where the final-state photon is replaced by a lepton pair with large timelike virtuality. It builds on the theoretical framework for TCS introduced by the same authors (Pire, Szymanowski, Wagner, 2008) and on the use of ultraperipheral collisions at hadron colliders as photon sources, as emphasized by earlier work (e.g., UPC reviews and LHC studies). The paper enables the extraction of GPDs at small skewedness (ξ), complementing measurements at large ξ from lower-energy electron accelerators such as JLab, and provides a test of QCD corrections via the crossing from spacelike to timelike probes, analogous to the Drell-Yan case.
