# Energy Correlators in Semi-Inclusive Electron-Positron Annihilation

> We investigate energy correlators in semi-inclusive electron-positron annihilation as precision probes of parton hadronization dynamics. Using soft-collinear effective theory, we analyze the correlation patterns between the examined hadron and the rest of QCD radiations in both large-angle and small-angle limits, which establishes a direct correspondence with transverse-momentum-dependent fragmentation functions and fragmentation energy correlators. The two complementary regimes encode the transition from perturbative parton branching to nonperturbative confinement, enabling a unified description of hadron formation across all kinematic regimes. Using renormalization group evolution, we obtain joint N${}^{3}$LL/NNLL quantitative predictions for energy correlations both in the sudakov and jet fragmentation region. Our results demonstrate that semi-inclusive energy correlators provide direct, theoretically controlled access to QCD dynamics underlying hadronization, opening new avenues for precision studies at future lepton colliders as well as through reanalyses of archival LEP data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that semi-inclusive energy correlators in electron-positron annihilation provide a unified, theoretically controlled description of hadronization across all kinematic regimes, but this claim is conditioned on the validity of the factorization formulas derived using soft-collinear effective theory in the back-to-back (large-angle) and collinear (small-angle) limits, with the explicit hedge that the predictions are joint N³LL/NNLL quantitative results that depend on renormalization group evolution and matching onto conventional fragmentation functions.

## Method

The authors analyze semi-inclusive energy correlators in electron-positron annihilation to a single identified pion, studying the polar angle correlation between the detected hadron and the energy flow operator in both the back-to-back limit (χ → π) and the collinear limit (χ → 0). In the back-to-back limit, they derive a factorization formula in terms of transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) fragmentation functions and an EEC TMD jet function using soft-collinear effective theory, while in the collinear limit they derive a factorization in terms of fragmentation energy correlators (FECs) that obey a modified DGLAP evolution equation. They obtain perturbative matching coefficients up to NNLO for the FECs onto conventional collinear fragmentation functions and perform renormalization group evolution to produce joint N³LL/NNLL predictions.

## Key numbers

- The center-of-mass energy of the virtual photon is Q, with the pion energy given by E_h = x Q/2, where x is the momentum fraction related to the Bjorken variable by x = 1/x_B
- The relation between the angular variable χ and transverse momentum q_T in the back-to-back limit is sin χ = q_T / (Q/2)
- The Collins-Soper kernel K(b_⊥, μ) satisfies d/d ln μ K(b_⊥, μ) = 2 γ^q_cusp(α_s(μ)), with the solution K(b_⊥, μ) = -2 A^q_cusp(μ, μ_b) + γ^q_R(μ_b) where μ_b = b_0/b_T and b_0 = 2e^{-γ_E}
- The perturbative accuracy for the CS kernel has reached up to N⁴LL
- The NNLL predictions for the collinear limit are presented at a center-of-mass energy corresponding to the Z boson threshold (m_Z), with scale variations around (μ_0, μ_f) ∼ (Q/2 sin χ, Q)
- The inclusive EEC in the collinear limit is obtained by taking the Mellin moment N = 3 in the factorization formula and summing over all hadron species

## Context

This work extends the study of energy-energy correlators first introduced by Basham et al. (1978-1979) as tests of QCD dynamics, and builds on recent developments in fragmentation energy correlators by Liu et al. (2022, 2024) and the application of soft-collinear effective theory to jet substructure by Moult et al. (2018). The semi-inclusive energy correlator formalism establishes a direct correspondence with TMD fragmentation functions in the back-to-back limit, consistent with the TMD factorization framework of Collins (2011), and with fragmentation energy correlators in the collinear limit. The results enable precision studies of hadronization dynamics at future lepton colliders and through reanalyses of archival LEP data, and the matching coefficients derived up to NNLO provide boundary conditions for NNLL resummation that can be tested against measurements from ALICE (2024) and STAR (2025).
