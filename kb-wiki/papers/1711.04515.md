# Heavy quarkonium suppression in a fireball

> We perform a comprehensive study of the time evolution of heavy-quarkonium states in an expanding hot QCD medium by implementing effective field theory techniques in the framework of open quantum systems. The formalism incorporates quarkonium production and its subsequent evolution in the fireball including quarkonium dissociation and recombination. We consider a fireball with a local temperature that is much smaller than the inverse size of the quarkonium and much larger than its binding energy. The calculation is performed at an accuracy that is leading-order in the heavy-quark density expansion and next-to-leading order in the multipole expansion. Within this accuracy, for a smooth variation of the temperature and large times, the evolution equation can be written as a Lindblad equation. We solve the Lindblad equation numerically both for a weakly-coupled quark-gluon plasma and a strongly-coupled medium. As an application, we compute the nuclear modification factor for the $Υ(1S)$ and $Υ(2S)$ states. We also consider the case of static quarks, which can be solved analytically. Our study fulfils three essential conditions: it conserves the total number of heavy quarks, it accounts for the non-Abelian nature of QCD and it avoids classical approximations.

## Key claim
The paper develops a formalism for heavy-quarkonium evolution in an expanding QCD fireball that conserves total heavy-quark number, accounts for non-Abelian QCD, and avoids classical approximations, but the calculation is performed at leading-order in the heavy-quark density expansion and next-to-leading order in the multipole expansion, with the evolution equation taking Lindblad form only under the conditions of smooth temperature variation and large times.

## Method
The authors use potential non-relativistic QCD (pNRQCD) within an open quantum systems framework to derive coupled evolution equations for the color-singlet and color-octet density matrices of heavy quark-antiquark pairs. They solve these equations numerically for both a weakly-coupled quark-gluon plasma (with hierarchy 1/a₀ ≫ T ≫ E ≫ m_D) and a strongly-coupled medium (with hierarchy 1/a₀ ≫ T ∼ m_D ≫ E), modeling the fireball expansion using Bjorken's evolution with temperature T = T₀(t₀/t)^(v_s²) and v_s² = 1/3.

## Key numbers
- Initial temperature T₀ = 475 MeV and initial time t₀ = 0.6 fm for central LHC collisions, taken from Alberico (2013)
- For 0-20% centrality: ⟨b⟩ = 4.76 fm, T₀ = 466 MeV
- For 20-90% centrality: ⟨b⟩ = 11.6 fm, T₀ = 360 MeV
- The inverse Bohr radius 1/a₀ of Υ(1S) in vacuum is about 1.3 GeV
- The crossover temperature to quark-gluon plasma T_c is expected at about 150 MeV (Borsanyi 2010, Bazavov 2014, Bazavov 2016)
- The nucleon scattering cross-section is taken as σ = 64 ± 5 mb = 164 ± 13 GeV⁻² (Chatrchyan 2011)
- Woods-Saxon parameters for lead: c = 6.62 ± 0.06 fm, ξ = 0.546 ± 0.01 fm, A = 207

## Context
This work extends the pNRQCD framework previously developed by Brambilla, Escobedo, Soto, and Vairo (2016) for quarkonium in thermal equilibrium to the non-equilibrium case of an expanding fireball, incorporating both dissociation and recombination. The formalism enables computation of the nuclear modification factor R_AA for Υ(1S) and Υ(2S) states in heavy-ion collisions, with the evolution equations solved from initial time t₀ = 0.6 fm until the fireball cools to about 250 MeV, leaving the evolution from 250 MeV to freeze-out as a modest remaining uncertainty.
