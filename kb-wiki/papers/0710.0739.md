# Transport and spectral functions in high-temperature QCD

> The current status of transport coefficients in relativistic field theories at high temperature is reviewed. I contrast weak coupling results obtained using kinetic theory/diagrammatic techniques with strong coupling results obtained using gauge/gravity duality, and describe the recent developments in extracting transport coefficients and spectral functions from lattice QCD simulations. The fate of quarkonium at high temperature as seen from the lattice is briefly mentioned as well.

# Transport and spectral functions in high-temperature QCD

## Key claim

Transport coefficients in QCD above the deconfinement transition are accessible via lattice simulations only when the coupling is strong enough that the transport peak becomes broad and smooth; the first lattice results support the picture of a strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma in the range 1 ≲ T/T_c ≲ 2.5, but all calculations to date are performed in quenched QCD and carry significant systematic uncertainties from the analytic continuation.

## Method

The paper reviews three complementary approaches: weak-coupling kinetic theory and diagram summation (including ladder resummations), strong-coupling gauge/gravity duality (AdS/CFT correspondence) applied to theories such as N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills, and lattice QCD simulations where Euclidean correlators are analytically continued to real time using the Maximum Entropy Method (MEM). A key technical development is the identification and resolution of a numerical instability in MEM at small frequencies, achieved by redefining the kernel and spectral function to remove the 1/ω divergence, which then allows extraction of transport coefficients from the zero-frequency intercept.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio at strong coupling via gauge/gravity duality: η/s = 1/(4π), universal for thermal gauge theories with a gravity dual.
- Shear viscosity in N=4 SYM at strong coupling: η = (π/8) N_c² T³, with first subleading correction η = (π/8) N_c² T³ (1 + 75ζ(3)/(4λ^{3/2}) + ...).
- Electrical conductivity from lattice QCD (quenched, single flavour): σ/T = 0.4 ± 0.1 at T/T_c ≈ 1.5 and 2.25, with no significant temperature dependence.
- Upper bound on shear viscosity from lattice SU(3) gauge theory: η/s ≲ 1 at T/T_c = 1.24 and 1.65.
- R-charge diffusion constant in N=4 SYM at strong coupling: D = 1/(2πT).
- Bulk viscosity at weak coupling: ζ ≈ 15η (1/3 - v_s²)², where for light quarks v_s² - 1/3 ∼ β(g²) ∼ g⁴.

## Context

This plenary review (Aarts, 2007) contrasts weak-coupling results from Arnold, Moore, and collaborators (2000-2006) with strong-coupling results from Policastro, Son, Starinets, Kovtun, and others (2001-2004) using gauge/gravity duality, and describes the first lattice extractions of transport coefficients by Gupta (2003), Aarts et al. (2007) for electrical conductivity, and Meyer (2007) for shear viscosity. The work enables first-principles nonperturbative determination of transport in QCD above T_c, extending earlier Ansatz-based attempts by Karsch and Wyld (1986) and Nakamura and Sakai (2004), and provides a foundation for comparing lattice results with heavy-ion phenomenology at RHIC.
