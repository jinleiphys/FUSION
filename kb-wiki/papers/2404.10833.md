# Vector meson production in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions

> We review model calculations of exclusive vector meson production in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions. We highlight differences and similarities between different dipole models and leading twist shadowing calculations. Recent color glass condensate calculations are presented with focus on effects from nuclear structure and azimuthal anisotropies driven by interference effects.

# Vector meson production in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions

## Key claim
The paper reviews that while many models of exclusive vector meson production in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions (UPCs) can describe some features of experimental data, **none of the models achieves a good simultaneous description of the experimental data at forward and mid-rapidities**, and the LHC data for the coherent suppression factor shows a steeper energy dependence than the model calculations.

## Method
The authors compare leading-order pQCD collinear factorization calculations (including leading twist shadowing, EPS09 nuclear PDFs, and STARLIGHT with Glauber rescattering) against multiple dipole model frameworks (IIM BG, BGK-I, IPSat, GG-HS, and b-BK) and a Color Glass Condensate (CGC) calculation using JIMWLK evolution with nucleon shape fluctuations. The cross sections for coherent diffractive J/psi production in Pb+Pb UPCs at the LHC are computed and compared to ALICE data, while nuclear suppression factors and azimuthal anisotropies from interference effects are evaluated using the CGC framework.

## Key numbers
- The fine-structure constant alpha_em = 1/137 is used in the photon flux calculation
- For the IPSat model, mu_0^2 = 1.17 GeV^2 is the result of a fit to HERA data
- The J/psi radial wave function at the origin squared is |phi(0)|^2 = 0.0447 GeV^3
- The charm quark charge is e_q = 2/3 in units of the elementary charge
- For the azimuthal anisotropy analysis, corrections from soft photon radiation predominantly affect results at q_perp > 0.12 GeV/c
- The incoherent cross section for gamma+U collisions is shown at x = 1.7 x 10^{-3} with default quadrupole deformation beta_2 = 0.28
- The LHC Pb+Pb UPC results are presented at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV

## Context
This review extends comparisons between dipole models and leading twist shadowing calculations previously studied by Guzey (2013) and Frankfurt (2003), and presents recent CGC calculations by Mantysaari (2022, 2023) that include JIMWLK evolution. The work enables future comparisons with next-to-leading order calculations in both the pQCD collinear factorization framework (Eskola, 2022) and the dipole picture (Mantysaari, 2021, 2022), and demonstrates that UPCs can probe nuclear structure effects including deformation and subnucleonic fluctuations before the Electron Ion Collider begins operations.
