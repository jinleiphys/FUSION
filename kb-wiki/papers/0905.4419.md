# Isospin equilibration in multi-fragmentation processes and dynamical correlations

> The asymptotic time derivative of the total dipole signal is proposed as an useful observable to study Isospin equilibration phenomenon in multi-fragmentation processes. The study proceeds through the investigation of the $^{40}Cl+^{28}Si$ system at 40 MeV/nucleon by means of semiclassical microscopic many-body calculations based on the CoMD-II model. In particular, the study has been developed to describe charge/mass equilibration processes involving the gas and liquid "phases" of the total system formed during the early stage of a collision. Through the investigation of dynamical many-body correlations, it is also shown how the proposed observable is rather sensitive to different parameterizations of the isospin dependent interaction.

## Key claim

The asymptotic time derivative of the total dipole signal, \(\overline{\overrightarrow{V}}\), is proposed as an observable for studying isospin equilibration in multi-fragmentation processes, with the caveat that its sensitivity to different parameterizations of the isospin-dependent interaction depends critically on including many-body correlations beyond the mean-field approximation.

## Method

The study used semiclassical microscopic many-body calculations with the Constrained Molecular Dynamics-II (CoMD-II) model for the \(^{40}\text{Cl}+^{28}\text{Si}\) system at 40 MeV/nucleon. The ensemble average of the time derivative of the total dipole signal \(\overline{\overrightarrow{V}}\) was computed as a function of time and impact parameter, and decomposed into contributions from the gas phase (light charged particles), liquid phase (heavy fragments), and their relative motion. Calculations were performed with three different parameterizations of the symmetry potential (Stiff1, Stiff2, and Soft) and compared to the Iso-vectorial Mean Field Approximation (I.M.F.A.) limit where many-body correlations are neglected.

## Key numbers

- For the \(^{40}\text{Cl}+^{28}\text{Si}\) system at 40 MeV/nucleon with impact parameter \(b = 3\) fm and the Stiff2 option, the asymptotic "liquid" contributions were \(\overline{V}^{x}_{L} = -0.120 c\) and \(\overline{V}^{z}_{L} = 0.162 c\), while the total contributions were \(\overline{V}^{x} = 0.044 c\) and \(\overline{V}^{z} = -0.027 c\).
- The maximum impact parameter used was \(b_{max} \simeq 7.5\) fm, defining the reduced impact parameter \(b_{r} = b / b_{max}\).
- For \(b_{r} = 0.4\), the I.M.F.A. approximation produced values of \(|\overline{V}^{z}|\) about four times larger than those obtained with full CoMD-II calculations, independent of the symmetry potential option used.
- The relative change \(r = \Delta R / R\) between the Stiff1 and Stiff2 options showed large sensitivity for \(b_{r} < 0.6\), with reduced sensitivity for \(b_{r} > 0.6\).

## Context

This work extends studies of isospin diffusion in binary reactions, such as those by Tsang et al. (2004) on Tin isotopes at 50 MeV/nucleon using the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck model, to the more complex case of multi-fragmentation processes. The proposed dipole observable generalizes the definition of isospin equilibration to reactions involving gas and liquid phases, and the results demonstrate that many-body correlations, as implemented in the CoMD-II model, are essential for sensitivity to the density dependence of the symmetry potential, in contrast to the I.M.F.A. limit. This enables future experimental studies of the symmetry energy using the asymptotic dipole signal in multi-fragmentation reactions.
