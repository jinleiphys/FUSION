# Power counting in relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory

> We consider a renormalization scheme for relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory which provides a simple and consistent power counting for renormalized diagrams. As an application we discuss the chiral expansion of the nucleon mass.

## Key claim

The paper presents a renormalization scheme for relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) that restores a simple and consistent power counting for renormalized diagrams, specifically by choosing finite parts of counterterms to cancel those loop contributions that violate the power counting.

## Method

The authors apply the extended on-mass-shell (EOMS) renormalization scheme to the nucleon self-energy at order O(q^4). They first render loop diagrams finite using the modified minimal subtraction scheme of ChPT (widetilde-MS) with renormalization parameter mu = m, then expand the w-MS-renormalized diagrams in powers of small quantities to identify terms that violate the power counting. These terms are subtracted by fixing finite counterterms from the most general effective Lagrangian, yielding renormalized diagrams that satisfy the desired chiral order.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon mass expansion at O(q^4) is parameterized as m_N = m + k_1 M^2 + k_2 M^3 + k_3 M^4 ln(M/m) + k_4 M^4 + O(M^5), where M is the pion mass and m is the nucleon mass in the chiral limit.
- The coefficient k_2 = -3 g_A^2 / (32 pi F^2), where g_A is the axial coupling and F is the pion decay constant.
- The coefficient k_3 = (3/(32 pi^2 F^2)) (8 c_1 - c_2 - 4 c_3 - g_A^2 / m), involving low-energy constants c_1, c_2, c_3.
- The subtraction term for the self-energy diagrams is given by Sigma_{r,a+b+c}^{subtr} = (3 g_{A,r}^2)/(32 pi^2 F_r^2) [m M^2 - (p^2 - m^2)^2/(4m)] + (3 c_1^r g_{A,r}^2 M^2)/(8 pi^2 F_r^2) [m(p/ + m) - (3/2)(p^2 - m^2)].

## Context

This work extends the relativistic baryon ChPT framework of Gasser, Sainio, and Svarc (1988), which suffered from higher-loop diagrams contributing to orders as low as O(q^2), a problem previously solved by the heavy-baryon formulation of Jenkins and Manohar (1990). The EOMS scheme provides an alternative to the infrared regularization method of Becher and Leutwyler (1999), with the two approaches yielding identical nonanalytic terms in the nucleon mass but differing in the analytic k_4 coefficient, a difference compensated by different values of renormalized parameters. This scheme enables consistent power counting for higher-order loop calculations and is not restricted to the single-nucleon sector.
