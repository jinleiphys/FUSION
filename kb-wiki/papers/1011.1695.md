# Flavor structure of the baryon-baryon interaction from lattice QCD

> We investigate baryon-baryon (BB) interactions in the 3-flavor full QCD simulations with degenerate quark masses for all flavors. The BB potentials in the orbital S-wave are extracted from the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter wave functions measured on the lattice. We observe strong flavor-spin dependences of the BB potentials at short distances. In particular, a strong repulsive core exists in the flavor-octet and spin-singlet channel(the 8_s representation), while an attractive core appears in the flavor singlet channel (the 1 representation). We discuss the relation of such flavor-spin dependence with the Pauli exclusion principle at the quark level. The possible existence of an H-dibaryon resonance above the Lambda-Lambda threshold is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper extracts all six independent baryon-baryon (BB) potentials in the S-wave from lattice QCD in the flavor SU(3) limit with degenerate quark masses, finding that the flavor-octet spin-singlet (8_s) channel has a very strong repulsive core while the flavor-singlet (1) channel shows attraction at all distances; the authors note that their data are insufficient for a definite conclusion on the H-dibaryon but suggest a possible resonance above the Lambda-Lambda threshold.

## Method

The authors performed 3-flavor full QCD simulations on a 16^3 x 32 lattice using gauge configurations from CP-PACS and JLQCD Collaborations with the Iwasaki gauge action and non-perturbatively O(a)-improved Wilson quark action. They extracted Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave functions from four-point functions at t-t0=10 and derived local potentials via the Schrodinger-type equation V(r) = (1/2μ)(∇²φ(r)/φ(r)) + E, using two sets of degenerate quark masses corresponding to pion masses of 1014 MeV and 835 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Pion masses: 1014(1) MeV and 835(1) MeV for the two hopping parameter sets
- Baryon masses: 2026(3) MeV and 1752(3) MeV for the two sets
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.121(2) fm; lattice size: L = 1.93(3) fm
- Energy contributions: E^(27) ≈ -5 MeV, E^(8_s) ≈ 25 MeV, E^(1) ≈ -30 MeV, E^(10*) ≈ -10 MeV, E^(10) ≈ 0 MeV, E^(8_a) ≈ -15 MeV
- Attractive pocket in V^(27) and V^(10*) appears at around 0.6 fm
- Number of gauge configurations: 700 for the heavier quark mass, 800 for the lighter

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD method previously applied to NN systems by Ishii (2006) and to ΞN and ΛN systems by Nemura (2010), now providing the complete set of six S-wave BB potentials in the SU(3) limit. The results confirm qualitative features predicted by phenomenological quark models from Oka (2000) and Jaffe (1976), particularly the role of quark Pauli blocking in generating repulsive cores. The extracted potentials enable future coupled-channel analyses of hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions relevant to hypernuclei and neutron star physics, and provide a foundation for studying the H-dibaryon with larger lattice volumes and flavor SU(3) breaking.
