# A new method to study lattice QCD at finite temperature and chemical potential

> Due to the sign problem, it is exponentially difficult to study QCD on the lattice at finite chemical potential. We propose a method --an overlap improving multi-parameter reweighting technique-- to alleviate this problem. We apply this method and give the phase diagram of four-flavor QCD obtained on lattices 4^4 and 4\cdot6^3. Our results are based on {\cal{O}}(10^3-10^4) configurations.

## Key claim

The authors propose an overlap-improving multi-parameter reweighting technique to alleviate the sign problem in lattice QCD at finite chemical potential, and demonstrate its superiority over the single-parameter Glasgow method for four-flavor QCD on small lattices (4^4 and 4·6^3), but note that the method does not provide a general solution to the sign problem and is most applicable for locating the critical point at relatively small μ and high T.

## Method

The method combines reweighting in both the gauge coupling β and the chemical potential μ, using configurations generated at Re(μ)=0 (purely imaginary μ) and the transition β value. For each independent gauge configuration, the average plaquette and the ratio of fermion determinants det M(μ')/det M(μ; Re(μ)=0) are calculated, allowing simultaneous reweighting to keep the system on the transition line as monitored by Lee-Yang zeros of the partition function at complex β.

## Key numbers

- 1200 independent V=4·6^3 configurations were collected at Re(μ)=Im(μ)=0 for method comparison
- 13000 independent configurations were used on the V=4^4 lattice at Im(μ)=0
- At μ=0.3 on the 4^4 lattice, the histogram method predicts β_c=4.939(5) for the equal-weight double-peak structure
- Lee-Yang zeros for Re(μ)=0: Re(β_0)=4.988(1) for V=4^4 and 5.040(2) for V=4·6^3
- Lee-Yang zeros for Re(μ)=0.3: Re(β_0)=4.938(2) for V=4^4 and 4.975(3) for V=4·6^3
- The last point on the phase diagram corresponds to μ≈190 MeV in physical units with m_ρ=770 MeV setting the scale
- The quark mass is m_q≈25 MeV in physical units

## Context

This work extends the Glasgow method (Glasgow, 1990s) by adding a second reweighting parameter to overcome the overlap problem that plagued single-parameter reweighting, and is similar in spirit to the technique used for the hot electroweak plasma phase diagram (ewpt). The method enables determination of the QCD phase diagram in the T-μ plane for small volumes and moderate chemical potentials, and is intended to be applied to locate the conjectured critical point in three-flavor QCD that can be explored by heavy-ion experiments.
