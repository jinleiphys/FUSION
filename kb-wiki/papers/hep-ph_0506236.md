# Extraction of radiative decay width for the non-strange partner of Theta^+

> Using the results of the GRAAL collaboration on the ηphotoproduction from the neutron target, we attempt to extract the partial radiative width of the possible new nucleon resonance N^*(1675). The obtained estimates support this resonance to be a very attractive candidate for the non-strange member of the exotic antidecuplet of baryons -- a partner of the Θ^+ pentaquark. Our phenomenological value for the transition magnetic moment μ(n^* n), appears to be in good agreement with predictions of the Chiral Quark Soliton Model.

# Extraction of radiative decay width for the non-strange partner of Theta^+

## Key claim
Using GRAAL collaboration data on eta photoproduction from the neutron target, the authors estimate the partial radiative width of the proposed N*(1675) resonance, finding it consistent with being the non-strange partner of the Theta^+ pentaquark in the exotic antidecuplet. The extracted transition magnetic moment agrees with Chiral Quark Soliton Model predictions, but the estimate is rough and depends on an assumed branching ratio range of 0.05–0.4 for the eta n decay mode.

## Method
The authors fit the differential cross section of gamma n → eta n near invariant mass W = 1675 MeV from GRAAL data using a Gaussian peak on a smooth background, assuming incoherence. They then apply a resonance formula relating the integrated cross section to partial widths, assuming spin 1/2 for the resonance, and extract the radiative width Gamma(gamma n) and transition magnetic moment mu(n* → n) as functions of the unknown eta n branching ratio.

## Key numbers
- Resonance mass M_R = 1675 MeV from the Gaussian fit to GRAAL data
- Peak width parameter Delta W = 30 MeV from the fit
- Center-of-mass relative momentum for initial gamma n state at resonance: k_gamma = 574 MeV
- Estimated true total width Gamma_tot ≈ 10 MeV from Monte Carlo simulation
- Extracted transition magnetic moment: |mu(n* → n)| = (0.13 – 0.37) mu_N, corresponding to Gamma_gamma n = (27 – 216) keV, for assumed eta n branching ratio range 0.05 – 0.4
- ChQSM prediction range for the transition magnetic moment: (0.10 – 0.56) mu_N
- Comparison value: mu(Delta → N) ≈ 3 mu_N

## Context
This work extends the search for members of the exotic antidecuplet of baryons predicted by the Chiral Quark Soliton Model (ChQSM) of Diakonov, Petrov, and Polyakov (1997), following the GRAAL collaboration’s indication of a narrow N*(1675) resonance in eta photoproduction from neutrons (Kuznetsov, 2005). The extracted small transition magnetic moment supports the antidecuplet interpretation and correlates with the predicted small width of the Theta^+ pentaquark. The absence of a corresponding peak in gamma p → eta p data implies the ratio mu(p* p)/mu(n* n) < 1/3, further supporting the antidecuplet nature. Future higher-statistics data and improved background modeling are needed to refine the estimate.
