# The Spin and Orbital Contributions to Magnetic Dipole Transitions

> In previous works we examined they systematics of magnetic dipole transitions in a single j shell. We here extend the study to large space calculations.We consider the nuclei $^{44}$ Ti, $^{46}$Ti and $^{48}$C. Of particular interest is the contributions to B(M1) of the spin and orbital parts of the magnetic dipole operator. Whereas usual scissors mode analyses have as an initial state the J=0+ ground state (of an even-even nucleus) we here start with the lowest J=1+ T=1 state. This enables us to reach many more states e.g. J=2,T=0,1, and 2 and thus getter a better picture of the collectivity of this state.

## Key claim

The paper extends previous single-j-shell studies of magnetic dipole (M1) transitions in the f7/2 shell to full fp-shell calculations for 44Ti, 46Ti, and 48Cr, finding that the B(M1) strength decreases exponentially with energy when starting from the lowest J=1+ T=1 state rather than the usual J=0+ ground state.

## Method

The authors performed large-space shell-model calculations using the GX1A interaction in the full fp model space for 44Ti, 46Ti, and 48Cr, computing B(M1) values from the lowest J=1+ T=1 state to final states with various spins (J=0,1,2) and isospins (T=0,1,2). They decomposed the total B(M1) into spin and orbital contributions to study their relative importance and interference patterns, comparing results with earlier single-j-shell calculations using the MBZE interaction from Harper and Zamick (2015).

## Key numbers

- For 44Ti in the large space, the B(M1) from the lowest J=1+ T=1 state to the ground state (J=0 T=0) is 1.503 (mu_N)^2, with spin contribution 0.6249 and orbit contribution 0.1897
- For 48Cr in the large space, the B(M1) from the lowest J=1+ T=1 state to the ground state is 1.101 (mu_N)^2, with spin contribution 0.2475 and orbit contribution 0.3046
- The strongest transition in 44Ti large space is to a J=2 T=0 state at 6.768 MeV with B(M1)=5.512 (mu_N)^2, reduced from 12.979 in the single-j calculation
- For 44Ti, the B(M1) to a J=0 T=2 state at 8.893 MeV is 2.675 (mu_N)^2, enhanced from 1.955 in the single-j calculation
- For 46Ti in the large space, the B(M1) from the lowest J=1+ T=1 state to the ground state is 0.504 (mu_N)^2
- For 48Cr, the B(M1) to a J=0 T=2 state at 8.571 MeV is 1.142 (mu_N)^2, and to a J=2 T=0 state at 7.206 MeV is 1.014 (mu_N)^2
- The exponential fit for 46Ti J=0 T=2 final states gives B(M1) = 0.2 * 10^(-(E-15)*0.20264) for E greater than 15 MeV

## Context

This work extends the single-j-shell studies of Harper and Zamick (2015) on magnetic dipole transitions in the f7/2 shell to full fp-space calculations, adding 48Cr to the previously studied 44Ti and 46Ti. Unlike standard scissors-mode analyses that start from the J=0+ ground state as in the review by Heyde, von Neumann-Cosel, and Richter (2010), the authors start from the lowest J=1+ T=1 state to access a wider range of final states and study collectivity. The results enable future investigations of how spin and orbital contributions to M1 transitions vary with deformation, and suggest that the spin contribution decreases with increasing deformation when comparing 44Ti to the more deformed 48Cr.
