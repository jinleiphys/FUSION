# The one-pion-exchange potential with contact terms from lattice QCD simulations

> The pion-mass-dependent nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) potentials in term of one-pion exchange and contact terms are obtained from the latest lattice QCD simulations of two-nucleon system, which employ the forms of leading order (LO) $NN$ potential from the chiral effective field theory and thus are named as the LO chiral potential in this work. We extract the coefficients of contact terms and cut-off momenta in these potentials, for the first time, by fitting the phase shifts of $^1S_0$ and $^3S_1$ channels generated by the results of HALQCD collaboration with various pion masses from $468.6$ MeV to $1170.9$ MeV. The low-energy constants in the $^1S_0$ and $^3S_1$ channels become weaker and approach each other for larger pion masses. These LO chiral potentials are applied to symmetric nuclear and pure neutron matter within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock method. At this moment, however, we do not have yet the information of the $P$-wave $NN$ interaction to be provided by the lattice QCD simulations for full description of nuclear matter. Our results will enhance the development of nuclear structure and nuclear matter by controlling the contribution of the pionic effect and illuminate the role of chiral symmetry of the strong interaction in complex system.

## Key claim

The authors extract, for the first time, the low-energy constants (LECs) and cutoff momenta of leading-order (LO) chiral nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials by fitting to phase shifts from HALQCD lattice QCD simulations at pion masses from 468.6 MeV to 1170.9 MeV, but note that the statistical errors of the lattice simulations are not yet satisfactory and that P-wave NN interaction information is still missing for a full description of nuclear matter.

## Method

The authors construct LO chiral NN potentials consisting of one-pion-exchange and contact terms, using the form of chiral effective field theory. They fit the two LECs (C_S and C_T, expressed as partial-wave components in the ^1S_0 and ^3S_1 channels), the cutoff momentum Lambda, and the power n of the exponential regulator to the phase shifts from HALQCD lattice NN potentials below laboratory energy 150.0 MeV, using the Blankenbeclar-Sugar scattering equation. The resulting potentials are then applied to symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) method.

## Key numbers

- Pion masses used: 468.6, 672.3, 836.5, 1015.2, and 1170.9 MeV, with corresponding nucleon masses 1161, 1484, 1749, 2031, and 2274 MeV.
- LECs for the ^1S_0 channel (in units of 10^4 GeV^-2): -0.077 (LO469), -0.027 (LO672), -0.024 (LO837), -0.021 (LO1015), -0.016 (LO1171).
- LECs for the ^3S_1 channel (in units of 10^4 GeV^-2): -0.091 (LO469), -0.029 (LO672), -0.028 (LO837), -0.025 (LO1015), -0.022 (LO1171).
- Cutoff momenta Lambda: 334.37 MeV (LO469), 550.00 MeV (LO672), 569.81 MeV (LO837), 622.93 MeV (LO1015), 664.91 MeV (LO1171).
- At physical pion mass (140 MeV), the LECs are -0.152 (^1S_0) and -0.214 (^3S_1) with Lambda = 322.49 MeV.
- For LO469 potential, the saturation binding energy in symmetric nuclear matter is E/A = -6.80 MeV at rho_B = 0.45 fm^-3.
- Uncertainties in LECs at m_pi = 468.6 MeV: -0.077 +/- 0.012 (^1S_0) and -0.091 +/- 0.010 (^3S_1).

## Context

This work builds a bridge between chiral effective field theory (ChEFT) and lattice QCD for the NN force, extending prior work by Song et al. (2018) on hyperon-hyperon scattering and Barnea et al. (2015) and Contessi et al. (2017) on light nuclei with pionless EFT at m_pi = 805 MeV. The extracted pion-mass-dependent LECs enable studies of how nuclear matter properties change with pion mass, and the results suggest that Wigner symmetry (equality of ^1S_0 and ^3S_1 LECs) is realized at large pion masses, while the physical point requires distinct LECs as seen in the experimental scattering lengths (a_{^1S_0} = -23.740 fm, a_{^3S_1} = 5.419 fm). Future lattice QCD calculations of P-wave interactions are needed to extend this approach to next-to-leading order.
