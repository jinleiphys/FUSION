# Quarkonia dissociation at finite magnetic field in the presence of momentum anisotropy

> In this study, we investigate the potential of heavy quarkonia within a magnetized hot QGP medium having finite momentum anisotropy. The phenomenon of inverse magnetic catalysis is introduced into the system, influencing the magnetic field-modified Debye mass and thereby altering the effective quark masses. Concurrently, the impact of momentum anisotropy in the medium is considered that influence the particle distribution in the medium. The thermal decay width and dissociation temperature of quarkonium states, specifically the 1S and 2S states of charmonium and bottomonium, are computed. Our results reveal that both momentum anisotropy and the inverse magnetic catalysis effects play a significant role in modifying the thermal decay width and dissociation temperature of these heavy quarkonia states.

## Key claim

The paper claims that both momentum anisotropy (parameterized by ξ) and the inverse magnetic catalysis (IMC) effect significantly modify the thermal decay width and dissociation temperature of heavy quarkonia states in a magnetized hot QGP medium, but the dissociation temperatures are only slightly higher for ξ = 0.3 compared to ξ = 0.1, and the effect of anisotropy on the imaginary part of the potential is less pronounced than on the real part.

## Method

The authors start from the Cornell potential (Coulomb plus string terms) and introduce momentum anisotropy by rescaling the isotropic particle distribution function in one direction using the Romatschke-Strickland formalism (parameter ξ). The magnetic field and IMC effects enter through a Debye mass m_D(T,eB) computed from lattice QCD-inspired quark condensates, which modify the effective constituent quark masses and the dispersion relation. The real and imaginary parts of the heavy quark potential are derived from the longitudinal gluon propagator in the static limit, and the thermal decay width is obtained by integrating the imaginary potential with Coulombic wave functions for 1S and 2S states. Dissociation temperatures are determined by the criterion Γ_n = 2 × B.E._n.

## Key numbers

- For ξ = 0.1, the J/ψ (1S charmonium) dissociates at T = 192 MeV for eB = 0, T = 123 MeV for eB = 0.2 GeV², and T = 95 MeV for eB = 0.4 GeV².
- For ξ = 0.1, the Υ (1S bottomonium) dissociates at T = 290 MeV for eB = 0, T = 217 MeV for eB = 0.2 GeV², and T = 207 MeV for eB = 0.4 GeV².
- For ξ = 0.1, the Υ' (2S bottomonium) dissociates at T = 180 MeV for eB = 0, T = 111 MeV for eB = 0.2 GeV², and T = 88 MeV for eB = 0.4 GeV².
- For ξ = 0.3, the J/ψ dissociates at T = 194 MeV for eB = 0, T = 126 MeV for eB = 0.2 GeV², and T = 97 MeV for eB = 0.4 GeV².
- For ξ = 0.3, the Υ dissociates at T = 297 MeV for eB = 0, T = 222 MeV for eB = 0.2 GeV², and T = 213 MeV for eB = 0.4 GeV².
- For ξ = 0.3, the Υ' dissociates at T = 186 MeV for eB = 0, T = 113 MeV for eB = 0.2 GeV², and T = 89 MeV for eB = 0.4 GeV².
- The magnetic field strengths considered are eB = 0, 0.2 GeV², and 0.4 GeV²; the temperatures studied include T = 100 MeV and T = 200 MeV.
- The string tension is fixed at σ = 0.184 GeV², and the Λ_MSbar renormalization scale is 176 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Nilima 2022) on heavy quark potential dissociation in finite magnetic fields by adding local momentum anisotropy to the medium. It incorporates inverse magnetic catalysis effects from lattice QCD calculations (Bali 2012, Bali 2014) through magnetic field-modified Debye masses and constituent quark masses. The results enable future studies of heavy quarkonia dissociation in hydrodynamically expanding viscous QGP, as the authors plan to incorporate viscous corrections. The formalism builds on the Romatschke-Strickland anisotropic distribution approach (Romatschke 2003) and the Cornell potential framework used in prior works (Agotiya 2008, Thakur 2012, Thakur 2013, Kakade 2015, Agotiya 2016, Jamal 2023).
