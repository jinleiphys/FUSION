# Effect of Coulomb energy on Skyrmions

> The Coulomb effect, an essential ingredient in nuclear systems, is quantitatively investigated for certain Skyrmions within the Skyrme model. To do this we calculate the Coulomb energy from numerically generated multi-Skyrmions and, simultaneously, introduce an effective alpha-like particle approximation for large Skyrmions with baryon number $B$, where $B$ is a multiple of four. The calculated Coulomb energies and the corresponding fitted curve from this approximation match well with the results from the numerical method, as well as the behavior of the Coulomb energy in the semi-empirical mass formula. The Skyrme model correctly reproduces the well known results for the Coulomb energy of nuclei. This suggests that the alpha-particles can be used as the fundamental degrees of freedom in the Skyrme model. The Coulomb effect on the binding energy of the Skyrmions increases with baryon number but has a small effect overall. However, the effect could be significantly more pronounced for loosely bound Skyrme models.

## Key claim
The Skyrme model reproduces the well-known Coulomb energy behavior of nuclei, but the Coulomb correction reduces the total binding energy of Skyrmions by only a small amount (around 2.4% for B=108). However, the effect could be significantly more pronounced for loosely bound Skyrme models, potentially reducing binding energy by around 25%.

## Method
The authors calculated Coulomb energies from numerically generated multi-Skyrmion solutions using a multipole expansion method, and simultaneously introduced an effective alpha-like particle approximation (APA) where each cubic B=4 Skyrmion is treated as an alpha-like particle with effective charge Q_eff. For larger Skyrmions with baryon number B=4N, the Coulomb energy was computed as the sum of self-interaction terms for each alpha-like particle plus pairwise interaction terms between clusters at known distances. The results were compared with the semi-empirical mass formula and with full numerical calculations for B=4, 8, 12, 16, 32, and 108.

## Key numbers
- Proton Coulomb energy: E_C^p = 0.116 MeV; neutron Coulomb energy: E_C^n = 0.28 × 10^{-3} MeV
- B=4 Skyrmion Coulomb energy: E_C^4 = 1.95 MeV (numerical method)
- B=8 Skyrmion Coulomb energy: E_C^8 = 5.45 MeV (numerical method)
- Effective alpha-like particle charge: Q_eff = 1.614 C (compared to naive expectation of 2 C)
- Distance between B=4 cube centers in B=8 Skyrmion: |r_1 - r_2| ≈ 2.4 fm
- B=12 Skyrmion Coulomb energy: E_C^{12} = 10.52 MeV (APA); numerical method gives 10.72 MeV
- B=16 Skyrmion Coulomb energy: E_C^{16} = 17.15 MeV (APA); numerical method gives 16.14 MeV
- B=32 Skyrmion Coulomb energy: E_C^{32} = 51.18 MeV (APA); numerical method gives 49.68 MeV
- B=108 Skyrmion Coulomb energy: E_C^{108} = 371.64 MeV (APA); numerical method gives 347.00 MeV
- Fitted curve from APA: E_C^APA = 0.152 B^{5/3} (compared to mass formula coefficient 0.156)
- For B=108 Skyrmion, total binding energy reduced by nearly 2.4% due to Coulomb correction
- For loosely bound B=4 Skyrmion with classical binding energy 8 MeV, Coulomb energy could reduce binding by nearly 25%

## Context
This work extends the Skyrme model by quantitatively including the Coulomb force, which is usually ignored in Skyrme model calculations. The results agree with the semi-empirical mass formula (Ning Wang, 2014) and with prior work on cluster structures in Skyrmions by Battye (2005, 2006) and Feist (2013). The alpha-like particle approximation enables future calculations for more difficult configurations, such as B=4N+n Skyrmions studied by Halcrow (2018), and for modified Skyrme models including loosely bound models by Gillard (2015) and Bjarke (2016, 2018) or models with rho mesons by Naya (2018).
