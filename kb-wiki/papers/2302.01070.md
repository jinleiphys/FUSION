# Linear stability analysis of Israel-Stewart theory in the case of a nonzero background charge

> Linear stability of Israel-Stewart theory in the presence of net-charge diffusion was investigated in [Phys.~Rev.~D 102 (2020) 116009] for the case of a massless, classical gas of noninteracting particles. However, in that work only a vanishing net-charge background was considered. In this work, we extend that study to the case of a nonvanishing background charge. We find that this effectively results in a change of the numeric value of the charge-diffusion coefficient, in a way that when the background charge goes to infinity, this coefficient can become at most four times its value at zero background charge. We also extend the analysis of [Phys.~Rev.~D 102 (2020) 116009] by performing a systematic parameter study in the plane of charge-diffusion coefficient vs.\ the coupling term between shear-stress and net-charge diffusion. In this plane, we identify regions where the solutions remain stable and causal and where they become acausal and/or unstable.

## Key claim

Extending the linear stability analysis of Israel-Stewart theory with net-charge diffusion to the case of a nonvanishing background charge effectively changes only the numerical value of the charge-diffusion coefficient, which can become at most four times its value at zero background charge when the background charge goes to infinity.

## Method

The authors linearize the equations of motion of second-order relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics around a global-equilibrium state with nonzero net-charge density, then Fourier-transform the perturbations and tensor-decompose them with respect to the direction of propagation. They derive the dispersion relation for longitudinal modes and perform a systematic parameter study in the plane of the charge-diffusion coefficient versus the coupling term between shear-stress and net-charge diffusion, identifying regions of stability, causality, and instability.

## Key numbers

- The charge-diffusion relaxation time \(\tilde{\tau}_\kappa\) varies from \(\hat{\tau}_\kappa = 9/16\) to \(4\hat{\tau}_\kappa = 9/4\) as the background charge \(n_0\) goes from 0 to \(\pm \bar{n}_0\) (the limiting values when \(\alpha_0 \rightarrow \pm \infty\)).
- Fixed parameters for the study: \(\hat{\tau}_\pi = 5\), \(\hat{\tau}_n = 27/4 = 6.75\), \(\hat{\tau}_\kappa = 9/16\).
- For an ultrarelativistic gas with constant cross section, kinetic theory in the 14-moment approximation gives \(\hat{\mathcal{L}}_{n\pi} \hat{\mathcal{L}}_{\pi n} = 0\), while a summation of all moments gives \(\hat{\mathcal{L}}_{n\pi} \hat{\mathcal{L}}_{\pi n} \simeq -0.54538\) for the chosen \(\hat{\tau}_\pi\) and \(\hat{\tau}_n\) values.

## Context

This work extends the linear stability analysis of Israel-Stewart theory with net-charge diffusion performed by Brito and Denicol (2020) for a massless, classical gas of noninteracting particles, which only considered a vanishing net-charge background. The present study generalizes that analysis to nonzero background charge and provides a more systematic parameter study in the plane of charge-diffusion coefficient versus shear-stress/charge-diffusion coupling. The results enable a more complete understanding of the stability and causality conditions for relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics applicable to heavy-ion collisions and neutron star matter.
