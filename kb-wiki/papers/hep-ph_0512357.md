# Combined Analysis of Two- and Three-Particle Correlations in q,p-Bose Gas Model

> q-deformed oscillators and the q-Bose gas model enable effective description of the observed non-Bose type behavior of the intercept ("strength") $λ^{(2)}\equiv C^{(2)}(K,K)-1$ of two-particle correlation function $C^{(2)}(p_1,p_2)$ of identical pions produced in heavy-ion collisions. Three- and n-particle correlation functions of pions (or kaons) encode more information on the nature of the emitting sources in such experiments. And so, the q-Bose gas model was further developed: the intercepts of n-th order correlators of q-bosons and the n-particle correlation intercepts within the q,p-Bose gas model have been obtained, the result useful for quantum optics, too. Here we present the combined analysis of two- and three-pion correlation intercepts for the q-Bose gas model and its q,p-extension, and confront with empirical data (from CERN SPS and STAR/RHIC) on pion correlations. Similar to explicit dependence of $λ^{(2)}$ on mean momenta of particles (pions, kaons) found earlier, here we explore the peculiar behavior, versus mean momentum, of the 3-particle correlation intercept $λ^{(3)}(K)$. The whole approach implies complete chaoticity of sources, unlike other joint descriptions of two- and three-pion correlations using two phenomenological parameters (e.g., core-halo fraction plus partial coherence of sources).

# Combined Analysis of Two- and Three-Particle Correlations in q,p-Bose Gas Model

## Key claim

The q-deformed and q,p-deformed Bose gas models can simultaneously describe the intercepts of two- and three-pion correlation functions from heavy-ion collisions, but only under the assumption of complete chaoticity of sources, unlike other approaches that use two phenomenological parameters (such as core-halo fraction plus partial coherence). The momentum dependence of the three-particle intercept shows peculiar behavior at both low and large mean momenta, with the large-momentum asymptotics determined solely by the deformation parameters.

## Method

The author derives analytic expressions for the intercepts (strengths) of two- and three-particle correlation functions using the q-Bose gas model in its Arik-Coon (AC) and Biedenharn-Macfarlane (BM) versions, as well as the two-parameter q,p-Bose gas model. These theoretical intercepts are compared with experimental data on negative pions from CERN SPS and STAR/RHIC experiments, and the combined analysis uses both the individual intercepts λ^(2) and λ^(3) and the special combination r^(3) that cancels contributions from long-lived resonances.

## Key numbers

- Experimental two-pion correlation intercept for negative pions: λ^(2), exp. = 0.57 ± 0.04
- Experimental three-pion correlation intercept for negative pions: λ^(3), exp. = 1.92 ± 0.49
- For the BM-type q-Bose gas at T = 180 MeV, the optimal deformation parameter for negative pions is q = exp(iθ) with θ = 28.5°
- For the AC-type q-Bose gas, the optimal deformation parameter for positive pions is q = 0.63
- The asymptotic value of λ^(2) for AC-type q-bosons at large βω is λ^(2), asym. = q
- The asymptotic value of λ^(2) for BM-type q-bosons at large βω is λ^(2), asym. = 2cosθ - 1
- The asymptotic value of λ^(2) for q,p-bosons at large βω is λ^(2), asym. = (p+q) - 1
- The asymptotic value of λ^(3) for AC-type q-bosons at large βω is λ^(3), asym. = (1+q)(1+q+q²) - 1
- The asymptotic value of λ^(3) for BM-type q-bosons at large βω is λ^(3), asym. = 2cosθ(4cos²θ - 1) - 1
- The asymptotic value of λ^(3) for q,p-bosons at large βω is λ^(3), asym. = (p+q)(p²+pq+q²) - 1
- Slicing analysis performed at w = 0.78 (corresponding to T = 180 MeV, K_t = 10 MeV) and w = 2.9 (T = 180 MeV, K_t = 500 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the q-Bose gas model approach previously developed by Gavrilik and collaborators (2003-2005) for two-particle correlations to include three-particle correlations, building on the general n-particle intercept formulas derived by AdGavrilik (2005). The approach contrasts with the core-halo plus partial coherence models of Csorgo and collaborators (1999-2003), which require two phenomenological parameters and assume partially coherent sources. The combined analysis of two- and three-pion intercepts enables determination of both the deformation parameters (from high-momentum data) and the temperature (from low-momentum data), and reveals that the Tamm-Dancoff (TD) version of q-Bose gas (p=q) may be more suitable than the AC or BM versions for describing the available experimental data.
