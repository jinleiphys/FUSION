# The Doppler Paradigm and the APEX-EPOS-ORANGE Quandary

> The experimental detection of the sharp lines of the \ee Puzzle is viewed as a struggle against Doppler broadening. Gedanken experiments which are realistic in zeroth order of detail are analyzed to show that the ORANGE and EPOS/I geometries select narrower slices of a Doppler broadened line than spherically inclusive (APEX and EPOS/II --like) apparati. Roughly speaking, the latter require event-by-event Doppler reconstruction simply to regain an even footing with the former. This suggests that APEX' or EPOS/II's coincident pair distributions must be statistically superior to those of EPOS/I or ORANGE in order to support a comparable inference about sharp structure. Under present circumstances, independent alternative data is invaluable. Therefore, a corroboration of Sakai's 330.1 keV ($<$ 3 keV wide) electron line in few MeV $e^+$ or $e^-$ bombardments of U and Th targets could prove crucial.

## Key claim

The experimental detection of sharp electron-positron pair lines in heavy-ion collisions is fundamentally a struggle against Doppler broadening; the ORANGE and EPOS/I apparati are inherently better "Doppler narrowers" than the spherically inclusive APEX and EPOS/II designs, which require event-by-event Doppler reconstruction merely to achieve comparable sensitivity, and even then the null results from APEX and EPOS/II do not necessarily contradict the earlier positive claims.

## Method

The author analyzes idealized "gedanken experiments" in which a uniform, isotropic distribution of sharp-energy electron-positron pairs is emitted from a source moving at beta_S = 0.1c, and the resulting laboratory-frame energy distributions are calculated for three detector geometries: a Spherically Inclusive (SI) geometry resembling APEX and EPOS/II, a Bi-Hemispheric (BH) selector resembling EPOS/I, and an Angular Selector (AS) resembling the double-ORANGE apparatus. The widths of the Doppler-broadened distributions accepted by each geometry are computed analytically from the transformation equations and the acceptance criteria.

## Key numbers

- For a pair kinetic energy of 758 keV in the source rest frame (average lab energy 750 keV), the Spherically Inclusive apparatus yields a Doppler width Gamma_SI ~ 120 keV.
- The Bi-Hemispheric (EPOS/I-like) selector narrows the Doppler line by about a factor of two relative to the SI apparatus: Gamma_BH ~ 0.5 Gamma_SI.
- The Angular Selector (ORANGE-like) narrows the line by about a factor of 1/4 relative to the SI apparatus: Gamma_AS / Gamma_SI ~ 0.25, based on acceptance cone angles from 35 degrees to 70 degrees.
- Event-by-event Doppler reconstruction of APEX and EPOS/II data on pairs from 206Pb has achieved re-narrowed widths of about 40 keV, a factor of about 1/3 reduction from the 120 keV SI width.
- Sakai et al. report a very sharp 330.1 keV electron line with width less than 3 keV from few-MeV positron bombardments of U and Th targets.

## Context

This paper extends the author's earlier work on the Composite Particle (Q0) scenario (Griffin 1991a, 1991b) and directly addresses the apparent contradiction between the sharp pair lines reported by the EPOS/I collaboration (Cowan 1985, Salabura 1990) and the ORANGE collaboration (Koenig 1989, 1993) and the null results from APEX (Bett 1995, Chant 1995) and EPOS/II (Bokemeyer 1995). The analysis suggests that the differences in measured peak energies between EPOS/I and ORANGE should be expected as second-order Doppler shifts rather than measurement errors, and it enables a reinterpretation of all existing data as consistent under a Doppler-broadening paradigm. The paper also urges independent corroboration of Sakai's (1988, 1991, 1992a, 1993) sharp electron line measurements using few-MeV electron or positron beams.
