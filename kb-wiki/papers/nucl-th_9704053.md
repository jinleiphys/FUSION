# Be7(p,gamma)B8 and the high-energy solar neutrino flux

> The importance of the Be7(p,gamma)B8 reaction in predicting the high-energy solar neutrino flux is discussed. I present a microscopic eight-body model and a potential model for the calculation of the Be7(p,gamma)B8 cross section.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the low-energy $^7$Be($p,\gamma$)$^8$B astrophysical $S$ factor $S_{17}(0)$ can be constrained by correlating it with other nuclear observables (the quadrupole moment of $^7$Be and the Coulomb displacement energy of A=8 states), but the models yield values slightly higher than the standard solar model input, and off-shell effects can cause 15-20% changes in extrapolated $S_{17}(0)$ from high-energy data.

## Method

A microscopic eight-body three-cluster model (variationally converged in the $^4$He+$^3$He+$p$ model space) and a separate $^7$Be+$p$ potential model were used to calculate the nonresonant part of the $^7$Be($p,\gamma$)$^8$B cross section. The microscopic model correlated the zero-energy $S$ factor with the quadrupole moment of $^7$Be, while the potential model related the Coulomb displacement energy for the A=8, J=2$^{+}$, T=1 state to the valence-proton density and asymptotic normalization in $^8$B.

## Key numbers

- The six direct capture measurements give $S_{17}(0)$ between 15 eVb and 40 eVb, with a weighted average of $22.2 \pm 2.3$ eVb.
- A recent Coulomb dissociation measurement gives $S_{17}(0) = 16.7 \pm 3.2$ eVb.
- The microscopic eight-body model predicts $S_{17}(0) = 25-26.5$ eVb when the most important $^8$B, $^7$Be and $^7$Li properties are reproduced simultaneously.
- The potential model predicts $S_{17}(0) = 24.5 \pm 2.9$ eVb at zero energy.
- Off-shell effects can cause 15-20% changes in the value of $S_{17}(0)$ extrapolated from high-energy ($E_{cm} > 0.7$ MeV) data.
- The energy dependence of the E1 component of the $S$ factor is virtually flat at $E_{cm} = 0.3-1.5$ MeV in the eight-body model.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical and experimental efforts to determine $S_{17}(0)$, which is the most uncertain nuclear input parameter in standard solar models (Bahcall 1995). The paper addresses the considerable confusion in the literature, where direct capture measurements give $S_{17}(0)$ between 15 and 40 eVb and theoretical predictions range from 16 to 30 eVb (Langanke 1996). The results enable tighter theoretical limits on $S_{17}(0)$ and help resolve the apparent disagreement between microscopic eight-body models and potential models by identifying off-shell effects as a significant source of uncertainty.
