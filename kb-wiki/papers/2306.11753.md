# Chiral symmetry breaking and phase diagram of dual chiral density wave in a rotating quark matter

> We study the inhomogeneous phase of a two-flavor quark matter under rotation at finite temperature and density using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. To do this, we consider the chiral broken phase, in particular, described by the so-called dual chiral density wave which is formed as a standing wave of simultaneous scalar and pseudoscalar condensates. The solution of the corresponding Dirac equation as well as the energy spectrum found in the mean-field approximation. We then use the thermodynamic potential calculated for this model, to study the $μ$ and $Ω$ dependence of constituent mass and the wave vector at $T = 0$. We find there exist two islands in the $μ- Ω$ plane that the dual-chiral density wave survives. The first region lies at intermediate densities and small $Ω$. We observe, by increasing the angular velocity of matter, dual-chiral density wave forms in regions with smaller chemical potential. On the other hand, in contrast to the former, the second region is located at the large $Ω$ and small densities. Finally, we study this phase of quark matter at finite temperature and present $T-μ$, $T-Ω$, and $μ-Ω$ phase portraits of a hot-rotating quark matter at finite density.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in a rotating two-flavor quark matter described by the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, the dual chiral density wave (DCDW) phase survives in two distinct regions of the chemical potential-angular velocity plane: one at intermediate densities and small angular velocity, and another at large angular velocity and small densities, with the hedge that this result depends on the choice of Minkowski vacuum (with positive energy modes) rather than the corotating vacuum.

## Method

The authors use a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in a corotating frame with constant angular velocity about the z-direction, employing the mean-field approximation to describe the inhomogeneous chiral condensate as a dual chiral density wave (DCDW) with scalar and pseudoscalar condensates forming a standing wave. They solve the Dirac equation in the rotating frame with the DCDW ansatz, derive the thermodynamic potential using proper time regularization with cutoff Lambda = 0.86 GeV and coupling G Lambda^2 = 11, and numerically minimize the potential to find the constituent mass m and wave vector q as functions of chemical potential, angular velocity, and temperature.

## Key numbers

* Constituent mass m and wave vector q are computed for angular velocities Omega = 0, 100, 200, and 400 MeV at zero temperature as functions of chemical potential.
* The model parameters are fixed as Lambda = 0.86 GeV and G Lambda^2 = 11 for N_f = 2 and N_c = 3.
* Numerical solutions are performed at a distance r = 0.1 GeV^{-1} from the axis of rotation, where the local temperature T(r) approximates the temperature at the axis T_0.
* The dispersion relation for the DCDW is given by epsilon_s^2 = k_perp^2 + (sqrt(k_z^2 + m^2) + s q/2)^2, with s = +/- 1 for spin direction.

## Context

This work extends studies of inhomogeneous chiral condensates in rotating quark matter, building on prior findings that rotation suppresses the scalar condensate and reduces the chiral symmetry restoration temperature (Jiang 2016, Sadooghi 2021). It connects two lines of research: the effect of rotation on chiral condensates in the NJL model, and the crystalline (inhomogeneous) phase of quark matter at intermediate chemical potential studied in the NJL model (Tatsumi 2004, Nakano 2004) and other approaches. The results enable mapping the phase diagram of rotating quark matter in the T-mu, T-Omega, and mu-Omega planes, and suggest that fast rotation can induce DCDW formation at smaller chemical potentials than in non-rotating systems.
