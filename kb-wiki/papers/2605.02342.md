# Measurement of isolated-prompt photon$-$hadron correlations in Pb$-$Pb collisions at $\mathbf{\sqrt{\textit{s}_{\rm NN}} = 5.02}$ TeV

> The ALICE Collaboration has measured the azimuthal correlation between trigger isolated-prompt photons and associated charged hadrons in Pb$-$Pb collisions at the CERN LHC, at a centre-of-mass energy per nucleon pair of \snnfive. The trigger isolated-prompt photons are measured in the transverse-momentum range $18< p_{\rm T}^γ < 40$ GeV/$c$ and pseudorapidity range $|η^γ| <0.67$. The isolation selection is based on a charged particle isolation momentum threshold $p_{\rm T}^{\rm iso, ch} = 1.5$ GeV/$c$ within a cone of radius $R=0.2$. The associated charged particles are measured in the transverse-momentum ranges $p_{\rm T}^{\rm h} > 1.8$ GeV/$c$ and pseudorapidity $|η^{\rm h}| <0.9$. The yield D$(z_{\rm T})$ of associated hadrons per trigger, with $z_{\rm T} = p_{\rm T}^{\rm h}/p_{\rm T}^γ$, is measured in three Pb$-$Pb collision centrality classes: central (0$-$30%), semicentral (30$-$50%), and peripheral (50$-$90%). An approximation to the standard $I_{\rm AA}$ is computed from the D$(z_{\rm T})$ conditional yields, using NLO pQCD predictions as pp reference. A strong suppression of this ratio is observed in central collisions compared to peripheral collisions. The result extends to a lower $p_{\rm T}^γ$ relative to those reported in previously published Pb$-$Pb collisions measurements at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02$ TeV. The measurement is compared to NLO pQCD calculations that include energy loss, and to the CoLBT-hydro model. The results from central collisions are also compared with measurements of jets correlated with isolated-prompt photons and of hadrons correlated with Z$^0$ bosons, both reported by the CMS Collaboration at the LHC, as well as with direct photon$-$hadron correlation measurements reported by the PHENIX and STAR Collaborations at RHIC.

## Key claim

The ALICE Collaboration reports a strong suppression of isolated-prompt photon-hadron correlations in central Pb-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, quantified via an approximation to the standard I_AA using NLO pQCD predictions as the pp reference, rather than a direct pp measurement.

## Method

The analysis measured the azimuthal correlation between trigger isolated-prompt photons (18 < p_T^gamma < 40 GeV/c, |eta^gamma| < 0.67) and associated charged hadrons (p_T^h > 1.8 GeV/c, |eta^h| < 0.9) in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV. Photon isolation used a charged particle isolation momentum threshold of p_T^iso,ch = 1.5 GeV/c within a cone of radius R = 0.2. The yield D(z_T) of associated hadrons per trigger, where z_T = p_T^h / p_T^gamma, was measured in three centrality classes (0-30%, 30-50%, 50-90%) and compared to NLO pQCD calculations including energy loss and the CoLBT-hydro model.

## Key numbers

- Trigger isolated-prompt photons measured in transverse-momentum range 18 < p_T^gamma < 40 GeV/c
- Associated charged hadrons measured for p_T^h > 1.8 GeV/c
- Photon pseudorapidity range |eta^gamma| < 0.67; hadron pseudorapidity range |eta^h| < 0.9
- Isolation cone radius R = 0.2 with charged particle isolation momentum threshold p_T^iso,ch = 1.5 GeV/c
- Three centrality classes analyzed: central (0-30%), semicentral (30-50%), and peripheral (50-90%)
- Center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV

## Context

This measurement extends previous Pb-Pb isolated-photon-hadron correlation results at the same energy to lower p_T^gamma values. The results are compared to NLO pQCD calculations with energy loss and the CoLBT-hydro model, as well as to CMS measurements of jets correlated with isolated-prompt photons and hadrons correlated with Z^0 bosons, and to direct photon-hadron correlation measurements from PHENIX and STAR at RHIC. The work enables further constraints on parton energy loss mechanisms in the quark-gluon plasma by probing the medium response at lower photon transverse momenta.
