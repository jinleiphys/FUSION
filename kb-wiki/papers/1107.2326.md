# Kaon and hyperon production in antiproton-induced reactions on nuclei

> We study the strangeness production in antiproton-nucleus collisions at the beam momenta from 200 MeV/c to 15 GeV/c within the Giessen Botzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) transport model. The GiBUU model contains a very detailed description of the underlying antinucleon-nucleon cross sections, in-particular, of the strangeness production channels. We compare our calculations with the bubble chamber data on Λ- and K^0_S-production in antiproton-nucleus collisions and with the earlier intranuclear cascade calculations. The contributions of the various partial channels to the hyperon production are reported and systematic differences with experiment are discussed. The possible formation of the bound Λ- and ΛΛ-nucleus systems is also considered. Finally, the results on the Ξ-hyperon production are presented in view of the forthcoming experiments with antiproton beams at FAIR.

# Kaon and hyperon production in antiproton-induced reactions on nuclei

## Key claim

The GiBUU transport model, which includes selfconsistent relativistic mean fields for baryons, antibaryons, kaons, and antikaons, can reproduce the experimentally observed large Λ/K⁰_S yield ratios in antiproton-nucleus collisions, but only if the strangeness exchange reactions (K̅N → Yπ) are sufficiently enhanced; the model systematically overproduces K⁰_S yields while matching Λ yields, and the agreement depends on the choice of critical distance criterion for distinguishing captured versus free hyperons.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled system of Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck kinetic equations for hadronic species (N, Δ, Y, Y*, Ξ, π, K and antiparticles) within the GiBUU framework, using the NL3 relativistic mean field model for meson-nucleon coupling constants and light quark counting rules for meson-hyperon and meson-kaon couplings. Antinucleon-nucleon annihilation was treated via a statistical annihilation model with SU(3) symmetry supplemented by empirical branching ratios at low beam momenta, while inelastic production above √s = 2.37 GeV was simulated with the Fritiof model; the strangeness exchange reactions K̅N → Yπ were included partly via hyperon resonance formations and decays Y* → Yπ.

## Key numbers

- Beam momentum range studied: 0.2–15 GeV/c for in-flight annihilation, plus annihilation at rest
- Schrödinger equivalent potential for antiproton at normal nuclear density ρ₀ = 0.148 fm⁻³: U_Ȳ = −150 MeV, obtained using factor ξ = 0.22 in the coupling constant relations
- Schrödinger equivalent potentials at ρ₀: Λ = −38 MeV, Σ = −39 MeV, Ξ = −22 MeV, K = −18 MeV, K̅ = −224 MeV, Λ̅ = −449 MeV, Ξ̅ = −227 MeV
- Maximum invariant energy for using annihilation tables at rest: √s_max = 2.6 GeV (corresponding to p_lab = 2.5 GeV/c)
- Critical distance for distinguishing inside/outside nucleus: d_c = 3 fm, with evolution time 200 fm/c
- Free Λ production probability per annihilation event at p_lab = 225 MeV/c: weakly sensitive to target mass number, agreeing with BNL data from Condo (1984) for targets ¹²C, ⁴⁸Ti, ¹⁸¹Ta, and ²⁰⁸Pb
- Probability of one and two captured Λ production grows by almost one order of magnitude from ¹²C to ²⁰⁸Pb at 225 MeV/c
- At 608 MeV/c on ²⁰Ne: GiBUU and INC calculations from Cugnon (1990) agree within ~30% for Λ rapidity distributions; calculated K⁰_S spectra lie above experimental data from Balestra (1987)
- Σ⁰ lifetime: τ = 7.4 × 10⁻²⁰ s ≈ 222000 fm/c, much longer than typical reaction time scale of ~100 fm/c

## Context

This work extends earlier intranuclear cascade (INC) calculations by Cugnon (1990) and Strottman (1985) that tended to overestimate both Λ and K⁰_S yields while underestimating the Λ/K⁰_S ratio; the GiBUU model improves upon those by including selfconsistent relativistic mean fields that depend on actual particle densities and currents. The results enable predictions for Ξ hyperon production relevant to the planned PANDA experiment at FAIR, and provide a baseline for distinguishing hadronic mechanisms from hypothetical cold quark-gluon plasma formation scenarios proposed by Rafelski (1988). The calculations also give estimates for single- and double-Λ hypernucleus formation probabilities, further developed in Gaitanos (2011).
