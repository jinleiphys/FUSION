# Terzan 5 transient IGR J17480-2446: variation of burst and spectral properties with spectral states

> We study the spectral state evolution of the Terzan 5 transient neutron star low-mass X-ray binary IGR J17480-2446, and how the best-fit spectral parameters and burst properties evolved with these states, using the Rossi X-ray Timing Explorer data. As reported by other authors, this is the second source which showed transitions between atoll state and `Z' state. We find large scale hysteresis in the almost `C'-like hardness-intensity track of the source in the atoll state. This discovery is likely to provide a missing piece of the jigsaw puzzle involving various types of hardness-intensity tracks from `q'-shaped for Aquila X-1, 4U 1608-52, and many black holes to `C'-shaped for many atoll sources. Furthermore, the regular pulsations, a diagonal transition between soft and hard states, and the large scale hysteresis observed from IGR J17480-2446 argue against some of the previous suggestions involving magnetic field about atolls and millisecond pulsars. Our results also suggest that the nature of spectral evolution throughout an outburst does not, at least entirely, depend on the peak luminosity of the outburst. Besides, the source took at least a month to trace the softer banana state, as opposed to a few hours to a day, which is typical for an atoll source. In addition, while the soft colour usually increases with intensity in the softer portion of an atoll source, IGR J17480-2446 showed an opposite behaviour. From the detailed spectral fitting we conclude that a blackbody+powerlaw model is the simplest one, which describes the source continuum spectra well throughout the outburst. We find that these two spectral components were plausibly connected with each other, and they worked together to cause the source state evolution. (Truncated).

# Terzan 5 transient IGR J17480-2446: variation of burst and spectral properties with spectral states

## Key claim

The paper claims that IGR J17480-2446 is only the second neutron star low-mass X-ray binary observed to transition between an atoll state and a Z state, and that it exhibits large-scale hysteresis in its hardness-intensity diagram (HID) that is more C-like than previously studied sources. However, the authors qualify that the hysteresis is observed only in the HID, not in the colour-colour diagram, and that the source's behaviour argues against previous magnetic field-based explanations for the distinction between vertical and diagonal transitions in atoll sources.

## Method

The authors analyzed Rossi X-ray Timing Explorer (RXTE) data from approximately 297 ks of observations spanning October 13 to November 19, 2010, dividing the data into 11 temporal segments based on source state. They constructed colour-colour diagrams (hard colour: 9.2-18.9 keV / 5.7-9.2 keV; soft colour: 3.9-5.7 keV / 2.6-3.9 keV) and hardness-intensity diagrams using background-subtracted PCU-2 top layer counts in the 2.6-18.9 keV band. Spectral fitting was performed on non-burst (persistent) spectra in the 3-15 keV range using a phabs*(bbodyrad+powerlaw+Gaussian) model with fixed neutral hydrogen column density at 3.8 x 10^22 cm^-2. Burst properties (peak flux, fluence, interval, duration) were measured from standard-2 and event mode data, with burst intervals for segment 5 estimated from mHz quasi-periodic oscillation frequencies.

## Context

This work extends the study of spectral state evolution in neutron star low-mass X-ray binaries, following the discovery that XTE J1701-462 (Homan et al. 2010) was the first source to show atoll-to-Z transitions. The paper builds on the analysis of EXO 1745-248 by Mukherjee and Bhattacharyya (2011), which showed an intermediate hysteresis track between q-shaped and C-shaped, and places IGR J17480-2446 as even more C-like than that source. The results challenge the classification scheme of Gladstone et al. (2007), which connected millisecond pulsars with vertical transitions and used magnetic field strength to distinguish verticals from diagonals, because IGR J17480-2446 is a pulsar (implying a strong magnetic field) yet shows diagonal transitions with large hysteresis. The burst properties are compared to the clock-like burster GS 1826-238 (Thompson et al. 2008), with IGR J17480-2446 showing much shorter burst intervals plausibly due to its pulsar nature concentrating accretion onto a smaller surface area. The work enables future quantitative modeling of thermonuclear burst behaviour near stability and further investigation of the connection between neutron star and black hole accretion processes.
