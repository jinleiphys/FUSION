# Nuclear and Non-Ionizing Energy-Loss for Coulomb Scattered Particles from Low Energy up to Relativistic Regime in Space Radiation Environment

> In the space environment, instruments onboard of spacecrafts can be affected by displacement damage due to radiation. The differential scattering cross section for screened nucleus--nucleus interactions - i.e., including the effects due to screened Coulomb nuclear fields -, nuclear stopping powers and non-ionization energy losses are treated from about 50 keV/nucleon up to relativistic energies.

## Key claim

The paper presents a relativistic treatment of screened Coulomb scattering for nucleus-nucleus interactions, using the Wentzel-Moliere model, to calculate nuclear stopping powers and non-ionizing energy loss (NIEL) from about 50 keV/nucleon up to relativistic energies, with the caveat that the simple screening parameter may require a more appropriate expression and practical correction factor for full accuracy at low energies.

## Method

The authors derived the differential cross section for screened Coulomb scattering using the Wentzel-Moliere model with a Thomas-Fermi based screening function, expressed in terms of the kinetic energy transferred to the recoil target nucleus. They then integrated this cross section to obtain the nuclear stopping power and, using the Lindhard partition function, the non-ionizing energy loss (NIEL) for various projectile-target combinations from 50 keV/nucleon up to 100 TeV/nucleon.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear stopping powers for protons and alpha-particles from the Wentzel-Moliere model agree with ICRU (1993) tabulated values to better than approximately 5% from 50 keV/nucleon up to about 32 MeV/nucleon (protons) and 31 MeV/nucleon (alpha-particles)
- Agreement with SRIM (2008) values is better than a few percent down to about 150 keV/nucleon, where one finds approximately 5.5% for alpha-particles and 9.9% for lead ions in silicon
- With a modified screening parameter (C = (10 pi z Z alpha)^0.04), stopping powers differ from SRIM (2008) values by less than approximately 3.5% for alpha-particles and 2.6% for lead ions in silicon down to about 50 keV/nucleon
- Non-ionizing stopping powers for protons in silicon agree with calculations by Jun and collaborators (2003) to better than approximately 6.5% at about 1 GeV
- Threshold energy for displacement is 21 eV in silicon and 25 eV in lead

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic screened potential approach of Ziegler, Biersack and Littmark (1985) and the ICRU Report 49 (1993) tabulations to relativistic energies, where the nuclear stopping power exhibits a slight logarithmic increase with energy rather than decreasing. The model is included in Geant4 version 9.4 (December 2010) and enables improved calculations of displacement damage in space radiation environments, building on earlier relativistic treatments by Jun and collaborators (2003) for protons in silicon.
