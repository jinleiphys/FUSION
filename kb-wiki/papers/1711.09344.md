# Comment on "Relation between scattering amplitude and Bethe-Salpeter wave function in quantum field theory"

> We invalidate the arguments given in [T.Yamazaki and Y.Kuramashi, Phys. Rev. D96, 114511 (2017)] over the HAL QCD method for hadron-hadron interactions on the lattice. We also pose questions on the practical usefulness of the method proposed in this reference.

## Key claim

The authors invalidate the arguments of Yamazaki and Kuramashi (2017) regarding the HAL QCD method for hadron-hadron interactions on the lattice, claiming that the critique is based on a confusion about the definition and properties of the non-local potential and its derivative expansion, and that the proposed alternative method in that reference has no practical advantage over existing approaches.

## Method

The authors re-derive the mathematical relation between the energy-independent non-local potential \(U(\mathbf{r},\mathbf{r}')\) and the momentum-dependent local potential \(V(\mathbf{r};\mathbf{k})\) using the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave function below the inelastic threshold, showing that the non-local potential is constructed from a sum over all momenta with dual basis functions. They then demonstrate that the derivative expansion of \(U(\mathbf{r},\mathbf{r}')\) yields momentum-independent coefficient functions \(V_{\mathbf{n}}(\mathbf{r})\), and provide an explicit two-term example showing how these coefficients can be reconstructed from NBS wave functions at two different momenta to reproduce exact phase shifts. They also analyze the effect of smearing interpolating operators on the NBS wave function and show that the on-shell scattering amplitude is unchanged when the correct normalization is taken into account.

## Key numbers

- The on-shell amplitude relation is given as \(H_{\phi}(k;k)/C_{\phi}(k) = 4\pi e^{i\delta(k)} \sin\delta(k)/k\), where \(C_{\phi}(k)\) is a normalization factor and \(\delta(k)\) is the phase shift.
- The smeared wave function normalization ratio is \(C_{\tilde{\phi}}(k)/C_{\phi}(k) = \int d^3r \ s(\mathbf{r}) e^{-i\mathbf{k}\cdot\mathbf{r}}\), with \(s(\mathbf{r})\) the smearing function.
- The "fundamental relation" (eq. (11) in Yamazaki and Kuramashi (2017)) is given as \(-\int d^3x \ h(x;k)e^{-i\mathbf{k}\cdot\mathbf{x}} = \frac{4\pi}{k}e^{i\delta(k)}\sin\delta(k)\), which the authors note requires independent determination of both \(C_{\phi}(k)\) and \(k\) on the lattice.

## Context

This Comment directly responds to Yamazaki and Kuramashi (2017), which criticized the HAL QCD method for extracting hadron-hadron interactions from lattice QCD. The authors defend their previously published works, particularly Aoki et al. (2009) and Aoki et al. (2012), which established the mathematical foundations of the HAL QCD method including the non-local potential formalism and its derivative expansion. The paper clarifies that the derivative expansion approach has historical precedent in nuclear force studies (Okubo and Marshak; Tamagaki and Watanabe) and that systematic errors from truncation can be controlled through order-by-order analysis using lattice QCD data as demonstrated in Iritani et al. (2017) and Kawai et al. (2017). The work also addresses the practical limitations of the alternative method proposed by Yamazaki and Kuramashi (2017), noting that it requires knowledge of the momentum \(k\) and normalization \(C_{\phi}(k)\) which are already available through Lüscher's finite volume method (Lüscher, 1990).
