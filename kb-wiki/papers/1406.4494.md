# Semi-inclusive charged-current neutrino-nucleus reactions

> The general, universal formalism for semi-inclusive charged-current (anti)neutrino-nucleus reactions is given for studies of any hadronic system, namely, either nuclei or the nucleon itself. The detailed developments are presented with the former in mind and are further specialized to cases where the final-state charged lepton and an ejected nucleon are presumed to be detected. General kinematics for such processes are summarized and then explicit expressions are developed for the leptonic and hadronic tensors involved and for the corresponding responses according to the usual charge, longitudinal and transverse projections, keeping finite the masses of all particles involved. In the case of the hadronic responses, general symmetry principles are invoked to determine which contributions can occur. Finally, the general leptonic-hadronic tensor contraction is given as well as the cross section for the process.

## Key claim

The paper presents a general, universal formalism for semi-inclusive charged-current (anti)neutrino-nucleus reactions that is valid for any hadronic system and any energy scale, but the detailed developments are specialized to cases where the final-state charged lepton and an ejected nucleon are detected, with the caveat that the formalism is kept fully relativistic except for one unspecified non-relativistic approximation discussed later in the text.

## Method

The authors develop the kinematics for semi-inclusive reactions of the type \(X(\nu_\ell,\ell^- N)\) and \(X(\bar{\nu}_\ell,\ell^+ N)\) using four-momentum conservation and introducing the daughter energy difference \(\mathcal{E}\) and missing momentum \(p\) as dynamical variables in place of the detected nucleon energy and angle. They then decompose the leptonic and hadronic tensors into symmetric and antisymmetric parts, defining a complete set of response functions (charge, longitudinal, transverse, and their interference terms) using spherical vector components, and express the leptonic tensor components in terms of the vector and axial coupling constants \(a_V\) and \(a_A\) with finite lepton masses.

## Key numbers

- The quasielastic peak energy is defined as \(\omega_{QE} \equiv \omega(y=0) \equiv \left\{ \sqrt{q^2+m_N^2} - m_N \right\} + E_s = |Q_{QE}^2|/2m_N + E_s\), where \(E_s\) is the separation energy.
- The minimum missing momentum \(p_{min} \equiv -y\) and maximum \(p_{max} \equiv +Y\) are given by explicit formulas involving \(M_A^0\), \(\omega\), \(q\), \(W_{A-1}^0\), and \(m_N\) (Eqs. (15) and (16)).
- The asymptotic limit of the upper boundary \(\mathcal{E}_-\) for large momentum transfer is \(\mathcal{E}_-^\infty = y + p - \left[ \sqrt{{W_{A-1}^0}^2 + p^2} - \sqrt{{W_{A-1}^0}^2 + y^2} \right]\).
- The leptonic kinematic variables include \(\rho \equiv |Q^2|/q^2 = 1 - \nu^2\) with \(\nu \equiv \omega/q\), and \(\tan^2 \widetilde{\theta}/2 = |Q^2|/v_0 = \rho \rho^{\prime 2}/(1-\rho^{\prime 2})\) where \(\rho^{\prime} \equiv q/(\varepsilon+\varepsilon^{\prime})\).

## Context

This work extends the formalism previously employed in electron scattering studies (Donnelly and Raskin, 1986; Raskin and Donnelly, 1989) to the case of semi-inclusive charged-current neutrino-nucleus reactions, providing a general framework that can accommodate any final-state particle species and includes the possibility of meson exchange currents. The formalism enables future model calculations for experiments such as ArgoNeuT and MicroBooNE, where protons and muons are detected in coincidence using argon TPCs, and can be applied to study the quasielastic regime as well as higher-energy reactions.
