# Variation of hadron masses in nuclear matter in the relativistic Hartree approximation

> We study the modification of hadron masses due to the vacuum polarization using the chiral sigma model, which is extended to generate the $ω$ meson mass by the sigma condensation in the vacuum in the same way as the nucleon mass. The results obtained in the chiral sigma model are compared with those obtained in the Walecka model which includes $σ$ and $ω$ mesons in a non-chiral fashion. It is shown that both the nucleon mass and the $ω$ meson mass decrease in nuclear medium, while the $σ$ meson mass increases at finite density in the chiral sigma model.

## Key claim

The chiral sigma model, when renormalized with chiral-symmetry constraints, predicts that the nucleon and omega meson masses decrease in nuclear matter while the sigma meson mass increases at finite density, in contrast to the Walecka model where the sigma mass first decreases then slightly increases. The authors note that this behavior depends on the renormalization procedure, particularly how counterterms are introduced.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic Hartree (mean field) calculations including vacuum polarization effects for both the non-chiral Walecka model and the chiral sigma model. They determined the effective masses of the nucleon, sigma meson, and omega meson by solving for the zeros of the renormalized inverse propagators as functions of baryon density, using parameters fitted to reproduce the saturation properties of nuclear matter (binding energy -15.75 MeV at Fermi momentum 1.3 fm^{-1}).

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass M = 939 MeV, omega meson mass m_omega = 783 MeV, pion mass m_pi = 139 MeV (experimental values used in both models)
- In the Walecka model: sigma meson mass m_sigma = 520 MeV, coupling constants g_sigma^2 = 66.117 and g_omega^2 = 79.927, fitted to binding energy -15.75 MeV and equilibrium Fermi momentum 1.3 fm^{-1}
- In the chiral sigma model: vacuum expectation value sigma_0 = f_pi = 93 MeV, g_sigma = M/f_pi = 10.1, tilde_g_omega = m_omega/f_pi = 8.42
- In the chiral sigma model: fitted parameters m_sigma = 715 MeV and g_omega = 4.025, reproducing binding energy -15.75 MeV and equilibrium Fermi momentum 1.3 fm^{-1}

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of hadron mass modification in nuclear matter, particularly the Walecka model calculations by Suzuki (2002) and the chiral sigma model approach of Boguta (1983). It contrasts with results from Hatsuda et al. (1993) and Vacas et al. (1994) who found decreasing sigma mass in medium, and with Thomas et al. (1998) who found nearly constant sigma mass. The paper enables further investigation of how renormalization procedures and chiral symmetry constraints affect predictions for in-medium hadron properties, which are relevant for interpreting dilepton production experiments from relativistic heavy ion collisions.
