# Mixing of the $f_0$ and $a_0$ scalar mesons in threshold photoproduction

> We examine the photoproduction of the light scalar mesons $f_0$-$a_0 (980)$ with emphasis on isospin-violating $f_0$ and $a_0$ mixing due to the mass difference between neutral and charged kaons. General forms for the invariant amplitude for scalar meson photoproduction are derived, which yield expressions for the invariant mass distribution of the final meson pairs. A final state interaction form factor is obtained which incorporates $f_0$ and $a_0$ mesons, $K\bar K$ threshold effects, and $f_0-a_0$ mixing. This form factor is applied to predict the effective mass distribution of the $ππ$ and $K\bar K$ pairs in the vicinity of the $K\bar K$ threshold. An estimate of the role of isospin mixing near threshold is provided. The potential role of polarized photons and protons is also discussed.

## Key claim
The paper argues that isospin-violating mixing between the $f_0$ and $a_0$ scalar mesons, driven by the 8 MeV mass difference between charged and neutral kaons, produces measurable effects in threshold photoproduction that could distinguish between competing models of these mesons' internal structure. The authors hedge that their doorway-state approach is complementary to, rather than a replacement for, existing final-state interaction models (Ji, 1997; MOT, 1999).

## Method
The authors derived general CGLN-type invariant amplitudes for scalar meson photoproduction and constructed a coupled-channel final-state interaction form factor as a $2 \times 2$ matrix in $f_0$-$a_0$ space. This form factor incorporates the $K^+K^-$ and $K^0\bar{K}^0$ thresholds explicitly, with the 8 MeV threshold splitting generating off-diagonal mixing terms. They then computed the invariant mass distributions for $\pi^+\pi^-$ and $K^+K^-$ pairs near the $K\bar{K}$ threshold using parameters extracted from $\phi \rightarrow \gamma f_0/a_0$ data (Achasov, 1998; Novosibirsk group, 1999) and the photoproduction amplitude from MOT (1999).

## Key numbers
- The $K^+K^-$ and $K^0\bar{K}^0$ thresholds are split by 8 MeV, equal to $2(m_{K^0} - m_{K^\pm})$.
- The $f_0$ width $\Gamma_f$ is set to 0.1 GeV, with $E_f = 0.947$ GeV obtained by setting $m_x = 0.98$ GeV in the propagator.
- The coupling $|V_{f K^+ K^-}|^2 / 4\pi = 0.83$ GeV$^2$, corresponding to $V_{f K^+ K^-} = 3.23$ GeV.
- The ratio $R = |V_{f K\bar{K}}|^2 / |V_{f \pi\pi}|^2 = 3.77$, with $|V_{f \pi\pi}|^2 / 4\pi = 0.44$ GeV$^2$.
- The background for $d\sigma_{\pi^+\pi^-}/dm_x$ is estimated at approximately 55 $\mu$b/GeV (from MOT, 1999).
- The scalar meson mixing enhancement can be as large as 70% near threshold.

## Context
This work extends the study of $f_0$-$a_0$ mixing first noted by Achasov (1979) and later studied by Barnes (1990), applying it specifically to threshold photoproduction. The authors' doorway-state approach differs from the final-state interaction models of Ji (1997) and MOT (1999), where scalar mesons enter via $K\bar{K}$ and $\pi\pi$ rescattering rather than as directly produced states. The formalism enables future experimental tests at Jefferson Laboratory and Fermilab to distinguish between four-quark, molecular, and quark-antiquark models of the scalar mesons through precise measurement of the $K\bar{K}$ invariant mass distribution near threshold.
