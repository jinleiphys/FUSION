# Improved Polyakov-loop potential for effective models from functional calculations

> We investigate the quark backreaction on the Polyakov loop and its impact on the thermodynamics of quantum chromodynamics. The dynamics of the gluons generating the Polyakov-loop potential is altered by the presence of dynamical quarks. However, this backreaction of the quarks has not yet been taken into account in Polyakov-loop extended model studies. In the present work, we show within a 2+1 flavour Polyakov-quark-meson model that a quark-improved Polyakov-loop potential leads to a smoother transition between the low-temperature hadronic phase and the high-temperature quark-gluon plasma phase. In particular, we discuss the dependence of our results on the remaining uncertainties that are the critical temperature and the parametrisation of the Polyakov-loop potential as well as the mass of the sigma-meson.

## Key claim

The quark backreaction on the gluon dynamics, which alters the Polyakov-loop potential from its pure Yang-Mills form, leads to a smoother transition between the hadronic and quark-gluon plasma phases in a 2+1 flavour Polyakov-quark-meson model, though the quantitative results depend on remaining uncertainties in the critical temperature, the parametrisation of the Polyakov-loop potential, and the sigma-meson mass.

## Method

The authors used functional renormalisation group (FRG) flows to compute the nonperturbative Polyakov-loop potential for the glue sector of QCD with two massless quark flavours, including the quark contribution to the gluonic vacuum polarisation. They compared this QCD glue potential to the pure Yang-Mills potential and extracted a linear relation between the reduced temperature scales of the two systems. This relation was then applied to modify the Polyakov-loop potential in a 2+1 flavour Polyakov-quark-meson (PQM) model to account for the quark backreaction on the gauge sector.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature of the glue effective potential for two massless quark flavours is \(T^{\text{glue}}_{\text{cr}} = 203\ \text{MeV}\) (from the FRG calculation).
- The critical temperature of pure SU(3) Yang-Mills theory is \(T^{\text{YM}}_{\text{cr}} = 276\ \text{MeV}\).
- The relation between reduced temperatures is \(t_\mathrm{YM}(t_\mathrm{glue}) \approx 0.57\, t_\mathrm{glue}\).
- The glue critical temperature is treated as a free parameter in the range \(180\ \text{MeV} \lesssim T^{\text{glue}}_{\text{cr}} \lesssim 270\ \text{MeV}\).
- For 2+1 flavours (two massless, one massive strange quark with current mass 95 MeV), the estimated glue critical temperature is 182 MeV, based on a hard-thermal-loop estimate from Schaefer (2007).

## Context

This work extends earlier Polyakov-loop extended model studies (e.g., Schaefer 2007, Skokov 2010, Herbst 2010) that used a pure Yang-Mills Polyakov-loop potential fitted to lattice data, without including the backreaction of dynamical quarks on the gauge sector. The authors build on FRG calculations of the QCD phase diagram (Braun 2009, Pawlowski 2010) and nonperturbative Polyakov-loop potentials (Braun 2007, Marhauser 2008, Fister 2013). The improved potential enables more realistic comparisons with lattice QCD results and functional QCD studies, and can be used in PNJL and PQM models to study the QCD phase diagram at finite temperature and density.
