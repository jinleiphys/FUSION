# The "minimal" viscosity and elliptic flow at RHIC

> We show from covariant transport theory that, for a massless ideal gas equation of state, even a small shear viscosity to entropy density ratio $η\approx s/(4 π)$ generates significant 15-30% dissipative corrections to elliptic flow for conditions expected in mid-peripheral (b = 8 fm) Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} \sim 200$ GeV at RHIC.

## Key claim

Using covariant transport theory with a massless ideal gas equation of state, even a shear viscosity to entropy density ratio as low as η ≈ s/(4π) produces 15-30% dissipative corrections to elliptic flow in mid-peripheral Au+Au collisions at RHIC, contradicting the assumption that such a "minimal" viscosity could be ignored.

## Method

The author used covariant parton transport theory (Boltzmann equation with elastic 2→2 scattering) to simulate Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV and impact parameter b = 8 fm, implementing a transport cross section σtr(τ) ∝ τ^(2/3) that grows with time to maintain a constant η/s ≈ 1/(4π). The numerical solutions were obtained using the MPC algorithm, with initial conditions matching those of earlier work by Molnar and collaborators (2004), and results were compared to ideal hydrodynamic calculations from Kolb (2000) and Huovinen (2001).

## Key numbers

- Dissipative reduction of elliptic flow relative to ideal hydrodynamics: ~25% for thermalization time τ0 = 0.1 fm/c, and ~15% for τ0 = 0.6 fm/c, at moderate pT ~ 2-3 GeV
- Transport cross section set to σ0 ≈ 2.7 mb to achieve average η/s ≈ 1/(4π)
- Transport opacity χ ≈ 16 for the growing-cross-section calculation, giving λtr(τ0=0.1 fm) ~ 0.09-0.11 fm and 4πη/s ~ 0.8-1
- For the constant cross section case in earlier work (Molnar 2004), initial η/s ~ 1/(60π) at the collision center, with λtr(τ0=0.1 fm) ≈ 7.1×10^(-3) fm
- Navier-Stokes estimate: relative pressure correction Δp/p ~ 20% for τ0T0 = 0.6 fm × 0.385 GeV, and nearly 100% for τ0T0 = 0.1 fm × 0.7 GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier transport studies by Molnar and collaborators (2004) that used constant large cross sections (σ ~ 45 mb) and found 20-30% elliptic flow reduction, but with η/s increasing over time. Here the author instead maintains a constant η/s ≈ 1/(4π), the conjectured lower bound from AdS/CFT duality (Son 2002), and finds comparable dissipative effects. The results are similar in magnitude to recent 2+1D dissipative hydrodynamic calculations by Romatschke (2007) and Teaney (2007), but the transport approach avoids uncontrolled truncation errors of Israel-Stewart theory. This enables future studies with more realistic equations of state and inclusion of bulk viscosity effects near T ~ 200 MeV.
