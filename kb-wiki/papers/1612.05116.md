# Medium Recoils and background subtraction in JEWEL

> \textsc{Jewel} is a fully dynamical event generator for jet evolution in a dense QCD medium, which has been validated for multiple jet and jet-like observables. Jet constituents (partons) undergo collisions with thermal partons from the medium, leading to both elastic and radiative energy loss. The recoiling medium scattering centers carry away energy and momentum from the jet. Keeping track of these recoils is essential for the description of intra-jet observables. Since the thermal component of the recoils is part of the soft background activity, comparison with data on jet observables requires the implementation of a background subtraction procedure. We will show two independent procedures through which background subtraction can be performed and discuss the impact of the medium recoil on jet shape observables and jet-background correlations. Keeping track of the medium recoil significantly improves the \textsc{Jewel} description of jet shape measurements.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that properly accounting for medium recoils and implementing a background subtraction procedure in the JEWEL event generator significantly improves the description of jet shape measurements in heavy ion collisions, though the subtraction methods introduce systematic biases that must be quantified.

## Method

JEWEL simulates jet evolution through a dense QCD medium by having jet partons undergo elastic and inelastic collisions with thermal scattering centers, where the recoiling medium partons carry away energy and momentum. Two background subtraction procedures were implemented to remove the thermal component of the recoils: 4MomSub, which vectorially subtracts the four-momenta of matched scattering centers from the jet, and GridSub, which superimposes a finite resolution grid on the event and subtracts scattering center four-momenta from final state particles within each cell. The methods were validated by comparing jets from JEWEL with recoils and background subtraction to jets from JEWEL without recoils, and systematic uncertainties were estimated by varying the grid size by a factor of two.

## Key numbers

- Jet asymmetry studied for mid-rapidity jets with pT > 30 GeV/c, comparing GridSub and 4MomSub methods at the parton level
- Grid size optimally chosen as 0.05 in eta-phi, with systematics estimated by varying that by a factor of two
- Softdrop grooming used a momentum fraction threshold z_cut of 10% and beta = 0 (no angular constraint)
- CMS data on subjet groomed momentum fraction ratio (PbPb/pp) compared with JEWEL+Pythia predictions, showing more asymmetrical parton splittings in PbPb

## Context

This work extends the JEWEL event generator (Zapp, 2013) by implementing background subtraction procedures necessary for comparing with experimental data from CMS and ALICE. The paper addresses the mismatch between JEWEL with recoils and data that arises because experimental measurements systematically remove the fluctuating heavy ion underlying event, while JEWEL's recoil partons carry the thermal component of scattering centers. The results enable comparisons with CMS softdrop grooming measurements and predictions for inclusive jet mass in heavy ion collisions, though JEWEL along with other energy loss models over-predicts the charged jet mass.
