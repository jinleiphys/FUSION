# Axial and Vector Correlator Mixing in Hot and Dense Hadronic Matter

> We study the manifestations of chiral symmetry restoration which have a significance for the parity mixing. Restricting to pions and nucleons we establish a formalism for the expression of the vector correlator, which displays the mixing of the axial correlator into the vector one and unifies the cases of the heat bath and the dense medium. We give examples of mixing cross-sections. We also establish a link between the energy integrated mixing cross-sections and the pion scalar density which governs the quenching factors of coupling constants, such as the pion decay one, as well as the quark condensate evolution.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a unified formalism for axial-vector correlator mixing in both hot and dense hadronic matter, showing that the mixing cross-sections are linked to the pion scalar density which governs chiral symmetry restoration; the formalism treats the heat bath and dense nuclear medium on the same footing, but the authors note that nuclear pions are virtual and not soft (their momentum is a few hundred MeV/c), which constitutes a major difference from the thermal case.

## Method

The authors start from a chiral Lagrangian in the Weinberg representation restricted to pions and nucleons, deriving expressions for the vector current and the photon self-energy in the nuclear medium. They compute the forward Compton amplitude for both the free proton and the nucleus, using once-subtracted dispersion relations to connect the high-energy seagull terms to the energy-integrated photoabsorption cross-section. The vector correlator is expressed in terms of the axial correlator and pion propagators, with the mixing terms identified as those where the photon attaches to a pion or to a pion-nucleon state through the Kroll-Ruderman and photoelectric terms.

## Key numbers

* Pion scalar density for the free proton: total pion number N_pi = m_pi * integral of <N|phi^2|N> = 0.35
* Pionic piece of the nucleon sigma commutator: Sigma_N^(pi) = 25 MeV (with 9 MeV coming from the Delta)
* Pion scalar density at normal nuclear density rho_0: scalar pion number = 0.38 per nucleon
* Quenching factor of coupling constants (e.g., pion decay constant) at normal nuclear density: r(rho_0) = 0.86
* Thomson amplitude: -e^2/M with e^2 = 1/137 and M the proton mass

## Context

This work extends the earlier formalism of Chanfray et al. (1996) for dense matter and Dey et al. (1990) for the heat bath, unifying both cases into a single framework applicable to heavy ion collisions where both density and temperature effects are present. The authors show that the mixing cross-sections can be extracted from photoabsorption data, and they connect the results to the quenching of the Gamow-Teller sum rule and to the excess of low-mass electron pairs in heavy ion collisions. The formalism enables future work on the simultaneous treatment of thermal and baryonic effects on the rho meson excitation.
