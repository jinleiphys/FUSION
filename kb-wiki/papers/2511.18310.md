# Proton mass decompositions in the NNLO QCD

> Proton matrix elements of the QCD energy-momentum tensor (EMT) are expressed by the gravitational form factors. The forward values of the gravitational form factors allow for a decomposition of the proton mass into contributions from quarks and gluons, and further subdivisions into contributions from quark masses and from the QCD trace anomaly may be considered. We present the most recent evaluations of these mass decompositions, using a recent quantitative evaluation of the forward values of relevant gravitational form factors at the next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) QCD. We also calculate the renormalization scale dependence of each component within these decompositions. Furthermore, similar calculations are performed with another decomposition of the proton mass, organized strictly according to the separation into the traceless part and trace part for each of the gauge-invariant quark/gluon parts of the EMT, such that the former (twist-two) quark/gluon contributions of the EMT embody the effects of the partonic motions inside the proton, while the latter (twist-four) contributions are induced as parton correlations by non-perturbative QCD interactions. We demonstrate the advantages of this new decomposition. We also present the results for the pion, which exhibit quite different parton-correlation behaviors from the proton.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first evaluations of proton mass decompositions at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) QCD, achieving a few percent accuracy, but the results depend significantly on the renormalization scale and on which decomposition scheme is chosen; the authors advocate a new "strict twist decomposition" that separates traceless (twist-two) and trace (twist-four) parts for each gauge-invariant quark/gluon component of the energy-momentum tensor, which avoids the strong scale dependence and incomplete separation issues present in earlier decompositions.

## Method

The forward values of the gravitational form factors A_r(0,μ) and C̄_r(0,μ) for quarks and gluons (r=q,g) were evaluated at NNLO using three-loop renormalization group equations in the MS-like schemes, with A_q(0,μ) determined from NNLO global PDF analysis (CT18) and C̄_r(0,μ) computed from the trace anomaly relations combined with empirical sigma terms. These form factors were then inserted into the standard four-term decomposition of Ji (1994) and into a new decomposition that strictly separates the traceless and trace parts of each quark and gluon EMT component, with results computed over the scale range 0.7 GeV ≲ μ ≲ 20 GeV for both the proton and the pion.

## Key numbers

- At μ = 2 GeV with n_f = 3: A_q(0, μ₀) = 0.57(1), A_g(0, μ₀) = 0.43(1)
- At μ = 2 GeV with n_f = 3: C̄_q(0, μ₀) = -C̄_g(0, μ₀) = -0.163(4)
- Pion-nucleon sigma term σ_πN = 0.0591(35) GeV from Hoferichter (2015)
- Strangeness content σ_s = 0.0456(62) GeV from Alexandrou (2019)
- Charm content σ_c = 0.107(22) GeV from Alexandrou (2019)
- At μ = 2 GeV, Ji's four-term decomposition gives M = [0.41(1) + 0.59(1)]M for the U_q + U_g two-term split

## Context

This work extends the model-independent framework for gravitational form factors developed by Hatta (2018) and Tanaka (2018, 2022) to provide the first NNLO evaluations of proton mass decompositions, updating earlier leading-order analyses by Ji (1994, 2021), Lorce (2017), and Metz (2020). The new strict twist decomposition enables comparison with lattice QCD calculations by Yang (2018) and Liu (2021, 2023), and the pion results reveal that partonic motion effects are similar between proton and pion while parton-correlation (twist-four) effects differ significantly, which is relevant for the upcoming Electron-Ion Collider program described by Accardi (2012) and Abdul Khalek (2021).
