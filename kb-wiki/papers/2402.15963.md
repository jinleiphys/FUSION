# Transport properties of asymmetric nuclear matter in the spinodal region

> We have studied the shear and bulk viscosities of asymmetric nuclear matter in the mechanical and chemical instability region based on IBUU transport simulations in a box system. The Green-Kubo method is used to calculate these viscosities with a prepared dynamically equilibrated nuclear system with hot clusters. While the behavior of the shear viscosity is largely affected by energy-dependent nucleon-nucleon cross sections, the bulk viscosity increases significantly in the presence of nuclear clusters compared to that in uniform nuclear matter. Increasing isospin asymmetry generally increases both viscosities, while their behaviors are qualitatively modified once the isospin asymmetry is large enough to affect significantly the spinodal region. Our calculation shows that the bulk viscosity is more sensitive to the nuclear clustering than the shear viscosity, and is thus a robust quantity related to the phase diagram of asymmetric nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The shear and bulk viscosities of asymmetric nuclear matter in the spinodal region are calculated via the Green-Kubo method from IBUU transport simulations, with the key qualifier that the bulk viscosity increases dramatically (by 2-3 orders of magnitude) in the presence of nuclear clusters compared to uniform matter, while the shear viscosity shows a more nuanced behavior that depends on temperature and the energy dependence of nucleon-nucleon cross sections.

## Method

The authors performed IBUU (isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck) transport simulations in a cubic box of size 20 × 20 × 20 fm³ with periodic boundary conditions, using a lattice Hamiltonian framework for mean-field evolution and energy- and isospin-dependent differential nucleon-nucleon cross sections from free-space data. Dynamically equilibrated systems containing hot nuclear clusters were prepared by starting from uniform matter in the spinodal region, allowing cluster formation, then resetting the temperature at t = 500 fm/c and evolving until equilibrium at t = 1250 fm/c. The shear and bulk viscosities were then extracted via the Green-Kubo formulas by fitting the time correlation functions of the energy-momentum tensor components, exponential decay for shear, and exponentially decaying sinusoidal functions for bulk.

## Key numbers

- Box size: 20 × 20 × 20 fm³
- Saturation density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Mean-field parameters: α = −0.218 GeV, β = 0.164 GeV, γ = 4/3, incompressibility K₀ = 237 MeV, symmetry energy 30.3 MeV at saturation, slope parameter L = 60 MeV
- Bulk viscosity ζ is 2–3 orders of magnitude larger in non-uniform systems (with clusters) than in uniform nuclear matter
- Initial conditions studied include densities ρ = 0.1, 0.3, and 0.6 ρ₀, temperatures T = 5, 10, 15 MeV, and isospin asymmetries δ = 0, 0.2, and 0.6
- For δ = 0.6, the spinodal region shrinks dramatically compared to δ = 0 and 0.2, causing ζ and ζ/s to drop at lower temperatures

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of shear viscosity in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter near the liquid-gas phase transition (Hua 2023) to include bulk viscosity and isospin asymmetry. It builds on the Green-Kubo method formalism (Kubo 1957, 1966) and the IBUU transport model calibrated by the Transport Model Evaluation Project (Colonna 2021, Zhang 2017). The results enable future studies of how transport properties, particularly bulk viscosity as a robust quantity sensitive to clustering, relate to the phase diagram of asymmetric nuclear matter, with implications for heavy-ion collisions and neutron-star mergers.
