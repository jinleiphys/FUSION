# Single- and double-beta decay Fermi-transitions in an exactly solvable model

> An exactly solvable model suitable for the description of single and double-beta decay processes of the Fermi-type is introduced. The model is equivalent to the exact shell-model treatment of protons and neutrons in a single j-shell. Exact eigenvalues and eigenvectors are compared to those corresponding to the hamiltonian in the quasiparticle basis (qp) and with the results of both the standard quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) and the renormalized one (RQRPA). The role of the scattering term of the quasiparticle hamiltonian is analyzed. The presence of an exact eigenstate with zero energy is shown to be related to the collapse of the QRPA. The RQRPA and the qp solutions do not include this zero-energy eigenvalue in their spectra, probably due to spurious correlations. The meaning of this result in terms of symmetries is presented.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the collapse of the standard QRPA for double-beta decay calculations coincides with the exact model's prediction of a zero-energy eigenstate, while the RQRPA and quasiparticle treatments avoid this collapse but produce spurious results due to the absence of this zero-energy mode.

## Method

The authors constructed an exactly solvable model equivalent to a single-j shell-model treatment of protons and neutrons, using an SO(5) algebraic framework to diagonalize a Hamiltonian containing single-particle terms, pairing terms, and charge-dependent residual interactions with both particle-hole and particle-particle channels. They compared exact eigenvalues and eigenvectors with those obtained from the quasiparticle basis Hamiltonian, the standard QRPA, and the renormalized QRPA (RQRPA), analyzing the role of the scattering term in the quasiparticle Hamiltonian and the average number of quasiparticles in the ground state.

## Key numbers

- For set I parameters (j=9/2, N=10, G=0.4 MeV), the excitation energy of the lowest 0+ state in the double-odd-mass nucleus goes to zero when 4κ/G ≈ 1.6 (χ=0) or 1.8 (χ=0.04 MeV)
- For set II parameters (j=19/2, N=20, G=0.2 MeV), the excitation energy goes to zero when 4κ/G ≈ 1.3 (χ=0 or 0.025 MeV)
- The QRPA collapses near the point where exact excitation energies become negative, with the QRPA eigenvalue given by E_QRPA = [(2ε + λ₁)² - 4λ₂²]^(1/2) becoming purely imaginary when 2λ₂ > 2ε + λ₁
- The RQRPA renormalization factor D = [1 + 2Y²/(2j+1)]⁻¹ keeps the RQRPA energy always real by reducing the residual interaction

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies using the schematic proton-neutron Lipkin model (Hirsch 1996a) and an earlier letter introducing the exactly solvable model (Hirsch 1996b). It directly addresses the well-known instability problem in QRPA calculations of two-neutrino double-beta decay matrix elements, particularly for 100Mo as studied by Vogel (1986), Engel (1987), Civitarese (1987, 1991), and Muto (1989). The results contradict the claim that RQRPA (as formulated by Toivanen (1995)) provides a reliable cure for QRPA instabilities, showing instead that the RQRPA introduces spurious correlations and overestimates ground-state correlations compared to exact solutions. The work enables future studies of the dynamical symmetry restoration mechanism and suggests that the QRPA's collapse signals a genuine physical phase transition rather than a mathematical artifact.
