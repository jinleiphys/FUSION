# Study of the $η-^7Be$ Interaction Near Threshold in $p-^6Li$ Fusion

> We present a calculation for $η$ production in the $p-^6Li$ fusion near threshold including the $η-^7Be$ final state interaction (FSI). We consider the $^6Li$ and $^7Be$ nuclei as $α-d$ and $α-^3He$ clusters respectively. The calculations are done for the lowest states of $^7Be$ with $J = ({3\over2}^-, {1\over2}^-)$ resulting from the $L = $1 radial wave function. The $η-^7Be$ interaction is incorporated through the $η-^7Be$ $T-$matrix, constructed from the medium modified matrices for the $η-^3He$ and $η-α$ systems. These medium modified matrices are obtained by solving few body equations, where the scattering in nuclear medium is taken into account.

## Key claim
The calculation shows that including the η–⁷Be final state interaction (FSI) drastically changes both the shape and magnitude of the total cross section for p–⁶Li fusion near threshold, but the authors note that contributions from excited states of ⁷Be and more detailed FSI treatment are still in progress, so the present results are not yet conclusive.

## Method
The authors model ⁶Li and ⁷Be as α–d and α–³He clusters, respectively, and compute the production T-matrix for the p–⁶Li → η–⁷Be reaction. The η–⁷Be FSI is incorporated via a half-off-shell T-matrix constructed from medium-modified η–³He and η–α scattering matrices, obtained by solving few-body equations with the factorized impulse approximation. Two prescriptions for the cluster wave functions are used: a Wood-Saxon cluster model and a Green’s function Monte Carlo variational wave function with the Urbana potential.

## Key numbers
- The ηN t-matrix used corresponds to a scattering length a_ηN = 0.88 + i0.41 fm.
- The lowest states of ⁷Be considered are the (3/2⁻, 1/2⁻) doublet, split by 0.48 MeV.
- At an excess energy of ~19 MeV, the calculated differential cross section for the low-lying L=1 states of ⁷Be is ~1 nb/sr, compared to the experimental value of 4.6 ± 3.8 nb/sr from the Turin group (1993).
- The incident proton energy for the experimental comparison is 683 MeV (Turin group) and 673 MeV (COSY Laboratory).

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of η interactions with light nuclei, specifically the deuteron (B. K. Jain, year not given) and ³He (Kelkar et al., year not given), to the heavier ⁷Be nucleus. It revisits the p–⁶Li → η–⁷Be reaction previously studied by the Turin group (1993) and later at COSY (Machner et al., year not given), where earlier analysis could not conclude the formation of an η–⁷Be quasi-bound state. The present calculation enables future work including contributions from excited states of ⁷Be and more detailed FSI treatment.
