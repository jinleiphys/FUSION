# Electron-Positron Pair Production in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> In recent years, a large number of papers have appeared that dealt with $\EPEM$ pair production in heavy ion collisions at high energies. To a large extent these studies were motivated by the existence of relativistic heavy ion accelerators all over the world. There pair production can be studied in so called ``ultra-peripheral collisions'', where the ions do not come close enough to interact strongly with each other. Various different methods have been used and it is the purpose of this review to present a unified picture of the present status of the field.

## Key claim

This review argues that electron-positron pair production in ultraperipheral relativistic heavy ion collisions can be well understood within QED perturbation theory, despite the very strong electromagnetic fields involved, because the interaction time is too short for non-perturbative effects to develop. The authors conclude that after 17 years of theoretical and experimental work, the answer to the question "Can RHIC be used to test QED?" is "no", meaning no significant deviation from perturbative QED has been found.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical treatment of electron-positron pair production in the Coulomb field of a single nucleus (the Bethe-Heitler process) and then extend this to the two-nucleus case relevant for heavy ion colliders. They compare two complementary approaches: the use of Sommerfeld-Maue wave functions (following Bethe and Maximon) which sum Feynman diagrams to infinite order, and the direct perturbative QED approach of Ivanov and Melnikov (1998) which uses Sudakov variables and impact-parameter space methods to sum multi-photon exchange diagrams. The review also compares these theoretical results with experimental data from RHIC for both free pair production and bound-free pair production.

## Key numbers

- The Bethe-Heitler total cross section for photon-nucleus pair production in the high-energy limit (no screening) is given by σ₁ = (28/9)(α³Z²/m²)(log(2ω/m) - 109/42), where ω is the photon energy and m is the electron mass.
- The Coulomb correction to the Bethe-Heitler cross section is given by the function f(Zα) = γ_E + Re Ψ(1+iZα) = (Zα)² Σ_{n=1}^{∞} 1/(n(n²+(Zα)²)), where γ_E = 0.57721 is the Euler-Mascheroni constant.
- The average impact parameter for pair production accompanied by nuclear breakup at RHIC is approximately 19 fm, given by 8R_a/3 where R_a is the nuclear radius.
- The STAR experiment at RHIC measured Au-Au collisions at a center-of-mass energy of 200 GeV per nucleon, with detector acceptance for electrons and positrons with transverse momentum p_t > 60 MeV/c and rapidity |y| < 1.15.
- For the LHC Pb-Pb mode, the nominal peak luminosity is L = 1.0 × 10²⁷ cm⁻² s⁻¹, and simulations indicate the secondary beam from bound-free pair production is not likely to quench a dipole magnet.
- The bound-free pair production cross section used for LHC simulations comes from calculations by Meier et al. (2001), with the highest-energy experimental test coming from a fixed-target measurement at 160 GeV per nucleon (γ = 9.3).

## Context

This review extends and unifies earlier reviews by Baur, Hencken, and Trautmann (1998, 2002) and Bertulani, Klein, and Nystrand (2005), covering the period when RHIC came into operation and provided experimental data. The theoretical framework builds on the classic Bethe-Maximon solution (1954) for the Bethe-Heitler process, and the work of Ivanov and Melnikov (1998) who showed the equivalence between the Sommerfeld-Maue wave function approach and direct summation of Feynman diagrams. The review enables future measurements at the LHC, particularly for using pair production as a luminosity monitor and for studying multiple pair production, which is predicted to contribute about 10% of all pairs produced together with nuclear excitation at LHC energies.
