# Hybrid stars within the framework of the Sigma-Omega-Rho model combined with the MIT and NJL models

> In this paper, we investigate the structure of hybrid stars consisting of hadrons (neutrons, protons, sigmas, lambdas), leptons (electrons, muons), and quarks (up, down, strange). We use a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model namely the Sigma-omega-rho model for the hadronic phase and the MIT bag model as well as the NJL model for the quark phase. In addition, Maxwell and Gibbs conditions are employed to investigate the hadron-Quark phase transition. Finally, by obtaining the mass-radius relation, $ M (M_{sun}) \leqslant 2.07 $ is predicted for such hybrid stars.

## Key claim
The paper predicts that hybrid stars (containing both hadrons and quarks) have a maximum mass of at most 2.07 solar masses, but this result depends on the specific combination of hadronic model parameters (the hyperon coupling parameter zeta) and quark model parameters (the bag constant B or NJL parameter set), as well as on whether the Maxwell or Gibbs construction is used for the hadron-quark phase transition.

## Method
The authors used the Sigma-omega-rho relativistic mean-field model for the hadronic phase, including neutrons, protons, sigma and lambda hyperons, electrons, and muons, with hyperon coupling constants scaled by a parameter zeta. For the quark phase, they employed both the MIT bag model (with bag constant B between 80 and 200 MeV/fm^3) and the three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with three different parameter sets (RKH, HK, LKW). They applied both Maxwell and Gibbs phase transition criteria to construct the hybrid star equation of state, then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain the mass-radius relation.

## Key numbers
- Maximum hybrid star mass predicted: M less than or equal to 2.07 solar masses (from the abstract)
- The most massive neutron star PSR J0952-0607 has mass 2.35 solar masses (cited from reference r01)
- The neutron star PSR J0740+6620 has mass above two solar masses (cited from reference r02)
- MIT bag model bag constant range considered: 80 MeV fm^{-3} less than or equal to B less than or equal to 200 MeV fm^{-3}
- For the MIT bag model with Maxwell construction, phase transition occurs at densities rho = 7-8 rho_0 when zeta = -0.5
- For Gibbs construction with the MIT bag model, the mixed phase starting density is around rho = 0.5 fm^{-3}
- NJL model cutoff parameters: Lambda_c = 602.3 MeV (RKH), 631.4 MeV (HK), 750 MeV (LKW)
- NJL model bare quark masses: m_{u,d} = 5.5 MeV (RKH and HK), 3.6 MeV (LKW); m_s = 140.7 MeV (RKH), 135.7 MeV (HK), 87 MeV (LKW)
- MIT bag model strange quark mass considered: m_s = 150 MeV and 300 MeV

## Context
This work extends previous studies of hybrid stars by combining the Sigma-omega-rho RMF model (with hyperon coupling corrections from reference r3) with both the MIT bag model and the NJL model, comparing Maxwell and Gibbs phase transition constructions. It builds on earlier work using other hadronic models such as the TM1 and IUFSU RMF parameter sets (references z1, z2), the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock many-body theory (reference 04000fe), and the quark-meson coupling model (references r05, e02). The results enable comparison with observational constraints from massive neutron stars like PSR J0952-0607 and PSR J0740+6620, and with gravitational-wave detections (reference fe12).
