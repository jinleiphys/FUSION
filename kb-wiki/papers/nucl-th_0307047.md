# Modelling nuclear effects in neutrino interactions in 1 GeV region

> We evaluate nuclear effects in neutrino reactions in a framework based on a model proposed by Marteau with quasi-elastic and $Δ$ production processes treated together. Nuclear effects include RPA corrections and $Δ$ width modification in nuclear matter.

## Key claim
Nuclear effects in neutrino-nucleus reactions near 1 GeV, including RPA corrections and delta width modification in nuclear matter, can reduce the charged-current pion production cross section by about 25%, but the quasi-elastic cross section is changed very little by RPA corrections; the author notes that the model omits 2p-2h excitations and non-resonant pion production, and that double counting with final-state interaction models in existing Monte Carlo codes must be understood.

## Method
The author modifies the Marteau model, which treats quasi-elastic and delta excitation processes together in a non-relativistic Fermi gas with RPA corrections from contact interactions, pion and rho meson exchange, and delta width modification from Oset-Salcedo results. Modifications include using a constant Fermi momentum of 225 MeV, relativistic nucleon kinematics via a relativistic Lindhard function, and adapting Oset's delta width to the neutrino kinematics (where the four-momentum transfer squared is negative). The inclusive cross section is computed for oxygen-16, and exclusive channels are separated by splitting the delta width into a pion-decay part and a part from nuclear absorption channels.

## Key numbers
- Fermi momentum kept constant at \(k_F = 225\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Delta width at rest: \(\Gamma_0 = 115\ \mathrm{MeV}\), delta mass \(M_\Delta = 1232\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The approximation of treating the target nucleon as at rest in the hadronic spin sums is accurate to 2-3% for quasi-elastic and very good for delta excitation.
- The prescription for the delta width in the neutrino case increases the imaginary part of the delta self-energy by 5-10% relative to the photon-nucleon value.
- RPA corrections reduce the total charged-current pion production cross section significantly, by about 25%.
- For quasi-elastic scattering, inclusion of delta-hole excitations increases the cross section for neutrino energies above 0.8 GeV, bringing it close to the Fermi gas result.
- The longitudinal part of the pion production differential cross section is reduced by order of 50% by RPA, with some increase at smaller energy transfer.

## Context
This work extends the Marteau model (Marteau, 1999) for neutrino-nucleus interactions, which itself treats quasi-elastic and delta excitation on equal footing, by simplifying the local density treatment to a constant Fermi momentum and adopting relativistic kinematics. The results are compared to the standard approach of combining the Rein-Sehgal pion production model (Rein and Sehgal, 1981) with a Fermi gas model, and the author notes that the model must be supplemented with non-resonant pion production (Fogli and Nardulli, 1979) and that local density effects can be investigated by averaging over Fermi momenta. The work enables more accurate implementation of nuclear effects in Monte Carlo codes for neutrino experiments, but the author cautions that double counting with final-state interaction models must be resolved.
