# Entanglement Negativity of Spin-Orbit Correlations in a general Qubit-Qudit Setup

> We present the complete eigenvalue spectrum of the partially transposed density matrix for a pure bipartite quantum state acting on a generic $2 \otimes n$ Hilbert space. The spectrum contains four non-zero eigenvalues, as, \begin{eqnarray} λ_{1,2}=\pm \sqrt{A}, ~~~ λ_{3,4}= \frac{1}{2}(1\pm\sqrt{1-4 A}), \nonumber \end{eqnarray} where $A$ is the determinant of the reduced density matrix (traced over the larger subspace). As $0 \leqslant A \leqslant1/4$, only one is negative among the four non-trivial eigenvalues. Within this qubit-qudit framework, we further studied the negativity as a measure of entanglement for the case of spin-orbit correlation of partons inside a proton. The entanglement negativity for spin-orbit correlations is found to be related to the gluon helicity PDF and the Hermitian angle of the associated Hilbert space for linearly polarized protons.

## Key claim

The paper derives the complete eigenvalue spectrum of the partially transposed density matrix for any pure bipartite state in a general $2 \otimes n$ Hilbert space, finding that only one of the four non-zero eigenvalues is negative. The authors then apply this result to spin-orbit correlations of partons inside a proton, but the connection to gluon helicity PDF and Hermitian angle is presented only for the specific case of linearly polarized protons, not for all hadronic states.

## Method

The authors construct a pure bipartite state in a $2 \otimes (2l+1)$ Hilbert space representing spin (qubit) and orbital angular momentum (qudit) degrees of freedom. They compute the partial transpose of the density matrix with respect to the larger (OAM) subspace and analytically solve for its eigenvalues. The determinant $A$ of the reduced density matrix (traced over the OAM subspace) is identified as the single parameter controlling all four non-zero eigenvalues. The negativity is then expressed in terms of the norms and Hermitian angle between the two complex vectors $\bm c_+$ and $\bm c_-$ that define the state coefficients.

## Key numbers

* The four non-zero eigenvalues of the partially transposed density matrix are $\lambda_{1,2} = \pm \sqrt{A}$ and $\lambda_{3,4} = \frac{1}{2}(1 \pm \sqrt{1-4A})$, where $A = \det(\rho_l)$ and $0 \leqslant A \leqslant 1/4$.
* For maximally entangled states ($A = 1/4$), the three positive eigenvalues become degenerate at $1/2$ and the negative eigenvalue saturates to $-1/2$.
* The concurrence and negativity are related as ${\cal C}(\rho) = 2{\cal N}^2(\rho)$ for the qubit-qudit system.
* For polarized hadrons, the negativity is ${\cal N}(\rho) = \frac{1}{2}\left(1 - \frac{(\Delta g(x))^2}{g^2(x)}\right)^{1/2} \sin\theta_H$, where $\Delta g(x)$ is the gluon helicity PDF and $g(x)$ is the gluon PDF.
* The maximum entanglement entropy reaches $\ln 2$ at $A = 1/4$.

## Context

This work extends the study of entanglement in proton structure beyond entanglement entropy, which has been the focus of most prior work in the interface of quantum information science and quantum chromodynamics (Kutak 2011, Peschanski 2012, Kharzeev 2017, Armesto 2019). The authors build directly on the spin-orbit correlation framework of Hatta (2024) and Bhattacharya (2024), who argued that partons in unpolarized hadrons are maximally entangled. The results enable experimental tests of entanglement in polarized scattering events at future facilities such as the Electron-Ion Collider, and connect entanglement measures to measurable parton distribution functions.
