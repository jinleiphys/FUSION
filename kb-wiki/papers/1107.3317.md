# Fluctuation Probes of Early-Time Correlations in Nuclear Collisions

> Correlation measurements imply that anisotropic flow in nuclear collisions includes a novel triangular component along with the more familiar elliptic-flow contribution. Triangular flow has been attributed to event-wise fluctuations in the initial shape of the collision volume. We ask two questions: 1) How do these shape fluctuations impact other event-by-event observables? 2) Can we disentangle fundamental information on the early time fluctuations from the complex flow that results? We study correlation and fluctuation observables in a framework in which flux tubes in an early Glasma stage later produce hydrodynamic flow. Calculated multiplicity and transverse momentum fluctuations are in excellent agreement with data from 62.4 GeV Au+Au up to 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb.

# Fluctuation Probes of Early-Time Correlations in Nuclear Collisions

## Key claim

Geometric shape fluctuations of the collision volume alone cannot explain measured multiplicity and transverse momentum fluctuations; the authors argue that dynamic multiplicity fluctuations from an early Glasma stage provide the necessary additional correlation strength, and that the product R dN/dy depends only on the saturation scale Q_s, constituting a scaling relation that can be tested across beam energies and centralities.

## Method

The authors develop a framework in which flux tubes in an early Glasma stage produce density correlations that later evolve via hydrodynamic flow, using a blast-wave model with boost-invariant longitudinal expansion and a Cooper-Frye freeze-out prescription. They derive integral relationships connecting the correlation function to fluctuation observables R (dynamic multiplicity variance) and <delta p_t1 delta p_t2> (transverse momentum covariance), showing that anisotropic flow contributions vanish upon angular integration in these fluctuation observables. They then compute R from Color Glass Condensate scaling relations and <delta p_t1 delta p_t2> from the same framework, constraining blast-wave parameters by fitting measured <p_t> and v_2 at each beam energy and centrality.

## Key numbers

- Calculated multiplicity and transverse momentum fluctuations are compared with data from 62.4 GeV Au+Au up to 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions
- At 2.76 TeV, the velocity is scaled up from the 200 GeV values by 6% and the temperature is scaled up by 7%
- The measured <p_t>_ch / <p_t>_pi ratio at LHC is approximately 1.13 using measured K/pi and p/pi ratios, while PYTHIA gives approximately 1.07
- The blast-wave parameters for temperature (in GeV) and average velocity are shown as functions of centrality, based on data from Kiyomichi (2005)
- The product R dN/dy is predicted as a function of N_part at three beam energies (62 GeV, 200 GeV, 2.76 TeV) with the dimensionless coefficient kappa fixed to fit the near-side ridge as in Gavin (2008) and Moschelli (2009)

## Context

This work extends the Color Glass Condensate description of the ridge developed by Dumitru (2008), Gavin (2008), and Moschelli (2009), where flux-tube correlations followed by transverse flow produce long-range rapidity correlations. The authors build on the realization by Alver and Roland (2010) that triangular flow v_3 from shape fluctuations is needed to describe correlation data, and on the fluctuation formalism of Pruneau (2002) that connects correlation functions to fluctuation observables. The results enable disentangling early-time Glasma information from hydrodynamic flow effects by using multiplicity and transverse momentum fluctuations that are insensitive to anisotropic flow, complementing traditional v_n analyses.
