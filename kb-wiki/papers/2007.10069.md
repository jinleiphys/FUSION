# Constraining dense matter physics using f-mode oscillations in neutron stars

> In this undergraduate project, f-mode oscillations in neutron stars are used to constrain the equation of state of dense matter. For the first time, a systematic investigation of the role of nuclear saturation parameters on the mode oscillations is performed. It is found that the uncertainty in the determination of effective nucleon mass plays the most significant role in controlling the f-mode frequencies. Correlations of the frequencies with astrophysical observables relevant for asteroseismology are also investigated. Future detection of f-mode frequencies could then provide a unique way of constraining nuclear empirical parameters and therefore the behaviour of dense matter.

## Key claim
For the first time, a systematic investigation of the role of nuclear saturation parameters on f-mode oscillations in neutron stars is performed, finding that the uncertainty in the effective nucleon mass is the most significant factor controlling f-mode frequencies, while other saturation parameters have negligible or small effects.

## Method
The authors used the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) model to generate equations of state (EoS) for neutron star matter, systematically varying each nuclear saturation parameter within ranges allowed by current experimental data. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equations to obtain neutron star structure, then computed f-mode oscillation frequencies in the Cowling approximation (neglecting metric perturbations) by solving the fluid perturbation equations for non-rotating neutron stars.

## Key numbers
- f-mode frequencies for the GM1 and GM3 parameter sets lie within 1-3 kHz, compatible with previous literature
- For varying effective nucleon mass m*/m between 0.55 and 0.75, f-mode frequencies vary between 2 and 2.6 kHz as a function of neutron star mass
- Linear fit for l=2 f-mode frequency (in kHz) as function of (Mbar/Rbar^3)^(1/2): y = 0.6397 + 1.6243 x (for effective mass variation)
- Linear fit for l=2 f-mode frequency (in kHz): y = 0.6499 + 1.6248 x (for saturation density variation)
- Linear fit for l=3 f-mode frequency (in kHz): y = 0.7510 + 2.9032 x (for effective mass variation)
- Linear fit for l=4 f-mode frequency (in kHz): y = 0.8454 + 2.4882 x (for effective mass variation)
- Tidal deformability Lambda varies in the range approximately 1-10 for corresponding f-mode frequencies of 2-2.6 kHz
- Lower limit of tidal deformability approximately 160 (from terrestrial nuclear experiments) and upper limit approximately 580 (from GW170817)

## Context
This work extends previous studies of f-mode asteroseismology that used polytropic or parametrized EoSs (Andersson 1996, Schutz) or only a few representative realistic EoS parameter sets (Andersson 1998, Doneva 2013). Unlike prior work, this study systematically explores the full RMF parameter space allowed by current nuclear experimental data. The results enable future detection of f-mode frequencies to directly constrain the effective nucleon mass and thus the dense matter equation of state, particularly in conjunction with tidal deformability measurements from gravitational wave events like GW170817 (Abbott 2017a).
