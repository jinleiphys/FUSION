# Nuclear Partition Functions at Temperatures Exceeding 10^10 K

> Nuclear partition functions were calculated for a grid of temperatures from 1.2x10^10 K to 2.75x10^11 K (1<=kT<=24 MeV) within a Fermi-gas approach, including all nuclides from the proton-dripline to the neutron-dripline with proton number 9<=Z<=85. The calculation is based on a nuclear level density description published elsewhere, thus extending the previous tables of partition functions beyond 10^10 K. Additional high temperature corrections had to be applied.

## Key claim
Nuclear partition functions for temperatures from 1.2×10¹⁰ K to 2.75×10¹¹ K (1 ≤ kT ≤ 24 MeV) were calculated using a Fermi-gas approach, extending previous tables beyond 10¹⁰ K, but only after applying additional high-temperature corrections that suppress the partition function by factors ranging from a few percent up to 10⁻⁴⁰ for the heaviest nuclides at the highest temperatures.

## Method
The partition function was computed by summing Boltzmann-weighted experimental discrete levels up to the last known state, then integrating over the nuclear level density from Rauscher, Thielemann, and Kratz (1997) for higher excitation energies. High-temperature corrections were applied by multiplying the uncorrected partition function by a correction factor C derived from the spherical square-well formalism of Tubbs and Koonin (1979), using the same particle separation energies and nuclear radii as in the earlier reaction-rate tabulation. The integration cut-off was set to the maximum of 35 MeV or the peak energy plus the full-width at half-maximum of the integrand, with the peak energy following E_peak = 0.0725 T₉²·⁰⁵⁵ MeV and the width Γ_FWHM = 3 T₉ – 37.0 MeV for T₉ > 12.

## Key numbers
- Temperature grid: 1.2×10¹⁰ K to 2.75×10¹¹ K (1 ≤ kT ≤ 24 MeV).
- Nuclide range: proton number 9 ≤ Z ≤ 85, from proton dripline to neutron dripline.
- Peak energy of the integrand for T₉ > 12: E_peak = 0.0725 T₉²·⁰⁵⁵ MeV.
- Width of the integrand for T₉ > 12: Γ_FWHM = 3 T₉ – 37.0 MeV.
- Integration cut-off: E_max = max(35, E_peak + Γ_FWHM) MeV.
- Corrections become non-negligible at T₉ ≈ 50–60 for light and intermediate nuclei, and as low as T₉ ≈ 14 for heavy nuclei.
- Suppression factor for heaviest nuclides: 10⁻⁵ at T₉ = 100, 10⁻⁴⁰ at T₉ = 275.
- For ⁵⁶Ni, the new partition function is 45–50% higher around kT = 10 MeV than the corrected value B of Tubbs and Koonin (1979).

## Context
This work extends the partition-function tables of Rauscher, Thielemann, and Kratz (1997) and Rauscher and Thielemann (2000), which were limited to T₉ ≤ 10, to temperatures up to T₉ = 275, using the same level-density description and mass formulas (FRDM and ETFSI-Q). It revisits the high-temperature correction debate from Fowler (1978), Mazurek (1979), and Tubbs and Koonin (1979), adopting the latter’s approach to avoid the over-suppression caused by simple truncation of the integral. The new partition functions enable more accurate modeling of nuclear statistical equilibrium in explosive nucleosynthesis (r- and rp-processes) and in core-collapse supernova simulations at extreme temperatures.
