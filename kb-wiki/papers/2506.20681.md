# On the local thermodynamic relations in relativistic spin hydrodynamics

> We demonstrate, by providing two specific examples, that the local differential thermodynamic relations used as educated guesses in relativistic hydrodynamics with spin, do not hold even at global thermodynamic equilibrium. We show, by using a rigorous quantum statistical method, that for massless free fermions and massive free fermions with rotation and acceleration at global thermodynamic equilibrium, the derivative of the pressure function with respect to the spin potential differs from the spin density and acquires a correction of the same order. Such correction cannot be eliminated by any redefinition of the entropy current, a so-called entropy-gauge transformation. Therefore, for an accurate determination of the constitutive relations in relativistic spin hydrodynamics, the traditional method of assuming differential thermodynamic relations is not appropriate.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the local differential thermodynamic relation ∂p/∂ω_{μν} = S^{μν} (where p is pressure, ω_{μν} is spin potential, and S^{μν} is spin density) does not hold even at global thermodynamic equilibrium for free fermions, contradicting the assumption commonly used in relativistic spin hydrodynamics. This failure persists for both massless and massive free fermions under rotation and acceleration, and cannot be fixed by any entropy-gauge transformation.

## Method

The authors used a rigorous quantum statistical method based on the local equilibrium density operator to compute thermodynamic functions (energy density, charge density, spin density, and pressure) up to second order in thermal vorticity for free Dirac fields. They evaluated these functions for massless free fermions using exact solutions and for massive free fermions using asymptotic expansions in the non-relativistic limit, both at global thermodynamic equilibrium with rotation and acceleration in the canonical pseudo-gauge. The pressure was defined via the thermodynamic potential current φ^μ, and its derivatives with respect to spin potential were compared to the independently calculated spin density.

## Key numbers

- For massless free fermions: the pressure is p = (7π²/180)T⁴ + (1/6)μ²T² - (a² + ω²)T²/24, where a is acceleration and ω is vorticity
- For massless free fermions: the derivative ∂p/∂ω_{λν}|_{T,μ} = (T²/12)(a^ν u^λ - a^λ u^ν) + S^{λν}, showing a correction term (T²/12)(a^ν u^λ - a^λ u^ν) beyond the spin density S^{λν}
- For massless free fermions: the spin density contraction ω_{μν}S^{μν} = -T²ω²/6
- For massive free fermions in the non-relativistic limit (m/T ≫ 1): the pressure expression contains terms proportional to e^{-(x-ζ)} where x = m/T and ζ = μ/T, with coefficients involving m, T, a², and ω²

## Context

This work extends the argument from Becattini (2023) that the differential thermodynamic relation ∂p/∂ω_{μν} = S^{μν} does not generally apply in relativistic spin hydrodynamics, providing explicit counterexamples. It contradicts the approach used in numerous derivations of constitutive relations by Hattori (2019), Fukushima (2020), Hongo (2021), Gallegos (2022), and Biswas (2023), which assumed the validity of such local thermodynamic relations. The result implies that future derivations of constitutive equations in relativistic spin hydrodynamics must use the rigorous quantum statistical method rather than assuming differential thermodynamic relations, and enables the development of more accurate hydrodynamic frameworks for describing spin polarization in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
