# High-precision determination of the $K_{e3}$ radiative corrections

> We report a high-precision calculation of the Standard Model electroweak radiative corrections in the $K\to πe^+ν(γ)$ decay as a part of the combined theory effort to understand the existing anomaly in the determinations of $V_{us}$. Our new analysis features a chiral resummation of the large infrared-singular terms in the radiative corrections and a well-under-control strong interaction uncertainty based on the most recent lattice QCD inputs. While being consistent with the current state-of-the-art results obtained from chiral perturbation theory, we reduce the existing theory uncertainty from $10^{-3}$ to $10^{-4}$. Our result suggests that the Standard Model electroweak effects cannot account for the $V_{us}$ anomaly.

## Key claim

The authors reduce the theory uncertainty in the electroweak radiative corrections for $K_{e3}$ decay from $10^{-3}$ to $10^{-4}$, but their result is consistent with the existing chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) calculations and therefore cannot explain the $V_{us}$ anomaly (the $K_{\mu 2}$–$K_{l3}$ discrepancy).

## Method

The calculation hybridizes the classical Sirlin approach with modern ChPT to resum the numerically largest infrared-singular terms in the electroweak radiative corrections to all orders in the chiral expansion. The authors split the virtual corrections into pieces that are independent of hadron properties (computed analytically), pieces dominated by pole contributions determined by hadronic form factors, and pieces involving the axial $\gamma W$-box diagram. For the latter, they use recent lattice QCD calculations of the forward axial $\gamma W$-box to constrain non-perturbative QCD contributions, and treat the non-forward kinematics as a correction. The bremsstrahlung contributions are split into a gauge-invariant piece containing the full infrared-divergent structure and a remainder subject to chiral expansion uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- The $K_{\mu 2}$ determination of $|V_{us}|$ is $0.2252(5)$ and the $K_{l3}$ determination is $0.2231(7)$, showing a disagreement at the $\sim 3\sigma$ level.
- The short-distance electroweak correction factor $S_\mathrm{EW}=1.0232(3)$.
- The final long-distance electromagnetic correction for $K^+e$ is $\delta_\mathrm{EM}^{K^+e}=0.21(2)_\mathrm{sg}(1)_{\left\langle r_K^2\right\rangle}(1)_\mathrm{lat}(4)_\mathrm{NF}(1)_{e^2p^4}\times 10^{-2}$.
- The final long-distance electromagnetic correction for $K^0e$ is $\delta_\mathrm{EM}^{K^0e}=1.16(2)_\mathrm{sg}(1)_\mathrm{lat}(1)_\mathrm{NF}(2)_{e^2p^4}\times 10^{-2}$.
- The previous ChPT results were $\left(\delta_\mathrm{EM}^{K^+e}\right)_\mathrm{ChPT}=0.10(19)_{e^2p^4}(16)_\mathrm{LEC}\times 10^{-2}$ and $\left(\delta_\mathrm{EM}^{K^0e}\right)_\mathrm{ChPT}=0.99(19)_{e^2p^4}(11)_\mathrm{LEC}\times 10^{-2}$.
- The forward axial $\gamma W$-box contributions from lattice QCD: $\Box_{\gamma W}^{VA<}(\pi^+,\pi^0,M_\pi)=0.67(3)_\mathrm{lat}\times 10^{-3}$, $\Box_{\gamma W}^{VA<}(K^0,\pi^-,M_\pi)=0.28(4)_\mathrm{lat}\times 10^{-3}$, and $\Box_{\gamma W}^{VA<}(K^+,\pi^0,M_\pi)=1.06(7)_\mathrm{lat}\times 10^{-3}$.
- The $K^+$ mean-square charge radius is $\left\langle r_K^2\right\rangle=0.34(5)$~fm$^2$.

## Context

This work extends the ChPT calculations of Cirigliano et al. (2008) by reducing the theory uncertainty from $10^{-3}$ to $10^{-4}$, using a new hybrid framework proposed by Seng et al. (2019, 2020) that resums infrared-singular terms and replaces the poorly-constrained low-energy constants (LECs) from ChPT with lattice QCD inputs from Feng et al. (2020) and Ma et al. (2021). The result confirms that Standard Model electroweak effects cannot account for the $K_{\mu 2}$–$K_{l3}$ discrepancy in $V_{us}$, redirecting attention to other inputs such as the lattice calculation of $|f_+^{K^0\pi^-}(0)|$. The improved precision also enables a new pathway for measuring $V_{us}/V_{ud}$ through the ratio of semileptonic kaon and pion decay rates, which has a much smaller theoretical uncertainty than the currently used ratio of leptonic decays.
