# A possible evidence of pion condensation

> This work demonstrates that once a large number of pion is condensed in a high-energy hadron collision, the gamma-ray spectrum from $ŽĆ^0$ decay takes on a typical broken power-law shape, which has been documented in many astronomical observations, but we have not yet recognized it. We show that this pion condensation is caused by a large number of soft gluons condensed in protons.

# A possible evidence of pion condensation

## Key claim

The authors argue that the broken power-law shape observed in many very-high-energy gamma-ray spectra from astrophysical sources is actually a signature of pion condensation in high-energy proton-proton collisions, and that this condensation is driven by gluon condensation in protons, but they acknowledge that this interpretation has not been previously recognized and that existing hadron colliders lack the energy to produce the effect in the laboratory.

## Context

This work extends the concept of pion condensation originally proposed by Migdal (1971) for nuclear matter, and connects it to gluon condensation in protons predicted by QCD evolution equations (Zhu et al., several papers from 2015 onward). The authors claim that their condensation-spectrum can explain more than seventy astronomical observations including active galactic nuclei, gamma-ray bursts, pulsars, and supernova remnants, which have previously been interpreted with lepton-based models such as synchrotron-self-Compton emission. The paper opens the possibility of using gamma-ray astronomy as a probe of QCD structure in protons, bridging nuclear physics with cosmic phenomena.
