# Behavior of the diffractive cross section in hadron-nucleus collisions

> A phenomenological analysis of diffractive dissociation of nuclei in proton-nucleus and meson-nucleus collisions is presented. The theoretical approach employed here is able to take into account at once data of the HELIOS and EHS/NA22 collaborations that exhibit quite different atomic mass dependences. Possible extensions of this approach to hard diffraction in nuclear processes are also discussed.

## Key claim

The apparently discrepant atomic-mass dependence of diffractive dissociation cross sections measured by HELIOS (α = 0.35 ± 0.02) and EHS/NA22 (α = 0.58 ± 0.06) can be simultaneously described by a single phenomenological model in which the nuclear diffractive cross section is proportional to the single-interaction partial inelastic cross section σ^{hA}_{ν=1}, with the different A-dependences arising naturally from the different hadron-proton inelastic cross sections.

## Method

The authors model nuclear diffractive dissociation by assuming that only single peripheral interactions (ν=1) contribute, expressing the hadron-nucleus single-diffractive cross section as σ^{hA→hX}_{SD} = (σ^{hA}_{ν=1} / σ^{hp}_{inel}) × σ^{hp→hX}_{SD}. The partial inelastic cross section σ^{hA}_{ν=1} is calculated from a Glauber-type multiple scattering formalism using nuclear densities (Gaussian for light nuclei, Woods-Saxon for A≥18) and Regge-based parametrizations of the hadron-proton inelastic cross sections. The hadron-proton single-diffractive cross section is computed using a renormalized Triple Pomeron model with Donnachie-Landshoff flux factors and a Pomeron trajectory α(t)=1+ε+α't with ε=0.104 and α'=0.25 GeV⁻².

## Key numbers

- HELIOS Collaboration measured target-diffraction cross section A-dependence exponent α = 0.35 ± 0.02 for pA collisions at 450 GeV/c.
- EHS/NA22 Collaboration measured α = 0.58 ± 0.06 for meson-nucleus collisions at 250 GeV/c.
- Hadron-Pomeron couplings used: β_p = 6.82 GeV⁻¹, β_π = 4.13 GeV⁻¹, β_K = 3.71 GeV⁻¹.
- Pomeron-proton coupling g_{IP} = 0.87 GeV⁻¹.
- Pomeron trajectory parameters: ε = 0.104, α' = 0.25 GeV⁻².
- Dipole form factor parameters: μ²_π = 0.92 GeV², μ²_K = 1.10 GeV².
- Nuclear density parameters: r₀ = 1.2 A^{1/3} fm, r_p = 0.8 fm, b₀ = 0.4 fm.

## Context

This work extends the earlier multiple-scattering analysis of Batista and Covolan (1999) for proton-nucleus collisions to include meson-nucleus collisions, resolving the apparent contradiction between the HELIOS (1990s) and EHS/NA22 (1990s) data sets. The model provides a framework for extending the Ingelman-Schlein model of hard diffraction to nuclear targets, with the authors discussing three possible formalisms (standard flux factor, renormalized flux factor, or diffraction factor) for making predictions of hard diffractive processes in hadron-nucleus collisions.
