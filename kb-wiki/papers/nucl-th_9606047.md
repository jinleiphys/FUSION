# Triton Binding Energy and Minimal Relativity

> For relativistic three-body calculations, essentially two different approaches are in use: field theory and relativistic direct interactions. Results for relativistic corrections of the triton binding energy obtained from the two approaches differ even in their sign, which is rather puzzling. In this paper, we discuss the origin of such discrepancy. We show that the use of an invariant two-body amplitude, as done in the field-theoretic approach, increases the triton binding energy by about 0.30 MeV. This may explain a large part of the discrepancy.

## Key claim
The use of an invariant two-body amplitude (minimal relativity) in the field-theoretic approach increases the triton binding energy by about 0.30 MeV, which may explain a large part of the discrepancy between field-theoretic and relativistic direct interaction approaches; however, the sign of the relativistic correction remains controversial and depends on how the non-relativistic limit is defined.

## Method
The authors apply a minimal relativity prescription that relates a relativistic invariant two-body t-matrix to the non-relativistic t-matrix via a factor of sqrt(E/m) for each leg, where E = sqrt(m^2 + q^2). This prescription is inserted into the relativistic Blankenbecler-Sugar (BbS) equation, and the effect on the triton binding energy is computed for a variety of nucleon-nucleon potentials without refitting phase parameters.

## Key numbers
- The non-relativistic triton binding energy predicted by local high-quality potentials in a 34-channel charge-dependent Faddeev calculation is 7.62(1) MeV (Friar, 1993).
- The non-relativistic CD-Bonn potential (non-local) predicts 8.00 MeV for the triton binding energy (Machleidt, Sammarruca, and Song, 1996).
- The experimental triton binding energy is 8.48 MeV.
- Using the Bonn B potential in the field-theoretic BbS approach, the relativistic increase in binding energy is 0.19 MeV (Sammarruca, Xu, and Machleidt, 1992).
- Applying the minimal relativity prescription across a variety of NN potentials increases the triton binding energy by 0.25–0.37 MeV.
- The field-theoretic approach of Rupp and Tjon (1992) predicts 0.29–0.38 MeV more binding relative to non-relativistic results.
- The relativistic direct interaction approach (Carlson, Koonin, and others, 1993) reports a relativistic reduction of the triton binding energy by 0.34 MeV.
- Including relativity, the predicted triton binding energy ranges from 7.3 to 8.2 MeV.

## Context
This work addresses the puzzling sign discrepancy between field-theoretic approaches (Rupp and Tjon, 1992; Sammarruca, Xu, and Machleidt, 1992; Gross and collaborators, 1996), which find increased triton binding from relativity, and relativistic direct interaction approaches (Carlson, Koonin, and others, 1993), which find reduced binding. The minimal relativity effect identified here is absent from direct interaction approaches because they are not manifestly covariant. The authors note that the ambiguity in defining a non-relativistic limit (Keister, 1991) and the risks of p/m expansions (Glockle and Muller, 1981) may underlie the discrepancy, and that a consistently covariant theory is the only safe way forward.
