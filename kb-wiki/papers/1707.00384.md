# Axion Production from Landau Quantization in the Strong Magnetic Field of Magnetars

> We utilize an exact quantum calculation to explore axion emission from electrons and protons in the presence of the strong magnetic field of magnetars. The axion is emitted via transitions between the Landau levels generated by the strong magnetic field. The luminosity of axions emitted by protons is shown to be much larger than that of electrons and becomes stronger with increasing matter density. Cooling by axion emission is shown to be much larger than neutrino cooling by the Urca processes. Consequently, axion emission in the crust may significantly contribute to the cooling of magnetars. In the high-density core, however, it may cause heating of the magnetar.

## Key claim

Axion emission via Landau-level transitions of protons and electrons in magnetar-strength magnetic fields (10^14-10^15 G) can produce luminosities much larger than neutrino cooling from the modified Urca process, but only when the coupling constants are taken as 10^-2 of the previously established upper limit; at high densities in the core, axion emission may cause heating rather than cooling.

## Method

The authors performed a fully relativistic quantum calculation of axion emission from electrons and protons in a uniform magnetic field, using the Dirac equation with Landau quantization and including the anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) of protons. They computed the differential decay width for axion emission via transitions between Landau levels, using relativistic mean-field theory for the proton equation of state and taking the temperature to be much less than 1 MeV. The axion-nucleon coupling was set to g_aNN = 6 × 10^-12 and the axion-electron coupling to g_aee = 9 × 10^-15, which are 10^-2 below the maximum values from Iwamoto (1984).

## Key numbers

- Axion luminosity per nucleon at B = 10^15 G and ρ_B = 0.1 ρ_0 is about 10^4 times larger than the neutrino luminosity from the modified Urca process
- For B = 10^15 G, √(eB) = 2.43 MeV; eB/E_F* = 6.6 keV at ρ_B = 0.1 ρ_0 for protons, and 43 keV at ρ_B = 0.1 ρ_0 for electrons
- The energy step from AMM for protons at B = 10^15 G is eBκ/M = 7.43 keV
- The maximum axion luminosity occurs at B ≈ 9 × 10^13 G for T = 0.7 keV and ρ_B = 0.1 ρ_0
- Estimated B_max for protons at T = 0.7 keV is B_max ∼ 8.4 × 10^13 G; for electrons B_max ∼ 1.7 × 10^12 G
- The anti-neutrino luminosity for the MU process per nucleon at T = 0.7 keV and ρ_B = 0.1 ρ_0 is 4.0 × 10^-25 keV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of axion emission from neutron stars, which had considered bremsstrahlung in NN collisions (Umeda et al., 1998) and classical/quantum calculations for electrons only (Bori 1994; KWW 1997). The key advance is including proton contributions with Landau quantization and the anomalous magnetic moment, which had been shown to be significant for pion production (P2Pi-1, P2Pi-2). The results enable future studies of magnetar cooling and heating, and suggest that fully quantum calculations give higher yields than semi-classical approaches for both pions and axions.
