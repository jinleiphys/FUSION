# A Poincaré Covariant Current Operator for Deep Inelastic Structure Functions and Deuteron Electromagnetic Properties

> In front-form dynamics the current operator can be constructed from auxiliary operators, defined in a Breit frame where initial and final three-momenta of the system are directed along the $z$ axis. Poincaré covariance constraints reduce for auxiliary operators to the ones imposed only by kinematical rotations around the $z$ axis. Elastic and transition form factors can be extracted without any ambiguity and in the elastic case the continuity equation is automatically satisfied, once Poincaré, ${\cal P}$ and ${\cal T}$ covariance, together with hermiticity, are imposed. Applications to deep inelastic structure functions in an exactly solvable model and to the calculation of the deuteron electromagnetic form factors are presented.

## Key claim

In front-form Hamiltonian dynamics, a Poincaré-covariant current operator can be constructed from auxiliary operators defined in a special Breit frame where the momentum transfer is along the spin-quantization axis, reducing the full Lorentz covariance constraints to those of kinematical rotations around the \(z\) axis only; this construction automatically satisfies the continuity equation for elastic scattering once extended Poincaré, parity, and time-reversal covariance together with hermiticity are imposed.

## Method

The authors construct the current operator via a spectral decomposition over subspaces of definite mass and spin eigenvalues, then evaluate matrix elements in a Breit frame where initial and final three-momenta are collinear with the \(z\) axis. The auxiliary current in this special Breit frame is required only to be covariant under rotations around the \(z\) axis, which are kinematical in front-form dynamics, and is taken as the projection of the free one-body current operator. For elastic scattering, hermiticity is enforced by symmetrizing the free-current matrix elements, which then automatically yields current conservation. The method is applied to deep inelastic scattering in an exactly solvable two-particle relativistic harmonic oscillator model and to the calculation of deuteron electromagnetic form factors using realistic \(N-N\) interactions (Reid soft core, AV14, AV18, Paris).

## Key numbers

- For the deuteron magnetic moment \(\mu_d\) (experimental 0.8574 nuclear magnetons), the covariant approach yields: 0.8611 (RSC, \(P_D=6.47\%\)), 0.8608 (AV14, \(P_D=6.08\%\)), 0.8632 (Paris, \(P_D=5.77\%\)), 0.8635 (AV18, \(P_D=5.76\%\)); these are about 2% above nonrelativistic results, compared to about 1% increase in the \(q^+=0\) frame approach of Chung et al. (1988).
- For the deuteron quadrupole moment \(Q_d\) (experimental 0.2859 fm\(^2\)), the covariant approach gives: 0.2852 fm\(^2\) (RSC), 0.2907 fm\(^2\) (AV14), 0.2841 fm\(^2\) (Paris), 0.2744 fm\(^2\) (AV18).
- In the exactly solvable harmonic oscillator model, the averaged structure functions \(\bar{F}_1(\bar{x},Q)\) and \(\bar{F}_2(\bar{x},Q)\) in the Bjorken limit (\(Q^2 \rightarrow \infty\)) satisfy \(\bar{F}_2(\bar{x}) = 2\bar{x}\bar{F}_1(\bar{x})\) and \(\bar{F}_1(\bar{x}) = \frac{1}{2}\rho(\bar{x})\), recovering the parton model results.

## Context

This work extends the front-form approach of Dirac (1949) and addresses the "angular condition" problem identified by Grach and Kondratyuk (1984) and Chung et al. (1988), where the free current in the \(q^+=0\) frame yields four independent matrix elements for a spin-1 system instead of the three physical form factors. The construction enables unambiguous extraction of elastic and transition form factors without the ambiguities present in earlier \(q^+=0\) frame calculations. The method provides a framework for incorporating two-body currents while maintaining Poincaré covariance, and the deuteron results can serve as a benchmark for extracting nucleon form factors, as previously attempted by Galster et al. (1971) and Saclay experiments.
