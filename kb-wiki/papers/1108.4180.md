# A formula for charmonium suppression

> In this work a formula for charmonium suppression obtained by Matsui in 1989 is analytically generalized for the case of complex c-cbar potential described by a 3-dimensional and isotropic time-dependent harmonic oscillator (THO). It is suggested that under certain conditions the formula can be applied to describe J/ψsuppression in heavy-ion collisions at CERN-SPS, RHIC, and LHC with the advantage of analytical tractability.

## Key claim

The paper analytically generalizes Matsui's 1989 formula for charmonium suppression to the case of a complex, time-dependent harmonic oscillator (THO) potential, and suggests that under certain conditions this formula can be applied to describe J/ψ suppression in heavy-ion collisions at CERN-SPS, RHIC, and LHC with the advantage of analytical tractability.

## Method

The authors start from Matsui's definition of the charmonium suppression ratio as a ratio of cross sections in the distorted wave Born approximation, expressed as a survival probability for the s-wave. They then calculate the time evolution operator for a 3-dimensional isotropic time-dependent harmonic oscillator using the path integral approach, solving the classical equation of motion via the Ermakov equation. The resulting suppression formulas for J/ψ, ψ', and χc states are derived by integrating the corresponding harmonic oscillator wavefunctions with the time evolution operator, and a final observable J/ψ suppression ratio is constructed including feed-down contributions from higher charmonia states.

## Key numbers

- The J/ψ suppression ratio is given by S_J/ψ(t) = |ρ(t)/ρ(0)|^3 × |cos γ(t) + (ρ̇(t)ρ(t)^(-1)/γ̇(t) + i ω_ψ/γ̇(t)) sin γ(t)|^(-3), where ρ(t) is the Ermakov function, γ(t) is an integral of 1/ρ^2(t'), and ω_ψ is the harmonic oscillator frequency for J/ψ.
- The ψ' suppression ratio is S_ψ'(t) = S_J/ψ(t) × |1 - (2 i ω_ψ sin γ(t))/((i ω_ψ + ρ̇(t)/ρ(t)) sin γ(t) + γ̇(t) cos γ(t))|^2.
- The χc suppression ratio is S_χ(t) = S_J/ψ(t)^(5/3).
- The total observable J/ψ suppression ratio including feed-down is S(t) = 0.6 S_J/ψ(t) + 0.3 S_χ(t) + 0.1 S_ψ'(t).

## Context

This work extends the harmonic oscillator model for charmonium suppression introduced by Matsui (1989) by allowing the oscillator frequency to be complex and time-dependent, which enables a more realistic description of the medium-modified interaction in heavy-ion collisions. The analytical formulas derived here enable comparison with experimental results from CERN SPS and RHIC, with preliminary comparisons reported in Blaschke (2011). The approach provides an analytically tractable alternative to fully numerical treatments of charmonium suppression in heavy-ion collisions.
