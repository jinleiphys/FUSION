# A Precise Determination of $α_s$ from the Heavy Jet Mass Distribution

> A global fit for $α_s(m_Z)$ is performed on available $e^+e^-$ data for the heavy jet mass distribution. The state-of-the-art theory prediction includes $\mathcal{O}(α_s^3)$ fixed-order results, N$^3$LL$^\prime$ dijet resummation, N$^2$LL Sudakov shoulder resummation, and a first-principles treatment of power corrections in the dijet region. Theoretical correlations are incorporated through a flat random-scan covariance matrix. The global fit results in $0.1148^{+ 0.0015}_{-0.0022}$, compatible with similar determinations from thrust and $C$-parameter. Dijet resummation is essential for a robust fit, as it engenders insensitivity to the fit-range lower cutoff; without resummation the fit-range sensitivity is overwhelming. In addition, we find evidence for a negative power correction in the trijet region if and only if Sudakov shoulder resummation is included.

## Key claim

The global fit yields $\alpha_s(m_Z) = 0.1148^{+0.0015}_{-0.0022}$, which is compatible with the world average at the 1.8-sigma level, but only when dijet resummation is included to eliminate sensitivity to the fit-range lower cutoff; without resummation the fit-range sensitivity is overwhelming and no robust extraction is possible.

## Method

The authors perform a global fit to 700 experimental data points from 50 datasets at 35 center-of-mass energies between 35 GeV and 207 GeV from the ALEPH, DELPHI, JADE, L3, OPAL, and SLD collaborations. The theory prediction combines $\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s^3)$ fixed-order results with N$^3$LL$^\prime$ dijet resummation, N$^2$LL Sudakov shoulder resummation, and a first-principles treatment of power corrections in the dijet region using a single non-perturbative parameter $\Om$ in the R-gap scheme. Theoretical correlations are incorporated through a flat random-scan covariance matrix with up to 16 theory parameters sampled uniformly over 5000 sets, and the total covariance matrix is the sum of this theory covariance and the experimental covariance.

## Key numbers

- $\alpha_s(m_Z) = 0.1148^{+0.0015}_{-0.0022}$ from the full 3-parameter fit including dijet resummation, Sudakov shoulder resummation, and non-perturbative parameters $\Om$ and $\Th$, with $\chi^2/\text{dof} = 1.039$
- $\Om = 0.61 \pm 0.08$ GeV for the dijet non-perturbative parameter in the R-gap scheme with reference scale 2 GeV
- $\Th = -0.46 \pm 0.17$ GeV for the trijet non-perturbative shift parameter, indicating a negative power correction in the tail
- Fixed-order-only fit gives $\alpha_s = 0.1166^{+0.0037}_{-0.0033}$ with significantly larger fit-range uncertainty
- Removing all DELPHI datasets gives $\alpha_s = 0.1142 \pm 0.0021$
- Using only data with $Q \ge 90$ GeV (355 points) gives $\alpha_s = 0.1145 \pm 0.0020$
- Using only data with $Q \le 100$ GeV (99 points) gives $\alpha_s = 0.1163 \pm 0.0021$

## Context

This work resolves a long-standing discrepancy where heavy jet mass extractions of $\alpha_s$ consistently gave values below those from thrust (Salam 2001, Becher 2008, Dissertori 2009, Alioli 2012, Banfi 2023) and below the PDG average. The result is compatible with recent thrust determinations (Benitez 2024, Abbate 2010) and C-parameter fits (Hoang 2015). The finding of a negative power correction in the trijet region only when Sudakov shoulder resummation is included contrasts with recent work by Nason (2023, 2025) and Caola (2021, 2022) which suggested a leftward shift throughout the distribution. The measured $\Om = 0.61 \pm 0.08$ GeV is compatible with Monte Carlo expectations that $\Omega_1^\rho \simeq \Omega_1^\tau$ when hadron mass effects are included, despite thrust and heavy jet mass belonging to different universality classes (Mateu 2012).
