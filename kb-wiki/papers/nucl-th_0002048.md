# Giant monopole resonance and nuclear compression modulus for 40Ca and 16O

> Using a collective potential derived on the basis of the Generator Coordinate Method with Skyrme interactions we obtain values for the compression modulus of 40Ca which are in good agreement with a recently obtained experimental value. Calculated values for the compression modulus for 16O are also given. The procedure involved in the derivation of the collective potential is briefly reviewed and discussed.

# Giant monopole resonance and nuclear compression modulus for 40Ca and 16O

## Key claim

Using a collective potential derived from the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) with Skyrme interactions, the authors obtain compression moduli for 40Ca that agree with the experimental value of Youngblood (1999), but only for the SII and SIII Skyrme parameter sets; the agreement is conditional on including off-diagonal contributions to the GCM energy kernel, which raise the calculated compression modulus above the value obtained from a simpler variational method.

## Method

The authors construct a collective potential for the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (breathing mode) using the Generator Coordinate Method with the oscillator parameter as the generator coordinate. They apply a Weyl-Wigner transformation to the GCM energy kernel to extract a collective potential, then compute the compression modulus as the second derivative of that potential at its minimum, divided by the mass number. Calculations are performed for five Skyrme interaction parameter sets (SI through SV) without Coulomb forces, for both 40Ca and 16O.

## Key numbers

- For 40Ca with Skyrme SII: K = 229.08 MeV (GCM collective potential) versus K_v = 222.28 MeV (simple variational method)
- For 40Ca with Skyrme SIII: K = 239.04 MeV (GCM) versus K_v = 231.73 MeV (variational)
- For 40Ca with Skyrme SI: K = 253.84 MeV; SIV: K = 217.86 MeV; SV: K = 205.37 MeV
- For 16O with Skyrme SII: K = 213.78 MeV (GCM) versus K_v = 192.17 MeV (variational)
- For 16O with Skyrme SIII: K = 223.93 MeV (GCM) versus K_v = 200.56 MeV (variational)
- For 16O with Skyrme SI: K = 242.02 MeV; SIV: K = 202.60 MeV; SV: K = 190.48 MeV
- The experimental value for 40Ca from Youngblood (1999) is cited as approximately 231 ± 5 MeV for nuclear matter

## Context

This work extends the GCM-based approach of Galetti and de Toledo Piza (1981) to compute compression moduli for finite nuclei, and directly addresses the experimental results of Youngblood (1997, 1999) for the giant monopole resonance. The method differs from self-consistent Hartree-Fock plus Random Phase Approximation treatments (Hamamoto, 1997), relativistic mean field calculations (Chossy, 1997), and Thomas-Fermi approaches (Myers and Swiatecki, 1998) by explicitly including off-diagonal contributions to the GCM energy kernel, which produce higher compression moduli than simple variational methods. The results enable comparison of GCM-derived incompressibilities with experimental values and with the nuclear matter incompressibility of 231 ± 5 MeV reported by Youngblood.
