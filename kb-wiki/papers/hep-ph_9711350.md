# Strange anti-baryons - QGP versus HG

> We study quark-gluon plasma (QGP) and hadronic gas (HG) models of the central fireball presumed to be the source of abundantly produced strange (anti-)baryons in S -> W collisions at 200 GeV A. We consider how multi-strange (anti-)baryon multiplicities depend on strangeness conservation and compare the HG and QGP fireball scenarios. We argue that the total particle multiplicity emerging from the central rapidity region as well as the variation of production rates with changes in the beam energy allows to distinguish between the two reaction scenarios.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while both quark-gluon plasma (QGP) and hadronic gas (HG) fireball models can reproduce the observed strange anti-baryon abundances at CERN-SPS energies (200 GeV A), the HG interpretation requires an unnaturally high temperature of about 215 MeV and cannot explain the measured charged particle multiplicity, whereas the QGP interpretation naturally accounts for both observables.

## Method

The authors compare QGP and HG fireball models by computing strange particle partition functions with explicit summation over hadronic resonances (kaons up to 1650 MeV, hyperons up to 1750 MeV, cascades up to 1820 MeV, omegas up to 2250 MeV) and imposing zero net strangeness constraint. They study how the strange-chemical potential relates to the baryo-chemical potential at fixed temperatures of 150, 200, and 300 MeV, and compare predicted particle ratios (R_Lambda = anti-Lambda/Lambda, R_Xi = anti-Xi-/Xi-) and entropy per baryon against experimental data from the WA85 and EMU05 collaborations.

## Key numbers

- For the HG fireball scenario at T=215 MeV, the entropy per baryon is S/B = 21.5 +/- 1.5, leading to a pion multiplicity of 4 +/- 0.5 per baryon, which is less than half the QGP expectation.
- In the QGP fireball scenario with gamma=0.7, the strange pair abundance per baryon is about one, yielding a factor of about 2.5 enhancement compared to the HG scenario (which gives about 0.4 strange pairs per baryon).
- The WA85 experimental data point for R_Lambda and R_Xi falls near the T=215 MeV HG curve and the QGP curve (mu_s=0), which nearly coincide at this temperature.
- The measured charged particle multiplicity difference D_Q = (N+ - N-)/(N+ + N-) at central rapidity is 0.08-0.09, while the HG scenario with mu_B ~ 0.35 GeV predicts D_Q ~ 0.27, incompatible with the EMU05 data.
- The condition lambda_s=1 (mu_s=0) in HG occurs only in a narrow temperature window around T=215 +/- 15 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier studies (Rafelski 1991, Letessier et al. 1992) comparing QGP and HG interpretations of strange baryon production, and contrasts with parallel efforts by Cleymans and Satz (1992) who used different assumptions about chemical potentials and did not consider partial strangeness saturation. The paper enables experimental discrimination between QGP and HG scenarios by combining strange anti-baryon ratio measurements with total charged particle multiplicity data, and by extending measurements to both higher (BNL-RHIC) and lower (CERN-SPS at 60 GeV) beam energies.
