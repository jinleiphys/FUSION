# The Lipkin Model in Many-Fermion System as an Example of the su(1,1) times su(1,1)-Algebraic Model

> Following the idea, recently, proposed by the present authors for the two-level pairing model, the Lipkin model is reexamined. It is a natural generalization of the method already developed by the present authors with Kuriyama. This model is a schematic model for many-fermion system and obeys the su(2)-algebra. It is shown that the use of the Schwinger boson representation, the model is expressed in terms of the su(1,1) times su(1,1)-algebra and with the aid of the MYT mapping method, it is disguised from the orginal form in terms of the Holstein-Primakoff representation. Further, under various coherent states, the classical counterparts are derived. It is concluded that the Lipkin model can be treated in the common ring as that of the two-level pairing model.

## Key claim

The Lipkin model, originally an su(2)-algebraic schematic model for many-fermion systems, can be reformulated as an su(1,1) ⊗ su(1,1)-algebraic model using the Schwinger boson representation and the MYT mapping method, allowing it to be treated in the same theoretical framework as the two-level pairing model, but with the su(1,1) quantum numbers restricted to t = 1/4 and 3/4 rather than the half-integer values of the pairing model.

## Method

The authors start from the standard Lipkin model Hamiltonian with su(2) generators expressed in terms of fermion operators for two single-particle levels of degeneracy Ω = 2j+1. They apply the Schwinger boson representation to express the su(2) generators in terms of two boson species, then rewrite the Hamiltonian using boson-pair operators that form an su(1,1) ⊗ su(1,1) algebra. The MYT mapping method is used to transform the system into a Holstein-Primakoff representation for the su(1,1) algebra, and various coherent states (su(1,1) ⊗ su(1,1)-coherent states and a mixed coherent state) are constructed to derive classical counterparts of the model.

## Key numbers

- The su(1,1) quantum numbers for the Lipkin model are t = 1/4 and t = 3/4, in contrast to the two-level pairing model where t = 1/2, 1, 3/2, ...
- The degeneracy of each single-particle level is Ω = 2j+1, with Ω an even integer and j a half-integer.
- The total fermion number N ranges from 0 to 2Ω, with specific allowed values for given spin s: for integer s, N = 2s, 2s+2, ..., Ω, 2Ω-2s, 2Ω-2s-2, ..., 2Ω; for half-integer s, N = 2s, 2s+2, ..., Ω-1, 2Ω-2s, 2Ω-2s-2, ..., 2Ω-1.
- The Hamiltonian parameters are ε (single-particle energy difference between levels) and G (interaction strength).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous reformulation of the two-level pairing model (Providencia et al., 2005, referred to as (I)) to the Lipkin model, originally proposed by Lipkin, Meshkov and Glick (1965). The paper builds on earlier boson realization methods developed by the same authors with Kuriyama (2005) and the MYT mapping method (Marshall, Yamamura, Tsue, 2000). The result enables both the Lipkin model and the two-level pairing model to be studied within a common su(1,1) ⊗ su(1,1) algebraic framework, with the difference appearing only in the allowed values of the su(1,1) quantum numbers t_+ and t_-.
