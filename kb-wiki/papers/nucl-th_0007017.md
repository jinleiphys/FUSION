# Simultaneous Heavy Ion Dissociation at Ultrarelativistic Energies

> We study the simultaneous dissociation of heavy ultrarelativistic nuclei followed by the forward-backward neutron emission in peripheral collisions at colliders. The main contribution to this particular heavy-ion dissociation process, which can be used as a beam luminosity monitor, is expected to be due to the electromagnetic interaction. The Weizsacker-Williams method is extended to the case of simultaneous excitation of collision partners which is simulated by the RELDIS code. A contribution to the dissociation cross section due to grazing nuclear interactions is estimated within the abrasion model and found to be relatively small.

# Simultaneous Heavy Ion Dissociation at Ultrarelativistic Energies

## Key claim
The simultaneous (mutual) electromagnetic dissociation of both colliding nuclei, followed by forward-backward neutron emission, can serve as a beam luminosity monitor at heavy-ion colliders, with the electromagnetic contribution dominating over grazing nuclear interactions. The Weizsäcker-Williams method is extended to treat the two photon exchanges as independent processes because the collision time is much shorter than the deexcitation period and the energy of the first emitted photon does not change the total energy of the emitting nucleus significantly.

## Method
The authors extend the Weizsäcker-Williams method to mutual electromagnetic dissociation by treating the two photon exchanges as independent processes, with the equivalent photon spectrum from an excited nucleus taken equal to that from the ground state. The cross section for mutual dissociation is calculated using the RELDIS code, which integrates over impact parameters with a Poisson distribution for multiphoton absorption. The contribution from grazing nuclear interactions is estimated separately using an abrasion model based on Glauber multiple scattering theory, with a simple parameterization for single and mutual neutron removal cross sections.

## Key numbers
- Mutual (1n,1n) dissociation cross section for AuAu collisions at RHIC: approximately 750 mb
- Mutual (1n,1n) dissociation cross section for PbPb collisions at LHC: approximately 970 mb
- Nuclear (abrasion) contribution to mutual (1n,1n) emission: approximately 100 mb
- Impact parameter cutoff for nuclear overlap: \( b_c = 1.34\bigl( A_1^{1/3}+A_2^{1/3}-0.75(A_1^{-1/3}+A_2^{-1/3})\bigr) \) fm
- Abrasion model parameters: \(\Delta b \approx 0.5\) fm and \(P_{esc} \approx 0.75\) for neutron escape probability

## Context
This work extends the Weizsäcker-Williams method for electromagnetic dissociation, previously applied to single-nucleus dissociation by Pshenichnov and collaborators, to the case of simultaneous excitation of both collision partners. The model is validated against experimental data for charge-changing cross sections of 158A GeV \(^{208}\)Pb ions at CERN SPS energies from Dekhissi (1998). The results enable the use of correlated forward neutron detection in zero-degree calorimeters for beam luminosity monitoring at RHIC and LHC, as proposed by Baltz and collaborators. The nuclear abrasion contribution is found to be relatively small compared to the electromagnetic contribution for few-nucleon removal processes.
