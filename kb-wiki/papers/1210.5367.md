# Uncertainties in nuclear transition matrix elements for $β^{+}β^{+}$ and $\varepsilon β^{+}$ modes of neutrinoless positron double-$β$ decay within PHFB model

> Uncertainties in the nuclear transition matrix elements $M^{(0ν)}$ and $M^{(0N)}$ of the double-positron emission $(β^{+}β^{+})_{0ν}$ and electron-positron conversion $(\varepsilon β^{+})_{0ν}$ modes due to the exchange of light and heavy Majorana neutrinos, respectively, are calculated for $^{96}$Ru, $^{102}$Pd, $^{106}$Cd, $^{124}$Xe, $^{130}$Ba and $^{156}$Dy isotopes by employing the PHFB model with four different parameterization of the pairing plus multipolar two-body interactions and three different parameterizations of the Jastrow short range correlations. In all cases but for $^{130}$Ba, the uncertainties are smaller than 14% for light Majorana neutrino exchange and 35% for the exchange of a heavy Majorana neutrino.

## Key claim

The paper quantifies theoretical uncertainties in nuclear transition matrix elements (NTMEs) for the double-positron emission and electron-positron conversion modes of neutrinoless double-beta decay, finding that for five of the six studied isotopes the uncertainties are smaller than 14% for light Majorana neutrino exchange and 35% for heavy Majorana neutrino exchange, with the exception of 130Ba where the uncertainties are larger.

## Method

The authors employed the Projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (PHFB) model to calculate NTMEs for the (β+β+)0ν and (εβ+)0ν decay modes of six isotopes (96Ru, 102Pd, 106Cd, 124Xe, 130Ba, 156Dy) for 0+ → 0+ transitions. They used four different parameterizations of the pairing plus multipolar two-body effective interaction (PQQ1, PQQHH1, PQQ2, PQQHH2) and three different parameterizations of Jastrow-type short range correlations (SRC1: Miller-Spencer, SRC2: Argonne V18, SRC3: CD-Bonn), generating sets of twelve NTMEs from which means and standard deviations were computed.

## Key numbers

- For light Majorana neutrino exchange, uncertainties in NTMEs are smaller than 14% for all studied isotopes except 130Ba.
- For heavy Majorana neutrino exchange, uncertainties in NTMEs are smaller than 35% for all studied isotopes except 130Ba.
- The NTMEs for 106Cd with the PQQ1 parameterization range from 7.2607 (SRC1) to 8.6835 (SRC3) for light neutrino exchange, and from 149.5474 (SRC1) to 452.6855 (no SRC) for heavy neutrino exchange.
- The three SRC parameterizations used are: SRC1 with a=1.1 fm-2, b=0.68 fm-2, c=1.0 (Miller-Spencer); SRC2 with a=1.59 fm-2, b=1.45 fm-2, c=0.92 (Argonne V18); SRC3 with a=1.52 fm-2, b=1.88 fm-2, c=0.46 (CD-Bonn).
- The dipole form factor parameters used are ΛV = 0.850 GeV and ΛA = 1.086 GeV, with gA = 1.254 and κ = 3.70.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous PHFB-based uncertainty studies of (β-β-)0ν decay (Rath 2010, Rath 2011) to the positron-emitting modes (β+β+)0ν and (εβ+)0ν, which are experimentally competing with double-electron capture. The approach follows the methodology of Rodin et al. (2003) for estimating theoretical uncertainties from model variations, and the results enable extraction of bounds on the effective light neutrino mass ⟨mν⟩ and heavy neutrino mass ⟨MN⟩ from future experimental half-life measurements of these decay modes.
