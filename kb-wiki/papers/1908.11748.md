# Unequal rapidity correlators in the dilute limit of JIMWLK

> We study unequal rapidity correlators in the stochastic Langevin picture of Jalilian-Marian - Iancu - McLerran - Weigert - Leonidov - Kovner (JIMWLK) evolution in the Color Glass Condensate effective field theory. By separately evolving the Wilson lines in the direct and complex conjugate amplitudes, we use the formalism to study two-particle production at large rapidity separations. We show that the evolution between the rapidities of the two produced particles can be expressed as a linear equation, even in the full nonlinear limit. We also show how the Langevin formalism for two-particle correlations reduces to a BFKL picture in the dilute limit and in momentum space, providing an interpretation of BFKL evolution as a stochastic process for color charges.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the evolution between the rapidities of two produced particles in JIMWLK evolution can be expressed as a linear equation even in the full nonlinear limit, and that in the dilute limit the Langevin formalism for two-particle correlations reduces to a BFKL picture, providing an interpretation of BFKL evolution as a stochastic process for color charges.

## Method

The authors use the Langevin formulation of JIMWLK evolution to study unequal rapidity correlators by separately evolving Wilson lines in the direct amplitude (DA) and complex conjugate amplitude (CCA). They derive the evolution equations for Lie derivatives acting on Wilson lines, showing these equations are linear and independent of the Wilson lines themselves. They then take the dilute limit by expanding Wilson lines to linear order in the color field \(\lambda\), obtaining the BFKL equation from the Langevin step and demonstrating that the two-particle cross section becomes \(k_T\)-factorized in this limit.

## Key numbers

* The evolution step size \(\epsilon\) satisfies \(\epsilon N = Y - Y_0\) with \(N \rightarrow \infty, \epsilon \rightarrow 0\), where \(N\) is the number of steps.
* The noise correlator is \(\left\langle \nu^{i,a}_{\ib{x},m} \nu^{j,b}_{\ib{y},n} \right\rangle = \frac{1}{\epsilon} \delta^{ij} \delta^{ab} \delta_{mn} \delta_\ib{xy}\).
* The BFKL equation obtained in the dilute limit has the form \(\phi^{n+1}({\ib{q}}) = \phi^n({\ib{q}}) + 4 \nc \epsilon \alpha_s \int_\ib{p} \frac{1}{\ib{(q - p)}^2} \bigg( \frac{\phi^n({\ib{p}}) \ib{p}^2}{\ib{q}^2} - \frac{1}{2} \frac{\phi^n({\ib{q}}) \ib{q}^2}{\ib{p}^2} \bigg) + \mathcal{O}(\epsilon^{3/2}, \phi^{3/2})\).
* The initial condition for the BFKL Green's function is \(\mathcal{F}^0(\ib{P,\bar{P},m,\bar{m}}) = (\nc^2 -1) \delta^{(2)}(\ib{P}+\ib{m}) \delta^{(2)}(\ib{\bar{P}}+\ib{\bar{m}})\).
* The equal rapidity cross section reduces to \(\left. \frac{d\sigma_{qg}}{dY d^2\ib{p} \, dY_A d^2\ib{k}_A} \right|_{Y=Y_A} = - \frac{\as}{(2 \pi)^2} \frac{(\ib{p}+\ib{k}_A)^2}{\ib{p}^2\ib{k}_A^2} \varphi^0(\ib{p}+\ib{k}_A)\).

## Context

This work extends the Langevin formulation of two-particle correlations in JIMWLK evolution developed by Iancu and Triantafyllopoulos (2013), building on earlier work by Kovner, Lublinsky, and collaborators (2006). The paper clarifies that the evolution of Lie derivatives encoding correlations between two rapidities is linear, confirming a result obtained in a different language by Jalilian-Marian (2004). The dilute limit derivation connects the JIMWLK Langevin formalism to the BFKL equation, enabling a stochastic interpretation of BFKL evolution and providing a framework for studying two-particle production at large rapidity separations in the Color Glass Condensate effective field theory.
