# $A$ dependence of the $γ$- and $p$-induced production of the $Λ(1520)$ from nuclei

> Using results of a recent calculation of the $Λ(1520)$ in the nuclear medium, which show that the medium width is about five times the free width, we study the A dependence of the $Λ(1520)$ production cross section in the reactions $γ~A \to K^+ Λ(1520) A^\prime$ and $p~ A \to p~ K^+ Λ(1520) A^\prime$. We find a sizable A dependence in the ratio of the nuclear cross sections for heavy nuclei with respect to a light one due to the large value of the $Λ(1520)$ width in the medium, showing that devoted experiments, easily within reach in present facilities, can provide good information on that magnitude by measuring the cross sections studied here.

## Key claim

The paper predicts a sizable A-dependence in the ratio of nuclear cross sections for heavy nuclei relative to light ones for both gamma-induced and proton-induced production of the Lambda(1520), based on the theoretical result that the Lambda(1520) width in nuclear matter is about five times its free width at normal nuclear matter density.

## Method

The authors calculated the nuclear cross sections for gamma A -> K+ Lambda(1520) A' and p A -> p K+ Lambda(1520) A' using a local Fermi sea approximation, incorporating the in-medium selfenergy of the Lambda(1520) from a prior many-body calculation (Kaskulov et al., 2005). The propagation of the Lambda(1520) through the nucleus was treated with an eikonal approximation, and for the proton-induced reaction an additional eikonal factor accounted for the initial-state distortion of the incident proton. Ratios of cross sections for various nuclei relative to carbon-12 were evaluated to cancel uncertainties in the elementary production amplitude.

## Key numbers

- The free width of the Lambda(1520) is Gamma_free = 15.6 MeV
- The in-medium width at normal nuclear matter density (rho_0 = 0.17 fm^-3) is predicted to be approximately five times the free width
- For gamma-induced production at p_gamma = 2 GeV, the ratio (sigma_gamma_A / Z) normalized to carbon-12 reaches about 0.4 to 0.5 for heavy nuclei
- For proton-induced production at T_p = 2.9 GeV, the ratio of the total result to the result with only initial-state interaction decreases to about 0.4 for heavy nuclei
- Calculations were performed for 15 nuclei ranging from carbon-12 to uranium-238
- Photon momenta considered: 2, 2.5, and 3 GeV
- Proton kinetic energies considered: 2.5, 2.7, and 2.9 GeV

## Context

This work extends the method previously applied to phi meson production in nuclei (Kaskulov et al., 2005; Magas et al., 2005), where the A-dependence of the cross section was used to infer changes of the phi width in the nuclear medium. The calculation is motivated by the theoretical prediction that the Lambda(1520) width increases dramatically in medium due to the opening of the pi Sigma*(1385) decay channel, which is forbidden in free space. The results suggest that experiments at facilities such as Spring-8, ELSA, and COSY can determine the in-medium width of the Lambda(1520) by measuring the A-dependence of these production cross sections.
