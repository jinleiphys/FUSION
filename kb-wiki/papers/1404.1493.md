# Gamow-Teller strength in deformed nuclei within the self-consistent charge-exchange quasi-particle random-phase approximation with the Gogny force

> The charge-exchange excitations in nuclei are studied within the fully self-consistent proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation using the finite-range Gogny interaction. No additional parameters beyond those included in the effective nuclear force are included. Axially symmetric deformations are consistently taken into account, both in the description of the ground states and spin-isospin excitations. We focus on the isobaric analog and Gamow-Teller resonances. A comparison of the predicted strength distributions to the existing experimental data is presented and the role of nuclear deformation analyzed. The Gamow-Teller strength is used to estimate the beta-decay half-life of nuclei for which experimental data exist. A satisfactory agreement with experimental half-lives is found and justifies the additional study of the exotic neutron-rich N=82, 126 and 184 isotonic chains of relevance for the r-process nucleosynthesis.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first fully self-consistent axially-symmetric-deformed proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (pnQRPA) calculation using the finite-range Gogny force with no additional free parameters, and finds that the predicted Gamow-Teller strength distributions and beta-decay half-lives agree satisfactorily with experimental data, though the model systematically overestimates half-lives and slightly overestimates the Gamow-Teller peak energy.

## Method

The authors perform axially-symmetric-deformed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the Gogny D1M and D1S interactions to describe ground states, then build the pnQRPA on top of these HFB states to compute charge-exchange excitations. The same Gogny force is used consistently in all particle-hole, particle-particle, and hole-hole channels, with no additional parameters. The QRPA matrix equation is solved separately for each K-parity block, and the response to the Fermi and Gamow-Teller operators is computed; the Gamow-Teller strength is then used to estimate beta-decay half-lives via the allowed GT decay approximation.

## Key numbers

- The Gamow-Teller energy shift between D1M and D1S interactions rarely exceeds 0.5 MeV for the nuclei analyzed (Zr-90, Sn-114, Pb-208).
- The spin-orbit strength parameters are W_LS = 115.4 MeV fm^5 for D1M and 130.0 MeV fm^5 for D1S; the Landau parameter g_0' = 0.71 for D1M and 0.61 for D1S.
- The spreading width for folding the GT strength is parametrized as Gamma [MeV] = 1 + 0.055 E_ex^2, with an upper limit of 6 MeV, based on experimental GT widths in Sn isotopes with A = 112-124.
- For the deformed nucleus Ge-76, the HFB ground state minimum occurs at deformation parameter beta_2 = 0.15.
- The beta-decay half-life comparison covers 145 even-even nuclei with experimental half-life T_{1/2} <= 1000 s; predictions rarely deviate by more than one order of magnitude from experiment.
- For the N=82, 126, and 184 isotonic chains, the D1M+QRPA half-life predictions are systematically larger than the DF3+cQRPA calculations of Borzov (2013).

## Context

This work extends the fully self-consistent deformed QRPA approach with the Gogny force, previously applied to electromagnetic excitations by Peru (2008) and Martini (2011), into the charge-exchange sector. It contrasts with many previous pnQRPA calculations that either treat deformation approximately or introduce a free coupling constant in the particle-particle channel, as noted for Skyrme and relativistic mean-field models. The results enable future large-scale calculations of beta-decay half-lives and neutrino capture rates for r-process nucleosynthesis, and may inform studies of neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements.
