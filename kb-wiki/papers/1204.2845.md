# Shell model description of Ge isotopes

> A shell model study of the low energy region of the spectra in Ge isotopes for $38\leq N\leq 50$ is presented, analyzing the excitation energies, quadrupole moments, $B(E2)$ values and occupation numbers. The theoretical results have been compared with the available experimental data. The shell model calculations have been performed employing three different effective interactions and valence spaces.We have used two effective shell model interactions, JUN45 and jj44b, for the valence space $f_{5/2} \, p \,g_{9/2}$ without truncation. To include the proton subshell $f_{7/2}$ in valence space we have employed the $fpg$ effective interaction due to Sorlin {\it et al.}, with $^{48}$Ca as a core and a truncation in the number of excited particles.

## Key claim

The paper presents a shell model study of Ge isotopes (N=38–50) that finds no single effective interaction can consistently reproduce all observables; the JUN45 interaction works best for lighter isotopes (70,72,74Ge and 80Ge) while the fpg interaction works better for 76,78Ge, and all three interactions fail to reproduce the sign and magnitude of quadrupole moments in most cases.

## Method

Shell model calculations were performed for even-mass 70–82Ge isotopes using three different effective interactions and valence spaces: JUN45 and jj44b in the f5/2 p g9/2 valence space with a 56Ni core and no truncation; and the fpg interaction of Sorlin et al. in the f p g9/2 valence space with a 48Ca core, allowing up to four particle-hole excitations (tπ and tν ≤ 4) from the πf7/2 orbital to upper fp orbitals and from upper fp orbitals to the νg9/2 orbital. Excitation energies, B(E2) values, quadrupole moments, and occupation numbers were calculated and compared with available experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Experimental 21+ state in 70Ge at 1039 keV; predicted by JUN45 at 906 keV, jj44b at 737 keV, fpg at 478 keV
- Experimental 21+ state in 72Ge at 834 keV; predicted by JUN45 at 811 keV, jj44b at 710 keV, fpg at 504 keV
- B(E2;21+→0g.s.+) for 70Ge: experimental 36(4) × 10−3 e²b²; JUN45 gives 24.8/47.5, jj44b gives 33.7/67.7, fpg gives 55.5/99.5 (two effective charge sets separated by "/")
- B(E2;21+→0g.s.+) for 76Ge: experimental 46(3) × 10−3 e²b²; JUN45 gives 31.3/56.9, jj44b gives 34.8/63.9, fpg gives 53.6/93.7
- Quadrupole moment Q(21+) for 76Ge: experimental −14(4) × 10−2 eb; JUN45 gives +1.69/+4.55, jj44b gives −14.51/−18.63, fpg gives +30.04/+40.89
- Quadrupole moment Q(22+) for 72Ge: experimental +23(8) × 10−2 eb; JUN45 gives −13.46/−22.27, jj44b gives −11.31/−19.02, fpg gives −32.01/−44.79
- Neutron g9/2 occupation in 76Ge ground state: experimental 6.48±0.30; JUN45 gives 5.85, jj44b gives 6.16, fpg gives 5.87

## Context

This work extends the shell model study of 70,72,74,76Ge by Zamick's group (2011), which showed that JUN45 and jj44b interactions could not properly reproduce B(E2) strengths and Q values. The authors enlarge the valence space to include the πf7/2 orbital and extend calculations up to 82Ge (N=50). The results highlight the need to include upper neutron sdg shell orbitals following the developments of Lenzi et al. (Nowacki101), and identify the quadrupole moments as a particular challenge that none of the three models can consistently describe.
