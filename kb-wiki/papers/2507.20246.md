# Meson-Exchange Currents in Quasielastic Charged-Current Neutrino Reactions with Single-Nucleon Knockout

> The effect of meson-exchange currents on charged-current quasielastic neutrino scattering with single-nucleon emission is computed and analyzed within the relativistic Fermi gas model. This contribution arises primarily from the interference between one-body and two-body currents, where the two-body operator excites a 1p1h state in the presence of a second, spectator nucleon. The results obtained show a reduction of the vector, axial and vector-axial transverse response functions and, consequently, a decrease in the total neutrino cross section. In addition to a comparison with the non-relativistic limit, other models are also explored, such as the relativistic mean field model for nuclear matter and the superscaling analysis with relativistic effective mass, both of which yield qualitatively similar results.

## Key claim

The interference between one-body and two-body currents (meson-exchange currents) in the 1p1h channel of charged-current quasielastic neutrino scattering reduces the vector, axial, and vector-axial transverse response functions, leading to a decrease in the total neutrino cross section; however, this result is obtained within the relativistic Fermi gas model without nucleon-nucleon correlations, and the sign and magnitude of the interference may depend on the nuclear model used.

## Method

The authors computed the hadronic tensor for 1p1h excitations within the relativistic Fermi gas model, including the interference between one-body and two-body currents. The two-body current operators (seagull, pion-in-flight, pion-pole, and Delta currents) were taken from a relativistic model previously used in electron scattering studies, and the effective one-body current from the two-body operator was obtained by summing over spectator nucleons in the Fermi sea. The response functions were evaluated numerically by integrating over hole energies, and the results were compared with the non-relativistic limit, the relativistic mean field model for nuclear matter, and the superscaling analysis with relativistic effective mass (SuSAM*) model.

## Key numbers

- The axial-vector form factor uses $g_A = 1.26$ and $M_A = 1032$ MeV
- The Delta width at rest is $\Gamma_0 = 120$ MeV
- The pion-nucleon form factor cutoff is $\Lambda = 1300$ MeV
- The vector form factor mass parameter is $M_V = 0.84$ GeV
- The axial Delta form factor mass parameter is $M_{A\Delta} = 1.05$ GeV
- The Fermi constant is $G_F = 1.666 \times 10^{-11}$ MeV$^{-2}$
- The pion-nucleon coupling constant is $f^2 = 1$ and the pion-nucleon-Delta coupling constant is $f^* = 2.13 f$

## Context

This work extends the study of meson-exchange current interference in the 1p1h channel from electron scattering (Casale 2025) to charged-current neutrino scattering, addressing an open issue where only one prior work (Umino 1995) had considered MEC in CCQE neutrino scattering in the 1p1h channel, without including the Delta current. The results are compared with the relativistic mean field model (Kim 1994, Kim 1996) and the SuSAM* model (Casale 2023), and the paper notes that variational calculations (Fabrocini 1997) showed the interference sign can reverse depending on correlations, while Green's function Monte Carlo results for $^4$He (Lovato 2016) showed a positive net MEC effect. This work enables future inclusion of MEC interference effects in neutrino event generators and provides a baseline for more sophisticated nuclear models.
