# Spectroscopy of the heaviest nuclei (theory)

> Recent progress in the applications of covariant density functional theory (CDFT) to the description of the spectroscopy of the heaviest nuclei is reviewed. The analysis of quasiparticle spectra in actinides and the heaviest A ~ 250 nuclei provides a measure of the accuracy of the description of single-particle energies in CDFT and an additional constraint for the choice of effective interactions for the description of superheavy nuclei. The response of these nuclei to the rotation is rather well described by cranked relativistic Hartree+Bogoliubov theory and it serves as a supplementary tool in configuration assignment in odd-mass nuclei. A systematic analysis of the fission barriers with allowance for triaxial deformation shows that covariant density functional theory is able to describe fission barriers on a level of accuracy comparable with the best phenomenological macroscopic+microscopic approaches.

## Key claim

Covariant density functional theory (CDFT) can describe the spectroscopy of the heaviest nuclei, including single-particle energies, rotational response, and fission barriers, at a level of accuracy comparable to the best phenomenological macroscopic+microscopic approaches, but only for specific parameterizations (NL1, NL3, NL3*) that predict spherical shell closures at Z=120 and N=172; other parameterizations (NLSH, NL-RA1) give unsatisfactory single-particle descriptions.

## Method

The authors review CDFT calculations of deformed one-quasiparticle states in actinides and A~250 nuclei using relativistic mean-field (RMF) plus BCS or relativistic Hartree+Bogoliubov (RHB) theory, with comparisons to experimental data for 251Cf, 241Am, 251Md, and 253No. Rotational bands in odd-mass nuclei are analyzed with cranked relativistic Hartree+Bogoliubov (CRHB) theory using the NL1 and NL3 parameterizations. A systematic study of inner fission barriers in even-even actinide nuclei is performed for the first time with triaxial deformation included, using the RMF+BCS approach with the NL3* parameterization and seniority pairing forces.

## Key numbers

- The energies of spherical subshells in A~250 nuclei are described with accuracy better than 0.5 MeV for most subshells in NL1 and NL3 parameterizations; discrepancies of 0.6-1.0 MeV exist for the π1h9/2 (NL3, NL1), ν1i11/2 (NL3), ν1j15/2 (NL1), and ν2g9/2 (NL3) subshells.
- The Lorentz mass (effective mass at the Fermi surface) in CDFT is m*(kF)/m ≈ 0.66.
- Allowing triaxial deformation reduces inner fission barrier heights by 1-3 MeV compared to axially symmetric solutions in the actinide nuclei studied.
- The average deviation between calculated and experimental inner fission barrier heights is 0.76 MeV for the triaxial RMF+BCS calculations with NL3*.
- Kinematic moments of inertia in the nuclei of interest are described with accuracy better than 10% of their absolute value, except for the rotational band in 255Lr where the discrepancy is around 15%.
- The kinematic moments of inertia for the π7/2[633] and π9/2[624] rotational bands in 251Es are described within 7% accuracy.

## Context

This review extends earlier CDFT work by Afanasjev et al. (2003) on one-quasiparticle states in 249,251Cf and 249Bk, and presents the first systematic triaxial fission barrier calculations in CDFT, filling a gap relative to non-relativistic approaches such as Skyrme energy density functional calculations (Bender et al., 2004) and macroscopic+microscopic methods (Dobrowolski et al., 2007; Moller et al., 2009). The results enable improved configuration assignments for rotational bands in odd-mass nuclei at the edge of the experimentally accessible region (A~255) and provide constraints on the location of the island of stability, which CDFT places at Z=120 and N=172, in contrast to Skyrme EDF predictions (Z=126, N=184) and macroscopic+microscopic predictions (Z=114, N=184).
