# Spectra and elliptic flow of light hadrons in an expanding fire-cylinder model for the RHIC Beam Energy Scan

> We investigate the transverse momentum spectra ($p_T$) and elliptic flow ($v_2$) of $π^{\pm}$, $K^{\pm}$, $p$, and $\bar{p}$ produced in peripheral Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 7.7$, 11.5, 19.6, 27, and 39 GeV in the Beam Energy Scan (BES) Program at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). The analysis is carried out within an expanding elliptic fire-cylinder model that incorporates longitudinal expansion and anisotropic transverse flow. Particle production at kinetic freeze-out is obtained using a local equilibrium distribution function with a blast-wave-like fluid velocity profile derived from the expansion dynamics of the elliptic fire-cylinder. The model parameters governing the collective expansion are first constrained by fitting the midrapidity $p_T$ spectra of $π^{\pm}$ and are then applied, without further adjustment, to $K^{\pm}$, $p$, and $\bar{p}$. The model provides a consistent description of the $p_T$ spectra and reproduces the qualitative behavior of the elliptic flow for all considered particle species.

## Key claim
The expanding elliptic fire-cylinder model, with parameters first constrained by fitting pion transverse momentum spectra, provides a consistent description of the transverse momentum spectra and reproduces the qualitative behavior of elliptic flow for pions, kaons, protons, and antiprotons across five beam energies in the RHIC Beam Energy Scan, though the model slightly underestimates the high-\(p_T\) region of proton and kaon spectra.

## Method
The authors model the expanding medium as an elliptic fire-cylinder with time-dependent transverse semi-axes \(a(t)\) and \(b(t)\) and longitudinal extent \(z_B(t)\), using a parametrization originally developed for thermal electromagnetic emission and heavy-quark diffusion. The fluid four-velocity is constructed from the expansion rates, and particle production at kinetic freeze-out is calculated via the Cooper-Frye prescription using a local equilibrium distribution function at constant laboratory time \(t_f\). The model has eight free parameters: chemical potential \(\mu\), kinetic freeze-out temperature \(T_{\rm kin}\), five parameters characterizing the expansion (\(A\), \(B\), \(v_\infty\), \(\Delta v\), \(v_0\)), and freeze-out time \(t_f\); these are first fitted to midrapidity \(p_T\) spectra of \(\pi^\pm\) and then applied without further adjustment to \(K^\pm\), \(p\), and \(\bar{p}\).

## Key numbers
- Kinetic freeze-out temperatures \(T_{\rm kin}\) range from 0.125 GeV at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 7.7\) GeV to 0.133 GeV at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 39\) GeV.
- Freeze-out times \(t_f\) range from 9.0 fm at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 7.7\) GeV to 10.5 fm at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 39\) GeV.
- The asymptotic transverse expansion velocity \(v_\infty\) increases from 0.45 at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 7.7\) GeV to 0.50 at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 39\) GeV.
- The anisotropic velocity component \(\Delta v\) is fixed at 0.1 for all five beam energies.
- The average surface velocity at freeze-out \(\langle v_B \rangle\) ranges from 0.403 at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 7.7\) GeV to 0.497 at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 39\) GeV, compared to STAR blast-wave values from 0.507 to 0.690.
- Kinetic freeze-out volumes \(V_{\rm kin}\) range from 1354.32 fm\(^3\) at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 7.7\) GeV to 2052.67 fm\(^3\) at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 39\) GeV.
- Proton chemical potentials \(\mu_p\) range from 323 MeV at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 7.7\) GeV to 124 MeV at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 39\) GeV; antiproton chemical potentials \(\mu_{\bar{p}}\) range from -238 MeV to -4 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the expanding elliptic fire-cylinder model of Gossiaux et al. (2011), originally applied to thermal electromagnetic emission and heavy-quark diffusion, to the systematic description of light hadron spectra and elliptic flow across the RHIC Beam Energy Scan. It contrasts with standard blast-wave models that assume boost-invariant longitudinal expansion and radially symmetric transverse flow, which become inadequate at low energies and for peripheral collisions. The model reproduces the decreasing trend of freeze-out spatial eccentricity with increasing beam energy seen in STAR HBT measurements (STAR Collaboration, 2014) and yields Gaussian rapidity distributions consistent with observations at CERN and GSI (NA49 Collaboration, 2002; HADES Collaboration, 2020). The approach enables a unified description of soft hadron production where differences between particle species arise solely from their masses and chemical potentials.
