# Global trends of nuclear $d_{5/2}^{2,3,4}$ configurations: Application of a simple effective-interaction model

> With new experimental information on nuclei far from stability being available, a systematic investigation of excitation energies and electromagnetic properties along the $N=10, 11, 12$ isotones and $Z=10, 11, 12$ isotopes is presented. The experimental data are discussed in the context of the appearance and disappearance of shell closures at $N=Z=8,14,16,20$, and compared to an effective-interaction approach applied to neutrons and protons in $d_{5/2}^{2,3,4}$ configurations. In spite of its simplicity the model is able to explain the observed properties.

# Global trends of nuclear $d_{5/2}^{2,3,4}$ configurations: Application of a simple effective-interaction model

## Key claim
A simple effective-interaction model with only short-range (pairing) and long-range (quadrupole) terms applied to neutrons and protons in $d_{5/2}^{2,3,4}$ configurations can explain the observed excitation energies and electromagnetic properties across $N=10,11,12$ isotones and $Z=10,11,12$ isotopes, including the appearance and disappearance of shell closures at $N=Z=8,14,16,20$, but the model may be limited for resonance states such as in $^{15}$Be where spin assignments remain tentative.

## Method
The authors compiled experimental data on $E(2^+_1)$, $E(4^+_1)$, $B(E2,2^+_1 \rightarrow 0^+ )$ for even-even nuclei and $E(3/2^+_1)$, $E(5/2^+_1)$, $E(9/2^+_1)$ for odd-A nuclei in the $d_{5/2}^{2,3,4}$ configurations. They applied Talmi's effective-interaction model using a two-body interaction $V_I = -G\delta_{I,0} + \chi I(I+1)$, extracting the ratio $\chi/G$ from dimensionless energy ratios $R_{24}$, $R_{39}$, and $R_{32}$ and comparing these to the $N_pN_n$ scheme and the Migdal parameter $\epsilon\hbar\omega_0/\Delta$.

## Key numbers
- $E(2^+_1)$ values range from 500(14) keV in $^{40}$Mg to 2018.28(10) keV in $^{26}$Ne for even-even nuclei in the $d_{5/2}^{2,4}$ configurations
- $B(E2,2^+_1 \rightarrow 0^+ )$ values range from 1.41(28) W.U. in $^{18}$C to 21.5(10) W.U. in $^{24}$Mg for $d_{5/2}^{2,4}$ configurations
- $R_{42}=E(4^+_1)/E(2^+_1)$ values range from 1.74247(26) in $^{26}$Ne to 3.102(41) in $^{34}$Mg
- The $3/2^+$ state becomes the ground state over the $5/2^+$ state for $\chi/G \gtrsim 1.4$ in the $d_{5/2}^3$ configuration
- For $^{33}$Na, $\chi/G = 30.7$ from $R_{39}$, the largest value observed, though the ($9/2^+$) spin assignment is tentative
- $^{28}$Ne has $\epsilon\hbar\omega_0/\Delta = 0.65$, $^{38}$Mg has $\epsilon\hbar\omega_0/\Delta = 0.63$, and $^{29}$Na has $\epsilon\hbar\omega_0/\Delta = 0.67$, all located at structural regime interfaces

## Context
This work extends the effective-interaction approach of Talmi (1962, 1993) to systematically survey $d_{5/2}^{2,3,4}$ configurations across a broad region of the nuclear chart, building on earlier applications to specific nuclei such as $^{18}$N and $^{19}$O by Wiedeking et al. (2008). The results connect to the $N_pN_n$ scheme of Casten (1985, 1996) and the Migdal parameter framework, showing that the simple model captures the competition between pairing and quadrupole forces that drives the erosion of the $N=20$ shell closure in the island of inversion and the emergence of shell closures at $N=Z=14,16$. The work enables systematic interpretation of future experimental data on exotic nuclei in the $13<A<41$ region and identifies specific cases such as $^{15}$Be and $^{33}$Na where further experimental clarification of spin assignments is needed.
