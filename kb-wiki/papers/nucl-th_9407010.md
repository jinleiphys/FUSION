# Electromagnetic Hadron Mass Differences and Estimation of Isotopic Symmetry Violation Parameters of QCD Vacuum from Quark Model

> The estimations of the light quark mass differences, $m_d-m_u$, and the light quark condensate differences, $<\bar DD> - <\bar UU>$, are obtained in the framework of the \qm\ with QCD vacuum induced \q\ \ia . We consider long - wave condensate and short - wave \s\ contributions to the electromagnetic \hn\ mass \di s and show that the latter significantly improve the results on baryon octet. The results are: $m_d-m_u \approx 3.5\ MeV$ and $<\bar DD> - <\bar UU> \approx -(0\sim 3)\cdot10^{-3}<\bar UU>$.

## Key claim

The paper estimates the light quark mass difference \(m_d - m_u \approx 3.5\ \text{MeV}\) and the light quark condensate difference \(<\bar DD> - <\bar UU> \approx -(0\sim 3)\cdot10^{-3}<\bar UU>\), but these values depend on including both long-wave condensate and short-wave instanton contributions to electromagnetic hadron mass differences, with the latter significantly improving results for the baryon octet.

## Method

The author uses a version of the bag model where hadron constituent quarks interact with background QCD vacuum fields: long-wave condensates provide confinement, while short-range instantons (small-size vacuum fluctuations) generate spin-dependent forces. The total energy difference between isospin multiplet members is computed from kinetic energy, electromagnetic interaction, strong interaction (including vacuum condensate, instanton, and gluon hyperfine contributions), and center-of-mass motion corrections. The quark mass difference and condensate difference are then fitted to reproduce experimental electromagnetic mass differences of hadrons, particularly the Coleman-Glashow relation for the baryon octet.

## Key numbers

*   \(m_d - m_u \approx 3.5\ \text{MeV}\) (light quark mass difference, fitted from hadron mass differences).
*   \(<\bar DD> - <\bar UU> \approx -(0\sim 3)\cdot10^{-3}<\bar UU>\) (light quark condensate difference, with lower bound \(0 > \gamma > -0.003\)).
*   \(p - n = -1.23\ \text{MeV}\) (calculated) vs. \(-1.2933 \pm 0.0002\ \text{MeV}\) (experimental).
*   \(\Sigma^+ - \Sigma^- = -8.06\ \text{MeV}\) (calculated) vs. \(-8.07 \pm 0.09\ \text{MeV}\) (experimental).
*   \(p - n + \Xi^0 - \Xi^- = -8.30\ \text{MeV}\) (calculated) vs. \(-7.7 \pm 0.6\ \text{MeV}\) (experimental).
*   \(\Sigma^+ + \Sigma^- - 2\Sigma^0 = 1.71\ \text{MeV}\) (calculated, at bag radius \(R = 5.6\ \text{GeV}^{-1}\)) vs. \(1.71 \pm 0.15\ \text{MeV}\) (experimental).
*   Bag stability radius for baryon octet: \(R = 5.6\ \text{GeV}^{-1}\).
*   Gluon condensate: \(<0| \frac{\alpha_s}{\pi} G^2 |0> = 0.031\ \text{GeV}^4\).
*   Quark condensate: \(<0| \bar UU |0> = -(221\ \text{MeV})^3\).
*   Instanton packing fraction: \(f \approx 1/50\).

## Context

This work extends earlier bag model calculations of electromagnetic mass differences by Deshpande et al. (1977) and Bickerstaff and Thomas (1982), and incorporates instanton effects previously considered by Dorokhov and Kochelev (1988). It contrasts with QCD sum rule approaches by Brown (1991), Ioffe (1993), and Kisslinger (1993), which the author argues miss the important short-range instanton interaction. The results enable more precise determination of isospin symmetry violation parameters in QCD and suggest future application to \(D\) and \(D^*\) isospin mass differences.
