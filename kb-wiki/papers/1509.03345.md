# The magnetized effective QCD phase diagram

> The QCD phase diagram in the temperature versus quark chemical potential plane is studied in the presence of a magnetic field, using the linear sigma model coupled to quarks. It is shown that the decrease of the couplings with increasing field strength obtained in this model leads to the critical temperature for the phase transition to decrease with increasing field intensity (inverse magnetic catalysis). This happens provided that plasma screening is properly accounted for. It is also found that with increasing field strength the location of the critical end point (CEP) in the phase diagram moves toward lower values of the critical quark chemical potential and larger values of the critical temperature. In addition, the CEP approaches the temperature axis for large values of the magnetic field. We argue that a similar behavior is to be expected in QCD, since the physical impact of the magnetic field, regardless of strength, is to produce a spatial dimension reduction, whereby virtual quark-antiquark pairs are closer on average and thus, the strength of their interaction decreases due to asymptotic freedom.

## Key claim

The paper shows that, within the linear sigma model coupled to quarks, the critical temperature for the chiral phase transition decreases with increasing magnetic field strength (inverse magnetic catalysis) only when plasma screening is properly accounted for and when the effective couplings are allowed to decrease with field strength; the critical end point moves toward lower quark chemical potential and higher temperature as the magnetic field increases.

## Method

The authors compute the one-loop effective potential for the linear sigma model coupled to quarks in a constant external magnetic field at finite temperature and quark chemical potential, using Schwinger's proper-time method in a high-temperature expansion. They then calculate the one-loop thermo-magnetic corrections to the boson self-coupling λ and the fermion-boson coupling g in the static limit, evaluating these effective couplings at v=0. The critical temperature is determined by setting the second derivative of the effective potential to zero at v=0 for second-order transitions, or by finding degenerate minima for first-order transitions, using the field-dependent effective couplings.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature at zero magnetic field and zero chemical potential is taken as T_c^0 = 172 MeV for two light quark flavors.
- The sigma meson vacuum mass is taken as m_σ = 300 MeV, giving a/T_c^0 = 0.77.
- Bare coupling values used are λ = 0.4 and g = 0.63 for the primary analysis.
- For the alternative parameter set, T_c^0 = 200 MeV gives a/T_c^0 = 0.66 with bare couplings λ = 0.36 and g = 0.51.
- The analysis is restricted to the weak field regime where qB/T^2 < 1.

## Context

This work extends the linear sigma model with quarks (LSMq) to include magnetic fields, building on prior lattice QCD results showing inverse magnetic catalysis (Fodor 2012, Bali 2012) and on earlier effective model studies that failed to reproduce this effect without magnetic field-dependent couplings (Andersen 2012, Fraga 2012). The authors previously showed that coupling constants decrease with magnetic field in the Abelian Higgs model and LSMq (Ayala 2014, Ayala 2015). The results enable predictions for how the critical end point location changes with magnetic field, which can be tested against future lattice QCD calculations at finite density.
