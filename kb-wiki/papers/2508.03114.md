# Response of asymmetric nuclear matter studied with a finite number of particles

> We investigate the ground-state properties of asymmetric nuclear matter and its response to a static perturbation using the density functional theory framework. Our method, which extends the finite-nucleon-number technique of arXiv:2211.07986 to the case of isospin-asymmetric matter, allows to study the impact of external isoscalar and isovector fields on the system. In particular, the densities and static response functions in the different isospin channels, and as a function of isospin asymmetry, are evaluated. Finite-size effects are discussed by comparing with Random Phase Approximation predictions in the thermodynamic limit. A peculiar non-monotonic behavior of the isovector response function is analyzed.

## Key claim

The paper extends the finite-nucleon-number density functional theory technique, previously limited to symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter, to asymmetric nuclear matter (ANM) with arbitrary isospin asymmetry, enabling the study of static response to both isoscalar and isovector external fields. The authors find that finite-size effects can be controlled by choosing magic numbers of neutrons and protons (specifically N=186 and Z=114) such that the kinetic energy deviation from the thermodynamic limit is minimized, and they report a peculiar non-monotonic behavior of the isovector response function.

## Method

The authors place a finite number of nucleons (neutrons and protons) in a cubic box with periodic boundary conditions, using the Kohn-Sham formulation of density functional theory with a Skyrme-type energy density functional. They solve the self-consistent Kohn-Sham equations separately for neutrons and protons in the presence of static external cosine potentials of the form \(v(z)=2v_q\cos(qz)\) in either the isoscalar or isovector channel, extracting the static response functions from the quadratic dependence of the energy per particle on the field strength. The finite-system results are benchmarked against analytic Random Phase Approximation response functions in the thermodynamic limit to quantify finite-size effects.

## Key numbers

- The system with N=186 neutrons and Z=114 protons (isospin asymmetry \(\beta=0.24\)) yields a kinetic energy very close to the thermodynamic limit while remaining computationally manageable.
- The relative difference between the finite-system equation of state and the thermodynamic limit drops below 0.1% for densities above 0.05 fm\(^{-3}\).
- External field strengths used for response extraction range from \(0.01\epsilon_F^N\) to \(0.16\epsilon_F^N\), where \(\epsilon_F^N\) is the neutron Fermi energy.
- For the isoscalar response, the quadratic fit of energy versus field strength is accurate for transferred momenta \(q > 2q_{\rm min}\), where \(q_{\rm min}=2\pi/L\).
- Fourier components of perturbed densities show higher harmonics suppressed by factors of \(10^{-1.50}\) to \(10^{-17.11}\) relative to the dominant mode, depending on the channel and momentum transfer.

## Context

This work extends the finite-nucleon DFT method of Marino et al. (2023, Phys. Rev. C 107, 044311), which was limited to symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter, to the case of isospin-asymmetric matter. It builds on the "Jacob's ladder" approach to constructing ab initio-based nuclear energy density functionals, following the strategy explored by Marino et al. (2021) and Colo et al. The ability to compute static response functions in asymmetric matter with controlled finite-size effects enables future mapping of ab initio calculations (such as quantum Monte Carlo or coupled-cluster results) onto energy density functionals for neutron-rich systems, which is relevant for understanding neutron star crust and outer core properties.
