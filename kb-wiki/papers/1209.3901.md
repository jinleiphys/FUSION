# Contribution of N* and Delta* resonances in K* Sigma(1190) photoproduction

> In this talk, we report theoretical studies on the K*0 Sigma+(1190) photoproduction in the tree-level Born approximation, employing the effective Lagrangian method. We present the energy and angular dependences of the cross sections. It turns out that the N* and Delta* resonance contributions are negligible in the vicinity of the threshold. On the contrary, we observe that the kappa and K exchanges in the t channel and Delta(1232) in the s channel dominate the scattering process, reproducing the experimental data qualitatively well.

## Key claim

The authors claim that, for the specific reaction γp → K*0Σ+(1190), the contributions from N* and Δ* resonance states (such as F15(2000), D13(2080), G17(2190), D15(2200), F35(2000), G37(2200), and F37(2390)) are almost negligible in the energy and angular dependences of the cross sections near threshold, contrary to what is observed in KΛ and K*Λ photoproduction.

## Method

The authors employed the tree-level Born approximation with an effective Lagrangian method, including Feynman diagrams for s-channel nucleon and Δ(1232) exchanges, u-channel Σ(1190) and Σ*(1385) exchanges, and t-channel K and κ meson exchanges. Strong coupling constants for ground-state baryons were taken from experimental data and the Nijmegen soft-core potential model (NSC97a), while couplings for baryon resonances were computed using the SU(6) quark model. Magnetic transition strengths for the resonances were estimated from experimental and theoretical helicity amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- Photon energy range studied: Eγ = 1.925 to 2.475 GeV
- Total cross sections are slightly underestimated for Eγ = 2 to 2.5 GeV compared to experimental data from TAPS (Nanova 2008)
- The D13 and G17 resonances provide "sizable contributions" near threshold, but these are still far smaller than the Born contributions
- The strong couplings for the Σ channel computed by the SU(6) quark model are much smaller than those for the Λ channel (Capstick 1998)

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies on K* photoproduction using effective Lagrangian methods (Oh 2006) and chiral quark models (Zhao 2001), and is compared to experimental data from the CLAS collaboration (Hleiqawi 2007) and the TAPS collaboration at CBELSA (Nanova 2008). The finding that resonance contributions are negligible for the Σ channel is markedly different from the K*Λ (Oh 2006, Kim 2011) and KΛ (Janssen 2001) photoproductions, which the authors attribute to much smaller strong couplings for the Σ channel. This work enables future studies to focus on the dominant t-channel strange meson exchanges and s-channel Δ(1232) contributions for this specific reaction.
