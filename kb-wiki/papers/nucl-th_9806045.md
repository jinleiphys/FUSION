# Forming Disoriented Chiral Condensates through Fluctuations

> Using the influence functional formalism, classical equations of motion for the O(N) model are derived in the presence of a heat bath, in both the symmetric phase as well as the phase of spontaneously broken symmetry. The heat bath leads to dissipation and fluctuation terms in the classical equations of motion, which are explicitly computed to lowest order in perturbation theory. In the broken phase these terms are found to be large for the sigma field, even at zero temperature, due to the decay process sigma -> pi pi, while they are small for the pi fields at temperatures below T_c = 160 MeV. It is shown that in large volumes the presence of dissipation and fluctuations suppresses the formation of disoriented chiral condensates (DCC's). In small volumes, however, fluctuations become sufficiently large to induce the formation of DCC's even if chiral symmetry has not been restored in the initial stage of the system's evolution.

## Key claim

The formation of disoriented chiral condensates (DCCs) is suppressed in large volumes by dissipation and fluctuations, but in small volumes fluctuations become large enough to induce DCC formation even if chiral symmetry has not been restored in the initial stage of the system's evolution, and even at zero temperature.

## Method

The author derives classical equations of motion for the O(N) model in the presence of a heat bath using the influence functional formalism, separating hard (short-wavelength) from soft (long-wavelength) modes and tracing over the hard degrees of freedom. The dissipation and fluctuation terms are computed perturbatively to lowest order in the coupling constant, both in the symmetric phase and in the phase of spontaneously broken symmetry, where additional three-particle interactions appear. The analysis focuses on the homogeneous (k=0) modes of the sigma and pion fields, with numerical solutions of the equations of motion at zero temperature.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration is T_c = 160 MeV.
- The dissipation coefficient for the sigma field at zero temperature is eta_sigma = 600 MeV, corresponding to the sigma mass m_sigma.
- The dissipation coefficient for the pion fields approaches zero as T approaches 0 or in the chiral limit m_pi approaches 0.
- DCC formation becomes likely in small volumes of approximately 10 fm^3.
- The coupling constant for the O(N) model is lambda = 20.

## Context

This work extends the treatment of dissipation and fluctuations in DCC formation by Rischke and collaborators (1997), who computed dissipation terms in the chirally symmetric phase and then used them for the broken phase, and who inferred fluctuation variances from a high-temperature approximation. The present paper corrects both approximations by computing dissipation and fluctuation terms directly in the broken phase, where the sigma field has a large dissipation coefficient even at T=0 due to the decay sigma -> pi pi. This enables future dynamical simulations including finite-momentum modes and realistic temperature evolution, and suggests that DCCs may form more readily in collisions of lighter ions or pp collisions (potentially explaining CENTAURO events) than in heavy-ion collisions.
