# Generalized Parton Distributions and Generalized Distribution Amplitudes : New Tools for Hadronic Physics

> The generalized parton distributions and the generalized distribution amplitudes give access to a deeper understanding of the quark and gluon content of hadrons. In this short review, we select some new developments of their interesting connections with the physics information that one can extract from exclusive reactions at medium and high energies.

## Key claim

Generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs) provide new access to understanding the quark and gluon content of hadrons, but extracting them from exclusive reactions requires studying multiple observables across different processes, and the cleanest channels are deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) and meson electroproduction at large photon virtuality \(Q^2\) and small momentum transfer \(t\).

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical definitions and experimental extraction of GPDs from DVCS (\(\gamma^* N \to \gamma N'\)) and meson electroproduction (\(\gamma^* N \to M N'\)), where the virtual photon has large spacelike \(Q^2\) and the invariant momentum transfer \(t\) to the proton is small. It also discusses the crossed version, GDAs, which describe the exclusive hadronization of a \(q\bar{q}\) or \(gg\) pair into a pair of hadrons (e.g., \(\pi\pi\)) via the process \(\gamma^* \gamma \to \pi\pi\) measurable in electron-positron colliders. The review further applies GDAs to search for the Odderon via charge asymmetry in two-pion diffractive electroproduction.

- DVCS spatial resolution is of order \(\Delta b_T \sim \hbar / \sqrt{Q^2}\), with \(Q^2 > \text{GeV}^2\) required for perturbative QCD applicability.

## Context

This review extends the theoretical framework of GPDs and GDAs as developed by the author and collaborators (e.g., Diehl, Gousset, Ralston, Szymanowski, Teryaev) and references prior work on DVCS (GPD, 2002) and timelike Compton scattering (TCS, 2002). It builds on experimental efforts at HERMES (DESY) and CEBAF (JLab) and enables future real-space imaging of hadrons ("femtophotography") with sub-femtometer resolution, as well as a new search for the Odderon at the amplitude level via charge asymmetries, complementing earlier Odderon searches in elastic \(pp\) scattering (Dosh, 2002) and meson production (Olsson, 2002; Dosh, 2002).
