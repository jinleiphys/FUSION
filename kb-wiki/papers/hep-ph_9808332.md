# Real time simulations of high energy nuclear collisions

> We discuss real time simulations of high energy nuclear collisions in a classical effective theory of QCD at small x. At high transverse momenta, our results match the lowest order predictions of pQCD based mini-jet calculations. We discuss novel non-perturbative behaviour of the small x modes seen at small transverse momenta.

## Key claim

The authors present first results from real-time simulations of the full, non-perturbative evolution of classical non-Abelian Weizsacker-Williams fields in high energy nuclear collisions, showing that at high transverse momenta the results match lowest-order perturbative QCD mini-jet calculations while revealing novel non-perturbative behavior at small transverse momenta where infrared singularities are naturally regulated by the scale \(k_t \sim \alpha_S \mu\).

## Method

The authors discretize the Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian in 2+1 dimensions coupled to an adjoint scalar field, which describes the evolution of small-x classical gauge fields under the assumption of boost invariance. Initial conditions for the lattice evolution are the Weizsacker-Williams fields of the two nuclei before the collision, depending on a single dimensional parameter \(\mu\) (the color charge per unit area per unit rapidity). The lattice equations of motion are solved numerically for a range of \(\mu\) values from 0.015 to 0.2 and lattice sizes from \(10\times 10\) to \(160\times 160\) in units of the lattice spacing, and results are compared to lattice perturbation theory (LPTh).

## Key numbers

* Scalar kinetic energy density \(\langle E_k \rangle / (L^2 \mu^4)\) is plotted as a function of lattice size \(L\) for \(\mu = 0.025\) and \(\mu = 0.05\), showing agreement with the LPTh prediction (solid line) within error bars smaller than the plotting symbols.
* The ratio \(\langle |A_\perp(\tau)|^2 \rangle / \langle |A_\perp(0)|^2 \rangle\) is shown as a function of proper time \(\tau\) for \(\mu = 0.025\) on a \(160\times 160\) lattice, with the ratio decreasing from approximately 1 at \(\tau=0\) to near 0.2 at \(\tau \approx 2.5\).
* Simulations are performed for \(\mu\) values in the range 0.015 to 0.2 and lattice sizes from \(10\times 10\) to \(160\times 160\) in units of the lattice spacing.

## Context

This work extends the classical effective theory approach to QCD at small x developed by McLerran and Venugopalan (1994) and later applied to nuclear collisions by Kovner, McLerran and Weigert (1995), who formulated the problem as the collision of Weizsacker-Williams fields and computed perturbative classical gluon radiation. The non-perturbative lattice simulation enables study of the full space-time evolution of the nuclei beyond the perturbative regime, which is essential for understanding possible thermalization of the system and providing initial temperature and velocity profiles for hydrodynamic evolution models such as those of Kolb, Kharzeev and Venugopalan (1998).
