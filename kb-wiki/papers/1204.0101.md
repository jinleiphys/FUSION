# Role of higher order couplings in the presence of kaons in relativistic mean field description of neutron stars

> We discuss the role of higher order couplings in conjunction with kaon condensation using recent versions of relativistic mean field models.We focus on an interaction (G2) in which all the parameters are obtained by fitting the finite nuclear data and successfully applied to reproduce a variety of nuclear properties. Our results show that the higher order couplings play a significant role at higher densities where kaons dominate the behavior of the equation of state. We compare our results with other interactions (NLl, NL3, G1, and FSUGold) and show that the new couplings bring down the mass of a neutron star (NS), which is further reduced in the presence of kaons to yield results consistent with the present observational constraints. We show that the composition of the NS varies with the parameter sets.

## Key claim

The higher-order couplings present in the E-RMF model (specifically the G2 interaction) significantly soften the equation of state at high densities, and the inclusion of kaon condensation further reduces the maximum neutron star mass, bringing results into consistency with observational constraints, but only for certain parameter sets; the NL1 and NL3 models still overshoot observed masses even with kaons.

## Method

The authors extended the effective field theory motivated relativistic mean field (E-RMF) model by including kaons via minimal coupling to the meson fields, solving the coupled field equations for both non-kaonic and kaonic phases under charge neutrality and beta equilibrium. They used five parameter sets (NL1, NL3, G1, G2, and FSUGold) with different higher-order coupling constants, all fitted to finite nuclear data, and solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations for neutron stars.

## Key numbers

- For the G2 parameter set with kaon optical potential U_K = -120 MeV, the onset of kaon condensation occurs at a baryon density of approximately 0.6 fm^{-3}.
- The maximum neutron star mass from NL3 without kaons is approximately 2.8 M_sun, reduced to approximately 2.4 M_sun with kaons, still exceeding observational constraints.
- The saturation density for all parameter sets is between 0.148 and 0.154 fm^{-3}, with binding energy per nucleon between -16.07 and -16.43 MeV.
- The compression modulus K_infinity ranges from 212 MeV (NL1) to 271.76 MeV (NL3), with G1 and G2 both at 215 MeV.
- The effective nucleon mass ratio m_n*/m_n ranges from 0.571 (NL1) to 0.664 (G2).

## Context

This work extends the E-RMF model studies of Furnstahl, Serot, and Tang (1997) and the kaon condensation framework of Glendenning and Schaffner-Bielich (1999) by systematically including higher-order couplings in the Lagrangian. The results show that while standard RMF models (NL1, NL3) produce neutron star masses exceeding 2.4 M_sun even with kaons, the E-RMF models G1, G2, and FSUGold yield maximum masses consistent with the 1.97 ± 0.04 M_sun observation by Demorest et al. (2010). The work enables future studies of mixed phases and first-order phase transitions in kaon condensation, which may occur for optical potentials stronger than -160 MeV.
