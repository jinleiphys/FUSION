# Antibaryon-nucleus bound states

> We calculated antibaryon ($\bar{B}$ = $\bar{p}$, $\barΛ$, $\barΣ$, $\barΞ$) bound states in selected nuclei within the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model. The G-parity motivated $\bar{B}$-meson coupling constants were scaled to yield corresponding potentials consistent with available experimental data. Large polarization of the nuclear core caused by $\bar{B}$ was confirmed. The $\bar{p}$ annihilation in the nuclear medium was incorporated by including a phenomenological imaginary part of the optical potential. The calculations using a complex $\bar{p}$-nucleus potential were performed fully self-consistently. The $\bar{p}$ widths significantly decrease when the phase space reduction is considered for $\bar{p}$ annihilation products, but they still remain sizeable for potentials consistent with $\bar{p}$-atom data.

# Antibaryon-nucleus bound states

## Key claim
Antibaryon-nucleus bound states exist with deeply attractive potentials, but even after accounting for phase-space suppression of annihilation products, antiproton widths remain sizeable (hundreds of MeV) for potentials consistent with antiproton-atom data, corresponding to lifetimes of only about 1 fm/c.

## Method
The authors solved the coupled Dirac and Klein-Gordon equations self-consistently within the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model for a system of nucleons and one antibaryon (antiproton, antilambda, antisigma, anti-Xi). The antibaryon-meson coupling constants were scaled from the baryon-meson couplings by a factor xi (set to 0.2) guided by G-parity and experimental data, and for antiprotons a phenomenological imaginary optical potential in t-rho form was added to account for annihilation, with phase-space suppression factors computed using the PLUTO Monte Carlo tool.

## Key numbers
- The antiproton-nuclear potential depth consistent with antiproton-atom data is in the range -(100-300) MeV in the nuclear interior.
- For antiproton 1s state in oxygen-16 with xi=0.2, the dynamical calculation with complex potential gives single-particle energy 175.6 MeV and width 552.3 MeV; including phase-space suppression reduces the width to 232.5 MeV.
- The antiproton width in oxygen-16 is further reduced by approximately 50 MeV when self-consistent evaluation of sqrt(s) including antiproton and nucleon momenta is performed.
- The antiproton lifetime in the nuclear medium is about 1 fm/c.
- Nucleon densities in antiproton nuclei reach 2-3 times nuclear matter density.
- The imaginary part of the optical potential parameter Im b0 = 1.9 fm for all nuclei considered, from fits to antiproton-atomic data.

## Context
This work extends the RMF calculations of Mishustin et al. (2005) on antiproton-nucleus bound states by performing fully self-consistent calculations including a complex potential for antiproton annihilation, and extends the study to antihyperons (antilambda, antisigma, anti-Xi) for the first time. The results contradict the conjecture that phase-space suppression could lead to relatively long-lived antiprotons inside nuclei, showing instead that widths remain sizeable. The calculations enable future experimental searches at FAIR for antibaryon-nucleus bound states and provide predictions for binding energies and widths across the periodic table.
