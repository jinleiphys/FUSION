# Polarized-deuteron scattering by spin-zero target nuclei at intermediate energies

> General analytical expressions for the observables in $dA$-scattering reaction have been derived in the diffraction approximation. The resulting formulas describe the cross section and polarization states of the deuteron when it scattering by nuclei with zero spin in the ground state. The tabulated distributions of the target nucleus density and the realistic deuteron wave functions calculated on the basis of Nijmegen nucleon-nucleon potentials were used. The nucleon-nucleus phases were calculated in the framework of Glauber formalism and making use of the double-folding potential. The calculated cross sections and analyzing powers in elastic scattering of deuterons by $^{16}\text{O}$ and $^{40}\text{Ca}$ nuclei at 700 MeV are compared with the corresponding experimental data.

## Key claim

General analytical expressions for deuteron-nucleus scattering observables are derived in the diffraction approximation, but the model's applicability is limited: the strong absorption version describes data only for scattering angles below 10 degrees, while adding refraction extends this to approximately 15-20 degrees, and vector analyzing powers are only described qualitatively except below 10 degrees.

## Method

The author derived analytical expressions for the cross section and spin-tensor components of polarized deuteron scattering from spin-zero nuclei using the Glauber eikonal approximation. The deuteron wave function (including S- and D-states) and nucleon-nucleus profile functions were expanded in Gaussian basis sets, with the profile functions calculated via the double-folding model using tabulated nuclear densities from DeVries (1987) and Nijmegen nucleon-nucleon potentials from Stoks (1994). The spin-orbit interaction parameters were determined from proton polarization experiments on 16O and 40Ca by Kelly (1991) and Frekers (1987), and a semi-microscopic approach added a real Woods-Saxon potential to the eikonal phase to improve agreement beyond the strong absorption model.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron projectile energy: 700 MeV for scattering on 16O and 40Ca targets
- Spin-orbit parameters for 16O target: delta = 0.77, gamma = 0.31 fm^2
- Spin-orbit parameters for 40Ca target: delta = 0.64, gamma = 0.34 fm^2
- Number of Gaussian expansion terms: N = 12 for both the deuteron wave function and profile functions
- Root-mean-square radius of 16O: 2.711 fm (from DeVries 1987)
- Mean-square radius of NN-interaction: r_NN^2 approximately 0.65 fm^2
- Strong absorption model valid only for scattering angles theta less than 10 degrees; semi-microscopic model extends validity to theta less than approximately 15-20 degrees

## Context

This work extends the diffraction approach of Kovalchuk (2015) for deuteron-nucleus scattering by incorporating realistic deuteron wave functions from Nijmegen potentials and tabulated nuclear densities, rather than using simplified model assumptions. The results are compared with experimental data from Nguyen (1985, 1987) for 16O and 40Ca at 700 MeV, and with calculations using a nine-parameter optical model by Nguyen (1987) and a relativistic folding model by Pinto (1996). The paper demonstrates that the semi-microscopic diffraction model with minimal fitting parameters can describe cross sections and tensor analyzing powers up to 20 degrees, but vector analyzing powers require more sophisticated spin-orbit treatments, pointing toward the need for optical model approaches with additional parameters.
