# The solution to a multichannel Bethe potential and its application to pion-nucleus reactions

> We solve a radial Schrödinger equation for the case of a multichannel square well plus an exponential potential in one of the channels. The solution is obtained by summing exactly the infinite terms of the perturbative series for the evolution operator of the system. Analytical expressions for the scattering matrix and the scattering length are given, a Deser-like formula which connects approximate ground energies and scattering length is obtained for a particular case. Possible physical applications of these results are discussed briefly.

## Key claim

The paper presents an exact analytical solution to a multichannel radial Schrödinger equation containing a square-well potential plus an exponential (Bethe) potential in one channel, obtained by summing the infinite perturbative series for the evolution operator. The authors note that while numerical solutions are readily available, exact analytical solutions serve as benchmarks for perturbative approximations and numerical calculations, and the non-relativistic formalism's shortcomings are "compensated by the advantages of having an exact analytical multichannel computation."

## Method

The authors reduce the second-order radial Schrödinger equation to a first-order system, diagonalize the free Hamiltonian, and compute the evolution operator by exactly summing the infinite perturbative series using the property that the interaction matrix W satisfies W²=0 and has a dyadic form. For the one-channel case, the series sums to generalized hypergeometric functions ₀F₁, and for the two-channel case, to ₂F₃ hypergeometric functions. The scattering matrix and scattering length are derived from the asymptotic form of the evolution operator.

## Key numbers

- The scattering function for the one-channel exponential potential is given by S(q) = - ₀F₁(1-2iq/λ, -ρ/λ²) / ₀F₁(1+2iq/λ, -ρ/λ²), where q² = k² - 2mV_N and ρ = 2mρ₀
- The scattering length for the one-channel case is 1/A = -(2/λ) [∂ₐ ₀F₁(1, -ρ/λ²) / ₀F₁(1, -ρ/λ²)]
- The derivative ∂ₐ ₀F₁(1,z) is expressed as J₀(2i√z)(log(i√z)+C) - (π/2)N₀(2i√z), where C is the Catalan number
- A Deser-like formula connects the approximate first eigenvalue shift to the scattering length: q₀ ≃ -iA

## Context

This work extends the approach of Bethe (1930s), who first used an exponential potential V(r)~e^{-r} to model the complex absorptive part of the short-range nuclear potential, and the multichannel square-well models used by many authors (e.g., Newton, Baranger) in nuclear, atomic, and molecular physics. The method is intended for application to pionic atoms, including deeply bound pionic states in Pb recently discovered at GSI (Yamazaki, 1990s) and predicted earlier (Friedman, Toki), as well as pion transfer reactions and neutron halo effects in neutron-rich nuclei. The authors plan to apply this potential, complemented with a Coulomb potential, to describe pionium and pionic bound states in exotic atoms as a function of A and Z in future publications.
