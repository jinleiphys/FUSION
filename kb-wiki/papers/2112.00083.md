# Equation of state of QCD at finite chemical potential from an alternative expansion scheme

> The equation of state of Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) at finite density is currently known only in a limited range in the baryon chemical potential $μ_B$. This is due to fundamental shortcomings of traditional methods such as Taylor expansion around $μ_B=0$. In this contribution, we present an alternative scheme that displays substantially improved convergence over the Taylor expansion method. We calculate the alternative expansion coefficients in the continuum, and show our results for the thermodynamic observables up to $μ_B/T\le3.5$.

## Key claim

The authors propose an alternative expansion scheme for the QCD equation of state at finite baryon chemical potential, based on a temperature rescaling along lines of constant physics, which displays substantially improved convergence over the standard Taylor expansion method and allows reliable results up to \(\mu_B/T \le 3.5\).

## Method

The method exploits the observation that normalized baryon density, strangeness density, and second strangeness susceptibility at imaginary chemical potentials all exhibit a similar temperature dependence that can be collapsed onto the \(\mu_B=0\) curves via a \(\mu_B\)-dependent temperature rescaling \(T' = T(1 + \kappa_2(T) \hat{\mu}_B^2 + \kappa_4(T) \hat{\mu}_B^4 + \mathcal{O}(\hat{\mu}_B^6))\). The expansion coefficients \(\kappa_2^{ij}(T)\) and \(\kappa_4^{ij}(T)\) are extracted from lattice simulations at imaginary chemical potentials \(\hat{\mu}_B = i n \pi/8\) with \(n=0,...,8\) on multiple lattice spacings (\(32^3\times8\), \(40^3\times10\), \(48^3\times12\), \(64^3\times16\)), followed by a combined fit in \(\hat{\mu}_B^2\) and \(1/N_\tau^2\) to obtain continuum-extrapolated results. Thermodynamic observables at real chemical potential are then reconstructed by integrating the baryon density obtained from the rescaled \(\chi_2^B(T')\) relation.

## Key numbers

* Continuum-extrapolated results for thermodynamic observables are presented up to \(\mu_B/T \le 3.5\).
* The temperature rescaling parameter for baryon density is \(\kappa = 0.0205\) (temperature-independent fit, top right panel of Fig. 1).
* Lattice simulations were performed at imaginary chemical potentials \(\hat{\mu}_B = i n \pi/8\) with \(n = 0,...,8\).
* Four lattice volumes were used: \(32^3\times8\), \(40^3\times10\), \(48^3\times12\), and \(64^3\times16\).
* The analysis combined 144 separate analyses, dropping those with Q-values below 0.01, to include both statistical and systematic uncertainties.

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD equation of state to larger baryon chemical potentials than achievable with the standard Taylor expansion method, which is limited by poor precision on higher-order derivatives (e.g., the sixth \(\mu_B\)-derivative of the pressure is available only with modest precision, as shown by Borsanyi (2018) and Bazavov (2020)). The alternative expansion scheme is a reorganization of the Taylor series along trajectories of constant physics rather than constant temperature, and shows good agreement with the hadron resonance gas model at low temperatures. This enables first-principles input for hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions and for neutron star physics, where the equation of state at finite density is a crucial ingredient.
