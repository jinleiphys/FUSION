# QCD thermodynamics on the lattice

> A remarkable progress has been made in the understanding of the hot and dense QCD matter using lattice gauge theory. The issues which are very well understood as well those which require both conceptual as well as algorithmic advances are highlighted. The recent lattice results on QCD thermodynamics which are important in the context of the heavy ion experiments are reviewed. Instances of greater synergy between the lattice theory and the experiments in the recent years are discussed where lattice results could be directly used as benchmarks for experiments and results from the experiments would be a crucial input for lattice computations.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD has made remarkable progress in understanding hot and dense QCD matter, but the paper emphasizes that while results at zero baryon density are very robust, studies at finite density suffer from the sign problem and the existence of a critical end-point is still not conclusively proven from lattice studies.

## Method

The paper reviews lattice gauge theory methods for QCD thermodynamics, where the QCD partition function is discretized on a Euclidean spacetime lattice with spacing \(a\) and temperature \(T = 1/(N_\tau a)\). Thermodynamic observables such as the trace anomaly, pressure, energy density, and chiral condensate are computed from Monte-Carlo generated gauge configurations using improved staggered fermion discretizations (HISQ, stout-smeared, p4, asqtad) and Wilson fermions, with continuum extrapolation performed by taking the \(a \rightarrow 0\) limit along lines of constant physics defined by hadron masses.

## Key numbers

- Pure gauge transition temperature: \(T_c(\text{pure-gauge}) = 276(2)\) MeV using the string tension \(\sqrt{\sigma} = 425\) MeV to set the scale.
- Pseudo-critical temperatures from stout-smeared staggered fermions for physical quark masses: \(T_c = 170(4)(3)\) MeV for the renormalized Polyakov loop \(L_R\), \(157(3)(3)\) MeV for the subtracted chiral condensate \(\Delta_{l,s}\), and \(147(2)(3)\) MeV for the normalized chiral susceptibility \(\chi_R\).
- From HISQ fermions using O(4) scaling of the renormalized chiral condensate, the chiral and continuum extrapolated \(T_c = 154 \pm 9\) MeV, in excellent agreement with the stout result.
- The entropy density at high temperatures is almost 20% off from the value of a free gas of quarks and gluons even at about 1000 MeV; the pressure deviation is close to 25% of its free theory value.
- The speed of sound shows significant deviation from the conformal value \(1/\sqrt{3}\) even at temperatures about 500 MeV.

## Context

This review extends the long-standing lattice QCD program that predicted the quark-gluon plasma phase transition (Karsch, 2002) and builds on precision results from the HotQCD collaboration using HISQ fermions (Petreczky, 2012) and the Budapest-Wuppertal collaboration using stout-smeared fermions (Borsanyi et al., 2010). The results enable direct use of lattice data as benchmarks for heavy ion experiments at RHIC and ALICE, particularly the equation of state as input for relativistic hydrodynamic simulations, and motivate further work on the chiral anomaly's role in the QCD phase diagram and the search for a critical end-point at finite baryon density.
