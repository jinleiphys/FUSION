# Analysis of quantum correlations within the ground state of a three level Lipkin model

> The performance of beyond mean field methods in solving the quantum many body problem for fermions is usually characterized by the correlation energy measured with respect to the underlying mean field value. In this paper we address the issue of characterizing the amount of correlations associated to different approximations from a quantum information perspective. With this goal in mind, we analyze the traditional Hartree-Fock (HF) method with spontaneous symmetry breaking, the HF with symmetry restoration and the generator coordinate method (GCM) in a exactly solvable fermion model known as the three-level Lipkin model. To characterize correlations including entanglement and beyond we use the quantum discord between different partition orbitals. We find that for physically motivated partitions, the quantum discord of the exact ground state is reasonably well reproduced by the different approximations. However, other partitions create "fake quantum correlations" in order to capture quantum correlations corresponding to partitions for which the Hartree-Fock solution fails. Those are removed and redistributed through a symmetry restoration process.

## Key claim

The paper shows that while Hartree-Fock (HF), Projected Hartree-Fock (PHF), and Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) approximations can reasonably reproduce the quantum discord of the exact ground state for physically motivated orbital partitions, the HF solution creates "fake quantum correlations" for other partitions that are removed and redistributed through symmetry restoration.

## Method

The authors computed the quantum discord, a measure of total quantum correlations including entanglement, for the exact ground state of the three-level Lipkin model and compared it with the quantum discord for HF, PHF, and GCM approximate ground states. They analyzed three different 4-orbital subsystems corresponding to pairs of energy levels (n0,n1), (n0,n2), and (n1,n2), computing quantum discord between different orbital partitions within each subsystem as a function of the interaction parameter chi = V(N-1)/epsilon.

## Key numbers

- Two quantum phase transitions occur at chi = 1 and chi = 3, corresponding to spontaneous parity symmetry breaking in the HF ground state for the second and third orbitals respectively
- For the {n0,n1} subsystem with partition A={1,3}, B={0,2}, the GCM solution is "very accurate when chi >= 1 but identical to the HF and PHF solutions if chi <= 1"
- For the {n0,n1} subsystem with partition A={2,3}, B={0,1}, the HF quantum discord is zero for all values of chi, while PHF and GCM capture the exact state correlations
- For the two-orbital partition A={1}, B={0} within the {n0,n1} subsystem, the exact, GCM, and PHF ground states have zero quantum discord due to parity symmetry conservation, while the HF solution shows non-zero "fake quantum correlations"

## Context

This work extends the study of quantum correlations in exactly solvable many-body fermion models, building on prior analysis of the two-level Lipkin model (Lipkin 1965) and its entanglement properties (Vidal 2004, Bao 2021). The three-level Lipkin model was previously shown to exhibit two quantum phase transitions (Holzwarth 1977). The paper introduces quantum information measures, specifically quantum discord (Ollivier and Zurek 2001, Henderson and Vedral 2001), as a tool to characterize correlations in beyond-mean-field approximations, complementing traditional correlation energy measures. This enables future work on understanding how symmetry restoration and configuration mixing redistribute quantum correlations in many-body approximations.
