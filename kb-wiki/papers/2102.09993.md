# Evolution of initial stage fluctuations in the glasma

> We perform a calculation of the one- and two-point correlation functions of energy density and axial charge deposited in the glasma in the initial stage of a heavy ion collision at finite proper time. We do this by describing the initial stage of heavy ion collisions in terms of freely evolving classical fields whose dynamics obey the linearized Yang-Mills equations. Our approach allows us to systematically resum the contributions of high momentum modes that would make a power series expansion in proper time divergent. We evaluate the field correlators in the McLerran-Venugopalan model using the glasma graph approximation, but our approach for the time dependence can be applied to a general four-point function of the initial color fields. Our results provide analytical insight into the preequilibrium phase of heavy ion collisions without requiring a numerical solution to the Yang-Mills equations.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the time evolution of one- and two-point correlation functions of energy density and axial charge in the glasma at finite proper time, using linearized Yang-Mills equations to resum high-momentum modes that would otherwise make a power series expansion in proper time divergent; this approach is valid for UV-dominated quantities but introduces some gauge dependence and neglects screening effects from nonlinearities.

## Method

The authors describe the initial stage of heavy ion collisions using freely evolving classical fields obeying the linearized Yang-Mills equations, with initial conditions at proper time τ=0⁺ given by the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model. They evaluate field correlators using the glasma graph approximation (a Gaussian decomposition of four-point functions into products of two-point functions) and compute the time evolution of the energy density and axial charge two-point functions by propagating the full nonlinear initial conditions through the linearized equations. The UV divergence of the MV model at τ=0⁺ is regularized by a running coupling prescription applied to the prefactors of the gluon distributions.

## Key numbers

- The energy density expectation value at τ=0⁺ in the GBW model is ⟨ε₀⟩ = C_F Q²_{s1} Q²_{s2} / g², where C_F = (N_c² − 1)/(2N_c)
- The infrared regulator mass is taken as m = 0.1 Q_s
- In the UV limit r→0, the MV model unpolarized gluon distribution G_MV(r_⊥) approaches Q_s²/(g²N_c), and the linearly polarized distribution h_MV(r_⊥) approaches 0
- The normalized energy density two-point function at τ=0⁺ in the GBW model is given by ⟨ε₀(x_⊥)ε₀(y_⊥)⟩/(⟨ε₀(x_⊥)⟩⟨ε₀(y_⊥)⟩) − 1 = [3/(N_c²−1)] × [(1/3)((1−e^{−Q_s²r²/4})/(Q_s²r²/4))⁴ + (2/3)((1−e^{−Q_s²r²/4})/(Q_s²r²/4))²]
- The normalized axial charge two-point function at τ=0⁺ in the GBW model is ⟨ν̇₀(x_⊥)ν̇₀(y_⊥)⟩/(⟨ε₀(x_⊥)⟩⟨ε₀(y_⊥)⟩) = [3/(8(N_c²−1))] × ((1−e^{−Q_s²r²/4})/(Q_s²r²/4))⁴

## Context

This work extends previous analytical calculations of glasma correlators at τ=0⁺ by Lappi (2006), Lappi (2017), and Albacete (2018) to finite proper time, providing analytical insight into the preequilibrium phase without requiring numerical solutions to the Yang-Mills equations as in the IP-glasma model by Schenke (2012). The approach builds on the method proposed by Fujii (2008) of using linearized Yang-Mills equations to resum singularities in the proper time expansion, and it enables analytical modeling of early-time fluctuations that serve as initial conditions for hydrodynamic simulations of heavy ion collisions.
