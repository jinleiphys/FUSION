# Induced Polarization in the $^2$H($γ,\vec n$)$^1$H Reaction at Low Energy

> The induced polarization, $P^\prime_y$, of the neutron in the deuteron photo-disintegration from threshold up to 30 MeV is calculated using a variety of different, latest-generation potentials--Argonne $v_{18}$, Bonn 2000, and Nijmegen I-- and a realistic model for the nuclear electromagnetic current operator, including one- and two-body terms. The model dependence of the theoretical predictions is found to be very small. These predictions are systematically larger in magnitude than the measured $P^\prime_y$ values, and corroborate the conclusions of an earlier, and much older, study. There is considerable scatter in the available experimental data. New and more accurate measurements of the induced polarization in the $^2$H($γ,\vec n$)$^1$H reaction are needed in order to establish unequivocally whether there is a discrepancy between theory and experiment.

# Induced Polarization in the $^2$H($\gamma,\vec n$)$^1$H Reaction at Low Energy

## Key claim
The theoretical predictions for the induced polarization $P^\prime_y$ of the neutron in deuteron photodisintegration from threshold to 30 MeV are systematically larger in magnitude than the measured values, with very small model dependence across modern potentials, but the experimental data show considerable scatter, preventing a definitive conclusion about a theory-experiment discrepancy.

## Method
The induced polarization $P^\prime_y$ was calculated using the Argonne $v_{18}$, Bonn 2000, and Nijmegen I nucleon-nucleon potentials with a realistic nuclear electromagnetic current operator including one- and two-body terms. The $n$$p$ scattering state included all channels up to total angular momentum $J=5$, with higher partial waves found numerically negligible. The Siegert form was used for the $E1$ operator, and two-body currents included $\pi$- and $\rho$-meson exchanges, $\Delta$-excitation, and $\rho\pi\gamma$ and $\omega\pi\gamma$ transition mechanisms.

## Key numbers
- The induced polarization $P^\prime_y$ was calculated for photon energies from threshold up to 30 MeV, with data shown at $E_\gamma = 2.75$ MeV for angular distributions and at $\theta = 90^\circ$, $45^\circ$, and $135^\circ$ for $E_\gamma = 6$–30 MeV.
- At the highest energy, relativistic corrections to the electric dipole operator reduce the AV18 IA results by about 5% in magnitude for $P^\prime_y$, while in the total cross section they decrease the AV18 IA values by less than 1% over the whole $E_\gamma$ range.
- The total cross section is reduced by less than 1% for $E_\gamma$ in the 4–6 MeV range when the $M1$ transition due to the $^1$S$_0$ state is neglected.
- The contributions of higher partial waves (beyond S- and P-waves) to $P^\prime_y$ are substantial at the highest photon energies.

## Context
This work extends the earlier study of Hadjimichael (1973) and Schmitt et al. (1991), who also found discrepancies between theory and experiment for the induced polarization. The calculations use modern potentials (Argonne $v_{18}$, Bonn 2000, Nijmegen I) and two-body currents that successfully reproduce the $n$$p$ radiative capture cross section at thermal neutron energies (Schiavilla 2004, Marcucci 2005). The results show that two-body currents, essential for the capture cross section, worsen the agreement for $P^\prime_y$ except at $\theta = 135^\circ$, and new, more accurate measurements are needed to resolve the discrepancy.
