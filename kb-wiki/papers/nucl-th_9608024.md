# Hydrodynamics and collective behaviour in relativistic nuclear collisions

> Hydrodynamics is applied to describe the dynamics of relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The focus of the present study is the influence of a possible (phase) transition to the quark-gluon plasma in the nuclear matter equation of state on collective observables, such as the lifetime of the system and the transverse directed flow of matter. It is shown that such a transition leads to a softening of the equation of state, and consequently to a time-delayed expansion which is in principle observable via two-particle correlation functions. Moreover, the delayed expansion leads to a local minimum in the excitation function of transverse directed flow around AGS energies.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a phase transition to the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) in the nuclear matter equation of state (EoS) leads to a "softening" of the EoS and a time-delayed expansion, which is observable via two-particle correlation functions and a local minimum in the excitation function of transverse directed flow around AGS energies; however, the strength of these signals depends on the width of the transition region (ΔT), which is only constrained by lattice data to be in the range 0 ≤ ΔT < 0.1 T_c, and on the ratio of degrees of freedom d_Q/d_H.

## Method

The study uses relativistic ideal hydrodynamics to model the collective expansion of nuclear matter in heavy-ion collisions, with the EoS as the only input linking matter properties to dynamics. The EoS is parametrized from lattice QCD data for the entropy density, allowing for either a sharp first-order phase transition (ΔT = 0) or a smooth transition (ΔT = 0.1 T_c), and the hydrodynamic equations are solved numerically using the relativistic Harten-Lax-van Leer-Einfeldt algorithm (for 1+1 dimensional Landau and Bjorken models) and the SHASTA algorithm (for 3+1 dimensional semi-peripheral Au+Au collisions). The lifetime of the system and the ratio of inverse widths of two-pion correlation functions (R_out / R_side) are computed from the freeze-out hypersurface at T = 0.7 T_c, and the transverse directed flow per baryon is calculated for Au+Au collisions at impact parameter b = 3 fm.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature: T_c ~ 160 MeV (from lattice QCD).
- Width of the transition region: 0 ≤ ΔT < 0.1 T_c ~ 16 MeV.
- For d_H = 3 (pion gas) and d_Q = 37 (two massless quark flavours and eight massless gluons), the latent heat is 4B = 1.7 T_c s_c ~ 1.272 GeV fm^{-3}.
- For d_Q/d_H = 3 (lower limit), the latent heat is ε_Q - ε_H = T_c s_c, corresponding to about 400 MeV fm^{-3} in physical units.
- In the Landau model with ΔT = 0 and initial energy density ε_0 ~ ε_Q ~ 1.4 GeV fm^{-3}, temperatures as high as T_c persist for times as long as 42 R (where R is half the initial slab extension).
- In the Bjorken model with initial energy density ε_0 ~ 10 ε_Q ~ 14 GeV fm^{-3}, the enhancement of R_out / R_side over the ideal gas case is of the order of 40-100% (for ΔT = 0.1 T_c to ΔT = 0).
- At energy densities estimated for CERN SPS Pb+Pb collisions (ε_0 ~ 1-2 T_c s_c), R_out / R_side ~ 1.5 - 2.
- The local minimum in the excitation function of transverse directed flow occurs around ~ 6 AGeV for Au+Au collisions at impact parameter b = 3 fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Shuryak (1990s), Testa (1990s), and Rischke et al. (1995) on the softening of the EoS due to the QGP transition and its effect on collective observables, and it builds on the proposal by Pratt and Bertsch (1990s) to use two-particle correlation functions as a lifetime signal. The results enable future experimental searches for QGP formation at RHIC and AGS energies, though the author notes that quantitative predictions are sensitive to the EoS details, numerical viscosity, and the neglect of physical viscosity and resonance decays, which were addressed in subsequent work by Schlei et al. (1990s) and others.
