# Self-consistent tilted-axis-cranking study of triaxial strongly deformed bands in $^{158}$Er at ultrahigh spin

> Stimulated by recent experimental discoveries, triaxial strongly deformed (TSD) states in $^{158}$Er at ultrahigh spins have been studied by means of the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model and the tilted-axis-cranking method. Restricting the rotational axis to one of the principal axes -- as done in previous cranking calculations -- two well-defined TSD minima in the total Routhian surface are found for a given configuration: one with positive and another with negative triaxial deformation $γ$. By allowing the rotational axis to change direction, the higher-energy minimum is shown to be a saddle point. This resolves the long-standing question of the physical interpretation of the two triaxial minima at a very similar quadrupole shape obtained in the principal axis cranking approach. Several TSD configurations have been predicted, including a highly deformed band expected to cross lesser elongated TSD bands at the highest spins. Its transitional quadrupole moment $Q_t \approx 10.5$\,eb is close to the measured value of $\sim$11\,eb; hence, it is a candidate for the structure observed in experiment.

## Key claim

By allowing the rotational axis to tilt away from the principal axes in self-consistent tilted-axis-cranking calculations, the higher-energy minimum previously found in principal-axis cranking (PAC) calculations for triaxial strongly deformed bands in 158Er is shown to be a saddle point rather than a stable minimum, resolving the long-standing question of the physical interpretation of the two triaxial minima at similar quadrupole shape.

## Method

The authors performed tilted-axis-cranking (TAC) calculations using two approaches: the shell-correction tilted-axis-cranking method (SCTAC) based on the phenomenological Nilsson potential, and for the first time, the three-dimensional self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock version of tilted-axis-cranking (SHFTAC) using the Skyrme energy density functionals SkM* and SLy4 supplemented with Landau parameters. The rotational axis was allowed to tilt in the x-y plane spanned by the short and medium principal axes, with the tilt angle θ measured with respect to the short axis, while enforcing the Kerman-Onishi conditions for triaxial rotation.

## Key numbers

- Equilibrium deformation parameters from SCTAC for rotation about the short axis: ε2 = 0.325, γ = 22° (lower minimum); for rotation about the medium axis: ε2 = 0.31, γ = -15° (higher minimum)
- Transition quadrupole moment for the stable TSD1 minimum from SCTAC: ~8 eb, compared to the experimental value of ~11 eb
- Transition quadrupole moment for configuration D from SHF-SkM* calculations at ω = 0.6 MeV: Qt ≈ 10.5 eb, close to the measured value of ~11 eb
- For configuration D at ω = 0.6 MeV: Q20ch = 12.13 eb, Q22ch = -2.45 eb, J = 54.82 ħ
- For configuration A at ω = 0.6 MeV: Qt = 7.57 eb, J = 51.61 ħ
- Band D crosses band B at about J ≈ 55 ħ and bands A and C at J ≈ 70 ħ
- Potential barrier between the two PAC minima from SHFPAC: ~1 MeV (SkM* and SLy4L); from SCTAC and CNS: ~0.7 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky (CNS) calculations by Wang et al. (2011) that found two triaxial strongly deformed minima (TSD1 and TSD2) in 158Er at ultrahigh spins, where the calculated Qt ≈ 7.5 eb for TSD1 underestimated the observed value of ~11 eb. The study also builds on earlier SHFPAC calculations by Matev et al. (2007) and the tilted-axis-cranking formalism developed by Frauendorf (1993) and Kerman and Onishi (1981). The results enable identification of a highly deformed band (configuration D) as the best candidate for the experimentally observed structure, and clarify that rotation of a well-deformed, slightly triaxial configuration can be about either a short or medium axis, but not about both.
