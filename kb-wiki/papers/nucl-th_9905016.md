# Dilepton Production from Rho-Mesons in a Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Assuming that rho-mesons exist in a quark-gluon plasma close to the QCD phase transition, we calculate the dilepton production rate from quark-antiquark annihilation via a rho-meson state using Vector Meson Dominance. The result is compared to the rates from direct quark-antiquark annihilation and from pion annihilation. Furthermore we discuss the suppression of low mass dileptons if the quarks assume an effective mass in the quark-gluon plasma.

# Dilepton Production from Rho-Mesons in a Quark-Gluon Plasma

## Key claim

Assuming that rho-mesons coexist with quarks in a quark-gluon plasma at temperatures close to the QCD phase transition (around T_c ≈ 0.15 GeV), the dilepton production rate from quark-antiquark annihilation via a rho-meson state using Vector Meson Dominance is comparable to the Born rate from direct quark-antiquark annihilation at low invariant masses and to the pion annihilation rate at higher masses, but this contribution is too small to explain the observed dilepton enhancement at SPS energies.

## Method

The authors calculate the dilepton production rate from the process q qbar → rho → e+ e- using Vector Meson Dominance, where the rho-meson self-energy is computed from a one-loop quark diagram at finite temperature beyond the hard thermal loop approximation. The rate is obtained from the imaginary part of the photon self-energy via the rho-meson propagator, using integral expressions for the longitudinal and transverse parts of the rho self-energy with a quark- rho coupling constant g = 6 derived from comparison with the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. The results are compared to the Born rate (direct quark-antiquark annihilation) and the pion annihilation rate via VMD from Gale and Kapusta (1991), and the effect of an effective quark mass m_q = 0.5 GeV is investigated.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constant for quark-rho interaction: g = 6, derived from comparison with the NJL model using G_2 from the literature (Polleri et al., 1997; He et al., 1998)
- VMD coupling constant: g_rho = 6.07 (from Gale and Kapusta, 1991)
- Bare rho-meson mass: m_rho = 770 MeV
- Temperature used for main comparison: T = 0.15 GeV, chosen to match the critical temperature for the QCD phase transition
- Virtual photon momentum for main comparison: p = 0.2 GeV, chosen to match the CERES-SPS transverse momentum range 0.2 GeV < p_T < 0.5 GeV
- Effective quark mass investigated: m_q = 0.5 GeV, based on quasiparticle fits to lattice equation of state (Peshier et al., 1994, 1996)
- The rho-quark rate vanishes for invariant masses M < 2m_q = 1.0 GeV when an effective quark mass of 0.5 GeV is used
- The rho-quark rate is about two orders of magnitude smaller than the observed SPS dilepton enhancement according to hydrodynamical calculations (Sollfrank et al., 1997)

## Context

This work extends the study of dilepton production from a quark-gluon plasma by considering non-perturbative hadronic states (rho-mesons) near the QCD phase transition, motivated by lattice QCD calculations (Boyd et al., 1994) and the gauged linear sigma model (Pisarski, 1995, 1996) that indicate the possible persistence of hadronic states above T_c. The calculation is analogous to the pion annihilation rate via VMD computed by Gale and Kapusta (1991), replacing the pion loop with a quark loop. The results show that the rho-quark contribution is insufficient to explain the SPS dilepton enhancement observed by CERES (Agakichiev et al., 1995), but may become relevant at RHIC and LHC where higher initial temperatures and longer QGP lifetimes are expected. The paper also discusses how an effective quark mass, as suggested by quasiparticle fits to lattice data (Peshier et al., 1994, 1996), would suppress dilepton production below M ≈ 1 GeV, though more realistic quark dispersion relations with plasmino branches (Braaten, Pisarski, and Yuan, 1991) could allow low-mass dileptons through electromagnetic transitions between collective modes.
