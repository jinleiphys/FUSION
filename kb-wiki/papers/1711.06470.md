# Scattering of charmed baryons on nucleons

> Chiral effective field theory is utilized for extrapolating results on the $Λ_c N$ interaction, obtained in lattice QCD at unphysical (large) quark masses, to the physical point. The pion-mass dependence of the components that constitute the $Λ_c N$ potential up to next-to-leading order (pion-exchange diagrams and four-baryon contact terms) is fixed by information from lattice QCD simulations. No recourse to SU(3) or SU(4) flavor symmetry is made. It is found that the results of the HAL QCD Collaboration for quark masses corresponding to $m_π= 410$--$570$ MeV imply a moderately attractive $Λ_c N$ interaction at $m_π= 138$ MeV with scattering lengths of $a\approx -1$ fm for the $^1S_0$ as well as the $^3S_1$ partial waves. For such an interaction the existence of a charmed counterpart of the hypertriton is unlikely but four- and/or five-baryons systems with a $Λ_c$ baryon could be indeed bound.

## Key claim

Chiral effective field theory extrapolation of HAL QCD Collaboration lattice data (at pion masses 410–570 MeV) yields a moderately attractive ΛcN interaction at the physical pion mass (138 MeV), with scattering lengths of approximately -1 fm for both the 1S0 and 3S1 partial waves; the abstract's claim of "moderately attractive" is hedged by the authors' explicit statement that the extrapolated values carry a cutoff variation of about 0.2 fm and additional systematic uncertainties of up to 0.3 fm from the omitted ΣcN channel coupling.

## Method

The authors constructed a ΛcN potential up to next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory, comprising one-pion exchange (with coupling to the ΣcN channel) and four-baryon contact terms whose low-energy constants were fitted to the HAL QCD Collaboration's phase shifts at mπ = 410 and 570 MeV for energies up to 30 MeV. The pion-mass dependence of the pion decay constant Fπ (taken from lattice QCD) and of the contact terms (via terms proportional to mπ²) was explicitly included; the axial couplings were held fixed at their physical values. The potential was then extrapolated to mπ = 138 MeV by substituting physical baryon and meson masses, and the Lippmann-Schwinger equation was solved with a momentum-space cutoff Λ = 500–600 MeV to produce phase shifts and scattering lengths.

## Key numbers

- Scattering length in the 1S0 partial wave at mπ = 138 MeV: a = −0.85 to −1.00 fm (cutoff variation Λ = 500–600 MeV).
- Scattering length in the 3S1 partial wave at mπ = 138 MeV: a = −0.81 to −0.98 fm (cutoff variation Λ = 500–600 MeV).
- Effective range in the 1S0 partial wave at mπ = 138 MeV: r = 2.88 to 2.61 fm.
- Effective range in the 3S1 partial wave at mπ = 138 MeV: r = 3.50 to 3.15 fm.
- At mπ = 410 MeV, the fitted scattering lengths are a = −0.45 fm (1S0) and a = −0.46 to −0.47 fm (3S1).
- At mπ = 570 MeV, the fitted scattering lengths are a = −0.21 fm (1S0) and a = −0.25 to −0.26 fm (3S1).
- The HAL QCD Collaboration's scattering lengths at mπ = 410 MeV are −0.49 ± 0.18 fm (1S0) and −0.51 ± 0.20 fm (3S1).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous chiral EFT extrapolations of lattice QCD results for baryon-baryon interactions in the strangeness sector (ΛΛ, ΞΞ, ΩΩ systems) to the charm sector, specifically the ΛcN system studied by the HAL QCD Collaboration (Miyamoto 2017). Unlike earlier phenomenological meson-exchange or constituent quark model predictions (Liu 2011, Maeda 2015) that found strongly attractive ΛcN interactions with two-body bound states, the present extrapolation yields only moderate attraction with no ΛcN bound state. The results suggest that a charmed hypertriton (ΛcNN three-body bound state with J=1/2) is unlikely, but four- and five-baryon systems with a Λc baryon could be bound, consistent with the earlier estimate of Gibson (1983) for similar scattering lengths. Future lattice data at smaller pion masses or with better statistics, as well as explicit ΣcN phase shifts promised by the HAL QCD Collaboration, would reduce the extrapolation uncertainty.
