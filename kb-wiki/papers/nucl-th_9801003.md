# Theoretical and Experimental K^+ + Nucleus Total and Reaction Cross Sections from the KDP-RIA Model

> The 5-dimensional spin-0 form of the Kemmer-Duffin-Petiau (KDP) equation is used to calculate scattering observables [elastic differential cross sections ($dσ/dΩ$), total cross sections ($σ_{Tot}$), and reaction cross sections ($σ_{Reac}$})] and to deduce $σ_{Tot}$ and $σ_{Reac}$ from transmission data for $K^+ + $ $^{6}$Li, $^{12}$C, $^{28}$Si, and $^{40}$Ca at several momenta in the range $488 - 714 MeV/c$. Realistic uncertainties are generated for the theoretical predictions. These errors, mainly due to uncertainties associated with the elementary $K^+ +$ nucleon amplitudes, are large, so that the disagreement that has been noted between experimental and theoretical $σ_{Tot}$ and $σ_{Reac}$ is not surprising. The results suggest that the $K^+ +$ nucleon amplitudes need to be much better determined before unconventional medium effects are invoked to explain the data.

## Key claim

The disagreement between experimental and theoretical K+ + nucleus total and reaction cross sections is not necessarily evidence for unconventional medium effects, because the theoretical predictions carry large uncertainties (primarily from the ±15% uncertainty in the elementary K+ + nucleon amplitudes) that can account for the discrepancies; the K+ + nucleon amplitudes must be much better determined before invoking new physics.

## Method

The 5-dimensional spin-0 form of the Kemmer-Duffin-Petiau (KDP) equation was used to construct a relativistic impulse approximation (RIA) optical potential for K+ + nucleus scattering, with scalar and vector components obtained by folding elementary K+ + nucleon amplitudes with relativistic mean-field Hartree nuclear densities. The same KDP-RIA model was then used both to calculate theoretical total and reaction cross sections and to extract experimental total and reaction cross sections from transmission data for K+ + 6Li, 12C, 28Si, and 40Ca at momenta from 488 to 714 MeV/c, following the extrapolation method of Friedman et al. (1997).

## Key numbers

- Theoretical predictions carry a ±15% uncertainty band arising from the elementary K+ + nucleon amplitudes, as estimated by Arndt (private communication)
- At 714 MeV/c, the KDP-RIA extracted reaction cross sections (in mb) are: 81.2 (6Li), 151.9 (12C), 316.9 (28Si), 413.9 (40Ca)
- At 714 MeV/c, the KDP-RIA extracted total cross sections (in mb) are: 88.9 (6Li), 180.4 (12C), 405.7 (28Si), 547.1 (40Ca)
- The difference between DD and t-rho potential extractions of total cross sections spans 5-11%, while reaction cross sections show much smaller model dependence
- The 715 MeV/c K+ + 12C elastic differential cross section data from Michael et al. (1996) are well described by the KDP-RIA model within the uncertainty band

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Friedman et al. (1997), who first noted that experimental total cross sections from transmission data are model-dependent and that self-consistent analysis is essential. The paper directly addresses the long-standing discrepancy between first-order impulse approximation calculations and K+ + nucleus data that prompted suggestions of unconventional medium effects by Siegel et al. (1984), Brown et al. (1988), and others. The results show that the situation parallels the early days of medium-energy proton-nucleus studies (Ray, 1979), where poorly known elementary amplitudes prevented definitive conclusions, and the authors recommend that future publications include the raw transmission cross section data to enable consistent reanalysis by the community.
