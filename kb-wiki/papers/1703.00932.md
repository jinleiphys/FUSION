# Pinning down the superfluid and nuclear equation of state and measuring neutron star mass using pulsar glitches

> Pulsars are rotating neutron stars that are renowned for their timing precision, although glitches can interrupt the regular timing behavior when these stars are young. Glitches are thought to be caused by interactions between normal and superfluid matter in the star. We update our recent work on a new technique using pulsar glitch data to constrain superfluid and nuclear equation of state models, demonstrating how current and future astronomy telescopes can probe fundamental physics such as superfluidity near nuclear saturation and matter at supranuclear densities. Unlike traditional methods of measuring a star's mass by its gravitational effect on another object, our technique relies on nuclear physics knowledge and therefore allows measurement of the mass of pulsars which are in isolation.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that pulsar glitch data, combined with the temperature dependence of neutron superfluidity, can constrain both the nuclear equation of state and the mass of isolated pulsars, but only if the superfluid reservoir extends into the stellar core rather than being confined to the inner crust.

## Method

The authors compare the observed glitch activity parameter G (which measures the average fractional spin-up from glitches) and the interior temperature T of pulsars to theoretical predictions from neutron star cooling simulations combined with models of the superfluid critical temperature as a function of baryon density. They use the BSk20 equation of state and the SFB superfluid model as their primary reference, and test additional equation of state and superfluid models to assess systematic uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- Vela pulsar: G = 1.62 ± 0.03%
- PSR J0537-6910: G = 0.875 ± 0.005%
- Vela pulsar mass derived using BSk20 EOS and SFB superfluid models: 1.51 ± 0.04 solar masses
- PSR J0537-6910 mass derived using BSk20 EOS and SFB superfluid models: 1.83 ± 0.04 solar masses
- Vela pulsar age used in cooling simulations: 11000 years
- Typical neutron star masses referenced: 1.2 to 2 solar masses

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous technique (Ho et al. 2015) by testing additional equation of state and superfluid models, and by demonstrating mass measurements for multiple glitching pulsars. It contrasts with earlier approaches (Link et al. 1999, Andersson et al. 2012, Chamel 2013, Piekarewicz et al. 2014, Steiner et al. 2015, Delsate et al. 2016, Li et al. 2016) that assumed glitches only tap the inner crust superfluid reservoir and largely ignored temperature dependence. The method enables mass measurements for isolated pulsars, which was previously impossible using traditional binary timing techniques (Lattimer 2016), and is expected to benefit from future facilities such as the Square Kilometer Array and Athena+.
