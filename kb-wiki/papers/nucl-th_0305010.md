# High-$p_T$ Hadron Spectra, Azimuthal Anisotropy and Back-to-Back Correlations in High-energy Heavy-ion Collisions

> The observed suppression of high-$p_T$ hadron spectra, finite azimuthal anisotropy, disappearance of jet-like back-to-back correlations, and their centrality dependence in $Au+Au$ collisions at RHIC are shown to be quantitatively described by jet quenching within a pQCD parton model. The difference between $h^{\pm}$ and $π^0$ suppression in intermediate $p_T$ is consistent with the observed $(K+p)/π$ enhancement which should disappear at $p_T>6$ GeV/$c$. The suppression of back-to-back correlations is shown to be directly related to the medium modification of jet fragmentation functions(FF).

# High-$p_T$ Hadron Spectra, Azimuthal Anisotropy and Back-to-Back Correlations in High-energy Heavy-ion Collisions

## Key claim
The observed suppression of high-$p_T$ hadron spectra, finite azimuthal anisotropy, and disappearance of jet-like back-to-back correlations in Au+Au collisions at RHIC can be simultaneously and quantitatively described by jet quenching within a lowest-order pQCD parton model, provided that the parton energy loss has a strong energy dependence and that an enhanced (K+p)/pi ratio at intermediate $p_T$ is included to account for the difference between charged hadron and pion suppression.

## Method
The author uses a lowest-order pQCD parton model that includes initial nuclear $k_T$ broadening, impact-parameter dependent parton shadowing (using either HIJING or EKS parameterizations), and medium-induced parton energy loss in a one-dimensional expanding medium with a gluon density proportional to the transverse profile of participant nucleons. The medium modification of jet fragmentation functions is approximated by a rescaling formula that accounts for the total parton energy loss during multiple scatterings, and an effective quark energy loss with a threshold and logarithmic energy dependence is employed. The model simultaneously calculates single hadron spectra, azimuthal anisotropy via the second Fourier coefficient $v_2$, and back-to-back di-hadron correlations using hadron-triggered fragmentation functions.

## Key numbers
- Average energy loss for a 10 GeV quark in the expanding medium: $\langle dE/dL\rangle_{1d} \approx 0.85 \pm 0.24$ GeV/fm (HIJING shadowing) or $0.99 \pm 0.24$ GeV/fm (EKS shadowing)
- Equivalent energy loss in a static uniform medium over distance $R_A = 6.5$ fm: $dE_0/dL \approx 13.8 \pm 3.9$ GeV/fm (HIJING) or $16.1 \pm 3.9$ GeV/fm (EKS)
- Parameters used to fit central $\pi^0$ suppression: $\mu_0 = 1.5$ GeV, $\epsilon_0 = 1.07$ GeV/fm, $\lambda_0 = 0.3$ fm (with HIJING shadowing); alternatively $\mu_0 = 1.3$ GeV, $\epsilon_0 = 1.09$ GeV/fm (with EKS shadowing)
- The $(K+p)/\pi$ ratio is approximately 2 at $p_T \sim 3$ GeV/$c$ in most central Au+Au collisions and approaches the $p+p$ value at $p_T > 5$ GeV/$c$
- The enhancement of $(K+p)/\pi$ should disappear at $p_T > 6$ GeV/$c$
- The soft component added to kaon and baryon fragmentation functions uses a weight proportional to $\langle N_{\rm bin}(b,r)\rangle/[1+\exp(2p_{Tc}-15)]$
- The $K \approx 1.3-2$ factor accounts for higher-order pQCD corrections
- The intra-jet distribution uses a Gaussian width of $\langle k_T\rangle = 0.8$ GeV/$c$

## Context
This work extends the jet quenching framework of Wang and Gyulassy (1992) and earlier pQCD parton model studies (Wang, 1998) by simultaneously describing three high-$p_T$ phenomena observed at RHIC by PHENIX and STAR collaborations. The model builds upon theoretical developments in medium-modified fragmentation functions (Guo and Wang, 2000; Wang and Wang, 2002) and parton energy loss with detailed balance (Wang and Wang, 2001). The extracted energy loss is about a factor of 2 larger than a previous estimate (Wang and Wang, 2002) due to the variation of gluon density along the propagation path and more precise RHIC data. The results enable quantitative extraction of medium properties such as gluon density from combined measurements of suppression, anisotropy, and correlations, and predict that the hadron-triggered fragmentation function suppression factor has an approximately universal shape reflecting the weak $p_T$ dependence of the single hadron suppression.
