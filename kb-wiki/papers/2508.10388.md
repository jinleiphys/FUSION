# Probing Nucleon-$Ω_{\rm ccc}$Interaction via Lattice QCD at Physical Quark Masses

> We study the S-wave interactions between the nucleon ($N$) and the triply charmed Omega baryon ($Ω_{\mathrm{ccc}}$) using (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD with a physical pion mass ($m_π\simeq 137.1$ MeV) on a lattice volume $\simeq (8.1~\mathrm{fm})^3$. The charm quark is implemented with a relativistic heavy-quark action at its physical mass. Employing the time-dependent HAL QCD method, the $N$-$Ω_{\mathrm{ccc}}$ potentials in the spin-1 ($^3\mathrm{S}_1$) and spin-2 ($^5\mathrm{S}_2$) channels are extracted. In both channels, overall attraction is found with the scattering parameters, $a_0 = 0.56(0.13)\left(^{+0.26}_{-0.03}\right)$ fm and $r_{\mathrm{eff}} = 1.60(0.05)\left(^{+0.04}_{-0.12}\right)$ fm for the $^3\mathrm{S}_1$ channel, and $a_0 = 0.38(0.12)\left(^{+0.25}_{-0.00}\right)$ fm and $r_{\mathrm{eff}} = 2.04(0.10)\left(^{+0.03}_{-0.22}\right)$ fm for the $^5\mathrm{S}_2$ channel, indicating the absence of a dibaryon bound state. The extracted potentials are further decomposed into spin-independent and spin-dependent components. The spin-independent potential is a dominant component and features a short-range attractive core and a long-range attractive tail, while the spin-dependent potential shows short-range attraction (repulsion) in the spin-1 (spin-2) channel. Qualitative comparisons with previous studies of the $N$-$J/ψ$ and $N$-$Ω_{\rm{sss}}$ systems at $m_π\simeq 146$ MeV are provided, emphasizing the role of heavy-hadron chromo-polarizability arising from soft-gluon exchange between the nucleon and flavor-singlet hadrons. The charm quark mass dependence of the $N$-$Ω_{\rm ccc}$ potential is investigated as well.

## Key claim

The S-wave nucleon-triply charmed Omega interaction is attractive in both spin-1 and spin-2 channels, but no dibaryon bound state exists in either channel, contrary to some quark-model predictions. The absence of a bound state is established by the scattering parameters extracted from lattice QCD at physical quark masses.

## Method

The authors employed (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD with a physical pion mass (mπ ≃ 137.1 MeV) on a 96⁴ lattice with spatial extent ≃ 8.1 fm, using the Iwasaki gauge action and nonperturbatively O(a)-improved Wilson quark action. Charm quarks were implemented with a relativistic heavy-quark action at physical mass, and the time-dependent HAL QCD method was used to extract N-Ωccc potentials in the ³S₁ and ⁵S₂ channels from 1,600 gauge configurations with 409,600 measurements per parameter set.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: mπ ≃ 137.1 MeV; lattice spacing a ≃ 0.084372 fm (a⁻¹ ≃ 2338.8 MeV); lattice volume ≃ (8.1 fm)³
- Spin-averaged 1S charmonium mass (interpolated to physical): (mηc + 3mJ/ψ)/4 ≃ 3068.5(0.1) MeV
- Ωccc mass (interpolated): mΩccc ≃ 4796.8(0.1) MeV; nucleon mass: mN = 938.9(2.8) MeV
- ³S₁ channel scattering length: a₀ = 0.56(0.13)(⁺⁰·²⁶₋₀.₀₃) fm; effective range: reff = 1.60(0.05)(⁺⁰·⁰⁴₋₀.₁₂) fm
- ⁵S₂ channel scattering length: a₀ = 0.38(0.12)(⁺⁰·²⁵₋₀.₀₀) fm; effective range: reff = 2.04(0.10)(⁺⁰·⁰³₋₀.₂₂) fm
- N-Ωccc threshold approximately 5740 MeV, lying about 200 MeV below the Λc-Ξcc threshold (5910 MeV) and Σc-Ξcc threshold (6080 MeV)

## Context

This work extends previous HAL QCD Collaboration studies of N-Ωsss (HAL QCD, 2018), N-φ (Lyu, 2022), and N-J/ψ and N-ηc (Lyu, 2024) systems, all of which found fully attractive interactions in the absence of quark Pauli blocking. Unlike the N-Ωsss system which exhibited a quasi-bound state near unitarity in the ⁵S₂ channel with binding energy about 2 MeV, the N-Ωccc system shows no bound state, with the potential being 2-5 times less attractive than N-Ωsss. The comparison between N-Ωccc and N-J/ψ spin-independent potentials enables extraction of the chromo-polarizability ratio βΩccc/βJ/ψ ≃ 1.6 from lattice QCD, providing input for gluonic van der Waals effective field theory (gWEFT) descriptions of heavy-hadron interactions.
