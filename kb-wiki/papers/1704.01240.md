# Reducing Uncertainties in the Production of the Gamma Emitting Nuclei 26Al, 44Ti, and 60Fe in Core Collapse Supernovae by Using Effective Helium Burning Rates

> We have used effective reaction rates (ERR) for the helium burning reactions to predict the yield of the gamma-emitting nuclei 26Al, 44Ti, and 60Fe in core col- lapse supernovae. The variations in the predicted yields for values of the reaction rates allowed by the ERR are much smaller than obtained previously, and smaller than other uncertainties. A "filter" for supernova nucleosynthesis yields based on pre-supernova structure was used to estimate the effect of failed supernovae on the initial mass function-averaged yields; this substantially reduced the yields of all these isotopes, but the predicted yield ratio 60Fe/26Al was little affected. The robustness of this ratio is promising for comparison with data, but it is larger than observed in nature; possible causes for this discrepancy are discussed.

## Key claim

Using effective reaction rates (ERR) for helium burning reactions reduces the predicted yield variations of 26Al, 44Ti, and 60Fe in core-collapse supernovae to much smaller values than obtained when treating the 3alpha and 12C(alpha,gamma)16O rates as independent, but the predicted 60Fe/26Al ratio remains larger than the observed range of 0.20-0.46 even after accounting for failed supernovae via a compactness parameter filter.

## Method

The authors used the KEPLER stellar evolution code to model 12 initial stellar masses from 12 to 30 solar masses from hydrogen burning to core collapse, then simulated a 1.2 x 10^51 erg explosion with a piston at the base of the oxygen shell. They applied the ERR line (r_alpha,gamma = r_3alpha + 0.35) determined by fitting nucleosynthesis yields to the solar abundance pattern of intermediate-mass and s-only isotopes, and calculated IMF-averaged yields using a Salpeter exponent of -2.35. A compactness parameter filter (xi_2.5 < 0.25) was applied to exclude stars likely to fail as supernovae, following the model of O'Connor (2011).

## Key numbers

- The ERR line is defined by r_alpha,gamma = r_3alpha + 0.35, with uncertainty b = 0.35 +/- 0.2
- Yield differences from ERR uncertainty: 18% for 26Al, 7% for 44Ti, and 22% for 60Fe when varying b from 0.2 to 0.5
- The observed 60Fe/26Al ratio from flux observations has a +/-1 sigma range of 0.20-0.46
- The galactic mass of 26Al is 1.5-3.6 solar masses (Diehl 2013)
- Previous calculations (Tur 2010) showed 60Fe yield variations extending to 17 x 10^-5 solar masses
- Previous calculations (Tur 2010) showed 60Fe/26Al ratio variations extending to 10
- The compactness parameter threshold used is xi_2.5 = 0.25

## Context

This work extends the ERR approach developed by Austin (2014) and West (2013a) to gamma-emitting nuclei, directly addressing the large yield variations found by Tur (2010) when treating helium burning rates as independent. The results enable more robust comparisons with INTEGRAL/SPI observations, though the authors note that the predicted 60Fe/26Al ratio remains higher than observed, with possible resolutions requiring improved rates for reactions such as 26Al(p,n)26Mg and 26Al(n,alpha)23Na (Iliadis 2011), updated opacities, and more sophisticated treatments of failed supernovae beyond the single compactness parameter of O'Connor (2011) as explored by Sukhbold (2016), Ertl (2016), and others.
