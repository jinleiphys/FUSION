# Generalized scaling of the transverse mass spectrum at the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider

> We argue that the transverse mass spectra of identified hadrons as measured in gold-gold collisions at BNL's Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC) follows a generalized scaling law. Such a scaling behavior is motivated by the idea of a Color Glass Condensate, or more generally, saturation of the gluon density. In particular, we describe the shapes of transverse mass spectra as a function of centrality. This scaling of the transverse mass spectrum is shown to be consistent with previously observed scaling of multiplicity with centrality.

## Key claim

The transverse mass spectra of identified hadrons in Au+Au collisions at RHIC follow a generalized scaling law, where all hadron species fall on a single universal curve when plotted as a function of transverse mass \(m_t\) (not \(p_t\) or \(m_t - m_0\)), modulo small normalization shifts \(\kappa_h\) for kaons (suppressed by about a factor of two) and protons (enhanced by about a factor of two). This scaling is motivated by the Color Glass Condensate picture but the authors note that radial flow effects, which would violate \(m_t\) scaling, are expected near threshold and that the observed pattern may have an alternative interpretation.

## Method

The authors test the scaling relation \({1 \over \sigma} {{dN_h} \over {dyd^2m_t}} = \frac{1}{\alpha_s(p_s)} \kappa_h \cdot f \left(\frac{m_t}{p_s}\right)\) against preliminary PHENIX data for pions, kaons, protons, and antiprotons in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=130\) AGeV. They extract the universal function \(f\) and the scaling parameters \(p_s\) and \(\sigma/\alpha_s\) for each centrality bin by rescaling the charged-particle \(p_t\) spectra, then check that the rescaled \(m_t\) distributions for all centralities collapse onto a single curve. The centrality dependence of the scaling parameters is compared to predictions from gluon saturation, and the charged multiplicity is computed from the scaling factors to cross-check consistency.

## Key numbers

- The universal transverse mass distribution for minimum-bias data is fitted by a power law \((1+m_t/p_s)^{-n}\) with \(n=16.3\) and \(p_s=2.71\) GeV (strongly correlated parameters).
- For the most central bin, a power law fit gives \(n=11.8\) and \(p_s=1.65\) GeV.
- The local slope parameter at \(m_t=m_h\) for different hadrons, derived from the power-law fit with \(n=16.3\) and \(p_s=2.71\) GeV, is: 175 MeV for pions, 196 MeV for kaons, 224 MeV for protons, 235 MeV for \(\Lambda\), and 247 MeV for \(\Xi\).
- The mean transverse momentum for charged particles in central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=130\) GeV is reported as \(\langle p_t \rangle = 508 \pm 12\) MeV (STAR preliminary).
- The mean \(p_t\) for \(p\bar{p}\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV is \(\langle p_t \rangle = 392 \pm 3\) MeV (UA1).
- The ratio of \(p_s^2\) at \(N_{\rm part}=2\) to that for central Au+Au collisions is calculated to be 0.60.
- The centrality dependence of \(p_s^2\) is fitted as \(p_s^2/p^2_{s,c}=0.61+0.39(N_{\rm part}/347)^{1/3}\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier observation by McLerran and collaborators (2001) that mean transverse momenta in heavy-ion collisions follow a universal scaling with multiplicity per unit transverse area, and the scaling of total hadron multiplicity with centrality from the gluon saturation picture (Kharzeev and Nardi, 2001). The authors note that a similar \(m_t\)-scaling analysis at RHIC was pursued by Braun (2001) within a different physical picture, and that universal \(m_t\)-scaling for hadrons was observed at lower energies at CERN SPS (Heinz, 1999; NuXu, 2000) but not interpreted in terms of scaling. The results enable a consistent phenomenological description of transverse mass spectra across centralities and hadron species, and provide a framework for testing the Color Glass Condensate against future data at higher \(p_t\) and for heavier particles like hyperons.
