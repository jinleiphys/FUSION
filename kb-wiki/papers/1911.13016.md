# Charmed excited baryons with heavy-quark spin symmetry

> Recently five $Ω_c$ excited states have been reported by the LHCb Collaboration, four of them corroborated by Belle. The Belle Collaboration has also discovered in 2017 one excited $Ξ_c$ with mass of 2930 MeV. In view of these recent detections, we analyze the possible molecular description of these states, using a unitarized baryon-meson model that incorporates both chiral and heavy-quark spin symmetries in a consistent manner. We pay a special attention to the renormalization procedure, so as to determine the robustness of our predictions. Within our model, we generate dynamically several $Ω_c$ and $Ξ_c$ states. We find that, at least, three of our $Ω_c$ states can be identified with the experimental ones and have spin-parity $J=1/2^-$ or $J=3/2^-$. Moreover, we find a plausible molecular description of not only the $Ξ_c(2930)$ state, but also $Ξ_c(2790)$, $Ξ_c(2815)$ and $Ξ_c(2970)$, reported in the PDG. Interestingly, we determine that $Ξ_c(2930)$ and $Ξ_c(2970)$ are heavy-quark spin partners with $J=1/2^-$ and $J=3/2^-$, respectively, while obtaining that $Ξ_c(2930)$, $Ω_c(3090)$ or the $Ω_c(3119)$, and $Σ_c(2800)$ would belong to the same SU(3) multiplet.

# Charmed excited baryons with heavy-quark spin symmetry

## Key claim
At least three of the five LHCb $\Omega_c$ excited states can be identified with dynamically generated molecular states in a unitarized baryon-meson model, provided the renormalization scheme is changed from a one-subtraction prescription to a common UV cutoff; the identification depends on the chosen cutoff value and is therefore not unique.

## Method
The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the on-shell approximation for S-wave scattering amplitudes, using a potential derived from a ${\rm SU(6)}_{\rm lsf} \times {\rm HQSS}$ extension of the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction between pseudoscalar or vector mesons and low-lying $1/2^+$ or $3/2^+$ baryons. They renormalize the logarithmically divergent baryon-meson loop functions by switching from a one-subtraction scheme to a common sharp-cutoff regulator $\Lambda$, and track the pole positions of the scattering amplitudes in the complex energy plane as the renormalization is varied adiabatically.

## Key numbers
- For a UV cutoff $\Lambda = 1090$ MeV, the experimental $\Omega_c(3000)$ and $\Omega_c(3090)/\Omega_c(3119)$ are identified with two $J=1/2^-$ states, and $\Omega_c(3050)$ with one $J=3/2^-$ state.
- The $\Xi_c(2930)$ and $\Xi_c(2970)$ are determined to be heavy-quark spin partners with $J=1/2^-$ and $J=3/2^-$, respectively.
- Using the equal spacing rule, a $J=1/2^-$ $\Sigma_c$ state is predicted around 2800 MeV, fitting the experimental $\Sigma_c(2800)$.

## Context
This work revises the earlier predictions of Romanets et al. (2012), which used a one-subtraction renormalization and produced $\Omega_c$ states below 3 GeV that could not be identified with the LHCb results. The present analysis extends that model by systematically studying the dependence on the renormalization scheme, and finds that at least three $\Omega_c$ states and four $\Xi_c$ states ($\Xi_c(2790)$, $\Xi_c(2815)$, $\Xi_c(2930)$, $\Xi_c(2970)$) can be described as molecular baryon-meson states. The results differ from those of Montana et al. (2017), Debastiani et al. (2017), and Yu et al. (2018) due to different renormalization schemes and interactions involving $D$, $D^*$ and light vector mesons with baryons. The work enables future searches for predicted sibling states not yet experimentally detected, such as the sibling of the wide $\Lambda_c(2595)$ in the $\Xi_c$ sector.
