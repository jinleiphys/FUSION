# Nucleonic Shells and Nuclear Masses

> The binding energy of an isotope is a sensitive indicator of the underlying shell structure as it reflects the net energy content of a nucleus. Since magic nuclei are significantly lighter, or more bound, compared to their neighbors, the presence of nucleonic shell structure makes an imprint on nuclear masses. In this work, using a carefully designed binding-energy indicator, we catalog the appearance of spherical and deformed shell and subshell closures throughout the nuclear landscape. After presenting experimental evidence for shell and subshell closures as seen through the lens of nuclear masses, we study the ability of global nuclear mass models to predict local binding-energy variations related to shell effects.

## Key claim

The single-particle energy splitting at the Fermi level, Δe_τ, extracted from measured nuclear masses, is a superb indicator of both spherical and deformed shell closures throughout the nuclear landscape, but the actual size of the predicted Δe_τ depends on zero-point correlations and small model differences (e.g., due to poorly known spin-isospin terms of energy density functionals).

## Method

The authors extracted the single-particle energy splitting Δe_τ (for neutrons and protons) from experimental binding energies in the AME2020 dataset, after subtracting a Wigner energy correction (the average of two parameterizations). They scaled Δe_τ by the average oscillator frequency ℏω₀ to obtain a dimensionless indicator Δẽ_τ, and mapped its values across the (N,Z) plane. They then compared these experimental landscapes to predictions from seven energy density functional (EDF) models (SkM*, SkP, SLy4, SV-min, UNEDF0, UNEDF1, UNEDF2) and the FRDM-2012 mass model, using the Bayesian Mass Explorer (BMEX) web application.

## Key numbers

- The average level spacing outside gap regions is estimated as ε̄ ≈ (60 ± 6)/A MeV; for the Zr isotopic chain this varies from 0.75(8) MeV at A=80 to 0.60(6) MeV at A=100.
- The Wigner energy correction uses two models: E_W(2) = a_W|N-Z|/A with a_W = 47 MeV, and E_W(1) = V_W exp(-λ_W((N-Z)/A)²) + V'_W|N-Z|exp(-(A/A₀)²) with V_W = 1.8 MeV, λ_W = 380, V'_W = -0.84 MeV, and A₀ = 26.
- The scaling factor for dimensionless splitting is ℏω₀ = 41A^{-1/3}(1 ± (N-Z)/(3A)) MeV, with plus for neutrons and minus for protons.
- The two-neutron shell gap indicator δ_{2τ} is related to Δe_τ by δ_{2τ} = Δe_τ + ΔP_τ, where ΔP_τ is a pairing correction.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing study of nuclear shell structure, building on the original identification of magic numbers by Mayer (1948) and Haxel (1949), and the Strutinsky shell-correction method (1968, 1972). It provides a systematic catalog of spherical and deformed shell closures across the nuclear chart using a single mass-based indicator, complementing prior work on binding-energy indicators by Janecke (1985) and Jensen (1984). The results enable future experimental campaigns at radioactive ion beam facilities to target regions where shell gaps are predicted to appear or disappear, and the BMEX tool will be updated with uncertainty estimates and Bayesian model mixing following the approach of the BAND collaboration.
