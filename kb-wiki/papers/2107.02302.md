# Baryon transport and the QCD critical point

> Fireballs created in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at different beam energies have been argued to follow different trajectories in the QCD phase diagram in which the QCD critical point serves as a landmark. Using a (1+1)-dimensional model setting with transverse homogeneity, we study the complexities introduced by the fact that the evolution history of each fireball cannot be characterized by a single trajectory but rather covers an entire swath of the phase diagram, with the finally emitted hadron spectra integrating over contributions from many different trajectories. Studying the phase diagram trajectories of fluid cells at different space-time rapidities, we explore how baryon diffusion shuffles them around, and how they are affected by critical dynamics near the QCD critical point. We find a striking insensitivity of baryon diffusion to critical effects. Its origins are analyzed and possible implications discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that, contrary to expectations, baryon diffusion is strikingly insensitive to critical effects near the QCD critical point, even when critical scaling of the baryon diffusion coefficient and relaxation time is included; this insensitivity is analyzed and its origins traced to the fact that the Navier-Stokes limit of the baryon diffusion current can be decomposed into density-gradient and temperature-gradient terms that scale differently with the correlation length.

## Method

The authors perform (1+1)-dimensional relativistic viscous hydrodynamic simulations with transverse homogeneity, evolving the baryon diffusion current using an Israel-Stewart type relaxation equation from the DNMR theory. They implement static critical behavior by parametrizing the correlation length as a function of temperature and baryon chemical potential using 3-dimensional Ising critical exponents, and dynamic critical behavior by scaling the isothermal susceptibility as ξ², the baryon diffusion coefficient as ξ, and the relaxation time as ξ³. The fireball is initialized to mimic Au-Au collisions at √s_NN = 19.6 GeV, and the evolution is studied both with and without critical scaling of transport coefficients.

## Key numbers

- Critical point location: μ_c = 250 MeV, T_c = 149 MeV, with α_1 = 4.6°
- Non-critical correlation length ξ_0 = 1 fm; maximum correlation length ξ_max = 10 fm
- Critical region extent: Δμ = 92 MeV, ΔT = 18 MeV
- Transition temperature at μ=0: T_0 = 155 MeV; curvature κ_2 = 0.0149
- Collision energy studied: √s_NN = 19.6 GeV
- Isothermal susceptibility scales as χ ∼ ξ²; baryon diffusion coefficient scales as κ_n ∼ ξ; baryon diffusion coefficient D_B ∼ ξ⁻¹; thermal diffusion coefficient D_T ∼ ξ

## Context

This work extends the Hydro+/++ framework of Stephanov (2017) and An (2019) for incorporating off-equilibrium critical fluctuations into hydrodynamic simulations, and builds on earlier studies of baryon diffusion in heavy-ion collisions by Shen (2017), Denicol (2018), Monnai (2019), and Du (2019). It also uses the critical equation of state construction of Parotto (2018) and Stafford (2021). The finding that baryon diffusion is insensitive to critical effects suggests that calibrating the bulk medium evolution at Beam Energy Scan energies may be possible without iteratively including critical back-reaction, simplifying the path toward locating the QCD critical point via model-data comparison.
