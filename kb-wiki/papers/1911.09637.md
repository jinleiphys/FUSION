# NN scattering and nuclear uncertainties

> Ab initio calculations in Nuclear physics for atomic nuclei require a specific knowledge of the interactions among their constituents, protons and neutrons. In particular, NN interactions can be constrained down to scale resolutions of $Δr \sim 0.6 {\rm fm}$ from the study of phase shifts below the pion production threshold. However, this allows for ambiguities and uncertainties which have an impact on finite nuclei, nuclear- and neutron-matter properties. On the other hand the nuclear many body problem is intrinsically difficult and the computational cost increases with numerical precision and number of nucleons. However, it is unclear what the physical precision should be for these calculations. In this contribution we review much of the work done in Granada to encompass both the uncertainties stemming from the NN scattering database in light nuclei such as triton and alpha particle and the numerical precision required by the solution method.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the Granada group's work on quantifying uncertainties in nuclear physics arising from the nucleon-nucleon (NN) scattering database, arguing that while NN interactions can be constrained down to scale resolutions of Δr ∼ 0.6 fm from phase shifts below pion production threshold, ambiguities and uncertainties in the interaction propagate to finite nuclei and nuclear matter properties, and it remains unclear what physical precision should be required for many-body calculations.

## Method

The authors review a coarse-grained approach to NN potentials where the short-range interaction is sampled at discrete radial points with spacing Δr = 0.6 fm, corresponding to the reduced de Broglie wavelength at maximum laboratory energy 350 MeV. The potential is decomposed into 23 operator components including charge-independent and charge-dependent terms, with long-range contributions from one-pion exchange and electromagnetic interactions fixed above a cutoff radius r_c = 1.8 fm. The short-range potential parameters at the grid points are determined by χ² minimization to pp and np scattering data, and the resulting uncertainties are propagated to binding energies of light nuclei (triton and alpha particle) using Faddeev and Yakubovsky equations.

## Key numbers

- Scale resolution of NN interactions: Δr ∼ 0.6 fm, corresponding to maximum laboratory energy 350 MeV
- Cutoff radius for known long-range potential: r_c = 1.8 fm, which coincides with the typical distance between nucleons in nuclear matter at density ρ = 0.17 fm⁻³
- Number of coarse-grained parameters for pp and np fits: approximately 40 grained points r_n
- Pion masses used: m_{π⁰} = 134.9739 MeV/c², m_{π±} = 139.5675 MeV/c²
- Pion coupling constant: f² = 0.075
- Experimental binding energy accuracy: ΔB = 0.01–10 keV, while liquid-drop model mass fits yield ΔB = 0.6 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Granada group's previous analyses of NN scattering uncertainties (Perez 2013, NavarroPerez 2012) and their propagation to three- and four-nucleon systems (Perez 2014, Perez 2016). It builds on the Nijmegen partial wave analysis (Stoks 1993) which fitted about 4000 experimental np and pp data with χ²/dof ∼ 1, and on modern NN potentials (Stoks 1994, Wiringa 1994, Machleidt 2000, Gross 2008) that achieved similar fit quality but without error estimates on potential parameters. The review enables future work on determining the required accuracy for many-body calculations and on distinguishing statistical from systematic uncertainties in nuclear theory.
