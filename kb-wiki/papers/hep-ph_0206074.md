# The Transverse Quark Distribution and Proton Electromagnetic Form Factors in Skew Distribution Formalism

> 4 pages, Presented May 23, 2000 by John Ralston. Published in the Proceedings of the 7th International Conference on Intersections of Particle and Nuclear Physics, (May 22-28, 2000 Quebec City), Edited by Z. Parseh and W. Marciano, (AIP Conference Proceedings Number 549.)

# The Transverse Quark Distribution and Proton Electromagnetic Form Factors in Skew Distribution Formalism

## Key claim
The power-counting prediction of perturbative QCD that $F_2(Q^2)/F_1(Q^2) \sim 1/Q^2$ is not correct; the proper kinematic treatment yields $F_2/F_1 \sim 1/\sqrt{|Q^2|}$, consistent with recent CEBAF data, because the probe momentum $\vec Q$ breaks rotational symmetry and carries angular momentum that modifies the power suppression.

## Method
The authors diagonalize skew (off-diagonal) parton distribution matrix elements through a series of coordinate transformations, including a Lorentz transformation to a frame where the momentum transfer $\Delta^\mu$ becomes entirely transverse in the infinite momentum limit. They then relate the electromagnetic form factors $F_1$ and $F_2$ to Fourier transforms of transverse spatial probability distributions, using an expansion of quark fields in light-cone $SO(2)$ orbital angular momentum basis functions involving modified Bessel functions $I_n(b)$.

## Key numbers
- The ratio $F_2(Q^2)/F_1(Q^2)$ scales as $1/\sqrt{|Q^2|}$ in the CEBAF data, contradicting the earlier prediction of $1/Q^2$ scaling
- The quantity $(Q/GeV) F_2(Q^2)/F_1(Q^2)$ is not far from unity in the CEBAF data, indicating that proton wave functions for quark angular momenta 1 and 0 are comparable in magnitude
- The form factor $F_2$ involves the product $I_0(b/(1-x)) I_1(b/(1-x))$ with an additional phase factor $e^{i\phi}$ from the probe momentum, leading to one power of $1/\sqrt{|Q^2|}$ suppression relative to $F_1$

## Context
This work extends the skew distribution formalism introduced by the authors in Jain and Ralston (1993) and builds on the transverse spin (transversity) framework of Ralston and Soper (1979). The analysis resolves a puzzle between the earlier pQCD prediction of $1/Q^2$ scaling for $F_2/F_1$ and the CEBAF data from Jones et al. (2000), showing that proper treatment of orbital angular momentum transfer from the probe yields the observed $1/\sqrt{|Q^2|}$ behavior. The approach enables extraction of transverse spatial probability distributions from form factor data and connects to the broader proton spin puzzle, with the result that constituent quark and non-relativistic models are ruled out by the comparable magnitudes of angular momentum 0 and 1 wave functions.
