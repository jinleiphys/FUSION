# Faddeev Calculation of the Hypertriton using the SU_6 Quark-Model Nucleon-Nucleon and Hyperon-Nucleon Interactions

> Quark-model nucleon-nucleon and hyperon-nucleon interactions by the Kyoto- Niigata group are applied to the hypertriton calculation in a new three-cluster Faddeev formalism using the two-cluster resonating-group method kernels. The most recent model, fss2, gives a reasonable result similar to the Nijmegen soft-core model NSC89, except for an appreciable contributions of higher partial waves.

## Key claim

The quark-model fss2 interaction yields a hypertriton Lambda separation energy of 289 keV, which is slightly larger than the experimental value of 130 ± 50 keV, indicating that the model's Lambda-nucleon interaction is somewhat too attractive and that the phase-shift difference between the singlet and triplet S-wave states should be reduced to 0–2 degrees to match experiment.

## Method

The authors applied the Kyoto-Niigata SU_6 quark-model nucleon-nucleon (NN) and hyperon-nucleon (YN) interactions to a three-cluster Faddeev calculation of the hypertriton (ΛNN-ΣNN system), using two-cluster resonating-group method (RGM) kernels. The Faddeev equation was formulated with a redundancy-free ΛN-ΣN T-matrix that exactly eliminates the Pauli-forbidden (0s)^6 state, and the energy dependence of the RGM kernels was treated self-consistently. The calculation included partial waves up to total angular momentum J ≤ 6, with 150 coupled channels for the ΛNN and ΣNN configurations.

## Key numbers

- Converged Lambda separation energy B_Λ = 289 keV for model fss2 (150 channels, J ≤ 6)
- Converged B_Λ = 878 keV for the earlier model FSS (150 channels, J ≤ 6)
- ΣNN probability P_Σ = 0.80% for fss2 and 1.36% for FSS
- Experimental B_Λ = 130 ± 50 keV
- For a modified fss2 with κ-meson mass changed from 936 MeV to 1000 MeV: B_Λ = 145 keV with P_Σ = 0.53%
- Effective range parameters for fss2: singlet scattering length a_s = -2.59 fm, singlet effective range r_s = 2.83 fm; triplet a_t = -1.60 fm, r_t = 3.01 fm
- Effective range parameters for FSS: a_s = -5.41 fm, r_s = 2.26 fm; a_t = -1.03 fm, r_t = 4.20 fm
- Triton binding energy from fss2: B_t = 8.519 MeV (50-channel calculation), compared to experimental B_t = 8.482 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Kyoto-Niigata group's quark-model approach from three-nucleon systems (triton) to the hypertriton, using the same Faddeev formalism previously applied to three-nucleon bound states (Fujiwara et al., 2002). The results are compared to the Nijmegen soft-core model NSC89 (Maessen et al., 1989), which gives B_Λ = 143 keV, and to earlier work by Miyagawa et al. (1995, 2000) and Nogga et al. (2002) showing that most meson-theoretical interactions fail to bind the hypertriton. The calculation enables fine-tuning of the quark-model YN interaction to reproduce the hypertriton binding energy, which will allow firmer predictions for the singlet and triplet S-wave ΛN phase shifts.
