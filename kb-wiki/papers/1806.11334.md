# Half lives of proton emitters with KDE0v1 Skyrme interaction

> The half-lives of proton radioactivity of proton emitters are investigated theoretically by using KDE0v1 Skyrme interaction. The total barrier potential in the proton radioactive nuclei is calculated as the sum of the nuclear, Coulomb and centrifugal contributions. The Hartree-Fock nuclear density distributions is used in calculating the nuclear as well as the Coulomb interaction potentials. The quantum mechanical tunneling probability is calculated within the WKB approximation. These calculations provide reasonable estimates for the observed proton radioactivity lifetimes.

## Key claim

The KDE0v1 Skyrme interaction, when used in a Hartree-Fock folding model with WKB tunneling, provides "reasonable estimates" for the observed proton radioactivity half-lives across 31 cases, with the caveat that discrepancies for some nuclei may arise from uncertainties in the experimental Q-values.

## Method

The total barrier potential for proton emission was constructed as the sum of nuclear, Coulomb, and centrifugal contributions. The nuclear potential was derived from the KDE0v1 Skyrme interaction via the functional derivative of the energy density, using Hartree-Fock density distributions for both the nuclear and Coulomb potentials. The quantum mechanical tunneling probability was calculated within the WKB approximation, and half-lives were obtained from the decay constant using the assault frequency and spectroscopic factor.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density for KDE0v1: \(\rho_0 = 0.165\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- Saturation energy per particle: \(e(\rho_0) = -16.0\) MeV
- Incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter: \(K(\rho_0) = 227.54\) MeV
- Effective mass at saturation: \(m^*/m(\rho_0, k_{f_0}) = 0.74\)
- Nuclear symmetry energy at saturation: \(E_s(\rho_0) = 34.58\) MeV
- Slope of symmetry energy: \(L(\rho_0) = 54.69\) MeV
- For \(^{105}\)Sb (l=2, Q=0.491 MeV): measured \(\log_{10}T_{1/2} = 2.049^{+0.058}_{-0.067}\) s; KDE0v1 calculation gives 1.98(46) s
- For \(^{109}\)I (l=2, Q=0.829 MeV): measured \(\log_{10}T_{1/2} = -3.987^{+0.020}_{-0.022}\) s; KDE0v1 calculation gives -4.20(5) s
- For \(^{185}\)Bi (l=0, Q=1.624 MeV): measured \(\log_{10}T_{1/2} = -4.229^{+0.068}_{-0.081}\) s; KDE0v1 calculation gives -5.52(13) s

## Context

This work extends earlier studies using Skyrme interactions for proton radioactivity (Routray et al., 2012) and folding model calculations with DDM3Y (Basu et al., 2008), JLM (Mohan et al., 2007), and YENI (Yen et al., 2011) effective interactions. The KDE0v1 force was selected because it is one of only five Skyrme parameter sets (CSkP* set) that satisfy all eleven microscopic constraints from Dutra et al. (2012), and it additionally reproduces neutron star structure with a maximum mass central density of 7.7 times saturation density (Madhuri et al., 2017). The results enable further testing of KDE0v1 across both finite-nuclei and astrophysical contexts, as it also fits a significant amount of finite-nuclei data (Stone et al., 2013).
