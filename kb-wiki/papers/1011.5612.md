# Antiproton-to-Proton Ratios for ALICE Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Assuming that the final state of hadronization takes place along the freezeout line, which is defined by a constant entropy density, the antiproton-to-proton ratios produced in heavy-ion collisions are studied in framework of the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model. A phase transition from quark--gluon plasma to hadrons, a hadronization, has been conjectured in order to allow modifications in the phase space volume and thus in single--particle distribution function. Implementing both modifications in the grand--canonical partition function and taking into account the experimental acceptance in heavy-ion collisions, the antiproton-to-proton ratios over center-of-mass energies $\sqrt{s}$ ranging from AGS to RHIC are very well reproduced by the HRG model. Comparing with the same particle ratios in $pp$ collisions results in a gradually narrowing discrepancy with increasing $\sqrt{s}$. At LHC energy, the ALICE antiproton-to-proton ratios in $pp$ collisions turn to be very well described by HRG model as well. It is likely that the ALICE heavy-ion program will produce the same antiproton-to-proton ratios as the $pp$ program. Furthermore, the ratio gets very close to unity indicating that the matter-antimatter asymmetry nearly vanishes. The chemical potential calculated at this energy strengthens the assumption of almost fully matter-antimatter symmetry at LHC energy.

# Antiproton-to-Proton Ratios for ALICE Heavy-Ion Collisions

## Key claim

Assuming a freezeout line defined by constant entropy density, the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model with constant parameters (phase space modification factor Q = 0.5 and quark occupancy gamma = 1.0) reproduces antiproton-to-proton ratios from AGS to RHIC energies in heavy-ion collisions without fitting, and also describes ALICE pp results at LHC energy, suggesting that ALICE heavy-ion collisions will produce the same ratios as the pp program.

## Method

The antiproton-to-proton ratios were calculated using the grand-canonical partition function of the hadron resonance gas model, which treats hadron resonances as an ideal gas with masses up to 1.8 GeV. The model incorporates a phase space modification factor Q (set to 0.5) and quark phase space occupancy parameters gamma (set to 1.0) to account for changes in phase space volume during hadronization from quark-gluon plasma. The calculations were performed over center-of-mass energies from AGS (5 GeV) through SPS (17 GeV) and RHIC (130 GeV) to LHC (7 TeV), with final particle yields including decay contributions from heavier resonances.

## Key numbers

- At AGS energy (sqrt(s) = 5 GeV), the antiproton-to-proton ratio in heavy-ion collisions is approximately 2.5 x 10^{-4} with 10% uncertainty
- At SPS energy (sqrt(s) = 17 GeV), the ratio in heavy-ion collisions is approximately 0.07 with 10% uncertainty
- At RHIC energy (sqrt(s) = 130 GeV), the ratio in heavy-ion collisions is 0.65 plus or minus 0.05
- The antiproton chemical potential calculated from ALICE data at 7 TeV is approximately 0.537 MeV, using a freezeout temperature of 0.175 plus or minus 0.02 GeV
- The photon number density derived from ALICE antiproton-to-proton ratios at 7 TeV ranges between 4.4 x 10^{4} and 7.7 x 10^{4}

## Context

This work extends the constant entropy density freezeout condition previously applied by Tawfik (2004) to the specific observable of antiproton-to-proton ratios, and builds on the HRG model framework used by Karsch (2003), Redlich (2004), and others to describe particle production in heavy-ion collisions. The results contradict the Regge model fit to pp data (Alice collaboration, 2010) by showing that a single HRG curve with constant parameters describes both heavy-ion and pp collisions at LHC energies, enabling future study of matter-antimatter asymmetry evolution across collision energies and potential connections to Sakharov's conditions for baryogenesis.
