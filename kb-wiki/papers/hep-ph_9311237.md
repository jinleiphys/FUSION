# Decisive test of color transparency in exclusive electroproduction of vector mesons

> The exclusive production of vector mesons in deep inelastic scattering is a hard scattering process with the well controlled size of quark configurations which dominate the production amplitude. This allows an unambiguous prediction of color transparency effects in the coherent and incoherent production of vector mesons on nuclei. We demonstrate how the very mechanism of color transparency leads to a belated onset of color transparency effects as a function of $Q^{2}$. We conclude that the $Q^{2}$ dependence of the exclusive $ρ^{0}$-meson production on nuclei and nucleons observed in the Fermilab E665 experiment gives a solid evidence for the onset of color transparency. We propose the scaling relation between the $ρ^{0}$ and the $J/Ψ$ production, which further tests the mechanism of color transparency in exclusive (virtual) photoproduction.

# Decisive test of color transparency in exclusive electroproduction of vector mesons

## Key claim
The exclusive electroproduction of vector mesons on nuclei provides an unambiguous test of color transparency, but the mechanism of color transparency itself leads to a belated onset of observable effects as a function of $Q^{2}$, requiring $r_{Q} \ll \frac{1}{5}R_{V}$ before nuclear attenuation significantly weakens. The $Q^{2}$ dependence observed in the Fermilab E665 experiment on exclusive $\rho^{0}$-meson production gives solid evidence for the onset of color transparency, though even at $Q^{2} \sim 10$ GeV$^{2}$ the residual final-state interaction is not yet vanishing.

## Method
The authors develop a lightcone approach to exclusive $\gamma^{*}N \rightarrow VN$ production, where the photon fluctuates into a $q\bar{q}$ pair at a large coherence length $l_{c} = 2\nu/(Q^{2}+m_{V}^{2})$ in front of the target. The transverse size $r$ and longitudinal momentum partition $z$ of the $q\bar{q}$ pair are frozen during the interaction, and the color-singlet pair interacts with the target nucleon via a cross section $\sigma(r) = (\pi^{2}/3) r^{2} \mathcal{F}(\nu,r)$ that vanishes as $r \rightarrow 0$. The nuclear transparency for incoherent production $T_{A}$ and the coherent production cross section are computed from matrix elements $\langle V|\sigma(r)|\gamma^{*}\rangle$ and $\langle V|\sigma(r)^{2}|\gamma^{*}\rangle$, with the dominant quark configuration sizes determined by the exponential cutoff of the photon wavefunction at $r \sim r_{Q} = 2/\sqrt{m_{V}^{2}+Q^{2}}$.

## Key numbers
- The transverse size of quark configurations dominating the production amplitude is $r_{Q} = 2/\sqrt{m_{V}^{2}+Q^{2}}$, giving $r_{Q} \approx 0.13$ fm at $Q^{2} \sim 10$ GeV$^{2}$.
- The final-state interaction is dominated by quark configurations of size $r_{FSI} = (4-5)r_{Q}$, yielding $r_{FSI} \sim 0.5$ fm at $Q^{2} \sim 10$ GeV$^{2}$.
- The nuclear transparency for incoherent production scales as $1-T_{A} \propto (A/R_{ch}(A)^{2}) \cdot 1/(Q^{2}+m_{V}^{2})$ when $1-T_{A} \ll 1$.
- For the E665 data at $\langle Q^{2} \rangle = 7$ GeV$^{2}$, the ratio $[T_{Pb}/T_{C}]_{\rho^{0}} = 0.6 \pm 0.25$, compared with the NMC result $[T_{Sn}/T_{C}]_{J/\Psi} = 0.7 \pm 0.1$ for real photoproduction of $J/\Psi$.
- For coherent production in the complete CT limit: $R_{coh}^{(CT)}(Ca/C) = 1.56$ and $R_{coh}^{(CT)}(Pb/C) = 3.25$.
- The observed attenuation at $Q^{2}=7$ GeV$^{2}$ corresponds to $\sigma(r=0.5$ fm$) \sim 8$ mb.
- The exponent $\alpha$ for the $A$-dependence of the no-FSI coherent cross section in the $C$-$Pb$ range approaches $\alpha_{coh} \approx 1.39$ as $Q^{2} \rightarrow \infty$.

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier formalism (Kopeliovich et al., 1993; Nikolaev and Zakharov, 1991) for exclusive vector meson production in deep inelastic scattering, applying it to predict color transparency effects in both coherent and incoherent production on nuclei. The predictions are compared with the Fermilab E665 experiment on $\rho^{0}$ production (E665 Collaboration, 1993) and with NMC and E691 data on $J/\Psi$ photoproduction (NMC Collaboration, 1993; E691 Collaboration, 1993). The paper proposes a scaling relation between $\rho^{0}$ and $J/\Psi$ production that enables further tests of the color transparency mechanism, and notes that earlier evidence for color transparency was seen in the $\pi^{-}A \rightarrow \pi^{0}A^{*}$ charge exchange reaction (ref. [23] in the paper). The results enable future high-precision measurements at higher $Q^{2}$ to probe the wave functions of vector mesons in the relativistic domain and to test the predicted slow onset of complete color transparency.
