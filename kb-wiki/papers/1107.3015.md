# On timelike Compton scattering at medium and high energies

> We emphasize the complementarity of timelike and spacelike studies of deep exclusive processes, taking as an example the case of timelike Compton Scattering (TCS) i.e. the exclusive photoproduction of a lepton pair with large invariant mass, vs deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) i.e. the exclusive leptoproduction of a real photon. Both amplitudes factorize with the same generalized parton distributions (GPDs) as their soft parts and coefficient functions which differ significantly at next to leading order in alpha_s. We also stress that data on TCS at very high energy should be available soon thanks to the study of ultraperipheral collisions at the LHC, opening a window on quark and gluon GPDs at very small skewness.

## Key claim

The paper argues that timelike Compton scattering (TCS) and deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) are complementary processes that factorize with the same generalized parton distributions (GPDs), but that their coefficient functions differ significantly at next-to-leading order in alpha_s, meaning that extracting universal GPDs from both reactions requires careful treatment of these higher-order corrections.

## Method

The authors calculate the cross sections for TCS (exclusive photoproduction of a lepton pair with large invariant mass) and the competing Bethe-Heitler process in ultraperipheral collisions at hadron colliders, using leading-order and next-to-leading order coefficient functions with GRVGJR2008 parton distribution parametrizations. They compute the TCS amplitude, the Bethe-Heitler amplitude, and their interference term, then evaluate the differential cross sections as functions of the photon-proton center-of-mass energy squared s, the timelike virtuality Q'^2, and the lepton angular variables theta and phi.

## Key numbers

* The Bethe-Heitler cross section integrated over theta in [pi/4, 3pi/4], phi in [0, 2pi], Q'^2 in [4.5, 5.5] GeV^2, and |t| in [0.05, 0.25] GeV^2 is constant at 28.4 pb in the limit of large s.
* The TCS cross section is evaluated as a function of gamma-p c.m. energy squared s from 10^5 GeV^2 to 10^7 GeV^2, with factorization scales mu_F^2 = 4, 5, and 6 GeV^2, using both LO and NLO GRVGJR2008 parametrizations.
* The interference contribution is shown for s = 10^7 GeV^2 and s = 10^5 GeV^2, with t = -0.2 GeV^2, Q'^2 = 5 GeV^2, and mu_F^2 = 5 GeV^2, integrated over theta = [pi/4, 3pi/4].
* The ratio R^q of the NLO quark coefficient function to the Born term is plotted for eta = 0.3, with alpha_s = 0.25, for both TCS and DVCS in the ERBL and DGLAP regions.
* The ratio of the NLO gluon coefficient function in TCS to that in DVCS is plotted for eta = 0.05 in the DGLAP region.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) from the well-studied DVCS process, which has been measured at HERA and JLab (DVCSexp, fitting), to the timelike regime. The authors build on earlier calculations of TCS (BDP) and their own prior work on ultraperipheral collisions (PSW) and NLO corrections (PSW2), following the methods of Ji and Osborne (JiO) and other NLO calculations (NLO2). The results enable future experimental programs at JLab@12 GeV (Hall B and Hall D) and ultraperipheral collisions at RHIC and LHC, which will test the universality of GPDs extracted from both spacelike and timelike processes, particularly at very small skewness where gluon GPDs become accessible.
