# Singularities in Twist-3 Quark Distributions

> We find in one-loop calculations and spectator models that twist-3 GPDs exhibit discontinuities. In the forward limit these discontinuities grow into Dirac delta functions which are essential to satisfy the sum rules involving twist-3 PDFs. We calculate twist-3 quasi-PDFs as a function of longitudinal momentum and identify the Dirac delta function terms with momentum components in the nucleon state that do not scale as the nucleon is boosted to the infinite momentum frame.

## Key claim

Twist-3 generalized parton distributions (GPDs) exhibit discontinuities at the points \(x = \pm \xi\) in both the quark target model and the scalar diquark model, and in the forward limit these discontinuities grow into Dirac delta functions that are essential for satisfying Lorentz invariance sum rules, contrary to the expectation that such singularities might endanger factorization.

## Method

The authors calculated the twist-3 GPDs \(G_2\) and \(\widetilde{G}_2\) using one-loop diagrams in the quark target model (QTM) and the scalar diquark model (SDM), extracting the divergent parts of the matrix elements. They then computed the twist-3 PDF \(g_2(x)\) and the quasi-PDF \(g_2^{\text{quasi}}(x)\) in the scalar diquark model, analyzing the scaling behavior of the distributions as the nucleon is boosted to the infinite momentum frame (IMF). The origin of Dirac delta function terms was traced to light-cone energy integrals of the form \(\int dk^- / (k^2 - m^2 + i\epsilon)^2\).

## Key numbers

* In QTM, the divergent part of \(G_2\) for \(\xi < x < 1\) is \(\frac{g^2}{2\pi^2} \frac{(1+x)}{(1-\xi^2)} \ln \Lambda_{\perp}\), where \(\Lambda_{\perp}\) is the transverse momentum cutoff.
* In QTM, the divergent part of \(\widetilde{G}_2\) for \(\xi < x < 1\) is \(\frac{g^2}{2\pi^2} \frac{(x+\xi^2)}{(1-\xi^2)} \ln \Lambda_{\perp}\).
* In SDM, the divergent part of \(G_2\) for \(\xi < x < 1\) is \(-\frac{g^2}{4\pi^2} \frac{(1-x)}{(1-\xi^2)} \ln \Lambda_{\perp}\).
* In SDM, the divergent part of \(\widetilde{G}_2\) for \(\xi < x < 1\) is \(\frac{g^2}{4\pi^2} \frac{(\xi^2 - x)}{(1-\xi^2)} \ln \Lambda_{\perp}\).
* The Dirac delta function term in \(g_2(x)\) from SDM is \(g_{2,\delta}(x) = \frac{g^2}{16 \pi^2} \frac{(x + m/M)}{(1-x)} \ln\left( \frac{\Lambda_{\perp}^2 + m^2}{m^2} \right) \delta(x)\), with quark mass \(m\) and nucleon mass \(M\) both set to 1 in the calculation.

## Context

This work extends studies of twist-3 GPD discontinuities previously investigated using the Wandzura-Wilczek approximation by Belitsky (2000), Kivel (2000), Radyushkin (2000), and others, by showing that the discontinuities persist without that approximation in explicit model calculations. The results enable a deeper understanding of the Lorentz invariance sum rules for twist-3 PDFs, such as the Burkhardt-Cottingham sum rule (Burkhardt, 1970), and suggest that experimental violation of these sum rules would provide indirect evidence for the Dirac delta function contributions. The paper also connects to the interpretation of twist-3 distributions as probes of quark-gluon correlations and the transverse force on quarks inside a nucleon (Burkardt, 2008).
