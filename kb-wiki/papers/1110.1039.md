# Parity Violation in nd Interactions

> We calculate the parity-violating amplitudes in the nd interaction with pionless effective field theory to LO. Matching the parity violating low energy constants to the DDH coefficients we make numerical predictions for parity-violating observables. In particular we give predictions for the spin rotation of a neutron on a deuteron target, and target and beam asymmetries in nd scattering.

## Key claim

The paper calculates parity-violating (PV) amplitudes in neutron-deuteron (nd) interactions at leading order (LO) using pionless effective field theory (EFT), but the numerical predictions for observables such as neutron spin rotation on a deuteron target and beam/target asymmetries are based on matching the five PV low-energy constants (LECs) to the DDH "best value" estimates, meaning the results inherit the model dependence of the DDH coefficients.

## Method

The author uses pionless effective field theory (EFT) with an auxiliary field formalism to describe the two-body parity-violating interaction at LO, which contains five LECs corresponding to S-P wave mixing with one derivative. The three-body PV amplitude is obtained by solving a set of coupled integral equations (shown diagrammatically in Fig. 3) that include parity-conserving scattering amplitudes from Fadeev equations, with the PV interaction treated to first order due to its small size (G_F m_pi^2 ~ 10^{-7}). The amplitudes are projected into a total angular momentum basis, and only S-P mixing amplitudes are calculated at low energies.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron binding momentum is gamma_d = 45.7025 MeV.
- The scattering length in the 1S0 channel is a_t = -23.714 fm, with gamma_t = 1/a_t.
- The parity-violating interaction is estimated to be of order G_F m_pi^2 ~ 10^{-7}, justifying the neglect of second-order PV terms.
- The three-body force term is given by H(E, Lambda) = 2 H_0(Lambda) / Lambda^2, where the cutoff Lambda is used to regulate momentum integrals.

## Context

This work extends previous parity-violating EFT calculations from the two-body sector (nucleon-nucleon scattering and np -> d gamma, as in Phillips (2008), Schindler (2009), and Shin (2009)) into the three-body nd system. It builds on the hybrid approach of Schiavilla (2008) and Song (2010) that used EFT potentials with variational or Fadeev wavefunctions, and complements the work of Griesshammer, Schindler, and Springer (2011) who predicted neutron spin rotation on deuterium up to NLO but only gave order-of-magnitude estimates for PV coefficients. The results enable assessment of whether nd interactions can serve as a realistic experimental probe for the five PV LECs, with higher-order corrections promised in a future publication.
