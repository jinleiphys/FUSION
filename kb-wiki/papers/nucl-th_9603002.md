# Average over energy effect of parity nonconservation in neutron scattering on heavy nuclei

> Using semiclassical approximation we consider parity nonconservation (PNC) averaged over compound resonances. We demonstrate that the result of the averaging crucially depends on the properties of residual strong nucleon-nucleon interaction. Natural way to elucidate this problem is to investigate experimentally PNC spin rotation with nonmonachromatic neutron beam: $E \sim ΔE \sim 1MeV$. Value of the effect can reach $ψ\sim 10^{-5}-10^{-4}$ per mean free path.

# Average over energy effect of parity nonconservation in neutron scattering on heavy nuclei

## Key claim

The average parity nonconservation (PNC) effect over an energy interval of about 1 MeV in neutron scattering on heavy nuclei is not necessarily zero, contrary to the expectation of statistical theory; the result of the averaging depends crucially on the properties of the residual strong nucleon-nucleon interaction, specifically on the memory time of initial helicity in the nuclear cascade.

## Method

The author uses a semiclassical approximation, replacing the averaging over compound resonances in an energy interval ΔE ~ 1 MeV by a space-time picture of neutron scattering via a wave packet localized in time. The neutron spin rotation angle is estimated by considering the "potential" (direct) process and then the cascade process where the neutron excites other nucleons and becomes trapped in the nucleus for a time τ ~ 1/D, with the weak interaction Hamiltonian proportional to σ·p. The ratio of the memory time of initial helicity τ_m to the free passage time τ_f is introduced as the key parameter determining the enhancement of the effect.

## Key numbers

- Neutron energy and averaging interval: E ~ ΔE ~ 1 MeV
- Nuclear potential depth: U ≈ 40 MeV
- Direct (potential) process spin rotation per mean free path: ψ ~ g_n × 10^{-6}, where |g_n| < 1
- Spin rotation including cascade with charge-exchange forces: ψ ~ g_p × 10^{-6} ~ 0.5 × 10^{-5}, where g_p ≈ 5
- Ratio of neutron life time in the quantum trap to free passage time: N = v/(2DR) ~ 5 MeV / D ~ 10^6
- Memory time ratio needed to fit experimental data: τ_m/τ_f ~ 10
- Predicted spin rotation per mean free path for nonmonochromatic beam (ΔE ~ 1 MeV): ψ ~ 10^{-5} to 10^{-4}
- Spread width of quasiparticle after first cascade step: Γ_spr' ~ (1/3)^2 Γ_spr ~ 0.3 MeV

## Context

This work addresses the puzzle arising from Los Alamos data for 238U (Bowman et al., 1990) and 232Th (Frankle et al., 1991, 1992), which showed a nonzero average PNC effect over many compound resonances, contradicting the expectation of the statistical theory developed by Sushkov and Flambaum (1980, 1982). The paper extends the semiclassical picture to explain this nonzero average and proposes that the effect can be investigated experimentally using a nonmonochromatic neutron beam. The approach is similar to the "quasielastic mechanism" suggested by Flambaum (1992) and discussed by Hussein, Kerman, and Lin (1995), but the author notes that the semiclassical result cannot be derived from the perturbation theory used in those works. The analysis enables Monte Carlo simulations of the nuclear cascade as a simpler alternative to exact quantum calculations for compound states.
