# Effect of nuclear quadrupole moment on parity nonconservation in atoms

> Nuclei with spin $I \ge 1$ have a weak quadrupole moment which leads to tensor contribution to the parity non-conserving interaction between nuclei and electrons. We calculate this contribution for Yb$^+$, Fr and Ra$^+$ and found it to be small. In contrast, in many lanthanides (e.g., Nd, Gd, Dy, Ho, Er, Pr, Sm) and Ra close levels of opposite parity lead to strong enhancement of the effect making it sufficiently large to be measured. Another possibility is to measure the PNC transitions between the hyperfine components of the ground state of Bi. Since nuclear weak charge is dominated by neutrons this opens a way of measuring quadrupole moments of neutron distribution in nuclei.

## Key claim

The tensor contribution to parity-nonconserving (PNC) interaction from the nuclear weak quadrupole moment is small in atoms previously used for PNC measurements (Yb⁺, Fr, Ra⁺), but can be strongly enhanced to measurable levels in lanthanides and Ra due to close atomic levels of opposite parity, provided the energy interval between such states is on the order of 1 cm⁻¹.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic many-body calculations of the weak quadrupole PNC amplitudes using the random phase approximation (RPA) for all operators of external fields, including the PNC operators and the electric dipole operator, and used Brueckner orbitals to include core-valence correlations. They also performed analytical estimations using radial wave functions near the nucleus to check numerical results, with agreement at the 30% level or better. For lanthanide atoms, they estimated PNC amplitudes by calculating weak matrix elements and Coulomb corrections from configuration mixing, using the NIST database to identify pairs of close opposite-parity states with ΔJ=1 or ΔJ=2.

## Key numbers

- For ¹⁷³Yb⁺ (I=7/2, Q_W=-96.84), the neutron quadrupole contribution to PNC amplitudes in s-d transitions is of order 10⁻⁶ Q_n times the spin-independent contribution, where Q_n is in barns (e.g., 4.8×10⁻⁶ Q_n for the 5d₃/₂-6s transition with F₁=1, F₂=2)
- For ²²³Fr (I=3/2, Q_W=-128.25), the neutron quadrupole contribution in the 7s-8s transition is at most 6.7×10⁻⁶ Q_n of the spin-independent contribution
- For ²²³Ra⁺ (I=3/2, Q_W=-127.2), the neutron quadrupole contribution in the 7s-6d₃/₂ transition is at most 1.4×10⁻⁵ Q_n of the spin-independent contribution
- For ²⁰⁹Bi (I=9/2, Q_W=-118.65), the neutron quadrupole contribution to PNC transitions between hyperfine components of the ground state is about one order of magnitude smaller than the anapole contribution, with Q_n ≈ -0.9 b and κ(Bi) ∼ 0.1
- For the second pair of close states in Ho (ΔE=0.021 cm⁻¹), the estimated PNC amplitude |E_PNC| is 1.9×10⁻⁹ a.u., comparable to the largest measured PNC atomic amplitude in Yb
- For the first pair of close states in Gd (ΔE=0.361 cm⁻¹), the estimated PNC amplitude is 3.9×10⁻¹¹ a.u.
- The electric quadrupole moments of the proton distribution are: Q_p(¹⁷³Yb)=2.80(4) b, Q_p(²²³Fr)=1.17(2) b, Q_p(²²³Ra)=1.25(7) b

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Flambaum (2016) on tensor effects of weak quadrupole moments in deformed nuclei, and builds on earlier work by Flambaum and Dzuba (2011) on spin-dependent PNC in single-valence-electron atoms. The authors note that previous studies (e.g., Khriplovich 1991) showed that combined action of weak charge and quadrupole hyperfine interaction produces a significantly smaller effect. The paper identifies specific lanthanide atoms (Nd, Gd, Dy, Ho, Er, Pr, Sm) and Ra as promising candidates for measuring neutron quadrupole moments via PNC, which would enable determination of the quadrupole moments of neutron distributions in nuclei, complementing neutron skin studies.
