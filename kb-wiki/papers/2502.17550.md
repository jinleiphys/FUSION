# Maximal Magic for Two-qubit States

> Magic is a quantum resource essential for universal quantum computation and represents the deviation of quantum states from those that can be simulated efficiently using classical algorithms. Using the Stabilizer Rényi Entropy (SRE), we investigate two-qubit states with maximal magic, which are most distinct from classical simulability, and provide strong numerical evidence that the maximal second order SRE is $\ln (16/7)\approx 0.827$, establishing a tighter bound than the prior $\ln (5/2)\approx 0.916$. We identify 480 states saturating the new bound, which turn out to be the fiducial states for the mutually unbiased bases (MUBs) generated by the orbits of the Weyl-Heisenberg (WH) group, and conjecture that WH-MUBs are the maximal magic states for $n$-qubit, when $n\neq 1$ and 3. We also reveal a striking interplay between magic and entanglement: the entanglement of maximal magic states is restricted to two possible values, $1/2$ and $1/\sqrt{2}$, as quantified by the concurrence; none is maximally entangled.

# Maximal Magic for Two-qubit States

## Key claim
The paper provides strong numerical evidence that the maximal second-order Stabilizer Rényi Entropy (SRE) for two-qubit states is $\ln(16/7) \approx 0.827$, which is a tighter bound than the previously known $\ln(5/2) \approx 0.916$ that applies to single qudit systems. The authors conjecture that for $n$-qubit systems where $n \neq 1$ and $n \neq 3$, the maximal magic states are the Weyl-Heisenberg mutually unbiased basis (WH-MUB) fiducial states.

## Method
The authors performed a numerical search over the full parameter space of two-qubit pure states using the second-order SRE ($M_2$) as the measure of magic, parameterizing states with angles and phases. They identified the global minimum of $\Xi_2$ (the quantity inside the logarithm of $M_2$) at $7/16$, confirmed it is an isolated local minimum by checking the gradient and Hessian, and then generated all maximal magic states via the orbit of the Clifford group. The resulting 480 states were identified as WH-MUB fiducial states by comparing their SRE values to the analytic expression for WH-MUB states.

## Key numbers
- Maximal second-order SRE for two-qubit states: $\ln(16/7) \approx 0.827$, tighter than the previous bound $\ln(5/2) \approx 0.916$ for single qudits at $d=4$.
- Global minimum of $\Xi_2$ for two-qubit states: $7/16$, found at parameters $\theta_{1,2,3} = \pi/4$, $\phi_{1,2,3} = \pi/2$, corresponding to the state $(i,i,i,1)/2$.
- 480 distinct two-qubit states saturate the maximal magic bound, generated by the Clifford group orbit from the state $(i,i,i,1)/2$.
- Concurrence of maximal magic states takes only two values: $1/2$ and $1/\sqrt{2}$; none is maximally entangled (concurrence $=1$).
- For a single qubit, maximal magic is $M_2 = \ln(3/2)$, saturated by the 8 "T-type" magic states originally defined by Bravyi and Kitaev (2005).

## Context
This work extends the study of maximal magic from single qudit systems, where the bound is given by Weyl-Heisenberg covariant Symmetric Informationally Complete (WH-SIC) states (Cuffaro 2024), to the two-qubit case where WH-SICs do not exist (Godsil 2009). The paper establishes a connection between maximal magic and mutually unbiased bases (MUBs), which are foundational in quantum cryptography and tomography (Durt 2010). The results enable further investigation into whether maximal SRE states also saturate other measures of magic, and open avenues for studying magic in quantum many-body systems, quantum field theories, and high-energy physics where magic has recently been discussed (White 2020, Leone 2022, Robin 2024).
