# Probing the initial longitudinal density profile and electromagnetic field in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions with heavy quarks

> Heavy quarks are valuable probes of the electromagnetic field and the initial condition of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) matter produced in high-energy nuclear collisions. Within an improved Langevin model that is coupled to a (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic model, we explore the origin of the directed flow coefficient ($v_1$) of heavy mesons and their decay leptons, and its splitting ($Δv_{1}$) between opposite charges. We find that while the rapidity dependence of the heavy flavor $v_1$ is mainly driven by the titled energy density profile of the QGP with respect to the longitudinal direction at the RHIC energy, it is dominated by the electromagnetic field at the LHC energy. The $Δv_{1}$ serves as a novel probe of the spacetime evolution profile of the electromagnetic field. Our results of $D$ mesons and their decay electrons are consistent with the available data at RHIC and LHC, and our predictions on the heavy flavor decay muons can be further tested by future measurements.

## Key claim
The rapidity dependence of heavy flavor directed flow ($v_1$) is mainly driven by the tilted energy density profile of the quark-gluon plasma at RHIC energy, but is dominated by the electromagnetic field at LHC energy; the charge-dependent splitting $\Delta v_1$ serves as a novel probe of the spacetime evolution of the electromagnetic field.

## Method
The authors coupled a modified Langevin transport model (including thermal diffusion, medium-induced gluon emission, and Lorentz force) to the (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic model CLVisc with a tilted initial energy density distribution. Heavy quarks were initialized via Monte-Carlo Glauber binary collision positions and leading-order perturbative QCD, then hadronized through a hybrid fragmentation and coalescence model, with final-state leptons simulated via Pythia. Two different model setups for the electromagnetic field were compared: one based on Maxwell equations with constant electric conductivity, and another using a parameterized magnetic field initialized from vacuum values.

## Key numbers
- Spatial diffusion coefficient $D(2\pi T) = 4.0$ at RHIC and $D(2\pi T) = 7.0$ at LHC for describing nuclear modification factors
- Tilt parameter $H_t = 3.9$ for 10-80% Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV and $H_t = 0.70$ for 10-40% Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV
- Initial magnetic field $eB_0 = 0.06$ GeV$^2$ ($\approx 2.97 m_\pi^2$) for RHIC and $eB_0 = 1.43$ GeV$^2$ ($\approx 73 m_\pi^2$) for LHC in setup-2
- Maximum $eB_y$ in setup-1 is approximately 30 times smaller than setup-2 at LHC and 2 times smaller at RHIC
- Decoupling temperature $T_d = 165$ MeV for heavy quark to hadron conversion

## Context
This work extends prior studies of heavy flavor directed flow using transport models coupled to tilted QGP media (Chatterjee 2017, Beraudo 2021, Oliva 2020) and investigations of electromagnetic field effects on heavy quarks (Das 2016, Sun 2020). The authors reconcile the different $v_1$ behaviors observed by STAR (2019) at RHIC and ALICE (2019) at LHC by identifying the competing roles of the tilted medium versus electromagnetic field at different energies. The results enable future measurements of heavy flavor decay muons to further constrain the spacetime evolution of the electromagnetic field in heavy-ion collisions.
