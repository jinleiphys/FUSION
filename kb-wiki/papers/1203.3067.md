# In search of quark gluon plasma in nuclear collisions

> At high temperatures and densities the nuclear matter undergoes a phase transition to a new state of matter called quark gluon plasma (QGP). This new state of matter which existed in the universe after a few microsecond of the big bang can be created in the laboratory by colliding two nuclei at relativistic energies. In this presentation we will discuss how the the properties of QGP can be extracted by analyzing the spectra of photons, dileptons and heavy flavours produced in nuclear collisions at Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and Large Hadron Collider (LHC) energies.

# In search of quark gluon plasma in nuclear collisions

## Key claim
Electromagnetic probes (photons and dileptons) and heavy flavour suppression can be used to extract the properties of quark gluon plasma (QGP) formed in relativistic nuclear collisions, but the extraction depends on the specific invariant mass windows chosen to isolate contributions from the partonic versus hadronic phases, and on the inclusion of medium-induced modifications to meson spectral functions.

## Method
The author uses ideal relativistic hydrodynamics with boost invariance to model the space-time evolution of the QGP and hadronic matter formed in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. The equation of state is constructed from lattice QCD results at high temperature and a hadron resonance gas up to 2.5 GeV at lower temperature. Dilepton production rates are computed from thermal QGP (via quark-antiquark annihilation) and from hadronic matter with in-medium spectral functions for vector mesons (rho, omega, phi), including interactions with thermal pions, omega, a1, h1, and nucleons. Heavy quark energy loss is treated via a Fokker-Planck equation with drag and diffusion coefficients, including both collisional and radiative processes, and the resulting nuclear suppression factor R_AA and elliptic flow v_2 are compared to experimental data.

## Key numbers
- Lattice QCD transition temperature for nuclear matter to QGP: ~175 MeV
- Kinetic freeze-out temperature: T_F = 120 MeV (fixed from hadron p_T spectra)
- Chemical freeze-out temperature: T_ch = 170 MeV
- Initial temperature for RHIC conditions: T_i = 300 MeV
- Initial temperature for LHC (Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV) conditions: T_i ~ 550 MeV
- Elliptic flow v_2 of heavy flavour electrons reaches a maximum of about 7% then saturates for p_T > 2 GeV (Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV)
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio extracted: eta/s ~ 0.1-0.2, close to the AdS/CFT bound
- For LHC, R_AA for D and B mesons is reproduced with initial temperature ~550 MeV
- Radial flow velocity v_T ~ 0.52 for mass window 0.5 < M(GeV) < 0.77 at LHC, compared to 0.25 at RHIC

## Context
This presentation extends the work of McLerran (1985) and reviews by Rapp, Alam (2001, 2002) on electromagnetic probes of QGP, and builds on the Fokker-Planck treatment of heavy quark energy loss by the author and collaborators (We1, 2010). The results for dilepton elliptic flow and HBT radii provide new tools to measure collectivity in different phases, complementing earlier work by Payal (2010) on HBT interferometry of lepton pairs. The heavy flavour suppression results are compared with experimental data from the PHENIX collaboration (2006) at RHIC and the ALICE collaboration (2011) at LHC, and the extracted eta/s values are consistent with the AdS/CFT bound of Kovtun, Son, and Starinets (2005).
