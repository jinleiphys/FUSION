# Dynamics of open quantum systems

> The coupling between the states of a system and the continuum into which it is embedded, induces correlations that are especially large in the short time scale. These correlations cannot be calculated by using a statistical or perturbational approach. They are, however, involved in an approach describing structure and reaction aspects in a unified manner. Such a model is the SMEC (shell model embedded in the continuum). Some characteristic results obtained from SMEC as well as some aspects of the correlations induced by the coupling to the continuum are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the coupling between discrete states and the continuum of decay channels induces correlations that are especially large at short time scales and cannot be captured by statistical or perturbational approaches; these correlations are, however, fully included in the Shell Model Embedded in the Continuum (SMEC), which provides a unified description of nuclear structure and reaction aspects.

## Method

The authors employ the SMEC framework, which solves the Schrodinger equation in a function space containing both discrete and continuous states, using a non-Hermitian effective Hamiltonian H_QQ = H_QQ + H_QP G_P^(+) H_PQ. The eigenvalues of this Hamiltonian are complex and energy-dependent, giving the positions and widths of resonance states; the S matrix is constructed from these quantities, ensuring unitarity even in the overlapping regime. Numerical calculations are performed for the nucleus 24Mg using the sd-shell model with a 16O core, coupling the 325 0+ states to one or two open channels.

## Key numbers

- For the reaction n + 23Mg(1/2+) -> 24Mg(0+), the resonance state 0_2+ has E* = 2.23 MeV and Gamma = 1.76 MeV when considered alone; when both the ground state and this resonance are included, the higher-lying state shifts to E* = 2.40 MeV with Gamma = 0.47 MeV.
- The 24Mg nucleus with a 16O core and sd-shell interaction has 325 states with J^pi = 0+, T = 0.
- In the overlapping regime, the sum of widths satisfies sum_{k=1}^N Gamma_k approx sum_{K=1}^K Gamma_k with K << N-K, meaning N-K states become long-lived (trapped) while K states become short-lived.

## Context

This work extends the Feshbach unified theory of nuclear reactions (Feshbach, 1962) by solving the Q-subspace problem microscopically rather than with statistical assumptions, and contrasts with the shell model approach to nuclear reactions of Mahaux and Weidenmuller (1969) which neglects bi-orthogonality of eigenfunctions. The SMEC approach enables the study of resonance trapping and the formation of different time scales in open quantum systems, phenomena that cannot be addressed by R-matrix approaches (Lane and Thomas, 1958) or statistical methods, and has been experimentally verified in microwave cavities (STM1 experiment).
