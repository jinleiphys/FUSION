# Energy level displacement of the excited nl state of pionic hydrogen

> The energy level displacements of the excited nl states of pionic hydrogen and the contribution of the ns -> 1s transitions and the (pi^-p)_Coul -> 1s transitions of the pi^-p pair, coupled by the attractive Coulomb field in the S-wave state with a continuous energy spectrum, to the shift of the energy level of the ground state of pionic hydrogen, caused by strong low-energy interactions, are calculated within a quantum field theoretic, relativistic covariant and model-independent approach developed in nucl-th/0306047.

## Key claim

The paper derives a general, model-independent formula for the energy level displacement (shift and width) of any excited \(n\ell\) state of pionic hydrogen within a relativistic covariant quantum field theoretic approach, and shows that the contribution of \(ns \to 1s\) transitions and continuum Coulomb-coupled \(\pi^-p\) transitions to the ground-state shift is only \(0.076\%\) of the Deser–Goldberger–Baumann–Thirring (DGBT) shift, a correction too small to affect the experimental extraction of the ground-state shift at current precision.

## Method

The authors generalize the DGBT formula to excited \(n\ell\) states by expressing the shift \(\epsilon_{n\ell}\) and width \(\Gamma_{n\ell}\) in terms of partial-wave \(\pi N\) scattering lengths and momentum-space wave functions \(\Phi_{n\ell}(k)\) of pionic hydrogen, derived from Coulomb wave functions. They then compute the specific case of the \(np\) state (\(\ell=1\)) analytically, using the radial integral \(\int d^3k\, k\,\Phi_{np}(k) = \sqrt{(n^2-1)/(\pi n^5 a_B^5)}\), and evaluate the second-order correction to the \(1s\) shift from intermediate \(ns\) bound states and continuum S-wave \(\pi^-p\) states via a unit operator insertion and low-energy approximation.

## Key numbers

*   Bohr radius of pionic hydrogen: \(a_B = 222.664\ \text{fm}\) (from \(\alpha = 1/137.036\), reduced mass \(\mu = 121.497\ \text{MeV}\)).
*   Binding energy of the ground state: \(E_{1s} = -3234.940\ \text{eV}\).
*   Shift of the \(np\) excited state: \(\epsilon_{np} = -\frac{1}{n^3}\left(1-\frac{1}{n^2}\right) \times (3.47 \pm 0.30) \times 10^{-5}\ \text{eV}\).
*   Width of the \(np\) excited state: \(\Gamma_{np} = \frac{1}{n^3}\left(1-\frac{1}{n^2}\right) \times (3.71 \pm 0.10) \times 10^{-7}\ \text{eV}\).
*   Experimental ground-state shift: \(\epsilon^{\exp}_{1s} = -7.108 \pm 0.036\ \text{eV}\).
*   Experimental ground-state width: \(\Gamma^{\exp}_{1s} = 0.868 \pm 0.054\ \text{eV}\).
*   Second-order correction to the ground-state shift from \(ns \to 1s\) and continuum transitions: \(0.076\%\) of the DGBT shift.

## Context

This work extends the quantum field theoretic, relativistic covariant approach developed by Ivanov et al. (2003) for the pionic hydrogen ground state to all excited \(n\ell\) states, providing a model-independent generalization of the DGBT formula. The analytical result for the \(np\) state shift agrees with the potential-model result of Ericson and Weise (1988). The calculated tiny second-order correction (\(0.076\%\)) confirms that the dominant contributions to the ground-state shift come from QCD isospin-breaking and electromagnetic interactions, as predicted by Gasser et al. (2002) within Chiral Perturbation Theory, and that the excited-state displacements are negligible for the next-generation PSI experiments aiming at \(0.2\%\) precision on the shift.
