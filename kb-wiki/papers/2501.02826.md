# In-medium $ΛN$ interactions with leading order covariant chiral hyperon/nucleon-nucleon forces

> In-medium $ΛN$ interactions are crucial in hypernuclei and neutron star physics. In this work, we study the in-medium $ΛN$ interaction within the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) framework, employing the leading-order covariant chiral hyperon/nucleon-nucleon forces for the first time. We demonstrate that a consistent description of both the experimental cross-section data and the `empirical value' of the $Λ$ single-particle potential can be achieved. This contrasts with the majority of studies in the non-relativistic framework, where higher-order two-body chiral forces are typically required. This study offers a new perspective on the in-medium $ΛN$ interactions, urgently needed in relativistic \textit{ab initio} hypernuclear physics studies.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the leading-order (LO) covariant chiral hyperon-nucleon (YN) force, when used within the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) framework, can simultaneously describe experimental YN scattering cross-section data and reproduce the empirical value of the Λ single-particle potential (approximately -27 to -30 MeV) at nuclear saturation density, without requiring higher-order chiral forces that are typically needed in non-relativistic frameworks.

## Method

The authors first re-derived the LO covariant chiral YN interaction using physical baryon masses (M_N = 938.918 MeV, M_Λ = 1115.68 MeV, M_Σ = 1193.1 MeV) and determined 12 low-energy constants by fitting to 36 YN scattering data points. They then solved the coupled RBHF equations self-consistently for symmetric nuclear matter containing a small fraction of Λ hyperons, using the Bethe-Goldstone equation with a coupled-channel formalism for the strangeness S = -1 system (ΛN and ΣN channels), and employed the LO covariant chiral NN interaction from Zou (2023) for the nucleon sector.

## Key numbers

*   The Λ single-particle potential at nuclear saturation density (k_F = 1.35 fm^{-1}) is -25.1 MeV for cutoff Λ_F = 550 MeV and -29.3 MeV for cutoff Λ_F = 700 MeV, consistent with the empirical value of -27 to -30 MeV.
*   The chi-squared value for the fit to 36 YN scattering data points is 15.85 for cutoff Λ_F = 550 MeV and 15.74 for cutoff Λ_F = 700 MeV.
*   The physical baryon masses used are M_N = 938.918 MeV, M_Λ = 1115.68 MeV, and M_Σ = 1193.1 MeV.
*   The partial-wave contributions to the Λ single-particle potential at saturation density for the LO(550) calculation are: ^1S_0 = -18.8 MeV, ^3S_1+^3D_1 = -23.5 MeV, ^3P_0 = 1.4 MeV, ^1P_1 = 4.3 MeV, ^3P_1 = 11.4 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock studies of nuclear matter by Brockmann (1990) and Zou (2023) to include hyperons, using the covariant chiral YN interactions developed by Li (2016) and Liu (2020). It contrasts with non-relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock studies by Haidenbauer (2014, 2019) that require next-to-leading order chiral forces to reproduce the empirical Λ potential, and with phenomenological potentials such as the Jülich Jul94 and Jul05 models and the Nijmegen NSC97f model. The results provide a microscopic input for relativistic ab initio hypernuclear calculations and for constructing covariant density functionals for neutron star studies.
