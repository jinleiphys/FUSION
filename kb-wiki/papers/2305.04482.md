# Stability of interlinked neutron vortex and proton flux-tube arrays in a neutron star -- III. Proton feedback

> The coupled, time-dependent Gross-Pitaevskii and Ginzburg-Landau equations are solved simultaneously in three dimensions to investigate the equilibrium state and far-from-equilibrium, spin-down dynamics of an interpenetrating neutron superfluid and proton type-II superconductor, as an idealized description of the outer core of a neutron star. The simulations generalize previous calculations without the time-dependent Ginzburg-Landau equation, where proton feedback is absent. If the angle $θ$ between the rotation and magnetic axes does not equal zero, the equilibrium state consists of geometrically complicated neutron vortex and proton flux-tube tangles, as the topological defects pin to one another locally but align with different axes globally. During spin-down, new types of motion are observed. For $θ= 0$, entire vortices pair rectilinearly with flux tubes and move together while pinned. For $θ\neq 0$, vortex segments pair with segments from one or more flux tubes, and the paired segments move together while pinned. The degree to which proton feedback impedes the deceleration of the crust is evaluated as a function of $θ$ and the pinning strength, $η$. Key geometric properties of vortex-flux-tube tangles, such as filament length, mean curvature, and polarity are analysed. It is found that proton feedback smooths the deceleration of the crust, reduces the rotational glitch sizes, and stabilizes the vortex tangle dynamics. The dimensionless control parameters in the simulations are mutually ordered to match what is expected in a real neutron star, but their central values and dynamics ranges differ from reality by many orders of magnitude due to computational limitations.

## Key claim
The paper claims that including proton feedback (via the time-dependent Ginzburg-Landau equation) in simulations of coupled neutron vortex and proton flux-tube arrays smooths the deceleration of the neutron star crust, reduces rotational glitch sizes, and stabilizes vortex tangle dynamics. However, the dimensionless control parameters in the simulations are ordered to match a real neutron star only in their mutual ordering, while their central values and dynamical ranges differ from reality by many orders of magnitude due to computational limitations.

## Method
The authors solved the coupled, time-dependent Gross-Pitaevskii and Ginzburg-Landau equations simultaneously in three dimensions to model an interpenetrating neutron superfluid and proton type-II superconductor in the outer core of a neutron star. They simulated both equilibrium states and far-from-equilibrium spin-down dynamics for different angles between the rotation and magnetic axes, and for varying pinning strengths. The simulations generalized previous calculations that omitted the time-dependent Ginzburg-Landau equation and thus lacked proton feedback.

## Context
This work extends previous simulations by the same group that did not include the time-dependent Ginzburg-Landau equation, meaning proton feedback was absent in those earlier calculations. The results enable more realistic modeling of neutron star spin-down and glitch phenomena by accounting for the mutual interaction between the neutron superfluid and proton superconductor. The paper is the third in a series, following earlier work by the same authors that established the baseline behavior without proton feedback.
