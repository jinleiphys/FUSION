# Bayesian Analysis for Extracting Properties of the Nuclear Equation of State from Observational Data including Tidal Deformability from GW170817

> We develop a Bayesian analysis method for selecting the most probable equation of state under a set of constraints from compact star physics, which now include the tidal deformability from GW170817. We apply this method for the first time to a two-parameter family of hybrid equations of state that is based on realistic models for the hadronic phase (KVORcut02) and the quark matter phase (SFM$α$) which produce a third family of hybrid stars in the mass-radius diagram. One parameter ($α$) characterizes the screening of the string tension in the string-flip model of quark matter while the other ($Δ_P$) belongs to the mixed phase construction that mimics the thermodynamics of pasta phases and includes the Maxwell construction as a limiting case for $Δ_P=0$. We present the corresponding results for compact star properties like mass, radius and tidal deformabilities and use empirical data for them in the newly developed Bayesian analysis method to obtain the probabilities for the model parameters within their considered range.

## Key claim

The paper develops a Bayesian analysis method that, for the first time, includes the tidal deformability constraint from the GW170817 event to select the most probable hybrid equation of state from a two-parameter family. The method is applied to a specific class of hybrid EoS that can produce a third family of compact stars (mass twins), and the results indicate that the most likely EoS are those with a strong mixed phase effect and a large screening parameter, but the authors note this is an exploratory calculation limited by the parameter range considered.

## Method

The authors construct a two-parameter family of hybrid equations of state combining the KVORcut02 hadronic model with the String-Flip Model for quark matter, using a mixed phase construction that mimics pasta phases and includes the Maxwell construction as a limiting case. They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities for each EoS, then compute posterior probabilities via Bayes' theorem using a uniform prior and likelihood functions derived from the GW170817 tidal deformability data and the mass measurement of PSR J0348+0432.

## Key numbers

- The hadronic phase uses the KVORcut02 EoS, which is the stiffest version from Maslov (2015) and allows fulfillment of the Seidov criterion for a disconnected hybrid star family.
- The screening parameter alpha is varied in the range [0.1, 0.3]; larger alpha corresponds to lower critical density for the phase transition.
- The mass constraint uses mu_A = 2.01 solar masses and sigma_A = 0.04 solar masses from the measurement by Antoniadis (2013).
- The most likely EoS are those with Delta_P > 0.04 and alpha > 0.28, at the limit of the parameter range considered.
- The paper reports that the neutron star radius has a value of 12.7 +/- 0.4 km for masses from 1 to 2 solar masses, citing Margueron (2017).

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian analyses of the nuclear equation of state, such as those by Steiner (2010) using X-ray bursters and Raithel (2017) using multipolytrope EoS, by incorporating for the first time the tidal deformability constraint from GW170817. The method is applied to the class of hybrid EoS introduced by Ayriyan (2017) that allow for a third family of compact stars (mass twins), and the results suggest that future gravitational wave observations could reveal a binodal structure in the Lambda1-Lambda2 plane as evidence for an early phase transition around twice nuclear saturation density.
