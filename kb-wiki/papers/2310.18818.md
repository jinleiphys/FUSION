# A thousand fermions in a 3D harmonic trap via Monte Carlo simulations

> By use of a special wave function derived from similarly transformed propagators, this work shows that the energy of a thousand spin-balanced fermions in a three-dimensional harmonic potential can be accurately computed using the Monte Carlo method.

## Key claim

By using a special wave function derived from similarly transformed propagators, the ground-state energy of up to a thousand spin-balanced fermions in a three-dimensional harmonic trap can be accurately computed via Monte Carlo methods, with systematic errors below 0.2% on the relative scale; the method works without needing to evaluate any single-particle wave functions explicitly.

## Method

The author constructs a trial wave function as a product of two Slater determinants (one for spin-up, one for spin-down) whose matrix elements are Gaussian functions of the form exp[-(x_i - s_j)^2/2], where the s_j are a set of random vectors that are taken to zero in the limit. The local energy is derived analytically in terms of the inverse of these matrices, and the Metropolis algorithm is used to sample the square of the wave function, avoiding the sign problem. The energy expectation value is computed for various system sizes by reducing the spread of the s_j vectors and extrapolating to the zero-spread limit.

## Key numbers

* For 100 spin-balanced fermions (2N=100), the exact energy is 510; the best calculated result is 510.024 ± 0.003, a systematic error of 0.005%.
* For 200 spin-balanced fermions (2N=200), the exact energy is 1280; the best calculated result is 1280.08 ± 0.01, a systematic error of 0.006%.
* For 400 spin-balanced fermions (2N=400), the exact energy is 3210; the best calculated result is 3211.17 ± 0.04, a systematic error of 0.03%.
* For 600 spin-balanced fermions (2N=600), the exact energy is 5498; the best calculated result is 5507.1 ± 0.2, a systematic error of 0.17%.
* For 800 spin-balanced fermions (2N=800), the exact energy is 8070; the best calculated result is 8076.6 ± 0.3, a systematic error of 0.08%.
* For 1000 spin-balanced fermions (2N=1000), the exact energy is 10860; the best calculated result is 10877 ± 1, a systematic error of 0.16%.

## Context

This work extends the approach of similarly transformed propagators (Chin 2020) to construct a simple many-fermion wave function for the harmonic oscillator, bypassing the need to enumerate single-particle states. It contrasts with the exact propagator-based thermodynamic study of harmonically confined fermions by Brosens et al. (1998), which computed ground-state energies as a final goal. The proposed wave function is intended as a trial function for future large-scale Variational Monte Carlo or Ground State Path Integral Monte Carlo calculations on interacting three-dimensional fermion systems.
