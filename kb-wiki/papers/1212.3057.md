# Two-nucleon scattering: merging chiral effective field theory with dispersion relations

> We consider two-nucleon scattering close to threshold. Partial-wave amplitudes are obtained by an analytic extrapolation of subthreshold reaction amplitudes calculated in a relativistic formulation of chiral perturbation theory. The constraints set by unitarity are used in order to stabilize the extrapolation. Neutron-proton phase shifts are analyzed up to laboratory energies $T_{\rm lab}\simeq250$ MeV based on the next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order expression for the subthreshold amplitudes. We find a reasonably accurate description of the empirical S- and P-waves and a good convergence of our approach. These results support the assumption that the subthreshold nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitude may be computed perturbatively by means of the chiral expansion. The intricate soft scales that govern the low-energy nucleon-nucleon scattering are generated dynamically via a controlled analytic continuation.

# Two-nucleon scattering: merging chiral effective field theory with dispersion relations

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitudes near threshold can be computed by analytically continuing perturbative chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) amplitudes from the subthreshold region into the physical region using dispersion relations and conformal mapping, provided the generalized potential is split into an explicitly computed long-range part (inside a defined domain) and an analytically continued short-range part. The approach generates the nonperturbative soft scales dynamically without solving a Schrödinger equation, but the results depend on fitting the short-range coefficients to empirical phase shifts up to 100 MeV, with higher-energy behavior then emerging as a prediction.

## Method

The authors compute the discontinuity across the left-hand cuts of the nucleon-nucleon partial-wave amplitude using a relativistic formulation of chiral perturbation theory up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (Q³). They split the generalized potential U(s) into an "inside" part (U_inside), containing the one-pion and two-pion exchange cuts computed explicitly from ChPT, and an "outside" part (U_outside), which is expanded in a conformal mapping variable ξ(s) that maps the analyticity domain onto a unit circle. The full scattering amplitude is then obtained by solving the nonlinear integral equation for T(s) using the N/D method, with the coefficients of the ξ-expansion fitted to Nijmegen partial-wave analysis phase shifts up to T_lab = 100 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Neutron-proton phase shifts are analyzed up to laboratory energies T_lab ≈ 250 MeV.
- The matching point is chosen at μ_M² = 4m_N² − 2M_π², with m_N the nucleon mass and M_π the pion mass.
- The left-hand cuts included explicitly in U_inside extend from s = 4m_N² − M_π² down to s = 4m_N² − 9M_π² (Λ² = 4m_N² − 9M_π²).
- The cutoff Λ_s is set at the two-pion production threshold Λ_s = 2m_N + 2M_π.
- The pion decay constant is taken as F_π ≈ 92.4 MeV; the effective axial coupling is g_A = F_π g_πNN / m_N ≈ 1.285, based on g_πNN²/(4π) ≈ 13.54.
- The low-energy constants used are c₁ = −0.75 GeV⁻¹, c₃ = −4.77 GeV⁻¹, c₄ = 3.34 GeV⁻¹ from Krebs (2012).
- Deuteron binding energy converges from −32.70 MeV at order Q⁰ to −2.73 MeV at Q¹, −2.10 MeV at Q², and −2.12 MeV at Q³ (experimental value: −2.22 MeV).
- Varying the matching point μ_M within 4m_N² − 3.5M_π² < μ_M² < 4m_N² − 1.1M_π² affects the ³D₁ phase shift at E_lab = 250 MeV by less than 10%.
- The g_πNN coupling constant, when treated as a free parameter fitted up to T_lab = 100 MeV, lies within 10% of the empirical value.

## Context

This work extends the dispersive effective field theory (DEFT) approach previously applied to pion- and photon-nucleon dynamics (Gasparyan 2010, 2011; Danilkin 2011, 2012) to the two-nucleon system. It is complementary to the potential-based chiral EFT approaches of Weinberg (1991), Entem and Machleidt (2003), and Epelbaum (2004), which solve the Schrödinger equation with chiral potentials. The method differs from earlier dispersion-relation studies of Goldberger (1960) and Ball (1965) by using modern ChPT to compute the left-hand cut discontinuities systematically. It also contrasts with the work of Albaladejo (2011, 2012), which studied one-pion exchange effects but did not employ conformal mapping to separate soft and hard cuts. The approach enables future investigation of whether two-pion exchange potentials can be treated perturbatively and provides a new perspective on the nonperturbative renormalization of chiral EFT for nucleon-nucleon scattering.
