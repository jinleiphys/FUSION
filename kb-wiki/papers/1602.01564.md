# Beam energy dependence of Hanbury-Brown-Twiss radii from a blast-wave model

> The beam energy dependence of correlation lengths (the Hanbury-Brown-Twiss radii) is calculated by using a blast-wave model and the results are comparable with those from RHIC-STAR beam energy scan data as well as the LHC-ALICE measurements. A set of parameter for the blast-wave model as a function of beam energy under study are obtained by fit to the HBT radii at each energy point. The transverse momentum dependence of HBT radii is presented with the extracted parameters for Au + Au collision at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = $ 200 GeV and for Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV. From our study one can learn that particle emission duration can not be ignored while calculating the HBT radii with the same parameters. And tuning kinetic freeze-out temperature in a range will result in system lifetime changing in the reverse direction as it is found in RHIC-STAR experiment measurements.

# Beam energy dependence of Hanbury-Brown-Twiss radii from a blast-wave model

## Key claim
The blast-wave model can reproduce the beam energy dependence of HBT radii from RHIC-STAR and LHC-ALICE data only when the particle emission duration (Δτ) is included as a nonzero parameter; with Δτ set to zero, the outward radius R_out cannot be fitted simultaneously with R_side and R_long, indicating that emission duration is essential for describing the system expansion.

## Method
The authors used a blast-wave model with an emission function including kinetic freeze-out temperature T_kin, radial flow, system lifetime τ_0, particle emission duration Δτ, and transverse source size R_0. They fitted the model parameters to experimental HBT radii (R_side, R_out, R_long) from STAR and ALICE at eight center-of-mass energies from 7.7 GeV to 2.76 TeV, using T_kin and average radial flow ⟨β⟩ as input functions of √s_NN from prior experimental fits. The HBT radii were calculated in the "out-side-long" coordinate system using the standard correlation function formalism.

## Key numbers
- HBT radii were calculated at √s_NN = 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, 200, and 2760 GeV for central Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions.
- T_kin and ⟨β⟩ were parametrized with T_lim = 169.171 MeV and β_lim = 0.399 as asymptotic values.
- The extracted R_0 shows a similar energy dependence trend as R_side; with fixed T_kin = 90, 100, or 120 MeV, a larger R_0 is needed when T_kin is smaller.
- τ_0 generally increases with √s_NN but shows a minimum value at √s_NN ≈ 39 GeV.
- The difference R_out^2 - R_side^2 shows a non-monotonic increasing trend with √s_NN, with a peak near √s_NN ≈ 17.3 GeV in experimental data, which the model reproduces with Δτ ≠ 0.
- At √s_NN = 200 GeV and 2.76 TeV, R_side, R_out, and R_long all decrease with increasing transverse momentum p_T.

## Context
This work extends the blast-wave model analysis of HBT radii from the RHIC top energy (STAR Collaboration, 2005; PHENIX Collaboration, 2005) and LHC energy (ALICE Collaboration, 2011) to the full RHIC Beam Energy Scan range (STAR Collaboration, 2014; PHENIX Collaboration, 2014). It builds on the systematic parameter study of Retiere and Lisa (2004) and the finite-size scaling analysis of HBT-BES-Th (2014) that suggested a critical end point at chemical freeze-out temperature ~165 MeV and baryon chemical potential ~95 MeV. The results enable further investigation of the QCD phase diagram and the search for the critical end point by combining HBT radii with other observables such as elliptic flow and fluctuations.
