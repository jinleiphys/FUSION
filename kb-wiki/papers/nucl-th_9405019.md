# Model for asymptotic $D$-state parameters of light nuclei: Application to $^4He$

> A simple method for calculating the asymptotic $D$ state observables for light nuclei is suggested. The method exploits the dominant clusters of the light nuclei. The method is applied to calculate the $^4He$ asymptotic $D$ to $S$ normalization ratio $ρ^α$ and the closely related $D$ state parameter $D_2^α$. The study predicts a correlation between $D_2^α$ and $B_α$, and between $ρ^α$ and $B_α$, where $B_α$ is the binding energy of $^4He$. The present study yields $ρ^α\simeq -0.14$ and $D_2^α\simeq -0.12$ fm$^2$ consistent with the correct experimental $η^D$ and the binding energies of the deuteron, triton, and the $α$ particle, where $η^d$ is the deuteron $D$ state to $S$ state normalization ratio.

## Key claim

The paper presents a simple cluster model for calculating the asymptotic D-state parameters of 4He, claiming that the D-state observables ρ^α and D_2^α are determined primarily by the binding energies of the deuteron, triton, and alpha particle together with the deuteron asymptotic D/S ratio η^d, rather than requiring a full four-body dynamical calculation. The authors hedge that the model is approximate and relies on neglecting the term containing a product of η^d and η^t, as well as treating vertex form factors as constants.

## Method

The authors construct a perturbative cluster model in momentum space using diagrammatic equations for the formation of the 4He D-state via the 4He → 2 2H channel, considering virtual breakup into a nucleon and trinucleon followed by rearrangement into two deuterons. They derive analytic expressions for the asymptotic D/S normalization ratio ρ^α and the D-state parameter D_2^α by evaluating loop integrals with constant vertex form factors and expanding propagators in spherical harmonics, keeping only terms linear in the deuteron and triton D-state admixtures.

## Key numbers

- ρ^α ≃ -0.14 for experimental binding energies B_α = 28.3 MeV, B_t = 8.48 MeV, B_d = 2.225 MeV, and η^d = 0.027
- D_2^α ≃ -0.12 fm^2 for the same experimental binding energies and η^d = 0.027
- ρ^α/(μ^2_dd D_2^α) ≃ 1.0, consistent with an estimate of 0.9 from Santos et al.
- For the Urbana potential (B_α = 28.2 MeV, mean B_t = 8.15 MeV), the model gives D_2^α = -0.15 fm^2 compared to -0.24 fm^2 from Schiavilla et al.
- For the Argonne potential (B_α = 27.8 MeV, mean B_t = 8.04 MeV), the model gives D_2^α = -0.12 fm^2 compared to -0.16 fm^2 from Schiavilla et al.
- η^t/η^d = 1.68 used in the calculation for experimental binding energies

## Context

This work extends the approach of Ericson and Rosa Clot (1983) for the deuteron D-state and the authors' own previous work on the triton D-state (Frederico et al., 1990, 1992), which established that η^t is proportional to η^d for fixed binding energies. The model predicts a linear correlation between D_2^α and B_α, and between ρ^α and B_α, which the authors suggest should be verified in realistic four-nucleon dynamical calculations using Faddeev-Yakubovskii equations. The results enable model-independent estimates of 4He D-state parameters without solving the full four-body problem, and the authors note that their values differ from earlier experimental extractions by Santos et al. (1985, 1988) who found ρ^α = -0.21 and -0.5 to -0.4 respectively.
