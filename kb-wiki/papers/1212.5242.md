# How long-range interactions tune the damping in compact stars

> Long-range interactions lead to non-Fermi liquid effects in dense matter. We show that, in contrast to other material properties, their effect on the bulk viscosity of quark matter is significant since they shift its resonant maximum and can thereby change the viscosity by many orders of magnitude. This is of importance for the damping of oscillations of compact stars, like in particular unstable r-modes, and the quest to detect signatures of deconfined matter in astrophysical observations. We find that, in contrast to neutron stars with standard damping mechanisms, compact stars that contain ungapped quark matter are consistent with the observed data on low mass x-ray binaries.

## Key claim

Long-range gauge interactions in dense quark matter produce non-Fermi liquid effects that shift the resonant maximum of the bulk viscosity, changing its value by many orders of magnitude; this enhanced damping can make compact stars containing ungapped quark matter consistent with observed low-mass X-ray binary data, whereas neutron stars with standard damping mechanisms cannot.

## Method

The author computes the non-leptonic and Urca weak interaction rates in dense quark matter using dispersion relations modified by non-Fermi liquid self-energy corrections from unscreened magnetic gluons, extending the analysis of Heiselberg (1992). The bulk viscosity is then obtained in the subthermal limit (chemical potential displacement much smaller than temperature) via the resonant form determined by the weak rate and strong susceptibilities, and the resulting damping is applied to the r-mode instability region of strange quark stars.

## Key numbers

- The non-Fermi liquid enhancement factor λ⁴ for the non-leptonic rate can take values in the range 2 ≲ λ⁴ ≲ 1000, based on σ in the range 0.05 ≲ σ ≲ 0.3 and Λ/T in the range 100 ≲ Λ/T ≲ 10⁶.
- At temperatures T < 10⁸ K, the bulk viscosity of interacting quark matter is more than ten orders of magnitude larger than the corresponding bulk viscosity in hadronic matter.
- The Urca contribution is only relevant at temperatures T > 10⁹ K and small frequencies.
- The bulk viscosity curves are computed for a baryon density n = 3 n₀ and a frequency ν = 1 kHz, using a strange quark mass mₛ = 150 MeV.
- The r-mode instability region is shown for a 1.4 M⊙ strange quark star at two coupling values (αₛ = 0 and αₛ = 1).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of non-Fermi liquid effects in dense matter by Schafer (2004) and Gerhold (2004) to the bulk viscosity, which had previously been computed for non-interacting quark matter by Madsen (1992) and Heiselberg (1992). The results contradict the conclusion from Alford (2010) that neutron stars with standard damping mechanisms (shear viscosity from leptons and bulk viscosity from modified Urca reactions) cannot explain the observed LMXB data, and instead show that ungapped quark matter with long-range interactions can resolve this discrepancy. The work enables further study of whether enhanced damping mechanisms in hadronic stars (e.g., boundary layer rubbing or mutual friction in hadron superfluids) can similarly explain the data, or whether the data may provide a signature for deconfined quark matter.
