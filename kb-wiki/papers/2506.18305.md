# Existence of nuclear modifications on longitudinal-transverse structure-function ratio

> It has been assumed that nuclear modification does not exist in the longitudinal-transverse structure-function ratio $R_N=F_L^N/(2xF_1^N)$ in lepton deep inelastic scattering. This assumption is widely used in obtaining structure functions of the "nucleon" from nuclear data such as the deuteron ones. However, nuclear modifications do exist theoretically at least in medium- and large-$x$ regions because nucleons in a nucleus move in any direction, which is not necessarily the longitudinal direction of the virtual-photon or weak-boson momentum in lepton scattering. Because of this transverse motion, the nucleon's transverse and longitudinal structure functions should mix with each other in nuclei with the mixture probability proportional to the nucleon's transverse momentum squared $\vec p_T^{\,\, 2}/Q^2$. In this work, numerical results are explicitly shown regarding such nuclear modifications in the deuteron. These nuclear modifications are important for determining precise structure functions of the nucleon. Furthermore, modifications of $R_N$ should be investigated also at small $x$ by the future electron-ion collider to find interesting gluon dynamics in nuclei. Hopefully, this nuclear effect of $R_N$ could be found by future experimental measurements at lepton accelerator facilities.

## Key claim

Nuclear modifications of the longitudinal-transverse structure-function ratio \(R_N = F_L^N/(2xF_1^N)\) do exist theoretically in the deuteron, at least in the medium- and large-\(x\) regions, contrary to the common assumption that they are absent. The modification arises because nucleons in a nucleus have transverse motion, causing the nucleon's transverse and longitudinal structure functions to mix with a probability proportional to \(\vec p_T^{\,\,2}/Q^2\).

## Method

The author uses the standard convolution formalism for nuclear structure functions, where the nuclear hadron tensor is obtained by convolving the nucleonic tensor with the nucleon momentum distribution (spectral function). For the deuteron, the Bonn wave function is used, and the nucleon structure functions are computed from the MSTW08 parton distribution functions at leading order, with the nucleon ratio \(R_N\) taken from the SLAC 1990 parametrization. The nuclear modifications of \(R_N\) are then calculated from the resulting nuclear structure functions \(F_1^D\), \(F_2^D\), and \(F_L^D\) at various \(Q^2\) values.

## Key numbers

- At \(Q^2 = 5\) GeV\(^2\), the nuclear modifications of the deuteron's structure functions are of the order of a few percent.
- Nuclear modifications of \(R_N\) are shown at \(Q^2 = 1\), 5, and 100 GeV\(^2\) in the deuteron; the modifications are largest at small \(Q^2\) (e.g., 1 GeV\(^2\)) due to the mixing term \(\sim \vec p_{N\perp}^{\ 2}/Q^2\).
- At \(Q^2 = 100\) GeV\(^2\), noticeable modifications of \(R_N\) persist even without the mixing terms, arising from convolution with the nucleon's lightcone momentum distribution.
- The modification dip in the medium-\(x\) region (\(x \sim 0.5\)) for \(F_L^D/F_L^N\) is not large, in contrast to the modifications of \(F_1^D/F_1^N\) and \(F_2^D/F_2^N\).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical proposal of Ek and Kumano (2003) that nuclear modifications of \(R_N\) should exist, providing explicit numerical results for the deuteron. It contradicts the common assumption used in extracting nucleon structure functions from nuclear data (e.g., deuteron and \(^3\)He) and in short-range correlation analyses at JLab, where the nucleon ratio \(R_N\) is used without nuclear corrections. The results are relevant for upcoming JLab experiments scheduled for 2026 (R-Jlab-pro-R) and for future electron-ion colliders, where measurements of \(R_A\) at small \(x\) could probe gluon dynamics in nuclei.
