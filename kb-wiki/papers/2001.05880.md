# Mapping QGP interaction through its temperature dependent degeneracy factor

## Key claim
The paper parameterizes a temperature-dependent degeneracy factor g(T) to match lattice QCD entropy density data, but the authors acknowledge that only one of their two parametrization sets (set-2) satisfies the expected Stefan-Boltzmann limit at infinite temperature, while set-1 provides better matching to the lattice data but fails this asymptotic condition.

## Method
The authors take the Stefan-Boltzmann entropy density formula for massless QGP, s = [g_g + (g_u + g_s)(7/8)] (4π²/90) T³ ≈ 20.8 T³, and introduce a temperature-dependent multiplicative factor g(T) applied to the quark and gluon degeneracies. They fit two parametrized functional forms of g(T) to lattice QCD data for s/T³ from Refs. LQCD1 and LQCD2, with set-1 using four free parameters and set-2 constrained to approach unity at high temperature.

## Key numbers
- Stefan-Boltzmann limit for massless QGP entropy density: s ≈ 20.8 T³
- Set-1 parameters: a₀ = 0.793, a₁ = 0.687, a₂ = 16.284, a₃ = 0.170, a₄ = 0.560
- At T ≈ 0.200 GeV, g(T) ≈ 0.5, giving effective degeneracy factor 0.5 × 52 = 26
- At T ≈ 0.120 GeV, g(T) ≈ 0.13, giving effective degeneracy factor 0.13 × 52 = 7, matching the hadronic degeneracy factor g_π + g_K = 7

## Context
This work extends lattice QCD calculations of the entropy density (LQCD1, LQCD2) by providing a simple analytic parametrization of the interaction-driven suppression relative to the Stefan-Boltzmann limit. The temperature-dependent degeneracy factor approach is compared to the behavior of diatomic or n-atomic molecules, where degrees of freedom increase with temperature as atomic bonds break. The parametrization enables phenomenological modeling of QGP thermodynamics without full lattice calculations, and the authors note that the effective degeneracy at T ≈ 0.120 GeV naturally reproduces the hadronic degeneracy factor of pions and kaons.
