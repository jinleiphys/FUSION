# Current status of Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations in asymmetric nuclear matter

> We review the current status of our microscopic calculations in asymmetric nuclear matter. Updated predictions of the equation of state are made available to potential users. We discuss the features of our EoS in relation to the predicted neutron star maximum masses.

## Key claim

The paper presents updated Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) calculations of the equation of state (EoS) for asymmetric nuclear matter, claiming the EoS is moderately soft at low to medium density but fairly stiff at high densities, a feature that may be desirable given constraints from neutron star phenomenology and heavy-ion flow data.

## Method

The authors performed DBHF calculations using the Bonn B potential to compute the EoS for pure neutron matter and beta-equilibrated matter (including protons, electrons, and muons). They improved the determination of the single-nucleon potential and the convergence of the high-density calculation compared to their earlier work. The resulting energy densities and pressures were tabulated for use in neutron star structure calculations via the public RNS code.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: 0.185 fm^-3
- Saturation energy of symmetric nuclear matter: -16.1 MeV
- Symmetry pressure L: 69.6 MeV
- Neutron star maximum mass for pure neutron matter: 2.33 solar masses, with radius 11.2 km and central density 2.17 x 10^15 g/cm^3
- Neutron star maximum mass for beta-equilibrated matter: 2.27 solar masses, with radius 10.8 km and central density 2.26 x 10^15 g/cm^3
- Contribution to potential energy from the ^3S_1 partial wave at density 0.1853 fm^-3: -20.30 MeV; at 1.0554 fm^-3: +8.676 MeV

## Context

This work refines the asymmetric matter predictions originally calculated by the authors in 2003 and used in a neutron star calculation in 2006. The results are compared to relativistic mean field (RMF) models with density-dependent meson couplings (DD-RMF) and non-linear sigma meson self-interactions (NL-RMF), as well as to the DBHF predictions of Fuchs (2006). The updated EoS tables are intended for use in neutron star calculations and for parametrizations of the symmetry energy applicable to nuclear reaction experiments. The authors note that the symmetry energy is poorly constrained above 2-3 times saturation density, and they plan to extend the work to include hyperons.
