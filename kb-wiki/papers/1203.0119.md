# Neutron conduction in the inner crust of a neutron star in the framework of the band theory of solids

> Even though the "free" neutrons in the inner crust of a neutron star are superfluid, they are still strongly coupled to nuclei due to non-dissipative entrainment effects. These effects have been systematically studied in all regions of the inner crust in the framework of the band theory of solids. Using concepts from solid-state physics, it is shown that the density of conduction neutrons, i.e. neutrons that are effectively "free", can be much smaller than the density of unbound neutrons (by an order of magnitude in some layers) due to Bragg scattering. These results suggest that a revision of the interpretation of various observable neutron-star phenomena may be necessary.

## Key claim

Even though the "free" neutrons in the inner crust of a neutron star are superfluid and can flow without friction, they are still strongly coupled to nuclei due to non-dissipative entrainment effects; the density of conduction neutrons (those effectively "free") can be much smaller than the density of unbound neutrons (by an order of magnitude in some layers) due to Bragg scattering, suggesting that a revision of the interpretation of various observable neutron-star phenomena may be necessary.

## Method

The author systematically studied entrainment effects in all regions of the inner crust of a cold non-accreting neutron star using the band theory of solids, with the nuclear energy density functional theory (EDF) using a Skyrme-type functional (BSk14). The equilibrium composition of the crust was taken from prior ETFSI calculations (Onsi et al. 2008), and the neutron band structure was computed by solving the EDF equations with Bloch boundary conditions using a plane-wave expansion. The static long-wavelength neutron current-current correlation function was computed for various densities from neutron drip to the crust-core transition, expressed in terms of an effective neutron mass or a density of conduction neutrons.

## Key numbers

- At average baryon density \(\bar n = 0.02\) fm\(^{-3}\), the ratio of conduction neutron density to free neutron density is \(n_n^{\rm c}/n_n^{\rm f} = 7.37\%\), and the neutron effective mass is \(m_n^\star/m_n = 13.6\).
- At \(\bar n = 0.03\) fm\(^{-3}\), \(n_n^{\rm c}/n_n^{\rm f} = 7.33\%\) and \(m_n^\star/m_n = 13.6\).
- At the shallowest layer \(\bar n = 0.0003\) fm\(^{-3}\), \(n_n^{\rm c}/n_n^{\rm f} = 82.6\%\) and \(m_n^\star/m_n = 1.21\).
- At the densest layer \(\bar n = 0.08\) fm\(^{-3}\), \(n_n^{\rm c}/n_n^{\rm f} = 64.8\%\) and \(m_n^\star/m_n = 1.54\).
- The number of unbound neutrons per nucleus peaks at about 1417 at \(\bar n = 0.03\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The Skyrme functional BSk14 was fitted to experimental atomic mass data with a root mean square deviation of 0.73 MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior estimates of entrainment effects in neutron-star crusts, which had only been studied in shallow layers near the neutron drip density (\(\sim 4.7\times 10^{11}\) g\(\cdot\)cm\(^{-3}\)) and near the crust bottom at densities above \(5\times 10^{13}\) g\(\cdot\)cm\(^{-3}\) using different crust models (Chamel 2005, Chamel and Carter 2005, Chamel 2006). The systematic study across all inner crust densities reveals that at intermediate densities (\(\bar n \sim 0.02-0.03\) fm\(^{-3}\)), neutron conduction is almost completely suppressed, with the effective mass \(m_n^\star \simeq 13.6 m_n\) in close agreement with a previous estimate of \(m_n^\star \simeq 15.4 m_n\) from Chamel (2005) using a different crust model. These results suggest that a revision of the interpretation of pulsar glitches, quasiperiodic oscillations in Soft-Gamma Repeaters, and neutron star cooling may be necessary, and similar entrainment effects are expected in laboratory superfluid systems such as a unitary Fermi gas in a 1D optical lattice (Watanabe et al. 2008).
