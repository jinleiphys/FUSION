# Nuclear level densities in the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov plus combinatorial framework

> A systematic study of nuclear level densities has been carried out within the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov plus combinatorial framework. Calculations were performed for even-even nuclei with available experimental data, based on the relativistic energy density functionals DD-ME2, DD-PC1, and PC-PK1. The overall performance of the model is assessed against experimental data. On this basis, the effects of different functionals, pairing correlations, deformation, and other relevant factors on nuclear level densities are examined. The results show that the present framework provides a good description of the experimental level density and reproduces the s-wave neutron resonance spacings with an accuracy comparable to that of the best existing global models. Furthermore, differences among the adopted relativistic density functionals in the nucleon effective mass at saturated nuclear matter are transmitted to the predicted level densities and constitute the main source of the differences among the results obtained with the three functionals.

## Key claim

The relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov plus combinatorial framework provides a good description of experimental nuclear level densities and reproduces s-wave neutron resonance spacings with accuracy comparable to the best existing global models, but the differences among the three adopted relativistic density functionals (DD-ME2, DD-PC1, PC-PK1) in the nucleon effective mass at saturated nuclear matter are transmitted to the predicted level densities and constitute the main source of the differences among the results.

## Method

The authors performed systematic nuclear level density calculations for nearly 70 even-even nuclei using the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) theory combined with a combinatorial method that counts particle-hole excitations from self-consistent single-particle levels. Three relativistic energy density functionals were employed: DD-ME2 (density-dependent meson-exchange), DD-PC1 (density-dependent point-coupling), and PC-PK1 (nonlinear point-coupling). The combinatorial counting was performed on an energy grid with 0.01 MeV spacing, and vibrational effects (quadrupole, octupole, hexadecapole modes) were included by folding phonon states, with rotational effects treated explicitly for axially symmetric deformed nuclei using the Inglis-Belyaev moment of inertia.

## Key numbers

- Calculations were performed for nearly 70 even-even nuclei with available experimental data
- A pairing strength of G = 728 MeV fm^3 was adopted for the separable pairing interaction
- The energy step size for combinatorial counting was epsilon_0 = 0.01 MeV
- The smoothing width for removing fluctuations in state density was sigma = 0.2 MeV
- For PC-PK1, the Dirac effective mass at saturation is M_D*/M = 0.591 and the Landau mass is M_L*/M = 0.652; for DD-ME2, M_D*/M = 0.572 and M_L*/M = 0.635
- For 238U at constrained deformation beta_2 = 0.3, the neutron single-particle level density near the Fermi surface (sigma = 2.0 MeV) is 4.685 MeV^-1 for DD-ME2 and 5.193 MeV^-1 for PC-PK1
- The maximum number of folded phonons was three for light and medium-mass nuclei and four for rare-earth nuclei around A ~ 150 and heavier systems
- Nuclear level densities were calculated up to J = 50 hbar

## Context

This work extends the combinatorial method for nuclear level density calculations from the Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov framework (Hilaire, 2006; Goriely, 2008) and the Gogny Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov framework (Hilaire, 2012) into the relativistic energy density functional theory. It builds upon earlier relativistic combinatorial calculations for 112Cd (Jiang, 2024) and Sn isotopes (He, 2026) by performing a systematic study across nearly 70 nuclei with three different functionals. The results enable improved reaction cross section predictions within the Hauser-Feshbach formalism for nuclei where experimental level density information is scarce, particularly for astrophysical nucleosynthesis applications.
