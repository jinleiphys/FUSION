# Isospin decomposition of $γN \to N^*$ transitions within a dynamical coupled-channels model

> By extending the dynamical coupled-channels analysis performed in our previous work [Phys. Rev. C 88, 035209 (2013)] to include the available data of photoproduction of pi meson off the neutron, the transition amplitudes for the photo-excitation of the neutron to nucleon resonances, gamma n --> N*, at the resonance pole positions are determined. The combined fits to the data for both the proton- and neutron-target reactions also revise our results for the resonance pole positions and the gamma p --> N* transition amplitudes. Our results allow an isospin decomposition of the gamma N --> N* transition amplitudes for the isospin I=1/2 N* resonances, which is necessary for testing hadron structure models and gives crucial inputs for constructing models of neutrino-induced reactions in the nucleon resonance region.

## Key claim

By extending their previous dynamical coupled-channels analysis (Kamano et al., 2013) to include photoproduction data of pions off the neutron, the authors determine the transition amplitudes for gamma n -> N* at resonance pole positions, enabling an isospin decomposition of the gamma N -> N* amplitudes for isospin I=1/2 N* resonances; however, the extracted gamma n -> N* amplitudes are a first step and rely on data extracted from deuteron targets that may contain inconsistencies between different data sets.

## Method

The authors performed a combined fit to meson production data on both proton and neutron targets using a dynamical coupled-channels (DCC) model, solving coupled integral equations for partial-wave amplitudes with channels including pi N, eta N, K Lambda, K Sigma, pi Delta, rho N, and sigma N. The model incorporates hadron-exchange potentials derived from effective Lagrangians via a unitary transformation method, and the gamma N channel is treated perturbatively. Resonance parameters were extracted by analytically continuing the scattering amplitudes to the complex energy plane and evaluating residues at the pole positions.

## Key numbers

- Approximately 3,200 data points of gamma 'n' -> pi N were included for neutron-target reactions, with 2,305 data points for d sigma/d Omega of gamma 'n' -> pi- p, 308 for photon asymmetry Sigma, 94 for target polarization T, and 88 for recoil polarization P
- For gamma 'n' -> pi0 n, 148 data points for d sigma/d Omega and 216 for Sigma were included
- The analysis covers the energy region up to W = 2 GeV
- The N(1/2-(S11)) pole mass was found at (1490, 102) MeV (real part, -imaginary part) compared to (1482, 98) MeV in the 2013 model
- The Delta(3/2+(P33)) pole mass was found at (1212, 52) MeV compared to (1211, 51) MeV in the 2013 model
- The gamma p -> X total cross section was included at W less than or equal to 1.75 GeV to improve fits

## Context

This work extends the dynamical coupled-channels analysis of Kamano et al. (2013) by including neutron-target photoproduction data, providing the first isospin decomposition of gamma N -> N* transition amplitudes for I=1/2 resonances from this DCC framework. The results are necessary for testing hadron structure models and provide crucial inputs for constructing models of neutrino-induced reactions in the nucleon resonance region, as discussed by Nakamura et al. (2015). The authors note that future direct analysis of gamma d reaction data within their DCC approach would be needed to overcome inconsistencies in the currently available gamma 'n' -> pi N data extracted from deuteron targets.
