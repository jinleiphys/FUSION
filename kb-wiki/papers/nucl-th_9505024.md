# Pre-Equilibrium Particle Emission and Critical Exponent Analysis

> In two different phase transition models of nuclear fragmentation we show that the emission of pre-equilibrium particles and mixing of events from different classes cannot be ignored in the analysis of nuclear fragmentation data in terms of critical exponents, and we show how the apparent values of the extracted exponents are affected.

## Key claim

In two different phase transition models of nuclear fragmentation (an intra-nuclear cascade coupled with percolation or with the statistical multifragmentation model), the emission of pre-equilibrium particles and the mixing of events from different classes into the same multiplicity bins significantly alter the apparent values of critical exponents extracted from data, making a model-independent determination of true critical exponents incorrect.

## Method

The authors constructed two hybrid models: one combining an intra-nuclear cascade (INC) for pre-equilibrium energy deposition and residue formation with a percolation model for the equilibrium decay of the residue, and another combining the same INC with a statistical multifragmentation model (SMM). For a given impact parameter, the INC provided the residue charge, mass, and excitation energy; the excitation energy was converted into a percolation bond-breaking probability or used directly in the SMM. Inclusive mass distributions and their moments were obtained by integrating over impact parameter with geometrical probability weighting, and the results were compared to EOS collaboration data for 1 A GeV Au + C reactions.

## Key numbers

- The average residue charge after pre-equilibrium emission in central collisions is less than 49, implying emission of 30 pre-equilibrium charges from the initial gold nucleus (Z=79).
- The INC/percolation model yielded an apparent exponent `beta' = 0.35, compared to the nominal percolation value of beta = 0.41 and the experimental value of 0.29 +/- 0.02.
- The INC/percolation model yielded an apparent exponent `gamma' = 1.4, compared to the known 3d-percolation critical exponent gamma = 1.8.
- The INC/SMM model yielded an apparent exponent `beta' = 0.50.
- The critical multiplicity used in the analysis was m_c = 26.

## Context

This work extends earlier percolation model studies by Bauer (1985) that showed mixing of event classes affected extracted exponents, and responds to claims by Gilkes (1994) and the EOS collaboration that critical exponents could be extracted from spectator fragmentation data. The paper contradicts the approach of Elliott (1994) by demonstrating that the convolution of events with different temperatures into the same multiplicity bins prevents model-independent extraction of critical exponents. It enables future work analyzing exclusive events to identify actual equilibrated residues for studying the phase transition.
