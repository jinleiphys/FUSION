# Stochastic features of dissipative large-amplitude dynamics and nuclear fission

> Within a density matrix approach for nuclear many--body system, it is derived non--Markovian Langevin equations of motion for nuclear collective parameters, where memory effects are defined by memory time. The developed stochastic approach is applied to study both the nuclear descent from fission barrier to a scission point and thermal diffusive overcoming of the barrier. The present paper is partly a review of our results obtained earlier and contains new results on the non--Markovian generalization of Kramers' theory of escape rate and on time features of the collective dynamics in the presence of periodic external modulation.

## Key claim

The paper derives non-Markovian Langevin equations for nuclear collective parameters from a density matrix approach, showing that memory effects (characterized by a memory time τ) significantly influence both the descent from the fission barrier and the thermal diffusive escape over the barrier. The authors claim that the stochastic acceleration of nuclear descent and the hindering of barrier diffusion are both governed by the memory time, but these conclusions are qualified by the assumption of weak coupling between collective and intrinsic degrees of freedom and by the use of random matrix ensemble averaging.

## Method

The authors start from the Liouville equation for the nuclear many-body density matrix and apply Zwanzig's projection technique to derive non-Markovian master equations for the occupancies of adiabatic many-body states. They then perform ensemble averaging over random matrix elements (using GOE, GUE, and GSE statistics) and over energy level spacings to obtain a quantum diffusion equation for the probability density of intrinsic excitation energy. The resulting non-Markovian Langevin equations for collective variables are obtained by requiring energy conservation between the collective and intrinsic subsystems, with the fluctuation-dissipation theorem linking the random force correlations to the memory kernel. The approach is applied to two-dimensional nuclear fission dynamics for ²³⁶U and to one-dimensional Kramers-type barrier crossing.

## Key numbers

- Memory time values used: τ₁ = 2 × 10⁻²³ s (weak memory effects) and τ₂ = 8 × 10⁻²³ s (fairly strong memory effects) for the fission descent calculations
- Temperature T = 2 MeV used for the symmetric fission of ²³⁶U
- A bunch of 2 × 10⁴ stochastic trajectories was generated for the fission descent simulations
- The estimated memory time from comparison with experimental fission fragment kinetic energy data is τ ≈ 8 × 10⁻²³ s
- The spreading width Γ of coupling matrix elements is stated to be of several (tens) MeV for nuclear shell-model Hamiltonians

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier results (Kolomietz and Radionov, 2009) on non-Markovian fission dynamics by providing a more rigorous microscopic derivation from random matrix theory and by presenting new results on the non-Markovian generalization of Kramers' escape rate theory. The approach builds on the random matrix framework of Weidenmüller (1980) and Bulgac (2001), and on the functional integral treatment of metastable decay by Grabert (1984) and Hänggi (1985). The paper enables future studies of memory effects in nuclear fission fragment distributions and in the time-dependent modulation of fission barriers by external fields.
