# Derivation of reaction cross sections from experimental elastic backscattering probabilities

> The relationship between the backward elastic scattering probabilities and reaction cross sections is derived. This is a a very simple and useful method to extract reaction cross sections for heavy ion systems. We compare the results of our method with those using the traditional full elastic scattering angular distributions, for several systems, at energies near and above the Coulomb barrier. From the calculated reaction and capture cross sections using the present method, we derive the cross sections of other mechanisms for nearly spherical systems.

## Key claim

The authors derive a simple formula that relates the reaction cross section directly to the elastic scattering excitation function measured at a single backward angle (near 180 degrees), rather than requiring full angular distributions. The method is validated for several heavy-ion systems at energies near and above the Coulomb barrier, but the authors note that the extracted cross sections sometimes require empirical energy shifts (e.g., 0.3–0.7 MeV) with no clear physical justification, and the method's reliability decreases at sub-barrier energies where the deviation from Rutherford scattering is comparable to experimental uncertainties.

## Method

The authors start from the conservation of total reaction flux, which gives the reaction probability at angular momentum J=0 as P_R(E,J=0) = 1 - P_el(E,J=0), where P_el is the ratio of elastic to Rutherford differential cross sections at 180 degrees. They then approximate the J-dependence of the reaction probability by an energy shift derived from the centrifugal potential and barrier curvature, convert the partial-wave sum into an integral, and obtain a closed-form expression (Eq. 3cn_eq) for the reaction cross section σ_R(E_cm) in terms of the measured elastic backscattering excitation function. The same formalism is extended to capture cross sections using quasielastic scattering probabilities and a critical angular momentum J_cr where the nuclear potential pocket vanishes.

## Key numbers

* For the ⁶Li + ⁶⁴Zn reaction, the extracted capture cross section required an energy shift of 0.7 MeV to higher energies to match experimental data.
* For the ¹⁶O + ²⁰⁸Pb reaction, the extracted reaction cross section required an energy shift of 0.3 MeV to higher energies.
* For the ⁶Li + ⁶⁴Zn reaction, the extracted reaction cross section required an energy shift of 0.4 MeV to lower energies.
* The maximum absolute value of the transfer cross section σ_tr for ⁷Li + ⁶⁴Zn at energies near the Coulomb barrier is about 30 mb.
* The extraction of reaction (capture) cross sections from elastic (quasielastic) backscattering probabilities leads to uncertainties of the order of 10% at energies above the Coulomb barrier.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Sargsyan 2013a, 2013b) on extracting cross sections from backscattering data, and builds on the theoretical framework of Canto (2006) relating elastic and reaction probabilities. The method is compared against the traditional approach of fitting full elastic scattering angular distributions with optical potentials, as done by Timmers et al. and others for systems like ⁴He + ⁹²Mo, ⁴He + ¹¹⁰,¹¹⁶Cd, ⁴He + ¹¹²,¹²⁰Sn, ⁶,⁷Li + ⁶⁴Zn, and ¹⁶O + ²⁰⁸Pb. The technique enables the approximate determination of the sum of transfer and inelastic scattering cross sections by subtracting the extracted capture cross section from the reaction cross section, which is demonstrated for the ⁶,⁷Li + ⁶⁴Zn systems.
