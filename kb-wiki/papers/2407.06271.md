# Circularly polarized photon emission from magnetized chiral plasmas

> We investigate the emission of circularly polarized photons from a magnetized quark-gluon plasma with nonzero quark-number and chiral charge chemical potentials. These chemical potentials qualitatively influence the differential emission rates of circularly polarized photons. A nonzero net electric charge density, induced by quark-number chemical potentials, enhances the overall emission of one circular polarization over the other, while a nonzero chiral charge density introduces a spatial asymmetry in the emission with respect to reflection in the transverse plane. The signs of the electrical and chiral charge densities determine which circular polarization dominates overall and whether the emission preferentially aligns with or opposes the magnetic field. Based on these findings, we propose that polarized photon emission is a promising observable for characterizing the quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The authors propose that circularly polarized photon emission from a magnetized quark-gluon plasma can serve as a promising observable for characterizing the plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions, but they note that their definitions for circular polarization rates are approximate and slightly overestimate the parity-odd contributions because unphysical polarization states contribute to the total rate.

## Method

The authors calculate the imaginary part of the retarded photon polarization tensor at leading order in the coupling using a one-loop diagram in a strongly magnetized quark-gluon plasma, assuming the chiral limit for up and down quarks and including nonzero quark-number and chiral chemical potentials. They separate the total emission rate into circularly polarized components using projection operators based on the polarization vectors epsilon_plus and epsilon_minus, then numerically evaluate the differential emission rates by summing over up to 500 Landau levels for various magnetic field strengths, temperatures, and chemical potentials.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field strengths considered: |eB| = m_pi^2 and |eB| = 5 m_pi^2, where m_pi is the pion mass
- Temperatures used: T = 200 MeV and T = 350 MeV
- Quark-number chemical potential representative value: mu = 200 MeV
- Chiral chemical potential representative value: mu_5 = 100 MeV
- The degree of circular polarization ranges from approximately 0.12 to 0.5 for the model parameters explored with nonzero mu
- Transverse momenta studied: k_T = 0.1 m_pi, 0.25 m_pi, 0.5 m_pi, and 0.9 m_pi
- Summation truncated at n_max = 500 Landau levels for numerical accuracy
- Angular step size used: delta phi = pi/600

## Context

This work extends previous studies of unpolarized photon emission from magnetized plasmas by Wang et al. (2020, 2021) and earlier attempts to study chiral charge effects on electromagnetic probes by Yee (2013) and Mamo (2013, 2015). The results suggest that polarized photon emission could provide a more definitive observable for detecting chiral plasma effects in heavy-ion collisions than the chiral magnetic effect, which has remained inconclusive in experiments at RHIC and LHC. The authors note that the threshold spikes in their rates would smooth out if quark damping rates from Ghosh (2024) were included, but the qualitative features would remain unchanged.
