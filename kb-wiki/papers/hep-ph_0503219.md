# A classical Odderon in QCD at high energies

> We show that the weight functional for color sources in the classical theory of the Color Glass Condensate includes a term which generates Odderon excitations. Remarkably, the classical origin of these excitations can be traced to the random walk of partons in the two dimensional space spanned by the SU(3) Casimirs. This term is naturally suppressed for a large nucleus at high energies.

## Key claim

The Color Glass Condensate (CGC) classical effective theory includes a term proportional to the cubic Casimir operator of SU(3) that generates Odderon excitations, but this term is naturally suppressed for a large nucleus at high energies.

## Method

The authors derived the distribution of color charges in the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model from a random walk analysis of SU(3) quark sources in transverse coordinate space, obtaining recursion relations for the multiplicity of color representations. They then constructed a path integral for the color charge density that includes both the quadratic and cubic Casimir terms, and computed expectation values of the dipole Odderon operator and the baryon Odderon operator in both the weak field limit and to all orders in the classical field.

## Key numbers

- The average color charge squared per unit area per color degree of freedom is μ_A^2 = g^2 A / (2π R^2), of order A^{1/3} fm^{-2}
- The cubic Casimir correction in the multiplicity distribution is of size O(1/√k) for large k, where k is the number of quarks in a coarse-grained cell
- The cubic Casimir coupling constant in the path integral is κ_A = g^3 A^2 N_c / (π^2 R^4)
- For A=1 and N_c=3, the weak field results for both the dipole Odderon operator and baryon Odderon operator agree exactly with Hatta et al. (2005)
- The all-orders result for the dipole Odderon operator contains an exponential suppression factor involving the saturation scale Q_{s,A}^2

## Context

This work extends the MV model (McLerran and Venugopalan, 1994) by showing that for SU(3) quark sources, the classical weight functional includes a cubic Casimir term beyond the original Gaussian ansatz. It directly connects to the work of Hatta et al. (2005), who identified Odderon operators in the CGC framework and argued they were absent in the original MV model, and to Kovchegov et al. (2003), who computed non-linear saturation corrections to the BKP Odderon equation. The results provide initial conditions for quantum evolution of Odderon operators under JIMWLK renormalization group equations.
