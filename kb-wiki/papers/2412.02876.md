# Data-driven analysis of dipole strength functions using artificial neural networks

> We present a data-driven analysis of dipole strength functions across the nuclear chart, employing an artificial neural network to model and predict nuclear dipole responses. We train the network on a dataset of experimentally measured dipole strength functions for 216 different nuclei. To assess its predictive capability, we test the trained model on an additional set of 10 new nuclei, where experimental data exist. Our results demonstrate that the artificial neural network not only accurately reproduces known data but also identifies potential inconsistencies in certain experimental datasets, indicating which results may warrant further review or possible rejection. Additionally, for nuclei where experimental data are sparse or unavailable, the network confirms theoretical calculations, reinforcing its utility as a predictive tool in nuclear physics. Finally, utilizing the predicted electric dipole polarizability, we extract the value of the symmetry energy at saturation density and find it consistent with results from the literature.

## Key claim

The artificial neural network (ANN) trained on dipole strength functions from 216 nuclei can accurately reproduce known data and identify potentially unreliable experimental datasets, but its predictive power is strongest for interpolation between known nuclei and weak for extrapolation beyond the training data, especially in the neutron-rich region.

## Method

The authors trained a feedforward neural network with four hidden layers (16, 32, 32, 32 neurons) on 32,958 data points of dipole strength functions from 226 nuclei, using mass number A, proton number Z, and excitation energy E as inputs. After spline interpolation augmentation to 166,370 points, the model was tested on 10 nuclei excluded from training, and uncertainty was quantified using ensemble learning with 68% highest density intervals.

## Key numbers

- Training dataset: 216 nuclei; testing set: 10 nuclei (19F, 58Ni, 70Ge, 89Y, 114Sn, 146Nd, 152Sm, 174Yb, 207Pb, 234U)
- Total dataset: 32,958 data points from 226 nuclei, augmented to 166,370 points
- Network architecture: 4 hidden layers with [16, 32, 32, 32] neurons
- Extracted symmetry energy at saturation density: J = 32(3) MeV from fitting the Migdal model to ANN predictions for A >= 48
- Integration range for alpha_D: 2 to 40 MeV
- Fits restricted to A >= 48 to avoid surface contributions in light nuclei

## Context

This work presents the first data-driven analysis of dipole strength functions using machine learning across the nuclear chart, extending prior approaches that relied on ab initio nuclear theory (Hagen 2016, Miorelli 2016, Bonaiti 2022) or density functional theory (Roca-Maza 2018). The ANN predictions enable comparison with coupled-cluster calculations for calcium isotopes and allow extraction of the symmetry energy, consistent with constraints from Lattimer (2023), Hu (2022), and Essick (2021). The method enables identification of potentially unreliable experimental datasets, such as those from McCarthy (1975) for 70Ge and Vasilev (1969) for 152Sm, and highlights the need for new experimental data in the neutron-rich sector to improve extrapolation capabilities.
