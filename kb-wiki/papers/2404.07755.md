# Flavor asymmetry from the non-perturbative nucleon sea

> We demonstrate, in the context of a scalar version of the chiral effective field theory, that the multi-sea quark contribution to the nucleon is significant and highly non-trivial in sharp contrast to the prediction of perturbation theory. The non-perturbative calculation is performed in the Fock sector dependent renormalization scheme on the light front, in which the non-perturbative renormalization is incorporated. The calculation suggests that a fully non-perturbative calculation of the chiral EFT is needed to obtain a robust result to be compared with the recent experimental measurement of flavor asymmetry in the proton.

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that multi-pion (multi-sea) contributions to the nucleon are significant and highly non-trivial in a scalar version of chiral effective field theory, in sharp contrast to perturbation theory predictions. However, they caution that a fully non-perturbative calculation of the actual chiral EFT is needed to obtain a robust result comparable to experimental measurements of flavor asymmetry in the proton.

## Method
The authors perform a non-perturbative calculation using the light-front Hamiltonian formalism with a Fock sector dependent renormalization scheme. They diagonalize the light-front quantized Hamiltonian in Fock space, truncating the expansion at four-body (one baryon plus up to three pions) and including states with nucleons, pions, and Delta baryons. The longitudinal momentum distribution functions (LMDFs) are computed from the light-front wave functions and then convoluted with pion and bare proton PDFs at a scale of 25.5 GeV^2 to obtain the flavor asymmetry.

## Key numbers
- Coupling constant used: g_{Npi} = 6.0 GeV (alpha = 0.8) and g_Delta = 6.5 GeV, chosen to reproduce SeaQuest data
- Integrated flavor asymmetry over 0.13 < x < 0.45: 0.0122(7), consistent with the SeaQuest/E906 measurement of 0.0159(60)
- Scale for PDFs: mu^2 = 25.5 GeV^2
- Effective coupling g_{pi N} approximately 13 is noted as large

## Context
This work extends the pion cloud model calculations of Alberg et al. (2021) which used leading-order light-cone perturbation theory and showed deviation from SeaQuest/E906 data at large x (x greater than 0.3). The present non-perturbative calculation includes multi-pion Fock sectors (up to three pions) that were neglected in the perturbative approach, and shows improved agreement with the SeaQuest/E906 measurement of the ratio dbar/ubar for x greater than 0.1. The results suggest that a fully non-perturbative calculation of chiral effective field theory is the natural next step.
