# How to include a three-nucleon force into Faddeev equations for the 3N continuum: a new form

> A new, more efficient approach to include a three nucleon force into three-nucleon continuum calculations is presented. Results obtained in the new and our old approach are compared both for elastic nucleon-deuteron scattering as well as for the breakup process. The advantages of the new scheme are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new, more efficient single Faddeev-type equation for three-nucleon (3N) continuum calculations that includes a three-nucleon force (3NF), replacing the previously used coupled set of two equations; the new form exploits the natural decomposition of the 3NF into three parts, each symmetric under exchange of two particles, to combine each part with the corresponding nucleon-nucleon (NN) force.

## Method

The authors split the 3NF V4 into three parts V4(i), each symmetric under exchange of two particles, and insert this decomposition into the 3N Schrodinger equation in integral form. They then derive a single Faddeev equation for the Faddeev component psi, introducing the NN t-matrix in the standard way, which yields Eq. (17): psi = phi + G0 t P psi + (1 + G0 t) G0 V4(1) (1 + P) psi. From this, they obtain a single equation for the breakup amplitude tilde T, Eq. (21), and the elastic amplitude U, Eq. (23), and demonstrate the formal equivalence to the earlier coupled set of equations (8) by algebraic manipulation.

## Key numbers

* The new single equation (21) for tilde T replaces the earlier coupled set of two equations (8) used in prior work by the same group (Huber et al., 1995; Witala et al., 1995; Glockle et al., 1995).
* The paper shows numerical comparisons for elastic nucleon-deuteron scattering observables (Cyy, 10 Kyx'z') and breakup observables (d5 sigma / dOmega1 dOmega2 dS, 100 Ay(d)) as functions of scattering angle theta, with the new and old approaches yielding essentially identical curves (solid, dashed, and dotted lines overlap in the figures).
* The new scheme is described as requiring less extensive resources in storage and computer time compared to the previous recursive expansion in powers of V4 used for the coupled set.

## Context

This work extends the approach previously applied to the 3N bound state by Stadler et al. (1994) and later by others, where the 3NF is split into three parts and combined with NN forces in a single Faddeev equation. It replaces the earlier coupled set of equations (8) used by the same authors for 3N continuum calculations (Huber et al., 1995; Witala et al., 1995; Glockle et al., 1995), which required expanding t4, T, and T4 in powers of V4 and was numerically expensive. The new single equation enables more efficient calculations of elastic nucleon-deuteron scattering and the breakup process with full inclusion of a three-nucleon force.
