# Heavy-ion collisions at the Large Hadron Collider: a review of the results from Run 1

> We present an overview of the results obtained in pPb and PbPb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider during Run 1. We first discuss the results for global characteristics: cross sections, hadron multiplicities, azimuthal asymmetries, correlations at low transverse momentum, hadrochemistry, and femtoscopy. We then review hard and electromagnetic probes: particles with high transverse momentum, jets, heavy quarks, quarkonium, electroweak bosons and high transverse momentum photons, low transverse momentum photons and dileptons, and ultraperipheral collisions. We mainly focus on the experimental results, and present very briefly the main current theoretical explanations.

## Key claim

This review paper summarizes the experimental results from the first run (Run 1) of heavy-ion collisions at the Large Hadron Collider, focusing on pPb and PbPb collisions, and presents only brief theoretical explanations; the authors note that pPb collisions cannot be considered a simple reference system because they show features in common with PbPb collisions.

## Method

The authors compiled and organized the main experimental measurements from the ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS collaborations for pPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV and PbPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV, covering both soft probes (global characteristics, azimuthal asymmetries, correlations, hadrochemistry, femtoscopy) and hard/electromagnetic probes (high-pT particles, jets, heavy quarks, quarkonia, electroweak bosons, photons, dileptons, ultraperipheral collisions). The review integrates pPb results within each observable category rather than treating them as a separate section.

## Key numbers

* Total inelastic cross section in pPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV: 2061 +/- 3 (stat.) +/- 34 (syst.) +/- 72 (lumi.) mb, measured by CMS
* Visible cross section in pPb collisions: 2.09 +/- 0.07 b, measured by ALICE for events with activity in pseudorapidity regions 2.8<eta<5.1 and -3.7<eta<-1.7
* Hadronic cross section in PbPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV: 7.7 +/- 0.1 (stat.) +0.6/-0.5 (syst.) b, measured by ALICE
* Electromagnetic dissociation cross section in PbPb collisions: 187.4 +/- 0.2 (stat.) +13.2/-11.2 (syst.) b, measured by ALICE
* Energy density estimate for central PbPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV: epsilon = 14 GeV/fm^3 for formation time tau_0 = 1 fm/c, from Bjorken formula
* Total integrated charged multiplicity for 0-5% most central PbPb collisions: N_ch = 17165 +/- 772, from ALICE
* Transverse energy in central PbPb collisions reaches up to approximately 2.1 TeV
* Charged particle multiplicity density per participant pair increases from RHIC to LHC with a power-law dependence s^0.15
* pT-integrated elliptic flow (v2) increases by about 30% from RHIC to LHC energies
* Elliptic flow measurements extended to pT > 50 GeV/c in PbPb collisions

## Context

This review extends the results from the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) program, where experiments at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV first indicated the creation of a strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma behaving as an almost ideal liquid (Gyulassy 2004, Muller 2006). The LHC results at higher energy (2.76 TeV for PbPb) show a denser, hotter, longer-lived medium with increased particle multiplicities and transverse energy. The findings enable detailed comparisons with hydrodynamic models (such as VISHNU by Song 2010) and initial-state models (such as the Color-Glass Condensate, MC-KLN by Drescher 2006, IP-Glasma by Schenke 2013, and EKRT by Niemi 2015), and raise open questions about the origin of collective-like behavior observed in high-multiplicity pPb and even pp collisions.
