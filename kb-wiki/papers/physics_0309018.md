# Finite nuclear size effect on Lamb shift of s1/2, p1/2, and p3/2 atomic states

> We consider one-loop self-energy and vacuum polarization radiative corrections to the shift of atomic energy level due to finite nuclear size. Analytic expressions for vacuum polarization corrections are derived. For the self-energy of p1/2 and p3/2 states in addition to already known terms we derive next-to-leading nonlogarithmic Zα-terms. Together with contributions obtained earlier the terms derived in the present work give explicit analytic expressions for s1/2 and p1/2 corrections which agree with results of previous numerical calculations up to Z=100 (Z is the nuclear charge number). We also show that the finite nuclear size radiative correction for a p3/2 state is not small compared to the similar correction for a p1/2 state at least for small Z.

## Key claim

The paper provides analytic expressions for the finite nuclear size (FNS) radiative corrections to the Lamb shift that agree with previous numerical calculations up to Z=100, and demonstrates that the self-energy and vertex FNS correction for a p3/2 state is comparable to that for a p1/2 state at least for small Z, contrary to what might be expected from the relative sizes of the leading-order FNS shifts.

## Method

The authors calculate one-loop self-energy and vertex (SEVFNS) and vacuum polarization (VPFNS) radiative corrections to atomic energy level shifts due to finite nuclear size, using a Zα-expansion approach. For the SEVFNS corrections, they compute next-to-leading nonlogarithmic α(Zα) terms for p1/2 and p3/2 states by evaluating scattering amplitudes in the Fried-Yennie gauge with an effective FNS perturbation, then averaging over nonrelativistic wave functions. For the VPFNS corrections, they derive analytic expressions for s1/2 and p1/2 states by incorporating the electron loop into the FNS interaction energy and evaluating the resulting integrals.

## Key numbers

* The relative SEVFNS correction for s1/2 states is given by Δ^{SEV}_{s1/2} = -α(Zα)(23/4 - 4 ln 2) - (α(Zα)²/π)(15/4 - π²/6) ln(bλ_C/r₀), with the leading unaccounted term of order ~α(Zα)²/π.
* The relative SEVFNS correction for p1/2 states is Δ^{SEV}_{p1/2} = α(8/(9π))(ln(1/(Zα)²) + 0.910) - α(Zα)(-379/432 + (16/3) ln 2) - (α(Zα)²/π)(15/4 - π²/6) ln(bλ_C/r₀), where 0.910 is the constant for the 2p1/2 state.
* The relative SEVFNS correction for p3/2 states (defined relative to the p1/2 FNS shift) is Δ^{SEV}_{p3/2} = α(8/(9π))(ln(1/(Zα)²) - 0.215) - α(Zα)(-559/432 + 4 ln 2), where -0.215 is the constant for the 2p3/2 state.
* The leading-order VPFNS correction for s1/2 states is Δ^{VP(1)}_{s1/2} = (3/4)α(Zα), with a double-logarithmic contribution (2α(Zα)²/(3πγ)) ln²(bλ_C/r₀).
* The zero-order VPFNS correction for p1/2 states is Δ^{VP(0)}_{p1/2} = -(8/(45π))α.
* The nuclear radius is approximated as r₀ ≈ 1.1 A^{1/3} fm ≈ 1.5 Z^{1/3} fm.
* The leading-order FNS shifts scale as ΔE_{ns1/2} ∝ ⟨r²⟩, ΔE_{np1/2} ∝ ⟨r²⟩, and ΔE_{np3/2} ∝ ⟨r⁴⟩.

## Context

This work extends the analytical Zα-expansion approach to finite nuclear size radiative corrections previously developed by the same authors (Milstein, Sushkov, and Terekhov) and others, building on numerical calculations by Mohr (1998), Cheng, Johnson, Sapirstein, and others (1993), and earlier analytical work by Pachucki (1993) and Milstein (2000). The analytic expressions for s1/2 and p1/2 SEVFNS corrections agree with numerical results up to Z=100, and the derived VPFNS formulas similarly match numerical calculations by Johnson and Sapirstein (1998). The finding that p3/2 SEVFNS corrections are comparable to p1/2 corrections is significant for precision tests of quantum electrodynamics in strong fields and for calculations of isotope shifts in heavy atoms and ions.
