# The discovery of gluon-dominant system in heavy ion collisions ---- the large elliptic flow of direct photons

> The discussion of the large elliptic flow of direct photons in Quark Matter 2014 has been reviewed. Then based on previous technique work, the physics meaning related to the large elliptic flow of direct photons is invetigated in this paper.

## Key claim

The large elliptic flow of direct photons in heavy ion collisions is explained by a two-timescale scenario in which the pre-equilibrium system is gluon-dominated, suppressing photon emission at early times despite high temperature, rather than by pre-equilibrium flow without photon emission.

## Method

The author constructs a model where the pre-equilibrium system evolves toward thermal equilibrium (fast, via elastic and inelastic scattering) and chemical equilibrium (slower, via inelastic channels only). The gluon-dominated early stage is described by a quark fugacity that increases linearly with time, suppressing the photon emission rate until QGP formation. Hydrodynamic evolution is constrained by hadron data, and the QGP formation time is extracted by fitting photon spectrum and elliptic flow data.

## Key numbers

- For PbPb collisions at 2.76 TeV, hadron data constrained hydro evolution with initial time 0.35 fm/c and QGP formation time 1.5 fm/c reproduce the magnitude of large direct photon v2 measured by ALICE.
- For AuAu collisions at 200 GeV, hadron data constrained hydro evolution produces only 60% of the measured photon v2, with best choice of QGP formation time 1.5 fm/c, due to initial time 0.6 fm/c.
- Typical x (ratio between temperature and inject energy) is about 0.001 at RHIC and LHC heavy ion collisions.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous technique paper (Liu, 2012) and addresses the puzzle highlighted at Quark Matter 2014, where multiple groups concluded that pre-equilibrium flow without photon emission was needed but could not explain why the hottest early stage emits few photons. The model enables predictions for higher harmonics (v3, v4, v5) of direct photons without new parameters, and the author notes that PHENIX (Adare, 2014) reported v3 of direct photons behaving similarly to hadrons, consistent with the picture. The discovery of a gluon-dominant system has applications to understanding charmonium production, dileptons, and broader questions about energy and light in the universe.
