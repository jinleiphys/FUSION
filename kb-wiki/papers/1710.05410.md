# The effect of quantum fluctuations in compact star observables

> Astrophysical measurements regarding compact stars are just ahead of a big evolution jump, since the NICER experiment deployed on ISS on 14 June 2017. This will soon provide data that would enable the determination of compact star radius with less than 10% error. This poses new challenges for nuclear models aiming to explain the structure of super dense nuclear matter found in neutron stars. Detailed studies of the QCD phase diagram shows the importance of bosonic quantum fluctuations in the cold dense matter equation of state. Here, we using a demonstrative model to show the effect of bosonic quantum fluctuations on compact star observables such as mass, radius, and compactness. We have also calculated the difference in the value of compressibility which is caused by quantum fluctuations. The above mentioned quantities are calculated in mean field, one-loop and in high order many-loop approximation. The results show that the magnitude of these effects is ~5%, which place it into the region where forthcoming high-accuracy measurements may detect it.

## Key claim

The effect of bosonic quantum fluctuations on compact star observables (mass, radius, compactness, compressibility) is in the range of 4–5%, which places it within the region where forthcoming high-accuracy measurements from NICER and gravitational-wave observations may detect it, but current data are not yet precise enough to provide constraints.

## Method

The authors used a demonstrative Yukawa-type model with one bosonic and one fermionic degree of freedom, coupled via a Yukawa interaction. They computed the equation of state at zero temperature and finite chemical potential using the functional renormalization group (FRG) method in the Local Potential Approximation (LPA) with the optimized Litim regulator, solving the Wetterich equation at mean field, one-loop, and high-order many-loop approximations. The resulting equations of state were then used to solve the Tolman–Oppenheimer–Volkov (TOV) equations to obtain mass-radius relations, compactness, and compressibility for compact stars.

## Key numbers

- The maximal deviation between approximations for mass and radius is about 5%.
- The high-order FRG-LPA calculation predicts compressibility approximately 8% higher than the mean field result.
- The one-loop calculation gives compressibility about 3–4% higher than the mean field reference.
- The maximum relative deviation in mass near the maximum mass is at least 0.1 (10%), and in radius near the maximum radius is at least 0.05 (5%).
- For other sections of the mass-radius diagram, the difference is approximately 4% for mass and 2% for radius.
- The cutoff scale for the model was chosen as Lambda = 1.4 GeV.
- The nuclear saturation density used for reference is n0 = 0.153 fm^-3.

## Context

This work extends prior FRG-based studies by Posfay (2015) and Jakovac (2017) on bosonic fluctuations in dense matter, applying them for the first time to compact star observables. The results are compared to phenomenological equations of state such as WFF1 and SQM3 from Ozel (2016). The paper demonstrates that quantum fluctuations produce effects at the 5% level, which is comparable to the expected ~10% precision of NICER data and the constraints from the GW170817 gravitational-wave event, suggesting that future multi-messenger observations may help resolve the masquerade problem in neutron star modeling.
