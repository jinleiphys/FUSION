# Viscous anisotropic hydrodynamics for the Gubser flow

> In this work we describe the dynamics of a highly anisotropic system undergoing boost-invariant longitudinal and azimuthally symmetric radial expansion (Gubser flow) for arbitrary shear viscosity to entropy density ratio. We derive the equations of motion of dissipative anisotropic hydrodynamics by applying to this situation the moments method recently derived by Molnár et al. (MNR) [1,2], based on an expansion around an arbitrary anisotropic one-particle distribution function. One requires an additional evolution equation in order to close the conservation laws. This is achieved by selecting the relaxation equation for the longitudinal pressure with a suitable Landau matching condition. As a result one obtains two coupled differential equations for the energy density and the longitudinal pressure which respect the $SO(3)_q\otimes SO(1,1)\otimes Z_2$ symmetry of the Gubser flow in the deSitter space. These equations are solved numerically and compared with the predictions of the recently found exact solution of the relaxation-time-approximation Boltzmann equation subject to the same flow. We also compare our numerical results with other fluid dynamical models. We observe that the MNR description of anisotropic fluid dynamics reproduces the space-time evolution of the system than all other currently known hydrodynamical approaches.

# Viscous anisotropic hydrodynamics for the Gubser flow

## Key claim
The authors claim that anisotropic hydrodynamics with the $\hat{\mathcal{P}}_L$ matching scheme (MNR method) reproduces the space-time evolution of a system undergoing Gubser flow more accurately than all other currently known hydrodynamical approaches, but this conclusion is qualified by the specific comparison to the exact solution of the relaxation-time-approximation (RTA) Boltzmann equation, not to experimental data or full quantum chromodynamics.

## Method
The authors apply the moments method of Molnár et al. (MNR) to derive equations of motion for dissipative anisotropic hydrodynamics under Gubser flow, which describes boost-invariant longitudinal and azimuthally symmetric radial expansion. They close the conservation laws by selecting a relaxation equation for the longitudinal pressure with a Landau matching condition, obtaining two coupled differential equations for the energy density and longitudinal pressure that respect the $SO(3)_q \otimes SO(1,1) \otimes Z_2$ symmetry of Gubser flow in de Sitter space. These equations are solved numerically and compared with the exact solution of the RTA Boltzmann equation for the same flow, as well as with predictions from second-order viscous hydrodynamics (DNMR), leading-order NRS anisotropic hydrodynamics, and next-to-leading order NRS anisotropic hydrodynamics.

## Key numbers
- Initial anisotropy parameter $\xi_0 = 100$, initial de Sitter time $\rho_0 = -10$, and initial temperature $\hat{T}_0 = 0.002$ are used for the numerical comparisons.
- Three specific shear viscosity values are studied: $4\pi\eta/s = 1$, 3, and 10, shown in the top, middle, and bottom rows of panels respectively.
- The normalized shear stress is defined as $\hat{\bar\pi} = 3\hat\pi/(4\hat\epsilon)$, where $\hat\epsilon$ is the energy density.
- At large $\rho$ values where the Knudsen number grows exponentially, anisotropic hydrodynamics with $\hat{\mathcal{P}}_L$ matching deviates from the exact solution by more than a few percent.

## Context
This work extends the MNR anisotropic hydrodynamics method previously applied to Bjorken flow by Molnár et al. (2016) to the more complex Gubser flow geometry, which includes both longitudinal and radial expansion. It builds on the exact solution of the RTA Boltzmann equation for Gubser flow found by Denicol et al. (2014) and compares against standard second-order viscous hydrodynamics (DNMR) from Denicol et al. (2012), the leading-order NRS scheme from Nopoush et al. (2014), and next-to-leading order NRS with residual viscous corrections. The results confirm that the $\hat{\mathcal{P}}_L$ matching scheme provides the most accurate description of hydrodynamic moments among the tested approaches, and the authors note this scheme can be generalized to non-conformal systems undergoing arbitrary expansion.
