# Particle Interferometry from 40 MeV to 40 TeV

> We review particle interferometry before 2000, including: basics and formalism of quantum-statistical correlations; model-independent analysis of short-range correlations; Coulomb wave-function corrections and the core/halo picture for $n$-particle Bose-Einstein correlations; graph rules to calculate these correlations even with partial coherence in the core; particle interferometry in $e^+e^-$ collisions including the Andersson-Hofmann model; invariant Buda-Lund particle interferometry; Buda-Lund, Bertsch-Pratt and Yano-Koonin-Podgoretskii parameterizations; Buda-Lund hydro model and its applications to (π/K)+p and Pb+Pb collisions at CERN SPS, as well as to low energy heavy ion collisions; binary source formalism and the related oscillations in the two-particle Bose-Einstein and Fermi-Dirac correlation functions; experimental signs of expanding rings of fire and shells of fire in particle and heavy ion physics and their similarity to planetary nebulae in stellar astronomy; signal of partial restoration of the U_A(1) symmetry restoration in the two-pion correlation function; back-to-back correlations of bosons with in-medium mass modifications; and the analytic solution of the pion-laser model.

# Particle Interferometry from 40 MeV to 40 TeV

## Key claim
Particle interferometry (Bose-Einstein and Fermi-Dirac correlations) provides a tool for reconstructing the space-time picture of particle emission across an enormous energy range, but the ultimate understanding of the form and meaning of the correlation functions remains incomplete, and the field requires simultaneous analysis of particle spectra and quantum-statistical correlations rather than either alone.

## Method
The paper reviews the theoretical formalism of quantum-statistical correlations, including the derivation of n-particle Bose-Einstein correlation functions from symmetrized amplitudes and the Wigner-function representation. It presents model-independent expansion techniques (Laguerre expansions for exponential shapes, Edgeworth expansions for Gaussian shapes) to characterize short-range correlations without assuming a specific source model, and describes Coulomb wave-function correction methods for multi-particle correlations using approximate n-body Coulomb wave functions. The review also covers the core/halo picture, the Buda-Lund hydrodynamical model and its parameterizations, the binary source formalism, and the analytic solution of the pion-laser model.

## Key numbers
- RHIC at Brookhaven will collide 100 + 100 AGeV 197Au nuclei, yielding about 40 TeV total energy in the center-of-mass frame.
- LHC at CERN will collide 208Pb nuclei with 2.76 + 2.76 ATeV bombarding energy, yielding 1150 TeV total energy in the center-of-mass frame.
- At AGS, 197Au nuclei were accelerated to 14.5 AGeV bombarding energy.
- At CERN SPS, collisions were made with 60 and 200 AGeV beams of 16O nuclei, 200 AGeV beams of 32S nuclei, and 40 and 158 AGeV beams of 208Pb nuclei.
- For the UA1 experiment, the Laguerre expansion gave R_L = 2.44 ± 0.12 fm, λ_L = 1.23 ± 0.07, and the core/halo intercept λ_* = 1.14 ± 0.10.
- For the NA22 experiment, the Laguerre expansion gave R_L = 1.35 ± 0.14 fm, λ_L = 1.37 ± 0.10, and the core/halo intercept λ_* = 1.11 ± 0.17.
- For typical effective source sizes in high energy heavy ion reactions (R = 5 fm), the new Coulomb wave-function integration method removes a systematic error as big as 100% from higher-order multi-particle Bose-Einstein correlation functions.
- For typical R = 1 fm effective source sizes in elementary particle reactions, the generalized n-body Gamow penetration factor gave Coulomb correction estimates within 5% of the Coulomb-wave correction.

## Context
This review extends the foundational work on Bose-Einstein correlations from the original Hanbury Brown-Twiss (HBT) discovery in astronomy and the early particle-physics applications by Goldhaber, Goldhaber, Lee and Pais (1960). It connects particle interferometry in e+e- collisions (including the Andersson-Hofmann model) to heavy-ion physics at AGS, CERN SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies, and highlights the unexpected similarity of multi-dimensional Bose-Einstein correlations between elementary particle and heavy-ion reactions. The work enables precision W-mass determination at LEP2 by controlling Bose-Einstein correlation systematics, and provides tools for searching for quark-gluon plasma formation through the time-reversed application of hydrodynamical solutions to infer initial conditions from measured spectra and correlations.
