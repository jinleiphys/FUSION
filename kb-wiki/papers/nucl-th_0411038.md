# Nuclear pairing from chiral pion-nucleon dynamics

> We use a recently improved version of the chiral nucleon-nucleon potential at next-to-next-to-leading order to calculate the $^1S_0$ pairing gap in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter. The pairing potential consists of the long-range one- and two-pion exchange terms and two short-distance NN-contact couplings. We find that the inclusion of the two-pion exchange at next-to-next-to-leading order reduces substantially the cut-off dependence of the $^1S_0$ pairing gap determined by solving a regularised BCS equation. Our results are close to those obtained with the universal low-momentum nucleon-nucleon potential $V_{\rm low-k}$ or the phenomenological Gogny D1S force.

## Key claim

The inclusion of two-pion exchange at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in the chiral nucleon-nucleon potential substantially reduces the cut-off dependence of the \(^1S_0\) pairing gap in symmetric nuclear matter, indicating good convergence of the chiral expansion; however, this result is obtained in the BCS approximation without including medium polarization effects, which the authors note can strongly suppress pairing in neutron matter but largely cancel in symmetric nuclear matter.

## Method

The authors solved the regularized BCS gap equation for isospin-symmetric nuclear matter using a chiral nucleon-nucleon potential at NLO and NNLO, consisting of one-pion exchange, irreducible two-pion exchange (with spectral function regularization at \(\tilde\Lambda = 600\) MeV), and two short-distance NN-contact terms whose low-energy constants were fitted to \(^1S_0\) phase shifts. The pairing potential was regulated with an exponential cut-off function \(f^\Lambda(p) = \exp(-p^6/\Lambda^6)\), and the single-particle spectrum was approximated by a quadratic form with an effective nucleon mass \(M^*(k_f)\) from a three-loop chiral perturbation theory calculation including explicit \(\Delta(1232)\)-isobar degrees of freedom.

## Key numbers

- At NLO, increasing the cut-off \(\Lambda\) from 450 MeV to 600 MeV caused the maximum pairing gap to rise from \(\Delta_f^{max} = 0.7\) MeV to \(\Delta_f^{max} = 5.81\) MeV, and the Fermi momentum at maximum shifted from \(p_f^{max} = 0.65\) fm\(^{-1}\) to \(p_f^{max} = 0.95\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- At NNLO, the maximum pairing gap increased by less than 0.8 MeV when \(\Lambda\) was varied from 450 MeV to 650 MeV, and the position of the maximum changed very little.
- For \(\Lambda = 650\) MeV at NLO, the maximum gap exceeded 10 MeV and the calculation diverged.
- The spectral function cut-off was fixed at \(\tilde\Lambda = 600\) MeV throughout.
- The low-energy constants used were \(c_1 = -0.81\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(c_3 = -3.40\) GeV\(^{-1}\), and \(c_4 = 3.40\) GeV\(^{-1}\).
- The pion mass was \(m_\pi = 135\) MeV, the axial coupling \(g_A = 1.3\), and the pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 92.4\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the application of chiral effective field theory to nuclear pairing, following the development of the improved chiral NN potential by Epelbaum, Gloeckle, and Meissner (2003, 2004) which removed an overly strong medium-range attraction in earlier versions. The results are compared with those obtained using the universal low-momentum potential \(V_{\rm low-k}\) (from the Stony-Brook group) and the phenomenological Gogny D1S force, finding remarkable agreement at NNLO. The study enables future inclusion of NNNLO terms and extension to neutron matter pairing, where medium polarization effects (vertex corrections, self-energy corrections, Pauli blocking) must be incorporated.
