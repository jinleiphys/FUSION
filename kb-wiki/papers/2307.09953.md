# Bulk viscosity of rotating, hot and dense spin 1/2 fermionic systems from correlation functions

> In this work we have presented the one-loop calculation of the bulk viscosity of a system of rotating, hot and dense spin 1/2 fermions within the framework of Kubo formalism calculated from correlation functions of fields which in turn is used to calculate the spectral function of energy-momentum tensors. The calculation has been done in curved space by the help of tetrad formalism, where the the gamma matrices in this set-up assume their generic structure by becoming space dependent. The techniques of thermal field theory have been employed which take into account the three energy scales viz. temperature, chemical potential and angular velocity into account in the Matsubara frequency summation. The study has been performed in the ambience of very large angular velocities, ranging from 0.1 to 1.0 GeV. The fermion propagator used in this work is appropiate for the regime of large angular velocities. We explore the behaviour of bulk viscosity with angular velocity, temperature and chemical potential through our plots.

## Key claim

The paper presents a one-loop calculation of the bulk viscosity for a system of rotating, hot, and dense spin-1/2 fermions using the Kubo formalism, but the calculation is restricted to the regime of very large angular velocities (0.1 to 1.0 GeV) where translational invariance of the fermion propagator is recovered, and the system is treated as massless and non-interacting.

## Method

The author employs the Kubo formalism within thermal field theory (Imaginary Time Formalism) to compute the bulk viscosity from the spectral function of the energy-momentum tensor, using curved-space tetrad formalism to describe the rotating background. The fermion propagator in momentum space, derived via the Fock-Schwinger method for large angular velocities, is used to evaluate the one-loop photon polarization diagram, with Matsubara frequency summations accounting for temperature, chemical potential, and angular velocity. The bulk viscosity is obtained by taking the zero-momentum and zero-frequency limit of the spectral function, retaining only the Landau cuts (finite-temperature contributions) and discarding the unitary cuts.

## Key numbers

* The angular velocity range studied is 0.1 to 1.0 GeV, corresponding to approximately 10^22 sec^-1.
* The speed of sound is taken as c_s^2 = 1/3, appropriate for a system of massless fermions.
* The effective chemical potentials appearing in the Fermi-Dirac distributions are shifted by the angular velocity: μ̃ = μ - Ω/2 and μ̄ = μ + Ω/2.

## Context

This work extends the field-theoretical calculation of transport coefficients for rotating relativistic fluids, building on the Kubo formulas derived from the Nonequilibrium Statistical Operator formalism by Hu (2021) and the torsionful QCD approach of Hongo et al. (2021). The calculation is analogous to studies of fermion propagators in magnetic fields by Ayala et al. (2021), where translational invariance is recovered at large field strengths. The results provide input for hydrodynamic simulations of the quark-gluon plasma created in non-central heavy-ion collisions, where angular velocities are produced, and complement kinetic theory approaches such as those by Bhadury et al. (2020).
