# Does the neutrino magnetic moment have an impact on solar neutrino physics?

> Solar neutrino observations coupled with the recent KamLAND data suggest that spin-flavor precession scenario does not play a major role in neutrino propagation in the solar matter. We provide approximate analytical formulas and numerical results to estimate the contribution of the spin-flavor precession, if any, to the electron neutrino survival probability when the magnetic moment and magnetic field combination is small.

## Key claim

The spin-flavor precession scenario does not play a major role in solar neutrino propagation, but the paper provides approximate analytical formulas and numerical results to estimate its contribution to the electron neutrino survival probability when the magnetic moment and magnetic field combination is small.

## Method

The authors derived approximate analytical expressions for the electron neutrino survival probability in the presence of both MSW and spin-flavor precession resonances, using logarithmic perturbation theory in the small mixing angle (non-adiabatic) limit where the resonances are well separated. For the large mixing angle (adiabatic) case where resonances overlap, they performed numerical calculations by solving the full evolution equation for Majorana neutrinos, using a gaussian magnetic field profile and the solar electron density parameterization of Bahcall (1989).

## Key numbers

- For a 10 MeV neutrino with magnetic field B ~ 10^6 G and magnetic moment 10^-11 μ_B, the width of the spin-flavor resonance is δr/R_⊙ ~ 0.002
- For a 10^5 G magnetic field, magnetic moment 10^-12 μ_B, and E ~ 10 MeV, the hopping reduction factor is ~ 10^-3
- The spin-flavor precession resonance for Majorana neutrinos occurs at r/R_⊙ = 0 for E_ν = 2.50 MeV, at 0.05 for 3.35 MeV, at 0.10 for 5.00 MeV, at 0.15 for 8.00 MeV, and at 0.20 for 13.00 MeV (using δm^2 = 8.2 × 10^-5 eV^2 and tan^2 θ = 0.4)
- The MSW resonance for the same parameters occurs at r/R_⊙ = 0.07 for 2.50 MeV, 0.10 for 3.35 MeV, 0.13 for 5.00 MeV, 0.18 for 8.00 MeV, and 0.22 for 13.00 MeV
- Numerical results with B = 10^5 G and μ = 10^-11 μ_B are identical to MSW-only results at a level of at least 10^5

## Context

This work extends the spin-flavor precession formalism of Lim and Marciano (1988) and Akhmedov (1988) by providing analytical estimates for the small-moment regime, following earlier numerical calculations by Balantekin, Loreti, and Learned (1990) that had hinted at a LOW-region solution. The paper confirms that the KamLAND-confirmed oscillation solution dominates, and that future solar neutrino measurements could not easily reach the precision needed to detect alternative spin-flavor precession contributions. The results also enable estimates of magnetic field effects on neutrino fluxes from core-collapse supernovae.
