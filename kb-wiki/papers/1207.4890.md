# Phase diagrams in nonlocal PNJL models constrained by Lattice QCD results

> Based on lattice QCD-adjusted SU(2) nonlocal Polyakov--Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) models, we investigate how the location of the critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram depends on the strenght of the vector meson coupling, as well as the Polyakov-loop (PL) potential and the form factors of the covariant model. The latter are constrained by lattice QCD data for the quark propagator. The strength of the vector coupling is adjusted such as to reproduce the slope of the pseudocritical temperature for the chiral phase transition at low chemical potential extracted recently from lattice QCD simulations. Our study supports the existence of a critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram albeit the constraint for the vector coupling shifts its location to lower temperatures and higher baryochemical potentials than in the case without it.

## Key claim

The study supports the existence of a critical endpoint (CEP) in the QCD phase diagram, but the constraint from lattice QCD data for the curvature of the pseudocritical line shifts the CEP location to lower temperatures and higher baryochemical potentials than in the case without the vector coupling constraint.

## Method

The authors used SU(2) nonlocal Polyakov–Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) models with three different form-factor parameterizations (Set A without wave function renormalization, Sets B and C with wave function renormalization) and a local PNJL model for comparison. They solved the mean-field gap equations for the scalar fields, vector field, and Polyakov loop at finite temperature and chemical potential, then tuned the vector coupling strength \( \eta_V = G_V/G_S \) to reproduce the lattice QCD curvature \( \kappa = 0.059(2)(4) \) of the pseudocritical temperature at low chemical potential from Kaczmarek (2011).

## Key numbers

* Pseudocritical temperature at zero chemical potential: \( T_c(0) = 169.9 \) MeV (Set A), 171.3 MeV (Set B), 173.2 MeV (Set C), and 200.9 MeV (local model), for the vector coupling values that best fit the lattice curvature.
* Lattice QCD curvature of the pseudocritical line: \( \kappa = 0.059(2)(4) \) from Kaczmarek (2011), used to constrain the vector coupling strength.
* CEP location for Set B (with wave function renormalization): \( (T_{\rm CEP}, \mu_{\rm CEP}) = (129.8\ {\rm MeV}, 276.6\ {\rm MeV}) \).
* CEP location for Set C (with wave function renormalization): \( (T_{\rm CEP}, \mu_{\rm CEP}) = (69.9\ {\rm MeV}, 319.1\ {\rm MeV}) \).
* Lattice QCD pseudocritical temperature for two flavors: \( T_c(0) \sim 170 \) MeV from Ejiri (2000).
* Deconfinement critical temperature used: \( T_0 = 208 \) MeV for two flavors, following Schaefer (2007).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the QCD phase diagram using PNJL models by incorporating nonlocal form factors constrained by lattice QCD data for the quark propagator from Parappilly (2005) and Kamleh (2007). It contrasts with the local PNJL model, where increasing the vector coupling can eliminate the CEP entirely (as in Bratovic (2012)), whereas all nonlocal models retain a CEP. The results provide a constrained region for the CEP location between the predictions of Sets B and C, which is relevant for the beam energy scan programs at RHIC, SPS, NICA, and FAIR.
