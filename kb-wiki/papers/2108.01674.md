# Extending Precision Perturbative QCD with Track Functions

> Collider experiments often exploit information about the quantum numbers of final state hadrons to maximize their sensitivity, with applications ranging from the use of tracking information (electric charge) for precision jet substructure measurements, to flavor tagging for nucleon structure studies. For such measurements, perturbative calculations in terms of quarks and gluons are insufficient, and non-perturbative track functions describing the energy fraction of a quark or gluon converted into a subset of hadrons (e.g., charged hadrons) must be incorporated. Unlike fragmentation functions, track functions describe correlations between hadrons and therefore satisfy complicated non-linear evolution equations whose structure has so far eluded calculation beyond the leading order. In this Letter, we develop an understanding of track functions and their interplay with energy flow observables beyond the leading order, allowing them to be used in state-of-the-art perturbative calculations for the first time. We identify a shift symmetry in the evolution of their moments that fixes their structure, and we explicitly compute the evolution of the first three moments at next-to-leading order, allowing for the description of up to three-point energy correlations. We then calculate the two-point energy correlator on charged particles at $O(α_s^2)$, illustrating explicitly that infrared singularities in perturbation theory are absorbed by moments of the track functions and also highlighting how these moments seamlessly interplay with modern techniques for perturbative calculations. Our results extend the boundaries of traditional perturbative QCD, enabling precision perturbative predictions for energy flow observables sensitive to the quantum numbers of hadronic states.

## Key claim
The authors show that nonperturbative track functions can be extended beyond leading order in perturbative QCD, enabling precision calculations of energy flow observables on subsets of hadrons (e.g., charged hadrons) for the first time. The key hedge is that this works because the N-point energy correlators only require the first N moments of the track functions, not their full functional form, and a newly identified shift symmetry in the moment evolution fixes the structure of the otherwise complicated nonlinear equations.

## Method
The authors computed the renormalization group equations (RGEs) for the first three moments of track functions at next-to-leading order by integrating collinear splitting functions to obtain a jet function differential in invariant mass, then extracting the track function evolution from the remaining infrared poles after renormalization. They then performed a complete analytic calculation of the two-point energy correlator on charged particles at O(α_s^2) using reverse unitarity to express phase space integrals as multiloop integrals, reduced to master integrals with LiteRed and fire6, and evaluated via differential equations in canonical form.

## Key numbers
- First three moments of track functions computed at next-to-leading order (NLO), allowing description of up to three-point energy correlations
- Two-point energy correlator calculated at O(α_s^2), matching the highest order available analytically for the two-point energy correlator on all partons
- Mixing anomalous dimension for second moment at NLO: γ_Δ^2^g = (4/3)a_s n_f T_F + a_s^2 n_f T_F [C_A(-16π^2/9 + 5897/675) + 39161 C_F/2700], where a_s = α_s/(4π)
- Mixing anomalous dimension for second moment at NLO: γ_Δ^2^q = (7/6)a_s C_F + a_s^2 C_F[817 C_A/108 + (1369/432 - 14π^2/9)C_F]
- Leading-order evolution of the second moment found consistent with dihadron fragmentation function from Konishi (1979) and Sukhatme (1980)
- NLO results for the energy-energy correlator asymmetry (AEEC) on charged particles show agreement with DELPHI data (1996), except in the cos(χ) → -1 region where resummation is required

## Context
This work extends the track function formalism originally introduced by Chang (2013) beyond leading logarithmic order, enabling precision perturbative predictions for energy flow observables sensitive to hadron quantum numbers. The results enable next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic resummation of the energy-energy correlator on tracks in the collinear limit using the factorization formula of Dixon (2019), with applications to jet substructure measurements at the LHC, heavy-ion collisions at ALICE, and nucleon structure studies at the Electron-Ion Collider. The approach contrasts with fragmentation functions (Georgi 1977, Ellis 1978, Curci 1980, Collins 1981) which describe single-hadron energy distributions, while track functions describe correlations between hadrons and satisfy nonlinear evolution equations.
