# Microscopic foundations of nuclear supersymmetry

> We discuss a microscopic framework for phenomenological boson-fermion models of nuclear structure based on the U($n/m$) type of superalgebras. The generalized Dyson mapping of fermion collective superalgebras provides a basis to do so and to understand how collectivity selects the required preservation of boson plus fermion number as a good quantum number. We also consider the difference between dynamical and invariant supersymmetries based on possible supermultiplets of spectra of neighboring odd and even nuclei. We point out that different criteria exist for choosing the appropriate single particle transfer operators in the two cases and discuss a microscopically based method to construct these operators in the case of dynamical supersymmetry.

## Key claim

The paper argues that dynamical supersymmetry in nuclear structure can have a microscopic foundation in fermion systems without violating the Pauli principle, but only when the original fermion Hamiltonian is expressible in terms of operators belonging to a collective algebra; this condition is distinct from invariant supersymmetry, for which far less experimental evidence exists.

## Method

The authors apply a generalized Dyson boson-fermion mapping, derived from supercoherent states and a similarity transformation, to map fermion collective superalgebras onto boson-fermion spaces. They illustrate the method using the SU(2) seniority model in a single-\(j\) shell, where the pairing Hamiltonian \(H = -G S^\dagger S\) is mapped to a boson-fermion Hamiltonian, and they analyze the resulting operator images for single-fermion transfer. The mapping preserves anticommutation relations exactly on the full ideal space, ensuring Pauli principle compatibility.

## Key numbers

* In the SU(2) seniority model, the pairing Hamiltonian spectrum is \(E(n,v) = -\frac{1}{4} G (n-v)(2\Omega - n - v + 2)\), where \(\Omega = j + 1/2\), \(n\) is the total fermion number, and \(v\) is the seniority quantum number.
* The mapped boson-fermion Hamiltonian is \(H_{\rm BF} = -G N_{\rm B} (\Omega - N_{\rm B} + 1 - N_{\rm F})\), with \(N_{\rm B}\) the number of bosons and \(N_{\rm F}\) the number of ideal fermions, yielding the same spectrum as the original.
* The physical subspace of the ideal boson-fermion space satisfies \(N_{\rm F} \leq \Omega\) and \(N_{\rm B} \leq \Omega - N_{\rm F}/2\).
* The total number of ideal particles is \(\aleph = N_{\rm F} + N_{\rm B}\), and the Hamiltonian preserves \(\aleph\) as a good quantum number.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological interacting boson-fermion model (IBFM) of Iachello (1980) and the experimental quartet of nuclei found by Metz, Jolie et al. (1999) by providing a microscopic mapping that justifies dynamical supersymmetry from fermion dynamics. It contrasts with the invariant supersymmetry approach of Jolos and von Brentano, which would require degenerate spectra not observed in nuclear data. The results enable future calculations of single-particle transfer strengths in dynamical supersymmetry using the constructed boson-fermion images, and they clarify the distinction between supercharge-based transfer operators (relevant for invariant SUSY) and the more general microscopically derived operators.
