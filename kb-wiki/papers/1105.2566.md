# ${\cal O}(α_s^3)$ analysis of inclusive jet and di-jet production in heavy ion reactions at the Large Hadron Collider

> Jets physics in heavy ion reactions is an important new area of active research at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) that paves the way for novel tests of QCD multi-parton dynamics in dense nuclear matter. At present, perturbative QCD calculations of hard probes in elementary nucleon-nucleon reactions can be consistently combined with the effects of the nuclear medium up to $ {\cal O}(α_s^3) $. While such accuracy is desirable but not necessary for leading particle tomography, it is absolutely essential for the new jet observables. With this motivation, we present first results and predictions to $ {\cal O}(α_s^3) $ for the recent LHC lead-lead (Pb+Pb) run at a center-of-mass energy of 2.76 TeV per nucleon-nucleon pair. Specifically, we focus on the suppression of the single and double inclusive jet cross sections. Our analysis includes not only final-state inelastic parton interactions in the QGP, but also initial-state cold nuclear matter effects and an estimate of the non-perturbative hadronization corrections. We demonstrate how an enhanced di-jet asymmetry in central Pb+Pb reactions at the LHC, recently measured by the ATLAS and CMS experiments, can be derived from these results. We show quantitatively that a fraction of this enhancement may be related to the ambiguity in the separation between the jet and the soft background medium and/or the diffusion of the parton shower energy away from the jet axis through collisional processes. We point to a suite of measurements that can help build a consistent picture of parton shower modification in heavy ion collisions at the LHC.

## Key claim

The authors present the first next-to-leading order (NLO) perturbative QCD results at ${\cal O}(\alpha_s^3)$ for inclusive jet and di-jet production in heavy ion collisions, showing that a fraction of the enhanced di-jet asymmetry observed by ATLAS and CMS may arise from the ambiguity in separating the jet from the soft background medium and/or the diffusion of parton shower energy away from the jet axis through collisional processes, rather than solely from jet quenching in the quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The authors combine ${\cal O}(\alpha_s^3)$ calculations for jet production in nucleon-nucleon reactions (validated against $\sqrt{s}=7$ TeV p+p data) with parton energy loss effects in the hot QCD medium at ${\cal O}(\alpha_s^2 \alpha_s^{\rm rad})$, using the Ellis-Kunszt-Soper (EKS) numerical implementation and CTEQ6.1M parton distribution functions. Medium-induced radiative corrections are computed using the Reaction operator approach, with the QGP density estimated from ALICE measurements of charged particle multiplicity, and the probability distribution for fractional energy loss is evaluated in the Poisson approximation for multiple gluon emissions.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy for Pb+Pb collisions: $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV per nucleon-nucleon pair
- Gluon rapidity density in central Pb+Pb collisions: $dN^g/dy = 2650$, estimated from ALICE $dN^{ch}/d\eta$ measurement
- Inclusive jet cross section in p+p at $\sqrt{s}=7$ TeV is described within 10% for $E_T$ between 50 GeV and 500 GeV, where the yield falls by more than 5 orders of magnitude
- Variation of factorization/renormalization scale $\mu_f=\mu_R = E_T$ in the interval $(E_T/2, 2E_T)$ has less than 10% effect on inclusive jet cross section and less than 2% effect on the $A_J$ distribution
- For a 100 GeV gluon jet with $p_T^{\min}=20$ GeV, collisional energy loss $\langle \delta E_T \rangle \simeq 15$ GeV for $R=0.5$ and $\langle \delta E_T \rangle \simeq 5$ GeV for $R=0.2$
- Coupling strength between jet and medium varied as $g_{\rm med}=1.8, 2.0, 2.2$ in the calculations

## Context

This work extends the perturbative QCD framework for jet production in heavy ion collisions from leading order to ${\cal O}(\alpha_s^3)$, which is essential for quantitative comparison to LHC jet data. The authors build upon the Reaction operator approach for medium-induced gluon radiation (Vitev 2007) and the EKS numerical implementation for NLO jet cross sections (Ellis, Kunszt, Soper). The results enable systematic study of how jet quenching, collisional energy loss, and jet/background separation ambiguities contribute to the di-jet asymmetry enhancement reported by ATLAS (Aad 2010) and CMS (Chatrchyan 2011), and point toward measurements that can differentiate between these mechanisms.
