# Two-proton radioactivity and three-body decay. IV. Connection to quasiclassical formulation

> We derive quasiclassical expressions for the three-body decay width and define the ``preexponential'' coefficients for them. The derivation is based on the integral formulae for the three-body width obtained in the semianalytical approach with simplified three-body Hamiltonian [L.V. Grigorenko and M.V.\ Zhukov, arXiv:0704.0920v1]. The model is applied to the decays of the first excited $3/2^{-}$ state of $^{17}$Ne and $3/2^{-}$ ground state of $^{45}$Fe. Various qualitative aspects of the model and relations with the other simplified approaches to the three-body decays are discussed.

## Key claim
The paper derives quasiclassical expressions for three-body decay widths with explicit "preexponential" coefficients, but the derivation relies on the assumption that narrow resonant states exist in both subsystems and that the auxiliary resonance energy is sufficiently close to the physical resonance energy; the resulting formulas are accurate only within a factor of 2 for states broader than 10⁻² MeV.

## Method
The authors start from integral formulae for the three-body decay width obtained in a semianalytical approach with a simplified three-body Hamiltonian that neglects proton-proton final state interaction and uses a simplified Coulomb interaction. They substitute the continuum wavefunctions of the subsystems with Breit-Wigner resonant forms, factorize the width expression into a "preexponent" (with dimension of energy) and a dimensionless "exponential" part involving penetrability factors, and apply the resulting quasiclassical formula to the decays of the first excited 3/2⁻ state of ¹⁷Ne and the 3/2⁻ ground state of ⁴⁵Fe.

## Key numbers
- For ¹⁷Ne (first excited 3/2⁻ state): the ratio ⟨V₃²⟩/⟨V₃⟩² is "reasonably consistent with the D₃ values and are quite close to unity," indicating wavefunctions are predominantly localized in internal regions.
- For ⁴⁵Fe (3/2⁻ ground state): the three-body width dependence on the two-body resonance position follows Γ(E₃ᵣ) ∼ (Eₓₐ − E₃ᵣ/2)⁻², observed in calculations within the simplified three-body model.
- The disagreement between the "correct" width Γ from Eq. (rmat-wid-dms-3s) and the "special case" Γ_b from Eq. (wid-3-bark) is as large as a factor 2–4 for the systems under consideration; for systems with Eₓₐ ≡ E_ya (ground state decays), the difference should be about a factor 4.
- Sensitivity to the choice of Coulomb treatment is relatively high: factor 1.5–5 depending on the particular model.

## Context
This work extends the semianalytical three-body model developed by Grigorenko and Zhukov (2007) by providing quasiclassical expressions that connect to earlier pioneering work by Goldansky (1960) and Galitsky and Cheltsov (1964). The derived formulas improve upon the quasiclassical model used in previous works by Grigorenko et al. (2001–2006) and the "R-matrix approach" of Kryger et al. (1995) and Barker and Brown (1999–2003), which lacked proper derivation and analysis of approximations. The results enable more reliable estimates of two-proton decay widths for astrophysical applications, particularly for the reverse two-proton radiative capture process, and provide a framework for calibrating full three-body calculations.
