# Lattice worldline representation of correlators in a background field

> We use a discrete worldline representation in order to study the continuum limit of the one-loop expectation value of dimension two and four local operators in a background field. We illustrate this technique in the case of a scalar field coupled to a non-Abelian background gauge field. The first two coefficients of the expansion in powers of the lattice spacing can be expressed as sums over random walks on a d-dimensional cubic lattice. Using combinatorial identities for the distribution of the areas of closed random walks on a lattice, these coefficients can be turned into simple integrals. Our results are valid for an anisotropic lattice, with arbitrary lattice spacings in each direction.

## Key claim

The paper uses a discrete worldline formalism to derive the continuum limit of one-loop expectation values of local operators in a background field on a lattice, showing that the first two coefficients of the expansion in lattice spacing can be expressed as sums over random walks and then turned into simple integrals; the results are valid for anisotropic lattices with arbitrary lattice spacings in each direction, but the derivation assumes a scalar field coupled to a non-Abelian background gauge field with no self-interactions.

## Method

The authors start from the one-loop expectation value of the operator \(\langle\phi_a^*(0)\phi_a(0)\rangle\) in a background gauge field, expressed as \(\langle 0|1/D^2|0\rangle\) on a discrete cubic lattice. They use a discrete heat kernel expansion \((2d)/(a^2 D^2) = \sum_{n=0}^\infty (1 - a^2 D^2/2d)^n\), which maps the problem onto sums over closed random walks of length \(2n\) on the lattice, weighted by Wilson loops \({\cal W}(\gamma)\) for the background field. In the continuum limit \(a\to 0\), the Wilson loops are expanded to second order in the field strength, yielding coefficients \({\bs C}_0\) and \({\bs C}_4^{\mu\nu;\rho\sigma}\) that are sums over closed random walks of the identity and of products of projected areas, respectively; these sums are then converted into integral representations using Borel transformations and combinatorial identities for the distribution of areas of closed random walks.

## Key numbers

* The zeroth-order coefficient in 3 dimensions: \({\bs C}_0 = (\sqrt{3}-1)/(32\pi^3)\,\Gamma^2(1/24)\,\Gamma^2(11/24) \approx 1.51638606\).
* The zeroth-order coefficient in 4 dimensions: \({\bs C}_0 \approx 1.23946712\) (numerical evaluation).
* In 2 dimensions, the variance of the algebraic area enclosed by closed random walks of length \(2n\) is \(\left[\sum_{\gamma\in\Gamma_{2n}(0,0)} (A_{12}(\gamma))^2\right] = \binom{2n}{n}^2 \frac{n^2(n-1)}{6(2n-1)}\).
* For 3 dimensions, the contribution to \({\bs C}_4^{12;12}\) from paths of length \(2l=20\) is 0.0249038527, and for \(2l=2000\) it is 0.2729940025 (from eq. (C4-3d)).

## Context

This work extends the worldline formalism, historically developed from string theory ideas (Bern and Kosower, 1991) and later given a field-theoretic understanding by Strassler (1992), to lattice field theory calculations in a background gauge field. It provides a gauge-invariant alternative to standard lattice perturbation theory (reviewed by Capitani, 2003), which is complicated by the need to combine non-gauge-invariant pieces. The results enable the study of cutoff effects in the classical statistical approximation (CSA) used in heavy ion collisions (Berges et al., 2008; Epelbaum and Gelis, 2011) and other real-time simulations, where anisotropic lattices are common; the combinatorial formulas for anisotropic lattices (generalizing those of Mingo and Nica, 1998) are new and are proved in a separate paper (Epelbaum, Gelis, and Wu, 2015).
