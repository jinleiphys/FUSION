# Variational Monte Carlo for Microscopic Cluster Models

> We examine the application of the Variational Monte Carlo (VMC) method to a cluster model for halo nuclei. Particular attention is paid to the error estimate in the presence of correlations in the underlying random walk. We analyse the required steps for a reliable application of the VMC in the case of a complicated many-body problem such as the direct solution of the nuclear hamiltonian with realistic interactions. We also examine the possibility of variance reduction through the `zero variance principle', paying particular attention to the complexity of the many-body problem.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that Variational Monte Carlo (VMC) can be applied to microscopic cluster models for halo nuclei, but only if careful attention is paid to error estimation in the presence of correlated random walks; the authors show that the covariance matrix approach can provide reliable error estimates, though the method's applicability is limited by the complexity of the many-body problem and the presence of correlations in the Metropolis sampling.

## Method

The authors construct trial wavefunctions for halo nuclei using a coupled-cluster-inspired approach, combining linear terms (determined from the eigenvalue problem) with non-linear Jastrow-type correlation operators that depend only on inter-particle distances to preserve translational invariance. They evaluate Hamiltonian and overlap matrix elements using the Metropolis algorithm for Monte Carlo integration, sampling all matrix elements from a single probability distribution. The error analysis employs auto-correlation and cross-correlation coefficients to estimate the variance and covariance of matrix elements, then diagonalizes the covariance matrix to obtain a set of uncorrelated observables whose errors can be added in quadrature.

## Key numbers

- The covariance matrix has dimensions \((2n^2) \times (2n^2)\), where \(n \times n\) is the dimension of the Hamiltonian and overlap matrices.
- The variance of the mean for uncorrelated samples is given by \(\sigma^2 = \frac{1}{N}\sigma_0^2\), where \(\sigma_0^2 = \langle O^2\rangle - \langle O\rangle^2\).
- For correlated samples, the variance of the mean becomes \(\sigma^2(\bar{O}) = \frac{1}{N}[\sigma_0 + 2\sum_{t=1}^{N-1}(1-\frac{t}{N})\rho_t]\), where \(\rho_t\) is the auto-correlation at lag \(t\).
- The error for the new uncorrelated observables is \((\Delta Q_k)^2 = \frac{\lambda_k}{N-1}\), where \(\lambda_k\) are the eigenvalues of the covariance matrix and \(N\) is the number of samples.

## Context

This work extends the application of VMC methods, previously developed for general many-body problems by Kalos and collaborators, to the specific case of light halo nuclei modeled as alpha-particle clusters with weakly-bound valence nucleons. The authors build on the Coupled Cluster Method (CCM) framework of Kummel and Bishop for constructing correlated wavefunctions, and on Jastrow-type correlation operators for short-range nuclear forces. The paper enables future applications of VMC to halo nuclei by providing a systematic framework for error estimation that accounts for correlations in the Metropolis random walk, which is essential before attempting more sophisticated methods like Diffusion Monte Carlo (DMC) or Green's function Monte Carlo (GMC) that require VMC wavefunctions as starting points.
