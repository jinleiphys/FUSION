# Efficient evaluation of AGP reduced density matrices

> We propose and implement an algorithm to calculate the norm and reduced density matrices of the antisymmetrized geminal power (AGP) of any rank with polynomial cost. Our method scales quadratically per element of the reduced density matrices. Numerical tests indicate that our method is very fast and capable of treating systems with a few thousand orbitals and hundreds of electrons reliably in double-precision. In addition, we present reconstruction formulae that allows one to decompose higher order reduced density matrices in terms of linear combinations of lower order ones, thereby reducing the computational cost significantly.

## Key claim

The paper presents an algorithm for computing AGP reduced density matrices (RDMs) that scales quadratically per matrix element, but the practical cost for constructing full \(n\)-pair RDMs remains combinatorial in the number of orbitals \(M\) (scaling as \(\mathcal{O}(M^{2n+1})\)) unless the reconstruction formulae are used to decompose higher-rank RDMs into lower-rank ones.

## Method

The authors formulate all AGP RDMs in terms of elementary symmetric polynomials (ESPs) and compute them using the sumESP algorithm, which exploits the recurrence \(S_N^M = S_N^{M-1} + \eta_p^2 S_{N-1}^{M-1}\) to achieve polynomial cost. They then derive exact reconstruction formulae that express any \(n\)-pair RDM as a linear combination of number RDMs \(\nu^{(n')}\) and geminal coefficients \(\eta_p\), and further decompose each number RDM into a sum of 1-body number RDMs \(\nu^{(1)}\) using factors of the form \(1/(\eta_p^2 - \eta_q^2)\).

## Key numbers

* The asymptotic cost of computing the norm of AGP (the overlap) scales as \(T[s] \propto M^{1.97}\) at half-filling (\(N = M/2\)) for \(M\) from \(10^2\) to \(10^3\), consistent with the theoretical cost \(\mathcal{O}(N(M-N))\).
* The runtime for constructing the 1-pair RDM scales as \(T[s] \propto M^{3.4}\), the 2-pair RDM as \(T[s] \propto M^{5.7}\), and the 3-pair RDM as \(T[s] \propto M^{7.6}\), for \(M \leq 100\) with \(\eta_p \sim \text{Unif}(0,1)\).
* The worst-case relative error bound for the sumESP algorithm is \(\frac{2(M-1)\epsilon}{1 - 2(M-1)\epsilon}\), where \(\epsilon\) is machine epsilon, assuming no overflow or underflow.

## Context

This work extends prior analytic expressions for AGP norms and RDMs from Dietrich (1964), Coleman (1965), and Cioslowski (2000) by providing a practical polynomial-cost algorithm. It enables AGP-based correlated theories such as AGP configuration interaction (AGP-CI) from Henderson (2019), which previously required up to 5-body density matrices. The reconstruction formulae are analogous to Hartree-Fock theory where all high-rank RDMs are expressible in terms of 1-body RDMs, but here the decomposition is exact and does not rely on cumulant expansions.
