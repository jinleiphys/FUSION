# TWO-PION EXCHANGE NUCLEAR POTENTIAL - CHIRAL CANCELLATIONS

> We show that chiral symmetry is responsible for large cancellations in the two-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon interaction, which are similar to those occuring in free pion-nucleon scattering.

## Key claim

Chiral symmetry produces large cancellations among the individual two-pion exchange contributions to the nucleon-nucleon potential, analogous to those in free pion-nucleon scattering, which prevent a perturbative explosion of the amplitude. The cancellations are demonstrated for partial waves with high orbital angular momentum to avoid undetermined short-range counterterms that affect only the origin.

## Method

The authors decomposed the full NN potential into one-pion exchange (OPEP), pseudoscalar (box and crossed box diagrams), chiral (triangle and bubble diagrams), and core contributions. Using the variable phase method, they computed perturbative phase shifts for the \(^1D_2\), \(^1F_3\), \(^1G_4\), and \(^1H_5\) waves, isolating the contributions from each potential component to reveal the cancellations.

## Key numbers

- The scale of the profile functions in Figure 1, showing the cancellation between triangle and bubble contributions, is given in thousands of MeV.
- For the \(^1F_3\) wave, the cancellation between the iterated OPEP, pseudoscalar, and chiral contributions is almost complete, leaving the total phase shift very close to that due to the long-OPEP term alone.
- The same near-complete cancellation pattern also holds for the \(^1G_4\) and \(^1H_5\) waves.

## Context

This work extends the chiral symmetry approach to the two-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon potential, building on the earlier coordinate-space potential of da Rocha and Robilotta (1994) and the variable phase method of Ballot and Robilotta (1994). The cancellations mirror those found in threshold pion-nucleon scattering and pion-deuteron scattering by Robilotta and Wilkin (1978). The results enable a deeper understanding of how chiral symmetry tames medium-range contributions in the NN interaction, though the authors note that a full chiral calculation in momentum space requires undetermined counterterms involving higher orders of momenta, as discussed by Ordonez and Van Kolk (1992).
