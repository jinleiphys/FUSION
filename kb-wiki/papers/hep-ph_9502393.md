# Chiral Perturbation Theory

> A short overview of the current state of Chiral Perturbation Theory is given. This includes a description of the basic assumptions, the usefulness of the external field method is emphasized using a simple lowest order example. Then at next-to-leading order the determination of the parameters is discussed. We also present the status of calculations at ${\cal O}(p^6)$. Finally I present the extension into 3 directions: estimates of the free parameters, inclusion of nonleptonic weak and electromagnetic interactions, and inclusion of non-Goldstone fields in the chiral Lagrangian. Invited Talk at the International Workshop on Nuclear \& Particle Physics ``Chiral Dynamics in Hadrons \& Nuclei,'' Feb 6 $\sim$ Feb 10, 1995, Seoul, Korea.

## Key claim

Chiral Perturbation Theory (CHPT) is a systematic method to use a spontaneously broken symmetry, applicable whenever there is a symmetry spontaneously broken, and its main underlying assumption is Weinberg's theorem that the most general solution of causality, unitarity, and symmetry in quantum field theory is given by the most general symmetric Lagrangian. The paper presents an overview of the current state of CHPT, emphasizing that while it is a mature field for processes with mesons only, it is in its adolescent stage for calculations involving one nucleon or one baryon, and the many-nucleon sector is in its infancy.

## Method

The paper reviews the construction of the chiral Lagrangian using the external field method, starting from the lowest order (p^2) Lagrangian with two free parameters (F and B_0) and proceeding to next-to-leading order (p^4) where 10 measurable parameters (L_i) are determined from experimental data. The method uses an expansion in derivatives (momenta) rather than a coupling constant, with loop diagrams systematically renormalized by absorbing infinities into the parameters of the higher-order Lagrangian. The review also covers the status of calculations at order p^6, estimates of parameters from resonance exchange and the ENJL model, and extensions to include nonleptonic weak and electromagnetic interactions via spurion fields, as well as inclusion of non-Goldstone fields like vector mesons and nucleons.

## Context

This review extends the foundational work of Gasser and Leutwyler (GL1, GL2) who established CHPT as a systematic low-energy effective theory of QCD. It discusses the current status of calculations at order p^6, which are technically demanding and still contain many free parameters, making it necessary to estimate coefficients from other sources such as resonance exchange (Ecker1, Ecker2, Donoghue4) and the ENJL model (Bijnens6). The paper enables future work by outlining the challenges in including non-Goldstone fields (vector mesons and nucleons) and nonleptonic weak and electromagnetic interactions, where the number of parameters at next-to-leading order becomes very large (e.g., 48 parameters for octet weak transitions, as classified by Kambor et al.).
