# Monte Carlo Study of Strongly-Interacting Degenerate Fermions: a Model for Voltage-Biased Bilayer Graphene

> We formulate a model of N_f=4 flavors of relativistic fermion in 2+1d in the presence of a chemical potential mu coupled to two flavor doublets with opposite sign, akin to isopsin chemical potential in QCD. This is argued to be an effective theory for low energy electronic excitations in bilayer graphene, in which an applied voltage between the layers ensures equal populations of particles on one layer and holes on the other. The model is then reformulated on a spacetime lattice using staggered fermions, and in the absence of a sign problem, simulated using an orthodox hybrid Monte Carlo algorithm. With the coupling strength chosen to be close to a quantum critical point believed to exist for N_f<N_fc\approx4.8, it is found that there is a region below saturation where both the carrier density and a particle-hole "excitonic" condensate scale anomalously with increasing mu, much more rapidly that the corresponding quantities in free field theory, while the conventional chiral condensate is strongly suppressed. The corresponding ground state is speculated to be a strongly-correlated degenerate fermion system, with a remnant Fermi surface distorted by a superfluid excitonic condensate. The model thus shows qualitatively different behaviour to any model with mu=/=0 previously studied by lattice simulation.

## Key claim

The authors claim that a lattice model of strongly-interacting relativistic fermions in 2+1 dimensions with an isospin chemical potential, intended as an effective theory for voltage-biased bilayer graphene, exhibits excitonic condensation and anomalously rapid scaling of both carrier density and exciton condensate with chemical potential when the coupling is chosen close to a quantum critical point believed to exist for flavor number \(N_f < N_{fc} \approx 4.8\). The hedge is that the model uses a simplified contact interaction rather than a realistic long-range Coulomb interaction, and that the coupling strength is chosen empirically to lie near the critical point rather than being precisely determined.

## Method

The authors formulate a lattice model with \(N_f = 4\) flavors of staggered fermions in 2+1 dimensions, coupled to a non-compact auxiliary field \(A_x\) that mediates a contact interaction between fermion densities. An isospin chemical potential \(\mu\) is introduced with opposite sign for two flavor doublets, analogous to isospin chemical potential in QCD, ensuring a real and positive Euclidean action. The model is simulated using a hybrid Monte Carlo algorithm on \(32^3\) and \(48^3\) lattices, with the coupling strength set to \(g^{-2} = 0.4\) to place the system near the quantum critical point. The chiral condensate, exciton condensate, and carrier density are measured as functions of \(\mu\), with extrapolations to zero bare mass \(m\) and zero interlayer source \(j\).

## Key numbers

* The critical flavor number for the quantum critical point in the strong-coupling limit is \(N_{fc} = 4.8(2)\), as obtained in prior work by Hands (2008).
* The peak of the chiral condensate as a function of inverse coupling occurs at \(g^{-2}_{\text{peak}} \approx 0.30\) for \(N_f = 4\), with the chiral limit extrapolation giving \(g^{-2}_{\text{peak}} \in (0.275, 0.35)\).
* The exciton condensate \(\langle \Psi\Psi(j=0) \rangle\) as a function of \(\mu\) on a \(48^3\) lattice fits a power law with exponent \(a_2 = 2.39(2)\) and prefactor \(a_1 = 7.0(2)\) for \(\mu a = 0.05\) to \(0.20\).
* The carrier density \(n_c(j=0)\) as a function of \(\mu\) on a \(48^3\) lattice fits a power law with exponent \(b_2 = 3.32(1)\) and prefactor \(b_1 = 18.6(4)\) for \(\mu a = 0.05\) to \(0.20\).
* Saturation of the carrier density sets in at \(\mu a \approx 0.5\) for the interacting model, compared to \(\mu a \approx 1.3\) for free fields.

## Context

This work extends lattice studies of quantum critical points in graphene-inspired models, specifically the investigation of chiral symmetry breaking in undoped monolayer graphene by Hands (2008) and the determination of the critical coupling for \(N_f = 2\) by Drut and Lahde (2009). It also addresses the theoretical prediction of excitonic condensation in biased bilayer graphene by Kharitonov (2012), which found a very small gap \(\Delta/\mu \sim O(10^{-7})\) using a self-consistent treatment. The results enable future studies of strongly-interacting degenerate fermion systems with a Fermi surface, and suggest that the vicinity of a quantum critical point can dramatically enhance both carrier density and excitonic pairing beyond weak-coupling expectations.
