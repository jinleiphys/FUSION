# Statistical hadronization of charm in heavy-ion collisions at SPS, RHIC and LHC

> We study the production of charmonia and charmed hadrons in nucleus-nucleus collisions at SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies within the framework of the statistical hadronization model. Results from this model are compared to the measured yields and centrality dependence of J/psi production at SPS and RHIC energies. We explore and contrast the centrality dependence of the production of mesons with open and hidden charm at SPS, RHIC, and LHC. The sensitivity of the results to various input parameters is analyzed in detail for RHIC energy.

## Key claim

The statistical hadronization model can describe the measured centrality dependence of J/ψ production at SPS and RHIC energies, but only if the charm production cross section is enhanced by a factor of 2.8 compared to next-to-leading order perturbative QCD predictions.

## Method

The model assumes all charm quarks are produced in primary hard collisions and thermalize in the quark-gluon plasma, with no J/ψ mesons preformed in the QGP due to complete color screening. Open and hidden charm hadrons are formed at chemical freeze-out according to statistical laws, with a charm balance equation using canonical thermodynamics to define a fugacity parameter g_c that accounts for deviations from complete chemical equilibrium. The yields are calculated using a constant freeze-out temperature of 170 MeV, baryonic chemical potential from a parametrization, fireball volumes derived from charged particle rapidity densities, and charm pair yields from NLO pQCD calculations scaled by the nuclear overlap function.

## Key numbers

- For central Pb-Pb collisions at SPS (√s_NN = 17.3 GeV): dN_ch/dy = 430, dN_c̄c^dir/dy = 0.064, V_Δy=1 = 861 fm³, g_c = 1.86
- For central Au-Au collisions at RHIC (√s_NN = 200 GeV): dN_ch/dy = 730, dN_c̄c^dir/dy = 1.92, V_Δy=1 = 1663 fm³, g_c = 8.33
- For central Pb-Pb collisions at LHC (√s_NN = 5500 GeV): dN_ch/dy = 2000, dN_c̄c^dir/dy = 16.8, V_Δy=1 = 4564 fm³, g_c = 23.2
- J/ψ rapidity density at SPS: dN_J/ψ/dy = 2.55 × 10⁻⁴; at RHIC: 0.011; at LHC: 0.226
- D⁺ rapidity density at SPS: 0.010; at RHIC: 0.404; at LHC: 3.56
- To explain SPS J/ψ data, the NLO charm cross section must be scaled up by a factor of 2.8
- At RHIC, a 20% variation in dN_c̄c^dir/dy produces about a ±30% change in J/ψ yield

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadronization model introduced by Braun-Munzinger and Stachel (2000) and Braun-Munzinger, Redlich, and Stachel (2001) to predict open and hidden charm production at RHIC and LHC energies, contrasting with the Matsui-Satz approach (1986) which predicts strong J/ψ suppression at collider energies. The model predictions are compared to NA50 data at SPS and PHENIX data at RHIC, and the results enable tests of whether charm quarks travel over significant distances in a deconfined phase, which would be a clear signal for quark-gluon plasma formation.
