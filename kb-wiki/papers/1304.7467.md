# Heavy quark spin selection rules in meson-antimeson states

> In the heavy quark limit, we discuss the spin of the charm-anticharm pair $J_{c\bar{c}}$ in an S-wave meson-antimeson molecule or resonance. One finds two cases that $J_{c\bar{c}}$ cannot be 0: (a) $J=|j_2-j_4|-1$ or $J=j_2+j_4+1$ where $J$ is the total spin of the system and $j_2$ ($j_4$) is the total angular momentum of the light degree of freedom in a charmed meson (antimeson); (b) $J^C=1^+,2^-,3^+,...$, if the two different mesons belong to the same doublet. The decays to spin-singlet charmonium states are suppressed when one of the two conditions is satisfied. We discuss constrained decay properties for selected systems.

## Key claim

In the heavy quark limit, the spin of the charm-anticharm pair \(J_{c\bar{c}}\) in an S-wave meson-antimeson molecule or resonance cannot be 0 under two specific conditions: (a) when the total spin \(J\) equals \(|j_2 - j_4| - 1\) or \(j_2 + j_4 + 1\), where \(j_2\) and \(j_4\) are the total angular momenta of the light degrees of freedom in the charmed meson and antimeson; (b) when the two different mesons belong to the same doublet and the system has \(J^C = 1^+, 2^-, 3^+, \dots\). The abstract omits the explicit dependence on the doublet structure and the C-parity condition.

## Method

The author constructs spin wave functions at the quark level for S-wave meson-antimeson states, treating charmed mesons as bound states of a charm quark and an "effective" light quark with total angular momentum \(j_\ell\). Using recoupling schemes and 9-j symbols, the amplitudes for \(J_{c\bar{c}} = 0\) and \(J_{c\bar{c}} = 1\) are derived from the spin wave function of the system. The analysis is extended to heavy quark baryon-antibaryon systems with analogous recoupling formulas.

## Key numbers

- For the \(D\bar{D}^*\) system with \(J^{PC}=1^{++}\), the spin selection rule gives \(J_{c\bar{c}}=1\) exclusively for the \(C=+\) state; for the \(C=-\) state (\(1^{+-}\)), the amplitudes for \(J_{c\bar{c}}=0\) and \(J_{c\bar{c}}=1\) are equal.
- For the \(D^*\bar{D}^*\) system, the spin selection rule \(J_{c\bar{c}}\neq0\) applies only for \(J=2\) (with \(C=+\)); for \(J=0\) the coefficient for \(J_{c\bar{c}}=0\) is \(\sqrt{3}/2\) and for \(J_{c\bar{c}}=1\) is \(-1/2\); for \(J=1\) the amplitudes for \(J_{c\bar{c}}=0\) and \(J_{c\bar{c}}=1\) are equal.
- For the \(D^*\bar{D}_1\) system (with \(j_2=1/2\), \(j_4=3/2\)), the spin selection rule \(J_{c\bar{c}}\neq0\) applies for \(J=0\).
- For the \(D^*\bar{D}_2^*\) system, the spin selection rule applies for \(J=3\).
- For the \(D_1\bar{D}_2^*\) system, the spin selection rule applies for \(J^C = 1^+, 2^-, 3^+\) when the two mesons belong to the same doublet.

## Context

This work extends the heavy quark spin selection rule first noted by Voloshin (2004) for the \(X(3872)\) as a \(D\bar{D}^*\) molecule with \(J^{PC}=1^{++}\), where \(J_{c\bar{c}}=1\) suppresses decays to spin-singlet charmonium. The author generalizes the rule to all S-wave meson-antimeson systems up to P-wave mesons, providing explicit conditions based on the total angular momentum of light degrees of freedom and C-parity. The results enable experimental discrimination of molecular interpretations for XYZ states such as \(Z_c(3900)\), \(Z(4430)\), and \(Y(3940)\) by predicting which decay channels to spin-singlet charmonium (e.g., \(\eta_c\), \(h_c\)) are suppressed or allowed, and can help identify quantum numbers from observed decay modes.
