# Flavor decomposition for the proton helicity parton distribution functions

> We present, for the first time, an \textit{ab initio} calculation of the individual up, down and strange quark helicity parton distribution functions for the proton. The calculation is performed within the twisted mass clover-improved fermion formulation of lattice QCD using one ensemble of dynamical up, down, strange and charm quarks with a pion mass of 260 MeV. The lattice matrix elements are non-perturbatively renormalized and the final results are presented in the $\overline{ \rm MS}$ scheme at a scale of 2 GeV. We give results on the $Δu^+(x)$ and $Δd^+(x)$, including disconnected quark loop contributions, as well as on the $Δs^+(x)$. For the latter we achieve unprecedented precision compared to the phenomenological estimates.

## Key claim
This paper presents the first *ab initio* lattice QCD calculation of the individual up, down, and strange quark helicity parton distribution functions for the proton, including disconnected quark loop contributions. The results are obtained using a single ensemble with a pion mass of 260 MeV and a maximum nucleon momentum of 1.24 GeV, and the authors note that systematic uncertainties from discretization, volume, and pion mass dependence remain to be quantified with additional ensembles.

## Method
The authors implement the quasi-PDF method of Ji (2013), computing nucleon matrix elements of a non-local axial operator with a Wilson line on a twisted mass clover-improved fermion ensemble with Nf=2+1+1 dynamical quarks at a lattice spacing of 0.093 fm and volume 32^3 x 64. Bare matrix elements are non-perturbatively renormalized using an RI'-type prescription and converted to the MSbar scheme at 2 GeV, then matched to light-cone PDFs using one-loop perturbation theory with nucleon mass corrections. Both connected and disconnected diagrams are computed, with the disconnected contributions evaluated using hierarchical probing and the one-end trick for stochastic estimation.

## Key numbers
- Pion mass of the ensemble: 260 MeV
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.093 fm
- Lattice volume: 32^3 x 64 (L approximately 3 fm)
- Nucleon momenta used: P3 = 0.41, 0.83, and 1.24 GeV
- Source-sink separation: 0.94 fm for the lowest momentum, 1.13 fm for the two higher momenta
- Number of measurements: 392 (P3=0.41 GeV), 1552 (P3=0.83 GeV), 6320 (P3=1.24 GeV)
- Number of configurations: 330 for the two smallest momenta, 480 for the largest
- Cutoff z_max for isoscalar matrix element at P3=1.24 GeV: 7a
- Cutoff z_max for isovector matrix element at P3=1.24 GeV: 9a

## Context
This work extends the lattice QCD program on unpolarized PDFs (Alexandrou 2019) to helicity PDFs with full flavor decomposition, and provides the first lattice determination of the strange helicity PDF with significantly higher precision than phenomenological fits such as JAM17 (Ethier 2017). The results enable future comparisons with global analyses like NNPDFpol1.1 (Nocera 2014) and DSSV14 (de Florian 2019), and the authors plan to assess systematic uncertainties using ensembles at the physical pion mass.
