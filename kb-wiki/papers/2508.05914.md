# Detecting Traces of Light-Quark Yukawa Couplings to the Higgs Boson in Fragmentation Products

> We point out that Yukawa interactions of light quarks with the Higgs boson are imprinted as unique azimuthal modulations in the density of fragmentation hadrons relative to the Higgs $p_T$. We introduce Yukawa Fragmentation Asymmetries (YFAs), interference observables that are linearly proportional to real (Standard Model) or CP-odd Yukawa couplings, respectively. The chiral suppression is lifted nonperturbatively by chiral-odd multi-hadron fragmentation functions. As a simple example process, we consider $VH$ production with a tagged target fragmentation hadron at the HL-LHC. We find promising projected sensitivities to first and second-generation couplings while maintaining superior control over theory systematics, flavor separation, and model independence compared to present methods. Our results point to deep synergies between precision studies of confinement and the in-depth exploration of the Higgs sector.

## Key claim

Yukawa interactions of light quarks with the Higgs boson produce unique azimuthal modulations in the density of fragmentation hadrons relative to the Higgs transverse momentum, with the signal being linearly proportional to the Yukawa coupling rather than quadratically suppressed, provided one exploits chiral-odd multi-hadron fragmentation functions that lift the chiral suppression nonperturbatively.

## Method

The authors introduce Yukawa Fragmentation Asymmetries (YFAs), interference observables formed by comparing rates of target fragmentation hadrons above and below the Higgs production plane in VH production at the HL-LHC. The asymmetry exploits the Boer-Mulders fracture function, a chiral-odd nonperturbative matrix element that correlates transverse quark polarization with the direction of the fragmentation hadron, combined with parity violation from the weak interaction to produce a sin(Delta phi) modulation. Sensitivity estimates are derived using a model where the Boer-Mulders fracture function is proportional to the unpolarized parton distribution function times an effective degree of polarization, with hadron yields from Pythia 8.3.16 and background cross sections from Sherpa 3.1.

## Key numbers

* Projected 95% CL limit on up quark Yukawa coupling deviation: |Delta kappa_u|/100 < 25_stat + 1.2_syst, from charged-pion YFAs in VH production at HL-LHC with 3/ab at 14 TeV
* Projected 95% CL limit on down quark Yukawa coupling deviation: |Delta kappa_d|/100 < 14_stat + 0.7_syst, from charged-pion YFAs in VH production at HL-LHC with 3/ab at 14 TeV
* Projected 95% CL limit on charm quark Yukawa coupling deviation: |Delta kappa_c| < 18_stat + 0.5_syst, from kaon and charm meson YFAs in VH production at HL-LHC with 3/ab at 14 TeV
* Projected 95% CL limit on strange quark Yukawa coupling deviation: |Delta kappa_s|/10 < 22_stat + 0.8_syst, from kaon and charm meson YFAs in VH production at HL-LHC with 3/ab at 14 TeV
* Average charged pion yield per VH event within acceptance (3 <= eta_h <= 5, 0.2 <= p_T^h <= 2 GeV): n_pi+ = 8.79, n_pi- = 8.73
* Average kaon yield per VH event: n_K+/- = 2.75
* Average charm meson yield per VH event: n_D+/- = alpha_s(m_c)/(2pi)
* Indirect 95% CL constraints on Yukawa couplings from Higgs total width: |y_u/y_u_SM| < 560, |y_d/y_d_SM| < 260, |y_s/y_s_SM| < 13, |y_c/y_c_SM| < 1.2

## Context

This work extends the program of constraining light-quark Yukawa couplings through exclusive decays (Bodwin 2013, Kagan 2014, Konig 2015), inclusive decays (Perez 2015, Carpenter 2016), off-shell rates (Zhou 2015, Falkowski 2020, Vignaroli 2022), and differential distributions in associated Higgs production (Brivio 2015, Bishara 2016, Soreq 2016, Yu 2016, Alasfar 2019). It builds on the theoretical observation that transverse parton polarization is imprinted on fragmentation products (Collins 1992, Artru 1995, Boer 1997, Sivers 2008) and the experimentally established Collins effect (Belle 2005, BaBar 2013). The results motivate experimental extraction of Boer-Mulders fracture functions from baseline processes like Drell-Yan production, and extend to applications at future colliders including LHeC and e+e- machines.
