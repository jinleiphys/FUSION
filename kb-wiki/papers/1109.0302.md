# Higher-point correlations from the JIMWLK evolution

> We develop a new approximation scheme aiming at extracting higher-point correlation functions from the JIMWLK evolution, in the limit where the number of colors is large. Namely, we show that by exploiting the structure of the 'virtual' terms in the Balitsky-JIMWLK equations, one can derive functional relations expressing arbitrary n-point functions of the Wilson lines in terms of the 2-point function (the scattering amplitude for a color dipole). These approximations are correct not only in the regime of strong scattering, where the evolution is indeed controlled by the 'virtual' terms, but also in the regime of weak scattering, where they reduce to the corresponding BFKL solutions. This last feature follows from the fact that the JIMWLK Hamiltonian is a linear combination of the pieces responsible for the 'real' and 'virtual' terms, respectively. We apply this scheme to two examples: the 'color quadrupole' (the 4-point function of the Wilson lines which enters the cross-section for the production of a pair of jets at forward rapidities) and the 'color sextupole' (the 6-point function). For particular configurations of the quadrupole, our general formula reduces to relatively simple expressions that have been previously proposed on the basis of the McLerran-Venugopalan model and which were recently shown to agree quite well with exact, numerical, solutions to the JIMWLK equation.

## Key claim

The authors develop an approximation scheme for extracting higher-point correlation functions (quadrupole, sextupole) from the JIMWLK evolution at large $N_c$ by keeping only the "virtual" terms in the Balitsky-JIMWLK equations, claiming this yields functional relations expressing arbitrary $n$-point functions in terms of the 2-point dipole amplitude that are correct both in the strong scattering regime (where virtual terms dominate) and in the weak scattering regime (where they reduce to BFKL solutions).

## Method

The authors solve a simplified version of the Balitsky-JIMWLK equations at large $N_c$ in which the "virtual" terms are retained but the "real" terms are dropped, exploiting the structure of the JIMWLK Hamiltonian as a linear combination of pieces responsible for real and virtual terms respectively. They derive explicit functional relations for the color quadrupole (4-point function) and color sextupole (6-point function) in terms of the dipole 2-point function, then verify that these expressions reduce to the correct BFKL results in the weak scattering regime and match McLerran-Venugopalan model results for particular quadrupole configurations.

## Context

This work extends the analytic understanding of higher-point correlations in the Color Glass Condensate framework beyond the dipole amplitude, which had been the primary focus of phenomenology for deep inelastic scattering and single-inclusive particle production (Iancu 2002, Iancu 2003, Gelis 2010). The paper addresses the need for analytic approximations to complement recent numerical JIMWLK solutions for the quadrupole (Dumitru 2011), which showed good agreement with heuristic extrapolations of McLerran-Venugopalan model formulas (Jalilian-Marian 2004, Dominguez 2011). The results enable systematic analytic calculations of multiparticle correlations in forward di-hadron production at RHIC and LHC, and provide a unified description from the dilute BFKL regime through the saturation regime.
