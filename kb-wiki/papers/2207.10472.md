# Multiparticle production in proton-nucleus collisions beyond eikonal accuracy

> We study the effects on multigluon production at mid-rapidity in the Color Glass Condensate of the non-eikonal corrections that stem from relaxing the shockwave approximation and giving the target a finite size. We extend previous works performed in the dilute-dilute approximation suitable for proton-proton collisions, to the dilute-dense one applicable to proton-nucleus. We employ the McLerran-Venugopalan model for the projectile averages. For the target averages, we use the Golec-Biernat--WÃ¼sthoff model and restrict to the leading contributions in overlap area that allow a factorization of ensembles of Wilson lines into products of dipoles. We make the connection with the jet quenching formalism and compare with previous results in the literature, providing a parametrization of the so-called decorated dipoles. We show that the non-eikonal effects on single inclusive particle production, contrary to what happens in jet quenching, are only sizable, of a few percent, for modest energies $\sqrt{s_{NN}}\leq 100$ GeV and central rapidities. On the other hand, we find that the effects on double inclusive gluon production are larger for the same kinematics. We show that, as found previously in the dilute-dilute situation, non-eikonal corrections break the accidental symmetry in the CGC, allowing for the existence of non-vanishing odd azimuthal harmonics.

## Key claim

Non-eikonal corrections from relaxing the shockwave approximation and giving the target a finite size produce only small effects (a few percent) on single inclusive gluon production at modest energies \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\leq 100\) GeV and central rapidities, but produce larger effects on double inclusive gluon production for the same kinematics; these corrections break the accidental symmetry in the Color Glass Condensate, allowing non-vanishing odd azimuthal harmonics.

## Method

The authors extend previous dilute-dilute calculations for proton-proton collisions to the dilute-dense limit applicable to proton-nucleus collisions, using the Color Glass Condensate effective theory. They employ the McLerran-Venugopalan model for projectile averages and the Golec-Biernat-Wusthoff model for target averages, restricting to leading contributions in overlap area that allow factorization of ensembles of Wilson lines into products of dipoles. The non-eikonal corrections are implemented by replacing eikonal Wilson lines with scalar retarded propagators that describe gluon propagation through a finite-thickness target, and target averaging is performed using a discretized longitudinal coordinate approach.

## Context

This work generalizes previous results by Agostini et al. (2019) that analyzed non-eikonal corrections in proton-proton collisions to the proton-nucleus case. It extends the eikonal calculation of the n-gluon spectrum developed by Agostini et al. (2021) to include finite longitudinal width effects. The approach connects with the jet quenching formalism and provides a parametrization of decorated dipole functions defined in earlier works by Altinoluk et al. (2014, 2015). The results are relevant for interpreting upcoming Electron-Ion Collider data where non-eikonal corrections become significant, and for explaining away- and near-side asymmetries seen in small collision systems at RHIC.
