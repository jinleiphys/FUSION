# Quarkonium production in heavy ion collisions: coupled Boltzmann transport equations

> We develop a set of coupled Boltzmann equations to describe the dynamical evolution of heavy quarks and quarkonia inside the quark-gluon plasma. The quarkonium dissociation and recombination terms are calculated from pNRQCD. Their interplay drives the system to a detailed balance. The heavy quark energy loss term is necessary for the system to reach kinematic thermalization. By coupling the transport equations with initial particles' momenta generated by PYTHIA and hydrodynamic medium evolutions, we can describe the $R_{AA}$ of $Υ$ family at both RHIC and LHC energies. The transverse momentum azimuthal anisotropy of $Υ$(1S) in $2.76$ TeV peripheral Pb-Pb collisions is also studied.

## Key claim

The paper develops a set of coupled Boltzmann transport equations for heavy quarks and quarkonia that, when combined with pNRQCD-based dissociation and recombination rates and heavy quark energy loss, can drive the system to detailed balance and kinematic thermalization, enabling description of Upsilon family suppression at both RHIC and LHC energies. The key qualifier is that the heavy quark energy loss term is necessary for the system to reach proper equilibrium, not just detailed balance from dissociation-recombination interplay alone.

## Method

The authors constructed coupled Boltzmann equations for heavy quarks, antiquarks, and each quarkonium state (with quantum numbers nl), where the left-hand side describes free streaming and the right-hand side contains collision terms. Quarkonium dissociation and recombination terms were calculated from potential non-relativistic QCD (pNRQCD) using tree-level diagrams up to next-to-leading order in the coupling constant, including gluon absorption/emission and inelastic scattering with light quarks and gluons. The equations were solved via Monte Carlo simulations, with initial particle momenta generated by PYTHIA, initial entropy density from TRENTo, and medium evolution from 2+1D relativistic viscous hydrodynamics.

## Key numbers

- Melting temperature of Upsilon(2S) set to 210 MeV, above which no Upsilon(2S) state can be formed
- Coupling constant fixed to alpha_s = 0.3; attractive potential V_S = -0.56/r
- Cold nuclear matter suppression factor assumed to be 0.72 for 200 GeV Au-Au collisions and 0.87 for 2.76 TeV Pb-Pb collisions
- Branching ratio of Upsilon(2S) to Upsilon(1S) in hadronic phase is 0.26
- QGP box test simulations performed at T = 300 MeV with N_b,tot = 25 and side length 10 fm
- Upsilon(1S) transverse momentum azimuthal anisotropy (v_2) studied in 2.76 TeV peripheral Pb-Pb collisions with centrality 30-50%

## Context

This work extends the Boltzmann transport approach to quarkonium production by incorporating pNRQCD-based dissociation and recombination rates, building on earlier studies of quarkonium dissociation rates from pNRQCD (Brambilla 1999, 2011, 2013) and the authors' prior work (Yao 2017). The framework enables consistent treatment of cold nuclear matter effects, screening, dissociation, recombination, and feed-down processes, and can be generalized to study doubly charm baryon production (Yao 2018). Future directions include using temperature-dependent potentials from lattice calculations, including additional quarkonium states (1P, 2P, 3S), and employing event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations.
