# Saturation of elliptic flow and shear viscosity

> Effect of shear viscosity on elliptic flow is studied in causal dissipative hydrodynamics in 2+1 dimensions. Elliptic flow is reduced in viscous dynamics. Causal evolution of minimally viscous fluid ($η/s$=0.08), can explain the PHENIX data on elliptic flow in 16-23% Au+Au collisions up to $p_T\approx$3.6 GeV. In contrast, ideal hydrodynamics, can explain the same data only up to $p_T\approx$1.5 GeV. $p_T$ spectra of identified particles are also better explained in minimally viscous fluid than in ideal dynamics. However, saturation of elliptic flow at large $p_T$ is not reproduced.

## Key claim

Causal dissipative hydrodynamics with minimal shear viscosity (η/s=0.08) can explain PHENIX elliptic flow data in 16-23% Au+Au collisions up to pT≈3.6 GeV, but the model does not reproduce the experimentally observed saturation of elliptic flow at large transverse momentum.

## Method

The author solved 2+1 dimensional causal dissipative hydrodynamics with longitudinal boost-invariance, including only shear viscosity as the dissipative force. Initial conditions used standard RHIC parameters (τi=0.6 fm, central entropy density Sini=110 fm⁻³, peak energy density ~35 GeV/fm³) with an equation of state (EOS-Q) incorporating a first-order phase transition at Tc=164 MeV. The shear stress tensors were initialized to their boost-invariant values, and the relaxation time was taken from kinetic theory as τrelx=6η/(sT).

## Key numbers

- η/s = 0.08 (minimal shear viscosity, corresponding to the ADS/CFT lower bound of 1/4π)
- Ideal hydrodynamics explains elliptic flow data only up to pT≈1.5 GeV, while minimally viscous dynamics (η/s=0.08) explains data up to pT≈3.6 GeV
- For η/s=0.04, PHENIX data are well explained up to pT≈3 GeV
- Freeze-out temperature TF=130 MeV yields the best agreement for minimally viscous dynamics
- Initial peak energy density ~35 GeV/fm³ at τi=0.6 fm
- Critical temperature Tc=164 MeV for the first-order phase transition

## Context

This work extends the study of elliptic flow in relativistic heavy-ion collisions beyond ideal hydrodynamics (Heinz 2005, QGP3) by incorporating causal dissipative hydrodynamics following Israel-Stewart theory. The results contrast with Romatschke and Romatschke (2007), who found that minimum bias data favor η/s≈0.03, lower than the ADS/CFT bound. The paper shows that while viscous dynamics improves agreement with data at intermediate pT, the saturation of elliptic flow at high pT remains unexplained, suggesting that jet contributions or other non-equilibrium effects not captured by this approach are needed.
