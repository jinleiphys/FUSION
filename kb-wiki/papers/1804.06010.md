# Systematic study of $\boldsymbol{\mathcalα}$ decay of nuclei around $\boldsymbol{Z=82}$, $\boldsymbol{N=126}$ shell closure within the cluster-formation model and proximity potential 1977 formalism

> In the present work, we systematically study the $\mathcalα$ decay preformation factors $P_α$ within the cluster-formation model and $\mathcalα$ decay half-lives by the proximity potential 1977 formalism for nuclei around $Z=82$, $N=126$ closed shells. The calculations show that the realistic $P_α$ is linearly dependent on the product of valance protons (holes) and valance neutrons (holes) $N_pN_n$. It is consistent with our previous works [X.-D. Sun \textit{et al.}, \href{https://journals.aps.org/prc/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevC.94.024338}{ Phys. Rev. C 94, 024338 (2016)}, J.-G. Deng \textit{et al.}, \href{https://journals.aps.org/prc/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevC.96.024318}{Phys. Rev. C 96, 024318 (2017)}], which $P_α$ are model-dependent and extracted from the ratios of calculated $\mathcalα$ half-lives to experimental data. Combining with our previous works, we confirm that the valance proton-neutron interaction plays a key role in the $\mathcalα$ preformation for nuclei around $Z=82$, $N=126$ shell closures whether the $P_α$ is model-dependent or microcosmic. In addition, our calculated $\mathcalα$ decay half-lives by using the proximity potential 1977 formalism taking $P_α$ evaluated by the cluster-formation model can well reproduce the experimental data and significantly reduce the errors.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the alpha decay preformation factor \(P_{\alpha}\) calculated within the cluster-formation model (CFM) for nuclei around the \(Z=82\), \(N=126\) shell closures is linearly dependent on the product of valence protons (holes) and valence neutrons (holes) \(N_p N_n\), confirming that the valence proton-neutron interaction plays a key role in alpha preformation regardless of whether \(P_{\alpha}\) is obtained model-dependently or microscopically.

## Method

The authors systematically calculated alpha decay preformation factors \(P_{\alpha}\) for even-even, odd-\(A\), and doubly-odd nuclei around the \(Z=82\), \(N=126\) closed shells using the cluster-formation model (CFM), which expresses \(P_{\alpha}\) as the ratio of the alpha cluster formation energy \(E_{f\alpha}\) to the total energy \(E\) of the system, both derived from nuclear binding energies. They then computed alpha decay half-lives using the proximity potential 1977 formalism (Prox.1977), incorporating the CFM-derived \(P_{\alpha}\) values, the WKB barrier penetration probability, and the assault frequency, and compared these to experimental half-lives and to calculations assuming \(P_{\alpha}=1\).

## Key numbers

- For even-even nuclei, \(P_{\alpha}\) values range from 0.105 (for \(^{210}\)Po) to 0.262 (for \(^{190}\)Po and \(^{194}\)Rn), showing a linear dependence on \(N_p N_n\).
- For the favored alpha decay of odd-\(A\) nuclei, \(P_{\alpha}\) values range from 0.093 (for \(^{211}\)At) to 0.247 (for \(^{199}\)Fr and \(^{205}\)Ac).
- The calculated half-life for \(^{212}\)Po using CFM \(P_{\alpha}=0.221\) and Prox.1977 is \(2.62\times10^{-7}\) s, compared to the experimental value of \(2.95\times10^{-7}\) s.
- For \(^{210}\)Po, the calculated half-life with CFM \(P_{\alpha}=0.105\) is \(8.31\times10^{6}\) s, versus the experimental \(1.20\times10^{7}\) s.
- The calculated half-life for \(^{214}\)Ra using CFM \(P_{\alpha}=0.139\) is \(2.34\times10^{0}\) s, compared to the experimental \(2.44\times10^{0}\) s.

## Context

This work extends the earlier findings of Seif et al. (2011), who proposed a linear relationship between \(P_{\alpha}\) and \(N_p N_n\) for even-even nuclei, and the authors' own previous studies (Sun et al., 2016; Deng et al., 2017), which extracted model-dependent \(P_{\alpha}\) from ratios of calculated to experimental half-lives. By applying the cluster-formation model to provide a more realistic, microscopically motivated \(P_{\alpha}\), the study confirms that the valence proton-neutron interaction governs alpha preformation across all nuclear types near the \(Z=82\), \(N=126\) shells, and it enables improved predictions of alpha decay half-lives that significantly reduce deviations from experimental data compared to calculations that assume \(P_{\alpha}=1\).
