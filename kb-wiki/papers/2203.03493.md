# Charge radii of Ca isotopes and correlations

> We study the effects of short- and long-range correlations on the charge radii of Ca isotopes. We start our investigation with an independent particle model consisting in Hartree-Fock plus Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer calculations with finite-range effective nucleon-nucleon interactions of Gogny type. The short-range correlations effects are evaluated by considering all the terms of a cluster expansion containing a single correlation line. The long-range correlations are taken into account by including the coupling with the quasi-particle random phase approximation phonons. While the effects of the short-range correlations are negligible, those of the long-range correlations largely modify the independent particle model results and improve the agreement with the experimental data.

## Key claim

Short-range correlations (SRC) have negligible effects on Ca isotope charge radii, while long-range correlations (LRC) from coupling to QRPA phonons significantly modify the independent particle model results and improve agreement with experiment, but the trend of experimental data showing a maximum for ⁴⁴Ca is not yet well described.

## Method

The authors start with an independent particle model (IPM) using Hartree-Fock plus Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (HF+BCS) calculations with three Gogny-type finite-range effective nucleon-nucleon interactions (D1S, D1M, D1ST2a). Short-range correlations are evaluated using a cluster expansion truncated to single correlation lines, with correlation functions taken from a Fermi Hypernetted-Chain calculation for ⁴⁸Ca. Long-range correlations are included by coupling the IPM ground state with quasi-particle random phase approximation (QRPA) phonons, considering multipoles from J=0⁺ to J=3 (plus 4⁺ and 6⁺ for ⁴²Ca, ⁴⁴Ca, ⁴⁶Ca) with a maximum energy cutoff of 20 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The average absolute difference between IPM isotope shift curves for the three interactions is smaller than 0.07 fm², with a maximum of 0.2 fm² (for ⁵⁴Ca)
- The reference charge radius used is R_ch(A=40) = 3.4776(19) fm (Angeli 2013)
- LRC enlarge the charge distributions and increase the r.m.s. radii by about 15%
- The maximum energy cutoff E_max = 20 MeV for all multipolarities ensures sufficient stability of QRPA results
- For ⁴²Ca, ⁴⁴Ca, and ⁴⁶Ca, 4⁺ and 6⁺ multipoles were included because some excited states appear below 4 MeV
- Binding energies per nucleon for all Ca isotopes from A=34 to A=60 are tabulated, with the largest experimental uncertainty being 0.012 MeV for ⁶⁰Ca

## Context

This work extends the study of Ca isotope charge radii following new laser spectroscopy measurements by Garcia Ruiz (2016) for neutron-rich ⁴⁹,⁵¹,⁵²Ca and Miller (2019) for proton-rich ³⁶,³⁷,³⁸Ca. The authors use a traditional IPM approach with Gogny interactions, contrasting with elaborate energy functionals containing many free parameters (Fayans 2000, Klupfel 2009, Reinhard 2017) that can reproduce the data but obscure the underlying physics. The LRC model extends the approach of Rowe (1970), Lenske (1990), and Anguiano (2001) by using QRPA backward amplitudes. The results disagree with the SRC findings of Miller (2019a) but align with the fully microscopic calculation of Hagen (2016) for ⁴⁸Ca. The work enables future studies incorporating effects beyond single quasi-particle excitations, as in the shell model calculation of Caurier (2001) which provides a good description of the intermediate region.
