# Non-standard pairing in asymmetric trapped Fermi gases

> We study an ultracold trapped Fermi gas of atoms in two hyperfine states with unequal populations. In this situation the usual BCS pairing is suppressed and non-standard pairing mechanisms become important. These are treated by solving the Bogoliubov-de Gennes equations, which at the same time correctly take into account the finite size of the trapped system. We find results which can be viewed as generalization of the LOFF phase to finite systems.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in trapped Fermi gases with unequal populations, non-standard pairing (generalizing the LOFF phase to finite systems) appears naturally in a fully microscopic Bogoliubov-de Gennes treatment, but the local-density approximation (LDA) fails quantitatively because the LOFF oscillation wavelength is comparable to the system size, even for systems with 17000 atoms.

## Method

The authors solved the Bogoliubov-de Gennes (BdG) equations numerically for a spherical harmonic trap containing atoms in two hyperfine states with an attractive s-wave interaction, using a regularization method for the pairing field. They studied two system sizes (average chemical potential 22 and 32 in harmonic oscillator units, corresponding to approximately 4900 and 17000 atoms) and varied the population asymmetry by adjusting the difference in chemical potentials between the two spin states. For comparison, they performed LDA calculations where at each radial point the system was treated as a uniform gas with a plane-wave LOFF gap, minimizing the thermodynamic potential with respect to gap amplitude and wave vector.

## Key numbers

- Small system: average chemical potential 22 in units of $\hbar\omega$, corresponding to total atom number $N \approx 4900$.
- Large system: average chemical potential 32 in units of $\hbar\omega$, corresponding to total atom number $N \approx 17000$.
- Coupling constant used: $g = -\hbar^2 l_{ho}/m$, where $l_{ho} = \sqrt{\hbar/(m\omega)}$ is the harmonic oscillator length.
- For the small system at zero asymmetry, the pairing gap at the trap center is $\Delta(0) \approx 2$ $\hbar\omega$.
- For the small system, BCS pairing disappears at $\delta\mu \approx 1.2$ $\hbar\omega$, corresponding to number asymmetry $\alpha = (N_+ - N_-)/N \approx 0.06$.
- For the small system, LOFF-type pairing reappears near $\delta\mu \approx 2$ $\hbar\omega$ ($\alpha \approx 0.07$).
- For the large system in LDA, the BCS-to-LOFF transition is first-order at $\delta\mu = 11.9$ $\hbar\omega$, with LOFF wave vector $q \sim l_{ho}^{-1}$ (wavelength $\sim 6.2$ $l_{ho}$).
- For the large system in BdG, the transition occurs between $\delta\mu = 10$ $\hbar\omega$ ($\alpha \approx 0.25$) and $\delta\mu = 11$ $\hbar\omega$ ($\alpha \approx 0.29$).
- For $\delta\mu = 12$ $\hbar\omega$ ($\alpha \approx 0.3$), the LDA gap goes to zero at radius $\sim 2$ $l_{ho}$, and the microscopic order parameter has a node at $\approx 3$ $l_{ho}$.
- For $\delta\mu = 6$ $\hbar\omega$ ($\alpha \approx 0.15$), LDA predicts BCS phase for $r < 3.8$ $l_{ho}$ and LOFF phase with $q \sim 0.7$ $l_{ho}^{-1}$ (wavelength $\sim 8.9$ $l_{ho}$) in the interval $3.8$ $l_{ho} < r < 4.1$ $l_{ho}$.
- The LOFF-type oscillation disappears between $T = \hbar\omega/k_B$ and $T = 2$ $\hbar\omega/k_B$, while the BCS gap in the center is still nonzero at $T = 2$ $\hbar\omega/k_B$.

## Context

This work extends studies of non-standard pairing in asymmetric Fermi gases (the LOFF phase, Sarma phase, and deformed Fermi surfaces) from infinite homogeneous systems or LDA treatments (as in Houbiers (1997), Combescot (2001), Liu and Wilczek (2003), and the DFS proposal) to a fully microscopic BdG treatment that respects the finite size and inhomogeneity of the trap. The results show that LDA, which is commonly used for trapped gases, gives qualitatively different predictions (first-order transitions instead of smooth crossovers) and fails quantitatively because the LOFF oscillation wavelength is comparable to the system size. The work enables future experimental searches for the LOFF phase in trapped Fermi gases by identifying the temperature range (below about $\hbar\omega/k_B$) and asymmetry range where the oscillating order parameter should be observable, and by providing a benchmark for more sophisticated treatments beyond mean field.
