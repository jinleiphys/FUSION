# Thermal properties of zero sound in asymmetric nuclear matter

> The zero-sound modes at finite temperature are investigated with the relativistic random phase approximation to signal the uncertainty of the equation of state (EOS) of asymmetric nuclear matter. It is observed that in typically selected stiff and soft relativistic mean-field (RMF) models, zero-sound modes arise at low temperature, whereas increasing the temperature gradually breaks the zero sound in soft models, with a smaller density range compared to stiff models. At high density, the presence or absence of zero sound turns out to be correspondingly the character of the stiff or soft RMF EOS. More strikingly, we find by analyzing the dispersion relation and sound velocity that at finite temperature the zero-sound modes in RMF models with the stiff EOS undergo a thermal bifurcation, resulting in the transform of zero sound into the first sound at some momentum $Q>T$. The thermally bifurcated sound branch in the stiff models and the zero-sound branch in the soft models are both highly sensitive to the slope of the symmetry energy, providing promising signals for the pending high-density symmetry energies. In addition, it is found that there exists a nonlinear dispersion relation for both the stiff and soft models that supports the zero sound in the relatively lower density region.

## Key claim
The paper claims that zero-sound modes in asymmetric nuclear matter at finite temperature can signal the stiffness or softness of the equation of state (EOS), but this diagnostic power is conditional: it holds primarily at high densities and temperatures, while at lower temperatures (up to about 25 MeV) the zero-sound regions for stiff and soft models overlap significantly, blurring the distinction.

## Method
The authors used the relativistic random phase approximation (RRPA) based on relativistic mean-field (RMF) models to compute the dielectric function of hot asymmetric nuclear matter. Zero-sound modes were identified as zeros of the dielectric function, and their existence and properties were mapped across temperature-density space for four RMF parameter sets (NL3w03, GM1, TM1w02, FSUGarnet) representing stiff and soft EOSs. The symmetry energy slope was varied by adjusting the isoscalar-isovector coupling constant and the rho-nucleon coupling.

## Key numbers
- Zero sound appears over a broad temperature-density region for stiff EOSs (NL3w03, GM1) but narrows noticeably for soft EOSs (TM1w02, FSUGarnet); at T=40 MeV, the EOSs of TM1w02 and FSUGarnet are softer than those of NL3w03 and GM1.
- Relaxation time estimates using the GM1 model: at T=50 MeV and rho_B=rho_0, tau = 4.6 x 10^{-23} s; at T=100 MeV and rho_B=1.5 rho_0, tau = 1.18 x 10^{-23} s; at T=50 MeV and rho_B=5 rho_0, tau = 4.0 x 10^{-23} s; at T=100 MeV and rho_B=5 rho_0, tau = 9.58 x 10^{-24} s.
- In the NL3 parametrization at finite temperature (T=10 MeV), when the slope parameter L exceeds 104.3 MeV, the lower zero-sound branch is missing across all density regions.
- At density 3 rho_0 and T=10-20 MeV, the dispersion relation shows two branches for each parametrization; the lower branch deviates from the zero-sound dispersion relation with a momentum stride Q > T.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous zero-temperature study (Ye 2023) on the relationship between zero sound and EOS stiffness in nuclear matter. It contradicts the earlier claim by Kolomietz (2001) that at elevated temperature zero sound transitions entirely to first sound, finding instead that a zero-sound branch persists at finite temperature while a thermal bifurcation produces a new branch that may transform into first sound. The results provide potential observables for constraining the high-density symmetry energy, which remains poorly constrained according to Oertel (2017) and Li (2008).
