# Single-flavor heavy baryons in a strong magnetic field

> In this work, we study the properties of single-flavor heavy baryons, $Ω_{\rm ccc}$ and $Ω_{\rm bbb}$, in a strong magnetic field. For that sake, we simply treat the baryons as quark-diquark two-body systems, and a systematic formalism is developed to deal with two-body Schr$\ddot{\text o}$dinger equations in a magnetic field. It is found that: 1. The orbital properties of $Ω_{\rm bbb}$ are almost not affected by the magnetic field. 2. $Ω_{\rm ccc}$ is more tightly bound in the presence of a magnetic field. 3. The magnetic-spin effect dominates over the magnetic-orbital effect. Applying to peripheral heavy ion collisions, $Ω_{\rm ccc}$ is much better than $Ω_{\rm bbb}$ to explore the magnetic effect, and the discovery of $Ω_{\rm ccc}$ could be more promising.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in a strong magnetic field, the orbital properties of $\Omega_{\rm bbb}$ are almost unaffected while $\Omega_{\rm ccc}$ becomes more tightly bound, with the magnetic-spin effect dominating over the magnetic-orbital effect; however, this conclusion is conditional on the quark-diquark isosceles (QDI) approximation and the specific choice of quantum numbers $l_{\rm r}=l_\rho=0$ for the lowest-lying eigenstates.

## Method

The authors treat single-flavor heavy baryons ($\Omega_{\rm ccc}$ and $\Omega_{\rm bbb}$) as quark-diquark two-body systems and develop a systematic formalism to solve the two-body Schrödinger equation in a constant magnetic field using the symmetric gauge. They separate center-of-mass and relative motions, apply the quark-diquark isosceles (QDI) approximation to handle the three-quark system, and solve the resulting two-dimensional Schrödinger equations numerically in cylindrical coordinates for the ground state with zero orbital angular momenta.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass: $m_{\rm c} = 1.29~{\rm GeV}$; bottom quark mass: $m_{\rm b} = 4.70~{\rm GeV}$
- Binding energy of $\Omega_{\rm ccc}$ ground state in vacuum: $E_{\Omega_{ccc}} = 0.964~{\rm GeV}$
- Binding energy of $\Omega_{\rm bbb}$ ground state in vacuum: $E_{\Omega_{\rm bbb}} = 1.60~{\rm GeV}$
- String tension: $\sigma = 0.2~{\rm GeV}^2$; Coulomb coupling constant: $\alpha_{\rm s} = 0.4105$
- Debye mass used for quark-gluon plasma phase: $m_{\rm D} = 0.4~{\rm GeV}$
- Effective magnetic length for $\Omega_{\rm bbb}$: $l_{\rm B} \equiv \sqrt{2/|q_{\rm b}B|} > 0.48~{\rm fm}$ in the considered region

## Context

This work extends the study of heavy hadrons in strong electromagnetic fields, previously focused on heavy mesons such as quarkonia by Guo (2015) and others, to the unexplored case of single-flavor heavy baryons. The formalism developed for two-body Schrödinger equations in a magnetic field provides a basis for future studies of equal-mass tetraquark and pentaquark states. The results suggest that $\Omega_{\rm ccc}$ is a more promising probe than $\Omega_{\rm bbb}$ for exploring magnetic effects in peripheral heavy ion collisions, and its discovery could be more feasible experimentally.
