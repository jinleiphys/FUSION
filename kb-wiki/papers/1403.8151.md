# Quarkonia at finite temperature in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> The behavior of quarkonia in relativistic heavy ion collisions is reviewed. After a detailed discussion of the current theoretical understanding of quarkonia in a static equilibriated plasma, we discuss quarkonia yield from the fireball created in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collision experiments. We end with a brief discussion of the experimental results and outlook.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the theoretical understanding of quarkonia in relativistic heavy ion collisions, arguing that while quarkonia suppression is a generic feature of deconfined medium formation, the detailed behavior is more complicated than originally thought and quarkonia likely provide insight into the nature of the plasma rather than acting simply as a thermometer.

## Method

The review examines lattice QCD calculations of spectral functions for charmonia and bottomonia using the maximum entropy method (MEM) to extract real-time information from Euclidean correlators, as well as effective field theory approaches including potential non-relativistic QCD (pNRQCD) and hard thermal loop (HTL) resummation to derive finite-temperature potentials. It also discusses regeneration models where charm quarks from hard collisions coalesce into quarkonia at freezeout according to statistical thermal distributions.

## Key numbers

* The transition temperature for deconfinement, \(T_c\), is estimated to be approximately 220 MeV for the lattice system with \(n_f=2\) and quark mass close to the strange quark mass
* Lattice NRQCD studies find that 1S bottomonia survive at least until temperatures of \(2 T_c\)
* The decay width of the \(\Upsilon(1S)\) extracted from lattice correlators shows a near-linear increase with temperature, with leading-order perturbation theory giving \(\Gamma \sim 14 \alpha_s^3 T\) for \(\alpha_s m_Q \gg T\)
* Using the leading-order HTL-resummed potential, the \(\Upsilon(1S)\) acquires considerable thermal width already at temperatures around 250 MeV, while for charmonium the peak structure is essentially absent at \(T \sim 250\) MeV
* A freezeout temperature of approximately 360 MeV was required in regeneration models to describe the \(p_T\) dependence of \(J/\psi\) production at RHIC

## Context

This review extends the original proposal by Matsui and Satz (1986) that \(J/\psi\) suppression signals deconfinement, and subsequent calculations of dissociation temperatures using Debye-screened potentials by Karsch, Mehr, and Satz (1988) and Digal, Goswami, and Srivastava (2001). It contrasts early lattice studies by Asakawa and Hatsuda (2004), Datta, Karsch, Petreczky, and Wetzorke (2004), and Matsufuru, Miyamura, and Nemoto (2004) that found \(J/\psi\) survival up to \(1.5 T_c\) with later work by Ding, Karsch, and Mukherjee (2012) showing significant modification already below \(1.5 T_c\). The review enables future work incorporating better-controlled lattice systematics and more realistic dynamical calculations for heavy ion collision phenomenology.
