# Monte Carlo evaluation of path integrals for the nuclear shell model

> We present in detail a formulation of the shell model as a path integral and Monte Carlo techniques for its evaluation. The formulation, which linearizes the two-body interaction by an auxiliary field, is quite general, both in the form of the effective `one-body' Hamiltonian and in the choice of ensemble. In particular, we derive formulas for the use of general (beyond monopole) pairing operators, as well as a novel extraction of the canonical (fixed-particle number) ensemble via an activity expansion. We discuss the advantages and disadvantages of the various formulations and ensembles and give several illustrative examples. We also discuss and illustrate calculation of the imaginary-time response function and the extraction, by maximum entropy methods, of the corresponding strength function. Finally, we discuss the "sign-problem" generic to fermion Monte Carlo calculations, and prove that a wide class of interactions are free of this limitation.

## Key claim

The paper presents a general auxiliary-field Monte Carlo method for evaluating path integrals of the nuclear shell model, claiming that a wide class of interactions are free of the sign problem that typically plagues fermion Monte Carlo calculations, but this freedom is conditional on the specific choice of decomposition and the nuclei under consideration.

## Method

The authors linearize the two-body interaction via a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation, writing the imaginary-time evolution operator as a path integral over auxiliary fields coupled to one-body operators. They decompose the Hamiltonian into quadratic forms using either density operators (which conserve particle number) or pairing operators (which mix particle number), and evaluate the resulting high-dimensional integral stochastically using Monte Carlo sampling with Metropolis random walks. The method propagates Slater determinants (for density decomposition) or Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov states (for pairing decomposition) through imaginary time, and extracts static observables from zero-temperature, grand-canonical, and canonical ensembles, with a novel activity expansion for the canonical (fixed-particle-number) ensemble.

## Key numbers

- The number of auxiliary fields grows only quadratically with the size of the single-particle basis, while the corresponding number of Slater determinants grows exponentially.
- The approximation in the Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation is valid through order Δβ, with corrections being commutator terms of order (Δβ)².
- For a "Pairing plus Quadrupole" model, only 8 auxiliary fields per time slice are required in the mixed decomposition.
- The matrix dimension for the isospin formalism is half that of the neutron-proton mixing decomposition, and the number of fields is also halved.

## Context

This work extends the auxiliary-field Monte Carlo methods previously applied to condensed matter systems such as the Hubbard Model (Linden 1992, Hirsch 1983) to the nuclear shell model, which involves a two-body interaction more general than simple on-site repulsion. It builds on earlier exact diagonalization studies of the shell model (Wildenthal 1984, 1988) that were limited by combinatorial scaling of the many-body space, and on the Static Path Approximation (Arve 1988, Puddu 1991). The method enables calculations for sd-shell and fp-shell nuclei that were previously inaccessible to direct diagonalization, and introduces techniques for extracting strength functions via maximum entropy methods and for handling the canonical ensemble through activity expansion or Fourier analysis (Dean 1993).
