# Laser spectroscopy and CP-violation sensitivity of actinium monofluoride

> The apparent invariance of the strong nuclear force under combined charge conjugation and parity (CP) remains an open question in modern physics. Precision experiments with heavy atoms and molecules can provide stringent constraints on CP violation via searches for effects due to permanent electric dipole moments and other CP-odd properties in leptons, hadrons, and nuclei. Radioactive molecules have been proposed as highly sensitive probes for such searches, but experiments with most such molecules have so far been beyond technical reach. Here we report the first production and spectroscopic study of a gas-phase actinium molecule, $^{227}$AcF. We observe the predicted strongest electronic transition from the ground state, which is necessary for efficient readout in searches of symmetry-violating interactions. Furthermore, we perform electronic- and nuclear-structure calculations for $^{227}$AcF to determine its sensitivity to various CP-violating parameters, and find that a realistic, near-term experiment with a precision of 1 mHz would improve current constraints on the CP-violating parameter hyperspace by three orders of magnitude. Our results thus highlight the potential of $^{227}$AcF for exceptionally sensitive searches of CP violation.

## Key claim
The paper reports the first production and spectroscopic study of a gas-phase actinium molecule, 227AcF, and claims that a realistic near-term experiment with a precision of 1 mHz would improve current constraints on the CP-violating parameter hyperspace by three orders of magnitude. The hedge is that this improvement depends on achieving that specific experimental precision and on the calculated molecular and nuclear sensitivity coefficients being correct.

## Method
The authors produced 227Ac19F+ ions at the CERN-ISOLDE facility via proton-induced nuclear reactions on a UCx target, with CF4 gas injected for molecular formation, followed by extraction using a FEBIAD ion source and mass separation. The ion beam was cooled and bunched in a linear Paul trap, then delivered to the CRIS experiment where two-step resonance ionization spectroscopy was performed using a scanned UV laser (377-400 nm) and a 355 nm Nd:YAG laser for non-resonant ionization. Electronic- and nuclear-structure calculations were performed using relativistic Fock-space coupled cluster methods and nuclear density functional theory to determine sensitivity coefficients for CP-violating parameters.

## Key numbers
- The 227Ac19F+ ion beam intensity was maintained between 6×10^6 and 2×10^7 ions per second during the experiment.
- The observed electronic transition from the X 1Σ+ ground state was found between 25,770 and 25,860 cm−1.
- The calculated radiative lifetime of the (8)1Π state is τ_calc = 6.65 ns, with an experimental upper limit of τ_exp ≤ 38(4) ns, giving a lower limit on the radiative decay rate of Γ ≥ 2.6(3)×10^7 s−1.
- The molecular sensitivity to the nuclear Schiff moment for the X 1Σ+ state is W_S = −8017(574) e/4πϵ0 a0^4.
- The intrinsic nuclear Schiff moment of 227Ac is S_int(227Ac) = 37.1(16) e fm^3, which is 40% higher than S_int(225Ra) = 26.6(19) e fm^3.
- The energy difference between the 227Ac ground state (Iπ = 3/2−) and the lowest-lying opposite-parity partner state (Iπ = 3/2+) is 27.369 keV.
- The sensitivity coefficients for the laboratory Schiff moment of 227Ac are: a0 = 4.4(10), a1 = −14.0(18), a2 = 8.3(31), b1 = −0.2(2), b2 = 0.3(3) (all in units of e fm^3).

## Context
This work extends the search for CP-violating effects using radioactive molecules, building on proposals that heavy nuclei with static octupole deformation (such as 225Ra and 227Ac) possess enhanced Schiff moments (Flambaum 2020; Dobaczewski 2018). The study provides the first experimental spectroscopic data for AcF, which was previously only studied theoretically (Skripnikov 2023), and demonstrates that actinium molecules can be produced and studied at ISOL-type facilities. The results enable future precision experiments with 227AcF that could complement ongoing searches using 199Hg (Graner 2016), 205Tl (Grasdijk 2021), 225Ra (Bishof 2016), ThO, and HfF+ for constraining CP-violating parameters, particularly the QCD θ-bar parameter and pion-nucleon coupling constants.
