# Final-state interactions in inclusive deep-inelastic scattering from the deuteron

> We explore the role of final-state interactions (FSI) in inclusive deep-inelastic scattering from the deuteron. Relating the inclusive cross section to the deuteron forward virtual Compton scattering amplitude, a general formula for the FSI contribution is derived in the generalized eikonal approximation, utilizing the diffractive nature of the effective hadron-nucleon interaction. The calculation uses a factorized model with a basis of three resonances with mass $W<2$ GeV and a continuum contribution for larger $W$ as the relevant set of effective hadron states entering the final-state interaction amplitude. The results show sizeable on-shell FSI contributions for Bjorken $x \gtrsim 0.6$ and $Q^2 \lesssim 10$ GeV$^2$, increasing in magnitude for lower $Q^2$, but vanishing in the high-$Q^2$ limit due to phase space constraints. The off-shell rescattering contributes at $x \gtrsim 0.8$ and is taken as an uncertainty on the on-shell result.

## Key claim

Final-state interactions (FSI) in inclusive deep-inelastic scattering from the deuteron produce sizeable on-shell contributions for Bjorken \(x \gtrsim 0.6\) and \(Q^2 \lesssim 10\) GeV\(^2\), but these contributions vanish in the high-\(Q^2\) limit due to phase space constraints; off-shell rescattering contributes only at \(x \gtrsim 0.8\) and is treated as an uncertainty on the on-shell result.

## Method

The inclusive cross section was related to the deuteron forward virtual Compton scattering amplitude via the optical theorem, and the FSI contribution was derived in the generalized eikonal approximation using the diffractive nature of the effective hadron-nucleon interaction. A factorized model was employed with a basis of three resonances (invariant mass \(W < 2\) GeV) plus a continuum contribution for larger \(W\) as the set of effective hadron states entering the FSI amplitude. The calculation separated on-shell and off-shell rescattering contributions, with the off-shell part treated as an uncertainty.

## Key numbers

- On-shell FSI contributions are sizeable for Bjorken \(x \gtrsim 0.6\) and \(Q^2 \lesssim 10\) GeV\(^2\).
- Off-shell rescattering contributes at \(x \gtrsim 0.8\).
- The eikonal approximation is valid for hadron momenta \(\gtrsim 500\) MeV in the nucleon rest frame; this defines a limiting Bjorken \(x_\text{lim}\) that depends on \(Q^2\) and the invariant mass \(W\).
- For \(Q^2\) up to 5 GeV\(^2\), care is needed when \(x > 0.5\) for \(W > 2\) GeV entering the rescattering amplitude.

## Context

This work extends the semi-inclusive DIS approach of Cosyn et al. (2010, 2011), which accounted for FSI using diffractive scattering, to inclusive DIS from the deuteron. It builds on earlier impulse-approximation calculations of nuclear corrections (e.g., Frankfurt 1976, Kulagin 1994, Melnitchouk 1995, Sargsian 2001) and contrasts with the traditional Glauber model used by Ciofi degli Atti et al. (2003) and Palli et al. (2009) for SIDIS. The results enable more reliable extraction of neutron parton distribution functions at large \(x\) by quantifying the FSI corrections that must be subtracted from deuteron data.
