# Three flavour Quark matter in chiral colour dielectric model

> We investigate the properties of quark matter at finite density and temperature using the nonlinear chiral extension of Colour Dielectric Model (CCM). Assuming that the square of the meson fields devlop non- zero vacuum expectation value, the thermodynamic potential for interacting three flavour matter has been calculated. It is found that $<K^2>$ and $<η^2>$ remain zero in the medium whereas $<π^2>$ changes in the medium. As a result, $u$ and $d$ quark masses decrease monotonically as the temperature and density of the quark matter is increased.In the present model, the deconfinement density and temperature is found to be lower compared to lattice results. We also study the behaviour of pressure and energy density above critical temperature.

## Key claim

Using the nonlinear chiral colour dielectric model (CCDM) with the ansatz that the square of meson fields develops a nonzero vacuum expectation value, the authors find that only the pion field squared ($<\pi^2>$) becomes nonzero in the medium, while $<K^2>$ and $<\eta^2>$ remain zero; consequently, chiral symmetry restoration occurs only in the $SU(2) \times SU(2)$ sector (u and d quarks), not for strange quarks, and the deconfinement temperature and density are lower than lattice QCD results.

## Method

The thermodynamic potential for three-flavour quark matter at finite temperature and density was calculated in the mean-field approximation for the colour dielectric field $\chi$ and the meson field squared expectation values $F_\phi = <\phi^2>/f_\pi^2$, with quark-gluon and quark-meson interactions treated perturbatively up to second order. The mean-field values of $\chi$ and $F_\phi$ were determined by numerically minimizing the thermodynamic potential $\Omega(T, \mu)$ with respect to these fields. The calculation used a representative parameter set ($B^{1/4}=152$ MeV, $m_{u,d}=92$ MeV, $m_s=295$ MeV, $\alpha=36$, $g=1.002$) that reproduces baryon masses.

## Key numbers

* Deconfinement critical temperature for three-flavour asymmetric matter: approximately 110 MeV, compared to lattice QCD results around 150 MeV.
* Deconfinement critical temperature for two-flavour matter: approximately 115 MeV, higher than the three-flavour value.
* Deconfinement critical temperature for flavour-symmetric three-flavour matter: approximately 105 MeV, the lowest among the three cases.
* At zero temperature, pressure becomes negative for baryon density $n_B < 0.35$ fm$^{-3}$, defining the boundary of the quark phase.
* For the representative parameter set, $F_\pi$ reaches values of about 2 or more at $T=400$ MeV and $\mu=500$ MeV.
* Fitted high-temperature behaviour at $\mu=0$: $E/T^4$ approaches a value close to 15 (ideal gas value is 15.63 for three flavours), with fit parameters $a_0=14.38$, $a_1=644.15$, $a_2=-1.15\times10^5$, $a_3=0$, $a_4=9.22\times10^8$; $3P/T^4$ fit parameters $a_0=5.68$, $a_1=-292.12$, $a_2=27753.10$, $a_3=0$, $a_4=-7.699\times10^8$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier two-flavour calculation in the nonlinear chiral colour dielectric model (Ghosh and Phatak, 1992) to three flavours, and applies the model previously used for baryon structure and star properties (Ghosh and Sahu, 1993; Ghosh, Phatak and Sahu, 1994). The finding that chiral restoration occurs only in the $SU(2) \times SU(2)$ sector and is a smooth crossover (rather than first-order) differs from the linear sigma model results of Meyer-Ortmanns, Pirner and Patkos (1992), where the nature of the transition changes with pion mass. The lower deconfinement temperature compared to lattice QCD (Engels, Karsch and Montvay, 1982; Blaizot, 1994) is noted as a limitation of the model. The calculation enables further study of quark matter equations of state for neutron star applications and comparison with heavy-ion collision phenomenology.
