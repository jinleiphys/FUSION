# $F_K/F_π$ as a precision test of a new four flavor Domain Wall Fermion action

> We present a new set of lattice QCD ensembles with four flavors of smeared Möbius Domain Wall Fermions with good chiral symmetry and small fifth-dimensional extent. A modest amount of computing resources was sufficient to generate 30 publicly-available ensembles spanning five lattice spacings and a broad range of pion masses down to physical. To scrutinize our action we determine $F_{K^{\pm}}/F_{π^{\pm}} = 1.1962(34)$, a key quantity for precision CKM unitarity tests, heralding a future of inexpensive high-precision calculations of hadronic observables with chiral fermions.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a new four-flavor lattice QCD action using smeared Möbius Domain Wall Fermions can produce a high-precision result for $F_{K^\pm}/F_{\pi^\pm}$ consistent with the world average, but with a modest computational cost of approximately 40k Perlmutter-GPU node hours, suggesting that the action enables inexpensive high-precision calculations despite using very short fifth-dimensional extents (as small as 4 lattice units).

## Method

The authors generated 30 ensembles of $N_f=2+1+1$ dynamical fermions using a tree-level Symanzik-improved gauge action combined with the Möbius Domain Wall Fermion action and 8 iterations of Stout link smearing. They computed two-point correlation functions of pseudoscalar and temporal axial-vector operators using stochastic wall sources and the Truncated Solver Method, extracting $F_K/F_\pi$ from simultaneous fits to the correlators. The results were extrapolated to the continuum and chiral limits using next-to-next-to-leading order chiral perturbation theory with finite-volume and discretization corrections, employing a Bayesian model averaging over 16 fit forms.

## Key numbers

* $F_{K^\pm}/F_{\pi^\pm} = 1.1962(34)$ from the Bayesian model average, with statistical and systematic uncertainties combined
* 30 ensembles generated across 5 lattice spacings ($a \approx 0.05$ fm to $a \approx 0.12$ fm) with pion masses down to physical
* Residual mass $m_\text{res} \lesssim 1.5$ MeV, with values as small as $23(0) \times 10^{-5}$ in lattice units at $\beta=4.333$
* Fifth-dimensional extent $L_5$ as small as 4 lattice units at the finest lattice spacings ($\beta=4.238$ and $\beta=4.333$)
* Computational cost of approximately 40k Perlmutter-GPU node hours for ensemble generation and correlation functions

## Context

This work extends the long-standing RBC/UKQCD program of Domain Wall Fermion simulations (RBC-UKQCD 2008, 2010, 2012, 2014) to four dynamical flavors with a new action that achieves good chiral symmetry at very small fifth-dimensional extent. The result is consistent with the FLAG 2024 average and provides a third completely independent determination using a different lattice action from the HPQCD (Dowdall 2013, Bazavov 2017, Berkowitz 2017) and ETMC (2021) collaborations. Adding this result to the $N_f=2+1+1$ FLAG average reduces the uncertainty by 10%, and the authors plan additional ensembles at the physical pion mass with finer lattice spacings to match the precision of the HPQCD results, which would make theoretical uncertainty sub-dominant to experimental uncertainties in $|V_{us}|/|V_{ud}|$ determinations.
