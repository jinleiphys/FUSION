# Spin and Resonant States in QCD

> I make the case that the nucleon excitations do not exist as isolated higher spin states but are fully absorbed by (K/2,K/2)*[(1/2,0)+ (0,1/2)] multiplets taking their origin from the rotational and vibrational excitations of an underlying quark--diquark string. The Δ(1232) spectrum presents itself as the exact replica (up to Δ(1600)) of the nucleon spectrum with the K- clusters being shifted upward by about 200 MeV. QCD inspired arguments support legitimacy of the quark-diquark string. The above K multiplets can be mapped (up to form-factors) onto Lorentz group representation spaces of the type ψ_μ_1...μ_K, thus guaranteeing covariant description of resonant states. The quantum ψ_μ_1...μ_K states are of multiple spins at rest, and of undetermined spins elsewhere.

## Key claim

The paper argues that nucleon and Δ(1232) excitations do not exist as isolated higher-spin states but are instead fully absorbed into (K/2, K/2) ⊗ [(1/2,0) ⊕ (0,1/2)] multiplets originating from rotational and vibrational excitations of an underlying quark–diquark string, with the caveat that the Δ(1600) resonance appears to drop out of this systematics as a likely independent hybrid state.

## Method

The author applies the rovibron model (RVM), originally developed for diatomic molecules, to the quark–diquark (q-Dq) system. The relative q-Dq motion is described using four types of boson creation operators (s⁺ and p⁺_m) within a U(4) algebraic framework, and the Hamiltonian is constructed as a function of Casimir operators of the SO(4) dynamical symmetry limit. The resulting K-clusters (with K = N, N-2, ..., 1 or 0) are coupled to the spin-1/2 of the three quarks to generate sequences of half-integer spin states, with isospin attached pragmatically as I=1/2 for nucleon and I=3/2 for Δ states.

## Key numbers

- The Δ(1232) spectrum is an exact replica of the nucleon spectrum up to Δ(1600), with K-clusters shifted upward by about 200 MeV.
- Hamiltonian parameters: f₁ = 600 MeV, f₂^N = 70 MeV, f₂^Δ = 40 MeV.
- Predicted masses for observed nucleon K-clusters: K=0 at 939 MeV, K=1 at 1441 MeV (experimental 1498 MeV), K=3 at 1764 MeV (experimental 1689 MeV), K=5 at 2135 MeV (experimental 2102 MeV).
- Predicted masses for observed Δ K-clusters: K=0 at 1232 MeV, K=1 at 1712 MeV (experimental 1690 MeV), K=3 at 1944 MeV (experimental 1922 MeV), K=5 at 2165 MeV (experimental 2276 MeV).
- Predicted masses for "missing" nucleon clusters: K=2 at 1612 MeV, K=4 at 1935 MeV.
- Predicted masses for "missing" Δ clusters: K=2 at 1846 MeV, K=4 at 2048 MeV.
- Reported widths for K=3 nucleon resonances range from 0.10 to 0.15 GeV; for K=5, widths are 0.49–0.50 GeV.
- Helicity amplitude A^p_{3/2} for N(5/2⁺;1680) from K=3 is 133 ± 12 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/², compared to 166 ± 5 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/² for N(3/2⁻;1520) from K=1.

## Context

This work extends the quark–diquark model approach previously applied to ground-state baryons by Rein and Sehgal (1981) and Kusaka (1997), and builds on the rovibron model from molecular physics (Iachello and Levine, 1994). The author's earlier papers (Kirchbach, 1997–1998) first drew attention to the "Come-Together" of resonances of different spins and parities into narrow mass bands. The paper enables a systematic classification of both observed and "missing" baryon resonances into distinct U(4) and SO(4) representations, and suggests that helicity amplitudes can be used to extract the predicted "missing" states, potentially revealing a manifest mode of chiral symmetry in baryonic spectra if confirmed by TJNAF data.
