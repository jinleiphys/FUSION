# Equation for the Nakanishi weight function using the inverse Stieltjes transform

> The bound state Bethe-Salpeter amplitude was expressed by Nakanishi in terms of a smooth weight function g. By using the generalized Stieltjes transform, we derive an integral equation for the Nakanishi function g for a bound state case. It has the standard form g= Vg, where V is a two-dimensional integral operator. The prescription for obtaining the kernel V starting with the kernel K of the Bethe-Salpeter equation is given.

## Key claim

The paper derives an integral equation for the Nakanishi weight function \(g\) in the canonical form \(g = \hat{\mathcal V} g\) for an arbitrary Bethe-Salpeter kernel, generalizing a previous derivation that was restricted to the ladder kernel; the derivation relies on the analytic inversion of the generalized Stieltjes transform rather than on the uniqueness of the Nakanishi representation.

## Method

Starting from the previously established equation \(\hat{L} g = \hat{V} g\) for the Nakanishi weight function, the authors identify the left-hand-side integral as a generalized Stieltjes transform. They apply the known analytic inverse of the Stieltjes transform to both sides of the equation, obtaining the canonical form \(g = \hat{\mathcal V} g\) where the kernel \(\hat{\mathcal V} = \hat{L}^{-1} \hat{V}\) is given by a contour integral over the original kernel \(V\). The kernel \(V\) is expressed in terms of the Bethe-Salpeter kernel \(K\) following the prescription of Karmanov et al. (2008), and explicit expressions for \(\hat{\mathcal V}\) are provided for the one-boson-exchange ladder kernel.

## Key numbers

* The coupling constant is defined as \(g^2 = 16\pi m^2 \alpha\) for the one-boson-exchange kernel with mass \(\mu\).
* In the limit \(\mu \to 0\), the derived equation reduces exactly to the Wick-Cutkosky equation, with kernel \(\widetilde{V}(z,z') = \frac{\alpha m^2}{2\pi} \frac{1}{\left[m^2 - \frac{1}{4}(1-{z'}^2)M^2\right]}\) multiplied by factors \((1+z)/(1+z')\) for \(z<z'\) or \((1-z)/(1-z')\) for \(z>z'\).
* The Nakanishi functions \(g(\gamma,z)\) obtained from the new canonical equation and from the original equation \(\hat{L}g = \hat{V}g\) coincide with each other within numerical accuracy and also agree with Euclidean Bethe-Salpeter results for selected values of \(M\) and \(\mu\).

## Context

This work extends the derivation of the canonical equation for the Nakanishi weight function, previously obtained by Frederico et al. (2014) for the ladder kernel only, to an arbitrary kernel given by a set of irreducible Feynman graphs. It builds on the earlier formulation of the double integral equation for \(g\) by Karmanov et al. (2008) and on the analytic inversion of the Stieltjes transform from Schwinger (1950). The result enables the solution of the Bethe-Salpeter equation via the Nakanishi representation for a wider class of interactions, and the method of analytic inversion of integral transforms may be useful in other areas of nuclear and hadronic physics where numerical inversion is unstable, following the approach pioneered by Efros (1985).
