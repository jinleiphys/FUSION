# Quantum Computation for Jets in Heavy Ion Collisions

> Quantum computing has recently emerged as a transformative tool for investigating the real-time dynamics of jets in heavy-ion collisions, offering novel approaches to simulate non-equilibrium processes and strongly coupled phenomena that are challenging for classical methods. Here, I summarize my talk at Hard Probes 2024 at Nagasaki.

## Key claim

Quantum computing offers a transformative approach to simulating the real-time dynamics of jets in heavy-ion collisions, but the numerical demonstrations to date remain limited to small lattice spaces and are largely performed on classical simulators rather than actual quantum hardware.

## Method

The author reviews three quantum simulation frameworks for studying jets in heavy-ion collisions: lattice QFT approaches, open quantum system methods using Lindblad equations, and light-front Hamiltonian formalism. The light-front approach treats the jet as a fully quantum state governed by QCD dynamics with the medium represented as a classical background field, evolving via unitary time evolution operators. The method encodes Fock states onto qubits using direct encoding schemes, where fermionic and bosonic creation/annihilation operators are mapped to quantum gates via Jordan-Wigner or Bravyi-Kitaev encoding for fermions and binary/Gray/unary encoding for bosons.

## Key numbers

- Simulations using matrix product state simulators reached up to **128 qubits** for studying quark jet propagation through medium (Qian 2024)
- The jet quenching parameter $\hat{q}$ is extracted from transverse momentum broadening via $\hat{q}L_\eta \equiv \braket{\vec{P}^2_\perp; x^+=L_\eta} - \braket{\vec{P}^2_\perp; 0}$, starting from a $\braket{\vec{P}^2_\perp; 0}=0$ initial quark state
- Hilbert space dimension for Fock states scales polynomially as $K (2 N_\perp)^2$, where $K$ is the harmonic resolution in the longitudinal direction and $N_\perp$ the transverse cutoff
- Trotter method gate cost scales as $\mathcal{O}(t^{1+\frac{1}{p}}\epsilon^{-\frac{1}{p}})$ at $p$-th order with precision $\epsilon$

## Context

This work extends the line of quantum simulation for high-energy physics reviewed in Bauer (2022), DiMeglio (2023), and Banuls (2019). It builds on prior light-front Hamiltonian approaches including the Discretized Light-Cone Quantization framework of Brodsky (1997) and Basis Light-Front Quantization. The paper summarizes progress from initial demonstrations of jet quenching parameter computation (Barata 2021, 2022) through multi-Fock sector studies (Barata 2023, Yao 2022) to unified direct encoding schemes reaching three gluons (Qian 2024). Future work should extend to more realistic scenarios including medium anisotropy, initial stage effects, and particle multiplicity.
