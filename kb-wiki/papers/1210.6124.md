# Finite-volume corrections to charge radii

> The finite-volume nature of lattice QCD entails a variety of effects that must be handled in the process of performing chiral extrapolations. Since the pion cloud that surrounds hadrons becomes distorted in a finite volume, hadronic observables must be corrected before one can compare with the experimental values. The electric charge radius of the nucleon is of particular interest when considering the implementation of finite-volume corrections. It is common practice in the literature to transform electric form factors from the lattice into charge radii prior to analysis. However, there is a fundamental difficulty with using these charge radii in a finite-volume extrapolation. The subtleties are a consequence of the absence of a continuous derivative on the lattice. A procedure is outlined for handling such finite-volume corrections, which must be applied directly to the electric form factors themselves rather than to the charge radii.

## Key claim

The paper argues that finite-volume corrections to nucleon charge radii cannot be applied directly to the radii themselves because the derivative needed to define the radius from the electric form factor is not well-defined on the lattice, where only discrete momentum values are available. Instead, the corrections must be applied to the electric form factors at finite momentum transfer before converting to a charge radius using an extrapolation in \(Q^2\).

## Method

The authors use heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (\(\chi\)PT) to compute the leading-order pion-loop, Delta-resonance, and tadpole contributions to the nucleon electric form factor. They evaluate the finite-volume correction as the difference between the discrete sum over lattice momenta and the continuum integral for each loop diagram, applied directly to the form factor \(G_E(Q^2)\) at the smallest allowed momentum transfer. The corrected form factors are then converted to charge radii using a phenomenological \(Q^2\) parametrization, such as the dipole form or the inverse-quadratic form of Kelly (2004) and Collins et al. (2011).

## Key numbers

- The minimum momentum transfer on a lattice of size \(L = 3\) fm is approximately \((0.58\ \text{GeV})^2\).
- The pion decay constant used is \(f_\pi = 92.4\ \text{MeV}\).
- The chiral couplings are estimated from SU(6) flavor-symmetry relations: \(D = 0.76\), \(F = 2D/3\), and \(\mathcal{C} = -2D\).
- The experimental value of the isovector nucleon electric charge radius is \(\langle r^2\rangle_E^{\text{isov}} = 0.88\ \text{fm}^2\) (Mohr 2008, Nakamura 2010).
- Lattice results from the QCDSF Collaboration (Collins 2011) for the isovector charge radius are corrected to infinite volume; the box sizes for the points range from \(1.7\) to \(2.9\) fm, satisfying \(m_\pi L > 3\).

## Context

This work extends the program of applying chiral effective field theory to correct lattice QCD results for finite-volume effects, as previously developed by Gasser (1987), Leinweber (2003, 2004), Young (2004), Beane (2004), and Hall (2012). It specifically addresses the subtlety that the charge radius, defined as the slope of the form factor at zero momentum transfer, cannot be directly corrected in a finite volume because the derivative is ill-defined on the lattice. The method enables future continuum \(\chi\)PT extrapolations of charge radii in quark mass, and the authors note that twisted boundary conditions (Jiang 2008) can provide access to non-integer momentum states, though with additional care required for finite-volume corrections.
