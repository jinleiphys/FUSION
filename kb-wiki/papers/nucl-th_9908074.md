# Nuclear Transparency and Effective NN Cross Section in Heavy Ion Collisions at 14.6 GeV/nucleon

> The probability of a projectile nucleon to traverse a target nucleus without interaction is calculated for central Si-Cu and Si-Pb collisions. Special attention is given to the impact parameter range which contributes to events with large transverse energy. A fit to the data from E814 requires an effective NN cross section of sigma_eff=54.2+-5.0mb (compared to the free space value sigma_in=30mb) and is interpreted as one related to wounded target nucleons.

## Key claim
The effective nucleon-nucleon cross section extracted from central Si-Cu and Si-Pb collisions at 14.6 GeV/nucleon is significantly larger than the free-space inelastic NN cross section, but only when the finite width of the impact-parameter distribution for events with a given transverse energy is properly accounted for; the naive assignment of b=0 to central events yields no anomaly.

## Method
The survival probability S(b,σ_eff) for a projectile nucleon to traverse the target without inelastic interaction was calculated using a Glauber-type approximation with straight-line trajectories and frozen nucleons, with Saxon-Woods densities. The relation between transverse energy E_t and impact parameter b was modeled via a Gaussian probability distribution P(E_t,b), with parameters ε_0 and ω fitted to the measured dσ_in/dE_t distributions for Si-Pb collisions. The effective NN cross section σ_eff was then extracted by solving S(E_t^c,σ_eff) = S_exp(E_t^c) for central events, using both the collision model and wounded nucleon model for N(b).

## Key numbers
- Extracted effective NN cross section for central Si-Pb collisions: σ_eff = 54.2 ± 5.0 ± 6.4 ± 5.6 mb (errors from statistics, ε_0 uncertainty, and ω uncertainty respectively)
- Extracted effective NN cross section for central Si-Cu collisions: σ_eff = 50.3 ± 15.0 ± 4.6 ± 6.4 mb
- Free-space inelastic NN cross section: σ_in^NN = 30 mb
- Experimental survival probability for central Si-Pb collisions: S_exp = 3.5 × 10^{-3} at E_t^c = 15.5 GeV
- Ratio S(E_t^c,σ_eff)/S(b=0,σ_eff) = 2.86 for σ_eff = 30 mb
- Fit parameters for collision model: ε_0 = 0.067 GeV, ω = 7.8
- Fit parameters for wounded nucleon model: ε_0 = 0.1 GeV, ω = 6.0
- Estimated time between first and last projectile nucleons: Δt ≈ 0.5 fm/c (Lorentz factor γ_p ≈ 15.6)
- Estimated intrinsic excitation energy uncertainty: ΔE ≥ 0.4 GeV

## Context
This work reanalyzes E814 collaboration data (1990s) on beam-rapidity nucleons in Si-Al, Si-Cu, and Si-Pb collisions at 14.6 GeV/nucleon. It contradicts the E814 collaboration's own conclusion that σ_eff equals the free-space value, by showing that the impact-parameter distribution for central events has a width that invalidates the b=0 assumption. The result also differs from the ARC cascade calculation by Schlagel et al., which found agreement with data without invoking wounded nucleons. The extracted σ_eff ≈ 54 mb is interpreted in terms of wounded target nucleons, with a semi-quantitative estimate using the additive quark model and a prompt gluon bremsstrahlung picture giving σ_in^{NN+} ≈ 50 mb. The work enables future studies of nuclear transparency and in-medium modifications of the NN cross section in heavy-ion collisions.
