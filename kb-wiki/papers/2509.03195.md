# Confronting the production mechanisms of nuclei with deuteron and proton-triggered balance functions

> In ultra high-energy collisions, nuclei with very low binding energies are not expected to survive the dense and hot final state environment. The traditional view has therefore been that nuclei form via coalescence after the hot environment has dissipated. However, statistical thermal models, where hadrons are produced from a fireball at thermal equilibrium, can describe the relative abundances of light nuclei in pp and heavy-ion collisions at the LHC equally well. In this paper we investigate if balance functions triggered by protons and deuterons can be used to distinguish between the two production mechanisms. The coalescence model is investigated using PYTHIA, while the statistical thermal model is examined using the Thermal FIST package. We find that for both models the same simple relation between proton and deuteron triggered balance functions is applicable. However, there is a striking difference between the two models when the transverse momentum of trigger particles is varied. This dependence offers a promising observable to discriminate between the two production scenarios that goes beyond nuclei production. Furthermore, we find that deuteron-meson balance functions vanish identically for both models due to baryon number conservation and isospin symmetry.

## Key claim

The authors show that while both the coalescence model (PYTHIA) and the statistical thermal model (Thermal FIST) predict the same simple relation between proton and deuteron triggered balance functions, a striking difference emerges when the transverse momentum of trigger particles is varied, offering a promising observable to discriminate between the two production scenarios.

## Method

The authors computed balance functions for triggered protons and deuterons in proton-proton collisions at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV using two models: PYTHIA 8.3 with the Monash tune and deuteron coalescence enabled, and Thermal FIST with a Blast-Wave boost tuned to ALICE data. Balance functions were defined as the difference between opposite-baryon-number and same-baryon-number associated yields as a function of rapidity difference Delta y, with trigger particles selected in pseudorapidity |eta| < 0.8 and associated particles having no acceptance restriction. The transverse momentum dependence was studied by varying the trigger pT intervals, and multiplicity dependence was examined using charged-particle multiplicity classes.

## Key numbers

- Trigger protons selected in transverse momentum range 0.5-4 GeV/c; trigger deuterons selected in range 1-8 GeV/c (twice the proton range, following coalescence picture)
- Thermal FIST default correlation volume Vc = 3 dV/dy; variations studied at 1 dV/dy, 1.6 dV/dy, and 4.8 dV/dy
- Thermal FIST default temperature T = 176 MeV; variation studied at 156 MeV
- PYTHIA multiplicity classes defined by charged particle counts Nch_mid in ranges 0-20, 20-40, and above 40 at mid-rapidity |eta| < 0.8
- Thermal FIST multiplicity classes correspond to mean dNch/d eta values of 4.5, 10, and 20

## Context

This work extends recent phenomenological studies of balance functions in pp collisions, particularly Xi-triggered balance functions measured by ALICE (2023) and studied by Pruneau (2024) and Bierlich (2025). The paper directly confronts the "nuclei puzzle" where both coalescence models (Sato 1981, Nagle 1996, Blum 2017) and statistical thermal models (SHM references) can describe light nuclei yields at the LHC. The proposed observable could discriminate between these mechanisms using existing LHC Run 3 data and may provide insight into hadronization mechanisms beyond nuclei production, including the question of deconfinement.
