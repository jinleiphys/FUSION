# Contact interaction analysis of octet baryon axialvector and pseudoscalar form factors

> Octet baryon axial, induced pseudoscalar, and pseudoscalar form factors are computed using a symmetry-preserving treatment of a vector$\,\times\,$vector contact interaction (SCI), thereby unifying them with an array of other baryon properties and analogous treatments of semileptonic decays of pseudoscalar mesons. The baryons are treated as quark--plus--interacting-diquark bound states, whose structure is obtained by solving a Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation. The approach is marked by algebraic simplicity, involves no free parameters, and since it is symmetry preserving, all consequences of partial conservation of the axial current are manifest. It is found that SCI results are consistent with only small violations of SU$(3)$-flavour symmetry, an outcome which may be understood as a dynamical consequence of emergent hadron mass. The spin-flavour structure of the Poincaré-covariant baryon wave functions is expressed in the presence of both flavour-antitriplet scalar diquarks and flavour-sextet axialvector diquarks and plays a key role in determining all form factors. Considering neutral axial currents, SCI predictions for the flavour separation of octet baryon axial charges and, therefrom, values for the associated SU$(3)$ singlet, triplet, and octet axial charges are obtained. The results indicate that at the hadron scale, $ζ_{\cal H}$, valence degrees-of-freedom carry roughly 50% of an octet baryon's total spin. Since there are no other degrees-of-freedom at $ζ_{\cal H}$, the remainder may be associated with quark+diquark orbital angular momentum.

## Key claim
The paper claims that a symmetry-preserving treatment of a vector×vector contact interaction (SCI) yields octet baryon axial, induced pseudoscalar, and pseudoscalar form factors that are consistent with only small violations of SU(3)-flavour symmetry, with the hedge that this near-symmetry is not a direct consequence of any basic symmetry but rather a dynamical outcome of emergent hadron mass (EHM) which strongly suppresses the expression of the Higgs-generated s-quark to light-quark mass ratio of approximately 20.

## Method
The authors solved a Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation treating baryons as quark-plus-interacting-diquark bound states, including both flavour-antitriplet scalar diquarks and flavour-sextet axialvector diquarks. They employed a symmetry-preserving formulation of a vector×vector contact interaction (SCI) to compute all propagators, diquark correlation amplitudes, and vertices appearing in the Faddeev equation and the axial/pseudoscalar current diagrams, ensuring that all consequences of partial conservation of the axial current (PCAC) are manifest. The approach involves no free parameters and unifies octet baryon axialvector transitions with an array of other baryon properties and analogous treatments of pseudoscalar meson semileptonic decays.

## Context
This work extends continuum Schwinger function methods (CSMs) previously applied to nucleon axial and pseudoscalar form factors by Chen (2020, 2021) and ChenChen (2022) to the full octet baryon sector, using the simpler SCI framework to unify these calculations with earlier studies of baryon properties by Wilson (2011), Xu (2015), Yin (2021), and Raya (2021), as well as semileptonic decays of pseudoscalar mesons by Chen (2012), Xu (2021), and Xing (2022). The results complement lattice QCD computations by Erkol (2009) and Bali (2022), covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory by Ledwig (2014), and Lorentz covariant quark model calculations by Faessler (2008). The approach enables parameter-free predictions for all six distinct charged-current semileptonic transitions between octet baryons and provides a means to check algorithms used in more computationally intensive QCD-kindred calculations.
