# Model-independent low momentum nucleon interaction from phase shift equivalence

> We present detailed results for the model-independent low momentum nucleon-nucleon interaction V_{low k}. By introducing a cutoff in momentum space, we separate the Hilbert space into a low momentum and a high momentum part. The renormalization group is used to construct the effective interaction V_{low k} in the low momentum space, starting from various high precision potential models commonly used in nuclear many-body calculations. With a cutoff in the range of $Λ~ 2.1 fm^{-1}$, the new potential V_{low k} is independent of the input model, and reproduces the experimental phase shift data for corresponding laboratory energies below $E_{lab} ~ 350 MeV$, as well as the deuteron binding energy with similar accuracy as the realistic input potentials. The model independence of V_{low k} demonstrates that the physics of nucleons interacting at low momenta does not depend on details of the high momentum dynamics assumed in conventional potential models. V_{low k} does not have momentum components larger than the cutoff, and as a consequence is considerably softer than the high precision potentials. Therefore, when V_{low k} is used as microscopic input in the many-body problem, the high momentum effects in the particle-particle channel do not have to be addressed by performing a Brueckner ladder resummation or short-range correlation methods. By varying the cutoff, we study how the model independence of V_{low k} is reached in different partial waves. This provides numerical evidence for the separation of scales in the nuclear problem, and physical insight into the nature of the low momentum interaction.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a model-independent low-momentum nucleon-nucleon interaction \(V_{\text{low k}}\) can be constructed by renormalization group decimation of various high-precision potential models, provided a momentum cutoff \(\Lambda \sim 2.1\,\text{fm}^{-1}\) is imposed; the resulting interaction reproduces experimental phase shifts for laboratory energies below \(E_{\text{lab}} \sim 350\,\text{MeV}\) and the deuteron binding energy, but the model independence holds only up to that cutoff scale, which corresponds to the momentum range constrained by scattering data.

## Method

The authors start from several high-precision nucleon-nucleon potential models (Paris, Bonn, CD-Bonn, Nijmegen, Argonne \(v_{18}\), and the Idaho chiral potential) and impose a momentum-space cutoff \(\Lambda\) to separate the Hilbert space into low-momentum (\(P\)-space) and high-momentum (\(Q\)-space) parts. They use the Lee-Suzuki similarity transformation method (equivalent to solving a renormalization group equation for half-on-shell \(T\)-matrices) to integrate out the high-momentum modes, producing an energy-independent effective interaction \(V_{\text{low k}}\) in the low-momentum space. A subsequent Okubo transformation yields a hermitian version \(\overline{V}_{\text{low k}}\). The procedure is applied to all partial waves with total angular momentum \(J \leq 4\).

## Key numbers

- Cutoff scale: \(\Lambda \sim 2.1\,\text{fm}^{-1}\), corresponding to the laboratory energy \(E_{\text{lab}} \sim 350\,\text{MeV}\) up which scattering data constrain the potentials.
- The \(^1\)S\(_0\) scattering length used in the schematic model: \(a_s = -23.73\,\text{fm}\); effective range: \(r_0 = 2.70\,\text{fm}\).
- In the schematic model, the zero-crossing of the phase shift occurs at relative momentum \(\kappa = 1.79\,\text{fm}^{-1}\).
- The \(Q\)-space matrix inversion for the iterative Andreozzi algorithm uses approximately 60 Gauss-Legendre mesh points extending to about \(25\,\text{fm}^{-1}\).
- The separable model parameters: Model I (\(n=1/2\), \(\alpha=1.8199\), \(g=3.1429\,\text{fm}^{(1-4n)/2}\), \(m=1.0962\,\text{fm}^{-1}\)); Model II (\(n=1\), \(\alpha=4.5354\), \(g=3.3542\,\text{fm}^{(1-4n)/2}\), \(m=1.3892\,\text{fm}^{-1}\)).

## Context

This work extends the renormalization group approach to nucleon-nucleon interactions previously introduced by the same authors (Bogner, Kuo, Schwenk, 2001, 2002) and provides a systematic demonstration of model independence across multiple partial waves. It contrasts with the traditional use of bare high-precision potentials (such as the Argonne \(v_{18}\) potential) in many-body calculations, where Brueckner ladder resummations or short-range correlation methods are needed to handle high-momentum components. The resulting \(V_{\text{low k}}\) enables direct use in many-body calculations without such resummations, and provides numerical evidence for the separation of scales in nuclear physics, complementing effective field theory approaches (Weinberg, 1990, 1991; van Kolck, 1994; Kaplan, Savage, Wise, 1998; Epelbaum, Gloeckle, Meissner, 2000; Entem, Machleidt, 2002).
