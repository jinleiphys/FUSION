# The equilibrium and dynamical cumulants of QCD chiral order parameter with parametric Landau free energy

> By linearly parameterizing the QCD Landau free energy near the critical point in the baryon chemical potential and temperature plane, we study the fluctuations of the QCD chiral order parameter field (the $σ$ field) in the equilibrium case and dynamical phase transition, respectively. By setting the system size to the typical size of the QGP fireball ($\approx 10^3$ fm$^3$), we show that in the equilibrium case, the discontinuity of the order parameter in the first order phase transition region is replaced by smooth crossover, and the corresponding fluctuations are broadened. Meanwhile, the quartic cumulant $κ_4$ of the $σ$ field is generally negative near the phase transition line. We further derive the dynamical evolution of the QCD Landau free energy in the Fokker-Plank framework, based on which we deduce the dynamical cumulants of the $σ$ field. Assuming the temperature decreases as a known function of time, we numerically evaluate the dynamical cumulants and confirm that the cumulants present clear memory effects. Moreover, the memory effects on the first order phase transition side is stronger than that on the crossover side, and the dynamical cumulants at the hypothetical freeze-out line present rich non-monotonic structures.

# The equilibrium and dynamical cumulants of QCD chiral order parameter with parametric Landau free energy

## Key claim

By linearly parameterizing the QCD Landau free energy near the critical point, the authors show that for a finite system volume typical of the QGP fireball (approximately 10^3 fm^3), the quartic cumulant κ₄ of the σ field is generally negative near the phase transition line on both the crossover and first-order sides, and that dynamical cumulants at a hypothetical freeze-out line present rich non-monotonic structures due to memory effects that are stronger on the first-order phase transition side than on the crossover side.

## Method

The authors linearly parameterize the coefficients of a Landau free energy density expanded to fourth order in the σ field, mapping it to the Ising free energy via translation to eliminate the cubic term. They compute equilibrium cumulants by numerical integration of the probability distribution P[σ] ∝ exp{-Ω[σ]V/T} for a fixed volume V = 10^3 fm^3, then derive and numerically solve coupled Fokker-Planck equations for the dynamical free energy coefficients under a prescribed temperature cooling function T(t) = T_ini ( (t + t_ref)/t_ref )^{-λ} with λ = 0.45.

## Key numbers

- System volume set to V = 10^3 fm^3, the typical size of the QGP fireball.
- Critical point parameters: (μ_c, T_c) = (240, 170) MeV; σ_c = 50 MeV.
- Parameter values: d₁ = 3 × 10^4 MeV², d₂ = 400 MeV, d₄ = 15; sinθ_b' = -cosθ_b = 0.99, cosθ_b' = sinθ_b = 0.141.
- Temperature cooling function parameters: t_ref = 10 fm, λ = 0.45.
- Initial temperature T_ini = 185 MeV for dynamical evolution; initial and freeze-out temperatures set approximately 5.05 MeV above and 3.37 MeV below the phase transition temperature respectively (η₁ = 1.5 × 10^5 MeV³ initial, η₁ = -1.0 × 10^5 MeV³ final).
- Relaxation rates studied: τ_rel = 0.05, 0.1, and 0.2 fm.
- Dynamical critical exponent z = 3 (Model H).

## Context

This work extends the study of critical fluctuations in QCD phase transitions beyond the thermodynamic limit by incorporating finite-size effects at the typical QGP fireball volume, building on the equilibrium cumulant framework of Stephanov (2008) and the dynamical Fokker-Planck approach of Mukherjee (2015). The parametric Landau free energy provides an alternative to the Ising mapping used by Guida (1997), Berdnikov (1999), Nonaka (2005), and Stephanov (2011), enabling unified treatment of both crossover and first-order phase transition regions. The results enable more realistic comparisons with STAR collaboration data on net-proton cumulants (Adamczyk 2013, Luo 2015, STAR 2020) and can be combined with chiral hydrodynamics approaches (Paech 2003, Nahrgang 2011, Herold 2013) and hydro+ (Stephanov 2017, Rajagopal 2019) for complete dynamical modeling of heavy-ion collisions.
