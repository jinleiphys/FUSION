# Weighted azimuthal asymmetries in a diquark spectator model

> We analytically calculate weighted azimuthal asymmetries in semi-inclusive lepton-nucleon deep-inelastic scattering and Drell-Yan processes, using transverse-momentum-dependent partonic densities obtained in a diquark spectator model. We compare the asymmetries with available preliminary experimental data, in particular for the Collins and the Sivers effect. We make predictions for other cases of interest in running and planned experiments.

## Key claim

The paper analytically calculates weighted azimuthal asymmetries in semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (SIDIS) and Drell-Yan processes using transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) partonic densities from a diquark spectator model, but the comparison with experimental data is limited to preliminary results and the model's T-odd functions are resized by using a running strong coupling constant at leading order rather than an ad-hoc nonperturbative value.

## Method

The authors computed all leading-twist T-even and T-odd TMDs in a spectator model of the nucleon with scalar and axial-vector diquarks, distinguishing isospin projections (ud and uu). T-odd functions (Sivers, Boer-Mulders, etc.) were generated by approximating the gauge-link operator with a single gluon-exchange interaction at leading order in the strong coupling constant. Weighted azimuthal asymmetries were then constructed analytically from these TMDs, breaking the convolutions in a model-independent way, and compared with preliminary HERMES data for the Collins and Sivers effects.

## Key numbers

- The strong coupling constant at the model scale was computed using renormalization-group equations at leading order (LO), resizing all T-odd functions by a global constant compared to the previous ad-hoc nonperturbative value.
- The double-spin asymmetry \(A_{LL}\) in SIDIS is given by \(A_{LL} = \frac{C(x,y)}{A(x,y)} \frac{\sum_a e_a^2 x g_1^a(x) D_1^a(z)}{\sum_a e_a^2 x f_1^a(x) D_1^a(z)}\), where \(C(x,y)\) and \(A(x,y)\) are kinematic factors defined in the text.
- The weighted Sivers asymmetry in SIDIS is \(A_{UT}^{Q_T \sin(\phi_h-\phi_S)} = -2 \frac{\sum_a e_a^2 x f_{1T}^{\perp (1)a}(x) D_1^a(z)}{\sum_a e_a^2 x f_1^a(x) D_1^a(z)}\).
- The weighted Collins asymmetry in SIDIS is \(A_{UT}^{Q_T \sin(\phi_h+\phi_S)} = 2 \frac{B(x,y)}{A(x,y)} \frac{\sum_a e_a^2 x h_1^a(x) H_1^{\perp (1)a}(z)}{\sum_a e_a^2 x f_1^a(x) D_1^a(z)}\).
- For Drell-Yan, the double-spin asymmetry \(\tilde{A}_{TT}\) gives access to transversity: \(\tilde{A}_{TT} = |\bm{S}_{1T}| |\bm{S}_{2T}| \tilde{a}_{TT} \frac{\sum_a e_a^2 x_1 h_1^{\bar a}(x_1) x_2 h_1^a(x_2)}{\sum_a e_a^2 x_1 f_1^{\bar a}(x_1) x_2 f_1^a(x_2)}\).
- The weighted Sivers asymmetry in Drell-Yan is \(\tilde{A}_{UT}^{q_T \sin(\phi-\phi_{S_2})} = 2 \frac{\sum_a e_a^2 x_1 f_1^{\bar a}(x_1) x_2 f_{1T}^{\perp (1)a}(x_2)}{\sum_a e_a^2 x_1 f_1^{\bar a}(x_1) x_2 f_1^a(x_2)}\).

## Context

This work extends the diquark spectator model of Bacchetta et al. (2008) by computing weighted azimuthal asymmetries rather than unweighted ones, which are simpler theoretically because they avoid convolutions and allow analytic results without assuming Gaussian transverse-momentum distributions. The predictions are relevant for ongoing and planned experiments: the Collins and Sivers effects on a transversely polarized 3He target at JLab (E06-010), the Drell-Yan process with antiprotons at FAIR (GSI), and single-spin asymmetry measurements with a pion beam at COMPASS (CERN). The paper also notes that the evolution of the Sivers function's first moment is known from Kang (2008), Vogelsang (2009), and Braun (2009), but the authors approximate evolution using only the diagonal part, which should be dominant at high x.
