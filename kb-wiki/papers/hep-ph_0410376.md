# KN and KbarN Elastic Scattering in the Quark Potential Model

> The KN and KbarN low-energy elastic scattering is consistently studied in the framework of the QCD-inspired quark potential model. The model is composed of the t-channel one-gluon exchange potential, the s-channel one-gluon exchange potential and the harmonic oscillator confinement potential. By means of the resonating group method, nonlocal effective interaction potentials for the KN and KbarN systems are derived and used to calculate the KN and KbarN elastic scattering phase shifts. By considering the effect of QCD renormalization, the contribution of the color octet of the clusters (qqbar) and (qqq) and the suppression of the spin-orbital coupling, the numerical results are in fairly good agreement with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a QCD-inspired quark potential model incorporating both t-channel and s-channel one-gluon exchange potentials, QCD renormalization effects, and color octet contributions from quark clusters can consistently describe both KN and KbarN low-energy elastic scattering phase shifts, with the condition that the spin-orbital coupling effect in the t-channel OGEP must be suppressed to reproduce P-wave phase shifts.

## Method

The authors constructed a quark potential model combining t-channel one-gluon exchange potential (OGEP), s-channel OGEP, and a harmonic oscillator confinement potential. Using the resonating group method (RGM), they derived nonlocal effective interaction potentials for the KN and KbarN systems and calculated elastic scattering phase shifts in the Born approximation. The model incorporated QCD renormalization effects by replacing the coupling constant and quark masses with effective running quantities, included contributions from color octet states of the quark clusters, and employed a suppression mechanism for the spin-orbital coupling term.

## Key numbers

- The effective fine structure constant is given by alpha_R(lambda) = alpha_R^0 / [1 + (alpha_R^0 / 2pi) G(lambda)], where G(lambda) has different forms for time-like and space-like momentum subtraction, with N_f = 3 quark flavors used in the calculation
- The suppression parameter gamma = 0.3 was tested but found to give worse P-wave phase shifts, requiring a larger value of gamma to achieve better agreement with experimental data
- The harmonic oscillator size parameter b appears in the wave function normalization factor (3*beta_2 / (pi * b^2))^(3/4) and in the exponential factor exp(b^2 * k^2 / (6 * beta_2)) in the transition matrix

## Context

This work extends previous quark potential model studies of KN scattering by authors such as those in Ref.[10] (S-wave phase shifts with only Coulomb, spin-spin and Darwin terms), Ref.[11] (P-wave phase shifts with spin-orbital coupling), and Ref.[18] (constituent quark model with Hill-Wheeler equation). The paper is the first to consistently study both KN and KbarN elastic scattering within the same quark potential model framework, incorporating s-channel OGEP for the annihilation channels present in KbarN interactions, building on the authors' previous investigations of pi pi and K Kbar interactions (Refs.[19,20]). The inclusion of QCD renormalization effects and color octet contributions represents new physical ingredients beyond prior approaches.
