# Charged-current quasielastic scattering of muon antineutrino and neutrino in the MINERvA experiment

> One of the largest sources of systematic uncertainties in ongoing neutrino-oscillation measurements is the description of nuclear effects. Its considerable reduction is expected thanks to the dedicated studies of (anti)neutrino-nucleus interactions in the MINERvA experiment. In this article, the calculations within the spectral function approach are compared to the charged-current quasielastic cross sections reported from MINERvA. The obtained results show that the effect of final-state interactions on the (anti)muon kinematics plays pivotal role in reproducing the experimental data.

# Charged-current quasielastic scattering of muon antineutrino and neutrino in the MINERvA experiment

## Key claim

The spectral function approach can reproduce the MINERvA charged-current quasielastic cross sections for both muon antineutrinos and neutrinos only when final-state interactions on the muon kinematics are included; without these effects, the description fails. The effective axial mass extracted from fits to the data is consistently around 1.16 GeV, higher than the deuteron-based value of 1.03 GeV, indicating a nonvanishing contribution from two-body currents.

## Method

The author calculated charged-current quasielastic cross sections for muon antineutrinos and neutrinos on a hydrocarbon target using the spectral function approach, which describes the carbon nucleus ground state via hole spectral functions accounting for shell structure and nucleon-nucleon correlations, and includes final-state interactions via the correlated Glauber approximation and a Dirac phenomenological optical potential. The cross sections were averaged over the MINERvA fluxes and expressed as a function of reconstructed four-momentum transfer squared, with the axial mass treated as a free parameter to accommodate two-body current contributions in an effective manner. Fits to the MINERvA data were performed including correlations between data points and theoretical uncertainties from the optical potential parametrization and low-excitation energy contributions.

## Key numbers

- Effective axial mass from combined fit including theoretical uncertainties: \(M_A = 1.16 \pm 0.06\) GeV, with \(\chi^2/\)d.o.f. = 0.93
- Effective axial mass from antineutrino fit including theoretical uncertainties: \(M_A = 1.16 \pm 0.06\) GeV, with \(\chi^2/\)d.o.f. = 0.38
- Effective axial mass from neutrino fit including theoretical uncertainties: \(M_A = 1.17 \pm 0.06\) GeV, with \(\chi^2/\)d.o.f. = 1.33
- When final-state interactions are neglected with \(M_A = 1.16\) GeV, the combined fit yields \(\chi^2/\)d.o.f. = 2.42
- Half of the MINERvA data correspond to reconstructed four-momentum transfer squared \(\qrec \leq 0.2\) GeV\(^2\)
- The deuteron-based axial mass value is 1.03 GeV, used as reference for comparison

## Context

This work extends the spectral function approach of Benhar et al. (Ref. FSI in the paper), which had previously shown agreement with a large body of electron-carbon scattering data, to the MINERvA neutrino and antineutrino measurements. It addresses the failure of five theoretical calculations compared in the original MINERvA papers to simultaneously describe both antineutrino and neutrino cross sections, and finds consistency with results from the relativistic Green's function model (Ref. MINERvA_RGF in the paper), which uses the same optical potential. The findings enable improved modeling of nuclear effects for neutrino oscillation experiments, and the extracted effective axial mass agrees with the MiniBooNE Collaboration result (Ref. MiniBooNE_kappa in the paper), suggesting a non-negligible role of two-body reaction mechanisms at MINERvA kinematics.
