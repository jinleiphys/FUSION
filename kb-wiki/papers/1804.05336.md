# Hadronic effects on the $cc\bar{q}\bar{q}$ tetraquark state in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We study the hadronic effects on the $cc\bar{q}\bar{q}$ tetraquark state by focusing on the $T_{cc}(1^+)$ meson during the hadronic stage of relativistic heavy ion collisions. We evaluate the absorption cross section of the $T_{cc}$ meson by pions in the quasi-free approximation, and investigate the time evolution of the $T_{cc}$ abundance in the hadronic medium based on the effective volume and temperature of the hadronic phase at both RHIC and LHC modelled by hydrodynamic calculations with the lattice equation of state. We probe two possible scenarios for the structure of $T_{cc}$, where it is assumed to be either a compact multiquark state or a larger sized molecular configuration composed of DD*. Our numerical results suggest that the hadronic effects on the $T_{cc}$ production is insignificant, and its final abundance depends on the initial yield of $T_{cc}$ produced from the quark-gluon plasma phase, which will depend on the assumed structure of the state.

## Key claim

The hadronic effects on the $T_{cc}(1^+)$ tetraquark during the hadronic stage of relativistic heavy ion collisions are insignificant, meaning the final abundance depends primarily on the initial yield from the quark-gluon plasma phase and the assumed structure of the state (compact multiquark versus molecular configuration), rather than on later interactions with pions.

## Method

The authors evaluated the absorption cross section of the $T_{cc}$ meson by pions using the quasifree approximation, where the dissociation process $T_{cc}+\pi\rightarrow D+D^*+\pi$ is approximated by the elastic scattering processes $D+\pi\rightarrow D+\pi$ and $D^*+\pi\rightarrow D^*+\pi$ with an effective Lagrangian. They then solved a rate equation for the time evolution of the $T_{cc}$ abundance during the hadronic phase, using parameterized temperature and volume profiles from hydrodynamic calculations (both ideal and viscous) with a lattice equation of state for RHIC and LHC conditions.

## Key numbers

* The initial yield of $T_{cc}$ at hadronization for the molecular configuration is $2.0\times10^{-3}$ at LHC and $5.1\times10^{-4}$ at RHIC; for the compact multiquark configuration it is $1.1\times10^{-4}$ at LHC and $5.0\times10^{-5}$ at RHIC.
* The absorption rate of the $T_{cc}$ by pions is approximately $0.06$ c/fm, based on a thermally averaged cross section $\langle\sigma_{D\pi\rightarrow D\pi}v\rangle(\tau)\sim 6$ mb and a pion density $n_\pi(\tau)\sim 0.1$ fm$^{-3}$.
* The typical lifetime of the hadronic phase is about 10 fm/c, which alone would lead to about 45% reduction of the $T_{cc}$ abundance if only absorption were considered.
* The coupling constant $g_{\pi DD^*}\simeq 7.8$ was determined from the $D^*\rightarrow D\pi$ decay width of 83.4 keV.
* The hadronization temperature $T_H$ is 156 MeV for LHC and 162 MeV for RHIC; the kinetic freeze-out temperature $T_F$ is 115 MeV for LHC and 119 MeV for RHIC.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hadronic effects on exotic candidates such as the $D_{sJ}(2317)$ (Ko, 2004) and $X(3872)$ (Cho, 2011; Pion, 2012) by applying similar methods to the doubly charmed tetraquark $T_{cc}$. The results enable future experimental searches at RHIC and LHC to use the final yield of $T_{cc}$ as a discriminant between a compact multiquark structure and a weakly bound molecular configuration, since the large difference between the statistical and coalescence expectations remains until kinetic freeze-out.
