# Flavor decomposition of the nucleon unpolarized, helicity and transversity parton distribution functions from lattice QCD simulations

> We present results on the quark unpolarized, helicity and transversity parton distributions functions of the nucleon. We use the quasi-parton distribution approach within the lattice QCD framework and perform the computation using an ensemble of twisted mass fermions with the strange and charm quark masses tuned to approximately their physical values and light quark masses giving pion mass of 260 MeV. We use hierarchical probing to evaluate the disconnected quark loops. We discuss identification of ground state dominance, the Fourier transform procedure and convergence with the momentum boost. We find non-zero results for the disconnected isoscalar and strange quark distributions. The determination of the quark parton distribution and in particular the strange quark contributions that are poorly known provide valuable input to the structure of the nucleon.

## Key claim

The authors present lattice QCD results for the unpolarized, helicity, and transversity parton distribution functions of the nucleon including disconnected contributions, but the calculation is performed at a pion mass of 260 MeV rather than the physical pion mass, and the matching to light-cone PDFs uses one-loop perturbation theory, leaving residual systematic uncertainties from the finite momentum boost and truncation effects.

## Method

The computation uses the quasi-parton distribution approach within lattice QCD on a single ensemble of twisted mass fermions with volume 32^3 x 64, lattice spacing 0.0938 fm, and pion mass 260 MeV. The authors evaluate connected three-point functions using sequential source methods with momentum smearing at three nucleon boosts (0.41, 0.83, and 1.24 GeV), and compute disconnected quark loops using hierarchical probing with stochastic techniques. Bare matrix elements are renormalized non-perturbatively in the RI' scheme, converted to a modified MSbar scheme, and matched to light-cone PDFs using one-loop perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: m_pi = 260 MeV, with m_pi L approximately 3
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.0938(3)(2) fm, volume 32^3 x 64, L = 3.0 fm
- Nucleon mass: m_N = 1.09(6) GeV
- Three nucleon boosts used: P_3 = 0.41 GeV, 0.83 GeV, and 1.24 GeV
- Source-sink separations: t_s = 0.94 fm for P_3 = 0.41 GeV, and t_s = 1.13 fm for P_3 = 0.83 and 1.24 GeV
- Number of measurements for connected diagrams: 400 at P_3 = 0.41 GeV, 1552 at P_3 = 0.83 GeV, and 9926 at P_3 = 1.24 GeV
- Renormalization scale: 2 GeV for unpolarized and helicity PDFs, sqrt(2) GeV for transversity PDFs

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous lattice QCD calculation of helicity PDFs including disconnected contributions (Alexandrou 2020) to all three types of collinear PDFs (unpolarized, helicity, and transversity). The flavor decomposition addresses the poorly constrained strange-quark PDFs, where global analyses disagree on the sign of the strange-quark helicity distribution, and provides lattice input that can complement phenomenological fits from deep inelastic scattering data. The calculation enables future comparisons with experimental extractions and can be improved by using ensembles at the physical pion mass and higher-order matching kernels.
