# Comparative Analysis of Pion, Kaon and Proton Spectra Produced at PHENIX

> The shapes of invariant differential cross section for identified pi,K, p and pbar production as function of transverse momentum measured in pp collisions by the PHENIX detector are analyzed. Simultaneous fit of these data to the sum of exponential and power-law terms show significant difference in the exponential term contributions. This effect qualitatively explains the observed shape of the experimental K/pi and p/pi yield ratios measured as function of transverse momentum of produced hadrons. A picture with two types of mechanisms for hadron production is given.

# Comparative Analysis of Pion, Kaon and Proton Spectra Produced at PHENIX

## Key claim
The simultaneous fit of pion, kaon, and proton spectra to a sum of exponential and power-law terms reveals that the exponential (Boltzmann-like) contribution dominates for pions (75%) but is much smaller for kaons (28%) and protons (18%), indicating different production mechanisms for different hadron species rather than a fit artifact. This difference in exponential contributions qualitatively explains the observed shape of K/pi and p/pi yield ratios as a function of transverse momentum.

## Method
The authors performed a simultaneous fit of identified pion, kaon, and proton invariant differential cross sections measured by PHENIX in pp collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV to the function dσ/(PT dPT) = A_e exp(-E_Tkin/T_e) + A/(1+PT^2/(T^2·n))^n, where E_Tkin = sqrt(PT^2+M^2)-M. The fit constrained the power-law parameter n to be the same for all three hadron species, and the charged kaon spectrum was extended using available K0_s data to compensate for its limited PT range.

## Key numbers
- Exponential term contribution to pion spectra: 75% ± 2%
- Exponential term contribution to kaon spectra: 28% ± 7%
- Exponential term contribution to proton spectra: 18% ± 8%
- Power-law term K/pi ratio: 0.29 ± 0.03
- Power-law term p/pi ratio: 0.25 ± 0.02
- Exponential term K/pi ratio: 0.04 ± 0.003
- Exponential term p/pi ratio: 0.02 ± 0.001
- Collision energy: sqrt(s)=200 GeV (PHENIX pp collisions)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous systematic analysis (Bylinkin and Rostovtsev, 2011) which demonstrated that charged hadron spectra from baryon collisions require an exponential Boltzmann-like component on top of a power-law pQCD-inspired term. The present analysis contradicts the assumption that the same spectral shape applies to all hadron species, showing that heavier hadrons (kaons and protons) are dominated by pQCD-like production. This enables a two-component picture of hadron production: quasi-thermalized particles (mostly pions) described by the exponential term, and pQCD-like production described by the power-law term with parameters independent of hadron type. The approach also explains the suppression of K/pi and p/pi ratios at low PT as resulting from the large exponential component in pion spectra.
