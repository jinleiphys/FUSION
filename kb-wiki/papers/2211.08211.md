# Molecular $P_Žł$ pentaquarks from light-meson exchange saturation

> Theoretical predictions for the spectrum of heavy meson-baryon bound states are a fundamental tool for disentangling the nature of the different pentaquark states that have been observed in experimental facilities. Here we explore this spectrum in a phenomenological model that describes the heavy meson-baryon interaction in terms of a contact-range interaction, where the coupling strength is saturated by the exchange of light scalar and vector mesons, i.e. $Žā$, $Žü$ and $Žē$ exchanges. Saturation determines the couplings modulo an unknown proportionality constant that can be calibrated from a molecular candidate. If we use the $P_Žł^N(4312)$ as input, we predict a series of molecular pentaquarks including the $P_Žł^N(4440)$ and $P_Žł^N(4457)$, the recent $P_{Žłs}^╬ø(4338)$ and the $P_{Žłs}^╬ø(4459)$.

## Key claim
The paper claims that a contact-range interaction model, with couplings saturated by light scalar and vector meson exchange (sigma, rho, omega) and calibrated using the P_psi^N(4312) as input, can simultaneously predict the masses of the P_psi^N(4440), P_psi^N(4457), P_psi_s^Lambda(4338), and P_psi_s^Lambda(4459) as molecular pentaquarks, provided the regularization scale and finite-size parameter are chosen appropriately and the model is applied with minimal coupled-channel dynamics.

## Method
The authors model the charmed antimeson-charmed baryon interaction as a non-relativistic S-wave contact-range potential with central and spin-spin couplings, regularized with a Gaussian cutoff at Lambda = 1.0 GeV. The coupling strengths are saturated by scalar (sigma) and vector (rho, omega) meson exchange, with the unknown overall proportionality constant fixed by reproducing the mass of the P_psi^N(4312) as a Sigma_c Dbar bound state. The renormalization group evolution of the couplings is used to combine scalar and vector contributions at a common scale, with a parameter alpha = 1 accounting for finite-size effects. Bound-state masses are then predicted by solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for each molecular configuration, including coupled-channel effects where SU(3)-flavor symmetry requires mixing between channels.

## Context
This work extends the phenomenological contact-range model proposed by Peng et al. (2021) for molecular pentaquarks, applying it simultaneously to both non-strange (P_psi^N) and strange (P_psi_s^Lambda) sectors to test whether these states are connected. It builds on previous molecular interpretations by Karliner et al. (2022), Wang et al. (2022), Yan et al. (2022), Meng et al. (2022), and Burns (2022) for the P_psi_s^Lambda(4338), and by Chen et al. (2020), Peng et al. (2020), Liu et al. (2020), and Chen et al. (2020) for the P_psi_s^Lambda(4459). The model differs from earlier one-boson-exchange approaches by using a contact-range interaction with light-meson saturation, enabling a unified treatment of multiple pentaquark candidates and predictions for yet-unobserved states in the Sigma_c D_s and Xi_c' D systems.
