# Dynamically generated axial-vector meson resonance in the chiral symmetry restored vacuum

> We study the modification of the properties of the axial-vector meson, dynamically generated through the unitarization procedure, in the vacuum where the chiral symmetry is restored. This is accomplished by scaling the pion decay constant as the chiral order parameter while keeping the other input parameters fixed. We find that the mass and width of the axial-vector meson reduce to those of the vector meson, as anticipated by the Weinberg sum rules. The findings are consistent with the results of a recent QCD sum rule calculation, wherein the chiral order parameter is expressed through chiral symmetry-breaking four-quark operators, leading to the mass-squared difference scaling in proportion to variations in the chiral order parameter. We calculate the scaling behavior for the mass differences obtained from the unitarization method using both the physical and massless pion masses.

## Key claim
The mass and width of the dynamically generated axial-vector meson reduce to those of the vector meson when chiral symmetry is restored, but this behavior depends on whether the system is in the chiral limit or includes explicit symmetry breaking via a physical pion mass; the linear scaling of the mass difference with the chiral order parameter holds only approximately in the unitarization approach.

## Method
The authors study the modification of axial-vector meson properties by scaling the pion decay constant \(f_{\pi}^2\) as the chiral order parameter in the potential \(V(s)\) of the unitarized scattering amplitude, while keeping all other input parameters fixed. They solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the \(\rho\pi \to \rho\pi\) channel in the SU(2) chiral limit and for the SU(3) case with physical meson masses, using dimensional regularization for the loop function \(G(\sqrt{s})\) with a fixed subtraction constant. The pole positions of the \(a_1\) and \(f_1\) resonances are extracted from the complex plane of the scattering amplitude.

## Key numbers
- In the SU(2) chiral limit (\(m_\pi = 0\)), the mass difference \(m_{a_1} - m_\rho\) scales as \(136.9 \left( f_{\pi}^{*} / f_{\pi} \right)^2\) MeV, and the \(a_1\) decay width scales as \(267.6 \left( f_{\pi}^{*} / f_{\pi} \right)^2\) MeV.
- Input parameters include: \(m_\rho = 775.2\) MeV, \(m_\pi = 137.5\) MeV, \(m_{K^*} = 891.6\) MeV, \(m_K = 497.6\) MeV, \(m_\omega = 782.6\) MeV, \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV, and subtraction scale \(\mu = 1\) GeV.
- The subtraction constant \(a(\mu = 1\ \mathrm{GeV})\) is set to \(-1.85\) for resonance states and \(-4.08\) for bound states to ensure continuity of the \(a_1\) mass shift.

## Context
This work extends the unitarization approach of Roca (2005) for dynamically generated axial-vector mesons by introducing chiral symmetry restoration via scaling of the pion decay constant. The results are consistent with the QCD sum rule calculations of Kim (2020, 2021), which found that chiral partner masses become degenerate when chiral symmetry-breaking four-quark operators are removed. The study enables future investigations into how the mass differences between chiral partners versus parity partners scale with the chiral order parameter, showing that parity partners retain a significant mass difference even after symmetry restoration, consistent with Gubler (2016).
