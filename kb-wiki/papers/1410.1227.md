# Separable Optical Potentials for (d,p) Reactions

> An important ingredient for applications of nuclear physics to e.g. astrophysics or nuclear energy are the cross sections for reactions of neutrons with rare isotopes. Since direct measurements are often not possible, indirect methods like (d,p) reactions must be used instead. Those (d,p) reactions may be viewed as effective three-body reactions and described with Faddeev techniques. An additional challenge posed by (d,p) reactions involving heavier nuclei is the treatment of the Coulomb force. To avoid numerical complications in dealing with the screening of the Coulomb force, recently a new approach using the Coulomb distorted basis in momentum space was suggested. In order to implement this suggestion, one needs not only to derive a separable representation of neutron- and proton-nucleus optical potentials, but also compute the Coulomb distorted form factors in this basis.

# Separable Optical Potentials for (d,p) Reactions

## Key claim

The generalized Ernst-Shakin-Thaler (EST) separable representation scheme, extended to complex non-Hermitian optical potentials and to the Coulomb distorted basis, can reproduce elastic scattering cross sections for neutrons and protons on medium-mass and heavy nuclei to high accuracy, provided that a sufficient rank and appropriate support points are chosen for each partial wave group.

## Method

The authors generalized the EST separable representation method to complex optical potentials by defining a separable potential from the regular radial wave functions of the original potential and its complex conjugate, ensuring the resulting S-matrix satisfies time-reversal invariance. For proton-nucleus scattering, they further extended the EST scheme to the Coulomb distorted basis by replacing plane-wave half-shell t-matrices with Coulomb distorted nuclear half-shell t-matrices, computing the required potential matrix elements in momentum space via Fourier transforms of Woods-Saxon functions and Coulomb wavefunction integrals. The separable representations were constructed for the CH89 phenomenological global optical potential for neutron and proton scattering on 48Ca and 208Pb, using systematic sets of support points at center-of-mass energies between 5 and 48 MeV.

## Key numbers

- For n+208Pb, a rank-5 separable potential with support points at 5, 11, 21, 36, and 47 MeV (c.m.) reproduces partial wave S-matrix elements to at least four significant figures for all partial waves (l >= 0) in the energy regime between 0 and 50 MeV c.m.
- For n+48Ca, a rank-4 separable potential with support points at 6, 15, 36, and 47 MeV (c.m.) is required for l >= 0 partial waves.
- The unpolarized differential cross section for n+48Ca at 38 MeV laboratory kinetic energy and n+208Pb at 45 MeV laboratory kinetic energy shows excellent agreement between the separable representation and the original coordinate space calculation over the entire angular range.
- For the Coulomb distorted basis calculations, a maximum radius of 14 fm with 300 grid points is sufficient to obtain potential matrix elements with six significant digits precision.
- The short-range Coulomb potential contribution to the proton-nucleus cross section is significant, as demonstrated by the difference between calculations with and without this term for p+48Ca and p+208Pb.

## Context

This work extends the EST separable representation method (Ernst, Shakin, and Thaler, 1973), previously applied to Hermitian nucleon-nucleon interactions, to complex optical potentials for nucleon-nucleus scattering. It builds on the three-body theory for (d,p) reactions derived by Mukhamedzhanov (2012), which requires separable interactions in a Coulomb-distorted partial-wave basis to avoid screening of the Coulomb force. The results enable the implementation of Faddeev-AGS equations for (d,p) reactions on medium-mass and heavy nuclei without Coulomb screening, which is necessary for reliable calculations of neutron capture cross sections on rare isotopes relevant to astrophysics and nuclear energy applications.
