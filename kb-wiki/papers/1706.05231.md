# The ridge effect and three-particle correlations

> Pseudorapidity and azimuthal three-particle correlations are studied based on a correlated-cluster model of multiparticle production. The model provides a common framework for correlations in proton-proton and heavy-ion collisions allowing easy comparison with the measurements. It is shown that azimuthal cluster correlations are definitely required in order to understand three-particle correlations in the near-side ridge effect. This is similar to the explanation of the ridge phenomenon found in our previous analysis of two-particle correlations and generalizes the model to higher-order correlations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that azimuthal correlations among clusters are definitely required to explain three-particle correlations in the near-side ridge effect, extending the authors' previous correlated-cluster model (CCM) explanation from two-particle to three-particle correlations, with the caveat that the model assumes factorization of rapidity and azimuthal dependences which may break down for well-separated pseudorapidity particles.

## Method

The authors extend their correlated-cluster model (CCM) to three-particle pseudorapidity and azimuthal correlations, using Gaussian distributions for both cluster densities and particle decay distributions in rapidity and azimuthal space. They derive analytic expressions for the three-particle correlation function as a sum of contributions from one-cluster, two-cluster, and three-cluster production, assuming Poisson-distributed cluster numbers and factorization of longitudinal and transverse dependences. The model is then used to generate contour plots and projections of the three-particle correlation function as a function of rapidity and azimuthal differences.

## Key numbers

- The cluster decay rapidity width parameter δ_y is taken as 0.9 (rapidity units), with δ_cl y = 4 for the cluster rapidity correlation length.
- The cluster decay azimuthal width δ_φ is taken as 0.14, with δ_cl φ = 0.5 for the cluster azimuthal correlation length.
- The single-particle density is expressed as ρ(y,φ) = ⟨N_cl⟩ ρ̄^(1) E_1(y,φ), where ⟨N_cl⟩ is the average cluster number per collision.
- For Poisson-distributed clusters, the three-particle correlation function becomes c_3 = (1/⟨N_cl⟩^2) h^(1) + (1/⟨N_cl⟩) h^(2) + h^(3), showing that the three-cluster term h^(3) dominates for large ⟨N_cl⟩ (high-multiplicity events).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of two-particle correlations in the CCM framework (Sanchis-Lozano, 2016) to three-particle correlations, providing a common framework for proton-proton, proton-nucleus, and heavy-ion collisions. The results agree qualitatively with early experimental measurements by Netrakanti (2008) and with predictions from the glasma interpretation by Dusling (2009). The model enables experimental tests of whether azimuthal cluster correlations are present, and any observed deviation from the predicted factorization for larger pseudorapidity separations could indicate a "torque effect" from event-to-event early state fluctuations as discussed by Bozek (2015).
