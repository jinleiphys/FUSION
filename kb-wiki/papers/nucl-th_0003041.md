# Isoscalar dipole mode in relativistic random phase approximation

> The isoscalar giant dipole resonance structure in $^{208}$Pb is calculated in the framework of a fully consistent relativistic random phase approximation, based on effective mean-field Lagrangians with nonlinear meson self-interaction terms. The results are compared with recent experimental data and with calculations performed in the Hartree-Fock plus RPA framework. Two basic isoscalar dipole modes are identified from the analysis of the velocity distributions. The discrepancy between the calculated strength distributions and current experimental data is discussed, as well as the implications for the determination of the nuclear matter incompressibility.

## Key claim
The relativistic RPA calculations for the isoscalar giant dipole resonance in $^{208}$Pb, using effective interactions with nuclear matter incompressibilities from 211.7 to 355.0 MeV, produce centroid energies 4–5 MeV higher than experimental values, and the low-energy dipole strength predicted by all theoretical models between 8–14 MeV is not observed in experimental spectra.

## Method
The authors performed fully self-consistent relativistic random phase approximation (RRPA) calculations for $^{208}$Pb based on effective mean-field Lagrangians with nonlinear meson self-interaction terms. The configuration space included both particle-hole pairs and pairs formed from hole states and negative-energy Dirac states, with two-body matrix elements including contributions from the spatial components of the vector meson fields. The isoscalar dipole operator included a term $\eta = \frac{5}{3} \langle r^2 \rangle_0$ to remove spurious center-of-mass components, and strength distributions were computed for three effective interactions (NL1, NL3, NL-SH) with different nuclear matter compression moduli.

## Key numbers
- The NL3 effective interaction has nuclear matter compression modulus $K_{\rm nm} = 271.8$ MeV and reproduces the GMR excitation energy in $^{208}$Pb at 14.1 MeV.
- The NL1 interaction has $K_{\rm nm} = 211.7$ MeV; the NL-SH interaction has $K_{\rm nm} = 355.0$ MeV.
- The calculated high-energy IS GDR main peak in $^{208}$Pb is at 26.01 MeV for NL3; the low-energy peak is at 10.35 MeV.
- Experimental IS GDR centroid energies in $^{208}$Pb are reported as $22.4 \pm 0.5$ MeV (Davis et al. 1997) and $19.3 \pm 0.3$ MeV (Clark et al. 1999).
- The calculated HEOR centroid is at approximately 22 MeV, more than 2 MeV above the experimental HEOR centroid from Davis et al. (1997).
- The flow pattern for the high-energy compression mode shows centers of compression and expansion on the symmetry axis at approximately $z \approx \pm 2.5$ fm.

## Context
This work extends previous relativistic mean-field calculations of giant monopole resonances by Vretenar et al. (1997) to the isoscalar dipole mode, and is compared with non-relativistic Hartree-Fock plus RPA calculations using Skyrme forces by Van Giai and Sagawa (1981), Hamamoto et al. (1998), and Colo et al. (1999). The results highlight a persistent discrepancy between theory and experiment for the IS GDR centroid energy, which is an order of magnitude larger than for other giant resonances, and show that the low-energy dipole mode (8–14 MeV) is not a compression mode but a surface effect. The paper enables future work to resolve whether the missing low-energy dipole strength in experimental spectra could explain the inconsistency between the nuclear incompressibility values extracted from the IS GDR and the GMR.
