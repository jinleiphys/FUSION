# Variation of multi-Slater determinants in antisymmetrized molecular dynamics and its application to $^{10}$Be with various clustering

> We propose a method to optimize the multi-Slater determinants of the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) in the linear combination form and apply it to the neutron-rich $^{10}$Be nucleus. The individual Slater determinants and their weights in the superposition are determined simultaneously according to the variational principle of the energy of the total wave function. The multi-AMD basis states of $^{10}$Be show various cluster structures as well as the shell-model type. In the cluster configurations, different intercluster distances are superposed automatically indicating the role of the generator coordinates. We further introduce a procedure to obtain the configurations for the excited states imposing the orthogonal condition to the ground-state configurations. In the excited states of $^{10}$Be, the linear-chain-like structure is confirmed consisting of various clusters. The energy spectrum using the obtained basis states reproduces the experiments. The present framework can be the method to find the optimal multi-configuration for nuclear ground and excited states.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a method ("multicool") to simultaneously optimize all parameters of multiple Slater determinants in antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) and their superposition weights according to the variational principle, without imposing constraints on the basis states. The method is applied to $^{10}$Be, where the ground-state configurations are obtained by energy minimization, and excited-state configurations are generated by adding a pseudo potential that enforces orthogonality to the ground-state configurations.

## Method

The multicool method extends the standard AMD cooling equation (imaginary-time evolution) to treat multiple Slater determinants in a linear combination, where the Gaussian centroid positions, spin parameters, and linear coefficients are all varied simultaneously to minimize the total energy of the parity-projected intrinsic wave function. For excited states, a pseudo potential $V_\lambda = \lambda \sum_c |\Phi_c\rangle\langle\Phi_c|$ is added to the Hamiltonian using the ground-state configurations $\{\Phi_c\}$ (including rotated copies), and the multicool equation is solved for increasing $\lambda$ values to generate basis states orthogonal to the ground state. Finally, all basis states from both steps are superposed in a generator coordinate method (GCM) calculation with angular momentum projection.

## Key numbers

- Total energy of the $\alpha$ particle: $-27.6$ MeV with the $(0s)^4$ configuration
- $\alpha+\alpha+n+n$ threshold energy of $^{10}$Be: $-55.2$ MeV (close to experimental $-56.6$ MeV)
- Gaussian range parameter $\nu = 0.235$ fm$^{-2}$
- Ground-state intrinsic energy from multicool (spin-fix, $N=16$): $-54.7$ MeV, compared to $-48.9$ MeV for a single AMD basis state (energy gain of 5.8 MeV from multi-configuration)
- Ground-state matter radius from multicool: 2.31 fm, compared to 2.20 fm for single basis state
- Excited-state intrinsic energy at $\lambda=10^5$ MeV: $-48.0$ MeV, with radius 2.90 fm (excitation energy approximately 7 MeV above ground state)
- Number of basis states used: $N=16$ for both ground and excited configurations
- Average component $|\langle\Phi_n|\Phi\rangle|^2$ for ground state: 0.51 (standard deviation 0.13); for excited state at $\lambda=10^5$ MeV: 0.46 (standard deviation 0.06)

## Context

This work extends the AMD framework by introducing a variational method to optimize multi-Slater determinants without constraints, building on prior AMD studies of $^{10}$Be clustering by Kanada-En'yo (1999), Itagaki et al. (2000), Suhara and Kanada-En'yo (2010), and others who used constrained AMD with GCM. The method is related to multi-configuration Hartree-Fock theory (Faessler et al., 1971; Kim, 1971) and self-consistent multiparticle-multihole configuration mixing (Pillet et al., 2017). The orthogonal projection technique for excited states follows the method of Kukulin et al. (1978) used in orthogonality condition model calculations. The approach enables automatic determination of generator coordinates (such as intercluster distances) and can be applied to more advanced AMD variants like TOAMD and HM-AMD with realistic interactions.
