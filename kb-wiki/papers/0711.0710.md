# Symmetry and Supersymmetry in Nuclear Physics

> A survey of algebraic approaches to various problems in nuclear physics is given. Examples are chosen from pairing of many-nucleon systems, nuclear structure, fusion reactions below the Coulomb barrier, and supernova neutrino physics to illustrate the utility of group-theoretical and related algebraic methods in nuclear physics.

## Key claim

This survey argues that algebraic methods, particularly Lie algebras, superalgebras, and dynamical symmetries, provide a unifying framework for diverse nuclear-physics problems, but the paper is a pedagogical review rather than a presentation of new results, and the utility of these methods is illustrated through selected examples rather than proven exhaustively.

## Method

The paper reviews the mathematical foundations of Lie algebras and superalgebras, then applies them to fermion pairing using quasi-spin SU(2) algebras, the Gaudin algebra, and Bethe ansatz solutions for exactly solvable pairing Hamiltonians. It further discusses supersymmetric quantum mechanics in the contexts of pseudo-orbital angular momentum and pseudospin in the nuclear shell model, and dynamical supersymmetries arising from the Interacting Boson-Fermion Model.

## Key numbers

- The quadratic Casimir operator for SU(1,1) is given as \( C_2 = K_0^2 - \frac{1}{2}(K_+K_- + K_-K_+) \).
- For the degenerate-spectra pairing Hamiltonian, the energy eigenvalue for a state with \( N \) pairs is \( E_N = -|G|\left( \sum_j \Omega_j |c_j|^2 - \sum_{k=1}^{N-1} \frac{2}{z^{(N)}_k} \right) \), where \( \Omega_j = j + \frac{1}{2} \) is the maximum number of pairs in level \( j \).
- For the two-shell pairing Hamiltonian, the energy eigenvalue is \( E_N = -\sum_{n=1}^N \frac{\delta x_n}{\beta - x_n} \), with parameters \( \beta = 2\frac{\varepsilon_{j_1} - \varepsilon_{j_2}}{|c_{j_1}|^2 - |c_{j_2}|^2} \) and \( \delta = 2\frac{\varepsilon_{j_2}|c_{j_1}|^2 - \varepsilon_{j_1}|c_{j_2}|^2}{|c_{j_1}|^2 - |c_{j_2}|^2} \).
- The Nilsson Hamiltonian for the spherical shell model is \( H = \omega b_i^\dagger b_i - 2k \mathbf{L} \cdot \mathbf{S} - k\mu \mathbf{L}^2 \), with fits indicating \( \mu \approx 0.5 \).

## Context

This review extends the long tradition of algebraic approaches in nuclear physics, including the quasi-spin scheme of Kerman (1961), the Interacting Boson Model of Arima and Iachello (1976-1979), and the Richardson solution for pairing (1963). It connects to the Gaudin algebra (1976) and the Bethe ansatz methods used in condensed matter physics for ultrasmall metallic grains (von Delft, 1999). The paper enables further application of supersymmetric quantum mechanics to nuclear structure, pairing, and critical-point symmetries such as E(5/4) supersymmetry for odd-even nuclei near phase transitions (Caprio, 2006).
