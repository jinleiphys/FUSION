# Direct and indirect constraints on CP-violating Higgs-quark and Higgs-gluon interactions

> We investigate direct and indirect constraints on the complete set of anomalous CP-violating Higgs couplings to quarks and gluons originating from dimension-6 operators, by studying their signatures at the LHC and in electric dipole moments (EDMs). We show that existing uncertainties in hadronic and nuclear matrix elements have a significant impact on the interpretation of EDM experiments, and we quantify the improvements needed to fully exploit the power of EDM searches. Currently, the best bounds on the anomalous CP-violating Higgs interactions come from a combination of EDM measurements and the data from LHC Run 1. We argue that Higgs production cross section and branching ratios measurements at the LHC Run 2 will not improve the constraints significantly. On the other hand, the bounds on the couplings scale roughly linearly with EDM limits, so that future theoretical and experimental EDM developments can have a major impact in pinning down interactions of the Higgs.

## Key claim

The paper systematically constrains the complete set of CP-violating Higgs-quark and Higgs-gluon interactions from dimension-6 operators, finding that current best bounds come from a combination of electric dipole moment (EDM) measurements and LHC Run 1 data, but that existing uncertainties in hadronic and nuclear matrix elements significantly impact the interpretation of EDM experiments, and that LHC Run 2 will not improve constraints significantly while future EDM developments can have major impact.

## Method

The authors construct an effective field theory (EFT) framework with dimension-6 CP-violating operators involving the Higgs doublet, quarks, and gluons, including anomalous Yukawa couplings (Im Y'_q), a CP-violating Higgs-gluon coupling (theta'), and a top chromo-electric dipole moment (CEDM, d_tilde_t). They perform renormalization-group evolution from the high scale (1 TeV) down to the hadronic scale (1 GeV), including threshold corrections at the top, bottom, and charm quark masses, and then compute contributions to nucleon, atomic, and molecular EDMs using hadronic and nuclear matrix elements from lattice QCD, QCD sum rules, and chiral perturbation theory. They also derive direct constraints from LHC Higgs production and decay, t tbar and t tbar h production, using CP-conserving observables that depend on the square of the CP-violating couplings.

## Key numbers

- Current limit on electron EDM: 8.7 x 10^{-29} e cm (from ThO molecule, Baron 2013)
- Current limit on neutron EDM: 2.9 x 10^{-26} e cm (Baker 2006)
- Current limit on Hg-199 atom EDM: 2.6 x 10^{-29} e cm (Griffith 2009)
- Expected future sensitivity for neutron EDM: 1.0 x 10^{-28} e cm
- Expected future sensitivity for proton/deuteron EDM: 1.0 x 10^{-29} e cm (from storage ring experiments)
- At high scale MT = 1 TeV, the top CEDM d_tilde_t/m_t(MT) = 0.87 contributes to the Weinberg operator at m_t as d_W(m_t^-) = -g_s^3/(32 pi^2 m_t) d_tilde_t(m_t^+)
- At MT = 1 TeV, Im Y'_t contributes to the electron EDM as d_e/m_e = 7.0 x 10^{-5} e
- Nucleon EDM matrix element for up quark EDM: d_n = -0.22 +/- 0.03 (in units of the quark EDM coefficient)
- Nucleon EDM matrix element for down quark EDM: d_n = 0.74 +/- 0.07
- Nucleon EDM matrix element for up quark CEDM: d_n = -0.55 +/- 0.28 (in units of e times the CEDM coefficient)
- Nucleon EDM matrix element for down quark CEDM: d_n = -1.1 +/- 0.55
- Weinberg operator contribution to neutron EDM: +/- (50 +/- 40) MeV

## Context

This work extends previous EFT analyses of CP-violating Higgs couplings that focused on subsets of operators, such as Brod (2013) for third-generation couplings, Dekens (2013) for light quark CEDM constraints, and Kamenik (2011) for the top CEDM. It provides a unified treatment including all flavor-diagonal CP-violating Higgs-quark and Higgs-gluon operators, renormalization-group mixing, and both LHC and EDM constraints. The results enable future work to assess the impact of improved hadronic matrix elements from lattice QCD and next-generation EDM experiments on pinning down CP-violating Higgs interactions.
