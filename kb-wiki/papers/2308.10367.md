# Transverse and non-boost longitudinal expansion of (2+1)dimensional relativistic ideal-hydrodynamics flow in heavy ion collisions

> This study investigates the evolution of quark gluon plasma (QGP) within a generalized Bjorken flow framework. The medium under consideration is assumed to possess a finite transverse size and to expand both radially and along the beam axis. However, we assume that the boost invariance of longitudinal expansion is broken. To be more specific, we generalize the Bjorken solution to include the acceleration and transverse expansion of the fluid. We analytically study the (2 + 1) dimensional longitudinal acceleration expansion of hot and dense quark matter, applying a perturbation approach to solve the relativistic hydrodynamics equations. This procedure enables us to obtain exact algebraic expressions for fluid velocities and energy densities in both transverse and longitudinal directions. To simplify our calculations, we assume that the fluid is produced in central collisions, and therefore, we consider azimuthal symmetry. We compare the radial velocity and correction energy density with those obtained from the Gubser model. Furthermore, we determine the fluid's acceleration parameter and longitudinal correction energy density, which exhibits a Gaussian distribution.

# Transverse and non-boost longitudinal expansion of (2+1)dimensional relativistic ideal-hydrodynamics flow in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The authors present an analytical perturbative solution for (2+1)-dimensional relativistic ideal hydrodynamics that generalizes the Bjorken flow to include both transverse radial expansion and longitudinal acceleration, but only under the condition that the perturbation parameter remains small and the initial conditions are matched to the Gubser conformal solution at a single proper time.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic ideal hydrodynamics conservation equations using a perturbation expansion in a small parameter λ₁, writing the energy density, longitudinal rapidity Y, and transverse rapidity K as power series around the boost-invariant Bjorken solution. They assume azimuthal symmetry for central collisions, use the equation of state P = ε/3 for a conformal fluid, and work in Milne coordinates (τ, x⊥, φ, η). The first-order equations are solved by separation of variables, with the transverse expansion matched to the Gubser conformal solution at initial time τ₀ to determine integration constants, while the longitudinal expansion yields solutions for the acceleration parameter and correction energy density.

## Key numbers

- The zeroth-order Bjorken energy density is ε⁰ = ε_c (τ₀/τ)^(4/3), where ε₀ = 5.4 GeV/fm³ at τ ≈ 1 fm
- The Gubser parameter 1/q = 6.4 fm and ε̂₀ = 1500 are chosen for numerical calculations
- The longitudinal acceleration parameters are set to A₁ = 0.3 and A₂ = -0.07, with the condition that the ratio ε_η^(1)(τ₀, η=0)/ε₀(τ₀) ≪ 1
- The decoupling temperature is taken as 130 MeV, below which the hydrodynamic approximation is considered invalid
- The transverse velocity v⊥ shows deviation from Gubser flow for τ > 5 fm

## Context

This work extends the Bjorken model (Bjorken, 1983) by breaking boost invariance and translation invariance while maintaining azimuthal symmetry, addressing limitations noted in earlier accelerating hydrodynamic solutions (Csörgő et al., 2003-2004; Nagy et al., 2007-2009). The authors compare their radial velocity and correction energy density with the Gubser conformal solution (Gubser, 2010), using it as an initial condition at τ₀. This enables analytical study of non-boost-invariant longitudinal expansion with transverse flow, providing a framework for more realistic modeling of quark-gluon plasma evolution in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
