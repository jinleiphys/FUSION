# Are there plasminos in superconductors?

> Hot and/or dense, normal-conducting systems of relativistic fermions exhibit a particular collective excitation, the so-called plasmino. We compute the one-loop self-energy, the dispersion relation and the spectral density for fermions interacting via attractive boson exchange. It is shown that plasminos also exist in superconductors.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that plasmino collective excitations, previously known only in hot and/or dense normal-conducting relativistic fermion systems, also exist in superconducting systems at zero temperature, provided the fermions interact via attractive boson exchange. The authors explicitly note that their treatment is not fully self-consistent (they use a constant gap parameter and do not iterate the Dyson-Schwinger equations), so the result is a proof of principle rather than a quantitatively precise prediction.

## Method

The authors compute the one-loop fermion self-energy in the imaginary-time formalism at zero temperature, first for normal-conducting massless fermions interacting via scalar boson exchange, then for superconducting fermions using a Nambu-Gor'kov propagator with a constant gap parameter \(\phi_+ = 0.25\,\mu\) (and zero antiparticle gap). They obtain the dispersion relations from the poles of the full fermion propagator after resumming the one-loop self-energy via the Dyson-Schwinger equation, and compute the spectral density from the imaginary part of the propagator. For vector boson exchange, the self-energy is simply twice the scalar boson result in the hard-dense-loop limit.

## Key numbers

* Gap parameter used for superconducting fermions: \(\phi_+ = 0.25\,\mu\), with antiparticle gap set to \(\phi_- = 0\).
* Coupling constant used for all numerical results: \(g^2/(4\pi) = 1\).
* The gap in the excitation spectrum is \(2\phi_+ = 0.5\,\mu\) when the self-energy is set to zero, but is shifted to smaller momenta relative to the Fermi surface \(p = \mu\) when the self-energy is included.
* Quark Fermi energy in compact stellar objects is of order \(\mu \sim 500\) MeV, with light quark masses \(m \sim 5\) MeV \(\ll \mu\).

## Context

This work extends the study of plasmino excitations from normal-conducting relativistic fermion systems (previously investigated by Klimov (1981), Weldon (1982), and Blaizot and Ollitrault (1993)) to superconducting systems, motivated by the possibility that quark matter in compact stellar objects is a color superconductor. The authors confirm their expectation that superconductivity, being a Fermi-surface phenomenon, does not destroy the low-momentum plasmino excitations. The results enable future studies of the full excitation spectrum in color-superconducting quark matter, though the authors note that a fully self-consistent treatment including momentum-dependent gap functions would be needed for quantitative predictions.
