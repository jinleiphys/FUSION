# Isospin Splitting in the Pion-Nucleon Couplings from QCD Sum Rules

> We use QCD sum rules for the three point function of a pseudoscalar and two nucleonic currents in order to estimate the charge dependence of the pion nucleon coupling constant coming from isospin violation in the strong interaction. The effect can be attributed primarily to the difference of the quark condensates <ubar u> and <dbar d>. Assuming that the pi0 is a pure isostate we obtain for the splitting between the coupling of proton and neutron to the neutral pion an interval of [0.008 ; 0.023], the uncertainties coming mainly from the input parameters. In order to obtain the coupling to a physical pi0 we have to take pi - eta mixing into account leading to an interval of [0.012 ; 0.037]. The charged pion nucleon coupling is found to be the average of the two neutral ones. Electromagnetic effects are not included.

## Key claim

Using QCD sum rules for the three-point function, the authors estimate that the strong-interaction isospin violation in the pion-nucleon coupling constants yields a splitting between proton and neutron couplings to the neutral pion in the range of 0.8% to 2.3% for a pure isospin state, and 1.2% to 3.7% after accounting for pi-eta mixing, with the caveat that electromagnetic effects are not included and the uncertainties come mainly from input parameters such as the quark condensate difference.

## Method

The authors compute the three-point correlation function of two nucleonic (Ioffe) currents and one pseudoscalar isovector interpolating current, keeping terms up to first order in isospin violation (i.e., the quark mass difference m_d - m_u). The operator product expansion (OPE) includes only the leading-order quark condensate term, and the Borel sum rule for the gamma_5 q-slash structure (which has a double nucleon pole) is used to extract the coupling splitting. The calculation is performed at large spacelike q^2 approximately 1 GeV^2, and pi-eta mixing is incorporated at tree level in chiral perturbation theory to obtain the physical pi0 coupling.

## Key numbers

* The splitting (|g_pp pi0| - |g_nn pi0|) / g_NNpi for a pure isospin pi0 state is in the interval [0.008, 0.023] (i.e., 0.8 x 10^{-2} to 2.3 x 10^{-2}).
* After including pi-eta mixing, the splitting for the physical pi0 becomes [0.012, 0.037] (i.e., 1.2 x 10^{-2} to 3.7 x 10^{-2}).
* The range for the isospin violation in the quark condensate gamma = (<dbar d>/<ubar u>) - 1 is 0.002 < -gamma < 0.010.
* The charged pion-nucleon coupling |g_pn pi+| / sqrt(2) equals the arithmetic average of |g_pp pi0| and |g_nn pi0|.

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach to isospin violation in hadronic couplings, building on prior studies of the proton-neutron mass splitting using two-point sum rules by Yang, Hwang, Henley, and Kisslinger (1993). The results are compared with several other approaches: the quark-gluon model (splitting approximately 0.010 to 0.014), the quark-pion model (approximately 0.006), pi-eta mixing alone (0.005 plus or minus 0.0018), the chiral bag model (approximately 0.0067), and the cloudy bag model (approximately -0.006). The authors note that the Nijmegen phase shift analysis of NN and N anti-N scattering data, which includes electromagnetic effects, finds a splitting of 0.002 with an error of 0.008, showing no evidence for a difference within statistical errors. This work enables more precise calculations of charge symmetry breaking phenomena in nuclear physics by providing QCD-based estimates of the strong-interaction contribution to isospin violation in pion-nucleon couplings.
