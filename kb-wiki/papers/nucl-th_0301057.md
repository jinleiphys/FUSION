# The role of E1-E2 interplay in multiphonon Coulomb excitation

> In this work we study the problem of a charged particle, bound in a harmonic-oscillator potential, being excited by the Coulomb field from a fast charged projectile. Based on a classical solution to the problem and using the squeezed-state formalism we are able to treat exactly both dipole and quadrupole Coulomb field components. Addressing various transition amplitudes and processes of multiphonon excitation we study different aspects resulting from the interplay between E1 and E2 fields, ranging from classical dynamic polarization effects to questions of quantum interference. We compare exact calculations with approximate methods. Results of this work and the formalism we present can be useful in studies of nuclear reaction physics and in atomic stopping theory.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the interplay between electric dipole (E1) and electric quadrupole (E2) fields in Coulomb excitation of a harmonic oscillator can be treated exactly using a squeezed-state formalism, but the quantum interference term between E1 and E2 processes contributes only at the 1% level and vanishes to order Z^3, making it a Z^4 term to leading order.

## Method

The authors model a charged particle bound in a harmonic-oscillator potential excited by the Coulomb field of a fast projectile moving along a straight-line trajectory in the xy-plane. They expand the Coulomb potential to dipole and quadrupole order, then solve the classical equations of motion exactly and use the squeezed-state formalism to reconstruct the full quantum solution via Bogoliubov transformations. The excitation probabilities and transition amplitudes are expressed in terms of a shift parameter c (dipole) and a squeezing parameter ρ (quadrupole), which are obtained by solving coupled differential equations numerically or perturbatively.

## Key numbers

- The dipole strength parameter is defined as 𝔡_i = C/(b v √(2ω_i)) and the quadrupole strength as 𝔮_ij = C/(2 b² v √(ω_i ω_j)), where C = Z e², b is impact parameter, v is velocity, and ω_i are oscillator frequencies.
- In the sudden limit (ξ → 0), the dipole contribution from the transverse direction gives c_x ≈ -2i𝔡, while quadrupole excitations give |ρ_xx| ≈ |ρ_zz| ≈ 2𝔮.
- The lowest-order correction to the dipole amplitude in the sudden limit is Im(𝔠_x^(2)) ≈ 3π ξ², leading to the Barkas correction P_x = 4𝔡²(1 - 3π𝔮ξ²).
- For a model of ¹⁷F excited by ⁵⁶Ni with oscillator frequency ~1 MeV (oscillator length 6.4 fm), C = 6.3, impact parameter b = 4 (25.6 fm), and velocities from 0.2 to 20 (0.02 to 200 MeV/u), the probability of finding the nucleon beyond impact parameter is less than 10⁻³%.
- The quantum interference term ΔP_inter contributes only at the 1% level relative to the first Born E1 probability.

## Context

This work extends the classical treatment by Ashley, Ritchie, and Brandt (1972) and the quantum perturbation theory by Hill (1974) and Mikkelsen (1989, 1990), providing an exact solution that does not require heavy numerical calculations. The exact numerical solutions to the time-dependent quantum problem were previously demonstrated by Mikkelsen and Flyvbjerg (1992). The results confirm recent realistic calculations by Esbensen (2002) showing significant reduction of dissociation probability compared to first-order Born calculations, and can be applied to studies of Coulomb dissociation of weakly bound nuclei and atomic stopping theory, particularly the Z³ correction to Bethe's formula observed in measurements by Andersen (1989).
