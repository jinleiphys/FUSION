# Hadronization in cold nuclear matter

> I review a recently proposed scaling analysis of hadron suppression in Deeply Inelastic Scattering on nuclear targets measured at the HERMES experiment. The analysis can distinguish 2 competing explanations for the observed suppression, namely, quark radiative energy loss with long hadron formation times, and prehadron nuclear absorption with hadronization starting inside the nucleus. Experimental data are shown to favor short formation times and prehadron absorption.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a scaling analysis of the hadron multiplicity ratio in deep inelastic scattering on nuclear targets can distinguish between quark radiative energy loss (with long hadron formation times) and prehadron nuclear absorption (with hadronization starting inside the nucleus), and that experimental data favor short formation times and prehadron absorption, with the caveat that the constant \(C\) in the scaling variable cannot be determined by the fit.

## Method

The author proposed a scaling analysis of the hadron multiplicity ratio \(R_M\) as a function of a scaling variable \(\tau = C\, z_h^\lambda (1-z_h) \nu\), where \(z_h\) is the hadron fractional energy, \(\nu\) is the virtual photon energy, and \(\lambda\) is a scaling exponent extracted by best fit to experimental data or theoretical models. The analysis was applied to HERMES data for charged and identified hadrons on nuclear targets at \(E_{lab}=27\) GeV, comparing the extracted \(\lambda\) values to predictions from energy loss models (which yield \(\lambda \lesssim 0\)) and absorption models (which yield \(\lambda \gneqq 0\)).

## Key numbers

- For HERMES data on \(\pi^+\) on Kr, the scaling exponent \(\lambda\) was extracted from a fit, with \(\chi^2/\text{d.o.f.} \lesssim 1.6\) for all hadron flavors and nuclei, demonstrating that \(R_M\) scales with \(\tau\).
- Experimental data on pion and charged hadron production exhibit \(\lambda \gtrsim 0.4\), indicating dominance of the prehadron absorption mechanism.
- In the Lund string model, the scaling exponent is \(\lambda \approx 0.7\).
- The hadrons observed at HERMES have energies \(E_h = z_h \nu \approx 2-20\) GeV, comparable to mid-rapidity hadrons at RHIC.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of hadron suppression in deep inelastic scattering on nuclear targets, previously observed by the HERMES experiment (HERMES1, HERMES2) and the EMC and JLab collaborations, by providing a method to distinguish between competing theoretical frameworks: quark energy loss models (Wang, Arleo, Accardi 2005) and prehadron absorption models (BG+AMP, AGMP05, Kopeliovich, Falter et al. 2004). The scaling analysis enables future cross-checks with measurements of hadron \(p_T\)-broadening proposed by Kopeliovich, and further constraints on the hadronization mechanism could come from establishing a scaling of the prehadron formation time with \(Q^2\).
