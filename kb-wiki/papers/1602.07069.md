# Understanding the nature of the heavy pentaquarks and searching for them in pion-induced reactions

> We investigate the reaction $π^- p \to π^- J/ψp$ via the open-charm hadron rescattering diagrams. Due to the presence of the triangle singularity (TS) in the rescattering amplitudes, the TS peaks can simulate the pentaquark-like resonances arising in the $J/ψp$ invariant mass distributions, which may bring ambiguities on our understanding of the nature of the exotic states. Searching for the heavy pentaquark in different processes may help us to clarify the ambiguities, because of the highly process-dependent characteristic of the TS mechanism.

## Key claim
The resonance-like peaks observed in the $J/\psi p$ invariant mass spectrum, which have been interpreted as heavy pentaquark states, may instead be kinematic artifacts produced by triangle singularities (TS) in open-charm hadron rescattering amplitudes, rather than genuine exotic resonances. The authors emphasize that the TS mechanism is highly process-dependent, so searching for these structures in different reactions (such as pion-induced processes) can help resolve the ambiguity.

## Method
The authors calculated the reaction $\pi^- p \to \pi^- J/\psi p$ via triangle rescattering diagrams involving intermediate open-charm mesons ($\bar{D}$, $\bar{D}^*$, $\bar{D}_1$, $\bar{D}_2$) and charmed baryons ($\Lambda_c$, $\Sigma_c$, $\Sigma_c^*$, $\Lambda_{c1}$, $\Lambda_{c1}^*$, $\Sigma_{c2}$). They constructed effective Lagrangians within heavy hadron chiral perturbation theory (HHChPT) to describe the vertices, and used Breit-Wigner propagators to account for finite widths of unstable intermediate particles. The coupling constants for the $\pi^- p \to \bar{\mathcal{D}} Y_c$ vertices were estimated by assuming each production cross section is 1 nb at 20 GeV pion-beam energy, based on the only available experimental upper limits from BNL and theoretical predictions.

## Key numbers
- The two LHCb pentaquark candidates: $P_c(4450)$ has mass $4449.8 \pm 1.7 \pm 2.5$ MeV and width $39 \pm 5 \pm 19$ MeV; $P_c(4380)$ has mass $4380 \pm 8 \pm 29$ MeV and width $205 \pm 18 \pm 86$ MeV.
- The triangle singularity kinematic regions for the rescattering diagrams span $\sqrt{s_{1N}}$ from 4317.7 MeV (diagram a1) to 5092.4 MeV (diagram d3), with maximum discrepancies $\Delta_{s_1}^{\max}$ ranging from 0.773 MeV (a1) to 46.8 MeV (d3).
- The total cross sections for $\pi^- p \to \pi^- J/\psi p$ via rescattering at the corresponding $\bar{\mathcal{D}} Y_c$ thresholds range from 0.003 nb (diagram a1) to 0.330 nb (diagram b3).
- The coupling constants used: $h^\prime = 0.43$, $g_2 = 0.565$, $|h_{10}| = 0.85$, with the chiral symmetry breaking scale $\Lambda_\chi = 1$ GeV and pion decay constant $f_\pi = 132$ MeV.
- The upper limit for $\sigma(\pi^- p \to D^{*-}\Lambda_c^+)$ and $\sigma(\pi^- p \to D^{*-}\Sigma_c^+)$ at 13 GeV pion-beam energy from BNL is about 7 nb.

## Context
This work extends the triangle singularity interpretation previously proposed by Liu (2015) for the LHCb pentaquark candidates, and is related to similar studies by Guo (2015) and Mikhasenko (2015). The paper proposes that the forthcoming J-PARC high-momentum beam line experiment, which aims to study open-charm hadron production with high luminosity, can test whether the observed $J/\psi p$ peaks are genuine resonances or kinematic effects, because the TS mechanism is highly process-dependent and would not appear in reactions where the kinematic conditions are not satisfied. The authors note that the TS peaks occur near meson-baryon thresholds and may mix with genuine molecular states if those exist.
