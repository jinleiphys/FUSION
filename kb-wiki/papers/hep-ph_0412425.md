# Global Study of Nuclear Structure Functions

> We present the results of a phenomenological study of unpolarized nuclear structure functions for a wide kinematical region of x and Q^2. As a basis of our phenomenology we develop a model which takes into account a number of different nuclear effects including nuclear shadowing, Fermi motion and binding, nuclear pion excess and off-shell correction to bound nucleon structure functions. Within this approach we perform a statistical analysis of available data on the ratio of the nuclear structure functions F_2 for different nuclei in the range from the deuteron to the lead. We express the off-shell effect and the effective scattering amplitude describing nuclear shadowing in terms of few parameters which are common to all nuclei and have a clear physical interpretation. The parameters are then extracted from statistical analysis of data. As a result, we obtain an excellent overall agreement between our calculations and data in the entire kinematical region of x and Q^2. We discuss a number of applications of our model which include the calculation of the deuteron structure functions, nuclear valence and sea quark distributions and nuclear structure functions for neutrino charged-current scattering.

## Key claim

The paper presents a phenomenological model of unpolarized nuclear structure functions that achieves excellent overall agreement with data across the entire kinematical region of \(x\) and \(Q^2\) by introducing a small number of universal parameters for off-shell corrections and nuclear shadowing, but the model's validity depends on the assumption that these parameters are common to all nuclei and are extracted from a statistical analysis of available data.

## Method

The authors develop a model incorporating nuclear shadowing, Fermi motion and binding, nuclear pion excess, and off-shell corrections to bound nucleon structure functions. They perform a statistical analysis of data on the ratio of nuclear structure functions \(F_2\) for nuclei from deuteron to lead, expressing the off-shell effect and effective scattering amplitude for shadowing in terms of a few parameters common to all nuclei. The parameters are extracted from fits to data, and the model is then applied to compute deuteron structure functions, nuclear valence and sea quark distributions, and neutrino charged-current structure functions.

## Key numbers

- The average distance between bound nucleons in nuclei is about 1.5 Fm, used to justify the incoherent approximation for \(x > 0.1\).
- The characteristic DIS time (Ioffe length) is \(L_I = 1/(Mx)\), with the separation between small and large \(x\) regimes occurring at \(x \sim 0.1\).
- The structure function \(F_2\) is required to vanish as \(Q^2\) for \(Q^2 \to 0\) due to electromagnetic current conservation, with a transition region between perturbative and non-perturbative regimes at \(Q^2\) about 1 GeV\(^2\).
- The EMC effect shows a depletion minimum around \(x = 0.6 \div 0.7\) followed by a rise at large \(x\), while shadowing occurs for \(x < 0.05\) and antishadowing for \(0.1 < x < 0.3\).

## Context

This work extends prior theoretical models of nuclear effects in deep inelastic scattering (e.g., the EMC effect discovered by the European Muon Collaboration, and earlier studies of Fermi motion and binding by various authors) by providing a unified phenomenological framework applicable across a wide range of nuclei and kinematics. The model enables improved extraction of neutron structure functions from deuterium data, calculation of nuclear parton distribution functions for high-energy collider applications (RHIC, LHC), and evaluation of nuclear corrections for neutrino experiments such as NuTeV and NOMAD, particularly relevant for precision measurements of the weak mixing angle.
