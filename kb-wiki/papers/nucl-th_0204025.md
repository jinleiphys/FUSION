# How much is RHIC different from SPS? Comparison of the pT-spectra

> We show, by means of a simple compilation of the available experimental results, that the pT-spectra obtained at RHIC and SPS are strikingly similar up to pT ~ 1.5-2 GeV. In fact, the discrepancies between various experimental groups working at the same experiment are of the same size as the differences between RHIC and SPS. Our observation is complementary to the well known fact of the equality of the measured R_side and R_out HBT radii at RHIC and SPS. In essence, it points out that the transverse size of the firecylinder and the strength of the transverse flow are not significantly changed between SPS and RHIC. This suggests that a saturation mechanism is effective already at SPS. We also point out that the dominance of protons over pi^+ at large pT can be seen not only in the RHIC data, but already in the SPS data.

## Key claim

The transverse-momentum spectra of hadrons produced at RHIC and SPS are strikingly similar up to about 1.5-2 GeV, with discrepancies between experimental groups at the same facility being as large as the differences between the two colliders; this suggests that a saturation mechanism is already effective at SPS energies and that the transverse flow is not significantly different between the two energies.

## Method

The authors compiled published mid-rapidity pT-spectra for pions, kaons, protons, antiprotons, Lambdas, and anti-Lambdas from the NA44 and NA49 experiments at SPS (Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN)=17 GeV) and from PHENIX and STAR at RHIC (Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN)=130 GeV). They performed exponential fits of the form A exp(-m_perp/lambda) to the rescaled spectra over the range pT < 2 GeV, and also fitted the data to a single-freeze-out model with parameters tau (invariant time) and beta_perp_max (maximal transverse flow velocity) to extract flow estimates.

## Key numbers

- SPS energy: sqrt(s_NN) = 17 GeV; RHIC energy: sqrt(s_NN) = 130 GeV
- Inverse-slope parameter lambda for pi- from NA44: 207 +/- 4 MeV; from NA49: 190 +/- 8 MeV; from PHENIX: 229 +/- 6 MeV; from STAR: 176 +/- 12 MeV
- Inverse-slope parameter lambda for antiprotons from NA44: 294 +/- 14 MeV; from NA49: 397 +/- 100 MeV; from PHENIX: 342 +/- 15 MeV; from STAR: 569 +/- 122 MeV
- Freeze-out temperature at SPS: T = 164 MeV, baryon chemical potential mu_B = 229 MeV; at RHIC: T = 165 MeV, mu_B = 41 MeV
- Charged particle multiplicity per participant pair at RHIC: 3.2 (PHOBOS and BRAHMS) and 3.6 (PHENIX); at SPS: 1.9 (NA49) and 2.6 (WA98)
- At SPS, protons dominate pi+ at pT > 1 GeV; at RHIC, antiprotons dominate pi- at pT > 2 GeV

## Context

This work extends the observation of weak beam-energy dependence of transverse HBT radii (R_side and R_out) to the pT-spectra themselves, showing that the transverse size and transverse flow are not significantly changed between SPS and RHIC. The results contradict the interpretation of STAR data alone as indicating much stronger flow at RHIC, and instead support saturation models (Kharzeev, Levin, Nardi; Iancu; Golec-Biernat, Wusthoff) where the onset of saturation occurs already at SPS energies. The paper enables future RHIC measurements at lower energies to verify whether the saturation mechanism persists, and cautions against using models that fail to reproduce RHIC data for detector simulations.
