# Study of Low-Lying Baryons with Hamiltonian Effective Field Theory

> Drawing on experimental data for baryon resonances, Hamiltonian effective field theory (HEFT) is used to predict the positions of the finite-volume energy levels to be observed in lattice QCD simulations. We have studied the low-lying baryons $N^*(1535)$, $N^*(1440)$, and $Λ(1405)$. In the initial analysis, the phenomenological parameters of the Hamiltonian model are constrained by experiment and the finite-volume eigenstate energies are a prediction of the model. The agreement between HEFT predictions and lattice QCD results obtained at finite volume is excellent. These lattice results also admit a more conventional analysis where the low-energy coefficients are constrained by lattice QCD results, enabling a determination of resonance properties from lattice QCD itself. The role and importance of various components of the Hamiltonian model are examined in the finite volume. The analysis of the lattice QCD data can help us to undertand the structure of these states better.

## Key claim

Hamiltonian effective field theory (HEFT) can predict finite-volume energy levels for low-lying baryon resonances that agree with lattice QCD results, but the structure of each resonance differs: the N*(1535) contains a strong three-quark core while the N*(1440) and Λ(1405) do not at the physical pion mass, with the bare baryon component becoming important for Λ(1405) only at large pion masses.

## Method

The authors construct a Hamiltonian model including a bare baryon state coupled to two-particle meson-baryon channels (e.g., πN, ηN) with direct two-to-two particle interactions. They fit the Hamiltonian parameters to experimental πN scattering phase shifts and inelasticities at infinite volume, then discretize the Hamiltonian in a finite cubic box of length L to predict the finite-volume energy spectrum. The analysis is performed for three baryon resonances: N*(1535), N*(1440), and Λ(1405), with the latter two examined under multiple scenarios (with and without a bare baryon component).

## Key numbers

- N*(1535) pole from experimental fit: 1531 ± 29 − i 88 ± 2 MeV
- N*(1535) pole extracted from lattice QCD data: 1563^(+52)_(−80) − i 89.2^(+0.2)_(−4.2) MeV
- Λ(1405) two poles from experimental data: 1430 − i 22 MeV and 1338 − i 89 MeV
- Finite-volume calculations performed at box length L ≈ 3 fm

## Context

This work extends the HEFT approach previously applied to hadronic physics by Wu (2014), Hall (2014), and Liu (2016) to simultaneously analyze experimental scattering data and lattice QCD results for low-lying baryon resonances. The method enables determination of resonance properties from lattice QCD itself, and the analysis of eigenstate compositions helps resolve the long-standing puzzle that naive quark models predict N*(1535) should be lighter than N*(1440), contrary to observation. The results enable future lattice QCD studies to distinguish between competing models of baryon structure, particularly for the N*(1440) and Λ(1405).
