# Baryon octupole moments

> We report on a calculation of higher electromagnetic multipole moments of baryons in a non-covariant quark model approach. The employed method is based on the underlying spin-flavor symmetry of the strong interaction and its breaking.We present results on magnetic octupole moments of decuplet baryons and discuss their implications.

## Key claim

The paper calculates magnetic octupole moments of decuplet baryons using a non-covariant quark model approach based on SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry and its breaking, but the numerical results depend on a single unknown parameter C that is estimated from a pion cloud model rather than directly measured.

## Method

The authors employ Morpurgo's general parametrization method, which formally maps QCD matrix elements onto matrix elements in a basis of pure three-quark states with orbital angular momentum L=0, using spin-flavor wave functions. They construct a magnetic octupole moment operator in spin-flavor space as a three-body operator involving Pauli spin matrices of three different quarks, then sandwich this operator between decuplet baryon spin-flavor wave functions to obtain analytical expressions in terms of one unknown constant C. Flavor symmetry breaking is introduced by replacing spin-spin terms with expressions containing a cubic quark mass dependence characterized by the ratio r = m_u/m_s.

## Key numbers

- The Δ⁺ magnetic octupole moment is estimated as Ω(Δ⁺) = -0.012 fm³, obtained from the pion cloud model using the experimental neutron charge radius
- In the SU(3) flavor symmetry limit (r=1): Ω(Δ⁻) = 0.012 fm³, Ω(Δ⁺) = -0.012 fm³, Ω(Δ⁺⁺) = -0.024 fm³
- With flavor symmetry breaking (r=0.6): Ω(Σ*⁻) = 0.008 fm³, Ω(Σ*⁺) = -0.004 fm³, Ω(Ξ*⁻) = 0.005 fm³, Ω(Ω⁻) = 0.003 fm³
- The GP constant is estimated as C = -0.003 (units of fm³)
- The Δ⁺ magnetic octupole moment equals the product of the Δ⁺ quadrupole moment and the nuclear magneton: Ω(Δ⁺) = Q(Δ⁺) × μ_N = r_n² × μ_N

## Context

This work extends the study of baryon electromagnetic multipole moments beyond the well-known magnetic dipole moments, following experimental evidence for a nonzero quadrupole moment in the Δ⁺(1232) resonance from electron-proton and photon-proton scattering experiments (Ber03, Tia03, Bla01). The authors build on previous quark model predictions (Buc97) and the pion cloud model (Hen01) that described the Δ⁺ quadrupole moment, and apply Morpurgo's general parametrization method previously used for charge radii and quadrupole moments (Mor99, Hen02). The results provide numerical predictions for magnetic octupole moments that could be tested in future experiments or other theoretical approaches, and suggest that the magnetic moment distribution in decuplet baryons is oblate, matching the shape of their charge distribution.
