# PHENIX photons and dileptons

> Electro-magnetic probes such as dileptons and photons are strong probes to investigate the thermodynamical state of the early stages of collisions since they leave the system unscathed. The PHENIX experiment has measured both photons and dileptons in p+p, d+Au and Au+Au collisions. An excess of dilepton yield over the expected hadronic contribution is seen in 0.2-0.8\,GeV/$c^2$ in Au+Au collisions, which is prominent in lower $p_T$ and most central. Direct photons are measured through their internal conversion to electron pairs. We saw a large enhancement in Au+Au collisions over p+p yield scaled by the number of binary collisions. It turned out from the latest results on d+Au collisions that this enhancement is not explainable by a nuclear effect.

## Key claim

The PHENIX experiment observes a large enhancement of direct photon yield in Au+Au collisions over p+p yield scaled by the number of binary collisions, and the latest d+Au results show this enhancement cannot be explained by cold nuclear effects such as the Cronin effect, implying the excess arises from a source created in hot dense medium.

## Method

The PHENIX detector measured dileptons (electron-positron pairs) and photons in p+p, d+Au, and Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV. For dileptons, combinatorial background from random electron combinations was subtracted using event mixing, and correlated pairs from back-to-back jets were estimated with PYTHIA and subtracted. Direct photons were measured through their internal conversion to electron pairs using the Kroll-Wada formula, fitting the invariant mass distribution with a cocktail of hadronic contributions plus a direct photon component to extract the direct-to-inclusive photon ratio \(r_{\gamma}\).

## Key numbers

* Dilepton excess over hadronic cocktail seen in the invariant mass range 0.2-0.8 GeV/\(c^2\) in minimum bias Au+Au collisions, prominent at lower \(p_T\) and most central collisions.
* Direct photon yield in 0-20% central Au+Au collisions: dN/dy = 1.50 \(\pm\) 0.23 \(\pm\) 0.35 (for \(p_T > 1\) GeV/c), with slope 221 \(\pm\) 19 \(\pm\) 19 MeV.
* Direct photon yield in 20-40% central Au+Au collisions: dN/dy = 0.65 \(\pm\) 0.08 \(\pm\) 0.15, with slope 217 \(\pm\) 18 \(\pm\) 16 MeV.
* Direct photon yield in minimum bias Au+Au collisions: dN/dy = 0.49 \(\pm\) 0.05 \(\pm\) 0.11, with slope 233 \(\pm\) 14 \(\pm\) 19 MeV.
* The Au+Au direct photon yield is higher than the \(N_{coll}\)-scaled d+Au yield for \(p_T < 2.5\) GeV/c.
* Signal-to-background ratio for dileptons is approximately \(10^{-2}\) in total.

## Context

This work extends the PHENIX program of electromagnetic probe measurements at RHIC, following earlier observations of single hadron suppression (Adcox, 2004) and elliptic flow. The direct photon excess in Au+Au is compared to theoretical models with varying initial temperatures and thermalization times (Turbide, 2003). The d+Au results provide a crucial control measurement to separate cold nuclear matter effects from hot medium effects, and the comparison shows the Au+Au excess cannot be attributed to the Cronin effect. The development of the Hadron Blind Detector (HBD) aims to improve signal-to-background ratios in future dilepton measurements by tagging and removing electrons from photon conversions and Dalitz decays.
