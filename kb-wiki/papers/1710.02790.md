# $α$-cluster states in $^{46,54}$Cr from double-folding potentials

> $α$--cluster states in $^{46}$Cr and $^{54}$Cr are investigated in the double-folding model. This study complements a recent similar work of Souza and Miyake \cite{Sou17} which was based on a specially shaped potential. Excitation energies, reduced widths, intercluster separations, and intra-band transition strengths are calculated and compared to experimental values for the ground state bands in $^{46}$Cr and $^{54}$Cr. The $α$-cluster potential is also applied to elastic scattering at low and intermediate energies. Here, as a byproduct, a larger radial extent of the neutron density in $^{50}$Ti is found.

## Key claim

The double-folding model with a slightly angular-momentum-dependent strength parameter can reproduce the excitation energies of alpha-cluster states in ⁴⁶Cr and ⁵⁴Cr, complementing the work of Souza and Miyake (2017) which used a specially shaped potential; however, the folding potential requires minor L-dependent adjustments to each state, whereas the specially shaped potential describes all states with a single potential shape.

## Method

The alpha-core interaction was calculated using the double-folding procedure with the energy- and density-dependent DDM3Y effective interaction, using theoretical density distributions from the TALYS statistical model code for the unstable ⁴²Ti and neutron-rich ⁵⁰Ti cores. The total potential consisted of the folded nuclear potential multiplied by a strength parameter lambda (approximately 1.1-1.3) plus a Coulomb potential from a homogeneously charged sphere. The strength parameter lambda was adjusted to reproduce the energies of bound and quasi-bound states for each angular momentum L, with the number of nodes determined by the Wildermuth condition using Q=12 for the ground state bands.

## Key numbers

- For ⁴⁶Cr: strength parameter lambda ranges from 1.2204 (4⁺ state) to 1.2657 (12⁺ state), with variations below 4%
- For ⁵⁴Cr: strength parameter lambda ranges from 1.0946 (10⁺ state) to 1.1213 (0⁺ state), with variations below 2%
- Reduced widths theta_alpha² for ⁴⁶Cr: 0.843% (0⁺) down to 0.003% (12⁺)
- Reduced widths theta_alpha² for ⁵⁴Cr: 0.392% (0⁺) down to 0.002% (12⁺)
- RMS intercluster separation for ⁴⁶Cr 0⁺ state: 4.416 fm; for ⁵⁴Cr 0⁺ state: 4.438 fm
- B(E2) transition strength for ⁴⁶Cr 2⁺→0⁺: 10.3 W.u. (experimental: 19±4 W.u.)
- B(E2) transition strength for ⁵⁴Cr 2⁺→0⁺: 8.5 W.u. (experimental: 14.4±0.6 W.u.)
- Real volume integral J_R from elastic scattering on ⁵⁰Ti at 25 MeV: approximately 342 MeV·fm³
- Width parameter w for ⁵⁰Ti elastic scattering at 25 MeV using theoretical density: 0.997 (very close to unity)

## Context

This work extends the study of Souza and Miyake (2017) on alpha-cluster states in ⁴⁶Cr and ⁵⁴Cr, which used a specially shaped Woods-Saxon plus cubed Woods-Saxon potential with a Gaussian modification. The double-folding approach has been widely applied to doubly-magic core systems such as ⁴⁴Ti = ⁴⁰Ca ⊗ alpha (Atzrott et al., 1996) and ²⁰Ne = ¹⁶O ⊗ alpha (Abe et al., 1993). The present study enables comparison between cluster model predictions and elastic scattering data, and as a byproduct finds evidence for a larger radial extent of the neutron density in ⁵⁰Ti, consistent with earlier work by Gils et al. (1984).
