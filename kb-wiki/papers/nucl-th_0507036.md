# Symmetric Density Functionals

> Variations in distinct restricted spaces of wave functions generate distinct density functionals. In particular, angular momentum projected Slater determinants define a new density functional, compatible simultaneously with angular momentum quantum number and mean field descriptions.

# Symmetric Density Functionals

## Key claim

This paper proves an existence theorem for a density functional that is compatible simultaneously with angular momentum zero (or other symmetries) and a mean field description, but only under the condition that the variational minimum after symmetry restoration is unique and smooth, a condition that is reasonable but not guaranteed for all systems.

## Method

The author considers variations in restricted spaces of wave functions, specifically angular momentum projected Slater determinants, and shows that the Hohenberg-Kohn-Mermin (HKM) Legendre transform argument, establishing a one-to-one correspondence between the external potential and the density, holds for these restricted variational spaces as well. The proof follows the standard ab absurdo argument: if two distinct external potentials generated the same density, two contradictory strict inequalities would result. The method is illustrated for zero temperature with angular momentum projection onto J=0 states of even-even nuclei, and generalized to finite temperatures and other symmetry restorations.

## Key numbers

- For the illustrative example with L=6 and z=4 (four particles in a cloud shell with orbital angular momentum 6), two distinct states with total angular momentum 0 can be constructed, denoted Ψ₀a and Ψ₀b.
- The density of any projected state from the cloud shell is exactly ρ(r) = z φ(|r|)², where z is the number of particles in the partially filled shell and φ is the radial orbital shape.
- The inequality F_m ≥ F_M holds for the restricted variational space, with F_m > F_M generally strict except for very special Hamiltonians.

## Context

This work addresses a frustration expressed in the literature (Duguet) regarding the contradiction between the Hohenberg-Kohn (HK) existence theorem (1964), which deals with exact ground states having good quantum numbers, and practical density functionals that often produce solutions with spontaneously broken symmetries due to their connection with mean field theories like Hartree-Fock (HF). The paper extends the HK-Mermin framework to symmetry-restricted variational spaces, showing that every restricted space and every symmetry restoration creates its own density functional. It also proposes a symmetry-respecting version of Kohn-Sham (KS) theory (1965) where KS orbitals must be incoherently mixed to account for symmetry projection, using a "filling approximation" that is shown to be exact for projected densities from a spherical shell. The work explicitly acknowledges that constructive algorithms are missing, a limitation shared with the broader field.
