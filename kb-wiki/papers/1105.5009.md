# Exploratory investigation of nucleon-nucleon interactions using Euclidean Monte Carlo simulations

> We present an exploratory study of chiral effective theories of nuclei with methods adopted from lattice quantum chromodynamics (QCD). We show that the simulations in the Euclidean path integral approach are feasible and that we can determine the energy of the two nucleon state. This opens up the possibility to determine in future simulations nucleon phase shifts by varying the parameters and the simulated volumes. The physical cut-off of the theory is realised by blocking of the lattice fields. By keeping the block size fixed in physical units the lattice cut-off (i.e. the lattice spacing) can be freely changed. This offers an effective way for controlling lattice artefacts.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that Euclidean Monte Carlo simulations of a relativistic chiral effective theory for nucleons are feasible, and that the energy of the two-nucleon state can be determined precisely enough to enable future extraction of nucleon phase shifts, but only in the quenched approximation (neglecting nucleon-anti-nucleon loops) and for the pion-less theory, with the pion coupling left to future work.

## Method

The authors simulate a relativistic chiral effective theory on a Euclidean space-time lattice using Markov chain Monte Carlo methods, with the physical cut-off implemented via block-field methods that keep hadron sizes fixed in physical units. They use a mixed-precision conjugate gradient solver on GPUs (12 NVIDIA Tesla C1060) with distance preconditioning to invert the Dirac operator, and determine nucleon and two-nucleon masses by fitting a 4x4 correlator matrix of local and smeared operators to extract energy eigenvalues. Simulations are performed in the quenched approximation on lattices of size 16^3·64, 24^3·96, and 32^3·128, with the nucleon mass set to 939 MeV/c^2 to fix the scale.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass set to M_N = 939 MeV/c^2 to fix the lattice scale; a nucleon mass in lattice units aM_N = 1 implies lattice spacing a ≈ 0.21 fm
- Spatial lattice extensions: 16 a ≈ 3.4 fm, 24 a ≈ 5.0 fm, 32 a ≈ 6.7 fm
- Smallest non-zero momentum at aM_N = 1: 369 MeV/c (16^3), 246 MeV/c (24^3), 184 MeV/c (32^3)
- Simulations performed on lattices: 16^3·64, 24^3·96, 32^3·128
- Statistical sample of 120 configurations used for the 32^3·128 run at κ_N=0.08, C_0=0.2, C_1=0.2i
- Nucleon mass plateau observed from time-slice distance t_1=18 onward; two-nucleon mass plateau sets in later
- Statistical errors of single mass points in the sub-percent region

## Context

This work extends the lattice approach to chiral effective theories pioneered by Borasoy and collaborators (2005-2011), who used non-relativistic effective theories with Fock-state quantum Monte Carlo methods and lattice spacings of about 2 fm. The authors instead adopt the Euclidean path integral formulation from lattice QCD, using block-field methods to separate the physical cut-off from the lattice cut-off, which allows varying the lattice spacing to control lattice artifacts. This approach enables future determination of nucleon phase shifts by varying parameters and volumes, and is similar to the Yukawa model simulations by De Soto and collaborators (2006-2011), but applied here to the chiral effective theory of nuclear physics.
