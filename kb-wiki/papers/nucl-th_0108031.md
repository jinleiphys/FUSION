# Coherent Photoproduction of eta-mesons on Three-Nucleon Systems

> A microscopic few-body description of near-threshold coherent photoproduction of the eta-meson on tritium and He3 targets is given. The photoproduction cross-section is calculated using the Finite Rank Approximation (FRA) of the nuclear Hamiltonian. The results indicate a strong final state interaction of the eta-meson with the residual nucleus. Sensitivity of the results to the choice of the eta-N T-matrix is investigated. The importance of obeying the two-body unitarity condition in the eta-N system is demonstrated.

# Coherent Photoproduction of eta-mesons on Three-Nucleon Systems

## Key claim

The near-threshold coherent photoproduction of eta-mesons on tritium and He-3 targets is strongly influenced by the final state interaction of the eta-meson with the residual nucleus, and the results are sensitive to whether the two-body unitarity condition in the eta-N system is obeyed, with versions of the eta-N T-matrix that satisfy unitarity giving nearly identical cross sections even when they produce different eta-N scattering lengths.

## Method

The authors employed the Finite Rank Approximation (FRA) of the nuclear Hamiltonian, which neglects the continuous spectrum of the nuclear Hamiltonian and retains only the ground state, to solve the few-body scattering equations for the eta-3N system. The photoproduction cross section was calculated by first solving for the elastic eta-nucleus scattering T-matrix using Faddeev-type equations, then computing the photoabsorption T-matrix via integration, and finally applying detailed balance to obtain the photoproduction cross section. Three different versions of the separable eta-N T-matrix were used: a simple Breit-Wigner form (Version I), a form derived from an energy-dependent separable potential satisfying the Lippmann-Schwinger equation (Version II), and a Breit-Wigner form with unitarity imposed at the resonance energy (Version III). The nuclear bound state wave functions were obtained using the Integro-Differential Equation Approach (IDEA) with the Malfliet-Tjon potential.

## Key numbers

- The eta-N scattering length used for Versions I and II was a_etaN = (0.55 + i 0.30) fm; for Version III, the unitarity condition gave a_etaN = (0.76 + i 0.61) fm.
- The S11 resonance parameters used were E0 = 1535 MeV - (m_N + m_eta) and Gamma = 150 MeV, taken from the Particle Data Group.
- The range parameter for the Yamaguchi form factor was alpha = 3.316 fm^{-1}, determined by Bennhold and Tanabe (1991).
- The parameter kappa for off-shell extension of the photoabsorption T-matrix was varied in the range 1 fm^{-1} < kappa < 10 fm^{-1}, with the basic value kappa = alpha = 3.316 fm^{-1}.
- The ratio A = t_n^{gamma eta} / t_p^{gamma eta} was taken as A = -0.84, based on the multipole analysis estimate of -0.84 +/- 0.15 from Mukhopadhyay et al. (1995).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous microscopic calculations of low-energy eta-meson scattering from He-3, He-4, and H-3 nuclei using the FRA method (Rakityansky et al., 1995-1998) to the photoproduction process, and complements earlier exact AGS calculations for the eta-d system (Shevchenko et al., 2000). The results contradict the first-order optical potential calculations of Tiator et al. (1994), which underestimated the cross section near threshold because such optical models are inadequate near resonances. The study enables future experimental determination of the neutron-to-proton ratio of the eta photoproduction amplitude (parameter A) by measuring the cross section on tritium, which shows greater sensitivity to this parameter than He-3, and provides a benchmark for testing eta-N interaction models against few-body data.
