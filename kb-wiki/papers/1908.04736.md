# Setting nonperturbative uncertainties on finite-temperature properties of neutron matter

> We present an error band on neutron matter properties at finite temperature (finite-T) which comprehends uncertainties on the nuclear interaction, the many-body method convergence, and the thermodynamical consistency of the approach. This study provides nonperturbative predictions for finite-T neutron matter employing chiral interactions which are selected on the basis of their performance in both finite nuclei and infinite matter at zero temperature. Since proper theoretical uncertainties at finite-T are still generally lacking, the band provided here represents a first step towards setting first-principles constraints on thermal aspects of the nuclear matter equation of state.

# Setting nonperturbative uncertainties on finite-temperature properties of neutron matter

## Key claim
The paper provides a first-principles error band on finite-temperature neutron matter properties that combines uncertainties from the chiral nuclear interaction, the many-body method convergence, and the thermodynamical consistency of the self-consistent Green's function approach. The band is a first step toward constraining thermal aspects of the nuclear matter equation of state, but the author notes that the chiral interactions used carry some model dependence because they are not yet based on a fully renormalization-group-invariant power counting scheme.

## Method
The author employs the self-consistent Green's function (SCGF) method within the ladder approximation to solve the Dyson equation for the dressed nucleon propagator at finite temperature, using chiral two- plus three-body nuclear interactions. Five chiral Hamiltonians are selected based on their performance in reproducing zero-temperature properties of symmetric nuclear matter, pure neutron matter, and the symmetry energy, with the final uncertainty band bounded by the N2LO$_{\rm sat}$ interaction (upper limit) and the 2.0/2.0 (EM) interaction (lower limit). The method convergence is tested by comparing SCGF results with self-consistent Hartree-Fock (SCHF) and self-consistent second-order (SC2O) truncations, and thermodynamical consistency is checked by comparing the microscopic pressure with the thermodynamic derivative of the free energy.

## Key numbers
- Spread of approximately 7 MeV in the zero-temperature symmetric nuclear matter energy per nucleon at saturation density $n_{\rm sat}=0.16$ fm$^{-3}$ across seven chiral interactions.
- Saturation points of selected interactions lie in the density range $n=[0.16-0.18]$ fm$^{-3}$ and energy range $E/A=-[13.5-16.2]$ MeV.
- At twice saturation density ($2n_{\rm sat}$), the many-body method uncertainty band on the finite-temperature pressure is approximately 1.5 MeV, compared to approximately 6 MeV from the chiral interaction error band.
- At T=30 MeV, the SC2O approximation for pressure and effective mass stands practically on top of the full SCGF results for both chiral interactions.
- The N2LO$_{\rm sat}$ symmetry energy violates the unitary-gas lower bound from Tews (2017) in the density region from $n_{\rm sat}/2$ to $1.25n_{\rm sat}$.
- The theoretical zero-temperature pressure at $2n_{\rm sat}$ stays within the 50% confidence level band from the LIGO/Virgo analysis of GW170817 (Ligo 2018).

## Context
This work extends first-principles SCGF calculations of neutron matter at finite temperature (Carbone 2018) by providing a systematic uncertainty band that accounts for interaction, method, and thermodynamic consistency errors. It contrasts with the widely used finite-temperature equations of state based on Skyrme-like models or relativistic mean field theory (Oertel 2017, Burgio 2018), and with the ideal-fluid approximation for the thermal part of the equation of state (Bauswein 2010, Constantinou 2015). The results enable more reliable constraints on the nuclear equation of state for astrophysical simulations of binary neutron star mergers and core-collapse supernovae, and the effective mass analysis connects to studies showing its importance for the thermal behavior of the equation of state (Constantinou 2015, Yasin 2018, Carbone 2019).
