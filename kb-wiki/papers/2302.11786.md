# Elliptic flow of inclusive charged hadrons in Au+Au collisions at $E_{lab} = 35\mathrm{~A~GeV}$ using the PHSD model

> Elliptic flow ($v_2$) measurements of inclusive charged hadrons at mid-rapidity ($|η| <$ 1.0) in Au+Au collisions at $E_{lab} = 35\mathrm{~A~GeV}$ using the Parton Hadron String Dynamics (PHSD) model are presented as a function of centrality, transverse momentum ($p_\mathrm{T}$) and pseudo-rapidity ($η$). The $v_2$ results are obtained using the $η$-sub event plane method with respect to event plane angle ($ψ_{2}$) and participant plane angle ($ψ_{2}^{PP}$). $p_\mathrm{T}$-integrated charged hadron $v_2$ shows a strong centrality dependence in Au+Au collisions at $E_{lab} = 35\mathrm{~A~GeV}$. The eccentricity scaled elliptic flow ($v_2/\varepsilon_2$) also shows centrality dependence. The higher values of $v_2/\varepsilon_2$ in central collisions suggest development of stronger collectivity. The measurements are compared with the results from Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 7.7\mathrm{~GeV}$ published by the STAR experiment at RHIC. We also compare results of HSD and PHSD modes of the model to investigate contribution of hadronic and partonic phases of the medium on the measured $v_2$. The current results serve as a prediction of the collective behavior of the matter produced in baryon rich and moderate temperature conditions for the upcoming Compressed Baryonic Matter (CBM) experiment at the Facility for Antiproton and Ion Research (FAIR). These predictions are also useful for the interpretation of data measured at RHIC Beam Energy Scan (BES) program.

## Key claim

The PHSD model predicts that elliptic flow of inclusive charged hadrons in Au+Au collisions at \(E_{lab} = 35~\mathrm{A~GeV}\) is consistent with STAR experimental data at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 7.7~\mathrm{GeV}\) within statistical uncertainties for most observables, except for the pseudo-rapidity dependence where the model yields lower \(v_2\) values than the data. The higher values of eccentricity-scaled elliptic flow in central collisions suggest development of stronger collectivity, but this interpretation is conditional on the model's partonic phase contribution.

## Method

The authors simulated 50 million Au+Au collision events at \(E_{lab} = 35~\mathrm{A~GeV}\) using the PHSD model (version 4.1), which includes both partonic (PHSD) and hadronic (HSD) modes. Elliptic flow (\(v_2\)) was calculated at mid-rapidity (\(|\eta| < 1.0\)) using the \(\eta\)-sub event plane method with respect to the event plane angle (\(\psi_2\)) and participant plane angle (\(\psi_2^{PP}\)), with a hadronic cascade time of 500 fm/c. Centrality was determined from charged particle multiplicity within \(|\eta| < 0.5\), divided into nine classes from 0–80%.

## Key numbers

- The maximum value of \(p_\mathrm{T}\)-integrated elliptic flow \(\langle v_2 \rangle\) occurs in the 30–40% centrality class in Au+Au collisions at \(E_{lab} = 35~\mathrm{A~GeV}\).
- Event plane resolution peaks at the 20–30% centrality class and decreases toward central and peripheral collisions, matching STAR data at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 7.7~\mathrm{GeV}\) except for most central collisions where a small deviation is observed.
- The ratio of \(v_2(p_\mathrm{T})\) between HSD and PHSD modes is less than unity for all centralities, indicating that the partonic phase contributes to larger \(v_2\) values.
- \(v_2\) as a function of pseudo-rapidity in 10–40% central collisions shows weak dependence over \(|\eta| < 1.0\), but the magnitude from PHSD is lower than STAR data at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 7.7~\mathrm{GeV}\).

## Context

This work extends PHSD model predictions to FAIR energy (\(E_{lab} = 35~\mathrm{A~GeV}\)) for the first time, providing benchmarks for the upcoming CBM experiment. The results are compared with STAR experimental data from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 7.7~\mathrm{GeV}\) (STAR Collaboration, 2014). The comparison between HSD and PHSD modes isolates the contribution of partonic interactions, suggesting QGP formation at this energy, and enables interpretation of future CBM and RHIC BES data.
