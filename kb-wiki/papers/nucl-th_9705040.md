# Skyrmion dynamics on the unstable manifold and the nucleon-nucleon interaction

> The unstable manifold of the B=2 sector of the Skyrme model is constructed numerically using the gradient-flow method. Following paths of steepest descent from the B=2 hedgehog, we apply a collective coordinate description for the motion on the manifold to extract a Hamiltonian, approximately valid for the quantum description of the low-energy nucleon-nucleon interaction. The resulting potential - obtained in the Born-Oppenheimer approximation - is in qualitative agreement with phenomenological potentials.

## Key claim

The authors construct the unstable manifold of the B=2 sector of the Skyrme model numerically and derive a collective-coordinate Hamiltonian that, when quantized and treated in the Born-Oppenheimer approximation, yields a nucleon-nucleon potential in qualitative agreement with phenomenological potentials, but only at medium and large distances; the metric structure on the manifold is approximated by its large-distance form, and a precise comparison of the resulting flow curves with the exact paths of steepest descent is not possible.

## Method

The gradient-flow method was used to evolve field configurations along paths of steepest descent from the B=2 hedgehog, which is a saddle point on the hyperplane of minimal-energy configurations. The set of all decay paths defines a twelve-dimensional unstable manifold M^12, parametrized by three relative collective coordinates (distance R and two Euler angles beta and chi describing relative isospin orientation) plus nine global collective coordinates. The interaction potential V(R,beta,chi) was expanded in Wigner-D-functions with radial coefficients V_00, V_10, and V_11 deduced from three specific paths on M^12, and the large-distance form of the mass tensor was used to define an approximate Lagrange function, which was then canonically quantized and treated in the Born-Oppenheimer approximation to extract a nucleon-nucleon potential.

## Key numbers

- The single-skyrmion mass M_s = 1463 MeV in the parameter set used.
- The moment of inertia of a B=1 skyrmion lambda_s = (265 MeV)^{-1}.
- The pion decay constant f_pi = 93 MeV, the Skyrme parameter e_s = 4.76, and the pion mass m_pi = 138 MeV.
- The torus configuration (minimal energy B=2 configuration) is classically bound by 120 MeV.
- The reduced mass on M^12 is fixed at its asymptotic value mu_s = M_s/2.

## Context

This work extends the gradient-flow method developed by the same authors (Waindzoch and Wambach, 1996) for constructing the unstable manifold in flat space, building on Manton's (1988) proposal to use the unstable manifold to exclude undesirable vibrational modes. The resulting nucleon-nucleon potential is compared to the phenomenological Argonne potential (Wiringa, Smith, and Ainsworth, 1984), showing qualitative agreement for the central part with correct asymptotic strength, medium-range attraction, and a repulsive core at short distances. The approach enables further study of bound states in the Skyrme model, but requires a more detailed examination of the metric structure on M^12 for quantitative improvement.
