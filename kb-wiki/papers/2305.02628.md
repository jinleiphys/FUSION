# Exotic spin-dependent interactions through unparticle exchange

> The potential discovery of unparticles could have far-reaching implications for particle physics and cosmology. For over a decade, high-energy physicists have extensively studied the effects of unparticles. In this study, we derive six types of nonrelativistic potentials between fermions induced by unparticle exchange in coordinate space. We consider all possible combinations of scalar, pseudo-scalar, vector, and axial-vector couplings to explore the full range of possibilities. Previous studies have only examined scalar-scalar (SS), pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar (PP), vector-vector (VV), and axial-axial-vector (AA) type interactions, which are all parity even. We propose SP and VA interactions to extend our understanding of unparticle physics, noting that parity conservation is not always guaranteed in modern physics. We explore the possibilities of detecting unparticles through the long-range interactions they may mediate with ordinary matter. Dedicated experiments using precision measurement methods can be employed to search for such interactions. We discuss the properties of these potentials and estimate constraints on several coupling constants based on existing experimental data. Our findings indicate that the coupling between vector unparticles and fermions is constrained by up to 9 orders of magnitude more tightly than the previous limits.

## Key claim

The paper derives six types of nonrelativistic potentials between fermions mediated by unparticle exchange, including two new parity-violating interactions (scalar-pseudoscalar and vector-axial-vector) that previous studies had omitted; the authors then estimate constraints on coupling constants from existing experimental data, finding that the vector unparticle coupling to fermions is constrained up to 9 orders of magnitude more tightly than previous limits, but only for a specific choice of the scaling dimension \(d_\mathcal{U}=1.5\) and with the energy scale fixed at 1 TeV.

## Method

The authors compute tree-level scattering amplitudes for fermion-fermion scattering via scalar and vector unparticle exchange, considering all combinations of scalar (S), pseudoscalar (P), vector (V), and axial-vector (A) couplings at the vertices. They take the nonrelativistic limit of the amplitudes, expand to order \(m^{-3}\), and perform Fourier transforms to obtain coordinate-space potentials. Constraints on the vector-vector coupling constant \(c_V\) are derived by comparing the leading spin-independent term of the VV potential to torsion-balance data on violations of the gravitational inverse-square law from Adelberger (2007), while constraints on the scalar-pseudoscalar coupling product \(|c_S^e c_P^N|\) are obtained by comparing the SP potential at \(d_\mathcal{U}\to 1\) to the upper limit on the electric dipole moment of \(^{199}\)Hg from Graner (2016, 2017).

## Key numbers

* Vector unparticle coupling constant \(c_V = 1.7 \times 10^{-10}\) for scaling dimension \(d_\mathcal{U}=1.5\) at 68% confidence level, with \(\Lambda_\mathcal{U}=1\) TeV; this improves the previous limit from Chang (2011) of \(3.7 \times 10^{-1}\) by about 9 orders of magnitude.
* Vector unparticle coupling constant \(c_V = 1.8 \times 10^{-2}\) for scaling dimension \(d_\mathcal{U}=2.0\) at 68% confidence level, with \(\Lambda_\mathcal{U}=1\) TeV.
* Constraint on the scalar-pseudoscalar coupling product \(|c_S^e c_P^N| = 7.0 \times 10^{-17}\) at the 95% confidence level, derived from the \(^{199}\)Hg EDM limit for \(d_\mathcal{U}=1.0\).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of unparticle-mediated long-range interactions by Liao (2007), which only considered parity-even couplings (SS, PP, VV, AA). The new SP and VA potentials violate discrete symmetries (P and T for SP; P and C for VA), providing a novel connection between unparticle physics and searches for CP violation beyond the Standard Model. The derived potentials can be probed by precision experiments such as torsion-balance tests of the inverse-square law (Adelberger, 2007) and atomic electric dipole moment measurements (Graner, 2016), and the authors suggest that spin-polarized quantum sensors operating at fT/\(\sqrt{\text{Hz}}\) sensitivity could further constrain the spin-dependent terms.
