# Relativistic Mean Field Study of Neutron Stars and Hyperon Stars

> This thesis focuses on a variety of active research topics, such as nuclear matter, neutron stars, and phase transition within the framework of the RMF model. We use the previously successful effective field theory-driven Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) and density-dependent RMF (DD-RMF)formalisms for analyzing hadron matter to examine the infinite nuclear matter and neutron stars. The presence of exotic phases such as quarks has been investigated using the MIT Bag model and its variants, such as the vBag model, at various bag constants. The other exotic phases, such as hyperons, have also been studied under the influence of a strong magnetic field.

## Key claim

This thesis uses the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) and density-dependent RMF (DD-RMF) formalisms to analyze infinite nuclear matter and neutron stars, including the investigation of exotic phases such as quarks (via the MIT Bag model and vBag model at various bag constants) and hyperons under the influence of a strong magnetic field, with the condition that the results depend on the specific choice of bag constant and magnetic field strength.

## Method

The author employed the RMF and DD-RMF effective field theory-driven formalisms to model hadronic matter, solving the equations of motion for nuclear matter and neutron star structure. For quark matter, the MIT Bag model and its vBag variant were used with different bag constant values, while hyperonic degrees of freedom were incorporated into the equation of state. The study also examined the effects of a strong magnetic field on hyperon stars and considered the inner crust equation of state's influence on neutron star properties.

## Key numbers

- The paper reports results for hybrid neutron stars with quark cores, constraining the bag constant for hybrid star configurations (Rather et al., Int. J. Mod. Phys. E 29, 2050044, 2020).
- The secondary component of GW190814 was studied as a maximally rotating hybrid star, with the hadron-quark phase transition context published in J. Phys. G: Nucl. and Part. Phys. 48, 085201 (2021).
- Heavy magnetic neutron stars were analyzed in Astrophys. Jour. 917, 46 (2021), examining the effect of magnetic fields on neutron star properties.
- Rotating neutron stars with quark cores were studied in Phys. Rev. C 103, 055814 (2021), providing results for rotating configurations.

## Context

This thesis extends prior work on RMF models of neutron stars by incorporating multiple exotic phases (hyperons and quarks) and magnetic field effects within a unified framework. It builds on the established RMF formalism developed by Walecka (1974) and Serot and Walecka (1986), and the density-dependent extensions by Horowitz and Serot (1981). The work enables future comparisons with multi-messenger observations, particularly gravitational wave events like GW190814, and provides constraints on the bag constant for hybrid star models that can be tested against future neutron star observations.
