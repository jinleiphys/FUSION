# $J/ψ$ normal and anomalous suppressions in a hadron and string cascade model

> A mechanism for the effective dissociation of a $c\bar{c}$ pair in the colour electric field of strings is introduced into a hadron and string cascade model, i.e. JPCIAE, which is based on the LUND model, simulating ultra-relativistic nucleus - nucleus collisions. This new mechanism together with the known mechanism of nuclear absorption (both baryons and mesons) could reproduce fairly the data of the normal and anomalous $J/ψ$ suppressions in minimum bias pA, AB (with light projectile), and Pb + Pb collisions at 200 A GeV/c. However the impact parameter (E_T) dependence of the $J/ψ$ suppression factor, both, in S + U and Pb + Pb reactions at 200 A GeV/c and 158 A GeV/c, respectively, is not well reproduced. We also tested the additional mechanism of the energy degradation of leading particles, with which both, the normal and anomalous $J/ψ$ suppressions in minimum bias pA, AB, and Pb + Pb collisions and the E_T dependence of the $J/ψ$ suppression factor are better reproduced.

## Key claim

The authors introduce a mechanism for the effective dissociation of \(c\bar{c}\) pairs in the colour electric field of strings into the JPCIAE hadron and string cascade model, and claim that this mechanism together with nuclear absorption can reproduce the normal and anomalous \(J/\psi\) suppression in minimum bias pA, AB, and Pb+Pb collisions at 200 A GeV/c, but the impact parameter (\(E_T\)) dependence of the suppression factor is not well reproduced; only after additionally including the energy degradation of leading particles is the \(E_T\) dependence better described.

## Method

The JPCIAE model, based on the LUND model and PYTHIA event generator, simulates ultra-relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions by treating nucleons as spheres with a Woods-Saxon spatial distribution and ordering all possible collision pairs into a collision time sequence. A new effective dissociation probability for a \(c\bar{c}\) state in the colour electric field of strings is modelled as \(P_d = c_s \sqrt{s_{NN}} f(b) AB\) with \(f(b) = \exp(-b^2/R_L^2)\), where \(c_s\) is a fit parameter, \(b\) is impact parameter, and \(R_L\) is the larger nuclear radius; surviving \(J/\psi\) mesons then undergo nuclear absorption via interactions with baryons and mesons using effective cross sections. The additional mechanism of leading particle energy degradation is tested by reducing the effective cross sections and the model parameter \(c_s\).

## Key numbers

- The effective dissociation probability is parameterized as \(P_d = c_s \sqrt{s_{NN}} f(b) AB\) with \(c_s = 1.0 \times 10^{-6}\) (without energy degradation) and \(c_s = 6.0 \times 10^{-7}\) (with energy degradation).
- Nuclear absorption cross sections used: \(\sigma_{J/\psi-B}^{abs} = 6\) mb and \(\sigma_{J/\psi-M}^{abs} = 3\) mb; total cross sections are 20% larger: \(\sigma_{J/\psi-B}^{tot} = 8.64\) mb and \(\sigma_{J/\psi-M}^{tot} = 4.80\) mb (without energy degradation), reduced to \(\sigma_{J/\psi-B}^{tot} = 7.2\) mb and \(\sigma_{J/\psi-M}^{tot} = 4.0\) mb (with energy degradation).
- Formation times: \(\tau_M = 1.2\) fm/c for mesons and \(\tau_{J/\psi} = 0.6\) fm/c for \(J/\psi\).
- The parameterized \(J/\psi\) hadronic production cross section is \(\sigma_{NN\rightarrow J/\psi +X} = d(1 - c/\sqrt{s})^{12}\) with \(c = 3.097\) GeV, \(d = 2.37/B_{\mu\mu}\) nb, and \(B_{\mu\mu} = 0.0597\).
- The nuclear radius is taken as \(\sim 1.05 A^{1/3}\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the JPCIAE hadron and string cascade model (Sa Ben-Hao et al., 1998) to address the anomalous \(J/\psi\) suppression observed in Pb+Pb collisions by NA50, which could not be explained by nuclear absorption alone. The dissociation of \(c\bar{c}\) pairs in colour electric fields was previously proposed in a geometrical transport approach by other authors (1997), and the energy degradation of leading particles follows a stopping law from experiments on proton-nucleus collisions (1990s). The results challenge the interpretation that \(J/\psi\) suppression necessarily signals quark-gluon plasma formation, as suggested by Matsui and Satz (1986), and call for further study of the dissociation mechanism in dense hadronic matter.
