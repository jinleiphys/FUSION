# On neutral pion electroproduction off deuterium

> Threshold neutral pion electroproduction on the deuteron is studied in the framework of baryon chiral perturbation theory at next-to-leading order in the chiral expansion. To this order in small momenta, the amplitude is finite and a sum of two- and three-body interactions with no undetermined parameters. We calculate the S-wave multipoles for threshold production and the deuteron S-wave cross section as a function of the photon virtuality. We also discuss the sensitivity to the elementary neutron amplitudes.

## Key claim

Threshold neutral pion electroproduction on the deuteron is calculated in baryon chiral perturbation theory at next-to-leading order (third order in small momenta) with no undetermined parameters, but the calculation uses a hybrid approach that introduces model dependence through phenomenological nuclear wavefunctions rather than a fully chiral-consistent treatment of the deuteron.

## Method

The authors apply Weinberg's hybrid approach, computing the interaction kernel to third order in chiral perturbation theory and sandwiching it between phenomenological deuteron wavefunctions (primarily from the Bonn potential). They calculate the S-wave transverse and longitudinal multipoles \(E_d\) and \(L_d\) at threshold (pion produced at rest) as functions of the photon virtuality \(k^2\), including both single-scattering contributions from the elementary proton and neutron amplitudes and two three-body (pion-exchange) diagrams.

## Key numbers

- Pion masses used: \(M_{\pi^0} = 134.97\) MeV and \(M_{\pi^+} = 140.11\) MeV, to account for neutron-proton mass difference in rescattering diagrams.
- Pion decay constant: \(F_\pi = 92.4\) MeV; axial-vector coupling constant: \(g_A = 1.33\) (from Goldberger-Treiman relation with \(g_{\pi N} = 13.4\)).
- At \(k^2 \simeq -0.1\) GeV\(^2\), the deuteron form factors \(S_d^i(k^2)\) suppress the single-scattering contribution by about a factor of two.
- The S-wave cross section on the proton for \(-0.10 \le k^2 \le -0.04\) GeV\(^2\) lies between 0.15 and 0.45 \(\mu\)b.
- At \(k^2 \simeq -0.1\) GeV\(^2\), the deuteron S-wave cross section \(a_{0d} \simeq a_0/2\), where \(a_0\) is the S-wave cross section for neutral pion production off the proton.

## Context

This work extends the successful application of chiral perturbation theory from neutral pion photoproduction on the deuteron (Bernard et al., 1998) to electroproduction, and builds on prior calculations for the proton (Bernard et al., 1996, 1997). The results enable extraction of the elementary \(\pi^0 n\) amplitude from deuteron measurements, as was demonstrated for photoproduction at SAL, and provide predictions for experiments at MAMI that are currently being analyzed. A full fourth-order calculation and a fully consistent effective field theory treatment using precise deuteron wavefunctions (Epelbaum et al., 1999) are identified as necessary next steps.
