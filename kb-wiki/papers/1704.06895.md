# Two-particle multiplets splitting as a guideline in nucleon pairing estimations

> The ground state multiplet structure for nuclei over the wide range of mass number $A$ was calculated in $δ$-approximation and different mass relations for pairing energy was analysed in this work. Correlation between the calculated multiplet structure and experimental data offer a guideline in deciding between mass relations for nucleon pairing.

## Key claim

The correlation between calculated ground-state multiplet (GSM) level energies and experimental data can serve as a guideline for choosing among different mass relations for nucleon pairing energy, but the best choice depends on which multiplet levels are included in the analysis.

## Method

The authors calculated ground-state multiplet structure for nuclei over a wide mass range using the delta-approximation in the seniority model, where the relative energy shift of levels with seniority v=2 is given by a 3j-symbol expression. They compared the calculated GSM energies with experimental data for about 50 even-even isotopes whose main configurations can be assumed as one or several pairs of identical nucleons in a state with momentum j >= 7/2 over a closed core, using linear approximation E_exp = A * E_calc as a guideline.

## Key numbers

- For the full multiplet (J>0), the coefficient A for Delta_nn (direct pairing energy) is 0.991 with standard deviation sigma = 0.621.
- For the full multiplet (J>0), the coefficient A for 2*Delta_N^(5) (five-point EOS) is 0.871 with sigma = 0.634.
- Excluding 2+ states (J>2), the coefficient A for 2*Delta_N^(5) is 0.923 with sigma = 0.488.
- For J=J_max only, the coefficient A for 2*Delta_N^(5) is 1.004 with sigma = 0.246.
- For J=J_max only, the coefficient A for Delta_nn is 1.163 with sigma = 0.560.

## Context

This work extends the seniority-model analysis of even-odd staggering (EOS) by Dobaczewski (2001) and the direct definition of pairing energy from Preston (1962), applying them to ground-state multiplet splitting as an alternative test of pairing energy estimates. The results enable a more nuanced choice between mass relations for pairing energy, showing that the five-point formula 2*Delta_N^(5) gives the best agreement when 2+ collective states are excluded or when only the highest-spin multiplet member is considered, while direct Delta_nn works best for the full multiplet.
