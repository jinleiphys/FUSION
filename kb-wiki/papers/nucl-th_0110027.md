# Lambda^0 polarization as a probe for production of deconfined matter in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We study the polarization change of Lambda^0's produced in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions with respect to the polarization observed in proton-proton collisions as a signal for the formation of a Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP). Assuming that, when the density of participants in the collision is larger than the critical density for QGP formation, the Lambda^0 production mechanism changes from recombination type processes to the coalescence of free valence quarks, we find that the Lambda^0 polarization depends on the relative contribution of each process to the total number of Lambda^0's produced in the collision. To describe the polarization of Lambda^0's in nuclear collisions for densities below the critical density for the QGP formation, we use the DeGrand-Miettinen model corrected for the effects introduced by multiple scattering of the produced Lambda^0 within the nuclear environment.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the change in Lambda^0 polarization between proton-proton collisions and ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions can serve as a signal for Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) formation, but only if the density of participants exceeds a critical density threshold; the observed polarization depends on the relative contribution of recombination-type processes (below threshold) versus coalescence of free valence quarks (above threshold) to the total Lambda^0 yield.

## Method

The authors model Lambda^0 production in heavy-ion collisions as arising from two competing mechanisms: recombination of (ud)-diquarks with sea s-quarks (producing polarized Lambda^0's) in regions below a critical participant density, and coalescence of independent u, d, and s quarks from the QGP (producing unpolarized Lambda^0's) in regions above that density. They use the DeGrand-Miettinen model to describe polarization from recombination, and incorporate multiple elastic scattering effects (longitudinal momentum shift, transverse momentum shift, and spin-flip depolarization) on Lambda^0's traversing the nuclear medium below the critical density. The formalism is applied to Pb-Pb collisions at SPS energies.

## Key numbers

- Critical participant density for QGP formation: n_c = 3.3 fm^{-2}, chosen based on J/psi suppression observations in Pb-Pb collisions at the SPS
- Lambda^0 production cross section in nucleon-nucleon collisions: sigma_{Lambda^0}^{NN} = 3.2 mb
- Nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross section: sigma_{NN} = 32 mb
- Total Lambda^0-nucleon cross section: sigma_{Lambda^0 N}^{tot} approx 25 mb
- Inelasticity parameter: lambda approx 0.4, giving I = lambda/2 = 0.2
- Average dispersion angle per collision at high energies: Gamma approx 0.01 rad
- Diquark mass: m_D = 2/3 GeV; s-quark mass: m_s = 1/2 GeV
- Average intrinsic transverse momentum squared: <k_T^2> = 0.25 GeV^2
- Proportionality constant c for strange quark production in QGP: range 0.001 <= c <= 0.005
- Woods-Saxon parameters for Pb-208: R_A = 1.1 * A^{1/3} fm, a = 0.53 fm, rho_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Angert and Panagiotou that Lambda^0 polarization change could signal QGP formation, providing the first quantitative model implementing this idea. It builds on the DeGrand-Miettinen model (DeGrand, 1981) for hyperon polarization in hadron-induced reactions, and uses the sequential collision model of Hwa and Csernai to describe multiple scattering effects. The critical density threshold is taken from the J/psi suppression analysis of Blaizot (1999). The paper enables future experimental measurements of Lambda^0 polarization as a function of impact parameter and transverse momentum in heavy-ion collisions, though it notes that more accurate data on high-energy Lambda^0-nucleon interactions are needed for stronger predictive power.
