# Convergence of the Born Series with Low-Momentum Interactions

> The nonperturbative nature of nucleon-nucleon interactions as a function of a momentum cutoff is studied using Weinberg eigenvalues as a diagnostic. This investigation extends an earlier study of the perturbative convergence of the Born series to partial waves beyond the 3S1-3D1 channel and to positive energies. As the cutoff is lowered using renormalization-group or model-space techniques, the evolution of nonperturbative features at large cutoffs from strong short-range repulsion and the iterated tensor interaction are monitored via the complex Weinberg eigenvalues. When all eigenvalues lie within the unit circle, the expansion of the scattering amplitude in terms of the interaction is perturbative, with the magnitude of the largest eigenvalue setting the rate of convergence. Major decreases in the magnitudes of repulsive eigenvalues are observed as the Argonne v18, CD-Bonn or Nijmegen potentials are evolved to low momentum, even though two-body observables are unchanged. For chiral EFT potentials, running the cutoff lower tames the impact of the tensor force and of new nonperturbative features entering at N3LO. The efficacy of separable approximations to nuclear interactions derived from the Weinberg analysis is studied as a function of cutoff, and the connection to inverse scattering is demonstrated.

## Key claim

The perturbative convergence of the Born series for nucleon-nucleon scattering, diagnosed via complex Weinberg eigenvalues, becomes achievable when the momentum cutoff is lowered sufficiently using renormalization-group evolution, but the abstract's claim of "major decreases" in repulsive eigenvalues glosses over the fact that in the triplet channel the tensor force remains a significant nonperturbative feature even at low cutoffs, with eigenvalues still within but near the boundary of the unit circle.

## Method

The authors computed complex Weinberg eigenvalues from the operator \(G_0(z)V\) for low-momentum potentials (\(V_{\text{low}\,k}\)) evolved via renormalization-group methods from the Argonne \(v_{18}\), CD-Bonn, Nijmegen I, and chiral EFT potentials at N\(^2\)LO and N\(^3\)LO. They solved the eigenvalue problem in partial-wave momentum space with sharp cutoffs ranging from \(\Lambda = 10\;\text{fm}^{-1}\) down to \(2\;\text{fm}^{-1}\), for center-of-mass energies from 0 to 150 MeV, and separately tracked the largest repulsive and attractive eigenvalues. They also constructed separable approximations to the potentials using Weinberg eigenvectors at zero energy and tested their convergence for phase shifts and the triton binding energy.

## Key numbers

* For the \(^1\)S\(_0\) channel from Argonne \(v_{18}\), the largest repulsive Weinberg eigenvalue trajectory lies completely inside the unit circle for cutoffs near \(4\;\text{fm}^{-1}\) and below, indicating perturbative convergence with respect to the repulsive core.
* In the \(^3\)S\(_1\)–\(^3\)D\(_1\) coupled channel from Argonne \(v_{18}\), the largest repulsive eigenvalue has magnitude \(\eta \approx 0.5\) at \(\Lambda = 2\;\text{fm}^{-1}\), still significant due to the tensor force.
* For the N\(^3\)LO potential of Entem and Machleidt, the largest repulsive eigenvalues in the singlet channel are close to the \(V_{\text{low}\,k}\) results for \(\Lambda = 4\;\text{fm}^{-1}\).
* The deuteron binding energy is \(E_{\text{d}} = -2.225\;\text{MeV}\), corresponding to a unit attractive Weinberg eigenvalue \(\eta(E_{\text{d}}) = 1\) in the triplet channel.
* For separable expansions at rank \(n=10\), the triton binding energy converges to four digits or better for \(\Lambda = 3\;\text{fm}^{-1}\), while for \(\Lambda = 10\;\text{fm}^{-1}\) convergence to the same precision requires rank 20.

## Context

This work extends an earlier study by the same group (Bogner et al., 2005) that applied Weinberg eigenvalue analysis to S-waves at the deuteron binding energy and in the nuclear medium; here the analysis is broadened to all partial waves and positive energies. The results contradict the long-standing conventional wisdom that nuclear matter calculations must be nonperturbative in the inter-nucleon interactions to obtain saturation, as shown in the authors' prior work (Bogner et al., 2005). The demonstration that low-momentum interactions make separable expansions converge more rapidly (compared to polynomial expansions studied by Kamada et al., 2005) and that inverse-scattering solutions become viable at low cutoffs opens the door to simplified few-body and many-body calculations with controlled perturbative expansions.
