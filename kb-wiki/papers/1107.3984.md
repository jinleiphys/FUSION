# Quark Wigner Distributions

> We study the Wigner functions of the nucleon which provide multidimensional images of the quark distributions in phase space and combine in a single picture all the information contained in the generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and the transverse-momentum dependent parton distributions (TMDs). In particular, we present results for the distribution of unpolarized quark in a longitudinally polarized nucleon. Treating this distribution as it was a classical distribution, we also obtain the expectation value of the orbital angular momentum operator and compare the corresponding results from different quark models with the values obtained using alternative definitions of the orbital angular momentum, as given in terms of the GPDs and the TMDs.

## Key claim

The paper presents quark Wigner distributions as phase-space functions that unify information from generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and transverse-momentum dependent parton distributions (TMDs), but the authors explicitly note that these distributions cannot have a strict probabilistic interpretation because the uncertainty principle prevents simultaneous determination of position and momentum, and the Wigner functions are not positively defined.

## Method

The authors define Hermitian Wigner operators for quarks at fixed light-cone time using twist-two Dirac operators, then compute the matrix elements between nucleon states with given polarization as two-dimensional Fourier transforms of generalized transverse-momentum dependent parton distributions (GTMDs) in impact-parameter space. They evaluate these distributions using light-cone wave functions from two quark models: the light-cone constituent quark model (LCCQM) and the light-cone version of the chiral quark-soliton model (χQSM) restricted to the three-quark sector. The expectation value of the quark orbital angular momentum operator is obtained by integrating the Wigner distribution multiplied by (b_perp × k_perp)_z over phase space.

## Key numbers

- For u quarks in the LCCQM, the orbital angular momentum from the Wigner distribution (ℓ_z^q) is 0.131, from the GPD-based definition (L_z^q) is 0.071, and from the TMD-based definition (L_z^q with calligraphic L) is 0.169
- For d quarks in the LCCQM, ℓ_z^q is -0.005, L_z^q is 0.055, and the TMD-based value is -0.042
- The total (u+d) orbital angular momentum in the LCCQM is 0.126 for all three definitions
- For u quarks in the χQSM, ℓ_z^q is 0.073, L_z^q is -0.008, and the TMD-based value is 0.093
- For d quarks in the χQSM, ℓ_z^q is -0.004, L_z^q is 0.077, and the TMD-based value is -0.023
- The total (u+d) orbital angular momentum in the χQSM is 0.069 for all three definitions
- The distortion plots use fixed transverse momentum k_perp = 0.3 GeV and fixed impact parameter b_perp = 0.4 fm

## Context

This work extends the concept of Wigner distributions in QCD first explored by Ji (2003) and Belitsky (2003), and builds on the authors' own previous work connecting Wigner distributions to GTMDs (Lorce 2011). The same light-cone wave function models had previously been used to describe GPDs (Boffi 2007, Lorce 2011), TMDs (Pasquini 2008, Boffi 2009, Pasquini 2010, Lorce 2011), and electroweak properties of the nucleon (Lorce 2006, 2007, Pasquini 2007). The results enable comparison of three different definitions of quark orbital angular momentum (from Wigner distributions, from Ji's sum rule using GPDs, and from the h_{1T}^⊥ TMD) and show that in models without gauge-field degrees of freedom, all three definitions give identical total quark contributions but differ for individual u and d quark contributions.
