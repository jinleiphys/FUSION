# Anomalous Hall instability in the Chern-Simons magnetohydrodynamics

> The Chern-Simons magnetohydrodynamics (CSMHD) is introduced using a Maxwell-Chern-Simons (MCS) Lagrangian including an axion-like field $Θ$. The MCS equation of motion derived from this Lagrangian consists of a modified current, including a chiral magnetic (CM) and an anomalous Hall (AH) current, in addition to the ordinary Ohm current of resistive magnetohydrodynamics (MHD). The former consists of an axial chemical potential, which is given in terms of the temporal comoving derivative of $Θ$, and the latter arises from the spatial gradient of $Θ$. As it turns out, the existence of the axial chemical potential is a nonequilibrium effect that plays no role in the linear stability analysis, whereas the AH current arises as in the first-order linear perturbation of the thermal equilibrium. We analyze the linear stability and causality of the CSMHD in a resistive and chiral medium. We show that the Alfven modes propagating sufficiently close to the direction of the magnetic field are unstable but causal. They are also accompanied by a genuine nonhydro mode. A stable mode in a particular direction can correspond to an unstable mode propagating in the exact opposite direction. The AH instability is a manifestation of a breakdown of the parity. A numerical analysis of the phase velocity confirms these results.

## Key claim

The paper introduces Chern-Simons magnetohydrodynamics (CSMHD) from a Maxwell-Chern-Simons Lagrangian with an axion-like field and shows that Alfven modes propagating sufficiently close to the direction of the magnetic field are unstable but causal, with the anomalous Hall (AH) instability arising as a first-order linear perturbation of thermal equilibrium while the axial chemical potential (a nonequilibrium effect) plays no role in the linear stability analysis.

## Method

The authors derive the equations of motion of CSMHD from a Maxwell-Chern-Simons Lagrangian including an axion-like field Theta, obtaining a modified current that includes a chiral magnetic (CM) current and an anomalous Hall (AH) current in addition to the ordinary Ohm current of resistive MHD. They perform a linear stability analysis of the CSMHD equations in a resistive and chiral medium, examining the dispersion relations and causality of the resulting modes. A numerical analysis of the phase velocity is carried out to confirm the analytical results.

## Key numbers

* The Alfven modes propagating close to the magnetic field direction are found to be unstable but causal, with a stable mode in a particular direction corresponding to an unstable mode propagating in the exact opposite direction.
* The analysis identifies a genuine nonhydro mode accompanying the Alfven modes in the CSMHD framework.
* The AH instability is characterized as a manifestation of parity breakdown in the system.

## Context

This work extends the framework of resistive relativistic magnetohydrodynamics (RMHD) as formulated by Denicol (2019) and others, incorporating anomalous transport effects from chiral matter that cannot be captured by ordinary MHD. The CSMHD formalism builds on the chiral magnetic effect (CME) and anomalous Hall effect, connecting to prior work on anomalous transport by Son (2009) and Florkowski (2017). The results enable future studies of stability and causality in chiral plasmas, particularly relevant for heavy-ion collision phenomenology where parity-violating effects may arise.
