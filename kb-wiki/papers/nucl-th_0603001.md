# Nuclear cross sections in $^{16}\text{O}$ for $β$ beam neutrinos at intermediate energies

> The nuclear cross sections for charged lepton production induced by $β$ beam neutrinos (anti-neutrinos) in $^{16}$O have been presented at intermediate energies corresponding to the Lorentz boost factor $γ<250 (150)$. The calculations for quasi-elastic lepton production includes the effect of Pauli blocking, Fermi motion and renormalization of weak transition strengths in the nuclear medium. The calculations for the inelastic lepton production is done in the $Δ$ dominance model. The renormalization of $Δ$ properties in a nuclear medium and final state interactions of pions with the final nucleus are taken into account. The results may be useful in performing feasibility studies for the future CERN-FREJUS base line neutrino oscillation experiments.

# Nuclear cross sections in $^{16}\text{O}$ for $\beta$ beam neutrinos at intermediate energies

## Key claim
The authors calculate that for $\beta$-beam neutrinos from $^{18}$Ne (antineutrinos from $^{6}$He) with Lorentz boost factor $\gamma$ up to 250 (150), quasielastic charged lepton production dominates the total cross section in $^{16}$O, but inelastic production becomes comparable near $E_\nu \sim 1.5$ GeV; the neutral current $\pi^0$ background to quasielastic events is about 12-15% of the quasielastic cross section in the energy range 0.8-1.0 GeV, becoming significant only at the highest $\gamma$ values.

## Method
The quasielastic charged lepton production cross section was calculated in a local density approximation using the standard model weak interaction Lagrangian with BBA03 form factors, including Pauli blocking, Fermi motion, and RPA renormalization of weak transition strengths via a nucleon-nucleon potential with pion and rho exchanges. The inelastic charged lepton production was computed in the $\Delta$ dominance model, with nuclear medium effects on the $\Delta$ mass and width included through a self-energy calculation in local density approximation, and final state interactions of pions described using energy-dependent absorption probabilities. Neutral current $\pi^0$ production was calculated using the same formalism to assess the background to quasielastic events.

## Key numbers
- Quasielastic cross section reduction from free nucleon case due to Pauli blocking and Fermi motion: 30% at $E_{\nu_e}=200$ MeV, 15% at $E_{\nu_e}=400$ MeV, 10% at $E_{\nu_e}=750$ MeV, and 9% at $E_{\nu_e}=1.0$ GeV.
- Total reduction including RPA correlations: 60% at $E_{\nu_e}=200$ MeV, 40% at $E_{\nu_e}=400$ MeV, 26% at $E_{\nu_e}=750$ MeV, and 23% at $E_{\nu_e}=1.0$ GeV.
- Inelastic charged lepton production cross section reduction from nuclear medium effects on $\Delta$ properties: around 15% for $E_{\nu_e}=0.5-1.0$ GeV; total reduction including pion final state interactions: around 40% for $E_{\nu_e}=0.5-1.0$ GeV.
- Flux-averaged quasielastic cross section for neutrinos at $\gamma=250$: $\langle\sigma\rangle_{qe}^{cc} = 617 \times 10^{-40}$ cm$^2$ at $\langle E_{\nu_e}\rangle = 940$ MeV.
- Flux-averaged inelastic charged lepton cross section for neutrinos at $\gamma=250$: $\langle\sigma\rangle_{inel}^{cc} = 331 \times 10^{-40}$ cm$^2$.
- Flux-averaged neutral current $\pi^0$ production cross section for neutrinos at $\gamma=250$: $\langle\sigma\rangle_{inel}^{nc} = 88.7 \times 10^{-40}$ cm$^2$.
- Flux-averaged quasielastic cross section for antineutrinos at $\gamma=150$: $\langle\sigma\rangle_{qe}^{cc} = 111 \times 10^{-40}$ cm$^2$ at $\langle E_{\bar{\nu}_e}\rangle = 580$ MeV.
- Contribution from non-zero angle flux: about 5% for $\gamma=60$, reducing to less than 1% for $\gamma=250$.

## Context
This work extends earlier calculations of neutrino-nucleus cross sections on $^{16}$O by Valverde et al. (2005), Gaisser and O'Connell (1984), and Marteau (1999), with which the authors report qualitative agreement, while noting their results are smaller than those of Maieron et al. (2003). The calculations are specifically tailored for the $\beta$-beam neutrino source concept proposed by Autin et al. (2002) and Zucchelli (2002), using $^{18}$Ne and $^{6}$He ions accelerated at CERN-SPS to Lorentz boost factors up to $\gamma=250$ and $\gamma=150$ respectively. The results enable feasibility studies for the proposed CERN-FREJUS baseline neutrino oscillation experiment at 130 km with a 440 kT water Cerenkov detector, particularly for determining the mixing angle $\theta_{13}$ and the CP violating phase $\delta$.
