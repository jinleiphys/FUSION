# Systematic study of proton radioactivity of spherical proton emitters with Skyrme interactions

> Proton radioactivity is an important and common process of the natural radioactivity of proton-rich nuclei. In our previous work [J. H. Cheng et al., Nucl. Rhys. A 997, 121717 (2020)], we systematically studied the proton radioactivity half-lives of 53< Z <83 nuclei within the two-potential approach with Skyrme-Hartree-Fock. The calculations can well reproduce the experimental data of spherical nuclei. The present work is an extension of the previous work. We systematically study 32 proton radioactivity half-lives of spherical nuclei with the two-potential approach with Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (TPA-SHF) within 115 different versions of the Skyrme interactions. The calculated results indicate that the SLy7 Skyrme interaction gives the lowest r.m.s. deviation in the description of the experimental data of the spherical proton emitters among all the different versions of the Skyrme interactions. In addition, we use this model with the SLy7 Skyrme interaction to predict the proton radioactivity half-lives of 7 spherical nuclei in the same region, whose proton radioactivity is energetically allowed or has been observed but is not yet quantified.

## Key claim

The paper claims that among 115 different Skyrme interactions, the SLy7 interaction yields the lowest root-mean-square deviation (σ = 0.3869) when used in the two-potential approach with Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (TPA-SHF) to calculate proton radioactivity half-lives of 32 spherical emitters, and that this model outperforms other contemporary approaches such as CPPM-Guo2013, UDLP, and the new Geiger-Nuttall law.

## Method

The authors employed the two-potential approach (TPA) combined with the spherical Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) model to calculate proton radioactivity half-lives for 32 spherical parent nuclei with atomic numbers 53 ≤ Z ≤ 83. The nuclear potential, Coulomb potential, and centrifugal potential (with Langer-modified form) were computed using 115 different sets of Skyrme interaction parameters, and the decay width was obtained via the WKB approximation for the penetration probability. The standard deviation between calculated and experimental logarithmic half-lives was used to rank the performance of each Skyrme interaction, and the best-performing interaction (SLy7) was then used to predict half-lives for 7 additional unmeasured spherical proton emitters.

## Key numbers

- Standard deviation σ = 0.3869 for the SLy7 Skyrme interaction, the lowest among 115 versions tested, for 32 spherical proton emitters with 53 ≤ Z ≤ 83.
- Standard deviations for all 115 Skyrme interactions ranged from 0.3869 to 0.5464; only 6 interactions gave σ values in the range 0.5–0.6.
- For comparison, σ values for other models were: CPPM-Guo2013 = 0.5198, UDLP = 0.6063, and NGNL = 0.4524.
- Predicted half-lives for 7 unmeasured spherical nuclei are within a factor of 2.50 based on the σ = 0.3869 of the best-fit model.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Cheng et al., 2020) that used TPA-SHF for proton emitters with 53 ≤ Z ≤ 83, by now testing 115 Skyrme interactions to identify the optimal one. It builds on prior approaches such as the universal decay law for proton radioactivity (UDLP) by Qi et al. (2012), the Coulomb and proximity potential model (CPPM-Guo2013) by Deng et al. (2019), and the new Geiger-Nuttall law (NGNL) by Chen et al. (2019). The results enable more reliable predictions of proton radioactivity half-lives for yet-unmeasured spherical proton emitters near the proton drip line.
