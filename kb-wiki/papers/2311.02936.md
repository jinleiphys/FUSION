# Magnetic moments of decuplet baryons in asymmetric strange hadronic matter at finite temperature

> In the present work we study the masses and magnetic moments of decuplet baryons in isospin asymmetric strange hadronic medium at finite temperature using chiral SU(3) quark mean field model. In the strange isospin asymmetric medium, the properties of baryons in chiral SU(3) mean field model are modified through the exchange of scalar fields $σ$, $ζ$ and $δ$ and the vector fields $ω$, $ρ$ and $ϕ$. The scalar-isovector field $δ$ and the vector-isovector field $ρ$ signifies the finite isospin asymmetry of the medium. We calculate the in-medium constituent quark masses and masses of decuplet baryons in asymmetric strange matter within the chiral SU(3) quark mean field model and use these as input in the constituent chiral quark model to calculate the in-medium magnetic moments of decuplet baryons for different values of isospin asymmetry and strangeness fraction of hot and dense medium. For calculating the magnetic moments of baryons, contributions of valence quarks, quark sea and orbital angular momentum of quark sea are considered in the calculations.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the magnetic moments of decuplet baryons in a hot, dense, isospin-asymmetric strange hadronic medium, but the results are conditional on the specific choice of the chiral SU(3) quark mean field model parameters and the inclusion of sea quark and orbital angular momentum contributions, which the abstract does not explicitly qualify as model-dependent.

## Method

The authors first solve the coupled mean-field equations of the chiral SU(3) quark mean field model to obtain the in-medium values of the scalar fields (σ, ζ, δ) and vector fields (ω, ρ, φ) for a given baryon density, temperature, isospin asymmetry parameter η, and strangeness fraction f_s. From these fields, they compute the in-medium constituent quark masses and the masses of the decuplet baryons. These masses are then used as input in the chiral constituent quark model, where the magnetic moments are calculated by summing the contributions from valence quarks, the spin polarization of the quark sea, and the orbital angular momentum of the quark sea.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum masses of the light quarks are set to m_u = m_d = 253 MeV, and the strange quark mass is set to m_s = 450 MeV.
- The vacuum expectation values of the scalar fields are σ_0 = -93.49 MeV and ζ_0 = -97.98 MeV, derived from f_π = 92.8 MeV and f_K = 115 MeV.
- The dilaton field vacuum value is χ_0 = 254.6 MeV, and the confining potential coupling constant is k_c = 98 MeV·fm⁻².
- The coupling constant g_4 for vector meson self-interactions is 37.4.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of octet baryon magnetic moments in symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter by Singh, Kumar, and Dahiya (2017, 2019) and of decuplet baryons in symmetric strange matter by Singh, Kumar, and Dahiya (2020) to the case of isospin-asymmetric strange matter at finite temperature. It also complements calculations of baryon magnetic moments in nuclear matter using the quark-meson coupling model by Ryu, Hyun, and Cheoun (2010) and by Tsushima (2022). The results enable future studies of the role of baryon magnetic moments in the properties of neutron stars and magnetars under strong magnetic fields, as well as in heavy-ion collision observables.
