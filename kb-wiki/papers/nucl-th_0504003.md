# Density inhomogeneities in heavy ion collisions around the critical point

> We study the hydrodynamical expansion of a hot and baryon-dense quark fluid coupled to classical real-time evolution of the long wavelength modes of the chiral field. Significant density inhomogeneities develop dynamically when the transition to the symmetry-broken state occurs. We find that the amplitude of the density inhomogeneities is larger for expansion trajectories crossing the line of first-order transitions than for crossovers, which could provide some information on the location of a critical point. A few possible experimental signatures for inhomogeneous decoupling surfaces are mentioned briefly.

## Key claim

The amplitude of density inhomogeneities that develop dynamically during the hydrodynamical expansion of a hot, baryon-dense quark fluid is larger for expansion trajectories crossing the line of first-order transitions than for crossovers, which could provide some information on the location of a critical point; the authors caution that their model is too schematic for quantitative predictions of particle production.

## Method

The authors couple a hydrodynamical description of a thermalized quark fluid (treated as a perfect fluid) to classical real-time evolution of the long-wavelength modes of the chiral field, using the Gell-Mann–Levy linear sigma model with quarks. They initialize a sphere of hot, dense quarks (radius 5 fm) with Gaussian random fluctuations of the chiral fields and evolve the system via conservation laws for energy, momentum, and baryon charge together with the classical equations of motion for the chiral fields, without explicit damping terms. The type of transition (crossover versus first-order) is controlled by varying the initial baryon density or energy density relative to the location of the critical point in the model.

## Key numbers

- The second-order critical point in the model (with coupling g=3.3) is located at T_E ≈ 100 MeV, μ_E ≈ 200 MeV; this differs from lattice QCD results (T_E ≈ 160 MeV, μ_E ≈ 120 MeV).
- Initial conditions use a sphere of radius R = 5 fm with surface thickness a = 0.3 fm; nuclear matter saturation density is ρ₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³ and e₀ = m_N ρ₀ ≈ 0.15 GeV/fm³.
- For initial condition set (I), the initial energy density is fixed at e_eq = 2.8 e₀ while the initial baryon density is varied among ρ_eq = (0, 0.6, 1.6, 2.1, 2.4, 2.8) ρ₀.
- For initial condition set (II), the initial baryon density is fixed at ρ_eq = 1.7 ρ₀ while the initial energy density is varied among e_eq = (1.4, 1.9, 2.9) e₀.
- The RMS baryon density fluctuations reach Δρ/ρ₀ ∼ 0.1 – 1, with larger values for first-order transitions than for crossovers.

## Context

This work extends the model of Paech (2003) to include nonvanishing baryon density and studies the dynamical generation of density inhomogeneities during the QCD phase transition, in contrast to earlier work by Gyulassy, Rischke, and Zhang (1997) which focused on the washout of initial-state inhomogeneities via equilibrium hydrodynamics. The results suggest that inhomogeneities created during a non-equilibrium transition could survive to the decoupling surface and might be detectable through modified hadron abundances, analogous to inhomogeneous Big Bang nucleosynthesis; the authors propose analyzing thermal model fits with Gaussian distributions of temperature and baryon-chemical potential to search for such effects at SPS, RHIC, and AGS energies.
