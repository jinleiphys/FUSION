# Facets of Neutrino-Nucleus Interactions

> Different approaches to the calculation of neutrino-nucleus cross sections are summarized. Potential impact of improving the nuclear physics input into neutrino interactions and cross section calculations on uncovering new physics is discussed using the example of reactor anomaly. Importance of a thorough understanding of neutrino interactions in astrophysics and cosmology is highlighted.

# Facets of Neutrino-Nucleus Interactions

## Key claim
Improved nuclear physics input into neutrino-nucleus cross section calculations could help resolve the reactor antineutrino anomaly, but the sterile neutrino explanation for the flux discrepancy is not universally agreed upon because corrections to the predicted flux are uncertain for the 30% of the flux that arises from forbidden decays.

## Method
The paper summarizes three distinct theoretical approaches to neutrino-nucleus cross section calculations: pionless effective field theory applied to deuteron breakup reactions, the coherent elastic scattering formalism using weak charge and nuclear form factors, and shell-model calculations with the SFO Hamiltonian for neutrino-12C interactions. Each approach is illustrated with specific cross section results and compared to experimental data where available.

## Key numbers
- The isovector axial two-body current counter term L1A is estimated at ~4 fm^3 from reactor and solar neutrino experiments, with a recent lattice QCD calculation giving L1A = 3.9 (0.1) (1.0) (0.3) (0.9) fm^3 at a renormalization scale set by the physical pion mass.
- Natural abundance of 13C in organic scintillators is 1.07%, meaning a sizable detector contains a substantial amount of this isotope.
- Three beta decays (92Rb, 96Y, and 142Cs) contribute 43% of the antineutrino flux emitted by nuclear reactors near 5.5 MeV.
- Flux of neutrinos from fissions of 235U and 239Pu measured by Daya Bay was found to be about 5% less than predictions of the Huber (2011) and Mueller (2011) models.
- Including a shape correction of about +6% MeV^-1 in conversion calculations fits the experimental Daya Bay spectrum better.

## Context
This proceedings paper extends the discussion of neutrino-nucleus interactions summarized in the review by Alvarez-Ruso et al. (2017) and addresses the reactor antineutrino anomaly identified by Mention et al. (2011). The work connects to ongoing experimental efforts including the PROSPECT experiment at HFIR (Ashenfelter et al., 2015) and the recent COHERENT measurement of coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (Akimov et al., 2017). The paper enables future work on sterile neutrino searches, supernova neutrino detection, and improved reactor neutrino flux predictions through better nuclear data from rare ion facilities.
