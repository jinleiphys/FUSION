# Hot third family of compact stars and the possibility of core-collapse supernova explosions

> A phase transition to quark matter can lead to interesting phenomenological consequences in core-collapse supernovae, e.g., triggering an explosion in spherically symmetric models. However, until now this explosion mechanism was only shown to be working for equations of state that are in contradiction with recent pulsar mass measurements. Here, we identify that this explosion mechanism is related to the existence of a third family of compact stars. For the equations of state investigated, the third family is only pronounced in the hot, early stages of the protocompact star and absent or negligibly small at zero temperature and thus represents a novel kind of third family. This interesting behavior is a result of unusual thermal properties induced by the phase transition, e.g., characterized by a decrease of temperature with increasing density for isentropes, and can be related to a negative slope of the phase transition line in the temperature-pressure phase diagram.

## Key claim

The explosion mechanism in core-collapse supernovae triggered by a phase transition to quark matter is related to the existence of a third family of compact stars, but for the equations of state investigated this third family is only pronounced in the hot, early stages of the protocompact star and absent or negligibly small at zero temperature, representing a novel kind of third family.

## Method

The authors constructed two hybrid equations of state (B165 and B139) combining hadronic models (STOS11 and STOS98 respectively) with a quark matter description using a bag model with strong interactions. They computed mass-radius relations for these equations of state at various entropies per baryon and lepton fractions by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations, and analyzed the thermal properties of the phase transition including the temperature-density relation along isentropes and the pressure-energy density relation.

## Key numbers

- B165 hybrid EOS: bag constant B^{1/4}=165 MeV, maximum mass 1.51 M_sun (ruled out by pulsar mass observations)
- B139 hybrid EOS: bag constant B^{1/4}=139 MeV, coupling constant alpha_S=0.7, maximum mass 2.08 M_sun (consistent with 2 M_sun pulsar observations)
- Gravitational binding energy release from collapse from second to third family ranges from 0 to approximately 120 x 10^51 erg, increasing with entropy S and decreasing with lepton fraction Y_L
- For B165 with Y_L=0.4, the third family is visible for S >= 3; for B139 with Y_L=0.4, the third family is visible only for S >= 4
- For B139, the negative slope of temperature with density (dT/dn_B < 0) is found only for entropies S above 4

## Context

This work extends the explosion mechanism first demonstrated by Sagert et al. (2009) and Fischer et al. (2011) for core-collapse supernovae with quark matter phase transitions, which previously only worked for equations of state with maximum masses below 2 M_sun that contradicted pulsar mass measurements by Demorest et al. (2010) and Antoniadis et al. (2013). The authors connect the explosion mechanism to the existence of a third family of compact stars, a concept from Gerlach (1968) and Schertler et al. (2000), and show that the unusual thermal properties (negative slope of the phase transition line in the temperature-pressure diagram) are responsible for the novel hot third family. This enables future work to constrain the QCD phase diagram through heavy-ion collisions or lattice QCD calculations, and suggests that multidimensional supernova simulations could reveal additional effects.
