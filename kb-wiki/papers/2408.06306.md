# Dissecting Lepton Number Violating Interactions in the Left-Right Symmetric Model: $0νββ$ decay, Møller scattering, and collider searches

> In the context of the left-right symmetric model, we study the interplay of neutrinoless double beta ($0νββ$) decay, parity-violating Møller scattering, and high-energy colliders, resulting from the Yukawa interaction of the right-handed doubly-charged scalar to electrons, which could evade the severe constraints from charged lepton flavor violation. The $0νββ$ decay amplitude receives additional contributions from right-handed sterile neutrinos. The half-life, calculated in the effective field theory (EFT) framework, allows for an improved description of the contributions involving non-zero mixing between left- and right-handed $W$ bosons and those arising from exchanging a light right-handed neutrino. We find that the relative sensitivities between the low-energy (or high-precision) and high-energy experiments are affected by the left-right mixing. On the other hand, our results show how the interplay of collider and low-energy searches provides a manner to explore regions that are inaccessible to $0νββ$ decay experiments.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in the left-right symmetric model, the interplay between neutrinoless double beta decay, parity-violating Møller scattering, and collider searches can probe the Yukawa coupling of the right-handed doubly-charged scalar to electrons down to values as small as 10^{-7}, but the relative sensitivity between low-energy and high-energy experiments depends on whether left-right W boson mixing is present.

## Method

The authors calculate the neutrinoless double beta decay half-life using an effective field theory (EFT) framework that includes contributions from right-handed sterile neutrinos, left-right W boson mixing, and right-handed doubly-charged scalar exchange, treating sterile neutrinos as dynamical degrees of freedom when their masses are below the chiral symmetry breaking scale. They compare the sensitivity of the KamLAND-Zen and future ton-scale neutrinoless double beta decay experiments with the MOLLER parity-violating Møller scattering experiment at Jefferson Lab and with collider constraints from the LHC, future hadron colliders (SPPC, FCC-hh), and future electron-positron colliders (CEPC, FCC-ee). Four regimes are considered depending on the magnitude of the Yukawa coupling and the corresponding right-handed neutrino mass.

## Key numbers

- The MOLLER experiment sensitivity gives a lower bound of M_{δ_R^{±±}} / |(f_R)_{ee}| ≳ 7.6 TeV at 95% confidence level for the right-handed doubly-charged scalar mass over its Yukawa coupling to electrons.
- The LHC full Run-2 data excludes doubly-charged scalar masses below 1080 GeV from same-sign dilepton searches by ATLAS.
- The most stringent lower bound on the right-handed W boson mass from ATLAS is 6.4 TeV with 138 fb^{-1} of data.
- A future 100 TeV pp collider (SPPC or FCC-hh) with 30 ab^{-1} could probe doubly-charged scalar masses up to 5.1 TeV for M_{W_R}=7 TeV and up to 2.5 TeV for M_{W_R}≳15 TeV.
- The LEP Bhabha scattering constraint gives M_{δ_R^{±±}} / |(f_R)_{ee}| > 2.43 TeV.
- Neutrinoless double beta decay experiments have unique sensitivity to exclude values of |(f_R)_{ee}| as small as 10^{-7}, independent of the doubly-charged scalar contribution.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Dev (2018) by including non-zero W_L-W_R mixing, all possible contributions to neutrinoless double beta decay in the left-right symmetric model (left-handed neutrinos, right-handed neutrinos, and doubly-charged scalars), and using an EFT description that properly handles light sterile neutrinos where the propagator expansion breaks down. The results show that the EFT framework provides a more realistic assessment of neutrinoless double beta decay sensitivity and yields generally significantly greater parameter space reach than earlier work. The study expands the relatively scarce literature connecting parity-violating Møller scattering with neutrinoless double beta decay and provides updated collider constraints that incorporate the very light sterile neutrino region and small Yukawa coupling region not thoroughly explored before.
