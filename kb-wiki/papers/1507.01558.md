# The road to precision: Extraction of the specific shear viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma

> Recent progress in extracting the specific shear viscosity of the Quark-Gluon Plasma from phenomenological studies is highlighted.

## Key claim

The specific shear viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) can be constrained phenomenologically by comparing viscous hydrodynamic simulations with anisotropic flow measurements, but the extraction remains limited by model uncertainties in the initial-state conditions and by the need to include higher-order flow observables beyond elliptic flow.

## Method

The authors review the use of (3+1)-dimensional viscous fluid dynamical simulations (such as the MUSIC code) with initial-state models like IP-Glasma to compute the anisotropic flow coefficients \(v_n\) of charged hadrons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC. By confronting these simulations with experimental measurements of the elliptic flow \(v_2\) and higher-order flow coefficients \(v_3, v_4\), as well as event-by-event probability distributions of \(v_n\), the specific shear viscosity \(\eta/s\) of the QGP is extracted. The conversion efficiency from initial spatial eccentricities \(\varepsilon_n\) to final momentum anisotropies \(v_n\) is degraded by viscosity, which provides the key observable for the extraction.

## Key numbers

- The specific shear viscosity of the QGP is constrained to the range \(\frac{1}{4\pi} \le (\eta/s)_\mathrm{QGP} \le 2.5 \times \frac{1}{4\pi}\) from RHIC data using viscous hydrodynamic simulations (Song 2010).
- With the IP-Glasma initial-state model and (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics, a value of \((\eta/s)_\mathrm{QGP} = 0.2\) (later refined to 0.18) provides a precise description of charged hadron anisotropic flow coefficients \(v_2\) to \(v_4\) for Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 \(A\) TeV (Gale 2012, Schenke 2014).
- A phenomenological extraction of \((\eta/s)_\mathrm{QGP}\) with a relative precision of order 5-10% is now considered within reach.
- The QGP fireballs have volume \(V \sim 10^{-42} \mathrm{m}^3\) and cool in \(\sim 5 \times 10^{-23} \mathrm{s}\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier extraction of QGP shear viscosity from elliptic flow alone (Song 2010) by incorporating higher-order anisotropic flow coefficients and event-by-event flow distributions, which disentangle the effects of viscosity from initial-state quantum fluctuations. The approach mirrors modern studies of the cosmic microwave background (Ade 2013) and enables future combined analyses of rare probes (high-\(p_T\) hadrons, direct photons, heavy flavor mesons) to constrain the temperature dependence of \(\eta/s\) and other transport coefficients such as bulk viscosity. Current challenges include the "direct photon flow puzzle" (where models underestimate measured photon elliptic flow by factors of 2 to 4) and the failure of IP-Glasma hydrodynamics to simultaneously describe p+Pb and Pb+Pb collisions (Schenke 2014).
