# Order, Chaos and (Quasi-) Dynamical Symmetries across 1st-order Quantum Phase Transitions in Nuclei

> First order quantum phase transition (QPT) between spherical and axially deformed nuclei shows coexisting, but well-separated regions of regular and chaotic dynamics. We employ a Hamiltonian of the Arima-Iachello Interacting Boson Model (IBM) with an arbitrarily high potential barrier separating the phases. Classical and quantum analyses reveal markedly distinct behavior of the two phases: Deformed phase is completely regular, while the spherical phase shows highly chaotic dynamics, similar to the Hénon-Heiles system. Rotational bands with quasi-SU(3) characteristics built upon the regular vibrational spectrum of beta- and gamma-vibrations are observed in the deformed phase up to very high excitation energies.

## Key claim

First-order quantum phase transitions between spherical and axially deformed nuclei exhibit coexisting but well-separated regions of completely regular and completely chaotic dynamics within the same energy ranges, with the regularity arising from quasi-dynamical symmetry rather than exact dynamical symmetry.

## Method

The authors employed a Hamiltonian of the Interacting Boson Model (IBM) with an arbitrarily high potential barrier separating the spherical and deformed phases, using two parameterized forms (H₁ for the spherical side and H₂ for the deformed side) that coincide at the critical point. Classical dynamics were analyzed via expectation values in Glauber coherent states, while quantum dynamics were studied through Peres lattices, correlation coefficients in the SU(3) basis, and decomposition of rotational band states into SU(3) irreducible representations for N=50 bosons.

## Key numbers

- The correlation coefficient C_SU3(0-6) was calculated at 86 points between the U(5) dynamical symmetry limit (ρ=0) and the SU(3) dynamical symmetry limit (ξ=1) for N=50 bosons
- At the critical point (ξ=0) with β₀=√2, the deformed ground band shows Shannon entropy S_SU3(0₁)=0 reflecting partial dynamical symmetry, while other multiplets show S_SU3(0_i)>0 indicating quasi-dynamical symmetry
- SU(3) quasi-dynamical symmetry dominates the spectrum on the deformed side of the quantum phase transition for ξ≥0 at energies roughly below E=4ξ
- Multiple "triangular features" with C_SU3(0-6)≈1 appear in the phase coexistence region (between spinodal point ρ* and anti-spinodal point ξ**) up to high energies above E=2

## Context

This work extends the analysis of quantum phase transitions in the Interacting Boson Model beyond the three basic dynamical symmetries (U(5), SU(3), SO(6)) identified by Iachello, building on prior studies of partial dynamical symmetry (PDS) and quasi-dynamical symmetry (QDS) by Rowe and others. The finding that SU(3) quasi-dynamical symmetry persists to very high excitation energies connects to the Alhassid-Whelan arc of regularity and suggests that adiabatic separation of rotations and vibrations arises from underlying regular vibrational dynamics, enabling further study of excited-state quantum phase transitions in nuclear collective motion.
