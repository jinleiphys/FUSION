# On the relation between nuclear and nucleon Structure Functions and their moments

> Calculations of nuclear Structure Functions (SF) F_k^A(x,Q^2) routinely exploit a generalized convolution, involving the SF for nucleons F_k^N and the linking SF f^{PN,A} of a fictitious nucleus, composed of point-particles, with the latter usually expressed in terms of hadronic degrees of freedom. For finite Q^2 the approach seemed to be lacking a solid justification and the same is the case for recently proposed, effective nuclear parton distribution functions (pdf), which exactly reproduce the above-mentioned hadronically computed F_k^A. Many years ago Jaffe and West proved the above convolution in the Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (PWIA) for the nuclear components in the convolution. In the present note we extend the above proof to include classes of nuclear Final State Interactions (FSI). One and the same function appears to relate parton distribution functions (pdf) in nuclei and nucleons, and SF for nuclear targets and for nucleons. That relation is the previously conjectured one,with an entirely different interpretation of f^{PN,A}. We conclude with an extensive analysis of moments of nuclear SF based on the generalized convolution. Characteristics of those moments are shown to be quite similar to the same for a nucleon. We conclude that the above evidences asymptotic freedom of a nucleon in a medium and not of a composite nucleus.

## Key claim

The paper extends the proof of the generalized convolution relating nuclear and nucleon structure functions beyond the Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (PWIA) to include classes of nuclear Final State Interactions (FSI), showing that the previously conjectured convolution holds for finite Q² with an entirely different interpretation of the linking function f^{PN,A}.

## Method

The authors generalize the work of Jaffe and West by decomposing the forward scattering amplitude for a gamma-nucleus system into amplitudes for gamma-nucleon and nucleon-spectator nucleus scattering, then including classes of nuclear FSI such as those described by the Distorted Wave Impulse Approximation (DWIA) and the Gersch-Rodriguez-Smith (GRS) non-perturbative approach. They then analyze moments of nuclear structure functions using Mellin transforms of the generalized convolution, comparing theoretical predictions with experimental data from CERN NA-4, NA-2, and JLab experiments for deuterium, carbon, and iron targets.

## Key numbers

- The lower Q² limit for validity of the generalized convolution is approximately Q₀² ≈ 3 GeV²
- For Q² ≳ 20 GeV², FSI parts in the calculated structure function f^{PN,A} may be neglected
- The asymptotic limit of M^{A,th}(n=2, Q²→∞) equals 5/6 × N_f/(3N_f+16) = 0.1471 for N_f=6
- Moments m^A(n) for medium and heavy nuclei (A ≳ 12) reach asymptotic limits of approximately 1.082 for n=7
- For deuterium, moments m^A(n) reach asymptotic limits of approximately 1.027 for n=7
- The exponent d(n,N_f=6) increases from 0.507 to 1.397 for n between 2 and 7
- All F₂^A(x≈0.18, Q²) have a common value of approximately 0.30, approximately independent of A and Q²
- For x ≳ 0.22, Fe data from CERN NA-2 may have a normalization error of order 15-20%

## Context

This work extends the foundational proofs by Jaffe and West (1980s), which demonstrated the generalized convolution only in the PWIA and were published in conference proceedings and subsequently overlooked. The paper also builds on the GRS approach originally formulated for non-relativistic point-particles by Gersch, Rodriguez, and Smith (1973) and later extended to composite systems by Gurvitz and Rinat (1995). The analysis enables a separation of nucleonic and nuclear dependencies in structure function moments, showing that the observed ln(Q²) behavior in nuclear moments reflects asymptotic freedom of isolated nucleons rather than composite nuclei, with medium modifications of nucleon parameters controlled by the function σ^A.
