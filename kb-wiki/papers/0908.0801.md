# Partial Dynamical Symmetry and Anharmonicity in Gamma-Soft Nuclei

> Partial dynamical symmetry is shown to be relevant for describing the anharmonicity of excited bands in $^{196}$Pt while retaining solvability and good SO(6) symmetry for the ground band.

## Key claim

Partial dynamical symmetry (PDS) is shown to be relevant for describing the anharmonicity of excited bands in \(^{196}\)Pt while retaining exact solvability and good SO(6) symmetry for the ground band, but the claim is conditional on the specific choice of a three-body interaction term \(\hat{P}_{+}\hat{n}_s\hat{P}_{-}\) that breaks the dynamical symmetry only in excited bands.

## Method

The authors construct a Hamiltonian \(\hat{H}_{\rm PDS}\) in the interacting boson model (IBM) by adding a three-body term \(\eta\hat{P}_{+}\hat{n}_s\hat{P}_{-}\) to the standard SO(6) dynamical symmetry (DS) Hamiltonian \(\hat{H}_{\rm DS}\). This three-body term is built from SO(6) tensor operators that annihilate all states in the \(\Sigma=N\) ground-band irrep, ensuring those states remain solvable with good SO(6) quantum numbers, while states in excited bands (\(\Sigma < N\)) become admixed. The resulting spectrum and E2 transition rates are compared to experimental data for \(^{196}\)Pt.

## Key numbers

- The empirical anharmonicity of the \(0^+\) bandhead states in \(^{196}\)Pt, defined as \(R = E(v=2)/E(v=1) - 2\), is \(R = -0.70\).
- The SO(6)-DS value for the anharmonicity with boson number \(N=6\) is \(R = -0.29\), in marked disagreement with experiment.
- The SO(6)-PDS calculation yields an anharmonicity of \(R = -0.63\), much closer to the empirical value of \(-0.70\).
- The Hamiltonian parameters for \(\hat{H}_{\rm PDS}\) are \(A = 122.9\) keV, \(B = 44.0\) keV, \(C = 17.9\) keV, and \(\eta = 34.9\) keV.
- The E2 operator uses effective charge \(e_{\rm b} = 0.151\) \(e\)b and \(\chi = 0.29\).
- The \(0^+_2\) state (first excited bandhead) at 1403 keV has 76.5% \(\Sigma=4\) purity, while the \(0^+_3\) state (second excited bandhead) at 1823 keV has 62.0% \(\Sigma=0\) purity in the PDS calculation.

## Context

This work extends the SO(6) dynamical symmetry description of \(^{196}\)Pt, originally proposed by Cizewski (1978) based on ground-band properties, by addressing the poor fit to excited bands. It builds on the finding by Ramos (2000) that large anharmonicities in double-phonon excitations require at least cubic terms in the IBM Hamiltonian. The PDS approach provides a systematic method to introduce such higher-order interactions without destroying the good DS results for the ground band, and the same procedure can be applied to general dynamical symmetry chains in both bosonic and fermionic systems.
