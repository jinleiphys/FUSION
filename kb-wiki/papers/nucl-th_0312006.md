# Electron Scattering on 3He - a Playground to Test Nuclear Dynamics

> The big spectrum of electron induced processes on 3He is illustrated by several examples based on Faddeev calculations with modern nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon forces as well as exchange currents. The kinematical region is restricted to a mostly nonrelativistic one where the three-nucleon c.m. energy is below the pion production threshold and the three-momentum of the virtual photon is sufficiently below the nucleon mass. Comparisons with available data are shown and cases of agreement and disagreement are found. It is argued that new and precise data are needed to systematically check the present day dynamical ingredients.

## Key claim
The paper argues that electron scattering on 3He provides a sensitive test of nuclear dynamics, but that new and precise data are needed to systematically check the present-day dynamical ingredients, because comparisons with available data show both agreement and disagreement.

## Method
The authors performed Faddeev calculations for electron-induced processes on 3He using modern nucleon-nucleon (NN) forces (AV18) and three-nucleon forces (Urbana IX), together with pi- and rho-exchange meson-exchange currents (MEC). They solved Faddeev-like integral equations for the nuclear matrix elements and response functions, restricting kinematics to a nonrelativistic regime where the three-nucleon center-of-mass energy is below the pion production threshold and the virtual photon three-momentum is sufficiently below the nucleon mass.

## Key numbers
- Asymmetry A calculated at q^2 = 0.1 (GeV/c)^2 for the inclusive scattering of polarized electrons on polarized 3He, showing sensitivity to final-state interactions, MEC, and three-nucleon forces.
- Magnetic neutron form factor G_M^n extracted from 3He at q^2 = 0.1 and 0.2 (GeV/c)^2, agreeing with values extracted from deuteron measurements.
- Response functions R_L and R_T calculated at Q = 300, 400, 500, and 600 MeV/c; at Q = 300 and 400 MeV/c, inclusion of three-nucleon forces leads to agreement with data for R_L.
- Elastic charge and magnetic form factors of 3He and 3H shown for momentum transfers up to q = 3 fm^-1, where relativistic effects become important.
- For the pd-breakup of 3He at Q = 250 MeV/c, final-state interaction effects are strong in both proton and deuteron knockout peaks; at Q = 431 MeV/c, only the deuteron peak is strongly affected by FSI.

## Context
This work extends earlier Faddeev calculations of three-nucleon scattering (Gloeckle et al., 1996) and photon-induced breakup (Golak et al., 2002) to electron-induced processes on 3He. It uses the AV18 NN potential (Wiringa et al., 1995) and Urbana IX three-nucleon force (Pieper et al., 2001) with currents from Riska (1985). The results enable systematic tests of nuclear dynamics in the three-nucleon system and provide a framework for extracting neutron form factors from 3He data, though relativistic effects need to be included at higher momentum transfers.
