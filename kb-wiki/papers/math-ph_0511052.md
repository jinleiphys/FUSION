# Vector coherent state theory of the generic representations of so(5) in an so(3) basis

> For applications of group theory in quantum mechanics, one generally needs explicit matrix representations of the spectrum generating algebras that arise in bases that reduce the symmetry group of some Hamiltonian of interest. Here we use vector coherent state techniques to develop an algorithm for constructing the matrices for arbitrary finite-dimensional irreps of the SO(5) Lie algebra in an SO(3) basis. The SO(3) subgroup of SO(5) is defined by regarding SO(5) as linear transformations of the five-dimensional space of an SO(3) irrep of angular momentum two. A need for such irreps arises in the nuclear collective model of quadrupole vibrations and rotations. The algorithm has been implemented in MAPLE, and some tables of results are presented.

## Key claim

The paper develops an algorithm for constructing explicit matrix representations of arbitrary finite-dimensional irreducible representations (irreps) of the SO(5) Lie algebra in an SO(3) basis, using vector coherent state (VCS) techniques, but the construction is restricted to generic irreps labeled by two integers (v,f) and requires that the SO(3) subgroup be chosen such that no angular momentum operator lies within the intrinsic u(2) subalgebra (Theorem 1).

## Method

The authors construct VCS wave functions for so(5) irreps as vector-valued functions over SO(3), using a model space built from tensor products of five-dimensional and four-dimensional harmonic oscillator Hilbert spaces that carry the fundamental (10) and (0,1/2) irreps. They define an SO(3) subgroup embedded in SO(5) via a specific transformation of the oscillator creation operators, express the so(5) Lie algebra in both a Cartan basis and an SO(3) tensor basis, and use Theorem 2 to generate basis wave functions for arbitrary (v,f) irreps by taking model products of wave functions from the fundamental irreps. The algorithm is implemented in MAPLE.

## Key numbers

- The fundamental five-dimensional irrep (10) has highest weight (10) and is carried by creation operators {η†_ν; ν = 0, ±1, ±2} of a five-dimensional harmonic oscillator.
- The fundamental four-dimensional irrep (0,1/2) has highest weight (0,1/2) and is carried by creation operators {ζ†_m; m = ±1/2, ±3/2} of a four-dimensional harmonic oscillator.
- The so(5) Lie algebra is spanned by SO(3) tensors of angular momentum L=1 and L=3, with the L=1 operators {L_k} and L=3 operators {O_ν} satisfying commutation relations with specific coefficients: [L_k, O_ν] = 2√3 (1ν,3k|1,k+ν) O_{k+ν} and [O_μ, O_ν] = 2√7 (3ν,3μ|1,μ+ν) L_{μ+ν} - √6 (3ν,3μ|3,μ+ν) O_{μ+ν}.
- The highest grade states for a generic (v,f) irrep are given by |(vf)m⟩ = (ζ†_{3/2})^{f+m} (ζ†_{1/2})^{f-m} (η†_2)^v / √((f+m)!(f-m)!v!) |0⟩, with m = -f, ..., f.

## Context

This work extends earlier VCS constructions of so(5) irreps in an su(2) (isospin) basis by Hecht (1965) and Kemmer, and the construction of (v,0) and (0,f) irreps in an so(3) basis by Rowe and Hecht. It also parallels the VCS construction of su(3) irreps in an so(3) basis by Rowe, Repka, and collaborators. The algorithm enables explicit matrix element calculations for the nuclear collective model of quadrupole vibrations and rotations (Bohr-Mottelson model) and the Interacting Boson Model, where so(5) irreps in an angular momentum basis are required.
