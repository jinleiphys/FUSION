# Directed flow as a signal of a liquid state of transient matter

> Directed flow $v_1$, observable introduced for description of nucleus collisions is discussed. We consider a possible origin of the flow in hadronic reactions as a result of rotation of the transient matter and trace analogy with nucleus collisions. It is argued that the presence of directed flow can serve as a signal that transient matter is in a liquid state.

# Directed flow as a signal of a liquid state of transient matter

## Key claim
Directed flow $v_1$ in hadronic reactions can originate from coherent rotation of the transient matter in the overlap region, and the presence of such flow signals that the transient matter is in a strongly interacting liquid state rather than a weakly interacting gas. The claim is hedged by the assumption that rotation is the only mechanism generating directed flow; if the transient matter becomes weakly interacting at higher energies, directed flow would vanish.

## Method
The author extends a chiral quark model of hadron interactions (Troshin and Tyurin, earlier works) by incorporating collective rotation of the quark-pion liquid formed in the overlap region of non-central collisions. The model assumes that the orbital angular momentum of the overlap region is realized as coherent rotation of the strongly interacting transient matter, and that valence constituent quarks excite peripheral parts of this rotating liquid, producing hadrons with a net transverse momentum asymmetry. The directed flow $v_1$ is related to the inverse depth of excitation $\Delta l$, which in turn depends on the rapidity difference $|y - y_{beam}|$ and on the product of the elastic quark scattering cross-section and the liquid density.

## Key numbers
- Constituent quark interaction radius: $R_Q \simeq 1$ fm (from elastic scattering analysis with $\kappa \simeq 2$ in $R_Q = \kappa/m_Q$).
- Constituent quark geometrical radius: $r_Q \simeq 0.2$ fm.
- Transverse momentum where maximal $v_1$ is expected: $p_\perp \sim 1$ GeV/$c$ (since $r_Q \simeq 0.2$ fm).
- Directed flow scaling: $|v_1| \sim 1/|y - y_{beam}|$, independent of incident energy.
- Asymptotic cross-section dependencies: $\sigma_{tot,el} \sim \ln^2 s$, $\sigma_{inel} \sim \ln s$, $\bar{n} \sim s^\delta$.

## Context
This work extends the concept of anisotropic flow from heavy-ion collisions at RHIC (where the sQGP was discovered and characterized as a perfect liquid) to hadronic reactions, arguing that the same liquid state of transient matter can appear in $pp$ collisions. The author draws on the experimental observation of incident-energy scaling of $v_1$ at RHIC (STAR Collaboration, preliminary data) and proposes that this scaling follows from the rotation mechanism. The model builds on earlier work by Troshin and Tyurin on elastic scattering and multiparticle production using a chiral quark model, and references the shock-wave production mechanism of Heisenberg. The paper suggests that at LHC and NICA energies, if directed flow persists with the same scaling, it indicates the transient matter remains strongly interacting; if it vanishes, that would signal formation of a weakly interacting quark-gluon plasma.
