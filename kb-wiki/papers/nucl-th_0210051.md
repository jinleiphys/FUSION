# Goldstone-Brueckner Perturbation Theory Extended in Terms of Mixed Non-Orthogonal Slater-Determinants

> The Goldstone-Brueckner perturbation theory is extended to incorporate in a simple way correlations associated with large amplitude collective motions in nuclei. The new energy expansion making use of non-orthogonal vacua still allows to remove the divergences originating from the hard-core of the bare interaction. This is done through the definition of a new Brueckner matrix summing generalized Brueckner ladders. At the lowest-order, this formalism motivates variational calculations beyond the mean-field such as the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) and the Projected Mean-Field Method from a perturbative point of view for the first time. Going to higher orders amounts to incorporate diabatic effects in the GCM and to extend the projection technique from product states to well-defined correlated states.

## Key claim

The paper extends Goldstone-Brueckner perturbation theory to incorporate correlations from large amplitude collective motions in nuclei by using a linear combination of non-orthogonal Slater determinants as the unperturbed reference, rather than a single mean-field state. The formalism provides a perturbative justification for variational methods like the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) and Projected Mean-Field Method, but only at lowest order; higher orders introduce diabatic effects not captured by those variational approaches.

## Method

The author chooses a set of mean-field Hamiltonians \(h_0^\alpha\) with corresponding non-degenerate ground states \(|\Phi_0^\alpha\rangle\) and residual interactions \(h_1^\alpha\). Each vacuum is evolved independently via the Gell-Mann-Low adiabatic theorem to obtain the exact ground state \(|\Theta_0^\alpha\rangle\) in the standard Brueckner-Goldstone linked/irreducible expansion using a generalized \(G\)-matrix. An arbitrary linear combination \(|\Theta_0\rangle = \sum_\alpha f_\alpha |\Theta_0^\alpha\rangle\) is then formed, and the total energy is expressed as the mean value of the Hamiltonian in this superposition, yielding a double sum over \(\alpha,\beta\) of vacuum-to-vacuum diagrams connecting different non-orthogonal Slater determinants.

## Key numbers

* No explicit numerical results are reported in the paper; the work is purely formal and diagrammatic.

## Context

This work extends the standard Goldstone-Brueckner perturbation theory (Brueckner, 1955; Goldstone, 1957) which was limited to small amplitude correlations around a single mean-field vacuum. It provides the first perturbative foundation for variational configuration mixing methods such as the Generator Coordinate Method (Hill, 1953; Bonche, 1976) and the Projected Mean-Field Method (Yoccoz, 1957; Dietrich, 1964). The formalism enables future systematic classification of correlations included or omitted in such variational calculations and defines a microscopically derived effective interaction for use in large amplitude collective motion studies.
