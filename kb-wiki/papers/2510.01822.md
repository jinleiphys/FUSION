# Particle momentum spectra, correlations, and maximum entropy principle in high-multiplicity collision events

> In this paper, we utilize the maximum entropy prescription to determine a quantum state of a small collision system at the kinetic freeze-out. We derive expressions for multiplicity-selected particle momentum spectra and correlation functions by applying a fixed particle number constraint to this state. The results of our analysis can be useful for interpreting the multiplicity dependence of the particle momentum spectra and correlations in high-multiplicity $pp$ collision events at a fixed LHC energy.

# Particle momentum spectra, correlations, and maximum entropy principle in high-multiplicity collision events

## Key claim

The maximum entropy principle can be used to construct a quantum state at kinetic freeze-out for small collision systems, and the resulting multiplicity-selected correlation functions show that for very high multiplicities (N >> ⟨N⟩), the correlation radius parameters become approximately independent of multiplicity, potentially resolving the apparent contradiction with hydrodynamic expectations observed in LHC pp data.

## Method

The author applies the maximum entropy prescription to determine a quantum state at the kinetic freeze-out hypersurface, constrained by the one-particle Wigner function derived from hydrodynamics. A fixed particle number constraint is then applied to this generalized Gibbs state, yielding expressions for multiplicity-selected one- and two-particle momentum spectra and correlation functions via Gaudin's method. The analysis assumes homogeneity regions where the Lagrange multiplier function λ(x,p) varies slowly, and that the characteristic size of these regions is much larger than microscopic correlation lengths.

## Key numbers

- The one-particle Wigner function at freeze-out is given by ⟨f̂(x,p)⟩ = 1/(2π)³ × 1/(e^{λ(x,p)} - 1), analogous to an ideal Bose gas distribution
- The Lagrange multiplier λ(x,p) is related to the Wigner function by λ(x,p) ≈ ln[1 + 1/((2π)³f(x,p))]
- The recursive formula for partition functions is nZ_n = Σ_{s=1}^{n} Z_{n-s} ∫ d³p G_s*(p,p), with Z_0 = 1 by definition
- The correlation function C_N(p₁,p₂) is defined as the ratio of the two-particle spectrum to the product of single-particle spectra at fixed multiplicity N

## Context

This work extends the maximum entropy approach of Jaynes (1957) and Zubarev's nonequilibrium statistical operator formalism to small collision systems at LHC energies. It addresses the puzzle from experimental measurements by ATLAS (2017) and CMS (2011) showing that correlation radius parameters become approximately independent of charged-particle multiplicity in very high-multiplicity pp events, contrary to expectations from hydrodynamic scaling observed in heavy-ion collisions (Sinyukov, 1994). The results enable interpretation of multiplicity-dependent momentum spectra and Bose-Einstein correlations in high-multiplicity pp collisions at fixed LHC energy, and suggest that the effective system size depends on the mean multiplicity ⟨N⟩ rather than the selected multiplicity N.
