# Fermion Systems with Fuzzy Symmetries (Leveraging the Known to Understand the Unknown)

> Microscopic models, which embody the simplicity and significance of a dynamical symmetry approach to nuclear structure, are reviewed. They can reveal striking features of atomic nuclei when a symmetry dominates and solutions in domains that may otherwise be unreachable.

## Key claim

Microscopic models based on dynamical symmetries can reveal striking features of atomic nuclei even when the symmetries are not exact but only "fuzzy" (broken but dominant), allowing truncation of model spaces to a fraction of the size required by models that do not exploit such approximate symmetries.

## Method

The paper reviews three algebraic fermion models: the symplectic Sp(4) pairing model for medium-mass nuclei, the pseudo-SU(3) plus intruder level model for heavy nuclei, and the no-core shell model (NCSM) combined with the Sp(3) symplectic extension for light nuclei. The Sp(4) model uses a six-parameter Hamiltonian with dynamical symmetry to describe pairing-governed 0+ states across 306 nuclei. The pseudo-SU(3) model performs m-scheme shell-model calculations in the fpg model space for 58Cu and 64Ge, comparing results with a renormalized interaction in a truncated upper-fp subspace. The NCSM+Sp(3) approach constructs symplectic basis states up to 6-hbar-omega for 12C and compares their overlaps with full NCSM wavefunctions.

## Key numbers

- The Sp(4) model predicts the binding energy of 48Ni to be 348.1 MeV, which is 0.07% greater than the semi-empirical estimate of Moller (1997) and 4% away from the later experimental value.
- The 68Se waiting-point nuclide along the rp-path is estimated to be 574.3 MeV, only 2.7% away from the 2004 precise mass measurement.
- The model predicts the lowest isobaric analog 0+ state energies for odd-odd nuclei: 358.62 MeV (44V), 359.34 MeV (46Mn), 357.49 MeV (48Co), and 394.20 MeV (50Co).
- For the fpg shell, the two-proton drip line predicted by the Sp(4) model lies near even-even nuclei including 60Ge, 64Se, 68Kr, 72Sr, 76Zr, 78Zr, 82Mo, 86Ru, 90Pd, and 94Cd.
- The quadratic mean difference in two-proton separation energies between the Sp(4) model and other theoretical predictions is 0.32 MeV compared to Ormand (1997), 0.78 MeV compared to Moller (1997), and 0.43 MeV compared to Brown (2002).
- For 12C, three Sp(3) irreducible representations (1098 symplectic states, only 0.003% of the NCSM space dimension) capture approximately 78% of the NCSM wavefunction for J=0, 2, and 4 states at hbar-Omega=15 MeV.
- The leading (0 4)S=0 Sp(3) irrep contributes 62.91% to the J=0 ground state, 63.18% to the J=2 state, and 67.33% to the J=4 state.
- The B(E2:2+1 -> 0+g.s.) value from the three-irrep symplectic approximation is 81% of the full NCSM estimate.

## Context

This review extends the dynamical symmetry approach to nuclear structure pioneered by Elliott (1958) for SU(3) in light nuclei and by Racah (1943) and Flowers (1952) for seniority-based classification. The Sp(4) pairing model builds on earlier work by Helmers (1961) and Hecht and Ginocchio (1970), while the pseudo-SU(3) model extends the pseudo-spin symmetry discovered by Hecht and Adler (1969) and Arima, Harvey, and Shimizu (1969). The NCSM+Sp(3) approach combines the symplectic shell model reviewed by Rowe (1985) with the no-core shell model of Navratil, Vary, and Barrett (2000), enabling future extensions to higher oscillator quanta and heavier nuclei that would otherwise be computationally inaccessible.
