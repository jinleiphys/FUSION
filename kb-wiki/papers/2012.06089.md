# Collisional energy loss and the Chiral Magnetic Effect

> Collisional energy loss of a fast particle in a medium is mostly due to the medium polarization by the electromagnetic fields of the particle. A small fraction of energy is carried away by the Cherenkov radiation. In chiral medium there is an additional contribution to the energy loss due to induction of the anomalous current proportional to the magnetic field. It causes the particle to lose energy mostly in the form of the \emph{chiral} Cherenkov radiation. We employ classical electrodynamics, adequate in a wide range of particle energies, to compute the collisional energy loss by a fast particle in a homogenous chiral plasma and apply the results to Quark-Gluon Plasma and a Weyl semimetal. In the later case photon spectrum is strongly enhanced in the ultraviolet and X-ray regions which makes it amenable to experimental investigation. Our main observation is that while the collisional energy loss in a non-chiral medium is a slow, at most logarithmic, function of energy $E$, the chiral Cherenkov radiation is proportional to $E^2$ when the recoil is neglected and to $E$ when it is taken into account.

## Key claim

While the abstract states that chiral Cherenkov radiation is proportional to E² when recoil is neglected and to E when it is taken into account, the paper's actual hedge is that the classical calculation (neglecting recoil) is adequate only when the particle's Lorentz factor γ is much less than m/σχ, where m is the particle mass and σχ is the chiral conductivity; beyond that regime, quantum recoil effects reduce the energy dependence from E² to E.

## Method

The authors solve Maxwell's equations modified by the anomalous current j_A = σχ B (the Chiral Magnetic Effect) for a point charge q moving at constant velocity v through a homogeneous chiral plasma. They compute the electromagnetic fields in terms of helicity eigenstates and then evaluate the energy loss rate as the flux of the Poynting vector through a coaxial cylinder of radius a around the particle's path. The frequency spectrum of the energy loss is obtained by integrating over the field components, with the chiral Cherenkov radiation isolated as the part of the energy flux that remains finite as the cylinder radius goes to infinity.

## Key numbers

- Energy loss due to the anomaly for an ultrarelativistic particle: -dε/dz = (q²/4πv²)[ω_p² ln(v/(aω_p)) + (1/4)γ²σχ²], where the second term dominates at high energies (Eq. b25).
- Total radiated power from chiral Cherenkov radiation in the classical limit: P = (q²/4π)(σχ²γ²/4) (Eq. k4).
- Total radiated power with quantum recoil effects included: P^quant = (q²/4π)(σχ ε/3) (Eq. d60), reducing the energy dependence from ε² to ε.
- Ratio of chiral Cherenkov energy loss to Bethe-Heitler radiative loss: Δε^{χC}/Δε^{BH} ~ σχ/(e²T) ~ μ₅/T, where T is plasma temperature and μ₅ is the axial chemical potential (Eq. s1).
- For a d-quark in Quark-Gluon Plasma with γ=20, plasma parameters ω_p=0.16T, Γ=1.11T, m=T=250 MeV, and σχ=10 MeV: the rate of energy loss due to the anomaly is about 10⁻⁴ of particle energy per fm (from discussion of Fig. 1).
- For a Weyl semimetal: b=(α/π)80 eV, ω_p=0.5 eV, Γ=0.025 eV, m=0.5 MeV, σχ=0.19 eV (from Fig. 2 caption).
- Photon spectrum in the chiral Cherenkov radiation extends up to ω_M = ε/(1+m²/(σχ ε)) (Eq. d56), and for the semimetal case ω_M ≲ σχγ² ≪ ε, making recoil negligible.

## Context

This work extends Fermi's (1940) classical calculation of collisional energy loss in non-chiral media to chiral media with anomalous electromagnetic response, using field solutions derived by Tuchin (2020). The chiral Cherenkov radiation was previously computed in leading-order QED by Tuchin (2018), and the present paper provides the classical electrodynamic counterpart valid when recoil is negligible. The results are applied to Quark-Gluon Plasma (using lattice data from Ding 2010 for plasma parameters) and to Weyl semimetals (using material parameters from Xu 2015 and Lv 2015). The paper enables experimental investigation of the chiral anomaly via enhanced ultraviolet and X-ray photon emission from fast particles in Weyl semimetals, and suggests that in nuclear matter with topological domains of nearly constant density (Zhitnitsky 2012, 2013), coherence effects on chiral Cherenkov radiation may be negligible.
