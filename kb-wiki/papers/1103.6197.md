# Charmonium production in heavy-ion collisions from SPS to LHC

> Using the two-component model that includes charmonium production from initial nucleon-nucleon hard scattering and regeneration in the produced quark-gluon plasma, we study $J/ψ$ production in heavy ion collisions at SPS, RHIC and LHC. For the expansion dynamics of produced hot dense matter, we use a schematic viscous hydrodynamic model with the specific shear viscosity taken, respectively, to be twice and ten times the lower bound of $1/4π$ suggested by the Ads/CFT correspondence in the quark-gluon plasma and in the hadron gas. For the initial dissociation and the subsequent thermal decay of charmonia in the hot dense matter, we use the screened Cornell potential to describe the properties of charmonia and the perturbative QCD to calculate their dissociation cross sections. Including regeneration of charmonia in the quark-gluon plasma via a kinetic equation with in-medium chamonium decay widths, we obtain a good description of measured nuclear modification factors at SPS and RHIC. A reasonable description of measured nuclear modification factor of $J/ψ$s of high transverse momenta in heavy ion collisions at LHC is also obtained if we include the contribution to the $J/ψ$ yield from the decay of bottomed hadrons.

## Key claim

Using a two-component model that includes both initial hard-scattering production and regeneration in quark-gluon plasma, the authors obtain a good description of measured J/ψ nuclear modification factors at SPS and RHIC, and a reasonable description at LHC only when including the contribution from bottom-hadron decay for high-transverse-momentum J/ψ.

## Method

The authors model J/ψ production in heavy-ion collisions at SPS (√sNN=17.3 GeV), RHIC (√sNN=200 GeV), and LHC (√sNN=2.76 TeV) using a two-component framework that combines primordial production from nucleon-nucleon hard scattering with regeneration from charm and anticharm quarks in the quark-gluon plasma. The expansion dynamics of the hot dense matter is described by a schematic viscous hydrodynamic model with specific shear viscosity of 0.16 (twice the AdS/CFT lower bound) in the QGP and 5/2π (ten times the lower bound) in the hadron gas. Charmonium properties in the QGP are calculated using the screened Cornell potential, and dissociation cross sections are computed from perturbative QCD, with the evolution of charmonium abundances governed by a rate equation that includes in-medium decay widths.

## Key numbers

- Initial average temperatures from viscous hydrodynamics: 218 MeV at SPS, 269 MeV at RHIC, and 311 MeV at LHC for central collisions
- Firecylinder transverse radius at initial thermalization: 6.5 fm at SPS, 6.6 fm at RHIC, and 7.1 fm at LHC for central collisions
- J/ψ dissociation temperature: approximately 300 MeV for QCD coupling constant g=1.87
- Specific shear viscosity in QGP: 0.16 for SPS and RHIC, 0.2 for LHC
- Specific shear viscosity in hadron gas: 5/2π for all collision energies
- Initial thermalization time: 1.0 fm/c at SPS, 0.9 fm/c at RHIC, 1.05 fm/c at LHC
- J/ψ absorption cross section by nucleons: 4.18 mb at SPS, 2.8 mb at RHIC
- Differential J/ψ production cross section in p+p collisions: 0.05 μb at SPS, 0.774 μb at RHIC, 4.0 μb at LHC
- Differential cc-bar pair production cross section in p+p collisions: 5.7 μb at SPS, 119 μb at RHIC, 615 μb at LHC
- Charged particle multiplicity per half participant in central collisions: approximately 2 at SPS, 4 at RHIC, 8.4 at LHC

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous two-component model study of J/ψ production at RHIC (Song, 2010) to include SPS and LHC energies, while incorporating a schematic viscous hydrodynamic model that was neglected in their earlier studies. The approach builds on the framework developed by Grandchamp and Rapp (2002, 2003) for charmonium regeneration in the QGP, and uses the screened Cornell potential from Karsch (1987) for in-medium charmonium properties. The results enable predictions for J/ψ production at LHC energies and demonstrate that bottom-hadron decay contributions become important for high-transverse-momentum J/ψ at LHC, which will inform future experimental analyses by the ALICE, CMS, and ATLAS collaborations.
