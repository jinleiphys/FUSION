# Weakly Bound Cluster States of Efimov Character

> We study the behavior of weakly bound clusters and their relation to the well-known three-body Efimov states. We adopt a model to describe universal behavior of strongly interacting bosonic systems, and we test its validity by reproducing predictions of three- and four-body universal states. Then, we extend our study to larger systems and identify a series of universal cluster states that can be qualitatively interpreted as adding one particle at a time to an Efimov trimer. The properties of these cluster states and their experimental signatures are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for each Efimov trimer state, there exists at least one universal N-body cluster state (for N up to 13) that is controlled only by two-body scattering length and the three-body parameter, contradicting earlier proposals of an "N-body Efimov effect" that would require additional parameters. The hedge is that these cluster states are only predicted for the regime of negative scattering length (a < 0) and that nonuniversal corrections may be crucial for determining the existence of excited states under experimental conditions.

## Method

The author constructs a model Hamiltonian with two-body finite-range potentials and a repulsive three-body hard-wall potential in the hyperradius, designed to reproduce Efimov physics while avoiding the Thomas collapse. Using diffusion Monte Carlo (DMC) for systems up to N=13 and correlated-Gaussian (CG) basis-set expansion for systems up to N=6, the ground-state energies and pair-correlation functions of bosonic clusters are computed at unitarity and as functions of scattering length.

## Key numbers

- The lowest two Efimov trimer momenta at unitarity are κ₀ ≈ 0.04622/Rc and κ₁ ≈ 0.0020359/Rc, obeying the Efimov scaling relation within 0.1%
- At unitarity, the four-body ground-state energy is E^U_4b ≈ 4.66(4) × E^U_3b
- At unitarity, the five-body ground-state energy is E^U_5b ≈ 10.64(4) × E^U_3b
- At unitarity, the six-body ground-state energy is E^U_6b ≈ 18.59(5) × E^U_3b
- At unitarity, the thirteen-body ground-state energy is E^U_13b ≈ 88.0(7) × E^U_3b
- The critical scattering-length ratios are a*_4b/a*_3b ≈ 0.42(1), a*_5b/a*_4b ≈ 0.60(1), and a*_6b/a*_5b ≈ 0.71(1)
- The critical scattering lengths for 3 ≤ N ≤ 8 follow 1/(κ₀ a*_Nb) ≈ 2.3(1) - N
- The Tjon line relation for four-body states is E_4b ≈ 2.66 E^U_3b + 2.0 E_3b
- The trimer energies are approximately 10⁵ times smaller than the typical two-body interaction energy ħ²/(m r₀²)

## Context

This work extends the universal Efimov physics framework, previously established for three-body systems by Efimov (1970) and for four-body systems by von Stecher (2009) and Hammer & Platter (2007), to clusters with up to 13 particles. The results contradict earlier restricted-approximation studies by Faddeev and collaborators that proposed an "N-body Efimov effect" requiring new parameters. The predictions enable experimental searches for resonant loss features in ultracold bosonic gases, such as a predicted five-body resonance at approximately 250 a₀ in cesium experiments, and suggest that cluster states can be created in optical lattices by extending the trimer production proposal of Stoll & Köhler (2005).
