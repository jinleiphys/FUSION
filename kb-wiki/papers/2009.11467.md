# Theoretical Analysis of Antineutron-Nucleus Data needed for Antineutron Mirrors in Neutron-Antineutron Oscillation Experiments

> The values of the antineutron-nucleus scattering lengths, and in particular their imaginary parts, are needed to evaluate the feasibility of using neutron mirrors in laboratory experiments to search for neutron-antineutron oscillations. We analyze existing experimental and theoretical constraints on these values with emphasis on low $A$ nuclei and use the results to suggest materials for the neutron/antineutron guide and to evaluate the systematic uncertainties in estimating the neutron-antineutron oscillation time. As an example we discuss a scenario for a future neutron-antineutron oscillation experiment proposed for the European Spallation Source. We also suggest future experiments which can provide a better determination of the values of antineutron-nuclei scattering lengths.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the systematic uncertainty in the neutron-antineutron oscillation time due to the present uncertainty in the imaginary part of the antineutron-nucleus scattering length is only about 0.5% for a typical uncertainty of 10% in that scattering length, and even an order-of-magnitude worse knowledge would not cause unacceptable loss of experimental sensitivity, provided the observation time is noticeably shorter than the antineutron lifetime in the guide.

## Method

The authors analyze existing experimental data on antiproton-nucleus interactions (atomic level shifts and widths, annihilation cross sections, and elastic differential cross sections) for deuterium and carbon, and use optical model potentials to extract the corresponding antineutron-nucleus scattering lengths. They compare two classes of optical models (strong annihilation with standard diffuseness versus shallow annihilation with double diffuseness) against the available data to determine which model is consistent with all observables, and then use the preferred model to estimate the antineutron scattering lengths needed for guide design.

## Key numbers

- For the antineutron-deuteron system, the spin-averaged scattering length is estimated as \(a^{s}_0(\bar n ^2\text{H}) = [(0.7\pm 0.2) - i(0.79\pm 0.07)]\) fm.
- For the antineutron-carbon system, the strong annihilation model with standard diffuseness gives \(a^{s}_0(\bar n\text{C}) = 3.22 - 1.16 i\) fm, while the double-diffuseness model gives \(a^{s}_0(\bar n\text{C}) = 5.36 - 3.57 i\) fm.
- The characteristic lifetime due to annihilation of antineutrons inside a neutron/antineutron guide is \(\tau_{\bar n} \sim 2\) s.
- For a proposed experiment at the European Spallation Source with a guide length of \(L_{\text{guide}} \sim 300\) m and mean neutron velocity \(v_n \sim 750\) m/s, the mean observation time is \(\tau_{\text{obs}} \sim 0.4\) s, giving a ratio \(\tau_{\bar n}/\tau_{\text{obs}} \sim 5\).
- For a 10% uncertainty in the imaginary part of the antineutron scattering length, the uncertainty in estimating the neutron-antineutron oscillation time is \(\sim 0.5\%\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous proposal for using coherent reflection of a neutron-antineutron superposition from guide walls to increase observation time in oscillation experiments (Protasov et al., 2019). It addresses the lack of direct experimental measurements of antineutron-nucleus scattering lengths by analyzing constraints from antiproton data on deuterium (Augsburger, 1999; Zenoni, 1999) and carbon (Roberson, 1977; Garreta, 1984), and comparing optical models by Batty et al. (1989, 1995, 2001) and Wong et al. (1984) with newer data. The results enable optimization of guide materials and evaluation of systematic uncertainties for future experiments at the European Spallation Source, and also apply to coherent propagation in gas (Gudkov, 2020).
