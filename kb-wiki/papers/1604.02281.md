# Clustering in stable and exotic nuclei

> Since the pioneering discovery of molecular resonances in the 12C+12C reaction more than half a century ago a great deal of research work has been undertaken in alpha clustering. Our knowledge on physics of nuclear molecules has increased considerably and nuclear clustering remains one of the most fruitful domains of nuclear physics, facing some of the greatest challenges and opportunities in the years ahead. The occurrence of "exotic" shapes and Bose-Einstein alpha condensates in light N=Z alpha-conjugate nuclei is investigated. Various approaches of the superdeformed and hyperdeformed bands associated with quasimolecular resonant structures are presented. Evolution of clustering from stability to the drip-lines is examined: clustering aspects are, in particular, discussed for light exotic nuclei with large neutron excess such as neutron-rich Oxygen isotopes with their complete spectroscopy.

# Clustering in stable and exotic nuclei

## Key claim

Clustering phenomena, including quasimolecular resonances, superdeformed and hyperdeformed shapes, and Bose-Einstein alpha condensates, are observed across light N=Z alpha-conjugate nuclei and extend to neutron-rich isotopes, but many predicted exotic configurations (such as linear alpha chains and ternary clusterizations) have not yet been definitively observed experimentally despite decades of investigation.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental and theoretical work on alpha clustering in light nuclei, covering resonant scattering measurements (e.g., $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C, $^{12}$C+$^{16}$O, $^{28}$Si+$\alpha$ reactions), gamma-ray spectroscopy with arrays such as Euroball IV and Gammasphere, radiative capture experiments using the DRAGON spectrometer at Triumf, and charged-particle coincidence measurements with the Binary Reaction Spectrometer at the Vivitron facility. Theoretical frameworks discussed include the Nilsson-Strutinsky approach, cranked alpha-cluster model, Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (AMD), Generator-Coordinate-Method, and energy density functional (EDF) calculations.

## Key numbers

- The Hoyle state in $^{12}$C is an excited 0$^+$ state at 7.654 MeV excitation energy
- The 2$^{+}_{2}$ "Hoyle" rotational excitation in $^{12}$C appears at around 10 MeV
- A state with structure of the Hoyle state coupled to an alpha particle is predicted in $^{16}$O at about 15.1 MeV (the 0$^{+}_{6}$ state), approximately 700 keV above the 4$\alpha$-particle breakup threshold
- The 3$^{+}$ state of $^{16}$O at 11.085 MeV $\pm$ 3 keV has a gamma-decay branching: 54.6 $\pm$ 2% to the 2$^{+}$ state at 6.92 MeV and 45.4% to the 3$^-$ state at 6.13 MeV
- The upper limit for the radiative partial width of the 10$^+$ $\rightarrow$ 8$^+$ transition in $^{24}$Mg from $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C resonances is 1.2 $\times$ 10$^{-5}$ (relative width)
- The $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C resonances have widths of approximately 100 keV
- Nuclear shapes with major-to-minor axis ratios of 2:1 correspond to quadrupole deformation parameter $\beta_2$ $\approx$ 0.6; 3:1 shapes (hyperdeformation) correspond to $\beta_2$ $\approx$ 1.0
- In $^{28}$Si, the 2$^+$ member of the SD band candidate is at 9.8 MeV, with 4$^+$ and 6$^+$ members identified
- The $^{12}$C($^{16}$O,$\gamma$)$^{28}$Si radiative capture reaction was studied at five resonant energies around the Coulomb barrier
- In $^{32}$S via the $^{28}$Si+$\alpha$ reaction, 30 new level assignments were found spanning 132 resonances in total

## Context

This review extends the pioneering work on molecular resonances in the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C reaction discovered over half a century ago, building on the Ikeda diagram (Ikeda, 1968) for N=Z nuclei and its extension to neutron-rich nuclei by von Oertzen (2001, 2003). The paper discusses connections between clustering and the Hoyle state (Hoyle, 1954; Cook, 1957), which is critical for stellar nucleosynthesis of $^{12}$C. It enables future searches for Bose-Einstein alpha condensates in $^{16}$O and heavier N=Z nuclei, and motivates further experimental investigations of linear chain configurations and molecular structures in neutron-rich oxygen isotopes using advanced particle-gamma coincidence techniques.
