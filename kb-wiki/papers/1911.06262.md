# Current-driven tricritical point in large-$N_{c}$ gauge theory

> We discover a new tricritical point realized only in non-equilibrium steady states, using the AdS/CFT correspondence. Our system is a (3+1)-dimensional strongly-coupled large-$N_{c}$ gauge theory. The tricritical point is associated with a chiral symmetry breaking under the presence of an electric current and a magnetic field. The critical exponents agree with those of the Landau theory of equilibrium phase transitions. This suggests that the presence of a Landau-like phenomenological theory behind our non-equilibrium phase transitions.

## Key claim

The authors discover a tricritical point that exists only in non-equilibrium steady states (NESSs) of a strongly-coupled large-\(N_c\) gauge theory, driven by an electric current and a magnetic field. The critical exponents at this tricritical point agree with those of Landau theory for equilibrium phase transitions, suggesting an effective Landau-like phenomenological description exists even for these non-equilibrium transitions.

## Method

The authors employ the AdS/CFT correspondence, specifically the D3-D7 probe brane model, to study chiral symmetry breaking in a (3+1)-dimensional \(SU(N_c)\) \(\mathcal{N}=4\) supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory with an \(\mathcal{N}=2\) hypermultiplet, under the presence of an electric current density \(J\) and a magnetic field \(B\) perpendicular to the current. They solve the Dirac-Born-Infeld (DBI) action for the D7-brane embedding numerically, imposing boundary conditions at an effective horizon, and compute the chiral condensate \(\langle \bar{q}q \rangle\) and thermodynamic potential for massless charge carriers (\(m=0\)) to map the phase diagram.

## Key numbers

* The tricritical point \(B_{\rm TCP}\) is located at a specific magnetic field value on the \(m=0\) slice of the phase diagram, separating first-order and second-order phase transition lines.
* The critical exponent \(\beta\) for the chiral condensate is \(\beta = 1/2\) for \(B > B_{\rm TCP}\) (on the second-order critical line) and \(\beta = 1/4\) at \(B = B_{\rm TCP}\) (the tricritical point), where \(\langle \bar{q}q \rangle \propto (J_c - J)^\beta\).
* The Hawking temperature of the dual black hole is \(T = \sqrt{2}/(\pi z_H)\), identified with the heat bath temperature.
* The dimensionless electric field and magnetic field are defined as \(e = 2E/(\pi \sqrt{\lambda} T^2)\) and \(b = 2B/(\pi \sqrt{\lambda} T^2)\), where \(\lambda\) is the 't Hooft coupling.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of non-equilibrium phase transitions in holographic probe brane models, particularly the discovery of negative differential conductivity (Nakamura 2010) and non-equilibrium phase transitions (Nakamura 2012), as well as the observation of similarity to Landau theory critical phenomena (Matsumoto 2018). It also builds on studies of chiral symmetry breaking in AdS/CFT with magnetic fields (Filev 2007, Albash 2008, Erdmenger 2007) by introducing the electric current as an additional control parameter in the non-equilibrium steady state regime where \(J \cdot E \neq 0\). The results suggest that such current-driven tricritical points may be experimentally observable in systems of gapless chiral fermions, such as Weyl semimetals, through measurements of the transition between negative differential conductivity (NDC) and positive differential conductivity (PDC) phases.
