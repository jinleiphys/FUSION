# Double, triple, and $n$-parton scatterings in high-energy proton and nuclear collisions

> The framework to compute the cross sections for the production of particles with high mass and/or large transverse momentum in double- (DPS), triple- (TPS), and in general $n$-parton scatterings, from the corresponding single-parton ($σ_{\rm SPS}$) values in high-energy proton-proton, proton-nucleus, and nucleus-nucleus is reviewed. The basic parameter of the factorized $n$-parton scattering ansatz is an effective cross section $σ_{\rm eff}$ encoding all unknowns about the underlying generalized $n$-parton distribution in the proton (nucleon). In its simplest and most economical form, the $σ_{\rm eff}$ parameter can be derived from the transverse parton profile of the colliding protons and/or nucleus, using a Glauber approach. Numerical examples for the cross sections and yields expected for the concurrent DPS or TPS production of heavy-quarks, quarkonia, and/or gauge bosons in proton and nuclear collisions at LHC and Future Circular Collider (FCC) energies are provided. The obtained cross sections are based on perturbative QCD predictions for $σ_{\rm SPS}$ at next-to-leading-order (NLO) or next-to-NLO (NNLO) accuracy including, when needed, nuclear modifications of the corresponding parton densities.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the factorized framework for computing double-, triple-, and n-parton scattering cross sections in proton-proton, proton-nucleus, and nucleus-nucleus collisions, where the effective cross section parameter sigma_eff can be derived from the transverse parton profile of colliding hadrons using a Glauber approach, but the actual numerical predictions for triple-parton scattering cross sections carry large uncertainties from scale dependence, PDF variations, and the sigma_eff value itself.

## Method

The authors express n-parton scattering cross sections as the n-th product of single-parton scattering (SPS) cross sections divided by the (n-1)-th power of an effective cross section sigma_eff, which encodes generalized n-parton distribution functions. They compute sigma_eff values from proton transverse density profiles using either the Pythia 8 parametrization (exponential-like with exponent m) or the Herwig dipole form factor, and derive a relationship sigma_eff_TPS = 0.82 +/- 0.11 times sigma_eff_DPS. For numerical examples, they calculate SPS cross sections at NNLO accuracy using the Top++ code with ABMP16 proton PDFs and heavy-quark pole masses m_c = 1.67 GeV and m_b = 4.66 GeV, then apply the factorized formula to obtain TPS cross sections for charm and bottom production.

## Key numbers

- sigma_eff_DPS = 15 +/- 5 mb extracted from multiple DPS measurements at Tevatron and LHC
- sigma_eff_TPS = 12.5 +/- 4.5 mb derived from the relationship sigma_eff_TPS = 0.82 +/- 0.11 x sigma_eff_DPS
- SPS charm cross section at sqrt(s) = 14 TeV: 7.1 +/- 3.5 (scale) +/- 0.3 (PDF) mb
- SPS charm cross section at sqrt(s) = 100 TeV: 25.0 +/- 16.0 (scale) +/- 1.3 (PDF) mb
- TPS triple-charm cross section at sqrt(s) = 14 TeV: 0.39 +/- 0.28 (total) mb
- TPS triple-charm cross section at sqrt(s) = 100 TeV: 16.7 +/- 11.8 (total) mb
- SPS bottom cross section at sqrt(s) = 14 TeV: 0.56 +/- 0.09 (scale) +/- 0.01 (PDF) mb
- SPS bottom cross section at sqrt(s) = 100 TeV: 2.8 +/- 0.6 (scale) +/- 0.1 (PDF) mb
- TPS triple-bottom cross section at sqrt(s) = 14 TeV: 0.19 +/- 0.12 (total) microbarns
- TPS triple-bottom cross section at sqrt(s) = 100 TeV: 24 +/- 17 (total) microbarns
- Triple-charm production amounts to 5% of inclusive charm yields at LHC (14 TeV) and more than half of total charm cross section at FCC (100 TeV)
- Total pp inelastic cross section at sqrt(s) = 100 TeV is approximately 105 mb

## Context

This review extends the authors' prior work on DPS and TPS in pp, pA, and AA collisions (d'Enterria and Snigirev, 2012-2016) and provides the first-ever estimates of triple-parton scattering cross sections for heavy-quark production. The framework builds on the factorized ansatz for multiparton interactions developed by Sjostrand (1987) and the Glauber approach for nuclear collisions. The results enable experimental searches for triple-parton scattering at LHC and FCC energies, particularly in triple charm-meson final states as discussed further by Maciula (2017).
