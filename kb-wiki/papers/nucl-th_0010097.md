# Molecular Collective Vibrations in the Ternary Neutronless Fission of $^{252}$Cf

> Based on a recent experimental finding which may suggest the existence of a tri-nuclear molecular structure before the cold ternary fragmentation of $^{252}$Cf takes place, we solved the eigenvalue problem of a certain class of vibrations which are very likely to occur in these molecules. These oscillations are the result of the joined action of rotations of the heavier fragments and the transversal vibrations of the lighter spherical cluster with respect to the fission axis. In the calculation of the interaction between the heavier fragments we took into account higher multipole deformations, including the hexadecupole one, and introduced a repulsive nuclear part to insure the creation of a potential pocket in which a few molecular states can be accommodated. The possibility to observe the de-excitation of such states is discussed in connection with the molecular life-time.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that a tri-nuclear molecular configuration may exist transiently before the cold ternary fragmentation of $^{252}$Cf, and that this configuration supports collective butterfly (bending) vibrations with a discrete spectrum; the authors hedge that the experimental evidence only "may suggest" such a structure, and the molecular states are expected to be short-lived relative to the overall fission process.

## Method

The authors constructed a classical Hamiltonian for a linear tri-nuclear system (two heavy deformed fragments with a light spherical cluster between them) using Jacobi coordinates and angular velocities, then quantized the kinetic energy after imposing constraints that freeze bond-stretching and allow only small bending angles (butterfly modes). The interaction potential between the two heavy fragments was computed via double folding with the M3Y nucleon-nucleon effective interaction plus a repulsive core, including quadrupole, octupole, and hexadecupole deformations; the potential was expanded to second order in the bending angle to extract a stiffness parameter. The vibrational spectrum was then obtained from a harmonic oscillator Hamiltonian, and the half-life of the corresponding di-nuclear configuration was estimated using a one-dimensional WKB formula.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous investigations on molecular configurations in binary cold fission (Misicu et al., 1997; Misicu, 1999) and ternary cold fission (Hess et al., 1999), motivated by experimental indications of a long-lived ($\geq 10^{-13}$ s) structure in $^{10}$Be-accompanied ternary cold fission (Hamilton et al., 1998) and a molecular structure with $^{12}$C (Hwang et al., 1999). The study enables predictions for gamma-ray de-excitation of molecular vibrational states that could be observed before tunneling ends, with the expectation that such gammas would not be strongly Doppler-shifted.
