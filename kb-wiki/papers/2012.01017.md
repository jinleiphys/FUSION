# Chiral phase transition inside a rotating cylinder within the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model

> We study the chiral phase transition inside a rotating cylinder within the framework of the Namb--Jona-Lasinio model. A spectral boundary condition is imposed to avoid faster than light. We investigate how the geometry of the cylinder and rotation influence the chiral phase transition at finite temperature and chemical potential. The inhomogeneous effects caused by the finite size and rotation are also taken into account. It is found that finite size will reduce the chiral transition temperature and raises the chiral transition chemical potential, while the rotation reduces both the chiral transition temperature and chemical potential. In addition, we discuss the implications of our results in heavy-ion collisions and equation of states of neutron star.

## Key claim

Within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with a spectral boundary condition that enforces causality (no faster-than-light motion), finite size reduces the chiral transition temperature and raises the chiral transition chemical potential, while rotation reduces both; however, at zero temperature and zero chemical potential, rotation has no effect on the chiral condensate because the rotating and nonrotating vacua are identical when the system is properly bounded.

## Method

The authors solve the Dirac equation in rotating cylindrical coordinates with a spectral boundary condition that discretizes the transverse momentum via Bessel function roots. They compute the chiral condensate in the mean-field approximation using the local density approximation, where the effective quark mass at each radial coordinate is determined self-consistently from the gap equation. Proper-time regularization is employed to handle divergences, and the effects of finite cylinder length are included by imposing antiperiodic boundary conditions along the z-axis.

## Key numbers

- For a cylinder radius R = 1.97 fm at r = 0 and zero rotation, the M-T curve overlaps with the infinite-space result, indicating this radius is large enough to approximate infinite volume for finite-temperature phase transitions.
- At T = 120 MeV and R = 1.97 fm, rotation suppresses the effective mass, with the effect becoming more pronounced at larger radial coordinates (e.g., r = 0.8 R).
- For a cylinder of radius R = 1.97 fm and length L = 3 fm at r = 0, T = 0, and zero rotation, the effective mass approaches a constant limit as R increases, with L = 3 fm being large enough that the M-R curve nearly overlaps the infinite-L curve.
- At zero temperature and zero chemical potential, the condensate is independent of angular velocity Omega as long as Omega R < 1, because E * tilde{E} > 0 ensures the rotating and nonrotating vacua are identical.
- At T = 50 MeV and zero rotation, finite size raises the chiral transition chemical potential, while at T = 0, the phase transition at finite chemical potential becomes discontinuous in a finite volume (e.g., R = 2.955 fm shows complex M-mu behavior).

## Context

This work extends studies of rotating strongly interacting matter using NJL-type models (Yin and Jiang, 2016; Chernodub and Gongyo, 2017) by imposing a spectral boundary condition that preserves chiral symmetry and avoids unphysical faster-than-light effects. It contrasts with earlier unbounded treatments (Yin and Jiang, 2016; Fukushima and Hidaka, 2018) where rotation could produce large effects at unrealistically high angular velocities. The results are relevant for heavy-ion collisions (where finite-size effects are negligible above 3 fm at high temperature) and neutron star equations of state (where rotation effects are small unless Omega R exceeds 0.3, though interplay with magnetic fields may alter this).
