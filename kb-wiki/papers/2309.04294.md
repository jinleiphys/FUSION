# Microscopic analysis of dipole electric and magnetic strengths in $^{156}$Gd

> The dipole electric ($E1$) and magnetic ($M1$) strengths in strongly deformed $^{156}$Gd are investigated within a fully self-consistent Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) with Skyrme forces SVbas, SLy6 and SG2. We inspect, on the same theoretical footing, low-lying dipole states and the isovector giant dipole resonance in $E1$ channel and the orbital scissors resonance as well as the spin-flip giant resonance (SFGR) in $M1$ channel. Besides, $E1$ toroidal mode and low-energy spin-flip $M1$ excitations are considered. The deformation splitting and dipole-octupole coupling of electric excitations are analyzed. The origin of SFGR gross structure, impact of the residual interaction and interference of orbital and spin contributions to SFGR are discussed. The effect of the central exchange $\textbf{J}^2$-term from the Skyrme functional is demonstrated. The calculations show a satisfactory agreement with available experimental data, except for the recent NRF measurements of M. Tamkas et al for $M1$ strength at 4-6 MeV, where, in contradiction with our calculations and previous $(p,p')$ data, almost no $M1$ strength was observed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that fully self-consistent QRPA calculations with Skyrme forces can simultaneously describe electric and magnetic dipole excitations in strongly deformed $^{156}$Gd, but the calculations disagree with recent NRF measurements by Tamkas et al. (2019) for $M1$ strength at 4-6 MeV, where almost no $M1$ strength was observed in contradiction to both the calculations and previous $(p,p')$ data.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) calculations using three Skyrme forces (SVbas, SLy6, and SG2) with a matrix-form implementation that includes both mean field and residual interaction derived from the same functional, pairing-induced particle-particle channels, and Coulomb contributions. The calculations used a large configuration space (e.g., 683 proton and 787 neutron levels for SVbas) with axial quadrupole equilibrium deformation obtained by energy minimization, and employed Lorentz averaging parameters of 2 MeV for GDR and 1 MeV for SFGR to simulate smoothing effects beyond QRPA.

## Key numbers

- Deformation parameter $\beta_2$ for SVbas: 0.327, SLy6: 0.333, SG2 (no $\textbf{J}^2$): 0.329, compared to experimental value 0.34
- Energy of $2^+_1$ state: 106 keV (SVbas), 63 keV (SLy6), 90 keV (SG2 no $\textbf{J}^2$), 103 keV (SG2 with $\textbf{J}^2$), experimental: 89 keV
- Energy difference between M1 peaks: approximately 2.1 MeV, well described by all Skyrme forces
- M1 peak energies overestimated by 0.7-0.8 MeV (SVbas), 1.8-2.0 MeV (SLy6), and 0.7-0.9 MeV (SG2 no $\textbf{J}^2$)
- Summed $B(E1)$ strength at 3.1-6.2 MeV: 0.054 $\rm{e^2 fm^2}$ (SVbas), 0.058 $\rm{e^2 fm^2}$ (SLy6), 0.050 $\rm{e^2 fm^2}$ (SG2), experimental: 0.073 $\rm{e^2 fm^2}$
- Summed $B(E1)$ strength at PDR region 5-9 MeV: 0.68 $\rm{e^2 fm^2}$ (SVbas), 0.43 $\rm{e^2 fm^2}$ (SLy6), 0.53 $\rm{e^2 fm^2}$ (SG2)
- Summed $B(M1)$ strength at OSR region 2.5-4 MeV: 3.57 $\mu^2_N$ (SVbas), 5.59 $\mu^2_N$ (SLy6), 3.74 $\mu^2_N$ (SG2), experimental for $^{156}$Gd: 3.08 $\mu^2_N$
- Summed $B(M1)$ strength at 2.95-6.25 MeV: 6.89 $\mu^2_N$ (SVbas), 7.22 $\mu^2_N$ (SLy6), 6.97 $\mu^2_N$ (SG2), experimental: 3.12 $\mu^2_N$
- Interference factor R at 2.5-4 MeV: 1.48 (SVbas), 2.08 (SLy6), 1.42 (SG2)

## Context

This work extends previous QRPA studies of $^{156}$Gd by Nojarov (1997), Sarriguren (1996), and Guliev (2020) that used non-self-consistent models with separable residual interaction, as well as the recent self-consistent FAM study by Sasaki (2023) with Skyrme force SLy4. The paper also builds on earlier Skyrme QRPA analyses of electric modes in deformed nuclei by Kleinig (2008) and Repko (2017) and magnetic modes by Vesely (2009) and Nesterenko (2010). The simultaneous exploration of $E1$ and $M1$ modes in a strongly deformed nucleus enables testing of Skyrme energy-density functionals, particularly the spin-isospin channel and the central exchange $\textbf{J}^2$-term, and can help outline ways for further improvement of these functionals.
