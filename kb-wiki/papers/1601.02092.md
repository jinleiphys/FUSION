# The hidden-charm pentaquark and tetraquark states

> In the past decade many charmonium-like states were observed experimentally. Especially those charged charmonium-like $Z_c$ states and bottomonium-like $Z_b$ states can not be accommodated within the naive quark model. These charged $Z_c$ states are good candidates of either the hidden-charm tetraquark states or molecules composed of a pair of charmed mesons. Recently, the LHCb Collaboration discovered two hidden-charm pentaquark states, which are also beyond the quark model. In this work, we review the current experimental progress and investigate various theoretical interpretations of these candidates of the multiquark states. We list the puzzles and theoretical challenges of these models when confronted with the experimental data. We also discuss possible future measurements which may distinguish the theoretical schemes on the underlying structures of the hidden-charm multiquark states.

## Key claim
This review argues that the numerous charmonium-like and bottomonium-like states observed experimentally, particularly the charged $Z_c$ and $Z_b$ states and the LHCb's hidden-charm pentaquarks, cannot be explained by the naive quark model and are strong candidates for exotic multiquark states (tetraquarks, pentaquarks, or hadronic molecules), but the theoretical models face significant puzzles and challenges when confronted with the experimental data.

## Method
The authors systematically review the experimental progress on hidden-charm multiquark states, including the $XYZ$ states and the $P_c$ pentaquarks. They then survey and compare the applications of various theoretical frameworks, such as the one-boson-exchange model, QCD sum rules, the diquark-antidiquark model, and lattice QCD, to interpret these states, listing the specific puzzles and challenges each model faces.

## Context
This work is a comprehensive review that extends the discussion of exotic hadrons beyond the light-quark sector (e.g., the $\Theta$ pentaquark from LEPS in 2003) into the hidden-charm and hidden-bottom sectors. It synthesizes the experimental findings from collaborations like Belle, BaBar, BESIII, and LHCb, and contrasts the predictions of the traditional quark model (e.g., the Godfrey-Isgur model) with the properties of states like the $X(3872)$ and $P_c(4380)/P_c(4450)$. The review enables future work by clearly outlining the open questions and suggesting specific measurements that could distinguish between competing theoretical schemes, such as the tetraquark and molecular interpretations.
