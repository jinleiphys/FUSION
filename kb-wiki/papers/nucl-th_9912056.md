# Low momentum nucleon-nucleon potential and shell model effective interactions

> A low momentum nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential V-low-k is derived from meson exhange potentials by integrating out the model dependent high momentum modes of V_NN. The smooth and approximately unique V-low-k is used as input for shell model calculations instead of the usual Brueckner G matrix. Such an approach eliminates the nuclear mass dependence of the input interaction one finds in the G matrix approach, allowing the same input interaction to be used in different nuclear regions. Shell model calculations of 18O, 134Te and 135I using the same input V-low-k have been performed. For cut-off momentum Lambda in the vicinity of 2 fm-1, our calculated low-lying spectra for these nuclei are in good agreement with experiments, and are weakly dependent on Lambda.

# Low momentum nucleon-nucleon potential and shell model effective interactions

## Key claim
A low-momentum nucleon-nucleon potential V_low-k, derived by integrating out high-momentum components of meson-exchange potentials, can serve as a nearly model-independent input for shell model calculations across different nuclear mass regions, eliminating the nuclear-mass dependence inherent in the traditional Brueckner G-matrix approach. The claim is hedged by the condition that the cutoff momentum Lambda must be in the vicinity of 2 fm^-1 for the calculated spectra to be weakly dependent on Lambda and in good agreement with experiment.

## Method
The authors derive V_low-k from the CD-Bonn NN potential by integrating out high-momentum modes using a renormalization-group-inspired decimation based on the Kuo-Lee-Ratcliff folded-diagram effective interaction theory, implemented via the Andreozzi-Lee-Suzuki iteration method for non-degenerate model spaces. They then use V_low-k directly as input for shell model calculations of 18O, 134Te, and 135I, without first calculating a Brueckner G-matrix, employing a folded-diagram formulation to compute energy differences of neighboring many-body systems from experimental single-particle energies.

## Key numbers
- Cutoff momentum Lambda = 2.0 fm^-1 and 2.2 fm^-1 were used; calculated low-lying spectra are insensitive to this choice.
- Deuteron binding energy calculated from V_low-k agrees with that from the full CD-Bonn V_NN to four decimal places for all values of Lambda tested.
- For 135I, the valence interaction energy for three valence nucleons is slightly overbound by about 0.3 MeV, suggesting a possible need for a weak three-body force.
- Lambda_min is estimated as approximately 1.4 fm^-1 (corresponding to the 2-pi exchange scale); Lambda_max should not be much greater than 2.0-2.5 fm^-1 to avoid model dependence from the underlying V_NN.

## Context
This work extends the renormalization-group and effective-field-theory approach to nuclear interactions, as developed by Lepage (1997), Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998), and Epelbaum, Gloeckle, and Meissner (1998), by applying it to shell model effective interactions. It contrasts with the traditional Brueckner G-matrix approach used by Covello et al. (1997) and Jiang, Machleidt, Stout, and Kuo (1992), which yields different input interactions for different nuclear mass regions due to Pauli blocking operators. The results enable the possibility of a common shell-model interaction suitable for a wide range of nuclei, with future work needed to investigate three-body forces for systems with more than two valence nucleons.
