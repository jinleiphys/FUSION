# Low lying excited states of $^{24}$O investigated by self-consistent microscopic description of proton inelastic scattering

> The proton inelastic scattering of $^{24}$O($p,p'$) at 62 MeV/nucleon is described by a self-consistent microscopic calculation with the continuum particle-vibration coupling (cPVC) method. The SLy5, SkM*, and SGII parameters are adopted as an effective nucleon-nucleon interaction. For all the parameters, the cPVC calculation reproduces very well the first peak at 4.65 MeV in the $^{24}$O excitation energy spectrum as well as its angular distribution. The role of the cPVC self-energy strongly depends on the effective interactions. The higher-lying strength around 7.3 MeV is suggested to be a superposition of the $3^-$ and $4^+$ states by the results with SLy5 and SGII, whereas the SkM* calculation indicates it is a pure $3^-$ state. This difference gives a rather strong interaction dependence of the angular distribution corresponding to the higher-lying strength.

## Key claim

The continuum particle-vibration coupling (cPVC) method reproduces the experimental energy spectrum and angular distribution of the first 2+ state of 24O, but the spin-parity assignment of the higher-lying strength around 7.3 MeV depends strongly on which Skyrme effective interaction is used: SLy5 and SGII suggest a superposition of 3- and 4+ states, while SkM* indicates a pure 3- state.

## Method

The authors applied the continuum particle-vibration coupling (cPVC) method to calculate the double differential cross section for 24O(p,p') at 62 MeV/nucleon, using continuum RPA response functions for the target excitations and Hartree-Fock scattering wave functions for the outgoing proton. They adopted three Skyrme parameter sets (SLy5, SkM*, and SGII) as the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, including RPA phonons with multipolarities 1-, 2+, 3-, 4+, and 5- up to 60 MeV excitation energy. The theoretical spectra were smeared with the experimental resolution of 0.5√(Ex - 4.09) MeV in FWHM, and the absolute cross sections were not renormalized.

## Key numbers

- First excited state of 24O at 4.65 ± 0.14 MeV, assigned 2+ with quadrupole transition parameter β2 = 0.15 ± 0.04
- Proton beam energy: 62 MeV/nucleon
- Energy shift applied to theoretical spectra: ΔEx = 1.3 MeV for SLy5 and SGII, ΔEx = 0.5 MeV for SkM*
- Maximum excitation energy of 24O in RPA calculation: 60 MeV
- Angular momentum cut-off lmax = 12 for single-particle orbits
- Fermi momentum kF = 1.33 fm-1 for residual force with Landau-Migdal approximation
- Radial mesh size: 0.2 fm; maximum radial integration: 20 fm

## Context

This work extends the continuum particle-vibration coupling (cPVC) method, previously applied to neutron elastic scattering on 16O (Mizuyama and Ogata, 2012) and single-particle structures in 40Ca, 208Pb, and 24O (CPVC paper), to proton inelastic scattering. The study directly addresses the experimental measurement of 24O(p,p') at 62 MeV/nucleon (24Oexp, 2013), which confirmed the N=16 shell gap and found a strong higher-lying strength around 7.3 MeV suggestive of quenching between the sd and fp neutron shells. The results enable future experimental angular distribution measurements to discriminate between Skyrme interactions and clarify the spin-parity of the higher-lying states.
