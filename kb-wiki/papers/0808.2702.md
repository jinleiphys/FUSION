# $J/ψ$ suppression and $p_T$ spectra in RHIC and LHC energy collisions

> In a hydrodynamic model, we have studied $J/ψ$ production in Au+Au/Cu+Cu collisions at RHIC energy $\sqrt{s}$=200 GeV. At the initial time, $J/ψ$'s are randomly distributed in the fluid. As the fluid evolve in time, the free streaming $J/ψ$'s are dissolved if the local fluid temperature exceeds a threshold temperature $T_{J/ψ}$. Sequential melting of charmonium states ($χ_c$, $ψ\prime$ and $J/ψ$), with melting temperatures $T_{χ_c}=T_{ψ\prime} \approx 1.2T_c$, $T_{J/ψ} \approx2T_c$ and feed-down fraction $F\approx 0.3$, explains the PHENIX data on the centrality dependence of $J/ψ$ suppression in Au+Au collisions. $J/ψ$ $p_T$ spectra and the nuclear modification factor in Au+Au collisions are also well explained in the model. The model however over predict centrality dependence of $J/ψ$ suppression in Cu+Cu collisions by 20-30%. The $J/ψ$ $p_T$ spectra are under predicted by 20-30%. The model predict that in central Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energy, $\sqrt{s}$=5500 GeV, $J/ψ$'s are suppressed by a factor of $\sim$ 10. The model predicted $J/ψ$ $p_T$ distribution in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC is similar to that in Au+Au collisions at RHIC.

## Key claim

In a hydrodynamic model with sequential melting of charmonium states, the PHENIX data on centrality dependence of J/psi suppression and pT spectra in Au+Au collisions at RHIC are well explained, but the model overpredicts suppression in Cu+Cu collisions by 20-30% and underpredicts the pT spectra by 20-30%, indicating that charmonium production in Cu+Cu collisions is at variance with Au+Au collisions.

## Method

The authors model J/psi production by assuming a deconfined QGP fluid that evolves in 2+1 dimensions with longitudinal boost-invariance, undergoing first-order phase transition at Tc=164 MeV and freezing out at TF=130 MeV. J/psi mesons are randomly distributed in the transverse plane according to the binary collision number profile at initial time tau_i=0.6 fm/c, with their pT distribution following a power law fit to p+p data. The survival probability is computed by integrating a temperature-dependent decay width (with controlling parameter alpha=0.2) along each J/psi trajectory, and sequential melting of chi_c, psi', and J/psi is implemented with melting temperatures T_chi=T_psi'=1.2Tc, T_J/psi=2Tc and feed-down fraction F=0.3, including cold nuclear matter effects via Glauber absorption with sigma_abs=2 mb.

## Key numbers

- Melting temperatures: T_J/psi = 2Tc, T_chi = T_psi' = 1.2Tc (Tc=164 MeV)
- Feed-down fraction from excited states: F = 0.3
- In central Pb+Pb collisions at LHC (sqrt(s)=5500 GeV), J/psi suppressed by factor ~10
- Central temperature in b=0 Pb+Pb collisions at LHC: Ti=421 MeV; at RHIC Au+Au: Ti=351 MeV
- In Au+Au collisions, changing decay width parameter alpha from 0.1 to 0.4 changes R_AA by less than a few percent in most central collisions (Npart=325)
- Mean square transverse momentum <pT^2> in 0-20% Au+Au: experimental 3.6+/-0.6+/-0.1 GeV^2, model 3.76 GeV^2
- J/psi multiplicity increase from RHIC to LHC: factor ~1.6
- In central Cu+Cu collisions, suppression overpredicted by 20-30%; pT spectra underpredicted by 20-30%

## Context

This work extends the author's previous "Hydro+J/psi" model (Chaudhuri, 2008) by improving the spatial distribution of J/psi and studying pT spectra, building on the approach of Gunji et al. (2007). The sequential melting framework with lattice-motivated dissociation temperatures (Satz, 2006) successfully explains Au+Au data but fails for Cu+Cu, contrasting with the comover model of Capella et al. (2007) which claimed consistency with both systems. The predictions for LHC provide an upper bound on J/psi suppression, as recombination effects (important in the statistical hadronization model of Andronic et al., 2007) are neglected, and the results suggest that LHC experiments will need longer running times due to reduced J/psi yields.
