# Pairing correlation involving the continuum states

> The Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equation for the ground states of even-even atomic nuclei is solved using the canonical representation in the coordinate space for zero range interactions like the Skyrme force. The gradient method is improved for faster convergence to the solutions under constraint of orthogonality between canonical orbitals. Necessity of the cut-off of the pairing interaction is shown even when the number of the canonical orbitals are restricted. A repulsive dependence of the interaction on the pairing density is introduced as an implementation of the cut-off which leaves the HFB super matrix state-independent.

## Key claim

The canonical-basis Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) method can be efficiently applied to deformed nuclei in a three-dimensional cubic mesh, but a cut-off of the pairing interaction remains necessary even when the number of canonical orbitals is restricted, because without it high-lying orbitals can shrink unphysically to a mesh point, producing spurious energy gains.

## Method

The author solves the HFB equation for ground states of even-even nuclei using the canonical (natural-orbital) representation in coordinate space with a zero-range Skyrme-like force for the mean field and a density-dependent delta pairing interaction. A gradient method is employed, accelerated by scaling the single-particle Hamiltonian with a factor \(\alpha_i = v_i^{-2\nu}\) (typically \(\nu = 1/2\) to \(<1\)), and the Lagrange multipliers \(\lambda_{ij}\) are modified to \(\lambda_{ij} = (\alpha_i + \alpha_j)^{-1} \langle \psi_j | \alpha_i \mathcal{H}_i + \alpha_j \mathcal{H}_j | \psi_i \rangle\) to preserve orthogonality along the steepest-descent path. Periodic orthogonalization and diagonalization of the HFB super matrix in the canonical basis are also used.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{40}\)Ca: wavefunctions expressed on a \(39 \times 39 \times 39\) mesh with spacing \(a = 0.8\) fm; \(K = 20\) canonical orbitals (containing \(Kg = 80\) nucleons, i.e., \(2 \times A\)).
- Imaginary time step size \(\Delta \tau = 1/T_{\text{max}}\) with \(T_{\text{max}} = 3 (\hbar^2 / 2m) (\pi / a)^2\).
- For \(^{32}\)S: wavefunctions on a \(19 \times 19 \times 19\) mesh with spacing \(a = 1.0\) fm; \(K = 16\) canonical orbitals (containing \(Kg = 64\) nucleons).
- Mean-field force parameters: \(t_0 = -983.4\) MeV fm\(^3\), \(t_3 = 13106\) MeV fm\(^{3+3\alpha}\), \(\alpha = 0.98\).
- Pairing force parameters: \(v_p = -440\) MeV fm\(^3\), \(\rho_c = 0.32\) fm\(^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends the canonical-basis HFB method originally applied to spherical states by Reinhard et al. (1997) and the two-basis method for deformed neutron-rich nuclei developed by Girod et al. (1994), Terasaki et al. (1996), and Tajima et al. (1997). It enables efficient HFB calculations for deformed nuclei without requiring a large normalization box, and it demonstrates that a cut-off of the pairing interaction is mandatory even in the canonical-basis approach to prevent unphysical orbital collapse, motivating the introduction of a repulsive density-dependent term in the pairing interaction.
