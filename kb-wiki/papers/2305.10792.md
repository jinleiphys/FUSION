# Hyperon global polarization in heavy-ion collisions at NICA energies. Feed-down effects and the role of $Œ£^0$ hyperons

> Global polarization of hyperons induced by the local vorticity of the medium created in heavy-ion collisions at energies 2.3\,GeV$\le\sqrt{s_{NN}}\le$11.5 GeV is calculated in the parton-hadron-string dynamic (PHSD) model. The separation of spectator nucleons and the fluidization of the generated particle distributions are performed. The polarization of all anti-hyperon species is found significantly larger than that of hyperons. The $\overlineŒû$ hyperons are found to be polarized as strong as $\overlineŒõ$s but $Œû$ hyperons have weaker polarization compared to $Œõ$s. The $Œ©$ and $\overlineŒ©$ polarizations show the strongest dependence on the collision energy. Despite the strong polarization of the produced $Œõ$s and $\overlineŒõ$s induced by the vortical flows in the medium, the observed polarization signal is significantly depleted because of the feed down from weak and electromagnetic decays of heavier hyperons. Particularly strong suppression is found to be due to electromagnetic decays of $Œ£^0$ hyperons, which multiplicities obtained in the transport are poorly constrained both from the microscopic input of the $Œ£^0$ production reactions and from the experimental data. The final $Œõ(\overlineŒõ)$ polarization signal strongly depends on the $Œ£^0$ multiplicity generated in the model. With all these effects we can reproduce the measured global $Œõ$ polarization in collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7$ and $11.5$ GeV and the global $\overlineŒõ$ polarization at 11.5 GeV. For energies $< 3$ GeV, the calculated $Œõ$ polarization is smaller than the observed one. The polarization of $Œû(\overlineŒû)$ hyperons is calculated. The signal of $Œû(\overlineŒû)$ polarization is argued to be insensitive to feed-down effects and be a more direct probe of the degree of the vorticity in the system.

## Context

This work extends previous PHSD calculations by Voronyuk, Kolomeitsev, and Tsegelnik (2023) that reproduced Œõ and anti-Œõ polarization at 11.5 GeV but underpredicted anti-Œõ polarization at 7.7 GeV. It systematically analyzes the collision energy dependence of hyperon polarization and quantifies feed-down effects from heavier hyperons, particularly Œ£‚Å∞, which were not fully accounted for in earlier transport calculations using AMPT (Wei 2019), UrQMD (Vitiuk 2020), or QGSM (Baznat 2018). The authors argue that Œû and anti-Œû polarization signals are insensitive to feed-down effects and therefore provide a more direct probe of the vorticity in the system, enabling future experimental measurements at NICA to distinguish between different polarization mechanisms.
