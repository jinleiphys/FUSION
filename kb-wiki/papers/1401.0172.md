# Dissociation of Quarkonium in a Complex Potential

> We have studied the quasi-free dissociation of quarkonia through a complex potential which is obtained by correcting both the perturbative and nonperturbative terms of the $Q \bar Q$ potential at T=0 through the dielectric function in real-time formalism. The presence of confining nonperturbative term even above the transition temperature makes the real-part of the potential more stronger and thus makes the quarkonia more bound and also enhances the (magnitude) imaginary-part which, in turn contributes more to the thermal width, compared to the medium-contribution of the perturbative term alone. These cumulative observations result the quarkonia to dissociate at higher temperatures. Finally we extend our calculation to a medium, exhibiting local momentum anisotropy, by calculating the leading anisotropic corrections to the propagators in Keldysh representation. The presence of anisotropy makes the real-part of the potential stronger but the imaginary-part is weakened slightly. However, since the medium corrections to the imaginary-part is a small perturbation to the vacuum part, overall the anisotropy makes the dissociation temperatures higher, compared to isotropic medium.

## Key claim

The authors claim that including the nonperturbative (string) term in the heavy quark potential above the deconfinement temperature makes quarkonium states more bound and increases their dissociation temperatures compared to using only the perturbative (Coulomb) term, but this conclusion depends on the assumption that the string tension does not vanish abruptly at the transition temperature and on using a multiplication factor of 1.4 to the leading-order Debye mass to account for next-to-leading corrections.

## Method

The authors computed the real and imaginary parts of the static heavy quark potential by correcting both the Coulomb and string terms of the Cornell potential at T=0 through a dielectric function derived from hard thermal loop (HTL) resummed gluon propagators in the real-time Keldysh formalism. They then extended the calculation to a weakly anisotropic medium by expanding the propagators and self-energies around the isotropic limit to first order in the anisotropy parameter ξ, and determined dissociation temperatures using the criterion that the thermal width (from the imaginary part of the potential) equals twice the real part of the binding energy.

## Key numbers

- Debye mass squared: \( m_D^2 = \frac{g^2 T^2}{6}(N_f + 2N_c) \), where \( N_f \) is the number of quark flavors and \( N_c = 3 \) is the number of colors
- The real part of the potential in isotropic medium: \( Re V_{(iso)}(\hat{r},T) = \left(\frac{2\sigma}{m_D} - \alpha m_D\right)\frac{e^{-\hat{r}}}{\hat{r}} - \frac{2\sigma}{m_D \hat{r}} + \frac{2\sigma}{m_D} - \alpha m_D \), where \( \hat{r} = r m_D \)
- The imaginary part of the temporal component of the symmetric propagator in isotropic medium: \( Im D^{00}_F(0,p) = \frac{-2\pi T m_D^2}{p(p^2 + m_D^2)^2} \)
- The anisotropic correction to the real part of the static propagator: \( \xi \frac{m_D^2}{6(p^2 + m_D^2)^2}(3\cos 2\theta_p - 1) \)
- The anisotropic correction to the imaginary part of the symmetric propagator: \( \xi\left(\frac{3\pi T m_D^2}{2p(p^2 + m_D^2)^2}\sin^2\theta_p - \frac{4\pi T m_D^4}{p(p^2 + m_D^2)^3}(\sin^2\theta_p - \frac{1}{3})\right) \)
- The potential was evaluated at temperatures \( 1.2T_c \), \( 2.0T_c \), and \( 2.5T_c \)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation of the real part of the heavy quark potential in an isotropic medium (Thakur et al., 2013) by adding the imaginary part and extending to anisotropic media. It builds on the complex potential approach of Laine (2006) and Brambilla (2008) where the imaginary part arises from Landau damping, and on studies of anisotropy effects by Dumitru (2007, 2009) and Burnier (2009). The inclusion of the nonperturbative string term above \( T_c \) follows the observation from lattice QCD that the string tension does not vanish abruptly at deconfinement (string1, string2, string3). The work enables more realistic calculations of quarkonium suppression in heavy-ion collisions by incorporating both real and imaginary parts of the potential with nonperturbative contributions and momentum-space anisotropy.
