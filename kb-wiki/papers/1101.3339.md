# Generating heavy particles with energy and momentum conservation

> We propose a novel algorithm, called REGGAE, for the generation of momenta of a given sample of particle masses, evenly distributed in Lorentz invariant phase space and obeying energy and momentum conservation. In comparison to other existing algorithms, REGGAE is designed for the use in multiparticle production in hadronic and nuclear collisions where many hadrons are produced and a large part of the available energy is stored in the form of their masses. The algorithm uses a loop simulating multiple collisions which lead to production of configurations with reasonably large weights.

## Key claim

The REGGAE algorithm generates particle momenta that are evenly distributed in Lorentz invariant phase space while conserving energy and momentum, specifically for multiparticle production in hadronic and nuclear collisions where a large fraction of the available energy is stored in particle masses. The algorithm achieves this through a rescattering procedure that drives the system toward equilibrium configurations, with the claim that a small number of collisions per particle (typically 6-12) is sufficient for practical applications.

## Method

The algorithm first generates initial momentum configurations using the GENBOD method, which treats multiparticle production as a sequence of two-body decays of auxiliary resonances with randomly chosen masses. Then, a rescattering loop simulates multiple s-wave collisions between randomly paired particles: each pair is boosted into its center-of-mass frame, the outgoing momenta are distributed isotropically, and the system is boosted back. The procedure repeats for a specified number of collisions per particle (typically 6-12), and the authors demonstrate that this drives the system toward uniform filling of the available Lorentz invariant phase space.

## Key numbers

- For 100 pions (mass 139 MeV) with total energy 50 GeV, the LIPS-temperature is k_B T = 0.20688 GeV and the equilibrium information entropy is S = 0.6724; entropy saturates at this value after 12 collisions per particle.
- For 5 particles with mass 1 GeV and total four-momentum (100,0,0,0) GeV, REGGAE with 6 collisions per particle yields a mean value of 209.8 for the test function f_5 using 10^7 configurations, consistent with RAMBO (208.97) and NUPHAZ (207.71).
- For 30 particles with mass 1 GeV and total four-momentum (100,0,0,0) GeV, REGGAE with 12 collisions per particle takes 28 minutes for 10^6 configurations, while weighted GENBOD takes 300 minutes.
- For 60 particles with mass 1 GeV (60% of energy in masses), REGGAE with 12 collisions per particle takes 6 minutes for 10^5 configurations, while NUPHAZ takes 63 minutes.
- For 500 particles with mass 0.1 GeV and total four-momentum (500,0,0,0) GeV/c, REGGAE with 10 collisions per particle takes 8 minutes for 10^4 configurations, while NUPHAZ takes 23 minutes.

## Context

This work extends the GENBOD algorithm (Kopylov 1960, James 1968) and provides an alternative to RAMBO (Kleiss 1986) and NUPHAZ, which become inefficient when particle masses constitute a large fraction of the total energy. The algorithm is designed for simulations of multiparticle production in nuclear collisions and high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions at the LHC, where existing methods like the thermal rescaling approach (Toneev) or last-pair calculation (Ferroni) are not guaranteed to work reliably. The rescattering approach enables efficient Monte Carlo integration over phase space for systems with many heavy particles, and the authors show that REGGAE can be faster than NUPHAZ even when only 10% of the energy is in masses, provided the particle multiplicity is high.
