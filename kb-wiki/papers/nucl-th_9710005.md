# Nuclear dependence of the coherent eta photoproduction reaction in a relativistic approach

> We study the nuclear (or A) dependence of the coherent eta photoproduction reaction in a relativistic impulse approximation approach. We use a standard relativistic parameterization of the elementary amplitude, based on a set of four Lorentz- and gauge-invariant amplitudes, to calculate the coherent production cross section from He-4, C-12, and Ca-40. In contrast to nonrelativistic treatments, our approach maintains the full relativistic structure of the process. The nuclear structure affects the process through the ground-state tensor density. This density is sensitive to relativistic effects and depends on A in a different manner than the vector density used in nonrelativistic approaches. This peculiar dependence results in He-4 having a cross section significantly smaller than that of C-12---in contrast to existent nonrelativistic calculations. Distortion effects are incorporated through an eta-nucleus optical potential that is computed in a simple ``t rho'' approximation.

# Nuclear dependence of the coherent eta photoproduction reaction in a relativistic approach

## Key claim

The coherent eta photoproduction cross section from He-4 is significantly smaller than that from C-12, in contrast to nonrelativistic calculations (Bennhold and Tanabe 1990; Fix and Arenhoevel 1997) which predicted He-4 to have the largest cross section. This reversal arises because the reaction is driven by the ground-state tensor density rather than the vector density used in nonrelativistic treatments, and the tensor density depends on A in a fundamentally different manner.

## Method

The authors computed the coherent eta photoproduction differential and total cross sections from He-4, C-12, and Ca-40 using a relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation (RPWIA) and a relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (RDWIA). The elementary gamma N to eta N amplitude was parameterized using four Lorentz- and gauge-invariant amplitudes, preserving the full relativistic structure without nonrelativistic reduction. Nuclear structure was described via a self-consistent mean-field approximation to the Walecka (sigma-omega) model, and distortion effects were incorporated through an eta-nucleus optical potential computed in a t-rho approximation.

## Key numbers

* At photon laboratory energies of 625, 700, and 800 MeV, the RPWIA differential cross section for He-4 is smaller than that for C-12 and Ca-40, reversing the ordering predicted by nonrelativistic calculations.
* The sigma meson mass was modified to 564 MeV for He-4 to reproduce the experimental charge density, while standard Walecka model parameters were used for C-12 and Ca-40 (g_s^2 = 109.63, g_v^2 = 190.43, m_s = 520 MeV, m_v = 783 MeV).
* Distortion effects at low energy (E_gamma = 625 MeV) produce a cross section that differs little from the plane-wave value due to competition between attractive real and absorptive imaginary parts of the optical potential; at higher energies the real part becomes repulsive, leading to substantial reduction.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier relativistic analysis (Piekarewicz, Sarty, and Benmerrouche 1997) and directly contradicts nonrelativistic predictions by Bennhold and Tanabe (1990) and Fix and Arenhoevel (1997), who found He-4 to have the largest coherent cross section. The results suggest that the tensor density, rather than the vector density, is the fundamental nuclear-structure quantity driving the reaction, and that the elementary amplitude used (Benmerrouche, Zhang, and Mukhopadhyay 1995) includes significant D13(1520) resonance and non-resonant background contributions that are suppressed in other models. The work enables experimental tests at facilities such as TJNAF and MAMI to distinguish between relativistic and nonrelativistic approaches and to better constrain the poorly determined tensor density.
