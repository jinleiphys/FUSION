# Quark-hadron Phase Transition in Proto-Neutron Stars Cores based on a Non-local NJL Model

> We study the QCD phase diagram using a non-local SU(3) NJL model with vector interactions among quarks. We analyze several thermodynamic quantities such as entropy and specific heat, and study the influence of vector interactions on the thermodynamic properties of quark matter. Upon imposing electric charge neutrality and baryon number conservation on the field equations, we compute models for the equation of state of the inner cores of proto-neutron stars providing a non-local treatment of quark matter for astrophysics.

## Key claim

The paper constructs models for the equation of state of hot quark matter in proto-neutron star cores using a non-local SU(3) Polyakov Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (n3PNJL) model with vector interactions, finding that vector interactions stiffen the equation of state while neutrino trapping softens it, but the results are limited to the specific parameter choices and mean-field approximation used.

## Method

The authors use a non-local SU(3) NJL model extended with the Polyakov loop and vector interactions among quarks, applying the Matsubara imaginary time formalism to extend the model to finite temperatures. After bosonization of the effective action, they compute the grand canonical potential in the mean-field approximation, from which the phase diagram and thermodynamic quantities such as specific heat and entropy are derived. Electric charge neutrality and baryon number conservation are imposed on the field equations to compute equations of state for proto-neutron star inner cores.

## Key numbers

- Strange quark mass set to \(m_s = 95\) MeV, with light quark masses \(m_u = m_d = 3.63\) MeV
- Non-locality scale parameter \(\Lambda = 1071.38\) MeV
- Scalar coupling constant \(G_s \Lambda^2 = 10.78\), vector coupling constant \(G_v = 0.5 G_s\), and 't Hooft coupling \(H \Lambda^5 = -353.29\)
- Lepton-to-baryon ratio for trapped neutrinos in proto-neutron star matter is \(Y_{L_e} \simeq 0.4\), with muon lepton number \(Y_{L_\mu} = 0\)
- Temperatures in the inner 50% of the stellar core at peak heating are in the range of 30 to 50 MeV, cooling to \(\sim 1\) MeV within minutes

## Context

This work extends previous studies of non-local NJL models at zero temperature for hybrid stars (Spinella, Carvalho, Ranea) to finite temperatures, enabling exploration of quark-hadron phase transitions in proto-neutron stars and core-collapse supernovae. The results enable future investigations of quark matter nucleation in proto-neutron star cores due to high compression (Lugones, 2016) and the effect of neutrino trapping in hot beta-stable quark-hybrid matter.
