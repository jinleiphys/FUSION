# {\Large $γ$} induced multiparticle emissions of medium mass nuclei at intermediate energies

> A comprehensive analysis of multiparticle emissions following photon induced reactions at intermediate energies is provided. Photon induced reaction is described in the energy range of $\sim 30-140$ MeV with an approach based on the quasideuteron nuclear photoabsorption model followed by the process of competition between light particle evaporation and fission for the excited nucleus. The evaporation-fission process of the compound nucleus is simulated in a Monte-Carlo framework. The study shows almost no fission events for the medium mass nuclei and reproduces satisfactorily well the available experimental data of photonuclear reaction cross sections at energies $\sim 30-140$ MeV.

## Key claim

The quasideuteron photoabsorption model followed by a Monte-Carlo evaporation-fission simulation reproduces available experimental photonuclear cross sections for medium mass nuclei at 30–140 MeV, but the model begins to overestimate average neutron multiplicities above 50–60 MeV, suggesting either a need for more precise measurements or a breakdown of the simple evaporation picture.

## Method

The authors model photonuclear reactions as a two-step process: first, the incident gamma ray is absorbed by a correlated neutron-proton pair inside the nucleus (quasideuteron model), forming a compound nucleus with the same composition as the target. Second, the de-excitation of the compound nucleus via competition between light particle evaporation (neutrons, protons, deuterons, tritons, helium-3, alpha particles) and fission is simulated using a Monte-Carlo routine based on Weisskopf statistical theory and the Bohr-Wheeler liquid-drop fission model, with branching ratios calculated from nuclear level densities and Vandenbosch-Huizenga's equation for fission-to-neutron width ratios.

## Key numbers

- Total photoabsorption cross sections for 10 nuclei (V, Zn, Sn, Ce, Sm, Yb, Ta, Au, Pb, Bi) are calculated at photon energies 30, 40, 60, 80, 100, 120, and 140 MeV; for example, for 118Sn at 30 MeV: 19.32 mb; at 140 MeV: 5.16 mb.
- Photofission cross sections are essentially zero for medium mass nuclei below 40 MeV; for 209Bi at 140 MeV the calculated photofission cross section is 1.18 mb.
- The largest photonuclear reaction cross section for 64Zn at 30 MeV is 9.67 mb for the (gamma,1n1p) channel; at 140 MeV it is 0.43 mb for (gamma,5n4p).
- For 208Pb at 30 MeV, the largest channel is (gamma,3n) at 15.47 mb; at 140 MeV the largest is (gamma,12n) at 3.53 mb.
- Each Monte-Carlo calculation uses 40000 events per target-energy combination.

## Context

This work extends the quasideuteron model approach previously applied by the same group to photofission of actinides (Mukhopadhyay 2007) into the medium mass region, where fission is negligible and (gamma,xn) and (gamma,xnyp) channels dominate. The results are compared to experimental photoabsorption cross sections from Leprêtre (1981) and average neutron multiplicities from Leprêtre (1982); the model agrees well for photoabsorption but overestimates neutron multiplicities above 50–60 MeV, in contrast to earlier work by Blann (1983) where precompound decay was needed to increase neutron yields. The authors suggest that more precise experimental measurements of neutron multiplicities are needed before definitive conclusions can be drawn.
