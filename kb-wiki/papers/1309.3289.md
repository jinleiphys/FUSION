# Nuclear landscape in covariant density functional theory

> The neutron and proton drip lines represent the limits of the nuclear landscape. While the proton drip line is measured experimentally up to rather high $Z$-values, the location of the neutron drip line for absolute majority of elements is based on theoretical predictions which involve extreme extrapolations. The first ever systematic investigation of the location of the proton and neutron drip lines in the covariant density functional theory has been performed by employing a set of the state-of-the-art parametrizations. Calculated theoretical uncertainties in the position of two-neutron drip line are compared with those obtained in non-relativistic DFT calculations. Shell effects drastically affect the shape of two-neutron drip line. In particular, model uncertainties in the definition of two-neutron drip line at $Z\sim 54, N=126$ and $Z\sim 82, N=184$ are very small due to the impact of spherical shell closures at N=126 and 184.

## Key claim

This paper presents the first systematic investigation of the location of proton and neutron drip lines within covariant density functional theory (CDFT), using a set of state-of-the-art parametrizations. The authors find that shell effects drastically affect the shape of the two-neutron drip line, and that model uncertainties in its definition are very small at spherical shell closures (N=126 and 184) but increase significantly in transitional and deformed regions.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations for all even-even nuclei from the proton to neutron drip line, using four CDFT parametrizations: NL3*, DD-ME2, DD-MEδ, and DD-PC1. They employed a separable version of the finite-range Brink-Booker part of the Gogny D1S force in the particle-particle channel, with an A-dependent strength variation. The RHB equations were solved in an axially deformed oscillator basis with up to 20 fermionic and 20 bosonic shells, and the ground state was determined by calculating potential energy curves over a deformation range from β2=-0.4 to β2=1.0.

## Key numbers

* The rms deviation between calculated and experimental binding energies for the "measured+estimated" mass set ranges from 2.17 MeV (DD-PC1) to 3.01 MeV (NL3*).
* Two-neutron and two-proton separation energies are described with typical accuracy of approximately 1 MeV across all parametrizations (Δ(S2n)rms from 1.09 to 1.25 MeV; Δ(S2p)rms from 0.99 to 1.28 MeV).
* The number of particle-bound even-even Z≤120 nuclei predicted is 2040 (DD-PC1), 2050 (DD-ME2), 2057 (DD-MEδ), and 2216 (NL3*).
* The two-neutron drip line is uniquely defined at Z∼54, N=126 and Z∼82, N=184 due to spherical shell closures.
* Regions of two-neutron binding reappearance beyond the primary drip line exist, for example at (Z=90, N=194-206) for DD-ME2 and (Z=62, N=132-146) for DD-PC1, but are absent in RHB(NL3*) calculations.

## Context

This work extends the first systematic uncertainty estimate for the neutron drip line within Skyrme density functional theory (SDFT) by Erler et al. (2012) to the covariant DFT framework, and compares CDFT results with those from non-relativistic models including the finite-range droplet model (FRDM) by Moller et al. (1995), Skyrme-HFB calculations by Goriely et al. (2010), and Gogny D1S and D1M calculations. The results enable a global estimate of theoretical uncertainties in drip line predictions and support the estimate that approximately 7000 different nuclides exist between the drip lines. The largest model differences appear in superheavy nuclei, where CDFT predicts a significant impact of the N=258 spherical shell closure that is not seen in Skyrme DFT.
