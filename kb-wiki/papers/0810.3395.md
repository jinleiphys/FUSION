# Spinodal instabilities and the distillation effect in nuclear matter under strong magnetic fields

> We study the effect of strong magnetic fields, of the order of $10^{18}$-$10^{19}$ G, on the instability region of nuclear matter at subsaturation densities. Relativistic nuclear models both with constant couplings and with density dependent parameters are considered. It is shown that a strong magnetic field can have large effects on the instability regions giving rise to bands of instability and wider unstable regions. As a consequence we predict larger transition densities at the inner edge of the crust of compact stars with strong magnetic field. The direction of instability gives rise to a very strong distillation effect if the last Landau level is only partially filled. However, for almost completed Landau levels an anti-distillation effect may occur.

## Key claim

Strong magnetic fields of order \(10^{18}\)–\(10^{19}\) G can significantly enlarge the spinodal instability region of nuclear matter at subsaturation densities, producing multiple instability bands; however, the distillation effect (enrichment of the dense phase in protons) occurs only when the last Landau level is partially filled, while nearly filled Landau levels can instead produce an anti-distillation effect.

## Method

The authors used two relativistic mean-field models, the nonlinear Walecka model (NLWM) with constant couplings (TM1 parametrization) and a density-dependent relativistic hadronic (DDRH) model (TW parametrization), to compute the free-energy curvature matrix of nuclear matter at zero temperature in a uniform magnetic field along the \(z\)-axis. They included the anomalous magnetic moments (AMM) of nucleons and, for stellar matter, added electrons under charge neutrality and \(\beta\)-equilibrium conditions. The spinodal boundary was defined by the zero of the smallest eigenvalue of the stability matrix, and the direction of instability was obtained from the corresponding eigenvector.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field strengths considered: \(B = B^* \, B^c_e\) with \(B^c_e = 4.414 \times 10^{13}\) G, for \(B^* = 10^5\), \(2\times10^5\), \(3\times10^5\), and \(5\times10^5\).
- For \(B=0\), the transition density (crossing of \(\beta\)-equilibrium EOS with thermodynamical spinodal) is 0.0695 fm\(^{-3}\) for TM1 and 0.0850 fm\(^{-3}\) for TW (without AMM).
- At \(B^*=10^5\) (without AMM), the transition density increases to 0.0970 fm\(^{-3}\) (TM1) and 0.1010 fm\(^{-3}\) (TW), with corresponding pressures 0.959 MeV fm\(^{-3}\) and 0.673 MeV fm\(^{-3}\).
- At \(B^*=3\times10^5\) (without AMM), the transition density reaches 0.1409 fm\(^{-3}\) (TM1) and 0.1516 fm\(^{-3}\) (TW).
- For \(npe\) matter with AMM at \(B^*=5\times10^5\), the transition density is 0.1282 fm\(^{-3}\) (TM1) and 0.1488 fm\(^{-3}\) (TW).
- The spinodal extends to a finite neutron density at zero proton fraction: \(\sim 0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\) for TW and \(\sim 0.186\) fm\(^{-3}\) for TM1, independent of \(B\) when AMM is omitted.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the liquid-gas phase transition in asymmetric nuclear matter (Chomaz 2004) and of magnetic field effects on the equation of state of compact stars (Broderick 2000, Chakrabarty 1996) to the subsaturation density regime relevant for the inner crust of magnetars. It builds on the finding by Aziz (2008) that fields of order \(10^{18}\) G strongly affect the EOS at crust densities, and on the spinodal analysis framework of Margueron (2003) and Providencia (2006). The results enable improved predictions for the crust thickness, pasta-phase extension, and mechanical properties of magnetars, which are relevant for understanding neutron-star glitches and quakes (Horowitz 2004).
