# Light $Ξ$ hypernuclei

> Arguments in favor of a light $ΞNN$ hypernucleus with $(I)J^P=(3/2)1/2^+$ are presented, within the uncertainties of our knowledge of the baryon-baryon strangeness $-2$ interactions. If bound, this $ΞNN$ state, being decoupled from the lowest $NΛΛ$ system, would be stable. It will also benefit from additional binding due to the electromagnetic interaction what makes it worthwhile to look for. We show how the equivalent state with $J=3/2$ could never be bound in spite of the attractive interaction of the two-body subsystems. We illustrate our discussion with a full-fledged Faddeev calculation of the $ΞNN$ system using simple potentials that mimic more elaborate interactions. We also make contact with different recent phenomenological interactions from the literature, like the ESC08 Nijmegen potential or quark-model based potentials.

# Light Ξ hypernuclei

## Key claim

Within the uncertainties of current baryon-baryon strangeness -2 interactions, a light ΞNN hypernucleus with quantum numbers (I)J^P = (3/2)1/2^+ may exist as a bound state, provided the isospin 1 NΞ interaction is sufficiently attractive. This state would be stable because it is decoupled from the lowest NΛΛ system, and it would gain additional binding from the electromagnetic interaction.

## Method

The authors performed Faddeev calculations for the ΞNN three-baryon system using two approaches: first, a simplified calculation with the Reid soft-core potential for the NN subsystem and a phenomenological Yukawa-type potential for the NΞ subsystem; second, a full-fledged Faddeev calculation using realistic NΞ interactions from the ESC08c Nijmegen potential and from a constituent quark cluster model (CQCM). The spin-isospin recoupling coefficients and the phase from identical-particle reduction were incorporated into the Faddeev equations to determine which quantum numbers permit binding.

## Key numbers

- With the ESC08c Nijmegen model giving a bound (i,j)=(1,1) NΞ subsystem of 8.3 MeV, the three-body (I)J^P=(3/2)1/2^+ state has a binding energy of 6.35 MeV.
- With the updated ESC08c Nijmegen model giving a bound (i,j)=(1,1) NΞ subsystem of 1.56 MeV, the three-body state has a binding energy of 2.50 MeV.
- Using the CQCM analysis with only the (i,j)=(1,1) NΞ channel, the three-body binding energy is 84 keV; including both NΞ channels gives 429 keV.
- Using the simple phenomenological potential with only the (i,j)=(1,1) NΞ channel, the three-body binding energy is 269 keV.
- The deuteron is bound by 2.225 MeV, the triton by 8.480 MeV, and the α particle by 28.295 MeV, illustrating the cooperative effect of attraction in few-baryon systems.
- The hypertriton ³ΛH has a separation energy of 130 ± 50 keV, and ⁴ΛH has a separation energy of 2.04 ± 0.04 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of strangeness -2 hypernuclei, including predictions of Ξ hypernuclei by Yamaguchi (2010), Hiyama (2008), and Yamada (2001), as well as the strangeness -2 hypertriton by Garcilazo (2013). It complements these studies by examining the simplest three-baryon system that can be solved exactly, using NΞ interactions from the ESC08c Nijmegen potential (Rijken, 2013; Nagels, 2014) and the constituent quark cluster model (Carames, 2012). The results are directly relevant to planned experiments at J-PARC using (K⁻,K⁺) reactions on ¹²C targets, and to the recent emulsion evidence of a deeply bound Ξ⁻-¹⁴N state (Nakazawa, 2015).
