# Spinodal Enhancement of Light Nuclei Yield Ratio in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> Using a relativistic transport model to describe the evolution of the quantum chromodynamic matter produced in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3-200$ GeV, we study the effect of a first-order phase transition in the equation of state of this matter on the yield ratio $N_tN_p/ N_d^2$ ($tp/d^2$) of produced proton ($p$), deuteron ($d$), and triton ($t$). We find that the large density inhomogeneities generated by the spinodal instability during the first-order phase transition can survive the fast expansion of the subsequent hadronic matter and lead to an enhanced $tp/d^2$ in central collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3-5$ GeV as seen in the experiments by the STAR Collaboration and the E864 Collaboration. However, this enhancement subsides with increasing collision centrality, and the resulting almost flat centrality dependence of $tp/d^2$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3$ GeV can also be used as a signal for the first-order phase transition.

## Key claim

The large density inhomogeneities generated by spinodal instability during a first-order QCD phase transition can survive the fast hadronic expansion and enhance the light-nuclei yield ratio \(N_t N_p / N_d^2\) in central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3\text{–}5\) GeV, but only for equations of state with a critical temperature \(T_c \gtrsim 80\) MeV; the enhancement subsides with increasing collision centrality, producing an almost flat centrality dependence at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3\) GeV that can serve as a signal for the first-order phase transition.

## Method

The authors developed a relativistic transport model based on an extended Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) Lagrangian with an eight-quark scalar-vector interaction, allowing flexible critical temperatures. Initial parton distributions were taken from the AMPT model; parton evolution included mean-field potentials and scatterings, followed by hadronization via spatial coalescence and hadronic rescattering via the ART model. Light-nuclei yields (deuteron, triton) were computed at kinetic freeze-out using a nucleon coalescence model with Gaussian Wigner functions, and the density fluctuations were quantified by the second-order scaled density moment \(y_2\).

## Key numbers

- For EoS-I with \(T_c = 154\) MeV, the yield ratio \(tp/d^2\) shows a maximum enhancement of more than 50% at low collision energies compared to the case without a first-order phase transition.
- At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3\) GeV, the centrality dependence of \(tp/d^2\) for EoS-I (first-order transition) has a slope of \(0.09 \pm 0.05\), while for EoS-II (crossover) the slope is \(0.44 \pm 0.03\).
- The experimental data from STAR and E864 at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3\text{–}5\) GeV are consistent with theoretical results using an EoS with \(T_c \approx 150\) MeV.
- The hadronization condition was set when the average quark mass exceeded 200 MeV, corresponding to an energy density around 0.26 GeV/fm\(^3\).
- The size parameters in the Wigner functions are \(\sigma_d = 2.26\) fm and \(\sigma_t = 1.59\) fm.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of spinodal instability effects in heavy-ion collisions using hydrodynamic approaches (Steinheimer 2012, 2013) and transport models (Li 2016) by implementing a flexible NJL-based equation of state with variable critical temperature. It directly addresses the inability of models without a first-order phase transition, such as AMPT+COAL (Sun 2020), UrQMD+COAL (Bleicher 1999), and MUSIC+UrQMD+COAL (Zhao 2021), to explain the enhanced \(tp/d^2\) observed by STAR and E864 at low collision energies. The results enable future experimental tests at RHIC FXT, CBM/FAIR, HADES/GSI, CEE/HIAF, NICA/MPD, NA61/SHINE, and J-PARC-HI, and suggest that including hadronic mean-field potentials could reduce the required \(T_c\) to values closer to theoretical predictions from functional renormalization (Fu 2019) and Dyson-Schwinger (Gao 2015) approaches.
