# Dynamical Evolution of the Scalar Condensate in Heavy Ion Collisions

> We derive the effective coarse-grained field equation for the scalar condensate of the linear sigma model in a simple and straightforward manner using linear response theory. The dissipative coefficient is calculated at tree level on the basis of the physical processes of sigma-meson decay and of thermal sigma-mesons and pions knocking sigma-mesons out of the condensate. The field equation is solved for hot matter undergoing either one or three dimensional expansion and cooling in the aftermath of a high energy nuclear collision. The results show that the time constant for returning the scalar condensate to thermal equilibrium is of order 2 fm/c.

## Key claim

The scalar condensate returns to thermal equilibrium with a time constant of order 2 fm/c after the phase transition from quark-gluon plasma to hadrons, but this result depends on the assumption that the system reaches local thermal equilibrium at a temperature above the critical temperature and that the deviation from equilibrium remains small enough for linear response theory to apply.

## Method

The authors derived a coarse-grained field equation for the scalar condensate of the linear sigma model using linear response theory, calculating the dissipative coefficient at tree level from sigma-meson decay into two pions and from thermal sigma-mesons and pions knocking sigma-mesons out of the condensate. They solved this equation for hot matter undergoing either one-dimensional or three-dimensional expansion and cooling after a high-energy nuclear collision, assuming a free massless gas of pions for the equation of state and a temperature evolution law T(t) = T_c (t_c/t)^{D/3} with t_c = 3 fm/c.

## Key numbers

- The time constant for returning the scalar condensate to thermal equilibrium is of order 2 fm/c.
- The critical temperature for the second-order phase transition is given by T_c^2 = 12 f_pi^2/(N+2), where f_pi is the pion decay constant and N=4 for two flavors of massless quarks.
- The sigma mass in vacuum is 6 f_pi, corresponding to lambda = 18.
- The field is overdamped when T > 0.8 T_c.
- The low-temperature limit of the sigma-pion scattering contribution to the imaginary self-energy is Im Pi_{sigma pi} = -13,440/pi^3 zeta(7) lambda^2 T^7/m_sigma^5.
- The high-temperature limit of the sigma-pion scattering contribution is Im Pi_{sigma pi} = -lambda^2 T^2/(96 pi).
- The low-temperature limit of the sigma-sigma scattering contribution is Im Pi_{sigma sigma} = -9/(2 pi^3) lambda^2 T^2 exp(-m_sigma/T).
- The high-temperature limit of the sigma-sigma scattering contribution is Im Pi_{sigma sigma} = -3/(64 pi) lambda^2 T^2.
- The deviation from equilibrium sigma_s is maximal less than 1/2 fm/c after the critical temperature is passed.

## Context

This work extends the equation-of-motion approach used by Linde (pioneering work on phase transitions in relativistic quantum field theory) and improves upon the influence functional and closed time path methods used by Rischke (1998) by employing linear response theory, which the authors argue is quicker, easier, and more intuitive. The inclusion of scattering of thermal pions and sigma mesons from sigma mesons in the condensate had not been studied before in this context. The results enable future studies of disoriented chiral condensates (DCC) and can be applied to other theories including nuclear matter, QCD, and electroweak theory.
