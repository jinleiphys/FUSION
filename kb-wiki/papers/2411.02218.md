# Radially excited pion: electromagnetic form factor and the box contribution to the muon's $g-2$

> We investigate the properties of the radially excited charged pion, with a specific focus on its electromagnetic form factor (EFF) and its box contribution to the hadronic light-by-light (HLbL) component of the muon's anomalous magnetic moment, $a_μ$. Utilizing a coupled non-perturbative framework combining Schwinger-Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter equations, we first compute the mass and weak decay constant of the pion's first radial excitation. Initial results are provided for the Rainbow-Ladder (RL) approximation, followed by an extended beyond RL (BRL) analysis that incorporates meson cloud effects. Building on our previous work, this analysis demonstrates that an accurate description of the first radial excitation can be achieved without the need for a reparametrization of the interaction kernels. Having demonstrated the effectiveness of the truncation scheme, we proceed to calculate the corresponding EFF, from which we derive the contribution of the pion's first radial excitation to the HLbL component of the muon's anomalous magnetic moment, producing $a_μ^{π_1-\text{box}}(\text{RL}) = -(2.03 \pm 0.12) \times 10 ^{-13}$, $a_μ^{π_1-\text{box}}(\text{BRL}) = -(2.02 \pm 0.10) \times 10 ^{-13}$. Our computation also sets the groundwork for calculating related pole contributions of excited pseudoscalar mesons to $a_μ$.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the first radial excitation of the pion can be accurately described within the Schwinger-Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter formalism using a single unified set of parameters for both ground and excited states, but only when the beyond-Rainbow-Ladder truncation incorporating meson cloud effects is employed; the simpler Rainbow-Ladder truncation fails to simultaneously describe both states.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled Schwinger-Dyson equation for the quark propagator and the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for the meson bound state, using both the Rainbow-Ladder (RL) approximation and an extended beyond-Rainbow-Ladder (BRL) scheme that includes explicit meson exchange contributions. The Maris-Tandy interaction model was employed with parameters fixed to ground-state pion properties, and the Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes were expanded in Chebyshev polynomials with analytic continuation to the complex plane via the Cauchy theorem. The electromagnetic form factor was then computed within the impulse approximation, and the box contribution to hadronic light-by-light was evaluated using the master formula of Colangelo et al. (2017).

## Key numbers

* Mass of the first radial excitation: \(m_{\pi(1300)} = 1.29(4)\) GeV (BRL), compared to the experimental value of 1.30 GeV
* Decay constant of the first radial excitation: \(f_{\pi_1} = -0.0017(4)\) GeV (BRL), matching the QCD sum rule expectation of \(-1.6(3)\) MeV from Maltman (2001)
* Ground-state pion mass and decay constant from BRL: \(m_\pi = 0.139(2)\) GeV, \(f_\pi = 0.130(2)\) GeV, in agreement with experimental values of 0.139 GeV and 0.130 GeV respectively
* Box contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment: \(a_\mu^{\pi_1-\text{box}}(\text{RL}) = -(2.03 \pm 0.12) \times 10^{-13}\), \(a_\mu^{\pi_1-\text{box}}(\text{BRL}) = -(2.02 \pm 0.10) \times 10^{-13}\)
* Charge radius of the first radial excitation: \(r_{\pi(1300)} = (0.583 \pm 0.010)\) fm, with ratio \(r_{\pi(1300)}/r_{\pi} \approx 0.88\) despite the mass ratio \(m_{\pi(1300)}/m_{\pi} \approx 9.3\)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies on ground-state pion and kaon box contributions to the muon \(g-2\) (Miramontes et al., 2021) and pseudoscalar-pole contributions (Raya et al., 2019). It addresses the known limitation of the Rainbow-Ladder truncation, which Holl et al. (2004) and Xu et al. (2022) showed produces insufficiently large excited pion masses without artificial parameter inflation. The results enable future calculation of related pole contributions from excited pseudoscalar mesons to the muon anomalous magnetic moment, and the framework provides a foundation for studying time-like electromagnetic form factors with proper analytic structure.
