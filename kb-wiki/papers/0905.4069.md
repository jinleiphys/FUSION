# Improved Hydrodynamics from the AdS/CFT

> We generalize (linearized) relativistic hydrodynamics by including all order gradient expansion of the energy momentum tensor, parametrized by four momenta-dependend transport coefficients, one of which is the usual shear viscosity. We then apply the AdS/CFT duality for ${\cal N}=4$ SUSY in order to compute the retarded correlators of the energy-momentum tensor. From these correlators we determine a large set of transport coefficients of third- and fourth-order hydrodynamics. We find that higher order terms have a tendency to reduce the effect of viscosity.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes linearized relativistic hydrodynamics to all orders in the gradient expansion of the energy-momentum tensor, but the four momentum-dependent transport coefficients cannot be uniquely determined from the three independent retarded correlators of the stress tensor, leaving the full functional forms of the generalized viscosity and gravitational susceptibilities incompletely constrained.

## Method

The authors first write a generic hydro-like representation of the energy-momentum tensor in terms of the fluid velocity field, identifying four independent structures consistent with Lorentz and Weyl symmetries: one shear viscosity term and three gravitational susceptibility terms involving the Weyl tensor. They then compute the retarded correlators of the energy-momentum tensor in the sound, shear, and scalar channels using the AdS/CFT duality for N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory at strong coupling, solving linearized gravity equations in the AdS-Schwarzschild black hole background with incoming wave boundary conditions at the horizon. The momentum-dependent transport coefficients are extracted by matching the hydro-like representation to the correlators computed from the gravity side, obtaining perturbative expansions at small momenta up to fifth order in the gradient expansion.

## Key numbers

* The ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density at zero frequency and momentum is eta_0/s = 1/(4 pi), the universal lower bound conjectured by Kovtun, Son, and Starinets (2005).
* The equilibrium pressure for N=4 SUSY at strong coupling is P = pi^2 N_c^2 T^4/8, with energy density epsilon = 3P due to conformal symmetry.
* The QCD critical temperature is T_c approximately 170 MeV, with RHIC initial temperatures T_i approximately 2 T_c and freezeout temperature T_f approximately T_c/2.
* The saturation scale for initial state fluctuations is 1/Q_s approximately 0.2 fm, about 30 times smaller than the fireball radius.
* The shear viscosity function is determined to fifth order in the gradient expansion, involving several new transport coefficients obtained numerically.

## Context

This work extends the program of computing transport coefficients via the AdS/CFT correspondence pioneered by Kovtun, Son, and Starinets (2005), who found the universal viscosity bound and computed second-order coefficients. It goes beyond the Israel-Stewart resummation scheme (Israel and Stewart, 1979), which the authors show is qualitatively inconsistent with AdS/CFT results starting from third order in the gradient expansion. The paper builds on earlier work by Lublinsky and Shuryak (2009) that attempted to extract a momentum-dependent viscosity from the sound dispersion curve, but the present analysis demonstrates that the sound dispersion curve alone contains insufficient information to define a generalized viscosity function. The results enable improved hydrodynamic modeling of small-scale perturbations in the quark-gluon plasma produced at RHIC, such as the "cone" and "ridge" phenomena associated with jet propagation and initial state fluctuations.
