# Thomas-Fermi theory for atomic nuclei revisited

> The recently developed semiclassical variational Wigner-Kirkwood (VWK) approach is applied to finite nuclei using external potentials and self-consistent mean fields derived from Skyrme interactions and from relativistic mean field theory. VWK consists of the Thomas-Fermi part plus a pure, perturbative hbar^2 correction. In external potentials, VWK passes through the average of the quantal values of the accumulated level density and total energy as a function of the Fermi energy. However, there is a problem of overbinding when the energy per particle is displayed as a function of the particle number. The situation is analyzed comparing spherical and deformed harmonic oscillator potentials. In the self-consistent case, we show for Skyrme forces that VWK binding energies are very close to those obtained from extended Thomas-Fermi functionals of hbar^4 order, pointing to the rapid convergence of the VWK theory. This satisfying result, however, does not cure the overbinding problem, i.e., the semiclassical energies show more binding than they should. This feature is more pronounced in the case of Skyrme forces than with the relativistic mean field approach. However, even in the latter case the shell correction energy for e.g. 208Pb turns out to be only -6 MeV what is about a factor two or three off the generally accepted value. As an ad hoc remedy, increasing the kinetic energy by 2.5%, leads to shell correction energies well acceptable throughout the periodic table. The general importance of the present studies for other finite Fermi systems, self-bound or in external potentials, is pointed out.

## Key claim

The variational Wigner-Kirkwood (VWK) semiclassical method, when applied to finite nuclei with self-consistent mean fields, systematically overbinds (predicts more binding energy than the quantal Hartree-Fock results), and this overbinding is more severe for Skyrme forces than for relativistic mean field theory; an ad hoc increase of the kinetic energy by 2.5% can restore acceptable shell correction energies across the periodic table.

## Method

The authors applied the variational Wigner-Kirkwood (VWK) approach, which consists of the Thomas-Fermi (TF) part plus a perturbative \(\hbar^2\) correction, to finite nuclei using both external potentials (harmonic oscillator and Woods-Saxon) and self-consistent mean fields derived from Skyrme interactions and relativistic mean field (RMF) theory. In the self-consistent case, the VWK2 energy was computed by taking the ETF2 energy expression but evaluating it with the TF density solution \(\rho_0\) obtained from the lowest-order variational TF equation, ensuring proper sorting of powers in \(\hbar\). For Skyrme forces, VWK2 results were compared with ETF4 calculations, and for RMF, the relativistic TF equations were solved and used with the VWK2 formalism.

## Key numbers

- For the spherical harmonic oscillator potential, the semiclassical WK accumulated level density is \(N_{\text{WK}}(\varepsilon) = \frac{1}{3} (\varepsilon / \hbar \omega)^3 - \frac{1}{4} (\varepsilon / \hbar \omega)\) and the WK energy is \(E_{\text{WK}}(\varepsilon) = [\frac{1}{4} (\varepsilon / \hbar \omega)^4 - \frac{1}{8} (\varepsilon / \hbar \omega)^2 - \frac{17}{960}] \hbar \omega\).
- For \(^{208}\text{Pb}\) in the relativistic mean field approach, the shell correction energy from VWK is only \(\sim -6\) MeV, which is about a factor of two or three off the generally accepted value.
- Increasing the kinetic energy by 2.5% as an ad hoc remedy leads to shell correction energies well acceptable throughout the periodic table.
- In the external Woods-Saxon potential with parameters \(V_0 = -44\) MeV, \(a = 0.67\) fm, and \(R = 1.27 A^{1/3}\) fm for \(A = 208\), the WK accumulated level density and energy accurately average the quantal values even as the Fermi energy approaches zero.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of VWK theory applied to half-infinite nuclear matter (Centelles et al., 2005; Centelles et al., 1998) and surface energy calculations (Centelles et al., 1998; Centelles et al., 2000) to finite nuclei with self-consistent mean fields. The VWK approach is contrasted with the extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) method, which does not properly sort out powers in \(\hbar\) and shows slower convergence; the authors note that ETF surface energies deviate from quantal values by 10% at \(\hbar^2\) order and 7% at \(\hbar^4\) order, while VWK reproduces quantal surface energies within 1% at \(\hbar^2\) order for external potentials. The overbinding problem identified here challenges the reliability of semiclassical methods for precise nuclear mass predictions and motivates further development of improved semiclassical functionals or alternative treatments of shell corrections.
