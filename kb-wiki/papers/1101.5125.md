# Probing the QCD Critical Point by Higher Moments of Net-Charge Distribution

> The Beam Energy Scan program has been undertaken at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) to search for the QCD critical point. The presence of the critical point is expected to lead to non-monotonic behavior of several quantities. Here we report the result of higher moments of net-charge distributions for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 39 GeV as measured by the STAR experiment. The STAR results are compared with results from HIJING event generator and Hadron Resonance (HRG) Models.

## Key claim

The STAR experiment reports preliminary higher moments of net-charge distributions for Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 39 GeV, but within current experimental uncertainties the results show no clear non-monotonic signature of the QCD critical point, as the measured Kσ² and Sσ values are comparable for 39 and 200 GeV and are consistent with both the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model and the HIJING event generator which does not include critical point physics.

## Method

The STAR experiment used the Time Projection Chamber (TPC) to identify charged particles within full azimuthal and |η| < 0.5 pseudorapidity, with transverse momentum between 0.15 and 1.0 GeV/c, from approximately 6 × 10⁶ minimum bias Au+Au events at √sNN = 39 GeV. The Time Of Flight (TOF) detector was used to identify electron and positron backgrounds. The mean, standard deviation, skewness, and kurtosis of the net-charge distribution were measured as a function of centrality (characterized by average number of participants from Glauber model calculations), and the volume-independent products Kσ² and Sσ were compared with HIJING and HRG model predictions.

## Key numbers

- Nearly 6 × 10⁶ minimum bias events were used for Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 39 GeV
- Charged particles were measured in the range 0.15 < pT < 1.0 GeV/c at |η| < 0.5
- The Kσ² values for net-charge distributions are larger than unity for all centralities at both 39 and 200 GeV
- The Sσ values are close to zero for different centralities at both 39 and 200 GeV
- The Kσ² and Sσ values are similar for Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 39 and 200 GeV

## Context

This work is part of the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program (started in 2010) which aims to map the QCD phase diagram and locate the critical point predicted by lattice calculations, scanning beam energies from √sNN = 5 to 200 GeV corresponding to baryon chemical potentials from 550 MeV down to 14 MeV. The results extend prior measurements at 200 GeV (STAR, 2010) and are compared with the Hadron Resonance Gas model (Karsch and Redlich, 2010) and HIJING event generator; the consistency of data with HIJING (which contains no critical point physics) indicates that further analysis at other BES energies is needed to identify any non-monotonic behavior.
