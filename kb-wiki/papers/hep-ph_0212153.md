# The new analysis of the KLOE data on the phi->eta pi0 gamma decay

> In this paper we present the fit to the recent high-statistical KLOE data on the phi -> eta pi^0 gamma decay. This decay mainly goes through the a_0 gamma intermediate state. The obtained results differ from those of the previous fits: data prefer a high a_0 mass and a considerably large a_0 coupling to the K\bar K}.

# Wiki Page: New Analysis of KLOE Data on φ → η π⁰ γ Decay

## Key claim
The KLOE data on φ → η π⁰ γ decay, when fitted with the a₀ mass as a free parameter and the interference phase δ as a free parameter, prefer a higher a₀ mass (around 992–1003 MeV) and a considerably larger a₀ coupling to K̄K than previous fits that fixed the a₀ mass at the PDG-2000 value of 984.8 MeV. The phase δ is consistent with zero in all fits.

## Method
The authors performed a χ² fit to the KLOE data on the mass spectrum of φ → η π⁰ γ decay, modeling the dominant signal process φ → a₀ γ → η π⁰ γ via a one-loop mechanism (φ → K⁺K⁻ → γ a₀) and the background process φ → ρ⁰ π⁰ → η π⁰ γ. The free parameters were the a₀ mass m_{a₀}, the coupling g²_{a₀K⁺K⁻}/(4π), the ratio g_{a₀ηπ}/g_{a₀K⁺K⁻}, and the interference phase δ; four variants of the fit were performed, including fits with δ fixed to zero and fits omitting a potentially problematic data point at 0.999 GeV. The theoretical mass spectrum was averaged over each experimental bin before comparison.

## Key numbers
- a₀ mass from fit 1 (all parameters free): m_{a₀} = 1003^{+32}_{-13} MeV
- a₀ mass from fit 4 (δ=0, omitting 0.999 GeV point): m_{a₀} = 992^{+14}_{-7} MeV
- Coupling from fit 1: g²_{a₀K⁺K⁻}/(4π) = 0.82^{+0.81}_{-0.27} GeV²
- Coupling from fit 4: g²_{a₀K⁺K⁻}/(4π) = 0.55^{+0.27}_{-0.13} GeV²
- Ratio g_{a₀ηπ}/g_{a₀K⁺K⁻} from fit 1: 1.06^{+0.20}_{-0.27}
- Ratio from fit 4: 1.26^{+0.16}_{-0.20}
- Total branching ratio Br(φ → (a₀γ + ρπ⁰) → ηπ⁰γ) = (7.6 ± 0.4) × 10⁻⁵ for all fits
- Signal branching ratio Br(φ → a₀γ → ηπ⁰γ) = (7.1–7.3 ± 0.4) × 10⁻⁵ across fits
- a₀ → ηπ⁰ width Γ_{a₀ηπ⁰} from fit 1: 153^{+22}_{-17} MeV
- a₀ → ηπ⁰ width from fit 4: 146^{+17}_{-15} MeV
- Ratio R = g²_{f₀K⁺K⁻}/g²_{a₀K⁺K⁻} from fit 1: 3.4 ± 1.7
- Ratio R from fit 4: 5.0 ± 1.6
- Background branching ratio Br(φ → ρπ⁰ → ηπ⁰γ) = (0.5 ± 0.1) × 10⁻⁵
- Coupling constants used as input: g_{φK⁺K⁻} = 4.376 ± 0.074, g_{φρπ} = 0.814 ± 0.018 GeV⁻¹, g_{ρηγ} = 0.56 ± 0.05 GeV⁻¹

## Context
This work extends the analysis of the KLOE Collaboration (2002) on φ → ηπ⁰γ decay by relaxing the constraints imposed in the original KLOE fit, which fixed the a₀ mass at the PDG-2000 value of 984.8 MeV and did not treat the interference phase as a free parameter. The results differ from those of the earlier KLOE analysis (which found g²_{a₀K⁺K⁻}/(4π) = 0.4 ± 0.04 GeV²) and from the predictions of the four-quark model of Jaffe (1977) and Achasov (1989), which gave g_{a₀ηπ}/g_{a₀K⁺K⁻} ≈ 0.85–0.93. The analysis confirms that the relation between a₀ and f₀ production in φ radiative decays depends strongly on the a₀–f₀ mass splitting, as previously shown by Achasov (1997). The extracted a₀ mass is consistent with that needed to describe γγ → ηπ⁰ data.
