# The effective g_A in the pf-shell

> We have calculated the Gamow-Teller matrix elements of 64 decays of nuclei in the mass range A=41--50. In all the cases the valence space of the full pf-shell is used. Agreement with the experimental results demands the introduction of an average quenching factor, q=0.744 +/- 0.015, slightly smaller but statistically compatible with the $sd$-shell value, thus indicating that the present number is close to the limit for large A.

# The effective g_A in the pf-shell

## Key claim

The average quenching factor required to bring full pf-shell Gamow-Teller calculations into agreement with experimental beta-decay data is q = 0.744 ± 0.015, which is slightly smaller than the sd-shell value of 0.77 ± 0.02 but statistically compatible with it, suggesting that the quenching factor has reached its large-A limit.

## Method

The authors performed full 0ℏω shell-model diagonalizations using the ANTOINE code with the KB3 effective interaction (a minimally monopole-modified version of the original Kuo-Brown matrix elements) for 64 Gamow-Teller decays of nuclei in the mass range A = 41–50. For each transition populating well-defined isolated states in the daughter nucleus, they computed the ratio of the experimental rate to the calculated rate, defining the square root of this ratio as the quenching factor for that transition, and extracted an average quenching factor q from the slope of a linear fit to the normalized strength R(GT) = M(GT)/W.

## Key numbers

- Average quenching factor: q = 0.744 ± 0.015 for the pf-shell (A = 41–50)
- sd-shell quenching factor: q = 0.77 ± 0.02 (from prior work by the same group, cited as quen-sd2)
- p-shell quenching factor: q = 0.82 ± 0.015 (from prior work cited as quen-p)
- Free-nucleon axial coupling constant: g_A = −1.2599(25) (from Towner, cited in text)
- Superallowed decay constant used in ft-value calculation: 6146 ± 6 (from the nine best-known 0⁺ → 0⁺ decays, cited as Towner)

## Context

This work extends the systematic analysis of Gamow-Teller quenching from the sd-shell (A ≤ 40) into the lower pf-shell, using the same methodology as the sd-shell studies of Towner (cited as quen-sd1, quen-sd2) and the p-shell study (quen-p). The results confirm that the quenching factor decreases with mass number, with the pf-shell value being close to the sd-shell value, indicating that the large-A regime has been reached; this is consistent with the quenching factors extracted by Osterfeld from (p,n) data in heavier nuclei. The work enables more reliable predictions of beta-decay lifetimes and Gamow-Teller strengths in pf-shell nuclei, and the q factor itself serves as a fundamental quantity reflecting the reduction in the discontinuity at the Fermi surface due to nuclear correlations.
