# SU(N) Multi-Skyrmions at Finite Volume

> We study multi-soliton solutions of the four-dimensional SU(N) Skyrme model by combining the hedgehog ansatz for SU(N) based on the harmonic maps of $S^{2}$ into $CP^{N-1}$ and a geometrical trick which allows to analyze explicitly finite-volume effects without breaking the relevant symmetries of the ansatz. The geometric setup allows to introduce a parameter which is related to the 't Hooft coupling of a suitable large $N$ limit, in which $N\rightarrow\infty$ and the curvature of the background metric approaches zero, in such a way that their product is constant. The relevance of such a parameter to the physics of the system is pointed out. In particular, we discuss how the discrete symmetries of the configurations depend on it.

## Key claim

The authors construct multi-Skyrmion solutions of the SU(N) Skyrme model in a finite-volume cylindrical geometry, showing that a simultaneous large-N and flat limit (where N → ∞ and the curvature radius R₀ → ∞ such that R₀²/(N-1) remains constant) yields a well-defined effective theory with a geometric 't Hooft coupling, though the cylindrical topology persists even in the flat limit.

## Method

The authors combine the hedgehog ansatz for SU(N) Skyrmions based on harmonic maps of S² into CP^(N-1) with a cylindrical metric ds² = -dt² + dx² + R₀²(dθ² + sin²θ dφ²) that has finite spatial volume V = 4πR₀²L. This metric preserves SO(3) symmetry while making the field equations autonomous (independent of the radial coordinate), unlike the non-autonomous equations in flat space studied by previous authors. The system of N-1 coupled differential equations for the profile functions F_k is analyzed using dynamical systems theory, with stability analysis of fixed points performed explicitly for SU(3) and numerical solutions exhibited for SU(4).

## Key numbers

- The spatial volume is V = 4πR₀²L, where R₀ is the radius of the spherical cross-sections and L is the length of the cylinder.
- In the flat-large N limit, the effective radius R² = R₀²/(N-1) is kept finite, defining a geometric 't Hooft coupling 1/R².
- The winding number formula for SU(N) is W = (1/2π) Σ_{i=0}^{N-2} (1+i)(N-i-1) [F_i - sin F_i] evaluated at the boundaries x = ±L/2.
- For SU(3), the fixed point at F₀* = F₁* = 0 has eigenvalues λ = ±1/R and λ = ±√3/R, making it unstable for all R.
- For SU(3), the fixed point at F₀* = F₁* = π has purely imaginary eigenvalues λ = ±(i√6/2)√((R²+3)(2R²+1))/((R²+3)R) and λ = ±(i√2/2)√((R²+1)(2R²+1))/((R²+1)R), making it stable for all R.
- The energy unit is [E] = F_π/(4e) ≈ 1 GeV, and the length unit is [L] = 2/(eF_π) ≈ 0.6 fm.

## Context

This work extends the finite-volume multi-Skyrmion construction of Canfora et al. (2015) from SU(2) to SU(N), building on the SU(N) hedgehog ansatz developed by Ioannidou et al. (2000, 2001) based on harmonic maps of S² into CP^(N-1). The cylindrical metric technique, previously applied to Yang-Mills-Higgs systems by Canfora et al. (2013), allows analytic treatment of finite-volume effects that are relevant for nuclear pasta phases and many-nucleon systems. The results enable future thermodynamic analysis of multi-Skyrmion systems at large baryon number and provide a framework for studying how discrete symmetries of Skyrmion configurations depend on the geometric 't Hooft parameter.
