# The influence of the enhanced vector meson sector on the properties of the matter of neutron stars

> This paper gives an overview of the model of a neutron star with non-zero strangeness constructed within the framework of the nonlinear realization of the chiral $SU(3)_{L}\times SU(3)_{R}$ symmetry. The emphasis is put on the physical properties of the matter of a neutron star. The obtained solution is particularly aimed at the problem of the construction of a theoretical model of a neutron star matter with hyperons that will give high value of the maximum mass.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a neutron star model including hyperons can achieve a high maximum mass (consistent with the observed ~2 solar mass pulsars) if the vector meson sector is enhanced with nonlinear mixed couplings, specifically the ω-ρ coupling parameter ΛV, which stiffens the equation of state at high densities.

## Method

The authors construct a relativistic mean-field model based on nonlinear realization of chiral SU(3)L × SU(3)R symmetry, extending the TM1 parameter set with an enhanced vector meson sector that includes nonlinear self-interactions and mixed ω-ρ-φ couplings. They solve the mean-field equations of motion for β-equilibrated neutron star matter containing the full baryon octet (n, p, Λ, Σ, Ξ) and leptons, using coupling constants constrained by hyperon potentials from hypernuclear data and the quark model. The equation of state is then computed for various values of the ω-ρ coupling strength ΛV, and the resulting pressure-density relations are compared with those from other parameter sets (NL3, FSUGold, TM1-weak) and with a quark matter EoS.

## Key numbers

- The symmetry energy is fixed to Esym = 25.68 MeV at kF = 1.15 fm−1 (corresponding to baryon density n0)
- The Λ hyperon potential in nuclear matter is taken as UΛ(N) = −28 MeV, the Σ potential as UΣ(N) = +30 MeV, and the Ξ potential as UΞ(N) = −18 MeV
- The slope parameter L of the symmetry energy ranges from 108.58 MeV (ΛV = 0) down to 72.56 MeV (ΛV = 0.017), compared to the experimental range L = 88 ± 25 MeV and a narrower estimate L = 40.5–61.9 MeV
- The critical value of the ω-ρ coupling parameter ΛV,cr ranges from 0.0156 (TM1) to 0.0517 (FSUGold), above which the mean-field equations no longer yield a single real solution
- The scalar coupling constants for hyperons are: gΛσ = 6.169, gΞσ = 3.201, gΣσ = 4.476, gΛσ* = 5.262, gΞσ* = 11.623, gΣσ* = 5.626

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean-field approach of Walecka (2004) and the TM1 parameter set of Sugahara (1994) by incorporating the full SU(3) baryon octet with enhanced vector meson couplings, building on the chiral model framework of Papazoglou (1998, 1999). The paper directly addresses the "hyperon puzzle", the tension between observations of ~2 solar mass neutron stars (Demorest 2010, Antoniadis 2013) and theoretical models where hyperon softening limits maximum masses to lower values. The results enable construction of stiffer hyperonic equations of state that may reconcile theory with observation, and the analysis of the symmetry energy slope L connects to constraints from nuclear experiments and astrophysical observations.
