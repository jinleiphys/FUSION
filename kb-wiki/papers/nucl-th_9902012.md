# Deuteron formation in nuclear matter within the Faddeev approach

> We consider deuteron formation in heavy ion collisions at intermediate energies. The elementary reaction rates (Nd -> NNN) in this context are calculated using rigorous Faddeev methods. To this end an in-medium Faddeev equation that consistently includes the energy shift and Pauli blocking effects has been derived and solved numerically. As a first application we have calculated the life-time of deuteron fluctuations for nuclear densities and temperatures typical for the final stage of heavy ion collisions. We find substantial differences between using the isolated and the in-medium rates.

## Key claim

The in-medium break-up cross section for deuteron formation in nuclear matter is significantly enhanced compared to the isolated cross section, leading to shorter reaction time scales for chemical processes; however, this medium dependence weakens at higher energies, which justifies the use of isolated cross sections in that regime.

## Method

The authors derived and solved an in-medium Faddeev equation using the AGS formalism extended to finite temperatures and densities, consistently including energy shifts and Pauli blocking effects. They solved the three-body equations for a separable Yamaguchi potential and calculated the break-up transition operator for the reaction Nd → NNN. From linearizing the Boltzmann equation, they extracted the break-up time for small fluctuations of the deuteron distribution and the chemical relaxation time for deuteron density fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- Isolated cross section compared with experimental data on neutron-deuteron scattering from Sch83
- In-medium break-up cross section calculated at temperature T = 10 MeV
- Deuteron break-up time calculated at T = 10 MeV and nuclear density n = 0.007 fm⁻³
- Duration of the heavy ion collision approximately 200 fm, used as comparison for calculated time scales
- Relaxation time for small fluctuations of the deuteron density from chemical equilibrium calculated at T = 10 MeV as a function of uncorrelated nuclear density

## Context

This work extends the cluster Hartree-Fock approach of Roepke and the self-consistent RPA approach of Schuck to include three-body correlations via rigorous Faddeev/AGS equations. It builds on prior work by Danielewicz (1991) who implemented experimental cross sections into the Boltzmann equation for heavy ion collisions, and by Beyer (1996, 1997) who derived the in-medium three-body Faddeev equations. The results enable improved calculations of light charged particle production rates and spectra in heavy ion collisions at intermediate energies, particularly for the final stage where densities and temperatures are lower.
