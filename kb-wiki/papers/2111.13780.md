# Space-time symmetric qubit regularization of the asymptotically free two-dimensional O(4) model

> We explore if space-time symmetric lattice field theory models with a finite Hilbert space per lattice site can reproduce asymptotic freedom in the two-dimensional $O(4)$ model. We focus on a simple class of such models with a five dimensional local Hilbert space. We demonstrate how even the simplest model reproduces asymptotic freedom within the D-theory formalism but at the cost of increasing the size of the Hilbert space through coupling several layers of a two-dimensional lattice. We then argue that qubit regularization can be viewed as an effective field theory (EFT) even if the continuum limit cannot be reached, as long as we can tune the model close enough to the continuum limit where perturbation theory, or other analytical techniques, become viable. We construct a simple lattice model on a single layer with a four dimensional local Hilbert space that acts like an excellent EFT of the original theory.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a space-time symmetric lattice model with a finite five-dimensional local Hilbert space per site can reproduce asymptotic freedom in the two-dimensional O(4) model, but only through the D-theory formalism by coupling multiple layers of the two-dimensional lattice; alternatively, a single-layer model with a four-dimensional local Hilbert space can serve as an effective field theory (EFT) even without reaching the true continuum limit, provided it can be tuned close enough to make contact with perturbation theory.

## Method

The authors compute the step scaling function σ(2,u) for the traditional O(4) nonlinear sigma model using the Wolff cluster algorithm on lattices from L=32 to L=512 at couplings g = 0.22, 0.25, 0.30, 0.35, 0.40, and 0.50, with a cubic smoothing spline to obtain a universal curve. They then construct a qubit-regularized loop-gas model with a five-dimensional local Hilbert space (singlets and two flavors of oriented loops) on L_y coupled layers of square lattices at fixed U=0.1, and compute the step scaling function for L_y=1 through 6 using worm-update Monte Carlo methods. Finally, they extend the single-layer model by adding diagonal bonds with tunable weight J, exploring parameters (U,J) = (0.1,0), (0,3.0), and (0,5.0).

## Key numbers

- The traditional O(4) model step scaling function was computed at couplings g = 0.22, 0.25, 0.30, 0.35, 0.40, and 0.50 on lattices from L=32 up to L=512.
- The three-dimensional O(4) model undergoes a phase transition at U_c ≈ 3.85(5).
- For the D-theory model at U=0.1 with L_y=6, the infinite volume correlation length ξ_∞ = 860(30) lattice units, with ξ(L_a)/ξ_∞ = 0.015.
- For the single-layer extended model at (U,J) = (0,5.0), ξ_∞ = 61(1) lattice units, with ξ(L_a)/L_a = 0.66 and ξ(L_a)/ξ_∞ = 0.13.
- The single-layer model at (U,J) = (0,5.0) requires only two qubits per two-dimensional lattice site.

## Context

This work extends the D-theory approach of Brower (2003) and Wiese (2006) to space-time symmetric qubit regularizations of asymptotically free theories, building on earlier demonstrations that O(N) symmetric fixed points can be reproduced with finite-dimensional Hilbert spaces (Singh, 2019). The authors show that while the simple five-dimensional model reproduces asymptotic freedom only through multi-layer D-theory, a tuned single-layer model with four-dimensional Hilbert space can serve as an EFT, enabling quantum computation of the O(4) model with minimal qubit resources. This approach contrasts with Hamiltonian qubit regularizations that lose manifest space-time symmetry, and opens the possibility of extending these methods to SU(2) gauge theories.
