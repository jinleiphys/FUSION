# Phenomenological Relationship between the Ridge and Inclusive Distributions

> A relationship between the ridge distribution in $Δη$ and the single-particle distribution in $η$ is proposed. It is then verified by use of the data from PHOBOS on both distributions. The implication seems to point to the possibility that there is no long-range longitudinal correlation. An interpretation of the phenomenological observation along that possibility is developed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes and verifies a phenomenological relationship between the ridge distribution in Δη and the single-particle pseudorapidity distribution, suggesting that the wide Δη range of the ridge does not necessarily imply long-range longitudinal correlation, contrary to conventional wisdom.

## Method

The authors compare PHOBOS data on the per-trigger ridge yield (1/N^trig) dN_R^ch/dΔη for Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV with the single-particle charged hadron distribution dN^ch/dη, using a fit of three Gaussians to represent the η distribution. They propose a formula relating the ridge distribution to an integral of the single-particle distribution over the trigger η range (0 < η_1 < 1.5), with a single scaling parameter r adjusted to match the ridge height.

## Key numbers

- The ridge distribution data are for Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV with trigger p_T^trig > 2.5 GeV/c and trigger acceptance 0 < η^trig < 1.5, for 0-30% centrality.
- The single-particle η distribution fit parameters are: A = 468, σ_0 = 2.69, a_1 = 0.31, η̂ = 2.43, σ_1 = 1.15.
- The scaling parameter r is 4.4 × 10^{-4}.
- The associated particle p_T acceptance lower limit is 35(1-η/3.75) MeV/c, ranging from about 7 MeV/c at η = 3 to 35 MeV/c at η = 0.
- For the interpretation, illustrative values are T = 0.28 GeV and T' = 0.32 GeV, giving T'' = 2.24 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of the ridge structure in two-particle correlations at RHIC, previously observed by PHOBOS (Back et al., 2005) and others, and now also seen in pp collisions at LHC. The paper challenges the view of Adcox et al. (2005) and Gavin et al. (2009) that the wide Δη distribution implies long-range longitudinal correlation, instead showing that the ridge can be explained by the single-particle η distribution and trigger smearing. The interpretation builds on the recombination model of Hwa and Yang (2004-2008), treating ridge formation as a consequence of energy loss by hard partons traversing the medium, with transverse broadening analogous to the Cronin effect.
