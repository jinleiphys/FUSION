# Rigid triaxiality has the SU(3) symmetry: $^{166}$Er as an example

> The emergence of triaxiality in the low-lying collective bands of $^{166}$Er is systematically explored within the SU3-IBM. In this framework, SU(3) higher-order interactions are included, which enable the descriptions of various quadrupole deformations. The triaxiality of $^{166}$Er is described with a triaxiality angle $γ=9.7^{\circ}$. In addition, the calculated energy spectra, $B(E2)$ transition strengths, and quadrupole moments show excellent agreement with experimental data. These results provide further evidence supporting the SU(3) triaxial interpretation of $^{166}$Er and confirm its triaxial deformation rather than the prolate shape.

# Rigid triaxiality has the SU(3) symmetry: $^{166}$Er as an example

## Key claim
The paper claims that $^{166}$Er exhibits rigid triaxial deformation with a triaxiality angle $\gamma=9.7^{\circ}$ within the SU3-IBM framework, rather than the traditionally assumed prolate shape. However, this $\gamma$ value is an effective mean value obtained from a weighted average over mixed SU(3) irreps, not a value determined by a single irrep, and the SU(3) symmetry is broken by the $\hat{n}_d$ and $\hat{H}_D$ terms in the Hamiltonian.

## Method
The authors employ the SU3-IBM Hamiltonian containing SU(3) higher-order interactions (second-, third-, and fourth-order Casimir operators) plus a dynamical rotor term and a $d$-boson number operator. For $^{166}$Er with boson number $N=15$, they fix the Hamiltonian parameters by fitting to experimental ground-state energies, $B(E2)$ transition strengths, and energy levels, using the SU(3) irrep (22,4) as the dominant component. The effective triaxiality angle $\bar{\gamma}$ is computed as a weighted average over the $\gamma$ values of individual SU(3) irreps contributing to each state, weighted by their decomposition probabilities.

## Key numbers
- Triaxiality angle $\gamma = 9.7^{\circ}$ for the $0_1^+$ ground state of $^{166}$Er, obtained from SU3-IBM calculation
- Dominant SU(3) irrep for the ground state and several low-lying states is (22,4), with decomposition probability 0.882 for $0_1^+$, 0.883 for $2_1^+$, 0.907 for $2_2^+$, 0.909 for $3_1^+$, 0.887 for $4_1^+$, and 0.936 for $4_6^+$
- Hamiltonian parameters: $\alpha = 167.4$ KeV, $a_1 = -4016.6$ KeV, $a_2 = 1205.0$ KeV, $a_3 = 522.2$ KeV, $t_1 = 10.5$ KeV, $t_2 = -0.99$ KeV, $t_3 = 25.9$ KeV
- $B(E2; 2_1^+ \rightarrow 0_1^+) = 217$ W.u. (calculated) versus 217(5) W.u. (experimental)
- $B(E2; 2_2^+ \rightarrow 0_1^+) = 5.66$ W.u. (calculated) versus 5.17(21) W.u. (experimental)
- Quadrupole moment $Q(2_1^+) = -2.19$ eb (calculated) versus -1.9(4) eb (experimental)
- Quadrupole moment $Q(2_2^+) = 2.19$ eb (calculated) versus 2.2(3) eb (experimental)
- Quadrupole moment $Q(4_1^+) = -2.79$ eb (calculated) versus -2.7(9) eb (experimental)
- Effective charge $e = 1.832$ (W.u.)$^{1/2}$

## Context
This work extends the SU3-IBM framework previously developed by Wang et al. (2020, 2022) to describe triaxial deformation in $^{166}$Er, a nucleus traditionally considered a textbook example of prolate axial symmetry as emphasized by Bohr in his Nobel lecture. The results support the recent reinterpretation by Otsuka et al. (2019, 2023, 2025) using Monte Carlo Shell Model calculations, which found $\gamma=8.2^{\circ}$ for $^{166}$Er, and are consistent with the Davydov-Filippov model extraction yielding $\gamma=9.1^{\circ}$. The paper enables further application of the SU3-IBM to other heavy deformed nuclei where triaxiality may be present, and predicts several unmeasured $B(E2)$ values awaiting experimental verification.
