# The hadronic equation of state of HESS J1731-347 from the relativistic mean-field model with tensor coupling

> A recent report has identified a central compact object (CCO) within the supernova remnant HESS J1731-347, with a mass and radius of $M=0.77^{+0.20}_{-0.17}M{\odot}$ and $R=10.4^{+0.86}_{-0.78}$ km, respectively. To investigate this light compact star, a density-dependent relativistic mean-field (DDRMF) model, specifically the DDVT model, has been employed. The DDVT model incorporates tensor couplings of vector mesons, which {can} successfully describe the properties of finite nuclei, such as charge radius, binding energy, and spin-orbit splitting. The introduction of tensor coupling reduces the influence of scalar mesons and generates a softer equation of state (EOS) in the outer core of the neutron star. Moreover, it has been found that the crust segment plays a crucial role in reproducing the mass-radius relation of HESS J1731-347, indicating a preference for a soft crust EOS. By manipulating the coupling strength of the isovector meson in the DDVT parameter set, a reasonable hadronic EOS has been obtained, satisfying the constraints from the gravitational-wave signal GW170817, the simultaneous mass-radius measurements from the NICER collaboration, and the properties of finite nuclei. Notably, the mass-radius relations derived from this hadronic EOS also accurately describe the observables of HESS J1731-347. Therefore, based on our estimation, the CCO in HESS J1731-347 may represent the lightest known neutron star.

## Key claim
The central compact object in HESS J1731-347 may represent the lightest known neutron star, but this conclusion depends on constructing a hadronic equation of state that combines a soft crust with a specific core EOS from the DDVT model, where the slope of symmetry energy in the crust and core must be tuned in opposite directions to reproduce the observed mass and radius.

## Method
The authors employed a density-dependent relativistic mean-field (DDRMF) model, specifically the DDVT parameterization that includes tensor couplings of vector mesons, to compute the equation of state for neutron star matter. They constructed combined EOSs by matching crust EOSs from the IUFSU model with varying symmetry energy slopes (L = 47, 60, 80, 110 MeV) to core EOSs from the DDVT model with adjusted L values (26, 30, 40, 42.35 MeV), then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation to obtain mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers
- HESS J1731-347 mass: M = 0.77^{+0.20}_{-0.17} solar masses; radius: R = 10.4^{+0.86}_{-0.78} km (from Doroshenko 2022)
- DDVT model at saturation density: rho_0 = 0.1536 fm^{-3}, E/A = -16.92 MeV, E_sym = 31.56 MeV, L = 42.35 MeV, M_N*/M_N = 0.667
- BSk19 set: L = 31.90 MeV, produces radius at 0.77 solar masses of R_{0.77} = 11.17 km, maximum mass M_max = 1.86 solar masses
- For combined EOS with crust L=47 MeV and core L=26 MeV: R_{0.77} = 11.50 km, R_{1.4} = 11.61 km, Lambda_{1.4} = 316
- For combined EOS with crust L=110 MeV and core L=26 MeV: R_{0.77} = 11.17 km (from figure, matching BSk19 value)
- GW170817 constraint: Lambda_{1.4} = 190^{+390}_{-120} (from Abbott 2018)

## Context
This work extends previous studies by Huang (2020) and Hu (2020) on the role of symmetry energy in neutron star structure, specifically addressing the unusually low-mass CCO reported by Doroshenko (2022). The authors show that the DDVT model with tensor coupling can simultaneously satisfy constraints from GW170817 (Abbott 2017), NICER measurements of PSR J0030+0451 (Riley 2019, Miller 2019) and PSR J0740+6620 (Riley 2021, Miller 2021), and finite nuclei properties, while also describing HESS J1731-347. The work enables future studies to distinguish between neutron star, hybrid star, and quark star interpretations of this object, as alternative explanations have been proposed by Clemente (2022) and Horvath (2023).
