# Radiative corrections to proton-proton fusion in pionless EFT

> We study the leading radiative correction to proton-proton fusion using the pionless effective field theory framework at leading order. We derive the relevant matrix elements and evaluate them using the method of regions. We benchmark the accuracy of our approximations by carrying out numerical computations of the full expressions. We show that the first order radiative corrections due to the exchange of a Coulomb photon between positron and proton-proton systems map onto the Sirlin function and the $\mathcal{O}(α)$ contribution from the Fermi function. We furthermore find that the nuclear structure dependent radiative correction omitted in the previous analysis by Kurylov {\it et al} gives an up to 0.2~\% correction to the pp fusion S-factor with its size ultimately depending on a two-nucleon counterterm that renormalizes the axial two-body current $L_{1A}$.

## Key claim

The leading nuclear-structure-dependent radiative correction to proton-proton fusion, omitted in the previous analysis by Kurylov et al., gives an up to 0.2% correction to the pp fusion S-factor, with its size ultimately depending on a two-nucleon counterterm that renormalizes the axial two-body current \(L_{1A}\).

## Method

The authors used pionless effective field theory at leading order to compute the leading radiative correction to proton-proton fusion. They derived the relevant matrix elements and evaluated them using the method of regions, which separates contributions from photons with different energy and momentum scales (ultrasoft, soft, and potential regions). They benchmarked the accuracy of their approximations by carrying out numerical computations of the full expressions and identified which contributions map onto the Sirlin function and the Fermi function, and which depend on nuclear structure.

## Key numbers

- The combined inner and outer radiative corrections shift the cross section by a factor of \(1.04141(25)\) when the renormalization scale is set to \(\mu = m_N\), and \(1.04156(25)\) when set to \(\mu = 2E_0\); the difference of \(1.5 \times 10^{-4}\) estimates missing \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha^2)\) contributions.
- The nuclear-structure-dependent correction \(\delta_{\rm NS}\) yields a per-mil level correction of approximately \(-0.0011\) to \(-0.0018\) (i.e., up to 0.2% correction to the S-factor), evaluated with a cutoff between \(m_\pi/2\) and \(3m_\pi/2\).
- The Sirlin function correction at threshold (\(E=0\)) with \(\mu = m_N\) gives a factor of \(1.0163\) when integrated over electron energy.
- The deuteron binding momentum is \(\gamma_t \sim 40\) MeV; the scale separation between external energy \(E \sim m_e\) and \(\gamma_t\) is exploited in the method of regions.

## Context

This work extends the previous analysis by Kurylov et al. (2002), which computed radiative corrections in the one-nucleon approximation and neglected photon exchanges with the second nucleon. The authors use the pionless EFT framework originally developed by Kong and Ravndal (2000) for pp fusion, and apply the method of regions from Beneke (1997). The calculation is similar to the EFT treatment of superallowed \(\beta\) decays in Cirigliano et al. (2024). The results enable improved uncertainty quantification for the pp fusion S-factor, with the largest remaining uncertainties coming from EFT truncation, parameter fitting, and the axial coupling \(g_A\).
