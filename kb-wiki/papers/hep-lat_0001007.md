# Lattice QCD Calculations of the Sigma Commutator

> As a direct source of information on chiral symmetry breaking within QCD, the sigma commutator is of considerable importance. With recent advances in the calculation of hadron masses within full QCD it is of interest to see whether the sigma commutator can be calculated directly from the dependence of the nucleon mass on the input quark mass. We show that provided the correct chiral behaviour of QCD is respected in the extrapolation to realistic quark masses one can indeed obtain a fairly reliable determination of the sigma commutator using present lattice data. Within two-flavour, dynamical-fermion QCD the value obtained lies in the range 45 to 55 MeV.

# Lattice QCD Calculations of the Sigma Commutator

## Key claim
Provided the correct chiral behaviour of QCD is respected in the extrapolation to realistic quark masses, the sigma commutator can be determined fairly reliably from present lattice data. Within two-flavour, dynamical-fermion QCD the value obtained lies in the range 45 to 55 MeV, but this result depends critically on including the correct leading non-analytic (LNA) chiral behaviour in the extrapolation function.

## Method
The authors used lattice QCD data for the nucleon mass as a function of pion mass (proportional to the average light quark mass) from the UKQCD and CP-PACS collaborations, with pion masses down to approximately 300 MeV. They applied the Feynman-Hellman theorem to extract the sigma commutator from the derivative of the nucleon mass with respect to the quark mass, using an extrapolation function that includes the correct LNA term proportional to m_pi^3 and the next-to-leading non-analytic behaviour from pion-nucleon and pion-Delta self-energy diagrams with a sharp cut-off or dipole form factor.

## Key numbers
- Sigma commutator from the dipole form factor fit: 47.2 ± 1.8 MeV (best estimate), with fit parameters (alpha, beta, Lambda_D) = (2.02, 0.398, 1.225) GeV.
- Sigma commutator from the sharp cut-off fit: 54.6 ± 2.0 MeV, with fit parameters (alpha, beta, Lambda) = (1.42, 0.564, 0.661) GeV.
- Sigma commutator from the unconstrained cubic fit (alpha + beta m_pi^2 + gamma m_pi^3): 29.7 MeV, with parameters (tilde-alpha, tilde-beta, tilde-gamma) = (0.912, 1.69, -0.761) in appropriate powers of GeV.
- The model-independent LNA coefficient gamma^{LNA} = -5.60 GeV^{-2}, while the unconstrained cubic fit gave tilde-gamma = -0.761 GeV^{-2}, almost an order of magnitude smaller.
- Omitting the lowest data point (near m_pi^2 ~ 0.1 GeV^2) from the sharp cut-off fit gave sigma_N = 65.8 MeV.
- Scaling the CP-PACS data up by 15% and UKQCD data by 5% shifted the sharp cut-off result from 54.6 ± 2.0 to 55.2 ± 2.1 MeV.

## Context
This work extends earlier attempts to extract the sigma commutator from lattice QCD, which had produced values as low as 15-25 MeV in quenched calculations (Cabasino, 1995) and 40-60 MeV in quenched calculations of the scalar matrix element (Liu, 1995; Fukugita, 1995). The only previous two-flavour dynamical-fermion calculation, by the SESAM collaboration, obtained 18 ± 5 MeV through direct calculation of the scalar matrix element, but the authors argue that approach suffers from renormalization scheme dependence. The present work enables a more reliable determination by using the Feynman-Hellman theorem with renormalization-group-invariant quantities and by incorporating the correct chiral behaviour in the extrapolation, following the approach of Leinweber (1999). The results suggest that future three-flavour full QCD calculations at masses closer to physical values are urgently needed.
