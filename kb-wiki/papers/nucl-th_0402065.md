# A proposed reaction channel for the synthesis of the superheavy nucleus Z = 109

> We apply a statistical-evaporation model (HIVAP) to calculate the cross sections of superheavy elements, mainly about actinide targets and compare with some available experimental data. A reaction channel $^{30}$Si + $^{243}$Am is proposed for the synthesis of the element Z = 109 and the cross section is estimated.

# A proposed reaction channel for the synthesis of the superheavy nucleus Z = 109

## Key claim
The reaction channel 30Si + 243Am is proposed for synthesizing new isotopes of element 109 (Mt), with estimated cross sections of about 21 pb for the 3n channel and 13 pb for the 4n channel. The authors qualify that their HIVAP statistical-evaporation model calculations reproduce known experimental data within a factor of about 5, so the predictions should be considered reasonable but not precise.

## Method
The authors used the HIVAP statistical-evaporation model, which treats superheavy element synthesis as a two-stage process: complete fusion of projectile and target to form a compound nucleus, followed by de-excitation through fission or emission of light particles and gamma rays. The fusion probability was calculated using a quasi-Gaussian barrier distribution with WKB transmission coefficients, and the survival probability was determined from level densities of compound and equilibrium configurations using the Fermi gas model with shell corrections and Ignatyuk's energy-dependent level density parameter. The model was first benchmarked against experimental data for reactions producing elements Z = 103-107, then applied to predict cross sections for element 109.

## Key numbers
- For the proposed channel 30Si + 243Am → 270Mt + 3n at Elab = 151 MeV, the calculated cross section is 21.6 pb.
- For the proposed channel 30Si + 243Am → 269Mt + 4n at Elab = 161.3 MeV, the calculated cross section is 13 pb.
- The maximum error of the HIVAP calculations compared with experimental data for Z = 103-107 reactions is within a factor of 5.
- For the benchmark reaction 248Cm(18O,5n)261Rf, the HIVAP calculation gives 13 nb at Elab = 97 MeV, compared to an experimental value of 5 nb.
- For the benchmark reaction 248Cm(18O,5n)261Rf, the HIVAP calculation gives 11.3 nb at Elab = 94 MeV, compared to an experimental value of 13 ± 3 nb.
- For the benchmark reaction 248Cm(18O,5n)261Rf, the HIVAP calculation gives 15 nb at Elab = 99 MeV, compared to an experimental value of 8 ± 2 nb.

## Context
This work extends the application of the HIVAP statistical-evaporation model, originally developed by Reisdorf and collaborators, to predict cross sections for superheavy element synthesis. The authors benchmarked against experimental data from Dubna and other laboratories for reactions producing elements Z = 103-107, including data from Oganessian (various years) and other groups. The proposed 30Si + 243Am channel offers an alternative to the cold fusion route used at GSI Darmstadt, where element 109 was first produced via 58Fe + 209Bi. The work aims to guide experimental planning at the Heavy Ion Research Facility in Lanzhou (HIRFL), where synthesizing elements above Z = 112 is currently difficult but element 109 may be feasible.
