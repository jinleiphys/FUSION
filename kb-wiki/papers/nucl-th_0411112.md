# $Δ$-scaling and heat capacity in relativistic ion collisions

> The $Δ$-scaling method has been applied to the total multiplicity distribution of the relativistic ion collisions of p+p, C+C and Pb+Pb which were simulated by a Monte Carlo package, LUCIAE 3.0. It is found that the $Δ$-scaling parameter decreases with the increasing of the system size. Moreover, the heat capacities of different mesons and baryons have been extracted from the event-by-event temperature fluctuation in the region of low transverse mass and they show the dropping trend with the increasing of impact parameter.

# Δ-scaling and heat capacity in relativistic ion collisions

## Key claim
The Δ-scaling parameter extracted from multiplicity distributions in LUCIAE 3.0 simulations decreases with increasing system size (from p+p to Pb+Pb), and the normalized heat capacity per hadron extracted from event-by-event temperature fluctuations decreases with increasing incident energy and impact parameter, but these results are obtained from a string-hadronic model that does not include partonic effects, which become important at RHIC energies.

## Method
The authors used the LUCIAE 3.0 Monte Carlo model (an extension of FRITIOF) to simulate p+p, C+C, and Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies from 20 to 200 AGeV, with head-on (b=0 fm) geometry. They applied Δ-scaling to the total charged-particle multiplicity distributions by fitting the power-law relation between the RMS width and the mean multiplicity. Heat capacities were extracted from event-by-event temperature distributions of π⁺, k⁺, p, and Λ in the low transverse mass region (m_T − m_0 < 0.5 GeV) using a Gaussian fluctuation formula.

## Key numbers
- Δ-scaling parameter values: Δ = 1.35 for p+p, Δ = 1.00 for C+C, Δ = 0.80 for Pb+Pb, determined from power-law fits to m_RMS vs. ⟨m⟩ over beam energies from 20 AGeV to 200 AGeV in 20 AGeV steps.
- The normalized heat capacity C_v/N for various hadrons (π⁺, k⁺, p, Λ) in head-on Pb+Pb collisions decreases with increasing incident energy and tends toward saturation; the right panel of Figure 3 shows C_v/N for π⁺ decreasing with increasing impact parameter at 160 AGeV Pb+Pb collisions.

## Context
This work extends the Δ-scaling method, previously applied to low-intermediate energy heavy-ion collisions (Ma, 2004; scaling reference), to relativistic energies using the LUCIAE model. The heat capacity extraction from temperature fluctuations follows the formalism of Landau and Shuryak. The decreasing heat capacity with centrality is noted to be similar to the centrality dependence of P_T fluctuation and balance function observed in STAR data (Westfall, 2004). The results are limited by the absence of partonic effects in the string-hadronic LUCIAE model, which the authors identify as needing investigation at RHIC energies.
