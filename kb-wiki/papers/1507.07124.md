# Further study of the $NΩ$ dibaryon within constituent quark models

> Inspired by the discovery of the dibaryon $d^{*}$ and the experimental search of $NΩ$ dibaryon with the STAR data, we study the strange dibaryon $NΩ$ further in the framework of quark delocalization color screening model and chiral quark model. We have shown $NΩ$ is a narrow resonance in $ΛΞ$ D-wave scattering before. However, the $Λ$-$Ξ$ scattering data analysis is quite complicated. Here we calculate the low-energy $NΩ$ scattering phase shifts, scattering length, effective range and binding energy to provide another approach of STAR data analysis. Our results show there exists an $NΩ$ "bound" state, which can be observed by the $N$-$Ω$ correlation analysis with RHIC and LHC data, or by the new developed automatic scanning system at J-PARC. Besides, we also find that the hidden color channel-coupling is important for the $NΩ$ system to develop intermediate-range attraction.

# Further study of the $N\Omega$ dibaryon within constituent quark models

## Key claim

The authors claim that an $N\Omega$ dibaryon state exists as a bound state or narrow resonance across multiple constituent quark models, but only when hidden-color channel coupling is included; in the single-channel approximation the state is unbound in all models except one that uses a universal $\sigma$ meson exchange between any quark pair.

## Method

The authors performed resonating group method (RGM) calculations of the $N\Omega$ system with quantum numbers $S=-3$, $I=1/2$, $J=2$ using four variants of constituent quark models: the quark delocalization color screening model (QDCSM) and three chiral quark models (SU(2)ChQM1 with universal $\sigma$ exchange, SU(2)ChQM2 with $\sigma$ restricted to $u/d$ pairs only, and SU(3)ChQM with full scalar octet exchange). They computed binding energies from a generalized eigenvalue equation for the coupled-channel system including up to 16 channels (color-singlet and hidden-color), and extracted low-energy scattering phase shifts, scattering length, and effective range using the Kohn-Hulthen-Kato variational method.

## Key numbers

* QDCSM with 5 color-singlet channels coupling: binding energy $B_{5cc} = -6.4$ MeV (unbound in single channel)
* SU(2)ChQM1 with 16 channels coupling: binding energy $B_{16cc} = -119.5$ MeV
* SU(2)ChQM2 with 16 channels coupling: binding energy $B_{16cc} = -38.3$ MeV
* SU(3)ChQM with 16 channels coupling: binding energy $B_{16cc} = -13.7$ MeV
* QDCSM scattering length $a_0 = 2.8007$ fm, effective range $r_0 = 0.5770$ fm, binding energy $B' = -5.2$ MeV
* SU(2)ChQM1 scattering length $a_0 = 0.8103$ fm, effective range $r_0 = 0.3609$ fm, binding energy $B' = -110.3$ MeV
* SU(2)ChQM2 scattering length $a_0 = 1.3808$ fm, effective range $r_0 = 0.6018$ fm, binding energy $B' = -37.3$ MeV
* SU(3)ChQM scattering length $a_0 = 1.9870$ fm, effective range $r_0 = 0.7064$ fm, binding energy $B' = -13.7$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of the $N\Omega$ system as a narrow resonance in $\Lambda\Xi$ D-wave scattering (Huang, Ping, Wang, earlier papers) and responds to lattice QCD calculations by the HAL QCD Collaboration (2015) which found a bound $N\Omega$ at pion mass 875 MeV. The study also builds on the experimental confirmation of the $d^*$ dibaryon by the WASA-at-COSY collaboration and on earlier predictions by Goldman et al. (1987) in a relativistic quark model. The results provide scattering parameters that can be used to search for the $N\Omega$ dibaryon via $N$-$\Omega$ correlation analysis in RHIC and LHC data, or with the automatic scanning system at J-PARC.
