# Effects of initial state fluctuations on jet energy loss

> The effect of initial state fluctuations on jet energy loss in relativistic heavy-ion collisions is studied in a 2+1 dimension ideal hydrodynamic model. Within the next-to-leading order perturbative QCD description of hard scatterings, we find that a jet loses slightly more energy in the expanding quark-gluon plasma if the latter is described by the hydrodynamic evolution with fluctuating initial conditions compared to the case with smooth initial conditions. A detailed analysis indicates that this is mainly due to the positive correlation between the fluctuation in the production probability of parton jets from initial nucleon-nucleon hard collisions and the fluctuation in the medium density along the path traversed by the jet. This effect is larger in non-central than in central relativistic heavy ion collisions and also for jet energy loss that has a linear than a quadratic dependence on its path length in the medium.

## Key claim

Including initial state fluctuations in a 2+1D ideal hydrodynamic model leads to slightly *more* jet energy loss than using smooth initial conditions, contrary to a prior 1+1D study (Rodriguez 2010) that found reduced energy loss. This difference arises because the transverse expansion in 2+1D causes most energy loss to occur at early times when the correlation between jet production probability and medium density is positive.

## Method

The authors used a 2+1D boost-invariant ideal hydrodynamic model with fluctuating initial conditions generated from Monte Carlo Glauber sampling of participant and binary collision densities, using a Gaussian smearing parameter σ = 0.4 fm or 0.8 fm. Jet energy loss was calculated within next-to-leading order (NLO) perturbative QCD, using the CTEQ6M parton distributions and AKK08 fragmentation functions, with the energy loss parameterized to have linear (α=0), quadratic (α=1), or cubic (α=2) path-length dependence. The nuclear modification factor R_AA was computed by averaging over many fluctuating events and compared to the smooth-initial-condition case.

## Key numbers

- In 0-5% central Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV, including initial fluctuations with σ = 0.4 fm decreases R_AA by 4% compared to smooth initial conditions.
- In 40-50% central Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV, including initial fluctuations with σ = 0.4 fm decreases R_AA by 8% compared to smooth initial conditions.
- For linear path-length dependence (α=0) in central collisions with σ = 0.4 fm, the suppression factor decreases by 8% due to fluctuations.
- For quadratic path-length dependence (α=1) in central collisions with σ = 0.4 fm, the suppression factor decreases by 4% due to fluctuations.
- The smearing parameter σ was set to 0.4 fm and 0.8 fm; the initial thermalization time τ_0 = 0.6 fm/c.
- The critical temperature for the phase transition was T_c = 170 MeV; chemical freeze-out temperature was T = 160 MeV.
- The nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross section used was σ_in = 42 mb.

## Context

This work extends the study of initial-state fluctuation effects on jet quenching from 1+1D Bjorken hydrodynamics (Rodriguez 2010) to 2+1D hydrodynamics that includes transverse expansion. It contradicts the earlier finding of reduced energy loss with fluctuations, showing instead a slight enhancement due to the positive early-time correlation between jet production sites and medium density. The results enable more realistic event-by-event studies of jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions and clarify how the competition between path-length dependence and medium density evolution determines the net fluctuation effect.
