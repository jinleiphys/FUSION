# The Chiral Critical Point in 3-Flavour QCD

> We determine the second order endpoint of the line of first order phase transitions, which occur in the light quark mass regime of 3-flavour QCD at finite temperature, and analyze universal properties of this chiral critical point. A detailed analysis of Binder cumulants and the joint probability distributions of energy like and ordering-field like observables confirms that the chiral critical point belongs to the universality class of the three dimensional Ising model. From a calculation with improved gauge and staggered fermion actions we estimate that the transition is first order for pseudo-scalar meson masses less than about 200 MeV.

## Key claim

The chiral critical point in 3-flavour QCD belongs to the universality class of the three-dimensional Ising model, but the analysis relies on constructing appropriate mixed operators from the gauge action and chiral condensate because neither the chiral condensate nor the Polyakov loop alone serves as the order parameter for the effective Z(2) symmetry at this critical point.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with standard (unimproved) Wilson gauge and staggered fermion actions on lattices of size N_sigma^3 x 4 with N_sigma = 8, 12, and 16, using the hybrid-R algorithm. They located the chiral critical point by analyzing Binder cumulants constructed from linear combinations of the chiral condensate and gauge action, and determined mixing parameters r and s by demanding that the ordering-field like operator be independent of the energy-like operator at the critical point. Additional calculations with improved gauge and staggered fermion actions (p4-action) on 12^3 x 4 and 16^3 x 4 lattices provided a physical scale estimate.

## Key numbers

- Critical bare quark mass from unimproved actions: m_bar = 0.0331(12) with Binder cumulant B_4 = 1.639(24), consistent with the 3-d Ising model value of 1.604
- Mixing parameters determined: r = 0.550(7) and s = 0.430(23)
- Pseudo-scalar meson mass at the chiral critical point from unimproved actions: m_ps approximately 290 MeV
- Critical bare quark mass from improved (p4) actions: m_bar = 0.0075(25)
- Pseudo-scalar meson mass at the chiral critical point from improved actions: m_ps approximately 192(25) MeV
- Ratio of critical exponents gamma/nu = 1.8(5) from finite size scaling of susceptibilities at m=0.035

## Context

This work extends earlier conjectures by Pisarski and Wilczek and numerical studies by Gavai (1987), Kogut (1988), and Brown (1990) that the chiral phase transition is first order for three massless quark flavours, by precisely locating the second-order endpoint where the first-order line terminates. The analysis uses methods developed for the liquid-gas phase transition by Wilding and for the electroweak critical endpoint, and confirms the earlier estimate by Aoki (1999) for the endpoint location on N_tau=4 lattices. The results enable future determination of the chiral critical line in the plane of up/down and strange quark masses, with a leading-order estimate suggesting that for m_u,d = 0.025 the critical strange quark mass is approximately 0.049.
