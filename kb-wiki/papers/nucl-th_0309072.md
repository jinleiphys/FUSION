# Neutrino Bremsstrahlung in Neutron Matter from Effective Nuclear Interactions

> We revisit the emissivity from neutrino pair bremsstrahlung in neutron-neutron scattering, nn -> nn nu nubar, which was calculated from the one-pion exchange potential including correlation effects by Friman and Maxwell. Starting from the free-space low-momentum nucleon-nucleon interaction V_{low k}, we include tensor, spin-orbit and second-order medium-induced non-central contributions to the scattering amplitude in neutron matter. We find that the screening of the nucleon-nucleon interaction reduces the emissivity from neutrino bremsstrahlung for densities below nuclear matter density. We discuss the implications of medium modifications for the cooling of neutron stars via neutrino emission, taking into account recent results for the polarization effects on neutron superfluidity.

# Neutrino Bremsstrahlung in Neutron Matter from Effective Nuclear Interactions

## Key claim

The emissivity from neutrino pair bremsstrahlung in neutron-neutron scattering is reduced when medium modifications, specifically particle-hole screening of the tensor and spin-orbit forces, are included, but the authors caution that a second-order calculation cannot give final results and that higher orders may somewhat decrease the second-order results, though this argument is not straightforward for non-central interactions.

## Method

The authors start from the free-space low-momentum nucleon-nucleon interaction V_low_k and compute the neutron-neutron scattering amplitude on the Fermi surface to second order, including tensor, spin-orbit, and medium-induced non-central contributions. They then evaluate the emissivity from neutrino pair bremsstrahlung using the formalism of Friman and Maxwell (1985), performing the phase-space integrals numerically over the Fermi surface with the full spin-dependent amplitude, including both particle-hole and particle-particle channels.

## Key numbers

- At nuclear matter density (k_F = 1.7 fm^{-1}), the full second-order result reduces the emissivity by a multiplicative factor of 0.64 relative to the tree-level V_low_k result, or a factor of 0.5 relative to the direct one-pion exchange estimate.
- At sub-nuclear densities (one fifth of nuclear matter density), the reduction factor is 0.2.
- The effective mass varies from m*_n/m_n = 0.95 at k_F = 1.0 fm^{-1} to m*_n/m_n = 0.78 at k_F = 2.0 fm^{-1}.
- The emissivity formula is given as epsilon_nn = 0.781 * T_9^8 * N_nu * (m*_n/m_n)^2 * (1.7 fm^{-1} / k_F) * <A^2_nn> erg cm^{-3} s^{-1}, where T_9 is temperature in units of 10^9 K and N_nu = 3 neutrino flavors.
- The maximum ^1S_0 pairing gap from the RG calculation is reduced to 0.8 MeV at k_F ≈ 0.8 fm^{-1}, with the gap disappearing at k_F ≈ 1.5 fm^{-1} (ρ ≈ 2/3 ρ_0), corresponding to a maximum critical temperature of T_c = 5.3 × 10^9 K.
- The ^3P_2 pairing gap is suppressed from Δ ≈ 0.3 MeV (free-space V_low_k) to gaps on the level of few keV at nuclear matter density, corresponding to critical temperatures T_c = 10^{6.8–7.8} K.

## Context

This work extends the benchmark calculation of Friman and Maxwell (1985), who computed the emissivity from one-pion exchange including correlation effects, by starting from the model-independent low-momentum interaction V_low_k and including particle-hole screening effects. It also builds on the second-order results for the neutron-neutron scattering amplitude in neutron matter from Schwenk et al. (2003), which found substantial reduction of the tensor force and significant medium-induced non-central contributions. The results enable more realistic neutron star cooling simulations that incorporate medium modifications of the nuclear force, and the authors note that revised estimates of np and pp bremsstrahlung and modified Urca rates will be reported in a subsequent publication.
