# Study of the $f_2(1270)$, $f'_2(1525)$, $\bar{K}^*_{2}(1430)$, $f_0(1370)$ and $f_0(1710)$ production from $ψ(nS)$ and $Υ(nS)$ decays

> Based on previous studies that support the important role of the $f_2(1270)$, $f'_2(1525)$, and $\bar{K}^{*}_{2}(1430)$ resonances in the $J/ψ[ψ(2S)] \to ϕ(ω) VV$ decays, we make an analysis of the analogous decays of $Υ(1S)$ and $Υ(2S)$, taking into account recent experimental data. In addition, we study the $J/ψ$ and $ψ(2S)$ radiative decays and we also made predictions for the radiative decay of $Υ(1S)$ and $Υ(2S)$ into $γf_2(1270)$, $γf'_2(1525)$, $γf_0(1370)$ and $γf_0(1710)$, comparing with the recent results of a CLEO experiment. We can compare our results for ratios of decay rates with eight experimental ratios and find agreement in all but one case, where experimental problems are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper tests the hypothesis that the tensor resonances \(f_2(1270)\), \(f'_2(1525)\), \(\bar{K}^{*}_{2}(1430)\) and the scalar resonances \(f_0(1370)\), \(f_0(1710)\) are dynamically generated states from vector-vector interactions, by comparing theoretical predictions for their production in \(\psi(nS)\) and \(\Upsilon(nS)\) decays with recent experimental data; the authors find agreement in seven out of eight experimental ratios, with the single discrepancy attributed to a problematic experimental datum for \(\psi(2S) \to \gamma f'_2(1525)\).

## Method

The authors extend a previously developed formalism based on the local hidden gauge approach for vector-vector interactions, where the resonances emerge as quasimolecular states from coupled-channel unitarization. For hadronic decays, they compute transition amplitudes for \(\Upsilon(1S)\) and \(\Upsilon(2S)\) decaying into \(\omega(\phi, K^{*0})\) plus a resonance, using SU(3) singlet properties of the \(b\bar{b}\) states and a parameter \(\nu = T^{(1,1)}/T^{(8,8)}\) to account for OZI violation. For radiative decays, they assume the photon is radiated from the initial \(c\bar{c}\) or \(b\bar{b}\) state (dominant over photon emission from final vector mesons), and evaluate the same ratios of decay widths for \(J/\psi\), \(\psi(2S)\), \(\Upsilon(1S)\), and \(\Upsilon(2S)\) into \(\gamma f_2(1270)\), \(\gamma f'_2(1525)\), \(\gamma f_0(1370)\), and \(\gamma f_0(1710)\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Dai, Xie, and Oset (2013) on \(\psi(2S)\) decays and by Hanhart (2012) on \(J/\psi\) radiative decays, applying the same vector-vector molecular framework to \(\Upsilon(1S)\) and \(\Upsilon(2S)\) decays and comparing with new CLEO data from Seth (2014). The results provide further support for the interpretation of \(f_2(1270)\), \(f'_2(1525)\), \(\bar{K}^{*}_{2}(1430)\), \(f_0(1370)\), and \(f_0(1710)\) as dynamically generated states from vector-vector interactions, as originally proposed by Geng and Oset (2008, 2009) and Molina (2008). The single discrepancy in the \(\psi(2S) \to \gamma f'_2(1525)\) channel calls for a reanalysis of that experimental datum.
