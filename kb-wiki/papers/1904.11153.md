# Trinity of Strangeon Matter

> Strangeon is proposed to be the constituent of bulk strong matter, as an analogy of nucleon for an atomic nucleus. The nature of both nucleon matter (2 quark flavors, u and d) and strangeon matter (3 flavors, u, d and s) is controlled by the strong-force, but the baryon number of the former is much smaller than that of the latter, to be separated by a critical number of $A_{\rm c}\sim 10^9$. While micro nucleon matter (i.e., nuclei) is focused by nuclear physicists, astrophysical/macro strangeon matter could be manifested in the form of compact stars (strangeon star), cosmic rays (strangeon cosmic ray), and even dark matter (strangeon dark matter). This trinity of strangeon matter is explained, that may impact dramatically on today's physics.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that bulk strong matter with baryon number greater than a critical value of \(A_{\rm c}\sim 10^9\) should be composed of 3-flavored "strangeon" units (localized quark clusters) rather than 2-flavored nucleons, based on a principle of quark flavor maximization and the need to avoid energetic electrons in macroscopic systems.

## Method

The author develops a theoretical framework comparing 2-flavored nucleon matter (normal nuclei) with 3-flavored strangeon matter, using the electron Compton wavelength \(\lambda_{\rm c} = h/(m_{\rm e}c)\) to estimate the critical baryon number separating micro- from macro-strong matter. A Lennard-Jones 6-12 potential is applied to model inter-strangeon interactions, and the equation of state is derived for condensed strangeon matter. The paper then explores three observational manifestations: strangeon stars/planets, strangeon cosmic rays, and strangeon dark matter.

## Key numbers

- Critical baryon number separating micro- and macro-strong matter: \(A_{\rm c}\sim 10^9\), estimated from \(\lambda_{\rm c}^3/{\rm fm}^3\) where \(\lambda_{\rm c} = h/(m_{\rm e}c)=2.4\times 10^3\) fm
- Strangeon star maximum mass could reach \(M_{\rm max}\sim 3M_\odot\), with tidal deformability of a \(1.4M_\odot\) star as low as \(\Lambda\sim 200\)
- Strangeon cosmic ray nugget with baryon number \(A\sim 10^{10}\) has rest mass \(\sim 10^{19}\) eV, with deposit energy during air shower of order \(10^{18\sim 20}\) eV
- Atmospheric depth for strangeon cosmic ray: \(X\sim (400~{\rm g/cm}^2)\cdot A_{10}^{-2/3}\) where \(A_{10}=A/10^{10}\)
- Self-interaction cross section for strangeon dark matter: \((\sigma/m)_{\rm strangeon~dark~matter}\sim (3~{\rm cm}^2/{\rm g})\cdot T_5 n_1 A_{30}^{-1}\) where \(T=T_5\times 10^5\) K and \(n=n_1\times 1~{\rm cm}^{-3}\)
- Moon-quake rate from strangeon dark matter: \(\sim 6/A_{30}\) yr\(^{-1}\) for dynamical velocity \(\sim 200\) km/s
- Quark masses from lattice gauge simulations: \(m_{\rm u}=2.15\) MeV, \(m_{\rm d}=4.70\) MeV, \(m_{\rm s}=93.8\) MeV

## Context

This work extends the strange quark matter conjecture of Bodmer (1971) and Witten (1984) by proposing that quarks in bulk strong matter are localized in strangeon clusters rather than free, due to non-perturbative QCD effects at energy scales below \(\Lambda_\chi\sim 1\) GeV. The paper contrasts with conventional neutron star models (Landau, 1932) and offers an alternative to the WIMP paradigm for dark matter, suggesting strangeon dark matter could explain the comparable ratio of dark to baryonic matter (\(\sim 5:1\)) and the lithium problem in big bang nucleosynthesis. The framework enables predictions for gravitational wave observations (GW 170817), kilonova signatures, and future detection experiments including LHAASO and lunar seismology.
