# Unified approach to structure factors and neutrino processes in nucleon matter

> We present a unified approach to neutrino processes in nucleon matter based on Landau's theory of Fermi liquids that includes one- and two-quasiparticle-quasihole pair states as well as mean-field effects. We show how rates of neutrino processes involving two nucleons may be calculated in terms of the collision integral in the Landau transport equation for quasiparticles. Using a relaxation time approximation, we solve the transport equation for density and spin-density fluctuations and derive a general form for the response functions. We apply our approach to neutral-current processes in neutron matter, where the spin response function is crucial for calculations of neutrino elastic and inelastic scattering, neutrino-pair bremsstrahlung and absorption from strongly-interacting nucleons. We calculate the relaxation rates using modern nuclear interactions and including many-body contributions, and find that rates of neutrino processes are reduced compared with estimates based on the one-pion exchange interaction, which is used in current simulations of core-collapse supernovae.

## Key claim

The paper presents a unified framework for calculating neutrino processes in nucleon matter that includes both one- and two-quasiparticle-quasihole pair states, and finds that rates of neutrino processes are reduced compared with estimates based on the one-pion exchange interaction used in current simulations of core-collapse supernovae.

## Method

The authors use Landau's theory of normal Fermi liquids to derive response functions for density and spin-density fluctuations, solving the quasiparticle transport equation in a relaxation time approximation. They calculate the relaxation rates using modern nuclear interactions beyond one-pion exchange, including many-body contributions, and apply the formalism to neutral-current processes in neutron matter such as neutrino elastic and inelastic scattering, neutrino-pair bremsstrahlung, and absorption.

## Context

This work extends the diagrammatic perturbation theory approach of Lykasov, Pethick, and Schwenk (2005) by using the quasiparticle transport equation to include two-particle-hole pair states and mean-field effects consistently. It contrasts with earlier calculations by Sawyer (1975), Iwamoto and Pethick (1982), and Prakash et al. (1988) that considered only single particle-hole pair states, and with the one-pion exchange model used by Friman and Maxwell (1979) and Raffelt et al. (1996) for supernova simulations. The results enable improved calculations of neutrino mean free paths, energy loss, and energy transfer in core-collapse supernovae and neutron stars, and can be extended to mixtures of neutrons and protons, charged-current reactions, and superfluid phases.
