# Magnetic field effect on pion superfluid

> Magnetic field effect on pion superfluid phase transition is investigated in frame of a Pauli-Villars regularized NJL model. Instead of directly dealing with charged pion condensate, we apply the Goldstone's theorem (massless Goldstone boson $π^+$) to determine the onset of pion superfluid phase, and obtain the phase diagram in magnetic field, temperature, isospin and baryon chemical potential space. At weak magnetic field, it is analytically proved that the critical isospin chemical potential of pion superfluid phase transition is equal to the mass of $π^+$ meson in magnetic field. The pion superfluid phase is retarded to higher isospin chemical potential, and can survive at higher temperature and higher baryon chemical potential under external magnetic field.

## Key claim

The paper claims that an external magnetic field delays the onset of pion superfluidity to higher isospin chemical potential, and that at weak magnetic field, zero temperature, and zero baryon chemical potential, the critical isospin chemical potential for the phase transition is exactly equal to the mass of the \(\pi^+\) meson in that magnetic field, a result that holds analytically only below a threshold of \(eB = 4.75 m_\pi^2\).

## Method

The author uses a two-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model regularized with the Pauli–Villars scheme, incorporating an external magnetic field via the covariant derivative. Instead of directly computing the charged pion condensate, the onset of the pion superfluid phase is determined by the Goldstone theorem: the critical point is identified when the \(\pi^+\) meson becomes massless. The \(\pi^+\) propagator is constructed in the random phase approximation using quark bubble summations, accounting for the breaking of translational invariance by the magnetic field, and the pole mass is solved numerically.

## Key numbers

- The NJL model parameters are fixed by fitting vacuum observables: current quark mass \(m_0 = 5\) MeV, coupling constant \(G = 3.44\) GeV\(^{-2}\), and Pauli–Villars mass parameter \(\Lambda = 1127\) MeV, yielding chiral condensate \(\langle\bar\psi\psi\rangle = -(250\ \text{MeV})^3\), pion mass \(m_\pi = 134\) MeV, and pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV.
- At \(T = \mu_B = 0\), the critical isospin chemical potential \(\mu_I^{c\pi}\) equals the \(\pi^+\) mass in the magnetic field for \(eB < 4.75 m_\pi^2\); at \(eB = 4.75 m_\pi^2\), the pion superfluid and chiral restoration phase transitions coincide.
- For \(eB/m_\pi^2 = 5\), the pion superfluid phase boundary in the \(\mu_I\)–\(T\) plane is shifted to higher isospin chemical potential and survives to higher temperature compared to the \(eB = 0\) case.
- For \(eB/m_\pi^2 = 5\), the pion superfluid phase in the \(\mu_I\)–\(\mu_B\) plane at \(T=0\) is shifted to higher isospin chemical potential and survives to higher baryon chemical potential compared to the \(eB = 0\) case.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the pion superfluid phase transition using effective models such as the NJL model, linear sigma model, and chiral perturbation theory (e.g., model1–model23), and addresses the open question of magnetic field effects that were difficult to treat due to the charged condensate breaking translational invariance. It is qualitatively consistent with lattice QCD results (lqcdb1) that found the onset of pion condensate shifts to larger isospin chemical potential under magnetic fields, and with the enhancement of the charged pion mass with growing magnetic fields (lqcdb2). The method avoids the complexity of directly handling the charged condensate, enabling a phase diagram in magnetic field, temperature, isospin, and baryon chemical potential space, and opens the door to future studies of competition between color superconductor and pion superfluid phases.
