# Maximum mass of compact stars from gravitational wave events with finite-temperature equations of state

> We conjecture and verify a set of universal relations between global parameters of hot and fast-rotating compact stars, including a relation connecting the masses of the mass-shedding (Kepler) and static configurations. We apply these relations to the GW170817 event by adopting the scenario in which a hypermassive compact star remnant formed in a merger evolves into a supramassive compact star that collapses into a black hole once the stability line for such stars is crossed. We deduce an upper limit on the maximum mass of static, cold neutron stars $ 2.15^{+0.10}_{-0.07}\le M^\star_{\mathrm{TOV}} \le 2.24^{+0.12}_{-0.10} $ for the typical range of entropy per baryon $2 \le S/A \le 3$ and electron fraction $Y_e = 0.1$ characterizing the hot hypermassive star. Our result implies that accounting for the finite temperature of the merger remnant relaxes previously derived constraints on the value of the maximum mass of a cold, static compact star.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that universal relations connecting the maximum mass of Keplerian (mass-shedding) and static compact stars remain valid for hot, rapidly rotating configurations when the entropy per baryon and electron fraction are held constant, but the proportionality coefficients depend on these thermodynamic conditions. Applying these relations to the GW170817 event yields a relaxed upper limit on the maximum mass of cold, static neutron stars, specifically \( 2.15^{+0.10}_{-0.07} \le M^\star_{\mathrm{TOV}} \le 2.24^{+0.12}_{-0.10} \) for entropy per baryon \(2 \le S/A \le 3\) and electron fraction \(Y_e = 0.1\), which is less restrictive than previous constraints that ignored finite-temperature effects.

## Method

The authors used the LORENE library to solve the combined Einstein and equilibrium equations for stationary, axisymmetric, uniformly rotating relativistic stars with finite-temperature equations of state. They computed equilibrium configurations for eleven different equation-of-state models (including both purely nucleonic models and models with hyperons) under fixed entropy per baryon (\(S/A = 2\) or \(3\)) and fixed electron fraction (\(Y_e = 0.1\) or \(0.4\)), then derived linear fits for the relations between the maximum gravitational mass, radius, and Kepler frequency of rotating and static configurations. These universal relations were then applied to the GW170817 merger scenario, assuming a hypermassive remnant evolved into a supramassive star that collapsed to a black hole upon crossing the stability line.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of cold, static neutron stars constrained to \( 2.15^{+0.10}_{-0.07} \le M^\star_{\mathrm{TOV}} \le 2.24^{+0.12}_{-0.10} \) for entropy per baryon \(S/A = 2\) to \(3\) and electron fraction \(Y_e = 0.1\).
- Fitting parameter \(C^\star_M\) (ratio of maximum Keplerian mass to maximum static mass) for cold, beta-equilibrated stars: \(1.2187 \pm 0.0064\); for hot stars with \(S/A=2, Y_e=0.1\): \(1.1617 \pm 0.0032\); for \(S/A=3, Y_e=0.4\): \(1.0877 \pm 0.0023\).
- Fitting parameter \(C^\star_R\) (ratio of equatorial radius at Kepler limit to static radius) for cold stars: \(1.3587 \pm 0.0104\); for hot stars with \(S/A=2, Y_e=0.1\): \(1.3459 \pm 0.0051\).
- Fitting parameter \(C^\star_f\) (relating Kepler frequency to static compactness) for cold stars: \(1259.63 \pm 9.72\) (in units of Hz); for hot stars with \(S/A=2, Y_e=0.1\): \(1237.68 \pm 5.47\) Hz.
- Maximum gravitational masses of cold static stars for individual EoS models range from \(1.95 M_\odot\) (HS(IUF)) to \(2.75 M_\odot\) (NL3-omega rho).
- Maximum baryonic masses for cold stars range from \(2.27 M_\odot\) (HS(IUF)) to \(3.39 M_\odot\) (NL3-omega rho).

## Context

This work extends the study of universal relations for hot compact stars from non-rotating or slowly rotating configurations (Raduta, 2020) to rapidly rotating stars at the Kepler limit. It directly addresses the inference of the maximum neutron star mass from the GW170817 event, previously studied by Margalit (2017), Ruiz (2018), Rezzolla (2018), and Shibata (2019), who used a cold, beta-equilibrium value of \(C^\star_M \approx 1.2\) for the relation between Keplerian and TOV masses. By accounting for finite temperature and out-of-beta-equilibrium conditions in the merger remnant, the authors obtain a less restrictive upper bound on the maximum mass of cold static neutron stars, enabling more realistic constraints from future gravitational wave events and informing the interpretation of the GW190814 event's light companion.
