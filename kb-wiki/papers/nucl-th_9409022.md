# Nucleon-Nucleon and Pion-Nucleon Potentials from Phase Shifts using Quantum Inversion

> NN and Pi-N partial wave radial potentials have been generated using the latest SM94-VPI(NN) and FA93-VPI(Pi-N) phase shifts. The potentials are used to determine the deuteron properties and to compute the 3H and 3He binding energies. The e_1 mixing angles of SM94-VPI and NY93-Nijmegen differ significantly and inversion potentials yield a P_d of 6.37% and 5.78%, respectively. Underbinding of 3H and 3He is enhanced by SM94, which signals more nonlocality and/or three-body potential effects than predicted from Nijmegen phase shifts and boson exchange models. The local pion-nucleon S_31, P_31 and P_33 channel potentials have been generated for guidance and to obtain a quantitative impression in r-space of what the Pi-N FA93-VPI phase shifts imply.

## Key claim

The SM94-VPI phase shifts yield inversion potentials that produce a deuteron D-state probability (P_d) of 6.37% and an underbinding of three-body systems by almost 1 MeV, which signals more nonlocality and/or three-body potential effects than predicted from Nijmegen phase shifts and boson exchange models; this discrepancy is primarily caused by the very different ε_1 mixing angles between the SM94-VPI and NY93-Nijmegen analyses.

## Method

The authors used the differential form of the Blankenbecler-Sugar equation and the radial Schrödinger equation as equations of motion to determine local, energy-independent radial potentials from elastic nucleon-nucleon and pion-nucleon scattering phase shifts. Single and coupled channel inversion algorithms based on Gelfand-Levitan and Marchenko integral equations were applied to the SM94-VPI (NN) and FA93-VPI (πN) phase shifts as boundary conditions. The resulting NN potentials (for partial waves with L ≤ 3, separately for np and pp scattering) reproduce input phases within 0.02 degrees in the subthreshold domain 0–300 MeV and follow data up to 1.6 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron D-state probability P_d = 6.3695% from SM94-VPI inversion potentials, compared to 5.7881% from NY93-Nijmegen inversion potentials
- Three-body binding energies from SM94-VPI inversion potentials (with charge independence breaking, 26 channels): 7.146 MeV for ³H and 6.616 MeV for ³He
- Three-body binding energies from Nijmegen-II inversion potentials (with CIB, 34 channels): 7.597 MeV for ³H and 6.949 MeV for ³He
- Deuteron binding energy from SM94 inversion potentials: 2.22459 MeV (experimental value: 2.22458900 ± 0.00000022 MeV)
- Deuteron asymptotic S-state amplitude A_s = 0.8802 fm^{1/2} from SM94 (experimental: 0.8802 ± 0.0020 fm^{1/2})
- Deuteron quadrupole moment Q = 0.276415 fm² from SM94 (experimental: 0.2859 fm²)
- Deuteron magnetic moment μ = 0.84342 μ_0 from SM94 (experimental: 0.857406 ± 0.000001 μ_0)
- Deuteron RMS radius = 1.962706 fm from SM94 (experimental: 1.9650 ± 0.0045 fm)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous inversion studies using VPI phase shifts and directly compares with the Nijmegen phase shift analysis (Stoks, 1993) and the Argonne V18 potential (Wiringa, 1994). The differing ε_1 mixing angles between the VPI and Nijmegen groups produce significantly different predictions for three-body binding energies, suggesting the need for a critical review of the phase shift analyses. The pion-nucleon inversion potentials for S_31, P_31, and P_33 channels provide quantitative r-space potentials for the latest FA93-VPI data, with the P_33 resonance appearing as a shape-resonance localized within 0–0.5 fm, enabling future tuning of theoretical models to experiment.
