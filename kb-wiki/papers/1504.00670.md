# Sequential Regeneration of Charmonia in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We investigate the production of psi(2S) in nuclear collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. We first address charmonium production in 200 GeV d-Au collisions at RHIC; the strong suppression of psi' mesons observed in these reactions indicates mechanisms beyond initial cold nuclear matter effects. We find that a more complete treatment of hadronic dissociation reactions leads to appreciable psi' suppression in the hadronic medium of an expanding fireball background for d-Au collisions. When implementing the updated hadronic reaction rates into a fireball for 2.76 TeV Pb-Pb collisions at LHC, the regeneration of psi' mesons occurs significantly later than for J/psi's. Despite a smaller total number of regenerated psi', the stronger radial flow at their time of production induces a marked enhancement of their R_{AA} relative to J/psi's in a momentum range p_t \simeq 3-6 GeV. We explore the consequences and uncertainties of this "sequential regeneration" mechanism on the R_{AA} double ratio and find that it can reproduce the trends observed in recent CMS data.

# Sequential Regeneration of Charmonia in Heavy-Ion Collisions

## Key claim

The observed enhancement of the $\psi'/J/\psi$ $R_{\rm AA}$ double ratio in central Pb-Pb collisions at LHC can be partially explained by a "sequential regeneration" mechanism where $\psi'$ mesons are produced later in the fireball evolution than $J/\psi$ mesons, acquiring a harder transverse-momentum spectrum from stronger radial flow; however, quantitative predictions remain beyond current theoretical control due to uncertainties in hadronic reaction rates, formfactor cutoffs, and blast-wave parameters.

## Method

The authors constructed a thermal fireball model for d-Au collisions at 200 GeV and Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, solving kinetic rate equations for charmonium abundances with updated hadronic dissociation rates computed from a meson resonance gas containing 52 non-strange and single-strange meson species up to mass 2 GeV. For the regeneration components, transverse-momentum spectra were approximated using a blast-wave expression evaluated at sequential freezeout times determined by the dissociation temperatures of each charmonium state. The primordial components were treated with a Boltzmann transport equation without gain term, and the results were compared to PHENIX d-Au data and CMS Pb-Pb data for the $\psi'/J/\psi$ $R_{\rm AA}$ double ratio.

## Key numbers

- $\psi'$ binding energy: approximately 45 MeV relative to the $D\bar D$ threshold
- $J/\psi$ dissociation rate at $T=170$ MeV: 1-2 MeV (corresponding to lifetime 100-200 fm/$c$) from $\pi\rho$ gas; maximal rate 10.5 MeV when including 52 meson species with formfactor cutoff $\Lambda=2$ GeV
- $\psi'$ maximal dissociation rate: up to 35 MeV (translating to lifetime ~6 fm/$c$) with geometric scaling and $\Lambda=2$ GeV
- d-Au fireball lifetimes: 0.5-3 fm/$c$, with freezeout temperature $T_{\rm fo}\simeq155$ MeV (peripheral) to ~142 MeV (central)
- d-Au effective nuclear absorption cross section: $\sigma_{\rm abs}=2.4$ mb (mimicking shadowing suppression)
- Pb-Pb $\psi'$ regeneration onset: $\tau\simeq5.5$ fm/$c$, leveling off at $\tau\simeq9-10$ fm/$c$ when temperature drops to ~150-160 MeV
- Sequential freezeout temperatures: $T_{\rm fo}=180$ MeV for $J/\psi$ (at $\tau=5.5$ fm/$c$) and $T_{\rm fo}=155$ MeV for $\psi'$ (at $\tau=9.7$ fm/$c$), with average flow velocities $\bar{v}=0.32c$ and $\bar{v}=0.46c$ respectively
- CMS double ratio for central collisions (1.6$<$$|y|$$<$2.4, 3$<$$p_t$$<$30 GeV): $2.3\pm0.5\pm0.35$; for semi-central: $0.9\pm0.45\pm0.3$
- CMS double ratio at midrapidity (6.5$<$$p_t$$<$30 GeV): ~0.5
- Primordial $R_{\rm AA}$ baseline for central Pb-Pb: $J/\psi$ 0.15-0.25, $\psi'$ 0-0.075
- Total momentum-integrated $R_{\rm AA}$ for central Pb-Pb: $J/\psi$ 0.55-0.65, $\psi'$ 0.4

## Context

This work extends kinetic transport approaches for charmonium production in heavy-ion collisions, previously applied to $J/\psi$ at SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies by Zhao and Rapp (2011) and Zhou et al. (2014). It addresses the "$\psi'$ puzzle" raised by CMS data (Khachatryan 2014) showing a double ratio exceeding unity in central collisions, which had evaded explanation in earlier studies by Chen et al. (2013). The hadronic dissociation rates update earlier work by Grandchamp and Rapp (2002) using flavor-$SU(4)$ meson exchange models, now including 52 meson species rather than only $\pi$ and $\rho$ mesons. The comover suppression model of Ferreiro (2014) provides an alternative explanation for the d-Au suppression. If corroborated, the sequential regeneration mechanism could serve as a tool to extract in-medium properties of the $\psi'$ from heavy-ion collision data.
