# Drell-Yan Massive Lepton-Pair's Angular Distributions at Large $Q_T$

> By measuring Drell-Yan massive lepton-pair's angular distributions, we can identify the polarization of the virtual photon of invariant mass $Q$ which decays immediately into the lepton-pair. In terms of a modified QCD factorization formula for Drell-Yan process, which is valid even if $Q_T\gg Q$, we calculate the massive lepton-pair's angular distributions at large $Q_T$. We find that the virtual photons produced at high $Q_T$ are more likely to be transversely polarized. We discuss the implications of this finding to the J/$ψ$ mesons' polarization measured recently at Fermilab.

# Drell-Yan Massive Lepton-Pair's Angular Distributions at Large Q_T

## Key claim

Using a modified QCD factorization formula valid when the transverse momentum Q_T is much larger than the invariant mass Q, the authors calculate that virtual photons produced in Drell-Yan processes at high Q_T are likely to be transversely polarized. This prediction contrasts with the CDF measurement of J/psi mesons at Fermilab, which become more longitudinally polarized as Q_T increases, suggesting that non-perturbative final-state interactions are important for J/psi formation.

## Method

The authors apply a modified QCD factorization formula derived by Berger, Qiu, and Zhang (in preparation) that resums large logarithms ln^n(Q_T^2/Q^2) to all orders in alpha_s by absorbing them into perturbative fragmentation functions for partons fragmenting into virtual photons. They calculate the polarization parameter alpha_DY at leading order by computing the two-to-two partonic tree diagrams at O(alpha_s^2) for the resummed contributions and the O(alpha_em alpha_s) subprocesses q qbar -> gamma* g and q g -> gamma* q for the Y-term, using the S-helicity frame with the z-axis along the virtual photon momentum.

## Key numbers

- Polarization parameter alpha_DY is calculated as a function of Q_T for proton-antiproton collisions at sqrt(S) = 2 TeV (Tevatron energy) and for proton-proton collisions at sqrt(S) = 500 GeV (RHIC energy)
- Calculations are performed for virtual photon invariant masses Q = 2 GeV and Q = 5 GeV, chosen to match kinematics similar to J/psi production
- The renormalization, factorization, and fragmentation scales are set to mu = kappa * sqrt(Q^2 + Q_T^2) with kappa = 1; varying kappa from 1/2 to 2 leaves the numerical values of alpha_DY relatively stable
- The CDF J/psi data referenced were measured at sqrt(S) = 1.8 TeV

## Context

This work extends the modified QCD factorization approach of Berger, Qiu, and Zhang (in preparation) and the virtual photon fragmentation functions of Qiu and Zhang (2001) to the calculation of angular distributions. It directly addresses the puzzle raised by CDF data (Affolder et al., 2000) showing J/psi mesons becoming more longitudinally polarized at high Q_T, contradicting predictions from the NRQCD model of Braaten, Kniehl, and Lee (2000). The authors propose that measuring alpha_DY in Drell-Yan production at similar kinematics can test the reliability of perturbative calculations for this kinematic region, and the difference between alpha_DY and alpha_J/psi can help isolate the role of final-state effects in J/psi formation.
