# Asymmetry in omega meson photoproduction and the phase of omega-pi-gamma coupling

> We analyze the double polarization asymmetry of the omega-meson photoproduction in the vector-meson-dominance model of diffractive production and the one-pion exchange model. We find that the longitudinal beam-target asymmetry is very sensitive to the real part of the diffractive photoproduction amplitude and to the sign of the omega-pi-gamma coupling because of the different spin structures of the amplitudes associated with the different mechanisms.

## Key claim

The longitudinal beam-target double polarization asymmetry \(L_{\rm BT}\) in omega meson photoproduction is sensitive to the real part of the vector-meson-dominance (VDM) diffractive amplitude and to the sign of the \(\omega\pi\gamma\) coupling constant, but only if the real part of the VDM amplitude is nonzero; if the VDM amplitude is purely imaginary, the asymmetry vanishes at forward angles.

## Method

The authors model omega photoproduction using two mechanisms: a diffractive vector-meson-dominance (VDM) amplitude (Pomeron exchange, assumed mostly imaginary) and a one-pion exchange (OPE) amplitude (pure real). They compute the double polarization asymmetry \(L_{\rm BT}\) for circularly polarized photons on a longitudinally polarized proton target, using helicity amplitudes derived from the VDM and OPE models. The real part of the VDM amplitude is estimated via a subtracted dispersion relation, yielding a ratio \({\cal R} = \text{Re}\,f / \text{Im}\,f \approx 0.1\) at photon energies of 2–3 GeV, and the sign of the \(\omega\pi\gamma\) coupling \(\eta_\omega\) is treated as either \(+1\) or \(-1\).

## Key numbers

- The ratio of real to imaginary parts of the VDM amplitude is \({\cal R} = 0.12 \sim 0.087\) at laboratory photon energies \(E_\gamma^L = 2 \sim 3\) GeV; the authors adopt \({\cal R} = 0.1\) for their qualitative estimates.
- The VDM differential cross section parameterization uses \(b_\omega = 6.7\) GeV\(^{-2}\), \(c = 9.3\) \(\mu\)b/GeV\(^2\), and \(d = 1.4\) GeV, determined from data at \(\sqrt{s} = 2.8 \sim 9.7\) GeV.
- The effective \(\omega\pi\gamma\) coupling constant squared is \(\tilde g_{\omega\pi\gamma}^2 = 0.498\) GeV\(^{-2}\), determined from the decay \(\omega \to (\rho\pi) \to \gamma\pi\).
- The pion-nucleon coupling constant is \(g^2_{NN\pi}/4\pi = 12.562\).
- The asymmetry \(L_{\rm BT}\) at forward angles is proportional to \(-2\,{\cal R}\,\eta_\omega \sqrt{d\sigma^{\rm OPE}\, d\sigma^{\rm VDM} / d\sigma^{\rm tot}}\), and vanishes when \({\cal R}=0\).
- The optimal initial photon laboratory energy for measuring the asymmetry is less than 5 GeV; calculations are shown at \(E_\gamma^L = 2.8\) GeV and 4.7 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of double polarization observables in vector-meson photoproduction, previously applied to \(\phi\) mesons by Titov, Oh, and Yang (1997) for probing hidden strangeness in the nucleon. It addresses the controversial phase of the \(\omega\pi\gamma\) coupling, which has been discussed in the context of pion photoproduction (Garcilazo and Guerra, 1993; Fuda and Miller, 1997) and meson exchange currents in deuteron form factors (Gari and Hyuga, 1976). The results suggest that measurements of \(L_{\rm BT}\) at facilities with photon energies of 2–4 GeV could directly determine both the real part of the VDM amplitude and the sign of the \(\omega\pi\gamma\) coupling, thereby resolving the phase ambiguity in pion photoproduction models.
