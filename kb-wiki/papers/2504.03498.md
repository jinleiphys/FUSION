# Predictions in the superheavy region from the quark-meson coupling model QMC$π$-III

> The Quark-Meson Coupling (QMC) model establishes a self-consistent relationship between the quark structure dynamics of a nucleon and the relativistic mean fields that arise within the nuclear medium. The model has been successful in calculating the ground-state observables of finite nuclei and in predicting the properties of dense nuclear matter, as well as cold, nonaccreting neutron stars. This paper focuses on the latest predictions from the model for the superheavy region, encompassing energies and deformations. Despite utilizing significantly fewer model parameters, the results have consistently improved as the model evolved, yielding better predictions for binding energies.

# Predictions in the superheavy region from the quark-meson coupling model QMCπ-III

## Key claim
The QMCπ-III model predicts binding energies and alpha-decay energies for superheavy elements (Z ≥ 100) with improved accuracy compared to earlier QMC versions and other nuclear models, achieving root-mean-square binding energy residuals of approximately 0.7 MeV for 60 known even-even superheavy nuclei, despite using only five adjustable parameters.

## Method
The QMCπ-III energy density functional was solved using an axially symmetric Hartree-Fock plus Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (HF+BCS) code, with parameters optimized via the POUNDerS derivative-free procedure. The five model parameters were fitted to 129 data points comprising binding energies and root-mean-square charge radii of 70 magic and semi-magic nuclei. Calculations were then performed for ground-state properties of even-even superheavy nuclei with Z ≥ 100, and residuals were computed against the Atomic Mass Evaluation (AME) 2020.

## Key numbers
- Root-mean-square binding energy residual for QMCπ-III across 60 known even-even superheavy nuclei: 0.7 MeV (compared to 2.0 MeV for QMCπ-II, 1.4 MeV for UNEDF1, 2.3 MeV for DDMEδ, 2.6 MeV for FRDM, and 7.1 MeV for SVmin-HFBTHO)
- Optimized parameter values: Gσ = 9.62 fm², Gω = 5.21 fm², Gρ = 4.71 fm², Mσ = 503 MeV, λ3 = 0.05 fm⁻¹
- Nuclear matter properties from optimized parameters: saturation density ρ0 = 0.15 fm⁻³, saturation energy E0 = -15.7 MeV, symmetry energy S0 = 29 MeV, slope of symmetry energy L0 = 43 MeV, incompressibility K0 = 233 MeV
- Absolute total binding energy residuals for QMCπ-III in the Z=100-114 region: less than approximately 1.5 MeV
- Binding energy residuals for Livermorium (Z=116, N=174): -0.3 MeV; for Oganesson (Z=118, N=176): -0.8 MeV

## Context
This work extends the earlier QMCπ-I predictions for superheavy elements (Stone, 2019) and the QMCπ-II model (Martinez, 2019; Martinez, 2020) by incorporating a density-dependent pairing functional derived from Bogoliubov theory, which eliminates two additional pairing parameters. The results improve upon predictions from other models such as UNEDF1, DDMEδ, FRDM, and SVmin-HFBTHO, and capture subshell closures at neutron numbers N=152 and N=162. The model enables further theoretical exploration of the superheavy region beyond the current nuclear landscape, particularly near the predicted neutron magic number N=184 and the hypothesized island of stability.
