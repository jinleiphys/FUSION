# Neutral-current neutrino-nucleus quasielastic scattering

> A relativistic distorted-wave impulse-approximation model to quasielastic neutral-current neutrino-nucleus scattering is developed, using the relativistic mean field theory for the bound state and taking into account the final state interaction in the relativistic scattering state. Results for the neutrino (antineutrino) reaction on 12C target nucleus are presented in an energy range between 500 and 1000 MeV. The sensitivity to the strange quark content of the axial-vector form factor is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper develops a relativistic distorted-wave impulse-approximation (RDWIA) model for neutral-current neutrino-nucleus quasielastic scattering and finds that final-state interactions (FSI) produce a large reduction of cross sections (approximately 50% for proton knockout and 60% for neutron knockout), while the ratio of proton to neutron yields remains sensitive to the strange-quark content of the axial-vector form factor and is actually enhanced by distortion effects.

## Method

The authors solve a relativistic mean-field theory for bound nucleon wave functions and use a relativistic optical model potential (the EDAD1 potential fitted to proton elastic scattering data up to 1040 MeV) to describe the final-state interaction of the emitted nucleon. The transition amplitudes are computed in the impulse approximation, where the neutrino interacts with a single nucleon, and the cross sections are obtained by contracting the lepton and hadron tensors, then integrating over the outgoing nucleon solid angle and the lepton kinematics. Calculations are performed for a 12C target at three incident neutrino (antineutrino) energies: 500, 700, and 1000 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Cross section reduction of approximately 50% for proton knockout and approximately 60% for neutron knockout when comparing RDWIA to RPWIA (plane-wave) calculations.
- With a strange axial-vector form factor value of g_s_A = -0.19, the cross section for proton knockout is enhanced by approximately 50% relative to the case with no strange-quark content; for neutron knockout the cross section is reduced by approximately 30%.
- The ratio of proton to neutron cross sections for neutrino scattering is enhanced by a factor of approximately 2 when including the strange-quark contribution; for antineutrino scattering the enhancement is approximately 50%.
- The strange vector form factor F_2^s(0) = -0.40 decreases the cross sections, while F_1^s = -⟨r²_s⟩/6 = 0.53 GeV⁻² gives an enhancement that at E_ν = 1 GeV almost cancels the effect of F_2^s.
- The ratio of proton to neutron yields for neutrino scattering with g_s_A = -0.19 exhibits a pronounced maximum at T_N ≈ 0.6 E_ν.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic distorted-wave impulse-approximation approach previously applied to electromagnetic knockout reactions (Meucci et al., 2001-2003) and charged-current neutrino-nucleus scattering (Meucci et al., 2003) to neutral-current quasielastic scattering. It improves upon earlier relativistic Fermi gas models (Horowitz et al., 1993) and hybrid models (Barbaro et al., 1996) by including a full treatment of final-state interactions via a relativistic optical potential. The results enable more reliable extraction of the strange-quark content of the nucleon axial-vector form factor from future experiments such as BooNE and FINeSSE, and the authors show that the ratio of proton to neutron yields, which is less sensitive to flux uncertainties, remains a viable observable despite distortion effects.
