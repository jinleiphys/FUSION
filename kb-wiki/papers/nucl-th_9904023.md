# Convergence Issues in Nucleon-Nucleon Effective Field Theory

> The origin of the breakdown scale in an effective field theory treatment of nuclear forces is investigated. Different organizational schemes used in the nonperturbative calculation of nucleon-nucleon scattering seem to break down for different physical reasons. It is argued that the same physics is actually responsible, namely strong two-pion exchange as also observed in the pion scalar form factor. The prospect of extending the applicability of the effective field theory beyond this breakdown point is discussed.

## Key claim

The breakdown scale of nucleon-nucleon effective field theory, observed around 300 MeV in both Λ-counting and Q-counting schemes, originates from strong two-pion exchange rather than from the ρ meson or Δ resonance scales; this is the same physics that causes poor convergence in the pion scalar form factor, and extending the EFT beyond this point requires properly accounting for two-pion exchange effects.

## Method

The author constructs solvable two-Yukawa and three-Yukawa potential models (with pion, rho, and scalar meson exchanges) to mimic nucleon-nucleon scattering in the ¹S₀ channel, then applies both Λ-counting (Weinberg-style, with nonperturbative pions) and Q-counting (Kaplan-Savage-Wise style, with perturbative pions) power-counting schemes. The low-energy constants are fit using either a modified effective range expansion (to remove long-distance pion physics) or a constrained global fit minimized with respect to modified effective ranges, and the radius of convergence is extracted from error plots or from comparing different orders of the amplitude expansion.

## Key numbers

- The observed breakdown scale for NN EFT is at center-of-mass momentum **p ∼ 300 MeV** for both Λ-counting and Q-counting when applied to actual ¹S₀ NN scattering data from the Nijmegen partial wave analysis.
- The Fermi momentum at nuclear saturation is **p_F ∼ 280 MeV**, which is too close to the breakdown scale for EFT analysis to be useful.
- In the two-Yukawa model with m_π = 140 MeV and m_ρ = 770 MeV, the pion coupling is α_π = g_A² m_π²/(16π f_π²) ≈ **0.075**, and the rho coupling α_ρ = **1.05** is tuned to give a scattering length a_s = **-23.4 fm**.
- The effective range in the ¹S₀ channel from data is **r_0 = 2.63 fm**.
- The three-Yukawa model uses scalar couplings (α_σ, α_ρ) = **(7, 14.65)** with scalar mass between **500 and 600 MeV** to reproduce the data; the breakdown scale tracks with m_σ/2 ∼ 300 MeV.
- The Q-counting expansion parameter involves m_π/Λ_NN ∼ **1/2**, where Λ_NN ≈ **300 MeV**.
- The pion scalar form factor in chiral perturbation theory breaks down just above threshold at **√s ≈ 400 MeV**.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of effective field theory for nuclear forces following the foundational approaches of Weinberg (1990, 1991) and the power-divergence subtraction scheme of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998, PDS). It addresses the discrepancy between the expected breakdown scale (m_ρ ∼ 770 MeV or the Δ threshold ∼ 525 MeV) and the observed lower scale, using model studies by Steele, Furnstahl, and Kaplan (1997, 1998) to isolate the role of two-pion exchange. The connection to the pion scalar form factor (Meissner, 1988) suggests that infrared effects requiring summation of logarithmic terms generate a scalar-resonance-like shape around 600 MeV. The modified effective range expansion, adapted from proton-proton scattering analyses, provides a method to remove long-distance pion physics from the fitting procedure. The work enables future calculations that include two-pion exchange nonperturbatively, potentially extending the EFT radius of convergence to the ρ scale and making nuclear matter calculations at saturation density more tractable.
