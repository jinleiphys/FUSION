# Non-perturbative study of spectral function and its application in Quark Gluon Plasma

> The thesis contains studies of properties quark-gluon plasma, using some non-perturbative techniques. It contains a brief introduction of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) and discussion on various signatures along with a motivation for this thesis work. It presents the basic mathematical tools and ingredients required for the thesis, i.e. basics of QCD, Imaginary and Real Time Formalism, Hard Thermal Loop perturbation theory (HTLpt), Gribov-Zwanziger (GZ) action, the Correlation Function along with the Spectral Function and Operator Product Expansion (OPE) and QCD in magnetized medium. OPE is used to compute the dilepton rate in intermediate mass range by incorporating the non-perturbative dynamics of QCD through the inclusion of non-vanishing quark and gluon condensates in combination with the Green functions in momentum space. Also the magnetic scale (g^2T) in the HTL perturbation theory, related to the confining properties of the QCD is taken into account using the GZ action through a mass parameter, which reflects a new space-like quark mode in the collective excitation. The impact of this new exciting mode on the DPR has been studied and its important consequences has been discussed. A hot magnetized medium introduces another scale in the system in addition to temperature. Electromagnetic spectral properties and DPR are studied completely analytically in presence of both strong and weak background magnetic fields at finite temperature. The Debye screening in a hot and magnetized medium has been studied which reveals some of the intriguing properties of the medium in presence of both strong and weak magnetic field. Also an important quantity that characterizes the QGP, namely quark number susceptibility has been investigated. Most of the non-perturbative results discussed in this thesis are compared with those of perturbative ones and lattice QCD.

## Key claim

The thesis uses non-perturbative techniques, specifically the Gribov-Zwanziger (GZ) action and Operator Product Expansion (OPE), to compute spectral functions and related observables in the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP), with the hedge that the Gribov parameter introduces a new space-like quark mode whose impact on the dilepton production rate (DPR) is studied, and that most non-perturbative results are compared with perturbative ones and lattice QCD, implying the results are not definitive but rather comparative.

## Method

The author employs Hard Thermal Loop perturbation theory (HTLpt) combined with the Gribov-Zwanziger (GZ) action to incorporate the magnetic scale (g²T) and confinement effects via a mass parameter (the Gribov parameter γ_G), modifying the gluon propagator to include γ_G in the denominator. OPE is used to compute the dilepton rate in the intermediate mass range by including non-vanishing quark and gluon condensates in momentum-space Green functions. For magnetized media, the author analytically computes electromagnetic spectral properties and DPR in both strong and weak background magnetic fields at finite temperature, using the Landau level formalism for fermions.

## Key numbers

- The Gribov parameter at asymptotically high temperatures is given by γ_G = (D-1)/D * N_c/(4√2π) * g²T, where D is the dimension of the theory and N_c=3.
- The one-loop running strong coupling is g²(T) = 48π² / [(33-2N_f) ln(Q₀²/Λ₀²)], with Λ₀ = 176 MeV fixed by requiring α_s(1.5 GeV) = 0.326.
- The Debye screening mass m_D and thermal quark mass m_q appear in the HTL Lagrangian as parameters accounting for screening effects.
- The energy spectrum for fermions in a constant magnetic field is E_n(p_z) = ±√(p_z² + m_f² + 2n q_f B), where n = 0,1,2,... are Landau levels.

## Context

This thesis extends prior work on QGP characterization using HTLpt, which has been applied to thermodynamic quantities (e.g., by Haque et al. (2014) showing agreement with lattice QCD) and to DPR, QNS, and photon production. It incorporates the Gribov-Zwanziger action to address the Gribov ambiguity in non-Abelian gauge theory, following Gribov (1978) and Zwanziger (1989), and uses OPE as pioneered by Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1978) to include non-perturbative condensates. The work enables future studies of QGP properties in magnetized media and comparisons with lattice QCD results for spectral functions, which are currently limited by the ill-defined analytic continuation from Euclidean to Minkowski space.
