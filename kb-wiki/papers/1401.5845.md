# Medium Effects on Charmonium Production at ultrarelativistic energies available at the CERN Large Haron Collider

> We investigate with a transport approach the cold and hot nuclear matter effects on the charmonium transverse momentum distributions in relativistic heavy ion collisions. The newly defined nuclear modification factor $r_{AA}=\langle p_{T}^2\rangle_{AA}/\langle p_{T}^2\rangle_{pp}$ and elliptic flow $v_2$ for $J/ψ$ are sensitive to the nature of the hot medium and the thermalization of heavy quarks. From SPS through RHIC to LHC colliding energies, we observe dramatic changes in the centrality dependence of $r_{AA}$. We find that at LHC energy, the finally observed charmonia are dominated by the regeneration from thermalized heavy quarks.

## Key claim
The paper claims that at LHC energy (2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions), the observed charmonia are dominated by regeneration from thermalized heavy quarks rather than initial production, but this conclusion depends on assuming a kinetically thermalized charm quark distribution and a 20% reduction in charm quark number due to shadowing effects.

## Method
The authors employed a 2+1 dimensional ideal hydrodynamic model to simulate the QGP evolution, coupled with a Boltzmann-type transport equation for charmonium states (J/ψ, ψ', χ_c) including both dissociation and regeneration terms. The cold nuclear matter effects (shadowing via EKS98 parameterization and Cronin effect via Gaussian smearing) were incorporated into the initial conditions, while the hot medium effects included gluon dissociation and regeneration through detailed balance, using a thermalized charm quark distribution from the hydrodynamic solution.

## Key numbers
- Maximum initial temperature T₀ = 484 MeV at mid rapidity and 430 MeV at forward rapidity, corresponding to dNch/dy = 1600 and 1200 respectively, at initial time τ₀ = 0.6 fm/c
- Critical temperature T_c = 165 MeV from the equation of state with bag constant B^{1/4} = 236 MeV and mean field repulsion K = 450 MeV fm³
- Cronin parameter a_{gN} = 0.15 GeV²/fm extracted from empirical estimations
- Free p+p J/ψ production cross section dσ^{pp}_Ψ/dy = 4.1 μb at |y| < 0.9 and 2.3 μb at 2.5 < y < 4
- Charm quark production cross section dσ_{c\bar c}^{pp}/dy = 0.65 mb at mid rapidity and 0.4 mb at forward rapidity (with upper limits 0.8 mb and 0.5 mb respectively)
- B decay fraction parameterized as f_B(p_T) = 0.04 + 0.023 p_T/(GeV/c)
- B quench factor R_{AA}^B = 0.4 applied for non-prompt J/ψ in the region 5 < p_T < 8 GeV/c
- The ratio r_{AA} = ⟨p_T²⟩_{AA}/⟨p_T²⟩_{pp} shows systematic behavior: >1 at SPS, ∼1 at RHIC, <1 at LHC at mid rapidity

## Context
This work extends the transport approach previously applied at SPS and RHIC energies (Zhuang et al., Yan et al.) to LHC energy, where the much larger charm quark production cross section makes regeneration the dominant production mechanism. The newly defined ratio r_{AA} provides a more sensitive probe of hot medium properties than the standard R_{AA}, as it is less affected by cold nuclear matter effects like shadowing. The results enable future experimental measurements at LHC to test the thermalization assumption for heavy quarks and the competition between suppression and regeneration across different collision energies.
