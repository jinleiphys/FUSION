# From colored glass condensate to gluon plasma: equilibration in high energy heavy ion collisions

> The initial distribution of gluons at the very early times after a high energy heavy ion collision is described by the bulk scale $Q_s$ of gluon saturation in the nuclear wavefunction. The subsequent evolution of the system towards kinetic equilibrium is described by a non-linear Landau equation for the single particle distributions \cite{Mueller1,Mueller2}. In this paper, we solve this equation numerically for the idealized initial conditions proposed by Mueller, and study the evolution of the system to equilibrium. We discuss the sensitivity of our results on the dynamical screening of collinear divergences. In a particular model of dynamical screening, the convergence to the hydrodynamic limit is seen to be rapid relative to hydrodynamic time scales. The equilibration time, the initial temperature, and the chemical potential are shown to have a strong functional dependence on the initial gluon saturation scale $Q_s$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for RHIC and LHC energies, the equilibration time for gluons produced in heavy ion collisions is much shorter than the hydrodynamic expansion time, suggesting favorable conditions for formation of a hot gluon plasma; however, this conclusion is based on idealized initial conditions (a theta-function distribution) and considers only number-conserving 2-to-2 scattering processes, which should set an upper bound on the equilibration time.

## Method

The authors numerically solved the non-linear Landau transport equation for single-particle gluon distributions under the assumption of boost-invariant 1+1-dimensional expansion, using idealized initial conditions proposed by Mueller (a theta-function distribution in transverse momentum). They implemented a particular model of dynamical screening of collinear divergences based on the Debye screening mass derived from kinetic theory, and studied the time evolution of the system from the initial non-equilibrium state toward kinetic equilibrium.

## Key numbers

- Formation time for gluons to come on-shell: \(t_i \sim 1.40\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\) for RHIC (\(Q_s = 1\ \mathrm{GeV}\)) and \(t_i \sim 0.62\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\) for LHC (\(Q_s = 2\text{--}3\ \mathrm{GeV}\))
- Initial gluon number density per unit rapidity and transverse area: \(\frac{1}{L^2}\frac{dN}{d\eta} = c\frac{N_c^2-1}{4\pi^2\alpha_S N_c} Q_s^2\), with \(c = 1.29 \pm 0.09\) for SU(2) gauge theory
- The initial gluon distribution at late times: \(f(x,p) = \frac{c}{\alpha_S N_c} \frac{1}{t} \delta(p_z) \theta(Q_s^2 - p_t^2)\)
- The screening mass from Mueller's condition: \(M_m^2 = \left(\frac{c\alpha_S N_c}{\pi}\right)^{2/3} \frac{Q_s^2}{(Q_s t)^{2/3}}\)
- The Debye screening mass expression: \(m_T^2 = \frac{\alpha_S N_c}{\pi^2} \int \frac{d^3 p}{|\vec{p}|} f(p)\)

## Context

This work extends the analytical studies of Mueller (1999, 2000) on equilibration in heavy ion collisions by providing the first numerical solution of the non-linear Landau equation, which could not be solved analytically all the way to equilibrium. The initial conditions are based on the color glass condensate effective field theory developed by McLerran and Venugopalan (1994) and the numerical Yang-Mills solutions of Krasnitz and Venugopalan (1999, 2000). The paper enables future work using more realistic initial distributions from lattice simulations and sets the stage for including number-changing 2-to-3 processes, which may be more efficient at driving thermalization as suggested by Baier et al. (2000).
