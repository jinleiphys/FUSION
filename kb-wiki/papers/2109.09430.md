# Dipole excitation of $^6$Li and $^9$Be studied with an extended quantum molecular dynamics model

> The $α$ ($^4$He) - clustering structure is a common phenomenon in light nuclei due to the decreasing contribution of mean field in few-body system. In this work we presented calculations of giant dipole resonance (GDR) excitations for two non $α$-conjugate light nuclei, namely $^{6}$Li and $^{9}$Be, within a framework of an extended quantum molecular dynamics model. For $^{6}$Li, we investigated the GDR spectra from the two-body clustering structure with $α$ + deuteron as well as the three-body structure with $α$ + $n$ + $p$, and found that the major $α$-clustering contribution on the GDR peak is located at around 31 MeV, while the resonance contributions between clusters, namely $α$ and deuteron or ($n$ + $p$), are located on the lower energy side, which can be regarded as pygmy dipole resonance (PDR). For $^{9}$Be, a mixture configuration contribution for the Chain-like structure and Borromean-like structure of the $α$ + n + $α$ configuration can somehow explain its GDR results.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the giant dipole resonance (GDR) spectra of non-α-conjugate light nuclei \(^{6}\)Li and \(^{9}\)Be can be explained by assuming specific α-clustering configurations within an extended quantum molecular dynamics (EQMD) model, but the quantitative fits are not achieved and the specific mixture proportions of configurations are model dependent.

## Method

The authors used the EQMD model to generate initial clustering configurations for \(^{6}\)Li (α + deuteron and α + n + p) and \(^{9}\)Be (Chain-like and Borromean-like α + n + α) through a cooling process. They then calculated the dipole resonance spectra by applying the Goldhaber-Teller assumption, using the Fourier transform of the second time derivative of the dipole moment in coordinate space to obtain the γ-emission probability as a function of energy.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{6}\)Li with α + deuteron configuration: the α-clustering GDR peak is located at around 31 MeV, and a deuteron-like contribution peak appears at around 41 MeV.
- For \(^{6}\)Li: the cluster dipole resonance between α and deuteron peaks at about 10 MeV, while the resonance between α and (n + p) peaks at around 14 MeV; together these give an average main peak at about 12 MeV.
- For \(^{9}\)Be Chain-like configuration: double peaks appear at about 20 MeV (low energy) and about 30 MeV (high energy, from α-cluster GDR).
- Binding energies per nucleon for \(^{6}\)Li configurations: α + d gives −3.49 MeV/A, α + n + p gives −3.26 MeV/A; experimental values are −5.087 MeV/A and −4.715 MeV/A respectively.
- The mixture proportion used for \(^{6}\)Li combined spectra was 1.0 : 1.17 : 0.9 : 1.0 for the four components (α + d, α + n + p, α-cluster peak, deuteron-like peak).

## Context

This work extends previous EQMD studies of GDR in α-conjugate nuclei like \(^{12}\)C and \(^{16}\)O (He and Ma, 2014) to non-α-conjugate light nuclei. It builds on experimental GDR measurements for \(^{6}\)Li by Yamagata et al. (2011) and for \(^{9}\)Be by Utsunomiya et al. (2015), as well as theoretical calculations using microscopic cluster models (Arai, 1995; Arai, 1996) and the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics model (Yoshiko, 2015). The results suggest that GDR spectra can serve as a probe for distinguishing different α-clustering configurations in light nuclei, enabling future studies of cluster structure in exotic nuclei.
