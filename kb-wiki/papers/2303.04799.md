# Entanglement entropy of nuclear systems

> We study entanglement entropies between the single-particle states of the hole space and its complement in nuclear systems. Analytical results based on the coupled-cluster method show that entanglement entropies are proportional to the particle number fluctuation and the depletion number of the hole space for sufficiently weak interactions. General arguments also suggest that the entanglement entropy in nuclear systems fulfills a volume instead of an area law. We test and confirm these results by computing entanglement entropies of the pairing model and neutron matter, and the depletion number of finite nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for sufficiently weak interactions (quantified by the condition \(t^2 \ll 1\)), the Rényi entanglement entropies between hole and particle single-particle states in nuclear systems are proportional to the particle-number fluctuation and the depletion number of the hole space, and that the entanglement entropy in nuclear systems follows a volume law rather than an area law.

## Method

The authors use coupled-cluster theory with the doubles approximation (CCD) to derive analytical expressions for the Rényi entropy of the reduced hole-space density matrix, assuming the cluster operator \(\hat{T}_2\) is small. They test these analytical results numerically by computing entanglement entropies for the exactly solvable pairing model and for neutron matter described by the Minnesota potential in a cubic momentum-space basis, and they compute the depletion number for finite nuclei using coupled-cluster singles-and-doubles (CCSD) with interactions from Hebeler (2011).

## Key numbers

- For the pairing model at half filling with \(\delta = 1.0\), the analytical result \(S_\alpha \approx \frac{\alpha}{\alpha-1} t^2\) for \(\alpha > 1\) and \(S_1 \approx -t^2 \log t^2\) for \(t^2 \to 0\) is confirmed, with \(t^2 \approx (\Delta N_\mathrm{H})^2/4\).
- In the pairing model, second-order perturbation theory gives \(t^2 \approx \frac{g^2}{16\delta^2} \log{\frac{N}{4}}\) for large \(N\) at half filling, leading to \(S_\alpha \propto \log(N)\) for \(\alpha \geq 2\).
- For neutron matter at density \(n \approx 0.08\) fm\(^{-3}\) with \(N_\mathrm{max}=5\), the values of \(t^2\) range from 0.106 for \(N=14\) to 0.581 for \(N=66\); for \(N=114\), \(t^2 = 1.565\) violates the weak-interaction condition.
- The depletion number \(\delta A\) for finite nuclei \(^4\)He, \(^{16}\)O, \(^{40}\)Ca, and \(^{100}\)Sn scales approximately linearly with mass number \(A\), consistent with a volume law rather than an area law.

## Context

This work extends the pioneering results of Klich (2006) on the relation between entanglement entropy and particle-number fluctuation from free fermions to weakly interacting finite Fermi systems. The analytical results are tested on the pairing model of Dukelsky (2004) and on neutron matter, and the depletion number for finite nuclei is computed using interactions from Hebeler (2011). The findings enable the use of the depletion number as a computationally accessible entanglement witness in nuclear structure calculations and suggest that entanglement in nuclei grows with volume, which has implications for both classical and quantum many-body computations.
