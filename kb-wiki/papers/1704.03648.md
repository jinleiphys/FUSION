# Effects of jet-induced medium excitation in $γ$-hadron correlation in A+A collisions

> Coupled Linear Boltzmann Transport and hydrodynamics (CoLBT-hydro) is developed for co-current and event-by-event simulations of jet transport and jet-induced medium excitation (j.i.m.e.) in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. This is made possible by a GPU parallelized (3+1)D hydrodynamics that has a source term from the energy-momentum deposition by propagating jet shower partons and provides real time update of the bulk medium evolution for subsequent jet transport. Hadron spectra in $γ$-jet events of A+A collisions at RHIC and LHC are calculated for the first time that include hadrons from both the modified jet and j.i.m.e.. CoLBT-hydro describes well experimental data at RHIC on the suppression of leading hadrons due to parton energy loss. It also predicts the enhancement of soft hadrons from j.i.m.e. The onset of soft hadron enhancement occurs at a constant transverse momentum due to the thermal nature of soft hadrons from j.i.m.e. which also have a significantly broadened azimuthal distribution relative to the jet direction. Soft hadrons in the $γ$ direction are, on the other hand, depleted due to a diffusion wake behind the jet.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the onset of soft hadron enhancement from jet-induced medium excitation occurs at a constant transverse momentum of about 2 GeV/c, independent of the photon or jet energy, which is a signature of the thermal nature of these hadrons; this enhancement is accompanied by a depletion of soft hadrons in the photon direction due to a diffusion wake behind the jet.

## Method

The authors developed the Coupled Linear Boltzmann Transport and hydrodynamics (CoLBT-hydro) model, which couples the Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model for jet propagation to a GPU-parallelized (3+1)D relativistic hydrodynamic code (CLVisc) in real time. LBT provides a source term from energy-momentum deposition by soft and "negative" partons into the hydrodynamic equations, while the hydrodynamics updates the local temperature and fluid velocity for subsequent jet transport in each time step. Hadron spectra in gamma-jet events are computed by summing contributions from LBT via parton recombination and from CLVisc via Cooper-Frye freeze-out, with the background from the same event without the gamma-jet subtracted.

## Key numbers

- The strong coupling constant is set to alpha_s = 0.3 to fit the STAR data on leading hadron suppression.
- The cut-off parameter for soft partons contributing to the hydrodynamic source term is p^0_cut = 2 GeV.
- The onset of soft hadron enhancement (I_AA >= 1) due to jet-induced medium excitation occurs at a constant p_T^h ~ 2 GeV/c.
- The initial time for hydrodynamics is tau_0 = 0.4 fm/c and the freeze-out temperature is T_f = 137 MeV.
- Viscous correction to the final fragmentation function is about 10-20% for eta/s = 0.16 at low z where jet-induced medium excitation dominates.
- The half width sigma of the azimuthal distribution for large p_T hadrons in Au+Au collisions remains approximately unchanged from p+p collisions.

## Context

This work extends the LBT model (Wang 2013, He 2015, Cao 2016) by coupling it to real-time hydrodynamic evolution, enabling the first calculation of gamma-hadron correlations that include both modified jet fragmentation and jet-induced medium excitation in heavy-ion collisions. It builds on prior studies of jet-induced medium excitation using parton transport (Xu 2004, Schenke 2009, Zapp 2008), hydrodynamics with source terms (Betz 2008, Qin 2009, Tachibana 2015), and AdS/CFT (Gubser 2007, Chesler 2007). The results enable experimental tests at RHIC (sPHENIX) and LHC to provide new constraints on jet tomography of the quark-gluon plasma.
