# Momentum distribution of Delta- isobar in closed shell nuclei

## Key claim

The paper calculates the momentum distribution of internal (off-mass-shell) Delta- isobars in closed-shell light nuclei using a harmonic oscillator model with pi- and rho-exchange transition potentials, but the calculation is limited to one-Delta-isobar configurations and neglects interactions between isobars and between isobars and nucleons beyond the impulse approximation.

## Method

The author constructs wave functions for one-Delta-isobar configurations in closed-shell nuclei within the framework of the harmonic oscillator model, using the formalism of Arenhövel et al. (1980s) that introduces an intrinsic coordinate space for nucleons and isobars. The transition from nucleon-nucleon to nucleon-Delta pairs is driven by pi- and rho-exchange potentials, and the resulting two-body Delta-N wave function is obtained via a Green's function approach in the impulse approximation. For the nucleus 12C, which does not have closed 1p shells in the ls-coupling scheme, the author employs a jj-coupling shell model with closed s1/2 and p3/2 shells, transforming the wave functions between coupling schemes using Clebsch-Gordan coefficients.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Arenhövel et al. (1980s) for treating isobar configurations in nuclei, specifically calculating momentum distributions needed for impulse-approximation descriptions of knockout reactions that probe preexisting Delta-isobars in the ground state. The results are intended to enable comparison with experiments attempting to find manifestations of exotic Delta-isobar configurations in light nuclei, as previously pursued by several experimental groups (1990s-2000s). The paper provides the formal framework and numerical results for 4He, 16O, and 12C, though only the 4He and 16O calculations are detailed in the truncated text.
