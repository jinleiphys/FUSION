# The many-nucleon unified model and the dynamics of nuclear rotations

> It is determined that a many-nucleon version of the Bohr-Mottelson unified model that contains the essential observables of that model and has irreducible representations that span the Hilbert space of fully anti-symmetric states of nuclei, is given uniquely by the symplectic model. This model is shown to provide a framework for an examination of the dynamics of nuclear rotations. A first discovery is that rotational energies are mixtures of potential and kinetic energies even in an adiabatic limit.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a many-nucleon version of the Bohr-Mottelson unified model, which contains the essential observables of that model and spans the Hilbert space of fully anti-symmetric nuclear states, is given uniquely by the symplectic model. A key discovery is that rotational energies are mixtures of potential and kinetic energies even in an adiabatic limit, contrary to the standard assumption that they are purely kinetic.

## Method

The author identifies the Lie algebra of the direct product group Sp(3,R) x SU(2)_S x SU(2)_T as containing all essential rotor-model observables (monopole and quadrupole moments, angular-momentum operators, kinetic energy, and spin/isospin operators). The unified model is then obtained as a mean-field approximation to an Sp(3,R) irrep, where the harmonic-oscillator frequencies are chosen to minimize the energy for a rotationally invariant many-nucleon Hamiltonian. The kinetic energy contributions to rotational energies are calculated algebraically for an axially symmetric Sp(3,R) x SU(2)_S x SU(2)_T irrep using explicit expressions for angular-momentum states projected from a shape-consistent lowest-weight state.

## Key numbers

- The kinetic energy component accounted for approximately 35% of the observed rotational energies for the ground-state rotational band of 166Er.
- The kinetic energies derived are proportional to L(L+1) to a high level of accuracy, for angular-momentum values 0 ≤ L ≤ 14.
- The Sp(3,R) irrep used for 166Er had the weight ⟨327½, 249½, 249½⟩.

## Context

This work extends the Bohr-Mottelson unified model (Bohr and Mottelson, 1953, 1975) and the SU(3) model by providing a many-nucleon framework that respects anti-symmetrization. It contradicts the standard assumption that rotational energies are purely kinetic (like translations) and instead supports earlier schematic model calculations by Park et al. (1984) and Bahri and Rowe (2000) that showed comparable kinetic and potential energy components. The results enable future full many-nucleon calculations in single symplectic-model irreps for nuclei such as 12C (Hoyle state), 16O, and 20Ne, and can be extended to triaxial irreps and spin-orbit interactions using symmetry-adapted no-core shell model (SA-NCSM) methods as developed by Dytrych et al. (2018).
