# Lattice Monte Carlo Simulations with Two Impurity Worldlines

> We develop the impurity lattice Monte Carlo formalism, for the case of two distinguishable impurities in a bath of polarized fermions. The majority particles are treated as explicit degrees of freedom, while the impurities are described by worldlines. The latter serve as localized auxiliary fields, which affect the majority particles. We apply the method to non-relativistic three-dimensional systems of two impurities and a number of majority particles where both the impurity-impurity interaction and the impurity-majority interaction have zero range. We consider the case of an attractive impurity-majority interaction, and we study the formation and disintegration of bound states as a function of the impurity-impurity interaction strength. We also discuss the potential applications of this formalism to other quantum many-body systems.

## Key claim
The paper extends the impurity lattice Monte Carlo (ILMC) formalism from one impurity to two distinguishable impurities in a bath of polarized fermions, demonstrating that the method can describe the formation and disintegration of bound states as a function of the impurity-impurity interaction strength. The authors note that while the system studied does not directly correspond to double-Lambda hypernuclei, there are interesting parallels worth noting, and the attractive Lambda-Lambda interaction is not strong enough to produce a Lambda-Lambda bound state.

## Method
The authors develop a hybrid algorithm combining auxiliary-field quantum Monte Carlo (AFQMC) with worldline Monte Carlo simulations, where the two distinguishable impurities are represented by worldlines in Euclidean projection time while the majority background fermions are treated as explicit degrees of freedom. The impurities are integrated out from the lattice action, producing a reduced transfer matrix that acts only on the background particles, with the worldline positions updated using Metropolis moves. The method is applied to non-relativistic three-dimensional systems on a periodic cubic lattice with zero-range impurity-impurity and impurity-majority interactions, using a spatial lattice spacing of 1/a = 100 MeV and temporal lattice spacing of 1/a_t = 300 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Spatial lattice spacing: 1/a = 100 MeV; temporal lattice spacing: 1/a_t = 300 MeV; lattice spacing ratio alpha = a_t/a = 1/3
- Lattice size: L = 10 in units of the spatial lattice spacing a
- Fermion mass: m = 938.92 MeV (average nucleon mass)
- Impurity-background scattering length tuned to 3 fm, comparable to hyperon-nucleon interaction scattering length
- For attractive impurity-impurity interaction, the N=4 ground state energy drops below the N=3 ground state at approximately C_II = 0.02, where the N=6 and N=8 ground states also cross below the N=4 and N=6 states respectively

## Context
This work extends the ILMC method previously developed for single impurities (Elhatisari, 2014; Bour, 2014) and applied to Lambda hyperons in nuclear lattice effective field theory (Frame, 2020), where the computational effort was found to scale approximately linearly with the number of nucleons. The two-impurity extension enables future studies of double-Lambda hypernuclei within NLEFT, as reviewed by Gal (2016) and Hiyama (2018), and also connects to studies of two heavy impurities in a Fermi bath (Sighinolfi, 2021). The benchmark calculation validates the method against exact Lanczos calculations and the established NLEFT code for the triton binding energy.
