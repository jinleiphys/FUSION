# Correlations of mixed systems in confining backgrounds

> We show that the entanglement of purification and the critical distance between the two mixed systems is a powerful measure in probing the phase structures of QCD and confining backgrounds, as it can distinguish the scale of chiral symmetry breaking versus the scale of confinement/deconfinement phase transitions. For two symmetric strips with equal and finite width and infinite length, and in the background of several confining geometries, we numerically calculate the critical distance between them where the mutual information vanishes and show that this quantity can probe the very rich phase structures of these backgrounds. The geometries that we study here are AdS-soliton, Witten Sakai Sugimoto and deformed Sakai-Sugimoto, Witten-QCD, Klebanov-Strassler, Klebanov-Tseytlin, Klebanov-Witten, Maldacena-Nunez, Nunez-Legramandi metric, and Domain-Wall QCD model. For each background we also present the relation for the entanglement of purification. Finally, we show that the Crofton forms of these geometries also behave in a universal form where a "well" is being observed around the IR wall, and therefore for all confining backgrounds, the Crofton form would also be capable of distinguishing the confining versus conformal backgrounds as it is also a tool in the reconstruction of various bulk geometries.

## Key claim

The critical distance between two mixed strips, where mutual information vanishes, can distinguish the scale of chiral symmetry breaking from the scale of confinement/deconfinement phase transitions in holographic QCD models, going beyond what single-strip measures can achieve.

## Method

For two symmetric strips with equal finite width and infinite length, the author numerically calculates the critical distance where mutual information drops to zero across ten confining geometries: AdS-soliton, Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto, deformed Sakai-Sugimoto, Witten-QCD, Klebanov-Strassler, Klebanov-Tseytlin, Klebanov-Witten, Maldacena-Nunez, Nunez-Legramandi metric, and Domain-Wall QCD model. The entanglement entropy for connected and disconnected solutions is computed using the generalized formula that includes the non-constant dilaton and internal volume factors, and the critical distance is found by solving I(D_c,l)=0 where I is the mutual information.

## Context

This work extends the use of quantum information measures to probe QCD phase structures, continuing the author's previous work (Ghodrati 2018) which used complexity to probe Gubser's models of QCD. The critical distance for two strips is shown to be a stronger probe than the critical width for one strip, which could only distinguish confinement/deconfinement transitions. The results are compatible with the behavior of logarithmic negativity in end-of-the-world brane setups (Dong, McBride, Weng 2021), and the phase structure follows the pattern first presented by Jain (2020). The Crofton forms of all confining geometries show a universal well around the IR wall, enabling distinction between confining and conformal backgrounds for bulk reconstruction.
