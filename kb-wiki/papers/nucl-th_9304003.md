# Collective Modes in a Slab of Interacting Nuclear Matter: The effects of finite range interactions

> We consider a slab of nuclear matter and investigate the collective excitations, which develop in the response function of the system. We introduce a finite-range realistic interaction among the nucleons, which reproduces the full G-matrix by a linear combination of gaussian potentials in the various spin-isospin channels. We then analyze the collective modes of the slab in the S=T=1 channel: for moderate momenta hard and soft zero-sound modes are found, which exhaust most of the excitation strength. At variance with the results obtained with a zero range force, new "massive" excitations are found for the vector-isovector channel .

## Key claim

Using a finite-range realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction (fitted to the G-matrix) in a slab of nuclear matter, the authors find that the collective modes in the S=T=1 channel include not only the expected hard and soft zero-sound modes but also new "massive" excitations at higher energies, which are absent when a zero-range force is employed and which depend critically on the interplay between the interaction range and the slab thickness.

## Method

The authors model a slab of nuclear matter confined in one dimension by an infinite potential well, with translational invariance in the other two dimensions. They solve the Random Phase Approximation (RPA) equations for the polarization propagator using a finite-range effective particle-hole interaction derived from a linear combination of gaussian potentials fitted to reproduce the full Brueckner G-matrix (from the Bethe-Goldstone equation) in each spin-isospin channel. The collective modes are identified from poles in the real part of the RPA polarization propagator and peaks in its imaginary part, which gives the response function.

## Key numbers

- Fermi momentum fixed at k_F = 1.36 fm^{-1}.
- In infinite nuclear matter, the zero-sound velocity is v_s^{n.m.} = 1.62 v_F (where v_F = 0.286 c); with a delta-force of approximately the same strength at q=0, it would be 1.71 v_F.
- For the M=2 slab (thinner), the parallel mode velocity is v_parallel = 1.36 v_F (1.5 v_F with delta-force) and the perpendicular mode velocity is v_perp = 1.54 v_F (1.89 v_F with delta-force).
- For the M=8 slab (thicker, L approximately 18 fm), the parallel mode velocity is v_parallel = 1.56 v_F (1.67 v_F with delta-force) and the perpendicular mode velocity is v_perp = 1.58 v_F (1.79 v_F with delta-force).
- The new "massive" modes appear at higher energies, do not vanish in the zero-momentum limit, and show a weak quadratic dependence; in the M=8 slab only the lowest energy mode displays significant strength.

## Context

This work extends the slab nuclear matter model of Alberico, Czerski, Manfredi, and Molinari (1991), which used a zero-range Landau-Migdal force, by introducing a finite-range realistic interaction derived from G-matrix calculations of Dickhoff (1983) and Czerski, Dickhoff, Faessler, and Muther (1984). The new massive excitations, which resemble optical phonons or plasmons, arise only when the interaction range (2-3 fm) is comparable to the slab thickness and disappear in the infinite nuclear matter limit; they may offer insights for experimental research in highly deformed nuclei and are analogous to modes in Wigner lattices and spin systems.
