# Constraining the dense matter equation of state with new NICER mass-radius measurements and new chiral effective field theory inputs

> Pulse profile modeling of X-ray data from NICER is now enabling precision inference of neutron star mass and radius. Combined with nuclear physics constraints from chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT), and masses and tidal deformabilities inferred from gravitational wave detections of binary neutron star mergers, this has lead to a steady improvement in our understanding of the dense matter equation of state (EOS). Here we consider the impact of several new results: the radius measurement for the 1.42$\,M_\odot$ pulsar PSR J0437$-$4715 presented by Choudhury et al. (2024), updates to the masses and radii of PSR J0740$+$6620 and PSR J0030$+$0451, and new $χ$EFT results for neutron star matter up to 1.5 times nuclear saturation density. Using two different high-density EOS extensions -- a piecewise-polytropic (PP) model and a model based on the speed of sound in a neutron star (CS) -- we find the radius of a 1.4$\,M_\odot$ (2.0$\,M_\odot$) neutron star to be constrained to the 95% credible ranges $12.28^{+0.50}_{-0.76}\,$km ($12.33^{+0.70}_{-1.34}\,$km) for the PP model and $12.01^{+0.56}_{-0.75}\,$km ($11.55^{+0.94}_{-1.09}\,$km) for the CS model. The maximum neutron star mass is predicted to be $2.15^{+0.14}_{-0.16}\,$$M_\odot$ and $2.08^{+0.28}_{-0.16}\,$$M_\odot$ for the PP and CS model, respectively. We explore the sensitivity of our results to different orders and different densities up to which $χ$EFT is used, and show how the astrophysical observations provide constraints for the pressure at intermediate densities. Moreover, we investigate the difference $R_{2.0} - R_{1.4}$ of the radius of 2$\,M_\odot$ and 1.4$\,M_\odot$ neutron stars within our EOS inference.

## Key claim

The paper finds that combining new NICER mass-radius measurements (including the 1.42 solar mass pulsar PSR J0437-4715) with new chiral effective field theory calculations up to 1.5 times nuclear saturation density constrains neutron star radii to approximately 12 km with uncertainties of about 0.5-1 km at 95% credibility, though the exact values depend on the choice of high-density equation of state extension (piecewise-polytropic versus speed-of-sound model) and the density up to which chiral effective field theory is trusted.

## Method

The authors performed Bayesian inference using the NEoST framework, combining posterior distributions from NICER pulse profile modeling of three pulsars (PSR J0437-4715, PSR J0740+6620, and PSR J0030+0451) with gravitational wave data from GW170817 and GW190425 and new chiral effective field theory calculations of neutron star matter in beta equilibrium up to 1.5 times nuclear saturation density. They used two different high-density equation of state parameterizations: a piecewise-polytropic model with three segments and a speed-of-sound parameterization, both matched to the chiral effective field theory bands at a transition density of either 1.1 or 1.5 times nuclear saturation density.

## Key numbers

- Radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star: 12.28 (+0.50, -0.76) km at 95% credible range for the piecewise-polytropic model, and 12.01 (+0.56, -0.75) km for the speed-of-sound model
- Radius of a 2.0 solar mass neutron star: 12.33 (+0.70, -1.34) km at 95% credible range for the piecewise-polytropic model, and 11.55 (+0.94, -1.09) km for the speed-of-sound model
- Maximum neutron star mass: 2.15 (+0.14, -0.16) solar masses for the piecewise-polytropic model, and 2.08 (+0.28, -0.16) solar masses for the speed-of-sound model
- PSR J0437-4715 mass from radio timing: 1.418 +/- 0.044 solar masses (Reardon 2024)
- PSR J0437-4715 radius from NICER: 11.36 (+0.95, -0.63) km at 68% credible interval (Choudhury 2024)
- PSR J0740+6620 mass from radio timing: 2.08 +/- 0.07 solar masses (Fonseca 2021)
- PSR J0740+6620 radius from NICER: 12.49 (-0.88, +1.28) km at 68% credible interval (Salmi 2024)
- Chiral effective field theory polytropic parameters for N3LO band: K in [2.207, 3.056] MeV fm^-3 and Gamma in [2.361, 2.814]

## Context

This work extends the multimessenger equation of state inference framework of Raaijmakers (2021) by incorporating new NICER results for PSR J0437-4715 from Choudhury (2024), updated analyses for PSR J0740+6620 from Salmi (2024) and PSR J0030+0451 from Vinciguerra (2024), and new chiral effective field theory calculations from Keller (2023) that extend to 1.5 times nuclear saturation density. The results enable tighter constraints on the pressure of neutron-rich matter at intermediate densities and provide improved predictions for neutron star radii that can be tested by future NICER observations and gravitational wave detections.
