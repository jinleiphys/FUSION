# First-principle calculations of Dark Matter scattering off light nuclei

> We study the scattering of Dark Matter particles off various light nuclei within the framework of chiral effective field theory. We focus on scalar interactions and include one- and two-nucleon scattering processes whose form and strength are dictated by chiral symmetry. The nuclear wave functions are calculated from chiral effective field theory interactions as well and we investigate the convergence pattern of the chiral expansion in the nuclear potential and the Dark Matter-nucleus currents. This allows us to provide a systematic uncertainty estimate of our calculations. We provide results for ${}^2$H, ${}^3$H, and ${}^3$He nuclei which are theoretically interesting and the latter is a potential target for experiments. We show that two-nucleon currents can be systematically included but are generally smaller than predicted by power counting and suffer from significant theoretical uncertainties even in light nuclei. We demonstrate that accurate high-order wave functions are necessary in order to incorporate two-nucleon currents. We discuss scenarios in which one-nucleon contributions are suppressed such that higher-order currents become dominant.

## Key claim

The authors show that two-nucleon currents in scalar Dark Matter scattering off light nuclei are systematically included within chiral effective field theory but are generally smaller than predicted by power counting and suffer from significant theoretical uncertainties, even for light nuclei such as deuteron, triton, and helium-3.

## Method

The authors derived one- and two-nucleon Dark Matter currents from chiral effective field theory for scalar interactions, using nuclear wave functions obtained from chiral effective field theory interactions at different chiral orders (LO, NLO, N2LO). They solved bound-state equations for 2H, 3H, and 3He nuclei and varied both the chiral order of the nuclear potential and the coordinate-space cutoff to estimate theoretical uncertainties in the Dark Matter-nucleus cross sections.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon sigma term: sigma_piN = (59.1 +/- 3.5) MeV, from a Roy-Steiner analysis of pion-nucleon scattering
- Strong proton-neutron mass splitting: delta m_N = (2.32 +/- 0.17) MeV, from lattice-QCD calculations
- Strange sigma term: sigma_s = (40 +/- 10) MeV, from an average of lattice-QCD calculations
- Momentum-dependent strange form factor slope: dot sigma_s = (0.3 +/- 0.2) GeV^{-1}, from a dispersive extraction
- Pion decay constant: f_pi = 92.4 MeV
- Quark mass ratio parameter: epsilon = (m_d - m_u)/(m_d + m_u) = 0.37 +/- 0.03

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory approach to Dark Matter scattering previously applied to heavier nuclei by Klos (2013) and Hoferichter (2016), who performed shell-model calculations of structure factors for nuclei like 132Xe. The authors provide first-principle calculations for light nuclei (2H, 3H, 3He) using consistent chiral wave functions, complementing the recent work by Gazda (2016) on 4He which used fixed-order chiral wave functions with one-nucleon currents not derived from chiral perturbation theory. This enables systematic uncertainty estimates for Dark Matter scattering off light nuclei, which are potential experimental targets for low-mass Dark Matter detection below 10 GeV.
