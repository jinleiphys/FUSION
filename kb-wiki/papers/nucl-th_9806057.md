# Reinvestigation of $pp\to ppπ^0$ and $pp\to ppη$ at threshold

> $pp\to ppπ^0$ and $pp\to ppη$ were considered close to threshold. The pion production was found not only to be dominated by impulse approximation (IA) and rescattering contributions, but also strongly by box-diagrams, resonances and heavy-meson exchanges. New relevant contributions for the IA were found. The role of the offshell behaviour of nucleon-resonance couplings ($P_{11}(1440), S_{11}(1535)$) and of interference effects for the eta production was investigated. The results opened a large door for microsopic understanding of short range physics. A covariant model for $pp\to ppη$ being under construction is going to be finished.

# Reinvestigation of $pp\to pp\pi^0$ and $pp\to pp\eta$ at threshold

## Key claim

The complex production mechanism of $pp\to pp\pi^0$ is still not clearly understood, as the total cross section calculated with the full model (including impulse approximation, rescattering, box diagrams, resonances, and heavy-meson exchanges) is about a factor 0.042 smaller than experiment at $\eta_\pi = 0.31$, mostly due to the large heavy-meson exchange contribution. For $pp\to pp\eta$, the observations of Moalem et al. (1995) are confirmed, but the mechanisms leading to the absolute magnitude of the total cross section are very nontrivial, with the relative signs between meson-exchange amplitudes no longer fixed by hand but derived from a quark-model calculation of the coupling real parts.

## Method

The short-range production part of the matrix elements was multiplicatively separated from the long-range initial and final state interactions using the Watson-Migdal approach, with the total cross section expressed as a product of a FSI-modified three-body phase space integral and the squared sum of amplitudes from impulse approximation (including a newly identified irreducible contribution with one exchange pion), non-resonant S-wave rescattering, resonant $S_{11}(1535)$ and $P_{11}(1440)$ contributions, heavy-meson ($\sigma$ and $\omega$) exchanges, and $\pi\pi$ box diagrams (both crossed and non-crossed, with intermediate $N$ and $\Delta$ states). For the $\eta$ production, the real parts of the meson-nucleon-resonance couplings $g_{\lambda NN^\ast}$ were calculated from a quark model where mesons couple to quarks in the proton and resonances are treated as harmonic oscillators with a single range parameter $a$, while the imaginary parts were obtained from nonrelativistic lowest-order meson loop corrections.

## Key numbers

- The total cross section for $pp\to pp\pi^0$ calculated with FSI corrections (Reid-Soft-Core and Coulomb potential) is a factor 0.042 smaller than experiment at $\eta_\pi = 0.31$.
- On-shell meson-nucleon-resonance couplings calculated from branching ratios: $|f_{\pi NP_{11}}| = 0.17$, $|f_{\eta NP_{11}}| = 0.66$, $|f_{\rho NP_{11}}| = 0.13$, $|g_{\pi NS_{11}}| = 0.77$, $|g_{\eta NS_{11}}| = 2.08$, $|g_{\rho NS_{11}}| = 0.22$.
- Threshold matrix elements for heavy-meson exchanges: $\langle \mathcal{M}_{\sigma} \rangle \simeq -208.7$ fm and $\langle \mathcal{M}_{\omega} \rangle \simeq 406.7$ fm.
- The harmonic oscillator range parameter $a$ is about $(0.5\ \text{fm})^{-1}$.
- The mixing angle $\theta$ between spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 components in the $S_{11}(1535)$ resonance is favoured at $\theta = -5^\circ$.

## Context

This work extends the meson-exchange model calculations of Moalem et al. (1995) for $pp\to pp\eta$ by replacing the ad-hoc fixing of relative signs between amplitudes with a quark-model derivation of the real parts of the meson-nucleon-resonance couplings. For $pp\to pp\pi^0$, it contradicts earlier heavy-meson exchange results by finding opposite signs for the $\sigma$ and $\omega$ contributions compared to previous publications, and reveals that the production mechanism remains poorly understood despite including many additional diagrams. The results motivate a covariant model for $pp\to pp\eta$ under construction, and the method for measuring the mixing angle $\theta$ in the $S_{11}(1535)$ resonance could enable future experimental constraints on short-range nuclear physics.
