# Producing $Λ(1405)$ and $Λ(1520)$ in $π^-p$ reaction to explore their inner structures

> In this work, the production mechanisms of the hyperon resonances $Λ(1405)$ and $Λ(1520)$ in the $π^- p$ scattering are investigated within an effective Lagrangian approach incorporating Regge trajectories. By including contributions from $t$-channel $K^*$ and $u$-channel $Σ$ exchanges, we perform global fits to the total and differential cross sections for $π^{-} p \rightarrow KΛ(1405)$ and $π^{-} p \rightarrow KΛ(1520)$. The results show good agreement with available experimental data. For the total cross section of $Λ(1405)$ production, the $u$-channel contribution is dominant, whereas the $t$-channel contribution plays the primary role in $Λ(1520)$ production. Furthermore, the differential cross sections of the two processes exhibit distinctly different shapes, reflecting their distinct underlying reaction mechanisms. An analysis based on the constituent counting rule indicates that $Λ(1520)$ is consistent with a conventional three-quark configuration, while $Λ(1405)$ shows a clear deviation, suggesting a more exotic structure. Owing to the large branching ratio of $Λ^* \to πΣ$, the Dalitz process $π^{-} p \rightarrow K Λ^{*} \rightarrow K πΣ$ is also calculated. Our results demonstrate that reconstructing $Λ^*$ via the $KπΣ$ final state is experimentally feasible. This study provides important theoretical insights into the production dynamics of these hyperon resonances, and suggests future high-precision measurements of the $t$-distribution at large momentum transfer at facilities such as AMBER, J-PARC, HIKE, and HIAF, which can further clarify their reaction mechanisms and structural properties.

## Key claim

The constituent counting rule analysis indicates that $\Lambda(1520)$ is consistent with a conventional three-quark configuration, while $\Lambda(1405)$ shows a clear deviation from both three-quark and five-quark expectations, suggesting a more exotic structure possibly influenced by unquenched effects.

## Method

The authors employed an effective Lagrangian approach incorporating Regge trajectories for the $t$-channel $K^*$ exchange and $u$-channel $\Sigma$ exchange contributions to $\pi^- p \rightarrow K\Lambda(1405)$ and $\pi^- p \rightarrow K\Lambda(1520)$. They performed global fits to total and differential cross section data from Dahl (1967), Crennell (1972), and Thomas (1973) using the MINUIT code, treating the coupling constants $g_{K^*N\Lambda(1405)}$ and $g_{K^*N\Lambda(1520)}$ and cutoff parameters $\Lambda_t$ and $\Lambda_u$ as free parameters. The constituent counting rule was then applied to the calculated differential cross sections at $\theta_{\mathrm{c.m.}}=90^\circ$ to extract the scaling exponent $n$ and infer the internal quark configurations.

## Key numbers

- For $\pi^- p \rightarrow K\Lambda(1405)$: fitted parameters $\Lambda_t = 1.856 \pm 0.051$, $\Lambda_u = 1.345 \pm 0.056$, $g_{K^*N\Lambda(1405)} = 2.476 \pm 0.370$, with $\chi^2/\mathrm{d.o.f.} = 2.343$.
- For $\pi^- p \rightarrow K\Lambda(1520)$: fitted parameters $\Lambda_t = 1.488 \pm 0.036$, $\Lambda_u = 0.514 \pm 0.040$, $g_{K^*N\Lambda(1520)} = 13.200 \pm 1.883$, with $\chi^2/\mathrm{d.o.f.} = 1.259$.
- The coupling constant $g_{\pi\Sigma\Lambda(1405)} = 1.58$ was determined from the decay width $\Gamma_{\Lambda(1405)\rightarrow \pi\Sigma} = 50.50$ MeV; $g_{\pi\Sigma\Lambda(1520)} = 2.13$ from $\Gamma_{\Lambda(1520)\rightarrow \pi\Sigma} = 6.61$ MeV.
- For $\pi^- p \rightarrow K\Lambda(1520)$ at $\theta_{\mathrm{c.m.}}=90^\circ$, the fitted scaling factor $n = 10.291 \pm 0.363$ in the energy range $2.3-2.8$ GeV, consistent with a three-quark configuration ($n=10$).
- For $\pi^- p \rightarrow K\Lambda(1405)$ at $\theta_{\mathrm{c.m.}}=90^\circ$, the fitted scaling factor $n = 7.568 \pm 0.347$ in the energy range $2.3-2.8$ GeV, deviating from both the three-quark expectation ($n=10$) and five-quark expectation ($n=12$).
- The Dalitz process $\pi^- p \rightarrow K\Lambda(1405) \rightarrow K\pi\Sigma$ shows peak values at $M_{\pi\Sigma} = 1405$ MeV not less than 381.97 $\mu$b/GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of hyperon photoproduction by Nam (2004-2008), Sibirtsev (2005), and Xie (2010) to the pion-induced reactions $\pi^- p \rightarrow K\Lambda(1405)$ and $\pi^- p \rightarrow K\Lambda(1520)$, which had received scarce theoretical attention despite available experimental data from Dahl (1967), Crennell (1972), and Thomas (1973). The constituent counting rule analysis, previously applied by the authors to $\pi^- p \rightarrow K^*\Sigma$ reactions, is here used to probe the internal structure of $\Lambda(1405)$ and $\Lambda(1520)$. The results suggest that future high-precision measurements of the $t$-distribution at large momentum transfer at facilities such as AMBER, J-PARC, HIKE, and HIAF could clarify the reaction mechanisms and structural properties, particularly for the exotic hadron candidate $\Lambda(1405)$.
