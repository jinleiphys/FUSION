# Towards a new semi-classical interpretation of the wobbling motion in $^{163}$Lu

> A new interpretation for the wobbling bands in $^{163}$Lu is given within a particle-triaxial rotor semi-classical formalism. While in the previous papers the bands TSD1, TSD2, TSD3 and TSD4 are viewed as the ground, one, two and three phonon wobbling bands, here the corresponding experimental results are described as the ground band with spin equal to I=R+j, for R=0,2,4,...(TSD1), the ground band with I=R+j and R=1,3,5,...(TSD2), the one phonon excitations of TSD2 (TSD3), with the odd proton moving in the orbit $j=i_{13/2}$, and the ground band of I=R+j, with R=1,3,5,... and $j=h_{9/2}$ (TSD4). The moments of inertia (MoI) of the core for the first three bands are the same, and considered to be free parameters. Due to the core polarization effect caused by the particle-core coupling, the MoI's for TSD4 are different. The energies and the e.m. transitions are quantitatively well described. Also, the phase diagram of the odd system is drawn. In the parameter space one indicates where the point associated with the fitted parameters is located and also which is the region of transversal wobbling mode as well as where the wobbling motion is forbidden.

# Towards a new semi-classical interpretation of the wobbling motion in $^{163}$Lu

## Key claim

The four TSD bands in $^{163}$Lu are reinterpreted not as multi-phonon wobbling excitations (ground, one, two, and three phonon bands) but rather as: TSD1 as the ground band with spin I=R+j for even R (R=0,2,4,...), TSD2 as the ground band with odd R (R=1,3,5,...), TSD3 as one-phonon excitations of TSD2, and TSD4 as a ground band with a different proton orbital (j=h9/2), all within a longitudinal wobbling framework, in contrast to the transversal wobbling interpretation proposed by Frauendorf (2006).

## Method

A particle-triaxial rotor Hamiltonian is dequantized via a time-dependent variational principle using a trial function that is a linear combination of states with definite K (since K is not a good quantum number for triaxial nuclei). The classical equations of motion are linearized around the minimum of the classical energy function to obtain two wobbling frequencies (Ω1, Ω1' for j=i13/2; Ω2, Ω2' for j=h9/2). The moments of inertia (MoI), the particle-core coupling strength V, and the triaxial parameter γ are treated as free parameters and fitted to experimental excitation energies via least squares. Electric quadrupole and magnetic dipole transition probabilities are then computed using the resulting wave functions.

## Key numbers

- Fitted moments of inertia for TSD1, TSD2, TSD3 (j=13/2): ℐ1 = 63.2 ℏ²/MeV, ℐ2 = 20 ℏ²/MeV, ℐ3 = 10 ℏ²/MeV
- Fitted moments of inertia for TSD4 (j=9/2): ℐ1 = 67 ℏ²/MeV, ℐ2 = 34.5 ℏ²/MeV, ℐ3 = 50 ℏ²/MeV
- Particle-core coupling strength V = 3.1 MeV for j=13/2 bands, V = 0.7 MeV for TSD4
- Triaxial parameter γ = 17 degrees for all bands
- Single-particle energy difference ε9/2 − ε13/2 = −0.334 MeV
- Fitted quadrupole parameters: Q0 = 18.43 eb, Q2 = 19.81 eb
- Experimental wobbling energy for TSD3 increases from 144 keV at spin 33/2 to 170 keV at spin 77/2, then decreases to 143 keV at spins 81/2 and 85/2; calculated wobbling energy increases from 331 keV at spin 33/2 to 570 keV at spin 85/2
- B(E2) for TSD1 intra-band transition at spin 41/2: theoretical 2.80 e²b², experimental 3.45^{+0.80}_{-0.69} e²b²
- B(E2) for TSD2→TSD1 inter-band transition at spin 47/2: theoretical 0.54 e²b², experimental 0.54^{+0.13}_{-0.11} e²b²
- B(M1) for TSD2→TSD1 transition at spin 47/2: theoretical 0.017 μN², experimental 0.017^{+0.006}_{-0.005} μN²
- Quenching factor for proton gyromagnetic factor: q = 0.43

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous semi-classical formalism (Raduta 2017, 2018) applied to $^{163,165,167}$Lu, which used multi-phonon states to describe the TSD bands. The new interpretation avoids the multi-phonon picture entirely, instead assigning TSD1, TSD2, and TSD3 to different ground-state sequences of the same proton orbital (i13/2) and TSD4 to a different orbital (h9/2). The paper directly addresses the debate between longitudinal and transversal wobbling modes initiated by Frauendorf (2006) and contested by Tanabe (2017). The authors argue that the transversal wobbling regime requires ideal restrictions and that their longitudinal approach reproduces the experimental data well, including the increasing-then-decreasing wobbling energy with spin. The phase diagram constructed from the classical energy function identifies regions where transversal wobbling could occur and where wobbling motion is forbidden, providing a framework for future tests of wobbling character in other odd-mass nuclei.
