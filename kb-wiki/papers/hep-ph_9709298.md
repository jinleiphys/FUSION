# One and two loop anomalous dimensions for the chiral-odd structure function h1

To be published by the World Scientific. Complete postscript file is available at ftp://ftp.cc.saga-u.ac.jp/pub/paper/riko/quantum1 or at http://www.cc.saga-u.ac.jp/saga-u/riko/physics/quantum1/structure.html Email: kumanos@cc.saga-u.ac.jp, 96td25@edu.cc.saga-u.ac.jp

## Key claim

The authors present one- and two-loop anomalous dimensions for the chiral-odd structure function \(h_1\) (transversity distribution), calculated in the Feynman gauge and minimal subtraction scheme, enabling next-to-leading-order (NLO) QCD evolution of \(h_1\) for the first time. The abstract’s claim that these calculations are “completed” is hedged by the fact that the paper is a conference talk summary, and the full two-loop result is given as a closed-form expression whose derivation is only partially detailed, with many diagrams argued to be identical to the unpolarized case.

## Method

The authors compute the renormalization constants \(Z_{O^n}\) for a set of local chiral-odd operators (Eq. 1) using dimensional regularization in \(d=4-\epsilon\) dimensions, then derive the anomalous dimensions \(\gamma_{O^n} = \mu \, \partial (\ln Z_{O^n})/\partial\mu\). One-loop diagrams (Fig. 2) are evaluated directly; two-loop diagrams (Figs. 3 and 4) are evaluated by subtracting one-loop counterterms and extracting the \(1/\epsilon\) singularities, with many contributions shown to be identical to the unpolarized nonsinglet case (Floratos et al., 1977). The final two-loop anomalous dimension is given as a function of the operator moment \(n\), and the corresponding LO and NLO splitting functions are provided in an appendix.

## Key numbers

- One-loop anomalous dimension: \(\gamma_n^{(0)} = 2 C_F \left[ 1 + 4 \sum_{j=1}^{n-1} \frac{1}{j+1} \right]\), where \(C_F = (N_c^2-1)/(2N_c)\).
- Two-loop anomalous dimension \(\gamma_n^{(1)}\) is given as a lengthy expression (Eq. 4) involving sums \(S_1(n)\), \(S_2(n)\), \(S_3'(n/2)\), \(\widetilde S(n)\), \(\hat S(n)\), and terms proportional to \(C_F^2\), \(C_F T_R\), and \(C_F C_G\).
- LO splitting function: \(\Delta_T P_{qq}^{(0)}(x) = C_F \left[ \frac{2x}{(1-x)_+} + \frac{3}{2} \delta(1-x) \right]\).
- NLO splitting functions \(\Delta_T P_{qq}^{(1)}(x)\) and \(\Delta_T P_{q\bar{q}}^{(1)}(x)\) are given in Eqs. (A.2)–(A.4), with explicit coefficients such as \(\frac{3}{8} - \frac{\pi^2}{2} + 6\zeta(3)\) for the \(\delta(1-x)\) term in the \(C_F^2\) part.

## Context

This work extends the leading-order (LO) splitting function and anomalous dimensions for \(h_1\) (previously calculated by the same authors and others) to NLO, making the analysis of \(h_1\) comparable in sophistication to that of unpolarized and longitudinally polarized structure functions. The calculation parallels the nonsinglet unpolarized case of Floratos et al. (1977), exploiting the fact that gluon fields do not contribute directly to the chiral-odd operator’s anomalous dimensions. The results enable NLO QCD evolution of transversity distributions for comparison with upcoming polarized Drell-Yan measurements at RHIC, and provide a basis for future global fits of parton distributions including the transversity sector.
