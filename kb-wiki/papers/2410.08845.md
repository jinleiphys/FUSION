# A further study on the renormalization group aspect of perturbative corrections

> I perform a further study regarding a renormalization-group (RG) issue -- which concerns a wide variety of the so-called perturbative power counting under effective field theories (EFT) -- as pointed out by A. M. Gasparyan and E. Epelbaum [Phys. Rev. C 107, 034001 (2023)]. I show that the issue could originate from a wrong power counting, or from treating those incomplete, truncated amplitudes beyond the degree to which they should be trusted. Meanwhile, under EFT principles, one should always associate the result with an uncertainty that is adequate to its EFT order. One way to accommodate this is to encode its effect in a more general form of contact terms. In this regard, no RG issue is found in the $^3$P$_0$ nucleon-nucleon scattering under the Long and Yang power counting. In contrast, the RG issue under Weinberg's pragmatic proposal remains a problem even with uncertainty taken into account.

# A further study on the renormalization group aspect of perturbative corrections

## Key claim

The renormalization-group issue identified by Gasparyan and Epelbaum (2023) in perturbative power counting under effective field theories can originate either from a wrong power counting or from demanding an accuracy that exceeds the uncertainty prescribed by the effective field theory order; when the theoretical uncertainty is properly taken into account, no RG issue is found in the 3P0 nucleon-nucleon scattering under the Long and Yang power counting, whereas the RG issue under Weinberg's pragmatic proposal remains a problem even with uncertainty included.

## Method

The author analyzes the RG behavior of perturbative corrections in nucleon-nucleon scattering by incorporating theoretical uncertainty into the renormalization procedure through a generalized regulator that is a linear combination of two different regulator forms (sharp cutoff and super-Gaussian). The LO amplitude is obtained by solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation non-perturbatively, and NLO corrections are added via distorted-wave Born approximation. The analysis is applied to a toy model, the Long and Yang power counting in the 3P0 channel, and Weinberg's pragmatic proposal, comparing the width of problematic cutoff regions against the uncertainty allowed by the power counting.

## Key numbers

- The exceptional cutoff for the Long and Yang power counting at NLO occurs between 2.6990 and 2.6991 GeV, a window of approximately 0.1 MeV width at a cutoff around 2.7 GeV.
- Under Weinberg's pragmatic proposal, the problematic cutoff region spans approximately 100-200 MeV for cutoffs around 1000 MeV at both NLO(Q^2) and NNLO(Q^3).
- The toy model with NLO cutoff set to half the LO value (Lambda/2 versus Lambda) produces more than 200% difference in phase shifts at laboratory energies above 100 MeV.
- The nucleon mass used in the Lippmann-Schwinger equation is M = 939 MeV.
- The estimate for the minimum requirement to eliminate problematic cutoffs uses M_lo/M_hi approximately 1/3, giving (1/3)^2 = 1/9 approximately 0.11 for NLO(Q^2).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Gasparyan and Epelbaum (2023), who discovered an RG issue in perturbative treatments and argued that a wide variety of DWBA-based power countings are generally not RG invariant. The study responds to that work by showing that the issue can be resolved in the Long and Yang power counting (Long and Yang, various works 2007-2018) by incorporating theoretical uncertainty, but cannot be resolved in Weinberg's pragmatic proposal (Weinberg, 1990, 1991), which has known RG failures documented by Nogga, Timmermans, and van Kolck (2005) and others. The work enables a refined methodology for testing power countings that distinguishes between genuine power-counting failures and artifacts of demanding excessive precision.
