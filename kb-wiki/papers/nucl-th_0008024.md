# Missing Nucleon Resonances in Kaon Production with Pions and Photons

> Resonances with strong couplings to strangeness channels are investigated in a coupled-channels framework of kaon production with pions and photons. We find evidence for an additional D13 resonance around 1900 MeV that has a significant decay width into the K Lambda final state. Such a state has been predicted in a relativistic quark model at 1960 MeV with significant gamma N and K Lambda branching ratios.

## Key claim

The authors find evidence for an additional D13 resonance around 1900 MeV that has a significant decay width into the K Lambda final state, but they hedge that the remarkable quantitative agreement with the quark model prediction at 1960 MeV is "probably fortuitous" and that only a detailed multipole analysis can verify the observed structure is indeed due to a resonance.

## Method

The authors performed a tree-level isobar model analysis of p(gamma, K+)Lambda photoproduction and a coupled-channels K-matrix approach extending the work of Feuster and Mosel (1998) to energies up to 2.0 GeV, including the K Sigma final state. They simultaneously fitted all available hadronic data for reactions involving pi N, pi pi N, eta N, K Lambda, and K Sigma final states, allowing the fit to determine the mass, width, and coupling constants of candidate resonances predicted by the quark model of Capstick and Roberts (1994, 1998).

## Key numbers

- The new D13 resonance was fitted with a mass of 1895 MeV and a total width of 372 MeV in the tree-level analysis
- The quark model predicted the D13 state at 1960 MeV with a width of 535 MeV
- The extracted coupling strength sqrt(Gamma_{N*N gamma} Gamma_{N*K Lambda})/Gamma_{N*} was 2.292^{+0.722}_{-0.204} x 10^{-3} from the fit, compared to the quark model value of -2.722 +/- 0.729 x 10^{-3}
- In the coupled-channels K-matrix approach, the new D13 state was found at 1945 MeV with a large width of 583 MeV
- The well-known D13(1520) and the 3-star D13(1700) were also included, with the latter fitted at 1706 MeV

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channels analysis of Feuster and Mosel (1998) to higher energies and includes the K Sigma final state, while also building on the tree-level isobar model described in earlier work by the authors. The results support the quark model predictions of Capstick and Roberts (1994, 1998) for a missing D13 resonance around 1960 MeV, and the structure seen in the new SAPHIR data at 1900 MeV is argued to be identical to one previously seen in hadronic K Lambda production analyses by Saxon and by Bell. The work enables future experimental tests through measurement of the photon asymmetry, which the authors predict shows a sign change of almost 100 percent at intermediate angles for W greater than or equal to 1800 MeV when the new resonance is included.
