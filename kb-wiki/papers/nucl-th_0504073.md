# Spin correlations in the reaction "pi \vec D --> \vecΣΘ^+" and the parity of Theta^+

> We analyze two types of spin observables in the reaction $π\vec D\to\vecΣΘ^+$ near the threshold. One concerns the spin-transfer coefficients $K_x^x$ and $K_z^z$. The second one is the deuteron spin anisotropy. These observables are sensitive to the $Θ^+$ parity and can be used as a tool for the $Θ^+$ parity determination.

## Key claim

The spin-transfer coefficients \(K_x^x\) and \(K_z^z\) and the deuteron spin anisotropy in the reaction \(\pi \vec D \to \vec\Sigma \Theta^+\) near threshold are sensitive to the parity of the \(\Theta^+\) pentaquark, but the correlation \(K_x^x(K_z^z)\) depends on the relative phases of partial amplitudes and is not fully model-independent; only the relation between the anisotropy and \(K_z^z\) is model-independent.

## Method

The authors constructed the production amplitude for \(\pi \vec D \to \vec\Sigma \Theta^+\) near threshold assuming suppression of final orbital momenta \(L_f > 0\), expressing it in terms of partial amplitudes \(a^J_L\) with total angular momentum \(J\) and initial orbital momentum \(L\). They derived analytic expressions for the spin-transfer coefficients \(K_x^x\) and \(K_z^z\) and the deuteron spin anisotropy \(\mathcal{A}\) for both positive and negative \(\Theta^+\) parity, using the Pauli spin matrices for spin-1/2 and spin-1 operators. The analysis considered the dominant pole contributions from loop diagrams and weaker background contributions to estimate the relative phases \(\delta^\pm\).

## Key numbers

- For positive \(\Theta^+\) parity with a democratic choice of partial amplitudes \(r = q = 1\): \(K_z^z \simeq 0.75\) and \(K_x^x \simeq \pm 0.61\).
- For negative \(\Theta^+\) parity with \(r = q = 1\): two solutions exist, \(K_x^x \simeq -0.23\) at \(K_z^z \simeq 0.97\) (for positive \(\beta\)) and \(K_x^x \simeq 0.23\) at \(K_z^z \simeq 0.03\) (for negative \(\beta\)).
- For positive \(\Theta^+\) parity with \(r = q = 1\): the deuteron spin anisotropy \(\mathcal{A} \simeq 0.2\).
- For negative \(\Theta^+\) parity with \(r = q = 1\): \(\mathcal{A} \simeq \pm 1\), depending on the phase \(\beta\).
- The allowed range for \(K_z^z\) for negative parity is \(\frac{3 - \sqrt{1 + 8\beta^2}}{6} \leq K_z^z \leq \frac{3 + \sqrt{1 + 8\beta^2}}{6}\), which for \(\beta^2 \simeq 1\) reduces to \(0 \lesssim K_z^z \lesssim 1\).

## Context

This work extends the model-independent method proposed by Thomas, Hicks, and Hosaka (2004) for determining \(\Theta^+\) parity in \(\vec p \vec p \to \Sigma^+ \Theta^+\) to the reaction \(\pi \vec D \to \vec\Sigma \Theta^+\), where the deuteron provides fixed total isospin and spin in the initial state. It parallels earlier analyses of spin-transfer coefficients in \(NN \to Y\Theta^+\) reactions by Hanhart et al. (2004), Rekalo and Tomasi-Gustafsson (2004), Uzikov (2004), and Nam, Hosaka, and Kim (2004), but offers the practical advantage that the deuteron spin anisotropy requires only a polarized deuteron target without measuring the spin of outgoing particles. The results enable experimental parity determination using existing facilities such as SIS/GSI Darmstadt with FOPI and HADES spectrometers.
