# Nuclear Physics on the Light Front

> High energy scattering experiments involving nuclei are typically analyzed in terms of light front variables. The desire to provide realistic, relativistic wave functions expressed in terms of these variables led me to try to use light front dynamics to compute nuclear wave functions. The progress is summarized here.

# Nuclear Physics on the Light Front

## Key claim

Light front dynamics can be used to compute realistic nuclear wave functions and plus-momentum distributions for high-energy scattering experiments, but the approach is still in its infancy and much work remains to achieve satisfactory agreement with deep inelastic scattering data.

## Method

The author applies light front quantization to hadronic Lagrangians (nucleons, vector and scalar mesons, pions) to derive nuclear wave functions in terms of light front variables. The method is tested on infinite nuclear matter in the mean field approximation using the Walecka model, finite nuclei in mean field approximation, nucleon-nucleon scattering via a one-boson-exchange potential derived from a chiral Lagrangian, and nuclear matter with nucleon-nucleon correlations using Hartree-Fock and Brueckner Hartree-Fock equations.

## Key numbers

- Vector mesons carry about one third (0.35 M_N) of the nuclear plus momentum in infinite nuclear matter mean field approximation, with support only at k^+ = 0
- Nucleons carry 65% of the nuclear plus momentum in the mean field approximation
- Nucleons carry 81% of the nuclear plus momentum when nucleon-nucleon correlations are included (neglecting two-particle-two-hole states)
- The computed nuclear matter compressibility is 180 MeV
- There are about 0.05 excess pions per nucleon in correlated nuclear matter
- The minimum value of the ratio F_{2A}/F_{2N} is increased by a factor of twenty toward the data when correlations are included

## Context

This work extends earlier light front quantization methods by Des71 and Yan34 to realistic nuclear physics problems, and builds on the Walecka model results of Bsjdw for nuclear matter. The computed binding energies for finite nuclei are essentially the same as equal-time theory results from Blunden (1999). The approach enables future calculations of plus-momentum distributions needed to analyze deep inelastic scattering, (e,e'p), and (p,pp) reactions, and provides a framework to understand the Drell-Yan data from Alde (1990) showing no enhancement of nuclear pions within 5-10% error.
