# Neutrino neutral-current elastic scattering on 12C

> The neutral current elastic scattering of neutrinos on Carbon and $CH_2$ targets is computed using the relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation with relativistic optical potential. Results for exclusive and inclusive neutrino reactions on ${}^{12}$C target are presented. We show that the nuclear effects on the shape of four-momentum transferred squared distribution $dσ/dQ^2_{QE}$ in neutrino neutral-current and charged-current quasi-elastic scattering are similar. We also calculate flux-averaged neutral current elastic differential cross section $dσ/dQ^2_{QE}$ for neutrino scattering from $CH_2$, as well as, the neutral-current to charged-current cross section ratio as functions of $Q^2_{QE}$. The value of axial mass $M_A$ is extracted from a fit of $dσ/dQ^2_{QE}$ cross section measured in MiniBooNE experiment. The extracted value of $M_A=1.28\pm 0.05$ GeV is consistent within errors with the MiniBooNE result. Additionally, for proton kinetic energies above the Cherenkov threshold, the strange quark contribution to the neutral current axial vector form factor at $Q^2_{QE}=0$, $Δs$, was extracted from a fit of MiniBoone data for $νp \to νp$ to $νN \to νN$ cross section ratio. This value is found to be $Δs=-0.11\pm 0.36$

## Key claim

The authors compute neutrino neutral-current elastic scattering on carbon and CH₂ targets using the relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) with a relativistic optical potential, finding that the extracted axial mass M_A = 1.28 ± 0.05 GeV is consistent within errors with the MiniBooNE result, and that the extracted strange quark contribution Δs = -0.11 ± 0.36 is consistent with other measurements but carries large uncertainty.

## Method

The authors employed the relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) with the EDAD1 parametrization of the relativistic optical potential to compute exclusive and inclusive neutral-current elastic scattering cross sections on ¹²C and CH₂ targets. They calculated flux-averaged differential cross sections dσ/dQ²_QE and the neutral-current to charged-current quasi-elastic cross section ratio, then performed fits to MiniBooNE data to extract the axial mass M_A and the strange quark contribution Δs to the neutral-current axial vector form factor.

## Key numbers

- Extracted axial mass M_A = 1.28 ± 0.05 GeV from a fit of the flux-averaged dσ/dQ²_QE cross section measured in the MiniBooNE experiment
- Extracted strange quark contribution Δs = -0.11 ± 0.36 from a fit of MiniBooNE data for the νp → νp to νN → νN cross section ratio, with χ²_min = 33.4 for 29 degrees of freedom
- At Q²_QE < 0.25 (GeV/c)², the calculation underestimates both the cross section and the NCE/CCQE ratio by 17% or less
- The σ_p/σ_n NCE cross section ratio increases from approximately 0.7 for T_N ≈ 20-50 MeV up to approximately 0.82 for T_N = 700 MeV for neutrinos

## Context

This work extends previous RDWIA calculations of charged-current quasi-elastic scattering by Butkevich and collaborators (2005-2009) to neutral-current elastic scattering on carbon, and compares with earlier RDWIA calculations by Meucci et al. (2003-2004) and Martinez et al. (2006). The results enable improved interpretation of MiniBooNE neutral-current data for extracting the strange quark contribution to the nucleon spin and for searches for sterile neutrino oscillations, where the neutral-current event rate would be affected by ν_active → ν_s transitions.
