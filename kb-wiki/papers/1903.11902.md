# Nucleon Structure from Lattice QCD Calculations

> Parton distribution and correlation functions describe the relation between a hadron and the quarks and gluons (or collectively, the partons) within it, and carry rich information on hadron's partonic structure that cannot be calculated by QCD perturbation theory. In this talk, I will review what lattice QCD can and cannot do for calculating the parton distribution and correlation functions, and the new ideas and efforts around the world to explore nucleon structure from lattice QCD calculations by combining the strength of both lattice QCD and perturbative QCD in such a way that is complementary to our on-going effort to extract these fundamental functions from experimental data.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD cannot directly calculate parton distribution functions (PDFs) because the relevant operators live on the light-cone and are time-dependent in Minkowski space, while lattice calculations are performed in Euclidean space; however, new approaches such as quasi-PDFs, pseudo-PDFs, and good lattice cross sections can extract x-dependent PDFs by combining lattice QCD with perturbative QCD matching, provided technical challenges like power-divergent renormalization and the need for large hadron momenta are overcome.

## Method

The paper reviews three main lattice QCD approaches for extracting parton distribution and correlation functions: (1) quasi-PDFs, which compute hadron matrix elements of parton operators along the spatial z-direction at finite hadron momentum P_z, then match to light-cone PDFs via perturbative coefficients; (2) pseudo-PDFs, which define a ratio of matrix elements that cancels power UV divergence and uses the Ioffe-time dependence to access PDFs; (3) good lattice cross sections, which use current-current correlators that are renormalizable and factorizable to PDFs, analogous to how experimental cross sections are analyzed. The review also covers the traditional approach of computing Mellin moments from local twist-2 operators, which is limited to the lowest few moments.

## Context

This review extends the long-standing effort to determine PDFs from QCD global analyses by collaborations such as MMHT (Harland-Lang, 2014), CT (Dulat, 2015), NNPDF (Ball, 2017), HERAPDF (Alekhin, 2017), and JAM (Ethier, 2017), which have large uncertainties at both large and small x. The new lattice approaches build on Ji's (2013) proposal for quasi-PDFs, Radyushkin's (2017) pseudo-PDFs, and Ma and Qiu's (2014, 2017) good lattice cross section formalism, and enable complementary access to kinematic regimes hard to reach experimentally (such as large x) and to hadrons like the free neutron that are difficult to study with experiments.
