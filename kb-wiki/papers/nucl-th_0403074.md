# Indication of quark deconfinement and evidence for a Hubble flow in 130 and 200 GeV Au+Au collisions

> Buda-Lund hydro model fits are compared to BRAHMS, PHENIX, PHOBOS and STAR data on identified particle spectra, two-particle Bose-Einstein or HBT correlations, charged particle pseudorapidity distributions and pseudorapidity as well as p_t dependent elliptic flow in sqrt(s_NN) = 130 and 200 GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC. Preliminary results indicate that 7/8-th of the particle emitting volume is rather cold, with surface temperature of 105 MeV, but the temperature has a distribution and the most central 1/8-th of the volume is superheated to temperatures above the critical value, T(x) > T_c = 172 \pm 3 MeV.

## Key claim

The Buda-Lund hydro model fits to RHIC Au+Au data indicate that the central temperature of the emitting source exceeds the lattice QCD critical temperature for deconfinement (T_c = 172 ± 3 MeV), but this result is statistically significant only for the 130 GeV data (more than 5 sigma) while for the 200 GeV data the excess is only 2 sigma and thus not statistically significant.

## Method

The authors performed simultaneous Buda-Lund hydrodynamical model fits to BRAHMS, PHENIX, PHOBOS, and STAR data on identified particle spectra, two-particle Bose-Einstein (HBT) correlations, charged particle pseudorapidity distributions, and pseudorapidity- and p_t-dependent elliptic flow in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 130 and 200 GeV. The model parameters were extracted by fitting the data with an ellipsoidally symmetric, three-dimensional Hubble flow profile, and the resulting central temperature T_0 was compared to the lattice QCD critical temperature T_c.

## Key numbers

- Central temperature T_0 = 214 ± 7 MeV for 0-5(6)% central Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 130 GeV, exceeding T_c = 172 ± 3 MeV by more than 5 sigma
- Central temperature T_0 = 196 ± 13 MeV for 0-30% central Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, exceeding T_c by 2 sigma (not statistically significant)
- Surface temperature T_s = 105 MeV for the 200 GeV fit, with 7/8 of the particle emitting volume at this temperature
- Critical temperature from lattice QCD: T_c = 172 ± 3 MeV (Fodor and Katz, 2002)
- Freeze-out proper time tau_0 = 5.8 ± 0.3 fm/c (200 GeV) and 6.0 ± 0.2 fm/c (130 GeV)
- Chi-squared per number of degrees of freedom: 114/208 (200 GeV) and 158.2/180 (130 GeV)

## Context

This work extends the Buda-Lund hydro model fits previously applied to CERN SPS Pb+Pb collisions (Ster et al., 1999), where central temperatures were found below T_c, to the higher RHIC energies where deconfinement is indicated. The results confirm and refine the earlier lattice QCD calculation of the phase diagram by Fodor and Katz (2002), which found a cross-over transition at T_c = 172 ± 3 MeV. The finding of a three-dimensional Hubble flow profile and a superheated central region enables further studies of the quark-gluon plasma equation of state and the nature of the QCD phase transition in heavy-ion collisions.
