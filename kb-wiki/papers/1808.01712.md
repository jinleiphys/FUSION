# Non-perturbative collective inertias for fission: a comparative study

> The non-perturbative method to compute Adiabatic Time Dependent Hartree Fock Bogoliubov (ATDHFB) collective inertias is extended to the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) including the case of density dependent forces. The two inertias schemes are computed along the fission path of the $^{234}$U and compared with the perturbative results. We find that the non-perturbative schemes predict very similar collective inertias with a much richer structure than the one predicted by perturbative calculations. Moreover, the non-perturbative inertias show an extraordinary similitude with the exact GCM inertias computed numerically from the energy overlap. These results indicate that the non-perturbative inertias provide the right structure as a function of the collective variable and only a phenomenological factor is required to mock up the exact GCM inertia, bringing new soundness to the microscopic description of fission.

## Key claim

The non-perturbative ATDHFB and GCM-GOA collective inertias for fission show a much richer peak structure than perturbative calculations and are remarkably similar to the exact GCM inertia, differing only by an almost constant factor; this suggests that the non-perturbative schemes provide the correct structure as a function of the collective variable and only a phenomenological factor is needed to reproduce the exact GCM inertia.

## Method

The authors extended the non-perturbative cranking method for computing collective inertias from the ATDHFB framework to the GCM with the Gaussian overlap approximation (GOA), including density-dependent forces. They computed the ATDHFB non-perturbative, GCM-GOA non-perturbative, and exact GCM-GOA inertias along the fission path of ²³⁴U using the Gogny force, and compared these with the traditional perturbative (cranking) inertias. The exact GCM-GOA inertia was obtained by numerically evaluating the second derivatives of the Hamiltonian and norm overlaps using finite difference formulas.

## Key numbers

- The normal deformed minimum of ²³⁴U occurs at Q₂₀ = 12 b (β₂ = 0.25), followed by a fission isomer at Q₂₀ = 40 b (β₂ = 0.71) with an excitation energy of 4.2 MeV.
- The second fission barrier has a height of 9.5 MeV.
- The ATDHFB non-perturbative inertia is larger than the GCM-GOA non-perturbative inertia by a factor around 1.5 over most of the deformation range up to Q₂₀ > 126 b, after which the ratio approaches 1.
- The GCM-GOA non-perturbative inertia divided by the exact GCM-GOA inertia is approximately 0.6, virtually independent of Q₂₀.
- The ratio of perturbative to exact GCM-GOA masses varies with quadrupole deformation, with discrepancies as large as a factor of 5 in some cases.

## Context

This work extends the non-perturbative cranking approach introduced by Baran et al. (2011) from the ATDHFB framework to the GCM-GOA framework, and provides the first comparison of non-perturbative and exact GCM-GOA inertias for a fissioning nucleus. The results resolve uncertainties arising from the choice between ATDHFB and GCM-GOA theoretical frameworks and between perturbative and non-perturbative approximations, showing that non-perturbative schemes converge toward the exact solution. This enables more reliable microscopic calculations of spontaneous fission lifetimes and addresses questions such as the existence of nuclei beyond oganesson, as discussed by Nazarewicz (2018).
