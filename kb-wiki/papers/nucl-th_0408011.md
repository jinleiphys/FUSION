# A quark model framework for the study of nuclear medium effects

> A quark-model framework for studying nuclear medium effects on nucleon resonances is described and applied here to pion photoproduction on the deuteron, which is the simplest composite nucleon system and serves as a first test case. Pion photoproduction on nuclei is discussed within a chiral constituent quark model in which the quark degrees of freedom are explicitly introduced through an effective chiral Lagrangian for the quark-pseudoscalar-meson coupling. The advantage of this model is that a complete set of nucleon resonances can be systematically included with a limited number of parameters. Also, the systematic description of the nucleon and its resonances at quark level allows us to self-consistently relate the nuclear medium's influence on the baryon properties to the intrinsic dynamic aspects of the baryons. As the simplest composite nucleus, the deuteron represents the first application of this effective theory for meson photoproduction on light nuclei. The influence of the medium on the transition operators for a free nucleon is investigated in the Delta resonance region. No evidence is found for a change of the Delta properties in the pion photoproduction reaction on the deuteron since the nuclear medium here involves just one other nucleon and the low binding energy implies low nuclear density. However, we show that the reaction mechanism is in principle sensitive to changes of Delta properties that would be produced by the denser nuclear medium of heavier nuclei through the modification of the quark model parameters.

# A quark model framework for the study of nuclear medium effects

## Key claim
The paper presents a quark-model framework for studying nuclear medium effects on nucleon resonances, applied to pion photoproduction on the deuteron. No evidence is found for a change of the Delta properties in this reaction on the deuteron because the nuclear medium involves just one other nucleon and the low binding energy implies low nuclear density; however, the reaction mechanism is in principle sensitive to changes of Delta properties that would be produced by the denser nuclear medium of heavier nuclei through modification of the quark model parameters.

## Method
The authors employ a chiral constituent quark model with an effective chiral Lagrangian for quark-pseudoscalar-meson coupling, where quark degrees of freedom are explicitly introduced. The model is applied to the direct scattering process in gamma d -> pi0 d at the Delta resonance region (E_gamma = 300 MeV), using the Paris model wavefunction for the deuteron and comparing with a Hulthén wavefunction. The transition operators derived from free nucleon reactions are used in the impulse approximation, and the sensitivity to medium effects is tested by varying the quark model potential strength alpha_h while fixing the constituent quark mass.

## Key numbers
- Photon energy considered: E_gamma = 300 MeV
- Quark potential strength in free nucleon reaction: alpha_h = 330.31 MeV
- Test change of quark potential strength to: bar_alpha_h = 342 MeV
- Resulting Delta mass increase: about 36 MeV
- Resulting Delta total width change: from Gamma_Delta = 120 MeV to bar_Gamma_Delta = 156.7 MeV

## Context
This work extends the chiral constituent quark model previously applied to single nucleon pion photoproduction (Zhao, Al-Khalili, Li, Saghai, 2002) to the simplest composite nucleus, the deuteron. It contrasts with isobaric models (Blomqvist and Laget, 1977; Bosted and Laget, 1978) and hadronic dynamical approaches (Garcilazo, 1995) by providing a quark-level prescription with fewer parameters. The framework enables future systematic investigation of medium effects on heavier nuclei and extension to the second resonance region (P11(1440), D13(1520), S11(1535)).
