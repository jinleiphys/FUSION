# Transport with three-particle interaction

> Starting from a point - like two - and three - particle interaction the kinetic equation is derived. While the drift term of the kinetic equation turns out to be determined by the known Skyrme mean field the collision integral appears in two - and three - particle parts. The cross section results from the same microscopic footing and is naturally density dependent due to the three - particle force. By this way no hybrid model for drift and cross section is needed for nuclear transport. Besides the mean field correlation energy the resulting equation of state has also a two - and three - particle correlation energy which are both calculated analytically for the ground state. These energies contribute to the equation of state and lead to an occurrence of a maximum at 3 times nuclear density in the total energy.

## Key claim

Starting from a microscopic two- and three-particle contact interaction, a kinetic equation is derived where both the drift term (Skyrme mean field) and the collision integral (with density-dependent cross sections) follow from the same footing, eliminating the need for hybrid models; however, the Born approximation used leads to a maximum in the total energy at 3 times nuclear density, beyond which the system collapses toward diverging negative energy, marking the limit of this approximation.

## Method

The author starts from a Hamiltonian with point-like two- and three-particle contact interactions and derives the Kadanoff-Baym equation using nonequilibrium Green's functions. The self energy is expanded in the Born approximation to obtain the kinetic equation, which includes both two- and three-particle collision integrals. The two- and three-particle correlation energies for the ground state are then calculated analytically from the balance equations, and the parameters \(t_0\) and \(t_3\) are refitted to nuclear matter saturation properties including these correlation energies.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: \(n_0 = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) with binding energy \(-16\ \text{MeV}\).
- Hartree-Fock fit parameters: \(t_0 = -1026.67\ \text{MeV fm}^3\), \(t_3 = 14625\ \text{MeV fm}^6\), giving compressibility \(K = 377\ \text{MeV}\).
- Fit including two- and three-particle correlation energies: \(\tilde{t}_0 = -745.71\ \text{MeV fm}^3\), \(\tilde{t}_3 = 8272.8\ \text{MeV fm}^6\), giving compressibility \(\tilde{K} = 351\ \text{MeV}\).
- Two-particle correlation energy per particle: \(E_{\text{corr}_2}/n = -5.79 \times 10^{-5}\ \text{MeV}^{-1}\ \text{fm}^{-2}\ n^{4/3} [9 t_0^2 + \frac{3}{4} t_3^2 n^2 + 5 t_3 t_0 n]\).
- Three-particle correlation energy per particle: \(E_{\text{corr}_3}/n = 2.37 \times 10^{-6}\ \text{MeV}^{-1}\ \text{fm}^{-2}\ n^{10/3} t_3^2\).
- Ratio of two- to three-particle correlation energies at saturation density: \(|E_2/E_3|_{n_0} = 19.2\).
- Minimum of this ratio occurs at density \(n_{\text{min}} = 0.32\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) (twice nuclear density) with value \(|E_2/E_3|_{\text{min}} = 1.4\).
- Asymptotic high-density ratio: \(|E_2/E_3|_{\infty} = 18.3\).

## Context

This work extends the Skyrme mean-field approach (Skyrme 1956, 1959) and three-particle interaction models (Carlson et al. 1983, Jackson et al. 1983) by providing a fully microscopic transport theory with consistent drift and collision terms. It contrasts with hybrid models that use separate parameterizations for the mean field and cross section, and with the effective two-particle kinetic theory of Ayik et al. (1998) which does not include explicit three-particle collisions. The derived density-dependent cross sections and correlation energies enable a unified description of nuclear transport from sub-saturation to supra-saturation densities, though the Born approximation limits validity to densities below about 3 times nuclear density.
