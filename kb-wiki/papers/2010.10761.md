# Effects of hadronic repulsive interactions on the fluctuations of conserved charges

> We investigate the effects of repulsive interaction between hadrons on the fluctuations of the conserved charges. We calculate the baryon,the electric charge and the strangeness susceptibilities within the ambit of hadron resonance gas model extended to include the short range repulsive interactions.The repulsive interactions are included through a mean-field approach where the single particle energy gets modified due to mean field interactions between hadrons proportional to the number density of hadrons.We assume different mean-field interactions for mesons and baryons. It is shown that the repulsive interactions play a very crucial role to describe hadronic matter near transition temperature. We also show that in order to consistently describe higher order conserved charge fluctuations mesonic repulsive interactions cannot be neglected. Further, we demonstrate that the repulsive interaction of baryons are essential to describe the lattice simulation results at finite baryonchemical potential for higher order fluctuations.

## Key claim

The paper shows that including short-range repulsive interactions between hadrons via a mean-field approach, with separate repulsive parameters for baryons and mesons, is essential to reproduce lattice QCD results for higher-order fluctuations of conserved charges (especially baryon number) near the transition temperature, whereas the ideal hadron resonance gas model fails for these higher-order susceptibilities.

## Method

The authors extend the ideal hadron resonance gas (HRG) model by adding a density-dependent mean-field repulsive potential \(U = K n\) to the single-particle energy, with separate repulsive parameters \(K_B\) for baryons/antibaryons and \(K_M\) for mesons. They then compute the second- and fourth-order susceptibilities \(\chi_B^n\), \(\chi_Q^n\), and \(\chi_S^n\) for baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness by solving the self-consistent number density equations and taking appropriate derivatives of the pressure. Results are compared to lattice QCD data from Bazavov et al. (2013) at zero chemical potential and from the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration (2018) at finite baryon chemical potential.

## Key numbers

* Baryon mean-field parameter fixed at \(K_B = 0.45\) GeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^3\); meson mean-field parameters tested: \(K_M = 0\), \(0.1\), and \(0.15\) GeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^3\).
* For \(\chi_B^4\) at zero chemical potential, the ideal HRG model deviates from lattice data above \(T \approx 160\) MeV, while the MFHRG model reproduces the broad bump seen in lattice simulations.
* For \(\chi_Q^2\) at zero chemical potential, reasonable quantitative agreement with lattice data is achieved with \(K_M = 0.1\) GeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^3\).
* At finite baryon chemical potential and \(T = 158\) MeV, the ratio \(R_{31}^B = \chi_3^B / \chi_1^B\) and \(R_{42}^B = \chi_4^B / \chi_2^B\) from the ideal HRG model are unity, while the MFHRG model (with \(K_B = 0.45\) GeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^3\)) reproduces the lattice results showing deviations from unity.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of conserved charge fluctuations using the ideal HRG model (Karsch et al., 2010; Garg et al., 2013; Mishra et al., 2016) and the mean-field HRG approach for baryon number fluctuations at zero chemical potential (Huovinen et al., 2017). It also builds on earlier mean-field treatments of repulsive interactions in hadron gas thermodynamics (Kapusta, 1982; Olive, 1980). The results enable more reliable modeling of higher-order fluctuations used to search for the QCD critical point in heavy-ion collision experiments at RHIC, FAIR, and NICA, and demonstrate that mesonic repulsive interactions cannot be neglected for electric charge and strangeness fluctuations.
