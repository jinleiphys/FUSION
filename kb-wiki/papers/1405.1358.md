# An update of muon capture on hydrogen

> The successful precision measurement of the rate of muon capture on a proton by the MuCap Collaboration allows for a stringent test of the current theoretical understanding of this process. Chiral perturbation theory, which is a low-energy effective field theory that preserves the symmetries and the pattern of symmetry breaking in the underlying theory of QCD, offers a systematic framework for describing $μp$ capture and provides a basic test of QCD at the hadronic level. We describe how this effective theory with no free parameters reproduces the measured capture rate. A recent study has addressed new sources of uncertainties that were not considered in the previous works, and we review to what extent these uncertainties are now under control. Finally, the rationale for studying muon capture on the deuteron and some recent theoretical developments regarding this process are discussed.

## Key claim

Chiral perturbation theory with no free parameters reproduces the measured muon capture rate on a proton, but the theoretical precision is limited by uncertainties in the nucleon axial-vector coupling constant \(g_A\) and the pion-nucleon coupling constant \(g_{\pi NN}\), and it does not seem warranted at present to go on to N\(^3\)LO calculations because variations from these coupling uncertainties are comparable to the estimated N\(^3\)LO contributions.

## Method

The authors review heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HB\(\chi\)PT) calculations of the \(\mu^- p\) capture rate from the hyperfine-singlet state, including nucleon recoil corrections up to next-to-next-to-leading order (N\(^2\)LO) and radiative corrections of order \(\alpha \sim 1/137\). They evaluate the pseudoscalar form factor \(G_P(q^2)\) at one-loop order and compute the capture rate \(\Gamma_0\) for different values of \(g_A\) and \(g_{\pi NN}\), accounting for electroweak loop corrections and QED vacuum polarization effects. For the two-nucleon case, they describe how HB\(\chi\)PT transition operators are derived and how the unknown low-energy constant \(d^R\) enters the \(\mu^- d\) capture rate.

## Key numbers

- The MuCap Collaboration measured the hyperfine-singlet capture rate \(\Gamma_0^{\rm exp}(\mu^- p \to \nu_\mu n) = 714.9 \pm 5.4 (stat) \pm 5.1 (syst)\, {\rm s}^{-1}\) with \(\sim 1\%\) precision.
- HB\(\chi\)PT at one-loop order gives \(G_P(q^2=-0.88m_\pi^2) = 8.26 \pm 0.23\), in agreement with the empirical value \(8.06 \pm 0.55\) from MuCap.
- The axial radius used is \(\langle r_A^2 \rangle = 0.44 \pm 0.02\) fm\(^2\).
- The calculated \(\Gamma_0\) values range from 707.1 s\(^{-1}\) (for \(g_A=1.266\), \(g_{\pi NN}=13.40\)) to 719.2 s\(^{-1}\) (for \(g_A=1.2761\), \(g_{\pi NN}=13.044\)).
- The pion-nucleon coupling constant range is \(g_{\pi NN} = 13.044\) to 13.40.
- The latest PDG value for the axial coupling is \(g_A=1.2701 \pm 0.0025\), while recent re-measurements give \(g_A \simeq 1.276\).
- The Fermi coupling constant is \(G_F=1.16637(5)\times 10^{-5}\) GeV\(^{-2}\) and the CKM matrix element is \(V_{ud}=0.97418(27)\).

## Context

This review updates the theoretical status of muon capture on hydrogen following the MuCap Collaboration's precision measurement (2013), extending earlier HB\(\chi\)PT work by Fearing et al. (1997), Ando et al. (2000), and Bernard et al. (2001). The authors discuss new uncertainties from the changing value of \(g_A\) (Mund 2013, Mendenhall 2013) and the unresolved range of \(g_{\pi NN}\) (Nijmegen 1997, KH83). The paper also reviews the ongoing MuSun experiment at PSI aiming to measure \(\mu^- d\) capture at 1.5% precision, which will help determine the two-nucleon LEC \(d^R\) that also enters solar \(pp\) fusion, tritium \(\beta\)-decay, and \(\nu d\) scattering (Park et al. 2003).
