# Consistent one-pion exchange currents in electron scattering from a relativistic Fermi gas

> A set of fully relativistic one-pion-exchange electromagnetic operators is developed for use in one-particle emission reactions induced by electrons. To preserve the gauge invariance of the theory additional pionic correlation operators are required beyond the usual meson-exchange current operators. Of these, in the present work emphasis is placed on the self-energy current which is infinite and needs to be renormalized. The renormalized current is expanded to first order to obtain a genuine one-pion-exchange contribution to the inclusive responses.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a fully relativistic, gauge-invariant one-pion-exchange electromagnetic current for use in inclusive electron scattering from a relativistic Fermi gas, but the self-energy contribution to this current is infinite when computed directly from Feynman rules and must be renormalized by dressing the external nucleon lines with a Hartree-Fock self-energy, then expanded to first order in the square of the pion-nucleon coupling constant to obtain a finite, well-defined operator.

## Method

The authors start from the set of two-body Feynman diagrams with one exchanged pion (seagull, pion-in-flight, and correlation currents) and isolate the self-energy diagrams (particle and hole self-energy insertions). They renormalize these divergent diagrams by solving the relativistic Hartree-Fock equations for nucleons in nuclear matter, obtaining dressed spinors and a modified energy-momentum relation. The resulting one-body current is then expanded to first order in the square of the pion-nucleon coupling constant \(f^2/m_\pi^2\), yielding a finite self-energy current operator that acts on free Dirac spinors. Gauge invariance of the full set of currents (MEC, vertex correlation, and renormalized self-energy) is verified to first order.

## Key numbers

* The self-energy current is expanded to first order in the square of the pion-nucleon coupling constant \(f^2/m_\pi^2\), which is the expansion parameter for the one-pion-exchange contribution.
* The non-relativistic self-energy function is given by \(\Sigma_{nr}(\np)=3\frac{f^2}{Vm_\pi^2}\sum_{\nk}\frac{(\np-\nk)^2}{(\np-\nk)^2+m_\pi^2}\), where \(m_\pi\) is the pion mass.
* The non-relativistic nucleon energy in the medium becomes \(\epsilon_{nr}(\np)=\frac{\np^2}{2m}+\Sigma_{nr}(\np)\), with the self-energy depending only on momentum magnitude.
* The relativistic self-energy decomposition uses the functions \(A(P)\), \(B(P)\), and \(C(P)\) defined in terms of integrals \(I(P)\) and \(L^\mu(P)\) over the Fermi sea with cutoff \(k_F\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of relativistic effects in nuclear electromagnetic responses (Amaro 1998, Amaro 1999) and their recent investigation of pions in inclusive electron scattering (Amaro 2001). It provides a consistent, gauge-invariant treatment of one-pion-exchange currents that avoids the divergence problem of self-energy insertions by using a Hartree-Fock renormalization procedure, in contrast to earlier relativistic calculations (Blunden 1989, Horowitz 1990) that introduced in-medium form factors or mean fields from the start. The approach enables the computation of inclusive response functions for quasielastic electron scattering using a relativistic Fermi gas model with explicit one-pion-exchange currents, and the results agree with those obtained via the polarization propagator technique in Amaro (2001).
