# Superconductivity from gauge/gravity duality with flavor

> We consider thermal strongly-coupled N=2 SYM theory with fundamental matter at finite isospin chemical potential. Using gauge/gravity duality, i.e. a probe of two flavor D7-branes embedded in the AdS black hole background, we find a critical temperature at which the system undergoes a second order phase transition. The critical exponent of this transition is one half and coincides with the result from mean field theory. In the thermodynamically favored phase, a flavor current acquires a vev and breaks an Abelian symmetry spontaneously. This new phase shows signatures known from superconductivity, such as an infinite dc conductivity and a gap in the frequency-dependent conductivity. The gravity setup allows for an explicit identification of the degrees of freedom in the dual field theory, as well as for a dual string picture of the condensation process.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in strongly-coupled N=2 SYM theory with fundamental matter at finite isospin chemical potential, a new stable phase exists that exhibits signatures of superconductivity, including infinite dc conductivity and a gap in the frequency-dependent conductivity. The hedge is that this phase is realized within a probe approximation (two flavor D7-branes in an AdS black hole background) and that the probe approximation ignoring backreaction is expected to break down near T=0.

## Method

The authors use gauge/gravity duality by embedding two flavor D7-brane probes in an AdS black hole background, introducing a non-Abelian gauge field on the branes with a non-zero time component A_0^3 (dual to isospin chemical potential) and a non-zero spatial component A_3^1 (dual to a flavor current vev). They expand the Dirac-Born-Infeld (DBI) action to fourth order and also use a modified symmetrized trace prescription to compute the full action numerically, then Legendre-transform to obtain first-order equations of motion. The frequency-dependent conductivity is calculated via the Kubo formula from the retarded Green function of the fluctuation a_3^2.

## Key numbers

* Critical chemical potential: dimensionless critical chemical potential mu_tilde_c ≈ 2.85.
* Critical density: dimensionless critical density (d_tilde_0^3)_c ≈ 20.7.
* Critical exponent of the phase transition: 1/2, up to an error of 10%.
* Superconductive density n_s vanishes linearly at the critical temperature: n_s ∝ (1 - T/T_c) for T ≈ T_c.
* Conductivity is shown at temperatures T ≈ 0.90 T_c, T ≈ 0.66 T_c, T ≈ 0.46 T_c, and T ≈ 0.28 T_c (from figure caption).

## Context

This work extends the gauge/gravity duality with flavor to realize a superconducting state from a top-down supergravity model, in contrast to phenomenological bottom-up AdS/QCD models such as Gubser (2008). It builds on the earlier analysis of isospin density in Erdmenger (2008) where an instability was found at high densities, and stabilizes that system via a rho-meson superfluid. The results provide an explicit identification of the dual field theory degrees of freedom and enable further study of the D7-D7 string condensate, including drag force calculations and extensions to other brane systems such as D2/D6 or D3/D5.
