# Systematic investigation of the rotational bands in nuclei with $Z \approx 100$ using a particle-number conserving method based on a cranked shell model

> The rotational bands in nuclei with $Z \approx 100$ are investigated systematically by using a cranked shell model (CSM) with the pairing correlations treated by a particle-number conserving (PNC) method, in which the blocking effects are taken into account exactly. By fitting the experimental single-particle spectra in these nuclei, a new set of Nilsson parameters ($κ$ and $μ$) and deformation parameters ($\varepsilon_2$ and $\varepsilon_4$) are proposed. The experimental kinematic moments of inertia for the rotational bands in even-even, odd-$A$ and odd-odd nuclei, and the bandhead energies of the 1-quasiparticle bands in odd-$A$ nuclei, are reproduced quite well by the PNC-CSM calculations. By analyzing the $ω$-dependence of the occupation probability of each cranked Nilsson orbital near the Fermi surface and the contributions of valence orbitals in each major shell to the angular momentum alignment, the upbending mechanism in this region is understood clearly.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a new set of Nilsson parameters (κ and μ) and deformation parameters (ε₂ and ε₄), fitted to experimental single-particle spectra in nuclei with Z ≈ 100, allows the cranked shell model with particle-number conserving pairing to reproduce experimental kinematic moments of inertia and 1-quasiparticle bandhead energies quite well, but the agreement is qualified by the fact that the level sequence in N = 151 isotones (specifically the ν5/2⁺[622] orbital) is not reproduced, and the ground state configuration for N = 147 isotones disagrees with experiment.

## Method

The authors used a cranked shell model (CSM) with pairing correlations treated by a particle-number conserving (PNC) method, which exactly accounts for Pauli blocking effects. They constructed a cranked many-particle configuration (CMPC) space in the proton N = 4, 5, 6 shells and neutron N = 6, 7 shells, with dimensions of about 1000 for both protons and neutrons. The Hamiltonian was diagonalized in this truncated Fock space to obtain eigenstates, from which kinematic moments of inertia and occupation probabilities were calculated.

## Key numbers

- Quadrupole deformation parameter β₂ ≈ 0.28 ± 0.02 for ²⁵⁰Fm, ²⁵²No, and ²⁵⁴No (experimental values).
- The root-mean-square deviation of theoretical 1-quasiparticle bandhead energies from experimental values is about 270 keV for neutrons using the modified Nilsson parameters.
- The root-mean-square deviation for neutrons using the Woods-Saxon potential is about 200 keV.
- Effective pairing strengths for even-even nuclei: G₀ₚ = 0.40 MeV, G₂ₚ = 0.035 MeV, G₀ₙ = 0.30 MeV, G₂ₙ = 0.020 MeV.
- For ²⁴⁸Cm, calculations with CMPC space dimensions of 1000 and 1500 gave consistent results; the larger space used Gₚ = 0.35 MeV, G₂ₚ = 0.03 MeV, Gₙ = 0.27 MeV, G₂ₙ = 0.013 MeV.
- The ν7/2⁺[624] level in ²⁵³No was observed at 355 keV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Rapid Communication (Zhang 2011) on ²⁴⁷,²⁴⁹Cm and ²⁴⁹Cf to a systematic study of nuclei with Z ≈ 100. It provides a new set of Nilsson parameters for this region, where the conventional parameters from Nilsson (1969) and Bengtsson (1985) could not describe experimental level schemes well. The results enable further investigation of the shell structure relevant to superheavy nuclei, particularly the proton gap at Z = 100 and neutron gap at N = 152, and the spin-orbital splitting of the π2f₅/₂,₇/₂ doublet that determines whether the next proton magic number is Z = 114 or 120.
