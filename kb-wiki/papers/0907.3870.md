# Do p+p Collisions Flow at RHIC? Understanding One-Particle Distributions, Multiplicity Evolution, and Conservation Laws

> Collective, explosive flow in central heavy ion collisions manifests itself in the mass dependence of $p_T$ distributions and femtoscopic length scales, measured in the soft sector ($p_T\lesssim 1$ GeV/c). Measured $p_T$ distributions from proton-proton collisions differ significantly from those from heavy ion collisions. This has been taken as evidence that p+p collisions generate little collective flow, a conclusion in line with naive expectations. We point out possible hazards of ignoring phase-space restrictions due to conservation laws when comparing high- and low-multiplicity final states. Already in two-particle correlation functions, we see clear signals of such phase-space restrictions in low-multiplicity collisions at RHIC. We discuss how these same effects, then, {\it must} appear in the single particle spectra. We argue that the effects of energy and momentum conservation actually dominate the observed systematics, and that $p+p$ collisions may be much more similar to heavy ion collisions than generally thought.

## Key claim

The observed evolution of transverse momentum spectra from p+p to central Au+Au collisions at RHIC may be dominated by phase-space restrictions due to energy and momentum conservation rather than by changes in collective flow dynamics; the parent distributions underlying p+p and Au+Au collisions may be almost identical.

## Method

The authors derive an analytic expression (Equation 4) relating measured single-particle distributions to a "parent" distribution multiplied by a phase-space factor that depends on event multiplicity N and average kinematic quantities. They test the extreme postulate that parent distributions are identical for p+p and Au+Au collisions at all centralities by comparing the ratio of measured spectra from different multiplicity classes to the ratio of their phase-space factors, using fixed parameters ⟨p_T²⟩=0.12 GeV², ⟨E²⟩=0.43 GeV², and ⟨E⟩=0.61 GeV, with only N varying (N_cent=500, N_periph=18, N_p+p=10).

## Key numbers

- ⟨p_T²⟩ = 0.12 GeV², ⟨E²⟩ = 0.43 GeV², and ⟨E⟩ = 0.61 GeV used as fixed parameters in the phase-space factor for all multiplicity classes
- Multiplicity values used: N_cent = 500 (central Au+Au), N_periph = 18 (peripheral Au+Au), N_p+p = 10 (minimum-bias p+p)
- The phase-space-based description works for p_T ≲ 1 GeV/c and breaks down for p_T > ~1.5 GeV/c
- PYTHIA simulations return kinematic quantities within a factor of ~2 of the values used
- Underlying Maxwell-Boltzmann estimate assumes temperatures T = 0.15–0.35 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier findings that pion HBT measurements in p+p collisions at RHIC show identical flow signals as in Au+Au collisions (Chajecki 2005), and that similar systematics appear in several hadron-hadron measurements (Chajecki 2009). It directly challenges the standard interpretation of blast-wave fits (Retiere 2003) to STAR spectra (Adams 2003), which had suggested much smaller transverse flow in p+p collisions. The paper enables a reinterpretation of multiplicity evolution of spectral shapes and motivates extraction of "system" kinematic parameters from two-particle correlation functions to test whether p+p collisions may exhibit collective flow similar to heavy ion collisions.
