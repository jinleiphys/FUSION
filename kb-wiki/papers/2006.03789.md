# Constraining the onset density of the hadron-quark phase transition with gravitational-wave observations

> We study the possible occurrence of the hadron-quark phase transition (PT) during the merging of neutron star binaries by hydrodynamical simulations employing a set of temperature dependent hybrid equations of state (EoSs). Following previous work we describe an unambiguous and measurable signature of deconfined quark matter in the gravitational-wave (GW) signal of neutron star binary mergers including equal-mass and unequal-mass systems of different total binary mass. The softening of the EoS by the PT at higher densities, i.e. after merging, leads to a characteristic increase of the dominant postmerger GW frequency f_peak relative to the tidal deformability Lambda inferred during the premerger inspiral phase. Hence, measuring such an increase of the postmerger frequency provides evidence for the presence of a strong PT. If the postmerger frequency and the tidal deformability are compatible with results from purely baryonic EoS models yielding very tight relations between f_peak and Lambda, a strong PT can be excluded up to a certain density. We find tight correlations of f_peak and Lambda with the maximum density during the early postmerger remnant evolution. These GW observables thus inform about the density regime which is probed by the remnant and its GW emission. Exploiting such relations we devise a directly applicable, concrete procedure to constrain the onset density of the QCD PT from future GW measurements. We point out two interesting scenarios: if no indications for a PT are inferred from a GW detection, our procedure yields a lower limit on the onset density of the hadron quark PT. On the contrary, if a merger event reveals evidence for the occurrence of deconfined quark matter, the inferred GW parameters set an upper limit on the PT onset density. (abridged)

## Key claim

The paper claims that a simultaneous measurement of the tidal deformability (from the inspiral phase) and the dominant postmerger gravitational-wave frequency can either provide a lower limit on the onset density of the hadron-quark phase transition (if no PT signature is seen) or an upper limit (if a PT signature is detected), but this procedure only works for a sufficiently strong first-order phase transition that significantly softens the equation of state.

## Method

The authors performed general relativistic smoothed particle hydrodynamics simulations of neutron star binary mergers using a set of seven temperature-dependent hybrid equations of state (DD2F-SF-n) that feature a strong first-order hadron-quark phase transition, alongside a purely hadronic reference model (DD2F) and 15 other purely hadronic EoSs. They simulated equal-mass binaries of 1.2-1.2, 1.35-1.35, 1.4-1.4, and 1.5-1.5 solar masses, plus an unequal-mass system of 1.3-1.4 solar masses. The dominant postmerger gravitational-wave frequency and tidal deformability were extracted from each simulation and compared to establish empirical relations.

## Key numbers

- For the DD2F-SF-1 hybrid EoS at zero temperature, the onset density of the phase transition is 3.26 times nuclear saturation density (rho_sat), and the density where pure quark matter appears is 3.87 rho_sat; at T=30 MeV, these shift to 2.52 rho_sat and 3.68 rho_sat respectively.
- Nuclear saturation density is given as rho_sat = 2.7 x 10^14 g cm^-3.
- For the 1.35-1.35 solar mass merger with the DD2F purely hadronic EoS, the maximum density in the early postmerger phase (rho_max^max) is 1.013 x 10^15 g cm^-3 at the highest numerical resolution (600,000 SPH particles).
- The dominant postmerger GW frequency f_peak for neutron star mergers typically lies in the range between 2 and 4 kHz.
- The pseudocritical temperature for the QCD crossover at vanishing baryon chemical potential is 156.5 +/- 1.5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the findings of Bauswein (2019), which first demonstrated that a strong first-order phase transition produces a characteristic increase in the postmerger GW frequency relative to the tidal deformability. The procedure contrasts with earlier approaches that sought to identify a phase transition solely from a kink in the tidal deformability as a function of mass (Chen 2019, Chatziioannou 2019, Pang 2020), which require many observations. The method enables constraints from a single simultaneous detection of pre- and postmerger GW signals, and the authors stress that dedicated high-frequency GW instruments would add significant value for such measurements.
