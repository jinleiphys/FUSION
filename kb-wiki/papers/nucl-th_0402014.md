# Electromagnetic Signatures of the Color Glass Condensate: Dileptons

> We evaluate the invariant cross section for production of dileptons in forward rapidities at RHIC and LHC, using the Color Glass Condensate formalism and present results for the nuclear modification factor $R_{d(p)A}$ as a function of dilepton invariant mass for the most central deuteron (proton)-nucleus collisions.

## Key claim

Dilepton production in forward-rapidity deuteron-gold (proton-lead) collisions is suppressed relative to scaled proton-proton collisions, similar to hadron suppression, but the predictions are most reliable for dilepton invariant masses below the extended scaling scale (approximately 3 GeV at RHIC and 13 GeV at LHC) and may break down at higher masses.

## Method

The invariant cross section for dilepton production in forward rapidities is evaluated using the Color Glass Condensate formalism, starting from the quark-nucleus scattering cross section derived in Jalilian-Marian (2004) and integrating over the transverse momentum of the dilepton pair. The result is convoluted with the deuteron (proton) structure function F2 using the GRV98 parameterization for protons and the HKM parameterization for deuterons (which includes nuclear shadowing), while the target nucleus is treated as a Color Glass Condensate using the Iancu-Itakura-Munier (IIM) dipole cross section ansatz fitted to HERA data, with the nuclear saturation scale taken as QsA^2 = A^(1/3) Qsp^2.

## Key numbers

- At RHIC (y=2.2, central deuteron-gold collisions), the nuclear modification factor R_dA for dileptons is shown as a function of invariant mass M up to approximately 3 GeV, where the extended scaling scale of a proton is ~3 GeV.
- At LHC (y=5, central proton-lead collisions), the nuclear modification factor R_pA is shown for dilepton invariant masses up to approximately 13 GeV, where the extended scaling scale reaches 13 GeV.
- The saturation scale for the nucleus is taken as QsA^2 = A^(1/3) Qsp^2, with the proton saturation scale parameterized as Qs^2 = (x0/x)^lambda GeV^2 using constants from the IIM fit to HERA data.
- The IIM dipole cross section fit to HERA data covers the kinematic region Q^2 < 50 GeV^2 and x_bj < 0.01, with anomalous dimension gamma_s = 0.63 and kappa = 9.9 from leading-order BFKL.

## Context

This work extends the Color Glass Condensate formalism from hadron production (where BRAHMS data at RHIC showed suppression of R_dA in forward rapidity, as reported by the BRAHMS collaboration) to electromagnetic probes (dileptons), which are cleaner because they do not undergo strong final-state interactions and do not require fragmentation functions. The results enable experimental tests at RHIC (PHENIX at y=2.2) and LHC (y=5) to distinguish Color Glass Condensate effects from other mechanisms, and the author notes that the dipole model used does not match the double-log DGLAP limit at high p_t, though this may be unimportant at forward rapidities due to kinematic constraints.
