# Uncovering a chirally suppressed mechanism of $0νββ$ decay with LHC searches

> $ΔL =2$ lepton number violation (LNV) at the TeV scale could provide an alternative interpretation of positive signal(s) in future neutrinoless double beta $(0νββ)$ decay experiments. An interesting class of models from this point of view are those that at low energies give rise to dimension-9 vector operators and a dimension-7 operator, both of whose $0νββ$-decay rates are "chirally suppressed". We study and compare the sensitivities of $0νββ$-decay experiments and LHC searches to a simplified model in this class of TeV-scale LNV that is also $SU(2)_L \times U(1)_Y$ gauge invariant. The searches for $0νββ$ decay, which are here diluted by a chiral suppression of the vector operators, are found to be less constraining than LHC searches whose reach is increased by the assumed kinematic accessibility of the mediator particles. For the chirally suppressed dimension-7 operator generated by TeV-scale mediators, in contrast, $0νββ$-decay searches place strong constraints on the size of the new Yukawa coupling. Signals of this model at the LHC and $0νββ$-decay experiments are entirely uncorrelated with the observed neutrinos masses, as these new sources of LNV give negligible contributions to the latter. We find the prospects for the high-luminosity LHC and ton-scale $0νββ$-decay experiments to uncover the chirally-suppressed mechanism with TeV-scale LNV to be promising. We also comment on the sensitivity of the $0νββ$-decay lifetime to certain unknown low-energy constants that in the case of dimension-9 {\it scalar} operators are expected to be large due to non-perturbative renormalization.

## Key claim

For a class of TeV-scale lepton number violating models that generate chirally suppressed dimension-9 vector operators, LHC searches are more constraining than neutrinoless double beta decay experiments because the hadronic chiral suppression of the decay rate (by a factor of approximately 2000) offsets the sensitivity of the latter, while the kinematic accessibility of mediator particles at the LHC enhances collider reach.

## Method

The authors constructed a simplified model with a scalar field S, a leptoquark field R, and a Dirac fermion field Psi that is SU(2)L x U(1)Y gauge invariant and generates dimension-9 vector operators and a dimension-7 operator at low energies. They computed the neutrinoless double beta decay half-life using chiral effective field theory with Weinberg power counting, including one-loop QCD renormalization group evolution of the Wilson coefficients from the TeV scale down to the hadronic scale, and recast existing LHC searches using parton showering, jet reconstruction, and fast detector simulation with Delphes3.

## Key numbers

- The chiral suppression factor for vector operators relative to scalar operators is approximately (Lambda_chi/m_pi)^4 ~ 2 x 10^3, where Lambda_chi ~ 1 GeV and m_pi is the pion mass
- The half-life sensitivity for current KamLAND-Zen experiment is T_{1/2}^{0nu} > 1.07 x 10^26 years at 90% confidence level
- Future ton-scale experiments aim to reach T_{1/2}^{0nu} > 10^28 years at 90% confidence level
- The reduced amplitude for vector operators is proportional to m_pi^2, while amplitudes for leading-order hadron-lepton operators are proportional to Lambda_chi^2
- The phase space factor G_{09} = 2.8 x 10^{-14} per year for Xe-136
- The nuclear matrix elements used are M_{GT,sd}^{AP} = -2.80, M_{T,sd}^{AP} = -0.92, and M_{F,sd} = -1.53 for Xe-136 calculated using QRPA
- The ratio of nuclear matrix elements between vector and scalar operators is about 6-8 across various isotopes
- The one-loop QCD running matrix Z from 2 TeV to 2 GeV has diagonal elements 1.43 and 0.68

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Helo (2013) by using a fully SU(2)L x U(1)Y gauge invariant simplified model rather than only SU(3)c x U(1)em invariant models, and by including pion contributions, color-octet operators, and perturbative QCD renormalization group evolution that were absent in that earlier study. The results demonstrate that for chirally suppressed vector operators, LHC searches have greater relative sensitivity compared to neutrinoless double beta decay experiments than for scalar operators, and that signals at the LHC and neutrinoless double beta decay experiments are entirely uncorrelated with observed neutrino masses since the new LNV sources give negligible contributions to the latter.
