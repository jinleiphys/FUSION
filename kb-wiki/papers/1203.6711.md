# Negative parity $Λ$ and $Σ$ resonances coupled to pseudoscalar and vector mesons

> An exploration of the dynamically generated $Λ$ and $Σ$ resonances has been made in the vector meson-baryon (VB) systems coupled to pseudoscalar meson-baryon (PB) channels. The VB interactions are obtained from the Lagrangians written within the hidden local symmetry which, in addition to Yukawa-type vector-baryon-baryon (VBB) vertices, naturally gives rise to a contact interaction. Using the VBB vertices, we calculate the $t$-, $s$- and $u$-channel diagrams considering the octet baryon exchange in the latter two cases. For the PB channels we rely on the the Weingberg-Tomozawa interactions and calculate the PB $\leftrightarrow$ VB transition amplitudes extending the Kroll-Ruderman theorem by replacing the photon by the vector mesons. In addition to the low-lying $Λ(1405)$, $Λ(1670)$ found in previous works, we find resonances which coincide well with: $Λ(2000)$, $Σ(1750) S_{11}$, $Σ(1940) D_{13}$, $Σ(2000) S_{11}$ and predict three $Λ^*$'s with $J^π= 3/2^-$ and masses: $1754 - i2$ MeV, $1862 - i33$ MeV, $2139 - i14$ MeV together with a broad $1/2^-$ $Σ^*$ with mass $\sim$ 1430 MeV. We find that most of these next-to-low-lying states couple weakly with the pseudoscalar mesons, which implies that it is important to study the reactions with VB final states in order to obtain a reliable information on the properties of such resonances.

## Key claim
The paper claims that most of the next-to-low-lying Lambda and Sigma resonances generated dynamically from coupled vector meson-baryon (VB) and pseudoscalar meson-baryon (PB) channels couple weakly to pseudoscalar mesons, implying that reliable information on such resonances requires studying reactions with VB final states rather than relying solely on PB channels.

## Method
The authors calculated vector meson-baryon interactions using Lagrangians based on hidden local symmetry, including Yukawa-type vertices, contact interactions, and t-, s-, and u-channel diagrams with octet baryon exchange. For pseudoscalar meson-baryon channels they used Weinberg-Tomozawa interactions, and obtained PB-VB transition amplitudes by extending the Kroll-Ruderman theorem with vector meson dominance. The coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equations were solved with a Gaussian cutoff (750 MeV for PB channels, 545 MeV for VB channels) and loops were convoluted over the widths of rho and K* mesons.

## Key numbers
- Three predicted Lambda* resonances with J^pi = 3/2^- and complex masses: 1754 - i2 MeV, 1862 - i33 MeV, 2139 - i14 MeV
- One broad Sigma* resonance with J^pi = 1/2^- and mass approximately 1430 MeV
- Poles found in VB-only calculations (isospin 0, spin 3/2) at: 1795 - i0 MeV, 1923 - i4 MeV, and 2138 - i21 MeV
- Cutoff parameter Lambda = 650 MeV used for form factors in s- and u-channel diagrams
- Coupling constants: D = 2.4, F = 0.82, g_KR = 6 (from KSRF relation), tilde_F = 0.46, tilde_D = 0.8

## Context
This work extends previous studies of dynamically generated baryon resonances from pseudoscalar meson-baryon interactions (Kaiser, Jido, Oka, Hyodo) and the authors' own prior work on vector meson-baryon systems with strangeness zero (Khemchandani 2011) and low-lying strangeness -1 resonances (Khemchandani 2012). It improves upon the simplified t-channel-only treatment of VB interactions used in earlier coupled-channel calculations by including s- and u-channel diagrams and contact interactions. The results enable future experimental studies of hyperon resonances through reactions with vector mesons in the final state, which the authors argue are essential for observing these states.
