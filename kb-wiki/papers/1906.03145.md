# Canonical statistical model analysis of p-p, p-Pb, and Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC

> The system-size dependence of hadrochemistry at vanishing baryon density is considered within the canonical statistical model (CSM) with local exact conservation of three conserved charges, allowing for a possibility of strangeness undersaturation, i.e. $γ_S \leq 1$. Exact baryon number conservation is found to be even more important than that of strangeness in the canonical suppression picture at the LHC, in contrast to intermediate and low collision energies. The model is applied to p-p, p-Pb, and Pb-Pb data of the ALICE collaboration. A chemical equilibrium CSM with a fixed $T_{\rm ch} = 155$ MeV describes the trends seen in most yield ratios. However, this vanilla version of CSM predicts an enhancement of the $ϕ/π$ ratio at smaller multiplicities, in stark contrast to the suppression seen in the data. The data are described with a 15% relative accuracy level whence a multiplicity dependence of both the temperature and the strangeness saturation parameter $γ_S \leq 1$ is accepted. Both the canonical suppression and the strangeness undersaturation effects are small at $d N_{\rm ch} / dη\gtrsim 100$, but they do improve substantially the description of hadron yields in p-p collisions, in particular the $Ω$ yields. A possibility to constrain the rapidity correlation volume using net-proton fluctuation measurements is pointed out.

## Key claim

The canonical statistical model (CSM) with simultaneous exact conservation of baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness can describe the multiplicity dependence of hadron yield ratios in p-p, p-Pb, and Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies, but only if both a multiplicity-dependent chemical freeze-out temperature and a strangeness undersaturation parameter gamma_S <= 1 are introduced; the simplest "vanilla" CSM with fixed temperature fails because it predicts an enhancement of the phi/pi ratio at small multiplicities, contrary to the observed suppression.

## Method

The authors applied the canonical statistical model (CSM) with exact conservation of baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness to hadron yield data from the ALICE collaboration for p-p collisions at 7 TeV, p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, and Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV. They performed chi-squared minimization fits to the yields of pions, kaons, K0_S, phi, protons, Lambda, Xi, and Omega, extracting the chemical freeze-out temperature T, the volume per unit rapidity dV/dy, and the strangeness saturation parameter gamma_S for each multiplicity bin, using a correlation volume V_c = 3 dV/dy as suggested by net-proton fluctuation measurements.

## Key numbers

* The chemical freeze-out temperature extracted from the gamma_S CSM fits with V_c = 3 dV/dy decreases monotonically from T ~ 175 MeV for the lowest multiplicity bins in p-p collisions to T ~ 155 MeV in the most central Pb-Pb collisions.
* The strangeness saturation parameter gamma_S reaches gamma_S ~ 1 at charged particle multiplicities dN_ch/deta >= 100-200.
* The volume per unit rapidity extracted from the canonical fits follows dV/dy [fm^3] ~ 2.4 * dN_ch/deta.
* The parametrized temperature dependence is T = T_0 - Delta T * ln(dN_ch/deta) with T_0 = (176 +/- 1) MeV and Delta T = (2.6 +/- 0.2) MeV.
* The parametrized gamma_S dependence is gamma_S = 1 - A * exp[-(dN_ch/deta)/B] with A = 0.25 +/- 0.01 and B = 59 +/- 6.
* The data are described with a 15% relative accuracy level when multiplicity-dependent temperature and gamma_S are accepted.
* For the most peripheral Pb-Pb bin (60-70%), net-proton fluctuation data give alpha ~ 0.07, <p> ~ 1.14, and dN_p/dy ~ 1.9, yielding k ~ 3 for the correlation volume factor.

## Context

This work extends prior canonical statistical model analyses that treated only strangeness conservation (ALICE, 2018) and the authors' own previous CSM study of light nuclei yields (Vovchenko, 2018) to cover all stable light-flavored hadrons measured by ALICE. The analysis demonstrates that exact baryon number conservation is more important than strangeness conservation at LHC energies, in contrast to intermediate and low collision energies where strangeness-canonical ensembles were sufficient (Cleymans, 1990; Becattini, 2000). The results enable future constraints on the rapidity correlation volume using net-proton fluctuation measurements and motivate more accurate measurements of Omega yields in small systems to distinguish canonical suppression from incomplete strangeness equilibration.
