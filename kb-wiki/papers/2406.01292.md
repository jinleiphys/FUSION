# Reexamination of antinucleon-nucleon interactions in covariant chiral effective field theory

> Motivated by the recent progress in developing high-precision relativistic chiral nucleon-nucleon interactions, we study the antinucleon-nucleon interaction in a hybrid approach where the real part of the potential is constructed in the leading-order covariant chiral effective field theory, and the imaginary part is described following the procedure adopted in the heavy baryon chiral effective field theory. The phase shifts and inelasticities with $J\leq 1$ are obtained and compared to those calculated in the next-to-leading order heavy baryon chiral effective field theory. For most partial waves, the descriptions of phase shifts and inelasticities in the hybrid approach are comparable to those in the next-to-leading order heavy baryon chiral effective field theory, confirming the relatively faster convergence of the relativistic approach observed in the nucleon-nucleon sector. In addition, we search for bound states/resonances near the $\bar{N}N$ threshold and find several structures that can be associated with those states recently observed by the BESIII Collaboration.

## Key claim
The authors construct an antinucleon-nucleon interaction using a hybrid approach where the real part of the potential is derived from leading-order covariant chiral effective field theory while the imaginary part (annihilation) is treated at next-to-leading order in heavy baryon chiral effective field theory, finding that the description of phase shifts and inelasticities for J ≤ 1 is comparable to the next-to-leading order heavy baryon approach, with the caveat that the annihilation part is not fully covariant but expanded in powers of three-momenta as an approximation.

## Method
The real part of the antinucleon-nucleon potential was constructed from leading-order covariant chiral effective field theory, including one-pion-exchange and contact terms with five independent low-energy constants. The imaginary part describing annihilation was taken from next-to-leading order heavy baryon chiral effective field theory with energy-dependent contact terms. The Kadyshevsky equation was solved in the LSJ basis with a non-local Gaussian regulator (cutoff Λ = 450–600 MeV), and all 26 low-energy constants were fitted to the Nijmegen partial wave analysis of antiproton-proton data at laboratory energies below 125 MeV (plab ≤ 500 MeV).

## Key numbers
- Pion decay constant fπ = 92.4 MeV; axial coupling constant gA = 1.29; isospin-averaged pion mass mπ = 138 MeV; isospin-averaged nucleon mass mN = 939 MeV
- Cutoff range Λ = 450–600 MeV used for regularization
- Data fitted at laboratory energies below 125 MeV (plab ≤ 500 MeV), with extra data at plab = 600 MeV for the 31S0 and 33P0 partial waves
- Low-energy constants for the elastic process are in units of 10^4 GeV^{-2}; annihilation parameters are in units of 10^2 GeV^{-1} (see Table I for full numerical values)
- Phase shifts and inelasticities calculated for all partial waves with J ≤ 1

## Context
This work extends the covariant chiral effective field theory approach previously developed for nucleon-nucleon interactions (Ren 2016, Xiao 2018, Lu 2021) to the antinucleon-nucleon system, motivated by near-threshold enhancements observed by BESIII and other experiments. It builds on the heavy baryon chiral effective field theory antinucleon-nucleon interactions constructed by the Julich group (Kang 2013, Dai 2017), which have been applied to nucleon electromagnetic form factors, baryonic decays, and neutron-antineutron oscillations. The covariant framework aims to address the renormalization-group invariance issues associated with Weinberg power counting and demonstrates faster convergence than the heavy baryon approach, as previously seen in the nucleon-nucleon sector. The results enable searches for near-threshold bound states and resonances that could be associated with structures observed by BESIII.
