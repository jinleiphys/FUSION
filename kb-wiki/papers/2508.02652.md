# PSR J0614-3329: A NICER case for Strange Quark Stars

> Precise measurements of neutron star masses and radii by the NICER mission impose important constraints on the nuclear equation of state. The most recent NICER measurement of PSR J0614-3329 reported an equatorial radius of $R_{eq} = 10.29^{+1.01}_{-0.86}$ km for a mass of $M = 1.44^{+0.06}_{-0.07} M_{\odot}$. Considering all the NICER measurements to date, we find substantial evidence using Bayesian hypothesis ranking for strange quark stars over physically motivated models of neutron stars compatible with this low radius. This provides a strong case for quark matter in neutron stars and also for the possible existence of strange quark stars, a consequence of the Bodmer-Witten hypothesis, suggesting that they could be considered among the population of compact stars during analyses of astrophysical data. Using a wide sample of equations of state, we report the nucleonic equations of state that best fit current observations and rule out one model of strange quark matter.

## Key claim

Using Bayesian hypothesis ranking, the authors find substantial evidence that strange quark stars (SQSs) are favored over physically motivated neutron star models when all NICER mass-radius measurements are considered, including the latest PSR J0614-3329 result. The claim is hedged by noting that this conclusion holds for the specific sample of equations of state considered and that the evidence is an underestimate because the analysis uses not-the-best SQS models against the best neutron star models.

## Method

The authors collected 26 nucleonic neutron star equations of state (from CompOSE, LALSuite, and relativistic mean-field models) and 7 strange quark star equations of state (from MIT Bag model, vector-enhanced vMIT Bag model, and a running-parameter model). They computed Bayes factors for each SQS equation of state against each neutron star equation of state using Gaussian kernel density estimates of the NICER mass-radius posteriors for PSR J0740+6620, PSR J0030+0451, PSR J0437-4715, and PSR J0614-3329, with a uniform prior on mass between 0.5 and the maximum TOV mass of each equation of state.

## Key numbers

- PSR J0614-3329 equatorial radius: \(R_{eq} = 10.29^{+1.01}_{-0.86}\) km at mass \(M = 1.44^{+0.06}_{-0.07} M_{\odot}\) (68% credible interval, ST+PDT headline model)
- Median Bayes factor for SQS over neutron stars including all four NICER measurements: \(\langle\log_{10}{(B^{SQS}_{NS}}\rangle = 0.76\) (substantial evidence)
- Median Bayes factor for SQS over neutron stars excluding PSR J0614-3329: \(\langle\log_{10}{(B^{SQS}_{NS}}\rangle = 0.52\) (marginal/substantial boundary)
- Median Bayes factor for SQS over neutron stars including HESS J1731-347: \(\langle\log_{10}{(B^{SQS}_{NS}}\rangle = 1.32\) (strong evidence)
- Total probability that SQS is the correct model given all four NICER measurements: \(\log_{10}{(P(SQS|\vec{d})/P(NS|\vec{d}))} = 0.68\)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of NICER mass-radius measurements (Salmi 2024 for PSR J0740, Vinciguerra 2024 for PSR J0030, Choudhury 2024 for PSR J0437, and Mauviard 2025 for PSR J0614) by testing physically motivated equations of state rather than parametric forms. It contradicts the assumption that pulsars are purely nucleonic neutron stars and supports the Bodmer-Witten hypothesis that strange quark matter could be the true ground state of matter, as previously suggested by Madsen (1998, 2000) based on r-mode stability and by Annala (2020, 2022, 2023) from perturbative QCD. The results enable future analyses to include strange quark star models when interpreting multimessenger data from NICER, gravitational wave detectors, and next-generation missions like eXTP and STROBE-X.
