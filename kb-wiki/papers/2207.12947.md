# Charged quark stars in $f(R,T)$ gravity

> Recent advances in nuclear theory combined with new astrophysical observations have led to the need for specific theoretical models that actually apply to phenomena on dense-matter physics. At the same time, quantum chromodynamics (QCD) predicts the existence of non-nucleonic degrees of freedom at high densities in neutron-star matter, such as quark matter. Within a confining quark matter model, which consists of homogeneous, neutral 3-flavor interacting quark matter with $\mathcal{O}(m_s^4)$ corrections, we study the structure of compact stars made of a charged perfect fluid in the context of $f(R,T)$ gravity. The system of differential equations that describe the structure of charged compact stars have been derived and solved numerically for a gravity model with $f(R,T)= R+ 2βT$. For simplicity, we assume that the charge density is proportional to the energy density, namely, $ρ_{\rm ch} = αρ$. It is demonstrated that matter-geometry coupling constant $β$ and the charge parameter $α$ affect the total gravitational mass and the radius of the star.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in the $f(R,T) = R + 2\beta T$ gravity model, both the matter-geometry coupling constant $\beta$ and the charge parameter $\alpha$ (where charge density is proportional to energy density) increase the total gravitational mass and radius of quark stars, with the largest deviations from General Relativity reaching approximately 16% for the maximum mass when $\alpha = 0.7$ and $\beta = -0.8$.

## Method

The authors derived modified Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for charged perfect fluid spheres in $f(R,T) = R + 2\beta T$ gravity, assuming a spherically symmetric metric and a charge density proportional to energy density ($\rho_{\rm ch} = \alpha\rho$). They solved these equations numerically using the fourth-order Runge-Kutta method for a QCD-motivated equation of state describing homogeneous, neutral 3-flavor interacting quark matter with $\mathcal{O}(m_s^4)$ corrections, using bag constant $B = 60$ MeV/fm$^3$, interaction parameter $a_4 = 0.7$, and strange quark mass $m_s = 100$ MeV.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass deviation from GR reaches approximately 16% for $\alpha = 0.7$ and $\beta = -0.8$
- For the uncharged case ($\alpha = 0$), the deviation from GR grows to about 6% for $\beta = -0.8$
- Central energy density used for numerical integration: $\rho_c = 1.0 \times 10^{18}$ kg/m$^3$
- Bag constant range cited: $57 \leq B \leq 92$ MeV/fm$^3$, with adopted value $B = 60$ MeV/fm$^3$
- Strange quark mass adopted: $m_s = 100$ MeV
- Interaction parameter adopted: $a_4 = 0.7$
- Charge parameter range explored: $\alpha \in [0, 0.6]$ (with additional results up to $\alpha = 0.7$)
- Coupling constant range explored: $\beta \in [-1.2, 0]$

## Context

This work extends previous studies of compact stars in $f(R,T)$ gravity by Pretel and collaborators (2020, 2021) and Tangphati and collaborators (2022) to include electric charge, building on earlier charged star analyses in General Relativity by Ray and collaborators (2003) and Arbanil and collaborators (2013). The results enable comparison with the Starobinsky $f(R)$ gravity model studied by Pretel (2022) for charged quark stars, and provide theoretical predictions that can be tested against observational data from massive pulsars such as those from the GW190814 event.
