# Pulsar kicks by anisotropic neutrino emission from quark matter in strong magnetic fields

> We discuss a pulsar acceleration mechanism based on asymmetric neutrino emission from the direct quark Urca process in the interior of proto neutron stars. The anisotropy is caused by a strong magnetic field which polarises the spin of the electrons opposite to the field direction. Due to parity violation the neutrinos and anti-neutrinos leave the star in one direction accelerating the pulsar. We calculate for varying quark chemical potentials the kick velocity in dependence of the quark phase temperature and its radius. Ignoring neutrino quark scattering we find that within a quark phase radius of 10 km and temperatures larger than 5 MeV kick velocities of 1000km s$^{-1}$ can be reached very easily. On the other hand taking into account the small neutrino mean free paths it seems impossible to reach velocities higher than 100km s$^{-1}$ even when including effects from colour superconductivity where the neutrino quark interactions are suppressed.

# Pulsar kicks by anisotropic neutrino emission from quark matter in strong magnetic fields

## Key claim
The paper proposes that anisotropic neutrino emission from the direct quark Urca process in a proto-neutron star's quark matter core, driven by electron spin polarisation in strong magnetic fields, can produce pulsar kick velocities up to 1000 km/s only if neutrino-quark scattering is ignored; when realistic neutrino mean free paths are included, the mechanism cannot produce velocities exceeding 100 km/s, even with colour superconductivity suppressing interactions.

## Method

They derive analytical and numerical expressions for the polarisation in four limiting cases (zero temperature, non-degenerate massless, non-degenerate massive, and large magnetic field), and use these to estimate kick velocities as functions of quark phase temperature, radius, and magnetic field strength. The analysis separately considers the idealized case without neutrino reabsorption and the realistic case including neutrino mean free paths in quark matter.

## Context
This work extends the mechanism of Horowitz and Piekarewicz (2002) for neutrino asymmetry from polarised electrons in strong magnetic fields, but applies it to quark matter rather than nucleonic matter, noting that for quarks the axial and vector coupling constants are equal, which would give vanishing asymmetry in the nucleonic treatment. The paper addresses the "no-go theorem" of Vilenkin (1995) regarding parity violation in thermal equilibrium. It builds on studies of the direct quark Urca process in magnetic fields by Iwamoto (1981), Steiner (2001), Jaikumar (2006), and others, while providing the first angular dependence calculations for neutrino emissivities. The work enables future studies of pulsar kick mechanisms in hybrid stars and strange stars with colour superconducting quark matter cores, particularly in the 2SC and CFL phases, and connects to observational constraints from pulsar proper motion measurements by Hobbs (2005) and Brisken (2005).
