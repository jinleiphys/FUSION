# Effective degrees of freedom in QCD thermodynamics

> An effective model reproducing the equation of state of hadronic matter as obtained in recent lattice QCD simulations and from hadron resonance gas data is presented. The hadronic phase is described by means of an extended Mott-Hagedorn resonance gas while the QGP phase is described by the extended PNJL model. The dissociation of hadrons is obtained by including the state dependent hadron resonance width. The model gives a quantitative estimate for partial fractions of hadronic and partonic degrees of freedom above $T_c$.

## Key claim

The paper presents an effective model that reproduces the equation of state from lattice QCD simulations, but the authors explicitly note that the high-temperature phase of the simpler Mott-Hagedorn resonance gas model is "unphysical" because it ignores the Mott effect for light hadrons, and the agreement with lattice data at high temperatures is "accidental" due to the number of light hadron degrees of freedom coinciding with that of quarks and gluons.

## Method

The hadronic phase is described by an extended Mott-Hagedorn resonance gas (MHRG) with a state-dependent hadron resonance width \(\Gamma_i(T)\) given by the inverse collision time scale from a binary collision approximation. The quark-gluon plasma phase is described by the Polyakov-loop improved Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model, further refined by adding perturbative corrections to \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s)\) for the high-momentum region above the three-momentum cutoff. The total pressure is the sum of the MHRG pressure, the PNJL mean-field pressure, and the perturbative correction pressure.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature region is \(T_c \sim 150 - 170\) MeV, where the rapid rise in degrees of freedom occurs.
- The mass threshold for light hadrons treated as stable is \(m_0 = 1\) GeV; the number of light hadron degrees of freedom below this threshold is 32, which accidentally coincides with the quark and gluon degrees of freedom (31.5) for \(N_c = N_f = 3\).
- The Hagedorn temperature used in the state-independent width ansatz is \(T_H = 165\) MeV, with parameters \(C_{\Gamma} = 10^{-4}\) MeV, \(N_m = 2.5\), \(N_T = 6.5\).
- The MHRG contribution vanishes at \(T \sim 250\) MeV, and the transition region where both hadronic and partonic degrees of freedom contribute is \(170 \le T[\text{MeV}] \le 250\).
- The pion vacuum radius is \(r_\pi = 0.59\) fm, the nucleon vacuum radius is \(r_N = 0.74\) fm, and the nucleon hard core radius is \(r_0 = 0.45\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the Mott-Hagedorn resonance gas model of Blaschke (2003) and Turko (2011, 2013) by introducing a state-dependent hadron resonance width based on the collision time scale from Blaschke (2011), replacing the earlier heuristic ansatz. It also incorporates the PNJL model of Fukushima (2003) and Ratti (2005) with perturbative \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s)\) corrections. The model enables quantitative extraction of the partial fractions of hadronic and partonic degrees of freedom above \(T_c\), which can be applied to phenomenology of heavy quark observables in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions, improving upon the arbitrary assumptions used by Nahrgang (2013).
