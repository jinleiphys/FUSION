# A new look at $ψ(4160)$ and $ψ(4230)$

> By simultaneously analyzing the cross section data of $e^+e^-\rightarrow D\bar D, D\bar D^*, D^*\bar D^*, D\bar Dπ$ in a coupled-channel scheme with unitarity, we found that, in contrast to the conventional wisdom, the pole of $ψ(2^3D_1)$ might be located at about $\sqrt{s}=4222-32i\mathrm{MeV}$. This observation implies a possibility that the two resonances, dubbed the $ψ(4160)$ and $ψ(4230)$ in the PDG table now, might be the same $ψ(2^3D_1)$ state. Such a suggestion could provide more insight to our understanding the enigmatic decay properties of $ψ(4160)$ and $ψ(4230)$. Furthermore, this coupled-channel scheme could be applied to study other phenomena with several interfering resonances.

## Key claim

The pole of the $\psi(2^3D_1)$ state might be located at about $\sqrt{s}=4222-32i$ MeV, which is higher than the conventionally accepted mass of $\psi(4160)$ and close to the mass of $\psi(4230)$, suggesting that these two resonances listed separately in the PDG table could actually be the same state.

## Method

The authors employed a generalized Friedrichs-Lee (GFL) coupled-channel scheme that satisfies unitarity to simultaneously fit Belle cross-section data for $e^+e^-\rightarrow D\bar D$, $D\bar D^*$, $D^*\bar D^*$, and $D\bar D\pi$ processes in the energy range 3.73 to 5.00 GeV. They used the Quark Pair Creation (QPC) model with Godfrey-Isgur wave functions to calculate coupling vertex functions between five conventional charmonium bare states ($\psi(2^3S_1)$, $\psi(1^3D_1)$, $\psi(3^3S_1)$, $\psi(2^3D_1)$, $\psi(4^3S_1)$) and 16 coupled hadronic channels, then extracted pole positions on unphysical Riemann sheets through analytical continuation of the amplitude to the complex energy plane.

## Key numbers

* The extracted pole of $\psi(2^3D_1)$ on the fourth Riemann sheet is located at $z_{0,2}^{IV}=4.222-\frac{0.064}{2}i$ GeV, corresponding to a pole mass of 4222 MeV and width of 64 MeV.
* The fit achieved $\chi^2/d.o.f.=379/(293-15)=1.36$.
* The pole mass and width of $\psi(2^3D_1)$ (4222 MeV, 64 MeV) are similar to the PDG average values for $\psi(4230)$: $M=4222.7\pm 2.6$ MeV, $\Gamma=49\pm 8$ MeV.
* The second-sheet pole at $z_0^{II}=3.762-\frac{0.020}{2}i$ GeV agrees with $\psi(3770)$, and the fourth-sheet pole at $z_{0,1}^{IV}=4.028-\frac{0.068}{2}i$ GeV agrees with $\psi(4040)$.
* The bare mass of $\psi(2D)$ was found to be 4.62 GeV, with a QPC strength parameter $\gamma=4.48$.

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel analysis approach of Uglov (2016) by providing an analytical framework with proper unitarity cuts and pole extraction, rather than just describing cross-section lineshapes. The result contradicts the conventional wisdom that $\psi(4160)$ and $\psi(4230)$ are distinct states, as supported by quark potential model predictions from Godfrey and Isgur (1985) and Eichten (1979). If confirmed by further analyses of $R$ values and $e^+e^-\rightarrow J/\psi\pi\pi$ data, this interpretation could resolve the enigmatic difference in open-charm versus hidden-charm decay modes of these two states without invoking exotic origins.
