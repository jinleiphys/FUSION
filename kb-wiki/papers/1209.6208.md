# Antikaon-nucleon dynamics and its application to few-body systems

> We overview the recent progress in the investigation of the antikaon dynamics with single nucleon and with few-body nuclei, based on chiral SU(3) symmetry. We first show how the Lambda(1405) resonance emerges from the coupled-channel Kbar N-pi Sigma interaction. Next, we present the construction of an up-to-date Kbar N interaction in response to the recent measurement of kaonic hydrogen. Finally, we discuss the possibility of the antikaon bound states in nuclei from the perspective of few-body physics.

## Key claim

The Lambda(1405) resonance emerges as a quasi-bound state of the antikaon-nucleon (Kbar N) system embedded in the strongly correlated pi-Sigma continuum, rather than as a conventional three-quark state; this picture is supported by chiral coupled-channel dynamics, and the recent SIDDHARTA measurement of kaonic hydrogen provides a strong constraint that reduces uncertainty in the subthreshold extrapolation of the Kbar N interaction.

## Method

The author reviews the chiral SU(3) coupled-channel approach to Kbar N-pi Sigma scattering, where the interaction kernel derived from chiral perturbation theory (including Weinberg-Tomozawa, Born, and next-to-leading order terms) is inserted into a scattering equation to generate the scattering amplitude. A systematic chi-squared analysis is performed including total cross sections of K- p scattering, threshold branching ratios, and the SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen shift and width, while the pi Sigma mass spectrum is not used in the fit but compared afterward. For the Kbar NN few-body system, results from Faddeev and variational calculations by several groups are compared, noting which calculations include energy-dependent versus energy-independent Kbar N interactions.

## Key numbers

- The Lambda(1405) resonance lies slightly below the Kbar N threshold, with a binding energy of approximately 15-30 MeV relative to that threshold.
- The best-fit model including NLO terms achieves chi-squared per degree of freedom = 0.96, while models with only Weinberg-Tomozawa or Weinberg-Tomozawa plus Born terms give 1.12 and 1.15 respectively.
- For the Kbar NN system with total spin J=0, theoretical binding energies range from 9-16 MeV (Ikeda 2010tk, energy-dependent interaction) to 50-70 MeV (Shevchenko 2006xy, energy-independent interaction), with three-body decay widths ranging from 34-46 MeV to approximately 100 MeV.
- The Lambda_c(2595) resonance in the charm sector has a width of approximately 3 MeV, much narrower than the Lambda(1405).

## Context

This work extends the chiral coupled-channel framework previously applied to meson-baryon scattering by Kaiser (1995), Oset (1998), Oller (2000), and Lutz (2001), and builds on the two-pole structure of Lambda(1405) identified by Jido (2003). The inclusion of the SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen measurement (Bazzi 2011) significantly reduces the uncertainty in the subthreshold Kbar N interaction compared to earlier analyses by Borasoy (2004, 2005). The review enables more reliable predictions for antikaon bound states in few-nucleon systems, such as Kbar NN, and opens the possibility of studying analogous systems in the charm sector, where the narrow width of Lambda_c(2595) may facilitate experimental observation.
