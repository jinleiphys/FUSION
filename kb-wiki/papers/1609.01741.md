# Partial-Wave Analysis of Nucleon-Nucleon Elastic Scattering Data

> Energy-dependent and single-energy fits to the existing nucleon-nucleon database have been updated to incorporate recent measurements. The fits cover a region from threshold to 3 GeV, in the laboratory kinetic energy, for proton-proton scattering, with an upper limit of 1.3 GeV for neutron-proton scattering. Experiments carried out at the COSY-WASA and COSY-ANKE facilities have had a significant impact on the partial-wave solutions. Results are discussed in terms of both partial-wave and direct reconstruction amplitudes.

## Key claim

The updated partial-wave analysis (PWA) of nucleon-nucleon elastic scattering data, incorporating recent COSY-WASA and COSY-ANKE measurements, finds that the sharp energy dependence in the neutron-proton (np) analyzing power A_y can be reproduced by introducing a pole in the coupled ^3D_3–^3G_3 partial waves, providing supporting evidence for the WASA dibaryon candidate; however, the weighted fit (WF16) that best accommodates the new data yields a higher overall χ²/datum for the full database, indicating that older data are implicitly given less weight.

## Method

Energy-dependent and single-energy fits were performed to the global nucleon-nucleon database using a K-matrix formalism with exchange and production S-matrices, including one-pion exchange and a Chew-Mandelstam K-matrix for inelastic channels. The fits cover proton-proton (pp) scattering from threshold to 3 GeV in laboratory kinetic energy and neutron-proton (np) scattering up to 1.3 GeV. To accommodate new COSY data, the pp A_y data were weighted by a factor of 4 in the fit, while for the forward pp cross sections the renormalization penalty term in the χ² function was increased until fitted normalization factors matched quoted systematic errors.

## Key numbers

- The np analyzing power A_y data from COSY-WASA show a narrow structure reproduced by a pole at [(2380 ± 10) – i(40 ± 5)] MeV in the coupled ^3D_3–^3G_3 partial waves, corresponding to a resonance mass M ≈ 2380 MeV and width Γ ≈ 70 MeV.
- The SP07 partial-wave solution was determined from approximately 25,000 pp data points (to 3 GeV) and 13,000 np data points (to 1.3 GeV).
- For pp scattering from threshold to 350 MeV, all fits give a χ²/datum of about 1.25 over the full database; for pp scattering up to 1 GeV, the χ²/datum increases to about 1.5, compared to slightly below 2.0 for the SM16 fit covering the full pp range (threshold to 3 GeV).
- The total cross section dataset includes measurements with statistical errors of the order of 0.1% (Ref. bu66).

## Context

This work updates the previous SP07 analysis (Workman et al., 2007) and extends the older SM97 fit (Stoks et al., 1997), incorporating new COSY-WASA and COSY-ANKE measurements that were poorly predicted by SP07. The results are compared to direct amplitude reconstructions from Saclay (Bystricky et al., 1998; Ball et al., 1998) and to the Nijmegen partial-wave and potential model analyses, which achieved a χ²/datum closer to unity over a more restricted dataset and energy range. The updated fits enable improved input for proton-deuteron scattering, deuteron electro-disintegration, and Geant4 simulations, while the np PWA remains limited to 1.3 GeV and the sharp structure in A_y is localized to a narrow energy range, requiring further programmatic study to extend the analysis.
