# Violation of geometric scaling in DIS due to Coulomb corrections

> I compute the Coulomb correction to the total and diffractive cross sections for virtual photon scattering off a heavy nucleus at low $x$. I show that it violates the geometric scaling in a wide range of photon virtualities and is weakly $x$-independent. In heavy nuclei at low $Q^2$ the Coulomb correction to the total and diffractive cross sections is about 20% and 40% correspondingly.

# Violation of geometric scaling in DIS due to Coulomb corrections

## Key claim

The Coulomb correction to the total and diffractive cross sections for virtual photon scattering off a heavy nucleus at low x violates geometric scaling in a wide range of photon virtualities, and is weakly x-independent; the claim is conditional on the nucleus being heavy (large A) and the photon virtuality being low, where the correction reaches about 20% for total and 40% for diffractive cross sections.

## Method

The author computes the Coulomb correction by employing the Glauber-Mueller model, which accounts for multiple scatterings of the q-qbar dipole in the nucleus, separating QCD and QED contributions to the dipole-nucleon elastic scattering amplitude. The dipole-nucleus cross section is derived using a "cylindrical nucleus" model that approximates the nuclear thickness function as a step function, and the QCD contribution is modeled using the Golec-Biernat-Wusthoff (GBW) parameterization of the saturation momentum. The total and diffractive cross sections for virtual photon scattering are then obtained by integrating the dipole cross section over the light-cone wave functions of the photon.

## Key numbers

- For heavy nuclei at low Q^2, the Coulomb correction to the total cross section is about 20% and to the diffractive cross section is about 40%.
- The saturation momentum is parameterized as Q_s^2 = Q_0^2 (x_0/x)^lambda with Q_0 = 1 GeV, x_0 = 3.04 x 10^{-4}, lambda = 0.288, and effective proton radius R_p = 3.1 GeV^{-1}.
- The relative size of the electromagnetic contribution to the total cross section in the regime m_f^2 << Q_s^2 << Q^2 is given by (8 ln(Q^2/m_f^2))/(Q_s^2 R_A^2 ln(Q^2/Q_s^2)) (alpha Z)^2 ln(W^2/(4 m_f^2 m_N R_A)).
- In the regime m_f^2 << Q^2 << Q_s^2, the relative size is (8 ln(Q^2/m_f^2))/(Q^2 R_A^2) (alpha Z)^2 ln(W^2/(4 m_f^2 m_N R_A)).
- The constituent quark mass used in the calculation is m_f = 140 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of Coulomb corrections in deep inelastic scattering off heavy nuclei, which were previously discussed at medium x by Solvignon (2009), to the low-x regime where geometric scaling is a fundamental property of high-energy QCD derived from the Balitsky-Kovchegov evolution equation. The paper demonstrates that the Coulomb correction, which arises from the electromagnetic interaction of the q-qbar dipole with the nuclear Coulomb field, violates the geometric scaling property established by Stasto, Golec-Biernat, and Wusthoff (2000) and derived from low-x evolution by Levin, Iancu, and others. The results indicate that for experiments at the proposed Electron Ion Collider, the Coulomb correction must be controlled to reliably extract information about cold nuclear matter structure.
