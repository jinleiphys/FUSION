# Field-theoretical approach to particle oscillations in absorbing matter

> The $ab$ oscillations in absorbing matter are considered. The standard model based on optical potential does not describe the total $ab$ transition probability as well as the channel corresponding to absorption of the $b$-particle. We calculate directly the off-diagonal matrix element in the framework of field-theoretical approach. Contrary to one-particle model, the final state absorption does not tend to suppress the channels mentioned above or, similarly, calculation with hermitian Hamiltonian leads to increase the corresponding values. The model reproduces all the results on the particle oscillations, however it is oriented to the description of the above-mentioned channels. Also we touch on the problem of infrared singularities. The approach under study is infrared-free.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the standard optical-potential model for particle oscillations in absorbing matter fails to describe the total transition probability and the absorption channel because it uses a non-Hermitian Hamiltonian that violates unitarity; the field-theoretical approach with a finite time interval corrects this by directly calculating the off-diagonal matrix element, and contrary to the one-particle model, final-state absorption does not suppress these channels but instead increases the corresponding values.

## Method

The author formulates the \(n\bar{n}\) transition in matter using a field-theoretical Hamiltonian that separates the \(n\bar{n}\) conversion vertex from the Hermitian \(\bar{n}\)-medium interaction, then calculates the \(S\)-matrix elements for the two-step process (conversion followed by annihilation) using a finite-time-interval approach to avoid infrared singularities arising from zero momentum transfer in the conversion vertex. The antineutron propagator is kept bare (self-energy \(\Sigma = 0\)), and the annihilation amplitude is defined directly from the \(\bar{n}\)-medium interaction Hamiltonian, preserving unitarity. The model is verified by reproducing the potential-model results for the case of a constant potential.

## Key numbers

* For the potential model, the total \(n\bar{n}\) transition probability in matter is \(W_t^{\text{pot}}(t) \approx 4\epsilon^2 t / \Gamma\), where \(\epsilon = 1/\tau_{n\bar{n}}\) and \(\Gamma\) is the annihilation width of \(\bar{n}\) in the medium.
* The suppression factor relative to free-space oscillations is \(R_{\text{pot}} = W_t^{\text{pot}} / W_f \sim 1/(|V|t) \ll 1\), where \(V\) is the energy gap (self-energy) and \(W_f = \epsilon^2 t^2\).
* For the free-space \(\bar{n}N\) annihilation, the ratio of annihilation to total cross section is \(r = \sigma_{\text{ann}}^{\bar{n}p} / \sigma_{\text{el}}^{\bar{n}p} > 2.5\) at low energies.
* In the low-density limit, the relative annihilation probability of the intermediate antineutron is \(r_1 = \sigma_a / \sigma_t > 0.7\), where \(\sigma_t = \sigma_a + \sigma_s\).

## Context

This work extends the critique of the optical-potential model for particle oscillations in absorbing matter, previously noted by Nazaruk (2005, 2006), and provides a field-theoretical alternative that respects unitarity. It directly addresses the \(n\bar{n}\) transition in nuclear matter, where final-state absorption is extremely strong, and enables a correct calculation of the annihilation channel probability, which the potential model fails to describe. The approach is infrared-free and reproduces standard oscillation results (e.g., neutrino oscillations, \(K^0\bar{K}^0\)) as special cases, while also resolving the competition between scattering and annihilation of the intermediate antineutron.
