# Hadrons in strongly interacting matter

> We review the current status of theories and experiments aiming at an understanding and a determination of the properties of light vector and scalar mesons inside strongly interacting hadronic matter. Starting from a discussion of the relevant symmetries of QCD and their connection with the hadronic description through QCD sum rules we then discuss hadronic models used to calculate the in-medium self-energies of hadrons and their spectral functions. The difficulties to link these calculated properties to actual observables are emphasized. Finally, we review in detail all the running experiments searching for in-medium changes of vector and scalar mesons, both with relativistic heavy-ion reactions as well as with elementary reactions on (cold) nuclei. Inconsistencies among experimental results are discussed. While almost all experiments observe a considerable broadening of vector mesons inside the nuclear medium, no evidence for mass changes is observed in the majority of the experiments.

## Key claim

This review finds that while almost all experiments observe a considerable broadening of vector mesons inside the nuclear medium, no evidence for mass changes is observed in the majority of the experiments, with the abstract's claim of "no evidence for mass changes" qualified by the existence of some experimental inconsistencies and the difficulty of linking calculated in-medium properties to actual observables.

- The CBELSA/TAPS Collaboration reported modification of the omega-meson lifetime in nuclear matter (Kotulla et al., 2008, Phys. Rev. Lett. 100, 192302).

## Context

This review extends and updates earlier reviews on hadron properties in the nuclear medium by Hayano and Hatsuda (2008) and on chiral symmetry restoration and dileptons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions by Rapp and Wambach (2000) and Rapp, Wambach, and van Hees (2009). It also builds on the hadronic and electromagnetic probes review by Cassing and Bratkovskaya (1999). The work enables a systematic comparison of theoretical predictions from hadronic models and QCD sum rules with experimental data from running experiments at facilities such as CERN SPS, KEK, and CBELSA/TAPS, while highlighting the need to resolve inconsistencies among experimental results regarding mass shifts versus broadening of vector mesons.
