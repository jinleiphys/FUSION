# Local polarization and isothermal local equilibrium in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We show that the inclusion of a recently found additional term of the spin polarization vector at local equilibrium which is linear in the symmetrized gradients of the velocity field, and the assumption of hadron production at constant temperature restore the quantitative agreement between hydrodynamic model predictions and local polarization measurements in relativistic heavy ion collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200$ GeV. The longitudinal component of the spin polarization vector turns out to be very sensitive to the temperature value, with a good fit around 155 MeV. The implications of this finding are discussed.

## Key claim

Including a recently found additional term of the spin polarization vector at local equilibrium that is linear in the symmetrized gradients of the velocity field (thermal shear), combined with the assumption that hadron production occurs at constant temperature (isothermal local equilibrium), restores quantitative agreement between hydrodynamic model predictions and local polarization measurements in relativistic heavy ion collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV.

## Method

The authors used two different 3+1D viscous hydrodynamic codes in the Israel-Stewart formulation (vHLLE and ECHO-QGP) to simulate Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV. They computed the spin polarization vector of Lambda hyperons using a new formula that incorporates both kinematic vorticity and kinematic shear tensors, derived from an isothermal local equilibrium density operator that expands only the four-velocity field (not the temperature) on the decoupling hypersurface. The results were compared with experimental data on local polarization as a function of azimuthal angle and transverse momentum.

## Key numbers

- The longitudinal component of the spin polarization vector shows a good fit around a decoupling temperature of 155 MeV.
- For temperatures below approximately 135 MeV, the sign of the longitudinal polarization flips.
- The analysis was performed for 20-60% central Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV.
- The transverse momentum integration range for comparison with data was 0.5-6 GeV.
- The conversion from angular measurements to polarization used the hyperon decay parameter alpha_H = 0.732.
- The impact parameter for ECHO-QGP simulations was set to 9.2 fm.

## Context

This work extends the standard local equilibrium formula for spin polarization based on thermal vorticity (Becattini 2013) which successfully predicted global polarization but failed to reproduce local polarization patterns, particularly the sign of the longitudinal component and the azimuthal dependence of the perpendicular component. The new thermal shear term was recently identified in theoretical work (Becattini 2021, Liu 2021). The finding that the QGP hadronizes at constant temperature to a high accuracy enables spin polarization to serve as a sensitive probe of the space-time details of hadron formation, and opens the way for comparisons as a function of transverse momentum and rapidity, as well as extensions to lower energy collisions where chemical potentials become relevant.
