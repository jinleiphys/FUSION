# $Λ^+_c$, $Σ_c$, $Ξ_c$ and $Λ_b$ hypernuclei in the quark-meson coupling model

> Charmed (and bottom) hypernuclei are studied in the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model. This completes systematic studies of charmed ($Λ_c^+, Σ_c, Ξ_c$), and $Λ_b$ hypernuclei in the QMC model. Effects of the Pauli blocking due to the underlying quark structure of baryons, and the $Σ_c N - Λ_c N$ channel coupling are phenomenologically taken into account at the hadronic level in the same way as those included for strange hypernuclei. Our results suggest that the $Σ_c^{++}$ and $Ξ_c^+$ hypernuclei are very unlikely to be formed, while the $Λ_c^+$, $Ξ_c^0$ and $Λ_b$ hypernuclei are quite likely to be formed. For the $Σ_c^+$ hypernuclei, the formation probability is non-zero though small. A detailed analysis is also made about the phenomenologically introduced Pauli blocking and channel coupling effects for the $Σ_c^0$ hypernuclei.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that $\Sigma_c^{++}$ and $\Xi_c^+$ hypernuclei are very unlikely to be formed, while $\Lambda_c^+$, $\Xi_c^0$ and $\Lambda_b$ hypernuclei are quite likely to be formed, with the caveat that these conclusions depend on phenomenological treatments of Pauli blocking at the quark level and $\Sigma_c N - \Lambda_c N$ channel coupling effects that are modeled after similar treatments for strange hypernuclei.

## Method

The authors solve relativistic mean-field equations of motion for a closed-shell nuclear core plus one charmed or bottom baryon within the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, using the Hartree approximation. The effective masses and coupling strengths for the heavy baryons are calculated self-consistently from the MIT bag model, with the scalar and vector meson fields coupled directly to the light ($u$, $d$) quarks inside the baryons. Phenomenological Pauli blocking and $\Sigma_{c,b} N - \Lambda_{c,b} N$ channel coupling effects are incorporated at the hadronic level following the same approach used previously for strange hypernuclei by Tsushima et al. (1998).

## Key numbers

- The bag pressure is $B = (170\ \text{MeV})^4$, with the nucleon bag radius in free space $R_N = 0.8\ \text{fm}$
- The quark-meson coupling constants are $(g^q_\sigma, g^q_\omega, g^q_\rho) = (5.69, 2.72, 9.33)$, yielding $g_\sigma \equiv g^N_\sigma = 8.23$
- At normal nuclear matter density $\rho_B = 0.15\ \text{fm}^{-3}$, the effective masses are: $M_N^\star = 754.5\ \text{MeV}$, $M_{\Lambda_c^+}^\star = 2162.5\ \text{MeV}$, $M_{\Sigma_c}^\star = 2330.2\ \text{MeV}$, $M_{\Xi_c}^\star = 2408.0\ \text{MeV}$, $M_{\Lambda_b}^\star = 5502.9\ \text{MeV}$
- The slope parameters $a_j$ for the linear parametrization of $C_j(\overline{\sigma})$ are: $a_N = 8.8 \times 10^{-4}\ \text{MeV}^{-1}$, $a_{\Lambda_c^+} = 9.8 \times 10^{-4}\ \text{MeV}^{-1}$, $a_{\Sigma_c} = 10.3 \times 10^{-4}\ \text{MeV}^{-1}$, $a_{\Xi_c} = 9.9 \times 10^{-4}\ \text{MeV}^{-1}$, $a_{\Lambda_b} = 10.9 \times 10^{-4}\ \text{MeV}^{-1}$
- Spin-orbit contributions to single-particle energies are typically of order $0.01\ \text{MeV}$, with the largest case being $\simeq 0.5\ \text{MeV}$ among all heavy baryon hypernuclei calculated
- The difference between QMC-I and QMC-II results is $\sim 16\%$ for the largest case, typically $\sim 10\%$ or less, and for hyperon effective masses the differences are less than $\sim 8\%$

## Context

This work extends the QMC model studies of strange hypernuclei by Tsushima et al. (1998) to charmed and bottom hypernuclei, completing a systematic study that began with predictions for $\Lambda_c^+$ and $\Lambda_b$ hypernuclei by Tsushima and Khanna (2003). The formation of $\Lambda_c^+$ and $\Lambda_b$ hypernuclei was first predicted in the mid-1970s by Tyapkin (1975) and by Dover and Kahana (1977). The results suggest that future experiments at the Japan Hadron Facility (JHF) with 50 GeV beam energy, as well as at RHIC, CERN, and Fermilab, could search for these heavy baryon hypernuclei, with the $\Lambda_c^+$, $\Xi_c^0$ and $\Lambda_b$ species being the most promising candidates.
