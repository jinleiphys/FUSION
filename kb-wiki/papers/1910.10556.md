# Unified description of hadron yield ratios from dynamical core-corona initialization

> We develop the dynamical core-corona initialization framework as a phenomenological description of the formation of quark gluon plasma (QGP) fluids in high-energy nuclear collisions. Using this framework, we investigate the fraction of the fluidized energy to the total energy and strange hadron yield ratios as functions of multiplicity and scrutinize the multiplicity scaling of hadron yield ratios recently reported by the ALICE Collaboration. Our results strongly indicate that the QGP fluids are partly formed even at the averaged multiplicity for nonsingle diffractive p+p events.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the dynamical core-corona initialization (DCCI) framework can reproduce the continuous increase of strange hadron yield ratios with multiplicity observed by ALICE, and that QGP fluids are partly formed even at the average multiplicity of non-single diffractive p+p events. The hedge is that this conclusion depends on tuning the free parameter \(a_0\) to approximately 368 to match experimental data, and the framework assumes instantaneous thermalization of deposited energy.

## Method

The authors developed the DCCI model by combining PYTHIA 8.230 for initial parton generation with relativistic hydrodynamics including source terms. Partons deposit four-momentum into QGP fluids at a rate proportional to the local parton density \(\rho_i\) and inversely proportional to \(p_T^2\), with a free parameter \(a_0\) controlling the fluidization intensity. After initialization from proper time \(\tau_{00}=0.1\) fm to \(\tau_0=0.6\) fm, the hydrodynamic evolution continues without source terms until the decoupling temperature \(T_{\mathrm{dec}}=160\) MeV, where fluids are particlized via the Cooper-Frye formula; surviving partons undergo string fragmentation in PYTHIA.

## Key numbers

- The free parameter \(a_0\) was varied among 10, 20, and 100 for Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV; the value most consistent with ALICE data was extracted as \(a_0 \approx 368\).
- The saturation multiplicity from global fitting of ALICE data for p+p, p+Pb, and Pb+Pb collisions was estimated as \(\langle dN_{\mathrm{ch}}/d\eta \rangle \approx 31\).
- The optimized power-law fit for saturation multiplicity as a function of \(a_0\) gave \(f(a_0) = a_0^{-1.8} \times 10^6\) with reduced chi-square \(\chi^2 \approx 1.2\).
- The decoupling temperature used for particlization was \(T_{\mathrm{dec}} = 160\) MeV.
- The formation time and hydrodynamic initial time were \(\tau_{00}=0.1\) fm and \(\tau_0=0.6\) fm, respectively.
- Gaussian widths in transverse and longitudinal directions were \(\sigma_\perp = 0.5\) fm and \(\sigma_{\eta_s} = 0.5\).
- The cascade-to-pion ratio from the core (fluid) was approximately 0.0055, and from string fragmentation approximately 0.0018.

## Context

This work extends the dynamical initialization framework of Okai (2017), Shen (2017), Akamatsu (2018), and Kanakubo (2018) by incorporating the core-corona picture to describe the transition from string fragmentation to statistical hadronization with increasing multiplicity. It directly addresses the ALICE Collaboration (2017) observation of strangeness enhancement in small systems, which had been interpreted as a QGP signal by Rafelski (1982) and Koch (1986). The DCCI model enables future studies of how the fraction of fluidized energy varies across system sizes from p+p to Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies.
