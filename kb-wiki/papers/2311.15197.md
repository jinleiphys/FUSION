# Spin polarization and spin alignment from quantum kinetic theory with self-energy corrections

> We derive the quantum kinetic theory for massive fermions with collision terms and self-energy corrections based on quantum field theory. We adopt an effective power counting scheme with $\hbar$ expansion to obtain the leading-order perturbative solutions of the vector and axial Wigner functions and the corresponding kinetic equations. We observe that both the onshell relation and the structure of Wigner functions, along with the kinetic equations, are modified due to the presence of self-energies and their space-time gradients. We further apply our formalism to investigate the spin polarization phenomena in relativistic heavy ion collisions and derive the modification to the spin polarization spectrum of massive quarks. We find that the gradient of vector self-energy plays a similar role to the background electromagnetic fields, which induces a more dominant contribution than the collisional effects by a naive power counting in the gradient expansion and weak coupling. Our findings could further modify the spin polarization of strange quarks and spin alignment of $ϕ$ mesons beyond local thermal equilibrium.

## Key claim

The authors derive a quantum kinetic theory for massive fermions that includes both collision terms and self-energy corrections, finding that the gradient of the vector self-energy plays a role similar to background electromagnetic fields and induces a more dominant contribution than collisional effects by naive power counting in the gradient expansion and weak coupling.

## Method

Starting from quantum field theory with a background U(1) field and using the Schwinger-Keldysh formalism, the authors derive the Kadanoff-Baym equations for gauge-invariant Wigner functions and obtain perturbative solutions for the vector and axial-vector Wigner functions up to first order in an \(\hbar\) expansion. They then derive the corresponding scalar kinetic equation and axial kinetic equation, incorporating both the tadpole (one-point potential) and retarded self-energy contributions, and apply the formalism to compute modifications to the spin polarization spectrum of massive quarks in relativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Key numbers

* The tadpole self-energy is of order \(\mathcal{O}(\hbar^{-1})\) while the one-loop (bubble) self-energy is of order \(\mathcal{O}(\hbar^{0})\) in the \(\hbar\) power counting scheme.
* The one-loop self-energy correction is found to be of first order in gradient and with coupling dependence, and simple numerical estimation shows these self-energy corrections are important at low transverse momenta in the \(s\) equilibrium scenario.

## Context

This work extends the quantum kinetic theory framework previously developed for massless chiral fermions (e.g., chiral kinetic theory by Son and Yamamoto (2012), Hidaka et al. (2016-2017)) to massive fermions with self-energy corrections, generalizing the approach of Yang et al. (2020). It also builds on the spin polarization studies for hyperons observed by STAR (2017, 2019) and ALICE (2019) collaborations, and the spin alignment of vector mesons studied by Sheng et al. (2019-2023). The findings provide theoretical support for the vector-meson field model proposed by Sheng et al. and enable further investigation of off-equilibrium corrections to local spin polarization beyond the collisional effects previously considered by Wang et al. (2020-2022) and Weickgenannt et al. (2022).
