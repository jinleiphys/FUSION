# Scaling behaviour of charged particles generated in Xe$-$Xe collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}}$ = 5.44 TeV using the AMPT model

> The spatial configurations of particles produced in the kinematic phase space during a heavy-ion collision reflect the characteristics of the system created in the collision. The scaling behaviour of the multiplicity fluctuations is studied for the charged particles generated in Xe--Xe collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}}$~=~5.44~TeV using the String Melting (SM) mode of the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model. The scaling behaviour of the normalized factorial moments ($F_\text{q}$) give significant information about the dynamics of the system under study. A linear power-law growth of the $F_\text{q}$ with the increasing phase space resolution, termed as intermittency, is investigated. The anomalous fractal dimension $D_\text{q}$ is determined, which is linked to the self-similarity and fractal nature of the particle emission spectra, a dependence of which on the order of the moment ($q$) is characterised by the intermittency index ($φ_{\text{q}}$). Relating $q^{\rm{th}}$ order Normalised Factorial Moment (NFM) with $F_{2}$, the scaling exponent ($ν$) is determined that quantifies the dynamics of the system created by these collisions and is analyzed for its dependence on the transverse momentum bin width ($Δp_\text{T}$). Results presented may be interpreted as model predictions and baseline expectations.

# Scaling behaviour of charged particles generated in Xe–Xe collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.44 TeV using the AMPT model

## Key claim

The authors report that charged particles generated in Xe–Xe collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.44 TeV using the String Melting mode of the AMPT model do not exhibit intermittent scaling or critical fluctuations, and the scaling exponent nu is found to be approximately 1.78 plus or minus 0.04, significantly larger than the value of 1.304 predicted by Ginzburg-Landau theory for second-order phase transitions. The results are presented as model predictions and baseline expectations for experimental comparisons, with the hedge that the AMPT model does not incorporate critical fluctuation dynamics.

## Method

The authors generated approximately half a million minimum-bias Xe–Xe collision events at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.44 TeV using the String Melting mode of the AMPT model (version Ampt-v1.26t9b-v2.26t9b), selecting around 300,000 central events with impact parameter b less than or equal to 3.5 fm. They calculated normalized factorial moments F_q(M) for orders q = 2, 3, 4, and 5 in a two-dimensional (eta, phi) phase space with |eta| less than or equal to 0.8 and full azimuth, partitioning the space into M^2 cells with M ranging from 4 to 80, for charged particles (protons, pions, kaons) in several transverse momentum bins between 0.4 and 2.0 GeV/c. The intermittency indices phi_q were extracted from power-law fits of ln F_q versus ln M^2, and the scaling exponent nu was determined from the relation between beta_q and (q-1) using F-scaling of higher-order NFMs with the second-order NFM.

## Key numbers

- Scaling exponent nu = 1.78 plus or minus 0.04, averaged over all transverse momentum bins studied, for Xe–Xe collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.44 TeV using the SM AMPT model
- The Ginzburg-Landau theory prediction for second-order phase transition gives nu = 1.304, cited as a reference value
- Pb–Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV using the SM AMPT model gave nu approximately 1.7 (Sharma 2018)
- The Successive Contraction and Randomization (SCR) model with critical fluctuations gives nu = 1.41 (Yang 2017)
- The two-dimensional Ising model intermittency analysis gives nu approximately 1.3, while with critical fluctuations nu = 1.04
- Central events selected with impact parameter b less than or equal to 3.5 fm from approximately 500,000 minimum-bias events
- Pseudorapidity acceptance |eta| less than or equal to 0.8, with M ranging from 4 to 80 bins per dimension
- Transverse momentum bins studied: narrow bins of width Delta p_T = 0.2 GeV/c (0.4 to 0.6 and 0.6 to 0.8 GeV/c) and wide bins (0.4 to 1.0, 0.6 to 1.0, 0.4 to 2.0, and 0.6 to 2.0 GeV/c)

## Context

This work extends previous intermittency analyses within the AMPT framework for Pb–Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV (Sharma 2018) and Au–Au collisions at RHIC energies (Xie 2013) to the intermediate-sized, deformed Xe–Xe system at LHC energies. The results establish baseline expectations for multiplicity fluctuations and scaling behaviour from the AMPT String Melting model, which does not incorporate critical dynamics, enabling future comparisons with experimental data from the LHC to distinguish non-critical dynamical contributions from possible critical effects near the QCD critical endpoint. The authors note that the deformed nuclear geometry of Xe nuclei and the corresponding initial-state conditions provide a unique opportunity to investigate system-size dependence of fluctuation patterns within a consistent framework.
