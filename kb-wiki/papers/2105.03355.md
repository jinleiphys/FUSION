# Dalitz plot studies of $D^0 \to K^0_S K^+ K^-$ decays in a factorization approach

> The $\textit{BABAR}$ Collaboration data of the $D^0 \to K^0_S K^+ K^-$ process are analyzed within a quasi two-body factorization framework. In earlier studies, assuming $D^0$ transitions to two kaons and the transitions between one kaon and two kaons to proceed through the dominant intermediate resonances, we approximated them as being proportional to the kaon form factors. To obtain good fits, one has to multiply the scalar-kaon form factors, derived from unitary relativistic coupled-channel models or in a dispersion relation approach, by phenomenological energy-dependent functions. The final state kaon-kaon interactions in the $S$-, $P$- and $D$- waves are taken into account. All $S$-wave channels are treated in a unitary way. The $K^+K^-$ and $\bar K^0 K^+$ $S$-wave effective mass squared distributions, corrected for phase space, are shown, in a model-independent manner, to be significantly different. Then the $f_0(980)$ resonance must be included at variance with the BABAR analysis. The best fit has 19 free parameters and indicates i) the dominance of annihilation amplitudes, ii) a large dominance of the $f_0(980)$ meson in the near threshold $K^+K^-$ invariant mass distribution and iii) a sizable branching fraction to the $K_S^0 \ [ρ(770)^+ + ρ(1450)^+ + ρ(1700)^+] $ final states. An appendix provides an update of the determination of the isoscalar-scalar meson-meson amplitudes based on an enlarged set of data. A second appendix proposes two alternative fits based on scalar-kaon form factors calculated from the Muskhelishvili-Omnès dispersion relation approach. These fits have $χ^2$ quite close to that of the best fit but they show important contributions from both the $f_0$ and $a_0^0$ mesons and a weaker role of the $ρ^+$ mesons.

## Key claim

The authors show that, contrary to the BABAR analysis which omitted it, the \(f_0(980)\) resonance must be included in the phenomenological description of the \(D^0 \to K^0_S K^+ K^-\) decay, because the \(K^+K^-\) and \(\bar K^0 K^+\) S-wave effective mass squared distributions, corrected for phase space, are significantly different in a model-independent way.

## Method

The authors analyze BABAR Dalitz plot data for \(D^0 \to K^0_S K^+ K^-\) within a quasi-two-body factorization framework derived from the weak effective Hamiltonian, separating tree and annihilation (W-exchange) amplitudes for both Cabibbo-favored and doubly Cabibbo-suppressed transitions. They express the amplitudes in terms of scalar, vector, and tensor kaon form factors, with S-wave channels treated unitarily via a relativistic three-coupled-channel model (\(\pi\pi\), \(K\bar K\), effective \((2\pi)(2\pi)\)) and with scalar-kaon form factors multiplied by phenomenological energy-dependent functions to achieve good fits. The best fit uses 19 free parameters and includes S-, P-, and D-wave final-state kaon-kaon interactions.

## Key numbers

* The best fit has \(\chi^2/ndf = 1.25\).
* The \(D^0\) mass is \(m_{D^0}=1864.83\) MeV, the \(K^0\) mass is \(m_{K^0}=497.611\) MeV, and the charged kaon mass is \(m_K=493.677\) MeV.
* The effective QCD coefficients are taken as \(a_1=1.1\) and \(a_2=-0.5\).
* The transition form factor \(F_0^{D^0K^-}(s_+)\) is parameterized as \(0.78/(1-0.38 s_+/M_V^2+0.46 s_+^2/M_V^4)\) with \(M_V=2.11\) GeV.
* The vector transition form factor \(F_1^{D^0K^-}(s_+)\) is parameterized as \(0.78/[(1-s_+/M_V^2)(1-0.24 s_+/M_V^2)]\) with \(M_V=2.11\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous factorization approach to \(D^0 \to K^0_S \pi^+ \pi^-\) decays (Dedonder et al., 2014) and applies it to the \(D^0 \to K^0_S K^+ K^-\) channel using BABAR data (BABAR Collaboration, 2010). It contrasts with the isobar model analyses used by BABAR (2008, 2010) and BESIII (Ablikim et al., 2020), which lack unitarity in two-body subchannels and require ad hoc resonance choices; the present unitary treatment of S-wave channels and inclusion of the \(f_0(980)\) resolves discrepancies in the near-threshold \(K^+K^-\) mass distribution. The updated isoscalar-scalar meson-meson amplitudes (Appendix A) and alternative fits using Muskhelishvili-Omnes dispersion relation form factors (Appendix B) provide tools for future precision studies of \(D^0\)-\(\bar D^0\) mixing and the CKM angle \(\gamma\).
