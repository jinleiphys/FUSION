# Exact Solutions of Model Hamiltonian Problems with Effective Interactions

> We demonstrate with soluble models how to employ the effective Hamiltonian approach of Lee and Suzuki to obtain all the exact eigenvalues of the full Hamiltonian. We propose a new iteration scheme to obtain the effective Hamiltonian and demonstrate its convergence properties.

## Key claim

The Lee-Suzuki effective Hamiltonian approach, when applied with a one-dimensional model space, yields all exact eigenvalues of the full Hamiltonian, and the resulting effective Hamiltonian is independent of both the starting energy ω and the choice of the unperturbed single-particle Hamiltonian H₀.

## Method

The authors solve the eigenvalue problem HΨ = EΨ by constructing a similarity transformation e⁻ˢHeˢ and imposing the decoupling condition Qe⁻ˢHeˢP = 0 to obtain an effective Hamiltonian H_eff in the P-space. They derive an alternative iteration equation for the effective interaction Z (Eq. iter2) that uses a fixed number of derivatives of the generalized G-matrix (Q-box), and test it on 2×2 and 3×3 matrix models where the full Hamiltonian and unperturbed Hamiltonian are specified explicitly.

## Key numbers

- For the 3×3 test case with H = [[1, -0.5, -0.25], [-0.5, 3, -√2], [-0.25, -√2, 4]] and H₀ = diag(1,2,3), the generalized G-matrix has poles at ω = 2 and ω = 5.
- Using the alternative iteration Eq. (iter2) with k_max = 200 derivatives, convergence (|V_eff(N) - V_eff(N-1)| < 10⁻⁷) requires no more than 15 iterations for a wide range of starting energies.
- With the original Lee-Suzuki iteration Eq. (iter), the number of iterations needed for convergence grows rapidly as the starting energy is shifted away from the self-consistent value; for starting energies close to the converged energy, the iteration count is small.

## Context

This work extends the Lee-Suzuki approach (Lee and Suzuki, 1985) to effective interactions by demonstrating H₀-independence and the ability to obtain all exact eigenvalues, building on the no-core model-space application of Poppelier and Brussaard (1990). The results enable future large-scale shell-model calculations using Brueckner G-matrices computed from realistic free nucleon-nucleon potentials, with the goal of constructing accurate effective-interaction matrix elements that are independent of arbitrary parameters like ω and H₀.
