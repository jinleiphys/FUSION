# Lattice effective field theory calculations for A = 3,4,6,12 nuclei

> We present lattice results for the ground state energies of tritium, helium-3, helium-4, lithium-6, and carbon-12 nuclei. Our analysis includes isospin-breaking, Coulomb effects, and interactions up to next-to-next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that lattice-regularized chiral effective field theory can be applied to ground-state calculations of nuclei up to carbon-12, with the caveat that the three-nucleon parameter \(c_D\) is only estimated as \(c_D \sim O(1)\) and its dependence on observables is checked rather than fully constrained.

## Method
The authors use a lattice action with spatial spacing \(a = (100\ \text{MeV})^{-1} = 1.97\ \text{fm}\) and temporal spacing \(a_t = (150\ \text{MeV})^{-1} = 1.32\ \text{fm}\). Interactions are treated at leading order (LO) non-perturbatively, with next-to-leading order (NLO) and next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) corrections included via perturbation theory; isospin-breaking and Coulomb interactions are also added. For \(A=3\) nuclei, iterative sparse-matrix eigenvector methods are used on periodic cubes up to \(16\ \text{fm}\), while for \(A=4,6,12\) systems, projection Monte Carlo with auxiliary fields and Euclidean-time projection is employed.

## Key numbers
- Energy splitting between helium-3 and triton at NNLO: \(0.78(5)\ \text{MeV}\), compared to experimental value \(0.76\ \text{MeV}\).
- Helium-4 ground-state energy at NNLO with \(c_D=1\): \(-30.1(5)\ \text{MeV}\) (in a \(9.9\ \text{fm}\) cube).
- Lithium-6 ground-state energy at NNLO with \(c_D=1\): \(-34.5(9)\ \text{MeV}\) (in a \(9.9\ \text{fm}\) cube); adding effective four-nucleon interaction gives \(-32.9(9)\ \text{MeV}\), within error of physical \(-32.0\ \text{MeV}\).
- Carbon-12 ground-state energy at NNLO with \(c_D=1\): \(-106(2)\ \text{MeV}\) (in a \(13.8\ \text{fm}\) cube); adding effective four-nucleon interaction gives \(-99(2)\ \text{MeV}\), an overbinding of \(7\%\) from physical \(-92.2\ \text{MeV}\).

## Context
This work extends previous lattice effective field theory calculations for \(A \leq 4\) nuclei (Borasoy 2006, Epelbaum 2009) to lithium-6 and carbon-12 for the first time, and includes isospin-breaking and Coulomb effects not treated in earlier lattice studies. The results are competitive with constrained-path Green's function Monte Carlo and no-core shell model calculations (Pieper 2004, Navratil 2007), and suggest a path toward larger nuclei and nuclear matter by varying lattice boundary conditions and including higher-order effects.
