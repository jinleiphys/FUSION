# Direct WIMP detection rates for transitions in isomeric nuclei

> The direct detection of dark matter constituents, in particular the weakly interacting massive particles (WIMPs), is central to particle physics and cosmology. In this paper we study WIMP induced transitions from isomeric nuclear states for two possible isomeric candidates: $\rm^{180}Ta$ and $\rm^{166}Ho$. The experimental setup, which can measure the possible decay of $\rm^{180}Ta$ induced by WIMPs, was proposed. The corresponding estimates of the half-life of $\rm^{180}Ta$ are given in the sense that the WIMP-nucleon interaction can be interpreted as ordinary radioactive decay.

## Key claim

The paper calculates WIMP-induced de-excitation rates for the long-lived nuclear isomers \(^{180}\)Ta and \(^{166}\)Ho, finding that the nuclear matrix elements for \(^{166}\)Ho are greatly suppressed due to geometric factors and small expansion coefficients, making detection with that isotope "very problematic," while the \(^{180}\)Ta case is treated with a transition energy of 37 keV and a different nuclear structure that may be more favorable.

## Method

The authors model the WIMP-nucleus interaction as spin-dependent scattering, using the Nilsson deformed nuclear model to describe the initial isomeric state and final excited state for each odd-odd deformed nucleus. They expand the Nilsson orbitals into spherical shell-model basis states, compute reduced nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) using Racah techniques, and fold the differential cross section with a Maxwell-Boltzmann velocity distribution for the WIMPs, including the escape velocity cutoff. For \(^{166}\)Ho they employ both shell-model single-particle form factors and Helm-type form factors; for \(^{180}\)Ta they use shell-model form factors with the proton as a spectator and eight neutron transition types.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{166}\)Ho: the transition is from the \(7^{-}\) isomer to the \(0^{-}\) ground state; the coefficients in the NME expansion are \(C_{0i,8}=0.0203\), \(C_{0i,6}=-0.01935\), \(C_{0f,8}=0.000737\), \(C_{0f,6}=0.00374\), leading to strong suppression.
- For \(^{166}\)Ho: the nucleon cross section is \(\sigma_N = 3.5 \times 10^{-39} (f_V^2 + 3 f_A^2)\) cm\(^2\); the nucleon rate is \(R_N = 0.72 \times (f_V^2 + 3 f_A^2)\) y\(^{-1}\).
- For \(^{180}\)Ta: the transition energy from the \(9^{-}\) isomer to the \(2^{+}\) state is \(\Delta = 37\) keV.
- For \(^{180}\)Ta: the maximum recoil energy for WIMP-nucleon scattering is \((E_R)_{\rm max} = 8.0\) keV / \((1+x)^2\) where \(x = m_N / m_\chi\).
- The half-life of \(^{180}\)Ta is given as \(>7 \times 10^{15}\) years; the isomer energy is 76.79(55) keV.
- The half-life of \(^{166}\)Ho is 1130 years; its isomer energy is 5.965(12) keV.

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Petricca et al. (2020) to search for dark-matter-induced de-excitation of long-lived nuclear isomers, providing detailed nuclear-structure calculations for two specific candidates. It contrasts with earlier inelastic WIMP scattering studies (e.g., Ejiri et al., 1993; Vergados et al., 2013) that focused on short-lived excited states (e.g., \(^{127}\)I, \(^{129}\)Xe, \(^{125}\)Te, \(^{83}\)Kr) with lifetimes much less than 1 \(\mu\)s, which are unsuitable for long-term exposures. The results enable experimental design using cryogenic microcalorimetry to search for WIMP-induced decay of \(^{180}\)Ta, which is naturally abundant and quasi-stable.
