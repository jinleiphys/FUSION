# Orbitally Excited Baryon Spectroscopy in the 1/Nc Expansion

> The discussion of the 70-plet of negative parity baryons illustrates the large Nc QCD approach to orbitally excited baryons. In the case of the l=1 baryons the existing data allows to make numerous predictions to first order in the SU(3) symmetry breaking. New relations between splittings are found that follow from the spin-flavor symmetry breaking. The Lambda(1405) is well described as a three-quark state and a spin-orbit partner of the Lambda(1520). Singlet states with higher orbital angular momentum are briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The large \(N_c\) expansion provides a systematic, model-independent framework for orbitally excited baryon spectroscopy; the \(\Lambda(1405)\) is well described as a three-quark state and a spin-orbit partner of the \(\Lambda(1520)\), and the long-standing spin-orbit puzzle in the quark model is resolved by the presence of additional operators (notably \(O_4\)) that are naturally accounted for in the \(1/N_c\) counting.

## Method

The mass operator for the \((3,70)\) multiplet of negative parity \(\ell=1\) baryons was constructed using a basis of \(SU(3)\)-singlet operators built from generators of \(O(3)\otimes SU(2F)\), with explicit \(1/N_c\) factors. The operator basis was extended to include four improved flavor-breaking octet operators at linear order in \(SU(3)\) breaking (\(\mathcal{O}(\epsilon N_c^0)\)). The unknown coefficients were determined by a fit to 19 empirical masses (three or more stars in the PDG listing) and two leading-order mixing angles, yielding a \(\chi^2/4 = 1.29\).

## Key numbers

- The best-fit coefficient for the identity operator \(O_1\) is \(c_1 = 449 \pm 2\) MeV, setting the natural mass scale.
- The spin-orbit operator \(O_2\) has coefficient \(c_2 = 52 \pm 15\) MeV; the operator \(O_4\) (flavor-exchange type) has coefficient \(c_4 = 110 \pm 16\) MeV.
- The hyperfine operator \(O_6\) has coefficient \(c_6 = 480 \pm 15\) MeV, the dominant spin-flavor breaking contribution.
- The fitted mixing angles are \(\theta_1 = 0.61\) and \(\theta_3 = 3.04\) (radians).
- The predicted mass of the \(\Sigma_{1/2}\) (identified with the two-star \(\Sigma(1620)\)) is 1637 MeV, 103 MeV above the \(N_{1/2}\).
- The singlet \(\Lambda\) masses are predicted as \(\Lambda(1407)\) and \(\Lambda(1520)\), matching the experimental \(\Lambda(1405)\) and \(\Lambda(1520)\).
- Keeping only \(O_1\) and \(O_6\) gives \(^2 8\) masses at 1510 MeV, \(^4 8\) and \(^2 10\) masses at 1670 MeV, and \(^2 1\) masses at 1350 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the large \(N_c\) approach to excited baryons, previously developed for two flavors by Goity (1997) and Carone, Georgi, and collaborators, to the full 70-plet with three flavors. It resolves the spin-orbit puzzle that plagued quark model calculations (e.g., Isgur and Karl, 1978) by showing that the operator \(O_4\) cancels the unwanted spin-orbit contribution from \(O_2\) for non-singlet states, consistent with \(1/N_c\) counting. The analysis enables testable predictions for unmeasured states (e.g., \(\Xi\) and \(\Omega\) resonances) and provides new mass splitting relations independent of the fitted coefficients, which can be tested against future experimental data.
