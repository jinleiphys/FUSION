# AdS/CFT and the dynamics of quark-gluon plasma

> In this talk we first discuss the motivation for using the AdS/CFT correspondence as a tool for the understanding of real-time dynamics of quark-gluon plasma. After describing the effective dual degrees of freedom for a strongly coupled gauge theory system, we review the subsequent theoretical `layers' in the physics of quark-gluon plasma: a uniform plasma at fixed temperature, linearized fluctuations, nonlinear hydrodynamic behaviour and finally some aspects of addressing non-equilibrium physics. The discussion is brief and aimed at nonspecialists.

## Key claim

The AdS/CFT correspondence provides a powerful tool for studying real-time dynamics of strongly coupled plasma, but only in a different theory (N=4 Super-Yang-Mills) rather than QCD directly, so the insight gained is qualitative rather than quantitative.

## Method

The paper reviews how the AdS/CFT correspondence maps the four-dimensional gauge theory plasma to a five-dimensional gravitational description in AdS space, where the energy-momentum tensor of the plasma is encoded in the asymptotic expansion of the metric near the boundary. The time-dependent evolution of the plasma is then studied by solving five-dimensional Einstein's equations with appropriate boundary conditions, requiring the dual geometry to be free of naked curvature singularities. The approach covers uniform plasma at fixed temperature, linearized fluctuations, nonlinear hydrodynamic behavior, and far-from-equilibrium dynamics.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: η/s = 1/4π, extracted from quasinormal mode analysis of gravitational perturbations of the planar black hole
- Energy density of static uniform plasma: E = (3 N_c^2)/(2π^2 z_0^4), where z_0 is the horizon position
- Late-time asymptotic energy density for boost-invariant flow: ε(τ) ~ 1/τ^(4/3), matching Bjorken flow from nonlinear hydrodynamics
- Expansion of energy density for boost-invariant flow: ε(τ) = 1/τ^(4/3) - (2/(2^(1/2) 3^(3/4))) 1/τ^2 + ((1+2 log 2)/(12 √3)) 1/τ^(8/3) + ...

## Context

This review extends the program of using AdS/CFT to study strongly coupled plasma dynamics, building on the foundational work of Maldacena (1997) on the correspondence and the derivation of shear viscosity by Policastro, Son, and Starinets (2001). The paper describes how the boost-invariant flow analysis by Janik and Peschanski (2006) derived the 4/3 power law for energy density decay, and how subsequent work by Minwalla and collaborators (2008) generalized the hydrodynamic description without symmetry assumptions. The approach enables future numerical studies of far-from-equilibrium phenomena such as isotropisation of anisotropic plasma and the approach to thermalization, which may help understand the fast thermalization times observed at RHIC.
