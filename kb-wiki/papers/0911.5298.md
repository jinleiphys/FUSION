# Microscopic study of collective states of even-even Molybdenum isotopes

> Low energy quadrupole excitations of the 84-110Mo nuclei are studied in the frame of the general Bohr collective model based on the ATDHFB method with the Skyrme effective interaction. Obtained energies of the 2_1, 4_1, 2_2 and 0_2 levels and B(E2) values for the 2_1 -> 0_g.s transitions are in good agreement with experimental data.

# Microscopic study of collective states of even-even Molybdenum isotopes

## Key claim

The general Bohr collective model based on the ATDHFB method with Skyrme effective interactions (SIII and SLy4) reproduces low-energy quadrupole excitations across the 84-110Mo isotopic chain reasonably well, but the SIII variant consistently yields better agreement with experimental data than SLy4, and the approach struggles with the semi-magic 92Mo nucleus and with 0+2 states in 96-100Mo.

## Method

The potential energy and six inertial functions (mass parameters and moments of inertia) of the general Bohr Hamiltonian were calculated using the Adiabatic Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (ATDHFB) method with Skyrme SIII and SLy4 interactions, plus a constant seniority-type pairing interaction whose strength was fixed by comparing experimental pairing gaps from mass differences with calculated minimal quasiparticle energies for 104-108Mo. The collective Schrodinger equation was then solved in the full quadrupole deformation space (beta and gamma) to obtain energy levels and E2 transition probabilities, with mass parameters multiplied by a constant factor of 1.3 to simulate Thouless-Valatin corrections and pairing vibration effects.

## Key numbers

- B(E2) for 2_1 -> 0_1 transition in 100Mo: experimental 37.0 (0.7) W.u., SIII gives 42.9 W.u., SLy4 gives 29.2 W.u.
- B(E2) for 4_1 -> 2_1 transition in 100Mo: experimental 69 (4) W.u., SIII gives 76.1 W.u., SLy4 gives 53.8 W.u.
- B(E2) for 6_1 -> 4_1 transition in 100Mo: experimental 94 (14) W.u., SIII gives 102.7 W.u., SLy4 gives 75.6 W.u.
- B(E2) for 8_1 -> 6_1 transition in 100Mo: experimental 123 (18) W.u., SIII gives 124.9 W.u., SLy4 gives 97.8 W.u.
- B(E2) for 0_2 -> 2_1 transition in 100Mo: experimental 92 (4) W.u., SIII gives 50.3 W.u., SLy4 gives 42.1 W.u.
- B(E2) for 2_2 -> 2_1 transition in 100Mo: experimental 51 (5) W.u., SIII gives 50.5 W.u., SLy4 gives 53.7 W.u.
- B(E2) for 2_2 -> 0_1 transition in 100Mo: experimental 0.62 (0.05) W.u., SIII gives 0.8 W.u., SLy4 gives 0.06 W.u.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous applications of the ATDHFB-based Bohr model to other nuclear regions (Prochniak 2004, 2006, 2007) and provides a systematic test across an entire isotopic chain from spherical (near semi-magic 92Mo) to well-deformed, gamma-soft heavy molybdenum isotopes. The results enable future refinement of Skyrme energy density functionals by adding collective properties as a selection criterion, and highlight the need for more precise treatment of Thouless-Valatin corrections and pairing vibration effects beyond the simple 1.3 scaling factor used here.
