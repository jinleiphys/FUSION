# The proton-deuteron system in pionless EFT revisited

> We provide a detailed discussion of the low-energy proton-deuteron system in pionless effective field theory, considering both the spin-quartet and doublet S-wave channels. Extending and amending our previous work on the subject, we calculate the 3He-3H binding energy difference both perturbatively (using properly normalized trinucleon wave functions) and non-perturbatively by resumming all O(alpha) Coulomb diagrams in the doublet channel. Our nonperturbative result agrees well with a calculation that involves the full off-shell Coulomb T-matrix. Carefully examining the cutoff-dependence in the doublet channel, we present numerical evidence for a new three-nucleon counterterm being necessary at next-to-leading order if Coulomb effects are included. Indeed, such a term has recently been identified analytically. We furthermore make a case for a simplified Coulomb power counting that is consistent throughout the bound-state and scattering regimes. Finally, using a "partially screened" full off-shell Coulomb T-matrix, we investigate the importance of higher-order Coulomb corrections in low-energy quartet-channel scattering.

## Key claim

The paper extends and corrects earlier pionless EFT calculations of the proton-deuteron system, showing that a new three-nucleon counterterm is necessary at next-to-leading order when Coulomb effects are included, and that the full off-shell Coulomb T-matrix is not required for bound-state calculations.

## Method

The authors use pionless effective field theory with dibaryon fields to describe low-energy proton-deuteron scattering in both spin-quartet and spin-doublet channels. They calculate the 3He-3H binding energy difference both perturbatively (using properly normalized trinucleon wave functions) and non-perturbatively by resumming all O(alpha) Coulomb diagrams in the doublet channel, and examine cutoff-dependence to identify the need for a new counterterm. They also investigate higher-order Coulomb corrections in quartet-channel scattering using a "partially screened" full off-shell Coulomb T-matrix.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron binding momentum is approximately 45 MeV, setting the low-energy scale Q of the theory.
- The S-wave nucleon-nucleon scattering lengths are a_d approximately 5.42 fm (3S1 channel) and a_t approximately -23.71 fm (1S0 channel), with effective ranges 1.75 fm and 2.73 fm respectively.
- The expansion parameter of pionless EFT is Q/Lambda_slash_pi approximately 1/3, where Lambda_slash_pi is of order the pion mass.
- The Coulomb parameter alpha is approximately 1/137.
- The average nucleon mass M_N is approximately 938.918 MeV.
- The product gamma_d * rho_d is approximately 0.4, indicating slow convergence of the geometric series for the deuteron wave function renormalization.
- Diagrams (b) and (c) in the leading Coulomb set are 7% and 15% effects respectively at zero-momentum scattering threshold.

## Context

This work extends and corrects the authors' previous calculation (Konig 2011) of proton-deuteron scattering in the spin-doublet channel, and builds on the leading-order calculation of 3He including nonperturbative Coulomb interactions by Ando and Birse (2010). The paper enables future fully-perturbative calculations of the charged three-nucleon system at next-to-leading order, and the analytical identification of the new alpha-dependent counterterm has been confirmed by Vanasse (2014).
