# Mass and Isotope Dependence of Limiting Temperatures for Hot Nuclei

> The mass and isotope dependence of limiting temperatures for hot nuclei are investigated. The predicted mass dependence of limiting temperatures is in good agreement with data derived from the caloric curve data. The predicted isotope distribution of limiting temperatures appears to be a parabolic shape and its centroid is not located at the isotope on the $β$-stability line(T=0) but at neutron-rich side. Our study shows that the mass and isotope dependence of limiting temperatures depend on the interaction and the form of surface tension and its isopin dependence sensitively.

# Mass and Isotope Dependence of Limiting Temperatures for Hot Nuclei

## Key claim
The mass and isotope dependence of limiting temperatures for hot nuclei depends sensitively on the choice of Skyrme interaction and the form of surface tension, with the best agreement to experimental caloric curve data obtained using the SLy7 Skyrme force combined with a specific surface tension form (Surf2). The isotope distribution of limiting temperatures follows a parabolic shape whose centroid shifts to the neutron-rich side, not to the beta-stability line.

## Method
The authors used a liquid-drop model treating a hot nucleus as a uniformly charged spherical drop with sharp edge in thermal and chemical equilibrium with surrounding vapor. Coexistence equations for chemical potentials of neutrons and protons and for pressure (including bulk, Coulomb, and surface tension terms) were solved to find the upper temperature boundary where solutions exist, defining the limiting temperature. Calculations employed three Skyrme interactions (SLy7, SkM*, SIII) and two forms of surface tension (Surf1 and Surf2), with the SLy7 force and Surf2 giving the best match to data.

## Key numbers
- Limiting temperatures for nuclei along the beta-stability line with mass numbers from 20 to 250 were calculated (Table 1 in the paper).
- The largest difference between limiting temperatures calculated with SLy7 and SkM* is about or less than 0.1 MeV.
- The surface tension Surf2 uses gamma(0) approximately 18 MeV / (4 pi r0^2) with r0 = 1.12 fm.
- The symmetry-surface tension term uses a_s = 28.5 MeV.
- At temperatures around T ~ 6 MeV and densities around rho ~ 0.14 fm^{-3}, at least 6 terms in the virial expansion are needed for accurate results.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of limiting temperatures for nuclei along the beta-stability line using Skyrme interactions (Ja83, Bo84, Be89, So91, Zh96, Liu03) and the FST model (Zh99). It applies the modern SLy7 Skyrme force, designed for nuclei away from beta-stability (Ch97), to investigate isotope dependence for the first time. The results enable predictions for future experiments with rare isotope beams and provide constraints on the isospin dependence of the nuclear equation of state and surface tension.
