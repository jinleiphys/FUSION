# Analytical E1 strength functions of two-neutron halo nuclei: 11-Li and 14-Be

> An analytical model, recently developed to study the electromagnetic dissociation (EMD) energy spectra of two-neutron halo nuclei, is applied to 11-Li and 14-Be. We find that a reliable set of experimental data on EMD could help to resolve the problem concerning the structure of the 11-Li ground state. For 14-Be we find a mutual inconsistency between the existing experimental data on the binding energy, radius and EMD energy spectrum. We also conclude that the structure of 14-Be is essentially more complicated than the structure of other Borromean two--neutron halo nuclei.

# Analytical E1 strength functions of two-neutron halo nuclei: 11-Li and 14-Be

## Key claim

The analytical model can help resolve the ground-state structure of 11-Li if reliable experimental EMD data become available, but for 14-Be the existing experimental data on binding energy, radius, and EMD energy spectrum are mutually inconsistent, and the structure of 14-Be is essentially more complicated than that of other Borromean two-neutron halo nuclei.

## Method

The authors apply a semi-phenomenological three-body model using hyperspherical harmonics (HH) expansions for bound-state wave functions and no-interaction final states, allowing analytic calculation of E1 strength functions. Ground-state wave functions are constructed with one- or two-parameter hyperradial functions fitted to experimental binding energies and rms radii, with HH amplitudes representing different configurations such as $(s_{1/2})^2$ and $(p_{1/2})^2$ for the valence neutrons. The model uses a complete set of no-interaction final states expressed as coordinate-space HH, and the E1 strength function is computed analytically via hypergeometric functions.

## Key numbers

- For 11-Li, the peak position of the E1 strength function is approximately 0.5–0.7 MeV for all ground-state wave functions tested, close to all experimental findings.
- The two-neutron separation energy of 14-Be is tabulated as 1.34 ± 0.11 MeV (Gilbert 1984, Wouters 1988).
- The most recent rms radius of 14-Be is 3.10 ± 0.15 fm (Suzuki 1999).
- The EMD energy spectrum for 14-Be measured by Labiche (2001) was modeled with a Breit-Wigner shape having parameters E_BW = 1.8 ± 0.1 MeV and Gamma_BW = 0.8 ± 0.4 MeV.
- The experimental 14-Be spectrum peak corresponds to a binding energy of approximately 0.9 MeV, considerably lower than the tabulated 1.34 MeV.
- For 14-Be, using the two-parameter hyperradial function in the limit kappa_1 → kappa_0 gives R_rms = 3.0 fm.
- The integrated E1 strength from the 14-Be experimental data up to the 12-Be threshold is substantially larger than theoretical strengths obtained with the model wave functions and with the D4 potential wave function of Thompson (1996).

## Context

This work extends the analytical model previously developed and tested on 6-He (Forssen 2002) to the more poorly understood Borromean halo nuclei 11-Li and 14-Be. The model provides a simpler alternative to full microscopic three-body calculations such as those by Cobis (1998) and Thompson (1996), and to Quantum Monte Carlo A-nucleon calculations by Pane (1999). For 11-Li, the analysis addresses the ongoing debate about the relative importance of $(s_{1/2})^2$ and $(p_{1/2})^2$ configurations in the ground state, building on experimental evidence from fragmentation reactions (Simon 1999) and studies of the 10-Li subsystem (Chartier 2001). For 14-Be, the findings highlight inconsistencies that call for new experimental measurements of the binding energy and for considering more complex structures such as five-body models or excited-core configurations, similar to analyses performed for 8-He (Alkhazov 1998).
