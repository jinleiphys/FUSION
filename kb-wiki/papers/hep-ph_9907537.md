# The asymptotic behavior of the pi^0 gamma^* gamma transition

> sprocl.sty, 4 pages, slight enlargement of the version to appear in Proc. of Int. Workshop on Understanding Deconfinement in QCD, ECT* Trento, Italy, March 01 - 13, 1999 (World Scientific)

## Key claim

The asymptotic coefficient of the $\pi^0 \gamma^* \gamma$ transition form factor depends on the choice of the dressed quark-photon vertex Ansatz; for the Ball-Chiu vertex, the coefficient becomes $4 \widetilde{f}_\pi / 3$ rather than $4 f_\pi / 3$, contrary to claims that no modification occurs even for that vertex.

## Method

The authors compute the $\gamma^*(k) \gamma(k') \to \pi^0(p)$ transition form factor $T_{\pi^0}(k^2, k'^2)$ using the Schwinger-Dyson (SD) approach with dynamically dressed quark propagators $S(q) = [A(q^2) \slashed{q} - B(q^2)]^{-1}$ and the generalized impulse approximation (GIA) triangle diagram. They compare the asymptotic behavior for large spacelike $Q^2 = -k^2$ when using different WTI-satisfying Ansätze for the dressed quark-photon vertex $\Gamma^\mu(q,q')$, specifically the Ball-Chiu (BC), Curtis-Pennington (CP), and modified Curtis-Pennington (mCP) vertices. The analysis examines whether soft loop momenta in the triangle diagram prevent the BC vertex from reducing to the bare $\gamma^\mu$ vertex even when one photon leg is hard.

## Key numbers

- For the CP and mCP vertices (and any vertex reducing to bare $\gamma^\mu$ when one quark leg is hard): asymptotic coefficient ${\cal K} = 4 f_\pi / 3$ for $T_{\pi^0}(-Q^2,0) = {\cal K} / Q^2$ as $Q^2 \to \infty$
- For the BC vertex: asymptotic coefficient ${\cal K} = 4 \widetilde{f}_\pi / 3$, where $\widetilde{f}_\pi = 1.334 f_\pi = 124$ MeV for the authors' solutions
- For the symmetric case with both photons off-shell: $T_{\pi^0}(-Q^2, -Q'^2) = (4/3) f_\pi / (Q^2 + Q'^2)$ for vertices reducing to bare $\gamma^\mu$ with one hard leg; BC vertex again modifies this by $f_\pi \to \widetilde{f}_\pi$

## Context

This work extends the Schwinger-Dyson approach of Klabucar and Kekez (1999) and addresses a disagreement with Tandy and Dubnicka (1999), who claimed that even the Ball-Chiu vertex yields ${\cal K} = 4 f_\pi / 3$. The result for vertices consistent with multiplicative renormalizability (CP, mCP) agrees with the operator product expansion result of Manohar (1990) giving ${\cal K} = 4 f_\pi / 3$, and with perturbative QCD of Brodsky and Lepage (where ${\cal K} = 2 f_\pi$ up to the precise value). The symmetric double-off-shell result agrees with Novikov et al. (1984) and with pQCD approaches. The work clarifies that the BC vertex, though widely used phenomenologically, gives a modified asymptotic coefficient due to its failure to reduce to the bare vertex when only one quark leg is hard.
