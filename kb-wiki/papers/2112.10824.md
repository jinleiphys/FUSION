# Constraining neutron star properties with a new equation of state insensitive approach

> Instead of parameterizing the pressure-density relation of a neutron star (NS), one can parameterize its macroscopic properties such as mass ($M$), radius ($R$), and dimensionless tidal deformability ($Λ$) to infer the equation of state (EoS) combining electromagnetic and gravitational wave (GW) observations. We present a new method to parameterize $R(M)$ and $Λ(M)$ relations, which approximate the candidate EoSs with accuracy better than 5\% for all masses and span a broad region of $M-R-Λ$ plane. Using this method we combine the $M-Λ$ measurement from GW170817 and GW190425, and simultaneous $M-R$ measurement of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620 to place joint constraints on NS properties. At 90 \% confidence, we infer $R_{1.4}=12.05_{-0.87}^{+0.98}$ km and $Λ_{1.4}=372_{-150}^{+220}$ for a $1.4 M_{\odot}$ NS, and $R_{2.08}=12.65_{-1.46}^{+1.36}$ km for a $2.08 M_{\odot}$ NS. Furthermore, we use the inferred values of the maximum mass of a nonrotating NS $M_{\rm max}=2.52_{-0.29}^{+0.33} M_{\odot}$ to investigate the nature of the secondary objects in three potential neutron star-black hole merger (NSBH) system.

## Key claim

The authors present a new method to directly parameterize the mass-radius and mass-tidal deformability relations of neutron stars using a polynomial in the logarithm of the tidal deformability, achieving fitting accuracy better than 5% for all masses across a broad set of candidate equations of state, thereby avoiding the computational cost of solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation repeatedly.

## Method

The authors parameterize the mass-tidal deformability relation as M/M_sun = sum_{i=0}^{3} b_i (ln Lambda)^i, fitting this four-parameter polynomial to 27 candidate equations of state from the LALSuite library using least squares. They then combine likelihoods from gravitational wave events GW170817 and GW190425, NICER X-ray mass-radius measurements of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620, and radio mass measurements of PSR J0740+6620 using nested sampling with the PyMultinest algorithm, marginalizing over component masses with a flat prior between 1 M_sun and the EoS-dependent maximum mass.

## Key numbers

- Fitting error in the M-Lambda relation is less than or equal to 5% for all considered mass ranges, and less than 2.7% excluding the MS1 family
- At 90% confidence, R_{1.4} = 12.05_{-0.87}^{+0.98} km for a 1.4 M_sun neutron star
- At 90% confidence, Lambda_{1.4} = 372_{-150}^{+220} for a 1.4 M_sun neutron star
- At 90% confidence, R_{2.08} = 12.65_{-1.46}^{+1.36} km for a 2.08 M_sun neutron star
- At 90% confidence, M_max = 2.52_{-0.29}^{+0.33} M_sun for a nonrotating neutron star
- The secondary object in GW190814 has a maximum 62% probability of being a black hole, while for GW200105 and GW200115 the probabilities are 2% and 1% respectively
- The C-Lambda relation used for radius computation has a maximum of approximately 3% error

## Context

This work extends previous parametric approaches that parameterize the pressure-density relation (e.g., Raaijmakers 2019, Jiang 2019, Most 2018) and nonparametric approaches (Landry 2020, Han 2021) by directly parameterizing macroscopic properties to reduce computational cost. It improves upon earlier expansions of lambda(M) about 1.4 M_sun (Del Pozzo 2013, Agathos 2015) which broke down above 1.8 M_sun and could not incorporate maximum mass constraints. The method enables rapid joint inference from gravitational wave and electromagnetic observations, and the inferred maximum mass distribution can classify secondary objects in candidate neutron star-black hole merger systems.
