# Critical endpoint in the presence of a chiral chemical potential

> A class of Polyakov-loop-modified Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) models have been used to support a conjecture that numerical simulations of lattice-regularized quantum chromodynamics (QCD) defined with a chiral chemical potential can provide information about the existence and location of a critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram drawn in the plane spanned by baryon chemical potential and temperature. That conjecture is challenged by conflicts between the model results and analyses of the same problem using simulations of lattice-regularized QCD (lQCD) and well-constrained Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) studies. We find the conflict is resolved in favor of the lQCD and DSE predictions when both a physically-motivated regularization is employed to suppress the contribution of high-momentum quark modes in the definition of the effective potential connected with the PNJL models and the four-fermion coupling in those models does not react strongly to changes in the mean-field that is assumed to mock-up Polyakov loop dynamics. With the lQCD and DSE predictions thus confirmed, it seems unlikely that simulations of lQCD with $μ_5>0$ can shed any light on a critical endpoint in the regular QCD phase diagram.

## Key claim

The paper resolves a contradiction between Polyakov-loop-modified Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model predictions and lattice QCD (lQCD) plus Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) results regarding the existence of a critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram when a chiral chemical potential is present. The resolution requires both a physically-motivated regularization that suppresses high-momentum quark modes in the effective potential and a four-fermion coupling that does not react strongly to changes in the Polyakov-loop mean field; under these conditions, no critical endpoint exists for nonzero chiral chemical potential, contradicting earlier PNJL claims.

## Method

The authors compare two regularization schemes for the PNJL model: one where the finite integral over the logarithm term in the effective potential is left unregulated (Omega_F^infinity) and one where it is regulated with the same hard cutoff Lambda used for the divergent vacuum term (Omega_F^Lambda). They also test two choices for the four-fermion coupling: a constant coupling and a coupling that depends on the Polyakov loop. The results are benchmarked against DSE predictions obtained using the rainbow-ladder truncation of the dressed-quark gap equation with an interaction kernel that reproduces hadron properties, and against lQCD simulations from the literature.

## Key numbers

- The hard cutoff used in the PNJL model is Lambda = 631.5 MeV, with current-quark mass m = 5.5 MeV and four-fermion coupling g Lambda^2 = 2.2.
- For the DSE analysis with current-quark mass m = 5 MeV, the critical chemical potential mu associated with the chiral transition becomes independent of mu_5 above a threshold, reaching mu^i approximately 40 MeV for m = 5 MeV and approximately 90 MeV for m = 15 MeV.
- The Polyakov-loop potential parameters are a0 = 3.51, a1 = -2.47, a2 = 15.2, b3 = -1.75, and T0 = 190 MeV.
- There exists a critical cutoff value Lambda^c approximately equal to Lambda (631.5 MeV) such that no critical endpoint in the (mu_5, T) plane exists for any cutoff Lambda' less than Lambda^c when using the constant coupling.

## Context

This work extends and reconciles conflicting predictions between PNJL model studies (Ruggieri 2011, Fukushima 2010, Gatto 2011) that found a critical endpoint in the presence of chiral chemical potential, and lQCD simulations (Yamamoto 2011, Braguta 2015) plus DSE studies (Wang 2015, Xu 2015) that found no such endpoint. The resolution shows that earlier PNJL results were artifacts of using an unregulated effective potential and a Polyakov-loop-dependent four-fermion coupling. The paper concludes that lQCD simulations with nonzero chiral chemical potential cannot provide information about the critical endpoint in the regular QCD phase diagram, closing off a previously proposed surrogate approach.
