# Ab initio calculations of reactions with light nuclei

> An {\em ab initio} (i.e., from first principles) theoretical framework capable of providing a unified description of the structure and low-energy reaction properties of light nuclei is desirable to further our understanding of the fundamental interactions among nucleons, and provide accurate predictions of crucial reaction rates for nuclear astrophysics, fusion-energy research, and other applications. In this contribution we review {\em ab initio} calculations for nucleon and deuterium scattering on light nuclei starting from chiral two- and three-body Hamiltonians, obtained within the framework of the {\em ab initio} no-core shell model with continuum. This is a unified approach to nuclear bound and scattering states, in which square-integrable energy eigenstates of the $A$-nucleon system are coupled to $(A-a)+a$ target-plus-projectile wave functions in the spirit of the resonating group method to obtain an efficient description of the many-body nuclear dynamics both at short and medium distances and at long ranges.

## Key claim

The no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC) can provide a unified *ab initio* description of both bound states and low-energy scattering for light nuclei using chiral two- and three-nucleon forces, but the accuracy of the results depends on including sufficient square-integrable eigenstates of the compound nucleus and on the specific model of the three-nucleon force, particularly regarding the spin-orbit interaction strength.

## Method

The authors employ the NCSMC framework, which superposes square-integrable energy eigenstates of the A-nucleon system (obtained from the no-core shell model) with continuous cluster wave functions in the spirit of the resonating group method. The many-body wave function is expanded in a basis of harmonic oscillator states, and the coupled-channel equations derived from the Schrodinger equation are solved to obtain scattering observables. The chiral N3LO nucleon-nucleon interaction of Entem and Machleidt (2003) and the N2LO three-nucleon force of Navratil et al. (2007) are used, unitarily softened via the similarity renormalization group method with a resolution scale of 2.0 fm^-1.

## Key numbers

- Proton-4He scattering: NCSMC calculations used N_max=13, hbar-Omega=20 MeV HO model space, including first seven 4He eigenstates and first fourteen square-integrable eigenstates of 5Li; angular differential cross sections computed for proton incident energies up to 10 MeV.
- 9Be spectrum: negative-parity NCSMC calculation at N_max=12, hbar-Omega=20 MeV; positive-parity at N_max=11, hbar-Omega=20 MeV; coupling 9Be eigenstates with 8Be(0+,2+) binary cluster states.
- 6Li ground state: computed binding energy yields asymptotic D- to S-state ratio of the 6Li wave function in d+alpha configuration of -0.027, in agreement with determination from 6Li-4He elastic scattering.
- d-4He scattering: calculations at N_max=11, hbar-Omega=20 MeV with fifteen discrete eigenstates of 6Li and continuous d-4He(g.s.) binary-cluster states with up to seven deuteron pseudostates; angular distributions computed for incident deuteron energies 2.93, 6.96, 8.97, and 12.0 MeV.
- n-4He scattering phase shifts: preliminary results from zero to 24 MeV center-of-mass energy at N_max=9, including n+4He(g.s.) and d+3H(g.s.) continuous basis states with up to two 2H pseudostates.

## Context

This work extends the NCSMC approach, initially developed for nucleon-nucleus collisions with two-body Hamiltonians (Baroni 2013), to include explicit three-nucleon forces necessary for quantitative light-nucleus scattering (Nollett 2007, Viviani 2013). The results for p-4He scattering improve upon earlier calculations using only cluster states (Hupin 2013) by including square-integrable eigenstates of the compound 5Li system. The 9Be study addresses discrepancies found in bound-state NCSM calculations (Forssen 2005) where positive-parity resonances appeared too high in energy. The d-4He and 6Li work enables the first ab initio calculation of the 3H(d,n)4He fusion reaction, currently underway, and sets the stage for studies of 4He(p,p'gamma)4He bremsstrahlung, 2H(alpha,gamma)6Li radiative capture, and the 11Be halo nucleus.
