# Gluon knots as the dynamical core of baryons

> We propose a conjectural picture of baryon structure in which gluon knots, a type of topologically nontrivial configuration of color--magnetic monopole condensates, forms the dynamical core of the baryon. Within this framework, quarks interact with the gluon knot via abelian-dominated color-electric fields, which are squeezed into flux tubes by the dual Meissner effect, leading naturally to quark confinement. The color--magnetic fields associated with the gluon knot also induce a local chiral condensate, contributing to spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking and the baryon mass. Extending this conjecture to heavy-flavor mesons, we argue that stable flux tubes and gluon knots may also play a role in their internal structure, whereas light-flavor mesons are dominated by alternative confinement mechanisms. Our approach provides a unified, topologically motivated picture linking confinement, chiral symmetry breaking, and the internal dynamics of baryons and certain mesons, suggesting that gluon knots constitute fundamental infrared degrees of freedom of Yang--Mills theory.

# Gluon knots as the dynamical core of baryons

## Key claim

The authors propose that baryons contain a gluon knot, a topologically nontrivial configuration of color-magnetic monopole condensates, as their dynamical core, but this is presented as a conjectural picture rather than a proven mechanism, and the authors explicitly note that the equations of motion do not uniquely determine a gluon-knot configuration without imposing a specific ansatz.

## Method

The authors employ the Cho–Faddeev–Niemi (CFN) decomposition of SU(N) Yang–Mills theory to separate diagonal U(1)^{N-1} gluon fields from off-diagonal components, then integrate out the Abelian Higgs multiplets to obtain an effective Lagrangian on the coset space SU(N)/U(1)^{N-1}. They analyze the topological solitonic solutions (gluon knots) classified by π₃(SU(N)/U(1)^{N-1}) ≅ ℤ, and construct a dual magnetic Ginzburg–Landau-type gauge theory to describe the interaction between quarks and the gluon-knot background, incorporating the dual Meissner effect to produce color flux tubes.

## Key numbers

* For N=3 QCD, the monopole condensate generates an effective mass for off-diagonal gluons given by m_X² = (1/2) g² ⟨(H_μ^a)²⟩, where g is the Yang–Mills coupling and H_μ^a is the field associated with color-magnetic monopole contributions.
* The effective interaction potential between a static quark and antiquark separated by distance r takes the Cornell form: V(r) ≃ -(Q²/4π) (e^{-m_M r}/r) + (Q² m_M²/4π) K₀(√2 m_M/m_χ) r, where m_M and m_χ are the masses of the dual gauge fields and scalar fields respectively, and K₀ is the modified Bessel function of the second kind.
* The diagonal U(1)² flux charges of quarks with colors r, g, b under C_{1,μ} are (-1/2, 1/2, 0) and under C_{2,μ} are (-1/(2√3), -1/(2√3), 1/√3), satisfying Q_ψr + Q_ψg + Q_ψb = 0.

## Context

This work extends the 't Hooft proposal (1981) that SU(N) Yang–Mills theory can be reformulated as an Abelian U(1)^{N-1} theory with color-magnetic monopoles, and builds on the Cho–Faddeev–Niemi decomposition (Cho 1979, Faddeev 1998) which separates diagonal and off-diagonal gluon fields. The authors argue that gluon knots, previously studied by Faddeev and Niemi (1996, 1998) as topological solitons in the effective Lagrangian, constitute the fundamental infrared degrees of freedom of Yang–Mills theory. The picture enables a unified topological description linking confinement via the dual Meissner effect, spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking through induced local chiral condensates, and baryon structure, while suggesting that heavy-flavor mesons may also involve gluon knots but light-flavor mesons are dominated by alternative confinement mechanisms.
