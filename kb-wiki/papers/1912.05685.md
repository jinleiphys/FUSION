# Variational and parquet-diagram calculations for neutron matter. I. Structure and Energetics

> We develop the variational/parquet diagram approach to the structure of nuclear systems with strongly state-dependent interactions. For that purpose, we combine ideas of the general Jastrow-Feenberg variational method and the local parquet-diagram theory for bosons with state-dependent interactions (R. A. Smith and A. D. Jackson, Nucl. Phys. {\bf 476}, 448 (1988)). The most tedious aspect of variational approaches, namely the symmetrization of an operator dependent variational wave function, is thereby avoided. We carry out calculations for neutron matter interacting via the Reid and Argonne $v_6$ models of the nucleon-nucleon interaction. While the equation of state is a rather robust quantity that comes out reasonably well even in very simplistic approaches, we show that effective interactions, which are the essential input for calculating dynamic properties, depend sensitively on the quality of the treatment of the many-body problem.

## Key claim

The paper develops a combined variational/parquet-diagram approach for neutron matter with state-dependent interactions that avoids the difficult symmetrization of operator-dependent wave functions required in standard Jastrow-Feenberg methods, but the authors note that the problem of commutator diagrams (which correspond to "twisted" ladder rungs in parquet language) does not entirely disappear, the parquet framework merely provides a clearer procedure to handle these effects.

## Method

The authors combine the Jastrow-Feenberg variational method with the local parquet-diagram theory for bosons with state-dependent interactions originally developed by Smith and Jackson (1988), generalizing it to Fermi systems. They derive connections between the FHNC-EL (Fermi-Hypernetted-Chain Euler-Lagrange) equations and parquet-diagram summations, specifically focusing on ring diagrams and the Bethe-Goldstone equation, then formulate the method for fermions with a \(v_6\) interaction. Calculations are carried out for neutron matter using the Reid and Argonne \(v_6\) models of the nucleon-nucleon interaction, with the equations solved in the operator basis \(\{\mathbf{1}, \hat{L}, \hat{T}\}\) for momentum space and the projector basis \(\{\hat{P}_s, \hat{P}_{t+}, \hat{P}_{t-}\}\) for coordinate space.

## Key numbers

* The static structure function for fermions in the FHNC//0 approximation is given by \(S(q) = S_F(q) / \sqrt{1 + 2 S_F^2(q) \tilde{V}_{\text{p-h}}(q) / t(q)}\), where \(S_F(q) = 3q/(4k_F) - q^3/(16k_F^3)\) for \(q < 2k_F\) and \(S_F(q) = 1\) for \(q \ge 2k_F\).
* The induced interaction for fermions is \(\tilde{w}_I(q) = -t(q) [1/S_F(q) - 1/S(q)]^2 [S(q)/S_F(q) + 1/2]\).
* The tensor kinetic energy term contributes \(\frac{36}{r^2} f_S^2(r) \hat{P}_{t+} + \frac{72}{r^2} f_S^2(r) \hat{P}_{t-}\) to the particle-hole interaction in the projector basis, where \(f_S(r) = (f_{t+}(r) - f_{t-}(r))/6\).
* The factor \(\lambda(q)\) in the fermion Bethe-Goldstone equation never differs from 1 by more than 20 percent.

## Context

This work extends the parquet-diagram approach of Smith and Jackson (1988) from bosonic nucleons to Fermi systems, building on the FHNC-EL framework developed by Fantoni and Rosati and others. It addresses the long-standing difficulty of symmetrizing operator-dependent wave functions in variational calculations of nuclear matter, which had previously limited implementations to the "single operator chain (SOC)" approximation of Pandharipande and Wiringa (1979). The method enables calculation of effective interactions for neutron matter that are essential input for computing dynamic properties, going beyond the equation of state which the authors note is "a rather robust quantity that comes out reasonably well even in very simplistic approaches."
