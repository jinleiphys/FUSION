# Charmonium Production off Nuclei: from SPS to RHIC

> Physics of charmonium suppression in nuclear collisions drastically changes between the energies of SPS and RHIC. Mechanisms suppressing charmonia at SPS are reviewed, neither of which is important at RHIC. On the other hand, coherence, or shadowing of c-quarks and gluons barely seen at SPS, become a dominant effect at RHIC providing a much stronger suppression. A onset of coherence at Fermilab energies explains the observed cross section ratio steeply falling at large Feynman x_F. In nuclear collisions variation of charmonium suppression with x_F suggests a sensitive probe for QGP.

# Charmonium Production off Nuclei: from SPS to RHIC

## Key claim
The mechanisms suppressing charmonium production in nuclear collisions change completely between SPS and RHIC energies: absorption and energy loss dominate at SPS but become negligible at RHIC, while coherence (shadowing of c-quarks and gluons) becomes the dominant suppression mechanism at RHIC. The variation of charmonium suppression with Feynman x_F in nuclear collisions is proposed as a sensitive probe for quark-gluon plasma (QGP) formation, but the nuclear suppression factor S_nucl must be reliably understood before final-state interaction effects can be isolated.

## Method
The paper reviews existing mechanisms for charmonium suppression in pA and AB collisions at SPS energies (absorption, formation time effects, energy loss, gluon enhancement, excitation of nuclear matter) and compares them with predictions for RHIC energies based on quantum coherence and gluon shadowing. Parameter-free QCD calculations from Kopeliovich, Tarasov, and Hufner (2001) are compared with E866 data at Fermilab energies to demonstrate the onset of coherence, and predictions are extended to p-Au and Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV.

## Key numbers
- Formation time of charmonium: t_f = 2*E_Psi/(M_Psi'^2 - M_J/Psi^2), with E_Psi ≈ 50 GeV at NA38/50 experiments, giving t_f greater than or comparable to nuclear radius R_A
- Energy loss rate from E772/E866 Drell-Yan data: dE/dz = -2.3 ± 0.52 ± 0.5 GeV/fm
- Gluon density enhancement in heavy nuclei: 10-20% at large x_2 ≈ 0.1, giving sigma_pA^Psi/sigma_pN^Psi proportional to G_A(x_2)/G_N(x_2) ≈ 1.1-1.2
- RHIC collision energy for predictions: sqrt(s) = 200 GeV

## Context
This work extends the earlier absorption model used by NA38/NA50 collaborations (1990s) which assumed instantaneous charmonium production with constant absorption cross section, but which failed to reproduce the x_F dependence observed by NA3 and E537 experiments. The paper builds on the quantum-mechanical description of charmonium evolution in absorptive media by Kopeliovich and Zakharov (1991) and the energy loss mechanism proposed by Kopeliovich and Nikolaev (1980s). It enables future experimental searches at RHIC to distinguish between QGP formation and conventional nuclear effects by measuring the x_F dependence of charmonium suppression, as suggested by Kopeliovich, Hufner, and Polleri (2001).
