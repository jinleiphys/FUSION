# Radiative corrections for factorized jet observables in heavy ion collisions

> I look at the renormalization of the medium structure function and a medium induced jet function in a factorized cross section for jet substructure observables in Heavy Ion collisions. This is based on the formalism developed in \cite{Vaidya:2020lih}, which uses an Open quantum system approach combined with the Effective Field Theory(EFT) for forward scattering to derive a factorization formula for jet observables which work as hard probes of a long lived dilute Quark Gluon Plasma(QGP) medium. I show that the universal medium structure function that captures the observable independent physics of the QGP has both UV and rapidity anomalous dimensions that appear due to medium induced Bremsstrahlung. The resulting Renormalization Group(RG) equations correspond to the BFKL equation and the running of the QCD coupling respectively. I present the first results for the numerical impact of resummation using these RG equations on the mean free path of the jet in the medium. I also briefly discuss the prospects of extending this formalism for a short lived dense medium.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the universal medium structure function for the Quark Gluon Plasma has both UV and rapidity anomalous dimensions arising from medium-induced Bremsstrahlung, with the resulting Renormalization Group equations corresponding to the BFKL equation and the running of the QCD coupling respectively, but only under the simplifying assumption of a long-lived dilute medium where the formation time is much smaller than both the mean free path and the medium length.

## Method

The author computes one-loop radiative corrections for medium-induced Bremsstrahlung in the medium structure function and medium-induced jet function within the Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) framework combined with an open quantum systems approach, using a thermal density matrix for the Quark Gluon Plasma medium. The calculation identifies UV and rapidity divergences in these functions, which determine their Renormalization Group evolution equations, and the solutions to these equations resum logarithms of widely separated scales. The formalism assumes a weakly coupled QGP with temperature much larger than Lambda_QCD, a long-lived dilute medium where the mean free path and medium length exceed the jet formation time, and homogeneous medium properties over the scales probed by the jet.

## Key numbers

* The temperature of the QGP medium in current heavy ion collision experiments lies in the range 150-500 MeV.
* The medium exists for approximately 10 fm/c.
* The Debye screening mass m_D is parametrically much smaller than the temperature T under the weak coupling assumption g << 1.
* The expansion parameter lambda of the EFT is defined as lambda = q_T/Q ~ T/Q ~ sqrt(e_i), where e_i is the cumulative jet mass measurement with scaling e_i ~ T^2/Q^2.
* The tree level medium jet function result is (e^{-i k_perp dot b} - 1) / k_perp^2.
* The elastic collision contributions to the medium jet function contain single logarithms of the form ln(Q^2 e_n / m_D^2) ~ ln(T^2 / m_D^2) ~ ln(1/g^2).

## Context

This work extends the formalism developed in Vaidya (2020) which combined SCET with open quantum systems to derive a factorization formula for jet substructure observables in heavy ion collisions, analogous to how Parton Distribution Functions factorize universal hadron structure in Deep Inelastic Scattering and Drell-Yan processes. The paper addresses the previously uncalculated radiative corrections from medium-induced Bremsstrahlung, complementing the elastic collision corrections computed in Vaidya (2020) which were UV finite but IR sensitive. This enables resummation of large logarithms via Renormalization Group equations, with the long-term goal of developing a theoretically robust formalism for calculating jet substructure observables for both light parton and heavy quark jets (such as bottom quark jets) in heavy ion collisions at LHC and RHIC.
