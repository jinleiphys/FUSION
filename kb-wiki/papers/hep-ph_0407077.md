# Radiative tritium beta-decay and the neutrino mass

> The shape of the electron energy spectrum in 3H beta-decay permits a direct assay of the absolute scale of the neutrino mass; a highly accurate theoretical description of the electron energy spectrum is necessary to the empirical task. We update Sirlin's calculation of the outer radiative correction to nuclear beta-decay to take into account the non-zero energy resolution of the electron detector. In previous 3H beta-decay studies the outer radiative corrections were neglected all together; only Coulomb corrections to the spectrum were included. This neglect artificially pushes m_nu^2 < 0 in a potentially significant way. We present a computation of the theoretical spectrum appropriate to the extraction of the neutrino mass in the sub-eV regime.

# Radiative tritium beta-decay and the neutrino mass

## Key claim

The outer radiative correction to the electron energy spectrum in tritium beta-decay, which was neglected in all previous experimental analyses, produces a shape change that artificially pushes the inferred squared neutrino mass toward negative values. The magnitude of this shift is at the order of 1 eV², but the precise value depends on the detector energy resolution and the energy interval used in the fit.

## Method

The authors update Sirlin's (1967) calculation of the outer radiative correction to nuclear beta-decay by incorporating the finite energy resolution ΔE of the electron detector. They separate the bremsstrahlung contribution into two regimes: for photon energies Eγ ≤ ΔE, the electron and photon energies are summed and recorded as a single "electron" energy, while for Eγ > ΔE they are recorded separately. The resulting correction function g(ΔE, Ee, Ee^max) is computed using the matrix elements from radiative neutron beta-decay, adapted to tritium, and combined with virtual photon contributions to yield an infrared-finite result. Recoil corrections are also included using nuclear form factors determined from the conserved-vector-current hypothesis and measured magnetic moments of ³H and ³He.

## Key numbers

- The Fermi function F ∼ 1.19 in the endpoint region (Ee − me = 18.5 keV), while the Sirlin radiative correction (α/2π)gS ∼ 0.02
- The tritium endpoint energy is Ee^max − me = 18.57 keV
- The tritium mass MA = 2809.4319 MeV, giving Ee^max/MA ∼ 1.9 × 10⁻⁴
- The half-life t1/2 = 12.3 years
- The nuclear radius Re = 1.68 fm
- The ratio gA/gV' = 1.22, determined from the half-life and phase-space integral f(1 + δR) = 2.9109 × 10⁻⁶
- The weak-magnetism coupling f2/gV' = −3.0533 from measured magnetic moments
- The charge-symmetry breaking correction δc = 0.06%
- The Mainz experiment (Weinheimer 1999) used ΔE = 4.4 eV and reported mν² = −3.7 ± 5.3 ± 2.1 eV²
- Fitting the corrected spectrum over the last 70 eV with ΔE = 4.4 eV yields fits with mν² values ranging from −0.01 eV² to −67 eV² depending on normalization

## Context

This work extends Sirlin's (1967) calculation of outer radiative corrections to nuclear beta-decay by incorporating the finite energy resolution of electron detectors, a feature previously considered by Vogel (1984) for neutrino capture on deuterium. The authors show that previous tritium beta-decay experiments (Robertson 1991, Mainz collaboration 1999, Troitsk collaboration) neglected outer radiative corrections entirely, including only Coulomb corrections via the Fermi function. This omission artificially biases the inferred neutrino mass toward negative values. The results enable a more accurate theoretical spectrum for future sub-eV neutrino mass experiments such as KATRIN, by providing the complete O(α) correction as F* + (α/2π)g(ΔE, Ee, Ee^max) + R, where R is the recoil correction and F* includes finite nuclear size and screening effects from Wilkinson (1982).
