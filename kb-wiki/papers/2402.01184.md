# Residual Tensor Force Effects on the Gamow-Teller states in Magic Nuclei, 48Ca, 90Zr, 132Sn, and 208Pb

> We investigate the tensor force (TF) effect %in the residual interaction on the Gamow-Teller (GT) transitions in four magic nuclei, $^{48}$Ca, $^{90}$Zr, $^{132}$Sn and $^{208}$Pb. The TF is taken into account by using the Brückner $G$-matrix theory with the charge-dependent (CD) Bonn potential as the residual interaction of charge-exchange quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA). We found that particle-particle ($p-p$) tensor interaction does not affect the GT transitions because of the closed shell nature in the nuclei, but repulsive particle-hole ($p-h$) residual interaction for the $p-h$ configuration of spin-orbit partners dominates the high-lying giant GT states for all of the nuclei. It is also shown that appreciable GT strengths are shifted to lower energy region by the attractive $p-h$ TF for the same $j_π=j_ν$ configuration, and produce the low-lying GT peak about 2.5 MeV in $^{48}$Ca. Simultaneously, in $^{90}$Zr and $^{132}$Sn, the low-energy strength appears as a lower energy shoulder near the main GT peak. On the other hand, the shift of the low-lying GT state is not seen clearly for $^{208}$Pb because of the strong spin-orbit splitting of high $j$ orbits, which dominates the GT strength.

## Key claim

The tensor force in the residual interaction shifts Gamow-Teller strength to lower energy in magic nuclei through the attractive particle-hole tensor force for same-j configurations, but only when the particle-hole interaction is dominant (as in closed-shell nuclei), in contrast to the particle-particle tensor force which is inactive in these systems.

## Method

The authors performed charge-exchange quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) calculations using a Woods-Saxon mean field and residual interactions derived from the Brückner G-matrix with the charge-dependent Bonn potential. The tensor force was explicitly included in the G-matrix by decomposing the nucleon-nucleon potential into spin-singlet, uncoupled spin-triplet, and coupled spin-triplet channels, with the coupled spin-triplet channels (J = L ± 1) corresponding to the tensor force from pi- and rho-meson exchange. The particle-particle and particle-hole renormalization factors g_pp and g_ph were varied to study the tensor force effects on the Gamow-Teller strength distributions in the four magic nuclei.

## Key numbers

- In 48Ca, the low-lying GT peak appears at E_ex = 2.5 MeV, attributed to the attractive p-h tensor force on the same-shell configuration (nu 0f_7/2 -> pi 0f_7/2)
- The high-lying GT peak in 48Ca is dominated by the spin-flip p-h excitation (nu 0f_7/2 -> pi 0f_5/2) around E_ex = 7-15 MeV
- Empirical np pairing gaps used for fitting: 0.418 MeV (48Ca), 0.190 MeV (90Zr), 0.409 MeV (132Sn), and 0.237 MeV (208Pb)
- The particle model space was extended up to N_0 = 10 hbar omega in the spherical basis
- For 90Zr, a left shoulder of the main GT state appears around E_ex = 9 MeV in experimental data

## Context

This work extends previous QRPA calculations by Ha (2022) that studied tensor force effects on GT transitions in N=Z+2 nuclei, where the attractive particle-particle interaction produced low-energy super GT states. The present study focuses on closed-shell nuclei (48Ca, 90Zr, 132Sn, 208Pb) where the particle-hole interaction dominates, showing that the tensor force shifts strength to lower energy through the attractive p-h channel for same-j configurations, as opposed to the p-p mechanism in open-shell nuclei. The results confirm the tensor force properties discussed by Otsuka (2005, 2010) and by shell model studies, and demonstrate that the Pandya transformation converts the repulsive p-p tensor force into an attractive p-h tensor force for magic nuclei. This enables future studies of tensor force effects in heavier closed-shell systems and provides microscopic input for density functional theory approaches.
