# Near-threshold dipole strength in {^{10}}Be with isoscalar character

> Isoscalar dipole transitions are a distinctive fingerprint of cluster structures. A {1^-} resonance at 7.27(10) MeV, located just below the α-emission threshold, has been observed in the deuteron inelastic scattering reactions off 10Be. The deformation lengths of the excited states in 10Be below 9 MeV have been inferred from the differential cross sections using coupled channel calculations. This observed {1^-} resonance has isoscalar characteristics and exhausts approximately 5{\%}-15{\%} of the isoscalar dipole energy-weighted sum rule, providing evidence for pronounced α cluster structure in 10Be. The Gamow coupled channel approach supports this interpretation and suggests the near-threshold effect might be playing an important role in this excitation energy domain. The α+α+n+n four-body calculation reproduces the observed enhanced dipole strength, implying that the four-body cluster structure is essential to describe the {1^-} states in 10Be.

## Key claim

The paper reports the observation of a 1^- resonance at 7.27(10) MeV in 10Be, located just below the alpha-emission threshold, which exhibits isoscalar dipole character and exhausts approximately 5%-15% of the isoscalar dipole energy-weighted sum rule. The abstract's claim of "pronounced alpha cluster structure" is qualified by the fact that the assignment depends on coupled-channel fits to angular distributions where the slope of the first maximum (20° to 50° in center-of-mass angle) drives the spin-parity assignment, and the resonance may correspond to a previously known 7.37-MeV state whose spin assignment is now disputed.

## Method

Deuteron inelastic scattering reactions on a 10Be beam at 9.06 MeV/u were measured using the Active-Target Time Projection Chamber (AT-TPC) coupled to the SOLARIS solenoidal spectrometer at the ReA6 facility of NSCL. Differential cross sections for elastic and inelastic scattering were extracted over center-of-mass angles from 20° to 120°, and analyzed using coupled-channel calculations with the code FRESCO to infer deformation lengths and spin-parity assignments. The Gamow coupled channel (GCC) approach and an alpha+alpha+n+n four-body cluster model using generator coordinate method (GCM) Brink wave functions were employed for theoretical interpretation.

## Key numbers

- 1^- resonance observed at 7.27(10) MeV excitation energy in 10Be, located just below the alpha-emission threshold S_alpha = 7.41 MeV
- Nuclear deformation length delta_1 = 0.76 fm for the 7.27-MeV level, with approximately 25% uncertainty dominated by optical model potential variations and absolute cross section uncertainties
- The resonance exhausts approximately 5%-15% of the isoscalar dipole energy-weighted sum rule (IS-EWSR)
- Deformation length for the 3.37-MeV 2^+ state: delta_2 = 1.89 fm, in agreement with literature value delta_p ~ 1.89(18) fm from proton inelastic scattering
- Beam energy at center of detector: 9.06 MeV/u; beam intensity approximately 2000 particles per second with purity close to 100%
- Excitation spectrum resolution approximately 700 keV (FWHM)
- The four-body calculation predicts B(E1) = 1.56 x 10^-5 e^2fm^2 for the 1^-_1 state, consistent with experimental value

## Context

This work extends studies of cluster structures in neutron-rich beryllium isotopes, where 10Be can be considered as two valence neutrons coupled to a 2-alpha (8Be) core. The observed near-threshold dipole resonance provides evidence for the "threshold alignment" effect previously seen in the Hoyle state in 12C (Freer), the 11.425-MeV state in 11B (Ayyad), and the 3.2-MeV 0^- state in 12Be (Chen). The results contradict the previous spin assignment of the 7.37-MeV state as 3^- from neutron resonance scattering (Lane), and the four-body calculation improves upon the three-body cluster model of Kanada-En'yo (Kanada-AMD) which treated the two valence neutrons as a single cluster. The work enables future measurements of direct reactions with rare exotic beams using the AT-TPC coupled to SOLARIS to study threshold phenomena in other neutron-rich nuclei.
