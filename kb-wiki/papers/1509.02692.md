# Anisotropic flow fluctuations in hydro-inspired freeze-out model for relativistic heavy ion collisions

> The LHC data on event-by-event harmonic flow coefficients measured in PbPb collisions at center-of-mass energy 2.76 TeV per nucleon pair are analyzed and interpreted within the HYDJET++ model. To compare the model results with the experimental data the unfolding procedure is employed. The essentially dynamical origin of the flow fluctuations in hydro-inspired freeze-out approach has been established. It is shown that the simple modification of the model via introducing the distribution over spatial anisotropy parameters permits HYDJET++ to reproduce both elliptic and triangular flow fluctuations and related to it eccentricity fluctuations of the initial state at the LHC energy.

## Key claim

The paper establishes that flow fluctuations in the HYDJET++ model have an essentially dynamical origin, arising from momentum-coordinate correlations in the hydro-inspired freeze-out approach, and that a simple modification introducing Gaussian smearing of spatial anisotropy parameters allows the model to reproduce both elliptic and triangular flow fluctuations measured by ATLAS, provided the unfolding procedure is applied to remove non-flow effects.

## Method

The authors used the HYDJET++ event generator to simulate PbPb collisions at 2.76 TeV per nucleon pair, incorporating both a soft hydro-type freeze-out component and a hard jet-quenching component. They introduced event-by-event Gaussian smearing of the spatial anisotropy parameters epsilon(b) and epsilon_3(b) to enhance flow fluctuations, then applied the Bayesian unfolding procedure (using the RooUnfold package) with a response function constructed from the two sub-event method to remove non-flow effects and finite multiplicity contributions. The unfolded distributions of the flow vector modulus V_n for n=2 and n=3 were compared directly with ATLAS data across multiple centrality bins.

## Key numbers

- The Gaussian width of the flow vector distributions in central collisions (b=0) is sigma_n approximately 0.013 for all harmonics n=2,3,4, with the ratio sigma_{V_n}/<V_n> = sqrt(4/pi - 1) = 0.523
- For non-central collisions at 20-25% centrality, the widths sigma_n are approximately 0.02 for n=2,3,4
- The model reproduces the correlation between event-averaged elliptic and triangular flow coefficients within 7% accuracy compared to ATLAS data
- The analysis was performed for three centrality intervals: 5-10%, 20-25%, and 35-40%, plus peripheral 60-65% with retuned parameters epsilon=0.14 and delta=0.25 (versus default epsilon=0.16 and delta=0.38)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous HYDJET++ studies (Bravina 2013) that analyzed event-averaged flow harmonics, by now examining event-by-event flow fluctuations directly connected to initial-state geometry fluctuations as studied by Petersen (2013), Luzum (2013), and Niemi (2015). The paper enables quantitative comparison of hydro-inspired freeze-out models with unfolded LHC data from the ATLAS Collaboration (Aad 2013), and demonstrates that the extracted eccentricity distributions from HYDJET++ agree with the Elliptic Power distribution parametrization proposed by Yan (2014).
