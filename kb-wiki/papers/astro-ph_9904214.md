# Phase Transitions in Neutron Stars and Maximum Masses

> Using the most recent realistic effective interactions for nuclear matter with a smooth extrapolation to high densities including causality, we constrain the equation of state and calculate maximum masses of rotating neutron stars. First and second order phase transitions to, e.g., quark matter at high densities are included. If neutron star masses of $\sim 2.3M_\odot$ from quasi-periodic oscillations in low mass X-ray binaries are confirmed, a soft equation of state as well as strong phase transitions can be excluded in neutron star cores.

## Key claim

If neutron star masses of approximately 2.3 solar masses from quasi-periodic oscillations in low-mass X-ray binaries are confirmed, a soft equation of state as well as strong phase transitions can be excluded in neutron star cores, but this conclusion depends on the interpretation that the observed maximum QPO frequencies correspond to the innermost stable circular orbit.

## Method

The authors parametrize the Akmal, Pandharipande, and Ravenhall (1998) equation of state for nuclear matter using a simple form for compressional and symmetry energies that reproduces saturation properties and smoothly incorporates causality at high densities by forcing the sound speed to approach the speed of light. They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equation with and without rotational corrections following the approach of Hartle (1967), using equations of state for pure neutron-proton matter with varying softness parameter s, and also incorporating first-order (Maxwell construction) and second-order (mixed phase) transitions to quark matter described by the bag model with bag constants B = 150 and 200 MeV fm^{-3}.

## Key numbers

- The softness parameter s must satisfy s >= 0.13 from the causality condition v_s <= c, derived from the relation s >= sqrt(E_0/m_n) where E_0 = 15.8 MeV is the binding energy per nucleon at saturation.
- For the mixed phase with B = 150 MeV fm^{-3}, the mixed phase begins at 0.51 fm^{-3} and the pure quark matter phase begins at 1.89 fm^{-3}; for B = 200 MeV fm^{-3}, the mixed phase starts at 0.72 fm^{-3} and the pure quark phase at 2.11 fm^{-3}.
- For the Maxwell construction with B = 150 MeV fm^{-3}, the pure pn phase ends at 0.92 fm^{-3} and the pure quark phase starts at 1.215 fm^{-3}; for B = 200 MeV fm^{-3}, the corresponding densities are 1.04 and 1.57 fm^{-3}.
- The maximum mass for pure quark stars scales as M_max = 2.0 M_sun (58 MeV fm^{-3} / B)^{1/2} (for alpha_s = 0).
- The observed maximum QPO frequency of 1060 Hz in 4U 1820-30 gives M ~ 2.25 M_sun after correcting for the 275 Hz neutron star spin frequency; frequencies of 1125 Hz in 4U 1608-52 and 1228 Hz in 4U 1636-536 give masses of 2.1 and 1.9 M_sun respectively.
- Rotation increases the maximum mass and corresponding radii by 10-15% for spin frequencies around 300 Hz.

## Context

This work extends the analyses of Akmal, Pandharipande, and Ravenhall (1998) and Kalogera and Baym (1996) by incorporating causality smoothly into the nuclear equation of state rather than discontinuously setting the sound speed to the speed of light above a chosen density. The authors find that their maximum masses are smaller than those obtained by Kalogera and Baym (1996), who obtained upper bounds of 2.2 to 2.9 solar masses by varying the density at which the sound speed jumps to c. The results enable constraints on the bag constant B (requiring B >= 200 MeV fm^{-3}) and exclude phase transitions to quark matter below about 5 times nuclear saturation density if the 2.3 solar mass QPO masses are confirmed.
