# Resonance Production in Heavy Ion Collisions: Suppression of $Λ(1520)$ and Enhancement of $Σ(1385)$

> We investigate the yield of $Λ(1520)$ resonance in heavy ion collisions within the framework of a kinetic master equation without the assumption of chemical equilibrium. We show that reactions such as $Λ(1520)+π\leftrightarrow Σ^*$ can favor $Σ^*$ production, thereby decreasing the $Λ(1520)$ yield. Within the same approach we thus find a yield enhancement for $Σ(1385)$ and a yield suppression for $Λ(1520)$.

# Resonance Production in Heavy Ion Collisions: Suppression of Λ(1520) and Enhancement of Σ(1385)

## Key claim

The yield of the long-lived resonance Λ(1520) can be considerably suppressed, and the yield of Σ(1385) enhanced, relative to statistical hadron gas benchmark expectations, specifically when the initial hadron fugacities are above chemical equilibrium (as expected for a hadronizing quark-gluon plasma phase) and when reactions such as Λ(1520)+π ↔ Σ* (where Σ* denotes heavier Σ resonances) favor Σ* production, thereby depleting Λ(1520).

## Method

The authors solve a system of coupled kinetic master equations for the time-dependent chemical fugacities of Λ(1520), Σ(1385), five Σ* resonances (Σ(1670), Σ(1750), Σ(1775), Σ(1940), Σ(2030)), and ground-state hadrons (Λ(1115), Σ(1190), N, K, K(892), Δ), using a fourth-order Runge-Kutta method. The evolution is embedded in a fireball expansion model with conserved entropy content and a hydrodynamic-inspired temperature profile, assuming thermal (Boltzmann) momentum distributions for heavy baryons and complete Bose distributions for pions, with the pion fugacity held constant by entropy conservation. Two limiting scenarios are considered: one where all reactions proceed in both directions (full detailed balance), and a "dead channel" scenario where resonance formation is switched off for mass differences m3 - (m1 + m2) > 300 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Λ(1520) total decay width: approximately 15.6 MeV, with main channels Σ+π (Γ ≈ 6.5 MeV) and N+K (Γ ≈ 7 MeV).
- For hadronization at T0 = 140 MeV with γq = 1.6, the in-medium lifespan ratio τ3/τ0 for Σ(1385) ↔ Λπ reaches approximately 0.65 at the lowest temperatures, compared to an inverse Bose enhancement factor of 0.54.
- Three initial hadronization temperatures are considered: T0 = 180 MeV (γq = 1.0), T0 = 160 MeV (γq = 1.27), and T0 = 140 MeV (γq = 1.6), with the kinetic freeze-out temperature assumed at T = 120 MeV.
- The Bose enhancement factor 1+fπ(E*2) for the reaction Σ(1385) ↔ Λπ at T = 140 MeV and γq = 1.6 is shown to be the largest among the three temperature scenarios.

## Context

This work extends the approach previously used for Σ(1385)/Λ0 ratios by Kuznetsova (2008) to the longer-lived Λ(1520) resonance, incorporating a more complete reaction network including multiple Σ* resonances and Bose enhancement effects. The results contradict the expectation from simple statistical hadron gas models that long-lived resonances should be enhanced, and instead show suppression for Λ(1520) consistent with experimental reports from both RHIC and SPS experiments cited as Markert (2002) and Adams (2006). The findings enable quantitative interpretation of observed resonance yields as probes of the duration and conditions of the hadronic kinetic phase following QGP hadronization, and suggest that the ratio Λ(1520)/Λ0 may serve as a sensitive observable for chemical non-equilibrium at hadronization.
