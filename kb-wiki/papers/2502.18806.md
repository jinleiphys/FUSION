# Quarkonium Polarization Kinetic Equation from Open Quantum Systems and Effective Field Theories

> Recent measurements of polarization phenomena in relativistic heavy ion collisions have aroused a great interest in understanding dynamical spin evolution of the QCD matter. In particular, the spin alignment signature of $J/ψ$ has been recently observed in Pb-Pb collisions at LHC, which may infer nontrivial spin transport of quarkonia in quark gluon plasmas. Motivated by this, we study the spin-dependent in-medium dynamics of quarkonia by using the potential nonrelativistic QCD (pNRQCD) and the open quantum system framework. By applying the Markovian approximation and Wigner transformation, we systematically derive the Boltzmann transport equation for vector quarkonia with polarization dependence in the quantum optical limit. As opposed to the previous study for the spin-independent case where the collision terms depend on chromoelectric correlators, the new kinetic equation incorporates gauge invariant correlators of chromomagnetic fields that determine the recombination and dissociation terms with polarization dependence at the order we are working in the multipole expansion. In the quantum Brownian motion limit, the Lindblad equation with new transport coefficients defined in terms of the chromomagnetic field correlators have also been derived. Our formalism is generic and valid for both weakly-coupled and strongly-coupled quark gluon plasmas. It may be further applied to study spin alignment of vector quarkonia in heavy ion collisions.

# Quarkonium Polarization Kinetic Equation from Open Quantum Systems and Effective Field Theories

## Key claim
The authors derive a polarization-dependent Boltzmann transport equation for vector quarkonia in a quark-gluon plasma by combining potential nonrelativistic QCD (pNRQCD) with the open quantum system framework, showing that chromomagnetic field correlators, not just chromoelectric ones, govern spin-dependent dissociation and recombination terms. The formalism is valid for both weakly-coupled and strongly-coupled QGP, but the multipole expansion may break down for charmonia at early times in the QGP phase when the temperature exceeds the inverse of the quarkonium size.

## Method
The authors start from the pNRQCD effective Lagrangian, which describes color-singlet and color-octet heavy quark-antiquark fields, and include interaction terms up to order |r| in the multipole expansion and order 1/M in the nonrelativistic expansion. They apply the Markovian approximation and the quantum optical limit (where the system relaxation time is much larger than both the environment correlation time and the intrinsic system timescale) to the open quantum system master equation, then perform a Wigner transformation to obtain a Boltzmann equation for the phase-space distribution function of vector quarkonia with explicit polarization dependence. The collision kernels involve gauge-invariant correlators of chromoelectric and chromomagnetic fields, with the latter coupling to the polarization vectors.

## Key numbers
- The hierarchy of energy scales assumed is M >> Mv >> Mv^2, T, Lambda_QCD, where M is the heavy-quark mass and v is the relative velocity of the heavy quark-antiquark pair.
- For charmonia, the inverse of the typical relative distance |r|^{-1} ~ Mv ~ 0.9 GeV, while the QGP temperature T_QGP ~ 0.15-0.45 GeV, so the |r|T expansion may break down at early times.
- The spin alignment observable rho_00 deviates from 1/3 when the three polarization distribution functions f_0, f_+, f_- are not equal; rho_00 = 1/3 corresponds to no spin alignment.

## Context
This work extends the spin-independent quarkonium transport framework developed by Yao (2018, 2020) by incorporating polarization dependence through chromomagnetic field correlators, building on the open quantum system approach to heavy quark-antiquark pairs in a thermal environment (Rothkopf 2019, Akamatsu 2020, Yao 2021, Sharma 2021). It is motivated by the recent observation of J/psi spin alignment in Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC by the ALICE Collaboration (2022), and complements studies of light vector meson spin alignment using quantum kinetic theory (Yang 2021, Kumar 2022, 2023, Yang 2024). The derived formalism enables future calculations of vector quarkonium spin alignment in heavy ion collisions, though the color-octet distribution functions required as input remain to be solved from a coupled kinetic equation, and the spin-dependent case for bottomonia extends the spin-independent study by Yao (2020).
