# The nucleon and the nuclear force in the context of effective theory and path-integral methods

> The nucleon structure and the nuclear force are investigated in the context of the non-perturbative path-integral method of hadronization. Starting from a microscopic quark-diquark model, the nucleon is generated as a relativistic bound state and an effective chiral meson-nucleon Lagrangian is derived. Many of the nucleon physical properties are studied using a theory of at most two free parameters.

## Key claim

The nucleon can be generated as a relativistic bound state from a microscopic quark-diquark model using the non-perturbative path-integral method of hadronization, leading to an effective chiral meson-nucleon Lagrangian. The approach reproduces many nucleon properties with at most two free parameters, though the numerical results presented use only scalar diquarks and therefore show missing strength for magnetic properties and the axial-vector coupling.

## Method

Starting from a Nambu Jona-Lasinio (NJL) Lagrangian with SU(2)L x SU(2)R chiral symmetry, the authors introduce composite scalar and pseudoscalar meson fields via a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation, then incorporate scalar isoscalar and axial-vector isovector diquark fields. A quark-diquark interaction term is added to generate the nucleon as a linear combination of these diquarks, and a second Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation introduces collective nucleon fields. By integrating over quark and diquark fields, the theory is "hadronized" into an effective meson-nucleon Lagrangian, with the nucleon mass determined as the pole of the dynamically generated propagator.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass: m_q = 0.390 GeV
- Scalar diquark mass: M_D = 0.600 GeV
- Quark-diquark coupling constant: G_tilde = 159.1 GeV^{-1}
- Pauli-Villars mass: Lambda = 0.600 GeV
- Nucleon mass: M_B = 0.94 GeV (used to fix the free parameter)
- Binding energy: Delta_E_bin = m_q + M_D - M_B = 50 MeV, suggesting a loosely bound state
- Proton magnetic moment (with or without intrinsic diquark form factor): mu_p = 1.57 (experiment: 2.79)
- Neutron magnetic moment: mu_n = -0.75 (experiment: -1.91)
- Axial-vector coupling: g_A = 0.87 (experiment: 1.26)
- Proton electric charge radius (with IDFF): <r^2>^p_E = 0.77 fm^2 (experiment: 0.74 fm^2)
- Neutron electric charge radius (with IDFF): <r^2>^n_E = -0.11 fm^2 (experiment: -0.12 fm^2)
- Proton magnetic radius (with IDFF): <r^2>^p_M = 0.82 fm^2 (experiment: 0.74 fm^2)
- Neutron magnetic radius (with IDFF): <r^2>^n_M = 0.84 fm^2 (experiment: 0.77 fm^2)

## Context

This work extends the hadronization approach of Cahill (1989) and Reinhardt (1990) and the earlier nucleon construction using only scalar diquarks by Ebert and Jurke (1998). It is the first calculation of an extensive set of nucleon observables using path-integral hadronization since the idea was introduced over a decade prior. The derived chiral meson-nucleon Lagrangian is of the quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) type from Serot and Walecka (1986), and the formalism can be applied to other baryons and their interactions, potentially providing nuclear physics with a foundation derived from QCD-based effective theory.
