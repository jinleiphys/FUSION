# Non-Newtonian gravity in strange quark stars and constraints from the observations of PSR J0740+6620 and GW170817

> We investigate the effects of non-Newtonian gravity on the properties of strange quark stars (QSs) and constrain the parameters of the standard MIT bag model used to describe strange quark matter (SQM) by employing the mass of PSR J0740+6620 and the tidal deformability of GW170817. We find that, for the standard MIT bag model, these mass and tidal deformability observations would rule out the existence of QSs if non-Newtonian gravity effects are ignored. For a strange quark mass of $m_{s}=95$ MeV, we find that QSs can exist for values of the non-Newtonian gravity parameter $g^{2}/μ^{2}$ in the range of 1.37 GeV$^{-2}\leq g^{2}/μ^{2}\leq$ 7.28 GeV$^{-2}$ and limits on the bag constant and the strong interaction coupling constant of the SQM model given by 141.3 MeV$\leq B^{1/4}\leq$ 150.9 MeV and $α_{S}\leq 0.56$. For a strange quark mass of $m_{s}=150$ MeV, QSs can exist for 1.88 GeV$^{-2}\leq g^{2}/μ^{2}\leq$ 6.27 GeV$^{-2}$ and limits on the parameters of the SQM model given by 139.7 MeV$\leq B^{1/4}\leq$ 147.3 MeV and $α_{S}\leq 0.49$.

## Key claim

The existence of strange quark stars (QSs) would be ruled out by the mass of PSR J0740+6620 and the tidal deformability of GW170817 if non-Newtonian gravity effects are ignored in the standard MIT bag model; however, including a Yukawa-type non-Newtonian gravity term allows QSs to simultaneously satisfy both observational constraints for specific ranges of the non-Newtonian gravity parameter.

## Method

The authors modeled strange quark matter (SQM) using the standard MIT bag model with first-order perturbative corrections in the strong coupling constant, for strange quark masses of 95 MeV and 150 MeV. They added a Yukawa-type non-Newtonian gravity term (vector boson exchange) to the energy density and pressure, then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to compute mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities. They imposed four constraints: absolute stability of three-flavor SQM, instability of two-flavor quark matter, maximum mass at least 2.14 solar masses (PSR J0740+6620), and dimensionless tidal deformability at 1.4 solar masses below 580 (GW170817).

## Key numbers

- For strange quark mass m_s = 95 MeV: allowed non-Newtonian gravity parameter range is 1.37 GeV^{-2} ≤ g^2/μ^2 ≤ 7.28 GeV^{-2}
- For m_s = 95 MeV: bag constant range is 141.3 MeV ≤ B^{1/4} ≤ 150.9 MeV and strong coupling constant α_S ≤ 0.56
- For strange quark mass m_s = 150 MeV: allowed non-Newtonian gravity parameter range is 1.88 GeV^{-2} ≤ g^2/μ^2 ≤ 6.27 GeV^{-2}
- For m_s = 150 MeV: bag constant range is 139.7 MeV ≤ B^{1/4} ≤ 147.3 MeV and strong coupling constant α_S ≤ 0.49
- The maximum mass constraint uses PSR J0740+6620 at 2.14^{+0.10}_{-0.09} solar masses (68.3% credibility)
- The tidal deformability constraint uses Λ(1.4) ≤ 580 from GW170817 (Abbott et al. 2018)

## Context

This work extends prior studies of non-Newtonian gravity in compact stars (Krivoruchenko et al. 2009, Wen et al. 2009, Long et al. 2014) by applying updated observational constraints. It contradicts the earlier finding by Zhou et al. (2018) that GW170817 data could not rule out QSs, because those authors used a weaker tidal deformability limit (Λ(1.4) ≤ 800) and the older pulsar mass PSR J0348+0432 (2.01 solar masses). The results enable future tests of non-Newtonian gravity using neutron star observations from NICER and gravitational-wave detectors, and suggest extending the analysis to other SQM models such as the quasi-particle model.
