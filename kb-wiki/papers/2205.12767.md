# Variational thermal quantum simulation of the lattice Schwinger model

> Confinement of quarks due to the strong interaction and the deconfinement at high temperatures and high densities are a basic paradigm for understanding the nuclear matter. Their simulation, however, is very challenging for classical computers due to the sign problem of solving equilibrium states of finite-temperature quantum chromodynamical systems at finite density. In this paper, we propose a variational approach, using the lattice Schwinger model, to simulate the confinement or deconfinement by investigating the string tension. We adopt an ansatz that the string tension can be evaluated without referring to quantum protocols for measuring the entropy in the free energy. Results of numeral simulation show that the string tension decreases both along the increasing of the temperature and the chemical potential, which can be an analog of the phase diagram of QCD. Our work paves a way for exploiting near-term quantum computers for investigating the phase diagram of finite-temperature and finite density for nuclear matters.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a variational quantum algorithm to simulate the confinement-deconfinement behavior of the lattice Schwinger model at finite temperature and finite density, but the results are limited to numerical simulations on a classical computer rather than on actual quantum hardware, and the string tension only vanishes at infinite temperature with no confinement-deconfinement phase transition as in QCD.

## Method

The authors map the 1+1D lattice Schwinger model to a qubit Hamiltonian via the Kogut-Susskind formulation and Jordan-Wigner transformation, then apply a variational quantum algorithm that minimizes the free energy using a product spectrum ansatz. The ansatz parameterizes the thermal state as a product of single-qubit mixed states evolved by a parameterized quantum circuit, allowing the von Neumann entropy to be computed analytically from the initial product state. The string tension is evaluated as the free energy difference between systems with and without a background electric field corresponding to trial charges at the chain ends.

## Key numbers

- The qubit Hamiltonian for the Schwinger model contains a number of Pauli operators proportional to O(N³), where N is the number of lattice sites.
- The variational algorithm requires 2N qubits to simulate a system of N qubits (N system qubits plus N ancilla qubits for preparing mixed states).
- The string tension decreases exponentially with increasing temperature, consistent with theoretical results from Buyens et al. (2016).
- At high temperature (β=0.1) and low temperature (β=10), the variational algorithm achieves accurate results with small circuit depth p, while intermediate temperatures require larger p for convergence.

## Context

This work extends tensor network studies of the Schwinger model at finite temperature by Buyens et al. (2016) to include finite chemical potential, and addresses the sign problem that limits quantum Monte Carlo methods for finite-density QCD systems. The approach enables future studies of the QCD phase diagram on near-term quantum computers, though the authors note that extensions to non-Abelian gauge theories and larger system sizes will require solutions to the barren plateau problem in variational quantum algorithms and efficient qubit encodings for gauge fields.
