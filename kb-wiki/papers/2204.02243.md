# Molecular formations and spectra due to electron correlations in three-electron hybrid double-well qubits

> We show that systematic full configuration-interaction (FCI) calculations enable prediction of the energy spectra and the intrinsic spatial and spin structures of the many-body wave functions as a function of the detuning parameter for the case of three-electron hybrid qubits based on GaAs asymmetric double quantum dots. Specifically, in comparison with the case of weak interactions and treating the entire three-electron double-dot hybrid qubit as an integral unit, it is shown that the predicted spectroscopic patterns, originating from strong electron correlations, manifest the formation of Wigner molecules (WMs). Signatures of WM formation include: (1) a strong suppression of the energy gaps relative to the non-interacting-electrons modeling, and (2) the appearance of a pair of avoided crossings arising between states associated with two-electron occupancies in the left and right wells. The Wigner molecule is a physical entity associated with electron localization within each well and it cannot be captured by the previously employed independent-particle or two-site-Hubbard theoretical modeling of the hybrid qubits. The emergence of strong WMs is investigated in depth through the concerted use of FCI-adapted diagnostic tools like charge and spin densities, as well as conditional probability distributions. Furthermore, the energy spectrum as a function of the strength of the Coulomb repulsion (at constant detuning) is calculated in order to complement the thorough analysis of the factors contributing to WM emergence. We report remarkable agreement with recent experimental measurements. The present FCI methodology for multi-well quantum dots can be straightforwardly extended to treat valleytronic two-band Si/SiGe hybrid qubits, where the central role of the WMs was confirmed recently.

## Key claim

The paper claims that full configuration-interaction (FCI) calculations, treating the entire three-electron double-dot hybrid qubit as an integral unit, reveal that strong electron correlations cause Wigner molecule formation, which dramatically modifies the energy spectra compared to independent-particle or two-site-Hubbard models; the claim is hedged by the condition that these effects are demonstrated for a specific GaAs asymmetric double quantum dot with parameters comparable to those in Kim (2021), and that the Wigner molecule signatures depend on the dielectric constant and detuning values.

## Method

The authors performed full configuration-interaction (FCI) calculations for three electrons confined in an asymmetric two-dimensional double-well potential modeled by a two-center-oscillator (TCO) potential with a smooth neck, using the Coulomb repulsion as the two-body interaction. They solved for the many-body energy spectra as a function of the interdot detuning parameter at two values of the dielectric constant (κ=1000 for weak interactions and κ=12.5 for GaAs), and also as a function of the dielectric constant at constant detuning. The many-body wave functions were analyzed using charge densities, spin-resolved densities, and spin-resolved conditional probability distributions (CPDs) to diagnose Wigner molecule formation and spin structures.

## Key numbers

- The left dot has confinement frequencies ℏωx1=0.413567 meV and ℏωy1=1.22 meV; the right dot is circular with ℏωx2=ℏωy2=1.22 meV.
- The interdot distance is d=195 nm (left dot at x1=-120 nm, right dot at x2=75 nm), and the interdot barrier is Vb=3.3123 meV.
- For GaAs (κ=12.5), the Wigner parameter values are RW,1=5.31 for the left dot and RW,2=3.09 for the right dot.
- The two avoided crossings in the GaAs spectrum are separated by a detuning energy interval of approximately 400 μeV, agreeing with the experimental value from Kim (2021).
- The energy gap between the ground state and first excited state for GaAs at κ=12.5 is approximately 3 h·GHz (about 12.4 μeV).
- The left avoided crossing occurs in the detuning range 1.49 meV < ε < 1.54 meV; the right avoided crossing occurs in the range 1.885 meV < ε < 1.908 meV.

## Context

This work extends recent experimental findings by Kim (2021) and Ercan (2021) that demonstrated Wigner molecule signatures in two-electron and three-electron quantum dot qubits, going beyond earlier two-particle configuration-interaction calculations in single dots. The paper contradicts the adequacy of independent-particle modeling (as used by Coppersmith (2012) and others) and two-site Hubbard models (as used by Stafford (1994), Loss (1999), and Burkard (2017)) for describing hybrid qubit spectra, showing that only FCI captures the strong electron correlation effects. The methodology can be extended to valleytronic two-band Si/SiGe hybrid qubits and to simulations of Si-based two-qubit logical gates with up to six electrons.
