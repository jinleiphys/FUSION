# Conversion between the formal and observed parameters in $R$-matrix theory

> The conversion between the formal and observed parameters is obtained from the phase equivalence between the $R$-matrix and Breit-Wigner formula, and it is applied to a study of the low-energy $E$1 $S$-factor of $^{12}$C($α$,$γ_0$)$^{16}$O. As an example, weak interference between 1$^-$ states in $^{16}$O is discussed. As well as the ordinary $R$-matrix method, the present method calculates the $S$-matrix and cross sections, independent of the boundary condition. Therefore, the $E$1 $S$-factor at $E_{c.m.} = 300$ keV is found to be reduced from the current evaluation.

# Conversion between the formal and observed parameters in $R$-matrix theory

## Key claim
The conversion between formal and observed $R$-matrix resonance parameters, derived from phase equivalence with the Breit-Wigner formula, shows that the $E1$ $S$-factor for $^{12}$C($\alpha$,$\gamma_0$)$^{16}$O at $E_{c.m.}=300$ keV is reduced from the current evaluation, provided that the broad 1$^-_2$ resonance is treated accurately and the weak interference between 1$^-$ states is properly accounted for.

## Method
The author derives the conversion between formal resonance parameters (energy $\tilde{E}_{nL}$ and reduced width $\tilde{\gamma}_{nL}^2$) and observed parameters (energy $E_{nL}$ and width $\Gamma_{nL}$) by equating the $S$-matrix from $R$-matrix theory with that from the Breit-Wigner formula in the single-pole approximation. This conversion is then applied to calculate the $E1$ $S$-factor for $^{12}$C($\alpha$,$\gamma_0$)$^{16}$O, including four 1$^-$ states in $^{16}$O (1$^-_1$ at $E_x=7.12$ MeV, 1$^-_2$ at $E_x=9.59$ MeV, 1$^-_3$ at $E_x=12.44$ MeV, and 1$^-_4$ at $E_x=13.09$ MeV), with the boundary condition parameter $\tilde{b}_c$ set to zero. The method is independent of the boundary condition, as the $S$-matrix and cross sections remain invariant under transformation of the formal parameter set.

## Key numbers
- The $E1$ $S$-factor at $E_{c.m.}=300$ keV is reduced from the current evaluation (no explicit numerical value for the reduction factor is given in the text).
- The observed $\alpha$-width of the 1$^-_2$ state is $\Gamma_{21}=359$ keV from Azu94, compared to $\Gamma_{21}=(420\pm20)$ keV from Til93 and $\Gamma_{21}=432$ keV in the present example.
- The observed resonance energy of the 1$^-_2$ state is $E_{21}=2.434$ MeV in the center-of-mass frame.
- The 1$^-_2$ state has excitation energy $E_x=9.59$ MeV in $^{16}$O.
- The 1$^-_1$ state has excitation energy $E_x=7.12$ MeV in $^{16}$O.
- The 1$^-_3$ state has excitation energy $E_x=12.44$ MeV in $^{16}$O.
- The 1$^-_4$ state has excitation energy $E_x=13.09$ MeV in $^{16}$O.
- A very sharp peak in the $R$-matrix is found at $E_{c.m.}=-0.0451$ MeV when using $a_c\tilde{b}_c=\Delta_1(E_{c.m.},a_c)$.

## Context
This work extends the standard $R$-matrix formalism of Lane and Thomas (1958) by providing an explicit conversion between formal and observed resonance parameters that accounts for broad resonances near the single-particle limit. The method is applied to the astrophysically important $^{12}$C($\alpha$,$\gamma_0$)$^{16}$O reaction, which governs helium burning in stars. The results contradict the earlier $R$-matrix analysis of Azu94, which assumed narrow reduced widths and failed to reproduce the $\alpha$-width of the 1$^-_2$ state, and instead support the findings of Katsuma (2017) regarding weak interference between 1$^-$ states. The present method enables consistent calculation of cross sections independent of the boundary condition parameter, and the results are consistent with experimental data on $\beta$-delayed $\alpha$-particle spectra of $^{16}$N from Azu94 and Tan10, as well as $p$-wave phase shifts of $\alpha$+$^{12}$C elastic scattering from Katsuma (2017).
