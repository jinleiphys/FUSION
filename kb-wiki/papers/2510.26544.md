# One-pion exchange potential in a strong magnetic field

> We derive the one-pion exchange potential (OPEP) in the presence of a homogeneous magnetic field using chiral perturbation theory with nonrelativistic nucleons. Our approach is applicable not only to weak magnetic fields but also to strong ones up to around the pion-mass scale. The Green's function of charged pions is modified by the magnetic field, leading to changes in the nuclear force. By numerically evaluating the modified OPEP incorporating its spin and isospin dependencies, we show that the range of the potential decreases in both directions parallel and perpendicular to the magnetic field as the field strength increases. We also compute the resulting energy shift of the deuteron due to the modified OPEP, which can reach the order of 1 MeV around $|eB| = m_π^2$, which is comparable to the deuteron binding energy.

## Key claim
The authors derive the one-pion exchange potential (OPEP) in a homogeneous magnetic field using chiral perturbation theory with nonrelativistic nucleons, finding that the range of the potential decreases in both directions parallel and perpendicular to the field as field strength increases, with the deuteron energy shift reaching order 1 MeV near \(|eB| = m_\pi^2\), but this result is a leading-order estimate subject to truncation uncertainties from higher-order chiral contributions, especially for fields approaching the ultraviolet cutoff scale \(m_\rho^2 \sim 30 m_\pi^2\).

## Method
The authors start from the leading-order chiral Lagrangian with heavy (nonrelativistic) nucleons in a background electromagnetic field, expand to linear order in pion fields, and solve the static equations of motion for charged pions using Schwinger's proper-time method to obtain the full Green's function in a homogeneous magnetic field. They define a gauge-invariant OPEP by introducing a Wilson line to dress the nucleon fields, which cancels the Schwinger phase and reduces the potential to an expression in the Fock-Schwinger gauge, then project the resulting potential onto definite spin and isospin channels (\(T=0, S=1\) and \(T=1, S=0\)) and compute the deuteron energy shift via first-order perturbation theory.

## Key numbers
- The deuteron energy shift due to the modified OPEP can reach the order of 1 MeV around \(|eB| = m_\pi^2\), which is comparable to the deuteron binding energy.
- The ultraviolet cutoff is taken as \(\Lambda_{\mathrm{UV}} \simeq m_\rho = 770\) MeV, corresponding to magnetic field strengths up to \(|eB| \lesssim m_\rho^2 \sim 30 m_\pi^2\).
- The weak-field expansion of the charged-pion propagator is given to \(\mathcal{O}(|eB|^2)\) with the leading correction term \(-\frac{|eB|^2}{96\pi m_\pi^3}(1+m_\pi|\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{x}'|+m_\pi^2|\mathbf{x}_\perp-\mathbf{x}_\perp'|^2)e^{-m_\pi|\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{x}'|}\).

## Context
This work extends recent theoretical efforts to understand how external magnetic fields modify nuclear forces, specifically going beyond the weak-field \(\mathcal{O}((eB)^2)\) corrections to the Yukawa potential evaluated by Braghin (2023) for one-pion exchange between constituent quarks, which showed predominantly isotropic effects with small anisotropies. It also builds on the operator-form formulation of OPEP in chiral effective field theory by Deshmukh (2018), which did not project onto definite spin-isospin channels or account for magnetic-field-induced modifications of observables like the deuteron binding energy. The results enable future studies of two-nucleon bound states and scattering in strong magnetic fields relevant to magnetars and relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
