# Resolving conclusions about the early Universe requires accurate nuclear measurements

> Nuclear physics experiments give reaction rates that, via modelling and comparison with primordial abundances, constrain cosmological parameters. The error bars of a key reaction, \dpg, were tightened in 2020, bringing to light discrepancies between different analyses and calling for more accurate measurements of other reactions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the tightening of the D(p,γ)³He reaction error bars in 2020 has led to conflicting conclusions about the consistency between Big Bang nucleosynthesis (BBN) and cosmic microwave background (CMB) determinations of the baryon density, with some analyses finding perfect agreement and others finding a mild 1.8σ tension, and that this disagreement stems primarily from different modeling choices for the D(d,n)³He and D(d,p)³H reaction rates rather than from the D(p,γ)³He rate itself.

## Method

The authors compare predictions from three BBN codes (PRIMAT, PArThENoPE, and an independent analysis) that all use the same updated LUNA D(p,γ)³He rate but differ in their treatment of the S-factors for the D(d,n)³He and D(d,p)³H reactions, specifically in the selection of experimental datasets, the modeling of energy-dependent S-factors (ab initio theoretical dependence versus polynomial expansion), and the statistical treatment of systematic errors. They also examine the weak interaction rates that interconvert neutrons and protons, finding these contribute less than 0.2% difference in the relevant temperature range.

## Key numbers

- Primordial deuterium abundance D/H = (2.527 ± 0.030) × 10⁻⁵ at redshift z ∼ 2.5–3.1 (the most recent recommended observed value from Cooke (2018))
- Using the NACRE-II D(p,γ)³He rate with CMB-determined baryon density gives D/H = (2.57 ± 0.13) × 10⁻⁵
- Using the new LUNA D(p,γ)³He rate yields D/H = (2.51 ± 0.11) × 10⁻⁵ (Yeh et al. 2020) or D/H = (2.52 ± 0.07) × 10⁻⁵ (Nature DPG paper) or D/H = (2.54 ± 0.07) × 10⁻⁵ (Pisanti et al. 2020) depending on the code and re-evaluation
- The PRIMAT code with the LUNA rate gives D/H = (2.439 ± 0.037) × 10⁻⁵, leading to a 1.8σ tension on the baryon density Ωb h² between BBN and CMB-inferred values

## Context

This work extends the debate following the 2020 LUNA measurement of the D(p,γ)³He reaction (Nature 2020), which tightened error bars on a key BBN rate. The paper contrasts the conclusions of Pisanti et al. (2020) and Yeh et al. (2020), who claim BBN and CMB are in perfect agreement, with the authors' own analysis (Pitrou et al. 2020) finding a mild tension. It identifies the D(d,n)³He and D(d,p)³H reaction rates as the primary source of discrepancy between BBN codes, and calls for new percent-level measurements of these reactions to resolve whether the tension is real or reflects the limits of current BBN predictions.
