# On the density and temperature of neutron-rich systems at the energy of vanishing flow in heavy-ion collisions

> We study nuclear dynamics at the the energy of vanishing flow for neutron-rich systems. In particular, we shall study the collision rate, density and temperature reached in a heavy-ion reaction with neutron-rich systems. We shall also study the mass dependence of these quantities. Our results indicate nearly mass independent nature for the density reached whereas a significant mass dependence exists for the temperature of neutron-rich systems.

## Key claim

The density reached in heavy-ion reactions at the energy of vanishing flow is nearly independent of system mass for neutron-rich systems, whereas the temperature shows a significant mass dependence; these conclusions are drawn from simulations using the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model with a soft equation of state and a cross section reduced to 80% of the free nucleon-nucleon cross section.

## Method

The author performed IQMD model simulations of symmetric collisions of Ca+Ca, Ni+Ni, Zr+Zr, Sn+Sn, and Xe+Xe series with neutron-to-proton ratios (N/Z) of 1.0, 1.6, and 2.0 at impact parameters b/b_max = 0.2–0.4 and at the energy of vanishing flow (balance energy) for each system. The time evolution of average and maximum density was extracted from the nuclear density distributions, and temperature was derived using a local density approximation based on the hot Thomas-Fermi approach within a central sphere of 2 fm radius. The system size dependence of density and temperature was fitted to a power law A^τ.

## Key numbers

- The power law exponent τ for the maximal value of average density (ρ^avg) is 0.01 ± 0.04 for N/Z = 1.0, -0.04 ± 0.01 for N/Z = 1.6, and -0.07 ± 0.008 for N/Z = 2.0.
- The power law exponent τ for the maximal value of maximum density (ρ^max) is -0.03 ± 0.04 for N/Z = 1.0, -0.08 ± 0.02 for N/Z = 1.6, and -0.10 ± 0.02 for N/Z = 2.0.
- The power law exponent τ for the maximal value of average temperature (<T^avg>) is -0.16 ± 0.06 for N/Z = 1.0, -0.15 ± 0.07 for N/Z = 1.6, and -0.19 ± 0.09 for N/Z = 2.0.
- The power law exponent τ for the maximal value of maximum temperature (<T^max>) is -0.08 ± 0.05 for N/Z = 1.0, -0.16 ± 0.06 for N/Z = 1.6, and -0.15 ± 0.07 for N/Z = 2.0.
- The collision rate reaches its maximum at around 20–40 fm/c and becomes constant at around 80 fm/c for the systems studied.

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear dynamics at the energy of vanishing flow from stable systems, previously investigated by Sood and Puri (2010), to neutron-rich systems with varying N/Z ratios. It also builds on the author's prior work on participant-spectator matter in neutron-rich systems (Gautam, 2011) and on isospin effects in balance energy (Gautam et al., 2010). The results enable future studies of how isospin asymmetry affects the thermodynamic properties of nuclear matter formed in heavy-ion collisions, particularly for systems accessible with radioactive ion beams.
