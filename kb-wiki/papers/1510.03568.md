# Light (anti-)nuclei production and flow in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Using the coalescence model based on the phase-space distributions of protons, neutrons, Lambdas and their antiparticles from a multiphase transport (AMPT) model, we study the production of deuteron, triton, helium 3, hypertriton, hyperhelium 3 and their antinuclei in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV. The resulting transverse momentum spectra, elliptic flows and coalescence parameters for these nuclei are presented and compared with available experimental data. We also show the constituent number scaled elliptic flows of these nuclei and discuss their implications.

## Key claim
Using the coalescence model with phase-space distributions from the AMPT model, the authors find that the default version of AMPT describes proton and deuteron transverse momentum spectra reasonably but overestimates deuteron yield by about a factor of two, while the string melting version overestimates both proton and deuteron spectra due to a known baryon production problem in that model.

## Method
The authors used the coalescence model, projecting Gaussian Wigner functions onto the freeze-out phase-space distributions of protons, neutrons, Lambdas, and their antiparticles obtained from both the default and string melting versions of the AMPT model (v1.25t7-v2.25t7). The width parameters for the Wigner functions were determined from the known root-mean-square radii of the nuclei, assuming harmonic oscillator ground-state wave functions. Transverse momentum spectra, elliptic flows, and coalescence parameters were calculated for deuteron, triton, helium-3, hypertriton, hyperhelium-3, and their antinuclei in Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV with impact parameter \(b=8\) fm, using 40,000 AMPT events with 50-event mixing for flow calculations.

## Key numbers
- For the default AMPT model, total yields per event: 24 nucleon-like, 16 Lambda-like, 0.36 deuteron-like, \(9.2\times 10^{-4}\) triton-like, and \(5.8\times 10^{-4}\) hypertriton-like nuclei.
- For the string melting AMPT model, total yields per event: 37 nucleon-like, 20 Lambda-like, 2.1 deuteron-like, \(2.5\times 10^{-2}\) triton-like, and \(2.7\times 10^{-2}\) hypertriton-like nuclei.
- The coalescence parameter \(B_2\) for deuteron-like nuclei at low \(p_T\) is a few times \(10^{-3}\) GeV\(^2/c^3\); the default AMPT gives a \(B_2\) about a factor of two larger than ALICE data.
- The coalescence parameter \(B_3\) for triton-like nuclei at low \(p_T\) is about \(10^{-6}\) GeV\(^4/c^6\); the default AMPT gives a \(B_3\) about a factor of two smaller than ALICE data.
- The string melting AMPT gives a \(B_2\) almost an order of magnitude larger than the empirical value, but a \(B_3\) that agrees with data.
- Width parameters used: deuteron \(\sigma_\rho = 2.263\) fm (radius 1.96 fm, oscillator frequency 0.2077 sec\(^{-1}\)); triton \(\sigma_\rho = 1.61\) fm (radius 1.61 fm, oscillator frequency 0.4104 sec\(^{-1}\)); helium-3 \(\sigma_\rho = 1.74\) fm (radius 1.74 fm, oscillator frequency 0.3514 sec\(^{-1}\)).

## Context
This work extends the coalescence model study of deuteron production in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC by Oh et al. (2009) to include triton, helium-3, hypertriton, hyperhelium-3, and their antinuclei at LHC energies. It uses the AMPT model, which was previously tuned by Xu et al. (2011) to describe charged particle multiplicity and elliptic flow at LHC. The results are compared with ALICE Collaboration data (Adam et al., 2015) for proton and deuteron spectra and coalescence parameters. The study enables future experimental measurements of light nuclei elliptic flows to distinguish between the coalescence model prediction of positive elliptic flow and the hydrodynamic model prediction of negative elliptic flow for these heavy composite particles.
