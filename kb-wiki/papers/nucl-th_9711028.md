# A clue to the mechanism of $ΛK^+$ production in $pp$-reactions

> We analyse the $p p \to p ΛK^+$ total cross section data which were recently measured by the COSY-11 collaboration at an energy 2~MeV above the reaction threshold. Our analysis suggests that the measurement of the invariant mass spectrum for the $ΛK^+$ at energies around 100 MeV above the threshold can provide a new constraint on the theoretical calculations for this reaction. In particular, the measurement can give a clue as to whether the reaction is dominated by resonance excitation or not. Thus, it should contribute to further understanding of the strangeness production mechanism. We show the invariant mass spectra for the $ΛK^+$ system in these optimal kinematic conditions calculated by several approaches, and demonstrate that our suggestion is experimentally feasible.

# A clue to the mechanism of ΛK⁺ production in pp-reactions

## Key claim
The measurement of the ΛK⁺ invariant mass spectrum at excess energies around 100 MeV above threshold can distinguish between resonance-dominated and non-resonant (kaon-exchange) mechanisms for the pp → pΛK⁺ reaction, whereas total cross section data at only 2 MeV above threshold cannot discriminate between these models.

## Method
The authors analyzed the pp → pΛK⁺ total cross section data from the COSY-11 collaboration at 2 MeV above threshold and compared them with several theoretical models: a resonance model including N*(1650), N*(1710), and N*(1720) resonances with π, η, and ρ-meson exchanges; a kaon-exchange model without resonances (Sibirtsev, Li and Ko); and a phase-space distribution with constant matrix element. They then calculated the ΛK⁺ invariant mass spectra at excess energies of 10 MeV and 100 MeV using the resonance model and compared these to phase-space distributions to identify kinematic conditions where resonance signatures become visible.

## Key numbers
- The COSY-11 measurement was performed at an energy 2 MeV above the reaction threshold (√s₀ = 2.5503 GeV, where √s₀ = m_N + m_Λ + m_K).
- The resonance model underestimated the COSY-11 data at 2 MeV above threshold by about a factor of 2.
- Adopting upper limits for the branching ratios of N*(1650) → πN and N*(1650) → ΛK⁺ increased the calculated cross section by a factor of about 1.8.
- The condition for the N*(1650) resonance to affect the ΛK⁺ invariant mass spectrum is ε ≃ m_N*(1650) + Γ_N*(1650)/2 − (m_Λ + m_K) ≃ 100 MeV.
- The resonance model reproduces existing data at energies more than 100 MeV above threshold fairly well.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of the COSY-11 measurement (Balewski) and compares the resonance model of Tsushima with the kaon-exchange models of Sibirtsev and Li and Ko, as well as the nonrelativistic one-pion-exchange-plus-resonance model of Fäldt and Wilkin. The authors show that total cross sections near threshold cannot distinguish between these approaches because all can fit the data with parameter adjustments. The proposed measurement of the ΛK⁺ invariant mass spectrum at 100 MeV excess energy would provide a decisive test, and the authors note that such an experiment is planned by the COSY-TOF Collaboration.
