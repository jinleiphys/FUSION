# Genetic Algorithm based Inverse Potentials for Resonant States of $α-^{12}C$ Using Variable Phase Approach

> Elastic scattering between $α$-particles and $^{12}\mathrm{C}$ nuclei plays a crucial role in understanding resonance phenomena in light nuclear systems. In this work, we construct inverse potentials for resonant states in $α$-$^{12}\mathrm{C}$ elastic scattering using the variable phase approach, in tandem with a genetic algorithm based optimization technique. The reference function for the potential in the phase equation is chosen as a combination of three smoothly joined Morse-type functions. The parameters of the reference function are genetically evolved to minimize the the mean squared error (MSE) between the numerically obtained scattering phase shifts and the expected values. The resulting inverse potentials accurately reproduce the resonance energies ($E_r$) and the resonance widths ($Γ_r$) for the $\ell^π$ states, $1^-$, $2^+$, $3^-$, and $4^+$, showing excellent agreement with experimental data. This computational approach to constructing inverse potentials serves as a complementary to conventional direct methods for investigating nuclear scattering phenomena.

# Genetic Algorithm based Inverse Potentials for Resonant States of α-¹²C Using Variable Phase Approach

## Key claim
The inverse potentials constructed using three smoothly joined Morse-type functions within the variable phase approach, optimized by a genetic algorithm, accurately reproduce resonance energies and widths for the ℓ^π = 1⁻, 2⁺, 3⁻, and 4⁺ states of α-¹²C scattering, with the Coulomb barrier emerging naturally without imposing a separate Coulomb ansatz. The agreement with experimental data is conditional on careful curation of phase shift data into three energy regions and refinement of the 10-dimensional parameter space over 10,000 iterations.

## Method
The authors model the interaction potential V(r) as a piecewise function of three Morse-type potentials representing short-range nuclear, intermediate nuclear-Coulomb, and long-range Coulomb regions, smoothly joined at two boundary points with continuity conditions on the potential and its first derivative. They solve the first-order Riccati-type phase equation (the variable phase approach) using a fifth-order Runge-Kutta method, with the 10 independent model parameters optimized via a genetic algorithm that minimizes the mean squared error between simulated and experimental phase shifts from Plaga et al. (1987). The optimized potentials are then used to compute partial cross sections, from which resonance energies and widths are extracted by Gaussian fitting.

## Key numbers
- For ℓ^π = 1⁻: resonance energy E_r = 2.398 ± 0.004 MeV (experimental: 2.423 ± 0.011 MeV); resonance width Γ_r = 410 ± 11 keV (experimental: 420 ± 20 keV); potential depth V_d = -124.92 MeV at r_d = 1.06 fm; Coulomb barrier V_CB = 27.59 MeV at r_CB = 1.77 fm; MSE = 1.87.
- For ℓ^π = 2⁺: resonance energy E_r = 4.355 ± 0.003 MeV (experimental: 4.357 ± 0.004 MeV); resonance width Γ_r = 71 ± 6 keV (experimental: 73 ± 5 keV); potential depth V_d = -85.62 MeV at r_d = 0.54 fm; Coulomb barrier V_CB = 40.46 MeV at r_CB = 1.38 fm; MSE = 1.51.
- For ℓ^π = 3⁻: resonance energy E_r = 4.538 ± 0.008 MeV (experimental: 4.440 ± 0.015 MeV); resonance width Γ_r = 882 ± 17 keV (experimental: 800 ± 100 keV); potential depth V_d = -5.22 MeV at r_d = 1.39 fm; Coulomb barrier V_CB = 4.85 MeV at r_CB = 3.84 fm; MSE = 0.75.
- For ℓ^π = 4⁺: resonance energy E_r = 3.1728 ± 0.0001 MeV (experimental: 3.194 ± 0.003 MeV); resonance width Γ_r = 26 ± 0.1 keV (experimental: 27 ± 3 keV); potential depth V_d = -13.75 MeV at r_d = 1.34 fm; Coulomb barrier V_CB = 8.8 MeV at r_CB = 3.26 fm; MSE = 0.89.
- Phase shift data used cover incident α energies from 1.0 to 6.6 MeV (Plaga et al., 1987), with the energy range up to 6.56 MeV partitioned into three regions for each partial wave.

## Context
This work extends the variable phase approach inverse scattering methodology previously applied by the same group to n-p, α-α, α-³He, α-³H, and α-d systems (Awasthi et al., 2022; Kant et al., 2024; Sharma et al., 2025) to the α-¹²C system, which is critical for understanding the ¹²C(α,γ)¹⁶O radiative capture reaction in stellar helium burning. The study builds on the phase shift analysis of Becker et al. (1987) and the high-precision measurements of Tischhauser et al. (2009), using their experimental phase shifts as input. The resulting inverse potentials, which naturally incorporate both nuclear and Coulomb interactions without separate ansatze, enable direct calculation of the Coulomb barrier for low-energy astrophysical S-factor calculations, potentially improving extrapolations to the Gamow peak energy (~0.3 MeV) where direct measurement is hindered by the Coulomb barrier.
