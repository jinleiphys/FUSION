# The electric dipole response of $^{76}$Se above 4 MeV

> The dipole response of $^{76}_{34}$Se in the energy range 4 to 9 MeV has been analyzed using a $(\vecγ,γ')$ polarized photon scattering technique, performed at the High Intensity $γ$-Ray Source facility, to complement previous work performed using unpolarized photons. The results of this work offer both an enhanced sensitivity scan of the dipole response and an unambiguous determination of the parities of the observed J=1 states. The dipole response is found to be dominated by $E1$ excitations, and can reasonably be attributed to a pygmy dipole resonance. Evidence is presented to suggest that a significant amount of directly unobserved excitation strength is present in the region, due to unobserved branching transitions in the decays of resonantly excited states. The dipole response of the region is underestimated when considering only ground state decay branches. We investigate the electric dipole response theoretically, performing calculations in a 3D cartesian-basis time-dependent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock framework.

## Key claim

The dipole response of $^{76}$Se between 4 and 9 MeV is dominated by electric dipole (E1) excitations attributable to a pygmy dipole resonance, but the paper presents evidence that a significant amount of excitation strength is missed when only ground-state decay branches are considered, meaning the total dipole response is underestimated.

## Method

The authors performed a polarized photon scattering experiment using $(\vec\gamma,\gamma')$ at the High Intensity $\gamma$-Ray Source (HI$\gamma$S) facility, with nearly monoenergetic, fully polarized photon beams from 4.2 to 8.8 MeV. Scattered $\gamma$-rays were detected by four HPGe detectors at 90 degrees (two horizontal, two vertical) and one at 135 degrees, allowing unambiguous parity determination of J=1 states via azimuthal count-rate asymmetry. Cross sections for newly observed states were determined relative to known states from previous unpolarized bremsstrahlung data at the DHIPS facility, and theoretical calculations were performed using a 3D cartesian-basis time-dependent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock framework.

## Key numbers

- Mean azimuthal asymmetry for negative parity states: $\epsilon = -0.77(2)$; for positive parity states: $\epsilon = 0.94(6)$
- A parity doublet of close-lying E1 and M1 states observed at 5297.7(3) keV and 5298.4(2) keV, with an energy difference of 0.7(3) keV
- The doublet at 5298 keV was resolved into two states with cross sections of 53(3) eVb (E1) and 13.7(8) eVb (M1), from a total DHIPS cross section of 66.6(42) eVb
- The strongest E1 transition observed: state at 5375.6(4) keV with $\Gamma = 319(35)$ meV and $B(E1)\uparrow = 2.67(29) \times 10^{-3}$ e$^2$fm$^2$
- The strongest M1 transition observed: state at 4218.8(3) keV with $\Gamma = 154(17)$ meV and $B(M1)\uparrow = 259(30) \times 10^{-3}$ $\mu_N^2$
- The semi-empirical formula by Pietralla (1998) predicts the Scissors Mode at approximately 3.9 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous unpolarized photon scattering measurements on $^{76}$Se by Cooper (2010) at the DHIPS facility, which could not distinguish parities of observed states. The unambiguous parity determination is critical because Genilloud (2013) discussed evidence of a Giant M1 resonance in the same energy region as the expected pygmy dipole resonance. The results provide experimental data to test theoretical models such as time-dependent Hartree-Fock and random phase approximation, which are also used to calculate matrix elements for $0\nu2\beta$ decay in $^{76}$Ge, where $^{76}$Se is the daughter nucleus.
