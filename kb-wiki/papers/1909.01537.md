# Nuclear structure and reaction with quantum shape fluctuation

> We present recent results in theoretical studies on nuclear structure and reaction beyond mean field, using the adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate method and its extension. We also present new results with the finite-temperature Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculation with the three-dimensional-coordinate-space representation.

## Key claim

The paper presents theoretical methods for nuclear structure and reaction beyond mean field, but the results are limited to model systems (BKN interaction with an added derivative term and a two-level pairing model) rather than realistic heavy nuclei, and the full ASCC solutions require large numbers of iterations to determine the collective subspace.

## Method

The authors use the adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate (ASCC) method to define a collective subspace from time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (TDHFB) trajectories, solving equations at zeroth, first, and second order in momenta to obtain collective potentials, inertial masses, and generators. For requantization, they employ either a collective Hamiltonian approach with Pauli's prescription or a stationary phase approximation (SPA) of the path integral, where periodic trajectories satisfying the Einstein-Brillouin-Keller quantization rule are used to superpose Slater determinants. They also present a finite-temperature Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculation on a three-dimensional coordinate space using Green's function techniques.

## Key numbers

- For the two-level pairing model with particle number N=100, level degeneracy 2Ω with Ω=50, and pair strength g=Δε/Ω=0.2, the ASCC+SPA method produces wave functions that agree almost perfectly with the exact solution for the pairing vibrational and rotational modes.
- For ¹⁴⁶Ba at T=0 calculated with SLy4 and volume pairing strength g=-250 MeV fm³ with cut-off E_c=100 MeV, the neutron average pairing gap is Δ_n=0.77 MeV, and the density distribution shows parity-violating octupole deformation.
- For the α+α scattering system with the BKN interaction plus a B₃ derivative term, the ASCC inertial mass converges to the correct reduced mass in the asymptotic limit (R→∞) regardless of the B₃ value, while the cranking formula fails for B₃≠0.
- For ¹⁰⁶Pd in the quadrupole deformation plane (β,γ) calculated with the SkM* Skyrme interaction, the time-odd mean fields enhance the rotational moments of inertia by 30-40% compared to the cranking (Inglis-Belyaev) formula.

## Context

This work extends the ASCC method previously developed by Nakatsukasa et al. (2016) and applies it to fusion reactions of light nuclei using the BKN interaction, following earlier studies by Wen et al. (2016, 2017, 2018) on optimal reaction paths and astrophysical S-factors. The method addresses difficulties in the conventional generator coordinate method (GCM) such as overcompleteness of basis states, numerical instability from near-zero norm eigenvalues, and the need for complex generator coordinates to reproduce correct masses. The finite-temperature HFB code in three-dimensional coordinate space extends the Green's function approach of Jin et al. (2017) and enables future studies of superfluid neutron dynamics in neutron-star crusts, building on work by Kashiwaba and Nakatsukasa (2019).
