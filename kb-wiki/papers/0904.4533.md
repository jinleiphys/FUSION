# Chiral phase transition in relativistic heavy-ion collisions with weak magnetic fields: ring diagrams in the linear sigma model

> Working in the linear sigma model with quarks, we compute the finite-temperature effective potential in the presence of a weak magnetic field, including the contribution of the pion ring diagrams and considering the sigma as a classical field. In the approximation where the pion self-energy is computed perturbatively, we show that there is a region of the parameter space where the effect of the ring diagrams is to preclude the phase transition from happening. Inclusion of the magnetic field has small effects that however become more important as the system evolves to the lowest temperatures allowed in the analysis.

## Key claim

The paper shows that, within the linear sigma model with quarks and using a perturbative approximation for the pion self-energy, there exists a region of parameter space where the inclusion of ring diagrams prevents the chiral phase transition from occurring altogether, even in the absence of a magnetic field. The weak magnetic field has only small effects (5-10% differences) that become slightly more important at the lowest temperatures considered.

## Method

The authors compute the finite-temperature effective potential in the linear sigma model with quarks, treating the sigma field as classical and including the contribution of pion ring diagrams via resummation. They work in the hierarchy of energy scales where the magnetic field is the smallest scale (eB << m^2 << T^2), using the Schwinger proper time method to expand charged particle propagators in the weak field limit. Because the coupling constants are too large for perturbative calculations to be strictly reliable, they introduce a free parameter c (0 < c < 1) multiplying the perturbatively computed pion self-energies to explore the parameter space qualitatively.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature without ring diagrams: T_c = 147.5 MeV, computed using standard parameters lambda = 20, mu = 380 MeV, g = 3.3
- Maximum temperature for stability: T_max = 7,196.5 MeV, confirming T_c < T_max
- Critical value of the parameter c below which the phase transition is precluded: c_c ≈ 0.3
- Magnetic field intensity used for comparison: eB = 0.9 × (m_pi^vac)^2, where m_pi^vac is the vacuum pion mass
- Relative difference between effective potentials with and without magnetic field: 5% to 10% for the chosen temperatures

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Fraga (2008) on the chiral phase transition in strong magnetic fields (where eB >> T^2) to the more realistic heavy-ion collision scenario of weak magnetic fields (eB << T^2), where the field decays to eB ≲ 0.1 (m_pi^vac)^2 by the time the quark-gluon plasma equilibrates. The study incorporates ring diagram contributions that were neglected in prior work, following the resummation techniques developed by Carrington (1992) and Petropoulos (2000) for high-temperature field theory. The results enable future non-perturbative calculations of the pion self-energy in the presence of a heat bath and magnetic field, which would be needed to extract more reliable quantitative conclusions.
