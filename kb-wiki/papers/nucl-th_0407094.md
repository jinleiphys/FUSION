# Final state interaction effects in neutrino-nucleus quasielastic scattering

> We consider the charged-current quasielastic scattering of muon neutrinos on an Oxygen 16 target, described within a relativistic shell model and, for comparison, the relativistic Fermi gas. Final state interactions are described in the distorted wave impulse approximation, using both a relativistic mean field potential and a relativistic optical potential, with and without imaginary part. We present results for inclusive cross sections at fixed neutrino energies in the range $E_ν=$ 200 MeV - 1 GeV, showing that final state interaction effects can remain sizable even at large energies.

## Key claim

Final state interaction (FSI) effects in charged-current quasielastic neutrino-nucleus scattering on Oxygen-16 remain sizable even at 1 GeV neutrino energy, producing a reduction of about 15% in the integrated cross section when described with the more realistic real relativistic optical potential (ROP) approach, contrary to the expectation that such effects become negligible at high energies.

## Method

The authors computed inclusive charged-current quasielastic cross sections for muon neutrinos on an Oxygen-16 target using both a relativistic shell model (RSM) and a relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) model within the impulse approximation. Final state interactions were incorporated via the distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) using three different descriptions of the outgoing nucleon: a relativistic mean field (RMF) potential, a real relativistic optical potential (real ROP), and a complex ROP including an imaginary absorptive part.

## Key numbers

- At Eν = 200 MeV, FSI effects produce a reduction of about 30–40% in the differential cross section for the RMF and real ROP approaches, and about 60% for the complex ROP approach.
- At Eν = 500 MeV and 1 GeV, the reduction from RMF and real ROP is about 20%, while the complex ROP gives about 50% reduction.
- For the integrated cross section at Eν = 1 GeV, the more reliable RMF and real ROP approaches show a reduction of about 15% relative to the plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA).
- The RFG model used a Fermi momentum pF = 225 MeV and binding energy eB = 0 or 20 MeV.
- The axial form factor used a dipole parameterization with cutoff mass MA = 1.026 GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of FSI effects in neutrino-nucleus scattering by Co and Meucci (various years) and builds on relativistic descriptions of (e,e'p) reactions developed by the same authors (2003). The results show that while nuclear model dependence (RSM vs. RFG) becomes negligible at 1 GeV when FSI are omitted, FSI effects remain appreciable and must be included in Monte Carlo codes for neutrino detector simulations, which currently rely on the Fermi gas model. The paper also notes that for neutral current quasielastic processes where the outgoing nucleon must be detected, the complex ROP description would be more appropriate, implying even larger FSI effects at high energy.
