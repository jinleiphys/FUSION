# Reply Comment on "Meson Masses in Nuclear Matter"

> We reply to the Comment by Hatsuda and Lee on our recent paper "Meson Masses in Nuclear Matter" (Phys.Rev.Lett. 78, 1010, (1997)). We argue that, contrary to the claim in the Comment, the short distance operator product expansion is not applicable for calculation of meson mass shifts in nuclear matter, since they are determined by long distance physics. We also remark that the suggestion made in the Comment that we overlooked in our paper the mean field description of the mass shifts of low-q mesons is perhaps due to a misunderstanding.

# Reply Comment on "Meson Masses in Nuclear Matter"

## Key claim

The operator product expansion (OPE) and QCD sum rule method are not applicable for calculating meson mass shifts in nuclear matter because these shifts are determined by long-distance physics (distances ≥ 1 fm), not short distances. The authors argue that even if short-distance methods were applicable, the resulting mass shift would still disagree with the sum rule results of Hatsuda and Lee.

## Method

The authors derive meson mass shifts Δm in nuclear matter from the relation Δm(E) = −2π(ρ/m) Re f(E), where ρ is nuclear density and Re f is the real part of the meson-nucleon forward scattering amplitude. They show that for ρ-mesons, |Re f| > 1 fm, confirming the long-distance nature of the problem. The authors also argue that the light-cone expansion and short-distance expansion are not independent options, and that the short-distance expansion is inapplicable because longitudinal distances along the light cone are of order l ∼ 0.5 fm for a ρ-meson at rest.

## Key numbers

- For ρ-mesons, |Re f| > 1 fm, indicating long-distance physics.
- For π-mesons, |Re f| > 1 fm at E > 170 MeV.
- The QCD scale is Λ ∼ 0.5 fm, defining the boundary between short and long distances.
- For ρ-mesons at normal nuclear density, the kinetic energy must satisfy E_ρ^{kin} > 10 MeV for the basic relation to be valid.
- The authors considered ρ-meson mass shifts at high energies, E_ρ^{kin} ≥ 1.2 GeV, due to limitations of the vector dominance model.
- If short-distance methods were applicable, the resulting mass shift would be |Δm_ρ| ≤ 10 MeV.
- For a ρ-meson at rest, the longitudinal distance along the light cone is l ∼ 2/m_ρ ≈ 0.5 fm.

## Context

This reply addresses the Comment by Hatsuda and Lee on the authors' earlier paper "Meson Masses in Nuclear Matter" (Phys.Rev.Lett. 78, 1010, 1997). The authors argue that the mean-field description of low-momentum mesons was not overlooked, as their basic formula is a direct consequence of mean-field theory. The work clarifies the limitations of applying OPE and QCD sum rules to nuclear matter problems, and notes that at very low meson momenta (|q|⁻¹ ≫ d, the internucleon distance), the mean-field approximation breaks down due to interactions with multiple nucleons and screening effects not accounted for in short-distance expansions.
