# Phases of QCD: Summary of the Rutgers Long Range Plan Town Meeting

> This White Paper summarizes the outcome of the Town Meeting on Phases of QCD that took place January 12-14, 2007 at Rutgers University, as part of the NSAC 2007 Long Range Planning process.

## Key claim

The community’s central goal is a dramatic advance in understanding QCD Matter through quantitative comparison of theory and experiment, but the abstract’s framing of “discovery” is hedged: the matter produced at RHIC is “far from weakly coupled” and a “strong first-order phase transition can be excluded,” yet the claim of a “near-perfect liquid” is qualified by the recognition that any real fluid must have nonvanishing viscosity, and the matter is only “not far from” the conjectured lower bound on η/s.

## Method

The paper summarizes the outcome of a community town meeting held January 12-14, 2007 at Rutgers University as part of the NSAC 2007 Long Range Planning process. It synthesizes experimental results from the first six RHIC runs (2000-2006) by the BRAHMS, PHENIX, PHOBOS, and STAR collaborations, covering Au+Au, d+Au, Cu+Cu, and p+p collisions at four collision energies (√sNN = 19.6, 63, 130, and 200 GeV). The document also reviews theoretical advances including lattice QCD at finite temperature and density, spectral function calculations, AdS/CFT correspondence results, and cold dense quark matter theory, and presents prioritized recommendations for future facilities and detector upgrades.

## Key numbers

* Initial energy density at thermalization time must be at least 15 GeV/fm³, which is 15 times the energy density needed for color deconfinement.
* Chemical freeze-out temperature Tch = 160-170 MeV, independent of collision system and centrality.
* Lattice QCD transition temperature Tc = 192(7)(4) MeV (one recent calculation).
* High transverse momentum hadrons in central Au+Au collisions are suppressed by a factor of up to five relative to the proton-proton baseline.
* Jet quenching parameter q̂ values extracted from RHIC data are more than 10 times larger than the stopping power of normal nuclear matter.
* The conjectured lower bound on shear viscosity to entropy density ratio is η/s ≥ 1/4π.
* Charmonium ground state (J/ψ) can survive up to temperatures at least as high as 1.6 Tc according to lattice spectral function calculations.
* Thermalization time scale is less than 1 fm/c.

## Context

This white paper extends the experimental and theoretical program initiated by the first five years of RHIC operations (2000-2005), which discovered a strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma exhibiting near-perfect liquid behavior, jet quenching, novel hadronization via parton recombination, and indications of gluon saturation. It builds on lattice QCD calculations of the equation of state and transition temperature (e.g., calculations using improved staggered fermion actions), and on AdS/CFT correspondence results that provide strong-coupling benchmarks for transport coefficients. The document enables future planning for the RHIC II luminosity upgrade, U.S. participation in the LHC heavy ion program, and the development of an Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) as the highest priority for new construction after JLab 12 GeV and RHIC II upgrades.
