# The Calculation of Vacuum Properties from the Global Color Symmetry Model

> A modified method for calculating the non-perturbative quark vacuum condensates from the global color symmetry model is derived. Within this approach it is shown that the vacuum condensates are free of ultraviolet divergence which is different from previous studies. As a special, the two-quark condensate and the mixed quark-gluon condensate are calculated. A comparision with the results of the other nonperturbative QCD approaches is given.

# The Calculation of Vacuum Properties from the Global Color Symmetry Model

## Key claim

The authors derive a modified method for calculating non-perturbative quark vacuum condensates within the Global Color Symmetry Model (GCM) that, unlike previous GCM studies, yields condensates free of ultraviolet divergence by subtracting perturbative contributions consistently with the QCD sum rule definition of vacuum condensates. The method requires taking the upper limit of integration to infinity rather than imposing a hard cutoff at 1 GeV² as in prior work by Meissner (1997).

## Method

The authors work at the mean-field (saddle-point) level of the GCM generating functional in the chiral limit, solving the rainbow Dyson-Schwinger equations for the quark self-energy functions A(p²) and B(p²) using two different parameterized model gluon propagators. They define the vacuum condensate as the difference between the "exact" (Nambu-Goldstone phase) and "perturbative" (Wigner phase) quark propagators, employing the Wick theorem to subtract perturbative contributions consistently with QCD sum rule definitions. The two-quark condensate and mixed quark-gluon condensate are then computed from the subtracted propagators using momentum-space integrals taken to infinite cutoff.

- The two-quark condensate value at cutoff μ = 1 GeV² is 170 MeV (model 1) and 177 MeV (model 2), rising to 212 MeV and 264 MeV respectively at μ = 500 GeV², demonstrating sensitivity to the integration limit

## Context

This work extends the GCM framework of Cahill and Roberts (1985) and corrects the earlier condensate calculations by Meissner (1997), which used a hard cutoff at 1 GeV² and a definition inconsistent with QCD sum rule conventions. The authors show that the previous approach underestimated the two-quark condensate and produced ultraviolet-divergent four-quark condensates. The results are compatible with the standard QCD sum rule values from Shifman, Vainshtein and Zakharov (1979) and provide a consistency check for the GCM, enabling future extension to nonzero temperature and density studies. The method parallels the instanton liquid model approach of Polyakov and Weiss (1996) and Dorokhov, Esaibegian and Mikhailov (1997), where the integration cutoff is taken to infinity.
