# Heavy Quarks in Turbulent QCD Plasmas

> The quark-gluon plasma, which is produced at an early stage of ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, is expected to be initially strongly populated with chromodynamic fields. We address the question how heavy quarks interact with such a turbulent plasma in comparison with an equilibrated one of the same energy density. For this purpose we derive a Fokker-Planck transport equation of heavy quarks embedded in a plasma of light quarks and gluons. We first discuss the equilibrium plasma and then the turbulent one applying the same approach, where the heavy quarks interact not with the plasma constituents but rather with the long wavelength classical fields. We first consider the three schematic models of isotropic trubulent plasma and then the simplified model of glasma with the chromodynamic fields only along the beam direction. The momentum broadening and collisional energy loss of a test heavy quark are computed and compared to those of equilibrium plasma of the same energy density.

## Key claim

Heavy quarks interact significantly more strongly with a turbulent (non-equilibrium, field-dominated) QCD plasma than with an equilibrium plasma of the same energy density; the momentum broadening parameter \(\hat{q}\) is argued to be of order \(g\) in a turbulent plasma rather than \(g^4\) as in equilibrium, though the paper computes this via schematic models rather than from first-principles QCD.

## Method

The author derives a Fokker-Planck transport equation for heavy quarks using the quasi-linear theory of plasmas, where the distribution function is split into a regular (color-neutral) part and a fluctuating part, and the heavy quarks interact with long-wavelength classical chromodynamic fields rather than with individual plasma constituents. The Fokker-Planck coefficients (momentum diffusion tensor \(X^{ij}\) and drift vector \(Y^i\)) are computed from the fluctuation spectra of chromoelectric and chromomagnetic fields, first for an equilibrium plasma using known dielectric functions, then for three schematic models of isotropic turbulent plasma and for a simplified glasma model with fields only along the beam direction.

## Key numbers

- For equilibrium plasma at \(T = 0.5\ \text{GeV}\) and \(\alpha_s = 0.1\), the coefficients \(X_L(v)\) and \(X_T(v)\) are shown numerically as functions of velocity \(v\) (Fig. 1) and temperature (Fig. 2); specific numerical values are given in the figures but not as isolated numbers in the text.
- In the small-velocity limit for equilibrium plasma, the inverse diffusion constant is \(1/D = \frac{g^2 C_F}{12\pi} \frac{m_D^2}{T} \log(T/m_D)\) (Eq. 4.23).
- For the turbulent plasma, the momentum broadening parameter \(\hat{q}\) is argued to be of order \(g\) (not \(g^4\) or \(g^2\)), but no explicit numerical result for \(\hat{q}\) in the turbulent case is given in the truncated text.

## Context

This work extends the Fokker-Planck approach to heavy quarks (previously applied to equilibrium plasmas by Svetitsky (1987) and Moore & Teaney (2004)) into the non-equilibrium, field-dominated regime relevant to the early stage of heavy-ion collisions. It contrasts with kinetic-theory treatments (e.g., Das et al. (2017)) by considering the phase where the medium is described by classical fields rather than quasi-particles. The results enable future studies of heavy-quark transport in the glasma phase and may help explain why heavy quarks in heavy-ion collisions behave similarly to light hadrons, contrary to early expectations.
