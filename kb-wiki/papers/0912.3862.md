# Stopping distance for high energy jets in weakly-coupled quark-gluon plasmas

> We derive a simple formula for the stopping distance for a high-energy quark traveling through a weakly-coupled quark gluon plasma. The result is given to next-to-leading-order in an expansion in inverse logarithms ln(E/T), where T is the temperature of the plasma. We also define a stopping distance for gluons and give a leading-log result. Discussion of stopping distance has a theoretical advantage over discussion of energy loss rates in that stopping distances can be generalized to the case of strong coupling, where one may not speak of individual partons.

## Key claim

The paper derives a simple formula for the stopping distance of a high-energy quark in a weakly-coupled quark-gluon plasma, valid to next-to-leading-logarithmic order in an expansion in inverse logarithms \(\ln(E/T)\), under the assumption that \(\alpha_s \ln(E/T) \ll 1\) so that running of the coupling can be ignored.

## Method

The authors solve self-consistent integral equations for the stopping distance based on the leading-log scaling of the hard bremsstrahlung rate \(\Gamma(E) \sim E^{-1/2}\), using the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect to determine the energy dependence. For the quark number stopping distance, they compute to next-to-leading-log order by solving an equation for the logarithm \(L\) self-consistently; for gluon energy stopping distances, they solve coupled equations for quark and gluon energy stopping distances at leading-log order. The analysis uses the UV-regulated transport coefficient \(\hat q(\Lambda)\) and DGLAP splitting functions to evaluate the relevant integrals numerically.

## Key numbers

- For QCD with \(N_f = 2\) and \(Q_\perp \gg T\) (high-energy regime), the quark number stopping distance coefficient \(a = 2.06480\), with \(b = 0.092141\) and \(c = 0.432769\) in the formula \(\ell_{\rm stop,q} \simeq \frac{1}{a \alpha_s^2 T} \sqrt{\frac{E}{T L}}\) where \(L = \ln\left(\frac{b E L}{\alpha_s^c T}\right)\).
- For QCD with \(N_f = 2\) and \(Q_\perp \ll T\) (lower-energy regime), the quark number stopping distance coefficient \(a = 2.27727\), with \(b = 0.533221\) and \(c = 0\).
- For QCD with \(N_f = 2\) and \(Q_\perp \gg T\), the leading-log gluon energy stopping distance coefficient \(a^{\rm(e)}_{\rm g} = 5.98760\) and quark energy stopping distance coefficient \(a^{\rm(e)}_{\rm q} = 3.61355\).
- For QCD with \(N_f = 2\) and \(Q_\perp \ll T\), the leading-log gluon energy stopping distance coefficient \(a^{\rm(e)}_{\rm g} = 6.60372\) and quark energy stopping distance coefficient \(a^{\rm(e)}_{\rm q} = 3.98538\).

## Context

This work extends the perturbative calculation of jet energy loss in weakly-coupled quark-gluon plasmas, building on the LPM effect formalism of Baier-Dokshitzer-Mueller-Peigne-Schiff (BDMPS) and Zakharov. The stopping distance approach, originally defined by Chesler, Jensen, Karch, and Yaffe (2009) for strongly-coupled theories using AdS/CFT duality, is here adapted to weak coupling, enabling comparison between the weakly-coupled \(E^{1/2}/\sqrt{\ln E}\) scaling and the strongly-coupled \(E^{1/3}\) scaling found by Chesler et al., Gubser, and Hatta. The results provide a benchmark for future calculations in supersymmetric gauge theories and for incorporating running coupling effects.
