# Shear viscosity in weakly coupled ${\cal N} {=} 4$ Super Yang-Mills theory compared to QCD

> We compute the shear viscosity of weakly coupled N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills (SYM) theory. Our result for [eta/s], the viscosity to entropy-density ratio, is many times smaller than the corresponding weak-coupling result in QCD. This suggests that [eta/s] of QCD near the transition point is several times larger than the viscosity bound, [eta/s] >= 1/4 pi.

# Shear viscosity in weakly coupled ${\cal N} {=} 4$ Super Yang-Mills theory compared to QCD

## Key claim

The weak-coupling calculation of $\eta/s$ in ${\cal N}=4$ SYM theory is dramatically smaller (about 1/7) than the corresponding weak-coupling result in QCD at the same coupling, which suggests that QCD near the transition point will **not** approach the viscosity bound $\eta/s \geq 1/4\pi$, contrary to expectations from strong-coupling SYM results.

## Method

The authors apply the kinetic theory (Boltzmann equation) variational method of Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2000) to compute the shear viscosity of weakly coupled ${\cal N}=4$ SYM theory. They evaluate the collision operator including both elastic $2\leftrightarrow 2$ processes and inelastic effective $1\leftrightarrow 2$ splitting processes, using numerical quadratures to integrate matrix elements over external momenta and solving the integral equation for the splitting kernel. The result is expressed as $\eta/s$ as a function of the 't Hooft coupling $\lambda \equiv g^2 N_c$, and compared to the corresponding QCD result from the same method.

## Key numbers

- $\eta/s$ for weakly coupled ${\cal N}=4$ SYM at next-to-leading log order: $\eta_{\rm SYM}/s_{\rm SYM} \simeq 6.174 / [\lambda^2 \ln(2.36/\sqrt{\lambda})]$
- For massless QCD with $n_f=3$: $\eta/s \approx 46.1 / [N_c^2 g^4 \ln(4.17/g\sqrt{N_c})]$
- For massless QCD with $n_f=0$: $\eta/s \approx 34.8 / [N_c^2 g^4 \ln(4.67/g\sqrt{N_c})]$
- Strong-coupling asymptotic for SYM: $\eta/s = 1/(4\pi) \left(1 + 135\,\zeta(3)/[8 (2\lambda)^{3/2}] + \cdots\right)$
- Debye mass in SYM: $m_D^2 = 2\lambda T^2$; in $N_c=3$, $n_f=3$ QCD: $m_D^2 = \frac12 \lambda T^2$
- Degree-of-freedom count: SYM has 128 total degrees of freedom (64 fermionic, 64 bosonic); $n_f=3$ QCD has 52 total (36 fermionic, 16 bosonic)
- Weak-coupling SYM $\eta/s$ is about 1/7 that of QCD at the same coupling

## Context

This work extends the kinetic theory approach of Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2000) for QCD shear viscosity to ${\cal N}=4$ SYM theory, enabling a direct weak-coupling comparison between the two theories. The strong-coupling result $\eta/s = 1/4\pi$ for SYM was computed by Policastro, Son, and Starinets (2001), and Kovtun, Son, and Starinets (2005) conjectured this as a universal lower bound. The present paper challenges the inference that QCD near the transition saturates this bound, showing that at weak coupling SYM has much lower $\eta/s$ than QCD due to its larger number of degrees of freedom and adjoint-representation matter fields. The results enable future work to assess whether QCD at RHIC temperatures falls in a regime where $\eta/s$ is still many times larger than the bound, with the authors noting that at $\alpha_s=0.5$ ($\lambda=4.7$) SYM thermodynamics is already near strong-coupling behavior but $\eta/s$ remains nearly 10 times larger than $1/4\pi$.
