# Fragmentation of the fireball and how to observe it

> We argue that fragmentation at hadronisation is likely scenario in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions. In case of crossover phase transition it is driven by a singularity of the bulk viscosity. We claim that such a scenario can explain the ``HBT puzzle'' and can be identified by non-statistical differences between event-wise rapidity distributions and by proton-proton rapidity correlations.

# Fragmentation of the fireball and how to observe it

## Key claim

The authors argue that fragmentation at hadronisation is a likely scenario in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions, but only if the crossover phase transition is rapid enough that the singular behaviour of bulk viscosity near T_c can drive the fireball to break up. The scenario is presented as a possible explanation for the "HBT puzzle", but the authors hedge that this is a proposed mechanism rather than a confirmed observation.

## Method

The authors develop a Monte Carlo event generator (similar in spirit to THERMINATOR) that emits hadrons from fragments (droplets) rather than from a continuous freeze-out hypersurface. They simulate events with varying fragment sizes and average fragment volumes (e.g., 10 fm³), then apply a Kolmogorov-Smirnov test to compare event-wise rapidity distributions and compute proton-proton rapidity correlation functions. No resonance decays were included in the simulations.

## Key numbers

- Fragment size estimate from energy considerations for Bjorken one-dimensional expansion: L² = 24 ζ_c τ_c / ε_c, where τ_c and ε_c are proper time and energy density at T_c, and ζ_c parametrizes the singular bulk viscosity as ζ(τ) = ζ_c δ(τ − τ_c).
- Kolmogorov-Smirnov test performed on a sample of 10⁵ pairs out of 10⁵ events in which 20% of hadrons are emitted from fragments with average volume 10 fm³.
- Proton-proton correlation functions in rapidity shown for varying average fragment sizes, with total multiplicity kept constant across simulations.

## Context

This work extends earlier suggestions that fragmentation can occur even in a crossover transition (oil2honey) and that bulk viscosity shows singular behaviour near T_c (Kharzeev 2007, Karsch 2007). It builds on prior proposals that droplet formation could affect HBT radii (Wong, oil2honey) and that clustering would appear in rapidity correlations (Pratt 1994, Randrup). The paper aims to provide observable signatures, non-statistical event-wise rapidity differences and proton-proton rapidity correlations, that could distinguish fragmentation from standard hydrodynamic freeze-out at RHIC and LHC.
