# Statistical Behavior of Lepton Pair Spectrum in Drell-Yan Process and Signal from Quark-Gluon Plasma in High Energy Collisions

> We analyze the transverse momentum ($p_{T}$) spectra of lepton pairs ($\ell\bar \ell$) generated in the Drell-Yan process, as detected in proton-nucleus (pion-nucleus) and proton-(anti)proton collisions by ten collaborations over a center-of-mass energy ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ or $\sqrt{s}$ if in a simplified form) range from $\sim20$ GeV to above 10 TeV. Three types of probability density functions (the convolution of two Lévy-Tsallis functions, the two-component Erlang distribution, and the convolution of two Hagedorn functions) are utilized to fit and analyze the $p_{T}$ spectra. The fit results are approximately in agreement with the collected experimental data. Consecutively, we obtained the variation law of related parameters as a function of $\sqrt{s}$ and invariant mass ($Q$). In the fit procedure, a given Lévy-Tsallis (or Hagedorn) function can be regarded as the probability density function of transverse momenta contributed by a single quark ($q$) or anti-quark ($\bar q$). The Drell-Yan process is then described by the statistical method.

## Key claim

The transverse momentum spectra of lepton pairs from the Drell-Yan process can be approximately described by three statistical functions (convolution of two Lévy-Tsallis functions, two-component Erlang distribution, and convolution of two Hagedorn functions), with the two-component Erlang distribution generally providing better fits; the extracted parameters show systematic variations with center-of-mass energy and invariant mass, and the non-monotonous behavior of the Lévy-Tsallis parameter \(n\) suggests a softest point near \(\sqrt{s}\approx 40\) GeV that may indicate the onset of a phase transition from hadron matter to quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The authors fitted the transverse momentum (\(p_T\)) spectra of lepton pairs from Drell-Yan process measured by ten collaborations (E288, E605, FNAL-615, R209, PHENIX, STAR, CDF, D0, ATLAS, CMS, LHCb) in proton-nucleus, pion-nucleus, proton-proton, and proton-antiproton collisions over a center-of-mass energy range from 19.4 GeV to 13 TeV. Three probability density functions were used: the convolution of two Lévy-Tsallis functions, the two-component Erlang distribution (with soft and hard components), and the convolution of two Hagedorn functions; each function was interpreted as describing the transverse momentum contributions from a single quark and anti-quark, with the convolution representing the Drell-Yan annihilation process. The goodness of fit was evaluated using minimum-\(\chi^2\), and the extracted parameters were studied as functions of \(\sqrt{s}\) and invariant mass \(Q\).

## Key numbers

- The E288 Collaboration data for \(p\)-Cu collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=19.4\) GeV with \(4\leq Q<9\) GeV/\(c^2\) yielded Lévy-Tsallis parameters \(n=3.910\pm 0.089\) and \(T=0.161\pm 0.005\) GeV for the \(Q=4-5\) GeV/\(c^2\) bin.
- The two-component Erlang distribution fit to the same E288 data gave \(\langle p_t\rangle_S = 0.294\pm 0.003\) GeV/\(c\), \(\langle p_t\rangle_H = 0.611\pm 0.006\) GeV/\(c\), and \(k=0.961\pm 0.003\) for the \(Q=4-5\) GeV/\(c^2\) bin.
- A knee point for the Lévy-Tsallis parameter \(n\) was observed at \(\sqrt{s}\approx 40-50\) GeV, and a boundary for \(T\) at \(\sqrt{s}\approx 200-500\) GeV above which \(T\) increases significantly.
- For the invariant mass dependence, a knee point for \(n\) occurred at \(Q\approx 14-15\) GeV/\(c^2\), and a boundary for \(T\) at \(Q\approx 20-60\) GeV/\(c^2\).
- The softest point of the equation of state from the excitation function of the entropy index \(q\) (where \(n=1/(q-1)\)) was identified at \(\sqrt{s}\approx 39\) GeV with an uncertainty of 1-2 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the statistical description of Drell-Yan lepton-pair production using phenomenological functions (Lévy-Tsallis, Erlang, Hagedorn) as an alternative to the full Collins-Soper-Sterman (CSS) formalism and perturbative QCD factorization approaches (Collins 2011). The study enables a systematic comparison of parameter trends across a wide energy range (20 GeV to 10 TeV) and suggests that the non-monotonous behavior of the Lévy-Tsallis parameter \(n\) may signal the influence of quark-gluon plasma formation, consistent with the authors' prior work (Zhang and Liu 2020) on the onset energy of the phase transition from hadron matter to QGP at 39 GeV. The results provide a statistical baseline for separating Drell-Yan contributions from QGP signals in future heavy-ion collision analyses.
