# Effects of Ground-State Correlations on High Energy Scattering off Nuclei: the Case of the Total Neutron-Nucleus Cross Section

> With the aim at quantitatively investigating the longstanding problem concerning the effect of short range nucleon-nucleon correlations on scattering processes at high energies, the total neutron-nucleus cross section is calculated within a parameter-free approach which, for the first time, takes into account, simultaneously, central, spin, isospin and tensor nucleon-nucleon (NN) correlations, and Glauber elastic and Gribov inelastic shadowing corrections. Nuclei ranging from 4He to 208Pb and incident neutron momenta in the range 3 GeV/c - 300 GeV/c are considered; the commonly used approach which approximates the square of the nuclear wave function by a product of one-body densities is carefully analyzed, showing that NN correlations can play a non-negligible role in high energy scattering off nuclei.

## Key claim

Short-range nucleon-nucleon (NN) correlations produce a non-negligible effect on the total neutron-nucleus cross section at high energies, but this effect is small in absolute value (2% to 5-6% depending on the nucleus) and must be considered together with Gribov inelastic shadowing corrections to achieve agreement with experimental data; the commonly used one-body-density approximation, which neglects all NN correlations, is insufficient when realistic nuclear radii are used.

## Method

The total neutron-nucleus cross section was calculated using a parameter-free approach that simultaneously includes central, spin, isospin, and tensor NN correlations in the nuclear ground-state wave function, together with Glauber elastic and Gribov inelastic shadowing corrections. The fully-correlated wave function was generated by applying a correlation operator based on the Argonne V8' interaction to a mean-field wave function, and the two-body density was used to compute the Glauber profile function including the two-body contraction term. Calculations were performed for nuclei from 4He to 208Pb at incident neutron momenta from 3 GeV/c to 300 GeV/c.

## Key numbers

- The effect of NN correlations increases the total cross section by about 2% in 208Pb and by about 5-6% in 3He (the text mentions 3He, though the abstract lists 4He as the lightest nucleus considered).
- The inclusion of inelastic shadowing corrections brings theoretical calculations back into good agreement with experimental data after correlations are included.
- Preliminary results show that SRC reduce the quasi-elastic cross section σ_qel^{pA} by up to 15% in 12C and 208Pb.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of the total neutron-nucleus cross section that relied on the one-body-density approximation, which neglects all NN correlations, as used in the Glauber (1959) and Foldy (1961) formalisms. It directly addresses the need for a realistic treatment of short-range correlations previously stressed by Glauber (1959), Jenkovszky (2001), and Nikolaev (various), and contrasts with the approach of Murthy (1975) which used nuclear radii too large by about 15%. The results enable systematic investigation of SRC effects on other high-energy scattering processes such as electro-production of hadrons, large rapidity gap processes, and heavy-ion collisions, and motivate an improved treatment of Gribov inelastic shadowing beyond lowest-order intermediate diffractive excitations.
