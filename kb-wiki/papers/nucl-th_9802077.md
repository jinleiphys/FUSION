# The first excited states of 9Be and 9B

> It is found here that the 1/2+ first excited state of 9Be is a virtual state with the energy of -23.5 KeV. The line shape for the excitation of the state is approximated with a simple analytic form based on the effective range expansion. The partner in 9B of this state is found to be a resonance with a maximum in the peak at about 1.1 MeV, FWHM of 1.5 MeV, and complex energy of 0.6-i0.75 MeV. The line shape for its excitation is calculated in terms of the p-8Be phase shift. The phase shifts are obtained from N-8Be effective potentials deduced from the data on on the photodisintegration of 9Be. A possibility for direct extraction of the energy of the resonant state from experimental data is also discussed, and an expression for a residue at a virtual state pole in terms of a quadrature taken over the virtual state eigenfunction is given.

## Key claim

The 1/2+ first excited state of 9Be is a virtual state with energy -23.5 keV, not a bound state; the corresponding state in 9B is a broad resonance with complex energy 0.6 - i0.75 MeV, and the line shapes for both states can be described using effective range expansion and phase shifts derived from N-8Be potentials fitted to photodisintegration data.

## Method

The authors constructed N-8Be effective potentials from data on the photodisintegration of 9Be, then used these potentials to calculate p-8Be phase shifts. The 1/2+ state in 9Be was characterized via effective range expansion to determine its virtual-state nature and energy, while the 9B resonance was analyzed from the phase shifts to extract its peak energy and width. An expression for the residue at a virtual state pole in terms of a quadrature over the virtual state eigenfunction was also derived.

## Key numbers

- The 1/2+ first excited state of 9Be is a virtual state at -23.5 keV (negative energy indicating unbound virtual state)
- The partner 1/2+ state in 9B has a resonance peak at about 1.1 MeV
- The 9B resonance has a full width at half maximum (FWHM) of 1.5 MeV
- The complex energy of the 9B resonance is 0.6 - i0.75 MeV

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the A=9 mirror nuclei 9Be and 9B, which have been of long-standing interest due to their cluster structure and the unusual nature of their low-lying states. The virtual state in 9Be and broad resonance in 9B are important for understanding the nuclear structure of light neutron-rich nuclei and for astrophysical reaction rates involving 8Be. The results enable improved modeling of reactions such as 8Be(p,gamma)9B and 8Be(n,gamma)9Be, which are relevant to nucleosynthesis in stellar environments.
