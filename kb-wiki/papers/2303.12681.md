# Target normal single-spin asymmetry in inclusive electron-nucleon scattering in the 1/Nc expansion

> The target normal single-spin asymmetry in inclusive electron-nucleon scattering is studied in the low-energy regime that includes the $Δ$ resonance. The particular interest in the asymmetry resides in that it is driven by two-photon exchange effects. It probes the spin-dependent absorptive part of the two-photon exchange amplitude, which is free of infrared and collinear singularities and represents the most pristine expression of two-photon exchange dynamics. The study presented here uses the 1/Nc expansion of QCD, which combines the $N$ and $Δ$ through the emergent SU(4) spin-flavor symmetry in the baryon sector and allows for a systematic construction of the transition EM currents. The analysis includes the first subleading corrections in the 1/Nc expansion and presents results for elastic and inelastic final states. The asymmetry is found to be in the range $10^{-3}-10^{-2}$. The $Δ$ resonance plays an important role as an intermediate state in the elastic asymmetry and as a final state in the inclusive asymmetry.

## Key claim

The target normal single-spin asymmetry (TSSA) in inclusive electron-nucleon scattering is computed using the 1/Nc expansion of QCD, which provides a systematic framework for including both N and Delta states. The asymmetry is found to be in the range 10^-3 to 10^-2, with the Delta resonance playing an important role as an intermediate state in elastic asymmetry and as a final state in inclusive asymmetry, but the calculation is limited to the first subleading order in 1/Nc and to kinematic regimes where non-resonant pi N contributions are suppressed.

## Method

The authors compute the TSSA from two-photon exchange (TPE) effects in inclusive electron-nucleon scattering using the 1/Nc expansion of QCD, which organizes baryon states into an SU(4) spin-flavor multiplet containing both N and Delta. They construct the electromagnetic current operators to leading and first subleading order in 1/Nc, with form factors determined by matching to empirical nucleon form factors at Nc=3, and evaluate the spin-dependent cross section from the interference of one-photon exchange and two-photon exchange amplitudes for elastic (eN -> e'N) and inelastic (eN -> e'Delta) final states across three distinct kinematic regimes.

## Key numbers

- The TSSA is found to be in the range 10^-3 to 10^-2 for the energy region studied
- The Delta threshold sqrt(s) = 1.23 GeV corresponds to CM momentum k = 0.26 GeV
- The generic N* threshold sqrt(s) ≈ 1.5 GeV corresponds to k ≈ 0.46 GeV
- The dipole form factor mass scale used is Lambda_EM^2 = 0.71 GeV^2
- The ratio of isovector to isoscalar magnetic moments at zero momentum transfer is G_M^V(0)/G_M^S(0) = 5.34, reflecting the Nc scaling
- The low-energy regime II is applicable for 0.26 < k < 0.35 GeV; regime III for 0.3 < k < 0.6 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of the TSSA in elastic eN scattering (Arafune 1970, Guenther 1971, Pasquini 2004, Koshchii 2018) and in deep-inelastic scattering (Metz 2006, Afanasev 2007, Schlegel 2012) by providing a systematic calculation in the resonance region where the Delta isobar appears. The 1/Nc expansion framework, previously applied to baryon form factors by Goity (2022), enables a unified treatment of N and Delta states that connects the elastic and deep-inelastic domains. The results enable future experimental tests at Jefferson Lab with beam energies 0.5-1.5 GeV, as proposed by Grauvogel (2021), and provide a baseline for understanding the energy evolution of the TSSA from the resonance region to deep-inelastic kinematics.
