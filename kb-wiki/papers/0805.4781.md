# Statistical hadronization of charm: from FAIR to the LHC

> We discuss the production of charmonium in nuclear collisions within the framework of the statistical hadronization model. We demonstrate that the model reproduces very well the availble data at RHIC. We provide predictions for the LHC energy where, dependently on the charm production cross section, a dramatically different behaviour of charmonium production as a function of centrality might be expected. We extend our predictions for charm production towards the threshold energies, where charm is expected to be measured at the future FAIR facility.

# Statistical hadronization of charm: from FAIR to the LHC

## Key claim
The statistical hadronization model (SHM) reproduces J/ψ and ψ' production data at SPS and RHIC energies, but at LHC energy the centrality dependence of J/ψ production may reverse dramatically, with R_AA exceeding unity for central collisions, depending on the charm production cross section, which could be up to two times larger than presently assumed.

## Method
The authors assume charm quarks are produced in primary hard collisions and their total number remains constant until hadronization at chemical freeze‑out, with thermal equilibration in the QGP near T_c. The model uses the charm production cross section from NLO pQCD or experiment, plus freeze‑out parameters (T, μ_b, volume per unit rapidity) from thermal fits of non‑charmed hadrons. Predictions are computed for central Au‑Au collisions from RHIC energy down to the charm production threshold.

## Key numbers
- At RHIC (√s_NN = 200 GeV), the rapidity density of initially produced charm quark pairs in central collisions is dN_c̄c/dy = 1.6 (using the FONLL charm production cross section).
- The minimal volume for QGP creation is assumed to be 400 fm³, corresponding to N_part ≈ 50 where R_AA approaches the pp value.
- At LHC energy (√s_NN = 5.5 TeV), the charm production cross section (including shadowing in PbPb) is expected to be about an order of magnitude larger than at RHIC.
- If the charm production cross section is two times larger than the present assumption, J/ψ enhancement in central collisions at LHC could reach about a factor of 2 relative to the number of initially produced c̄c pairs.
- The charm quark mass used is m_c ≈ 1.3 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the statistical hadronization model previously proposed by the same authors (Andronic et al., 2007; Braun-Munzinger et al., 2003) to describe J/ψ and ψ' production at SPS (√s_NN ≈ 17 GeV) and RHIC (√s_NN = 200 GeV). It provides predictions for LHC energy and for energies near the charm production threshold (√s_NN ≈ 6 GeV) relevant for the future FAIR facility. The results contrast with the original Debye‑screening suppression scenario (Satz, 1986; updated in Satz, 2005) and show that in‑medium mass modifications of charmed hadrons do not measurably change open‑charm production cross sections, though charmonium yields may be affected near threshold.
