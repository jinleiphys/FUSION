# Description of alpha-clustering of 8Be nucleus states in high-precision theoretical approach

> Scrupulous theoretical study of 8Be nucleus states, both clustered and non-clustered, is performed over a wide range of the excitation energies. The quantities which characterize the degree of the alpha-clustering of these states: spectroscopic factors, cluster form factors as well as the alpha-decay widths are computed in the framework of an accurate ab initio approach developed. Other basic properties of 8Be spectrum: the binding and excitation energies, mean values of the isospin are calculated simultaneously. In the majority of instances the results of the computations turn out to be in a good agreement with the spectroscopic data. A number of predictions are made and corresponding verification experiment is proposed. Prospects of the developed approach for nuclear spectroscopy are demonstrated.

# Description of alpha-clustering of 8Be nucleus states in high-precision theoretical approach

## Key claim

The authors claim that their combined No-Core Shell Model (NCSM) and Cluster Channel Orthogonal Functions Method (CCOFM) approach can simultaneously compute binding energies, excitation energies, and alpha-decay widths for nearly all positive parity states of 8Be up to about 25 MeV excitation, but the accuracy of decay width calculations depends critically on using experimental rather than theoretical resonance energies because even small energy discrepancies (e.g., 335 keV for the ground state) can cause order-of-magnitude errors in widths.

## Method

The authors perform NCSM calculations with a complete basis up to Nmax_tot = 14 using the Daejeon16 and JISP16 NN-potentials, then project the resulting wave functions onto alpha+alpha cluster channel wave functions built via CCOFM. The cluster form factors obtained from this projection are matched to asymptotic Coulomb wave functions to compute alpha-decay widths, using either a narrow-resonance approximation (Eq. 17) for states where the barrier penetrability is small or R-matrix theory (Eq. 20) for wider resonances.

## Key numbers

- Total binding energy of 8Be ground state: 56.50 MeV (experimental); Daejeon16 calculation reproduces this with high precision; JISP16 calculation gives 2.73 MeV less (53.77 MeV)
- Ground state resonance energy (0+): 92 keV (experimental) vs 427 keV (calculated with Daejeon16), a difference of 335 keV
- 4+ state energy difference between calculation and experiment: 270 keV
- Basis size: Nmax_tot = 14 corresponds to approximately 2 x 10^8 Slater determinants
- Matching points for decay widths: 3.92 fm (0+), 3.96 fm (2+), and 4.34 fm (4+)
- Outer maxima of cluster form factors located in range 3.0 to 3.4 fm
- 4He total binding energy from Daejeon16: 28.372 MeV (experimental: 28.296 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Rodkin and Tchuvil'sky, earlier work) which computed only alpha-particle spectroscopic factors for the rotational band of 8Be, by now including alpha-decay widths and covering a wider range of states. It contrasts with earlier RGM-based calculations using realistic NN-potentials (Kv1, Kv2, Kv3) that were limited to Nmax_tot = 10 or 12 and used NCSM only as a control method. The approach enables systematic ab initio calculation of both bound-state properties and decay characteristics for light nuclei, with the authors proposing specific verification experiments for their predictions.
