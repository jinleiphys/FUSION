# Three-loop HTLpt thermodynamics at finite temperature and chemical potential

> In this proceedings we present a state-of-the-art method of calculating thermodynamic potential at finite temperature and finite chemical potential, using Hard Thermal Loop perturbation theory (HTLpt) up to next-to-next-leading-order (NNLO). The resulting thermodynamic potential enables us to evaluate different thermodynamic quantities including pressure and various quark number susceptibilities (QNS). Comparison between our analytic results for those thermodynamic quantities with the available lattice data shows a good agreement.

# Three-loop HTLpt thermodynamics at finite temperature and chemical potential

## Key claim

The NNLO HTLpt thermodynamic potential yields pressure and quark number susceptibilities that show good agreement with available lattice QCD data, but only when using a one-loop running coupling with fixed renormalization scales set to central values (Λ_g = 2πT and Λ = 2π√(T²+μ²/π²)), and the agreement holds down to temperatures relevant to LHC for some quantities.

## Method

The authors computed the QCD thermodynamic potential using Hard Thermal Loop perturbation theory (HTLpt) up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO, three-loop level) at finite temperature and finite chemical potential, for both flavor-independent and flavor-dependent quark chemical potentials. The calculation is completely analytic and gauge independent, using separate renormalization scales for gluonic (Λ_g) and fermionic (Λ) diagrams, with the Debye mass prescription of Braaten and Nieto generalized to finite chemical potential by Vuorinen. Thermodynamic quantities including pressure and various quark number susceptibilities were then obtained by taking appropriate derivatives of the thermodynamic potential.

## Key numbers

- α_s(1.5 GeV) = 0.326 taken from lattice measurements, yielding Λ_MSbar = 176 MeV
- Scaled NNLO HTLpt pressure shown for μ_B = 0 and μ_B = 400 MeV, compared with lattice data from Borsányi et al.
- Second-order and fourth-order baryon number susceptibilities (BNS) compared with lattice data from multiple groups (WB, BNL-BI, MILC, TIFR)
- Fourth-order diagonal and off-diagonal single quark number susceptibilities (sQNS) compared with lattice data from BNL-BI and TIFR groups
- Renormalization scales varied by a factor of two: πT ≤ Λ_g ≤ 4πT around central value Λ_g = 2πT

## Context

This work extends the HTLpt program from one-loop (LO) and two-loop (NLO) calculations to three-loop (NNLO) for finite temperature and chemical potential, building on prior developments by Andersen, Braaten, and Strickland (early 2000s) and the Hard Thermal Loop concept of Braaten and Pisarski (1990). Previous HTLpt calculations of QCD thermodynamic potentials up to one-loop were done by Andersen et al. and others, two-loop by Andersen et al. and Haque et al., and three-loop for gluonic and full QCD at zero chemical potential by several groups. The results enable further study of transport properties of the quark-gluon plasma and provide a perturbative benchmark for comparison with lattice QCD simulations in the phenomenologically important region near the transition temperature.
