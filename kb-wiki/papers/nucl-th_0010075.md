# First Order Kaon Condensation in Neutron Stars: Finite Size Effects in the Mixed Phase

> We study the role of Coulomb and surface effects on the phase transition from dense nuclear matter to a mixed phase of nuclear and kaon-condensed matter. We calculate corrections to the bulk calculation of the equation of state (EOS) and the critical density for the transition by solving explicitly for spherical, cylindrical, and planar structures. The importance of Debye screening in the determination of the charged particle profiles is studied in some detail. We find that the surface and Coulomb contributions to the energy density are small, but that they play an important role in the determination of the critical pressure for the transition, as well as affecting the size and geometry of favored structures. This changes the EOS over a wide range of pressure and consequently increases the maximum mass by about 0.1 solar masses. Implications for transport properties of the mixed phase are also discussed.

## Key claim

The surface and Coulomb contributions to the energy density in the mixed phase of nuclear and kaon-condensed matter are small, but they significantly increase the critical pressure for the onset of the mixed phase by about 50% compared to the bulk calculation, which in turn increases the maximum neutron star mass by about 0.1 solar masses.

## Method

The authors solved the kaon equation of motion explicitly for spherical, cylindrical, and planar structures in the Wigner-Seitz approximation, including gradient terms to capture surface effects and computing Coulomb energy from Gauss' law. They constructed sequences of droplets, rods, and slabs at fixed external pressure, minimizing the Gibbs free energy per baryon with respect to structure size to determine the ground state geometry. Debye screening was incorporated through an iterative relaxation procedure that adjusted the electric potential and charged particle profiles until convergence.

## Key numbers

- Bulk critical pressure for onset of mixed phase: p = 0.28 fm^{-4}, corresponding to baryon number density n_B = 0.43 fm^{-3}
- New critical pressure including finite-size effects (without screening): approximately 0.40 fm^{-4}, about 50% higher than the bulk value
- Critical pressure with Debye screening included: 0.375 fm^{-4}
- Typical Debye screening length of electrons at relevant densities: 7-10 fm
- Boundary layer thickness between phases: approximately 5 fm
- Size of energetically favored structures: 5-7 fm
- Increase in maximum neutron star mass: about 0.1 solar masses

## Context

This work extends the model of first-order kaon condensation proposed by Glendenning and Schaffner (1999), who predicted a mixed phase of nuclear and kaon-condensed matter. It builds on earlier studies of finite-size effects in the quark-hadron phase transition by Heiselberg, Pethick, and Staubo (1993), and uses the bulk calculation methodology developed by Ravenhall, Pethick, and Wilson (1983) and Pethick and Ravenhall (1995). The results enable more accurate predictions of neutron star mass-radius relations and transport properties in the mixed phase region, and show that Debye screening must be accounted for to determine the detailed geometry and particle profiles, though it has a relatively small effect (less than 1 MeV per baryon) on the free energy density.
