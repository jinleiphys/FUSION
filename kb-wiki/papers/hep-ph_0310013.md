# Relativistic Faddeev approach to a non-local NJL model

> The diquark and nucleon are studied in a non-local NJL model. We solve the relativistic Faddeev equation and compare the results with the ordinary NJL model. Although the model is quark confining, it is not diquark confining in the rainbow-ladder approximation. We show that the off-shell contribution to the diquark $T$ matrix is crucial for the structure of the nucleon: without its inclusion the attraction in the scalar channel is too weak to form a three-body bound state.

## Key claim

The authors show that in a non-local Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, the on-shell approximation for the diquark T-matrix is insufficient to produce a three-body nucleon bound state; the off-shell contribution to the diquark T-matrix is crucial, and without it the attraction in the scalar channel is too weak to bind the nucleon.

## Method

The authors construct a non-local NJL model with SU(2) flavour and SU(3) colour symmetry, using a Gaussian form factor in Euclidean space. They solve the Schwinger-Dyson equation for the dressed quark propagator in the rainbow-ladder approximation, then compute the scalar diquark T-matrix from the quark-quark interaction. The relativistic Faddeev equation for the nucleon is reduced to an effective two-body Bethe-Salpeter equation between a quark and a scalar diquark, exploiting the separability of the non-local interaction. The equations are solved in the nucleon rest frame by expanding the scalar vertex functions in Chebyshev polynomials.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass fixed to 136.6 MeV and decay constant to 92.4 MeV for all parameter sets.
- Three parameter sets are analysed: set A (non-confining) with dynamical quark mass m(0) = 297.9 MeV and cutoff Lambda = 1046.8 MeV; set B (confining) with m(0) = 406.2 MeV and Lambda = 723.4 MeV; set C (confining) with m(0) = 461.3 MeV and Lambda = 638.1 MeV.
- No bound scalar diquark exists for r_s < 0.110 (set A), 0.112 (set B), or 0.146 (set C), where r_s = g_s/g_pi.
- The on-shell approximation requires an artificial enhancement of the quark-diquark coupling by a factor of about 3 to generate a three-body bound state.

## Context

This work extends the non-local NJL model approach of Plant and Birse (1998), which demonstrated quark confinement through the absence of real poles in the quark propagator, to the baryon sector. It contrasts with the ordinary (local) NJL model, where similar difficulties with the on-shell approximation for the nucleon were noted by previous authors. The results enable further studies of nucleon structure using covariant quark-diquark dynamics with momentum-dependent quark masses consistent with lattice QCD simulations.
