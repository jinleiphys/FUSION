# Deuteron radial moments for renormalized chiral potentials

> We calculate deuteron positive and negative radial moments involving any bilinear function of the deuteron S and D wave functions for renormalized OPE and TPE chiral potentials. The role played by the strong singularities of the potentials at the origin and the short distance insensitivity of the results when the potentials are fully iterated is emphasized as compared to realistic potentials.

## Key claim

For renormalized chiral one-pion-exchange (OPE) and two-pion-exchange (TPE) potentials that are fully iterated in the Schrodinger equation, the deuteron inverse radial moments become finite up to a higher order than for regular phenomenological potentials, but only up to a finite power that depends on the singularity strength of the potential at the origin; the abstract's claim of "short distance insensitivity" applies only to those moments that converge, while higher inverse moments still diverge.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled-channel Schrodinger equation for the deuteron S- and D-wave reduced wave functions u(r) and w(r) using renormalized chiral OPE and TPE potentials that are singular at the origin (behaving as 1/r^3 and 1/r^6 respectively). They computed the radial moments ⟨r^n⟩_u, ⟨r^n⟩_w, and ⟨r^n⟩_uw for powers n from -3 to 2 by integrating the bilinear products of these wave functions, and compared the results to those from the phenomenological NijmII and Reid93 potentials.

## Key numbers

- For the OPE potential (g_A = 1.29), ⟨r^2⟩_u = 14.582(6) fm^2, ⟨r^2⟩_w = 0.3849(2) fm^2, and ⟨r^2⟩_uw = 2.0883(9) fm^2.
- For the TPE potential (Set IV), ⟨r^2⟩_u = 15.09(13) fm^2, ⟨r^2⟩_w = 0.38(2) fm^2, and ⟨r^2⟩_uw = 2.09(3) fm^2.
- For the OPE potential, ⟨r^{-1}⟩_u = 0.4259(3) fm^{-1} and ⟨r^{-2}⟩_u = 0.3464(8) fm^{-2} are finite, but ⟨r^{-3}⟩_u diverges (∞).
- For the TPE potential (Set IV), ⟨r^{-3}⟩_u = 0.1851(8) fm^{-3} is finite, unlike for OPE or regular potentials.
- The asymptotic D/S ratio was taken as η = 0.0256(4) for TPE and η = 0.026333 for OPE.
- The OPE short-distance scale R = 1.07764 fm from the 1/r^3 singularity strength.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous renormalization program for NN interactions with chiral potentials (Pavon Valderrama and Ruiz Arriola, 2003-2005) and addresses the finiteness of inverse deuteron moments recently discussed for OPE by Nogga and Hanhart (2006) and Platter and Phillips (2006). The results enable model-independent calculations of pion-deuteron and kaon-deuteron scattering lengths within the multiple scattering formalism of Deloff (2001), where inverse moments appear as expansion coefficients, and show that chiral potentials when fully iterated yield more convergent inverse moments than regular phenomenological potentials like NijmII or Reid93 (Stoks et al., 1994).
