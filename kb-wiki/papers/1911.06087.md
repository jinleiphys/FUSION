# Statistical double $Λ$ hypernuclear formation from $Ξ^-$ absorption at rest in light nuclei

> We investigate double $Λ$ hyperfragment formation from the statistical decay of double $Λ$ compound nuclei produced in the $Ξ^-$ absorption at rest in light nuclei, $^{12}\mathrm{C}$, $^{14}\mathrm{N}$ and $^{16}\mathrm{O}$. We examine the target and the $ΛΛ$ bond energy dependence of the double $Λ$ hyperfragment formation probabilities, especially of those double hypernuclei observed in experiments. For the $^{12}\mathrm{C}$ ($^{14}\mathrm{N}$) target, the formation probabilities of $^{6}_{ΛΛ}\mathrm{He}$ and $^{10}_{ΛΛ}\mathrm{Be}$ ($^{13}_{ΛΛ}\mathrm{B}$) are found to be reasonably large as they are observed in the KEK-E373 (KEK-E176) experiment. By comparison, for $^{16}\mathrm{O}$ target, the formation probability of $^{11}_{ΛΛ}\mathrm{Be}$ is calculated to be small with $ΔB_{ΛΛ}$ consistent with the Nagara event. We also evaluate the formation probability of ${}^{5}_{ΛΛ}\mathrm{H}$ from a $Ξ^-$-${}^{6}\mathrm{He}$ bound state, ${}^{7}_Ξ\mathrm{H}$.

## Key claim

The formation probabilities of specific double Λ hypernuclei from Ξ⁻ absorption at rest in light nuclei depend sensitively on the assumed ΛΛ bond energy, and the observed events in KEK-E373 and KEK-E176 experiments are consistent with the statistical decay model when using the weaker ΛΛ bond energy (model B or C) rather than the older, stronger value.

## Method

The authors used a sequential binary statistical decay model (SDM) to simulate the decay of double Λ compound nuclei formed via Ξ⁻ absorption at rest in ¹²C, ¹⁴N, and ¹⁶O targets. The compound nuclei were assumed to form from Ξ⁻ capture from the 3D atomic orbit, and the SDM calculated binary decay rates using the back-shifted Fermi gas model for level densities, with transmission coefficients assuming strong absorption in the inverse fusion process. Three models for the ΛΛ bond energy were compared: model A (ΔB_ΛΛ = 4.9 MeV, the old rejected value), model B (ΔB_ΛΛ = 0.67 MeV, from the Nagara event), and model C (ΔB_ΛΛ varying linearly with mass number, constrained by both Nagara and Mino events).

## Key numbers

- For Ξ⁻ + ¹²C, the formation probability of ⁶_ΛΛHe from the compound nucleus is 4.3% (model A), 1.6% (model B), and 1.3% (model C)
- For Ξ⁻ + ¹²C, the formation probability of ¹⁰_ΛΛBe is 18.7% (model A), 5.5% (model B), and 8.0% (model C)
- For Ξ⁻ + ¹⁴N, the formation probability of ¹³_ΛΛB is 18.5% (model A), 11.2% (model B), and 15.5% (model C)
- For Ξ⁻ + ¹⁶O, the formation probability of ¹¹_ΛΛBe is 1.1% (model A), 0.2% (model B), and 0.3% (model C)
- The total double Λ hypernucleus formation probability from the compound nucleus is 71.2% (model A), 41.0% (model B), and 50.6% (model C) for ¹²C target
- The estimated number of ⁶_ΛΛHe events in the KEK-E373 experiment is 1.2 (model A), 0.5 (model B), and 0.4 (model C), assuming 160 Ξ⁻ absorption events in ¹²C and 30% compound nucleus formation probability
- The energy released in the Ξ⁻p → ΛΛ conversion process is 28.62 MeV
- The Ξ⁻ binding energy in the 3D atomic orbit is 0.126 MeV for ¹²C, 0.174 MeV for ¹⁴N, and 0.230 MeV for ¹⁶O

## Context

This work extends the statistical decay model approach of Hirata (1999), who analyzed Ξ⁻ absorption in ¹²C using a combined transport model and statistical decay framework with a strongly attractive ΛΛ interaction (ΔB_ΛΛ = 4.9 MeV). The present study updates those calculations using the weaker ΛΛ bond energy determined from the Nagara event (ΔB_ΛΛ = 0.67 ± 0.12 MeV) and the Mino event from J-PARC E07, and extends the analysis to ¹⁴N and ¹⁶O targets. The results enable predictions for the expected number of double hypernucleus events in the ongoing J-PARC E07 experiment, which aims to observe 10⁴ Ξ⁻ absorption events, and provide constraints on the ΛΛ interaction needed to address the hyperon puzzle in neutron star physics.
