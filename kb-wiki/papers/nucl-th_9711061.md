# Vector Meson Photoproduction with an Effective Lagrangian in the Quark Model I: Formalism

> A quark model approach to the photoproduction of vector mesons off nucleons is proposed. Its starting point is an effective Lagrangian of the interaction between the vector meson and the quarks inside the baryon. This paper develops the formalism and highlights the dynamical roles of s-channel resonances. It is demonstrated that the proposed approach is ideal to support searching for the missing resonances in the vector meson photoproduction experiments proposed at TJNAF.

## Key claim

The paper develops a quark-model-based formalism for vector meson photoproduction off nucleons using an effective Lagrangian for the quark-meson interaction, claiming this approach is ideal for searching for "missing resonances" in experiments at TJNAF, though the formalism itself is presented without numerical validation and the authors note that whether additional t-channel contributions such as Pomeron exchange are needed remains an open question.

## Method

The authors construct an effective Lagrangian for the interaction between vector mesons and quarks inside baryons, then derive transition amplitudes for s-channel, u-channel, and t-channel contributions at tree level. S-channel resonances below 2 GeV are included explicitly with their helicity amplitudes, while resonances above 2 GeV are treated as degenerate in the harmonic oscillator basis; u-channel contributions separate the spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 ground-state resonances from excited degenerate states, and a gauge-fixing term is added to restore gauge invariance broken by the t-channel vector meson exchange term.

## Key numbers

- The formalism targets vector meson photoproduction in the resonance region below 2 GeV, where s-channel resonances are included explicitly
- The harmonic oscillator parameter α² appears throughout the spatial integrals and form factors, with the Gaussian factor e^{-(q²+k²)/(6α²)} common to all amplitudes
- The decay width for s-channel resonances is given by Γ(q) = Γ_R (√s/M_R) Σ_i x_i (|q_i|/|q_i^R|)^{2l+1} D_l(q_i)/D_l(q_i^R), where Γ_R is the total decay width at resonance mass M_R
- The fission barrier function D_l(q) = exp(-q²/(3α²)) is independent of l in the harmonic oscillator basis
- The spin-3/2 resonance with n=0 in the u-channel has a transition amplitude proportional to 1/(P_f·k + δM²/2) with the mass splitting δM² = (M_n² - M_f²)/2

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model approach previously successful for pseudoscalar meson photoproduction (Zhao, Li, Bennhold, pseudoscalar) to the vector meson case, addressing the limitation that earlier models such as the Söding Model (Söding, 1966) and the quark pair creation model (Yaouanc) could only describe t-channel exchange or s-channel resonances separately. The formalism enables systematic inclusion of s-channel, u-channel, and t-channel contributions needed to analyze upcoming high-precision data from ELSA and TJNAF, though the authors note that the applicability of the duality hypothesis to vector meson photoproduction remains unclear and requires comparison with experimental data.
