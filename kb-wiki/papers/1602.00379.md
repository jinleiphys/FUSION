# IR-improved Soft-wall AdS/QCD Model for Baryons

> We construct an infrared-improved soft-wall AdS/QCD model for baryons by considering the infrared-modified 5D conformal mass and Yukawa coupling of the bulk baryon field. The model is also built by taking into account the parity-doublet pattern for the excited baryons. When taking the bulk vacuum structure of the meson field to be the one obtained consistently in the infrared-improved soft-wall AdS/QCD model for mesons, we arrive at a consistent prediction for the baryon mass spectrum in even and odd parity. The prediction shows a remarkable agreement with the experimental data. We also perform a calculation for the $ρ(a_1)$ meson-nucleon coupling constant and obtain a consistent result in comparison with the experimental data and many other models.

## Key claim

The authors construct an infrared-improved soft-wall AdS/QCD model for baryons that, by incorporating an IR-modified 5D conformal mass and Yukawa coupling along with a parity-doublet pattern, yields a baryon mass spectrum in "remarkable agreement" with experimental data, contingent on taking the bulk vacuum structure of the meson field from a previously developed consistent meson model.

## Method

The model introduces two 5D Dirac spinor fields (N1, N2) corresponding to chiral baryon operators, with an IR-modified 5D conformal mass and an IR-modified Yukawa coupling to the bulk scalar field X. The bulk vacuum expectation value (bVEV) of X is taken from the IR-improved soft-wall AdS/QCD model for mesons (Cui 2013), parameterized as v(z) = (A z + B z^3)/(1 + C z^2). The equations of motion for the baryon fields are decoupled into even- and odd-parity sectors, and the mass eigenvalues are obtained numerically by solving these equations with UV boundary conditions.

## Key numbers

- Even parity nucleon ground state mass: 939 MeV (theory) vs. 939 MeV (experiment)
- Even parity first excited state mass: 1435 MeV (theory) vs. 1440 MeV (experiment)
- Even parity second excited state mass: 1698 MeV (theory) vs. 1710 MeV (experiment)
- Odd parity ground state mass: 1473 MeV (theory) vs. 1535 MeV (experiment)
- Odd parity first excited state mass: 1717 MeV (theory) vs. 1650 MeV (experiment)
- rho meson-nucleon coupling constant g_rho_NN: 2.48 (present model), compared to experimental range 4.2 to 6.5 (Hohler 1974, Machleidt 2000, Gross 2007) and 2.52 plus or minus 0.06 (Stoks 1996)
- a_1 meson-nucleon coupling constant g_a1_NN: 0.14 (present model)
- Input parameters: quark mass m_q = 3.52 MeV, quark condensate sigma^(1/3) = 290 MeV, energy scale mu_g = 473 MeV, energy scale mu_c = 375 MeV, lambda_A = 3.93, lambda_B = 16.58, lambda_N = 2.55

## Context

This work extends the IR-improved soft-wall AdS/QCD model for mesons (Cui 2013) to the baryon sector, addressing the parity-doublet pattern of excited baryons as previously discussed in a hard-wall baryon model (Hong 2006). The model improves upon earlier soft-wall baryon models (Gutsche 2011, Gutsche 2012) that exhibited a massless mode due to insufficient chiral symmetry breaking, and it provides a more consistent baryon mass spectrum than the hard-wall results (Hong 2006). The calculation of rho and a_1 meson-nucleon coupling constants enables comparison with empirical data (Hohler 1974, Machleidt 2000, Gross 2007, Stoks 1996), QCD sum rules (Aliev 2009), chiral quark models (Riska 2000), and other holographic models (Maru 2009, Ahn 2009, Huseynova 2014).
