# Pion-nucleon scattering in an effective chiral field theory with explicit spin-3/2 fields

> We analyze elastic-pion nucleon scattering to third order in the so-called small scale expansion. It is based on an effective Lagrangian including pions, nucleons and deltas as active degrees of freedom and counting external momenta, the pion mass and the nucleon-delta mass splitting as small parameters. The fermion fields are considered as very heavy. We present results for phase shifts, threshold parameters and the sigma term. We discuss the convergence of the approach. A detailed comparison with results obtained in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory to third and fourth order is also given.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including explicit spin-3/2 delta fields in an effective chiral field theory for pion-nucleon scattering, using the small scale expansion (SSE) to third order, yields improved results compared to the third-order chiral expansion without deltas, and performs only slightly worse than fourth-order chiral perturbation theory, but with the caveat that this conclusion depends on fitting to specific phase shift datasets (Matsinos or Karlsruhe) and on the energy range considered (40–100 MeV or 40–200 MeV pion lab momentum).

## Method

The authors constructed a one-loop amplitude for elastic pion-nucleon scattering to third order in the small scale expansion (SSE), using an effective Lagrangian that includes pions, nucleons, and delta(1232) resonances as active degrees of freedom, with external momenta, the pion mass, and the nucleon-delta mass splitting (Δ = 294 MeV) treated as small parameters. They employed heavy baryon techniques to project out the large components of the fermion fields, computed the necessary 1/m corrections, and determined the 14 low-energy constants (LECs) by fitting to S-wave and P-wave phase shifts from the Matsinos dataset (40–100 MeV pion lab momentum) and the Karlsruhe dataset (40–200 MeV pion lab momentum).

## Key numbers

- Nucleon-delta mass splitting: Δ = 294 MeV, approximately equal to 3Fπ with Fπ = 92.4 MeV (the weak pion decay constant)
- The third-order SSE fit to Matsinos phase shifts (40–100 MeV pion lab momentum) is "clearly better" than third-order chiral expansion results and "only slightly worse" than fourth-order chiral perturbation theory results
- Fits to Karlsruhe phase shifts were performed for pion lab momenta between 40 and 200 MeV, allowing study of the resonance region
- The sigma term extracted from fits to KA85 phases gives a value "consistent with previous determinations"
- The chiral symmetry breaking scale Λχ is approximately 1 GeV

## Context

This work extends the heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory framework of Jenkins and Manohar (1991) and the formalized small scale expansion of Hemmert, Holstein, and Kambor (1997) by providing the first systematic evaluation of 1/m corrections for pion-nucleon scattering with explicit delta degrees of freedom. It builds on previous third- and fourth-order chiral expansion studies by Fettes, Meißner, and collaborators (1998, 1999) that used only pion and nucleon fields, showing that including the delta improves convergence and extends the energy range of applicability. The results enable future fourth-order SSE calculations and provide a foundation for extending the approach to other processes involving the delta resonance.
