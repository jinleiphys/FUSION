# Crossing the coexistence line at constant magnetization

> Using Monte Carlo histogram methods, the microcanonical caloric curve is computed for the Ising model in two and three dimensions with fixed magnetization. Whereas the signatures of a first order phase transition are clearly visible for large systems, intriguing finite size effects are revealed for smaller system sizes. The behaviour of the caloric curve is studied in a systematic way. Furthermore, results for the thermal stability of three-dimensional droplets of minority spins inside the two-phase region are presented. The effect of the percolation transition on the stability of these droplets is discussed.

## Key claim

Using Monte Carlo histogram methods, the microcanonical caloric curve for the Ising model with fixed magnetization reveals a first-order phase transition (S-shaped backbending) only for surprisingly large system sizes (above about L=250 in 2D and L=50 in 3D), whereas smaller systems show a monovalued curve with no indication of a discontinuous transition.

## Method

The authors performed canonical Monte Carlo simulations of the nearest-neighbor Ising model on square lattices (L=100 to L=900) and simple cubic lattices (L=10 to L=80) with periodic boundary conditions, using a spin-flip algorithm that restricts magnetization to three allowed values (M0, M0±2). From the collected histograms they extracted the degeneracy g(E,M0) and computed the microcanonical inverse temperature β*(E) = dS(E)/dE via Gaussian smoothing. For the droplet studies, they additionally used a nonlocal spin-exchange algorithm adapted for equilibrium crystal shapes.

## Key numbers

- In two dimensions at fixed magnetization m0=0.92, the S-shaped backbending of the caloric curve appears only for system sizes above a threshold around L=250
- In three dimensions at fixed magnetization m0=0.568, the S-shape requires system sizes around L=50 or larger
- For a 60^3 system at various magnetizations, the discontinuous character is much more pronounced at higher magnetization (m0=0.75) and disappears at lower magnetization (m0=0.45075 shows a monovalued curve)
- The reduced droplet size nc(L) for m0=0.75 approaches the theoretical infinite-system prediction from below as L increases, while for m0=0.568 it approaches from above

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of two-dimensional Ising model with fixed magnetization (Pleimling 2000) and addresses conflicting interpretations from simulations of three-dimensional moderate-sized systems (up to 40^3 spins) that were erroneously interpreted as evidence for a continuous phase transition (Carr 1998). The results refute a recent claim (Gulminelli 2000) that the microcanonical caloric curve at constant volume in the corresponding lattice gas model does not possess the typical S-shape. The study enables proper interpretation of finite-size effects in models of nuclear matter fragmentation and surface physics, and shows that the percolation transition (below the critical temperature in 3D) qualitatively changes the behavior of minority-spin droplets.
