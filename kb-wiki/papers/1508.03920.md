# Nontopological Soliton in the Polyakov Quark Meson Model

> Within a mean field approximation, we study a nontopological soliton solution of the Polyakov quark-meson model in the presence of a fermionic vacuum term with two flavors at finite temperature and density. The profile of the effective potential exhibits a stable soliton solution below a critical temperature $T\leq T_χ^c$ for both the crossover and the first-order phase transitions, and these solutions are calculated here with appropriate boundary conditions. However, it is found that only if $T\leq T^c_d$,the energy of the soliton $M_N$ is less than the energy of the three free constituent quarks $3M_q$. As $T> T^c_d$, there is an instant delocalization phase transition from hadron matter to quark matter. The phase diagram together with the location of a critical end point (CEP) has been obtained in $T$ and $μ$ plane. We notice that two critical temperatures always satisfy $T^c_d\leq T_χ^c$. Finally, we present and compare the result of thermodynamic pressure at zero chemical potential with lattice data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that stable nontopological soliton solutions exist only for temperatures \(T \leq T_{\chi}^c\) (the chiral critical temperature), but that the soliton energy \(M_N\) is less than the energy of three free constituent quarks \(3M_q\) only for \(T \leq T_d^c\); above \(T_d^c\) an instant delocalization phase transition from hadron matter to quark matter occurs, with the two critical temperatures always satisfying \(T_d^c \leq T_{\chi}^c\).

## Method

The authors work within a mean-field approximation to the Polyakov quark-meson (PQM) model with two quark flavors, including a renormalized fermionic vacuum one-loop term. They compute the thermodynamic grand potential as a function of the chiral order parameter \(\sigma_v\) and the Polyakov loop fields \(\Phi, \Phi^*\), then solve the coupled Euler-Lagrange equations of motion for the spherically symmetric "hedgehog" ansatz to obtain nontopological soliton solutions at finite temperature and chemical potential. The soliton stability is assessed by comparing the soliton energy to the energy of three free constituent quarks, and the phase diagram is mapped in the \(T\)-\(\mu\) plane.

## Key numbers

- Chiral critical temperature at zero chemical potential: \(T_{\chi}^c \simeq 201\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Chiral critical temperature at \(\mu = 320\ \mathrm{MeV}\): \(T_{\chi}^c \simeq 105\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Pion decay constant: \(f_{\pi} = 93\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Pion mass: \(m_{\pi} = 138\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Sigma meson mass: \(m_{\sigma} = 472\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Yukawa coupling constant: \(g = 4.5\).
- Critical temperature for deconfinement in the pure gauge sector (two flavors): \(T_0 = 208\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The critical end point (CEP) is located in the \(T\)-\(\mu\) plane, but its numerical coordinates are not given in the text.

## Context

This work extends earlier chiral soliton model studies by Christov (1991) and Abu-Shady and Mansour (2012), which only predicted first-order phase transitions with critical temperatures around \(110\ \mathrm{MeV}\), too low compared to lattice data. By incorporating the Polyakov loop field into the nontopological soliton framework, the authors obtain both crossover and first-order phase transitions and can compare thermodynamic pressure at zero chemical potential with lattice QCD results. The approach enables a unified description of hadron and quark phases beyond the first-order transition, including crossover regimes, and provides a definition of the deconfinement critical temperature based on soliton dissolution rather than Polyakov loop derivatives.
