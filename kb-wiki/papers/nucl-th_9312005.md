# Cluster Structure of Disoriented Chiral Condensates in Rapidity Distribution

> We study the creation of disoriented chiral condensates with some initial boundary conditions that may be expected in the relativistic heavy ion collisions. The equations of motion in the linear $σ$-model are solved numerically with and without a Lorentz-boost invariance. We suggest that a distinct cluster structure of coherent pion production in the rapidity distribution may emerge due to a quench and may be observed in experiments.

# Cluster Structure of Disoriented Chiral Condensates in Rapidity Distribution

## Key claim

The authors suggest that a distinct cluster structure of coherent pion production in the rapidity distribution may emerge due to a quench and may be observed in experiments, but they note that the quench condition is an idealization of the more complex situation where temperature relaxes to zero only gradually, and more theoretical work is needed to examine if DCC can be created in the process of cooling.

## Method

The authors solved the classical equations of motion of the linear sigma model numerically in (1+1) dimensions using proper time and rapidity coordinates, both with and without Lorentz-boost invariance. For the boost-invariant case, they assumed the system starts from a single point with a small initial kick; for the non-boost-invariant case, they initialized the fields as random Gaussian fluctuations with zero average and finite variance to model a thermal quench, then computed the isospin correlation function in rapidity.

## Key numbers

- The linear sigma model parameters used: v = 87.4 MeV, H = (119 MeV)^3, lambda = 19.97, yielding f_pi = 92.5 MeV, m_pi = 135 MeV, and m_sigma = 600 MeV.
- The sigma field takes about 1 fm/c proper time to reach the true vacuum expectation value near f_pi.
- The pion field oscillations decay to zero at proper time tau_max ~ 20-30 fm/c.
- The correlation function C(tau, Delta_eta) fits an exponential exp(-c0 m_pi tau |Delta_eta|) with c0 ~ 0.4 for tau - tau0 >= 1 fm/c.
- The rapidity interval over which the pion field is correlated in isospin direction could be as large as 2-3 units.
- Initial proper time tau0 = 1 fm/c was used for the numerical solutions.

## Context

This work extends the quench mechanism for disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) proposed by Rajagopal and Wilczek (1993) by using more realistic initial conditions appropriate for relativistic heavy ion collisions, including thermal fluctuations and the space-time geometry of expanding nuclear matter. The results support the "Baked Alaska" picture sketched by Bjorken, Kowalski and Taylor (1993) and are consistent with cosmic ray events from the JACEE experiment where dominant neutral pions were found in an interval Delta_eta ~ 3. The authors note that a similar correlation result was derived from a nonlinear sigma model in (1+1) dimensions, but emphasize that the correlation emerges only when the initial average field squared is less than f_pi^2, confirming the quench mechanism. The work enables experimental searches for DCC cluster structures in rapidity distributions at relativistic heavy ion colliders.
