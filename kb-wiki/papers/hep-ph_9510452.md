# The Nucleon's Antiquark Sea: A Virtual Meson Cloud or Gluons?

> We study the possible contribution of the nucleon's virtual meson cloud to the sea quark distribution as observed in deep inelastic lepton scattering. We adjust the meson-nucleon cut-offs to the large-x tails of the antiquark distributions, find qualitatively different behavior in the flavor singlet and non-singlet channels and study the scale dependence of our results. We demonstrate that, within convolution models, to reproduce the sea quark distribution the relevant pion momenta should be around 0.8 GeV.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while the nucleon's virtual meson cloud can account for the flavor non-singlet (isovector and SU(3)-breaking) components of the antiquark sea, it fails to describe the flavor singlet component, indicating that gluon splitting into quark-antiquark pairs is essential even at low momentum scales.

## Method

The authors compute the meson cloud contribution to the nucleon's antiquark distributions using a convolution model with exponential form factors for the meson-nucleon vertices. They adjust the cut-off parameters of the form factors to fit the large-x tails of the strange antiquark distribution and the SU(3) and SU(2) flavor-breaking components from data-based parametrizations at Q^2 = 1 GeV^2. The resulting meson light-cone distributions are then compared to the MRS(A) and CTEQ3M parametrizations and to CCFR collaboration data.

## Key numbers

- The cut-off parameters at Q^2 = 1 GeV^2 are: Lambda_piNN ≈ 1000 MeV, Lambda_piNDelta ≈ 800 MeV, Lambda_KNY ≈ 1200 MeV.
- The typical pion momenta dominating the convolution integral are |k| ≈ 0.8 GeV.
- The meson virtualities are typically -t ≈ 0.4 GeV^2.
- The "number of mesons in the air" is n_pi = 0.66 and n_K = 0.10.
- The meson cloud contribution to the Gottfried sum rule violation is Delta_G = 0.17 at Q^2 = 4 GeV^2, compared to the NMC experimental value of Delta_G^exp = 0.148 ± 0.039.
- The shadowing condition for the meson cloud picture is already satisfied at x ≤ 0.2, with significant distances of order ⟨r_MN⟩ ≈ 0.25 fm.

## Context

This work extends the convolution model approach of Melnitchouk and Thomas (1993) and contrasts with prescriptions by Zoller (1992) and Szczurek and Speth (1993) that renormalize the piNN coupling constant. The authors find that their cut-offs are softer than those used in most meson-exchange models of the NN interaction by Machleidt, Holinde and Elster (1987), and harder than those inferred from sum rule considerations by Thomas (1983). The paper enables a clearer separation of mesonic versus gluonic contributions to the nucleon sea, showing that the Gottfried sum rule violation may not be purely a mesonic effect, as argued by Strikman (1992) regarding A2 Reggeon exchange at small x.
