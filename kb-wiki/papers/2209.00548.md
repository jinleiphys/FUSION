# Longitudinal momentum fraction of heavy-flavor mesons in jets in high-energy nuclear collisions

> Heavy-flavor jets are powerful tools to gain insight into the in-medium partonic energy-loss mechanisms and the quark-gluon plasma's (QGP) transport properties in high-energy nuclear collisions. In this work we present the first theoretical study of the longitudinal momentum fraction $z_{||}$ carried by heavy-flavor mesons in jets in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV. The p+p baseline is provided by POWHEG+PYTHIA8, which matches the next-to-leading-order hard processes with the parton shower. We employ a Monte Carlo transport model, which considers the collisional and radiative partonic energy loss, to simulate the evolution of heavy-flavor jets in the expanding QGP medium. We observe steeper $z_{||}$ distributions of $\rm{B^0}$ jets compared to those of $\rm{D^0}$ jets at the same kinematics region in p+p collisions, which may be a hint of the harder jet fragmentation function of b jets compared to c jets in vacuum. In A+A collisions, it is shown that the jet quenching effect would generally decrease the values of $z_{||}$. We have made a systematical study on how several factors, including jet $p_{\rm{T}}$, jet radius $R$, and collision centrality, would influence the medium modification of $z_{||}$ distributions of a $\rm{D^0}$ jet. In addition, we predict visibly stronger nuclear modifications of $\rm{B^0}$-jet $z_{||}$ distributions compared to a $\rm{D^0}$ jet within the same $p_{\rm T}$ windows as a result of the much steeper initial $z_{||}$ distribution of the $\rm{B^0}$ jet in vacuum.

## Key claim

This work presents the first theoretical study of the longitudinal momentum fraction $z_{||}$ carried by heavy-flavor mesons in jets in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV, finding that jet quenching generally decreases $z_{||}$ values. The authors predict visibly stronger nuclear modifications of $\rm{B^0}$-jet $z_{||}$ distributions compared to $\rm{D^0}$ jets within the same $p_{\rm T}$ windows, which they attribute to the much steeper initial $z_{||}$ distribution of $\rm{B^0}$ jets in vacuum rather than to a simple mass hierarchy of energy loss.

## Method

The p+p baseline was generated using POWHEG+PYTHIA8, matching next-to-leading-order hard processes with parton shower. The in-medium evolution of heavy-flavor jets in the expanding quark-gluon plasma was simulated with a Monte Carlo transport model (SHELL) that uses a modified Langevin equation incorporating both collisional (elastic) and radiative (inelastic) partonic energy loss, with the radiative gluon spectrum computed via the higher-twist scheme including the dead-cone effect. The space-time evolution of the QGP medium was provided by the iEBE-VISHNU hydrodynamic model, and hadronization was performed using the Lund string model in PYTHIA8.

## Key numbers

- $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV for Pb+Pb collisions and $\sqrt{s}$ = 5.02 TeV for p+p collisions
- Jet transverse momentum intervals: $5 < p_{\rm{T,jet}} < 7$ GeV/c, $7 < p_{\rm{T,jet}} < 10$ GeV/c, $10 < p_{\rm{T,jet}} < 15$ GeV/c, and $15 < p_{\rm{T,jet}} < 50$ GeV/c
- $\rm{D^0}$ meson transverse momentum range: $2 < p_{\rm{T,D^0}} < 36$ GeV/c
- Jet radius values studied: $R$ = 0.2, 0.4, and 0.6
- Charged hadron cuts: $p_{\rm{T}} > 0.15$ GeV/c and $|\eta| < 0.9$
- Spatial diffusion coefficient: $2\pi T D_s \sim 4.0$, extracted by $\chi^2$ fitting to D meson $R_{\rm AA}$ data
- Jet transport parameter: $q_0 = 1.2$ GeV$^2$/fm (LHC)
- Heavy quark masses: $m_c \sim 1.5$ GeV, $m_b \sim 4.8$ GeV
- Critical temperature for medium: $T_c = 165$ MeV
- Single-constituent $\rm{D^0}$ jet fractions at $R=0.2$: roughly 43%, 38%, 27%, and 17% in the four $p_{\rm{T,jet}}$ intervals; at $R=0.6$: roughly 5.0%, 2.6%, 2.0%, and 0.85%
- About 20% difference in the PbPb/pp ratio at $z_{||}=0.4 \sim 0.5$ with $R=0.4$ between SHELL and LBT models, and less than 10% when $z_{||} > 0.6$

## Context

This work extends the experimental measurement of $z_{||}$ distributions of $\rm{D^0}$ mesons in jets in p+p collisions by ALICE (2022) to theoretical predictions for Pb+Pb collisions. It builds on prior studies of heavy-flavor jet radial profiles by Wang et al. (2019, 2020) and the SHELL model framework developed by Dai et al. (2018) and Wang et al. (2020, 2021). The comparison of $\rm{D^0}$ and $\rm{B^0}$ jet modifications enables tests of the mass hierarchy of parton energy loss and the dead-cone effect, complementing the first direct observation of the dead-cone effect for charm quarks in vacuum by ALICE (2021).
