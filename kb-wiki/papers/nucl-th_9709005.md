# N N bar,Delta bar N, Delta N bar excitation for the pion propagator in nuclear matter

> The particle-hole and Delta -hole excitations are well-known elementary excitation modes for the pion propagator in nuclear matter. But, the excitation also involves antiparticles, namely, nucleon-antinucleon, anti-Delta-nucleon and Delta-antinucleon excitations. These are important for high-energy momentum as well, and have not been studied before, to our knowledge. In this paper, we give both the formulas and the numerical calculations for the real and the imaginary parts of these excitations.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first study of antiparticle excitations (nucleon-antinucleon, antidelta-nucleon, and delta-antinucleon) for the pion propagator in nuclear matter, which were previously ignored; however, the real part of these polarization insertions is divergent and nonrenormalizable under pseudo-vector coupling, requiring the introduction of an exponential form factor with a cutoff parameter to obtain finite results.

## Method

The authors calculated the imaginary part of the polarization insertion for the pion propagator in nuclear matter by decomposing the nucleon and delta-isobar propagators into particle, hole, and antiparticle components. They derived analytic expressions for the imaginary parts of the nucleon-antinucleon (N bar N), antidelta-nucleon (bar Delta N), and delta-antinucleon (Delta bar N) excitations using standard field theory with pseudo-vector coupling, then obtained the real parts via the dispersion relation with an exponential form factor F(q^2) = exp(-q^2/Lambda^2) for the time-like region.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon effective mass ratio used was x = m_tilde_N / m_N = 0.72, derived from relativistic Hartree approximation binding energy calculations
- The delta-isobar effective mass ratio was set to x_Delta = m_tilde_Delta / m_Delta = 1.0 for the first calculation
- The Fermi momentum used was k_F = 1.42 fm^{-1}
- The coupling constants used were f_{pi NN} = 0.988 and f_{pi N Delta} = 2 f_{pi NN}
- The cutoff parameter for the exponential form factor was Lambda = 6.8 m_pi
- In a second calculation with explicit pion and delta degrees of freedom, the effective mass ratios were x = 0.905 and x_Delta = 0.928 at k_F = 1.42 fm^{-1}
- The magnitude of Re Pi for N bar N excitation was about 10^2 larger than for bar Delta N and Delta bar N excitations

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous relativistic formalism for particle-hole and delta-hole excitations (Liu 1995, Liu and Nakano 1997) to include antiparticle contributions that had been ignored in all prior calculations, including those by Dmitriev and Suzuki (1985) and Herbert, Wehrberger, and Beck (1993). The results enable improved calculations of the pion propagator for high-energy-momentum regions relevant to dilepton production in heavy ion collisions (Helgesson and Randrup 1995), and the authors note that future work should include the natural width of the delta-isobar and compare different renormalization schemes.
