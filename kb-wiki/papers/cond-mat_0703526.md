# Local Density Functional Theory for Superfluid Fermionic Systems: The Unitary Gas

> The first detailed comparison between ab initio calculations of finite fermionic superfluid systems, performed recently by Chang and Bertsch [Phys. Rev. A 76, 021603(R), (2007)] and by von Stecher, Greene and Blume [e-print arXiv:00705:0671v1], and the extension of the density functional theory Superfluid Local Density Approximation (SLDA) is presented. It is shown that SLDA reproduces the total energies, number density distributions in inhomogeneous systems along with the energy of the normal state in homogeneous systems. Unlike the Kohn-Sham LDA, in SLDA the effective fermion mass differs from the bare fermion mass and the spectrum of elementary excitations is also reproduced.

## Key claim
The Superfluid Local Density Approximation (SLDA) reproduces total energies, number density distributions, and the quasiparticle spectrum of unitary fermionic systems when compared to ab initio calculations, provided that an effective fermion mass differing from the bare mass is introduced. The agreement is surprisingly good but the author notes that even better agreement would have been "bad news" due to several known limitations of the functional form.

## Method
The author constructs an SLDA energy density functional for the unitary Fermi gas (infinite scattering length) using three dimensionless parameters (α, β, γ) that are determined by fitting to ab initio results for homogeneous matter: the Bertsch parameter ξ_S (energy per particle) and the pairing gap η. The functional is then applied to finite systems in a harmonic oscillator trap using the Bogoliubov-de Gennes equations with a renormalization procedure for the divergent kinetic and anomalous densities, and the results are compared to Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) and Fixed-Node Diffusion Monte Carlo (FN-DMC) calculations from Chang and Bertsch (2007) and von Stecher, Greene and Blume (2007).

## Key numbers
- Using ξ_S = 0.42(2) and η = 0.504(24) from Carlson and Reddy, the SLDA parameters are α = 1.14, β = -0.553, and 1/γ = -0.0906, giving an effective mass m_eff/m = 0.877.
- Using ξ_S = 0.44 and η = 0.486 from Carlson et al., the parameters are α = 1.12, β = -0.520, and 1/γ = -0.0955.
- Using ξ_S = 0.449(9) and η = 0.442(3) from Juillet, the parameters are α = 0.812, β = -0.172, and 1/γ = -0.0705, giving m_eff/m = 1.23.
- For N=20 particles in a harmonic trap, the Thomas-Fermi energy is 38.05, compared to GFMC (43.2), FN-DMC (41.35), and SLDA (41.51) in units where ℏ=m=ω=1.
- The SLDA energy for N=1 is 1.37 ℏω instead of the exact 1.5 ℏω.
- The minimum of the quasiparticle spectrum occurs at k_0 = 0.906 k_F for the Carlson-Reddy parameters.

## Context
This work extends the density functional theory (DFT) framework to superfluid fermionic systems, building on the earlier SLDA formulation applied to nuclear binding and quantized vortices. It provides the first detailed comparison of SLDA with ab initio Monte Carlo calculations for finite superfluid systems at unitarity, specifically the GFMC results of Chang and Bertsch (2007) and the FN-DMC results of von Stecher, Greene and Blume (2007). The validation enables high-accuracy calculations of atomic nuclei, neutron matter in neutron stars, dilute atomic Fermi gases, and potentially high-Tc superconductivity using mean-field techniques instead of more expensive ab initio methods.
