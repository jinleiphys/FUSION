# Tensor correlation, pairing interaction and deformation in Ne isotopes and Ne hypernuclei

> We study tensor and pairing effects on the quadruple deformation of neon isotopes based on a deformed Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model with BCS approximation for the pairing channel. We extend the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock formalism for the description of single- and double-lambda hypernuclei adopting two different hyperon-nucleon interactions. It is found that the interplay of pairing and tensor interactions is crucial to derive the deformations in several neon isotopes. Especially, the shapes of $^{26,30}$Ne are studied in details in comparisons with experimentally observed shapes. Furthermore the deformations of the hypernuclei are compared with the corresponding neon isotopic cores in the presence of tensor force. We find the same shapes with somewhat smaller deformations for single $Λ$-hypernuclei compared with their core deformations. It is also pointed out that the latest version of hyperon interaction, the ESC08b model, having a deeper $Λ$ potential makes smaller deformations for hypernuclei than those of another NSC97f model.

# Tensor correlation, pairing interaction and deformation in Ne isotopes and Ne hypernuclei

## Key claim

The interplay of tensor and pairing interactions is crucial to reproduce the experimentally observed prolate deformations of neutron-rich neon isotopes, particularly $^{26}$Ne and $^{30}$Ne, within a deformed Skyrme-Hartree-Fock plus BCS model. However, the experimentally desired prolate shapes are only obtained when the pairing strength is weakened (to $V_{\text{pair}}^{\text{full}}/4$ for $^{30}$Ne) and a sufficiently large tensor coupling is included simultaneously; with the standard full pairing strength, even strong tensor forces fail to produce deformed minima for these nuclei.

## Method

The authors performed axially-symmetric deformed Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) calculations with a density-dependent contact pairing interaction treated in the BCS approximation, using 11 different Skyrme parameter sets (T14–T66) that include tensor terms with varying like-particle ($\alpha$) and proton-neutron ($\beta$) coupling strengths. For hypernuclei, they extended the SHF formalism by including an effective $\Lambda$-nucleon interaction derived from Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations using two Nijmegen hyperon-nucleon potentials: the NSC97f model and the newer ESC08b model. The deformation parameter $\beta_2$ was obtained by minimizing the total energy as a function of the quadrupole deformation.

## Key numbers

- For $^{26}$Ne with T64 ($\alpha = 120$ MeV, $\beta = 240$ MeV) and medium pairing ($V_{\text{pair}} = V_{\text{pair}}^{\text{full}}/2$), a prolate deformation of $\beta_2 = 0.087$ is obtained.
- For $^{30}$Ne with T62, T64, or T66 and weak pairing ($V_{\text{pair}} = V_{\text{pair}}^{\text{full}}/4$), a large prolate deformation of $\beta_2 \sim 0.35$ is achieved.
- The neutron $1f_{7/2}-1d_{3/2}$ gap is reduced from 4.49 MeV (T24) to 3.76 MeV (T64); the proton $1d_{5/2}-1p_{1/2}$ gap is reduced from 8.82 MeV to 7.73 MeV.
- The ESC08b potential gives a $\Lambda$ potential depth of $-40$ MeV in nuclear matter, compared to $-36$ MeV for the NSC97f potential.
- The full pairing strength used is $V_{\text{pair}} = 900$ MeV, with $\rho_0 = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$.

## Context

This work extends earlier deformed SHF studies of neon isotopes by Sagawa and Zhou (2004) using Skyrme forces SGII and SIII, which did not include tensor terms and gave spherical results for $^{30}$Ne. It also addresses the failure of Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations by Hilaire and Girod (2007) and relativistic HFB calculations by Ebran et al. (2011) to reproduce the experimentally observed large deformations of $^{26}$Ne and $^{30}$Ne. The tensor-force-driven deformation mechanism was previously proposed by Otsuka et al. (2008) for $^{42}$Si. The hypernuclear part builds on earlier mean-field studies of shape changes in $^{12}$C and $^{28,30,32}$Si hypernuclei by Zhou et al. (2007) and Win et al. (2008), and on the gluelike role of the $\Lambda$ particle in $p$-shell hypernuclei studied by Motoba et al. The results enable future improvements of Skyrme parameterizations with microscopically determined tensor strengths and isospin-dependent pairing forces, and motivate inclusion of beyond-mean-field effects such as particle-vibration coupling.
