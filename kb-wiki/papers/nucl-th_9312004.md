# Reply to "Comment on 'Comparison of potential models with the pp-scattering data below 350 MeV.' "

> Replying to the above Comment by G.Q.Li and R.Machleidt, we point out that the supposed "flaws" in our Comparison of NN potential models with the pp- scattering data [ Phys.Rev.C47,761(1993) ] are in reality not flaws at all.

## Key claim

The authors defend their earlier comparison of nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential models against pp scattering data, arguing that the supposed "flaws" identified by Li and Machleidt are not actually flaws, and that potentials fitted only to np data do not automatically give a good description of pp data even after adjusting the 1S0 partial wave.

## Method

The authors respond to two specific criticisms from Li and Machleidt: first, that comparing the I=1 part of an NN potential to pp data is invalid when the 1S0 partial wave was fitted to the np scattering length; second, that comparing only to pp data is itself a flaw. They demonstrate that the coordinate-space Bonn potentials (OBEPR, Bonn A, Bonn B) have poor chi-squared values when confronted with pp data, and show that replacing the 1S0 phase shifts with those from the Nijmegen partial-wave analysis does not fully resolve the discrepancy. They also argue that the real potential concept remains valid up to at least 350 MeV, contrary to Li and Machleidt's claim.

## Key numbers

- The coordinate-space Bonn OBEPR potential has chi-squared/N_data = 13.4 in the 2-350 MeV range; after replacing its 1S0 phase shift with the Nijmegen analysis, chi-squared/N_data drops to 8.1.
- Bonn A has chi-squared/N_data = 10.4; after 1S0 replacement, chi-squared/N_data = 6.8.
- Bonn B has chi-squared/N_data = 9.2; after 1S0 replacement, chi-squared/N_data = 5.5.
- The updated Nijmegen potential (Nijm92) describes all NN scattering data (pp and np) with chi-squared/N_data = 1.9.
- Including inelasticities from a 0-500 MeV pp partial-wave analysis changes chi-squared/N_data by about 0.0006, showing the real potential concept is valid to at least 350 MeV.

## Context

This reply extends the authors' earlier comparison (Stoks and de Swart, 1993) of NN potential models with pp scattering data, directly responding to a Comment by Li and Machleidt. The work contradicts the claim by Machleidt and collaborators that their Bonn potentials achieve an "excellent description" of the data, showing instead that the coordinate-space Bonn potentials (OBEPR, Bonn A, Bonn B) give worse fits than the 25-year-old Hamada-Johnston potential (1962). The authors also note that older potentials like Nijm78 (Nagels, Rijken, and de Swart, 1978) and Paris80 (Lacombe et al., 1980) still give good descriptions of pp data despite being fitted to older databases, and that the Nijmegen group has since produced an updated potential (Nijm92) that fits all NN data with chi-squared/N_data = 1.9.
