# A critical view on the deeply bound K^- pp system

> We briefly review the situation around the claimed deeply bound K^- states in different recent experiments and concentrate particularly on the state K^- pp advocated by the FINUDA collaboration in nuclear K^- absorption. We perform a theoretical simulation of the process and show that the peak in the Lambda p spectrum that was interpreted as a deep K^- pp bound state corresponds mostly to the process K^- p p --> Lambda p followed by final state interactions of the produced particles with the daughter nucleus.

## Key claim
The broad peak in the $\Lambda p$ invariant mass spectrum that the FINUDA collaboration interpreted as a deeply bound $K^- pp$ state with binding energy of 115 MeV is actually produced by the standard two-nucleon absorption process $K^- pp \rightarrow \Lambda p$ followed by final state interactions of the outgoing particles with the daughter nucleus, without requiring any exotic bound state.

## Method
The authors performed a Monte Carlo simulation of stopped kaon absorption on nuclei ($^6$Li, $^7$Li, $^{12}$C, $^{27}$Al, $^{51}$V) using a local density formalism where the kaon is absorbed from a specific atomic orbit by two nucleons from the local Fermi sea. The primary nucleon and $\Lambda$ produced in the $K^- pp \rightarrow \Lambda p$ and $K^- pn \rightarrow \Lambda n$ reactions are then allowed to undergo multiple re-scatterings with nucleons in the nucleus using experimental $NN$ cross sections and a $\Lambda N$ cross section taken as 2/3 of the nucleon-nucleon value, with the resulting $\Lambda p$ invariant mass distributions compared to the FINUDA data.

## Key numbers
- The survival probability for both $p$ and $\Lambda$ to exit $^{12}$C without any secondary collision is $P_s = 0.397$ for absorption from the $2p$ orbit and $P_s = 0.396$ for absorption from the $3d$ orbit.
- The survival probability in $^7$Li is $P_s = 0.56$, in $^6$Li $P_s = 0.59$, in $^{27}$Al $P_s = 0.26$, and in $^{51}$V $P_s = 0.18$.
- The estimated probability for forming the daughter nucleus in its ground state after $K^- pp$ absorption is smaller than about 15\% (combining a formation probability $\leq 0.3$ with the survival probability).
- The calculated quasielastic peak in $^{12}$C appears around $\sqrt{s} = 2260$ MeV, consistent with the broad peak in the FINUDA data.
- The binding energy correction $\Delta$ used in the model is 15.2 MeV for $^6$Li, 16.2 MeV for $^7$Li, 12.8 MeV for $^{12}$C, 10.7 MeV for $^{27}$Al, and 9.6 MeV for $^{51}$V.

## Context
This work extends the critical re-examination of deeply bound kaon states begun by Oset and Toki (2005), who showed that the peak observed at KEK by the experiment of Iwasaki et al. (1997) could be explained by the $K^- NN \rightarrow \Sigma p$ reaction with spectator nucleons rather than a deeply bound $K^-$ state. The present paper applies the same logic to the FINUDA data, contradicting the interpretation of the FINUDA collaboration (2005) and the theoretical predictions of deeply bound $K^-$ states by Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002) which used a potential about twenty times stronger than that from chiral unitary approaches. The results enable a conventional explanation of the FINUDA $\Lambda p$ spectrum without invoking exotic bound states, and suggest that separating data by target nucleus would help distinguish between the quasielastic interpretation and alternative bound-state interpretations such as that of Mares et al. (2006).
