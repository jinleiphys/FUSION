# E(5) and X(5) critical point symmetries obtained from Davidson potentials through a variational procedure

> Davidson potentials of the form $β^2 +β_0^4/β^2$, when used in the E(5) framework, bridge the U(5) and O(6) symmetries, while they bridge the U(5) and SU(3) symmetries when used in the X(5) framework. Using a variational procedure, we determine for each value of angular momentum $L$ thevalue of $β_0$ at which the rate of change of various physical quantities (energy ratios, intraband B(E2) ratios, quadrupole moment ratios) has a maximum, the collection of the values of the physical quantity formed in this way being a candidate for describing its behavior at the relevant critical point. Energy ratios lead to the E(5) and X(5) results (whice correspond to an infinite well potential in $β$), while intraband B(E2) ratios and quadrupole moments lead to the E(5)-$β^4$ and X(5)-$β^4$ results, which correspond to the use of a $β^4$ potential in the relevant framework. A new derivation of the Holmberg-Lipas formula for nuclear energy spectra is obtained as a by-product.

## Key claim

Davidson potentials of the form β² + β₀⁴/β², when used in the E(5) and X(5) frameworks with a variational procedure that maximizes the rate of change of physical quantities for each angular momentum L separately, yield energy ratios matching the infinite-well E(5) and X(5) results, while intraband B(E2) ratios and quadrupole moment ratios match the E(5)-β⁴ and X(5)-β⁴ results, indicating that the infinite well is optimal for energy ratios but not for electromagnetic transition ratios.

## Method

The authors inserted Davidson potentials u(β) = β² + β₀⁴/β² into the Bohr Hamiltonian within the E(5) framework (separable in β and γ, describing the U(5) to O(6) transition) and the X(5) framework (separable with u(β)+u(γ), describing the U(5) to SU(3) transition). For each angular momentum L, they varied β₀ and located the value where the first derivative of a given physical quantity (energy ratios, intraband B(E2) ratios, or quadrupole moment ratios) reached a maximum and the second derivative vanished; the collection of values at these β₀,m points was taken as the critical-point behavior. The resulting spectra and transition rates were compared to the infinite-well E(5) and X(5) models and to the E(5)-β⁴ and X(5)-β⁴ models.

## Key numbers

- For the E(5) framework, the variational procedure applied to energy ratios R_L within the ground state band produced values in very close agreement with the E(5) infinite-well results (e.g., R_4, R_12, R_20 values listed in Table 2 of the paper).
- For the E(5) framework, the variational procedure applied to intraband B(E2) ratios gave results close to the E(5)-β⁴ model (which uses u(β)=β⁴/2), for both the ground state band (n=0) and the n=1 band (Table 2, Figs. 3b and 3c).
- For the X(5) framework, the Davidson potential with β₀=8 gave R_L ratios approaching the SU(3) rigid-rotator values (e.g., R_4 = L(L+1)/6 = 3.333 for the rigid rotator; Table 3 shows the approach with increasing β₀).
- The Holmberg-Lipas formula E_H(L) = a_H(√(1+b_H L(L+1)) - 1) was derived as a by-product, with a_H=1 and b_H = 1/[3(9/4 + β₀⁴)].
- For β₀=0 in the X(5) framework, the X(5)-β² model is obtained with R_4 = 2.646.

## Context

This work extends the E(5) model of Iachello (2001) and the X(5) model of Iachello (2001) by replacing the infinite-well β-potential with a one-parameter Davidson potential that can interpolate between the U(5) and O(6) limits (in the E(5) framework) or between U(5) and SU(3) limits (in the X(5) framework). The variational procedure is analogous to the Variable Moment of Inertia (VMI) model of Mariscotti, Scharff-Goldhaber, and Buck (1969), where each L is treated separately. The results confirm that the infinite well is the optimal choice for energy ratios at the critical point, while B(E2) ratios favor a β⁴ potential, consistent with the earlier E(5)-β⁴ model of Arias (2001) and the X(5)-β⁴ model. The work also provides a new derivation of the Holmberg-Lipas formula (Holmberg and Lipas, 1968) for nuclear energy spectra.
