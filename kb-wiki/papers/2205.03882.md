# Gravitational Wave Signal for Quark Matter with Realistic Phase Transition

> The cores of neutron stars (NSs) near the maximum mass realize the most highly compressed matter in the universe where quark degrees of freedom may be liberated. Such a state of dense matter is hypothesized as quark matter (QM) and its presence has awaited to be confirmed for decades in nuclear physics. Gravitational waves from binary NS mergers are expected to convey useful information called the equation of state (EOS). However, the signature for QM with realistic EOS is not yet established. Here, we show that the gravitational wave in the post-merger stage can distinguish the theory scenarios with and without a transition to QM. Instead of adopting specific EOSs as studied previously, we compile reliable EOS constraints from the ab initio approaches. We demonstrate that early collapse to a black hole after NS merger signifies softening of the EOS associated with the onset of QM in accord with ab initio constraints. Nature of hadron-quark phase transition can be further constrained by the condition that electromagnetic counterparts need to be energized by the material left outside the remnant black hole.

## Key claim

The gravitational wave signal in the post-merger stage can distinguish between scenarios with and without a transition to quark matter, but only when the equation of state is constrained by ab initio approaches (chiral effective theory at low density and perturbative QCD at high density) rather than by adopting specific phenomenological EOSs as in prior studies.

## Method

The authors constructed two representative EOS scenarios: a smooth crossover to quark matter and a strong first-order phase transition at high density, both interpolated between the nuclear branch from N3LO chiral effective theory and the quark branch from perturbative QCD. They performed numerical relativity simulations of binary neutron star mergers using the SACRA code for equal-mass (1.375 Msun - 1.375 Msun) and unequal-mass (1.55 Msun - 1.2 Msun) systems consistent with GW170817, and analyzed the resulting gravitational waveforms and spectrograms from the post-merger stage.

## Key numbers

- The maximum rest-mass density in neutron stars can reach approximately 5 times the nuclear saturation density (n0 ~ 0.16 fm^-3) or higher
- The crossover scenario uses a transition density of rho_stiff = 1.6 rho0 and adiabatic index Gamma = 3.5
- The strong first-order transition uses critical pressure p_1st = 0.5 GeV/fm^3 with rho_stiff = 1.6 rho0 and Gamma = 3.5
- The first-order transition is insensitive to NS structure for p_1st greater than or approximately equal to 0.4 GeV/fm^3
- The transient NS collapses into a black hole at approximately 7-8 ms after the first bounce of merging NSs in the crossover scenario
- The total mass of GW170817 was 2.74 (+0.04/-0.01) solar masses (90% credibility)
- The associated kilonova AT 2017gfo requires ejection of approximately 0.05 solar masses

## Context

This work extends the line of research on quark matter in neutron stars initiated by Itoh (1970) and Collins and Perry (1974), and directly addresses the dispute between Ozel (2006) and Alford (2006) over whether EXO 0748-676 rules out quark matter. The authors incorporate the ab initio constraints from chiral effective theory (Drischler 2020) and perturbative QCD (Kurkela 2009, Gorda 2021) that prior merger simulations by Bauswein (2018), Most (2018), and Weih (2019) did not properly include. The results enable future multimessenger observations to constrain the location of the quark matter onset and the nature of the hadron-quark phase transition.
