# Deciphering QCD dynamics in small collision systems using event shape and final state multiplicity at the Large Hadron Collider

> The high-multiplicity pp collisions at the Large Hadron Collider energies with various heavy-ion-like signatures have warranted a deeper understanding of the underlying physics and particle production mechanisms. It is a common practice to use experimental data on the hadronic transverse momentum ($p_T$) spectra to extract thermodynamical properties of the system formed in heavy ion and high multiplicity pp collisions. The non-availability of event topology dependent experimental data for pp collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 13 TeV on the spectra of non-strange and strange hadrons constrains us to use the PYTHIA8 simulated numbers to extract temperature-like parameters to study the event shape and multiplicity dependence of specific heat capacity, conformal symmetry breaking measure (CSBM) and speed of sound. The observables show a clear dependence on event multiplicity and event topology. Thermodynamics of the system is largely governed by the light particles because of their relatively larger abundances. In this regards, a threshold in the particle production, $\rm N_{ch} \simeq$ (10-20) in the final state multiplicity emerges out from the present study, confirming some of the earlier findings in this direction. As for heavier hadrons with relatively small abundances, a similar threshold is observed for $\langle \rm N_{ch} \rangle \simeq$ 40 hinting towards formation of a thermal bath where all the heavier hadrons are in equilibrium.

## Key claim

Using PYTHIA8-simulated data for pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV (due to the non-availability of event-topology-dependent experimental data for non-strange and strange hadron spectra), the authors extract temperature-like parameters to study the event-shape and multiplicity dependence of specific heat capacity, conformal symmetry breaking measure, and speed of sound, finding a threshold in final-state multiplicity near N_ch ~ 10-20 for light particles and near <N_ch> ~ 40 for heavier hadrons, hinting at thermal bath formation.

## Method

The authors generated approximately 250 million pp collision events at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV using the PYTHIA8 event generator (version 8.235) with the Monash 2013 tune, including multipartonic interactions and color reconnection. They classified events by charged-particle multiplicity (V0M classes, ten bins from 0-9 to 50-140) and by transverse spherocity (S0) to separate jetty (low S0) and isotropic (high S0) event topologies. Using previously extracted Tsallis non-extensive distribution parameters (temperature T and non-extensive parameter q) from fits to identified particle pT spectra (pions, kaons, protons, K*0, Lambda), they computed specific heat capacity, conformal symmetry breaking measure (epsilon-3P)/T^4, and squared speed of sound via thermodynamic integrals.

## Key numbers

* Specific heat capacity for light flavor particles (pions) shows a clear dependence on event multiplicity and topology, with values ranging from approximately 0.092 GeV (jetty events, lowest multiplicity) to 0.167 GeV (isotropic events, highest multiplicity) for the temperature parameter T.
* A threshold in particle production emerges at N_ch ~ 10-20 for light particles, confirming earlier findings.
* For heavier hadrons (K*0, Lambda), a similar threshold is observed at <N_ch> ~ 40, hinting at formation of a thermal bath where all heavier hadrons are in equilibrium.
* The Tsallis non-extensive parameter q for pions ranges from 1.084 (isotropic events, lowest multiplicity) to 1.220 (jetty events, highest multiplicity).
* The squared speed of sound and conformal symmetry breaking measure are computed as functions of average charged-particle multiplicity for different event shapes and identified particles.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Tripathy et al. (2019) and Khuntia et al. (2018) that used event shape and multiplicity in pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV to study chemical and kinetic freeze-out scenarios and system thermodynamics. It also builds on the use of Tsallis non-extensive statistics to describe particle pT spectra in high-energy hadronic collisions, as established by Cleymans et al. (2011) and others. The findings enable further experimental investigations into possible QGP-droplet formation in small collision systems, as suggested by Sahoo et al. (2020, 2019), and provide a basis for comparing PYTHIA8 simulations with future event-topology-dependent experimental data from the LHC.
