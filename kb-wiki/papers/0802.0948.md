# Cluster decay in very heavy nuclei in Relativistic Mean Field

> Exotic cluster decay of very heavy nuclei has been studied in the microscopic Super-Asymmetric Fission Model. Relativistic Mean Field model with the force FSU Gold has been employed to obtain the densities of the cluster and the daughter nuclei. The microscopic nuclear interaction DDM3Y1, which has an exponential density dependence, and the Coulomb interaction have been used in the double folding model to obtain the potential between the cluster and the daughter. Half life values have been calculated in the WKB approximation and the spectroscopic factors have been extracted. The latter values are seen to have a simple dependence of the mass of the cluster as has been observed earlier. Predictions have been made for some possible decays.

# Cluster decay in very heavy nuclei in Relativistic Mean Field

## Key claim

The authors show that microscopic densities from Relativistic Mean Field theory with the FSU Gold force, combined with the DDM3Y1 interaction in a double folding model, can reproduce experimental cluster decay half-lives and spectroscopic factors, with the spectroscopic factors following a simple linear dependence on cluster mass number as previously observed. The predictive power is limited by the need to use experimental Q-values rather than theoretical ones, as the decay probability changes very rapidly with Q-value.

## Method

The authors employed the microscopic Super-Asymmetric Fission Model (SAFM) using densities obtained from Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) calculations with the FSU Gold parameter set, solved in coordinate space with BCS pairing. The nuclear potential between the cluster and daughter nucleus was obtained via the double folding model using the DDM3Y1 interaction (which has an exponential density dependence) together with the Coulomb interaction, and half-lives were calculated in the WKB approximation. Spectroscopic factors were extracted as the ratio of calculated to experimental half-lives, and a linear fit of log10(S) versus cluster mass number was used to predict half-lives for unmeasured decays.

## Key numbers

- Spectroscopic factor S for 212Po: 1.88×10⁻² (calculated), compared to 2.5×10⁻² from prior theoretical work and 3.1×10⁻² from Mohr's double folding calculation using experimental charge distributions
- Best-fit Sα value for even-even parent-daughter decays: 1.93×10⁻², comparable to 1.61×10⁻² obtained by Poenaru et al.
- Best-fit Sα value for odd-mass parent-daughter decays: 1.35×10⁻²
- Pairing strength used in RMF+BCS: 300 MeV·fm for both protons and neutrons
- DDM3Y1 interaction parameters: C=0.2845, α=3.6391, β=2.9605 fm²

## Context

This work extends the Super-Asymmetric Fission Model approach of Basu (using phenomenological densities with M3Y interaction) and the RMF-based calculations of Bhagwat and Gambhir (using the NL3 force) by employing the FSU Gold Lagrangian density and the DDM3Y1 interaction with exponential density dependence. The DDM3Y1 interaction was previously used by Khoa to study alpha-nucleus scattering and is consistent with low-energy scattering in the double folding approach. The results enable predictions of half-lives for unmeasured cluster decays, though the authors note that except for 233U, their predictions are consistent with experimental observations.
