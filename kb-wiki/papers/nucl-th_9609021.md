# New Insights into the Coupling of $η$ and $f_1$ Mesons to the Nucleon

> We show that the contact couplings of neutral pseudoscalar and axial mesons to the nucleon are proportional to $Δs$, the fraction of nucleon spin carried by the strange quark sea, and thus are strongly suppressed relative to the couplings of charged mesons to the nucleon. On the other side, recent high accuracy data on $η$ photoproduction at threshold reveal the need for non--negligibile $ηNN$ vertices, while fitting $\bar N N$ phase shifts by means of effective meson exchange potentials requires a substantial presence of $f_1$ mesons there. We here advocate the idea to attribute the couplings of neutral pseudoscalar and axial vector mesons to effective triangular diagrams containig non--strange mesons and demonstrate its usefulness in describing available data.

## Key claim

The contact couplings of neutral pseudoscalar (eta) and axial vector (f1) mesons to the nucleon are proportional to Delta s, the fraction of nucleon spin carried by the strange quark sea, and are therefore suppressed by a factor of about 0.1 relative to charged meson couplings; however, the authors argue that the observed non-negligible eta NN and f1 NN couplings arise not from direct contact terms but from effective triangular diagrams containing non-strange mesons.

## Method

The authors evaluated the neutral axial vector current of the nucleon within the Glashow-Weinberg-Salam theory and showed that the isosinglet part coincides with the strange axial vector current, making eta NN and f1 NN vertices proportional to Delta s. They then computed the eta NN coupling constant from an a0(980) pi N triangle diagram using monopole vertex factors with cutoff parameters, and similarly computed f1(1285)NN and f1(1420)NN couplings from effective triangular diagrams involving non-strange mesons.

## Key numbers

- Delta s = -0.13 +/- 0.04, the fraction of nucleon spin carried by the strange quark sea
- Suppression factor of eta NN and f1 NN couplings relative to charged meson couplings: Delta s / g_A ~ 0.1, where g_A = 1.26
- Pseudoscalar eta NN coupling constant at q^2 = 0: g_eta NN = 2.03, with g_eta NN^2 / 4pi = 0.33
- Pseudovector eta NN coupling constant at q^2 = 0: f_eta NN = 0.58, with f_eta NN^2 / 4pi = 0.03
- a0(980) NN coupling constant: g_a0 NN^2 / 4pi = 0.77 (smallest value from the Bonn potential)
- a0 to pi + eta decay coupling constant: f_a0 eta pi = 0.4, corresponding to a decay width of 50 MeV
- Cutoff parameters: Lambda_pi = 1.05 GeV, Lambda_a0 = 2.0 GeV
- f1(1285) NN coupling constant: g_f1(1285)NN = 1.46 at q^2 = 27 fm^-2
- f1(1420) NN coupling constant: g_f1(1420)NN = 10.5, using a ratio of 4.5 between tensor and vector couplings of K*(892) mesons in the K*(892) Lambda N vertex
- Mixing angle for f1 mesons: theta approximately 17 degrees

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Jaffe (1990s) on the role of the strange quark sea in nucleon spin structure and contradicts the constituent quark model predictions for neutral meson-nucleon couplings, which the authors show are substantially overestimated. The paper builds on prior work by Kirchbach and Tiator (1990s) on triangular diagram mechanisms, and by Kirchbach and Riska (1990s) on f1 meson couplings, to provide a unified description of eta and f1 couplings that can describe high-accuracy eta photoproduction data at threshold from Kruger (1990s) and the substantial f1 meson presence required in antinucleon-nucleon phase shift analyses by Timmermans, Beyer, and Kamalov (1990s) and by Mull (1990s). This enables a consistent treatment of neutral meson exchange in nucleon-nucleon potentials and photoproduction reactions.
