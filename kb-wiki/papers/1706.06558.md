# Constructing probability density function of net-proton multiplicity distributions using Pearson curve method

> The probability density functions of proton, anti-proton, and net-proton multiplicity distributions are constructed from the Beam Energy Scan results of the STAR experiment using the Pearson curve method. The constructed distributions of proton and anti-proton are compared with Poisson and Binomial distributions. The net-proton probability distributions are compared with Skellam distributions to study the O(4) criticality near the chiral crossover transition. The $C_{6}/C_{2}$ results estimated from the obtained PDFs are compared with Skellam and Binomial baselines for the Beam Energy Scan data. The current study shows some signatures of O(4) criticality, which can be further investigated by precision measurements of the cumulants and understanding the contribution of non-critical fluctuations to them. This study also provides a baseline for the higher-order cumulant measurement in the upcoming RHIC BES II program and future LHC run.

## Key claim

The study uses the Pearson curve method to construct probability density functions of net-proton multiplicity distributions from efficiency-corrected cumulant data, finding "some signatures of O(4) criticality" near the chiral crossover transition, but qualifies that these signatures require further investigation through precision measurements and understanding of non-critical fluctuations.

## Method

The authors applied the Pearson curve method to construct probability density functions from the first four cumulants (mean, variance, skewness, kurtosis) of proton, anti-proton, and net-proton multiplicity distributions measured by the STAR experiment in Au-Au collisions at beam energies from 7.7 to 200 GeV. They derived the functional form of each distribution by solving the Pearson differential equation with parameters determined from the experimental cumulant values, then compared the constructed net-proton distributions with Skellam distributions and estimated C6/C2 ratios from the obtained PDFs.

## Key numbers

- For Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, the constructed proton multiplicity distribution is a shifted and rescaled Beta distribution with parameters a = -11.02, b = 140.058, alpha = 39.69, beta = 161.193
- The N6 values (maximum |Delta N_p| needed to reproduce C6 from Skellam distribution) range from 67 at sqrt(s_NN) = 14.5 GeV down to 44 at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV
- For anti-proton at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, the SR Beta distribution parameters are a = -7.926, b = 100.635, alpha = 28.494, beta = 110.911
- The net-proton PDF at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV is a type 4 Pearson curve with parameters given in equation (pdf7hpt): f(x) = (1/sqrt(pi)) * exp(1186.02 + 180.332 * arctanh(0.184 + 0.012x)) / (7445.21 + 31.301x + x^2)^139.926

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Morita et al. (2013, 2014) who demonstrated that O(4) criticality near the chiral crossover transition can be studied through net-baryon probability distributions, and who previously analyzed efficiency-uncorrected STAR data. The study provides a baseline for higher-order cumulant measurements in the upcoming RHIC BES II program and future LHC runs, and addresses the need for efficiency-corrected probability distributions that were not previously available from experiment.
