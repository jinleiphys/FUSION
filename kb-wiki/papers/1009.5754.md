# Compositeness of bound states in chiral unitary approach

> We study the structure of dynamically generated bound states in the chiral unitary approach. The compositeness of a bound state is defined through the wavefunction renormalization constant in the nonrelativistic field theory. We apply this argument to the chiral unitary approach and derive the relation between compositeness of the bound state and the subtraction constant of the loop integral. The compositeness condition is fairly compatible with the natural renormalization scheme, previously introduced in a different context.

## Key claim

The compositeness (molecular component) of a dynamically generated bound state in the chiral unitary approach is shown to be directly related to the subtraction constant of the loop integral, with the natural renormalization scheme yielding a nearly pure composite state (Z ≈ 0) for small binding energies, but this result relies on the small-binding approximation and the specific choice of interaction Hamiltonian.

## Method

The authors define compositeness 1−Z through the field renormalization constant Z in nonrelativistic field theory following Weinberg (1965), then derive an expression for the compositeness in terms of the scattering amplitude. They apply this formalism to the chiral unitary approach, where the s-wave scattering amplitude is obtained via the N/D method using the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction, and derive an analytic formula for 1−Z in the small binding limit that depends on the subtraction constant a and the bound state mass M_B.

## Key numbers

- For binding energy B = 10 MeV with the natural subtraction constant a_natural, the compositeness 1−Z is close to 1 (Z ≈ 0), indicating a nearly pure composite (molecular) state.
- For B ≳ 25 MeV with the natural subtraction constant, the quantity 1−Z exceeds 1, signaling breakdown of the small-binding approximation.
- Particle masses used in the numerical calculation: m = 495 MeV (meson mass) and M = 939 MeV (target hadron mass).
- The coupling constant g(M_B;a) is computed from the pole residue as g^2 = −(M_B−M)/[G(M_B;a) + (M_B−M)G′(M_B)], where G is the loop function with subtraction constant a.

## Context

This work extends the compositeness analysis of Weinberg (1965) to the chiral unitary approach, which has been used to dynamically generate resonances such as Λ(1405), a0(980), and f0(980) as hadronic molecules (Kaiser 1995, Oset 1998, Oller 2000, Lutz 2001). The natural renormalization scheme was previously introduced by Hyodo (2008) to exclude CDD pole contributions from the loop function. The results support using the natural renormalization condition to identify the origin of dynamically generated resonances, and the authors note that a relativistic formulation of compositeness is needed for future work.
