# Relativistic description of BCS-BEC crossover in nuclear matter

> We study theoretically the di-neutron spatial correlations and the crossover from superfluidity of neutron Cooper pairs in the $^1S_0$ pairing channel to Bose-Einstein condensation (BEC) of di-neutron pairs for both symmetric and neutron matter in the microscopic relativistic pairing theory. We take the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction Bonn-B in the particle-particle channel and the effective interaction PK1 of the relativistic mean-field approach in the particle-hole channel. It is found that the spatial structure of neutron Cooper pair wave function evolves continuously from BCS-type to BEC-type as density decreases. We see a strong concentration of the probability density revealed for the neutron pairs in the fairly small relative distance around $1.5 {\rm fm}$ and the neutron Fermi momentum $k_{Fn}\in[0.6,1.0] {\rm fm^{-1}}$. However, from the effective chemical potential and the quasiparticle excitation spectrum, there is no evidence for the appearance of a true BEC state of neutron pairs at any density. The most BEC-like state may appear at $k_{Fn}\thicksim0.2 {\rm fm^{-1}}$ by examining the density correlation function. From the coherence length and the probability distribution of neutron Cooper pairs as well as the ratio between the neutron pairing gap and the kinetic energy at the Fermi surface, some features of the BCS-BEC crossover are seen in the density regions, $0.05 {\rm fm^{-1}}<k_{Fn}<0.7 (0.75) {\rm fm^{-1}}$, for the symmetric nuclear (pure neutron) matter.

## Key claim

Using the realistic bare nucleon-nucleon interaction Bonn-B in the particle-particle channel, the spatial structure of neutron Cooper pairs evolves continuously from BCS-type to BEC-type as density decreases, but a true di-neutron BEC bound state does not occur at any density in nuclear matter; the system is only in the transition region between BCS and BEC regimes.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (RHFB) equation for infinite symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter in the \(^1S_0\) pairing channel, using the relativistic Bonn-B potential as the bare interaction in the particle-particle channel and the PK1 effective interaction from relativistic mean-field theory in the particle-hole channel. The Cooper pair wave function, effective chemical potential, quasiparticle excitation spectrum, density correlation function, coherence length, and the ratio of the pairing gap to Fermi kinetic energy are computed self-consistently as functions of neutron Fermi momentum \(k_{Fn}\).

## Key numbers

- The probability density of neutron Cooper pairs is strongly concentrated at a relative distance around \(1.5\ \mathrm{fm}\) for neutron Fermi momenta \(k_{Fn}\in[0.6,1.0]\ \mathrm{fm^{-1}}\).
- The most BEC-like state appears at \(k_{Fn}\sim 0.2\ \mathrm{fm^{-1}}\), based on the density correlation function.
- The BCS-BEC crossover region is identified for \(0.05\ \mathrm{fm^{-1}} < k_{Fn} < 0.7\ \mathrm{fm^{-1}}\) in symmetric nuclear matter and for \(0.05\ \mathrm{fm^{-1}} < k_{Fn} < 0.75\ \mathrm{fm^{-1}}\) in pure neutron matter.
- The coherence length \(\xi_{\mathrm{rms}}\) shrinks to \(\sim 5\ \mathrm{fm}\) in the density range \(0.4\ \mathrm{fm^{-1}} < k_{Fn} < 0.8\ \mathrm{fm^{-1}}\) (symmetric matter) or \(0.4\ \mathrm{fm^{-1}} < k_{Fn} < 0.9\ \mathrm{fm^{-1}}\) (pure neutron matter).
- The probability \(P(r)\) for pair partners within \(r=3\ \mathrm{fm}\) reaches a maximum of about \(0.75\) around \(k_{Fn}=0.75\ \mathrm{fm^{-1}}\).
- The ratio \(\Delta_{Fn}/e_{Fn}\) enters the BCS-BEC crossover region at \(k_{Fn}\sim 0.75\ \mathrm{fm^{-1}}\) for symmetric matter and \(k_{Fn}\sim 0.8\ \mathrm{fm^{-1}}\) for pure neutron matter.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of di-neutron correlations and BCS-BEC crossover in nuclear matter that used phenomenological forces such as the finite-range Gogny interaction or zero-range contact interactions (Matsuo 2006, Margueron 2007, Mao 2009). By employing the realistic bare Bonn-B interaction, the authors provide a more microscopic check of whether a true di-neutron BEC state can form, finding that it does not, in contrast to some earlier screened-interaction results. The results enable future studies of di-neutron correlations in finite neutron-rich nuclei and clarify the density regime where BCS-BEC crossover features appear in uniform matter.
