# Production of QED pairs at small impact parameter in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> The STAR collaboration at RHIC is measuring the production of electron-positron pairs at small impact parameters, larger than but already close to the range, where the ions interact strongly with each other. We calculate the total cross section, as well as, differential distributions of the pair production process with the electromagnetic excitation of both ions in a semiclassical approach and within a lowest order QED calculation. We compare the distribution of electron and positron with the one coming from the cross section calculation without restriction on impact parameter. Finally we give an outlook of possible results at the LHC.

# Production of QED pairs at small impact parameter in relativistic heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The authors calculate that the transverse momentum distribution of electron-positron pairs produced in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions with simultaneous electromagnetic excitation of both ions (triggering on giant dipole resonance excitation) differs most significantly from the unrestricted cross section, but that for the STAR experimental conditions at RHIC the data show no sign that higher order Coulomb effects are large for pairs produced with large transverse momenta.

## Method

The authors use a semiclassical approach in lowest order QED, calculating the differential probability for pair production via two Feynman diagrams with the external Coulomb fields of both ions, combined with the probability for giant dipole resonance (GDR) excitation of each ion (approximated as P_GDR(b) = S/b^2). The cross section is obtained by integrating over impact parameter b from b_min = 2R_a (where the nuclei touch) to infinity, using a two-dimensional Fourier transform of the impact-parameter-dependent pair production probability and a monopole nuclear form factor with Lambda^2 = 6/<R^2> ≈ 80 MeV. The six-dimensional phase-space integration over electron and positron momenta is performed with the VEGAS Monte Carlo method.

## Key numbers

- Total pair production cross section for AuAu collisions at RHIC with STAR kinematic restrictions (p_t > 60 MeV/c, |y| < 1.15): 2.30 mb for b_min = 13 fm, 1.76 mb for b_min = 14 fm, 1.43 mb for b_min = 15 fm
- Average impact parameter for the triggered process: approximately 19 fm (derived from 8R_a/3 with R_a ≈ 7 fm)
- Total pair production multiplicity in ultraperipheral collisions: about 1.5 for AuAu at RHIC and about 3.9 for PbPb at the LHC
- GDR excitation probability at b_min: approximately 0.5
- For ALICE/LHC conditions with p_t > 2.6 MeV/c and |y| < 1.5, the probability for pair production at impact parameters close to b_min is approximately 20%, and about 10% of all events are multiple pair production events
- The S parameter for GDR excitation: S = 5.45 × 10^(-5) Z^3 N A^(-2/3) fm^2

## Context

This work extends the lowest-order QED calculations of pair production in ultraperipheral collisions by Hencken, Baur, and Trautmann (1995) to the specific case where both ions are simultaneously excited to the giant dipole resonance, as measured by the STAR collaboration at RHIC using a trigger based on forward neutron detection in the Zero Degree Calorimeter. The approach uses the semiclassical impact-parameter-dependent equivalent photon approximation developed by Baur (1990) and others, and the GDR excitation probability follows the formalism of Bertulani and Baur (1988). The results enable comparison with STAR data (Klein, 2004; Meissner, 2003; Morozov, 2003) and provide predictions for future measurements at the LHC with ALICE, where the lower transverse momentum cutoff of the Internal Trigger System (ITS) should allow observation of multiple pair production, a phenomenon not accessible at RHIC due to the low pair production probability (a few permille) under the available kinematic conditions.
