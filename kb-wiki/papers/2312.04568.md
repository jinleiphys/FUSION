# Multimodality of $^{187}$Ir fission studied by Langevin approach

> [Background] The fission mechanism of sub-lead nuclides remains unclear, especially the types of fission modes involved and their corresponding shell effects. [Purpose] The aim is to identify the different modes in the fission of $^{187}$Ir, and investigate the corresponding mechanism. [Method] The three-dimensional Langevin approach considering nucleus elongation, deformation, and mass asymmetry is applied to simulate fission dynamics. The macro-microscopic models are used to calculate the transport coefficients. [Results] The fragment mass, deformation, and total kinetic energy (TKE) of $^{187}$Ir fission at different excitation energies are calculated. Based on the mass-TKE correlations, four fission modes are identified, namely two asymmetric standard modes, a symmetric super-long mode, and a symmetric liquid-drop mode. Strong excitation-energy resistance of two asymmetric modes is found. The mass distributions show the dominance of single-peak shape, which is in good agreement with experimental data. The fission potential energy surface and the fission dynamics are analyzed to investigate the origins of the modes and the competition between neutron and proton shell effects. [Conclusions] Multiple fission modes are included in the $^{187}$Ir fission behind the single-peak-like distribution of observables. The proton and neutron magic numbers with different asymmetry parameter might heighten the sensitivity to the uncertainties of shell corrections.

## Key claim

The single-peak mass distributions observed experimentally in $^{187}$Ir fission actually conceal four distinct fission modes (two asymmetric standard modes, a symmetric super-long mode, and a symmetric liquid-drop mode), which can only be disentangled through mass-TKE correlations rather than mass distributions alone.

## Method

A three-dimensional Langevin approach was applied with generalized coordinates representing nucleus elongation ($z_0/R_0$), deformation ($\delta$), and mass asymmetry ($\eta$). The macro-microscopic model with Strutinsky shell corrections from the two-center shell model was used to calculate the fission potential energy surface, and the Werner-Wheeler approximation for inertia tensors with one-body wall-and-window friction was employed for transport coefficients. Langevin trajectories were evolved from the ground-state potential minimum until the scission point (neck radius less than 2 fm), and a two-dimensional six-Gaussian fitting of fragment mass and relative TKE was used to extract mode proportions.

## Key numbers

- Excitation energies studied: 30.2, 32.8, 36.7, 40.8, 43.3, and 44.8 MeV
- Fission barrier of $^{187}$Ir: 20 MeV
- Asymmetric mode S1 at fragment mass ratio $A_L/A_H$ = 84/103 with deformation $\delta$ = 0 (spherical)
- Asymmetric mode S2 at $A_L/A_H$ = 63/124
- Symmetric super-long (SL) mode with deformation $\delta$ = 0.15 (oblate)
- Calculated average TKE values range from 130.5 MeV (at 30.2 MeV excitation) to 133.8 MeV (at 44.8 MeV excitation)
- Experimental average TKE values: 130.9 $\pm$ 0.5 MeV (36.7 MeV), 130.1 $\pm$ 0.3 MeV (40.8 MeV), 129.6 $\pm$ 0.4 MeV (43.3 MeV), 130.7 $\pm$ 0.4 MeV (44.8 MeV)
- Inertia tensor element $m_{\eta,\eta}$ is about one order of magnitude higher than $m_{\delta,\delta}$ in the saddle-to-scission region
- Damping parameter of shell correction $E_d$ = 28 MeV
- Neck parameter $\epsilon$ fixed at 0.24

## Context

This work extends the multimodality analysis of sub-lead fission previously applied to $^{178}$Pt by Swinton-Bland et al. (2023) using two-dimensional fitting of mass and TKE, and addresses discrepancies between theoretical predictions (which gave double-peak mass distributions) and experimental measurements by Dhuri et al. (2022) on $^{187}$Ir fission. The identification of four fission modes in $^{187}$Ir, including the strong excitation-energy resistance of asymmetric modes S1 and S2, enables future studies of how proton and neutron shell effects compete in sub-lead fission and how the sensitivity to shell correction uncertainties may be heightened by the interplay of magic numbers at different asymmetry parameters.
