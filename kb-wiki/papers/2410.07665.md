# Quantum Hall liquids in high-density QCD

> There exist metastable domain walls of the flavor-singlet meson $Œ∑$ for the ${\rm U}(1)$ axial symmetry in two-flavor color superconductivity (2SC) in QCD at large baryon density. We show that, due to the coupling of $Œ∑$ to confined ${\rm SU}(2)$ gluons in the 2SC phase, the effective theory on the domain wall is described by the ${\rm SU}(2)_{-1}$ Chern-Simons theory, which is dual to the ${\rm U}(1)_{2}$ Chern-Simons theory. This theory has a spin-1 droplet excitation that does not carry a baryon number, which we identify as a vector meson. We also discuss the effective theories and baryonic droplet excitations on the domain walls of the flavor-singlet mesons in the superfluid phases of QCD at large isospin density and two-color QCD at large baryon density.

## Key claim

The authors show that metastable domain walls of the flavor-singlet meson Œ∑ in the two-flavor color superconductivity (2SC) phase of QCD at high baryon density host quantum Hall states described by the SU(2)_{-1} Chern-Simons theory, which is dual to the U(1)_{2} Chern-Simons theory. Unlike the analogous scenario in the QCD vacuum, this description does not require the large-N_c limit and is under theoretical control for real N_c=3 QCD, provided the density is sufficiently high that Œ∑ is a light pseudo-Nambu-Goldstone mode.

## Method

The authors start from the low-energy effective theory for the Œ∑ meson in the 2SC phase, which includes a topological coupling of Œ∑ to the confined SU(2)_c gluon sector via the QCD anomaly term. By integrating this coupling across the Œ∑ domain wall (where Œ∑ changes by 2œÄ), they derive a (2+1)-dimensional SU(2)_{-1} Chern-Simons theory on the wall. They then apply level-rank duality to map this to a U(1)_{2} Chern-Simons theory, analyze the edge modes of a finite droplet using the Wen (1992) gauge-fixing procedure, and compute the spin and baryon number of the resulting quantum Hall droplet. The same method is extended to QCD at large isospin density and two-color QCD at large baryon density.

## Context

This work extends the scenario of Komargodski (2018), who interpreted spin-N_c/2 baryons in the QCD vacuum as quantum Hall droplets in the large-N_c limit, to the color superconducting phase at high density where the large-N_c limit is not needed. Unlike the vacuum case, where the Œ∑ meson is heavy due to the QCD anomaly and the cusp singularity dominates the domain wall tension, in the 2SC phase the instanton effects are suppressed (Son 2000, Son 2001), making Œ∑ a light pseudo-Nambu-Goldstone mode and the cusp contribution negligible. The results enable the study of higher-spin hadrons (vector mesons and baryons) in dense QCD phases, which had not been previously understood, and may help clarify the possible continuity between nuclear matter and color superconducting quark matter.
