# The Effect of Deformation on the Twist Mode

> Using $^{12}$C as an example of a strongly deformed nucleus we calculate the strengths and energies in the asymptotic (oblate) deformed limit for the isovector twist mode operator $[rY^{1}\vec{l}]^{λ=2}t_{+}$ where l is the orbital angular momentum. We also consider the $λ=1$ case. For $λ=0$, the operator vanishes. Whereas in a $ΔN=0$ Nilsson model the summed strength is independent of the relative P$_{3/2}$ and P$_{1/2}$ occupancy when we allow for different frequencies $ω_{i}$ in the x, y, and z directions there is a weak dependency on deformation.

# The Effect of Deformation on the Twist Mode

## Key claim

Using deformed harmonic oscillator wavefunctions rather than the earlier ΔN=0 Nilsson model, the summed strength of the λ=2 twist mode in ¹²C increases by only 30% when going from the spherical to the asymptotic oblate limit, in contrast to the factor-of-three increase seen for the scissors mode; however, the main effect of deformation is to redistribute the strength across multiple excitation energies rather than to dramatically increase the total strength.

## Method

The authors calculated the strengths and excitation energies of the isovector twist mode operator [rY¹l]^{λ=2}t₊ and the corresponding λ=1 mode for ¹²C in the asymptotic oblate deformed limit, using deformed harmonic oscillator wavefunctions with Mottelson conditions to determine the deformation parameters (ω_x = ω_y = 12.651 MeV, ω_z = 21.086 MeV). They computed the ΔN=1 and ΔN=3 contributions to the summed strength and compared these to the spherical oscillator limit, and also derived analytic expansions in the deformation parameter δ up to second order.

## Key numbers

- For the λ=2 twist mode, the summed ΔN=1 strength 4π Strength/̄b₀² increases from 30 (spherical) to 35.558 (deformed), a 30% increase
- The λ=2 ΔN=1 strength is split into three components: 12.520 at 12.651 MeV, 18.259 at 21.086 MeV, and 4.779 at 29.521 MeV (units: 4π Strength/̄b₀²)
- The λ=2 ΔN=3 strength appears at 46.388 MeV (2.372) and 54.823 MeV (0.854), totaling 3.226, compared to zero in the spherical limit
- For the λ=1 mode, the summed ΔN=1 strength increases from 18 (spherical) to 20.156 (deformed), with components 9.675 at 12.651 MeV, 5.928 at 21.086 MeV, and 4.553 at 29.521 MeV
- The energy-weighted summed strength for λ=2 is 854.136 MeV in the deformed limit versus 450 MeV in the spherical limit
- The energy-weighted summed strength for λ=1 is 516.622 MeV in the deformed limit versus 270 MeV in the spherical limit
- The deformation parameter δ for ¹²C is -0.55814 (oblate)
- The oscillator frequencies are ħω_x = 12.651 MeV, ħω_z = 21.086 MeV, with ħ̄ω₀ = 15 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous ΔN=0 Nilsson model calculations by Zamick and Auerbach (1999) and Robinson, Zamick, Mekjian, and Auerbach (2000), which found summed strengths independent of deformation for various isovector dipole modes. The authors show that removing the ΔN=0 restriction introduces a weak deformation dependence, in contrast to the scissors mode operator (L_π - L_ν), which shows a factor-of-three increase in summed strength for ¹²C. The results enable comparison with experimental attempts to separate orbital M2 from spin M2 strength in nuclei such as ⁴⁸Ca and ⁹⁰Zr, as performed by von Neumann-Cosel et al. (1999).
