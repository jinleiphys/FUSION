# Electromagnetic response and break-up of light weakly-bound nuclei in a dicluster model

> The light weakly-bound nucleus $^7$Li is studied within a dicluster $α+t$ picture. Different observables obtained within our simple model are compared with previous calculations and experiments showing good agreement. In particular we calculate dipole and quadrupole electromagnetic response to the continuum. The energy distribution of B(E$λ$) values are consistent with the energy weighted molecular sum rule and display a sizable contribution of non-resonant character arising from the weak binding property. The corresponding form factors for excitations to the continuum are used in a semiclassical coupled channel scheme to get estimates for the break-up cross section in a heavy ion reaction. The nuclear contribution is found to play an important role in the process for bombarding energies around the Coulomb barrier. The masses and charges ratios of the two clusters are shown to lead to features of the cluster halo that may significantly differ from the one usually associated with one-nucleon haloes.

# Electromagnetic response and break-up of light weakly-bound nuclei in a dicluster model

## Key claim

The dicluster α+t model for ⁷Li reproduces the electromagnetic response to the continuum, including both resonant and non-resonant contributions, and shows that the nuclear quadrupole mechanism dominates the breakup cross section for systems with two clusters of similar size and charge, contrary to the usual expectation that dipole transitions are dominant.

## Method

The authors describe ⁷Li as a frozen α cluster and triton cluster interacting via a Woods-Saxon potential plus Coulomb and spin-orbit terms, with the depth adjusted to reproduce the two bound states. They calculate dipole (E1) and quadrupole (E2) transition strengths to continuum states by integrating the radial wavefunctions of the relative cluster-cluster motion. The resulting form factors are used in a semiclassical coupled-channel scheme to compute breakup cross sections for the reactions ⁷Li + ¹⁶⁵Ho and ⁷Li + ²⁰⁸Pb, including both Coulomb and nuclear contributions.

## Key numbers

- Ground state binding energy: −2.467 MeV relative to the α−t breakup threshold
- First excited state (1/2⁻) energy: −1.989 MeV relative to threshold
- Charge radius: 2.44 fm (experimental: 2.55 ± 0.07 fm)
- Electric quadrupole moment: −3.77 fm² (experimental: −3.70 ± 0.08 fm²)
- B(E2, 3/2⁻ → 1/2⁻): 7.55 e²fm⁴ (experimental: 8.3 ± 0.6 e²fm⁴)
- B(M1, 3/2⁻ → 1/2⁻): 2.45 μ² (experimental: 2.50 ± 0.12 μ²)
- Width of 7/2⁻ resonance: ~110 keV (experimental: 93 ± 8 keV)
- Width of 5/2⁻ resonance: ~930 keV (experimental: 875 +200/−100 keV)
- Low-lying dipole strength exhausts 2.6% of the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn sum rule and 94% of the energy weighted molecular dipole sum rule
- Quadrupole strength is 9.2% of the energy weighted quadrupole sum rule and about 42% of the EWMSR
- Total Coulomb breakup cross section for ⁷Li + ¹⁶⁵Ho at E_cm = 40 MeV: ~4.85 mb (dipole ~3.0 mb, quadrupole ~1.85 mb)
- Total nuclear breakup cross section for ⁷Li + ¹⁶⁵Ho at E_cm = 40 MeV: ~24.1 mb (dipole ~1.3 mb, quadrupole ~22.8 mb)
- Total (Coulomb + nuclear + interference) breakup cross section for ⁷Li + ¹⁶⁵Ho at E_cm = 40 MeV: ~29.0 mb (dipole ~6.9 mb, quadrupole ~22.1 mb)
- Total breakup cross section for ⁷Li + ²⁰⁸Pb at 48 MeV: 59.3 mb (this work) compared to 48.2 mb from Kelly et al.

## Context

This work extends the dicluster description of weakly-bound nuclei, previously applied to one-nucleon haloes by authors such as Cata, Das2, Hama, Alka, and Typel, to the specific case of ⁷Li as an α+t system. The model builds on the cluster picture of Walliser and Fliessbach and provides a framework that includes both resonant and non-resonant continuum contributions simultaneously, avoiding the crude energy binning limitations of CDCC calculations by Kelly and collaborators. The results enable more accurate predictions for breakup probabilities in sub-barrier fusion reactions, particularly for the ⁷Li + ¹⁶⁵Ho system where experimental data exist from the Indian group, and demonstrate that the nuclear quadrupole mechanism, often neglected, can dominate over dipole contributions for symmetric dicluster systems.
