# Nonperturbative Formulation of Resonances in Quantum Mechanics Based on Exact WKB Method

> We study quasi-stationary states in quantum mechanics using the exact Wentzel--Kramers--Brillouin (WKB) analysis as a nonperturbative framework. Whereas previous works focused mainly on stable systems, we explore unstable states such as resonances. As a concrete example, we analyze the inverted Rosen--Morse potential, which exhibits barrier resonance. This model allows exact solutions, enabling a direct comparison with exact WKB predictions. We provide a simple analytic picture of resonance and demonstrate consistency between exact and WKB-based results, extending the applicability of exact WKB analysis to nonpolynomial potentials.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that exact WKB analysis can be applied to nonpolynomial potentials to describe resonant (unstable) states, not just stable bound states, by showing that the complex energies of barrier resonances in the inverted Rosen–Morse potential derived from exact WKB quantization conditions match those from the exact analytic solution.

## Method

The authors analyze the one-dimensional Schrödinger equation with the inverted Rosen–Morse potential \(V(x) = U_0 / \cosh^2(\beta x)\) (with \(U_0 > 0\)). They derive exact resonant energies by imposing Siegert (purely outgoing) boundary conditions on the hypergeometric-function solution, obtaining discrete complex energies from the poles of Gamma functions. Separately, they construct the Stokes graph for this potential using exact WKB analysis, identify a normalizable integration path in the complex plane (analogous to complex scaling), and derive a quantization condition \(1 - A = 0\) from the monodromy around a nontrivial cycle \(A\).

## Key numbers

* For the inverted Rosen–Morse potential with \(8mU_0/(\beta^2 \hbar^2) > 1\), the resonant wave numbers are \(k^{\rm R}_n = \frac{\beta}{2} \left[ \sqrt{ \frac{8mU_0}{\beta^2 \hbar^2} - 1 } - i(2n+1) \right]\) for \(n = 0, 1, 2, \dots\).
* The corresponding complex resonant energies are \(E^{\rm R}_n = \frac{\hbar^2 \beta^2}{8m} \left[ \sqrt{ \frac{8mU_0}{\beta^2 \hbar^2} - 1 } - i(2n+1) \right]^2\).
* The antiresonant wave numbers are \(k^{\rm AR}_n = \frac{\beta}{2} \left[ -\sqrt{ \frac{8mU_0}{\beta^2 \hbar^2} - 1 } - i(2n+1) \right]\), satisfying \(k^{\rm R}_n = -(k^{\rm AR}_n)^*\).

## Context

This work extends exact WKB analysis, previously applied to stable systems such as double-well potentials (Sueishi 2020) and periodic-well potentials (Sueishi 2021), to unstable resonant states. It provides a nonperturbative alternative to the complex scaling method (Aguilar 1971, Balslev 1971, Myo 2014) and Zel'dovich regularization (Zel'dovich 1961, Berggren 1968) for treating resonant states, and it enables the application of exact WKB methods to nonpolynomial potentials relevant in nuclear physics and scattering theory.
