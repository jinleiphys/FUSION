# Exactly solvable models of baryon structure

> We present a qualitative analysis of the gross features of baryon spectroscopy (masses and form factors) in terms of various exactly solvable models. It is shown that a collective model, in which baryon resonances are interpreted as rotations and vibrations of an oblate symmetric top, provides a good starting point for a more detailed quantitative study.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a collective model interpreting baryon resonances as rotations and vibrations of an oblate symmetric top provides a good starting point for quantitative study, but the analysis is explicitly presented as qualitative, not as a definitive fit or proof.

## Method

The authors construct a U(7) interacting boson model for the spatial (radial/orbital) degrees of freedom of three constituent quarks, using Jacobi coordinates and bosonic quantization. They identify three dynamical symmetry limits (U(6)⊃SU(3)⊗SU(2), U(6)⊃SO(6), and SO(7)) plus the oblate symmetric top and the hypercoulomb potential as exactly solvable cases, then compare the predicted mass spectra and proton electric form factors from each model against known nonstrange baryon data.

## Key numbers

- A simultaneous fit of 25 well-established (3 and 4 star) nucleon and delta resonances from the Particle Data Group gives a root-mean-square deviation of 39 MeV for the oblate symmetric top model.
- The proton electric form factor in the hypercoulomb potential model is given by \( G_E^p(k) = 1/(1+k^2 b^2)^{7/2} \), with \( b^2 = \langle r^2 \rangle_p/21 \).
- For the oblate top with a collective charge distribution, the proton electric form factor takes the dipole form \( G_E^p(k) = 1/(1+k^2 a^2)^2 \), with \( a^2 = \langle r^2 \rangle_p/12 \).
- The harmonic oscillator form factor drops exponentially as \( e^{-k^2 \beta^2/6} \); the deformed oscillator form factor drops as \( k^{-3} \); the oblate top form factor drops as \( k^{-4} \); the hypercoulomb form factor drops as \( k^{-7} \).

## Context

This work extends the algebraic approach to baryon structure previously developed by Bijker, Iachello, and Leviatan (1994) and by Koniuk and Isgur (1980) for harmonic oscillator quark models. It contrasts with the harmonic oscillator and hypercoulomb potential models, which cannot simultaneously reproduce the low mass of the Roper resonance (N(1440) with \( J^P=1/2^+ \)) and linear Regge trajectories. The oblate top model enables systematic classification of baryon resonances into rotational and vibrational bands, and predicts many "missing" resonances (especially unnatural parity states with \( L^P=1^+, 2^- \)) that are difficult to observe experimentally.
