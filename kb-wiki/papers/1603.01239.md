# Neutron stars: from the inner crust to the core with the (Extended) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model

> Nucleonic matter is described within a su(2) extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. Several parametrizations with different nuclear matter saturation properties are proposed. At subsaturation, nuclear pasta phases are calculated within two methods: the coexistence-phases approximation and the compressible liquid drop model, with the surface tension coefficient determined using a geometrical approach at zero temperature. An unified equation of state of stellar matter for the inner crust, with the nuclear pasta phases, and the core is calculated. The mass and radius of neutron stars within this framework are obtained for several families of hadronic and hybrid stars. The quark phase of hybrid stars is described within the su(3) NJL model including a vector term. Stellar macroscopic properties are in accordance with some of the recent results in the literature.

# Neutron stars: from the inner crust to the core with the (Extended) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model

## Key claim

The extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (eNJL) model, which incorporates chiral symmetry, can produce a unified equation of state for neutron star matter from the inner crust through the core, but only for parametrizations that do not predict a transition to pure neutron matter at densities below the central density of the most massive star; models with a too-low chiral symmetry restoration density become inadequate for describing stellar matter.

## Method

The authors describe nucleonic matter using an SU(2) extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with several parametrizations differing in saturation properties, including models with and without a current quark mass term. At subsaturation densities, nuclear pasta phases are calculated using two approaches: the coexistence-phases (CP) approximation and the compressible liquid drop (CLD) model, with the surface tension coefficient determined via a geometrical approach at zero temperature. For hybrid stars, the quark phase is described using an SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with a vector interaction term, and the hadron-quark phase transition is treated via the Maxwell construction. The complete stellar equation of state combines the BPS outer crust, the pasta-phase inner crust, and either a purely nucleonic or hybrid core, from which the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are integrated to obtain mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density for all eNJL parametrizations: 0.148 fm$^{-3}$
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation ranges from -15.56 MeV (eNJL2) to -16.34 MeV (eNJL1)
- Incompressibility at saturation ranges from 231.13 MeV (eNJL2) to 286.63 MeV (eNJL2m)
- Symmetry energy at saturation ranges from 29.91 MeV (eNJL3$\sigma\rho$1) to 33.66 MeV (eNJL2m)
- Symmetry energy slope L at saturation ranges from 59.04 MeV (eNJL2m$\sigma\rho$1) to 99.90 MeV (eNJL1)
- Chiral symmetry restoration density: 0.35 fm$^{-3}$ (eNJL1), 0.45 fm$^{-3}$ (eNJL2), 0.998 fm$^{-3}$ (eNJL3)
- Maximum gravitational mass for hadronic stars: 2.607 M$_\odot$ (eNJL1) down to 1.884 M$_\odot$ (eNJL1m$\sigma\rho$1)
- Radius of a 1.4 M$_\odot$ star ranges from 12.809 km (eNJL1m$\sigma\rho$1) to 14.406 km (eNJL1)
- Difference in radius for a 1.4 M$_\odot$ star when including pasta versus homogeneous crust: up to 500 m
- Difference in radius for a 1.0 M$_\odot$ star when including pasta versus homogeneous crust: up to 1 km
- For the SU(3) NJL quark model: scalar coupling G$_s$ = 1.781/$\Lambda^2$ MeV$^{-2}$, t'Hooft coupling G$_t$ = -9.29/$\Lambda^5$ MeV$^{-5}$, cutoff $\Lambda$ = 630 MeV; vector coupling G$_v$ = x G$_s$ with x = 0, 0.05, 0.12, 0.2

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nuclear pasta phases using relativistic mean-field models (Avancini 2008, 2010, 2012) and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio approach (Koch 1987, Bentz 2001, Providencia 2003, Mishustin 2004) by constructing a fully unified equation of state from the inner crust to the core within a chirally symmetric framework. The results are compared to experimental constraints from heavy-ion collision flow data (Danielewicz 2002) and the KaoS experiment (Lynch 2009, Fuchs 2006), as well as microscopic calculations from chiral NN and 3N interactions (Hebeler 2013) and Quantum Monte Carlo results (Gandolfi 2012). The work enables future studies of neutron star structure and cooling where the pasta phase affects neutrino transport, as discussed by Horowitz (2004) and Pons (2013), and allows testing of how chiral symmetry restoration at different densities impacts the maximum mass and radius of both hadronic and hybrid stars.
