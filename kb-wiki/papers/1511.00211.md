# Multi-Nucleon Exchange in Quasi-Fission Reactions

> Nucleon exchange mechanism is investigated in the central collisions of ${}^{40}$Ca + ${}^{238}$U and ${}^{48}$Ca + ${}^{238}$U systems near the quasi-fission regime in the framework of the Stochastic Mean-Field (SMF) approach. Sufficiently below the fusion barrier, di-nuclear structure in the collisions is maintained to a large extend. Consequently, it is possible to describe nucleon exchange as a diffusion process familiar from deep-inelastic collisions. Diffusion coefficients for proton and neutron exchange are determined from the microscopic basis of the SMF approach in the semi-classical framework. Calculations show that after a fast charge equilibration the system drifts toward symmetry over a very long interaction time. Large dispersions of proton and neutron distributions of the produced fragments indicate that diffusion mechanism may help to populate heavy trans-uranium elements near the quasi-fission regime in these collisions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in quasi-fission reactions of \({}^{40}\)Ca + \({}^{238}\)U and \({}^{48}\)Ca + \({}^{238}\)U at energies slightly below the fusion barrier, the large dispersions of proton and neutron distributions (beyond the mean drift) indicate that nucleon diffusion may help populate heavy trans-uranium elements, but this is presented as a possibility rather than a definitive prediction, and the authors note the Gaussian approximation becomes unreliable near the tails of the distribution.

## Method

The authors employed the Stochastic Mean-Field (SMF) approach to describe nucleon exchange as a diffusion process in head-on collisions of \({}^{40}\)Ca + \({}^{238}\)U at \(E_{\text{c.m.}} = 202.0\) MeV and \({}^{48}\)Ca + \({}^{238}\)U at \(E_{\text{c.m.}} = 198.7\) MeV. They extracted proton and neutron diffusion coefficients from the semi-classical framework using time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) solutions with the SLy4d Skyrme interaction, and determined drift coefficients by matching to the mean-field TDHF drift paths. The coupled Langevin equations for the co-variances of proton and neutron numbers were then solved to obtain the fragment charge and mass distributions.

## Key numbers

* In the \({}^{40}\)Ca + \({}^{238}\)U system, the mean proton drift at separation is \(z(t_f) = 10\) and mean neutron drift is \(n(t_f) = 21\), with dispersions \(\sigma_{ZZ}(t_f) = 19\) and \(\sigma_{NN}(t_f) = 29\).
* In the \({}^{48}\)Ca + \({}^{238}\)U system, the mean proton drift at separation is \(z(t_f) = 6\) and mean neutron drift is \(n(t_f) = 9\), with dispersions \(\sigma_{ZZ}(t_f) = 14\) and \(\sigma_{NN}(t_f) = 21\).
* The contact time for both systems is approximately from \(t_i = 8.0 \times 10^{-22}\) seconds to \(t_f = 1.4 \times 10^{-20}\) seconds.
* The relative probability of populating a heavy trans-uranium element with \((N_2 = 155, Z_2 = 98)\) compared to the mean fragment is about \(e^{-0.5} = 0.6\) in both the \({}^{40}\)Ca and \({}^{48}\)Ca induced collisions.
* The tangent of the angle of the mean-drift path with the N-axis is \(\tan \theta = 2/3\) for both systems.

## Context

This work extends the TDHF calculations of Oberacker (2015) on quasi-fission in the same systems by going beyond mean-field to include fluctuations via the SMF approach, providing fragment mass and charge distributions rather than just mean drifts. It builds on the phenomenological nucleon exchange model used for deep-inelastic collisions (as referenced by the authors) and enables estimates of the probability to populate heavy trans-uranium elements in the quasi-fission regime, though the authors note that more accurate descriptions are needed near the tails of the distribution.
