# Isotopic shift and search of magic number in the superheavy region

> The ground state bulk properties such as binding energy, root-mean-square radius, pairing energy, nuclear density distributions, and single-particle energies are calculated for the isotopic chain of Ca, Sn, Pb, and Z = 120 nuclei. The relativistic mean-field with recently developed G3, IOPB-1, and Relativistic-Hartree-Bogoliubov with density-dependent DD-ME1 and DD-ME2 parameter sets are used in the present analysis. The respective shifts over the isotopic chain for the structural observables and surface property like symmetry energy are also estimated using a three-point method, which is crucial for the systematic analysis of the shell/sub-shell closure. The calculated results are compared with the available experimental data for various bulk properties, wherever available. A multiple isotopic shifts leads to the shell/sub-shell closure at N = (20 \& 28), (50 \& 82), and 126 for Ca, Sn, and Pb isotopes, respectively, are observed. The analysis also supports the neutron magic at N = 40 and 184 for highly neutron-rich $^{60}$Ca, and $^{304}$120, predicted to be the next double magic beyond $^{208}$Pb, respectively. Observing the occupancy number, we notice the higher neutron orbitals are mostly occupied before the lower one, which causes the kinks at neutron magic with an amalgam in the isotopic chain trend above nuclei. We also notice the correlation between the occupation probabilities and the magicity of a nucleus and vice-versa.

## Key claim
The paper claims that isotopic shifts in bulk properties (binding energy, charge radius, pairing energy, chemical potential, symmetry energy) reveal shell/sub-shell closures at N = 20, 28, 40, 50, 82, 126, 172, and 184 for Ca, Sn, Pb, and Z = 120 isotopic chains, with the caveat that the analysis is based on relativistic mean-field calculations using four parameter sets (G3, IOPB-1, DD-ME1, DD-ME2) and the three-point method, and that the results are force-independent only where they agree across all parameter sets.

## Method
The authors performed relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations using the G3, IOPB-1, DD-ME1, and DD-ME2 parameter sets to compute ground-state bulk properties (binding energy, charge radius, two-neutron separation energy, neutron skin thickness, neutron chemical potential, pairing energy, and symmetry energy) for isotopic chains of Ca, Sn, Pb, and Z = 120 nuclei. They applied the three-point formula (curvature/kink parameter with k = 2) to compute isotopic shifts of these observables as functions of neutron number, and used the Coherent Density Fluctuation Model (CDFM) with the Bruckner prescription to calculate finite-nucleus symmetry energy from nuclear matter quantities. Single-particle energies and occupation probabilities were also computed from the converged RMF solutions.

## Key numbers
- Mean deviation in binding energy for Ca isotopes: 0.06 MeV (G3), 0.5 MeV (IOPB-1), 0.91 MeV (DD-ME1), 0.92 MeV (DD-ME2) relative to experimental data from Wang (2017).
- Mean deviation in binding energy for Sn isotopes: 1.99 MeV (G3), 1.51 MeV (IOPB-1), 0.72 MeV (DD-ME1), 1.16 MeV (DD-ME2).
- Mean deviation in binding energy for Pb isotopes: 3.30 MeV (G3), 2.95 MeV (IOPB-1), -1.95 MeV (DD-ME1), 0.07 MeV (DD-ME2).
- Mean deviation in binding energy for superheavy nuclei (Z = 104 to 118): 11.66 MeV (G3), 8.12 MeV (IOPB-1), 9.42 MeV (DD-ME1), 11.88 MeV (DD-ME2).
- Overall mean deviation across all four isotopic chains: 4.15 MeV (G3), 3.27 MeV (IOPB-1), 2.27 MeV (DD-ME1), 3.51 MeV (DD-ME2).
- Isotopic shift peaks (kinks) in binding energy, charge radius, two-neutron separation energy, neutron chemical potential, pairing energy, and symmetry energy observed at N = 20, 28, and 40 for Ca; N = 50 and 82 for Sn; N = 126 for Pb; and N = 172 and 184 for Z = 120.
- The analysis supports neutron magic at N = 40 for highly neutron-rich Ca-60 and N = 184 for superheavy 304-120, predicted as the next double magic nucleus beyond Pb-208.

## Context
This work extends the study of isotopic shifts as indicators of shell closure, following the approach of Goddard et al. (2013) who revisited the isotopic shift at N = 126 for Pb isotopes using advanced Skyrme functionals, and Gorges et al. (2019) who reported isotopic shift at N = 82 for Sn nuclei. The paper also builds on earlier RMF calculations by Sharma et al. (1993) that successfully reproduced the isotopic shift of charge radius for Pb nuclei. The results enable systematic identification of shell/sub-shell closures in the superheavy region, particularly for Z = 120 isotopes at N = 172 and 184, and suggest that further study of the highly neutron-rich isotopes 318-120 and 332-120 is warranted.
