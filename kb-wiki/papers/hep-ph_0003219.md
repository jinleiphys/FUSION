# Neutrino mass spectrum and neutrinoless double beta decay

> The relations between the effective Majorana mass of the electron neutrino, $m_{ee}$, responsible for neutrinoless double beta decay, and the neutrino oscillation parameters are considered. We show that for any specific oscillation pattern $m_{ee}$ can take any value (from zero to the existing upper bound) for normal mass hierarchy and it can have a minimum for inverse hierarchy. This means that oscillation experiments cannot fix in general $m_{ee}$. Mass ranges for $m_{ee}$ can be predicted in terms of oscillation parameters with additional assumptions about the level of degeneracy and the type of hierarchy of the neutrino mass spectrum. These predictions for $m_{ee}$ are systematically studied in the specific schemes of neutrino mass and flavor which explain the solar and atmospheric neutrino data. The contributions from individual mass eigenstates in terms of oscillation parameters have been quantified. We study the dependence of $m_{ee}$ on the non-oscillation parameters: the overall scale of the neutrino mass and the relative mass phases. We analyze how forthcoming oscillation experiments will improve the predictions for $m_{ee}$. On the basis of these studies we evaluate the discovery potential of future \znbb decay searches. The role \znbb decay searches will play in the reconstruction of the neutrino mass spectrum is clarified. The key scales of $m_{ee}$, which will lead to the discrimination among various schemes are: $m_{ee} \sim 0.1$ eV and $m_{ee} \sim 0.005$ eV.

## Key claim

The paper shows that oscillation experiments alone cannot fix the effective Majorana mass \(m_{ee}\) for neutrinoless double beta decay: for normal mass hierarchy, \(m_{ee}\) can take any value from zero up to the existing upper bound, while for inverse hierarchy a non-zero minimum exists, but only if the heaviest state mixes strongly with the electron neutrino (\(|U_{e3}|^2 > 0.5\)).

## Method

The authors systematically relate the effective Majorana mass \(m_{ee}\) to neutrino oscillation parameters (\(\Delta m^2_{ij}\), \(|U_{ej}|^2\)) and non-oscillation parameters (absolute mass scale \(m_1\) and relative Majorana phases \(\phi_j\)). They analyze three-neutrino schemes with normal hierarchy, inverse hierarchy, partial degeneracy, and total degeneracy, as well as four-neutrino schemes accommodating the LSND result, quantifying contributions from individual mass eigenstates and the dependence on unknown phases.

## Key numbers

* The key scales of \(m_{ee}\) that discriminate among schemes are \(m_{ee} \sim 0.1\) eV and \(m_{ee} \sim 0.005\) eV.
* For the atmospheric neutrino data, the best-fit oscillation parameters are \(\Delta m^2_{atm} = 3.5 \times 10^{-3}\) eV\(^2\) and \(\sin^2 2\theta_{atm} = 1.0\), with the allowed range \(\Delta m^2_{atm} = (2 \div 6) \times 10^{-3}\) eV\(^2\) and \(\sin^2 2\theta_{atm} = 0.84 - 1\).
* For the small mixing angle MSW solution of the solar neutrino problem: \(\Delta m^2_{\odot} = (0.4 \div 1) \times 10^{-5}\) eV\(^2\), \(\sin^2 2\theta_{\odot} = (0.2 \div 1.2) \times 10^{-2}\).
* For the large mixing angle MSW solution: \(\Delta m^2_{\odot} = (0.1 \div 1.5) \times 10^{-4}\) eV\(^2\), \(\sin^2 2\theta_{\odot} = (0.53 \div 1)\).
* For the LOW solution: \(\Delta m^2_{\odot} = (0.3 \div 2.5) \times 10^{-7}\) eV\(^2\), \(\sin^2 2\theta_{\odot} = (0.8 \div 1)\).
* For vacuum oscillation solutions: \(\Delta m^2_{\odot} < 10^{-9}\) eV\(^2\), \(\sin^2 2\theta_{\odot} > 0.7\).
* For the LSND result: \(\Delta m^2_{LSND} = (0.2 \div 2)\) eV\(^2\), \(\sin^2 2\theta_{LSND} = (0.2 \div 4) \times 10^{-2}\).
* In the two-neutrino case with inverse hierarchy, the minimum value of \(m_{ee}\) is \(m_{ee}^{min} = \sqrt{|\cos 2\theta| \Delta m^2_{21}}\).
* In the normal hierarchy case with strong mass hierarchy, the heaviest neutrino mass \(m_3 \leq 0.1\) eV, giving a hot dark matter contribution \(\Omega_{\nu} < 0.01\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of neutrinoless double beta decay predictions in specific neutrino mass schemes, including those by Petcov and Smirnov (1994), Bilenky et al. (1997, 1998), and Vissani (1997, 1999). It systematically covers all proposed solutions to the solar and atmospheric neutrino problems and clarifies that oscillation experiments alone cannot determine \(m_{ee}\) without additional assumptions about the mass hierarchy and degeneracy. The paper enables future experiments to use combined results from oscillation searches, direct mass measurements, and double beta decay to reconstruct the neutrino mass spectrum.
