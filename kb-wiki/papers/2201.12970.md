# Signatures of the spin Hall effect in hot and dense QCD matter

> The spin Hall effect (SHE) is a generation of spin polarization for moving spin carriers in materials under an external electric field and has been observed in semiconductors, metals, and insulators at or below room temperature. Recent theoretical analyses show that spin Hall current can be induced by the baryon chemical potential gradient which plays the role of the analogous electric field and which becomes sizable in the fireballs created in heavy-ion collisions at beam energy of ${\cal O}(10)$~GeV. In this letter, we study this important mechanism for spin polarization generation that has not been systematically explored before and predict the signature of the SHE in those collisions using a (3+1)~D viscous hydrodynamic model MUSIC with AMPT initial condition. We propose to use the second Fourier coefficients of the net spin polarization of Lambda hyperon as sensitive probes to search for the SHE. Those SHE observables show a qualitative difference in both the sign and beam energy dependence for the situations with and without the SHE. Future experimental observation of these distinct qualitative features would provide strong evidence for the existence of the SHE in the hot and dense QCD matter at trillions of degrees.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the baryonic spin Hall effect (SHE), spin polarization induced by a gradient of baryon chemical potential, produces a qualitative difference in both the sign and beam-energy dependence of the second Fourier coefficients of net Lambda hyperon polarization, which can serve as unambiguous observables to search for the SHE in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC beam energy scan (BES) energies. The authors hedge that while the magnitude depends quantitatively on the mass of spin carriers and the chosen hadronization scenario ("Lambda equilibrium" vs. "strange memory"), the distinctive qualitative features are robust in both scenarios.

## Method

The authors used the (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic model MUSIC with AMPT initial conditions to generate freeze-out profiles for Au+Au collisions at beam energies from 7.7 to 200 GeV. They computed the spin polarization of Lambda and anti-Lambda hyperons from the axial Wigner function to first order in hydrodynamic gradients, including contributions from vorticity, temperature gradient, shear-induced polarization (SIP), and the baryonic SHE term proportional to the gradient of baryon chemical potential. The second Fourier coefficients of the net spin polarization (Lambda minus anti-Lambda) along the beam direction and out-of-plane direction were extracted as proposed SHE observables.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies studied: \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7\) to 200 GeV for Au+Au collisions in 20-50% centrality.
- Without the baryonic SHE, the second sine harmonic of longitudinal net spin polarization \(P^{\text{net}}_{2,z}\) is negative at beam energy of \(\mathcal{O}(10)\) GeV; with the SHE it becomes positive and grows as collision energy decreases.
- The out-of-plane observable \(P^{\text{net}}_{2,y}\) shows a sign change from negative to positive with decreasing beam energy in the presence of the SHE, while without the SHE it first increases to some positive value and then drops.
- Hydrodynamic parameters used include shear viscosity \(\eta/s\) ranging from 0.16 at 7.7 GeV to 0.08 at 200 GeV, and freeze-out energy density \(\epsilon_{\text{SW}}\) from 0.35 GeV/fm³ at 7.7 GeV to 0.65 GeV/fm³ at 200 GeV.
- The mass of Lambda hyperon used in the "Lambda equilibrium" scenario is 1.116 GeV with baryon charge \(q_B = \pm 1\); in the "strange memory" scenario the strange quark mass is 0.3 GeV with \(q_B = \pm 1/3\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of spin polarization in heavy-ion collisions, particularly the shear-induced polarization (SIP) mechanism of Liu (2021) and Fu (2021) that helped resolve the "polarization sign puzzle" at top RHIC and LHC energies. Unlike the earlier blastwave model study of Liu (2020) which only accounted for the SHE alone, this work includes all first-order gradient effects systematically for the first time. The results provide quantitative predictions for the STAR experiment at RHIC BES energies, and the proposed observables could enable the first detection of the baryonic SHE in hot and dense QCD matter at temperatures above \(10^{12}\) K.
