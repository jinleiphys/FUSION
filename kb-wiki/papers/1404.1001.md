# $\bar B_s\to K$ semileptonic decay from an Omnès improved constituent quark model

> We study the $f^+$ form factor for the semileptonic $\bar B_s\to K^+\ell^-\barν_\ell$ decay in a constituent quark model. The valence quark estimate is supplemented with the contribution from the $\bar B^*$ pole that dominates the high $q^2$ region. We use a multiply-subtracted Omnès dispersion relation to extend the quark model predictions from its region of applicability near $q^2_{\rm max}=(M_{B_s}-M_K)^2\sim 23.75$ GeV$^2$ to all $q^2$ values accessible in the physical decay. To better constrain the dependence of $f^+$ on $q^2$, we fit the subtraction constants to a combined input from previous light cone sum rule [Phys. Rev. D 78 (2008) 054015] and the present quark model results. From this analysis, we obtain $Γ(\bar B_s\to K^+\ell^-\barν_\ell)=(5.45^{+0.83}_{-0.80})|V_{ub}|^2\times 10^{-9}\,{\rm MeV}$, which is about 20\% higher than the prediction based only on QCD light cone sum rule estimates. Differences are much larger for the $f^+$ form factor in the region above $q^2=15$ GeV$^2$.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the total decay width for $\bar B_s\to K^+\ell^-\bar\nu_\ell$ is about 20% higher than predictions based only on QCD light cone sum rule estimates, but they hedge that this result depends on combining quark model predictions (reliable at high $q^2$) with LCSR results (reliable at low $q^2$) via a multiply-subtracted Omnès dispersion relation, and that the uncertainties are inherited from the 10% error on LCSR inputs and 25% error on quark model inputs.

## Method

The authors computed the $f^+$ form factor for the $\bar B_s\to K$ semileptonic decay using a constituent quark model with the AL1 interquark potential, including both the valence quark contribution and the $\bar B^*$-pole contribution. They then used a multiply-subtracted Omnès dispersion relation with four subtraction points ($q^2=0$, $q^2_{\rm max}/3$, $2q^2_{\rm max}/3$, $q^2_{\rm max}$) to combine their quark model results (for high $q^2$ from 19 to 23 GeV$^2$) with LCSR results from Duplancic (2008) (for low $q^2$ from 0 to 10 GeV$^2$) in a combined $\chi^2$ fit, treating the subtraction constants as free parameters.

## Key numbers

- $q^2_{\rm max} = (M_{B_s}-M_K)^2 \sim 23.75$ GeV$^2$
- $f^+(0) = 0.297 \pm 0.026$ from the combined Omnès fit
- $f^+(q^2_{\rm max}/3) = 0.460 \pm 0.025$ from the combined Omnès fit
- $f^+(2q^2_{\rm max}/3) = 0.896 \pm 0.084$ from the combined Omnès fit
- $f^+(q^2_{\rm max}) = 4.792 \pm 0.808$ from the combined Omnès fit
- $\Gamma(\bar B_s\to K^+\ell^-\bar\nu_\ell) = (5.45^{+0.83}_{-0.80})|V_{ub}|^2 \times 10^{-9}$ MeV
- $g_{B^*B_sK}f_{B^*} = 7.49 \pm 1.85$ GeV from SU(3) symmetry and lattice data
- $f_{B^*} = 151$ MeV from the quark model
- $g_{B^*B_sK} = 49.88$ from the quark model at $q^2=M_{B^*}^2$ in the chiral limit

## Context

This work extends the strategy of Albertus (2005) for $B\to\pi$, $D\to\pi$, and $D_s\to K$ decays to the $\bar B_s\to K$ channel, combining constituent quark model results with LCSR from Duplancic (2008) and Li (2001) via an Omnès representation following Flynn (2006, 2007). The result is about 20% larger than the LCSR-only prediction and differs significantly from the RQM calculation of Faustov (2013) at low and intermediate $q^2$, though the integrated widths agree within uncertainties. The analysis enables future extraction of $|V_{ub}|$ from LHCb and Belle data with a theoretical error of order 7%, which could be reduced by improved LCSR and lattice QCD results, particularly for the $g_{B^*B_sK}f_{B^*}$ coupling.
