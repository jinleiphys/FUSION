# Binding, bonding and charge symmetry breaking in $Λ$-hypernuclei

> Recent experiments have presented more accurate data on the $ΛΛ$-binding energies of a few $ΛΛ$- hypernuclei. This is important as the $ΛΛ$- bond energies ($ΔB_{ΛΛ}$) of double-$Λ$ hypernuclei provide a measure of the in-medium strength of the $ΛΛ$ interaction. A mass formula, optimized with the newly available $ΛΛ$ binding energy data, is used to estimate the binding energy and bond energy over a wide range of hypernuclei. The $ΔB_{ΛΛ}$ values calculated with this mass formula are in good agreement with the experimental data, predictions of the quark mean-field (QMF) model and the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model as well. The $ΛΛ$-bond energy is found to diminish with neutron numbers, approaching zero near the neutron-drip line. In this formalism, the calculated binding energy difference in mirror nuclei arises from the Coulomb contributions and can be utilized to extract the Coulomb-corrected charge symmetry breaking effect.

## Key claim

The paper uses a generalized mass formula, optimized with newly available double-Λ binding energy data, to show that the ΛΛ-bond energy diminishes with increasing neutron number and approaches zero near the neutron-drip line, while the binding energy difference in mirror hypernuclei arises solely from Coulomb contributions and can be used to extract the Coulomb-corrected charge symmetry breaking effect.

## Method

The authors employed a generalized mass formula (SAM) that treats hypernuclei as a core nucleus plus hyperon(s), with explicit parameters for hyperon mass, charge, and strangeness. They optimized the formula's parameters using recent experimental Λ and ΛΛ separation energies from JLab and emulsion data, then calculated binding energies and bond energies for a wide range of single-Λ and double-Λ hypernuclei. The results were compared with experimental data and with predictions from the Quark Mean Field (QMF) and Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) models.

## Key numbers

- ΛΛ-bond energy ΔBΛΛ for 6ΛΛHe: calculated 7.12 MeV, experimental 6.91 ± 0.16 MeV
- ΛΛ-bond energy ΔBΛΛ for 10ΛΛBe: calculated 18.34 MeV, experimental 14.94 ± 0.13 MeV
- ΛΛ-bond energy ΔBΛΛ for 13ΛΛB: calculated 22.91 MeV, experimental 23.30 ± 0.70 MeV
- Binding energy difference in mirror pair 16ΛO - 16ΛN: calculated Coulomb difference -0.086 MeV, experimental -0.74 ± 0.52 MeV
- Binding energy difference in mirror pair 12ΛC - 12ΛB: calculated Coulomb difference -0.091 MeV, experimental -0.61 ± 0.20 MeV
- Binding energy difference in mirror pair 10ΛB - 10ΛBe: calculated Coulomb difference -0.094 MeV, experimental -0.41 ± 0.24 MeV
- Binding energy difference in mirror pair 9ΛB - 9ΛLi: calculated Coulomb difference -0.191 MeV, experimental -0.24 ± 0.23 MeV
- Binding energy difference in mirror pair 8ΛBe - 8ΛLi: calculated Coulomb difference -0.097 MeV, experimental 0.04 ± 0.06 MeV

## Context

This work extends the generalized mass formula approach of Samanta (2006, 2010, 2018) to incorporate new, more accurate experimental data from JLab on 10ΛBe and 7ΛHe, as well as recent emulsion data on double-Λ hypernuclei. The calculated ΛΛ-bond energies agree with the QMF and RMF model predictions of Hu (2014) except in the low-mass region where experimental uncertainties remain large. The paper identifies that the ΛΛ-bond energy decreases with both increasing proton and neutron numbers, a feature not previously reported, and shows that the mass formula can provide Coulomb corrections needed to extract the charge symmetry breaking effect from mirror hypernuclei data, a puzzle highlighted by Gal (2015, 2016) and Bottenborn (2017). The results enable future experiments to target specific neutron-rich light hypernuclei where data are needed, and the simple formula can be used in multifragmentation and fission calculations for hypernuclei.
