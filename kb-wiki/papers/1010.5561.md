# Shape of Lambda hypernuclei in (beta,gamma) deformation plane

> We study the shape of $Λ$ hypernuclei in the full ($β,γ$) deformation plane, including both axially symmetric and triaxial quadrupole deformations. To this end, we use the constrained Skyrme Hartree-Fock+BCS method on the three-dimensional Cartesian mesh. The potential energy surface is analyzed for carbon hypernuclei as well as for sd-shell hypernuclei such as $^{27,29}_Λ$Si and $^{25,27}_Λ$Mg. We show that the potential energy surface in the ($β,γ$) plane is similar to each other between the hypernuclei and the corresponding core nuclei, although the addition of $Λ$ hyperon makes the energy surface somewhat softer along the $γ$ direction. Our calculation implies that the energy of the $γ$ vibration for $^{25,27}_Λ$Mg nuclei is lowered by about 0.15 MeV with respect to that of $^{24,26}$Mg nuclei.

## Key claim

The potential energy surface of hypernuclei in the full (beta,gamma) deformation plane is similar to that of the corresponding core nuclei, but the addition of a Lambda hyperon makes the energy surface somewhat softer along the gamma direction, implying that the energy of the gamma vibration for 25,27_Lambda Mg nuclei is lowered by about 0.15 MeV relative to that of 24,26Mg nuclei.

## Method

The authors used the constrained Skyrme Hartree-Fock+BCS method on a three-dimensional Cartesian mesh to compute potential energy surfaces for carbon hypernuclei (11_Lambda C to 23_Lambda C) and sd-shell hypernuclei (27,29_Lambda Si and 25,27_Lambda Mg) as functions of both beta and gamma quadrupole deformation parameters. The SGII Skyrme interaction was used for the nucleon-nucleon interaction, and the set No.1 from Yamamoto (1988) for the Lambda-nucleon interaction, with the Lambda particle occupying the lowest single-particle state.

## Key numbers

* For 10C, the ground state corresponds to a prolate configuration with beta=0.35, with an energy difference of less than 40 keV between prolate and oblate configurations.
* For 18C, a triaxial minimum is found at beta=0.34 and gamma=18.0 degrees, at an energy of -0.154 MeV with respect to the prolate configuration.
* For 19_Lambda C, the triaxial minimum has an energy difference of 0.077 MeV between the optimum deformation and the prolate configuration.
* The energy difference between the oblate and prolate configurations is 0.12 MeV for 26Si and 0.39 MeV for 26Mg.
* The ratio of second derivatives of the energy curve with respect to gamma is E''_25_Lambda Mg / E''_24Mg = 0.961 and E''_27_Lambda Mg / E''_26Mg = 0.988.
* The well depth for a Lambda particle in infinite nuclear matter is D_Lambda = 29.38 MeV for the LambdaN interaction used.

## Context

This work extends previous mean-field studies of hypernuclear deformation, which had assumed axial symmetry (Zhou 2007; Myaing Thi Win 2008; Schulze 2010), by including the triaxial degree of freedom. It also extends the study of shape evolution of carbon isotopes in the full (beta,gamma) plane by Zhang et al. (2008) to include a hyperon degree of freedom. The results enable future experimental measurements of gamma-ray spectroscopy of sd-shell hypernuclei at facilities such as J-PARC, and motivate three-dimensional relativistic mean-field calculations and angular-momentum-projected calculations for more quantitative predictions.
