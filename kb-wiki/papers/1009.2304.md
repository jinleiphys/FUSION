# Radiative energy loss in an anisotropic Quark-Gluon-Plasma

> We calculate radiative energy loss of heavy and light quarks in anisotropic medium (static) in first order opacity expansion. Such an anisotropy can result from the initial rapid longitudinal expansion of the matter, created in relativistic heavy ion collisions. Significant dependency of the energy loss on the anisotropy parameter ($ξ$) and the direction of propagation of the partons with respect to the anisotropy axis is found. It is shown that the introduction of early time momentum-space anisotropy can enhance the fractional energy loss in the direction of the anisotropy, whereas it decreases when the parton propagates perpendicular to the direction of the anisotropy.

## Key claim

Radiative energy loss of both heavy and light quarks in a static, anisotropic quark-gluon plasma depends significantly on the anisotropy parameter (ξ) and the direction of parton propagation relative to the anisotropy axis; the fractional energy loss is enhanced along the anisotropy direction but reduced perpendicular to it, with the caveat that the calculation does not include the growth of unstable modes and therefore represents a "zeroth order approximation."

## Method

The authors calculate the radiative energy loss of quarks in an infinitely extended static anisotropic QCD medium using first-order opacity expansion, modifying the Gyulassy-Wang formalism for static color-screened Yukawa potentials. The anisotropic two-body potential is derived from the hard thermal loop (HTL) gluon propagator constructed with an anisotropic parton distribution function parameterized by ξ, where the anisotropy vector n̂ makes an angle θₙ with the parton propagation direction. The fractional energy loss ΔE/E is obtained by folding the gluon radiation rate with the gluon energy, incorporating the ξ-dependent mean free path and the modified Debye screening mass.

## Key numbers

- Temperature T = 250 MeV used for quantitative estimates of fractional energy loss
- Strong coupling constant αₛ = 0.3 and path length L = 5 fm used in calculations
- Effective number of degrees of freedom N_F = 2.5 assumed
- For collisional energy loss in anisotropic media, deviations from isotropic results are of order 10% for ξ = 1 and of order 20% for ξ = 10 (cited from prior work)
- The collisional energy loss varies with propagation angle by up to 50% (cited from prior work)
- Charm quark mass m_c = 1.2 GeV and bottom quark mass m_b = 4.75 GeV used for heavy quark calculations at T = 500 MeV

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of radiative energy loss in isotropic QGP (Magdalena et al.) to include early-time momentum-space anisotropy arising from rapid longitudinal expansion in relativistic heavy ion collisions. It builds on calculations of the heavy-quark potential in anisotropic plasma (Dumitru et al., 2008) and on collisional energy loss in anisotropic media (Strickland et al.), which found deviations from isotropic results of order 10-20% for moderate anisotropy. The results enable future studies of jet-quenching observables at RHIC and LHC energies, though the authors note that inclusion of unstable mode growth and finite-size effects would be needed for more realistic scenarios.
