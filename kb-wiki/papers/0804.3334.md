# Two-photon contributions to the Rosenbluth cross-section in the Skyrme model

> We study two-photon contributions to the elastic electron nucleon scattering within the Skyrme model. In particular we focus on the role of the anomaly that enters via the Wess-Zumino term and explain how this induces an axial current interaction.

## Key claim

The anomaly-induced two-photon exchange contribution from the Wess-Zumino term in the Skyrme model produces a non-negligible effect on the Rosenbluth cross section for elastic electron-nucleon scattering, though this process alone cannot fully explain the discrepancy between Rosenbluth and polarization measurements of the nucleon electromagnetic form factors.

## Method

The authors compute two-photon exchange contributions to elastic electron-nucleon scattering within the two-flavor Skyrme model, focusing on the local two-photon vertex (figure 2) that arises from the gauged Wess-Zumino term. They extract the axial form factor \(F_A(Q^2)\) from the nucleon matrix element of the anomaly-induced current \(W_\mu\) using the rotating hedgehog soliton configuration and collective coordinate quantization, then evaluate the interference of this two-photon amplitude with the one-photon exchange Born term. The momentum-dependent form factors are computed via Fourier transforms of the chiral angle profile and subsequently boosted using a Lorentz transformation with \(n_{\rm WZ}=2\) to extend the calculation to larger momentum transfers.

## Key numbers

* The ratio \(R(Q^2) = \mu_p G_E(Q^2)/G_M(Q^2)\) from polarization measurements follows \(R(Q^2) \approx 1 - 0.13\,(Q^2[{\rm GeV}]^2 - 0.04)\), indicating a root at about \(Q^2 \sim 10\,{\rm GeV}^2\).
* The empirical ratio \(\Gamma(\pi^0 \to e^+e^-)/\Gamma(\pi^0 \to \gamma\gamma) = (6.3 \pm 0.5) \times 10^{-8}\) is reproduced in the range \(-24 < \chi_{\rm fin}(\Lambda) < -10\) for renormalization scale \(\Lambda = 1\,{\rm GeV}\).
* The Skyrme model parameters are \(f_\pi = 93\,{\rm MeV}\), \(m_\pi = 138\,{\rm MeV}\), and \(e_{\rm Sk} \approx 4.0\) (with \(e_{\rm Sk} = 3.8\) used for the form factor calculations).
* The predicted magnetic moments from the model are \(\mu_p = G_M^{\rm p}(0) \approx 2.33\) and \(\mu_n = G_M^{\rm n}(0) \approx -1.99\).
* The dipole form factor is \(G_D(Q^2) = 1/(1 + Q^2/0.71\,{\rm GeV}^2)^2\).

## Context

This work extends the chiral soliton model approach to nucleon electromagnetic form factors, previously applied to static nucleon polarizabilities (Polar), by computing two-photon exchange contributions that may resolve the discrepancy between Rosenbluth separation results (which yield \(R \approx 1\)) and polarization measurements (which show a linear fall of \(R\)). The anomaly-induced local two-photon vertex from the Wess-Zumino term had not been considered in earlier two-photon exchange calculations using box diagrams (Guichon 2003, Blunden 2005, Jain 2006, Arrington 2007, Kondratyuk 2007) or generalized parton distributions (Carlson 2007). The results enable future inclusion of this contribution in full Rosenbluth cross section analyses and motivate similar studies in vector meson soliton models (Holzwarth 2005).
