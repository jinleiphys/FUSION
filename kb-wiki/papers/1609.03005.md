# Role of correlations on spin-polarized neutron matter

> Using the Hellmann--Feynman theorem we analyze the contribution of the different terms of the nucleon-nucleon interaction to the spin symmetry energy of neutron matter. The analysis is performed within the microscopic Brueckner--Hartree--Fock approach using the Argonne V18 realistic potential plus the Urbana IX three-body force. The main contribution to the spin-symmetry energy of neutron matter comes from the S=0 channel, acting only in the non-polarized neutron matter, in particular the $^1S_0$ and the $^1D_2$ partial waves. The importance of correlations in spin-polarized neutron matter is estimated by evaluating the kinetic energy difference between the correlated system and the underlying Fermi sea.

## Key claim

Using the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach with the Argonne V18 potential plus Urbana IX three-body force, the authors find no indication of a ferromagnetic transition in neutron matter, as the spin symmetry energy is always an increasing function of density across the range explored. The main hedge is that this conclusion applies only within the specific microscopic framework and density range considered, and that the result depends on the different role of correlations in polarized versus unpolarized neutron matter.

## Method

The authors performed Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations of spin-polarized neutron matter using the Argonne V18 realistic nucleon-nucleon potential supplemented with the Urbana IX three-body force, which was reduced to an effective density-dependent two-body force. They applied the Hellmann-Feynman theorem to separate kinetic and potential energy contributions to the total energy, and decomposed the potential energy by partial wave and by operatorial component of the interaction. The degree of correlation was estimated by comparing the kinetic energy of the correlated system to that of the underlying free Fermi gas.

## Key numbers

- At saturation density rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}, the total spin symmetry energy S_sym = 42.891 MeV, with kinetic contribution 16.625 MeV and potential contribution 26.266 MeV.
- At rho_0, the slope parameter L_S = 101.139 MeV, with kinetic contribution 25.225 MeV and potential contribution 75.914 MeV.
- The kinetic energy of the free Fermi gas for totally polarized neutron matter at rho_0 is 55.669 MeV; for non-polarized it is 35.069 MeV.
- The correlated kinetic energy at rho_0 is 64.452 MeV for totally polarized and 47.827 MeV for non-polarized neutron matter.
- The potential energy at rho_0 is -4.784 MeV for totally polarized and -31.050 MeV for non-polarized neutron matter.
- The S=0 channel contributes 26.875 MeV to the potential part of the spin symmetry energy at rho_0, with the ^1S_0 partial wave contributing 21.432 MeV and the ^1D_2 contributing 4.787 MeV.
- The S=1 channel contributes -0.609 MeV to the potential part of the spin symmetry energy at rho_0.

## Context

This work extends previous microscopic calculations by the same authors (Vidana 2002, 2002b, 2006) and others (Quantum Monte Carlo, Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock) that found no ferromagnetic transition in neutron matter, in contrast to predictions from phenomenological Skyrme-type interactions (Vidaurre 2005, Rios 2005) and Gogny forces (Lopez-Val 2006). The analysis using the Hellmann-Feynman theorem to separate kinetic and potential contributions follows the approach of Muether (1999), Sartor (2000, 2001), and Vidana (2005). The results enable a more quantitative understanding of why correlations suppress ferromagnetism, and provide input for calculations of neutrino mean free paths in dense matter and protoneutron star evolution.
