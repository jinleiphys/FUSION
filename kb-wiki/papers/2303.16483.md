# Low-energy quadrupole collectivity of Sn nuclei in self-consistent calculations with a semi-realistic interaction

> Quadrupole collectivity of the lowest-lying states, focusing on $E_x(2^+_1)$ and $B(E2;0^+_1\to 2^+_1)$, have been investigated for the $N=50-82$ Sn nuclei by applying the self-consistent approaches with the semi-realistic interaction M3Y-P6. Both $E_x(2^+_1)$ and $B(E2;0^+_1\to 2^+_1)$ are well reproduced by the spherical Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) plus quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) calculations in $N\geq 64$, without adjustable parameters. The measured $B(E2)$ values in the neutron-deficient Sn nuclei cast a puzzle. In $54\leq N\leq 62$, the spherical HFB\,+\,QRPA calculations give too strong $B(E2)$, opposite to the shell-model predictions within the one major shell. Via the constrained-HFB (CHFB) calculations, it is found that the neutron-deficient Sn nuclei are soft against the quadrupole deformation, accounting for the limited applicability of the HFB\,+\,QRPA approach. In particular, the potential energy curves (PECs) are almost flat in the range of $|q_0|\lesssim 200\,\mathrm{fm}^2$ in $^{106-110}$Sn. We confirm that the near degeneracy of $n0g_{7/2}$ and $n1d_{5/2}$ triggers weak quadrupole deformation and its balance with the pairing makes PECs flat, which is qualitatively consistent with a recent shell model result in an extended model space, by the calculations shifting the single-particle energy spacing and the pairing strength. These conclusions are supported by the proton-to-neutron ratios of the transition matrix elements and the reference values of $B(E2)$ with the angular-momentum projection on top of the CHFB solutions.

## Key claim

The spherical HFB+QRPA calculations with the M3Y-P6 interaction reproduce measured $E_x(2^+_1)$ and $B(E2;0^+_1\to 2^+_1)$ for Sn nuclei with $N\geq 64$ without adjustable parameters, but fail for neutron-deficient Sn nuclei ($54\leq N\leq 62$) where they overestimate $B(E2)$; this failure is attributed to the near degeneracy of the $n0g_{7/2}$ and $n1d_{5/2}$ orbitals combined with pairing, which produces flat potential energy curves and invalidates the spherical approximation.

## Method

The authors performed spherical Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) plus quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) calculations using the semi-realistic M3Y-P6 interaction for even-N Sn isotopes from $^{100}$Sn to $^{132}$Sn. They also carried out axially-symmetric constrained-HFB (CHFB) calculations to obtain potential energy curves (PECs) as a function of the mass quadrupole moment $q_0$, and applied angular-momentum projection (AMP) to the CHFB solutions. The single-particle wavefunctions were expanded in spherical Gaussian bases (Gaussian expansion method, GEM) with $\ell \leq 7$, and the QRPA calculations used a cutoff $\Omega_\mathrm{cut}=300$ MeV.

## Key numbers

- $E_x(2^+_1)$ predicted at $^{100}$Sn: $4.6$ MeV; at $^{132}$Sn: $4.7$ MeV (measured $4.0$ MeV at $^{132}$Sn)
- $^{106-110}$Sn: PECs are almost flat in the range $|q_0| \lesssim 200$ fm$^2$, corresponding to deformation parameter $|\beta_2| \lesssim 0.085$
- $^{108}$Sn: spherical HFB solution is unstable against quadrupole deformation
- $^{110}$Sn: M3Y-P6 gives $\epsilon(n0g_{7/2})-\epsilon(n1d_{5/2}) = 0.03$ MeV in spherical HF, compared to $1.4$ MeV with Gogny-D1S
- $^{116}$Sn: convergence test shows $\Omega_\mathrm{cut}\approx 300$ MeV needed for stable $E_x(2^+_1)$ and $B(E2)$ values
- $^{107,109}$Sn: reproducing both $E_x(2^+_1)$ and $B(E2)$ simultaneously requires $g \geq 1.15$ for the latter but $g \approx 1.1$ for the former, inconsistent with $\Delta_n^{(3)}$ data

## Context

This work extends self-consistent QRPA studies of Sn quadrupole collectivity using Skyrme interactions (SkM$^*$, SLy4, SkX; reported by C. T. P. et al. 2012) and Gogny-D1S (S. P. et al. 2014; H. N. et al. 2011), which also overestimated $B(E2)$ in neutron-deficient Sn. The results contradict conventional shell-model predictions within a single major shell (A. B. et al. 2005) that underestimated $B(E2)$ in this region, but are qualitatively consistent with an extended shell-model calculation (T. T. et al. 2018) suggesting weak deformation. The flat PECs and the role of $n0g_{7/2}$-$n1d_{5/2}$ degeneracy enable future studies of shape coexistence and the $E(5)$ symmetry in neutron-deficient Sn isotopes.
