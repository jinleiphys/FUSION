# Charged multiplicities and $J/ψ$ suppression at SPS and RHIC energies

> Charged multiplicities in nucleus--nucleus collisions are calculated in the Dual Parton Model taking into account shadowing corrections. Its dependence on the number of collisions and participants is analyzed and found in agreement with experiment at SPS and RHIC energies. Using these results, we compute the $J/ψ$ suppression at SPS as a function of the transverse energy and of the energy of the zero degree calorimeter. Predictions for RHIC are presented.

# Charged multiplicities and J/ψ suppression at SPS and RHIC energies

## Key claim

The Dual Parton Model with shadowing corrections reproduces charged multiplicities at SPS and RHIC energies, and the comover approach to J/ψ suppression agrees with NA50 data at SPS; however, predictions for RHIC are highly uncertain because the nuclear absorption mechanism at high energy depends on whether the c-cbar pair is produced in a colorless dipole state (σ̃ ≈ σ_abs) or in a color state with light quarks (σ̃ ~ 15–20 mb), leading to very different suppression patterns.

## Method

Charged multiplicities in nucleus-nucleus collisions are computed using the Dual Parton Model (DPM) with Glauber-model geometry, where the multiplicity per unit rapidity is expressed as a sum over strings stretched between quark constituents, weighted by the number of participants and binary collisions. Shadowing corrections from Gribov theory are included and become significant at RHIC energies. The J/ψ survival probability is factorized into a nuclear absorption term (using either a probabilistic Glauber expression or an asymptotic coherent formula) and a comover-interaction term, with the comover density obtained from the DPM multiplicity calculation; the results are compared to NA50 data as functions of transverse energy E_T and zero-degree calorimeter energy E_ZDC.

## Key numbers

- At SPS energy √s = 17.3 GeV, shadowing corrections are negligible; at RHIC √s = 130 GeV, shadowing reduces the multiplicity by 40–50%.
- The non-diffractive pp cross-section used is σ_ND = 26 mb at √s = 17.3 GeV and 33 mb at √s = 130 GeV.
- The average number of inelastic pp collisions is taken as k = 1.4 at SPS and k = 2.0 at RHIC, corresponding to dN_pp^ND/dy = 1.56 and 2.72 respectively.
- The predicted increase in charged multiplicity between √s = 130 and 200 GeV is 13%.
- For J/ψ suppression at SPS, the absorptive cross-section is σ_abs = 4.5 mb and the comover cross-section is σ_co = 1 mb.
- The coefficient q relating E_T to multiplicity is q = 0.62 GeV; the parameter α for E_ZDC is α = 0.076.
- The comover density in pp is N_f = 1.15 fm^{-2}.
- For the asymptotic nuclear absorption formula at RHIC, the alternative cross-section considered is σ̃ = 15 mb.
- Using the finite-energy formula with σ̃ = 15 mb, the J/ψ over DY ratio for central events at RHIC is estimated to be 40% higher than the asymptotic prediction.

## Context

This work extends the Dual Parton Model calculations of Capella and Sousa (2001) to include shadowing corrections and applies the comover approach to J/ψ suppression developed by Capella, Ferreiro, and Kaidalov (2000). The charged multiplicity results are compared to WA98 data at SPS and PHENIX data at RHIC, and the J/ψ suppression predictions are compared to NA50 data. The paper highlights that at RHIC energies, the nuclear absorption mechanism becomes uncertain due to coherence-length effects, as discussed by Kopeliovich, Tarasov, and Hüfner (2001) and Armesto, Braun, Capella, Pajares, and Salgado (1998); the authors emphasize that accurate pA measurements are needed to resolve whether the c-cbar pair interacts as a dipole or in a colored state, which determines whether suppression is moderate or very strong at RHIC.
