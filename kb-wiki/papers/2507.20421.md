# Low-energy atomic scattering: s-wave relation between the interaction potential and the phase shift

> We investigate the on-shell approximation in the context of s-wave scattering for ultracold two-body collisions. Our analysis systematically covers spatial dimensions D=1,2,3 , with the aim of identifying the regimes in which the approximation remains valid when applied to commonly used model interaction potentials. Specifically, we focus on the square well and delta shell potentials, both of which admit analytical solutions for the s-wave scattering problem in all dimensions considered. By employing the exact analytical expressions for the s-wave scattering phase shift, we perform a direct comparison between the exact on-shell matrix element of the interaction potential and their corresponding approximations across a range of collision momenta. Particular attention is given to the low-energy regime. Our findings indicate that, although the on-shell approximation generally improves with increasing momentum, its accuracy also improves for weaker potentials. Remarkably, in the limit of weak interactions, we demonstrate that the on-shell approximation becomes exact at leading order. In this regime, the approximation offers a controlled means of deriving the low-momentum expansion of the potential and may serve as a useful tool in constructing effective interactions for quantum field theories.

## Key claim

The on-shell (OS) approximation, which relates the s-wave component of the interaction potential to the s-wave scattering phase shift, becomes exact at leading order in the limit of weak interactions for all spatial dimensions D=1,2,3, but its accuracy depends on both the momentum and the potential strength, improving for weaker potentials and higher momenta.

## Method

The authors analytically solved the s-wave radial Schrodinger equation for square well and delta shell potentials in D=1,2,3 dimensions, obtaining exact expressions for the s-wave scattering phase shift delta_0(k). They then compared the exact s-wave component V_0(k) of each potential, computed via Eq. (4) using the known potential forms, against the OS approximation given by Eq. ## Key numbers

- Square well potential: For D=1, the exact s-wave component is V_0(k) = gamma * (kR + cos(kR)sin(kR))/k; for D=2, V_0(k) = pi*R^2 * gamma * (J_0(kR)^2 + J_1(kR)^2); for D=3, V_0(k) = 2*pi*gamma * (kR - cos(kR)sin(kR))/k^3
- Delta shell potential: For D=1, V_0(k) = 2*gamma*(cos(kR))^2; for D=2, V_0(k) = 2*pi*R*gamma*(J_0(kR))^2; for D=3, V_0(k) = 4*pi^2*R^2*gamma*(j_0(kR))^2
- The OS approximation systematically overestimates (D=3) or underestimates (D=1,2) the low-momentum values of V_0(k) regardless of the sign of the potential
- In the limit gamma -> 0, the leading order of the OS approximation coincides with the exact result, which is a linear function of gamma, valid in every dimension

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Lorenzi 2023) where the OS approximation was introduced and applied to derive the two-dimensional equation of state for a Bose gas with effective range correction. The paper systematically tests the approximation against exactly solvable square well and delta shell potentials, complementing methods that relate phase shifts to energy shifts in external potentials (Fukuda 1956, Yu 2011, Collin 2007) and aligning more closely with effective field theory approaches (Braaten 2008) for constructing effective interactions that reproduce low-energy scattering properties. The results enable future extension of the OS approximation to finite-temperature many-body systems.
