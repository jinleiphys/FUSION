# Impact of dark boson mediated feeble interaction between dark matter and hadronic matter on $f$-mode oscillation of neutron stars

> We studied the possible presence of dark matter (DM) in neutron stars (NSs) and the structural properties of the DM admixed NSs (DMANSs) in one of our recent works \cite{Guha:2024pnn}. The feeble interaction between the fermionic DM ($χ$) with the hadronic matter is introduced through a dark scalar ($ϕ$) and a dark vector ($ξ$) boson as mediators. The allowed range of the mass of the fermionic DM ($m_χ$), for a particular range of DM Fermi momentum ($k_F^χ$), was obtained in the same work \cite{Guha:2024pnn} with respect to the various astrophysical constraints on the structural properties of compact stars viz. the mass, radius and tidal deformability. The present work is dedicated to the calculation and study of non-radial oscillation of the DMANSs using Cowling approximation. We particularly investigate the effect of presence of DM on the fundamental ($f$) mode oscillation frequencies of the DMANSs utilizing the previously obtained range of $m_χ$ for four different hadronic models. In this work we thoroughly investigate how the individual and combined effects of $m_χ$ and $k_F^χ$ affect the $f$-mode oscillation frequency. Within the framework of our DMANS models, for a particular value of $k_F^χ$, the range of $f_{max}^{DMANS}$ with respect to the allowed range of $m_χ$, is also obtained in the present work for four different hadronic models. Since in the present era, the 1.4 and 2.01 $M_{\odot}$ NSs are of special interest after the detection of GW170817 and PSR J0740+6620, we particularly investigate, for the four hadronic models, the range of $f_{1.4}^{DMANS}$ and $f_{2.01}^{DMANS}$ with respect to the acceptable range of $m_χ$ corresponding to the range of $k_F^χ$.

## Key claim

The presence of fermionic dark matter with feeble interaction to hadronic matter, mediated by dark scalar and vector bosons, systematically increases the fundamental (f) mode oscillation frequency of neutron stars, with the effect being more pronounced for heavier dark matter particles; however, the magnitude of this increase depends on the specific hadronic model used and is bounded by astrophysical constraints on mass, radius, and tidal deformability from PSR J0740+6620 and GW170817.

## Method

The authors computed non-radial f-mode oscillation frequencies of dark matter admixed neutron stars (DMANSs) using the Cowling approximation, which neglects metric perturbations. They employed four relativistic mean field (RMF) hadronic models (NL3, GM1, DD2, DD-ME2) and introduced feeble interaction between fermionic dark matter and hadronic matter via dark scalar and vector mediators with coupling strength ~10^-4. The structural properties (mass, radius, tidal deformability) were obtained from the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations, and the f-mode frequencies were calculated by solving coupled differential equations for the perturbation functions W(r) and V(r) with appropriate boundary conditions.

## Key numbers

- For the GM1 model without dark matter, f_max = 2.39 kHz at M_max = 2.36 solar masses; with dark matter at k_F^chi = 0.07 GeV, f_max ranges from 2.26 kHz (m_chi = 300 MeV, M_max = 2.46 solar masses) to 2.56 kHz (m_chi = 20 GeV, M_max = 2.24 solar masses)
- For the NL3 model at k_F^chi = 0.07 GeV, f_ll^DMANS = 2.13 kHz (m_chi^min = 1 GeV, M_ul^DMANS = 2.77 solar masses) and f_ul^DMANS = 2.36 kHz (m_chi^max = 30 GeV, M_ll^DMANS = 2.54 solar masses)
- For the GM1 model at k_F^chi = 0.07 GeV, f_ll^DMANS = 2.26 kHz (m_chi^min = 0.3 GeV, M_ul^DMANS = 2.46 solar masses) and f_ul^DMANS = 2.56 kHz (m_chi^max = 20 GeV, M_ll^DMANS = 2.24 solar masses)
- For the DD2 model at k_F^chi = 0.07 GeV, f_ll^DMANS = 2.36 kHz (m_chi^min = 0.5 GeV, M_ul^DMANS = 2.45 solar masses) and f_ul^DMANS = 2.52 kHz (m_chi^max = 15 GeV, M_ll^DMANS = 2.33 solar masses)
- For the DD-ME2 model at k_F^chi = 0.07 GeV, f_ll^DMANS = 2.33 kHz (m_chi^min = 0.5 GeV, M_ul^DMANS = 2.50 solar masses) and f_ul^DMANS = 2.47 kHz (m_chi^max = 15 GeV, M_ll^DMANS = 2.38 solar masses)
- The common allowed range of dark matter mass across all four models is m_chi = (1 - 15) GeV for k_F^chi = (0.01 - 0.07) GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Guha 2024) on structural properties of DMANSs by calculating non-radial f-mode oscillations, which are relevant for gravitational wave asteroseismology. It differs from studies such as Liu (2024) that considered two-fluid DM models without interaction, and from earlier works on f-mode oscillations of DMANSs by Das (2021), Vasquez Flores (2019), and Hong (2023). The results provide predictions for f-mode frequencies at 1.4 and 2.01 solar masses that could be tested by future gravitational wave detectors like the Einstein Telescope and Cosmic Explorer, and enable further constraints on dark matter properties through combined analysis of mass, radius, tidal deformability, and oscillation frequencies.
