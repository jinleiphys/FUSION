# Chiral Symmetry and hadron properties at finite temperature -A numerical experiment

> We study the hadron properties at finite temperature from measurement of the screening masses, using two-flavor full QCD of the hybrid Monte Carlo (HMC) algorithm with the renormalization group improved Iwasaki gauge action and the clover improved Wilson quark action on a $16^3 \times 4$ lattice. We explore rather heavy quark mass regions. Disconnected quark diagram is dropped. We observe the tendency that the screening masses in all the channels degenerate, which is in accord with the effective restoration of U$_{\rm A}$(1) symmetry, and then eventually approach 2$πT$, i.e. the free quark value. In the low temperature region below pseudocritical temperature $T_c$, the screening masses in all the channels decrease. We discuss the different features between these calculations and the previous ones.

## Key claim

Using two-flavor full QCD with heavy quark masses on a $16^3 \times 4$ lattice, the authors observe that screening masses in all channels degenerate at high temperature in accord with effective U$_A$(1) restoration, but below $T_c$ all screening masses decrease, a behavior opposite to previous simulations with near-physical quark masses, suggesting this decrease may be due to the absence of hadron resonance contributions in the heavy-quark regime rather than changes in the QCD vacuum.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations using the hybrid Monte Carlo algorithm with the renormalization group improved Iwasaki gauge action and the clover improved Wilson quark action on a $16^3 \times 4$ lattice. They measured spatial correlation functions of mesons in the $z$ direction for scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector channels, extracting screening masses from exponential damping in the range $3 < z < 15$. The disconnected diagram in the scalar channel was dropped, so the scalar channel represents only the connected part (the a$_0$ meson). Simulations were performed at three quark mass ratios $m_{\rm ps}/m_{\rm v} = 0.65, 0.75, 0.80$ across multiple temperatures.

## Key numbers

- At $T/T_c = 2.5$ ($\beta = 2.3$), clear degeneracy between screening masses of negative parity mesons and positive parity mesons is observed for $m_{\rm ps}/m_{\rm v} = 0.80$.
- At the highest temperature $T/T_c = 3.0$ ($\beta = 2.4$), screening masses in all channels approach $2\pi T$, the free quark value.
- For $m_{\rm ps}/m_{\rm v} = 0.80$, screening masses were measured at $T/T_c$ values including 0.80(4), 0.84(4), 0.93(5), 0.99(5), 1.08(5), 1.20(6), 1.35(7), 1.69(8), 2.07(10), 2.51(13), and 3.01(15).
- For $m_{\rm ps}/m_{\rm v} = 0.75$, screening masses were evaluated at $T/T_c = 0.80, 1.35, 1.70$.
- For $m_{\rm ps}/m_{\rm v} = 0.65$, screening masses were evaluated at $T/T_c = 1.32(5)$ and $1.67(6)$.
- Around $\beta = 1.95$ ($T/T_c = 1.20$), screening masses of negative and positive parity mesons begin to approach each other.

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD studies of screening masses at finite temperature, specifically contrasting with results from Bazavov (2014) and Maezawa (2016) who used the highly improved staggered quark (HISQ) action with near-physical quark masses and found that negative-parity screening masses increase with temperature below $T_c$. The present simulation uses heavy quark masses and a different fermion action (clover improved Wilson), finding that all screening masses decrease below $T_c$, which the authors attribute to the suppression of hadron resonance gas dynamics. The results enable future comparisons between different lattice actions and quark mass regimes to disentangle thermal hadron resonance effects from intrinsic QCD vacuum changes near the chiral transition.
