# Relativistic descriptions of few-body systems

> A brief review of relativistic effects in few-body systems, of theoretical approaches, recent developments and applications is given. Manifestations of relativistic effects in the binding energies, in the electromagnetic form factors and in three-body observables are demonstrated. The three-body forces of relativistic origin are also discussed.

## Key claim

Relativistic effects in few-body systems can be important even when the binding energy is very small (of order 0.1% of the mass), because the small binding energy results from cancellation of much larger kinetic and potential energies; the paper reviews theoretical approaches to handle these effects.

## Method

The paper reviews relativistic descriptions of few-body systems using two main approaches: relativistic quantum mechanics with fixed particle number (where Poincaré generators are constructed phenomenologically) and field theory (where generators are derived from a Lagrangian and the state vector is decomposed into Fock components). Within these approaches, three forms of relativistic dynamics are distinguished: instant form, front form (light-front dynamics), and point form. The Bethe-Salpeter equation and its reduction to three-dimensional forms, including the covariant spectator theory, are also discussed.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy B is of order 0.1% of the mass M for light nuclei, but relativistic effects are much larger than the ratio B/M.
- For two spinless particles with exchange mass μ=1 (in units of constituent mass m=1), relativistic calculations (Bethe-Salpeter and light-front) strongly differ from the non-relativistic result even at extremely small binding energy B→0.
- For exchange masses μ_ω ≈ μ_ρ ≈ 0.8 m (meson masses in units of nucleon mass), the system is relativistic because particle momenta are comparable to their masses.
- Non-relativistic calculations of elastic ed cross section fail to describe data at Q² ≥ 1 GeV²/c².
- For strong enough interaction where the two-body mass M₂ < M_c = 1.43 m, the three-body mass becomes negative and a physical solution disappears.
- For three spinless particles with exchange mass μ=0.01 and μ=0.5, the Bethe-Salpeter and light-front calculations of binding energy differ considerably from each other and from non-relativistic results.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on relativistic few-body systems that includes the Bethe-Salpeter equation (BS) and light-front dynamics (LFD), with prior results from MC_2000 for two-body binding energies and from maris09 for three-body systems. The paper discusses how relativistic effects generate three-body forces (first considered by Yang (1974)) that must be included explicitly in light-front calculations to match Bethe-Salpeter results. The work enables improved treatment of discrepancies in three-body observables such as elastic pd scattering and nd breakup cross sections, where non-relativistic calculations with three-body forces still show deviations from experimental data.
