# An energy independent scaling of transverse momentum spectra of direct (prompt) photons from two-body processes in high-energy proton-proton collisions

> Transverse momentum spectra of direct (prompt) photons from two-body processes in high-energy proton-proton (p+p) collisions are analyzed in this paper. We collected the experimental invariant cross-sections at mid-rapidity in p+p collisions measured by the UA6, CCOR, R806, R110, PHENIX, NA24, CMS, ALICE, and ATLAS Collaborations over a center-of-mass energy range from 24.3 GeV to 13 TeV. In fitting the data, we used different kinds of functions which include the revised Tsallis--Pareto-type function (the TP-like function) at the particle level, the convolution of two TP-like functions at the quark level, and the root-sum-of-squares of two revised Tsallis-like functions in which the quark chemical potentials $μ_i=μ_B/3$ or $μ_i=0$, where $μ_B$ is the baryon chemical potential. We have extracted the values of three main free parameters: the effective temperature $T$, power index $n_0$ (or entropy index $q$), and correction index $a_0$. After analyzing the changing trends of the parameters, we found that $T$, $q$, and $a_0$ increase and $n_0$ decreases with the increase of collision energy. Based on the analyses of transverse momentum spectra, an energy independent scaling, i.e. the $x_T$ scaling, is obtained.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the transverse momentum spectra of direct (prompt) photons from two-body processes in proton-proton collisions exhibit an energy independent scaling, the $x_T$ scaling, where $x_T = 2p_T/\sqrt{s}$, based on fits using revised Tsallis-Pareto-type and Tsallis-like functions across a center-of-mass energy range from 24.3 GeV to 13 TeV. The claim is hedged by the fact that the scaling is obtained from fits using empirical functions with a correction index $a_0$, and the quality of the scaling depends on the specific function used (particle-level TP-like function, quark-level convolution, or root-sum-of-squares of two revised Tsallis-like functions).

## Method

The authors collected experimental invariant cross-sections of direct (prompt) photons at mid-rapidity from nine collaborations (UA6, CCOR, R806, R110, PHENIX, NA24, CMS, ALICE, ATLAS) over center-of-mass energies from 24.3 GeV to 13 TeV. They fitted the transverse momentum spectra using four function forms: a revised Tsallis-Pareto-type (TP-like) function at the particle level, a convolution of two TP-like functions at the quark level, and a root-sum-of-squares of two revised Tsallis-like functions with quark chemical potentials set to either $\mu_i = \mu_B/3$ or $\mu_i = 0$. The free parameters extracted were the effective temperature $T$, power index $n_0$ (or entropy index $q$), and correction index $a_0$, and their energy dependence was analyzed to demonstrate the $x_T$ scaling.

## Key numbers

- Effective temperature $T$ increases from $0.088 \pm 0.001$ GeV (UA6, 24.3 GeV) to $0.733 \pm 0.001$ GeV (CMS, 13 TeV) for the TP-like function fits.
- Power index $n_0$ decreases from $12.921 \pm 0.004$ (UA6, 24.3 GeV) to $4.993 \pm 0.005$ (CMS, 13 TeV) for the TP-like function fits.
- Correction index $a_0$ increases from $0.301 \pm 0.003$ (UA6, 24.3 GeV) to $0.854 \pm 0.002$ (CMS, 13 TeV) for the TP-like function fits.
- The center-of-mass energy range covered is from 24.3 GeV to 13 TeV, with data from nine collaborations.
- For the convolution of two TP-like functions at the quark level, $T$ ranges from $0.097 \pm 0.001$ GeV (UA6, 24.3 GeV) to $0.772 \pm 0.001$ GeV (CMS, 13 TeV), and $a_0$ is negative, ranging from $-0.337 \pm 0.003$ to $-0.098 \pm 0.001$.

## Context

This work extends the application of non-extensive statistical functions (Tsallis-Pareto-type and Tsallis-like distributions) to direct (prompt) photon production in proton-proton collisions, which had previously been used mainly for charged particle production. The paper builds on the $x_T$ scaling behavior first observed at the CERN Intersecting Storage Rings (ISR) and later used by other authors (e.g., references 79 and 80 in the paper) to analyze soft and hard scattering processes. The results enable future studies of direct photon production mechanisms and the properties of the initial state of protons, and provide a framework for comparing data across different collision energies using a unified scaling function.
