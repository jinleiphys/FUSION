# The large j limit for certain 9-j symbols-power law behaviour

> In a previous work certain unitary9-j symbols were shown to go asymptotically to zero in the large j limit. In this work we examine this inmore detail. We find an approximate power law for the behaviour of ccertain U9-j's in the large j limit and exponential decreased for others.

## Key claim

Certain unitary 9-j symbols in the large-j limit exhibit a power-law decrease for angular momenta near the maximum possible value, while for the smallest angular momentum (I=2) the decrease is approximately exponential; only the unitary 9-j symbol with the maximum total angular momentum approaches a finite value (1/2) in the large-j limit.

## Method

The authors numerically compute specific unitary 9-j symbols of the form ⟨(jj)^{2j}(jj)^{2j}|(jj)^{2j}(jj)^{(2j-2)}⟩^I for even I and ⟨(jj)^{2j}(jj)^{(2j-1)}|(jj)^{2j}(jj)^{(2j-3)}⟩^I for odd I, for several j values (9/2, 21/2, 25/2, 41/2, 40.5, 60.5, 100.5). They test power-law scaling by comparing predictions from j=25/2 to actual values at j=41/2, and test exponential behavior by examining ratios of successive U9-j values for I=2.

## Key numbers

- For j=9/2, even I: M_j^even(2) = -0.000182, M_j^even(16) = 0.491530 (the only value near 1/2)
- For j=21/2, even I: M_j^even(2) = -3.57861×10^{-11}, M_j^even(40) = 0.496870
- For j=9/2, odd I: M_j^odd(15) = 0.425648; for j=21/2, odd I: M_j^odd(39) = 0.430223
- For I = I_max - 2n, the U9-j scales approximately as 1/j^n; for n=1 at j=41/2, predicted/actual ratio = 1.0145; for n=5, ratio = 1.1423
- For I=2, j=100.5: U(j) = -0.426386×10^{-117}, with successive ratios R1=0.157652×10^2, R2=0.157675×10^2, differing by 0.231934×10^{-2} (better than 1 part in 6000)
- The best-fit power-law prefactor exponent for the I=2 exponential form is m=1.5; for j=40.5, R2/R1 = 1.0008477742 (exact) vs 1.00089299679 (m=1.5 model)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Zamick and Escuderos, 2013) which showed that certain unitary 9-j symbols vanish asymptotically. It connects to foundational work by Wigner (1958) and Racah (1942) on 3-j, 6-j, and 9-j symbols, as well as recent applications in quantum gravity and chemistry. The results are relevant to understanding the overlap of approximate wave functions for two I=2 states in the single-j shell model, particularly for the E(9) interaction in the g_{9/2} shell. The findings enable further analytic study of the asymptotic behavior of recoupling coefficients and their application to nuclear structure problems such as the shears mechanism studied by Van Isacker and Macchiavelli.
