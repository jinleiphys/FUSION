# Probing non-linearity of higher order anisotropic flow in Pb--Pb collisions

> The second and the third order anisotropic flow, $V_{2}$ and $V_3$, are determined by the corresponding initial spatial anisotropy coefficients, $\varepsilon_{2}$ and $\varepsilon_{3}$, in the initial density distribution. On the contrary, the higher order anisotropic flow $V_n$ ($n > 3$), in addition to their dependence on the same order initial anisotropy coefficient $\varepsilon_{n}$, have a significant contribution from lower order initial anisotropy coefficients, which leads to mode-coupling effects. In this contribution, we present the investigations on linear and non-linear modes in higher order anisotropic flow ($V_{4}$, $V_{5}$ and $V_{6}$) in Pb--Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} =$ 2.76 TeV using the ALICE detector at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). A significant contribution from a non-linear mode is observed. A new observable, the non-linear response coefficient, is measured as well. The comparison to theoretical calculations provides crucial information on dynamic of the created system especially at the freeze-out conditions, which are poorly known from previous flow measurements.

## Key claim

Higher order anisotropic flow coefficients \(V_4\), \(V_5\), and \(V_6\) receive significant non-linear contributions from lower-order initial anisotropy coefficients via mode-coupling effects, and the newly measured non-linear response coefficient \(\chi\) provides constraints on freeze-out dynamics that are poorly constrained by previous flow measurements.

## Method

The analysis used approximately 16 million minimum-bias Pb–Pb collision events at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2.76\) TeV recorded by the ALICE detector at the LHC. Charged tracks were reconstructed using the Inner Tracking System and Time Projection Chamber within \(|\eta| < 0.8\) with full azimuthal coverage, and a pseudorapidity gap \(|\Delta\eta| > 0.8\) was applied. The linear and non-linear modes of \(V_4\), \(V_5\), and \(V_6\) were decomposed using the generic framework for multi-particle correlations, and the non-linear mode coefficient \(\chi\) was extracted from the data.

## Key numbers

- \(v_4^{\rm L}\) dominates \(v_4\{2\}\) for 0–30% centrality and changes modestly from central to peripheral collisions, while \(v_{4,22}\) increases significantly and dominates above 30% centrality.
- \(v_{6,222}\) shows a strong centrality dependence, whereas \(v_{6,33}\) has a weak centrality dependence.
- \(\rho_{422}\), \(\rho_{532}\), and \(\rho_{6222}\) increase with centrality; \(\rho_{633}\) shows no strong centrality dependence within larger statistical uncertainties.
- \(\chi_{422}\) and \(\chi_{6222}\) decrease weakly from central to mid-central collisions and stay constant toward more peripheral collisions; \(\chi_{633}\) shows a decreasing trend toward peripheral collisions within large uncertainties; \(\chi_{532}\) is nearly independent of centrality.
- The data reproduce the hydrodynamic prediction \(\chi_{422} \sim \chi_{633} \sim \chi_{532}/2\).

## Context

This work extends the study of anisotropic flow beyond the second and third harmonics, which are linearly determined by initial spatial anisotropy, to higher harmonics where mode-coupling from lower-order anisotropies is significant. The results are compared to hybrid hydrodynamic calculations from IP-Glasma+MUSIC+UrQMD (McDonald 2016) and viscous hydrodynamics from VISH2+1 (Qian 2016), and the data favor descriptions using IP-Glasma and MC-KLN initial conditions over MC-Glauber regardless of shear viscosity over entropy density ratio. The measurements of \(\rho_{mn}\) and \(\chi\) provide new constraints on initial conditions and the temperature dependence of \(\eta/s\) in model calculations.
