# The baryon static potential from lattice QCD

> Lattice QCD simulations offer the possibility of determining the potential between three static quarks from first principles. We review the status of such simulations, and the relative standing of the two theoretical proposals for the baryonic potential: the Delta law (sum of two-body terms) and the Y law (length of three flux strings joined together at a junction). We also present new results on the leading Luscher-like corrections to the asymptotic linear potential.

# The baryon static potential from lattice QCD

## Key claim

The paper reviews lattice QCD evidence that the static three-quark potential transitions from the Delta-law (sum of two-body interactions) at short distances to the Y-law (three flux strings meeting at a junction) at distances above approximately 0.8 fm, and presents new numerical results confirming the universal Luscher-like correction to the Y-law in a 3d Z3 gauge theory. The authors argue that the transition is gradual and that claims of Y-law accuracy at the 1% level are inconsistent with the data, which show a systematic reduction of the effective string tension at short distances.

## Method

The authors perform Monte Carlo simulations of the 3d Z3 gauge theory, dual to the 3-states Potts model, on a 48^3 lattice at beta=0.60 using Swendsen-Wang updates with a multishell variance reduction scheme. They measure the baryonic force for three geometries (equilateral, right-angled isosceles, and quark-diquark) by using a "snake" algorithm that varies the interface area one plaquette at a time, allowing determination of the force with computer resources independent of the quark separation. The analytic Luscher-like correction for the three-string junction is computed by integrating over Gaussian fluctuations of the worldsheet with mixed Dirichlet-Neumann boundary conditions at the junction.

## Key numbers

- The Delta-law to Y-law transition occurs at quark-quark separations of approximately 0.8 fm, based on data from Ref. [us2] showing clear deviations from the Delta-law above this distance.
- For equilateral geometry in d=3, the coefficient C of the Luscher-like correction (1/L_Y) is zero, due to cancellation between eigenmodes; the data are consistent with this prediction after including a subleading 1/r^4 correction.
- The mesonic Luscher term approaches the expected value of pi/24 times 1/r^2, with a subleading 1/r^4 correction of the predicted sign, measured at distances up to approximately 6 fm (lattice spacing a ~ 0.18 fm from fixing sqrt(sigma) = 440 MeV).
- In d=4, the coefficient C is always negative due to fluctuations of the junction outside the qqq plane, with less sensitivity to the triangle geometry than in d=3.
- The baryonic string tension sigma_qqq equals sigma_q-qbar within statistical errors at large distances, with the equilateral and right-angled isosceles force data clearly establishing the Y-law asymptotically.

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice studies of the static baryon potential by two groups: Ref. [us1,us2] (the authors' own group) and Ref. [them1,them2] (another group), which had reached different conclusions about the accuracy of the Y-law. The paper reconciles these by showing the transition is gradual and occurs around 0.8 fm, consistent with the flux tube picture breaking down at short distances where the string is as fat as it is long. The analytic calculation of the Luscher-like correction for the three-string junction (Ref. [us3]) is confirmed numerically for the first time, enabling future studies of baryon spectroscopy with heavy quarks and providing a universal prediction independent of the confining theory. The results also suggest that flux tube descriptions of baryons (e.g., Page) may be a mediocre approximation at typical hadronic scales.
