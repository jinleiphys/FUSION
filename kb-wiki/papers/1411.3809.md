# Parametric instability of classical Yang-Mills fields in a color magnetic background

> We investigate instabilities of classical Yang-Mills fields in a time-dependent spatially homogeneous color magnetic background field in a non-expanding geometry for elucidating the earliest stage dynamics of ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The background gauge field configuration considered in this article is spatially homogeneous and temporally periodic, and is alluded by Berges-Scheffler-Schlichting-Sexty (BSSS). We discuss the whole structure of instability bands of fluctuations around the BSSS background gauge field on the basis of Floquet theory, which enables us to discuss the stability in a systematic way. We find various instability bands on the $(p_z, p_T)$-plane. These instability bands are caused by parametric resonance despite the fact that the momentum dependence of the growth rate for $|\mathbf{p}| \leq \sqrt{B}$ is similar to a Nielsen-Olesen instability. Moreover, some of instability bands are found to emerge not only in the low momentum but also in the high momentum region; typically of the order of the saturation momentum as $|\mathbf{p}| \sim \sqrt{B} \sim Q_{\rm s}$.

## Key claim

The instabilities of classical Yang-Mills fields in a time-dependent, spatially homogeneous color magnetic background are caused by parametric resonance, not by the Nielsen-Olesen mechanism, despite the fact that the momentum dependence of the growth rate for momenta \(|\mathbf{p}| \leq \sqrt{B}\) is similar to a Nielsen-Olesen instability.

## Method

The authors consider a spatially homogeneous, temporally periodic background gauge field configuration (the BSSS configuration) in SU(2) Yang-Mills theory in the temporal gauge, with a non-expanding geometry. They linearize the classical Yang-Mills equations around this background, obtaining a Hill-type differential equation for the fluctuations, and then apply Floquet theory to systematically determine the instability bands by numerically computing the monodromy matrix eigenvalues for each momentum mode \((p_z, p_T)\).

## Key numbers

* Maximum characteristic multiplier \(|\mu|_\mathrm{max} = 129\) at \((p_z, p_T) = (0, 0)\), corresponding to the largest growth rate.
* The broad instability region in the transverse momentum direction extends up to \(p_T^2/B_0 \simeq 1.75\).
* The instability region in the longitudinal momentum direction extends up to \(p_z^2/B_0 = 0.81\).
* The region with \(|\mu|_\mathrm{max} > 5\) covers \(p_z^2/B_0 < 0.37\) and \(p_T^2/B_0 < 1.19\).
* For the zero transverse momentum mode \(a_-\) (Lamé equation with \(\epsilon = -1\)), the first instability band lies in \(0 \leq p_z^2/B_0 \leq 0.41\).
* For the zero transverse momentum mode \(a_+\) (Lamé equation with \(\epsilon = 3\)), the instability band lies in \(3/2 \leq p_z^2/B_0 \leq \sqrt{3}\).
* For the zero longitudinal momentum mode \(a_{2z}\) (Lamé equation with \(\epsilon = 1\)), the instability band lies in \(0 \leq p_T^2/B_0 \leq 1/2\).
* The period of the background field is \(T = 4K(1/\sqrt{2})/\sqrt{B_0} \simeq 7.42/\sqrt{B_0}\).

## Context

This work extends the classical statistical simulation study of Berges, Scheffler, Schlichting, and Sexty (Berges 2012), who first observed low-momentum instabilities under a time-dependent color magnetic field and suggested a sub-dominant high-momentum band due to parametric resonance. The present paper provides a systematic Floquet analysis that clarifies the complete band structure on the \((p_z, p_T)\)-plane, distinguishing parametric resonance from the Nielsen-Olesen instability (Nielsen 1978). This enables future studies of early thermalization in heavy-ion collisions by identifying which momentum modes grow most rapidly and by providing a mathematical basis for parametric instability in Yang-Mills fields, analogous to analyses in O(N) scalar field theory (Berges 2002, Berges 2005).
