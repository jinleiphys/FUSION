# Nuclear matter fourth-order symmetry energy in the relativistic mean field models

> Within the nonlinear relativistic mean field model, we derive the analytical expression of the nuclear matter fourth-order symmetry energy $E_{sym,4}(ρ)$. Based on two accurately calibrated interactions FSUGold and IU-FSU, our results show that the value of $E_{sym,4}(ρ)$ at normal nuclear matter density $ρ_{0}$ is generally less than 1 MeV, confirming the empirical parabolic approximation to the equation of state for asymmetric nuclear matter at $ρ_{0}$. On the other hand, we find that the $E_{sym,4}(ρ)$ may become nonnegligible at high densities. Furthermore, the analytical form of the $E_{sym,4}(ρ)$ provides the possibility to study the higher-order effects on the isobaric incompressibility of asymmetric nuclear matter, i.e., $K_{sat}(δ)=K_{0}+K_{sat,2}δ^{2}+K_{sat,4}δ^{4}+\mathcal{O}(δ^{6})$ where $δ=(ρ_{n}-ρ_{p})/ρ$ is the isospin asymmetry, and we find that the value of $K_{sat,4}$ is generally small compared with that of the $K_{sat,2}$. In addition, we study the effects of the $E_{sym,4}(ρ)$ on the proton fraction $x_{p}$ and the core-crust transition density $ρ_{t}$ and pressure $P_{t}$ in neutron stars. Interestingly, we find that, compared with the results from the empirical parabolic approximation, including the $E_{sym,4}(ρ)$ contribution can significantly enhance the $x_{p}$ at high densities and strongly reduce the $ρ_{t}$ and $P_{t}$ in neutron stars, demonstrating that the widely used empirical parabolic approximation may cause large errors in determining the $x_{p}$ at high densities as well as the $ρ_{t}$ and $P_{t}$ in neutron stars within the nonlinear relativistic mean field model, consistent with previous nonrelativistic calculations.

## Key claim

The fourth-order symmetry energy \(E_{\mathrm{sym,4}}(\rho)\) is generally less than 1 MeV at normal nuclear matter density, confirming the empirical parabolic approximation at that density, but it becomes nonnegligible at high densities and can significantly affect the proton fraction and core-crust transition properties in neutron stars, contrary to what the parabolic approximation would predict.

## Method

Within the nonlinear relativistic mean field (RMF) model, the authors derived an analytical expression for the fourth-order symmetry energy \(E_{\mathrm{sym,4}}(\rho)\) using the interacting Lagrangian density with couplings between isoscalar and isovector mesons. They evaluated this expression using two accurately calibrated interactions, FSUGold and IU-FSU, and also studied five additional parameter sets (FSU-I through FSU-V) to explore variations in the density dependence of the symmetry energy. The effects of \(E_{\mathrm{sym,4}}(\rho)\) on the isobaric incompressibility, proton fraction in \(\beta\)-stable neutron star matter, and core-crust transition density and pressure were then computed.

## Key numbers

- \(E_{\mathrm{sym,4}}(\rho_0)\) is about 0.66 MeV for FSUGold and 0.67 MeV for IU-FSU at normal nuclear matter density \(\rho_0\).
- The ratio \(E_{\mathrm{sym,4}}(\rho)/E_{\mathrm{sym}}(\rho)\) is about 2% at \(\rho_0\) and reaches about 6% to 7% at high densities (1.0 fm\(^{-3}\), or about 6 to 7 \(\rho_0\)).
- For FSUGold, the slope parameter \(L_{\mathrm{sym,4}} = 1.9\) MeV and the curvature parameter \(K_{\mathrm{sym,4}} = 0.5\) MeV; for IU-FSU, \(L_{\mathrm{sym,4}} = 1.8\) MeV and \(K_{\mathrm{sym,4}} = 0.1\) MeV.
- The isobaric incompressibility coefficient \(K_{\mathrm{sat,4}}\) is 3.0 MeV for FSUGold and 47.6 MeV for IU-FSU, generally small compared to \(K_{\mathrm{sat,2}}\) which is -276.4 MeV for FSUGold and -196.3 MeV for IU-FSU.
- Neglecting the \(J_0 L / K_0\) term in \(K_{\mathrm{sat,2}}\) leads to a 30% to 40% relative error.

## Context

This work extends previous nonrelativistic studies of higher-order symmetry energy effects, such as those by Chen (2009) using MDI and Skyrme-Hartree-Fock models, into the relativistic mean field framework. The analytical expression for \(E_{\mathrm{sym,4}}(\rho)\) in the RMF model is derived for the first time, enabling accurate evaluation of higher-order corrections to the empirical parabolic law. The results enable future studies of neutron star properties, including the direct Urca process and cooling rates, where the fourth-order symmetry energy may play a significant role at supra-normal densities.
