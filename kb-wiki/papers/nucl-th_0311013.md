# Nuclear Dynamics at the Balance Energy

> We study the mass dependence of various quantities (like the average and maximum density, collision rate, participant-spectator matter, temperature as well as time zones for higher density) by simulating the reactions at the energy of vanishing flow. This study is carried out within the framework of Quantum Molecular Dynamics model. Our findings clearly indicate an existence of a power law in all the above quantities calculated at the balance energy. The only significant mass dependence was obtained for the temperature reached in the central sphere. All other quantities are rather either insensitive or depend weakly on the system size at balance energy. The time zone for higher density as well as the time of maximal density and collision rate follow a power law inverse to the energy of vanishing flow.

## Key claim

The paper claims that at the balance energy (the energy of vanishing flow), nearly all dynamical quantities follow a power law in the composite mass of the system, but the only quantity with a significant mass dependence is the temperature reached in the central sphere; all other quantities are either insensitive or depend weakly on system size.

## Method

The authors simulated 14 nearly symmetric heavy-ion reactions (from 20Ne+27Al to 197Au+197Au) at their respective theoretical balance energies using the Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) model with a hard equation of state and an energy-independent nucleon-nucleon cross-section of 40 mb strength. They extracted the time evolution of average and maximum central density, collision rate, participant and spectator matter, and temperature within a 2 fm radius sphere around the center of mass, then fitted power-law functions to the maximal values of these quantities versus composite mass.

## Key numbers

- Balance energy for 20Ne+27Al: 119 MeV/nucleon; for 197Au+197Au: 43 MeV/nucleon.
- Power-law exponent for average central density: tau = -0.05182 ± 0.00776.
- Power-law exponent for maximum central density: tau = -0.11477 ± 0.01217.
- Power-law exponent for allowed nucleon-nucleon collisions: tau = 0.87829 ± 0.01833.
- Power-law exponent for participant matter: tau = -0.03621 ± 0.00954.
- Power-law exponent for spectator matter: tau = 0.08323 ± 0.02232.
- Power-law exponent for average temperature: tau = -0.83743 ± 0.11355.
- Power-law exponent for maximum temperature: tau = -0.51079 ± 0.08218.
- Power-law exponent for time interval with density >= rho_0: tau = 0.33853 ± 0.05358.
- Power-law exponent for time interval with density >= rho_0/2: tau = 0.46833 ± 0.04265.
- Theoretical balance energy power-law exponent: tau = -0.41540 ± 0.08166; experimental: tau = -0.42079 ± 0.04594.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the mass dependence of balance energy (Sood et al., 2003; Sood et al., 2002) by examining whether other dynamical variables also show mass dependence at the balance energy. Prior theoretical work on disappearance of flow was mostly within the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck model (Krofcheck et al., 1992; Magestro et al., 2000; Westfall et al., 1993; Sullivan et al., 1990; He et al., 1996; Pak et al., 1997; Li et al., 1993; Mota et al., 1992; Xu et al., 1992; Zhou et al., 1994) with some QMD studies (Pak et al., 1997; Lehmann et al., 1996; Soff et al., 1995; Suneel et al., 1998; Sood et al., 2002; Sood et al., 2003). The finding that participant matter is nearly mass independent at balance energy suggests it could serve as an alternative barometer for determining the balance energy, and the scaling of dense-zone time intervals as inverse of balance energy connects the dynamics to the known power-law behavior of the energy of vanishing flow.
