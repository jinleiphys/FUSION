# Light-Baryon Spectroscopy and the Electromagnetic Form Factors in the Quark Model

> The momentum distributions of the constituent quarks inside the nucleon and the prominent electroproduced nucleon resonances are investigated in the two most sophisticated, available quark potential models, based respectively on the assumption of the valence + gluon dominance and on the exchange of the pseudoscalar Goldstone-bosons arising from the spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry. It is shown that both models predict a large, similar content of high-momentum components, due to the short-range part of the interquark interaction, which affect the behaviour of both elastic and transition electromagnetic form factors at large values of the momentum transfer. The electromagnetic form factors are calculated within a relativistic approach formulated on the light-front, adopting a one-body current with constituent quark form factors. The results suggest that soft, non-perturbative effects can play a relevant role for explaining the existing data on elastic as well as transition form factors (at least) for Q**2 ~ 10 - 20 (GeV/c)**2.

## Key claim

Soft, non-perturbative effects from constituent quark models can explain existing data on elastic and transition electromagnetic form factors at least up to momentum transfers of Q^2 ~ 10-20 (GeV/c)^2, contrary to the expectation that perturbative QCD hard scattering dominates in this region.

## Method

The authors solved the semi-relativistic Schroedinger-type equation for baryon wave functions using two quark potential models: the one-gluon-exchange (OGE) model of Isgur and the pseudoscalar Goldstone-boson exchange (GBE) model of Glozman. They expanded the wave functions onto a truncated set of harmonic oscillator basis states and applied the Rayleigh-Ritz variational principle. Electromagnetic form factors were then calculated within a light-front relativistic approach using a one-body current with constituent quark form factors, which were fixed by reproducing nucleon elastic data and then applied without free parameters to transition form factors.

## Key numbers

- The constituent quark size extracted from the full OGE wave function was approximately 0.5 fm, while the much softer wave function from the linear confining interaction alone gave approximately 0.2 fm
- The dipole form used for normalization was G_D(Q^2) = 1 / (1 + Q^2 / 0.71)^2
- The results suggest soft effects can explain data at least up to Q^2 ~ 10-20 (GeV/c)^2
- The N - Delta(1232) magnetic transition form factor shows a faster-than-dipole fall-off at large Q^2 when short-range spin-spin interaction effects are included
- The N - S_{11}(1535) magnetic transition form factor exhibits an approximate dipole fall-off at large Q^2

## Context

This work extends the light-front quark model of Cardarelli, Pace, Salme, and Simula (1995-1996) for nucleon and Delta form factors to include the S_{11}(1535) and F_{15}(1680) resonances, comparing the OGE model of Isgur and the GBE model of Glozman. The results contradict the expectation that perturbative QCD hard scattering dominates at high Q^2, instead supporting the QCD sum rule findings of Radyushkin that soft Feynman mechanisms can account for the same data. The work enables parameter-free predictions for transition form factors to other nucleon resonances and tests the consistency of the constituent quark picture across multiple baryon states.
