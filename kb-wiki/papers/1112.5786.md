# Q-Value and Half-Lives for the Double-Beta-Decay Nuclide 110Pd

> The 110Pd double-beta decay Q-value was measured with the Penning-trap mass spectrometer ISOLTRAP to be Q = 2017.85(64) keV. This value shifted by 14 keV compared to the literature value and is 17 times more precise, resulting in new phase-space factors for the two-neutrino and neutrinoless decay modes. In addition a new set of the relevant matrix elements has been calculated. The expected half-life of the two-neutrino mode was reevaluated as 1.5(6) E20 yr. With its high natural abundance, the new results reveal 110Pd to be an excellent candidate for double-beta decay studies.

## Key claim

The double-beta decay Q-value of 110Pd was measured to be 2017.85(64) keV using Penning-trap mass spectrometry, which is 14 keV higher and 17 times more precise than the previous literature value from AME2003, but the authors note that the residual systematic uncertainty of ISOLTRAP dominates the total uncertainty by more than 70%.

## Method

The authors used the Penning-trap mass spectrometer ISOLTRAP at CERN-ISOLDE to perform direct mass comparisons between 110Pd and its double-beta decay daughter 110Cd. Singly charged ions of both isotopes were produced via laser ablation and their cyclotron frequencies were measured using the time-of-flight ion-cyclotron resonance technique with two different Ramsey excitation schemes (30-840-30 ms and 50-600-50 ms). The frequency ratios were extracted by fitting simultaneous polynomials to the time-series data of both ion species, and the Q-value was derived from the mass difference using the relation Q = (nu_d/nu_m - 1)(m_d - m_e).

## Key numbers

- Q-value for 110Pd double-beta decay: 2017.85(64) keV, measured with ISOLTRAP
- Previous literature Q-value (AME2003): 2004(11) keV, showing a 14 keV shift
- Expected half-life for the two-neutrino double-beta decay mode (SSDH): 1.5(6) x 10^20 yr
- Phase-space factor for two-neutrino mode with new Q-value: G(2nu) = 1.391(4)(19) x 10^(-19) yr^(-1)
- Phase-space factor for neutrinoless mode with new Q-value: G(0nu) = 4.824(2)(12) x 10^(-15) yr^(-1)
- Nuclear matrix element for neutrinoless decay from QRPA with CD-Bonn potential: M(0nu) = 6.5(9)
- Nuclear matrix element for neutrinoless decay from IBM-2 with CD-Bonn potential: M(0nu) = 4.3(1.3)
- Mass excess of 110Pd: -88333.23(1.34) keV, deviating by more than 20 keV from AME2003
- Mass excess of 110Cd: -90348.84(1.30) keV, in good agreement with literature

## Context

This work extends the program of high-precision Penning-trap mass measurements for double-beta decay candidates, following similar measurements for other isotopes. The new Q-value significantly improves the phase-space calculations for both two-neutrino and neutrinoless decay modes of 110Pd, which has the second highest natural abundance among all double-beta decay candidates. The results enable more reliable half-life predictions for future experimental searches, and the authors note that to explore the effective Majorana neutrino mass range suggested by Klapdor-Kleingrothaus (2006) for 76Ge, a 0nubb experiment on 110Pd must be sensitive to half-lives from 5 x 10^24 yr to 1 x 10^25 yr.
