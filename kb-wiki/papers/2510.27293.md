# Double pole $S$-matrix singularity in the continuum of $^7$Be

> The double pole singularity of the $S$-matrix, the so-called exceptional point, associated with the $5/2^-$ doublet of resonances in the spectrum of $^{7}$Be has been identified in the framework of the Gamow shell model. The exceptional point singularity is demonstrated by the coalescence of wave functions and spectral functions of the two resonances, as well as by the singular behavior of spectroscopic factors and electromagnetic transitions.

## Key claim

The paper identifies an exceptional point (double pole S-matrix singularity) associated with the 5/2⁻ doublet of resonances in ⁷Be using the Gamow shell model in coupled-channel representation, but this singularity is demonstrated by varying the proton and neutron spin-orbit strengths as control parameters rather than being directly observed at the physical Hamiltonian parameters.

## Method

The authors employed the Gamow shell model in coupled-channel representation (GSM-CC) to model ⁷Be as a ⁴He core with three valence nucleons, using a channel basis comprising ⁴He+³He, ⁵Li+²H, and ⁶Li+p configurations. The Hamiltonian consisted of a Woods-Saxon one-body potential with spin-orbit term plus a FHT nucleon-nucleon interaction, with parameters adjusted to reproduce experimental data from the ENSDF database. The proton and neutron l=1 spin-orbit strengths were used as variable control parameters to locate the exceptional point by tracking the coalescence of eigenvalues and eigenfunctions of the two 5/2⁻ resonances.

## Key numbers

- The two 5/2⁻ resonances at the fitted Hamiltonian parameters have energies E₁ = -2.557 MeV with width Γ₁ = 691 keV and E₂ = -2.077 MeV with width Γ₂ = 179 keV
- The exceptional point occurs at E_EP = -2.36 MeV with Γ_EP = 477 keV
- The exceptional point is found at spin-orbit strengths V^(l=1)_SO(p) = 4.38 MeV and V^(l=1)_SO(n) = 4.48 MeV
- Corrective factors for channel-channel couplings involving one-nucleon reaction channels: c(3/2⁻) = 1.0008, c(1/2⁻) = 1.007, c(7/2⁻) = 1.009, c(5/2⁻) = 0.9713
- Corrective factors for cluster reaction channels: c_³He(5/2⁻) = 1.0152 and c_d(5/2⁻) = 1.0945

## Context

This work extends previous studies of exceptional points in nuclear physics using the shell model embedded in the continuum by PhysRevC.80.034619, now applied within the Gamow shell model framework to a specific nucleus, ⁷Be. The identification of an exceptional point in a realistic nuclear system demonstrates that spectroscopic factors and electromagnetic transition probabilities diverge when individual eigenstates approach self-orthogonality, while their sum remains finite and continuous, which has implications for interpreting nuclear structure observables in open quantum systems and could guide future experimental searches for exceptional point signatures in nuclear spectra.
