# Field Theories Without a Holographic Dual

> In applying the gauge-gravity duality to the quark-gluon plasma, one models the plasma using a particular kind of field theory with specified values of the temperature, magnetic field, and so forth. One then assumes that the bulk, an asymptotically AdS black hole spacetime with properties chosen to match those of the boundary field theory, can be embedded in string theory. But this is not always the case: there are field theories with no bulk dual. The question is whether these theories might include those used to study the actual plasmas produced at such facilities as the RHIC experiment or the relevant experiments at the LHC. We argue that, \emph{provided} that due care is taken to include the effects of the angular momentum associated with the magnetic fields experienced by the plasmas produced by peripheral collisions, the existence of the dual can be established for the RHIC plasmas. In the case of the LHC plasmas, the situation is much more doubtful.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the existence of a holographic dual for quark-gluon plasmas produced in heavy-ion collisions depends critically on including the effects of angular momentum (shear and vorticity) associated with the magnetic fields; without this, plasmas at RHIC appear to violate the string-theoretic consistency condition, while LHC plasmas remain doubtful even with angular momentum included.

## Method

The author applies the gauge-gravity duality to model the quark-gluon plasma using a boundary field theory with specified temperature, magnetic field, and baryonic chemical potential, then checks whether the corresponding bulk asymptotically AdS black hole spacetime satisfies the isoperimetric inequality condition derived from string theory consistency requirements. The analysis uses the Euclidean dyonic Reissner-Nordström black hole geometry (without angular momentum) and then generalizes to the rotating ℓdyKMV₀ metric that incorporates shearing angular momentum, translating the consistency condition into an inequality between magnetic field B and temperature T.

## Key numbers

- For RHIC plasmas with T ≈ 1.12 fm⁻¹ and B ≈ 16.64 fm⁻² (eB ≈ 10 × m_π²), the inequality B ≤ 2π^(3/2) T² ≈ 13.97 fm⁻² is violated, indicating no holographic dual exists without angular momentum.
- For RHIC plasmas with the classic Skokov estimate eB ≈ m_π² (B ≈ 1.67 fm⁻²) and T ≈ 1.12 fm⁻¹, the inequality gives 1.67 ≤ 13.97 fm⁻², so it is satisfied.
- For LHC plasmas with B ≈ 25.1 fm⁻² and T ≈ 300 MeV (≈ 1.53 fm⁻¹), the right side is ≈ 25.8 fm⁻², placing the system near the violation boundary.
- For beam energy scan plasmas near the critical point (T ≈ 0.74 fm⁻¹, μ_B ≈ 1.52 fm⁻¹, B ≈ 12.48 fm⁻²), the consistency condition is satisfied despite lower temperature due to the large baryonic chemical potential.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Ferrari et al. (2015-2016) on string-theoretic consistency conditions for holographic duals, applying their isoperimetric inequality to realistic quark-gluon plasma parameters from RHIC and LHC experiments. It builds on magnetic field estimates by Skokov et al. (2009) and the more recent work of Holliday and Tuchin (2016) on quantum diffusion effects, which raise the estimated magnetic fields. The paper enables future holographic studies of rotating plasmas and suggests that angular momentum effects must be included in any holographic description of peripheral heavy-ion collisions.
