# The $ρ$ meson in hot hadron matter and low mass dilepton spectra

> The structure of the one loop self-energy graphs of the $ρ$ meson is analyzed in the real time formulation of thermal field theory. The modified spectral function of the $ρ$ meson in hot hadronic matter leads to a large enhancement of lepton pair production below the bare peak of the $ρ$. It has been shown that the effective temperature extracted from the inverse slope of the transverse momentum distributions for various invariant mass ($M$) windows of the pair can be used as an efficient tool to characterize different phases of the evolving matter.

# The $\rho$ meson in hot hadron matter and low mass dilepton spectra

## Key claim

The effective temperature extracted from the inverse slope of transverse momentum distributions for different invariant mass windows of lepton pairs can distinguish QGP from hadronic phase emissions, but this diagnostic relies on the specific interplay between radial flow (maximal at the $\rho$ mass) and thermal temperature, producing a nonmonotonic behavior in the inverse slope that would be absent without flow.

## Method

The authors computed the in-medium spectral function of the $\rho$ meson using one-loop self-energy diagrams in the real-time formulation of thermal field theory, considering $\pi-h$ loops with $h = \pi, \omega, h_1, a_1$. They separated the unitary cut (from decay processes) and Landau cut (from scattering processes) contributions, then folded the static dilepton emission rates with a relativistic hydrodynamic model (cylindrical symmetry, boost invariance) to obtain transverse mass spectra for various invariant mass windows at LHC energies.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for phase transition: $T_c = 175$ MeV
- Chemical freeze-out temperature: $T_{ch} = 175$ MeV
- Kinetic freeze-out temperature: $T_F = 120$ MeV
- Initial temperature for LHC: $T_i = 756$ MeV, with initial thermalization time $\tau_i = 0.1$ fm/c, constrained by total hadronic multiplicity $dN/dy = 2600$
- Landau cut for $\pi-\omega$ loop ends at $M = m_\omega - m_\pi$; unitary cut starts at $M = m_\omega + m_\pi$
- Landau cut for $\pi-a_1$ self-energy extends up to about 1100 MeV
- The effective temperature formula: $T_{eff} = T_{th} + M_{av} v_r^2$ (where $M_{av}$ is the average invariant mass in a given window)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Ghosh et al. (previous publications) on $\rho$ meson spectral functions in hot hadronic matter by systematically separating unitary and Landau cut contributions from different meson loops. It builds on the real-time thermal field theory formalism of Bellac and Mallik, and uses the hydrodynamic framework of Bjorken (boost invariance) and others. The results enable experimental discrimination of QGP versus hadronic phase contributions in low-mass dilepton spectra at LHC energies, particularly through the nonmonotonic behavior of the inverse slope as a function of invariant mass.
