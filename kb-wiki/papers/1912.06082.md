# Ab initio rotation in 10Be

> Ab initio theory describes nuclei from a fully microscopic formulation, with no presupposition of collective degrees of freedom, yet signatures of clustering and rotation nonetheless arise. We can therefore look to ab initio theory for an understanding of the nature of these emergent phenomena. To probe the nature of rotation in 10Be, we examine the predicted rotational spectroscopy from no-core configuration interaction (NCCI) calculations with the Daejeon16 internucleon interaction, and find spectra suggestive of coexisting rotational structures having qualitatively different intrinsic deformations: one triaxial and the other with large axial deformation arising primarily from the neutrons.

## Key claim

Ab initio no-core configuration interaction (NCCI) calculations with the Daejeon16 interaction produce a rotational spectrum for 10Be that is qualitatively and, within the limitations of convergence, quantitatively consistent with experiment, suggesting coexisting rotational structures with different intrinsic deformations, one triaxial and the other with large axial deformation arising primarily from the neutrons.

## Method

The authors performed no-core configuration interaction (NCCI) calculations for 10Be using the Daejeon16 internucleon interaction, which is derived from chiral perturbation theory and softened via similarity renormalization group transformation. The many-body Hamiltonian was diagonalized in a harmonic oscillator basis with oscillator parameter ħω = 15 MeV and truncation Nmax = 12, using the M-scheme NCCI code MFDn. The calculated energies, E2 transition strengths, and wave function decompositions in oscillator space were analyzed to identify rotational band structures.

## Key numbers

- Ground state band rotational constant A ≈ 0.62 MeV, consistent with the experimental value A ≈ 0.59 MeV
- Excited K^P=0^+ band rotational constant A ≈ 0.26 MeV, compared to the experimental value A ≈ 0.20 MeV
- Side band (K^P=2^+) rotational constant A ≈ 0.62 MeV, essentially identical to the ground state band
- Ratio of neutron to proton quadrupole moments Q_n/Q_p ≈ 0.7 for the ground state band, but Q_n/Q_p ≈ 2.1 for the excited K^P=0^+ band
- At Nmax=12, the calculated excited band members lie above experiment by ∼0.7 MeV for the band head and ∼2 MeV for the 4^+ member
- The ground state band terminates at J=4, which is the maximal angular momentum in the 0ħω valence space

## Context

This work extends previous ab initio NCCI studies of rotational structure in beryllium isotopes (Caprio 2013, Maris 2015) and builds on antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) calculations (Kanada-En'yo 1997, 1999; Suhara 2010) that suggested triaxial deformation and molecular cluster interpretations for 10Be. The calculations confirm the experimental picture of two coexisting K^P=0^+ bands with very different moments of inertia, and provide evidence for a K^P=2^+ side band whose E2 transition pattern is consistent with triaxial rotation (γ ≈ 30°) rather than axially symmetric rotation, as predicted by the Davydov (1958) triaxial rotor model. The results enable further investigation of how collective rotational degrees of freedom emerge from microscopic nucleon-nucleon interactions.
