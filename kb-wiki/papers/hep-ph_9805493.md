# Critical Behavior of phi^4-Theory from the Thermal Renormalization Group

> We discuss the universal critical behavior of a selfinteracting scalar field theory at finite temperature as obtained from approximate solutions to nonperturbative renormalization group (RG) equations. We employ a formulation of the RG-equations in real-time formalism which is particularly well suited for a discussion of the thermal behavior of theories which are weakly coupled at T=0. We obtain the equation of state and critical exponents of the theory with a few percent accuracy even for a relatively simple approximation to the exact renormalization group equations.

## Key claim
The thermal renormalization group (TRG) yields the equation of state and critical exponents of one-component phi^4 theory with a few percent accuracy even in a simple derivative expansion, but the approximation assumes zero anomalous dimension (eta = 0) and thus cannot improve on other methods for the exponent eta.

## Method
The author solves the exact TRG flow equation for the effective potential in the real-time formalism, using a sharp momentum cutoff on thermal fluctuations only. The flow equation is reduced to a partial differential equation for the potential in a derivative expansion truncated at leading order (no wavefunction renormalization), and solved numerically by discretizing in the field variable rho and using matching relations for higher derivatives. The starting potential at a high scale Lambda_0 ~ 30 T is taken as the tree-level potential with a small coupling g = 0.1.

## Key numbers
- Critical exponent beta = 0.35 (compared to best value 0.325 from Zinn-Justin (2001))
- Critical exponent gamma = 1.32 (compared to best value 1.240)
- Critical exponent delta = 5.0 (compared to best value 4.81)
- Critical exponent nu = 0.66 (given as nu/2; compared to best value 0.630)
- Anomalous dimension eta = 0 by assumption (best value 0.032)
- Starting coupling g(Lambda_0) = 0.1; starting scale Lambda_0 = exp(3.5) T ≈ 30 T

## Context
This work extends the TRG formalism of D'Attanasio and Pietroni (1997) from a local polynomial approximation to a full numerical solution of the potential flow equation, improving on the earlier TRG+LPA results (which gave delta = 3.57, nu = 0.58). The results are compared to three-dimensional ERG studies (Comellas and Nunez; J. N. C. et al.), lattice calculations (Tsypin, 1996), and the epsilon-expansion (Guida and Zinn-Justin). The TRG approach is gauge-invariant and directly yields nonuniversal quantities in terms of physical parameters, enabling future study of gauge theories like the electroweak sector of the Standard Model.
