# Double beta decay with large scale Yb-loaded scintillators

> The potential of large scale Yb-loaded liquid scintillators as proposed for solar neutrino spectroscopy are investigated with respect to double beta decay. The potential for beta-beta- - decay of 176Yb as well as the beta+/EC - decay for 168Yb is discussed. Not only getting for the first time an experimental half-life limit on 176Yb - decay, this will even be at least comparable or better than existing ones from other isotopes. Also for the first time a realistic chance to detect beta+/EC - decay exists. Effects of MeV-neutrinos are discussed as well.

## Key claim

Using large-scale Yb-loaded liquid scintillators originally proposed for solar neutrino spectroscopy (the LENS detector concept) would allow, for the first time, to obtain an experimental half-life limit on the double beta decay of 176Yb that is at least comparable to or better than existing limits from other isotopes, and also provides a realistic chance to detect the beta+/EC decay mode of 168Yb.

## Method

The paper evaluates the potential for both beta-beta- decay of 176Yb and beta+/EC decay of 168Yb using a 20-ton Yb-loaded liquid scintillator detector (LENS design). For 176Yb, the achievable half-life sensitivity is estimated using a standard formula based on detector mass, measuring time, energy resolution, and background index. For 168Yb, the event rates for the beta+/EC and EC/EC decay modes are estimated using theoretical half-life predictions from prior nuclear structure calculations.

## Key numbers

- The Q-value for the ground state transition (0+ to 0+) of 176Yb double beta decay is 1079 keV; the natural abundance of 176Yb is 12.7%.
- The predicted half-life for the two-neutrino double beta decay ground state transition of 176Yb is 9.8 x 10^21 yr (Staudt 1990).
- With a five-year measuring time and 10% energy resolution, the achievable half-life limit for neutrinoless double beta decay of 176Yb is > 1.5 x 10^26 / sqrt(B) yr, where B is the background index in counts/keV/kg/yr.
- An assumed half-life of 10^26 yr for 176Yb would correspond to 60 events/year and, using matrix elements from Staudt (1990), would give a bound on the effective Majorana neutrino mass of < 0.1 eV.
- For 168Yb (natural abundance 0.13%), the Q-value for beta+/EC decay is 399 keV and for EC/EC decay is 1421 keV.
- The theoretical half-life prediction for the two-neutrino EC/EC ground state transition of 168Yb is 2 x 10^23 yr (Ceron and Hirsch 1999).
- The estimated half-life for the beta+/EC mode of 168Yb is approximately 10^24 yr, yielding an expected event rate of about 180 per year.
- The estimated half-life for the 0_1+ state at 1217 keV in 168Yb EC/EC decay is 5.4 x 10^33 yr (Ceron and Hirsch 1999).

## Context

This work extends the concept of using double beta decay candidates for solar neutrino spectroscopy, originally proposed by Raghavan (1997) and developed into the LENS detector design (LENS Letter of Intent 1999). The paper shows that the same detector can probe neutrinoless double beta decay of 176Yb with a sensitivity to effective Majorana neutrino mass below 0.1 eV, which is an independent probe in the sub-eV mass region currently only reached by 76Ge experiments (Baudis 1999). The combination of 76Ge and 176Yb results would also constrain mixing between light and heavy (MeV-scale) neutrino states, as discussed by Halprin, Petcov, and Rosen (1983) and Zuber (1997).
