# Gap-bridging enhancement of modified Urca processes in nuclear matter

> In nuclear matter at neutron-star densities and temperatures, Cooper pairing leads to the formation of a gap in the nucleon excitation spectra resulting in exponentially strong Boltzmann suppression of many transport coefficients. Previous calculations have shown evidence that density oscillations of sufficiently large amplitude can overcome this suppression for flavor-changing $β$ processes, via the mechanism of "gap bridging". We address the simplifications made in that initial work, and show that gap bridging can counteract Boltzmann suppression of neutrino emissivity for the realistic case of modified Urca processes in matter with $^3P_2$ neutron pairing.

## Key claim

The authors show that gap bridging can counteract the exponential Boltzmann suppression of neutrino emissivity for the realistic case of modified Urca processes in nuclear matter with \(^3P_2\) neutron pairing, not just for the simplified isotropic \(^1S_0\) pairing and direct Urca processes considered in prior work.

## Method

The authors calculated the modification factor \(R_{\bar{\eps}}\) for the neutrino emissivity of modified Urca processes, defined as the ratio of the emissivity averaged over one oscillation cycle to the emissivity of non-superfluid matter without oscillations. They performed numerical integration of the phase-space integrals for the emissivity, including the angular dependence of the anisotropic \(^3P_2\) neutron pairing gap, for both \(J_z=0\) and \(J_z=\pm 2\) condensate orientations, with protons paired in the \(^1S_0\) channel.

## Key numbers

- For \(^3P_2\) (\(J_z=0\)) neutron pairing with \(^1S_0\) proton pairing, the modification factor \(R_{\bar{\eps}}\) is smaller than for \(^1S_0\) pairing by a factor that varies between 400 and 20 as \(\hat{\mu}_\Delta/\Delta_n\) ranges from 0 to 5.
- For \(^3P_2\) (\(J_z=\pm 2\)) neutron pairing, the low-amplitude suppression is roughly \(\exp(-1.73 \Delta_{n0}/T)\), compared to \(\exp(-2\Delta_n/T)\) for \(^1S_0\) neutron pairing.
- For \(^1S_0\) pairing with \(\Delta_p/\Delta_n = 1/2\), full gap bridging occurs when \(\hat{\mu}_\Delta/\Delta_n \approx 3.5\); for \(\Delta_p/\Delta_n = 1\), at \(\hat{\mu}_\Delta/\Delta_n \approx 4\); for \(\Delta_p/\Delta_n = 2\), at \(\hat{\mu}_\Delta/\Delta_n \approx 5\).
- For \(^1S_0\) pairing with \(\Delta_n/T = 60\), the emissivity at \(\hat{\mu}_\Delta = 0\) is suppressed by roughly \(\exp(-2\Delta_n/T)\) for the least suppressed channel (class 3).

## Context

This work extends the initial gap-bridging calculation by Alford (2011) which considered only isotropic \(^1S_0\) neutron pairing and direct Urca processes, to the more realistic modified Urca processes with anisotropic \(^3P_2\) neutron pairing relevant for neutron star interiors. The results enable quantitative predictions for neutrino emissivity in high-amplitude neutron star oscillations such as f-modes, r-modes, star quakes, or neutron star mergers, where density oscillations can reach amplitudes sufficient to overcome superfluid suppression.
