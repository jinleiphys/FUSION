# Confinement and domain walls in high density quark matter

> In this talk I will review some recent progress in our understanding of properties of high density quark matter. This talk is based mainly on the papers hep-ph/0011379, hep-ph/0012041, hep-ph/0108260, and cond-mat/0103451, done in collaboration with D. Rischke, M. Stephanov, and A. Zhitnitsky.

# Confinement and domain walls in high density quark matter

## Key claim

High-density quark matter in the 2SC phase exhibits both confinement (with an exponentially large confinement radius) and domain walls arising from spontaneously broken global symmetries, but these phenomena depend on the specific phase and the density regime, the confinement radius grows exponentially with density, and domain walls exist only when the U(1)A symmetry or strangeness symmetry is broken and then explicitly violated by instantons or weak interactions.

## Method

The author reviews effective field theory descriptions of the 2SC color-superconducting phase of QCD at high baryon density, where the unbroken SU(2)c sector is described by a pure gluodynamics with modified dielectric constant and magnetic permeability. For domain walls, the effective Lagrangian for the Goldstone boson of spontaneously broken U(1)A symmetry is constructed as a sine-Gordon model, with the instanton-induced potential computed using the dilute instanton gas approximation valid at high chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- Dielectric constant in the 2SC phase: $\epsilon = 1 + \frac{g^2\mu^2}{18\pi^2\Delta^2}$, which becomes very large at high densities since $\Delta \ll \mu$
- Effective strong coupling constant at the matching scale: $\alpha_s' = \frac{3}{2\sqrt{2}} \frac{g\Delta}{\mu}$
- Confinement scale in the SU(2) sector: $\Lambda_{\rm QCD}' \sim \Delta \exp\left(-\frac{2\sqrt{2}\pi}{11} \frac{\mu}{g\Delta}\right)$
- Decay constant of the $\eta$ boson: $f^2 = \frac{\mu^2}{8\pi^2}$, with velocity $u^2 = 1/3$
- Mass of the $\eta$ boson: $m = 2\pi \sqrt{a} \Delta$, where $a$ is a dimensionless function that vanishes as $\mu \to \infty$
- Domain wall tension: $\sigma = 8\sqrt{2a}\, u f \mu \Delta$
- Upper limit on superfluid Goldstone boson mass from $n\bar{n}$ oscillations: $m < 10^{-7}$ eV, corresponding to domain wall thickness larger than about 1 m

## Context

This talk reviews and extends work on color superconductivity in dense QCD, building on the 2SC ground state identified by earlier authors (Alford, Rajagopal, Wilczek) and the effective theory for the unbroken SU(2) sector developed by Son, Rischke, Stephanov, and Zhitnitsky (2000-2001). The domain wall predictions connect to the instanton calculations of Schäfer and Shuryak, and the kaon condensation scenario in the CFL phase studied by Schäfer and others. The results enable searches for observable signatures of domain walls in neutron stars, including possible constraints from vortex densities and baryon number violation bounds.
