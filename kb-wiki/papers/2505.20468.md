# Mapping the transverse spin sum rule in position space

> We discuss in detail the relativistic spatial distribution of transverse angular momentum, including both orbital and intrinsic spin contributions. Using the quantum phase-space formalism, we begin with the definition of the three-dimensional spatial distributions of transverse orbital angular momentum and intrinsic spin in a generic Lorentz frame. By integrating these three-dimensional spatial distributions over the longitudinal axis, we derive for the first time the relativistic spatial distributions of transverse orbital angular momentum, intrinsic spin, and total angular momentum for spin-0 and spin-1/2 targets in the transverse plane. We verify the transverse spin sum rule about the relativistic center of spin for spin-0 and spin-1/2 systems, and find that the transverse total angular momentum distribution is non-trivial, even for spin-0 targets. We also show how the distributions of transverse orbital angular momentum, intrinsic spin, and total angular momentum change with the target momentum.

## Key claim
The paper derives for the first time the relativistic spatial distributions of transverse orbital angular momentum, intrinsic spin, and total angular momentum in the transverse plane for spin-0 and spin-1/2 targets, verifying that the transverse spin sum rule holds about the relativistic center of spin but that the transverse total angular momentum distribution is non-trivial even for spin-0 targets, where it arises purely from Lorentz boost effects.

## Method
Using the quantum phase-space formalism, the authors define three-dimensional spatial distributions of transverse orbital angular momentum and intrinsic spin in a generic Lorentz frame with non-zero target momentum. They then integrate these three-dimensional distributions over the longitudinal axis to obtain two-dimensional relativistic spatial distributions in the transverse plane, which restores the elastic condition. The analysis uses matrix elements of the energy-momentum tensor and generalized spin operator parametrized in terms of gravitational form factors, with numerical illustrations based on a simple multipole Ansatz fitted to lattice QCD calculations from Hackett et al. (2023) for the pion and nucleon.

## Key numbers
- For spin-0 targets: the transverse total angular momentum distribution integrated over the transverse plane vanishes, \(J^i_\perp(s',s) = 0\), as required by the spin sum rule.
- For spin-1/2 targets: the integrated transverse total angular momentum satisfies \(J^i_\perp(P^z;s',s) = (\sigma_\perp)^i_{s^{\prime}s}/2\), independent of target momentum \(P^z\).
- The transverse intrinsic spin distribution integrates to \(S^i_\perp(P^z;s',s) = (\sigma_\perp)^i_{s^{\prime}s} (M/E_P) \, G^q_A(0)/2\), where \(E_P = \sqrt{(P^z)^2 + M^2}\).
- Numerical illustrations use target masses \(M = 0.139\) GeV for the pion and \(M = 0.938\) GeV for the nucleon, with momentum values up to \(P^z \leq 10\) GeV.
- The transverse orbital angular momentum and intrinsic spin split is \(P^z\)-dependent, with transverse total angular momentum becoming more orbital-like as momentum increases.

## Context
This work extends the quantum phase-space formalism previously applied to electromagnetic and energy-momentum tensor distributions (Lorce 2018, 2020; Chen 2022, 2023) to the transverse angular momentum sector, resolving limitations where the elastic frame could not accommodate transverse orbital angular momentum distributions. It resolves discrepancies between Ji (2020) and Leader (2011) regarding transverse spin sum rules by confirming the \(P^z\)-independence of the total transverse angular momentum about the relativistic center of spin, as discussed in Lorce (2021). The results enable future studies of how different pivot choices (center of mass versus center of spin) affect the spatial distributions of transverse angular momentum, and provide a framework for interpreting measurements at the Electron-Ion Collider.
