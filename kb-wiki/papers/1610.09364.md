# Jet structure modifications in heavy-ion collisions with JEWEL

> Key features of jet-medium interactions in heavy-ion collisions are modifications to the jet structure. Recent results from experiments at the LHC and RHIC have motivated several theoretical calculations and monte carlo models towards predicting these observables simultaneously. In this report, the recoil picture in \textsc{Jewel} is summarized and two independent procedures through which background subtraction can be performed in \textsc{Jewel} are introduced. Information of the medium recoil in \textsc{Jewel} significantly improves its description of several jet shape measurements.

# Jet structure modifications in heavy-ion collisions with JEWEL

## Key claim
Including medium recoil information in the JEWEL Monte Carlo framework, combined with two newly introduced background subtraction procedures (4MomSub and GridSub), significantly improves the description of several jet shape measurements in heavy-ion collisions, but the recoiled partons are treated in a limiting approximation where they propagate without further interaction with the medium.

## Method
The JEWEL Monte Carlo framework uses Pythia6 for hard scattering and initial-state radiation, JEWEL for the final-state parton shower with in-medium interactions via perturbative matrix elements, and Pythia6 again for hadronization and hadron decays. The medium is modeled as a thermal distribution of scattering centers; at each interaction a recoiled parton is created and its energy (collisional plus thermal components) is tracked. Two background subtraction procedures are introduced: 4MomSub, which performs a four-momentum vectorial subtraction of scattering center contributions matched to jet constituents, and GridSub, which superimposes a finite-resolution grid and subtracts scattering center momenta per cell before jet clustering.

## Key numbers
- CMS data at 2.76 TeV for most central PbPb collisions: JEWEL+Pythia predictions using the 4MomSub method reproduce the differential jet shape ratio (PbPb to pp) for anti-kT jets with radius parameter R = 0.3.
- CMS data for subjet groomed momentum fraction ratios (PbPb to pp): JEWEL+Pythia reproduces the trend of more asymmetric jet splittings at low jet pT and more symmetric splittings as jet pT increases, for anti-kT jets with R = 0.4.
- Systematic uncertainties in JEWEL+Pythia predictions for the subjet groomed momentum fraction are estimated by varying the grid resolution by a factor of two.

## Context
This work extends the JEWEL Monte Carlo framework (Zapp 2013) by introducing background subtraction procedures needed to compare with experimental data from the LHC, specifically CMS measurements of jet shapes (CMS 2014) and subjet groomed momentum fractions (CMS 2015). The results enable more quantitative comparisons between jet quenching models and data, moving beyond the jet nuclear modification factor R_AA toward differential jet structure observables that probe medium-jet interactions.
