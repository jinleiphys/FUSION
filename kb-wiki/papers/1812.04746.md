# Transverse momentum $p_{\mathrm{T}}$ spectra of strange particles production in different collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}= 2.76, 5.02,$ and $7$ TeV

> We analyse the transverse momentum $p_{\mathrm{T}}$ spectra of strange particles \Kslxi produced in $\textsf{Pb+Pb}$ collision at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}= 2.76$ TeV, $\textsf{p+Pb}$ collision at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}= 5.02$ TeV, and $\textsf{p+p}$ collision at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}= 7$ TeV in different multiplicity events measured by the \textit{CMS} experiment at the Large Hadron Collider. The $p_{\mathrm{T}}$ spectra of strange particles are fitted by Tsallis statistics and Boltzmann statistics, respectively. The fitting parameters are studied as a function of the multiplicity events for all systems. The Tsallis temperature ($T_{\mathtt{Ts}}$), Boltzmann temperature ($T_{\mathtt{Boltz}}$), and radius of the system ($R$) increase with both the mass and strangeness number of the particle and also increase with the multiplicity events. The non-extensive parameter ($q$) decreases with the increase in the mass of the particle and also decrease with the increase in the multiplicity events which means that the system tends to thermodynamic stabilization. The extracted temperatures from the two statistics for the strange particles are exhibited a linear correlation.

## Key claim

The Tsallis and Boltzmann statistics both provide good fits to the transverse momentum spectra of strange particles ($K_s^0$, $\Lambda$, $\Xi^-$) across different collision systems and energies, but the Tsallis statistics gives better agreement than Boltzmann statistics, especially at high $p_{\mathrm{T}}$ and for $K_s^0$ particles where the Boltzmann fit quality decreases with increasing multiplicity.

## Method

The authors fitted the transverse momentum ($p_{\mathrm{T}}$) spectra of $K_s^0$, $\Lambda$, and $\Xi^-$ particles measured by the CMS experiment in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV, p+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV, and p+p collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7$ TeV using both Tsallis statistics (Eq. 6) and Boltzmann statistics (Eq. 3) at mid-rapidity ($y=0$) with zero chemical potential. The fitting parameters (Tsallis temperature $T_{\mathrm{Ts}}$, non-extensive parameter $q$, Boltzmann temperature $T_{\mathrm{Boltz}}$, and system radius $R$) were extracted for different multiplicity classes and studied as functions of multiplicity, particle mass, and strangeness number.

## Key numbers

- For Pb+Pb at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV, $K_s^0$ Tsallis temperature ranges from $87.169 \pm 0.105$ MeV (lowest multiplicity, $<N_{\mathrm{track}}> = 21$) to $188.54 \pm 0.499$ MeV (highest multiplicity, $<N_{\mathrm{track}}> = 299$)
- For Pb+Pb at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV, $\Xi^-$ Tsallis temperature ranges from $156.108 \pm 0.513$ MeV ($<N_{\mathrm{track}}> = 21$) to $425.295 \pm 2.719$ MeV ($<N_{\mathrm{track}}> = 299$)
- For p+Pb at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV, $\Lambda$ Tsallis temperature ranges from $172.072 \pm 0.124$ MeV ($<N_{\mathrm{track}}> = 21$) to $531.914 \pm 2.423$ MeV ($<N_{\mathrm{track}}> = 280$)
- For p+p at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7$ TeV, $K_s^0$ Tsallis temperature ranges from $91.407 \pm 0.129$ MeV ($<N_{\mathrm{track}}> = 14$) to $313.052 \pm 1.92$ MeV ($<N_{\mathrm{track}}> = 158$)
- The non-extensive parameter $q$ for $K_s^0$ in Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV decreases from $1.15 \pm 0.001$ ($<N_{\mathrm{track}}> = 21$) to $1.079 \pm 0.004$ ($<N_{\mathrm{track}}> = 299$)
- The linear correlation between Tsallis and Boltzmann temperatures for all strange particles combined gives $T_{\mathrm{Ts}} = (1.2465 \pm 0.0138) T_{\mathrm{Boltz}} - (160.499 \pm 5.386)$ with $\chi^2/\mathrm{dof} = 0.0528$

## Context

This work extends the analysis of transverse momentum spectra using Tsallis and Boltzmann statistics previously applied to charged particles by Gao (2015) and to strange particles with radial flow effects by Saraswat (2017). The authors compare their extracted system radii with Hanbury-Brown-Twiss (HBT) radii from Aamodt (2011), Okorokov (2014), and Bialas (2014), finding similar behavior especially with $R_{\mathrm{long}}$. The linear correlation between Tsallis and Boltzmann temperatures found for strange particles confirms and extends the similar relation found for charged non-strange particles by Gao (2015), with the constants $a$ and $b$ depending on particle mass. This enables future work to use Boltzmann temperature as a reference temperature and to investigate the dependence of temperature on strangeness number in both extensive and non-extensive particle yield models.
