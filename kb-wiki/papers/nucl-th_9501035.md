# Resolving Power of 2-D Pion Interferometry

> A $χ^2$ analysis is performed to test the resolving power of two-dimensional pion interferometry using for illustration the preliminary E802 data on $Si+Au$ at 14.6 AGeV/c. We find that the resolving power to distinguish two decoupling geometries of different dynamical models is enhanced by studying the variation of the mean $χ^2$ per degrees of freedom with respect to the range of the analysis in the $q_T,q_L$ plane. The preliminary data seem to rule out dynamical models with significant $ω,η$ resonance formation yields.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that two-dimensional pion interferometry has sufficient resolving power to distinguish between different decoupling geometries (with and without long-lived resonance production) in heavy-ion collisions, but this resolving power depends critically on restricting the analysis to the kinematic domain where the correlation function deviates significantly from unity (Q_max ≈ 100 MeV/c); the preliminary E802 data appear to rule out dynamical models with significant ω, η resonance formation yields, though the authors caution that the data are preliminary and subject to further analysis.

## Method

The authors performed a χ² analysis of preliminary E802 π⁻π⁻ correlation data from central Si+Au reactions at 14.6 AGeV/c, comparing two dynamical scenarios from the non-ideal inside-outside cascade model: one neglecting long-lived resonances and one including Lund model resonance fractions (f(π⁻/ω)=0.16, f(π⁻/K*)=f(π⁻/(η+η′))=0.09). They computed the mean χ² per degree of freedom over a 30×30 grid in the (q_T, q_L) plane with bins of 0.01 GeV/c, varying the analysis range from 0.005 to 0.3 GeV/c, and extracted optimal values of the rms transverse radius R_T and rms decoupling proper time interval Δτ by fitting a quadratic surface to the χ² minimum.

## Key numbers

* For the no-resonance scenario with Gamow-corrected E802 data: R_T₀ = 4.6 ± 0.9 fm, Δτ₀ = 3.4 ± 0.7 fm/c, with ⟨χ²_min⟩ = 1.098
* For the Lund resonance scenario with Gamow-corrected E802 data: R_T₀ = 3.1 ± 1.3 fm, Δτ₀ = 1.6 ± 1.0 fm/c, with ⟨χ²_min⟩ = 1.104
* For the no-resonance scenario without Gamow correction: R_T₀ = 4.1 ± 1.3 fm, Δτ₀ = 3.3 ± 1.0 fm/c, ⟨χ²_min⟩ = 1.125
* For the Lund resonance scenario without Gamow correction: R_T₀ = 2.5 ± 1.3 fm, Δτ₀ = 1.4 ± 1.0 fm/c, ⟨χ²_min⟩ = 1.128
* The analysis grid covered q_T, q_L < 0.3 GeV/c with N=900 bins, giving σ ≈ 0.047 for the mean χ² distribution
* The effective transverse mass slope was fixed at T = Δp²/m = 0.17 GeV from inclusive pion distributions
* The space-time rapidity correlation width was fixed at Δη ≈ 0.8 from Lund model estimates

## Context

This work extends the multidimensional interferometry methods developed by the authors in earlier studies (Padula, Gyulassy 1994) to the AGS energy range, where they had previously shown at SPS energies that long-lived resonance production could mimic the correlation signatures expected from quark-gluon plasma formation (Pratt 1986). The analysis was motivated by fragmentary data suggesting resonance suppression at AGS, while the Lund model predicted similar resonance yields at both energy ranges. The paper enables future quantitative comparisons with cascade models such as RQMD and ARC, which showed good agreement with one-dimensional projections but poor global χ², and emphasizes the need for independent measurements of long-lived resonance abundances to reduce interferometric distortions.
