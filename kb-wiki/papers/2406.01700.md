# Anomalous thermal relaxation in warm ion plasmas

> We perform microscopic simulations of the thermal relaxation of warm neutral plasmas of astrophysical importance. Using Molecular Dynamics we study the thermal relaxation of a hot neutral fluid of finite-size neutron-rich ions kept in a fixed-wall cool box. We show how the interplay among particle size, Yukawa interaction range and density are key to understand the features of the time-dependent thermal relaxation curve $T(t)$. We show that, under certain conditions, these systems exhibit faster cooling from increasingly larger initial temperature values. They also display non-Newtonian thermal behavior, including oscillations, that can be {\it effectively} interpreted as the consequence of the existence of a non-trivial system memory function. Finally, we consider the impact of multicomponent admixtures in the simulated system. We discuss these results and their possible extensions to astrophysical scenarios, where screened plasmas with Yukawa-like electrostatic potentials are usually involved.

## Key claim

The paper claims that warm neutral plasmas of finite-size neutron-rich ions can exhibit anomalous thermal relaxation, including faster cooling from higher initial temperatures (a form of the Mpemba effect) and non-Newtonian oscillatory behavior, but only under specific conditions determined by the interplay of particle size, Yukawa interaction range, and density, not universally for all screened plasmas.

## Method

The authors performed Molecular Dynamics (MD) simulations of N ions (N ≲ 10³) in a cubic box with fixed cool walls (T_wall ≈ 0.1 MeV), using Yukawa-like screened electrostatic potentials with Gaussian charge distributions for finite-size ions. They initialized the system at high temperatures (T_init = 6, 9, 15 MeV), then tracked thermal relaxation by rescaling velocities of particles crossing box walls to extract heat, and computed the normalized velocity autocorrelation function R(t) to characterize memory effects.

## Key numbers

- Ion density: n_I = 1.1 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁻³
- Screening lengths: λ/L = 0.3 and 0.465, corresponding to λ = 45.15 fm and 70 fm respectively
- Ion species: (Z, A) = (38, 128), typical of warm proto-neutron star crust environments
- Stretched exponential exponent β = 0.38 ± 0.02 for the early temperature drop
- Relaxation times from stretched exponential fits: τ_6 = 118600 ± 9300 fm/c (T_init = 6 MeV), τ_9 = 53600 ± 3500 fm/c (T_init = 9 MeV), τ_15 = 27750 ± 990 fm/c (T_init = 15 MeV)
- Crystallization onset occurs at t ∼ 2 × 10⁴ fm/c
- Coulomb plasma parameter for classical one-component plasma in weakly screened regime: Γ_C ∼ 171.8 (reference value from Hamaguchi (1997))

## Context

This work extends previous MD studies of warm dense astrophysical plasmas by Barba-González et al. (2024) and Barba-González et al. (2022), focusing specifically on thermal relaxation dynamics rather than static properties. It connects to the Mpemba effect literature (Mpemba 1969) and memory effects in complex systems (Coleman 1964, Zwan 1960), and is relevant to understanding cooling in proto-neutron star crusts where neutrino emission (Pethick 1994) and impurity scattering (Jones 1999, Potekhin 1999) dominate. The results enable future studies of non-Markovian relaxation in astrophysical plasmas with screened Yukawa interactions.
