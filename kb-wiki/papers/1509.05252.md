# Excitation function Analysis for Charmonium Production in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Both color screening and regeneration are hot medium effects on charmonium production in heavy ion collisions. While they affect in an opposite way the charmonium yield, their competition in transverse dynamics bring sensitivity to the ratio of averaged transverse momentum suqare for charmonium, which thus can reveal more nature of the QCD medium created from the collisions. We make an excitation analysis based on transport approach to illustrate such a picuture.

# Excitation function Analysis for Charmonium Production in Heavy-Ion Collisions

## Key claim
The ratio of averaged transverse momentum square for charmonium, defined as \( r_{AA} = \langle p_t^2 \rangle_{AA} / \langle p_t^2 \rangle_{pp} \), shows a decreasing trend from SPS to RHIC to LHC, which robustly indicates a hotter QCD medium created in higher energy collisions; this trend emerges from the competition between initial production (dominant at low energy) and regeneration (dominant at high energy), unlike the yield \( R_{AA} \) which can cancel out these competing effects.

## Method
A Boltzmann-type transport equation for the charmonium phase-space distribution was solved, with loss and gain terms describing gluon dissociation (including color screening melting evaluated from a potential approach) and regeneration via detailed balance. The medium was modeled with ideal hydrodynamics providing local temperature and fluid velocity, and charm quarks were approximated as kinetically thermalized based on observed large quenching and anisotropic flow of open charm mesons. The excitation function was computed for central Au+Au collisions at RHIC and Pb+Pb collisions at SPS and LHC.

## Key numbers
- Regeneration fraction \( g_{AA} = N^{reg}_{AA}/N_{AA} \) for \( J/\psi \): near zero at SPS, equally important to initial production at RHIC mid-rapidity, and dominant at LHC in both mid and forward rapidity.
- \( r_{AA} = \langle p_t^2 \rangle_{AA} / \langle p_t^2 \rangle_{pp} \) for charmonium: greater than 1 at SPS (Cronin effect dominant), around 1.3 at RHIC forward rapidity, and decreasing at LHC where regeneration dominates; the LHC band reflects charm quark cross section uncertainty.
- High \( p_T \) \( J/\psi \) \( R_{AA} \) in mid-rapidity central collisions shows a decreasing trend from SPS to RHIC to LHC, confirming stronger hot medium effects at higher collision energies.

## Context
This work extends transport model studies of charmonium production (Tsinghua group, 2015) by focusing on the excitation function of transverse momentum observables, complementing earlier analyses of \( R_{AA} \) and anisotropic flow. The approach contrasts with potential-based analyses that treat quarkonium as a color-neutral entity, which have been questioned by lattice QCD results (Kharzeev, 2014; Lattice collaboration, 2014) showing strong entanglement between heavy quarks and the medium near \( T_c \). The proposed \( r_{AA} \) observable enables distinguishing medium effects across collision energies where yield-based \( R_{AA} \) alone is ambiguous, and the predictions are confirmed by experimental data from SPS (2000), RHIC (2012), and LHC (2013).
