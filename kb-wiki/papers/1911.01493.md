# On the effective theory of neutrino-electron and neutrino-quark interactions

> We determine the four Fermi effective theory of neutrino interactions within the Standard Model including one-loop electroweak radiative corrections, in combination with the measured muon lifetime and precision electroweak data. Including two-loop matching and three-loop running corrections, we determine lepton coefficients accounting for all large logarithms through relative order $\cal{O}(αα_s)$ and quark coefficients accounting for all large logarithms through ${\cal{O}}(α)$. We present four-fermion coefficients valid in $n_f=3$ and $n_f=4$ flavor quark theories, as well as in the extreme low-energy limit. We relate the coefficients in this limit to neutrino charge radii governing matter effects via forward neutrino scattering on charged particles.

## Key claim

The paper determines the four-fermi effective theory coefficients for neutrino-lepton and neutrino-quark interactions within the Standard Model, including one-loop electroweak radiative corrections, with the condition that the results are valid for sub-percent precision predictions only when combined with the measured muon lifetime and precision electroweak data, and when accounting for large logarithms through two-loop matching and three-loop running corrections.

## Method

The authors match the Standard Model to a low-energy effective field theory at the electroweak scale by integrating out the W and Z bosons, Higgs boson, and top quark, computing one-loop matching conditions in the MS-bar renormalization scheme. They then solve renormalization group equations to evolve the four-fermion coefficients down to the GeV energy scale, incorporating heavy quark threshold matching for bottom and charm quarks, and present results for n_f=3 and n_f=4 flavor QCD as well as the extreme low-energy QED limit.

## Context

This work extends the one-loop electroweak radiative corrections to neutrino scattering originally computed by Marciano and Sirlin (1980) using current algebra and on-shell renormalization, and by Aoki (1980), Bohm (1986), Hollik (1988), and Denner (1991) via direct Feynman diagram evaluation. The effective theory approach systematically separates electroweak and hadronic scales, enabling sub-percent precision predictions for neutrino-electron scattering (used as a standard candle for neutrino flux normalization, as demonstrated by MINERvA reducing NUMI beam flux uncertainties from 7.5% to 3.9%) and providing a rigorous starting point for nonperturbative lattice QCD evaluation of hadronic amplitudes.
