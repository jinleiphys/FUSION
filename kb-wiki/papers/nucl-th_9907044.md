# The State Dependence Calculation of Three-body Cluster Energy for Nuclear Matter

> It is shown that the method of lowest order contrained variational (LOCV) which is based on the cluster expansion theory is a reliable many-body technique to calculate the nuclear matter equation of states. In this respect the state-dependence correlation functions and effective interactions which have been produce by LOCV calculation with the Reid Soft Core and $Δ$-Reid interactions are used to estimate the size of higher order cluster terms such as the effect of three body cluster energy in nuclear matter. We find that the three-body cluster energy is less than 1 MeV beyond the nuclear matter saturation density and it has weaker density dependence than our previous calculation with the state-averaged coorrelation functions and effective interactions. Finally, we conclude that the LOCV method is good enough to calculate other propertiesn of quantal fluids.

## Key claim

The three-body cluster energy in nuclear matter, calculated using state-dependent correlation functions and effective interactions within the lowest order constrained variational (LOCV) method, is less than 1 MeV beyond the nuclear matter saturation density and has a weaker density dependence than previously estimated using a state-averaged approximation.

## Method

The authors used the LOCV method with Reid soft core and Δ-Reid nucleon-nucleon potentials to generate state-dependent correlation functions and effective interactions. They then calculated the three-body cluster energy terms \(E_{3h}^{(2)}\) and \(E_{3h}^{(3)}\) exactly using these state-dependent functions, rather than the state-averaged approximation used in prior work. The calculations were performed for nuclear matter at various densities, and the contributions from central and non-central parts of the correlation functions were separated.

## Key numbers

- The three-body cluster energy is less than 1 MeV beyond the nuclear matter saturation density.
- The state-dependent calculation yields a three-body cluster energy smaller than the state-averaged calculation, with the difference becoming sizable beyond nuclear matter saturation density.
- For \(\rho \leq 0.3 \, \text{fm}^{-3}\), the LOCV nuclear matter results agree extremely well with Variational Hypernetted Chain (VHC) calculations.
- The Fermi momentum is defined as \(k_F = (3/2 \pi^2 \rho)^{1/3}\), where \(\rho\) is the density of uniform nuclear matter.

## Context

This work extends the LOCV method developed in 1977-1979 by the same group and others, which had previously calculated three-body cluster energy using a state-averaged approximation (Moshfegh and Modarres, prior work). The present state-dependent calculation shows that the earlier state-averaged approximation overestimates the three-body cluster energy at high densities. The results are comparable to more complicated many-body approaches such as Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF), Correlated Basis Function (CBF), Brueckner-Bethe (BB), and Variational Hypernetted Chain (VHC) techniques. The work enables more reliable calculations of nuclear matter properties using the LOCV method, which is simpler than these other approaches.
