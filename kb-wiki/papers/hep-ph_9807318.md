# Three-Flavor QCD at High Density: Color Flavor Locking and Chiral Symmetry Breaking

> We propose a symmetry breaking scheme for QCD with three massless quarks at high baryon density wherein the color and flavor SU(3)_{color}xSU(3)_{L}xSU(3)_{R} symmetries are broken down to the diagonal subgroup SU(3)_{color+L+R} by the formation of a condensate of quark Cooper pairs. We discuss general properties that follow from this hypothesis, including the existence of gaps for quark and gluon excitations, the existence of Nambu-Goldstone bosons which are excitations of the diquark condensate, and the existence of a modified electromagnetic gauge interaction which is unbroken and which assigns integral charge to the elementary excitations. We present mean-field results for a Hamiltonian in which the interaction between quarks is modelled by that induced by single-gluon exchange. We find gaps of order 10-100 MeV for plausible values of the coupling. We discuss the effects of nonzero temperature, nonzero quark masses and instanton-induced interactions on our results.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that at high baryon density, QCD with three massless quarks undergoes a symmetry breaking pattern where color and flavor SU(3) symmetries are locked together by a diquark condensate, breaking to a diagonal SU(3) subgroup; this claim is presented as a hypothesis whose viability is tested through mean-field calculations in a model Hamiltonian, with the caveat that the analysis uses a simplified interaction and neglects the effects of nonzero strange quark mass and instanton-induced interactions in the quantitative estimates.

## Method

The authors construct a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model Hamiltonian whose four-fermion interaction has the color, flavor, and spinor structure of single-gluon exchange, including a schematic form factor to account for the weakening of QCD interactions at high momentum. They derive mean-field gap equations via the Bogoliubov-Valatin approach for the two independent gap parameters κ₁ and κ₂ that characterize the diquark condensate, and solve these equations to estimate the gap magnitude as a function of the interaction strength K. The coupling strength K is calibrated by computing the zero-density chiral gap from the same interaction.

## Key numbers

* Gaps for quark excitations are found to be of order 10-100 MeV for plausible values of the coupling.
* The strange quark mass m_s is taken to be of order 100 MeV, which is argued to be small compared to the chemical potentials of interest.
* The condensate involves two independent gap parameters κ₁ and κ₂; solutions of the coupled gap equations do not yield κ₁ = -κ₂ (the antisymmetric case), meaning the Cooper pairs are symmetric under simultaneous color and flavor exchange.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous two-flavor analysis (Rajagopal, 1998) where a diquark condensate broke color SU(3) to SU(2) while leaving chiral symmetry unbroken, and where gaps of order 50-100 MeV were found using an instanton-based interaction. The three-flavor color-flavor locked phase proposed here is a natural generalization that allows all three colors and all three flavors to participate in the condensate, and it enables future studies of the QCD phase diagram at high density, including the effects of nonzero temperature, nonzero quark masses, and instanton-induced interactions on the symmetry breaking pattern.
