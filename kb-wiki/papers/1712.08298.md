# Nucleon shadowing effects in $Cu+Cu$ and $Au+Au$ collisions at RHIC within the HIJING code

> The centrality dependence of pseudorapidity density of charged particles ($ dN_{ch}/dη$) in $Cu+Cu$ ($Au+Au$) collisions at RHIC energy of $ \sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}=22.4$ , $62.4 $ and $200$ ($19.6$, $62.4$ and $200$) GeV, is investigated within an improved HIJING code. The standard HIJING model is enhanced by a prescription for collective nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) interactions and more modern parton distribution functions. The collective $NN$-interactions are used to induce both cascade and nucleon shadowing effects. We find collective cascade broadens the pseudorapidity distributions in the tails (at $ |η|> y_{\rm beam} $) above $25-30\%$ collision centrality to be consistent with the $ dN_{ch}/dη$ data at $ \sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}=19.6$, $22.4$, $62.4 $ GeV. The overall contribution of nucleon shadowing is shown to depress the whole shape of $ dN_{ch}/dη$ in the primary interaction region (at $ |η|< y_{\rm beam} $) for semiperipheral ($ 20-25 $ \%) and peripheral ($\ge 35-40$\%) $Cu+Cu$ ($Au+Au$) interactions at $ \sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}=200$ GeV, in accordance with the PHOBOS data.

# Nucleon shadowing effects in Cu+Cu and Au+Au collisions at RHIC within the HIJING code

## Key claim

The improved HIJING model (ImHIJING/Cas) reproduces the centrality dependence of charged particle pseudorapidity density in Cu+Cu and Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies only when both collective cascade effects and an impact-parameter-dependent nucleon shadowing prescription are included, with the nucleon shadowing effects becoming pronounced only at the highest RHIC energy of sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV and exhibiting a strong centrality dependence.

## Method

The authors modified the standard HIJING 1.383 code by implementing a collective cascade recipe that induces both cascade and nucleon shadowing effects, using MSTW2009 parton distribution functions. The collective cascade model determines the probability of a primary interacting nucleon involving additional secondary nucleons via a Gaussian transverse-distance weighting, with the strength parameter C varied between 0.25 and 0.5. An impact-parameter-dependent width parameter gamma(c) was introduced for the fractional momentum distribution of wounded nucleons, constrained by fitting Au+Au data at sqrt(s_NN)=19.6 GeV, while parton shadowing parameters s_q(g) were separately tuned at each higher energy.

## Key numbers

- Cascade strength parameter C=0.5 for Au+Au and C=0.25 for Cu+Cu collisions at lower RHIC energies (19.6 and 22.4 GeV)
- Parton shadowing parameter s_q(g)=0.01 (0.014) for Cu+Cu (Au+Au) at sqrt(s_NN)=62.4 GeV, and s_q(g)=0.07 (0.08) for Cu+Cu (Au+Au) at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV
- Width parameter gamma(c)=0.1+2c(1-0.75*sqrt(c)), yielding gamma approximately 0.1-0.2 for central (0-10%) collisions and 0.6 for peripheral (>50-60%) collisions
- Ratio of primary to secondary nucleons approximately 11 (62) for 0-10% most central Cu+Cu (Au+Au) collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, dropping to roughly 0.5 for peripheral (35-50%) collisions
- Collective cascade broadens pseudorapidity distributions in tails at |eta|>y_beam above 25-30% collision centrality at sqrt(s_NN)=19.6, 22.4, and 62.4 GeV
- Nucleon shadowing depresses dN_ch/deta in the primary interaction region (|eta|<y_beam) for semiperipheral (20-25%) and peripheral (>=35-40%) collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous ImHIJING/Cas model (Abdel-Waged and Felemban, 2015-2016) from LHC p+Pb and Pb+Pb collisions down to RHIC energies, where a single parameter set was previously used. The study constrains the collective cascade parameters using PHOBOS data (2005) for Au+Au at 19.6 GeV, then applies the model to Cu+Cu and Au+Au at higher RHIC energies. The extracted parton shadowing parameters (s_q(g)=0.01-0.08) are significantly smaller than the HIJING 2.0 estimate of 0.17-0.22 and the AMPT estimate of 0.1-0.17, indicating stronger constraints from RHIC data. The work enables future studies of the interplay between nucleon shadowing, parton shadowing, and parton energy loss in heavy ion collisions, though the authors note the model calculations remain narrower than data at 200 GeV, suggesting the need for improved string fragmentation or final state interaction treatments.
