# Impact of active-sterile neutrino mixing on supernova explosion and nucleosynthesis

> We show that for the active-sterile flavor mixing parameters suggested by the reactor neutrino anomaly, substantial \ν_e-\ν_s and \bar \ν_e-\bar \ν_s conversion occurs in regions with electron fraction of \approx 1/3 near the core of an 8.8 M_sun electron-capture supernova. Compared to the case without such conversion, the neutron-richness of the ejected material is enhanced to allow production of elements from Sr, Y, and Zr up to Cd in broad agreement with observations of the metal-poor star HD 122563. Active-sterile flavor conversion also strongly suppresses neutrino heating at times when it is important for the revival of the shock. Our results suggest that simulations of supernova explosion and nucleosynthesis may be used to constrain active-sterile mixing parameters in combination with neutrino experiments and cosmological considerations.

## Key claim

For the active-sterile mixing parameters suggested by the reactor neutrino anomaly (best-fit values of δm² = 1.75 eV² and sin²2θ = 0.10), substantial νₑ-νₛ and ν̄ₑ-ν̄ₛ conversion occurs in regions with electron fraction ≈ 1/3 near the core of an 8.8 M☉ electron-capture supernova, but the exact effects on supernova explosion and nucleosynthesis remain to be studied by implementing active-sterile flavor conversion self-consistently in simulations.

## Method

The authors used data from an 8.8 M☉ electron-capture supernova simulation with three-flavor Boltzmann neutrino transport and increased the resolution by adding approximately 2,000 mass elements in the inner resonance region. They recalculated the electron fraction Yₑ for each mass element using a self-consistent feedback treatment where the rates of νₑ + n → p + e⁻ and ν̄ₑ + p → n + e⁺ are modified by the hopping probabilities of νₑ and ν̄ₑ through the inner resonance, computed via the Landau-Zener formula. The recalculated Yₑ profiles were then used to determine which neutrinos crossed the inner resonance and to compute the resulting nucleosynthesis yields.

## Key numbers

- The supernova progenitor mass is 8.8 M☉ (electron-capture supernova).
- The best-fit mixing parameters used are δm² = 1.75 eV² and sin²2θ = 0.10, inferred from reactor neutrino experiments.
- The inner resonance occurs at baryon densities ρ ∼ 10⁹–10¹² g cm⁻³.
- For Eν = 10 MeV with the best-fit parameters, the oscillation length at resonance L ≈ 45 m and the resonance width δr ≈ 48 m (for ρres = 10⁹ g cm⁻³, |dYₑ/dr|res = 10⁻² km⁻¹, μ = 1).
- The Yₑ plateau forms at Yₑ = 1/3⁺ corresponding to r ∼ 106 km at tpb ∼ 34 ms.
- Approximately 10⁻² M☉ of ejecta have their Yₑ reduced by active-sterile flavor conversion.
- Without active-sterile flavor conversion, only elements with Z ≲ 30 are produced; with conversion, elements from Z = 38 (Sr) to Z = 48 (Cd) are produced.
- The Yₑ of an example mass element is reduced from 0.49 to 0.37–0.39 for tpb ≳ 200 ms.

## Context

This work extends the earlier suggestion by Nunokawa (1997) that νₑ-νₛ and ν̄ₑ-ν̄ₛ conversion could impact supernova explosion and nucleosynthesis, but goes beyond previous studies (e.g., Fetter 2002, Beun 2006, Tamborra 2011) that focused on outer resonances at ρ < 10⁸ g cm⁻³ by examining the inner resonances at ρ ∼ 10⁹–10¹² g cm⁻³ with full feedback between flavor conversion and electron fraction. The results enable future self-consistent simulations of active-sterile flavor conversion in supernovae, which could constrain sterile neutrino mixing parameters in combination with neutrino experiments and cosmological considerations, and may help resolve difficulties in producing elements with Z > 42 in neutrino-driven winds from more massive supernovae.
