# Hydrodynamics of nuclear collisions with initial conditions from perturbative QCD

> We compute the longitudinal hydrodynamic flow in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 5500 GeV by using boost non-invariant initial conditions following from perturbative QCD. The transfer of entropy and energy from the central region to larger rapidities caused by boost non-invariance is determined and the associated decrease in the lifetime of the system is estimated.

## Key claim

The paper shows that at LHC energies (√s = 5500 GeV), the boost non-invariant initial conditions from perturbative QCD lead to only modest deviations from the Bjorken similarity flow: the energy density in the central region decreases about 12% faster than in the boost-invariant case, and the lifetime of the plasma phase decreases by about 9%, with the longitudinal flow acceleration being at the 1% level rather than 10%.

## Method

The authors solve the 1+1 dimensional relativistic hydrodynamic equations for longitudinal expansion using the method of characteristics, starting from initial energy density distributions computed from perturbative QCD minijet production. They assume an ideal fluid with a massless equation of state (p = ε/3), zero chemical potential, and an initial similarity flow (Θ = η), then numerically integrate the equations to determine the evolution of energy density, flow rapidity, and entropy distributions as functions of proper time and space-time rapidity.

## Key numbers

- Initial energy density at η=0 for √s = 5500 GeV: approximately 1000 GeV/fm³ at τ_i = 0.1 fm
- Gaussian width parameter σ = 3.8 for the initial energy density distribution at LHC (√s = 5500 GeV); at RHIC (√s = 200 GeV) the width is σ = 2.05
- The additional decrease in energy density at η=0 relative to Bjorken flow is about 12% over the plasma phase duration (from τ_i to about 100τ_i, down to ε ≈ 2 GeV/fm³)
- The lifetime of the system in the plasma phase decreases by about 9% due to the longitudinal density gradient
- Net baryon number-to-entropy ratio at y=0 is about 1/5000
- The initial thermalization time τ_i = 0.1 fm, with minimum p_T = p_0 = 2 GeV corresponding to the saturation limit at LHC
- Nuclear overlap function T_AA(0) ≈ 32/mb for Pb+Pb

## Context

This work extends the Bjorken similarity flow model (Bjorken, 1983) by using realistic initial conditions from perturbative QCD calculations (Eskola, Kajantie, and Ruuskanen, previous work) rather than assuming boost invariance. The results confirm the assumptions of the "frozen motion" model used by von Gersdorff et al. (previous work) that at LHC energies the longitudinal flow velocity approximately scales even without boost invariance. The study is limited to LHC energies where perturbative calculations are more reliable and the plasma phase dominates; at RHIC and SPS energies, soft components and phase transition effects would require additional modeling.
