# Flavor Fragmentation Function Factorization

> A definition of partonic jet flavor that is both theoretically well-defined and experimentally robust would have profound implications for measurements and predictions especially for heavy flavor applications. Recently, a definition of jet flavor was introduced as the net flavor flowing along the direction of the Winner-Take-All axis of a jet which is soft safe to all orders, but not collinear safe. Here, we exploit the lack of collinear safety and propose a factorization theorem of perturbative flavor fragmentation functions that resum collinear divergences and describe the evolution of flavor from the short distance of jet production to the long distance at which hadronization occurs. Collinear flavor evolution is governed by a small modification of the DGLAP equations. We present a detailed all-orders analysis and identify exact relations that must hold amongst the various anomalous dimensions by probability conservation and the existence of fixed points of the renormalization group flow. We explicitly validate the factorization theorem at one-loop order, and demonstrate its consistency at two loops in particular flavor channels. Starting at two-loops, constraints on phase space imposed by flavor measurements potentially allow for non-trivial soft contributions, but we demonstrate that they are scaleless and so explicitly vanish, ensuring that soft particles are summed inclusively and all divergences are exclusively collinear in nature. This factorization theorem opens the door to precision calculations with identified flavor in the infrared.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a factorization theorem for the Winner-Take-All (WTA) jet flavor definition that resums collinear divergences and describes flavor evolution from the short-distance scale of jet production to the long-distance scale where hadronization occurs, but this factorization is only valid when the transverse momentum cutoff scale \(k_\perp^2\) is much larger than the QCD scale \(\Lambda_\text{QCD}^2\), and the authors acknowledge they lack a formal rigorous proof of the factorization within soft-collinear effective theory.

## Method

The authors define jet flavor as the net flavor flowing along the Winner-Take-All axis of a jet, obtained by reclustering jet constituents with the \(k_T\) algorithm using the WTA recombination scheme. They formulate a factorization theorem in soft-collinear effective theory (SCET) where the cross section for producing hemisphere WTA flavors \(f_L f_R\) in \(e^+e^-\) collisions factorizes into a hard function describing production at scale \(Q^2\) and two jet functions describing flavor evolution from the factorization scale \(\mu^2\) down to the measurement scale \(k_\perp^2\), with evolution governed by modified DGLAP equations. The authors validate the factorization at one-loop order and demonstrate consistency at two loops in particular flavor channels, showing that soft contributions are scaleless and vanish.

## Key numbers

- The factorization theorem is valid in the limit \(Q^2/k_\perp^2 \to \infty\), with corrections expected as powers of \(k_\perp^2/Q^2 \ll 1\).
- The termination scale of the parton shower is taken as \(k_\perp^2 \sim 1 \text{ GeV}^2\), just before hadronization, with the condition \(k_\perp^2 \gg \Lambda_\text{QCD}^2\).
- For \(n_f\) active quark flavors, the equilibrium quark flavor fraction is \(H_q = \gamma_{g\to q}/(2n_f \gamma_{g\to q} + \gamma_{q\to g})\) and the gluon fraction is \(H_g = \gamma_{q\to g}/(2n_f \gamma_{g\to q} + \gamma_{q\to g})\).
- There are six independent anomalous dimensions regardless of the number of active quark flavors: \(\gamma_{q\to q}\), \(\gamma_{q\to \bar q}\), \(\gamma_{q\to q'}\), \(\gamma_{g\to g}\), \(\gamma_{g\to q}\), and \(\gamma_{q\to g}\).

## Context

This work extends the leading-logarithmic analysis of Caletti (2022), who introduced the WTA flavor fragmentation function but only performed resummation to leading-logarithmic accuracy. The factorization theorem enables next-to-leading logarithmic resummation and matching to next-to-next-to-leading fixed order calculations, opening the door to precision calculations with identified flavor in the infrared for heavy-flavor jet production at the LHC, building on fixed-order calculations by Czakon (2020), Gauld (2020), and Catani (2020).
