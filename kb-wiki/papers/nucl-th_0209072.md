# Resolving the Large-N Nuclear Potential Puzzle

> The large $N_c$ nuclear potential puzzle arose because three- and higher-meson exchange contributions to the nucleon-nucleon potential did not automatically yield cancellations that make these contributions consistent with the general large $N_c$ scaling rules for the potential. Here it is proposed that the resolution to this puzzle is that the scaling rules only apply for energy-independent potentials while all of the cases with apparent inconsistencies were for energy-dependent potentials. It is shown explicitly how energy-dependent potentials can have radically different large N behavior than an equivalent energy-independent one. One class of three-meson graphs is computed in which the contribution to the energy-independent potential is consistent with the general large N rules even though the energy-dependent potential is not.

## Key claim

The large-\(N_c\) nuclear potential puzzle, that three- and higher-meson exchange contributions to the nucleon-nucleon potential appeared inconsistent with the general large-\(N_c\) scaling rules, is resolved by recognizing that the scaling rules apply only to energy-independent potentials, whereas the earlier analyses that found inconsistencies had used energy-dependent potentials.

## Method

The paper first demonstrates with a toy model how an energy-dependent potential and an energy-independent potential that yield identical on-shell scattering amplitudes can have radically different large-\(N_c\) scaling behavior. It then proposes a new organizing principle: for two-particle irreducible diagrams, the potential contribution is taken as the static (zero-energy) limit of the diagram; for two-particle reducible graphs, one subtracts all contributions from places where one nucleon is on-shell and replaces the remaining subdiagrams by their static limits. This procedure is applied to the specific case of three scalar-isoscalar meson exchange with a seagull vertex (fig. 1), and the resulting energy-independent potential is shown to be consistent with the Kaplan-Manohar scaling rules.

## Key numbers

* The leading large-\(N_c\) scaling rules for the nucleon-nucleon potential are \(V_{I=J} \sim N_c\) and \(V_{I \ne J} \sim N_c^{-1}\) (eq. 1).
* For the three-meson exchange seagull graphs, the overall prefactor from coupling constants is \(N_c^2\) (from \(g_{1m}^4 g_{2m}\) with \(g_{1m} \sim N_c^{1/2}\) and \(g_{2m} \sim N_c^0\)), which must cancel to relative order \(N_c^{-1}\) for consistency.
* The nucleon mass scales as \(M_N \sim N_c\); meson masses are of order \(N_c^0\); momenta are of order \(N_c^0\).
* The recoil correction in the fermion propagator is a \(1/N_c\) correction except near the pole; the delta function fixing \(\omega_1\) restricts it to order \(N_c^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Banerjee, Cohen, and Gelman (2001), who showed that two-meson exchange cancellations are consistent with large-\(N_c\) scaling, and directly addresses the puzzle raised by Belitsky and Cohen (2002), who found that three- and higher-meson exchange graphs did not automatically cancel. The resolution, that energy-dependent potentials can have different \(N_c\) scaling than energy-independent ones, enables future work to test consistency for more complex multi-meson exchange graphs (such as ladders and crossed-ladders) using the energy-independent organizing principle, and supports the meson-exchange picture of nucleon-nucleon interactions derived from large-\(N_c\) QCD.
