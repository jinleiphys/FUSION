# Nonperturbative Gluon Radiation and Energy Dependence of Elastic Scattering

> The energy dependence of the total hadronic cross sections is caused by gluon bremsstrahlung which we treat nonperturbatively. It is located at small transverse distances about 0.3 fm from the valence quarks. The cross section of gluon radiation is predicted to exponentiate and rise with energy as s^Δ with Δ=0.17 +/- 0.01. The total cross section also includes a large energy independent Born term which corresponds to no gluon radiation. The calculated total cross section and the slope of elastic scattering are in good agreement with the data.

# Nonperturbative Gluon Radiation and Energy Dependence of Elastic Scattering

## Key claim

The energy dependence of total hadronic cross sections arises from nonperturbative gluon bremsstrahlung occurring at small transverse distances of about 0.3 fm from valence quarks, but this radiative component constitutes only a small fraction of the total cross section; a large energy-independent Born term from scattering of the valence quark skeleton without gluon radiation dominates, and the steep rise with energy as s^Δ (Δ=0.17±0.01) does not contradict data because the radiative part is small.

## Method

The authors sum contributions of different Fock components of the incident hadron, starting with a Born term containing only valence quarks and adding contributions from radiation of n gluons. The nonperturbative quark-gluon interaction is treated using a light-cone potential with a parameter b₀=0.65 GeV fixed by data on large-mass diffractive dissociation, and the total cross section is obtained by exponentiating the single-gluon radiation cross section in leading-log approximation. The partial amplitudes are unitarized using a quasi-eikonal model, and the elastic slope is computed from the impact-parameter distribution of the unitarized amplitude.

## Key numbers

- Gluon radiation occurs at transverse distances r₀ ≈ 0.3 fm from valence quarks, corresponding to the parameter b₀ = 0.65 GeV
- The exponent governing the energy rise is Δ = 0.17 ± 0.01, derived from the mean QCD coupling ⟨α_s⟩ = 0.4
- The energy-independent Born term is fixed at σ̃₀ = 39.7 mb using data at √s = 546 GeV
- The mean number of gluons in a quark is ⟨n⟩ = 0.7–1 at ISR energies and reaches about 2 at the Tevatron
- The Reggeon term for pp is σ_R^{pp} = 17.8 mb/√(s/s₀) and for p̄p is σ_R^{p̄p} = 32.8 mb/√(s/s₀), with s₀ = 30 GeV²
- The dipole cross section factor C = 2.3 at ρ = 1/b₀, calculated with gluon effective mass 0.15 GeV and α_s = 0.4

## Context

This work extends the nonperturbative treatment of gluon radiation developed by Kopeliovich, Schäfer, and Tarasov (1999) to explain the energy dependence of total hadronic cross sections, a challenge since the effect was first observed at the ISR in 1973. It contrasts with the perturbative BFKL approach applicable to deep inelastic scattering, and with the approach of Kharzeev and Levin (1999) and Shuryak (2000) who found Δ ≈ 0.05 from radiation of colorless gluon pairs. The results enable predictions for total pp and p̄p cross sections and elastic slopes that agree with data from the Particle Data Group compilation, and the framework can be extended to further elastic scattering comparisons.
