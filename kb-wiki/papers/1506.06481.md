# Neutron star structure in an in-medium modified chiral soliton model

> We study the internal structure of a static and spherically symmetric neutron star in the framework of an in-medium modified chiral soliton model. The Equations of State describing an infinite and asymmetric nuclear matter are obtained introducing the density dependent functions into the low energy free space Lagrangian of the model starting from the phenomenology of pionic atoms. The parametrizations of density dependent functions are related to the properties of isospin asymmetric nuclear systems at saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter $ρ_0\simeq 0.16$~fm$^{-3}$. Our results, corresponding to the compressibility of symmetric nuclear matter in the range $250\,\mbox{MeV}\le K_0\le 270\,\mbox{MeV}$ and the slop parameter value of symmetry energy in the range $30\,\mbox{MeV}\le L_S\le 50\,\mbox{MeV}$, are consistent with the results from other approaches and with the experimental indications. Using the modified Equations of State, near the saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter $ρ_0$, the extrapolations to the high density and highly isospin asymmetric regions have been performed. The calculations showed that the properties of $\sim 1.4M_\odot$ and $\sim 2M_\odot$ neutron stars can be well reproduced in the framework of present approach.

## Key claim

The in-medium modified chiral soliton model, using density-dependent functions fitted to symmetric nuclear matter properties at saturation density, can reproduce the properties of both ~1.4M⊙ and ~2M⊙ neutron stars when extrapolated to high density and highly isospin asymmetric regions, provided the compressibility of symmetric nuclear matter is in the range 250 MeV ≤ K₀ ≤ 270 MeV and the slope parameter of the symmetry energy is in the range 30 MeV ≤ L_S ≤ 50 MeV.

## Method

The author starts from an in-medium modified Skyrme Lagrangian with density-dependent functions (ατ, αs, ζτ, ζs, αm, αe) that encode the influence of the nuclear environment on single-soliton properties. These functions are parametrized linearly in density using five parameters (C₁–C₅), which are fixed by reproducing the saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter (binding energy per nucleon ≈ −16 MeV at ρ₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³) and the symmetry energy parameters at saturation. The resulting equations of state for neutron matter (δ = 1) are then used as input to the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to compute the mass-radius relation and internal structure of static, spherically symmetric neutron stars.

## Key numbers

* Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: ρ₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³.
* Compressibility of symmetric nuclear matter: K₀ = 250 MeV (Model II, softer EOS) or K₀ = 270 MeV (Model III, stiffer EOS).
* Slope parameter of symmetry energy: L_S = 30, 40, or 50 MeV (various parameter sets in Table II).
* Symmetry energy at saturation density: ε_S(ρ₀) = 30 MeV or 32 MeV (Table II).
* For Model III (K₀ = 270 MeV), maximal neutron star mass: M_max = 2.226 M⊙ (Set III-a) down to 2.216 M⊙ (Set III-f), with radii between 10.498 km and 10.616 km (Table III).
* For Model III, a ~1.4 M⊙ neutron star has radius between 11.587 km (Set III-a) and 11.992 km (Set III-f) (Table III).
* Central number density for maximal mass stars in Model III: n_c ≈ 1.037–1.047 fm⁻³; central energy density ρ_c ≈ 2.424–2.452 × 10¹⁵ g/cm³ (Table III).
* Skewness parameter Q for symmetric matter: Q = −545 MeV (Set I, K₀=230 MeV), −279 MeV (Set II, K₀=250 MeV), −133 MeV (Set III, K₀=270 MeV) (Table I).
* Symmetry energy at subnormal density: ε_S(ρ=0.1 fm⁻³) ranges from 22.06 MeV (Set II-c) to 26.46 MeV (Set III-d); at ρ=0.11 fm⁻³ from 23.64 MeV (Set II-c) to 27.69 MeV (Set III-d) (Table II).

## Context

This work extends the in-medium modified chiral soliton model previously developed by Yakhshiev (2014, 2013) from studies of symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter at saturation density to the high-density, highly isospin-asymmetric regime relevant for neutron stars. The results are compared with the APR (Akmal-Pandharipande-Ravenhall) predictions (1998) based on Argonne v₁₈ two-nucleon interactions, with Skyrme-Hartree-Fock results from Chabanat et al. (1997), and with the MDI (isospin- and momentum-dependent interaction) model of Das et al. (2002) and the MID (momentum-independent) model of Chen et al. (2009). The calculated neutron star masses and radii are consistent with observational constraints from quiescent low-mass X-ray binaries (Guillot et al., 2013) and from accreting and bursting sources (Steiner et al., 2012). The approach enables further tests of the model's applicability to extreme-density systems and provides a framework for connecting pionic-atom phenomenology to neutron star structure.
