# A novel treatment of the proton-proton Coulomb force in elastic proton-deuteron Faddeev calculations

> We propose a novel approach to incorporate the proton-proton (pp) Coulomb force into the three-nucleon (3N) Faddeev calculations. The main new ingredient is a 3-dimensional screened pp Coulomb t-matrix obtained by a numerical solution of the 3-dimensional Lippmann-Schwinger (LS) equation. We demonstrate numerically and provide analytical insight that the elastic proton-deuteron (pd) observables can be determined directly from the resulting on shell 3N amplitude increasing the screening radius. The screening limit exists without the need of renormalisation not only for observables but for the elastic pd amplitude itself.

## Key claim

The authors propose that elastic proton-deuteron observables and even the elastic scattering amplitude itself can be obtained directly from the on-shell three-nucleon amplitude by increasing the screening radius of the proton-proton Coulomb force, without requiring any renormalisation procedure, contrary to the standard approach where renormalisation is necessary.

## Method

The authors solve the three-nucleon Faddeev equations in momentum space using a screened proton-proton Coulomb interaction, but replace the usual partial-wave decomposition of the Coulomb t-matrix with a fully three-dimensional screened pp Coulomb t-matrix obtained by numerically solving the three-dimensional Lippmann-Schwinger equation. The Faddeev equations are reformulated so that high partial-wave contributions of the Coulomb force are treated via this three-dimensional t-matrix, while low partial waves (where the strong nuclear force also acts) are handled in the standard partial-wave basis. The screening limit is then approached by increasing the screening radius numerically.

## Key numbers

- For exponential screening \(e^{-(r/R)^n}\) with \(n=4\) and screening radius \(R=20\) fm, all partial waves \(l \le l_{max}=10\) are required to reproduce the three-dimensional screened pp Coulomb t-matrix at \(E_p^{lab}=13\) MeV.
- Increasing the screening radius to \(R=120\) fm requires \(l_{max} \approx 50\) partial wave states for the same energy.
- At energies below approximately 20 MeV, theory without Coulomb underestimates the space-star (SST) pd cross sections by about 10% and overestimates the pp quasi-free-scattering (QFS) cross sections by about 20%.

## Context

This work extends the momentum-space Faddeev approach for three-nucleon scattering, which previously included the Coulomb force only through a partial-wave decomposition with renormalisation (Deltuva et al., 2005). It contrasts with the variational hyperspherical harmonic approach (Kievsky et al., 2000) that uses the exact Coulomb force in coordinate representation. The new method enables more reliable treatment of the Coulomb force in configurations where partial-wave convergence is problematic, such as the pp quasi-free-scattering and space-star geometries, and provides a foundation for testing chiral effective field theory forces (Epelbaum, 2006) in proton-deuteron scattering without Coulomb uncertainties.
