# Scalar quanta in Fermi liquids: zero sounds, instabilities, Bose condensation, and a metastable state in dilute nuclear matter

> Spectrum of bosonic scalar-mode excitations in a normal Fermi liquid with a local scalar interaction is investigated for various values and momentum dependence of the scalar Landau parameter $f_0$ in the particle-hole channel. For $f_0 >0$ the conditions are found when the phase velocity on the spectrum of the zero sound acquires a minimum at a non-zero momentum. For $-1<f_0 <0$ there are only damped excitations, and for $f_0<-1$ the spectrum becomes unstable against a growth of scalar-mode excitations. An effective Lagrangian for the scalar excitation modes is derived after performing a bosonization procedure. We demonstrate that the instability may be tamed by the formation of a static Bose condensate of the scalar modes. The condensation may occur in a homogeneous or inhomogeneous state relying on the momentum dependence of the scalar Landau parameter. We show that in the isospin-symmetric nuclear matter there may appear a metastable state at a subsaturation nuclear density owing to the condensate. Then we consider a possibility of the condensation of the zero-sound-like excitations in a state with a non-zero momentum in Fermi liquids moving with overcritical velocities, provided an appropriate momentum dependence of the Landau parameter $f_0 (k)>0$. We also argue that in peripheral heavy-ion collisions the Pomeranchuk instability may occur already for $f_0 >-1$.

## Key claim

For a strongly attractive scalar Landau parameter \(f_0 < -1\), the usual Pomeranchuk instability (which would lead to exponential growth of density fluctuations and a liquid-gas phase transition) may instead be tamed by the formation of a static Bose condensate of scalar modes, producing a metastable state in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter at subsaturation densities.

## Method

The authors solve the Landau particle-hole amplitude equation for a one-component normal Fermi liquid with a local scalar interaction, using the Lindhard function to determine the spectrum of scalar-mode excitations for various values and momentum dependence of the scalar Landau parameter \(f_0\). They perform a bosonization procedure to derive an effective Lagrangian for the scalar excitation modes, and then study the conditions under which a static Bose condensate of these modes can form, either homogeneously or inhomogeneously, to stabilize the system against the Pomeranchuk instability.

## Key numbers

- For \(f_{00} \ll 1\), the zero-sound phase velocity parameter \(s_0\) is given by \(s_0 = 1 + C[1 + (4+5f_{00})(C/2f_{00}) + (24+52f_{00}+29f_{00}^2)(C/2f_{00})^2]\) where \(C = (2/e^2)\exp(-2/f_{00})\).
- For \(f_{00} \gg 1\), the asymptotic solution for the zero-sound phase velocity is \(s_0 = \sqrt{f_{00}/3}\).
- The instability increment for \(f_{00}<-1\) has a maximum at momentum \(k_m = 2p_F\sqrt{z_f}\) with value \(-i\omega_m = (8/3\pi) v_F p_F z_f^{3/2}\), where \(z_f = 1 - 1/|f_{00}|\).
- For isospin-symmetric nuclear matter, the linear density dependence of the scalar Landau parameter is \(\tilde{f}_0(n) = -2.5 + 2.75 n/n_0\), where \(n_0\) is the nuclear saturation density.
- The liquid-gas phase transition in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter occurs for low temperatures \(T \lesssim 20\) MeV and baryon densities \(n \lesssim 0.7 n_0\).

## Context

This work extends the Landau Fermi liquid theory (Landau, 1956) and the Pomeranchuk stability analysis (Pomeranchuk, 1958) by showing that the instability for \(f_0<-1\) need not lead exclusively to a liquid-gas phase transition (as studied by Chomaz (2003) and others) but can instead result in a Bose condensate of scalar modes. The authors apply their results to isospin-symmetric nuclear matter, where the density-dependent Landau parameters from Migdal (1967) and Saperstein and Fayans (1990) indicate that \(f_{00}<-1\) occurs at subsaturation densities. This work enables future studies of metastable nuclear matter states and suggests that in peripheral heavy-ion collisions, the Pomeranchuk instability may occur already for \(f_0 > -1\).
