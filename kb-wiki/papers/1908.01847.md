# A cross-channel study of pion scattering from lattice QCD

> We use a chiral model for pion interactions, in the inverse amplitude formalism, to perform a simultaneous analysis of lattice QCD results for pion-pion scattering in all three isospin channels. The input is the finite-volume two-pion spectrum computed using lattice QCD from six ensembles on lattices elongated in one of the spatial dimensions. A two-flavor dynamical lattice QCD action is used with two quark masses corresponding to a pion mass of 315 MeV and 224 MeV. The spectrum in the elastic region is subjected to a global fit which takes into account full correlations across isospin, pion mass and decay constant. The parameters from the fit are used to perform a chiral extrapolation to the physical point. The cross-channel fit results in a more precise determination of the parameters of the model when compared with single channel fits. We obtain $m_πa_0^{I=0}=0.2132(9)$, and $m_πa_0^{I=2}=0.0433(2)$ as well as $m_σ=443(3)-i221(6)$ MeV and $m_ρ=724(4)-i67(1)$ MeV. Several aspects of scale setting and consistency with previous analyses of lattice QCD results are discussed as well.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a simultaneous (cross-channel) fit of lattice QCD data for pion-pion scattering in all three isospin channels, using the modified inverse amplitude method (mIAM) matched to next-to-leading-order chiral perturbation theory, yields significantly more precise low-energy constants than single-channel fits, but the fit quality is limited by tensions between heavy and light pion mass ensembles and by the model's chiral truncation at the heavier pion mass (315 MeV).

## Method

The authors performed a global correlated fit of the mIAM scattering amplitude to 88 finite-volume energy eigenvalues from six lattice QCD ensembles (two quark masses corresponding to pion masses of 224 MeV and 315 MeV, with three box geometries each) across all three isospin channels (I=0,1,2). The fit simultaneously varied three low-energy constants (l1^r, l2^r, l4^r) and four parameters for the pion masses and decay constants of the heavy and light ensembles, using the full covariance matrices that include correlations between different isospin channels, energy levels, pion masses, and decay constants within each ensemble. The finite-volume spectrum was computed using Luscher's method with elongated boxes, and the fitted parameters were then used to perform a chiral extrapolation to the physical pion mass.

## Key numbers

* Scattering lengths at physical pion mass: m_pi * a_0^{I=0} = 0.2132(9) and m_pi * a_0^{I=2} = 0.0433(2)
* Sigma meson pole: m_sigma = 443(3) - i 221(6) MeV
* Rho meson pole: m_rho = 724(4) - i 67(1) MeV
* Fitted low-energy constants (at scale mu = 770 MeV): l1^r = -4.07^{+0.12}_{-0.13} x 10^{-3}, l2^r = +5.14^{+0.23}_{-0.19} x 10^{-3}, l4^r = +9.05^{+0.54}_{-0.70} x 10^{-3}
* Fit quality: chi^2/dof = 218/(88-7) ≈ 2.7, with separate fits giving chi^2_light = 63.8/(47-5) ≈ 1.5 and chi^2_heavy = 107.2/(43-5) ≈ 2.8
* Lattice spacings: a = 0.1210(2)(24) fm for the heavy ensembles and a = 0.1215(3)(24) fm for the light ensembles

## Context

This work extends previous single-channel analyses of the same lattice QCD ensembles for I=2 (Culver 2019), I=1 (Guo 2016), and I=0 (Guo 2018) by performing the first simultaneous cross-channel fit that accounts for correlations between different isospin channels. The approach addresses discrepancies noted in earlier studies using SU(3) unitarized chiral perturbation theory (Hu 2016, Hu 2017) where data from different N_f=2 lattice QCD groups extrapolated to consistently low physical rho masses. The results enable more precise chiral extrapolations to the physical point and provide a testbed for comparing lattice QCD predictions with experimental phase shifts from Batley (2010), Froggatt (1977), and others.
