# Ground-state correlations and final state interactions in exclusive lepton scattering off few-nucleon systems

> The two nucleon emission process off ^3He induced by medium energy electrons has been theoretically analyzed using realistic three-nucleon wave functions and taking the final state interaction into account. Various kinematical conditions have been considered in order to clarify the question whether the effects of the final state interaction could be minimized by a proper choice of the kinematics.

## Key claim

The authors show that final state interaction (FSI) effects in two-nucleon emission from ³He can be minimized by selecting kinematics in the "two-nucleon correlation" region, where the excitation energy of the spectator pair satisfies \(E^* \simeq \mathbf{k}_1^2/4M\), but this minimization does not hold for all kinematics, in particular, when the struck nucleon is initially at rest (\(k_1 \simeq 0\)), the NN rescattering fully distorts the plane-wave predictions.

## Method

The authors calculated the cross section for the reactions ³He(e,e'pp)n and ³He(e,e'np)p using realistic three-nucleon wave functions from the AV18 interaction, solving the Schrödinger equation exactly. They computed the spectral function and the transition amplitude \(M(\mathbf{k}_1, \mathbf{t})\) under three levels of approximation: no FSI (plane waves), PWIA including NN rescattering between the two detected nucleons, and a full treatment including three-body rescattering via an extended Glauber approach. The calculations were performed for various values of the missing momentum \(k_1\) and the relative momentum \(\mathbf{t}\) of the emitted pair.

## Key numbers

- The two-nucleon relative energy was fixed at \(E^* = 50\ \text{MeV}\) for the angular dependence calculations shown in Fig. 5.
- The correlation region condition is \(E^* = \mathbf{t}^2/M \simeq \mathbf{k}_1^2/4M\); calculations were performed at \(k_1 = 2.2\ \text{fm}^{-1}\) to probe this region.
- The "back-to-back" kinematics corresponded to \(\mathbf{p}_2 = -\mathbf{p}_3\) and \(\mathbf{p}_m = 0\), with the struck nucleon initially at rest (\(\mathbf{k}_1 = 0\)).
- In the three-body rescattering calculation (Fig. 7), the relative momentum was fixed at \(|\mathbf{t}| = 240\ \text{MeV}/c\).
- The spectral function was computed for several values of \(k_1\): \(0.5\ \text{fm}^{-1}\), \(1\ \text{fm}^{-1}\), \(2.2\ \text{fm}^{-1}\), and \(3\ \text{fm}^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Ciofi degli Atti, Pace, and Salme (CPS) using the Reid Soft Core interaction, now employing the more modern AV18 interaction and variational wave functions from Rosati and collaborators. The paper addresses the long-standing question of whether FSI hinders the investigation of ground-state correlations (GSC) in nuclei, a problem previously studied by Gloeckle and collaborators. The results enable future experimental studies of short-range and tensor correlations in few-body systems by identifying kinematic regions where FSI effects are minimized, particularly at high missing momentum in the two-nucleon correlation region.
