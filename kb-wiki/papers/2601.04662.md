# Scattering of a weakly bound dimer from a hard wall in one dimension

> We consider a dimer formed by two particles with an attractive contact interaction in one dimension, colliding with a hard wall. We compute the scattering phase shifts and the reflection coefficients for various collision energies and various mass ratios of the two particles. For low-energy collisions (with dimer kinetic energies much smaller than the binding energy) our results are consistent with those of D. Lee and M. Pine, The European Physical Journal A 47, 41 (2011). For mass ratios much greater than 1 we use the Born-Oppenheimer approximation to show that the scattering length and the effective range of the dimer-wall collision both depend logarithmically on the mass ratio. For collision energies much greater than the binding energy, the dissociation probability is inversely proportional to the square of the incident momentum of the dimer and we find the constant of proportionality analytically, and we use a semiclassical analysis to approximately derive the ``angular distribution" of the dissociated pair, where the ``angle" $θ$ depends on the ratio of the velocities of the two outgoing unbound particles.

# Scattering of a weakly bound dimer from a hard wall in one dimension

## Key claim
For a weakly bound dimer colliding with a hard wall in one dimension, the reflection coefficient can vanish completely at a specific collision energy for a critical mass ratio of approximately 75.8, but only when the mass ratio is not exactly 1 or 3 (the integrable cases where reflection is always total). The dissociation probability at high energies scales as the inverse square of the incident momentum with a prefactor determined analytically by the mass ratio.

## Method
The authors solve the two-body Schrödinger equation with an attractive contact interaction and hard-wall boundary conditions using a Lippmann-Schwinger integral equation derived from the Green's function of the noninteracting problem. For the special integrable cases of mass ratios 1 and 3, exact Bethe ansatz solutions are constructed. For large mass ratios, the Born-Oppenheimer approximation is employed, treating the heavy particle as moving in an effective potential generated by the light particle's bound state. A semiclassical analysis is used for high-energy collisions to derive the dissociation probability and angular distribution of the dissociated pair.

## Key numbers
- For mass ratio 1: dimer-wall scattering length a_R = a/2, effective range r_R = 0 (exact)
- For mass ratio 3: dimer-wall scattering length a_R = 3a/4, effective range r_R = 0 (exact)
- Critical mass ratio where reflection coefficient reaches zero: m_1/m_2 ≈ 75.8, occurring at K/K_th ≈ 1.24
- Born-Oppenheimer approximation for large mass ratios: a_R ≈ (1/2)[ln(m_1/m_2) + 2γ - ln 2]a, where γ ≈ 0.577216 is Euler's constant
- High-energy dissociation probability: D ≈ (m_1+m_2)^2 K_th^2 / (4 m_1 m_2 K^2)
- Angular distribution peak at high energy: θ_c = arctan(|3m_1 - m_2|/|3m_2 - m_1| * sqrt(m_2/m_1))

## Context
This work extends the study of dimer-wall collisions by Lee and Pine (2011), who computed the scattering length for the equal-mass case, to arbitrary mass ratios and to the inelastic regime above the dissociation threshold. The integrable cases at mass ratios 1 and 3 build on the Bethe ansatz solutions of Gaudin (1971) and Liu et al. (2019). The results enable quantitative predictions for ultracold heteronuclear molecule experiments, such as ⁴⁰K-⁶Li mixtures (mass ratio ≈ 6.7) and ¹⁷³Yb-⁶Li mixtures (mass ratio ≈ 29), and suggest that extreme mass ratios achievable through optical lattice tuning could produce complete dissociation at specific collision energies.
