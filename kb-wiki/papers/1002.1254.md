# Recent Developments in Few-Nucleon Scattering

> Using modern nucleon-nucleon interactions in the description of the $A=3,4$ nuclei, it is not possible to reproduce both the three- and four-nucleon binding energies simultaneously. This is one manifestation of the necessity of including a three-nucleon force in the nuclear Hamiltonian. Several models of the three-nucleon force exist and are applied in the description of light nuclei. However, as it is discussed here, a simultaneous description of the three- and four-body binding energies and the $n-d$ doublet scattering length seems to be problematic. Accordingly, a comparative study of some of these models is performed. In a different analysis, we study applications of the Kohn Variational Principle, formulated in terms of integral relations, to describe $N-d$ scattering processes.

## Key claim

Modern nucleon-nucleon potentials combined with three-nucleon force models cannot simultaneously reproduce the three-body binding energy, four-body binding energy, and the neutron-deuteron doublet scattering length, despite each model being able to describe some subset of these observables.

## Method

The author used the hyperspherical harmonic (HH) method to solve bound and scattering states for A=3 and A=4 nuclear systems, employing the AV18 and N3LO-Idaho nucleon-nucleon potentials combined with three-nucleon force models (TM', URIX, N2LOL, and URIXp). For scattering states below deuteron breakup threshold, the Kohn variational principle was applied to determine the reactance matrix elements and scattering lengths. The calculations were performed in both coordinate and momentum space to accommodate local and nonlocal interactions.

## Key numbers

- AV18 potential alone gives triton binding energy B(³H)=7.624 MeV, ⁴He binding energy B(⁴He)=24.22 MeV, and doublet scattering length ²a_nd=1.258 fm
- AV18+TM' gives B(³H)=8.440 MeV, B(⁴He)=28.31 MeV, and ²a_nd=0.623 fm
- AV18+URIX gives B(³H)=8.479 MeV, B(⁴He)=28.48 MeV, and ²a_nd=0.578 fm
- N3LO-Idaho+N2LOL gives B(³H)=8.474 MeV, B(⁴He)=28.37 MeV, and ²a_nd=0.675 fm
- N3LO-Idaho+URIXp gives B(³H)=8.481 MeV, B(⁴He)=28.53 MeV, and ²a_nd=0.623 fm
- V_low-k (cutoff Λ=2.2 fm⁻¹) gives B(³H)=8.477 MeV, B(⁴He)=29.15 MeV, and ²a_nd=0.572 fm
- Experimental values: B(³H)=8.482 MeV, B(⁴He)=28.30 MeV, ²a_nd=0.645±0.003±0.007 fm
- In the TM' model, the cutoff was fixed to Λ=4.756 m_π with constants a=-0.87 m_π⁻¹, b=-2.58 m_π⁻³, d=-0.753 m_π⁻³
- In the URIX model, bW_0=4×A^PW_2π with A^PW_2π=-0.0293 MeV, and the repulsive term A_R=0.0048 MeV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Phillips (1966) and Bedaque (2003) on the correlation between triton binding energy and doublet scattering length, showing that three-nucleon forces break this correlation and make ²a_nd an independent observable. The study compares local three-nucleon force models (URIX, TM', N2LOL) that had not been systematically analyzed together for simultaneous description of A=3 and A=4 binding energies and scattering length, following the chiral effective field theory analysis by Epelbaum (2002). The results enable future work to refine three-nucleon force parametrizations or develop new models that can simultaneously describe all three observables.
