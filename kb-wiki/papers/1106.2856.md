# Plasma damping effects on the radiative energy loss of relativistic particles

> The energy loss of a relativistic charge undergoing multiple scatterings while traversing an infinite, polarizable and absorptive plasma is investigated. Polarization and damping mechanisms in the medium are phenomenologically modelled by a complex index of refraction. Apart from the known Ter-Mikaelian effect related to the dielectric polarization of matter, we find an additional, substantial reduction of the energy loss due to damping of radiation. The observed effect is more prominent for larger damping and/or larger energy of the charge. A conceivable analog of this phenomenon in QCD could influence the study of jet quenching phenomena in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim

The radiative energy loss of a relativistic charge is substantially reduced in an absorptive plasma (modelled by a complex index of refraction with non-zero imaginary part), beyond the known Ter-Mikaelian reduction from dielectric polarization alone; this additional damping effect increases with larger medium damping and/or larger energy of the charge.

## Method

The authors model the plasma phenomenologically with a complex index of refraction \(n^2(\omega)=1-m^2/\omega^2+2i\Gamma/\omega\), where \(m\) is an in-medium mass and \(\Gamma\) a damping rate. They compute the mechanical work \(W\) performed on the charge by its electric field in linear response theory, following the Landau (1953) approach of modelling multiple scatterings via small deflection angles. The radiative energy loss spectrum per unit length is obtained from the negative of \(d^2W/(dz\,d\omega)\) after averaging over deflection angles, with the momentum-space integrals evaluated analytically by contour integration.

## Key numbers

- For a polarizable non-damping medium (\(\Gamma=0\)), the radiative energy loss spectrum is shown for \(m=0.3\), \(0.6\), and \(0.9\) GeV (red long-dashed curves from top to bottom) at \(E=20\) GeV, charge mass \(M=1\) GeV, and \(\hat{q}=2.5\) GeV\(^2\)/fm.
- For an absorptive medium with fixed \(m=0.6\) GeV, the spectrum is shown for \(\Gamma=5\), \(10\), and \(50\) MeV (blue short-dashed curves from top to bottom), with the maximal ratio \(\Gamma/m=0.083\).
- The ratio \(\Delta\equiv d^2W_{\Gamma\neq0}/d^2W_{\Gamma=0}\) is plotted as a function of \(E\) for constant \(\omega=3\), \(5\), and \(7\) GeV (solid curves from bottom to top) with fixed \(\Gamma=50\) MeV and \(m=0\).

## Context

This work extends the Ter-Mikaelian effect (Ter-Mikaelian, 1950s) for dielectric polarization in QED and its QCD analogs studied by Kampfer (2000) and Djordjevic (2003) using constant thermal gluon masses or hard thermal loop self-energies, none of which included damping of radiation. The results suggest that damping effects could influence jet quenching studies in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, though the authors note that QCD-specific effects such as gluon rescatterings are still missing and will be explored in future work.
