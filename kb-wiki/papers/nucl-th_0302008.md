# Quadrupole collectivity of neutron-rich Neon isotopes

> The angular momentum projected Generator Coordinate Method, with the quadrupole moment as collective coordinate and the Gogny force (D1S) as the effective interaction, is used to describe the properties of the ground state and low-lying excited states of the even-even Neon isotopes $^{20-34}$Ne, that is, from the stability valley up to the drip-line. It is found that the ground state of the N=20 nucleus $^{30}$Ne is deformed but to a lesser extent than the N=20 isotope of the Magnesium. In the calculations, the isotope $^{32}$Ne is at the drip-line in good agreement with other theoretical predictions. On the other hand, rather good agreement with experimental data for many observables is obtained.

## Key claim

The ground state of the N=20 nucleus ³⁰Ne is deformed, but to a lesser extent than the N=20 isotope of Magnesium, with the deformation effects in ³⁰Ne being the result of a subtle balance between zero-point rotational corrections and fluctuations in the collective quadrupole parameter.

## Method

The authors used the Angular Momentum Projected Generator Coordinate Method (AMP-GCM) with the axially symmetric quadrupole moment as the collective coordinate and the Gogny force (D1S parameterization) as the effective interaction. Intrinsic wave functions were obtained by solving the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equation constrained to the desired mass quadrupole moment, using a Harmonic Oscillator basis with eleven major shells. The Hill-Wheeler equation was then solved to obtain the collective amplitudes and energies for the even-even Neon isotopes ²⁰⁻³⁴Ne.

## Key numbers

- In ²⁰Ne, the prolate ground state corresponds to q₂₀ = 0.4 b (β₂ = 0.37) with an oblate local minimum at q₂₀ = -0.1 b (β₂ = -0.09) at an excitation energy of 1.71 MeV
- In ²²Ne, the ground state corresponds to q₂₀ = 0.5 b (β₂ = 0.40) with an oblate local minimum at q₂₀ = -0.2 b (β₂ = -0.17) at an excitation energy of 2.24 MeV
- In ²⁴Ne, the oblate ground state is at q₂₀ = -0.3 b (β₂ = -0.22) with a prolate isomeric state at q₂₀ = 0.2 b (β₂ = 0.15) at an excitation energy of 77 keV
- In ³⁰Ne, a prolate shoulder appears at q₂₀ = 0.8 b (β₂ = 0.37) at an excitation energy of 2.70 MeV relative to the spherical ground state
- In ³²Ne and ³⁴Ne, prolate deformed ground states are found with q₂₀ = 0.6 b (β₂ = 0.26) and q₂₀ = 0.8 b (β₂ = 0.31), respectively
- The energy gain due to configuration mixing for the ground state ranges from 0.6 MeV in ²⁰Ne up to 0.9 MeV for ²⁴Ne and ²⁶Ne, then decreases to 0.55 MeV in ³⁴Ne
- The 0⁺₁ ground state in ³⁰Ne has an average deformation of 0.16 b, representing one third of the value corresponding to the absolute minimum in the Iπ=0⁺ AMPPES
- The two neutron separation energy of ³⁴Ne computed with Nshell=11 differs by 17 keV from the one computed with Nshell=17
- For ³²Ne, excitation energies of the ground state rotational band obtained with Nshell=12 are around 7 keV higher than with Nshell=11

## Context

This work extends the AMP-GCM calculations previously applied to Magnesium isotopes (Rodriguez-Guzman 2000) and Silicon isotopes (Rodriguez-Guzman 2001) to the Neon isotopic chain from stability to the drip-line. The study examines the possible erosion of the N=20 spherical shell closure near the neutron drip-line and tests the suggested magicity of N=16 from Ozawa (2000). The results enable comparison with experimental data for 2⁺ excitation energies and B(E2) transition probabilities, and provide predictions for spectroscopic quadrupole moments that can be tested by future radioactive ion beam experiments.
