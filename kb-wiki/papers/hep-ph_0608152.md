# Spectral patterns in the nonstrange baryon spectrum

> We extract, from a quark model potential that reproduces the number and ordering of nonstrange baryonic resonances up to 2.3 GeV, the quantum numbers for the dominant configurations in the ground and first non-radial excited states. From the pattern of quantum numbers we identify, from data, spectral regularities that allow us to predict the expected high spin low-lying spectrum from 2.3 to 3.0 GeV. $N-Δ$ degeneracies and $N$ parity doublets showing up can be interpreted in terms of a simple dynamics.

## Key claim

The authors extract quantum numbers for dominant configurations in nonstrange baryon resonances from a quark model with a screened (saturating) potential, and use the resulting spectral patterns to predict high-spin, low-lying state masses between 2.3 and 3.0 GeV, noting that the predictions are plausible despite large experimental uncertainties in this energy region.

## Method

The authors employ a nonrelativistic quark potential model with a confinement term and one-gluon exchange, where the potential is multiplied by a smooth screening factor that saturates at about 2.4 GeV. They compare this smooth screened potential to a sharply saturated potential, both fitted to reproduce the number and ordering of known N and Delta resonances up to 2.3 GeV. The dominant spatial-spin configurations for ground states and first non-radial excitations are identified using hyperspherical harmonic quantum numbers, and the resulting patterns are extrapolated to higher energies to predict masses of states with J >= 11/2 (ground) and J >= 7/2 (first non-radial excitations).

## Key numbers

- The smooth screened potential has a screening parameter mu^{-1} approximately 1 fm for nonstrange baryons, giving a saturation energy of about 2.4 GeV (3m_q + M_0 + 3 sigma/(2 mu) approximately 2.4 GeV).
- The experimental Delta(11/2+) at 2420 MeV is the highest four-star resonance catalogued by the Particle Data Group.
- The energy step between successive dominant configurations (Delta K = Delta L = 2) is approximately constant at 400-500 MeV from data: N(9/2+)-N(5/2+) approximately 540 MeV, Delta(11/2+)-Delta(7/2+) approximately 470 MeV, N(13/2+)-N(9/2+) approximately 480 MeV, Delta(15/2+)-Delta(11/2+) approximately 530 MeV.
- The model predicts a N(17/2+) at 3200 MeV from extrapolation of the positive-parity ground-state pattern.
- For negative parity, data show mass differences of at most 180 MeV for K=L=1, 60 MeV for K=L=3 between N and Delta partners.
- The first non-radial excitation of N,Delta(J) and the ground state of N,Delta(J+1) are almost degenerate at the level of 3% from data.

## Context

This work extends the screened-potential quark model of Vijande, Gonzalez, Garcilazo, and Valcarce (2004), which correctly predicted the number and ordering of nonstrange baryon resonances up to 2.3 GeV, to higher energies. The spectral patterns derived are consistent with the phenomenological multiplet structure proposed by Klempt (2002), but here they are justified by an underlying dynamics rather than purely empirical rules. The predictions for states above 2.3 GeV are intended to guide experimental searches in a region where data are sparse and imprecise, and to inform theoretical progress on the dynamical interpretation of the baryon spectrum.
