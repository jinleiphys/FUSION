# Transition from hadronic to partonic interactions for a composite spin-1/2 model of a nucleon

> A simple model of a composite nucleon is developed in which a fermion and a boson, representing quark and diquark constituents of the nucleon, form a bound state owing to a contact interaction. Photon and pion couplings to the quark provide vertex functions for the photon and pion interactions with the composite nucleon. By a suitable choice of cutoff parameters of the model, realistic electromagnetic form factors are obtained. When a pseudoscalar pion-quark coupling is used, the pion-nucleon coupling is predominantly pseudovector. A virtual photopion amplitude is considered in which there are two types of contributions: hadronic contributions where the photon and pion interactions have an intervening propagator of the nucleon or its excited states, and contact-like contributions where the photon and pion interactions occur within a single vertex. At large Q, the contact-like contributions are dominant. The model nucleon exhibits scaling behavior in deep-inelastic scattering and the normalization of the parton distribution provides a rough normalization of the contact-like contributions. Calculations for the virtual photopion amplitude are performed using kinematics appropriate to its occurrence as a meson-exchange current in electron-deuteron scattering. The results show that the contact-like terms can dominate the meson-exchange current for Q > 1 GeV/c. There is a direct connection of the contact-like terms to the off-forward parton distributions of the model nucleon.

## Key claim

The paper develops a simple covariant model of a nucleon as a bound state of a quark and a diquark to study the transition from hadronic to partonic descriptions, showing that contact-like terms (where photon and pion interactions occur within a single vertex) can dominate meson-exchange currents for momentum transfers Q > 1 GeV/c, but the model lacks confinement and treats excited states as a continuum of scattering states, making it mainly useful where nucleon resonances do not play an important role.

## Method

A composite nucleon is constructed from a fermion (quark) and a boson (diquark) bound by a contact interaction, regulated by Pauli-Villars subtraction terms. Photon and pion couplings are introduced only to the quark constituent, and the resulting vertex functions are extracted from the residues of bound-state poles in the fermion-boson correlator. The virtual photopion amplitude is computed for kinematics relevant to meson-exchange currents in electron-deuteron scattering, separating hadronic contributions (with intervening nucleon or excited-state propagation) from contact-like contributions (where both interactions occur within a single vertex).

## Key numbers

- Bound state mass M = 0.93826 GeV, obtained with quark mass m = 0.38 GeV, diquark mass μ = 0.79 GeV, and regulator masses Λ₁ = 0.85 GeV, Λ₂ = 0.90 GeV
- Anomalous magnetic moment of the composite nucleon κ = 2.086, compared to the proton value κ_proton = 1.79
- Contact-like terms dominate meson-exchange currents for Q > 1 GeV/c
- The pion-nucleon vertex is approximately 75% pseudovector and 25% pseudoscalar at low Q, becoming closer to pseudoscalar at Q ≈ 1 GeV/c
- Electromagnetic form factors fit the dipole form F_dipole = (1 + Q²/0.71 GeV²)⁻²

## Context

This work extends the composite nucleon approach of Tiemeijer and Tjon (1990) and Naus and Koch (1989) by providing a covariant model that smoothly connects hadronic descriptions (meson-exchange dynamics as in the work of Machleidt (1989) and the Nijmegen group) with partonic descriptions (Bjorken (1969), Feynman (1972)). The model enables quantitative study of the transition region around Q ≈ 1 GeV/c where both hadronic and quark-based descriptions apply, and directly connects contact-like terms to off-forward parton distributions as developed by Ji (1997) and Radyushkin (1996).
