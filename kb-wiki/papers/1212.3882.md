# Three-body calculation of the rate of reaction $p+p+e \rightarrow d+ν_e$ in the Sun

> Using expansion of the three-body wave function of the $pep$ system in the initial state on hyperharmonic functions, the rate of the $p+p+e^{-} \rightarrow d+ν_e$ reaction in the Sun is calculated. The results of calculation of the flux at 1 AU are compared with the results of a measurement made by the Borexino collaboration and Bahcall \textit{et al.} theoretical predictions.

## Key claim

The three-body treatment of the initial \(pep\) state yields a neutrino flux approximately 40% larger than the two-body model used by Bahcall et al., and this result lies within the confidence interval of the Borexino measurement, whereas the Bahcall values fall outside that interval when the same survival probability is applied.

## Method

The authors solved the three-body Schrödinger equation for the \(pep\) system by expanding the wave function in hyperspherical harmonics, retaining only the \(K=l_x=l_y=0\) component. They used Gauss and Yukawa nucleon-nucleon potentials fitted to low-energy scattering data and the deuteron binding energy, and computed the transition matrix element using the nonrelativistic weak Hamiltonian with the Gamow-Teller term. The reaction rate was obtained by integrating the probability over Maxwell-Boltzmann distributions for the protons and electron, and the neutrino flux at 1 AU was derived using the BS05(OP) Standard Solar Model.

## Key numbers

- Neutrino energy from the \(pep\) reaction: \(E_\nu = 1.442\) MeV.
- Three-body phase shift at \(E = 1.4\) keV: \(\delta_3 = 3.7 \times 10^{-16}\) radian; at \(E = 6.0\) keV: \(\delta_3 = 2.3 \times 10^{-9}\) radian (Gauss potential).
- Astrophysical \(S_{pep}\) factor coefficients for Gauss potential: \(S_0 = 2.38 \times 10^{10}\) fm\(^6\)/s, \(S_1 = 3.03 \times 10^{10}\) fm\(^6\)/(MeV s), \(S_2 = 1.45 \times 10^{10}\) fm\(^6\)/(MeV\(^2\) s).
- Astrophysical \(S_{pep}\) factor coefficients for Yukawa potential: \(S_0 = 2.33 \times 10^{10}\) fm\(^6\)/s, \(S_1 = 3.01 \times 10^{10}\) fm\(^6\)/(MeV s), \(S_2 = 1.78 \times 10^{10}\) fm\(^6\)/(MeV\(^2\) s).
- Predicted \(pep\) neutrino flux at 1 AU (without survival probability): \(\Phi_{pep} = 2.04 \times 10^8\) cm\(^{-2}\)s\(^{-1}\) (Gauss) and \(1.99 \times 10^8\) cm\(^{-2}\)s\(^{-1}\) (Yukawa), using BS05(OP).
- Predicted \(pep\) neutrino flux including Borexino survival probability \(P = 0.62 \pm 0.17\): \(\Phi_{pep} = (1.27 \pm 0.35) \times 10^8\) cm\(^{-2}\)s\(^{-1}\) (Gauss) and \((1.24 \pm 0.34) \times 10^8\) cm\(^{-2}\)s\(^{-1}\) (Yukawa).
- Borexino measured flux: \(\Phi_{pep} = (1.6 \pm 0.3) \times 10^8\) cm\(^{-2}\)s\(^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier two-body treatment of the \(pep\) reaction by Bahcall and May (1969), which factorized the initial wave function as a product of the proton-proton relative motion and the electron Coulomb wave. By solving the full three-body problem with hyperspherical harmonics, the authors remove that factorization approximation and obtain a neutrino flux about 40% larger than the predictions of Bahcall et al. (2005) for the BS05(OP) and other Standard Solar Models. The results enable a more precise test of solar models against the Borexino measurement and suggest that reducing experimental errors could discriminate between different solar models.
