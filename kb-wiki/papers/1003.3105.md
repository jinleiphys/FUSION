# Resonance scattering and singularities of the scattering function

> Recent studies of transport phenomena with complex potentials are explained by generic square root singularities of spectrum and eigenfunctions of non-Hermitian Hamiltonians. Using a two channel problem we demonstrate that such singularities produce a significant effect upon the pole behaviour of the scattering matrix, and more significantly upon the associated residues. This mechanism explains why by proper choice of the system parameters the resonance cross section is increased drastically in one channel and suppressed in the other channel.

# Resonance scattering and singularities of the scattering function

## Key claim
Exceptional points (square root singularities) of non-Hermitian Hamiltonians produce a dramatic effect not only on pole positions of the scattering matrix but more significantly on the associated residues, explaining why resonance cross sections can be drastically increased in one channel and suppressed in another channel by proper choice of system parameters. The effect occurs only in the immediate vicinity of the critical parameter value and requires the system to be in the vicinity of an exceptional point.

## Method
The authors constructed a generic two-channel 2x2 matrix model Hamiltonian with complex parameters, where the unperturbed resonance energies have negative imaginary parts. They computed the scattering matrix T from this effective Hamiltonian and evaluated the cross sections (TT†)kk in a rotated basis (π/4 rotation) that ensures both resonances appear equally in both channels when the coupling δ is zero. By varying the strength parameter λ between 0.5 and 0.6 with a purely absorptive coupling δ = 0.0115i, they tracked the eigenvalue trajectories and the energy dependence of the cross sections at the critical point where the two exceptional points lie.

## Key numbers
- The crossing of resonances without coupling occurs at λ_cross = 0.5625 − 0.00077i.
- For δ = 0.0115i, the two exceptional points are at λ_+ = 0.579 − 0.00082i and λ_- = 0.547 − 0.00073i, with energies 1.325 − 0.007029i and 1.325 − 0.007027i respectively.
- The dramatic change in cross sections takes place within the narrow interval {λ_cr − 0.03, λ_cr + 0.03}, where λ_cr = 0.563.
- The residues of the Green's function blow up like 1/√((λ − λ_+)(λ − λ_-)) at the exceptional points.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical understanding of exceptional points in non-Hermitian Hamiltonians, previously discussed by Heiss (various years), Seyranian (various years), and others, into the domain of resonance scattering. The mechanism explains findings in quantum transport models by Kaplan (various years) where transmission increases drastically for specific barrier widths, and in studies of loosely bound nuclei by Zelenova (various years) where continuum coupling shows sensitive dependence on threshold energy. The results enable interpretation of resonance phenomena in atomic physics, optics, nuclear physics, and PT-symmetric models where exceptional points have been experimentally verified by Dembowski (various years) and others.
