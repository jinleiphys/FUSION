# Induced p-wave superfluidity in two dimensions: Brane world in cold atoms and nonrelativistic defect CFTs

> We propose to use a two-species Fermi gas with the interspecies s-wave Feshbach resonance to realize p-wave superfluidity in two dimensions. By confining one species of fermions in a two-dimensional plane immersed in the background three-dimensional Fermi sea of the other species, an attractive interaction is induced between two-dimensional fermions. We compute the pairing gap in the weak-coupling regime and show that it has the symmetry of p_x+ip_y. Because the magnitude of the pairing gap increases toward the unitarity limit, it is possible that the critical temperature for the p_x+ip_y-wave superfluidity becomes within experimental reach. The resulting system has a potential application to topological quantum computation using vortices with non-Abelian statistics. We also discuss aspects of our system in the unitarity limit as a "nonrelativistic defect conformal field theory (CFT)". The reduced Schrödinger algebra, operator-state correspondence, scaling dimensions of composite operators, and operator product expansions are investigated.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a $p_x+ip_y$-wave superfluid can be realized in two dimensions without using $p$-wave Feshbach resonances, by instead using an interspecies $s$-wave Feshbach resonance in a 2D-3D mixed-dimensional Fermi gas; the claim is hedged by the condition that the controlled perturbative analysis is only available in the weak-coupling regime $a_\text{eff}\to-0$, and the critical temperature reaching experimental feasibility is an expectation based on extrapolation toward the unitarity limit.

## Method

The author considers a two-species Fermi gas where species $A$ is confined to a two-dimensional plane while species $B$ occupies the three-dimensional bulk, with an interspecies $s$-wave contact interaction occurring only on the plane. Using field-theoretical perturbation theory in the weak-coupling limit (small effective scattering length $a_\text{eff}$), the induced interaction between $A$ atoms mediated by the 3D Fermi sea of $B$ atoms is computed from a one-loop Feynman diagram. The BCS gap equation is then solved for the $p$-wave pairing channel at zero temperature, with the angular dependence of the pairing gap determined by minimizing the ground state energy.

## Key numbers

* The maximum $p$-wave pairing gap at weak coupling, optimized over density ratio, is $\Delta_\p^\text{max} / \varepsilon_\text{FA} \sim \exp\!\left(-22.1 \, m_{AB} / (m_A a_\text{eff}^2 k_\text{FB}^2)\right)$, where $m_{AB}$ is the reduced mass, $m_A$ is the mass of the 2D fermions, $a_\text{eff}$ is the effective scattering length, and $k_\text{FB}$ is the 3D Fermi momentum of $B$ atoms.
* For the mass ratio $m_A/m_B = 6.67$ (corresponding to $A={}^{40}\mathrm{K}$, $B={}^6\mathrm{Li}$), the optimized pairing gap becomes $\Delta_\p^\text{Max} / \varepsilon_\text{FA} \sim \exp\!\left(-3.29 / (a_\text{eff}^2 k_\text{FB}^2)\right)$.
* Extrapolating the perturbative result to $|a_\text{eff} k_\text{FB}| \approx 1$ gives $\exp\!\left(-3.29 / (a_\text{eff}^2 k_\text{FB}^2)\right) \approx 0.04$.
* The function $v^{(1)}(k_\text{FA}/k_\text{FB})$ appearing in the $p$-wave projection of the induced interaction has a minimum value $v^{(1)} = -0.0452$ at $k_\text{FA}/k_\text{FB} = 1.75$.
* The two-body binding energy for $a_\text{eff} > 0$ is $\varepsilon_\text{b} = 1/(2 m_{AB} a_\text{eff}^2)$.
* The broadest confinement-induced resonance occurs at $l/a = -0.882$ for $m_A/m_B = 0.15$ and at $l/a = -0.0237$ for $m_A/m_B = 6.67$, where $l$ is the oscillator length of the confining potential.

## Context

This work extends the concept of induced interactions in mixed dimensions, previously studied by Nishida (2008) for the 2D-3D mixture, to realize $p$-wave superfluidity without relying on unstable $p$-wave Feshbach resonances, which were shown by Regal (2003), Zhang (2004), and others to produce molecules with lifetimes up to only 20 ms due to inelastic collisions. The proposed system enables a potential platform for topological quantum computation using vortices with non-Abelian statistics, as proposed by Read (2000) and Tewari (2007), and also provides a realization of a nonrelativistic defect conformal field theory in the unitarity limit, with connections to the brane-world model of the universe.
