# Surface behaviour of the pairing gap in a slab of nuclear matter

> The surface behaviour of the pairing gap previously studied for semi-infinite nuclear matter is analyzed in the slab geometry. The gap-shape function is calculated in two cases: (a) pairing with the Gogny force in a hard-wall potential and (b) pairing with the separable Paris interaction in a Saxon-Woods mean-field potential. It is shown that the surface features are preserved in the case of slab geometry, being almost independent of the width of the slab. It is also demonstrated that the surface enhancement is strengthened as the absolute value of chemical potential $|μ|$ decreases which simulates the approach to the nucleon drip line.

## Key claim

The surface enhancement of the pairing gap previously observed in semi-infinite nuclear matter is preserved in slab geometry and is almost independent of the slab width; the enhancement is strengthened as the absolute value of the chemical potential decreases, simulating approach to the nucleon drip line.

## Method

The authors calculated the gap-shape function for a slab of nuclear matter using two distinct approaches: (a) pairing with the Gogny force D1 in a hard-wall potential, solved analytically within BCS approximation; (b) pairing with the separable Paris interaction in a Saxon-Woods mean-field potential, solved numerically using the KKC method with the local potential approximation (LPA) for the effective interaction. In both cases, the Wigner transform of the gap operator was used to extract the spatial dependence of the pairing gap at the local Fermi surface.

## Key numbers

- For the Gogny force model, the surface enhancement of the gap-shape function is moderate, not exceeding 20%–30%, for Fermi energies of 42 MeV and 46 MeV (corresponding to Fermi momenta 1.4232 fm⁻¹ and 1.4894 fm⁻¹, respectively).
- For the Paris force model with chemical potential μ = −8 MeV, the surface enhancement is approximately 80%–100%; for μ = −4 MeV, the enhancement increases by about 30% relative to the μ = −8 MeV case.
- The dependence of the surface enhancement on slab half-width L (4, 6, 8, and 10 fm) is approximately 10% variation for the Gogny model and only about 5% for the Paris model.
- The Saxon-Woods potential parameters were U₀ = −50 MeV and diffuseness d = 0.65 fm.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the pairing gap in semi-infinite nuclear matter by Baldo et al. (2002) and Farine et al. (2001) to the more realistic slab geometry, which is closer to finite atomic nuclei. The results confirm that the surface enhancement found in semi-infinite matter persists with similar magnitude in slabs, with a much stronger effect (∼100%) for the Paris interaction compared to the Gogny force (∼30%). The authors note that the mechanism here differs from that of Bortignon et al., which involves virtual exchange of collective surface vibrations, and that both effects should be considered together for a consistent description of pairing in nuclei.
