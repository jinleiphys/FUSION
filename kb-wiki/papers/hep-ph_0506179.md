# Thermalization vs. Isotropization & Azimuthal Fluctuations

> Hydrodynamic description requires a local thermodynamic equilibrium of the system under study but an approximate hydrodynamic behaviour is already manifested when a momentum distribution of liquid components is not of equilibrium form but merely isotropic. While the process of equilibration is relatively slow, the parton system becomes isotropic rather fast due to the plasma instabilities. Azimuthal fluctuations observed in relativistic heavy-ion collisions are argued to distinguish between a fully equilibrated and only isotropic parton system produced in the collision early stage.

## Key claim

Hydrodynamic collective behavior does not strictly require local thermodynamic equilibrium but only an isotropic momentum distribution; the author argues that azimuthal fluctuations can distinguish between a fully equilibrated parton system and one that is merely isotropic due to fast plasma instabilities, with the caveat that the proposed distinction relies on the assumption that non-equilibrium fluctuations are significantly larger than equilibrium ones.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical mechanism of Weibel (filamentation) instabilities in an anisotropic quark-gluon plasma, deriving the dispersion relation from the gluon self-energy and permittivity tensor. It then proposes two experimental approaches to measure azimuthal fluctuations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions: event-by-event fluctuations of the elliptic flow coefficient \(v_2\), and the integral \(\Phi\) measure of azimuthal correlations, both intended to compare equilibrium versus non-equilibrium scenarios.

## Key numbers

- Equilibration time from parton-parton collisions in a weakly coupled plasma: at least 2.6 fm/\(c\) (Baier 2002)
- Inverse time scale of collisional equilibration: of order \(g^4 \ln(1/g)\,T\) where \(g\) is the QCD coupling constant
- Characteristic inverse time of instability development: roughly of order \(gT\) for a sufficiently anisotropic momentum distribution
- Example simulation parameters: \(\alpha_s = 0.3\), \(\sigma_\perp = 0.3\) GeV, effective parton density 6 fm\(^{-3}\)
- Simulation initial condition: \(p_{\rm hard} = 10\) GeV, lattice size \(L = 40\) fm
- For \(b = 5\) fm where \(\langle v_2 \rangle \approx 0.03\): \(\delta v_2 \approx 8 \times 10^{-4} \, \delta N_p\) from impact parameter variation
- Ratio of thermodynamic fluctuations to statistical noise: 0.04 for \(\langle v_2 \rangle = 0.07\) and power index \(P = 0.4\)

## Context

This work extends the argument by Arnold et al. (2004) that hydrodynamic behavior requires only isotropization, not full thermalization, and builds on earlier proposals by Mrowczynski (1993) that plasma instabilities drive fast isotropization in the quark-gluon plasma. The paper contrasts the fast instability-driven isotropization (time scale \(\sim 1/(gT)\)) with the slower collisional equilibration (time scale \(\sim 1/(g^4 T)\)) and proposes experimental tests using azimuthal fluctuations to distinguish these scenarios, enabling future measurements at RHIC and LHC to probe the early-stage dynamics of heavy-ion collisions.
