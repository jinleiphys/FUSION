# Landau-Zener-Stückelberg-Majorana dynamics of magnetized quarkonia

> The mass spectrum of hadrons in magnetic fields features avoided level-crossing structures arising from the mixing of spin eigenstates. In this work, we investigate the impact of level-crossing dynamics of charmonia subjected to time-dependent magnetic fields, where we particularly focus on the occupation probabilities of two or more states as they undergo transitions at avoided crossings. Using a static spectrum of charmonia in magnetic fields, we construct a multi-channel Landau-Zener Hamiltonian. Within this framework, we analyze the time evolution under several representative magnetic-field profiles, including linear ramps and Gaussian decays corresponding to single-passage dynamics, as well as Gaussian pulses realizing double-passage dynamics, and compute the occupation probabilities over a wide range of sweep rates and initial conditions. Our results show that nonadiabatic dynamics, including Landau-Zener transitions and Stückelberg interference, strongly influences the occupation probabilities of charmonia. These findings provide new insights into the real-time dynamics of magnetized hadrons and offer useful guidance for future lattice simulation studies.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that nonadiabatic dynamics, including Landau-Zener transitions and Stückelberg interference, strongly influences the occupation probabilities of charmonia in time-dependent magnetic fields, but the analysis is based on an effective multi-channel Landau-Zener Hamiltonian fitted to a static quark-model spectrum rather than on direct lattice QCD simulations.

## Method

The authors constructed a multi-channel Landau-Zener Hamiltonian by fitting its parameters (energy slopes, level offsets, and nearest-neighbor couplings) to the static charmonium spectrum obtained from a nonrelativistic constituent quark model solved via the cylindrical Gaussian expansion method. They then solved the time-dependent Schrödinger equation under three magnetic-field profiles (linear ramps, Gaussian decays, and Gaussian pulses) to compute occupation probabilities in both the diabatic and adiabatic bases for two- through five-channel models.

## Key numbers

* The avoided-crossing gap between the J/ψ and ηc′-started states is Δ1,2 = 0.028 GeV in the two-channel model, with slope parameters α1 = 0.562 GeV−1 and α2 = −0.049 GeV−1
* The coupling between ηc and J/ψ in the three-channel model is Δ2,3 = 0.031 GeV, with level offsets δ1 = 3.005 GeV, δ2 = 3.010 GeV, and δ3 = 3.647 GeV
* The quark model parameters used are mc = 1.784 GeV, √σ = 0.402 GeV, A = 0.713 GeV, β = 0.4778 GeV, Λ = 1.020 GeV2, and C = −0.5693 GeV
* The linear ramp sweep rates studied are νB = 1, 0.1, and 0.01 GeV2/fm, with a maximum field of eBmax = 2 GeV2

## Context

This work extends previous studies of static charmonia in magnetic fields (Suzuki 2016, Yoshida 2016) and earlier time-dependent studies motivated by heavy-ion collisions (Guo 2015, Dutta 2017, Bagchi 2018, Iwasaki 2021) by introducing a multi-channel Landau-Zener framework that isolates the essential parameters governing nonadiabatic transitions. The approach provides a tractable method for studying real-time dynamics of magnetized hadrons and offers guidance for future lattice QCD simulations using sign-problem-free methods such as tensor networks (Banuls 2019).
