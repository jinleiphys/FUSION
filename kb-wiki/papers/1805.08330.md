# $K^*$ mesons with hidden charm arising from $KX(3872)$ and $KZ_c(3900)$ dynamics

> Inspired by the recent discovery of the pentaquark states $P_c(4450)$ and $P_c(4380)$, which can be viewed as excited nucleon states with hidden charm, we study the three-body interaction of a kaon and a pair of $D\bar{D}^*$ in isospin 0 and 1. We show that the two body interactions stringently constrained by the existence of the $D_{s0}^*(2317)$, $D^*_{s1}(2460)$, $X(3872)$, and $Z_c(3900)$, which are widely believed to contain large $DK$, $D^* K$, and $D\bar{D}^*$ components, inevitably lead to the existence of a heavy $K^*$ meson with hidden charm. Concrete coupled channel three-body calculations yield its mass and width as $(4307\pm 2)- i (9\pm 2)$ MeV with $I(J^P)=1/2(1^-)$. This state, if found experimentally, definitely cannot be accommodated in a $q\bar{q}$ picture, and therefore presents a clear case of an exotic hadron.

## Key claim
The three-body interaction of a kaon with a $D\bar{D}^*$ pair, constrained by the known two-body molecular states $D_{s0}^*(2317)$, $D_{s1}^*(2460)$, $X(3872)$, and $Z_c(3900)$, inevitably produces a heavy $K^*$ meson with hidden charm at $(4307\pm 2) - i(9\pm 2)$ MeV with quantum numbers $I(J^P)=1/2(1^-)$. This state, if observed, cannot be accommodated in a simple $q\bar{q}$ picture and therefore constitutes an exotic hadron; the prediction depends on the validity of the fixed-center approximation to the Faddeev equations and on the molecular interpretation of the input two-body states.

## Method
The authors solved the three-body $KD\bar{D}^*$ system using the fixed-center approximation (FCA) to the Faddeev equations, treating the $D\bar{D}^*$ pair as a cluster (either $X(3872)$ in isospin 0 or $Z_c(3900)$ in isospin 1) and the kaon as the third particle scattering off that cluster. The two-body $t$-matrices for $KD$, $K\bar{D}^*$, and $D\bar{D}^*$ subsystems were obtained from Bethe-Salpeter equations with kernels derived from heavy-quark spin symmetry, constrained to reproduce the known molecular states. The calculation was performed in a coupled-channel formalism allowing transitions between $KX$ and $KZ$ configurations, with the loop function $G_0$ regularized by a cutoff $\Lambda$ varied between 700 and 750 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Mass and width of the predicted $K^*$ state: $(4307\pm 2) - i(9\pm 2)$ MeV, with $I(J^P)=1/2(1^-)$.
- In the coupled-channel calculation including $KX$ and $KZ$ transitions and the $Z_c$ width of 28 MeV, the $KX$ configuration yields $M - i\Gamma/2 = (4308 \pm 1) - i(8 \pm 1)$ MeV, and the $KZ$ configuration yields $M - i\Gamma/2 = (4306 \pm 1) - i(9 \pm 1)$ MeV.
- The state lies approximately 70 MeV below the $KZ$ threshold (treating $Z$ as stable).
- The cutoff $\Lambda$ was varied from 700 to 750 MeV to estimate uncertainties; results were stable across this range.
- In the uncoupled $KZ$ calculation including the $Z$ width of 28 MeV, a state appears at approximately 4292 MeV with a width of 20 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the molecular interpretation of the $X(3872)$ and $Z_c(3900)$ states (Gamermann et al., 2006; Aceti et al., 2012, 2014) into the three-body sector by adding a kaon, analogous to studies of the $\bar{K}NN$ system (Bayar et al., 2012; Agakishiev et al., 2014). The FCA approach has been previously applied to systems such as $\phi K\bar{K}$, $\rho D\bar{D}$, and $DKK$ (Dias et al., 2017; Sekihara et al., 2016). A recent study by Ma et al. (2017) solving the Schrodinger equation for the $D\bar{D}^*K$ system with different dynamics found a state at 4317 MeV. The prediction awaits experimental confirmation at facilities such as BEPC, potentially in final states like $\bar{K}^0 D_s^+ D^-$.
