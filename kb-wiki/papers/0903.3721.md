# Deuteron stripping and pickup involving halo nuclei 11Be and 15C

> Three-body calculations of (d,p) and (p,d) reactions involving one-neutron halo nuclei 11Be and 15C are performed using the framework of Faddeev-type scattering equations. Important effects of the optical potential nonlocality are found improving the description of the experimental data. The obtained values for the neutron spectroscopic factor are consistent with estimations from other approaches.

## Key claim

Three-body Faddeev/AGS calculations of (d,p) and (p,d) reactions on the one-neutron halo nuclei 11Be and 15C show that including the nonlocality of the proton-core optical potential significantly improves the description of experimental transfer data, but the extracted neutron spectroscopic factors remain below unity for 11Be, likely due to neglecting core excitation in the bound state.

## Method

The author solved momentum-space Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations, which are exact Faddeev-type three-body scattering equations, for the (p,n,core) system. The long-range Coulomb force between charged particles was included via the screening and renormalization method. The nucleon-nucleon interaction used the CD Bonn potential, the neutron-core potential was a real Woods-Saxon form fitted to the halo-nucleus bound-state spectrum, and the proton-core interaction employed the nonlocal optical potential (NLOP) of Giannini et al., adjusted to reproduce elastic scattering data.

## Key numbers

- For the 11Be ground state (1/2+), the neutron spectroscopic factor extracted from the (d,p) reaction at Ed = 12 MeV is approximately 0.45, consistent with the DWBA-type result of Lee (2007).
- For the 11Be ground state (1/2+), the neutron spectroscopic factor extracted from the (d,p) reaction at Ed = 25 MeV is approximately 0.75, consistent with most other estimations collected by Winfield (2001).
- For the 11Be excited state (1/2-), the neutron spectroscopic factor is approximately 0.6 at Ed = 12 MeV and approximately 0.85 at Ed = 25 MeV.
- For the 15C ground state (1/2+), the extracted neutron spectroscopic factor is close to 1, in agreement with the DWBA-type result of Lee (2007).
- The nonlocality parameters of the proton-core potential are beta_c = 1.015 fm, beta_t = 1.633 fm, and beta_s = 0.789 fm.

## Context

This work extends the application of exact Faddeev/AGS three-body calculations, previously applied to reactions on 12C and 16O by Deltuva (2009), to the halo nuclei 11Be and 15C. It provides the first inclusion of a nonlocal optical potential in transfer reactions on halo nuclei, showing that the nonlocality effect depends mainly on reaction energy and internal orbital angular momentum, not on binding energy or mass. The results enable more reliable extraction of spectroscopic factors from transfer reactions on weakly-bound systems and highlight the need to include core excitation in the structure model for 11Be, as noted by Winfield (2001).
