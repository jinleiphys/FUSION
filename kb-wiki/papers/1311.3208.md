# Bottomonium spectrum at finite temperature

> We investigate the modification of S and P wave states in the bottomonium spectrum above and below the deconfinement crossover temperature through their spectral functions obtained from the maximum entropy method. Anisotropic ensembles with $N_f=2+1$ Wilson clover fermions with tadpole-improvement are used while the bottom quark is treated with an improved non-relativistic action. We observe significant modifications of the P wave $χ_{b1}$ ground state directly above the crossover temperature, $T_c$, while the S wave $Υ$ ground state remains relatively unchanged up to temperatures of almost $2T_c$. This evidence supports earlier conclusions from our FASTSUM collaboration of the immediate dissociation of the P wave states above $T_c$ and the survival of the S wave ground states up to $2T_c$.

## Key claim

The P wave χb1 ground state dissociates immediately above the deconfinement crossover temperature Tc, while the S wave Υ ground state survives up to at least 1.90Tc, with the caveat that a larger threshold in the current study compared with earlier work (Aarts 2010) may make the P wave modification appear less pronounced than previously observed.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations using anisotropic ensembles with Nf=2+1 flavors of tadpole-improved Wilson clover fermions, treating the bottom quark with an improved non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) action including relative O(v²) and O(v⁴) corrections. They computed hadronic correlation functions at eight temperatures ranging from 0.76Tc to 1.90Tc and reconstructed spectral functions using the maximum entropy method (MEM) with Bryan's algorithm.

## Key numbers

- Spatial lattice spacing as = 0.1227(8) fm, anisotropy ξ = as/aτ = 3.5, pion mass Mπ ≈ 400 MeV
- Temperatures studied: 0.76Tc (141 MeV), 0.84Tc (156 MeV), 0.95Tc (176 MeV), 1.09Tc (201 MeV), 1.27Tc (235 MeV), 1.52Tc (281 MeV), and 1.90Tc (352 MeV)
- At the physical heavy quark mass (asmb = 2.92) and T = 1.90Tc, the Υ channel correlation function shows only about 3% enhancement at large separations relative to zero temperature
- At the same conditions, the χb1 channel shows almost 20% enhancement relative to zero temperature
- Up to six bound state peaks are visible in the zero-temperature S wave spectral functions (ηb and Υ channels)

## Context

This work extends earlier FASTSUM collaboration studies (Aarts 2010, Aarts 2011, Aarts 2013) that suggested P wave dissociation above Tc and S wave survival to 2Tc, using improved ensembles with dynamical strange quarks, finer spatial lattice spacing, and a pion mass closer to the physical value compared with those earlier studies. The results are obtained in the same temperature range as heavy-ion collision experiments and are intended to help interpret experimental spectra from the CMS collaboration (Chatrchyan 2012). The calculations use NRQCD, which avoids the need for the separation mb ≫ T required by other approaches, and the asymmetric correlation functions from NRQCD allow access to larger temporal separations than for relativistic quarks, improving spectral function reconstruction.
