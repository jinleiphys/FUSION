# Form factors from lattice QCD

> Precision computation of hadronic physics with lattice QCD is becoming feasible. The last decade has seen percent-level calculations of many simple properties of mesons, and the last few years have seen calculations of baryon masses, including the nucleon mass, accurate to a few percent. As computational power increases and algorithms advance, the precise calculation of a variety of more demanding hadronic properties will become realistic. With this in mind, I discuss the current lattice QCD calculations of generalized parton distributions with an emphasis on the prospects for well-controlled calculations for these observables as well. I will do this by way of several examples: the pion and nucleon form factors and moments of the nucleon parton and generalized-parton distributions.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD calculations of nucleon form factors are not yet at the level of fully controlled uncertainties, but the pion form factor demonstrates that once all relevant uncertainties are accounted for (including finite-size effects and excited-state pollution), lattice results agree with experiment; the same path is expected for nucleon observables.

## Method

The paper reviews lattice QCD calculations of hadronic form factors, focusing on the pion electromagnetic form factor and the proton isovector charge radius and momentum fraction. It compares lattice results from multiple collaborations (ETMC, QCDSF, LHPC, RBC/UKQCD, JLQCD) to experimental measurements and chiral perturbation theory, examining the impact of finite-volume effects (requiring \(m_\pi L > 4\)) and excited-state contamination on the apparent disagreements.

## Key numbers

- Pion charge radius \(\langle r^2\rangle_\pi\): only two lattice calculations (QCDSF/UKQCD from Brommel:2006ww and ETMC from Frezzotti:2008dr) have fully controlled uncertainties and agree with the experimental value.
- Nucleon mass \(m_N\): now calculable at the few-percent level, a milestone achieved only in the last few years.
- Pion form factor \(F(Q^2)\): lattice calculations explore the low \(Q^2\) regime \(Q^2 < 1\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) with reasonably controlled uncertainties.
- Proton isovector charge radius \(\langle r^2\rangle^{u-d}_p\): applying the finite-volume criterion \(m_\pi L > 4\) alleviates the disagreement with the measured value and weakens tension with chiral perturbation theory.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on precision lattice QCD calculations of hadron properties, following percent-level meson calculations (Bethke:2011tr, Laiho:2009eu) and few-percent baryon mass calculations (BMW from Durr:2008zz, PACS-CS from Aoki:2008sm, ETMC from Alexandrou:2009qu). It contrasts the misleading summary plot of the pion charge radius (similar to Frezzotti:2008dr) with a time-history plot that reveals convergence once controlled uncertainties are required. The paper enables future lattice QCD predictions for nucleon generalized parton distributions (GPDs), including the spin decomposition and transverse structure of the nucleon, by establishing the methodology for controlling uncertainties in nucleon matrix elements.
