# Interference Between Light and Heavy Neutrinos for $0νββ$ Decay in the Left-Right Symmetric Model

> Neutrinoless double-beta decay is proposed as an important low energy phenomenon that could test beyond the Standard Model physics. There are several potentially competing beyond the Standard Model mechanisms that can induce the process. It thus becomes important to disentangle the different processes. In the present study we consider the interference effect between the light left-handed and heavy right-handed Majorana neutrino exchange mechanisms. The decay rate, and consequently, the phase-space factors for the interference term are derived, based on the left-right symmetric model. The numerical values for the interference phase-space factors for several nuclides are calculated, taking into consideration the relativistic Coulomb distortion of the electron wave function and finite-size of the nucleus. The variation of the interference effect with the Q-value of the process is studied.

## Key claim

The interference between light left-handed and heavy right-handed Majorana neutrino exchange mechanisms in neutrinoless double-beta decay can contribute up to approximately 44% to the decay rate for certain isotopes with small Q-values, contrary to the long-held assumption that such interference effects are negligible (below 10%).

## Method

The authors derived the decay rate and phase-space factors for the interference term between light and heavy neutrino exchange within the left-right symmetric model, starting from the effective current-current Hamiltonian incorporating both left-handed and right-handed leptonic and hadronic currents. They numerically calculated the interference phase-space factors for 12 isotopes for ground-state-to-ground-state transitions and 11 isotopes for ground-state-to-first-excited-0+ transitions, using relativistic Fermi functions to account for Coulomb distortion of electron wave functions and including finite nuclear size effects via a screening factor of 94.5% applied to the nuclear charge.

## Key numbers

- For ground-state-to-ground-state transitions, the ratio ε = G₁⁰ν'/G₁⁰ν (interference PSF to individual PSF) ranges from 4.44% for ⁴⁸Ca (Q_ββ = 4.27226 MeV) to 31.33% for ¹²⁸Te (Q_ββ = 0.86795 MeV)
- For ground-state-to-first-excited-0+ transitions, ε reaches 44.15% for ¹¹⁰Pd (Q_ββ = 0.5472 MeV) and 35.89% for ¹³⁰Te (Q_ββ = 0.7335 MeV)
- The common dimensionful constant g⁰ν = 2.8 × 10⁻²² yr⁻¹
- The individual PSF G₁⁰ν for ¹⁵⁰Nd ground-state-to-ground-state is 6.60043 × 10⁻¹⁴ yr⁻¹, the largest among the isotopes studied
- The interference PSF G₁⁰ν' for ¹⁵⁰Nd ground-state-to-first-excited-0+ is 25.3183 × 10⁻¹⁶ yr⁻¹

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Halprin (1983), who first considered interference between light and heavy neutrino exchange but used non-relativistic Fermi factors and assumed ultra-relativistic electrons, and whose analytical expressions contained typos. The paper also updates the treatment of phase-space factors following recent re-evaluations by Suhonen and Civitarese (1998), Stoica and Mirea (2013), and Neacsu and Horoi (2015), which showed differences of up to 100% for heavier nuclei compared to older results. The results enable more accurate disentangling of competing beyond-Standard-Model mechanisms in neutrinoless double-beta decay experiments, particularly for isotopes with small Q-values where interference effects are largest.
