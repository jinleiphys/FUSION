# Opportunities with ultra-soft photons: Bremsstrahlung from stopping

> We compute the spectra of bremsstrahlung photons for different stopping scenarios which give rise to different initial charge-rapidity distributions. In the light of novel experimental opportunities that may arise with a new heavy-ion detector ALICE-3 at the CERN LHC, we discuss how to discriminate between these stopping scenarios and how to disentangle bremsstrahlung photons from other photon sources.

# Opportunities with ultra-soft photons: Bremsstrahlung from stopping

## Key claim
The bremsstrahlung photon spectrum from heavy-ion collisions can discriminate between different stopping scenarios (charge-rapidity distributions), but the signal-to-background ratio for the most realistic transparent scenarios may be as low as 0.5 photons per event in the kinematic range 10 MeV < pT < 20 MeV at forward rapidity, making detection experimentally challenging and possibly only able to set upper bounds.

## Method
The classical bremsstrahlung formula is applied to heavy-ion collisions by modeling the electric current as incoming Lorentz-contracted nuclear charge distributions plus an outgoing current parametrized by a charge-rapidity distribution ρ(y). Five different charge-rapidity distribution models (Landau, Gaussian, Bjorken, Camel 2, Camel 1) are compared, and the resulting photon spectra are computed in the kinematic regime ω < 200 MeV and pseudorapidity η > 3 where the transverse form factor is approximately unity.

## Key numbers
- The kinematic range studied is ω < 200 MeV and η > 3, ensuring q = ωR sinθ < 1 for the transverse form factor.
- The signal-to-background ratio for bremsstrahlung photons is approximately 0.1 ≲ S/B ≲ 1 for pT ≲ 50 MeV and η ≳ 3.
- For the most transparent stopping scenarios, the signal amounts to as little as 0.5 photons per event in the kinematic range 10 MeV < pT < 20 MeV, 3 ≤ η ≤ 4.
- The pT-differential charged pion spectrum at mid-rapidity is approximately dNπ±/dpT dy ≃ 2000/GeV for pT < 500 MeV at y=0, giving an estimated dNπ0/dpT dy ≃ 500/GeV for pT < 500 MeV and 4 < η < 5, corresponding to ten background photons per event per 10 MeV bin.
- The ALICE-3 detector may give experimental access to ultra-soft photons with transverse momentum as low as 10 MeV at forward rapidity up to y = 4.

## Context
This work extends the classical bremsstrahlung calculations from stopping originally developed by Kapusta (1977), Bjorken (1984), and Dumitru (1993), with refined calculations by Jeon (1998), Kapusta (1999), and Wong (2000) that demonstrated measurability at RHIC. The present work reassesses these ideas for the LHC kinematic range and the proposed ALICE-3 detector, and is one of several recent works by Lebiedowicz (2021) and Floerchinger (2021) pointing to interest in more precise soft photon measurements at the LHC. The calculations identify the physics interest in equipping the kinematic range of ultra-soft photons (pT ∼ 10 MeV) at forward rapidity with sensitive detectors, while noting that phenomenological models by Mehtar-Tani (2008, 2009) indicate heavy-ion collisions are rather transparent to electric charge, making discrimination between stopping scenarios experimentally challenging.
