# Charmonium dynamics in nucleus-nucleus collisions at SPS and FAIR energies

> Charmonium production and suppression in In+In and Pb+Pb reactions at SPS energies is investigated with the HSD transport approach within the 'hadronic comover model' as well as the 'QGP threshold scenario'. The results of the transport calculations for J/Psi suppression and the Psi prime to J/Psi ratio are compared with the recent data of the NA50 and NA60 Collaborations. We find that the comover absorption model - with a single parameter |M_0|^2 for the matrix element squared for charmonium-meson dissociation - performs best with respect to all data sets. The 'threshold scenario' - within different assumptions for the melting energy densities - yields a reasonable suppression for J/Psi but fails in reproducing the Psi prime to J/Psi ratio for Pb+Pb at 158 A GeV. Predictions for Au+Au reactions are presented for a bombarding energy of 25 A GeV in the different scenarios which will allow for a clear distinction between the models from the experimental side at the future FAIR facility.

## Key claim

The hadronic comover absorption model, with a single parameter \(|M_0|^2\) for the matrix element squared for charmonium-meson dissociation, performs best with respect to all data sets for charmonium suppression in In+In and Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies, while the QGP threshold scenario fails to reproduce the \(\Psi^\prime\) to \(J/\Psi\) ratio for Pb+Pb at 158 A GeV.

## Method

The authors used the Hadron-String-Dynamics (HSD) transport approach to simulate charmonium production and suppression in In+In and Pb+Pb collisions at 158 A GeV. They implemented two models: the "hadronic comover model," where charmonia are dissociated by interactions with secondary hadrons (comovers) using a single parameter \(|M_0|^2 = 0.18\) fm\(^2\)/GeV\(^2\) for the matrix element squared, and the "QGP threshold scenario," where charmonia are fully dissociated when the local energy density exceeds a threshold value (e.g., \(\varepsilon_{J/\Psi} = 16\) GeV/fm\(^3\), \(\varepsilon_{\chi_c} = 2\) GeV/fm\(^3\), \(\varepsilon_{\Psi^\prime} = 2\) GeV/fm\(^3\)). The calculations included nuclear absorption with cross sections of 4.18 mb for \(J/\Psi\) and \(\chi_c\) with baryons and 7.6 mb for \(\Psi^\prime\) with baryons, and the results were compared to NA50 and NA60 data.

## Key numbers

- The best fit for the comover absorption model uses \(|M_0|^2 = 0.18\) fm\(^2\)/GeV\(^2\) for the matrix element squared for charmonium-meson dissociation.
- The nuclear absorption cross sections used are: \(\sigma_{J/\Psi B} = 4.18\) mb, \(\sigma_{\chi_c B} = 4.18\) mb, and \(\sigma_{\Psi^\prime B} = 7.6\) mb.
- The threshold energy densities for the QGP threshold scenario are \(\varepsilon_{J/\Psi} = 16\) GeV/fm\(^3\), \(\varepsilon_{\chi_c} = 2\) GeV/fm\(^3\), and \(\varepsilon_{\Psi^\prime} = 2\) GeV/fm\(^3\) (with a variant using \(\varepsilon_{\Psi^\prime} = 6.55\) GeV/fm\(^3\) also tested).
- The normalization factor for the \(J/\Psi\) to Drell-Yan ratio in elementary nucleon-nucleon collisions is \(B_{\mu\mu}\sigma_{NN}(J/\Psi) / \sigma_{NN}(DY) = 36\).
- The \(\Psi^\prime\) to \(J/\Psi\) ratio for nucleon-nucleon collisions is taken as 0.0165.
- The life-time of the pre-resonance \(c\bar{c}\) pair is assumed to be \(\tau_{c\bar{c}} = 0.3\) fm/c.

## Context

This work extends previous HSD transport studies of charmonium dynamics (Bratkovskaya 2003, 2004; Cassing 2001) by testing both the comover absorption model and the QGP threshold scenario against the combined NA50 and NA60 data sets for In+In and Pb+Pb collisions at 158 A GeV. The comover model had previously failed to describe In+In data with sufficient accuracy in simpler approaches (NA60 theoretical predictions 2005), but the dynamical HSD treatment with updated parameters now matches all data. The paper provides predictions for Au+Au collisions at 25 A GeV for the future FAIR facility, which will allow experimental distinction between the models.
