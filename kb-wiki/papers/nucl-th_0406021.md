# Nuclear Structure based on Correlated Realistic Nucleon-Nucleon Potentials

> We present a novel scheme for nuclear structure calculations based on realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials. The essential ingredient is the explicit treatment of the dominant interaction-induced correlations by means of the Unitary Correlation Operator Method (UCOM). Short-range central and tensor correlations are imprinted into simple, uncorrelated many-body states through a state-independent unitary transformation. Applying the unitary transformation to the realistic Hamiltonian leads to a correlated, low-momentum interaction, well suited for all kinds of many-body models, e.g., Hartree-Fock or shell-model. We employ the correlated interaction, supplemented by a phenomenological correction to account for genuine three-body forces, in the framework of variational calculations with antisymmetrised Gaussian trial states (Fermionic Molecular Dynamics). Ground state properties of nuclei up to mass numbers A<~60 are discussed. Binding energies, charge radii, and charge distributions are in good agreement with experimental data. We perform angular momentum projections of the intrinsically deformed variational states to extract rotational spectra.

## Key claim

The Unitary Correlation Operator Method (UCOM) can incorporate short-range central and tensor correlations into simple many-body states via a state-independent unitary transformation, but the two-body approximation for the correlated Hamiltonian is only justified when the tensor correlation function is artificially restricted in range to account for screening effects that would otherwise require computationally prohibitive higher-order cluster terms.

## Method

The authors constructed a correlated Hamiltonian by applying a unitary correlation operator, decomposed into separate central and tensor parts, to a realistic nucleon-nucleon potential (Argonne V18 or Bonn). The correlated interaction was evaluated in a two-body approximation using a Baker-Campbell-Hausdorff expansion, then supplemented with a phenomenological three-body force correction. Variational calculations were performed using antisymmetrised Gaussian wave packets (Fermionic Molecular Dynamics) for ground states, followed by angular momentum projection of the intrinsically deformed states to extract rotational spectra.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies, charge radii, and charge distributions were calculated for nuclei up to mass numbers A less than or approximately 60.
- The central correlation function Rp(r) shifts nucleon pairs out of the repulsive core region where the interparticle distance is less than about 0.5 fm, as shown by the deuteron two-body density from the AV18 potential.
- The tensor correlation function theta(r) generates a D-wave admixture in the deuteron; the amplitude of the D-wave component is given by sin(3 sqrt(2) theta(r)).
- The mean interparticle distance at saturation density is about 1.8 fm, which is larger than the range of the central shift function s(r) approximately equal to Rp(r) minus r.

## Context

This work extends the Unitary Correlation Operator Method previously developed by Feldmeier and Neff (1998) and Neff and Feldmeier (2003) to heavier nuclei, building on ab initio approaches such as Green's Function Monte Carlo (Pieper and Wiringa, 2002) and the no-core shell model (Caurier and Navratil, 2002) that are limited to A less than approximately 12. The method enables nuclear structure calculations for medium-mass nuclei using realistic potentials while avoiding the full complexity of correlated many-body states, though it requires a phenomenological three-body force correction similar to those constructed by the Argonne group (Pieper and Pandharipande, 2001).
