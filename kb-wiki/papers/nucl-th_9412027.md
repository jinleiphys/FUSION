# Role of Cloud Renormalization in Convolution Models for EMC and Drell-Yan Ratios

> Generalizing a recent convolution model of the nucleon which explicitly conserves charge and momentum we reexamine the influence of an enhanced pion field in nuclei on EMC and Drell-Yan ratios. Due to wave function renormalization constants the effect is more than 50% smaller than predicted by the standard pion excess model. In particular there is no discrepancy between the EMC data and our results in the x-region which was expected to be most sensitive to the pion.

## Key claim

When wave function renormalization constants are properly included as an overall prefactor in a convolution model that iterates meson-baryon loops to infinite order, the effect of an enhanced pion field in nuclei on EMC and Drell-Yan ratios is more than 50% smaller than predicted by the standard pion excess model, and there is no discrepancy between EMC data and the model in the x-region (0.1 ≤ x ≤ 0.3) previously thought most sensitive to pions.

## Method

The author generalizes the two-phase convolution model of Szczurek and Speth (1993), which treats the nucleon as a bare core plus a pion-nucleon phase, to nuclear matter by introducing a nuclear pion distribution function and a Fermi motion distribution. The model enforces charge and momentum conservation via renormalization constants and a binding parameter, and the nuclear pion distribution is calculated microscopically using a nuclear matter RPA calculation with NN⁻¹ and ΔN⁻¹ excitations, including short-range correlations.

## Key numbers

- The renormalization constant for the free nucleon, Z_N, is approximately 0.7 for the chosen parameters (monopole πNN formfactor with cutoff Λ_πNN = 800 MeV).
- In the microscopic calculation for ⁵⁶Fe, the pion field carries approximately 4.5% of the total momentum, requiring a binding parameter η = 0.94 to conserve momentum, corresponding to an average separation energy of (1-η)m_N ≈ 56 MeV.
- The Fermi momentum used for ⁵⁶Fe is k_F = 260 MeV, corresponding to a density ρ = 0.87 ρ₀.
- The EMC ratio F₂^A/F₂^D remains unity at x ≈ 0.2 independently of the enhancement factor λ in the schematic model.
- The short-range correlation cutoff in the NN channel is q_c = 3.94 fm⁻¹, giving a Migdal parameter g'_NN(0) = 0.57; in the NΔ and ΔΔ channels q_c is tuned to give g'_NΔ(0) = g'_ΔΔ(0) = 1/3.

## Context

This work extends the two-phase nucleon model of Szczurek and Speth (1993) and contrasts with the approach of Melnitchouk and Thomas, who expand only to order g_πNN² and renormalize only the bare nucleon part, which the author argues is unsuitable for nuclear matter. The paper contradicts the standard pion excess model of Ericson and Thomas and Berger et al., showing that with proper renormalization, EMC data are not sensitive enough to detect an enhanced pion field, while Drell-Yan data still rule out such an enhancement even after the 50% reduction. This enables future work to focus on more unconventional explanations, such as changes in gluon properties or partial restoration of chiral symmetry.
