# Anomalies and the Helicity of the Thermal State

> We study the thermal expectation value of the following observeable at finite temperature T and chemical potential μ: < L_{12} L_{34} ... L_{d-3,d-2} P_{d-1} > where L_{ij} denote the angular momenta, and P_i denotes the spatial momentum in d spacetime dimensions with d even. We call this observeable the thermal helicity. Using a variety of arguments, we motivate the surprising assertion that thermal helicity per unit volume is a polynomial in T and μ. Further, in field theories without chiral gravitino, we conjecture that this polynomial can be derived from the anomaly polynomial of the theory. We show that this conjecture is related to the recent conjecture on gravitational anomaly induced transport made in arXiv:1201.2812 . We support these statements by various sphere partition function computations in free theories.

## Key claim

The paper asserts that the thermal helicity per unit volume, defined as the thermal expectation value of a product of angular momenta and a spatial momentum, is a polynomial in temperature \(T\) and chemical potential \(\mu\), and that for field theories without chiral gravitini, this polynomial can be derived directly from the anomaly polynomial of the theory via a specific substitution rule.

## Method

The author computes the thermal helicity by first defining a thermal partition function on \(S^{2n-1} \times \mathbb{R}\) with chemical potentials \(\Omega_a\) turned on for the mutually commuting angular momenta on the sphere. Taking the flat-space limit \(R \to \infty\) extracts the flat-space thermal helicity per unit volume from derivatives of the partition function with respect to \(\Omega_a\). The resulting quantity \(\mathfrak{F}_{anom}^\omega[T,\mu]\) is then compared to the anomaly polynomial \(\mathcal{P}_{anom}\) for free Weyl fermions, chiral p-form bosons, and chiral gravitini, and is also evaluated for 2d CFTs in the Cardy regime using the Cardy formula.

## Key numbers

- For a 2d CFT, the thermal helicity (momentum density) per unit volume is given by \(2\pi\left(\frac{c_R - c_L}{24}T^2 + k_R\left(\frac{\mu_R}{2\pi}\right)^2 - k_L\left(\frac{\mu_L}{2\pi}\right)^2\right)\), where \(c_{L,R}\) are central charges and \(k_{L,R}\) are Kac-Moody levels.
- The thermal helicity per unit volume in \(d=2n\) dimensions is expressed as \(-(n-1)! (2T)^{n-1} \mathfrak{F}_{anom}^\omega[T,\mu]\).
- The function \(\mathfrak{F}_{anom}^\omega\) is obtained from the anomaly polynomial via the replacement rule: \(\mathfrak{F}_{anom}^\omega[T,\mu] = \mathcal{P}_{anom}\left(F \mapsto \mu, p_1(\mathfrak{R}) \mapsto -T^2, p_{k>1}(\mathfrak{R}) \mapsto 0\right)\).
- The volume of \(S^{2n-1}\) with radius \(R\) is \(\forall_{2n-1} = \frac{2\pi^n R^{2n-1}}{(n-1)!}\).

## Context

This work extends the study of anomaly-induced transport coefficients, previously computed for free Weyl fermions by Loganayagam (2012) using adiabaticity arguments, to a broader class of theories including chiral p-form bosons and 2d CFTs. It connects the thermal helicity observable to the anomaly polynomial, generalizing the replacement rule found in that earlier work, and relates the conjecture to the gravitational anomaly-induced transport conjecture of arXiv:1201.2812. The results enable a systematic computation of thermal helicity and anomaly-induced transport coefficients in arbitrary even dimensions for theories without chiral gravitini, and suggest that the finite-temperature relation to gravitational anomalies is qualitatively different from the zero-temperature case.
