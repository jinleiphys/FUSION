# Universal relationships for neutron stars from perturbative approach

> The universal relationships for compact stars have been investigated employing perturbative approach using canonical (APR) and Brussels-Montreal Skyrme (BSk22, BSk24, BSk26) equations of state describing hadronic matter of neutron stars. The neutron star matter has been considered to be $β$-equilibrated neutron-proton-electron-muon matter at the core with a rigid crust. The multipole moments of a slowly rotating neutron star characterize its external gravitational field. These variables are dependent on the interior structure of the neutron star described by the equation of state of the neutron star matter. The properties of neutron stars, such as the mass, the radius, the dimensionless moment of inertia, the compactness, the Love number, the dimensionless tidal deformability and the dimensionless quadrupole moment have been calculated and relations among these quantities have been explored. It is found that most of these relations do not depend sensitively on the details of the internal structure of neutron stars. Such universality implies that the measurement of a single quantity appearing in a universal relation would automatically provide information about the others, notwithstanding the fact that those may not be accessible observationally. These can be used to estimate deformability of compact stars through moment of inertia measurements, to quantify spin in binary inspirals by breaking degeneracies in the detection of gravitational waves and test General Relativity in a way that is independent of nuclear structure.

## Key claim

The paper finds that most relations among neutron star properties (mass, radius, compactness, moment of inertia, Love number, tidal deformability, and quadrupole moment) do not depend sensitively on the internal structure of the star, but this universality is weaker for relations involving the dimensionless quadrupole moment, Love number, and tidal deformability plotted directly against mass.

## Method

The authors solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for hydrostatic equilibrium, combined with the equations for tidal deformability (Love number \(k_2\) and dimensionless tidal deformability \(\Lambda\)) and the Hartle formalism for slow rotation (dimensionless moment of inertia \(I\) and dimensionless quadrupole moment \(Q\)). They used four equations of state (APR, BSk22, BSk24, BSk26) for \(\beta\)-equilibrated neutron-proton-electron-muon matter with a rigid crust, and fitted the resulting universal relations with a fourth-order polynomial.

## Key numbers

* Maximum masses obtained: 2.19 \(M_\odot\) (APR), 2.27 \(M_\odot\) (BSk22), 2.28 \(M_\odot\) (BSk24), and 2.18 \(M_\odot\) (BSk26).
* The supernova remnant HESS J1731-347 has mass \(M = 0.77_{-0.17}^{+0.20} M_\odot\) and radius \(R = 10.4_{-0.78}^{+0.86}\) km.
* Pulsar PSR J0952-0607 has mass \(M = 2.35 \pm 0.17 M_\odot\), the precisely measured largest mass found to date.
* Pulsar PSR J0740+6620 has mass \(2.08 \pm 0.07 M_\odot\) (one measurement) and \(2.072^{+0.067}_{-0.066} M_\odot\) (another measurement).
* Pulsar PSR J1614-2230 has mass in the range \(1.97 \pm 0.04 M_\odot\).
* Pulsar PSR J0348+0432 has mass in the range \(1.97\) to \(2.18 M_\odot\).

## Context

This work extends the search for equation-of-state-independent universal relations for neutron stars, following earlier studies by Benhar (2005), Berti (2004, 2005), Pappas (2012), Yagi (2013, 2015), and others. The authors note that the slow-rotation approximation is appropriate only for neutron stars with periods of one millisecond or longer, and that the universality may hold even for rapid rotation but with distinct relations for different spin periods, with variation expected to be limited within 10%. The results enable estimation of tidal deformability from moment of inertia measurements, quantification of spin in binary inspirals by breaking degeneracies in gravitational wave detection, and tests of General Relativity independent of nuclear structure.
