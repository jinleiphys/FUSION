# Lambda over Kaon Enhancement in Heavy Ion Collisions at Several TeV

> We introduced recently a new theoretical scheme which accounts for hydrodynamically expanding bulk matter, jets, and the interaction between the two. Important for the particle production at intermediate values of transverse momentum (p_t) are jet-hadrons produced inside the fluid. They pick up quarks and antiquarks (or diquarks) from the thermal matter rather than creating them via the Schwinger mechanism -- the usual mechanism of hadron production from string fragmentation. These hadrons carry plasma properties (flavor, flow), but also the large momentum of the transversely moving string segment connecting quark and antiquark (or diquark). They therefore show up at quite large values of p_t, not polluted by soft particle production. We will show that this mechanism leads to a pronounced peak in the lambda / kaon ratio at intermediate p_t. The effect increases substantially with centrality, which reflects the increasing transverse size with centrality.

## Key claim
The observed peak in the lambda/kaon ratio at intermediate transverse momentum (2–6 GeV/c) in PbPb collisions at 2.76 TeV is reproduced by a model where jet-hadrons produced inside the fluid pick up quarks and diquarks from the thermal matter rather than via the Schwinger mechanism, with the effect increasing substantially with centrality due to the increasing transverse size.

## Method
The author uses the EPOS2.17v3 framework, which combines hydrodynamically expanding bulk matter, jets from hard scatterings, and fluid-jet interactions. For string segments formed inside the fluid that have enough energy to escape, the quark, antiquark, or diquark needed for flux-tube breaking is taken from the fluid (carrying its flavor and flow properties) rather than produced via the Schwinger mechanism. The probability of forming such hadrons inside the fluid is estimated as a function of transverse momentum and centrality, and the resulting lambda and kaon spectra are compared to ALICE data.

## Key numbers
- Lambda/kaon ratio shows a pronounced peak in the transverse momentum range between 2 and 6 GeV/c in PbPb collisions at 2.76 TeV.
- Transverse fluid velocities up to 0.7c can provide a "push" of a couple of GeV to the transverse momentum of the string segment.
- The probability P_inside of forming (pre)hadrons inside the fluid is shown for 0-5%, 30-40%, and 60-70% most central PbPb events at 2.76 TeV, decreasing strongly towards peripheral collisions.
- The full model reproduces the trend that going from peripheral towards central collisions produces a more pronounced peak that moves to higher p_t.

## Context
This work extends the author's earlier theoretical scheme (Werner, 2012, "jetbulk") which already covered spectra from very low to very high p_t in both pp and PbPb collisions. It addresses the lambda/kaon enhancement previously observed in AuAu collisions at 200 GeV (STAR collaboration) and more recently in PbPb collisions at 2.76 TeV (ALICE collaboration). The approach contrasts with coalescence models and standard string fragmentation, and enables using the lambda/kaon ratio as an observable that carries information about the quark-gluon plasma despite hadronization.
