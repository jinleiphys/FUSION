# Extension of effective lagrangian approach to structure of selected nucle i far from stability

> In a previous paper, the convergence of a new effective field theory and density functional theory (EFT/DFT) approach to the description of the nuclear many-body system was studied. The most sophisticated parameter set (here G1) determined by Furnstahl, Serot and Tang from a fit along the valley of stability was found to provide quantitative predictions for total binding energies and single-particle and single-hole binding energies, spins, and parities for selected doubly-magic nuclei far from stability. Binding energies of all the even Sn isotopes (Z=50) were also well described. Here the calculations are extended to all isotones with magic numbers N=28, 50, 82, 126, and isotopes with Z=28, 50, 82 with similar predictive results.

## Key claim

The G1 parameter set, fitted to stable nuclei along the valley of stability, provides quantitative predictions for total binding energies of semi-magic isotones and isotopes far from stability with overall agreement below 1%, though predictions of ground-state spin and parity become less accurate when neutron level density is high and energy spacings are on the order of tens of keV.

## Method

The author solves the Kohn-Sham equations derived from an effective lagrangian density built from nucleon, scalar, vector, isovector, and electromagnetic fields, using the G1 parameter set from Furnstahl, Serot, and Tang (1997). The coupled, nonlinear Dirac and meson field equations are solved self-consistently using a Green's function method for spherical nuclei, with no refitting of parameters. Total binding energies and chemical potentials are computed for all known even-even isotones with magic neutron numbers N=28, 50, 82, 126 and isotopes with magic proton numbers Z=28, 50, 82, as well as for neighboring odd nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Total binding energy predictions for all semi-magic isotones and isotopes agree with experiment below 1% deviation, with errors generally less than 0.5% except for N=28 isotones
- For N=82 isotones, the predicted ground-state of $^{147}_{\ 65}\textrm{Tb}_{82}$ is 11/2- while the experimental ground-state is 1/2+, with the metastable 11/2- state lying only 50 keV above the true ground state
- For Z=28 isotopes, the predicted ground-state of $^{61}_{28}\textrm{Ni}_{33}$ is 5/2- while the experimental value is 3/2-, with an excited 5/2- state lying only 70 keV above the ground state
- The G1 parameter set includes 18 fitted constants, with scalar meson mass m_s = 506.7 MeV and coupling constants g_s^2 = 97.39, g_v^2 = 147.09, g_rho^2 = 77.033

## Context

This work extends the EFT/DFT calculations of Huertas (2002) from doubly-magic nuclei and even Sn isotopes to all known semi-magic isotones and isotopes with magic numbers N=28, 50, 82, 126 and Z=28, 50, 82. The approach builds on the density functional theory framework of Hohenberg and Kohn (1964, 1965) and the effective field theory parameterization of Furnstahl, Serot, and Tang (1997), who fitted the G1 parameter set to stable nuclei. The results demonstrate that the EFT/DFT approach, without parameter refitting, can predict ground-state properties of nuclei far from stability, though the detailed ordering of single-particle levels becomes sensitive to the central potential when level densities are high, motivating further studies of beta-transition rates and excited states.
