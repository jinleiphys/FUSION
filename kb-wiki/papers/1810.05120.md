# Initial-state energy loss in cold QCD matter and the Drell-Yan process

> The effects of parton energy loss in nuclear matter on the Drell-Yan process in pA and $π$A collisions at fixed-target energies are investigated. Calculations are based on the Baier-Dokshitzer-Mueller-Peigné-Schiff (BDMPS) framework embedded in a next-to-leading order calculation, using the transport coefficient extracted from $J/ψ$ measurements. Model calculations prove in good agreement with preliminary measurements by the E906 experiment, despite a slightly different magnitude, supporting a consistent picture between Drell-Yan and $J/ψ$ data. Predictions for the COMPASS future measurements in $π$A collisions at $\sqrt{s}=18.9$~GeV are also performed. At higher collision energy ($\sqrt{s}=38.7$~GeV), Drell-Yan measurements are only slightly affected by energy loss effects. On the contrary, the E906 results turn out in clear disagreement with nuclear PDF effects alone. The comparison of E772, E866, and E906 measurements indicates for the first time a clear violation of QCD factorization in Drell-Yan production in pA collisions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed suppression of Drell-Yan production in proton-nucleus collisions at low energy (E906, sqrt(s)=15 GeV) is primarily due to initial-state parton energy loss in the LPM regime, rather than nuclear PDF effects, but the magnitude of the predicted suppression is slightly smaller than the preliminary data indicate, leaving room for additional contributions.

## Method

The authors compute Drell-Yan production cross sections at next-to-leading order using the DYNNLO Monte Carlo program, incorporating initial-state energy loss via the BDMPS formalism with a Poisson-distributed energy loss probability. The transport coefficient qhat_0 is taken from previous J/psi suppression analyses (0.07-0.09 GeV^2/fm) without refitting, and calculations are compared to E906, E866, and NA10 data, with predictions for COMPASS.

## Key numbers

- Transport coefficient used: qhat_0 = 0.07-0.09 GeV^2/fm, extracted from J/psi measurements in the fully coherent regime
- E906 experiment: sqrt(s)=15 GeV, mass range 4.5 < M < 5.5 GeV, with 0.1 < x2 < 0.3; model predicts R_pA(W/C) roughly 5% higher than preliminary data and R_pA(Fe/C) roughly 10% higher
- E866 experiment: sqrt(s)=38.7 GeV, mass range 4 < M < 8 GeV; energy loss effects are negligible except at xF > 0.8
- NA10 experiment: sqrt(s)=16.2 GeV, mass range 4.35 < M < 15 GeV; energy loss gives approximately 3% suppression (R_piA approx 0.97), independent of x2
- COMPASS predictions: at xF=0.9, model predicts R_piA(W/NH3) approx 0.8 from energy loss, compared to approx 0.94 from isospin effects alone
- Average LPM energy loss: <epsilon_i> = (1/4) alpha_s C_R qhat L^2, with alpha_s=1/2 frozen at low scales
- Medium length: L = 3/4 R, with R = r_0 A^(1/3), r_0 = 1.12 fm

## Context

This work extends the BDMPS energy loss framework (Baier, Dokshitzer, Mueller, Peigné, Schiff, 1996) from J/psi suppression studies (Arleo, 2012, 2013) to Drell-Yan production, which is uniquely sensitive to LPM initial-state energy loss because the color-neutral final state eliminates fully coherent energy loss. The results contradict the assumption in nPDF global fits (EPPS16 by Eskola et al., 2016; nCTEQ15 by Kovarik et al., 2015) that Drell-Yan data can be described by nuclear PDF effects alone, and the comparison of E772, E866, and E906 data shows for the first time a clear violation of QCD factorization in Drell-Yan production in pA collisions. The findings enable future extraction of the transport coefficient from COMPASS piA data at sqrt(s)=18.9 GeV and support using Drell-Yan at higher energies (LHC) for clean nPDF extraction.
