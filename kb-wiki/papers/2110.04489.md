# Probing gluon helicity with heavy flavor at the EIC

> We propose a new measurement of the heavy flavor hadron double spin asymmetry in deep-inelastic scattering at a future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) to constrain the polarized gluon distribution function inside the proton. Statistical projection on $D^0$ meson double spin asymmetry is calculated with an EIC central detector using an all-silicon tracker and vertexing subsystem. A first impact study was done by interpreting pseudo-data at next-to-leading order in QCD. The sensitivity of the experimental observable in constraining gluon helicity distribution in a wide range of parton momentum fraction $x$ has been investigated considering different beam energy configurations. This measurement complements the inclusive spin-dependent structure function measurement and provides an opportunity to constrain the gluon helicity distribution in the moderate $x$ region.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that measuring the double spin asymmetry of $D^0$ mesons in deep-inelastic scattering at a future Electron-Ion Collider can provide complementary constraints on the polarized gluon distribution, particularly in the moderate $x$ region, but the authors hedge that the classic inclusive $g_1$ measurements will still play the dominant role in constraining gluon helicity and its contribution to the proton spin.

## Method

The authors performed a Monte Carlo simulation study using pythiaeRHIC (PYTHIA V6.4) with a fast smearing framework that incorporated detector response tables from a full GEANT4 simulation of an all-silicon tracker and vertexing subsystem. They generated pseudo-data for three beam energy configurations (18 GeV x 275 GeV, 5 GeV x 100 GeV, and 5 GeV x 41 GeV) and extracted $D^0$ meson yields via $K\pi$ invariant mass fits after applying topological decay cuts and kinematic cuts ($Q^2 > 2$ GeV$^2$, $0.05 < y < 0.8$, $W^2 > 4$ GeV$^2$). The impact on polarized parton distribution functions was assessed using a Bayesian reweighting method applied to the NNPDFpol1.1 and DSSV14 polarized PDF sets at next-to-leading order in QCD.

## Key numbers

- Beam polarizations assumed: 80% for the electron beam and 70% for the proton beam
- Integrated luminosity: 100 fb$^{-1}$ for each beam energy configuration
- Kinematic cuts applied: $Q^2 > 2$ GeV$^2$, $0.05 < y < 0.8$, $W^2 > 4$ GeV$^2$
- Predicted asymmetry $A_1^c$ at moderate $x$ region is around 10-20% (using DSSV PDF set at $Q^2 = 5$ GeV$^2$)
- For the 5 GeV x 41 GeV configuration with NNPDFpol1.1, the precision of the gluon spin contribution to the proton spin from gluons with momentum fraction down to the intermediate-$x$ region is increased up to a factor of 1.5
- For the 5 GeV x 100 GeV configuration with NNPDFpol1.1, the precision improvement goes up to a factor of 2 around $10^{-3} \lesssim x_{\text{min}} \lesssim 10^{-2}$
- After reweighting, surviving replicas were approximately 70 for NNPDFpol1.1 (from 100 original) and approximately 850 for DSSV14 (from 1000 original)
- Pion/kaon identification assumed feasible up to momentum limits of 10, 6, and 50 GeV/c in pseudo-rapidity regions (-3,-1), (-1,1), and (1,3) respectively

## Context

This work extends the COMPASS collaboration measurement (2012) which yielded only one data point for heavy flavor double spin asymmetry and was interpreted based on the photo-production approximation of Bojak (2000). The study builds on recent next-to-leading order perturbative QCD calculations for heavy flavor production in polarized DIS by Hekhorn et al. (2018, 2019, 2021) and the all-silicon tracker conceptual design demonstrated in EIC simulations by Arrington et al. (2021) and Kelsey et al. (2021). The proposed measurement complements inclusive spin-dependent structure function measurements at the EIC and provides a unique opportunity to constrain the gluon helicity distribution in the moderate $x$ region, where lower center-of-mass energy configurations offer particular sensitivity.
