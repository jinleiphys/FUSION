# Chiral transport in strong fields from holography

> Anomaly-induced transport phenomena in presence of strong external electromagnetic fields are explored within a 4D field theory defined holographically as $U(1)_V\times U(1)_A$ Maxwell-Chern-Simons theory in Schwarzschild-$AdS_5$. Two complementary studies are reported. In the first one, we present results on the Ohmic conductivity, diffusion constant, chiral magnetic conductivity, and additional anomaly-induced transport coefficients as functions of external e/m fields. Next, gradient resummation in a constant background magnetic field is performed. All-order resummed constitutive relations are parameterised by four momenta-dependent transport coefficient functions (TCFs). A highlight of this part is a thorough study of {\it non-dissipative} chiral magnetic waves (CMW) in strong magnetic fields.

## Key claim

The paper explores anomaly-induced transport phenomena in a holographically defined 4D field theory, going beyond the weak-field limit to compute transport coefficients and resummed constitutive relations as functions of strong external electromagnetic fields, with the key condition that the results are derived within the specific holographic model of \(U(1)_V\times U(1)_A\) Maxwell-Chern-Simons theory in Schwarzschild-\(AdS_5\) and are thus model-dependent rather than universal.

## Method

The authors use holographic duality, specifically a \(U(1)_V\times U(1)_A\) Maxwell-Chern-Simons theory in Schwarzschild-\(AdS_5\), to compute transport coefficients for a chiral plasma. In the first part, they compute Ohmic conductivity, chiral magnetic conductivity, and other transport coefficients as functions of constant external electric and magnetic fields up to first order in gradient expansion. In the second part, they perform gradient resummation in a constant background magnetic field to obtain all-order resummed constitutive relations parameterized by four momentum-dependent transport coefficient functions (TCFs), and study non-dissipative chiral magnetic waves (CMW).

## Key numbers

- The chiral magnetic conductivity \(\sigma_\chi^0\) in the strong magnetic field limit (\(\kappa\mathbf{B}\rightarrow\infty\)) approaches \(\sigma_\chi^0\rightarrow 1/(\kappa\mathbf{B})\), making the CMW speed approach that of light.
- For large magnetic field (\(\kappa\mathbf{B}\rightarrow\infty\), \(\kappa\mathbf{E}=0\)): \(\delta\sigma_\chi^0 \rightarrow 3.349/(\kappa\mathbf{B})^{3/2}\), \(\sigma_{\chi H}^0 \rightarrow -1/(\kappa\mathbf{B})^2\), \(\sigma_{\chi e}^0 \rightarrow 0.977/(\kappa\mathbf{B})^{3}\), \(\sigma_{s}^0 \rightarrow -6.751/(\kappa\mathbf{B})^2\).
- For large electric field (\(\kappa\mathbf{B}=0\), \(\kappa\mathbf{E}\rightarrow\infty\)): \(\delta\sigma_\chi^0 \rightarrow -2.243/(\kappa\mathbf{E})^{3/2}\), \(\sigma_{\chi H}^0 \rightarrow -1/(\kappa\mathbf{E})^2\), \(\sigma_{\chi e}^0 \rightarrow 6.04/(\kappa\mathbf{E})^{2}\), \(\sigma_{s}^0 \rightarrow -3.069/(\kappa\mathbf{E})^{5/2}\).
- The diffusion constant \(\mathcal{D}_0\) at large magnetic field (\(\kappa\mathbf{B}\rightarrow\infty\), \(\kappa\mathbf{E}=0\)) decays as \(\mathcal{D}_0 \rightarrow 0.083/(\kappa\mathbf{B})\).
- The relaxation time \(\tau_{\bar \chi}\) at large magnetic field (\(\kappa\mathbf{B}\rightarrow\infty\), \(\kappa\mathbf{E}=0\)) decays as \(\tau_{\bar \chi} \rightarrow -0.36/(\kappa\mathbf{B})^{3/2}\).
- The Ohmic conductivity \(\sigma_e^0\) at large equal fields (\(\kappa\mathbf{B}=\kappa\mathbf{E}\rightarrow\infty\)) behaves as \(\sigma_e^0 \simeq 1 + 3.09 (\kappa\mathbf{E}\cdot\kappa\mathbf{B})^{0.27}\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Bu et al., 2016; Bu et al., 2018) on chiral transport in holographic plasmas, which were limited to weak-field approximations, and also builds on prior holographic studies by Yee (2009) and Gynther (2010). The results enable the construction of fully nonlinear chiral magneto-hydrodynamics (MHD) for systems such as quark-gluon plasma in heavy ion collisions or primordial plasma in the early universe, where strong electromagnetic fields are present. The discovery of non-dissipative chiral magnetic waves at strong magnetic fields opens the possibility of studying anomalous resonance phenomena, as noted by Haack (2018).
