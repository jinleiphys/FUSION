# Mean field effects in heavy-ion collisions at AGS energies

> The question whether mean field effects exist in heavy-ion collisions at AGS energies is studied in the framework of A Relativistic Transport (ART) model. It is found that in central collisions of Au+Au at $P_{beam}/A=$11.6 GeV/c a simple, Skyrme-type nuclear mean field satisfying the causality requirement reduces the maximum baryon and energy densities reached in the cascade model by about 30\% and 40\%, respectively. The mean field affects the inclusive, single particle observables of various hadrons by at most 20\%. We show, however, that the mean field causes a factor of 2.5 increase in the strength of the baryon transverse collective flow.

# Mean field effects in heavy-ion collisions at AGS energies

## Key claim

Mean field effects exist in heavy-ion collisions at AGS energies, but they affect inclusive single-particle observables by at most 20%, which is compatible with current systematic error bars; the strongest signature is a factor of 2.5 increase in the baryon transverse collective flow, making flow analysis a more sensitive probe than single-particle spectra.

## Method

The authors developed a Relativistic Transport (ART) model extending the Boltzmann-Uhling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) approach to AGS energies, including baryons (N, Delta(1232), N*(1440), N*(1535), Lambda, Sigma) and mesons (pi, rho, omega, eta, K) with explicit isospin degrees of freedom. Elastic and inelastic hadron-hadron collisions were simulated using experimental cross sections and resonance formation, with a Skyrme-type mean field (soft equation of state satisfying causality up to about 7 rho_0) applied via the test-particle method. Central Au+Au collisions at P_beam/A = 11.6 GeV/c were simulated with and without the mean field to compare densities and flow observables.

## Key numbers

- Maximum baryon density in the cascade model: about 9 rho_0; with the soft mean field: reduced to about 7 rho_0 (approximately 30% reduction).
- Maximum energy density in the cascade model: about 3.6 GeV/fm^3; with the soft mean field: reduced to about 2.6 GeV/fm^3 (approximately 40% reduction).
- The high energy density region (e_l >= 2.0 GeV/fm^3) in the cascade model has a maximum volume of about 200 fm^3 at about 4 fm/c.
- The mean field affects inclusive single-particle observables by at most 20% (largest difference found in the proton transverse momentum distribution).
- The flow parameter (slope of transverse momentum at midrapidity) is about a factor of 2.5 larger in calculations with the mean field compared to without, for impact parameters of 2, 6, and 10 fm.

## Context

This work extends the Boltzmann-Uhling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) model from intermediate energies to AGS energies, and directly addresses the omission of mean field effects in the ARC model. The authors note that cascade model results (similar to ARC) predict baryon and energy densities above the estimated critical values for quark-gluon plasma formation (about 5 rho_0 and 2.5 GeV/fm^3, as cited from Wong). The finding that collective flow is sensitive to the mean field, while single-particle observables are not, parallels findings at Bevalac energies (Danielewicz) and suggests that experimentally measured flow significantly smaller than cascade predictions could indicate quark-gluon plasma formation. The work enables future experimental tests using transverse collective flow as a tool to distinguish between hadronic models with and without mean field effects at AGS energies.
