# Erroneous Wave Functions of Ciuchi et al for Collective Modes in Neutron Production on Metallic Hydride Cathodes

> There is a recent comment (Ciuchi et al., 2012) concerning the theory of collective many body effects on the neutron production rates in a chemical battery cathode. Ciuchi et al employ an inverse beta decay expression that contains a two body amplitude. Only one electron and one proton may exist in the Ciuchi et al model initial state wave function. A flaw in their reasoning is that one cannot in reality describe collective many body correlations with only a two particle wave function. One needs very many particles to describe collective effects. In the model wave functions of Ciuchi et al there are no metallic hydrides, there are no cathodes and there are no chemical batteries. Employing a wave function with only one electron and one proton is inadequate for describing collective metallic hydride surface quantum plasma physics in cathodes accurately.

# Erroneous Wave Functions of Ciuchi et al for Collective Modes in Neutron Production on Metallic Hydride Cathodes

## Key claim

The authors argue that the criticism by Ciuchi et al (2012) of their collective many-body theory of neutron production in hydride battery cathodes is invalid because Ciuchi et al used a two-body wave function (one electron and one proton) that cannot describe the collective many-body correlations required for the actual physical system. The disagreement in predicted neutron production rates has been reduced from an initial factor of 10^40 down to "a mere two orders of magnitude," and the authors assert that even if their own theoretical rates were high by a factor of 300, the experimental observations would still be detectable.

## Method

The authors compare the two-body wave function approach of Ciuchi et al, which uses the formula Gamma = |psi(0)|^2 v sigma for electron-proton inverse beta decay, against the proper many-body treatment involving N electrons and N protons (with N ~ 10^16 participating in a surface plasmon). They replace the two-body probability |psi(0)|^2 with a thermal-averaged many-body correlation function C_T, computed at an optical noise temperature of T ~ 5000 K, which accounts for collective plasmon modes in the hot metallic hydride cathode.

## Key numbers

- The initial disagreement in neutron production rates between the authors and Ciuchi et al was a factor of 10^40, later reduced to a factor of 10^20, and then to 10^7 in an internal report by Polosa.
- The remaining disagreement in neutron production rates between the authors and Ciuchi et al is now "a mere two orders of magnitude" (factor of approximately 100).
- The authors state that even if their theoretical neutron counting rates were high by a factor of 300, the experiments by Cirillo et al (2012) could still observe nuclear transmutations.
- The many-body system involves N ~ 10^16 particles participating in a surface plasmon.
- The thermal average correlation function C_T is computed at an optical noise temperature of T ~ 5000 K, which the authors claim agrees with experiment by Cirillo et al (2012).

## Context

This paper responds to a comment by Ciuchi et al (2012) criticizing the authors' earlier work on collective many-body effects in neutron production (Widom and Srivastava, 2006, 2007). The authors extend their prior theoretical framework for inverse beta decay in condensed matter to specifically address the hydride battery cathode system, and they cite experimental observations of neutron production and nuclear transmutations by Cirillo et al (2012) as supporting their theoretical predictions. The work aims to reconcile the remaining factor-of-100 discrepancy between their collective-mode calculations and the two-body calculations of Ciuchi et al.
