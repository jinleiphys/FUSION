# The Laplacian Gauge Gluon Propagator in SU(N_c)

> We examine the gluon propagator in the Laplacian gauge in quenched lattice QCD as a function of the number of colours. We observe a weak dependence on $N_c$ over the whole momentum range. This implies an almost $N_c$-independent gluon pole mass in units of the string tension.

# The Laplacian Gauge Gluon Propagator in SU(N_c)

## Key claim
The gluon propagator in the Laplacian gauge shows an extremely weak dependence on the number of colors N_c over the entire momentum range, implying an almost N_c-independent gluon pole mass in units of the string tension. However, small systematic differences are visible between SU(3) and SU(2)/SU(4) in the infrared region below approximately 1 GeV, with the SU(3) propagator being slightly larger.

## Method
The authors performed quenched lattice QCD simulations for SU(2), SU(3), and SU(4) gauge groups at beta values chosen to yield similar lattice spacings in units of the string tension (beta=2.4, 6.0, and 10.9 respectively). They fixed the gauge using the Laplacian gauge, which avoids spurious Gribov copies, and computed the transverse gluon propagator on lattices of various volumes including 4^4, 8^4, 16^4, 16^3x64, and 32^4 for SU(2), and 4^4, 8^4, 8^3x32, and 16^3x32 for SU(4). The propagator was renormalized at a scale mu=1.94 GeV using the condition D_R(q)|_{q^2=mu^2} = 1/mu^2, and the pole mass was extracted via analytic continuation using Cornwall's Ansatz and polynomial fits.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacings in units of string tension: a*sqrt(sigma) = 0.2634(16) for SU(2) at beta=2.4, 0.2265(55) for SU(3) at beta=6.0, and 0.2429(14) for SU(4) at beta=10.9
- Inverse lattice spacings: 1.67(1) GeV for SU(2), 1.94(5) GeV for SU(3), and 1.81(1) GeV for SU(4)
- Renormalization constant ratios: Z_3^{SU(3)}/Z_3^{SU(2)} = 1.07(4) and Z_3^{SU(3)}/Z_3^{SU(4)} = 0.96(2) at mu=1.94 GeV
- Renormalized zero momentum gluon propagator: D(0) = 5.85(5) GeV^{-2} (independent of N_c within statistics)
- Gluon pole masses from Cornwall's Ansatz and polynomial fits: (1.85 +/- 0.30)*sqrt(sigma) for SU(2), (1.52 +/- 0.06)*sqrt(sigma) for SU(3), and (1.74 +/- 0.27)*sqrt(sigma) for SU(4), corresponding to approximately 600-800 MeV using sqrt(sigma)=440 MeV
- Gauge fields decorrelate at a distance of approximately 0.8 fm, or about 1.7 times the confining correlation length

## Context
This work extends previous lattice studies of the gluon propagator in the Laplacian gauge for SU(3) by Alexandrou, de Forcrand, and Follana (2000, 2001) to SU(2) and SU(4). It complements investigations of SU(N_c) Yang-Mills theories by Teper (1998) and Harris (1999) that focused on string tensions and glueball masses. The results support the conclusion that SU(2) is already close to the large N_c limit of QCD, consistent with findings from string tension and glueball mass studies. The near N_c-independence of the gluon propagator has implications for confinement models based on vortex condensation and for Dyson-Schwinger studies of chiral symmetry breaking, where the gluon propagator affects the quark propagator.
