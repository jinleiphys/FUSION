# Comment on "$Λ_c N$ interaction in leading order covariant chiral effective field theory"

> Song et al. [Phys. Rev. C 102, 065208 (2020)] presented results for the $Λ_c N$ interaction based on an extrapolation of lattice simulations by the HAL QCD Collaboration at unphysical quark masses to the physical point via covariant chiral effective field theory. We point out that their predictions for the $^3D_1$ partial wave disagree with available lattice results. We discuss the origin of that disagreement and present a comparison with predictions from conventional (non-relativistic) chiral effective field theory.

## Key claim

Song et al. (2020) claimed that leading-order covariant chiral effective field theory can extrapolate HAL QCD lattice simulations of the $\Lambda_c N$ interaction from unphysical quark masses to the physical point, but the authors show that this approach produces $^3D_1$ phase shifts that are strongly repulsive and disagree with available lattice results from Miyamoto (2019), whereas conventional (non-relativistic) chiral effective field theory yields qualitative agreement.

## Method

The authors compared the $^3D_1$ phase shifts and mixing angle $\varepsilon_1$ predicted by Song et al. (2020) using covariant chiral effective field theory at leading order against lattice QCD results from the HAL QCD Collaboration reported in the PhD thesis of Takaya Miyamoto (2019). They also contrasted these with predictions from their own conventional (non-relativistic) chiral effective field theory up to next-to-leading order from Haidenbauer (2018), analyzing the origin of the discrepancy in terms of how contact interactions contribute to partial waves in each framework.

## Key numbers

- For $m_\pi = 410$ MeV, the covariant $\chi$EFT predictions for the $^3D_1$ phase shift are strongly repulsive, while lattice QCD results (filled circles from Miyamoto 2019) indicate a weakly attractive interaction.
- For $m_\pi = 570$ MeV, the covariant $\chi$EFT predictions for the $^3D_1$ phase shift are also strongly repulsive, while lattice QCD results (open circles from Miyamoto 2019) show weak attraction.
- The conventional $\chi$EFT results from Haidenbauer (2018) for $m_\pi = 410$ MeV are "rather well in line" with the HAL QCD lattice results for the $^3D_1$ partial wave.
- The HAL QCD lattice simulations were performed at pion masses of $m_\pi = 410 - 700$ MeV.

## Context

This comment extends the debate on extrapolating lattice QCD results for charmed baryon-nucleon interactions to the physical point, specifically challenging the covariant chiral effective field theory approach of Song et al. (2020). It contrasts with the conventional (non-relativistic) chiral effective field theory used by Haidenbauer (2018) for $\Lambda_c N$ and Haidenbauer (2020) for $\Sigma_c N$, as well as the heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory approach of Meng (2020) for $\Sigma_c N$. The work enables future lattice QCD simulations at quark masses closer to the physical point and direct experimental constraints to resolve the extrapolation issue.
