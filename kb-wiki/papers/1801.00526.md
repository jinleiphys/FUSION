# Tomography and gravitational radii for hadrons by three-dimensional structure functions

> Three-dimensional tomography of hadrons can be investigated by generalized parton distributions (GPDs), transverse-momentum-dependent parton distributions (TMDs), and generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs). The GDA studies had been only theoretical for a long time because there was no experimental measurement until recently, whereas the GPDs and TMDs have been investigated extensively by deeply virtual Compton scattering and semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering. Here, we report our studies to determine pion GDAs from recent KEKB measurements on the differential cross section of $γ^* γ\to π^0 π^0$. Since an exotic-hadron pair can be produced in the final state, the GDAs can be used also for probing internal structure of exotic hadron candidates in future. The other important feature of the GDAs is that the GDAs contain information on form factors of the energy-momentum tensor for quarks and gluons, so that gravitational form factors and radii can be calculated from the determined GDAs. We show the mass (energy) and the mechanical (pressure, shear force) form factors and radii for the pion. Our analysis should be the first attempt for obtaining gravitational form factors and radii of hadrons by analysis of actual experimental measurements. We believe that a new field of gravitational physics is created from the microscopic level in terms of elementary quarks and gluons.

## Key claim
The authors claim the first extraction of gravitational form factors and radii for a hadron (the pion) from actual experimental measurements, specifically by analyzing Belle data on the two-photon process $\gamma^* \gamma \to \pi^0 \pi^0$. The hedge is that the extracted gravitational radii have systematic ambiguities depending on whether phase parameters are assigned to the S-wave or D-wave part, yielding a range rather than a single value.

## Method
The authors analyze Belle cross-section data for $\gamma^* \gamma \to \pi^0 \pi^0$ at $Q^2 \ge 8.92$ GeV$^2$ to extract pion generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs). The GDAs are parametrized with continuum and resonance contributions from $f_0(500)$ and $f_2(1270)$, including S- and D-wave terms and $\pi\pi$ phase shifts. From the first moment of the determined GDAs, timelike gravitational form factors $\Theta_1(s)$ and $\Theta_2(s)$ are obtained, then converted to spacelike form factors via a dispersion relation, and finally Fourier-transformed to spatial densities and root-mean-square radii.

## Key numbers
- The analysis uses 550 data points from Belle at $Q^2 = 8.92$, 10.93, 13.37, 17.23, and 24.25 GeV$^2$, with invariant mass $0.5\ \text{GeV} < W < 2.1\ \text{GeV}$ and $\cos\theta = 0.1, 0.3, 0.5, 0.7, 0.9$.
- The gravitational mass (energy) radius of the pion: $\sqrt{\langle r^2 \rangle_{\text{mass}}} = 0.56 \sim 0.69\ \text{fm}$.
- The mechanical (pressure, shear force) radius of the pion: $\sqrt{\langle r^2 \rangle_{\text{mech}}} = 1.45 \sim 1.56\ \text{fm}$.
- The charge radius of the pion is given as $0.672 \pm 0.008\ \text{fm}$ for comparison.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework of generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs), which had been studied only theoretically until the Belle collaboration reported cross-section data in 2016 (Masuda et al., 2016). The authors build on their own prior analysis (Kumano, Song, Teryaev, 2017) to extract GDAs and then gravitational form factors. The results enable a new field of "gravitational physics from the microscopic quark and gluon level," allowing future studies of hadron structure via 3D tomography at facilities such as LHC, RHIC, COMPASS, JLab, and the upgraded super-KEKB.
