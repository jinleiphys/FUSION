# T-violating effect in $ν_τ (\barν_τ)-$nucleon quasielastic scattering

> The production cross sections and polarization observables of the $τ$ leptons produced in the $|ΔS| = 0$ and $1$ induced $ν_τ(\barν_τ)-N$ quasielastic scattering have been studied. The effect of T violation, in the case of $ΔS=0$ and 1 processes, and the SU(3) symmetry breaking effects, in the case of $ΔS=1$ processes, on the total scattering cross sections as well polarization observables are explored. Experimentally, it would be possible to observe these effects in the forthcoming (anti)neutrino experiments like DUNE, SHiP and DsTau.

## Key claim

The paper claims that T-violating effects (via an imaginary component of the second-class axial form factor \(g_2(0)\)) produce a non-zero transverse polarization component \(P_T^\tau\) in \(\nu_\tau(\bar{\nu}_\tau)\)-nucleon quasielastic scattering, but only when the T-violating parameter \(g_2^I(0)\) is taken to be \(\ge 1\); the effect is negligible for smaller values.

## Method

The authors calculated the differential and total cross sections and the longitudinal, perpendicular, and transverse polarization components of the \(\tau\) lepton produced in quasielastic \(\nu_\tau(\bar{\nu}_\tau)\)-nucleon scattering for both \(\Delta S=0\) (nucleon production) and \(\Delta S=1\) (hyperon production) processes. They incorporated T violation by introducing an imaginary part of the second-class axial form factor \(g_2(Q^2)\) and included SU(3) symmetry breaking effects using the models of Faessler et al. (2008) and Schlumpf (1994) for the vector and axial-vector form factors.

## Key numbers

- For the process \(\nu_\tau + n \rightarrow \tau^- + p\), the total cross section and polarization observables \(P_P^\tau\) and \(P_T^\tau\) are sensitive to variations of \(g_2^I(0)\) in the range \(0\) to \(2\).
- The transverse polarization component \(P_T^\tau\) becomes non-zero when the T-violating parameter \(g_2^I(0) > 0\).
- For the \(\bar{\nu}_\tau + p \rightarrow \tau^+ + \Lambda\) process, the SU(3) symmetry breaking parameters from Schlumpf are \(\alpha = 0.976\) and \(\beta = 1.072\) for the \(p \rightarrow \Lambda\) transition.
- For the \(\bar{\nu}_\tau + n \rightarrow \tau^+ + \Sigma^-\) process, the SU(3) symmetry breaking parameters from Schlumpf are \(\alpha = 0.975\) and \(\beta = 1.056\) for the \(n \rightarrow \Sigma^-\) transition.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier studies of G and T violation in quasielastic (anti)neutrino-nucleon scattering (Fatima et al., 2018; Fatima et al., 2020; Fatima et al., 2021) by focusing specifically on \(\tau\) lepton production and including SU(3) symmetry breaking effects. The results are relevant for upcoming experiments such as DUNE, SHiP, and DsTau, where the non-zero measurement of \(P_T^\tau\) could independently determine the degree of time-reversal violation in weak interactions.
