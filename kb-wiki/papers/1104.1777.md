# Geometrical scaling in hadronic collisions

> We show that the p_T spectra measured in pp collisions at the LHC exhibit geometrical scaling introduced earlier in the context of deep inelastic scattering. We also argue that the onset of geometrical scaling can be seen in nucleus-nucleus collisions at lower RHIC energies.

# Geometrical scaling in hadronic collisions

## Key claim

The transverse momentum spectra of charged particles measured in pp collisions at the LHC exhibit geometrical scaling, a phenomenon previously observed only in deep inelastic scattering, and the onset of this scaling can also be seen in nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC energies, though the scaling is approximate and the exponent λ may have a residual dependence on pT.

## Method

The author applies the geometrical scaling framework from the Golec-Biernat–Wüsthoff (GBW) model of deep inelastic scattering to hadronic collisions, using a kT-factorized cross-section formula with an Ansatz for the unintegrated gluon distribution from Kharzeev and Levin. The scaling variable τ = pT²/Qs² is defined with a saturation scale Qs² = Q0² (pT/W)^{-λ}, and the quality of scaling is tested by plotting ratios of CMS pT spectra at different LHC energies (0.9, 2.36, and 7 TeV) as functions of τ and checking whether they collapse to unity. The analysis is extended to PHOBOS data for Au-Au and Cu-Cu collisions at RHIC energies of 62.4 and 200 GeV per nucleon, using a modified scaling variable τA that includes an A^{1/3} factor for nuclear collisions.

## Key numbers

- The charged particle multiplicity at central rapidity in pp collisions rises as dNch/dη ∼ s^{λ̃} with λ̃ ≃ 0.23.
- For λ = 0.27, the effective power of multiplicity growth is λ̃ = 2λ/(2+λ) = 0.238.
- The effective saturation scale exponent extracted from HERA data is parameterized as λeff(Q) = 0.13 + 0.1 (Q²/10)^{0.35}.
- Ratios of CMS pT spectra at 7 TeV to those at 0.9 and 2.36 TeV range from 1.5 to 7 when plotted as functions of pT, but collapse to approximately unity when plotted as functions of √τ with λ = 0.27.
- For heavy ion collisions, a slightly higher exponent λ = 0.3 is used to show approximate scaling of Au-Au and Cu-Cu data at 62.4 and 200 GeV per nucleon.

## Context

This work extends the concept of geometrical scaling, originally introduced by Stasto, Golec-Biernat, and Kharzeev (2000) for deep inelastic scattering at HERA, to hadronic collisions at the LHC. It builds on the saturation model of Gribov, Levin, and Ryskin (1981) and the dipole model of Golec-Biernat and Wüsthoff (1998), and follows earlier work by McLerran (2010) and Praszalowicz (2011) showing that CMS data scale with τ. The finding that the quality of scaling improves when using a pT-dependent exponent λeff(2pT) from DIS supports the picture that medium-pT particles are produced from saturated gluonic matter. The analysis of RHIC heavy ion data shows an onset of scaling, enabling future studies of whether hydrodynamical evolution of the quark-gluon plasma washes out the geometrical scaling present in the initial state.
