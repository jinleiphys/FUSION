# Gamow Shell Model description of Li isotopes and their mirror partners

> Background: Weakly bound and unbound nuclei close to particle drip lines are laboratories of new nuclear structure physics at the extremes of neutron/proton excess. The comprehensive description of these systems requires an open quantum system framework that is capable of treating resonant and nonresonant many-body states on equal footing. Purpose: In this work, we construct the minimal complex-energy configuration interaction approach to describe binding energies and spectra of selected 5 $\leq$ A $\leq$ 11 nuclei. Method: We employ the complex-energy Gamow shell model (GSM) assuming a rigid $^4$He core. The effective Hamiltonian, consisting of a core-nucleon Woods-Saxon potential and a simplified version of the Furutani-Horiuchi-Tamagaki interaction with the mass-dependent scaling, is optimized in the sp space. To diagonalize the Hamiltonian matrix, we employ the Davidson method and the Density Matrix Renormalization Group technique. Results: Our optimized GSM Hamiltonian offers a good reproduction of binding energies and spectra with the root-mean-square (rms) deviation from experiment of 160 keV. Since the model performs well when used to predict known excitations that have not been included in the fit, it can serve as a reliable tool to describe poorly known states. A case in point is our prediction for the pair of unbound mirror nuclei $^{10}$Li-$^{10}$N in which a huge Thomas-Ehrman shift dramatically alters the pattern of low-energy excitations. Conclusion: The new model will enable comprehensive studies of structure and reactions aspects of light drip-line nuclei.

## Key claim

The optimized Gamow Shell Model Hamiltonian, with only four core-nucleon potential strengths and four two-body interaction parameters, reproduces binding energies and spectra of lithium isotopes and their mirror partners with a root-mean-square deviation of 160 keV from experiment, but the model space is restricted to the $sp$ shell and the interaction includes a mass-dependent scaling factor $(6/A)^{1/3}$ to effectively account for missing three-body forces.

## Method

The authors employed the complex-energy Gamow Shell Model (GSM) assuming a rigid $^4$He core, with valence nucleons coupled to the core. The effective Hamiltonian consisted of a core-nucleon Woods-Saxon potential and a simplified version of the Furutani-Horiuchi-Tamagaki interaction with mass-dependent scaling, optimized simultaneously in the $sp$ model space. The Hamiltonian matrix was diagonalized using the Davidson method and the Density Matrix Renormalization Group technique, with natural orbitals used to truncate the single-particle basis.

## Key numbers

- Root-mean-square deviation from experiment for binding energies and spectra: 160 keV
- Ground-state energy of $^5$He: 0.74 MeV (GSM) versus 0.798 MeV (experiment); width: 640 keV (GSM) versus 648 keV (experiment)
- Ground-state energy of $^5$Li: 1.6 MeV (GSM) versus 1.69 MeV (experiment); width: 1300 keV (GSM) versus 1230 keV (experiment)
- Predicted $^{10}$Li ground state (2$^+$) at 0.35 MeV above the $n+^9$Li threshold; first excited state (1$^+$) at 0.68 MeV
- Predicted $^{10}$N ground state (1$^-$) at $-$8.93 MeV with width 0.9 MeV, lying 1.92 MeV above the one-proton threshold
- Central term $V_c^{00}$ has the largest uncertainty at approximately 12%

## Context

This work extends previous GSM studies by Yannen (2017) and Fossez (2018), which described nuclei with $A \leq 9$ and helium isotopes respectively, to a broader range of light nuclei ($5 \leq A \leq 11$) including lithium isotopes and their mirror partners. The simplified interaction, informed by Effective Field Theory arguments and prior statistical analysis, enables predictions for poorly known unbound mirror nuclei such as $^{10}$Li and $^{10}$N, where a large Thomas-Ehrman shift dramatically alters the low-energy excitation pattern. The model will enable comprehensive studies of structure and reactions of light drip-line nuclei.
