# From the lightest nuclei to the equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter with realistic nuclear interactions

> We present microscopic calculations of light and medium mass nuclei and the equation of state of symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter using different nucleon-nucleon forces, including a new Argonne version that has the same spin/isospin structure as local chiral forces at next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO). The calculations are performed using Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) combined with an improved variational wave function. We show that the AFDMC method can now be used to successfully calculate the energies of very light to medium mass nuclei as well as the energy of isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter, demonstrating microscopically the quadratic dependence of the energy on the symmetry energy.

## Key claim

The Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) method, when combined with an improved variational wave function that includes tensor correlations and a better sampling technique, can now achieve accuracy comparable to Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) for light nuclei and can be extended to medium-mass nuclei and asymmetric nuclear matter, demonstrating the quadratic dependence of the energy on isospin asymmetry at saturation density.

## Method

The authors performed AFDMC simulations using the Argonne AV6' and a new AV7' nucleon-nucleon interaction, the latter including a spin-orbit term and having the same spin/isospin structure as local chiral forces at next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO). They implemented an improved trial wave function with tensor correlations and a propagation technique that removes time-step errors associated with gradients of the trial wave function. For nuclear matter, they simulated 28 nucleons in a periodic box and applied finite-size corrections for the interaction truncation and kinetic energy.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of 4He with AV6': -27.09(3) MeV (AFDMC) compared to -26.85(2) MeV (GFMC), a difference less than 0.125 MeV per nucleon.
- Binding energy of 4He with AV7': -25.7(2) MeV (AFDMC) compared to -26.2(1) MeV (GFMC).
- Binding energy of 4He with N2LO (R0=1.0 fm): -24.41(3) MeV (AFDMC) compared to -24.56(1) MeV (GFMC).
- Binding energy of 4He with N2LO (R0=1.2 fm): -25.77(2) MeV (AFDMC) compared to -25.75(1) MeV (GFMC).
- Binding energy of 6Li with AV6': -28.9(2) MeV (AFDMC) compared to -29.57(4) MeV (GFMC).
- Binding energy of 16O with AV6': -115.6(3) MeV; with AV7': -90.6(4) MeV (experimental: -127.619 MeV).
- Binding energy of 40Ca with AV6': -322(2) MeV; with AV7': -209(1) MeV (experimental: -342.051 MeV).
- Energy per nucleon of symmetric nuclear matter at density 0.16 fm^-3 with AV6': -14.17(2) MeV for 28 nucleons; -14.16(2) MeV for 76 nucleons; -13.91(2) MeV for 108 nucleons; -12.98(4) MeV for 132 nucleons.

## Context

This work extends previous AFDMC calculations by Gandolfi (2007) that were limited by poor variational wave functions and time-step errors, and it provides the first quantum Monte Carlo calculation of asymmetric nuclear matter using bare nucleon-nucleon interactions. The results enable systematic studies of medium-mass nuclei, neutron-rich nuclei, and nuclear matter using both Argonne and chiral forces, and they confirm microscopically the quadratic dependence of the energy on isospin asymmetry that had previously been assumed in variational Fermi Hypernetted Chain/Single Operator Chain calculations by Lagaris (1981), Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory by Zuo (1999), and perturbative approaches by Drischler (2014).
