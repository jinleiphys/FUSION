# Probing the Nuclear Symmetry Energy with Heavy Ion Collisions

> Heavy Ion Collisions (HIC) represent a unique tool to probe the in-medium nuclear interaction in regions away from saturation. In this report we present a selection of new reaction observables in dissipative collisions particularly sensitive to the symmetry term of the nuclear Equation of State ($Iso-EoS$). We will first discuss the Isospin Equilibration Dynamics. At low energies this manifests via the recently observed Dynamical Dipole Radiation, due to a collective neutron-proton oscillation with the symmetry term acting as a restoring force. At higher beam energies Iso-EoS effects will be seen in an Isospin Diffusion mechanism, via Imbalance Ratio Measurements, in particular from correlations to the total kinetic energy loss. For fragmentation reactions in central events we suggest to look at the coupling between isospin distillation and radial flow. In Neck Fragmentation reactions important Iso-EoS information can be obtained from fragment isospin content, velocity and alignement correlations. The high density symmetry term can be probed from isospin effects on heavy ion reactions at relativistic energies (few AGeV range), in particular for high transverse momentum selections of the reaction products. Rather isospin sensitive observables are proposed from nucleon/cluster emissions, collective flows and meson production. The possibility to shed light on the controversial neutron/proton effective mass splitting in asymmetric matter is also suggested. A large symmetry repulsion at high baryon density will also lead to an "earlier" hadron-deconfinement transition in n-rich matter. The binodal transition line of the (T,ρ_B) diagram is lowered to a region accessible through heavy ion collisions in the energy range of the new planned facilities, e.g. the FAIR/NICA projects. Some observable effects of the formation of a Mixed Phase are suggested, in particular a Neutron Trapping mechanism. The dependence of the results on a suitable treatment of the isovector part of the interaction in effective QCD Lagrangian approaches is critically discussed. We stress the interest of this study in nuclear astrophysics, in particular for supernovae explosions and neutron star structure, where the knowledge of the Iso-EoS is important at low as well as at high baryon density.

## Key claim

Heavy Ion Collisions can probe the symmetry term of the nuclear Equation of State (Iso-EoS) across a wide density range, but the sensitivity of each proposed observable depends critically on the specific density regime probed (sub-saturation vs. supra-saturation) and on the choice of the symmetry energy parametrization (Asy-soft, Asy-stiff, or Asy-superstiff), with the additional complication that momentum-dependent interactions and the controversial neutron/proton effective mass splitting can produce effects comparable to or larger than those from the stiffness of the symmetry term alone.

## Method

The authors employ the Stochastic Mean Field (SMF) transport model, a Boltzmann-Langevin approach with fluctuating terms, to simulate heavy ion collisions from Coulomb barrier to relativistic energies. For low to Fermi energies, non-relativistic mean field dynamics with Skyrme-like effective interactions are used, while relativistic collisions are described via a fully covariant transport approach based on an effective field exchange model. The symmetry energy is tested using three different density parametrizations (Asy-soft, Asy-stiff, Asy-superstiff) that cross at normal density at the empirical symmetry energy coefficient of 31.7 MeV, and momentum-dependent interactions are implemented to study the neutron/proton effective mass splitting.

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy saturation value: 31.7 MeV, given by the Bethe-Weizsacker formula coefficient
- Compressibility modulus of the isoscalar EOS: K = 220 MeV, adopted as a soft isoscalar EOS
- Initial dipole moment for the exotic 132Sn+58Ni system at 10 AMeV: approximately 50 fm, about two times the largest values probed so far
- Beam energies studied: from just above the Coulomb barrier up to the AGeV range, with Fermi energy domain at 30-100 AMeV
- For Sn+Sn reactions at 35 AMeV and 50 AMeV, impact parameters studied: b = 6, 8, and 10 fm
- Asymmetry parameter beta = 0.2 used for 197Au and 124Sn systems

## Context

This review extends the work of Baran et al. (2005) on isospin dynamics in heavy ion collisions and the broader effort to constrain the nuclear symmetry energy through laboratory experiments, complementing astrophysical constraints from neutron star structure and supernova dynamics (Horowitz and Piekarewicz, 2002; Steiner et al., 2005). The paper synthesizes results from multiple theoretical approaches including the SMF model (Guarnera et al., 1996; Colonna et al., 1998) and covariant transport (Ferini et al., 2005, 2006), and proposes specific observables for the new FAIR/NICA facilities and the SPIRAL2 radioactive beam facility. The work enables future experimental campaigns to distinguish between different symmetry energy parametrizations and to resolve the neutron/proton effective mass splitting controversy, with implications for understanding neutron star crust properties and the hadron-deconfinement transition in neutron-rich matter.
