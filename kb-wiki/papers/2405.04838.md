# Amplitude analysis of $ B^0 \to K^0_S K^+ K^-$ decays in a quasi-two-body QCD factorization approach

> The $B^0 \to K^0_S K^+ K^- $ decay amplitude is derived within a quasi-two-body QCD factorization framework in terms of kaon form factors and $B^0$ to two-kaon-transition functions. The final state kaon-kaon interactions in the $S$, $P$, and $D$ waves are taken into account. The unitarity constraints are satisfied for the two kaons in scalar states. It is shown that with few terms of the full decay amplitude one may reach a fair agreement with the total branching fraction and Dalitz-plot projections published in 2010 by the Belle Collaboration and in 2012 by the $\textit{BABAR}$ Collaboration. With 13 free parameters, our model fits the corresponding 422 data with a $χ^2$ of 583.6 which leads to a $χ^2$ per degree of freedom equal to 1.43. The dominant branching fraction arises from the $f_0(K^+K^-) K^0_S$ mode with 83.0$\%$ of the total branching. The next important mode is dominated by $ϕK^0_S$ plus small $ωK^0_S$ and $ρ^0 K^0_S$ modes with 18.3$\%$ of the total. Then follows the $a_0^\pm K^\mp$ mode with 6.2$\%$. Adding the other smaller modes, the total percentage sum is 107.7$\%$ which indicates a small interference contribution. In most regions of the Dalitz plot, our model gives rather small $CP$ asymmetry, but in some parts its values can be large and positive or negative. Its predicted total value is equal to -0.11$\%$. The calculated time dependent $\textit{CP}$-asymmetry parameters agree, within errors, with those obtained by the $\textit{BABAR}$ analysis. Our model amplitude can be the basis for a parametrization in experimental Dalitz plot analyses of LHCb and Belle II Collaborations.

## Key claim

The paper derives the $B^0 \to K^0_S K^+ K^-$ decay amplitude within a quasi-two-body QCD factorization framework, showing that with only 13 free parameters and neglecting OZI-suppressed terms, the model achieves a fair agreement with Belle (2010) and BABAR (2012) data, though the total percentage sum of fitted branching fractions reaches 107.7%, indicating small interference contributions not fully captured.

## Method

The decay amplitude was constructed from the effective weak Hamiltonian using quasi-two-body QCD factorization, expressing the amplitude in terms of kaon form factors and $B^0$ to two-kaon-transition functions. Final-state kaon-kaon interactions in $S$, $P$, and $D$ waves were included, with unitarity constraints satisfied for scalar two-kaon states. The dominant contributions came from terms ${\cal{H}}^{(p)}_1$, ${\cal{H}}^{(p)}_3$, ${\cal{H}}^{(p)}_5$, and ${\cal{H}}^{(p)}_6$, while OZI-suppressed terms (${\cal{H}}^{(p)}_2$, ${\cal{H}}^{(p)}_4$, ${\cal{H}}^{(p)}_7$, ${\cal{H}}^{(p)}_{8,9}$) were neglected; the strange scalar-isoscalar kaon form factor $\Gamma_2^{s}(s_0)$ was calculated using dispersion relations with a coupled-channel model, and the scalar-isovector transition function $G_1(s)$ was obtained from a unitary two-channel model.

## Key numbers

- Total fit: 13 free parameters, 422 data points, $\chi^2 = 583.6$, $\chi^2$ per degree of freedom = 1.43
- Dominant branching fraction: $f_0(K^+K^-) K^0_S$ mode contributes 83.0% of the total branching
- Next mode: $\phi K^0_S$ plus small $\omega K^0_S$ and $\rho^0 K^0_S$ modes contribute 18.3% of the total
- $a_0^\pm K^\mp$ mode contributes 6.2% of the total
- Sum of all fitted branching percentages: 107.7%, indicating small interference
- Predicted total $CP$ asymmetry: -0.11%
- Fermi coupling constant $G_F = 1.166379 \times 10^{-5}$ GeV$^{-2}$
- $\bar B^0$ to $\bar K^0$ transition form factor parameters: $r_0 = 0.33$, $s_t = 37.46$ GeV$^2$
- Charged kaon decay constant $f_K = 0.1561$ GeV
- $\rho$ meson decay constant $f_\rho = 0.209$ GeV
- $F_0^{\bar{B^0} a_0^+}(m_K^2) = 0.18$
- $A_0^{B^0 \rho^+}(m_K^2) = 0.52$
- $F^{\bar{B^0} a_2^+}(m_K^2, m_{a_2}^2) = 0.14$

## Context

This work extends the quasi-two-body QCD factorization approach previously applied to $B^\pm \to K^+ K^- K^\pm$ decays (PLB699_102, 2011) and to $B^\pm \to \pi^+ \pi^- \pi^\pm$ decays (DedonderPol) by incorporating $S$, $P$, and $D$ wave final-state interactions for the $B^0 \to K^0_S K^+ K^-$ channel. It improves upon the isobar model used by Belle (PRD82_073011, 2010) and BABAR (PRD85_112010, 2012) by satisfying unitarity for scalar states and including resonances (such as $a_0$ and $\omega$) omitted in those experimental analyses. The model amplitude is intended as a basis for parametrization in future Dalitz plot analyses by LHCb and Belle II Collaborations.
