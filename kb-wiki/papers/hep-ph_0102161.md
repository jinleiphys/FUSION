# Effective Field Theory Dimensional Regularization

> A Lorentz-covariant regularization scheme for effective field theories with an arbitrary number of propagating heavy and light particles is given. This regularization scheme leaves the low-energy analytic structure of Greens functions intact and preserves all the symmetries of the underlying Lagrangian. The power divergences of regularized loop integrals are controlled by the low-energy kinematic variables. Simple diagrammatic rules are derived for the regularization of arbitrary one-loop graphs and the generalization to higher loops is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a Lorentz-covariant regularization scheme for effective field theories that works for an arbitrary number of propagating heavy and light particles, not just the single-heavy-particle case previously treated by Becher and Leutwyler (1999). The scheme preserves low-energy analytic structure and all symmetries, but the key condition is that power divergences are controlled by low-energy kinematic variables rather than by the heavy mass scale.

## Method

The authors extend the infrared regularization idea of Becher and Leutwyler (1999) to arbitrary one-loop graphs with multiple heavy and light particles by separating Feynman parameter integration domains into "infrared" and "regular" regions. They derive diagrammatic rules for identifying which subgraphs produce power-counting-violating terms proportional to fractional powers of heavy masses, and show that these terms can be absorbed into low-energy constants. The generalization to higher loops is discussed through the transitivity property of the cut structure.

## Key numbers

- The regularization scheme works for heavy particle masses M greater than or approximately equal to the EFT cutoff Lambda_EFT (M ≳ Lambda_EFT), with light particle masses m much smaller than Lambda_EFT.
- For the nucleon self-energy graph, the infrared regularization separates out terms of the form M^(d-4)/(d-4), where d is the spacetime dimension.
- The chiral symmetry breaking scale Lambda_chi is approximately 1 GeV, cited as the relevant scale for chiral perturbation theory.

## Context

This work extends the infrared regularization method of Becher and Leutwyler (1999) and Ellis and Tang (1998), which was limited to one-loop graphs with a single heavy particle. It also builds on the two-heavy-particle regularization developed by Gegelia, Leupold, and Scherer (2000). The scheme enables systematic power counting in effective field theories with multiple heavy particles, such as nuclear forces or systems with charm and bottom quarks, without resorting to 1/M expansions that fail near anomalous thresholds as shown by Meissner (1998) for the nucleon scalar form factor.
