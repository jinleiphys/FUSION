# Nuclear responses with neural-network quantum states

> We introduce a variational Monte Carlo framework that combines neural-network quantum states with the Lorentz integral transform technique to compute the dynamical properties of self-bound quantum many-body systems in continuous Hilbert spaces. While broadly applicable to various quantum systems, including atoms and molecules, in this initial application we focus on the photoabsorption cross section of light nuclei, where benchmarks against numerically exact techniques are available. Our accurate theoretical predictions are complemented by robust uncertainty quantification, enabling meaningful comparisons with experiments. We demonstrate that a simple nuclear Hamiltonian, based on a leading-order pionless effective field theory expansion and known to accurately reproduce the ground-state energies of nuclei with $A\leq 20$ nucleons also provides a reliable description of the photoabsorption cross section.

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that a simple nuclear Hamiltonian based on a leading-order pionless effective field theory expansion, which is known to accurately reproduce ground-state energies of nuclei with A ≤ 20 nucleons, also provides a reliable description of the photoabsorption cross section for light nuclei when combined with neural-network quantum states and the Lorentz integral transform technique.

## Method
The authors combine neural-network quantum states (NQS) with the Lorentz integral transform (LIT) technique to compute response functions of self-bound quantum many-body systems. They parameterize both the ground-state wave function and the LIT wave function using a neural Pfaffian architecture, then solve the inhomogeneous Schrodinger equation by maximizing the quantum fidelity between the state (H - z)|Ψ_L⟩ and the source term O|Ψ_0⟩ using stochastic reconfiguration. The LIT is computed as the norm of Ψ_L using a numerically stable expression involving the imaginary part of ⟨Ψ_L|Φ⟩, and the response function is recovered through two inversion methods: a regularized basis expansion and a maximum entropy method.

## Key numbers
- NQS results for the deuteron LIT agree with the exact solution to an accuracy better than 1% at Γ = 10 MeV
- For ⁴He, the maximum discrepancy between NQS and effective interaction hyperspherical harmonics (EIHH) results remains at the percent level, except in the high-energy region where the LIT is already small
- The theoretical error bound for ⁴He exceeds 20% for certain ω ≳ 75 MeV, indicating the need for refined error estimates
- The photoabsorption cross sections were obtained with Γ = 10 MeV, and using Γ = 5 MeV produces fully compatible results with the basis-expansion inversion curves overlapping

## Context
This work extends continuum quantum Monte Carlo methods, which have been limited to relatively light nuclei due to exponentially increasing computational cost and the fermion-sign problem (Carlson, 2014), to low-energy response functions that previously could only be computed using basis-expansion methods such as coupled-cluster theory (Sobczyk, 2021; Bonaiti, 2021) and the symmetry-adapted no-core shell model (Burrows, 2023). The approach enables computation of photodisintegration cross sections for nuclei with up to A = 20 nucleons, though the authors note that the model "o" Hamiltonian fails to reproduce charge radii for nuclei with A > 4, indicating the need to include p-wave components in the nucleon-nucleon interaction.
