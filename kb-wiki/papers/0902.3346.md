# Non-relativistic bound states: the long way back from the Bethe-Salpeter to the Schroedinger equation

> I review, in a personal perspective, the history of the theory of non-relativistic bound states in QED and QCD from the Bethe-Salpeter equation to the construction of effective field theories.

# Non-relativistic bound states: the long way back from the Bethe-Salpeter to the Schroedinger equation

## Key claim
The Bethe-Salpeter equation, while formally the correct relativistic field-theoretic description of bound states, is impractical for high-precision calculations because it entangles contributions from different energy scales (hard, soft, ultrasoft). The effective field theory approach (NRQED/NRQCD followed by pNRQED/pNRQCD) systematically separates these scales, recovering the Schroedinger equation as the equation of motion for the ultrasoft degrees of freedom, but now with a field-theoretically defined potential that undergoes renormalization and includes effects beyond the quantum-mechanical Schroedinger equation.

## Method
The paper reviews the historical development from the Bethe-Salpeter equation to effective field theories for non-relativistic bound states in QED and QCD. The key methodological steps are: (1) constructing NRQED/NRQCD by integrating out the mass scale m, organizing the Lagrangian as an expansion in 1/m; (2) further integrating out the soft scale mv to obtain pNRQED/pNRQCD, where the Lagrangian is organized as an expansion in 1/m and in the inter-particle distance r (multipole expansion); (3) matching calculations between the full theory and the EFTs at each stage to determine Wilson coefficients, performed in dimensional regularization.

## Key numbers
- The bound-state energy scale is mv^2, which for the Coulombic case gives E_n = -m alpha^2/(2n^2), implying v ~ alpha and 1/r ~ m alpha.
- For the hyperfine splitting of the positronium ground state, going from the m alpha^5 correction (Karplus 1952) to the m alpha^6 ln alpha term (Caswell 1978, Bodwin 1977) took twenty-five years.
- The leading logarithmic correction to the QCD static potential at order alpha_s^4 is delta V(r, mu') = -(3/r) (alpha_s(mu')/pi) alpha_s^3(1/r) ln(r mu').

## Context
This review extends the line of work from the Bethe-Salpeter equation (Bethe and Salpeter 1951) through early quarkonium potential derivations (Gupta 1981, Buchmuller 1981, Titard 1993) and the Vienna group's work on toponium (Kummer 1981, 1994, 1995, 1996). It contrasts with the earlier Bethe-Salpeter approach by showing how effective field theories (NRQCD from Caswell and Lepage 1986, pNRQCD from Pineda 1998 and Brambilla 1999) provide a systematic framework that has enabled calculations of alpha^6, alpha^7 ln^2 alpha, and alpha^7 ln alpha corrections to positronium hyperfine splitting after decades of stagnation. The approach enables factorization of short- and long-range contributions in quarkonium physics and has been extended to hadronic atoms, nucleon-nucleon systems, and finite-temperature bound states.
