# Nuclear Reactions of Astrophysical Interest Involving Light Nuclei

> An introduction to nucleosynthesis, the creation of the elements in the big bang, in interstellar matter and in stars is given. The two--step process $^4$He(2n,$γ$)$^6$He and the reverse photodisintegration $^6$He($γ$,2n)$^4$He involving the halo nucleus $^6$He could be of importance in the $α$--process in type--II supernovae. The reaction rates for the above processes are calculated using three--body methods and show an enhancement of more than three orders of magnitude compared to the previous adopted value. Direct--capture calculations give similar values for the above reaction rates. Therefore, this method was also used to calculate the reaction rates of the two--step processes $^6$He(2n,$γ$)$^8$He and $^9$Li(2n,$γ$)$^{11}$Li and the reverse photodisintegration of $^8$He and $^{11}$Li that could be also of importance in the $α$-process.

## Key claim

The reaction rates for the two-step process ⁴He(2n,γ)⁶He and its reverse photodisintegration are enhanced by more than three orders of magnitude compared to the previously adopted value, but this enhancement is conditional on the inclusion of a non-resonant E1 transition in the second step (⁵He(n,γ)⁶He) that was not accounted for in prior work.

## Method

The authors calculated the reaction rate for ⁴He(2n,γ)⁶He using a three-body model of the α+n+n system, solving for the ground-state wave function of ⁶He and the scattering wave function of ⁵He+n with folding-type potentials fitted to the ⁵He ground-state energy and width. They then applied a direct-capture (DC) model for the second step, using spectroscopic factors from shell-model calculations with the Cohen-Kurath interaction, and extended the DC method to compute reaction rates for ⁶He(2n,γ)⁸He and ⁹Li(2n,γ)¹¹Li and their reverse photodisintegrations.

## Key numbers

- The cross section for ⁵He(n,γ)⁶He follows a 1/v behavior and is parametrized as σ = σ₁ / (E₂[MeV])^(1/2) with σ₁ = 0.152 MeV^(1/2) μb in the three-body calculation and σ₁ = 0.270 MeV^(1/2) μb in the direct-capture calculation.
- The reaction rate for ⁴He(2n,γ)⁶He is at least more than three orders of magnitude larger than the previously adopted value from Fowler (1975).
- The reaction rate for ⁶He(2n,γ)⁸He at T₉ ≈ 1 is about a factor 40 larger than for ⁴He(2n,γ)⁶He.
- The reaction rate for ⁹Li(2n,γ)¹¹Li at T₉ ≈ 1 is almost a factor two enhanced compared to ⁴He(2n,γ)⁶He.
- The Q-value for ⁴He(2n,γ)⁶He is 0.98 MeV; for ⁶He(2n,γ)⁸He it is 2.14 MeV; for ⁹Li(2n,γ)¹¹Li it is 0.31 MeV.
- The spectroscopic factor for ⁵He(n,γ)⁶He is S_(ℓ=1,j=3/2) = 1.3139; for ⁷He(n,γ)⁸He it is 3.2047; for ¹⁰Li(n,γ)¹¹Li it is 0.647 (ℓ=1,j=3/2) and 0.024 (ℓ=1,j=1/2).

## Context

This work extends prior nucleosynthesis network calculations for the α-process in type-II supernovae and the Inhomogeneous Big Bang (IBB), where neutron densities reach 10^(20-30) cm^(-3). The authors note that their reaction rate for ⁴He(2n,γ)⁶He agrees with a separate direct-capture calculation by Goerres et al. (1995), but their rate for ⁶He(2n,γ)⁸He is more than a factor 10 larger than that of Goerres et al. (1995) due to a larger spectroscopic factor. The results enable improved modeling of nucleosynthesis in neutrino-heated bubbles of type-II supernovae, particularly for neutron-rich nuclei up to mass A ≈ 100.
