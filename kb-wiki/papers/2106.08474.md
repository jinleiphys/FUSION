# Transport coefficients of hyperonic neutron star cores

> We consider transport properties of the hypernuclear matter in neutron star cores. In particular, we calculate the thermal conductivity, the shear viscosity, and the momentum transfer rates for np$Σ^{-}Λeμ$ composition of dense matter in $β$--equilibrium for baryon number densities in the range $0.1-1$~fm$^{-3}$. The calculations are based on baryon interactions treated within the framework of the non-relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory. Bare nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions are described by the Argonne v18 phenomenological potential supplemented with the Urbana IX three-nucleon force. Nucleon-hyperon (NY) and hyperon-hyperon (YY) interactions are based on the \new{NSC97e and NSC97a models} of the Nijmegen group. We find that the baryon contribution to transport coefficients is dominated by the neutron one as in the case of neutron star cores containing only nucleons. In particular, we find that neutrons dominate the total thermal conductivity over the whole range of densities explored and that, due to the onset of $Σ^-$ which leads to the deleptonization of the neutron star core, they dominate also the shear viscosity in the high density region, in contrast with the pure nucleonic case where the lepton contribution is always the dominant one.

## Key claim

The baryon contribution to thermal conductivity and shear viscosity in hyperonic neutron star cores is dominated by neutrons across the entire density range studied (0.1-1 fm^-3), and the onset of Sigma^- hyperons leads to deleptonization of the core, causing neutrons to dominate shear viscosity at high densities -- in contrast to pure nucleonic cores where leptons always dominate the shear viscosity.

## Method

The authors employed non-relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory to compute the thermal conductivity, shear viscosity, and momentum transfer rates for dense matter with an np Sigma^- Lambda e mu composition in beta-equilibrium. Bare nucleon-nucleon interactions were described by the Argonne v18 potential supplemented with the Urbana IX three-nucleon force, while nucleon-hyperon and hyperon-hyperon interactions were based on the Nijmegen NSC97e and NSC97a models.

## Key numbers

- Baryon number density range explored: 0.1-1 fm^-3
- Matter composition: np Sigma^- Lambda e mu (protons, neutrons, Sigma^- hyperons, Lambda hyperons, electrons, muons)
- Thermal conductivity dominated by neutrons over the entire density range of 0.1-1 fm^-3
- Shear viscosity dominated by neutrons at high densities due to Sigma^- onset and deleptonization, in contrast to pure nucleonic case where lepton contribution is always dominant

## Context

This work extends previous studies of transport in pure nucleonic neutron star cores (which found lepton-dominated shear viscosity) to matter containing hyperons, using the same Brueckner-Hartree-Fock framework. The results enable more realistic modeling of neutron star cooling and merger dynamics in scenarios where hyperons are present at supranuclear densities. The calculations rely on the Nijmegen hyperon-nucleon potentials (NSC97e and NSC97a) and the Argonne v18 nucleon-nucleon potential, and the findings highlight the need for improved hyperon-nucleon interactions to reduce uncertainties in transport predictions.
