# Quasi equilibrium state of expanding quantum fields and two-pion Bose-Einstein correlations in $pp$ collisions at the LHC

> We argue that the two-particle momentum correlation functions of high-multiplicity $p+p$ collisions at the LHC provide a signal for a ground state structure of a quasi equilibrium state of the longitudinally boost-invariant expanding quantum field which lies in the future light cone of a collision. The physical picture is that pions are produced by the expanding quantum emitter with two different scales approximately attributed to the expanding ideal gas in local equilibrium state and ground-state condensate. Specifically, we show that the effect of suppressing the two-particle Bose-Einstein momentum correlation functions increases with increasing transverse momentum of a like-sign pion pair due to different momentum-dependence of the corresponding particle emission regions.

## Key claim

The two-pion Bose-Einstein correlation functions in high-multiplicity $p+p$ collisions at the LHC can be explained by a quasi equilibrium state of a longitudinally boost-invariant expanding quantum field, where pions are produced by an emitter with two different scales: an expanding ideal gas in local equilibrium and a ground-state condensate; the suppression of the correlation functions increases with transverse momentum of the like-sign pion pair due to different momentum-dependence of the corresponding particle emission regions.

## Method

The author defines a quasi equilibrium statistical operator on Minkowski spacetime using Zubarev's formalism, applied to a boost-invariant expanding free scalar quantum field in Bjorken coordinates. The Hamiltonian that generates translation in proper time is diagonalized via Bogolyubov transformations, revealing that the ground state is a two-mode squeezed state (condensate) of correlated particle pairs with zero total momentum. Using this diagonalized Hamiltonian, the author calculates the one-particle momentum spectra and two-particle Bose-Einstein correlation functions for identical charged pions, separating contributions from the local equilibrium ideal gas and the ground-state condensate.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out proper time: $\tau \gtrsim 1$ fm (when $\beta \simeq 1/m$ where $m$ is pion mass)
- The longitudinal radius for the local equilibrium component: $R_{l.eq.} = \frac{\tau}{\sqrt{\beta m_{T}}\cosh\theta}$
- The longitudinal scale of the ground-state condensate: $R_{cond} = \frac{2\tau}{\cosh\theta}$
- Intercept of the Bose-Einstein correlation function at zero relative momentum: $C(\textbf{p},\textbf{0}) = 2$
- The one-particle momentum spectrum for the condensate: $n_{cond}(p) = \frac{R_{T}^{2}}{(2\pi)^{3} \omega_{p}}\frac{\pi}{16m_{T}\tau}$
- The one-particle momentum spectrum for local equilibrium (for $\beta m_{T} \gg 1$): $n_{l.eq.}(p) = \frac{R_{T}^{2}}{(2\pi)^{3} \omega_{p}}\tau m_{T} \sqrt{\frac{2\pi}{\beta m_{T}}}\exp(-\beta m_{T})$

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Sinyukov (1990s) for boost-invariant expanding quantum fields, where previously the ground-state condensate contributions to particle momentum spectra were treated as physically meaningless and subtracted. The author argues instead for the physical significance of this condensate and its relevance to the observed suppression of two-pion Bose-Einstein correlation functions in high-multiplicity $p+p$ collisions at the LHC, as measured by ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS. The results contrast with hydrodynamic models of $A+A$ collisions (Shapoval, 2015) where the $\lambda$ parameter does not exhibit the same momentum-dependent suppression, and suggest that the quasi equilibrium ground state provides a mechanism for the observed correlation strength decrease with increasing pair momentum.
