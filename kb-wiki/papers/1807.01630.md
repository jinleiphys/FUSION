# Thermodynamic Geometry and Deconfinement Temperature

> The application of Riemannian geometry to the analysis of the equilibrium thermodynamics in Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) at finite temperature and baryon density gives a new method to evaluate the critical temperature, $T_c$, of the deconfinement transition. In the confined phase, described by the thermodynamic geometry of the Hadron Resonance Gas, the estimate of $T_c$ turns out completely consistent with lattice QCD simulations of the quark-gluon plasma phase if the hadron excluded volume and the interaction effects are taken into account.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the deconfinement critical temperature \(T_c\) estimated from the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model using the thermodynamic geometry criterion \(R=0\) becomes completely consistent with lattice QCD results only when hadron excluded volume and interaction effects are included; the ideal point-like HRG model gives a higher \(T_c\) than lattice data.

## Method

The authors compute the scalar curvature \(R\) of the thermodynamic metric (derived from the Hessian of the entropy density) for the confined phase using three HRG models: an ideal point-like gas, a van der Waals (VdW) HRG with repulsive and attractive interactions, and a Carnahan-Starling (CS) HRG with Clausius-type attraction. The deconfinement temperature is determined by the condition \(R=0\) as a function of baryon chemical potential, using a series expansion of the thermodynamic potential \(\phi = P/T\) in powers of \(\gamma^2 = (-\mu_B/T)^2\).

## Key numbers

- For the ideal HRG model, the \(R=0\) criterion yields a deconfinement temperature that is larger than the lattice QCD result (the discrepancy is described qualitatively; no single numerical value for the ideal HRG \(T_c\) is given in the text).
- The VdW-HRG model uses parameters \(a = 329\ \text{MeV}\,\text{fm}^3\) and \(b = 3.42\ \text{fm}^3\) for baryon-baryon interactions (Table 1).
- The CS-Clausius-HRG model uses parameters \(a = 423\ \text{MeV}\,\text{fm}^3\) and \(b = 2.80\ \text{fm}^3\) for baryon-baryon interactions (Table 1).
- The \(R=0\) calculation from lattice QCD data agrees with lattice QCD results on the critical temperature within \(10\%\) (stated for the quark-gluon plasma phase).

## Context

This work extends the earlier application of thermodynamic geometry to QCD by Castorina et al. (2018) (cited as [CIL]), which used only point-like hadrons and found a discrepancy with lattice QCD. By incorporating excluded volume and interaction effects via VdW and CS-Clausius HRG models, the authors show that the \(R=0\) criterion now matches lattice QCD results from Bazavov et al. (2017) and the freeze-out temperatures from ALICE and STAR collaborations. The approach enables future calculations at large baryon density once the thermodynamic potential is known.
