# Neutron Skin from Conserved Charge Measurements at Collider Experiments

> We propose a novel method for measuring the neutron skin of heavy nuclei using collider experiments. Specifically, we demonstrate that the neutron skin thickness of the lead nucleus can be extracted in $p$+$^{208}$Pb collisions by analyzing a double ratio: The ratio of net electric charge to net baryon number measured near the lead-going rapidity, taken for high-multiplicity events and divided by the same ratio for low-multiplicity events. We compute the expected sensitivity of the double ratio to the neutron skin within a comprehensive (3+1)D relativistic hydrodynamic framework that incorporates multiple conserved charge currents and a charge-dependent lattice-QCD-based equation of state. We provide predictions for both $p$+$^{208}$Pb collisions at ${\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=72}$~GeV and $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=5.02$~TeV, corresponding to the center of mass energies realized in the SMOG2 fixed-target setup at LHCb and the LHC collider mode, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the neutron skin thickness of lead can be extracted in p+208Pb collisions by measuring a double ratio: the ratio of net electric charge to net baryon number near the lead-going rapidity in high-multiplicity events divided by the same ratio in low-multiplicity events. The method relies on the correlation between event activity and impact parameter, and the observable shows an approximately linear dependence on neutron skin thickness, though systematic uncertainties from particlization and baryon stopping effects can complicate extraction in certain rapidity ranges.

## Method

The authors performed (3+1)D event-by-event relativistic hydrodynamic simulations of p+208Pb collisions using the iEBE-MUSIC framework with initial conditions from the 3D-Glauber model, which incorporates baryon junction dynamics for baryon stopping. They varied the neutron skin thickness by adjusting the neutron diffuseness parameter in an isospin-dependent Woods-Saxon parametrization while keeping the proton distribution fixed, then evolved the system using second-order viscous hydrodynamics with a charge-dependent lattice-QCD-based equation of state (NEOS-4D) and final-state hadronic transport via UrQMD.

## Key numbers

- Neutron skin thickness from PREX-II: ΔR_np[208Pb] = 0.283 ± 0.071 fm
- Proton half-density radius: R_p^WS = 6.68 fm; neutron half-density radius: R_n^WS = 6.69 fm
- Proton diffuseness: a_p = 0.448 fm (held constant)
- Hydrodynamic initialization time: τ_hydro = 0.5 fm/c
- Switching energy density for hadronization: ε_sw = 0.2 GeV/fm^3
- Collision energies studied: √s_NN = 5.02 TeV (LHC collider mode) and √s_NN = 72 GeV (LHCb SMOG2 fixed-target mode)
- In the experimentally accessible forward rapidity bin (4.5 < y < 5.5), the double ratio deviates from unity by approximately 20% for fixed-target mode and approximately 4% for collider mode

## Context

This work extends the approach of Giacalone (2023) who used Bayesian analysis of relativistic nuclear collision data to constrain neutron skin, and contrasts with parity-violating electron scattering measurements from PREX-II (2021) which yielded a relatively large neutron skin thickness. The proposed method offers a new experimental avenue at LHC energies that could help resolve the tension between ab-initio determinations and the PREX-II result, and can be applied to other nuclei such as 48Ca in high-energy p+A collisions.
