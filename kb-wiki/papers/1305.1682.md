# Grassmann integral and Balian-Brézin decomposition in Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov matrix elements

> We present a new formula to calculate matrix elements of a general unitary operator with respect to Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov states allowing multiple quasi-particle excitations. The Balian-Brézin decomposition of the unitary operator (Il Nuovo Cimento B 64, 37 (1969)) is employed in the derivation. We found that this decomposition is extremely suitable for an application of Fermion coherent state and Grassmann integrals in the quasi-particle basis. The resultant formula is compactly expressed in terms of the Pfaffian, and shows the similar bipartite structure to the formula that we have previously derived in the bare-particles basis (Phys. Lett. B 707, 305 (2012)).

## Key claim

The paper presents a new compact Pfaffian formula for matrix elements of a general unitary operator between Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) states with multiple quasi-particle excitations, which overcomes the combinatorial complexity of the earlier Hara-Iwasaki formula (1979) that becomes impractical for more than four quasi-particle states.

## Method

The authors employ the Balian-Brézin decomposition (1969) to express the unitary operator as a product of three exponential operators in the quasi-particle basis. They then insert Fermion coherent state closure relations and evaluate the resulting Grassmann integrals, systematically integrating over Grassmann variables to reduce the matrix element to a Pfaffian of a skew-symmetric matrix constructed from the contraction matrices A, B, and C that appear in the Balian-Brézin decomposition.

## Key numbers

- The matrix element involves quasi-particle excitations with indices n and n', where the conventional Hara-Iwasaki formula contains (n+n'-1)!! terms, making it difficult for more than four quasi-particle HFB states.
- The dimension of the single-particle model space M is taken to be an even integer.
- The final Pfaffian formula yields a matrix M of dimension (n+n') x (n+n'), extracted from a 2M x 2M skew-symmetric matrix.
- The sign factor in the derivation includes terms such as (-)^(n+n')/2 and (-)^n, where n and n' are the numbers of quasi-particle creation and annihilation operators.

## Context

This work extends the Pfaffian approach pioneered by Robledo (2009) for HFB overlaps and matrix elements, and directly addresses the combinatorial complexity problem identified in the Hara-Iwasaki formula (1979) used in the Projected Shell Model (PSM). The new formula shows a bipartite structure similar to the authors' previous work in the bare-particle basis (Mizusaki et al., 2012), but now incorporates the unitary operator via the Balian-Brézin decomposition, enabling practical beyond-mean-field calculations such as angular momentum projection with multiple quasi-particle HFB states.
