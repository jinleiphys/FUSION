# Quantitative complementarity in two-path interferometry

> The quantitative formulation of Bohr's complementarity proposed by Greenberger and Yasin is applied to some physical situations for which analytical expressions are available. This includes a variety of conventional double-slit experiments, but also particle oscillations, as in the case of the neutral-kaon system, and Mott scattering of identical nuclei. For all these cases, a unified description can be achieved including a new parameter, $ν$, which quantifies the effective number of fringes one can observe in each specific interferometric set-up.

# Quantitative complementarity in two-path interferometry

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a unified quantitative description of Bohr's complementarity, based on the Greenberger-Yasin predictability–visibility relation, can be applied to double-slit experiments, neutral-kaon oscillations, and Mott scattering of identical nuclei, provided the analytical expressions for the interference pattern are available. The unification relies on the assumption that the state entering the interferometer is a pure state (or a specific mixed state for unpolarized spin-S nuclei), and the authors introduce a new parameter ν that estimates the effective number of observable fringes before visibility drops by a factor of e.

## Method

The authors apply the Greenberger-Yasin complementarity relation P²(y) + V₀²(y) ≤ 1, where P(y) is the path predictability and V₀(y) is the fringe visibility, to three distinct physical systems for which analytical expressions exist. For double-slit experiments with Gaussian transmission filters, they derive the intensity pattern I(y) and extract A and B constants from the visibility and phase. For neutral-kaon oscillations, they treat the K_S and K_L propagating components as the two interferometric paths and compute the time-dependent visibility and predictability from the kaon decay widths and mass difference. For Mott scattering of identical nuclei, they transform the scattering angle variable to obtain the same functional form and incorporate spin-dependent factors C_S for unpolarized beams.

## Key numbers

- For the double-slit example with parameters k = 10⁷ m⁻¹, x₀ = 10⁻⁴ m, d = 3·10⁻³ m, l = 0.1 m, and f = 0.11 m, the ratio R = |A/B| ≃ 0.10 and the effective number of fringes ν ≃ 2.6.
- For the neutral-kaon system, the ratio R = |ΔΓ|/(2Δm) ≃ 1.05, where ΔΓ = Γ_L – Γ_S and Δm = m_L – m_S.
- For Mott scattering, the parameter R = 1/η, where η = Z²α √(Mc²/(2E)) is the Sommerfeld parameter; for α–α scattering at E = 75, 150, and 200 keV, and for C¹² + C¹² at E = 3 and 5 MeV and O¹⁶ + O¹⁶ at E = 7, 8.8, and 10 MeV, the data are fitted by the theoretical curves with S = 0.
- For spin-1/2 nuclei (C¹³ + C¹³ at E = 75 keV), the visibility is V₀(x) = 1/(2 cosh x) with K = 1/2.
- The effective number of fringes is defined as ν = 0.264 |B/A| = 0.264/R.

## Context

This work extends the quantitative complementarity framework of Greenberger and Yasin (1988) and the interferometric duality formulation of Englert (1996) to three physically distinct systems, showing they all obey the same functional form for visibility and predictability. The paper unifies conventional double-slit interferometry (following Bartell, 1980) with particle oscillations in the neutral-kaon system (reviewed by Kabir) and Mott scattering of identical nuclei (experiments by Heydenburg and Temmer, 1956, and Bromley et al.). The new parameter ν enables direct comparison of the effective number of fringes across these different experimental setups. This work enables future quantitative analyses of other interferometric systems, such as B⁰–B̄⁰ oscillations (where Δm_B >> ΔΓ_B gives ν >> 1) and neutron interferometry, once analytical expressions become available.
