# On viscous flow and azimuthal anisotropy of quark-gluon plasma in strong magnetic field

> We calculate the viscous pressure tensor of the quark-gluon plasma in strong magnetic field. It is azimuthally anisotropic and is characterized by five shear viscosity coefficients, four of which vanish when the field strength eB is much larger than the plasma temperature squared. We argue, that the azimuthally anisotropic viscous pressure tensor generates the transverse flow with asymmetry as large as 1/3, even not taking into account the collision geometry. We conclude, that the magnitude of the shear viscosity extracted from the experimental data ignoring the magnetic field must be underestimated.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a strong magnetic field (eB much larger than the plasma temperature squared) makes the viscous pressure tensor of the quark-gluon plasma azimuthally anisotropic, which can generate transverse flow asymmetry as large as 1/3 even without considering collision geometry; the authors conclude that shear viscosity extracted from experimental data ignoring the magnetic field must be underestimated.

## Method

The authors derive the viscous pressure tensor of quark-gluon plasma in a strong magnetic field using the relativistic kinetic equation, expanding the distribution function deviation in terms of five shear viscosity coefficients. They solve the kinetic equation in the strong-field limit where the synchrotron frequency ω_B = zeB/ε is much larger than the collision rate ν, first neglecting collisions entirely to obtain η_3 and η_4, then including collisions in the relaxation-time approximation to obtain η_1 and η_2. The resulting anisotropic viscous pressure tensor is then used in the Navier-Stokes equations to compute transverse flow in the xz plane under the assumptions of non-relativistic, non-turbulent, incompressible flow.

## Key numbers

- The azimuthal anisotropy of hydrodynamic flow is V_x² - V_z² / V_x² + V_z² = 1/3, generated exclusively by the magnetic field under isotropic initial conditions and pressure.
- In the ultra-relativistic limit (m ≪ T), η_3^UR = 2 n T² / (zeB), where n is number density.
- In the non-relativistic limit (m ≫ T), η_3^NR = ρ T / (2 zeB), where ρ is mass density.
- The collision rate ν is estimated as ν ∼ 40 MeV for typical QGP parameters (Q_s ∼ 1 GeV, α_s² P ∼ 1 GeV/fm³).
- The magnetic field strength is eB ≃ m_π², as cited from Kharzeev (2007) and Skokov (2009).
- The ratio V_x/V_z = √2 from the late-time solution of the Navier-Stokes equations.

## Context

This work extends the magneto-hydrodynamic analysis of QGP in strong magnetic fields, building on the weak-field approach of Mohapatra (2011) which found azimuthal anisotropy of order 30% using different arguments. The paper provides an analytic calculation of viscosity coefficients in the strong-field limit, complementing earlier work on non-relativistic electromagnetic plasma by Landau and Lifshitz and the general relativistic approach of Erkelens (1977). The results suggest that phenomenological viscosity values extracted from data by Song (2007), Romatschke (2007), and Dusling (2007) may be underestimated because they ignore magnetic field effects, and that the true viscosity may be closer to perturbative calculations by Arnold (2003) and Baym (1990).
