# On the Energy and Centrality Dependence of Higher Order Moments of Net-Proton Distributions in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> The higher order moments of the net-baryon distributions in relativistic heavy ion collisions are useful probes for the QCD critical point and fluctuations. Within a simple model we study the colliding energy and centrality dependence of the net-proton distributions in the central rapidity region. The model is based on considering the baryon stopping and pair production effects in the processes. Based on some physical reasoning, the dependence is parameterized. Predictions for the net-proton distributions for Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions at different centralities at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=39 and 2760 GeV, respectively, are presented from the parameterizations for the model parameters. A possible test of our model is proposed from investigating the net proton distributions in the non-central rapidity region for different colliding centralities and energies.

## Key claim

The higher order moments of net-proton distributions in relativistic heavy ion collisions can be described by a simple model that considers only baryon stopping and baryon pair production, without invoking thermal equilibrium or critical fluctuations; the model's results can therefore serve as a baseline for detecting novel physics, but the paper does not claim that the observed moments are evidence for the QCD critical point.

## Method

The authors model the net-proton distribution in a central rapidity window (|y| < 0.5) using an analytical formula that depends on two parameters: the mean net-proton number from initial nuclear stopping (B) and the mean number of produced baryon pairs (mu). The centrality dependence of B and mu is parameterized as functions of the number of participant nucleons (N_p) with four energy-dependent coefficients (a1, a2, b1, b2). These coefficients are fitted to STAR data for Au+Au collisions at four RHIC energies, then parameterized as functions of sqrt(s_NN) to extrapolate predictions to LHC energy.

## Key numbers

* For Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 19.6 GeV, the fitted parameters are a1 = 0.022, 10^4*a2 = -0.126, b1 = 0.0132, 10^4*b2 = 0.121.
* For Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 39 GeV, the fitted parameters are a1 = 0.0012, 10^4*a2 = -1.97, b1 = 0.0300, 10^4*b2 = 4.90.
* For Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 62.4 GeV, the fitted parameters are a1 = 0.0096, 10^4*a2 = -0.159, b1 = 0.0383, 10^4*b2 = 5.10.
* For Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, the fitted parameters are a1 = 0.0052, 10^4*a2 = 1.88, b1 = 0.0585, 10^4*b2 = 5.00.
* Predictions are presented for net-proton distributions in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2760 GeV for different centralities.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous model (Wang and Yang, 2011) for net-proton distributions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV to lower RHIC energies (19.6, 39, and 62.4 GeV) and to LHC energy (2.76 TeV). It contrasts with approaches that assume thermal equilibrium or critical fluctuations, such as the hadron resonance gas model and event generators like AMPT, UrQMD, and HIJING. The results provide a baseline from "normal physics" (baryon stopping and pair production) against which signals of the QCD critical point can be identified in future experimental data.
