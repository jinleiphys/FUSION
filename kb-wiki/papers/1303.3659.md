# Nuclear symmetry energy from the Fermi-energy difference in nuclei

> The neutron-proton Fermi-energy difference and the correlation to nucleon separation energies for some magic nuclei are investigated with the Skyrme energy density functionals and nuclear masses, with which the nuclear symmetry energy at sub-saturation densities is constrained from 54 Skyrme parameter sets. The extracted nuclear symmetry energy at sub-saturation density of 0.11 fm$^{-3}$ is 26.2 $\pm$ 1.0 MeV with 1.5 $σ$ uncertainty. By further combining the neutron-skin thickness of 208Pb, ten Skyrme forces with slope parameter of 28<L<65 are selected for the description of the symmetry energy around saturation densities.

## Key claim

The neutron-proton Fermi-energy difference in doubly-magic and semi-magic nuclei is shown to closely equal the difference between proton and neutron separation energies, and this correlation can be used to constrain the nuclear symmetry energy at sub-saturation densities. The extracted symmetry energy at 0.11 fm⁻³ is 26.2 ± 1.0 MeV with 1.5σ uncertainty, but the slope parameter L cannot be well constrained from the Fermi-energy difference alone and requires additional data from the neutron-skin thickness of ²⁰⁸Pb.

## Method

The authors performed Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculations with 54 different Skyrme parameter sets to compute the neutron-proton Fermi-energy difference for 19 doubly-magic or semi-magic nuclei. They compared these calculated values to the corresponding separation-energy differences (obtained from experimental masses and the WS3ᴿᴮᶠ mass model for unmeasured nuclei) and computed the average absolute deviation ⟨σ⟩. The nuclear symmetry energy at sub-saturation densities was extracted by fitting the parabolic dependence of ⟨σ⟩ on Esym(ρ) for densities of 0.09, 0.11, 0.13, and 0.16 fm⁻³, and the neutron-skin thickness of ²⁰⁸Pb was used to further constrain the slope parameter L.

## Key numbers

- Extracted nuclear symmetry energy at ρ = 0.11 fm⁻³: 26.2 ± 1.0 MeV with 1.5σ uncertainty, from 17 selected Skyrme forces with ⟨σ⟩ ≤ 1.19 MeV.
- Minimal average deviation ⟨σ⟩ from the parabolic fit: 1.19 MeV.
- For the four Skyrme forces with the smallest average deviation (⟨σ⟩ ≤ 1.12 MeV) and reasonable neutron-skin thickness: slope parameter L = 56 ± 9 MeV.
- Ten Skyrme forces with 28 ≤ L ≤ 65 MeV selected by combining Fermi-energy difference and neutron-skin thickness of ²⁰⁸Pb (0.131 ≤ ΔRnp ≤ 0.218 fm).
- Symmetry energy at saturation density from the ten selected forces: Esym(ρ₀) = 31.9 ± 2.1 MeV with 1.5σ uncertainty.
- Experimental Fermi-energy differences for six doubly-magic nuclei: ¹⁶O (−3.53 MeV), ⁴⁰Ca (−7.31 MeV), ⁴⁸Ca (6.10 MeV), ⁵⁶Ni (−9.47 MeV), ¹³²Sn (8.40 MeV), ²⁰⁸Pb (0.64 MeV).

## Context

This work extends previous constraints on the nuclear symmetry energy from finite-nuclei properties such as binding energies (Danielewicz 2009; Wang 2010; Liu 2010), neutron-skin thickness (Centelles 2009; Warda 2009; Roca-Maza 2011), and pygmy dipole resonance (Klimkiewicz 2007; Carbone 2010). The approach using Fermi-energy differences is complementary to constraints from heavy-ion collisions (Tsang 2009; Zhang 2008) and neutron-star observations (Steiner 2005; Lattimer 2012). The results enable further refinement of Skyrme interactions by combining multiple observables, and the authors note that more isospin-sensitive observations from heavy-ion collisions and neutron stars are still needed to constrain the symmetry energy at saturation and super-saturation densities.
