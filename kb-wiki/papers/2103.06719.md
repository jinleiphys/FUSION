# Positronium: an illustration of nonperturbative renormalization in a basis light-front approach

> We calculate the mass spectrum and the structure of the positronium system at a strong coupling in a basis light-front approach. We start from the light-front QED Hamiltonian and retain one dynamical photon in our basis. We perform the fermion mass renormalization associated with the nonperturbative fermion self-energy correction. We present the resulting mass spectrum and wave functions for the selected low-lying states. Next, we apply this approach to QCD and calculate the heavy meson system with one dynamical gluon retained. We illustrate the obtained mass spectrum and wave functions for the selected low-lying states.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that Basis Light-front Quantization (BLFQ) with Fock-sector dependent renormalization can reproduce the mass spectrum and wave functions of positronium at an artificially increased coupling constant \(\alpha=0.15\), and that the same approach, augmented with a phenomenological confining potential, yields heavy meson spectra in reasonable agreement with experiment. The hedge is that the QED calculation uses an artificially strong coupling and the QCD calculation introduces several phenomenological parameters (separate kinetic and interaction quark masses, a gluon mass, and a confining strength) to achieve agreement.

## Method
The authors solve the light-front QED Hamiltonian eigenvalue problem in a truncated basis retaining the \(|e^+ e^-\rangle\) and \(|e^+ e^- \gamma\rangle\) Fock sectors, with transverse and longitudinal basis truncation parameters \(N_{\text{max}}\) and \(K\). Fermion mass renormalization is performed nonperturbatively via Fock-sector dependent renormalization, where the bare mass in the \(|e^+ e^-\rangle\) sector is determined by solving single-electron systems in the \(|e\rangle + |e\gamma\rangle\) sectors. For heavy mesons, the same BLFQ framework is applied to QCD with the \(|q\bar{q}\rangle\) and \(|q\bar{q}g\rangle\) sectors, adding a phenomenological confining potential in both longitudinal and transverse directions, and introducing separate quark masses for kinetic and interaction terms plus a nonzero gluon mass.

## Key numbers
- Positronium binding energy spectrum calculated at \(\alpha = 0.15\) with truncation parameters \(N_{\text{max}} = K - 1 = 16\); the ground state (\(1^1S_0\)) binding energy is compared to the nonrelativistic quantum mechanics value.
- For charmonium: charm quark kinetic mass \(m_c = 1.565\) GeV, interaction mass \(m'_c = 6.259\) GeV, gluon mass \(m_g = 0.5\) GeV, confining strength \(\kappa = 1.117\) GeV, strong coupling \(g = 1.51\).
- For bottomonium: bottom quark kinetic mass \(m_b = 4.767\) GeV, interaction mass \(m'_b = 12.191\) GeV, gluon mass \(m_g = 0.5\) GeV, confining strength \(\kappa = 1.894\) GeV, strong coupling \(g = 1.75\).
- Heavy meson spectra calculated at truncation parameters \(N_{\text{max}} = K - 1 = 10\) for charmonium, bottomonium, and \(B_c\) meson, compared with experimental values from PDG and lattice results from Allison (2004), Gregory (2009), and Davies (1996).

## Context
This work extends the BLFQ approach from effective one-photon-exchange interactions (Wiecki, 2014) to a full QED Hamiltonian with dynamical photons, demonstrating nonperturbative renormalization in a bound state calculation. It also extends the same framework to QCD heavy meson systems, building on earlier BLFQ studies with effective one-gluon-exchange interactions (Li, 2017; Tang, 2018). The results enable future applications of BLFQ to hadron structure beyond the valence sector, including systems with more dynamical partons.
