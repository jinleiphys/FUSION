# A way to estimate the heavy quark thermalization rate from the lattice

> The thermalization rate of a heavy quark is related to its momentum diffusion coefficient. Starting from a Kubo relation and using the framework of the heavy quark effective theory, we argue that in the large-mass limit the momentum diffusion coefficient can be defined through a certain Euclidean correlation function, involving color-electric fields along a Polyakov loop. Furthermore, carrying out a perturbative computation, we demonstrate that the spectral function corresponding to this correlator is relatively flat at small frequencies. Therefore, unlike in the case of several other transport coefficients, for which the narrowness of the transport peak makes analytic continuation from Euclidean lattice data susceptible to severe systematic uncertainties, it appears that the determination of the heavy quark thermalization rate could be relatively well under control.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the heavy quark momentum diffusion coefficient \(\kappa\) can be extracted from a Euclidean correlation function of color-electric fields along a Polyakov loop, and that the corresponding spectral function is relatively flat at small frequencies, making the analytic continuation from lattice data more reliable than for other transport coefficients.

## Method

Starting from a Kubo relation for the heavy quark current-current correlator and using heavy quark effective theory (HQET) in the large-mass limit, the authors derive an expression for the momentum diffusion coefficient \(\kappa\) in terms of a Euclidean correlator involving color-electric fields \(gE_i\) connected by straight fundamental Wilson lines in the Euclidean time direction. They then carry out a perturbative computation of the spectral function corresponding to this correlator to demonstrate its flat behavior at small frequencies.

## Key numbers

* The heavy quark thermalization rate at weak coupling scales as \(\Gamma \sim \alpha_s^2 T^2 / M\), where \(T\) is the temperature and \(M\) is the heavy quark mass.
* The width of the transport peak in weakly coupled QCD is \(\eta_D \sim g^4 T^2 / M\) (with \(g^2 \equiv 4\pi\alpha_s\)), and the perturbative ultraviolet contribution departs from the Lorentzian tail at the scale \(\omega_{\rm ir} \sim gT\).
* In strongly coupled \(\mathcal{N}=4\) Super-Yang-Mills theory with 't Hooft coupling \(\lambda = g^2 N_c\), the transport peak width is \(\eta_D \sim \sqrt{\lambda} T^2 / M\) and the continuum takes over at \(\omega_{\rm ir} \sim T\).

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts at a lattice determination of heavy quark transport coefficients (Petreczky and Teaney, 2006) which found the task extremely hard due to the narrow transport peak. It parallels the real-time study of Casalderrey-Solana and Teaney (2006) but uses the imaginary-time formalism to connect with Euclidean lattice techniques, and explicitly tracks \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s)\) quantum corrections and renormalization issues. The result enables a lattice determination of the heavy quark thermalization rate that could be relatively well under control, unlike for other transport coefficients.
