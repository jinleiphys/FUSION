# Beyond the relativistic mean-field approximation (II): configuration mixing of mean-field wave functions projected on angular momentum and particle number

> The framework of relativistic self-consistent mean-field models is extended to include correlations related to the restoration of broken symmetries and to fluctuations of collective variables. The generator coordinate method is used to perform configuration mixing of angular-momentum and particle-number projected relativistic wave functions. The geometry is restricted to axially symmetric shapes, and the intrinsic wave functions are generated from the solutions of the relativistic mean-field + Lipkin-Nogami BCS equations, with a constraint on the mass quadrupole moment. The model employs a relativistic point-coupling (contact) nucleon-nucleon effective interaction in the particle-hole channel, and a density-independent $δ$-interaction in the pairing channel. Illustrative calculations are performed for $^{24}$Mg, $^{32}$S and $^{36}$Ar, and compared with results obtained employing the model developed in the first part of this work, i.e. without particle-number projection, as well as with the corresponding non-relativistic models based on Skyrme and Gogny effective interactions.

## Key claim

The paper extends the relativistic mean-field framework to include both angular-momentum and particle-number projection before configuration mixing via the generator coordinate method, but the projection is performed after variation rather than before variation, which leads to rotational bands with smaller moments of inertia than experiment.

## Method

The authors generate intrinsic wave functions from constrained relativistic mean-field plus Lipkin-Nogami BCS equations with a point-coupling nucleon-nucleon effective interaction in the particle-hole channel and a density-independent delta-interaction in the pairing channel. They then perform generator coordinate method configuration mixing of these wave functions after projecting onto good angular momentum and particle number, restricting the geometry to axially symmetric shapes and using the mass quadrupole moment as the generating coordinate. The Hill-Wheeler equation is solved to obtain energies and wave functions, and transition probabilities and spectroscopic quadrupole moments are computed using the bare proton charge.

## Key numbers

- Neutron pairing strength reduced from V_n = -308 MeV to V_n = -285 MeV; proton pairing strength reduced from V_p = -321 MeV to V_p = -260 MeV when including Lipkin-Nogami projection
- For ^24Mg: spectroscopic quadrupole moment Q_spec(2_1^+) = -16.56 e fm^2, compared to experimental value -16.6(6) e fm^2
- For ^24Mg: the 0_2^+ state is found at 6.432 MeV excitation energy experimentally
- L=9 points in Fomenko's expansion for particle-number projection and 13 points in the Gauss-Legendre quadrature over Euler angle beta produce numerically stable results

## Context

This work extends the model developed in the first part of the study (NVR.06) by adding particle-number projection to the previously implemented angular-momentum projection and configuration mixing. The results are compared with nonrelativistic GCM calculations using Skyrme SLy4 (VHB.00) and Gogny D1S (RER.02b) effective interactions. The paper enables future applications to shell evolution, shape coexistence phenomena, and nuclei with soft potential energy surfaces, though the authors note that projection before variation or cranked mean-field wave functions would be needed to improve agreement with experimental moments of inertia.
