# Chiral O(Q^4) two-body operators for s-wave pion photoproduction on the NN system

> The two-body currents for s-wave pion photoproduction on the NN system are derived to O(Q^4) in chiral perturbation theory. For the interesting case of 3S1 <-> 1S0 transitions, we show that an axial isovector two-nucleon contact term connects the short-distance physics of pion photoproduction to pion production and several important electroweak reactions. We also find that the standard chiral Lagrangian gives a gamma pi pi N N vertex that have not been explicitly mentioned in previous literature. The corresponding Feynman rule is presented here and some processes where it should be important are briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The paper derives the complete set of two-body currents for s-wave pion photoproduction on the NN system at order Q^4 in chiral perturbation theory, showing that the axial isovector two-nucleon contact term connects the short-distance physics of this reaction to pion production and several electroweak reactions, but only if a previously unpublished gamma-pi-pi-NN vertex (parametrized by the low-energy constant c_4) is included.

## Method

The author derived the O(Q^4) two-body amplitudes for s-wave pion photoproduction in Coulomb gauge using the standard chiral Lagrangian, grouping diagrams by their pion propagator structure into Coulombic, non-Coulombic, and double-propagator categories. Explicit momentum-space expressions were given for the surviving diagrams, and the contact terms were isolated by identifying constant pieces that Fourier transform into delta functions in configuration space, with particular focus on 3S1 ↔ 1S0 transitions.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant f_pi = 93 MeV, used as the scale for expanding the axial four-vector u_mu
- The combined contact term coefficient for 3S1 ↔ 1S0 transitions is given by hat_d = hat_d_1 + 2*hat_d_2 + hat_c_3/3 + 2*hat_c_4/3 + 1/6
- Low-energy constants scale as c_i = O(1/M) where M is the nucleon mass (~1 GeV), and d_i = O(1/(M*f_pi^2))
- The reduced LECs are defined through c_i ≡ hat_c_i/M and d_i ≡ g_A*hat_d_i/(M*f_pi^2)
- The contact term for 3S1 ↔ 1S0 transitions reduces to (2ie*g_A/(M*f_pi^3))*(hat_d - g_A^2/6)*epsilon_d_dagger·epsilon_gamma

## Context

This work extends previous chiral perturbation theory calculations of pion photoproduction on deuterons by Silasgd and Ulfgd (no year given) and radiative pion absorption on deuterons by GP1 and GP2 (no year given), providing explicit expressions that were previously omitted. The paper establishes that the same axial isovector contact term appearing in weak pp fusion, tritium beta decay, the hep process (3He p → 4He e+ nu_e), neutrino-induced deuteron breakup, muon capture on deuterium, p-wave pion production on two nucleons, and the chiral three-nucleon force also governs the short-distance physics of s-wave pion photoproduction, enabling a precision extraction of the neutron-neutron scattering length a_nn from pi- d → nn gamma measurements.
