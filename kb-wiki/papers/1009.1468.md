# Non-linear equation: energy conservation and impact parameter dependence

> In this paper we address two questions: how energy conservation affects the solution to the non-linear equation, and how impact parameter dependence influences the inclusive production. Answering the first question we solve the modified BK equation which takes into account energy conservation. In spite of the fact that we used the simplified kernel, we believe that the main result of the paper: the small ($\leq 40%$) suppression of the inclusive productiondue to energy conservation, reflects a general feature. This result leads us to believe that the small value of the nuclear modification factor is of a non-perturbative nature. In the solution a new scale appears $Q_{fr} = Q_s \exp(-1/(2 \bas))$ and the production of dipoles with the size larger than $2/Q_{fr}$ is suppressed. Therefore, we can expect that the typical temperature for hadron production is about $Q_{fr}$ ($ T \approx Q_{fr}$). The simplified equation allows us to obtain a solution to Balitsky-Kovchegov equation taking into account the impact parameter dependence. We show that the impact parameter ($b$) dependence can be absorbed into the non-perturbative $b$ dependence of the saturation scale. The solution of the BK equation, as well as of the modified BK equation without $b$ dependence, is only accurate up to $\pm 25%$.

## Key claim

Energy conservation in the modified Balitsky-Kovchegov equation leads to a small suppression of inclusive production (at most 40%), suggesting that the small nuclear modification factor observed in experiments is of non-perturbative origin rather than due to perturbative energy-loss mechanisms.

## Method

The authors solve a modified BK equation that incorporates energy conservation by using a simplified kernel derived from the NLO BFKL kernel, where the NLO correction is approximated as \(\chi_{NLO}(\gamma) = -\omega \chi_{LO}(\gamma)\). The solution is obtained analytically using the method of Levin and Tuchin (2001), replacing the full BFKL kernel with a piecewise simplified kernel that treats the regions \(z \leq 0\) and \(z > 0\) separately, where \(z = \ln(r^2 Q_s^2)\). For the impact parameter dependence, the simplified equation allows the b-dependence to be absorbed into the non-perturbative b-dependence of the saturation scale.

## Key numbers

- The suppression of inclusive production due to energy conservation is at most 40% (\(\leq 40\%\)).
- The solution of the BK equation, as well as the modified BK equation without b-dependence, is accurate only up to \(\pm 25\%\).
- A new scale appears: \(Q_{fr} = Q_s \exp(-1/(2 \bar{\alpha}_S))\), and dipoles with size larger than \(2/Q_{fr}\) are suppressed.
- The critical anomalous dimension is \(\gamma_{cr} = \frac{1}{2}(1 - \bar{\alpha}_S)\).
- The saturation momentum evolves as \(\ln(Q_s^2/Q_0^2) = \frac{4 \bar{\alpha}_S}{(1 + \bar{\alpha}_S)^2} Y\) where \(Y = \ln(1/x)\).

## Context

This work extends the modified BK equation proposed by Gotsman, Levin, Maor, and Naftali (2005) which incorporates energy conservation into the non-linear evolution. The simplified analytical approach follows the method of Levin and Tuchin (2001) for solving the BK equation. The result that energy conservation gives only a modest suppression contrasts with the large suppression expected from energy-loss mechanisms (Dokshitzer and Kharzeev, 2001; Baier et al., 1998; Zakharov, 1996; Gyulassy, Levai and Vitev, 2000; Salgado and Wiedemann, 2003), and leads the authors to conclude that the small nuclear modification factor observed by BRAHMS (2003, 2004, 2007) and PHENIX (2006) is of non-perturbative origin. The treatment of impact parameter dependence builds on earlier work by Gotsman et al. (2004) and Golec-Biernat and Stasto (2003), showing that the b-dependence can be absorbed into the saturation scale, providing guidance for solving the impact parameter problem in high-density QCD.
