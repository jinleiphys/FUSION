# The Relevant Scale Parameter in the High Temperature Phase of QCD

> We introduce the running coupling constant of QCD in the high temperature phase, $\tilde{g}^2(T)$, through a renormalization scheme where the dimensional reduction is optimal at the one-loop level. We then calculate the relevant scale parameter, $Λ_T$, which characterizes the running of $\tilde{g}^2(T)$ with $T$, using the background field method in the static sector. It is found that $Λ_T/Λ_{\overline{\text{MS}}} =e^{(γ_E+1/22)}/(4π)\approx 0.148$. We further verify that the coupling $\tilde{g}^2(T)$ is also optimal for lattice perturbative calculations. Our result naturally explains why the high temperature limit of QCD sets in at temperatures as low as a few times the critical temperature. In addition, our $Λ_T$ agrees remarkably well with the scale parameter determined from the lattice measurement of the spatial string tension of the SU(2) gauge theory at high $T$.

## Key claim

The paper defines a temperature-dependent running coupling constant for QCD at high temperature, $\tilde{g}^2(T)$, using a renormalization scheme that achieves maximal decoupling of non-static modes at the one-loop level; the resulting scale parameter $\Lambda_T$ is approximately $0.148\,\Lambda_{\overline{\text{MS}}}$ for pure Yang-Mills, which is much smaller than $\Lambda_{\overline{\text{MS}}}$ and explains why the high-temperature regime sets in at temperatures as low as a few times the critical temperature.

## Method

The authors used the background field method in the static sector to compute the one-loop effective action for the background gauge field at finite temperature. They imposed a renormalization condition requiring that the non-static mode contribution to the two-point function vanish at zero external momentum, thereby defining an optimal subtraction scale $\mu(T)$. The calculation was performed both in the continuum using dimensional regularization with the $\overline{\text{MS}}$ scheme and on the lattice using the Wilson action for SU($N$) gauge theory.

## Key numbers

- $\Lambda_T / \Lambda_{\overline{\text{MS}}} = e^{(\gamma_E + 1/22)} / (4\pi) \approx 0.148$ for pure SU($N$) Yang-Mills theory (quenched case).
- $\Lambda_T / \Lambda_{\overline{\text{MS}}} \approx 0.110$ for the phenomenologically relevant case $N = N_f = 3$.
- The optimal subtraction scale is $\mu(T) = 4\pi T e^{-(\gamma_E + 1/22)}$.
- For SU(2) gauge theory, the lattice measurement of the spatial string tension by Bali et al. gave $\Lambda_T^{\sigma} = (0.123 \pm 0.021)\,\Lambda_{\overline{\text{MS}}}$, which is close to the authors' result $\Lambda_T \approx 0.148\,\Lambda_{\overline{\text{MS}}}$.
- The critical temperature for SU(2) was expressed as $T_c = 1.62\,\Lambda_{\overline{\text{MS}}}$ from the lattice data of Bali et al.

## Context

This work extends the concept of maximal decoupling of non-static modes introduced by Landsman (1993) to define a temperature-dependent running coupling for QCD. The authors show that their scale parameter $\Lambda_T$ agrees remarkably well with the non-perturbative determination from the spatial string tension measured on the lattice by Bali et al. (1993), providing a natural explanation for why dimensional reduction becomes effective at temperatures as low as $2T_c$ to $3T_c$. The result enables improved perturbative calculations in the high-temperature phase of QCD and suggests that other observables, such as the gluonic Debye-screening mass, could be used to define consistent optimal couplings.
