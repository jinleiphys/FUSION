# The Phase Diagram of Strongly-Interacting Matter

> A fundamental question of physics is what ultimately happens to matter as it is heated or compressed. In the realm of very high temperature and density the fundamental degrees of freedom of the strong interaction, quarks and gluons, come into play and a transition from matter consisting of confined baryons and mesons to a state with 'liberated' quarks and gluons is expected. The study of the possible phases of strongly-interacting matter is at the focus of many research activities worldwide. In this article we discuss physical aspects of the phase diagram, its relation to the evolution of the early universe as well as the inner core of neutron stars. We also summarize recent progress in the experimental study of hadronic or quark-gluon matter under extreme conditions with ultrarelativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that strongly-interacting matter undergoes a transition from hadronic matter to a quark-gluon plasma at high temperature and density, but notes that at vanishing baryo-chemical potential the transition appears to be a cross over rather than a true phase transition, with the exact critical temperature still poorly known in the range 150-190 MeV.

## Method

The authors review the phase diagram of strongly-interacting matter by combining results from effective models (MIT-Bag model, Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model) with ab-initio lattice QCD calculations at vanishing quark chemical potential, where the partition function is evaluated via Monte Carlo sampling of field configurations on a discrete Euclidean space-time lattice. They also discuss experimental progress from ultrarelativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions at SPS, RHIC, and future LHC facilities.

## Key numbers

- The Hagedorn temperature from fitting the exponential growth of hadronic mass states yields T_H ~ 160-180 MeV
- The fundamental QCD scale parameter is Lambda_QCD ≈ 200 MeV
- The MIT-Bag model with bag constant B = 57.5 MeV/fm^3 gives T_c ~ 100 MeV at mu_q = 0 and mu_c ~ 300 MeV at vanishing temperature
- The critical temperature from lattice QCD is T_c = 175 MeV, with critical energy density epsilon(T_c) = 700 ± 300 MeV/fm^3
- The reduced energy density shows a rapid variation with Delta T/T_c ≤ 0.1 near the transition
- The critical energy density is roughly 5 times higher than the energy density in the center of a heavy nucleus like ^208Pb
- A systematic discrepancy of about 15% between calculated energy density and the free gas Stefan-Boltzmann limit is observed for T > 2T_c
- The pion mass is ~ 140 MeV, significantly smaller than the rho or omega meson mass (~ 800 MeV ~ 2M_q)

## Context

This review extends the early work of Hagedorn (1965) on the limiting temperature of hadronic matter and the bootstrap model, as well as the proposals by Cabibbo and Parisi (1975) and Collins and Perry (1975) that the quark-gluon substructure of hadrons enables a phase transition to deconfined matter. The paper connects lattice QCD results from Karsch (2002) and Fodor (2004) with effective model studies from the NJL model (Nambu and Jona-Lasinio, 1961) and the MIT-Bag model, and discusses implications for the early universe and neutron star interiors. The work enables future experimental searches for the critical endpoint in the phase diagram using heavy-ion collisions at facilities such as the future FAIR facility.
