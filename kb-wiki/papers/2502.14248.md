# Thermal and baryon density modifications to the $σ$-boson propagator: A road to describe the transfer of vorticity to spin in a nuclear environment in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> In the context of the description of how the vortical motion, produced in peripheral heavy-ion collisions, is transferred to the spin of hadrons, we compute the $σ$-meson propagator at finite temperature and baryon density. This propagator encodes the properties of a medium consisting mainly of nucleons{, and can be used to model the main interactions between hadrons} in the corona region of the reaction. We compute the one-loop $σ$ self-energy in an approximation that accounts for the large nucleon mass. From the real part of the self-energy, we find the dispersion relation and show that the $σ$-mass receives a non-negligible thermal and baryon chemical dependent {contribution}. From the imaginary part, we also compute the spectral density, which we show to contain a piece coming from the branch cut associated with Landau damping. We also present approximations for the dispersion relation and the residue at the pole in the small- and large-momentum regimes and complement the calculation, providing the sum rules satisfied by the propagator. This study aims to determine one of the elements needed to compute how the vortical motion in the corona region of the reaction is transferred to the spin of $Λ$ hyperons that can interact with nucleons by $σ$-meson exchange.

## Key claim

The authors compute the one-loop σ-meson propagator at finite temperature and baryon density using an approximation scheme analogous to the Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) method but adapted for the case where the nucleon mass is much larger than the temperature, finding that the σ-mass receives a non-negligible thermal and baryon chemical potential dependent contribution that could affect how vortical motion is transferred to Λ hyperon spin in the corona region of heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The authors start from the linear Relativistic Mean Field model Lagrangian describing nucleon-σ coupling and compute the one-loop σ self-energy using the imaginary time formalism of finite temperature field theory. Since the nucleon mass is larger than the temperature, the standard HTL approximation cannot be used; instead, the authors develop an analogous approximation where the loop momentum ξ is treated as larger than any external momentum component, allowing expansion of the nucleon energies and Fermi-Dirac distributions. The self-energy is separated into three contributions (Π₁, Π₂, Π₃), with Π₂ found to be of order zero in the nucleon mass and thus negligible compared to Π₁ and Π₃, leading to a final expression involving a thermal mass M_T and a Landau damping term γ_T.

## Key numbers

- The thermal mass M_T is defined as M_T² = (2 g_σ² M_N T / π²) K₁(M_N/T) cosh(μ/T), where K₁ is a modified Bessel function of the second kind
- The Landau damping parameter γ_T is defined as γ_T = M_T² tanh(μ/T) / T
- The coupling constant is taken as g_σ = 10.5 and the vacuum σ-mass as m_σ = 510 MeV, based on averages from Long (2003) and Sugahara (1993)
- In the small-momentum limit p → 0, the dispersion relation gives ω²(p=0) = m_σ² + M_T², showing the thermal mass contribution to the σ-mass
- The damping rate is γ(p) = (γ_T/2) π A(ω(p)/p) θ(p² - ω(p)²), where A(x) = (5x²+1)(x - 2x²/√(3x²+1))

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous core-corona model for Λ and anti-Λ global polarization in heavy-ion collisions (Ayala 2023, Ayala 2021, Ayala 2020), where the corona region was previously assumed to give negligible polarization. The calculation provides a necessary element for computing how vortical motion is transferred to Λ hyperon spin via σ-meson exchange in the nucleon-dominated corona, addressing a discrepancy between the core-corona model and experimental data at low collision energies. The approach builds on effective models for nucleon-σ interactions used in the literature (Csernai 2018, Santos 2018, Sun 2017, Oertel 2014, Wang 2013, Song 2010, Barros 2000) and will enable computation of the relaxation time for Λ spin alignment in a baryonic gas environment.
