# Flash of photons from the early stage of heavy-ion collisions

> The dynamics of partonic cascades may be an important aspect for particle production in relativistic collisions of nuclei at CERN SPS and BNL RHIC energies. Within the Parton-Cascade Model, we estimate the production of single photons from such cascades due to scattering of quarks and gluons q g -> q gamma, quark-antiquark annihilation q qbar -> g gamma, or gamma gamma, and from electromagnetic brems-strahlung of quarks q -> q gamma. We find that the latter QED branching process plays the dominant role for photon production, similarly as the QCD branchings q -> q g and g -> g g play a crucial role for parton multiplication. We conclude therefore that photons accompanying the parton cascade evolution during the early stage of heavy-ion collisions shed light on the formation of a partonic plasma.

## Key claim

The authors argue that electromagnetic bremsstrahlung from quarks (q -> q gamma) dominates photon production during the early partonic stage of heavy-ion collisions, and that this radiative mechanism is potentially much richer in magnitude and complexity than collisional processes (Compton scattering and annihilation), contrary to the abstract's implication that both mechanisms are simply estimated.

## Method

The authors used the Parton Cascade Model (PCM), implemented in the Monte Carlo code VNI, to simulate the space-time evolution of partons (quarks and gluons) in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. They computed single photon yields from two sources: (1) collisional processes (q g -> q gamma, q qbar -> g gamma, q qbar -> gamma gamma) using perturbative QCD Born cross-sections, and (2) radiative photon emission from quark branchings (q -> q gamma), treated on equal footing with QCD branchings (q -> q g, g -> g g) but without angular ordering or the same cut-off scale restrictions. The simulations were performed for S+S and Pb+Pb collisions at CERN SPS energy (sqrt(s) ~ 18-20 A GeV) and for S+S and Au+Au collisions at BNL RHIC energy (sqrt(s) = 200 A GeV).

## Key numbers

* The relative probability for q -> q gamma versus q -> q g branching is proportional to (alpha_em * <e_q^2>) / (alpha_s * C_F) ~ 1/200, using alpha_em = 1/137, alpha_s = 0.25, <e_q^2> = 0.22, and C_F = 4/3.
* At SPS energy, the initial partonic composition has relative proportions N_q : N_g ~ 1 : 0.4; at RHIC energy, N_q : N_g ~ 1 : 1.9.
* The ratio of nuclear overlap functions for central collisions is T_PbPb / T_SS ~ 15.4 at SPS and T_AuAu / T_SS ~ 14.2 at RHIC.
* The number of hard scatterings in Pb+Pb at SPS is ~17 times that in S+S, and in Au+Au at RHIC it is ~12 times that in S+S.
* At SPS energy, the partonic system can reach energy densities up to 5 GeV/fm^3; at RHIC, the energy density can become twice as large.
* At SPS energy, photon emission from early hard partonic scatterings is of the same order as photon production from later hadronic reactions for p_T <= 2-3 GeV, and dominates at higher transverse momenta.

## Context

This work extends the Parton Cascade Model (PCM) developed by Geiger and others (1990s) to include photon production, building on earlier suggestions by Shuryak (1978) that photons would accompany quark-gluon plasma formation. The authors contrast their approach with studies of collisional photon production in various contexts (referenced as "scat") and with the treatment of radiative photon emission by Sjostrand (referenced as "sjoestrand"). The results enable future comparison with hadronic photon production studied recently (referenced as "crs") and open the possibility of studying dilepton (virtual photon) production from similar branching mechanisms.
