# Mass-number and isotope dependence of the local microscopic optical potential for polarized proton scattering

> We derive local microscopic optical potentials $U$ systematically for polarized proton scattering at 65~MeV using the local-potential version of the Melbourne $g$-matrix folding model. As target nuclei, we take $^{6}$He and neutron-rich Ne isotopes in addition to stable nuclei of mass number $A=4$--$208$ in order to clarify mass-number and isotope dependence of $U$. The local potentials reproduce the experimental data systematically and have geometries similar to the phenomenological optical potentials for stable targets. The target density is broadened by the weak-binding nature and/or deformation of unstable nuclei. For the real spin-orbit part of $U$ the density broadening weakens the strength and enlarges the radius, whereas for the central part it enlarges both of the strength and the radius. The density-broadening effect is conspicuous for halo nuclei such as $^{6}$He and $^{31}$Ne. Similar discussions are made briefly for proton scattering at 200~MeV. We briefly investigate how the isovector and the non spherical components of $U$ affect proton scattering.

## Key claim

The local microscopic optical potentials derived from the Melbourne \(g\)-matrix folding model reproduce experimental data for polarized proton scattering at 65 MeV systematically, but the agreement is not perfect for light targets such as \(^{4}\)He and \(^{6}\)He; for \(^{6}\)He a potential search required a large enhancement of the imaginary spin-orbit part \(W_{\rm LS}\) (by a factor of 4.0) to fit the vector analyzing power, and the origin of this enhancement remains an open question.

## Method

The authors used the local-potential version of the Melbourne \(g\)-matrix folding model with the Brieva-Rook approximation to derive local microscopic optical potentials for polarized proton scattering at 65 MeV (and briefly at 200 MeV). Target densities were obtained from spherical Hartree-Fock calculations with the Gogny-D1S interaction for all nuclei, supplemented by phenomenological densities from electron scattering for stable nuclei with \(A \le 40\) and by antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) plus AMD-RGM for neutron-rich Ne isotopes. The resulting local potentials were compared with experimental elastic cross sections and vector analyzing powers for targets ranging from \(^{4}\)He to \(^{208}\)Pb, including unstable nuclei \(^{6}\)He and \(^{20-32}\)Ne.

## Key numbers

- Reaction cross sections \(\sigma_{\rm R}\) for proton scattering at \(E \approx 65\) MeV were reproduced within 10% error across the mass range \(A = 4\)–\(208\).
- For \(p+^{6}\)He scattering at 71 MeV, the best-fit potential search yielded parameters \((f_V^{\rm CE}, f_W^{\rm CE}, f_{V}^{\rm LS}, f_{W}^{\rm LS}) = (0.7, 0.7, 0.9, 4.0)\).
- The rms radius of \(^{6}\)He is enlarged from the stable-nucleus line by approximately 18% for \(V_{\rm CE}\), 25% for \(W_{\rm CE}\), and 12% for \(V_{\rm LS}\) at 65 MeV.
- For \(p+^{4}\)He scattering at 65 MeV, a parity-dependent potential with parameter \(\alpha = 0.13\) was needed to fit backward-angle data.

## Context

This work extends the Melbourne \(g\)-matrix folding model, previously successful for stable nuclei (Amos, 2000) and for \(^{12}\)C+\(^{12}\)C and Ne+\(^{12}\)C scattering (Minomo, 2011; 2012), to polarized proton scattering on both stable and unstable targets. The local-potential version enables systematic comparison with phenomenological global potentials such as the Koning-Delaroche (2003) and Dirac phenomenology potentials. The results enable predictions for planned measurements at the Radioactive Isotope Beam Factory (RIBF), particularly for \(^{6}\)He and neutron-rich Ne isotopes, while the need for an enhanced \(W_{\rm LS}\) in \(^{6}\)He raises a question about missing dynamics in the folding model.
