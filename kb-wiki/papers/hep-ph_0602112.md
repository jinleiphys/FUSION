# K* photoproduction off the nucleon: gamma N --> K* Lambda

> We study the photoproduction of $K^*(892)$ vector meson from both the charged and neutral reactions, $γp \to K^{*+} Λ$ and $γn \to K^{*0} Λ$. The production mechanisms that we consider include $t$-channel $K^*$, $K$, $κ$ exchanges, s-channel nucleon diagram, and $u$-channel $Λ$, $Σ$, $Σ^*$ diagrams. These could constitute important backgrounds for future investigation of ``missing'' resonances that can be searched for especially in these reactions. The $t$-channel $K$ meson exchange is found to dominate both reactions. The total and differential cross sections are presented together with some spin asymmetries.

# K* photoproduction off the nucleon: gamma N → K* Lambda

## Key claim

The t-channel kaon exchange dominates both charged and neutral K* photoproduction reactions, but the authors note that this conclusion depends on suppressing the K* vector meson exchange contribution with a soft form factor (Λ_K* = 0.9 GeV) because the K* exchange alone would produce an increasing total cross section with energy that is inconsistent with experimental data from the CLAS Collaboration.

## Method

The authors compute tree-level diagrams for γp → K*+ Λ and γn → K*0 Λ using effective Lagrangians, including t-channel K*, K, and κ meson exchanges, s-channel nucleon exchange, u-channel Λ, Σ, and Σ* hyperon exchanges, and a contact diagram for the charged reaction. Coupling constants are taken from the Nijmegen potential (NSC97a and NSC97f) and SU(3) flavor symmetry relations, with form factors applied to dress the vertices and a common form factor prescription used to preserve gauge invariance for the charged reaction.

## Key numbers

- g_K*NΛ = -4.26 (NSC97a) or -6.11 (NSC97f), with tensor coupling κ_K*NΛ = 2.66 (NSC97a) or 2.43 (NSC97f)
- g_γKK*^0 = -0.388 GeV^-1 for neutral K* production, g_γKK*^c = 0.254 GeV^-1 for charged K* production
- g_KNΛ = -13.24, obtained from SU(3) with f = 0.365 and g_πNN^2/4π = 14.0
- Γ(K* → Kγ) decay width Γ_K* = 50.8 MeV used in propagator
- Γ(Σ* → Λγ) = 479 ± 120 +81/-100 keV from CLAS experiment, with E2/M1 ratio R_EM = -2.0% from chiral quark model
- Cutoff parameters: Λ_K* = 0.9 GeV, Λ_K = Λ_κ = 1.1 GeV, s- and u-channel form factors Λ = 0.9 GeV
- Cross section for neutral K* production is larger than charged due to ratio |g_γKK*^0/g_γKK*^c| ≈ 1.53

## Context

This work extends the limited theoretical studies of K* photoproduction, following earlier work by Zhao et al. (2001) on K*Σ photoproduction using a quark model, and is motivated by preliminary CLAS data from the Jefferson Lab (Halyo 2005, Guo 2006) showing that K* photoproduction cross sections are not as suppressed as previously thought. The calculation provides background mechanisms for future searches of "missing" nucleon resonances predicted by quark models (Capstick and Roberts 2000) that have small couplings to πN but may couple strongly to the K* channel. The authors note that the scalar κ meson exchange, which is prohibited in kaon photoproduction, is suppressed in this reaction and would be difficult to identify experimentally.
