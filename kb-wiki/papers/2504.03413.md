# Exact Wigner function for chiral spirals

> The exact solution of the Dirac equation for fermions coupled to an external periodic chiral condensate (chiral spiral) is used to obtain the exact formula for the Wigner function (up to the quantum loop corrections). We find that the resulting expressions for various coefficients of the Wigner function exhibit properties that cannot be reproduced within the standard semiclassical expansion. The formula for the axial vector component of the Wigner function can be conveniently used to study spin polarization effects and illustrate connections between the spin density matrix and axial current. In particular, we find that during an adiabatic change of the periodic potential into a uniform one, the polarization vector is twisted from its original direction.

## Key claim

The paper derives an exact (up to quantum loop corrections) Wigner function for Dirac fermions coupled to an external periodic chiral condensate (chiral spiral), and shows that the resulting coefficients exhibit properties that cannot be reproduced by the standard semiclassical expansion in the Planck constant and gradients.

## Method

The authors solve the Dirac equation exactly for fermions coupled to external scalar and pseudoscalar fields of the form \(M \cos(\mathbf{q}\cdot\mathbf{x}/\hbar)\) and \(M \sin(\mathbf{q}\cdot\mathbf{x}/\hbar)\), obtaining analytic expressions for the Dirac spinors and their eigenvalues. Using these spinors, they perform second quantization and compute the Wigner function as the Fourier transform of the expectation value of two field operators, assuming distribution functions diagonal in momentum and spin space with a spin chemical potential \(\mu_{\mathfrak{s}}\).

## Key numbers

* The positive-energy eigenvalues for the two spin states are \(E_{\mathbf{p}}^{(r)} = \sqrt{(E_{\mathbf{p}}^{\parallel} + (-1)^{r-1} q/2)^2 + p_\perp^2}\) for \(r=1,2\), where \(E_{\mathbf{p}}^{\parallel} = \sqrt{M^2 + (p^3)^2}\) and \(p_\perp = \sqrt{(p^1)^2 + (p^2)^2}\).
* The spinors at rest (\(\mathbf{p} \to 0\)) yield \(\chi^{(1)}_+ = -\sgn(\cos\theta)\sqrt{2M+q}\,(0,1,0,0)^T\) and \(\chi^{(2)}_+ = e^{-i\phi}\sqrt{|2M-q|}\,(1,0,0,0)^T\), identifying \(r=1\) as spin-down and \(r=2\) as spin-up along the \(z\)-axis.
* In the limit \(q \to 0\) (uniform condensate), the spinors reduce to linear combinations of standard free Dirac spinors, indicating a twist of the original polarization direction.

## Context

This work extends the exact treatment of the Wigner function beyond the free Dirac field case studied by PhysRevC.94.024904, providing the first exact solution for a nontrivial interacting system. It contradicts the applicability of the standard semiclassical expansion (Florkowski 1997, Weickgenannt 2019) for systems with periodic chiral condensates, showing that such expansions cannot reproduce the exact results. The findings enable detailed studies of spin polarization effects in heavy-ion collisions and neutron stars, building on earlier work by Dautry (1979), Banerjee (1981), Kutschera (1990), and Broniowski (1990) on inhomogeneous condensates and their polarization properties.
