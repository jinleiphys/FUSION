# Nucleons and parity doubling across the deconfinement transition

> It is expected that nucleons and their parity partners become degenerate when chiral symmetry is restored. We investigate this question in the context of the thermal transition from the hadronic phase to the quark-gluon plasma, using lattice QCD simulations with N_f=2+1 flavours. We observe a clear sign of parity doubling in the quark-gluon plasma. Besides, we find that the nucleon ground state is, within the uncertainty, largely independent of the temperature, whereas temperature effects are substantial in the negative-parity (N^*) channel, already in the confined phase.

## Key claim
Using lattice QCD simulations with N_f=2+1 dynamical quarks, the authors observe clear parity doubling (degeneracy between positive- and negative-parity nucleon channels) in the quark-gluon plasma, but note that the nucleon ground state mass is largely temperature-independent within uncertainty, while the negative-parity N* channel shows substantial temperature effects already in the confined phase.

## Method
The study uses anisotropic lattice QCD with a Symanzik-improved gauge action and tadpole-improved Wilson-clover fermion action, with a spatial lattice spacing a_s=0.1227(8) fm and anisotropy a_s/a_tau=3.5. Nucleon correlation functions are computed using Gaussian-smeared sources and sinks, with the positive- and negative-parity channels extracted from the same correlator via the exponential Ansatz G_±(tau) = A_+ e^{-m_+ tau} + A_- e^{-m_- (1/T - tau)}. The analysis covers eight temperatures from T=44 MeV to T=352 MeV, corresponding to T/T_c from 0.24 to 1.90.

## Key numbers
- Pion mass M_pi = 384(4) MeV, with M_pi/M_rho = 0.466(3) at zero temperature
- At the lowest temperature (T=44 MeV, T/T_c=0.24): positive-parity nucleon mass m_+ = 1.20(3) GeV, negative-parity mass m_- = 1.9(3) GeV, with mass splitting parameter Delta = (m_- - m_+)/(m_- + m_+) = 0.209(28)(082)
- At T/T_c=0.95: m_+ = 1.12(14) GeV, m_- = 1.3(2) GeV, Delta = 0.052(35)(190)
- In nature at T=0, Delta = 0.241; parity doubling corresponds to Delta = 0
- The pseudo-critical temperature T_c is determined via the renormalised Polyakov loop; the larger T_c than expected is primarily due to light quarks being heavier than in nature

## Context
This work extends earlier quenched lattice QCD studies of parity doubling by Datta (2012) to full QCD with N_f=2+1 dynamical quarks, and complements extensive mesonic-sector studies of chiral symmetry restoration by Rapp (1999), Karsch (2003), and Ding (2012). The results enable comparison with heavy-ion collision phenomenology, where the temperature independence of the nucleon mass justifies treating protons as unmodified by the thermal medium, while the temperature-dependent N* mass may affect model predictions. The authors note that repeating the analysis with chirally symmetric fermions (e.g., domain-wall fermions as used by Sasaki (2001) at zero temperature) would be valuable, especially in the chiral limit.
