# Neutron-proton effective mass splitting in neutron-rich matter and its impacts on nuclear reactions

> The neutron-proton effective mass splitting in neutron-rich nucleonic matter reflects the space-time nonlocality of the isovector nuclear interaction. It affects the neutron/proton ratio during the earlier evolution of the Universe, cooling of protoneutron stars, structure of rare isotopes and dynamics of heavy-ion collisions. While there is still no consensus on whether the neutron-proton effective mass splitting is negative, zero or positive and how it depends on the density as well as the isospin-asymmetry of the medium, significant progress has been made in recent yeas in addressing these issues. We first recall the connections among the neutron-proton effective mass splitting, the momentum dependence of the isovector potential and the density dependence of the symmetry energy. We then make a few observations about the progress in calculating the neutron-proton effective mass splitting using various nuclear many-body theories and its effects on the isospin-dependence of in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections. Perhaps, our most reliable knowledge so far about the neutron-proton effective mass splitting at saturation density of nuclear matter comes from optical model analyses of huge sets of nucleon-nucleus scattering data accumulated over the last five decades. The momentum dependence of the symmetry potential from these analyses provide a useful boundary condition at saturation density for calibrating nuclear many-body calculations. Several observables in heavy-ion collisions have been identified as sensitive probes of the neutron-proton effective mass splitting in dense neutron-rich matter based on transport model simulations. We review these observables and comment on the latest experimental findings.

## Key claim

The neutron-proton effective mass splitting at saturation density is positive, with a value of \(m^{*}_{n-p} = (0.41 \pm 0.15)\delta\), based on the most comprehensive optical model analysis of nucleon-nucleus scattering data; however, there remains no consensus on the sign or magnitude of the splitting at supra-saturation densities, where different many-body theories predict widely varying results.

## Method

The authors review connections among the neutron-proton effective mass splitting, the momentum dependence of the isovector (symmetry) potential, and the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy, using the Hugenholtz-Van Hove theorem and expansions of the single-nucleon potential in isospin asymmetry. They summarize results from optical model analyses of 2249 data sets of reaction and angular differential cross sections for neutron and proton scattering on 234 targets at beam energies from 0.05 to 200 MeV, extracted from the EXFOR database. They also survey predictions from microscopic many-body theories (Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock, non-relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock, chiral perturbation theory) and phenomenological approaches (Skyrme-Hartree-Fock, relativistic mean-field, Gogny-Hartree-Fock) and compare transport model simulations of heavy-ion collision observables sensitive to the effective mass splitting.

## Key numbers

- Neutron-proton effective mass splitting at saturation density: \(m^{*}_{n-p} = (0.41 \pm 0.15)\delta\) from global optical model analysis of nucleon-nucleus scattering data (Li et al., 2014).
- Isoscalar effective mass at saturation density: \(m^{*}_0/m = 0.65 \pm 0.06\) from the same optical model analysis.
- Symmetry energy at saturation density: \(E_{\text{sym}}(\rho_0) = 31\) MeV (used as a well-determined input).
- Fermi energy at saturation density: \(E_F(\rho_0) = 36\) MeV (used as input).
- Isoscalar effective mass ratio: \(m_0^*/m = 0.7\) (used as input).
- Global average of the density slope of symmetry energy at saturation density: \(L(\rho_0) = 58.9 \pm 16\) MeV from 28 analyses of terrestrial experiments and astrophysical observations (2013).
- Partial derivatives at Fermi momentum from optical model analysis: \(-dU_{\text{sym},1}/dk = 30.44\), \(-k_F/3 \cdot d^2U_0/dk^2 = -12.88\), and \(1/3 \cdot dU_0/dk = 10.17\) (all in appropriate units at \(k_F\)).

## Context

This review extends earlier work by Li et al. (2004, 2005, 2013) and Chen et al. (2005, 2010) on the momentum dependence of the symmetry potential and its relation to the effective mass splitting. It contrasts predictions from microscopic many-body theories such as Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (Fuchs et al., 2005; Rong et al., 2006) and Gogny-Hartree-Fock with different interactions (D1, D1S, D1N, D1M) against the empirical constraint from optical model analyses (Li et al., 2014). The review enables future calibration of nuclear many-body calculations at supra-saturation densities using the saturation-density boundary condition and motivates further transport model analyses of heavy-ion collision data, particularly from rare isotope beam facilities, to resolve the sign and magnitude of the splitting at higher densities.
