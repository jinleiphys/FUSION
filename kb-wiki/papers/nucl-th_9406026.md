# Photons from axial-vector radiative decay in a hadron gas

> Strange and non-strange axial-vector meson radiative decays contribute to photon production in hadron gas. One- and two-hadron radiative decay modes of $b_{1}(1235)$, $a_{1}(1260)$ and $K_{1}(1270)$ are studied. At 200 MeV temperature and for a narrow range in photon energies they contribute more to the net thermal photon production rate than $πρ\rightarrow πγ$, $ππ\rightarrow ργ$ or $ρ\rightarrowππγ$. They provide significant contribution to the rate for photon energies as high as 1.5--2.0 GeV. For higher energies they are less important.

# Photons from axial-vector radiative decay in a hadron gas

## Key claim
Strange and non-strange axial-vector meson radiative decays (specifically $b_1(1235)$, $a_1(1260)$ and $K_1(1270)$) contribute more to the thermal photon production rate than the dominant hadronic scattering processes $\pi\rho \rightarrow \pi\gamma$, $\pi\pi \rightarrow \rho\gamma$ and $\rho \rightarrow \pi\pi\gamma$, but only for a narrow range of photon energies (approximately 0.4–0.75 GeV) and at temperatures near 200 MeV; for higher photon energies they become less important.

## Method
The author computed thermal photon production rates from one-hadron radiative decays ($a_1 \rightarrow \pi\gamma$, $K_1 \rightarrow K\gamma$) and two-hadron radiative decays ($b_1 \rightarrow \pi\pi^0\gamma$) using effective Lagrangians for $AV\phi$ and $VV'\phi$ three-point vertices, with coupling constants fixed by matching known partial decay widths from the Review of Particle Properties. The thermal rate was obtained by integrating the squared matrix elements over phase space with Bose-Einstein distributions at temperatures of 100, 150 and 200 MeV, and the effect of collisional broadening of the $\omega$ meson in the $b_1$ decay chain was estimated by adding an energy-dependent collisional width computed from $\pi\omega$ scattering through the $b_1$ resonance.

## Key numbers
- At $T = 200$ MeV, the peak thermal photon production rate from $b_1 \rightarrow \pi\pi^0\gamma$ is seven times larger than from $a_1 \rightarrow \pi\gamma$ or $K_1 \rightarrow K\gamma$.
- The product $\Gamma_{b_1\rightarrow\pi\omega} \Gamma_{\omega\rightarrow\pi^0\gamma} / \Gamma_{\omega}^{\rm full} = 13.2$ MeV, which is a factor 18 larger than the simple $\omega \rightarrow \pi^0\gamma$ decay width of 0.7 MeV.
- At $T = 200$ MeV, the equilibrium number densities are: $2.9\times10^{-1}$ $\pi$'s per fm$^3$, $1.5\times10^{-1}$ $\rho$'s per fm$^3$, $1.3\times10^{-1}$ $K^*$'s per fm$^3$, $2.9\times10^{-2}$ $K_1(1270)$'s per fm$^3$, and $2.6\times10^{-2}$ $b_1(1235)$'s per fm$^3$.
- The collisional width of the $\omega$ meson is of order 30 MeV at $T = 200$ MeV, compared to the vacuum width of 8.43 MeV.
- The mean free path of the $\omega$ in hot matter is 3.5, 1.2 and 0.7 fermis at $T = 100$, 150 and 200 MeV, respectively.
- The axial-vector radiative decays contribute more than scattering processes for photon energies in the range 0.4–0.75 GeV at $T = 200$ MeV.

## Context
This work extends the study of photon production in hadron gas beyond the standard processes considered by Kapusta, Lichard and Seibert (1991) and the $a_1$ resonance effects in $\pi\rho \rightarrow \pi\gamma$ scattering studied by Xiong, Shuryak and Brown (1992) and Song (1993). The author shows that the $b_1(1235)$ meson, which decays predominantly to $\pi\omega$ with a full width of 155 $\pm$ 8 MeV, provides a significant additional photon source through its two-pion radiative decay chain. This enables more complete calculations of thermal photon spectra from heavy-ion collisions, particularly for photon energies around 0.5 GeV, and raises questions about the role of axial-vector mesons in dilepton production at intermediate invariant masses.
