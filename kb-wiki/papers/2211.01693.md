# Study on $Λnn$ Bound State and Resonance

> We perform the ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) calculation to investigate the bound state problem of the three-body $Λnn$ system in chiral next-to-next-to-leading-order NN and chiral leading-order YN interactions. The calculations show that no $Λnn$ bound state exists, but predict a low-lying $Λnn$ resonant state near the threshold with the energy of $E_r= 0.124$ MeV and the width of about $Γ=1.161$ MeV. In searching for $Λnn$ resonances, we extend the NCSM calculation to the continuum state by employing the J-matrix formalism in the scattering theory with the hyperspherical oscillator basis.

## Key claim

The paper finds that no bound state of the Λnn system exists, but predicts a low-lying resonant state near threshold with energy Er = 0.124 MeV and width Γ = 1.161 MeV, based on ab initio no-core shell model calculations using chiral NN and YN interactions without tuning the potential strengths.

## Method

The authors performed ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations for the Λnn system (Jπ = 1/2+, T = 1) in a Jacobi coordinate harmonic oscillator basis, using chiral next-to-next-to-leading-order NN interactions (from the NNLOsim family) and chiral leading-order YN interactions with full ΛN-ΣN coupling. To search for resonances, they extended the NCSM calculation to the continuum by applying the SS-HORSE (single-state harmonic oscillator representation of scattering equations) formalism with a hyperspherical oscillator basis, treating the system as a true three-body (3→3) democratic decay.

## Key numbers

- No bound state of the Λnn system was found across model space truncations Nmax ≤ 36 and HO frequencies ℏω = 1–40 MeV.
- The predicted Λnn resonant state has energy Er = 0.124 MeV and width Γ = 1.161 MeV, extracted from parametrization of the scattering amplitude.
- The potential scattering phase shift δ0(E) was parametrized with coefficients a0 = 1.856, a2 = −0.014 MeV⁻¹, and a4 = 2.959 × 10⁻⁴ MeV⁻².

## Context

This work extends prior theoretical studies that consistently found no Λnn bound state, including variational calculations by Dalitz and Downs (1959), Faddeev calculations by Garcilazo (1987) and others, hyperspherical harmonics calculations by Belyaev (2008), and pionless effective field theory studies by Ando (2015) and Schafer (2020). The result contradicts the HypHI collaboration's claim (Rappold, 2013) of evidence for a bound Λnn state. The predicted resonance parameters agree with those of Filikhin (2016) and Gibson (2019), and lie within the range estimated by Schafer (2021). The work enables future comparison with the ongoing JLab experiment E12-17-003 (Tang, 2018), which may constrain Λn interactions.
