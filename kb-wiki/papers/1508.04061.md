# Vector meson spectral function and dilepton rate in the presence of strong entanglement effect between the chiral and the Polyakov loop dynamics

> In this work we have re-explored our earlier study on the vector meson spectral function and its spectral property in the form of dilepton rate in a two-flavour Polyakov loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model in presence of a strong entanglement between the chiral and Polyakov loop dynamics. The entanglement considered here is generated through the four-quark scalar type interaction in which the coupling strength depends on the Polyakov loop and runs with temperature and chemical potential. The entanglement effect is also considered for the four-quark vector type interaction in the same manner. We observe that the entanglement effect relatively enhances the color degrees of freedom due to the running of the both scalar and vector couplings. This modifies the vector meson spectral function and thus the spectral property such as the dilepton production rate in low invariant mass also gets modified.

## Key claim

The entanglement effect between chiral and Polyakov loop dynamics, implemented through Polyakov-loop-dependent four-quark couplings, modifies the vector meson spectral function and dilepton production rate at low invariant mass by relatively enhancing color degrees of freedom, but the magnitude of this modification depends on temperature and chemical potential and is more prominent at lower temperatures.

## Method

The authors use a two-flavor Polyakov loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model with an additional strong entanglement interaction (EPNJL model) where both the scalar and vector four-quark coupling strengths depend on the Polyakov loop field through the forms \(\tilde{G}_S(\Phi)=G_S[1-\alpha_1\Phi\bar\Phi-\alpha_2(\Phi^3+\bar\Phi^3)]\) and \(\tilde{G}_V(\Phi)=G_V[1-\alpha_1\Phi\bar\Phi-\alpha_2(\Phi^3+\bar\Phi^3)]\). The thermodynamic potential is minimized self-consistently with respect to the mean fields (chiral condensate \(\sigma\), Polyakov loop \(\Phi\), and quark number density \(n\)), and the resummed vector meson spectral function is computed within ring approximation using the one-loop self-energies from Islam (2014).

## Key numbers

- The chiral and deconfinement transition temperatures coincide at approximately 184 MeV in the EPNJL model at zero chemical potential, within the two-flavor lattice QCD range of \(T_c = (173 \pm 8)\) MeV from Karsch (2001) and Karsch (2000).
- For \(\mu = 150\) MeV in EPNJL with \(G_V = 0\), \(T_\sigma = 167\) MeV, \(T_\Phi = 166\) MeV, and \(T_{\bar\Phi} = 160\) MeV; the average deconfinement temperature is 163 MeV.
- For \(\mu = 200\) MeV in EPNJL with \(G_V = 0\), \(T_\sigma = 153\) MeV, \(T_\Phi = 153\) MeV, and \(T_{\bar\Phi} = 151\) MeV.
- With vector interaction at \(\mu = 150\) MeV and \(x = 0.8\) (where \(G_V = x \times G_S\)), \(T_\sigma = 175\) MeV and the average deconfinement temperature is 164 MeV.
- The entanglement parameters are fixed as \((\alpha_1, \alpha_2) = (0.1, 0.1)\), with a tolerance of \(\pm 0.05\).
- The spectral function and dilepton rate are computed at \(T = 0.25\) GeV for various chemical potentials (0 and 0.2 GeV) and three-momenta (0 and 0.2 GeV).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier study in Islam (2014) on vector meson spectral functions in the PNJL model by incorporating the strong entanglement effect between chiral and Polyakov loop dynamics, originally proposed by Sakai (2010) for the scalar channel and generalized by Sugano (2014) to the vector channel. The EPNJL model used here differs from Sakai (2010) in the choice of Polyakov loop potential (from Ratti (2005) rather than Roessner (2006)), leading to different values of the entanglement parameters. The results enable more realistic modeling of dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions by accounting for the running of couplings with temperature and chemical potential, and they suggest that the entanglement effect reduces dilepton yields compared to the standard PNJL model while still exceeding the leading-order Born rate.
