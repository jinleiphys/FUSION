# 3+1D hydrodynamic simulation of relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We present MUSIC, an implementation of the Kurganov-Tadmor algorithm for relativistic 3+1 dimensional fluid dynamics in heavy-ion collision scenarios. This Riemann-solver-free, second-order, high-resolution scheme is characterized by a very small numerical viscosity and its ability to treat shocks and discontinuities very well. We also incorporate a sophisticated algorithm for the determination of the freeze-out surface using a three dimensional triangulation of the hyper-surface. Implementing a recent lattice based equation of state, we compute p_T-spectra and pseudorapidity distributions for Au+Au collisions at root s = 200 GeV and present results for the anisotropic flow coefficients v_2 and v_4 as a function of both p_T and pseudorapidity. We were able to determine v_4 with high numerical precision, finding that it does not strongly depend on the choice of initial condition or equation of state.

## Key claim

The paper presents MUSIC, a new 3+1D ideal hydrodynamic simulation for relativistic heavy-ion collisions, and claims that the anisotropic flow coefficient \(v_4\) can be determined with high numerical precision and does not strongly depend on the choice of initial condition or equation of state, but this conclusion is qualified by the demonstration that \(v_4\) is highly sensitive to discretization errors which must be well controlled with fine enough lattices.

## Method

The authors implement the Kurganov-Tadmor (KT) algorithm, a Riemann-solver-free, second-order, high-resolution central difference scheme with small numerical viscosity, to solve the conservation equations for energy, momentum, and net baryon current in a \((\tau, \eta_s, x, y)\) coordinate system. The code uses a piecewise linear reconstruction with a minmod flux limiter, Heun's rule for time integration, and a three-dimensional triangulation of the freeze-out hyper-surface to compute particle spectra via the Cooper-Frye formula. Initial conditions are set using a Glauber model with a mixture of wounded-nucleon and binary-collision scaling, and two equations of state are employed: a modeled EOS-Q with a first-order phase transition and a lattice-based EOS-L (parametrization s95p-v1).

## Key numbers

- Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 200\,{\rm GeV}\) are simulated.
- The typical time step size is of the order of \(0.01\,{\rm fm}/c\).
- Energy conservation is fulfilled to better than 1 part in 30,000 per time step.
- For the Woods-Saxon parametrization of Au nuclei: \(R = 6.38\,{\rm fm}\), \(d = 0.535\,{\rm fm}\), and \(\rho_0 = 0.17\,{\rm fm}^{-3}\).
- The critical temperature for EOS-Q is \(T_{\rm crit} = 164\,{\rm MeV}\), with a bag constant \(B^{1/4} = 230\,{\rm MeV}\).
- For EOS-L, the fit to lattice data was done above \(T = 250\,{\rm MeV}\), and the entropy density was constrained at \(T = 800\,{\rm MeV}\) to be 95% of the Stefan-Boltzmann value.
- The hadron resonance gas sums over all resonance states up to \(2\,{\rm GeV}\).

## Context

This work extends prior 2+1D ideal hydrodynamic calculations (e.g., Kolb et al., 2000; Huovinen et al., 2001) by adding full three-dimensional longitudinal dynamics, and it provides an independent implementation alongside other 3+1D codes such as those by Hirano (2001) and Nonaka (2006). The modular code is designed to be coupled with jet quenching models like MARTINI (Schenke, 2009) and enables future studies of viscous effects and the rapidity dependence of transverse dynamics.
