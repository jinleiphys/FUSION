# Lifetime estimations from RHIC Au+Au data

> We discuss a recently found family of exact and analytic, finite and accelerating, 1+1 dimensional solutions of perfect fluid relativistic hydrodynamics to describe the pseudorapidity densities and longitudinal HBT-radii and to estimate the lifetime parameter and the initial energy density of the expanding fireball in Au+Au collisions at RHIC with $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=130$ GeV and $200$ GeV colliding energies. From these exact solutions of relativistic hydrodynamics, we derive a simple and powerful formula to describe the pseudorapidity density distributions in high energy proton-proton and heavy ion collisions, and derive the scaling of the longitudinal HBT radius parameter as a function of the pseudorapidity density. We improve upon several oversimplifications in Bjorken's famous initial energy density estimate, and apply our results to estimate the initial energy densities of high energy reactions with data-driven pseudorapidity distributions. When compared to similar estimates at the LHC energies, our results indicate a surprising and non-monotonic dependence of the initial energy density on the energy of heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The authors claim that by applying a recently discovered family of exact, analytic, finite, and accelerating 1+1 dimensional solutions of perfect fluid relativistic hydrodynamics (the CKCJ solution) to RHIC Au+Au data, they can estimate the lifetime parameter and initial energy density of the expanding fireball, with the caveat that the initial proper time dependence of the initial energy density remains an explicit free parameter.

## Method

The authors embed the 1+1 dimensional CKCJ exact solution of perfect fluid hydrodynamics into 1+3 dimensional space-time and derive analytic expressions for the pseudorapidity density and longitudinal HBT radii. They perform simultaneous fits to the pseudorapidity density distributions and longitudinal HBT radius data from Au+Au collisions at RHIC at sqrt(s_NN) = 130 GeV and 200 GeV, using a temperature-independent average speed of sound of 0.35 from PHENIX measurements. From these fits, they extract the acceleration parameter, effective temperature, and fireball lifetime, then use a new exact formula for initial energy density that improves upon Bjorken's (1982) estimate.

## Key numbers

* The average speed of sound used is 0.35 +/- 0.05, corresponding to kappa approximately 10 (+1/-3), taken from a PHENIX measurement (Adare, 2006).
* The lattice QCD equation of state corresponds to kappa approximately 4 +/- 1 according to Fig. 2 of Csanad (2012).
* The pseudorapidity density distribution in the Gaussian approximation depends on three parameters: normalization dN/dy at y=0, width parameter Delta^2 y, and dimensionless dip parameter D^2 = m^2/M^2.
* The rapidity distribution width is given by 1/Delta^2 y = (lambda - 1)^2 * [1 + (1 - 1/kappa) * (1/2 + m/T_eff)].
* The average transverse mass is approximated as m_T(y) approximately m + T_eff / cosh^{alpha(kappa,lambda)}(y / alpha(1,lambda)).

## Context

This work extends the CKCJ solution series (Csorgo, Kasza, Csanad, and Jiang, 2018) and is the fourth part of a manuscript series following Csorgo (2018), Kasza (2018), and Csorgo (2018). It improves upon Bjorken's (1982) initial energy density estimate by removing several oversimplifications, and applies the new method to RHIC data at 130 and 200 GeV. The results enable future comparisons with LHC energies, where the authors indicate a surprising non-monotonic dependence of initial energy density on collision energy.
