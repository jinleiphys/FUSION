# Dissipative fluid dynamics for the dilute Fermi gas at unitarity: Free expansion and rotation

> We investigate the expansion dynamics of a dilute Fermi gas at unitarity in the context of dissipative fluid dynamics. Our aim is to quantify the effects of shear viscosity on the time evolution of the system. We compare exact numerical solutions of the equations of viscous hydrodynamics to various approximations that have been proposed in the literature. Our main findings are: i) Shear viscosity leads to characteristic features in the expansion dynamics; ii) a quantitative description of these effects has to include reheating; iii) dissipative effects are not sensitive to the equation of state P(n,T) as long as the universal relation P=(2/3)E is satisfied; iv) the expansion dynamics mainly constrains the cloud average of the shear viscosity.

## Key claim

Shear viscosity produces characteristic features in the expansion dynamics of a dilute Fermi gas at unitarity, but a quantitative description requires including reheating effects, and the expansion dynamics mainly constrains the cloud average of the shear viscosity rather than its local value.

## Method

The author performed exact numerical solutions of the full equations of viscous hydrodynamics (continuity, Navier-Stokes, and energy conservation equations) for an expanding cloud of a dilute Fermi gas at unitarity, using the VH1 code with the PPMLR method modified to include viscous corrections. These numerical results were compared to two approximate analytic approaches: a scaling solution that conserves entropy by removing heat via a sink, and a linear-force model that includes reheating by assuming the shear viscosity is proportional to density and the velocity field remains linear in the coordinates.

## Key numbers

- The shear viscosity of a weakly interacting gas scales as $\eta\sim \bar{p}/\sigma$, and at unitarity the cross section is $\sigma=4\pi/k^2$ with $\sigma\simeq \frac{4\pi}{3} mT$ in a thermal gas, giving $\eta\simeq \frac{15}{32\sqrt{\pi}}(mT)^{3/2}$ for $T\gg T_c$.
- The ratio of trap-averaged shear viscosity to entropy density was found to have a minimum value of $\langle \eta\rangle/\langle s\rangle \simeq 0.5$ from damping of collective modes, and values as small as $\langle \eta\rangle/\langle s\rangle \simeq 0.2$ from expansion of a rotating cloud.
- For a Gaussian initial density profile with $E_0/E_F=1$ and $\bar\alpha_n=0.1$, the viscous correction to the crossing time $\delta \bar{t}_{\it cr}$ (defined by $A_R(t_{\it cr})\equiv 1$) is linear in $\bar\alpha_n$ but reduced by about a factor of 2 when reheating is included compared to the calculation without reheating.
- The entropy produced per particle scales asymptotically as $\Delta S/N \simeq 4\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^{1/3} \frac{\langle \bar\alpha_n\rangle}{(T_0/T_F)} \left(\omega_\perp t\right)^{1/3}$ in the model without reheating.

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses of dissipative effects in the unitary Fermi gas that used approximate solutions of viscous hydrodynamics, such as the entropy-conserving scaling approximation and moment methods used by Clancy (2007, 2008), Thomas (2009), and Cao (2010). The paper tests these approximations against full numerical solutions and finds that the linear-force model including reheating (proposed by Cao in 2010) accurately describes the dynamics for shear viscosity proportional to density. The results enable more reliable extraction of the shear viscosity from expansion experiments and show that the crossing time measurement constrains the trap-averaged shear viscosity rather than its local value.
