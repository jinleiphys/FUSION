# The three point asymmetric cumulants in high multiplicity pp collisions

> We study the influence of quantum interference and colour flow on three point correlations described by asymmetric cumulants in high multiplicity events in pp collisions. We use the model previously developed for the study of the collectivity in symmetric cumulants. We show that the resulting three point asymmetric cumulant is in qualitative agreement with the experimental data for the same parameters of the model as it was with the symmetric cumulants. Our results show that the initial state correlations must play a major role and may be even dominant in the explanation of the correlations in high multiplicity pp events.

## Key claim

The authors show that the three-point asymmetric cumulant calculated from their quantum interference and colour flow model is in qualitative agreement with experimental data for the same model parameters that previously described symmetric cumulants, but they note that there is not enough experimental data for detailed comparison.

## Method

The model treats each pp collision as N emitting sources (multiparton interactions) with colour charges in the adjoint representation, distributed in transverse space according to a Gaussian density with width parameter B. The authors calculate the three-point asymmetric cumulant ac_n{3} by summing contributions from two types of diagrams: a "tripole" diagram involving three sources and three off-diagonal gluons, and a "dipole with three off-diagonal gluons" diagram involving two sources. They then resum the leading contributions in the expansion parameters 1/(N_c^2-1) and 1/N for arbitrary multiplicity m and number of sources N, including diagrams with nonintersecting two-gluon dipoles.

## Key numbers

- The parameter B for the Gaussian source distribution takes values B = 1, 2, 4 GeV^{-2}, where B = 1 GeV^{-2} corresponds to the best fit for even symmetric cumulants in prior work.
- For the case N=m=3 with N_c=3 and equal momenta k_1=k_2=k_3=k, the tripole integral T_2 reaches a maximum value of approximately 0.04, while the 3-gluon dipole integral \tilde{T}_2 reaches approximately 0.07.
- The fourth harmonic integrals T_4 and \tilde{T}_4 are 4-5 times smaller than the corresponding second harmonic integrals, with maxima shifted to larger k values.
- For N=m=3 and N_c=3, the 3-gluon dipole diagram contributes approximately 90 percent of the total cumulant value.
- The cumulant ac_2 for N=m=3, N_c=3 with equal momenta shows maximum at k approximately 1/sqrt(B) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the QCD-based model developed by the same authors (Blok and Segev, prior work) for symmetric cumulants in high-multiplicity pp collisions to the case of three-point asymmetric cumulants recently measured experimentally (experimental reference in the paper). The model is based on multiparton interactions (MPI) theory and does not involve saturation effects, in contrast to the Colour Glass Condensate (CGC) approach. The results suggest that initial state correlations may play a dominant role in explaining correlations in high-multiplicity pp events, challenging final-state interaction explanations such as viscous fluid dynamics simulations.
