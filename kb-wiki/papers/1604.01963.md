# Constraining the equation of state of nuclear matter from competition of fusion and quasi-fission in the reactions leading to production of the superheavy elements

> The mechanism of fusion hindrance, an effect preventing the synthesis of superheavy elements in the reactions of cold and hot fusion, is investigated using the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck equation, where Coulomb interaction is introduced. A strong sensitivity is observed both to the modulus of incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter, controlling the competition of surface tension and Coulomb repulsion, and to the stiffness of the density-dependence of symmetry energy, influencing the formation of the neck prior to scission. The experimental fusion probabilities were for the first time used to derive constraints on the nuclear equation of state. A strict constraint on the modulus of incompressibility of nuclear matter $K_0 = 240 - 260$ MeV is obtained while the stiff density-dependences of the symmetry energy ($γ>1.$) are rejected.

# Constraining the equation of state of nuclear matter from competition of fusion and quasi-fission in the reactions leading to production of the superheavy elements

## Key claim

The competition between fusion and quasi-fission in reactions forming superheavy elements, simulated with the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) equation including Coulomb interaction, shows strong sensitivity to both the incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter and the stiffness of the density-dependence of symmetry energy. For the first time, experimental fusion probabilities are used to derive constraints on the nuclear equation of state, yielding a strict bound on the incompressibility modulus \(K_0 = 240-260\) MeV while rejecting stiff density-dependences of the symmetry energy (\(\gamma > 1.0\)).

## Method

The authors solve the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck equation with test particles, implementing a single-particle mean-field potential with an isospin-dependent symmetry energy term of the form \(U = a\rho + b\rho^\kappa + 2a_s(\rho/\rho_0)^\gamma \tau_z I\). Coulomb interaction is introduced by adding the summary force from the proton distribution outside each grid cell, avoiding divergence in infinite matter. Simulations are performed for central collisions (impact parameter 0.5 fm) at beam energy 5 AMeV for six representative reactions, using 600 test particles and 20 random sequences, with the exponent \(\kappa\) varied between 7/6 and 2 (incompressibilities 200-380 MeV) and \(\gamma\) varied between 0.5 and 1.5.

## Key numbers

- Modulus of incompressibility constrained to \(K_0 = 240 - 260\) MeV, based on simulations where maximum density reaches 1.4-1.5 times saturation density.
- Stiff density-dependences of the symmetry energy with \(\gamma > 1.0\) are rejected; allowed range is \(\gamma = 0.5 - 1.0\).
- For the reaction \(^{64}\text{Ni}+^{186}\text{W}\), fusion probability at central collisions is constrained between 40-80%, based on experimental mass distributions.
- For the reaction \(^{48}\text{Ca}+^{238}\text{U}\), fusion probability at central collisions is constrained between 20-50%.
- Simulations run up to 3000 fm/c, sufficient for final configuration formation in all cases.

## Context

This work extends prior transport-model studies of fusion hindrance using ImQMD (ImQMD) and time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) by employing the BUU equation with explicit Coulomb treatment. It contrasts with constraints from nuclear giant resonances (Youngblood 1999, Stone, Piekarewicz) and flow observables in relativistic collisions (Li et al.), where the equation-of-state effect is indirect or entangled with dissipation. The extracted constraints enable more precise predictions for superheavy element synthesis and highlight the need for additional experimental data near the onset of quasi-fission to further constrain the symmetry energy density dependence.
