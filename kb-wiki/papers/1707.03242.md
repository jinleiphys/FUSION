# Phenomenological and Theoretical Optical Potentials

> The optical potential is a powerful instrument for calculations on a wide variety of nuclear reactions, in particular, for quasi-elastic lepton-nucleus scattering. Phenomenological optical potentials are successful in the description of data but may produce uncertainties in the interpretation of the results. Two recent theoretical optical potentials are presented: a global relativistic folding optical potential, that has been employed in relativistic models for quasi-elastic lepton-nucleus scattering, and a non relativistic optical potential derived from nucleon-nucleon chiral potentials at fourth order (N4LO), that has been applied to elastic proton-nucleus scattering.

## Key claim

Phenomenological optical potentials are successful in describing data but produce uncertainties in the interpretation of results; two recently derived theoretical optical potentials, a global relativistic folding potential and a nonrelativistic chiral potential at N⁴LO, can reduce these uncertainties, though the chiral potential's applicability is limited to energies below about 200 MeV.

## Method

The global relativistic folding optical potential (GRFOP) for ¹²C was built by folding the Horowitz-Love-Franey (HLF) t-matrix with relativistic mean-field Lorentz densities via the tρ-approximation, spanning nucleon kinetic energies from 20 to 1040 MeV. The nonrelativistic chiral optical potential was derived from nucleon-nucleon chiral potentials at N³LO (two versions: Entem-Machleidt and Epelbaum-Glöckle-Meissner) using the spectator expansion of multiple-scattering theory in the optimum factorization approximation, with nuclear densities from a relativistic mean-field density-dependent meson-exchange model.

## Key numbers

- GRFOP energy range: 20 to 1040 MeV for elastic proton scattering on ¹²C
- Momentum transfer at QE peak for electron scattering comparisons: q ≈ 0.55 GeV/c
- Chiral potentials tested at laboratory energy 200 MeV for elastic proton scattering on ¹⁶O
- Chiral potential cutoff values tested: Λ = 450, 500, 550, and 600 MeV (EM and EGM versions)
- For energies larger than 200 MeV, agreement between chiral potential results and data worsens

## Context

This work extends the Green's Function model for quasi-elastic lepton-nucleus scattering, previously applied to electron (e,e') reactions and to CCQE and NCE neutrino scattering (MiniBooNE, Minerνa data), by replacing phenomenological optical potentials (such as EDAI and EDAD1 from Cooper-Hama-Charagi, 2009) with microscopically derived alternatives. The GRFOP reduces theoretical uncertainties in the relativistic Green's Function model, while the chiral potential approach tests the domain of applicability of chiral perturbation theory for constructing optical potentials, finding that lower cutoffs (450 MeV) fail at 200 MeV and that chiral potentials are not applicable above 200 MeV.
