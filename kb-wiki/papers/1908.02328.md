# Further tests of lepton flavour universality from the charged lepton energy distribution in $b\to c$ semileptonic decays: The case of $Λ_b\to Λ_c \ell \barν_\ell$

> In a general framework, valid for any $H\to H'\ell^-\barν_\ell$ semileptonic decay, we analyze the $d^2Γ/(dωd\cosθ_\ell)$ and $d^2Γ/ (dωdE_\ell)$ distributions, with $ω$ being the product of the hadron four-velocities, $θ_\ell$ the angle made by the three-momenta of the charged lepton and the final hadron in the $W^-$ center of mass frame and $E_\ell$ the charged lepton energy in the decaying hadron rest frame. Within the Standard Model (SM), $ d^2Γ/(dωdE_\ell)\propto \left(c_0(ω)+c_1(ω)E_\ell/M+c_2(ω)E^2_\ell/M^2\right)$, with $M$ the initial hadron mass. We find that $c_2(ω)$ is independent of the lepton flavor and thus it is an ideal candidate to look for lepton flavor universality (LFU) violations. We also find a correlation between the $a_2(ω)$ structure function, that governs the $(\cosθ_\ell)^2$ dependence of $d^2Γ/(dωd\cosθ_\ell)$, and $c_2(ω)$. Apart from trivial kinematical and mass factors, the ratio of $a_2(ω)/c_2(ω)$ is a universal function that can be measured in any semileptonic decay, involving not only $b\to c$ transitions. These two SM predictions can be used as new tests in the present search for signatures of LFU violations. We also generalize the formalism to account for some new physics (NP) terms. Finally, in order to illustrate our findings, we apply our general framework to the $Λ_b\to Λ_c \ell \barν_\ell$ decay. We show that a measurement of $c_2$ (or $a_2$) for $τ$ decay would not only be a direct measurement of the possible existence of NP, but it would also allow to distinguish from NP fits to $b\to cτ\barν_τ$ anomalies in the meson sector, that otherwise give the same total and differential $dΓ/dω$ widths.

# Further tests of lepton flavour universality from the charged lepton energy distribution in $b\to c$ semileptonic decays: The case of $\Lambda_b\to \Lambda_c \ell \bar\nu_\ell$

## Key claim

Within the Standard Model, the $c_2(\omega)$ structure function appearing in the charged lepton energy distribution $d^2\Gamma/(d\omega dE_\ell)$ is independent of the lepton flavor, and the ratio $a_2(\omega)/c_2(\omega)$ (up to kinematical factors) is a universal function for any semileptonic decay; these predictions can serve as new tests for lepton flavour universality violations, though left and right vector new physics contributions would not modify the $a_2/c_2$ ratio itself.

## Method

The authors derive general expressions for the double differential decay widths $d^2\Gamma/(d\omega d\cos\theta_\ell)$ and $d^2\Gamma/(d\omega dE_\ell)$ for any $H\to H'\ell^-\bar\nu_\ell$ semileptonic decay, expressing each in terms of three structure functions ($a_0,a_1,a_2$ and $c_0,c_1,c_2$ respectively). They then apply this formalism to the $\Lambda_b\to \Lambda_c \ell \bar\nu_\ell$ decay using lattice QCD form factors from Detmold (2015), and generalize the framework to include left and right scalar and vector new physics contributions following the effective Hamiltonian approach of Murgui (2019).

## Key numbers

- The $c_2(\omega)$ structure function is identical for all charged lepton flavors in the Standard Model, providing a new test of lepton flavour universality
- The ratio $M^2 a_2(\omega) / [M'^2 (1-m_\ell^2/q^2) c_2(\omega)] = (\omega^2-1)/4$ is a universal function valid for any $q\to q'\ell\bar\nu_\ell$ transition
- For the $\Lambda_b\to \Lambda_c$ decay, the Standard Model prediction for ${\cal R}_{\Lambda_c}$ is $0.33\pm 0.02$, while Fits 6 and 7 from Murgui (2019) give approximately $0.42$
- The ratio $(c_2)_{NP}/(c_2)_{SM}$ differs significantly between Fit 6 and Fit 7 of Murgui (2019), allowing discrimination between new physics scenarios that otherwise yield identical total and differential $d\Gamma/d\omega$ widths
- The Fermi coupling constant used is $G_F = 1.166 \times 10^{-5}$ GeV$^{-2}$
- From the LQCD form factors and measured branching fraction, $|V_{cb}| = 0.044 \pm 0.005$ is obtained

## Context

This work extends the study of lepton flavour universality violations in $b\to c$ transitions, which have been observed in ${\cal R}_{D^{(*)}}$ ratios with tensions up to 4.4 standard deviations (HFLAV, using BaBar, Belle, and LHCb data) and more recently reduced to 1.2 standard deviations by new Belle measurements. The authors introduce the charged lepton energy distribution in the decaying hadron rest frame as a new observable, which had not been previously considered for $\Lambda_b\to \Lambda_c$ or $B\to D^{(*)}$ decays. This complements existing work on angular distributions by Fajfer (2012), Murgui (2019), and Boer (2019), and the sum rule relating ${\cal R}_{\Lambda_c}$ to ${\cal R}_{D^{(*)}}$ found in earlier studies. The results enable discrimination between new physics scenarios that produce identical total decay widths, providing a method to identify the Lorentz structure of potential new physics contributions.
