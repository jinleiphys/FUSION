# How baryons appear in low-energy QCD: Domain-wall Skyrmion phase in strong magnetic fields

> Low-energy dynamics of QCD can be described by pion degrees of freedom in terms of the chiral perturbation theory(ChPT). A chiral soliton lattice(CSL), an array of solitons, is the ground state due to the chiral anomaly in the presence of a magnetic field larger than a certain critical value at finite density. Here, we show in a model-independent and fully analytic manner (at the leading order of ChPT) that the CSL phase transits to a {\it domain-wall Skyrmion phase} when the chemical potential is larger than the critical value $μ_{\rm c} = 16πf_π^2/3m_π \sim 1.03 \;\; {\rm GeV}$ with the pion's decay constant $f_π$ and mass $m_π$, which can be regarded as the nuclear saturation density. There spontaneously appear stable two-dimensional Skyrmions or lumps on a soliton surface, which can be viewed as three-dimensional Skyrmions carrying even baryon numbers from the bulk despite no Skyrme term. They behave as superconducting rings with persistent currents due to a charged pion condensation, and areas of the rings' interiors are quantized. This phase is in scope of future heavy-ion collider experiments.

## Key claim

The chiral soliton lattice (CSL) phase in QCD at finite baryon density under a strong magnetic field transitions to a domain-wall Skyrmion phase when the chemical potential exceeds a critical value μc = 16πfπ²/3mπ ∼ 1.03 GeV, which can be regarded as the nuclear saturation density; this result is obtained at leading order of chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) without requiring a Skyrme term, making it model-independent.

## Method

The authors used the moduli approximation to construct a low-energy effective field theory of a single non-Abelian sine-Gordon soliton (domain wall) from the leading-order ChPT Lagrangian including the Wess-Zumino-Witten term. They promoted the CP¹ moduli parameters of the soliton to fields on the 2+1-dimensional world volume, derived the effective Hamiltonian, and applied Bogomol'nyi-Prasad-Sommerfield (BPS) bounds to find stable lump (baby Skyrmion) solutions on the domain wall. They then included the U(1)EM gauge coupling and derived flux quantization conditions for the superconducting rings formed by charged pion condensation at the lump boundaries.

## Key numbers

- Critical chemical potential: μc = 16πfπ²/3mπ ∼ 1.03 GeV, using vacuum values fπ ≈ 93 MeV and mπ ≈ 140 MeV
- Critical magnetic field for CSL formation: Bc = 16πmπfπ²/(eμB)
- Tricritical point magnetic field: 3mπ²/e ∼ 0.06 GeV² ∼ 1.0 × 10¹⁹ G
- Baryon number quantization: NB = 2k, where k is the lump topological charge (integer), so baryon numbers are even integers
- Flux quantization condition: B SD = 2πk/e, where SD is the area of the superconducting ring interior and k is the lump number
- For axially symmetric k-lumps: |b0| = √(2k/eB) from flux quantization

## Context

This work extends the chiral soliton lattice framework of Son and Stephanov (2007) by including the full SU(2) structure of pions rather than only the neutral pion π0. It resolves the instability of CSLs via charged pion condensation found by Brauner (2017) by identifying the domain-wall Skyrmion phase as the fate of that instability. The result provides a model-independent description of baryons as topological solitons in low-energy QCD without the Skyrme term, and predicts a phase accessible to future heavy-ion collider experiments, as the required magnetic field of ∼10¹⁹ G is only about 10 times stronger than current capabilities.
