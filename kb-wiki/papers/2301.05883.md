# QED corrections to elastic electron-nucleus scattering beyond the first-order Born approximation

> A potential for the vertex and self-energy correction is derived from the first-order Born theory. The inclusion of this potential in the Dirac equation, together with the Uehling potential for vacuum polarization, allows for a nonperturbative treatment of these QED effects within the phase-shift analysis. Investigating the 12C and 208Pb targets, a considerable deviation of the respective cross-section change from the Born results is found, which becomes larger with increasing momentum transfer. Estimates for the correction to the beam-normal spin asymmetry are also provided. For the 12C nucleus, dispersion effects are considered as well.

# QED corrections to elastic electron-nucleus scattering beyond the first-order Born approximation

## Key claim

The vertex and self-energy correction can be incorporated into a nonperturbative phase-shift analysis by deriving a potential from the first-order Born amplitude, but the resulting cross-section changes deviate considerably from Born predictions, especially at high momentum transfer and for heavy nuclei; the commonly assumed factor-of-two magnitude for the vertex correction relative to vacuum polarization is shown to be an underestimate.

## Method

A potential for the vertex and self-energy correction was derived from the proportionality of the first-order Born vertex amplitude to the scattering amplitude, using the Fourier transform relation between potential and Born amplitude. This potential was added to the Dirac equation alongside the Uehling potential for vacuum polarization, and the resulting scattering states were obtained from a partial-wave phase-shift analysis using the RADIAL code, with matching points at approximately 2000 fm to accommodate the long-range nature of the QED potentials. Cross-section changes and spin-asymmetry modifications were computed for electron energies between 1 MeV and 240 MeV on 12C and 208Pb targets, and compared with Coulomb-distorted Born results.

## Key numbers

- For 56 MeV electrons on 12C, the combined nonperturbative QED cross-section change deviates from Born results by extremely small amounts (not quantified numerically in the text for this case).
- For 56 MeV electrons on 208Pb, the difference between the combined inclusion of Uehling and vertex potentials and the sum of separate treatments is up to 3% (compared to 1% for 12C at the same energy).
- The soft-bremsstrahlung contribution for 56 MeV electrons on 208Pb is approximately constant in angle, with strength increasing as the cutoff frequency ω0 is lowered according to the logarithmic dependence ln(2ω0/c^2).
- For 56 MeV electrons on 12C, the vacuum polarization contribution to the spin-asymmetry change dS is at most 1%, while the vertex and self-energy contribution is of opposite sign and considerably larger in magnitude, well beyond a factor of 2.
- For 56 MeV electrons on 208Pb at 173°, dS from QED effects is approximately -0.5% at 3.5 MeV and -0.9% at 5 MeV.
- The numerical accuracy of the QED cross-section changes is better than 0.5% at 56 MeV for 12C, deteriorating to 5% at 238 MeV for backmost angles; for 208Pb the accuracy is below 1% even at 150 MeV.
- The numerical accuracy of dS for 12C is up to 0.5% at 3 MeV, 3% at 10 MeV, and 10-15% at 56 MeV; for 208Pb it is less than 0.25% at 30 MeV and less than 1% at 56 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the method of Koshchii et al. (2021), who constructed an absorptive potential from the Born amplitude for dispersion effects, by applying the same procedure to generate a potential for the vertex and self-energy correction. It builds on prior treatments of vacuum polarization via the Uehling potential (Uehling, 1935; Salat and Mitter, 1988) and on first-order Born calculations of QED corrections by Tsai (1960) and Maximon and Tjon (2000). The nonperturbative approach enables consistent estimates of both cross-section modifications and spin-asymmetry changes for light and heavy nuclei, revealing that Coulomb distortion effects become significant for 208Pb even at low energies. The results enable improved comparisons with high-precision electron scattering experiments, such as those by Reuter et al. (1982) and Offermann et al. (1991) for 12C, and by Friedrich and Lenz (1972) for 208Pb, and provide input for accuracy tests of detectors in experiments like that of Grieser et al. (2020) using 5 MeV electrons on gold.
