# Comment on `Charged Particle Ratio Fluctuation as a Signal for Quark-Gluon Plasma' and `Fluctuation Probes of Quark Deconfinement'

## Key claim

The smallness of charge fluctuations, which has been proposed as an unambiguous signal of equilibrium quark-gluon plasma (QGP) production, can instead be explained by fluctuations generated at the very early collision stage when the energy of participating nucleons is released, before any equilibrium QGP is formed. The authors argue that if charge fluctuations are determined at this initial stage, the measure \( V(Q)/\langle N_{\rm ch} \rangle \) will be small regardless of whether QGP is subsequently produced.

## Method

The authors derive an estimate for the charge fluctuation measure \( V(Q)/\langle N_{\rm ch} \rangle \) under the assumption that fluctuations are created at the initial stage of the collision, when the number of charge carriers is much smaller than in the final state. They assume the initial fluctuations of electric charge in a rapidity window are due to Poissonian variation of the number of participating protons, leading to the expression \( \langle Z \rangle / \langle N_{\rm ch} \rangle \). They also discuss how using the alternative measure \( \frac{1}{4} \langle N_{\rm ch} \rangle V(N^+/N^-) \) further reduces the observed fluctuations because it removes contributions proportional to the produced entropy.

## Key numbers

- For central collisions at CERN SPS energy, the charged particle multiplicity per participating proton is about 8, based on data from the NA49 Collaboration (Appelshauser et al., 1999).
- The resulting value of the fluctuation measure \( V(Q)/\langle N_{\rm ch} \rangle \) from the initial-stage estimate is significantly smaller than unity, comparable to the 2 to 3 times reduction predicted for equilibrium QGP by Jeon and Koch (2000) and Asakawa, Heinz and Muller (2000).

## Context

This comment directly challenges the interpretation proposed by Jeon and Koch (2000) and Asakawa, Heinz and Muller (2000), who argued that a small value of the charge fluctuation measure would be an unambiguous signal of equilibrium QGP production. The authors point out that the same smallness can arise from fluctuations generated at the initial stage of the collision, before any thermalized QGP exists. This work implies that experimental observation of small charge fluctuations cannot by itself distinguish between initial-stage fluctuations and equilibrium QGP fluctuations, and that additional observables or theoretical developments are needed to resolve the ambiguity.
