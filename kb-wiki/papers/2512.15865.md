# Weak Charge Form Factor Determination at the Electron-Ion Collider

> Determining the weak charge form factor, $F_W(Q^2)$, of nuclei over a continuous range of momentum transfers, $0\lesssim Q^2 \lesssim 0.1$ GeV$^2$, is essential for mapping out the distribution of neutrons in nuclei. The neutron density distribution has significant implications for a broad range of areas, including studies of nuclear structure, neutron stars, and physics beyond the Standard Model. Currently, our knowledge of $F_W(Q^2)$ comes primarily from fixed target experiments that measure the parity-violating asymmetry in coherent elastic electron-ion scattering. Fixed target experiments, such as CREX and PREX-1,2, have provided high-precision weak charge form factor extractions for the $^{48}{\rm Ca}$ and $^{208}{\rm Pb}$ nuclei, respectively. However, a major limitation of fixed target experiments is that they each provide data only at a single value of $Q^2$. With the proposed Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) on the horizon, we explore its potential to impact the determination of the weak charge form factor. While it cannot compete with the precision of fixed target experiments, it can provide data over a wide and continuous range of $Q^2$ values, and for a wide variety of nuclei. We show that with data corresponding to an integrated luminosity of ${\cal L} \sim $ 500/$A$ fb$^{-1}$, where $A$ is the nucleus atomic weight, the EIC can significantly impact constraints by lifting degeneracies in theoretical models of the neutron density distribution. Ensuring EIC detector coverage at low $Q^2$ and large negative pseudorapidities will be essential for such $F_W(Q^2)$ measurements.

## Key claim

The Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) cannot compete with the precision of fixed-target experiments like CREX and PREX-1,2 for weak charge form factor measurements, but it can provide data over a wide and continuous range of momentum transfers (0.001 to 0.1 GeV²) that lifts degeneracies in theoretical models of the neutron density distribution, provided far-backward detector upgrades are implemented and an integrated luminosity of approximately 500/A fb⁻¹ is achieved.

## Method

The authors simulate parity-violating asymmetry measurements in coherent elastic electron-ion scattering at the EIC using electron and ion beam energies of 10 GeV and 110×A GeV respectively, with 80% electron beam polarization. They model the weak charge density using a symmetrized two-parameter Fermi (S2pF) function with parameters (a_W, c_W) for half-density radius and surface diffuseness, and compute projected statistical and systematic uncertainties across Q² bins from 0.001 to 0.1 GeV², assuming 99% veto efficiency for incoherent background events and 1% systematic uncertainty from polarization.

## Key numbers

- Integrated luminosity required: approximately 500/A fb⁻¹, where A is the nucleus atomic weight
- Q² range covered: 0.001 GeV² to 0.1 GeV²
- Q² bin resolution: 5% for 10⁻³ < Q² < 10⁻² GeV², 2% for Q² > 10⁻² GeV²
- Electron beam energy: 10 GeV; ion beam energy: 110×A GeV
- Electron polarization: 80% (P_e = 0.80)
- Systematic uncertainty from polarization: 1% of A_PV (δP_e = 0.01 P_e)
- For ⁴⁸Ca: CREX measured A_PV = 2.668 ± 0.113 ppm at Q² = 0.030 ± 0.0002 GeV², yielding F_W = 0.1304 ± 0.0056
- For ²⁰⁸Pb: PREX-1 measured F_W = 0.204 ± 0.028 at Q² = 0.0088 ± 0.00011 GeV²; PREX-2 measured F_W = 0.368 ± 0.013 at Q² = 0.0062 ± 0.00005 GeV²
- Fiducial S2pF parameters for ⁴⁸Ca: a_W = 0.515 fm, c_W = 3.995 fm, Q_W = -26.0
- Fiducial S2pF parameters for ²⁰⁸Pb: a_W = 0.614 fm, c_W = 6.815 fm, Q_W = -117.9

## Context

This work extends the single-Q² measurements from the CREX experiment on ⁴⁸Ca (CREX Collaboration, 2022) and the PREX-1 and PREX-2 experiments on ²⁰⁸Pb (Abrahamyan et al., 2012; PREX Collaboration, 2021) by proposing that the EIC can provide continuous Q² coverage that breaks degeneracies in the (a_W, c_W) parameter space of neutron density models. The results enable improved constraints on neutron distributions in nuclei, with implications for nuclear structure theory, neutron star properties, and coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (CEνNS) experiments, and the authors also provide projections for ¹³²Xe and ⁴⁰Ar nuclei relevant for dark matter direct detection and CEνNS experiments.
