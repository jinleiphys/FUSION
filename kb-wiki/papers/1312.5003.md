# Extracting jet transport coefficient from jet quenching at RHIC and LHC

> Within five different approaches to parton propagation and energy loss in dense matter, a phenomenological study of experimental data on suppression of large $p_T$ single inclusive hadrons in heavy-ion collisions at both RHIC and LHC was carried out. The evolution of bulk medium used in the study for parton propagation was given by 2+1D or 3+1D hydrodynamic models which are also constrained by experimental data on bulk hadron spectra. Values for the jet transport parameter $\hat q$ at the center of the most central heavy-ion collisions are extracted or calculated within each model, with parameters for the medium properties that are constrained by experimental data on the hadron suppression factor $R_{AA}$. For a quark with initial energy of 10 GeV we find that $\hat q\approx 1.2 \pm 0.3$ GeV$^2$/fm at an initial time $τ_0=0.6$ fm/$c$ in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV/n and $\hat q\approx 1.9 \pm 0.7 $ GeV$^2$/fm in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=2.76 $ TeV/n. Compared to earlier studies, these represent significant convergence on values of the extracted jet transport parameter, reflecting recent advances in theory and the availability of new experiment data from the LHC.

## Key claim

The paper extracts values of the jet transport parameter $\hat q$ from combined RHIC and LHC data on single hadron suppression, finding significant convergence across five different theoretical approaches, but the extracted values depend on the assumed medium evolution model and the specific energy and collision system, with the quoted central values applying only to a quark with initial energy of 10 GeV at an initial time $\tau_0=0.6$ fm/$c$.

## Method

Five independent parton energy loss approaches (GLV-CUJET, HT-M, HT-BW, MARTINI, and McGill-AMY) were each used to compute the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ for single inclusive hadrons at high transverse momentum in central heavy-ion collisions. Each model was coupled to either 2+1D or 3+1D hydrodynamic simulations of the bulk medium evolution that were constrained by experimental data on bulk hadron spectra. The jet transport parameter $\hat q$ was either directly fitted (in HT approaches) or computed from fitted model parameters (in other approaches) by minimizing $\chi^2$ between calculated $R_{AA}$ and experimental data from PHENIX at RHIC and ALICE/CMS at LHC.

## Key numbers

- For a quark with initial energy of 10 GeV at $\tau_0=0.6$ fm/$c$ in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV/n: $\hat q \approx 1.2 \pm 0.3$ GeV$^2$/fm
- For a quark with initial energy of 10 GeV at $\tau_0=0.6$ fm/$c$ in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV/n: $\hat q \approx 1.9 \pm 0.7$ GeV$^2$/fm
- HT-BW best fit to RHIC data gives $\hat q_0 = 1.20 \pm 0.30$ GeV$^2$/fm at $\tau_0=0.6$ fm/$c$ for Au+Au at $\sqrt{s}=0.2$ TeV/n
- HT-BW best fit to LHC data gives $\hat q_0 = 2.2 \pm 0.5$ GeV$^2$/fm at $\tau_0=0.6$ fm/$c$ for Pb+Pb at $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV/n
- Ratio of initial jet transport parameters between LHC and RHIC from independent fits: $1.83 \pm 0.26$
- Ratio of transverse hadron density between central Pb+Pb at LHC and Au+Au at RHIC: $2.2 \pm 0.23$
- Charged hadron pseudorapidity density at mid-rapidity in 0-5% central Pb+Pb at LHC: $dN_{ch}/d\eta = 1584 \pm 4$ (stat.) $\pm 76$ (sys.)
- Charged hadron pseudorapidity density at mid-rapidity in 0-5% central Au+Au at RHIC: $dN_{ch}/d\eta = 687 \pm 37$
- Initial temperatures in HT-BW: $T_0 = 373$ MeV for Au+Au at RHIC and $T_0 = 473$ MeV for Pb+Pb at LHC at $\tau_0=0.6$ fm/$c$
- Initial temperatures in HT-M: $T_0 = 346$ MeV for Au+Au at RHIC and $T_0 = 447$ MeV for Pb+Pb at LHC

## Context

This work extends earlier phenomenological studies by the Hard Probes Collaboration and by Bass et al. (2008) and Armesto et al. (2009) that found diverging values of $\hat q$ varying by up to a factor of 8 across different models. The availability of new LHC data on Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV/n provides unprecedented constraints that reduce the spread in extracted $\hat q$ values. The results enable future systematic extraction of the temperature and jet energy dependence of the jet transport coefficient, and the paper identifies the need for global fits including centrality dependence of $R_{AA}$ and elliptic flow $v_2$ as next steps.
