# Baryon phase-space density in heavy-ion collisions

> The baryon phase-space density at mid-rapidity from central heavy-ion collisions is estimated from proton spectra with interferometry and deuteron coalescence measurements. It is found that the mid-rapidity phase-space density of baryons is significantly lower at the SPS than the AGS, while those of total particles (pion + baryon) are comparable. Thermal and chemical equilibrium model calculations tend to over-estimate the phase-space densities at both energies.

# Baryon phase-space density in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The mid-rapidity baryon phase-space density at freeze-out is significantly lower at SPS energies than at AGS energies, while the total (pion plus baryon) phase-space densities are comparable at both energies; however, the estimates rely on the assumption of a transverse-momentum-independent source volume and suffer from large systematic uncertainties.

## Method
The authors estimated the freeze-out baryon phase-space density at mid-rapidity in central Au+Au (AGS) and Pb+Pb (SPS) collisions using two independent methods: (1) from proton yields combined with two-proton interferometry source size parameters, and (2) from the ratio of deuteron to proton invariant cross-sections via the coalescence model. The results were then compared to thermal and chemical equilibrium model calculations using temperature and baryon chemical potential extracted from particle ratios.

## Key numbers
- Pion phase-space density at freeze-out: approximately 0.1 (in units of (2π)^(-3)) at both AGS (√s ≈ 5 AGeV) and SPS (√s ≈ 20 AGeV) energies.
- Proton phase-space density from two-proton interferometry: significantly lower than pions at both energies, with baryon density at AGS about three times that at SPS.
- Proton phase-space density from deuteron coalescence: ⟨f⟩(2π)^3 = 0.015 ± 0.001 (stat.) at AGS and 0.0029 ± 0.0004 (stat.) at SPS.
- Thermal and chemical equilibrium model calculations overestimate the phase-space density: by a factor of 2.7 ± 1.2 (AGS) and 4.0 ± 1.3 (SPS) for chemical freeze-out, and by 1.5 ± 0.7 (AGS) and 2.2 ± 1.2 (SPS) for kinetic freeze-out.
- Proton source size at AGS estimated as R_G = 3.5 ± 0.5 (syst.) fm from two-proton correlation measurements at forward rapidity.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of pion phase-space density by Bertsch (1994) and others (Barz 1997, Ferenc 1999, Tomasik 1999) to baryons, using both two-proton interferometry and deuteron coalescence methods originally proposed by Siemens and Kapusta (1979) and Nagamiya (1986). The finding that total (pion plus baryon) phase-space density is similar at AGS and SPS supports the freeze-out condition argument by Pomeranchuk (1951). The results enable future comparisons with thermal model predictions and motivate measurements of transverse-momentum-dependent proton interferometry or deuteron coalescence data to reduce systematic uncertainties.
