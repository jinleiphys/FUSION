# Deeply virtual Compton scattering at small-$x$ in future Electron - Ion Colliders

> The study of exclusive processes in the future electron-ion ($eA$) colliders will be an important tool to investigate the QCD dynamics at high energies as they are in general driven by the gluon content of the target which is strongly subject to parton saturation effects. In this paper we compute the coherent and incoherent cross sections for the deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) process relying on the color dipole approach and considering different models for the dipole - proton scattering amplitude. The dependencies of the cross sections with the energy, photon virtuality, nuclear mass number and squared momentum transfer are analysed in detail. We demonstrate that the ratio between the incoherent and coherent cross sections decreases at smaller values of $Q^2$ and increases at smaller values of $A$. We show that the coherent cross section dominates at small $t$ and exhibits the typical diffractive pattern, with the number of dips in the range $|t| \le 0.3$ GeV$^2$ increasing with the mass atomic number. Our results indicate that the position of the dips are independent of the model used to treat the dipole - proton interaction as well as of the center-of-mass energy.

## Key claim

The paper computes coherent and incoherent DVCS cross sections in electron-ion collisions using the color dipole approach, demonstrating that the ratio between incoherent and coherent cross sections decreases at smaller values of $Q^2$ and increases at smaller values of $A$, with the caveat that these predictions depend on the specific model for the dipole-proton scattering amplitude and are restricted to $Q^2$ values smaller than 15 GeV$^2$ due to limitations in the dipole models.

## Method

The authors calculate the coherent and incoherent DVCS cross sections using the color dipole formalism, where the virtual photon fluctuates into a quark-antiquark dipole that scatters off the nucleus via a color singlet exchange. The dipole-nucleus scattering amplitude is constructed from the dipole-proton scattering amplitude using the Glauber-Gribov formalism with a 3-parameter Fermi distribution for the nuclear density. Several models for the dipole-proton scattering amplitude are compared: the running coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (rcBK) solution, the AAMQS solutions (with and without heavy quarks), the bCGC model with impact parameter dependence, and the phenomenological GBW and IIMS models.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear saturation scale is parametrized as $Q_{s,A}^2 = A^{1/3} \times Q_0^2 (x_0/x)^\lambda$ with $Q_0^2 = 1.0$ GeV$^2$, $x_0 = 1.632 \times 10^{-5}$, and $\lambda = 0.2197$
- For the bCGC model, the fitted parameters are: $\gamma_s = 0.6599$, $\kappa = 9.9$, $B_{CGC} = 5.5$ GeV$^{-2}$, $\mathcal{N}_0 = 0.3358$, $x_0 = 0.00105$, and $\lambda = 0.2063$
- For the GBW model: $x_0 = 3 \times 10^{-4}$, $\lambda = 0.288$, and $\sigma_0 = 23.03$ mb
- For the IIMS model: $\mathcal{N}_0 = 0.7$, $\gamma_s = 0.7376$, $\kappa = 9.9$, $\lambda = 0.2197$, $x_0 = 1.632 \times 10^{-5}$, and $\sigma_0 = 27.28$ mb
- The $A$ dependence of the predictions can be approximated by $\sigma_{coh} \propto A^{1.3}$ and $\sigma_{inc} \propto A^{0.75}$ for $Q^2 = 8$ GeV$^2$ and $W = 120$ GeV
- The number of dips in the coherent differential cross section increases with atomic mass number in the range $|t| \le 0.3$ GeV$^2$
- The two AAMQS predictions differ by approximately 50% in the energy range probed by future $eA$ colliders ($W \le 140$ GeV)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of exclusive processes in electron-ion collisions by the same authors (Goncalves and Pires, 2012) and others (Caldwell, Lappi, Toll) that focused on vector meson production, providing the first detailed analysis of the $t$-dependence of nuclear DVCS cross sections. The calculations update earlier DVCS studies (Kirchner, Freund, Goeke, Kopeliovich) by incorporating more recent models for the dipole-proton scattering amplitude, particularly the AAMQS model. The results enable future experimental searches at the Electron-Ion Collider to use DVCS as a clean probe of gluon saturation effects, since DVCS is not affected by theoretical uncertainties from vector meson wave functions.
