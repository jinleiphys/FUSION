# Density matrix expansion for the MDI interaction

> By assuming that the isospin- and momentum-dependent MDI interaction has a form similar to the Gogny-like effective two-body interaction with a Yukawa finite-range term and the momentum dependence only originates from the finite-range exchange interaction, we determine its parameters by comparing the predicted potential energy density functional in uniform nuclear matter with what has been usually given and used extensively in transport models for studying isospin effects in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions as well as in investigating the properties of hot asymmetric nuclear matter and neutron star matter. We then use the density matrix expansion to derive from the resulting finite-range exchange interaction an effective Skyrme-like zero-range interaction with density-dependent parameters. As an application, we study the transition density and pressure at the inner edge of neutron star crusts using the stability conditions derived from the linearized Vlasov equation for the neutron star matter.

## Key claim

By assuming that all momentum dependence in the MDI interaction originates solely from the finite-range exchange term of a Gogny-like Yukawa interaction, the authors derive an effective Skyrme-like zero-range interaction with density-dependent parameters; the resulting density-gradient coefficients are much larger than those extracted from nuclear radii fits, indicating that this assumption likely overestimates the finite-range exchange contribution.

## Method

The authors first determine the parameters of an underlying finite-range nucleon-nucleon interaction by requiring that its potential energy density functional for uniform nuclear matter matches the standard MDI functional used in transport models. They then apply the density matrix expansion of Negele and Vautherin (1972) to the finite-range exchange term, obtaining a zero-range Skyrme-like interaction with density-dependent coefficients A, B, C, and D. As an application, they use the linearized Vlasov equation with the resulting interaction to compute the spinodal instability boundary and extract the transition density and pressure at the inner edge of neutron star crusts.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of normal nuclear matter: ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Binding energy per nucleon in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation: −16 MeV
- Incompressibility K₀ at saturation: 212 MeV
- Symmetry energy at saturation density: E_sym(ρ₀) = 30.5 MeV
- Constrained slope parameter L of the symmetry energy from earlier analyses: L = 86 ± 25 MeV (corresponding to −1 < x < 0)
- More recent constraint on L: L = 58 ± 18 MeV
- For T = 0 MeV and L = 58 ± 18 MeV, transition density range: 0.050 fm⁻³ < ρ_t < 0.071 fm⁻³
- For T = 0 MeV and L = 58 ± 18 MeV, transition pressure range: 0.12 MeV/fm³ < P_t < 0.31 MeV/fm³
- For T = 1 MeV and L = 58 ± 18 MeV, transition density range: 0.038 fm⁻³ < ρ_t < 0.070 fm⁻³
- For T = 1 MeV and L = 58 ± 18 MeV, transition pressure range: 0.06 MeV/fm³ < P_t < 0.30 MeV/fm³
- Density-gradient coefficient from Oyamatsu (2003) used for comparison: 132 ± 12 MeV fm⁵

## Context

This work extends the MDI interaction, previously used only through its uniform-matter energy density functional in transport models by Das et al. (2003) and in neutron star studies by Xu et al. (2009), by providing its explicit underlying nucleon-nucleon interaction form. The density matrix expansion follows the approach of Negele and Vautherin (1972) and Sprung et al. (1975) for other finite-range interactions. The application to neutron star crust transition densities enables consistent treatment of finite-range effects, correcting previous work by Xu et al. (2009, 2010) that used inconsistent density-gradient coefficients from Oyamatsu (2003). The results can be used to impose constraints on neutron star masses and radii through crustal moment of inertia comparisons with pulsar glitch data.
