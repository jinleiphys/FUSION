# Unintegrated parton distributions and particle production in hadronic collisions

> The inclusive distributions of gluons and pions for high-energy NN collisions are calculated. The results for several unintegrated gluon distributions (UGD's) from the literature are compared. We find huge differences in both rapidity and $p_t$ of gluons and $π$'s in NN collisions for different models of UGD's. The Karzeev-Levin UGD gives good description of momentum distribution of charged hadrons at midrapidities. We find, however, that the gluonic mechanism discussed does not describe the inclusive spectra of charged particles in the fragmentation region. Some of the missing mechanisms are calculated with the help of unintegrated parton distributions from the solution of the CCFM equation.

# Unintegrated parton distributions and particle production in hadronic collisions

## Key claim

The gluonic mechanism ($gg \rightarrow g$) alone does not describe inclusive charged-particle spectra in the fragmentation region at RHIC energies, contrary to a prior claim by Kharzeev and Levin (2001); the missing mechanisms involve (anti)quark-gluon and gluon-(anti)quark fusion processes, which can be estimated using unintegrated parton distributions from the solution of the CCFM equation.

## Method

Inclusive gluon production cross sections in nucleon-nucleon collisions were calculated in the $k_t$-factorization approach using the formula $d\sigma/(dy d^2 p_t) = (16 N_c/(N_c^2-1))(1/p_t^2) \int \alpha_s(\Omega^2) \mathcal{F}_1(x_1,\kappa_1^2) \mathcal{F}_2(x_2,\kappa_2^2) \delta(\vec{\kappa}_1+\vec{\kappa}_2-\vec{p}_t) d^2\kappa_1 d^2\kappa_2$, with longitudinal momentum fractions $x_{1/2} = (p_t/\sqrt{s}) \cdot \exp(\pm y)$. Charged-pion distributions were obtained by convoluting the gluon distributions with leading-order phenomenological fragmentation functions from Binnewies, Kniehl, and Kramer (1995) or Kretzer (2000), assuming $\eta_h = y_g$ and using $E_h = z E_g$ to define the fragmentation variable $z$.

## Key numbers

- At RHIC energy W = 200 GeV and midrapidity ($y \sim 0$), the unintegrated gluon distributions are probed at $x_g = 10^{-3}$ to $10^{-2}$.
- For large $|y|$, either $x_1 < 10^{-4}$ and $x_2 \rightarrow 1$, or $x_1 \rightarrow 1$ and $x_2 < 10^{-4}$.
- The nonperturbative form factor used with CCFM parton distributions was $F^{np}(b) = \exp(b^2/(4 b_0^2))$ with $b_0 = 0.5$ GeV$^{-1}$, adjusted to describe UA5 collaboration data.
- The transverse momentum cut for charged pions in the pseudorapidity distribution calculation was $p_{t,h} > 0.2$ GeV.
- For extrapolating gluon distributions to $x_g \rightarrow 1$, a factor $(1-x_g)^n$ was applied with $n = 5-7$.

## Context

This work extends the $k_t$-factorization approach of Gribov, Levin, and Ryskin (1981) and directly tests the claim of Kharzeev and Levin (2001) that gluonic mechanisms alone describe the full rapidity distribution of charged particles at RHIC. The author finds that the Kharzeev-Levin UGD gives good description of transverse momentum distributions at midrapidity (consistent with UA1 data), but fails in fragmentation regions, where quark-gluon and antiquark-gluon fusion processes become important. The paper introduces unintegrated parton distributions from the CCFM equation solution by Kwiecinski and collaborators (2003) as a consistent framework to include both gluonic and quark-initiated mechanisms, enabling future tests of UGD models against RHIC measurements of $\pi^+/\pi^-$ ratios as a function of pseudorapidity.
