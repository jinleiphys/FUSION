# Coherent Hypernucleus Production in Antiproton-Nucleus Annihilation Reactions as a Probe for $őļ$ meson Exchange

> The hypernucleus production reaction $\bar p + {}^AZ \to {}^A_őõ(Z-1) + \barőõ$ in the beam momentum range 1.5-20 GeV/c is addressed theoretically as a coherent process. The calculations are based on a covariant $t$-channel meson exchange model for the elementary $\bar p p \to \barőõőõ$ annihilation amplitude with parameters fixed by comparison with empirical data. Besides pseudo-scalar $K$ and vector $K^*$ mesons we also account for correlated $ŌÄK$ contributions, modelled by the scalar $K^*_0(800)$ or $őļ$ meson. Initial and final state nuclear interactions are taken into account in eikonal approximation. The bound baryon wave functions are obtained self-consistently in a covariant mean-field approach. It is shown that the hypernucleus production cross sections populating discrete states are dominated by the vector and scalar interaction channels. The pronounced sensitivity on the scalar $őļ$ meson exchange contributions indicates that these reactions are well suited as a probe for correlated $ŌÄK$ exchange in the scalar $őļ/K^*_0$ interaction channel.

## Key claim

Coherent hypernucleus production in antiproton-nucleus annihilation reactions is dominated by vector (K*) and scalar (őļ) meson exchange, with pseudoscalar kaon exchange strongly suppressed for bound proton-to-Lambda transitions; the pronounced sensitivity to scalar őļ meson exchange indicates these reactions are well suited as a probe for correlated ŌÄK exchange in the scalar őļ/K*0 interaction channel.

## Method

The authors developed a covariant t-channel meson exchange model for the elementary pŐÖp ‚Üí őõŐÖőõ annihilation amplitude, including K, K*, and őļ (K*0(800)) meson exchanges with parameters fitted to empirical cross section data. Bound baryon wave functions for the target nucleus (‚Āī‚ĀįAr) and final hypernucleus (‚Āī‚ĀįőõCl) were obtained self-consistently from a covariant relativistic mean-field approach with Woods-Saxon potentials. Initial and final state interactions of the antiproton and antilambda were treated in the eikonal approximation using momentum-dependent total cross sections and absorption factors.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of incoherent hypernucleus production in antiproton-nucleus reactions (Bando 1989, Cugnon 1990, Gaitanos 2011, Gaitanos 2014, Feng 2015, Gaitanos 2016) by treating the process as a coherent peripheral reaction suitable for spectroscopic studies of discrete hypernuclear states. It builds on coherent hypernuclear production approaches developed for proton-induced reactions (Shyam 2005), pion-induced reactions (Bender 2009), and photo-induced reactions (Shyam 2007). The inclusion of scalar őļ meson exchange, motivated by the Nijmegen group's baryon-baryon interaction models (Timmermans 1988, Timmermans 1992) and the Juelich model's dynamical treatment of ŌÄK correlations (Haidenbauer 2005), enables the reaction to probe the scalar interaction channel that is otherwise difficult to access experimentally. The results are relevant for the upcoming PANDA experiment at FAIR, where such reactions could be realized with noble gas targets.
