# Coherent Hypernucleus Production in Antiproton-Nucleus Annihilation Reactions as a Probe for $κ$ meson Exchange

> The hypernucleus production reaction $\bar p + {}^AZ \to {}^A_Λ(Z-1) + \barΛ$ in the beam momentum range 1.5-20 GeV/c is addressed theoretically as a coherent process. The calculations are based on a covariant $t$-channel meson exchange model for the elementary $\bar p p \to \barΛΛ$ annihilation amplitude with parameters fixed by comparison with empirical data. Besides pseudo-scalar $K$ and vector $K^*$ mesons we also account for correlated $πK$ contributions, modelled by the scalar $K^*_0(800)$ or $κ$ meson. Initial and final state nuclear interactions are taken into account in eikonal approximation. The bound baryon wave functions are obtained self-consistently in a covariant mean-field approach. It is shown that the hypernucleus production cross sections populating discrete states are dominated by the vector and scalar interaction channels. The pronounced sensitivity on the scalar $κ$ meson exchange contributions indicates that these reactions are well suited as a probe for correlated $πK$ exchange in the scalar $κ/K^*_0$ interaction channel.

## Key claim

Coherent hypernucleus production in antiproton-nucleus annihilation reactions is dominated by vector (K*) and scalar (κ) meson exchange, with pseudoscalar kaon exchange strongly suppressed for bound proton-to-Lambda transitions; the pronounced sensitivity to scalar κ meson exchange indicates these reactions are well suited as a probe for correlated πK exchange in the scalar κ/K*0 interaction channel.

## Method

The authors developed a covariant t-channel meson exchange model for the elementary p̅p → Λ̅Λ annihilation amplitude, including K, K*, and κ (K*0(800)) meson exchanges with parameters fitted to empirical cross section data. Bound baryon wave functions for the target nucleus (⁴⁰Ar) and final hypernucleus (⁴⁰ΛCl) were obtained self-consistently from a covariant relativistic mean-field approach with Woods-Saxon potentials. Initial and final state interactions of the antiproton and antilambda were treated in the eikonal approximation using momentum-dependent total cross sections and absorption factors.

## Key numbers

- Beam momentum range considered: 1.5–20 GeV/c
- For the elementary p̅p → Λ̅Λ reaction, two parameter sets were fitted: Set 1 (no κ meson) with g_KNΛ = -13.981, G_v = -4.6, G_t = -8.5, Λ_K = 2.0 GeV, Λ_K* = 1.6 GeV, Ω = 0.015; Set 2 (with κ meson) with g_κNΛ = -7.5, m_κ = 682 MeV, Γ_κ = 547 MeV, Λ_κ = 1.8 GeV, Ω = 0.005
- Calculated binding energy of ⁴⁰Ar: B = 343.58 MeV (experimental: 343.81 MeV)
- Proton rms radius of ⁴⁰Ar: 3.30 fm; neutron rms radius: 3.41 fm
- Lambda binding energies in ⁴⁰ΛCl: 1s1/2 = 18.55 MeV, 1p3/2 = 10.20 MeV, 1p1/2 = 9.26 MeV, 1d5/2 = 2.14 MeV, 2s1/2 = 1.44 MeV, 1d3/2 = 0.84 MeV
- At p_lab = 2 GeV/c, absorption reduces the forward-angle differential cross section by about two orders of magnitude relative to the impulse approximation

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of incoherent hypernucleus production in antiproton-nucleus reactions (Bando 1989, Cugnon 1990, Gaitanos 2011, Gaitanos 2014, Feng 2015, Gaitanos 2016) by treating the process as a coherent peripheral reaction suitable for spectroscopic studies of discrete hypernuclear states. It builds on coherent hypernuclear production approaches developed for proton-induced reactions (Shyam 2005), pion-induced reactions (Bender 2009), and photo-induced reactions (Shyam 2007). The inclusion of scalar κ meson exchange, motivated by the Nijmegen group's baryon-baryon interaction models (Timmermans 1988, Timmermans 1992) and the Juelich model's dynamical treatment of πK correlations (Haidenbauer 2005), enables the reaction to probe the scalar interaction channel that is otherwise difficult to access experimentally. The results are relevant for the upcoming PANDA experiment at FAIR, where such reactions could be realized with noble gas targets.
