# Application of the generalized two-center cluster model to 10Be

> A generalized two-center cluster model (GTCM), including various partitions of the valence nucleons around two alpha-cores, is proposed for studies on the exotic cluster structures of Be isotopes. This model is applied to the 10Be = alpha + alpha + n + n system and the adiabatic energy surfaces for alpha-alpha distances are calculated. It is found that this model naturally describes the formation of the molecular orbitals as well as that of asymptotic cluster states dependeing on their relative distance. In the negative-parity state, a new type of the alpha + 6He cluster structure is also predicted.

## Key claim

The generalized two-center cluster model (GTCM) can describe both molecular-orbital configurations and asymptotic cluster-coupling states of the \(^{10}\text{Be} = \alpha + \alpha + n + n\) system on an equal footing, with a dynamical transition between the two schemes occurring at an intermediate \(\alpha\)-\(\alpha\) distance of about 5–6 fm. In the negative-parity state, a new type of \(\alpha + ^{6}\text{He}\) cluster structure is predicted.

## Method

The GTCM basis functions for \(^{10}\text{Be}\) are constructed from two \(\alpha\)-clusters (each described by a \((0s)^4\) harmonic-oscillator configuration) plus two valence neutrons placed in atomic orbitals around the left or right \(\alpha\)-core. The wave function is projected onto total angular momentum \(J\) and parity \(\pi\), and the coefficients are determined by solving a coupled-channel Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) equation at fixed \(\alpha\)-\(\alpha\) distances \(S\) to obtain adiabatic energy surfaces, then by superposing over \(S\) and intrinsic angular projection \(K\) to obtain the full GCM solutions.

## Key numbers

* The adiabatic energy surfaces for \(J^\pi = 0^+\) show that at \(S \geq 6\) fm, the calculated surfaces coincide with those of cluster-coupling wave functions; at \(S \leq 4\) fm, molecular-orbital configurations dominate.
* At \(S = 2\) fm and \(S = 4\) fm, the lowest adiabatic surface A has a dominant component of \((\pi_{3/2}^-)^2\) with squared amplitudes of 0.93 and 0.81, respectively.
* At \(S = 8\) fm, the lowest surface A is dominated by the \([\alpha + ^{6}\text{He}(0_1^+)]_{L=0}\) channel with a squared amplitude of 0.99.
* At the intermediate distance \(S = 5\)–6 fm, the dominant component of surface A changes to \((\sigma_{1/2}^+)^2\) (72%) while the \([\alpha + ^{6}\text{He}(0_1^+)]_{L=0}\) channel amplitude is 61%.
* The GCM solutions for \(0^+\) states yield energy gains of about 1–2 MeV relative to the adiabatic surfaces; for \(1^-\) states, the energy gain is about 1–3 MeV.
* The harmonic-oscillator radius parameter \(b\) is taken as 1.44 fm; the Volkov No.2 interaction is used with Majorana parameter \(m = 0.576\).

## Context

This work extends the molecular orbital model (MOM) of Itagaki et al. and the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) studies of Kanada-En'yo et al., which described low-lying states of Be isotopes, by also incorporating the asymptotic cluster-coupling states previously treated by Descouvemont and Baye (1994) with a traditional cluster model. The GTCM enables unified studies of both low-lying molecular-orbital states and high-lying resonant cluster states in Be isotopes, and the authors note that direct extension to \(^{12}\text{Be} = \alpha + \alpha + 4n\) is in progress.
