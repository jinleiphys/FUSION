# The Astrophysical S-Factor of the Reaction $^7$be(p,$γ$)$^8$B in the Direct Capture Model

> The astrophysical $S$-factor for the reaction $^7$Be(p,$γ$)$^8$B up to an energy of 2~MeV (c.m.) and the capture cross section of $^7$Li(n,$γ$)$^8$Li up to 1~MeV (c.m.) are calculated using the Direct Capture model (DC). Both calculations are in good agreement with experimental data.

## Key claim
The Direct Capture model, using folding potentials with no parameters adjusted to reaction data, reproduces the absolute value of the astrophysical S-factor for \(^7\)Be(p,\(\gamma\))\(^8\)B over 100 keV to 2 MeV, except near the 632 keV resonance where the calculated width of 100 keV overestimates the experimental width by a factor of 2.5.

## Method
The authors solved the Schrodinger equation with local optical potentials in the entrance channel and bound state, using single folding potentials derived from the density-dependent M3Y nucleon-nucleon interaction. The normalization constants of the folding potentials were adjusted to reproduce the experimentally known binding energy of \(^8\)B (138 keV) and the energy of the resonant state at 632 keV, rather than fitting to reaction cross sections. Calculations were performed with the direct capture code TEDCA, considering contributions from E1, M1, and E2 multipoles, and were repeated under three assumptions: no spin-orbit coupling, spin-orbit coupling in the entrance channel only, and spin-orbit coupling in both channels.

## Key numbers
- The calculated astrophysical S-factor for \(^7\)Be(p,\(\gamma\))\(^8\)B agrees with experimental data over the energy range 100 keV to 2 MeV (c.m.).
- The resonance in the p-wave was set at an energy of 632 keV (c.m.), with a calculated width of 100 keV, which is too large by a factor of 2.5 compared to experiment.
- The bound state separation energy of the proton in \(^8\)B is 138 keV; the bound state wave function required radial integration up to 200 fm.
- The folding potential depth was 43 MeV (\(\lambda = 1\)), with a volume integral per nucleon of 660 MeV fm\(^3\).
- Spectroscopic factors used were \(\mathcal{S}_{\rm 1p_{3/2}} = 0.977\) and \(\mathcal{S}_{\rm 1p_{1/2}} = 0.0561\) from shell model calculations of Cohen (1967).
- For the calculation with spin-orbit coupling in the entrance channel, the spin-orbit strength was \(V_{\rm so} = 2.44\) MeV, reproducing the two \(1^+\) resonances at 632 keV and 1.4 MeV (c.m.).
- For the calculation with spin-orbit coupling in both channels, the spin-orbit strength was \(V_{\rm so} = 2.01\) MeV, with \(\lambda_{\rm b} = \lambda_{\rm sc} = 1.01733\).
- For the mirror reaction \(^7\)Li(n,\(\gamma\))\(^8\)Li, the binding energies of the ground state and first excited state (0.9808 MeV) were reproduced with \(\lambda_{\rm b} = 1.0361\) and \(\lambda_{\rm b} = 0.9627\), respectively.

## Context
This work extends the potential-model approach using folding potentials to the \(^7\)Be(p,\(\gamma\))\(^8\)B reaction, which is critical for determining the high-energy solar neutrino flux from the ppIII chain. The authors contrast their parameter-free method with phenomenological fits by Barker (1986) and Kim (1987), where potential parameters were fitted to reaction data, and with microscopic calculations using the resonating group method (Johnson, 1990) and generator coordinate method (Descouvemont, 1988, 1992). The results enable a more theoretically grounded determination of the low-energy S-factor for solar neutrino calculations, though the model overestimates the width of the 632 keV resonance, suggesting the need for coupled-channel calculations including the \(^7\)Be(1/2)\(^-\) channel.
