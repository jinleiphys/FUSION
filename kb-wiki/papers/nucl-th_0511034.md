# Momentum Correlations for Identical Fermions

> The effects of intensity interferometry (HBT), which are mainly reflected by the measured momentum correlations, have been fully discussed since 1950's. The analogous momentum correlations between identical fermions are however less argued. Besides the fermion statistics, the momentum correlations between identical fermions are also determined by some other aspects, such as coulomb interaction, spin interactions and resonances formation. In this paper, we discussed the factors that influence the momentum correlations between $Λ$'s, especially spin interaction. It is also argued that the momentum correlations between $Λ$'s are affected significantly by the yield ratio of $Σ^0/Λ$, due to the limitation of experimental measurement. The mixture of $Σ^0$'s and $Λ$s would significantly weaken the measured two-particle momentum correlations of $Λ$'s.

# Momentum Correlations for Identical Fermions

## Key claim
The measured momentum correlations between Λ hyperons are significantly weakened by the unavoidable mixture of Σ⁰ decay products, with the observable correlation reduced to approximately one quarter of the pure Λ-Λ correlation when the Σ⁰/Λ yield ratio equals unity; the sign and magnitude of the correlation also depend critically on the unknown spin-state composition of the Λ-Λ system, which could produce either Fermi-Dirac suppression or Bose-Einstein-like enhancement.

## Method
The authors derive the two-particle correlation function C₂(p_rel) for identical fermions using a non-relativistic Gaussian source model with freeze-out radius r₀, incorporating the antisymmetrization of the total wave function as a product of spatial and spin wave functions. They then compute the experimentally accessible correlation function by separating reconstructed Λ's into "primordial" Λ's from the fireball and "decayed" Λ's from Σ⁰ → Λγ, assuming no correlations between decayed Λ's or between decayed and primordial Λ's, and weighting the pure Λ-Λ correlation by the fraction of primordial-primordial pairs.

## Key numbers
- For a pure Λ-Λ system with equal probability of spin singlet and each triplet component (α=1/2, β=√3/2), the correlation function is C₂ = 1 − (1/2)exp(−p_rel² r₀²/2).
- For Σ⁰/Λ yield ratio γ = 1 and N ≫ 1, the experimentally measured correlation function becomes C₂_exp ≈ 1 + (1/4)R_Λ = 1 − (1/8)exp(−p_rel² r₀²/2), reducing the pure Λ-Λ correlation effect to one quarter.
- The Σ⁰ mean lifetime is τ ∼ 10⁻²⁰ s, with decay length of order 10⁴ fm, preventing experimental distinction of Σ⁰ decay vertices from primary vertices.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework of intensity interferometry (HBT) from bosons to identical fermions, specifically addressing Λ-Λ correlations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The authors build on the formalism of Greiner and Müller (1989) for Λ-Λ correlations and the HBT methodology developed by Hanbury-Brown and Twiss (1956), Goldhaber et al. (1960), Heinz (1999), and Pratt (1984). The paper identifies two unresolved issues that prevent definite conclusions from experimental measurements at RHIC and SPS: the unknown spin interactions between strange baryons and the contamination from Σ⁰ feed-down. If spin interactions can be understood, the Σ⁰/Λ ratio could be extracted from measured correlations, providing information on hadronization mechanisms such as recombination and fragmentation as discussed by Greco et al. (2005) and Fries et al. (2005). Conversely, if the Σ⁰/Λ ratio is determined by other means, the Λ-Λ interaction could be studied.
