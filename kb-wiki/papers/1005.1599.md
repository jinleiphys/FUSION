# Systematics of binding energies and radii based on realistic two-nucleon plus phenomenological three-nucleon interactions

> We investigate the influence of phenomenological three-nucleon interactions on the systematics of ground-state energies and charge radii throughout the whole nuclear mass range from 4-He to 208-Pb. The three-nucleon interactions supplement unitarily transformed two-body interactions constructed within the Unitary Correlation Operator Method or the Similarity Renormalization Group approach. To be able to address heavy nuclei as well, we treat the many-body problem in Hartree-Fock plus many-body perturbation theory, which is sufficient to assess the systematics of energies and radii, and limit ourselves to regularized three-body contact interactions. We show that even with such a simplistic three-nucleon interaction a simultaneous reproduction of the experimental ground-state energies and charge radii can be achieved, which is not possible with unitarily transformed two-body interactions alone.

## Key claim

Even with a simplistic regularized three-body contact interaction, a simultaneous reproduction of experimental ground-state energies and charge radii across the full nuclear mass range from 4-He to 208-Pb can be achieved, which is not possible with unitarily transformed two-body interactions alone; however, the fully SRG-transformed two-body interaction produces such large systematic deviations that a simple phenomenological three-body interaction cannot adequately compensate them.

## Method

The authors start from the Argonne V18 high-precision two-nucleon potential and apply unitary transformations via the Unitary Correlation Operator Method (UCOM) or the Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) to construct softened two-body interactions, evaluated only in two-body space. They supplement these with a phenomenological spin-isospin-independent three-body contact interaction with variable strength C_3N, regularized by an energy cutoff e_3N on the total oscillator energy of the three-particle state. The many-body problem is solved using Hartree-Fock plus many-body perturbation theory in a harmonic oscillator basis for closed-shell nuclei from 4-He to 208-Pb.

## Key numbers

* For 4-He, the ground-state energy from converged no-core shell model calculations using UCOM(SRG)-, S-UCOM(SRG)-, SRG-, and S-SRG-transformed AV18 interactions is shown as a function of flow parameter alpha; the experimental binding energy and the exact ground-state energy for the bare AV18 two-body interaction are given as horizontal lines.
* For UCOM(SRG), S-UCOM(SRG), and S-SRG interactions, binding energies are within 2 MeV per nucleon of experimental values across the whole mass range at the Hartree-Fock level.
* The Hartree-Fock model space is truncated at major oscillator quantum number e_max = 10, which is sufficient to obtain converged ground-state energies and radii.
* The oscillator parameter is chosen for each nucleus separately such that the experimental charge radius is reproduced by a shell-model Slater determinant built from harmonic oscillator single-particle states.

## Context

This work extends earlier Hartree-Fock studies using unitarily transformed two-body interactions (Roth 2005, Roth 2009) which showed that such interactions yielding realistic binding energies tend to underestimate charge radii. The authors use a pragmatic phenomenological three-body contact interaction rather than the computationally demanding consistent chiral two-plus-three-nucleon interactions used in no-core shell model calculations (Navratil 2007, Jurgenson 2009). The approach enables calculations across the full mass range from 4-He to 208-Pb and prepares the ground for future calculations with consistently transformed two-plus-three-nucleon interactions.
