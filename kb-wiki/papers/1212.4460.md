# Interplay of anisotropies of momentum distribution and mean field in heavy-ion collisions

> Two important parametrizations of momentum-dependent nucleonic fields, proposed for the simulations of central heavy-ion collisions, one by Gale et al. and the other by Welke et al., suffer from practical limitations. The first gives rise to mean fields isotropic in momentum, even when underlying momentum distributions are anisotropic, making descriptions of early nonequilibrium stages of collisions unrealistic. The second parametrization gives rise to anisotropic mean fields, but is computationally expensive, because the mean field has to be computed separately for every location of a nucleon in phase space, through folding. Here we construct a parametrization of the nucleonic mean field that yields an anisotropic mean field for an anisotropic momentum distribution and is inexpensive computationally. To demonstrate the versatility of our parametrization, we take the case of results from the parametrization by Welke et al. and attempt to approximate them. In arriving at a suitable anisotropic mean-field potential, we draw, on one hand, from the idea behind the parametrization of Gale et al., of a separable expansion of the potential energy, and, on the other, from the idea of a parallel expansion of the energy and mean field in anisotropy. We show that using our novel parametrization we can qualitatively and partially quantitatively reproduce the features of the mean-field parametrization of Welke et al.. This opens up the possibility of exploring the effects of mean-field anisotropy in collisions, without the penalty of computational cost behind the folding parametrization.

## Key claim

The authors construct a new parametrization of the nucleonic mean field that yields an anisotropic mean field for anisotropic momentum distributions while remaining computationally inexpensive, unlike the Welke et al. parametrization which requires folding integrals for every phase-space location. The new approach can qualitatively and partially quantitatively reproduce the features of the Welke et al. parametrization, but the authors acknowledge that discrepancies appear at low momenta in idealized collision scenarios, showing limitations of the approach when momentum distributions change abruptly.

## Method

The authors develop a spherical harmonics expansion (SHE) parametrization of the potential energy density that combines a Skyrme-type density-dependent part with separable scalar and tensorial quadrupole terms in momentum space. The mean field is obtained by taking the functional derivative of the potential energy density with respect to the phase-space density, yielding an explicit dependence on anisotropy through a term proportional to the spherical harmonic Y20 and the traceless tensor Tzz. The SHE results are compared against the Welke et al. (WPKDG) parametrization for ground-state properties, anisotropic Gaussian momentum distributions at twice saturation density, and an idealized two-Fermi-sphere collision scenario with separation momentum p0 = 800 MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the Gale et al. (1987) parametrization, which produces mean fields isotropic in momentum even for anisotropic distributions, by adding a tensorial quadrupole term that captures anisotropy without the computational cost of the Welke et al. (1988) folding parametrization. The approach enables exploration of mean-field anisotropy effects in heavy-ion collision simulations using the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) equation, where the computational effort scales linearly with test-particle number Nt rather than quadratically as in the Welke et al. approach. The authors note that earlier efforts by Gaitanos et al. (1999, 2004) and Fuchs et al. (2003) specifically addressed early-stage momentum anisotropies using two-Fermi-sphere superpositions.
