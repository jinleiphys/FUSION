# Lattice Study of the High Density State of SU(2)-QCD

> We investigate high density state of SU(2) QCD by using Lattice QCD simulation with Wilson fermions. The ratio of fermion determinants is evaluated at each step of the Metropol is link update by Woodbury formula. At $β=0.7$, and $κ= 0.150$, we calculate the baryon number density, the Polyakov lines, and the energy density of gluon sector with chemical potential $μ$=0 to 0.8 on the $4^{3} \times 12$ lattice. Behavior of the meson propagators and diquark propagators with finite chemical potential are also investigated.

# Lattice Study of the High Density State of SU(2)-QCD

## Key claim

The authors present evidence that SU(2) QCD with Wilson fermions at β=0.7 and κ=0.150 exhibits a confinement/deconfinement transition in the chemical potential range 0.4 < μ < 0.8, but they hedge that none of the measured quantities is an exact order parameter and the lattice size is too small to show a sharp change. The diquark propagator behavior does not reproduce the parallel structure reported by Hands (1999) that would indicate diquark condensation, though the authors note their statistics are insufficient for a definitive conclusion.

## Method

The authors perform lattice QCD simulations of SU(2) with Wilson fermions and the Iwasaki improved gauge action on a 4³ × 12 lattice at β=0.7 and κ=0.150, scanning chemical potential μ from 0 to 0.8. They use a locally updating exact algorithm based on the Woodbury formula to evaluate the ratio of fermion determinants at each Metropolis link update, updating link variables on 2⁴ hypercubes. They compute the baryon number density, Polyakov lines, gluon energy density, and propagators for mesons (π, a₀, ρ) and diquarks (scalar and pseudoscalar) as functions of chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- Simulation performed on a 4³ × 12 lattice at β = 0.7 and κ = 0.150
- Chemical potential range scanned: μ = 0 to 0.8 (in lattice units)
- All measured quantities (Polyakov line, gluon energy density, baryon number density) begin to acquire non-zero values at approximately μ = 0.4 and rise in the region 0.4 < μ < 0.8
- Numerical convergence degrades with increasing chemical potential, and at μ = 1.0 the vanishing determinant causes simulation breakdown

## Context

This work extends the finite-density lattice QCD program for SU(2) initiated by Nakamura (1984) and responds to the proposal by Kogut (2000) that "QCD-like" theories such as SU(2)-QCD can inform real SU(3)-QCD at finite density. The authors compare their diquark propagator results to those of Hands (1999), finding that unlike that work, their scalar and pseudoscalar diquark propagators do not become parallel at finite chemical potential, which would have indicated diquark condensation. The study enables future work on eigenvalue distributions of the fermion matrix at finite chemical potential and mass extraction in the chiral limit, which the authors report as in progress.
