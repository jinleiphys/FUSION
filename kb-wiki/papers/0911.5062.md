# Comparison of Self-Consistent Skyrme and Gogny Calculations for Light Hg Isotopes

> The ground-state properties of neutron-deficient Hg isotopes have been investigated by the constrained self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approach with the Skyrme and Gogny effective forces. In the case of the Skyrme interaction we h ave also applied the Hartree-Fock+BCS model with the state-dependent $δ$-pairing interaction. Potential energy surfaces and pairing properties have been compared for the both types of forces.

## Key claim

The constrained self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approach with Skyrme and Gogny effective forces yields quite similar deformation dependence of the potential energy surfaces for light Hg isotopes, despite differences in absolute binding energies; however, the SkM* parameterization fails to reproduce experimental binding energies in this region, overestimating them by around 10 MeV.

## Method

The authors performed constrained self-consistent HFB calculations for the neutron-deficient isotopes 178,180,182,184Hg using the SkM* and SLy4 parameterizations of the Skyrme force and the D1S, D1N, and D1M parameter sets of the Gogny force. For the Skyrme interaction, they also applied the Hartree-Fock+BCS (HF+BCS) model with a state-dependent δ-pairing interaction. The Skyrme calculations used the symmetry-unrestricted code HFODD with a Cartesian deformed harmonic-oscillator basis, while the Gogny calculations used a deformed axial basis; potential energy surfaces and pairing properties were compared across all models.

## Key numbers

- Pairing strengths for the SkM*-BCS model: V₀ⁿ = -372.0 MeV, V₀ᵖ = -438.0 MeV; for SkM*-HFB: V₀ⁿ = -268.9 MeV, V₀ᵖ = -332.5 MeV; for SLy4-HFB: V₀ⁿ = -308.0 MeV, V₀ᵖ = -343.4 MeV; all adjusted to reproduce experimental pairing gaps in 252Fm.
- The deepest oblate minimum at Q₂ ≈ -10 b lies no more than 1.2 MeV above the prolate ground state for Gogny forces, and even less for Skyrme forces.
- Potential energy surfaces calculated with D1N and D1M Gogny forces lie higher by about 6 MeV than values obtained with the D1S force.
- The Skyrme SLy4-HFB model potential energy surfaces are shifted up by around 3 MeV relative to the D1S Gogny force values.
- The SkM*-HFB and SkM*-MIX models overestimate experimental binding energies by around 10 MeV, the worst agreement among all models tested.
- For 180Hg and 182Hg, the prolate ground state at β ≈ 0.3 (Q₂ = 20 b) is best seen in all models, in accordance with experimental results from Grahn (2009).

## Context

This work extends the systematic comparison of Skyrme and Gogny effective forces in the context of shape coexistence in neutron-deficient Hg isotopes near the Z=82 shell closure and the N=104 mid-shell, following studies by Wood (1992), Andreyev (2000), and Julin (2001). The results show that while the D1S Gogny set gives binding energies closest to experiment for these isotopes, the SkM* Skyrme set fails in absolute binding energy prediction. The findings enable future tests of mean-field models using self-consistent treatment of quadrupole collective excitations via the Generator Coordinate Method or Adiabatic Time Dependent HFB method, as noted by the authors.
