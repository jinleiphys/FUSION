# Comment on "Subleading Corrections to Parity Violating Pion Photoproduction"

> In a recent preprint, hep-ph/0012253, Zhu et al. calculated a part of the next-to-leading order corrections to parity-violating pion photoproduction (pol)gamma p-> pi^+ n in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory. They claim they have found the contribution as large as the leading-order. If correct, the process will not be a clean way to extract the longest range PV pion-nucleon coupling constant h_{πNN}^(1), as was asserted in our previous publications (hep-ph/0011230, nucl-th/0011100). In this comment we show that there is no solid evidence to support Zhu et al.'s claim. Moreover, we show that the subleading parity-violating coupling h_V cannot be extracted from observables at the order of interest based on the formalism of effective field theory.

# Comment on "Subleading Corrections to Parity Violating Pion Photoproduction"

## Key claim
The authors argue that there is no solid evidence that the subleading parity-violating coupling constant \(h_V\) is enhanced or that the leading-order coupling \(h_{\pi NN}^{(1)}\) is suppressed relative to natural power-counting expectations. They further claim that \(h_V\) cannot be extracted independently from observables at the order of interest, because it only appears in combination with other unknown parameters after field redefinitions.

## Method
The authors analyze the effective chiral Lagrangian for parity-violating pion photoproduction \(\overrightarrow{\gamma}p \rightarrow \pi^+ n\) in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HB\(\chi\)PT). They perform a field redefinition of the nucleon fields to eliminate the equation-of-motion operator containing \(h_V\), showing that it can be absorbed into redefinitions of the couplings \(h_A^{(2)}\) and \(C\). They then compare the natural size of \(h_V\) from naive dimensional analysis with the claims of Zhu et al. (2000) that its contribution could be as large as the leading-order term.

## Key numbers
- The natural size of \(h_{\pi NN}^{(1)}\) from naive dimensional analysis is \(|h_{\pi NN}^{(1)}| \sim 5 \times 10^{-7}\), consistent with the DDH "best guesstimate" (Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein, 1980).
- The natural size of \(h_V\) from naive dimensional analysis is \(|h_V| \sim 5 \times 10^{-8}\), which is a factor of 10 smaller than \(h_{\pi NN}^{(1)}\).
- If natural power counting holds, \(h_V\) contributes only at the 10% level to the asymmetry in \(\overrightarrow{\gamma}p \rightarrow \pi^+ n\), according to Eq. (13) of Zhu et al. (2000).

## Context
This comment directly responds to the claim by Zhu et al. (2000, hep-ph/0012253) that next-to-leading order corrections to parity-violating pion photoproduction could be as large as the leading-order term, which would undermine the extraction of \(h_{\pi NN}^{(1)}\) proposed by Chen and Ji (2000, hep-ph/0011230 and nucl-th/0011100). The analysis extends the effective field theory approach of Kaplan and Savage (1993) and Maekawa, Veiga, and van Kolck (2000), and it notes that the SAMPLE experiment (Hasty, 2000) on PV \(\vec{e}-p\) and \(\vec{e}-d\) scattering provides the only current constraint on \(h_V\), though that constraint is unreliable due to breakdown of power counting in the radiative corrections. The result enables a cleaner interpretation of future PV photoproduction experiments by clarifying which couplings are independently measurable.
