# Revisiting heavy quark radiative energy loss in nuclei within the high-twist approach

> We revisit the calculation of multiple parton scattering of a heavy quark in nuclei within the framework of recently improved high-twist factorization formalism, in which gauge invariance is ensured by a delicate setup of the initial partons' transverse momenta. We derive a new result for medium modified heavy quark fragmentation functions in deeply inelastic scattering. It is consistent with the previous calculation of light quark energy loss in the massless limit, but leads to a new correction term in the heavy quark case, which vanishes in the soft gluon radiation limit. We show numerically the significance of the new correction term in the calculation of heavy quark energy loss as compared to previous studies and with soft gluon radiation approximation.

## Key claim

The authors derive a new gauge-invariant result for medium-modified heavy quark fragmentation functions in deeply inelastic scattering, which differs from previous calculations by a correction term proportional to the heavy quark mass squared that vanishes in both the soft gluon radiation limit (z→1) and the massless limit (M=0).

## Method

The calculation applies the recently improved high-twist factorization formalism to heavy quark production via charged-current interactions in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering off nuclear targets, ensuring gauge invariance through a delicate assignment of initial partons' transverse momenta. The authors compute all quark-gluon double scattering subprocesses (soft-soft, hard-hard, and their interferences) as well as interference between single and triple scatterings, using light-cone gauge for the radiated gluon to simplify the diagram selection. The final result is expressed as a medium-modified heavy quark fragmentation function that factorizes the twist-4 quark-gluon correlation functions from the perturbatively calculable splitting kernels.

## Key numbers

* The new correction term in the soft-hard interference subprocess is proportional to (1−z)²M²/ℓ_T², vanishing in the limits z→1 or M=0, as shown in Eq. (27) for the central-cut diagrams.
* The hard partonic calculation uses the Lorentz invariants x_B = Q²/(2p·q), x_M = M²/(2p·q), and z = p·ℓ_Q/(p·q), where M is the heavy quark mass and Q² is the virtuality of the exchanged W boson.
* The collinear expansion yields a factor 2C_A ℓ_T⁴ / [ℓ_T² + (1−z)²M²]⁴ in the integrand for the double scattering contributions, with C_A = 3.

## Context

This work extends the high-twist factorization approach previously applied to light quark energy loss in SIDIS by Guo and Wang (2000) and Wang (2001), and to heavy quark energy loss by Zhang and Wang (2004). The key improvement over Zhang and Wang (2004) is the use of a gauge-invariant transverse momentum assignment for initial partons, following the technique developed by Kang, Vitev, and Xing (2014) for light hadron production. The new correction terms enable more accurate calculations of heavy quark energy loss in nuclear media, which is relevant for interpreting jet quenching data from heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC.
