# Absence of the London limit for the first-order phase transition to a color superconductor

> We study the effects of gauge-field fluctuations on the free energy of a homogeneous color superconductor in the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase. Gluonic fluctuations induce a strong first-order phase transition, in contrast to electronic superconductors where this transition is weakly first order. The critical temperature for this transition is larger than the one corresponding to the diquark pairing instability. The physical reason is that the gluonic Meissner masses suppress long-wavelength fluctuations as compared to the normal conducting phase where gluons are massless, which stabilizes the superconducting phase. In weak coupling, we analytically compute the temperatures associated with the limits of metastability of the normal and superconducting phases, as well as the latent heat associated with the first-order phase transition. We then extrapolate our results to intermediate densities and numerically evaluate the temperature of the fluctuation-induced first-order phase transition, as well as the discontinuity of the diquark condensate at the critical point. We find that the London limit of magnetic interactions is absent in color superconductivity.

## Key claim

Gauge-field fluctuations induce a strong first-order phase transition to the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase, with a critical temperature larger than the diquark pairing instability temperature; the London limit of magnetic interactions is absent in color superconductivity, meaning the coupling between the gauge field and the order parameter remains nonlocal even near the transition.

## Method

The authors used the Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis (CJT) effective potential at two-loop approximation to compute the free energy difference between the CFL superconducting phase and the normal phase, including gluonic fluctuation contributions via a plasmon ring resummation. They analytically solved the resulting generalized Ginzburg-Landau (GL) free energy in weak coupling to obtain the characteristic temperatures of the first-order transition, then numerically extrapolated to intermediate densities using a 3-loop running coupling with three flavors and three colors.

## Key numbers

- The transition temperature in weak coupling: \( T_c^* = \left(1 + \frac{\pi^2}{12\sqrt{2}} g\right) T_c \), where \( T_c \) is the diquark pairing instability temperature.
- The discontinuity of the diquark condensate at the transition in weak coupling: \( \Delta_{c^*}^2 = \frac{\pi^2}{63\zeta(3)} g^2 T_c^2 \).
- The latent heat in weak coupling: \( L = \frac{2g^2}{21\zeta(3)} \mu^2 T_c^2 \).
- The strength parameter of the first-order transition: \( t_{\rm HLM}/T_c = g^2/36 \).
- The ratio \( \xi_0/\lambda_{c^*} \gg 1 \) for all chemical potentials explored, with a minimum near \( \mu = 700 \) MeV, confirming the Pippard limit applies.
- The superheated phase temperature: \( T_{\rm sh} = \left[1 + \frac{g^2}{72}(1-\ln 2)\right] T_c^* \).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Noronha et al., 2005) on gauge-field fluctuations in color superconductors and contrasts with the treatment of Baym et al. (1990), who omitted the exchange energy subtraction and used a constant Meissner mass, leading to a London-limit approximation and a smaller shift in transition temperature. The results rule out the crossover from nonlocal (Pippard) to local (London) interactions near the critical temperature that has been observed in strong type-I electronic superconductors like aluminum (Bonalde, 2000). The findings enable future studies of nonlocal diquark-gluon interactions in inhomogeneous GL theories for vortex formation and in phases with nonzero quark masses and charge neutrality.
