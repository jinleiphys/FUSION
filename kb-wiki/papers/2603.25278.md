# Binding Energy of Muonic Beryllium: Perturbative versus All--Order Calculations

> We compute the ground-state binding energy of muonic $^9$Be in two ways: first, the fully perturbative treatment of the nuclear-size effect often employed in light systems, and second, an approach that accounts for the finite-nuclear-size to all orders (and is inspired by calculations otherwise employed for heavy muonic ions). The results are compared term by term and show that both approaches agree to better than one part-per-million of the total energy. The objective of this work is twofold. The first is practical: to provide a parametrization that allows the extraction of the $^9$Be charge radius from recent and forthcoming experiments with high precision. The second is more conceptual: to act as a bridge between the community working on calculations for light systems and those focusing on heavy systems, demonstrating that the fully relativistic approach otherwise chosen for heavy systems can be enhanced to cover theoretical predictions for all charge numbers.

## Key claim

The ground-state binding energy of muonic \(^9\)Be computed via a fully perturbative treatment of the finite-nuclear-size effect (typical for light systems) agrees with an all-order (in nuclear size) relativistic approach (typical for heavy systems) to better than one part-per-million of the total energy, specifically to within 41 meV for the sum of the main contributions, validating the hybrid framework for intermediate-Z nuclei.

## Method

The authors computed the ground-state binding energy of muonic \(^9\)Be using two independent methods: (1) a fully perturbative approach based on nonrelativistic quantum electrodynamics (NRQED) expanded in the finite-size parameter \(\rho = r_C/a_0\), and (2) an all-order approach solving the Dirac equation with a finite-size Gaussian charge distribution and the Uehling potential self-consistently using the MDFGME code. They compared term-by-term contributions including Dirac-Coulomb-Uehling, nonrelativistic recoil, Källén-Sabry vacuum polarization, muon self-energy, muonic and hadronic Uehling corrections, and their recoil corrections, using a Gaussian charge distribution with RMS radius \(r_C = 2.519\) fm and nuclear mass \(M = 9.009989\) amu.

## Key numbers

- Dirac-Coulomb-Uehling energy (all-order): \(-45\,072.59\) eV; perturbative sum: \(-45\,072.62\) eV; difference: 30 meV
- Nonrelativistic recoil correction (all-order): \(556.50\) eV; perturbative sum: \(556.51\) eV; difference: \(-3\) meV
- Källén-Sabry two-loop eVP (all-order): \(-1.00\) eV; perturbative point-nucleus: \(-1.01\) eV; difference: 8 meV
- Muon self-energy (all-order): \(1.17\) eV; perturbative point-nucleus: \(1.17\) eV; difference: \(-11\) meV
- Muonic Uehling (all-order): \(-0.05\) eV; perturbative point-nucleus: \(-0.05\) eV; difference: 4 meV
- Hadronic Uehling (all-order): \(-0.03\) eV; perturbative point-nucleus: \(-0.03\) eV; difference: 3 meV
- Nonrelativistic eVP recoil (all-order): \(2.89\) eV; perturbative sum: \(2.88\) eV; difference: 10 meV
- Nonrelativistic Källén-Sabry recoil (all-order): \(0.02\) eV; perturbative: \(0.02\) eV; difference: 0 meV
- Sum of main contributions (all-order): \(-44\,513.08\) eV; perturbative: \(-44\,513.12\) eV; difference: 41 meV
- Recommended total binding energy: \(-44\,513.07(2)\) eV

## Context

This work extends the hybrid theoretical framework introduced by Rathi et al. (2026) that treats finite nuclear size non-perturbatively for all charge numbers, bridging the gap between NRQED methods used for \(Z=1,2\) (e.g., Antognini 2013, Pohl 2016) and fully relativistic calculations for heavy muonic ions (e.g., Beyer 2022, Nesterenko 2023). The results provide a parametrization for extracting the \(^9\)Be charge radius from recent and forthcoming muonic-atom x-ray spectroscopy experiments at intermediate \(Z\) (\(3 \le Z < 30\)), where neither NRQED nor fully relativistic frameworks alone perform well.
