# Larger Domains from Resonant Decay of Disoriented Chiral Condensates

> The decay of disoriented chiral condensates into soft pions is considered within the context of a linear sigma model. Unlike earlier analytic studies, which focused on the production of pions as the sigma field rolled down toward its new equilibrium value, here we focus on the amplification of long-wavelength pion modes due to parametric resonance as the sigma field oscillates around the minimum of its potential. This process can create larger domains of pion fluctuations than the usual spinodal decomposition process, and hence may provide a viable experimental signature for chiral symmetry breaking in relativistic heavy ion collisions; it may also better explain physically the large growth of domains found in several numerical simulations.

## Key claim

Parametric resonance from the oscillating sigma field, rather than spinodal decomposition from the rolling sigma field, can create disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) domains of 3-5 fm, which may be large enough to produce observable experimental signatures in relativistic heavy ion collisions; this mechanism may also explain the large domain growth found in numerical simulations that spinodal decomposition alone cannot account for.

## Method

The author studies the decay of DCCs within a linear sigma model using a Hartree approximation, writing the sigma field as a homogeneous zero mode plus fluctuations and replacing pion self-interactions with mean-field terms. The coupled equations of motion for the sigma zero mode and the pion mode functions are solved analytically for early times after the sigma field begins oscillating around its minimum, neglecting backreaction until the pion fluctuation integral equals the time-averaged sigma field squared. The sigma field's oscillations are described by a Jacobian elliptic function, reducing the pion equation to a Lame equation of order one, from which the characteristic Floquet exponent and the two-point correlation function are computed to determine domain sizes.

## Key numbers

- Domain sizes from parametric resonance lie between 3-5 fm over much of the range \(1 \leq \varphi_0 \leq \sqrt{2}\) for the initial oscillation amplitude.
- The parametric resonance lasts until \(t_{\rm end} \sim 5\) fm/\(c\), compared to the spinodal decomposition timescale \(t_{\rm spinodal} \simeq \sqrt{2}/m_\sigma \simeq 0.47\) fm/\(c\).
- The sigma field parameters are \(\lambda = 20.0\), \(v = 87.4\) MeV, yielding \(m_\sigma = 600\) MeV, with pion mass \(m_\pi = 135\) MeV and pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 92.5\) MeV.
- The resonance band includes modes with \(k \leq m_\pi\) for all \(\varphi_0 \geq 1.23\), with the largest Floquet exponents \(Re[\mu_\kappa] \sim 0.1-0.3\) near the band center.
- Spinodal decomposition alone produces domains of order 1.4 fm, as found by Gavin, Gocksch, and Pisarski (1994).

## Context

This work extends earlier analytic studies by Rajagopal and Wilczek (1993) and Gavin, Gocksch, and Pisarski (1994) that considered only spinodal decomposition during the sigma field's roll to equilibrium, which produced domains too small (about 1.4 fm) for clear experimental signatures. The parametric resonance mechanism is analogous to cosmological post-inflation reheating studied by Kofman, Linde, and Starobinsky (1994) and improves upon the earlier application by Mrowczynski and Muller (1995) by using the correct Lame equation rather than the Mathieu equation, yielding a single resonance band with different characteristic exponents. Future work should incorporate plasma expansion effects and scatterings neglected in the Hartree approximation.
