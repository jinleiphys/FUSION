# A microscopic equation of state for neutron-rich matter and its effect on neutron star properties

> Chapter prepared for the book "Astrophysics", ISBN 979-953-307-389-6, INTECH Publishers (in press).

## Key claim

The Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach provides a parameter-free microscopic equation of state for neutron-rich matter that is moderately soft at low to medium density but fairly stiff at high densities, with the caveat that the predicted symmetry pressure of approximately 70 MeV falls within the range of constraints from heavy-ion data but lies on the higher side of flow-data constraints for symmetric matter pressure.

## Method

The author applies the DBHF method to isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter, starting from a realistic one-boson-exchange nucleon-nucleon potential (Bonn B) fit to free-space scattering data with no free parameters in the medium. The in-medium reaction matrix is solved self-consistently using the Thompson three-dimensional reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation, with the nucleon self-energy parametrized by scalar and vector fields that are determined iteratively for neutrons and protons separately. The energy per particle is then computed from the average kinetic and potential energies for each species and combined to obtain the equation of state as a function of density and asymmetry parameter.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: 0.185 fm\(^{-3}\)
- Saturation energy per particle of symmetric nuclear matter: -16.14 MeV
- Compression modulus of symmetric nuclear matter: 252 MeV
- Symmetry pressure \(L\): approximately 70 MeV
- Symmetry energy at nuclear matter density: typical value 30 MeV, with theoretical predictions spreading between 26 and 35 MeV
- Exponent \(\gamma_i\) in the potential part of the symmetry energy parametrization: between 0.4 and 1.0, with elliptic flow measurements favoring \(0.9 \pm 0.4\)
- Symmetry pressure \(L\) from heavy-ion data: \(85 \pm 25\) MeV
- Symmetry pressure \(L\) from global optical potential analyses: \(52.7 \pm 22.5\) MeV

## Context

This work extends the DBHF approach previously applied to symmetric nuclear matter to the case of isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter, building on the formalism developed by Brockmann and Machleidt (1984) and the author's earlier work with collaborators. The microscopic predictions are compared with phenomenological parametrizations of the symmetry energy from heavy-ion collision analyses by groups at GSI, MSU, and other facilities, as well as with flow data constraints from Danielewicz et al. (2002). The results enable more reliable predictions of neutron star properties, including mass-radius relations and proton fractions, and can be tested against upcoming measurements of neutron skins from the PREX experiment at Jefferson Lab.
