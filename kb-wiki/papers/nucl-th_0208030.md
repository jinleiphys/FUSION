# Influence of final state interaction on incoherent pion photoproduction on the deuteron in the region of the Delta-resonance

> The influence of final state $NN$- and pi N-rescattering in incoherent pion photoproduction on the deuteron has been investigated. For the elementary photoproduction operator an effective Lagrangian model is used which describes well the elementary reaction. The interactions in the final two-body subsystems are taken in separable form. While NN-rescattering shows quite a significant effect, particularly strong for neutral pion production, pi N-rescattering is almost negligible. Inclusion of such effects leads to an improved and quite satisfactory agreement with experiment.

## Key claim

Final state NN-rescattering has a significant effect on incoherent pion photoproduction on the deuteron, particularly strong for neutral pion production, while πN-rescattering is almost negligible; inclusion of these effects leads to improved but not perfect agreement with experiment, with the model still overestimating the measured total cross section for π⁰ production by about 6% at the maximum.

## Method

The authors used an effective Lagrangian model for the elementary photoproduction operator, including pseudovector Born terms and the Δ(1232) resonance, with parameters fitted to the experimental M_{1+}^{3/2} multipole. The final state interactions in the NN and πN subsystems were treated using separable potentials: the Paris potential for NN scattering (all partial waves with J ≤ 3) and the separable energy-dependent πN potential of NoB90 (all S-, P-, and D-waves). The three contributions to the transition matrix (impulse approximation, NN-rescattering, and πN-rescattering) were evaluated numerically, with the deuteron wave function taken from the Paris potential.

## Key numbers

- NN-rescattering reduces the total cross section for neutral pion production by about 60% at 175 MeV and about 35% in the Δ(1232) resonance region
- For charged pion photoproduction, FSI effects are relatively small, not more than about 5% of the total cross section
- πN-rescattering is about an order of magnitude smaller than NN-rescattering in the Δ resonance region; for neutral pion production it amounts to about 40% of the total FSI effect near 175 MeV
- The model overestimates the measured total cross section for π⁰ production by about 6% at the maximum
- At forward angles (θ_π = 0°), NN-rescattering reduces the differential cross section by about 15% for charged pions and about 40% for neutral pions
- More than 70% of the differential cross section at θ_π = 0° for charged pion production comes from the Born terms
- The Δ(1232) resonance mass used was 1233 MeV; the pion-nucleon coupling constant was f²_{πN}/4π = 0.069

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous impulse approximation study (Schmidt et al., 1996) by including final state interactions in the NN and πN subsystems. It confirms and quantifies earlier findings by Laget (1978, 1981) and Levchuk et al. (1996, 2000) that NN-rescattering causes strong reduction of the cross section for neutral pion production, particularly at forward angles. The results are compared with experimental data from Becchi et al. (1973) for π⁻ production and from Krusche et al. (1999) for π⁰ production, and with theoretical predictions from Levchuk et al. (2000) who used the Bonn r-space potential and SAID/MAID multipole analyses. The work enables future studies of three-body effects and unitarity violations that are not captured by the present two-body rescattering approximation.
