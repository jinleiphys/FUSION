# A model-independent analysis of p + p --> pp(1S0) + gamma

> The pp hard bremsstrahlung reaction, p + p --> pp(1S0) + gamma, in which the two protons in the final state are in the 1S0 state, is investigated theoretically. Here, the most general spin structure of the NN hard bremsstrahlung reaction, consistent with symmetry principles, is derived from a partial-wave expansion of this amplitude. Based on this spin structure, it is shown that there are only four independent spin matrix elements in this reaction, which is a direct consequence of reflection symmetry in the reaction plane. It is also shown that it requires at least eight independent observables to determine them uniquely. The present method provides the coefficients multiplying each spin operator in terms of the partial-wave or, equivalently, multipole amplitudes. Some observables are expressed explicitly in terms of these multipoles and a partial-wave analysis is performed. The results should be useful in the analyses of the experimental data on the p + p --> pp(1S0) + gamma reaction being taken, in particular, at the COSY accelerator facility, as well as in providing some theoretical guidance to the future experiments in this area.

## Key claim

The paper derives the most general spin structure of the NN hard bremsstrahlung amplitude for the reaction p + p → pp(¹S₀) + γ, showing that there are only four independent spin matrix elements (a consequence of reflection symmetry in the reaction plane) and that at least eight independent observables are required to determine them uniquely, though the authors note that a complete determination "poses an enormous experimental challenge."

## Method

The authors performed a partial-wave expansion of the NN bremsstrahlung reaction amplitude, then restricted the final NN subsystem to the ¹S₀ state to derive the most general spin structure consistent with symmetry principles. They expressed the coefficients multiplying each spin operator in terms of partial-wave (multipole) amplitudes and related these to observables such as the unpolarized cross section, analyzing powers, and spin correlation coefficients. A partial-wave analysis was performed restricting total angular momentum J ≤ 2, yielding explicit expressions for observables in terms of the multipole amplitudes E₁₁₁, M₂₁₁, M₂₁₃, and E₂₀₂.

## Key numbers

- The unpolarized cross section for J ≤ 2 is given by dσ/dΩ = (3/(8π)³){|E₁₁₁+M2⁺|² + [2|E₁₁₁−M2⁺|² − |E₁₁₁+M2⁺|²]cos²θ_k + [10|E₂₀₂|² + 4(|M2⁻|²−|M2⁺|²)]cos²θ_k sin²θ_k}, where M2± ≡ M₂₁₁ ± (3/2)^(∓1/2) M₂₁₃.
- The CELSIUS data at 310 MeV proton incident energy show an angular distribution dσ/dΩ_k = (3/(8π))[a + 3b cos²θ_k + c sin²θ_k cos²θ_k] with a = (2.3 ± 0.3) nb, b = (11.9 ± 0.5) nb, and c < 2 nb.
- The invariant mass of the two protons in the final state is constrained experimentally to be less than 3 MeV above its minimum value of twice the proton mass.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for NN bremsstrahlung reactions, which have been studied primarily to learn about off-shell properties of the NN interaction, though the authors note that in field theories off-shell properties are unmeasurable. The reaction p + p → pp(¹S₀) + γ was measured for the first time by the COSY-ANKE Collaboration (ANKE1, ANKE2) and at CELSIUS-Uppsala (JW09) as an alternative to the γ + pp(¹S₀) → p + p process, which is complementary to deuteron photo-disintegration. The present results should be useful for analyzing experimental data being taken at the COSY accelerator facility and for providing theoretical guidance to future experiments.
