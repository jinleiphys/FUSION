# Baryon chiral perturbation theory: an update

> The issue of consistent power counting in baryon chiral perturbation theory is revisited.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the Extended on-mass-shell (EOMS) scheme provides a consistent power counting in baryon chiral perturbation theory (BχPT), whereas the Heavy-baryon (HB) and Infrared Regularization (IR) schemes suffer from either unnatural higher-order coefficients or incorrect analytic properties, depending on the observable.

## Method

The author compares three versions of baryon chiral perturbation theory, Heavy-baryon (HBχPT), Infrared Regularization (IR-BχPT), and Extended on-mass-shell (EOMS-BχPT), by evaluating the chiral-loop correction to the nucleon mass at order p³. The calculation uses the standard chiral Lagrangian with pseudovector πN couplings, and the resulting self-energy is renormalized in each scheme to examine whether the power-counting formula (Weinberg’s counting) is preserved. The analytic structure and the size of nominally higher-order terms are then contrasted across schemes.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass M_N ≈ 939 MeV, axial coupling g_A ≈ 1.267, pion decay constant f_π ≈ 92.4 MeV (used in the loop calculation).
- Δ(1232) excitation energy Δ ≡ M_Δ − M_N ≈ 293 MeV.
- In the EOMS scheme, the p³ contribution to the nucleon mass is proportional to m_π³, with the coefficient containing a factor √(1 − m_π²/(4M_N²)) arccos(m_π/(2M_N)) + (m_π/(2M_N)) ln(m_π/M_N).
- In the HB scheme, the p³ contribution reduces to −(3 g_A² m_π³)/( (4π f_π)² ) × (π/2).
- The magnetic polarizability of the proton β_p at order p³ expands as (α_em g_A²)/(192 π f_π² M_N) × { M_N/m_π + (72/π) ln(m_π/M_N) + 126/π − (981/8)(m_π/M_N) + O(m_π²) }, showing large coefficients for nominally higher-order terms.

## Context

This work revisits the long-standing issue of power-counting consistency in baryon chiral perturbation theory, first raised by Gasser, Sainio, and Svarc (1988). It extends the comparison of renormalization schemes performed by Geng et al. (2009) for SU(3) baryon magnetic moments, and it enables reliable next-to-next-to-leading-order calculations of observables such as proton Compton scattering (Lensky 2009) and nucleon and Δ(1232) electromagnetic form factors (Ledwig 2011). The paper concludes that the EOMS scheme is favored over HBχPT and IR-BχPT for maintaining natural coefficients and correct analytic properties.
