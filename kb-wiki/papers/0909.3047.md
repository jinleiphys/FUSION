# Study of Higgs boson production and its b-bbar decay in gamma-gamma processes in proton-nucleus collisions at the LHC

> We explore for the first time the possibilities to measure an intermediate-mass (mH = 115-140 GeV/c^2) Standard-Model Higgs boson in electromagnetic proton-lead (pPb) interactions at the CERN Large Hadron Collider (LHC) via its b-bbar decay. Using equivalent Weizsaecker-Williams photon fluxes and Higgs effective field theory for the coupling gamma-gamma --> H, we obtain a leading-order cross section of the order of 0.3 pb for exclusive Higgs production in elastic (pPb --> gamma-gamma --> p H Pb) and semielastic (pPb --> gamma-gamma --> Pb H X) processes at sqrt(s) = 8.8 TeV. After applying various kinematics cuts to remove the main backgrounds (gamma-gamma --> b-bbar and misidentified gamma-gamma-->q-qbar events), we find that a Higgs boson with mH = 120 GeV/c^2 could be observed in the b-bbar channel with a 3sigma-significance integrating 300 pb^-1 with an upgraded pA luminosity of 10^31 cm^-2s^-1. We also provide for the first time semielastic Higgs cross sections, along with elastic t-tbar cross sections, for electromagnetic pp, pA and AA collisions at the LHC.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that an intermediate-mass Standard Model Higgs boson (mH = 115-140 GeV/c²) decaying to b-bbar could be observed in exclusive and semielastic photon-photon collisions in proton-lead (pPb) interactions at the LHC, provided an upgraded pA luminosity of 10³¹ cm⁻²s⁻¹ can be achieved. The claim is conditional on integrating 300 pb⁻¹ of data and applying specific kinematic cuts to suppress backgrounds.

## Method

The authors computed leading-order cross sections for exclusive Higgs production in elastic (pPb → γγ → p H Pb) and semielastic (pPb → γγ → Pb H X) processes using equivalent Weizsäcker-Williams photon fluxes for the proton and lead ion, combined with Higgs effective field theory for the γγ → H coupling. They implemented the photon spectra in the MadGraph event generator, using the dipole approximation for the proton elastic form factor and a hard-sphere impact-parameter formulation for the nuclear photon flux, and applied kinematic cuts to reject the dominant backgrounds from γγ → b-bbar and misidentified γγ → q-qbar events.

## Key numbers

- Leading-order cross section of approximately 0.3 pb for exclusive Higgs production in elastic and semielastic pPb processes at sqrt(s_NN) = 8.8 TeV
- A Higgs boson with mH = 120 GeV/c² could be observed in the b-bbar channel with 3-sigma significance after integrating 300 pb⁻¹ at an upgraded pA luminosity of 10³¹ cm⁻²s⁻¹
- Maximum two-photon center-of-mass energy for pPb collisions at the LHC is approximately 260 GeV, more than twice the most probable SM Higgs boson mass
- The effective two-photon luminosities for pPb at the LHC are similar to those for PbPb at low W_γγ and become higher for W_γγ > 50 GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of Higgs production via photon-photon fusion in pp collisions (Papageorgiu 1995) and PbPb collisions (Grabiak 1987, Drees 1989, Baltz 1997, Kryukov 2008) by considering the asymmetric pPb system, which combines the advantages of higher luminosity than PbPb (10³¹ vs 10²⁷ cm⁻²s⁻¹) and cleaner event topologies than pp due to reduced pileup. The study enables a potential measurement of the Higgs-b-quark Yukawa coupling at the LHC, which was previously considered inaccessible in the dominant H → b-bbar decay channel, and also provides an independent measurement of the Higgs-gamma coupling sensitive to new charged particles beyond the Standard Model.
