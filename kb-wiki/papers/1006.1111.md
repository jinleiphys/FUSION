# Scaling of Elliptic Flow, Recombination and Sequential Freeze-Out of Hadrons in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> The scaling properties of elliptic flow of hadrons produced in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions are investigated at low transverse momenta, $p_T\lsim 2$\,GeV. Utilizing empirical parameterizations of a thermalized fireball with collective-flow fields, Resonance Recombination Model (RRM) is employed to describe hadronization via quark coalescence at the hadronization transition. We reconfirm that RRM converts equilibrium quark distribution functions into equilibrated hadron spectra including the effects of space-momentum correlations on elliptic flow. This provides the basis for a controlled extraction of quark distributions of the bulk matter at hadronization from spectra of multi-strange hadrons which are believed to decouple close to the critical temperature. The resulting elliptic flow from empirical fits at RHIC exhibits transverse kinetic-energy and valence-quark scaling. Utilizing the well-established concept of sequential freeze-out we find that the scaling at low momenta can be extended to bulk hadrons ($Ï€$, $K$, $p$) at thermal freeze-out, and thus result in an overall description compatible with both equilibrium hydrodynamics and quark recombination.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Resonance Recombination Model (RRM) converts equilibrium quark distribution functions into equilibrated hadron spectra with identical collective properties, and that by incorporating sequential freeze-out (two distinct freeze-out temperatures for different hadron groups), the observed transverse kinetic-energy scaling of elliptic flow can be extended from multi-strange hadrons to bulk hadrons (pions, kaons, protons) at low transverse momenta, provided the hadronic phase does not significantly distort the spectra from their hadronization values.

## Method

The authors employ the Resonance Recombination Model (RRM), which implements quark coalescence via resonant $q\bar{q} \to M$ scattering using a Breit-Wigner cross section in a Boltzmann transport equation, satisfying both energy-momentum conservation and detailed balance. They use the RetiÃ¨re-Lisa (RL) blast-wave parameterization to model anisotropic flow fields with space-momentum correlations, fitting these parameters to STAR data for multi-strange hadrons ($\phi$, $\Xi$, $\Omega$) at $T_c = 180$ MeV (group-I) and separately for bulk hadrons ($p$, $K$) at $T_{\rm fo} = 110$ MeV (group-II) in semi-central Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV.

## Key numbers

- Hadronization temperature for group-I particles: $T_c = 180$ MeV; kinetic freeze-out temperature for group-II particles: $T_{\rm fo} = 110$ MeV
- Group-I fit parameters: $\rho_0 = 0.75$, $\rho_2 = 0.072$, $R_{\rm x} = 7.5$ fm, $R_{\rm y} = 8.9$ fm
- Group-II fit parameters: $\rho_0 = 0.93$, $\rho_2 = 0.055$, $R_{\rm x} = 11.0$ fm, $R_{\rm y} = 12.4$ fm
- Quark-number scaled $v_2$ saturates at about 7-8% for light and strange quarks at $p_t \simeq 1$ GeV
- $\phi$ meson width: $\Gamma_\phi = 0.05$ GeV; $D$ and $J/\psi$ widths: $\Gamma = 0.1$ GeV
- Strange quark mass: $m_s = 0.45$ GeV; charm quark mass: $m_c = 1.5$ GeV; light quark mass: $m_{u,d} = 0.3$ GeV

## Context

This work extends the Resonance Recombination Model developed by Ravagli et al. (2007, 2008) by applying it to the low-$p_T$ thermal regime with empirical blast-wave fits, rather than using dynamical Langevin simulations. It addresses the long-standing difficulty of incorporating realistic space-momentum correlations into quark coalescence models noted by Pratt (2004), and provides a framework to extract quark distribution functions at hadronization from multi-strange hadron data. The sequential freeze-out concept, supported by earlier blast-wave fits to RHIC and SPS data, enables a unified description that reconciles equilibrium hydrodynamics at low $p_T$ with quark recombination scaling, and opens the door to extracting partonic collective properties from experimental data.
