# Moments of $ϕ$ meson spectral functions in vacuum and nuclear matter

> Moments of the $ϕ$ meson spectral function in vacuum and in nuclear matter are analyzed, combining a model based on chiral SU(3) effective field theory (with kaonic degrees of freedom) and finite-energy QCD sum rules. For the vacuum we show that the spectral density is strongly constrained by a recent accurate measurement of the $e^+ e^- \to K^+ K^-$ cross section. In nuclear matter the $ϕ$ spectrum is modified by interactions of the decay kaons with the surrounding nuclear medium, leading to a significant broadening and an asymmetric deformation of the $ϕ$ meson peak. We demonstrate that both in vacuum and nuclear matter, the first two moments of the spectral function are compatible with finite-energy QCD sum rules. A brief discussion of the next-higher spectral moment involving strange four-quark condensates is also presented.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the first two moments of the phi meson spectral function in both vacuum and nuclear matter are consistent with finite-energy QCD sum rules, but only under the condition that the density dependence of the vector current-phi meson coupling is constrained to be small (at most about 2% at normal nuclear matter density).

## Method

The authors construct the phi meson spectral function using an improved vector dominance model with kaon loop self-energies, fitting the model parameters to BABAR Collaboration data for the e+e- -> K+K- cross section. For nuclear matter, they incorporate kaon-nucleon forward scattering amplitudes derived from chiral SU(3) effective field theory with coupled channels, including both S-wave and P-wave interactions. They then test the consistency of the resulting spectral functions with finite-energy QCD sum rules by comparing the zeroth and first moments of the spectral functions to operator product expansion predictions.

## Key numbers

- Bare mass of the phi meson from fit: mathring{m}_phi = 797 MeV
- Coupling constant from fit: g_phi = 0.74 x (-3g/sqrt(2)) approx -10.2
- Delineation scale sqrt(s_0) = 1.55 +/- 0.02 GeV from zeroth moment and sqrt(s_0) = 1.55 +/- 0.01 GeV from first moment in vacuum
- Vacuum averaged mass over spectrum up to sqrt(overline{s}) = 1.2 GeV: overline{m}_phi approx 1038 MeV
- In-medium phi meson width at normal nuclear matter density (rho_0 = 0.17 fm^-3) with only S-wave interactions: Gamma_phi approx 24 MeV
- In-medium phi meson width at normal nuclear matter density with S- and P-wave interactions: Gamma_phi approx 45 MeV (vacuum width is 4.3 MeV)
- In-medium averaged mass with S- and P-wave interactions: overline{m}_phi^*(rho=rho_0) approx 1022 MeV, a downward shift of about 16 MeV from vacuum
- Factorization parameter for four-quark condensate in vacuum: kappa_0 ~ 7 +/- 2 at sqrt(s_0) = 1.55 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous QCD sum rule studies of vector mesons in nuclear matter by Hatsuda, Klingl, Zschocke, and Gubler, using updated input from chiral SU(3) effective field theory and recent BABAR data. The analysis confirms findings of earlier chiral SU(3) model studies by Klingl and Cabrera that the in-medium phi meson mass shift is small (less than 2% of vacuum mass) while the broadening is significant. The results provide theoretical support for interpreting measurements from experiments at KEK, COSY-ANKE, and the planned E16 experiment at J-PARC, though the authors caution that their analysis is restricted to phi mesons at rest and finite-momentum effects require further study.
