# Radii and Binding Energies of Nuclei in the Alpha-Cluster Model

> The alpha-cluster model is based on two assumptions that the proton-neutron pair interactions are responsible for adherence between alpha-clusters and that the NN-interaction in the alpha-clusters is isospin independent. It allows one to estimate the Coulomb energy and the short range inter-cluster bond energy in dependence on the number of clusters. The charge radii are calculated on the number of alpha-clusters too. Unlike the Weizsacker formula in this model the binding energies of alpha-clusters and excess neutrons are estimated separately. The calculated values are in a good agreement with the experimental data.

# Radii and Binding Energies of Nuclei in the Alpha-Cluster Model

## Key claim

The alpha-cluster model, based on the assumptions that proton-neutron pair interactions govern inter-cluster adhesion and that NN-interactions within alpha-clusters are isospin independent, can separately estimate binding energies of alpha-clusters and excess neutrons to achieve agreement with experimental data comparable to the Weizsäcker formula; however, the model is explicitly applicable only to nuclei with zero or positive excess neutron number (ΔN ≥ 0), meaning every proton must be coupled with a neutron, which includes all stable nuclei and unstable nuclei near the beta-stability path.

## Method

The nuclear binding energy is decomposed into four components: nuclear force energy of alpha-clusters (calculated from cluster number and inter-cluster bond energies), Coulomb energy (computed using a spherical charged-body formula with a Coulomb radius derived from cluster number), surface tension energy (obtained from empirical values of the last cluster's Coulomb energy and parameterized as proportional to the square of the Coulomb radius), and excess neutron binding energy (summed from nn-pair energies fitted to experimental separation energies). The charge radius is calculated from the number of alpha-clusters using a constant alpha-cluster radius that takes three different values depending on the cluster number range.

## Key numbers

- The short-range inter-cluster bond energy εαα = 2.425 MeV, with nuclear force component εnucαα = 4.350 MeV and Coulomb repulsion component εCαα = 1.925 MeV, obtained from analysis of lightest nuclei with Z ≤ 6.
- The internal Coulomb energy of one alpha-cluster εCα = 0.764 MeV, equal to the Coulomb energy of 4He.
- The binding energy of a single alpha-cluster εα = 28.296 MeV, obtained from solving equations for light nuclei with experimental binding energies accurate to 1 keV.
- The Coulomb radius formula RC = 1.869 (Nα)1/3 fm, where Nα is the number of alpha-clusters.
- The radius of the last proton position Rp = 2.168 (Nα − 4)1/3 fm for nuclei with Z ≥ 10.
- The nn-pair binding energy is fitted by Enn_i = 22.5 − 1.358 i2/3 MeV, where i indexes the pair number.
- For 16466Gd, the model gives Coulomb energy 627 MeV and total nuclear force energy 1964 MeV, with ΔEb = 1 MeV, matching the Weizsäcker formula deviation of 1 MeV.
- The surface tension energy parameter γ1 = 0.471 MeV/fm2, obtained from fitting empirical ΔEα values.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous alpha-cluster model developments (Nie, 2004, 2005) that established the framework for calculating radii and binding energies using cluster number rather than mass number. It contrasts with the traditional Weizsäcker liquid-drop formula by treating alpha-cluster binding and excess neutron binding separately, and builds on the finding (Nie, 2003, 2004) that nuclear radii can be described independently of excess neutron number by R = const (Z)1/3. The model enables calculation of alpha-particle and deuteron separation energies across a wide range of nuclei, and provides a framework for estimating binding energies of nuclei with odd numbers of excess neutrons, which was not covered in the earlier formulations.
