# Thermo field hydrodynamic and kinetic equations of dense quantum nuclear systems

> Basic equations of nonequilibrium thermo field dynamics of dense quantum systems are presented. A formulation of nonequilibrium thermo field dynamics has been performed using the nonequilibrium statistical operator method by D.N.Zubarev. Hydrodynamic equations have been obtained in thermo field representation. Two levels of the description of kinetics and hydrodynamics of a dense nuclear matter are considered. The first one is a quantum system with strongly coupled states, the second one is a quark-gluon plasma. Generalized transfer equations of a consistent description of kinetics and hydrodynamics have been obtained, as well as limiting cases are considered.

## Key claim

The paper presents a formulation of nonequilibrium thermo field dynamics for dense quantum nuclear systems using the nonequilibrium statistical operator method of D.N. Zubarev, claiming that this approach provides a consistent description of kinetics and hydrodynamics for two distinct regimes: strongly coupled nuclear states and quark-gluon plasma. The hedge is that the method is presented as a formal framework with generalized transport equations, but the paper does not provide explicit numerical solutions or comparisons to experimental data.

## Method

The authors reformulate nonequilibrium statistical mechanics using thermo field dynamics, doubling the number of creation and annihilation operators by introducing tilde and non-tilde superoperators in a thermal Liouville space. They construct a nonequilibrium statistical operator by solving the Schrodinger equation for the thermo vacuum state vector with a boundary condition that selects retarded solutions, using the Kawasaki-Gunton projection operator method in thermo field representation. Generalized transport equations for the consistent description of kinetics and hydrodynamics are then derived for two levels: strongly coupled quantum systems and quark-gluon plasma.

## Key numbers

- Electron density in the corona of laser fusion targets: \(n_e \sim 10^{21} \div 10^{22}\) cm\(^{-3}\)
- Ion density in the core of laser fusion targets: \(n \sim 10^{24} \div 10^{26}\) cm\(^{-3}\)
- Neutron concentration in synthesis reactions: \(\sim 10^{24}\) cm\(^{-3}\)
- Distance between nucleons in compressed target core: \(\sim 10^{-13}\) cm
- Spontaneous magnetic field inductivity: thousands of Tesla

## Context

This work extends the nonequilibrium thermo field dynamics formalism developed by Arimitsu and Umezawa (1985-1987) and the nonequilibrium statistical operator method of Zubarev (1974, 1980), building on prior kinetic equation work by Morozov and Ropke (1995) for dense systems. The paper aims to provide a theoretical framework applicable to strongly correlated nonequilibrium processes in laser thermonuclear fusion (D-D, D-T, D-\(^3\)He, p-B systems) and ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions where quark-gluon plasma is created, enabling future calculation of transport coefficients such as thermal conductivity and electrical conductivity in these extreme density regimes.
