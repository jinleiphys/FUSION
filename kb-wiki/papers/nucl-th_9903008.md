# Production of Dileptons in Heavy Ion Collisions at SPS-Energies

> In this contribution we will discuss the production of low mass dileptons in SPS-energy heavy ion collisions. We briefly review the current theoretical situation before we turn to the analysis of the recent data for Pb+Au. We also will discuss the role of baryons as a source for dileptons.

## Key claim

The Pb+Au dilepton data from CERES are consistent with a simple hadronic scenario without large in-medium modifications, provided the still-preliminary 1996 data remain unchanged; this contradicts the need for a dropping rho mass scenario or sizable baryon-driven enhancements.

## Method

The author uses a transport model similar to that in Koch (1997) to calculate dilepton invariant mass spectra for Pb+Au collisions at SPS energies, including the channel π + ρ → π + e⁺e⁻ via vector dominance and a collisional broadening of the rho meson. The calculation is compared to CERES data from the 1995 and 1996 runs, and the role of baryon Dalitz decays (especially from the N*(1520) resonance) is estimated by extrapolating the Delta Dalitz decay formula to higher masses.

## Key numbers

- The observed dilepton enhancement in S+Au collisions occurs at invariant masses around 400–500 MeV, as reported by the CERES collaboration.
- For Pb+Au, the transverse momentum spectra are shown for intervals p_t < 400 MeV and p_t > 400 MeV.
- The pion-to-baryon ratio for Pb+Au is about 3 using freeze-out parameters from Rapp et al., whereas the experimentally observed ratio is close to 6.
- The photon decay width for higher-lying baryon resonances was chosen as 1 MeV in the conservative upper-limit estimate.
- The baryon Dalitz decay yield is less than half that of the omega Dalitz decay, as shown in the ratio plot.

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis of S+Au data by Koch (1997) and compares with the Pb+Au data from the CERES collaboration (1995 and 1996 runs). It directly addresses the claim by Rapp et al. that baryons, especially the N*(1520) resonance, are a major source of low-mass dilepton enhancement, and it contradicts the dropping rho mass scenario of Li et al. (following Brown and Rho, 1991). The results enable future searches for chiral symmetry restoration effects, particularly if the CERES detector upgrade allows subtraction of the omega decay contribution.
