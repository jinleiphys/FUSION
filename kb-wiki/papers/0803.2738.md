# The structure of N(1535) in the aspect of chiral symmetry

> The structure of N(1535) is discussed in dynamical and symmetry aspects based on chiral symmetry. We find that the N(1535) in chiral unitary model has implicitly some components other than meson-baryon one. We also discuss the N(1535) in the chiral doublet picture.

## Key claim

The N(1535) resonance, while well described by the chiral unitary model as a dynamically generated meson-baryon state, implicitly contains a component originating from a genuine quark state, which leaves room for interpreting it as the chiral partner of the nucleon.

## Method

The authors revisit the chiral unitary model (ChUM) by proposing a "natural renormalization scheme" where the loop function G is fixed so that G(M_N) = 0, and then derive the interaction kernel V(W) that would yield the same scattering amplitude as the phenomenological scheme. By comparing the two renormalization schemes, they extract an effective pole mass M_eff that reveals whether the resonance has contributions beyond the meson-baryon dynamics. They also discuss the chiral doublet picture, where the nucleon and N(1535) form a parity doublet under linear chiral symmetry.

## Key numbers

- The N(1535) sits approximately 50 MeV above the ηN threshold.
- The effective mass extracted from the renormalization scheme comparison is M_eff ≈ 1700 ± 40i MeV.
- For the Λ(1405), the effective mass is found to be M_eff ≈ 17 GeV, which is irrelevant for that resonance's dynamics.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary model studies of N(1535) by Kaiser (1995) and Oller (2000), and the chiral doublet model of the nucleon and its parity partner. The finding that N(1535) has a quark-originated component contrasts with the Λ(1405), which is purely dynamically generated. The chiral doublet picture enables predictions for eta mesic nuclei, where the mass difference between N and N(1535) is expected to decrease with nuclear density due to partial chiral symmetry restoration, as studied by Jido (2008).
