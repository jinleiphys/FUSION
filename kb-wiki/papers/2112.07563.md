# Embedding a critical point in a hadron to quark-gluon crossover equation of state

> Lattice QCD simulations have shown unequivocally that the transition from hadrons to quarks and gluons is a crossover when the baryon chemical potential is zero or small. Many model calculations predict the existence of a critical point at a value of the chemical potential where current lattice simulations are unreliable. We show how to embed a critical point in a smooth background equation of state so as to yield the critical exponents and critical amplitude ratios expected of a transition in the same universality class as the liquid-gas phase transition and the 3D Ising model. The resulting equation of state has parameters which may be inferred by hydrodynamic modeling of heavy ion collisions in the Beam Energy Scan II at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider or in experiments at other accelerators.

## Key claim
The authors construct a general parametric equation of state that embeds a critical point (in the universality class of the liquid-gas transition and 3D Ising model) into a smooth crossover background, with the explicit hedge that the construction is "informed guesswork" and that parameters must be adjusted to fit experimental data from heavy ion collisions.

## Method
The pressure is factorized as \(P(T,\mu) = P_{BG}(T,\mu) R(T,\mu)\), where \(P_{BG}\) is a switching-function crossover from hadron resonance gas to perturbative quark-gluon plasma. The critical function \(R\) is built from auxiliary functions \(Q_\pm\) involving a variable \(r(T,\mu)\) that vanishes on the coexistence curve and a function \(\Delta^2(T)\) that controls the distance from \(T_c\). The exponents \(k\) and \(p\) in these functions are fixed to reproduce the critical exponents \(\alpha,\beta,\gamma,\delta\) of the 3D Ising universality class, and the remaining parameters (e.g., \(a_0, T_a, T_d, d_\pm\)) are chosen to control the spatial extent of critical behavior.

## Key numbers
- Lattice QCD crossover temperature at \(\mu=0\): \(T \approx 155\) MeV.
- Illustrative critical point location: \(T_c = 100\) MeV, \(\mu_c = 750\) MeV.
- True 3D Ising critical exponents used: \(\alpha \approx 0.1101\), \(\beta \approx 0.3264\), \(\gamma \approx 1.2371\), \(\delta \approx 4.7898\), giving \(p \approx 1.564\) and \(k \approx 1.209\).
- Mean-field critical exponents: \(\alpha = 0\), \(\beta = 1/2\), \(\gamma = 1\), \(\delta = 3\), giving \(p = 3/2\) and \(k = 4/3\).
- Amplitude ratio for susceptibility with true exponents and \(d_+/d_- = 1/3\): \(\chi_+/\chi_- = 4.769\) (exact 3D Ising value \(\approx 5\)).
- Amplitude ratio for heat capacity with same parameters: \(c_+/c_- = 0.530\).
- Background switching function parameters: \(T_s = 195\) MeV, \(\mu_s = 1300\) MeV.
- Model parameters for illustration: \(m=4\), \(d_- = 50\), \(a_0 = 0.15\), \(T_a = 80\) MeV, \(T_d = 200\) MeV.

## Context
This work extends the approach of Refs. [attract] and [Taylor1] (which are based on Zinn-Justin and Guida) by providing an alternative parametric construction for embedding a critical point into a crossover equation of state. It is designed to be used in hydrodynamic simulations of the Beam Energy Scan II at RHIC, enabling experimental inference of critical behavior. The background equation of state follows the switching-function method of Ref. [matchingpaper], which matches a hadron resonance gas with excluded volume to a perturbative quark-gluon plasma.
