# The neutron star in the Relativistic Mean-Field Theory

> The nucleon effective mass, binding energy and neutron star configuration in the Relativistic Mean-Field Theory (RMF) is considered. The calculation are motivated by the construction of the equation of state for a neutron star in the RMF. The equation of state for the parameters set TM1 is calculated using the Feynman - Bogolubov variational method for temperature different from zero. The structure of the neutron star is presented. The maximal stable configuration are obtained for: M_max=1.91 M_Sun and R=12.84 km.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for the TM1 parameter set, the maximal stable neutron star configuration has a gravitational mass of 1.91 solar masses and a radius of 12.84 km, but this result is obtained using the Feynman-Bogolubov variational method at finite temperature and is specific to the chosen parameter set and the relativistic mean-field approximation.

## Method

The authors constructed an equation of state for neutron star matter using the Relativistic Mean-Field Theory (RMF) with the TM1 parameter set, incorporating interactions mediated by sigma, omega, and rho mesons. They applied the Feynman-Bogolubov variational method to treat the system at finite temperature, treating the boson condensates and effective masses as variational parameters. The structure of static, spherical neutron stars was then obtained by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations using the resulting equation of state.

## Key numbers

- Maximal stable neutron star mass: M_max = 1.91 M_sun with radius R = 12.84 km for the TM1 parameter set
- Saturation binding energy for symmetric nuclear matter with TM1: E_0 = -16.26 MeV at Fermi momentum k_F,0 = 1.29 fm^-1 and density rho_0 = 0.1452 fm^-3
- Effective nucleon mass ratio at saturation for TM1: delta_0 = m_F/M = 0.634
- Incompressibility factor for TM1: K = 281.53 MeV
- For a star with central density rho_c = 9 x 10^14 g/cm^3, the nucleon effective mass varies from approximately 300 MeV at the center to approximately 762 MeV at the surface
- Adiabatic sound speed at saturation for TM1: v = 0.184c

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutron stars in the Relativistic Mean-Field Theory, particularly the earlier work by the same authors using the simpler L2 parameter set (Manka and Bednarek, previous work). The paper builds on the framework established by Walecka (QHD model), Glendenning (nuclear relativistic field theory for neutron stars), and Prakash et al. (hot neutron stars and protoneutron stars). The TM1 parameter set, originally developed by Toki and collaborators, is used because it reproduces known finite nuclei and normal nuclear matter properties while providing a distinctively stiffer equation of state. This work enables further studies of hot neutron stars and protoneutron star evolution at finite temperature.
