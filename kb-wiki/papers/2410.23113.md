# Ab initio nuclear shape coexistence and emergence of island of inversion around $N=20$

> We extend a nuclear ab initio framework based on chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions to investigate shape coexistence and the degradation of the $N=20$ magic number in both even-even and odd-even magnesium isotopes. The quantum-number projected generator coordinate method, combined with the in-medium similarity renormalization group (IMSRG), is employed to compute their low-lying states. This approach reasonably reproduces the coexistence of weakly and strongly deformed states at comparable energies, and allows us to track the emergence of the $N=20$ island of inversion through the continuous IMSRG evolution of the chiral Hamiltonian. Our results indicate that the ground state of $^{33}$Mg with spin-parity $3/2^-$ is predominantly a strongly deformed configuration with $K^π= 3/2^-$, while the lowest $7/2^-$ state is predicted to be a shape isomer, consisting of a mixture of weakly deformed configurations with different $K$ values. The results highlight the essential roles of both dynamical and static collective correlations in reproducing the ordering of nuclear states with distinct shapes.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the ground state of $^{33}$Mg with spin-parity $3/2^-$ is predominantly a strongly deformed configuration, while the lowest $7/2^-$ state is predicted to be a shape isomer consisting of a mixture of weakly deformed configurations; however, this result depends on the IMSRG evolution of the chiral Hamiltonian to a specific flow parameter, and without such evolution the ground state is incorrectly predicted to be $3/2^+$.

## Method

The authors employ the in-medium generator coordinate method (IM-GCM), which combines the multi-reference in-medium similarity renormalization group (MR-IMSRG) with quantum-number projected generator coordinate method (PGCM). Starting from the chiral N3LO potential (EM1.8/2.0) with two- and three-nucleon interactions, they evolve the Hamiltonian via the IMSRG flow equation using the Magnus expansion and the Brillouin generator, truncating at the normal-ordered two-body (NO2B) level. Low-lying states are then obtained through PGCM calculations where wave functions are constructed as linear superpositions of axially deformed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov states projected onto good angular momentum, neutron number, and proton number.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of $^{32}$Mg extrapolated to $s\to\infty$: $-246.9$ MeV; extrapolated to $\eMax\to\infty$: $-249.5$ MeV
- Excitation energy of the $0^+_2$ state in $^{32}$Mg increases from 2.0 MeV to 2.7 MeV when extrapolating $s\to\infty$
- $B(E2: 2^+_1 \to 0^+_1)$ value changes by about 6% when extrapolating $\eMax\to\infty$; $0^+_2$ excitation energy varies by up to 25%
- Electric monopole transition strength $\rho^2(E0; 0^+_2 \to 0^+_1)$ for $^{32}$Mg: $6.8\times 10^{-3}$ with shape mixing, compared to $1.6\times 10^{-6}$ without shape mixing
- Predicted half-life of the $7/2^-_1$ state in $^{33}$Mg: approximately 6.1 ns using calculated energies, or about 7.1 $\mu$s using experimental energies
- Magnetic dipole moment of the $3/2^-_1$ state in $^{33}$Mg: $-0.75$ $\mu_N$ (IM-GCM) compared to experimental $-0.7456(5)$ $\mu_N$
- Electric quadrupole moment of the $3/2^-_1$ state in $^{33}$Mg: $11.9$ $e$fm$^2$ (IM-GCM) compared to experimental $13.4(92)$ $e$fm$^2$

## Context

This work extends the ab initio IM-GCM framework, previously benchmarked against the no-core shell model for nuclei with mass numbers A=6 to A=22 (Yao 2021, Frosini 2022), to study shape coexistence in neutron-rich magnesium isotopes around $N=20$. It addresses the long-standing challenge of describing the $N=20$ island of inversion, where prior approaches such as the valence-space IMSRG (Stroberg 2021, Miyagi 2020) systematically underestimated $B(E2)$ values in deformed nuclei, and angular-momentum projected coupled-cluster theory (Sun 2024) struggled to predict the correct ground-state spin-parity of $^{33}$Mg. The results enable future studies of shape-mixing effects in odd-mass nuclei and provide a framework for investigating electric monopole transitions as probes of shape coexistence.
