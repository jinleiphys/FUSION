# High energy nuclear interactions and QCD: an introduction

> The goal of these lectures, oriented towards the students just entering the field, is to provide an elementary introduction to QCD and the physics of nuclear interactions at high energies. We first introduce the general structure of QCD and discuss its main properties. Then we proceed to Glauber multiple scattering theory which lays the foundation for the theoretical treatment of nuclear interactions at high energies. We introduce the concept of Gribov's inelastic shadowing, crucial for the understanding of quantum formation effects. We outline the problems facing Glauber approach at high energies, and discuss how asymptotic freedom of QCD helps to resolve them, introducing the concepts of parton saturation and color glass condensate.

## Key claim
These lectures provide an elementary introduction to QCD and high-energy nuclear interactions for students new to the field, but they explicitly outline the problems facing the Glauber approach at high energies and discuss how asymptotic freedom of QCD helps resolve them through the concepts of parton saturation and color glass condensate, rather than presenting these as fully established solutions.

## Method
The lectures first introduce the general structure of QCD, its Lagrangian, and its main properties including asymptotic freedom, confinement, and chiral symmetry breaking. They then present Glauber multiple scattering theory as the foundation for theoretical treatment of nuclear interactions at high energies, followed by Gribov's inelastic shadowing concept for understanding quantum formation effects. The authors outline the limitations of the Glauber approach at high energies and discuss how QCD's asymptotic freedom leads to parton saturation and the color glass condensate framework.

## Key numbers
- The string tension sigma is approximately 1 GeV/fm, characterizing the confining potential between heavy quarks at large distances.
- The QCD beta function leading coefficient is b = 11 N_c - 2 N_f, where N_c = 3 is the number of colors and N_f is the number of flavors.
- The critical temperature T_c marks the transition from confined phase (where the Polyakov loop L(T) = 0) to deconfined phase (where L(T) is nonzero).
- The strong CP parameter theta is constrained to theta < 10^(-9) by precision measurements of the neutron electric dipole moment.

## Context
This work extends the pedagogical tradition of introducing QCD to newcomers, following the foundational developments of asymptotic freedom by Gross, Wilczek, and Politzer (1973) and the Glauber multiple scattering theory. It enables students to understand the transition from perturbative QCD at short distances to the non-perturbative regime at large distances, and sets the stage for studying the color glass condensate and parton saturation as frameworks for high-energy nuclear collisions. The lectures reference the work of Gribov on confinement and the zero-charge problem, as well as lattice QCD results on the deconfinement phase transition.
