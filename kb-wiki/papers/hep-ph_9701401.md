# Coulomb Effects on Charged Kaon Distributions From Vlasov Dynamics

> We compute the influence of Coulomb effects on the charged kaon distribution at low momentum in high energy nucleus-nucleus collisions. This is accomplished by solving the Vlasov equation for the kaons in the presence of an expanding, highly charged, fireball.

## Key claim

Coulomb effects from the expanding, highly charged fireball in high-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions significantly modify the low-momentum charged kaon distributions, and the commonly used Gamow factor approach fails for heavy systems where \(Z\alpha \sim 1\); the Vlasov dynamics approach shows that the kaon distribution remains finite at zero energy because the fireball expands and leaves low-energy kaons behind, unlike the static fireball case.

## Method

The authors solve the Vlasov equation for charged kaons in the presence of a uniformly charged, expanding spherical fireball whose radius grows linearly with time, modeling the fireball expansion with a similarity solution to hydrodynamics. For positive kaons, the asymptotic momentum distribution is obtained analytically via the method of characteristics and flux integration across the expanding surface; for negative kaons, trajectories are computed numerically for 720,000 initial phase-space points (radial position, speed, and angle bins) to handle the possibility of multiple surface crossings.

## Key numbers

- Fireball total charge \(Z = 158\) (gold-gold collision), initial radius \(R_0 = 8\) fm, kaon temperature \(T = 120\) MeV.
- Expansion velocities considered: \(v_s/c = 0.2, 0.4, 0.6\); for the reference case \(v_s/c = 0.4\), the characteristic time \(t_s = 28.7\) fm/c and initial time \(t_0 = 20\) fm/c.
- For the reference case: \(p_C = 1390\) MeV/c, \(\chi = 1.19\), \(\delta = 0.330\).
- Numerical trajectory calculation used 720,000 initial phase-space points: radial position in 0.1 fm bins, speed in 0.01c bins, angle in \(2^\circ\) bins.
- Kinetic energy threshold for classical treatment: kaon kinetic energy greater than about 10 MeV (DeBroglie wavelength 2 fm).

## Context

This work extends the treatment of Coulomb effects beyond the Gamow factor approach of Gyulassy (1982) and the static fireball model, showing that fireball expansion qualitatively changes the low-momentum charged kaon spectra. The paper was motivated by preliminary data from the E814/E877 experiment at Brookhaven AGS (Barrette, 1995) which showed an enhancement later attributed to an albedo source (Lacasse, 1996). The results enable quantitative comparison with event generators like ARC or RQMD and suggest that a relativistic treatment is needed for kinetic energies above about 100 MeV to compare with data at forward rapidities (2.1–2.5) in the nucleus-nucleus center-of-mass frame (rapidity ~1.5).
