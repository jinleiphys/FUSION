# In-medium modifications of the S11(1535) resonance and eta photoproduction

> We investigate the influence of possible in-medium modifications of the S11(1535) in eta photoproduction in nuclei. Besides Fermi motion, Pauli blocking and binding effects also collisional broadening is accounted for. The in-medium width is obtained from a realistic resonance-hole model. Results on eta photoproduction are obtained from a semi-classical BUU transport model and compared with data. We find that calculations including a momentum dependent nucleon and resonance potential are in agreement with the recent KEK data. In contrast, collisional broadening has only little influence.

## Key claim

The paper finds that including a momentum-dependent nucleon and resonance potential in the BUU transport model reproduces the KEK eta photoproduction data, while collisional broadening of the S11(1535) resonance has only a small effect on the cross sections, contrary to earlier QMD calculations by Yorita et al.

## Method

The authors used a semi-classical Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model to simulate eta photoproduction on nuclei (C, Al, Ca, Cu, Nb, Pb) for photon energies from threshold up to 1 GeV. The in-medium width of the S11(1535) resonance was calculated from a self-consistent resonance-hole model that includes meson dressing and Pauli blocking, and the collisional broadening was implemented via explicit collision terms in the BUU equations. The elementary gamma N to S11 to eta N cross section was parametrized with a Breit-Wigner form using the photocoupling helicity amplitude A1/2^p = 0.109 GeV^{-1/2} and a pole-mass decay width Gamma_0 = 0.151 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The pole-mass decay width of the S11(1535) resonance: Gamma_0 = 0.151 GeV.
- Photocoupling helicity amplitude for the proton: A1/2^p = 0.109 GeV^{-1/2}.
- Photocoupling helicity amplitude for the neutron (derived from the ratio sigma_gamma n = (2/3) sigma_gamma p): A1/2^n ~ 0.089 GeV^{-1/2}.
- Collisional broadening of the S11(1535) at nuclear saturation density rho_0: about 35 MeV for an on-shell resonance.
- The mean free path of the S11(1535) at rho_0 is about 3 fm, compared to less than 1 fm for the eta meson.
- The momentum-dependent potential (M) shifts the peak of the cross section by about 50-70 MeV relative to the momentum-independent potential (H).

## Context

This work extends earlier BUU model calculations of eta photoproduction by Effenberger et al. (2000) and Hombach et al. (1999) to higher photon energies covered by the KEK data (Yorita et al., 2000; Yamazaki et al., 2000). It contradicts the QMD model results of Yorita et al. (2000), who found a strong effect from S11 collisional reactions, and refines the nuclear matter calculations of Maruyama et al. (1998), who assumed vanishing scalar and vector potentials for the S11 and used a constant absorption factor for eta final state interactions. The self-consistent resonance-hole model for the S11 in-medium width builds on the work of Post et al. (2003) and Oset et al. (1998). The results enable more realistic modeling of in-medium resonance properties and show that a constant absorption factor for eta final state interactions is inadequate.
