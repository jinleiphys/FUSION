# Spectral properties of a tractable collective Hamiltonian

> The spectral properties of a tractable collective model Hamiltonian are studied. The potential energy is truncated up to quartic terms in the quadrupole deformation variables, incorporating vibrational, $γ$-independent rotational and axially deformed rotational structures. These physically significant limits are analysed in detail and confronted with well-established approximation schemes. Furthermore, transitional Hamiltonians in between the limits are presented and discussed. All results are obtained within a recently presented Cartan-Weyl based framework to calculate $SU(1,1)\times SO(5)$ embedded quadrupole collective observables.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a collective Bohr-Mottelson Hamiltonian with a potential truncated to quartic terms in the quadrupole deformation variables can reproduce the three limiting collective structures (vibrational, gamma-independent rotational, and axially deformed rotational) but emphasizes that these limits are not genuine symmetry limits associated with a spectrum-generating algebra, except for the SO(5) invariant gamma-independent case.

## Method

The authors diagonalize a collective Hamiltonian with kinetic energy (1/2B₂)π·π and potential energy c₂(α·α) + c₃([αα]²·α) + c₄(α·α)² using a Cartan-Weyl basis of the SU(1,1)×SO(5) algebra to compute matrix elements without constructing explicit wavefunctions. They then transform from the Cartan-Weyl basis to the angular momentum basis by diagonalizing the L·L operator, and diagonalize the Hamiltonian within separate angular momentum L subspaces, using the Margetan-Williams method to optimize the harmonic oscillator spring constant and achieve convergence within 100 harmonic oscillator shells.

## Key numbers

- Harmonic oscillator limit: excitation energy of the 2₁ state is 1264.91 keV; B(E2;2₁→0₁) = 0.00158 in units of (3ZeR₀²)²/(4π)²
- Gamma-independent rotor limit: excitation energy of the 2₁ state is 233.8 keV; B(E2;2₁→0₁) = 0.00759 in the same units; the 0₃ state (β-vibrational bandhead) lies at 1700.6 keV
- Axially deformed rotor limit: excitation energy of the 2₁ state is 71.29 keV; B(E2;2₁→0₁) = 0.01245 in the same units; RVM parameters extracted from the potential minimum (β₀=0.264, γ₀=0) give E_γ = 1885.22 keV, E_β = 2093.96 keV, ε = 19.15 keV
- The mass parameter ℏ²/B₂ is set to 4 keV for all calculations

## Context

This work extends the tractable collective Hamiltonian approach of Caprio (2003) by implementing a Cartan-Weyl algebraic framework previously developed by De Baerdemacker (2007, 2008) to compute matrix elements without explicit basis wavefunctions. The three limiting cases correspond to the U(5), O(6), and SU(3) symmetry limits of the Interacting Boson Model (IBM) as established by Iachello (1987) and Casten (1993), and the numerical results are validated against the Wilets-Jean displaced harmonic oscillator approximation (1956) for the gamma-independent case and the rotation-vibration model (RVM) of Faessler and Greiner (1962) for the axial rotor case. The approach enables systematic study of transitional Hamiltonians between the three limits and provides a framework for connecting IBM total-energy surfaces from the coherent state formalism of Van Roosmalen (1982) to Bohr-Mottelson model calculations.
