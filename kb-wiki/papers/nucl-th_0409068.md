# Apparent Thermalization due to Plasma Instabilities in Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Hydrodynamical modeling of heavy ion collisions at RHIC suggests that the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) "thermalizes" in a remarkably short time scale, about 0.6 fm/c. We argue that this should be viewed as indicating fast isotropization, but not necessarily complete thermalization, of the non-equilibrium QGP. Non-Abelian plasma instabilities can drive local isotropization of an anisotropic QGP on a time scale which is faster than ordinary perturbative scattering processes. As a result, we argue that theoretical expectations based on weak coupling analysis are not necessarily in conflict with hydrodynamic modeling of the early part of RHIC collisions, provided one recognizes the key role of non-Abelian plasma instabilities.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the short "thermalization" time (~0.6 fm/c) inferred from hydrodynamic modeling of RHIC collisions should be reinterpreted as evidence of fast isotropization, not full thermalization, and that non-Abelian plasma instabilities can drive this isotropization on time scales parametrically faster than perturbative scattering rates, resolving the apparent conflict between weak-coupling theory and RHIC data.

## Method

The authors analyze the linearized Boltzmann-Vlasov equations for an anisotropic non-Abelian plasma, deriving the retarded gauge-field self-energy and identifying unstable solutions (non-Abelian Weibel instabilities) when the hard particle distribution is anisotropic. They then examine the effective potential for fields varying in one spatial direction and estimate the growth rates, saturation amplitudes, and isotropization time scales of these instabilities, comparing them to perturbative scattering rates.

## Key numbers

- The hydrodynamic modeling of RHIC collisions assumes initial partons "thermalize" in about **0.6 fm/c**.
- For a simple example with typical particle energy **1 GeV**, plasma energy density **27 GeV/fm³**, and α_s=0.5, the effective mass m_∞ ≈ **740 MeV**, the maximal growth rate γ ≈ **280 MeV = (0.7 fm/c)⁻¹**, and the most unstable wavevector k ≈ **575 MeV**.
- The parametric growth rate γ for O(1) anisotropy is O(g p_hard), compared to O(g⁴ p_hard) for large-angle scattering rates and O(g² p_hard) for small-angle scattering rates.
- The number of e-foldings required for instabilities to reach non-perturbative amplitude is only of order **ln(1/g)**.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of non-Abelian plasma instabilities by Arnold (2002) and Mrowczynski (1989) and directly addresses the discrepancy between perturbative thermalization time estimates (2.5 fm/c or above) and the fast apparent thermalization required by RHIC hydrodynamic models. The paper enables future full 3+1 dimensional Boltzmann-Vlasov simulations to verify the instability-driven isotropization scenario, and it reconciles weak-coupling analysis with experimental observations by identifying plasma instabilities as the key missing dynamical process.
