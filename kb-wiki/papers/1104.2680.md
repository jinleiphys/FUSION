# Kaon photoproduction and electroproduction near threshold

> We analyze the electromagnetic production of K^+Lambda and K^0Lambda near their production thresholds by using isobar models. In the K^+Lambda channel we show that the model can nicely describe the available experimental data. In the K^0Lambda channel we demonstrate that the K^0 charge form factor has sizable effects on the longitudinal cross section. By extending the model up to W = 1730 MeV, we are able to observe the existence of the narrow P_{11} (J^p = 1/2^+) resonance in the kaon photoproduction process. It is found that the most convincing mass (width) of this resonance is 1650 MeV (5 MeV).

## Key claim

The paper claims that a narrow $P_{11}$ ($J^p = 1/2^+$) resonance exists in kaon photoproduction, with the most convincing mass and width of 1650 MeV and 5 MeV respectively, but notes that an $S_{11}$ ($J^p = 1/2^-$) resonance could also generate the same minimum in the chi-squared scan, making the parity assignment ambiguous without more precise data.

## Method

The author constructed isobar models for $K^+\Lambda$ and $K^0\Lambda$ photoproduction and electroproduction near threshold, using tree-level Feynman diagrams for background amplitudes and resonance terms from known PDG states. For the narrow resonance search, the model was extended up to $W = 1730$ MeV by adding five additional resonance states, and the change in total $\chi^2$ was scanned as a function of resonance mass (1620–1730 MeV), total width (1–10 MeV), and $K\Lambda$ branching ratio.

## Key numbers

- The $K^+\Lambda$ threshold energy is 1610 MeV; the model describes data up to $W = 50$ MeV above threshold.
- The most convincing mass (width) of the narrow $P_{11}$ resonance is 1650 MeV (5 MeV), found with $K\Lambda$ branching ratio 0.2.
- The $K^0\Lambda$ total cross section predicted by Kaon-Maid overpredicts the present calculation by about 30% near threshold.
- At forward angles, the LCQ $K^0$ form factor raises the longitudinal cross section by up to 50% at $W = 1.65$ GeV.
- Experimental data with about 10% uncertainties would be needed to distinguish between $K^0$ form factor models.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous isobar model study of $K^+\Lambda$ photoproduction (Mart, 2010) and uses isospin symmetry to predict $K^0\Lambda$ observables. It addresses the search for the nonstrange member of the antidecuplet predicted by the chiral quark soliton model (Diakonov), following a narrow $P_{11}$ resonance effect found in $\eta$ photoproduction off the neutron (Kuznetsov). The results enable future experimental checks of the $K^0$ charge form factor via electroproduction and provide a method to determine the quantum numbers of the narrow resonance using total cross section and double polarization observables.
