# A braid model for the particle X(3872)

> The Model of Quark Exchange (MQE) describes the particle X(3872) as a meson molecule. We asked whether braids influence the meson potential in the MQE. We used the Burau representation that parameterized braids with a variable $t$. The present result shows that $t$ rescales the coupling of the meson potential determining if it is attractive or repulsive. As a consequence, a capture diagram favored the molecular state for $t=0.85$, it breaks for other values. For the future, braids may help to study others exotic states in geometrical terms.

## Key claim
The braid parameter \(t\) in the Burau representation rescales the coupling of the meson potential in the Model of Quark Exchange (MQE), determining whether the potential is attractive or repulsive; a molecular state for the X(3872) is favored only for \(t=0.85\) and breaks for other values.

## Method
The authors applied the Burau representation of the braid group \(\mathcal{B}_3\) to the quark exchange diagrams in the MQE, specifically the capture diagram \(D1\) for the scattering process \(D^0+\bar{D}^{0*}\to J/\psi+\rho\). They computed the trace of the product of the Burau matrix \(\varphi_3(\sigma_1^{-1})\) with the permutation matrix \(\mathbf{D}(\Pi_2)\) from the quark exchange, yielding a rescaling factor \(1-1/t\) for the coupling \(\lambda_{ex}\). The phase-shift was then calculated using the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with the rescaled coupling and Lorentzian form factors with cutoff \(\gamma=0.8\).

## Key numbers
- The coupling from the capture diagram \(D1\) is \(\lambda_{ex} = -114.35\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\).
- The meson potential coupling used in the phase-shift calculation is \(\lambda = 20.3\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\).
- The cutoff parameter for the Lorentzian form factors is \(\gamma = 0.8\).
- The mass of the X(3872) as a molecule of \(D^0\) and \(\bar{D}^{0*}\) is \(3.872\ \text{GeV}\).
- The braid parameter value that produces the molecular state is \(t = 0.85\).
- The quark-quark interaction strength used to fit the meson spectrum is \(V = 113.39\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\).
- The product of meson wave function amplitudes is \(\frac{\phi_A \phi_B \phi_C \phi_D}{(2\pi)^6} = 6.312\ \text{GeV}^{-6}\).
- The integration over the continuum limit for the invariant matrix yields a factor of \(0.12\ \text{GeV}^6\).

## Context
This work extends the Model of Quark Exchange (MQE) for the X(3872) as developed by Barnes (1992), Martins (1994), and earlier work by the same authors (CarlosX, 2011), which described the particle as a meson molecule but left the role of the quark-exchange vertex unclear. By introducing braid group methods from Artin (1947) and Burau (1935), the authors provide a geometric interpretation of the quark exchange that explains the origin of the coupling \(\lambda\) at the quark level. This approach enables future studies of other exotic states using braid geometry and may be tested in predictions for heavy ion collisions.
