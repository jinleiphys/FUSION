# Fission mode identification in the 180Hg region: derivative analysis approach

> Experimental setups commonly used to study fission properties of nuclei in the exotic neutron-deficient 180Hg region are based on the time-of-flight technique for the fission-product identification. The nuclei of interest are created via fusion reactions at excitation energies of several tens of MeV and identified with limited mass resolution. The deduced final fission-fragment mass distributions are in general structureless, which makes the identification of fission modes, along with their properties, ambiguous and author-dependent. The standard functional-analysis technique applied to the simulated limited-resolution fusion-fission data appears to provide consistent results on the number and parameters of fission modes, even in cases of strong symmetric-mode dominance, i.e. for Gaussian-like fission-fragment mass distribution shapes. The method is shown to work also on data sets with limited statistics (real experimental data with integral of a few tens of thousands of events).

# Fission mode identification in the 180Hg region: derivative analysis approach

## Key claim

The second-derivative analysis of fission-fragment mass distributions (FFMDs) can reliably identify the number and parameters of fission modes even in cases of strong symmetric-mode dominance (Gaussian-like FFMD shapes) and with limited experimental statistics (a few tens of thousands of events), provided the experimental mass resolution is approximately 2 u or better.

## Method

Simulated FFMD and mass-TKE datasets with predefined fission-mode compositions were generated for 240Pu (using the GEF code) and a hypothetical A=180 nucleus, then convoluted with experimental resolutions of 2 u and 4 u for mass and 6 MeV and 8 MeV for TKE. The second derivative of each FFMD was calculated using a sliding window, and the number of minima in the derivative curve was used to determine the number of Gaussian components in the fit function. The method was then applied to experimental fusion-fission data for 180Hg from Nishio (2015).

## Key numbers

- Experimental mass resolution in the 180Hg region: 2 u (best case), with TKE resolution of 6 MeV
- For 240Pu at E* = 10 MeV: the S3 mode weight is 6.0% with light-fragment peak position at 88.8 u; at E* = 70 MeV the SL mode weight is 92.9% with light-fragment peak at 117.9 u
- For the A=180 simulated nucleus: the second-derivative method can detect an A2 mode with weight as low as 4.6% (input: W=4.6%, A_L=66 u, sigma=8.5 u) when mass resolution is 2 u
- For 180Hg experimental data at E* = 33.4 MeV: the S-mode weight is 42(3)% and A-mode weight is 58(3)% with fixed A-mode position at A=78 u; at E* = 47.8 MeV: S-mode weight is 51(3)% and A-mode weight is 49(3)%
- The overall uncertainty on mode weights from 1D and 2D fits is approximately 4%, exceeding the statistical uncertainty by a factor of 4

## Context

This work addresses the long-standing problem of author-dependent fission-mode identification in the neutron-deficient 180Hg region, where previous analyses by Nishio (2015), Prasad (2020), and Nag (2021) used 2-Gaussian fits without considering the symmetric mode, while Bogachev (2021) and Kozulin (2022) employed 5G or 7G fits justified only by chi-squared values. The derivative approach provides an objective criterion for determining the number of fission modes present, independent of the fitting function choice. This enables consistent analysis of fusion-fission data from facilities such as GANIL-SPIRAL2 and JAEA, and resolves the question of whether the symmetric mode contributes to 180Hg fission at excitation energies above 30 MeV.
