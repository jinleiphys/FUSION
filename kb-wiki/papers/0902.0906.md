# Three-body direct nuclear reactions: Nonlocal optical potential

> The calculations of three-body direct nuclear reactions with nonlocal optical potentials are performed for the first time using the framework of Faddeev-type scattering equations. Important nonlocality effect is found for transfer reactions like d+16O -> p + 17O often improving the description of the experimental data.

## Key claim

For the first time, three-body direct nuclear reactions are calculated using nonlocal optical potentials within the Faddeev-type AGS scattering framework; the nonlocality effect is found to be significant for transfer reactions such as d + 16O → p + 17O, often improving the description of experimental data, though the improvement is conditional on the specific final state and angular region.

## Method

The author solves the Alt, Grassberger, and Sandhas (AGS) equations, which are Faddeev-type connected-kernel integral equations for transition operators, in momentum space where both local and nonlocal potentials are treated identically. The long-range Coulomb force between charged particles is included via the screening and renormalization method. For the nucleon-nucleus interaction, the nonlocal optical potential of Giannini and Ricco is used, along with its approximately equivalent local optical potential; the parameter W_N (strength of the imaginary part) is adjusted to improve agreement with nucleon-nucleus scattering data in the 10–40 MeV lab energy range.

## Key numbers

- For the d + 16O → p + 17O transfer reaction at deuteron lab energies of 25.4 and 36.0 MeV, the nonlocal optical potential reproduces the differential cross section for the 1/2+ excited state of 17O "almost perfectly," while the local optical potential fails.
- For d + 12C → p + 13C transfer at Ed = 30 MeV, the nonlocal optical potential successfully accounts for data for the 1/2+ and 5/2+ excited states of 13C, in contrast to the local optical potential and prior work (Deltuva 2009).
- For p + 16O elastic scattering at Ep = 35.2 MeV, the nonlocal and local optical potentials produce nearly equivalent differential cross sections divided by Rutherford, with both agreeing well with experimental data.
- For d + 16O elastic scattering at Ed = 25.4 MeV, the nonlocality effect is visible at Θc.m. > 30 deg, moving predictions toward data up to 60 deg; beyond 60 deg both potentials strongly underpredict the data.
- For d + 16O and d + 40Ca elastic scattering at Ed = 56 MeV, visible differences between nonlocal and local optical potentials appear at Θc.m. > 60 deg, with nonlocal predictions closer to data but showing "too sharp oscillations."

## Context

This work extends the exact three-body Faddeev/AGS approach previously applied to nuclear reactions (Deltuva 2006, 2007) by incorporating nonlocal optical potentials for the first time, whereas prior methods such as CDCC (Austern 1987), DWBA, and adiabatic approaches (Timofeyuk 1999) have only used local optical potentials. The results demonstrate that nonlocality is negligible for p+(An) elastic scattering but important for transfer reactions, and the author suggests this will stimulate development of more precise nonlocal nuclear interaction models.
