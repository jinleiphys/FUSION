# N phi state in chiral quark model

> The structures of N phi states with spin-parity J^{p}=3/2^- and J^p=1/2^- are dynamically studied in both the chiral SU(3) quark model and the extended chiral SU(3) quark model by solving a resonating group method (RGM) equation. The model parameters are taken from our previous work, which gave a satisfactory description of the energies of the baryon ground states, the binding energy of the deuteron, the nucleon-nucleon (NN) scattering phase shifts, and the hyperon-nucleon (YN) cross sections. The channel coupling of N phi and Lambda K* is considered, and the effect of the tensor force which results in the mixing of S and D waves is also investigated. The results show that the N phi state has an attractive interaction, and in the extended chiral SU(3) quark model such an attraction plus the channel coupling effect can consequently make for an N phi quasi-bound state with several MeV binding energy.

## Key claim

The N phi system can form a quasi-bound state with several MeV binding energy, but only in the extended chiral SU(3) quark model with specific parameter choices (model II, where the vector-meson tensor-to-vector coupling ratio f_chv/g_chv = 0); in the original chiral SU(3) quark model and in the extended model with f_chv/g_chv = 2/3, the N phi states remain unbound despite attractive interactions.

## Method

The authors solved the resonating group method (RGM) equation for the N phi system in both the chiral SU(3) quark model and the extended chiral SU(3) quark model, treating N and phi as two clusters. They considered channel coupling between N phi and Lambda K* and investigated the effect of the tensor force from one-gluon exchange, pseudoscalar, and vector field couplings that mixes S and D waves. All model parameters were taken from previous work that reproduced baryon ground-state energies, the deuteron binding energy, and NN scattering phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- In the extended chiral SU(3) quark model with f_chv/g_chv = 0 (model II), the one-channel calculation gives a binding energy of about 1 MeV for spin S = 1/2 and 3 MeV for spin S = 3/2.
- With channel coupling between N phi and Lambda K* included, model II yields binding energies of about 3 MeV for S = 1/2 and 9 MeV for S = 3/2.
- In the extended chiral SU(3) quark model with f_chv/g_chv = 2/3 (model III), the coupled-channel calculation gives binding energies of about 1 MeV for S = 1/2 and 6 MeV for S = 3/2.
- The Lambda K* threshold is 49 MeV higher than the N phi threshold.
- The sigma meson mass used in model II is 535 MeV, compared to 595 MeV in model I and 547 MeV in model III.

## Context

This work extends the chiral quark model approach, previously applied to baryon-baryon and baryon-meson scattering, to the N phi system, which is special because N and phi have no common flavor quarks and no one-gluon exchange interaction. It contrasts with the earlier QCD van der Waals calculation by Gao et al. (2001), which predicted a bound N phi state with about 1.8 MeV binding energy using a different theoretical framework. The results are model- and parameter-dependent, and the authors suggest that experimental measurement of a possible N phi bound state would test the strength of the quark-sigma chiral field coupling.
