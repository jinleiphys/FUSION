# Probing the Short-Range Dynamics in Exclusive Scattering off Polarized Deuteron

> Employing the polarization degrees of freedom in the deuteron allows to isolate smaller than average inter-nucleon distances in the deuteron. As a result one can identify set of high $Q^2$ reactions off polarized deuteron which are particularly sensitive to the short range dynamics of strong interaction. We concentrate on the studies of several aspects of the short range phenomena. These are the relativistic dynamics of electron--bound-nucleon scattering, color coherence in high $Q^2$ electro-production as well as the formation of the vector mesons in coherent electro-production from the deuteron. We address also the issue of extraction of polarized deep inelastic structure function of the neutron.

# Probing the Short-Range Dynamics in Exclusive Scattering off Polarized Deuteron

## Key claim
Polarizing the deuteron target allows one to cancel the dominant S-wave contribution to the density matrix, thereby isolating the D-wave component and enhancing sensitivity to inter-nucleon distances smaller than the average deuteron radius of about 4 fm. This technique makes it possible to identify kinematic regions where either impulse approximation or final-state interactions dominate, enabling separate studies of bound-nucleon dynamics and short-range hadronic re-interaction.

## Method
The paper analyzes exclusive electro-disintegration of the polarized deuteron, $e + \vec{d} \rightarrow e' + p + n$, at $Q^2 \ge 1$ GeV$^2$ within a distorted-wave impulse approximation framework. Final-state interactions are treated using the generalized eikonal approximation (GEA), which accounts for finite recoil and missing momenta beyond the standard Glauber approximation. The cross section is expressed in terms of a distorted spectral function built from the deuteron's polarized density matrices, which involve the S-wave ($u(k)$) and D-wave ($w(k)$) partial waves. For coherent vector meson production, $e + \vec{d} \rightarrow e' + d' + V$, the transition form factors of the deuteron are used to identify polarization choices that suppress the impulse approximation term.

## Key numbers
- Deuteron rms radius: approximately 4 fm.
- Kinematic condition for the electro-disintegration study: $Q^2 > 1$ GeV$^2$, with recoil nucleon momenta $p_s$ in the range 0–500 MeV/c.
- For the tensor polarization asymmetry $A_d$ in $d(e,e'p)n$ at $p_t = 300$ MeV/c and $Q^2 \sim 10$ GeV$^2$, color transparency effects can change $A_d$ by as much as a factor of two.
- For coherent vector meson production, the transversely polarized deuteron density matrix $\rho^{1,0}(l/2)$ vanishes at $l \approx 0.7$ GeV/c, corresponding to $-t \approx 0.5$ GeV$^2$.
- The expansion length for a point-like configuration is given by $l_h \sim 0.4 (p/\text{GeV})$ fm.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of deuteron structure and final-state interactions in electron scattering, particularly the generalized eikonal approximation developed by FGMSS95 and the virtual nucleon versus light-cone approaches to relativistic deuteron dynamics (rep, MST, deF). The use of polarization to isolate short-range dynamics builds on the observation that the D-wave vanishes at small momenta, as shown in the polarized density matrices. The color transparency predictions are compared with the quantum diffusion model (FLFS) and a three-state model (FGMS93), and the experimental observation of color transparency in $\pi + A \rightarrow 2\text{jets} + A'$ reactions (Ashery) is cited as motivation. The proposed extraction of the neutron polarized structure function $g_{1n}$ via spectator tagging and Chew-Low extrapolation is analogous to the method used for pion scattering (CL59). The work enables future experiments with polarized deuteron targets at high-current electron beams and at a polarized electron-ion collider.
