# Physics-Informed Priors Improve Gravitational-Wave Constraints on Neutron-Star Matter

> Gravitational-wave astronomy shows great promise in determining nuclear physics in a regime not accessible to terrestrial experiments. We introduce physics-informed priors constrained by nuclear theory and perturbative Quantum Chromodynamics calculations, as well as astrophysical measurements of neutron-star masses and radii. When these priors are used in gravitational-wave astrophysical inference, we show a significant improvement on nuclear equation of state constraints. Applying these to the first observed gravitational-wave binary neutron-star merger GW170817, the constraints on the radius of a $1.4\,M_\odot$ neutron star improve from $R_{1.4} ={12.54^{+1.05}_{-1.54}} \, {\rm km}$ to $R_{1.4} = 12.11^{+0.91}_{-1.11} \,{\rm km}$ and those on the tidal deformability from $\tildeΛ_{1.186} < 720$ to $\tildeΛ_{1.186} = 384^{+306}_{-158}$ ($90\%$ confidence intervals) at the events measured chirp mass $\mathcal{M}=1.186\,M_\odot$. We also show these priors can be used to perform model selection between binary neutron star and neutron star-black hole mergers; in the case of GW190425, the results provide only marginal evidence with a Bayes factor $\mathcal{BF}=1.33$ in favour of the binary neutron star merger hypothesis. Given their ability to improve the astrophysical inference of binary mergers involving neutron stars, we advocate for these physics-informed priors to be used as standard in the literature and provide open-source code for reproducibility and adaptation of the method.

## Key claim

Introducing physics-informed priors that incorporate constraints from nuclear theory, perturbative QCD, and astrophysical measurements of neutron-star masses and radii significantly improves gravitational-wave constraints on the neutron-star equation of state, reducing the 90% credible interval on the radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star from GW170817 by about 0.4 km compared to the standard uniform-prior analysis.

## Method

The authors construct approximately 10^6 equation-of-state realizations by stitching together a low-density crust model (Baym-Pethick-Sutherland), randomly sampled polytropes at intermediate densities, and perturbative QCD constraints at high densities, using a seven-segment piecewise-linear sound-speed parametrization. They retain only those equations of state satisfying mass measurements of pulsars J0348+0432 and J0740+6620 (maximum TOV mass above 2.0 solar masses) and NICER radius constraints, then derive joint priors on chirp mass and tidal deformability for both binary neutron star and neutron star-black hole systems. These priors are implemented in the Bilby Bayesian inference framework and applied to reanalyze the gravitational-wave data from GW170817 and GW190425 using the IMRPhenomPv2_NRTidal waveform.

## Key numbers

- Radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star from GW170817: improves from R_1.4 = 12.54^{+1.05}_{-1.54} km (uniform priors) to R_1.4 = 12.11^{+0.91}_{-1.11} km (physics-informed priors), 90% confidence intervals
- Tidal deformability for GW170817 at chirp mass 1.186 solar masses: improves from an upper limit of Lambda_tilde_1.186 < 720 (uniform priors) to Lambda_tilde_1.186 = 384^{+306}_{-158} (physics-informed priors), 90% confidence intervals
- Tidal deformability for GW190425 at chirp mass 1.44 solar masses: improves from Lambda_tilde_1.44 = 381^{+1065}_{-271} (uniform priors) to Lambda_tilde_1.44 = 183^{+180}_{-100} (physics-informed BNS prior)
- Bayes factor for GW190425 between BNS and NSBH hypotheses: BF = 1.33 in favor of BNS origin
- Radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star from GW190425: R_1.4 = 12.69^{+1.24}_{-1.22} km (BNS prior) and R_1.4 = 12.06^{+1.44}_{-1.15} km (NSBH prior)

## Context

This work extends the approach of Altiparmak (2022) and Ecker (2022), who demonstrated a tight statistical correlation between chirp mass and binary tidal deformability from physically consistent parameterized equations of state, by applying these physics-informed priors directly to gravitational-wave parameter estimation rather than using them as post-processing constraints. The method improves upon the standard LVK analysis (Abbott 2019) that employed uniform priors on chirp mass and tidal deformability, and provides consistent but slightly broader radius constraints compared to joint-likelihood approaches combining nuclear and astrophysical measurements (e.g., Dietrich 2020, Breschi 2024). The open-source code enables routine adoption of these priors in future gravitational-wave analyses of neutron-star mergers.
