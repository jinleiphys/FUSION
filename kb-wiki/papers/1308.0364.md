# FFLO state with angle dependent Gap in Asymmetric Nuclear Matter

> We consider the Fulde-Ferrell-Larkin-Ovchinnikov (FFLO) state with an angle dependent-gap (ADG) for the arbitrary angle θ_0 between the direction of the Cooper pair momentum and the symmetry axis of the ADG in asymmetric nuclear matter. We find two kinds of locally stable states, i.e., the FFLO-ADG-orthogonal and FFLO-ADG-parallel states, which correspond to θ_0=π/2 and θ_0=0, respectively. Furthermore, the-FFLO-ADG-orthogonal state is located at small asymmetry, whereas the FFLO-ADG-parallel state is favored for large asymmetry. The critical isospin asymmetry α_c, where the superfluid vanishes, is enhanced largely by considering the Cooper pair momentum with an ADG.

# FFLO state with angle dependent Gap in Asymmetric Nuclear Matter

## Key claim

The authors find that in asymmetric nuclear matter, the Fulde-Ferrell-Larkin-Ovchinnikov (FFLO) state combined with an angle-dependent gap (ADG) yields two locally stable configurations depending on the isospin asymmetry: the FFLO-ADG-Orthogonal state (Cooper pair momentum perpendicular to the ADG symmetry axis) is favored at small asymmetry, while the FFLO-ADG-Parallel state (parallel alignment) is favored at large asymmetry, with the critical isospin asymmetry where superfluidity vanishes being substantially enhanced compared to previous treatments that neglected the angle dependence.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled gap equations for the $^{3}SD_{1}$ pairing channel in asymmetric nuclear matter using the Argonne $V_{18}$ potential, incorporating both a finite Cooper pair momentum $2\mathbf{Q}$ and an axi-symmetric angle-dependent gap (ADG) with a symmetry-breaking axis. The angle $\theta_{0}$ between the Cooper pair momentum direction and the ADG symmetry axis was treated as a variational parameter, and the free energy was minimized to determine the ground state at fixed density $\rho_{0}=0.17\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}$ and temperature $\beta^{-1}=0.5\ \mathrm{MeV}$.

## Key numbers

- The density was fixed at $\rho_{0}=0.17\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}$ and the temperature at $\beta^{-1}=0.5\ \mathrm{MeV}$.
- At isospin asymmetry $\alpha=0.15$, the sole local minimum of the free-energy difference was located at $(\theta_{0}=\pi/2,\ Q=0.097\ k_{F})$, corresponding to the FFLO-ADG-Orthogonal state.
- At $\alpha=0.25$, two local minima were found: $(\theta_{0}=\pi/2,\ Q=0.145\ k_{F})$ and $(\theta_{0}=0,\ Q=0.18\ k_{F})$, corresponding to the Orthogonal and Parallel states respectively.
- The FFLO-ADG-Parallel state emerged above $\alpha=0.16$ and became more stable than the Orthogonal state for $\alpha>0.286$.
- The FFLO-ADG-Orthogonal state vanished at $\alpha=0.37$, while the FFLO-ADG-Parallel state persisted up to $\alpha=0.47$.
- The pairing gap in symmetric matter at zero total momentum, $\Delta_{s0}$, was approximately $12\ \mathrm{MeV}$ with the free single-particle spectrum (citing Zuo, tt, and jme).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the FFLO state in nuclear matter (ffn) and the angle-dependent gap (ADG) state (min) by treating both effects simultaneously within the same model, allowing for an arbitrary angle between the two symmetry-breaking axes. The authors' previous work (min) had shown that the angle dependence of the pairing gap must be accounted for in asymmetric nuclear matter at low temperature, while earlier studies of both the FFLO state (ffn) and the deformed Fermi surfaces (DFS) state (dfs, dfsn) had employed an angle-averaging procedure that treated the gap as isotropic. The results enable a more complete determination of the ground state structure of isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter and provide a phase diagram showing which configuration (Orthogonal or Parallel) is favored as a function of asymmetry.
