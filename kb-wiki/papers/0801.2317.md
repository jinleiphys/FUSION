# Functional renormalisation group for two-body scattering

> The functional renormalisation group is applied to the effective action for scattering of two nonrelativistic fermions. The resulting physical effective action is shown to contain the correct threshold singularity. The corresponding "bare" action respects Galilean invariance up to second order in momenta. Beyond that order it contains terms that violate this symmetry and, for the particular regulator considered, nonanalytic third-order terms. The corresponding potential can be expanded around a nontrivial fixed point using the power counting appropriate to a system with large scattering length.

# Functional renormalisation group for two-body scattering

## Key claim
The functional renormalisation group applied to two-body scattering of nonrelativistic fermions generates the correct threshold singularity in the physical effective action, but the particular regulator used (a sharp cut-off) introduces violations of Galilean invariance beyond second order in momenta and nonanalytic third-order terms that must be included in the bare action with carefully tuned coefficients.

## Method
The author applies the exact (functional) renormalisation group to the Legendre-transformed effective action for two nonrelativistic fermions, introducing a boson field to describe correlated pairs (dimers) as in Refs. Birse et al. (2005), Krippa (2006), and Diehl et al. (2007). Unlike previous work, the boson self-energy is not expanded in derivatives but kept to all orders in energy and momenta. The evolution equation is integrated analytically using the optimised regulator of Diehl et al. (2007), which is a nonrelativistic version of Litim's (2001) sharp cut-off, to obtain the full momentum- and energy-dependent boson self-energy.

## Key numbers
- The boson self-energy at the starting scale K contains a term of order P^3 (the leading Galilean-violating term), with coefficient -1/(24K^2) in the expansion of the effective potential.
- The wave-function renormalisation factor Z_phi(k) and kinetic-mass renormalisation factor Z_m(k) are identical and equal to (g^2 M^2)/(4 pi^2) * (8/(3k) - (pi/2) r_e), where r_e is the effective range.
- The rescaled potential at the nontrivial fixed point contains a constant term of order unity, specifically -2/3, which is stated to be universal.
- The initial values for Z_phi and Z_m become negative if the effective range r_e is positive and the starting scale K exceeds 16/(3 pi r_e).

## Context
This work extends the functional RG calculations of the Kyushu group (Harada et al., 2007) for nucleon-nucleon scattering and the three-body calculations of Diehl et al. (2007), by keeping the boson self-energy to all orders rather than using a derivative expansion. It connects to the Wilsonian RG methods of Birse et al. (1999) for determining power countings in effective field theories, and to the effective field theory approaches for few-body systems reviewed by Bedaque and van Kolck (2002) and Epelbaum (2006). The analysis identifies artefacts of the sharp cut-off regulator that will require careful treatment in numerical applications to fermionic matter, particularly the need for nonanalytic terms in the bare action and the divergence of derivative expansions as the physical limit is approached.
