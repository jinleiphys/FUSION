# Randomness and Emerging Order in Nuclear Structure

> In order to investigate to what extent is the low-lying behavior of even-even nuclei dependent on particular nucleon-nucleon interactions, we consider systems of bosons where these interactions are taken as gaussian random numbers with equal likelyhood of being attractive or repulsive. We find a statistical dominance of L=0 ground states and other correlations, which we analyze in terms of a mean field approach.

# Randomness and Emerging Order in Nuclear Structure

## Key claim

The statistical dominance of L=0 ground states and the appearance of vibrational and rotational band structures in boson models with random interactions are robust, general properties that arise from the many-body dynamics rather than from specific forms of the nucleon-nucleon interaction; however, the authors qualify that these results are obtained for boson systems (the Interacting Boson Model and vibron model) and that a clear explanation for the same dominance in fermion shell-model systems remains an open question.

## Method

The authors performed numerical studies of the Interacting Boson Model (IBM) and the vibron model, taking the one- and two-body interaction parameters as independent Gaussian random numbers with zero mean and equal likelihood of being attractive or repulsive. They then carried out a mean-field analysis using coherent states to derive the potential energy surface as a function of a deformation parameter, and computed the joint probability distribution of the surface coefficients to determine the probabilities of spherical, deformed, and p-boson condensate equilibrium configurations.

## Key numbers

- In the IBM with random interactions, 63.4% of ground states have L^P=0^+, 13.8% have L^P=2^+, and 16.7% have the maximum angular momentum L^P=32^+.
- For the vibron model with even boson number N, the ground state has L=0 in approximately 71% of cases; for odd N, approximately 55% of cases.
- In the vibron model, the probability distribution of the energy ratio R = E(2^+_1)/E(1^-_1) shows peaks at the vibrational value R=2 and the rotational value R=3.
- The mean-field analysis yields probabilities for equilibrium configurations: spherical shape 39.6%, deformed shape 21.6%, and p-boson condensate 38.8%.
- The mean-field estimate for L=0 ground states gives 69.8% for even N and 50.4% for odd N, in agreement with the exact numerical results.

## Context

This work extends the surprising findings of Johnson et al. (late 1990s) that random two-body interactions in the shell model (TBRE) produce a statistical preference for L=0 ground states, which contradicted the conventional view that such order requires specific interactions like pairing or quadrupole-quadrupole forces. The authors build on their own prior numerical studies of the IBM with random interactions (Bijker and Frank, 2000) and provide an analytic mean-field explanation using coherent states, which had previously been applied to the vibron model by other groups. The results enable future work on whether similar shape-based explanations can account for the L=0 dominance observed in fermion shell-model calculations, and whether vibrational and rotational correlations can emerge from truncated fermion spaces.
