# Study of Multiquarks Systems in a Chiral Quark Model

> We discuss the stability of multiquark systems within the recent model of Glozman et al. where the chromomagnetic hyperfine interaction is replaced by pseudoscalar-meson exchange contributions. In this model the (u,d) diquark S=0 I=0 is strongly bound (more than for the chromomagnetic interaction) and this leads to a bound heavy tetraquark QQ\bar q\bar q where the light quarks are in the S=0 I=0 configuration. We study the stability of other multiquark systems as well.

## Key claim

The Glozman et al. chiral quark model, which replaces the chromomagnetic hyperfine interaction with pseudoscalar-meson exchange, predicts that the doubly charmed tetraquark \(cc\bar q\bar q\) is strongly bound, in contrast to conventional models where it is unbound or only weakly bound; this binding is substantially independent of the choice of confining potential.

## Method

The authors performed variational calculations for tetraquark (\(QQ\bar q\bar q\)) and hexaquark (\(QQqqqq\) and \(q^6\)) systems using the Hamiltonian of Glozman et al., which includes confining, chromomagnetic, and meson-exchange terms. They used two confining potentials: a very weak linear potential (C1) from Glozman et al. and a more conventional Coulomb-plus-linear potential (C2) from Silvestre-Brac and Semay (1995). Trial Gaussian wave functions with variational parameters were employed for the few-body systems, and the spin-flavour-averaged interaction was computed using Clebsch-Gordan coefficients of the permutation group.

## Key numbers

* The \(cc\bar q\bar q\) tetraquark is bound by 185 MeV using confining potential C1 plus meson exchange, and by 332 MeV using potential C2 plus meson exchange.
* The \(bb\bar q\bar q\) tetraquark is bound by 226 MeV using potential C1 plus meson exchange, and by 497 MeV using potential C2 plus meson exchange.
* The light quark masses were fixed to 0.34 GeV; the charm quark mass was set to 1.35 GeV (potential C1) or 1.87 GeV (potential C2), and the bottom quark mass to 4.66 GeV (C1) or 5.259 GeV (C2).
* The calculated \(\Lambda_c\) mass is 2.32 GeV and the \(\Sigma_c/\Sigma_c^*\) mass is 2.48 GeV, close to experimental values.
* The \(ccqqqq\) hexaquark is unbound by about 500 MeV with potential C1; the \(q^6\) system is unbound by almost 1.5 GeV with potential C1.

## Context

This work extends the chiral quark model of Glozman et al. (1995, 1996), which successfully describes the baryon spectrum, to multiquark systems containing heavy quarks. It contradicts earlier results from conventional chromomagnetic models: Silvestre-Brac and Semay (1995) found the \(cc\bar q\bar q\) state about 20 MeV above threshold, and a diquark model study found it 30 MeV above threshold, while the present model predicts strong binding. The results enable experimental tests of the Glozman model through searches for doubly charmed tetraquarks at Fermilab and CERN, and show that the model does not lead to a proliferation of bound multiquark states, as both the \(QQqqqq\) and \(q^6\) systems are unbound.
