# Q-phonon description of low lying 1^- two-phonon states in spherical nuclei

> The properties of 1^-_1 two-phonon states and the characteristics of E1 transition probabilities between low-lying collective states in spherical nuclei are analysed within the Q-phonon approach to the description of collective states. Several relations between observables are obtained. Microscopic calculations of the E1 0^+_1 -> 1^-_1 transition matrix elements are performed on the basis of the RPA. A satisfactory description of the experimental data is obtained.

# Q-phonon description of low lying 1^- two-phonon states in spherical nuclei

## Key claim

The Q-phonon approach, formulated in fermionic configurational space, can describe low-lying 1^- states as quadrupole-octupole two-phonon states and derive relations between E1 transition matrix elements that explain experimental correlations, but the ratio B(E1;1^-_1→0^+_1)/B(E1;3^-_1→2^+_1) obtained from the model is 7/3, which deviates from the experimentally observed value of 1 to 2, indicating that admixtures not captured by the pure Q-phonon approach are present.

## Method

The authors formulated the Q-phonon approach in fermionic configurational space, representing the 2^+_1, 3^-_1, and 1^-_1 state vectors as Q_2, Q_3, and (Q_2 Q_3) operators acting on the ground state, respectively. They derived analytic relations between E1 transition matrix elements using the commutativity of multipole operators, and performed microscopic RPA-based calculations of the E1 0^+_1→1^-_1 transition matrix elements for Cd, Sn, Ba, Ce, Nd, and Sm isotopes, including both collective two-phonon and two-quasiparticle components.

## Key numbers

- The ratio B(E1;1^-_1→0^+_1)/B(E1;3^-_1→2^+_1) equals 7/3 in the Q-phonon model, while experimental observations give a value of 1 to 2.
- The two-quasiparticle admixture to the 1^-_1 state contributes less than 1% to the total norm of the wave vector.
- For a pure alpha-cluster state, the E1 reduced matrix element is approximately 6 e·fm; for a 1p-1h (1h_11/2 1g^-1_9/2)_1^- proton state, it is 1.7 e·fm; for the two-quasiparticle component extracted from the 1^-_1 state, it is 0.6-0.7 e·fm.
- The mixing of the (Q_2 Q_2)_4 Q_3 component into the 1^- state in deformed regions can be estimated as 70% of one component plus 30% of the other.

## Context

This work extends the Q-phonon approach previously developed within the Interacting Boson Model by Pietralla et al. (2001) and Palchikov et al. (2001) for positive parity states, now formulated in fermionic space and applied to both positive and negative parity states. The paper addresses experimental observations of low-lying 1^- states in spherical nuclei including Cd, Sn, Ba, Ce, Nd, and Sm isotopes, where strong B(E1) values of several units × 10^-3 e^2 fm^2 have been reported by Guhr et al., Zilges et al., Kneissl et al., and others. The results enable further microscopic investigation of anharmonic effects beyond pure RPA, which the authors argue are essential for describing enhanced E1 transitions.
