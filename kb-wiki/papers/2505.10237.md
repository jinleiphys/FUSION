# Competition between the neutron-proton pair break-ups delineating the level structure of 202Po

> High-spin spectroscopic study of $^{202}$Po ($Z$ = 84, $N$ = 118) has been carried out using the $^{195}$Pt($^{12}$C, 5n)$^{202}$Po fusion-evaporation reaction. An extended level scheme has been proposed up to an excitation energy of $E_x\approx$ 8 MeV and angular momentum of 27$\hbar$, with the addition of 57 newly observed $γ$-ray transitions, along with the revisions in the placement of 8 already known transitions and the multipolarities of 4 of these transitions. The energy of the unobserved 8$^+ \rightarrow 6^+$ transition has been proposed to be 9.0(5) keV, which resolves the uncertainty in the excitation energy of the levels above the 6$^{+}$ state. Three new sequences of $M1$ transitions have also been identified in the high excitation energy regime and included in the proposed level scheme. The large-scale shell model calculations for $Z>82$ and $N<126$ valence space have been carried out using PBPOP interaction which explained the overall level scheme for both the positive and negative parity states. The calculations successfully reproduced the purity of the proton $πh_{9/2}$ dominated $8^+$ isomeric state, and also explained the missing $E2$ decay of the ${12}^+$ isomeric state in terms of changing nucleonic configurations.

## Key claim

The paper extends the level scheme of 202Po up to 27ℏ and approximately 8 MeV excitation energy, but the abstract's claim of "explaining the overall level scheme" is hedged by the fact that the large-scale shell-model calculations using the PBPOP interaction successfully reproduce only the purity of the proton πh9/2 dominated 8+ isomeric state and the missing E2 decay of the 12+ isomeric state, without claiming full reproduction of all newly observed sequences.

## Method

High-spin states in 202Po were populated via the 195Pt(12C, 5n)202Po fusion-evaporation reaction at beam energies of 83 and 87 MeV, using a 3.2 mg/cm2 thick isotopically enriched 195Pt target with a 197Au catcher foil. De-exciting γ rays were detected with an array of 16 Compton-suppressed HPGe clover detectors arranged in six rings, and γγ coincidence events were sorted into symmetric and asymmetric matrices for Directional Correlation of γ-ray de-exciting Oriented states (DCO ratio) and integrated polarization directional correlation from oriented states (IPDCO) analyses to assign spins and parities. Large-scale shell-model calculations were performed for the Z>82 and N<126 valence space using the PBPOP interaction.

## Key numbers

- 57 newly observed γ-ray transitions were added to the level scheme, along with revisions in the placement of 8 already known transitions and multipolarities of 4 transitions.
- The energy of the unobserved 8+ → 6+ transition was proposed to be 9.0(5) keV, resolving the uncertainty in excitation energy of levels above the 6+ state.
- The level scheme was extended up to an excitation energy of Ex ≈ 8 MeV and angular momentum of 27ℏ.
- The yrast 8+ state has a half-life of 114 ns (from prior work by the same group, Sahab 2024).
- The 11−1 isomeric state has a half-life of 83 ns (from prior work by the same group, Sahab 2024).
- The 677.2-keV transition (2+ → 0+) was used as the intensity reference, normalized to 100 units.
- The 443.0-keV transition (6+ → 4+) has a relative intensity of 91.0(48).
- The 912.5-keV transition (11− → 8+) was assigned E3 multipolarity based on RDCO and ΔIPDCO values.

## Context

This work extends the prior high-spin study of 202Po by Fant (1990), which reported states up to Jπ = (22+), and the β-decay study by Bijnens (1998) which explored low-medium spin states up to (9−). The new measurement resolves the previously unknown energy of the 8+ → 6+ transition (9.0(5) keV) using parallel decay branches, a technique similar to that used for 200Po by Maj (1990). The identification of three new sequences of M1 transitions in the high-excitation regime addresses the previous absence of such magnetic dipole sequences in lighter Po isotopes, which have been observed in neighboring Pb isotopes since the 1990s by Baldsiefen (1992), Clark (1992), and Hubel (1992). The results enable further testing of large-scale shell-model calculations with the PBPOP interaction and provide new data on the competition between neutron-proton pair break-ups at N=118, which was predicted by Maheshwari (2021) to be a transition point for seniority isomers in Po isotopes.
