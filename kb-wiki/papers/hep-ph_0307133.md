# On heavy-light meson resonances and chiral symmetry

> We study heavy-light meson resonances with quantum numbers J^P=0^+ and J^P=1^+ in terms of the non-linear chiral SU(3) Lagrangian. At leading order a parameter-free prediction is obtained for the scattering of Goldstone bosons off heavy-light pseudo-scalar and vector mesons once we insist on approximate crossing symmetry of the unitarized scattering amplitude. The recently announced narrow open charm states observed by the BABAR and CLEO collaborations are reproduced. We suggest the existence of states that form an anti-triplet and a sextet representation of the SU(3) group. In particular, so far unobserved narrow isospin-singlet states with negative strangeness (I,S)=(0,-1) are predicted at 2361 MeV J^P=0^+ and 2501 MeV (J^P=1^+). Similarly, open bottom states are found at 5719 MeV (J^P=0^+) and 5622 MeV (J^P=1^+). Additional narrow states of mass 5580 MeV (J^P=0^+) and 5650 MeV (J^P=1^+) with widths of about 50 MeV are obtained in the (I,S)=(1,1) sector. For the anti-triplet states our results differ most significantly from predictions that are based on a linear realization of the chiral SU(3) symmetry in the open bottom sector. Strongly bound 0^+-and 1^+-states with (I,S)=(0,1) at 5507 MeV and 5553 MeV are predicted.

## Key claim

Using a non-linear chiral SU(3) Lagrangian with approximate crossing symmetry of the unitarized scattering amplitude, the authors obtain a parameter-free prediction for heavy-light meson resonances with \(J^P=0^+\) and \(J^P=1^+\), which reproduces the narrow open charm states observed by BABAR and CLEO, but the results depend on the choice of the subtraction scale and the specific SU(3) realization (non-linear versus linear).

## Method

The authors apply the \(\chi\)-BS(3) approach, a coupled-channel description of Goldstone boson scattering off heavy-light pseudo-scalar (\(0^-\)) and vector (\(1^-\)) mesons, using the leading-order chiral SU(3) Lagrangian with a Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction kernel. The scattering amplitudes are unitarized via a matrix equation involving loop functions, with subtraction scales chosen to enforce approximate crossing symmetry, and resonance poles are identified from speed plots of the s-wave amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- Open charm bound state in the \((I,S)=(0,1)\) channel at 2303 MeV (\(J^P=0^+\)), identified with the BABAR state at 2317 MeV.
- Open charm bound state in the \((I,S)=(0,1)\) channel at 2440 MeV (\(J^P=1^+\)), consistent with the CLEO state at 2460 MeV (mass splitting of about 140 MeV).
- Narrow open charm state in the \((I,S)=(\frac{1}{2},0)\) channel at 2413 MeV (\(J^P=0^+\)) and at 2552 MeV (\(J^P=1^+\)), the latter identified with the \(D(2420)\) resonance (overestimated by about 130 MeV).
- Open bottom bound state in the \((I,S)=(0,1)\) channel at 5643 MeV (\(J^P=0^+\)) and at 5690 MeV (\(J^P=1^+\)).
- Open bottom bound state in the \((I,S)=(0,-1)\) channel at 5761 MeV (\(J^P=0^+\)) and at 5807 MeV (\(J^P=1^+\)).
- Open bottom narrow resonance in the \((I,S)=(\frac{1}{2},0)\) channel at 5760 MeV (\(J^P=0^+\)) with width about 30 MeV.
- In the 'heavy' SU(3) limit (\(m_{\pi,K,\eta}=500\) MeV, \(M_D=1800\) MeV), an anti-triplet bound state at 2204 MeV is found; with \(f=80\) MeV, sextet poles appear at 2298 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the \(\chi\)-BS(3) approach, previously applied to light meson resonances (Lutz and Kolomeitsev, 2003) and meson-baryon scattering (Lutz and Kolomeitsev, 2000-2002), to the heavy-light sector with open charm and bottom. It contrasts with predictions based on a linear realization of chiral SU(3) symmetry (Nowak et al., 1993; Bardeen and Hill, 1994; Bardeen et al., 2003; Nowak et al., 2003), which yield only anti-triplet states, by predicting additional sextet states with unconventional quantum numbers \((I,S)=(1,1)\) and \((0,-1)\). The results enable experimental searches for the predicted narrow open bottom states and further tests of chiral symmetry breaking in the heavy-quark sector.
