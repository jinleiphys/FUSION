# The $\sin2Ď•$ azimuthal asymmetry in single longitudinally polarized $Ď€N$ Drell-Yan process

> We study the $\sin2Ď•$ azimuthal asymmetry in the $Ď€N$ Drell-Yan process, when the nucleon is longitudinally polarized. The asymmetry is contributed by the combination of the Boer-Mulders function and the longitudinal transversity distribution function. We consider the Drell-Yan processes by $Ď€^\pm$ beams colliding on the proton and deuteron targets, respectively. We calculate the $\sin2Ď•$ azimuthal asymmetries in these processes using the Boer-Mulders function and the longitudinal transversity from spectator models. We show that the study on single polarized $Ď€N$ Drell-Yan processes can not only give the information on the new 3-dimensional parton distribution functions in momentum space, but also shed light on the chiral-odd structure of the longitudinally polarized nucleon.

## Key claim

The $\sin2\phi$ azimuthal asymmetry in single longitudinally polarized $\pi N$ Drell-Yan processes, arising from the combination of the Boer-Mulders function and the longitudinal transversity distribution $h_{1L}^\perp$, is predicted to be several percent at COMPASS kinematics, making it measurable with good accuracy; the authors note that this asymmetry provides a clean probe of $h_{1L}^\perp$ because no competing mechanism exists even at higher twist.

## Method

The authors calculate the $\sin2\phi$ weighted asymmetry $A^{\sin(2\phi)}_{UL}$ in the Collins-Soper frame using leading-order TMD factorization for the process $\pi N^\Rightarrow \rightarrow \ell^+\ell^- X$, where the nucleon is longitudinally polarized. They employ the Boer-Mulders function for pions from a quark spectator antiquark model (Lu, 2009) and the longitudinal transversity $h_{1L}^\perp$ for nucleons from a light-cone quark spectator diquark model with Melosh-Wigner rotation (Ma and Zhu, 2009). The asymmetry is evaluated for $\pi^\pm$ beams on proton and deuteron targets at COMPASS ($\sqrt{s}=18.9$ GeV) and for $pp^\Rightarrow$ at RHIC ($\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV), with the deuteron TMDs approximated as the sum of free proton and neutron distributions under isospin symmetry.

## Key numbers

- The predicted $\sin2\phi$ asymmetries for $\pi^\pm p^\Rightarrow$ and $\pi^\pm d^\Rightarrow$ processes at COMPASS are about several percent, with the $\pi^-$ beam on proton giving the largest magnitude.
- COMPASS kinematics used: $\sqrt{s}=18.9$ GeV, $0.1<x_1<1$, $0.05<x_2<0.5$, $4\leq M\leq 8.5$ GeV, $0\leq q_T \leq 4$ GeV.
- For the $pp^\Rightarrow$ Drell-Yan process at RHIC ($\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV), the predicted asymmetries are also several percent and negative, with kinematics: $4<M<20$ GeV, $-2<\frac{1}{2}\ln(x_1/x_2)<-2$, $0<q_T<3$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the $\sin2\phi_h$ asymmetry in longitudinally polarized SIDIS (Ma, 2001; Ma, 2002; Zhu, 2009) to the Drell-Yan process, where the asymmetry arises from the same $h_{1L}^\perp$ distribution combined with the Boer-Mulders function. The authors note that transversely polarized and unpolarized $\pi N$ Drell-Yan processes have been studied by Efremov (2005), Bianconi (2006), Lu (2006), and Anselmino (2009), while the present work provides the first phenomenological study of the $\sin2\phi$ asymmetry in single longitudinally polarized $\pi N$ Drell-Yan. The predictions enable experimental tests at COMPASS (Takekawa, 2010) and RHIC (Bunce, 2000), which could probe the chiral-odd structure of the longitudinally polarized nucleon via the TMD $h_{1L}^\perp$ (longi-transversity).
