# The Low energy structure of the Nucleon-Nucleon interaction: Statistical vs Systematic Uncertainties

> We analyze the low energy NN interaction by confronting statistical vs systematic uncertainties. This is carried out with the help of model potentials fitted to the Granada-2013 database where a statistically meaningful partial wave analysis comprising a total of $6713$ np and pp published scattering data from 1950 till 2013 below $350 {\rm MeV}$ has been made. We extract threshold parameters uncertainties from the coupled channel effective range expansion up to $j \le 5$. We find that for threshold parameters systematic uncertainties are generally at least an order of magnitude larger than statistical uncertainties. Similar results are found for np phase-shifts and amplitude parameters.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for low-energy nucleon-nucleon (NN) threshold parameters, systematic uncertainties arising from the choice of potential model are generally at least an order of magnitude larger than the statistical uncertainties derived from experimental data, even in the long-wavelength limit where model dependence was expected to be minimal.

## Method

The authors analyzed six statistically equivalent model potentials (with χ²/d.o.f. ~ 1) fitted to the same self-consistent Granada-2013 database of 6713 np and pp scattering data below 350 MeV laboratory energy. They extracted threshold parameters from the coupled-channel effective range expansion up to j ≤ 5 using a discrete variable S-matrix method, then compared the statistical uncertainties (from Monte Carlo bootstrap sampling of the DS-OPE potential) with systematic uncertainties (from the spread across all six potentials plus three additional realistic potentials: NijmII, Reid93, and AV18).

## Key numbers

- The Granada-2013 database contains 6713 np and pp published scattering data from 1950 to 2013 below 350 MeV
- For the ¹S₀ partial wave: scattering length α = -23.735(6) fm (statistical) vs -23.735(16) fm (systematic); effective range r₀ = 2.673(9) fm (statistical) vs 2.68(3) fm (systematic)
- For the ³S₁ eigen-channel: scattering length α = 5.435(2) fm (statistical) vs 5.42(1) fm (systematic); effective range r₀ = 1.852(2) fm (statistical) vs 1.84(1) fm (systematic)
- The six model potentials have χ²/d.o.f. values ranging from 1.05 to 1.14, with p-values between 0.20 and 0.50
- The SAID PWA gives χ²/N_dat = 1.4157, which is 25σ away from the expected value of 1

## Context

This work extends the Nijmegen-1993 partial wave analysis (Stoks 1993) by updating the database through 2013 and providing the first systematic uncertainty quantification for low-energy NN threshold parameters. It complements the authors' previous work on the Granada-2013 database (Perez 2013) and their studies of statistical uncertainty propagation (Perez 2014). The results enable future ab initio nuclear structure calculations to incorporate realistic uncertainty estimates from NN scattering data, and provide lower bounds on systematic uncertainties that can be used to test chiral effective field theory predictions and causality bounds in np scattering.
