# A systematic study of alpha and cluster decay in Platinum isotopes

> The feasibility of alpha and cluster decay from Pt isotopes has been investigated within the framework of Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory. Calculation has been carried out for various Skyrme forces. Harmonic oscillator and transformed harmonic oscillator basis are used to solve HFB equations. The role played by shell closure is withal analysed. Half-lives are estimated with the help of Universal Decay Law (UDL). Geiger-Nuttel plots are also plotted and successfully preserves its linear nature.

## Key claim

The paper systematically investigates alpha and cluster decay feasibility in even-even Pt isotopes using Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory, but the results are conditional on the specific Skyrme force parametrization used; the authors note that UNEDF forces show less deviation in predicting alpha-decay half-lives compared to others, and that the choice of harmonic oscillator versus transformed harmonic oscillator basis produces only a few keV difference in half-lives.

## Method

The authors solved axially deformed Skyrme HFB equations using both harmonic oscillator (HO) and transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) basis, employing six Skyrme parametrizations (SIII, SkP, SkM*, SLy5, UNEDF0, UNEDF1) with density-dependent delta pairing in mixed form. Q-values for alpha and cluster decays were computed from the HFB binding energies, and half-lives were estimated using the Universal Decay Law (UDL). The study covered even-even Pt isotopes from the two-proton drip line to the beta stability line, examining clusters from alpha up to 24Mg.

## Key numbers

- For alpha decay of 174Pt, the experimental Q-value is 6.03 MeV; UNEDF0 predicts 6.0872 MeV (HO basis) and UNEDF1 predicts 6.6242 MeV (HO basis), compared to the ELDM value of 6.1831 MeV.
- Standard deviation of alpha-decay half-lives for UNEDF0 is 0.7660 (HO basis) and 0.7127 (THO basis), the lowest among all Skyrme forces tested; SLy5 shows the largest deviation at 8.0337 (HO basis).
- For 168Pt → 16O + 152Yb, the Q-value ranges from 39.5458 MeV (UNEDF0, HO) to 43.8462 MeV (SkM*, HO), with ELDM giving 39.9970 MeV.
- The Geiger-Nuttall plot slopes for alpha decay range from 126.690 (SkM*, HO) to 128.445 (UNEDF0, HO); for 24Mg decay, slopes range from 1485.281 (UNEDF0, HO) to 1521.823 (SLy5, HO).
- Half-lives were only considered for decays with T1/2 < 10^30 s, the experimentally measurable range.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous systematic studies of cluster decay in tungsten (W) and osmium (Os) isotopes into the platinum (Z=78) isotopic chain, which is a transitional region expected to be deformed in its ground state. The results are compared with the Effective Liquid Drop Model (ELDM) and, where available, experimental alpha-decay data. The study confirms that cluster decay is most probable when the daughter nucleus has a magic neutron number (N=82), consistent with the established role of shell closure in cluster radioactivity, and that as parent atomic number increases, more massive clusters can be emitted. The work enables future global calculations across the nuclear chart to better understand Skyrme force dependence in half-life predictions.
