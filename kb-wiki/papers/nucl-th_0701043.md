# J/psi hadron interaction in vacuum and in QGP

> Motivated by the recent lattice data that $J/ψ$ will survive up to 1.6$T_c$, we calculate the thermal width of $J/ψ$ at finite temperature in perturbative QCD. The inputs of the calculation are the parton quarkonium dissociation cross sections at the NLO in QCD, which were previously obtained by Song and Lee, and a gaussian charmonium wave function, whose size were fitted to an estimate by Wong by solving the schrodinger equation for charmonium in a potential extracted from the lattice at finite temperature. We find that the total thermal width above 1.4$T_c$ becomes larger than 100 to 200 MeV, depending on the effective thermal masses of the quark and gluon, which we take it to vary from 600 to 400 MeV.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the thermal width of J/ψ in a quark-gluon plasma using perturbative QCD at next-to-leading order (NLO), finding that above 1.4Tc the total thermal width exceeds 100–200 MeV depending on the assumed effective thermal masses of quarks and gluons (taken to vary from 600 to 400 MeV), which suggests that even if J/ψ forms at 1.6Tc, its large thermal width prevents accumulation until the system cools further.

## Method

The authors fold NLO parton–quarkonium dissociation cross sections (previously obtained by Song and Lee) with thermal parton distribution functions, using a Gaussian charmonium wave function whose size is fitted to an estimate by Wong from solving the Schrödinger equation for charmonium in a lattice-extracted potential at finite temperature. The LO and NLO invariant matrix elements for J/ψ dissociation by gluons and quarks are used, with collinear divergences at NLO regularized by introducing thermal masses for partons (600–400 MeV).

## Key numbers

- Total thermal width above 1.4Tc becomes larger than 100 to 200 MeV, depending on effective thermal masses of quark and gluon (varied from 600 to 400 MeV)
- Tc is taken as 170 MeV
- J/ψ is expected to survive up to 1.6Tc based on lattice data (Hatsuda 2003, Hatsuda 2004, Datta 2003, Datta 2005)

## Context

This work extends prior LO calculations of J/ψ dissociation by gluons (Peskin 1979, Wong 2004) to NLO in QCD, incorporating both gluon- and quark-induced dissociation processes. The results address the apparent tension between lattice evidence that J/ψ survives up to 1.6Tc and the statistical coalescence model for J/ψ production near Tc (Gorenstein 1999, PBM 2006), by showing that even if the bound state exists at those temperatures, its large thermal width prevents significant accumulation until the system cools.
