# Final State Interaction in Neutron Deuteron Charge Exchange Reaction at Small Transfer Momentum

> Analysis of the $nd\to p(nn)$ reaction in a Gev-energy region is performed in the framework based on the multiple-scattering theory for the few nucleon system. The special kinematic condition, when momentum transfer from neutron beam to final proton closes to zero, is considered. The possibility to extract the spin-flip term of the elementary $np\to pn $ amplitude from nd-breakup process is investigated. The energy dependence of the ratio $R=\frac{dσ_{nd}} {dΩ} / \frac{dσ_{np}}{dΩ}$ is obtained taking account of the final state interaction two outgoing neutrons in $^1 S_0$-state.

# Final State Interaction in Neutron Deuteron Charge Exchange Reaction at Small Transfer Momentum

## Key claim

The ratio of the nd charge exchange differential cross section to the free np scattering differential cross section can be factorized to extract the spin-dependent part of the elementary np amplitude, but the extracted value will depend on the applied model for final state interaction (FSI) description and the choice of deuteron wave function, meaning the nd reaction cannot define the precise value of the spin-dependent part of free np scattering, only approximate information such as sign and rough magnitude.

## Method

The analysis uses the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas formulation of multiple-scattering theory for the three-nucleon system, with the matrix inversion method applied to describe the interaction of the two slow neutrons. The amplitude is separated into a plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) term and a term accounting for FSI in the \(^1S_0\) state of the two outgoing neutrons, with the t-matrix for free np elastic scattering at angle \(\theta = \pi\) described by three independent amplitudes (A, F, B). The calculation is performed in the deuteron rest frame for neutron kinetic energies from 0.8 to 1.3 GeV under the kinematic condition where transfer momentum from the neutron beam to the final proton is close to zero.

## Key numbers

- The R ratio varies by a factor of 10 while transfer momentum changes by a few MeV in the region where the final proton momentum is close to the beam momentum.
- The difference between PWIA and full calculation results is about 30% for \(\Delta p = 10\) MeV, about 15% for \(\Delta p = 20\) MeV, and the lines are practically indistinguishable when \(\Delta p = 60\) MeV, at \(T_n = 1\) GeV.
- The difference between the FSI-included result and the PWIA result is about 10% for kinetic energy 800 MeV and a few percent for kinetic energy 1300 MeV, with integration over \(\Delta p = 30\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the idea originally suggested by Pomeranchuk (1951) that nucleon-deuteron charge exchange reactions can provide information about the spin-dependent part of the elementary np to pn process, which was later shown in PWIA by Aladashvili et al. and the COSY collaboration. The paper addresses the important role of FSI effects under kinematic conditions where the relative momentum of the two slow nucleons is small, which were neglected in earlier PWIA treatments. The results are relevant to experiments at VBLHE JINR (STRELA and DELTA SIGMA setups) and COSY (ANKE spectrometer), and enable extraction of approximate spin-dependent information from nd breakup, though with model-dependent FSI corrections.
