# Effective Field Theories in Nuclear Particle and Atomic Physics

> These are the proceedings of the workshop on ``Effective Field Theories in Nuclear, Particle and Atomic Physics'' held at the Physikzentrum Bad Honnef of the Deutsche Physikalische Gesellschaft, Bad Honnef, Germany from December 13 to 17, 2005. The workshop concentrated on Effective Field Theory in many contexts. A first part was concerned with Chiral Perturbation Theory in its various settings and explored strongly its use in relation with lattice QCD. The second part consisted of progress in effective field theories in systems with one, two or more nucleons as well as atomic physics. Included are a short contribution per talk.

## Key claim

This paper is a workshop proceedings volume, not a single research paper; it collects short contributions from 48 talks presented at the 337th WE-Heraeus-Seminar on effective field theories in nuclear, particle, and atomic physics, held in Bad Honnef, Germany, from December 13 to 17, 2004. The workshop aimed to bring together communities working on Chiral Perturbation Theory, nuclear effective field theory, lattice QCD, and atomic few-body systems, with an emphasis on discussion rather than presentation.

## Method

The workshop consisted of five days of invited talks organized into sessions on Chiral Perturbation Theory, Nuclear Effective Theory, EFT in Atomic and Nuclear Physics, EFT in Particle Physics, and Lattice QCD and ChPT. Each of the 48 speakers provided a short written contribution summarizing their results and most relevant references, which are compiled in these proceedings. The meeting followed a series of similar topical workshops held in Ringberg (1988), Dobogókő (1991), Karrebæksminde (1993), Trento (1996), and Bad Honnef (1998 and 2001).

Review-type paper: no original numerics.

## Context

This proceedings volume documents the state of the art in effective field theory applications across nuclear, particle, and atomic physics as of late 2004. It extends the series of workshops that began in Ringberg (1988) and includes contributions on topics such as the determination of piN scattering lengths from pionic deuterium experiments (Meissner, Raha, Rusetsky), the linearized GDH sum rule (Holstein, Pascalutsa, Vanderhaeghen), and the role of strange sea quarks in chiral extrapolations on the lattice (Descotes-Genon). The volume enables future work by providing a snapshot of ongoing research and open questions, particularly in the interface between Chiral Perturbation Theory and lattice QCD, and in the application of effective field theory to few-nucleon and atomic systems.
