# Statistical uncertainties of the $v_{n}\{2k\}$ harmonics from Q-cumulants

> Analytic formulas to calculate statistical uncertainties of $v_{n}\{2k\}$ harmonics extracted from the Q-cumulants are presented. The Q-cumulants are multivariate polynomial functions of the weighted means of $2m$-particle azimuthal correlations, $\llangle 2m \rrangle$. Variances and covariances of the $\llangle 2m \rrangle$ are included in the analytic formulas of the uncertainties that can be calculated simultaneously with the calculations of the $v_{n}\{2k\}$ harmonics. The calculations are performed using a simple toy model which roughly simulates elliptic flow azimuthal anisotropy with magnitudes around 0.05. The results are compared with the results obtained by the many data sub-sets, and by the bootstrapping method. The first one is a common way of estimation of the statistical uncertainties of the $v_{n}\{2k\}$ harmonics in a real experiment. In order to increase precision in the measurement of the $v_{n}\{2k\}$ harmonics, a large number of 15000 sub-sets and the same number of the re-sampling in the bootstrap method is used. A very good agreement in the comparison with the proposed way of the analytic calculation of the $v_{n}\{2k\}$ statistical uncertainties is found. Additionally, a recurrence equation for Q-cumulants calculation is also presented.

## Key claim

Analytic formulas for the statistical uncertainties of \(v_{n}\{2k\}\) harmonics from Q-cumulants are presented, but the key condition is that the best agreement with data sub-set and bootstrap methods is achieved only when the Cochran-based variance and covariance formulas (which include squared weights) are used, rather than the previously proposed Bilandzic or Galloway formulas.

## Method

The authors derived analytic expressions for the statistical uncertainties of \(v_{n}\{2k\}\) harmonics by propagating variances and covariances of the weighted means of \(2m\)-particle azimuthal correlations \(\llangle 2m \rrangle\) through the Q-cumulant formulas. They tested these analytic formulas using a toy model that simulates elliptic flow with a Bessel-Gaussian distribution of \(v_2\) (mean 5%, \(\sigma_v = 3\mu/4.4\)) and uniform multiplicity from 300 to 900, comparing the analytic results to those from 15000 data sub-sets and 15000 bootstrap resamples. A recurrence relation for Q-cumulants of arbitrary order was also derived using Faà di Bruno's formula.

## Key numbers

- Mean \(v_2\) magnitude of 5% used in the toy model, with flow fluctuation \(\sigma_v = 3\mu/4.4\).
- 15000 data sub-sets and 15000 bootstrap resamples were used for comparison.
- Multiplicity varied uniformly from 300 to 900 to simulate lower-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions at NICA.
- From 15000 simulations of 10000 events each: \(\llangle 2 \rrangle\) gave \(v_2 = 0.069456 \pm 0.000004\); \(\llangle 4 \rrangle\) gave \(v_2 = 0.04994 \pm 0.00002\); \(\llangle 6 \rrangle\) and \(\llangle 8 \rrangle\) both gave \(v_2 = 0.04980 \pm 0.00002\).
- For \(k=4\) (8-particle cumulant), using the Bilandzic variance formula produced statistical uncertainties nearly four times larger than the simulation results.

## Context

This work extends the Q-cumulant method introduced by Bilandzic (2010) for measuring azimuthal anisotropy harmonics in heavy-ion collisions. It addresses the challenge of calculating statistical uncertainties analytically rather than relying on computationally intensive data sub-set or bootstrap methods. The paper shows that the Cochran-based variance formula (Cochran, 1977) and the corresponding covariance formula give results in excellent agreement with simulations, correcting earlier approaches by Bilandzic (2012) and Galloway (1984) that produced biased uncertainties. This enables efficient, simultaneous calculation of both \(v_n\{2k\}\) harmonics and their statistical uncertainties in experimental analyses.
