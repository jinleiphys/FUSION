# Spin asymmetries for confined Dirac particles

> We study the Bjorken `x' (or equivalently Nachtmann `xi') dependence of the virtual photon spin asymmetry in polarized deep inelastic scattering of electrons from hadrons. We use an exactly solved relativistic potential model of the hadron, treating the constituents as independent massless Dirac particles confined to an infinitely massive force center. The importance of including the p-wave components of the Dirac wave function is demonstrated. Comparisons are made to the observed data on the proton by taking into account the observed flavor dependence of the valence quark distribution functions.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the x-dependence of the proton's virtual photon spin asymmetry A₁ can be largely explained by two effects within a simple relativistic model: the interference between s-wave and p-wave components of the confined Dirac quark wave function suppresses the asymmetry at small x, while the flavor dependence of the valence quark distributions enhances it at large x. The model neglects sea quarks and gluons, and the authors hedge that the single-quark confinement model is "certainly too simple" but that the p-wave effects "will presumably exist in more refined treatments."

## Method

The authors model a hadron as a single massless Dirac quark confined by a linear potential with a half-vector plus half-scalar structure (string tension 1 GeV/fm), treating the confining center as infinitely massive. They compute the virtual photon helicity cross sections σ₁/₂ and σ₃/₂ exactly from the eigenstates of this Hamiltonian, then extract the single-quark asymmetry A₁^q = g₁^q/F₁^q as a function of the scaling variable y = q - ν (related to the Nachtmann variable ξ). To obtain the proton asymmetry, they combine this calculated A₁^q with empirical valence quark distributions u(ξ) and d(ξ) from the MRST next-to-leading order fit at Q² = 5 GeV², using the proton spin-flavor wave function to relate the polarized and unpolarized parton distribution functions.

## Key numbers

- String tension √σ = 1 GeV/fm, chosen to match baryon Regge trajectory slopes.
- Ground state energy E₀ = 840 MeV for this string tension.
- Target mass M_T = 2.5 GeV ≈ 3E₀ used to convert the scaling variable y to ξ.
- The ratio g₁^p/F₁^p is computed using MRST PDFs evolved to Q² = 5 GeV² at next-to-leading order, and is "fairly insensitive to Q² in the range 2 < Q² < 20 GeV²."
- In the SU(6) limit with u(ξ) = 2d(ξ) and A₁^q = 1, the result reduces to the Kuti-Weisskopf value g₁^p/F₁^p = 5/9, which is ξ-independent.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic quark model approach to spin structure functions, following earlier calculations with the MIT bag model by Jaffe (1990) and light-cone quantization by Brodsky (1994), which showed that lower Dirac components reduce the quark spin contribution by a factor of about 0.65 (Jaffe and Manohar, 1990). The model is based on the exactly solvable Hamiltonian of Paris (2003) and addresses the "spin crisis", the experimental observation that the integral of g₁(x) is much smaller than the naive quark model prediction (Filippone, 2001; Anselmino, 1995; Hughes, 1999). The results enable a systematic understanding of how relativistic p-wave components and flavor-dependent diquark correlations shape the x-dependence of the proton spin asymmetry, and provide a baseline for more complete treatments including sea quarks and QCD evolution.
