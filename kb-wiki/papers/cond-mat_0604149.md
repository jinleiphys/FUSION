# BCS-BEC Crossover in Atomic Fermi Gases with a Narrow Resonance

> We determine the effects on the BCS-BEC crossover of the energy dependence of the effective two-body interaction, which at low energies is determined by the effective range. To describe interactions with an effective range of either sign, we consider a single-channel model with a two-body interaction having an attractive square well and a repulsive square barrier. We investigate the two-body scattering properties of the model, and then solve the Eagles-Leggett equations for the zero temperature crossover, determining the momentum dependent gap and the chemical potential self-consistently. From this we investigate the dependence of the crossover on the effective range of the interaction.

## Key claim

The paper investigates how the energy dependence of the effective two-body interaction, parameterized by the effective range \(r_e\), modifies the BCS-BEC crossover in atomic Fermi gases, finding that negative effective ranges produce qualitatively different behavior in the momentum-dependent gap and chemical potential compared to the universal single-channel model where only the scattering length matters.

## Method

The authors model the interatomic interaction using a single-channel square-well plus square-barrier potential, which can produce both positive and negative effective ranges. They solve the two-body scattering problem analytically to obtain the scattering length and effective range, then numerically solve the Eagles-Leggett equations at zero temperature for the momentum-dependent gap and chemical potential self-consistently, using the s-wave projection of the potential in momentum space.

## Key numbers

- The effective range at resonance \(r_{e0}\) can be tuned from positive to negative values; for a square-well potential alone \(r_{e0} = r_0\), while adding a barrier yields values such as \(r_{e0}/r_0 \approx 0.78, 0.12, -0.23, -0.66, -1.20\) for barrier widths \((r_1 - r_0)/r_0 = 0.1, 0.25, 0.30, 0.35, 0.40\) respectively (with \(k_1 r_0 = 2.0\))
- The density is set to \(n = 0.001 r_0^{-3}\), giving \(k_{\mathrm{F}} r_0 \simeq 0.31\)
- Numerical calculations use a momentum grid spacing \(\Delta k = k_{\mathrm{F}}/15\) and maximum momentum \(k_{\mathrm{max}} = 2500 \Delta k \simeq 50 r_0^{-1}\)
- For large barrier widths, the scattering length behaves as \(a \simeq r_1 - 1/k_1\) and the effective range as \(r_e \sim (2/3) r_1\)

## Context

This work extends the Eagles-Leggett model (Eagles 1969, Leggett 1980) beyond the zero-range approximation by including effective range corrections relevant for narrow Feshbach resonances. It builds on prior studies using coupled square wells (Kokkelmans 2002), the modified Pöschl-Teller potential (Carlson 2003), and Gaussian potentials (Andrenacci 1999, Parish 2005), and directly continues the square-well plus square-barrier approach of Palo (2004) and Diener (2004). The results provide a guide for future experiments on the BCS-BEC crossover with narrow resonances, where the effective range can be negative and strongly momentum-dependent.
