# Nuclear Structure and Response based on Correlated Realistic NN Interactions

> Starting from the Argonne V18 nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction and using the Unitary Correlation Operator Method, a correlated interaction v_UCOM has been constructed, which is suitable for calculations within restricted Hilbert spaces. In this work we employ the v_UCOM in Hartree-Fock, perturbation-theory and RPA calculations and we study the ground-state properties of various closed-shell nuclei, as well as some excited states. The present calculations provide also important feedback for the optimization of the v_UCOM and valuable information on its properties. The above scheme offers the prospect of ab initio calculations in nuclei, regardless of their mass number. It can be used in conjunction with other realistic NN interactions as well, and with various many-body methods (Second RPA, QRPA, Shell Model, etc.).

## Key claim

The Unitary Correlation Operator Method (UCOM), applied to the Argonne V18 nucleon-nucleon interaction, produces a correlated interaction v_UCOM that can be used in Hartree-Fock, perturbation-theory, and RPA calculations for closed-shell nuclei across the mass table, but the optimal separation of short-range and long-range correlations remains an open task, and three-body interactions are not yet included.

## Method

The authors start from the Argonne V18 nucleon-nucleon interaction and apply the Unitary Correlation Operator Method to construct a correlated interaction v_UCOM, with correlation functions determined for each spin-isospin channel by minimizing the two-nucleon system energy. They then use this interaction in spherical Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations, second-order perturbation theory (HF+PT) on the HF basis, self-consistent RPA (HF+RPA), and extended RPA (ERPA) to study ground-state properties and collective excitations of closed-shell nuclei. The two-body matrix elements of v_UCOM, Coulomb interaction, and intrinsic kinetic energy are calculated in a harmonic-oscillator basis with maximum quantum numbers N_max=12 and l_max=10.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per nucleon for various closed-shell nuclei is shown in HF and HF+PT calculations, with HF+PT bringing results close to experimental data (Figure 1).
- The tensor correlation volume used for results in Figures 1 and 2 is I_theta^(S=1,T=0) = 0.09 fm^3, which best reproduces the binding energy of ^4He and ^3H in exact no-core shell model calculations.
- The isoscalar giant monopole resonance of ^40Ca is shown in HF+RPA and ERPA calculations, with an arrow indicating the experimental centroid (Figure 2).
- Occupation numbers n_i = <a^dagger_i a_i> have been calculated within ERPA for ^16O and ^40Ca, showing small depletion of the Fermi sea reflecting ground-state long-range correlations.

## Context

This work extends the UCOM approach, previously applied in Fermionic Molecular Dynamics calculations for nuclei up to A≈60 by Roth et al. (2004), to heavier systems using HF- and RPA-based models without restricting mass number. The results provide feedback for optimizing the v_UCOM interaction and enable future applications with other many-body methods such as Second RPA, QRPA, and the Shell Model, as well as with other realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions.
