# Asymmetric longitudinal flow decorrelations in proton-nucleus collisions

> We perform the first study on asymmetric longitudinal decorrelations of elliptic, triangular and quadrangular flows in proton-nucleus collisions at the LHC and RHIC energies. To measure the longitudinal flow decorrelations for asymmetric collision systems, we propose a new set of rapidity-asymmetric flow decorrelation functions. Our event-by-event hydrodynamic calculations show that the flow decorrelations in proton-going direction are larger than those in nucleus-going direction. We also find that proton-nucleus collisions at RHIC have larger longitudinal flow decorrelation effects than those at the LHC. Our study opens a new window to probe the longitudinal properties and the origin of flows in relativistic nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in proton-nucleus collisions, longitudinal flow decorrelations are larger in the proton-going direction than in the nucleus-going direction, and that these effects are larger at RHIC energies than at LHC energies. The hedge is that these results are obtained from event-by-event (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic simulations with initial conditions from the AMPT model, and the claim is conditional on the specific model parameters and the newly proposed rapidity-asymmetric decorrelation functions.

## Method

The authors performed (3+1)-dimensional event-by-event relativistic hydrodynamic simulations using the CLVisc model, with initial conditions provided by the AMPT model with string melting. They proposed a new set of rapidity-asymmetric flow decorrelation functions \(w_n^{(+)}(\eta|\eta_{\rm r})\) and \(w_n^{(-)}(\eta|\eta_{\rm r})\) to measure longitudinal decorrelations separately in the forward and backward rapidity directions. The slope parameters \(g_n^{(\pm)}\) and \(g_n\) were extracted from the nearly linear dependence of these functions on pseudorapidity \(\eta\) in the range \(0<\eta<2.4\).

## Key numbers

- For 0-10% p-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 5.02\) TeV, the forward-backward asymmetry for longitudinal flow decorrelations (difference between \(g_n^{(+)}\) and \(g_n^{(-)}\)) can reach about 40%.
- For 0-10% p-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200\) GeV, the forward-backward asymmetry for longitudinal flow decorrelations can reach about 15%.
- The initial proper time was taken as \(\tau_0 = 0.2\) fm/c for p-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 5.02\) TeV and \(\tau_0 = 0.4\) fm/c for p-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200\) GeV.
- The specific shear viscosity was set to \(\eta/s = 0.16\) for p-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 5.02\) TeV and \(\eta/s = 0.08\) for p-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200\) GeV.
- The freezeout temperature was taken as \(T_f = 137\) MeV.
- For each centrality class, 2000 hydrodynamic events were run for analysis.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of longitudinal flow decorrelations in symmetric systems (Pb+Pb, Au+Au) by the CMS Collaboration (2015) and ATLAS Collaboration (2017) to the asymmetric proton-nucleus system. It builds on prior hydrodynamic studies of longitudinal fluctuations by Pang (2015) and Wu (2018) in AA collisions. The new rapidity-asymmetric decorrelation functions enable future experimental tests at the LHC and RHIC, and the authors suggest applications to other asymmetric systems such as deuteron-nucleus and \(^3\)He-nucleus collisions, as well as comparisons with Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework calculations.
