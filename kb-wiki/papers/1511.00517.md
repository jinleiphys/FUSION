# Gauge-Invariant Gluon TMD and Evolution in the Coordinate Space

> Maximally path-dependent gauge-invariant operator definition of the gluon transverse-momentum dependent pdf (gTMD) is discussed. It is argued that the evolution equations for the gTMD in the coordinate representation can be derived from the equations of motion in the generalised loop space, whose elements are the hadronic averages of the Wilson loops of entirely arbitrary shape.

# Gauge-Invariant Gluon TMD and Evolution in the Coordinate Space

## Key claim
The paper proposes that evolution equations for the gluon transverse-momentum dependent parton distribution function (gTMD) in coordinate representation can be derived from the Makeenko-Migdal loop equations in a generalized loop space, but only if one first constructs a maximally path-dependent, fully gauge-invariant Wilson-loop object *before* imposing any specific factorisation scheme. The approach is presented as a framework rather than a fully worked-out non-Abelian solution, with the explicit demonstration limited to an Abelian example.

## Method
The author constructs a generic gauge-invariant gluon correlator using Wilson loops defined on arbitrary paths, then applies the non-Abelian Stokes theorem and Mandelstam formula to express the standard gTMD operator definition as area derivatives of hadronic matrix elements of Wilson loops. The shape evolution of these Wilson loops is formulated via the Makeenko-Migdal loop equations, which relate area and path derivatives of the loop-space elements. The method is illustrated for an Abelian gauge theory where exponentiation holds, yielding a modified evolution equation that includes hadron momentum-dependent terms absent in the vacuum case.

## Key numbers
- The Makeenko-Migdal loop equation in the large-\(N_c\) limit is given as: \(\partial^\nu \frac{\delta}{\delta \sigma_{\mu\nu}(x)} \langle 0| \mathcal{W}^1_\gamma |0\rangle = N_c g^2 \oint_\gamma dz^\mu \delta^{(4)}(x-z) \langle 0 | \mathcal{W}^2_{\gamma_{xz} \gamma_{zx}} | 0 \rangle\)
- In the Abelian example, the vacuum Makeenko-Migdal equation at leading order yields: \(\partial_\mu^z \frac{\delta}{\delta \sigma_{\mu\nu}(z)} \langle 0 | \mathcal{W}_\gamma | 0 \rangle = g^2 \oint_\gamma d\zeta^\nu \delta^{(4)}(z-\zeta)\)
- The 2D area law for vacuum Wilson loops is: \(\langle 0 | \mathcal{W}_\gamma | 0 \rangle = \exp\left[-\frac{g^2}{2} S_\gamma\right]\)
- The hadronic Wilson loop in the Abelian case exponentiates as: \(\langle h | \mathcal{W}_\gamma | h \rangle = \exp\left[-\frac{g^2}{2} \oint_\gamma d\zeta_\rho \oint_\gamma d\zeta'_{\rho'} D^{\rho\rho'}(\zeta-\zeta')\right]\)

## Context
This work extends the Makeenko-Migdal loop-space formalism (Makeenko and Migdal, 1979; Makeenko, 2002) from vacuum matrix elements to hadronic matrix elements, addressing the gluon TMD definition problem where gauge-link path-dependence complicates universality (Bomhof and Mulders, 2006; Bomhof et al., 2007). The approach contrasts with standard factorisation-based definitions (Collins, 1981; Collins, 2011) by starting from arbitrary Wilson loops and then evolving them to the specific geometry required by a factorisation scheme. The Abelian example shows how the loop equations acquire hadron momentum-dependent terms (\(D_4\)) absent in vacuum, indicating that the standard area law does not hold for hadronic Wilson loops. The work enables a systematic method to connect arbitrary Wilson-loop definitions to process-specific gTMDs, with further development promised in a subsequent publication (Cherednikov, Mertens, and Taels, 2016).
