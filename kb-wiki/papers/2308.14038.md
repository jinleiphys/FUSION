# Momentum dependence of $ϕ$ meson's spin alignment

> We study the rapidity and azimuthal angle dependences of the global spin alignment $ρ_{00}$ for $ϕ$ mesons with respect to the reaction plane in Au+Au collisions at RHIC by the relativistic coalescence model in the spin transport theory. The global spin alignment of $ϕ$ mesons arises from local fluctuations of strong force fields whose values are extracted from the STAR's data. The calculated results show that $ρ_{00}<1/3$ at the rapidity $Y=0$, and then it increases with rapidity and becomes $ρ_{00}>1/3$ at $Y=1$. Such a rapidity dependence is dominated by the relative motion of the $ϕ$ meson in the bulk matter. We also give prediction for the azimuthal angle dependence of $ρ_{00}$ at different rapidities.

## Key claim

The rapidity dependence of the phi meson's spin alignment ρ00 is dominated by the isotropic part of local strong-force field fluctuations, with the meson's motion relative to the bulk matter breaking rotational symmetry; the anisotropy parameter Δ in the lab frame contributes only at the one-order-of-magnitude smaller level.

## Method

The authors use a relativistic coalescence model based on the spin Boltzmann equation in transport theory to compute ρ00 for phi mesons in Au+Au collisions at RHIC. They assume the strong-force field fluctuations in the lab frame are nearly isotropic (parameterized by an isotropic part F² and a small anisotropy Δ along the beam direction), then Lorentz-transform these fields into the meson's rest frame to obtain the spin alignment. The parameters F² and Δ are extracted by fitting STAR data on momentum-integrated ρ00 at six collision energies from 11.5 to 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- At mid-rapidity Y=0, ρ00 < 1/3; at Y=1, ρ00 > 1/3, for all collision energies studied (11.5–200 GeV).
- Extracted fluctuation parameters at 200 GeV: F²/mπ² = 0.359, Δ/mπ² = 0.128.
- Extracted fluctuation parameters at 11.5 GeV: F²/mπ² = 16.5, Δ/mπ² = 3.33.
- The contribution from the anisotropy parameter Δ to ρ00 is one order of magnitude smaller than ρ00 − 1/3.
- The analytical expression for the deviation δρ00^y is proportional to (p_x² + p_z²)/2 − p_y².
- The azimuthal angle dependence shows a cos(2φ) structure, with δρ00^y positive at φ=0 and negative at φ=π/2 for Y=0 at p_T=2 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic coalescence model developed by Sheng, Pu, and Wang (2022) to predict the momentum dependence of phi meson spin alignment, following the STAR collaboration's measurement (2022) showing a significant positive deviation from 1/3. The mechanism of local fluctuations of the strong force field (the phi field) is the only one that quantitatively explains the STAR data, unlike conventional mechanisms such as vorticity fields and magnetic fields (Liang and Wang, 2005; Yang et al., 2017; Xia et al., 2020). The predictions for rapidity and azimuthal angle dependence can be tested in future experiments.
