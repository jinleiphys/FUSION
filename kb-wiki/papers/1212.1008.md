# Event-by-event distributions of azimuthal asymmetries in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics is a common tool to describe the space-time evolution of the strongly interacting matter created in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. For a proper comparison to experimental data, fluid-dynamical calculations have to be performed on an event-by-event basis. Therefore, fluid dynamics should be able to reproduce, not only the event-averaged momentum anisotropies, $<v_{n}>$, but also their distributions. In this paper, we investigate the event-by-event distributions of the initial-state and momentum anisotropies $ε_n$ and $v_n$, and their correlations. We demonstrate that the event-by-event distributions of relative $v_n$ fluctuations are almost equal to the event-by-event distributions of corresponding $ε_n$ fluctuations, allowing experimental determination of the relative anisotropy fluctuations of the initial state. Furthermore, the correlation $c(v_2,v_4)$ turns out to be sensitive to the viscosity of the fluid providing an additional constraint to the properties of the strongly interacting matter.

## Key claim

The event-by-event distributions of relative fluctuations of the flow harmonics \(v_n\) are nearly identical to the corresponding distributions of the initial-state anisotropy fluctuations \(\epsilon_n\) for \(n=2,3,4\), even when \(v_4\) is not linearly correlated with \(\epsilon_4\); this allows experimental extraction of initial-state anisotropy fluctuations from measured \(v_n\) distributions, provided that fluid dynamics provides a correct description of heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics simulations of Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) A GeV using a Monte-Carlo Glauber model to generate initial entropy density profiles from either wounded nucleons (sWN) or binary collisions (sBC). The fluid evolution was computed with a shear viscosity to entropy density ratio \(\eta/s\) of either 0 or 0.16, using the Israel-Stewart transient fluid dynamics equations with a lattice QCD-based equation of state, and final hadron spectra were obtained via the Cooper-Frye freeze-out procedure at \(T_f=100\) MeV including resonance decays. For each centrality class, 2000 events were generated and the Fourier coefficients \(v_n\) and initial anisotropies \(\epsilon_n\) were calculated event-by-event.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratios used: \(\eta/s = 0\) and \(\eta/s = 0.16\).
- For \(n=2\) in the 20-30% centrality class, \(v_2\) deviates only \(\sim 10\%\) from a linear relation \(v_2 = C_2 \epsilon_2\) at a given \(\epsilon_2\).
- For \(n=3\) in the 20-30% centrality class, \(v_3\) deviates \(\sim 50\%\) from a linear relation \(v_3 = C_3 \epsilon_3\) at a given \(\epsilon_3\).
- Linear correlation coefficient \(c(\epsilon_2^2, v_4)\) in the 20-30% centrality class: 0.40 (sBC, \(\eta/s=0\)), 0.69 (sBC, \(\eta/s=0.16\)), and 0.46 (sWN, \(\eta/s=0.16\)).
- In the 0-5% centrality class, the linear correlation coefficient \(c(\epsilon_4, v_4)\) can be as large as \(\sim 0.81\) for sWN initialization with \(\eta/s=0.16\).
- Linear correlation coefficients for \(p_T\)-integrated \(v_n\) in the 20-30% centrality class: \(c(v_2, v_3) \sim -0.11\) for both sBC cases; \(c(v_2, v_4) = 0.32\) (sBC, \(\eta/s=0\)), 0.63 (sBC, \(\eta/s=0.16\)), and 0.42 (sWN, \(\eta/s=0.16\)); \(c(v_3, v_4) \sim -0.11\) for sBC cases.

## Context

This work extends the event-by-event fluid dynamics approach pioneered by Kodama et al. (2006) and Alver and Roland (2010), who showed that triangular flow \(v_3\) arises from event-by-event fluctuations of the initial geometry. The authors build on earlier studies of event-averaged anisotropies by Kolb et al. (2003) and Song et al. (2008), and on the first fluid-dynamical calculations of \(v_n\) distributions by Gale et al. (2011). The key finding that \(P(\delta v_n) \simeq P(\delta \epsilon_n)\) enables direct experimental comparison to initial condition models such as Monte-Carlo Glauber or Color-Glass Condensate based approaches, while the sensitivity of \(c(v_2, v_4)\) to viscosity provides an additional constraint for extracting the shear viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma from data.
