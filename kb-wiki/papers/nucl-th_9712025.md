# Bremsstrahlung in intermediate-energy nucleon reactions within an effective one-boson exchange model

> Within a covariant effective one-boson exchange model for the $T$ matrix of $NN$ interactions we present detailed calculations of bremsstrahlung cross sections for proton - proton and proton - neutron reactions at beam energies in the 1 GeV region. Besides pure bremsstrahlung processes we consider photons from $Δ$ decays and contributions from the $η\to γγ$ process. At beam energies above 700 MeV the $Δ$ decay channel dominates the spectra at large photon energies, where the interference between non-resonance processes and the $Δ$ decay channel becomes also important. Low energy photons stem from pure bremsstrahlung processes. The available experimental data at 730 MeV beam energy is well described. We extrapolate the model down to 280 MeV, where more detailed experimental data exist, and find agreement with angular distributions.

# Bremsstrahlung in intermediate-energy nucleon reactions within an effective one-boson exchange model

## Key claim

The effective one-boson exchange (OBE) model, with parameters adjusted to elastic NN scattering at higher energies, can describe bremsstrahlung cross sections for pp and pn reactions from 2 GeV down to the pion production threshold (280 MeV), but the model's applicability at low energies is not obvious and the authors note that more elaborate potential models are usually considered adequate in that region.

## Method

The authors performed diagrammatic calculations of bremsstrahlung processes on the tree level using a covariant effective OBE model with four exchanged mesons (pi, rho, omega, sigma) for the description of two-body pp and pn T matrices. They included pure bremsstrahlung diagrams with pre-emission and post-emission of photons, internal radiative meson conversions (V to pi gamma), contributions from Delta(1232) excitation and decay, and eta to gamma gamma decay contributions via N1535 resonance excitation. The strong NNM vertices were dressed by monopole form factors, and energy-dependent parametrizations of the elastic scattering were used where effective coupling constants change with beam energy.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies considered: 280 MeV, 700 MeV, 730 MeV, 1.0 GeV, 1.35 GeV, and 1.7 GeV
- Delta resonance mass: M_Delta = 1.232 GeV with total width Gamma_Delta^0 = 0.12 GeV
- Delta to N gamma branching ratio: 6 x 10^-2
- Delta decay vertex parameter G_Delta = 2.18
- Anomalous magnetic moments: kappa_N = 1.79 for protons and -1.91 for neutrons
- Rho tensor coupling strength: kappa_rho = 6
- Coupling constants for V to pi gamma decays: g^2_omega-pi-gamma / (4 pi) = 3.94 x 10^-2 GeV^-2, g^2_rho0-pi0-gamma / (4 pi) = 0.692 x 10^-2 GeV^-2, g^2_rho+-pi+-gamma / (4 pi) = 0.396 x 10^-2 GeV^-2
- Eta to gamma gamma branching ratio: 0.39
- N1535 resonance mass: M* = 1535 MeV with width Gamma* = 180 MeV
- Delta cut-off parameter: Lambda_Delta = 0.7 GeV
- At beam energies above 700 MeV, the Delta decay channel dominates spectra at large photon energies (above omega/omega_max = 0.4 - 0.5)
- The model describes experimental data at 730 MeV beam energy for pp to pp gamma
- At 280 MeV, the OBE model describes the structure of angular distribution data satisfactorily without fine tuning of parameters

## Context

This work extends earlier investigations by Shklyar et al. (previous study) and is conceptually close to the model of Mosel et al. (1990s) for di-electron production, but here the authors consider real photon angular and energy distributions instead of inclusive invariant mass distributions of di-electrons. The study goes beyond Jetter et al. (1990s) by including sigma and omega meson exchange and radiation from internal lines with vector mesons in V-pi-gamma vertices. The work addresses controversial explanations of virtual photon production mechanisms at 1-1.5 GeV beam energy, where either subtle interferences between non-resonance and resonance channels or strong contributions of eta Dalitz decays can explain the non-trivial beam energy dependence of cross sections. The results enable predictions for upcoming COSY-TOF measurements and suggest that both eta decay and strong interference of resonance and non-resonance bremsstrahlung must be taken into account for understanding inclusive real and virtual photon spectra, especially for photon energies close to the kinematical limit.
