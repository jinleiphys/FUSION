# Slow cross-symmetry phase relaxation in complex collisions

> We discuss the effect of slow phase relaxation and the spin off-diagonal $S$-matrix correlations on the cross section energy oscillations and the time evolution of the highly excited intermediate systems formed in complex collisions. Such deformed intermediate complexes with strongly overlapping resonances can be formed in heavy ion collisions, bimolecular chemical reactions and atomic cluster collisions. The effects of quasiperiodic energy dependence of the cross sections, coherent rotation of the hyperdeformed $\simeq (3:1)$ intermediate complex, Schrödinger cat states and quantum-classical transition are studied for $^{24}$Mg+$^{28}$Si heavy ion scattering.

# Slow cross-symmetry phase relaxation in complex collisions

## Key claim
The paper argues that anomalously long-lived spin off-diagonal S-matrix correlations, implying a slow phase relaxation many orders of magnitude longer than energy relaxation, persist in the highly excited intermediate complex formed in ²⁴Mg+²⁸Si scattering even though the system is in the regime of strongly overlapping resonances (Γ/D ≥ 10⁵) where conventional quantum chaotic scattering theory predicts no such correlations.

## Method
The authors measured excitation functions for ²⁴Mg+²⁸Si elastic and inelastic scattering over the center-of-mass energy range 49–57 MeV, averaging over an angular range of θ_cm = 77°–98°. They constructed the cross section energy autocorrelation function C(ε) from these data and fitted it using a theoretical expression that incorporates spin off-diagonal S-matrix correlations, a coherent rotation angular velocity ω, a spin phase relaxation width β, and a total decay width Γ. They also computed the time power spectrum P(t,θ) of the collision to study the time evolution of the intermediate complex.

## Key numbers
- The experimental C(ε) oscillates with a period of approximately 0.75 MeV.
- The best fit to all elastic and inelastic channels yields Γ = 0.15 MeV, β = 0.1 MeV, ħω = 0.75 MeV, and d = 5.
- For a (2:1) superdeformed complex of two touching spherical ²⁴Mg and ²⁸Si nuclei, the expected ħω is approximately 1.9 MeV, which is about 2.5 times larger than the observed oscillation period, indicating a (3:1) hyperdeformed complex.
- The intrinsic excitation energy of the intermediate complex is about 15 MeV or more, corresponding to an average level spacing D ≤ 10⁻⁶ MeV, giving Γ/D ≥ 10⁵.
- An alternative fit uses the same Γ = 0.15 MeV and ħω = 0.75 MeV but with β = 0.03 MeV and d = 1.
- The average spin of the intermediate complex is estimated as J ≈ 34–38.

## Context
This work extends earlier findings of slow phase relaxation and stable coherent rotation in heavy ion collisions such as ¹⁹F+⁸⁹Y (Kun 1993), ²⁸Si+⁶⁴Ni, ¹²C+²⁴Mg, ²⁴Mg+²⁴Mg, and ²⁸Si+²⁸Si (Kun 1994), as well as in state-to-state chemical reactions like H+D₂, F+HD, and He+H₂⁺. The results contradict the standard predictions of quantum chaotic scattering and random matrix theory (Ericson 1960), which assume vanishing spin off-diagonal S-matrix correlations and yield a Lorentzian C(ε) = 1/[1+(ε/Γ)²]. The extracted parameters enable future experimental determination of the phase relaxation width β from fine-resolution excitation function data, which is relevant for understanding phase memory in many-qubit quantum computation.
