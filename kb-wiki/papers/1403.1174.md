# Geometrical Scaling of Direct-Photon Production in Hadron Collisions from RHIC to the LHC

> We consider pp, dAu and AuAu production of photons at RHIC energies, and PbPb collisions at LHC energy. We show that the inclusive spectrum of photons in the transverse momentum range of 1 GeV < pT <= 4 GeV satisfies geometric scaling. Geometric scaling is a property of hadronic interactions predicted by theories of gluon saturation, and expresses rates in terms of dimensionless ratios of the transverse momentum to saturation momentum. We show excellent agreement with geometric scaling with the only input being the previously measured dependence of the saturation momentum upon Bjorken x and centrality.

## Key claim

Direct-photon production in hadron collisions from RHIC to LHC energies satisfies geometric scaling in the transverse momentum range 1 GeV < pT ≤ 4 GeV, with the only free parameter being the previously measured dependence of the saturation momentum upon Bjorken x and centrality; the authors note this is remarkable because geometric scaling is a property of particle emission that ignores final state interactions, yet photons in heavy-ion collisions are expected to arise from quarks and gluons that have undergone thermalization.

## Method

The authors test geometric scaling by rescaling the invariant yields of direct photons measured in pp, dAu, and AuAu collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV (RHIC) and PbPb collisions at √sNN = 2760 GeV (LHC) using a power-law parameterization of the photon distribution with slope n = 6.1. For symmetric systems, the scaling factor depends on Npart and center-of-mass energy E via the saturation momentum parameterization Qsat² = Q0² · Npart^(1/3) · (E/pT)^δ with δ = 0.25; for asymmetric dAu collisions, a modified scaling relation is derived using the geometric mean of the saturation scales of the two partners and the average number of participants from the deuteron.

## Key numbers

- The transverse momentum range studied is 1 GeV < pT ≤ 4 GeV/c
- The power-law slopes extracted from data for different systems range between approximately 5.2 and 6.9; a default slope of n = 6.1 is used
- The saturation momentum parameter δ is in the range 0.22 to 0.28, with a default value of δ = 0.25
- The invariant yield changes with Npart as Npart^(1.43 - 1.70); using default values n = 6.1 and δ = 0.25 gives Npart^1.57
- The measured centrality dependence of integrated direct-photon yields in AuAu collisions at RHIC varies with Npart with power 1.48 ± 0.08 (stat) ± 0.04 (syst)
- For dAu collisions, the average number of participants from the deuteron is ⟨Npart[d]⟩ = 1.62 ± 0.01
- The Npart values used: pp = 2, dAu (Npart from deuteron) = 1.6, AuAu (0-20%) = 280, PbPb (0-40%) = 233, PbPb (0-13%) at 17.3 GeV = 322.5

## Context

This work extends earlier studies where geometric scaling was applied to reproduce the multiplicity dependence of photon production data for AuAu collisions at RHIC by Chiu (2012), and builds on the theoretical framework of gluon saturation and geometric scaling first discovered in deep-inelastic scattering by Stasto (2000) and Iancu (2002). The results enable further investigation into the apparent mystery of how geometric scaling can be maintained in the presence of transverse flow, which is conventionally associated with system-size-dependent expansion; the authors suggest this may require explicit computation comparing early-time glasma emission with thermalized quark-gluon plasma production.
