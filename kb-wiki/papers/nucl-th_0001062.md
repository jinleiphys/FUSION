# Meson Particle-Hole Dynamics

> Particle-hole modes with quantum numbers of pions and negative kaons can propagate in nuclear matter. We discuss possible manifestations of these modes in experiments on heavy-ion collisions and on neutrino-nucleus scattering. Calculations of reaction rates in medium can be harmed by double counting, which arises because the very same quantum numbers can be carried in medium as by single particle excitations as by multi-particle ones. We argue that the optical theorem written in terms of the non-equilibrium Green's functions provides a convenient formalism (closed diagram technique) void of double counting.

## Key claim

Particle-hole modes carrying mesonic quantum numbers (pions and negative kaons) can propagate in nuclear matter, and their effects on reaction rates can be calculated without double counting by using the optical theorem formulated with non-equilibrium Green's functions (closed diagram technique), rather than the standard Feynman diagram approach which double-counts when full in-medium propagators and vertices are used.

## Method

The authors decompose the in-medium meson polarization operator into contributions from baryon-nucleon-hole states (e.g., nucleon-nucleon-hole and Delta-nucleon-hole for pions; Lambda-proton-hole for kaons), a regular part parameterized from empirical data, and meson-meson interactions. They compute the in-medium spectral density for pions and kaons in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature, then apply a breakup model for heavy-ion collisions and a direct reaction formalism for antineutrino-nucleus scattering, using the closed diagram technique based on the Schwinger-Kadanoff-Baym-Keldysh non-equilibrium Green's function formalism to avoid double counting.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear matter density: rho_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}
- For pions at saturation density: beta approx 0.7; effective pion gap tilde{omega}_c(rho_0) approx 0.8 m_pi
- Critical density for pion condensation: rho_c approx (2-3) rho_0
- For kaons at saturation density: net attractive potential approx 100 MeV
- Breakup density in heavy-ion collisions: rho_b approx (0.5-0.7) rho_0; breakup time tau_b approx 1/m_pi
- For K- production in Ni+Ni at 1.8 GeV per nucleon: calculations with free kaon spectrum underestimate experiment by factor 2
- Antineutrino beam energy considered for kaon production: 1 GeV

## Context

This work extends the Migdal theory of finite Fermi systems (Migdal, various years) to meson particle-hole dynamics in nuclear matter, building on prior treatments for pions (Voskresensky, 1989; Voskresensky et al., 1995) and kaons (Kolomeitsev et al., various years). The closed diagram formalism based on non-equilibrium Green's functions (Schwinger-Kadanoff-Baym-Keldysh) is argued to resolve double-counting problems that arise when standard Feynman diagram techniques are applied to in-medium reactions. The results enable calculations of pion and kaon yields in heavy-ion collisions and predictions for antineutrino-nucleus scattering experiments that directly probe in-medium spectral densities.
