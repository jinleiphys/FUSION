# Nucleon-nucleon interactions from dispersion relations: Elastic partial waves

> We consider nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions from chiral effective field theory. In this work we restrict ourselves to the elastic NN scattering. We apply the N/D method to calculate the NN partial waves taking as input the one-pion exchange discontinuity along the left-hand cut. This discontinuity is amenable to a chiral power counting as discussed by Lacour, Oller and Meissner [Ann. Phys. (NY) 326, 241 (2011)], with one-pion exchange as its leading order contribution. The resulting linear integral equation for a partial wave with orbital angular momentum L>=2 is solved in the presence of L-1 constraints, so as to guarantee the right behavior of the D and higher partial waves near threshold. The calculated NN partial waves are based on dispersion relations and are independent of regulator. This method can also be applied to higher orders in the calculation of the discontinuity along the left-hand cut and extended to triplet coupled partial waves.

## Key claim

The authors apply the N/D method to nucleon-nucleon (NN) scattering from chiral effective field theory, using only the one-pion exchange (OPE) discontinuity along the left-hand cut as input at leading order, and demonstrate that the resulting partial wave amplitudes are independent of any regulator. The method requires the introduction of Castillejo-Dalitz-Dyson (CDD) poles at infinity to enforce the correct threshold behavior for partial waves with orbital angular momentum ℓ ≥ 2.

## Method

The N/D method separates the NN partial wave amplitude into a function N(A) containing only the left-hand cut and a function D(A) containing only the right-hand cut, where A is the squared center-of-mass three-momentum. The discontinuity along the left-hand cut, Δ(A), is calculated perturbatively from chiral perturbation theory, with OPE as the leading order contribution. For partial waves with ℓ ≥ 2, the N/D equations are solved subject to ℓ−1 constraints (sum rules) that guarantee the amplitude vanishes as A^ℓ near threshold; these constraints are satisfied by adding ℓ−1 CDD poles at infinity to the D function. The resulting linear integral equation for D(A) is solved iteratively, and the phase shifts are compared to the Nijmegen partial wave analysis.

## Key numbers

- The scattering length for the ¹S₀ partial wave is a_s = −23.758 ± 0.04 fm, used as input to fix the subtraction constant N(0) for S-waves.
- The upper limit of the left-hand cut is L = −m_π²/4, with m_π the pion mass, corresponding to the OPE contribution.
- Phase shifts are calculated up to |p| = 300 MeV, below the pion production threshold at |p| ≈ 360 MeV.
- For the ³G₄ partial wave, results are shown to be stable for CDD pole positions B = 10^n m_π² with n = 2, 3, 4, 5, and 6, producing a narrow band of results.

## Context

This work extends the dispersive approach to NN interactions developed by Lacour, Oller, and Meissner (2011), who established the chiral power counting for the left-hand cut discontinuity. The method contrasts with the standard Weinberg approach (1990, 1991) of solving a Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a chiral NN potential, which requires a momentum cutoff regulator and leaves residual cutoff dependence. The N/D method provides a regulator-independent, unitary description with correct analytic properties, and can be extended to higher orders by including two-pion exchange contributions in the left-hand cut discontinuity, as well as to triplet coupled partial waves and inelastic channels.
