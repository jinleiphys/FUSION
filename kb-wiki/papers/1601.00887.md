# Charge-dependent correlations from event-by-event anomalous hydrodynamics

> We report on our recent attempt of quantitative modeling of the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) in heavy-ion collisions. We perform 3+1 dimensional anomalous hydrodynamic simulations on an event-by-event basis, with constitutive equations that contain the anomaly-induced effects. We also develop a model of the initial condition for the axial charge density that captures the statistical nature of random chirality imbalances created by the color flux tubes. Basing on the event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations for hundreds of thousands of collisions, we calculate the correlation functions that are measured in experiments, and discuss how the anomalous transport affects these observables.

## Key claim

The authors perform event-by-event 3+1 dimensional anomalous hydrodynamic simulations to quantitatively model the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) in heavy-ion collisions, finding that the calculated charge-dependent correlation observables show the expected qualitative behavior and are of comparable order of magnitude to experimentally measured values, but note that the largest uncertainty arises from the choice of the lifetime of the magnetic fields.

## Method

The authors solve 3+1 dimensional dissipationless anomalous hydrodynamic equations with constitutive equations containing anomaly-induced transport coefficients for the chiral magnetic and chiral separation effects (CME/CSE), treating electromagnetic fields as non-dynamical background fields. They develop a Monte-Carlo Glauber-based model for initial axial charge density where each binary collision is randomly assigned a sign of color E·B, and perform event-by-event simulations for thousands of collisions, using the Cooper-Frye formula with freezeout temperature 160 MeV to obtain particle distributions and calculate charge-dependent two-particle correlation functions.

## Key numbers

* Magnetic field parameters: eB₀ = 0.5 GeV², τ_B = 3 fm, giving eB_y(τ_in, 0, 0) ∼ (3m_π)² at impact parameter b = 7.2 fm
* Gold nucleus radius: R = 6.38 fm
* Freezeout temperature: T_fo = 160 MeV
* Axial chemical potential strength constant: C_μ₅ = 0.1 GeV
* Statistics: 10,000 events used for both anomalous and non-anomalous cases at impact parameter b = 7.2 fm
* Magnetic field widths: σ_x = 0.8(R - b/2), σ_y = 0.8√(R² - (b/2)²), σ_η = √2

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of the Chiral Magnetic Effect (Kharzeev 2004, Fukushima 2008) and anomalous hydrodynamics (Son 2009) by providing the first event-by-event 3+1 dimensional anomalous hydrodynamic simulations for heavy-ion collisions. The results are compared to experimental data from STAR (Abelev 2009) and PHENIX (Ajitanand 2010) at RHIC and ALICE (Abelev 2012) at the LHC, and the authors note that background effects such as transverse momentum conservation (Bzdak 2010, Pratt 2010), charge conservation (Schlichting 2010), and cluster correlations (Wang 2009) are absent in their Cooper-Frye sampling procedure. The work enables future studies coupling hydrodynamic evolution with dynamical Maxwell equations to determine the magnetic field lifetime self-consistently.
