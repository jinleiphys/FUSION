# Phi-Meson Production at RHIC, Strong Color Fields and Intrinsic Transverse Momenta

> We investigate the effects of strong color fields and of the associated enhanced intrinsic transverse momenta on the phi-meson production in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC. The observed consequences include a change of the spectral slopes, varying particle ratios, and also modified mean transverse momenta. In particular, the composition of the production processes of phi mesons, that is, direct production vs. coalescence-like production, depends strongly on the strength of the color fields and intrinsic transverse momenta and thus represents a sensitive probe for their measurement.

## Key claim

The composition of phi-meson production (direct string decays versus coalescence-like K anti-K collisions) depends strongly on the strength of color fields and intrinsic transverse momenta, and this composition can serve as a sensitive probe for measuring those field properties; however, the effect is conditional on the specific values of the string tension kappa and the intrinsic transverse momentum sigma, which are treated as independent parameters in the model.

## Method

The authors used the Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMDv1.3) transport model, which simulates multiple interactions of incoming and newly produced particles, creation and fragmentation of color strings, and formation and decay of baryonic and mesonic resonances. They varied the string tension kappa (mass term) from 1 GeV/fm to 3 GeV/fm and the intrinsic transverse momentum sigma independently, then calculated transverse momentum spectra, particle ratios, and mean transverse momenta for phi mesons in central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- String tension kappa varied from default 1 GeV/fm to 3 GeV/fm; the larger value increases the strange quark pair production probability ratio gamma_s from 0.37 to 0.72
- Intrinsic transverse momentum sigma varied from 0.55 GeV/c (vacuum value) to larger values; the vacuum sigma is approximately sqrt(kappa/pi)
- For vacuum parameters (kappa=1 GeV/fm, sigma=0.55 GeV/c), direct production and coalescence-like K anti-K production contribute about equally to phi-meson yields
- Typical field strength at RHIC energies suggested to be as large as 5-12 GeV/fm (from Magas et al., 2001)
- Regge slope for baryons is approximately 1 GeV^{-2} (Green, 1987); Pomeron slope is approximately 0.4 GeV^{-2} (Veneziano, 1974; Collins, 1977)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of strangeness enhancement in heavy ion collisions by Soff et al. (1999, 2001) and the color rope formation model of Sorge et al. (1992), applying the strong color field concept specifically to phi-meson production at RHIC energies. The results connect to the color glass condensate (CGC) framework of McLerran and Schaffner-Bielich (2001) and Schaffner-Bielich et al. (2002), which predicts intrinsic p_t broadening, and to calculations of phi production via K anti-K coalescence by Pal, Ko, and Lin (2002) and Bravina et al. (2003). The paper enables experimental discrimination between direct and coalescence production mechanisms by comparing phi-meson yields from hadronic (K+K-) and leptonic (dielectron) decay channels, which should show different spectral slopes and yields due to rescattering effects.
