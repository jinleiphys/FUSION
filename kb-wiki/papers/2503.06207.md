# Transport and response coefficients in second-order dissipative relativistic hydrodynamics with quantum corrections: probing the quark-gluon plasma

> A functional measure encompasses quantum corrections and is explored in the fluid/gravity correspondence. Corrections to response and transport coefficients in the second-order dissipative relativistic hydrodynamics are proposed, including the ones to the pressure, the relaxation time, and the shear relaxation. Their dependence on the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) temperature sets a temperature dependence on the running parameter encoding the one-loop quantum gravity correction, driven by a functional measure. The experimental range of the bulk viscosity-to-entropy density ratio of the QGP corroborates the existence of the functional measure.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that quantum gravity corrections, encoded in a functional measure parameter gamma, modify second-order dissipative relativistic hydrodynamics coefficients (pressure, relaxation time, shear relaxation) for the quark-gluon plasma, and that the experimentally measured range of the bulk viscosity-to-entropy density ratio from five independent phenomenological analyses corroborates the existence of this non-vanishing renormalized parameter.

## Method

The authors compute the functional measure correction to the effective action in the AdS/CFT framework using an AdS5-Schwarzschild background, then derive the effective energy-momentum tensor including the quantum correction term. They perform a second-order derivative expansion of relativistic hydrodynamics to extract corrections to transport and response coefficients, and compare the resulting bulk viscosity-to-entropy density ratio against experimental data from five different phenomenological analyses (JETSCAPE Bayesian model, Duke, Jyvaskyla-Helsinki-Munich, MIT-Utrecht-Geneve, and Shanghai groups) to constrain the running parameter gamma as a function of QGP temperature.

## Key numbers

* The effective bulk viscosity carries a quantum correction: zeta^eff = zeta + 4 i gamma, where gamma is the renormalized functional measure parameter
* The effective shear viscosity carries no quantum correction: eta^eff = eta
* The effective pressure: P^eff = P + 2 i gamma
* The effective energy density: epsilon^eff = epsilon - 2 i gamma
* The entropy density carries no quantum correction: s^eff = s
* The Kovtun-Son-Starinets ratio remains invariant: eta^eff / s^eff = eta / s

## Context

This work extends the previous analysis by Kuntz (2022) that computed functional measure corrections to first-order transport coefficients, now including second-order coefficients such as relaxation time and shear relaxation. The results are compared against five independent phenomenological analyses of QGP properties: the JETSCAPE Bayesian model, Duke group (Bernhard 2019), Jyvaskyla-Helsinki-Munich group (Parkkila 2021), MIT-Utrecht-Geneve group (Nijs 2020, 2022), and Shanghai group (Yang 2022). The work enables experimental constraints on quantum gravity corrections using high-temperature QGP data from RHIC and LHC heavy-ion collisions.
