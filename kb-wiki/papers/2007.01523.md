# Lattice QCD evaluation of the Compton amplitude employing the Feynman-Hellmann theorem

> The forward Compton amplitude describes the process of virtual photon scattering from a hadron and provides an essential ingredient for the understanding of hadron structure. As a physical amplitude, the Compton tensor naturally includes all target mass corrections and higher twist effects at a fixed virtuality, $Q^2$. By making use of the second-order Feynman-Hellmann theorem, the nucleon Compton tensor is calculated in lattice QCD at an unphysical quark mass across a range of photon momenta $3 \lesssim Q^2 \lesssim 7$ GeV$^2$. This allows for the $Q^2$ dependence of the low moments of the nucleon structure functions to be studied in a lattice calculation for the first time. The results demonstrate that a systematic investigation of power corrections and the approach to parton asymptotics is now within reach.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the forward Compton amplitude of the nucleon can be calculated in lattice QCD using the second-order Feynman-Hellmann theorem, enabling the first lattice study of the \(Q^2\) dependence of low moments of nucleon structure functions, but only at an unphysical quark mass (pion mass \(\simeq 470\) MeV) and under the condition that the kinematics remain below the elastic threshold (\(|\omega|<1\)) to ensure the Euclidean and Minkowski amplitudes are identical.

## Method

The authors introduce a perturbation to the fermion action of the form \(\lambda \int d^4z (e^{i q \cdot z} + e^{-i q \cdot z}) \mathcal{J}_{\mu}(z)\), where \(\lambda\) is the coupling strength and \(\mathcal{J}_{\mu}\) is the electromagnetic current. They then compute two-point correlation functions of the nucleon in the presence of this background field on a \(32^3 \times 64\) lattice with \(2+1\) flavors of dynamical quarks at the SU(3) flavor symmetric point. The second-order energy shift of the nucleon, extracted from the time-enhanced term in the correlator, is related to the forward Compton amplitude via the derived Feynman-Hellmann relation, allowing extraction of the Compton tensor for multiple kinematic points by varying the nucleon momentum \(\mathbf{p}\) and external current momentum \(\mathbf{q}\).

## Key numbers

* The lattice spacing is \(a = 0.074(2)\) fm, determined using flavor-singlet quantities.
* The pion mass is \(m_\pi = 0.467(12)\) GeV and the nucleon mass is \(m_N = 1.250(39)\) GeV at the SU(3) flavor symmetric point.
* The photon virtuality range studied is \(3 \lesssim Q^2 \lesssim 7\) GeV\(^2\).
* The renormalization constant for the local vector current is \(Z_V = 0.8611(84)\), determined from charge conservation at \(Q^2=0\).
* The lattice volume is \(32^3 \times 64\) with \(m_\pi L = 5.6\), and the ensemble contains 1763 configurations.

## Context

This work extends the approach outlined in Chambers (2017) for determining nucleon structure functions from lattice QCD calculations of the forward Compton amplitude, moving beyond traditional calculations limited to leading-twist matrix elements of parton distribution functions as in Martinelli (1988) and Gockeler (1995, 1996, 1998). By working with the physical Compton amplitude, the method avoids issues of operator mixing and renormalization that plague quasi-PDF approaches (Ji 2013) and pseudo-PDF approaches (Radyushkin 2017), and enables future systematic investigations of power corrections and the approach to parton asymptotics, including the possibility of extracting nucleon structure functions in closed form.
