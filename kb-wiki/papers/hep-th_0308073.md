# Understanding the Strong Coupling Limit of the ${\cal N}=4$ Supersymmetric Yang-Mills at Finite Temperature

> Recently, a number of intriguing results have been obtained for strongly coupled ${\cal N}=4$ Supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory in vacuum and matter, using the AdS/CFT correspondence. In this work, we provide a physical picture supporting and explaining most of these results within the gauge theory. The modified Coulomb's law at strong coupling forces static charges to communicate via the high frequency modes of the gauge/scalar fields. Therefore, the interaction between even relativistically moving charges can be approximated by a potential. At strong coupling, WKB arguments yield a series of deeply bound states, whereby the large Coulomb attraction is balanced by centrifugation. The result is a constant density of light bound states at {\bf any} value of the strong coupling, explaining why the thermodynamics and kinetics are coupling constant independent. In essence, at strong coupling the matter is not made of the original quasiparticles but of much lighter (binary) composites. A transition from weak to strong coupling is reminiscent to a transition from high to low $T$ in QCD. We establish novel results for screening in vacuum and matter through a dominant set of diagrams some of which are in qualitative agreement with known strong coupling results.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the thermodynamics and kinetics of strongly coupled \({\cal N}=4\) Supersymmetric Yang-Mills at finite temperature are independent of the coupling constant \(\lambda\) because the matter consists not of original quasiparticles but of much lighter binary composites, with the caveat that this explanation holds for any value of the strong coupling but relies on a WKB approximation yielding a constant density of light bound states.

## Method

The authors use a Bethe-Salpeter equation to resum ladder diagrams for static charges in the gauge theory, identifying that high-frequency modes of gauge and scalar fields mediate interactions with an effective superluminal velocity \(v \approx \lambda^{1/4}\). They then apply a WKB approximation to the Klein-Gordon and Dirac equations with a screened Coulomb potential to derive the spectrum of deeply bound states, and compare these results to known AdS/CFT predictions for the static potential, free energy, and screening.

## Key numbers

- The modified Coulomb potential at strong coupling is \(V(L) = - (4\pi^2 / \Gamma(1/4)^4) \sqrt{\lambda} / L\) with a numerical coefficient of 0.228, compared to the ladder-generated potential \(V_{\text{lad}}(L) = -\sqrt{\lambda} / (\pi L)\) where \(1/\pi = 0.318\).
- The Debye screening radius at strong coupling is of order \(1/T\), contrasting with \(\sqrt{\lambda} T\) for electric modes and \(\lambda T\) for magnetic modes in weak coupling.
- The free energy at strong coupling is \(\mathbf{F}(T, N_c, \lambda) = (3/4 + O(1/\lambda^{3/2})) \mathbf{F}(T, N_c, 0)\), where \(\mathbf{F}(T, N_c, 0) \approx N_c^2 T\) is the zero-coupling result.
- The critical coupling for S-states to bind is \(\lambda_c = \pi^2\).
- The effective screening length in the screened Coulomb potential is \(1/(\pi T)\), independent of the coupling constant.

## Context

This work extends the AdS/CFT results for strongly coupled \({\cal N}=4\) SYM, such as the static heavy-quark potential from Maldacena (1998) and Rey et al. (1998), and the finite-temperature results for free energy, electric Debye screening, and viscosity from various authors. It also builds on the observation by Semenoff and Zarembo (2000) that ladder resummation reproduces the modified Coulomb law for circular Wilson loops. The paper enables a physical picture within gauge theory for the coupling-constant independence of thermodynamics and kinetics, and draws a parallel to the near-critical Quark-Gluon Plasma in QCD discussed by the same authors in a prior work (Shuryak and Zahed, 2003).
