# Validity of the Weizsäcker-Williams Approximation and the Analysis of Beam Dump Experiments: Production of an axion, a dark photon, or a new axial-vector boson

> Beam dump experiments have been used to search for new particles, $ϕ$, with null results interpreted in terms of limits on masses $m_ϕ$ and coupling constants $ε$. However these limits have been obtained by using approximations [including the Weizsäcker-Williams (WW) approximation] or Monte-Carlo simulations. We display methods to obtain the cross section and the resulting particle production rates without using approximations on the phase space integral or Monte-Carlo simulations. In our previous work we examined the case of the new scalar boson production; in this paper we explore all possible new spin-0 and spin-1 particles. We show that the approximations cannot be used to obtain accurate values of cross sections. The corresponding exclusion plots differ by substantial amounts when seen on a linear scale. Furthermore, a new region ($m_ϕ<2m_e$) of parameter space can be explored without using one of the common approximations, $m_ϕ\gg m_e$. We derive new expressions for the three photon decays of dark photon and four photon decays of new axial-vector bosons. As a result, the production cross section and exclusion region of different low mass ($m_ϕ<2m_e$) bosons are very different. Moreover, our method can be used as a consistency check for Monte-Carlo simulations.

## Key claim

The Weizsacker-Williams (WW) and improved WW (IWW) approximations, commonly used to compute cross sections for new particle production in beam dump experiments, introduce order-one relative errors in cross sections (up to 100% for the WW approximation when the new boson mass is below about 1 MeV, and for the IWW approximation when the mass exceeds about 100 MeV), yet the resulting exclusion plots on a log-log scale appear similar because the exponential factors from decay and absorption lengths partially compensate for the cross-section errors.

## Method

The authors performed a complete, analytic calculation of the 2-to-3 bremsstrahlung-like production cross section for pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector bosons in electron-nucleus collisions, without any phase-space approximations or Monte Carlo simulations. They then compared this exact cross section to results obtained using the WW approximation (which replaces the 2-to-3 process with a 2-to-2 process via virtual quanta) and the IWW approximation (which further simplifies the integration limits to be independent of kinematic variables). The analysis was applied to the beam dump experiment E137 (20 GeV electron beam on an aluminum target) to compute exclusion limits on the coupling constant epsilon as a function of boson mass, including the low-mass region m_phi < 2m_e where the dominant decay channels involve photons rather than electron-positron pairs.

## Key numbers

- For the WW approximation, the relative error in the differential cross section can reach 100% when m_phi is less than about 1 MeV (see Figs. 3-5).
- For the IWW approximation, the approximation fails when m_phi is greater than about 100 MeV (see Figs. 3-5).
- The beam dump E137 parameters used: E_0 = 20 GeV, E_cut = 2 GeV, N_e = 1.87 x 10^20 incident electrons, L_sh = 179 m (target plus shield length), L_dec = 204 m (decay region length).
- The null result from E137 translates to a 95% confidence level upper limit of N_phi = 3 events.
- The leading term of the vector boson decay to 3 photons is Gamma_V(phi -> 3gamma) = epsilon_V^2 (alpha^4 / (2^7 3^6 5^2 pi^3)) (m_phi^9 / m_e^8) * (17/5 + ...).
- The leading term of the axial-vector boson decay to 4 photons is Gamma_A(phi -> 4gamma) = epsilon_A^2 (127 alpha^5 / (2^11 3^8 5^4 7^2 pi^4)) (m_phi^13 / m_e^12) + O(m_phi^15 / m_e^14).
- The maximum angular acceptance for E137 is theta_max approximately 4.4 x 10^-3 radians.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of scalar boson production (Liu and Miller, 2016) to all possible spin-0 and spin-1 particles (pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector). It directly tests the validity of the WW approximation as applied in beam dump analyses by Bjorken et al. (2009) and Tsai (1986), and the IWW approximation as developed by Kim and Tsai (1973). The paper provides new analytic expressions for the three-photon decay of the dark photon and the four-photon decay of a new axial-vector boson, which are necessary for exploring the low-mass region m_phi < 2m_e. The results enable future beam dump experiments to use exact calculations as a consistency check for Monte Carlo simulations (e.g., MadGraph/MadEvent as used by Essig et al., 2010) and to correctly interpret exclusion limits in the low-mass regime where different boson species produce very different signatures.
