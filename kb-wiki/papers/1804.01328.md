# Light clusters in warm stellar matter: explicit mass shifts and universal cluster-meson couplings

> In-medium modifications of light cluster properties in warm stellar matter are studied within the relativistic mean-field approximation. In-medium effects are included by introducing an explicit binding energy shift analytically calculated in the Thomas-Fermi approximation, supplemented with a phenomenological modification of the cluster couplings to the $σ$ meson. A linear dependence on the $σ$ meson is assumed for the cluster mass, and the associated coupling constant is fixed imposing that the virial limit at low density is recovered. The resulting cluster abundances come out to be in reasonable agreement with constraints at higher density coming from heavy ion collision data. Some comparisons with microscopic calculations are also shown.

## Key claim

The authors propose that a universal, phenomenological coupling of all light clusters to the scalar sigma meson, with a coupling fraction of \(x_s = 0.85 \pm 0.05\) relative to the nucleon-sigma coupling, combined with an explicit binding energy shift derived from the Thomas-Fermi approximation, can reproduce both the virial equation of state at low densities and the cluster dissolution at higher densities, though the deuteron is excluded from this universal treatment due to its weak binding.

## Method

The authors employ a relativistic mean-field (RMF) Lagrangian including neutrons, protons, and light clusters (deuteron, triton, helion, alpha) as point-like particles. The cluster effective masses are modified by a binding energy shift \(\delta B_j\) calculated analytically from the Thomas-Fermi excluded-volume formalism of Raduta and Gulminelli, which removes the energy states already occupied by the free nucleon gas. The cluster-sigma meson couplings are parametrized as \(g_{sj} = x_s A_j g_s\) with a universal fraction \(x_s\), while the vector couplings are fixed to \(g_{vj} = A_j g_v\); the fraction \(x_s\) is determined by fitting the RMF cluster fractions to the virial equation of state (VEoS) of Typel et al. (2012) at low densities between \(10^{-6}\) fm\(^{-3}\) and \(10^{-4}\) fm\(^{-3}\) for temperatures of 4 and 10 MeV.

## Key numbers

* The universal cluster-sigma coupling fraction is determined as \(x_s = 0.85 \pm 0.05\), based on fits to the VEoS at \(T = 4\) and 10 MeV with proton fraction \(y_p = 0.5\).
* The nuclear saturation density for the FSU model used is \(\rho_0 = 0.148\) fm\(^{-3}\).
* At \(T = 4\) MeV and density \(\rho = 91.2 \times 10^{-6}\) fm\(^{-3}\), the VEoS cluster fractions are: triton \(X_t = 6.8 \times 10^{-3}\), helion \(X_h = 5.7 \times 10^{-3}\), and alpha \(X_\alpha = 1.1 \times 10^{-1}\).
* At \(T = 10\) MeV and density \(\rho = 91.2 \times 10^{-6}\) fm\(^{-3}\), the VEoS cluster fractions are: triton \(X_t = 1.5 \times 10^{-4}\), helion \(X_h = 1.4 \times 10^{-4}\), and alpha \(X_\alpha = 2.3 \times 10^{-6}\).
* With the optimal coupling \(x_s = 0.85\) at \(T = 4\) MeV and \(\rho = 91.2 \times 10^{-6}\) fm\(^{-3}\), the relative difference between RMF and VEoS fractions is \(-2.85\%\) for triton, \(-2.99\%\) for helion, and \(-4.02\%\) for alpha.
* The dissolution densities for light clusters (fraction \(X_j < 10^{-4}\)) at \(T = 5\) MeV and \(y_p = 0.41\) are in the range \(0.04\) fm\(^{-3} < \rho < 0.06\) fm\(^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends the RMF treatment of light clusters by Typel et al. (2010) and Avancini et al. (2017), who used either fitted effective mass shifts or phenomenological meson-cluster couplings, by combining both an explicit binding energy shift and a universal scalar coupling. The approach is constrained by the virial equation of state of Typel et al. (2012) at low densities and compared to quantum statistical calculations by Roepke et al. (2015) and the empirical cluster dissolution model of Typel et al. (2010). The formalism enables a unified description of arbitrary light cluster species in warm stellar matter, with the next step being the inclusion of heavier clusters and pasta phases.
