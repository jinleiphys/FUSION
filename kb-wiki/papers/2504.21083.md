# Neutrino-antineutrino synchrotron emission from magnetized dense quark matter

> Using the Kadanoff-Baym formalism, we perform a detailed study of neutrino-antineutrino synchrotron emission from strongly magnetized, dense quark matter under conditions relevant to compact stars. Starting from an exact expression for the emission rate that fully accounts for Landau-level quantization of quarks, we derive an approximate formula applicable in the regime where quark chemical potentials are much larger than all other relevant energy scales. We demonstrate that the emission rate is largely controlled by a single dimensionless ratio between two low-energy scales: the Landau-level spacing at the Fermi surface, $|e_f B|/μ_{f}$, and the temperature of the quark matter, $T$. When the ratio $|e_f B|/(μ_{f} T)$ approaches zero, many closely spaced Landau levels contribute to the emission, but the total rate vanishes as $B\to 0$. In the opposite limit, where the ratio is large, the rate is dominated by transitions between adjacent levels and is exponentially suppressed due to Landau-level quantization, which limits the thermal activation of quarks near the Fermi surface. Our results show that, even in the presence of the strongest magnetic fields expected in compact stars, the synchrotron emission remains suppressed by more than 3 orders of magnitude compared to the direct Urca process. This implies that such emission is unlikely to play any substantial role in the cooling of magnetized quark stars, at least those made of unpaired quark matter phases.

# Neutrino-antineutrino synchrotron emission from magnetized dense quark matter

## Key claim

The neutrino-antineutrino synchrotron emission rate from magnetized dense quark matter is controlled by a single dimensionless ratio $|e_f B|/(\mu_f T)$, and even under the strongest magnetic fields expected in compact stars (up to $10^{17}$ G), the emission remains suppressed by more than 3 orders of magnitude compared to the direct Urca process, making it unlikely to play any substantial role in cooling magnetized quark stars made of unpaired quark matter phases.

## Method

Using the Kadanoff-Baym formalism, the authors derived an exact expression for the neutrino-antineutrino synchrotron emission rate that fully accounts for Landau-level quantization of quarks in a magnetic field. They then derived an approximate formula applicable in the high-density regime where quark chemical potentials are much larger than all other relevant energy scales, replacing the sum over Landau levels with an integral and using asymptotic forms of generalized Laguerre polynomials. The final emission rate is expressed in terms of a scaling function $F(b)$ of the dimensionless parameter $b = |e_f B|/(T \mu_f)$, which is evaluated numerically.

## Key numbers

- The emission rate scales as $\dot{\mathcal{E}}_\nu \propto |e_f B|^2 T^5 F(b)$ in the weak-field regime, where $F(b)$ approaches a constant value of approximately 11.06 as $b \to 0$.
- In the strong-field regime ($b \gg 1$), the emission rate decreases rapidly with an approximate scaling $F(b) \sim \exp(-b/2)$.
- The synchrotron emission is suppressed by more than 3 orders of magnitude compared to the direct Urca process, even for magnetic fields up to $10^{17}$ G.
- Representative chemical potentials used in numerical calculations: $\mu_u = 300$ MeV, $\mu_d = 350$ MeV, and $\mu_e = 50$ MeV, satisfying $\beta$-equilibrium condition $\mu_d = \mu_u + \mu_e$.
- The temperature is assumed to be $T \lesssim 10$ MeV to avoid neutrino trapping.
- Magnetic field strengths considered range from $10^{14}$ G to $10^{17}$ G.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutrino-antineutrino synchrotron emission from magnetized matter, which were extensively investigated in white dwarfs and neutron stars by Yakovlev (1981), Kaminker (1992, 1993), and Bezchastnov (1997), where such emission from electrons was found to be significant and even dominant in certain density and temperature regimes. The authors had previously examined the impact of strong magnetic fields on direct Urca neutrino emission in Ghosh (2025). This study contradicts earlier scaling arguments by Kaminker (1992) that suggested synchrotron and direct Urca processes could become comparable under certain conditions in quark matter. The results enable future work on cooling scenarios for magnetized quark stars, particularly those involving paired quark matter phases where different suppression mechanisms may operate.
