# Repulsive Four-Body Interactions of $α$ Particles and Quasi-Stable Nuclear $α$-Particle Condensates in Heavy Self-Conjugate Nuclei

> We study the effects of repulsive four-body interactions of $α$ particles on nuclear $α$-particle condensates in heavy self-conjugate nuclei using a semi-analytic approach, and find that the repulsive four-body interactions could decrease the critical number of $α$ particles, beyond which quasi-stable $α$-particle condensate states can no longer exist, even if these four-body interactions make only tiny contributions to the total energy of the Hoyle-like state of $^{16}$O. Explicitly, we study eight benchmark parameter sets, and find that the critical number $N_\text{cr}$ decreases by $|ΔN_\text{cr}|\sim1-4$ from $N_\text{cr}\sim11$ with vanishing four-body interactions. We also discuss the effects of four-body interactions on energies and radii of $α$-particle condensates. Our study can be useful for future experiments to study $α$-particle condensates in heavy self-conjugate nuclei. Also, the experimental determination of $N_\text{cr}$ will eventually help establish a better understanding on the $α$-particle interactions, especially the four-body interactions.

## Key claim

Repulsive four-body interactions of alpha particles, even when they contribute less than 5% to the total energy of the Hoyle-like state in 16O, can reduce the critical number of alpha particles (Ncr) for which quasi-stable alpha-particle condensates can exist in heavy self-conjugate nuclei, from Ncr ~ 11 down to Ncr ~ 7-10 depending on the parameter set.

## Method

The authors used a semi-analytic mean-field approach treating alpha particles as structureless bosons, extending the Gross-Pitaevskii framework of Yamada and Schuck (2003). They employed harmonic-oscillator trial wave functions and the Ali-Bodmer ansatz for two-body, three-body, and four-body alpha interactions, with eight benchmark parameter sets for the four-body forces chosen to contribute less than 5% to the 16O total energy. The energy functional was minimized with respect to the oscillator parameter b to obtain energies and charge root-mean-square radii for N-alpha systems from N=4 to N=11.

## Key numbers

- Critical number Ncr decreases by |ΔNcr| ~ 1-4 from Ncr ~ 11 (with vanishing four-body interactions) to Ncr ~ 7-10 for the eight benchmark parameter sets
- For the Yamada-Schuck parameter set without four-body interactions: Ncr = 11, with energies ranging from 0.99 MeV (12C) to 18.58 MeV (44Ti) and radii from 4.88 fm to 7.86 fm
- For parameter set ◁4α▷ (U4 = 2500 MeV, μυ = 0.387 fm-1): Ncr = 10, with 40Ca energy 15.27 MeV and radius 7.96 fm
- For parameter set ♡4α♡ (U4 = 10000 MeV, μυ = 0.387 fm-1): Ncr = 8, with 32S energy 9.56 MeV and radius 7.75 fm
- For parameter set ♠4α♠ (U4 = 100 MeV, μυ = 0.2 fm-1): Ncr = 7, with 28Si energy 7.30 MeV and radius 7.96 fm
- Four-body contributions to 16O total energy are less than 5% for all benchmark parameter sets (three-body contributions are ~18%)
- Repulsive four-body interactions cause increases in total energies (less than 10%) and rms radii (less than 30%) of N-alpha systems

## Context

This work extends the study of Yamada and Schuck (2003), who used Gross-Pitaevskii and Hill-Wheeler equations with two-body and repulsive three-body alpha interactions to predict quasi-stable alpha condensates up to around 40Ca. The present paper adds repulsive four-body interactions, motivated by orthogonality condition model calculations on 16O by Funaki (2008) which showed such interactions are needed to fit the ground-state energy. The results suggest that the critical number of alpha particles for condensate stability is lower than previously estimated, which can guide future experiments searching for alpha-particle condensates in heavy self-conjugate nuclei such as those attempted by Akimune (2013), Swartz (2015), and Bishop (2017).
