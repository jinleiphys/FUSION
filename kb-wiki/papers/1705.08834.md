# Photon and Pomeron -- induced production of Dijets in $pp$, $pA$ and $AA$ collisions

> In this paper we present a detailed comparison of the dijet production by photon -- photon, photon -- pomeron and pomeron -- pomeron interactions in $pp$, $pA$ and ${\rm AA}$ collisions at the LHC energy. The transverse momentum, pseudo -- rapidity and angular dependencies of the cross sections are calculated at LHC energy using the Forward Physics Monte Carlo (FPMC), which allows to obtain realistic predictions for the dijet production with two leading intact hadrons. We obtain that $γ\pom$ channel is dominant at forward rapidities in $pp$ collisions and in the full kinematical range in the nuclear collisions of heavy nuclei. Our results indicate that the analysis of dijet production at the LHC can be useful to test the Resolved Pomeron model as well as to constrain the magnitude of the absorption effects.

## Key claim

The gamma-pomeron channel dominates dijet production at forward rapidities in pp collisions and across the full kinematic range in heavy-ion (AA) collisions, but this conclusion depends on the assumed gap survival probability for pomeron-pomeron interactions, which is taken as an upper bound for nuclear collisions.

## Method

The authors used the Forward Physics Monte Carlo (FPMC) to calculate leading-order cross sections for dijet production via gamma-gamma, gamma-pomeron, and pomeron-pomeron interactions in pp, pPb, and PbPb collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV. The Resolved Pomeron model was employed for diffractive gluon distributions, using H1 fit B parameters, with the elastic photon flux from the proton described by the Drees-Zeppenfeld approximation and nuclear photon flux including the Z^2 enhancement. Jets were reconstructed using the anti-kT algorithm with R=0.5, selecting jets with pT > 20 GeV and |eta| < 6.0, and the gap survival probability for pomeron-pomeron interactions was calculated in impact parameter space for nuclear collisions.

## Key numbers

- In pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV, the pomeron-pomeron contribution dominates at central pseudorapidities by a factor of approximately 10 over gamma-pomeron and 10^4 over gamma-gamma; gamma-pomeron becomes dominant for |eta(jet)| >= 2.8
- Gap survival probability for pomeron-pomeron interactions in PbPb collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV: 0.000034 for AA and 0.0123 for pA
- Gap survival probability for pomeron-pomeron interactions in pp collisions: assumed to be 0.02
- In PbPb collisions, the gamma-pomeron contribution dominates over pomeron-pomeron by a factor of approximately 100 at central pseudorapidities
- Nuclear diffractive gluon distribution includes a suppression factor R_g = 0.15 for nuclear shadowing effects

## Context

This work extends the analysis of dijet production in ultraperipheral collisions previously investigated by Vadim (2015) for gamma-pomeron interactions, now including gamma-gamma and pomeron-pomeron channels within a common FPMC framework. The results enable experimental tests of the Resolved Pomeron model proposed by Ingelman and Schlein (1985) using LHC data from ATLAS, CMS, TOTEM, and LHCb forward detectors, and can constrain the magnitude of absorption effects in diffractive interactions. The predictions for pomeron-pomeron interactions in nuclear collisions are considered upper bounds compared to alternative models by Miller (2008) and Radion (2010), which predict smaller gap survival probabilities.
