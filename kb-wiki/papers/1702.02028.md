# Multiplicity fluctuations near the QCD critical point

> Statistical moments of particle multiplicities in heavy-ion collision experiments are an important probe in the exploration of the phase diagram of strongly interacting matter and, particularly, in the search for the QCD critical end point. In order to appropriately interpret experimental measures of these moments, however, it is necessary to understand the role of experimental limitations, as well as background contributions, providing expectations on how critical behavior should be affected by them. We here present a framework for calculating moments of particle multiplicities in the presence of correlations of both critical and spurious origins. We also include effects from resonance decay and a limited acceptance window, as well as detector efficiency. Although we focus on second-order moments, for simplicity, an extension to higher-order moments is straightforward.

## Key claim

The paper presents a framework for calculating second-order multiplicity fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions that incorporates both critical fluctuations (from the QCD critical point) and spurious background fluctuations (from temperature, volume, and impact parameter variations), while including effects from resonance decay, limited acceptance windows, and detector efficiency; the authors note that their equilibrium and isotropy assumptions provide an "optimistic, yet valuable tool" rather than a fully realistic model.

## Method

The authors model critical fluctuations as Gaussian fluctuations of an order parameter field σ(x) with correlation length ξ, coupled linearly to pions and protons via mass corrections. They compute multiplicity moments by expanding grand-canonical ensemble averages as power series in energy-level shifts induced by fluctuations of σ, volume R, temperature T, and chemical potential μ, then averaging over these fluctuations assuming independent Gaussian distributions. For finite-size effects, they impose Dirichlet boundary conditions on a sphere and compute allowed momentum modes from spherical Bessel functions. Acceptance cuts are incorporated via a momentum-dependent acceptance probability F(p) assuming isotropic emission, and resonance decay effects are treated by sampling two-body decay phase space to compute acceptance probabilities for decay products.

## Key numbers

- The coupling constant G for pion-σ interaction is estimated as ~300 MeV near the critical point
- The coupling constant g for proton-σ interaction has vacuum value ~10 from the linear sigma model
- For τ = 1 fm and ξ₀ = 1.6 fm, the maximum correlation length ratio is ξ/ξ₀ = 1.3 (compared to a previous estimate of ξ/ξ₀ = 1.9 with τ = 5.5 fm)
- Temperature fluctuations are modeled as Gaussian with 5% width
- The nuclear radius for gold nuclei is taken as R_N = 6.38 fm
- The critical exponents used are α = 0.11, ν = 0.63, β = 0.326, δ = 4.80, and z = 2 + α/ν

## Context

This work extends the treatment of critical fluctuations from Stephanov (1999, 2007) and the authors' own Monte Carlo framework (Hippert 2016) by providing an analytical method for computing multiplicity moments that naturally includes finite-size effects. The framework enables systematic inclusion of background fluctuations (volume, temperature, impact parameter) previously studied by Gorenstein (2011, 2015), Luo (2013, 2017), and Skokov (2012), as well as acceptance cut effects discussed by Bzdak (2012), Ling (2016), and Karsch (2015). The results can be used to generate quantitative expectations for critical fluctuation signatures in the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program, accounting for experimental limitations that could obscure or mimic critical behavior.
