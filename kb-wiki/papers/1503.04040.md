# Bose-Einstein condensation of pions in heavy-ion collisions at the CERN Large Hadron Collider (LHC) energies

> We analyse in detail the possibility of Bose-Einstein condensation of pions produced in heavy-ion collisions at the beam energy $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV. Our approach is based on the chemical non-equilibrium thermal model of hadron production which has been generalised to include separately the contribution from the local zero-momentum state. In order to study both the hadronic multiplicities and the transverse-momentum spectra, we use the Cracow freeze-out model which parameterises the flow and space-time geometry of the system at freeze-out in a very economic way. Our analysis indicates that about 5% of all pions may form the Bose-Einstein condensate.

## Key claim

The analysis indicates that about 5% of all pions produced in Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV may form a Bose-Einstein condensate, but this result is conditional on the use of a chemical non-equilibrium thermal model that explicitly includes the local zero-momentum state; the condensate fraction varies with centrality from 2% in the most central collisions to 7-8% in peripheral collisions, with a sudden increase to 19% in the most peripheral bin.

## Method

The authors extended the chemical non-equilibrium thermal model (NEQ) by explicitly separating the contribution from the local zero-momentum state (BEC model), using the Cracow freeze-out model to parameterize flow and space-time geometry. They fitted the model to ALICE data on rapidity densities of π±, p, p̄, K±, K0S, Λ, φ, Ξ±, and Ω± across six centrality intervals, and also compared the model predictions to transverse-momentum spectra of pions. The analysis used modified versions of the SHARE and THERMINATOR codes, and included an estimate for neutral pion multiplicities based on isospin symmetry.

## Key numbers

- The condensate rate is 2% in the most central (0-10%) Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV
- The condensate rate is 7-8% in peripheral (60-80%) collisions, with a sudden increase to 19% in the most peripheral (80-90%) bin
- The χ²/Ndof values drop below unity in the BEC model, indicating the experimental errors are probably too large
- The maximum transverse momentum for condensate particles is given by pTmax = m × rmax/τf, where m is the pion mass, rmax is the freeze-out radius, and τf is the freeze-out proper time
- The number of particles in the ground state in the equilibrium model is NcondEQ ≈ 0.7 for all centralities, while the total number of pions decreases from about 700 to 7 in the most peripheral collisions

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous chemical non-equilibrium analyses (Begun 2013, Begun 2014) which showed that pion chemical potentials at LHC energies are close to the critical value for Bose-Einstein condensation. It addresses the failure of the standard equilibrium thermal model to describe the proton-to-pion ratio at LHC (Abelev 2013) and the problems with low-pT pion spectra in hydrodynamic calculations (Gale 2012, Molnar 2014). The results are consistent with ALICE's observation of a coherent fraction in charged pion emission reaching 23% (Abelev 2013), and the framework enables future studies of condensate formation in expanding systems by providing a covariant generalization of the Cooper-Frye formula for boost-invariant and cylindrically symmetric systems.
