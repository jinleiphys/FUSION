# Pionic dispersion relations in the presence of a weak magnetic field

> In this work, dispersion relations of $π^0$ and $π^{\pm}$ have been studied in vacuum in the limit of weak external magnetic field using a phenomenological pion-nucleon $(πN)$ Lagrangian. For our purpose, we have calculated the results up to one loop order in self energy diagrams with the pseudoscalar $(PS)$ and pseudovector $(PV)$ pion-nucleon interactions. By assuming weak external magnetic field it is seen that the effective mass of pion gets explicit magnetic field dependence and it is modified significantly for the case of PS coupling. However, for the PV coupling, only a modest increase in the effective mass is observed. These modified dispersion relations due to the presence of the external field can have substantial influence in the phenomenological aspect of the mesons both in the context of neutron stars as well as relativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The effective mass of pions acquires an explicit dependence on a weak external magnetic field (eB << mπ²), but the sign and magnitude of the modification depend strongly on the choice of pion-nucleon coupling: pseudoscalar (PS) coupling produces a significant decrease in effective mass, while pseudovector (PV) coupling yields only a modest increase.

## Method

The authors computed the one-loop pion self-energy in vacuum using Schwinger's proper-time fermion propagator expanded to order (eB)² in a constant weak magnetic field, employing both pseudoscalar and pseudovector πN interaction Lagrangians. Vacuum divergences were removed by mass counterterm renormalization for PS coupling and by subtraction at the on-shell point for PV coupling. The effective pion mass was then obtained from the pole of the Dyson-resummed propagator at zero three-momentum.

## Key numbers

* For PS coupling, the effective pion mass decreases significantly with increasing magnetic field; at eB = 0.018 GeV², the neutral pion mass drops from its bare value of 0.134 GeV, and the charged pion mass drops from 0.139 GeV, with the neutral pion showing a greater rate of decrease than the charged pion.
* For PV coupling, the effective pion mass increases slightly with magnetic field over the same range (eB up to 0.018 GeV²), in contrast to the PS case.
* The dispersion relation ω(qz) was evaluated numerically for qz up to 0.175 GeV, at perpendicular momenta q⊥ = 0 GeV and 0.25 GeV, and at field strengths eB = 0.001 GeV² and 0.018 GeV².
* The PS coupling constant used is gπ²/(4π) = 14.1; the PV coupling constant is fπ²/(4π) = 0.08, with the relation g²/(2mp)² = (f/mπ)².

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of pion propagation in asymmetric nuclear matter without magnetic fields by Biswas et al. (2006, 2007), who used chiral effective Lagrangians with PS and PV couplings. It also builds on the weak-field expansion of fermion propagators developed by Chyi et al. (1999) and Navarro et al. (2010), and on the study of charged B-meson mass modification in magnetic fields by Machado et al. (2014). The results provide a vacuum baseline for future inclusion of finite density and temperature, relevant for neutron-star interiors and relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
