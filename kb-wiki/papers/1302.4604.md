# Nucleon form factors, generalized parton distributions and quark angular momentum

> We extract the individual contributions from u and d quarks to the Dirac and Pauli form factors of the proton, after a critical examination of the available measurements of electromagnetic nucleon form factors. From this data we determine generalized parton distributions for valence quarks, assuming a particular form for their functional dependence. The result allows us to study various aspects of nucleon structure in the valence region. In particular, we evaluate Ji's sum rule and estimate the total angular momentum carried by valence quarks at the scale mu = 2 GeV to be J_v^u = 0.230 +0.009 -0.024 and J_v^d = -0.004 +0.010 -0.016.

## Key claim

The authors extract individual u- and d-quark contributions to the proton's Dirac and Pauli form factors from a critically selected set of electromagnetic nucleon form factor data, then use these to determine generalized parton distributions (GPDs) for valence quarks under a specific functional ansatz; the resulting estimate of the total angular momentum carried by valence quarks at scale mu = 2 GeV is J_v^u = 0.230 +0.009 -0.024 and J_v^d = -0.004 +0.010 -0.016, with the caveat that this extraction depends on the assumed form of the GPDs and the data selection.

## Method

The authors critically examined available measurements of electromagnetic nucleon form factors (Sachs form factors G_E and G_M for proton and neutron) and selected a consistent data set, interpolating the data to obtain flavor form factors F_1^u, F_1^d, F_2^u, and F_2^d at discrete values of momentum transfer -t. They then assumed a specific functional form for the zero-skewness valence GPDs H_v^q(x,t) and E_v^q(x,t) (an exponential t-dependence with x-dependent profile functions) and fitted the parameters to the flavor form factor data using a chi-squared minimization. The resulting GPDs were used to evaluate Ji's sum rule for quark angular momentum and to compute Compton form factors, the axial form factor, and the Sivers distribution via a chromodynamic lensing model.

## Context

This work extends the program of extracting GPDs from form factor data, building on earlier analyses by Kelly (2002), Friedrich and Walcher (2003), and Gao (2004) for form factor compilations, and on the authors' previous work (Diehl, Kroll 2010) for Compton form factors. The extracted GPDs enable predictions for wide-angle Compton scattering (compared to Hall A data from JLab), the axial form factor (compared to Kitagaki data), and the Sivers distribution via chromodynamic lensing (compared to the extraction by Anselmino et al. 2012). The results provide a benchmark for future lattice QCD calculations and for analyses of deeply virtual Compton scattering and exclusive meson production.
