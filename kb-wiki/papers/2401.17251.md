# Diffractive deep inelastic scattering at NLO in the dipole picture

> We compute the transverse and longitudinal diffractive structure functions to full next-to-leading order accuracy in the dipole picture of deep inelastic scattering. Our calculation uses the standard light-cone perturbation theory method for the partonic content of the virtual photon, together with the Color Glass Condensate description of the target color field. Our result includes as a subset the $q\bar{q}g$ contribution calculated earlier. We show that there is a rapidity divergence that can be factorized into the BK/JIMWLK evolution of the target Wilson lines, and that all other divergences cancel.

## Key claim

The authors compute the transverse and longitudinal diffractive structure functions to full next-to-leading order (NLO) accuracy in the dipole picture of deep inelastic scattering, showing that a rapidity divergence can be factorized into the BK/JIMWLK evolution of the target Wilson lines and that all other divergences cancel, but the division of the full NLO cross section into dip, tripole, and dip-tripole parts is not unique and depends on the chosen UV subtraction scheme.

## Method

The calculation uses light-cone perturbation theory (LCPT) to decompose the virtual photon into its Fock state components (the \(q\bar{q}\) dipole and the \(q\bar{q}g\) tripole) combined with the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) description of the target color field. The diffractive cross section is separated into three parts: the dip term (leading-order \(q\bar{q}\) interaction plus vertex corrections and gluon exchange after the target), the tripole term (where the \(q\bar{q}g\) system interacts with the target in both amplitude and conjugate amplitude), and the dip-tripole interference term; ultraviolet and infrared divergences are canceled between these contributions by adding and subtracting a subtraction term.

## Key numbers

- The diffractive structure function is related to the cross section by \(\xpom F_{\lambda}^{\mathrm{D}(4)}(\xpom, Q^2, M_X^2, t) = \frac{Q^2}{4\pi^2 \aem} \frac{Q^2}{\beta} \frac{\dd[]{ \sigma^{\text{D}}_{\gamma^*_{\lambda} + A } }}{\dd{t} \dd{M_X^2}}\)
- The leading-order hard factor for longitudinal polarization is \(\mathcal{F}_L = 4[z_0 z_1 z_{\ov 0} z_{\ov 1}]^{3/2} Q^2 K_0(|\xt_{01}| Q \sqrt{z_0 z_1})K_0(|\xt_{\ov 0 \ov 1}| Q \sqrt{z_{\ov 0} z_{\ov 1}})\)
- The leading-order hard factor for transverse polarization is \(\mathcal{F}_T = z_0 z_1 z_{\ov 0} z_{\ov 1} (z_0 z_{\ov 0} + z_1 z_{\ov 1}) \frac{\xt_{01} \cdot \xt_{\ov 0 \ov 1}}{|\xt_{01}||\xt_{\ov 0\ov 1}|} Q^2K_1(|\xt_{01}| Q \sqrt{z_0 z_1})K_1(|\xt_{\ov 0 \ov 1}| Q \sqrt{z_{\ov 0} z_{\ov 1}})\)
- The NLO dip term hard factor includes a UV-finite term \(f^\text{UV} = (2\log(z_0 z_1)-3) \times [\log(\frac{M_X e^{\gamma_E} |\xt_{01}||\xt_{\ov 0\ov 1}|}{2 |\xt_{01}-\xt_{\ov 0\ov 1}|}) J_0(\mxbar |\xt_{01}-\xt_{\overline 0 \overline 1}|) + \frac{\pi}{2} Y_0(\mxbar |\xt_{01}-\xt_{\overline 0 \overline 1}|)] + [11 - \pi^2 +\log^2 z_0 + \log^2 z_1 - 4 \log z_0 \log z_1] J_0(\mxbar |\xt_{01}-\xt_{\overline 0 \overline 1}|)\)

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculation of the \(q\bar{q}g\) contribution to diffractive DIS by Beuf et al. (2022) to include the full NLO result, completing the missing contributions that were outlined but not calculated in that paper. The result reproduces the Wüsthoff result (1997) in the large-\(Q^2\) limit and the Bartels et al. (1999) and Kopeliovich et al. (1999) results in the large-\(M_X^2\) limit. This enables precise theoretical predictions for diffractive structure functions at the upcoming Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) and LHeC/FCC-he, where measurements of diffractive DIS will provide strong constraints on the structure of protons and nuclei in the saturation regime.
