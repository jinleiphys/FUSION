# Threshold cusp structures in multi-channel scattering

> We study the behavior of the cusp structures focusing on the isospin symmetry breaking effects. The properties of the exotic hadrons are reflected in the shape of the cusp structures. In realistic hadron scatterings, the threshold energies of the isospin partners appear within a small energy region. For a detailed analysis of such systems, it is essential to study the behavior of the cusp structures arising at two closely spaced thresholds. In this work, we introduce a convenient formulation of the scattering amplitude and demonstrate that the cusp structures at two nearby thresholds are related through the isospin symmetry.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in the $\Lambda N$-$\Sigma N$ coupled-channel system, the cusp structures at two closely spaced thresholds (due to isospin partners) are related through isospin symmetry, and the isospin-breaking effects on the cusp shapes are small when the scattering length is of typical hadronic scale.

## Method

The authors introduce a convenient representation of the multi-channel $s$-wave scattering amplitude using a $K$-matrix formalism, expressing the $(1,1)$ component near the $N$-th threshold as $f_{11}(E) = f_{11}^{0} (1 + i b_{11}^{(N)} p_N(E))/(1 + i a_N p_N(E))$. They apply this to the $\Lambda N$-$\Sigma N$ scattering system with total charge $Q=+1$, labeling channels as $\Lambda p$ (channel 1), $\Sigma^+ n$ (channel 2), and $\Sigma^0 p$ (channel 3), with an isospin-symmetric $K$-matrix containing four parameters. The isospin-broken case uses the physical mass difference $\Delta$ between the $\Sigma^+ n$ and $\Sigma^0 p$ thresholds, while the isospin-symmetric limit takes $\Delta \to 0$.

## Key numbers

- The energy difference between the $\Sigma^+ n$ and $\Sigma^0 p$ thresholds is $\Delta \approx 2$ MeV.
- For the isospin-symmetric limit, the scattering length is taken as $a_{\rm II} = -1.0 - i\,0.8$ fm.
- In the isospin-broken case, the scattering lengths are $a_2 = -0.65 - i\,0.46$ fm (for $\Sigma^+ n$) and $a_3 = -0.26 - i\,0.27$ fm (for $\Sigma^0 p$).
- The sum $a_2 + a_3 = -0.91 - i\,0.73$ fm is approximately equal to $a_{\rm II}$.

## Context

This work extends the study of threshold cusp structures in multi-channel scattering, building on prior analyses of exotic hadrons near thresholds by Esposito (2021), LHCb (2021), and the Particle Data Group (2024). The formulation enables future detailed analysis of isospin-breaking effects in systems like $\Lambda N$-$\Sigma N$ scattering, where the cusp shapes encode information about low-energy scattering dynamics and nearby resonance states.
