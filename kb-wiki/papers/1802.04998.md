# Interacting hadron resonance gas model in K-matrix formalism

> An extension of Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model is constructed to include interactions using relativistic virial expansion of partition function. The non-interacting part of the expansion contains all the stable baryons and mesons and the interacting part contains all the higher mass resonances which decay into two stable hadrons. The virial coefficients are related to the phase shifts which are calculated using K-matrix formalism in the present work. We have calculated various thermodynamics quantities like pressure, energy density, and entropy density of the system. A comparison of thermodynamic quantities with non interacting HRG model, calculated using the same number of hadrons, shows that the results of above formalism are larger. A good agreement between equation of state calculated in K-matrix formalism and lattice QCD simulations is observed. Specifically the lattice QCD calculated interaction measure is well described in our formalism. We have also calculated second order fluctuations and correlations of conserved charges in K-matrix formalism. We observe a good agreement of second order fluctuations and baryon-strangeness correlation with lattice data below the cross-over temperature.

## Key claim

The paper extends the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model by including interactions via relativistic virial expansion, where the interacting part contains only higher mass resonances that decay into two stable hadrons. The key hedge is that the formalism is limited to two-body elastic scattering channels and does not include repulsive channels or inelastic interactions, which the authors note are needed to fully describe certain observables like baryon-charge and charge-strangeness correlations.

## Method

The authors constructed an interacting HRG model using relativistic virial expansion of the partition function, where the non-interacting part contains all stable baryons and mesons and the interacting part contains higher mass resonances decaying into two stable hadrons. Virial coefficients were related to phase shifts calculated using K-matrix formalism, which preserves unitarity of the S-matrix and handles multiple overlapping resonances. Thermodynamic quantities (pressure, energy density, entropy density) and second-order fluctuations and correlations of conserved charges were computed and compared to lattice QCD data and ideal HRG models.

## Key numbers

- The K-matrix formalism results for thermodynamic quantities are larger than those from the non-interacting HRG model using the same number of hadrons
- For two separated resonances f0(980) and f0(1500), masses are m1=990 MeV with width Gamma1=55 MeV and m2=1505 MeV with width Gamma2=109 MeV
- For two overlapping resonances f0(1370) and f0(1500), masses are m1=1370 MeV with width Gamma1=350 MeV and m2=1505 MeV with width Gamma2=109 MeV
- The cross-over temperature from lattice QCD is around 145-163 MeV, below which all results are meaningful
- Effects of three-body interaction in equation of state are less than 5% in the temperature range considered
- The interaction measure (epsilon-3P)/T^4 is well described in the K-matrix formalism and improved compared to previous studies by inclusion of additional hadrons (Xi, Lambda, Sigma and corresponding resonances)
- Second-order strangeness susceptibility chi_S^2 shows better agreement with lattice data in K-matrix formalism than IDHRG 1 up to cross-over temperature
- Baryon-strangeness correlation chi_BS^11 agrees with lattice data, but chi_BQ^11 and chi_QS^11 are not described satisfactorily

## Context

This work extends the S-matrix approach to hadron resonance gas modeling, building on the relativistic virial expansion formalism of Dashen (1969) and earlier applications of K-matrix formalism by Wiranata (2013) for shear viscosity and interaction measure. The authors improve upon Wiranata (2013) by including additional hadrons (Xi, Lambda, Sigma) and their resonances, achieving better agreement with lattice QCD data from the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration (Borsanyi 2013) and HotQCD collaboration (Bazavov 2014). The work contrasts with excluded volume HRG models (Hagedorn 1980, Rischke 1991) and van der Waals HRG models (Vovchenko 2015) that introduce additional parameters, while the K-matrix approach uses only known resonance masses and widths from the Particle Data Group (2016). Future work is needed to incorporate inelastic collisions and repulsive interactions to improve description of chi_BQ^11 and chi_QS^11.
