# Chiral Extrapolations and the Covariant Small Scale Expansion

> We calculate the nucleon and the delta mass to fourth order in a covariant formulation of the small scale expansion. We analyze lattice data from the MILC collaboration and demonstrate that the available lattice data combined with our knowledge of the physical values for the nucleon and delta masses lead to consistent chiral extrapolation functions for both observables up to fairly large pion masses. This holds in particular for very recent data on the delta mass from the QCDSF collaboration. The resulting pion-nucleon sigma term is sigma_{piN} = 48.9 MeV. This first quantitative analysis of the quark-mass dependence of the structure of the Delta(1232) in full QCD within chiral effective field theory suggests that (the real part of) the nucleon-delta mass-splitting in the chiral limit, Delta_0 = 0.33 GeV, is slightly larger than at the physical point. Further analysis of simultaneous fits to nucleon and delta lattice data are needed for a precision determination of the properties of the first excited state of the nucleon.

# Chiral Extrapolations and the Covariant Small Scale Expansion

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a covariant formulation of the small scale expansion (SSE) at fourth order provides consistent chiral extrapolation functions for both the nucleon and delta masses when fitting MILC lattice data together with physical-point values, but they emphasize that the resulting low-energy constants are indicative rather than precisely determined, and that more refined simultaneous fits to multiple observables are needed.

## Method

The authors calculate the nucleon and delta self-energies to fourth order in the covariant small scale expansion, using an effective Lagrangian with explicit nucleon, delta, and pion fields. They employ a modified spin-3/2 propagator (form B) that eliminates the 1/p^2 structure present in the standard Rarita-Schwinger propagator, simplifying loop calculations in the infrared regularization scheme. The resulting mass formulas are then used to analyze lattice data from the MILC collaboration for both nucleon and delta masses as functions of the pion mass, with low-energy constants constrained partly from pion-nucleon scattering and partly fitted to the lattice data.

## Key numbers

- Pion-nucleon sigma term: sigma_{piN} = 48.9 MeV (to order epsilon^4)
- Nucleon-delta mass splitting in the chiral limit: Delta_0 = 0.33 GeV, slightly larger than at the physical point
- Axial Delta Delta coupling from fit: h_A = 2, compared to the SU(6) value of 9 g_A/5 = 2.28
- Pion-Delta sigma term: sigma_{piDelta} = 20.6 MeV
- MILC lattice data cover pion masses down to M_pi approximately 350 MeV on a lattice with L = 2.6 fm
- Low-energy constants from fit: c_1 = -0.8 GeV^{-1}, c_3 = 0.5 GeV^{-1}, b_3 = 0.75 GeV^{-1}, b_6 = -0.75, a_1 = -0.3 GeV^{-1}, e_1^Delta = -1 GeV^{-3}

## Context

This work extends the covariant formulation of the small scale expansion previously developed by the same authors (Bernard, Hemmert, Meissner, 2004) to fourth order in the nucleon and delta masses, building on the heavy baryon SSE framework of Hemmert, Holstein, and Kambor (1998) and the infrared regularization method of Becher and Leutwyler (1999). The analysis connects lattice data from the MILC collaboration (2004) and recent QCDSF collaboration data for the delta mass with chiral effective field theory predictions, enabling chiral extrapolations for both the nucleon and the Delta(1232) resonance. The results suggest significant SU(6) breaking effects and indicate that more precise determinations will require simultaneous fits to nucleon and delta lattice data combined with constraints from other processes such as pion-nucleon scattering.
