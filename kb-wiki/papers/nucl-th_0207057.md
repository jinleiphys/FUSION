# Single-particle potential in a chiral approach to nuclear matter including short range NN-terms

> We extend a recent chiral approach to nuclear matter of Lutz et al. [Phys. Lett. B474 (2000) 7] by calculating the underlying (complex-valued) single-particle potential U(p,k_f) + i W(p,k_f). The potential for a nucleon at the bottom of the Fermi-sea, U(0,k_{f0})= - 20.0 MeV, comes out as much too weakly attractive in this approach. Even more seriously, the total single-particle energy does not rise monotonically with the nucleon momentum p, implying a negative effective nucleon mass at the Fermi-surface. Also, the imaginary single-particle potential, W(0,k_{f0}) = 51.1 MeV, is too large. More realistic single-particle properties together with a good nuclear matter equation of state can be obtained if the short range contributions of non-pionic origin are treated in mean-field approximation (i.e. if they are not further iterated with 1pi-exchange). We also consider the equation of state of pure neutron matter $bar E_n(k_n)$ and the asymmetry energy A(k_f) in that approach. The downward bending of these quantities above nuclear matter saturation density seems to be a generic feature of perturbative chiral pion-nucleon dynamics.

# Single-particle potential in a chiral approach to nuclear matter including short range NN-terms

## Key claim

The chiral approach to nuclear matter of Lutz et al. (2000) produces a single-particle potential that is much too weakly attractive (U(0,k_f0) = -20.0 MeV) and yields a total single-particle energy that does not rise monotonically with nucleon momentum, implying a negative effective nucleon mass at the Fermi-surface; these pathological features can be avoided if short-range NN-contact interactions of non-pionic origin are treated in mean-field approximation rather than iterated with 1π-exchange.

## Method

The authors extended the chiral perturbation theory calculation of Lutz et al. (2000) for nuclear matter by computing the complex-valued single-particle potential U(p,k_f) + iW(p,k_f) in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter, as well as the neutron matter equation of state and asymmetry energy. They evaluated contributions from 1π-exchange Fock diagrams, iterated 1π-exchange Hartree and Fock diagrams, and NN-contact interactions parameterized by coefficients γ and γ_n, comparing the full iterative treatment against a mean-field approximation where the contact interactions are not further iterated with pion exchange.

## Key numbers

- Real single-particle potential at bottom of Fermi-sea in Lutz approach: U(0,k_f0) = -20.0 MeV (at saturation density k_f0 = 250.1 MeV)
- Imaginary single-particle potential at bottom of Fermi-sea in Lutz approach: W(0,k_f0) = 51.1 MeV
- Real single-particle potential at bottom of Fermi-sea in mean-field treatment: U(0,k_f0) = -54.8 MeV (at saturation density k_f0 = 270.3 MeV)
- Imaginary single-particle potential at bottom of Fermi-sea in mean-field treatment: W(0,k_f0) = 28.4 MeV
- Effective nucleon mass at Fermi-surface in Lutz approach: M*(k_f0) ≈ -3.5 M
- Effective nucleon mass at Fermi-surface in mean-field treatment: M*(k_f0) ≈ 2.9 M
- Saturation density in Lutz approach: ρ_0 = 0.138 fm^{-3} (k_f0 = 250.1 MeV)
- Saturation density in mean-field treatment: ρ_0 = 0.174 fm^{-3} (k_f0 = 270.3 MeV)
- Nuclear matter compressibility in Lutz approach: K = 202 MeV
- Nuclear matter compressibility in mean-field treatment: K = 253 MeV
- Asymmetry energy at saturation: A(k_f0) = 33.2 MeV (adjusted to empirical value)
- Coefficient γ in Lutz approach: γ = 4.086 (adjusted to binding energy -15.3 MeV)
- Coefficient γ in mean-field treatment: γ = 6.198

## Context

This work extends the chiral approach to nuclear matter of Lutz et al. (2000) by computing single-particle properties, and contrasts with the Munich group's earlier work (Nucmat, Pot) which used a momentum cut-off Λ ≈ 0.65 GeV and obtained good results for the asymmetry energy (33.8 MeV) and single-particle potential depth (-53.2 MeV). The authors find that the Lutz et al. scheme produces unrealistic single-particle properties, and propose that short-range NN-contact interactions should be kept at mean-field level rather than iterated with pion exchange. The downward bending of neutron matter energy and asymmetry energy above saturation density is identified as a generic feature of perturbative chiral πN-dynamics truncated at three-loop order, pointing to the need for more elaborate effective field theory calculations of nuclear matter.
