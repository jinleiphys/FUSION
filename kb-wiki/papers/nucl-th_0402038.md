# Density Perturbations in Heavy-Ion Collisions below the Critical Point

> Heavy ion collisions at large baryon density may exhibit a first order phase transition from a chirally symmetric phase to the symmetry broken ground state. This should then lead to large density inhomogeneities, which affect the relative hadron multiplicities.

## Key claim

Heavy-ion collisions at beam energies below the QCD critical endpoint may produce large density inhomogeneities if the system passes through a first-order chiral phase transition, and these inhomogeneities could affect observable hadron multiplicities and collective flow even though they are too small in scale to be resolved in rapidity space.

## Method

The authors couple a relativistic fluid of quarks to the non-equilibrium dynamics of long-wavelength classical modes of the chiral condensate via the equations of motion ∂_μ∂^μ φ + ∂V_eff/∂φ = 0 and ∂_μ (T_fl^μν + T_φ^μν) = 0. They introduce primordial Gaussian fluctuations of the condensate φ on length scales ~1 fm on top of a smoothly varying mean field, then propagate these fluctuations through a first-order chiral phase transition at vanishing baryon density, where the nature of the transition is determined by the effective quark-field coupling rather than the baryon-chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- The QCD critical point for 2+1 quark flavors has been located at T = 160 MeV and μ_B = 725 MeV (Fodor 2001), though a reliable extrapolation to the continuum limit and physical pion mass has not been attempted.
- Primordial fluctuations of the condensate are introduced on length scales ~1 fm.
- Observable consequences require a rapidity resolution Δy < 1, which is about the thermal width of the local particle momentum distributions.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of density inhomogeneities from the QCD transition in the early universe (BBN) to the heavy-ion collision context, where such inhomogeneities might affect rare hadron abundances (Back 2001, NA49) and collective flow. The authors build on their own coupled fluid-chiral condensate model (Paech 2003) and on universality arguments for the chiral phase transition (Pisarski and Wilczek) and predictions of a first-order transition at large baryon density (various low-energy effective theories). The results enable experimental searches for the QCD critical point by varying beam energy to switch between first-order transition and crossover regimes.
