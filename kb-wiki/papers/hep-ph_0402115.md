# QCD phase diagram and the critical point

> The recent progress in understanding the QCD phase diagram and the physics of the QCD critical point is reviewed.

## Key claim

The QCD phase diagram likely contains a critical point (the endpoint of a first-order phase transition line), but this conclusion relies on an argument that assumes the transition at zero baryon chemical potential is a crossover (established by lattice QCD) and the transition at zero temperature is first order (indicated by models but not yet proven by first-principles calculations). The existence of the critical point is therefore a plausible theoretical expectation rather than a rigorously established fact.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical arguments for the existence of a QCD critical point based on the mismatch between the crossover at zero baryochemical potential (established by lattice Monte Carlo simulations) and the expected first-order transition at zero temperature (inferred from model calculations such as Nambu–Jona-Lasinio models, random matrix models, and linear sigma models). It surveys lattice reweighting methods (the "Glasgow method") and newer approaches using imaginary chemical potential or Taylor expansions, as well as model predictions for the critical point's location. The review also discusses how heavy-ion collision experiments could search for the critical point via event-by-event fluctuations of conserved quantities.

## Key numbers

* The crossover at zero baryochemical potential occurs at a temperature of order 100 MeV, commensurate with the QCD dynamical scale.
* Model predictions for the critical point location vary widely: e.g., Asakawa and Yazaki (1989) give (T = 40 MeV, μ_B = 1050 MeV) for one NJL case; Berges and Rajagopal (1998) give a tricritical point at (T = 101 MeV, μ_B = 633 MeV); Fodor and Katz (2001) give (T = 160 MeV, μ_B = 725 MeV) from lattice reweighting; Antoniou and Kapoyannis (2002) give (T = 171 MeV, μ_B = 385 MeV) from hadronic bootstrap.
* The critical exponent η for the Ising universality class (to which the QCD critical point belongs) is approximately 0.04.
* The baryon number diffusion rate vanishes at the critical point as D ~ ξ^{-x_D} with exponent x_D ≈ 1.
* The water-steam critical point occurs at pressure p = 218 atm and T = 374°C, used as an analogy.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on the QCD phase diagram initiated by lattice calculations at zero chemical potential (Columbia group, 1990s) and by model studies of finite-density QCD (Asakawa and Yazaki, 1989; Barducci et al., 1989-1994; Berges and Rajagopal, 1998; Halasz et al., 1998). It synthesizes theoretical predictions for the critical point's location and discusses how heavy-ion collision experiments (e.g., at SPS and RHIC) could search for it via non-monotonous behavior of fluctuation observables as a function of collision energy. The review enables future experimental searches by identifying specific signatures (e.g., divergent baryon number susceptibility, critical slowing down) and by highlighting the need for new lattice methods to overcome the sign problem.
