# Prompt ${J/ψ}$-pair production at the LHC: impact of loop-induced contributions and of the colour-octet mechanism

> Prompt double-$J/ψ$ production at high-energy hadron colliders can be considered as a golden channel to probe double parton scatterings (DPS) --in particular to study gluon-gluon correlations inside the proton-- and, at the same time, to measure the distribution of linearly-polarised gluons inside the proton. Such studies however require a good control of both single and DPS in the respective regions where they are carried out. In this context, we have critically examined two mechanisms of single parton scatterings (SPS) that may be kinematically enhanced where DPS are thought to be dominant, even though they are either at higher orders in the strong-coupling or velocity expansion. First, we have considered a gauge-invariant and infrared-safe subset of the loop-induced contribution via Colour-Singlet (CS) transitions. We have found it to become the leading CS SPS contributions at large rapidity separation, yet too small to account for the data without invoking the presence of DPS yields. Second, we have surveyed the possible Colour-Octet (CO) contributions using both old and up-to-date non-perturbative long distance matrix elements (LDMEs). We have found that the pure CO yields crucially depend on the LDMEs. Among all the LDMEs we used, only two result into a visible modification of the NRQCD (CS+CO) yield, but only in two kinematical distributions measured by ATLAS, those of the rapidity separation and of the pair invariant mass. These modifications however do not impact the control region used for their DPS study.

## Key claim

The loop-induced colour-singlet contributions become the leading colour-singlet single parton scattering contributions at large rapidity separation, yet remain too small to explain the data without invoking double parton scatterings; the colour-octet contributions depend crucially on the long-distance matrix elements used, and only two of the tested LDME sets produce a visible modification of the NRQCD yield, and only in two kinematical distributions measured by ATLAS.

## Method

The authors computed a gauge-invariant and infrared-safe subset of loop-induced contributions to prompt double-J/ψ production via colour-singlet transitions, implemented in the HELAC-Onia code. They also performed a comprehensive survey of colour-octet contributions using nine different sets of non-perturbative long-distance matrix elements from the literature, categorized by the QCD accuracy of their short-distance coefficients (LO, NLO, leading-logarithm resummed, and leading-power fragmentation matched to NLO). The theoretical predictions were compared to CMS data at 7 TeV, ATLAS data at 8 TeV, and LHCb data at 13 TeV, with an empirical intrinsic kT smearing of 3.0 GeV applied to account for non-perturbative transverse-momentum effects.

## Key numbers

* The loop-induced colour-singlet contribution becomes significant at rapidity separations |Δy(J/ψ,J/ψ)| ≥ 3.0, where it enhances the colour-singlet cross section by about an order of magnitude.
* The charm quark mass was fixed to 1.5 GeV, and the central scale μ0 was chosen as μ0 = √(PT(J/ψ)² + (4 mc)²).
* The J/ψ radial wavefunction at the origin was taken as |R^(J/ψ)(0)|² = 0.8 GeV³, and for ψ(2S) as |R^(ψ(2S))(0)|² = 0.5 GeV³, derived from the Buchmüller-and-Tye potential.
* The feed-down contribution from ψ(2S) decays to the double J/ψ yield was taken to be equal to 2.
* The intrinsic kT smearing parameter was fixed to ⟨kT⟩ = 3.0 GeV.
* The PDF dependence was checked to be less than 20%, a minor source of uncertainty compared to the dominant scale uncertainty.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of double-J/ψ production at hadron colliders, particularly the NLO* colour-singlet calculations by Lansberg et al. (2013, 2014) and the colour-octet analysis by He and Kniehl (2015) which used only a single out-of-date LDME set from Braaten et al. (1999). The loop-induced analysis follows the methodology of Shao et al. (2016) for J/ψ+Υ production. The results confirm that double parton scatterings are required to describe the data at large rapidity separation and high pair invariant mass, and demonstrate that the colour-octet contributions do not affect the control region used for DPS studies by ATLAS. The work enables more reliable extraction of gluon-gluon correlations inside the proton and measurements of linearly-polarised gluon distributions.
