# Near threshold Lambda and Sigma production in pp collisions

> A model calculation for the reactions $pp\to p╬ЫK^+$ and $pp\to N╬гK$ near their thresholds is presented. It is argued that the experimentally observed strong suppression of $╬г^0$ production compared to $╬Ы$ production at the same excess energy could be due to a destructive interference between the $╧А$ and $K$ exchange contributions in the reaction $pp\to p╬г^0 K^+$. Predictions for $pp\to p╬г^+ K^0$ and $pp\to n╬г^+ K^+$ are given.

## Key claim

The experimentally observed strong suppression of Sigma0 production relative to Lambda production in near-threshold pp collisions (a factor of about 30) could be explained by destructive interference between pion and kaon exchange contributions in the reaction pp -> p Sigma0 K+, rather than by Sigma N to Lambda N conversion effects from the final state interaction alone.

## Method

The authors performed a distorted wave Born approximation calculation for pp -> p Lambda K+ and pp -> N Sigma K, using pi and K exchange as the production mechanisms. The YN final state interaction was included via a microscopic meson-exchange model (Juelich model A) that couples the Lambda N and Sigma N channels. The elementary KN and pi N -> K Y amplitudes were taken from existing microscopic models but approximated by on-shell threshold amplitudes, with off-shell extrapolation using the same form factors as at the meson-exchange vertices.

## Key numbers

- At excess energy Q = 13.2 MeV for Lambda and Q = 13.0 MeV for Sigma0, the experimental cross sections are sigma_Lambda = 505 +/- 33 nb and sigma_Sigma0 = 20.1 +/- 3.0 nb, giving a ratio sigma_Lambda/sigma_Sigma0 = 25 +/- 6
- With only K exchange including FSI, the calculated ratio is 43 (sigma_Lambda = 2426 nb, sigma_Sigma0 = 57 nb)
- With only pi exchange including FSI, the calculated ratio is 1.1 (sigma_Lambda = 113 nb, sigma_Sigma0 = 105 nb)
- With both pi and K exchange including FSI and destructive interference ("K - pi" choice), the calculated ratio is 36 (sigma_Lambda = 2607 nb, sigma_Sigma0 = 73 nb)
- For pp -> n Sigma+ K+ at 13 MeV excess energy, the predicted cross section is 229 nb for the "K - pi" choice and 86 nb for the "K + pi" choice
- For pp -> p Sigma+ K0 at 13 MeV excess energy, the predicted cross section is 423 nb for the "K - pi" choice and 725 nb for the "K + pi" choice

## Context

This work extends the experimental findings of Sewerin et al. (1999), who first reported the factor-of-30 suppression of Sigma0 production relative to Lambda production near threshold. The authors test the conversion mechanism hypothesis (Sigma N -> Lambda N via FSI) suggested in that experimental paper, but find it insufficient. The calculation instead points to destructive interference between pi and K exchange as the dominant explanation. The predictions for pp -> n Sigma+ K+ and pp -> p Sigma+ K0 provide testable signatures that could distinguish between the constructive and destructive interference scenarios, and the authors note that the "K - pi" choice favored by the Sigma0 data yields cross sections more consistent with existing higher-energy data from Laget (1994).
