# Hadron rapidity spectra within a hybrid model

> A 2-stage hybrid model is proposed that joins the fast initial state of interaction, described by the hadron string dynamics (HSD) model, to subsequent evolution of the expanding system at the second stage, treated within ideal hydrodynamics. The developed hybrid model is assigned to describe heavy-ion collisions in the energy range of the NICA collider under construction in Dubna. Generally, the model is in reasonable agreement with the available data on proton rapidity spectra. However, reproducing proton rapidity spectra, our hybrid model cannot describe the rapidity distributions of pions. The model should be improved by taking into consideration viscosity effects at the hydrodynamical stage of system evolution.

## Key claim

The proposed 2-stage hybrid model, combining hadron string dynamics (HSD) with ideal hydrodynamics, can reproduce proton rapidity spectra reasonably well but **cannot** describe the rapidity distributions of pions; the authors state the model must be improved by including viscosity effects at the hydrodynamic stage.

## Method

The authors first simulate the initial nonequilibrium stage of heavy-ion collisions using the hadron string dynamics (HSD) kinetic model, then transition to an ideal (non-viscous) 3+1 dimensional hydrodynamic description at a time \(t_{\rm start}\) determined by the flattening of the entropy-to-baryon-number ratio \(S/N_B\). The hydrodynamic evolution uses the SHASTA algorithm with a hadron-gas equation of state including a sigma meson, and observables are computed via a Monte-Carlo particlization procedure (Cooper-Frye formalism) with three freeze-out scenarios: isochronous, isothermal, and isoenergetic.

## Key numbers

- Calculations are performed for Au+Au collisions at \(E_{\rm lab} = 6\) and \(10.7\ A\text{GeV}\) (AGS energies) and for Pb+Pb at \(E_{\rm lab} = 40\ A\text{GeV}\) (SPS energy).
- The transition time to hydrodynamics is estimated as \(t_{\rm start} = 2R / (\gamma v) = 2R \sqrt{2 m_N / E_{\rm lab}}\), with nuclear radius \(R = r_0 A^{1/3}\), \(r_0 = 1.124\ \text{fm}\).
- The initial state averaging uses \(5 \times 10^4\) events.
- The numerical grid uses cell size \(\mathrm{d}x = 0.2\ \text{fm}\) and time step parameter \(\lambda = \mathrm{d}t / \mathrm{d}x = 0.4\).
- For the isothermal freeze-out scenario, the best agreement for protons is achieved using freeze-out temperatures \(T_{\rm frz} = 86^{+13}_{-3}\ \text{MeV}\) (at \(E_{\rm lab}=6\ A\text{GeV}\)), \(124 \pm 7\ \text{MeV}\) (at \(10.7\ A\text{GeV}\)), and \(156 \pm 11\ \text{MeV}\) (at \(40\ A\text{GeV}\)), taken from the statistical model of Andronic.
- For the isoenergetic freeze-out scenario at \(E_{\rm lab}=40\ A\text{GeV}\), a freeze-out energy density \(\varepsilon_{\rm frz} = 200\ \text{MeV/fm}^3\) is used.

## Context

This work extends the hybrid hydro+UrQMD approach (Petersen et al.) to the moderate collision energy range (\(\sqrt{s} \lesssim 10\) GeV) relevant for the NICA collider under construction in Dubna. The model is contrasted with the three-fluid hydrodynamics model (Ivanov, Toneev, et al., 2006), which successfully describes a variety of experimental data in this energy range by incorporating dissipation and entropy generation. The failure to reproduce pion spectra points to the need for viscous hydrodynamics or a multi-fluid approach, and the results enable future studies of the hadron-quark phase transition by investigating irregularities in proton rapidity spectra.
