# Tomography of the Quark-Gluon-Plasma by Charm Quarks

> We study charm production in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions by using the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach. The initial charm quarks are produced by the Pythia event generator tuned to fit the transverse momentum spectrum and rapidity distribution of charm quarks from Fixed-Order Next-to-Leading Logarithm (FONLL) calculations. The produced charm quarks scatter in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) with the off-shell partons whose masses and widths are given by the Dynamical Quasi-Particle Model (DQPM) which reproduces the lattice QCD equation-of-state in thermal equilibrium. The relevant cross section are calculated in a consistent way by employing the effective propagators and couplings from the DQPM. Close to the critical energy density of the phase transition, the charm quarks are hadronized into $D$ mesons through coalescence and/or fragmentation depending on transverse momentum. The hadronized $D$ mesons then interact with the various hadrons in the hadronic phase with cross sections calculated in an effective lagrangian approach with heavy-quark spin symmetry. Finally, the nuclear modification factor $\rm R_{AA}$ and the elliptic flow $v_2$ of $D^0$ mesons from PHSD are compared with the experimental data from the STAR Collaboration for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ =200 GeV. We find that in the PHSD the energy loss of $D$ mesons at high $p_T$ can be dominantly attributed to partonic scattering while the actual shape of $\rm R_{AA}$ versus $p_T$ reflects the heavy quark hadronization scenario, i.e. coalescence versus fragmentation. Also the hadronic rescattering is important for the $\rm R_{AA}$ at low $p_T$ and enhances the $D$-meson elliptic flow $v_2$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the energy loss of D mesons at high transverse momentum in Au+Au collisions at RHIC can be dominantly attributed to partonic scattering in the quark-gluon plasma, while the shape of the nuclear modification factor versus transverse momentum reflects the heavy-quark hadronization scenario (coalescence versus fragmentation), and hadronic rescattering is important for the nuclear modification factor at low transverse momentum and enhances the D-meson elliptic flow.

## Method

The authors used the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, which incorporates explicit partonic degrees-of-freedom as strongly interacting quasiparticles with masses and widths from the Dynamical Quasi-Particle Model (DQPM) that reproduces lattice QCD thermodynamics. Initial charm quarks were produced by the Pythia event generator tuned to match Fixed-Order Next-to-Leading Logarithm (FONLL) calculations, and their elastic scattering cross sections with off-shell partons were calculated using effective propagators and couplings from the DQPM. Charm quarks were hadronized into D mesons through coalescence and fragmentation depending on transverse momentum, and the resulting D mesons interacted with hadrons via cross sections from an effective Lagrangian approach with heavy-quark spin symmetry.

## Key numbers

- The charm quark mass was taken to be 1.5 GeV throughout the study.
- The critical temperature for the phase transition was taken to be T_c = 0.158 GeV.
- In 0-10 percent central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, about 19 pairs of charm and anticharm quarks are produced, and each charm or anticharm quark experiences on average 6 elastic scatterings with partons before hadronization.
- The total number of charm and anticharm quark scatterings with light quarks and antiquarks is about 135, and with gluons about 83, in 0-10 percent central Au+Au collisions.
- The elastic cross section for charm quark and gluon scattering is about twice (9/4, the ratio of color Casimir operators squared) that for charm quark and light quark scattering.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of charm dynamics in heavy-ion collisions by using a consistent microscopic transport approach (PHSD) that includes non-equilibrium off-shell parton dynamics, dynamical hadronization, and hadronic rescattering, in contrast to earlier models that used Fokker-Planck equations with QGP hydrodynamics or Boltzmann-type partonic cascades with massless light quarks. The approach differs from prior work by Gossiaux (2010, 2012), Cao (2013), and Das (2013, 2015) by employing massive strongly interacting quasiparticles rather than massless pQCD partons, and by including D-meson rescattering in the hadronic phase using cross sections from Tolos (2013). The results enable a more complete tomographic study of the quark-gluon plasma using charm quarks as probes, and provide a framework for comparing with experimental data from the STAR Collaboration at RHIC and future measurements at LHC energies.
