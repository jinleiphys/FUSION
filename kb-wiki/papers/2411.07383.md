# Heavy quark potential and thermal charm production in heavy-ion collisions

> Heavy quark mass in QGP is related to the heavy quark potential at a large distance. In this study we test three different heavy quark potentials, namely, the free energy, the internal energy of the heavy quark pair in QGP, and the unscreened potential, which was recently proposed by the HotQCD Collaboration, through the thermal production of charm quarks in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC. We find that the free energy potential overestimates charm production in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC, while the unscreened potential produces results closest to the experimental data from the ALICE Collaboration among the three potentials.

## Key claim

The effective charm quark mass in QGP, which controls the thermal production rate of charm quarks, is related to the heavy quark potential at large distance; the unscreened potential recently proposed by the HotQCD Collaboration produces results closest to ALICE experimental data among the three tested potentials, but the study only tests the potential in the long-distance range and does not confirm it in all spatial ranges.

## Method

The authors calculated thermal charm production cross sections from quark-antiquark annihilation and two-gluon fusion using the dynamical quasi-particle model (DQPM), which reproduces the equation of state from lattice QCD and the spatial diffusion coefficients of heavy quarks. They then implemented these processes in the parton-hadron-string dynamics (PHSD) transport approach to simulate Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies, testing three different heavy quark potentials: the free energy, the internal energy, and the unscreened potential from the HotQCD Collaboration. The effective charm quark mass was derived from each potential via the relation m(T) = m_0 + (1/2) lim_{r→∞} V(r,T), and the resulting D meson nuclear modification factor R_AA was compared to ALICE data.

## Key numbers

- The effective charm quark mass from the unscreened potential is taken as a constant 1.8 GeV, based on a recent study matching charm density at T_c using the statistical model and quasi-particle model.
- The numbers of thermally produced charm quark pairs in mid-rapidity for 0-10% central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV are: 6.4 (free energy), 2.4 (internal energy), and 0.6 (unscreened potential).
- The numbers of annihilated charm quark pairs are 0.5, 0.3, and 0.2 for the free energy, internal energy, and unscreened potential, respectively.
- Assuming dσ_{c\bar{c}}^{NN}/dy = 1.165 mb at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV and average nuclear overlap function T_AA = 23.3 mb^{-1} in 0-10% central collisions, the expected number of charm pairs from initial hard scattering is roughly 27.1 per rapidity.
- The thermal production enhances charm by 23% (free energy), 9% (internal energy), and 2% (unscreened potential) relative to initial production.
- If the effective charm quark mass changes from 1.2 GeV to 1.8 GeV, the production rate decreases by three orders of magnitude at T_c and by a factor of 10 at 3 T_c.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Song et al. (2024) on thermal charm production using the DQPM, which found that thermal production overestimates experimental R_AA data when assuming the bare charm quark mass. It also builds on the recent HotQCD Collaboration result (Bazavov et al., 2023) that the real part of the heavy quark potential shows no screening up to T ≈ 400 MeV, contrary to earlier expectations. The study tests three potential models, the free energy (Kaczmarek et al., 2003), the internal energy (Satz, 2005), and the unscreened potential, and finds that only the unscreened potential yields results consistent with ALICE data, ruling out the free energy as the heavy quark potential at large distances. This provides constraints for understanding quarkonium dissociation in heavy-ion collisions and for future lattice QCD studies of the heavy quark potential.
