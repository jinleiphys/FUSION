# Electromagnetic mean squared radii of Lambda(1405) in chiral dynamics

> The electromagnetic mean squared radii, <r^2>_E and <r^2>_M, of Lambda(1405) are calculated in the chiral unitary model. We describe the excited baryons as dynamically generated resonances in the octet meson and octet baryon scattering. We evaluate values of <r^2>_E and <r^2>_M for the Lambda(1405) on the resonance pole and obtain their complex values. We also consider Lambda(1405) obtained by neglecting decay channels. For the latter case, we obtain negative and larger absolute electric mean squared radius than that of typical ground state baryons. This implies that Lambda(1405) has structure that K^- is widely spread around p.

## Key claim

The electromagnetic mean squared radii of the Lambda(1405) are calculated in the chiral unitary model, yielding complex values at the resonance poles; the negative and larger absolute electric mean squared radius obtained when decay channels are neglected implies that the Lambda(1405) has a structure where K- is widely spread around a proton, but this interpretation depends on the specific pole considered and the binding energy.

## Method

The authors describe the Lambda(1405) as a dynamically generated resonance in s-wave octet meson-octet baryon scattering using the Bethe-Salpeter equation with the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction kernel from chiral perturbation theory. They evaluate the electromagnetic form factors by computing the scattering amplitude for the MB gamma* -> M'B' process, attaching photons to constituent mesons and baryons in a gauge-invariant manner, and extracting the matrix elements as residues at the resonance pole in the complex energy plane.

## Key numbers

- Two poles are found for the Lambda(1405): z1 = 1390 - 66i MeV and z2 = 1426 - 17i MeV
- For the higher pole z2: electric mean squared radius <r^2>_E = -0.131 + 0.303i fm^2; magnetic mean squared radius <r^2>_M = 0.267 - 0.407i fm^2
- For the lower pole z1: <r^2>_E = (1.8 - 0.2i) x 10^-2 fm^2; <r^2>_M = (-1.3 + 2.1i) x 10^-2 fm^2
- For the KbarN bound state (no decay channels): <r^2>_E = -2.193 fm^2; <r^2>_M = 1.972 fm^2; electric root mean squared radius sqrt(|<r^2>_E|) ~ 1.48 fm
- For Lambda(1405) without pi Sigma decay channel: <r^2>_E = -0.519 - 0.008i fm^2; <r^2>_M = 0.683 - 0.023i fm^2; pole at 1422.34 - 0.02i MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary model description of the Lambda(1405) as a dynamically generated resonance, previously developed by Kaiser (1995), Oset (1997), Oller (2000), and Jido (2003), to calculate electromagnetic properties. The results enable comparison with photon-induced production experiments and provide theoretical predictions for the size of the Lambda(1405) that can be contrasted with estimates from single-channel KbarN potential models by Yamazaki (2007) and Dote (2008), which gave relative mean distances of about 1.36 fm and 1.8 fm respectively. The finding that the higher pole z2 has a larger absolute electric mean squared radius than the neutron (~0.12 fm^2) supports the molecular picture of the Lambda(1405) as a meson-baryon quasi-bound state.
