# Diffractive dijet production in impact parameter dependent saturation models

> We study coherent diffractive dijet production in electron-hadron and electron-nucleus collisions within the dipole picture. We provide semi-analytic results for the differential cross section and elliptic anisotropy in the angle between dijet transverse momentum and hadron recoil momentum. We demonstrate the direct relation between angular moments of the dipole amplitude in coordinate space and angular moments of the diffractive dijet cross sections. To perform explicit calculations we employ two different saturation models, extended to include the target geometry. In the limit of large photon virtuality or quark masses, we find fully analytic results that allow direct insight into how the differential cross section and elliptic anisotropy depend on the saturation scale, target geometry, and kinematic variables. We further provide numerical results for more general kinematics in collisions at a future electron-ion collider, and study the effects of approaching the saturated regime on diffractive dijet observables.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that angular moments of the diffractive dijet cross section are directly related to angular moments of the dipole amplitude in coordinate space, and that in the limit of large photon virtuality or quark masses, fully analytic expressions can be obtained that reveal how the cross section and elliptic anisotropy depend on the saturation scale, target geometry, and kinematic variables, though these analytic results rely on the simplifying assumption that the saturation scale is much smaller than the effective scale set by the photon virtuality or quark mass.

## Method

The authors study coherent diffractive dijet production in electron-hadron and electron-nucleus collisions within the dipole picture of the Color Glass Condensate framework, using the eikonal approximation for dipole-target scattering. They decompose the dipole amplitude into Fourier modes in the angle between the dipole orientation and impact parameter vectors, and derive semi-analytic expressions connecting these modes to the corresponding Fourier modes of the differential cross section and elliptic anisotropy. Explicit calculations are performed using two saturation models extended to include target geometry: the Golec-Biernat Wusthoff (GBW) model with added angular correlations, and the impact parameter dependent McLerran Venugopalan (IP-MV) model where angular correlations arise from spatial gradients of the color charge density.

## Key numbers

- Saturation scale \(Q_s\) is the characteristic scale of the target's color fields, which grows with decreasing Bjorken-\(x\)
- For longitudinally polarized photons, the differential cross section develops a dip at \(P = \varepsilon_f\), where \(\varepsilon_f^2 = z_0 z_1 Q^2 + m_f^2\) (with \(z_0, z_1\) the longitudinal momentum fractions of quark and antiquark, \(Q^2\) the photon virtuality, and \(m_f\) the quark mass)
- In the large \(Q^2\) or large \(m_f\) limit (\(Q_s \ll \varepsilon_f\)), both the differential cross section and elliptic anisotropy scale as \(Q_s^4\) in the GBW model
- The elliptic anisotropy coefficient for longitudinally polarized photons is proportional to \(-2c \frac{(P^2 - \varepsilon_f^2)P^2}{(P^2 + \varepsilon_f^2)^6} \tilde{T}(\Delta) \tilde{T}_2(\Delta)\), where \(c\) is the strength of angular correlations (\(-1 < c < 1\)), \(\tilde{T}(\Delta)\) is the Fourier transform of the transverse profile, and \(\tilde{T}_2(\Delta)\) is its 2nd order Hankel transform
- For transversely polarized photons, the elliptic anisotropy is proportional to \((\varepsilon_f^2 \zeta^2 - m_f^2) \times 2c \frac{(P^2 - \varepsilon_f^2)P^2}{(P^2 + \varepsilon_f^2)^6} \tilde{T}(\Delta) \tilde{T}_2(\Delta)\), where \(\zeta^2 = z_0^2 + z_1^2\)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of diffractive dijet production in the dipole picture by Altinoluk et al. (2015) and Mantysaari et al. (2019), which found interesting structures such as diffractive dips and complex angular behavior. The paper provides analytic insight into how target geometry and saturation effects produce these features, building on the relation between diffractive dijet cross sections and the 5-dimensional gluon Wigner distribution established by Hatta et al. (2016) and Hagiwara et al. (2017). The results are intended to guide future experiments at a future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC), where the spatial and momentum structure of nuclear targets can be probed with unprecedented precision.
