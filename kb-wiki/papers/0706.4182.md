# Experimental tests of small-x QCD

> Current and future experimental studies of the high-energy limit of QCD, dominated by non-linear gluon dynamics in the low-x sector of the hadron wavefunctions, are presented. Results at HERA (proton) and RHIC (nucleus) pointing to the possible onset of parton saturation phenomena, and perspectives at the LHC and new proposed DIS facilities are outlined.

## Key claim

Current experimental results from HERA (proton) and RHIC (nucleus) provide hints of parton saturation phenomena, but no strong deviation from linear QCD evolution equations has been conclusively observed in the perturbative kinematical range covered at HERA; the saturation scale at HERA energies is in a regime of relatively low virtualities (Q_s^2 ≈ 1 GeV^2), blurring the interpretation of the truly low-x range probed.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental tests of small-x QCD by analyzing inclusive and diffractive deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) data from HERA (e-p collisions at √s = 320 GeV) and hadron production data from RHIC (Au-Au and d-Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV). It compares these data to predictions from linear DGLAP/BFKL evolution equations and from non-linear saturation models (Color Glass Condensate, BK/JIMWLK evolution). The review also outlines future measurement capabilities at the LHC and proposed DIS facilities (LHeC, EIC/eRHIC).

## Key numbers

- Saturation scale at HERA: Q_s^2 ≈ 1 GeV^2 for the proton
- Geometric scaling parameter λ ≈ 0.3, with Q_s(x) = Q_0(x/x_0)^λ, Q_0 = 1 GeV, x_0 ≈ 3·10^{-4}
- Diffractive scattering accounts for 10–15% of the total DIS cross-section at HERA
- RHIC Au-Au hadron multiplicity: dN_ch/dη|_(η=0) ≈ 700, compared to ≈ 1000 predicted by minijet or Regge models
- BRAHMS nuclear modification factor R_dAu ≈ 0.8 for semi-hard hadrons (p_T ≈ 2–4 GeV/c) at η ≈ 3.2 in d-Au at √s_NN = 200 GeV
- LHC collision energies: p-p at √s = 14 TeV, p-Pb at √s_NN = 8.8 TeV, Pb-Pb at √s_NN = 5.5 TeV
- LHC saturation momentum: Q_s^2 ≈ 1 GeV^2 (proton) to 5 GeV^2 (Pb)
- CGC prediction for charged hadron multiplicity in central Pb-Pb at 5.5 TeV: dN_ch/dη ≈ 1500
- LHeC e-A collision energy: √s_eN = 880 GeV (with E_e = 70 GeV)
- EIC/eRHIC e-A collision energy: √s_eN = 63 GeV (with E_e = 10 GeV)

## Context

This review extends the discussion of parton saturation from the theoretical framework of Gribov, Levin, Ryskin (1983) and Mueller, Qiu (1986) to experimental tests at HERA and RHIC, and outlines future tests at the LHC and proposed facilities. The HERA results on geometric scaling (Stasto, Golec-Biernat, Wusthoff, 2001) and the longitudinal structure function (Martin, Stirling, Thorne, 2006) show tension with DGLAP fits, while RHIC hadron multiplicity data (PHOBOS, PHENIX, STAR, BRAHMS) and forward suppression (BRAHMS) favor CGC approaches over pQCD calculations (Guzey, 2004; Accardi, 2004). The review enables planning for low-x QCD measurements at the LHC and motivates the construction of LHeC and EIC/eRHIC to map the saturation region at Q_s^2 ≈ 1–10 GeV^2.
