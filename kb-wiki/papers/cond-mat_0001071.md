# Generalized symmetric nonextensive thermostatistics and q-modified structures

> We formulate a convenient generalization of the q-expectation value, based on the analogy of the symmetric quantum groups and q-calculus, and show that the q->q^{-1} symmetric nonextensive entropy preserves all of the mathematical structure of thermodynamics just as in the case of non-symmetric Tsallis statistics. Basic properties and analogies with quantum groups are discussed.

# Generalized symmetric nonextensive thermostatistics and q-modified structures

## Key claim

The authors show that a $q \leftrightarrow q^{-1}$ symmetric generalization of Tsallis entropy, combined with a correspondingly generalized $q$-expectation value, preserves all standard thermodynamic relations (including Legendre transformations), a result that Abe (1999) could not achieve because he did not extend the expectation value definition to the symmetric case.

## Method

The authors construct a symmetric Tsallis entropy $S_q^S$ using the symmetric Jackson derivative, then define a symmetric internal energy $U_q^S$ and symmetric $q$-expectation value $\langle A \rangle_q^S$ as linear combinations of the nonsymmetric Tsallis quantities with coefficients $c_1 = (q-1)/(q-q^{-1})$ and $c_2 = (1-q^{-1})/(q-q^{-1})$. They verify that the symmetric partition function $\ln_q^S Z_q$ satisfies $U_q^S = -\partial (\ln_q^S Z_q)/\partial\beta$ and that the Legendre transform yields $S_q^S = \beta U_q^S + \ln_q^S Z_q$, establishing the standard thermodynamic relation $\partial S_q^S/\partial U_q^S = 1/T$.

## Key numbers

- The symmetric entropy is defined as $S_q^S = -\sum_i (p_i^q - p_i^{q^{-1}})/(q - q^{-1})$, reducing to Boltzmann-Gibbs entropy in the limit $q \rightarrow 1$.
- The symmetric internal energy is $U_q^S = \sum_i \epsilon_i \; [(q-1) p_i^q - (q^{-1}-1) p_i^{q^{-1}}]/(q-q^{-1})$.
- The symmetric $q$-expectation value of an observable $A$ is $\langle A \rangle_q^S = \sum_i A_i \; [(q-1) p_i^q - (q^{-1}-1) p_i^{q^{-1}}]/(q-q^{-1})$.
- The symmetric probability distribution is $p_i^S = c_1 [1-(1-q)\beta\epsilon_i]^{1/(1-q)}/Z_q + c_2 [1-(1-q^{-1})\beta\epsilon_i]^{1/(1-q^{-1})}/Z_{q^{-1}}$.
- The symmetric free energy is $F_q^S = -(1/\beta) \ln_q^S Z_q = U_q^S - T S_q^S$.

## Context

This work extends the connection between Tsallis statistics and $q$-deformed structures first noted by Abe (1999), who introduced a symmetric entropy via the symmetric Jackson derivative but did not formulate a complete thermostatistics. The authors also reference a two-parameter modification by Borges (1998) that likewise failed to produce consistent thermodynamic relations. The paper enables future applications of $q \leftrightarrow q^{-1}$ symmetric thermostatistics to systems where such symmetry is natural, analogous to its importance in $q$-oscillator theory as studied by Biedenharn (1989) and others.
