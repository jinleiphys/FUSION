# Muon Capture on 3He and the Weak Structure of the Nucleon

> The weak form factors of the nucleon, including the induced pseudoscalar form factor and second class terms, are constrained using a microscopic calculation of the weak capture process $^3He(μ^{-},ν_μ)^3{H}$. The calculation is parameter free, and yields a rate of 1499(16) Hz, in agreement with the remarkable experimental measurement 1496(4) Hz. The nuclear wave functions are obtained using the EIHH method with the Argonne V18 nucleon--nucleon potential and the Urbana-IX three nucleon force. The weak currents in the nuclei are described using HB$χ$PT formalism. The induced pseudoscalar form factor is found to agree with HB$χ$PT prediction. The result is compatible with vanishing second class currents, with the tightest constraint to date on the conservation of vector current (CVC) hypothesis.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a parameter-free microscopic calculation of muon capture on 3He yields a capture rate of 1499(16) Hz, which agrees with the experimental measurement of 1496(4) Hz, but the theoretical error is dominated by uncertainties in radiative corrections and the triton half-life, not by the nuclear model itself.

## Method

The author solves the three-body nuclear Schrodinger equation using the effective interaction hyperspherical harmonics (EIHH) method with the Argonne v18 nucleon-nucleon potential and the Urbana-IX three-nucleon force. The weak nuclear currents are derived from heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBchiPT) expanded to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order, including both single-nucleon impulse approximation and meson exchange currents. The one free low-energy constant in the meson exchange currents is fixed by reproducing the experimental triton half-life of 12.264 years.

## Key numbers

- Calculated capture rate: 1499(16) Hz, compared to experimental rate: 1496(4) Hz
- Nuclear matrix element Gamma_N = 0.7075(10) with nominal parameters, showing a 9% effect from meson exchange currents
- Induced pseudoscalar form factor constrained to g_P(q^2 = -0.954 m_mu^2) = 8.13 +/- 0.6, in agreement with HBchiPT prediction of 7.99(20)
- Vector second class current constraint: g_s = -0.005 +/- 0.040, providing the tightest limit on the conservation of vector current hypothesis
- Axial second class current constraint: g_t/g_A = -0.1 +/- 0.68

## Context

This work extends previous microscopic studies of muon capture on 3He by Marcucci et al. and Truhlik et al., which did not include the recently calculated electroweak radiative corrections of 0.030(4) from the MuCap collaboration. The calculation enables improved constraints on the induced pseudoscalar form factor and second class currents, and demonstrates that ab initio nuclear calculations of muon capture can achieve percentage-level accuracy for testing the weak structure of the nucleon. The approach, called EFT*, has been previously applied to electroweak reactions in A=2-4 nuclei by Park et al. (2003).
