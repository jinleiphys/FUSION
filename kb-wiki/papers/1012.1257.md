# Taste symmetry and QCD thermodynamics with improved staggered fermions

> Taste symmetry violations in staggered fermion formulations correlate strongly with the cut-off (lattice spacing) dependence in thermodynamic quantities. Better taste symmetry on the lattice can be achieved either by decreasing the lattice spacing and going to larger temporal extent in finite-temperature calculations, or by further improving the action. The highly improved staggered quark (HISQ) action offers a further degree of improvement and substantially reduces taste violations. We report on our studies of the 2+1 flavor QCD thermodynamics with the HISQ/tree action. By systematically comparing HISQ/tree, asqtad, p4 and stout calculations we quantify how the cut-off effects manifest themselves in different thermodynamic quantities, including the renormalized Polyakov loop, chiral condensate, various fluctuations and correlations of conserved charges. The implications for the equation of state and a comparison to the hadron resonance gas model are also discussed. The chiral aspects of the finite-temperature transition are discussed in the companion HotQCD contribution [1].

## Key claim

The Highly Improved Staggered Quark (HISQ/tree) action reduces taste symmetry violations enough that simulations on \(N_\tau=8\) lattices at \(T=164\) MeV are comparable to asqtad simulations on \(N_\tau\simeq 11.8\) or stout simulations on \(N_\tau\simeq 11.0\) at the same temperature, though the exact comparison depends on the chosen RMS pion mass threshold.

## Method

The authors generated zero- and finite-temperature 2+1 flavor QCD ensembles using the HISQ/tree action along two lines of constant physics (LCP): \(m_l=m_s/5\) and \(m_l=m_s/20\), with lattice spacings from 0.08 fm to 0.23 fm. They measured the quadratic splittings of the staggered pion spectrum into eight taste multiplets and computed the root-mean-squared (RMS) pion mass as a function of lattice spacing. They then systematically compared the renormalized Polyakov loop, chiral condensate, quark number susceptibilities, strangeness-baryon correlations, and trace anomaly across HISQ/tree, asqtad, p4, and stout actions.

## Key numbers

- At lattice spacing \(a=0.15\) fm (corresponding to \(T=164\) MeV on \(N_\tau=8\)), the RMS pion mass is 306 MeV for HISQ/tree, 394 MeV for stout, and 496 MeV for asqtad.
- To achieve the same RMS pion mass as HISQ/tree at \(a=0.15\) fm requires \(a=0.102\) fm for asqtad and \(a=0.109\) fm for stout.
- To achieve an RMS pion mass of 200 MeV at \(T=164\) MeV requires \(a=0.089\) fm for HISQ/tree, \(a=0.067\) fm for asqtad, and \(a=0.062\) fm for stout, corresponding to temporal extents \(N_\tau\simeq 13.5\), 18.0, and 19.4 respectively.
- The improvement in taste symmetry from asqtad to HISQ/tree shifts the chiral transition region by about 15 MeV.
- The lattice spacings range from \(a=0.15\) fm to 0.23 fm for the \(m_s/5\) LCP and from \(a=0.08\) fm to 0.23 fm for the \(m_s/20\) LCP.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier HISQ/tree thermodynamics studies (Bazavov et al., 2010) and compares directly with the stout-action results of the Budapest-Wuppertal group (Borsanyi et al., 2010) and with asqtad and p4 results from the HotQCD collaboration. The systematic comparison quantifies how taste symmetry breaking, the dominant cutoff effect in staggered fermion formulations, manifests differently in chiral observables versus deconfinement observables. The results enable more reliable continuum extrapolations of QCD thermodynamics at moderate lattice spacings and show that the HISQ/tree action on \(N_\tau=8\) lattices can achieve accuracy comparable to finer lattices with other actions, reducing computational cost for finite-temperature studies.
