# Open Problems in $α$ Particle Condensation

> $α$ particle condensation is a novel state in nuclear systems. We briefly review the present status on the study of $α$ particle condensation and address the open problems in this research field: $α$ particle condensation in heavier systems other than the Hoyle state, linear chain and $α$ particle rings, Hoyle-analogue states with extra neutrons, $α$ particle condensation related to astrophysics, etc.

## Key claim

Alpha particle condensation, where all alpha particles occupy the same lowest 0S orbit, is a novel state in nuclear systems; however, the paper argues that many open questions remain, including whether such condensation exists in heavier nuclei beyond the Hoyle state, in linear chains or rings, and in infinite nuclear matter relevant to astrophysics.

## Method

The authors review theoretical predictions for alpha condensate states using the Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Roepke (THSR) wave function and the Orthogonality Condition Model (OCM). They present constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations with the D1S force to explore ring structures, and discuss OCM calculations for Hoyle-analogue states with extra neutrons in nuclei such as 13C and 11B. For infinite matter, they apply the ideal Bose gas relation for the critical temperature and discuss the need for a generalized Nozieres-Schmitt-Rink interpolation scheme for quartet condensation.

## Key numbers

- The Hoyle state in 12C is the 0+2 state at Ex = 7.65 MeV, with its wave function described by a product state of three alphas with 70% probability.
- The 6th 0+ state in 16O at 15.1 MeV is predicted as a 4-alpha condensate analogue, with a calculated monopole transition matrix element M_calc = 1.0 fm2 (compared to 5.4 fm2 for 12C).
- The Hoyle state lies 380 keV above the 3-alpha threshold (7.27 MeV); the 15.1 MeV state in 16O lies 700 keV above the 4-alpha threshold (14.4 MeV).
- The width of the 15.1 MeV state in 16O is 160 keV, much larger than the Hoyle state width of 8.5 eV.
- For 13C, the 1/2+3 state at 12.1 MeV has three alpha clusters occupying an identical 0S orbit with 55% probability, indicating a candidate Hoyle-analogue state.
- For 11B, the 12.56-MeV state (Jpi = 1/2+) around the alpha+alpha+t threshold is a candidate Hoyle-analogue state.
- The ideal Bose gas relation gives Tc_alpha (MeV) = 13.634 (n_N / fm-3)2/3, so Tc_alpha = 1 MeV occurs at nucleon density n_N = 0.02 fm-3.
- The Mott density for alpha particle dissolution is approximately 0.01 fm-3.
- A one-dimensional THSR wave function for three alphas on a line gives an energy lower than the Brink wave function by about 3.5 MeV.

## Context

This paper reviews and extends the work on alpha particle condensation first identified in the Hoyle state of 12C, following predictions by Tohsaki, Horiuchi, Schuck, and Roepke (2001) and later studies by Funaki et al. (2008) on 16O. It addresses open problems raised by earlier experimental work on linear chain states by Morinaga (1956) and Chevalier et al. (1970s), and discusses the need for experimental verification using 4-alpha coincidence measurements as performed at LNS Catania with the CHIMERA detector. The astrophysical context builds on nuclear matter equation of state calculations by Lattimer and Swesty, Toki and Shen, and Shimiyoshi and Roepke, and calls for a generalized treatment of quartet condensation analogous to the Nozieres-Schmitt-Rink (1985) scheme for pairing.
