# Stability Analysis of the Instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter Equation and the Consequences for Meson Spectroscopy

> We investigate the light and heavy meson spectra in the context of the instantaneous approximation to the Bethe-Salpeter equation (Salpeter's equation). We use a static kernel consisting of a one-gluon-exchange component and a confining contribution. Salpeter's equation is known to be formally equivalent to a random-phase-approximation equation; as such, it can develop imaginary eigenvalues. Thus, our study can not be complete without first discussing the stability of Salpeter's equation. The stability analysis limits the form of the kernel and reveals that, contrary to the usual assumption, the confining component can not transform as a Lorentz scalar; it must transform as the timelike component of a vector. Moreover, the stability analysis sets an upper limit on the size of the one-gluon-exchange component; the value for the critical coupling is determined through a solution of the ``semirelativistic'' Coulomb problem. These limits place important constraints on the interaction and suggest that a more sophisticated model is needed to describe the light and heavy quarkonia.

## Key claim

The instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter (Salpeter) equation, which is formally equivalent to a random-phase-approximation equation, can develop imaginary eigenvalues that signal unphysical instabilities; these instabilities constrain the Lorentz structure of the confining kernel to transform as the timelike component of a vector (not as a scalar) and impose an upper limit on the strong coupling constant for the one-gluon-exchange component.

## Method

The authors solved Salpeter's equation for meson bound states using a static kernel with a one-gluon-exchange (Coulomb) component and a confining contribution, expanding the Salpeter amplitudes in a basis of nonrelativistic harmonic oscillator radial eigenfunctions. They performed a stability analysis by examining the positive-definiteness of the Hermitian matrices H^+ and H^- derived from the RPA-like structure of the equation, evaluating these for scalar, timelike-vector, and vector Lorentz structures of the potential in the pseudoscalar (J^pi=0^-) channel. For the Coulomb kernel, they determined the critical coupling by solving the semirelativistic Coulomb problem variationally using a trial wave function motivated by the Dirac-Coulomb problem.

## Key numbers

- The critical coupling for the semirelativistic Coulomb Hamiltonian is alpha_c = 4/pi (approximately 1.273), above which the Hamiltonian is unbounded from below (Herbst, 1977).
- For the vector Lorentz structure of the OGE kernel, the effective coupling entering the stability condition is alpha = 4*alpha_s, making the upper limit on alpha_s one-fourth that of the timelike case.
- The variational ground-state energy of the semirelativistic Coulomb problem at the critical coupling alpha = alpha_c is 0.968514 M (with two-parameter wave function: a=0.9359, c_1=0.1779), representing the most accurate value presented to date.
- For a mixture of scalar and timelike Lorentz structures in the confining kernel, stable solutions exist only for the fraction of timelike structure x in the interval 0.5 <= x <= 1.

## Context

This work extends the stability analysis of Parramore (1994) for the confining kernel to include the one-gluon-exchange component, and builds on the formal equivalence between Salpeter's equation and RPA equations established by Long (1984), Resag (1993), and Piekarewicz (1992). The results constrain the form of the instantaneous kernel used in meson spectroscopy, showing that a scalar confining potential leads to imaginary eigenvalues regardless of quark mass, and that the OGE coupling must remain below a critical value; this suggests that a more sophisticated model beyond the instantaneous approximation is needed to describe light and heavy quarkonia, and enables future work to incorporate these stability constraints into relativistic bound-state calculations.
