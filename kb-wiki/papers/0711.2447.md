# Production of hypernuclei with hadronic and electromagnetic probes

> We present an overview of a fully covariant formulation of describing the hypernuclear production with hadronic and electromagnetic probes. This theory is based on an effective Lagrangian picture and it focuses on production amplitudes that are described via creation, propagation and decay into relevant channel of N*(1650), N*(1710) and N*(1720) intermediate baryonic resonance states in the initial collision of the projectile with one of the target nucleons. The bound state nucleon and hyperon wave functions are obtained by solving the Dirac equation with appropriate scalar and vector potentials. Specific examples are discussed for reactions which are of interest to current and future experiments on the hypernuclear production.

## Key claim

The paper presents a fully covariant, effective-Lagrangian framework for hypernuclear production that treats hadronic and electromagnetic probes within a single formalism, but the actual predictive power is hedged: the calculations rely on intermediate baryonic resonance states (N*(1650), N*(1710), N*(1720)) whose parameters and cut-off functions are fixed independently from elementary reactions, and the results are shown only for selected cases without systematic comparison to a broad set of experimental data.

## Method

The production amplitudes are constructed from Feynman diagrams where the projectile (proton, pion, or photon) interacts with a bound nucleon, exciting intermediate N* resonances that decay into a kaon and a Lambda hyperon captured into a nuclear orbit. Bound-state nucleon and hyperon wave functions are obtained by solving the Dirac equation with Woods-Saxon scalar and vector potentials. For the (p,K+) reaction, the initial proton–nucleon interaction is modeled via π, ρ, and ω exchange, with the pion propagator renormalized by a self-energy that includes particle-hole and delta-hole excitations and a Landau–Migdal parameter g′.

## Key numbers

- For the 12C(p,K+)13ΛC reaction at 1.8 GeV incident proton energy, the differential cross section for the p-shell hypernuclear state is more than an order of magnitude larger than that for the s-shell state.
- The binding energies of the p-shell states in 13ΛC are in the range 0.7–0.9 MeV, compared to 11.70 MeV for the s-shell.
- For the 16O(γ,K+)16ΛN reaction, the total cross section peaks at photon energies around 900 MeV, about 200 MeV above the production threshold, with peak values near 20 nb.
- In the 16O(γ,K+) reaction, the cross section for the unnatural parity 3+ state is larger than that for the natural parity 2+ state by about a factor of 2.5.
- For the 40Ca(p,K+)41ΛCa(0d3/2) reaction at 2.0 GeV, the differential cross section shows strong sensitivity to the Landau–Migdal parameter g′ in the range 0.5–0.7.

## Context

This work extends earlier covariant treatments of hypernuclear production by Shyam and collaborators (Shyam 2004, 2006, 2007) and contrasts with nonrelativistic calculations that do not capture the full Dirac spinor structure at the momentum transfers relevant to kaon production. The formalism is designed to be applied to data from recent JLab experiments on 7Li and 12C targets (Iodice 2007, Yuan 2006) and to upcoming measurements at MAMI-C and ELSA, enabling extraction of bound Lambda wave functions and constraints on the spin-dependent part of the Lambda–nucleon interaction.
