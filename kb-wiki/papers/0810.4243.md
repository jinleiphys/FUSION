# Supercurrents in color-superconducting quark matter

> We review the basic properties of the currCFL-K^0 phase in dense quark matter. At asymptotically large densities, three-flavor quark matter is in the color-flavor locked (CFL) state. The currCFL-K^0 state is a way to respond to ``stress'' on the quark Cooper pairing, imposed at more moderate densities by the strange quark mass and the conditions of electric and color neutrality. We show how a kaon supercurrent is incorporated in a purely fermionic formalism, and show that the net current vanishes due to cancellation of fermion and charge-conjugate fermion contributions.

# Supercurrents in color-superconducting quark matter

## Key claim

The currCFL-K^0 phase, which incorporates a kaon supercurrent as a response to stress from the strange quark mass and neutrality conditions, has a net electric current that vanishes exactly due to cancellation between fermion and charge-conjugate fermion contributions, even though the phase is anisotropic and contains a supercurrent.

## Method

The author constructs a purely fermionic Nambu-Gorkov formalism for the currCFL-K^0 phase, starting from the mean-field Lagrangian with gap matrices that include spatial dependence from kaon and baryon supercurrents. By applying a field redefinition that absorbs the spatial dependence of the gap matrices, the inverse propagator is derived and shown to be gauge-invariant under color rotations. The propagator is then inverted in the approximation of small currents (neglecting transverse gauge field components), and the resulting occupation numbers are used to compute the electric charge density and current.

## Key numbers

- The kaon condensate angle φ satisfies cos φ = m²_K⁰ / μ²_K⁰, with condensation occurring when m²_K⁰ < μ²_K⁰; the paper assumes m²_K⁰ ≪ μ²_K⁰, i.e., φ ≃ π/2.
- The effective chemical potential for the strange-up Cooper pair example is given by μ̄ = μ − 7m²_s/(24μ), with a mismatch δμ = μ_e + m²_s/(8μ).
- The kaon mass from weak interactions is stated to be in the keV range, citing Son (2001).

## Context

This work reviews and extends the currCFL-K^0 phase, which was introduced by Kryjevski (2008) and Schafer (2005) as the next phase below the CFL-K^0 phase in density. The CFL-K^0 phase itself was established by Bedaque (2001) and Kaplan (2001) as a kaon-condensed state. The currCFL-K^0 phase is an anisotropic phase with a kaon supercurrent, distinct from crystalline phases (Rajagopal, 2006) which contain counter-propagating currents in multiple spatial directions. The formalism presented here enables calculation of the current from free energy minimization and determination of the onset of this phase in terms of the stress parameter m²_s/μ, as pursued in Kryjevski (2008), Schafer (2005), and Gerhold (2006).
