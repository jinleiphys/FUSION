# Integrating Holographic Vector Dominance to Hidden Local Symmetry for the Nucleon Form Factor

> We derive a two-parameter formula for the electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon described as an instanton by "integrating out" all KK modes other than the lowest mesons from the infinite-tower of vector mesons in holographic QCD while preserving hidden local symmetry for the resultant vector fields. With only two parameters, the proton Sachs form factors can be fit surprisingly well to the available experimental data for momentum transfers $Q^2\lsim 0.5$ GeV$^2$ with $χ^2$/dof $\lsim 2$. We interpret this agreement as indicating the importance of an infinite tower in the soliton structure of the nucleon. The prediction of the Sakai-Sugimoto holographic dual model is checked against the fit values to assess its accuracy in describing the proton structure. We find that the structure of the "core" of roughly 1/3 in the proton size indicated in experiments and commonly associated with an intrinsic quark-gluon structure in QCD is "hidden" in the infinite tower in the holographic model.

## Key claim

The authors derive a two-parameter formula for the nucleon electromagnetic form factors by integrating out all higher Kaluza-Klein modes from the infinite tower of vector mesons in holographic QCD, preserving hidden local symmetry for the remaining lowest vector mesons. The fit to experimental data is limited to momentum transfers \(Q^2 \lesssim 0.5\) GeV\(^2\) and achieves \(\chi^2/\text{dof} \lesssim 2\), which the authors interpret as indicating the importance of the infinite tower in the soliton structure of the nucleon rather than a fundamental quark-gluon core.

## Method

The authors start from the Sakai-Sugimoto holographic dual QCD model, which describes baryons as instantons in a five-dimensional warped space with an infinite tower of vector mesons. They integrate out all vector mesons except the lowest \(\rho\) and \(\omega\) mesons at tree level, expanding to next-to-leading order in momentum (\({\cal O}(p^4)\)) while preserving hidden local symmetry for the remaining vector fields. The resulting Sachs electric and magnetic form factors for the proton are expressed in a generic two-parameter form \(G(Q^2)/\beta = (1 - a/2) + z\,Q^2/m_V^2 + (a/2)\,m_V^2/(m_V^2 + Q^2)\), and the parameters \(a\) and \(z\) are determined by fitting to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit parameters for the proton electric Sachs form factor: \(a_E^{\text{(best)}} = 4.55\), \(z_E^{\text{(best)}} = 0.45\); \(\chi_E^2/\text{dof} = 1.5\) for \(Q^2 \leq 0.5\) GeV\(^2\).
- Best-fit parameters for the proton magnetic Sachs form factor: \(a_M^{\text{(best)}} = 4.31\), \(z_M^{\text{(best)}} = 0.40\); \(\chi_M^2/\text{dof} = 1.1\) for \(Q^2 \leq 0.5\) GeV\(^2\).
- Combined best-fit with \(a_E = a_M\) and \(z_E = z_M\): \(a^{\text{(best)}} = 4.42\), \(z^{\text{(best)}} = 0.42\); \(\chi^2/\text{dof} = 1.90\).
- The vector meson mass used in the fit is \(m_V = 0.775\) GeV; the nucleon mass is \(m_N = 0.938\) GeV.
- For comparison, the Sakurai vector dominance model (with \(a=2\), \(z=0\)) gives \(\chi^2/\text{dof} = 4.3\) for the pion form factor up to \(Q^2 \sim 1\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the holographic vector dominance approach previously applied to the pion form factor by Harada et al. (2010) to the nucleon, using the same integrating-out procedure from the Sakai-Sugimoto model. The authors contrast their results with the standard Sakurai vector dominance model, which famously fails for the nucleon (with \(\chi^2/\text{dof} = 1116\) for the electric form factor up to \(Q^2 \sim 1\) GeV\(^2\)), and with the Bijker-Iachello two-component model. The findings suggest that the "core" of roughly 1/3 the proton size, often attributed to intrinsic quark-gluon structure in QCD, is instead "hidden" in the infinite tower of vector mesons in the holographic description, enabling future work on whether this core is a physical observable or an artifact of the model.
