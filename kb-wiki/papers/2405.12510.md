# Electric field induction in quark-gluon plasma due to thermoelectric effects

> Relativistic heavy-ion collisions produce quark-gluon plasma (QGP), which is locally thermalized. Due to electrically charged particles (quarks), QGP exhibits interesting thermoelectric phenomena during its evolution, resulting in an electromagnetic (EM) field in the medium. In this study, for the first time, we estimate the induced electric field in QGP due to the thermoelectric effect. This phenomenon can induce an EM field even in QGP produced by the head-on heavy-ion collision. In peripheral heavy-ion collisions, the presence of a spectator current generates a transient magnetic field at the early stage, which disrupts the isotropy of the induced electric field. For the numerical estimation, we use a quasiparticle-based model that incorporates the lattice quantum chromodynamics equation of state for QGP. The induced electric field is estimated with cooling rates derived from Gubser hydrodynamic flow. Thermoelectric coefficients such as Seebeck, magneto-Seebeck, and Nernst coefficients play a crucial role in determining the induced field. Additionally, we account for the temperature evolution of QGP using different hydrodynamic cooling rates to calculate the transport coefficients. We also estimate the transport coefficients and the induced electric field in the presence of an external time-varying magnetic field, including the quantum effect of Landau quantization, and explore the effects of the intensity and decay parameter of the magnetic field on the induced electric field. Our findings reveal that the space-time profile of the induced electric field is zero at the center and increases as we go away from the center. During the early stages of evolution, the electric field can reach a maximum value of $eE \approx 1~m_π^2$, decreasing in strength over time.

## Key claim

For the first time, the authors estimate that an electric field can be induced in quark-gluon plasma (QGP) solely through thermoelectric effects, even in head-on heavy-ion collisions where no initial electromagnetic field from spectator currents exists. The induced field reaches a maximum of approximately \(eE \approx 1~m_\pi^2\) during early evolution, but this value depends on the specific hydrodynamic cooling rate and the presence or absence of an external magnetic field.

## Method

The authors use a quasiparticle model incorporating the lattice QCD equation of state to compute thermoelectric transport coefficients (Seebeck, magneto-Seebeck, and Nernst coefficients) for an evolving QGP medium. They solve the Boltzmann transport equation in the relaxation time approximation, including terms from both spatial temperature gradients and the time derivative of temperature (cooling rate), and then derive the induced electric field from the condition of zero net electric current. The temperature evolution of the medium is modeled using Bjorken flow (ideal hydrodynamics and ideal magnetohydrodynamics with an exponential magnetic field decay) and Gubser flow (including viscous effects and radial expansion).

## Key numbers

* Induced electric field reaches a maximum of \(eE \approx 1~m_\pi^2\) during the early stages of QGP evolution, decreasing in strength over time.
* The initial magnetic field in peripheral collisions can be as high as \(10 - 15~m_\pi^2\) (approximately \(10^{18}\) Gauss), which submerges the QCD energy scale \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD} \sim 1.5~m_\pi\).
* For the SPS energy range, previously reported induced electric and magnetic fields from hydrodynamic flow and charge density profiles are as high as \(0.15~m_\pi^2\) and \(1~m_\pi^2\) for RHIC energies, respectively (Dash and Panda, 2024).
* Numerical estimates use initial conditions for a central gold-gold collision at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200~{\rm GeV}\) with \(\hat{T}_0 = 5.55\) and \({\rm H}_0 = 0.33\) for Gubser flow, with \(1/q = 4.3~{\rm fm}\).
* The Seebeck coefficient is calculated at a baryon chemical potential \(\mu_B = 0.3~{\rm GeV}\) and strong coupling constant \(\alpha_s = 0.5\).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of thermoelectric coefficients in QGP by Das (2021) and others, which considered a static picture with local temperature gradients but neglected the time evolution of the medium temperature. It also builds on the work of Dash and Panda (2024), who first showed that electromagnetic fields can arise from hydrodynamic flow and charge density profiles in QGP. The present study enables future investigations into the spatiotemporal magnetic field generated by the induced electric field and the impact of thermoelectric effects on flow harmonics and other final-state observables in heavy-ion collisions.
