# Neutral Weak Form Factors of Proton and Neutron

> We determine the nucleon neutral weak electromagnetic form factors $G^{Z,p(n)}_{E,M}$ by combining results from light-front holographic QCD and lattice QCD calculations. We deduce nucleon electromagnetic form factors from light-front holographic QCD which provides a good parametrization of the experimental data of the nucleon electromagnetic form factors in the entire momentum transfer range and isolate the strange quark electromagnetic form factors $G^{s}_{E,M}$ using lattice QCD. From these calculations, we obtain precise estimates of the neutral weak form factors in the momentum transfer range of $0\,\text{GeV}^2\leq Q^2 \leq 0.5 \,\text{GeV}^2 $. From the lattice QCD calculation, we present $Q^2$-dependence of the strange quark form factors. We also deduce the neutral weak Dirac and Pauli form factors $F_{1,2}^{Z,p(n)}$ of the proton and the neutron.

## Key claim

The paper determines nucleon neutral weak form factors by combining light-front holographic QCD predictions of nucleon electromagnetic form factors with lattice QCD calculations of strange quark form factors, obtaining results in the momentum transfer range of 0 to 0.5 GeV². The authors note that the light-front holographic QCD approach serves essentially as a parametrization of the average of world experimental data, with a model uncertainty of about 10% based on the confinement scale value.

## Method

The authors combine nucleon electromagnetic form factors from light-front holographic QCD with strange quark electromagnetic form factors from lattice QCD calculations using overlap fermions on (2+1) flavor domain wall fermion gauge configurations. The strange quark form factors are obtained from global chiral extrapolation fits of lattice data across four ensembles with pion masses ranging from 135 to 400 MeV, using z-expansion interpolation and empirical fit formulas that account for quark mass dependence, finite lattice spacing, volume corrections, and partial quenching effects. The neutral weak form factors are then computed using the standard expression relating them to nucleon electromagnetic form factors and strange quark form factors under isospin symmetry, including electroweak radiative corrections.

## Key numbers

- The strange quark magnetic form factor at Q²=0.25 GeV² is G^s_M = -0.018(4) nucleon magnetons, with χ²/d.o.f. = 1.13 from the global fit
- The strange quark electric form factor at Q²=0.25 GeV² is G^s_E = 0.0024(8), with χ²/d.o.f. = 1.1 from the global fit
- The confinement scale used is κ = 0.548 GeV, determined from the rho meson mass M_ρ = 0.775 GeV via κ = M_ρ/√2
- The higher Fock component probability for the proton Pauli form factor is γ_p = 0.27 ± 0.03, and for the neutron γ_n = 0.38 ± 0.05
- The SU(6) symmetry breaking parameter for the neutron Dirac form factor is r = 2.08 ± 0.09
- The SAMPLE collaboration measured G^{Z,p}_M(Q²=0.1 GeV²) = 0.34(11) nucleon magnetons, corresponding to G^s_M = 0.23(44) nucleon magnetons

## Context

This work extends prior lattice QCD calculations of strange quark form factors by Sufian (2016) and light-front holographic QCD calculations of nucleon electromagnetic form factors by the same author, combining them to predict neutral weak form factors that are difficult to extract from parity-violating experiments alone. The approach addresses the challenge that individual experiments like SAMPLE (Mueller 1997) and its reanalyses (Forest 1998, Spayde 2003) provide only limited precision measurements at single Q² values, while global fits (Liu 2007, Gonzalez-Jimenez 2014, Young 2006) give G^s_M values consistent with zero but differing in sign. The results enable improved predictions for future parity-violating electron scattering experiments without requiring prior knowledge of the weak axial vector form factor.
