# Funny business from the large $N_c$ finite temperature crossover

> It is well known that the deconfinement transition temperature for $SU(N_c)$ gauge theory is almost independent of $N_c$, and the transition is first order for $N_c \ge 3$. In the real world ($N_c=3$, light quarks) it is a crossover located far away from the pure gauge value. What happens to the transition temperature at fixed fermion mass if the number of fermion flavors is held constant ($N_f=2$) and $N_c$ is varied? There are multiple plausible stories, only one of which appears to be true when the systems are simulated on the lattice. I describe the physics issues which surround the question and my lattice - based answer to it.

## Key claim
The finite-temperature crossover for QCD with two fermion flavors is nearly independent of the number of colors \(N_c\) for \(N_c = 3, 4, 5\), contradicting both the naive large-\(N_c\) expectation that the pure gauge first-order transition persists and the linear sigma model prediction that the crossover temperature scales as \(\sqrt{N_c}\). Instead, the data support a Hagedorn-like scenario where the crossover temperature is set by the spectrum of meson resonances, which is itself approximately \(N_c\)-independent.

## Method
Lattice simulations of \(SU(N_c)\) gauge theory with \(N_c = 3, 4, 5\) and \(N_f = 2\) dynamical clover fermions were performed at fixed bare 't Hooft coupling to match lattice spacings across \(N_c\). The chiral condensate was measured as a function of temperature using the subtracted condensate \(\langle \bar\psi\psi\rangle_{\text{sub}} = \langle \bar\psi\psi\rangle_T - \langle \bar\psi\psi\rangle_{T=0}\), rescaled by \(3/N_c\) and made dimensionless with the flow scale \(t_0\). Three fermion masses were studied, corresponding to \((m_{PS}/m_V)^2 \approx 0.63, 0.5, 0.25\).

## Key numbers
- The crossover temperature for all three \(N_c\) values (3, 4, 5) is the same within uncertainties, at each of the three quark masses studied: \((m_{PS}/m_V)^2 \sim 0.63\), \(0.5\), and \(0.25\).
- The subtracted condensate \(\frac{3}{N_c} t_0^{3/2} \Sigma(T)\) collapses to a common curve across \(N_c\) when plotted against temperature in units of \(t_0\).
- The derivative \(\Delta\Sigma(T)/\Delta T\) shows a broad peak at the same temperature for all \(N_c\), with no sign of a first-order transition.
- The crossover temperature lies intermediate between the pure gauge transition temperature (from Lucini (2005) and Lucini (2012)) and the \(SU(3)\) physical-point crossover.

## Context
This work extends lattice studies of large-\(N_c\) QCD (reviewed by Lucini (2012), Garcia-Perez (2020), and Hernandez (2020)) to finite temperature with dynamical fermions. It directly tests three competing scenarios: the naive large-\(N_c\) gluon-dominance picture, the Pisarski-Wilczek (1983) linear sigma model prediction of \(T_c \propto \sqrt{N_c}\), and the Hagedorn (1968) spectrum-based scenario. The results favor the Hagedorn picture and contradict the first two, showing that fermionic degrees of freedom remain important at large \(N_c\). The work enables future lattice tests of the hadron resonance gas model across \(N_c\) and suggests that large-\(N_c\) thermodynamics with staggered fermions could test continuum quark-gluon plasma phenomenology derived from AdS/CFT.
