# Temperature-dependence of single-particle properties in isospin-symmetric and -asymmetric matter within the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock model

> The understanding of the interaction of nucleons in nuclear and neutron-rich matter at non-zero temperature is important for a variety of applications ranging from heavy-ion collisions to nuclear astrophysics. In this papre we apply the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock method along with the Bonn B potential to predict single-particle properties in symmetric nuclear matter and neutron-rich matter at finite temperature. It is found that temperature effects are generally small but can be significant at low density and momentum.

## Key claim

Temperature effects on single-particle properties in nuclear and neutron-rich matter are generally small for temperatures up to a few tens of MeV, but can be significant at low density and low momentum, with the single-particle potential becoming slightly more repulsive with increasing temperature.

## Method

The Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) method with the Bonn B nucleon-nucleon potential was extended to finite temperature by replacing the zero-temperature step-function occupation numbers with Fermi-Dirac distributions. The single-particle potential, chemical potential, and effective mass were obtained self-consistently for symmetric nuclear matter, pure neutron matter, and isospin-asymmetric matter at temperatures from 0 to 30 MeV, with the angle-averaged Pauli operator evaluated numerically using the temperature-dependent occupation numbers.

## Key numbers

- Chemical potential in symmetric nuclear matter decreases with temperature from T=0 to T=30 MeV in steps of 5 MeV, with larger effects at lower densities

- Effective mass in symmetric matter shown for T=0, T=10, T=15, and T=20 MeV as a function of Fermi momentum

- Single-neutron and single-proton potentials in isospin-asymmetric matter shown for asymmetry parameters alpha=0.3 and alpha=0.5 at T=0, T=10, and T=20 MeV, with average Fermi momentum of 1.4 fm^-1

## Context

This work extends the author's previous zero-temperature DBHF calculations of isospin-asymmetric matter (Alonso and Sammarruca, 2003; Sammarruca, 2010) to include finite temperature effects, and complements earlier finite-temperature studies using the Bloch-De Dominicis diagrammatic expansion (Baldo and Ferreira, 1999), Brueckner-Hartree-Fock with and without three-body forces (Zuo et al., 2004), and the self-consistent Green's function approach (Rios et al., 2006; Rios et al., 2009). The results enable future studies of temperature-dependent in-medium effective cross sections, hot spin-asymmetric matter, and proton fractions in hot beta-stable matter relevant to heavy-ion collisions and supernova evolution.
