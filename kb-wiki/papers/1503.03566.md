# Spectroscopy of $η'$-nucleus bound states at GSI and FAIR --- very preliminary results and future prospects ---

> The possible existence of η'-nucleus bound states has been put forward through theoretical and experimental studies. It is strongly related to the η' mass at finite density, which is expected to be reduced because of the interplay between the $U_A(1)$ anomaly and partial restoration of chiral symmetry. The investigation of the C(p,d) reaction at GSI and FAIR, as well as an overview of the experimental program at GSI and future plans at FAIR are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents very preliminary results from the first inclusive measurement of the C(p,d) reaction at GSI to search for eta'-nucleus bound states, with the caveat that the analysis is still in progress and only an estimated missing-mass resolution of sigma ~ 2 MeV has been reported so far.

## Method

The experiment used a 2.5 GeV proton beam incident on a 4 g/cm^2-thick carbon target to produce eta'-mesic nuclei via the (p,d) reaction. The momentum of ejectile deuterons was analyzed using the Fragment Separator (FRS) as a high-resolution spectrometer, with particle identification achieved through time-of-flight measurements between scintillators and Cherenkov detectors. Data were taken for three reactions: 12C(p,d) at 2.5 GeV, D(p,d) at 2.5 GeV for background study, and D(p,d)p at 1.6 GeV for calibration.

## Key numbers

- The eta' meson mass in vacuum is around 958 MeV/c^2
- The missing-mass resolution is estimated to be less than 2 MeV/c^2 in sigma
- The deuteron-to-proton ratio in the raw data was approximately 1/200
- The two peaks in the TOF distribution are approximately 20 ns distant from each other
- The real part of the eta'-nucleus optical potential from CBELSA/TAPS was -(37 +/- 10(stat) +/- 10(syst)) MeV
- The imaginary part (half of absorption width) from CBELSA/TAPS was 10 +/- 2.5 MeV
- The eta'-proton scattering length from COSY-11 was (0 +/- 0.43) + i(0.37+0.40-0.16) fm
- Theoretical predictions for eta' mass reduction at normal nuclear density: 150 MeV (Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model), 80 MeV (linear sigma model), 37 MeV (QMC model)

## Context

This work extends previous experimental studies of eta'-nucleus interaction from the CBELSA/TAPS experiment (Nanova 2013, Nanova 2012) which measured the optical potential through photoproduction, and the COSY-11 experiment (Moskal, Czerwinski 2014) which determined the eta'-proton scattering length. The planned semi-exclusive measurement at FAIR's Super-FRS aims to improve the signal-to-noise ratio (currently of order 1/100 in the inclusive spectrum) by tagging decay protons from the two-nucleon absorption process, using an intra-nuclear cascade simulation with the JAM transport model.
