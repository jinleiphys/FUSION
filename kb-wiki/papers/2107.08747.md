# Two-neutrino double-beta decay matrix elements based on relativistic nuclear energy density functional

> Nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for two-neutrino double-beta decay ($2νββ$) are studied in the framework of relativistic nuclear energy density functional (REDF). The properties of nuclei involved in the decay are obtained using the relativistic Hartree-Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer theory and relevant nuclear transitions are described using the relativistic proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation based on relativistic energy density functional (REDF-QRPA). Three effective interactions have been employed, including density-dependent meson-exchange (DD-ME2) and point coupling interactions (DD-PC1 and DD-PCX), and pairing correlations are described consistently both in $T=1$ and $T=0$ channels using a separable pairing interaction. The optimal values of $T=0$ pairing strength parameter $V_{0pp}$ are constrained by the experimental data on $β$-decay half lives. The $2νββ$ matrix elements and half-lives are calculated for several nuclides experimentally known to undergo this kind of decay: $^{48}$Ca, $^{76}$Ge, $^{82}$Se, $^{96}$Zr, $^{100}$Mo, $^{116}$Cd, $^{124}$Xe, $^{128}$Te, $^{130}$Te, $^{136}$Xe and $^{150}$Nd. The model dependence of the NMEs and their sensitivity on $V_{0pp}$ is investigated, and the NMEs obtained using optimal values of $V_{0pp}$ are discussed in comparison to previous studies. The results of the present work represent an important benchmark for the future applications of the relativistic framework in studies of neutrinoless double-beta decay.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a relativistic nuclear energy density functional framework (REDF-QRPA) for calculating two-neutrino double-beta decay matrix elements, but the authors explicitly note that this is the first implementation of relativistic density-dependent interactions in such a study, and that some effects (deformation, renormalization, gauge symmetry, isospin restoration) have not yet been taken into account.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic Hartree-Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (RH-BCS) theory to describe ground-state properties of the initial and final nuclei, then applied the relativistic proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (REDF-QRPA) to describe the Gamow-Teller transitions involved in the decay. Three effective interactions were employed: the density-dependent meson-exchange interaction DD-ME2 and the point-coupling interactions DD-PC1 and DD-PCX, with pairing correlations treated consistently in both T=1 and T=0 channels using a separable pairing interaction. The isoscalar (T=0) pairing strength parameter V0pp was constrained by fitting to experimental beta-decay half-lives for even-even nuclei in the range 8 ≤ Z ≤ 82.

## Key numbers

- For 48Ca, the optimal V0pp values are 0.98 ± 0.12 (DD-ME2), 1.04 ± 0.12 (DD-PC1), and 0.96 ± 0.11 (DD-PCX)
- For 76Ge, the optimal V0pp values are 0.96 ± 0.13 (DD-ME2), 1.01 ± 0.14 (DD-PC1), and 0.94 ± 0.12 (DD-PCX)
- For 130Te, the optimal V0pp values are 1.03 ± 0.09 (DD-ME2), 1.10 ± 0.09 (DD-PC1), and 1.01 ± 0.08 (DD-PCX)
- For 136Xe, the optimal V0pp values are 1.06 ± 0.08 (DD-ME2), 1.14 ± 0.08 (DD-PC1), and 1.03 ± 0.07 (DD-PCX)
- The fit parameters for the T=0 pairing strength functional form V0pp = V1 + V2((N-Z)/A) yield V1 = 0.574 ± 0.338 and V2 = 2.301 ± 1.422 for DD-ME2; V1 = 0.522 ± 0.407 and V2 = 3.092 ± 1.768 for DD-PC1; V1 = 0.592 ± 0.366 and V2 = 2.321 ± 1.559 for DD-PCX
- A quenched value of the axial-vector coupling constant gA = 1.0 was used instead of the free nucleon value gA = 1.26

## Context

This work extends the relativistic nuclear energy density functional approach to two-neutrino double-beta decay, which had not been previously addressed in relativistic EDF studies (Song 2014, Yao 2015, Song 2017). The results serve as a benchmark for future applications of the relativistic framework to neutrinoless double-beta decay calculations. The approach differs from recent pn-QRPA studies that start from realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions (Fang 2011, Hyvarinen 2016) by instead using an interaction derived from a relativistic nuclear energy density functional in the particle-hole channel.
