# Low-energy neutrino scattering in experiment and astrophysics

> We review the relevance of neutrino-nucleus interactions at energy transfers below 100 MeV for accelerator-based experiments, experiments at lower energies and for astrophysical neutrinos. The impact of low-energy scattering processes in the energy reconstruction analysis of oscillation experiments is investigated. We discuss the modeling of coherent scattering processes and compare its strength to that of inelastic interactions. The presented results are obtained within a continuum random phase approximation approach.

## Key claim

The paper argues that low-energy neutrino-nucleus interactions (energy transfers below 100 MeV) are relevant not only for dedicated low-energy experiments and supernova neutrinos, but also for accelerator-based oscillation experiments, because forward lepton scattering events favor low energy transfers regardless of the incoming neutrino energy, meaning that even high-energy experiments contain signal components sensitive to nuclear structure details that are poorly described by simple models like the relativistic Fermi gas.

## Method

The authors use a continuum random phase approximation (CRPA) approach, starting from a Hartree-Fock calculation with the SkE2 Skyrme force parametrization to generate single-particle wave functions and the mean-field potential. The same Skyrme parametrization is used as the residual nucleon-nucleon interaction in the RPA calculation, making the approach self-consistent. The RPA polarization propagator is solved in coordinate space, allowing exact treatment of the energy continuum, and the resulting transition matrix elements are used to compute inclusive neutrino-nucleus cross sections via multipole expansion of the hadronic current.

## Key numbers

- Differential neutrino scattering cross sections on $^{40}$Ar at 50 MeV are typically of order $10^{-42}$ cm$^2$ per MeV
- Mono-energetic muon neutrinos from pion decay-at-rest at the SNS have energy $E_{\nu_\mu} = 29.8$ MeV, while electron neutrinos and muon antineutrinos follow Michel spectra with energies up to 52.8 MeV
- The effective width used to fold CRPA responses to account for finite resonance widths is 3 MeV
- The free axial coupling constant value used is $g_A \approx 1.28$
- The Fermi function for Coulomb distortion uses nuclear radius $R \approx 1.2 A^{1/3}$ fm and fine structure constant $\alpha \approx 1/137.036$

## Context

This review extends the authors' previous CRPA work (Jachowicz 2002) and compares with other approaches including shell model calculations (Hayes 1999, Kostensalo 2018), QRPA (Paar 2007, Samana 2008), and local Fermi Gas-based RPA (Nieves 2004, Sajjad Athar 2005). The paper addresses the need for accurate low-energy nuclear modeling in oscillation experiments like T2K, MiniBooNE, MicroBooNE, and DUNE, and in supernova neutrino detection. The work enables improved energy reconstruction in oscillation analyses and provides theoretical support for experiments at the Spallation Neutron Source, including the CAPTAIN program.
