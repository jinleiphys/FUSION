# Feynman versus Bakamjian-Thomas in Light Front Dynamics

> We compare the Bakamjian-Thomas (BT) formulation of relativistic few-body systems with light front field theories that maintain closer contact with Feynman diagrams. We find that Feynman diagrams distinguish Melosh rotations and other kinematical quantities belonging to various composite subsystem frames that correspond to different loop integrals. The BT formalism knows only the rest frame of the whole composite system, where everything is evaluated.

## Key claim

Feynman diagrams in light front dynamics require Melosh rotations and other kinematical quantities to be evaluated in the rest frames of composite subsystems (e.g., the two-quark pair), whereas the Bakamjian-Thomas (BT) formulation evaluates everything only in the rest frame of the whole composite system; this difference affects the normalization of spin-flavor invariants and may become important at higher momentum transfers.

## Method

The authors compare the Bakamjian-Thomas (BT) formulation of relativistic few-body systems with light front field theories that maintain closer contact with Feynman diagrams, specifically analyzing the triangle diagram used in electromagnetic and weak baryon form factor evaluations. They derive the spin coupling of quarks to the nucleon from an effective N-3q Lagrangian, evaluating the residues of the triangle Feynman diagram at the on-\(k^-\)-shell poles of spectator particles, and compare the resulting expressions with those obtained from the BT construction. The analysis focuses on the spin-zero and spin-one couplings of the quark pair (12) to the third quark, showing how the Melosh rotations and boost transformations differ between the two approaches.

## Key numbers

- The mass squared of the two-body (12)-subsystem is given by \(M_2^2 = \frac{1-x_3}{x_1x_2} (x_2m_1^2 + x_1m_2^2) - \frac{(1-x_3)^2}{x_1x_2} q_3^2\), where \(x_i = p_i^+/P^+\) and \(q_3\) is a Jacobi relative momentum.
- The mass squared of the nucleon (three-body system) is \(M_0^2 = \sum_{i=1}^3 \frac{m_i^2}{x_i} - \frac{1-x_3}{x_1x_2} q_3^2 - \frac{Q_3^2}{x_3(1-x_3)}\), where \(Q_3\) is another Jacobi relative momentum.
- The projection \(p_1 \cdot P = \frac{(m_1^2 - q_3^2)}{2x_1} + \frac{q_3 \cdot Q_3}{1-x_3} + \frac{x_1}{2} \left(M_0^2 - \frac{Q_3^2}{(1-x_3)^2}\right)\) depends on both \(M_0\) and \(Q_3\).
- The projection \(p_1 \cdot (p_1+p_2) = \frac{1-x_3}{2x_1} (m_1^2 - q_3^2) + \frac{x_1 M_2^2}{2(1-x_3)}\) depends only on \(M_2\) and \(q_3\).

## Context

This work extends the comparison between the Bakamjian-Thomas (BT) formulation (Bakamjian and Thomas, 1953) and light front field theories that maintain closer contact with Feynman diagrams, as used in form factor evaluations by Karmanov and others. The authors show that Feynman diagrams require Melosh rotations and other kinematical quantities to be evaluated in the rest frames of composite subsystems (e.g., the two-quark pair), while the BT formalism knows only the rest frame of the whole composite system. This distinction has consequences for the normalization of spin-flavor invariants and may become important at higher momentum transfers, particularly for the orthogonality of wave functions at \(q^2=0\).
