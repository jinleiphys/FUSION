# Paramagnetism in color superconductivity and compact stars

> It is quite plausible that color superconductivity occurs in the inner regions of neutron stars. At the same time, it is known that strong magnetic fields exist in the interior of these compact objects. In this paper we discuss some important effects that can occur in the color superconducting core of compact stars due to the presence of the stars' magnetic field. In particular, we consider the modification of the gluon dynamics for a color superconductor with three massless quark flavors in the presence of an external magnetic field. We show that the long-range component of the external magnetic field that penetrates the color-flavor locked phase produces an instability for field values larger than the charged gluons' Meissner mass. As a consequence, the ground state is restructured forming a vortex state characterized by the condensation of charged gluons and the creation of magnetic flux tubes. In the vortex state the magnetic field outside the flux tubes is equal to the applied one, while inside the tubes its strength increases by an amount that depends on the amplitude of the gluon condensate. This paramagnetic behavior of the color superconductor can be relevant for the physics of compact stars.

# Paramagnetism in color superconductivity and compact stars

## Key claim
The long-range component of an external magnetic field that penetrates the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase of three-flavor quark matter produces an instability for field values larger than the charged gluons' Meissner mass, leading to a vortex ground state that amplifies the magnetic field inside flux tubes rather than screening it, a paramagnetic (anti-Meissner) effect that could boost internal magnetic fields in compact stars beyond values found in neutron stars with nuclear-matter cores.

## Method
The authors analyze the gluon dynamics in a color superconductor with three massless quark flavors in the CFL phase under an external "rotated" magnetic field (the linear combination of the photon and the eighth gluon that remains long-range). They derive the Gibbs free energy density near the critical field, minimize it to obtain Ginzburg-Landau-type equations for the charged gluon condensate and induced magnetic field, and find vortex solutions following Abrikosov's approach for type-II superconductors, with the key difference that the sign of the condensate's coupling to the magnetic field is reversed due to the anomalous magnetic moment of spin-1 gluons.

## Key numbers
- The critical field for the instability is given by $\widetilde{H}_{C} = m_{M}^{2}/\widetilde{e}$, where $m_{M}$ is the charged gluon Meissner mass and $\widetilde{e} = e \cos\theta$ is the rotated electromagnetic charge.
- A rough estimate at baryon densities typical of neutron-star cores ($\mu \simeq 200-400$ MeV, $\alpha_{s}(\mu) \simeq 1/3$) gives $\widetilde{H}_{C} \simeq 9.5 \times 10^{16}$ G to $3.8 \times 10^{17}$ G.
- The magnetic flux through each periodicity cell in the vortex lattice is quantized as $\widetilde{\Phi}_{0} = 2\pi/\widetilde{e}$, with the unit cell area $A = \widetilde{\Phi}_{0}/\widetilde{H}_{C}$.
- Surface magnetic fields of neutron stars are cited as $B \sim 10^{12} - 10^{14}$ G, magnetars as $B \sim 10^{14} - 10^{15}$ G (possibly up to $10^{16}$ G), and interior fields estimated from the virial theorem as high as $10^{18} - 10^{19}$ G.

## Context
This work extends the authors' prior studies (Ferrer and de la Incera, 2005, 2006) on how magnetic fields modify color superconductivity, specifically the MCFL (magnetic-color-flavor-locked) phase and vortex formation. The paramagnetic anti-screening effect is analogous to that found in the electroweak theory for magnetic fields above $H \geq M_{W}^{2}/e \sim 10^{24}$ G by Olesen (1999) and Skalozub (1985), and is a consequence of asymptotic freedom. The result provides a potential astrophysical signature to differentiate neutron stars with quark-matter cores from those composed entirely of nuclear matter, complementing other proposed diagnostics such as cooling processes and rotational properties.
