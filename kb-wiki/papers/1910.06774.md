# Spin Polarizations in a Covariant Angular-Momentum-Conserved Chiral Transport Model

> Using a covariant and angular-momentum-conserved chiral transport model, which takes into account the spin-orbit interactions of chiral fermions in their scatterings via the side jumps, we study the quark spin polarization in quark matter. For a system of rotating and unpolarized massless quarks in an expanding box, we find that side jumps can dynamically polarize the quark spin and result in a final quark spin polarization consistent with that of thermally equilibrated massless quarks in a self-consistent vorticity field. For the quark matter produced in noncentral relativistic heavy ion collisions, we find that in the medium rest frame both the quark local spin polarizations in the direction perpendicular to the reaction plane and along the longitudinal beam direction show an azimuthal angle dependence in the transverse plane similar to those observed in experiments for the Lambda hyperon.

## Key claim

Using a covariant chiral transport model that conserves total angular momentum via side jumps, the authors find that the azimuthal angle dependence of quark spin polarizations in the medium rest frame is similar to that observed for Lambda hyperons in experiments, even though the model does not yet include finite strange quark masses or a coalescence hadronization mechanism.

## Method

The authors extended the standard parton scattering in a transport model to include side jumps, which are anomalous coordinate shifts required to conserve the total angular momentum in two-body scatterings of chiral fermions. They implemented this using an isotropic cross section of 10 mb and a geometric scattering condition, then evolved an initially unpolarized quark matter in an expanding box with a constant vorticity field, and separately evolved quark matter from AMPT initial conditions for Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV and impact parameter 8.87 fm.

## Key numbers

- Box simulation: initial temperature 300 MeV, constant vorticity field omega = (0, 0.12, 0) (fm/c)^-1, box size 5x5x5 fm^3, expansion time 2 fm/c.
- Box simulation: spin angular momentum increases from zero to 12 hbar within 0.5 fm/c; orbital angular momentum decreases by the same amount; total angular momentum is conserved.
- Heavy ion collisions: Au+Au at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV, impact parameter b = 8.87 fm (30%-40% centrality).
- Pseudorapidity cut used: -1 < eta < 1 to mimic experimental cuts.
- The azimuthal angle dependence of P_y in the medium rest frame is of the form -a + b cos(2 phi) for the orbital contribution and -a - b cos(2 phi) for the spin contribution (a,b > 0).
- The azimuthal angle dependence of P_z in the LAB frame is sin(2 phi) for the spin contribution and -sin(2 phi) for the orbital contribution.

## Context

This work extends the covariant chiral kinetic theory developed by Chen et al. (2014, 2015) and others for side jumps in chiral fermion scattering, and addresses the "spin puzzle" raised by STAR Collaboration measurements (2019) of Lambda hyperon polarization that disagreed with predictions based on local thermal vorticity by Becattini et al. (2017) and Xia et al. (2018). The model enables future studies of spin polarization in heavy ion collisions, though the authors note that quantitative understanding of the sign problem requires including finite strange quark masses (Li et al., 2020) and an angular-momentum-conserved coalescence model for Lambda formation.
