# QCD Instantons and the Soft Pomeron

> We study the role of semi-classical QCD vacuum solutions in high energy scattering by considering the instanton contribution to hadronic cross sections. We propose a new type of instanton-induced interactions (``instanton ladder'') that leads to the rising with energy hadronic cross section of Regge type (the Pomeron). We argue that this interaction may be responsible for the structure of the soft Pomeron. The intercept is calculated. It has a non-analytic dependence on the strong coupling constant, allowing a non-singular continuation into the non-perturbative region. We derive the Pomeron trajectory, which appears to be approximately linear in some range of (negative) momentum transfer t, but exhibits a curvature at small t. Possible role of instantons in multiparticle production is also discussed.

## Key claim

The authors propose that instanton-induced interactions, specifically an "instanton ladder" resummed in the t-channel, can generate a rising hadronic cross section of Regge type (the soft Pomeron), with the intercept having a non-analytic dependence on the strong coupling constant that allows a non-singular continuation into the non-perturbative region. The calculation assumes the strong coupling at the instanton scale is small (α_s(1/ρ_0) ≪ 1), which is only marginally satisfied in QCD, and the result depends on an upper cutoff ρ_0 for the instanton size distribution.

## Method

The authors construct a multiple-gluon vertex from the BPST instanton solution in singular gauge, averaging over instanton sizes with a cutoff ρ_0 and using the instanton size distribution n(ρ). They then build a ladder diagram in the t-channel, summing over the number of produced gluons per rung (n from 2 to ∞), using the amputated Green function in momentum space with light-cone gauge for the t-channel gluons, and approximating the sum over polarizations and color orientations following Zakharov (1991). The intercept Δ_soft is extracted from the kernel of the integral equation for the forward amplitude, with the transverse momentum integration factorized and evaluated numerically.

## Key numbers

* The sphaleron energy is estimated as E_sph ≈ 2.4–2.6 GeV, using ρ ≈ (700 MeV)^(−1) and α_s(1/ρ) ≈ 0.7.
* The numerical value of the transverse momentum integral I_0 ≈ 0.014 (dimensionless, from Eq. (i01)).
* The instanton size distribution is sharply peaked at a maximum ρ_0, with the perturbative distribution increasing near ρ_0 and falling off steeply for ρ > ρ_0, as supported by lattice data (R. S. Lattice, 1998).
* The intercept Δ_soft is given by Eq. (int5) as a sum over n from 2 to ∞, involving factors of (π E_sph^4 ρ_0^4)/(6 α e^2) raised to the (n−1) power, with α = g^2/(4π).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Kharzeev and Levin (1998), which used low-energy theorems of broken scale invariance to argue that semi-classical QCD fields could generate a soft Pomeron with intercept α_P − 1 ≈ 0.1. It contrasts with Shuryak and Zahed (2000), who analyzed Euclidean instanton scattering and obtained a constant cross section not rising with energy. The paper enables future work on the full Pomeron trajectory including curvature at small t, and on the role of instantons in multiparticle production, while noting that the calculation is complementary to the BFKL Pomeron (Kuraev, Lipatov, 1977; Balitsky, Fadin, 1978) and may also apply to electroweak interactions.
