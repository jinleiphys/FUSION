# Detailed analysis of two-boson exchange in parity-violating e-p scattering

> We present a comprehensive study of two-boson exchange (TBE) corrections in parity-violating electron-proton elastic scattering. Within a hadronic framework, we compute contributions from box (and crossed box) diagrams in which the intermediate states are described by nucleons and Delta baryons. The Delta contribution is found to be much smaller than the nucleon one at backward angles (small epsilon), but becomes dominant in the forward scattering limit (epsilon -> 1), where the nucleon contribution vanishes. The dependence of the corrections on the input hadronic form factors is small for Q^2 < 1 GeV^2, but becomes significant at larger Q^2. We compute the nucleon and Delta TBE corrections relevant for recent and planned parity-violating experiments, with the total corrections ranging from -1% for forward angles to 1-2% at backward kinematics.

## Key claim

The two-boson exchange (TBE) corrections to parity-violating electron-proton scattering are computed within a hadronic framework including both nucleon and Delta(1232) intermediate states, with the Delta contribution found to be much smaller than the nucleon one at backward angles but dominant in the forward scattering limit where the nucleon contribution vanishes; the total corrections range from -1% for forward angles to 1-2% at backward kinematics, with the dependence on input hadronic form factors small for Q^2 < 1 GeV^2 but significant at larger Q^2.

## Method

The authors computed box and crossed-box diagrams for two-boson exchange (both gamma-gamma and gamma-Z interference) in parity-violating electron-proton elastic scattering using a hadronic formalism with nucleon and Delta(1232) intermediate states. The electromagnetic nucleon form factors were taken from global fits by Arrington et al. (2007) and Bosted, parameterized as sums of three monopoles, while the weak ZNN form factors used CVC relations for the vector part and a dipole fit for the axial-vector form factor with G_A(0)=1.267 and Lambda_N(A)=1 GeV. For the Delta contributions, the gamma N Delta vertex used couplings g_1=7, g_2=9, and g_3=5.8 from a K-matrix analysis of pion photoproduction data, with dipole form factors of mass Lambda_Delta(V)=0.84 GeV, while the axial Z N Delta form factors were constrained by neutrino scattering data from Lalakulich and Paschos with C_5^A(0)=1.2 and Lambda_Delta(A)=1.0 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Total TBE corrections range from -1% for forward angles to 1-2% at backward kinematics
- At Q^2=0.01 GeV^2, the nucleon TBE correction is relatively flat over epsilon range 0.1 to 0.8, then drops rapidly as epsilon approaches 1
- The Delta contribution corrections do not exceed ~1% in magnitude for epsilon less than 0.8 and Q^2 less than 1 GeV^2, and ~2% for Q^2 less than 5 GeV^2
- The axial vector charge used is G_A(0)=1.267, with dipole mass parameter Lambda_N(A)=1 GeV
- The dipole mass for the gamma N Delta transition form factors is Lambda_Delta(V)=0.84 GeV
- The axial dipole cut-off mass for the Z N Delta vertex is Lambda_Delta(A)=1.0 GeV
- The Coulomb coupling g_3=5.8 was used, with tests at g_3=-2 and 0 showing almost indistinguishable results

## Context

This work extends the earlier two-photon exchange calculations by Blunden, Melnitchouk, and Tjon (2003, 2005) and the TBE calculations with nucleon intermediate states by Yang (2007) and Tjon and Melnitchouk (2008), and with Delta intermediate states by Yang (2008), by providing a comprehensive analysis of both nucleon and Delta contributions within a unified hadronic framework. The results are directly relevant for interpreting parity-violating experiments at Jefferson Lab (HAPPEX, G0) and elsewhere (SAMPLE, A4) that aim to extract the proton's strange form factors and weak charge, and enable more precise extraction of these quantities by quantifying the model dependence of TBE corrections at the level of precision required by next-generation experiments such as Qweak.
