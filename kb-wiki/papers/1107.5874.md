# Study of elliptical flow at VECC-SCC500 energies

> We study the transverse momentum dependence of elliptical flow at VECC energies by using the projectiles having masses lying between 16 and 56 units. The detailed study in this direction will be fruitful for experimentlists.

## Key claim

The paper claims that at VECC-SCC500 energies (50 MeV/nucleon), elliptical flow and its transverse momentum dependence are sensitive to the mass asymmetry of the reaction, with projectile-like nucleons and light mass fragments experiencing more squeeze-out than target-like ones, but only for the specific case of a soft nuclear equation of state and semi-central impact parameter.

## Method

The authors used the semi-classical microscopic improved Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) model, where nucleons interact via Skyrme, Yukawa, Coulomb, momentum-dependent (MDI), and symmetry potentials, and propagate according to classical Hamilton equations of motion. They simulated several thousand events for three different projectile-target combinations (Cr+Ru, S+Sn, O+Xe) with a fixed total reacting mass of 152 units and varying mass asymmetry parameter η (0.3, 0.5, 0.7) at an incident energy of 50 MeV/nucleon and semi-central impact parameter, using a soft equation of state. The elliptical flow was quantified by the second-order Fourier coefficient V2 = ⟨cos2φ⟩ and analyzed as a function of transverse momentum, separated into contributions from target-like, mid-rapidity, and projectile-like matter for both free nucleons (A=1) and light mass fragments (LMFs, 2 ≤ A ≤ 4).

## Key numbers

- Total reacting mass fixed at 152 units for all simulated reactions.
- Mass asymmetry parameters used: η = 0.3 (Cr+Ru), η = 0.5 (S+Sn), η = 0.7 (O+Xe).
- Incident beam energy: 50 MeV/nucleon.
- Light mass fragments (LMFs) defined as fragments with mass number 2 ≤ A ≤ 4.

## Context

This work extends the study of elliptical flow to the energy range of the upcoming Super Conducting Cyclotron (SCC500) at VECC Kolkata, building on prior analyses of transverse-momentum dependence of elliptical flow by Lukasik (year not given) and the Fourier coefficient formalism of Voloshin (year not given). The results are in agreement with earlier calculations where fragments exhibited similar trends, and the authors note that the detailed study will be useful for experimentalists at the VECC facility.
