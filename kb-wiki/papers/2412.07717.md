# Probing nonperturbative transverse momentum dependent PDFs with chiral perturbation theory: the $\bar{d}-\bar{u}$ asymmetry

> We use chiral perturbation theory to study the long distance regime of transverse momentum dependent parton distribution functions (TMD PDFs). Chiral corrections to the TMD PDFs are computed from proton to pion/baryon splittings. For consistent power counting, we find that the fraction of the proton's momentum that a pion may carry must be kept small. We make predictions for a $\bar{d}-\bar{u}$ asymmetry in the proton's TMD PDFs and find that the effective theory gives a natural exponential suppression of the TMD PDF at long distances. We then explore the effects that additional nonperturbative physics may have on the TMD $\bar{d}-\bar{u}$ asymmetry.

# Probing nonperturbative transverse momentum dependent PDFs with chiral perturbation theory: the \(\bar{d}-\bar{u}\) asymmetry

## Key claim
Chiral perturbation theory can predict the \(\bar{d}-\bar{u}\) asymmetry in transverse momentum dependent parton distribution functions (TMD PDFs) at long distances, but only if the fraction of the proton's longitudinal momentum carried by the pion is restricted to small values (\(y \lesssim 0.25\)–\(0.4\)) to preserve chiral power counting; otherwise the naive convolution formalism overestimates experimental data by a factor of 10–15.

## Method
The authors compute hadronic distribution functions (HDFs) for the proton fluctuating into \(\pi^+ n\), \(\pi^+ \Delta^0\), and \(\pi^- \Delta^{++}\) intermediate states using one-loop diagrams in chiral perturbation theory with dimensional regularization and minimal subtraction. They match TMD PDFs onto chiral EFT by convolving collinear valence PDFs in the intermediate pion with TMD HDFs, separating the integration over the pion's longitudinal momentum fraction \(y\) into a small-\(y\) region (using the full chiral operator) and a large-\(y\) region (where the operator vanishes after expansion). For the TMD case, they further expand the valence TMD PDFs in the pion as collinear PDFs plus perturbative QCD matching coefficients, producing three matching prescriptions: \(\chi\)PT only, \(\chi\)PT\(\times\)pQCD, and pQCD only.

## Key numbers
- The naive convolution without the \(y_{max}\) restriction overestimates the NuSea E866 and SeaQuest E906 collinear \(\bar{d}-\bar{u}\) asymmetry data by a factor of 10–15.
- The \(y_{max}\) parameter is varied between \(0.25 \sim 2 m_\pi/M\) and \(0.4 \sim 3 m_\pi/M\) for collinear comparisons, and between 0.3 and 0.35 for TMD predictions.
- The chiral TMD HDFs scale as \(\sim K_0(b_T m_\pi)\) at large \(b_T\), giving exponential suppression; the pQCD-only result shows no such suppression.
- The Gaussian model for additional nonperturbative effects uses \(\kappa = 200\) MeV and \(\kappa = 400\) MeV, producing additional suppression at \(b_T \sim 5\) GeV\(^{-1}\).
- Pion PDFs and matching coefficients are evaluated at \(\mu^2 = 54\) GeV\(^2\) to compare with E866 data.

## Context
This work extends chiral perturbation theory calculations of the collinear \(\bar{d}-\bar{u}\) asymmetry, previously studied by Salamu (2014, 2019), Barry (2018), and Alberg (2021) using hard cutoff, Pauli-Villars, or dipole regulators, to the TMD case using dimensional regularization for the first time. It builds on the TMD matching formalism of Copeland (2024) and He (2019). The results enable experimental tests of the \(\bar{d}-\bar{u}\) asymmetry in TMD PDFs, which could distinguish whether long-distance TMD behavior is dominated by chiral dynamics or by additional nonperturbative effects such as confinement. The authors suggest future extensions to SU(3) \(\chi\)PT calculations of \(s-\bar{s}\) asymmetries and to valence quark TMD PDFs.
