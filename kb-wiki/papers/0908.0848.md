# Single photo and electroproduction of pions at EBAC@JLAB

> Within the Excited Baryon Analysis Center we have performed a dynamical coupled-channels analysis of the available $p(e,e' π)N$ data in the region of $W \leq $ 1.6 GeV and $Q^2 \leq$ 1.45 (GeV/c)$^2$. The channels included are $γ^* N$, $πN$, $ηN$, and $ππN$ which has $πΔ$, $ρN$, and $σN$ components. With the hadronic parameters of the model determined in our previous investigations of $πN\to πN$ reaction, we have found that the available data in the considered $W \leq $ 1.6 GeV region can be fitted well by only adjusting the bare $γ^* N \to N^*$ helicity amplitudes for the lowest $N^*$ states in $P_{33}$, $P_{11}$, $S_{11}$ and $D_{13}$ partial waves. The meson cloud effect, as required by the unitarity conditions, on the $γ^* N \to N^*$ form factors are examined.

# Single photo and electroproduction of pions at EBAC@JLAB

## Key claim

Within a dynamical coupled-channels model, the available $p(e,e'\pi)N$ data up to $W \leq 1.6$ GeV and $Q^2 \leq 1.45$ (GeV/c)$^2$ can be fitted well by adjusting only the bare $\gamma^* N \to N^*$ helicity amplitudes for the lowest $N^*$ states in $P_{33}$, $P_{11}$, $S_{11}$, and $D_{13}$ partial waves, but the extracted helicity amplitudes show significant variation depending on which subset of data is included in the fit, indicating that the available data are insufficient to uniquely determine the transition form factors.

## Method

A dynamical coupled-channels model was employed with channels $\gamma^* N$, $\pi N$, $\eta N$, and $\pi\pi N$ (including $\pi\Delta$, $\rho N$, and $\sigma N$ components). The hadronic parameters were fixed from previous analyses of $\pi N \to \pi N$ reactions, and only the bare $\gamma^* N \to N^*$ helicity amplitudes were varied to fit the $p(e,e'\pi^0)p$ and $p(e,e'\pi^+)n$ structure function data ($\sigma_T+\epsilon\sigma_L$, $\sigma_{LT}$, $\sigma_{TT}$, and $\sigma_{LT'}$) at each $Q^2$ independently using the MINUIT library.

## Key numbers

- Data analyzed up to $W \leq 1.6$ GeV and $Q^2 \leq 1.45$ (GeV/c)$^2$
- $Q^2$ values for $\gamma^* p \to \pi^0 p$ structure functions: 0.4, 0.525, 0.65, 0.75, 0.9, 1.15, and 1.46 (GeV/c)$^2$; for $\sigma_{LT'}$ only at 0.4 and 0.65 (GeV/c)$^2$
- $Q^2$ values for $\gamma^* p \to \pi^+ n$ structure functions: 0.3, 0.4, 0.5, and 0.6 (GeV/c)$^2$; for $\sigma_{LT'}$ only at 0.4 and 0.65 (GeV/c)$^2$
- Coupled-channels effect on $\sigma_T$ at high $W \sim 1.5$ GeV is small (10-20%) already at $Q^2 = 0.4$ (GeV/c)$^2$, compared to about 30-40% at $Q^2=0$
- Only 4 out of 11 independent $p(e,e'\pi)N$ observables were available for the analysis

## Context

This work extends the dynamical coupled-channels analysis previously performed for pion photoproduction by Julia-Diaz et al. (2008) to the electroproduction regime. The model builds on the hadronic framework developed by Matsuyama et al. (2007), Julia-Diaz et al. (2007), and others for $\pi N \to \pi N$ and $\pi\pi N$ reactions. The results indicate that complete or over-complete measurements of polarization observables, such as those performed by Kelly et al. in the $\Delta(1232)$ region and planned at JLab, are necessary to reliably extract $\gamma^* N \to N^*$ transition form factors for comparison with lattice QCD predictions and hadron structure models.
