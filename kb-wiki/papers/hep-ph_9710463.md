# Massive gluons and quarks and the equation of state obtained from SU(3) lattice QCD

> We analyze recent results of SU(3) lattice QCD calculations with a phenomenological parametrization for the quark-gluon plasma equation of state based on a quasi-particle picture with massive quarks and gluons. At high temperature we obtain a good fit to the lattice data using perturbative thermal quark and gluon masses from an improved HTL scheme. At temperatures close to the confinement phase transition the fitted masses increase above the perturbative value, and a non-zero (but small) bag constant is required to fit the lattice data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a quasi-particle model with massive gluons and quarks, combined with a small temperature-dependent bag constant, can accurately reproduce SU(3) lattice QCD results for the equation of state, but only if the effective masses are allowed to exceed perturbative values near the phase transition temperature.

## Method

The authors fit lattice QCD data for the pressure and energy density of pure SU(3) gauge theory and for QCD with N_f=2 and N_f=4 dynamical fermions using a thermodynamically consistent quasi-particle model. They extract temperature-dependent effective gluon and quark masses from the perturbative Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) expressions, with a running coupling constant g(T) parametrized by a function K(T/T_c), and determine a temperature-dependent bag constant B(T) to account for non-perturbative effects not absorbed by the masses.

## Key numbers

- For pure SU(3) gauge theory at high temperature (T > 2.3 T_c), the effective number of gluonic degrees of freedom is D(T) = 14.5 ± 1, close to the perturbative expectation of 16 transverse gluon modes.
- The effective gluon mass at T > 2 T_c corresponds to a coupling constant g = √2 M_g(T)/T ≈ 1.3–1.4, consistent with α ≈ 0.15 from the interquark potential.
- At high temperatures (T > 2.3 T_c), the bag constant B(T) is only about 2–3% of the total energy density, though slightly negative.
- The function K(T/T_c) extracted from the running coupling approaches a constant K = 18 ± 8 at high temperature and decreases to K ≈ 1.5 near T_c.
- For pure SU(3), the critical temperature is T_c = 260 MeV with T_c/Λ_MS = 1.03 ± 0.19; for N_f=4, T_c = 170 MeV with T_c/Λ_MS = 1.05; for N_f=2, T_c = 140 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier quasi-particle analyses of pure SU(2) and SU(3) lattice data by Gorenstein et al. (1993) and Peshier et al. (1996) to include dynamical quarks with N_f=2 and N_f=4 flavors, which had not been published previously. The extracted temperature-dependent masses and bag constant provide a complete analytical parametrization of the QCD equation of state that can be used in phenomenological hadronization models such as the ALCOR coalescence model and in transport descriptions of heavy ion collisions.
