# Nucleon-Nucleon Short-Range Correlations and Gribov Inelastic Shadowing in High Energy Hadron-Nucleus Collisions

> Different types of high-energy hadron-nucleus cross sections are discussed emphasizing the role played by Nucleon-Nucleon (NN) Short-Range Correlations (SRC) and Gribov Inelastic Shadowing (IS)

## Key claim

Nucleon-Nucleon Short-Range Correlations (SRC) and Gribov Inelastic Shadowing (IS) both modify the standard Glauber multiple scattering description of high-energy hadron-nucleus collisions, but they act in opposite directions: SRC increase the total cross section (making the nucleus more opaque), while Gribov IS increases nuclear transparency. The net effect depends on the specific cross section channel and the energy regime.

## Method

The author calculates total, elastic, quasi-elastic, inelastic, and diffractive dissociation cross sections for hadron-nucleus collisions by extending the Glauber multiple scattering theory to include both SRC (via two-body density contractions in the nuclear wave function) and Gribov IS (summed to all orders using the dipole approach). The nuclear thickness function is modified by a correlation contribution derived from the two-body density contraction, and the dipole-nucleon cross section and profile function are used to incorporate the transverse dimensions and light-cone variables of the dipole. Calculations are performed for p-208Pb collisions at RHIC and LHC energies using realistic one- and two-body densities and correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- For p-208Pb at LHC energies (sigma_in_NN = 68.30 mb): Glauber-only total cross section = 3850.63 mb; Glauber+SRC total cross section = 3885.77 mb; Glauber+SRC+Gribov (q-2q model) total cross section = 3833.26 mb
- For p-208Pb at LHC energies: Glauber-only elastic cross section = 1664.76 mb; Glauber+SRC elastic cross section = 1690.48 mb; Glauber+SRC+Gribov (q-2q model) elastic cross section = 1655.70 mb
- For p-208Pb at LHC energies: Glauber-only quasi-elastic cross section = 120.92 mb; Glauber+SRC quasi-elastic cross section = 112.65 mb; Glauber+SRC+Gribov (q-2q model) quasi-elastic cross section = 113.37 mb
- Number of inelastic collisions N_coll for p-208Pb at LHC: Glauber = 6.88; Glauber+SRC = 6.82; Glauber+SRC+Gribov (q-2q) = 6.88
- Number of inelastic collisions N_coll for p-208Pb at RHIC (sigma_in_NN = 42.10 mb): Glauber = 4.70; Glauber+SRC = 4.65; Glauber+SRC+Gribov (q-2q) = 4.75

## Context

This work extends the standard Glauber multiple scattering theory (Glauber, 1959) by incorporating both SRC and Gribov IS, which are absent in the independent-particle model description of the nucleus. The SRC effects are motivated by experimental evidence from Subedi (2008) showing correlated nucleon configurations in 12C, and the theoretical two-body densities are taken from calculations by Panda (2005). The Gribov IS corrections are treated using the dipole approach developed by Kopeliovich, Lapidus, and Zakharov (KLZ) and further by Boris Kopeliovich and collaborators. The results enable more accurate calculations of hard reaction cross sections in heavy-ion collisions, particularly for the normalization factor N_coll used to obtain nucleus-to-nucleon ratios.
