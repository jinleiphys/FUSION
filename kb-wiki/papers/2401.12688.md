# Uncertainty quantification in the machine-learning inference from neutron star probability distribution to the equation of state

> We discuss the machine-learning inference and uncertainty quantification for the equation of state (EoS) of the neutron star (NS) matter directly using the NS probability distribution from the observations. We previously proposed a prescription for uncertainty quantification based on ensemble learning by evaluating output variance from independently trained models. We adopt a different principle for uncertainty quantification to confirm the reliability of our previous results. To this end, we carry out the MC sampling of data to infer an EoS and take the convolution with the probability distribution of the observational data. In this newly proposed method, we can deal with arbitrary probability distribution not relying on the Gaussian approximation. We incorporate observational data from the recent multimessenger sources including precise mass measurements and radius measurements. We also quantify the importance of data augmentation and the effects of prior dependence.

## Key claim

The authors propose an alternative method for uncertainty quantification in machine-learning inference of the neutron star equation of state, using Monte Carlo sampling of observational data convolved with the full probability distribution of mass and radius measurements, which does not rely on Gaussian approximations and can handle arbitrary distribution shapes.

## Method

The authors train a deep neural network to map sets of neutron star mass-radius pairs to a piecewise polytropic parameterization of the equation of state (EoS) expressed as squared sound speeds at different densities. They then perform Monte Carlo integration over the input space by sampling mass-radius points according to the full observational probability distributions, and take the ensemble average to obtain the probability distribution of the EoS parameters. The training dataset is generated by randomly sampling EoS parameters, solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation to obtain mass-radius curves, and adding Gaussian noise to match the standard deviations of the observational data.

## Key numbers

- The squared sound speed from the previous ML method rose to \(c_s^2 \simeq 0.61 \pm 0.16\) (with 68% credence) around \(\varepsilon / \varepsilon_0 \simeq 4\)–\(5\), where \(\varepsilon_0\) is the energy density at nuclear saturation density \(n_0 \simeq 0.16~\text{fm}^{-3}\)
- The high-density part around \(\varepsilon/\varepsilon_0 \simeq 6\)–\(8\) gave \(c_s^2 \simeq 0.55 \pm 0.10\) in the previous work
- The nuclear saturation density is \(n_0 \simeq 0.16~\text{fm}^{-3}\)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous machine-learning inference approach (Fujimoto 2019, Fujimoto 2021) by replacing the bagging-based uncertainty quantification with a Monte Carlo convolution method that preserves the full shape of observational probability distributions. The new method addresses limitations of the previous Gaussian approximation and discarding of mass-radius correlations, and incorporates newer data including the NICER radius measurement of the heavy neutron star J0740+6620 (Miller 2021, Riley 2021). The approach is complementary to Bayesian inference methods (Steiner 2010, Ozel 2015) and Gaussian process methods (Landry 2018) used in the community, and enables more reliable uncertainty quantification for the inferred equation of state.
