# Is isospin I=2 assignment preferable for d_1^*(1956) resonance candidate seen in reaction pp-> 2 gamma X below pion threshold ?

> We compare the virtues of two options for the isospin (I=1 or 2) of the apparently exotic dibarion resonance d_1^*(1956), as it is noticed by the DIB2gamma Collaboration (JINR) in the reaction pp -> 2 gamma X below the pion threshold, and using recent results following from the RMC Collaboration (TRIUMF) measurement of the relative probability of the double-photon pion capture in the pi-mesic deuterium atoms.

## Key claim

The paper argues that assigning isospin I=2 to the exotic dibaryon resonance candidate d₁*(1956) is preferable over I=1, because this assignment would suppress the resonance excitation in the double-radiative pion capture on deuterium enough to reconcile the positive signal in pp → 2γX with the non-observation of resonance effects in the RMC Collaboration experiment, provided additional hindrance from spin-parity selection also operates.

## Method

The authors compare the predicted branching ratio for the reaction π⁻d → γ d₁*(1956) → 2γ nn under two isospin assignments (I=1 or I=2) using an effective NΔ cluster model within an SU(6)×O(3) symmetry basis. They estimate the suppression factor for the I=2 case by evaluating the Kroll-Ruderman term cancellation and the retardation factor from the P-wave nn intermediate state, and also derive an upper bound from scaling the measured pp → γ d₁* cross section to the pion capture kinematics.

## Key numbers

- The mass of the d₁* resonance candidate is 1956 ± 6 MeV, from the DIB2γ Collaboration experiment at initial proton kinetic energy 216 MeV in the lab system.
- The statistical significances for the narrow and broad peaks in the pp → 2γX photon spectrum are 5.3σ and 3.5σ, respectively.
- The experimental branching ratio for π⁻d → 2γ 2n from the RMC Collaboration is 1.6 × 10⁻⁵.
- Under the I=1 assumption, the model predicts BR(π⁻d → γ d₁* → 2γ nn) of order 10⁻³, about two orders of magnitude larger than the experimental value.
- For the I=2 case with the NΔ model, the estimated suppression factor K is approximately 2.7 × 10⁻³, bringing the predicted branching ratio down to O(10⁻⁷ to 10⁻⁶).
- The upper bound derived from scaling the experimental pp → γ d₁* cross section (≈ 9 nb) yields BR ≤ 9 × 10⁻⁵ when multiplied by a hindrance factor of 2 × 10⁻³.
- The correction to the Kroll-Ruderman term in the deuteron is of order 4 × 10⁻⁴.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier model (Gerasimov 1998, 2000, 2001) for the narrow dibaryon resonance seen by the DIB2γ Collaboration (2001) and addresses the apparent contradiction with the non-observation of resonance effects in the RMC Collaboration measurement (Zolotarev 2000, TRIUMF 2003). The analysis enables future experimental discrimination by predicting that at initial nucleon kinetic energy T_N ≈ 163 MeV, the reaction pn → d₁* → pn could proceed via isospin-violating mechanisms while pp or nn reactions would be Pauli-forbidden, and suggests searching for the resonance in the KVI experiment at T_p = 190 MeV where a narrower photon peak at ω ≈ 13 MeV is expected.
