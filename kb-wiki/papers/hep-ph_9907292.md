# Condensation of a Strongly Interacting Parton Plasma into a Hadron Gas in High Energy Nuclear Collisions

> We examine the effects of color screening on the transition of a parton plasma into a hadron gas at RHIC energies. It is found that as expected, color screening posed itself as a significant barrier for hadronization. Parton-hadron conversion would therefore be delayed and prolonged when compared to that occuring in a vacuum. Due to the on-going expansion, the resulting hadron densities are lowered. Parton equilibration is also shown to be seriously disrupted in the process.

## Key claim

Color screening in a parton plasma at RHIC energies acts as a significant barrier to hadronization, delaying and prolonging the parton-hadron conversion compared to vacuum, and lowering the resulting hadron densities due to ongoing expansion; the effect is conditional on the screening length being comparable to or smaller than typical hadron sizes.

## Method

The author extended a previously developed one-dimensional expansion model for a parton plasma (based on a reduced Boltzmann equation with relaxation time approximation) to include hadronization into pions and kaons via clustering of color-singlet quark-antiquark pairs. A non-perturbative gluon splitting mechanism was introduced to convert gluons into quarks, and color screening effects were incorporated by weighting hadron formation and dissolution probabilities with a distribution of internal quark-antiquark separation compared to the screening length. The time evolution equations for parton and hadron distribution functions were solved both with and without color screening to compare the results.

## Key numbers

* Screening length at T ~ 200 MeV: l_D ~ 0.16 fm (using lattice results where m_D ~ 3.3 times the leading-order value m_D^LO ~ 405 MeV).
* Screening length at T ~ 150 MeV: l_D ~ 0.20 fm (using m_D^LO ~ 332 MeV scaled by the same factor of 3.3).
* Screening length from the author's earlier time evolution at estimated temperature 200 MeV: l_D ~ 0.4 fm.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies of parton plasma time evolution (Wong, 1998) by adding the hadronization stage and medium effects, which were neglected in earlier hadronization schemes such as that of the Parton Cascade Model (Geiger and Muller, 1992). The results contradict the assumption that a fully equilibrated quark-gluon plasma should be expected before hadronization, and they highlight the need to incorporate color screening into numerical models for RHIC, as the delay and lower hadron densities will affect estimates of hadron gas backgrounds to proposed quark-gluon plasma signatures.
