# $Ψ'/Ψ$ ratio in Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions : a Measure for the Chiral Symmetry Restoration Temperature ?

> We argue that a decrease of the chiral scalar meson mass is responsible for re-creation of $Ψ'$ from $J/Ψ$ in ultrarelativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions. This causes the charmonium yields to freeze out at temperatures close to the chiral symmetry restoration temperature $T_c$. As a result $Ψ'/Ψ$ may serve as a thermometer for $T_c$ itself. Results in a detailed reaction model support the conjecture. They show good agreement with recent data of NA38 and NA50 for $J/Ψ$ and $Ψ'$ production in S on U and Pb on Pb collisions.

## Key claim

The observed leveling-off of the Psi'/Psi ratio in non-peripheral Pb+Pb collisions is not due to accidental fine-tuning of survival rates, but because Psi' is re-created from J/Psi via a dropping scalar (sigma) meson mass near the chiral symmetry restoration temperature. The ratio may therefore serve as a thermometer for T_c itself, provided the initial energy densities in collisions are clearly above 2 GeV/fm^3.

## Method

The authors calculate charmonium transition rates between J/Psi and Psi' in thermal matter using a linear sigma model at tree level, where the sigma meson mass decreases with temperature tracking the quark condensate. They implement the temperature-dependent transition rates into a simple reaction model with Glauber initial production, isentropic expansion, and coupled rate equations for charmonium densities, then compare the calculated yields to NA38 and NA50 data for S+U and Pb+Pb collisions.

## Key numbers

- The measured Psi'/Psi ratio levels off at around 4% in non-peripheral Pb+Pb collisions, roughly a factor of 4 lower than in pp and pA reactions.
- The vacuum sigma meson mass is fixed at 1400 MeV; the coupling constant g^2/4pi is approximately 0.36 from the partial decay width Gamma(Psi' -> Psi pi pi).
- The critical energy densities for immediate charmonium dissolution are set at 3.2 GeV/fm^3 (J/Psi), 1.5 GeV/fm^3 (Psi'), and 2.4 GeV/fm^3 (chi).
- Maximum local energy densities for most central collisions are 2.3 GeV/fm^3 (S+U) and 3.5 GeV/fm^3 (Pb+Pb).
- The Psi'/Psi ratio changes from 5.64% before feed-down from chi states to 4.07% after feed-down in central Pb+Pb collisions.
- Best agreement with data is found for T_c = 170 MeV; calculations are also shown for T_c = 150 and 160 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of anomalous J/Psi suppression observed by the NA50 collaboration, which had been interpreted as a hint for quark-gluon plasma formation (Blaizot and Ollitrault, 1996; Kharzeev, 1996; Wong, 1996). It offers an alternative explanation to the comover dissociation models (Gavin and Vogt, 1997; Capella et al., 1996) by proposing that the Psi'/Psi ratio equilibrates near T_c due to chiral symmetry restoration. The mechanism enables using the Psi'/Psi ratio as a thermometer for the chiral transition temperature, and predicts that the ratio will not decrease further in more violent collisions at RHIC and LHC.
