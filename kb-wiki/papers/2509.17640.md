# Thermodynamics of rotating fermions

> We consider the thermodynamic properties of a rotating gas of fermions. We begin by constructing the thermodynamic potential $Φ$ and its associated current $ϕ^μ$ within the grand canonical ensemble of a macroscopic rigidly rotating body, where the ensemble parameters are the temperature $T_0$ and chemical potential $μ_0$ on the rotation axis, as well as the rotation angular velocity $Ω_0$. We then consider the problem of local thermodynamics, where the thermodynamic state is defined by the local temperature $T$ and chemical potential $μ$, as well as the local spin potential tensor, $Ω_{μν}$. We find the thermodynamic pressure $P$, given as the sum of the usual classical (non-quantum) pressure and other corrections due to the spin potential and the kinematic state of the fluid. We compute the associated entropy, charge and spin densities, and show they are consistent with the Euler relation.

# Thermodynamics of rotating fermions

## Key claim

The paper provides a thermodynamically consistent description of a rigidly rotating gas of massless Dirac fermions, showing that the thermodynamic pressure can simultaneously satisfy both the Euler relation and the differential thermodynamic relations (entropy, charge, and spin densities as derivatives of pressure), but only if the pressure is allowed to depend on both the spin potential and the vorticity tensor separately, and if a dynamic pressure is introduced to absorb the difference between effective and thermodynamic pressure.

## Method

The authors start from the grand canonical ensemble for a rigidly rotating system with global parameters (temperature T₀, chemical potential μ₀, angular velocity Ω₀) and construct the thermodynamic potential Φ and its associated four-current φ^μ. They then transition to a local thermodynamic description using the local temperature T = Γ T₀ and chemical potential μ = Γ μ₀ (where Γ is the Lorentz factor of rigid rotation), and introduce the spin potential tensor Ω_{μν} as a distinct thermodynamic parameter from the kinematic vorticity ω_{μν}. For the exactly-solvable case of massless fermions, they compute analytic expressions for the thermodynamic potential current, pressure, entropy density, charge density, and spin density, and verify consistency with the Euler relation and differential thermodynamic identities.

## Key numbers

- Classical pressure: P_cl = 7π² T⁴/180 + μ² T²/6 + μ⁴/(12π²)
- Classical axial vortical conductivity: σ^ω_{A;cl} = T²/6 + μ²/(2π²)
- Classical charge density: Q_{V;cl} = μ T²/3 + μ³/(3π²)
- Classical entropy density: s_cl = 7π² T³/45 + μ² T/3
- Quantum correction to entropy density: s = s_cl − T(Ω² + a²)/12, where Ω² and a² are the squared spin-potential magnetic component and acceleration
- Quantum correction to charge density: Q = Q_cl − μ(Ω² + a²)/(4π²)
- Quantum correction to axial vortical conductivity: σ^ω_A = σ^ω_{A;cl} − (Ω² + 3a²)/(24π²)
- Dynamic pressure: Π = (a²/6) σ^ω_{A;cl} − (Ω² a² + (44/45) ω² a² + (17/5) a⁴)/(48π²)

## Context

This work extends the line of research on rotating quantum gases initiated by Vilenkin (late 1970s), who showed that rotating neutrinos develop an axial flow (the axial vortical effect). It addresses a long-standing problem raised by Becattini and collaborators (2013–2025) regarding the thermodynamic consistency of rotating fermion systems, where previous approaches could not simultaneously satisfy the Euler relation and the differential thermodynamic relations. The results provide a quantum-field-theoretical foundation for spin hydrodynamics, enabling thermodynamically consistent formulations of fluid dynamics with spin and vorticity, and are consistent with earlier analytic results for massless fermions derived by Buzzegoli, Becattini, Prokhorov, and Palermo (2017–2021).
