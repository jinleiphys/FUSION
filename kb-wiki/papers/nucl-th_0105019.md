# Uncorrelated scattering approximation for the scattering and break-up of weakly bound nuclei on heavy targets

> The scattering of a weakly bound (halo) projectile nucleus by a heavy target nucleus is investigated. A new approach, called the Uncorrelated Scattering Approximation, is proposed. The main approximation involved is to neglect the correlation between the fragments of the projectile in the region where the interaction with the target is important. The formalism makes use of hyper-spherical harmonics, Raynal-Revay coefficients and momentum-localized wave functions to expand projectile channel wave functions in terms of products of the channel wave function of the individual fragments. Within this approach, the kinetic energy and angular momentum of each fragment is conserved during the scattering process. The elastic, inelastic and break-up S-matrices are obtained as an analytic combination involving the bound wave function of the projectile and the product of the S-matrices of the fragments. The approach is applied to describe the scattering of deuteron on $^{58}$Ni at several energies. The results are compared with experimental data and continuum-discretized coupled-channels calculations.

## Key claim

The Uncorrelated Scattering Approximation (USA) neglects the correlation between projectile fragments in the region where tidal forces from the target dominate over the binding force between fragments, with the critical distance \(R_m\) (or equivalently the hyper-angular momentum \(K_m\)) separating the region where the folding potential approximation applies from the region where the fragments scatter independently.

## Method

The authors expand the projectile-target wave function in a basis of hyper-spherical harmonics characterized by the hyper-angular momentum \(K\). For components with \(K > K_m\), the Hamiltonian is approximated by the folding potential \(H^F\), which does not excite or break the projectile. For components with \(K \le K_m\), the Hamiltonian is approximated by \(\bar H\), in which the two fragments \(A\) and \(B\) interact independently with the target via their own optical potentials, and the binding potential \(v_{AB}\) is replaced by a constant. The S-matrix for the composite system is then obtained as the sum of the folding S-matrix (diagonal in the channel basis) plus a correction term \(\Delta S = P(\bar S - S_F)P\) that accounts for excitation and break-up, where \(\bar S\) is expressed as the product of the fragment S-matrices \(S_A(L_A, E_A^\ell) S_B(L_B, E_B^\ell)\) in a basis of momentum-localized states.

## Key numbers

- The critical angular momentum \(L_m\) is defined by the turning-point condition: \({L_m (L_m+1) \over 2 M {R_m}^2} + v_f(R_m) = E - \epsilon_0\).
- The number of momentum-localized states is \(n_\ell = [(K_m - L_A - L_B)/2] + 1\).
- The hyper-momentum is given by \({\cal P}^2/2M = E - \bar v\), and the hyper-radius by \({\cal R}^2 = R^2 + r^2 m/M\).
- The fragment energies in the MLS basis are \(E_A^\ell = (E - \bar v) \cos^2(\beta_\ell)\) and \(E_B^\ell = (E - \bar v) \sin^2(\beta_\ell)\).

## Context

This work extends the sudden approximation approach of the Aarhus group (Hansen, Jensen, Jonson, 1995; Zhukov, 1993) and the adiabatic approach of the Surrey group (Johnson, 1997; Tostevin, 1998) by treating the interactions of all fragments with the target on the same footing, rather than assuming instantaneous removal or core-only interaction. The USA formalism enables analytic calculation of elastic, inelastic, and break-up S-matrices for weakly bound (halo) projectiles on heavy targets, and is applied to deuteron scattering on \(^{58}\)Ni as a test case. The approach can be extended to three-body halo systems and to inclusive break-up cross sections integrated over fragment energies.
