# The sextic oscillator as a $γ$-independent potential

> The sextic oscillator is proposed as a two-parameter solvable $γ$-independent potential in the Bohr Hamiltonian. It is shown that closed analytical expressions can be derived for the energies and wavefunctions of the first few levels and for the strength of electric quadrupole transitions between them. Depending on the parameters this potential has a minimum at $β=0$ or at $β>0$, and might also have a local maximum before reaching its minimum. A comparison with the spectral properties of the infinite square well and the $β^4$ potential is presented, together with a brief analysis of the experimental spectrum and E2 transitions of the $^{134}$Ba nucleus.

# The sextic oscillator as a γ-independent potential

## Key claim
The sextic oscillator, a quasi-exactly solvable potential, can serve as a flexible two-parameter γ-independent potential in the Bohr Hamiltonian, providing closed analytical expressions for the energies and wavefunctions of the first few levels and for E2 transition strengths. However, the potential is not exactly solvable in the classical sense; only the lowest few solutions (corresponding to small principal quantum numbers) can be obtained algebraically, which is sufficient for the Bohr Hamiltonian because few levels with the same angular momentum are typically needed.

## Method
The authors map the sextic oscillator Hamiltonian with a centrifugal barrier onto the radial Schrödinger equation derived from the Bohr Hamiltonian for γ-independent potentials. By setting the radial variable x equal to β and choosing s = τ/2 + 5/4, they obtain quasi-exactly solvable solutions for M=0 and M=1, which yield the ground state and first excited state for each τ value. The wavefunctions take the form of a polynomial times a Gaussian factor exp(-aβ⁴/4 - bβ²/2), and the normalization integrals are expressed in closed form using parabolic cylinder functions or confluent hypergeometric functions.

## Key numbers
- For M=1, τ=0: energies are E₁,₀ = 7b - 2(b² + 10a)^(1/2) + u₀⁺ and E₂,₀ = 7b + 2(b² + 10a)^(1/2) + u₀⁺
- For M=1, τ=1: energies are E₁,₁ = 9b - 2(b² + 14a)^(1/2) + u₀⁻ and E₂,₁ = 9b + 2(b² + 14a)^(1/2) + u₀⁻
- For M=0, τ=2: energy is E₁,₂ = 9b + u₀⁺
- For M=0, τ=3: energy is E₁,₃ = 11b + u₀⁻
- For the ¹³⁴Ba comparison, parameters a=40000 and b=200 were used
- The potential minimum at β>0 occurs when b² < 4ac^π, i.e., for -2(ac^π)^(1/2) < b < 2(ac^π)^(1/2)
- A local maximum before the minimum appears when b² > 4ac^π and b < 0, i.e., b < -2(ac^π)^(1/2)

## Context
This work extends the family of exactly solvable γ-independent potentials for the Bohr Hamiltonian, which previously included the harmonic oscillator (Wilets and Jean, 1956), the Coulomb and Kratzer potentials (Fortunato, 2002), and the infinite square well used in the E(5) symmetry (Iachello, 2000). The sextic oscillator offers more flexible potential shapes than these alternatives, including the possibility of a minimum at β=0, a minimum at β>0, or a local maximum followed by a minimum. The authors compare their results with the infinite square well and the β⁴ potential, and present a preliminary application to ¹³⁴Ba, the first candidate for E(5) symmetry (Casten, 2000). The approach enables modeling the full shape phase transition from spherical to deformed γ-unstable nuclei by varying the two parameters a and b, and could potentially be extended to describe X(5) symmetry using potential shapes with both a local maximum and minimum at β>0.
