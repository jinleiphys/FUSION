# Updated analysis of $π$N elastic scattering data to 2.1~GeV: The Baryon Spectrum

> We present the results of energy-dependent and single-energy partial-wave analyses of $π$N elastic scattering data with laboratory kinetic energies below 2.1~GeV. Resonance structures have been extracted using Breit-Wigner fits, speed plots, and a complex plane mapping of the associated poles and zeroes. This is the first set of resonance parameters from a VPI analysis constrained by fixed-t dispersion relations. We have searched our solutions for structures which may have been missed in our previous analyses, finding candidates in the $S_{11}$ and $F_{15}$ partial-wave amplitudes. Our results are compared with those found by the Karlsruhe, Carnegie-Mellon$-$Berkeley, and Kent State groups.

## Key claim

This analysis presents the first set of resonance parameters from a VPI (Virginia Polytechnic Institute) partial-wave analysis of pion-nucleon elastic scattering that is constrained by fixed-t dispersion relations, and it finds candidate new structures in the S11 and F15 partial-wave amplitudes that may correspond to previously reported 1-star or 2-star resonances.

## Method

Energy-dependent and single-energy partial-wave analyses were performed on pion-nucleon elastic scattering data up to a laboratory kinetic energy of 2.1 GeV, using a coupled-channel Chew-Mandelstam K-matrix parameterization. The fits were constrained by fixed-t dispersion relations in the range 0 to -0.3 (GeV/c)^2, and resonance parameters were extracted using Breit-Wigner fits, speed plots, and complex plane mapping of poles and zeroes. An additional search for missing structures was conducted by sweeping an adjustable Breit-Wigner resonance contribution through each partial wave.

## Key numbers

- Pion-nucleon coupling constant determined as g^2/4pi = 13.75 +/- 0.15 from a previous study (Arndt 1994), with the present analysis finding a minimum chi^2 value of 46,221 for g^2/4pi = 13.77 using J_GMO = -1.05 mb and a_pi-p = 0.085 mu^{-1}
- Database consisted of 10,197 pi+ p data points, 9,421 pi- p data points, and 1,625 charge-exchange data points in the SM95 solution, with chi^2 values of 22,593, 18,855, and 4,442 respectively
- Single-energy solutions were produced up to 2026 MeV center-of-mass energy, with the energy-dependent fit using 94 parameters for I=1/2 and 80 parameters for I=3/2
- Two candidate new structures were identified: one in the S11 partial wave approximately 150 MeV below the S11(2090) 1-star resonance, and one in the F15 partial wave near 1882 MeV (comparable to the Karlsruhe group's F15(2000) 2-star resonance)

## Context

This work supersedes the authors' previous SM90 analysis (Arndt 1991) and extends the FA93 analysis (Arndt 1994) that focused on the pion-nucleon coupling constant, now concentrating on the baryon spectrum. The results are compared with analyses from the Karlsruhe group (Hohler 1983, 1992, 1994), the Carnegie-Mellon-Berkeley group (Kelly and Cutkosky 1979), and the Kent State group (Manley and Saleski 1992). The new amplitudes will serve as input for an upcoming analysis of pion photoproduction data, and the candidate S11 and F15 resonances are of particular interest as they have no assigned photo-coupling estimates in the Review of Particle Properties.
