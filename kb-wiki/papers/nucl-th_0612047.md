# Nuclear Superfluidity in Exotic Nuclei and Neutron Stars

> Nuclear superfludity in exotic nuclei close to the drip lines and in the inner crust matter of neutron stars have common features which can be treated with the same theoretical tools. In the first part of my lecture I discuss how two such tools, namely the HFB approach and the linear response theory can be used to describe the pairing correlations in weakly bound nuclei, in which the unbound part of the energy spectrum becomes important. Then, using the same models, I shall discuss how the nuclear superfluidity can affect the thermal properties of the inner crust of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The pairing correlations in weakly bound nuclei near the drip lines and in the inner crust of neutron stars can be treated with the same theoretical tools (HFB approach and linear response theory), but the specific heat and cooling time of the inner crust depend very strongly on the intensity of pairing correlations in nuclear matter, which remains uncertain.

## Method

The author applies the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approach with a Skyrme mean field and a zero-range density-dependent pairing force to describe pairing correlations in neutron-rich oxygen isotopes and in Wigner-Seitz cells representing the inner crust of neutron stars. For the oxygen isotopes, the continuum coupling is treated by including resonant quasiparticle states via phase shift analysis, and collective excitations are calculated using the linear response theory (QRPA Green's function). For the inner crust, finite-temperature HFB (FT-HFB) equations are solved for spherical Wigner-Seitz cells with Dirichlet-Neumann boundary conditions, and the specific heat and cooling time are computed from the resulting quasiparticle spectra and collective modes.

## Key numbers

- For the oxygen isotopes, the HF single-particle energy of the 1d₃/₂ state is given as (0.46;0.02) MeV for ¹⁸O, (0.51;0.03) MeV for ²⁰O, and (0.42;0.02) MeV for ²²O, where the numbers in parentheses indicate the resonance energy and width.
- The cHFB quasiparticle energy of the 1f₇/₂ state is (12.77;1.13) MeV for ¹⁸O, (12.14;0.83) MeV for ²⁰O, and (10.05;0.69) MeV for ²²O.
- For the inner crust cell ¹⁸⁰⁰Sn, at T=0.1 MeV the presence of the nuclear cluster increases the specific heat by about 6 times compared to the value for the uniform neutron gas, using the first pairing force.
- For the same cell at T=0.1 MeV, the difference in specific heat between the two pairing force scenarios amounts to about 7 orders of magnitude.
- The low-energy quadrupole mode in the ¹⁸⁰⁰Sn cell has a reduced transition probability B(E2) equal to 25×10³ Weisskopf units.
- The cooling time of the inner crust increases by more than 80% when using a weak pairing force instead of a strong pairing force.

## Context

This lecture extends the microscopic treatment of pairing correlations from finite nuclei near the drip lines to the inner crust of neutron stars, building on prior work by Pines, Sauls, and Prakash on post-glitch timing and cooling processes, and by Lombardo on neutron star superfluidity. The most sophisticated previous microscopic calculations for inner crust superfluidity used the HFB approach at finite temperature (Sandulescu, 2004). The results enable systematic studies of pairing in both infinite and finite nuclear systems to reduce the uncertainty in cooling time predictions for neutron stars.
