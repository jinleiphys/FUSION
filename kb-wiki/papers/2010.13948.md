# On the stability of the open-string QED neutron and dark matter

> We study the stability of a hypothetical QED neutron, which consists of a color-singlet system of two $d$ quarks and a $u$ quark interacting with the QED interaction. As a quark cannot be isolated, the intrinsic motion of the three quarks in the lowest-energy state may lie predominantly in 1+1 dimensions, as in a $d$-$u$-$d$ open string. The attractive $d$-$u$ and $u$-$d$ QED interactions may overcome the weaker repulsive $d$-$d$ QED interaction to bind the three quarks together. We examine the QED neutron in a phenomenological three-body problem in 1+1 dimensions with an effective interaction extracted from Schwinger's exact QED solution in 1+1 dimensions. The phenomenological model in a variational calculation yields a stable QED neutron at 44.5 MeV. The analogous QED proton with two $u$ quarks and a $d$ quark has been found to be too repulsive to be stable and does not have a bound or continuum state, onto which the QED neutron can decay via the weak interaction. Consequently, the QED neutron is stable against the weak decay, has a long lifetime, and is in fact a QED dark neutron. It may be produced following the deconfinement-to-confinement phase transition of the quark gluon plasma in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. Because of the long lifetime of the QED dark neutron, self-gravitating assemblies of QED dark neutrons or dark antineutrons may be good candidates for a part of the primordial dark matter produced during the phase transition of the quark gluon plasma in the evolution of the early Universe.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a color-singlet system of two $d$ quarks and one $u$ quark interacting via QED alone can form a stable bound state (the "QED neutron") at 44.5 MeV, and that this state is stable against weak decay because the analogous QED proton (two $u$ quarks and one $d$ quark) has no bound or continuum state onto which the QED neutron could decay.

## Method

The author models the three-quark system as a relativistic three-body problem in 1+1 dimensions, using an effective two-body QED interaction extracted from Schwinger's exact QED solution for massless fermions in 1+1 dimensions. A variational calculation is performed to find the ground-state energy of the $d$-$u$-$d$ configuration, with the quarks treated as positive-energy entities obeying mass-shell constraints in the center-of-mass frame.

## Key numbers

- The QED neutron ground-state energy is found to be **44.5 MeV** from a variational calculation in the phenomenological three-body model.
- The effective two-body QED interaction strength is given by $\kappa = e^2/(4\pi)$, where $e$ is the QED coupling constant in 1+1 dimensions.
- The ratio of QED meson mass to QCD meson mass is estimated as $\sim \sqrt{\alpha_{\rm QED}/\alpha_{\rm QCD}} \sim \sqrt{(1/137)/0.6} \sim 1/9$, placing QED mesons in the tens-of-MeV range.

## Context

This work extends the author's prior proposal of QED mesons (Wong, 2010, 2011, 2014, 2020) as explanations for the anomalous soft photons, the X17 particle at about 17 MeV (Krasznahorkay, 2016, 2019), and the E38 particle at about 38 MeV (Abraamyan, 2012, 2019). The QED neutron is presented as a candidate for primordial dark matter, potentially produced during the deconfinement-to-confinement phase transition of the quark-gluon plasma in the early Universe or in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. The work calls for future lattice gauge calculations to test whether quarks in compact QED U(1) gauge fields are confined, and for experimental tests of the predicted stable QED neutron.
