# Heavy-ion collisions, Gubser flow, and Carroll hydrodynamics

> Gubser flow provides an analytic model for describing the spacetime dynamics of the quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions. Along with boost and rotation invariance along the beam axis, the model assumes invariance under a combination of translations and special conformal transformations in the transverse plane, leading to a flow profile which evolves not just along the beam axis, but also radially. We argue that Gubser flow and its associated symmetry assumptions arise naturally as a consequence of Carrollian symmetries for a conformal Carroll fluid, thereby providing a dual geometric picture for the flow. Given the inherent ultrarelativistic nature of the flow, this duality with Carroll hydrodynamics - which arises in the $c \to 0$ limit of relativistic hydrodynamics, is natural. We provide a precise map between Gubser flow and the conformal Carroll fluid, appropriate to capture the duality between the two not just at the ideal level, but also with the inclusion of hydrodynamic derivative corrections.

## Key claim

Gubser flow, which provides a more realistic model of quark-gluon plasma evolution than Bjorken flow by allowing radial dependence, arises naturally as a consequence of Carrollian symmetries for a conformal Carroll fluid, providing a dual geometric picture that holds not just at the ideal level but also with the inclusion of hydrodynamic derivative corrections.

## Method

The authors establish a duality between Gubser flow and conformal Carroll hydrodynamics by identifying a Carroll manifold with a specific degenerate metric and kernel such that the equations of conformal Carroll hydrodynamics on this manifold become the Gubser flow equations. They construct explicit maps for two different coordinate systems: Milne coordinates on flat spacetime and coordinates on a dS$_3 \times \mathbb{R}$ background, demonstrating that the phenomenological assumption of boost invariance in Gubser flow follows from the underlying Carrollian symmetries in the dual description.

## Key numbers

- The energy density solution for Gubser flow in Milne coordinates is $\epsilon(\tau, r) = \frac{\epsilon_0}{\tau^{4/3}} \frac{(2q)^{8/3}}{[1+2q^2(\tau^2+r^2)+q^4(\tau^2-r^2)^2]^{4/3}}$, where $q$ is a tunable parameter with dimensions of inverse length and $\epsilon_0$ is a constant of integration.
- On the dS$_3 \times \mathbb{R}$ background, the energy density solution is $\epsilon(\varsigma) = \frac{\epsilon_0}{(\cosh \varsigma)^{8/3}}$.
- The $q \to 0$ limit of Gubser flow reduces to Bjorken flow with $\epsilon \propto \tau^{-4/3}$.
- The fluid velocity profile for Gubser flow is given by $\kappa(\tau,r) = \tanh^{-1} \left[\frac{2q^2 \tau r}{1+q^2(\tau^2 + r^2)}\right]$.

## Context

This work extends the Carrollian framework previously applied to Bjorken flow by Bagchi (2023) to the more general Gubser flow model proposed by Gubser (2010), which allows for nontrivial radial flow in the transverse plane. The duality provides a geometric picture for ultrarelativistic fluids and paves the way to systematically compute rapidity-dependent corrections using the Carrollian perspective, building on earlier work by Ciambelli (2018) and others on Carroll hydrodynamics as the $c \to 0$ limit of relativistic hydrodynamics.
