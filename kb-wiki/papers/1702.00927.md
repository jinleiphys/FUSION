# Probing the exotic structure of $^{8}$B by its elastic scattering and breakup reaction on nuclear targets

> The structure of the exotic $^{8}$B nucleus is studied by means of elastic scattering, as well as its breakup on nuclear targets. We present microscopic calculations of the optical potentials (OPs) and cross sections of elastic scattering of $^{8}$B on $^{12}$C, $^{58}$Ni, and $^{208}$Pb targets at energies $20<E<170$ MeV. The density distributions of $^{8}$B obtained within the variational Monte Carlo (VMC) model and the three-cluster model (3CM) are used to construct the potentials. The real part of the hybrid OP is calculated using the folding model with the direct and exchange terms included, while the imaginary part is obtained on the base of the high-energy approximation (HEA) and also taken to be equal to the microscopic real part of the OP. In this model the only free parameters are the depths of the real and imaginary parts of OP obtained by fitting the elastic scattering experimental data. It is found a dependence of their values on the model density of $^{8}$B. In addition, cluster model, in which $^{8}$B consists of a $p$-halo and the $^{7}$Be core, is applied to calculate the breakup cross sections of $^{8}$B nucleus on $^{9}$Be, $^{12}$C, and $^{197}$Au targets, as well as momentum distributions of $^{7}$Be fragments, and a comparison with the existing experimental data is made.

## Key claim

The authors show that a hybrid microscopic optical potential, with only two free parameters (the depths of the real and imaginary parts), can reproduce elastic scattering data for \(^{8}\)B on \(^{12}\)C, \(^{58}\)Ni, and \(^{208}\)Pb across 20–170 MeV, but the fitted renormalization parameters depend on which density model (VMC or three-cluster model) is used for \(^{8}\)B, and the surface region of the imaginary potential is the decisive part for the cross sections.

## Method

The real part of the optical potential was calculated using a double-folding model with direct and exchange terms, employing M3Y effective nucleon-nucleon interactions and density distributions of \(^{8}\)B from the variational Monte Carlo (VMC) model and the three-cluster model (3CM). The imaginary part was obtained either from the high-energy approximation (HEA) or set equal to the real folding potential, and the depths of both parts were renormalized by fitting parameters \(N_R\) and \(N_I\) to elastic scattering data. For breakup reactions, a cluster model treating \(^{8}\)B as a \(^{7}\)Be core plus a valence proton was used within an eikonal formalism to calculate stripping cross sections and longitudinal momentum distributions of \(^{7}\)Be fragments.

## Key numbers

- Proton separation energy of \(^{8}\)B: 136.4 keV (or 137 keV in the cluster model fitting).
- Root-mean-square (rms) radii from VMC density: proton 2.45 fm, neutron 2.14 fm, matter 2.34 fm; from 3CM density: proton 2.73 fm, neutron 2.24 fm, matter 2.56 fm.
- Empirical rms radii from Tanihata (1988): proton 2.45 fm, neutron 2.27 fm, matter 2.38 fm; from Fukuda (1999): proton 2.53 fm, neutron 2.31 fm, matter 2.45 fm.
- For \(^{8}\)B+\(^{12}\)C at 25.8 MeV: best-fit parameters with 3CM density gave \(N_R = 1.075\), \(N_I = 0.433\), \(\sigma_R = 1507.63\) mb; with VMC density gave \(N_R = 2.200\), \(N_I = 0.165\), \(\sigma_R = 1251.90\) mb.
- For \(^{8}\)B+\(^{208}\)Pb at 170.3 MeV: with 3CM density \(N_R = 0.661\), \(N_I = 0.389\), \(\sigma_R = 3226.73\) mb; with VMC density \(N_R = 1.358\), \(N_I = 0.908\), \(\sigma_R = 3158.15\) mb.
- For \(^{8}\)B+\(^{58}\)Ni at five energies (20.7–29.3 MeV): total reaction cross sections ranged from 208.69 to 987.29 mb (VMC) and from 201.99 to 847.11 mb (3CM).
- Cluster wave function rms radii from fitting Woods-Saxon potentials to the proton separation energy: 4.51 fm, 5.08 fm, and 6.24 fm for three different parameter sets.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous hybrid-model analyses of neutron-rich He, Li, and Be isotopes (Lukyanov et al., 2007–2015) to the proton-rich \(^{8}\)B nucleus, testing both VMC densities (Carlson and Pieper, 2015) and three-cluster model densities (Varga et al., 1995). The elastic scattering results are compared with earlier optical-model analyses using Woods-Saxon potentials (Aguilera et al., 2009) and CDCC calculations (Barioni et al., 2011; Yang et al., 2013; Paes et al., 2012; Lubian et al., 2009), confirming that breakup channel couplings have negligible effect on elastic scattering for light and heavy targets. The breakup calculations provide momentum distributions of \(^{7}\)Be fragments that can be compared with experimental data from Schwab (1995), Kelley (1996), and Jin (2015), enabling further tests of the proton-halo structure of \(^{8}\)B.
