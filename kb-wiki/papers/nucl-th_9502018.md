# THE COUPLING OF THE $f_1(1285)$ MESON TO THE ISOSCALAR AXIAL CURRENT OF THE NUCLEON

> The weak decay as well as the strong nucleon coupling constants of the isoscalar axial vector meson $f_1 (1285)$ are estimated within the octet quark model. It is shown that the empirical value for the coupling of $\overline{s}s$ quarkonium to nucleons can be understood by attributing the $f_1(1285)$-nucleon coupling to the $a_0πN$ triangle diagram.

# The Coupling of the $f_1(1285)$ Meson to the Isoscalar Axial Current of the Nucleon

## Key claim
The empirical coupling of the $f_1(1285)$ meson to nucleons, which arises from its $\overline{s}s$ quarkonium component, can be explained by attributing the $f_1(1285)$-nucleon coupling to the $a_0\pi N$ triangle diagram, rather than requiring direct quark-model predictions. The agreement holds despite the small decay width of the $f_1(1285)$ meson.

## Method
The authors estimate the weak decay and strong nucleon coupling constants of the $f_1(1285)$ meson within the octet quark model, using vector meson dominance (VMD) to relate hadronic and semileptonic couplings. They then calculate the contribution of the $D \to (a_0 + \pi) NN$ triangle diagram to the $DNN$ vertex, employing monopole form factors at the $\pi NN$ and $a_0 NN$ vertices with mass parameters 1.3 GeV and 2.0 GeV respectively, and using the experimental partial decay width to fix $g_{Da_0\pi}$.

## Key numbers
- The weak decay constant for the $f_1(1285)$ meson (denoted $D$) under ideal mixing is $f_D = -3f_{a_1}$, where $f_{a_1}^2 = f_\rho^2$ from the second Weinberg sum rule, yielding $f_{D_0} = -7.093$ and $f_{E_8} = 5.016$.
- The empirical mixing angle for the axial vector meson nonet is $\theta = 50^\circ \pm 2.7^\circ \pm 3.6^\circ$, deviating from the ideal mixing angle $\theta_0 = \tan^{-1}(1/\sqrt{2})$.
- The weak isoscalar nucleon coupling is $G_1^s = -0.13 \pm 0.04$ (Ellis 1993).
- The coupling of purely strange quarkonia to the nucleon is taken as $g_{(\overline{s}s)} = 6.75$ (Ge 1976), leading to $|g_{DNN}| = \sin\epsilon \, g_{(\overline{s}s)} = 1.72$ from the quark model estimate.
- The triangle diagram calculation yields $|g_{DNN}| = 1.9$ at $q^2 = -m_D^2$, using $g_{Da_0\pi} = 5.7$ from the experimental partial decay width.
- The pseudovector $\pi NN$ coupling constant is $f_{\pi NN}^2/4\pi = 0.08$, and the scalar $a_0 NN$ coupling constant is $g_{a_0 NN}^2/4\pi = 1.075$, taken from the Bonn one boson exchange model (Els 1987).
- The $q^2$ dependence of $g_{DNN}$ corresponds approximately to a monopole form with mass scale parameter $\Lambda \sim 2$ GeV.
- Using $G_1^s$ from Ellis (1993), the value $f_{DNN} = -14.61$ is obtained, compatible with $\hat{f}_D = -3f_{a_1}\cos\epsilon = -17.2$ from the KSFR relation.

## Context
This work extends the vector meson dominance (VMD) hypothesis, previously applied to the isovector axial form factor of the nucleon via the $a_1$ meson (Ivanov 1979), to the isoscalar axial form factor via the $f_1(1285)$ meson. It builds on earlier work by Hjorth-Jensen (1993) and Kirchbach (1994) on the isoscalar axial current. The result addresses the long-standing puzzle that the $\phi NN$ coupling is strongly underpredicted in the quark model, as seen from the unexpectedly large $(\overline{s}s)NN$ coupling extracted from data analyses (Ge 1976). The finding enables improved modeling of $N\overline{N}$ annihilation processes and neutrino scattering, and suggests that VMD may be applicable to isoscalar axial vector currents.
