# Microscopic calculations of double and triple Giant Resonance excitation in heavy ion collisions

> We perform microscopic calculations of the inelastic cross sections for the double and triple excitation of giant resonances induced by heavy ion probes within a semicalssical coupled channels formalism. The channels are defined as eigenstates of a bosonic quartic Hamiltonian constructed in terms of collective RPA phonons. Therefore, they are superpositions of several multiphonon states, also with different numbers of phonons and the spectrum is anharmonic. The inclusion of (n+1) phonon configurations affects the states whose main component is a n-phonon one and leads to an appreacible lowering of their energies. We check the effects of such further anharmonicities on the previous published results for the cross section for the double excitation of Giant Resonances. We find that the only effect is a shift of the peaks towards lower energies, the double GR cross section being not modified by the explicity inclusion of the three-phonon channels in the dynamical calculations. The latters give an important contribution to the cross section in the triple GR energy region which however is still smaller than the experimental available data. The inclusion of four phonon configurations in the structure calculations does not modify the results.

## Key claim

The inclusion of three-phonon configurations in the structure calculation appreciably lowers the energies of two-phonon states, but when these three-phonon channels are included in the dynamical coupled-channels calculation, the double giant resonance cross section is not modified; only a shift of the peaks towards lower energies occurs. The triple giant resonance cross section from the calculation remains smaller than the experimental data even after including three-phonon channels, and adding four-phonon configurations in the structure calculation does not further change the results.

## Method

The authors performed semiclassical coupled-channels calculations for heavy-ion collisions, where the internal nuclear states are eigenstates of a bosonic quartic Hamiltonian built from collective RPA phonons. The Hamiltonian was diagonalized in a space including up to three-phonon (and later four-phonon) configurations to obtain anharmonic eigenstates, and the time-dependent coupled equations were integrated along classical trajectories for each impact parameter to compute inelastic cross sections. The calculations were done for the reactions 40Ca + 40Ca at 50 MeV/A and 208Pb + 208Pb at 641 MeV/A, using a discrete self-consistent HF+RPA with SGII interaction and including all one-phonon states with J ≤ 3 that exhaust at least 5% of the energy-weighted sum rule.

## Key numbers

* For 40Ca + 40Ca at 50 MeV/A, the integrated cross section in the two-phonon region (28-38 MeV) is 5.44 mb (with 1.34 mb for the double GQR states) when three-phonon states are included, compared to 5.48 mb (1.97 mb for double GQR) in the two-phonon-only calculation.
* In the three-phonon region (38-60 MeV), the integrated cross section is 2.02 mb (0.20 mb for triple GQR) with three-phonon states included, versus 0.66 mb in the two-phonon-only calculation.
* The one-phonon region (14-20 MeV) gives an integrated cross section of 20.83 mb (14.71 mb for single GQR) with three-phonon states, compared to 22.80 mb (15.62 mb) in the two-phonon-only calculation.
* For 40Ca, the double GQR state (Q1 ⊗ Q1) with Jπ = 2+ has an energy of 33.82 MeV in the harmonic case, which shifts to 32.47 MeV when up to three-phonon states are included, and to 32.01 MeV when up to four-phonon states are included.
* The triple GQR state (Q1 ⊗ Q1 ⊗ Q1) with Jπ = 0+ has a harmonic energy of 54.74 MeV, shifting to 54.48 MeV with up to three-phonon states and to 53.12 MeV with up to four-phonon states.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations (Lanza et al., 2001; Andres et al., 2001) that included only one- and two-phonon states, by incorporating three-phonon configurations in both the structure and dynamics, following the earlier spectrum calculations of Fal et al. (2005) for 40Ca and 208Pb. The results show that the double giant resonance cross section is converged at the two-phonon level when three-phonon states are included in the structure but not in the dynamics, while the triple giant resonance region still falls short of experimental data from GANIL (Scarpaci et al., 2002; Frascaria et al., 2002), suggesting that additional reaction mechanisms such as the towing mode may contribute above 35 MeV excitation energy.
