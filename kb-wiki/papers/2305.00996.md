# Detecting the critical point through entanglement in Schwinger model

> Using quantum simulations on classical hardware, we study the phase diagram of the massive Schwinger model with a $θ$-term at finite chemical potential $μ$. We find that the quantum critical point in the phase diagram of the model can be detected through the entanglement entropy and entanglement spectrum. As a first step, we chart the phase diagram using conventional methods by computing the dependence of the charge and chiral condensates on the fermion mass $m$, coupling constant $g$, and the chemical potential $μ$. At zero density, the Schwinger model possesses a quantum critical point at $θ=π$ and $m/g \simeq 0.33$. We find that the position of this quantum critical point depends on the chemical potential. Near this quantum critical point, we observe a sharp maximum in the entanglement entropy. Moreover, we find that the quantum critical point can be located from the entanglement spectrum by detecting the position of the gap closing point.

## Key claim

The quantum critical point of the massive Schwinger model at finite chemical potential can be detected through entanglement entropy and entanglement spectrum, but the authors note that the discontinuity in entanglement entropy is not always related to a critical point in a phase diagram, as stressed in prior work by Filippone (2011).

## Method

The authors discretize the massive Schwinger model with a theta term on a lattice using staggered fermions and the Jordan-Wigner transformation, then diagonalize the resulting Hamiltonian to obtain eigenstates. They compute thermal expectation values of charge, chiral condensate, and charge susceptibility at finite temperature, and study the ground-state entanglement entropy and entanglement spectrum between spatial subsystems. The central charge is extracted by fitting the subsystem-length dependence of entanglement entropy to the conformal field theory prediction.

## Key numbers

- At zero density, the quantum critical point is located at m/g approximately 0.33 and theta = pi
- The critical mass at vanishing chemical potential is found to be m* approximately -0.11 g, where the chiral condensate vanishes and entanglement entropy peaks
- The central charge extracted from entanglement entropy is within approximately 15 percent of c = 1, approaching unity as lattice spacing decreases and number of sites increases
- Lattice parameters used include N = 10, a = 0.5/g for the phase diagram, and N = 8, 12, 16, 20 for finite-size studies
- Temperature for thermal observables is set to T = g/10

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study by Ikeda (2020) that used real-time topological susceptibility to detect the critical point, now confirming the critical point through entanglement measures. The massive Schwinger model at critical mass belongs to the universality class of the classical 2d Ising model, and the results suggest that entanglement entropy and entanglement spectrum could serve as diagnostic tools for critical points in more complicated field theories including QCD, though the authors note that this connection requires confirmation in other theories such as holographic QCD by Sakai (2004) and Yee (2011).
