# Quantum chaos and nuclear mass systematics

> The presence of quantum chaos in nuclear mass systematics is analyzed by considering the differences between measured and calculated nuclear masses as a time series described by the power law 1/ f^alpha. While for the liquid droplet model plus shell corrections a quantum chaotic behavior alpha approx 1 is found, errors in the microscopic mass formula have alpha approx 0.5, closer to white noise. The chaotic behavior seems to arise from many body effects not included in the mass formula.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the power-law exponent \(\alpha\) of the mass-error fluctuations depends on the mass model: the liquid droplet model plus shell corrections (FRDM) yields \(\alpha \approx 1\), characteristic of quantum chaos, while the microscopic Duflo-Zuker (DZ) formula yields \(\alpha \approx 0.5\), closer to white noise. The authors suggest that the chaotic behavior arises from many-body effects not included in the mass formula, implying that the accuracy limit claimed in earlier work may be reducible.

## Method

The authors treat the differences between measured and calculated nuclear masses as a discrete time series, organized in two ways: (1) as 18 chains of nuclei with constant \(\tilde T_z\) (a transformed isospin-like variable) ordered by \(\tilde A\), and (2) as a single "boustrophedon" list of 1654 nuclei ordered by mass number and N-Z. They compute the discrete Fourier transform of each series and fit the log-log slope of the squared Fourier amplitudes versus frequency to extract the exponent \(\alpha\) in the power law \(1/f^\alpha\). The analysis is applied to the Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM) of Möller et al. and the microscopic mass formula of Duflo and Zuker (DZ).

## Key numbers

- For the 18 \(\tilde T_z\) chains: \(\alpha^{(1)}_{FRDM} = -1.18 \pm 0.17\) and \(\alpha^{(1)}_{DZ} = -0.67 \pm 0.16\).
- For the single ordered list of 1654 nuclei: \(\alpha^{(2)}_{FRDM} = -0.91 \pm 0.05\) and \(\alpha^{(2)}_{DZ} = -0.51 \pm 0.05\).
- The rms error for 2135 nuclei is 674 keV for HFB, 676 keV for FRDM, and 373 keV for DZ.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of nuclear mass deviations by Bohigas and Leboeuf (2002), who interpreted errors in the FRDM as containing a chaotic component and suggested a fundamental accuracy limit in mean-field theories. The authors apply the \(1/f\) noise test for quantum chaos proposed by Relaño et al. (2002) to mass errors, finding that the DZ formula, which includes residual correlations, shows less chaotic behavior. This suggests that the chaotic component may arise from incomplete treatment of many-body quantum correlations, and that further reduction of mass errors may be possible beyond the limit claimed by Abele and collaborators (2002).
