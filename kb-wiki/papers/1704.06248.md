# Chiral Extrapolations of the $\boldsymbol{ρ(770)}$ Meson in $\mathbf{N_f=2+1}$ Lattice QCD Simulations

> Recent $N_f=2+1$ lattice data for meson-meson scattering in $p$-wave and isospin $I=1$ are analyzed using a unitarized model inspired by Chiral Perturbation Theory in the inverse-amplitude formulation for two and three flavors. Chiral extrapolations are performed that postdict phase shifts extracted from experiment quite well. In addition, the low-energy constants are compared to the ones from a recent analysis of $N_f=2$ lattice QCD simulations to check for the consistency of the hadronic model used here. Some inconsistencies are detected in the fits to $N_f=2+1$ data, in contrast to the previous analysis of $N_f=2$ data.

## Key claim
The unitarized Chiral Perturbation Theory model used to analyze $N_f=2+1$ lattice data for $\rho(770)$ meson scattering shows some inconsistencies in the fits, in contrast to previous analyses of $N_f=2$ data where low-energy constants showed a common overlap of 68% confidence regions for almost all considered data.

## Method
The authors analyzed $N_f=2+1$ lattice QCD data for $p$-wave $\pi\pi$ scattering in isospin $I=1$ using a unitarized model based on the inverse-amplitude formulation of Chiral Perturbation Theory for two and three flavors. They fitted the model to extracted phase shifts from multiple lattice collaborations using four different minimization strategies, varying which low-energy constants (LECs) were fixed or free, and compared the resulting LECs to those from $N_f=2$ analyses and experimental fits.

## Key numbers
- For the Wilson15 data at $M_\pi=236$ MeV, the SU(3) fit gave $\hat{l}_1=3.7\pm 1.2$, $\hat{l}_2=-3.2\pm0.3$ with $\chi^2_{\mathrm{d.o.f}}=0.9$
- For the Dudek13 data at $M_\pi=391$ MeV, the SU(3) fit gave $\hat{l}_1=1.8\pm 0.5$, $\hat{l}_2=-3.7\pm 0.3$ with $\chi^2_{\mathrm{d.o.f}}=1.2$
- For the Bulava16 data at $M_\pi=230$ MeV, the SU(3) fit gave $\hat{l}_1=5\pm 2$, $\hat{l}_2=-3.1\pm 0.4$ with $\chi^2_{\mathrm{d.o.f}}=1.1$
- For the Aoki11 data at $M_\pi=300$ and $400$ MeV, the SU(3) fit gave $\hat{l}_1=2.5\pm 0.7$, $\hat{l}_2=-2.8\pm 0.3$ with $\chi^2_{\mathrm{d.o.f}}=1.1$
- For the Alexandrou17 data at $M_\pi=317$ MeV, the SU(3) fit gave $\hat{l}_1=2.8\pm 0.8$, $\hat{l}_2=-3.8\pm 0.3$ with $\chi^2_{\mathrm{d.o.f}}=0.6$
- The extrapolated $\rho$ mass from the Wilson15 data was $760\pm 11$ MeV with coupling $g=6.11\pm 0.10$ in the SU(3) fit
- The experimental fit gave LECs $\hat{l}_1=0.26\pm 0.05$, $\hat{l}_2=-3.96\pm 0.04$, $L_3=-3.01\pm 0.02$, $L_5=0.64\pm 0.03$

## Context
This work extends previous analyses of $N_f=2$ lattice data by Guo (2016) and Hu (2016) to $N_f=2+1$ simulations, testing whether the absence of the strange quark in $N_f=2$ simulations could explain the consistently light $\rho$ masses (about 60 MeV below physical value) found in those earlier studies. The analysis uses lattice data from the Hadron Spectrum Collaboration (Wilson 2015, Dudek 2013), Bulava (2016), Aoki (2011), and Alexandrou (2017). The results enable future combined analyses of lattice data across different numbers of flavors and can help determine whether the strange quark dynamics or other effects like scale setting are responsible for discrepancies in $\rho$ meson properties from lattice QCD.
