# From CREX to CEvNS: The Weak Radius of 40Ar

> Despite significant theoretical efforts, the CREX-PREX dilemma remains unresolved, preventing the reliable prediction of neutron (or weak-charge) radii that, besides their intrinsic nuclear-structure interest, often serve to quantify the impact of nuclear uncertainties in searches for new physics. Coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering is a clean and attractive portal to new physics whose sensitivity may be impacted by such nuclear uncertainties. In this paper we use CREX as our main anchor, together with a strong calcium-argon correlation, to provide a robust baseline for the weak radius of ${}^{40}$Ar: $R_{\rm wk}^{\,40} = 3.452 \pm 0.028~\text{(stat)} \pm 0.022~\text{(syst)}\,\text{fm}$.The weak radius of argon is an observable highly relevant to ongoing and future liquid-argon campaigns that encodes the loss of coherence at small momentum transfers.

## Key claim

Using the CREX measurement of the weak radius of ⁴⁸Ca as an anchor, together with a strong linear correlation between the weak radii of calcium and argon, the paper provides a robust baseline for the weak radius of ⁴⁰Ar without relying on absolute model predictions that are compromised by the unresolved CREX-PREX dilemma.

## Method

The author employed 17 covariant energy density functionals (EDFs) to predict weak-charge densities and form factors for ⁴⁰Ar, ⁴⁸Ca, and ²⁰⁸Pb. Statistical uncertainties were quantified using the covariance matrices from two accurately calibrated functionals (FSUGold2 and FSUGold2R), while systematic uncertainties were assessed from the spread across the full ensemble of 17 EDFs. Rather than using absolute model predictions, the author exploited the linear correlation between the weak radii of ⁴⁸Ca and ⁴⁰Ar to infer the argon weak radius directly from the CREX measurement.

## Key numbers

- Weak radius of ⁴⁰Ar: \( R_{\rm wk}^{\,40} = 3.452 \pm 0.028~\text{(stat)} \pm 0.022~\text{(syst)}\,\text{fm} \), obtained by inverse-variance weighting of statistical and systematic estimates
- CREX result for ⁴⁸Ca used as anchor: \( R_{\rm wk}^{\,48} = (3.636 \pm 0.035)\,\text{fm} \)
- PREX result for ²⁰⁸Pb: \( R_{\rm wk}^{208} = (5.800 \pm 0.075)\,\text{fm} \)
- Statistical-only CREX-informed estimate for ⁴⁰Ar from FSUGold2R: \( R_{\rm wk}^{\,40} = (3.443 \pm 0.028)\,\text{fm} \)
- Systematic-only CREX-informed estimate for ⁴⁰Ar from 17 EDFs: \( R_{\rm wk}^{\,40} = (3.457 \pm 0.022)\,\text{fm} \)
- Correlation coefficient between weak radii of ⁴⁸Ca and ⁴⁰Ar from FSUGold2R: nearly one (dominates uncertainty via product \( b\,\sigma_{48} \))
- Slope of symmetry energy for FSUGold2: \( L = (112.8 \pm 16.1)\,\text{MeV} \); for FSUGold2R: \( L = (57.2 \pm 1.0)\,\text{MeV} \)

## Context

This work addresses the CREX-PREX dilemma, where state-of-the-art EDFs cannot simultaneously reproduce the neutron skin thicknesses of ⁴⁸Ca and ²⁰⁸Pb as measured by parity-violating electron scattering at Jefferson Lab (Adhikari 2021, Adhikari 2022). By using CREX as the primary anchor rather than relying on absolute model predictions, the paper provides a benchmark for the weak radius of ⁴⁰Ar that is directly relevant to ongoing and future coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering experiments with liquid-argon detectors, such as those by the COHERENT Collaboration (Akimov 2017, Akimov 2019). The result enables improved quantification of nuclear uncertainties in searches for new physics via CEvNS and can serve as an experimental target for future measurements.
