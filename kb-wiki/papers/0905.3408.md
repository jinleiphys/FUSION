# Nonextensive effects in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model of QCD

> We present a nonextensive version of the recently discussed QCD-based Nambu - Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model of many-body field theory describing behavior of strongly interacting matter. It is based on the nonextensive generalization of the Boltzmann-Gibbs (BG) statistical mechanics used in NJL model, which was taken in the form proposed by Tsallis and characterized by a dimensionless nonextensivity parameter q (for q -> 1 one recovers the usual BG case). This is a new phenomenological parameter of the model which accounts summarily for all possible (so far unspecified) effects leading to departure from the conditions required by the usual BG approach to be fully applicable. It turns out that temperature dependencies of chiral symmetry restoration, masses of pion and sigma meson and characteristic features of spinodal decomposition are sensitive to the departures from the usual BG case.

# Nonextensive effects in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model of QCD

## Key claim

The paper shows that introducing a nonextensive generalization of Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics (via the Tsallis parameter q) into the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model of QCD produces measurable sensitivity in chiral symmetry restoration, meson masses, and spinodal decomposition, but only for sufficiently high temperatures since at T=0 the q-distributions reduce to the standard Fermi-Dirac form regardless of q.

## Method

The authors formulate a q-NJL model by replacing the standard Fermi-Dirac distributions for quarks and antiquarks with q-deformed distributions, using a symmetric prescription for q<1 and q>1 that preserves particle-hole symmetry. They solve the q-version of the gap equations for constituent quark masses and quark condensates numerically, using the same parameter set as Sousa (2008) with cutoff Lambda = 602.3 MeV, and compute the temperature dependence of chiral condensates, effective quark masses, pi and sigma meson masses, and the spinodal decomposition behavior at finite density.

## Key numbers

- Quark condensates at T=0: <bar{u}u> = <bar{d}d> = -(241.9 MeV)^3 and <bar{s}s> = -(257.7 MeV)^3
- Constituent quark masses at T=0: M_u = M_d = 367.7 MeV, M_s = 549.5 MeV
- Critical temperature T_cr changes by approximately 10 MeV between q = 0.98 and q = 1.02
- For q = 1 at critical temperature, the compression at the inflection point is rho/rho_0 = 1.67 with mu = 318 MeV; for q = 1.01, mu = 321 MeV; for q = 1.02, mu = 325 MeV; for q = 0.99, mu = 313 MeV; for q = 0.98, mu = 307 MeV
- For T = 30 MeV, the critical q value is q = 1.19; for T = 50 MeV, the critical q value is q = 1.063
- The absolute minimum of energy per particle at T = 30 MeV is located at rho/rho_0 = 2.45, independent of q
- Normal nuclear density rho_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}

## Context

This work extends the NJL model of Sousa (2008) by incorporating nonextensive statistics, and follows the approach of Pereira (2006) who applied q-statistics to the Walecka model (QHD-I) at the hadronic level, whereas this paper works at the quark level and considers both q>1 and q<1 cases. The q>1 case is interpreted as temperature fluctuations following Wilk and Wlodarczyk (2000), while q<1 is attributed to correlations or phase space limitations following Kodama (2005). The results enable future systematic investigation of the full phase diagram of strongly interacting matter in the q-NJL framework, which the authors state they plan to address elsewhere.
