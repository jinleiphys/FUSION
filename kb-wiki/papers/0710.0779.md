# Many-body methods for nuclear systems at subnuclear densities

> This article provides a concise review of selected topics in the many-body physics of low density nuclear systems. The discussion includes the condensation of alpha particles in supernova envelopes, formation of three-body bound states and the BEC-BCS crossover in dilute nuclear matter, and neutrino production in $S$-wave paired superfluid neutron matter.

## Key claim

This review argues that subnuclear-density matter exhibits a range of salient many-body phenomena, Bose-Einstein condensation of alpha particles, BEC-BCS crossover in the deuteron channel, many-body extinction of bound states, and non-trivial modifications of weak interaction rates, but the authors explicitly state they "pay less attention to the physical setting and implications of the results," focusing instead on describing the many-body methods themselves.

## Method

The paper reviews three distinct many-body approaches: (1) a lattice Monte Carlo method for Bose-Einstein condensation of alpha particles, using a classical O(2) symmetric scalar φ⁶ field theory in three spatial dimensions derived from a Hamiltonian with attractive two-body and repulsive three-body forces; (2) an iterative algorithm for solving the BCS gap equation in the ³S₁-³D₁ channel to study the BEC-BCS crossover, combined with three-body scattering matrix equations (type II Fredholm integral equations) to compute in-medium bound states; (3) a real-time Green's function formalism for neutrino transport, computing the polarization tensor of superfluid neutron matter via random-phase resummation of particle-hole diagrams with Nambu-Gorkov propagators.

## Key numbers

- Subnuclear density range considered: ρ ∈ [10¹¹ – 10¹⁴] g cm⁻³
- Temperature range for "hot" stage: tens of MeV; for "cold" stage: T < 0.1 MeV
- Saturation density of nuclear matter: n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Asymptotic free-space two-body binding energy: E_d(∞) = -2.23 MeV
- Asymptotic free-space three-body binding energy: E_t(∞) = -7.53 MeV
- Ratio of three-body to two-body binding energy: η = 3.38 (temperature-independent constant)
- For neutron Fermi momentum k_F = 0.8 fm⁻¹: gap Δ = 3.15 MeV, critical temperature T_c = 1.78 MeV, ratio R(0.5) = 0.014, R(0.8) = 1.0, R(0.9) = 6.5
- For neutron Fermi momentum k_F = 1.6 fm⁻¹: gap Δ = 0.57 MeV, critical temperature T_c = 0.38 MeV, ratio R(0.5) = 0.022, R(0.8) = 1.0, R(0.9) = 7.4
- Polarization tensor suppression factor from T/m: approximately 5 × 10⁻³
- Factor κ = 0.2 accounts for correction to one-pion-exchange rate from full ladder resummation

## Context

This review extends the line of work on many-body methods for nuclear matter at subnuclear densities, building on the BEC-BCS crossover framework of Nozieres and Schmitt-Rink (1985) and earlier studies by Alm (1991) and Lombardo (2001). The lattice Monte Carlo approach for alpha condensation follows the method developed by Baym (1999) and Zinn-Justin (2000) for dilute gases, reformulated for strongly correlated systems with attractive two-body and repulsive three-body forces by Sedrakian (2004). The neutrino emission calculations extend the one-loop treatment of the polarization tensor in superfluid matter, which Sedrakian (2006) showed to be inadequate for S-wave superfluids in neutron-star crusts, and the random-phase resummation framework presented here enables more accurate calculations of neutrino emissivity from pair-breaking processes that compete with the modified bremsstrahlung process of Friman and Maxwell.
