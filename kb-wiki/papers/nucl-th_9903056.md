# The complete version of Moscow NN potential

> A complete version of the Moscow NN potential model is presented. The excellent description for all essential partial waves has been found in the energy range 0 -- 350 MeV. The one-channel version of the model includes the orthogonality condition to most symmetric six-quark states in all lowest partial waves and thus, from this point of view, the model generalizes the well known Saito's orthogonality condition model (OCM) for the baryon-baryon interaction case. The specific features of the presented model which distinguish it from many conventional force models are discussed in details. One of them is a specific tensor mixing between nodal and nodeless wavefunctions which results in very reasonable values of the OPE cut-off parameter Λ=0.78 GeV and the πNN-coupling constant value f^2=0.075 in nice agreement with modern trends. The model, in case of its confirmation in precise few-nucleon calculations, can lead to noticeable revisions for many nuclear properties given by conventional force models.

## Key claim

The paper presents a complete one-channel version of the Moscow NN potential that includes an orthogonality condition to the most symmetric six-quark states in all lowest partial waves, but the authors acknowledge that the model's confirmation requires precise few-nucleon calculations and that the short-range part of the tensor force still needs improvement.

## Method

The authors construct an effective one-channel NN potential by combining a local exponential attractive well with a state-dependent separable term and a one-pion-exchange (OPE) potential with a dipole form factor, where the orthogonality condition to six-quark bag states is enforced via projection operators with very large positive coupling constants in S- and P-waves. The potential parameters were determined by fitting the Nijmegen phase shift analysis (PWA93) for all essential partial waves in the energy range 0–350 MeV, with 32 free parameters total.

## Key numbers

- OPE cut-off parameter Λ = 0.78 GeV (dipole form factor)
- πNN coupling constant f² = 0.075
- Energy range of phase shift description: 0–350 MeV
- Range parameters for P-wave projectors: r₀ ≈ 0.32 fm for ³P₀ and ³P₁ channels
- Local attractive well depth for odd partial waves: ~220 MeV
- Average relative deviation of phase shifts from PSA: 0.2–0.4%
- Singlet effective range r₀ shows disagreement with the 1983 Dumbrajs compilation value

## Context

This work extends the earlier Moscow potential models (Kukulin 1985, 1992; Kukulin and Faessler 1998) by providing a complete version with improved description of P-waves, replacing the earlier local attractive potential approach with a generalized orthogonality condition model (GOCM). The model generalizes Saito's orthogonality condition model (OCM) to baryon-baryon interactions and contrasts with conventional meson-exchange models like the Nijmegen potential (Stoks 1993) by incorporating quark degrees of freedom at short range. The soft OPE cut-off parameter Λ=0.78 GeV is in agreement with experimental results and theoretical estimations from π-N dynamics, whereas traditional OBE models require Λ≥1.3 GeV (Machleidt 1989). The model enables future few-nucleon calculations that could test whether the quark-inspired short-range treatment resolves discrepancies found in three- and four-nucleon calculations using conventional force models.
