# Further considerations concerning claims for deeply bound kaon atoms and reply to criticisms

> We briefly review the experiments of KEK and FINUDA, that claim evidence for deeply bound kaon states, from the perspective of recent theoretical papers and experiments that provide an alternative explanation of the peaks seen. At the same time we show that recent criticisms raised by Akaishi and Yamazaki, and exposed by Akaishi in this Conference, have no base.

## Key claim

The peaks observed by KEK and FINUDA that were claimed as evidence for deeply bound kaon states can be explained by conventional nuclear absorption mechanisms (K⁻ absorption on two nucleons with a spectator remnant, or rescattering after absorption) and do not require exotic bound kaon states; the criticisms raised by Akaishi and Yamazaki against these alternative explanations are unfounded.

## Method

The authors review the KEK and FINUDA experimental results and provide kinematic calculations for K⁻ absorption on two nucleons (K⁻ pp → Λp and K⁻ NN → ΣN) with a spectator nucleus, showing that the observed peak widths and positions are consistent with Fermi motion of the initial nucleon pair. They also present the rescattering mechanism (K⁻ absorption going to ΛN followed by rescattering of the Λ or nucleon with the remnant nucleus) to explain the broad FINUDA peak, and compare their theoretical calculations to the experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Chiral potentials provide depth around 50 MeV attraction with width around 100 MeV at ρ = ρ₀
- Deeply bound states from chiral potentials have binding energy around 30-40 MeV with width of order 100 MeV
- The narrow FINUDA peak is at M_I = 2340 MeV (Λp back-to-back invariant mass)
- The wider FINUDA peak is at M_I = 2275 MeV
- For K⁻ absorption in ⁴He: proton momentum dispersion of ±35 MeV/c for p_NN = 150 MeV/c
- For K⁻ absorption in ⁷Li: proton momentum dispersion of ±11 MeV/c for p_NN = 50 MeV/c
- The KEK peak in proton spectrum appears around 475 MeV/c
- A second KEK peak appears at p ∼ 545 MeV/c when slow pions are selected
- The reaction K⁻ ⁴He → Σ⁻ p d has a measured rate of 1.6%
- The fraction of the KEK peak is estimated at less than 1%, of order 0.34%
- The new calculations predict a K⁻ pp bound state with 50-70 MeV binding but more than 100 MeV width

## Context

This paper extends the earlier work of Oset and Toki (2004) and Magas, Oset, Ramos, and Toki (2005) that provided alternative explanations for the KEK and FINUDA peaks, respectively, without invoking deeply bound kaon states. It directly contradicts the claims of Akaishi and Yamazaki (2005) who proposed a very large attractive potential (around 650 MeV at nuclear center with matter compressed to 10 times nuclear matter density) and criticized the chiral approach. The paper enables future work by pointing to new calculations by Dote and Weise and by Schevchenko, Gal, and Mares predicting a K⁻ pp state with 50-70 MeV binding but width over 100 MeV, and to new measurements of ⁴He X-rays by Hayano and Iwasaki that contradict Akaishi's predictions.
