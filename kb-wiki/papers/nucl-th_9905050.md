# Gamma-Deuteron Scattering

> We discuss a recent computation of Compton scattering on the deuteron at photon energies of order the pion mass. An interaction kernel is computed in baryon chiral perturbation theory and sewn to phenomenological deuteron wave functions. As an appetizer, we consider a computation of the pion-deuteron scattering length using this method.

## Key claim

Compton scattering on the deuteron at photon energies of order the pion mass can be computed to order Q^3 in baryon chiral perturbation theory without any unknown counterterms, but a full calculation to order Q^4 is necessary before neutron polarizabilities can be reliably extracted from experimental data at photon energies near 100 MeV.

## Method

An irreducible scattering kernel for gamma-deuteron Compton scattering was computed to order Q^3 in baryon chiral perturbation theory, then sewn to phenomenological deuteron wave functions (specifically the energy-independent Bonn OBEPQ parameterization). The amplitude includes single-nucleon contributions (one-loop graphs at orders Q^2 and Q^3) and two-nucleon contributions (two-loop graphs with an exchanged pion at order Q^3). The laboratory differential cross section was evaluated from the T-matrix using numerical quadratures accurate to about 1%.

## Key numbers

- The order Q^3 chiral perturbation theory predictions for nucleon polarizabilities are: alpha_p = alpha_n = 12.2 x 10^{-4} fm^3 and beta_p = beta_n = 1.2 x 10^{-4} fm^3 (using g_A = 1.26, f_pi = 93 MeV)
- Experimental proton polarizabilities from Compton scattering on the proton at 33-309 MeV: alpha_p + beta_p = 13.23 +/- 0.86^{+0.20}_{-0.49} x 10^{-4} fm^3, alpha_p - beta_p = 10.11 +/- 1.74^{+1.22}_{-0.86} x 10^{-4} fm^3
- At 95 MeV photon energy, the O(Q^3) correction to the differential cross section is of order 50%, although the contribution to the amplitude is about 25%
- The O(Q^3) calculation at 69 MeV shows good agreement with the two available Illinois data points
- The O(Q^2) calculation reproduces the 49 MeV Illinois data remarkably well, though this agreement is somewhat fortuitous given significant O(Q^3) corrections

## Context

This work extends the Weinberg formulation of nuclear effective field theory to Compton scattering on the deuteron, following earlier applications to pion-deuteron scattering (Weinberg 1992, Beane 1995) and threshold neutral pion photoproduction on the deuteron (Beane 1995). The calculation is compared to conventional potential model calculations (Wilbois 1994, Levchuk 1994, Jerry 1994) and to the Kaplan-Savage-Wise (KSW) formulation calculation (Martin 1998). The results enable future extraction of neutron polarizabilities from experimental data at the Illinois (Lucas 1994), Saskatoon (SAL), and Lund facilities, but only after a full O(Q^4) calculation is performed.
