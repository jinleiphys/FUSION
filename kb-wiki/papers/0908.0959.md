# Structure of the Particle-Hole Amplitudes in No-core Shell Model Wave Functions

> We study the structure of the no-core shell model wave functions for $^6$Li and $^{12}$C by investigating the ground state and first excited state electron scattering charge form factors. In both nuclei, large particle-hole ($ph$) amplitudes in the wave functions appear with the opposite sign to that needed to reproduce the shape of the $(e,e')$ form factors, the charge radii, and the B(E2) values for the lowest two states. The difference in sign appears to arise mainly from the monopole $Δ\hbarω=2$ matrix elements of the kinetic and potential energy (T+V) that transform under the harmonic oscillator SU(3) symmetries as $(λ,μ)=(2,0)$. These are difficult to determine self-consistently, but they have a strong effect on the structure of the low-lying states and on the giant monopole and quadrupole resonances. The Lee-Suzuki transformation, used to account for the restricted nature of the space in terms of an effective interaction, introduces large higher-order $Δ\hbarω=n, n>$2, $ph$ amplitudes in the wave functions. The latter $ph$ excitations aggravate the disagreement between the experimental and predicted $(e,e')$ form factors with increasing model spaces, especially at high momentum transfers. For sufficiently large model spaces the situation begins to resolve itself for $^6$Li, but the convergence is slow. A prescription to constrain the $ph$ excitations would likely accelerate convergence of the calculations.

## Key claim

The no-core shell model (NCSM) wave functions for \(^6\)Li and \(^{12}\)C contain large particle-hole (\(ph\)) amplitudes that appear with the opposite sign to that needed to reproduce experimental electron scattering form factors, charge radii, and B(E2) values; this sign problem arises mainly from the monopole \(\Delta\hbar\omega=2\) matrix elements of the kinetic and potential energy that transform under harmonic oscillator SU(3) symmetries as \((\lambda,\mu)=(2,0)\), which are difficult to determine self-consistently.

## Method

The authors performed no-core shell model calculations for \(^6\)Li and \(^{12}\)C using the CD Bonn nucleon-nucleon interaction, and for \(^{12}\)C also the AV8' interaction and AV8' plus the Tucson-Melbourne TM'(99) three-body interaction. They computed elastic C0 and inelastic C2 electron scattering charge form factors using bare one-body operators, and compared the predicted form factors, charge radii, B(E2) values, and C2 matrix elements with experimental data for model spaces up to \(14\hbar\omega\) for \(^6\)Li and \(6\hbar\omega\) for \(^{12}\)C. They also investigated the dependence of the results on the oscillator parameter \(b\) and on the inclusion of a three-body interaction.

## Key numbers

- Point charge radius of \(^6\)Li: full model space gives 2.23 fm (\(0\hbar\omega\)), 2.08 fm (\(2\hbar\omega\)), 2.1 fm (\(4\hbar\omega\)), 2.14 fm (\(10\hbar\omega\)), 2.21 fm (\(14\hbar\omega\)); experimental value is 2.38 \(\pm\) 0.1 fm.
- Point charge radius of \(^{12}\)C: full model space gives 3.18 fm (\(0\hbar\omega\)), 2.99 fm (\(2\hbar\omega\)), 2.95 fm (\(4\hbar\omega\)), 2.95 fm (\(6\hbar\omega\)); experimental value is 2.32 \(\pm\) 0.022 fm.
- B(E2) values for \(^6\)Li with \(b = 1.79\) fm: 4.91 e\(^2\)fm\(^4\) (\(0\hbar\omega\)), 6.25 e\(^2\)fm\(^4\) (\(2\hbar\omega\)), 7.03 e\(^2\)fm\(^4\) (\(4\hbar\omega\)), 8.16 e\(^2\)fm\(^4\) (\(6\hbar\omega\)), 9.14 e\(^2\)fm\(^4\) (\(8\hbar\omega\)), 10.22 e\(^2\)fm\(^4\) (\(10\hbar\omega\)), 12.2 e\(^2\)fm\(^4\) (\(14\hbar\omega\)); experimental value is 21.8 \(\pm\) 4.8 e\(^2\)fm\(^4\).
- B(E2) values for \(^6\)Li with \(b = 1.94\) fm: 6.84 e\(^2\)fm\(^4\) (\(0\hbar\omega\)), 8.14 e\(^2\)fm\(^4\) (\(2\hbar\omega\)), 8.93 e\(^2\)fm\(^4\) (\(4\hbar\omega\)), 9.89 e\(^2\)fm\(^4\) (\(6\hbar\omega\)), 10.73 e\(^2\)fm\(^4\) (\(8\hbar\omega\)), 11.63 e\(^2\)fm\(^4\) (\(10\hbar\omega\)), 13.4 e\(^2\)fm\(^4\) (\(14\hbar\omega\)).
- For \(^6\)Li, the ratio \(B/A\) from the C2 matrix element polynomial changes sign between \(4\hbar\omega\) and \(6\hbar\omega\) for \(b = 1.79\) fm, and between \(8\hbar\omega\) and \(10\hbar\omega\) for \(b = 1.94\) fm.
- The predicted giant monopole resonance (GMR) excitation energy in \(^{12}\)C shifts from approximately 35 MeV to approximately 60 MeV when the oscillator parameter is changed in a \((0+2)\hbar\omega\) model space.

## Context

This work extends the no-core shell model studies of Navratil and collaborators (2000-2003) for \(^6\)Li and \(^{12}\)C by examining the structure of multi-\(\hbar\omega\) particle-hole amplitudes through comparison with electron scattering data. The sign problem identified for the \(\Delta\hbar\omega=2\) symplectic excitations resembles a similar issue found in standard multi-\(\hbar\omega\) harmonic oscillator shell model calculations by Hoshino and collaborators (1980s). The results indicate that a prescription to constrain the \(\Delta\hbar\omega=2\) particle-hole excitations, such as imposing a Hartree-Fock condition as suggested by Hoshino and collaborators, could accelerate convergence of NCSM calculations for momentum-based observables.
