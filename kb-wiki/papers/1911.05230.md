# Structure of Multicomponent Coulomb Crystals

> Coulomb plasmas crystallize in a number of physical systems, such as dusty plasmas, neutron star crusts, and white dwarf cores. The crystal structure of the one component and binary plasma has received significant attention in the literature, though the less studied multicomponent plasma may be most relevant for many physical systems which contain a large range of particle charges. We report on molecular dynamics simulations of multicomponent plasmas near the melting temperature with mixtures taken to be realistic X-ray burst ash compositions. We quantify the structure of the crystal with the bond order parameters and radial distribution function. Consistent with past work, low charge particles form interstitial defects and we argue that they are in a quasi-liquid state within the lattice. The lattice shows screening effects which preserves long range order despite the large variance in particle charges which may impact transport properties relevant to astrophysics.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in multicomponent Coulomb plasmas near the melting temperature, low-charge ions form interstitial defects and exist in a quasi-liquid state within the otherwise solid bcc lattice, and that screening effects preserve long-range order despite large variance in particle charges, which may impact transport properties relevant to astrophysics.

## Method

The author performed molecular dynamics simulations using the IUMD CUDA-Fortran code with a screened Coulomb (Yukawa) potential, evolving systems with a velocity Verlet scheme. Six multicomponent plasma mixtures were taken from realistic X-ray burst ash compositions (Mckinven 2016), each containing 102,400 ions (204,800 for one mixture) at a density of 7.18 × 10⁻⁵ fm⁻³. Simulations were initialized as liquids, cooled until freezing, then equilibrated at constant temperature for 8 × 10⁸ timesteps. Crystal structure was quantified using the bond order parameter Q₆ and radial distribution functions g(r).

## Context

This work extends previous molecular dynamics studies of one-component and binary Coulomb plasmas (Vaulina 2002, Baus 1980, Daligault 2012, Shaffer 2017) to realistic multicomponent mixtures relevant to neutron star crusts and white dwarf cores. It builds on simulations by Horowitz (2007, 2009) and Caplan (2018) that studied phase separation and defect formation in astrophysical Coulomb crystals, and on the Path Integral Monte Carlo work of Roggero (2016) which found that effective lattice impurity is reduced relative to the impurity parameter formalism. The results enable improved modeling of thermal and electrical transport in accreting neutron star crusts and cooling white dwarfs, and may motivate terrestrial dusty plasma experiments with analogous charge mixtures.
