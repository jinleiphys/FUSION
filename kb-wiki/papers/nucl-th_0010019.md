# Charge-dependent nucleon-nucleon potential from chiral effective field theory

> We discuss charge symmetry and charge independence breaking in a chiral effective field theory approach for few-nucleon systems based on a modified Weinberg power counting. We construct a two-nucleon potential with bound and scattering states generated by means of a properly regularized Lippmann-Schwinger equation. We systematically introduce strong isospin-violating and electromagnetic operators in the theory. We use standard procedures to treat the Coulomb potential between two protons in momentum space. We present results for phase shifts in the proton-proton, the neutron-proton and the neutron-neutron systems. We discuss the various contributions to charge dependence and charge symmetry breaking observed in the nucleon-nucleon scattering lengths.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a charge-dependent nucleon-nucleon potential from chiral effective field theory, showing that charge independence breaking (CIB) and charge symmetry breaking (CSB) can be systematically included at leading and next-to-leading order in a modified Weinberg power counting, with the caveat that the leading-order isospin-violating contributions scale as α/Q² and are therefore enhanced relative to naive expectations.

## Method

The authors start from an effective chiral Lagrangian of pions and nucleons, including local four-nucleon contact interactions, and derive a two-nucleon potential by applying a modified Weinberg power counting. Strong isospin violation from the light quark mass difference (parameter ε) and electromagnetic isospin violation from virtual photons (parameter α) are introduced systematically via external scalar sources. The potential is regularized with a sharp cutoff and inserted into a regularized Lippmann-Schwinger equation to generate bound and scattering states, with the Coulomb potential treated in momentum space using a technique that matches wave functions at a finite radius R.

## Key numbers

- Charge independence breaking in the scattering length: a_CIB = 5.64 ± 0.40 fm, from the compilation of Machleidt
- Charge symmetry breaking in the scattering length: a_CSB = a_pp - a_nn = 1.6 ± 0.6 fm
- Central values for singlet scattering lengths: a_nn = -18.9 fm, a_pp = -17.3 fm
- Pion mass difference: ΔM_π = M_π± - M_π0 = 4.6 MeV, almost entirely electromagnetic
- Neutron-proton mass difference: (m_n - m_p)^QCD ≈ 2.1 MeV, (m_n - m_p)^QED ≈ -0.8 MeV, physical value 1.3 MeV
- Quark mass ratio: m_u/m_d = 0.55 ± 0.15 at renormalization scale 1 GeV
- Fine structure constant: α = e²/4π = 1/137.036
- Leading OPE contribution to CIB: a_CIB^OPE ≈ 3.6 ± 0.2 fm
- ρ-ω mixing contribution to CSB: a_CSB^(ρ-ω) ≈ 1.2 ± 0.4 fm

## Context

This work extends the isospin-symmetric chiral NN potential developed at next-to-next-to-leading order by Epelbaum, Glöckle, and Meißner (2000) for the neutron-proton system, which was shown to be close in accuracy to modern phenomenological potentials. It provides a systematic EFT treatment of charge dependence and charge symmetry breaking that had previously only been studied within potential models using meson-exchange pictures (e.g., ρ-ω mixing for CSB, pion mass difference for CIB). The results enable a controlled, order-by-order calculation of isospin-violating effects in few-nucleon systems up to laboratory energies of about 300 MeV, and clarify the suppression of possible charge-dependent πN coupling constants as a higher-order (N³LOI) effect.
