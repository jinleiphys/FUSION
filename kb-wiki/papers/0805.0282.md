# Search for Squeezed-Pair Correlations at RHIC

> Squeezed correlations of particle-antiparticle pairs, also called Back-to-Back Correlations, are predicted to appear if the hadron masses are modified in the hot and dense hadronic medium formed in high energy nucleus-nucleus collisions. Although well-established theoretically, the squeezed-particle correlations have not yet been searched for experimentally in high energy hadronic or heavy ion collisions, clearly requiring optimized forms to experimentally search for this effect. Within a non-relativistic treatment developed earlier we show that one promising way to search for the BBC signal is to look into the squeezed correlation function of pairs of phi-mesons at RHIC energies, plotted in terms of the average momentum of the pair, K12=(k1+k2)/2. This variable's modulus, 2|K12|, is the non-relativistic limit of the variable Q_bbc, introduced herewith. The squeezing effects on the HBT correlation function are also discussed.

# Search for Squeezed-Pair Correlations at RHIC

## Key claim

Squeezed (Back-to-Back) correlations of particle-antiparticle pairs, predicted to arise from in-medium mass modification of hadrons, have not yet been observed experimentally; the authors show that within a non-relativistic treatment, the squeezed correlation function of phi-meson pairs at RHIC energies, plotted as a function of the average pair momentum, provides a promising experimental search channel, and that the squeezing effect also distorts the HBT correlation function in a way that opposes flow broadening.

## Method

The authors adopt a non-relativistic treatment with a flow-independent squeezing parameter, derived from a Bogoliubov-Valatin transformation relating in-medium quasi-particle operators to asymptotic free-particle operators, to obtain analytic expressions for the two-particle correlation function. They compute the squeezed correlation function for phi-phi boson pairs (where the particle is its own antiparticle) as a function of the average momentum K = (k1 + k2)/2 and the relative momentum q = k1 - k2, for finite-size systems with and without radial flow, and also examine the effect of squeezing on the HBT correlation function.

## Key numbers

- The squeezed correlation signal is analyzed for momentum regions roughly 0 ≤ |K| ≤ 100 MeV and 500 ≤ |q| ≤ 1200 MeV, where the signal is stronger in the presence of flow.
- System sizes of R = 7 fm and R = 3 fm are compared, with the inverse width of the correlation curve as a function of 2|K| reflecting the system size.
- A finite emission time factor F(Δt) = [1 + (ω1 + ω2)² Δt²/ħ]⁻¹ reduces the squeezed signal by about two orders of magnitude, yet the phi-phi BBC correlation intensity remains sizable.
- The non-relativistic limit of the proposed relativistic variable is Q²_bbc → (2K)², where Q²_bbc = 4(ω1ω2 - K^μ K_μ).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical formulation of Back-to-Back Correlations (BBC) originally established for static infinite media by Asakawa, Csorgo, and Gyulassy (1999) and for fermions by Padula, Chen, Huang, Kao, and Pan (2001), and later developed for finite-size expanding systems by Padula, Huang, Kao, Pan, and Csorgo (2005). The paper proposes practical experimental variables for searching at RHIC and future LHC energies, and identifies that squeezing effects oppose flow broadening in HBT correlation functions, providing an additional observable signature of in-medium mass modification.
