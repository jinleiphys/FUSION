# Nuclear collective excitations using correlated realistic interactions: the role of explicit RPA correlations

> We examine to which extent correlated realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions, derived within the Unitary Correlation Operator Method (UCOM), can describe nuclear collective motion in the framework of first-order random-phase approximation (RPA). To this end we employ the correlated Argonne V18 interaction in calculations within the so-called "Extended" RPA (ERPA) and investigate the response of closed-shell nuclei. The ERPA is a renormalized RPA version which considers explicitly the depletion of the Fermi sea due to long-range correlations and thus allows us to examine how these affect the excitation spectra. It is found that the effect on the properties of giant resonances is rather small. Compared to the standard RPA, where excitations are built on top of the uncorrelated Hartree-Fock (HF) ground state, their centroid energies decrease by up to 1 MeV, approximately, in the isovector channel. The isoscalar response is less affected in general. Thus, the disagreement between our previous UCOM-based RPA calculations and the experimental data are to be attributed to other effects, mainly to a residual three-body force and higher-order configurations. Ground-state properties obtained within the ERPA are compared with corresponding HF and perturbation-theory results and are discussed as well. The ERPA formalism is presented in detail.

## Key claim

The paper examines whether explicit inclusion of long-range RPA ground-state correlations, via the Extended RPA (ERPA) method, can resolve the disagreement between previous UCOM-based standard RPA calculations and experimental data for giant resonances. The authors find that the effect of these correlations on giant resonance properties is rather small, with centroid energies decreasing by up to approximately 1 MeV in the isovector channel, and conclude that the remaining disagreement must be attributed mainly to a residual three-body force and higher-order configurations.

## Method

The authors employ the correlated Argonne V18 interaction derived within the Unitary Correlation Operator Method (UCOM) and perform calculations using the Extended RPA (ERPA), a renormalized RPA version that explicitly considers depletion of the Fermi sea due to long-range correlations. The ERPA equations are solved iteratively in the natural-orbital basis, using the number-operator method to avoid double-counting of second-order contributions, and the response of closed-shell nuclei is investigated for isoscalar and isovector giant resonances.

## Key numbers

- Centroid energies of giant resonances in the isovector channel decrease by up to 1 MeV compared to standard RPA results.
- The tensor correlator range parameter used is \(I_{\vartheta}^{(1,0)} = 0.09\) fm\(^3\), optimized for the Argonne V18 potential based on no-core shell model calculations for \(^3\)H and \(^4\)He.
- The harmonic-oscillator basis is typically restricted to 13 major shells for convergence of Hartree-Fock results.
- The mean square deviation per particle \(\sigma_{\min}\) of the one-body density matrix is expected to take values of order \(10^{-2}\) in realistic cases, corresponding to a Fermi-sea depletion of at least 10%.

## Context

This work extends previous UCOM-based standard RPA calculations by Papakonstantinou et al. (2006) which found that while isoscalar giant monopole resonance energies agreed reasonably with experiment, the isovector giant dipole resonance and isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance energies were overestimated by several MeV. The ERPA method used here was developed in earlier work by Catara et al. (1996), Chomaz et al. (1998), and Van Giai et al. (2000). The results indicate that the missing physics in the UCOM-based description is not primarily ground-state correlations but rather residual three-body forces and higher-order configurations such as two-particle-two-hole excitations, pointing toward the need for second-order RPA or inclusion of three-nucleon forces.
