# Recent Results of the Hadron Resonance Gas Model and the Chemical Freeze-out of Strange Hadrons

> A detailed discussion of recent results obtained within the hadron resonance gas model with the multi-component hard core repulsion is presented. Among them there are the adiabatic chemical freeze-out criterion, the concept of separate chemical freeze-out of strange particles and the effects of enhancement and sharpening of wide resonances and quark gluon bags occurring in a thermal medium. These findings are discussed in order to strengthen the planned heavy-ion collision experimental programs at low collision energies. We argue, that due to found effects, at the center of mass collision energy 4-8 GeV the quark gluon bags may appear directly or in decays as new heavy resonances with the narrow width of about 50-150 MeV and with the mass above 2.5 GeV.

## Key claim

The authors argue that at center-of-mass collision energies of 4-8 GeV, quark gluon bags may appear directly or in decays as new heavy resonances with narrow width (about 50-150 MeV) and mass above 2.5 GeV, but this prediction is conditional on the effects of thermal enhancement and sharpening of wide resonances occurring in a thermal medium.

## Method

The authors used the hadron resonance gas model with multi-component hard-core repulsion (HRGM2), which includes all hadronic resonances with masses up to 2.5 GeV with their finite width, accounts for thermal and nonthermal hadronic multiplicities from resonance decays, and enforces strangeness conservation. They analyzed chemical freeze-out parameters by fitting experimental mid-rapidity hadron yield ratios from low AGS to highest RHIC energies, and developed a separate chemical freeze-out concept for strange particles (SPFO) using isentropic evolution and conservation laws.

## Key numbers

- Entropy per particle at chemical freeze-out is constant at \(s/\rho \simeq 7.18\)
- Best global fit of hadron yields gives \(\chi^2/dof = 80.5/69 \simeq 1.16\) with hard-core radii: pions 0.1 fm, kaons 0.38 fm, other mesons 0.4 fm, baryons 0.2 fm
- For the SPFO approach, fit quality is \(\chi^2/dof = 58.5/55 \simeq 1.06\)
- Predicted narrow resonances from quark gluon bags have width about 50-150 MeV and mass above 2.5 GeV at chemical freeze-out temperatures \(T \simeq 80-140\) MeV
- The power-law exponents for the effective mass spectrum are \(A_B \simeq 6.097 \pm 0.38\) for baryons and \(A_M \simeq 5.31 \pm 0.14\) for mesons
- In the energy range \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \in [4.3; 4.9]\) GeV, a 15% increase in collision energy leads to about 6.75 times increase in entropy density and particle density

## Context

This work extends the hadron resonance gas model developed by Bugaev and Andronic (2005, 2009) and resolves long-standing puzzles in chemical freeze-out criteria, particularly showing that the constant energy per particle criterion (Cleymans, 2006) and constant \(s/T^3\) criterion (Tawfik, 2006) are not robust. The authors introduce a separate chemical freeze-out concept for strange particles that improves upon the strangeness suppression factor \(\gamma_s\) approach (StrangSuppr, 2003, 2005) and provides better fits to the Strangeness Horn data. The predicted narrow resonances from quark gluon bags could be searched for at the NICA JINR and FAIR GSI experimental programs, and the observed irregularities in thermodynamic quantities at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \in [4.3; 4.9]\) GeV may indicate formation of a mixed quark-gluon-hadron phase.
