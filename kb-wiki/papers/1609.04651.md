# Strong Couplings of Three Mesons with Charm(ing) Involvement

> We determine the strong couplings of three mesons that involve, at least, one $η_c$ or $J/ψ$ meson, within the framework of a constituent-quark model by means of relativistic dispersion formulations. For strong couplings of $J/ψ$ mesons to two charmed mesons, our approach leads to predictions roughly twice as large as those arising from QCD sum rules.

### Key claim
The authors determine strong couplings of three mesons involving at least one charmonium (η_c or J/ψ) using a relativistic constituent-quark model with dispersion relations, and find that for J/ψ couplings to two charmed mesons, their predictions are roughly twice as large as those from QCD sum rules.

### Method
The strong couplings are extracted from the residues of poles in meson–meson transition form factors (F_+, V, A_0), which are computed via double dispersion integrals of one-loop Feynman diagrams within a relativistic constituent-quark model. The meson wave functions are taken as Gaussians with slopes β fixed to reproduce leptonic decay constants f_P,V, and the form factors are extrapolated to the resonance poles using three-parameter fits to extract the couplings.

### Key numbers
- η_c-η_c-J/ψ coupling g_{PP'V} = 25.8 ± 1.7 (dimensionless)
- D-D-J/ψ coupling g_{PP'V} = 26.04 ± 1.43 (dimensionless)
- D-D*-η_c coupling g_{PP'V} = 15.51 ± 0.45 (dimensionless)
- D_s-D_s-J/ψ coupling g_{PP'V} = 23.83 ± 0.78 (dimensionless)
- D_s-D_s*-η_c coupling g_{PP'V} = 14.15 ± 0.52 (dimensionless)
- η_c-J/ψ-J/ψ coupling g_{PV'V} = 10.6 ± 1.5 GeV^{-1}
- D-D*-J/ψ coupling g_{PV'V} = 10.7 ± 0.4 GeV^{-1}
- D*-D*-η_c coupling g_{PV'V} = 9.76 ± 0.32 GeV^{-1}
- D_s-D_s*-J/ψ coupling g_{PV'V} = 9.6 ± 0.8 GeV^{-1}
- D_s*-D_s*-η_c coupling g_{PV'V} = 8.27 ± 0.37 GeV^{-1}
- For comparison, QCD sum-rule values: D-D-J/ψ = 11.6 ± 1.8 (Mat05); D-D*-J/ψ = (4.0 ± 0.6) GeV^{-1} (Mat05); D_s-D_s-J/ψ = 11.96^{+1.34}_{-1.16} (Bra14); D_s-D_s*-J/ψ = (4.30^{+1.53}_{-1.22}) GeV^{-1} (Bra15)

### Context
This work extends previous quark-model studies (Lucha et al., 2015) by systematically computing strong couplings for charmonia and charmed mesons, and directly contradicts QCD sum-rule results (Mat05; Bra14; Bra15) which yield values roughly half as large. The results are intended for use in studies of long-distance QCD effects in hadron decays involving charmed mesons or charmonia in the final state, similar to the analysis in Ref. PS.
