# New QCD Sum Rules for Nucleons in Nuclear Matter

> Two new QCD sum rules for nucleons in nuclear matter are obtained from a mixed correlator of spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 interpolating fields. These new sum rules, which are insensitive to the poorly known four-quark condensates, provide additional information on the nucleon scalar self-energy. These new sum rules are analyzed along with previous spin-1/2 interpolator-based sum rules which are also insensitive to the poorly known four-quark condensates. The analysis indicates consistency with the expectations of relativistic nuclear phenomenology at nuclear matter saturation density. However, a weaker density dependence near saturation is suggested. Using previous estimates of in-medium condensate uncertainties, we find $M^* = 0.64^{+0.13}_{-0.09}$ GeV and $Σ_v = 0.29^{+0.06}_{-0.10}$ GeV at nuclear matter saturation density.

## Key claim

The new QCD sum rules derived from a mixed spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 interpolating field correlator are consistent with the magnitudes of nucleon self-energies predicted by relativistic nuclear phenomenology at saturation density, but suggest a weaker density dependence near saturation than those phenomenological models.

## Method

The authors constructed a mixed correlator of spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 interpolating fields for nucleons in nuclear matter, evaluated the operator product expansion (OPE) at large space-like momenta, and equated this to a phenomenological spectral representation using Borel-transformed sum rules. Two new sum rules insensitive to the poorly known even-chirality four-quark condensates were obtained and analyzed together with two previous spin-1/2 interpolator-based sum rules that also avoid these condensates. A Monte Carlo error analysis with Gaussian distributions for condensate values and related QCD parameters was used to estimate uncertainties in the extracted spectral parameters.

## Key numbers

- Effective nucleon mass at saturation density: $M^* = 0.64^{+0.13}_{-0.09}$ GeV (normalized to vacuum nucleon mass)
- Vector self-energy at saturation density: $\Sigma_v = 0.29^{+0.06}_{-0.10}$ GeV (normalized to vacuum nucleon mass)
- Vacuum ratio $M^*/M_N$ at saturation density: $0.69 \pm {}^{0.14}_{0.07}$
- Vacuum ratio $\Sigma_v/M_N$ at saturation density: $0.30 \pm {}^{0.06}_{0.14}$
- Vacuum ratio $(M^* + \Sigma_v)/M_N$ at saturation density: $0.99 \pm 0.03$
- Saturation ratio for $M^*$ at $\rho_N = 0.5$: $1.06 \pm 0.05$; at $\rho_N = 1.5$: $0.96 \pm 0.05$
- Saturation ratio for $\Sigma_v$ at $\rho_N = 0.5$: $0.88 \pm 0.09$; at $\rho_N = 1.5$: $1.09 \pm 0.07$

## Context

This work extends the finite-density QCD sum-rule approach for nucleons in nuclear matter previously developed by Cohen, Furnstahl, Griegel, and Jin (1991-1995), which had been sensitive to poorly known four-quark condensates. The new sum rules from the mixed correlator provide independent information on the scalar self-energy that avoids this sensitivity. The results are consistent with relativistic mean-field phenomenology (Serot and Walecka, 1986; Serot, 1992) at saturation density but indicate a weaker density dependence, and the uncertainties in $(M^* + \Sigma_v)$ confirm that determining binding energies on the order of 16 MeV is beyond current QCD sum-rule precision.
