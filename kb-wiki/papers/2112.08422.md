# Holographic approach to compact stars and their binary mergers

> In this review article, we describe the role of holography in deciphering the physics of dense QCD matter, relevant for the description of compact stars and their binary mergers. We review the strengths and limitations of the holographic duality in describing strongly interacting matter at large baryon density, walk the reader through the most important results derived using the holographic approach so far, and highlight a number of outstanding open problems in the field. Finally, we discuss how we foresee holography contributing to compact-star physics in the coming years.

## Key claim

Holography can serve as a nonperturbative tool to describe strongly coupled QCD matter at high baryon density, a regime inaccessible to lattice QCD due to the sign problem, but the method is limited by the large-\(N_c\) approximation and the fact that top-down models describe field theories rather different from QCD, while bottom-up models are more phenomenological.

## Method

The paper reviews the application of the gauge/gravity duality (holography) to dense QCD matter relevant for neutron stars and their mergers. It surveys both top-down approaches (rigorous string-theory constructions of field theories with some similarities to QCD) and bottom-up approaches (phenomenological models tuned to reproduce QCD-like behavior). The review covers how holographic calculations yield the equation of state (EoS) and transport coefficients of unpaired quark matter, and how these results are used as input for the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equations to generate mass-radius relations and for simulations of binary neutron-star mergers.

## Key numbers

* The nuclear saturation density is \(n_s \approx 0.16\)/fm\(^3\).
* Neutron stars have masses of approximately 1–2\(M_\odot\) and radii of approximately 11–13 km.
* The most massive known neutron star, pulsar J0740+6620, has a mass of \(2.08 \pm 0.07 M_\odot\) (one-sigma uncertainty).
* From the GW170817 event, LIGO and Virgo placed a 90% confidence interval of \(70 < \tilde{\Lambda} < 720\) for the binary tidal deformability parameter.
* Perturbative QCD becomes accurate only around \(40 n_s\), while chiral effective theory is quantitative only up to densities slightly above \(n_s\) (1–1.5\(n_s\)).

## Context

This review extends the line of work applying holography to strongly coupled matter, previously successful in heavy-ion physics (e.g., the shear-viscosity-to-entropy ratio bound from Policastro (2001) and Kovtun (2004), and thermalization studies by Chesler (2008) and Heller (2012)). It contrasts holography with first-principles approaches: chiral effective theory (Hebeler 2009, Tews 2012) at low densities and perturbative QCD (Gorda 2018, 2021) at very high densities, which leave the neutron-star core region (5–9\(n_s\)) unconstrained. The review enables future holographic studies to fill this gap by providing EoS and transport inputs for neutron-star structure calculations and merger simulations.
