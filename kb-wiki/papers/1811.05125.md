# In-medium masses and magnetic moments of decuplet baryons

> The magnetic moments of baryon decuplet are studied in vacuum as well as in the symmetric nuclear matter at finite temperature using a chiral SU(3) quark mean field model approach. The contributions coming from the valence quarks, quark sea and the orbital angular momentum of the quark sea have been considered to calculate magnetic moment of decuplet baryons. The decuplet baryon masses decrease, whereas, the magnetic moments increase significantly with the rise of baryonic density of the symmetric nuclear medium. This is because of the reason that constituent quark magnetic moment and the quark spin polarizations show considerable variation in the nuclear medium especially in the low temperature and baryonic density regime. The increase is however quantitatively less as compared to the case of octet baryon members.

## Key claim

The magnetic moments of decuplet baryons increase significantly with baryonic density of symmetric nuclear matter, but this increase is quantitatively less than that observed for octet baryon members, particularly in the low temperature and baryonic density regime.

## Method

The authors used a chiral SU(3) quark mean field model to calculate in-medium masses and magnetic moments of decuplet baryons in symmetric nuclear matter at finite temperature. The magnetic moments were computed by summing contributions from valence quarks, quark sea, and orbital angular momentum of the quark sea, with medium-modified constituent quark masses and spin polarizations obtained from self-consistent solutions of scalar meson mean field equations.

## Key numbers

- At zero temperature, for baryonic density increase from 0 to nuclear saturation density ρ₀, Δ baryons show a 29% decrease in effective mass from their vacuum value of 1232 MeV.
- At zero temperature, for density increase from 0 to ρ₀, Σ*, Ξ*, and Ω baryons show decreases of 21.3%, 15.1%, and 10% in effective masses, respectively.
- At zero temperature, for density increase from ρ₀ to 2ρ₀, the percentage decreases in baryon masses are 22% for Δ, 11% for Σ*, 7% for Ξ*, and 4.7% for Ω.
- At ρ_B = 0, effective masses of Δ, Σ*, Ξ*, and Ω increase by 5 MeV, 9 MeV, 13 MeV, and 16 MeV, respectively, for temperature rise from 0 to 100 MeV.
- At nuclear saturation density ρ₀, for temperature increase from 0 to 100 MeV, effective masses of Δ, Σ*, Ξ*, and Ω increase by 80.7 MeV, 69 MeV, 58 MeV, and 43 MeV, respectively.
- The spin energy corrections used are: E_Δ_spin = -214 MeV, E_Ω_spin = -192 MeV, E_Σ*_spin = -201 MeV, E_Ξ*0_spin = -193 MeV, E_Ξ*-_spin = -190 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of octet baryon magnetic moments in symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter (Singh, Kumar, Dahiya, 2018) to the decuplet baryon sector, where medium modification of magnetic moments had not been previously discussed in the literature. The chiral SU(3) quark mean field model approach provides thermodynamically consistent predictions that can inform future experiments at facilities such as FAIR at GSI, CSR at HIRFL, and FRIB, which aim to measure medium-modified baryon properties. The vacuum magnetic moments obtained are comparable to those from Large-N_c approaches (Aarti, 2014) and χCQM model calculations (Aarti, 2014), with the present work using constituent quark masses m_u = m_d = 313 MeV and m_s = 490 MeV.
