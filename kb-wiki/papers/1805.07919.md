# Precision of finite-difference representation in 3D coordinate-space Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations

> The current work plans to study the accuracy due to FD approximation to the 3D nuclear HFB problem. By (1) taking the wave functions solved in harmonic oscillator (HO) basis, (2) representing the HFB problem in coordinate space using FD method, the current work carefully evaluates the error due to box discretization by examining the deviation of the resulted HFB matrix, the total energies in the coordinate space, from those calculated with HO method, the latter of which is free from numerical error within its model configuration. To estimate how the error (given by the box discretization schemes suggested above) accumulates with self-consistent iterations, self-consistent HF and HFB calculations (with two-basis method) has been carried out for doubly magic nuclei, $^{40}$Ca, $^{132}$Sn, and $^{110}$Mo. The resulted total energies are compared with those of HO basis, and 3D coordinate space calculations in literatures. The analysis shows that, for grid spacing $\le$0.6\,fm, the off-diagonal elements of the resulted HFB matrix elements (M.E.) are extremely small ($<$1\,keV). The resulted quasi-particle (q.p.) spectra differ from those of HO calculations by a few keV. Self-consistent HF and HFB calculations within the current FD method with the above box discretizatioin schemes give results similar to those calculations of existing HO basis, and coordinate space method.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for 3D coordinate-space Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations using finite-difference discretization, grid spacings of 0.7 fm or smaller yield total energy contributions accurate to a few tens of keV, but this conclusion applies specifically to calculations without self-consistency and requires verification through self-consistent iterations.

## Method

The author evaluated finite-difference approximation errors by taking converged wave functions from harmonic oscillator basis calculations (using the HFODD code) and representing them in 3D coordinate space with finite-difference discretization, then comparing the resulting HFB matrix elements, quasi-particle spectra, and total energies against the harmonic oscillator results. Self-consistent Hartree-Fock and Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations using the two-basis method were performed for doubly magic nuclei ⁴⁰Ca, ¹³²Sn, and ¹¹⁰Mo with the SkM* Skyrme force and volume pairing. The finite-difference method used seven-point formulas for derivatives and nine-point formulas for Laplacians, with trapezoidal rule integration evaluated at cell centers rather than grid points.

## Key numbers

- For grid spacing ≤0.6 fm, off-diagonal HFB matrix elements are <1 keV
- For grid spacing ≤0.7 fm (schemes C-G), the number of off-diagonal matrix elements ≥10 keV is less than 10% of the total number of off-diagonal matrix elements
- For ¹¹⁰Mo with grid spacing 0.4 fm (scheme G), the total energy using the improved kinetic density evaluation differs from HFODD by ≤200 keV (‑920.751 MeV vs ‑920.745 MeV)
- For ⁴⁰Ca with grid spacing 0.625 fm and box size 30 fm, the total energy is ‑344.259 MeV compared to HFODD value of ‑344.251 MeV
- For ¹³²Sn with grid spacing 0.625 fm and box size 30 fm, the total energy is ‑1103.508 MeV compared to HFODD value of ‑1102.935 MeV
- Quasi-particle spectra from finite-difference calculations differ from harmonic oscillator calculations by a few keV (mostly under 1 keV throughout the spectra)

## Context

This work extends previous precision studies of finite-difference methods by Blum (1992), who used a Woods-Saxon mean-field to simulate energy density functional results, by now evaluating errors directly within self-consistent Skyrme energy density functional calculations. The study enables practitioners to choose appropriate grid spacings and box sizes for 3D coordinate-space Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations, particularly for time-dependent simulations where the error analysis at fixed iteration provides a useful starting point. The results are consistent with coordinate-space calculations by Pei (2014) and Ryssens (2015), and the self-consistent Hartree-Fock total energies agree within a few tens of keV with those reported in Ryssens (2015).
