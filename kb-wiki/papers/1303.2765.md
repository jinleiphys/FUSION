# Superheavy magic structures in the relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approach

> We have explored the occurrence of the spherical shell closures for superheavy nuclei in the framework of the relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (RHFB) theory. Shell effects are characterized in terms of two-nucleon gaps $δ_{2n(p)}$. Although the results depend slightly on the effective Lagrangians used, the general set of magic numbers beyond $^{208}$Pb are predicted to be $Z = 120$, $138$ for protons and $N = 172$, 184, 228 and 258 for neutrons, respectively. Specifically the RHFB calculations favor the nuclide $^{304}$120 as the next spherical doubly magic one beyond $^{208}$Pb. Shell effects are sensitive to various terms of the mean-field, such as the spin-orbit coupling, the scalar and effective masses.

## Key claim

The relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (RHFB) calculations predict that the nuclide 304120 is the most probable next spherical doubly magic system beyond 208Pb, but the results depend on the effective Lagrangian used and the shell effects are much weaker than in ordinary nuclei.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (RHFB) calculations for superheavy nuclei with proton numbers Z = 110-140, using the PKA1, PKO2, and PKO3 effective Lagrangians, and compared with relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations using PKDD and DD-ME2 Lagrangians. Shell effects were characterized by two-nucleon gaps δ2p and δ2n, defined as differences of two-nucleon separation energies between neighboring isotopes or isotones. The pairing channel employed the finite-range Gogny force D1S renormalized by a strength factor f = 0.9, adjusted to reproduce odd-even mass differences of odd Pb isotopes.

## Key numbers

- The predicted proton magic numbers beyond 208Pb are Z = 120 and 138; the predicted neutron magic numbers are N = 172, 184, 228, and 258.
- The nuclide 304120 (Z=120, N=184) is favored as the next spherical doubly magic one beyond 208Pb.
- The two-nucleon gaps for the predicted magic numbers range between 3 and 5 MeV.
- The radial cutoff for the Dirac Woods-Saxon basis was R = 28 fm, with 44 positive and 12 negative energy states per single-particle angular momentum.
- The strength factor f = 0.9 was used to renormalize the Gogny D1S pairing force.
- The PKA1 Lagrangian predicts saturation density ρ0 = 0.160 fm-3, binding energy per particle EB/A = -15.83 MeV, incompressibility K = 229.96 MeV, asymmetry energy coefficient J = 36.02 MeV, scalar mass M*S = 0.547, and non-relativistic effective mass M*NR = 0.681 (all in units of nucleon mass M).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of superheavy magic numbers using relativistic mean field (RMF) models (Rutz 1997) and RHB models (Zhang 2005) by including the Fock term explicitly through the RHFB framework, which allows inclusion of π and ρ-tensor meson couplings absent in Hartree-type approaches. The results are compared with non-relativistic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) predictions (Rutz 1997) and Gogny force calculations (Decharge 2003). The study enables future experimental searches for Z = 120 isotopes, noting that measurement of Qα for at least one isotope of Z = 120 would help constrain shell effects, and acknowledges that deformation effects need to be included for more extensive exploration.
