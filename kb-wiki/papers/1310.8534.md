# Energy evolution of the moments of the hadron distribution in QCD jets including NNLL resummation and NLO running-coupling corrections

> The moments of the single inclusive momentum distribution of hadrons in QCD jets, are studied in the next-to-modified-leading-log approximation (NMLLA) including next-to-leading-order (NLO) corrections to the alpha_s strong coupling. The evolution equations are solved using a distorted Gaussian parametrisation, which successfully reproduces the spectrum of charged hadrons of jets measured in e+e- collisions. The energy dependencies of the maximum peak, multiplicity, width, kurtosis and skewness of the jet hadron distribution are computed analytically. Comparisons of all the existing jet data measured in e+e- collisions in the range sqrt(s)~2-200 GeV to the NMLLA+NLO* predictions allow one to extract a value of the QCD parameter Lambda_QCD, and associated two-loop coupling constant at the Z resonance alpha_s(m_Z^2)= 0.1195 +/- 0.0022, in excellent numerical agreement with the current world average obtained using other methods.

## Key claim

The paper computes the energy evolution of the moments of the hadron momentum distribution in QCD jets at next-to-modified-leading-logarithmic approximation (NMLLA) including next-to-leading-order (NLO) running-coupling corrections, and extracts a value of the QCD coupling constant at the Z resonance of αs(mZ²) = 0.1195 ± 0.0022 from fits to all existing e+e− jet data in the range √s ≈ 2–200 GeV, under the assumption that the hadronisation scale can be taken as low as ΛQCD (the limiting spectrum).

## Method

The authors solve the evolution equations for the single inclusive fragmentation functions in Mellin space, expanding the splitting functions to order Ω (which corresponds to including NMLLA corrections of order αs that improve energy conservation) and using the two-loop (NLO) running of the strong coupling αs. The solution is expressed via a distorted Gaussian parametrisation of the hadron energy spectrum, whose moments (mean multiplicity, peak position, width, skewness, and kurtosis) are evolved analytically as functions of the jet energy. The theoretical predictions are compared to all available e+e− jet data to extract ΛQCD and the associated αs(mZ²).

## Key numbers

- Extracted strong coupling at the Z resonance: αs(mZ²) = 0.1195 ± 0.0022.
- The analysis covers e+e− collision energies in the range √s ≈ 2–200 GeV.
- The QCD scale ΛQCD is the single free parameter in the fits, with the hadronisation scale Q0 taken to ΛQCD (limiting spectrum).
- The two-loop strong coupling expression used is αs(q²) = (4π/β₀ ln q²)[1 − (2β₁/β₀²)(ln ln q²/ln q²)], with β₀ = (11/3)Nc − (4nfTR/3) and β₁ = (51/3)Nc − (38nfTR/3).

## Context

This work extends previous MLLA calculations of the hump-backed plateau moments (Fong and Webber, 1991) by including NMLLA corrections of order αs and NLO running-coupling effects, which improve energy conservation and the description of data at lower energies. It also generalises the NMLLA+NLO* treatment previously applied only to jet multiplicities (Dremin, 2000; Capella, 1999) to the full set of distribution moments. The extracted αs(mZ²) agrees with the world average from other methods, and the approach enables more precise determinations of ΛQCD from jet fragmentation data than earlier studies (Albino, 2004–2005) which used LO approximations and smaller data sets.
