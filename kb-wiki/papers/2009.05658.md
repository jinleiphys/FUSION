# Coupled Transport Equations for Quarkonium Production in Heavy Ion Collisions

> Motivated by recent applications of the open quantum system formalism to understand quarkonium transport in the quark-gluon plasma, we develop a set of coupled Boltzmann equations for open heavy quark-antiquark pairs and quarkonia. Our approach keeps track of the correlation between the heavy quark-antiquark pair from quarkonium dissociation and thus is able to account for both uncorrelated and correlated recombination. By solving the coupled Boltzmann equations for current heavy ion collision experiments, we find correlated recombination is crucial to describe the data of bottomonia nuclear modification factors. To further test the importance of correlated recombination in experiments, we propose a new observable: $\frac{R_{AA}[χ_b(1P)]}{R_{AA}[Υ(2S)]}$. Future measurements of this ratio will help distinguish calculations with and without correlated recombination.

## Key claim

The authors develop coupled Boltzmann equations for open heavy quark-antiquark pairs and quarkonia that explicitly track the correlation between the pair from quarkonium dissociation, enabling both uncorrelated and correlated recombination; they find that correlated recombination is crucial to describe bottomonia nuclear modification factor data, but acknowledge that tuning parameters in calculations without correlated recombination might also achieve similar agreement with currently available data.

## Method

The authors construct a set of coupled Boltzmann transport equations for unbound heavy quark-antiquark pairs and individual quarkonium states (labeled by quantum numbers n, l, s). The equations include collision terms for dissociation and recombination, where the heavy quark and antiquark are treated as interacting independently with the quark-gluon plasma medium. The coupled equations are solved via Monte Carlo simulations, with initial distributions sampled from Pythia for momenta and TRENTo for positions, using the EPPS16 nuclear parton distribution function parametrization and a calibrated 2+1D viscous hydrodynamic description of the bulk medium.

## Key numbers

- Bottomonia nuclear modification factor R_AA as a function of centrality at 5.02 TeV Pb-Pb collisions: calculations with correlated recombination describe CMS data, while calculations without correlated recombination fail to describe the data even when accounting for uncertainties from model parameters and nPDF.
- The ratio R_AA[chi_b(1P)] / R_AA[Upsilon(2S)] is approximately 1 in calculations without correlated recombination, but approximately 1/3 in calculations with correlated recombination.
- Model parameters used for the upper and lower curves in the R_AA plots differ by 10% from the middle curve.

## Context

This work extends the open quantum system formalism for quarkonium transport in the quark-gluon plasma, as developed by Akamatsu (2011), Blaizot (2018), Yao (2018), Brambilla (2019), and Miura (2019), by incorporating correlated recombination into semiclassical transport calculations. Previous studies by Thews (2000), Grandchamp (2003), and Yao (2017) investigated uncorrelated recombination in transport equations, and Andronic (2007) studied it in statistical hadronization models, but few explored the phenomenological consequence of correlated recombination. The proposed new observable R_AA[chi_b(1P)] / R_AA[Upsilon(2S)] enables future experimental tests to distinguish between models with and without correlated recombination, complementing the approach of Krouppa (2015) which had no correlated recombination.
