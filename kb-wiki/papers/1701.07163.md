# Spin polarization observables of the deuteron photodisintegration at low energies in pionless effective field theory

> Spin polarization observables of the deuteron photodisintegration at low energies are studied in a pionless effective field theory up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO). The total and differential cross sections, induced neutron polarization $P_{y'}$, and tensor analyzing powers $T_{20}$ and $T_{22}$ of the process are calculated at photon energies from the breakup threshold to 20~MeV. We find that the NNLO corrections in the cross sections and $P_{y'}$ converge well whereas they turn out to be important contributions in $T_{20}$ and $T_{22}$. We discuss the discrepancy between theory and experiment in $P_{y'}$ still persisting as well as an implication of our result to the first measurement of $T_{20}$ at low energies in the HIGS facility.

# Spin polarization observables of the deuteron photodisintegration at low energies in pionless effective field theory

## Key claim

The authors calculate spin polarization observables of deuteron photodisintegration using pionless effective field theory up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) and find that while NNLO corrections converge well for cross sections and induced neutron polarization, they are essential for tensor analyzing powers T20 and T22. However, the persistent discrepancy between theory and experiment for the induced neutron polarization Py' at 2.75 MeV remains unresolved even after including NNLO corrections.

## Method

The authors employ pionless effective field theory in the dibaryon formalism, constructing an effective Lagrangian with nucleon fields and dibaryon fields for the 1S0 and 3S1 two-nucleon channels. They compute hadronic currents for the gamma + d → n + p process up to NNLO, including sd-wave mixing effects from the deuteron wavefunction, and calculate total and differential cross sections, induced neutron polarization Py', and tensor analyzing powers T20 and T22 at photon energies from threshold to 19.8 MeV in the center-of-mass frame.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section: NNLO correction is about 10% of the NLO contribution at E_gamma_lab ~ 20 MeV, and about 20% at E_gamma_lab ~ 30 MeV
- Deuteron binding momentum: gamma = 45.70 MeV, corresponding to deuteron binding energy B = 2.225 MeV
- Effective range parameters: a0 = -23.71 fm and r0 = 2.73 fm in the 1S0 channel; rho_d = 1.764 fm in the deuteron channel
- Asymptotic s/d wave ratio: eta_sd = 0.0254, used to fix the C_sd parameter
- L1 parameter: L1 = -4.41 fm, fixed by thermal neutron capture cross section
- Photon energy range studied: from breakup threshold to 19.8 MeV (laboratory frame limited to E_gamma_lab ≤ 20 MeV for spin observables)

## Context

This work extends previous pionless EFT calculations of deuteron photodisintegration at NLO by Ando (2011) to NNLO, incorporating sd-wave mixing effects. The authors employ the same hadronic current expressions used by Christlmeier and Grießhammer (2008) for electrodisintegration. The calculation is motivated by the HIGS facility proposal to measure T20 at low energies, where no experimental data currently exist below 19.8 MeV. The results are compared with phenomenological potential model calculations using the Bonn potential by Arenhövel (1990) and the Av18 model by Schiavilla (2005), with the pionless EFT results supporting the Av18 predictions over the experimental measurements by Nath (1972) for Py'.
