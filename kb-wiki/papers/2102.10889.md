# ADG: Automated generation and evaluation of many-body diagrams III. Bogoliubov in-medium similarity renormalization group formalism

> The goal of the present paper is twofold. First, a novel expansion many-body method applicable to superfluid open-shell nuclei, the so-called Bogoliubov in-medium similarity renormalization group (BIMSRG) theory, is formulated. This generalization of standard single-reference IMSRG theory for closed-shell systems parallels the recent extensions of coupled cluster, self-consistent Green's function or many-body perturbation theory. Within the realm of IMSRG theories, BIMSRG provides an interesting alternative to the already existing multi-reference IMSRG (MR-IMSRG) method applicable to open-shell nuclei. The algebraic equations for low-order approximations, i.e., BIMSRG(1) and BIMSRG(2), can be derived manually without much difficulty. However, such a methodology becomes already impractical and error prone for the derivation of the BIMSRG(3) equations, which are eventually needed to reach high accuracy. Based on a diagrammatic formulation of BIMSRG theory, the second objective of the present paper is thus to describe the third version (v3.0.0) of the ADG code that automatically (1) generates all valid BIMSRG(n) diagrams and (2) evaluates their algebraic expressions in a matter of seconds. This is achieved in such a way that equations can easily be retrieved for both the flow equation and the Magnus expansion formulations of BIMSRG. Expanding on this work, the first future objective is to numerically implement BIMSRG(2) (eventually BIMSRG(3)) equations and perform ab initio calculations of mid-mass open-shell nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper formulates a novel Bogoliubov in-medium similarity renormalization group (BIMSRG) theory for superfluid open-shell nuclei as an alternative to the existing multi-reference IMSRG (MR-IMSRG) approach, while also presenting version v3.0.0 of the ADG code that automatically generates and evaluates all valid BIMSRG(n) diagrams and their algebraic expressions in seconds, though the numerical implementation of BIMSRG(2) and eventually BIMSRG(3) equations for actual ab initio calculations of mid-mass open-shell nuclei remains a future objective.

## Method

The BIMSRG formalism generalizes single-reference IMSRG theory by employing a Bogoliubov quasiparticle vacuum as the reference state that breaks U(1) global-gauge symmetry associated with particle-number conservation, with operators expressed in normal-ordered form with respect to this vacuum. The flow equation dΩ(s)/ds = [η(s), Ω(s)] and the Magnus formulation using U(s) = exp(Ω(s)) are both derived, with truncation schemes BIMSRG(n) that retain only operator terms O^[2m] with m ≤ n throughout the flow. The ADG code encodes BIMSRG diagrams as square matrices (oriented adjacency matrices) and converts them into graph objects using the NetworkX package to evaluate many-body expressions on a purely diagrammatic basis, adding to existing capabilities for HF-MBPT and BMBPT diagrams from version v2.0.0.

## Context

This work extends the IMSRG paradigm to open-shell nuclei via symmetry breaking, paralleling recent extensions of coupled cluster (BCC theory by Signoracci et al., 2014 and Henderson et al., 2014), self-consistent Green's function (GGF theory by Soma et al., 2011-2014), and many-body perturbation theory (BMBPT by Duguet et al., 2015 and Tichai et al., 2018). It provides an alternative to the multi-reference IMSRG (MR-IMSRG) approach developed by Hergert et al. (2013-2014) for open-shell nuclei, and the automated diagram generation enables future numerical implementation of BIMSRG(2) and BIMSRG(3) for ab initio calculations of mid-mass open-shell nuclei up to systems with approximately one hundred interacting particles as demonstrated in prior IMSRG calculations by Morris et al. (2017).
