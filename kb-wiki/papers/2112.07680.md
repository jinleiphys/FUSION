# Factorization for Azimuthal Asymmetries in SIDIS at Next-to-Leading Power

> Differential measurements of the semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) process with polarized beams provide important information on the three-dimensional structure of hadrons. Among the various observables are azimuthal asymmetries that start at subleading power, and which give access to novel transverse momentum dependent distributions (TMDs). Theoretical predictions for these distributions are currently based on the parton model rather than a rigorous factorization based analysis. Working under the assumption that leading power Glauber interactions do not spoil factorization at this order, we use the Soft Collinear Effective Theory to derive a complete factorization formula for power suppressed hard scattering effects in SIDIS. This yields generalized definitions of the TMDs that depend on two longitudinal momentum fractions (one of them only relevant beyond tree level), and a complete proof that only the same leading power soft function appears and can be absorbed into the TMD distributions at this order. We also show that perturbative corrections can be accounted for with only one new hard coefficient. Factorization formulae are given for all spin dependent structure functions which start at next-to-leading power. Prospects for improved subleading power predictions that include resummation are discussed.

## Key claim

The authors derive a complete factorization formula for all spin-dependent SIDIS structure functions at next-to-leading power (NLP) using Soft Collinear Effective Theory, but only under the assumption that leading-power Glauber interactions do not spoil factorization at this order, a full proof of Glauber cancellation at NLP is left as a missing ingredient.

## Method

The authors employ Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) to systematically classify power corrections in the SIDIS hadronic tensor, expanding in the power counting parameter λ ∼ P_hT/Q. They decompose the hadronic tensor into nine independent structure functions using helicity projections in the hadronic Breit frame, then derive factorization formulae by considering three sources of power corrections: kinematic corrections, subleading-power hard scattering operators, and subleading-power Lagrangian contributions. The analysis shows that only the same leading-power soft function appears and can be absorbed into TMD distributions at this order, with perturbative corrections accounted for by only one new hard coefficient.

## Key numbers

- The power counting parameter λ ∼ P_hT/Q, where P_hT is the hadron transverse momentum and Q is the momentum transfer
- Leading-power structure functions scale as 1/P_hT²; NLP structure functions scale as 1/(P_hT Q); next-to-next-to-leading power (NNLP) structure function W_0^U scales as 1/Q²
- The SIDIS cross section is described by six kinematic variables: x = Q²/(2 P_N · q), y = (P_N · q)/(P_N · p_ℓ), z = (P_N · P_h)/(P_N · q), the transverse momentum P_hT, and an overall azimuthal angle ψ
- The leptonic structure functions L_i are proportional to 2Q²/(1−ε) where ε = (1 − y − ¼ y² γ²)/(1 − y + ¼ y² γ² + ½ y²) with γ = 2x M_N/Q

## Context

This work extends the rigorous TMD factorization theorems established by Collins, Soper and Sterman (1980s) for leading-power structure functions in SIDIS, Drell-Yan, and e⁺e⁻ annihilation, which have reached three-loop accuracy. It addresses the long-standing problem that subleading-power azimuthal asymmetries (such as the cos φ_h asymmetry) have only been treated at the parton model level, with prior work by Bacchetta et al. (2008) finding incompatibility with collinear factorization and later proposing a soft function inclusion validated only at first order. The results enable improved subleading-power predictions including resummation for all spin-dependent structure functions, providing the target form that a complete proof of factorization (including Glauber analysis) would need to achieve.
