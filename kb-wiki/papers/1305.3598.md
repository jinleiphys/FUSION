# Partial-wave analysis of $\bar K N$ scattering reactions

> We investigate the two-body reactions $\bar K N\rightarrow \bar K N$, $\bar K N\rightarrow πΛ$, and $\bar K N\rightarrow πΣ$ via single-energy partial-wave analyses in the c.m.\ energy range 1480 to 2100 MeV. The partial-wave amplitudes for these reactions thus extracted were constrained by a multichannel energy-dependent model satisfying unitarity of the partial-wave $S$-matrix. We obtain excellent predictions of differential cross sections, polarizations, polarized cross sections, and cross sections for these reactions from a global energy-dependent solution.

## Key claim

The authors perform single-energy partial-wave analyses of \(\bar K N\) scattering reactions in the c.m. energy range 1480 to 2100 MeV, extracting amplitudes that are then constrained by a multichannel energy-dependent model that satisfies unitarity of the partial-wave \(S\)-matrix, unlike previous analyses that violated unitarity by representing amplitudes as a simple sum of resonant and background terms.

## Method

The authors analyzed data for \(K^-p\rightarrow K^-p\), \(K^-p\rightarrow \bar K^0n\), \(K^-p\rightarrow \pi^0\Lambda\), and three charge states of \(K^-p\rightarrow \pi\Sigma\) in 20 MeV c.m. energy bins from 1480 to 2100 MeV. Within each bin, partial-wave isospin amplitudes were approximated by a first-order Taylor series expansion with slope parameters fixed from an initial global multichannel fit; the real and imaginary parts of the amplitudes at the bin center were the free parameters. A penalty term constrained fitted amplitudes from differing greatly from the initial global fit, and very small amplitudes ( \(|T(W_0)|<0.05\) ) were held fixed; the \(D_{03}\) amplitude was fixed at 1520 MeV due to the narrow \(\Lambda(1520)\) resonance. After obtaining single-energy amplitudes, a new global multichannel energy-dependent fit was performed, and the procedure was iterated, ultimately freeing only the \(S_{01}\), \(S_{11}\), and \(P_{01}\) amplitudes in the final single-energy stage.

## Key numbers

* The analysis covers the c.m. energy range 1480 to 2100 MeV, using 20 MeV energy bins.
* The \(D_{03}\) amplitude was held fixed at the bin with central energy \(W_0 = 1520\) MeV because the \(\Lambda(1520)\) resonance has a width of only about 16 MeV.
* Amplitudes with \(|T(W_0)| < 0.05\) were held fixed to reduce the number of free parameters.
* Uncertainties assigned to published partial-wave amplitudes were \(\pm 0.025\) for \(\bar K N\), \(\pm 0.035\) for \(\pi\Lambda\) and \(\pi\Sigma\), and \(\pm 0.050\) for other channels.
* The database includes differential cross section, polarization, polarized cross section, integrated cross section, and total cross section data; for example, the bin at \(W_0 = 1680\) MeV for \(\bar K N \rightarrow \bar K N\) contains 434 total data points.

## Context

This work extends earlier partial-wave analyses of \(\bar K N\) scattering (Armenteros 1969, Conforto 1971, Horn 1975, Hemingway 1975, Baillon 1975, Gopal 1977) which violated unitarity by representing amplitudes as a simple sum of resonant and background terms. The present analysis uses a multichannel energy-dependent model that satisfies unitarity, building on the initial global fit of Tulpan (2007) which included channels such as \(\bar K N\), \(\bar K^* N\), \(\bar K\Delta\), \(\pi\Lambda\), \(\pi\Lambda(1520)\), \(\pi\Sigma\), and \(\pi\Sigma(1385)\). The resulting single-energy amplitudes and global energy-dependent solution enable improved determination of resonance parameters for \(\Lambda^*\) and \(\Sigma^*\) hyperons, discussed in a companion paper (Manley 2013).
