# Sub-barrier capture reactions with $^{16,18}$O and $^{40,48}$Ca beams

> Various sub-barrier capture reactions with beams $^{16,18}$O and $^{40,48}$Ca are treated within the quantum diffusion approach. The role of neutron transfer in these capture reactions is discussed. The quasielastic and capture barrier distributions are analyzed and compared with the recent experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the influence of two-neutron transfer on sub-barrier capture reactions is not simply determined by the sign or magnitude of the Q-value, but rather by the change in nuclear deformations that results from the transfer; if deformations decrease after transfer, the capture cross section is suppressed even for positive Q-values.

## Method

The authors apply the quantum diffusion approach, which treats the relative distance between colliding nuclei as a single collective variable and incorporates fluctuation and dissipation effects to model channel couplings. They calculate capture cross sections for reactions with beams of 16,18O and 40,48Ca on various targets, assuming that two-neutron transfer with positive Q2n occurs just before barrier crossing and changes the mass numbers, deformation parameters, and thus the Coulomb barrier height. The nucleus-nucleus potential is calculated using a double-folding formalism with a Skyrme-type density-dependent effective nucleon-nucleon interaction.

## Key numbers

* For the reaction 40Ca+124Sn → 42Ca+122Sn, the two-neutron transfer Q-value is Q2n = 5.4 MeV, and the quadrupole deformation of the light nucleus increases from β2=0 (40Ca) to β2=0.25 (42Ca).
* For the reaction 40Ca+132Sn → 42Ca+130Sn, the two-neutron transfer Q-value is Q2n = 7.3 MeV, and the deformation of the light nucleus increases from β2=0 (40Ca) to β2=0.25 (42Ca).
* The difference in extracted Coulomb barrier heights between 40Ca+124Sn and 48Ca+124Sn is 1.1 MeV, and between 40Ca+132Sn and 48Ca+132Sn is 1.0 MeV.
* The friction coefficient used in the calculations is ℏλ = 2 MeV.
* For 18O, the extracted ground-state quadrupole deformation parameter is β2 = 0.1, obtained from describing the 18O+208Pb reaction where no positive-Q neutron transfer channels exist.

## Context

This work extends the quantum diffusion approach previously applied to heavy-ion capture reactions (Sargsyan et al., 2012) to systematically study the role of neutron transfer in sub-barrier capture. It directly addresses the experimental puzzle from Jia et al. (2012), where no fusion enhancement was observed for 18O+74Ge despite a positive Q2n-value, in contrast to the strong enhancements seen in 40Ca+124,132Sn reactions by Kolata et al. (2006). The paper enables future experiments to use quasielastic barrier distributions as a tool to probe sub-barrier capture dynamics and predicts a sharp change in barrier distribution slope at deep sub-barrier energies as an experimental signature of a change in interaction regime.
