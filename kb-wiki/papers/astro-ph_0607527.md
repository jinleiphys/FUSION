# Neutrino-Neutrino Interactions and Flavor Mixing in Dense Matter

> An algebraic approach to the neutrino propagation in dense media is presented. The Hamiltonian describing a gas of neutrinos interacting with each other and with background fermions is written in terms of the appropriate SU(N) operators, where N is the number of neutrino flavors. The evolution of the resulting many-body problem is formulated as a coherent-state path integral. Some commonly used approximations are shown to represent the saddle-point solution of the path integral for the full many-body system.

## Key claim

The paper presents an algebraic formulation of neutrino propagation in dense media using SU(N) operators and coherent-state path integrals, showing that the commonly used mean-field approximation for neutrino-neutrino interactions corresponds to the saddle-point (classical) solution of the full many-body path integral, not an exact result.

## Method

The authors construct the Hamiltonian for a neutrino gas with flavor mixing, background fermion interactions, and neutrino-neutrino forward scattering in terms of SU(2) generators for two flavors (generalizable to SU(N) for N flavors). They formulate the evolution as a coherent-state path integral and derive the Euler-Lagrange equations from the action functional, showing that the stationary path yields the standard mean-field evolution equations. They also demonstrate that the same result follows from a random-phase approximation linearization of the quadratic Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- The neutrino-neutrino forward scattering Hamiltonian coefficient includes the factor \(R_{pq} = (1 - \cos\vartheta_{pq})\), where \(\vartheta_{pq}\) is the angle between neutrino momenta.
- The Wolfenstein potential for electron interaction is \(V_c = \sqrt{2} G_F N_e\), where \(N_e = n_{e^-} - n_{e^+}\) is the net electron density.
- The MSW evolution equation for two flavors involves \(\Delta = \delta m^2 / (2p)\) and \(A = \sqrt{2} G_F N_e\).
- The neutrino-neutrino interaction Hamiltonian in algebraic form is \(H_{\nu \nu} = (\sqrt{2} G_F / V) \int d^3p \, d^3q \, R_{pq} \, \mathbf{J}(p) \cdot \mathbf{J}(q)\).

## Context

This work extends the algebraic treatment of neutrino oscillations in matter, building on the MSW mechanism (Wolfenstein 1978, Mikheev and Smirnov 1986) and earlier mean-field approaches to neutrino-neutrino scattering in supernovae (Qian and Fuller 1995, Pastor et al. 2002, Balantekin and Yuksel 2004). The path integral formulation provides a framework for systematic corrections beyond the mean-field approximation, enabling future calculations of quantum fluctuations in collective neutrino flavor evolution.
