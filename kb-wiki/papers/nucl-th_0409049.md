# The light-front vacuum and dynamics

> I give a quantum theoretical description of kinematically invariant vacuua on the algebra of free fields restricted to a light front and discuss the relation between the light-front Hamiltonian, P-, the vacuum, and Poincare invariance. This provides a quantum theoretical description of zero modes.

# The light-front vacuum and dynamics

## Key claim

The paper provides a quantum theoretical description of kinematically invariant vacuua on the algebra of free fields restricted to a light front, showing that the singularities in the light-front scalar product lead to a restriction on test functions that nevertheless permits a large class of inequivalent vacuum representations; however, a given dynamical operator can only annihilate one of these vacuum vectors, and full Poincaré invariance requires the scattering matrix to be independent of the light-front orientation, which is a strong but testable condition.

## Method

The author constructs the light-front Fock algebra \( \mathcal{A}_f \) as a Weyl algebra generated by finite linear combinations of exponentials of light-front fields smeared with Schlieder-Seiler test functions, which are Schwartz functions of the form \( \tilde{f}(\tilde{p}) = p^+ \tilde{g}(\tilde{p}) \) that remove the logarithmic divergence in the light-front scalar product. Vacuum generating functionals are expressed as products of the Fock representation functional \( S_0\{f\} = e^{-\frac{1}{4}(f,f)} \) and a functional \( s\{\hat{f}\} \) depending only on the zero-mode limit \( \hat{f}(\vec{p}_\perp) = \lim_{p^+ \to 0} \tilde{f}(p^+, \vec{p}_\perp)/p^+ \), which is the Fourier transform of a positive measure on Schwartz distributions in two variables. A mass operator \( M = \Pi O \Pi \) (with \( \Pi = I - |0\rangle\langle 0| \)) is constructed from any non-negative kinematically invariant operator \( O \), and the light-front Hamiltonian is given by \( P^- = (\vec{P}_\perp^2 + M^2)/P^+ \); full Poincaré invariance is then achieved by constructing rotation operators that leave the vacuum invariant, provided the scattering matrix is independent of the light-front orientation.

## Key numbers

- The light-front scalar product \( (f,g)_f := \int (d\tilde{p} \, \theta(p^+)/p^+) \, \tilde{f}^*(\tilde{p}) \tilde{g}(\tilde{p}) \) diverges logarithmically due to the \( p^+ = 0 \) singularity in the denominator when test functions are unrestricted.
- The generating functional of the Fock representation is \( S_0\{f\} := e^{-\frac{1}{4}(f,f)} \), which satisfies all required vacuum properties (normalization, reality, continuity, non-negativity, kinematic invariance, cluster properties).
- Vacuum generating functionals have the form \( S\{f\} = S_0\{f\} \, s\{\hat{f}\} \), where \( s\{\hat{f}\} = e^{\sum_n i^n s_n(\hat{f}, \ldots, \hat{f})} \) and the distributions \( s_n \) contain a factor \( \delta(\sum_i \vec{p}_{i\perp}) \) times a Euclidean-invariant Schwartz distribution in \( 2(n-1) \) independent variables.

## Context

This work extends the algebraic approach to light-front field theory by Schlieder and Seiler (no year given) by constructing a rigorous \( * \)-algebra of light-front fields with well-defined test functions and characterizing its full class of kinematically invariant vacuua. The construction provides a direct means to formulate dynamical models with non-trivial zero modes without relying on classical equations of motion or finite-degree-of-freedom models, and it enables the systematic study of inequivalent vacuum representations in relativistic quantum theories on the light front. The key open question is whether the condition of light-front-orientation-independent scattering matrices can be satisfied in practice for non-trivial interactions.
