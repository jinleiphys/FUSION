# Charged-current scattering off $^{16}$O nucleus as a detection channel for supernova neutrinos

> Event spectra of the neutrino-$^{16}$O charged-current reactions in Super-Kamiokande are evaluated for a future supernova neutrino burst. Since these channels are expected to be useful for diagnosing a neutrino spectrum with high average energy, the evaluations are performed not only for an ordinary supernova neutrino model but also for a model of neutrino emission from a black-hole-forming collapse. Using shell model results, whose excitation energies are consistent with the experimental data, the cross sections of the $^{16}$O($ν_e, e^-$)X and $^{16}$O($\barν_e, e^+$)X reactions for each nuclear state with a different excitation energy are employed in this study. It is found that, owing to the components of the reaction with higher excitation energy, the event spectrum becomes 4-7 MeV softer than that in the case without considering the excitation energies. In addition, a simplified approach to evaluate the event spectra is proposed for convenience and its validity is examined.

## Key claim

The event spectrum of recoil electrons/positrons from neutrino-16O charged-current reactions becomes 4-7 MeV softer than previously estimated when the excitation energies of individual nuclear states are properly taken into account, rather than assuming all reactions proceed to the ground state of the final nucleus.

## Method

The authors used shell model calculations with the SFO-tls Hamiltonian to compute partial cross sections for 42 excited states of 16F and 16N for the 16O(νe, e-)X and 16O(ν̄e, e+)X reactions, respectively. They then evaluated event spectra for Super-Kamiokande (32 kton fiducial volume, 10 kpc supernova distance) by summing these partial cross sections weighted by supernova neutrino fluxes, using both analytic spectral models and numerical models from the Supernova Neutrino Database. A simplified four-group approach was also developed by grouping the 42 states by excitation energy and selecting representative states for each group.

## Key numbers

- The energy thresholds are approximately 15 MeV for 16O(νe, e-)X and 11 MeV for 16O(ν̄e, e+)X reactions
- The ground-state excitation energy difference is 14.91 MeV for 16O(νe, e-)X and 10.93 MeV for 16O(ν̄e, e+)X
- The event spectrum becomes 4-7 MeV softer than the "single-energy case" that ignores excitation energies
- For the ordinary supernova model (20 M⊙ progenitor), the average νe energy is 9.32 MeV and average ν̄e energy is 11.1 MeV
- For the black-hole-forming collapse model (30 M⊙ progenitor), the average νe energy is 17.5 MeV and average ν̄e energy is 21.7 MeV
- The total energy emitted in νe for the ordinary supernova model is 3.30 × 10^52 erg
- The total energy emitted in νe for the black-hole-forming collapse model is 9.49 × 10^52 erg

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutrino-16O reactions by Haxton (1987), Kolbe et al. (2002), and others, but is the first to consistently use cross sections for each excited state to evaluate the energy distribution of recoil electrons/positrons. Prior work by Nikrant et al. (2018) and the Super-Kamiokande collaboration (2016) investigated event numbers using total cross sections without considering excitation energies for the recoil energy calculation. The results enable more accurate diagnosis of supernova neutrino spectra, particularly for high-energy neutrino events from black-hole-forming collapses and the early accretion phase of supernova neutrino bursts, and are directly applicable to the SK-Gd project where neutron tagging can discriminate inverse beta decay events from neutrino-oxygen charged-current events.
