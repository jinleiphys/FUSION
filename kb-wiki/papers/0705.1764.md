# Is the Pentaquark the Only Justification for Research on KN Physics ?

> The talk is intended to motivate the use of DA$Φ$NE--2 running at the $ϕ$ peak as an intense, clean source of low--momentum charged and neutral kaons. It covers a few open problems still unsolved after more than twenty--five years and the physics (some of it still novel) that could be learned only in this way. And, of course, the answer to the above question is {\sl NO}.

## Key claim
The pentaquark is not the only justification for continued research on kaon-nucleon (KN) physics; many long-standing open problems in low-energy KN interactions remain unsolved after more than twenty-five years, and the DAΦNE-2 facility running at the φ peak offers a unique opportunity to address them with far higher statistics than previously available.

## Method
The authors review the current status of low-energy KN scattering data, comparing the quality of KN and πN total cross sections and coupling-constant determinations. They examine the multichannel parametrization of the S = -1 sector (originally due to Dalitz and Tuan, and later extended by Kim) and identify inconsistencies when dispersion relations for πY scattering are applied to those amplitudes. They then propose a program of measurements at DAΦNE-2 using monochromatic charged and neutral kaons at momenta around 127 and 110 MeV/c, exploiting overdetermined isospin relations among elastic, charge-exchange, and regeneration amplitudes on hydrogen and helium targets.

## Key numbers
* DAΦNE-2 produces monochromatic charged kaons at about 127 MeV/c and neutral kaons at about 110 MeV/c, allowing exploration down to about 90 MeV/c via energy losses in the detector.
* The K⁺N S-wave scattering length for I=1 is about -0.3 fm; for I=0 it is very small, between -0.1 and 0.2 fm.
* At DAΦNE one could expect about 10⁷ two-body and 10⁵ three-body final-state events per year, orders of magnitude above the lowest-energy available data from thirty years ago.
* For radiative captures on the Λ(1405), one can expect 10⁴–10⁵ events per year.
* The uncertainty on the πNN coupling constant is a few percent; on KΛp it is 10%; on KΣN it is 30%; on πΛΣ and πΣΣ it is 100%.

## Context
This work extends the authors’ earlier proposals (Gensini and Violini, 1991–1998) for low-energy KN physics at a φ-factory and responds to renewed interest following the pentaquark claim by Nakano et al. (2003) and subsequent doubts by Battaglieri et al. (2006). It contrasts the poor quality of existing KN data with the well-studied πN sector and highlights that recent chiral SU(3) analyses by Oller et al. (2005) and Borasoy et al. (2006) still leave open questions about the Λ(1405) and the K⁻p scattering length. The paper argues that new DAΦNE-2 measurements could resolve these issues and provide a reliable experimental basis for testing theoretical models, complementing future programs at JPARC and other facilities.
