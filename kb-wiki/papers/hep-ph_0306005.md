# Non-renormalization Theorem Originating in a New Fixed Point of the Vector Manifestation

> We study the pion velocity at the critical temperature of chiral symmetry restoration in QCD. Starting from the premise that the bare effective field theory is to be defined from the underlying QCD, we incorporate the effects of Lorentz non-invariance into the bare theory by matching an effective field theory to QCD at a suitable matching scale, and investigate how the Lorentz non-invariance existing in the bare theory influences physical quantities. Using the hidden local symmetry model as the effective field theory, where the chiral symmetry restoration is realized as the vector manifestation (VM), we find that the pion velocity at the critical temperature receives neither quantum nor (thermal) hadronic corrections at the critical temperature even when we start from the bare theory with Lorentz symmetry breaking. This is likely the manifestation of a new fixed point in the Lorentz non-invariant formulation of the VM.

## Key claim

The pion velocity at the critical temperature of chiral symmetry restoration is exactly equal to the bare pion velocity, receiving neither quantum nor hadronic thermal corrections, even when the bare effective field theory includes Lorentz symmetry breaking effects from matching to QCD. This non-renormalization property holds independently of the specific value of the bare pion velocity.

## Method

The author starts from a Lorentz non-invariant hidden local symmetry (HLS) Lagrangian at bare level, where Lorentz breaking enters through intrinsic temperature dependence obtained by matching the effective field theory to QCD at a suitable matching scale. Using the vector manifestation (VM) framework, where chiral symmetry restoration is realized as a fixed point with parameters \((g_L, a^t, a^s) \to (0,1,1)\) as \(T \to T_c\), the quantum and hadronic thermal corrections to the pion velocity are computed at one-loop order. The analysis evaluates the two-point function of the axial-vector current, separating vacuum (quantum) and thermal contributions, and examines the renormalization group running of the pion decay constants and the parametric pion velocity.

## Key numbers

- The quantum correction to the temporal part of the axial-vector two-point function in the VM limit is \(\lim_{\rm VM} \Pi_\perp^{\rm{(vac)}t}(0) = -\frac{N_f}{4}\frac{1}{V_\pi}\frac{\Lambda^2}{8\pi^2}\), where \(\Lambda\) is the cutoff and \(N_f\) is the number of flavors.
- The quantum correction to the spatial part in the VM limit is \(\lim_{\rm VM} \Pi_\perp^{\rm{(vac)}s}(0) = -\frac{N_f}{4} V_\pi \frac{\Lambda^2}{8\pi^2}\).
- The hadronic thermal corrections at the critical temperature are \(\lim_{\rm VM} \bar{\Pi}_\perp^{t} = -\frac{N_f}{24}\frac{1}{V_\pi}T_c^2\) and \(\lim_{\rm VM} \bar{\Pi}_\perp^{s} = -\frac{N_f}{24} V_\pi T_c^2\).
- The renormalization group equation for the parametric pion velocity yields \(\mu \frac{dV_\pi^2}{d\mu} = 0\), showing that \(V_\pi\) does not scale with energy at the critical temperature.

## Context

This work extends the vector manifestation framework developed by Harada and Yamawaki (2001) and Harada, Kim, and Rho (2000) for chiral symmetry restoration in hot and dense matter, where the vector meson becomes massless and the chiral partner of the pion at the critical point. Previous analysis by Harada, Kim, Rho, and Sasaki (2002) showed that starting from a Lorentz-invariant bare theory, the pion velocity approaches the speed of light at \(T_c\). The present paper generalizes this to include Lorentz non-invariance in the bare theory from matching to QCD, demonstrating that the pion velocity at \(T_c\) equals the bare pion velocity regardless of its value, which constitutes a new fixed point in the Lorentz non-invariant formulation. This result enables determination of the physical pion velocity at the critical temperature once the bare pion velocity is fixed through Wilsonian matching to QCD, as pursued by Harada, Kim, Rho, and Sasaki (2003).
