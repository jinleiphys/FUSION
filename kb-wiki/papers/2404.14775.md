# HESS J1731-347 is likely a Quark Star Based on the Density-Dependent vMIT Bag Model

> In this study, we extend the MIT bag model by incorporating the vector interaction among quarks and introducing a density-dependent bag pressure.Then we proceed to investigate the thermodynamic properties of strange quark matter (SQM) and pure up-down quark matter (udQM) in quark stars (QSs).Our findings demonstrate that the density dependence of bag pressure $B(n_b)$ and the vector interaction $G_V$ among quarks can significantly stiffen the equation of state (EOS) for both SQM and udQM which allows for the description of massive compact stars such as those observed in GW190814 and PSR J0740+6620 as plausible candidates for QSs.Ultimately, we derived a series of mass-radius relations of QS based on several combinations of ($B_{as}$, $β$). Our results support the hypothesis that HESS J1731-347 is a quark star.

## Key claim

The authors claim that HESS J1731-347 is likely a quark star, but only for specific parameter combinations within the density-dependent vector MIT (vMIT) bag model; the mass-radius curve only passes through the HESS J1731-347 observational constraint when the parameter β exceeds a certain threshold (e.g., β > 0.7 for Bas = 30 MeV fm⁻³), meaning the hypothesis is conditional on the chosen model parameters.

## Method

The authors extended the MIT bag model by adding a vector interaction among quarks (vMIT model) and introducing a density-dependent bag pressure B(nb) following a Gaussian distribution with parameters Bas (asymptotic value) and β (rate of decrease). They solved the thermodynamic equations for strange quark matter (SQM) and pure up-down quark matter (udQM) under β-equilibrium and charge neutrality, then used the resulting equations of state as input to the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equations to compute mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities of quark stars.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of quark stars reaches approximately 2.90 M⊙ for GV = 0.3 fm², Bas = 10 MeV fm⁻³, and β = 0.60.
- The upper limit of Bas within the stability window is 58 MeV fm⁻³ for B0 = 257.3 MeV fm⁻³ and GV = 0.3 fm².
- The tidal deformability for a 1.4 M⊙ quark star (Λ1.4) ranges from 28.6 to 91.9 across different parameter combinations, all below 100.
- The surface density of quark stars ranges from 0.25 to 0.52 fm⁻³ depending on parameters.
- The central energy density at maximum mass ranges from 724.4 to 1062.9 MeV fm⁻³.

## Context

This work extends the vMIT bag model approach previously used by Lopes (2020) and others, which showed that constant bag pressure cannot satisfy massive pulsar constraints. The density-dependent bag pressure follows a Gaussian form used by Bordbar (2020) and Miyatsu (2015). The results support the hypothesis from Doro (2022) that HESS J1731-347 could be a quark star, and also suggest that massive compact objects like GW190814 and PSR J0740+6620 could be quark stars within this model. The work enables future studies to constrain the density dependence of the bag constant using multi-messenger observations.
