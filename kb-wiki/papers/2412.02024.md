# Regulated chiral gauge theory and the strong CP problem

> Four-dimensional chiral gauge theory can be formulated as the boundary theory on a five-dimensional manifold in a manner that may be realized on a finite lattice. There are interesting features of these theories which defy a purely four-dimensional conception of universality. We find that QCD when embedded in a chiral gauge theory (the Standard Model) and regulated this way appears to suffer neither from a $U(1)_A$ problem nor a strong $CP$ problem, with a central role played by fermion zeromodes localized far away in the fifth dimension. In this way it differs from conventional lattice QCD formulated as a stand-alone theory. Our analysis builds on recent work by others that highlights the role of global $U(1)$ symmetries in five dimensional formulations of four-dimensional chiral gauge theories, and the generic appearance of fermion zeromodes in the bulk.

## Key claim

QCD embedded in a chiral gauge theory and regulated via a five-dimensional boundary construction appears to suffer neither from a U(1)_A problem nor a strong CP problem, because fermion zeromodes localized far away in the fifth dimension enforce trivial topology on the vacuum state without affecting the hadron spectrum of the boundary theory.

## Method

The authors analyze a five-dimensional model with two Dirac fermions of opposite sign mass coupled to SU(3) gauge fields on a solid 4-torus, where the four-dimensional boundary supports a massless Dirac fermion. They integrate out the bulk fermions to obtain an effective theory with Chern-Simons terms, then match onto a chiral Lagrangian for the boundary theory after integration over boundary gauge fields, using a dilute instanton gas model to study the effects of nontrivial gauge topology. The bulk gauge fields are defined via a Yang-Mills flow from the boundary values, which they call "annealing flow," that maximally damps local topological features while preserving global topological constraints.

## Key numbers

- The Chern-Simons coefficient is given by κ = i/(24π²)
- The 't Hooft operator sum yields an effective η' mass term where ΛΣ/f² = M_η'², with Σ = ⟨q̄q⟩ being the quark condensate
- The five-dimensional manifold is parametrized with coordinates {x,y,z,θ,r} where 0 ≤ r ≤ R, with the four-dimensional boundary at r = R

## Context

This work extends the boundary chiral fermion (BCF) proposal of Kaplan (2024) for regulating chiral gauge theories on a finite lattice, and directly addresses an objection raised by Golterman and Shamir (2024) concerning exact global U(1) symmetries in the five-dimensional construction. The analysis builds on the anomaly inflow mechanism of Callan and Harvey (1984) and the 't Hooft (1976) solution to the U(1)_A problem, while also engaging with numerical observations of bulk zeromodes by Aoki et al. (2023, 2024). The paper enables a potential nonperturbative regulator for the Standard Model that resolves both the U(1)_A problem and the strong CP problem without requiring a massless up quark.
