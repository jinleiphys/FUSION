# The Search for the QGP: A Critical Appraisal

> Over the past 15 years, an extensive program of high energy nuclear collisions at BNL and CERN has been devoted to the experimental search for the quark-gluon plasma predicted by QCD. The start of RHIC this year will increase the highest available collision energy by a factor 10. This seems a good time for a critical assessment: what have we learned so far and what can we hope to learn in the coming years?

## Key claim

The paper argues that while many proposed QGP signatures have been observed in nuclear collisions, they also appear in p-p or p-A interactions where no QGP can form; the crucial test is therefore whether the observed behavior shows an unconventional, non-monotonic onset that can be interpreted as critical behavior, as seen in the two-step anomalous J/ψ suppression pattern in Pb-Pb collisions at the SPS.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental results from the BNL-AGS and CERN-SPS heavy-ion programs (1986-2000), focusing on J/ψ suppression, dilepton spectra, and strangeness production. For J/ψ suppression, the author advocates a three-step procedure: first study production in p-p collisions, then in the confined nuclear medium (p-A), and finally look for deviations in A-B collisions beyond pre-resonance absorption. The analysis uses the colour evaporation model for charmonium production and a Glauber analysis of p-A data to determine the pre-resonance absorption cross section, then compares Pb-Pb data to this baseline.

## Key numbers

- Initial energy density in SPS Pb-Pb collisions reaches 1-3 GeV/fm³, the range required for QGP formation according to lattice QCD (deconfinement temperature 170-180 MeV)
- Pre-resonance charmonium absorption cross section in nuclear matter: σ_{Cg-N} = 6.9 ± 1.1 mb from p-A data at 200 and 450 GeV
- Anomalous J/ψ suppression in Pb-Pb sets in suddenly at E_T ≈ 40 GeV, corresponding to N_w ≈ 100 participating nucleons
- A second drop in J/ψ production occurs at E_T ≈ 100 GeV, corresponding to N_w ≈ 300 participating nucleons
- The first drop occurs just above 2 GeV/fm³ energy density, the second around 3 GeV/fm³
- Hadron abundances in p-p collisions are described by an ideal resonance gas at T_h ≈ 170 MeV with strangeness suppression factor γ_s ≈ 0.5
- In Pb-Pb collisions, the same T_h ≈ 170 MeV applies but γ_s approaches unity

## Context

This critical appraisal reviews the first 15 years of experimental QGP search at BNL-AGS and CERN-SPS, extending the theoretical framework of Matsui and Satz (1986) on J/ψ suppression as a deconfinement probe. The paper argues that the NA50 collaboration's observation of a two-step anomalous J/ψ suppression pattern in Pb-Pb collisions matches predictions for sequential χ_c and J/ψ melting in a deconfined medium, in contrast to hadronic comover models. The work sets the stage for RHIC operations starting in 2000, which will increase collision energy by a factor of 10 and enable studies of jet quenching and thermal dileptons that were inaccessible at SPS energies.
