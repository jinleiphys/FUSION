# Anomalous internal conversion - the key to understanding the 209Bi riddle

> Theory of anomalous internal conversion is developed, and extended for the description of the hyperfine splitting. Experimental data on the hyperfine splitting in the H- and Li-like heavy ions of 209Bi are analyzed in terms of the Bohr-Weisskopf effect. Agreement with the theory is achieved, scheduling light on the structure of the magnetization distribution over the nuclear volume.

# Anomalous internal conversion, the key to understanding the 209Bi riddle

## Key claim
The specific-difference method for eliminating the Bohr-Weisskopf effect from hyperfine splitting is not truly nuclear-model independent; its precision is limited to the fourth decimal place, which is already below experimental precision. The method of anomalous nuclear moments, based on internal conversion theory, resolves the apparent discrepancy between theory and experiment for 209Bi hyperfine splitting by showing that the experimental value falls between the predictions of the surface-current and volume-current nuclear models.

## Method
The authors apply the method of anomalous nuclear moments, which expresses the hyperfine splitting as a series expansion of the diagonal internal conversion matrix element in the limit of zero transition energy, with the Bohr-Weisskopf effect determined by even moments of the magnetization distribution. They solve the coupled equations for the 1s and 2s hyperfine splittings using coefficients from Dirac wavefunctions calculated with the RAINE code, fitting to experimental data from Lochmann et al. (2014) and comparing results from the surface-current (SC) and volume-current (VC) nuclear models.

## Key numbers
- The parameter ζ₀ (nuclear-model-independent approximation) = 0.17549, identical for both SC and VC models.
- ζ₂ (including second terms with R₄ moments) = 0.175433 for the SC model and 0.175449 for the VC model, a difference of 9.1×10⁻⁵.
- The ζ value reported by Shabaev et al. (2015) and Lochmann et al. (2014) is 0.16886; adjusting to the VC model gives ζ = 0.16888, an increase of 2.6×10⁻⁴.
- Experimental specific difference Δ'E = −61.373 meV from Lochmann et al. (2014); theoretical values range from −61.320 meV (SC/Weisskopf model) to −61.461 meV (VC model).
- Experimental hyperfine splittings: w_exp(1s) = 5.0863 eV, w_exp(2s) = 0.7975 eV.
- Coefficients c₁: −4.4123×10⁻⁹ fm⁻² (1s), −7.7431×10⁻¹⁰ fm⁻² (2s); c₃: 3.7255×10⁻¹¹ fm⁻⁴ (1s), 6.5494×10⁻¹² fm⁻⁴ (2s).

## Context
This work extends the anomalous nuclear moments method developed by Karpeshin and Trzhaskovskaya (2017) to analyze the hyperfine splitting data of Lochmann et al. (2014) for H-like and Li-like 209Bi ions. It directly addresses the discrepancy noted by Lochmann et al. (2014) and Shabaev et al. (2015) between the specific-difference method predictions and experiment, which was predicted by Karpeshin and Trzhaskovskaya (2017). The method enables model-independent extraction of nuclear magnetization distribution moments from hyperfine splitting measurements, offering a more stringent test of QED than the specific-difference method and opening the way to direct nuclear structure studies at storage-ring facilities such as Lanzhou and GSI Darmstadt.
