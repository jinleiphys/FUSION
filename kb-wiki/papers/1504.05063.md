# Electromagnetic Structure of Few-Nucleon Ground States

> Experimental form factors of the hydrogen and helium isotopes, extracted from an up-to-date global analysis of cross sections and polarization observables measured in elastic electron scattering from these systems, are compared to predictions obtained in three different theoretical approaches: the first is based on realistic interactions and currents, including relativistic corrections (labeled as the conventional approach); the second relies on a chiral effective field theory description of the strong and electromagnetic interactions in nuclei (labeled $χ$EFT); the third utilizes a fully relativistic treatment of nuclear dynamics as implemented in the covariant spectator theory (labeled CST). For momentum transfers below $Q \lesssim 5$ fm$^{-1}$ there is satisfactory agreement between experimental data and theoretical results in all three approaches. However, at $Q \gtrsim 5$ fm$^{-1}$, particularly in the case of the deuteron, a relativistic treatment of the dynamics, as is done in the CST, is necessary. The experimental data on the deuteron $A$ structure function extend to $Q \simeq 12$ fm$^{-1}$, and the close agreement between these data and the CST results suggests that, even in this extreme kinematical regime, there is no evidence for new effects coming from quark and gluon degrees of freedom at short distances.

# Electromagnetic Structure of Few-Nucleon Ground States

## Key claim

The experimental form factors of hydrogen and helium isotopes extracted from elastic electron scattering agree with three different theoretical approaches (conventional, chiral effective field theory, and covariant spectator theory) for momentum transfers below Q ≲ 5 fm⁻¹, but at Q ≳ 5 fm⁻¹ a relativistic treatment as implemented in the covariant spectator theory becomes necessary, and the close agreement between deuteron A structure function data and CST results up to Q ≃ 12 fm⁻¹ suggests no evidence for quark and gluon degrees of freedom at short distances.

## Method

The authors compared experimental form factors of hydrogen and helium isotopes, extracted from a global analysis of cross sections and polarization observables in elastic electron scattering, to predictions from three theoretical approaches: the conventional approach using realistic interactions and currents with relativistic corrections; chiral effective field theory (χEFT) describing strong and electromagnetic interactions in nuclei; and the covariant spectator theory (CST) providing a fully relativistic treatment of nuclear dynamics. The χEFT electromagnetic charge and current operators were derived up to one-loop level using time-ordered perturbation theory, with low-energy constants determined by fitting two-nucleon scattering data, deuteron and trinucleon magnetic moments, and np radiative capture cross sections.

## Context

This review extends the work of Weinberg (1990s) on chiral effective field theory for nuclear interactions and the subsequent derivations of two- and three-nucleon potentials by Entem and Machleidt (2003), Epelbaum (2009), and others, as well as electromagnetic current derivations by Park et al. (1996), Pastore et al. (2009, 2011), Piarulli et al. (2013), and Koelling et al. (2009, 2011). The paper enables systematic comparison of three fundamentally different theoretical frameworks for describing few-nucleon electromagnetic structure, and identifies the momentum transfer regime where relativistic effects become essential. The results suggest that future work should extend CST calculations beyond A=3 systems and develop consistent higher-order χEFT electromagnetic operators.
