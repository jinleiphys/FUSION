# Generalized Seniority on Deformed Single-Particle Basis

> Recently we proposed [62] a fast computing scheme for generalized seniority on spherical single-particle basis. This work redesigns the scheme to make it applicable to deformed single-particle basis. The algorithm is applied to the rare-earth nucleus $^{158}_{~64}$Gd$_{94}$ for intrinsic (body-fixed frame) neutron excitations under the low-momentum {\emph{NN}} interaction $V_{\rm{low}-k}$. By allowing as many as four broken pairs, we compute the lowest $300$ intrinsic states of several multipolarity. These states converge well to the exact ones, showing generalized seniority is very effective in truncating the deformed shell model. Under realistic interactions, the picture remains approximately valid that the ground state is a coherent pair condensate, and the pairs gradually break up as excitation energy increases.

## Key claim

The paper claims that generalized seniority, previously developed for spherical single-particle bases, can be effectively applied to deformed single-particle bases for truncating the deformed shell model, with the ground state remaining approximately a coherent pair condensate and pairs gradually breaking up as excitation energy increases, though the authors note this is demonstrated only for a semi-realistic example where proton-neutron interaction generating deformation is neglected.

## Method

The author redesigns a previously proposed fast computing scheme for generalized seniority on spherical single-particle basis to work on deformed single-particle basis, using the many-pair density matrix expressed through Pauli-blocked normalizations rather than through rotational-symmetry-based occupation number representations. The algorithm is applied to the rare-earth nucleus 158Gd94 for intrinsic neutron excitations under the low-momentum NN interaction Vlow-k derived from the N3LO potential, using Nilsson model single-particle levels with quadrupole deformation β = 0.349. The Hamiltonian is diagonalized in the Lanczos method for states up to four broken pairs (generalized seniority S = 8) in valence spaces of dimension 34 and 46.

## Key numbers

- Quadrupole deformation parameter β = 0.349 for 158Gd, taken from experimental data
- Momentum cutoff for Vlow-k interaction: 2.1 fm^-1
- Nilsson model parameters: κ = 0.0637, μ = 0.60, with ħω0 = 41 A^(-1/3) MeV where A = 158
- Lowest 300 intrinsic states computed for K = 0, 2, 3, 6, 10 and both parities
- Two valence spaces used: dimension 34 (18 levels below and 16 above Fermi surface) with truncation up to S = 8; dimension 46 (22 below and 24 above) with truncation up to S = 6

## Context

This work extends the generalized seniority truncation scheme previously developed for spherical shell model calculations on semi-magic nuclei (Jia 2015, Jia 2016, Qi 2016) to deformed single-particle bases, addressing the computational difficulty that rotational symmetry reduction prevents using occupation number representation simplifications. The approach contrasts with methods that break particle number such as deformed QRPA (Peru 2011, Terasaki 2011) or HFB plus generator coordinate method (Delaroche 2010), and with the projected shell model (Hara 1995, Wang 2016) which breaks particle number but respects rotational symmetry. The work enables future large-scale deformed shell model calculations with modern realistic interactions, though the authors note the current example is semi-realistic as it neglects proton-neutron residual interactions and does not remove the mean-field contribution from the two-body interaction.
