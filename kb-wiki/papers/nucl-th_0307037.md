# Effects of nuclear fragmentation on single particle and collective motions at low energy

> The Faddeev technique is employed to study the influence of both particle-particle and particle-hole phonons on the one-hole spectral function of 16O. The formalism includes the effects of nuclear fragmentation and accounts for collective excitations at a random phase approximation (RPA) level. The latter are subsequently summed to all orders by the Faddeev equations to obtain the nucleon self-energy. The results indicate that the characteristics of hole fragmentation are related to the low-lying states of 16O and an improvement of the description of this spectrum is required to understand the details of the experimental strength distribution. A first calculation to improve on this includes the mixing of two-phonon configuration with particle-hole ones. It is seen that this allows to understand the formation of excited states which cannot be described by the RPA approach.

## Key claim

The Faddeev technique, combined with self-consistent Green's function theory, can treat the coupling of particle-hole and particle-particle collective modes within an RPA framework while including fragmentation effects, but a proper description of the experimental spectral strength for 16O requires improving the description of the low-lying excited states beyond the RPA approach.

## Method

The authors employed the Faddeev technique within the self-consistent Green's function formalism to compute the nucleon self-energy for 16O, coupling both particle-hole and particle-particle (hole-hole) collective excitations to the single-particle motion. The collective excitations were first evaluated at the dressed TDA/RPA level, then summed to all orders by solving Faddeev equations for the 2p1h/2h1p propagators. The Dyson equation was solved iteratively within a harmonic oscillator model space (first four major shells plus 1g9/2, oscillator parameter b=1.76 fm) using a Brueckner G-matrix derived from the Bonn-C potential.

## Key numbers

- Spectroscopic factors for proton knockout from the p-shell in 16O: TDA gives 0.775 for p1/2 and 0.766 for p3/2; RPA reduces these to 0.745 and 0.725 respectively
- After four iterations including fragmentation effects (DRPA), the p1/2 spectroscopic factor becomes 0.776 and the p3/2 becomes 0.724, with a secondary p3/2 fragment appearing at -26.3 MeV with spectroscopic factor 2.6%
- The total number of nucleons deduced from the fully self-consistent spectral function is about 10% lower than A=16 (14.63 after 4th iteration)
- The lowest isoscalar 0+ state in DRPA appears at approximately 17 MeV; two-phonon ERPA yields a predominantly two-phonon 0+ state at 11.28 MeV and a predominantly ph state at 17.21 MeV
- The 3- and 1- states in DRPA appear at 9.50 MeV and 11.19 MeV respectively; two-phonon ERPA shifts them to 9.23 MeV and 10.90 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations for 16O by Geurts et al. and Muther et al. that showed long-range correlations from coupling single-particle motion to low-energy collective excitations were needed to explain the discrepancy between experimental spectroscopic factors from (e,e'p) reactions and theory. The Faddeev approach allows for the first time treatment of both ph and pp(hh) collective modes within an RPA framework while including fragmentation through dressing of the single-particle propagator. The results identify that improving the description of the 16O excitation spectrum beyond RPA is necessary, and the two-phonon extended RPA approach partially addresses this by explaining the formation of states (such as the 0+, 2+, 4+ triplet near 12 MeV) that RPA cannot describe, though future work must include up to four-phonon configurations and pp/hh channel interactions as suggested by Iachello.
