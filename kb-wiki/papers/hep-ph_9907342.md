# Duality and Chiral Restoration from Low-Mass Dileptons at the CERN-SpS

> We review recent theoretical progress in low-mass dilepton production at CERN-SpS energies. Various hadronic approaches to calculate the vector correlator in hot/dense hadronic matter are discussed and confronted with each other. Possible consequences for the nature of chiral restoration are indicated.

## Key claim

The paper argues that medium modifications of the vector meson spectral function, particularly a strong broadening of the rho meson driven by baryonic interactions, can lower the "duality threshold" where hadronic and partonic descriptions of the dilepton production rate coincide, potentially down to about 0.5 GeV, but notes that a conclusive discrimination of the in-medium contribution requires improved experimental mass resolution and statistics.

## Method

The paper reviews and compares several hadronic approaches for calculating the vector current correlator in hot and dense matter: low-temperature chiral mixing theorems (Dey et al.), the chiral reduction/master formula framework (Steele, Yamagishi, Zahed), effective chiral Lagrangian models (gauged linear sigma model, Hidden Local Symmetry), and many-body calculations incorporating rho-meson selfenergies from mesonic and baryonic scattering processes. These theoretical rates are then convolved with space-time fireball evolution models (both transport and hydrodynamic simulations) to compute dilepton spectra for comparison with CERN-SpS data from the CERES and NA50 experiments.

## Key numbers

* The low-temperature mixing parameter epsilon = T^2/(6 f_pi^2) gives a chiral restoration temperature T_c^chi = 160 MeV when extrapolated to epsilon = 1/2.
* In a meson gas at T = 150 MeV, the in-medium rho broadening amounts to about 80 MeV with insignificant mass shift.
* At normal nuclear matter density (rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}), the rho meson broadening is 200-300 MeV, a factor of 2-3 larger than at comparable densities in a pure meson gas.
* The duality threshold in vacuum is at M_dual about 1.5 GeV; in hot baryon-free matter at T = 160 MeV it reduces to about 1 GeV; in hot and dense matter it may reduce further to about 0.5 GeV.
* Thermal fireball calculations using entropy and baryon number conservation yield pion chemical potentials reaching almost 80 MeV.
* The NA50 data show a factor of about 3 enhancement for M about 1.5-2.5 GeV that can be accounted for without anomalous open charm enhancement.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on in-medium vector meson modifications initiated by the Brown-Rho scaling conjecture (1991) and the QCD sum rule analysis of Hatsuda and Lee (1992). It confronts multiple theoretical approaches with each other and with CERN-SpS dilepton data from the CERES and NA50 collaborations. The paper enables future work on discriminating between the broadening scenario and the dropping-mass scenario through upcoming improved CERES measurements and the 40 GeV run at the SpS, which will help assess high baryon-density effects.
