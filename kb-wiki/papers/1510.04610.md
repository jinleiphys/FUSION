# Theoretical studies of possible toroidal high-spin isomers in the light-mass region

> We review our theoretical knowledge of possible toroidal high-spin isomers in the light mass region in 28$\le$$A$$\le$52 obtained previously in cranked Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations. We report additional toroidal high-spin isomers in $^{56}$Ni with $I$=114$\hbar$ and 140$\hbar$, which follow the same (multi-particle)--(multi-hole) systematics as other toroidal high-spin isomers. We examine the production of these exotic nuclei by fusion of various projectiles on $^{20}$Ne or $^{28}$Si as an active target in time-projection-chamber (TCP) experiments.

# Theoretical studies of possible toroidal high-spin isomers in the light-mass region

## Key claim

Toroidal high-spin isomers exist as equilibrium configurations in light nuclei with mass numbers 28 ≤ A ≤ 52, and the systematic n-particle n-hole pattern predicts additional isomers in ⁵⁶Ni at I = 114ħ and 140ħ, provided these states have mean half-lives longer than ħ/MeV or approximately 200 fm/c to be observable as resonances or metastable nuclei.

## Method

The authors performed cranked Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) calculations to locate energy minima corresponding to toroidal high-spin isomeric states in even-even N=Z and N≠Z nuclei. They classified the isomers according to n-particle n-hole configurations relative to the toroidal nucleus at I=0 and examined the systematics of total energy as a function of the torus aspect ratio R/d. For production studies, they calculated resonance energies in the laboratory system for fusion reactions of various projectiles on ²⁰Ne or ²⁸Si as active targets in time-projection-chamber (TPC) experiments.

## Key numbers

- ⁵⁶Ni toroidal high-spin isomers found at I = 114ħ and I = 140ħ
- For ⁵⁶Ni at I = 114ħ, the torus aspect ratio R/d = 6.15
- For ⁵⁶Ni at I = 140ħ, the torus aspect ratio R/d = 6.27
- 21 toroidal high-spin isomers previously identified in the mass range 28 ≤ A ≤ 52 up to ⁵²Fe
- Estimated minimum observable half-life threshold: ħ/MeV or approximately 200 fm/c
- Mass range studied: 28 ≤ A ≤ 52 for even-even N=Z and N≠Z nuclei

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous cranked Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations (Staszczak 2014) that first demonstrated toroidal high-spin isomers in the A=28-52 region, and is consistent with similar Hartree-Fock calculations by Ichikawa (2012). The paper builds on the rotating liquid-drop model of Wong (1973) and Wheeler's original suggestion (Gamow 1961) that nuclear fluid may assume toroidal shapes. The production scheme using active-target TPC experiments follows techniques previously applied with helium targets (Ahn 2012, Rogachevskiy 2011), and the technology for a TPC using 90% ²⁰Ne has been developed by the ALICE collaboration (Ketzer 2013). The results provide specific resonance energy predictions to guide experimental searches for these exotic nuclear shapes.
