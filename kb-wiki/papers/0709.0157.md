# The effect of partonic wind on charm quark correlations in high-energy nuclear collisions

> In high-energy collisions, massive heavy quarks are produced back-to-back initially and they are sensitive to early dynamical conditions. The strong collective partonic wind from the fast expanding quark-gluon plasma created in high-energy nuclear collisions modifies the correlation pattern significantly. As a result, the angular correlation function for D$\bar{\rm D}$ pairs is suppressed at the angle $Δϕ=π$. While the hot and dense medium in collisions at RHIC ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV) can only smear the initial back-to-back D$\bar {\rm D}$ correlation, a clear and strong near side D$\bar{\rm D}$ correlation is expected at LHC ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5500$ GeV).

## Key claim

The strong collective partonic wind from the expanding quark-gluon plasma can turn the initial back-to-back D\(\overline{\rm D}\) correlation into a near-side correlation, but only if the drag coefficient in the Langevin equation is sufficiently large (at least \(4 \times 10^{-6}\) (fm/\(c\))\(^{-1}\)MeV\(^{-2}\)); at RHIC the correlation is merely washed out, while at LHC a clear near-side peak emerges.

## Method

The authors used PYTHIA (v. 6.327) to generate charm quark pairs in p+p collisions at both leading order (MSEL=4) and next-to-leading order (MSEL=1). The propagation of charm quarks through the quark-gluon plasma was modeled with a non-relativistic Langevin equation, with a drag coefficient \(\gamma(T) = a T^2\) and a momentum-space diffusion coefficient \(\alpha\) determined by the fluctuation-dissipation relation. The expanding medium was described by 2+1 dimensional Bjorken hydrodynamics with an ideal quark-gluon plasma equation of state and a first-order phase transition at \(T_c = 165\) MeV; the Langevin equation was solved in the local rest frame of the fluid and then transformed to the laboratory frame to capture the partonic wind effect.

## Key numbers

* At RHIC (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV), the maximum initial temperature \(T_0 = 340\) MeV at thermalization time \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm/\(c\); at LHC (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5500\) GeV), \(T_0 = 610\) MeV at \(\tau_0 = 0.3\) fm/\(c\).
* The critical charm quark momentum for the partonic wind to turn the c\(\overline{\rm c}\) correlation in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC is of order 1 GeV/\(c\), assuming a radial flow velocity \(v_f \simeq 0.6c\).
* At RHIC, with drag parameter \(a \sim 6 \times 10^{-6}\) (fm/\(c\))\(^{-1}\)MeV\(^{-2}\), the initial back-to-back D\(\overline{\rm D}\) correlation is completely washed out.
* At LHC, for \(a \sim 4 \times 10^{-6}\) (fm/\(c\))\(^{-1}\)MeV\(^{-2}\), a strong near-side D\(\overline{\rm D}\) correlation appears.
* The total number of charm pairs at mid-rapidity (\(|y| \le 1\)) is 2-4 at RHIC and 6-10 at LHC.
* The charm quark mass was taken as \(m_c = 1.5\) GeV/\(c^2\).

## Context

This work extends earlier leading-order pQCD studies of D\(\overline{\rm D}\) correlations (Zhu et al., 2005) by including next-to-leading order effects via PYTHIA and by coupling the Langevin dynamics to a hydrodynamic medium. It builds on experimental evidence for strong collective flow at RHIC (STAR Collaboration, 2004-2005) and on Langevin-based analyses of heavy quark energy loss and elliptic flow (Moore and Teaney, 2005; van Hees et al., 2006). The prediction of a near-side D\(\overline{\rm D}\) correlation at LHC provides a new observable for testing whether charm quarks thermalize rapidly in the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma, complementing existing measurements of non-photonic electron \(R_{AA}\) and \(v_2\) at RHIC (PHENIX Collaboration, 2006).
