# Quark cores in extensions of the MIT Bag model

> Recent observations of massive pulsars having masses of the order of two solar mass pose a new challenge for compact objects such as hybrid stars and neutron stars. Extensions of the bag model and the Nambu-Jona-Lasino model have been used to model these stars to get higher mass stars. Quark matter has been predicted in the cores of these massive stars. In this work we show that an extension of the bag model, with a chemical potential dependent bag parameter can lead to an isentropic phase transition in the core of the neutron star. Our model shows that an EoS having all three quarks $u$, $d$ and $s$ would lead to massive stars with stable quark matter. We find that the mass of the stars not only depends on the bag constant but also on the mass of the strange quark. The mass - radius ratio which determines the redshift values on the surface, indicates that it is possible to obtain stable self bound strange quark matter stars with reasonable values of the bag pressure which correspond to the recent observations of large mass stars.

# Quark cores in extensions of the MIT Bag model

## Key claim

An extension of the MIT bag model with a chemical-potential- and temperature-dependent bag parameter can produce an isentropic phase transition in neutron star cores, yielding stable strange quark matter stars with masses up to approximately 2.2 solar masses, but only if the strange quark mass is taken as massless or below about 75 MeV; for strange quark masses at or above 90 MeV, the model does not produce stars exceeding 2 solar masses.

## Method

The authors adopt the grand canonical partition function approach of Leonidov et al., making the bag constant B a function of chemical potential μ and temperature T. They construct equations of state (EoS) for three cases: two massless flavors (u,d), three massless flavors (u,d,s), and three flavors with a massive strange quark. For each EoS, they enforce charge neutrality and beta equilibrium, then solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations numerically to obtain mass-radius relations. Stability is checked by requiring the energy per baryon of the quark plasma to be lower than that of ⁵⁶Fe and of nuclear matter (940 MeV).

## Key numbers

- For three massless flavors, the model yields maximum masses near **2.2 M₀** with a radius of **14 km**, occurring for bag constants in the range **130–140 MeV**.
- For the massive strange quark case with m_s = **100 MeV** and μ_u = **100 MeV**, the maximum star mass is below 2 M₀; with m_s = **150 MeV**, masses are further reduced.
- The chemical potential for the two-flavor massless case is constrained between **313.5 MeV** and **331 MeV**, with the bag constant between **149 MeV** and **154 MeV**; the baryon density is **0.32 fm⁻³**.
- Surface redshift values reach **0.4** for stars of 2 M₀ or higher in the massless three-flavor case, matching the observed redshift of PSR J0348+0432.
- For massive strange quark stars with m_s = 100 MeV, typical surface redshifts are around **0.22** for stars of about **1.5 M₀**.
- The lower limit on the strange quark mass is taken as **90 MeV** from other experimental constraints; lowering it to **75 MeV** would allow masses above 2 M₀.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Leonidov et al., who introduced a chemical-potential- and temperature-dependent bag constant for two massless flavors, to include the strange quark and to study compact star observables. It builds on prior studies of hybrid stars and strange stars using density-dependent bag models (Ritam Malik; N. Prasad) and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. The results are compared to observed massive pulsars PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348+0432, as well as candidate strange stars XTE J1739-285 and EXO 0748-676. The work enables future studies of rotational effects on these stars and further constraints on the strange quark mass from astrophysical observations.
