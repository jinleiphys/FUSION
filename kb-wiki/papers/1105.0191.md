# Jet quenching from RHIC to LHC

> We perform a joint analysis of the data from PHENIX at RHIC and ALICE at LHC on the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$. The computations are performed within the light-cone path integral approach to induced gluon emission. Our results show that slow variation of $R_{AA}$ from RHIC to LHC energies indicates that the QCD coupling constant is suppressed in the quark-gluon plasma produced at LHC.

## Key claim

The slow variation of the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) from RHIC to LHC energies indicates that the QCD coupling constant is suppressed in the hotter quark-gluon plasma produced at LHC, based on a joint analysis of PHENIX and ALICE data.

## Method

The analysis uses the light-cone path integral (LCPI) approach to compute induced gluon emission, with multiple gluon emission accounted for via Landau's method. The nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) is evaluated for central \(Au+Au\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV and \(Pb+Pb\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) TeV, using a Bjorken model for the QGP evolution with initial temperature \(T_0\) derived from entropy/multiplicity ratios. The running coupling \(\alpha_s\) is frozen at low momenta at several trial values (0.7, 0.6, 0.5, 0.4) to test for in-medium suppression, with collisional energy loss included by renormalizing the QGP temperature.

## Key numbers

- For central \(Au+Au\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV, the initial QGP temperature \(T_0 \approx 300\) MeV.
- For central \(Pb+Pb\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) TeV, the initial QGP temperature \(T_0 \approx 400\) MeV.
- The RHIC data for \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV can be described with frozen coupling \(\alpha_s^{fr} \approx 0.6\div 0.7\).
- The LHC data for \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) TeV agree better with frozen coupling \(\alpha_s^{fr} \approx 0.4\div 0.5\).
- Collisional energy loss suppresses \(R_{AA}\) by about 15-25%.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Zakharov (2008) on the nuclear modification factor to a joint comparison of RHIC and LHC data, using the LCPI approach for induced gluon emission. The results contradict the expectation that a simple scaling of the coupling from vacuum values would describe both datasets, and instead support the hypothesis of thermal suppression of \(\alpha_s\) in the hotter QGP at LHC. This enables future studies of the temperature dependence of the QCD coupling in heavy-ion collisions and motivates more precise measurements of \(R_{AA}\) across a range of collision energies.
