# Double-heavy tetraquark states with heavy diquark-antiquark symmetry

> We calculate the masses of the $QQ\bar{q}\bar{q}$ ($Q=c,b$; $q=u,d,s$) tetraquark states with the aid of heavy diquark-antiquark symmetry (HDAS) and the chromomagnetic interaction (CMI) model. The masses of the highest-spin ($J=2$) tetraquarks that have only the $(QQ)_{\bar{3}_c}(\bar{q}\bar{q})_{3_c}$ color structure are related with those of conventional hadrons using HDAS. Thereafter, the masses of their partner states are determined with the mass splittings in the CMI model. Our numerical results reveal that: (i) the lightest $cc\bar{n}\bar{n}$ ($n=u,d$) is an $I(J^P)=0(1^+)$ state around 3929 MeV (53 MeV above the $DD^*$ threshold) and none of the double-charm tetraquarks are stable; (ii) the stable double-bottom tetraquarks are the lowest $0(1^+)$ $bb\bar{n}\bar{n}$ around 10488 MeV ($\approx116$ MeV below the $BB^*$ threshold) and the lowest $1/2(1^+)$ $bb\bar{n}\bar{s}$ around 10671 MeV ($\approx20$ MeV below the $BB_s^*/B_sB^*$ threshold); and (iii) the two lowest $bc\bar{n}\bar{n}$ tetraquarks, namely the lowest $0(0^+)$ around 7167 MeV and the lowest $0(1^+)$ around 7223 MeV, are near-threshold states. Moreover, we discuss the constraints on the masses of double-heavy hadrons. Specifically, for the lowest nonstrange tetraquarks, we obtain $T_{cc}<3965$ MeV, $T_{bb}<10627$ MeV, and $T_{bc}<7199$ MeV.

## Key claim

The masses of double-heavy tetraquark states are estimated by relating the highest-spin (J=2) tetraquarks to known baryons via heavy diquark-antiquark symmetry, then using the chromomagnetic interaction model for the lower-spin partner states; the resulting masses fall between the theoretical lower and upper limits from previous methods, but the authors note that the accuracy of the heavy diquark-antiquark symmetry approximation "should be tested in future experiments."

## Method

The authors first use heavy diquark-antiquark symmetry to relate the mass of the J=2 tetraquark with color structure (QQ)₃̅c(q̄q̄)₃c to the mass of the doubly heavy baryon Ξcc* (estimated from the LHCb Ξcc mass of 3621 MeV using the CMI model), via relations such as T_ccn̄n̄^(J=2) - Ξcc* = Σb* - B̄*. They then determine the masses of lower-spin tetraquark states by calculating the mass splittings from the chromomagnetic interaction Hamiltonian, using coupling parameters extracted from conventional hadrons and including mixing between the (QQ)₃̅c(q̄q̄)₃c and (QQ)₆c(q̄q̄)₆̅c color configurations.

## Key numbers

- The lightest cc̄n̄n̄ (n=u,d) tetraquark is an I(J^P)=0(1^+) state at 3929 MeV, which is 53 MeV above the DD* threshold
- The stable double-bottom tetraquark bb̄n̄n̄ with I(J^P)=0(1^+) is at 10488 MeV, approximately 116 MeV below the BB* threshold
- The stable double-bottom tetraquark bb̄n̄s̄ with J^P=1^+ is at 10671 MeV, approximately 20 MeV below the BBs*/BsB* threshold
- The two lowest bc̄n̄n̄ tetraquarks are the 0(0^+) state at 7167 MeV and the 0(1^+) state at 7223 MeV, both near-threshold states
- Upper limits for the lowest nonstrange tetraquarks: T_cc < 3965 MeV, T_bb < 10627 MeV, T_bc < 7199 MeV
- The J=2 cc̄n̄n̄ tetraquark mass is 4194 MeV (using Σb* and B̄*)
- The J=2 cc̄n̄s̄ tetraquark mass is 4313 MeV (using Ξb* and B̄*)
- The J=2 cc̄s̄s̄ tetraquark mass is 4430 MeV (using Ωc* and Ds*)

## Context

This work extends the study of double-heavy tetraquarks by combining heavy diquark-antiquark symmetry with the chromomagnetic interaction model, building on the systematic CMI model study of Luo et al. (2017) which used meson-meson thresholds as reference scales and likely underestimated masses. The approach contrasts with the authors' own previous works (Wu et al., 2016; Chen et al., 2016; Luo et al., 2017) where multiquark masses were determined from lower mass scales. The results enable experimental searches for these tetraquark states, particularly the predicted stable double-bottom tetraquarks, and provide a framework for testing heavy diquark-antiquark symmetry in future measurements.
