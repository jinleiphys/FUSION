# Improved Convergence in the Three-Nucleon System at Very Low Energies

> Neutron-deuteron scattering in the context of ``pion-less'' Effective Field Theory at very low energies is investigated to next-to-next-to-leading order. Convergence is improved by fitting the two-nucleon contact interactions to the tail of the deuteron wave-function, a procedure known as Z-parameterisation and extended here to the three-nucleon system. The improvement is particularly striking in the doublet-S wave (triton) channel, where better agreement to potential-model calculations and better convergence from order to order in the power counting is achieved for momenta as high as \sim 120 MeV. Investigating the cut-off dependence of the phase-shifts, one confirms numerically the analytical finding that the first momentum-dependent three-body force enters at N2LO. The other partial waves converge also substantially faster. Effective-range parameters of the nd-system are determined, e.g. for the quartet-S-wave scattering length a_q=[6.35\pm0.02] fm, which compares favourably both in magnitude and uncertainty with recent high-precision potential-model determinations. Differential cross-sections up to E_{lab}\approx 15 MeV agree with data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that using Z-parameterisation (fitting two-nucleon contact interactions to the tail of the deuteron wavefunction) substantially improves the convergence of pion-less Effective Field Theory calculations for neutron-deuteron scattering, particularly in the doublet-S wave (triton) channel, achieving better agreement with potential-model calculations for momenta up to approximately 120 MeV.

## Method

The author extends the Z-parameterisation procedure, previously applied to two-nucleon systems, to the three-nucleon system within the framework of pion-less Effective Field Theory (EFT(π/)). The two-nucleon contact interactions are fitted to reproduce the correct deuteron pole position and asymptotic normalisation, effectively summing up dominant effective-range contributions. Neutron-deuteron scattering is then computed to next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO) using Faddeev equations with an auxiliary-field formalism, and the cutoff dependence of phase shifts is analysed to verify the power counting of three-body forces.

## Key numbers

- Quartet-S-wave scattering length: a_q = [6.35 ± 0.02] fm, comparing favourably with high-precision potential-model determinations
- Deuteron binding momentum: γ_t = 45.7025 MeV
- Effective range in triplet channel: ρ_{0t} = 1.764 fm
- Shape parameter: ρ_{1t} = 0.389 fm^3
- γ_t ρ_{0t} ≈ 0.41, indicating the effective range is numerically larger than naively expected
- Z_t = 1/(1 - γ_t ρ_{0t}) = 1.690(3), the deuteron wavefunction normalisation constant
- Improved convergence achieved for momenta as high as ∼ 120 MeV in the doublet-S wave channel
- Differential cross-sections agree with data up to E_lab ≈ 15 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Z-parameterisation approach of Phillips et al. (1999) from the two-nucleon to the three-nucleon system within pion-less Effective Field Theory. It builds upon the foundational work of Bedaque et al. on three-body forces in EFT(π/) and the rigorous power-counting developed for three-nucleon forces. The results confirm numerically the analytical finding that the first momentum-dependent three-body force enters at N2LO, supporting the power counting scheme, and enable model-independent extractions of neutron properties and reactions relevant for nuclear astrophysics at very low energies.
