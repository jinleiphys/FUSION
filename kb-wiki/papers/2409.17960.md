# Relativistic diffusion model for hadron production in p-Pb collisions at the LHC

> We investigate charged-hadron production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions of asymmetric systems within a nonequilibrium-statistical framework. Calculated centrality-dependent pseudorapidity distributions for p-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 and 8.16 TeV are compared with data from the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). Our approach combines a relativistic diffusion model with formulations based on quantum chromodynamics while utilizing numerical solutions of a Fokker-Planck equation to account for the shift and broadening of the fragmentation sources for particle-production with respect to the stopping (net-baryon) rapidity distributions. To represent the centrality dependence of charged-hadron production in asymmetric systems over a broad region of pseudorapidities, the consideration and precise modelling of the fragmentation sources - along with the central gluon-gluon source - is found to be essential. Specifically, this results in an inversion of the particle-production amplitude from backward- to forward-dominance when transitioning from central to peripheral collisions, in agreement with recent ATLAS and ALICE p-Pb data at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV.

## Key claim
The paper claims that a three-source relativistic diffusion model (RDM) with color-glass condensate (CGC) initial conditions and a Fokker-Planck time evolution can reproduce the centrality-dependent pseudorapidity distributions of charged hadrons in p-Pb collisions at LHC energies, but only if the fragmentation sources are modeled precisely and the gluon saturation scale \(Q_0^2\) is treated as a free, impact-parameter-dependent parameter rather than following the naive \(A^{1/3}\) scaling.

## Method
The authors combine a relativistic diffusion model with CGC-based initial conditions: a central gluon-gluon source from \(k_T\)-factorization and two fragmentation sources from hybrid factorization (valence quark–soft gluon interactions). The time evolution of the fragmentation sources toward thermal equilibrium is computed via numerical solutions of a one-dimensional Fokker-Planck equation with constant diffusion and linear drift, using the CGC distributions as initial states. The centrality dependence is incorporated by allowing the gluon saturation scale parameter \(Q_0\) to vary with impact parameter, and the model results are compared to ATLAS and ALICE data after a Jacobian transformation from rapidity to pseudorapidity.

## Context
This work extends earlier phenomenological three-source diffusion models for asymmetric systems (Wolschin and collaborators, 2006; Schulz and Wolschin, 2015, 2018) by replacing simplified delta-function or Gaussian initial conditions with QCD-inspired CGC initial states, requiring numerical solutions of the Fokker-Planck equation. It also builds on the authors' recent treatment of symmetric Pb-Pb collisions (Höck, Wolschin, 2024) and on prior CGC-based stopping calculations (Mehtar-Tani, Wolschin, 2009; Höck, Wolschin, 2020, 2023). The model explains the inversion from backward- to forward-dominance in peripheral p-Pb collisions observed by ALICE (2023) and ATLAS (2016), and enables future studies of the centrality dependence of nuclear suppression and the role of fragmentation sources in asymmetric systems.
