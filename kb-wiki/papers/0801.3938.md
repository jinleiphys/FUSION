# Sound waves and solitons in hot and dense nuclear matter

> Assuming that nuclear matter can be treated as a perfect fluid, we study the propagation of perturbations in the baryon density. The equation of state is derived from a relativistic mean field model, which is a variant of the non-linear Walecka model. The expansion of the Euler and continuity equations of relativistic hydrodynamics around equilibrium configurations leads to differential equations for the density fluctuations. We solve them numerically for linear and spherical perturbations and follow the time evolution of the initial pulses. For linear perturbations we find single soliton solutions and solutions with one or more solitons followed by radiation. Depending on the equation of state a strong damping may occur. Spherical perturbations are strongly damped and almost do not propagate. We study these equations also for matter at finite temperature. Finally we consider the limiting case of shock wave formation.

## Key claim

Under the assumption that nuclear matter behaves as a perfect fluid, perturbations in baryon density can propagate as Korteweg-de Vries (KdV) solitons in linear geometry, but only when derivative couplings between the nucleon and the vector field are included in the interaction Lagrangian; spherical perturbations are strongly damped and almost do not propagate.

## Method

The authors start from relativistic Euler and continuity equations for a perfect fluid, using an equation of state derived from a variant of the non-linear Walecka model that includes a higher-derivative baryon-vector meson coupling term. They expand the equations around equilibrium density using stretched coordinates and a small expansion parameter, retaining terms up to second order to obtain KdV-like differential equations for the density perturbation. These equations are solved numerically for both linear (Cartesian) and spherical geometries, at zero and finite temperature, and the limiting case of shock wave formation is also considered.

## Key numbers

- The Fermi momentum is \(k_F \simeq 0.28\) GeV and the vector meson mass is \(m_V \simeq 0.8\) GeV, giving the new interaction term an estimated size of \(p^2/m_V^2 \sim k_F^2/m_V^2 \sim 0.12\).
- The speed of sound \(c_s\) for the NL1 parameter set is 0.16 (in units of the speed of light); for other parameter sets it ranges from 0.16 to 0.25.
- The saturation density \(\rho_0\) for the NL1 set is 0.15 fm\(^{-3}\); the incompressibility \(K\) for NL1 is 211 MeV.
- The diffusion constant \(D\) for baryon number propagation in nuclear matter is \(D \simeq 0.35\) fm at densities comparable to equilibrium nuclear density and temperatures around 80 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of KdV solitons in cold nuclear matter (Fogaça et al. 2005, 2006) by including finite temperature effects and spherical geometry, and by providing numerical solutions. It builds on earlier pioneering work on soliton formation in nuclear matter by other groups (Fröbrich et al., 1980s; Rau et al., 1990s; Abul-Magd, 1990s) and on the application of hydrodynamics to strongly interacting systems at RHIC. The results enable future searches for solitons in heavy-ion collisions and in the core of dense stars, where perturbations may be caused by neutrino interactions.
