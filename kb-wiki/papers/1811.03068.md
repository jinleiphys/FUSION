# Chemical Equilibration in Weakly Coupled QCD

> We study thermalization, hydrodynamization, and chemical equilibration in out-of-equilibrium Quark-Gluon Plasma starting from various initial conditions using QCD effective kinetic theory, valid at weak coupling. In non-expanding systems gauge bosons rapidly lose information of the initial state and achieve kinetic equilibrium among themselves, while fermions approach the equilibrium distribution only at later time. In systems undergoing rapid longitudinal expansion, both gluons and quarks are kept away from equilibrium by the expansion, but the evolution is well described by fluid dynamics even before local thermal equilibrium is reached. For realistic couplings we determine the ordering between the separate hydrodynamization, chemical equilibration and thermalization time scales to be $τ_\text{hydro}<τ_\text{chem}<τ_\text{therm}$.

## Key claim
For realistic QCD couplings, the ordering of equilibration time scales in out-of-equilibrium quark-gluon plasma is hydrodynamization before chemical equilibration before thermalization ($\tau_\text{hydro}<\tau_\text{chem}<\tau_\text{therm}$), but this ordering is established only for systems where the coupling is weak enough that the effective kinetic theory remains valid.

## Method
The authors extended the QCD effective kinetic theory (EKT) of Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2002) to include dynamical fermions alongside gluons, solving the Boltzmann equation with leading-order $2\leftrightarrow 2$ scattering and $1\leftrightarrow 2$ splitting processes including in-medium screening and Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal suppression. They simulated the time evolution of homogeneous non-expanding systems and boost-invariant longitudinally expanding systems initialized with various out-of-equilibrium conditions, using Monte Carlo sampling of collision integrals on a discretized momentum grid.

## Key numbers
- For non-expanding systems starting with thermal gluons and no fermions at $\lambda=0.1$, the chemical equilibration time (90% of equilibrium fermion energy density) is $t_\text{chem} \approx 1.4 \tau_R$, where $\tau_R = 4\pi (\eta/s)/T_\text{final}$.
- For non-expanding systems with overoccupied gluon initial conditions, gluons reach kinetic equilibrium among themselves at $t_\text{kinetic}^g \approx 0.2 \tau_R$, significantly faster than chemical equilibration.
- For expanding systems with overoccupied gluon initial conditions at $\lambda=5$ (corresponding to $\eta/s \approx 2.75$), the evolution follows a universal curve after rapid memory loss of initial conditions.
- The specific shear viscosity values studied range from $\eta/s \approx 1$ (for $\lambda=10$) to $\eta/s \approx 1900$ (for $\lambda=0.1$), with chemical equilibration times scaling as $t_\text{chem}/\tau_R \sim 1.1-1.4$ across this range.

## Context
This work extends the weak-coupling treatment of Kurkela and Zhu (2015) and Kurkela et al. (2015) from pure gauge theory to full QCD with dynamical fermions, addressing the question of whether quark equilibration is a bottleneck for thermalization as suggested by Biro et al. (1981). The results enable multi-stage phenomenological simulations of heavy-ion collisions that include chemical equilibration, building on the framework of Kurkela et al. (2018) for linking initial conditions to hydrodynamic evolution.
