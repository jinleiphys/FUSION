# Heavy quarkonia production at LHC and EIC using Basis Light-Front Quantization wavefunctions

> We study exclusive charmonium and bottomonium production in ultra-peripheral heavy-ion collisions and electron-ion collisions within the dipole picture. We employ heavy quarkonium light-front wavefunctions obtained within the basis light-front quantization framework, which has some features of the light-front holographic QCD. We focus on comparison with measurements of exclusive charmonium and bottomonium production in ultraperipheral $pp$, $p$Pb and PbPb collisions at LHC and find reasonable agreement with the cross sections. We also discuss the coherent production cross-section ratios of excited states to the ground state for charmonium and bottomonium, which exhibit insensitivity to the dipole model parameters. We show that electron-ion collisions provide an opportunity for a quantitative study of heavy quarkonium structure.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that heavy quarkonium light-front wavefunctions obtained from the basis light-front quantization (BLFQ) approach, when used within the dipole picture, yield reasonable agreement with measured cross sections for exclusive charmonium and bottomonium production in ultra-peripheral proton-proton, proton-lead, and lead-lead collisions at the LHC, but the agreement is qualified by the choice of dipole model parameters and the use of effective quark masses that differ between the bound-state wavefunction and the photon wavefunction.

## Method

The authors calculate diffractive vector meson production cross sections by convoluting the BLFQ-derived heavy quarkonium light-front wavefunctions with the photon light-front wavefunction and the dipole-proton cross section, using two impact-parameter-dependent dipole models (bSat and bCGC) whose parameters were previously fitted to inclusive deep inelastic scattering data from HERA. For ultra-peripheral heavy-ion collisions, they employ the equivalent photon approximation to convert photon-nucleus cross sections into hadron-hadron cross sections, using the Woods-Saxon distribution for nuclear density and averaging over nucleon configurations for coherent production.

## Key numbers

- Charmonium mass spectrum fitted with r.m.s. deviation from experiment of about 50 MeV (initial study) and about 31 MeV (with running coupling), using parameters m_c = 1.522 GeV, κ_con = 0.938 GeV (initial) and m_c = 1.603 GeV, κ_con = 0.966 GeV (running coupling)
- Bottomonium mass spectrum fitted with r.m.s. deviation from experiment of about 50 MeV (initial) and about 38 MeV (with running coupling), using parameters m_b = 4.763 GeV, κ_con = 1.490 GeV (initial) and m_b = 4.902 GeV, κ_con = 1.389 GeV (running coupling)
- Strong coupling fixed at α_s(M_{c\bar c}) ≈ 0.36 for charmonium and α_s(M_{b\bar b}) ≈ 0.25 for bottomonium in the initial study
- Diffractive production cross-section ratio σ_{Ψ(2s)}/σ_{J/Ψ} exhibits significant independence of model parameters, especially for deeply virtual processes
- Predictions using BLFQ LFWFs differ from those using LFWFs from the updated running-coupling calculation by less than 2%

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation (Chen et al., 2016) that focused on charmonium production at HERA and LHC PbPb collisions, now including bottomonium states and comparisons with LHC run 2 data for pp, pPb, and PbPb collisions. The BLFQ approach improves upon the boosted Gaussian wavefunctions (Brodsky, 1980; Nemchik, 1996) and holographic QCD wavefunctions (de Teramond, 2005; Forshaw, 2012) by providing a unified description of all heavy quarkonium states below open-flavor thresholds without additional assumptions for excited states, and enables predictions for future Electron-Ion Collider measurements where higher excited vector meson states can be precisely studied.
