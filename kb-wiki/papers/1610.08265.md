# Confinement-Deconfinement transition in $SU(2)+$Higgs Theory

> We study the confinement-deconfinement transition in $SU(2)$ gauge theory in the presence of massless bosons using lattice Monte Carlo simulations. The nature of this transition depends on the temporal extent ($N_τ$) of the Euclidean lattice. We find that the transition is a cross-over for $N_τ=2,4$ and second order with $3D$ Ising universality class for $N_τ=8$. Our results show that the second order transition is accompanied by realization of the $Z_2$ symmetry.

## Key claim

The confinement-deconfinement transition in SU(2) gauge theory coupled to massless Higgs bosons (with vanishing bare mass and quartic coupling) is a cross-over for coarse lattices (Nτ=2,4) but becomes second order with 3D Ising universality class for Nτ=8, suggesting that in the continuum limit the transition is second order and accompanied by effective realization of Z2 symmetry, contrary to perturbative expectations of maximal symmetry breaking.

## Method

Lattice Monte Carlo simulations were performed using the standard heat bath algorithm for gauge fields and pseudo heat bath algorithm for Higgs fields, with 4 over-relaxation steps between updates, on Nτ×Ns³ lattices. The Polyakov loop, its susceptibility, and Binder cumulant were computed for Nτ=2 (Ns=8,10,12), Nτ=4 (Ns=16,20,24), and Nτ=8 (Ns=32,40,48) using 100,000 configurations per volume, with error analysis via Jackknife method with bin size 10,000 configurations. Critical exponents were extracted using finite-size scaling of susceptibility maxima and Binder cumulant crossing points, employing 3D Ising values ν=0.6298 and ω=0.825 for the extrapolation.

## Key numbers

- For Nτ=8, the ratio γ/ν = 1.98(2) from finite-size scaling of susceptibility maxima for L=32,40,48.
- For Nτ=8, the infinite-volume Binder cumulant Uc = 0.468(4) from extrapolation of effective crossing values.
- For Nτ=8, the ratio β/ν = 0.52(2) from magnetization at βc for each volume.
- For Nτ=8, the critical coupling βc = 2.5064(4) from Binder cumulant crossing analysis.
- The interaction term between gauge and Higgs fields increases by a factor of approximately 5.12 from Nτ=4 to Nτ=6, and by a factor of approximately 3.18 from Nτ=6 to Nτ=8.
- Approximate crossover couplings: βc ~ 1.8 for Nτ=2 and βc ~ 2.29 for Nτ=4.

## Context

This work extends non-perturbative studies of the confinement-deconfinement transition in SU(2) gauge theory with matter fields, contrasting with perturbative 1-loop calculations by Gross (1980) and Weiss (1981) which predicted maximal Z2 symmetry breaking for massless bosons. It builds on earlier lattice studies by Damgaard (1986) which found critical behavior of the Polyakov loop above the transition for heavy Higgs, and by Biswal (2015) which showed vanishing explicit symmetry breaking for large but finite Higgs mass. The results enable future investigations of ZN symmetry realization for different N and in the presence of fermion fields, and suggest that the conventional expectation of crossover behavior in the massless limit may not hold for bosonic matter fields in the continuum limit.
