# Radial Oscillations in Hybrid Stars with Slow Quark Phase Transition

> This study investigates the radial oscillations of hybrid neutron stars, characterized by a composition of hadronic external layers and a quark matter core. Utilizing a density-dependent relativistic mean-field model that incorporates hyperons and baryons for describing hadronic matter, and a density-dependent quark model for quark matter, we analyze the ten lowest eigenfrequencies and their corresponding oscillation functions. Our focus lies on neutron stars with equations-of-state involving N, N + $Δ$, N + H, and N + H + $Δ$, featuring a phase transition to quark matter. Emphasizing the effects of a slow phase transition at the hadron-quark interface, we observe that the maximum mass is attained before the fundamental mode's frequency decreases for slow phase transitions. This observation implies the stability of stellar configurations with higher central densities than the maximum mass, called Slow Stable Hybrid Stars (SSHSs), even under small radial perturbations. The length of these SSHS branch depends upon the energy density jump between two phases and the stiffness of the quark EoS.

# Radial Oscillations in Hybrid Stars with Slow Quark Phase Transition

## Key claim
The maximum mass of a hybrid neutron star is attained before the fundamental oscillation mode frequency decreases to zero for slow phase transitions at the hadron-quark interface, implying that stellar configurations with central densities higher than the maximum mass configuration can remain stable against small radial perturbations. These configurations, called Slow Stable Hybrid Stars (SSHSs), exist only when the phase transition timescale is slow compared to the oscillation period.

## Method
The authors constructed hybrid neutron star equations of state using a density-dependent relativistic mean-field (DD-RMF) model with the DDME2 parametrization for hadronic matter, including nucleons, hyperons, and delta baryons, and a density-dependent quark mass (DDQM) model for quark matter. The phase transition between hadronic and quark phases was implemented via the Maxwell construction, imposing equal pressure and chemical potential at the interface. The ten lowest radial oscillation eigenfrequencies and their corresponding eigenfunctions were computed by solving the Sturm-Liouville perturbation equations using a shooting method, with separate treatments for slow and rapid conversion timescales at the hadron-quark interface.

## Key numbers
- Maximum mass decreases from 2.30 solar masses for nucleonic-only (N) composition to 2.25 solar masses when delta baryons are included.
- For N+H (nucleons plus hyperons) and N+H+Δ (nucleons plus hyperons plus deltas) compositions, the maximum mass changes from 1.97 to 1.98 solar masses.
- The smallest energy density jump at the phase transition is 38 MeV/fm^3 for the N+H equation of state.
- The largest SSHS branch radius is 0.88 km, obtained for the N+H equation of state with the smallest energy density jump and stiff quark equation of state.
- Oscillation frequencies were computed at 1.4 solar masses for the fundamental (f-mode) and pressure (p-mode) modes.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of hybrid neutron stars with phase transitions by Rather and collaborators (2024) and builds on the formalism for radial oscillations in neutron stars developed by Chandrasekhar (1964) and Thorne and Campolattaro (1967). The concept of slow-stable hybrid stars (SSHSs) follows from the work of Lugones (2023) and Pereira and collaborators (2017), who identified that slow conversion timescales at the hadron-quark interface can stabilize configurations that would otherwise be unstable. The results enable future studies incorporating temperature, rotation, and magnetic fields for more realistic modeling of hybrid stars, and provide oscillation mode predictions that could be tested through multi-messenger astronomy.
