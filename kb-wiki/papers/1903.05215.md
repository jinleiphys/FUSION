# Local chiral EFT potentials in nuclei and neutron matter: results and issues

> In recent years, the combination of advanced quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) methods and local interactions derived from chiral effective field theory (EFT) has been shown to provide a versatile and systematic approach to nuclear systems. Calculations at next-to-next-to-leading order in chiral EFT have lead to fascinating results for nuclei and nucleonic matter. On the one hand, ground-state properties of nuclei are well reproduced up to $A\leq16$, even though these potentials have been fit to nucleon-nucleon scattering and few-body observables only. On the other hand, a reasonable description of neutron-matter properties emerges. While regulator functions applied to two- and three-nucleon forces are a necessary ingredient in these many-body calculations, the use of local regulators leads to a substantial residual regulator and cutoff dependence that increases current theoretical uncertainties. In this contribution, we review local chiral interactions, their applications, and QMC results for nuclei and neutron matter. In addition, we address regulator issues for such potentials and present a possible path forward.

## Key claim

Local chiral effective field theory potentials up to next-to-next-to-leading order, when combined with quantum Monte Carlo methods, can simultaneously reproduce ground-state properties of nuclei up to A=16 and provide a reasonable description of neutron-matter properties, but the use of local regulators introduces a substantial residual regulator and cutoff dependence that increases current theoretical uncertainties beyond the truncation uncertainty estimate.

## Method

The authors employed auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) calculations using local chiral interactions up to next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO) that include consistent two- and three-nucleon forces. The low-energy couplings for the three-nucleon forces were fit to reproduce the binding energy of 4He and the P-wave n-alpha elastic scattering phase shifts using the Green's function Monte Carlo method. For neutron matter, the equation of state was computed and then extended to neutron-star conditions using a speed-of-sound extension up to densities of 1-2 times nuclear saturation density.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state properties of nuclei are well reproduced up to A=16 using local chiral potentials with coordinate-space cutoff R0=1.0 fm
- For the softer potential with R0=1.2 fm, 16O is significantly overbound and very compact, with the first diffraction minimum in the charge form factor occurring at a significantly higher momentum than experimentally observed
- The uncertainty due to regulator artifacts in neutron matter is approximately 4 MeV per nucleon at nuclear saturation density n0=0.16 fm^-3, larger than the truncation-uncertainty estimate of approximately 3 MeV per nucleon
- The energy per particle of pure neutron matter at saturation density is in the range approximately 12-19 MeV
- At twice nuclear saturation density, the total uncertainty in the neutron-matter equation of state is approximately 6-42 MeV
- Using the equation of state up to saturation density as a constraint, the radius of a typical 1.4 solar mass neutron star is constrained to the range 8.4-15.2 km
- Using the equation of state up to twice nuclear saturation density, the radius range reduces to 8.7-12.6 km and the upper limit on the maximum mass reduces to 2.9 solar masses

## Context

This work extends the program of combining quantum Monte Carlo methods with chiral effective field theory interactions, building on earlier developments by Gezerlis (2013, 2014) for local chiral potentials and by Lynn (2016, 2017) for three-nucleon forces. The results enable systematic uncertainty quantification for nuclear structure and neutron-star properties, but also highlight that regulator artifacts from local regulators violate Fierz rearrangement freedom, leading to sizable contributions in the three-nucleon sector that must be addressed at higher orders in the chiral expansion.
