# Nonperturbative photon $q\bar{q}$ light front wave functions from a contact interaction model

> We propose a method to calculate the $q\bar{q}$ light front wave functions (LFWFs) of photon at low-virtuality, i.e., the light front amplitude of $γ^*\rightarrow q\bar{q}$ at low $Q^2$, based on a light front projection approach. We exemplify this method using a contact interaction model within Dyson-Schwinger equations formalism and obtain the nonperturbative photon $q\bar{q}$ LFWFs. In this case, we find the nonperturbative effects are encoded in the enhanced quark mass and a dressing function of covariant quark-photon vertex, as compared to the leading order quantum electrodynamics photon $q\bar{q}$ LFWFs. We then use nonperturbative-effect modified photon $q\bar{q}$ LFWFs to study the inclusive deep inelastic scattering HERA data in the framework of the color dipole model. The results demonstrate that the theoretical description of data at low $Q^2$ can be significantly improved once the nonperturbative corrections are included in the photon LFWFs.

## Key claim

The paper claims that nonperturbative photon \(q\bar{q}\) light front wave functions (LFWFs) derived from a contact interaction model within Dyson-Schwinger equations can significantly improve the description of inclusive deep inelastic scattering (DIS) HERA data at low \(Q^2\) when used in the color dipole model, but this improvement is conditional on the LFWFs being valid only at low virtuality and requiring a phenomenological transition function to connect to perturbative results at higher \(Q^2\).

## Method

The authors extract photon \(q\bar{q}\) LFWFs from the covariant un-amputated quark-photon vertex using a light front projection method previously developed for vector mesons. They employ a contact interaction model within the Dyson-Schwinger equations formalism in rainbow-ladder truncation, which yields a momentum-independent dressed quark mass \(M_f\) and a dressing function \(P_T^{(f)}(Q^2)\) for the transverse part of the quark-photon vertex. These nonperturbative LFWFs are then incorporated into the bCGC (b-dependent Color Glass Condensate) dipole model to compute the reduced cross section \(\sigma_r\) for inclusive DIS, with a linear combination of nonperturbative and perturbative LFWFs weighted by a \(Q^2\)-dependent factor \(F_{\text{part}}(Q^2) = Q_0^{2n}/(Q^2 + Q_0^2)^n\).

## Key numbers

* Dressed quark masses from the contact interaction model: \(M_{u/d} = 0.37\) GeV and \(M_s = 0.53\) GeV, compared to current quark masses \(m_{u/d} = 0.007\) GeV and \(m_s = 0.17\) GeV.
* Model parameters for the contact interaction: \(m_G = 0.5\) GeV, \(\alpha_{\text{IR}}/\pi = 0.36\), \(\Lambda_{\text{ir}} = 0.24\) GeV, \(\Lambda_{\text{uv}} = 0.91\) GeV.
* Fitting the reduced DIS cross section data from HERA for \(Q^2 \in [0.25, 50]\) GeV\(^2\) with nonperturbative-effect modified LFWFs yields \(\chi^2/\text{d.o.f} = 337.9/280 = 1.207\), with fitted parameters \(\gamma_s = 0.6177\), \(N_0 = 0.4596\), \(x_0 = 1.326 \times 10^{-4}\), \(\lambda = 0.1875\), \(Q_0^2 = 1.052\) GeV\(^2\), and \(n = 3.970\).
* For the same \(Q^2\) range using purely perturbative LFWFs with current quark masses (\(m_{u/d}=4\) MeV, \(m_s=95\) MeV), the fit gives \(\chi^2/\text{d.o.f} = 678.4/282 = 2.406\).
* For the restricted range \(Q^2 \in [0.85, 50]\) GeV\(^2\) with perturbative LFWFs, the fit yields \(\chi^2/\text{d.o.f} = 265.8/223 = 1.192\).

## Context

This work extends the light front projection method of Shi (2021) from vector meson LFWFs to photon LFWFs, providing a first theoretical calculation of nonperturbative photon \(q\bar{q}\) LFWFs at low virtuality. It contrasts with earlier phenomenological approaches that introduced model functions to mimic nonperturbative effects, such as those by Forshaw (2003), Flensburg (2008), Berger (2012), and Goncalves (2020), and with perturbative next-to-leading order QCD calculations by Beuf (2016, 2021, 2022). The results enable improved color dipole model descriptions of low-\(Q^2\) DIS data and suggest that dynamical chiral symmetry breaking plays a significant role in shaping photon LFWFs, with the dressing function \(P_T(Q^2)\) providing a few percent suppression at low \(Q^2\) and reducing to unity at \(Q^2 = 0\).
