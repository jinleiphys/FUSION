# Hadron production in central nucleus-nucleus collisions at chemical freeze-out

> We analyze the experimental hadron yield ratios for central nucleus-nucleus collisions in terms of thermal model calculations over a broad energy range, $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=2.7-200 GeV. The fits of the experimental data with the model calculations provide the thermal parameters, temperature and baryo-chemical potential at chemical freeze-out. We compare our results with the values obtained in other studies and also investigate more technical aspects such as a potential bias in the fits when fitting particle ratios or yields. Using parametrizations of the temperature and baryonic chemical potential as a function of energy, we compare the model calculations with data for a large variety of hadron yield ratios. We provide quantitative predictions for experiments at LHC energy, as well as for the low RHIC energy of 62.4 GeV. The relation of the determined parameters with the QCD phase boundary is discussed.

## Key claim

The thermal model with only two free parameters (temperature T and baryo-chemical potential μb) successfully describes hadron yield ratios in central nucleus-nucleus collisions across √sNN = 2.7–200 GeV, but the extracted parameters show systematic discrepancies between different experimental data sets at the top SPS energy (158 AGeV), particularly for the temperature, which differs significantly between NA49 and NA44+NA57 data.

## Method

The authors performed χ² and δ² minimization fits of a grand-canonical thermal model (including all known hadrons and resonances with vacuum masses, finite-width effects at AGS energies, and excluded-volume corrections) to experimental mid-rapidity hadron yield ratios from central Au-Au or Pb-Pb collisions. They analyzed data at five AGS energies (√sNN = 2.70–4.85 GeV), three SPS energies (√sNN = 6.27–17.3 GeV), and RHIC energies (√sNN = 62.4 and 200 GeV), extracting T and μb at chemical freeze-out for each energy.

## Key numbers

- At top AGS energy (10.7 AGeV, √sNN = 4.85 GeV): T = 124 ± 3 MeV, μb = 537 ± 10 MeV, χ²/Ndf = 6.9/7
- At SPS 40 AGeV (√sNN = 8.76 GeV): T = 156⁺⁴₋₃ MeV, μb = 403⁺¹⁸₋₁₄ MeV, χ²/Ndf = 15.4/13
- At SPS 80 AGeV (√sNN = 12.3 GeV): T = 154 ± 6 MeV, μb = 298 ± 20 MeV, χ²/Ndf = 23.4/5
- At top SPS energy (158 AGeV, √sNN = 17.3 GeV): combined fit gives T = 160 ± 5 MeV, μb = 240 ± 18 MeV, χ²/Ndf = 56/22; NA44+NA57 data alone give T = 179 ± 7.5 MeV, μb = 267 ± 26 MeV; NA49 data alone give T = 150 ± 4.5 MeV, μb = 226 ± 15 MeV
- AGS energies (2, 4, 6, 8 AGeV): T ranges from 64 to 93 MeV, μb from 760 to 580 MeV, with χ²/Ndf values of 1.0/2, 0.43/3, 1.15/4, 1.14/3 respectively

## Context

This work extends the thermal model analyses of Braun-Munzinger et al. (1999) and Cleymans et al. (1999) by performing a consistent second-generation analysis of all available data over a broader energy range (√sNN = 2.7–200 GeV). The extracted freeze-out parameters are compared to lattice QCD calculations of the phase boundary by Karsch et al. (2003) and to the universal freeze-out condition of Cleymans et al. (2001). The results enable quantitative predictions for LHC energies and for the RHIC energy of 62.4 GeV, and the discrepancy between NA49 and NA44+NA57 data at top SPS energy leaves open the question of whether the unsatisfactory thermal fits signal a change in physics near the QCD critical point.
