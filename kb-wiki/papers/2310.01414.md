# Broadening of particle distributions in electron- and (anti)neutrino-nucleus scattering from QED interactions

> Proper interpretation of past, current, and future data on lepton-nucleus reactions requires a clear separation between quantum electrodynamics (QED) and strong interaction effects inside the nucleus. First studies of QED in-medium lepton dynamics have set a theoretical framework to derive electron-nucleus and (anti)neutrino-nucleus cross-section corrections. We employ this approach to quantitatively compute the effects of Glauber photon-mediated multiple re-scattering within the nuclear medium. We find that the relativistic charged lepton acquires momentum of order $10~\mathrm{MeV}$ transverse to its direction of propagation inside the nucleus. This broadening sizably deflects expected electron tracks and suppresses scattering cross sections. Precise extraction of the nucleon and nuclear structure by electron and muon probes should, thus, take the QED nuclear medium angular redistribution of particles into account. Our results further show that the associated effects in (anti)neutrino-nucleus scattering with measured final-lepton energy are significant only at the kinematical endpoints.

## Key claim

The paper quantitatively computes that multiple QED Glauber-photon exchanges within the nuclear medium give charged leptons a transverse momentum of order 10 MeV, which sizably deflects electron tracks and suppresses scattering cross sections; however, the associated effects in (anti)neutrino-nucleus scattering with measured final-lepton energy are significant only at the kinematical endpoints.

## Method

The authors employ a theoretical framework for QED in-medium lepton dynamics to compute Glauber photon-mediated multiple re-scattering of charged leptons inside a large nucleus. They model the nucleus as a sphere of uniform charge distribution with radius R_rms, derive the transverse momentum distribution of outgoing leptons from an integral over the transverse coordinate space, and evaluate cross-section corrections by averaging over the angular redistribution from both initial- and final-state re-scattering. For (anti)neutrino scattering, the reconstructed neutrino energy is determined from the final lepton energy and scattering angle, and the ratio of broadened to expected cross sections is computed via an integral over the transverse momentum distribution.

## Key numbers

- The relativistic charged lepton acquires transverse momentum of order 10 MeV inside the nucleus.
- For ultrarelativistic leptons, the number of QED interactions inside the nucleus reaches χ ~ 10^5–10^7.
- Considering the 2σ range of the Gaussian distribution, the charged lepton gains around 8–26 MeV of transverse momentum while traveling in the nuclear medium.
- The r.m.s. deflection angle for 2 GeV electrons reaches 1–1.5 degrees and decreases with increasing recoil electron energy.
- Cross-section suppression from QED broadening in electron-nucleus scattering can reach 1–2% for heavy nuclei at 2 GeV beam energy.
- In (anti)neutrino-nucleus scattering, relative cross-section corrections are typically below or at the permille level, but can exceed the percent level near kinematic endpoints.

## Context

This work extends the initial studies of QED in-medium lepton dynamics by Tomalak (2022) that found permille-level effects in (anti)neutrino-nucleus scattering and up to percent-level effects in electron-nucleus scattering in forward kinematics. The authors apply the formalism of Glauber photon exchanges, analogous to QCD studies of the Cronin effect by Accardi (2002) and Gyulassy (2002), to quantify angular de-correlation and cross-section modifications. The results enable precise extraction of nucleon and nuclear structure from electron and muon scattering experiments by requiring that QED nuclear medium angular redistribution be unfolded from the data, and they identify that for (anti)neutrino experiments the effect is only relevant near kinematic endpoints.
