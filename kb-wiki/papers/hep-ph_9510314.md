# Dispersion relations from the Hard Thermal Loop effective action in a magnetic field

> Dispersion relations for fermions at high temperature and in a background magnetic field are calculated in two different ways. First from a straightforward one-loop calculation where, in the weak field limit, we find an expression closely related to the standard dispersion relations in the absence of the magnetic field. Secondly, we derive the dispersion relations directly from the Hard Thermal Loop effective action, which allows for an exact solution (i.e. to all orders in the external field), up to the last numerical integrals.

## Key claim

The paper derives fermion dispersion relations at high temperature in a constant background magnetic field, showing that in the weak field limit the result is closely related to the standard zero-field Hard Thermal Loop dispersion relations, while the HTL effective action method provides an exact solution to all orders in the external field, up to the final numerical integrals.

## Method

The author first performs a one-loop calculation of the fermion self-energy at finite temperature using exact electron propagators in the Furry picture and Schwinger's proper-time method, taking the weak field limit while keeping the combination of canonical momentum and magnetic field exactly. Second, the dispersion relations are derived directly from the Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) effective action by computing matrix elements of the non-local operator between vacuum eigenstates (Landau level wavefunctions), which reduces the Dirac equation to a 4x4 matrix equation that factorizes into left- and right-handed components.

## Key numbers

- Thermal mass defined as M = e^2 T^2 / 8, used as the unit for all dimensionful parameters
- For the lowest Landau level (n=0) with eB = 0.2 M^2, the HTL and weak-field dispersion relations agree rather well (shown in Figure 2)
- Dispersion relations computed for eB values of 0.5, 1.0, 2.0, and 10.0 (in units of M^2) for the right-handed branch in the lowest Landau level (Figure 3)
- For n >= 1, the dispersion relations involve the term sqrt(2eBn) in the coupling between chiral components

## Context

This work extends the standard HTL formalism developed by Klimov (1982) and Weldon (1982, 1989) for fermion dispersion relations at high temperature to include a constant background magnetic field. It also builds on the author's previous one-loop calculation (Elmfors, Persson, Skagerstam, 1995) by providing an alternative derivation from the HTL effective action of Braaten and Pisarski (1990) and Frenkel and Taylor (1990). The results enable future study of how strong magnetic fields modify thermal excitations in hot QED plasmas, particularly the spectral weight of new massless modes that appear at large field strengths.
