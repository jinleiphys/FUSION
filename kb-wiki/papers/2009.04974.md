# Early Time Dynamics and the Bulk

> Deciphering the origin of collective phenomena in small colliding systems is one of contemporary focuses in heavy-ion physics. It entails penetrating the barrier between two previously separated research topics: thermalization/hydrodynamization and phenomenological studies of collectivity. I first review some recent progress in understanding thermalization/hydrodynamization in large colliding systems, centralized on bottom-up thermalization. Then, using a simple kinetic theory I demonstrate how the investigation of hydrodynamization is intertwined with the study of flow in small colliding systems. Connections of these studies to "hard probes" are also commented where possible.

## Key claim

The paper argues that understanding the origin of collective flow in small colliding systems requires penetrating the barrier between thermalization/hydrodynamization studies and phenomenological studies of collectivity, using a simple kinetic theory to demonstrate that non-hydrodynamic modes (particle-like excitations) dominate flow in small systems such as pA collisions, contrary to the hydrodynamic-mode dominance assumed in many phenomenological models.

## Method

The author reviews bottom-up thermalization in large colliding systems using perturbative QCD and kinetic theory, then applies a simple kinetic theory (similar to that in Baym (1984)) to study the interplay between hydrodynamic and non-hydrodynamic modes in small colliding systems. The analysis uses the concept of "fluid quality" to quantify the dominance of hydrodynamic modes, and computes linear and nonlinear response of flow coefficients to spatial eccentricities in systems with arbitrary transverse profiles.

## Key numbers

- Pressure anisotropy scaling in Stage I: \(P_L/P_T \sim 1/(\tau Q_s)^{2/3}\) for \(\alpha_s^{-3/2} > \tau Q_s > 1\)
- Pressure anisotropy in Stage II: \(P_L/P_T \sim \alpha_s\) for \(\alpha_s^{-5/2} > \tau Q_s > \alpha_s^{-3/2}\)
- Pressure anisotropy in Stage III: \(P_L/P_T \sim \alpha_s^{13} (\tau Q_s)^5\) for \(\alpha_s^{-13/5} > \tau Q_s > \alpha_s^{-5/2}\)
- Thermal equilibrium established at \(\tau \sim \alpha_s^{-13/5}/Q_s\) where \(P_L/P_T \sim 1\)
- Hydrodynamic modes dominate for opacity \(\hat{\gamma} \gtrsim 4\) while non-hydrodynamic modes dominate for \(\hat{\gamma} \lesssim 2\), using the criterion \(Q < 0.1\) for fluid quality

## Context

This proceeding extends the bottom-up thermalization scenario of Baier et al. (2000) and connects it to phenomenological studies of flow in small colliding systems, building on kinetic theory work by Kurkela et al. (2018, 2019). The work enables experimental tests of the mechanism underlying thermalization/hydrodynamization by using flow data from small systems to discern the interplay between hydrodynamic and non-hydrodynamic modes, and calls for scrutiny of hydrodynamic models used for small colliding systems as reviewed by Nagle and Zajc (2018).
