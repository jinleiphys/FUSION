# Effects of angular momentum projection on the nuclear partition function and the observation of the giant dipole resonance in hot nuclei

> Procedures for projecting angular momentum in a model describing a hot nucleus that takes into account large-amplitude quadrupole fluctuations are discussed. Particular attention is paid to the effect angular-momentum projection has on the observables associated with the $γ$-decay of the giant-dipole resonance (GDR). We also elaborate on which of the different projection methods provides the best overall description of the GDR, including angular distributions. The main consequence of angular-momentum projection is the appearance of an effective volume element in the integrals associated with the thermal average of the physical observables. This effective volume element is controlled by the value of the moments of inertia of the system. In the limit of rigid-body moments of inertia, the effective volume element is found to differ only slightly from the volume element associated with the normalization of the five-dimensional quadrupole oscillator wavefunction in the $β$, $γ$, and $Ω$ space, namely $D[α]=β^4dβsin(3γ)dγdΩ$. In the limit of irrotational flow moments of inertia, the leading behavior in the $β$ degree of freedom is $D[α]\propto βdβ$.

# Effects of angular momentum projection on the nuclear partition function and the observation of the giant dipole resonance in hot nuclei

## Key claim
Angular momentum projection in a hot nucleus leads to an effective volume element in the thermal average integrals that is controlled by the moments of inertia; the choice of projection method affects the sampling of large-amplitude thermal fluctuations, but the three methods give essentially the same GDR cross section for low to medium angular momentum values, with the Jz-projection method providing the best overall description including angular distributions.

## Method
The authors investigated three methods for projecting angular momentum onto the nuclear partition function at finite temperature: (1) fixed rotational frequency projection, where a constant rotational frequency is applied at all deformations; (2) full J-projection, where the saddle-point integration is performed before integrating over deformation degrees of freedom, yielding a deformation-dependent rotational frequency; and (3) Jz-projection, where only the z-component of angular momentum is projected. The GDR cross section and a2-coefficient were computed for 106Sn and 208Pb using Nilsson-Strutinsky free energies with rigid-body moments of inertia and shell corrections, comparing results across the three methods as functions of temperature and angular momentum.

## Key numbers
- For 106Sn at T=1.6 MeV and J=0, 20, 40, and 60 ħ, the three projection methods give approximately the same total GDR cross section, except at J=60 ħ where the fixed ω-procedure yields a somewhat broader strength function.
- For 208Pb at T=1.5 MeV and J=0, 20, 40, and 60 ħ, the three methods give roughly the same total cross section, with a weaker dependence on angular momentum than for 106Sn due to the larger moment of inertia in Pb.
- The effective volume element with rigid-body moments of inertia differs from the standard β⁴dβ sin(3γ)dγdΩ by at most a factor of 2.5 for β ≤ 1.0.
- In the limit of irrotational flow moments of inertia, the leading behavior in β for the effective volume element is proportional to β dβ.
- Shell corrections to the moment of inertia in 208Pb reduce the moment of inertia for the spherical shape by over 40% at zero temperature, but have very little effect on the GDR cross section beyond that produced by the free energy.
- At T=1.5 MeV, the a2-coefficient obtained at J=60 ħ with and without shell corrections to the moment of inertia are essentially identical.

## Context
This work extends the static-path approximation (SPA) framework of Lauritzen et al. (1988) and Johnson et al. (1991) for describing the GDR in hot nuclei, addressing the controversy over the correct volume element in the thermal average integrals that was debated by Gallardo et al. (1985), Pacheco et al. (1988), and Alhassid et al. (1988). The paper introduces angular momentum projection methods that overcome the unbound fission barrier problem present in the fixed rotational frequency approach at high spin, and enables the study of the Jacobi-shape phase transition at very high angular momentum, which was previously introduced by Alhassid et al. (1993). The Jz-projection method is recommended as the best all-around approach for describing the GDR in hot nuclei at high spin.
