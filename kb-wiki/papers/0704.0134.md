# Causal dissipative hydrodynamics for QGP fluid in 2+1 dimensions

> In 2nd order causal dissipative theory, space-time evolution of QGP fluid is studied in 2+1 dimensions. Relaxation equations for shear stress tensors are solved simultaneously with the energy-momentum conservation equations. Comparison of evolution of ideal and viscous QGP fluid, initialized under the same conditions, e.g. same equilibration time, energy density and velocity profile, indicate that in a viscous dynamics, energy density or temperature of the fluid evolve slowly, than in an ideal fluid. Cooling gets slower as viscosity increases. Transverse expansion also increases in a viscous dynamics. For the first time we have also studied elliptic flow of 'quarks' in causal viscous dynamics. It is shown that elliptic flow of quarks saturates due to non-equilibrium correction to equilibrium distribution function, and can not be mimicked by an ideal hydrodynamics.

# Causal dissipative hydrodynamics for QGP fluid in 2+1 dimensions

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in second-order causal dissipative hydrodynamics, the space-time evolution of a QGP fluid in 2+1 dimensions shows slower cooling and increased transverse expansion compared to ideal hydrodynamics, but only for the QGP phase without phase transition; the elliptic flow of quarks saturates due to non-equilibrium corrections to the equilibrium distribution function and cannot be reproduced by ideal hydrodynamics.

## Method

The authors solve the energy-momentum conservation equations simultaneously with relaxation equations for the shear stress tensors in Israel-Stewart's second-order causal dissipative theory, assuming longitudinal boost invariance and baryon-free QGP fluid in 2+1 dimensions. The code AZHYDRO-KOLKATA is used to evolve the fluid with a simple equation of state p = ε/3 and shear viscosity treated as a parameter, using initial conditions from a Glauber model with central energy density ε₀ = 30 GeV/fm³ and equilibration time τᵢ = 0.6 fm. Only shear viscosity is included; bulk viscosity and heat conduction are neglected.

## Key numbers

- Central energy density initial condition: ε₀ = 30 GeV/fm³
- Equilibration time: τᵢ = 0.6 fm
- ADS/CFT motivated viscosity: η/s = 0.08 (≈ 1/4π)
- Perturbative QCD estimate of viscosity: η/s = 0.135 (for two-flavored QGP with αₛ ≈ 0.5)
- Degeneracy factor for QGP with N_f ≈ 2.5: g_qgp = 42.25
- For η/s = 0.135, QGP lifetime at the fluid center (x=0, y=0) is increased by approximately 5% compared to ideal fluid
- For η/s = 0.135, QGP lifetime at x=0, y=5 fm is enhanced by approximately 10%
- Shear pressure tensors πˣˣ and πʸʸ reduce by approximately a factor of 6 after 2 fm of evolution
- Entropy generation saturates after approximately 3 fm of evolution
- In second-order theory, approximately 80% more entropy is generated compared to first-order theory

## Context

This work extends earlier first-order dissipative hydrodynamics results from the same group (Chaudhuri 2006, 2007) to second-order causal theory, addressing the causality violation problem of first-order Eckart and Landau-Lifshitz formulations. The study is motivated by the paradigm from RHIC experiments that a nearly perfect fluid is created in Au+Au collisions, with ideal hydrodynamics (Heinz 2004) only partially successful in explaining data up to p_T ≈ 1.5 GeV and failing to reproduce HBT radii (the "HBT puzzle") or elliptic flow saturation at high p_T. The paper enables future studies including phase transitions and hadronization, and provides a framework for phenomenological extraction of transport coefficients of strongly coupled QGP using experimental data.
