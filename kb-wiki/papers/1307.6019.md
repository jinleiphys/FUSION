# Covariant gauges without Gribov ambiguities in Yang-Mills theories

> We propose a one-parameter family of nonlinear covariant gauges which can be formulated as an extremization procedure that may be amenable to lattice implementation. At high energies, where the Gribov ambiguities can be ignored, this reduces to the Curci-Ferrari-Delbourgo-Jarvis gauges. We further propose a continuum formulation in terms of a local action which is free of Gribov ambiguities and avoids the Neuberger zero problem of the standard Faddeev-Popov construction. This involves an averaging over Gribov copies with a nonuniform weight, which introduces a new gauge-fixing parameter. We show that the proposed gauge-fixed action is perturbatively renormalizable in four dimensions and we provide explicit expressions of the renormalization factors at one loop. We discuss the possible implications of the present proposal for the calculation of Yang-Mills correlators.

## Key claim

The authors propose a one-parameter family of nonlinear covariant gauges that can be formulated as an extremization procedure suitable for lattice implementation, and they further propose a continuum formulation that is free of Gribov ambiguities and avoids the Neuberger zero problem, but only for a specific choice of weighting functional that introduces a new gauge-fixing parameter and leads to a theory that is perturbatively renormalizable in four dimensions.

## Method

The authors define a gauge-fixing functional that depends on a matrix field η and a gauge transformation U, then average over η with a Gaussian distribution to obtain the Curci-Ferrari-Delbourgo-Jarvis gauge-fixing action. To handle Gribov copies, they introduce a nonuniform weighting over copies using a parameter β₀ and employ the replica trick from statistical physics to express the resulting two-step average (first over Gribov copies, then over Yang-Mills field configurations) as a local field theory involving n replicas of a supersymmetric nonlinear sigma model coupled to a massive extension of the Curci-Ferrari action.

## Key numbers

- The gauge-fixing parameter ξ₀ appears in the Gaussian distribution P[η] = N exp(-(g₀²/4ξ₀) ∫_x tr(η†η))
- The new gauge-fixing parameter β₀ controls the lifting of degeneracy between Gribov copies through the weighting factor exp(-β₀ H[A,η,U_i])
- The action S_CF = S_β₀ + S_CFDJ includes a mass term for gluons and ghosts proportional to β₀: S_β₀ = β₀ ∫_x {½(A_μ^a)² + ξ₀ c̄^a c^a}
- The theory is proven perturbatively renormalizable in d=4 dimensions, with all free propagators decreasing at least as fast as 1/p² at large momentum

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous proposal for averaging over Gribov copies in the Landau gauge (Serreau 2012) to a broader class of nonlinear covariant gauges, generalizing the Curci-Ferrari-Delbourgo-Jarvis gauges (Curci 1976, Delbourgo 1981). The extremization functional generalizes the one used in lattice gauge-fixing proposals by Cucchieri (2009), and the approach addresses the Neuberger zero problem (Neuberger 1986) that plagues the standard Faddeev-Popov construction. The resulting theory enables comparison between lattice and continuum calculations of Yang-Mills correlators in gauges beyond the Landau gauge, and the authors note that a one-loop calculation in the Curci-Ferrari model had already given good agreement with lattice data in the deep infrared (Tissier 2010).
