# S-wave $π^0$ Production in pp Collision in a Covariant OBE Model

> The total cross section for the pp -> pp pi^0 reaction at energies close to threshold is calculated using a covariant one-boson-exchange model, where a boson B created on one of the incoming protons is converted into a neutral pion on the second. The amplitudes for the conversion processes, B N -> N pi^0, are taken to be the sum of s, u and t-channel pole terms. The main contributions to the primary production amplitude is due to an effective isoscalar sigma meson pole in a t-channel, which is enhanced strongly due to offshellness. With this contribution included the model reproduces, both the scale and energy dependence of the cross section.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a covariant one-boson-exchange (OBE) model, including an effective isoscalar sigma meson pole in the t-channel whose contribution is strongly enhanced due to offshellness, can reproduce both the scale and energy dependence of the total cross section for the pp → pp π⁰ reaction near threshold, but only if the πN σ-term is taken to be 35 MeV; the model's predictions depend strongly on this value.

## Method

The authors calculate the total cross section for pp → pp π⁰ using a covariant OBE model where a virtual boson B (π, σ, η, ρ, ω, δ) created on one incoming proton is converted into a neutral pion on the second via a BN → π⁰N conversion process. The conversion amplitudes are taken as the sum of s, u, and t-channel pole terms, with the σππ vertex determined by applying Adler's consistency conditions and fitting to the isospin-even π⁰p scattering length. The primary production amplitude includes contributions from all six meson exchanges, with final state interactions (FSI) and initial state interactions (ISI) corrections introduced for comparison with data.

## Key numbers

- The transferred 4-momentum at π⁰ production threshold is q² = -3.3 fm⁻² (space-like).
- The σππ vertex constants extracted are g₀σ = 0.22 ± 0.04, g₁σ = -0.82 ± 0.42, g₂σ = -2.62 ± 0.7, using σπN(0) = 35 MeV, Fπ = 93.5 MeV, a⁺ = (-0.010 ± 0.003) mπ⁻¹, and g²σNN/4π = 8.03.
- The effective σππ coupling at the σ pole is g_eff_σππ = (2.5 ± 0.9) mσ, compared to the soft-pion estimate of 2.8 mσ.
- The off-mass-shell t-pole term for π⁰p → π⁰p at threshold is approximately -i 1.0 (gσNN/mσ), a factor of about 9 larger than the on-mass-shell value of approximately -i 0.11 (gσNN/mσ).
- The relative ratios of partial exchange amplitudes are Mπ : Mρ : Mω : Mσ : Mη : Mδ ≈ 138 : 42 : 8 : 6 : 5 : 0.6.
- The ρ meson contribution is Mρ(s+u) ≈ 30% and Mρ(t) ≈ 4.5% of M(in).
- The δηπ vertex constants are g₀δ = 0.05 ± 0.035, g₁δ = -5.95 ± 1.5, g₂δ = -3.46 ± 0.1, g₃δ = 3.30 ± 0.2.
- The conversion amplitude Tηp→π⁰p at threshold is approximately 0.98 fm, with the t-pole contribution only approximately 0.04 fm.
- The πN σ-term is taken as σπN(0) = 35 ± 5 MeV for y=0 (OZI rule), with an alternative experimental value of 45 ± 8 MeV from the Cheng-Dashen point.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by Koltun (1966), Laget (1987), Miller (1991), and Niskanen (1992) that underestimated the pp → pp π⁰ cross section by a factor of 3-5 using single nucleon and pion rescattering mechanisms. It builds on the approach of Lee and Riska (1993) showing meson exchange currents could explain the scale, and Horowitz et al. (1994) who performed OBE-based calculations including Z-graphs. The paper contrasts with chiral perturbation theory results by Park et al. (1996), Cohen et al. (1996), and Sato et al. (1997), where off-shell rescattering amplitudes had opposite sign leading to destructive interference and much smaller cross sections. The model enables a consistent treatment of all meson exchanges within the OBE picture of Machleidt (1989), preserving crossing symmetry and accounting for relativistic effects.
