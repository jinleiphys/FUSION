# Production Processes as a Tool to Study Parameterizations of Quark Confinement

> We introduce diquarks as separable correlations in the two-quark Green's function to facilitate the description of baryons as relativistic three-quark bound states. These states then emerge as solutions of Bethe-Salpeter equations for quarks and diquarks that interact via quark exchange. When solving these equations we consider various dressing functions for the free quark and diquark propagators that prohibit the existence of corresponding asymptotic states and thus effectively parameterize confinement. We study the implications of qualitatively different dressing functions on the model predictions for the masses of the octet baryons as well as the electromagnetic and strong form factors of the nucleon. For different dressing functions we in particular compare the predictions for kaon photoproduction, $γp\to KΛ$, and associated strangeness production, $pp\to pKΛ$ with experimental data. This leads to conclusions on the permissibility of different dressing functions.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether different parameterizations of quark and diquark propagators that remove poles (thereby modeling confinement) can produce acceptable predictions for baryon masses, form factors, and production cross sections, concluding that only certain dressing functions are phenomenologically permissible when compared to experimental data for kaon photoproduction and associated strangeness production.

## Method

The authors model baryons as relativistic three-quark bound states by reducing the problem to an effective Bethe-Salpeter equation for quarks and diquarks interacting via quark exchange, using separable diquark correlations. They implement confinement by replacing free (di)quark propagators with dressed versions using three different multiplicative dressing functions (complex conjugate poles, entire functions, or non-analytic functions) that eliminate asymptotic states. The model predictions for octet baryon masses, nucleon electromagnetic form factors, and strong form factors are computed, and the resulting cross sections for kaon photoproduction ($\gamma p \to K\Lambda$) and associated strangeness production ($pp \to pK\Lambda$) are compared with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass is used to fix the scalar diquark-quark coupling constant $g_s$; the ratio $g_a/g_s = 0.2$ is assumed for axialvector diquarks based on results from Oettel (2000).
- For the non-analytic dressing function $f_3$, choosing $d > 5$ makes the propagator resemble the free propagator in a large domain, mitigating the $\eta$-dependence of the Bethe-Salpeter eigenvalue.
- The nucleon isovector magnetic moments come out too small by about 30% when using free propagators with model parameters that do not properly reproduce the baryon spectrum, as reported in Oettel (2000).
- The dressing functions $f_1$, $f_2$, and $f_3$ are constructed such that for asymptotically large spacelike momenta, the three model propagators match the bare propagator $S^{(0)}$.

## Context

This work extends the covariant diquark model previously applied to nucleon form factors in the spacelike regime by Bloch (1999, 2000) and Oettel (2000) to include timelike momenta relevant for production processes, which were inaccessible with free propagators due to unphysical thresholds. The study contrasts three confinement parameterizations (complex conjugate poles from Stingl (1996), entire functions from Efimov (1993) and Bloch (1999), and non-analytic functions) and finds that the choice of dressing function significantly affects predictions for strangeness production, enabling discrimination between permissible and impermissible confinement models.
