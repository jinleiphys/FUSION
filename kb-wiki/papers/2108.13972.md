# Stellar Structure and Stability of Charged Interacting Quark Stars and Their Scaling Behaviour

> We explore the stellar structure and radial stability of charged quark stars composed of interacting quark matter (IQM) in three classes of commonly used charge models. We adopt a general parametrization of IQM equation of state that includes the corrections from perturbative QCD, color superconductivity, and the strange quark mass into one parameter $λ$, or one dimensionless parameter $\barλ=λ^2/(4B_{\rm eff})$ after being rescaled with the effective bag constant $B_{\rm eff}$. We find that increasing charge tends to increase the mass and radius profiles, and enlarges the separation size in mass between the maximum mass point and the point where zero eigenfrequencies $ω^2_0=0$ of the fundamental radial oscillation mode occur. The sign of the separation in central density depends on the charge model; this separation also has a dependence on $λ$ such that increasing $λ$ (which can occur for either large color superconductivity or small strange quark mass) tends to decrease this separation size for the first and third classes of charge models monotonically. Moreover, for the second and third classes of charge models, we manage to numerically and analytically identify a new kind of stellar structure with a zero central pressure but a finite radius and mass. All the calculations and analysis are performed in a general dimensionless rescaling approach so that the results are independent of explicit values of dimensional parameters.

## Key claim

Increasing electric charge in interacting quark stars tends to increase their mass and radius, but the relative position of the maximum mass point and the point of zero fundamental radial oscillation eigenfrequency depends on the charge model; for model B (charge proportional to volume) the zero-frequency point occurs at higher central density than the maximum mass point, while for models A and C it occurs at lower central density, with the separation size depending on the interaction parameter \(\bar{\lambda}\).

## Method

The authors solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations with charge terms included, using a unified equation of state for interacting quark matter that incorporates perturbative QCD corrections, color superconductivity, and strange quark mass into a single parameter \(\lambda\) (or its dimensionless form \(\bar{\lambda} = \lambda^2/(4B_{\rm eff})\)). They studied three charge models: model A (charge density proportional to energy density with parameter \(\alpha\)), model B (charge proportional to volume with parameter \(\beta\)), and model C (fixed total charge \(Q\)). Radial stability was determined by solving the equations for infinitesimal radial oscillations and finding the fundamental mode eigenfrequency \(\omega_0^2\), with stability requiring \(\omega_0^2 \geq 0\). All calculations were performed in a dimensionless rescaling scheme that removes explicit dependence on the effective bag constant \(B_{\rm eff}\).

## Key numbers

- For model A with \(\alpha = 0.999\), increasing \(\alpha\) and \(\bar{\lambda}\) results in larger mass and radius; the stability point \(\bar{\omega}^2_0=0\) occurs for smaller central densities than the maximum mass point, with the density separation size decreasing with increasing \(\bar{\lambda}\).
- For model B with \(\bar{\beta} = 3.5\) and \(\bar{\lambda} = 10\) or \(\bar{\lambda} = \infty\), the configurations are radially unstable for all central densities (no stability points exist).
- For model B, the critical \(\bar{\beta}_{c0}\) at zero central pressure ranges from \(\bar{\beta}_{c0} = 4\pi/3 \approx 4.189\) for \(\bar{\lambda}=0\) to \(\bar{\beta}_{c0} = 2\pi/3 \approx 2.094\) for \(\bar{\lambda}\to\infty\) (positive \(\lambda\) case).
- For model C, fixed total charges \(\bar{Q} = (0, 1.538, 3.076) \times 10^{-2}\) were studied, corresponding to \(Q = (0, 1, 2) \times 10^{20}\) C for \(B_{\rm eff} = 60\) MeV/fm\(^3\).
- For negative \(\lambda\) in model B with \(\bar{\lambda} = 0.5\) and \(\bar{\lambda} = 10\), the critical \(\bar{\beta}_{c0}\) values are approximately 7.11 and 40.12, respectively.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of charged quark stars by Negreiros (2009), Arbanil (2015), Panotopoulos (2019, 2021), Goncalves (2020), and Jasim (2021) by incorporating a unified interacting quark matter equation of state that includes both perturbative QCD corrections and color superconductivity effects into a single parameter, rather than relying on ad hoc values of empirical parameters. The dimensionless rescaling approach makes the results independent of the explicit value of the effective bag constant. The identification of a new stellar structure with zero central pressure but finite radius and mass for models B and C enables future investigations of exotic compact object configurations and their observational signatures.
