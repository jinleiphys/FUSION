# Basic-deformed thermostatistics

> Starting from the basic-exponential, a q-deformed version of the exponential function established in the framework of the basic-hypergeometric series, we present a possible formulation of a generalized statistical mechanics. In a q-nonuniform lattice we introduce the basic-entropy related to the basic-exponential by means of a q-variational principle. Remarkably, this distribution exhibits a natural cut-off in the energy spectrum. This fact, already encountered in other formulations of generalized statistical mechanics, is expected to be relevant to the applications of the theory to those systems governed by long-range interactions. By employing the q-calculus, it is shown that the standard thermodynamic functional relationships are preserved, mimicking, in this way, the mathematical structure of the ordinary thermostatistics which is recovered in the q=1 limit.

## Key claim

Starting from the basic-exponential function defined within basic-hypergeometric series, the authors propose a generalized statistical mechanics where the equilibrium distribution exhibits a natural cut-off in the energy spectrum for \(q>1\), a feature expected to be relevant for systems governed by long-range interactions, while the standard thermodynamic functional relationships are preserved and ordinary thermostatistics is recovered in the \(q\to1\) limit.

## Method

The authors introduce a basic-entropy \(S_q(p) = -\int p(\lambda)\,\mathrm{Ln}_q\,p(\lambda)\,d_q\lambda\) using the basic-logarithm and the Jackson \(q\)-integral over a \(q\)-nonuniform hierarchical lattice. They derive equilibrium distributions for microcanonical, canonical, and grand canonical ensembles by solving a \(q\)-variational principle with the ansatz \(p(\lambda) = \mathrm{E}_q(-f(\lambda))\), employing the Jackson derivative and the algebraic properties of the basic-exponential. The thermodynamic structure is then explored by showing that the partition function and its \(q\)-derivatives yield expectation values, and that an Euler-type equation and a generalized state equation \(PV = T\,\mathrm{Ln}_{1/q}\,\mathcal{Z}_q\) hold.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of \(q\)-calculus and basic-hypergeometric series, previously applied to \(q\)-deformed bosons and fermions (Lavagno, 2006), into a classical deformed thermostatistics. It differs from other generalized statistical mechanics such as those of Tsallis (1988), Abe (1997), Kaniadakis (2001), and Scarfone (2004) because the basic-exponential lacks a power-law tail and instead exhibits a stretched-exponential-like behavior with a natural energy cut-off, making it complementary rather than alternative to those theories. The cut-off feature, previously suggested by Spitzer (1942) for isothermal globular clusters, enables applications to complex systems with long-range interactions where a finite phase-space volume is expected.
