# The Quark Pauli Principle and the Transmutation of Nuclear Matter

> The phase space density, $ρ^Q$, of quarks in nuclei is studied using realistic models of unintegrated quark distributions, known as transverse momentum densities (TMDs). If this density exceeds unity for matter at normal nuclear densities, the effects of the quark Pauli principle must play a role in nuclei, and models in which the nucleon density at low momentum is small (Quarkyonic matter) may become a starting point for an entirely new description of nuclei. We denote the nuclear density for which $ρ^Q=1$ to be a transmutation density, $n_T$, because quark degrees of freedom must be relevant at that density. Including the TMDs of [G. de Teramond et. al, \href{DOI:https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevLett.120.182001} Phys. Rev. Lett. {\bf 120}, 182002, (2018)] for the valence quarks and phenomenological TMDs for the sea quarks we find that $n_T=0.17 \pm 0.04\,\rm fm^{-3}$, the density of normal nuclear matter. Some of fhe implications of this finding are discussed.

## Key claim

The quark phase space density in nuclei reaches unity at a transmutation density of \(n_T = 0.17 \pm 0.04\,\rm fm^{-3}\), which is the density of normal nuclear matter, meaning that quark Pauli blocking effects must be relevant in ordinary nuclei and that normal nuclear matter may already be Quarkyonic matter.

## Method

The authors computed the quark phase space density \(\rho^Q(\vec k=0)\) by convolving light-front wave functions for valence quarks from de Teramond et al. (2018) with a Fermi gas nucleon momentum distribution, and added sea quark contributions using CTEQ10 parton distribution functions. They determined the nuclear density at which \(\rho^Q(0)=1\) by evaluating the squared wave functions at the longitudinal momentum fraction \(x\) corresponding to zero three-momentum for each Fock-space component, using the relation \(x_{\rm trans} = \omega_q/M\) where \(\omega_q\) is the quark energy per component.

## Key numbers

* Transmutation density: \(n_T = 0.17 \pm 0.04\,\rm fm^{-3}\), equal to the density of normal nuclear matter \(\rho_0 = 0.17\,\rm fm^{-3}\) from Bohr (1969).
* For the valence quark Fock-space components, the average longitudinal momentum fractions are \(x_3 = 0.184\) for the three-parton component and \(x_4 = 0.136\) for the four-parton component, consistent with the estimates \(1/6\) and \(1/8\) of the nucleon mass.
* The valence quark contribution to the denominator of the transmutation density formula is about 55% of the total, and the sea quark contribution is about 45%.

## Context

This work extends the IdylliQ model of McLerran (2007) and the Quarkyonic matter treatment of Koch (2024) by replacing the simplified Gaussian probability distribution for quarks inside nucleons with realistic, experimentally constrained transverse momentum densities from de Teramond et al. (2018) and CTEQ10 sea quark distributions. The result that the transmutation density coincides with normal nuclear density suggests that the repulsive forces stabilizing nuclear matter (traditionally attributed to omega meson exchange or contact interactions) may originate from quark Pauli repulsion, and that nuclear matter might not be a normal Fermi liquid but rather Quarkyonic matter with a shell-like nucleon momentum distribution.
