# Tensor supercurrent in QCD

> An external Abelian magnetic field excites in the QCD vacuum a tensor supercurrent that represents the tensor polarization of the chiral condensate. This tensor supercurrent can be deduced from the chiral lagrangian in the presence of anomalies; a similar tensor supercurrent emerges in rotating systems at finite chemical potential. We discuss the microscopic origin of this supercurrent and argue that it screens the instanton--anti-instanton $I\bar{I}$ molecules in the QCD vacuum, similarly to the vector supercurrent screening Abrikosov vortices in a superconductor. A number of possible experimental manifestations of the tensor supercurrent are discussed: {\it i}) spin alignment of axial-vector and vector mesons in heavy ion collisions; {\it ii}) tensor charge of the nucleon; {\it iii}) transversity of quark distributions in polarized nucleons.

# Tensor supercurrent in QCD

## Key claim

An external Abelian magnetic field induces a tensor supercurrent in the QCD vacuum that represents the tensor polarization of the chiral condensate; this supercurrent screens instanton-anti-instanton molecules in the vacuum ensemble, analogous to how vector supercurrents screen Abrikosov vortices in a superconductor. The effect arises at zero temperature and zero chemical potential, and the tensor current is proportional to the quark condensate times the external field strength tensor.

## Method

The authors derive the tensor supercurrent from the chiral Lagrangian with an anomalous term that couples the magnetic susceptibility of the quark condensate to the external electromagnetic field. They analyze the microscopic origin using the spectral representation of the Dirac operator, showing that the tensor current is saturated by zero modes in the background of instanton-anti-instanton molecules. The paper also computes a one-loop correction from charged pion fluctuations using the Heisenberg-Euler effective Lagrangian, and discusses experimental signatures including vector meson spin alignment in heavy ion collisions and the tensor charge of the nucleon.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic susceptibility of the quark condensate from the Vainshtein relation: chi = -N_c / (4 pi^2 f_pi^2), where N_c = 3 and f_pi is the pion decay constant
- Effective pion mass in external fields in the chiral limit: m^2_{pi,eff} = chi <bar{Psi}Psi> F_{munu} B_{munu} f_pi^{-2}
- Pion size: R_pi ~ (4 pi f_pi)^{-1} ~ 0.2 fm
- Tensor current one-loop correction from charged pions: J_{munu} = chi <bar{Psi}Psi> F_{munu} (1 + (e B ln 2)/(16 pi^2 f_pi^2))
- Axial-vector meson coupling to nucleon: g_{a1 NN} = 9.3 +/- 1
- Vector rho meson coupling to nucleon: g_{rho NN} = 2.52 +/- 0.06

## Context

This work extends the dual superconductor picture of the QCD vacuum originally proposed by 't Hooft and Mandelstam, and builds on prior studies of magnetic susceptibility of the quark condensate by Smilga (2000), Vainshtein (2002), and lattice QCD calculations by Buividovich (2009). The tensor supercurrent is related to the chiral magnetic effect and chiral separation effect reviewed by Kharzeev (2012) and Miransky (2015), and connects to the 1-form hydrodynamics formalism of Hofman (2018), Jain (2018), and Son (2018). The paper enables experimental searches for tensor supercurrent signatures in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, particularly through vector meson spin alignment measurements, and provides a framework for understanding the tensor charge of the nucleon and transversity distributions in polarized deep-inelastic scattering.
