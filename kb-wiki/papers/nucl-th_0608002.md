# Breaking of nucleon Cooper pairs at finite temperature in 93-98Mo

> The S shape of the canonical heat capacity is known as a signature of the pairing transition and along an isotopic chain it is significantly more pronounced for nuclei with an even number of neutrons than with an odd number. Although the heat capacities extracted from experimental level densities in 93-98Mo exhibit a clear S shape, they do not show such an odd-even staggering. To understand the underlying physics, we analyze thermal quantities evaluated from the partition function calculated using the static-path plus random-phase approximation (SPA+RPA) in a monopole pairing model with number-parity projection. The calculated level densities reproduce very well the experimental data and they also agree with estimates using the back-shifted Fermi-gas model. We clarify the reason why the heat capacities for Mo isotopes do not show odd-even staggering of the S shape. We also discuss thermal odd-even mass differences in 94-97Mo using the three-, four-, and five-point formula. These thermal mass differences are regarded as indicators of pairing correlations at finite temperature.

## Key claim

Although the heat capacities extracted from experimental level densities in 93-98Mo exhibit a clear S shape, they do not show the odd-even staggering (more pronounced S shape for even-neutron nuclei) that has been observed in other nuclei; the authors attribute this absence to the dominant contribution of proton pairing correlations, which are similar across the isotopic chain, rather than neutron pairing.

## Method

The authors performed static-path plus random-phase approximation (SPA+RPA) calculations within a monopole pairing model using a deformed Woods-Saxon potential with spin-orbit interaction, with number-parity projection of the partition function. They computed level densities via saddle-point approximation of the partition function and extracted heat capacities and thermal odd-even mass differences (three-, four-, and five-point formulas) from both the calculated and experimental level densities, the latter extrapolated using the back-shifted Fermi-gas model.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for the S shape of the heat capacity in 93-98Mo is \(T_c \sim 0.7\)–0.9 MeV.
- For the neutron part of the heat capacity, the critical temperature is \(\sim 0.55\) MeV; for the proton part, \(T_c \sim 0.75\) MeV.
- Pairing force strengths used: \(G_n = 22/A\) MeV for neutrons and \(G_p = 26.5/A\) MeV for protons.
- Deformation parameters: \(\beta_2 = 0.15\) for 94Mo, 0.17 for 96Mo and 98Mo; for odd nuclei 93Mo, 95Mo, 97Mo: \(\beta_2 = 0.10\), 0.08, and 0.17, respectively.
- At high temperature \(T = 1.25\) MeV, the calculated effective pairing gap for neutrons is \(\Delta^n_{\rm eff} = 0.6\)–0.7 MeV.
- Back-shifted Fermi-gas parameters for 98Mo: pairing energy \(E_{\rm pair} = 2.080\) MeV, level-density parameter \(a = 11.33\) MeV\(^{-1}\), back-shift \(C_1 = -1.521\) MeV, normalization factor \(\eta = 0.87\).

## Context

This work extends previous SPA+RPA studies of pairing transitions (Rossignoli, 2000) and shell-model Monte-Carlo calculations (Liu, 2001; Alhassid, 2002) that showed odd-even staggering of the S-shaped heat capacity in other nuclei. It contradicts the pattern observed in earlier experimental work on 161-167Dy and 170-172Yb (Schiller, 2003; Melby, 2001), where even-neutron nuclei showed a more pronounced S shape than odd-neutron nuclei. The results enable future theoretical studies of additional correlations (e.g., quadrupole) that may wash out the expected sudden quenching of thermal odd-even mass differences, and motivate experiments to extract proton odd-even mass differences at finite temperature.
