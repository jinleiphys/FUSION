# 3D Initial-State Dynamics across scales: A Comparative Study of saturation and string-based descriptions

> We compare the longitudinal deposition of various conserved quantities in the initial condition models of a string based (SMASH) and a saturation based (McDipper) approach. SMASH has been shown to work reasonably well at lower collision energies as an initial condition for the SMASH-vHLLE hybrid approach, while McDipper, based on the color-glass-condensate (CGC), works well in the regime of perturbative QCD. The two models are capable of providing longitudinally resolved initial conditions, which is essential for 3D hydrodynamical simulations. The goal of this study is to interface the different regions of applicability of the two models, to investigate the initial state dynamics in the intermediate energy regime. We analyze the deposition of transverse energy, charge and baryon number across a large range of collision energies (62.4 GeV to 5.02 TeV) and find that, while they are good agreement at lower energies, their energy and baryon deposition differs substantially at higher center of mass energies.

## Key claim

The two models agree on transverse energy deposition at lower collision energies (62.4 GeV) but diverge substantially at higher center-of-mass energies (up to 5.02 TeV), with the saturation-based model (McDipper) depositing more transverse energy than the string-based model (SMASH), while SMASH retains more baryons at midrapidity across all energies.

## Method

The authors compared the longitudinal deposition of transverse energy, net-baryon number, and electric charge from two initial-state models: McDipper, which computes energy and charge densities as moments of single-inclusive gluon and quark distributions using kT-factorization in the Color Glass Condensate framework at leading order, and SMASH, which propagates hadrons via the relativistic Boltzmann equation and uses Pythia for inelastic interactions via string fragmentation, then constructs initial conditions on a constant proper-time hypersurface. Both models were applied to nucleus-nucleus collisions at center-of-mass energies of 62.4, 200, and 5020 GeV for fixed impact parameters b = 2, 5, and 9 fm, with results presented as ensemble averages over event-by-event fluctuations.

## Key numbers

* Transverse energy deposition dE_T/deta_s at midrapidity for Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 62.4 GeV and b = 2 fm: SMASH and McDipper produce similar values, but McDipper's longitudinal profile extends considerably further toward forward and backward rapidities.
* At sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV, McDipper deposits substantially more transverse energy than SMASH across all impact parameter classes (b = 2, 5, 9 fm).
* The smearing parameters used for SMASH initial conditions were R_perp = 0.63 fm and R_eta = 1.69 fm, inferred from experimental data via Bayesian analysis.
* The nucleon size parameter in McDipper is B_G = 0.156 fm^2, determined through fits to HERA data.
* The lower bound on the switching proper time for SMASH initial conditions is tau_sw = 0.5 fm.
* Nuclear radius for Au: R_Au = 6.38 fm with diffusiveness a_Au = 0.535 fm; for Pb: R_Pb = 6.67 fm with a_Pb = 0.54 fm.

## Context

This work extends comparisons between saturation-based and string-based initial-state models for heavy-ion collisions, building on the SMASH-vHLLE hybrid approach (Schafer 2021) which works at lower collision energies, and the IP-Glasma + MUSIC framework (Schenke 2012) which is successful at higher energies. The McDipper model (Garcia-Montero 2023) provides a simplified CGC-based approach that connects rapidity to initial gluon and quark distributions. The study enables future work on 3D hydrodynamical simulations in the intermediate energy regime (sqrt(s_NN) ~ 10-200 GeV), where neither purely partonic nor purely hadronic descriptions are expected to be complete, and informs understanding of phenomena such as flow decorrelations and baryon stopping.
