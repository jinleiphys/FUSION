# The Three-Point Energy Correlator in the Coplanar Limit

> Energy correlators are a type of observables that measure how energy is distributed across multiple detectors as a function of the angles between pairs of detectors. In this paper, we study the three-point energy correlator (EEEC) at lepton colliders in the three-particle near-to-plane (coplanar) limit. The leading-power contribution in this limit is governed by the three-jet (trijet) configuration. We introduce a new approach by projecting the EEEC onto the volume of the parallelepiped formed by the unit vectors aligned with three detected final-state particles. Analogous to the back-to-back limit of the two-point energy correlator probing the dijet configuration, the small-volume limit of the EEEC probes the trijet configuration. We derive a transverse momentum dependent (TMD) based factorization theorem that captures the soft and collinear logarithms in the coplanar limit, which enables us to achieve the next-to-next-to-next-to-leading logarithm (N$^3$LL) resummation. To our knowledge, this is the first N$^3$LL result for a trijet event shape. Additionally, we demonstrate that a similar factorization theorem can be applied to the fully differential EEEC in the three-particle coplanar limit, which provides a clean environment for studying different coplanar trijet shapes.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the three-point energy correlator (EEEC) in the coplanar limit can be resummed to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading logarithm (N$^3$LL) accuracy using a transverse momentum dependent (TMD) factorization theorem, which is the first N$^3$LL result for a trijet event shape, but this result is specifically derived for the quark channel ($e^+e^-\to q\bar q g$) at lepton colliders and relies on a jet algorithm cut ($y_3>y_{\rm cut}$) to isolate the coplanar trijet configuration.

## Method

The authors introduce a new "volume projection" of the EEEC, defined as $\tau_p = |(\hat{n}_1\times \hat{n}_2)\cdot \hat{n}_3|$, which measures the volume of the parallelepiped formed by three unit vectors of detected hadrons, and restrict to trijet events using the $k_T$ (Durham) algorithm with a cut on the 3-jet resolution variable $y_3$. They derive a factorization theorem in the soft-collinear effective theory (SCET) framework that separates the cross section into a hard function (from the $e^+e^-\to q\bar q g$ process), three TMD fragmentation functions (one per jet), and a trijet TMD soft function, then perform resummation of soft and collinear logarithms to N$^3$LL accuracy using renormalization group evolution.

## Key numbers

* The leading-order hard function for the $q\bar q g$ channel is $H_q^{(0)}(v,w,\mu) = 2\frac{(1-v)^2+(1-w)^2}{vw}$, where $v=s_{qg}/Q^2$ and $w=s_{\bar q g}/Q^2$ satisfy $u+v+w=1$ in the massless limit.
* The normalization cross section is $\sigma_{\text{LO}} = \frac{\alpha_s(Q)}{4\pi} C_F \, \sigma_0$, where $\sigma_0$ is the Born cross-section for $e^+e^-\to q\bar q$.
* The phase space for the trijet selection uses $y_{\rm cut}=0.1$ as the lower bound on the 3-jet resolution variable $y_3$.
* The $ggg$ channel contribution is $\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s^2)$ suppressed relative to the $q\bar q g$ channel.

## Context

This work extends the resummation program for energy correlators from the two-point case (EEC), which has been resummed to N$^4$LL in the back-to-back limit (Collins (1981), Ellis (1984), de Florian (2004), Moult (2018-2022), Duhr (2022)), to the three-point case (EEEC) in the coplanar limit. It builds on the fixed-order EEEC results of Yang (2022) and the projected energy correlator resummation to NNLL by Lee (2022) and Chen (2023), and enables precise theoretical predictions for trijet observables at future lepton colliders (CEPC, ILC, FCC-ee) as well as potential applications to top quark mass measurements via the EEEC Jacobian peak in top decay (Holguin (2022-2024)).
