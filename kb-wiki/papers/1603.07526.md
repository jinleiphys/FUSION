# Self-energies of ground-state octet and decuplet baryon states due to the coupling to the baryon-meson continuum

> We present an unquenched quark model calculation of the mass shifts of ground-state octet and decuplet baryons due to the coupling to the meson-baryon continuum. The $q\bar{q}$ pair-creation effects are taken explicitly into account through a microscopic, QCD-inspired, quark-antiquark pair- creation mechanism.

## Key claim

The paper presents an unquenched quark model calculation of mass shifts for ground-state octet and decuplet baryons due to coupling to the baryon-meson continuum, but the results show that the self-energy corrections are so large that they may invalidate the quenched quark model approximation for low-energy hadrons.

## Method

The authors use the unquenched quark model (UQM) with a QCD-inspired $^{3}P_0$ pair-creation mechanism to compute self-energy corrections via loop integrals over all accessible ground-state octet and decuplet baryon intermediate states coupled to a complete set of Pseudo-Nambu-Goldstone bosons. The bare energies are treated as free parameters fitted to reproduce the physical baryon masses, and the self-energies are calculated using Eqs. (2) and (3) with the $^{3}P_0$ model parameters given in Table 1, including flux-tube overlap effects and an effective pair-creation strength fitted to the $\Delta^{++} \rightarrow p \pi^+$ decay width.

## Key numbers

- Self-energy of the nucleon: $\Sigma(E_a) = -0.368$ GeV, with bare energy $E_a = 1.307$ GeV yielding physical mass $M_a = 0.939$ GeV
- Self-energy of the $\Delta$: $\Sigma(E_a) = -0.314$ GeV, with bare energy $E_a = 1.546$ GeV yielding physical mass $M_a = 1.232$ GeV
- Self-energy of the $\Lambda$: $\Sigma(E_a) = -0.465$ GeV, with bare energy $E_a = 1.581$ GeV yielding physical mass $M_a = 1.116$ GeV
- Self-energy of the $\Sigma$: $\Sigma(E_a) = -0.303$ GeV, with bare energy $E_a = 1.498$ GeV yielding physical mass $M_a = 1.195$ GeV
- Self-energy of the $\Xi$: $\Sigma(E_a) = -0.474$ GeV, with bare energy $E_a = 1.792$ GeV yielding physical mass $M_a = 1.318$ GeV
- Self-energy of the $\Sigma^*$: $\Sigma(E_a) = -0.319$ GeV, with bare energy $E_a = 1.702$ GeV yielding physical mass $M_a = 1.383$ GeV
- Self-energy of the $\Xi^*$: $\Sigma(E_a) = -0.345$ GeV, with bare energy $E_a = 1.876$ GeV yielding physical mass $M_a = 1.532$ GeV
- Self-energy of the $\Omega$: $\Sigma(E_a) = -0.352$ GeV, with bare energy $E_a = 2.024$ GeV yielding physical mass $M_a = 1.672$ GeV
- $\Delta-N$ mass splitting: 54 MeV when all Pseudo-Nambu-Goldstone bosons are included, compared to 100 MeV with only pion loops and the experimental value of 293 MeV
- The harmonic oscillator parameters used: $\hbar \omega_{\rm baryon} = 0.32$ GeV for baryons and $\hbar \omega_{\rm meson} = 0.40$ GeV for mesons
- Pair-creation strength $\gamma_0 = 17.3$, fitted to the $\Delta^{++} \rightarrow p \pi^+$ decay width

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts to include continuum effects in baryon mass calculations, starting with Tornqvist and collaborators in the 1980s, and specifically builds on the unquenched quark model formalism previously applied to mesons (charmonium, bottomonium) and to the nucleon sea flavor asymmetry by Santopinto (2010). The results are compared to those of Horacsek et al. (1986), who only considered pion-quark coupling with a Gaussian form factor, and to Brack and Bhaduri (1987), who computed pionic self-energy contributions perturbatively. The paper identifies that the large self-energy corrections suggest the quenched approximation may be ineffective for low-energy hadrons, and that states near decay thresholds (like the $\Lambda^*(1405)$) may require similar unquenched treatment as was done for the $X(3872)$ meson in charmonium studies.
