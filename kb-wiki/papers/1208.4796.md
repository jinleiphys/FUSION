# Colour screening scenario for quarkonia suppression in a quasiparticle model compared with data obtained from experiments at the CERN SPS, BNL RHIC, and CERN LHC

> We present a modified colour screening model for $J/ψ$ suppression in the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) using quasi-particle model (QPM) as equation of state (EOS). Other theoretical ingredients incorporated in the model are feed-down from higher resonances namely, $χ_c$, and $ψ^{'}$, dilated formation time for quarkonia and viscous effects of the QGP medium. Assuming further that the QGP is expanding with Bjorken's hydrodynamical expansion, the present model is used to analyze the centrality dependence of the $J/ψ$ suppression in mid-rapidity region and compare it with the data obtained from SPS, RHIC and LHC experiments. We find that the centrality dependence of the data for the survival probability at all energies is well reproduced by our model. We further compare our model predictions with the results obtained from the bag model EOS for QGP which has usually been used earlier in all such calculations.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a modified colour-screening model using a quasiparticle model (QPM) equation of state for the quark-gluon plasma, together with feed-down from higher resonances, dilated formation time, and viscous hydrodynamics, can simultaneously reproduce the centrality dependence of J/psi suppression data from SPS, RHIC, and LHC experiments. The hedge is that this agreement requires a specific choice of dissociation temperatures (set II) for the excited charmonium states, and that the model works only for mid-rapidity data after normalization for cold nuclear matter effects.

## Method

The authors model J/psi suppression in a deconfined QGP using Debye colour screening as the dominant mechanism. They employ a quasiparticle model (QPM) equation of state for the QGP, assume Bjorken boost-invariant longitudinal expansion with viscous hydrodynamics (shear viscosity eta), and include feed-down contributions from chi_c (30%) and psi' (10%) decays. The survival probability is computed by solving for the screening radius where the QGP pressure equals the dissociation pressure, then integrating over the transverse plane with a radial production probability distribution and over transverse momentum.

## Key numbers

* Initial temperature T_i = 0.5 GeV for SPS and RHIC, T_i = 1.0 GeV for LHC
* J/psi mass m = 3.1 GeV, formation time tau_F = 0.89 fm; chi_c mass 3.5 GeV, tau_F = 2.0 fm; psi' mass 3.7 GeV, tau_F = 1.5 fm
* Critical temperature T_c = 0.17 GeV used throughout
* Dissociation temperatures (set II): T_D/T_c = 2.1 for J/psi, 1.5 for chi_c, 1.5 for psi'
* Dissociation temperatures (set I): T_D/T_c = 2.1 for J/psi, 1.16 for chi_c, 1.12 for psi'
* Initial pressure density p_i = 0.25 GeV^4 for SPS and RHIC, p_i = 4.5 GeV^4 for LHC
* Initial entropy density s_i = 2.009 GeV^3 for SPS and RHIC, s_i = 16.41 GeV^3 for LHC
* Speed of sound c_s from QPM; Reynold's number R used in cooling law; shear viscosity eta treated as nearly temperature-independent

## Context

This work extends the colour-screening model of Chu and Matsui (1988) and the authors' own previous model (Mishra et al., 2012) which used a bag model equation of state and ideal hydrodynamics. The key improvement is replacing the bag model with a thermodynamically consistent quasiparticle model (QPM) that better matches lattice QCD results, and adding viscous hydrodynamics. The model is compared with experimental data from NA50/NA60 at SPS, PHENIX at RHIC, and CMS at LHC. The authors note that other approaches (Yunpeng et al., 2011; Zhen Qu et al., 2011; Sharma and Vitev, 2012) use transport models with regeneration, but none simultaneously explain data at all three energies. This work enables a consistent QGP-only explanation of J/psi suppression across collision energies, with further extension planned for rapidity dependence and bottomonium states.
