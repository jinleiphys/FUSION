# Limiting attractors in heavy-ion collisions

> We study universal features of the hydrodynamization process in heavy-ion collisions using QCD kinetic theory simulations for a wide range of couplings. We introduce the new concept of limiting attractors, which are obtained by extrapolation to vanishing and strong couplings. While the hydrodynamic limiting attractor emerges at strong couplings and is governed by the viscosity-related relaxation time scale $τ_R$, we identify a bottom-up limiting attractor at weak couplings. It corresponds to the late stages of the perturbative bottom-up thermalization scenario and exhibits isotropization on the time scale $τ_{\text{BMSS}} = α_s^{-13/5}/Q_s$. In contrast to hydrodynamic limiting attractors, at finite couplings the bottom-up limiting attractor provides a good universal description of the pre-hydrodynamic evolution of jet and heavy-quark momentum broadening ratios $\hat q^{yy} / \hat q^{zz}$ and $κ_T/κ_z$. We also provide parametrizations for these ratios for phenomenological studies of pre-equilibrium effects on jets and heavy quarks.

## Key claim
The paper introduces the concept of "limiting attractors" obtained by extrapolating QCD kinetic theory results to vanishing and infinite coupling, showing that the hydrodynamic attractor (strong coupling) and the bottom-up thermalization attractor (weak coupling) are complementary limiting cases rather than contradictory descriptions of pre-equilibrium dynamics.

## Method
The authors performed numerical simulations of the effective kinetic theory of QCD (Arnold, Moore, Yaffe 2002) for a purely gluonic system, solving the Boltzmann equation in proper time with 1-to-2 splittings, 2-to-2 scatterings, and boost-invariant expansion. They extracted the pressure anisotropy, heavy quark momentum diffusion coefficients κ_T and κ_z, and jet quenching parameters q̂^yy and q̂^zz for couplings λ = 0.5 to 20 and two different initial momentum anisotropies (ξ = 4 and ξ = 10). Limiting attractors were constructed by linear extrapolation of observables at fixed rescaled times to λ → 0 (using the bottom-up time scale τ_BMSS) and to λ → ∞ (using the relaxation time τ_R).

## Key numbers
- Bottom-up thermalization time scale: τ_BMSS = α_s^{-13/5} / Q_s, where α_s = λ/(4π N_c)
- For λ = 0.5, η/s = 80; for λ = 20, η/s = 0.22
- Fit parameters for jet quenching ratio R_q̂(τ) ≈ q̂^yy/q̂^zz: c_1^q̂ = 0.12, c_2^q̂ = 3.45
- Fit parameters for heavy quark diffusion ratio R_κ(τ) ≈ κ_T/κ_z: c_1^κ = 0.093, c_2^κ = 1.33
- The bottom-up limiting attractor describes the anisotropy ratios after τ ≳ 0.01 τ_BMSS

## Context
This work extends the study of nonequilibrium attractors in heavy-ion collisions (Heller 2015, Blaizot 2017, Kurkela 2019) by explicitly constructing limiting attractors through coupling extrapolation. It connects the hydrodynamic attractor framework (Keegan 2015, Kurkela 2018) with the perturbative bottom-up thermalization scenario (Baier 2000) and provides parametrizations for pre-equilibrium effects on hard probes. The results enable phenomenological studies of jet polarization (Hauksson 2023) and heavy-quark observables in medium-sized systems like oxygen-oxygen collisions.
