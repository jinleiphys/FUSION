# Electric, Magnetic and Quadrupole Form Factors and Charge Radii of Vector Mesons: From Light to Heavy Sector in a Contact Interaction

> We present a detailed survey of electric, magnetic and quadrupole form factors of light and heavy spin-1 vector mesons. It complements our analogous analysis of the electromagnetic form factors of pseudoscalar and scalar mesons reported earlier. Our formalism is based upon the Schwinger-Dyson equations treatment of a vector $\times$ vector contact interaction and the Bethe-Salpeter equation description of relativistic two-body bound states. We compute the form factors, associated moments and charge radii, comparing these quantities to earlier theoretical studies and experimental results if and when possible. We also investigate the quark-mass dependence of the charge radii and find the anticipated hierarchy such that it decreases with increasing dressed quark masses. In addition, our analysis shows that the magnetic moment is independent of the mass of the light and heavy mesons. Our results agree with most measurements reported earlier, finding a negative quadrupole moment, implying the charge distribution is oblate.

## Key claim

The paper computes electric, magnetic, and quadrupole form factors for light and heavy vector mesons using a momentum-independent contact interaction model within the Schwinger-Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter framework, finding that the magnetic moment is independent of meson mass and that the quadrupole moment is negative (indicating an oblate charge distribution), with results that agree with most available experimental measurements where comparison is possible.

## Method

The authors employ a symmetry-preserving vector × vector contact interaction (CI) model, solving the gap equation to obtain momentum-independent dressed quark masses and the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation to compute vector meson masses and Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes. The electromagnetic form factors are then computed via the triangle diagram in the impulse approximation, using a dressed quark-photon vertex that satisfies the vector Ward-Takahashi identity. The model uses a mass-dependent coupling constant and ultraviolet regulator, with parameters fitted separately for light, heavy-light, and heavy quark combinations.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of pseudoscalar and scalar meson electromagnetic form factors (Hernández-Pinto et al., 2022, 2023) to the vector meson sector using the same contact interaction framework. It complements earlier theoretical studies of vector meson form factors using more sophisticated Schwinger-Dyson/Bethe-Salpeter treatments (Hawes et al., 1998; Bhagwat et al., 2006; Xu et al., 2019), lattice QCD (Owen et al., 2015; Shultz et al., 2015), holographic models (Grigoryan et al., 2007), and Nambu-Jona-Lasinio models (Zhang et al., 2024). The results enable future comparisons with experimental measurements at Jefferson Lab and the Electron-Ion Collider, and provide input for quark-diquark models of baryon structure.
