# Baryon-to-meson transition distribution amplitudes: formalism and models

> In specific kinematics, hard exclusive amplitudes may be factorized into a short distance dominated part computable in a perturbative way on the one hand, and universal, confinement related hadronic matrix elements on the other hand. The extension of this description to processes such as backward meson electroproduction and forward meson production in antiproton-nucleon scattering leads to define new hadronic matrix elements of three quark operators on the light cone, the nucleon-to-meson transition distribution amplitudes, which shed a new light on the nucleon structure.

## Key claim

The paper defines baryon-to-meson transition distribution amplitudes (TDAs) as new non-perturbative hadronic matrix elements that factorize hard exclusive amplitudes in specific kinematics, but the factorization is only conjectured to hold in the generalized Bjorken limit where both the photon virtuality and the center-of-mass energy are large, with the momentum transfer squared being small compared to these scales.

## Method

The authors introduce baryon-to-meson TDAs through baryon-meson matrix elements of nonlocal three-quark (antiquark) operators on the light cone, parametrizing the leading twist-3 nucleon-to-pion TDA in terms of 8 invariant functions depending on three longitudinal momentum fractions, the skewness parameter, momentum transfer squared, and factorization scale. They construct phenomenological models for these TDAs using a soft-pion theorem that relates them to nucleon distribution amplitudes in the ξ=1 limit, and a cross-channel nucleon exchange model that provides a D-term-like contribution in the ERBL-like support region. A "two component" model combines a spectral part fixed from the ξ→1 limit with a nucleon exchange contribution, implemented via a quadruple distribution representation.

## Key numbers

- The pion weak decay constant is f_π = 93 MeV
- The pion-nucleon phenomenological coupling is g_πNN ≈ 13
- The soft-pion limit model gives V_1^{(pπ^0)} and A_1^{(pπ^0)} = -1/2 × 1/(4ξ) times the nucleon DA, and T_1^{(pπ^0)} = -1/2 × 3/(4ξ) times the nucleon DA
- The cross channel nucleon exchange model includes a factor 1/(Δ² - m_N²) with the nucleon mass m_N
- Integrated cross section estimates for PANDA are shown for W² = 10 GeV² and W² = 20 GeV² with |Δ_T²| ≤ 0.2 GeV²

## Context

This work extends the formalism of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and baryon distribution amplitudes (DAs) introduced by Lepage and Brodsky (1980) and Chernyak and Zhitnitsky (1983) to define new hadronic matrix elements for processes like backward meson electroproduction and forward meson production in antiproton-nucleon scattering. The paper builds on earlier studies by Frankfurt et al. (1999) and Pire et al. (2004, 2005) and provides models that enable feasibility studies for experiments at JLab (backward electroproduction) and PANDA at GSI-FAIR (antiproton-nucleon annihilation to lepton pairs and mesons, or J/ψ and mesons). The approach enables tests of factorization universality by comparing TDAs extracted from space-like and time-like reactions.
