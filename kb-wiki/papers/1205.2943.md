# Charm-strange baryon strong decays in a chiral quark model

> The strong decays of charm-strange baryons up to N=2 shell are studied in a chiral quark model. ## Key claim

The chiral quark model, with parameters fixed from previous studies of charmed baryons and heavy-light mesons, successfully reproduces the strong decay widths of the well-established charm-strange baryons and then provides specific quantum-number assignments for the newly observed states, though many assignments remain tentative (e.g., "might be," "favors," "most likely") due to limited experimental data and overlapping possible configurations.

## Method

The authors used a non-relativistic chiral quark model with harmonic-oscillator spatial wave functions to compute partial decay amplitudes for two-body strong decays of charm-strange baryons up to the N=2 shell. The quark-meson coupling was treated via a tree-level effective Lagrangian expanded to non-relativistic order, with the global coupling strength Œ¥ fixed to 0.557 from prior work. Decay widths were calculated using standard constituent quark masses (m_u=m_d=330 MeV, m_s=450 MeV, m_c=1700 MeV), a harmonic oscillator parameter Œ±=0.40 GeV, and meson decay constants f_œÄ=132 MeV, f_K=f_Œ∑=160 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of charm-strange baryon strong decays performed in heavy hadron chiral perturbation theory by Cheng and Chua (2006) and in the ^3P_0 model by Chen et al. (2007) and Liu et al. (2007), which could only exclude some assignments. The chiral quark model approach had been previously developed and successfully applied to charmed baryons and heavy-light mesons by Zhong et al. (2007-2011). The predictions for newly observed states provide guidance for future experimental confirmation and for distinguishing between competing quantum-number assignments, particularly for Œû_c(2930), Œû_c(2980), Œû_c(3055), Œû_c(3080), and Œû_c(3123).
