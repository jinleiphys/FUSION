# Towards reliable calculations of the correlation function

> The correlation function of two identical pions interacting via Coulomb potential is computed for a general case of anisotropic particle's source of finite life time. The effect of halo is taken into account as an additional particle's source of large spatial extension. Due to the Coulomb interaction, the effect of halo is not limited to very small relative momenta but it influences the correlation function in a relatively large domain. The relativistic effects are discussed in detail and it is argued that the calculations have to be performed in the center-of-mass frame of particle's pair where the (nonrelativistic) wave function of particle's relative motion is meaningful. The Bowler-Sinyukov procedure to remove the Coulomb interaction is tested and it is shown to significantly underestimate the source's life time.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the standard Bowler-Sinyukov procedure for removing Coulomb effects from two-pion correlation functions significantly underestimates the source lifetime, and that the commonly used "fake covariance" treatment of the non-relativistic wave function as a Lorentz scalar produces numerically different results from a proper calculation performed in the center-of-mass frame of the pair.

## Method

The authors computed the correlation function of two identical pions interacting via the Coulomb potential using the Koonin formula applied in the center-of-mass frame of the pair, then Lorentz-transformed the result to the source rest frame. They used a Gaussian parameterization of the single-particle source function with anisotropic spatial sizes and finite lifetime, and included the effect of a halo as an additional large-radius source component. The Bowler-Sinyukov factorization procedure was tested by comparing the free correlation function expected from the input source parameters with the correlation function extracted from the full Coulomb calculation using that procedure.

## Key numbers

- Source parameters used for the anisotropic Coulomb correlation calculations: \(R_x = 2\) fm, \(R_y = 2\) fm, \(R_z = 3\) fm, \(\tau = 2.5\) fm, with pair velocity \(v = 0.9\) along the \(x\) axis.
- For the halo calculations, the fireball radius was \(R_f = 2\) fm and the halo radius was \(R_h = 60\) fm, with zero lifetime for both; three values of the halo fraction parameter \(\lambda\) were used: 1.0, 0.9, and 0.7.
- The spherically symmetric source used for the Bowler-Sinyukov test had radius \(R = 2\) fm and zero lifetime.
- The pair velocity for the "fake covariance" comparison was \(v = 0.9\), with a spherically symmetric source of \(R = 2\) fm and \(\tau = 0\).

## Context

This work extends the standard femtoscopy method by addressing three issues: relativistic treatment of the source function, the effect of a halo on Coulomb correlation functions, and the validity of the Bowler-Sinyukov correction procedure (Bowler 1991, Sinyukov 1998). The authors note that previous calculations of Coulomb correlation functions for anisotropic sources with finite lifetime were absent except for Monte Carlo studies using blast-wave models (Kisiel 2006). The results challenge the reliability of standard Coulomb subtraction methods used in experimental HBT analyses at RHIC, which have produced the "HBT Puzzle" of unexpectedly short emission times (Gyulassy 2001, Pratt 2003).
