# New mechanism for the enhancement of $sd$ dominance in interacting boson models

> We introduce an exactly solvable model for interacting bosons that extend up to high spin and interact through a repulsive pairing force. The model exhibits a phase transition to a state with almost complete $sd$ dominance. The repulsive pairing interaction that underlies the model has a natural microscopic origin in the Pauli exclusion principle between contituent nucleons. As such, repulsive pairing between bosons seems to provide a new mechanism for the enhancement of $sd$ dominance, giving further support for the validity of the $sd$ Interacting Boson Model.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a repulsive pairing interaction between bosons provides a new mechanism for producing almost complete $sd$ dominance in interacting boson models, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific choice of interaction parameters (model II with $\eta_l = l^2$) and only holds for ground states after a critical boson number is reached.

## Method

The authors construct an exactly solvable boson model using an $SU(1,1)$ algebra with bosons up to high even angular momentum ($L=12$), interacting through a repulsive pairing force. They solve the model using the Richardson method, obtaining the Richardson equations for pair energies, and compute ground-state occupation probabilities for $s$, $d$, and higher-spin bosons as functions of the pairing strength $g$ and the number of boson pairs $M$, comparing two scenarios: a standard pairing interaction (model I, $\eta_l = \varepsilon_l = l$) and a modified pairing interaction (model II, $\eta_l = l^2$, $\varepsilon_l = l$).

## Key numbers

- For a system of $M=5$ boson pairs (10 bosons) with maximum angular momentum $L=12$ and model II, the depletion (summed occupation of bosons with $l>2$) is negligible in the deformed phase, while for model I the depletion is small but non-negligible (Fig. 1).
- The critical boson number after which depletion decreases and $sd$ dominance improves is 12 for the standard pairing interaction (model I) and 6 for the modified pairing interaction (model II), for pairing strength $g=0.5$ (Fig. 2).
- The ground state solution of the Richardson equations for repulsive pairing has all pair energies in the interval $2\eta_s < e_\mu < 2\eta_d$, meaning $s$ boson pairs have opposite phase to all other boson pairs.

## Context

This work extends the exactly solvable model of Draayer (Ref. 6) from the $sd$ subspace to include higher-spin bosons, and generalizes the Richardson method (Richardson, 1960s) to boson systems. The results provide microscopic support for the validity of the $sd$ Interacting Boson Model (IBM) by showing that repulsive pairing, which arises naturally from the Pauli principle at the nucleon level (as in molecular physics and nuclear forces), suppresses higher-spin bosons. The work enables further study of the microscopic foundation of the IBM beyond vibrational nuclei, particularly for the $SU(3)$ and $O(6)$ dynamical symmetry limits.
