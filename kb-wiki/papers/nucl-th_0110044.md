# Three- and Four-body correlations in nuclear matter

> Few-nucleon correlations in nuclear matter at finite densities and temperatures are explored. Using the Dyson equation approach leads to effective few-body equations that include self energy corrections and Pauli blocking factors in a systematic way. Examples given are the nucleon deuteron in-medium reaction rates, few-body bound states including the $\ga$-particle, and $\ga$-particle condensation.

## Key claim

The Dyson equation approach can systematically derive effective in-medium few-body equations that include self-energy corrections and Pauli blocking factors, but the results depend on approximations such as using only the cluster mean field contribution and evaluating the density operator for an uncorrelated medium.

## Method

The author uses the Dyson equation approach to decouple the many-particle hierarchy and derive effective few-body equations for nuclear matter at finite densities and temperatures. The method incorporates medium effects through Pauli blocking factors and self-energy corrections in the resolvents, leading to in-medium Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations for three- and four-body systems. The two-body input is given by the Feynman-Galitskii equation, and the three- and four-body bound states are solved using homogeneous AGS equations with medium-dependent form factors.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron production net effect (gain-minus-loss) in BUU simulation of central collision of 129Xe+119Sn at 50 MeV/A: in-medium rates (solid line) show larger effect than isolated rates (dashed line), with the ratio of protons to deuterons closer to experimental data from the INDRA collaboration
- Binding energy dependence for different clusters shown at temperature T=10 MeV and at rest in the medium, with the difference between pole energy and continuum B(n,T)=E_pole-E_cont plotted as a function of density
- Critical temperatures of condensation/pairing leading to superfluid nuclear matter: dashed line from Ropke (1998), with possible area of alpha condensation (solid line) based on variational calculation using the 2+2 component of the alpha particle to evaluate condition B(T_c,mu,P=0)=4mu
- Critical temperature found by solving homogeneous AGS equation for mu<0 confirms onset of alpha condensation at higher values (dotted line)

## Context

This work extends the Bethe-Goldstone and Feynman-Galitskii equations for two-body correlations to three- and four-body correlations in nuclear matter, building on the quantum statistical approach of Fetter and Walecka (1971). The in-medium AGS equations are applied to heavy ion collisions studied by the INDRA collaboration (129Xe+119Sn at 50 MeV/A), showing that medium-dependent reaction rates lead to faster time scales for deuteron lifetime and chemical relaxation compared to previous calculations that neglected medium dependence. The results enable further investigation of alpha-particle condensation as an alternative state of matter to superfluidity from pairing, as suggested by Ropke (1998), and open questions about possible Efimov states near the Mott transition and the behavior of the homogeneous AGS equation for mu>0.
