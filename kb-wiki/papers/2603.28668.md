# Hadron spectra and thermodynamics for all quark flavors from a universal Hagedorn temperature

> We show that hadrons in QCD follow a spectrum determined by string dynamics characterized by a universal Hagedorn temperature linked to the string tension. While this behavior was recently established for light hadrons and glueballs, we demonstrate that the same dynamics describes the heavy-flavor sector. After separating the current quark masses, the resulting spectrum reproduces lattice QCD thermodynamics of charmed hadrons and the observed spectra of hadrons across quark flavors without additional parameters. These results reflect the universal confining dynamics of QCD through the string tension.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the exponential growth of hadronic states across all quark flavors (light, strange, charm, bottom) is governed by a single universal Hagedorn temperature, provided the current quark masses are subtracted from the total hadron mass to isolate the excitation energy of the confining flux tube. The hedge is that this universality holds only after separating the non-dynamical heavy-quark rest masses; without this subtraction, the Hagedorn spectrum fails to reproduce lattice QCD thermodynamics.

## Method

The authors construct a continuous hadronic density of states from open-string quantization, characterized by a Hagedorn temperature \(T_H = 323(3)\) MeV fixed from light-hadron thermodynamics in prior work (Marczenko 2026). They introduce mass thresholds for each hadronic sector (open-charm mesons, charmed baryons, hidden-charm mesons, and corresponding bottom sectors) and shift the string spectrum by subtracting the sum of current quark masses (\(m_u=m_d=0\), \(m_s=93.5\) MeV, \(m_c=1.273\) GeV) from the total hadron mass to obtain the excitation energy \(E = m - \sum_q n_q m_q\). The resulting parameter-free density of states is then used to compute thermodynamic observables (partial pressures and generalized susceptibilities) via the grand-canonical pressure integral, and cumulative spectral distributions are compared to Particle Data Group (PDG) listings and quark-model predictions.

## Key numbers

- Hagedorn temperature used throughout: \(T_H = 323(3)\) MeV, determined from light-hadron thermodynamics (Marczenko 2026).
- Chiral crossover temperature: \(T_c = 156.5 \pm 1.5\) MeV (HotQCD 2018).
- String tension from lattice: \(\sqrt\sigma = 0.485(6)\) GeV (Athenodorou 2020) and \(\sqrt\sigma = 0.4817(97)\) GeV (Brambilla 2022), corresponding to \(T_H = 0.335(4)\) GeV and \(T_H = 0.3329(67)\) GeV respectively.
- Current quark masses used: \(m_s = 93.5\) MeV, \(m_c = 1.273\) GeV (PDG \(\overline{\mathrm{MS}}\) scheme).
- Extracted bottom quark mass from threshold matching: \(m_b \simeq 4.5\text{--}4.6\) GeV.
- Open-charm meson threshold masses: \(D\) meson at 1.867 GeV, \(D_s\) meson at 1.968 GeV.
- Hidden-charm meson threshold: \(\eta_c\) at 2.984 GeV.
- Charmed baryon thresholds: \(\Lambda_c\) at 2.286 GeV, \(\Sigma_c\) at 2.453 GeV, \(\Xi_c\) at 2.468 GeV, \(\Xi'_c\) at 2.576 GeV, \(\Omega_c\) at 2.695 GeV.
- Bottom hadron thresholds: \(B\) meson at 5.279 GeV, \(B_s\) at 5.367 GeV, \(\eta_b\) at 9.398 GeV, \(\Lambda_b\) at 5.620 GeV, \(\Sigma_b\) at 5.811 GeV, \(\Xi_b\) at 5.793 GeV, \(\Xi'_b\) at 5.935 GeV, \(\Omega_b\) at 6.046 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the recent finding that a Hagedorn spectrum with \(T_H \simeq 300\text{--}340\) MeV describes light-hadron and glueball thermodynamics (Fujimoto 2025, Marczenko 2025, 2026) to the heavy-flavor sector, demonstrating that the same universal Hagedorn temperature governs charm and bottom hadrons after subtracting current quark masses. It contradicts the expectation that heavy quarks would introduce a different scale, and it enables a unified framework connecting hadronic spectroscopy, lattice QCD thermodynamics, and thermal hadron yields in heavy-ion collisions. The approach builds on string descriptions of confinement (Nambu 1974, Isgur 1984) and the quark-diquark model for baryons (Selem 2006, Fujimoto 2025), and it directly confronts lattice QCD results for charmed hadron thermodynamics (Kaczmarek 2025) and PDG spectral data.
