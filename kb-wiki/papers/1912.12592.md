# Spectrum of the strange hidden charm molecular pentaquarks in chiral effective field theory

> We calculate the effective potentials of the $Ξ_c\bar{D}^{(\ast)}$, $Ξ_c^\prime\bar{D}^{(\ast)}$ and $Ξ_c^\ast\bar{D}^{(\ast)}$ systems with the chiral effective field theory up to the next-to-leading order. We simultaneously consider the short-, intermediate- and long-range interactions. With the newly observed $P_c$ spectra as inputs, we construct the quark-level contact Lagrangians to relate the low energy constants to those of $Σ_c\bar{D}^{(\ast)}$ with the help of quark model. Our calculation indicates there are seven bound states in the $I=0$ strange hidden charm $[Ξ_c^\prime\bar{D}^{(\ast)}]_J~(J=\frac{1}{2},\frac{3}{2})$ and $[Ξ_c^\ast\bar{D}^{(\ast)}]_J~(J=\frac{1}{2},\frac{3}{2},\frac{5}{2})$ systems. Our analyses also disfavor the $Λ_c\bar{D}^{(\ast)}$ bound states. However, we obtain three new hadronic molecules in the isoscalar $[Ξ_c\bar{D}^{(\ast)}]_J~(J=\frac{1}{2},\frac{3}{2})$ systems. The masses of $[Ξ_c\bar{D}]_{1/2}$, $[Ξ_c\bar{D}^{\ast}]_{1/2}$ and $[Ξ_c\bar{D}^{\ast}]_{3/2}$ are predicted to be $4319.4^{+2.8}_{-3.0}$ MeV, $4456.9^{+3.2}_{-3.3}$ MeV and $4463.0^{+2.8}_{-3.0}$ MeV, respectively. We also notice the one-eta-exchange influence is rather feeble. Binding solutions in the $I=1$ channels are nonexistent. We hope the future analyses at LHCb can seek for these new $P_{cs}$s in the $JψΛ$ final states, especially near the thresholds of $Ξ_c\bar{D}^{(\ast)}$.

## Key claim

The paper predicts the existence of ten bound states in the strange hidden charm pentaquark systems, but the authors explicitly hedge that one state (the $[\Xi_c^\ast\bar{D}^\ast]_{5/2}$) "may be nonexistent at the upper limit" of the parameter range, and all predictions depend on the assumption that the low-energy constants extracted from the $P_c$ spectra can be related via a quark-model mapping to the strange sector.

## Method

The authors calculated the effective potentials of the $\Xi_c\bar{D}^{(\ast)}$, $\Xi_c^\prime\bar{D}^{(\ast)}$ and $\Xi_c^\ast\bar{D}^{(\ast)}$ systems using chiral effective field theory up to next-to-leading order, including contact (short-range), one-pion-exchange (long-range), and two-pion-exchange (intermediate-range) interactions. They determined the low-energy constants by fitting the observed $P_c(4312)$, $P_c(4440)$ and $P_c(4457)$ spectra and then relating these constants to the strange sector via a quark-level contact Lagrangian and SU(3) flavor symmetry. Bound states were identified by solving the Schrodinger equation with a Gaussian regulator cutoff around 0.5 GeV.

## Key numbers

* $[\Xi_c\bar{D}]_{1/2}$ mass predicted: $4319.4^{+2.8}_{-3.0}$ MeV
* $[\Xi_c\bar{D}^\ast]_{1/2}$ mass predicted: $4456.9^{+3.2}_{-3.3}$ MeV
* $[\Xi_c\bar{D}^\ast]_{3/2}$ mass predicted: $4463.0^{+2.8}_{-3.0}$ MeV
* $[\Xi_c^\prime\bar{D}]_{1/2}$ binding energy: $-18.5^{+6.4}_{-6.8}$ MeV (mass $4423.7^{+6.4}_{-6.8}$ MeV)
* $[\Xi_c^\prime\bar{D}^\ast]_{1/2}$ binding energy: $-15.6^{+6.4}_{-7.2}$ MeV (mass $4568.7^{+6.4}_{-7.2}$ MeV)
* $[\Xi_c^\prime\bar{D}^\ast]_{3/2}$ binding energy: $-2.0^{+1.8}_{-3.3}$ MeV (mass $4582.3^{+1.8}_{-3.3}$ MeV)
* $[\Xi_c^\ast\bar{D}]_{3/2}$ binding energy: $-7.5^{+4.2}_{-5.3}$ MeV (mass $4502.9^{+4.2}_{-5.3}$ MeV)
* $[\Xi_c^\ast\bar{D}^\ast]_{1/2}$ binding energy: $-17.0^{+6.7}_{-7.5}$ MeV (mass $4635.4^{+6.7}_{-7.5}$ MeV)
* $[\Xi_c^\ast\bar{D}^\ast]_{3/2}$ binding energy: $-8.0^{+4.5}_{-5.6}$ MeV (mass $4644.4^{+4.5}_{-5.6}$ MeV)
* $[\Xi_c^\ast\bar{D}^\ast]_{5/2}$ binding energy: $-0.7^{+0.7}_{-2.2}$ MeV (mass $4651.7^{+0.7}_{-2.2}$ MeV; marked as possibly nonexistent)
* One-eta-exchange contributes only about 1% correction to binding energies
* No bound states found in any $I=1$ channels

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous chiral EFT study of $\Sigma_c\bar{D}^{(\ast)}$ and $\Sigma_c^\ast\bar{D}^{(\ast)}$ systems (Wang, Meng, Zhu, 2019) to the strange sector, predicting $P_{cs}$ pentaquarks as partners of the $P_c$ states observed by LHCb (Aaij et al., 2019). The results contradict the absence of $\Lambda_c\bar{D}^{(\ast)}$ bound states, which the authors attribute to vanishing isospin-isospin interaction in the $\Lambda_c$ case, and are consistent with a recent local hidden gauge approach study (Xiao et al., 2019). The predicted states could be searched for in $J/\psi\Lambda$ final states at LHCb, particularly near the $\Xi_c\bar{D}^{(\ast)}$ thresholds.
