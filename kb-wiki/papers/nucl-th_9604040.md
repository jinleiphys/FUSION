# Inclusion of Quantum Fluctuations in Wave Packet Dynamics

> We discuss a method by which quantum fluctuations can be included in microscopic transport models based on wave packets that are not energy eigenstates. By including the next-to-leading order term in the cumulant expansion of the statistical weight, which corresponds to the wave packets having Poisson energy distributions, we obtain a much improved global description of the quantum statistical properties of the many-body system. In the case of atomic nuclei, exemplified by 12C and 40Ca, the standard liquid-drop results are reproduced at low temperatures and a phase transformation to a fragment gas occurs as the temperature is raised. The treatment can be extended to dynamical scenarios by means of a Langevin force emulating the transitions between the wave packets. The general form of the associated transport coefficients is derived and it is shown that the appropriate microcanonical equilibrium distribution is achieved in the course of the time evolution. Finally, invoking Fermi's golden rule, we derive specific expressions for the transport coefficients and verify that they satisfy the fluctuation-dissipation theorem.

## Key claim

By including the next-to-leading order term in the cumulant expansion of the statistical weight (corresponding to wave packets having Poisson energy distributions), the quantum statistical properties of many-body systems can be much better reproduced than with the standard mean-field approximation, though the treatment remains approximate and relies on the harmonic approximation for the energy dependence.

## Method

The authors start from the exact quantal partition function expressed as an integral over wave packet parameters and approximate the statistical weight using a first-order logarithmic expansion (harmonic approximation) of the beta-dependent energy expectation. They then apply this improved statistical weight within the Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (AMD) framework, using Metropolis sampling to evaluate the 6A-dimensional integrals over wave packet parameter space for canonical ensembles of 12C and 40Ca nuclei. For the dynamical extension, they derive a Langevin force based on Fermi's golden rule and verify that the resulting transport coefficients satisfy the fluctuation-dissipation theorem.

## Key numbers

- For 12C, calculations were performed with radius constants r0 = 1.2, 1.5, and 2.0 fm, corresponding to freeze-out densities of about 20% of normal nuclear density for r0 = 2.0 fm
- For 40Ca, the level-density parameter used was a = A/(8 MeV)
- The sample size for each scenario in the 12C calculations was 5 x 10^4 states
- For the harmonic oscillator test case, about 10^4 states were sampled for each temperature by means of the Metropolis method
- The effective level spacing D is defined as D = sigma^2_E / H, where sigma^2_E is the energy variance and H is the mean energy of the wave packet

## Context

This work extends the authors' initial study (Ohnishi and Randrup, 1995) which showed that standard AMD exhibits classical statistical properties and suggested including energy fluctuations. It addresses counterclaims by Schnack and Feldmeier (SF) and Ono and Horiuchi (OH) who argued that wave packet systems already have quantal statistical properties. The treatment enables dynamical simulations with quantum fluctuations via a Langevin force, applicable to nuclear multifragmentation and the liquid-gas phase transition, and builds on prior work by Fai on level-density parameters and by Abe on nuclear fission with fluctuations.
