# $Ψ(2s)$ production in $p+A$ collisions

> We have analyzed the data on $ψ'$ production in proton-nucleus ($p+A$) collisions, available from the NA50 Collaboration in the SPS energy domain. The investigated data sets include the absolute production cross sections as well as $ψ'$-to-Drell Yan (DY) cross section ratios. An adapted version of two component QVZ model has been employed to calculate $ψ'$ production cross sections. For both $ψ'$ and DY production, nuclear modifications to the free nucleon structure functions are taken into account. For $ψ'$, final state interaction of the produced $c\bar{c}$ pairs with the nuclear medium is also taken into account, in accordance with the previously analyzed $J/ψ$ data. A satisfactory description of the data in $p+A$ collisions is obtained. Model calculations are extrapolated to low energy regime to predict the $ψ'$ suppression in proton induced collisions at near threshold energies.

## Key claim
The adapted QVZ model, previously successful for J/ψ production, provides a satisfactory description of ψ(2S) production data in p+A collisions at SPS energies, but the analysis is complicated by the lack of p+p data for ψ(2S), forcing all free parameters to be determined from p+A data alone.

## Method
The authors used a two-component QVZ model factorizing charmonium production into leading-order perturbative QCD c-cbar pair production followed by non-perturbative resonance formation via either Gaussian or power-law transition probabilities. Nuclear modifications to parton distributions were included using LO EPS09 nPDFs, and final-state dissociation of pre-resonant c-cbar pairs was modeled via a q² shift proportional to the mean path length in the nucleus, with the ε² parameter fixed from previous J/ψ analysis. The model was fitted to NA50 data on ψ(2S) production cross sections and ψ(2S)-to-Drell-Yan ratios at 400 and 450 GeV beam energies.

## Key numbers
- α_F values for Gaussian parametrization: 1.07 ± 0.06 (400 GeV), 1.09 ± 0.07 (450 GeV HI), 1.20 ± 0.17 (450 GeV LI)
- α_F values for power-law parametrization: 1.5 ± 0.3 (400 GeV), 1.4 ± 0.3 (450 GeV HI), 0.95 ± 0.54 (450 GeV LI)
- Extrapolated α_F values for FAIR: α_F^(G) = 1.08 ± 0.04, α_F^(P) = 1.4 ± 0.2
- Extrapolated ε² values at 30 GeV: ε²_(G) = 0.44 ± 0.08, ε²_(P) = 0.31 ± 0.05
- Absorption cross section for ψ(2S) from Gaussian parametrization: σ_abs = 12.4 ± 2.2 mb (compared to J/ψ value of 10.1 ± 1.77 mb)
- Inclusive ψ(2S) production cross section in p+N collisions at 30 GeV: σ ≈ 0.1 nb
- Estimated ψ(2S) cross section in 30 GeV p+Au collisions: ≈ 15 nb (using α=0.95 parametrization)
- Charm quark mass: m_C = 1.5 GeV; open charm threshold mass: m_D = 1.85 GeV

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous QVZ model analysis of J/ψ suppression in p+A collisions (Bhaduri and Bhattacharyya, 2016) to the ψ(2S) state, using NA50 Collaboration data from SPS. The model predictions for FAIR SIS100 energies (30 GeV proton beams) provide a baseline for cold nuclear matter effects needed by the CBM experiment, complementing HSD transport model studies of charmonium production in heavy-ion collisions at similar energies. The results are also relevant for future facilities like NICA and J-PARC investigating charmonium production near threshold energies.
