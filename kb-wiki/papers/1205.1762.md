# Thermodynamical Consistency of Excluded Volume Hadron Gas Models

> The new excluded volume hadron gas model by Singh et al. [1-7] is critically discussed. We demonstrate that in this model the results obtained from relations between thermodynamical quantities disagree with the corresponding results obtained by statistical ensemble averaging. Thus, the model does not satisfy the requirements of thermodynamical consistency.

# Thermodynamical Consistency of Excluded Volume Hadron Gas Models

## Key claim
The new excluded volume hadron gas model (NEVM) by Singh et al. is not thermodynamically consistent: the results obtained from thermodynamic relations between pressure, particle number density, and energy density disagree with the corresponding results obtained by direct statistical ensemble averaging, contrary to the authors' claims.

## Method
The author examines the grand canonical ensemble (GCE) partition function proposed in NEVM, which substitutes the system volume V by the available volume V(1 - v n) where n is the particle number density and v is the proper volume parameter. For a single particle type obeying Boltzmann statistics, the author derives the pressure from the partition function and then computes the particle number density using the thermodynamic relation n = ∂p/∂μ. The author compares these results to the statistical averages ⟨N⟩ and ⟨E⟩ obtained directly from the partition function via the standard GCE averaging formulas, showing they are not equal. The author contrasts this with the thermodynamically consistent excluded volume model of Ref. [vdw], which uses (V - vN) with a theta function in the canonical partition function and obtains pressure from a Laplace transform method.

## Key numbers
- The NEVM pressure is given by p = (1 - vn) p_id, where p_id is the ideal gas pressure and n is the particle number density.
- The NEVM particle number density satisfies n = (1 - vn - vT ∂n/∂μ) n_id, where n_id = exp(μ/T) z(T) is the ideal gas number density and z(T) is the one-particle partition function.
- The thermodynamically consistent model yields the transcendental equation p = exp(-vp/T) p_id for the pressure.
- The thermodynamically consistent model yields n = n_id exp(-vp/T) / [1 + v n_id exp(-vp/T)] for the particle number density.
- The consistent model satisfies the van der Waals relation p (1 - vn) = nT.

## Context
This work extends the critique of thermodynamically inconsistent excluded volume procedures previously identified in Refs. [vd1, vd2, vd3], where the system volume was substituted by the available volume in all ideal gas thermodynamic functions. The paper specifically targets the NEVM of Singh et al. (2010-2012), showing that substituting V by V(1 - vn) in the GCE partition function destroys the correct structure required for thermodynamic consistency. The correct formulation, introduced in Ref. [vdw], uses (V - vN) in the canonical partition function with a theta function and obtains pressure from the farthest-right singularity of the Laplace transform, which can be extended to many particle species with different proper volume parameters as shown in Ref. [GY]. This enables reliable phenomenological modeling of hadron gas systems with conserved charges and variable particle numbers.
