# Pentaquarks in SU(3) quark model

> Based on the flavor SU(3) symmetry, we classify all the possible pentaquark baryons made of four quarks and one antiquark. In particular, we present possible multiplets of pentaquarks, their interactions with mesons, and the mass relations within a multiplet. We also study the pentaquark decays in the generalized OZI rule.

## Key claim

Based on flavor SU(3) symmetry, the authors classify all possible pentaquark baryons made of four quarks and one antiquark, but they note that the existence of pentaquark baryons is not fully confirmed as some experiments report null results for those states.

## Method

The authors use SU(3) flavor symmetry to classify pentaquark multiplets from the product of four quark triplets and one antiquark triplet, yielding 91 possible ground-state pentaquarks. They construct SU(3)-symmetric Lagrangians for pentaquark interactions with baryon octets, baryon decuplets, and meson octets using tensor notation. Mass relations within each multiplet are derived by including the hypercharge matrix Y to leading order in SU(3) breaking, and decay modes are studied under the generalized OZI rule with ideal mixing.

## Key numbers

- 91 different ground-state pentaquarks are predicted in total from the SU(3) decomposition: 35 ⊕ (3)27 ⊕ (2)10̅ ⊕ (4)10 ⊕ (8)8 ⊕ (3)1
- For the octet pentaquark, the Gell-Mann–Okubo mass formula is: 2(N₈ + Ξ₈) = 3Λ₈ + Σ₈
- For the antidecuplet, the equal-spacing rule is: Ξ₁₀̅,₃/₂ − Σ₁₀̅ = Σ₁₀̅ − N₁₀̅ = N₁₀̅ − Θ
- For the 27-plet, a GMO-type relation is: 2(N₂₇ + Ξ₂₇) = 3Λ₂₇ + Σ₂₇
- Under the generalized OZI rule, the f/d ratio for octet pentaquark interactions is fixed as f/d = 1/3

## Context

This work extends the classification of pentaquarks beyond the antidecuplet states predicted by the soliton model of Diakonov, Petrov, and Polyakov (1997) and the diquark-diquark-antiquark model of Jaffe and Wilczek (2003). The authors provide a systematic SU(3) framework for identifying both exotic and crypto-exotic pentaquark states, enabling future experimental searches to use the derived mass relations and selection rules. The paper also connects the generalized OZI rule to the ideal mixing and fall-apart mechanism proposed by Jaffe and Wilczek (2003) and Chaichian and Dobrev (2004).
