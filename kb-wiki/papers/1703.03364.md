# Variation of hadronic and nuclei mass level oscillation periods for different spins

> A systematic study of hadronic masses shows regular oscillations that can be fitted by a simple cosine function. This property can be observed when the difference between adjacent masses of each family is plotted versus the mean mass. This symmetry of oscillation is also observed for the nuclear level masses of given spin.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the mass differences between successive hadronic states (mesons and baryons) and nuclear level masses, when plotted against their mean masses, exhibit regular oscillations that can be fitted by a simple cosine function; the key qualifier is that this property is best observed when particle families are restricted to a given spin and at least five masses are available, and the fit often spoils over a few MeV for higher masses.

## Method

The author plotted the difference between successive masses, ΔM = m(n+1) – m(n), against the mean mass (m(n+1) + m(n))/2 for hadron families (mesons and baryons) and for nuclear level masses, using data from the Review of Particle Physics (Olive et al.) and nuclear data compilations (Lederer, Ajzenberg-Selove). The data were fitted with a cosine function ΔM = α0 + α1 cos((M – M0)/M1), where the period P = 2π M1, and the largest possible periods were selected. The analysis was performed separately for families with fixed spin and parity, requiring at least five known masses per family.

## Key numbers

- For N* (J=1/2) baryons, the oscillation period is P = 390 MeV.
- For N* (J=3/2) baryons, the oscillation period is P = 201 MeV.
- For N* (J=5/2) baryons, the oscillation period is P = 390 MeV.
- For Δ (J=1/2 and J=3/2) baryons, both families are fitted with the same period P = 201 MeV.
- For η mesons (J=0), the period is P = 622 MeV.
- For f0 mesons (J=0), the period is P = 647 MeV.
- For K0 mesons (J=0), the period is P = 691 MeV.
- For K1 mesons (J=1), the period is P = 408 MeV.
- For K2 mesons (J=2), the period is P = 201 MeV.
- For charmonium (c cbar) 1-- mesons, the period is P = 358 MeV.
- For bottomonium (b bbar) 1-- mesons, the period is P = 452 MeV.
- For 10B nucleus (J=2 levels), the period is P = 2.2 MeV.
- For 16O nucleus (J=0 levels), the period is P = 5.03 MeV.
- For 56Fe nucleus (J=2 levels), the period is P = 1.76 MeV.
- For 208Pb nucleus (J=6 levels), the period is P = 0.503 MeV.
- The three mean period ranges for hadrons are approximately 655 MeV, 385 MeV, and 195 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous study (Tatischeff, 2016) which first reported regular oscillations in hadronic mass differences without spin selection, by now restricting the analysis to families with fixed spin and parity. The paper also extends the same oscillatory analysis to nuclear level masses, motivated by the idea that nucleons in nuclei are bound by opposite forces analogous to those in hadrons. The results enable predictions of yet-unobserved hadron masses (e.g., next f0 mesons at approximately 2670 and 2760 MeV, next charmonium states at approximately 4805 and 5080 MeV) and suggest that the oscillation periods for nuclei show a dependence on mass number A, with larger periods near doubly magic nuclei such as 4He and 16O.
