# Electric dipole moment of light nuclei - 6Li, 7Li, 9Be, 11B, and 13C -

> In this proceeding, we present the results of the theoretical evaluations of the electric dipole moment (EDM) of light nuclei, including the preliminary value for the 11B nucleus. From the data, we can infer an approximate counting rule, and predict the EDM of other light nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper presents theoretical evaluations of the electric dipole moment (EDM) of several light nuclei using a cluster model, including a preliminary value for 11B, and from these results infers an approximate counting rule that can predict the EDM of other light nuclei; however, the counting rule does not apply to all nuclei, as demonstrated by the suppressed EDM of 13C due to its specific nuclear structure.

## Method

The author calculated the EDM of 6Li, 7Li, 9Be, 11B, and 13C using a cluster model where nucleons, alpha clusters (4He), and triton clusters (3H) are the relevant degrees of freedom. The CP-odd nuclear force was modeled using a one-pion exchange potential with three isospin structures (isoscalar, isovector, isotensor), and the CP-odd alpha-nucleon and alpha-triton potentials were obtained by folding the CP-odd nucleon-nucleon interaction. The nuclear EDM was expressed as a linear combination of the intrinsic nucleon EDM contributions and the polarization contributions from the CP-odd nuclear force, with coefficients extracted from the cluster model calculations.

## Key numbers

* 6Li: isovector coefficient a_pi^(1) = 0.022 e fm, with spin expectation values <sigma_p> = 0.86 and <sigma_n> = 0.86
* 7Li: isovector coefficient a_pi^(1) = 0.016 e fm, isoscalar coefficient a_pi^(0) = -0.006 e fm, isotensor coefficient a_pi^(2) = -0.017 e fm, with <sigma_p> = 0.9
* 9Be: isovector coefficient a_pi^(1) = 0.014 e fm, with <sigma_n> = 0.75
* 11B (preliminary): isovector coefficient a_pi^(1) = 0.02 e fm, isoscalar coefficient a_pi^(0) = -0.004 e fm, isotensor coefficient a_pi^(2) = -0.01 e fm, with <sigma_p> = 0.7
* 13C: isovector coefficient a_pi^(1) = -0.0020 e fm, with <sigma_n> = -0.33
* The alpha-nucleon polarization extracted from the counting rule is approximately (0.005 to 0.007) * bar_G_pi^(1) e fm
* Predicted EDM of 10B: approximately 0.03 * bar_G_pi^(1) e fm
* Predicted EDM of 14N: approximately 0.04 * bar_G_pi^(1) e fm

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of light nuclear EDMs, including the deuteron (Liu and Yamanaka), three-nucleon systems (Bsaisou), and earlier results for 6Li, 7Li, 9Be, and 13C (Yamanaka), by adding the preliminary 11B result and deriving a counting rule. The counting rule is based on constructive interference between the EDM of deuteron or triton clusters and CP-odd alpha-nucleon or alpha-triton polarizations, and enables predictions for nuclei such as 10B and 14N. The paper also notes that heavy nuclei like 129Xe (Yoshinaga) have much smaller EDM coefficients due to configuration mixing, supporting the conclusion that light nuclei are the most suitable candidates for storage ring EDM experiments.
