# Real-time nonequilibrium dynamics in hot QED plasmas: dynamical renormalization group approach

> We study the real-time nonequilibrium dynamics in hot QED plasmas implementing a dynamical renormalization group and using the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation. The focus is on the study of the relaxation of gauge and fermionic mean fields and on the quantum kinetics of the photon and fermion distribution functions. For semihard photons of momentum eT << k << T we find to leading order in the HTL that the gauge mean field relaxes in time with a power law as a result of infrared enhancement of the spectral density near the Landau damping threshold. The dynamical renormalization group reveals the emergence of detailed balance for microscopic time scales larger than 1/k while the rates are still varying with time. The quantum kinetic equation for the photon distribution function allows us to study photon production from a thermalized quark-gluon plasma (QGP) by off-shell effects. We find that for a QGP at temperature T ~ 200 MeV and of lifetime 10 < t < 50 fm/c the hard (k ~ T) photon production from off-shell bremsstrahlung (q -> q γand \bar{q} -> \bar{q}γ) at O(α) grows logarithmically in time and is comparable to that produced from on-shell Compton scattering and pair annihilation at O(αα_s). Fermion mean fields relax as e^{-αT t ln(ω_P t)} with ω_P=eT/3 the plasma frequency, as a consequence of the emission and absorption of soft magnetic photons. A quantum kinetic equation for hard fermions is obtained directly in real time from a field theoretical approach improved by the dynamical renormalization group. The collision kernel is time-dependent and infrared finite.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for semihard photons (momentum \(eT \ll k \ll T\)) in a hot QED plasma, the gauge mean field relaxes with a power law rather than an exponential, due to infrared enhancement of the spectral density near the Landau damping threshold; this result is obtained to leading order in the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation and relies on the dynamical renormalization group to resum secular terms that would otherwise break perturbation theory at long times.

## Method

The authors implement the dynamical renormalization group (DRG) within the Schwinger-Keldysh closed-time-path (CTP) nonequilibrium field theory, using the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation to compute the retarded photon self-energy and fermion self-energy to leading order in the electromagnetic coupling. They set up an initial value problem for the relaxation of gauge and fermion mean fields by adiabatically switching on an external source at \(t = -\infty\) and switching it off at \(t = 0\), then solving the resulting causal equations of motion via Laplace transform. For quantum kinetics, they derive rate equations for the photon and fermion distribution functions from the Heisenberg equations of motion, expand them perturbatively, identify secular terms, and resum them using the DRG to obtain renormalized kinetic equations.

## Key numbers

- For semihard photons (\(eT \ll k \ll T\)), the gauge mean field relaxes as \((t/\tau_0)^{-e^2 T^2 / (12 k^2)}\) with \(\tau_0 \sim 1/k\).
- For a quark-gluon plasma at temperature \(T \sim 200\ \text{MeV}\) and lifetime \(10 \lesssim t \lesssim 50\ \text{fm}/c\), hard photon production (\(k \sim T\)) from off-shell bremsstrahlung at \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha)\) grows logarithmically in time and is comparable to on-shell Compton scattering and pair annihilation at \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha \alpha_s)\).
- The total number of hard photons per invariant phase space volume at time \(t\) is \(N(t) = (5 \alpha T^3)/(18 \pi^2 k^2) \left\{ \ln[2k(t-t_0)] + \gamma_E - 1 \right\}\) for \(k(t-t_0) > 1\), with \(t_0 \approx 1\ \text{fm}/c\).
- Hard fermion mean fields relax as \(e^{-\alpha T t [\ln(\omega_P t) + 0.12652\ldots]}\) with \(\omega_P = eT/3\) the plasma frequency.
- The fermion distribution function relaxes with an exponent twice that of the mean field: \(e^{-2\alpha T (t-t_0) [\ln(\omega_P t) + 0.12652\ldots]}\).
- For soft photons (\(k \sim eT\)), the gauge mean field relaxes with a power law tail \(\propto \cos(kt)/t^2\) from Landau damping.
- For ultrasoft photons (\(k \ll eT\)), the mean field amplitude decays as \(e^{-\Gamma_k t}\) with \(\Gamma_k = 12 k^3 / (\pi e^2 T^2)\).

## Context

This work extends the real-time nonequilibrium treatment of hot QED plasmas beyond previous studies of scalar QED by Boyanovsky et al. (1998) and numerical work by Rajantie (1999), applying the dynamical renormalization group method developed by Boyanovsky et al. (1998, 1999) to spinor QED. It provides an alternative to the Bloch-Nordsieck resummation for fermion relaxation used by Blaizot and Iancu (1997) and Takashima (1997), and it enables a first-principles calculation of off-shell photon production from a thermalized quark-gluon plasma, which is relevant for interpreting photon signals in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
