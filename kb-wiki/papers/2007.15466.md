# Some Properties of the $QQq$-Quark Potential in String Models

> We propose a string theory construction which allows us to study properties of the potential of two heavy quarks coupled to a light quark. In such a case, the potential is a function of separation between the heavy quarks. The results show the universality of the string tension and factorization at small separations expected from heavy quark-diquark symmetry. In addition, we make an estimate of the string breaking distance. With the parameter values we use, this distance is found to be almost the same as that for the heavy quark-antiquark potential. We also discuss the heavy quark-quark potential and its relation to Lipkin rule.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a string theory construction for the $QQq$ system (two heavy quarks coupled to a light quark) and claims that the resulting potential shows universality of the string tension and factorization at small separations as expected from heavy quark-diquark symmetry, but these properties hold only up to linear terms in the separation $\ell$ and depend on the specific parameter values used in the model.

## Method

The author constructs a string theory model based on a deformed Euclidean AdS$_5$ geometry with a deformation parameter $\s$ and a scalar tachyon field T that describes light quarks at string endpoints. The total action includes Nambu-Goto strings, a baryon vertex (modeled as a five-brane wrapped on an internal space), and a tachyon field coupling at string endpoints. The $QQq$ potential is computed by extremizing the action with respect to string profiles and the positions of the baryon vertex and light quark, yielding a piecewise function of the heavy quark separation $\ell$ that is analyzed in three regimes: small, intermediate, and large separations.

## Key numbers

- String tension $\sigma = \g \ep \s$ is universal across quark-antiquark, hybrid, three-quark, and $QQq$ potentials, with model parameters $\s = 0.45\,\text{GeV}^2$, $\g = 0.176$, and $\ep$ the base of natural logarithms.
- The factorization $E_{\text{QQq}}(\ell) = E_{\text{Q}\bar{\text{q}}} + E_{\text{QQ}}(\ell)$ holds at small $\ell$ up to linear terms, where $E_{\text{Q}\bar{\text{q}}}$ is the heavy meson mass in the static limit given by $E_{\text{Q}\bar{\text{q}}} = \g\sqrt{\s}\bigl({\cal Q}(q) + \n \frac{\ep^{\frac{1}{2}q}}{\sqrt{q}}\bigr) + c$ with $\n = 3.057$ and $c = 0.623\,\text{GeV}$.
- The upper limit of the interval where the asymptotic small-$\ell$ formula applies depends on $\k$: about $0.2\,\text{fm}$ at $\k = -\frac{1}{4}\ep^{\frac{1}{4}} \approx -0.321$, and up to $0.8\,\text{fm}$ at $\k = -0.102$.
- The linear behavior of $E_{\text{QQq}}$ as a function of $\ell$ sets in for $\ell \gtrsim 0.25\,\text{fm}$ independently of the $\k$ value.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies of heavy quark potentials using the same string model, including the quark-antiquark potential (Andreev and Zakharov, 2006), the hybrid $\Sigma_u^-$ potential (Andreev, 2015), and the three-quark potential (Andreev, 2015). The model is not exactly dual to QCD but is intended as a guide when the string dual to QCD is unknown and lattice results are limited. The results enable comparison with lattice QCD studies of the $QQq$ potential by Suganuma (2008) and Bali (2013), which are limited to interquark separations not exceeding $0.8\,\text{fm}$ with Coulomb-like behavior at short distances. The paper also discusses the relation to the Lipkin rule (Lipkin, 1981) and finds that the rule $E_{\text{QQ}} = \frac{1}{2} E_{\text{Q}\bar{\text{Q}}}$ does not hold in this model.
