# Testing the impact of electromagnetic fields on the directed flow of constituent quarks in heavy-ion collisions

> It has been proposed that strong electromagnetic fields produced in the early stages of heavy-ion collisions can lead to splitting of the rapidity-odd directed flow of positive and negative hadrons. For light hadrons, the interpretation of such measurements is complicated by the low magnitude of directed flow as well as by ambiguities arising from transported quarks. To overcome these complications, we propose measurements using only hadrons carrying produced quarks ($\bar{u},\bar{d},s,\bar{s}$). We discuss how to identify the kinematics where such hadrons are produced via the coalescence mechanism and therefore their flow is the sum of the flow of their constituent quarks. With this sum rule verified for certain combinations of hadrons, the expected systematic violation of this rule with increasing electric charge can be measured, which could be a consequence of the electromagnetic fields produced in the collisions. Our approach can be tested with the high statistics data from Phase II of the Beam Energy Scan (BES) program at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC).

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the electromagnetic field-driven splitting of directed flow between positive and negative hadrons can be tested cleanly by measuring only hadrons composed entirely of produced (non-transported) quarks, provided the coalescence sum rule is first verified in a specific kinematic region where the directed flow of a hadron equals the sum of the directed flow of its constituent quarks.

## Method

The authors identify seven hadron species (K⁻, antiproton, antilambda, phi, anti-Xi⁺, Omega⁻, anti-Omega⁺) that contain only produced quarks (anti-u, anti-d, s, anti-s) and no transported u or d quarks. They construct linear combinations of these hadrons' directed flows such that both sides of each equation have identical constituent quark content and nearly identical total quark mass, but differ in net electric charge (Δq ranging from 0 to 7/3). By testing the coalescence sum rule using combinations with Δq=0 (e.g., v₁[antiproton] + v₁[phi] = v₁[K⁻] + v₁[antilambda]), and then measuring systematic deviations from zero in Δv₁ for combinations with increasing Δq, the method isolates electromagnetic field effects while avoiding complications from transported quarks.

- The proposed approach can be tested with BES-II data at RHIC, where 400 million events were collected at 11 beam energies in collider mode and 11 overlapping energies in fixed-target mode during 2019-2021.

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Das et al. (2016) to use charm-anticharm D⁰ mesons for electromagnetic field studies, but focuses on light-flavor hadrons that are more abundant and can be measured across the full RHIC Beam Energy Scan energy range. It addresses complications identified in prior STAR measurements (Adamczyk et al., 2014, 2017) where transported quarks in protons, pions, and kaons produce directed flow splitting that is indistinguishable from electromagnetic field effects. The approach enables systematic study of Δq-dependent splitting using BES-II data, complementing earlier measurements of charged hadron splitting in Cu+Au and Au+Au collisions at top RHIC energy (Adamczyk et al., 2016) and ALICE measurements at LHC energies (Acharya et al., 2019).
