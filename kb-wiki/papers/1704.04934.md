# Chemical freeze-out in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> One surprising result in relativistic heavy-ion collisions is that the abundance of various particles measured in experiments is consistent with the picture that they reach chemical equilibrium at a temperature much higher than the temperature they freeze out kinetically. Using a multiphase transport model to study particle production in these collisions, we find that the above result is due to the constancy of the entropy per particle during the evolution of the hadronic matter from the chemical to the kinetic freeze-out. We further use a hadron resonance gas model to illustrate the result from the transport model study.

## Key claim
The observed constancy of particle abundance ratios from chemical to kinetic freeze-out in relativistic heavy-ion collisions is explained by a constant entropy per particle during hadronic evolution, but this requires the hadronic matter to be out of chemical equilibrium (non-unity fugacities) after chemical freeze-out, contrary to the assumption of ongoing chemical equilibrium.

## Method
The authors used the string-melting version of the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model to simulate central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) and 7.7 GeV, tracking the time evolution of hadron yields, entropy per particle, and effective pion/nucleon ratios. They then employed a hadron resonance gas model to test three scenarios (constant pion/nucleon ratio, constant entropy per particle, or both constant) by varying the volume expansion rate and introducing non-unity fugacities for pion-like and nucleon-like particles.

## Key numbers
- Chemical freeze-out temperature \(T_{\rm ch}\) = 141 MeV at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV (at about \(t=8\) fm/c) and 134 MeV at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7\) GeV (at about \(t=6\) fm/c).
- Highest energy density in the central sphere (radius \(r=5\) fm) was about 0.65 GeV/fm\(^3\), corresponding to a highest temperature of about 152 MeV for both collision energies.
- The parton scattering cross section in AMPT was set to 1.5 mb, which reproduces experimentally measured collective flow.
- A total of 100 events were generated at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV and 1000 events at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7\) GeV.

## Context
This work extends the statistical model approach to chemical freeze-out (used by STAR, PHENIX, and LHC collaborations) by providing a dynamical explanation via the AMPT model. It contradicts the assumption that chemical equilibrium persists after chemical freeze-out, showing instead that non-unity fugacities are required to maintain constant entropy per particle and particle ratios. The study validates the use of the statistical model for extracting freeze-out temperatures and chemical potentials (as done by Cleymans et al. (2006) and Andronic et al. (2010)) while clarifying that the system is not in chemical equilibrium during the hadronic phase. It enables more accurate mapping of the QCD phase diagram in the temperature-chemical potential plane from heavy-ion collision data.
