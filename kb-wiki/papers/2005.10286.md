# Quark spin and orbital angular momentum from proton GPDs

> We calculate the leading-twist helicity-dependent generalized parton distributions (GPDs) of the proton at finite skewness in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model of quantum chromodynamics (QCD). From these (and previously calculated helicity-independent GPDs) we obtain the spin decomposition of the proton, including predictions for quark intrinsic spin and orbital angular momentum. The inclusion of multiple species of diquarks is found to have a significant effect on the flavor decomposition, and resolving the internal structure of these dynamical diquark correlations proves essential for the mechanical stability of the proton. At a scale of $Q^2=4\,$GeV$^2$ we find that the up and down quarks carry an intrinsic spin and orbital angular momentum of $S_u=0.534$, $S_d=-0.214$, $L_u=-0.189$, and $L_d=0.210$, whereas the gluons have a total angular momentum of $J_g=0.151$. The down quark is therefore found to carry almost no total angular momentum due to cancellations between spin and orbital contributions. Comparisons are made between these spin decomposition results and lattice QCD calculations.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the leading-twist helicity-dependent generalized parton distributions (GPDs) of the proton at finite skewness using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, and from these (together with previously calculated helicity-independent GPDs) obtain the Ji spin decomposition of the proton. The results show mixed agreement with lattice QCD calculations, with the NJL model overestimating quark spin and underestimating orbital angular momentum, suggesting that meson cloud effects (pion and kaon) are needed to resolve these discrepancies.

## Method

The proton is treated as a bound state of three dressed quarks using a quark-diquark approximation, where the Faddeev equation is solved with configurations containing both isoscalar scalar diquarks and isovector axial vector diquarks. The helicity-dependent GPDs are computed from axial bilocal lightcone correlators by evaluating Feynman diagrams with the bilocal operator inserted onto either a quark within a diquark or on the accompanying quark, using a convolution relation that folds the internal structure of the diquarks into the proton. The dressed quark GPDs are obtained by solving an inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation, and the results are evolved from the model scale to higher scales using leading-order QCD evolution kernels.

## Key numbers

* At the model scale of Q^2 = 0.16 GeV^2, the total quark spin S_tot = 0.358 and total quark orbital angular momentum L_tot = 0.142 from the full scalar+axial diquark model.
* At Q^2 = 4 GeV^2, the up and down quarks carry intrinsic spin S_u = 0.534 and S_d = -0.214, and orbital angular momentum L_u = -0.189 and L_d = 0.210.
* At Q^2 = 4 GeV^2, gluons carry total angular momentum J_g = 0.151.
* The isovector axial charge g_A = 1.551 at the model scale, compared to the experimental value of 1.2732(23).
* The scalar diquark mass is M_s = 576 MeV and the scalar diquark coupling G_s = 9.98 GeV^{-2} in the scalar-only model variant.

## Context

This work extends previous NJL model calculations of helicity-independent proton GPDs (Freese, 2019) to include helicity-dependent GPDs, enabling a complete Ji spin decomposition of the proton. The results are compared to lattice QCD calculations (Alexandrou, 2017) at physical pion mass, showing qualitative agreement on the sign and magnitude of the spin decomposition components. The findings enable future work incorporating meson cloud effects (pion and kaon clouds) to improve agreement with lattice results and address the overestimate of quark spin and underestimate of orbital angular momentum.
