# Evolution of non-stationary pulses in a cold magnetized quark-gluon plasma

> We study weakly nonlinear wave perturbations propagating in a cold nonrelativistic and magnetized ideal quark-gluon plasma. We show that such perturbations can be described by the Ostrovsky equation. The derivation of this equation is presented for the baryon density perturbations. Then we show that the generalized nonlinear Schr{ö}dinger (NLS) equation can be derived from the Ostrovsky equation for the description of quasi-harmonic wave trains. This equation is modulationally stable for the wave number $k < k_m$ and unstable for $k > k_m$, where $k_m$ is the wave number where the group velocity has a maximum. We study numerically the dynamics of initial wave packets with the different carrier wave numbers and demonstrate that depending on the initial parameters they can evolve either into the NLS envelope solitons or into dispersive wave trains.

## Key claim

Weakly nonlinear baryon density perturbations in a cold, nonrelativistic, magnetized ideal quark-gluon plasma are described by the Ostrovsky equation, which replaces the Korteweg-de Vries (KdV) equation that applies in the absence of a magnetic field. The Ostrovsky equation does not admit stationary soliton solutions under the condition of "normal dispersion" (beta*Gamma > 0), but quasi-harmonic wave trains can evolve into envelope solitons described by a nonlinear Schrodinger equation, provided the carrier wave number exceeds a critical value.

## Method

The authors start from nonrelativistic Euler and continuity equations for each quark flavor (up, down, strange) in a uniform external magnetic field, using a multi-fluid approach. They derive an equation of state (mQCD) that goes beyond the mean field approximation by including spatial inhomogeneities of the baryon density via Laplacian terms in the gluon field equation. Using the reductive perturbation method, they derive the Ostrovsky equation for baryon density perturbations, then obtain a generalized nonlinear Schrodinger (NLS) equation for quasi-harmonic wave trains. They perform direct numerical simulations of the dimensionless Ostrovsky equation using a finite-difference scheme, studying the evolution of initial envelope soliton and Gaussian pulse perturbations.

## Key numbers

- The critical wave number for modulation instability is \(k_m = (\Gamma/3\beta)^{1/4}\), where the group velocity has a maximum.
- The wave number for maximum modulation instability growth rate is \(k_c = (3\Gamma/4\beta)^{1/4}\), giving \(k_c/k_m = \sqrt{3/2} \approx 1.22\).
- The relative difference between critical wave numbers is \((k_c - k_m)/k_m = \sqrt{3/2} - 1 \approx 0.22\).
- For the numerical simulation of the envelope soliton with amplitude \(A_0 = 1\) and \(k = k_m = 1/\sqrt[4]{3}\), the pulse amplitude decays approximately as \(A(\tau) = A_0 e^{-0.017\tau}\).
- The Gaussian pulse simulation used amplitude \(A_0 = 0.1\) and dimensionless wave number \(k_c = (3/4)^{1/4}\) on a spatial interval \(L = 2000\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nonlinear waves in quark-gluon plasma, where Fogaça et al. (2011) showed that weakly nonlinear perturbations in unmagnetized QGP are described by the KdV equation, and Fogaça et al. (2018) studied the reduced Ostrovsky equation (ROE) in a mean field approximation. The present paper goes beyond the mean field approach by including spatial inhomogeneity via Laplacian terms, deriving the full Ostrovsky equation. The results enable the study of envelope soliton formation in magnetized QGP, with potential applications to deconfined cold quark matter in compact stars and to quark-gluon plasma formed in heavy ion collisions at facilities such as FAIR and NICA. The numerical analysis follows the approach of Grimshaw et al. (2016) and Whitfield et al. on the terminal decay of KdV solitons and subsequent envelope soliton formation.
