# Stellar $β$-decay rate of $s$-process branching-point $^{204}$Tl: forbidden transitions

> We propose a theoretical method to calculate the stellar $β$-decay rates of nuclei in stellar environments with high temperature and density, based on the projected shell model, where contributions from both allowed and first-forbidden transitions are taken into account. As the first example, the stellar $β$-decay rate of one of the last $s$-process branching-point nuclei, $^{204}$Tl, is calculated and studied, where all related transitions are first-forbidden transitions. For the terrestrial case, the ground-state to ground-state transition is unique first-forbidden transition, which is described reasonably by our calculations. At the typical $s$-process temperature ($T\approx 0.3$ GK), non-unique first-forbidden transitions from thermally populated excited states of the parent nucleus are involved, the effective rate from our calculations is much lower than the one from the widely used data tables by Takahashi and Yokoi. Effect of the quenching factors for nuclear matrix elements in first-forbidden transitions on the stellar $β$-decay rates is discussed as well.

## Key claim

The stellar beta-decay rate of 204Tl at typical s-process temperatures (around 0.3 GK) is much lower than the widely used Takahashi and Yokoi (1987) data tables predict, by about a factor of five without quenching factors and by about 1.5 orders of magnitude when appropriate quenching factors for nuclear matrix elements are adopted.

## Method

The authors developed a theoretical method based on the projected shell model (PSM) to calculate stellar beta-decay rates, incorporating both allowed Gamow-Teller and first-forbidden transitions. They computed nuclear matrix elements for first-forbidden transitions using angular-momentum-projected wave functions, then calculated the stellar beta-decay rate of 204Tl by summing over thermally populated initial states following the Boltzmann distribution, including the shape factor C(W) for each transition.

## Key numbers

- The terrestrial ground-state to ground-state transition of 204Tl has log ft = 10.10 experimentally; the PSM calculation without quenching gives log ft = 9.98, and with quenching factors reproduces the data reasonably
- The Q value for 204Tl beta decay is 764 keV
- The first and second excited states of 204Tl lie at 140 keV and 146 keV respectively
- At the typical s-process temperature T = 0.3 GK, the PSM rate without quenching is lower than the TY87 rate by about a factor of five; with quenching the reduction is about 1.5 orders of magnitude
- The electron number density adopted was rho Ye = 1.66 x 10^2 mol/cm^3, corresponding to n_e = 10^26 cm^-3
- At peak temperature T = 0.5 GK, only about 3% of the population is in excited states, with the 1- state having about 2.3% population probability
- The 1- to 0+ transition has log ft = 6.65 in the PSM calculation with quenching
- The quenching factors adopted are: f_q(xi' nu) = 1.266, f_q(w) = f_q(w') = 0.66, f_q(x) = f_q(x') = 0.51, f_q(u) = f_q(u') = 0.38, f_q(z) = 0.867

## Context

This work extends the projected shell model (PSM) framework, previously developed for allowed Gamow-Teller transitions and high-spin physics, to include first-forbidden transitions for stellar weak-interaction rates. It directly challenges the empirical estimates of Takahashi and Yokoi (1987), which have been the standard data tables for s-process branching-point studies and which estimated transition strengths from excited states using systematics rather than microscopic calculations. The results affect the interpretation of 204Tl as an s-process thermometer and the use of 204Pb and 205Pb as chronometers of the last s-process events, and the authors note that the quenching problem for first-forbidden transitions requires further investigation as experimental neutron-capture cross sections improve.
