# Investigation of doublet-bands in $^{124,126,130,132}$Cs odd-odd nuclei using triaxial projected shell model approach

> Doublet bands observed in $^{124,126,130,132}$Cs isotopes are studied using the recently developed multi-quasiparticle microscopic triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) approach. It is shown that TPSM results for energies and transition probabilities are in good agreement with known energies and the recently measured extensive data on transition probabilities for the bands in $^{126}$Cs. In particular, it is demonstrated that characteristics transition probabilities expected for the doublet bands to originate from the chiral symmetry breaking are well reproduced in the present work. The calculated energies for $^{124,130,132}$Cs are also shown to be in reasonable agreement with the available experimental data. Furthermore, a complete set of the calculated transition probabilities is provided for the doublet bands in $^{124,130,132}$Cs isotopes.

## Key claim

The triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) approach reproduces the characteristic transition probabilities expected for doublet bands originating from chiral symmetry breaking in \(^{124,126,130,132}\)Cs, but the near degeneracy of the doublet bands alone does not necessarily imply chiral symmetry breaking, the electromagnetic transition probabilities provide the critical test.

## Method

The authors employed the multi-quasiparticle triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) approach, constructing intrinsic states from one-neutron plus one-proton quasiparticle configurations using a triaxial Nilsson Hamiltonian with axial deformation \(\epsilon\) and triaxial deformation \(\epsilon'\) parameters. Three-dimensional angular-momentum projection was applied to restore rotational symmetry, followed by diagonalization of a shell model Hamiltonian containing quadrupole-quadrupole, monopole-pairing, and quadrupole-pairing interactions. Transition probabilities were calculated using effective charges \(e^\pi = 1.5e\) and \(e^\nu = 0.5e\) with spin g-factors \(g_s^\pi = 5.586 \times 0.85\) and \(g_s^\nu = -3.826 \times 0.85\).

## Key numbers

- Axial deformation parameters: \(\epsilon = 0.256\) for \(^{124}\)Cs, \(\epsilon = 0.260\) for \(^{126}\)Cs, \(\epsilon = 0.160\) for \(^{130}\)Cs, \(\epsilon = 0.170\) for \(^{132}\)Cs
- Triaxial deformation parameters: \(\epsilon' = 0.170\) for \(^{124}\)Cs, \(\epsilon' = 0.150\) for \(^{126}\)Cs, \(\epsilon' = 0.145\) for \(^{130}\)Cs, \(\epsilon' = 0.150\) for \(^{132}\)Cs, corresponding approximately to \(\gamma \sim 30^\circ\)
- Monopole-pairing force constants: \(G_M^\nu = [20.12 - 13.13 (N-Z)/A] A^{-1}\) and \(G_M^\pi = 20.12 A^{-1}\)
- Quadrupole pairing strength: \(G_Q = 0.16 G_M\)
- For \(^{126}\)Cs, 13 lifetimes and 26 corresponding absolute transition probabilities were obtained from Doppler shift attenuation measurements (cited as EG12)
- For \(^{128}\)Cs, the energy separation between doublet bands is approximately 200 keV

## Context

This work extends previous TPSM studies of \(^{128}\)Cs (JG12) and provides a systematic investigation of four Cs isotopes where doublet bands have been observed. The paper addresses the long-standing issue that near-degenerate doublet bands do not necessarily originate from chiral symmetry breaking, as demonstrated by prior work (CM06, DT07). The study enables future experimental lifetime measurements for \(^{124,130,132}\)Cs to confirm the chiral interpretation, building on the more exhaustive transition probability data recently obtained for \(^{126}\)Cs (EG12) compared to the previously best-studied \(^{128}\)Cs system (EG06).
