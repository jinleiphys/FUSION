# Nucleon sea in the effective chiral quark model

> The asymmetries of both light-flavor antiquark $\bar{d}(x)-\bar{u}(x)$ and strange-antistrange $s(x)-\bar{s}(x)$ distributions of the nucleon sea are considered with more details in the effective chiral quark model. We find that the asymmetric distribution of light-flavor antiquarks $\bar{d}(x)-\bar{u}(x)$ matches the experiment data well and that the asymmetry of strange and antistrange distributions can bring about 60-100% correction to the NuTeV anomaly of $\sin^{2}θ_{w}$, which are three standard deviations from the world average value measured in other electroweak processes. The results on the correction to the NuTeV anomaly are insensitive to the inputs of the constituent quark distributions and the cut-off parameters. The ratios of $\bar{d}(x)/\bar{u}(x)$ and $s(x)/\bar{s}(x)$ are also discussed, and it is found that the ratio $s(x)/\bar{s}(x)$ is compatible with the available experiments with an additional symmetric sea contribution being considered effectively.

## Key claim

The effective chiral quark model can reproduce the measured light-flavor antiquark asymmetry \(\bar{d}(x)-\bar{u}(x)\) and simultaneously produce a strange-antistrange asymmetry that can account for 60-100% of the NuTeV anomaly in \(\sin^{2}\theta_{w}\), with the correction being insensitive to the choice of constituent quark distribution inputs and cutoff parameters.

## Method

The authors used the effective chiral quark model, in which constituent quarks fluctuate into Goldstone bosons (pions, kaons, and etas) plus recoil quarks, to compute parton distribution functions via convolution of splitting functions with constituent quark inputs. Two different sets of constituent quark distributions were adopted as inputs: a constituent quark (CQ) model and CTEQ6 parametrizations, with cutoff parameters \(\Lambda_{\pi}=1500\) MeV and \(\Lambda_{K}=900\) or 1100 MeV. The resulting quark and antiquark distributions were then used to evaluate the Paschos-Wolfenstein relation and the shift in \(\sin^{2}\theta_{w}\) for the NuTeV experiment.

## Key numbers

* The Gottfried sum calculated with \(\Lambda_{\pi}=1500\) MeV is 0.234, matching the experimental value of \(0.235\pm0.0026\).
* The probabilities for Goldstone boson fluctuations are \(\langle P_{\pi}\rangle=0.149\), \(\langle P_{K}\rangle=0.085\), \(\langle P_{\eta}\rangle=0.063\), with renormalization constant \(Z=0.682\).
* The strange-antistrange asymmetry can bring about 60-100% correction to the NuTeV anomaly of \(\sin^{2}\theta_{w}\), which is three standard deviations from the world average value of \(0.2227\pm0.0004\).
* The NuTeV measured value is \(\sin^{2}\theta_{w}=0.2277\pm0.0013\ (\text{stat})\pm0.0009\ (\text{syst})\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis by Ding, Xu, and Ma (2004) with more detailed calculations of both light-flavor and strange sea asymmetries within the effective chiral quark model. It contrasts with the meson cloud model results of Cao and Signal (2003), which found a small effect on the NuTeV anomaly, and is consistent with the light-cone baryon-meson fluctuation model of Brodsky and Ma (1996) that predicted a 30-80% correction. The results enable improved understanding of nonperturbative contributions to the nucleon sea and their impact on electroweak precision measurements.
