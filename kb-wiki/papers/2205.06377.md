# Dibaryon resonances and short-range $NN$ interaction

> The dibaryon concept for the nuclear force is presented, assuming that the main attraction between the nucleons at medium distances is determined by the $s$-channel exchange of an intermediate six-quark (dibaryon) state. To construct the respective $NN$ interaction model, a microscopic six-quark description of the $NN$ system is used, in which symmetry aspects play a special role. It is shown that the $NN$ interaction in all important partial waves can be described properly by a superposition of the long-range $t$-channel one-pion exchange and the $s$-channel exchange by an intermediate dibaryon. The developed model gives a good description of both elastic phase shifts and inelasticities of $NN$ scattering in all $S$, $P$, $D$ and $F$ partial waves at energies from zero to 600 - 800 MeV and even higher.The parameters of the intermediate six-quark states corresponding to the best fit of $NN$ scattering data are found to be consistent with the parameters of the known dibaryon resonances in those $NN$ partial configurations where their existence has been experimentally confirmed. Predictions for new dibaryon states are given as well.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the main intermediate-range attraction between nucleons arises from the $s$-channel exchange of an intermediate six-quark (dibaryon) state, rather than from conventional $t$-channel multi-meson exchanges, and that this mechanism can describe $NN$ scattering in all important partial waves from zero to 600-800 MeV, provided the parameters of the intermediate six-quark states are consistent with known dibaryon resonances where they have been experimentally confirmed.

## Method

The authors construct a two-channel formalism coupling an external $NN$ channel (with long-range one-pion-exchange potential) to an internal dibaryon channel representing a six-quark bag dressed by meson fields, particularly a light scalar $\sigma$ meson. They use a microscopic six-quark description based on the quark shell-model representation, where mixed-symmetry $|s^4p^2[42]LST\rangle$ states can transition to fully symmetric $|s^6[6]\rangle$ states via $\sigma$-meson emission, and they fit the parameters of the intermediate dibaryon states to reproduce both elastic phase shifts and inelasticities from SAID partial-wave analysis data.

## Key numbers

- The model describes $NN$ scattering phase shifts and inelasticities in all $S$, $P$, $D$, and $F$ partial waves at energies from zero to 600-800 MeV (lab.) and even higher.
- The $\mathcal{D}_{12}$ dibaryon resonance has an average mass of about 2160 MeV and width of about 120 MeV.
- The $d^*(2380)$ dibaryon state ($T(J^P)=0(3^+)$) has a mass of 2380 MeV (80 MeV below the $\Delta\Delta$ threshold) and a width of about 70 MeV.
- The $\sigma$ meson emitted from the $d^*(2380)$ state is renormalized to a mass of about 300 MeV and width of about 100 MeV.
- The $^3P_0$ and $^3P_2$ dibaryon resonances have masses of about 2200 MeV, found in the ANKE-COSY experiment on $pp \to (pp)_S \pi^0$.
- The isoscalar resonance found in $\gamma d \to d \eta$ has a mass of about 2.43 GeV and a narrow width of 34 MeV.
- An isovector resonance with a mass of about 2.65 GeV and width of 260 MeV has been reported in $pp \to (pp)_S \pi^0$ analysis.

## Context

This work extends the earlier "dressed-bag model" developed by the same group (Kukulin et al., 2001, 2002) to higher partial waves and inelastic processes, and it contrasts with conventional meson-exchange approaches that rely on $t$-channel multi-meson exchanges at short distances. The dibaryon concept builds on the SU(6) predictions of Dyson and Xuong (1964) and the Nijmegen-ITEP $q^4-q^2$ string-like model (Mulders et al., 1980; Kondratyuk et al., 1980s), and it enables predictions for new dibaryon states near di-hadron thresholds, including those near the $NN^*(1440)$ threshold and the $\Delta N^*(1440)$ structure.
