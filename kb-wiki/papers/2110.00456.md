# Nuclear symmetry energy components and their ratio: A new approach within the coherent density fluctuation model

> A new alternative approach to calculate the ratio of the surface to volume components of the nuclear symmetry energy is proposed in the framework of the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM). A new expression (scheme II) for the ratio is derived consistently within the model. This expression appears in a form more direct and physically motivated than the expression (scheme I) that was used in our previous works within the CDFM and avoids preliminary assumptions and mathematical ambiguities in scheme I. The calculations are based on the Skyrme and Brueckner energy-density functionals for nuclear matter and on nonrelativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock method with realistic Bonn B and Bonn CD nucleon-nucleon potentials. The approach is applied to isotopic chains of Ni, Sn, and Pb nuclei using nuclear densities obtained in self-consistent Hartree-Fock+BCS calculations with SLy4 Skyrme effective interaction. The applicability of both schemes within the CDFM is demonstrated by a comparison of the results with the available empirical data and with results of other theoretical studies of the considered quantities. Although in some instances the results obtained for the studied ratio and the symmetry energy components are rather close in both schemes, the new scheme II leads to more realistic values that agree better with the empirical data and exhibits conceptual and operational advantages.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new, more direct expression (scheme II) for the ratio of surface to volume components of the nuclear symmetry energy within the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM), which avoids the preliminary assumptions and mathematical ambiguities (including integrand singularities) present in the previous scheme I, and yields values that agree better with empirical data.

## Method

The authors derive a new expression for the ratio q(A) = S^S(A)/S^V(A) by first determining the ratio for individual "fluctons" (spherical nuclear matter pieces) using the droplet model relation, then weighting this flucton ratio by the CDFM weight function |F(x)|^2 obtained from self-consistent Hartree-Fock+BCS densities with the SLy4 Skyrme interaction. Calculations are performed for isotopic chains of Ni (A=74-84), Sn (A=124-156), and Pb (A=202-214) using Skyrme and Brueckner energy-density functionals, as well as nonrelativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock results with realistic Bonn B and Bonn CD nucleon-nucleon potentials.

## Key numbers

* For the Ni isotopic chain with Skyrme EDF, the ratio 1/q = S^V/S^S ranges from 2.07 to 2.36, while the previous scheme I gave kappa values from 1.53 to 1.70.
* For the Sn isotopic chain with Brueckner EDF, 1/q ranges from 0.94 to 1.16, compared to kappa values from 2.40 to 2.90 in scheme I.
* For the Pb isotopic chain with Bonn CD potential, 1/q ranges from 0.81 to 0.83, while scheme I gave kappa from 2.54 to 2.80.
* The new scheme II results for 1/q using Skyrme EDF (1.70-2.40) cover the empirical estimates of kappa from Danielewicz et al. (2003, 2004) of 2.0 to 3.0 from binding energies and masses, and from Dieperink and Van Isacker (2007) of 1.6 to 2.0 from masses and skins.
* The values of 1/q for Sn and Pb using Brueckner EDF and both Bonn potentials are close to the value of 1.14 given by Bethe (1971) and the value of 1.1838 by Myers and Swiatecki (1969).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous CDFM studies of nuclear symmetry energy components (Antonov 2016, Danchev 2020) by introducing a new scheme that avoids the singularities and assumptions present in the earlier approach based on the Danielewicz (2006) and Dieperink and Van Isacker (2007) formalism. The new scheme enables more reliable calculations of the volume and surface symmetry energy components and their ratio for finite nuclei, which are important for constraining the nuclear equation of state and understanding the density dependence of the symmetry energy relevant to neutron-rich nuclei and astrophysical applications.
