# Baryon Spectroscopy in Lattice QCD

> We review recent developments in the study of excited baryon spectroscopy in lattice QCD. After introducing the basic methods used to extract masses from correlation functions, we discuss various interpolating fields and lattice actions commonly used in the literature. We present a survey of results of recent calculations of excited baryons in quenched QCD, and outline possible future directions in the study of baryon spectra.

## Key claim

This review surveys recent lattice QCD calculations of excited baryon spectra, emphasizing that while ground-state masses are now understood at the few-percent level, the extraction of excited-state masses remains heavily dependent on the choice of interpolating fields, lattice actions, and analysis methods, with results for states such as the Roper resonance still contested and not yet definitive.

## Method

The authors review the construction of baryon interpolating fields for spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 states, the use of two-point correlation functions with parity projection, and the application of variational methods and Bayesian fitting to extract excited-state masses from lattice QCD simulations. They survey results from multiple lattice actions, including Wilson, clover-improved, domain-wall, fat-link irrelevant clover (FLIC), and chirally improved fermions, applied to both quenched and dynamical QCD ensembles, with particular attention to octet and decuplet baryons.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state baryon masses are now understood at the "few percent level" from lattice QCD calculations.
- Pion masses as low as ~180 MeV were reached in studies using overlap fermions with constrained-fitting methods (Lee et al.).
- Lattice sizes used in early pentaquark studies ranged from L = 1.2–2.7 fm (standard Wilson action) to L = 2.2 fm and L = 1.8 fm (domain-wall fermions).
- The first positive-parity excitation of the nucleon (Roper resonance, N*(1440)) lies below the lowest odd-parity excitation in experiment, a puzzle that lattice studies have not yet definitively resolved.

## Context

This review extends earlier lattice work on baryon spectroscopy by the UKQCD collaboration (1993), Leinweber (using Wilson fermions and operator product expansion), DeGrand and Hecht (P-wave baryons with Wilson fermions), and Lee and Leinweber (parity projection for negative-parity states). It also covers more recent studies by the RIKEN-BNL group (domain-wall fermions), Richards et al. (clover fermions), the BGR collaboration (chirally improved and fixed-point fermions), and the CSSM Lattice Collaboration (FLIC fermions). The survey enables future work by identifying open questions, particularly the nature of the Roper resonance, the role of Goldstone bosons in hyperfine splittings, the missing-resonance problem, and the parity and spin of the lowest-lying pentaquark state, and by providing a technical foundation for constructing improved interpolating fields and correlation-matrix analyses.
