# Measuring charge fluctuations in high-energy nuclear collisions

> Various measures of charge fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions are discussed. Advantages of the Phi-measure are demonstrated and its relation to other fluctuation measures is established. To get the relation, Phi is expressed through the moments of multiplicity distribution. We study how the measures act in the case of a `background' model which represents the classical hadron gas in equilibrium. The model assumes statistical particle production constrained by charge conservation. It also takes into account both the effect of incomplete experimental apparatus acceptance and that of tracking inefficiency. The model is shown to approximately agree with the PHENIX and preliminary STAR data on the electric charge fluctuations. Finally, `background-free' measures are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Phi-measure, which is insensitive to collision centrality and statistical noise, can be applied to charge fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions; however, the background model of a classical hadron gas in equilibrium with charge conservation approximately explains the experimentally observed electric charge fluctuations, leaving little space for dynamical effects such as those expected from quark-gluon plasma formation.

## Method

The author expresses the Phi-measure and the related Gamma-measure through the moments of multiplicity distributions for two-component and three-component charge systems. A background model is constructed assuming statistical particle production constrained by charge conservation, with binomial sampling to account for finite detector acceptance and tracking inefficiency, parameterized by the registration probability p and the ratio r of negative to positive particle multiplicities. The model predictions are compared to PHENIX and STAR data on electric charge fluctuations in Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 130 GeV.

## Key numbers

- PHENIX measured v(Q) = 0.965 ± 0.007 (stat.) - 0.019 (syst.) for the 10% most central Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 130 GeV, with background estimated at 20% (b = 0.2)
- STAR preliminary result: nu/nu_stat = 0.80 ± 0.03, independent of centrality, for pseudorapidity range -0.5 < eta < 0.5 and transverse momentum 0.1 < p_T < 2.0 GeV/c
- BRAHMS data: 3860 ± 300 charged particles produced in most central Au-Au collisions with 352 participants; 14 ± 2% appear in interval -0.5 < eta < 0.5, 10 ± 1.4% in -0.35 < eta < 0.35
- Estimated registration probability p = 0.11 ± 0.02 for STAR and p = 0.015 ± 0.003 for PHENIX
- Estimated ratio r = 0.93 ± 0.14 from BRAHMS data
- Background model prediction for PHENIX: v(Q) = 0.990 ± 0.003
- Background model prediction for STAR: nu/nu_stat = 0.89 ± 0.02
- For the symmetric case (Q=0, r=1) with full acceptance (p=1), Phi = -1 and Gamma = 0

## Context

This work extends the application of the Phi-measure, previously used for transverse momentum fluctuations by Gazdzicki (1992) and Appelshauser (1999), to charge fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions. It establishes relationships between Phi, Gamma, and other fluctuation measures proposed by Jeon and Koch (1999, 2000), Asakawa, Heinz and Muller (2000), and the STAR collaboration (Voloshin, 2001). The background model approximately agrees with PHENIX data (Adcox, 2002) and preliminary STAR data (Voloshin, 2001), suggesting that the observed charge fluctuations are dominated by statistical effects rather than dynamical signals from quark-gluon plasma formation. The paper enables future work to construct background-free measures by subtracting the model predictions, particularly for systems where the ratio r differs significantly from unity, such as baryon number fluctuations.
