# Analysis of directed flow from elliptic flow

> The directed flow of particles produced in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions at SPS and RHIC is so small that currently available methods of analysis are at the border of applicability. Standard two-particle and flow-vector methods are biased by large nonflow correlations. On the other hand, cumulants of four-particle correlations, which are free from this bias, are plagued by large statistical errors. Here, we present a new method based on three-particle correlations, which uses the property that elliptic flow is large at these energies. This method may also be useful at intermediate energies, near the balance energy where directed flow vanishes.

# Analysis of directed flow from elliptic flow

## Key claim

The paper presents a method for measuring directed flow ($v_1$) in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions using three-particle correlations that mix the first and second Fourier harmonics, under the condition that elliptic flow ($v_2$) is large enough to provide a reference. This approach offers a compromise between standard two-particle methods (which are biased by nonflow correlations when $v_1$ is small) and four-particle cumulant methods (which have prohibitively large statistical errors).

## Method

The method extracts $v_1$ from the three-particle correlation $\langle e^{i(\phi_1+\phi_2-2\phi_3)}\rangle \simeq (v_1)^2 v_2$, where $v_2$ is obtained from a separate analysis using standard techniques. The implementation uses a cumulant expansion to eliminate detector acceptance effects and generating functions of two complex variables to efficiently sum over all particle triplets in each event, with computer time scaling linearly with multiplicity. Both integrated flow (averaged over phase space) and differential flow (as a function of transverse momentum and rapidity) are extracted using the same formalism.

## Key numbers

- At SPS energies: $v_1 \simeq 2\%$, $v_2 \simeq 3\%$, multiplicity $M \sim 300$, with 50k events; relative statistical error on $v_1\{3\}$ is $\delta v_1\{3\}/v_1 \simeq 4\%$, compared to $2\%$ for $v_1\{2\}$ and $16\%$ for $v_1\{4\}$.
- At RHIC energies: $v_1 \simeq 1\%$, $v_2 \simeq 5\%$, multiplicity $M \sim 300$, with 50k events; relative statistical error on $v_1\{3\}$ is $\delta v_1\{3\}/v_1 \simeq 11\%$, compared to $7\%$ for $v_1\{2\}$ and $250\%$ for $v_1\{4\}$.
- The systematic error from nonflow correlations on $v_1\{3\}$ is reduced by a factor of at least $M v_2$ (approximately a factor of 10 at SPS) compared to the standard two-particle estimate $v_1\{2\}$.

## Context

This work extends the cumulant-based flow analysis methods previously developed by Borghini, Dinh, and Ollitrault (2001) for higher-order correlations, which eliminated nonflow biases but suffered from large statistical errors at four-particle order. The new three-particle mixed-correlation method exploits the fact that elliptic flow is large ($v_2 \sim 3-5\%$) at SPS and RHIC energies where directed flow is very small ($v_1 \sim 1-2\%$), following earlier observations of directed flow at Bevalac by Gustafsson (1984) and at SPS by the NA49 collaboration. The method enables reliable $v_1$ measurements at ultrarelativistic energies where standard two-particle techniques (Danielewicz, 1985; Wang, 1991; Poskanzer, 1998) are dominated by nonflow correlations, and where four-particle cumulants would require millions of events.
