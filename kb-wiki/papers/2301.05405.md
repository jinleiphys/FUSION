# Imprints of $α$ clustering in the density profiles of $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O

> The $^{4}$He nucleus is a well bound and highly correlated four-nucleon system that is also found in form of $α$-clusters as substructure in nuclear many-body systems. The standard single-particle shell model cannot represent these four-body correlations. It is highly desirable to find a simple way to identify and distinguish these fundamental structures without large-scale computations. In this paper, we investigate with intrinsic Slater determinants as trial states in how far these two competing pictures prevail in the ground states of $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O. The trial states can describe both the $j$-$j$ coupling shell-model and $α$-cluster configurations in a unified way. One-body density distributions of the trial states are calculated and compared to elastic scattering cross section data. The parameters of the trial state are chosen to reproduce the experimental charge radii. A well developed cluster structure is characterized by an enhancement of the differential elastic scattering cross sections, as well as the elastic charge form factors around the first peak positions. The density profiles of the internal regions can also be probed at higher momentum transfer regions beyond the second minimum. With these trial states one can visualize the competition between cluster and shell structure in an intuitive and simple way.

## Key claim

The one-body density profiles of $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O, calculated from simple trial states that can represent both shell-model and $\alpha$-cluster configurations, show that a significant amount of $\alpha$-cluster component is present in the ground states, with the evidence imprinted in the enhancement of elastic scattering cross sections and charge form factors near the first peak positions. The claim is conditional on the trial states being constrained only to reproduce experimental charge radii, without any Hamiltonian or variational optimization.

## Method

The authors constructed intrinsic Slater determinant trial states using the antisymmetrized quasi-cluster model (AQCM), which can continuously describe both $j$-$j$ coupling shell-model configurations and $\alpha$-cluster configurations by varying the inter-cluster distance $d$ and the cluster-breaking parameter $\Lambda$. For $^{12}$C, three trial states were considered: a shell-model type (S-type, $d=0.01$ fm, $\Lambda=1$), a pure $\alpha$-cluster type (C-type, $d=2.636$ fm, $\Lambda=0$), and a mixed type (M-type, $d=2.0$ fm, $\Lambda=0.2$); for $^{16}$O, only S-type and C-type (with $d=3.058$ fm) were used. The one-body density distributions were calculated after removing center-of-mass motion, and these densities were used as input to the Glauber model (optical limit approximation) for proton-nucleus elastic scattering cross sections and to compute elastic charge form factors via Fourier transform.

## Key numbers

- $^{12}$C charge radius: 2.33 fm (experimental value from Angeli (2013), used to constrain trial states)
- $^{16}$O charge radius: 2.57 fm (experimental value from Angeli (2013), used to constrain trial states)
- $\alpha$-cluster distance for $^{12}$C C-type: $d=2.636$ fm
- $\alpha$-cluster distance for $^{16}$O C-type: $d=3.058$ fm
- Diffuseness parameter for $^{12}$C: S-type = 0.432 fm, C-type = 0.401 fm, M-type = 0.423 fm
- Diffuseness parameter for $^{16}$O: S-type = 0.455 fm, C-type = 0.405 fm
- $\nu$ parameter for $\alpha$ particle: 0.2656 fm$^{-2}$
- $\nu$ parameter for $^{12}$C S-type: 0.1886 fm$^{-2}$
- $\nu$ parameter for $^{16}$O S-type: 0.1635 fm$^{-2}$
- Proton-nucleus elastic scattering calculations performed at incident energy 1000 MeV
- Proton charge radius used in form factor calculation: 0.878 fm (with $a_p^2=0.514$ fm$^2$)

## Context

This work extends the AQCM approach of Horiuchi (2022) to multi-$\alpha$ systems and provides a simple method to distinguish shell and cluster configurations without large-scale computations. It builds on prior studies of $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O using three- and four-$\alpha$ cluster models (Uegaki 1977, Kamimura 1981, Descouvemont 1993, Itagaki 1995, THSR), as well as recent *ab initio* calculations (Lovato 2013, Epelbaum 2014) and shell-model calculations (Otsuka 2022) that suggested mixing of configurations. The results enable future applications to heavier $4N$ systems and neutron-rich unstable nuclei using proton-nucleus scattering in inverse kinematics, and may impact the understanding of astrophysically important reaction rates such as the triple-$\alpha$ process involving the Hoyle state.
