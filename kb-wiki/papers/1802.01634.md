# Quantum anomaly and thermodynamics of one-dimensional fermions with three-body interactions

> We show that a system of three species of one-dimensional fermions, with an attractive three-body contact interaction, features a scale anomaly directly related to the anomaly of two-dimensional fermions with two-body forces. We show, furthermore, that those two cases (and their multi species generalizations) are the only non-relativistic systems with contact interactions that display a scale anomaly. While the two-dimensional case is well-known and has been under study both experimentally and theoretically for years, the one-dimensional case presented here has remained unexplored. For the latter, we calculate the impact of the anomaly on the equation of state, which appears through the generalization of Tan's contact for three-body forces, and determine the pressure at finite temperature. In addition, we show that the third-order virial coefficient is proportional to the second-order coefficient of the two-dimensional two-body case.

## Key claim

A system of three-species one-dimensional fermions with an attractive three-body contact interaction exhibits a scale anomaly directly analogous to the well-known anomaly in two-dimensional fermions with two-body forces; these two cases (and their multi-species generalizations) are the only non-relativistic systems with contact interactions that display a scale anomaly.

## Method

The authors map the three-body problem in 1D onto a one-body problem in 2D with a Dirac delta potential, establishing the equivalence between the 1D three-body relative motion and the 2D two-body relative motion. They use a lattice regularization with a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to represent the three-body interaction, and compute the pressure at next-to-leading order in perturbation theory, tuning the lattice coupling to match the exact third-order virial coefficient derived from the mapping.

## Key numbers

- The third-order virial coefficient shift for the 1D system is related to the second-order virial coefficient shift of the 2D two-body case by \(\Delta b_3 = \Delta b_2^\text{2D} / \sqrt{3}\).
- The binding energy \(\epsilon_B\) of the three-body bound state depends on the effective coupling \(\tilde g\) as \(\epsilon_B / \Lambda^2 = e^{4\pi / \tilde g}\), where \(\Lambda\) is a momentum cutoff.
- The single-particle partition function for the 1D three-flavor system is \(Q_1 = 3 L / \lambda_T\) with \(\lambda_T = \sqrt{2\pi\beta}\).
- The center-of-mass partition function for the 1D three-body system is \(Q_{3}^\text{CM} = \sqrt{3} L / \lambda_T\).

## Context

This work extends the study of scale anomalies in nonrelativistic systems, previously established for 2D Fermi gases with two-body contact interactions (Jackiw, 1991; Pitaevskii and Rosch; Hofmann), to a 1D system with three-body forces. The relationship between the anomaly and Tan's contact was spelled out for the 2D case by Drut et al. (2012), and the present work generalizes that framework to three-body forces. The results enable future many-body studies of the 1D system, including Monte Carlo calculations and exploration of a fermion-trimer crossover, as well as investigations of asymmetric population cases.
