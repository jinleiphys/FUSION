# Shape phase transitions and random interactions

> The phenomenom of emerging regular spectral features from random interactions is addressed in the context of the interacting boson model. A mean-field analysis links different regions of the parameter space with definite geometric shapes. The results provide a clear and transparent interpretation of the high degree of order that has been observed before in numerical studies.}

## Key claim

The dominance of ground states with angular momentum L=0 in the Interacting Boson Model (IBM) with random interactions can be explained by a mean-field analysis that links different regions of the parameter space to definite geometric shapes (spherical, deformed, or d-boson condensate), with the specific percentages depending on the boson number N and the sign of the relevant moments of inertia.

## Method

A Hartree-Bose mean-field analysis was performed using an axially symmetric coherent state trial wave function to map the IBM Hamiltonian onto a potential energy surface. The equilibrium configurations of this surface were classified into three shape phases (spherical, deformed, d-boson condensate), and the ground state angular momentum was determined from the rotational structure and the sign of the Thouless-Valatin moments of inertia. The results were compared with exact numerical diagonalizations for both schematic Hamiltonians describing shape phase transitions and a general random IBM Hamiltonian with nine parameters drawn from a Gaussian distribution.

## Key numbers

- For the U(5)-SO(6) schematic Hamiltonian with constant probability distribution of the control parameter: ground state L=0 occurs in 75% of cases for N even and 50% for N odd; L=2N occurs in 25% of cases; L=2 occurs in 25% of cases for N odd
- For the U(5)-SU(3) schematic Hamiltonian: for N=3k, ground state L=0 in 75% of cases and L=2N in 25%; for N=3k+1 and N=3k+2, L=0 in 50%, L=2 in 25%, L=2N in 25%
- For the random IBM Hamiltonian with Gaussian-distributed parameters: ground state L=0 occurs for approximately 63-77% of cases; L=2N occurs for approximately 13% from spherical/deformed solutions plus approximately 10% from d-boson condensate
- The sum of L=0 and L=2 percentages is constant (75%) and independent of N for all schematic and random Hamiltonians studied

## Context

This work extends earlier numerical studies by Johnson, Bertsch, and Dean (1998) and Bijker and Frank (2000) that found a statistical preference for L=0 ground states in shell model and IBM calculations with random interactions. The mean-field analysis provides a geometric interpretation of these results, linking the observed regular spectral features to the shape phases of the IBM. The same approach is noted to hold for the vibron model, while the nuclear shell model case remains an open problem.
