# Masses of excited baryons from chirally improved quenched lattice QCD

> Whereas ground state spectroscopy for quenched QCD is well understood, it is still a challenge to obtain results for excited hadron states. In our study we present results from a new approach for determining spatially optimized operators for lattice spectroscopy of excited hadrons. In order to be able to approach physical quark masses we work with the chirally improved Dirac operator, i.e., approximate Ginsparg-Wilson fermions. Since these are computationally expensive we restrict ourselves to a few quark sources. We use Jacobi smeared quark sources with different widths and combine them to construct hadron operators with different spatial wave functions. This allows us to identify the Roper state and other excited baryons, also in the strange sector.

# Masses of excited baryons from chirally improved quenched lattice QCD

## Key claim

Using a variational method with spatially optimized operators built from Jacobi smeared quark sources of two different widths, the authors identify the Roper resonance and other excited baryons in quenched lattice QCD, but caution that the excited state masses lie above experimental values and may be affected by the small spatial lattice size (1.8 fm) and the quenched approximation.

## Method

The authors construct a 12×12 correlation matrix from two types of nucleon interpolating operators (χ₁ and χ₂) combined with narrow and wide Jacobi smeared quark sources (half-widths σ ≈ 0.27 fm and σ ≈ 0.41 fm) on a 12³ × 24 lattice with Lüscher-Weisz gauge action at β = 7.9 (a = 0.148(2) fm). They solve the generalized eigenvalue problem to extract the leading eigenvalues, which correspond to the ground state, first excited state (Roper), and second excited state (N(1710)), and fit the exponential decays to obtain masses. For strange baryons, they fix the strange quark mass to reproduce the physical K-meson mass and compute Σ and Ξ masses using the χ₁ interpolator.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: a = 0.148(2) fm, determined from the Sommer parameter
- Spatial lattice size: 1.8 fm (12³ × 24 lattice)
- Quark source half-widths: σ ≈ 0.27 fm (narrow) and σ ≈ 0.41 fm (wide)
- Quark masses studied: a m = 0.02 to a m = 0.20 for u, d quarks
- Statistics: 100 gauge configurations
- Pion masses down to 250 MeV reached in previous tests of the chirally improved Dirac operator

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts by the BGR Collaboration (Brommel et al., 2004) that used three interpolating fields for the nucleon sector but could not resolve the Roper excitation. The method builds on the variational approach of Michael (1985) and Luscher and Wolff (1990), and on Jacobi smearing techniques from Gusken et al. (1989) and Burch et al. (2004). The results enable future finite-volume and scaling studies to separate quenched approximation effects from genuine finite-volume effects on excited baryon masses, and provide a framework for extending excited-state spectroscopy to dynamical fermion simulations.
