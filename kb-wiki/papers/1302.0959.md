# Shell evolution of N=20 nuclei and Gamow-Teller strengths of $^{30,32,34}$Mg by the deformed QRPA

> Gamow-Teller (GT) strength distributions of Mg isotopes are investigated within a framework of the deformed quasi-particle random phase approximation(DQRPA). We found that the N=20 shell closure in $^{28 \sim 34}$Mg was broken by the prolate shape deformation originating from the {\it fp}-intruder states. The shell closure breaking gives rise to a shift of low-lying GT excited states into high-lying states. Discussions regarding the shell evolution trend of single particle states around N=20 nuclei are also presented with the comparison to other approaches.

# Shell evolution of N=20 nuclei and Gamow-Teller strengths of 30,32,34Mg by the deformed QRPA

## Key claim
The N=20 shell closure in 28–34Mg is broken by prolate shape deformation originating from fp-intruder states, but this conclusion depends on using deformation parameters β₂ extracted from B(E2) data rather than those from relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations, which predict zero deformation for these nuclei.

## Method
The authors apply a deformed quasi-particle random phase approximation (DQRPA) starting from a deformed axially symmetric Woods-Saxon potential. Single-particle states are transformed from the Nilsson basis to the spherical basis, where an ab initio Brueckner G-matrix based on the Bonn CD potential is recalculated in the deformed basis. Deformed BCS and DQRPA calculations are then performed using deformation parameters β₂ taken from both RMF calculations and experimental B(E2) data.

## Key numbers
- Deformation parameter β₂ for ³²Mg: 0.473 (from B(E2) data) vs. 0.1 (from RMF)
- Deformation parameter β₂ for ³⁴Mg: 0.54 (from B(E2) data) vs. 0.192 (from RMF)
- Deformation parameter β₂ for ³⁰Mg: 0.431 (from B(E2) data) vs. 0.17 (from RMF)
- Empirical proton pairing gap for ³²Mg: 2.510 MeV; theoretical: 2.522 MeV
- Empirical neutron pairing gap for ³²Mg: 1.560 MeV; theoretical: 1.576 MeV
- For ³²Mg with β₂ = 0.473, a dominant GT transition appears at 7.44 MeV (K=1) from combination of two (2001/2) states, and another at 10.18 MeV from (2203/2) and (2025/2) states

## Context
This work extends the DQRPA framework previously developed by Ha and Cheoun (2011, 2012) to nuclei in the N=20 "island of inversion," where experimental studies have shown breaking of the N=20 magic number in ³¹Na, ³²Mg, and ³³Al. The results contradict spherical shell model expectations and show that deformation causes low-lying GT states below 5 MeV to disappear when using experimental β₂ values. The predicted high-lying GT states are relevant for understanding the GT quenching problem and for neutrino-induced reactions in supernovae, as discussed by Wakasa (2006) and Cheoun (2013). The approach differs from earlier DQRPA work by Simkovic (using separable forces) and by Saleh (first using realistic two-body interaction).
