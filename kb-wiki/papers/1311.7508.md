# Thermal Description of Particle Production in Au-Au Collisions at STAR Energies

> The hadron ratios measured in central Au-Au collisions are analysed by means of Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model over a wide range of nucleon-nucleon center-of-mass energies ranging from 7.7 to 200 GeV as offered by the STAR Beam Energy Scan I (BES-I). We restrict the discussion on STAR BES-I, because of large statistics and over all homogeneity of STAR measurements (one detector) against previous experiments. Over the last three decades, various heavy-ion experiments utilizing different detectors (different certainties) have been carried out. Regularities in produced particles at different energies haven been studied. The temperature and baryon chemical potential are deduced from fits of experimental ratios to thermal model calculations assuming chemical equilibrium. We find that the resulting freeze-out parameters using single hard-core value and point-like constituents of HRG are identical. This implies that the excluded-volume comes up with no effect on the extracted parameters. We compare the results with other studies and with the lattice QCD calculations. Various freeze-out conditions are confronted with the resulting data set. The effect of feed-down contribution from week decay and of including new resonances are also analysed. At vanishing chemical potential, a limiting temperature was estimated as T=158.5 MeV with 3 MeV uncertainty.

## Key claim

The Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model with point-like constituents and with a single hard-core radius of 0.3 fm yields essentially identical freeze-out parameters when fitted to STAR Beam Energy Scan I data, implying that excluded-volume corrections have no practical effect on the extracted temperature and baryon chemical potential across the energy range 7.7 to 200 GeV.

## Method

The authors fitted 10 to 11 statistically independent hadron yield ratios measured in central Au-Au collisions at six STAR BES-I energies (7.7, 11.5, 39, 62.4, 130, and 200 GeV) to HRG model calculations assuming grand canonical ensemble and full chemical equilibrium. The fits minimized chi-squared per degree of freedom and quadratic deviation to extract the chemical freeze-out temperature T_ch and baryon chemical potential mu_b, using 388 hadron species from the PDG with decay branching ratios and including weak-decay feed-down contributions.

## Key numbers

* At 200 GeV: T_ch = 159.5 +/- 2 MeV, mu_b = 27.5 +/- 4 MeV (chi^2/dof = 9.24/9)
* At 130 GeV: T_ch = 157.5 +/- 4 MeV, mu_b = 34 +/- 3.5 MeV (chi^2/dof = 6.86/9)
* At 62.4 GeV: T_ch = 157.5 +/- 3.5 MeV, mu_b = 66 +/- 4 MeV (chi^2/dof = 10.17/9)
* At 39 GeV: T_ch = 160.5 +/- 3.5 MeV, mu_b = 110.5 +/- 4.5 MeV (chi^2/dof = 10.69/8)
* At 11.5 GeV: T_ch = 153 +/- 1.5 MeV, mu_b = 308 +/- 2.5 MeV (chi^2/dof = 6.06/8)
* At 7.7 GeV: T_ch = 145.5 +/- 1 MeV, mu_b = 410.5 +/- 4 MeV (chi^2/dof = 15.21/8)
* Limiting temperature at vanishing chemical potential: T_lim = 158.5 +/- 3 MeV
* Parametrization of mu_b vs sqrt(s_NN): a = 1.245 +/- 0.094 GeV, b = 0.264 +/- 0.028 GeV^-1

## Context

This work extends previous thermal model analyses by Andronic et al. (2009) and Cleymans et al. to the homogeneous STAR BES-I data set, avoiding the systematic uncertainties from combining different detectors (NA49, NA44, NA57) at SPS energies. The extracted freeze-out parameters are compared with lattice QCD calculations (which give T_lim = 154 +/- 9 MeV) and with the statistical hadronization model using partial chemical equilibrium. The results enable mapping the chemical freeze-out boundary in the QCD phase diagram and will be extended by future STAR BES-II data at 17 and 2.2 GeV, as well as NICA and FAIR measurements.
