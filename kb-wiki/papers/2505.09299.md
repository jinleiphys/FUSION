# A renormalizable theory for not-so-light nuclei

> We present an improved action for Pionless Effective Field Theory (EFT). Previous formulations of renormalizable nuclear EFTs have encountered instabilities in systems with more than four nucleons. We resolve this issue by introducing a finite interaction range at leading order, which is compensated for in perturbation theory at next-to-leading order. Calculated ground-state energies of $^4$He, $^6$Li, $^{12}$C, and $^{16}$O converge and agree with experiment within theoretical uncertainties. This first successful implementation of systematic renormalizability beyond the lightest nuclei enables not only applications to larger nuclei but also extensions to other EFTs in the strong-coupling regime.

# A renormalizable theory for not-so-light nuclei

## Key claim
The authors resolve the long-standing instability problem in Pionless Effective Field Theory (EFT) for systems with more than four nucleons by introducing a finite interaction range at leading order (LO), which is then compensated for perturbatively at next-to-leading order (NLO). The claim is conditional on the choice of a single improvement parameter x, with the optimal value x=1 providing cutoff-independent LO results and NLO energies that agree with experiment within theoretical uncertainties.

## Method
The authors construct an improved Pionless EFT action where artificial "fake ranges" are introduced into the LO two-body and three-body contact interactions, tied together by a single parameter x that measures the fraction of the physical effective ranges included at LO. Ground-state energies of ⁴He, ⁶Li, ¹²C, and ¹⁶O are computed using the Stochastic Variational Method (SVM) for A ≤ 6 and variational Monte Carlo with neural-network quantum states (VMC-NQS) for ¹²C and ¹⁶O, treating LO nonperturbatively and NLO corrections in first-order perturbation theory. Renormalization at NLO is demonstrated by extrapolating results to large momentum cutoffs Λ using a 1/Λ fit.

## Key numbers
- Two-body scattering lengths used to fix LO LECs: a_s = -18.95 fm (singlet), a_t = 5.4112 fm (triplet)
- Two-body effective ranges: r_s = 2.750 fm, r_t = 1.753 fm
- Triton binding energy used for three-body LEC calibration: E(³H) = -8.482 MeV
- Helion energy used for proton-proton LEC calibration: E(³He) = -7.718 MeV
- Alpha-particle binding energy used for four-body LEC calibration: E(⁴He) = -28.3 MeV
- Improved LO ⁴He binding energy at x=1: E^(0)(⁴He) ≈ -29.57 MeV (compared to unimproved LO result of -(31.77 ± 0.01 ± 6.6) MeV)
- ⁶Li NLO ground-state energy: E^(1)(⁶Li) = -(31.57 ± 0.02 ± 0.3) MeV (experimental: -31.994 MeV)
- ¹²C NLO ground-state energy: E^(1)(¹²C) = -(97.3 ± 0.1 ± 5) MeV (experimental: -92.162 MeV)
- ¹⁶O NLO ground-state energy: E^(1)(¹⁶O) = -(155.6 ± 0.3 ± 20) MeV (experimental: -127.619 MeV)
- Fake range parameters: R_s = 0.8970 fm, R_t = 0.7719 fm, R_3 = 0.4149 fm
- Doublet neutron-deuteron scattering length with improved three-body force: 0.70-0.72 fm (experimental: 0.65 ± 0.04 fm)
- Residual cutoff dependence coefficients: q_12^(1) ≈ 25.0 MeV, q_16^(1) ≈ 34.88 MeV

## Context
This work extends Pionless EFT, previously successful for A ≤ 5 systems (Hammer 2019, Bagnarol 2023), to heavier nuclei by resolving instabilities that had prevented renormalizable calculations for A ≥ 6 (Stetcu 2006, Contessi 2017, Bansal 2017). The improved action strategy was demonstrated earlier for atomic ⁴He clusters (Contessi 2023) and two-body systems (Contessi 2024). The resulting LO form is essentially equivalent to the successful phenomenological model of Kievsky (2015, 2018) and Gattobigio (2019, 2023), but the EFT framework enables systematic higher-order improvements. The methodology can be applied to other EFTs such as Chiral EFT (Yang 2020), potentially enabling model-independent ab initio calculations for a broader set of nuclei.
