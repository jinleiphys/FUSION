# Effect of Asymmetric Strange-Antistrange Sea to the NuTeV Anomaly

> We calculate the strange quark and antiquark distributions of the nucleon by using the effective chiral quark model, and find that the strange-antistrange asymmetry can bring a contribution of about 60--100% to the NuTeV deviation of $\sin^{2}θ_{w}$ from the standard value measured in other electroweak processes. The results are insensitive to different inputs. The light-flavor quark asymmetry of $\bar{d}-\bar{u}$ is also investigated and found to be consistent with the experimental measurements. Therefore the chiral quark model provides a successful picture to understand the NuTeV anomaly, as well as the light-flavor quark asymmetry and the proton spin problem in previous studies.

## Key claim

The strange-antistrange asymmetry of the nucleon sea, calculated within the effective chiral quark model, can account for about 60–100% of the NuTeV anomaly in the Weinberg angle \(\sin^{2}\theta_{w}\), with the exact fraction depending on the choice of the cutoff parameter \(\Lambda_{K}\) (900–1100 MeV) rather than on the specific input parton distributions.

## Method

The authors used the effective chiral quark model of Manohar and Georgi (1984), in which constituent quarks couple to Goldstone bosons (pions, kaons, eta) via a pseudoscalar interaction. They computed the strange quark and antiquark distributions \(s(x)\) and \(\bar{s}(x)\) by convoluting splitting functions for the fluctuation of constituent quarks into a quark plus a Goldstone boson with two different sets of input constituent quark distributions: a simple constituent quark (CQ) model and the CTEQ6 parametrization. The resulting second moment \(S^{-} = \int_{0}^{1} x[s(x)-\bar{s}(x)]\,dx\) was then used to compute the correction \(\delta R^{-}_{s}\) to the Paschos-Wolfenstein relation, which shifts the extracted value of \(\sin^{2}\theta_{w}\).

## Key numbers

- The NuTeV Collaboration measured \(\sin^{2}\theta_{w} = 0.2277 \pm 0.0013\ (\text{stat}) \pm 0.0009\ (\text{syst})\), which is three standard deviations above the standard value \(\sin^{2}\theta_{w} = 0.2227 \pm 0.0004\) from other electroweak processes.
- A correction \(\delta R^{-}_{s} \approx 0.005\) would be needed to fully explain the NuTeV deviation.
- With the CQ input and \(\Lambda_{K}=1100\) MeV, the calculated values are: \(S^{-} = 0.00879\), \(\delta R_{s}^{-} = 0.00473\); with \(\Lambda_{K}=900\) MeV, \(\delta R_{s}^{-} = 0.00297\).
- With the CTEQ6 input and \(\Lambda_{K}=1100\) MeV: \(S^{-} = 0.00398\), \(\delta R_{s}^{-} = 0.00498\); with \(\Lambda_{K}=900\) MeV, \(\delta R_{s}^{-} = 0.00312\).
- The strange-antistrange asymmetry accounts for about 60–100% of the NuTeV anomaly, corresponding to \(\Lambda_{K}=900\)–\(1100\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the effective chiral quark model, previously successful in explaining the Gottfried sum rule violation (Eichten, Hinchliffe and Quigg, 1992) and the proton spin problem (Cheng and Li, 1995), to the NuTeV anomaly. It contradicts the conclusion of Cao and Signal (2003) using the meson cloud model, who found the strange-antistrange asymmetry too small to affect the NuTeV extraction, and instead supports the larger asymmetry predicted by the light-cone meson-baryon fluctuation model of Brodsky and Ma (1997). The results suggest that precision measurements of \(s(x)\) and \(\bar{s}(x)\) in future experiments could resolve the anomaly without invoking physics beyond the Standard Model.
