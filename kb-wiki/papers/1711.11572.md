# Corrections to the higher moments of the relativistic ion energy-loss distribution beyond the Born approximation. I. Z dependence of Mott's corrections

> Using the Mott exact cross section for moderate relativistic energies, we calculated the corrections to the first-order Born higher moments of the energy-loss distribution of charged particles in a wide range of the particle charge numbers.

# Corrections to the higher moments of the relativistic ion energy-loss distribution beyond the Born approximation. I. Z dependence of Mott's corrections

## Key claim
Using the Mott exact cross section expressed as rapidly converging series bilinear in the Mott partial amplitudes, the relative corrections to the first-order Born central moments of the energy-loss distribution can reach about 200% for heavy ions with Z ~ 90 at moderate relativistic energies, but the normalized central moments (skewness and kurtosis) show that the Mott-corrected distributions are always less asymmetric and more Gaussian than the Born approximation predicts.

## Method
The author computed the second, third, and fourth central moments (μ₂, μ₃, μ₄) of the relativistic ion energy-loss distribution using the exact Mott cross section expressed via Mott phase shifts, represented as fast-converging infinite series bilinear in the Mott partial amplitudes. These moments were evaluated numerically for projectile charge numbers Z from 5 to 95 at β = 0.75, and compared with the corresponding first-order Born approximation results to obtain relative corrections δ₂, δ₃, δ₄ for the central moments and δ₃, δ₄ for the normalized central moments (skewness ρ₃ and kurtosis ρ₄).

## Key numbers
- For Z = 90 at β = 0.75, the relative correction to the second central moment (variance) is δ₂(μ) = 1.5186 (approximately 152% above the Born value).
- For Z = 90 at β = 0.75, the relative correction to the third central moment is δ₃(μ) = 2.0942 (approximately 209% above the Born value).
- For Z = 90 at β = 0.75, the relative correction to the fourth central moment is δ₄(μ) = 2.3507 (approximately 235% above the Born value).
- For Z = 90 at β = 0.75, the relative correction to the normalized third central moment (skewness) is −δ₃(ρ) = 0.2259 (approximately 22.6% below the Born value).
- For Z = 90 at β = 0.75, the relative correction to the normalized fourth central moment (kurtosis) is −δ₄(ρ) = 0.4718 (approximately 47.2% below the Born value).
- For Z = 10 at β = 0.75, the relative correction to the second central moment is δ₂(μ) = 0.0484 (approximately 4.8% above the Born value).

## Context
This work extends the exact Mott correction expression from Voskresenskaya et al. (VSTT) for the average energy loss to the higher central moments of the energy-loss distribution, addressing the inadequacy of the first-order Born approximation (Bethe formula) for heavy ions where Zα/β is not small. The results explain the energy-loss straggling measurements for relativistic heavy ions from GSI (GSI2), which found the straggling to exceed the relativistic Bohr result by up to a factor of 3, and are consistent with the observation by other authors that the energy straggling can be about a factor of 5 lower than the first-order Born result. The computed series converge as l⁻²ⁿ⁺¹ for the n-th moment, enabling practical numerical evaluation for applications in Rutherford backscattering spectrometry, nuclear reaction analysis, and scanning transmission ion microscopy.
