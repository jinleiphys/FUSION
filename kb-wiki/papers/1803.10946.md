# New determination of the spectroscopic factor of $^7$Be ground state and the $^6$Li($p,γ$)$^7$Be astrophysical S(E) factors

> The `lithium problem' in Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (BBN) has recently focused on the reactions involving $^7$Be. The $^6$Li($p, γ$)$^7$Be reaction can provide us not only the information for destroying $^6$Li but also the information for producing $^7$Be. In the present work, the proton spectroscopic factor in $^7$Be was extracted to be 0.70 $\pm$ 0.17 from the angular distribution of $^7$Be($d$, $^3$He)$^6$Li at $E_\mathrm{c.m.}$ = 6.7 MeV. The value was then used to compute the direct component of the astrophysical $^6$Li($p, γ$)$^7$Be$_\mathrm{g.s.}$ S(E) factors and determine the resonance parameters from the total S(E) factors.

## Key claim
The proton spectroscopic factor of the $^7$Be ground state is extracted as 0.70 $\pm$ 0.17 from the $^7$Be($d$, $^3$He)$^6$Li transfer reaction, but this value is an average over three sets of optical potential parameters and carries uncertainties of 10% from the potentials and 22% from statistics.

## Method
The angular distribution of $^7$Be($d$, $^3$He)$^6$Li was measured in inverse kinematics at $E_\mathrm{c.m.}$ = 6.7 MeV using a secondary $^7$Be beam at the HI-13 tandem accelerator in Beijing. The differential cross sections at forward angles were analyzed with the distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) using the code TWOFNR, and the spectroscopic factor was extracted by normalizing theoretical cross sections to the experimental data. The direct capture component of the $^6$Li($p, \gamma$)$^7$Be astrophysical S(E) factors was then computed using the extracted spectroscopic factor with the code FRESCO, and the total S(E) factors were fitted with the R-matrix code AZURE to determine resonance parameters.

## Key numbers
- Proton spectroscopic factor of $^7$Be ground state: $S^\mathrm{^7Be}_{tot}$ = 0.70 $\pm$ 0.17, extracted from $^7$Be($d$, $^3$He)$^6$Li at $E_\mathrm{c.m.}$ = 6.7 MeV.
- Corresponding asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC): 1.84 $\pm$ 0.45 fm$^{-1/2}$.
- Partial spectroscopic factors: $S^\mathrm{^7Be}_{p3/2}$ = 0.42 $\pm$ 0.10 and $S^\mathrm{^7Be}_{p1/2}$ = 0.28 $\pm$ 0.07 (averaged over three optical potential sets).
- Best-fit resonance parameters for the broad 3/2$^+$ resonance in $^7$Be: $E_{r}$ = 145 keV, $\Gamma_{p}(E_{r})$ = 10.2 eV, $\Gamma_{\gamma}(E_{r})$ = 7.6 eV, $\Gamma_{\alpha}(E_{r})$ = 232 keV, with $\chi^2_{min}$ = 15.4.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of the $^6$Li($p, \gamma$)$^7$Be reaction relevant to the BBN lithium problem, particularly addressing the broad resonance near 195 keV reported by He et al. (2013). The extracted spectroscopic factor agrees with shell-model values from Cohen (1967), Green's function Monte Carlo calculations by Brida (2011), and results from Burtebayev et al. (2013) and the authors' own earlier $^7$Li($^6$Li, $^7$Li)$^6$Li elastic transfer work (Su 2010). The improved resonance parameters enable better evaluation of this reaction's contribution to BBN and to the $^7$Be($p, \gamma$)$^8$B solar neutrino reaction.
