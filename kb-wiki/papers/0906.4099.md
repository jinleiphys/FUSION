# Constraints on AdS/CFT Gravity Dual Models of Heavy Ion Collisions

> We show that the five-fold constraints due to (1) the observed nuclear modification of heavy quark jets measured via non-photonic electrons $R_{AA}^e(p_T \sim 6 {\rm GeV})$ in central Au+Au collisions at 200 AGeV, (2) the "perfect fluid' elliptic transverse flow of low transverse momenta pions, $v_2(p_T\sim 1 {\rm GeV})$ reported for noncentral collisions, (3) the global pion rapidity density $dN_π/dy$, (4) the lattice QCD entropy density deficiency, $S/S_{SB}$, of strongly coupled Quark-Gluon Plasmas (sQGP), and (5) a causal requirement are analytically correlated in a class of gauge/string gravity dual models of sQGP dynamics. Current RHIC/BNL and lattice QCD data are found to be remarkably compatible with these models if the t'Hooft and Gauss-Bonnet coupling parameters lie in the range $λ\approx 10-25$ and $0< λ_{GB}< 0.09$. In addition, the observed five-fold correlation appears to favor color glass condensate over Glauber initial conditions within current systematic errors.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a class of AdS/CFT gravity dual models can simultaneously satisfy five phenomenological constraints from RHIC heavy ion data and lattice QCD, provided the t'Hooft coupling lies in the range λ ≈ 10–25 and the Gauss-Bonnet coupling in 0 < λ_GB < 0.09, with the caveat that the results are sensitive to the inversion of viscous hydrodynamic v₂(η/s) and a 10% reduction could falsify the description.

## Method

The authors analytically correlate three fundamental sQGP properties, entropy density S/S_SB, shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s, and heavy quark jet relaxation rate τ_Q⁻¹, using perturbative expansions in 1/λ^{3/2} (quartic curvature corrections) and λ_GB (Gauss-Bonnet quadratic curvature corrections) to the 5-dimensional dual gravity action. They convert the heavy quark relaxation rate into the nuclear modification factor R_AA^e for non-photonic electrons at p_T = 5.5 GeV using a path-length-dependent energy loss model with Woods-Saxon nuclear density profiles, and compute v₂ for pions at p_T = 1 GeV via a linear fit to viscous hydrodynamic results from Luzum and Romatschke (2008), using both Glauber and CGC initial eccentricities.

## Key numbers

- t'Hooft coupling range: λ ≈ 10–25
- Gauss-Bonnet coupling range: 0 < λ_GB < 0.09
- Causality upper bound on λ_GB: 0.09
- Lattice QCD entropy deficiency constraint: 0.8 < S/S_SB < 0.9
- Central R_AA^e(p_T = 5.5 GeV) = 0.25 ± 0.07 for 0–10% centrality Au+Au at 200 AGeV
- Elliptic flow v₂(p_T = 1 GeV, 20–60% centrality) = 0.11 ± 0.01
- Pion rapidity density: dN_π/dy = 1000 for central collisions
- Heavy quark jet speed limit: γ_m ≈ (4M_Q²)/(λ T²) [1 – (5/16)(4πη/s – 1)]
- KSS viscosity bound violation for N_c = 3: η/s reduced by 17% for λ_GB = 1/8N_c
- Causality bound range for λ_GB: –7/36 < λ_GB < 9/100
- Glauber eccentricity ratio: ε₂(20–60%)/ε₂(0–92%) = 0.317/0.281 = 1.128
- Fit parameters for v₂: b ≈ 2.5, a(p_T=1)ε₂(20–60%) ≈ 0.14 (CGC) and 0.098 (Glauber)
- Freeze-out temperature: T_f = 140 MeV
- Plasma equilibration time: τ₀ = 1 fm/c

## Context

This work extends the suggestion of Buchel (2008) to include both Gauss-Bonnet R² and quartic R⁴ curvature corrections perturbatively in AdS/CFT gravity dual models of the sQGP. It analytically correlates for the first time the equation of state, transport coefficients, and heavy quark energy loss within a single framework, enabling simultaneous constraints from RHIC data on hard probes (R_AA^e from Adler et al. 2005 and STAR electron data), soft flow (v₂ from v2data), and lattice QCD entropy density (latticedata). The results favor CGC initial conditions over Glauber initial conditions and show that future identified bottom and charm jet measurements at RHIC and LHC could provide stringent tests of AdS/CFT phenomenology.
