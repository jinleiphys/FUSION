# Ground state correlations on ground state densities and total binding energies of 40Ca, 48Ca and 208Pb

> Neutron and proton densities of doubly-closed shell nuclei 40Ca, 48Ca and 208Pb are studied based on a Hartree-Fock model with SAMi, SAMi-J27 and SAMi-T energy density functionals (EDFs). The ground state correlations (GSC) induced by isoscalar and isovector phonons are also evaluated by the second order perturbation theory with a self-consistent random phase approximation (RPA). We found that the interior part of the ground state densities is reduced by the GSC in consistent with the experimental data. On the other hand, the GSC enhances the neutron skin thickness of 48Ca and 208Pb. The effect of GSC on the total binding energy is also evaluated by the quasi-boson approximation. The effect of the tensor interaction is found small on both the density distributions and the binding energies.

# Ground state correlations on ground state densities and total binding energies of 40Ca, 48Ca and 208Pb

## Key claim
Ground state correlations (GSC) induced by isoscalar and isovector phonons reduce the interior part of ground state densities in doubly-closed shell nuclei, consistent with experimental data, but the effect is small enough that the tensor interaction contributes negligibly to both density distributions and binding energies. The GSC enhances neutron skin thickness in 48Ca and 208Pb, yet the quantitative agreement with experimental radii worsens for 48Ca when GSC is included.

## Method
The authors performed Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations with three modern Skyrme energy density functionals (SAMi, SAMi-J27, and SAMi-T) for 40Ca, 48Ca, and 208Pb. Ground state correlations were evaluated using second-order perturbation theory with a self-consistent random phase approximation (RPA), considering phonon states up to Jπ=5± and single-particle states up to 60 MeV with 1p-1h excitations limited to 100 MeV. The correlation energies were computed via the quasi-boson approximation, and the continuum was discretized in a box of R=20 fm.

## Key numbers
- For 40Ca with SAMi-T: HF+GSC gives neutron radius rn = 3.808 fm, proton radius rp = 3.845 fm, and neutron skin Δrnp = −0.037 fm (experimental Δrnp = −0.010 +0.049 −0.048 fm)
- For 48Ca with SAMi: HF+GSC gives rn = 4.003 fm, rp = 3.770 fm, Δrnp = 0.233 fm (experimental Δrnp = 0.168 +0.052 −0.055 fm)
- For 208Pb with SAMi: HF+GSC gives rn = 5.750 fm, rp = 5.555 fm, Δrnp = 0.195 fm (experimental Δrnp = 0.211 +0.054 −0.063 fm)
- Correlation energies: 4.25 MeV for 40Ca (SAMi), 4.93 MeV for 48Ca (SAMi), 3.95 MeV for 208Pb (SAMi), corresponding to about 1% of total binding energy for Ca isotopes and 0.2% for 208Pb
- The energy-weighted sum rules are satisfied over 99.5% for all Jπ in the RPA model space
- Occupation probabilities in pf-shell orbits from GSC: ∑(2j+1)vj2 ≈ 0.3 for 40Ca with both SAMi and SAMi-T

## Context
This work extends earlier studies by Faessler (1976) and Waroquier (1983) on GSC in 16O, 40,48Ca, and 208Pb, but uses modern Skyrme parameter sets (SAMi, SAMi-J27, SAMi-T) and specifically examines the tensor force contribution. The results are compared with experimental density data from Zenihiro and electron scattering, and with large-scale shell model calculations by Brown, Shimizu, and Suzuki. The findings indicate that to achieve consistent description of ground state properties in beyond-mean-field models, the Skyrme EDF parameters need to be refit including GSC effects.
