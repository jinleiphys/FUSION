# Proton-proton bremsstrahlung below and above pion-threshold: the influence of the $Δ$-isobar

> The proton-proton bremsstrahlung is investigated within a coupled-channel model with the $Δ$ degree of freedom. The model is consistent with the $NN$ scattering up to 1 GeV and the $γNΔ$ vertex determined in the study of pion photoproduction reactions. It is found that the $Δ$ excitation can significantly improve the agreements with the $pp \rightarrow ppγ$ at $E_{lab}=280$ MeV. Predictions at $E_{lab}=550$ and $800$ MeV are presented for future experimental tests.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including the Δ-isobar degree of freedom in a coupled-channel model can significantly improve agreement with experimental proton-proton bremsstrahlung data at Elab = 280 MeV, but this improvement depends on using a consistent Δ propagator and the specific form of the Δ self-energy, as different self-energy parametrizations that both fit the P33 phase shift yield rather different results.

## Method

The authors extended a coupled-channel NN ⊕ NΔ ⊕ πNN model, originally developed by Lee and Matsuyama, to include electromagnetic interactions via a one-baryon current operator defined from Feynman amplitudes. They solved the coupled-channel equations in momentum space to obtain T-matrices for NN and NΔ scattering, then used these to calculate the pp → ppγ amplitude including both single-scattering (external) Δ-decay diagrams and Δ rescattering diagrams, with "minimal relativity" corrections applied to ensure Lorentz invariance.

## Key numbers

- The coupled-channel model reproduces NN phase shifts up to about 1 GeV, with the subtraction energy Es = 10 MeV and monopole form factor cutoff Λ = 650 MeV/c chosen to best reproduce Paris potential phase shifts below about 300 MeV
- At Elab = 280 MeV, the Δ mechanism can increase the cross-section up to 30% at certain kinematic conditions, while decreasing it less substantially at others
- The Δ contribution alone at Elab = 280 MeV is less than 10% of the contribution from the nucleon current
- The physical Δ mass is mΔ* = 1.23 GeV; the bare Δ mass depends on the self-energy parametrization, with values of mΔ0 = 1.28 GeV (Lee), 1.46 GeV (ter Haar and Malfliet), or equal to the physical mass (Bransden-Moorhouse parametrization)
- The γNΔ coupling constants span a range: G1 from 2.024 GeV−1 (Nozawa et al.) to 2.68 GeV−1 (Jones and Scadron), and G2 from −0.851 GeV−2 to −1.84 GeV−2
- Predictions are presented at Elab = 550 and 800 MeV for future experimental tests at COSY

## Context

This work extends earlier qualitative studies by de Jong et al. (1993) on Δ effects in pp bremsstrahlung by using a more consistent coupled-channel T-matrix constrained by NN scattering up to 1 GeV, and by including rescattering diagrams with Δ intermediate states. It goes beyond the Born-approximation calculations of Bohannon, Heller and Thompson (1976) and Szyjewicz and Kamal (1985), and the Soft Photon Approximation approach of Tiator et al. (1986). The model enables realistic predictions for intermediate-energy bremsstrahlung experiments at COSY and provides a foundation for microscopic studies of hard-photon production in heavy-ion collisions and dilepton production in proton-nucleus collisions.
