# Deuteron electromagnetic form factors in a renormalizable formulation of chiral effective field theory

> We calculate the deuteron electromagnetic form factors in a modified version of Weinberg's chiral effective field theory approach to the two-nucleon system. We derive renormalizable integral equations for the deuteron without partial wave decomposition. Deuteron form factors are extracted by applying the Lehmann-Symanzik-Zimmermann reduction formalism to the three-point correlation function of deuteron interpolating fields and the electromagnetic current operator. Numerical results of a leading-order calculation with removed cutoff regularization agree well with experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors calculate deuteron electromagnetic form factors at leading order in a renormalizable chiral effective field theory framework, finding good agreement with experimental data after removing cutoff regularization; however, the deviations from data reach about 20% at momentum transfer q = 200 MeV when using chiral nucleon form factors, with the agreement improving dramatically when phenomenological nucleon form factors are substituted.

## Method

The authors derive renormalizable integral equations for the deuteron without partial wave decomposition, using a manifestly Lorentz-invariant effective Lagrangian and time-ordered perturbation theory based on the Kadyshevsky equation. They introduce a deuteron interpolating field and extract the form factors by applying the Lehmann-Symanzik-Zimmermann reduction formalism to the three-point correlation function of two deuteron interpolating fields and the electromagnetic current operator. The leading-order nucleon-nucleon potential includes one-pion exchange and two contact terms, with the low-energy constant C_{^3S_1} fixed to reproduce the experimental deuteron binding energy of 2.22 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: B = 2.22 MeV (used to fix the low-energy constant)
- Leading-order magnetic moment: μ_M^{LO} = 0.826 e/(2m), compared to experimental μ_M = 0.85741 e/(2m) (deviation ~3.5%)
- Leading-order quadrupole moment: μ_Q^{LO} = 0.271 fm², compared to experimental μ_Q = 0.2859 fm² (deviation ~5%)
- Deviations from experimental form factors reach about 20% at q = 200 MeV when using chiral nucleon form factors
- Pion mass: M_π = 138 MeV; pion decay constant: F_π = 92.4 MeV; axial coupling: g_A = 1.267
- The ^3S_1 and ^3D_1 phase shifts and mixing angle ε_1 are described up to laboratory energies E_lab ~ 250 MeV

## Context

This work extends the renormalizable chiral EFT framework of Epelbaum et al. (2012) from nucleon-nucleon scattering to electromagnetic observables of the deuteron. It contrasts with earlier EFT calculations by Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998) which used dimensional regularization and perturbative pions, and with Weinberg-type approaches by Phillips et al. (1999-2006) and others that employed finite ultraviolet cutoffs. The renormalizable formulation allows the cutoff to be taken to infinity after subtracting ultraviolet divergences, and the results enable future inclusion of higher-order corrections from two-nucleon currents and subleading chiral orders.
