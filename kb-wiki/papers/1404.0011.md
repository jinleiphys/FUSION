# The fifth dimension in the worldline formalism, holography, and the Wilson flow

> We develop a model for hadrons in the framework of the worldline formalism. While being based wholly in four-dimensional quantum field theory, it shares many features with holographic approaches: Already by the use of the worldline formalism the approach appears intrinsically quantum mechanical. As auxiliary fifth dimension Schwinger's proper time is grouped with the physical four spacetime dimensions into an AdS5 geometry, which is warped due to conformal-symmetry breaking effects. Hidden local symmetry is emergent. The four-dimensional sources are extended to five-dimensional fields by a Wilson flow (gradient flow). A variational principle for the flow reproduces exactly the corresponding holographic computation. The worldline approach also yields the higher-dimensional description in the non-relativistic case.

## Key claim

The paper develops a model for hadrons within the worldline formalism that reproduces the structure of holographic AdS/QCD models without invoking gravity or string theory, but the derivation requires adding a phenomenological ingredient (a linearly spaced tower of states) or a detuned harmonic oscillator to produce a physical hadron spectrum.

## Method

The author starts from the worldline effective action for scalar quarks coupled to a vector source, expresses it as a path integral over closed trajectories, and identifies the Schwinger proper time as an auxiliary fifth dimension that groups with the four spacetime dimensions into a warped AdS₅ geometry. A change of variables is performed on the proper time and the worldline coordinate to introduce a repulsive harmonic oscillator potential, and the gauge field is integrated out to incorporate a linear confining potential via the hadronic Born approximation. The four-dimensional sources are extended to five-dimensional fields through a Wilson (gradient) flow, and a variational principle for the flow reproduces the standard AdS/QCD computation.

## Key numbers

- The worldline propagator for the free case is given by G(τ) = T τ̂(1 − τ̂), where τ̂ = τ/T and T is the Schwinger proper time.
- The two-point function in the worldline formalism yields an integral ∫_ε^∞ (dT/T) exp[−T(m² + q²(τ̂ − τ̂²))] ≈ −γ − ln{ε[m² + q²(τ̂ − τ̂²)]} for small ε.
- The AdS/QCD two-point function gives ∂_T ṽ(p,ε) = −(p²/4)[ln(ε μ²) + γ + ψ(1 − p²/(4μ²)) + γ], where ψ is the digamma function and μ is the warp parameter.
- The change of variables cT = exp(cΘ) − 1 transforms the integration measure to include a factor 1/(1 − exp(−cΘ)), producing a tower of states with squared masses q² = 4nμ², n ∈ ℕ.
- The harmonic oscillator potential in the worldline action corresponds to a two-body interaction ∫₀¹ dτ̂₁ dτ̂₂ [y(τ₁) − y(τ₂)]² = 2 ∫₀¹ dτ̂ [y(τ)]² under the center-of-mass convention.
- The worldline propagator with an attractive harmonic oscillator is (c/2)H(τ) = sin((c/2)|τ|) − sin((c/2)T)[1 − cos((c/2)τ)]/[1 − cos((c/2)T)].

## Context

This work extends the author's previous research (Dietrich 2013) on the worldline formalism and builds on the worldline formalism developed by Strassler (1992). It directly compares with soft-wall AdS/QCD models (Karch 2006, Da Rold 2005) that describe hadron spectra phenomenologically but lack a first-principles derivation. The paper also relates to the work of de Alfaro, Fubini, and Furlan (1976) on conformal invariance in quantum mechanics and to the linear confinement framework of Dietrich (2012). The approach enables a derivation of holographic features from four-dimensional quantum field theory without recourse to string theory, potentially providing a foundation for bottom-up AdS/QCD models.
