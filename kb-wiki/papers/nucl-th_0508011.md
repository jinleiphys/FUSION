# On a variational principle model for the Nuclear Caloric curve

> Following the lead of a recent work we perform a variational principle model calculation for the nuclear caloric curve. A Skyrme type interaction with and without momentum dependence is used. The calculation is done for a large nucleus, i.e, in the nuclear matter limit. Thus we address the issue of volume fragmentation only. Nonetheless the results are similar to the previous, largely phenomenological calculation for a finite nucleus. We find the onset of fragmentation can be sudden as a function of temperature/excitation energy.

# On a variational principle model for the Nuclear Caloric curve

## Key claim
A variational principle calculation in the nuclear matter limit, using a Skyrme-type interaction with and without momentum dependence, produces a plateau in the caloric curve and a maximum in specific heat, but the model cannot be extended beyond the point where the free energy minimum disappears (around 12 MeV temperature), and the onset of fragmentation can be sudden as a function of temperature or excitation energy.

## Method
The author performs a finite-temperature mean field calculation in the nuclear matter limit, minimizing the free energy per particle at fixed temperature to find the equilibrium density. A momentum-dependent Skyrme interaction (with parameters A=-110.44 MeV, B=140.9 MeV, C=-64.95 MeV, sigma=1.24, rho_0=0.16 fm^-3, Lambda=1.58 p_F^0) is used, and the self-consistent occupation probabilities are obtained iteratively. For comparison, a density-dependent but momentum-independent interaction is also employed, and constant-energy (microcanonical) solutions are computed alongside the constant-temperature (grand canonical) ones.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density rho_0 = 0.16 fm^-3, binding energy per particle = 16 MeV, compressibility K = 215 MeV, effective mass m*/m = 0.67 at the Fermi energy for the momentum-dependent interaction.
- For the momentum-dependent interaction, the caloric curve flattens and the free energy minimum disappears just below temperature 12 MeV.
- For the density-dependent (momentum-independent) interaction, the free energy minimum disappears beyond temperature approximately 12.2 MeV.
- In the density-dependent model, at T=6 MeV the coexistence line intersects the isothermal at density 0.148 fm^-3; at T=12 MeV the intersection density is 0.111 fm^-3.
- In the constant-energy model, the caloric curve from the finite-temperature model and the finite-excitation model agree up to e* approximately 13.6 MeV, after which the finite-excitation model continues with negative slope for T, terminating at e* = 16 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the variational principle approach of Sobotka et al. (2004), who found a plateau in the caloric curve for a finite nucleus (mass 197) using a phenomenological density profile and effective mass parametrization. The present calculation uses a more microscopic Skyrme-type interaction in the nuclear matter limit, addressing only volume fragmentation. The results are similar to those of Sobotka et al. (2004) despite the different treatment of finite-size effects and momentum dependence. The work contrasts with the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) of Bondorf et al. (1985) and the canonical thermodynamic model of Das Gupta et al. (2005), which predict a sharp peak in specific heat at the phase transition, whereas the mean-field approach gives a gentle maximum. The model likely has validity for proton-induced reactions rather than central heavy-ion collisions, and extension to higher energies remains an open question.
