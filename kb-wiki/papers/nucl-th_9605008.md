# The position and the residues of the delta resonance pole in pion photoproduction

> We have analyzed the $M_{1+}^{(3/2)}$ and $E_{1+}^{(3/2)}$ multipole amplitudes of pion photoproduction in the framework of fixed-$t$ dispersion relations. Applying the speed plot technique to our results for these multipoles, we have determined the position and the residues of the $Δ$ (1232) resonance pole. The pole is found at total $c.m.$ energy $W = (1211 - 50i)$ MeV on the second Riemann sheet, and the ratio of the electric and magnetic residues is $R_Δ = - 0.035 - 0.046 i$, resulting in an E2/M1 ratio for the "dressed" delta resonance of $- 3.5 \%$.

# The position and the residues of the delta resonance pole in pion photoproduction

## Key claim

The pole of the Δ(1232) resonance in pion photoproduction is found at a total center-of-mass energy W = (1211 − 50i) MeV on the second Riemann sheet, yielding a dressed E2/M1 ratio of −3.5%; however, this result applies only after subtracting the well-defined nucleon pole terms from the amplitudes, and the ratio for the full (experimental) amplitudes is strongly energy-dependent, ranging from −10.4% to +0.1% across the resonance width.

## Method

The authors solved fixed-t dispersion relations for the M1+^(3/2) and E1+^(3/2) multipole amplitudes of pion photoproduction, imposing unitarity via the Watson final-state theorem below two-pion threshold and using an ansatz based on unitarity up to 2 GeV. They applied the speed plot technique, the derivative of the amplitude with respect to center-of-mass energy, to these multipoles after subtracting the nucleon pole terms, extracting the pole position and complex residues from the peak shape and Argand diagram of the speed.

## Key numbers

- Pole position: W_R = (1211 − 50i) MeV (total center-of-mass energy on the second Riemann sheet), corresponding to M_R = 1211 MeV and Γ_R = 100 MeV.
- Ratio of electric to magnetic residues: R_Δ = −0.035 − 0.046 i.
- Dressed E2/M1 ratio from the resonance pole alone: Re(R_Δ) = −3.5%.
- Ratio of speed plot heights: H_E / H_M = r_E / r_M = 5.8%.
- Energy-dependent R_EM for the full amplitude: −10.4% at W = M_R − Γ_R/2, −4.3% at W = M_R, and +0.1% at W = M_R + Γ_R/2.
- Nearly model-independent R_EM at W = M_Δ = 1232 MeV: (−2.5 ± 0.2)% from the Mainz group.

## Context

This work extends the speed plot technique, previously applied by Höhler (1992, 1993) to πN scattering, to pion photoproduction multipoles, and confirms that the Δ pole position agrees with πN scattering results (M_R = 1210 ± 1 MeV, Γ_R = 100 MeV). The analysis provides a model-independent extraction of the dressed resonance parameters, contrasting with earlier model-dependent determinations of the bare Δ E2/M1 ratio by Nozawa et al. (1990) (−3.1%) and Davidson et al. (1986) (−1.4%), and with the recent bare ratios of −1.43% and −1.46% from Van (1995) and Sur (1995). The results enable future studies of double polarization observables and the longitudinal-transverse interference term in electroproduction, which are sensitive to the electric quadrupole strength.
