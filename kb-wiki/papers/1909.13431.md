# To reach neutron-rich heavy and superheavy nuclei by multinucleon transfer reactions with radioactive isotopes

> The dynamical mechanism of multinucleon transfer (MNT) reactions has been investigated within the dinuclear system (DNS) model, in which the sequential nucleon transfer is described by solving a set of microscopically derived master equations. Production cross sections, total kinetic energy spectra, angular distribution of formed fragments in the reactions of $^{124,132}$Sn+ $^{238}$U/$^{248}$Cm near Coulomb barrier energies are thoroughly analyzed. It is found that the total kinetic energies of primary fragments are dissipated from the relative motion energy and rotational energy of the two colliding nuclei. The fragments are formed in the forward angle domain. The energy dependence of the angular spectra is different between projectile-like and target-like fragments. Isospin equilibrium is governed under the potential energy surface. The production cross sections of neutron-rich isotopes are enhanced around the shell closure.

## Key claim

Multinucleon transfer (MNT) reactions with radioactive isotopes such as $^{132}$Sn are a promising pathway to produce neutron-rich heavy and superheavy nuclei, particularly around the neutron shell closures N=82, 126, 152, and 162, but the production cross sections for the heaviest transfermium and superheavy elements drop to the picobarn level or below, making experimental detection challenging.

## Method

The authors employed the dinuclear system (DNS) model, in which sequential nucleon transfer is described by solving microscopically derived master equations for the time evolution of fragment distribution probabilities. The dissipation of relative motion energy and angular momentum was treated via a Fokker-Planck equation, and the potential energy surface (PES) of the DNS was calculated including deformation and shell effects. Fragment angular distributions were evaluated using a deflection function method, and the de-excitation of primary fragments via particle emission and fission was included to obtain survival cross sections.

## Key numbers

- For the reaction $^{132}$Sn + $^{248}$Cm at 6 MeV/nucleon, the predicted cross sections for new neutron-rich isotopes of element Md (Z=101) range from $10^{-7}$ to $10^{-9}$ mb (i.e., $10^{-7}$ to $10^{-9}$ millibarns).
- For the reaction $^{132}$Sn + $^{238}$U at 6 MeV/nucleon, the predicted cross section for new neutron-rich isotopes of element Ac (Z=89) reaches up to 1.6 mb.
- The neutron-to-proton ratios of the four reaction systems are: 1.548 for $^{124}$Sn+$^{248}$Cm, 1.549 for $^{124}$Sn+$^{238}$U, 1.603 for $^{132}$Sn+$^{248}$Cm, and 1.605 for $^{132}$Sn+$^{238}$U.
- The relaxation time for radial motion was set to $\tau_r = 5 \times 10^{-22}$ s, and for angular momentum relaxation to $\tau_J = 15 \times 10^{-22}$ s.
- For the reaction $^{124}$Sn+$^{238}$U at 6 MeV/nucleon, the production cross section for the new isotope $^{269}$Rf (Z=104) is predicted to be less than $10^{-9}$ mb.

## Context

This work extends the DNS model, previously applied to fusion-evaporation reactions by Feng (2006, 2007, 2009), to MNT reactions for producing neutron-rich heavy and superheavy nuclei. It builds on experimental measurements of MNT reactions such as $^{136}$Xe+$^{208}$Pb by Kozulin (2012) and $^{238}$U+$^{232}$Th by Wu (2018), and on earlier theoretical approaches including the time-dependent Hartree-Fock method and the GRAZING model. The predictions for new isotopes from Z=60 to Z=104 provide specific targets for future experiments at facilities such as the High Intensity Heavy-Ion Facility (HIAF) and the Beijing Isotope Separation On Line (BISOL) facility.
