# Effects of the triaxial deformation and pairing correlation on the proton emitter 145Tm

> The ground-state properties of the recent reported proton emitter 145Tm have been studied within the axially or triaxially deformed relativistic mean field (RMF) approaches, in which the pairing correlation is taken into account by the BCS-method with a constant pairing gap. It is found that triaxiality and pairing correlations play important roles in reproducing the experimental one proton separation energy. The single-particle level, the proton emission orbit, the deformation parameters beta = 0.22 and gamma = 28.98 and the corresponding spectroscopic factor for 145Tm in the triaxial RMF calculation are given as well.

## Key claim

The triaxial deformation degree of freedom and pairing correlations are both essential to reproduce the experimental one-proton separation energy of 145Tm; the axial RMF calculation alone cannot match the data, and the triaxial RMF calculation agrees only when the proton pairing gap is reduced by a factor of approximately 0.9.

## Method

The ground-state properties of 145Tm were calculated using both axially symmetric and triaxially deformed relativistic mean field (RMF) approaches with the PK1 parameter set. Pairing correlations were included via the BCS method with a constant pairing gap, and the blocking effect of the odd valence proton was treated by reducing the proton pairing gap by a factor f. The Dirac and Klein-Gordon equations were solved by expansion on a cylindrical (axial) or three-dimensional Cartesian (triaxial) harmonic oscillator basis with major shell numbers nf=14 for nucleons and nb=20 for mesons.

## Key numbers

- One-proton separation energy Sp = 1.71 MeV from the triaxial RMF+BCS/PK1 calculation (f=0.9), compared to the experimental value of 1.728(10) MeV (Batchelder 1998); the axial RMF+BCS/PK1 calculation gave Sp = 0.62 MeV.
- Deformation parameters for 145Tm from the triaxial RMF calculation: beta = 0.22 and gamma = 28.98 degrees.
- Spectroscopic factor (unoccupied probability u^2_k) of the odd valence proton in 145Tm: 0.67 from the triaxial RMF calculation, consistent with the WKB value of 0.51(16) (Batchelder 1998).
- Main spherical components of the valence proton wave function in the triaxial RMF calculation: 82.1% for 0h11/2, 7.9% for 1f7/2, and 2.3% for 0f7/2.
- For neighboring isotopes: 146Tm has beta=0.19, gamma=39.70 degrees, Sp=1.26 MeV (data: 1.120(10) MeV, Livingston 1993); 147Tm has beta=0.21, gamma=28.16 degrees, Sp=0.90 MeV (data: 1.054(19) MeV, Sellin 1993).
- Estimated pairing gaps from odd-even mass differences: Delta_n = 13.7/sqrt(A) MeV and Delta_p = 15.9/sqrt(A) MeV, with rms deviations of 0.17 MeV and 0.16 MeV respectively.

## Context

This work extends previous RMF studies of proton emitters (Vretenar 1999, Lalazissis 1999, Geng 2004) by including the triaxial degree of freedom self-consistently for the first time in a microscopic RMF approach. It addresses the debate on the relative importance of triaxial deformation versus pairing in proton emission, following conflicting results from the particle-core model (Davids 2004, Delion 2004) and non-adiabatic quasiparticle calculations (Arumugam 2007). The results enable future microscopic studies of fine structure in proton emission and provide self-consistent deformation parameters and spectroscopic factors for use in coupled-channels or particle-core models of 145Tm and neighboring thulium isotopes.
