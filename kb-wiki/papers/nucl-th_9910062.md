# State Dependent Effective Interaction for the Hyperspherical Formalism

> The method of effective interaction, traditionally used in the framework of an harmonic oscillator basis, is applied to the hyperspherical formalism of few-body nuclei (A=3-6). The separation of the hyperradial part leads to a state dependent effective potential. Undesirable features of the harmonic oscillator approach associated with the introduction of a spurious confining potential are avoided. It is shown that with the present method one obtains an enormous improvement of the convergence of the hyperspherical harmonics series in calculating ground state properties, excitation energies and transitions to continuum states.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a state-dependent effective interaction for the hyperspherical harmonics (HH) formalism that avoids the spurious confining potential required in harmonic oscillator (HO) effective interaction approaches, leading to parameter-free improvement of convergence for few-body nuclei with A=3-6.

## Method

The authors define a model space consisting of a complete hyperradial basis and HH functions with generalized angular momentum quantum number K ≤ K_max. They derive an effective two-body interaction from a hyperangular Hamiltonian H₂(ρ) that includes the hyperangular kinetic energy proportional to 1/ρ², where ρ is the collective hyperradial coordinate. The Lee-Suzuki similarity transformation method is employed to construct the effective interaction, which is then used to solve the A-body Hamiltonian via coupled differential equations in the hyperradius, expanded in generalized Laguerre polynomials.

## Key numbers

- For the A=3 system with the Minnesota (MN) potential, using the effective interaction with K=2 gives a deviation of only 0.6% for energy and 1.3% for radius from the converged value, while the bare interaction requires K=10 (energy) or K=8 (radius) for similarly good results.
- For ⁴He with the MTV potential, the effective interaction yields almost correct values for energy and radius with K=4, whereas the bare interaction does not give completely converged results even with K=20.
- Ground state energy of ³H with the MN potential: -8.3856(5) MeV; root mean square radius: 1.7036(1) fm.
- Ground state energy of ⁴He with the MTV potential: -31.358(9) MeV; root mean square radius: 1.40851(3) fm.
- First excitation energy of ³H with the MN potential: -0.421(9) MeV; root mean square radius: 4.757(5) fm.
- The calculations are limited to about 400 HH basis functions due to the increasing number of HH functions for larger A.

## Context

This work extends the effective interaction method, traditionally used in harmonic oscillator basis shell-model calculations (Zheng 1994, Navratil and Barrett 1998), to the hyperspherical formalism for few-body nuclei. It avoids the need for correlation functions previously used to accelerate HH convergence (Fenin and Efros 1972, Rosati 1992, Barnea 1999). The approach enables calculation of ground state properties, excitation energies, and transitions to continuum states via the Lorentz integral transform method (Efros, Leidemann, and Orlandini 1994), and can be extended to incorporate three-body effective interactions.
