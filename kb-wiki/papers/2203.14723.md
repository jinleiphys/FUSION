# $R$-matrix analysis of $^{22}$Ne($帢$, n)$^{25}$Mg and $^{22}$Ne($帢$, $帠$)$^{26}$Mg reaction

> The $^{22}$Ne($帢$, n)$^{25}$Mg and its competing channel $^{22}$Ne($帢$, $帠$)$^{26}$Mg has an major influence on neutron flux in weak s-process nucleosynthesis path in low mass AGB stars and massive stars of mass (M$\geq$ 10M$_\odot$). So the ratio rate of this two competing reaction control the neutron flux in weak s-process nucleosynthesis. Various experiment has been performed to study the properties of nuclear states of $^{26}$Mg to evaluate rate of $^{22}$Ne+$帢$ reaction rate and corresponding rate from these studies vary by up to a factor of 500 in the astrophysical relevant temperature. The recent evaluation by Philip et al. of $^{22}$Ne($帢$, n)$^{25}$Mg reaction rate using most recent nuclear data of $^{26}$Mg from number of sources shows similar result with previous estimation for $^{22}$Ne($帢$, $帠$)$^{26}$Mg but got lower rate for $^{22}$Ne($帢$, n)$^{25}$Mg reaction due to updated nuclear data. Also Philip et al. suggested that rate based on full $R$-matrix modeling will required to take into accounted the interference effects between distant levels and sub-threshold resonance. In present work full $R$-matrix calculation has been performed for $^{22}$Ne($帢$, n)$^{25}$Mg and $^{22}$Ne($帢$, $帠$)$^{26}$Mg reaction based on fitting the $^{22}$Ne($帢$, n)$^{25}$Mg reaction data of Jaeger et al; in energy range 0.8 to 1.45 MeV and updated nuclear data of $^{26}$Mg states. The $R$-matrix fitting for the $^{22}$Ne($帢$, n)$^{25}$Mg reaction nicely explain experimental data in 0.8 to 1.45 MeV energy range by changing spin, parity of E$_x$ = 11.784 and 11.63 MeV states from 1$^-$ to 0$^+$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a full $R$-matrix calculation, by changing the spin-parity assignment of two excited states in $^{26}$Mg from 1$^-$ to 0$^+$ and adjusting resonance parameters, can reproduce the $^{22}$Ne($\alpha$, n)$^{25}$Mg cross-section data of Jaeger et al. in the 0.8 to 1.45 MeV energy range, but the extrapolation to lower energies (0.57 to 0.8 MeV) remains uncertain and deviates from the data.

## Method

The author performed a multilevel $R$-matrix analysis using the AZURE2 code on the $^{22}$Ne($\alpha$, n)$^{25}$Mg reaction cross-section data from Jaeger et al. in the center-of-mass energy range 0.8 to 1.45 MeV, including interference effects between states of the same spin-parity. The calculation used channel radii of 5.37 fm for the $^{22}$Ne + $\alpha$ entrance channel and 4.21 fm for the $^{25}$Mg + n exit channel, and the resonance parameters (energies, partial widths, spins, parities) for states in $^{26}$Mg were adjusted to fit the data, with the spin-parity of the $E_x = 11.784$ and 11.63 MeV states changed from 1$^-$ to 0$^+$. The same $R$-matrix framework was then applied to simultaneously calculate the $^{22}$Ne($\alpha$, $\gamma$)$^{26}$Mg reaction using literature gamma widths and asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) derived from spectroscopic factors of $^{26}$Mg bound states.

## Key numbers

- The $R$-matrix fit was performed on $^{22}$Ne($\alpha$, n)$^{25}$Mg data in the energy range $E_{\alpha}^{c.m.} = 0.8$ to 1.45 MeV, with extrapolation down to 0.57 MeV.
- The channel radii used were 5.37 fm for the $^{22}$Ne + $\alpha$ channel and 4.21 fm for the $^{25}$Mg + n channel.
- The spin-parity of the $E_x = 11.784$ MeV state was changed from 1$^-$ to 0$^+$, with fitted $\Gamma_\alpha = 23.952 \times 10^{-3}$ eV and $\Gamma_n = 17.226$ keV.
- The spin-parity of the $E_x = 11.63$ MeV state was changed from 1$^-$ to 0$^+$, with fitted $\Gamma_\alpha = 9.106 \times 10^{-3}$ eV and $\Gamma_n = 14.341$ keV.
- The present reaction rate for $^{22}$Ne($\alpha$, n)$^{25}$Mg is higher by about an order of magnitude compared to the rate reported by Philip et al. at temperatures of 0.2 to 0.3 GK.

## Context

This work extends the evaluation by Philip et al. (2021), who suggested that a full $R$-matrix calculation was needed to account for interference effects between distant levels and sub-threshold resonances in the $^{22}$Ne($\alpha$, n)$^{25}$Mg and $^{22}$Ne($\alpha$, $\gamma$)$^{26}$Mg reactions. The present $R$-matrix analysis directly fits the experimental data of Jaeger et al. (2001) and provides updated resonance parameters, showing that the $^{22}$Ne($\alpha$, n)$^{25}$Mg reaction dominates over the competing $^{22}$Ne($\alpha$, $\gamma$)$^{26}$Mg channel across the entire energy range studied. The results enable more accurate reaction rates for weak s-process nucleosynthesis models in massive stars (M $\geq$ 10 M$_\odot$) and low-mass AGB stars, where these reactions control the neutron flux.
