# Proton Particle-Neutron Hole States in 132Sb with a Realistic Interaction

> The structure of the particle-hole nucleus 132Sb provides a direct source of information on the effective neutron-proton interaction in the 132Sn region. We have performed a shell-model calculation for this nucleus using a realistic effective interaction derived from the Bonn A nucleon-nucleon potential. The results are in very good agreement with the experimental data evidencing the reliability of our effective interaction matrix elements both with isospin T=1 and T=0.

## Key claim

The shell-model calculation using a realistic effective interaction derived from the Bonn A nucleon-nucleon potential reproduces the experimental spectrum of 132Sb with an rms deviation of only 32 keV for positive-parity states, providing the first test of the T=0 matrix elements of this interaction in the 100-150 mass region, though the agreement for electromagnetic transition rates is less definitive due to large experimental uncertainties.

## Method

The authors performed a shell-model calculation for 132Sb using a model space consisting of the five single-particle orbits 0g7/2, 1d5/2, 1d3/2, 2s1/2, and 0h11/2, with a two-body effective interaction derived from the Bonn A nucleon-nucleon potential via a G-matrix formalism including renormalizations from core polarization and folded diagrams. The Q-box was composed of G-matrix diagrams through second order in G, with a Pauli exclusion operator Q2 specified by (n1, n2, n3) = (11, 21, 45) and a shell-model oscillator parameter of hbar omega = 8 MeV. Single-particle energies were assumed identical for neutrons and protons, with values (in MeV): epsilon(d5/2)=0, epsilon(g7/2)=0.20, epsilon(s1/2)=1.72, epsilon(d3/2)=1.88, and epsilon(h11/2)=2.70.

## Key numbers

- rms deviation sigma = 32 keV for positive-parity states in 132Sb, with the largest discrepancy being 77 keV for the 5+1 state
- First excited 8- state lies at 126 keV; the 6-1, 5-1, 7-1, 3-1, and 4-1 states are grouped from 214 to 380 keV
- The 9- and 2- states from the pi g7/2 nu h11/2 configuration are predicted at 1.01 and 1.42 MeV, respectively
- Calculated B(E2; 3+1 -> 4+2) = 42.1 e2 fm4, falling within the experimental value of 36 +/- 11 e2 fm4
- Single-particle energies used (in MeV): epsilon(d5/2)=0, epsilon(g7/2)=0.20, epsilon(s1/2)=1.72, epsilon(d3/2)=1.88, epsilon(h11/2)=2.70
- Calculated position of the 3/2+ single proton level in 133Sb is 2.4 MeV, in agreement with the experimental value of 2.439 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous shell-model studies of 100Sn neighbors (Andreozzi et al., 1996) and N=82 isotones (Andreozzi et al., 1997) to the doubly odd nucleus 132Sb, providing the first test of T=0 matrix elements from the Bonn A potential in this mass region. The calculation improves upon earlier work by Erokhina and Isakov (1994) using the random-phase approximation with a phenomenological interaction, particularly for the higher-lying 1+ states, and demonstrates that the Bonn A potential's weak tensor force provides sufficient attraction for the T=0 effective interaction, addressing a known difficulty with other NN potentials. The results enable future shell-model studies of nuclei with both like and unlike nucleons in valence shells around 132Sn.
