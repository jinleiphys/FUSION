# Relevant polarization observables for the study of the Roper resonance with hadronic probes

> The purpose of this contribution is to stress the great importance of polarization observables for the study of nucleon resonances, in particular in the case of deuteron and proton probes. Polarization observables play a unique role in signing the reaction mechanism and bring new information in the microscopic description of baryon resonances. We will calculate the relevant polarization observables, for $p+d$- and $p+α$-collisions, in case of inclusive reactions and for some selected exclusive channels.

# Relevant polarization observables for the study of the Roper resonance with hadronic probes

## Key claim

Polarization observables in hadronic reactions such as $d+p\rightarrow d+X$ and $p+\alpha\rightarrow p+\pi^0+\alpha$ can provide a complementary tool to electromagnetic probes for studying the isoscalar structure of nucleon resonances, particularly the Roper resonance $P_{11}(1440)$, but only if the reaction mechanism (specifically $\omega$-exchange in the $t$-channel) is correctly identified and validated through experimental tests such as the linear $y^2$-dependence of the differential cross section.

## Method

The authors calculate polarization observables for inclusive $d+p\rightarrow d+X$ and exclusive $p+d\rightarrow p+M^0+d$ (with $M^0=\sigma,\eta,\pi^0$) reactions, and for $p+\alpha\rightarrow p+\pi^0+\alpha$, assuming $t$-channel exchange of mesons ($\omega$, $\sigma$, $\eta$) with definite isospin and spin-parity quantum numbers. They derive expressions for the tensor analyzing power $T_{20}$, vector polarization transfer coefficients $K_a^{a'}$, and analyzing powers in terms of deuteron electromagnetic form factors and isoscalar transition form factors for $N\rightarrow N^*$ excitations, using the collective string model of Bijker, Iachello, and Leviatan (1994, 1996, 1997) to compute the ratio $r(t)$ of longitudinal to transverse isoscalar excitation strengths.

## Key numbers

- The tensor analyzing power $T_{20}$ for $d+p\rightarrow d+X$ shows a minimum near $|t|\simeq 0.35$ GeV$^2$ and approaches zero at larger $|t|$, based on data at incident deuteron momenta of 3.75, 4.5, 5.5, and 9 GeV/c.
- For $r=0$ (no longitudinal isoscalar excitation), $T_{20}$ becomes $t$- and $w$-independent with value $T_{20} = -1/\sqrt{8}$.
- For $r=0$, all vector polarization transfer coefficients $K_x^{x'}=K_y^{y'}=K_z^{z'}=3/8 = 0.375$, $t$-independent.
- At $q=0$ (zero momentum transfer), $K_y^{y'}=1/2$ for any value of $r$.
- The largest sensitivity of $K_y^{y'}$ to $r$ occurs in the region $q \geq 3$ fm$^{-1}$.

## Context

This work extends the $\omega$-exchange model developed by Rekalo and Tomasi-Gustafsson (1996) and Tomasi-Gustafsson et al. (1999) for $d+p\rightarrow d+X$ to exclusive channels and to $p+\alpha$ collisions. It contrasts with electromagnetic probes, which cannot separate isoscalar and isovector contributions due to isospin non-conservation in electromagnetic interactions. The analysis shows that existing $T_{20}$ data from Saclay (LNS250 experiment) and Dubna (Azghirey et al., 1995, 1996) are consistent with $\omega$-exchange and require contributions from four overlapping resonances ($N_{11}(1440)$, $S_{11}(1535)$, $D_{13}(1520)$, $S_{11}(1650)$), with the Roper resonance providing the only nonzero isoscalar longitudinal form factor. Future measurements of polarization observables in $p+\alpha\rightarrow p+\pi^0+\alpha$ can distinguish between $\sigma$-exchange and $\omega$-exchange mechanisms, as the spin-1 nature of $\omega$ induces non-coplanar amplitudes that spin-0 exchange cannot produce.
