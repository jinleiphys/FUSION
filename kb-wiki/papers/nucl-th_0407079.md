# Quantum field theoretic model of metastable resonant spin-singlet state of the np pair

> The np pair in the spin-singlet state is treated as a Cooper np-pair within the extension of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model of light nuclei, describing the deuteron as a Cooper np-pair in the spin-triplet state. For the Cooper np-pair in the spin-singlet state we compute the binding energy and express the S-wave scattering length of np scattering in the spin-singlet state in terms of the binding energy. The theoretical value of the S-wave scattering length of np scattering agrees well with the experimental data.

## Key claim
The np pair in the spin-singlet state can be treated as a Cooper pair within an extension of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model of light nuclei, yielding a binding energy that agrees with the virtual-level energy inferred from the experimental S-wave scattering length, but the theoretical result carries a quoted uncertainty of ±0.012 MeV.

## Method
The authors extended the Nuclear Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NNJL) model, previously applied to the deuteron as a spin-triplet Cooper np-pair, to the spin-singlet state. They defined a local interpolating field operator for the spin-singlet np pair, constructed an effective low-energy Lagrangian with a coupling constant g_S related to the NNJL coupling g_V via g_S = g_V/√3, and integrated over nucleon fields to obtain the effective Lagrangian for the collective S-field. The binding energy was then computed from the renormalized mass of the S-field using a cutoff Λ_D = 46 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Binding energy of the spin-singlet np pair: ε_S = (0.079 ± 0.012) MeV, computed from the NNJL model.
- Experimental binding energy of the virtual level inferred from scattering length: ε_{¹S₀} = 0.074 MeV, derived from a_np = (−23.75 ± 0.01) fm and nucleon mass m_N = 940 MeV.
- Theoretical S-wave scattering length in the spin-singlet state: a_S = (−23.00 ± 1.74) fm, computed from ε_S.
- Experimental S-wave scattering length: a_np = (−23.75 ± 0.01) fm.
- Cutoff parameter: Λ_D = 46 MeV, used to restrict low-energy nucleon fluctuations.

## Context
This work extends the NNJL model of Ivanov et al. (2001–2004), which successfully described the deuteron as a Cooper np-pair in the spin-triplet state, to the spin-singlet channel following a suggestion by Ericson (2004). The model reproduces the virtual-level binding energy and scattering length of np scattering in the ¹S₀ state, enabling a unified quantum field theoretic description of both the bound deuteron and the metastable resonant spin-singlet state. Future work could apply this framework to low-energy nuclear reactions involving the spin-singlet np pair.
