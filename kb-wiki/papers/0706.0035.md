# Universality of Mixed Action Extrapolation Formulae

> Mixed action theories with chirally symmetric valence fermions exhibit very desirable features both at the level of the lattice calculations as well as in the construction and implementation of the low energy mixed action effective field theory. In this work we show that when such a mixed action effective field theory is projected onto the valence sector, both the Lagrangian and the extrapolation formulae become universal in form through next to leading order, for all variants of discretization methods used for the sea fermions. Our conclusion relies on the chiral nature of the valence quarks. The result implies that for all sea quark methods which are in the same universality class as QCD, the numerical values of the physical coefficients in the various mixed action chiral Lagrangians will be the same up to lattice spacing dependent corrections. This allows us to construct a prescription to determine the mixed action extrapolation formulae for a large class of hadronic correlation functions computed in partially quenched chiral perturbation theory at the one-loop level. For specific examples, we apply this prescription to the nucleon twist--2 matrix elements and the nucleon--nucleon system. In addition, we determine the mixed action extrapolation formula for the neutron EDM as this provides a nice example of a theta-dependent observable; these observables are exceptions to our prescription.

## Key claim

The authors show that for mixed action lattice QCD theories using chirally symmetric valence fermions (such as domain-wall or overlap fermions), the low-energy effective field theory Lagrangian and extrapolation formulae become universal in form through next-to-leading order, regardless of the discretization method used for the sea fermions, provided the sea quark methods are in the same universality class as QCD and the valence quarks are chiral.

## Method

The authors construct the mixed action effective field theory Lagrangian by performing a spurion analysis on the continuum Symanzik quark-level Lagrangian, identifying the dimension-6 four-quark operators that mix valence and sea fermions. They then project this Lagrangian onto the valence sector and demonstrate that the leading lattice-spacing-dependent operators are universal in form, with only the numerical coefficients depending on the specific actions used. This allows them to formulate a prescription for converting partially quenched chiral perturbation theory results into mixed action extrapolation formulae for a wide class of observables, with the neutron electric dipole moment identified as an exception requiring modification.

## Key numbers

- The power counting parameter is defined as epsilon^2 ~ m_pi^2 / Lambda_chi^2 ~ a^2 Lambda_chi^2, where a is the lattice spacing and Lambda_chi is the scale of chiral symmetry breaking
- The mixed meson mass receives an additive lattice spacing correction: a^2 Delta_Mix = a^2 (16 C_Mix / f^2), where C_Mix is the universal mixed action coefficient
- The LO meson masses are given by: m_{v1 v2}^2 = B_0 (m_{v1} + m_{v2}) for valence-valence mesons, and tilde{m}_{vs}^2 = B_0 (m_v + m_s) + a^2 Delta_Mix for mixed valence-sea mesons
- The partial quenching parameters are defined as tilde{Delta}_{ju}^2 = tilde{m}_{jj}^2 - m_{uu}^2 = 2 B_0 (m_j - m_u) + a^2 Delta_sea + ... for differences between sea-sea and valence-valence meson masses

## Context

This work extends the mixed action effective field theory framework developed by Bar (2003), Tiburzi (2005), and Chen (2006) by proving the universality of the extrapolation formulae for all chirally symmetric valence fermion actions. The results enable lattice practitioners using the popular mixed action scheme developed by the LHP Collaboration (Renner 2004, Edwards 2005) with domain-wall valence propagators on asqtad-improved staggered sea configurations (MILC Collaboration, Bernard 2001) to apply a single set of extrapolation formulae. The prescription allows conversion of the extensive partially quenched chiral perturbation theory literature into mixed action results, with the neutron electric dipole moment identified as a special case requiring modified treatment due to its dependence on the QCD theta parameter.
