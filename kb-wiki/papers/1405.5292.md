# Temperature dependence of cross sections for meson-meson nonresonant reactions in hadronic matter

> We study unpolarized cross sections for the endothermic nonresonant reactions: pion pion to rho rho for I=2, KK to K*K* for I=1, KK* to K*K* for I=1, pion K to rho K* for I=3/2, pion K* to rho K* for I=3/2, rho K to rho K* for I=3/2, and pion K* to rho K for I=3/2, which take place in hadronic matter. We provide a potential that is given by perturbative QCD with loop corrections at short distances, becomes a distance-independent and temperature-dependent value at long distances, and has a spin-spin interaction with relativistic modifications. The Schrodinger equation with the potential yields temperature-dependent meson masses and mesonic quark-antiquark relative-motion wave functions. In the first Born approximation with the quark-interchange mechanism, the temperature dependence of the potential, meson masses and wave functions brings about temperature dependence of unpolarized cross sections for the seven nonresonant reactions. Noticeably, rapid changes of pion and K radii cause an increase in peak cross sections while the temperature approaches the critical temperature. Parametrizations of the numerical cross sections are given for their future applications in heavy ion collisions.

# Temperature dependence of cross sections for meson-meson nonresonant reactions in hadronic matter

## Key claim
The unpolarized cross sections for seven endothermic nonresonant meson-meson reactions in hadronic matter exhibit a non-monotonic temperature dependence: peak cross sections decrease from zero temperature up to T/Tc ≈ 0.75–0.85, then increase as the temperature approaches the critical temperature Tc, driven by rapid changes in pion and kaon radii near Tc.

## Method
The authors construct a quark-antiquark potential combining perturbative QCD with loop corrections at short distances and a distance-independent, temperature-dependent value at long distances, with a smeared spin-spin interaction incorporating relativistic modifications. They solve the Schrödinger equation with this potential to obtain temperature-dependent meson masses and quark-antiquark relative-motion wave functions, then compute unpolarized cross sections in the first Born approximation using the quark-interchange mechanism for seven reactions: ππ → ρρ (I=2), KK → K*K* (I=1), KK* → K*K* (I=1), πK → ρK* (I=3/2), πK* → ρK* (I=3/2), ρK → ρK* (I=3/2), and πK* → ρK (I=3/2).

## Key numbers
- Mass splitting at T=0: mρ − mπ = 0.6294 GeV (experimental: 0.6304 GeV); mK* − mK = 0.39865 GeV (experimental: 0.3963 GeV)
- Constituent quark masses used: mu = md = 0.32 GeV, ms = 0.5 GeV
- Potential parameters: D = 0.7 GeV, E = 0.6 GeV, A = 1.5[0.75 + 0.25(T/Tc)^10]^6 GeV, σ0 = 0.15 GeV, σ1 = 0.705, Regge slope α' = 1.04 GeV^(−2)
- Cross sections calculated at six temperatures: T/Tc = 0, 0.65, 0.75, 0.85, 0.9, 0.95
- Parametrized meson mass formulas: mπ = 0.221[1 − (T/(0.99Tc))^6.59]^0.84, mρ = 0.73[1 − (T/(0.992Tc))^3.67]^0.989, mK = 0.46[1 − (T/(1.04Tc))^8.58]^0.88, mK* = 0.84[1 − (T/(1.05Tc))^4.16] (all in GeV, valid for 0.6 ≤ T/Tc < 1)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous vacuum calculations of the same seven nonresonant reactions (Xu, 2012) by incorporating temperature effects from hadronic matter, using a potential inspired by lattice QCD results (Kaczmarek et al., 2004) that replaces linear confinement with a distance-independent, temperature-dependent value. The smearing of the Fermi contact term in the spin-spin interaction follows approaches used for meson spectra (Godfrey and Isgur, 1985; Wong, 1986) and charmonium dissociation cross sections (Xu et al., 2010). The temperature-dependent cross sections and their parametrizations are intended for use in heavy-ion collision simulations at RHIC and LHC energies, where meson yield ratios such as π−/π+, K−/K+, and K+/π+ have been measured by PHENIX, STAR, and ALICE collaborations.
