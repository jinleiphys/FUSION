# Open problems in microscopic theory of large-amplitude collective motion

> Construction of the microscopic theory of large-amplitude collective motion, capable of describing a wide variety of quantum collective phenomena in nuclei, is a long-standing and fundamental subject in the study of nuclear many-body systems. Present status of the challenge toward this goal is discussed taking the shape coexistence/mixing phenomena as typical manifestations of the large-amplitude collective motion at zero temperature. Some open problems in rapidly rotating cold nuclei are also briefly discussed in this connection.

# Open problems in microscopic theory of large-amplitude collective motion

## Key claim

The paper argues that constructing a microscopic theory of large-amplitude collective motion (LACM) capable of describing shape coexistence and mixing phenomena remains an open challenge, despite promising developments such as the self-consistent collective coordinate (SCC) method and its adiabatic extension (ASCC). The authors hedge that the field is "still in its infancy" and that the selection of topics reflects their personal interest, not a comprehensive review.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical framework for extracting a collective submanifold from the time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (TDHFB) phase space, using the moving-frame HFB equation and moving-frame QRPA (local harmonic) equations to determine the collective coordinate, momentum, and infinitesimal generators self-consistently at each point along the collective path. They apply the adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate (ASCC) method to the oblate-prolate shape coexistence phenomena in proton-rich nuclei such as ⁶⁸Se and ⁷²Kr, solving the collective Schrödinger equation after requantization.

## Key numbers

- The first excited 0⁺ state in ⁷²Ge lies at 0.69 MeV, below the first excited 2⁺ state at 0.83 MeV.
- The minimum of the excited 0⁺ state excitation energy occurs around neutron number N=40, where neutron pairs begin occupying the g₉/₂ shell.
- The ASCC method has been applied to ⁶⁸Se and ⁷²Kr, revealing that ⁶⁸Se exhibits intermediate features between oblate-prolate shape coexistence and γ-soft rotors, while in ⁷²Kr the collective wave function localization in the (β,γ) plane develops significantly with increasing rotational angular momentum.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing effort to construct microscopic theories of LACM, tracing back to Belyaev (1965), Baranger and Kumar (1965), and the ATDHF approaches of Baranger and Vénéroni (1978), Brink et al. (1976), and Goeke and Reinhard (1978). It builds on the self-consistent collective coordinate method of Marumori et al. (1980) and its adiabatic extension by Matsuo et al. (2000). The paper identifies open problems including the treatment of time-odd mean-field effects on the collective mass, the relationship between different definitions of collective mass (cranking, ASCC, GCM-GOA, ATDHFB), and the need for deeper understanding of the pair-hopping mechanism at level crossings. The work enables future systematic microscopic analyses of shape coexistence and mixing phenomena across a wide range of nuclei.
