# Color fluctuation effects in proton-nucleus collisions

> Color fluctuations in hadron-hadron collisions are responsible for the presence of inelastic diffraction and lead to distinctive differences between the Gribov picture of high energy scattering and the low energy Glauber picture. We find that color fluctuations give a larger contribution to the fluctuations of the number of wounded nucleons than the fluctuations of the number of nucleons at a given impact parameter. The two contributions for the impact parameter averaged fluctuations are comparable. As a result, standard procedures for selecting peripheral (central) collisions lead to selection of configurations in the projectile which interact with smaller (larger) than average strength. We suggest that studies of pA collisions with a hard trigger may allow to observe effects of color fluctuations.

## Key claim

Color fluctuations in high-energy proton-nucleus collisions produce a larger contribution to the fluctuations of the number of wounded nucleons than the fluctuations of nucleon positions at a given impact parameter, and the two contributions become comparable only after averaging over impact parameter. Standard experimental procedures for selecting peripheral (central) collisions inadvertently select projectile configurations that interact with smaller (larger) than average interaction strength.

## Method

The authors developed a Monte Carlo event generator that extends the Glauber model by including event-by-event fluctuations of the total hadron-nucleon cross section according to a distribution \(P_h(\sigma_{tot})\) satisfying the Miettinen-Pumplin relation. They compared results from this full color fluctuation model, a simplified two-component (quasi-eikonal) model, and the standard Glauber model with fixed cross section, using both an optical approximation and a full Monte Carlo treatment that accounts for the finite radius of the nucleon-nucleon interaction and short-range correlations between nucleons.

## Key numbers

- For RHIC energies (\(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV), the variance \(\omega_N\) of the wounded-nucleon distribution in the optical model increases from 0.46 (Glauber) to 0.70 (full color fluctuation model); in the Monte Carlo calculation it increases from 0.51 to 0.72.
- For LHC energies, the optical model variance increases from 0.51 (Glauber) to 0.74 (full color fluctuation model); the Monte Carlo calculation gives an increase from 0.59 to 0.77.
- In the two-component model with \(\omega_\sigma = 0.25\), the two cross section components are \(\sigma^{(1)}_{tot}/\sigma^{hN}_{tot} = 0.5\) and \(\sigma^{(2)}_{tot}/\sigma^{hN}_{tot} = 1.5\), giving a ratio of 3 between the weak and strong components.
- The average number of wounded nucleons \(\langle N \rangle\) changes by only a few percent when color fluctuations are included: at RHIC from 4.6 (Glauber MC) to 4.8 (full fluctuation MC); at LHC from 6.7 to 6.8.
- For \(\omega=0.25\), selecting events with the same number of wounded nucleons as the average at \(b=0\) corresponds to impact parameter \(b \simeq 4.58\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the Gribov-Glauber theory of high-energy hadron-nucleus scattering, which Gribov (1968) formulated by expressing nuclear shadowing through diffraction cross sections, and builds on the Good and Walker (1960) formalism for inelastic diffraction. The authors implement the concept of color fluctuations first pointed out by Baym (1994, 1995) but never analyzed in detail before. The results enable experimental tests using pA collisions at the LHC, particularly by selecting events with hard triggers at large \(x_p > 0.6\) to probe configurations of smaller than average size, which could test the explanation of the EMC effect at large \(x\) as due to dominance of small-size configurations in nucleons (Frankfurt, 2012).
