# $^9$Be scattering within a four-body continuum-discretized coupled-channels framework

> The scattering of $^9$Be on $^{208}$Pb is addressed within a four-body Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) framework, considering a three-body $α+ α+ n$ projectile plus a structureless target. The projectile states are generated using the analytical Transformed Harmonic Oscillator (THO) basis in hyperspherical coordinates. Both the elastic and breakup channels are described on the same footing. We find a good agreement between our calculations and the experimental data at beam energies around and below the Coulomb barrier.

# $^9$Be scattering within a four-body continuum-discretized coupled-channels framework

## Key claim
The four-body Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) framework, treating $^9$Be as a three-body $\alpha + \alpha + n$ projectile, reproduces experimental elastic scattering and total breakup cross sections for $^9$Be on $^{208}$Pb at beam energies around and below the Coulomb barrier, but the calculations systematically underestimate the elastic data in the nuclear-Coulomb interference region, especially at 44 MeV.

## Method
The $^9$Be projectile was described as a three-body $\alpha + \alpha + n$ system using the analytical Transformed Harmonic Oscillator (THO) basis in hyperspherical coordinates to generate pseudo-states for the continuum. The coupled-channels equations were solved including $^9$Be states with $j^\pi = 3/2^\pm, 5/2^\pm, 1/2^\pm$ and multipole couplings to all orders, with the cutoff energy and number of projectile states fixed for convergence. Elastic scattering and breakup angular distributions were calculated on the same footing for the $^9$Be + $^{208}$Pb system.

## Key numbers
- Elastic cross sections relative to Rutherford were calculated at 44 MeV and 38 MeV (around and below the Coulomb barrier, respectively), showing improved agreement with experimental data from Woolliscroft (2004) and Yu (2010) when continuum couplings were included.
- Integrated breakup cross sections at 44 MeV and 38 MeV were 112.0 mb and 80.5 mb, respectively.
- The calculated breakup cross sections were compared to experimentally reported values of 92.1 mb (44 MeV) and 84.1 mb (38 MeV) from Woolliscroft (2003).

## Context
This work extends the CDCC method, originally developed for two-body projectiles by Austern (1987), to a four-body framework for the Borromean nucleus $^9$Be, which has no bound binary subsystems ($^8$Be or $^5$He). It builds on prior pseudo-state approaches for three-body projectiles by Rodriguez-Gallardo (2008) and Descouvemont (2015), and on the THO basis method developed by Casal (2013, 2014). The results enable future exclusive Coulomb dissociation experiments to test the model's simultaneous description of elastic and breakup processes, and highlight the need for further analysis of discrepancies in the nuclear-Coulomb interference region.
