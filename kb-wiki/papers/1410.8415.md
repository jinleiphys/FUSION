# $α_{s}$ from the (revised) ALEPH data for $τ$ decay

> We present a new analysis of $α_s$ from hadronic $τ$ decays based on the recently revised ALEPH data. The analysis is based on a strategy which we previously applied to the OPAL data. We critically compare our strategy to the one traditionally used and comment on the main differences. Our analysis yields the values $α_s(m_τ^2)=0.296\pm 0.010$ using fixed-order perturbation theory, and $α_s(m_τ^2)=0.310\pm 0.014$ using contour-improved perturbation theory. Averaging these values with our previously obtained values from the OPAL data, we find $α_s(m_τ^2)=0.303\pm 0.009$, respectively, $α_s(m_τ^2)=0.319\pm 0.012$, as the most reliable results for $α_s$ from $τ$ decays currently available.

## Key claim

The analysis yields values of the strong coupling constant from hadronic tau decays that are systematically lower than those from the traditional analysis, with larger uncertainties that reflect the inclusion of systematic effects from duality violations and higher-dimension condensates, which the traditional approach neglects.

## Method

The authors use finite-energy sum rules relating hadronic tau decay spectral functions to the operator product expansion (OPE) of the vacuum polarization, including an explicit parametrization of duality violations (DVs) based on large-Nc and Regge theory. They perform simultaneous fits to the ALEPH vector and axial-vector spectral functions for the strong coupling constant, OPE condensates, and DV parameters using three polynomial weights (w0=1, w2=1-y^2, w3=(1-y)^2(1+2y)) over a window of s0 values from 1.55 GeV^2 up to m_tau^2, rather than using only the endpoint at s0=m_tau^2 as in the traditional approach.

## Key numbers

- alpha_s(m_tau^2) = 0.296 ± 0.010 using fixed-order perturbation theory (FOPT) from the revised ALEPH data alone
- alpha_s(m_tau^2) = 0.310 ± 0.014 using contour-improved perturbation theory (CIPT) from the revised ALEPH data alone
- Averaged with previous OPAL results: alpha_s(m_tau^2) = 0.303 ± 0.009 (FOPT)
- Averaged with previous OPAL results: alpha_s(m_tau^2) = 0.319 ± 0.012 (CIPT)
- The fit window uses s_min = 1.55 GeV^2 as the lower bound for the fitting range

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous strategy applied to OPAL data (Boito et al., 2012, 2013) to the recently revised ALEPH data (ALEPH Collaboration, 2013), which corrected correlation matrix problems discovered by Maltman and collaborators (2010). The analysis contradicts the traditional approach pioneered by Braaten, Narison, and Pich (1992) and used in the ALEPH (2013) and OPAL analyses, which neglects duality violations and sets higher-dimension condensates to zero. The results enable a more reliable extraction of alpha_s from tau decays by demonstrating that the traditional method suffers from self-consistency problems, and they motivate future improvements from Belle and BaBar data as well as better theoretical understanding of the OPE and DVs.
