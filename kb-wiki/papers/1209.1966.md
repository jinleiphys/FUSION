# Strangeness production in antiproton-nucleus annihilation

> The results of the microscopic transport calculations of $\bar p$-nucleus interactions within a GiBUU model are presented. The dominating mechanism of hyperon production is the strangeness exchange processes $\bar K N \to Y π$ and $\bar K N \to ΞK$. The calculated rapidity spectra of $Ξ$ hyperons are significantly shifted to forward rapidities with respect to the spectra of $S=-1$ hyperons. We argue that this shift should be a sensitive test for the possible exotic mechanisms of $\bar p$-nucleus annihilation. The production of the double $Λ$-hypernuclei by $Ξ^-$ interaction with a secondary target is calculated.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the forward shift of $\Xi$ hyperon rapidity spectra relative to $S=-1$ hyperons should be a sensitive test for exotic mechanisms of $\bar p$-nucleus annihilation, but this claim is conditional on the assumption that the shift would vanish if strangeness were produced from a supercooled quark-gluon plasma rather than from hadronic two-body collisions.

## Method

The authors performed microscopic transport calculations using the Giessen Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) model, which solves relativistic transport equations for baryons, antibaryons, and mesons including mean-field potentials and collision terms. The calculations included annihilation channels, strangeness exchange reactions ($\bar K N \to Y \pi$ and $\bar K N \to \Xi K$), meson-nucleon collisions ($M N \to Y K$), and hyperon rescattering processes. For double-$\Lambda$ hypernuclei, they first calculated the momentum spectrum of emitted $\Xi^-$ in primary $\bar p$-nucleus reactions, then computed the production cross section of double-$\Lambda$ hyperfragments from $\Xi^-$ interaction with a secondary target.

## Key numbers

- $\Lambda$ production cross sections in $\bar p$ interactions with $^{12}$C, $^{64}$Cu, and $^{208}$Pb nuclei were calculated as a function of beam momentum and compared to experimental data from Ahmad (1997) and INC model calculations from Gibbs (1990)
- Approximately 60-80% of $S=-1$ hyperon production rate is due to $\bar K N \to Y X$, $\bar K N \to Y^*$, and $\bar K N \to Y^* \pi$ reactions
- The threshold $\bar K$ beam momentum for the endothermic $\bar K N \to \Xi K$ channel is 1.048 GeV/c, corresponding to a $\bar K N$ center-of-mass rapidity of 0.55
- The peaks of $\Xi^-$ rapidity distributions are shifted forwards by 0.5-1 units of rapidity relative to $\Lambda$ peaks in $\bar p+^{197}$Au collisions at 3, 9, and 15 GeV/c
- Double-$\Lambda$ hyperfragment production cross section in $\Xi^- + ^{64}$Cu interactions reaches hundreds of millibarns below 0.8 GeV/c $\Xi^-$ beam momentum

## Context

This work extends earlier intranuclear cascade (INC) model calculations by Cugnon (1990) and Gibbs (1990) that supported the usual mechanism of strangeness production via binary hadron-hadron collisions, while also addressing the original proposal by Rafelski and Muller (1982) of strangeness enhancement in a quark-gluon plasma. The results are directly relevant to the future PANDA experiment at FAIR, as described by Pochodzalla (2005), which plans to use a primary target to produce $\Xi^-$ hyperons that will be captured by a secondary target nucleus to create double-$\Lambda$ hypernuclei. The calculations enable experimental tests to distinguish between hadronic production mechanisms and QGP hadronization in antiproton-nucleus annihilation.
