# Parity Violation in Astrophysics

> Core collapse supernovae are gigantic explosions of massive stars that radiate 99% of their energy in neutrinos. This provides a unique opportunity for large scale parity or charge conjugation violation. Parity violation in a strong magnetic field could lead to an asymmetry in the neutrino radiation and recoil of the newly formed neutron star. Charge conjugation violation in the neutrino-nucleon interaction reduces the ratio of neutrons to protons in the neutrino driven wind above the neutron star. This is a problem for r-process nucleosynthesis in this wind. On earth, parity violation is an excellent probe of neutrons because the weak charge of a neutron is much larger than that of a proton. The Parity Radius Experiment (PREX) at Jefferson Laboratory aims to precisely measure the neutron radius of $^{208}$Pb with parity violating elastic electron scattering. This has many implications for astrophysics, including the structure of neutron stars, and for atomic parity nonconservation experiments.}

# Parity Violation in Astrophysics

## Key claim
Core collapse supernovae, which radiate 99% of their energy in neutrinos, may exhibit large-scale parity or charge conjugation violation; however, explicit calculations of the neutrino asymmetry from parity violation have proved complicated and uncertain, and a very strong magnetic field of order 10^15 gauss or more may be required to produce the few percent asymmetry needed to explain observed neutron star velocities.

## Method
The paper reviews the role of weak interactions in core collapse supernovae, examining how parity violation in a strong magnetic field could produce an asymmetry in neutrino emission, and how charge conjugation violation in neutrino-nucleon cross sections alters the neutron-to-proton ratio in the neutrino-driven wind. It also describes the Parity Radius Experiment (PREX) at Jefferson Laboratory, which will measure the parity-violating asymmetry A for elastic scattering of 850 MeV electrons from 208Pb at a scattering angle of six degrees, using exact numerical solutions of the Dirac equation to account for Coulomb distortions.

## Key numbers
- Core collapse supernovae radiate 99% of their energy in neutrinos, with the neutrino burst involving 10^58 neutrinos and carrying about 3 × 10^53 ergs.
- The proto-neutron star has a gravitational binding energy of over 100 MeV/nucleon and a radius of order 10 kilometers.
- Charge conjugation violation reduces the neutron-to-proton ratio in the neutrino-driven wind by 20%.
- PREX aims to measure the parity-violating asymmetry A to ±3%, allowing determination of the neutron rms radius to 1% (±0.05 fm).
- The experiment uses 850 MeV electrons scattering from 208Pb at a scattering angle of six degrees.
- Coulomb distortions reduce the parity-violating asymmetry A by about 30%.
- Many neutron stars have observed galactic velocities of 500 km/s or larger, presumably from kicks at birth.

## Context
This work extends the discussion of parity violation in astrophysical contexts, building on prior studies of neutrino transport in supernovae and neutron star kicks (PVSN). It addresses the long-standing problem of r-process nucleosynthesis in the neutrino-driven wind, noting that present simulations do not have enough neutrons to synthesize all r-process elements, and identifies alternative sites such as neutron star mergers and accretion disks around black holes. The PREX measurement connects to atomic parity nonconservation experiments (Colorado Cs experiment) and to neutron star structure, where combining PREX results with neutron star radius measurements could indicate exotic high-density phases such as quark matter, strange matter, pion or kaon condensates, or color superconductivity.
