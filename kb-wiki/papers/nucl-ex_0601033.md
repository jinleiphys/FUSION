# Identified hadron spectra at large transverse momentum in p+p and d+Au collisions at \sqrts_NN = 200 GeV

> We present the transverse momentum (pT) spectra for identified charged pions, protons and anti-protons from p+p and d+Au collisions at \sqrts_NN = 200 GeV. The spectra are measured around midrapidity (|y| < 0.5) over the range of 0.3 < pT < 10 GeV/c with particle identification from the ionization energy loss and its relativistic rise in the Time Projection Chamber and Time-of-Flight in STAR. The charged pion and proton+anti-proton spectra at high pT in p+p and d+Au collisions are in good agreement with a phenomenological model (EPOS) and with the next-to-leading order perturbative quantum chromodynamic (NLO pQCD) calculations with a specific fragmentation scheme and factorization scale. We found that all proton, anti-proton and charged pion spectra in p+p collisions follow xT-scalings for the momentum range where particle production is dominated by hard processes (pT > 2 GeV/c). The nuclear modification factor around midrapidity are found to be greater than unity for charged pions and to be even larger for protons at 2 < pT < 5 GeV/c.

## Key claim

The paper reports that identified charged pion and proton+anti-proton spectra at high transverse momentum in both p+p and d+Au collisions at 200 GeV agree with NLO pQCD calculations and the EPOS model, but only when using a specific fragmentation scheme and factorization scale; the agreement is not universal across all fragmentation function sets.

## Method

The STAR Collaboration measured transverse momentum spectra for charged pions, protons, and anti-protons around midrapidity (|y| < 0.5) over 0.3 < pT < 10 GeV/c using particle identification from ionization energy loss (dE/dx) in the Time Projection Chamber and Time-of-Flight detectors. A total of 8.2 million minimum bias p+p events and 11.7 million d+Au events were analyzed, with particle identification below pT = 2.5 GeV/c from TOF and above from the relativistic rise of dE/dx in the TPC. The spectra were compared to NLO pQCD calculations using KKP and AKK fragmentation functions and to the EPOS phenomenological model.

## Key numbers

- Spectra measured over pT range 0.3 < pT < 10 GeV/c at |y| < 0.5
- 8.2 million minimum bias p+p events and 11.7 million d+Au events analyzed
- Minimum-bias trigger captured 95 +/- 3% of the 2.21 +/- 0.09 barn d+Au inelastic cross-section
- Tracking efficiencies: ~88% in p+p and ~92% in d+Au collisions
- Momentum resolution: Delta(pT)/pT = 0.01 + 0.005 pT/(GeV/c)
- Systematic uncertainty for pion yields: <~ 15%
- Systematic uncertainty for proton and anti-proton yields at pT > 2.5 GeV/c: 12% to 23% (at pT = 10 GeV/c)
- Normalization uncertainties: 14% for p+p and 10% for d+Au collisions
- RdAu for charged pions at 2 < pT < 5 GeV/c: 1.24 +/- 0.13 in minimum bias collisions
- RdAu for p+anti-p at 2 < pT < 5 GeV/c: 1.49 +/- 0.17 in minimum bias collisions
- Anti-p/p ratio in p+p collisions: 0.81 +/- 0.1 at 2.5 < pT < 6.5 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends prior measurements of identified hadron spectra at RHIC by providing reference spectra for p+p and d+Au collisions at the same energy as Au+Au collisions, enabling studies of jet quenching and quark recombination in heavy-ion collisions. The results are compared to lower energy measurements from ISR (ISR 53 GeV) and FNAL fixed target experiments, and to NLO pQCD calculations using KKP and AKK fragmentation functions. The nuclear modification factors (RdAu) greater than unity for both pions and protons provide constraints on models of cold nuclear matter effects including nuclear shadowing, parton saturation, and transverse momentum broadening, as discussed in work by Vogt (2004).
