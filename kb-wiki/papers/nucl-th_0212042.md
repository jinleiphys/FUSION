# Weak axial nuclear heavy meson exchange currents and interactions of solar neutrinos with deuterons

> Starting from the axial heavy meson exchange currents, constructed earlier in conjunction with the Bethe--Salpeter equation, we first present the axial $ρ$--, $ω$-- and $a_1$ meson exchange Feynman amplitudes that satisfy the partial conservation of the axial current. Employing these amplitudes, we derive the corresponding weak axial heavy meson exchange currents in the leading order in the 1/M expansion ($M$ is the nucleon mass), suitable for the nuclear physics calculations beyond the threshold energies and with wave functions obtained by solving the Schrödinger equation with one--boson exchange potentials. The constructed currents obey the nuclear form of the partial conservation of the axial current. We apply the space component of these currents in calculations of the cross sections for the disintegration of deuterons by low energy (anti)neutrinos. The deuteron and the final state nucleon--nucleon wave functions are derived (i) from a variant of the OBEPQB potential, and (ii) from the Nijmegen 93 and Nijmegen I nucleon-nucleon interaction. The extracted values of the constant $L_{1, A}$, entering the axial exchange currents of the pionless effective field theory, are in a reasonable agreement with its value predicted by the dimensional analysis.

## Key claim

The paper constructs weak axial heavy meson exchange currents (WANECs) for the \(\rho\), \(\omega\), and \(a_1\) meson ranges that satisfy the nuclear form of partial conservation of the axial current (PCAC), making them suitable for nuclear physics calculations beyond threshold energies and with wave functions from one-boson exchange potentials, but the authors note that the model dependence should be expected at higher energies because it is of short range nature.

## Method

Starting from chiral Lagrangians for the \(N\Delta\pi\rho\omega a_1\) system that respect chiral symmetry and vector dominance, the authors first present relativistic two-nucleon weak axial exchange Feynman amplitudes for \(\rho\), \(\omega\), and \(a_1\) meson exchanges that satisfy PCAC. They then derive the corresponding nuclear exchange currents in the leading order in the \(1/M\) expansion (where \(M\) is the nucleon mass) by subtracting the first Born iteration of the one-nucleon current from the relativistic amplitudes, and apply the space component of these currents to calculate cross sections for deuteron disintegration by low energy (anti)neutrinos using deuteron and final-state nucleon-nucleon wave functions from the OBEPQB potential and from the Nijmegen 93 and Nijmegen I interactions.

## Key numbers

- The extracted values of the constant \(L_{1,A}\) (entering the axial exchange currents of pionless effective field theory) are in reasonable agreement with its value predicted by dimensional analysis (no explicit numerical value for \(L_{1,A}\) is given in the truncated text).
- The cross sections for backward deuteron electrodisintegration calculated in prior work (STG) describe data up to \(Q^2 \approx 1.2\ (\text{GeV}/c)^2\).
- The expansion parameter for the non-relativistic reduction is \(Q/M_h\), where \(Q\) is momentum or momentum transfer and \(M_h\) is heavy meson or nucleon mass; the model currents are expected to be valid up to energies/momenta \(\approx 0.4\ \text{GeV}\).
- The \(\kappa^B\) value for the \(\rho\) meson vertex current is \(\kappa^V_\rho \approx 6.6\).

## Context

This work extends the Tree Approximation Approach (TAA) for constructing meson exchange currents, previously applied to the pion range (AHHST, TK1, MRT1), to the heavy meson (\(\rho\), \(\omega\), \(a_1\)) range, providing currents that satisfy nuclear PCAC and are suitable for consistent calculations with one-boson exchange potentials. It enables improved calculations of weak processes in light nuclei such as solar neutrino-deuteron reactions, tritium beta decay, and muon capture, and provides a test of soundness for the TAA by comparison with existing effective field theory calculations (MB1, MB2) and with data. The approach contrasts with effective field theories that integrate out heavy mesons and are restricted to long wavelength limits (\(r \ge 0.6\) fm, as noted by PMR).
