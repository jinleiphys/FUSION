# Periodic-Orbit Bifurcations and Superdeformed Shell Structure

> We have derived a semiclassical trace formula for the level density of the three-dimensional spheroidal cavity. To overcome the divergences occurring at bifurcations and in the spherical limit, the trace integrals over the action-angle variables were performed using an improved stationary phase method. The resulting semiclassical level density oscillations and shell-correction energies are in good agreement with quantum-mechanical results. We find that the bifurcations of some dominant short periodic orbits lead to an enhancement of the shell structure for "superdeformed" shapes related to those known from atomic nuclei.

## Key claim

The authors derive a semiclassical trace formula for the level density of the three-dimensional spheroidal cavity that remains regular at all bifurcation points and in the spherical limit, using an improved stationary phase method. They find that bifurcations of short periodic orbits lead to an enhancement of the shell-structure amplitude near the bifurcation points, which they argue contributes to the stability of superdeformed shapes in atomic nuclei.

## Method

The authors evaluate trace integrals over action-angle variables using an improved stationary phase method (ISPM) that keeps the integration limits finite, rather than extending them to infinity as in the standard stationary-phase method. They apply this to the three-dimensional spheroidal cavity model, which is integrable but exhibits bifurcations of periodic orbits and symmetry-breaking transitions. The resulting semiclassical level density and shell-correction energy are expressed as sums over families of periodic orbits with amplitudes that remain finite through bifurcation points, using error functions and, where necessary, incomplete Airy and Gairy integrals.

## Key numbers

- The equatorial "star" orbit (5,2) undergoes a bifurcation at deformation parameter eta = 1.618..., where the 3D orbit (5,2,1) is born; the 3D orbit does not exist below this deformation.
- The double triangle 2(3,1) and the 3D orbit (6,2,1) have a bifurcation point at eta = sqrt(3) = 1.732...
- The most important orbits at superdeformation eta = 2 are the 3D orbits (5,2,1), (6,2,1), (7,2,1), and (8,2,1).
- The Gaussian averaging parameter used for the level density is gamma = 0.3.
- The most significant contributions near critical deformations come from bifurcating orbits with lengths smaller than about 10R.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous semiclassical approach from the two-dimensional elliptic billiard (Magner et al., 1999) to the three-dimensional spheroidal cavity, which serves as a simple model for deformed atomic nuclei and metal clusters. The results agree with the Fourier transform analysis of quantum level densities by Arita, Sugita, and Matsuyanagi (1998) at the same deformations. The paper enables a deeper semiclassical understanding of shell-structure effects in superdeformed fermionic systems, including not only nuclei and metal clusters but also deformed semiconductor quantum dots.
