# Fate of in-medium heavy quarks via a Lindblad equation

> What is the dynamics of heavy quarks and antiquarks in a quark gluon plasma? Can heavy-quark bound states dissociate? Can they (re)combine? These questions are addressed by investigating a Lindblad equation that describes the quantum dynamics of the heavy quarks in a medium. The Lindblad equations for a heavy quark and a heavy quark-antiquark pair are derived from the gauge theory, following a chain of well-defined approximations. In this work the case of an abelian plasma has been considered, but the extension to the non-abelian case is feasible. A one-dimensional simulation of the Lindblad equation is performed to extract information about bound-state dissociation, recombination and quantum decoherence for a heavy quark-antiquark pair. All these phenomena are found to depend strongly on the imaginary part of the inter-quark potential.

## Key claim

The paper derives a Lindblad master equation for heavy quarks in a quark-gluon plasma from first-principles gauge theory, but only for an abelian (QED-like) plasma rather than full QCD; the author states that extension to the non-abelian case is feasible but not performed here. All conclusions about bound-state dissociation, recombination, and quantum decoherence depend strongly on the imaginary part of the inter-quark potential.

## Method

The author starts from the gauge-theory Hamiltonian for heavy nonrelativistic quarks and antiquarks interacting with a thermal bath of light particles via Coulomb interactions, then integrates out the plasma degrees of freedom to obtain an influence functional. From this influence functional, a Lindblad master equation for the reduced density matrix of the heavy particles is read off, with all Lindblad terms expressed solely in terms of the imaginary part of the inter-quark potential. A one-dimensional numerical simulation of the Lindblad equation is then performed for the relative motion of a heavy quark-antiquark pair, after tracing out the center-of-mass motion, to extract bound-state probabilities and linear entropy.

## Key numbers

- The heavy quark mass \(m\) is taken to be much larger than the plasma temperature \(T\) (i.e., \(m \gg T\), with \(k_B = c = 1\)).
- The Debye mass \(m_D\) appears in the real part of the potential as \(V(x) = -\frac{\hbar}{4\pi x} e^{-m_D x/\hbar}\) and in the imaginary part \(W(x)\) via an integral over \(z\) up to \(\Lambda/m_D\).
- The coupling constant is \(g\), with \(\alpha_s = g^2/(4\pi)\) used as the expansion parameter for the perturbative influence functional up to order \(g^2\).
- The time step in the discretized path integral is \(\Delta t\), with the total time interval \([t_0, t]\) partitioned into \(N\) steps of size \(\Delta t\).

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical results of Blaizot (2015), who derived a Langevin equation from the same gauge-theory setup, to a full quantum master equation in Lindblad form that preserves positivity of the density operator. It contrasts with prior approaches using screened potential models (Mocsy, 2013), lattice spectral function reconstructions (Petreczky, 2012; Aarts, 2011-2014), and the stochastic Schrodinger equation (Akamatsu, 2011-2014; Rothkopf, 2013; Kajimoto, 2017), which either cannot describe recombination or lack a clear derivation from the underlying theory. The Lindblad equation enables simultaneous study of dissociation, recombination, and quantum decoherence for heavy quarkonia, and can in principle be generalized to \(N\) heavy quarks and antiquarks.
