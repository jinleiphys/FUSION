# New Bayesian analysis of hybrid EoS constraints with mass-radius data for compact stars

> We suggest a new Bayesian analysis using disjunct mass and radius constraints for extracting probability measures for cold, dense nuclear matter equations of state. One of the key issues of such an analysis is the question of a deconfinement transition in compact stars and whether it proceeds as a crossover or rather as a first order transition. The latter question is relevant for the possible existence of a critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram under scrutiny in present and upcoming heavy-ion collision experiments.

# New Bayesian analysis of hybrid EoS constraints with mass-radius data for compact stars

## Key claim
The paper presents a Bayesian analysis framework that uses disjunct mass and radius constraints to assign probabilities to hybrid equations of state (EoS) for compact stars, with the specific aim of determining whether a deconfinement transition in neutron star matter proceeds as a crossover or a first-order transition. The analysis suggests that high-mass twin star configurations, which would indicate a strong first-order phase transition, have the highest probability measure under the chosen observational constraints, but this result depends on the inclusion of a baryonic excluded volume correction and the choice of hadronic EoS.

## Method
The authors use the Alford-Han-Prakash (AHP) scheme to construct hybrid EoS with a first-order phase transition from hadronic to quark matter, parametrized by the transition energy density, the energy density jump, and the squared speed of sound in quark matter. They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for 2197 parameter combinations per hadronic EoS, using APR and DD2 hadronic EoS with and without baryonic excluded volume corrections. Bayesian probabilities are assigned to each EoS parameter set using three independent observational constraints: the mass of PSR J0348+0432, the radius of PSR J0437-4715, and the mass-baryon mass relation for PSR J0737-3039(B).

## Key numbers
- Mass constraint for PSR J0348+0432: mean 2.01 solar masses, standard deviation 0.04 solar masses (Antoniadis 2013)
- Radius constraint for PSR J0437-4715: mean 15.5 km, standard deviation 1.5 km (Bogdanov 2012)
- Mass and baryon mass constraints for PSR J0737-3039(B): gravitational mass mean 1.249 solar masses with standard deviation 0.001 solar masses, baryon mass mean 1.36 solar masses with standard deviation 0.002 solar masses (Kitaura 2006)
- Parameter ranges explored: transition energy density 400 to 1200 MeV/fm^3, energy density jump ratio gamma from 0 to 1.5, squared speed of sound in quark matter from 0.3 to 1.0
- Grid size: 13 values per parameter, giving 2197 hybrid EoS per hadronic EoS choice

## Context
This work extends the Bayesian analysis approach first applied to neutron star EoS by Steiner et al. (2010), who considered only X-ray bursters under strongly model-dependent assumptions. The present analysis uses a broader set of constraints including a mass-radius relation and a mass-baryon mass relation, and focuses on the possible existence of deconfined quark matter in massive neutron stars such as the 2 solar mass pulsars observed by Demorest (2010) and Antoniadis (2013). The results enable future tests of the high-mass twin star phenomenon through precise radius measurements of 2 solar mass pulsars, which would constrain the existence of a critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram.
