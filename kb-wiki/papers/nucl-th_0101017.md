# Proton-Proton Fusion in Effective Field Theory to Fifth Order

> The proton-proton fusion process p p->d e^+ nu_e is calculated at threshold to fifth order in pionless effective field theory. There are two unknown two-body currents contributing at the second and fourth orders. Combined with the previous results for neutrino-deuteron and antineutrino-deuteron scattering, computed to third order in the same approach, we conclude that a 10% measurement of reactor antineutrino-deuteron scattering measurement could constrain the p p->d e^+ nu_e rate to ~7% while a ~3% measurement of nu_e d-> e^- p p could constrain the pp rate to ~2%.

# Proton-Proton Fusion in Effective Field Theory to Fifth Order

## Key claim

The proton-proton fusion rate can be calculated to 1% precision using pionless effective field theory, but the result depends on an unknown two-body axial counterterm \(L_{1,A}\) that must be determined from neutrino-deuteron scattering experiments; a second counterterm \(K_{1,A}\) entering at fourth order contributes at less than the 1% level and can be estimated by dimensional analysis.

## Method

The authors compute the threshold matrix element for \(pp \rightarrow de^{+}\nu_e\) to fifth order in the \(Q\) expansion of pionless effective field theory (\(\mathrm{EFT}(\pi\hskip-0.6em/)\)), treating nucleons as non-relativistic with contact interactions and including Coulomb effects between protons. The hadronic axial current is expanded to include one-body and two-body currents, with the two-body counterterms \(L_{1,A}\) (second order) and \(K_{1,A}\) (fourth order) absorbing short-distance physics. The calculation is performed in the zero-recoil limit, and the renormalization scale is set to the pion mass \(m_\pi\).

## Key numbers

- The zero-energy matrix element is \(\Lambda(0) = 2.58 + 0.011\,(L_{1,A} / 1\ \mathrm{fm}^3) - 0.0003\,(K_{1,A} / 1\ \mathrm{fm}^5)\), with the \(K_{1,A}\) term contributing at less than 1% for natural-sized counterterms.
- Dimensional analysis estimates \(|L_{1,A}| \approx 6\ \mathrm{fm}^3\) and \(|K_{1,A}| \approx 20\ \mathrm{fm}^5\).
- The \(pp\) channel scattering length is \(a_{pp} = -7.82\ \mathrm{fm}\); the deuteron binding momentum is \(\gamma = 45.69\ \mathrm{MeV}\); the \(pp\) effective range is \(r_0^{(pp)} = 2.79\ \mathrm{fm}\); the deuteron effective range is \(\rho_d = 1.764\ \mathrm{fm}\).
- Fits to potential models yield \(L_{1,A}^{\mathrm{NSGK}} = 5.6 \pm 2\ \mathrm{fm}^3\) (Nakamura, Sato, Gudkov, Kubodera) and \(L_{1,A}^{\mathrm{YHH}} = 0.94 \pm 2\ \mathrm{fm}^3\) (Ying, Haxton, Henley).
- The Schiavilla et al. \(pp\) fusion rate (\(\Lambda^2(0) = 7.05\)–\(7.06\)) corresponds to \(L_{1,A}^{\mathrm{Schiavilla}} = 6.5 \pm 2.4\ \mathrm{fm}^3\).
- A 10% measurement of reactor \(\overline{\nu}_e d\) scattering could constrain the \(pp\) fusion rate to 7%; a 3% measurement of \(\nu_e d \rightarrow e^- pp\) could constrain it to 2%.

## Context

This work extends the pionless EFT treatment of neutrino-deuteron scattering by Butler and Chen (2000) to the \(pp\) fusion process at higher order, establishing a direct connection between the two reactions through the common counterterm \(L_{1,A}\). The calculation reproduces the range of potential model results from Nakamura et al. (2000) and Ying, Haxton, and Henley (1992), showing that their \(\sim 5\%\) difference arises from different treatments of short-distance axial two-body currents. The results enable experimental constraints from reactor antineutrino-deuteron scattering or the proposed ORLaND detector to determine the solar \(pp\) fusion rate, which is inaccessible to direct measurement but critical for solar neutrino physics.
