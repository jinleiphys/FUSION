# Chiral Transparency

> Color transparency is the vanishing of initial and final state interactions, predicted by QCD to occur in high momentum transfer quasielastic nuclear reactions. For specific reactions involving nucleons, the initial and final state interactions are expected to be dominated by exchanges of pions. We argue that these interactions are also suppressed in high momentum transfer nuclear quasielastic reactions; this is ``chiral transparency". We show that studies of the $e ^3He \to e'Δ^{++} nn$ reaction could reveal the influence of chiral transparency.

## Key claim

The authors argue that in high momentum transfer quasielastic nuclear reactions, not only color interactions (color transparency) but also pion-exchange interactions are suppressed for point-like configurations produced by the hard scattering; this suppression, termed "chiral transparency," is predicted to cause the cross section for reactions requiring pion exchange (such as $e + ^3He \to e' + \Delta^{++} + nn$) to vanish as $Q^2$ increases, much faster than conventional calculations would predict.

## Method

The authors model the nucleon wave function in a hadronic Fock state basis and assume that at high $Q^2$ ($> 1$ GeV$^2$) the electromagnetic probe produces a point-like configuration (PLC) in which the pion-quark interaction vanishes. They incorporate the time evolution of the PLC using a quantum diffusion model where the pion-baryon coupling constant grows with propagation distance according to $g_{\pi B,B'}(r) = g^0_{\pi B,B'} (1 - \kappa e^{-r/l_c})$, with $l_c = 2p/\mu^2$ the Lorentz-dilated expansion length and $\kappa = 1 - Q_0^2/Q^2$ for $Q^2 \geq Q_0^2$. They compute the cross section for $\gamma^* + (pp) \to Bp \to \Delta^{++}n$ in $^3He$ using a unitary model of coupled $NN \oplus N\Delta \oplus \pi NN$ reactions, and replace the intermediate baryon propagator $G_{B'}$ with a modified propagator $G^\chi_{B'}$ to implement chiral transparency.

## Key numbers

- The threshold for chiral transparency onset is parameterized by $Q_0^2$; the authors use values including $Q_0^2 = 0.55$ GeV$^2$ in their first set of results.
- The expansion length scale is $l_c = 2p/\mu^2$; for the (e,e'p) reaction $\mu^2 \approx 0.7$ GeV$^2$, while for the present case they estimate $\mu^2 = m_N/r_\pi \approx 0.3$ GeV$^2$.
- The pion-nucleon form factor used is a dipole with cutoff $\Lambda = 650$ MeV, corresponding to a three-quark confinement radius of 1.33 fm.
- The physical $\Delta$ mass used is 1236 MeV.
- The energy-transfer $\omega$ of the virtual photon is set to approximately 300 MeV to emphasize excitation of a proton to a $\Delta$ state.
- The harmonic oscillator parameter $b$ for the initial $pp$ wave function is taken as 1 fm in the first set of results.
- The ratio $CT \equiv d\sigma^\chi/d\sigma$ is computed for $Q^2$ values ranging from below 1 GeV$^2$ to approximately 6 GeV$^2$, and is shown to differ significantly from unity at fairly low $Q^2$ for all values of $\mu^2$ displayed.

## Context

This work extends the concept of color transparency, originally developed for gluon-exchange interactions (Low 1975; Frankfurt, Miller, Strikman 1985, 1992, 1994), to pion-exchange interactions in high momentum transfer quasielastic nuclear reactions. The authors build on the cloudy bag model (CBM) and Lee's (1983) unitary model of baryon-baryon interactions, and they note that previous color transparency experiments (Carroll et al. 1988 found evidence; NE18 experiment by Makins et al. 1994 did not) can be reconciled by including PLC expansion effects. The proposed $e + ^3He \to e' + \Delta^{++} + nn$ reaction at Jefferson Lab could provide a test of chiral transparency, and if observed, would provide evidence that high $Q^2$ electron-proton scattering proceeds via formation of a point-like configuration.
