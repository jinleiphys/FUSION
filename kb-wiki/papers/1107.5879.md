# A Higgs Quadruplet for Type III Seesaw and Implications for $μ\to eγ$ and $μ- e$ Conversion

> In Type III seesaw model the heavy neutrinos are contained in leptonic triplet representations. The Yukawa couplings of the triplet fermion and the left-handed neutrinos with the doublet Higgs field produce the Dirac mass terms. Together with the Majorana masses for the leptonic triplets, the light neutrinos obtain non-zero seesaw masses. We point out that it is also possible to have a quadruplet Higgs field to produce the Dirac mass terms to facilitate the seesaw mechanism. The vacuum expectation value of the quadruplet Higgs is constrained to be small by electroweak precision data. Therefore the Yukawa couplings of a quadruplet can be much larger than those for a doublet. We also find that unlike the usual Type III seesaw model where at least two copies of leptonic triplets are needed, with both doublet and quadruplet Higgs representations, just one leptonic triplet is possible to have a phenomenologically acceptable model because light neutrino masses can receive sizable contributions at both tree and one loop levels. Large Yukawa couplings of the quadruplet can induce observable effects for lepton flavor violating processes $μ\to e γ$ and $μ- e$ conversion. Implications of the recent $μ\to eγ$ limit from MEG and also limit on $μ- e $ conversion on Au are also given. Some interesting collider signatures for the doubly charged Higgs boson in the quadruplet are discussed.

## Key claim

The authors propose that introducing a Higgs quadruplet (rather than only the standard doublet) in the Type III seesaw model allows the Yukawa couplings to be much larger than in the usual setup, because the quadruplet's vacuum expectation value is constrained to be small (below 5.8 GeV) by electroweak precision data. This can make lepton flavor violating processes like μ→eγ and μ–e conversion observable, and with both doublet and quadruplet Higgs representations, just one leptonic triplet can produce a phenomenologically acceptable neutrino mass matrix (rank two) via combined tree-level and one-loop contributions.

## Method

The authors extend the Type III seesaw by adding a Higgs quadruplet χ (1,4,−1/2) whose Yukawa couplings with the leptonic triplet Σ_R and left-handed lepton doublets generate Dirac mass terms. They compute the tree-level and one-loop contributions to the light neutrino mass matrix, showing that the loop contribution (mediated by the quadruplet scalars and heavy leptons) raises the rank from one to two, allowing a fit to neutrino oscillation data with only one generation of Σ_R. They then calculate the branching ratio for μ→eγ and the conversion rate for μ–e on nuclei (Au, Ti, Al) using the one-loop effective Lagrangian, and compare with experimental limits from MEG and SINDRUM II.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum expectation value of the quadruplet Higgs is constrained to be less than 5.8 GeV (from Δρ = 0.0004^{+0.0029}_{-0.0011} at 95% c.l.).
- For normal hierarchy with m_N = 1 TeV, the required Yukawa couplings are y_ν ∼ 1.80×10⁻⁶ (m_N/1 TeV)^{1/2} and √κ y_χ = 0.38×10⁻⁶ (m_N/1 TeV)^{1/2}.
- For inverted hierarchy with m_N = 1 TeV, y_ν ∼ 1.78×10⁻⁶ (m_N/1 TeV)^{1/2} and √κ y_χ = 0.90×10⁻⁶ (m_N/1 TeV)^{1/2}.
- The MEG 90% c.l. upper limit on B(μ→eγ) is 2.4×10⁻¹², which constrains the quadruplet Yukawa coupling y_χ to be less than about 0.1 for a wide range of parameter space (with m_χ = 1 TeV).
- The best experimental bound on μ–e conversion is for Au: B^{Au}_{μ→e} < 7×10⁻¹³ (90% c.l.).
- Future sensitivities: B(μ→eγ) = 1×10⁻¹³ (MEG upgrade), μ–e conversion on Al/Ti down to 10⁻¹⁶ (COMET) and 10⁻¹⁸ (PRISM).

## Context

This work extends the Type III seesaw mechanism (which normally requires at least two leptonic triplets to fit neutrino data) by introducing a Higgs quadruplet, following earlier studies of Higgs quadruplets with different hypercharge (e.g., a (1,4,3/2) quadruplet that gives neutrino masses only at dimension-7). It contrasts with the standard Type III seesaw with only a doublet Higgs, where Yukawa couplings are typically below 10⁻⁵ for TeV-scale heavy neutrinos, making LFV effects tiny. The model enables testable predictions for μ→eγ and μ–e conversion at current and near-future experiments, and also predicts collider signatures for the doubly charged Higgs boson in the quadruplet. The one-loop rank-raising mechanism is similar to that in neutrinophilic two-Higgs-doublet models (He and Tsumura, 2009) but avoids tree-level flavor-changing neutral currents for charged leptons.
