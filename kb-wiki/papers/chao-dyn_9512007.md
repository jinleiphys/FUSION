# Folded Bifurcation in Coupled Asymmetric Logistic Maps

> A system of coupled two logistic maps, one periodic and the other chaotic, is studied. It is found that with the variation of the coupling strength, the system displays several curious features such as the appearance of quadrupling of period, occurrence of isolated period three attractor and the coexistence of the Hopf and pitchfork bifurcations. Possible applications and extensions are discussed.

# Folded Bifurcation in Coupled Asymmetric Logistic Maps

## Key claim

By linearly coupling two logistic maps with different strength parameters, one in a period-one stable attractor and the other in the chaotic regime, the system exhibits a folded period-doubling bifurcation sequence where a period-four cycle appears directly from period one, bypassing the usual period-two intermediate. This inversion of the bifurcation sequence, previously unobserved in simple maps, emerges naturally from the coupling without requiring special tuning of the map function.

## Method

The authors study two linearly coupled logistic maps, fixing one strength parameter above the chaos threshold (μ = 4.0) and the other below it (ν = 3.0 or ν = 2.0), with the coupling strength ε as the control parameter varied from 0 to 1. They numerically solve for periodic points of periods N = 3, 4, and 8 by finding fixed points of the N-th iterate of the map, and identify bifurcations by computing the maximum eigenvalue of the Jacobian matrix of the N-th iterate. Phase diagrams and spatial portraits of the attractors are generated for selected parameter values.

## Key numbers

- The period-four cycle appears at approximately ε = 0.77 for the case μ = 4.0, ν = 3.0.
- The period-two cycle starts at ε = 0.75 in the same parameter set, occurring after the quadrupling of period when moving from larger to smaller ε.
- The Hopf bifurcation onset occurs at approximately ε = 0.85 for μ = 4.0, ν = 3.0.
- A period-three cycle appears around ε = 0.87 in the same system.
- The critical value of the logistic map parameter for the onset of chaos is μ* = 3.56994.

## Context

This work extends the coupled-map lattice approach developed by Kaneko (1983, 1984, 1985, 1986) by relaxing the condition of identical strength parameters across coupled maps, which Kaneko's original models required. The authors find that loosening this uniformity condition produces novel bifurcation sequences not seen in identical coupled maps or in single logistic maps. The results suggest that coupled lattice maps with "impurities" (elements with different strength parameters) may exhibit non-conventional features, and the authors propose that similar folded bifurcations should be observable in higher-dimensional coupled map systems.
