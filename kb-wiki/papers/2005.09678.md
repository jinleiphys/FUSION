# Medium modification of $γ$-jet fragmentation functions in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC

> Coupled linear Boltzmann transport and hydrodynamic (CoLBT-hydro) model has been developed for simultaneous simulations of jet propagation and jet-induced medium excitation in heavy-ion collisions. Within this coupled approach, the final reconstructed jets in heavy-ion collisions include not only hadrons from the hadronization of medium modified jet shower partons from the linear Boltzmann transport (LBT) but also hadrons from the freeze-out of the jet-induced medium excitation in the hydrodynamic evolution of the bulk medium. Using the CoLBT-hydro model, we study medium modification of the fragmentation functions of $γ$-triggered jets in high-energy heavy-ion collisions at the Large Hadron Collider. The CoLBT-hydro model is shown to describe the experimental data not only on the suppression of leading hadrons within the jet cone at large momentum fraction $z_γ=p_T^h/p_T^γ$ relative to the transverse momentum of the trigger photon due to parton energy loss but also the enhancement of soft hadrons at small $z_γ$ and $z_{\rm jet}=p_T^h/p_T^{\rm jet}$ due to jet-induced medium excitation. There is no suppression of the fragmentation function, however, at large momentum fraction $z_{\rm jet}$ relative to the transverse momentum of the reconstructed jet due to trigger bias and medium modification of quark to gluon jet fraction. For jets whose final transverse momenta are comparable to or larger than that of the trigger photon, the trigger bias can lead to enhancement of the jet fragmentation function at large $z_{\rm jet}$.

## Key claim

The CoLBT-hydro model, which couples jet transport with hydrodynamic medium evolution, describes the experimental data on medium modification of gamma-jet fragmentation functions in Pb+Pb collisions, showing that the enhancement of soft hadrons at large momentum fraction variables arises from jet-induced medium excitation while the suppression at small xi_gamma_T is due to parton energy loss; however, there is no suppression at large z_jet relative to the reconstructed jet momentum due to trigger bias and medium modification of the quark-to-gluon jet fraction.

## Method

The authors developed the CoLBT-hydro model, coupling the Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model for jet propagation with the CLVisc (3+1)D relativistic hydrodynamic model for bulk medium evolution, where energy and momentum from soft partons below a cutoff of 2.0 GeV/c are deposited as a source term into the hydrodynamic equations. Initial gamma-jet events were generated using Pythia8, and jet reconstruction was performed with the anti-kT algorithm using FASTJET, with hadronization handled by a parton recombination model for both p+p and Pb+Pb collisions. The ratio of fragmentation functions from Pythia8 to the recombination model in p+p was applied as a multiplicative correction to the CoLBT-hydro results in Pb+Pb collisions.

## Key numbers

- Effective strong coupling constant set to alpha_s = 0.16, the only parameter controlling parton energy loss in LBT
- Energy cutoff for separating soft and hard partons: p^0_cut = 2.0 GeV/c in the comoving frame of the fluid cell
- Initial time tau_0 = 0.2 fm/c and freeze-out temperature T_f = 137 MeV, adjusted to reproduce charged hadron rapidity distributions
- Trigger photon transverse momentum range: p_T^gamma > 60 GeV/c with pseudo-rapidity |eta^gamma| < 1.44 for CMS comparison
- Reconstructed jets selected with |eta^jet| < 1.6 and p_T^jet > 30 GeV/c, azimuthal angle Delta_phi_jgamma > 7pi/8
- For ATLAS comparison: trigger photon 80 < p_T^gamma < 126 GeV/c, final jets 63 < p_T^jet < 144 GeV/c with |eta^jet| < 2.1
- Charged hadron minimum transverse momentum: p_T^h > 1 GeV/c
- Jet cone sizes studied: R = 0.2, 0.3, 0.4
- Collision energy: sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV for Pb+Pb collisions

## Context

This work extends the CoLBT-hydro model previously applied to gamma-hadron correlations at RHIC (Chen, 2017) to gamma-jet fragmentation functions at LHC energies, building on the LBT model developed by Wang (2013) and others for jet transport in QGP. The results are compared to experimental data from the CMS Collaboration (Sirunyan, 2018) and ATLAS Collaboration (Aaboud, 2019), showing that jet-induced medium excitation is essential to describe the soft hadron enhancement. The approach enables future studies of the kinematic dependence of medium modification by varying trigger photon or jet transverse momenta, and can be extended to investigate baryon-to-meson ratios in reconstructed jets.
