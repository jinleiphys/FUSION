# Structure Function Sum rules for Systems with Large Scattering Lengths

> We use a dispersion relation in conjunction with the operator product expansion (OPE) to derive model independent sum rules for the dynamic structure functions of systems with large scattering lengths. We present an explicit sum rule for the structure functions that control the density and spin response of the many-body ground state. Our methods are general, and apply to either fermions or bosons which interact through two-body contact interactions with large scattering lengths. By employing a Borel transform of the OPE, the relevant integrals are weighted towards infrared frequencies, thus allowing for greater overlap low energy data. Similar sum rules can be derived for other response functions. The sum rules can be used to extract the contact parameter introduced by Tan, including universality violating corrections at finite scattering lengths.

## Key claim

The authors derive model-independent sum rules for dynamic structure functions of systems with large scattering lengths by combining the operator product expansion (OPE) with Borel-transformed dispersion relations, but these sum rules are valid only in the energy window where the frequency \(\omega\) satisfies \(\mu \ll \omega \ll 1/(2 m r_0^2)\), where \(\mu\) is the chemical potential and \(r_0\) is the range of the two-body potential.

## Method

The authors use the operator product expansion (OPE) to determine the asymptotic large-frequency behavior of the Feynman Green's function for the density-density correlator, then combine this with a Borel-transformed dispersion relation (originally developed for QCD sum rules by Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov) to obtain weighted integrals of the imaginary part of the retarded Green's function. The Wilson coefficients in the OPE are computed exactly up to dimension-5 operators by matching matrix elements between one-particle and two-particle states in the non-relativistic field theory with contact interactions. The resulting sum rule relates a Borel-weighted frequency integral of the dynamic structure factor to thermodynamic condensates including the number density, energy density, and the Tan contact parameter.

## Key numbers

* The OPE expansion for the density-density correlator is valid in the energy window \(\mu \ll \omega \ll 1/(2 m r_0^2)\), where \(\mu\) is the chemical potential, \(m\) is the particle mass, and \(r_0\) is the range of the two-body potential.
* The Wilson coefficient for the number density operator \(n(0)\) in the OPE is \(C_n(q) = 2i E_{\vec q} / [(\omega - E_{\vec q})(\omega + E_{\vec q})]\), where \(E_{\vec q} = {\vec q}^2/(2m)\).
* The two-body scattering amplitude in the center-of-mass frame is \({\cal A} = -(4\pi/m) \cdot 1/(1/a + i\sqrt{m\omega})\), with scattering length \(a = mg/(4\pi)\).
* The Borel-transformed sum rule is given by \((1/\omega_0^2) \int_0^\infty (d\omega^2/\pi) e^{-\omega^2/\omega_0^2} \text{Im} G_R(\omega) = \sum_\alpha [a_\alpha({\vec q})/\Gamma(\alpha)] (\omega_0^2)^{-\alpha}\), where \(\omega_0\) is a free parameter that can be chosen to emphasize low-energy data.

## Context

This work extends the universal relations for systems with large scattering lengths originally developed by Tan (2005-2008) and later reformulated using the OPE by Braaten and Platter (2008). The sum rule methodology is adapted from the QCD sum rule approach of Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1979). The results enable extraction of the Tan contact parameter \({\cal C}\) from low-energy experimental data on dynamic structure functions, including universality-violating corrections at finite scattering lengths, and are applicable to both fermionic systems and bosons near unitarity such as helium-4.
