# Systematic analysis of inner crust composition using the extended Thomas-Fermi approximation with pairing correlations

> We perform a systematic investigation of the chemical composition of the inner crust of a neutron star, using the extended Thomas-Fermi approximation, the Strutinsky integral correction for shell effects, and the BCS approximation for pairing. Fifteen Skyrme functionals were selected, which cover the range of values of important bulk properties of infinite nuclear matter, while also having pure neutron matter (PNM) equation of states (EoS) with varying degrees of stiffness. We find that a functional's low-density PNM EoS is correlated with the number of protons found in the inner crust's nuclear clusters and, in the lower-density region of the inner crust, with the pressure.

## Key claim

The chemical composition of the inner crust (specifically the proton number Z of nuclear clusters) is correlated with the stiffness of a Skyrme functional's pure neutron matter equation of state at low densities, rather than with the symmetry energy J at saturation density as previously suggested. Functionals with a soft PNM EoS favor Z=20 clusters, while those with a stiff PNM EoS favor Z=40 clusters.

## Method

The authors used the extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) method with Strutinsky integral (SI) corrections for shell effects and BCS pairing correlations (ETFSI+pairing) to calculate the energy per particle of Wigner-Seitz cells in the inner crust. They selected 15 Skyrme functionals covering a range of infinite nuclear matter properties and pure neutron matter EoS stiffness, then minimized the total energy per particle at fixed baryonic densities between 0.00025 and 0.05 fm^{-3} to determine the proton number Z and cell radius R_WS.

## Key numbers

- Baryonic density range studied: n_b in [0.00025, 0.05] fm^{-3}
- Proton number range searched: Z in [16, 60]
- At n_b = 0.03 fm^{-3}, all Soft-set functionals favor Z=20, while Stiff-set functionals favor Z=40
- At around n_b = 0.02 fm^{-3}, the proton fraction Y_p varies by up to a factor of 2 between the Soft and Stiff sets
- SQMC650 maintains Z=40 up to n_b ≈ 0.04 fm^{-3}, the highest among the Soft set
- BSk24 has symmetry energy J = 30 MeV, SLy4 has J = 32 MeV, KDE0v1 has J = 34.6 MeV, yet all three Stiff-set functionals give Y_p largely in the range 0.03-0.04
- Soft-set functionals (BSk22, LNS, NRAPRii, SKRA, SQMC700) have Y_p mostly in the range 0.02-0.025 despite J ranging from 31.3 to 33.4 MeV

## Context

This work extends the ETFSI+pairing method previously developed by the same authors (Shelley 2020, Universe 2020) and compares results across 15 Skyrme functionals. It contradicts the claim by Pearson (2018, 2019) that the symmetry energy J is the primary driver of Y_p in the inner crust, showing instead that the low-density PNM EoS is the key factor. The results are consistent with findings by Baldo (2005) that Z=20 is favored by functionals compatible with ab-initio calculations. This work enables tighter constraints on Skyrme functionals using low-density ab-initio PNM calculations to produce more reliable unified neutron star equations of state.
