# Pion and kaon condensation at zero temperature in three-flavor $Ď‡$PT at nonzero isospin and strange chemical potentials at next-to-leading order

> We consider three-flavor chiral perturbation theory ($Ď‡$PT) at zero temperature and nonzero isospin ($ÎĽ_{I}$) and strange ($ÎĽ_{S}$) chemical potentials. The effective potential is calculated to next-to-leading order (NLO) in the $Ď€^{\pm}$-condensed phase, the $K^{\pm}$-condensed phase, and the $K^0/\bar{K}^0$-condensed phase. It is shown that the transitions from the vacuum phase to these phases are second order and take place when, $|ÎĽ_I|=m_Ď€$, $|{1\over2}ÎĽ_I+ÎĽ_S|=m_K$, and $|-{1\over2}ÎĽ_I+ÎĽ_S|=m_K$, respectively at tree level and remains unchanged at NLO. The transition between the two condensed phases is first order. The effective potential in the pion-condensed phase is independent of $ÎĽ_S$ and in the kaon-condensed phases, it only depends on the combinations $\pm{1\over2}ÎĽ_I+ÎĽ_S$ and not separately on $ÎĽ_I$ and $ÎĽ_S$. We calculate the pressure, isospin density and the equation of state in the pion-condensed phase and compare our results with recent $(2+1)$-flavor lattice QCD data. We find that the three-flavor $Ď‡$PT results are in good agreement with lattice QCD for $ÎĽ_I<200$ MeV, however for larger values $Ď‡$PT produces values for observables that are consistently above lattice results. For $ÎĽ_I>200$ MeV, the two-flavor results are in better agreement with lattice data. Finally, we consider the observables in the limit of very heavy $s$-quarks, where they reduce to their two-flavor counterparts with renormalized couplings. The disagreement between the predictions of two and three flavor $Ď‡$PT can largely be explained by the differences in the experimental values of the low-energy constants.

## Key claim

The transitions from the vacuum phase to the pion-condensed, charged kaon-condensed, and neutral kaon-condensed phases are second order and occur at the tree-level thresholds $|\mu_I| = m_\pi$, $|\frac{1}{2}\mu_I + \mu_S| = m_K$, and $|-\frac{1}{2}\mu_I + \mu_S| = m_K$, respectively, and these critical values remain unchanged at next-to-leading order (NLO). The three-flavor chiral perturbation theory results for pressure, isospin density, and equation of state agree well with lattice QCD for isospin chemical potentials below 200 MeV, but above this value the three-flavor results consistently overestimate observables compared to lattice data.

## Method

The authors computed the effective potential to next-to-leading order (NLO) in three-flavor chiral perturbation theory at zero temperature, using the $\mathcal{O}(p^4)$ Lagrangian with eight relevant low-energy constants $L_i$ and one contact term $H_2$, renormalized in the $\overline{\text{MS}}$ scheme. They expanded the Lagrangian around three distinct condensed ground states (pion-condensed, charged kaon-condensed, and neutral kaon-condensed) using a rotated parametrization that ensures canonical kinetic terms and proper renormalization of divergences. The pressure, isospin density, and equation of state were then calculated in the pion-condensed phase and compared with $(2+1)$-flavor lattice QCD data from Refs. [gergy1, gergy2, gergy3].

## Key numbers

- The second-order phase transition from vacuum to pion-condensed phase occurs at $|\mu_I| = m_\pi$ (tree level and NLO unchanged).
- The second-order phase transition from vacuum to charged kaon-condensed phase occurs at $|\frac{1}{2}\mu_I + \mu_S| = m_K$ (tree level and NLO unchanged).
- The second-order phase transition from vacuum to neutral kaon-condensed phase occurs at $|-\frac{1}{2}\mu_I + \mu_S| = m_K$ (tree level and NLO unchanged).
- Three-flavor $\chi$PT results agree well with lattice QCD for $\mu_I < 200$ MeV; for $\mu_I > 200$ MeV, two-flavor results agree better with lattice data.
- The tree-level pion mass relation is $m_{\pi,0}^2 = 2B_0 m$, and the tree-level kaon mass relation is $m_{K,0}^2 = B_0(m + m_s)$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous two-flavor NLO chiral perturbation theory study (Adhikari and Andersen, 2019) to include strange quark effects via three-flavor $\chi$PT, motivated by the need to compare with $(2+1)$-flavor lattice QCD simulations (Kogut and Sinclair, 2002; Brandt and Endrodi, 2016). The results enable improved modeling of pion stars (first discussed by Carignano, 2017) and provide a systematic comparison between effective field theory and lattice QCD at finite isospin density, showing that the discrepancy between two- and three-flavor predictions arises largely from differences in the experimental values of the low-energy constants.
