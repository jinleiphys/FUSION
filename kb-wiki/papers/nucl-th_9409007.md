# Open Charm as a Probe of Pre-Equilibrium Dynamics in Nuclear Collisions?

> The pre-equilibrium contribution to open charm production in nuclear collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ AGeV is calculated using three different models for the correlations between momentum and space-time coordinates. Ideal (Bjorken) correlation between the rapidity $y$ and space-time rapidity $η$ of mini-jet gluons suppresses greatly the pre-equilibrium yield and even allowing for the minimal uncertainty correlations leads, in contrast to previous estimates, only to a small pre-equilibrium charm yield as compared to initial yield due to gluon fusion. The ``intrinsic'' charm process is negligible in the mid-rapidity domain.

## Key claim

Pre-equilibrium open charm production in nuclear collisions at √s = 200 AGeV is found to be a very small fraction of the initial gluon fusion rate, in contrast to previous estimates that suggested enhancements of up to an order of magnitude. The suppression arises from correlations between momentum rapidity y and space-time rapidity η, which reduce the yield by about a factor of 40 relative to the uncorrelated case.

## Method

The authors calculated the pre-equilibrium charm yield from mini-jet gluon fusion using three models for η-y correlations: ideal Bjorken (perfect correlation), uncorrelated fireball, and a minimal correlation based on the uncertainty principle with a spatial spread d = 0.2/p⊥ fm. The mini-jet gluon spectrum was parameterized from HIJING Monte Carlo results including initial and final state radiation, and the pre-equilibrium charm production was computed via phase-space integration of the gluon fusion cross section over the evolving parton distribution.

## Key numbers

- Pre-equilibrium charm yield in the Bjorken-correlated case is suppressed by a factor of approximately 40 relative to the uncorrelated fireball case (N_Fb/N_Bj ∼ 35 from analytic estimate)
- The effective p⊥ cutoff for gluon fusion is p_c ∼ 1.0 GeV in the fireball case versus p_c ∼ 1.73 GeV in the Bjorken case, due to the rapidity difference allowing lower-p⊥ gluons to interact
- The pre-equilibrium stage is terminated at τ_max ∼ 0.9 fm/c for the hard mini-jet distribution and ∼ 1.7 fm/c for the soft+hard distribution, when energy density falls to ∼ 2 GeV/fm³
- The Lorentzian versus θ-function formation-time prescriptions differ by only about 10% in the resulting charm p⊥ distribution
- Using a new fit with more very soft gluons (0.265 e^{-2.6 p⊥} for p⊥ < 1.1 GeV) changes the minimally-correlated result by less than 10%

## Context

This work extends the debate between Müller and Wang (1992), who found pre-equilibrium charm comparable to initial fusion, and Geiger (1993), who claimed over an order-of-magnitude enhancement. The authors resolve the discrepancy by showing that Geiger's result came from including incoherent flavor excitation processes (which suffer destructive interference as shown by Collins, Ellis, and others) and using low-energy A^α scaling with α=0.76 instead of the Glauber value α=1 appropriate at high energies. The study enables future work to focus on initial gluon fusion as the dominant charm source, with pre-equilibrium contributions too small to serve as a sensitive probe of early-time dynamics.
