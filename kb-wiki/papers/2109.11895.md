# Constraining nuclear matter parameters and neutron star observables using PREX-2 and NICER data

> We try to constraints some of the nuclear matter parameters such as symmetry energy ($J$) and its slope ($L$) from the recent inferred data of the PREX-2. Other nuclear matter parameters are adopted from {\bf [Phys. Rev. C 85 035201 (2012), Phys. Rev. C 90 055203 (2014)]} papers and the linear correlation among them are checked by using the Pearson's formula. We find the correlation between $J-L$, $K_τ-J$ and $K_τ-L$ with coefficients 0.85, 0.81 and 0.76 respectively. The neutron star properties such as mass and radius are calculated with 50 unified equation of states. The results are consistent with recently observed pulsars and NICER data except few exceptions. From the radii constraints, we find that the new NICER data allows a narrow radius range contrary to a large range of PREX-2 and the old NICER data leaving us an inconclusive determination of the neutron star radius.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the PREX-2 data supports stiffer equations of state (EOSs) with larger symmetry energy and radius values, while the new NICER data supports softer EOSs with a narrower radius range, leaving the determination of the neutron star radius inconclusive.

## Method

The authors used 224 relativistic mean-field (RMF), 240 Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF), 7 density-dependent RMF (DD-RMF), and 18 point-coupling (PC) parameter sets from Dutra et al. (2012, 2014) to calculate nuclear matter properties. They constrained the symmetry energy (J) and its slope (L) using the PREX-2 inferred data from Reed et al. (2021). For neutron star properties, they selected 50 unified equations of state from Fortin et al. (2016) and Biswal et al. (2020) and solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to compute mass-radius profiles.

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy J = 38.1 ± 4.7 MeV and slope L = 106 ± 37 MeV, inferred from PREX-2 data by Reed et al. (2021)
- Correlation coefficients: J-L = 0.85, K_tau-J = 0.81, K_tau-L = 0.76, calculated using Pearson's formula
- PREX-2 neutron skin thickness of 208Pb: R_skin = 0.283 ± 0.071 fm (Adhikari et al., 2021)
- New NICER radius range for the canonical star: 12.45 ± 0.65 km (Miller et al., 2021)
- Reed et al. (2021) radius range for canonical star from PREX-2 and old NICER data: 13.25 to 14.26 km
- Old NICER radius range: 11.52 to 14.26 km (Miller et al., 2019; Riley et al., 2019)
- New NICER radius range: 11.8 to 13.1 km (Miller et al., 2021)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Reed et al. (2021) by testing a large set of nuclear matter parameter sets against the PREX-2 and NICER constraints. It builds on prior studies of nuclear matter parameters by Dutra et al. (2012, 2014) and neutron star EOSs by Fortin et al. (2016) and Biswal et al. (2020). The paper highlights the tension between the stiffer EOSs favored by PREX-2 data and the softer EOSs favored by new NICER data, motivating future experiments to resolve the neutron star radius uncertainty.
