# Chiral Superfluidity for the Heavy Ion Collisions

> We argue that the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma formed at LHC and RHIC can be considered as a chiral superfluid. The "normal" component of the fluid is the thermalized matter in common sense, while the "superfluid" part consists of long wavelength (chiral) fermionic states moving independently. We use the bosonization procedure with a finite cut-off and obtain a dynamical axion-like field out of the chiral fermionic modes. Then we use relativistic hydrodynamics for macroscopic description of the effective theory obtained after the bosonization. Finally, solving the hydrodynamic equations in gradient expansion, we find that in the presence of external electromagnetic fields the motion of the "superfluid" component gives rise to the chiral magnetic, chiral electric and dipole wave effects. Latter two effects are specific for a two-component fluid, which provides us with crucial experimental tests of the model. By considering probe quarks one can show that the fermionic spectrum at the intermediate temperatures (T_c < T < 2 T_c) has a gap between near-zero modes and the bulk of the spectrum - one more hint supporting the two-component model.

## Key claim
The strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma at LHC and RHIC can be modeled as a chiral superfluid, but only in the intermediate temperature range between the critical temperature and roughly twice that temperature (T_c < T < 2 T_c), where the fermionic spectrum shows a gap between near-zero modes and the bulk of the spectrum.

## Method
The author bosonizes QCD with a finite cutoff, obtaining a dynamical axion-like field from the chiral fermionic infrared modes. Relativistic hydrodynamics is then applied to the effective theory, and the hydrodynamic equations are solved in gradient expansion to extract transport effects. The model treats the plasma as a two-component fluid: a curl-free "superfluid" component of near-zero quark modes described by a pseudo-scalar field, and a thermalized "normal" component of dressed quarks described by a four-velocity field.

## Key numbers
- The cutoff parameter Λ is given by Λ = π√(2/3) √(T² + μ²/π²) at high temperatures
- At strong magnetic fields, Λ = 2√(|eB|)
- At small temperatures and weak or absent magnetic fields, Λ ≃ 3 GeV
- The model applies in the temperature range T_c < T < 2 T_c, where a gap separates near-zero modes from the bulk of the fermionic spectrum

## Context
This work extends the relativistic superfluid formalism of Son (2000) to the quark-gluon plasma context, building on the chiral magnetic effect proposed by Kharzeev (2007) and related work on chiral magnetic waves. The model provides a hydrodynamic framework for the chiral magnetic effect, chiral electric effect, and a new chiral dipole wave effect, offering experimental tests for two-component fluid behavior in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
