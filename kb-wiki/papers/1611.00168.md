# A chiral covariant approach to $ρρ$ scattering

> We analyze vector meson - vector meson scattering in a unitarized chiral theory based on a chiral covariant framework. We show that a pole assigned to the the scalar meson $f_0(1370)$ can be dynamically generated from the $ρρ$ interaction, while this is not the case for the tensor meson $f_2(1270)$ as found in earlier works. We show that the generation of the tensor state is untenable due to an artefact of the extreme non-relativistic kinematics used before. We further consider the effects arising from the coupling of channels with different orbital angular momenta. We suggest to use the formalism outlined here to obtain more reliable results for the dynamical generation of resonances in the vector-vector interaction.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the scalar meson $f_0(1370)$ can be dynamically generated from $\rho\rho$ interactions in a chiral covariant framework, but the tensor meson $f_2(1270)$ cannot be generated in this way, contrary to earlier claims. The generation of the tensor state in prior work is shown to be an artifact of the extreme non-relativistic kinematics used previously.

## Method

The authors analyze $\rho\rho$ scattering using a unitarized chiral theory based on hidden local symmetry, retaining the full covariant structure of the $\rho$ propagator rather than the non-relativistic approximation used in prior work. They perform partial-wave projection of the tree-level amplitudes into the $\ell S J I$ basis and unitarize using the Bethe-Salpeter equation with a once-subtracted dispersion relation for the two-point loop function. They also consider coupled-channel scattering including channels with nonzero orbital angular momentum, going beyond the $S$-wave scattering approximation of earlier work.

## Key numbers

- The $\rho\rho$ threshold is at $\sqrt{s_{\rm th}} = 2m_\rho = 1540$ MeV
- For the $(J,I)=(0,0)$ channel with cutoff $q_{\rm max}=875$ MeV, the pole position is at $1494.8$ MeV (compared to $1512$ MeV in the prior non-relativistic calculation)
- For the $(J,I)=(0,0)$ channel with cutoff $q_{\rm max}=1000$ MeV, the pole position is at $1467.2$ MeV (compared to $1491$ MeV in the prior non-relativistic calculation)
- For the $(J,I)=(0,0)$ channel with cutoff $q_{\rm max}=m_\rho=775$ MeV, the pole position is at $1515.9$ MeV
- The compositeness $X^{(00)}_{00}$ for the bound state ranges from $0.52$ (for $q_{\rm max}=1000$ MeV) to $0.73$ (for $q_{\rm max}=775$ MeV), indicating a dominant $\rho\rho$ component
- The branch point from the $\rho$-exchange amplitudes occurs at $s=3m_\rho^2$
- The nominal three-momentum of a $\rho$ around the mass of the $f_2(1270)$ is about $0.6m_\rho \simeq 460$ MeV

## Context

This work extends and contradicts the results of Oset et al. (2008), who found that both the $f_2(1270)$ and $f_0(1370)$ could be explained as $\rho\rho$ bound states using extreme non-relativistic kinematics. The present paper shows that the tensor state generation is an artifact of that approximation, while the scalar state remains viable but with shifted pole positions. The authors suggest using their covariant formalism for more reliable results in vector-vector interaction studies, and note that the $f_2(1270)$ fits well within a $q\bar{q}$ nonet picture supported by analyses of Belle data by Pennington et al. (2016) and by Regge theory.
