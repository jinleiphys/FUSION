# QCD Plasma Instabilities: The Nonabelian Cascade

> Magnetic plasma instabilities appear to play an important role in the early stages of quark-gluon plasma equilibration in the high energy (weak coupling) limit. Numerical studies of the growth of such instabilities from small seed fluctuations have found initial exponential growth in their energy, followed by linear growth once the associated color magnetic fields become so large that their non-abelian interaction are non-perturbative. In this paper, we use simulations to determine the nature of this linear energy growth. We find that the long-wavelength modes associated with the instability have ceased to grow, but that they cascade energy towards the ultraviolet in the form of plasmon excitations of ever increasing energy. We find a quasi-steady-state power-law distribution $f_k \propto k^{-ν}$ for this cascade, with spectral index $ν\simeq 2$.

## Key claim

The authors find that after the initial exponential growth of magnetic plasma instabilities in a weakly coupled quark-gluon plasma, the energy in soft gauge fields grows linearly with time not because the unstable infrared modes continue to grow, but because those modes enter a quasi-steady state and cascade energy towards ultraviolet plasmon excitations with a power-law spectrum \(f_k \propto k^{-\nu}\) where \(\nu \simeq 2\).

## Method

The authors performed 3+1 dimensional classical Yang-Mills simulations on a lattice, coupled to an anisotropic bath of high-momentum particles via a Vlasov-type effective theory (hard-loop effective theory). They used SU(2) gauge theory with a fixed anisotropic particle distribution, starting from initial conditions where the infrared gauge fields were already nonperturbatively large, and tracked the evolution of the gauge fields and the color current \(W\) over time using spherical harmonic expansions up to \(\ell_{\rm max}=15\).

## Key numbers

- The spectral index of the cascade power-law distribution is \(\nu = 2 \pm 0.2\), determined from fits to the Coulomb-gauge spectrum \(f_E(k)\) in the range \(3 < k/m < 8\).
- The linear growth of magnetic and electric energy density with time was observed for lattice spacings \(am = 0.2\), \(0.288\), and \(0.4\), and the behavior was robust to changes in lattice spacing.
- The Chern-Simons number diffusion rate was consistent between the first and second halves of the evolution to within \(10\%\) error bars, indicating the infrared fields remain in a steady nonperturbative state.
- Simulations were performed on \(64^3\) lattices with physical volumes up to \((16/m)^3\) and run times up to \(mt = 800\).

## Context

This work extends earlier numerical studies by the same authors (Arnold and Lenaghan, 2004; Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe, 2003) which showed that exponential growth of plasma instabilities in QCD shuts off and is replaced by linear energy growth once fields become nonperturbative. It contradicts the conjecture by Arnold and Lenaghan (2004) that exponential growth would continue, and it supersedes the "bottom-up" thermalization scenario of Baier, Mueller, Schiff, and Son (2001) by showing that plasma instabilities dominate the dynamics of anisotropic weakly coupled plasmas. The results enable future studies of isotropization and thermalization in heavy-ion collisions, particularly for understanding whether the small viscosity observed at RHIC is consistent with weak coupling.
