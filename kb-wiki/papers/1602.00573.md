# Derivation of anisotropic dissipative fluid dynamics from the Boltzmann equation

> Fluid-dynamical equations of motion can be derived from the Boltzmann equation in terms of an expansion around a single-particle distribution function which is in local thermodynamical equilibrium, i.e., isotropic in momentum space in the rest frame of a fluid element. However, in situations where the single-particle distribution function is highly anisotropic in momentum space, such as the initial stage of heavy-ion collisions at relativistic energies, such an expansion is bound to break down. Nevertheless, one can still derive a fluid-dynamical theory, called anisotropic dissipative fluid dynamics, in terms of an expansion around a single-particle distribution function, $\hat{f}_{0\bf k}$, which incorporates (at least parts of) the momentum anisotropy via a suitable parametrization. We construct such an expansion in terms of polynomials in energy and momentum in the direction of the anisotropy and of irreducible tensors in the two-dimensional momentum subspace orthogonal to both the fluid velocity and the direction of the anisotropy. From the Boltzmann equation we then derive the set of equations of motion for the irreducible moments of the deviation of the single-particle distribution function from $\hat{f}_{0\bf k}$. Truncating this set via the 14-moment approximation, we obtain the equations of motion of anisotropic dissipative fluid dynamics.

## Key claim

The authors derive a systematically improvable framework for anisotropic dissipative fluid dynamics by expanding the single-particle distribution function around an anisotropic reference state $\hat{f}_{0\mathbf{k}}$ (rather than the usual isotropic local-equilibrium distribution), using orthogonal polynomials in both energy and the momentum component along the anisotropy direction, plus irreducible tensors in the two-dimensional subspace orthogonal to both the fluid velocity and the anisotropy direction.

## Method

The authors decompose the particle four-current and energy-momentum tensor with respect to both the fluid four-velocity $u^\mu$ and a space-like four-vector $l^\mu$ that defines the anisotropy direction. They construct an orthogonal basis of polynomials in $E_{\mathbf{k}u} = k^\mu u_\mu$ and $E_{\mathbf{k}l} = -k^\mu l_\mu$, combined with irreducible tensors in the subspace orthogonal to both $u^\mu$ and $l^\mu$, to expand the deviation $\delta\hat{f}_\mathbf{k}$ of the single-particle distribution function from the anisotropic reference state $\hat{f}_{0\mathbf{k}}$. Taking moments of the Boltzmann equation yields equations of motion for the irreducible moments of $\delta\hat{f}_\mathbf{k}$, which are then truncated via the 14-moment approximation to obtain closed fluid-dynamical equations.

## Key numbers

* The particle four-current $N^\mu$ has 4 independent components and the energy-momentum tensor $T^{\mu\nu}$ has 10 independent components, giving 14 unknowns total in the general decomposition.
* The isotropic pressure $P$ is related to longitudinal and transverse pressures by $P = \frac{1}{3}(P_l + 2P_\perp)$, independent of how far the system is from local thermodynamical equilibrium.
* The bulk viscous pressure is defined as $\Pi_{iso} \equiv P - P_0$, where $P_0$ is the local equilibrium pressure.

## Context

This work extends the moment method for deriving dissipative fluid dynamics from the Boltzmann equation, previously developed for the isotropic case by Denicol, Niemi, Molnar, and Rischke (2012), to situations where the single-particle distribution function is highly anisotropic in momentum space. It generalizes the anisotropic hydrodynamics formalism of Florkowski, Martinez, Ryblewski, and Strickland (2009-2012), which assumed a specific spheroidal form of the distribution function with a single anisotropy parameter, by providing a systematically improvable expansion around an arbitrary anisotropic reference state. The framework enables a controlled treatment of dissipative effects in systems with large momentum-space anisotropies, such as the initial stage of ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, where the gradient $\partial_z v_z \sim 1/t$ can become arbitrarily large near $t=0$ as noted by Bjorken (1983).
