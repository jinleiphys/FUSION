# Mixing angle between $^3P_1$ and $^1P_1$ in HQET

> Some claim that there are two independent mixing angles ($θ= 35.3^\circ$, $-54.7^\circ$) between $^3P_1$ and $^1P_1$ states of heavy-light mesons in heavy quark symmetric limit, and others claim there is only one ($θ= 35.3^\circ$). We clarify the difference between these two and suggest which should be adopted. General arguments on the mixing angle between $^3L_L$ and $^1L_L$ of heavy-light mesons are given in HQET and a general relation is derived in heavy quark mass limit as well as that including the first order correction in $1/m_Q$.

## Key claim

The paper clarifies that the two mixing angles (35.3° and -54.7°) reported in earlier work for the \(^3P_1\) and \(^1P_1\) states of heavy-light mesons are not independent; they are equivalent under a 90° rotation, and heavy quark symmetry uniquely selects the single mixing angle \(\theta = 35.3^\circ\) (arctan(1/√2)) when the relation between heavy-quark-symmetric eigenstates and non-relativistic states is properly derived.

## Method

The authors re-express the mass matrix of Rosner (1986) and Godfrey and Kokoski (1991) as an explicit eigenvalue equation in operator form, diagonalizing it to show that the two reported mixing angles correspond to the same physical eigenstates up to a 90° rotation. They then derive the mixing angle from a relativistic potential model in the heavy quark symmetric limit (m_Q → ∞) using the eigenfunctions \(y_{jm}^k\) of the operator \(\vec L \cdot \vec \sigma_q\), which are directly related to heavy-quark-symmetric states labeled by light-quark total angular momentum \(j_\ell\). A general relation for mixing between \(^3L_L\) and \(^1L_L\) states is given, along with the first-order correction in \(1/m_Q\).

## Key numbers

* The mixing angle in the heavy quark symmetric limit is \(\theta = \arctan(1/\sqrt{2}) = 35.3^\circ\) (not a second independent angle of -54.7°).
* The two previously claimed angles are related by \(\theta_2 = \theta_1 - 90^\circ\).
* The general mixing angle for any \(L\) is \(\tan\theta = \sqrt{L/(L+1)}\), with first-order symmetry-breaking correction \(\delta\theta = O(1/m_Q)\).
* The operator \(\vec L \cdot \vec \sigma_q\) has eigenvalues -2 and +1 for the eigenstates \(|^3P_1\rangle'\) and \(|^1P_1\rangle'\), respectively, giving \(j_\ell = 1/2\) and \(3/2\).

## Context

This work resolves a discrepancy between the non-relativistic potential model of Rosner (1986) and Godfrey and Kokoski (1991), which allowed two mixing angles, and the heavy-quark-symmetry-based derivation by Matsuki, Morii, and Seo (1997, 2007), which yields a unique angle. The result is consistent with the 6-j symbol calculation of Close and Swanson (2003) and with the relativistic potential model predictions that the lower-mass state is \(|^3P_1\rangle'\) (corresponding to \(j_\ell = 1/2\)) and the higher-mass state is \(|^1P_1\rangle'\) (\(j_\ell = 3/2\)) for \(c\bar q\) and \(b\bar q\) systems. The general relation enables future studies of mixing in higher \(L\) states and of \(1/m_Q\) corrections, as discussed by the Belle collaboration (2007).
