# Spin polarization for massive fermion in a shear flow: complete results at $O(\partial)$

> Motivated by the key role of shear induced polarization in understanding the local spin polarization puzzle of $Λ$ hyperons in heavy ion collisions, we perform a complete analysis of spin polarization of massive fermion in a quantum electrodynamic plasma with shear flow. Apart from the well-known spin-shear coupling in free theory, we include two more collision dependent contributions: one is a non-dynamical contribution fixed by shifted spin-averaged distribution in steady state; the other is a dynamical contribution following from spin evolution. Despite of the dependencies on collision, we find the dependencies on coupling drop out in the final results. These contributions can lead to significant enhancement of the spin-shear coupling in phenomenologically interesting regime.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the complete spin polarization of a massive fermion in a shear flow at first order in gradients includes not only the well-known kinematic spin-shear coupling from free theory, but also two collision-dependent contributions (a non-dynamical part from shifted spin-averaged distributions and a dynamical part from spin evolution); despite each contribution depending on collisions, the final result is independent of the coupling strength.

## Method

The authors use collisional quantum kinetic theory (QKT) for a massive probe fermion in a massless QED plasma with a steady shear flow, considering only Coulomb scattering at leading logarithmic order. They solve the linearized Boltzmann equation to obtain the redistribution of the probe fermion, compute the self-energy correction to extract the collisional contribution to the axial Wigner function, and determine the dynamical contribution from the frame independence of the axial-vector current and from the vanishing of the collision term in the long-time steady state.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of collisional to kinematic contributions for the probe fermion, \(R^{\Sigma/\partial} \equiv N_\Sigma/N_\partial = -2T_{3p}^{\text{prob}}/p\), approaches \(1/2\) in the relativistic limit \(p \gg m, p \gg T\).
- In the non-relativistic limit \(m \gg p\), the ratio becomes \(R^{\Sigma/\partial}|_{m\gg p} \rightarrow -\frac{18 C_f N_f \zeta(3) T^2}{p^2} - \frac{2 m T}{5 p^2}\).
- The redistribution coefficient for the probe fermion in the high-momentum regime is \(\chi_p^{\text{prob}} = \frac{(2\pi)^3 C_f^{\text{prob}}}{e^4 \ln e^{-1}} \beta^2 p^2\), with \(C_f^{\text{prob}} = \frac{3}{2\pi^2 N_f} \cdot \frac{\tilde{v}^2}{\tilde{v} + (\tilde{v}^2-1) \text{arctanh}\,\tilde{v}}\) where \(\tilde{v} = p/p_0\).
- In the massless limit, the collisional coefficient for the probe fermion is \(T_{3p}^{\text{prob},m=0} = \frac{3C_f N_f T \zeta(3) (5 F p - 4 T)}{2 p} + \frac{C_f^{\text{prob}} N_f \pi^2 (4 T - F p)}{6}\), with \(F = 1-2f_p^\text{eq}\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis for massless fermions (Lin 2024) to the massive case, filling the gap left by their earlier study (Lin 2022) where the dynamical contribution was left undetermined. It also generalizes results from linear response theory (Liu 2021) and statistical field theory (Becattini 2021), which gave \(N_P=1\) and omitted collision-dependent contributions. The complete result enables more realistic phenomenological modeling of the local spin polarization puzzle of \(\Lambda\) hyperons in heavy-ion collisions, particularly in the regime where the strange quark mass is important.
