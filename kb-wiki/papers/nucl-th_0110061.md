# Novel Methods for Determining Effective Interactions for the Nuclear Shell Model

> The Contractor Renormalization (CORE) method is applied in combination with modern effective-theory techniques to the nuclear many-body problem. A one-dimensional--yet ``realistic''--nucleon-nucleon potential is introduced to test these novel ideas. It is found that the magnitude of ``model-space'' (CORE) corrections diminishes considerably when an effective potential that eliminates the hard-momentum components of the potential is first introduced. As a result, accurate predictions for the ground-state energy of the there-body system are made with relatively little computational effort when both techniques are used in a complementary fashion.

## Key claim

The Contractor Renormalization (CORE) method, when combined with an effective potential that removes the hard-momentum components of the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction, yields accurate ground-state energies for the three-body system with relatively small computational effort; the magnitude of CORE corrections is considerably diminished when the effective potential is used first.

## Method

The authors introduced a one-dimensional nucleon-nucleon potential with a short-range repulsive core and medium-range attraction, fitted to reproduce a deuteron binding energy of -2.2245 MeV and a point rms radius of 1.875 fm. They constructed a smooth effective potential using a gaussian form with parameters constrained to reproduce low-energy scattering phase shifts, then applied the CORE similarity transformation to generate effective Hamiltonians in truncated harmonic-oscillator bases. Ground-state energies of the three-body system were computed using both the bare and effective potentials, with and without CORE corrections, for basis sizes up to N=30.

## Key numbers

- Bare potential deuteron binding energy: -2.2245 MeV, with point rms radius 1.875 fm
- Bare potential odd-parity two-body bound state: -0.180358 MeV
- Exact three-body ground-state energy for the bare potential: -6.32 MeV
- Exact three-body ground-state energy for the effective potential: -6.89 MeV (slightly different due to omitted induced three-body interactions)
- With N=30 basis, bare potential without CORE gives -5.4477 MeV; with CORE gives -5.6327 MeV
- With N=30 basis, effective potential without CORE gives -6.4284 MeV; with CORE gives -6.4606 MeV
- Effective potential range parameter: a=1.16 fm (corresponding to cutoff Lambda=170 MeV)
- Particle mass: m=939 MeV

## Context

This work extends the CORE method, previously applied by the authors to low-dimensional quantum spin systems (Piekarewicz and Shepard, 1996-1999), to the nuclear many-body problem. It addresses criticisms by Haxton and coworkers (1999, 2001) that shell-model practitioners often use effective interactions that do not follow systematically from realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions. The approach combines CORE with effective-field theory techniques as outlined by Lepage (1997) and adapted by Steele and Furnstahl (1998, 1999), and the authors note that CORE gives slightly better ground-state energies than the Lee-Suzuki method (Suzuki and Lee, 1980; Suzuki, 1982; Suzuki and Okamoto, 1983) for the one-dimensional three-body system. Future work includes extending to more realistic systems, determining optimal meshing of effective theory and CORE, and developing renormalization-group-like equations for the effective potential.
