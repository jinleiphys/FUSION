# Charge Neutrality of the Color-Flavor Locked Phase from the Low Energy Effective Theory

> We investigate the issue of charge neutrality of the CFL$K^0$ phase of dense quark matter using the low energy effective theory of high density QCD. We show that the local electric and color charge neutrality of the ground state in a homogeneous color superconducting medium follows from its dynamics. We also consider the situation of a spatially inhomogeneous medium, such as may be found in a neutron star core. We find that spatial inhomogeneity results in the generation of electric fields, and positrons/electrons may be present in the ground state. We estimate the concentration of charged leptons in the ground state to be $n_{e}\sim{10^2}{cm}^{-3}$ and consider their influence on the opacity of the medium with respect to the modified photons.

## Key claim

The paper shows that local electric and color charge neutrality of the ground state in a homogeneous color superconducting medium follows from its dynamics, but for a spatially inhomogeneous medium (such as a neutron star core) nonzero electric fields are generated and positrons or electrons may be present in the ground state, with the homogeneous CFLK0 phase being a perfect insulator while the inhomogeneous case is not.

## Method

The author employs the low energy effective theory of QCD at high baryon density (HDET) with gauge fields treated as dynamical degrees of freedom, integrating out heavy gauge fields to obtain an effective Lagrangian for the Nambu-Goldstone bosons and the modified photon field. For the homogeneous case, equations of motion for the temporal components of gauge fields are solved to show charge densities vanish. For the inhomogeneous case, the author expands in the small parameter epsilon = lambda_D / L (Debye screening length over typical inhomogeneity scale) and solves the equations of motion including an electron-positron Dirac field added to the Lagrangian, using the Thomas-Fermi approximation.

## Key numbers

* Pion decay constant: f_pi approximately 0.209 mu, where mu is the quark number chemical potential.
* Electron concentration in the inhomogeneous ground state: n_e approximately 10^2 cm^{-3}, for mu = 400 MeV and m_s = 150 MeV.
* Positron Fermi momentum: k_{Fe+} approximately 10^{-7} MeV, for mu = 400 MeV and m_s = 150 MeV.
* Debye screening length: lambda_D approximately (g_s mu)^{-1} approximately 1 fm.
* Typical inhomogeneity scale used for estimates: L = 1 km (typical neutron star core size).
* Ratio lambda_D / L approximately 10^{-18}.
* Mean free path of modified photons in the medium: l approximately 10^{19} cm, much larger than a stellar radius.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of charge neutrality in color-flavor locked quark matter previously addressed by Alford and Rajagopal (2002) and by Steiner, Reddy and Prakash (2002), who used chemical potentials for color and electric charges without including gauge fields as dynamical degrees of freedom. The paper also builds on the low energy effective theory developed by Casalbuoni and Gatto (1999), Son and Stephanov (2000), and Beane, Bedaque and Savage (2000). The result enables future studies of electromagnetic properties of neutron star cores containing color superconducting quark matter, showing that spatial inhomogeneity qualitatively changes the ground state by introducing charged leptons.
