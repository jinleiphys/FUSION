# Galilean invariance restoration on the lattice

> We consider the breaking of Galilean invariance due to different lattice cutoff effects in moving frames and a nonlocal smearing parameter which is used in the construction of the nuclear lattice interaction. The dispersion relation and neutron-proton scattering phase shifts are used to investigate the Galilean invariance breaking effects and ways to restore it. For $S$-wave channels, ${}^1S_0$ and ${}^3S_1$, we present the neutron-proton scattering phase shifts in moving frames calculated using both Lüscher's formula and the spherical wall method, as well as the dispersion relation. For the $P$ and $D$ waves, we present the neutron-proton scattering phase shifts in moving frames calculated using the spherical wall method. We find that the Galilean invariance breaking effects stemming from the lattice artifacts partially cancel those caused by the nonlocal smearing parameter. Due to this cancellation, the Galilean invariance breaking effect is small, and the Galilean invariance can be restored by introducing Galilean invariance restoration operators.

# Galilean invariance restoration on the lattice

## Key claim

The Galilean invariance breaking effects on the lattice, caused by both finite-lattice-spacing artifacts in moving frames and a nonlocal smearing parameter used in the nuclear lattice interaction, partially cancel each other; the remaining breaking is small and can be restored by introducing Galilean invariance restoration (GIR) operators, though the restoration is not complete for all channels at all momenta.

## Method

The authors used a Hamiltonian formalism with an O(a^4)-improved free Hamiltonian and N3LO chiral effective field theory nucleon-nucleon interactions on a spatial lattice with spacing a = 1.32 fm. They calculated neutron-proton scattering phase shifts in both the rest frame and boosted frames using Lüscher's formula for S-wave channels and the spherical wall method for S, P, and D waves, as well as the dispersion relation for S-wave bound and continuum states. Galilean invariance breaking was quantified by comparing results at different center-of-mass momenta, and restoration was achieved by tuning coefficients of pair-hopping GIR operators with the constraint that the GIR correction vanishes at zero total momentum.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: a = 1.32 fm
- Nonlocal smearing parameter: s_NL = 0.18566 lattice units
- Local smearing parameter: s_L = 0.16985 lattice units
- SU(4) contact potential coefficient: C_0 = -0.04455 lattice units
- Box volumes used: V = (14a)^3, (16a)^3, (18a)^3 for smaller boxes; V = (24a)^3, (26a)^3, (28a)^3 for larger boxes
- For spherical wall method: L = 40, corresponding to radial distance La/2 = 26.4 fm
- Boosted frame momentum used for spherical wall method: k = [3, 3, 3]^T
- For the S-wave dispersion relation: box volume V = (32a)^3
- Galilean invariance breaking for epsilon_1 starts around p_rel = 120 MeV; for epsilon_2 starts around p_rel = 150 MeV
- For D waves, GIB starts around p_rel = 100 MeV for ^1D_2 and ^3D_3, and around p_rel = 150 MeV for ^3D_1 and ^3D_2; after GIR correction, starting points increase to around p_rel = 200 MeV
- P-wave GIB appears starting around p_rel = 120 MeV

## Context

This work extends the nonlocally smeared contact operators introduced by Elhatisari et al. (2016) for nuclear lattice effective field theory (NLEFT), which were previously extended to N3LO by Li et al. (2018) for neutron-proton scattering. The paper addresses the Galilean invariance breaking identified by Lee (2007) as a consequence of lattice regularization, and provides a practical restoration method using pair-hopping operators. This enables future NLEFT Monte Carlo simulations for light- and medium-mass nuclei to be performed in moving frames without systematic errors from Galilean invariance breaking, and the authors note this interaction has already been used in such simulations.
