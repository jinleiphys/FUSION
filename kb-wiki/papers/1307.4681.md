# Shear viscosity of hadrons with K-matrix cross sections

> Shear viscosity ηand entropy density s of a hadronic resonance gas are calculated using the Chapman-Enskog and virial expansion methods using the K-matrix parametrization of hadronic cross sections which preserves the unitarity of the T -matrix. In the π-K-N-η- mixture considered, a total of 57 resonances up to 2 GeV were included. Comparisons are also made to results with other hadronic cross sections such as the Breit-Wigner (BW) and, where available, experimental phase shift parametrizations. Hadronic interactions forming resonances are shown to decrease the shear viscosity and increase the entropy density leading to a substantial reduction of η/s as the QCD phase transition temperature is approached.

# Shear viscosity of hadrons with K-matrix cross sections

## Key claim

The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (η/s) of a hadronic resonance gas decreases substantially as the QCD phase transition temperature is approached, but this result depends on using the K-matrix parametrization of hadronic cross sections which preserves unitarity of the T-matrix; the Breit-Wigner parametrization overestimates cross sections for multiple overlapping resonances and therefore would yield different viscosity values.

## Method

The authors employed the Chapman-Enskog approximation (generalized to relativistic kinematics) to calculate shear viscosity and the virial expansion method (Dashen et al. 1969) to calculate entropy density for a hadronic resonance gas mixture of pions, kaons, nucleons, and eta mesons. A total of 57 resonances with masses up to 2 GeV from the Particle Data Group were included, using the K-matrix parametrization of hadronic cross sections to preserve unitarity of the T-matrix in all channels, with comparisons also made to Breit-Wigner and experimental phase shift parametrizations.

## Key numbers

- 57 resonances up to 2 GeV were included in the π-K-N-η mixture considered
- The ρ resonance mass used was m_ρ = 0.77 GeV with Γ_ρ→ππ^0 = 0.15 GeV
- For the σ resonance in phase shift parametrization: m_σ = 5.8 m_π (where m_π is the pion mass)
- For the ρ resonance in phase shift parametrization: m_ρ = 5.53 m_π
- The repulsive channel phase shift is given by δ_0^2 = -0.12 q/m_π
- For separated resonances example: m_α = 1.2 GeV with Γ_α = 0.1 GeV, m_β = 1.5 GeV with Γ_β = 0.2 GeV
- For overlapping resonances example: m_α = 1.35 GeV with Γ_α = 0.3 GeV, m_β = 1.5 GeV with Γ_β = 0.1 GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of shear viscosity in a pion gas using experimental π-π phase shifts (PPVW 1993) and provides a systematic treatment of the hadronic phase relevant for interpreting collective flow measurements from RHIC and LHC experiments. The results enable more realistic hydrodynamic model studies that currently assume constant η/s or use hadronic cascade afterburners (Huchao 2011, Jeon 2013), by providing a temperature-dependent η/s that accounts for multiple resonances while preserving unitarity through the K-matrix formalism. The study also complements perturbative QCD calculations (Yaffe 2000, Yaffe 2003, Xu 2008, Xu 2011) which predict η/s increasing logarithmically with temperature in the quark-gluon plasma phase.
