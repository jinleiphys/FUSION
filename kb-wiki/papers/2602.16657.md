# The spatial Wilson loops, string breaking, and AdS/QCD

> We consider the phenomenon of string breaking in the context of the spatial Wilson loops using the gauge/string duality. In particular, we discuss the impact of light flavors on the pseudopotential. We also introduce the notion of the spatial string breaking distance and estimate it for $SU(3)$ gauge theory in the temperature range $0\,\text{-}\,3\,T_c$.

# The spatial Wilson loops, string breaking, and AdS/QCD

## Key claim

String breaking modifies the large-length behavior of the pseudopotential extracted from spatial Wilson loops in the presence of light dynamical quarks, and the spatial string breaking distance can be estimated for SU(3) gauge theory across the temperature range 0–3 Tc, but the effective string model used is only reliable for temperatures below 2.5–3 Tc.

## Method

The authors compute the pseudopotential V from spatial Wilson loops using a five-dimensional AdS/QCD model with a one-parameter deformation of the Schwarzschild black hole in Euclidean AdS5, with light quarks modeled by a scalar tachyon field T at the string endpoints. Two string configurations are considered: a connected configuration representing a heavy quark-antiquark pair, and a disconnected configuration representing a pair of heavy-light mesons formed by string breaking. The pseudopotential is taken as the smaller eigenvalue of a 2×2 mixing Hamiltonian whose diagonal elements are the connected and disconnected pseudopotentials, with the off-diagonal mixing element Θ treated as a free parameter (set to 50 MeV based on lattice results at zero temperature).

## Key numbers

- The deformation parameter √s = 0.450 GeV², fixed from the Regge slope of ρ(n) mesons.
- The string tension parameter g = 0.176, obtained by fitting the physical string tension σ = g e s to lattice data.
- The parameter n = 3.057, adjusted to reproduce the lattice string breaking distance ℓc = 1.22 fm at zero temperature.
- The critical temperature Tc = √s / π.
- The constant term c = 0.623 GeV (used in pseudopotential normalization).
- The spatial string tension σs is temperature-independent below Tc and equals σ (T²/Tc²) exp{Tc²/T² − 1} for T ≥ Tc.
- The Coulomb coefficient α = g (2π)³ / Γ⁴(1/4) from the small-ℓ expansion.
- The spatial string breaking distance ℓs is defined by Vc(ℓs) = Vd; at T=0, ℓs = ℓc = 1.22 fm.
- The constant term C in the large-ℓ expansion reaches a maximum at T = 1.04 Tc.
- At high temperatures (T ≳ 5 Tc), Vd grows linearly as (1/2)g√s [B(1−n⁻², −1/4, 1/2) + 4n²/√(n²−1)] (T/Tc) + 2c + o(1).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous study of pseudopotentials in pure gauge theories (Andreev, 2022) to theories with two light quarks, using the same deformed AdS metric. The disconnected configuration approach follows the construction of the heavy quark-antiquark potential in the presence of light quarks (Andreev, 2015). The mixing analysis based on a correlation matrix adopts the model of Drummond (2004), which was used to determine the quark-antiquark potential on the lattice (Bulava, 2019). The results enable predictions for spatial Wilson loops in QCD with light flavors, where no phenomenological or lattice predictions yet exist, and suggest future lattice QCD or 3D effective field theory computations of the full mixing Hamiltonian.
