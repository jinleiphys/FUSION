# Gamma photons from parametric resonance in neutron stars

> Shock waves in cold nuclear matter, e.g. those induced by a collision of two neutron stars, can generate a large number of gamma photons via parametric resonance. We study the resonant production of gamma rays inside a shocked neutron star and discuss the possible astrophysical consequences of this phenomenon.

# Gamma photons from parametric resonance in neutron stars

## Key claim

Acoustic shock waves from neutron star mergers can induce repetitive superconducting phase transitions in nuclear matter, and the subsequent oscillations of the proton condensate can produce gamma-ray photons via parametric resonance, but only if the friction coefficient is small enough to allow several oscillations before the condensate settles, and only in the density range where proton superconductivity exists.

## Method

The authors model the proton condensate using time-dependent Ginzburg-Landau theory, writing an equation of motion for the order parameter with a friction term derived from the Fermi energy and critical temperature. They then couple the oscillating condensate to the electromagnetic field via the gauge coupling term, reduce the photon mode equation to a Mathieu equation with a time-dependent resonance parameter that decays exponentially due to friction, and compute the growth exponents and energy density numerically for sample values of nuclear density and magnetic field.

## Key numbers

- Proton fraction in neutron star matter: \(Y_p \approx 0.03\) of all baryons
- Energy gap at density \(\rho = 10^{14}\) g/cm³: \(\Delta_0 \simeq 0.35\) MeV
- Equilibrium order parameter at that density: \(\phi_0 \simeq 50.3\) MeV
- Oscillation frequency of the condensate: \(\omega \simeq 4.7\) MeV
- Friction coefficient relative to frequency: \(\epsilon = 4.2\Delta_0/\omega \simeq 0.31\)
- Resonance parameter: \(4q_0 \simeq 340\)
- Ultraviolet cut-off from electron Fermi energy: \(k_c \simeq 51\) MeV (corresponding to \(k/\omega \simeq 11\))
- Asymptotic photon energy density: \(\rho_\gamma \simeq 8 \times 10^4\) MeV⁴, which is \(\sim 10^{-4}\) of the nuclear matter density
- Total energy stored in gamma photons: \(E_\gamma \simeq 3 \times 10^{50}\) erg
- Power generated: \(\sim 10^{52\pm1}\) erg/s over a timescale of \(1\) ms to \(0.1\) s
- Magnetic field range studied: \(10^{12}\) G to \(10^{16}\) G
- Density range for superconductivity: \(10^{14} < \rho < 6 \times 10^{14}\) g/cm³
- Acoustic time scale: \(\tau_a \sim 10^{-7}\) to \(10^{-3}\) s
- Relaxation time of proton condensate: \(\sim 10^{-20}\) s (MeV⁻¹)

## Context

This work extends the proposal that neutron star mergers are the source of gamma-ray bursts (Rees, 1997) by providing a specific mechanism, parametric resonance of photons from oscillating proton condensates, that can convert gravitational energy into MeV gamma rays with a non-thermal spectrum. The mechanism is analogous to preheating after inflation (Kofman, Linde, Starobinsky, 1997) and resonant photon production from axion clumps (Tkachev, 1986). The paper identifies that the gamma rays remain trapped inside the degenerate nuclear matter until the fireball erupts, which could explain the non-thermal spectrum observed in GRB afterglows (Metzger et al., 1997). The main open question is whether the time-dependent Ginzburg-Landau description is valid for nuclear matter given the lack of empirical knowledge about proton superconductivity in neutron stars.
