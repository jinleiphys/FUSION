# Temperature Dependence of Pair Correlations in Nuclei in the Iron-Region

> We use the shell model Monte Carlo approach to study thermal properties and pair correlations in $^{54,56,58}$Fe and in $^{56}$Cr. The calculations are performed with the modified Kuo-Brown interaction in the complete $1p0f$ model space. We find generally that the proton-proton and neutron-neutron $J=0$ pairing correlations, which dominate the ground state properties of even-even nuclei, vanish at temperatures around 1 MeV. This pairing phase transition is accompanied by a rapid increase in the moment of inertia and a partial unquenching of the M1 strength. We find that the M1 strength totally unquenches at higher temperatures, related to the vanishing of isoscalar proton-neutron correlations, which persist to higher temperatures than the pairing between like nucleons. The Gamow-Teller strength is also correlated to the isoscalar proton-neutron pairing and hence also unquenches at a temperature larger than that of the pairing phase transition.

## Key claim

The like-nucleon (proton-proton and neutron-neutron) J=0 pairing correlations, which dominate ground-state properties of even-even nuclei, vanish at temperatures around 1 MeV, but this pairing phase transition is distinct from the higher-temperature unquenching of M1 and Gamow-Teller strength, which is driven by isoscalar proton-neutron correlations that persist to higher temperatures.

## Method

The authors used the shell model Monte Carlo (SMMC) approach to calculate thermal properties of 54,56,58Fe and 56Cr in the complete 1p0f model space (20 orbitals) with the modified Kuo-Brown (KB3) interaction. They defined a generalized pair matrix for isovector and isoscalar pairs at each angular momentum J, diagonalized it, and defined the physically relevant pairing correlation as the difference between the SMMC pairing strength and the mean-field pairing strength derived from occupation numbers. Observables including the moment of inertia, total B(M1) strength, and total B(GT+) strength were computed at selected temperatures between 0.5 and 8 MeV, with a linear extrapolation from sign-problem-free Hamiltonians to the physical Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

* The J=0 proton and neutron pair correlations vanish at temperatures around 1.2 MeV for all four nuclei studied.
* Fitted transition temperatures T0 for neutron J=0 pairing: 0.82 +/- 0.13 MeV for 54Fe, 0.96 +/- 0.03 MeV for 56Fe, 1.07 +/- 0.04 MeV for 58Fe, and 1.06 +/- 0.04 MeV for 56Cr.
* Fitted transition temperatures T0 for proton J=0 pairing: approximately 0.9 +/- 0.04 MeV for all nuclei.
* The isoscalar J=1 proton-neutron correlations reach their maximum at around 2 MeV and vanish slowly at temperatures above 3 MeV.
* The B(M1) strength unquenches in two steps: partial unquenching at the pairing phase transition near 1 MeV, then full unquenching starting at about 2.6 MeV.
* The B(GT+) strength remains roughly constant below 1 MeV, then unquenches at temperatures above 2.6 MeV.

## Context

This work extends a prior SMMC study of 54Fe by Dean et al. (1995) that first observed the pairing phase transition and its effect on the moment of inertia and M1 strength, but left open the question of why Gamow-Teller strength remained quenched through the transition. The present paper confirms that the same behavior occurs in 56Fe, 58Fe, and 56Cr, and demonstrates that isoscalar proton-neutron correlations, previously identified by Engel et al. (1988) as the main source of GT quenching, persist to higher temperatures than like-nucleon pairing. The results enable a more complete understanding of how thermal effects modify nuclear structure observables relevant to astrophysical processes and hot nuclear matter.
