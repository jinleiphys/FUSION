# Evaluation of the $ππ$ scattering amplitude in the $σ$-channel at finite density

> The $ππ$ scattering amplitude in the $σ$-channel is studied at finite baryonic density in the framework of a chiral unitary approach which successfully reproduces the meson meson phase shifts and generates the $f_0$ and $σ$ resonances in vacuum. We address here a new variety of mechanisms recently suggested to modify the $ππ$ interaction in the medium, as well as the role of the $s-$wave selfenergy, in addition to the $p-$wave, in the dressing of the pion propagators.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the new medium corrections from tadpole diagrams and t-channel particle-hole exchange, while sizeable at tree level, produce only modest modifications to the ππ scattering amplitude in the σ-channel once the Bethe-Salpeter equation is solved, confirming that the p-wave pion selfenergy remains the dominant medium effect.

## Method

The authors solve a Bethe-Salpeter equation for ππ and K\(\bar{K}\) coupled channels in nuclear matter, using the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian as the driving kernel. They incorporate p-wave pion selfenergies from particle-hole and Δ-hole excitations, and add s-wave selfenergies from the second-order πN Lagrangian with c_i coefficients from fits that include explicit Δ degrees of freedom. The new contributions include nucleon tadpole vertex corrections and t-channel particle-hole exchange diagrams, evaluated with a ρ-meson form factor and Lindhard functions.

## Key numbers

- The tadpole vertex correction at threshold gives δt_{ππ}/t_{ππ} = -0.154 ρ/ρ_0 (set I) and -0.124 ρ/ρ_0 (set II) before including the s-wave selfenergy insertion; after adding the insertion the sign reverses to +0.18 ρ/ρ_0 (set I) and +0.14 ρ/ρ_0 (set II)
- The p-wave pion selfenergy uses a Landau-Migdal parameter g' = 0.7 and (D+F) = 1.257
- The cut-off for the two-pion loop integral is Λ = 1.03 GeV
- The 2ph optical potential parameter is C_0^* = (0.105 + i 0.096) m_π^{-6}
- The c_i coefficients from the fit with explicit Δ are: set I: c_1 = -0.35 GeV^{-1}, c_2 = -1.49 GeV^{-1}, c_3 = 0.93 GeV^{-1}; set II: c_1 = -0.32 GeV^{-1}, c_2 = -1.59 GeV^{-1}, c_3 = 1.15 GeV^{-1}

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach of Oller, Oset, and collaborators (1997-2000) for ππ scattering in vacuum to finite nuclear density, building on earlier medium calculations by Chiang, Oset, and Vicente Vacas (1998) and Oset and collaborators (2000) that only included p-wave pion selfenergy. The authors address claims by Jido, Hatsuda, and Kunihiro (2000) about large attractive effects from tadpole diagrams, and vertex corrections suggested by Meissner, Oller, and Wirzba (2001). The results confirm the earlier predictions used by Roca, Oset, and Vicente Vacas (2002) for the (γ,2π) reaction, which were later confirmed experimentally by Messchendorp and collaborators (2002).
