# Ortho-para transition rate in $μ$-molecular hydrogen and the proton's induced pseudoscalar coupling $g_p$

> We report a measurement of the ortho-para transition rate in the p$μ$p molecule. The experiment was conducted at TRIUMF via the measurement of the time dependence of the 5.2 MeV neutrons from muon capture in liquid hydrogen. The measurement yielded an ortho-para rate $Λ_{op} = (11.1 \pm 1.7 \pm^{0.9}_{0.6}) \times 10^4$ s$^{-1}$ that is substantially larger than the earlier result of Bardin {\it et al.} We discuss the striking implications for the proton's induced pseudoscalar coupling $g_p$.

## Key claim

The measured ortho-para transition rate in the pμp molecule is substantially larger than both the earlier experimental result of Bardin et al. and the theoretical estimate of Bakalov et al., with the new value having dramatic consequences for extracting the proton's induced pseudoscalar coupling \(g_p\) from muon capture experiments in liquid hydrogen.

## Method

The experiment was conducted at TRIUMF using the M9B muon channel, where negative muons were stopped in a liquid hydrogen target and the time dependence of 5.2 MeV neutrons from muon capture was measured using a liquid scintillator counter array with pulse-shape discrimination. The ortho-para transition rate \(\Lambda_{op}\) was extracted by fitting the neutron time spectrum with a function based on the known muon disappearance rate and the hyperfine ratio of ortho and para capture rates, including corrections for pμp formation kinetics and deuterium contamination.

## Key numbers

- Ortho-para transition rate: \(\Lambda_{op} = (11.1 \pm 1.7 \pm^{0.9}_{0.6}) \times 10^4\) s\(^{-1}\) from the benchmark fit to all counters and all data-sets, with statistical and systematic errors respectively
- The new result is 3.1\(\sigma\) larger than the earlier measurement of Bardin et al. at \((4.1 \pm 1.4) \times 10^4\) s\(^{-1}\)
- The new result is 1.9\(\sigma\) larger than the theoretical estimate of Bakalov et al. at \((7.1 \pm 1.1) \times 10^4\) s\(^{-1}\)
- Total of \(3.7 \times 10^{10}\) \(\mu^-\) stops in liquid H\(_2\) were collected across four data-sets
- Photo-nuclear background contribution averaged about 14% for thick counters N2-N5 and rose to \(55 \pm 8\)% for the thin counter N1
- A limit of \(<7\)% was established on carbon backgrounds from muon capture in low-Z materials

## Context

This measurement resolves a long-standing discrepancy between the only previous measurement of the ortho-para transition rate by Bardin et al. (1981) and the theoretical calculation by Bakalov et al. (1982), which had important implications for extracting the induced pseudoscalar coupling \(g_p\) from muon capture experiments. The new result reconciles the TRIUMF radiative muon capture experiment (1996, 1998) with chiral perturbation theory predictions, yielding \(g_p = 10.6 \pm 1.1\), while making the Saclay ordinary muon capture result inconsistent with theory. The work enables a more reliable determination of \(g_p\) from liquid hydrogen experiments and motivates further theoretical and experimental investigation of the muon molecular chemistry in hydrogen.
