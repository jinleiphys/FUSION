# Probing in-medium vector mesons by dileptons at heavy-ion colliders

> An introduction to physics of in-medium hadrons with special emphasis towards modification of vector meson properties in dense nuclear matter is given. We start from remarkable analogy between the in-medium behavior of atoms in gases and hadrons in nuclear matter. Modifications of vector meson widths and masses can be registered experimentally in heavy-ion collisions by detecting dilepton spectra from decays of nucleon resonances and light unflavored mesons including $ρ$- and $ω$-mesons. Theoretical schemes for description of the in-medium hadrons are reviewed and recent experimental results of the NA60 and HADES collaborations on the dilepton production are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in-medium modifications of vector meson properties (masses and widths) in dense nuclear matter can be probed experimentally through dilepton spectra from heavy-ion collisions, but the abstract's claim that these modifications are "registered experimentally" glosses over the fact that the interpretation depends heavily on transport models and that the evidence for specific modifications (such as dropping mass versus collision broadening) remains contested, with the NA60 data showing no evidence for a change of the rho-meson mass.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical schemes for describing in-medium hadrons, including the Walecka model, Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, QCD sum rules, dispersion theory combined with the optical potential method, and unitarized thermal chiral perturbation theory. It then discusses experimental results from the NA60 and HADES collaborations on dilepton production, and presents the extended vector meson dominance (eVMD) model for describing transition form factors of nucleon resonances, including the Delta(1232) Dalitz decay.

## Key numbers

- The collision width for the omega meson at density approximately 1.5 times saturation density is constrained to be at least 50 MeV, compared to its vacuum width of 8 MeV
- The rho meson vacuum width is 150 MeV, with calculations exploring a total width of 300 MeV for comparison
- The Delta(1232) resonance magnetic transition form factor is parameterized as G_M = (2.461 - 0.485 M^2 - 0.004 M^4)G, where M^2 is in GeV^2
- The masses of the rho-meson family members used in the eVMD model are 0.769, 1.250, 1.450, and 1.720 GeV
- The NA60 collaboration studied dileptons with invariant masses in the range 0.2 to 1.2 GeV at beam energy 160 GeV per nucleon
- The HADES collaboration studied dileptons with invariant masses in the range 0.05 to 1 GeV at beam energies 1 to 2 GeV per nucleon

## Context

This lecture extends the analogy between atomic spectroscopy in gases and hadron spectroscopy in nuclear matter, building on the collision broadening concept from Lorentz theory. It reviews theoretical work by Walecka (1974) and Chin (1977) on nucleon mass in nuclear matter, the Brown-Rho scaling hypothesis, and experimental results from the CERES, HELIOS-3, DLS, KEK, NA60, and HADES collaborations. The paper enables further testing of in-medium vector meson modifications through comparison of transport model predictions with dilepton data, and highlights the need for more theoretical study of the decoherence effect in transition form factors of nucleon resonances.
