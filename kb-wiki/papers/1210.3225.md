# Neutrinoless $ββ$ decay nuclear matrix elements in an isotopic chain

> We analyze nuclear matrix elements (NME) of neutrinoless double beta decay calculated for the Cadmium isotopes. Energy density functional methods including beyond mean field effects such as symmetry restoration and shape mixing are used. Strong shell effects are found associated to the underlying nuclear structure of the initial and final nuclei. Furthermore, we show that NME for two-neutrino double beta decay evaluated in the closure approximation, $M^{2ν}_{\mathrm{cl}}$, display a constant proportionality with respect to the Gamow-Teller part of the neutrinoless NME, $M^{0ν}_{\mathrm{GT}}$. This opens the possibility of determining the $M^{0ν}_{\mathrm{GT}}$ matrix elements from $β^{\mp}$ Gamow-Teller strength functions. Finally, the interconnected role of deformation, pairing, configuration mixing and shell effects in the NMEs is discussed.

## Key claim

The authors show that the nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for neutrinoless double beta decay in the cadmium isotopic chain exhibit a nearly constant proportionality to the two-neutrino double beta decay matrix element evaluated in the closure approximation, specifically for the Gamow-Teller part, but this proportionality is observed within the energy density functional framework and has not been found in QRPA calculations (Rodríguez and Martínez-Pinedo 2012).

## Method

The authors used the generator coordinate method (GCM) with the Gogny D1S energy density functional, including particle number and angular momentum projection as well as shape mixing, to compute the initial and final nuclear states for the decay of Cadmium to Tin isotopes across the chain from A=98 to A=132. The neutrinoless double beta decay NMEs were evaluated as the sum of Fermi and Gamow-Teller terms using the closure approximation with a closure energy of 10.22 MeV, and the two-neutrino double beta decay matrix element in the closure approximation was computed with a constant neutrino potential.

## Key numbers

- The theoretical Q-value for the double beta decay overestimates the experimental value by approximately 1.4 MeV across the isotopic chain, with the Gogny D1S force accuracy of about 3.4 MeV.
- The mean quadrupole deformation for cadmium isotopes reaches maxima of approximately 0.12 for A=104,106 and 0.10 for A=118, with a local minimum of about 0.08 at A=112.
- For the specific decay of 116Cd, the unquenched value of the two-neutrino double beta decay matrix element in the closure approximation is M2νcl = 1.885, compared to the value extracted from charge exchange reactions of M2νcl,cer = 0.314.
- The NMEs for the spherical shape are always larger than those including configuration mixing, with the reduction being smaller near shell closures where the collective wave functions are predominantly spherical.

## Context

This work extends previous correlations found for individual decays between NME magnitude and pairing correlations, deformation, and shell effects (Rodríguez and Martínez-Pinedo 2010; Rodríguez and Martínez-Pinedo 2011) to a whole isotopic chain, showing these are general features independent of detailed nuclear wave function structure. The study uses virtual double beta decays of Cadmium to Tin isotopes (except 116Cd) to systematically investigate shell effects from N=50 to N=82, complementing earlier work using shell model (Caurier et al. 2008), QRPA (Simkovic et al. 1999), and IBM (Barea and Iachello 2009) approaches. The observed proportionality between the two-neutrino closure matrix element and the Gamow-Teller part of the neutrinoless NME opens the possibility of constraining the 0νββ NME from experimental Gamow-Teller strength functions measured via charge exchange reactions, though the authors note this proportionality has not been found in QRPA calculations (Šimkovic et al. 2011).
