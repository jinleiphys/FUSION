# Effects of Strong Color Fields on Baryon Dynamics

> We calculate the antibaryon-to-baryon ratios, anti-p/p, anti-Lambda/Lambda, anti-Xi/Xi, and anti-Omega/Omega for Au+Au collisions at RHIC (sqrt{s}_{NN}=200 GeV). The effects of strong color fields associated with an enhanced strangeness and diquark production probability and with an effective decrease of formation times are investigated. Antibaryon-to-baryon ratios increase with the color field strength. The ratios also increase with the strangeness content |S|. The netbaryon number at midrapidity considerably increases with the color field strength while the netproton number remains roughly the same. This shows that the enhanced baryon transport involves a conversion into the hyperon sector (hyperonization) which can be observed in the (Lambda - anti-Lambda)/(p - anti-p) ratio.

## Key claim

Strong color fields (modeled as an increased string tension from κ=1 GeV/fm to κ=3 GeV/fm) enhance baryon-antibaryon pair production and reduce formation times, leading to larger antibaryon-to-baryon ratios and stronger baryon transport to midrapidity; however, the netproton number at midrapidity remains roughly unchanged while the netbaryon number increases, indicating that the extra baryon transport involves conversion into strange baryons (hyperonization).

## Method

The authors used the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport model, which simulates multiple interactions of ingoing and newly produced particles, excitation and fragmentation of color strings, and formation and decay of hadronic resonances. They varied the string tension κ from the default 1 GeV/fm to 3 GeV/fm to represent strong color fields, which increases the production probability of strange quarks (γ_s from 0.37 to 0.72) and diquarks (γ_qq from 0.093 to 0.45) via the Schwinger mechanism, and reduces formation times as t_f ~ 1/κ. Calculations were performed for Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV at various centralities.

## Key numbers

- Antibaryon-to-baryon ratios at midrapidity for central Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV with strong color fields (κ=3 GeV/fm): p̄/p = 0.52 ± 0.01, Λ̄/Λ = 0.66 ± 0.02, Ξ̄/Ξ = 0.72 ± 0.04, Ω̄/Ω = 0.80 ± 0.08
- For comparison, with standard string tension (κ=1 GeV/fm): p̄/p = 0.41 ± 0.01, Λ̄/Λ = 0.56 ± 0.01, Ξ̄/Ξ = 0.64 ± 0.03, Ω̄/Ω = 0.60 ± 0.12
- Experimental STAR data at √s_NN = 130 GeV for comparison: p̄/p = 0.72 ± 0.01, Λ̄/Λ = 0.73 ± 0.03, Ξ̄/Ξ = 0.83 ± 0.03, Ω̄/Ω = 0.95 ± 0.15
- Netbaryon number at midrapidity increases from about 32 (κ=1 GeV/fm) to 42 (κ=3 GeV/fm) for central collisions
- Netproton number at midrapidity remains roughly constant at about 12 independent of color field strength
- (Λ-Λ̄)/(p-p̄) ratio at midrapidity: ~0.45 for κ=1 GeV/fm, ~0.8 for κ=3 GeV/fm
- Strangeness suppression factor γ_s increases from 0.37 (κ=1 GeV/fm) to 0.72 (κ=3 GeV/fm)
- Diquark suppression factor γ_qq increases from 0.093 (κ=1 GeV/fm) to 0.45 (κ=3 GeV/fm)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of strong color fields (color ropes) by Sorge et al. (1992) and Soff et al. (1999) that showed enhanced strange baryon production at CERN-SPS energies, applying the concept to RHIC energies. The calculations address the puzzle of finite netbaryon number at midrapidity observed by the STAR, PHOBOS, BRAHMS, and PHENIX collaborations, which contradicts the assumption of complete transparency. The hyperonization mechanism provides a testable prediction for the (Λ-Λ̄)/(p-p̄) ratio and suggests that strong color fields may also explain the large elliptic flow values observed at RHIC, which calculations using vacuum string tension (Bleicher and Stocker, 2002) fail to reproduce.
