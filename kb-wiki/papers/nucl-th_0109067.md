# Inter-band B(E2) transition strengths in odd-mass heavy deformed nuclei

> Inter-band B(E2) transition strengths between different normal parity bands in 163Dy and 165Er are described using the pseudo-SU(3) model. The Hamiltonian includes Nilsson single-particle energies, quadrupole-quadrupole and pairing interactions with fixed, parametrized strengths, and three extra rotor terms used to fine tune the energy spectra. In addition to inter-band transitions, the energy spectra and the ground state intra-band B(E2) strengths are reported. The results show the pseudo-SU(3) shell model to be a powerful microscopic theory for a description of the normal parity sector in heavy deformed odd-A nuclei.

## Key claim

The pseudo-SU(3) model can quantitatively describe inter-band B(E2) transition strengths in odd-mass heavy deformed nuclei, but the agreement is better for 163Dy than for 165Er, where differences in the SU(3) components of the wave functions between bands lead to some discrepancies with experiment.

## Method

The authors used a Hamiltonian with Nilsson single-particle energies, quadrupole-quadrupole and pairing interactions with fixed strengths taken from systematics, plus three rotor-like terms (K_J^2, J^2, and C_2) whose parameters a, b, and A_sym were fitted to the energy spectra. They constructed many-particle states by selecting the proton and neutron SU(3) irreps with the largest second-order Casimir operator values, coupling them to a total SU(3) irrep with good angular momentum, and then calculated B(E2) transition strengths using effective charges e_pi = 2.3 and e_nu = 1.3 for both nuclei.

## Key numbers

- For 163Dy, experimental intra-band B(E2) strengths: 5/2 → 9/2 = 97 ± 18 e^2b^2×10^-2; 9/2 → 13/2 = 148 ± 59 e^2b^2×10^-2; 11/2 → 15/2 = 205 ± 49 e^2b^2×10^-2
- For 163Dy inter-band transitions, theoretical vs experimental (e^2b^2×10^-2): 1/2^-_1 → 5/2^-_1: Theo. 3.95, Exp. 4.0 ± 0.8; 3/2^-_1 → 5/2^-_1: Theo. 2.16, Exp. 1.8 ± 0.6
- For 165Er inter-band transitions: 1/2^-_1 → 5/2^-_1: Theo. 1.83, Exp. 1.08 ± 0.13 e^2b^2×10^-2; 3/2^-_1 → 7/2^-_1: Theo. 1.22, Exp. 0.53 ± 0.12 e^2b^2×10^-2
- The leading SU(3) component in 163Dy wave functions is between 50 and 68% (30,6) and between 24 to 38% (32,2)
- In 165Er, the 1/2^-_1 state is 97% (32,2) and 2% (31,4), while the 5/2^-_1 state is 67% and 31% of these irreps respectively

## Context

This work extends the pseudo-SU(3) model applications by Vargas (2000) and Hirsch (2000) from energy spectra and intra-band B(E2) transitions to inter-band B(E2) transition strengths in odd-mass heavy deformed nuclei. The results demonstrate that the pseudo-SU(3) scheme, which was first applied to odd-mass nuclei nearly thirty years earlier by Ratcliff (1973), can provide a microscopic description of normal parity bands in heavy deformed odd-A nuclei. The ability to describe inter-band transitions enables future exploration of complementary properties such as g factors, the scissors mode, and beta decay transitions in these systems.
