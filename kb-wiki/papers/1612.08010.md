# Uncertainties in constraining low-energy constants from $^3$H $β$ decay

> We discuss the uncertainties in constraining low-energy constants of chiral effective field theory from $^3$H $β$ decay. The half-life is very precisely known, so that the Gamow-Teller matrix element has been used to fit the coupling $c_D$ of the axial-vector current to a short-range two-nucleon pair. Because the same coupling also describes the leading one-pion-exchange three-nucleon force, this in principle provides a very constraining fit, uncorrelated with the $^3$H binding energy fit used to constrain another low-energy coupling in three-nucleon forces. However, so far such $^3$H half-life fits have only been performed at a fixed cutoff value. We show that the cutoff dependence due to the regulators in the axial-vector two-body current can significantly affect the Gamow-Teller matrix elements and consequently also the extracted values for the $c_D$ coupling constant. The degree of the cutoff dependence is correlated with the softness of the employed NN interaction. As a result, present three-nucleon forces based on a fit to $^3$H $β$ decay underestimate the uncertainty in $c_D$. We explore a range of $c_D$ values that is compatible within cutoff variation with the experimental $^3$H half-life and estimate the resulting uncertainties for many-body systems by performing calculations of symmetric nuclear matter.

# Uncertainties in constraining low-energy constants from $^3$H $\beta$ decay

## Key claim
The authors show that the cutoff dependence due to regulators in axial-vector two-body currents can significantly affect Gamow-Teller matrix elements and consequently the extracted values of the low-energy coupling $c_D$, meaning that previous fits to the $^3$H half-life performed at a single cutoff value underestimate the uncertainty in $c_D$. The degree of cutoff dependence is correlated with the softness of the employed nucleon-nucleon interaction.

## Method
The authors solve Faddeev equations in a momentum-space partial-wave basis to obtain $^3$H and $^3$He wave functions using chiral nucleon-nucleon interactions at N$^2$LO and N$^3$LO plus three-nucleon interactions at N$^2$LO. They evaluate the Gamow-Teller matrix element including one-body and two-body axial-vector currents at order $Q^3$, applying either local or non-local regulators to the two-body current operators with varying cutoff scales, and then extract $c_D$ values by fitting to the experimental $^3$H half-life. The resulting uncertainties are propagated to symmetric nuclear matter calculations using the self-consistent Green's function framework.

## Key numbers
- The experimental Gamow-Teller matrix element that reproduces the measured $^3$H half-life is $\langle\text{GT}\rangle_\text{exp}=\sqrt{3}\cdot 0.956$.
- For the EM 500 MeV N$^3$LO potential with a local regulator at cutoff $\Lambda=400$ MeV, the extracted $c_D$ value is $-0.9$; for unregularized current operators, the extracted $c_D$ is $6.0$.
- At $\Lambda=500$ MeV with a local regulator, the result reproduces the earlier value $c_D=-0.24$ from Gazit (2008).
- The Fermi matrix element is $\langle \text{F} \rangle = 0.9998$ for all EM 500 MeV potentials, independent of three-nucleon force LECs.
- The one-body current alone underpredicts the experimental Gamow-Teller matrix element by about 2%.
- For symmetric nuclear matter using the EM 500 MeV potential, the saturation energy ranges from $E/A\sim -11$ MeV (smaller cutoffs) to $E/A\sim -21$ MeV (unregularized two-body current), with saturation densities $\rho\sim 0.13-0.15$ fm$^{-3}$ for cutoffs $\Lambda=400-700$ MeV.

## Context
This work extends the approach of Gazit (2008), who first proposed fitting $c_D$ and $c_E$ to the $^3$H binding energy and $\beta$-decay half-life, but only at a fixed cutoff. The authors show that the regulator dependence introduces significant additional uncertainty, contradicting the assumption that such fits provide a tightly constrained determination of $c_D$. The results also show that interactions fitted to other observables, such as the $^3$H binding energy and $^4$He charge radius (Hebeler 2011) or the neutron-deuteron scattering length (Epelbaum 2002), generally cannot simultaneously reproduce the $^3$H $\beta$-decay Gamow-Teller matrix element. This work enables future studies to incorporate regulator uncertainties when using $c_D$ and $c_E$ in many-body calculations, and motivates the development of consistently regularized chiral forces and currents.
