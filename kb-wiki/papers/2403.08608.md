# Nuclear coherent $π^0$ photoproduction with charge-exchange and spin-flip rescattering

> In this work, we present an updated model for nuclear $π^0$ photoproduction, which incorporates pion second-order rescattering on intermediate excited nuclear states. Our approach is based on the distorted wave impulse approximation in momentum space. The many-body medium effects are incorporated in the complex effective $Δ$ self-energy, employing the results of the recently developed second-order pion-nucleus scattering potential. The experimental data for ${}^{12}$C and ${}^{40}$Ca are successfully described without the need to fit the model parameters of the photoproduction amplitude as a result of incorporating the second-order part of nuclear photoproduction potential, which involves intermediate nucleon spin-flip and charge exchange.

# Nuclear coherent $\pi^0$ photoproduction with charge-exchange and spin-flip rescattering

## Key claim

The authors present an updated model for nuclear $\pi^0$ photoproduction that successfully describes experimental data for ${}^{12}$C and ${}^{40}$Ca without fitting the photoproduction amplitude parameters, provided that second-order rescattering involving intermediate nucleon spin-flip and charge-exchange interactions is incorporated. The model relies on using the effective $\Delta$ self-energy previously determined from pion-nucleus scattering data rather than fitting it to photoproduction data.

## Method

The authors employ a distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) in momentum space, constructing a second-order nuclear photoproduction potential based on their recently developed second-order pion-nucleus scattering potential. The many-body medium effects are incorporated through a complex effective $\Delta$ self-energy, with the resonant $M_{1+}^{3/2}$ multipole modified using the MAID98 separation into resonant and background parts. The elementary photoproduction amplitudes (except $M_{1+}^{3/2}$) are taken from the MAID2007 unitary isobar model, while the pion-nucleus scattering amplitude required for the final-state interaction is obtained from a fit to $\pi^\pm$-${}^{12}$C scattering data covering 80–180 MeV pion laboratory kinetic energy.

## Key numbers

- The $\Delta$-resonance mass used in the MAID98 parametrization is 1235 MeV, with a decay width $\Gamma_R = 130$ MeV and damping parameter $X = 500$ MeV
- The $\bar M_{3/2}$ normalization constant for the $M_{1+}^{3/2}$ resonant amplitude is 0.323 GeV$^{-1/2}$
- The off-shell vertex factor uses a cutoff parameter $\Lambda = 1.25$ GeV
- The phenomenological unitarization phase for $M_{1+}^{3/2}$ is given by $\phi(W) = (22.13 x - 3.769 x^2 + 0.184 x^3)$ degrees, with $x = (W - m_\pi - m_N) / 100$ MeV
- The model describes experimental data for ${}^{12}$C and ${}^{40}$Ca nuclei

## Context

This work extends the DWIA approach of Drechsel et al. (1999) for coherent $\pi^0$ photoproduction by incorporating second-order rescattering effects that were neglected in that earlier model. The inclusion of charge-exchange and spin-flip rescattering addresses a discrepancy noted by Miller (2019), who demonstrated that such effects could increase predicted cross sections by 5–6%, impacting neutron skin extraction from ${}^{208}$Pb measurements by the A2 Collaboration (Tarbert et al., 2013). The model builds directly on the authors' prior work on pion-nucleus scattering (Tsaran et al., 2023) and enables more reliable extraction of neutron skin thickness from coherent $\pi^0$ photoproduction data, which is relevant for constraining the symmetry energy of nuclear matter and understanding neutron star structure.
