# The holographic spectral function in non-equilibrium states

> We develop holographic prescriptions for obtaining spectral functions in non-equilibrium states and space-time dependent non-equilibrium shifts in the energy and spin of quasi-particle like excitations. We reproduce strongly coupled versions of aspects of non-equilibrium dynamics of Fermi surfaces in Landau's Fermi-liquid theory. We find that the incoming wave boundary condition at the horizon does not suffice to obtain a well-defined perturbative expansion for non-equilibrium observables. Our prescription, based on analysis of regularity at the horizon, allows such a perturbative expansion to be achieved nevertheless and can be precisely formulated in a universal manner independent of the non-equilibrium state, provided the state thermalizes. We also find that the non-equilibrium spectral function furnishes information about the relaxation modes of the system. Along the way, we argue that in a typical non-supersymmetric theory with a gravity dual, there may exist a window of temperature and chemical potential at large N, in which a generic non-equilibrium state can be characterized by just a finitely few operators with low scaling dimensions, even far away from the hydrodynamic limit.

## Key claim

The paper develops a holographic prescription for obtaining spectral functions in non-equilibrium states, finding that the standard incoming wave boundary condition at the horizon is insufficient for a well-defined perturbative expansion; instead, regularity at the horizon requires setting the coefficient of the homogeneous incoming solution to zero in the presence of background quasinormal modes, enabling a universal prescription independent of the non-equilibrium state provided the state thermalizes.

## Method

The authors study scalar and fermionic field perturbations on holographic duals of non-equilibrium backgrounds, working in the linearized approximation where the background fluctuation is treated as a small perturbation. They analyze the bulk field equations and show that the general solution contains homogeneous incoming and outgoing solutions plus a particular solution determined by the background perturbation; by imposing regularity at the horizon, they fix the arbitrary coefficient of the homogeneous incoming wave to zero, yielding a well-defined retarded Green's function. The prescription is then generalized to include nonlinearities in the dynamics of the variables characterizing the non-equilibrium state.

## Key numbers

- The paper discusses time scales for thermalization in heavy-ion collisions: thermalization occurs in time ≤ 1 fm at temperature about 175 MeV (at RHIC), though these are contextual numbers from the introduction, not results of the paper's own calculations.
- The paper defines a hierarchy of time scales in the Boltzmann limit: chemical equilibration time t_chem, time for conservative solution approximation t_cons, and thermalization time t_therm, with the ordering t_chem < t_cons < t_therm.
- The paper notes that in a typical non-supersymmetric theory with a gravity dual, there may exist a window of temperature and chemical potential at large N where a generic non-equilibrium state can be characterized by just a finitely few operators with low scaling dimensions, even far away from the hydrodynamic limit (no specific numerical values given for this window).

## Context

This work extends the holographic study of non-equilibrium dynamics beyond hydrodynamics and relaxation, which had been previously addressed by fluid-gravity correspondence (fluidgravity1, fluidgravity2, Janik, Sayantani) and relaxation studies (myself1, myself2, myself3). The paper builds on prior holographic treatments of equilibrium spectral functions using incoming wave boundary conditions (incoming1, incoming2) and on earlier work defining equilibrium statistical functions holographically (Herzog). The results enable future development of holographic quantum kinetic theories and provide a framework for computing non-equilibrium spectral functions that could be compared with time-resolved ARPES experiments, such as those across the metal-insulator transition (Perfetti). The paper also reproduces strongly coupled versions of aspects of non-equilibrium dynamics of Fermi surfaces from Landau's Fermi-liquid theory.
