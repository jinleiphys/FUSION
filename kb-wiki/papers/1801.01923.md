# Constraining the speed of sound inside neutron stars with chiral effective field theory interactions and observations

> The dense matter equation of state (EOS) determines neutron star (NS) structure but can be calculated reliably only up to one to two times the nuclear saturation density, using accurate many-body methods that employ nuclear interactions from chiral effective field theory constrained by scattering data. In this work, we use physically motivated ansatzes for the speed of sound $c_S$ at high density to extend microscopic calculations of neutron-rich matter to the highest densities encountered in stable NS cores. We show how existing and expected astrophysical constraints on NS masses and radii from X-ray observations can constrain the speed of sound in the NS core. We confirm earlier expectations that $c_S$ is likely to violate the conformal limit of $c_S^2\leq c^2/3 $, possibly reaching values closer to the speed of light $c$ at a few times the nuclear saturation density, independent of the nuclear Hamiltonian. If QCD obeys the conformal limit, we conclude that the rapid increase of $c_S$ required to accommodate a $2 $ M$_\odot$ NS suggests a form of strongly interacting matter where a description in terms of nucleons will be unwieldy, even between one and two times the nuclear saturation density. For typical NSs with masses in the range $1.2-1.4~$ M$_\odot$, we find radii between $10$ and $14$ km, and the smallest possible radius of a $1.4$ M$_{\odot}$ NS consistent with constraints from nuclear physics and observations is $8.4$ km. We also discuss how future observations could constrain the EOS and guide theoretical developments in nuclear physics.

## Key claim

The authors confirm earlier expectations that the speed of sound in neutron star cores is likely to violate the conformal limit of \(c_S^2 \leq c^2/3\), possibly reaching values close to the speed of light at a few times nuclear saturation density, independent of the nuclear Hamiltonian; however, if QCD does obey the conformal limit, the rapid increase in \(c_S\) required to support a 2 M\(_\odot\) neutron star suggests that a description in terms of nucleons becomes unwieldy even between one and two times nuclear saturation density.

## Method

The authors use auxiliary-field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) many-body calculations with local chiral effective field theory interactions (up to N\(^2\)LO) to compute the neutron-matter equation of state up to \(2n_0\) (where \(n_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) is nuclear saturation density). They then extend the speed of sound to higher densities using physically motivated continuous parameterizations (a Gaussian form for the conformal-limit case and a skewed Gaussian for the non-conformal case), match these to the microscopic calculations at a transition density \(n_{\text{tr}}\) varied between \(1-2n_0\), and solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain neutron star mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: \(n_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3} \simeq 2.7 \times 10^{14}\) g cm\(^{-3}\)
- For typical neutron stars with masses \(1.2-1.4\) M\(_\odot\), radii are found between 10 and 14 km
- The smallest possible radius of a 1.4 M\(_\odot\) neutron star consistent with nuclear physics and observations is 8.4 km
- The maximum observed neutron star mass constraint used is \(2.01 \pm 0.04\) M\(_\odot\) from Antoniadis (2013)
- At \(n_0\), the energy per particle for the N\(^2\)LO TPE-only interaction is \(15.6 \pm 1.4\) MeV (with uncertainty from chiral expansion parameter \(p = \sqrt{3/5} k_F\))
- At \(2n_0\), the pressure for the N\(^2\)LO TPE-only interaction is \(7.8 \pm 2.8\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\) (with uncertainty from chiral expansion parameter \(p = \sqrt{3/5} k_F\))
- For the chiral N\(^2\)LO TPE-only interaction, no curve satisfies both the conformal bound and the 2 M\(_\odot\) mass constraint for transition densities larger than approximately \(0.25\) fm\(^{-3}\)

## Context

This work extends the approach of Bedaque (2014) by using ab initio chiral effective field theory calculations (rather than parameterized equations of state) to constrain the speed of sound at high density, and by studying the influence of uncertainties from two- and three-nucleon interactions. The results confirm tension between the conformal bound and the existence of 2 M\(_\odot\) neutron stars, and enable future X-ray observations of neutron star masses and radii to distinguish between scenarios where QCD does or does not obey the conformal limit, guiding theoretical developments in nuclear physics.
