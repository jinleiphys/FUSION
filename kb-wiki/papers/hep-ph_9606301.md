# The Standard Model at Low Energies

## Key claim

This paper is a workshop proceedings booklet, not a research article presenting new results; it summarizes talks and provides a guide to the literature on Chiral Perturbation Theory (CHPT) as of 1996, with the caveat that most results presented at the workshop were to be published elsewhere and the booklet serves primarily as a reference collection rather than a definitive proceedings volume.

## Method

The workshop brought together approximately 50 participants at the European Center for Theoretical Studies in Nuclear Physics and related Areas (ECT*) in Trento, Italy, from April 29 to May 10, 1996, following earlier meetings in Ringberg (1988), Dobogoko (1991), and Karrebaeksminde (1993). The booklet contains one-page abstracts with references per speaker, a listing of review papers relevant to the field, and the workshop program organized by day and speaker.

## Key numbers

- The workshop had about 50 participants, consistent with the three previous workshops in the series (Ringberg 1988, Dobogoko 1991, Karrebaeksminde 1993).
- In the talk by Buettiker, the one-loop chiral coupling constants from pion-pion scattering were reported as l1 = -1.7 ± 0.15 and l2 ≈ 5.0.
- In the talk by Bernard, the pion-pion scattering length a0^0 was deduced from pion-nucleon to two-pion-nucleon data as 0.21 ± 0.07, and a0^2 as -0.031 ± 0.007.
- In the talk by Golowich, the two-loop isospin vector spectral function fit to data was successful for energies E ≤ 400 MeV.

## Context

This workshop proceedings follows the series of topical meetings on Chiral Perturbation Theory held at Ringberg (1988), Dobogoko (1991), and Karrebaeksminde (1993), and provides a snapshot of the field in 1996. The booklet enables researchers to locate the main references for each talk, including foundational work by Weinberg (1979) and Gasser and Leutwyler (1984, 1985), as well as contemporary review papers by Bijnens (1993), Meissner (1993), Ecker (1995), and Bernard, Kaiser and Meissner (1995). The workshop concentrated on CHPT in its various settings, including meson and baryon sectors, and the abstracts point toward ongoing work such as two-loop calculations, resonance saturation, and applications to nuclear systems.
