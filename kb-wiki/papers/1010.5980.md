# Holographic Nucleons

> Recent developments in holography have provided a new vista to the nucleon composition. A strongly coupled core nucleon tied with vector mesons emerge in line with the Cheshire cat principle. The cat is found to hide in the holographic direction. We discuss the one, two and many baryon problem in this context and point at the striking similarities between the holographic results and recent lattice simulations at strong coupling.

# Holographic Nucleons

## Key claim

Holographic QCD realizes baryons as holograms of flavor instantons in a curved five-dimensional space, with the Cheshire cat principle emerging naturally: the bag boundary (the "cat's smile") becomes an unphysical gauge artifact hidden in the holographic direction, and the baryon core size is set by the inverse square root of the 't Hooft coupling, making the delineation between core and cloud arbitrary at leading order.

## Method

The paper reviews the construction of holographic baryons starting from a flavor instanton in an AdS-like 5D space with a warped holographic direction, where the instanton is forced by gravity to the bottom of the holographic coordinate. The baryon at the boundary is obtained as the holonomy of the instanton gauge field along the holographic direction, yielding a Skyrmion profile. A semiclassical expansion around the instanton is performed using non-rigid constraints to separate zero modes from vector meson fluctuations, and the formalism is extended to the two-baryon sector using the ADHM 2-instanton configuration and to many-baryon systems via periodic instanton crystals.

## Key numbers

- Instanton size: $\rho \sim 1/\sqrt{\lambda}$, where $\lambda = g^2 N_c$ is the 't Hooft coupling
- Core baryon size in physical units: $\rho M_K \equiv \rho M_K / \sqrt{\lambda}$, with $M_K$ the Kaluza-Klein scale
- Core contribution to the central nucleon-nucleon potential: $V_{1,\mathrm{core}}^+ \approx (27\pi N_c)/(2\lambda) \cdot 1/d^2$
- Critical density for fcc-to-bcc transition: $n_B \approx 1/2\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}$, or approximately 3 times nuclear matter density
- Dyon mass splitting: $M_+ = M v$ and $M_- = M(1-v)$, with $v$ the Higgs vev; at equilibrium $v = 1/2$ giving equal masses $M/2$
- Binding energy estimate for dyonic salt: $\Delta \approx 350\ \mathrm{MeV}$ for $L \approx 1\ \mathrm{fm}$
- Melting temperature estimate: fcc Skyrmion crystal melts at approximately 30 MeV; dyonic salt melts at approximately 10 MeV

## Context

This review extends the holographic approach to baryons developed by Sakai and Sugimoto (2005) and follows the author's prior work with Kim (2009) on holographic baryons and nuclear forces. The results show striking similarities to strong-coupling lattice QCD simulations at finite density, and the 50/50 scenario for baryon form factors advocated by Gerry Brown is shown to emerge naturally from the holographic construction when the vector meson tower is truncated. The work enables further study of dense nuclear matter as a crystal of instantons or dyons, with the fcc-to-bcc transition providing a holographic description of chiral symmetry restoration at high density.
