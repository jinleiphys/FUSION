# New Primary Mechanisms for the Synthesis of Rare 9Be in Early Supernovae

> We present two new primary mechanisms for the synthesis of the rare nucleus 9Be, both triggered by nu-induced production of 3H followed by 4He(3H,gamma)7Li in the He shells of core-collapse supernovae. For progenitors of ~8M_sun, 7Li(3H,n_0)9Be occurs during the rapid expansion of the shocked He shell. Alternatively, for ultra-metal-poor progenitors of ~11-15M_sun, 7Li(n,gamma)8Li(n,gamma)9Li(e^-anti-nu_e)9Be occurs with neutrons produced by 4He(anti-nu_e,e^+n)3H, assuming a hard effective anti-nu_e spectrum from oscillations (which also leads to heavy element production through rapid neutron capture) and a weak explosion (so the 9Be survives shock passage). We discuss the associated production of 7Li and 11B, noting patterns in LiBeB production that might distinguish the new mechanisms from others.

## Key claim

Two new primary mechanisms for synthesizing the rare nucleus ⁹Be in core-collapse supernovae are presented, both triggered by neutrino-induced production of ³H followed by ⁴He(³H,γ)⁷Li in He shells, but the mechanisms operate only under specific conditions: for ~8 M⊙ progenitors the mechanism requires rapid expansion of the shocked He shell, while for ultra-metal-poor ~11–15 M⊙ progenitors it requires a hard effective antineutrino spectrum from oscillations, low explosion energies, and low metallicities.

## Method

The authors used the KEPLER hydrodynamic code with a full reaction network to evolve ultra-metal-poor progenitors of 8.1, 11, and 15 M⊙ (initial metallicity Z = 10⁻⁴ Z⊙) and simulate explosions by driving a piston into the collapsing progenitor. Neutrino spectra were approximated as Fermi-Dirac distributions with fixed temperatures, and calculations explored sensitivity to explosion energies (0.06–1 B), neutrino spectra (hard H or soft S), and full ν̄ₑ ↔ ν̄ₓ oscillations characteristic of an inverted mass hierarchy. For the 8.1 M⊙ progenitor, ⁹Be is produced via ⁴He(³H,γ)⁷Li(³H,n₀)⁹Be in the shocked He shell; for the 11–15 M⊙ progenitors, ⁹Be is produced via ⁷Li(n,γ)⁸Li(n,γ)⁹Li(e⁻ν̄ₑ)⁹Be in outer He shells prior to shock arrival.

## Key numbers

- ⁹Be yield for u8.1H.1 (hard spectra, 0.1 B explosion): 1.20 × 10⁻¹⁰ M⊙
- ⁹Be yield for u8.1S.1 (soft spectra, 0.1 B explosion): 2.55 × 10⁻¹¹ M⊙
- ⁹Be yield for u11*H̄.1 (11 M⊙, hard spectra with oscillations, 0.1 B): 9.14 × 10⁻⁹ M⊙
- ⁹Be yield for u8.1H̄.06 (8.1 M⊙, hard spectra with oscillations, 0.06 B): 1.57 × 10⁻¹⁰ M⊙
- ⁹Be yield for u8.1H̄.3 (8.1 M⊙, hard spectra with oscillations, 0.3 B): 2.56 × 10⁻¹⁰ M⊙
- Neutrino luminosity per species: Lν(0) = 16.7 B/s with time constant τν = 3 s
- Neutrino temperatures for hard spectra (H): (Tνₑ, Tν̄ₑ, Tνₓ) = (4, 5.33, 8) MeV
- Neutrino temperatures for soft spectra (S): (3, 4, 6) MeV
- ⁸Li half-life: 838 ms; ⁹Li branching ratio to particle-unstable excited states in ⁹Be: 49.5%
- ⁷Li and ¹¹B yields in u8.1 models stable within factor ~2 of 1.6 × 10⁻⁷ and 8.6 × 10⁻⁸ M⊙, respectively
- Number-yield ratio ¹¹B/⁷Li in u8.1 models: ~0.2–0.4 (solar value 0.29)
- ⁶Li/⁷Li ≲ 10⁻⁴ and ¹⁰B/¹¹B ~ (0.5–2) × 10⁻² for all models

## Context

This work extends the neutrino-process nucleosynthesis framework of Woosley and collaborators (1990) and the neutrino-induced r-process scenario of Banerjee et al. (2011) to explain ⁹Be production in early supernovae, challenging the long-held view that ⁹Be is produced almost exclusively by Galactic cosmic rays (GCRs) as argued by Prantzos (2007) and others. The mechanisms provide candidate primary processes that could naturally explain the observed linear correlation between log(Be/H) and log(E/H) with slope ~0.9–1 over ~3 dex reported by Boesgaard et al. (2011), and enable future observational tests distinguishing these mechanisms from GCR production through simultaneous measurements of Be and B at metallicities [Fe/H] ≤ −1.4.
