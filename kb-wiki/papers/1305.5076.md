# Pion photoproduction on nucleus with two-nucleon emission

> A model for the pion photoproduction on nuclei in the $A\left({γ,\, πNN} \right)B$ reaction is presented. This is an extension of our recent model for the $A\left({γ,\, πN} \right)B$ reaction. In this approach we have moved beyond the standard shell-model considering $Δ$\textit{N} correlations in the nuclear wave functions, which are caused by the virtual transitions $NN \to ΔN \to NN$ in the ground state of the nucleus. The main ingredients of the model are the two- and three-particle density matrices and the transition operators $γΔ\to Nπ$ and $γN \to Nπ$. The direct and exchange reaction mechanisms, which follow from the structure of the density matrices, are examined. The model is used to investigate the $^{12}$C$\left({γ,\, π^{+} p} \right)$ and $^{12}$C$\left({γ,\, π^{-} p} \right)$ reactions in the kinematic region of the large momentum transfers to the residual nuclear system, where the pion production occurs with the emission of two nucleons.

## Key claim

The paper presents a model for pion photoproduction on nuclei with two-nucleon emission that extends beyond the standard shell model by including ΔN correlations in the nuclear ground state wave functions, caused by virtual NN → ΔN → NN transitions. The model is applied to ¹²C(γ, π⁺p) and ¹²C(γ, π⁻p) reactions specifically in the kinematic region of large momentum transfers to the residual nuclear system, where the authors note that experimental data for the A(γ, πNN)B reaction are "practically absent."

## Method

The authors construct a theoretical framework for the A(γ, πNN)B reaction using two- and three-particle density matrices derived from nuclear wave functions that include both nucleon and Δ-isobar configurations. The direct and exchange reaction amplitudes are expressed in terms of these density matrices and single-particle transition operators for γN → Nπ (using the non-relativistic Blomqvist-Laget operator) and γΔ → Nπ (derived from an S-matrix approach). The model uses the oscillator shell model for the A-2 nucleon core, while the ΔN-system wave function is obtained from the Schrödinger equation with a π- and ρ-meson exchange potential describing the NN → ΔN transition.

## Key numbers

* The average photon energy in the ¹²C(γ, π⁺p) measurement was 355 MeV, with an average residual nucleus momentum of 300 MeV/c.
* The calculated cross-section of the ¹²C(γ, π⁺p)¹¹Be reaction is approximately 0.1 of the experimental cross-section.
* The proton energy interval for averaging was 80-120 MeV, the pion energy interval was 71.5-106.5 MeV, and the proton polar angle interval was 56-94 degrees.
* The bremsstrahlung electron beam energies used in the two experimental runs were 500 and 420 MeV.
* Positive pions were detected at an angle of 54 degrees to the photon beam axis.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous model for the A(γ, πN)B reaction (Glavanakov and Tabachenko, earlier work) to the two-nucleon knockout channel, moving beyond the independent particle model by incorporating ΔN correlations. The approach is contrasted with two other two-nucleon knockout mechanisms: meson exchange and isobar currents (two-body operators), and correlated nucleon pairs described by correlation functions reflecting the nucleon-nucleon potential structure, which are widely used in analysis of (e, e'NN) reactions oriented toward short-range correlations. The model is applied to existing data from the Tomsk synchrotron on ¹²C(γ, π⁻p) and ¹²C(γ, π⁺p) reactions, where the π⁺p channel is forbidden for quasi-free pion photoproduction and thus provides a test for isobar configurations in the nuclear ground state. The work enables future comparison with experimental A(γ, πNN)B data when such measurements become available.
