# Diphoton production in high-energy p+A collisions

> We consider semi-inclusive diphoton+jet and inclusive diphoton production in high-energy proton-nucleus collisions, treating the target nucleus as a Color-Glass-Condensate and the projectile proton in the parton model. We obtain the prompt diphoton production cross-section in terms of fragmentation and direct contributions. The fragmentation part is given in terms of single-photon and double-photon fragmentation functions. We study prompt, direct and fragmentation diphoton correlations in p+p and p+A collisions at the LHC, and show that at low values of transverse momenta of the produced photon pair, these correlations are sensitive to saturation effects. We show that back-to-back (de)-correlations in prompt diphoton production are stronger in fragmentation part than in the direct one.

## Key claim

The paper shows that at low transverse momenta of the produced photon pair, diphoton azimuthal correlations in high-energy proton-nucleus collisions are sensitive to saturation effects, with the caveat that this sensitivity is strongest in the fragmentation contribution rather than the direct contribution, and the analysis is performed in the soft-photon limit where the photon energies are much smaller than the quark energy.

## Method

The authors derive the cross-section for semi-inclusive diphoton+jet and inclusive diphoton production in high-energy proton-nucleus collisions using the Color-Glass-Condensate (CGC) framework for the target nucleus and the parton model for the projectile proton. They obtain the prompt diphoton production cross-section in the leading logarithmic approximation, separating it into direct and fragmentation contributions, where the fragmentation part is expressed in terms of single-photon and double-photon fragmentation functions. The calculation is simplified by taking the soft-photon limit, and the cross-section is expressed in terms of the dipole-target forward scattering amplitude.

## Key numbers

- The single-photon fragmentation function in the soft limit is given by \( D_{\gamma/h}(z,\mu_F^2)=\frac{\alpha_{em}e^2_q}{2\pi}\frac{2}{z}\log\left(\mu_F^2/\Lambda^2_{QCD}\right) \), where \( z \) is the fraction of the quark energy carried by the photon.
- The double-photon fragmentation function in the soft limit and leading-log approximation is \( D_{\gamma_1\gamma_2/h}(z_1,z_2,\mu_F^2)=\frac{\alpha_{em} e^2_q}{\pi}\frac{1}{z_1 z_2}\left({\frac{1}{\Lambda_{QCD}^2}}-\frac{1}{\mu_F^2}\right) \).

## Context

This work extends the study of saturation effects on particle correlations from dihadron production (Kovner and Rezaeian, previous work) and photon-hadron production (Rezaeian, 2012) to diphoton production in proton-nucleus collisions for the first time. The authors note that diphoton production has advantages over dihadron production because it avoids complications from hadronization and initial-state-final-state interference, and the cross-section depends only on the dipole amplitude rather than higher-order Wilson line correlators. The results enable future experimental searches for saturation signals at the LHC using diphoton azimuthal decorrelations at forward rapidity.
