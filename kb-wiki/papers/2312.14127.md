# Equation of state at neutron-star densities and beyond from perturbative QCD

> We explore the consequences of imposing robust thermodynamic constraints arising from perturbative Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) when inferring the dense-matter equation-of-state (EOS). We find that the termination density, up to which the EOS modeling is performed in an inference setup, strongly affects the constraining power of the QCD input. This sensitivity in the constraining power arises from EOSs that have a specific form, with drastic softening immediately above the termination density followed by a strong stiffening. We also perform explicit modeling of the EOS down from perturbative-QCD densities to construct a new QCD likelihood function that incorporates additional perturbative-QCD calculations of the sound speed and is insensitive to the termination density, which we make publicly available.

## Key claim

The constraining power of perturbative QCD (pQCD) calculations on the neutron-star equation of state depends strongly on the termination density up to which the EOS modeling is performed, with the effect becoming significant only when the termination density exceeds approximately 0.8 times the central density of the maximum-mass neutron star.

## Method

The authors compared two different EOS inference frameworks: a parametric sound-speed model (CSM) with 9 grid points between a breakdown density and 25 times nuclear saturation density, and a Gaussian-process (GP) model of the speed of sound. Both models were conditioned on astrophysical data including mass measurements of PSR J0348+0432 and PSR J1624-2230, gravitational-wave data from GW170817, and NICER observations of PSR J0740+6620, then evaluated against pQCD constraints using the Komoltsev-Kurkela (2021) construction with scale-averaged pQCD calculations at 2.4 and 2.6 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The critical termination density where QCD input begins to show any effect beyond astrophysical input is approximately 0.8 times the central density of the maximum-mass neutron star, or about 4 times nuclear saturation density (n_sat ≈ 0.16 fm^-3)
- At termination density n_term = n_TOV (approximately 5-8 n_sat) with μ_QCD = 2.6 GeV, roughly 20% of EOSs cannot be connected to pQCD for all values of the renormalization scale X
- At termination density n_term = 10 n_sat, roughly 50-60% of EOSs have a vanishing QCD likelihood
- The fraction of astrophysical evidence cut by QCD input reaches approximately 0.8 at n_term = 10 n_sat
- For n_term = 1.2 n_TOV, the fraction of astrophysical evidence cut by QCD input increases to 0.4 (for μ_QCD = 2.6 GeV) or 0.5 (for μ_QCD = 2.4 GeV)

## Context

This work resolves an apparent contradiction between Gorda et al. (2022), who found that QCD input provides considerable additional information beyond astrophysical data, and Somasundaram et al. (2022), who found this additional impact to be marginal. The discrepancy arises primarily from different choices of termination density: Gorda et al. (2022) used n_term = 10 n_sat while Somasundaram et al. (2022) used n_term = n_TOV. The authors also introduce a new QCD likelihood function that incorporates pQCD calculations of the sound speed and is insensitive to the termination density, which they make publicly available for future inference studies.
