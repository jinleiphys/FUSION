# Non-Gaussian equilibrium in a long-range Hamiltonian system

> We study the dynamics of a system of N classical spins with infinite-range interaction. We show that, when the thermodynamic limit is taken before the infinite-time limit, the system does not relax to the Boltzmann-Gibbs equilibrium, but exhibits different equilibrium properties, characterized by stable non-Gaussian velocity distributions and dynamical power-law correlation in phase-space.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for a system of N classical spins with infinite-range interaction, if the thermodynamic limit (N → ∞) is taken before the infinite-time limit (t → ∞), the system does not relax to the Boltzmann-Gibbs equilibrium but instead exhibits a metastable quasi-stationary state with non-Gaussian velocity distributions, though the authors note that a firm and unambiguous connection to non-extensive statistical mechanics remains a challenge for future studies.

## Method

The authors numerically integrate the equations of motion of the Hamiltonian Mean Field (HMF) model, a system of N planar classical spins with infinite-range ferromagnetic X-Y type interaction, using water bag initial conditions (all θ_i = 0 with M=1 and uniformly distributed velocities). They follow the time evolution of temperature, magnetization, and velocity distributions at a fixed energy density U=0.69, and analyze the N-dependence of the quasi-stationary state properties including its lifetime, temperature, magnetization, and velocity probability distribution functions.

## Key numbers

- The critical point of the HMF model is at energy density U_c = 0.75 and critical temperature T_c = 0.5
- At U=0.69, the canonical temperature is T_can = 0.476 and magnetization M_can = 0.307
- The quasi-stationary state temperature T_QSS(N) tends to T_∞ = 0.380 as N increases
- The scaling relations are [T_QSS(N) - T_∞] ∝ N^{-1/3} and M_QSS ∝ N^{-1/6}
- The lifetime of the quasi-stationary state has a linear dependence on N
- The best fit to the non-Gaussian velocity distribution uses entropic index q = 7 and temperature T = 0.38

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the HMF model by Antoni and Ruffo (1995) and the authors' own earlier work (Latora et al., 1998, 1999, 2000) that found Lévy walks, anomalous diffusion, and negative specific heat in this system. The results show consistency with the non-extensive statistical mechanics proposed by Tsallis (1988), and similar non-Gaussian velocity distributions have been observed in turbulent Couette-Taylor flow (Beck, 2000) and granular matter experiments (Kudrolli, 1997), though this is the first evidence in a Hamiltonian system. The findings suggest that similar scenarios should occur for systems with two-body interactions decaying like r^{-α} for 0 ≤ α ≤ α_c, where α_c equals the space dimension.
