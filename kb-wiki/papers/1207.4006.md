# Nuclear response for the Skyrme effective interaction with zero-range tensor terms. III. Neutron matter and neutrino propagation

> The formalism of the linear response for the Skyrme energy density functional including tensor terms derived in articles [1,2] for nuclear matter is applied here to the case of pure neutron matter. As in article [2] we present analytical results for the response function in all channels, the Landau parameters and the odd-power sum rules. Special emphasis is given to the inverse energy weighted sum rule because it can be used to detect non physical instabilities. Typical examples are discussed and numerical results shown. Moreover, as a direct application, neutrino propagation in neutron matter is investigated through its neutrino mean free path at zero temperature. This quantity turns out to be very sensitive to the tensor terms of the Skyrme energy density functional.

## Key claim

The tensor terms of the Skyrme energy density functional have a very sizable effect on the response functions of pure neutron matter, and the neutrino mean free path at zero temperature is highly sensitive to these tensor terms; however, the study is restricted to the academic case of zero-temperature neutron matter with only neutral currents, so the generalization to finite temperature and asymmetric nuclear matter is still in progress.

## Method

The authors applied the linear response formalism for the Skyrme energy density functional including zero-range tensor terms, previously derived for symmetric nuclear matter in articles I and II, to pure neutron matter. They solved the Bethe-Salpeter equations within the Random Phase Approximation (RPA) to obtain the response functions in all spin channels, derived analytical expressions for the Landau parameters and the odd-power sum rules (M1, M3, M-1), and used the inverse energy weighted sum rule (IEWSR) to detect non-physical instabilities. As a direct application, they computed the neutrino mean free path in neutron matter at zero temperature by integrating the double differential cross section for neutral current reactions, incorporating the spin longitudinal and spin transverse response functions separately.

## Key numbers

- For the T16 tensor parameterization at density ρ = 0.16 fm⁻³, a pole (instability) appears in the S=1, M=0 channel at transferred momentum q = 1.051 fm⁻¹.
- The critical density for the SkP Skyrme force shows a first instability in the S=0 channel at ρ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³ and a second at ρ ≈ 0.45 fm⁻³ due to a pole in the effective mass.
- The spin transverse contribution to the neutral current cross section varies between approximately 60% and 90% depending on the interaction (SLy5, T13, T16) and density (ρ = 0.08, 0.16, 0.24 fm⁻³).
- The response functions were calculated for transferred momenta q = 0.05 fm⁻¹ and q = 0.5 fm⁻¹ at densities ρ = 0.08 fm⁻³ and ρ = 0.16 fm⁻³.

## Context

This work extends the linear response formalism with tensor terms from symmetric nuclear matter (articles I and II by Davesne et al., 2009 and 2012) to pure neutron matter, which is essential for constructing energy density functionals reliable for both systems. The study of neutrino mean free path builds on previous non-relativistic approaches by Iwamoto (1982), Reddy (1998), Navarro (1999), Shen (2003), and Margueron (2003, 2006), which neglected tensor contributions. The results enable future investigations of neutron star cooling and finite-temperature generalizations, and the critical density analysis connects to studies of ferromagnetic finite-size instabilities in neutron matter by Marcos (1991), Fantoni (2001), Margueron (2002), Vidana (2002), and others.
