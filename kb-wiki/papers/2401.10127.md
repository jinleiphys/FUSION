# Heavy Quarks in Polarised Deep-Inelastic Scattering at the Electron-Ion Collider

> We extend the FONLL general-mass variable-flavour-number scheme to the case of longitudinally polarised DIS structure functions, accounting for perturbative corrections up to $\mathcal{O}(α_s^2)$. We quantify the impact of charm quark mass and higher-order perturbative corrections on projected measurements of inclusive and charm-tagged longitudinal asymmetries at the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) and at the Electron-ion collider in China (EicC). We demonstrate how the inclusion of these corrections is essential to compute predictions with an accuracy that matches the projected precision of the measurements. The computation is made publicly available through the open-source EKO and YADISM programs

## Key claim

The FONLL general-mass variable-flavour-number scheme is extended to longitudinally polarised deep-inelastic scattering structure functions at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO), and the inclusion of these heavy-quark mass and higher-order perturbative corrections is essential to match the projected precision of measurements at the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) and the Electron-ion collider in China (EicC), though the impact is quantified only for projected measurements rather than actual data.

## Method

The authors extend the FONLL general-mass variable-flavour-number scheme to longitudinally polarised DIS structure functions, implementing perturbative corrections up to O(α_s^2). They implement polarised DGLAP evolution up to NNLO in the EKO code, including variable-flavour-number scheme matching conditions computed only recently, and compute polarised structure functions using the YADISM program. The implementation is benchmarked against the Les Houches polarised PDF evolution tables and the PEGASUS and APFEL codes, finding agreement at the O(10^{-5}) level for NLO evolution.

## Key numbers

- Differences between EKO evolution, Les Houches benchmark tables, and PEGASUS are at the O(10^{-5}) level for polarised PDF evolution from Q^2=2 GeV^2 to Q^2=10^4 GeV^2 at NLO in a VFNS with n_f^(max)=5
- NLO corrections to polarised PDF evolution are up to a factor 2 for the small-x gluon and sea quarks, and up to 50% for sea quarks in the intermediate x region
- NNLO corrections to polarised PDF evolution are at the few percent level at most
- Target mass corrections to g_1 at x=0.5 grow from about 5% at Q^2=5 GeV^2 to about 15% at Q^2=2 GeV^2
- Target mass corrections at x=0.75 reach 30%, while at x=0.25 they are 2-3%
- The difference between exact and approximated target mass correction expressions reaches up to 25% for Q^2=2 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the FONLL general-mass variable-flavour-number scheme, previously developed for unpolarised DIS, to the polarised case, building on polarised splitting functions computed at NLO by Gluck (1995) and at NNLO by Moch (2014, 2015) and Blumlein (2021). The polarised operator matrix elements for VFN scheme matching were computed only recently by Bierenbaum (2022). The implementation enables NNLO-accurate predictions for polarised structure functions at the upcoming EIC and EicC facilities, where the few-percent-level NNLO corrections are comparable to or larger than projected experimental uncertainties.
