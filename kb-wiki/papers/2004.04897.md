# Constraints on the neutron drip-line with the newly observed 39Na

> The recently observed weakly-bound 39Na provides a stringent theoretical constraint on the neutron drip-line. We studied the properties of drip-line nuclei around 39Na with the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method and various Skyrme interactions. We adopted the extended SkM*-ext1 parameterization which can properly describe two-neutron separation energies of oxygen and fluorine isotopes and deformations at the center of the "island of inversion". Systematic calculations of drip lines of O, F, Ne, Na, Mg, and Al isotopes have been performed. We infer that 42Mg is weakly bound and 45Al is less weakly bound. 44Mg and 47Al could be barely existed. We also demonstrated the deformed halo properties of 39Na. Our studies could be valuable for experimental explorations of drip-line nuclei in the forthcoming FRIB and other rare-isotope beam facilities .

## Key claim

The newly observed weakly-bound 39Na provides a critical constraint that excludes both the overly-extended drip lines predicted by SkM* and the overly-compact drip lines predicted by SLy4; the SkM*_ext1 force is demonstrated to be the best choice for predicting drip lines from oxygen to aluminum, though its reproduction of the "island of inversion" deformation is not perfect.

## Method

The authors performed axially symmetric coordinate-space Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the HFB-AX code with B-spline techniques, employing a 21 fm box and 0.6 fm mesh spacing. They adopted five Skyrme forces (SkM*, SLy4, UNEDF0, SkM*_ext1, and UNEDF0_ext1) with a density-dependent pairing interaction, and implemented the quasiparticle blocking method for odd-A nuclei. Deformation constrained calculations using the augmented Lagrangian method were performed to study potential energy curves and shape coexistence.

## Key numbers

- 39Na is the drip-line nucleus for sodium isotopes in calculations with SkM*_ext1, UNEDF0, and UNEDF0_ext1 forces
- S2n of 39Na is 0.27 MeV with SkM*_ext1 and 0.63 MeV with UNEDF0_ext1
- S2n of 42Mg is 0.29 MeV with SkM*_ext1 and 0.86 MeV with UNEDF0_ext1
- S2n of 44Mg is -0.05 MeV with SkM*_ext1 and 0.49 MeV with UNEDF0_ext1
- S2n of 45Al is 2.0 MeV with SkM*_ext1 and 2.4 MeV with UNEDF0_ext1
- S2n of 47Al is -0.01 MeV with SkM*_ext1 and 0.99 MeV with UNEDF0_ext1
- S2n of 40Mg is 1.88 MeV with SkM*_ext1 and 1.95 MeV with UNEDF0_ext1
- 26O is slightly bound by 270 keV and 31F is slightly unbound by 220 keV with SkM*_ext1
- 39Na has a prolate quadrupole deformation beta2 = 0.35 with SkM*_ext1
- 32Mg has a prolate ground state (beta2 = 0.39) with prolate energy 0.29 MeV lower than spherical with SkM*_ext1

## Context

This work extends the experimental observation of 39Na by Ahn et al. (2019) at RIKEN, which provided the first critical constraint on theoretical predictions of the neutron drip-line in this region. The authors compare their results with prior calculations including the Weizsacker-Skyrme formula (WS4), Gogny-HFB with D1S force, the HFB-21 mass table, Relativistic-Hartree-Bogoliubov with PC-PK1 functional, and macro-microscopic model calculations by Moller et al. The results are also consistent with recent ab initio calculations by Holt et al. that give 78% probability for 42Mg to be bound and 91% for 45Al. The study enables experimental searches at FRIB and other rare-isotope beam facilities for the predicted weakly-bound nuclei 42Mg, 45Al, and possibly 44Mg and 47Al.
