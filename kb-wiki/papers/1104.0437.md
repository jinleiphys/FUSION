# Spin- and isospin-polarized states of nuclear matter in the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock model

> Spin-polarized isospin asymmetric nuclear matter is studied within the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach. After a brief review of the formalism, we present and discuss the self-consistent single-particle potentials at various levels of spin and isospin asymmetry. We then move to predictions of the energy per particle, also under different conditions of isospin and spin polarization. Comparison with the energy per particle in isospin symmetric or asymmetric unpolarized nuclear matter shows no evidence for a phase transition to a spin ordered state, neither ferromagnetic nor antiferromagnetic.

# Spin- and isospin-polarized states of nuclear matter in the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock model

## Key claim

The Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) model with the Bonn B potential predicts no phase transition to a spin-ordered state (neither ferromagnetic nor antiferromagnetic) in either symmetric or asymmetric nuclear matter, up to densities of about 0.4 fm$^{-3}$. This conclusion is conditional on the use of a microscopic approach where all meson-nucleon couplings are constrained by free-space nucleon-nucleon scattering data, in contrast to non-microscopic models that do predict such transitions.

## Method

The authors extended their previous DBHF calculations for spin-polarized pure neutron matter and symmetric matter to the general case of spin-polarized isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter (SPANM). They solved the coupled self-consistency problem for the single-particle potentials in each of the four nucleon species channels (neutron up/down, proton up/down) together with the two-nucleon G-matrix, using the Bonn B one-boson-exchange potential. The calculation included partial waves up to total angular momentum J=6 and employed an angle-averaged Pauli operator for scattering between particles with different Fermi momenta.

## Key numbers

* Total density range explored: up to approximately 0.4 fm$^{-3}$
* Average Fermi momentum used for fixed-momentum calculations: 1.4 fm$^{-1}$
* Density for single-particle potential calculations: 0.185 fm$^{-3}$
* Isospin asymmetry parameter values used: $\alpha$=0 (symmetric) and $\alpha$=0.5 (asymmetric)
* Spin polarization parameters: $\beta_n$=0.6 for neutron polarization in some calculations; fully polarized cases use $\beta_n$=1, $\beta_p$=0, $\beta_n$=1, $\beta_p$=1 (ferromagnetic), and $\beta_n$=1, $\beta_p$=-1 (antiferromagnetic)

## Context

This work extends previous DBHF calculations by Sammarruca (2010) for spin-polarized symmetric nuclear matter and by Sammarruca and Krastev (2007) for spin-polarized pure neutron matter, now incorporating both spin and isospin asymmetries simultaneously. The results contradict predictions from non-microscopic approaches such as Skyrme-force calculations (Isayev and Yang, 2004) which find a phase transition to an antiferromagnetic state at 2-3 times normal density, and relativistic Hartree-Fock models with effective Lagrangians (Marcos et al., 1991) that predict ferromagnetic transitions. The findings are qualitatively consistent with other microscopic calculations using conventional Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory (Vidana and Bombaci, 2002). The work enables future construction of a parametrized equation of state dependent on density, isospin asymmetry, and neutron/proton spin polarizations for applications in neutron star physics and nuclear structure.
