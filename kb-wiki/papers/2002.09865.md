# Bryan's Maximum Entropy Method -- diagnosis of a flawed argument and its remedy

> The Maximum Entropy Method (MEM) is a popular data analysis technique based on Bayesian inference, which has found various applications in the research literature. While the MEM itself is well-grounded in statistics, I argue that its state-of-the-art implementation, suggested originally by Bryan, artificially restricts its solution space. This restriction leads to a systematic error often unaccounted for in contemporary MEM studies. The goal of this paper is to carefully revisit Bryan's train of thought, point out its flaw in applying linear algebra arguments to an inherently nonlinear problem, and suggest possible ways to overcome it.

## Key claim

The Maximum Entropy Method (MEM) itself is statistically well-grounded, but Bryan's state-of-the-art implementation artificially restricts the solution space to the singular value decomposition (SVD) subspace, introducing a systematic error because linear algebra arguments are incorrectly applied to an inherently nonlinear optimization problem.

## Method

The paper revisits Bryan's derivation of the SVD subspace restriction for the MEM, contrasting it with the linear Tikhonov regularization case where the SVD argument is valid. It demonstrates analytically that exponentiating SVD basis functions componentwise does not project them into the null-space of the kernel, unlike in the linear case. A numerical example is constructed using a kernel of the form \(K(\tau,\omega)=1/(\omega^2 +\tau^2)\) with three datapoints and a delta-peak mock spectrum to show that the global extremum of the posterior lies outside the SVD subspace.

## Key numbers

* For a mock spectrum with a delta peak at \(\omega_0^{(1)}=25\), the minimum likelihood achievable within Bryan's SVD subspace is \({\cal L}_{\rm min}^1\approx 10^6 \kappa^{-2}\), where \(\kappa\) is the constant relative error \(\Delta D/D\).
* For a delta peak at \(\omega_0^{(2)}=125\), the minimum likelihood within the SVD subspace is \({\cal L}_{\rm min}^2\approx 10^9 \kappa^{-2}\).
* For a delta peak at \(\omega_0^{(3)}=250\), the minimum likelihood within the SVD subspace is \({\cal L}_{\rm min}^3 \approx 10^{10}\kappa^{-2}\).
* The Shannon-Jaynes entropy regulator value for Bryan's best fit within the SVD subspace is \({\cal S}\approx -0.24\) for all three mock functions, while a parameter set in the full search space yields \({\cal S}\approx -200\).

## Context

This work extends the critique of Bryan's MEM first raised by Rothkopf (2011) and addresses claims made by Asakawa (2020) that the SVD subspace is sufficient. The paper identifies a flaw in applying linear algebra arguments to the nonlinear Shannon-Jaynes entropy regulator, which has been used in MEM studies since Bryan (1990). The diagnosis enables practitioners to either extend the MEM search space using additional basis functions or adopt alternative Bayesian approaches such as the Bayesian Reconstruction (BR) method of Burnier (2013), stochastic analytic continuation (Sandvik, 1998), or neural network based methods.
