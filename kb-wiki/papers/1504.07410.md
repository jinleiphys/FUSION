# Vibrational and rotational excited states within Bohr Hamiltonian with deformation-dependent mass formalism

> In a recent work (Phys.Rev.C84, 044321, 2011) M.J. Ermamatov and P.R. Fraser have studied rotational and vibrational excited states of axially symmetric nuclei within the Bohr Hamiltonian with different mass parameters. However, the energy formula that the authors have used contains some inaccuracies. So, the numerical results they obtained seem to be controversial. In this paper, we revisit all calculations related to this problem and determine the appropriate formula for the energy spectrum. Moreover, in order to improve such calculations, we reconsider this problem within the framework of the deformation dependent mass formalism. Also, unlike the work of Bonatsos et al. (Phys.Rev.C83, 044321, 2011) where the mass parameter has not been considered, we will show the importance of this parameter and its effect on numerical predictions.

## Key claim

The authors revisit the work of Ermamatov and Fraser (2011) on rotational and vibrational excited states of axially symmetric nuclei within the Bohr Hamiltonian, claiming that the earlier energy formula contained inaccuracies that made the numerical results controversial. They derive a corrected energy formula and extend the calculations using deformation-dependent mass formalism (DDMF), showing that the mass parameter has a significant effect on numerical predictions, unlike in the work of Bonatsos et al. (2011) where it was not considered.

## Method

The authors start from the Bohr Hamiltonian with three different mass coefficients (for ground state, β-band, and γ-band) as introduced by Jolos et al., then apply the deformation-dependent mass formalism where the mass depends on the deformation coordinate β through a function f(β)=1+aβ². They use the Davidson potential for the β-part and a harmonic oscillator potential for the γ-part, separate the variables for axially symmetric prolate deformed nuclei, and solve the resulting equations using the asymptotic iteration method (AIM) to derive analytical expressions for energy levels and wave functions.

## Context

This work corrects and extends the calculations of Ermamatov and Fraser (2011) on rotational and vibrational spectra of axially symmetric nuclei, who used an inaccurate energy formula. The authors also go beyond the work of Bonatsos et al. (2011) by explicitly including the mass parameter dependence, which was hidden in that earlier work. The formalism enables improved calculations of energy spectra and B(E2) transition probabilities for deformed nuclei such as ¹⁵⁴Sm, ¹⁵⁶Gd, ¹⁷²Yb, and ¹⁸²W, with the free parameters determined from experimental data by solving a system of three nonlinear algebraic equations.
