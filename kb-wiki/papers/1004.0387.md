# Toward Large N Thermal QCD from Dual Gravity: The Heavy Quarkonium Potential

> We continue our study on the gravity duals for strongly coupled large N QCD with fundamental flavors both at zero and non-zero temperatures. The gravity dual at zero temperature captures the logarithmic runnings of the coupling constants at far IR and the almost conformal, albeit strongly coupled, behavior at the UV. The full UV completion of gauge theory is accomplished in the gravity side by attaching an AdS cap to the IR geometry described in our previous work. Attaching such an AdS cap is highly non-trivial because it amounts to finding the right interpolating geometry and sources that take us from a gravity solution with non-zero three-form fluxes to another one that has almost vanishing three-form fluxes. In this paper we give a concrete realisation of such a scenario, completing the program advocated in our earlier paper. One of the main advantage of having such a background, in addition to providing a dual description of the required gauge theory, is the absence of Landau poles and consequently the UV divergences of the Wilson loops. The potential for the heaviest fundamental quark anti-quark pairs, which are like the heavy quarkonium states in realistic QCD, can be computed and their linear behavior at large separations and zero temperature could be demonstrated. At small separations the expected Coulombic behavior appears to dominate. On the other hand, at non-zero temperatures interesting properties like heavy quarkonium type suppressions and melting are shown to emerge from our gravity dual. We provide some discussions of the melting temperature and compare our results with the Charmonium spectrum and lattice simulations. We argue that, in spite of the large N nature of our construction, certain model-independent predictions can be made.

## Key claim
The authors construct a gravity dual for large N thermal QCD that exhibits confinement at large quark-antiquark separations and Coulombic behavior at small separations at zero temperature, while at non-zero temperature it shows heavy quarkonium suppression and melting. The construction claims to be free of Landau poles and UV divergences in Wilson loops, though the authors acknowledge this is a large N approximation and argue that certain model-independent predictions can still be made despite this limitation.

## Method
The authors build a supergravity background on a resolved warped deformed conifold with seven branes and fluxes, attaching an AdS cap to the IR geometry from their previous work (FEP, 2009) to achieve UV completion. They compute the heavy quarkonium potential from the Nambu-Goto action at both zero and finite temperature, using analytic methods supplemented by numerical analysis for regimes inaccessible analytically. The geometry is divided into three regions: far IR, an interpolating region, and far UV, with the UV cap constructed by transitioning from a geometry with non-zero three-form fluxes to one with almost vanishing three-form fluxes.

## Key numbers
- The warp factor at large r scales as h = sum_alpha L^4_(alpha) / r^4_(alpha), where r_(alpha) = r^(1 + epsilon_(alpha)/4) and epsilon_(alpha) is a small positive number depending on g_s N_f, g_s M, and g_s N.
- The beta function for the gauge coupling is given by (partial / partial log Lambda) (8 pi^2 / g^2_YM) = 3N - 2N(1 - gamma_(A,B)) - N_f (1 - gamma_q), where Lambda is the energy scale related to the radial coordinate r.
- The resolution factor in the resolved warped deformed conifold is directly related to the temperature, as a consistent solution with a black hole requires a non-zero resolution factor for the two-cycle.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous construction (FEP, 2009) of a gravity dual for strongly coupled large N QCD with fundamental flavors, which itself built on the Klebanov-Strassler (KS) geometry and Ouyang-Klebanov-Strassler-Black-Hole (OKS-BH) geometry. The paper aims to bring string theory investigations of the quark-gluon plasma closer to traditional QCD studies, particularly lattice calculations of the quark-antiquark potential as a function of temperature (lat_T, various authors) and the phenomenology of heavy quarkonium suppression first suggested by Matsui and Satz (1986). The construction enables computation of heavy quarkonium melting temperatures that can be compared with charmonium spectrum data and lattice simulations.
