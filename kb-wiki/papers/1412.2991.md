# Do $н·н·$ bound states exist?

> The existence of baryon-baryon bound states in the strangeness sector is examined in the framework of SU(3) chiral effective field theory. Specifically, the role of SU(3) symmetry breaking contact terms that arise at next-to-leading order in the employed Weinberg power counting scheme is explored. We focus on the 1S0 partial wave and on baryon-baryon channels with maximal isospin since in this case there are only two independent SU(3) symmetry breaking contact terms. At the same time, those are the channels where most of the bound states have been predicted in the past. Utilizing $pp$ phase shifts and $нё^+ p$ cross section data allows us to pin down one of the SU(3) symmetry breaking contact terms and a clear indication for the decrease of attraction when going from the NN system to strangeness S=-2 is found, which rules out a bound state for $нёнё$ with isospin I=2. Assuming that the trend observed for S=0 to S=-2 is not reversed when going to $н·нё$ and $н·н·$ makes also bound states in those systems rather unlikely.

## Key claim

The authors use SU(3) chiral effective field theory to show that SU(3) symmetry breaking contact terms, constrained by fitting to both $pp$ phase shifts and $\Sigma^+ p$ cross section data, produce a clear decrease in attraction from strangeness $S=0$ to $S=-2$, ruling out a $\Sigma\Sigma$ bound state with isospin $I=2$ and making $\Xi\Sigma$ and $\Xi\Xi$ bound states unlikely provided the same trend continues.

## Method

The authors constructed baryon-baryon potentials up to next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory using the Weinberg power counting scheme, including SU(3) symmetry breaking contact terms proportional to $(m_K^2 - m_\pi^2)$. They performed combined fits to $pp$ $^1S_0$ phase shifts from the GWU analysis and to $\Sigma^+ p$ $^1S_0$ phase shifts derived from their own chiral EFT $YN$ interaction, solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a regulator cutoff $\Lambda$ varied between 500 and 650 MeV to estimate theoretical uncertainty.

## Key numbers

- The $\Sigma\Sigma$ ($I=2$) $^1S_0$ scattering lengths are predicted to be around $-3.2$ to $-3.4$ fm, ruling out a bound state.
- For $\Xi\Sigma$ ($I=3/2$) with $C^\chi_2 \approx -C^\chi_1/2$, scattering lengths range from $-1.3$ to $-1.8$ fm; for $\Xi\Xi$ ($I=1$) with the same choice, scattering lengths range from $-1.6$ to $-1.8$ fm.
- The reduced mass ratio $\mu_{\Xi\Xi}/\mu_{NN} \approx 1.40$ would increase effective interaction strength by 40% if SU(3) symmetry held, but the fitted SU(3) breaking LEC $C^\chi_1$ compensates this effect.
- Lattice QCD results from NPLQCD give a $\Xi\Xi$ binding energy of $-14.0 \pm 1.4 \pm 6.7$ MeV at $m_\pi = 389$ MeV, while HAL QCD finds only moderate attraction with phase shifts reaching about $20 \pm 10$ degrees at $m_\pi = 510$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous chiral EFT studies of baryon-baryon interactions at leading order (Haidenbauer 2010) and next-to-leading order (Haidenbauer 2013) by explicitly including SU(3) symmetry breaking contact terms. It contradicts predictions of bound states in $\Xi\Sigma$ and $\Xi\Xi$ channels from the Nijmegen NSC97 meson-exchange model (Stoks 1999, Rijken 2013), while agreeing qualitatively with the quark model of Fujiwara (fss2) which found no dibaryon bound states. The results enable future experimental searches via $\Xi\Sigma$ and $\Xi\Xi$ correlation measurements in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC or CERN, similar to recent $\Lambda\Lambda$ correlation studies (Shah 2014).
