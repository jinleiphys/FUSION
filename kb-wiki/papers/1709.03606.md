# Quasiparticle-vibration coupling effects on nuclear transitions of astrophysical interest

> The relativistic quasiparticle time-blocking approximation (RQTBA) is applied to the description of nuclear excitation modes of astrophysical interest. This method is based on the meson-nucleon Lagrangian and goes beyond the standard relativistic quasiparticle random-phase approximation (RQRPA) by treating the coupling between single quasiparticles and collective vibrations of the nucleus. We calculate electric dipole transitions and Gamow-Teller modes in the (p,n) direction in a few Sn isotopes and obtain the rates of (n,$γ$) reaction and $β^-$-decay processes, which govern the r-process nucleosynthesis, in a unified RQTBA framework. Gamow-Teller transitions in the (n,p) branch, which in principle can serve for the modeling of stellar evolution, are also investigated, and $^{90}$Zr is taken as a study case.

## Key claim

The relativistic quasiparticle time-blocking approximation (RQTBA), which includes quasiparticle-vibration coupling beyond the standard relativistic quasiparticle random-phase approximation (RQRPA), provides a unified microscopic framework for calculating both neutron-capture and beta-decay rates relevant to r-process nucleosynthesis, but the description of Gamow-Teller transitions in the (n,p) branch remains incomplete due to missing ground-state correlations.

## Method

The authors start from a covariant density functional theory description using the NL3 meson-nucleon Lagrangian and Hartree+BCS pairing, then compute collective phonon spectra within RQRPA. They solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the response function in both like-particle and charge-exchange channels, including a dynamical quasiparticle-vibration coupling term that introduces 2p-2h (or 4 quasiparticle) configurations. Electric dipole photo-absorption cross sections are converted to neutron-capture cross sections via the Hauser-Feshbach statistical model with the Brink-Axel hypothesis, while beta-decay half-lives are computed from Gamow-Teller strength distributions in the allowed approximation.

## Key numbers

- In $^{132}$Sn, the neutron-capture rates calculated within RQTBA are larger by a factor of approximately 5 compared to standard Lorentzian models.
- The smearing parameter used for strength distributions is $\Delta = 200$ keV for E1 and GT$^-$ strength functions, and $\Delta = 20$ keV for beta-decay half-life calculations.
- For $^{90}$Zr GT$^+$ and GT$^-$ calculations, the strength was folded with $\Delta = 750$ keV and $\Delta = 1$ MeV respectively to match experimental resolution.
- The bare weak axial coupling constant $g_A \simeq 1.27$ was used for beta-decay half-life calculations.
- The pn-RQRPA systematically overestimates the half-lives of Sn isotopes by at least an order of magnitude compared to experiment.

## Context

This work extends the RQTBA formalism developed by Litvinova (2008) and Robin (2016) to simultaneously describe both neutron-capture and beta-decay processes needed for r-process nucleosynthesis simulations. The results contradict standard Lorentzian parametrizations of the gamma strength function (Thielemann and Arnould, 1983; RIPL-2) by showing that microscopic effects, particularly the pygmy dipole resonance near the neutron threshold, significantly enhance neutron-capture rates in neutron-rich nuclei. The approach enables future calculations of electron-capture rates for stellar evolution modeling, but the authors note that additional ground-state correlations induced by quasiparticle-vibration coupling (Robin, 2017) are needed to improve the description of GT$^+$ transitions.
