# The electric dipole moment of the neutron in chiral perturbation theory

> We calculate the electric dipole moments of the neutron and the Lambda within the framework of heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. They are induced by strong CP-violating terms of the effective Lagrangian in the presence of the vacuum angle theta_0. The construction of such a Lagrangian is outlined and we are able to give an estimate for theta_0.

## Key claim
The electric dipole moment of the neutron (and the Lambda) is calculated in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, induced by the strong CP-violating vacuum angle theta_0. The abstract's claim of being "able to give an estimate for theta_0" is hedged by the fact that the tree-level contribution depends on unknown counterterms (w_13 and w_13^r), whose values are estimated only via large-N_c arguments, leading to a theoretical uncertainty of about ±40% on the final theta_0 bound.

## Method
The author constructs the most general effective Lagrangian for mesons and baryons up to second chiral order in the presence of the theta_0 term, including explicit CP-violating terms in the baryon sector that were omitted in prior work (Pich and de Rafael, 1994). The calculation is performed in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, which provides a consistent chiral counting scheme, and the electric dipole moments of the neutron and Lambda are evaluated at one-loop order (O(p^2)). The tree-level contributions from unknown counterterms are bounded using large-N_c arguments, comparing them to the known magnetic-moment coupling w_16.

## Key numbers
- Experimental upper limit on the neutron electric dipole moment: |d_n^gamma| < 6.3 × 10^{-26} e cm (from Harris et al., 1999).
- Loop contribution to the neutron electric dipole moment: d_n^{gamma (loop)} = -7.5 × 10^{-16} theta_0 e cm.
- Loop contribution to the Lambda electric dipole moment: d_Lambda^{gamma (loop)} = -1.7 × 10^{-16} theta_0 e cm.
- Estimated tree-level contribution to the neutron electric dipole moment (using |w_13^{(r)}/w_16| ≈ 1/3): |d_n^{gamma (tree)}| ≈ 3.2 × 10^{-16} theta_0 e cm.
- Estimated tree-level contribution to the Lambda electric dipole moment: |d_Lambda^{gamma (tree)}| ≈ 1.6 × 10^{-16} theta_0 e cm.
- Combined result for the neutron: d_n^gamma = (-7.5 ± 3.2) × 10^{-16} theta_0 e cm.
- Combined result for the Lambda: d_Lambda^gamma = (-1.7 ± 1.6) × 10^{-16} theta_0 e cm.
- Upper bound on the vacuum angle from the neutron: |theta_0| < 8.4 × 10^{-11}.
- Upper bound on the vacuum angle from the Lambda: |theta_0| < 0.9 (using d_Lambda^gamma < 1.5 × 10^{-16} e cm).
- Parameters used: b_D = 0.079 GeV^{-1}, b_F = -0.316 GeV^{-1}, D = 0.80, F = 0.46, F_pi ≈ 92.4 MeV, m_eta0 ≈ m_eta' = 958 MeV, V_0^{(2)} ≈ -5.0 × 10^{-4} GeV^4, V_3^{(1)} ≈ 3.5 × 10^{-4} GeV^2.

## Context
This work extends the earlier estimate of the vacuum angle by Pich and de Rafael (1994), who used a relativistic chiral Lagrangian approach without a systematic chiral counting scheme and omitted explicit CP-violating terms in the baryon Lagrangian. The present calculation uses heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (Jenkins and Manohar, 1991) to achieve consistent power counting, and includes the full set of CP-violating baryon terms constructed by Borasoy (1999). The result for the neutron (|theta_0| < 8.4 × 10^{-11}) is comparable to the Pich and de Rafael bound of |theta_0| ≤ 5 × 10^{-10}, but with a more rigorous treatment of the chiral expansion and an estimate of the theoretical uncertainty from unknown counterterms. The Lambda dipole moment is found to be much less constraining due to the absence of pion-loop dominance and larger relative uncertainty from tree-level contributions.
