# Three-Body Interactions Among Nucleons and Pions

> A chiral invariant effective Lagrangian may be used to calculate the three-body interactions among low-energy pions and nucleons in terms of known parameters. This method is illustrated by the calculation of the pion-nucleus scattering length.

## Key claim

The three-body interactions among low-energy pions and nucleons can be calculated from first principles using a chiral invariant effective Lagrangian with no unknown parameters, but only when the two-body interactions are taken from phenomenological models rather than from the effective Lagrangian itself, because the two-body corrections contain too many free parameters to be predictive.

## Method

The author develops a systematic power-counting scheme based on the index Δ_i for each interaction vertex, where chiral invariance rules out terms with Δ_i < 0. Irreducible graphs (those without anomalously small energy denominators from nucleon kinetic energies) are classified by the number ν of powers of small momenta or pion masses, given by ν = 4 − N_n − 2C + 2L + Σ_i V_i Δ_i. The leading contributions come from tree graphs with Δ_i = 0 vertices, while corrections of second order in small momenta include three-body tree graphs with C = N_n + N_π − 2 connected parts, which are calculated from the known Δ_i = 0 Hamiltonian. The method is applied to compute the pion-nucleus scattering length, with explicit formulas given for the three-body contributions to pion-deuteron scattering.

## Key numbers

- Axial coupling constant: g_A ≃ 1.25
- Pion decay amplitude: F_π ≃ 190 MeV
- Two-body term in π-d scattering length: (1 + m_π/m_N)/(1 + m_π/m_d)[a_πp + a_πn] = −(0.021 ± 0.006) m_π^{−1}
- Rescattering term a^{[4(a)]} = −(0.026 ± 0.001) m_π^{−1}
- Remaining three-body term a^{[4(b,c)]} = −0.0005 m_π^{−1}
- Final theoretical π-d scattering length: a_πd = −(0.050 ± 0.006) m_π^{−1}
- Experimental π-d scattering length: −(0.056 ± 0.009) m_π^{−1}

## Context

This work extends the systematic effective Lagrangian framework previously developed by Weinberg (1990, 1991) for reactions involving nucleons and low-energy pions. It demonstrates that three-body forces can be calculated from first principles using chiral perturbation theory, while two-body interactions must be taken from phenomenological models. The calculation of the pion-deuteron scattering length confirms earlier results by Robilotta and Wilkin (1978) and those in the textbooks of Eisenberg and Koltun and Ericson and Weise, but provides a systematic justification for why only certain graphs contribute. This enables future calculations of pion scattering on heavier nuclei where three-body terms may be more significant.
