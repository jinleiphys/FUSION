# The $Ω(2380)$ as a partner of the $Ω(2012)$

> We present a study of the $Ω(2380)$ resonance and show that it is consistent with a dynamically generated state arising from the $\bar{K}^*Ξ^*$, $ωΩ$, and $ϕΩ$ interactions. In this picture, the $Ω(2380)$ is analogous to the $Ω(2012)$, which is generated from the $\bar{K}Ξ^*$ and $ηΩ$ channels. The resulting mass, total width, and partial decay widths into the $\bar{K}Ξ^*$ and $\bar{K}^*Ξ$ channels are compatible with the available experimental data. We also discuss possible experimental observables that could provide further insight into the nature of this state.

## Key claim
The $\Omega(2380)$ resonance can be described as a dynamically generated hadronic molecule from the $\bar{K}^*\Xi^*$, $\omega\Omega$, and $\phi\Omega$ interactions, analogous to the molecular interpretation of the $\Omega(2012)$. The authors show that this picture is consistent with experimental data only after including the $\bar{K}\Xi^*$ and $\bar{K}^*\Xi$ decay channels via box diagrams, which provide the dominant contribution to the resonance width.

## Method
The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation in coupled channels for vector meson-decuplet baryon interactions using potentials derived from the local hidden gauge approach, with the $\bar{K}^*\Xi^*$, $\omega\Omega$, and $\phi\Omega$ channels in the $S=-3$, $I=0$ sector. To account for the experimentally observed two-body decays of the $\Omega(2380)$, they evaluate the imaginary parts of box diagrams corresponding to $\bar{K}^*\Xi^* \to \bar{K}\Xi^*$ and $\bar{K}^*\Xi^* \to \bar{K}^*\Xi$ transitions, using $VPP$ and $VVP$ Lagrangians together with quark-model-based $\pi\Xi^*\Xi^*$ and $\pi\Xi\Xi^*$ vertices. The loop function for the $\bar{K}^*\Xi^*$ channel includes a double convolution over the mass distributions of the $\bar{K}^*$ and $\Xi^*$ resonances.

## Key numbers
- The $\Omega(2380)$ mass is predicted near 2380 MeV, compatible with the PDG value, with a total width consistent with experimental data.
- The coupling constant $g = m_V/(2f_\pi)$ uses $m_V = 800$ MeV and $f_\pi = 93$ MeV.
- The form factor used in the box diagrams takes $\Lambda \simeq 1$ GeV, tuned from previous works (Geng 2008, Molina 2008, Molina 2020, Ikeno 2021).
- The anomalous $VVP$ coupling uses $G' \approx 14$ GeV$^{-1}$, derived from $G_v = 55$ MeV.

## Context
This work extends the molecular approach previously applied to the $\Omega(2012)$ by Pavao (2018) and others, where the $\Omega(2012)$ is generated from $\bar{K}\Xi^*$ and $\eta\Omega$ channels. It addresses a persistent difficulty in coupled-channel approaches including vector mesons and decuplet baryons, where earlier work by Gamermann (2011) predicted a bound state near 1930 MeV for the $\Omega(2380)$, far below the experimental mass. The present study also revisits the vector meson-decuplet baryon interaction of Sarkar (2010), who associated a pole near 2449 MeV with the $\Omega(2470)$. The results enable future experimental searches for the predicted decay patterns and could help distinguish the molecular interpretation from conventional quark-model descriptions.
