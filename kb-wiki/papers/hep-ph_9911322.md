# $x_F$ Dependence of Drell-Yan Transverse Moemtum Broadening

> We analyze $x_F$ dependence of Drell-Yan transverse momentum broadening in hadron-nucleus collisions. In terms of generalized factorization theorem, we show that the $x_F$ dependence of transverse momentum broadening, $ Δ< q_T^2 > (x_F) $, can be calculated in perturbative QCD. We demonstrate that $Δ< q_T^2 > (x_F)$ is a good observable for studying the effects of initial-state multiple scattering and extracting quark-gluon correlation functions.

## Key claim

The paper derives the $x_F$ dependence of Drell-Yan transverse momentum broadening $\Delta \langle q_T^2 \rangle (x_F)$ in perturbative QCD using a generalized factorization theorem, but the derivation is limited to leading order in $\alpha_s$ and to double scattering (the first non-leading power in $1/Q^2$), with all higher-power and non-$A^{1/3}$-enhanced corrections neglected.

## Method

The authors use the generalized factorization theorem of Qiu and Sterman (1991) to expand the Drell-Yan cross section in powers of $1/Q^2$, keeping only single and double scattering contributions. They define the nuclear broadening as the difference between the average transverse momentum squared on a nuclear target and on a nucleon target, $\Delta \langle q_T^2 \rangle \equiv \langle q_T^2 \rangle^{hA} - \langle q_T^2 \rangle^{hN}$. At leading order in $\alpha_s$, they express the $x_F$-differential broadening in terms of the four-parton correlation function $T_{q/A}^{(I)}(x)$ and the beam hadron's antiquark distribution, then evaluate it numerically using the LQS model (Luo, Qiu, and Sterman, 1994) for the correlation function, CTEQ4L parton distributions, and EKS98 nuclear parton distributions.

## Key numbers

* For $A=184$, $Q=5$ GeV, and 800 GeV beam energy, the integrated $\Delta \langle q_T^2 \rangle$ values for three $x_F$ ranges are: 0.10 GeV$^2$ (small $x_F$: $-0.1 \leq x_F \leq 0.3$), 0.043 GeV$^2$ (intermediate $x_F$: $0.2 \leq x_F \leq 0.6$), and 0.026 GeV$^2$ (large $x_F$: $0.3 \leq x_F \leq 0.93$).
* For $A=184$, $Q=11$ GeV, and 800 GeV beam energy, the corresponding $\Delta \langle q_T^2 \rangle$ values are: 0.078 GeV$^2$, 0.043 GeV$^2$, and 0.027 GeV$^2$ for the same three $x_F$ ranges.
* The LQS model parameter is taken as $\lambda^2 = 0.01$ GeV$^2$, estimated from NA10 and E772 Drell-Yan data (Guo, 1998).
* The nucleon target average transverse momentum squared is taken as $\langle q_T^2 \rangle^{hN} = 1.2$ GeV$^2$ (McGaughey et al., 1994).
* The effective nuclear parton distribution power $\alpha$ is found to be $\alpha \sim \pm (0.02-0.03)$ for the relevant $x$-range, much smaller than $1/3$.

## Context

This work extends the generalized factorization approach of Qiu and Sterman (1991) and the four-parton correlation function formalism of Luo, Qiu, and Sterman (1994) to the $x_F$ dependence of Drell-Yan transverse momentum broadening. It builds on earlier calculations of nuclear dependence in the Drell-Yan transverse momentum spectrum by Guo (1998) and Fries et al. (1999). The paper enables direct extraction of quark-gluon correlation functions from experimental data and provides a connection to parton energy loss via the BDMPS relation (Baier et al., 1997, 1998), offering an alternative to cross-section-ratio methods that are complicated by nuclear shadowing effects (Vasiliev et al., 1999).
