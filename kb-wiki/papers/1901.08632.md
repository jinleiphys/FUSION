# Dynamical systems and nonlinear transient rheology of the far-from-equilibrium Bjorken flow

> In relativistic kinetic theory, the one-particle distribution function is approximated by an asymptotic perturbative power series in Knudsen number which is divergent. For the Bjorken flow, we expand the distribution function in terms of its moments and study their nonlinear evolution equations. The resulting coupled dynamical system can be solved for each moment consistently using a multi-parameter transseries which makes the constitutive relations inherit the same structure. A new non-perturbative dynamical renormalization scheme is born out of this formalism that goes beyond the linear response theory. We show that there is a Lyapunov function, aka dynamical potential, which is, in general, a function of the moments and time satisfying Lyapunov stability conditions along RG flows connected to the asymptotic hydrodynamic fixed point. As a result, the transport coefficients get dynamically renormalized at every order in the time-dependent perturbative expansion by receiving non-perturbative corrections present in the transseries. The connection between the integration constants and the UV data is discussed using the language of dynamical systems. Furthermore, we show that the first dissipative correction in the Knudsen number to the distribution function is not only determined by the known effective shear viscous term but also a new high energy non-hydrodynamic mode. It is demonstrated that the survival of this new mode is intrinsically related to the nonlinear mode-to-mode coupling with the shear viscous term. Finally, we comment on some possible phenomenological applications of the proposed non-hydrodynamic transport theory.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the first dissipative correction to the distribution function in Bjorken flow is not solely determined by the known effective shear viscous term but also includes a new high-energy non-hydrodynamic mode, whose survival depends on nonlinear mode-to-mode coupling with the shear viscous term.

## Method

The authors expand the one-particle distribution function in terms of its moments using generalized Laguerre and Legendre polynomials for a weakly coupled relativistic system undergoing Bjorken expansion within the relaxation time approximation (RTA-BE). They study the nonlinear evolution equations of these moments as a coupled dynamical system and solve them using a multi-parameter transseries approach, which incorporates non-perturbative exponential corrections. The dynamical system is analyzed by diagonalizing the linearization matrix around the asymptotic hydrodynamic fixed point to obtain Lyapunov exponents and anomalous dimensions.

## Key numbers

- The physical range for the moment \(c_{01}\) is \(-2.5 \le c_{01} \le 5\), derived from nonnegativity of pressure components \(P_T, P_L \ge 0\)
- The first perturbative asymptotic term of the new high-energy non-hydrodynamic mode decays as \(\sim (\tau T)^{-1}\), the same decay rate as the Navier-Stokes shear viscous tensor component
- The Lyapunov exponent (IR data) is \(S_i = 3/(2\theta_0)\) and the anomalous dimension is \(\tilde{b}_i = -(b_i - 1/5)\), where \(\theta_0\) is the relaxation time parameter and \(b_i\) are eigenvalues of the matrix \(\mathfrak{B}\)
- The leading-order coefficient for the moment \(c_{01}\) in the asymptotic expansion is \(u^{(0)}_{11} = -8\theta_0/3\)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous findings (Behtash, 2018) on nonlinear dynamics of far-from-equilibrium weakly coupled plasmas undergoing Bjorken expansion, where they first treated moment evolution equations as a coupled nonlinear dynamical system solved by multi-parameter transseries. The paper builds on the observation that gradient expansions of the energy-momentum tensor are divergent (Heller, 2015), necessitating non-hydrodynamic modes, and generalizes the analysis to include higher non-hydrodynamic modes beyond the low-energy sector. The dynamical systems approach provides an alternative to Borel resummation techniques, which lose UV information, and enables full control over both early-time (UV) and late-time (IR) behavior of flow lines.
