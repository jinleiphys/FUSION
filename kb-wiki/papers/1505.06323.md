# Few-Nucleon Systems in a Quirky World: Lattice Nuclei in Effective Field Theory

> I describe how nuclear structure can be predicted from lattice QCD through low-energy effective field theories, using as an example a world simulation with relatively heavy up and down quarks.

## Key claim

The paper argues that light nuclei (A ≤ 6) in a lattice QCD world with relatively heavy up and down quarks (pion mass 805 MeV) can be described by Pionless Effective Field Theory, and that these nuclei appear to be weakly bound systems whose properties scale roughly uniformly with pion mass, suggesting nuclear saturation may not be tremendously sensitive to the pion mass.

## Method

The author uses Pionless (Contact) Effective Field Theory, where all degrees of freedom except nucleons are integrated out, to describe lattice QCD data for few-nucleon systems at unphysical pion masses. The two-nucleon low-energy constants are fitted to the deuteron and dineutron binding energies from NPLQCD at mπ = 805 MeV, and the three-nucleon constant is fitted to the triton binding energy. The Schrödinger equation is then solved for A = 4, 5, and 6 using the effective-interaction hyperspherical harmonics (EIHH) method and the auxiliary-field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) method, with cutoff variation between 2 and 14 fm⁻¹ to test renormalization group invariance.

## Key numbers

- At mπ = 805 MeV, the deuteron binding energy is 19.5 ± 4.8 MeV (from NPLQCD lattice data)
- At mπ = 805 MeV, the dineutron binding energy is 15.9 ± 3.8 MeV (from NPLQCD lattice data)
- At mπ = 805 MeV, the triton binding energy is 53.9 ± 10.7 MeV (from NPLQCD lattice data)
- The LO Pionless EFT prediction for ⁴He at mπ = 805 MeV is 89 ± 36 MeV, consistent with the LQCD value of 107.0 ± 24.2 MeV
- The predicted ⁵He binding energy at mπ = 805 MeV is 98 ± 39 MeV (for Λ = 2 fm⁻¹)
- The predicted ⁶Li binding energy at mπ = 805 MeV is 122 ± 50 MeV (for Λ = 2 fm⁻¹)
- The estimated expansion parameter Q/mπ at mπ = 805 MeV is 40%
- The scattering length a₂(1,0) from Pionless EFT at mπ = 805 MeV is (1.2 ± 0.5) fm with regulator f₁, compared to LQCD value 1.82⁺⁰·¹⁴⁺⁰·¹⁷₋₀·₁₃₋₀·₁₂ fm

## Context

This work extends the application of Pionless EFT, previously used for physical light nuclei (van Kolck, 1999; Bedaque et al., 2000; Platter et al., 2004), to lattice QCD data at unphysical pion masses from the NPLQCD collaboration (2013) and Yamazaki et al. (2012, 2015). It builds on the observation that typical nuclear momenta are smaller than the pion mass even at mπ = 805 MeV, making Pionless EFT more applicable at larger pion masses. The approach enables extrapolation of lattice QCD results to larger nucleon numbers (A ≥ 5) that are not yet accessible to direct lattice calculations, and suggests that nuclear saturation properties may persist across a range of pion masses.
