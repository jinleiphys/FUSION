# Nuclear landscape in a mapped collective Hamiltonian from covariant density functional theory

> The nuclear landscape has been investigated within the triaxial relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory with the PC-PK1 density functional, and the beyond-mean-field dynamical correlation energies are taken into account by a microscopically mapped five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian without additional free parameters. The effects of triaxial deformation and dynamical correlations on the nuclear landscape are analyzed. The present results provide the best description of the experimental binding energies, in particular for medium and heavy mass regions, in comparison with the results obtained previously with other state-of-the-art covariant density functionals. The inclusion of the dynamical correlation energies plays an important role in the PC-PK1 results. It is emphasized that the nuclear landscape is considerably extended by the PC-PK1 functional in comparison with the previous results with other density functionals, which may be due to the different isovector properties in the density functionals.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the PC-PK1 density functional, when combined with beyond-mean-field dynamical correlation energies from a mapped five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian, provides the best description of experimental binding energies for even-even nuclei compared to other state-of-the-art covariant density functionals (DD-PC1 and TMA), but this improvement is conditional on the inclusion of the dynamical correlation energies and is most pronounced in medium and heavy mass regions.

## Method

The authors performed triaxial relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations using the PC-PK1 density functional for even-even nuclei with proton numbers Z=8 to Z=104, from the proton drip line to the neutron drip line. The beyond-mean-field dynamical correlation energies were computed by solving a microscopically mapped five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian (5DCH) without introducing additional free parameters, using the quasiparticle energies and wavefunctions obtained from constrained RHB calculations in the full beta-gamma plane (0 to 60 degrees). Adiabatic deformation constrained RHB calculations were performed with maximum beta values of 1.20 for light nuclei (Z<20) and 0.72 for heavier nuclei, and a finite-range separable pairing force with strength G=728 MeV·fm^3 was adopted.

## Key numbers

- Root-mean-square deviation of binding energies sigma_B = 1.31 MeV for triaxial RHB with PC-PK1 including dynamical correlation energies, compared to 2.64 MeV without correlation energies
- Root-mean-square deviations of two-neutron separation energies sigma_S2n = 0.78 MeV and two-proton separation energies sigma_S2p = 0.80 MeV for the full calculation
- For comparison: sigma_B = 2.00 MeV for axial RHB with DD-PC1, 2.11 MeV for axial RMF+BCS with TMA, and 2.72 MeV for nonrelativistic triaxial HFB with D1S (including correlation energies)
- Dynamical correlation energies range from 2.0 to 4.0 MeV for most nuclei
- 107 even-even nuclei are predicted to be triaxially deformed
- Symmetry energy J = 35.6 MeV and its slope L = 113 MeV at saturation density for PC-PK1
- Predicted neutron skin thickness of 208Pb is 0.26 fm and predicted maximum neutron star mass is 2.3 solar masses

## Context

This work extends previous global calculations of the nuclear landscape by incorporating triaxial deformation and beyond-mean-field dynamical correlations within covariant density functional theory, which were absent in earlier systematic studies using axial RHB with DD-PC1 (Agbemava 2014) and axial RMF+BCS with TMA (Geng 2005). The approach builds on the triaxial RMF plus BCS calculations with 5DCH by Lu (2015) that achieved a 1.14 MeV rms deviation for even-even nuclei, and extends the method to the full nuclear landscape from proton to neutron drip lines. The results enable more reliable predictions of neutron drip line positions and nuclear masses for astrophysical r-process simulations, while also highlighting that the isovector properties of density functionals remain poorly constrained by current experimental data (Piekarewicz 2021).
