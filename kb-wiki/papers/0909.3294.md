# Singly and Doubly Charmed J=1/2 Baryon Spectrum from Lattice QCD

> We compute the masses of the singly and doubly charmed baryons in full QCD using the relativistic Fermilab action for the charm quark. For the light quarks we use domain-wall fermions in the valence sector and improved Kogut-Susskind sea quarks. We use the low-lying charmonium spectrum to tune our heavy-quark action and as a guide to understanding the discretization errors associated with the heavy quark. Our results are in good agreement with experiment within our systematics. For the Ξ_{cc}, we find the isospin-averaged mass to be M_{Ξ_{cc}} = 3665 +/- 17 +/- 14 +0 -78 MeV; the three given uncertainties are statistical, systematic and an estimate of lattice discretization errors, respectively. In addition, we predict the mass splitting of the (isospin-averaged) spin-1/2 Ω_{cc} with the Ξ_{cc} to be M_{Ω_{cc}} - M_{Ξ_{cc}} = 98 +/- 9 +/- 22 +/- 13 MeV (in this mass splitting, the leading discretization errors are also suppressed by SU(3) symmetry). Combining this splitting with our determination of M_{Ξ_{cc}} leads to our prediction of the spin-1/2 Ω_{cc} mass, M_{Ω_{cc}} = 3763 +/- 19 +/- 26 +13 -79 MeV.

## Key claim

The authors compute the masses of singly and doubly charmed baryons using lattice QCD with dynamical sea quarks, finding good agreement with experiment within their systematic uncertainties. However, the results are obtained at a single lattice spacing (a ≈ 0.125 fm) and rely on chiral extrapolation from pion masses as light as 290 MeV, with the charm quark mass tuned nonperturbatively using the spin-averaged J/Ψ-ηc mass.

## Method

The calculation uses 2+1-flavor asqtad-improved Kogut-Susskind sea quark ensembles from the MILC Collaboration at a single lattice spacing, with domain-wall fermions for the valence light quarks and the relativistic Fermilab action for the charm quark. The charm quark action parameters m0 and ν are tuned nonperturbatively by reproducing the spin-averaged J/Ψ-ηc mass and restoring the relativistic dispersion relation, respectively. Hadron masses are extracted from two-point correlation functions with Gaussian-smeared sources and point sinks, then extrapolated to the physical light-quark mass using heavy-hadron chiral perturbation theory (HHχPT) and polynomial fits, with the scale set by the pion decay constant fπ.

## Key numbers

- MΞcc = 3665 ± 17 ± 14 +0 −78 MeV (isospin-averaged mass; statistical, systematic, and discretization error estimates respectively)
- MΩcc − MΞcc = 98 ± 9 ± 22 ± 13 MeV (isospin-averaged spin-1/2 mass splitting; discretization errors suppressed by SU(3) symmetry)
- MΩcc = 3763 ± 19 ± 26 +13 −79 MeV (predicted spin-1/2 Ωcc mass)
- Pion masses as light as 290 MeV on the ensembles used
- Lattice spacing a ≈ 0.125 fm (a−1 = 1588 MeV from MILC scale setting)
- Speed of light c2 for ηc ranges from 0.963(5) to 0.997(4) across ensembles

## Context

This work extends previous quenched lattice calculations of charmed baryons by Bowler et al. (1996), Flynn et al. (2003), and Chiu et al. (2005) to full QCD with dynamical sea quarks, using a relativistic heavy-quark action rather than NRQCD or light-quark actions for the charm. The results provide first-principles lattice QCD predictions for doubly charmed baryon masses, particularly the Ωcc, which are timely for experimental programs at BES-III, PANDA, and LHCb. The calculation is limited to a single lattice spacing, preventing a full continuum extrapolation, and the mixed-action setup (different valence and sea quark actions) requires future work with multiple lattice spacings to control discretization errors.
