# Thermodynamics of explosions

> We present our first attempts to formulate a thermodynamics-like description of explosions. The motivation is partly a fundamental interest in non-equilibrium statistical physics, partly the resemblance of an explosion to the late stages of a heavy-ion collision. We perform numerical simulations on a microscopic model of interacting billiard-ball like particles, and we analyse the results of such simulations trying to identify collective variables describing the degree of equilibrium during the explosion.

# Thermodynamics of explosions

## Key claim
The authors propose that a "pseudo-entropy" variable, defined from a co-moving phase-space grid, can characterize the degree of equilibrium in highly non-equilibrium explosive processes such as the late stages of heavy-ion collisions, but they caution that this quantity does not retain the property of monotonic increase that true entropy possesses.

## Method
The authors performed classical non-relativistic hard-sphere molecular dynamics simulations of 50 identical billiard-ball particles (mass 940 MeV, radius 0.5 fm) initialized with various combinations of thermal (Maxwell-Boltzmann) and collective Hubble-like flow motion inside a spherical volume. They introduced a pseudo-entropy Σ computed from a 6-dimensional reduced phase-space grid (7 segments per axis) that expands uniformly with the particle swarm, and analyzed its time evolution for four different initial conditions: thermal equilibrium in a container, pure ingoing flow (implosion-explosion), 50% thermal plus 50% outgoing flow, and pure outgoing flow in a container.

## Key numbers
- System size: A = 50 particles, initial radius 4.2 fm (with R₀ = 1.2 fm)
- Particle parameters: mass m = 940 MeV, hard-sphere radius r_hc = 0.5 fm
- Flow velocity range: 0 ≤ v₀f ≤ 0.5 (in units of c = 1)
- Phase-space grid: D = 7 segments per axis, giving 7⁶ boxes
- Ensemble size: 20 events per initial condition
- Temperature in compressed state of implosion-explosion case: 47 MeV
- Power-law decay exponents: Σ follows approximately 1.52 t⁻⁰·² and 1.87 t⁻⁰·² during initial expansion from equilibrium
- Peak scattering time in implosion-explosion case: t ≈ 6 fm/c (maximum compression)
- Container removal time for 'th20' case: t = 20 fm/c

## Context
This work extends previous studies of thermodynamic equilibrium in heavy-ion collisions by addressing systems that have neither fixed volume nor established equilibrium, a combination that distinguishes it from prior work cited as "prevstud" and from standard extensions to small systems (Hill) or small deviations from equilibrium (Zubarev). The authors contrast their approach with microscopic molecular dynamics models such as RQMD, FMD, and NMD, which do not assume equilibrium. The pseudo-entropy method enables future investigation of whether and when equilibrium is achieved in implosion-explosion processes, and the observed power-law decay of Σ during expansion from equilibrium suggests a characteristic signature that could be tested in more realistic nuclear collision models.
