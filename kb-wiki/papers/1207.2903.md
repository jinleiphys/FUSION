# Kaon photoproduction from the deuteron in a Regge-plus-resonance approach

> We present a Regge-inspired effective-Lagrangian framework for kaon photoproduction from the deuteron. Quasi-free kaon production is investigated using the Regge-plus-resonance (RPR) elementary operator within the relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation. The RPR model was developed to describe photoinduced and electroinduced charged-kaon production off protons. We show how this elementary operator can be transformed in order to account for the production of neutral kaons from both protons and neutrons. The model results for kaon photoproduction from the deuteron compare favourably to the 2H(g,K)YN data published to date.

## Key claim
The Regge-plus-resonance (RPR) elementary operator, originally developed for charged-kaon photoproduction off protons, can be extended to neutral-kaon production from both protons and neutrons and embedded in a relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation (RPWIA) for the deuteron, yielding model results that compare favorably to existing \(^2\text{H}(\gamma,K)YN\) data, but with the important caveat that the predictive power for neutron-target channels is severely limited by large uncertainties in the photocoupling helicity amplitudes for nucleon resonances.

## Method
The authors transform the RPR-2007 elementary operator for \(p(\gamma,K^+)Y\) to all six \(N(\gamma,K)Y\) channels using SU(2) isospin relations for strong couplings and experimental photocoupling helicity amplitudes (from SAID SM95 analysis) for electromagnetic couplings. They embed this operator in the deuteron via the RPWIA, using covariant deuteron-nucleon vertices with \(S\)-, \(P\)-, and \(D\)-wave components, and compute semi-inclusive differential cross sections and recoil polarizations for quasi-free kaon production.

## Key numbers
- The ratio of magnetic transition moments for \(K^{\ast0}(892)K^{0}(494)\) to \(K^{\ast+}(892)K^{+}(494)\) is fitted to available \(p(\gamma,K^0)\Sigma^+\) data as \(0.054 \pm 0.010\), yielding \(\chi^2/N_{\text{data}} = 3.39\).
- For \(n(\gamma,K^+)\Sigma^-\), the RPR model with Reggeized background plus resonances reproduces CLAS differential cross section data from \(E_\gamma = 0.8\) GeV to \(3.6\) GeV, with destructive interference between background and resonances improving agreement except at the lowest photon energy.
- The RPWIA and on-shell reduction results for \(^2\text{H}(\gamma,n)K^+\Lambda\) nearly coincide for spectator nucleon momenta \(|\mathbf{p}_N| \lesssim 350\) MeV, but diverge at higher momenta.
- For \(^2\text{H}(\gamma,K^0)YN\) at \(E_\gamma = 950\) MeV and \(1050\) MeV, the full RPR model (including resonances) gives results that differ from the Reggeized background alone, with uncertainties from helicity amplitudes producing deviations up to 100% in the differential cross section.

## Context
This work extends the RPR-2007 model (Vancraeyveld et al., 2007) from proton targets to neutron targets and the deuteron, building on prior studies of kaon photoproduction from the deuteron by Yamamura et al. (1996), Miyagawa et al. (2006), and Salam et al. (2004, 2006, 2009) who used different elementary operators and included final-state interactions. The approach enables future extraction of the on-shell \(n(\gamma,K)Y\) amplitude from deuteron data in kinematic regions where off-shell effects and final-state interactions are minimal, and provides a framework for studying hyperon-nucleon interactions through rescattering effects.
