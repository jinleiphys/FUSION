# Momentum Flow Mechanisms and Color-Lorentz Forces on Quarks in the Nucleon

> Momentum conservation in the nucleon is examined in terms of continuous flow of the momentum current density (or in short, momentum flow), which receives contributions from both kinetic motion and interacting forces involving quarks and gluons. While quarks conduct momentum flow through their kinetic motion and the gluon scalar (anomaly) contributes via pure interactions, the gluon stress tensor has both effects. The quarks momentum flow encodes the information of the color-Lorentz force density on them, and the momentum conservation allows to trace its origin to the gluon tensor and anomaly (a ``negative pressure'' potential). From the state-of-the-art lattice calculations and experimental fits on the form factors of the QCD energy-momentum tensor, we exhibit pictures of the momentum flow and the color-Lorentz forces on the quarks in the nucleon. In particular, the anomaly contributes a critical attractive force with a strength similar to that of a heavy-quark confinement potential.

# Momentum Flow Mechanisms and Color-Lorentz Forces on Quarks in the Nucleon

## Key claim

The authors argue that the total momentum current density (MCD) in the nucleon, often interpreted as mechanical pressure and shear forces, does not have an overarching mechanical significance as surface forces between parts of the system; instead, only the divergence of the quark kinetic MCD has a direct physical meaning as the color-Lorentz force density on quarks, and the trace anomaly contributes a critical attractive force with an average strength of approximately 1 GeV/fm, similar to the QCD string tension.

## Method

The authors decompose the QCD energy-momentum tensor (EMT) spatial components into three gauge-invariant contributions: quark kinetic MCD, gluon tensor MCD, and gluon scalar (trace anomaly) MCD. They relate these components to gravitational form factors (GFFs) A(q^2), B(q^2), and C(q^2) and use a recent global phenomenological analysis (Guo 2025) that fits lattice QCD calculations and experimental data via Bayesian inference with Monte-Carlo Markov Chain sampling to obtain coordinate-space distributions of the MCD trace and force densities in the Breit frame.

## Key numbers

- The average radial force from the anomaly on quarks is -1.06 (+0.12/-0.11) GeV/fm, with a magnitude similar to the confinement string tension.
- The total MCD trace satisfies the virial relation: integral of [epsilon_q(r) + epsilon_g(r) - 3 epsilon_a(r)] d^3 r = 0.
- The anomaly MCD integrates to -M/4, where M is the proton mass.
- The GFFs are parametrized as F_i(q^2) = F_i0 (1 - q^2/M_Fi^2)^{-alpha} with alpha=2 for A_q,g and alpha=3 for C_q,g.

## Context

This work extends the ongoing discussion of nucleon mechanical properties (Polyakov 2002, Goeke 2007, Burkert 2018) by clarifying that the MCD, particularly the C/D form factor, cannot be directly interpreted as 2D surface forces or pressure in the nucleon due to ambiguities from superpotentials (Belinfante 1940, Rosenfeld 1940). The authors instead focus on force densities obtained from divergences of individual MCD components, finding that the trace anomaly provides a confining force comparable to the QCD string tension (Sun 2020, Brambilla 2021), in contrast to a previous model estimate (Polyakov 2018) that found a value an order of magnitude smaller. This work enables future studies of confinement mechanisms through improved precision on the C/D form factor from lattice QCD and experiments.
