# Isovector properties of quark matter and quark stars in an isospin-dependent confining model

> The confining quark matter (CQM) model, in which the confinement and asymptotic freedom are modeled via the Richardson potential for quark-quark vector interaction and the chiral symmetry restoration at high density is described by the density dependent quark mass, is extended to include isospin dependence of the quark mass. Within this extended isospin-dependent confining quark matter (ICQM) model, we study the properties of strange quark matter and quark stars. We find that including isospin dependence of the quark mass can significantly influence the quark matter symmetry energy, the stability of strange quark matter and the mass-radius relation of quark stars. In particular, we demonstrate although the recently discovered large mass pulsars PSR J1614.2230 and PSR J0348+0432 with masses around two times solar mass ($2M_{\odot}$) cannot be quark stars within the original CQM model, they can be well described by quark stars in the ICQM model if the isospin dependence of quark mass is strong enough so that the quark matter symmetry energy is about four times that of a free quark gas. We also discuss the effects of the density dependence of quark mass on the properties of quark stars. Our results indicate that the heavy quark stars with mass around $2M_{\odot}$ (if exist) can put strong constraints on isospin and density dependence of the quark mass as well as the quark matter symmetry energy.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the recently discovered massive pulsars PSR J1614.2230 and PSR J0348+0432, with masses around two solar masses, cannot be described as quark stars within the original confining quark matter (CQM) model, but can be well described within the extended isospin-dependent confining quark matter (ICQM) model if the isospin dependence of quark mass is strong enough such that the quark matter symmetry energy is about four times that of a free quark gas.

## Method

The authors extend the CQM model by introducing an isospin-dependent term into the density-dependent quark mass parametrization, adding a term proportional to the isospin asymmetry parameter and the isospin quantum number of each quark flavor. They then solve the equations of state for strange quark matter under beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions, and compute the mass-radius relation for static quark stars using the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equation.

## Key numbers

- The maximum mass of a quark star in the original CQM model (DI-0, with $\nu_{ud}=\nu_s=0.53$) is $1.80 M_{\odot}$.
- The quark matter symmetry energy at baryon density $1.5$ fm$^{-3}$ (around $10n_0$) is $39$ MeV for DI-0, $155$ MeV for DI-1000, and $31$ MeV for a free quark gas.
- At nuclear matter saturation density $n_0=0.17$ fm$^{-3}$, the symmetry energy is $59$ MeV for DI-1000, $16$ MeV for DI-0, and $15$ MeV for the free quark gas.
- The density slope parameter $L$ of the symmetry energy at $n_0$ is $24.33$ MeV for DI-0, $156.32$ MeV for DI-1000, and $14.93$ MeV for the free quark gas.
- The maximum mass of a quark star with DI-1000 ($D_I=1000$ MeV fm$^{3\alpha}$, $\nu_{ud}=\nu_s=0.68$) is $2.03 M_{\odot}$ with a corresponding radius of $9.815$ km.
- For the parameter set DI$^{*}$-0 ($D_I=0$, $\nu_{ud}=0.53$, $\nu_s=1$), the maximum mass is $2.04 M_{\odot}$ with radius $9.795$ km.
- For DI$^{*}$-m300 ($D_I=-300$ MeV fm$^{3\alpha}$, $\nu_{ud}=0.5$, $\nu_s=1.2$), the maximum mass is $2.03 M_{\odot}$ with radius $9.755$ km.
- For DI$^{*}$-600 ($D_I=600$ MeV fm$^{3\alpha}$, $\nu_{ud}=0.63$, $\nu_s=0.7$), the maximum mass is $2.02 M_{\odot}$ with radius $9.690$ km.

## Context

This work extends the confining quark matter (CQM) model of Dey et al. (1998) by incorporating isospin dependence of the quark mass, motivated by isospin-dependent chiral condensates studied in asymmetric nucleonic matter (Cai et al., 2017) and recent work on isovector properties in the PNJL model (Liu et al., 2016). The results enable constraints on the isospin and density dependence of quark mass and the quark matter symmetry energy from observations of massive pulsars, and have implications for the QCD phase diagram at finite baryon density under extreme isospin conditions.
