# Unitary Fermi gas in the epsilon expansion

> We construct systematic expansions around four and two spatial dimensions for a Fermi gas near the unitarity limit. Near four spatial dimensions such a Fermi gas can be understood as a weakly-interacting system of fermionic and bosonic degrees of freedom. To the leading and next-to-leading orders in the expansion over ε=4-d, with d being the dimensionality of space, we determine the thermodynamic functions, the fermion quasiparticle spectrum, the critical polarizations, and the critical temperature as functions of the binding energy of the two-body state. We also show that the unitary Fermi gas near two spatial dimensions reduces to a weakly-interacting Fermi gas and calculate the thermodynamic functions and the fermion quasiparticle spectrum in the expansion over \barε=d-2. We discuss the matching of the two systematic expansions around four and two spatial dimensions in order to extract physical observables at d=3. We find good agreement of the results with those from recent Monte Carlo simulations.

## Key claim
The unitary Fermi gas can be systematically studied via perturbative expansions in the parameter $\epsilon = 4-d$ (near four dimensions) and $\bar{\epsilon} = d-2$ (near two dimensions), where $d$ is the spatial dimension. The abstract’s claim of “good agreement” with Monte Carlo simulations is qualified by the fact that the naive extrapolation of the $\bar{\epsilon}$ expansion to $d=3$ yields poor results ($\xi \approx 0$), requiring a more sophisticated matching procedure (Borel-Padé approximants) to obtain reasonable values for physical observables.

## Method
The author constructs a field-theoretic Lagrangian for a Fermi gas with a contact interaction and uses dimensional regularization to analyze the system near four and two spatial dimensions. Near four dimensions, the two-body scattering is mediated by a bosonic bound state with a small effective coupling $g \sim \epsilon^{1/2}$, allowing for a perturbative expansion in $\epsilon = 4-d$. Near two dimensions, the system reduces to a weakly-interacting Fermi gas with a small effective coupling $\bar{g}^2 \sim \bar{\epsilon}$, enabling an expansion in $\bar{\epsilon} = d-2$. The two expansions are then matched using Borel-Padé approximants to extract results at the physical dimension $d=3$.

## Key numbers
*   **Universal parameter $\xi$ at $d=3$:** Borel-Padé approximants matching the $\epsilon$ and $\bar{\epsilon}$ expansions yield $\xi = 0.391$, $0.364$, and $0.378$, compared to the Monte Carlo result of $\xi \approx 0.42$ (Carlson, 2005).
*   **Condensate $\phi_0$ near $d=2$:** To next-to-leading order, the gap equation gives $\phi_0 = (2\mu/e) e^{-1/\bar{\epsilon}}$, where the pre-exponential factor is reduced by $e \approx 2.71828$ due to medium effects (Gor’kov correction).
*   **Universal parameter $\xi$ near $d=2$:** The expansion yields $\xi = 1 - \bar{\epsilon} + 1.80685\,\bar{\epsilon}^2 + O(\bar{\epsilon}^3)$.
*   **Universal parameter $\xi$ near $d=4$:** The expansion yields $\xi = (\epsilon^{3/2}/2) \exp[\epsilon \ln \epsilon / (8-2\epsilon)] (1 - 0.0492\epsilon + \dots)$.

## Context
This work extends the intuitive arguments of Nussinov and Nussinov (2004) by providing a rigorous diagrammatic framework for the unitary Fermi gas in $4-\epsilon$ and $2+\bar{\epsilon}$ dimensions. It enables the calculation of thermodynamic and spectral properties in a controlled expansion, which can then be extrapolated to three dimensions. The matching procedure using Borel-Padé approximants provides a method to improve upon the naive linear interpolation between the exact limits at $d=2$ ($\xi=1$) and $d=4$ ($\xi=0$), and the results are compared to Monte Carlo simulations (Carlson, 2005).
