# Outflows from accretion disks formed in neutron star mergers: effect of black hole spin

> The accretion disk that forms after a neutron star merger is a source of neutron-rich ejecta. The ejected material contributes to a radioactively-powered electromagnetic transient, with properties that depend sensitively on the composition of the outflow. Here we investigate how the spin of the black hole remnant influences mass ejection on the thermal and viscous timescales. We carry out two-dimensional, time-dependent hydrodynamic simulations of merger remnant accretion disks including viscous angular momentum transport and approximate neutrino self-irradiation. The gravity of the spinning black hole is included via a pseudo-Newtonian potential. We find that a disk around a spinning black hole ejects more mass, up to a factor of several, relative to the non-spinning case. The enhanced mass loss is due to energy release by accretion occurring deeper in the gravitational potential, raising the disk temperature and hence the rate of viscous heating in regions where neutrino cooling is ineffective. The mean electron fraction of the outflow increases moderately with BH spin due to a highly-irradiated (though not neutrino-driven) wind component. While the bulk of the ejecta is still very neutron-rich, thus generating heavy r-process elements, the leading edge of the wind contains a small amount of Lanthanide-free material. This component can give rise to a ~1 day blue optical `bump' in a kilonova light curve, even in the case of prompt BH formation, which may facilitate its detection.

## Key claim

The spin of the black hole remnant in a neutron star merger increases the mass ejected from the accretion disk by up to a factor of several relative to the non-spinning case, with the enhancement arising from deeper gravitational potential energy release rather than from neutrino-driven winds; the mean electron fraction of the outflow increases only moderately, and the bulk of the ejecta remains very neutron-rich.

## Method

The authors performed two-dimensional, time-dependent hydrodynamic simulations of merger remnant accretion disks using the FLASH3.2 code, including viscous angular momentum transport via the Shakura-Sunyaev alpha viscosity prescription and approximate neutrino self-irradiation. The gravity of the spinning black hole was modeled using the pseudo-Newtonian potential of Artemova et al. (1996), which reproduces the innermost stable circular orbit (ISCO) and horizon radii of the Kerr metric. Two sequences of models were evolved with black hole spin parameters a = {0, 0.4, 0.8, 0.95}, differing only in the neutrino treatment (a leakage scheme versus an optically-thin with corrections approach), while keeping a fixed black hole mass of 3 solar masses and initial disk mass of 0.03 solar masses.

## Key numbers

- Mass ejection increases by a factor larger than 5 when going from spin a=0 to a=0.95: for the leakage sequence, total ejected mass is 0.05 of initial disk mass at a=0 and 0.28 at a=0.95 (unbound fraction: 0.03 vs 0.21)
- The mean electron fraction in the equatorial outflow increases modestly from 0.19 (a=0) to 0.24 (a=0.95) in the leakage sequence, and from 0.18 to 0.24 in the optically-thin sequence
- For a=0.95, the polar outflow has electron fraction 0.32, entropy 51 k_B/baryon, and expansion time 10 ms, compared to equatorial values of 0.24, 21 k_B/baryon, and 42 ms
- The mass of ejecta with electron fraction >= 0.25 increases from 9.8 x 10^-6 solar masses at a=0 to 3.0 x 10^-3 solar masses at a=0.95 (leakage sequence)
- The integrated neutrino heating rate at radii > 10^7 cm is only a few percent of the net viscous heating rate
- For the comparison model with disk mass 0.3 solar masses and alpha=0.05, the ejected fraction is 0.17 of initial disk mass, compared to 0.23 reported by Just et al. (2014)

## Context

This work extends the approach of Fernandez and Metzger (2013) and Metzger and Fernandez (2014), who studied outflows from disks around non-spinning black holes, by systematically incorporating the effect of black hole spin via a pseudo-Newtonian potential. The results are qualitatively consistent with general relativistic magnetohydrodynamic simulations of radiatively-inefficient accretion disks by Sadowski et al. (2013), which also found a correlation between black hole spin and mass ejection. The finding that a small amount of Lanthanide-free material is ejected even in the case of prompt black hole formation suggests that a ~1 day blue optical bump in kilonova light curves may occur, potentially facilitating electromagnetic counterpart detection for gravitational wave sources.
