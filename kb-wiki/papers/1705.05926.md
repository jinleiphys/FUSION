# Low energy constituent quark and pion effective couplings in a weak external magnetic field

> An effective model with pions and constituent quarks in the presence of a weak external background electromagnetic field is derived by starting from a dressed one gluon exchange quark-quark interaction.By applying the auxiliary field and background field methods, the structureless pion limit is considered to extract effective pion and constituent quark couplings in the presence of a weak magnetic field. The leading terms of a large quark and gluon masses expansion are obtained by resolving effective coupling constants which turn out to depend on a weak magnetic field. Two pion field definitions are considered for that. Several relations between the effective coupling constants and parameters can be derived exactly or in the limit of very large quark mass at zero and weak constant magnetic field. Among these ratios, the Gell Mann Oakes Renner and the quark level Goldberger Treiman relations are obtained. In addition to that, in the pion sector, the leading terms of Chiral Perturbation Theory coupled to the electromagnetic field is recovered. Some numerical estimates are provided for the effective coupling constants and parameters.

# Low energy constituent quark and pion effective couplings in a weak external magnetic field

## Key claim

The paper derives effective couplings for pions and constituent quarks in the presence of a weak external magnetic field from a dressed one-gluon exchange quark-quark interaction, but only within the structureless pion limit and under a large quark and gluon mass expansion, meaning the results are valid when the parameter $eB_0/{M^*}^2$ is small (approximately 0.6 or less) and the pion internal structure is neglected.

## Method

Starting from a QCD effective action with a dressed one-gluon exchange interaction, the author applies the auxiliary field method to introduce scalar and pseudoscalar meson fields and the background field method to separate sea quarks (integrated out) from constituent quarks. The resulting quark determinant is expanded for large quark and gluon effective masses, and two different nonlinear definitions of the pion field (Weinberg's covariant derivative form and the exponential U-representation) are used to extract effective coupling constants that incorporate the weak magnetic field dependence. Numerical estimates are provided using an effective confining gluon propagator with parameters from Cornwall (1997).

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark effective mass at zero magnetic field: $M^* \simeq 220$ MeV (from gap equation with current quark mass $m = 10$ MeV and gluon mass $M_g = M \simeq 378$ MeV)
- For a constituent quark effective mass $M^* \simeq 0.31$ GeV and $eB_0/{M^*}^2 \sim 0.6$, the magnetic field strength is $eB_0 \sim 0.06$ GeV$^2$ or equivalently $eB_0 \sim 3 m_\pi^2$
- For $B_0 \sim 7 m_\pi^2$ (expected in RHIC), the quark effective mass may reach $M^* \sim 0.45$ GeV, giving $eB_0/{M^*}^2 \sim 0.6$
- Scalar auxiliary field at zero magnetic field: $s_0 \simeq 210$ MeV
- Pion decay constant used for canonical pion field definition: $F \simeq 92$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the derivation of the large-Nc effective field theory for constituent quarks and pions from a dressed gluon exchange (Braghin, EPJA 2016) by incorporating electromagnetic coupling to a weak background magnetic field. It recovers the leading terms of Chiral Perturbation Theory coupled to the electromagnetic field and reproduces the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner and Goldberger-Treiman relations at zero or weak magnetic field. The approach follows the global color model framework developed by Reinhardt (1990) and the auxiliary field method of Kleinert (1993), and it enables future studies of magnetic field corrections to hadron interactions in systems such as magnetars ($B_0 \simeq 10^{15}$ G) and non-central relativistic heavy ion collisions ($B_0 \simeq 10^{18}$ G).
