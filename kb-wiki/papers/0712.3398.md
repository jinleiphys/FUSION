# Eikonal zeros in the momentum transfer space from proton-proton scattering: An empirical analysis

> By means of improved empirical fits to the differential cross section data on $pp$ elastic scattering at $19.4 \le\sqrt{s}\le 62.5$ GeV and making use of a semi-analytical method, we determine the eikonal in the momentum transfer space (the inverse scattering problem). This method allows the propagation of the uncertainties from the fit parameters up to the extracted eikonal, providing statistical evidence that the imaginary part of the eikonal (real part of the opacity function) presents a zero (change of signal) in the momentum space, at $q^2 \approx 7 \pm 1$ GeV$^2$. We discuss the implication of this change of signal in the phenomenological context, showing that eikonal models with one zero provide good descriptions of the differential cross sections in the full momentum transfer range, but that is not the case for models without zero. Empirical connections between the extracted eikonal and results from a recent global analysis on the proton electric form factor are also discussed, in special the Wu-Yang conjecture. In addition, we present a critical review on the $pp$ differential cross section data presently available at high energies.

## Key claim

By fitting proton-proton elastic scattering data at center-of-mass energies between 19.4 and 62.5 GeV and using a semi-analytical method to extract the eikonal in momentum transfer space, the authors find statistical evidence that the imaginary part of the eikonal has a zero at approximately q² = 7 ± 1 GeV², but unlike their earlier analysis, they cannot infer that the position of this zero decreases with increasing energy.

## Method

The authors performed improved empirical fits to differential cross section data for pp elastic scattering at seven energies (19.4, 23.5, 27.4, 30.7, 44.7, 52.8, and 62.5 GeV) using a parametrization of the scattering amplitude as a sum of exponentials in q², with the total cross section and rho parameter as inputs. They then applied a semi-analytical method to extract the eikonal in momentum transfer space via Fourier-Bessel transforms, propagating uncertainties from the fit parameters to the extracted eikonal. The analysis used final published data from Faissler et al. (1981) at 27.4 GeV (39 data points) rather than the preliminary Landolt-Bornstein values used in prior work, and included data at 19.4 GeV up to q² = 11.9 GeV².

## Key numbers

- The imaginary part of the eikonal (real part of the opacity function) presents a zero at q² ≈ 7 ± 1 GeV².
- The analysis covers the energy range 19.4 ≤ √s ≤ 62.5 GeV.
- The largest momentum transfer data used are at √s = 27.4 GeV with q² up to 14.2 GeV² (39 data points from Faissler et al.).
- At √s = 19.4 GeV, data cover q² from 0.075 to 11.9 GeV² (156 data points from four experiments).
- The optical point at √s = 19.4 GeV is 77.66 ± 0.02 mb GeV⁻², based on σ_tot = 38.98 ± 0.04 mb and ρ = 0.019 ± 0.016.
- The optical point at √s = 62.5 GeV is 96.8 ± 1.1 mb GeV⁻², based on σ_tot = 43.32 ± 0.23 mb and ρ = 0.095 ± 0.011.

## Context

This work extends the earlier analyses of Avila and Menon (2003, 2005) by correcting the data ensemble at 27.4 GeV to use final published results from Faissler et al. (1981) instead of preliminary Landolt-Bornstein values, and by including additional data at 19.4 GeV. The authors find that, contrary to their previous claim, the position of the eikonal zero cannot be shown to decrease with energy. The extracted eikonal is compared with phenomenological models such as the Chou-Yang model, QCD-inspired models, the Bourrely-Soffer-Wu model, and multiple diffraction models, showing that models with one eikonal zero describe the differential cross section data well while models without a zero do not. The results also enable empirical connections to the proton electric form factor and the Wu-Yang conjecture.
