# Lectures on the Theory of High Energy A+A at RHIC

> In these lectures aspects of current theory used to interpret the preliminary data on ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions at RHIC energies in terms of the physical properties of QCD matter at extreme densities are introduced. Topics include: What are the physics questions at SPS and RHIC?, Geometrical vs Dynamical features of A+A, The interplay of computable Hard pQCD vs phenomenological Soft dynamics, Baryon number transport and Junctions, How can we compute and get experimental control over the initial conditions?, How to reconcile apparent hydrodynamic behavior with partonic/hadronic transport theory. I use the Preliminary RHIC data available up to June 1,2001 to illustrate these topics. Since the main new observable at RHIC relative to SPS is jet quenching, I elaborate on this ``tomographic'' probe of ultra-dense matter. The possible discovery of jet quenching at RHIC by STAR and PHENIX is highlighted.

## Key claim

The preliminary RHIC data available up to June 1, 2001 suggest a possible discovery of jet quenching in central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=130 AGeV, but the author emphasizes that most data shown are of PRELIMINARY nature and could change as better control over systematic errors is achieved in the next few years.

## Method

The lectures decompose hadron production in A+A collisions into a phenomenological "soft" component scaling with the number of participant nucleons and a perturbative QCD calculable "hard" component scaling with the number of binary nucleon-nucleon collisions, using the HIJING Monte Carlo event generator as a baseline model. The author compares these two-component model predictions with preliminary data from the STAR, PHENIX, PHOBOS, and BRAHMS experiments on charged particle multiplicities, transverse energy, and high-pT hadron suppression. The nuclear modification factor R_AA is constructed by dividing the observed invariant cross section in A+A by the scaled binary collision number times the p+p invariant cross section.

## Key numbers

- First Au+Au collisions at RHIC occurred at sqrt(s)=56 AGeV per nucleon pair on June 12, 2000, with collisions at sqrt(s)=130 AGeV measured soon thereafter
- Approximately 4000 charged particles are produced in each central Au+Au collision at 130 AGeV
- The Stefan-Boltzmann constant for QCD with 3 colors and N_f flavors at mu_B=0 is K_SB = 3P/T^4 = epsilon/T^4 approximately 12
- The critical temperature for QGP formation is T_c approximately 150 MeV, derived from the vacuum energy density B approximately 200 MeV/fm^3
- The pQCD Debye screening mass mu_D receives non-perturbative corrections of about a factor of 3 up to T approximately 200 GeV
- The scaled charged particle pseudorapidity density dN_ch/deta / N_part at RHIC is approximately 50% higher than in p+p at the same energy per baryon
- The average transverse energy per charged particle E_T/N_ch is approximately 0.8 GeV, independent of sqrt(s) from 20 to 130 AGeV and independent of centrality
- The nuclear modification factor for charged hadrons at p_T > 2 GeV in central Au+Au reaches approximately 0.5 at 4 GeV/c, in contrast to the Cronin enhancement factor of about 2 seen at SPS
- The initial gluon rapidity density at RHIC is estimated to be between HIJING's 200 and EKRT's 1000, corresponding to gluon densities of approximately 10-50/fm^3 at formation time 0.1-0.2 fm/c

## Context

These lectures extend the theoretical framework developed for SPS energies (sqrt(s)=20 AGeV) and AGS energies (sqrt(s)=5 AGeV) to the new RHIC energy regime, building on the two-component hard+soft model implemented in HIJING by Wang (1991) and the EKRT saturation model by Eskola, Kajantie, Ruuskanen, and Tuominen. The work contradicts the Cronin enhancement observed at SPS by WA98, where even a modest dE/dx=0.2 GeV/fm was ruled out by data from Wang (1998), and instead finds suppression at RHIC consistent with jet quenching predictions by Gyulassy and Wang (1994). The results enable future tomographic studies of ultra-dense QCD matter using hard probes, with the design energy sqrt(s)=200 AGeV anticipated for summer 2001.
