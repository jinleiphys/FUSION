# On Lattice Calculation of Electric Dipole Moments and Form Factors of the Nucleon

> We analyze commonly used expressions for computing the nucleon electric dipole form factors (EDFF) $F_3$ and moments (EDM) on a lattice and find that they lead to spurious contributions from the Pauli form factor $F_2$ due to inadequate definition of these form factors when parity mixing of lattice nucleon fields is involved. Using chirally symmetric domain wall fermions, we calculate the proton and the neutron EDFF induced by the CP-violating quark chromo-EDM interaction using the corrected expression. In addition, we calculate the electric dipole moment of the neutron using background electric field that respects time translation invariance and boundary conditions, and find that it decidedly agrees with the new formula but not the old formula for $F_3$. Finally, we analyze some selected lattice results for the nucleon EDM and observe that after the correction is applied, they either agree with zero or are substantially reduced in magnitude, thus reconciling their difference from phenomenological estimates of the nucleon EDM.

## Key claim

The commonly used lattice expression for computing the nucleon electric dipole form factor \(F_3\) suffers from spurious mixing with the Pauli form factor \(F_2\) due to inadequate treatment of parity mixing in lattice nucleon fields; after correcting this mixing, previously reported lattice results for the nucleon EDM either agree with zero or are substantially reduced, reconciling them with phenomenological estimates.

## Method

The authors derive the correct form of the nucleon-current vertex in a CP-violating vacuum, showing that the chirally rotated spinors must be removed by a field redefinition to extract the true electric dipole form factor \(F_3\) without contamination from \(F_2\). They compute nucleon two- and three-point correlation functions using chirally symmetric domain wall fermions with a CP-violating quark chromo-EDM interaction, extracting both the corrected form factors and the neutron EDM from energy shifts in a uniform background electric field that preserves translational invariance and periodic boundary conditions.

## Key numbers

- The parity-mixing angle \(\alpha_5\) is defined via the nucleon mass term \(m_{N'} e^{-2i\alpha_5\gamma_5}\); the mixing between \(\tilde{F}_2\) and \(\tilde{F}_3\) is given by \(\tilde{F}_3 = -\sin(2\alpha_5) F_2 + \cos(2\alpha_5) F_3\).
- The quantum of flux through a lattice plaquette is \(\Phi_{\mu\nu} = 6\pi/(L_\mu L_\nu)\), and the fractional quark charges \(Q_u=2/3\), \(Q_d=-1/3\) require a total flux of \(3\cdot 2\pi\) through the lattice edge.
- The energy shift of a neutral particle in background electric and magnetic fields is \(E_0 - m = -\frac{\kappa}{2m} \vec H\cdot\hat\Sigma - \frac{\zeta}{2m} \vec E \cdot \hat\Sigma + O(\kappa^2,\zeta^2)\), where \(\kappa = F_2(0)\) and \(\zeta = F_3(0)\) are the magnetic and electric dipole moments.

## Context

This work corrects a systematic error present in all previous lattice calculations of nucleon EDFFs and EDMs from nucleon-current correlators, including those studying the QCD \(\bar\theta\)-angle (Shintani 2005, Berruto 2005, Aoki 2008, Guo 2015, Shindler 2015, Alexandrou 2015, Shintani 2015) and the chromo-EDM (Bhattacharya 2016). The corrected formalism reconciles lattice results with phenomenological estimates from chiral perturbation theory and QCD sum rules (Crewther 1979, Pospelov 1999, Pospelov 2000), and enables reliable lattice QCD calculations of nucleon EDMs to constrain beyond-the-Standard-Model CP violation.
