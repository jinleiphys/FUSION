# Impact of momentum resolution on factorial moments due to power-law correlations between particles

> The effect of momentum resolution on factorial moments due to the power-law correlation function is studied. The study is motivated by the search for the critical point of the strongly interacting matter in heavy-ion collisions using the intermittency method. We observe that factorial moments are significantly affected by the finite momentum resolution. The effect is superficially significant compared to intuitive expectations. The results depend on the power of the correlation function and the number of uncorrelated particles.

## Key claim

The paper shows that finite momentum resolution significantly suppresses the second scaled factorial moment \(F_2\) even when the bin width is much larger than the momentum resolution, contradicting the intuitive expectation that resolution effects are negligible under those conditions (as assumed in earlier work such as EHS/NA22 (1993)).

## Method

The authors use a Critical Monte Carlo toy model in which correlated particle pairs are generated in one-dimensional transverse momentum \(X\) using a two-particle distribution function with a power-law correlation \(\rho(X_1,X_2) \propto |X_1-X_2|^{-\phi_2}\). The true momentum is smeared by adding a Gaussian fluctuation with standard deviation \(\sigma\) (1–20 MeV/\(c\)) to produce the measured momentum. The second scaled factorial moment \(F_2(M)\) is computed as a function of the number of bins \(M\) for both the true and smeared momenta, using either the original non-uniform \(X\) distribution or a cumulative variable \(Q_X\) that makes the distribution uniform.

## Key numbers

* For a single correlated pair (\(N_c=2\), no uncorrelated particles) with \(\sigma = 1\) MeV/\(c\), more than 5% deviation between smeared and true \(F_2\) occurs when \(M > 100\) for binning in \(X\); for \(\sigma = 20\) MeV/\(c\), the same deviation occurs at \(M \approx 5\).
* For the cumulative variable \(Q_X\) with the same single correlated pair and \(\sigma = 1\) MeV/\(c\), the 5% deviation occurs at \(M > 30\) (compared to \(M > 100\) for non-uniform binning).
* With one correlated pair plus uncorrelated particles (\(\langle N_{uc}\rangle = 30\), \(\sigma = 10\) MeV/\(c\)), the 5% deviation in \(Q_X\) binning occurs at \(M > 110\).
* For a single correlated pair with \(\phi_2 = 0.8\) and \(\sigma = 10\) MeV/\(c\) in \(Q_X\) binning, the 5% deviation occurs at \(M \approx 4\); for \(\phi_2 = 0.2\) with the same \(\sigma\), the deviation occurs at \(M \ge 20\).

## Context

This work extends the intermittency method used to search for the critical point of strongly interacting matter in heavy-ion collisions, specifically addressing the effect of finite momentum resolution on factorial moments. It contradicts the assumption made in the EHS/NA22 collaboration (1993) that resolution effects are negligible when the bin width exceeds the resolution. The results enable experimentalists to properly configure the intermittency analysis parameters and highlight the need for correction techniques (e.g., unfolding) to recover the true intermittency index from smeared data.
