# Isotropization in heavy ion collisions in the 2PI formalism

> An important question in heavy-ion collisions is how the initial far-from-equilibrium medium evolves and thermalizes while it undergoes a rapid longitudinal expansion. In this presentation, we show how to use the two-particle irreducible (2PI) effective action to address this question, focusing on $ϕ^4$ scalar theory truncated at three loops. We present numerical results for quantities such as the occupation number, the thermal mass, and the number density.

## Key claim

The 2PI formalism truncated at three loops in $\phi^4$ scalar theory shows that a longitudinally expanding system can approach an almost isotropic state, but this isotropization requires the inclusion of 2-loop self-energy graphs beyond the Hartree approximation; the Hartree truncation alone leads to increasing anisotropy consistent with free streaming.

## Method

The authors solved the equations of motion derived from the two-particle irreducible (2PI) effective action for a $\phi^4$ scalar field theory, truncating the 2PI functional $\Phi[\varphi,G]$ at order $g^4$ (including the sunset diagram). The propagator was decomposed into a spectral function $\rho$ and a statistical propagator $F$, and the equations were solved numerically in the Schwinger-Keldysh formalism under assumptions of transverse translation and rotation invariance, boost invariance along the collision axis, and identical projectiles. The occupation number, effective mass, and number density were extracted from the propagators using a quasi-particle ansatz.

## Key numbers

- The renormalized coupling constant used was $g_{R}^2 \approx 22$, described as a moderate coupling for $\phi^4$ theory, giving a shear viscosity to entropy ratio $\eta/s \sim 25 \gg 1$.
- The memory storage requirement for propagators with symmetries was reduced to approximately $0.5$ TeraBytes for $N_t=1000$ timesteps and $N_\perp=N_\eta=500$ grid points, compared to an estimated $10^9$ TeraBytes without symmetry assumptions.
- The mass counterterm $\delta m^2$ from the tadpole was the largest subtraction; $\delta g^2$ provided a $10-15\%$ correction, while $\delta m^2$ from 2-loop self-energies and field renormalization were both at the percent level.

## Context

This work extends the application of the 2PI formalism, previously reviewed by Berges (2004), to the problem of isotropization in heavy-ion collisions, which is not achieved at leading order in the color glass condensate (CGC) framework where the longitudinal pressure remains much smaller than the transverse pressure (Lappi, 2006; Fukushima, 2011). The next-to-leading order in CGC shows some trend toward isotropization but suffers from secular divergences due to instabilities (Romatschke, 2005). The 2PI approach avoids the assumptions of kinetic theory (long-lived quasiparticles, moderate occupation number, moderate gradients) that are not valid at early times around the inverse saturation scale, and enables the study of number-changing off-shell processes (such as $3\to 1$ processes) that are absent in kinetic theory's $2\to 2$ scattering.
