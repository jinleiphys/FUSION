# Density functionals derived from Feynman diagrams

> We construct a stationary density functional for the partition function from a chosen set of one (boson) line irreducible Feynman diagrams. The construction does not proceed by the inversion of a Legendre transform. It is formulated for fermions with Coulomb interactions, in which case a stationary functional of the particle density is contructed, as well as for nucleons interacting with mesons, which involves a stationary functional of several densities. The use of Kohn-Sham orbits is shown to be an unnecessary complication.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a stationary density functional for the partition function from a chosen subset of one (boson) line irreducible Feynman diagrams, without requiring the inversion of a Legendre transform. The construction works for both fermions with Coulomb interactions (yielding a functional of particle density) and nucleons interacting with mesons (yielding a functional of several densities), and the authors argue that the use of Kohn-Sham orbits is an unnecessary complication.

## Method

The authors express the partition function as a path integral over fermion and boson fields, then rewrite the particle densities in terms of one-line irreducible diagrams calculated with dressed fermion propagators. The potentials and densities are related by a simple analytic expression (equation U_a(x) = -∫ d⁴y ⟨x|K_ab|y⟩ ρ_b(y)), and the self-consistent potentials are obtained by iteratively diagonalizing the single-particle hamiltonian h₀ + U_a Γ_a and recalculating the chosen set of one-line irreducible diagrams until convergence.

## Key numbers

- The topological relation for diagrams with n_a articulation lines, n_c cycles, and n_I irreducible parts is n_I + n_c - n_a = 1 (equation 3.1).
- For diagrams with open-ended meson lines (source factor j), the topological relation becomes n_I + n_j + n_c - n_a = 1, where n_j is the number of open ends.
- The dressed fermion propagator G is defined as G = 1/(∂_τ + h₀ - μ + U_a Γ_a) (equation 5.2).
- The unperturbed fermion propagator g is defined as g = 1/(∂_τ + h₀ - μ) (equation 5.1).

## Context

This work extends the earlier construction of stationary functionals of the density matrix developed in Ripka (1986) and Ripka (1970), which described the theory of a static potential in the presence of two-particle two-hole excitations. The paper contrasts its diagrammatic approach with the more recent work on stationary density functionals obtained from Legendre transforms by Fukuda (1994), Fukuda (1995), Okumura (1996), Valiev and Fernando (1998), Polonyi (2004), and Furnstahl (2009). The formalism enables practical computation of finite nuclei, atoms, and crystals using a static, energy-independent potential, avoiding the need for non-local and energy-dependent mass operators.
