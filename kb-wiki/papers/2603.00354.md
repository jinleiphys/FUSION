# Relativistic Effects in Femtoscopy and Deuteron Formation

> For a long time studies of femtoscopic correlations have provided information about space-time characteristics of particle sources in high-energy collisions. Recently, the correlation functions have been also used to determine interaction parameters of correlated particles which is especially important for short-lived particles, for which scattering experiment are impossible. The abundance of experimental data and their high accuracy require an improved theoretical approach to femtoscopic correlations. We discuss relativistic effects and their role in detail. Since a general relativistic approach is currently unavailable, due to serious theoretical difficulties, the correlation functions must be computed in the center-of-mass frame where the correlated particles are mostly nonrelativistic. This requires transforming the source function to this frame, the consequences of which we discuss. Since the deutron formation has a similar physical origin to femtoscopic correlations, we also discuss relativistic effects in the former process. We illustrate our considerations with calculations of some correlation functions and the deuteron coalescence coefficient to demonstrate a magnitude of relativistic effects. We also argue that a discrepancy between the coalescence coefficient computed with the source radii inferred from the baryon-baryon correlation functions and the experimental data can be removed by taking into account the relativistic elongation of the source radius in the center of mass of correlated particles.

## Key claim

Relativistic effects in femtoscopy are dominated by the Lorentz transformation of the source function to the center-of-mass frame of the correlated particle pair, where the source radius along the direction of pair motion is elongated by the Lorentz factor γ rather than contracted; this effect can resolve the discrepancy between the deuteron coalescence coefficient computed from baryon-baryon correlation functions and experimental data, but a fully general relativistic formalism remains unavailable due to fundamental theoretical difficulties.

## Method

The author starts from the nonrelativistic Koonin formula in the source rest frame and transforms the single-particle source function, parameterized as a Gaussian in space and time, to the pair center-of-mass frame using the Lorentz boost. The effective relative source function is then obtained by integrating over the relative time coordinate, yielding an anisotropic Gaussian whose radius along the direction of pair motion is γ√(R_x² + v²τ²). Correlation functions for Λ-p and p-p are computed using the Lednický-Luboshitz method with the s-wave scattering amplitude, and the deuteron coalescence coefficient is evaluated via the Sato-Yazaki formula, both using the transformed source function.

## Key numbers

- For Λ-p correlations in STAR Au-Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV with protons in p_T ∈ [0.4, 1.1] GeV and y ∈ [-0.5, 0.5], the Lorentz factor γ varies from 1.09 (p_T = 0.4 GeV) to 1.54 (p_T = 1.1 GeV), giving a source radius increase over 40% in the out direction.
- For Λ-p correlations in ALICE p-p collisions at √s_NN = 7 TeV with protons in p_T ∈ [0.5, 4.05] GeV and η ∈ [-0.8, 0.8], γ varies from 1.13 (p_T = 0.5 GeV) to 4.4 (p_T = 4.05 GeV), giving a source radius increase by a factor of 4.
- The source radius inferred from Λ-p correlations assuming an isotropic source in the CM frame was R_s = 3.0 ± 0.7 fm for Au-Au and R_s = 1.13 ± 0.02 fm for p-p collisions.
- Singlet and triplet scattering lengths and effective ranges for Λ-p used in the calculation: a^s = -2.91 fm, d^s = 2.78 fm; a^t = -1.54 fm, d^t = 2.72 fm (from Haidenbauer (2013) chiral effective theory).
- The effective source radius in the CM frame along the direction of motion is γ√(R_x² + v²τ²), where R_x, τ are the source rest-frame parameters.

## Context

This work extends the Lednický-Luboshitz method (Lednicky 1981, Gmitro 1986, Lednicky 2005) by explicitly incorporating the Lorentz transformation of the source function, which had not been fully recognized in previous femtoscopic analyses. It addresses the need for improved theoretical treatment of femtoscopic correlations driven by high-precision experimental data from RHIC and LHC, as reviewed by Fabbietti (2020). The paper also connects to the coalescence model for deuteron production (Butler 1963, Schwarzschild 1963) and the Sato-Yazaki formula (Sato 1981), arguing that the relativistic elongation of the source radius can resolve the discrepancy between coalescence coefficients computed from baryon-baryon correlations and experimental data. The approach enables more accurate extraction of source radii from correlation functions measured over broad ranges of pair total momentum, and provides a framework for future studies of short-lived hadron interactions via femtoscopy.
