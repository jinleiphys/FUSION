# Fresh look at the Hagedorn mass spectrum as seen in the experiments

> The medium dependent finite width is introduced into an exactly solvable model with the general mass-volume spectrum of the QGP bags. The model allows us to estimate the minimal value of the QGP bags' width from the lattice QCD data. The large width of the QGP bags not only explains the observed deficit in the number of hadronic resonances comparing to the Hagedorn mass spectrum, but also clarifies the reason why the heavy QGP bags cannot be directly observed as metastable states in a hadronic phase.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a medium-dependent finite width into an exactly solvable model of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) bags, arguing that a volume-dependent width of the form \(\Gamma(v) = \gamma v^{1/2}\) leads to a "subthreshold suppression" of heavy QGP bags at low temperatures, which explains both the deficit of observed heavy hadronic resonances compared to the Hagedorn spectrum and the non-observation of QGP bags as metastable states in hadronic phase.

## Method

The authors construct a finite width model (FWM) by adding a Gaussian mass attenuation with volume-dependent width \(\Gamma(v)\) to the Hagedorn-like mass-volume spectrum of QGP bags, which also includes surface free energy and hard-core repulsion. They analyze the isobaric partition function and its singularities to derive the QGP bag pressure for two regimes: when the most probable bag mass \(\langle m \rangle\) is positive (yielding pressure \(p^+\)) and when it is negative (yielding pressure \(p^-\)). The model is then matched to lattice QCD data and to the MIT bag model equation of state to estimate the bag width.

## Key numbers

- The true width of QGP bags at zero temperature is estimated as \(\Gamma_R(V_0, T=0) \approx 587\) MeV for a bag of volume \(V_0 \approx 1\) fm\(^3\), using SU(3) color group with two flavors and \(T_c \approx 200\) MeV.
- The true width at the Hagedorn temperature is \(\Gamma_R(V_0, T=T_H) \approx 2034\) MeV, a factor of \(\sqrt{12}\) larger than the zero-temperature value.
- The Hagedorn temperature \(T_H\) is related to the MIT bag constant by \(B_{bag} \equiv \sigma T_H^4\) when reproducing the MIT bag model pressure \(p_{bag} = \sigma T^4 - B_{bag}\).
- The switching temperature between the \(p^+\) and \(p^-\) regimes is given by \(T_\pm = c_\pm T_H\) with \(0 < c_\pm < 1\), determined by the condition \(\gamma^2_\pm = -B/\beta\) where \(\beta = T_H^{-1} - T^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the gas of bags model (GBM) of Kapusta (1981) and Gorenstein (1981), and the QGP bags with surface tension model (QGBSTM) of Bugaev (2007), by introducing a finite, medium-dependent width for QGP bags. It addresses two conceptual problems: the deficit of observed hadronic resonances above 2.5 GeV compared to the Hagedorn spectrum (noted by Broniowski, 2004) and the non-observation of QGP bags in experiments. The model enables a bridge between lattice QCD results, the statistical description of QGP, and the experimentally observed hadronic mass spectrum, and can be generalized to non-zero baryonic densities.
