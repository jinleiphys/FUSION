# Removing Pions from Two-Nucleon Effective Field Theory

> Two-nucleon effective field theory (EFT) beyond momenta of order the pion mass is studied for both cutoff regularization and dimensional regularization with power divergence subtraction (PDS). Models with two mass scales illustrate how effects of long-distance pion physics must be removed from the coefficients that encode short-distance physics. The modified effective range expansion shows that treating pions perturbatively, as in the PDS power counting, limits the radius of convergence of the EFT. Predictions from both regularization schemes with one-pion contributions are compared to data. The breakdown of the effective field theory occurs for momenta of order 300 MeV, even using the modified effective range expansion. This behavior is shown to be consistent with that expected from two-pion contributions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the breakdown of two-nucleon effective field theory (EFT) occurs for momenta of order 300 MeV even when pions are included explicitly, and that this breakdown scale is consistent with expectations from two-pion contributions rather than from implementation artifacts of the regularization scheme.

## Method

The authors study two-nucleon EFT using both cutoff regularization and dimensional regularization with power divergence subtraction (PDS), employing toy models (a delta-shell plus exponential potential and a two-Yukawa model with masses mπ and mρ) to isolate long-distance pion physics from short-distance coefficients. They apply the modified effective range expansion to systematically remove long-distance pion contributions from the low-energy constants, and compare error plots of p cot δ as a function of momentum to assess the radius of convergence for each regularization scheme.

## Key numbers

- The breakdown of the EFT occurs for momenta of order 300 MeV, even using the modified effective range expansion.
- The scale ΛNN = 16π fπ² / (gA² M) ≈ 300 MeV appears as a new scale in the PDS renormalization group analysis.
- For the two-Yukawa model with απ = 0.075 and απ enhanced by a factor of three, the conventional ER fit gives a radius of convergence of about 300 MeV for the optimal cutoff Λc = 400 MeV.
- The modified effective range expansion for the two-Yukawa model yields a radius of convergence between 500 and 600 MeV, comparable to mρ/2.
- For the delta-shell plus exponential model, the conventional ER fit gives a radius of convergence of about 90 MeV, while the modified ER expansion increases it to about 1 GeV.
- The conventional effective range parameters for the two-Yukawa model include as = -25.1 fm, r0 = 1.63 fm (1.2/mπ), while the modified ER parameters give as = -1.72 fm, r0 = 0.667 fm (2.6/mρ).
- The optimal cutoff for the two-Yukawa model with modified ER is Λc = 600 MeV; for the delta-shell plus exponential model it is about 800 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier analysis of regularization schemes for short-range potentials (Steele and Furnstahl, 1997) by adding explicit pion contributions, and addresses open questions about power counting with pions raised by the PDS scheme of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998). The paper shows that the observed breakdown near 300 MeV is not an artifact of implementation but is consistent with two-pion exchange physics, which implies that extending the EFT to momenta near the Fermi momentum of nuclear matter (280 MeV) will require explicit inclusion of correlated two-pion exchange, pion production, or the Δ(1232) resonance.
