# Semi-inclusive charged-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections in the relativistic plane wave impulse approximation

> Neutrino-nucleus quasielastic scattering is studied in the plane wave impulse approximation for three nuclear models: the relativistic Fermi gas (RFG), the independent-particle shell model (IPSM) and the natural orbitals (NO) model with Lorentzian dependence of the excitation energy. A complete study of the kinematics of the semi-inclusive process and the associated cross sections are presented and discussed for 40 Ar and 12 C. Inclusive cross sections are also obtained by integrating the semi-inclusive expressions over the outgoing hadron. Results are consistent with previous studies restricted to the inclusive channel. In particular, a comparison with the analytical results for the RFG model is performed. Explicit expressions for the hadronic tensor and the 10 semi-inclusive nuclear responses are given. Theoretical predictions are compared with semi-inclusive experimental data from T2K experiment.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that semi-inclusive charged-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections computed within the plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA) using three nuclear models (relativistic Fermi gas, independent-particle shell model, and natural orbitals model) are consistent with previous inclusive-only studies, but the authors acknowledge that PWIA provides an oversimplified description of the scattering process and that final state interactions (to be treated in future work with the Relativistic Distorted Wave Impulse Approximation) will break the factorizable form of the cross section.

## Method

The authors computed semi-inclusive charged-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections for the reactions (nu_l, l- p) and (anti-nu_l, l+ n) within the plane wave impulse approximation, where the cross section factorizes into a single-nucleon weak interaction term and a nuclear spectral function. They evaluated the formalism for three nuclear models: the relativistic Fermi gas (RFG), the independent-particle shell model (IPSM), and the natural orbitals (NO) model with a Lorentzian dependence of the excitation energy, using targets of 40Ar and 12C. The flux-averaged semi-inclusive cross sections were obtained by integrating over the neutrino energy distribution from the T2K and DUNE experiments, and inclusive cross sections were recovered by integrating the semi-inclusive expressions over the outgoing nucleon variables.

## Key numbers

* For 40Ar and 12C targets, the semi-inclusive cross sections were computed for kinematics corresponding to T2K (using carbon in the near detector) and DUNE (argon) experiments, with results shown as functions of ejected nucleon momentum and angle.
* The allowed missing momentum range for quasielastic scattering is given by p_m^- <= p_m <= p_m^+ with p_m^+ = |p_N + q| and p_m^- = |p_N - q| (Eq. 2), illustrated for q = 0.5 GeV and omega = 0.06, 0.12, and 0.19 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the formalism developed by Donnelly (2014), Cenni (1997), Donnelly (2017), and Donnelly (2019) for semi-inclusive neutrino-nucleus scattering within the plane wave impulse approximation, and is consistent with previous inclusive studies by Amaro (2005), Caballero (2005), and Caballero (2006). The results are compared with semi-inclusive experimental data from the T2K collaboration (PhysRevD.98.032003, 2018), and the authors plan to extend the study to include final state interactions treated within the Relativistic Distorted Wave Impulse Approximation (RDWIA), building on their past experience with semi-inclusive (e,e'N) reactions.
