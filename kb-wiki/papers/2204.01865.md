# Calculations of $p(n,γ)d$ reaction in chiral effective field theory

> We present a calculation of the radiative capture cross section $p(n,γ)d$ in the low-energy range, where the $M1$ reaction channel dominates. Employing the LENPIC nucleon-nucleon interaction up to the fifth order (N4LO) that is regularized by the semi-local coordinate space regulators, we obtain the initial and final state wave functions, and evaluate the phase shifts of the scattering state and deuteron properties. We derive the transition operator from the chiral effective field theory up to the next-to-next-to leading order (N2LO), where we also regularize the transition operator using regulators consistent with those of the interactions. We compute the capture cross sections and the results show a converging pattern with the chiral-order expansion of the nucleon-nucleon interaction, where the regulator dependence of the results is weak when higher-order nucleon-nucleon interactions are employed. We quantify the uncertainties of the cross-section results due to the chiral-order truncation. The chirally complete and consistent cross-section results are performed up to N2LO and they compare well with the experiments and other theoretical predictions.

## Key claim

The authors present a calculation of the radiative capture cross section for the reaction p(n,γ)d at low center-of-mass energies (≤ 0.01 MeV) using chiral effective field theory, where the M1 transition channel dominates. The results show a converging pattern with the chiral-order expansion of the nucleon-nucleon interaction and weak regulator dependence when higher-order interactions are employed, with chirally complete and consistent cross-section results performed up to N2LO that compare well with experiments and other theoretical predictions.

## Method

The authors employed the LENPIC nucleon-nucleon interaction up to fifth order (N4LO) with semi-local coordinate space regulators (R = 0.9 and 1.0 fm) to construct Hamiltonian matrices for the initial and final nuclear systems. They computed bound and scattering state wave functions via direct matrix diagonalization and the harmonic oscillator representation of scattering equations (HORSE) method, respectively. The M1 transition operator was derived from chiral effective field theory up to next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO), with the two-nucleon current operator regularized using the same regulator function as the interactions, and the capture cross sections were calculated with uncertainty quantification from chiral-order truncation.

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory approach to the p(n,γ)d reaction using the LENPIC nucleon-nucleon interactions, complementing prior work by Piarulli et al. (2012) and Acharya and Bacca (2022) while differing in the microscopic approaches, input NN interactions, choice of regulator schemes, and systematic convergence and uncertainty analyses. The methodology is designed to be generalizable to study more complicated reactions involving complex nuclei through the no-core shell model (NCSM) and the SS-HORSE-NCSM approach, enabling first-principles calculations for nuclear reactions in the energy range of astrophysical interest, particularly for big bang nucleosynthesis.
