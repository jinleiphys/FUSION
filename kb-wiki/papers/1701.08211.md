# A microscopic nucleon spectral function for finite nuclei featuring two- and trhee-nucleon short-range correlations: I. The model vs. ab-initio calculations for the three-nucleon systems

> The main features of the convolution model which, in the region of two-nucleon (2N) and three-nucleon (3N) short-range correlations (SRC), describes the one-nucleon momentum distribution n(k_1) and the spectral function P(k_1,E) in terms of a convolution integral involving the relative n_{rel}(k_{rel}) and the center-of-mass n_{c.m.}(K_{c.m.}) momentum distributions of a nucleon-nucleon pair, are illustrated in detail. It is stressed that the model, which stems from the universal property of factorization exhibited by the nuclear wave function at short inter-nucleon distances, is only applicable in a well-defined region of k_{rel} and K_{c.m.}; it is shown, in particular, that the requirement of factorization introduces a severe constraint on the values of k_{rel} and K_{c.m.} that appear in the convolution integral. Using ab-initio relative and c.m. momentum distributions for ^3He, the validity of the convolution model is investigated by comparing the model spectral function and momentum distributions with the ones resulting from ab-initio calculations performed with realistic local NN interaction of the Argonne family. It is shown that when the constraint on the value of k_{rel} and K_{c.m.} are correctly taken into account and only 2N SRC are taken into account, the convolution model is in good agreement with the ab-initio spectral function in the region of the peak exhibited by the latter, whereas far from the peak, particularly at high values of the removal energy, the model spectral function can be qualitatively reconciled with the ab-initio spectral function only by considering the effects of 3N SRC. As for the nucleon momentum distribution, the effects of 3N SRC appears to be very small.

## Key claim

The convolution model for the spectral function, which relies on the factorization of the nuclear wave function at short inter-nucleon distances, provides a good approximation to the ab-initio spectral function of 3He in the region of the peak when the constraint on the relative and center-of-mass momenta is correctly applied, but it can only be qualitatively reconciled with the ab-initio result at high removal energies by including the effects of three-nucleon short-range correlations.

## Method

The authors constructed a microscopic, parameter-free one-nucleon spectral function using a convolution integral of ab-initio relative and center-of-mass momentum distributions of a nucleon-nucleon pair, derived from realistic local NN interactions of the Argonne family (AV18). They applied this model to the three-nucleon system (3He) and compared the resulting spectral function and momentum distribution to ab-initio calculations that include both the two-body break-up channel (deuteron plus nucleon) and the three-body break-up channel (continuum two-nucleon states). The comparison was performed for a range of momenta and removal energies, with the convolution integral constrained to the region where the wave function factorization property holds (k_rel >= 2 fm^{-1}).

## Key numbers

*   The minimum relative momentum for factorization is approximately k_rel^- ≈ 2 fm^{-1}, with a dependence on the center-of-mass momentum K_cm such that k_rel^-(K_cm) ≈ a + b φ(K_cm), where a ≈ 2 fm^{-1} and φ(0) ≈ 0.
*   The 2N SRC region is defined by k_rel >= 2 fm^{-1} and K_cm ≤ 1 fm^{-1}; the 3N SRC region is defined by k_rel >= 2 fm^{-1} and K_cm > 1 fm^{-1}.
*   The ground-state removal energy for the three-nucleon system is E_min = |E_3| - |E_2| ≈ 5.49 MeV.
*   The comparison between the convolution model and the ab-initio spectral function was performed in the momentum range 2.5 < k < 4 fm^{-1} and removal energy E ≤ 400 MeV.
*   The effects of three-nucleon short-range correlations on the one-nucleon momentum distribution are almost one order of magnitude less than the effect of two-nucleon short-range correlations.

## Context

This work extends the convolution model for the spectral function, first obtained by Ciofi degli Atti (1995), by incorporating ab-initio relative and center-of-mass momentum distributions from realistic NN interactions (AV18) and by properly accounting for the constraint on the integration region imposed by the factorization property. The paper demonstrates that the model, which previously used effective two-nucleon momentum distributions and neglected the constraint, can be improved by including both soft and hard components of the center-of-mass momentum distribution, the latter corresponding to three-nucleon short-range correlations. This enables a parameter-free microscopic description of the spectral function for the three-nucleon system, with the expectation that the model can be extended to complex nuclei.
