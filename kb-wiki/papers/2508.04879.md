# Ab Initio Study of $^7$Li with Coupled $^6$Li + $n$ and $^6$He + $p$ Mass Partitions

> Background: Lithium plays an important role in nuclear astrophysics, fusion energy generation, and nuclear technology. From a theoretical point of view, the nucleus $^7$Li presents a remarkable challenge, as its bound states and resonances can be understood as being formed by a $^4$He and $^3$H pair, or simultaneously, a single neutron/proton coupled to a $^6$Li/$^6$He core. In light of this complexity, a consistent description of $^7$Li bound-state and continuum properties in a unified model presents a significant advancement towards a predictive theory of nuclear structure and reactions. Purpose: Towards achieving such a predictive description, we carry out calculations for $^7$Li within an ab initio framework, taking into account the mass/charge partitions $^6$Li + $n$ and $^6$He + $p$ in a single coupled-channel calculation. This approach allows us to both investigate the effects of the coupling between partitions on the spectrum of $^7$Li, and calculate the cross sections for the $^6$Li($n,p)^6$He and $^6$He($p,n)^6$Li reactions. Method: We use the no-core shell model with continuum, which is capable of describing both bound and scattering states in a unified framework. Results: Our calculation reproduces all the experimentally observed states of $^7$Li in the correct order and predicts new resonances. We also calculated the cross section of the reaction $^6$He($p,n)^6$Li and differential cross sections of the elastic scattering of protons on $^6$He to provide theoretical results for comparison with planned experiments. Conclusions: The overall shape of the cross section of the reaction $^6$Li$(n,p)^6$He as a function of energy is reproduced, although the absolute magnitude is overestimated due to omission of the ($n,α$) reaction channel. A more accurate description of the cross section is achieved by phenomenological adjustmenting the energies of resonances.

## Key claim

The authors reproduce all experimentally observed states of \(^7\)Li in the correct order and predict new resonances by coupling the \(^6\)Li + \(n\) and \(^6\)He + \(p\) mass partitions in a single ab initio calculation, but the absolute magnitude of the \(^6\)Li(\(n,p\))\(^6\)He cross section is overestimated because the \(^4\)He + \(^3\)H partition (and thus the competing (\(n,\alpha\)) channel) is omitted.

## Method

The authors use the no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC), which expands the \(^7\)Li wave function on a basis of NCSM eigenstates of the \(A\)-body system and continuous binary-cluster states of the resonating-group method (RGM) for the \(^6\)Li + \(n\) and \(^6\)He + \(p\) partitions simultaneously. They employ a chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction at N\(^3\)LO softened by a similarity-renormalization-group transformation with flow parameter \(\lambda_{\rm SRG}=2.15\) fm\(^{-1}\) and a harmonic oscillator frequency \(\hbar\Omega=20\) MeV, with basis truncation \(N_{\rm max}\) up to 10 for negative-parity states and up to 11 for positive-parity states of \(^7\)Li. The NCSMC equations are solved using an \(R\)-matrix method on a Lagrange mesh to obtain bound-state energies, phase shifts, resonance parameters, and cross sections.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of \(^6\)Li extrapolated to \(N_{\rm max}\to\infty\): \(-32.04 \pm 0.36\) MeV, compared to the experimental value \(-31.99\) MeV.
- Ground-state energy of \(^7\)Li extrapolated to \(N_{\rm max}\to\infty\): \(-39.74 \pm 0.61\) MeV, compared to the experimental value \(-39.25\) MeV.
- NCSMC energy of the \(3/2^-1/2_1\) bound state of \(^7\)Li relative to the \(^6\)Li + \(n\) threshold: \(-7.38\) MeV (experimental: \(-7.25\) MeV).
- NCSMC energy of the \(1/2^-1/2_1\) bound state of \(^7\)Li relative to the \(^6\)Li + \(n\) threshold: \(-7.05\) MeV (experimental: \(-6.77\) MeV).
- Width of the \(5/2^-1/2_2\) resonance in the coupled-partition calculation: 0.2 MeV (experimental: 0.08 MeV).
- Width of the \(3/2^-1/2_2\) resonance in the coupled-partition calculation: 1.9 MeV (experimental: 4.71 MeV).
- Width of the \(1/2^-1/2_2\) resonance in the coupled-partition calculation: 2.7 MeV (experimental: 2.75 MeV).
- Width of the \(7/2^-1/2_2\) resonance in the coupled-partition calculation: 0.04 MeV (experimental: 0.44 MeV).
- Width of the \(3/2^-3/2_1\) resonance in the coupled-partition calculation: 0.4 MeV (experimental: 0.26 MeV).
- Energy of the \(^6\)He + \(p\) threshold obtained in the calculation: 3.08 MeV above the \(^6\)Li + \(n\) threshold.
- Predicted \(1/2^+1/2_1\) resonance energy relative to the \(^6\)Li + \(n\) threshold: 3.24 MeV, with width 1.0 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier NCSMC calculations of Vorabbi et al. (2019), which treated the \(^6\)Li + \(n\), \(^6\)He + \(p\), and \(^4\)He + \(^3\)H partitions in separate calculations, by coupling the \(^6\)Li + \(n\) and \(^6\)He + \(p\) partitions in a single calculation to investigate coupling effects and compute charge-exchange reaction cross sections. The results address a discrepancy noted by Dronchi et al. (2023), who searched for an \(S\)-wave \(1/2^+\) resonance in the \(^6\)He + \(p\) channel predicted by Vorabbi et al. (2019) but did not observe it; the present coupled calculation shows that this resonance is dominated by the \(^6\)Li + \(n\) channel, which may explain the null experimental result. The predicted cross sections for \(^6\)He(\(p,n\))\(^6\)Li and differential cross sections for elastic scattering of protons on \(^6\)He provide theoretical benchmarks for planned experiments at TRIUMF.
