# Widths of $Ξ$ Hypernuclear States

> The $Λ$ and neutron decay widths of $Ξ$ hypernuclear states, based on calculated $ΞN \to \lala$ mixing amplitudes, are estimated. The widths which result from using the Nijmegen Model D interaction are sufficiently small, of order 1.5 MeV, that experiments to observe $Ξ$ hypernuclear states using the $(K^-,K^+)$ reaction may be feasible.

## Key claim

The authors estimate that the total width of Ξ hypernuclear states produced via the (K⁻,K⁺) reaction on ¹²C is about 1.5 MeV, which is small enough to make experimental observation feasible, but they note that this result depends quadratically on the strength of the ΞN → ΛΛ conversion potential and could differ considerably for different baryon-baryon interaction models.

## Method

The authors performed a microscopic shell-model calculation of the mixing between Ξ⁻ and ΛΛ hypernuclear states via the ΞN → ΛΛ conversion interaction, using the Nijmegen Model D potential parametrized as a Gaussian with V₀ = 57.872 MeV and μ = 0.855 fm. They computed mixing amplitudes in first-order perturbation theory for the ¹²C(K⁻,K⁺)¹²_ΞBe reaction, then calculated spectroscopic factors for Λ and neutron emission channels and estimated partial decay widths using single-particle widths from a square-well potential with a reflection factor.

## Key numbers

- Total width of the 1⁻, 0s Ξ⁻ state in ¹²_ΞBe: approximately 1.5 MeV (sum of partial widths)
- Partial width for p-wave Λ emission from the 0s Ξ⁻ state: 398 keV
- Partial width for p-wave neutron emission from the 0s Ξ⁻ state: 622 keV
- Partial width for nucleonic decay from s²_Λ admixtures: 174 keV (155 keV neutron + 19 keV proton)
- Two-body matrix element I₀ for the Gaussian conversion potential: 2.39 MeV (for oscillator length b = 1.64 fm)
- Predicted 0° cross sections for ¹²C(K⁻,K⁺)¹²_ΞBe at p_K = 1.6 GeV/c: 0.25 μb/sr for the 1⁻ ground-state peak and 0.50 μb/sr for the 0⁺,2⁺ complex of p_Ξ states
- Binding energies of Ξ⁻ in a Woods-Saxon well (V_Ξ = 24 MeV, r₀ = 1.1 fm, a = 0.65 fm): 10.7 MeV for 0s state, 1.5 MeV for 0p state
- Q-value for Ξ⁻p → ΛΛ conversion: 28.3 MeV
- Q-value for Ξ⁰n → ΛΛ conversion: 23.2 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier estimates of Ξ hypernuclear decay widths for a ¹⁵N target (Millener, Dover, and Gal, 1994) to the more practical ¹²C target case, and compares with calculations by Ikeda et al. (1994) who used a δ-function interaction also based on Nijmegen Model D. The results suggest that the (K⁻,K⁺) reaction at the Brookhaven AGS D6 beamline, with a flux of 2.8×10⁶ K⁻ per spill, could observe bound Ξ hypernuclear states if the natural widths are indeed around 1.5 MeV, enabling strong constraints on baryon-baryon interaction models in the S = -2 sector.
