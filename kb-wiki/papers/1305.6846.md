# Two-loop perturbative corrections to the thermal effective potential in gluodynamics

> The thermodynamics of pure glue theories can be described in terms of an effective action for the Polyakov loop. This effective action is of the Landau-Ginzburg type and its variables are the angles parametrizing the loop. In this paper we compute perturbative corrections to this action. Remarkably, two-loop corrections turn out to be proportional to the one-loop action, independent of the eigenvalues of the loop. By a straightforward generalization of the 't Hooft coupling this surprisingly simple result holds for any of the classical and exceptional groups.

## Key claim

Two-loop perturbative corrections to the effective potential for the Polyakov loop in gluodynamics are proportional to the one-loop action, independent of the eigenvalues of the loop, for all classical and exceptional gauge groups.

## Method

The authors compute the constrained effective action for the Polyakov loop by expanding around a constant background field in covariant background gauge. They evaluate the two-loop free-energy diagrams and the insertion diagram arising from the nonlinear constraint that fixes the Polyakov loop eigenvalues, then show that the gauge-parameter dependence cancels between these contributions. The calculation is performed for SU(N), SO(2N), SO(2N+1), Sp(2N), and the exceptional group G(2) using dimensional regularization.

## Key numbers

* The two-loop effective potential relative to the one-loop result is \(\frac{\G^{(2)}}{\G^{(1)}} = -\frac{5 g^2 C_2(A)}{16\pi^2}\), where \(C_2(A)\) is the quadratic Casimir invariant in the adjoint representation.
* For SU(N) groups, \(C_2(A) = N\); for SO(2N), \(C_2(A) = N-1\); for SO(2N+1), \(C_2(A) = N-\frac{1}{2}\); for Sp(2N), \(C_2(A) = N+1\); for G(2), \(C_2(A) = 2\).
* The one-loop effective potential for SU(2) is \({\cal V}_{pert}(q) = -\frac{\pi^2}{15}T^4 + \frac{4\pi^2}{3}T^4 q^2(1-q)^2\), where \(q\) is the eigenphase modulo 1.

## Context

This work extends perturbative calculations of the Polyakov loop effective action beyond one-loop order, which were previously known only for straight paths from the origin to Z(N) minima in SU(N) (Bhattacharya 1992, Giovannangeli 2002). The result shows that the eigenvalue distribution from perturbation theory is not affected by two-loop corrections, though the pressure and energy density are modified. The authors note that when nonperturbative contributions are added (Dumitru 2010, Dumitru 2012), the two-loop corrections do modify the total result, and they postpone a detailed fit to lattice results to a future publication.
