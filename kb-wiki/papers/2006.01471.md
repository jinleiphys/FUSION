# Symmetry energy properties of neutron-rich nuclei from the coherent density fluctuation model applied to nuclear matter calculations with Bonn potentials

> We derive the values of nuclear symmetry energy, its components, as well as pressure in finite nuclei at saturation density from their corresponding values in nuclear matter obtained in non-relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations with the realistic Bonn B and Bonn CD potentials using the coherent density fluctuation model in the framework of a self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock plus BCS method. We focus on three isotopic chains of spherical nuclei (Ni, Sn, and Pb) and compare our results with those obtained with an effective Brueckner density-dependent potential. The role of the three-body forces on the considered quantities is also studied and discussed.

## Key claim

The nuclear symmetry energy, its volume and surface components, and pressure in finite spherical nuclei (Ni, Sn, Pb) can be derived from non-relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations in nuclear matter using realistic Bonn B and Bonn CD potentials, but the results depend on the choice of the relation between the symmetry energy and its volume and surface components in the droplet model, and the values for the volume and surface contributions differ from those obtained in previous work using a different form of that relation.

## Method

The authors performed non-relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations of symmetric and pure neutron matter using Bonn B and Bonn CD two-body potentials to obtain the density-dependent symmetry energy and pressure in nuclear matter. They then applied the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM) to translate these nuclear matter quantities into corresponding values for finite nuclei, using nuclear density distributions from self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock plus BCS calculations with SLy4, Sk3, and SGII Skyrme forces for isotopic chains of even-even Ni (A=74-84), Sn (A=124-152), and Pb (A=206-214) nuclei. The volume and surface components of the symmetry energy were extracted using the droplet model relation from Steiner (2005), which differs from the relation used in earlier work by Danielewicz (2003, 2004, 2006).

## Key numbers

- The symmetry energy in nuclear matter calculated with Bonn B potential is slightly smaller than that from the Brueckner energy-density functional for densities below about 0.25 fm^-3.
- The saturation point for nuclear matter with Bonn B potential including three-body forces is (0.17 fm^-3, -15.9 MeV) from Wang (2014); for Bonn CD including three-body forces it is (0.185 fm^-3, -15.5 MeV) from Baldo (2008a).
- The symmetry energy from the Bonn CD potential in the in-medium T-matrix approach is 30-32 MeV from Soma (2008).
- The experimental symmetry energy constraint is given as S = 32 +/- 6 MeV from Soma (2008).
- The weight function |F(x)|^2 for 78Ni has its maximum at a density of 0.05 fm^-3, with the relevant density range for finite nuclei calculations being approximately 0.01 to 0.12 fm^-3.

## Context

This work extends previous CDFM studies by Gaidarov (2011, 2012, 2014) and Antonov (2016, 2017, 2018) that used the Brueckner energy-density functional or Skyrme functionals, by instead employing realistic Bonn B and Bonn CD potentials from Machleidt (1989, 2001) in the nuclear matter input. The results are compared with those from the effective Brueckner density-dependent potential of Brueckner (1968, 1969) and with empirical constraints from isobaric analog states and neutron skin thickness from Danielewicz (2014), Roca-Maza (2013), and Zhang (2013). The study enables future work on the role of three-body forces in determining symmetry energy properties of finite nuclei and provides a bridge between realistic nuclear matter calculations and finite-nucleus observables.
