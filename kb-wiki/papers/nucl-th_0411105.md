# Inclusion of non-spherical components of the Pauli blocking operator in (p,p') reactions

> We present the first calculations of proton elastic and inelastic scattering in which the Pauli blocking operator contains the leading non-spherical components as well as the usual spherical (angle-averaged) part. We develop a formalism for including the contributions to the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction from the resulting new G-matrix elements that extend the usual two-nucleon spin structure and may not conserve angular momentum. We explore the consequences of parity conservation, time reversal invariance, and nucleon-nucleon antisymmetrization for the new effective interaction. Changes to the calculated cross section and spin observables are small in the energy range from 100 to 200 MeV.

## Key claim

The first calculations of proton elastic and inelastic scattering that include non-spherical components of the Pauli blocking operator produce changes to cross sections and spin observables that are small compared to typical medium effects in the energy range from 100 to 200 MeV, and the spherically-averaged treatment of the Pauli projection operator is adequate for intermediate-energy proton-nucleus scattering at the level of present theoretical accuracy.

## Method

The authors solved the Bethe-Goldstone equation for the G-matrix in nuclear matter using a modified Bonn-B potential, retaining the leading non-spherical (L=2) components of the Pauli blocking operator in addition to the usual spherical (L=0) angle-averaged part. They expanded the resulting G-matrix elements in a multipole series in L (where L recouples J to J'), truncated to L<4, and transformed these to coordinate-space NN scattering amplitudes using a sum of Yukawa functions. The effective interaction was then used in distorted-wave calculations for proton elastic scattering and inelastic transitions to natural-parity excited states at 100 and 200 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The non-spherical Pauli operator calculations were performed at beam energies of 100 and 200 MeV for the 3^- state at 3.736 MeV in 40Ca.
- At 200 MeV, the differences between spherical and non-spherical Pauli treatments were "no more than a small fraction of the changes" produced by including BHF or DBHF medium effects (no specific numerical difference given for the 200 MeV case).
- At 100 MeV, the differences between spherical and non-spherical treatments were larger than at 200 MeV but "still remain smaller than the changes associated with the baseline medium effects or a DBHF treatment."
- The G-matrix elements were generated from a free-space NN interaction that reproduces Nijmegen phase shift analysis results up to 325 MeV.
- Partial waves with J,J' > 6 were calculated using the usual angle-average approximation, and no partial waves with J > 15 were included.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of non-spherical Pauli blocking effects on nuclear binding energies (Refs. r8, r9) and on the M-dependence of G-matrix elements (Ref. r10) by implementing these effects for the first time in actual scattering calculations. The authors compare their results to standard Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations with spherical Pauli blocking and to Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) calculations that include relativistic mean-field effects. The work enables future studies to determine whether non-spherical Pauli effects might become significant at lower energies or for observables not examined here, and establishes that the spherical approximation is adequate for intermediate-energy proton-nucleus scattering given current theoretical uncertainties in the DWIA.
