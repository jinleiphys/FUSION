# The Changes in Properties of Hadrons in Nuclei and Nuclear Matter

> The changes of hadron properties in dense and/or hot matter are discussed in terms of effective chiral Lagrangians with the parameters of the theory scaled in a simple way. The phenomenologically successful Walecka model is identified as a mean field chiral Lagrangian with the scaled parameters. Kaon condensation and chiral restoration transitions can be described within the same mean field framework.

## Key claim
Hadron properties in dense and/or hot matter change according to a simple scaling rule (Brown-Rho scaling) where masses of baryons, vector mesons, and the pion decay constant all scale approximately together, but this "universal scaling" may not be strictly valid beyond mean field, and in finite nuclei the nucleon mass may scale somewhat faster than the pion decay constant.

## Method
The paper uses effective chiral Lagrangians with parameters scaled by density, identifying the Walecka mean-field model as a mean-field chiral Lagrangian with Brown-Rho scaling. The approach is applied to the thermal neutron capture process n + p → d + γ calculated to order Q³ in chiral perturbation theory, and extended to dense matter by replacing free-space hadron parameters with density-dependent starred quantities. The kaon-nuclear potential is derived from the same chiral Lagrangian framework and related to the nucleon-nuclear potential through the scaling relations.

## Key numbers
- Thermal neutron capture cross section σ = (334 ± 3) mb, compared to experimental value (334.2 ± 0.5) mb
- At nuclear matter density, m_N^★/m_N ≈ 0.75, giving axial charge enhancement factor ε_MEC ≈ 2
- Pion decay constant scaling at nuclear matter density: f_π^★/f_π ≈ 0.77
- Compressibility modulus K_0 ≈ 200 MeV obtained with anomalous dimension d_a ≈ 2.7
- Kaon-nuclear potential at ρ ≈ 0.97ρ_0: S_K⁻ + V_K⁻ = -200 ± 20 MeV (from K-mesic atom analysis)
- Predicted kaon condensation density: ρ_c ∼ 2ρ_0
- QCD sum rule prediction for vector meson mass at ρ ≈ ρ_0: m_ρ^★/m_ρ = 0.78 ± 0.08

## Context
This work extends the Brown-Rho scaling hypothesis (Brown and Rho, 1991) and connects it to the phenomenologically successful Walecka mean-field model of nuclear matter. The paper shows that the Walecka model can be identified as a mean-field chiral Lagrangian with scaled parameters, enabling a unified description of normal nuclear matter, kaon condensation, and chiral restoration transitions. The approach explains the CERES collaboration dilepton data (Li, Ko, and Brown) and provides a framework for studying phase transitions in heavy ion collisions, while also addressing the known defect of the Walecka model (excessively high compressibility modulus) through higher-loop corrections.
