# A Nonequilibrium Information Entropy Approach to Ternary Fission of Actinides

> Ternary fission of actinides probes the state of the nucleus at scission. Light clusters are produced in space and time very close to the scission point. Within the nonequilibrium statistical operator method, a generalized Gibbs distribution is constructed from the information given by the observed yields of isotopes. Using this relevant statistical operator, yields are calculated taking excited states and continuum correlations into account, in accordance with the virial expansion of the equation of state. Clusters with mass number $A \le 10$ are well described using the nonequilibrium generalizations of temperature and chemical potentials. Improving the virial expansion, in-medium effects may become of importance in determining the contribution of weakly bound states and continuum correlations to the intrinsic partition function. Yields of larger clusters, which fail to reach this quasi-equilibrium form of the relevant distribution, are described by nucleation kinetics, and a saddle-to-scission relaxation time of about 7000 fm/c is inferred. Light charged particle emission, described by reaction kinetics and virial expansions, may therefore be regarded as a very important tool to probe the nonequilibrium time evolution of actinide nuclei during fission.

## Key claim

The observed yields of light clusters (A ≤ 10) from ternary fission can be described by a nonequilibrium generalization of the Gibbs distribution using temperature and chemical potentials as Lagrange parameters, but only when continuum correlations are included via a virial expansion; for larger clusters the quasi-equilibrium description fails and nucleation kinetics with a saddle-to-scission relaxation time of about 7000 fm/c is required.

## Method

The authors applied the nonequilibrium statistical operator method to construct a generalized Gibbs distribution from the observed yields of isotopes produced in ternary fission of several actinides (²³³U, ²³⁵U, ²³⁹Pu, ²⁴¹Pu, ²⁴⁸Cm, ²⁵²Cf). They calculated yields using different approximations for the nuclear Hamiltonian: a final-state distribution considering only observed stable nuclei, a relevant distribution including excited bound states with gamma decay, a virial expansion incorporating continuum correlations via the Beth-Uhlenbeck formula, and an effective distribution accounting for in-medium effects such as Pauli blocking. For clusters with mass number A > 10 that fail to reach quasi-equilibrium, nucleation kinetics was employed.

## Context

This work extends the nonequilibrium statistical operator approach previously applied to ²⁵²Cf(sf) by Röpke et al. (2020) to five additional actinide systems (²³³U, ²³⁵U, ²³⁹Pu, ²⁴¹Pu, ²⁴⁸Cm). It improves upon earlier interpolation formulas by Valskii and the nuclear statistical equilibrium (NSE) approach by incorporating continuum correlations through the Beth-Uhlenbeck formula and in-medium effects. The results enable a systematic probe of the nonequilibrium time evolution of actinide nuclei during fission, with light charged particle emission serving as a tool to extract the saddle-to-scission relaxation time. The method also identifies where medium effects (Pauli blocking) are most important, particularly for weakly bound states like ⁶He, ¹¹Li, and unbound states like ⁴H, ⁵He, ⁷He, ⁹He.
