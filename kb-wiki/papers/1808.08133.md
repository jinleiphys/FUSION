# Density Renormalization Group for Classical Liquids

> We study response of liquid to a scale transformation, which generates a change of the liquid density, and obtain a set of differential equations for correlation functions. The set of equations, which we call density renormalization group equations (DRGEs), is similar to the BBGKY hierarchy as it relates different multiple-point correlation functions. In particular, we derive DRGEs for one-particle irreducible vertex functions of liquid by performing Legendre transformations, which enables us to calculate properties of liquid at higher density in terms of correlation functions at lower density.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a set of differential equations, called density renormalization group equations (DRGEs), that describe how correlation functions of a classical liquid change as the liquid density increases; the key condition is that this formalism is expected to be useful in the normal state at higher density rather than near the critical point, unlike the Wilsonian RG approach of Parola, Reatto, and Caillol which is powerful near the critical point.

## Method

The authors study the response of a classical liquid to a scale transformation of the two-body potential, which generates a change in the liquid density, and derive a set of partial differential equations for the density correlation functions. They use the field-theoretic method of Hubbard and Schofield to evaluate the perturbative corrections to the correlation functions due to the change in the potential, expressing these corrections in terms of higher-body correlation functions. They then perform Legendre transformations to obtain DRGEs for one-particle irreducible (1PI) vertex functions, which correspond to the Helmholtz free energy.

## Key numbers

*   For the hard-sphere potential, the virial expansion for pressure is given as \( \frac{p}{nT} = 1 + 4\eta + 10\eta^2 + 18.36\eta^3 + 28.22\eta^4 + \cdots \), where \(\eta\) is the packing fraction; at \(\eta=0.3\), the fifth term contributes \(\sim 0.37\).
*   The isothermal compressibility \(\kappa_T\) is related to the two-point correlation function by \(\kappa_T = \frac{1}{n} \int d^dy F_2(x,y)\) and can be rewritten as \(\kappa_T = 1 + n \int d^dx h_2(x,0)\), where \(h_2\) is the total correlation function.
*   The zero-point function correction (grand potential) is given by \(\Delta F_0 = -\frac{1}{2!} \int \frac{d^dp}{(2\pi)^d} \beta \delta \tilde{v}(p) \tilde{F}_1(p) \tilde{F}_1(-p) - \frac{1}{2!} \int \frac{d^dp}{(2\pi)^d} \beta \delta \tilde{v}(p) \tilde{F}_2(p,-p)\).

## Context

This work extends the analogy between classical liquid theory and quantum field theory, strongly stimulated by Nambu's seminar paper on the analogy between the renormalization group equation in gauge theories and thermodynamic equations of state. It differs from the hierarchical equations of Parola, Reatto, and Caillol, which are obtained by gradually including the attractive part of the interaction potential as an IR cutoff is changed; the present formalism instead gradually includes the effects of liquid density, making it useful for high-density normal states rather than near the critical point. The DRGEs are similar to the BBGKY hierarchy in that they relate different multiple-point correlation functions, and the approach enables the calculation of liquid properties at higher density from correlation functions at lower density.
