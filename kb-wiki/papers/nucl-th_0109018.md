# A Study of Parton Energy Loss in Au+Au Collisions at RHIC using Transport Theory

> Parton energy loss in Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies is studied by numerically solving the relativistic Boltzmann equation for the partons including $2 \leftrightarrow 2$ and $2 \to 2 + final state radiation$ collision processes. Final particle spectra are obtained using two hadronization models; the Lund string fragmentation and independent fragmentation models. Recent, preliminary $π^0$ transverse momentum distributions from central Au+Au collisions at RHIC are reproduced using gluon-gluon scattering cross sections of 5-12 mb, depending upon the hadronization model. Comparisons with the HIJING jet quenching algorithm are made.

## Key claim

The paper reproduces preliminary RHIC π⁰ transverse momentum distributions using a transport-theory approach with gluon-gluon scattering cross sections of 5–12 mb, but the required cross section depends on which hadronization model (Lund string fragmentation or independent fragmentation) is used, meaning the extracted parton energy loss is not uniquely determined.

## Method

The authors numerically solve the relativistic Boltzmann equation for partons in Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 130 GeV, including both 2 ↔ 2 elastic processes and 2 → 2 + final state radiation (modeled via PYTHIA time-like branching with angular ordering). Initial parton distributions are taken from the HIJING event generator (≈190 gluons at mid-rapidity), and the parton system is evolved using a full-ensemble Monte Carlo method with N_test = 6 test particles. Final hadron spectra are obtained by two hadronization schemes: the Lund string fragmentation model (preserving color connections) and the independent fragmentation model (fragmenting each parton separately).

## Key numbers

- Initial gluon rapidity density from HIJING without jet quenching: dN/dy_(g,y=0) = 190 for central Au+Au at √s_NN = 130 GeV.
- After dynamical evolution, gluon multiplicity at mid-rapidity becomes dN/dy_(g,y=0) = 210 for K=1.0 and dN/dy_(g,y=0) = 250 for K=2.5; HIJING with jet quenching (dE/dz = 2.0 GeV/fm) yields 280 gluons.
- Parton transverse energy at mid-rapidity is reduced by ≈13 GeV (K=1.0) and ≈27 GeV (K=2.5) after rescattering.
- Gluon-gluon cross section for K=1.0: σ_gg ≈ 5.3 mb; for K=2.5: σ_gg ≈ 13.3 mb (2.5 times the K=1.0 value).
- The π⁰ data at p_T > 2 GeV/c are reproduced using K=2.5 with Lund string fragmentation, and using K=1.0 with independent fragmentation.
- HIJING with jet quenching reproduces the data for dE/dz = 0.25–2.0 GeV/fm.
- Total hadron yields near mid-rapidity change by approximately −4% (Lund) and +2% (independent fragmentation) relative to HIJING without jet quenching.

## Context

This work extends earlier jet-quenching studies that used modified fragmentation functions (Wang 2001) or constant energy-loss assumptions (Levai, Quark Matter 2001) by embedding energy loss in a full transport-theory framework with dynamical parton scatterings. It contrasts with the HIJING jet-quenching algorithm (Wang 1991), which uses a fixed dE/dz and produces a much stronger rise of multiplicity with energy than observed by PHOBOS (2001). The approach provides a maximum incoherent bound for energy loss compared to the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect, and the authors note that future calculations should include hadron-gas stage effects and explore other initial conditions (e.g., from saturation models).
