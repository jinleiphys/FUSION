# Effective relativistic mean field model for finite nuclei and neutron stars

> New Relativistic mean field parameter set IOPB-I has been developed.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a new relativistic mean field parameter set, IOPB-I, which is optimized to simultaneously describe finite nuclei properties and neutron star observables, but the authors note that while IOPB-I gives a larger neutron star mass (2.15 solar masses) and radius (11.83 km) than the G3 set, the G3 set is more compatible with the observational limit of approximately 2.0 solar masses.

## Method

The authors constructed the IOPB-I parameter set within the effective field theory motivated relativistic mean field (ERMF) model, which includes contributions from the delta-meson to lowest order and cross-coupling between omega and rho mesons. They optimized the energy density functional using the simulated annealing method, fitting to experimental binding energies and charge radii of eight spherical nuclei (O-16, Ca-40, Ca-48, Ni-68, Zr-90, Sn-100, Sn-132, and Pb-208) with additional constraints on nuclear matter properties at saturation density. They then compared the equation of state for pure neutron matter at sub-saturation densities and calculated neutron star mass-radius relations for the IOPB-I, NL3, FSU2R, and G3 parameter sets.

## Key numbers

- IOPB-I saturation density: 0.149 fm^-3, with binding energy per nucleon -16.10 MeV
- IOPB-I incompressibility coefficient: 222.6 MeV
- IOPB-I symmetry energy J: 33.30 MeV, slope of symmetry energy L: 63.6 MeV
- IOPB-I effective mass ratio M*/M: 0.593
- IOPB-I maximum neutron star mass: 2.15 solar masses, radius 11.83 km
- G3 maximum neutron star mass: 1.997 solar masses, radius 10.82 km
- NL3 symmetry energy J: 37.40 MeV, slope L: 118.6 MeV
- FSU2R symmetry energy J: 30.7 MeV, slope L: 55.7 MeV

## Context

This work extends the line of relativistic mean field parameterizations such as NL3 (Lalazissis et al., 1997), FSU2R (Chen and Piekarewicz, 2015), and G3 (Kumar et al., 2015) by introducing a new set that balances finite-nuclei fits with neutron star constraints. The IOPB-I set enables future studies of neutron star structure and nuclear matter properties at supra-normal densities, though the authors note that the G3 set better matches the observational limit of approximately 2.0 solar masses for maximum neutron star mass.
