# Two Cold Atoms in a Time-Dependent Harmonic Trap in One Dimension

> We analyze the dynamics of two atoms with a short-ranged pair interaction in a one-dimensional harmonic trap with time-dependent frequency. Our analysis is focused on two representative cases: (i) a sudden change of the trapping frequency from one value to another, and (ii) a periodic trapping frequency. In case (i), the dynamics of the interacting and the corresponding non-interacting systems turn out to be similar. In the second case, however, the interacting system can behave quite differently, especially close to parametric resonance. For instance, in the regions where such resonance occurs we find that the interaction can significantly reduce the rate of energy increase. The implications for applications of our findings to cool or heat the system are also dicussed.

## Key claim

The dynamics of two interacting atoms in a time-dependent harmonic trap can be similar to the non-interacting case for a sudden frequency change, but can differ significantly for periodic driving, especially near parametric resonance where the interaction can reduce the rate of energy increase, though this effect depends on the driving strength and the specific resonance conditions.

## Method

The authors solve the time-dependent Schrödinger equation for two particles with a delta-function interaction in a one-dimensional harmonic trap with time-dependent frequency, decoupling center-of-mass and relative motion. For the non-interacting case, they use a scale transformation to map the time-dependent oscillator to a stationary one, obtaining analytic propagators and wave functions. For the interacting case, they expand the wave function in the eigenbasis of the stationary Hamiltonian and solve the resulting linear system of differential equations numerically, focusing on two scenarios: an exponential frequency change and a periodic (sinusoidal) frequency modulation.

## Context

This work extends studies of few-body systems in stationary traps (reviewed by Zinner, 2015) to time-dependent traps, complementing earlier work on time-dependent systems by Castin (1996), Kagan (1996), and others. The periodic driving scenario bears similarity to quantum control studies by Freyberger (2014) and relates to parametric heating/cooling experiments by Modugno (2002) and Yabuzaki (2003). The results suggest that parametric excitations could enable forced evaporative cooling in ideal harmonic traps, though many-body investigations are needed for quantitative predictions.
