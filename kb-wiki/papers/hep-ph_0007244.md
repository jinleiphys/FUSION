# Dynamics of the chiral phase transition at finite chemical potential

> We study the dynamics of the chiral phase transition at finite chemical potential in the Gross-Neveu model in the leading order in large-N approximation. We consider evolutions starting in local thermal equilibrium in the massless unbroken phase for conditions pertaining to traversing a first or second order phase transition. We assume boost invariant kinematics and determine the evolution of the the order parameter $σ$, the energy density and pressure as well as the effective temperature, chemical potential and interpolating number densities as a function of $τ$.

## Key claim

The dynamics of the chiral phase transition at finite chemical potential in the Gross-Neveu model, studied in the leading order of the large-N approximation, show that the order parameter sigma exhibits a sharp transition from the unbroken to the broken symmetry mode for both first and second order transitions, but the chemical potential falls faster than tau^{-1} for the first order transition while following tau^{-1} for the second order transition, with the caveat that these results depend on assuming boost invariant kinematics and initial local thermal equilibrium in the massless unbroken phase.

## Method

The authors solved the Dirac equation with a self-consistently determined mass term (gap equation) in the Gross-Neveu model using the leading order large-N approximation, assuming 1+1 dimensional boost invariant expansion along the beam axis. They introduced conformal time u and expanded fermion fields in Fourier modes, then solved the coupled mode equations and gap equation numerically with adiabatic initial conditions. The initial state was taken as Fermi-Dirac distributions with given temperature T0 and chemical potential mu0, and the evolution of the order parameter sigma, energy density, pressure, effective temperature, and chemical potential was determined as a function of proper time tau.

## Key numbers

- Tricritical point occurs at mu_c / m_f = 0.608 and T_c / m_f = 0.318
- In the true vacuum (sigma = m_f) at T=0, the energy density has value epsilon / m_f^2 = -1/(4 pi)
- Initial proper time was chosen as tau_0 = 1 / m_f
- The initial symmetry breaking seed was set to tsigma_dot(0) = 10^{-3}
- Before the phase transition, the equation of state p = epsilon leads to scaling epsilon / epsilon_0 = (tau_0 / tau)^2 and T / T_0 = tau_0 / tau
- After the phase transition, the energy density oscillates around the true broken symmetry value epsilon_0 = -1/(4 pi)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of a model with similar phase structure to QCD (Chodos et al., 2000) by focusing on the special limit without a superconducting phase, which reduces to the Gross-Neveu model. The phase structure of the Gross-Neveu model at finite temperature and chemical potential had been known previously (Gross and Neveu, 1974; Wolff, 1985). The boost invariant kinematics approximation follows Bjorken (1983) and was previously applied to disoriented chiral condensates by Cooper et al. (1995). This work enables future studies of correlation functions and the effects of a bare mass, as well as inclusion of hard scattering effects that would damp the oscillations of the order parameter after the phase transition.
