# Dense Matter in Neutron Stars with eXTP

> In this White Paper, we present the potential of the enhanced X-ray Timing and Polarimetry (eXTP) mission to constrain the equation of state of dense matter in neutron stars, exploring regimes not directly accessible to terrestrial experiments. By observing a diverse population of neutron stars - including isolated objects, X-ray bursters, and accreting systems - eXTP's unique combination of timing, spectroscopy, and polarimetry enables high-precision measurements of compactness, spin, surface temperature, polarimetric signals, and timing irregularity. These multifaceted observations, combined with advances in theoretical modeling, pave the way toward a comprehensive description of the properties and phases of dense matter from the crust to the core of neutron stars. Under development by an international Consortium led by the Institute of High Energy Physics of the Chinese Academy of Sciences, the eXTP mission is planned to be launched in early 2030.

## Key claim

The enhanced X-ray Timing and Polarimetry (eXTP) mission, planned for launch in early 2030, will constrain the equation of state of dense matter in neutron stars by combining timing, spectroscopy, and polarimetry observations across a diverse population of neutron stars, but the paper is a White Paper describing potential capabilities rather than presenting new observational results.

## Method

The paper reviews how eXTP's three main instruments, the Spectroscopic Focusing Array (SFA) for high-throughput spectroscopy, the Polarimetry Focusing Array (PFA) for sensitive X-ray polarimetry, and the Wide-band and Wide-field Camera (W2C) for transient monitoring, will enable measurements of neutron star compactness, spin, surface temperature, polarimetric signals, and timing irregularity. The authors outline observational techniques including pulse profile modeling, X-ray burst spectroscopy, and phase-resolved polarimetry, combined with theoretical modeling approaches such as chiral effective field theory, perturbative QCD, energy-density functional theory, and holographic QCD.

## Key numbers

- SFA total effective area: 2750 cm² at 1.5 keV and 1670 cm² at 6 keV
- SFA spectral resolution: approximately 180 eV at 1.5 keV
- SFA time resolution: 10 microseconds
- SFA overall sensitivity: approximately 3.3 × 10⁻¹⁵ erg cm⁻² s⁻¹ for a 1 Ms exposure
- PFA total effective area: 250 cm² at 3 keV
- PFA minimum detectable polarization (MDP₉₉): about 2% in 1 Ms for a milliCrab-like source
- W2C sensitivity: 4 × 10⁻⁷ erg cm⁻² s⁻¹ (1 s exposure) across 10–600 keV
- W2C angular resolution: 20 arcminutes
- W2C energy resolution: better than 30% at 60 keV
- SFA angular resolution: ≤ 1 arcminute (HPD) with an 18 arcminute field of view
- PFA angular resolution: better than 30 arcseconds (HPD) with a 9.8 × 9.8 arcminute field of view

## Context

This White Paper extends the planning work for the eXTP mission, which builds on heritage from the Rossi X-ray Timing Explorer (RXTE) and the Neutron star Interior Composition Explorer (NICER) for timing and spectroscopy, and from the Imaging X-ray Polarimetry Explorer (IXPE) for polarimetry. The paper outlines how eXTP will address key questions about the dense matter equation of state, including the possible presence of heavy baryons or deconfined quarks in neutron star cores, the role of superfluidity in thermal evolution and glitch behavior, and the structure of nuclear pasta in the crust.
