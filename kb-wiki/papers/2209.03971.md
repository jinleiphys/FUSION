# The LPM effect in sequential bremsstrahlung: incorporation of "instantaneous'' interactions for QCD

> The splitting processes of bremsstrahlung and pair production in a medium are coherent over large distances in the very high energy limit, which leads to a suppression known as the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect. We continue study of the case when the coherence lengths (formation lengths) of two consecutive splitting processes overlap, avoiding soft-emission approximations. Previous work made a ``nearly-complete'' calculation of the effect of overlapping formation times on gluonic splittings such as $g \to gg \to ggg$ (with simplifying assumptions such as an infinite QCD medium and the large-$N_c$ limit). In this paper, we extend those previous rate calculations from nearly-complete to complete by including processes involving the exchange of longitudinally-polarized gluons. In the context of Lightcone Pertubation Theory, used earlier for the ``nearly-complete'' calculation, such exchanges are instantaneous in lightcone time and have their own diagrammatic representation.

## Key claim

The paper completes the calculation of overlapping formation time effects on sequential gluon splittings in QCD by including all diagrams involving longitudinally-polarized gluon exchange (instantaneous in lightcone time) and fundamental 4-gluon vertices, which were omitted from the previous "nearly-complete" calculation of Arnold, Gorda, and Iqbal (2022) for processes such as \(g \to gg \to ggg\) and the associated virtual corrections.

## Method

The authors work in lightcone perturbation theory (LCPT) in the large-\(N_c\) limit, assuming an infinite, static, homogeneous QCD medium described by the transport parameter \(\hat q\). They compute the remaining gluonic QCD diagrams for double splitting \(g \to ggg\) and virtual corrections to single splitting \(g \to gg\) that involve either instantaneous longitudinal gluon exchange or fundamental 4-gluon vertices, using Feynman rules in lightcone gauge \(A^+=0\) and adapting previous results for the \(4\bar y\bar x\) diagram from Arnold, Gorda, and Iqbal (2022) by evaluating relative factors from helicity contractions, color contractions, and longitudinal momentum fraction dependence.

## Key numbers

* The paper works in the large-\(N_c\) limit; previous work by Arnold, Gorda, and Iqbal (2022) found that \(1/N_c^2\) corrections to \(g \to ggg\) interference diagrams were \(\le 17\%\) for the processes studied there.
* The relevant scale for the running coupling is parametrically \(\mu \sim (\hat q E)^{1/4}\) for quasi-democratic splittings in an infinite QCD medium.
* The formation length for quasi-democratic splittings is parametrically of order \(\sqrt{E/\hat q}\) in an infinite QCD medium.

## Context

This work extends the program of Arnold, Gorda, and Iqbal (2022) and earlier papers (Arnold, Gorda, and Iqbal, 2020; Arnold, Gorda, and Iqbal, 2021) that computed "nearly-complete" rates for overlapping formation times in gluonic splittings, by adding the missing instantaneous longitudinal gluon exchange and 4-gluon vertex diagrams. The results enable a complete calculation (within the stated approximations) of the LPM effect for sequential bremsstrahlung in QCD without soft-emission approximations, building on the foundational BDMPS-Z framework of Baier, Dokshitzer, Mueller, Peigne, and Schiff (1997) and Zakharov (1996).
