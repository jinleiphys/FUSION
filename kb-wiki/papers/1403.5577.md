# Neutron-proton effective mass splitting in neutron-rich matter at normal density from analyzing nucleon-nucleus scattering data within an isospin dependent optical model

> The neutron-proton effective $k$-mass splitting in asymmetric nucleonic matter of isospin asymmetry $δ$ and normal density is found to be $m^{*}_{n-p}\equiv(m^{*}_{n}-m^{*}_{p})/m=(0.41 \pm0.15)δ$ from analyzing globally 1088 sets of reaction and angular differential cross sections of proton elastic scattering on 130 targets with beam energies from 0.783 MeV to 200 MeV, and 1161 sets of data of neutron elastic scattering on 104 targets with beam energies from 0.05 MeV to 200 MeV within an isospin dependent non-relativistic optical potential model. It sets a useful reference for testing model predictions on the momentum dependence of the nucleon isovector potential necessary for understanding novel structures and reactions of rare isotopes.

## Key claim

The neutron-proton effective k-mass splitting in asymmetric nucleonic matter at normal density is found to be \(m^{*}_{n-p} \equiv (m^{*}_{n}-m^{*}_{p})/m = (0.41 \pm 0.15)\delta\), where \(\delta\) is the isospin asymmetry; this result is obtained from a global optical model analysis of all available nucleon-nucleus scattering data up to 200 MeV, and the abstract's claim of a "useful reference" is hedged by the fact that the uncertainty remains about 37% and that no systematic errors from model assumptions (such as the shape of the optical potential) have been quantified.

## Method

The authors performed a global \(\chi^2\) minimization using 2249 data sets of reaction and elastic angular differential cross sections for neutron and proton scattering on 234 targets (beam energies 0.05–200 MeV) from the EXFOR database, within a non-relativistic isospin-dependent optical potential model expanded to second order in isospin asymmetry. The optical potential parameters were optimized separately for neutron-only (Case I), proton-only (Case II), and all nucleon-nucleus (Case III) data, and the neutron-proton effective mass splitting was extracted by transforming the energy-dependent optical model potentials into momentum-dependent single-nucleon potentials in nuclear matter using the dispersion relation and the Lane potential formalism. A covariance analysis of the error matrix around the optimal parameters was carried out to evaluate statistical uncertainties consistently.

## Key numbers

- Neutron-proton effective mass splitting from the simultaneous analysis of all nucleon-nucleus data (Case III): \(m^{*}_{n-p} = (0.41 \pm 0.15)\delta\).
- Nucleon isoscalar effective mass from Case III: \(m^{*}_0/m = 0.65 \pm 0.06\).
- For neutron-only data (Case I): \(m^{*}_{n-p} = (0.41 \pm 0.14)\delta\) and \(m^{*}_0/m = 0.65 \pm 0.05\).
- For proton-only data (Case II): \(m^{*}_{n-p} = (0.44 \pm 0.16)\delta\) and \(m^{*}_0/m = 0.67 \pm 0.06\).
- The isovector potential slope at the Fermi momentum (Case III): \(-dU_{sym,1}/dk = 30.44\) (units implied by the formalism, in MeV·fm).
- The isoscalar potential contributions (Case III): \(-k_F/3 \cdot d^2U_0/dk^2 = -12.83\) and \(1/3 \cdot dU_0/dk = 10.17\) (units implied), which largely cancel.

## Context

This work extends earlier efforts to constrain the neutron-proton effective mass splitting, notably the average of six parameterizations by Xu et al. (2010) giving \((0.32 \pm 0.15)\delta\) and the extraction from symmetry energy analyses by Li et al. (2013) giving \((0.27 \pm 0.25)\delta\), both of which had larger or unquantified uncertainties. The present result provides the most stringent and reliable constraint at normal density to date, using a consistent covariance analysis on the complete EXFOR dataset; it enables calibration of the momentum-dependent isovector potentials used in transport model simulations of heavy-ion collisions and rare-isotope reactions, and serves as a boundary condition for nuclear many-body theories predicting the effective mass splitting.
