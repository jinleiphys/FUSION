# Scalar mesons in a linear sigma model with (axial-)vector mesons

> The structure of the scalar mesons has been a subject of debate for many decades. In this work we look for $\bar{q}q$ states among the physical resonances using an extended Linear Sigma Model that contains scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector mesons both in the non-strange and strange sectors. We perform global fits of meson masses, decay widths and amplitudes in order to ascertain whether the scalar $\bar{q}q$ states are below or above 1 GeV. We find the scalar states above 1 GeV to be preferred as $\bar{q}q$ states.

## Key claim

The scalar quark-antiquark states with isospin I=1 and I=1/2 must be identified with the resonances above 1 GeV (specifically a0(1450) and K0*(1430)), while the scalar-isoscalar quark-antiquark states also lie above 1 GeV, but a full quantitative study of the isoscalar sector requires including a glueball state that mixes with the two quark-antiquark states.

## Method

The authors constructed an extended Linear Sigma Model (eLSM) with global chiral U(3)L x U(3)R symmetry containing scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector meson nonets in both non-strange and strange sectors. They performed a global fit of the model parameters to 21 experimental quantities (masses, decay widths, and amplitudes), excluding the scalar-isoscalar states from the fit but testing a posteriori all four possible assignments of the isotriplet and isodoublet scalar states to the physical resonances a0(980), a0(1450), K0*(800), and K0*(1430).

## Key numbers

- Only the pair a0(1450) and K0*(1430) yields a good fit; the other three combinations (a0(980)/K0*(800), a0(980)/K0*(1430), a0(1450)/K0*(800)) deliver large and unacceptable values of chi-squared.
- The global fit describes all experimental quantities with an average error of 5%.
- In the large-Nc limit (with parameters lambda1 and h1 set to zero), the two scalar-isoscalar masses are about 1.36 GeV and 1.53 GeV.
- The model includes meson states up to energies of about 1.7 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the linear sigma model approach of Gell-Mann and Levy (1960) and the earlier eLSM framework by including vector and axial-vector mesons alongside scalars and pseudoscalars. The results contradict interpretations that place the scalar quark-antiquark states below 1 GeV (such as identifying a0(980) or K0*(800) as predominantly quark-antiquark). The study enables future work that includes the scalar glueball state (mass around 1.5 GeV) to properly describe the three physical isoscalar states f0(1370), f0(1500), and f0(1710) as a mixed system of two quark-antiquark states and a glueball.
