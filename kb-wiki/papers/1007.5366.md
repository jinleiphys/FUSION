# Electromagnetic production of kaon near threshold

> Kaon photo- and electroproduction off a proton near the production threshold are investigated by utilizing an isobar model. The background amplitude of the model is constructed from Feynman diagrams, whereas the resonance term is calculated by using the multipole formalism. It is found that both pseudoscalar and pseudovector models can nicely describe the available photoproduction data up to W = 50 MeV above the threshold. The Lambda resonance S_{01}(1800) is found to play an important role in improving the model. In the case of double polarization observables Ox and Oz our result corroborates the finding of Sandorfi et al. Due to the large contributions of the K* and K1 vector mesons, extending the model to the case of electroproduction is almost impossible unless either special form factors that strongly suppress their contributions are introduced or all hadronic coupling constants are refitted to both photo- and electroproduction databases, simultaneously. It is also concluded that investigation of the kaon electromagnetic form factor is not recommended near the threshold region.

## Key claim

Both pseudoscalar and pseudovector isobar models can describe available photoproduction data up to 50 MeV above threshold, but extending the model to electroproduction is almost impossible unless special form factors that strongly suppress the contributions of K* and K1 vector mesons are introduced, or all hadronic coupling constants are refitted to both photo- and electroproduction databases simultaneously.

## Method

The background amplitude was constructed from tree-level Feynman diagrams including s-, u-, and t-channel Born terms along with K*+(892) and K1(1270) vector meson exchanges, while the resonance term for the S11(1650) nucleon resonance was calculated using the Breit-Wigner multipole formalism. The main coupling constants g_KΛN and g_KΣN were fixed to SU(3) symmetry predictions, and the unknown coupling constants of K*, K1, and the hyperon resonance S01(1800) were fitted to 139 experimental data points using the CERN-MINUIT code.

## Key numbers

- The analysis covers energies up to W = 50 MeV above the production threshold (W_thr. = 1609 MeV for K+Λ photoproduction)
- The S11(1650) resonance contributes more than 20% to the total cross section in the pseudoscalar model, and from 15% to 70% in the pseudovector model depending on energy
- The pseudoscalar model yields χ² = 127.9 (χ²/N = 0.920) while the pseudovector model yields χ² = 212.2 (χ²/N = 1.526)
- The extracted hyperon resonance Y*3 (S01(1800)) coupling constant is G_Y3*/√(4π) = -4.93 in the pseudoscalar model
- The K* vector meson coupling constants are G^V_K*/4π = -0.65 and G^T_K*/4π = 0.29 in the pseudoscalar model
- The K1 vector meson coupling constants are G^V_K1/4π = 0.42 and G^T_K1/4π = -3.17 in the pseudoscalar model

## Context

This work extends the earlier threshold study by Cheoun et al. (1996), who predicted total cross sections around 5 μb at 10 MeV above threshold using SU(3) coupling values, while experimental data show less than 0.4 μb at that energy. The model corroborates the finding of Sandorfi et al. regarding double polarization observables Ox and Oz, and the extracted coupling constants are closer to those of the AS1 and AS2 models by Adelseck and Saghai (1990) than to the Kaon-Maid model. The result enables future construction of extended models describing photo- and electroproduction at higher energies, but the paper concludes that investigation of the kaon electromagnetic form factor is not recommended near the threshold region.
