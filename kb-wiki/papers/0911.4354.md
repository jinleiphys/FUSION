# Corrections to linear mixing in binary ionic mixtures and plasma screening at zero separation

> Using the results of extensive Monte Carlo simulations we discuss corrections to the linear mixing rule in strongly coupled binary ionic mixtures. We analyze the plasma screening function at zero separation, H_{jk}(0), for two ions (of types j=1,2 and k=1,2) in a strongly coupled binary mixture. The function H_{jk}(0) is estimated by two methods: (1) from the difference of Helmholtz Coulomb free energies at large and zero separations; (2) by fitting the Widom expansion of H_{jk}(x) in powers of interionic distance x to Monte Carlo data on the radial pair distribution function g_{jk}(x). These methods are shown to be in good agreement. For illustration, we analyze the plasma screening enhancement of nuclear burning rates in dense stellar matter.

# Corrections to linear mixing in binary ionic mixtures and plasma screening at zero separation

## Key claim

The linear mixing rule for the plasma screening enhancement of nuclear reaction rates is highly accurate for strongly coupled plasmas (Γjk ≳ 10), but relative corrections become much larger at lower coupling; the authors propose a simple analytic approximation that reproduces both the Debye-Hückel asymptote at weak coupling and the linear mixing result at strong coupling, though this approximation may be more reliable than direct thermodynamic extraction for mixtures with very large charge ratios and small fractions of the lighter ion.

## Method

The authors performed extensive Monte Carlo simulations of binary ionic mixtures and extracted the plasma screening function at zero separation Hjk(0) using two independent methods: (1) from the difference of Helmholtz Coulomb free energies at large and zero interionic separations (thermodynamic approach), and (2) by fitting the Widom expansion of Hjk(r) in even powers of r/ajk to Monte Carlo data on the radial pair distribution function gjk(r). They compared these results to the linear mixing model and to a new analytic approximation formula that interpolates between the Debye-Hückel and strong-coupling limits.

## Key numbers

- The linear mixing rule is highly accurate for Coulomb coupling parameters Γjk ≳ 10; for lower Γjk the relative corrections can be much larger.
- For simulations with low fractions of highly charged ions and large ratio Z2/Z1 ≥ 5 (simulations 6, 7, 8, 9), the difference between the Widom fitting results and the approximation translates into a correction to the reaction rate within a factor of two.
- Three large-Γ simulations (96, 97, 99) that started with lattice configurations showed larger corrections to linear mixing in the crystalline phase.
- The approximation formula reproduces the Debye-Hückel enhancement parameter h^DH_jk = 3^(1/2) Z_j Z_k ⟨Z^2⟩^(1/2) Γ_e^(3/2) / ⟨Z⟩^(1/2) at low Γ and the linear mixing result at strong coupling.
- For a mixture with Z2/Z1 = 5 and x1 = 0.01 (panel c), the thermodynamic model of h^0_11/Γ_11^(3/2) shows an increase at Γ_11 ∼ 10^(-2) that the authors consider likely not real.

## Context

This work extends the linear mixing rule first shown to be highly accurate over 30 years ago by Hansen and Vieillefosse (1976) and builds on recent precise thermodynamic results for multicomponent mixtures by Potekhin, Chabrier, and Rogers (2009) and others. Previous attempts to describe corrections to linear mixing, such as those by DeWitt, Slattery, and Chabrier (1996, 2003), were restricted by limited data points. The results enable improved calculations of nuclear burning rates in dense stellar matter, particularly for conditions where the linear mixing approximation becomes less accurate, and provide a simple analytic formula valid for any Coulomb coupling strength and mixture composition.
