# High-spin structures of 136Cs

> Odd-odd 136Cs nuclei have been produced in the 18O + 208Pb and 12C + 238U fusion-fission reactions and their gamma rays studied with the Euroball array. The high-spin level scheme has been built up to ~ 4.7 MeV excitation energy and spin I ~ 16 hbar from the triple gamma-ray coincidence data. The configurations of the three structures observed above ~ 2 MeV excitation energy are first discussed by analogy with the proton excitations identified in the semi-magic 137Cs nucleus, which involve the three high-j orbits lying above the Z=50 gap, pi g_{7/2}, pi d_{5/2} and pi h_{11/2}. This is confirmed by the results of shell-model calculations performed in this work.

## Key claim

The high-spin level scheme of odd-odd 136Cs has been built for the first time up to approximately 4.7 MeV excitation energy and spin I ~ 16 hbar, with the configurations of three structures above ~2 MeV assigned by analogy to proton excitations in 137Cs and confirmed by shell-model calculations using the SN100PN effective interaction, though the predicted spectrum is slightly more compressed than the experimental one.

## Method

136Cs nuclei were produced as fission fragments in two fusion-fission reactions: 18O + 208Pb at 85 MeV and 12C + 238U at 90 MeV. Gamma rays were detected with the Euroball array, and the high-spin level scheme was built from triple gamma-ray coincidence data using multigated spectra and three-dimensional cubes analyzed with the Radware package. Transitions were identified by gating on known gamma rays from complementary fragments (85,86Br and 103-105Tc) and then analyzing coincidence relationships among the new transitions assigned to 136Cs.

## Key numbers

* The high-spin level scheme was built up to approximately 4.7 MeV excitation energy and spin I ~ 16 hbar.
* The yrast cascade above the 8- isomeric state includes transitions at 66.0(5) keV, 1398.4(3) keV, 261.6(2) keV, and 729.8(3) keV.
* The 1398.4 keV transition has relative intensity 100(15), normalized to I_gamma(1398) = 100.
* The internal conversion coefficient of the 66.0 keV transition is alpha_tot(66) = 2.8(10), consistent with M1 multipolarity (theoretical M1 value 3.0(1)).
* The internal conversion coefficient of the 121.4 keV transition is alpha_tot(121) = 0.45(15), consistent with M1 multipolarity (theoretical M1 value 0.52(1)).
* The 11-1 state is calculated at 1779 keV and measured at 1982 keV; the 13+1 state is calculated at 3243 keV and measured at 3562 keV.

## Context

This work extends the study of high-spin states in the A=136 region, where the odd-odd 136Cs nucleus could not be populated by beta decay and only the ground state (I=5 hbar, T1/2=13.16 d) and an isomeric state (I=8 hbar, T1/2=19 s) were previously known from spallation and fission studies by Ra (1975) and Th (1981), with the isomeric state's excitation energy recently determined via a 518-keV E3 transition by Wi (2011). The yrast structures are discussed by analogy with proton excitations in 137Cs identified by Astier (2012b), and the shell-model calculations using the SN100PN effective interaction from Brown (2005) show good agreement, enabling further tests of this interaction using high-spin states of 138La, a work reported to be in progress.
