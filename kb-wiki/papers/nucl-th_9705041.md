# Interplay of parton and hadron cascades in nucleus-nucleus collisions at the CERN SPS and RHIC

> We introduce a Monte Carlo space-time model for high-energy collisions with nuclei, involving the dynamical interplay of perturbative QCD parton production and evolution, with non-perturbative parton-cluster formation and `afterburner' cascading of formed pre-hadronic clusters plus hadron excitations. This approach allows us to trace the space-time history of parton and hadron degrees of freedom of nuclear collisions on the microscopical level of parton and hadron cascades in both position and momentum space, from the instant of nuclear overlap to the final yield of particles. In applying this approach, we analyze Pb+Pb collisions at the CERN SPS with beam energy 158 GeV (sqrt{s}/A = 17 GeV) and Au+Au collisions at RHIC with collider energy \sqrt{s}/A = 200 GeV. We find that the perturbative QCD parton production and cascade development provides an important contribution to particle production at central rapidities, and that the `afterburner' cascading of pre-hadronic clusters and formed hadrons emerging from the parton cascade is essential. The overall agreement of our model calculations including the `afterburner' cascading with the observed particle spectra at the CERN SPS is fairly good, whereas the neglect of the final-state interactions among hadronic excitations deviates significantly.

## Key claim

The authors claim that a combined parton-hadron cascade model, which includes perturbative QCD parton production and evolution alongside non-perturbative parton-cluster formation and an "afterburner" hadron cascade, can reproduce the gross particle production properties observed at CERN SPS without fine-tuning, but they hedge that more detailed analysis may reveal disagreement that could indicate genuinely new physics.

## Method

The authors constructed a Monte Carlo space-time model that combines the parton-cascade/cluster-hadronization code VNI with the hadron cascade code HIJET, tracing the evolution of partons, pre-hadronic clusters, and hadrons in 7-dimensional phase-space (d³r d³k dE) from nuclear overlap to final particle yield. The simulation proceeds by evolving particles in small time steps (Δt ≈ 10⁻³ fm) through stages of parton cascade, parton-cluster formation via coalescence, cluster decay into hadrons, and afterburner hadron cascade including cluster-cluster, cluster-hadron, and hadron-hadron collisions with resonance formation and decay.

## Key numbers

- Pb+Pb collisions at CERN SPS: beam energy 158 GeV, corresponding to center-of-mass energy per nucleon √s/A = 17 GeV
- Au+Au collisions at RHIC: collider energy per nucleon √s/A = 200 GeV
- The simulation time step used was Δt ≈ 10⁻³ fm
- The proper decay time for off-shell partons or clusters is given by 1/M where M = √k² is the invariant mass, with formation time Δt = γ/M

## Context

This work extends the parton cascade approach of Geiger (1995-1996) and the hadron cascade model HIJET by merging them into a unified space-time description, contrasting with existing event generators like FRITIOF, VENUS, RQMD, and DPM that are based on string-excitation and fragmentation models, and HIJING which incorporates perturbative QCD minijet production but lacks space-time description. The approach enables future investigation of nucleus-nucleus collisions at even higher energies such as the LHC, and provides a baseline against which truly new physics in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC can be identified.
