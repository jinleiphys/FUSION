# Dynamical gluon effects in twist-3 generalized parton distributions of the proton

> Within the Basis Light-Front Quantization framework, we systematically investigate the subleading-twist (twist-3) generalized parton distributions (GPDs) of the proton's valence quarks beyond the Wandzura--Wilczek (WW) approximation. The twist-3 GPDs are not independent; through the equations of motion they decompose into a non-genuine contribution and a genuine twist-3 term. The latter encodes quark--quark--gluon correlations and involves interference between the light-front Fock sectors |qqq> and |qqqg>, which are typically neglected in the WW approximation. Using light-front wave functions obtained from diagonalizing the proton light-front Hamiltonian for its |qqq> and |qqqg> Fock components, we compute these GPDs via their overlap representations. To further explore their physical implications, we also evaluate several twist-3--related quantities, including the quark orbital angular momentum, the total quark spin contribution, and the quark spin--orbit correlation. Our results provide new nonperturbative input on higher-twist dynamics particularly multi-parton interference effects relevant for future measurements at the EicC and the EIC.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that genuine twist-3 contributions from quark–gluon–quark correlations, arising from interference between the |qqq⟩ and |qqqg⟩ Fock sectors, are non-negligible in the proton's twist-3 generalized parton distributions, contrary to the Wandzura–Wilczek approximation which neglects these dynamical gluon effects.

## Method

Within the Basis Light-Front Quantization framework, the authors diagonalize the proton's light-front Hamiltonian including both |qqq⟩ and |qqqg⟩ Fock components to obtain light-front wave functions. They then compute all twist-3 generalized parton distributions in the zero-skewness limit via overlap representations, decomposing each distribution into contributions from the |qqq⟩ sector, the |qqqg⟩ sector, and the genuine cross-overlap term. The Hamiltonian parameters are set to reproduce the proton mass and electromagnetic properties, with basis truncations N_max = 9 and K = 16.5.

## Key numbers

* The proton at the model scale contains approximately 44% probability in the |qqq⟩ sector and 56% in the |qqqg⟩ sector.
* The model scale is μ₀² ∼ 0.24 ± 0.01 GeV².
* The confinement strength κ = 0.54 GeV, with quark masses m_u = 0.31 GeV, m_d = 0.25 GeV, and gluon mass m_g = 0.50 GeV.
* The strong coupling g_s = 2.40, with basis scale parameter b = 0.70 GeV and ultraviolet cutoff b_inst = 3.00 GeV for the instantaneous interaction.
* Eight twist-3 GPDs (H₂T, E₂T, \tilde{H}₂T, \tilde{E}'₂T, \tilde{H}₂, H'₂, E'₂, and \tilde{E}'₂) are found to be consistent with zero within numerical uncertainty at zero skewness.
* The peaks of the four presented chiral-even GPDs cluster in the range 0.2 < x < 0.4, and all tend to zero in the large x regime.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior study (Zhang 2024) by including the |qqqg⟩ Fock sector beyond the leading |qqq⟩ sector, thereby incorporating dynamical gluon effects that are neglected in the Wandzura–Wilczek approximation (Kivel 2001, Radyushkin 2000). The results provide nonperturbative input on higher-twist dynamics and multi-parton interference effects relevant for future measurements at the Electron–Ion Collider in China (EicC) and the Electron–Ion Collider (EIC). The approach builds on previous BLFQ calculations of proton observables including electromagnetic form factors, parton distribution functions, and transverse momentum distributions (Xu 2023).
