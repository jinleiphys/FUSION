# Quantum Dissipation due to the Interaction with Chaos

> We discuss the possibility of having "quantum dissipation" due to the interaction with chaotic degrees of freedom. We define the conditions that should be satisfied in order to have a dissipative effect similar to the one due to an interaction with a (many body) bath. We also compare with the case where the environment is modeled by a random matrix model. In case of interaction with "chaos" we observe a regime where the relaxation process is non-universal, and reflects the underlaying semiclassical dynamics. As an example we consider a two level system (spin) that interacts with a two dimensional anharmonic oscillator.

## Key claim

Quantum dissipation can arise from interaction with only a few chaotic degrees of freedom, not just from a large many-body bath; however, when the coupling strength exceeds the environmental bandwidth, the relaxation becomes non-universal and reflects the underlying semiclassical dynamics, unlike the universal behavior predicted by random matrix theory or harmonic bath models.

## Method

The authors model a two-level system (spin) coupled to a two-dimensional anharmonic oscillator (2DW) whose classical dynamics is predominantly chaotic. They compare the resulting spin polarization and purity decay against a random matrix theory (RMT) model that has exactly the same power spectrum of fluctuations but randomized off-diagonal matrix elements. The system-environment coupling is characterized by a parameter Γ that quantifies how many environmental levels are mixed non-perturbatively, and the dynamics is analyzed via the Feynman-Vernon influence functional formalism.

## Key numbers

- Mean level spacing of the 2DW environment: Δ ≈ 0.004 (in dimensionless units, with ℏ = 0.03)
- Bandwidth of the 2DW environment: Δ_b ≈ 0.2
- Temperature of the 2DW environment in the specified energy range: T ≈ 1.3
- Heat capacity of the 2DW environment: d_T ≈ 2.4
- Classical correlation time of the 2DW: τ_c ∼ 1
- For coupling strength v > 0.16, the purity decay curves for the 2DW and RMT models differ by two orders of magnitude
- The Kondo parameter α = (1/16π)Γ/T satisfies α ≪ 1 in the simulations

## Context

This work extends the study of quantum dissipation beyond the standard Caldeira-Leggett approach (Weiss 1999, Leggett 1987) and random matrix modeling (Mello 1999, Pastur 1999, Esposito 2000) by considering few-degree-of-freedom chaotic environments. The authors show that the notion of universality versus non-universality, familiar from quantum chaos and mesoscopic physics, can be applied to dissipation problems. The results enable future studies of decoherence and dissipation in nano-scale quantum electronics, such as quantum dots interacting via chaotic electron motion, where the traditional thermodynamic limit of infinite bath degrees of freedom may not apply.
