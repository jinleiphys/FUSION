# Population of tetraneutron continuum in reactions of $^{8}$He on deuterium

> Search for the population of the low-energy continuum of a tetraneutron system was performed for reactions of the $^{8}$He beam on a deuterium target. These studies are based on the data [I.A. Muzalevskii \textit{et al.}, Phys.\ Rev.\ C \textbf{103}, 044313 (2021)], previously used for the studies of $^{7}$H and $^{6}$H in the $^2\text{H}(^8\text{He},{^3\text{He}})^{7}$H and $^2\text{H}(^8\text{He},{^4\text{He}})^{6}$H reactions. Evidence for a hump in the $^4$n continuum at $3.5 \pm 0.7$ and $3.2 \pm 0.8$ MeV was observed in the $^2$H($^8$He,$^6$Li)$^4$n and $^2$H($^8$He,$^3$He)$^7$H$\rightarrow ^3$H+$^4$n reactions, respectively. The observed statistics is quite low (6 events and up to 40 events) corresponding to very low cross sections of few microbarns or tens of microbarns. The background conditions for the $^2$H($^8$He,$^6$Li)$^4$n reaction are shown to be good, favoring the physical nature of the observed events. The $^2$H($^8$He,$^3$He)$^7$H$\rightarrow ^3$H+$^4$n process transforms to the $^2$H($^8$He,$^6$Li$^{\ast})^4n$ reaction in the limit of the highest $^7$H decay energies. The population of the low-energy region in the $^{4}$n spectrum is found to be perfectly correlated with the population of the lowest $^{6}$Li state in the $^{3}$He+$^{3}$H continuum with $E^*=18$ MeV. Theoretical calculations of $^{8}$He in a five-body $α$+$4n$ and of $^{4}$n in a four-body hyperspherical models are presented. The $^{8}$He wave function is shown to contain strong specific correlations, which may give rise to very low-energy structures in tetraneutron continuum in extreme-peripheral reaction scenarios.

## Key claim

Evidence for a hump in the tetraneutron continuum is observed at 3.5 ± 0.7 MeV and 3.2 ± 0.8 MeV in two different reactions, but the statistics are very low (6 events and up to 40 events) with correspondingly small cross sections of few microbarns or tens of microbarns, and the authors note that the observed structures may be connected with random background in one of the reaction channels.

## Method

The experiment used a 26 A MeV ⁸He beam impinging on a cryogenic gaseous deuterium target at the ACCULINNA-2 facility, detecting ⁶Li recoil nuclei in coincidence with neutrons from the ⁴n system using silicon strip detector telescopes and a stilbene scintillator neutron wall. The tetraneutron missing-mass spectrum was reconstructed from the ²H(⁸He,⁶Li)⁴n reaction, and additional analysis was performed on previously published ²H(⁸He,³He)⁷H data to extract ⁴n information from the ⁷H → ³H+⁴n decay channel. Theoretical calculations of ⁸He in a five-body α+4n model and of ⁴n in a four-body hyperspherical model were performed to interpret the observed structures.

## Key numbers

- Hump in the ⁴n continuum at 3.5 ± 0.7 MeV observed in the ²H(⁸He,⁶Li)⁴n reaction, based on 6 events
- Hump in the ⁴n continuum at 3.2 ± 0.8 MeV observed in the ²H(⁸He,³He)⁷H → ³H+⁴n reaction, based on up to 40 events
- Cross sections of few microbarns or tens of microbarns for the observed reactions
- Energy resolution of approximately 1.2 MeV FWHM for the ⁴n missing-mass spectrum in the ²H(⁸He,⁶Li)⁴n reaction
- Energy resolution of approximately 2.5 MeV FWHM for the ⁴n missing-mass spectrum in the ⁷H decay channel
- ⁸He beam energy of 26 A MeV with mean intensity around 10⁵ particles per second
- Target thickness of 3.7 × 10²⁰ atoms·cm⁻²
- ⁶Li recoil detection in angular range 6°–24° in the laboratory system
- Neutron detection efficiency of approximately 1% per single neutron, with approximately 3% probability for coincidence with ⁶Li recoil
- The ⁶Li* state in the ³He+³H continuum at E* = 18 MeV with Γ ≈ 3 MeV

## Context

This work extends the search for tetraneutron continuum structures beyond the previous observations by Kisamori (2016), who found 4 events in the ⁴He(⁸He,2α) reaction with cross section ≈ 4 nb, and Duer (2022), who reported a resonance-like structure of about 44 events at E_T(4n) = 2.37 MeV in the ¹H(⁸He,pα) experiment. The results are consistent with the earlier theoretical framework of Grigorenko (2004), which predicted low-energy ⁴n population from prearranged four-neutron configurations in ⁸He, but contradict the conclusions of Lazauskas (2023) that the ⁸He-induced source can explain the data without peripheral reaction assumptions. The findings enable further investigation of whether the observed low-energy structures represent genuine tetraneutron resonances or arise from specific correlations in the ⁸He wave function combined with peripheral reaction mechanisms.
