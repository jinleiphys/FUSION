# Evidence for flow in pPb collisions at 5 TeV from v2 mass splitting

> We show that a fluid dynamical scenario describes quantitatively the observed mass splitting of the elliptical flow coefficients v2 for pions, kaons, and protons. This provides a strong argument in favor of the existence of a fluid dynamical expansion in pPb collisions at 5TeV.

# Evidence for flow in pPb collisions at 5 TeV from v2 mass splitting

## Key claim
A fluid dynamical scenario (EPOS3 with event-by-event 3D+1 viscous hydrodynamics) quantitatively reproduces the observed mass splitting of the elliptical flow coefficient v2 for pions, kaons, and protons in pPb collisions at 5 TeV, providing a strong argument for collective flow in small systems. The claim is conditional on the specific model assumptions and the comparison being limited to the ALICE data set available at the time.

## Method
The authors used the EPOS3 event generator, which combines a Gribov-Regge multiple scattering approach with an event-by-event 3D+1 viscous hydrodynamic evolution (Israel-Stewart formulation, eta/S = 0.08, zeta/S = 0) and a core-corona separation. They computed dihadron correlation functions for high-multiplicity (0-20%) pPb events, subtracted the low-multiplicity (60-80%) correlation to remove jet contributions, extracted Fourier coefficients v_nDelta for four pair types (h-h, h-pi, h-K, h-p), and derived the identified particle v2 coefficients via v2^A = v2Delta^{h-A} / sqrt(v2Delta^{h-h}).

## Key numbers
- The analysis uses pPb collisions at a center-of-mass energy of 5 TeV.
- EPOS3 simulations were performed with version EPOS3.074.
- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio was fixed at eta/S = 0.08, with bulk viscosity zeta/S = 0.
- In the pt range 1-1.5 GeV/c, the kaon v2 is somewhat below the pion v2, while the proton v2 is clearly below both.
- A crossing occurs at approximately 2 GeV/c: below 2 GeV/c the proton v2 is below the pion v2, above 2 GeV/c the proton v2 exceeds the pion v2.
- The EPOS3 simulation results for the projected dihadron correlation were multiplied by a factor of 1.07 to match the ALICE data normalization.

## Context
This work extends the fluid dynamical interpretation of ridge structures from heavy-ion collisions to small systems, following the observation of ridge-like correlations in pp collisions by CMS (2010) and in pPb collisions by ALICE, CMS, and ATLAS (2012-2013). The mass splitting effect was predicted by Pasl (1998) and confirmed experimentally in heavy-ion collisions. The EPOS3 model builds on the earlier EPOS2 framework described in Werner et al. (2012), which successfully described jet spectra up to 200 GeV/c in pp at 7 TeV and harmonic flows up to 20 GeV/c in PbPb at 2.76 TeV. The results enable quantitative tests of collectivity in small systems using identified particle flow measurements, and challenge alternative explanations based on initial-state momentum correlations.
