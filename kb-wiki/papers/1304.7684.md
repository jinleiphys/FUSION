# Large-scale nuclear structure calculations for spin-dependent WIMP scattering with chiral effective field theory currents

> We perform state-of-the-art large-scale shell-model calculations of the structure factors for elastic spin-dependent WIMP scattering off 129,131Xe, 127I, 73Ge, 19F, 23Na, 27Al, and 29Si. This comprehensive survey covers the non-zero-spin nuclei relevant to direct dark matter detection. We include a pedagogical presentation of the formalism necessary to describe elastic and inelastic WIMP-nucleus scattering. The valence spaces and nuclear interactions employed have been previously used in nuclear structure calculations for these mass regions and yield a good spectroscopic description of these isotopes. We use spin-dependent WIMP-nucleus currents based on chiral effective field theory (EFT) at the one-body level and including the leading long-range two-body currents due to pion exchange, which are predicted in chiral EFT. Results for all structure factors are provided with theoretical error bands due to the nuclear uncertainties of WIMP currents in nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper provides state-of-the-art large-scale shell-model calculations of structure factors for elastic spin-dependent WIMP scattering off all experimentally relevant nonzero-spin nuclei, including chiral effective field theory currents at the one-body level and the leading long-range two-body currents due to pion exchange, with theoretical error bands that account for nuclear uncertainties in the WIMP currents.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell-model calculations using the ANTOINE code for seven target nuclei (129,131Xe, 127I, 73Ge, 19F, 23Na, 27Al, 29Si), solving the many-body problem in appropriate valence spaces with interactions previously tested in these mass regions. They derived spin-dependent WIMP-nucleus currents based on chiral effective field theory up to order Q^3, including one-body currents and the normal-ordered one-body part of long-range two-body currents due to pion exchange, with the two-body contributions expressed as density-dependent renormalizations of the axial and pseudo-scalar couplings. The structure factors were computed from the reduced matrix elements of longitudinal and transverse electric multipole operators, with only odd-L multipoles contributing to elastic scattering.

## Key numbers

- For 129Xe and 131Xe, the matrix dimension for the shell-model calculation was 3.5 × 10^8 (with excitation restrictions) and exact diagonalization, respectively; for 127I the matrix dimension was 4.3 × 10^8
- The two-body current contribution δa₁(p) reduces the axial part of the current by 14% to 32% at p=0, depending on the c₃,c₄ values and density range ρ=0.10 to 0.12 fm⁻³
- The two-body current contribution δa₁^P(p) increases the pseudo-scalar part by 23% to 54% at p=mπ (mπ=138.04 MeV)
- The axial form factor scale ΛA = 1040 MeV; the pion decay constant Fπ = 92.4 MeV; the pion-nucleon coupling gπpn = 13.05
- The function I^σ(ρ,p=P=0) = 0.58 to 0.60 depends only weakly on density in the range ρ=0.10 to 0.12 fm⁻³
- The P-dependence of the axial two-body current contribution varies by less than 12% over the relevant P range at ρ=0.10 fm⁻³

## Context

This work expands on the authors' previous study (Klos et al., 2013) that focused only on 129,131Xe, providing a comprehensive survey of all nonzero-spin nuclei relevant to direct dark matter detection. The calculations improve upon earlier phenomenological approaches (Engel, Ressell et al., Dean, Divari, Kortelainen, Toivanen) by incorporating chiral effective field theory currents with two-body contributions, which have been shown to be important for weak neutral currents in neutrino-deuteron scattering (SNO collaboration) and Gamow-Teller transitions. The results enable direct dark matter experiments (XENON, PICASSO, COUPP, SIMPLE, ZEPLIN) to interpret their spin-dependent WIMP scattering limits with improved nuclear structure factors and quantified theoretical uncertainties.
