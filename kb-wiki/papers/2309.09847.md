# Quasi-periodic oscillations during magnetar giant flares in the strangeon star model

> Soft gamma-ray repeaters (SGRs) are widely understood as slowly rotating isolated neutron stars. Their generally large spin-down rates, high magnetic fields, and strong outburst energies render them different from ordinary pulsars. In a few giant flares (GFs) and short bursts of SGRs, high-confidence quasi-periodic oscillations (QPOs) were observed. Although remaining an open question, many theoretical studies suggest that the torsional oscillations caused by starquakes could explain QPOs. Motivated by this scenario, we systematically investigate torsional oscillation frequencies based on the strangeon-star (SS) model with various values of harmonic indices and overtones. To characterize the strong-repulsive interaction at short distances and the non-relativistic nature of strangeons, a phenomenological Lennard-Jones model is adopted. We show that, attributing to the large shear modulus of SSs, our results explain well the high-frequency QPOs ($\gtrsim 150\,\mathrm{Hz}$) during the GFs. The low-frequency QPOs ($\lesssim 150\,\mathrm{Hz}$) can also be interpreted when the ocean-crust interface modes are included. We also discuss possible effects of the magnetic field on the torsional mode frequencies. Considering realistic models with general-relativistic corrections and magnetic fields, we further calculate torsional oscillation frequencies for quark stars. We show that it would be difficult for quark stars to explain all QPOs in GFs. Our work advances the understanding of the nature of QPOs and magnetar asteroseismology.

## Key claim

The strangeon star (SS) model, with its large shear modulus reaching ~10^32 erg cm^-3, can explain high-frequency QPOs (≥150 Hz) observed during magnetar giant flares, while low-frequency QPOs (≤150 Hz) require additional ocean-crust interface modes; the authors acknowledge that the SS model faces challenges explaining some low-frequency QPOs such as the 18 Hz, 30 Hz, and 92 Hz frequencies for SGR 1806-20.

## Method

The authors solved the general-relativistic perturbation equations for torsional oscillations of strangeon stars using the Cowling approximation, both with and without dipole magnetic fields. They adopted a phenomenological Lennard-Jones model to characterize the strong-repulsive interaction between strangeons and computed oscillation frequencies for various harmonic indices (ℓ=2 to 10) and overtones (n=0 to 7) across SS masses from 1.2 to 2.8 solar masses. They also calculated torsional mode frequencies for quark stars using both thin nuclear crust models and quark nugget crust models, including general-relativistic corrections and magnetic field effects.

## Key numbers

- SS fundamental ℓ=2 mode frequencies range from 145 Hz (for SS mass 2.8 M⊙) to 277 Hz (for SS mass 1.2 M⊙), compared to 17-29 Hz for neutron stars from Sotani (2006)
- SS model SS26 gives ₂f₀ = 155.6 Hz, matching the 155±6 Hz QPO for SGR 1900+14 within 2%
- SS model SS14 gives ₂f₁ = 612.3 Hz, matching the 625±2 Hz QPO for SGR 1806-20
- SS model SS16 gives ₃f₆ = 1878.7 Hz, close to the 1837±5 Hz QPO for SGR 1806-20
- Ocean-crust interface mode frequencies calculated using Eq. (9) give: ₁f = 16.3 Hz, ₂f = 28.5 Hz, and ₈f = 99 Hz for SGR 1806-20; ₄f = 52.1 Hz and ₇f = 87.3 Hz for SGR 1900+14; ₃f = 40.4 Hz for SGR J1935+2154
- For quark stars with thin nuclear crust, the fundamental ℓ=2 torsional mode frequency is 64 Hz for M = 1.4 M⊙, with the full range from 26 to 64 Hz
- For quark stars with nugget crust (m_s = 150 MeV), the fundamental mode frequency is 5.11 Hz and first overtone is 4282 Hz; for m_s = 250 MeV, fundamental mode is 7.49 Hz and first overtone is 2678 Hz
- The magnetic field strength where frequency shifts become significant for SSs is B_μ = 4×10^16 G, compared to ~4×10^15 G for neutron stars from Sotani (2006)

## Context

This work extends the strangeon star model proposed by Xu (2003) to magnetar asteroseismology, providing the first detailed calculation of torsional oscillation modes for SSs. It builds on prior studies of torsional oscillations in neutron stars by Sotani (2006) and the ocean-crust interface mode formalism from Piro (2004), while also comparing with quark star models from Watts (2006). The results enable future asteroseismological probes of compact star internal structure and suggest that observed QPO frequencies could help distinguish between strangeon stars, neutron stars, and quark stars.
