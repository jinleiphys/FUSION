# Asymmetric nuclear matter in the relativistic mean field approach with vector cross-interaction

> Asymmetric nuclear matter is studied in the frame of relativistic mean-field theory, using scalar-isoscalar sigma, vector-isoscalar omega meson together with their selfinteractions, vector-isovector rho meson with its cross-interaction with omega meson too, and scalar-isovector delta meson as degrees of freedom. The model is used to parameterize the nuclear matter properties results calculated by more fundamental Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory and thus to provide an effective DBHF model applicable also to finite nuclei. Vector omega-rho cross-interaction seems to be an useful degree of freedom for describing of the asymmetric nuclear matter, mostly due to its impact on density dependence of the symmetry energy.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including a vector cross-interaction between the omega and rho mesons in a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model is a useful degree of freedom for describing asymmetric nuclear matter, primarily because it significantly affects the density dependence of the symmetry energy. This claim is hedged by the fact that the model is used to parameterize results from the more fundamental Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) theory, and the quality of the fit depends on which DBHF dataset is used and which meson degrees of freedom are included.

## Method

The authors constructed a relativistic mean-field Lagrangian including sigma, omega, rho, and delta meson fields, with cubic and quartic scalar self-interactions, quartic vector self-couplings, and a cross-interaction term between the omega and rho mesons characterized by a coupling constant Lambda_V. They solved the field equations in the mean-field and no-sea approximations for static, homogeneous, infinite nuclear matter, and fitted the model parameters to reproduce binding energies per nucleon and isoscalar potentials from three different DBHF calculations (Machleidt et al., Lee et al., and Huber et al.) for symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy at saturation density: 33.3 MeV for the fit to Machleidt et al. DBHF results (parametrization MA with vector cross-interaction).
- Incompressibility of symmetric matter at saturation density: 347 MeV for the fit to Machleidt et al. DBHF results (parametrization MA).
- Incompressibility of nuclear matter: 235 MeV for the fit to Huber et al. DBHF results (parametrization HA).
- Chi-squared per data point (chi^2/N) for the fit to Machleidt et al. DBHF results: 2.76 with vector cross-interaction (MA) and 9.95 without (MB).
- Chi-squared per data point (chi^2/N) for the fit to Lee et al. DBHF results: 1.69 with vector cross-interaction (LA) and 2.62 without (LB).
- Chi-squared per data point (chi^2/N) for the fit to Huber et al. DBHF results: 2.05 with both cross-interaction and delta meson (HA) and 3.80 without cross-interaction but with delta meson (HB).
- Meson masses fixed in the model: m_sigma = 550 MeV, m_omega = 783 MeV, m_rho = 770 MeV, m_delta = 980 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean-field approach to asymmetric nuclear matter by incorporating the vector omega-rho cross-interaction, previously introduced by Mueller and Serot (1996), and the scalar-isovector delta meson, as studied by Kubis and Kutschera (1997). The model is fitted to reproduce results from three different DBHF calculations: Machleidt, Brockmann, and Li (1992); Lee, Kuo, Li, and Brown (1998, 1997); and Huber, Weber, and Weigel (1993, 1995). The resulting parameter sets provide an effective DBHF model applicable to finite nuclei, and the findings suggest that the omega-rho cross-interaction is important for describing the density dependence of the symmetry energy, with implications for exotic nuclei near the dripline, high-energy nuclear collisions, and neutron star properties.
