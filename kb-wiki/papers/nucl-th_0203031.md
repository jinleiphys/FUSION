# Searching for the statistically equilibrated systems formed in heavy ion collisions

> Further improvements and refinements are brought to the microcanonical multifragmentation model [Al. H. Raduta and Ad. R. Raduta, Phys. Rev. C {\bf 55}, 1344 (1997); {\it ibid.} {\bf 61}, 034611 (2000)]. The new version of the model is tested on the recently published experimental data concerning the Xe+Sn at 32 MeV/u and Gd+U at 36 MeV/u reactions. A remarkable good simultaneous reproduction of fragment size observables and kinematic observables is to be noticed. It is shown that the equilibrated source can be unambiguously identified.

# Searching for the statistically equilibrated systems formed in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The paper claims that the parameters of the experimentally observed statistically equilibrated nuclear source formed in heavy ion collisions can be uniquely determined by simultaneously fitting fragment size and kinematic observables with a refined microcanonical multifragmentation model, but this uniqueness is conditional on the freeze-out volume being large enough (8-9 times normal nuclear volume) that hard-core interaction effects become small and the Coulomb interaction dominates the determination.

## Method

The authors refined their existing microcanonical multifragmentation model by adding angular momentum conservation, a momentum generation stage for primary break-up fragments, the ability to superimpose radial flow with a parameterized profile, Coulomb propagation of primary fragments via Newtonian equations of motion integrated up to 500 fm/c, and secondary evaporation treated with the Weisskopf scheme. They tested the model against experimental data from the INDRA collaboration for Xe+Sn at 32 MeV/u and Gd+U at 36 MeV/u, using two opposite freeze-out scenarios: one with hard-sphere fragments in a spherical recipient and one without hard-core interactions using a Wigner-Seitz approximation for Coulomb energy.

## Key numbers

- Xe+Sn reaction: best-fit source parameters under hypothesis (1) are (A=230, Z=94), V/V0=9, excitation energy 5.3 MeV/u, flow energy 1.4 MeV/u, flow profile exponent α=2
- Xe+Sn reaction: best-fit source parameters under hypothesis (2) are (A=220, Z=92), V/V0=8.5, excitation energy 4.78 MeV/u, flow energy 1.9 MeV/u, flow profile exponent α=1.8
- Gd+U reaction: best-fit source parameters under hypothesis (1) are (A=343, Z=136), V/V0=8.5, excitation energy 5.95 MeV/u, flow energy 1.7 MeV/u, flow profile exponent α=2
- Gd+U reaction: best-fit source parameters under hypothesis (2) are (A=328, Z=130), V/V0=8, excitation energy 5.2 MeV/u, flow energy 1.9 MeV/u, flow profile exponent α=1.8
- Freeze-out volume region identified: 8.5-9 V0 for Xe+Sn and 8-8.5 V0 for Gd+U
- Coulomb propagation integration carried out up to 500 fm/c

## Context

This work extends the authors' own microcanonical multifragmentation model (Raduta and Raduta, 1997; 2000) by adding angular momentum conservation, radial flow, and Coulomb propagation, enabling the first complete simultaneous reproduction of both fragment size and kinematic observables from the INDRA collaboration data. The paper contrasts with the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM) of Bondorf et al. (1995), which could not reproduce the reduced velocity correlations for these reactions as noted by Tabacaru et al. (2000). The results enable locating the freeze-out process in the nuclear phase diagram and address the long-standing debate about whether a phase transition occurs in nuclear matter, by providing a robust determination of the freeze-out volume that is largely independent of the specific freeze-out hypothesis.
