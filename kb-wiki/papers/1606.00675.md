# Layers of deformed instantons in holographic baryonic matter

> We discuss homogeneous baryonic matter in the decompactified limit of the Sakai-Sugimoto model, improving existing approximations based on flat-space instantons. We allow for an anisotropic deformation of the instantons in the holographic and spatial directions and for a density-dependent distribution of arbitrarily many instanton layers in the bulk. Within our approximation, the baryon onset turns out to be a second-order phase transition, at odds with nature, and there is no transition to quark matter at high densities, at odds with expectations from QCD. This changes when we impose certain constraints on the shape of single instantons, motivated by known features of holographic baryons in the vacuum. Then, a first-order baryon onset and chiral restoration at high density are possible, and at sufficiently large densities two instanton layers are formed dynamically. Our results are a further step towards describing realistic, strongly interacting matter over a large density regime within a single model, desirable for studies of compact stars.

## Key claim

Within the Sakai-Sugimoto model, the authors show that a fully unconstrained minimization of the free energy for homogeneous baryonic matter yields a second-order baryon onset and no transition to quark matter at high densities, both at odds with nature; however, imposing constraints on the instanton shape motivated by known features of holographic baryons in the vacuum restores a first-order baryon onset and allows chiral restoration at high density, with two instanton layers forming dynamically at sufficiently large densities.

## Method

The authors work in the decompactified limit of the Sakai-Sugimoto model with two flavors, using a Dirac-Born-Infeld plus Chern-Simons action for gauge fields on flavor branes in the deconfined geometry at zero temperature. They approximate baryonic matter as a gas of anisotropic BPST instantons, introducing a deformation parameter γ to break the SO(4) symmetry between the holographic and spatial directions, and allow for an arbitrary number of instanton layers in the bulk with a density-dependent separation. The free energy is minimized with respect to the instanton width ρ, deformation γ, number of layers N_z, and layer extension z_0, both with and without additional constraints on ρ and γ.

## Key numbers

* The instanton density n_I is defined as n_I = (96π^4)/(λ^2 M_KK^3 N_f) * (N_I/V), where N_I is the total number of instantons, V the spatial volume, λ the 't Hooft coupling, M_KK the Kaluza-Klein mass, and N_f=2 the number of flavors.
* The instanton profile D(z) for a single layer (N_z=1) is given by D(z) = (9 u^{1/2})/(4 √(f_c)) * (ρ^2 u_c)^2 / (u^3 - u_c^3 + ρ^2 u_c)^{5/2}, where u is the dimensionless holographic coordinate and u_c its value at the tip of the connected branes.
* For N_z → ∞ at fixed extension z_0, the smooth distribution D_∞(z) is given by D_∞(z) = (1/(8 z_0)) * { (z+z_0)[3ρ^2+2(z+z_0)^2]/[(z+z_0)^2+ρ^2]^{3/2} - (z-z_0)[3ρ^2+2(z-z_0)^2]/[(z-z_0)^2+ρ^2]^{3/2} }.
* The model has three parameters in the version considered: the 't Hooft coupling λ, the Kaluza-Klein mass M_KK, and the asymptotic separation L of the D8- and anti-D8-branes; when constraints on the instanton shape are imposed, the number of free parameters increases to 5.

## Context

This work extends the instanton gas approach introduced by Ghoroku et al. (2012) and further developed by Li and Schmitt (2015), improving upon earlier pointlike approximations of baryons in the Sakai-Sugimoto model by Bergman et al. (2007) and Rozali et al. (2007). It incorporates the anisotropic deformation of instantons away from the λ=∞ limit, as studied by Rozali et al. (2013) and Bolognesi et al. (2013), and accounts for the repulsive spreading of instantons in the holographic direction previously seen in crystalline structures by Kaplunovsky et al. (2012, 2015). The results enable future studies of compact star matter using a single holographic model that can describe both nuclear and quark matter, though the present approximation still requires constraints to reproduce realistic phase transitions.
