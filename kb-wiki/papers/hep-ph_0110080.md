# Probing generalized parton distributions in pi N -> l+ l- N

> We study the exclusive reactions pi- p -> l+ l- n and pi+ n -> l+ l- p$ in view of possible future experiments with high-intensity pion beams. For large invariant mass of the lepton pair l+ l- and small squared momentum transfer to the nucleon these are hard-scattering processes providing access to generalized parton distributions. We estimate the cross section for these reactions, explore their connection with the pion form factor, and discuss the role they can play in improving our understanding of the relevant reaction mechanisms.

## Key claim

The exclusive reactions π⁻ p → ℓ⁺ ℓ⁻ n and π⁺ n → ℓ⁺ ℓ⁻ p can provide access to generalized parton distributions in the limit of large timelike photon virtuality and small momentum transfer to the nucleon, but the absolute cross section estimates are only rough due to substantial theoretical uncertainties from higher-order corrections and the choice of renormalization scale.

## Method

The authors apply a factorization theorem for the hard-scattering process π N → γ* N in the scaling limit of large Q'², expressing the amplitude in terms of a hard-scattering kernel, the pion distribution amplitude φ_π, and generalized parton distributions H̃ and Ẽ for the nucleon. They compute the leading-order (in α_S) helicity amplitudes for longitudinal virtual photons, which dominate at large Q', and estimate unpolarized cross sections using model ansätze for the generalized parton distributions: a factorized t-dependence for H̃ based on the axial form factor, and a pion-pole motivated form for Ẽ from the chiral soliton model.

## Key numbers

* Pion decay constant f_π ≈ 132 MeV, used in the leading-order helicity amplitude.
* Axial form factor parameter M_A = 1.06 GeV, used in the t-dependence of H̃ via g_A(t)/g_A(0) = (1 - t/M_A²)⁻².
* Running coupling α_S ≈ 0.31 at Q'² = 5 GeV², evaluated with one-loop running, 4 flavors, and Λ^(4) = 200 MeV.
* Cross section estimates shown for Q'² = 5 GeV², τ = 0.2, and |t| = 0.2 GeV², with separate contributions from |H̃|², Re(H̃* Ẽ), and |Ẽ|² terms.
* Form factor F(t) for Ẽ contains a pion-pole term with coefficient 4.4 GeV²/(m_π² - t) and parameters B = 1.7 GeV⁻², C = 0.5 GeV⁻².

## Context

This work extends the study of hard exclusive processes to timelike photon production with pion beams, complementing the spacelike processes γ* N → π N (meson electroproduction) and the timelike Compton process γ N → γ* N previously investigated by the same authors (Berger, Diehl, Pire 2001). It enables comparison of time- and spacelike data to test factorization, study power corrections, and extract the pion form factor, building on factorization theorems by Collins (1997) and models for generalized parton distributions by Radyushkin (1997), Ji (1997), and Penttinen et al. (2000).
