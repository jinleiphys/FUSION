# Tensor-force effects on single-particle levels and proton bubble structure around the $Z$ or $N=20$ magic number

> Applying the semi-realistic $NN$ interactions that include realistic tensor force to the Hartree-Fock calculations, we investigate tensor-force effects on the single-particle levels in the Ca isotopes. The semi-realistic interaction successfully describes the experimental difference between $\varepsilon(p1s_{1/2})$ and $\varepsilon(p0d_{3/2})$ (denoted by $\mathitΔ\varepsilon_{13}$) both at $^{40}$Ca and $^{48}$Ca, confirming importance of the tensor force. The tensor force plays a role in the $N$-dependence of $\mathitΔ\varepsilon_{13}$ also in neutron-rich Ca nuclei. While the $p1s_{1/2}$-$p0d_{3/2}$ inversion is predicted in heavier Ca nuclei as in $^{48}$Ca, it takes place only in $N\geq 46$, delayed by the tensor force. We further investigate possibility of proton bubble structure in Ar, which is suggested by the $p1s_{1/2}$-$p0d_{3/2}$ inversion in $^{48}$Ca and more neutron-rich Ca nuclei, by the spherical Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov calculations. Even with the inversion at $^{48}$Ca the pair correlation prohibits prominent bubble distribution in $^{46}$Ar. Bubble in Ar is unlikely also near neutron drip line because either of unboundness or of deformation. However, $^{34}$Si remains a candidate for proton bubble structure, owing to large shell gap between $p1s_{1/2}$ and $p0d_{5/2}$.

## Key claim

The semi-realistic M3Y-P5' and M3Y-P7 interactions, which include a realistic tensor force derived from the G-matrix without adjustment, successfully reproduce the experimental N-dependence of the proton 1s1/2-0d3/2 energy difference in Ca isotopes, whereas most effective interactions with phenomenological tensor channels have failed to reproduce this slope. The tensor force delays the 1s1/2-0d3/2 inversion in neutron-rich Ca nuclei until N≥46, contrary to predictions without tensor force.

## Method

The authors performed spherical Hartree-Fock (HF) and Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) calculations using the Gaussian expansion method with the semi-realistic M3Y-P5' and M3Y-P7 interactions, which maintain the tensor force from the M3Y-Paris interaction unchanged. For comparison, they also used the D1M Gogny interaction, which has no tensor channels. They computed single-particle energy differences and proton density distributions for Ca, Ar, Si, and S isotopes around the Z or N=20 magic number.

## Key numbers

- The experimental 1s1/2-0d3/2 energy difference (Δε13) in 40Ca and 48Ca is reproduced by M3Y-P5' and M3Y-P7, while D1M shows significant discrepancy at 40Ca despite agreement at 48Ca
- The ℓs splitting Δε35 = ε(0d3/2)-ε(0d5/2) decreases from 40Ca to 48Ca by 1.8 MeV experimentally; M3Y-P7 gives a reduction of 3.5 MeV and M3Y-P5' gives 3.1 MeV
- The 1s1/2-0d3/2 inversion in Ca isotopes occurs only at N≥46 with the semi-realistic interactions, whereas D1M predicts inversion already at 60Ca
- The energy difference Δε15 = ε(1s1/2)-ε(0d5/2) exceeds 5 MeV for 36S in spherical HF calculations for all interactions considered
- The occupation probability of p1s1/2 in 36S is 0.6-0.7 in HFB results

## Context

This work extends previous studies of shell evolution and tensor-force effects in mean-field approaches, specifically addressing the failure of most effective interactions (including those with phenomenological tensor channels studied by Grasso et al. (2007) and Wu et al. (2011)) to reproduce the N-dependence of the 1s1/2-0d3/2 difference in Ca isotopes. The only prior exception was SLy5+Tw (Bai et al., 2010), which gave the correct slope but substantially higher absolute values. The paper enables quantitative predictions for proton bubble structure in 34Si, building on suggestions by Khan et al. (2008) for 46Ar and by Grasso et al. (2009) for 34Si, while showing that bubble structure is unlikely in Ar isotopes due to pair correlations, unboundness, or deformation. The results also provide testable predictions for future electron scattering experiments using technology such as SCRIT.
