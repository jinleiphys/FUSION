# Influence of the stiffness of the equation of state and in-medium effects on the cooling of compact stars

> Measurements demonstrate the existence of compact stars with masses in a broad range from 1.2 to 2 $M_\odot$. The most massive of these objects might be hybrid stars. To fulfill the constraint $M_{\rm max}>2~M_{\odot}$ with a reserve we exploit the stiff DD2 hadronic equation of state (EoS) without and with excluded volume (DD2vex) correction, which produce maximum neutron star masses of $M_{\rm max} = 2.43~M_{\odot}$ and $2.70~M_{\odot}$, respectively. We show that the stiffness of the EoS does not preclude an explanation of the whole set of cooling data within "nuclear medium cooling" scenario for compact stars by a variation of the star masses. We select appropriate proton gap profiles from those exploited in the literature and allow for a variation of the effective pion gap controlling the efficiency of the medium modified Urca process. However, we suppress the possibility of pion condensation. In general, the stiffer the EoS the steeper a decrease with density of the effective pion gap is required. Results are compared with previously obtained ones for the HDD EoS for which $M_{\rm max} =2.06~M_{\odot}$. The cooling of the compact star in the supernova remnant Cassiopeia A (Cas A) is explained mainly by an efficient medium modified Urca process. With the DD2vex EoS and using an effective pion gap steeper decreasing with the density and/or a proton gap shifted to smaller densities we are also able to reproduce both a strong decline compatible with ACIS-S data and HRC-S instrument data. The mass of Cas A is estimated as $1.6 - 1.9~M_{\odot}$, above the value $1.5~M_{\odot}$, which we have evaluated with the softer HDD equation of state. Different mass choices for the hottest object XMMU J173203.3-344518 are discussed. We make general remarks also on hybrid star cooling and on its dependence on the stiffness of the hadronic EoS.

## Key claim

The stiffness of the hadronic equation of state does not preclude explaining the full set of compact star cooling data within the "nuclear medium cooling" scenario, provided that the effective pion gap decreases more steeply with density and/or the proton pairing gap is shifted to smaller densities as the EoS becomes stiffer.

## Method

The authors computed cooling curves for neutron stars using three hadronic equations of state of increasing stiffness: HDD (maximum mass 2.06 solar masses), DD2 (maximum mass 2.43 solar masses), and DD2vex (maximum mass 2.70 solar masses). They tuned the density-dependent proton pairing gap profiles and the effective pion gap (which controls the medium-modified Urca process emissivity) to fit observed surface temperatures of compact stars, including the Cassiopeia A source, while suppressing pion condensation. The neutrino emissivities, specific heat, crust properties, and heat conductivity were taken from earlier works based on the HHJ and HDD equations of state.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star masses: 2.43 solar masses for DD2 EoS, 2.70 solar masses for DD2vex EoS, compared to 2.06 solar masses for the softer HDD EoS
- Cas A mass estimated as 1.6 - 1.9 solar masses with DD2vex EoS, above the 1.5 solar mass value evaluated with the HDD EoS
- For the DD2vex EoS, the direct Urca processes occur for masses above 2.51 solar masses; for the DD2 EoS, the direct Urca threshold is not reached
- For a 1.5 solar mass star: radius approximately 14 km for DD2vex, 13 km for DD2, and 12 km for HDD
- The DD2vex EoS with excluded volume parameter v = 4.0 fm^3 (nucleon radius approximately 0.6 fm) breaches causality above a central density of about 2.55 times saturation density
- The DD2 EoS maximum central density is about 5.1 times saturation density; the HDD EoS maximum central density is about 7.7 times saturation density

## Context

This work extends the nuclear medium cooling scenario previously developed by the same group using the HHJ and HDD equations of state (Blaschke 2004, Grigorian 2005, Blaschke 2011, Blaschke 2013). It addresses the need for stiffer equations of state to accommodate the 2 solar mass pulsar measurements (Demorest, Anotoniadis) and possible even higher masses such as the 2.68 solar mass estimate for PSR J1311-3430 (Romani). The results enable future studies of hybrid stars with quark matter cores, since the stiff DD2vex EoS provides a hadronic baseline that can accommodate a phase transition to color-superconducting quark matter while still satisfying the 2 solar mass constraint.
