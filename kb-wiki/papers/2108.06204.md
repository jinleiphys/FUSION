# The impact of transverse Slavnov-Taylor identities on dynamical chiral symmetry breaking

> We extend earlier studies of transverse Ward-Fradkin-Green-Takahashi identities in QED, their usefulness to constrain the transverse fermion-boson vertex and their importance for multiplicative renormalizability, to the equivalent gauge identities in QCD. To this end, we consider transverse Slavnov-Taylor identities that constrain the transverse quark-gluon vertex and derive its eight associated scalar form factors. The complete vertex can be expressed in terms of the quark's mass and wave-renormalization functions, the ghost-dressing function, the quark-ghost scattering amplitude and a set of eight form factors. The latter parametrize the hitherto unknown nonlocal tensor structure in the transverse Slavnov-Taylor identity which arises from the Fourier transform of a four-point function involving a Wilson line in coordinate space. We determine the functional form of these eight form factors with the constraints provided by the Bashir-Bermudez vertex and study the effects of this novel vertex on the quark in the Dyson-Schwinger equation using lattice QCD input for the gluon and ghost propagators. We observe significant dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and a mass gap that leads to a constituent mass of the order of 500 MeV for the light quarks. The flavor dependence of the mass and wave-renormalization functions as well as their analytic behavior on the complex momentum plane is studied and as an application we calculate the quark condensate and the pion's weak decay constant in the chiral limit. Both are in very good agreement with their reference values.

## Key claim

The authors extend transverse Takahashi identities previously applied in QED to QCD, deriving a fully dressed quark-gluon vertex constrained by transverse Slavnov-Taylor identities, and show that this vertex produces significant dynamical chiral symmetry breaking with a constituent quark mass of approximately 500 MeV when using lattice QCD input for gluon and ghost propagators, without introducing additional strength via form factors or kernel modifications.

## Method

The authors decompose the quark-gluon vertex into longitudinal and transverse tensor structures using the basis of Kizilersu et al. (1995), solving the longitudinal form factors from the standard Slavnov-Taylor identity and deriving the eight transverse form factors from two transverse Slavnov-Taylor identities. The unknown nonlocal tensor structure in the transverse identities is parametrized using the Bashir-Bermudez ansatz (2011) with eight parameters constrained by multiplicative renormalizability, and the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation is solved with the complete vertex using lattice QCD gluon and ghost propagators as input.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass of approximately 500 MeV for light quarks, obtained with strong coupling α_s(μ) ≈ 0.3 renormalized at μ = 4.3 GeV
- Light current quark masses m_{u,d} ≈ 3–4 MeV at a perturbative scale, compared to the dynamically generated constituent mass
- Quark condensate and pion weak decay constant calculated in the chiral limit, both in very good agreement with reference values
- The sum of two u and one d current-quark masses at 2 GeV in the MS scheme amounts to merely 1% of the proton mass
- Lattice QCD simulation by Yang et al. (2018) concludes kinetic quark energy and gluon field are responsible for 68% of the proton mass, trace anomaly contributes 23%, and only 9% is due to the scalar condensate
- Nucleon sigma term σ_N ≈ 50–60 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Albino et al. (2018) that combined Ward-Fradkin-Green-Takahashi identities with transverse Takahashi identities in QED to constrain the fermion-boson vertex and ensure multiplicative renormalizability. The authors build upon the Bashir-Bermudez vertex ansatz (2011) and prior work on the quark-ghost scattering kernel by Rojas et al. (2013) and Aguilar et al. (2010, 2016, 2018), applying these techniques to QCD for the first time. This enables future parameter-free determinations of all transverse vertex functions and improves upon the rainbow-ladder truncation, which fails to describe higher excited meson states, scalar and axialvector channels, and heavy-light mesons as noted by Maris et al. (1997, 1999), Qin et al. (2011), and Rojas et al. (2014).
