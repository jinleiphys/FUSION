# Dynamical quark recombination in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions and the proton to pion ratio

> We study quark thermal recombination as a function of energy density during the evolution of a heavy-ion collision in a numerical model that reproduces aspects of QCD phenomenology. We show that starting with a set of free quarks (or quarks and antiquarks) the probability to form colorless clusters of three quarks differs from that to form colorless clusters of quark-antiquark and that the former has a sharp jump at a critical energy density whereas the latter transits smoothly from the low to the high energy density domains. We interpret this as a quantitative difference in the production of baryons and mesons with energy density. We use this approach to compute the proton and pion spectra in a Bjorken scenario that incorporates the evolution of these probabilities with energy density, and therefore with proper time. From the spectra, we compute the proton to pion ratio and compare to data at the highest RHIC energies. We show that for a standard choice of parameters, this ratio reaches one, though the maximum is very sensitive to the initial evolution proper time.

## Key claim
The probability to form colorless three-quark clusters (baryons) exhibits a sharp jump at a critical energy density around 0.7 GeV/fm³, whereas the probability to form quark-antiquark clusters (mesons) transitions smoothly from low to high energy density; this difference is used to compute the proton-to-pion ratio, which can reach one for a standard parameter choice, but the maximum is very sensitive to the initial evolution proper time.

## Method
The authors employed the string-flip model, a many-body quark potential that confines quarks into color-singlet clusters at low density and becomes a free Fermi gas at high density, using a variational Monte Carlo approach with 384 particles (192 quarks and 192 antiquarks) in a three-dimensional box. They computed the optimal variational parameter as a function of energy density for baryon-like and meson-like systems, then defined the probability to form baryons as the product of the normalized variational parameter and the fraction of three-quark clusters, and the probability to form mesons as the normalized variational parameter alone. These probabilities were incorporated into a Bjorken 1+1 expansion scenario to compute proton and pion transverse momentum spectra and their ratio.

## Key numbers
- The critical energy density for the sharp drop in the baryon variational parameter is around 0.7 GeV/fm³.
- The proton-to-pion ratio reaches one for a standard choice of parameters at the highest RHIC energies (Au + Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV).
- The initial evolution proper times tested were tau_0 = 0.5, 0.75, and 1 fm, with a final freeze-out proper time tau_f = 3.5 fm and initial temperature T_0 = 200 MeV, corresponding to a final freeze-out temperature of approximately 120 MeV.
- The simulation used 384 particles: 192 quarks and 192 antiquarks, comprising 32 u (anti-u) plus 32 d (anti-d) quarks in each of the three color charges.

## Context
This work extends the recombination model of Fries et al. (2003) by incorporating a dynamical, density-dependent probability for quark clustering, rather than assuming hadronization at a single temperature. It contrasts with the static degeneracy-factor approach of previous recombination models and with the behavior in p + p collisions, where the proton-to-pion ratio never exceeds one. The results enable future studies incorporating radial flow effects, which the authors note would shift the ratio's maximum to lower p_t values, and can be compared to lattice QCD results on the phase transition from Karsch (2002).
