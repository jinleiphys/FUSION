# Low Momentum Nucleon-Nucleon Interaction and Fermi Liquid Theory

> We use the induced interaction of Babu and Brown to derive two novel relations between the quasiparticle interaction in nuclear matter and the unique low momentum nucleon-nucleon interaction V_low k in vacuum. These relations provide two independent constraints on the Fermi liquid parameters of nuclear matter. We derive the full renormalization group equations in the particle-hole channels from the induced interaction. The new constraints, together with the Pauli principle sum rules, define four combinations of Fermi liquid parameters that are invariant under the renormalization group flow. Using empirical values for the spin-independent Fermi liquid parameters, we are able to compute the major spin-dependent ones by imposing the new constraints and the Pauli principle sum rules. The effects of tensor forces are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper derives two novel constraints that relate the Fermi liquid parameters of nuclear matter to the unique low momentum nucleon-nucleon interaction \(V_{\text{low k}}\) in vacuum, specifically at the cutoff \(\Lambda = k_{\text{F}}\), under the condition that the induced interaction of Babu and Brown generates the complete particle-hole parquet for the scattering amplitude.

## Method

The authors use the induced interaction formalism of Babu and Brown (1970) to derive integral equations connecting the quasiparticle interaction in nuclear matter to the s-wave low momentum potential \(V_{\text{low k}}(0,0)\). They solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the particle-hole vertex in the zero sound channel simultaneously with the induced interaction equation, taking the Landau limit where momentum transfer \(q \to 0\). The resulting constraints, together with the Pauli principle sum rules, define four combinations of Fermi liquid parameters that are invariant under the renormalization group flow, allowing the authors to compute spin-dependent parameters from empirical spin-independent ones.

## Key numbers

- The cutoff \(\Lambda\) for the unique \(V_{\text{low k}}\) is taken at \(\Lambda = k_{\text{F}}\), with \(k_{\text{F}}\) the Fermi momentum of nuclear matter.
- The low momentum potential \(V_{\text{low k}}(0,0)\) is nearly independent of \(\Lambda\) for \(1\;\text{fm}^{-1} \lesssim \Lambda \lesssim 3\;\text{fm}^{-1}\) in the \(^1S_0\) channel, while in the \(^3S_1\) channel only a weak linear dependence on \(\Lambda\) remains in the same range.
- The density of states at the Fermi surface is \(N(0) = \frac{2 m^{\star} k_{\text{F}}}{\pi^2}\), where \(m^{\star}\) is the effective mass.
- The effective mass relation is \(m^{\star}/m = 1 + F_1/3\).
- The incompressibility is \(K = \frac{3 \hbar^2 k_{\text{F}}^2}{m^{\star}} (1 + F_0)\).
- The symmetry energy is \(E_{\text{sym}} = \frac{\hbar^2 k_{\text{F}}^2}{6 m^{\star}} (1 + F_0^\prime)\).

## Context

This work extends the renormalization group approach to nuclear forces developed by Bogner, Kuo, and Corragio (2001), who constructed the unique low momentum potential \(V_{\text{low k}}\) from realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions. The paper connects this vacuum potential to Landau's Fermi liquid theory of nuclear matter, building on the induced interaction formalism of Babu and Brown (1970) and its application to nuclear matter by Sjoberg (1976). The derived constraints enable the computation of spin-dependent Fermi liquid parameters from empirical spin-independent ones, and the results are invariant under the renormalization group flow towards the Fermi surface, providing a bridge between effective field theory treatments of the nucleon-nucleon interaction and the phenomenology of nuclear matter.
