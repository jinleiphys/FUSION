# Heavy-to-light ratios as a test of medium-induced energy loss at RHIC and the LHC

> The ratio of nuclear modification factors of high-$p_T$ heavy-flavored mesons tolight-flavored hadrons (heavy-to-light ratio) is shown to be a sensitive tool to test medium-induced energy loss at RHIC and LHC energies. Heavy-to-light ratios of $D$ mesons at RHIC in the region $7<p_T<12$ GeV, and of $D$ and $B$ mesons at the LHC in the region $10<p_T<20$ GeV, are proposed for such a test. Finally, the different contributions to the nuclear modification factor for electrons at RHIC are analyzed. Preliminary PHENIX and STAR data are compatible with radiative energy loss provided the contribution of electrons from beauty decays is small compared to that from charm.

# Heavy-to-light ratios as a test of medium-induced energy loss at RHIC and the LHC

## Key claim
Heavy-to-light ratios of D mesons at RHIC and of D and B mesons at the LHC are sensitive tools for testing medium-induced energy loss, but the mass effect dominates only in the region below 7–8 GeV at RHIC where fragmentation uncertainties are large; the proposed test windows are 7 < pT < 12 GeV at RHIC and 10 < pT < 20 GeV at the LHC, where both color charge and mass effects play a comparable role.

## Method
The authors used a leading-order perturbative QCD formalism implemented in the PYTHIA event generator, supplemented by radiative energy loss via quenching weights that give the probability for a parton traversing a medium characterized by the transport coefficient q̂ and length L to lose a given fraction of its energy. The geometry of the medium was modeled realistically, and after quenching, partons were fragmented according to standard procedures. For electrons at RHIC, state-of-the-art FONLL (fixed-order NLO plus NLL resummation) computations of heavy flavor production were supplemented with energy loss via quenching weights.

## Key numbers
- At RHIC, the transport coefficient q̂ is fixed from light hadron suppression in central Au–Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, lying within the range 4–14 GeV^2/fm.
- For RHIC, the proposed test window for D meson heavy-to-light ratios is 7 < pT < 12 GeV.
- For the LHC, extrapolations from RHIC give q̂ in the range 2.5–7 GeV^2/fm, and a wider range q̂ = 4, 25, and 100 GeV^2/fm is explored.
- For the LHC, the proposed test window for D and B meson heavy-to-light ratios is 10 < pT < 20 GeV.
- Preliminary PHENIX and STAR measurements at RHIC show a suppression factor of 3–5 for non-photonic electrons, with R_AA^e ~ 0.2–0.4 in the region 4 < pT^e < 10 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the BDMPS formalism for medium-induced gluon radiation, which is the standard explanation for high-pT hadron suppression in nucleus-nucleus collisions, to heavy-flavor mesons. The authors build on prior calculations by Dokshitzer (2001) and Djordjevic (2003) showing that heavy quarks radiate less than light quarks due to their mass, and on their own earlier work (Armesto 2005) for the full model. The analysis enables tests of whether radiative energy loss alone can explain the observed suppression of electrons from heavy flavor decays at RHIC, and provides predictions for D and B meson measurements at the LHC. The results are compatible with preliminary PHENIX and STAR data only if the contribution of electrons from beauty decays is smaller than predicted by FONLL calculations.
