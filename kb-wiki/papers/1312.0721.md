# Thermal conductivity of the quark matter for the SU(2) light-flavor sector

> We investigate the thermal conductivity ($κ$) of the quark matter at finite quark chemical potential $(μ)$ and temperature $(T)$, employing the Green-Kubo formula, for the SU(2) light-flavor sector with the finite current-quark mass $m=5$ MeV. As a theoretical framework, we construct an effective thermodynamic potential from the $(μ,T)$-modified liquid-instanton model (mLIM). Note that all the relevant model parameters are designated as functions of $T$, using the trivial-holonomy caloron solution. By solving the self-consistent equation of mLIM, we acquire the constituent-quark mass $M_0$ as a function of $T$ and $μ$, satisfying the universal-class patterns of the chiral phase transition. From the numerical results for $κ$, we observe that there emerges a peak at $μ\approx200$ MeV for the low-$T$ region, i.e. $T\lesssim100$ MeV. As $T$ increase over $T\approx100$ MeV, the curve for $κ$ is almost saturated as a function of $T$ in the order of $\sim10^{-1}\,\mathrm{GeV}^2$, and grows with respect to $μ$ smoothly. At the normal nuclear-matter density $ρ_0=0.17\,\mathrm{fm}^{-3}$, $κ$ shows its maximum $6.22\,\mathrm{GeV}^2$ at $T\approx10$ MeV, then decreases exponentially down to $κ\approx0.2\,\mathrm{GeV}^2$. We also compute the ratio of $κ$ and the entropy density, i.e. $κ/s$ as a function of $(μ,T)$ which is a monotonically decreasing function for a wide range of $T$, then approaches a lower bound at very high $T$: $κ/s_\mathrm{min}\gtrsim0.3\,\mathrm{GeV}^{-1}$ in the vicinity of $μ=0$.

## Key claim
The thermal conductivity of quark matter shows a peak at μ ≈ 200 MeV for low temperatures (T ≲ 100 MeV), but this behavior is model-dependent and arises specifically from the Gaussian-type quark spectral density and Fermi distribution employed within the modified liquid-instanton model (mLIM). The high-temperature behavior (T ≳ 100 MeV) saturates at order 10⁻¹ GeV², which is about an order of magnitude larger than some prior NJL model results but an order of magnitude smaller than others.

## Method
The author constructs an effective thermodynamic potential from the (μ,T)-modified liquid-instanton model (mLIM) for SU(2) light-flavor quarks with current-quark mass m = 5 MeV. All model parameters (average instanton size ρ̄ and inter-instanton distance R̄) are made temperature-dependent using the trivial-holonomy caloron solution. The constituent-quark mass M₀(T,μ) is obtained by solving the self-consistent gap equation, and the thermal conductivity κ is computed via the Green-Kubo formula using a Gaussian-type quark spectral density. The entropy density s is derived from the thermodynamic potential, and the ratio κ/s is evaluated.

## Key numbers
- At normal nuclear-matter density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³, κ shows a maximum of 6.22 GeV² at T ≈ 10 MeV, then decreases exponentially to κ ≈ 0.2 GeV² for T = (100–200) MeV.
- At (μ,T) = (0,150) MeV, κ = 0.12 GeV².
- At T = 200 MeV, κ ≈ 0.13 GeV² for μ = 0 and κ ≈ 0.27 GeV² for μ = 300 MeV.
- The critical end point (CEP) is located at (μ,T) ≈ (235,56) MeV.
- The lower bound for κ/s at very high T (≈ 0.85 GeV) is κ/s_min ≳ 0.3 GeV⁻¹ in the vicinity of μ = 0, with numerical values (0.298–0.317) GeV⁻¹ for μ = (0–325) MeV.

## Context
This work extends the study of transport coefficients in quark matter using instanton-based models, following the author's previous work on shear viscosity (Nam 2013). It contrasts with results from the NJL model by Iwasaki (2009), which found κ ≈ 0.01 GeV² at μ = 0 for T = (150–230) MeV, and with the NJL model by Marty (2013), which found κ ≈ 4.4 GeV² at (μ,T) = (0,200) MeV. The results are qualitatively similar in shape to the meson-gas model of Fernandez-Fraile (2009) but differ in magnitude. The observed lower bound for κ/s is analogous to the KSS bound for η/s and invites further testing by AdS/QCD, lattice QCD, and other effective approaches.
