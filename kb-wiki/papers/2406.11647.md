# Helicity Evolution at Small $x$: Quark to Gluon and Gluon to Quark Transition Operators

> We include the quark to gluon and gluon to quark shock-wave transition operators into the small Bjorken-$x$ evolution equations for helicity in the flavor-singlet channel derived earlier. While such transitions do not affect the large-$N_c$ version of the evolution equations for helicity, the large-$N_c \& N_f$ equations are affected. ($N_c$ and $N_f$ are the numbers of quark colors and flavors, respectively.) We derive the corresponding corrected large-$N_c \& N_f$ equations for the polarized dipole amplitudes contributing to the flavor-singlet quark and gluon helicity distributions in the double-logarithmic approximation (DLA), resumming powers of $α_s \, \ln^2 (1/x)$ with $α_s$ the strong coupling constant. We solve these equations iteratively and extract the polarized splitting functions up to four loops. We show that our splitting functions agree with the fixed-order perturbative calculations up to and including the existing three-loops results. Similar to the large-$N_c$ helicity evolution in the shock-wave approach, our large-$N_c \& N_f$ small-$x$ splitting functions agree with those obtained in the infrared evolution equations framework up to three loops, but appear to slightly disagree at four loops.

## Key claim

The paper shows that quark-to-gluon and gluon-to-quark shock-wave transition operators, previously thought to cancel for flavor-singlet quantities, do generate double-logarithmic-approximation (DLA) terms and must be included in the large-\(N_c \& N_f\) helicity evolution equations at small \(x\); the large-\(N_c\) equations remain unchanged because they only involve gluon contributions.

## Method

The authors derive the missing \(q/\bar{q} \to G\) and \(G \to q/\bar{q}\) transition operators using the light-cone operator treatment (LCOT) method, then incorporate them into the previously derived large-\(N_c \& N_f\) helicity evolution equations for polarized dipole amplitudes. They solve the corrected equations iteratively in the double-logarithmic approximation (DLA), which resums powers of \(\alpha_s \ln^2(1/x)\), and extract the polarized DGLAP splitting functions up to four loops by expanding the solutions as power series in the strong coupling \(\alpha_s\).

## Key numbers

* The corrected large-\(N_c \& N_f\) helicity evolution equations yield polarized splitting functions that agree with fixed-order \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) calculations at one, two, and three loops (the three-loop agreement requires a minor scheme transformation).
* The splitting functions agree with those from the infrared evolution equations framework (Bartels, Ermolaev, Ryskin, 1996) up to three loops but appear to slightly disagree at four loops.
* The paper predicts the 4-loop polarized DGLAP splitting functions at small \(x\) and large-\(N_c \& N_f\) (no explicit numerical coefficients for the 4-loop result are given in the truncated text).

## Context

This work extends the shock-wave formalism for small-\(x\) helicity evolution developed by Kovchegov, Cougoulic, and collaborators (2015-2023), which had previously omitted the transition operators. The correction resolves a disagreement found in Adamiak et al. (2023) between the large-\(N_c \& N_f\) helicity evolution equations and the fixed-order NLO \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) results for \(N_f\)-dependent terms. The corrected equations now match the infrared evolution equations approach of Bartels, Ermolaev, and Ryskin (1996) through three loops, enabling consistent four-loop predictions for polarized splitting functions at small \(x\).
