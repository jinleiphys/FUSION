# Nucleon-Nucleon Scattering in a Harmonic Potential

> The discrete energy-eigenvalues of two nucleons interacting with a finite-range nuclear force and confined to a harmonic potential are used to numerically reconstruct the free-space scattering phase shifts. The extracted phase shifts are compared to those obtained from the exact continuum scattering solution and agree within the uncertainties of the calculations. Our results suggest that it might be possible to determine the amplitudes for the scattering of complex systems, such as n-d, n-t or n-alpha, from the energy-eigenvalues confined to finite volumes using ab-initio bound-state techniques.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that free-space nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts can be reconstructed from the discrete energy eigenvalues of two nucleons confined in a harmonic potential, but only after extrapolating to zero oscillator frequency to remove finite-range distortions of the nuclear force that are not exponentially suppressed.

## Method

The authors diagonalize the Hamiltonian for two nucleons interacting via the JISP16 potential in a harmonic oscillator basis with an external confining harmonic potential of frequency ω, for uncoupled partial waves (¹S₀, ³P₀, ³D₂, ¹F₃). They extract phase shifts from the energy eigenvalues using an analytic zero-range relation analogous to Lüscher's formula, then extrapolate the resulting p cot δ values to ω = 0 using a quadratic fit in ω² to recover the free-space scattering phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- For the toy spherical well model (V₀ = 48 MeV, R₀ = 1.7 fm) with binding energy B₀ = 2.22 MeV, extracted phase shifts agree with exact results within 0.1% for ω < 4 MeV
- For the ¹S₀ channel with JISP16, calculations were performed for ω from 0.4 MeV to 15.0 MeV, with a maximum basis dimension of 1800×1800
- For the deuteron bound state (B₀ = 2.224575 MeV, γ₀ ≈ 45.7 MeV, r₀ ≈ 1.74 fm), the zero-range coefficient C_ZR = 0.0944 MeV⁻¹
- For ω ≤ 1.0 MeV in the ¹S₀ channel, the lowest eight energy eigenvalues range from E₁ = 0.66642 MeV (δ = 58.5279°) at ω = 0.4 MeV to E₈ = 29.9188 MeV (δ = 48.6454°) at ω = 1.0 MeV

## Context

This work extends the concept of Lüscher's method (Lüscher, 1990) from finite periodic volumes to harmonic confinement, building on earlier suggestions by Bira (2009) and calculations at the unitary limit by Stetcu (2010). The approach is motivated by the possibility of using existing ab-initio bound-state techniques like the No Core Shell Model (Navratil, 2000) to compute scattering amplitudes for complex systems such as n-d, n-t, or n-α, which currently require more resource-intensive methods like the Resonating Group Method coupled with NCSM (Quaglioni, 2008).
