# CASCADE3 A Monte Carlo event generator based on TMDs

> The CASCADE3 Monte Carlo event generator based on Transverse Momentum Dependent (TMD) parton densities is described. Hard processes which are generated in collinear factorization with LO multileg or NLO parton level generators are extended by adding transverse momenta to the initial partons according to TMD densities and applying dedicated TMD parton showers and hadronization. Processes with off-shell kinematics within $k_t$-factorization, either internally implemented or from external packages via LHE files, can be processed for parton showering and hadronization. The initial state parton shower is tied to the TMD parton distribution, with all parameters fixed by the TMD distribution.

## Key claim

CASCADE3 extends collinear factorization to include transverse momenta in the initial state by either adding transverse momentum to on-shell processes or using off-shell processes directly, with the initial state parton shower tied to the TMD parton distribution such that all parameters are fixed by the TMD distribution rather than requiring separate tuning.

## Method

The generator replaces collinear parton densities with TMD densities and integrates over transverse momenta, ensuring the integral of the TMD density agrees with the collinear density by construction for PB-TMDs. For on-shell processes, transverse momenta are assigned according to the TMD density and the parton-parton system is boosted to its center-of-mass frame, rotated, and the energy and longitudinal components recalculated taking into account virtual masses from the transverse momenta. For off-shell processes within kt-factorization, matrix elements with off-shell kinematics are used either from internally implemented processes or from external packages via LHE files, then processed for parton showering and hadronization.

## Key numbers

- Drell-Yan production in pp collisions at sqrt(s)=13 TeV is shown in the mass range 30 < m_DY < 2000 GeV
- For bottom quarks in off-shell processes, a mass of m=5 GeV is used
- The cut-off parameter q0 for the parton shower evolution can be chosen as low as q0 ~ Lambda_QCD ~ 0.3 GeV
- PB-TMD parton densities are determined from fits to HERA DIS F2 measurements for Q^2 > 3 GeV^2
- The default scale for the QCD final state shower is mu^2 = 2*(m_{1_perp}^2 + m_{2_perp}^2)

## Context

This work extends the original Cascade Monte Carlo generator, which was developed for small-x processes based on high-energy factorization and the CCFM evolution equation, to cover the full kinematic range by applying the Parton Branching (PB) method and corresponding PB TMD parton densities (Hautmann 2017). The approach enables consistent treatment of transverse momentum effects across all flavor species including quarks, gluons and photons at both small and large x, and allows matching of TMD parton densities to collinear hard process calculations from automated tools like MadGraph5_aMC@NLO and Powheg. The PB-TMD approach allows parton shower uncertainties to be recast as uncertainties of the TMDs, which can be fitted to experimental data in a systematic global manner.
