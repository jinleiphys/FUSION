# P- and T-violation Tests with Polarized Resonance Neutrons

> The enhancements of CP-violating effects in resonance neutron transmissionthrough polarized targets are studied for 2 possible versions of experiment. The importance is stressed of error analysis and of pseudomagnetic effects' compensation.

# P- and T-violation Tests with Polarized Resonance Neutrons

## Key claim

The paper argues that the CP-violating observables T and X̃, previously proposed as alternatives to the original η_T measurement, do not automatically inherit the same 5-6 order of magnitude enhancement near p-wave resonances; the actual enhancement depends critically on whether pseudomagnetic precession is compensated by an external magnetic field, and without such compensation the relative error oscillates uncontrollably with target thickness.

## Method

The authors analytically derived the energy-dependent expressions for the CP-violating observables T and X̃ in terms of the complex scattering amplitudes A, B, C, and D, using the spin density matrix formalism from prior work. They then evaluated these expressions in the vicinity of a p-wave resonance coupled to an s-wave resonance, assuming a CP-violating matrix element V_T that is 10⁻⁴ of the P-violating one. The relative statistical error of each observable was computed as a function of target thickness x, with and without compensation of the pseudomagnetic field by an external magnetic field H.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of CP-violating to P-violating interaction strength is assumed to be V_T/V_P = 10⁻⁴.
- The dynamical enhancement factor V_T/d is approximately F_T × 10³, where F_T is the CP-violating strength relative to strong interaction.
- The resonance enhancement factor d/Γ is approximately 10³.
- The entrance channel hindrance factor γ^p/γ^s is approximately 10⁻³.
- The net enhancement of the T quantity is a factor of 10³, compared to 10⁵–10⁶ for η_T.
- The optimal target thickness for T with compensation is approximately x ≈ 2.5λ, where λ is the neutron mean free path.
- Without compensation, the first minimum of the relative error for T occurs at x ≈ 10⁻²λ.
- The relative error for T changes only by a factor of 2–3 when the spin rotation angle φ varies from 0° to 200°.
- The required accuracy for measuring the rotation angle φ is about 5°.
- The width of the second angular region (near θ = π/2) without compensation is 10⁻⁴ to 10⁻⁷ rad; with full compensation it increases to a few degrees.
- The energy resolution considered reasonable for experiment is ΔE ≈ 10⁻² eV.

## Context

This work extends the original proposals by Bunakov and collaborators (1980s) for CP-violation tests using resonance neutron transmission through polarized targets, which predicted 5-6 order enhancement of η_T near p-wave resonances. It addresses the criticism by Stodolsky (1980s) regarding pseudomagnetic precession effects camouflaging CP-violating signals, and the alternative observable proposed by Stodolsky (N_+- − N_-+) and later by Serebrov (1990s). The paper provides the first detailed energy-dependent analysis of these alternative observables T and X̃, showing that the compensation scheme originally suggested by Bunakov and Novikov (1990s) is essential for achieving practical measurement accuracy. The results enable experimentalists to design optimal target thickness and magnetic field compensation for future CP-violation searches at neutron resonance facilities.
