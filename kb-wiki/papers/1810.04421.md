# Study on alpha decay chains of Z = 122 superheavy nuclei with deformation effects and Langer modification

> In this work study on alpha decay chains emerging from isotopes of Z = 122 superheavy nuclei is carried out with emphasize on nuclear deformations and Langer modification. The interest in this particular superheavy nuclei is due to the recent experimental efforts to synthesize the isotope ^{299}120 in a fusion reaction at the velocity filter SHIP (GSI Darmstadt), which makes synthesis of Z = 122 nuclei to occur in the near future, and in turn will give the experimentalist the chance observe the decays associated with the isotopes of this nuclei. We perform our calculations by choosing the Woods Saxon potential for nuclear interaction, along with Coulomb potential and centrifugal potential within the framework of the WKB method. When the centrifugal term is taken in the total potential and WKB integral is done over 1D radial coordinate, it requires the use of Langer modification wherein (l + 1/2 )^2 replaces l(l +1) for consistency of WKB wave function. Hence we have used this Langer modified centrifugal potential. The orientations of deformed nuclei are important, as it affects the touching distance and also influence the nuclear and Coulomb potential, and thus can alter the values of penetration integral and half-life. The results obtained by our calculations are in agreement with the values obtained from various phenomenological models. This study brings out the unique characteristics of alpha chains associated with each isotope, which will be helpful for the experimentalist to decide the isotope they would like to synthesize and also for their post-synthesis study.

## Key claim

The paper claims that alpha decay chains from even isotopes of Z=122 superheavy nuclei can be predicted using a Woods-Saxon potential with deformation effects and the Langer modification, and that these predictions agree with phenomenological models. However, the results are conditional on the specific choice of deformation parameters (β₂, β₃, β₄, β₆) taken from Moller et al. (2016) and on the assumption that the Coulomb potential acts both inside and outside the parent nucleus, which the authors acknowledge is an approximation.

## Method

The authors used the WKB method with a Woods-Saxon nuclear potential, a Coulomb potential that includes deformation and orientation effects of both daughter and fragment nuclei, and a centrifugal potential with the Langer modification (replacing l(l+1) with (l+1/2)²). They calculated barrier penetrability by averaging over 400 combinations of orientation angles (θ₁, θ₂) for the daughter and fragment, then computed alpha decay half-lives using a spectroscopic factor Sα = 6.3×10⁻³ for even-mass parent nuclei. Spontaneous fission half-lives were calculated using the phenomenological formula of Xu et al. (2008) for comparison.

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of alpha decay in superheavy nuclei using the WKB method with deformation effects, building on approaches by Sandulescu et al. (1980) and Rahimi et al. (2018) who used Woods-Saxon potentials with deformation. It specifically incorporates the Langer modification, previously applied in decay studies by Soylu et al. (2015) and Ismail et al. (2017), and includes β₆ deformation parameters from Moller et al. (2016) which were often omitted in earlier works. The results are motivated by the recent experimental effort at SHIP (GSI Darmstadt) to synthesize ²⁹⁹¹²⁰ (Hofmann, 2016), and the predicted unique alpha chain signatures for each isotope of Z=122 are intended to guide experimentalists in isotope identification and synthesis planning.
