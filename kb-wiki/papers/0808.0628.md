# P-wave Lambda N - Sigma N coupling and the spin-orbit splitting of 9 Lambda Be

> We reexamine the spin-orbit splitting of 9 Lambda Be excited states in terms of the SU_6 quark-model baryon-baryon interaction. The previous folding procedure to generate the Lambda alpha spin-orbit potential from the quark-model Lambda N LS interaction kernel predicted three to five times larger values for Delta E_{ell s}=E_x(3/2^+)-E_x(5/2^+) in the model FSS and fss2. This time, we calculate Lambda alpha LS Born kernel, starting from the LS components of the nuclear-matter G-matrix for the Lambda hyperon. This framework makes it possible to take full account of an important P-wave Lambda N - Sigma N coupling through the antisymmetric LS^{(-)} force involved in the Fermi-Breit interaction. We find that the experimental value, Delta E^{exp}_{ell s}=43 pm 5 keV, is reproduced by the quark-model G-matrix LS interaction with a Fermi-momentum around k_F=1.0 fm^{-1}, when the model FSS is used in the energy-independent renormalized RGM formalism.

## Key claim

The quark-model FSS interaction can reproduce the experimental spin-orbit splitting of 9ΛBe excited states (43 ± 5 keV) when the Λα LS Born kernel is generated from nuclear-matter G-matrix components with a Fermi momentum around kF = 1.0 fm−1, but only if the energy-independent renormalized RGM formalism is used; the model fss2 gives too large splitting of almost 200 keV.

## Method

The authors performed Λαα Faddeev calculations in the jj-coupling scheme, generating the Λα LS Born kernel from the LS components of the nuclear-matter G-matrix for the Λ hyperon rather than from direct folding of the quark-model ΛN LS interaction kernel. The G-matrix equation was solved for the energy-independent renormalized RGM quark-model baryon-baryon interaction using a constant Fermi momentum kF, with the continuous prescription for intermediate spectra and self-consistent single-particle potentials. The folding formula derived in Ref. [B8a] was employed to obtain the Λα Born kernel from the hyperon-nucleon G-matrices, including explicit treatment of nonlocality and center-of-mass motion between the hyperon and the α cluster.

## Key numbers

- Experimental spin-orbit splitting of 9ΛBe: ΔEexpℓs = 43 ± 5 keV
- Model FSS with ω(q1,q) prescription: ΔEℓs = 39 keV (kF = 1.07 fm−1), 68 keV (kF = 1.20 fm−1), 96 keV (kF = 1.35 fm−1)
- Model FSS with ω(q,K) prescription: ΔEℓs = 56 keV (kF = 1.07 fm−1), 87 keV (kF = 1.20 fm−1), 118 keV (kF = 1.35 fm−1)
- Model fss2 with ω(q1,q) prescription: ΔEℓs = 205 keV (kF = 1.07 fm−1), 213 keV (kF = 1.20 fm−1), 223 keV (kF = 1.35 fm−1)
- Model fss2 with ω(q,K) prescription: ΔEℓs = 204 keV (kF = 1.07 fm−1), 214 keV (kF = 1.20 fm−1), 220 keV (kF = 1.35 fm−1)
- kF values required to reproduce ΔEexpℓs with FSS: 1.09 fm−1 (ω(q1,q) prescription), 1.02 fm−1 (ω(q,K) prescription), 1.25 fm−1 (energy-dependent RGM kernel)
- Ground-state energy of 9ΛBe: −6.596 to −6.598 MeV (experimental: −6.62 ± 0.04 MeV)
- Λα bound-state energy predicted by FSS: −2.95 MeV (kF = 1.20 fm−1, ω(q1,q)), −4.04 MeV (kF = 1.07 fm−1, ω(q1,q))

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Faddeev calculations of the Λαα system (Fujiwara et al., 2006) which used direct folding of the quark-model ΛN LS RGM kernel and predicted 3 to 5 times larger spin-orbit splitting values. The new G-matrix approach enables full treatment of the P-wave ΛN–ΣN coupling through the antisymmetric LS(−) force from the Fermi-Breit interaction, which was conjectured in the earlier work to be essential for reproducing the small experimental splitting. The results enable construction of improved quark-model baryon-baryon interactions with consistent central and LS components for Λ hypernuclei, as the current model FSS shows inconsistencies in the central ΛN interaction (overbinding in hypertriton calculations from Fujiwara et al., 2007) and the model fss2 requires improvement of its spin-orbit contribution from effective meson-exchange potentials.
