# Charmonium ground states in presence of strong magnetic fields

> The in-medium masses of the lowest S-wave charmonium states (vector meson, $J/ψ$ and pseudoscalar meson, $η_c$) and P-wave charmonium states (scalar, $χ_{c0}$ and axialvector $χ_{c1}$) are investigated in magnetized nuclear matter, within the framework of QCD sum rule approach. These are computed from the medium modifications of the scalar as well as twist--2 gluon condensates, obtained from the medium modifications of a scalar dilaton field, within a chiral effective model. The effects of the magnetic field, isospin asymmetry and density on the masses of these charmonium states have been investigated. The modifications of the masses of the P-wave charmonium states ($χ_{c0}$ and $χ_{c1}$) are observed to be much larger as compared to those of the S-wave states, $J/ψ$ and $η_c$ within the QCD sum rule approach. The effects of the coupling of the spin with the magnetic field are also investigated in the present work, which result in the mixing of the spin zero charmonium state with the longitudinal component of the vector meson. This leads to an increase (drop) in mass of the longitudinal $J/ψ$ ($η_c$) for the S-wave states. The effects of the spin-magnetic field interactions are observed to be dominant at high magnetic fields.

## Key claim

The in-medium masses of S-wave charmonium states (J/ψ and ηc) and P-wave states (χc0 and χc1) in magnetized nuclear matter are computed using QCD sum rules with gluon condensates from a chiral effective model, where the spin-magnetic field interaction causes mixing between the longitudinal J/ψ and ηc, producing an increase in the J/ψ mass and a drop in the ηc mass that dominates at high magnetic fields.

## Method

The scalar and twist-2 gluon condensates were calculated from the medium modification of a scalar dilaton field within a chiral SU(3) effective model incorporating broken scale invariance, with the nucleon anomalous magnetic moments included. These condensates were then used in the QCD sum rule framework to compute the in-medium masses of J/ψ, ηc, χc0, and χc1 via the operator product expansion up to dimension four, with the spin-magnetic field mixing effect applied to the S-wave states using a two-state mixing formalism.

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach for charmonium masses in nuclear matter, previously applied at zero magnetic field by Klingl et al. and Kim and Lee, to include strong magnetic fields. It also builds on the chiral effective model calculations of charmonium masses by Mishra et al. and generalizes the spin-magnetic field mixing treatment of Suzuki and Lee (2017) and Alford and Strickland (2013) to asymmetric nuclear matter. The results enable future studies of charmonium decay widths to D Dbar pairs in magnetized matter using the 3P0 model or field-theoretic composite hadron models.
