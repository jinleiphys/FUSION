# Boundary between Hadron and Quark/Gluon Structure of Nuclei

> We show that the boundary between quark-dominated and hadron-dominated regions of nuclear structure may be blurred by multi-nucleon quark clusters arising from color percolation. Recent experiments supporting partial percolation in cold nuclei and full percolation in hot/dense nuclear matter include: deep inelastic lepton-nucleus scattering, relativistic heavy-ion collisions and the binding energy in $^5 He_Λ$.

# Boundary between Hadron and Quark/Gluon Structure of Nuclei

## Key claim

The boundary between quark-dominated and hadron-dominated regions of nuclear structure may be blurred by multi-nucleon quark clusters arising from color percolation, with the transition being a cross-over rather than a sharp phase boundary in finite systems.

## Method

The authors apply classical percolation theory to nuclear matter, modeling nuclei as composed of color-neutral quark clusters containing 3, 6, 9, 12, ... quarks at zero temperature, with probabilities determined by a critical separation distance of 1 fm. They compare cluster model predictions to deep inelastic scattering data on nuclei at Bjorken x > 1, extend the model to finite temperature by including a pion component, and apply the Pauli principle to constituent quarks in hypernuclei to explain binding energy anomalies.

## Key numbers

- Critical separation distance for color percolation: d_c = 1 fm (corresponding to critical radius R_c = 0.5 fm)
- Theoretical ratio of per-nucleon responses for 56Fe to 12C in the 6-quark cluster region (1 < x < 2): 1.17 ± 0.12, in agreement with experimental value 1.17 ± 0.04 ± 0.11
- Theoretical ratio for 56Fe to 12C in the 9-quark cluster region (2 < x < 3): 1.39 ± 0.14, in agreement with experimental value 1.44 with approximately 33% uncertainty
- Normal nuclear matter density: 0.17 baryons/fm^3 (Fermi momentum k_f = 1.36 fm^{-1})
- Density for 50% percolation at T=0: ρ_baryon = 0.46 fm^{-3} (2.7 times nuclear density)
- Probability of 12-quark cluster in 4He: p_12 = 0.007
- Estimated mean spacing in constituent quark shell model: ℏω_Q = 3 ℏω_N = 75 MeV
- Estimated decrease in 5He_Λ binding from Pauli blocking in 12-quark cluster: 1-2 MeV

## Context

This work extends the percolation approach of Baym (1979), Satz (1998), and earlier work by Pirner (1980) and Sato (1986) to unify hadronic and quark descriptions of nuclei. The model successfully describes JLAB data from Egiyan (2006) on inclusive electron scattering at x > 1, which cannot be explained by isolated nucleon structure alone. The authors contrast their approach with an alternative interpretation by Strikman using short-range two-nucleon correlations, and note that future experiments at the 12 GeV JLAB facility and JPARC with hypernuclei could further test the quark cluster predictions. The work also connects to lattice QCD results and heavy-ion collision freeze-out data from Andronic (2005), suggesting that percolation theory can bridge cold nuclear structure and hot/dense matter physics.
