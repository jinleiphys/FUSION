# Three-pion exchange: a gap in the nucleon-nucleon potential

> The leading contribution to the three-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon potential is calculated in the framework of chiral symmetry. It has pseudoscalar and axial components and is dominated by the former, which has a range of about 1.5 fm and tends to enhance the OPEP. The strength of this force does not depend on the pion mass and hence it survives in the chiral limit.

## Key claim

The leading three-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon potential, calculated in chiral symmetry, has pseudoscalar and axial components; the pseudoscalar component dominates, has a range of about 1.5 fm, and tends to enhance the one-pion exchange potential (OPEP), with the strength surviving in the chiral limit (i.e., it does not vanish when the pion mass goes to zero).

## Method

The authors computed the leading contribution to the three-pion exchange potential (3PEP) by starting from the amplitude for pion production (πN → ππN) in chiral perturbation theory, using an effective Lagrangian that gives the leading contribution to that amplitude. They then constructed the Born amplitude for NN → NN with three uncorrelated pions exchanged, performed the loop integrals in dimensional regularization (neglecting contact terms), and took the non-relativistic limit to obtain the configuration-space potential. The resulting potential is expressed as integrals of Yukawa-like functions, evaluated numerically and asymptotically.

## Key numbers

- The pseudoscalar component of the three-pion exchange potential has a range of about 1.5 fm.
- The threshold amplitudes for pion production from the minimal chiral model are D1 = 0.87 μ⁻³ and D2 = -2.32 μ⁻³ (using eqs. 2.12–2.15), compared to experimental values D1 = 0.80 μ⁻³ and D2 = -3.20 μ⁻³.
- In the chiral limit (μ → 0), the three-pion exchange potential behaves as V³π(r) → (1/3)(g/(4m fπ²))² (1/(4π)⁵) τ(1)·τ(2) [125 σ(1)·σ(2) + 245 S₁₂] (1/r⁷).
- The asymptotic form (large r) of the pseudoscalar spin-spin component is U₀^PS(x) → (π/(4π)⁴)(80/√3)(1 + 3/x + 13/(3x²) + 10/(3x³) + 10/(9x⁴)) e⁻³ˣ/x⁴, where x = μ r.

## Context

This work extends the chiral-symmetry treatment of the nucleon-nucleon potential beyond one-pion exchange (OPEP, established in the 1960s) and two-pion exchange (TPEP, studied extensively in the 1990s by Ordonez and van Kolck (1992) and others). It fills a gap by calculating the leading three-pion exchange contribution, which is longer-ranged than vector-meson exchanges. The results enable future calculations of NN observables including this component, and the authors note that the potential is valid for internucleon distances larger than 0.7 fm (the typical bag radius).
