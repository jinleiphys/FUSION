# The Electric Dipole Moment of the Nucleons in Holographic QCD

> We introduce the strong CP-violation in the framework of AdS/QCD model and calculate the electric dipole moments of nucleons as well as the CP-violating pion-nucleon coupling. Our holographic estimate of the electric dipole moments gives for the neutron d_n=1.08 X 10^{-16} theta (e cm), which is comparable with previous estimates. We also predict that the electric dipole moment of the proton should be precisely the minus of the neutron electric dipole moment, thus leading to a new sum rule on the electric dipole moments of baryons.

## Key claim

The holographic AdS/QCD model predicts the neutron electric dipole moment to be \(d_n = 1.08 \times 10^{-16} \, \bar\theta \, e\cdot\text{cm}\), which is comparable with previous estimates from lattice QCD, current algebra, chiral perturbation theory, and QCD sum rules; however, this result depends on the specific choice of model parameters (the IR cutoff \(z_m\), the chiral condensate \(\sigma\), the quark mass \(m\), and the coupling \(g\)) fitted to reproduce the nucleon mass.

## Method

The authors introduce strong CP-violation into a hard-wall AdS/QCD model by encoding the physical CP-violating angle \(\bar\theta\) in the complex phase of the quark mass matrix within the vacuum expectation value of the 5D scalar field \(X\), which breaks chiral symmetry. They then solve the 5D Dirac equations for nucleon wave functions \(N_1\) and \(N_2\) (representing left- and right-handed chiral baryon doublets) in the presence of this CP-violating background, and compute the resulting electric dipole moment from a 5D dipole operator that couples the nucleon fields to the \(SU(2)_L\) and \(SU(2)_R\) gauge field strengths. The CP-violating pion-nucleon coupling \(\bar g_{\pi NN}\) is also calculated by analyzing the mixing between the pion profile and the modulus of the chiral condensate induced by the CP-violating phase.

## Key numbers

* Neutron electric dipole moment: \(d_n = 1.08 \times 10^{-16} \, \bar\theta \, e\cdot\text{cm}\), for model parameters \(z_m = (330\ \text{MeV})^{-1}\), \(\sigma = (311\ \text{MeV})^3\), \(m = 2.34\ \text{MeV}\), and \(g = 9.18\).
* The ratio of the electric dipole moment to the anomalous magnetic dipole moment of nucleons is predicted as \(d_e / \mu_m^{\text{ano}} = -(\alpha - \beta) = - (g/4)(m/m_N) \bar\theta\), where \(m_N = 0.94\ \text{GeV}\).
* The CP-violating phase difference between left- and right-handed nucleon wave functions is \((\alpha - \beta) = (g/4)(m/m_N) \bar\theta\).
* The experimental upper bound on the neutron EDM is \(|d_n| < 2.9 \times 10^{-26}\ e\cdot\text{cm}\) at 90% confidence level (Baker, 2006).

## Context

This work extends the holographic QCD approach of Erlich, Katz, Son, and Stephanov (2005) and Da Rold and Pomarol (2005) by incorporating strong CP-violation and calculating nucleon electric dipole moments, complementing prior estimates from lattice QCD (Shintani, 2006), current algebra (Baluni, 1978; Crewther, 1979; Kawarabayashi, 1980), chiral perturbation theory (Pich, 1991), and QCD sum rules (Pospelov, 1999). The new sum rule \(d_p = -d_n\) (proton EDM equals minus the neutron EDM) is a model-independent prediction of holographic QCD arising from the absence of \(U(1)_B\) in the dipole operator, which could be tested by future experiments and is consistent with recent lattice results (Shintani, 2006) and the Light-Front formalism (Brodsky, 2006).
