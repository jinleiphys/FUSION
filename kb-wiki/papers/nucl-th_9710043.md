# Dilepton production and $m_T$-scaling at BEVALAC/SIS energies

> We present a dynamical study of $e^+e^-$ production in C + C and Ca + Ca collisions at BEVALAC/SIS energies on the basis of the covariant transport approach HSD employing momentum-dependent $ρ$-meson spectral functions that include the pion modifications in the nuclear medium as well as the polarization of the $ρ$-meson due to resonant $ρ- N$ scattering. We find that the experimental data from the DLS collaboration cannot be described within the $ρ$-meson spectral function approach. A dropping $η$-mass scenario leads to a good reproduction of the DLS dilepton data, however, violates the $m_T$-scaling of $π^0$ and $η$ spectra as observed by the TAPS collaboration as well as $η$ photoproduction on nuclei.

## Key claim

The new DLS dilepton data for C+C and Ca+Ca collisions at BEVALAC/SIS energies cannot be described by the in-medium ρ-meson spectral function approach alone; a dropping η-mass scenario reproduces the dilepton data but violates the m_T-scaling of π^0 and η spectra observed by the TAPS collaboration as well as η photoproduction on nuclei.

## Method

The authors performed dynamical transport calculations using the covariant HSD (Hadron-String Dynamics) approach, incorporating momentum-dependent ρ-meson spectral functions that include pion modifications in the nuclear medium and resonant ρ-N scattering. They calculated e⁺e⁻ production from multiple channels including Dalitz decays, bremsstrahlung, and vector meson decays, and compared the results to DLS dilepton data and TAPS m_T-spectra for π⁰ and η mesons. They also tested a dropping η-mass scenario with a linear density dependence and examined η photoproduction on nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The new DLS data are underestimated by a factor of 6-7 in transport calculations for invariant mass 0.15 ≤ M ≤ 0.4 GeV when no medium effects are included
- Including ρ-meson spectral functions from RappNPA or Peters reduces the discrepancy but still leaves a factor of about 3 for 0.15 ≤ M ≤ 0.4 GeV
- The dropping η-mass scenario uses α ≈ 0.18 in m*_η = m_η⁰ (1 - α ρ_B/ρ_0), giving an 18% mass decrease at normal nuclear matter density
- Maximum baryon density reached in central Ca+Ca collisions is about 2.5 ρ₀
- η absorption on ⁴⁰Ca is about 65%
- The η angular anisotropy from rescattering gives at maximum a 20% correction for the total η cross section

## Context

This work extends earlier transport calculations by Bratkovskaya, Cassing, and collaborators (1996) that described the first-generation DLS data without medium effects, and addresses the "DLS puzzle" where new DLS data (1997) showed a factor 6-7 increase over previous measurements. The study contrasts with successful applications of in-medium ρ spectral functions at SPS energies by Rapp, Wambach, and collaborators (1997) and Peters et al. (1997), and with the dropping-mass scenario of Brown and Rho (1991). The results rule out both the ρ spectral function approach and the simple dropping η-mass scenario as explanations for the DLS data, leaving the DLS puzzle unresolved and motivating further investigation of η self-energies and momentum-dependent potentials as suggested by Oset and collaborators.
