# Trace anomaly, effective degrees of freedom, and chemical potential effects near the QCD crossover

> A compact analytical scheme is presented for describing ultra-dense hadronic matter, which combines a multicomponent van der Waals (vdW)-type description with temperature-dependent effective degrees of freedom. Although the vdW formalism successfully reproduces interactions at finite density, in its standard form it cannot describe lattice-QCD thermodynamics, since it uses a fixed degeneracy. It is shown that a consistent description of the equation of state requires a temperature-dependent degeneracy $g(T)$ and an effective chemical potential $μ(T)$. Within this approach, the trace anomaly (the trace of the energy-momentum tensor), i.e. the measure of nonconformality of the energy-momentum tensor normalized to $T^4$, is naturally reproduced together with its peak structure near the crossover region. The effective chemical-potential sector becomes particularly important in baryon-rich matter, whereas for the mesonic sector a separate dynamical description of the degrees of freedom is required.

# Trace anomaly, effective degrees of freedom, and chemical potential effects near the QCD crossover

## Key claim

The QCD trace anomaly near the crossover can be reproduced by a compact phenomenological model combining a temperature-dependent effective degeneracy \(g(T)\) and an effective chemical potential \(\mu(T)\), without requiring a strong explicit Hagedorn enhancement of the degrees of freedom. The fit drives the Hagedorn-related parameter \(\alpha\) toward very small values, indicating that the dominant contribution to the trace anomaly near the crossover is governed primarily by the effective chemical-potential sector rather than by a strong growth of thermodynamic degrees of freedom.

## Method

The author constructs a phenomenological model where the trace anomaly \(\Delta(T) = (\varepsilon - 3P)/T^4\) is parameterized as \(\Delta(T) = g(T)[1 + B(\mu(T)/T)^2]\), with \(g(T)\) interpolating between hadronic and quark-gluon regimes using a piecewise function below and above the Hagedorn temperature \(T_H\), and \(\mu(T) = e_f - T^A/m_{\mathrm{eff}}\) describing the suppression of effective chemical potential with temperature. The parameters are determined by a \(\chi^2\) fit to HotQCD lattice data for the trace anomaly near vanishing baryon chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- The fit to lattice data using the small-\(\mu/T\) expansion yields \(\chi^2 = 5.56\) with reduced \(\chi^2_{\mathrm{red}} \approx 0.11\).
- The alternative mixed thermal-chemical scaling parameterization yields \(\chi^2 = 40.22\) with \(\chi^2_{\mathrm{red}} \approx 0.83\).
- The position of the trace anomaly maximum is reproduced at \(T \approx 200\) MeV.
- For baryon-rich matter at \(T \sim 160\) MeV and \(\mu_B \sim 900\) MeV, the estimated degeneracy temperature of the quark component is \(T_{\mathrm{deg}}^{\mathrm{int}} \sim 400\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the multicomponent van der Waals description of hadronic matter developed by Krivenko (2017, 2019, 2023) by incorporating temperature-dependent effective degrees of freedom to reproduce lattice-QCD thermodynamics from Borsanyi (2014) and Bazavov (2014). The approach connects the Hagedorn exponential growth of the hadronic spectrum (Hagedorn, 1965) with the trace anomaly phenomenology of Kapusta (2006). The model enables a compact analytical description of the QCD crossover that can be generalized beyond the nearly baryon-symmetric lattice-QCD regime toward dense baryonic matter relevant for heavy-ion collisions and compact-star physics, though the strongly baryon-rich regime shows worse agreement and likely requires additional nonperturbative effects.
