# Correlations in light nuclei and their relation to fine tuning and uncertainty quantifications of many body forces in low-energy nuclear physics

> The large nucleon-nucleon scattering length, and the isospin approximate symmetry, are low energy properties of quantum chromodynamics (QCD). These entail correlations in the binding energies of light nuclei, e.g., the A=3 iso-multiplet, and Tjon's correlation between the binding energy of three and four body nuclei. Using a new representation of these, we establish that they translate into a correlation between different short-range contributions to three body forces in chiral effective field theory of low-energy nuclear physics. We demonstrate that these correlations should be taken into account in order to avoid fine-tuning in the calibration of three body forces. We relate this to the role of correlations in uncertainty quantification of non-renormalizable effective field theories of the nuclear regime. In addition, we show that correlations can be useful in assessing the importance of forces induced by renormalization group (RG) transformations. We give numerical evidence that such RG transformations can be represented effectively by adding a constant to the pure three nucleon contact low energy constant $c_E$.

## Key claim

Correlations between binding energies of light nuclei, known from pionless effective field theory (EFT), translate into correlations between the short-range three-body force low-energy constants \(c_D\) and \(c_E\) in chiral EFT; these correlations must be accounted for to avoid fine-tuning in the calibration of three-nucleon forces, and the difference between correlation lines for different nuclei provides an estimate of the EFT systematic uncertainty.

## Method

The authors performed a scan of the (\(c_D\), \(c_E\)) plane for several chiral EFT nucleon-nucleon potentials at N\(^2\)LO, solving for the binding energies of \(^3\)H, \(^3\)He, and \(^4\)He, and for the triton beta-decay axial matrix element. They plotted the lines in the (\(c_D\), \(c_E\)) plane that reproduce each observable, and compared the separation between these lines as a measure of higher-order EFT corrections. They also applied a \(v_{low\,k}\) renormalization group (RG) transformation to evolve a microscopic two-body potential to lower cutoffs and repeated the analysis to study the breaking of correlations.

## Key numbers

* The triton beta-decay axial matrix element used as a constraint is \(|\langle ^3\text{H} \| E_1^A \| ^3\text{He} \rangle| = 0.6848 (11)\).
* The chiral symmetry breaking scale is \(\Lambda_\chi \approx 4\pi f_\pi \approx 1\ \text{GeV}\), with \(f_\pi = 92.4\ \text{MeV}\).
* The actual breakdown scale of chiral EFT is significantly lower than 1 GeV, i.e., \(\Lambda \sim 500\ \text{MeV}\).
* For the \(v_{low\,k}\) study, cutoffs were set at \(\Lambda = 1.6, 1.8, 2.0, 2.2, 2.4, 2.6\ \text{fm}^{-1}\).
* 85% of the \(^3\)H–\(^3\)He binding energy difference is due to Coulomb repulsion, with the remainder from isospin-breaking effects of higher order in chiral EFT.

## Context

This work extends the known Tjon correlation (Tjon, 1976) and the \(A=3\) isomultiplet correlation from pionless EFT (Phillips, 1968; Platter, 2005) into the chiral EFT framework, providing a new representation that directly links these correlations to the three-body low-energy constants \(c_D\) and \(c_E\). It builds on the sequential calibration method for three-nucleon forces using triton binding energy and beta-decay rate (Gazit et al., 2009) and on the use of \(v_{low\,k}\) RG transformations (Bogner et al., 2003). The results enable a method to disentangle systematic EFT truncation errors from experimental statistical errors in calibrating few-body forces, and suggest that RG-induced many-body forces can be effectively approximated by a constant shift in \(c_E\).
