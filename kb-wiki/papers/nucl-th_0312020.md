# Unmasking the nuclear matter equation of state

> Accurately calibrated (or ``best fit'') relativistic mean-field models are used to compute the distribution of isoscalar monopole strength in 90Zr and 208Pb, and the isovector dipole strength in 208Pb using a continuum random-phase-approximation approach. It is shown that the distribution of isoscalar monopole strength in 208Pb--but not in 90Zr--is sensitive to the density dependence of the symmetry energy. This sensitivity hinders the extraction of the compression modulus of symmetric nuclear matter from the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) in 208Pb. Thus, one relies on 90Zr, a nucleus with both a small neutron-proton asymmetry and a well developed ISGMR peak, to constrain the compression modulus of symmetric nuclear matter to the range K=(248 +/- 6) MeV. In turn, the sensitivity of the ISGMR in 208Pb to the density dependence of the symmetry energy is used to constrain its neutron skin to the range Rn-Rp<=0.22 fm. The impact of this result on the enhanced cooling of neutron stars is briefly addressed.

## Key claim

The distribution of isoscalar monopole strength in 208Pb, but not in 90Zr, is sensitive to the density dependence of the symmetry energy, which hinders extraction of the compression modulus of symmetric nuclear matter from the ISGMR in 208Pb. Using 90Zr, the compression modulus is constrained to K = (248 ± 6) MeV, while the sensitivity in 208Pb constrains its neutron skin to Rn − Rp ≤ 0.22 fm.

## Method

Accurately calibrated relativistic mean-field models (based on the NL3 parametrization) were used to compute the distribution of isoscalar monopole strength in 90Zr and 208Pb, and isovector dipole strength in 208Pb, using a continuum random-phase-approximation (RPA) approach. The models included an isoscalar-isovector coupling (Λv) to modify the density dependence of the symmetry energy while preserving ground-state properties. The centroid energies of the ISGMR were extracted from the strength distributions, and a scaling relation (E_ISGMR ∝ √K) was used to adjust the compression modulus to match experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Compression modulus of symmetric nuclear matter constrained to K = (248 ± 6) MeV, derived from the ISGMR in 90Zr.
- Neutron skin of 208Pb constrained to Rn − Rp ≤ 0.22 fm, from the sensitivity of the ISGMR and IVGDR in 208Pb to the symmetry energy.
- For 90Zr, theoretical centroid energies ranged from 18.10 MeV (NL3000) to 18.25 MeV (NL3040), compared to the experimental centroid of 17.89 ± 0.20 MeV.
- For 208Pb, theoretical centroid energies ranged from 14.35 MeV (NL3000) to 15.03 MeV (NL3040), compared to the experimental centroid of 14.24 ± 0.11 MeV.
- The symmetry energy coefficient was fixed at J̃ = 25.67 MeV at a Fermi momentum of k̃_F^0 = 1.15 fm⁻¹.
- All models shared symmetric nuclear matter properties: Fermi momentum k_F^0 = 1.30 fm⁻¹, binding energy per nucleon ε₀ = −16.24 MeV, and compression modulus K = 271 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier proposal (Piekarewicz 2002) that the ISGMR in heavy nuclei is sensitive to the density dependence of the symmetry energy, and addresses the discrepancy between nonrelativistic predictions (K ≈ 220–235 MeV) and relativistic predictions (K ≈ 250–270 MeV) for the compression modulus. The results suggest that the compression modulus and neutron skin values are closer to nonrelativistic predictions, and that the enhanced cooling of the neutron star in 3C58 via the URCA process is unlikely, raising the possibility of an exotic star such as a quark star. The Parity Radius Experiment (PREX) at Jefferson Laboratory is identified as a future model-independent measurement of the neutron radius of 208Pb.
