# Excited Scalar and Pseudoscalar Mesons in the Extended Linear Sigma Model

> We present an in-depth study of masses and decays of excited scalar and pseudoscalar $\bar{q}q$ states in the Extended Linear Sigma Model (eLSM). The model also contains ground-state scalar, pseudoscalar, vector and axial-vector mesons. The main objective is to study the consequences of the hypothesis that the $f_0(1790)$ resonance, observed a decade ago by the BES Collaboration and recently by LHCb, represents an excited scalar quarkonium. In addition we also analyse the possibility that the new $a_0(1950)$ resonance, observed recently by BABAR, may also be an excited scalar state. Both hypotheses receive justification in our approach although there appears to be some tension between the simultaneous interpretation of $f_0(1790)$/$a_0(1950)$ and pseudoscalar mesons $η(1295)$, $π(1300)$, $η(1440)$ and $K(1460)$ as excited $\bar{q}q$ states.

## Key claim
The paper tests the hypothesis that the resonances \(f_0(1790)\) and \(a_0(1950)\) can be interpreted as excited scalar quarkonium states within the Extended Linear Sigma Model (eLSM), finding justification for both hypotheses but noting tension when simultaneously interpreting the pseudoscalar mesons \(\eta(1295)\), \(\pi(1300)\), \(\eta(1440)\), and \(K(1460)\) as excited \(\bar{q}q\) states.

## Method
The authors construct a Lagrangian for excited scalar and pseudoscalar mesons within the eLSM, which already contains ground-state scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector mesons plus a scalar glueball. They compute masses and decay widths at tree level for the excited states, comparing theoretical predictions with experimental data for \(f_0(1790)\), \(a_0(1950)\), and the pseudoscalar mesons \(\eta(1295)\), \(\pi(1300)\), \(\eta(1440)\), and \(K(1460)\). The model assumes that excited scalar and pseudoscalar fields do not condense, and no mixing between excited and ground states is considered.

## Key numbers
- The best-fit mass for the ground-state scalar \(\sigma_N\) (assigned to \(f_0(1370)\)) is 1363 MeV, and for \(\sigma_S\) (assigned to \(f_0(1710)\)) the preferred assignment is to \(f_0(1710)\); a subsequent analysis found \(f_0(1500)\) more preferable for \(\sigma_S\).
- The best-fit mass for the axial-vector kaon \(K_1\) is 1282 MeV, with a larger overlap with \(K_1(1400)\).
- The mixing angle between \(\eta_N\) and \(\eta_S\) is \(\theta_{\eta} = -44.6^\circ\).
- The pion decay constant \(f_\pi\) is fit to \(96.3 \pm 0.7\) MeV (experimental: \(92.2 \pm 4.6\) MeV), and the kaon decay constant \(f_K\) is fit to \(106.9 \pm 0.6\) MeV (experimental: \(110.4 \pm 5.5\) MeV).
- The model predicts more than 35 decays for various scalar and pseudoscalar resonances.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of ground-state \(\bar{q}q\) mesons and the scalar glueball in the eLSM (Parganlija et al. 2013, Stani et al. 2016) by incorporating first radial excitations of scalar and pseudoscalar states. It builds on earlier NJL model extensions (Volkov et al. 1999-2002) that identified \(f_0(1370)\), \(f_0(1710)\), and \(a_0(1450)\) as radial excitations, but finds that in the eLSM those states are better described as ground-state quarkonia. The results are directly relevant for experimental hadron searches at PANDA@FAIR and for interpreting data from BES II, LHCb, and BABAR on \(f_0(1790)\) and \(a_0(1950)\).
