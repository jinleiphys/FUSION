# Relativistic approach for the determination of nuclear and neutron star properties in consideration of PREX-II results

> The bulk properties of nuclear matter and neutron stars with the newly generated relativistic interaction DBHP are investigated which provides an opportunity to modify the coupling parameters keeping in view the finite nuclei, nuclear matter, PREX-II data for neutron skin thickness in $^{208}$Pb and astrophysical constraints. The relativistic interaction has been generated by including all possible self and mixed interactions between $σ$, $ω$, and $ρ$-meson up to the quartic order satisfying the naturalness behavior of parameters. A covariance analysis is performed to assess the statistical uncertainties on the model parameters and observables of interest along with correlations amongst them. We obtained a value of neutron skin thickness for $^{208}$Pb nucleus $Δr_{np}$ = 0.24 $\pm$ 0.02 fm. The maximum gravitational mass of neutron star and radius corresponding to the canonical mass ($R_{1.4}$) come out to be 2.03 $\pm$ 0.04 M$\odot$ and 13.39 $\pm$ 0.41 km respectively. The dimensionless tidal deformability, $Λ$ for a neutron star is also analyzed.

## Key claim

The newly generated relativistic interaction DBHP, which includes all possible self and mixed interactions between sigma, omega, and rho mesons up to quartic order, can simultaneously accommodate the PREX-II neutron skin thickness measurement for 208Pb (0.283 ± 0.071 fm) and astrophysical constraints on neutron star properties, but only by producing a neutron skin thickness of 0.24 ± 0.02 fm that lies within the PREX-II uncertainty range rather than matching its central value.

## Method

The authors constructed a relativistic mean-field (RMF) Lagrangian density including all self-couplings and mixed-interaction terms for sigma, omega, and rho mesons up to quartic order, ensuring naturalness of the dimensionless coupling parameters. They optimized the 14 model parameters using simulated annealing to fit experimental binding energies and charge radii of closed- and open-shell nuclei, the PREX-II neutron skin thickness of 208Pb, and the maximum neutron star mass constraint. A covariance analysis was performed to compute statistical uncertainties on parameters and observables, and the resulting equation of state was used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for neutron star structure.

## Key numbers

- Neutron skin thickness for 208Pb: Δr_np = 0.24 ± 0.02 fm
- Maximum gravitational mass of neutron star: M_max = 2.03 ± 0.04 solar masses
- Radius at canonical mass (1.4 solar masses): R_1.4 = 13.39 ± 0.41 km
- Saturation density: ρ_0 = 0.148 ± 0.003 fm^-3
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: E/A = -16.11 ± 0.05 MeV
- Nuclear matter incompressibility: K = 229.5 ± 5.6 MeV
- Symmetry energy coefficient at saturation: J = 34.7 ± 1.5 MeV
- Slope of symmetry energy: L = 83.9 ± 19.2 MeV
- Dimensionless tidal deformability at 1.4 solar masses: Λ_1.4 = 682 ± 125
- Neutron skin thickness for 48Ca: Δr_np = 0.21 ± 0.02 fm

## Context

This work extends the line of relativistic mean-field models such as NL3 (Lalazissis 1997), FSUGarnet (Chen 2015), IOPB-1 (Bharat 2018), and Big Apple (Fattoyev 2020) by including all possible mixed meson interaction terms up to quartic order to satisfy naturalness conditions from effective field theory (Furnstahl 1997). The DBHP parameterization is specifically calibrated to the PREX-II result for 208Pb neutron skin thickness (Adhikari 2021) and the GW170817 tidal deformability constraint (Abbott 2018), but it overestimates the CREX result for 48Ca neutron skin thickness (Adhikari 2022), a tension also noted in other recent relativistic and non-relativistic studies (Paar 2022, Reinhard 2022, Piekarewicz 2022). The model enables further investigation of the correlation between neutron skin thickness, symmetry energy slope, and neutron star observables.
