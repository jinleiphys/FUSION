# Do not forget the electrons: Extending moderately-sized nuclear networks for multidimensional hydrodynamic codes

> We present here an extended nuclear network, with 90 species, designed for being coupled with hydrodynamic simulations, which includes neutrons, protons, electrons, positrons, and the corresponding neutrino and anti-neutrino emission. This network is also coupled with temperature, making it extremely robust and, together with its size, unique of its kind. The inclusion of electron captures on free protons makes the network very appropriate for multidimensional studies of Type Ia supernova explosions, especially when the exploding object is a massive white dwarf. The results obtained with the proposed medium-sized network compare fairly well, to a few percent, with those computed with the extended network WinNet (> 2000 isotopes) in scenarios reproducing the gross physical conditions of current Type Ia supernova explosion models. In those cases where the carbon and oxygen fuel ignites at high density, the high-temperature plateau typical of the nuclear statistical equilibrium regime is well defined and stable, allowing large integration time steps. We show that the inclusion of electron captures on free protons substantially improves the estimation of the electron fraction of the mixture. Therefore, the pressure is better determined than in networks where electron captures are excluded, which will ultimately lead to more reliable hydrodynamic models. Explosive combustion of helium at low density, occurring near the surface layer of a white dwarf, is also better described with the proposed network, which gives nuclear energy generation rates much closer to WinNet than typical reduced alpha networks.

## Key claim

The authors present a 90-species nuclear network (net90) that includes electron and positron captures on free protons and neutrons, claiming it is the first time such weak interaction processes have been incorporated into a moderately-sized network for multidimensional hydrodynamic simulations of Type Ia supernovae. The network achieves energy generation rates within a few percent of the much larger WinNet (over 2000 isotopes), but only in scenarios reproducing the gross physical conditions of current Type Ia supernova explosion models.

## Method

The network couples the implicit integration of nuclear abundance equations with the temperature equation via a Newton-Raphson scheme, using a parameter theta=0.7 for stability. Electron and positron capture rates on free protons and neutrons are computed numerically following the formalism of Fuller (1980), with neutrino and antineutrino emissivities stored in interpolating tables. The network is tested in three scenarios: carbon-oxygen burning at high density (rho=2e9 g/cm3) relevant to Chandrasekhar-mass white dwarfs, helium burning at low density (rho=5e5 g/cm3) relevant to sub-Chandrasekhar mass models, and silicon photodisintegration relevant to core-collapse supernovae.

## Key numbers

- Network contains 90 species with approximately 150 reactions, compared to WinNet which contains 2,281 nuclei up to Z=50 with approximately 30,000 reaction rates
- In the CO test at initial density rho_9=2 (2e9 g/cm3) and temperature T_9=1 (1 GK), electron captures on free protons account for up to approximately 90% of all captures when compared to WinNet
- The inclusion of electron captures increases computing time by less than 0.5%
- The hydrodynamic timescale is defined as t_hd = 446 / sqrt(rho_0), where rho_0 is the initial density
- The alpha network net14 is expected to have accuracy in energy generation rate of approximately 20% (citing Timmes 2000)
- The CO test uses eta_1=50 and eta_2=2 for the artificial expansion parameters
- The He test uses initial density rho=5e5 g/cm3 with eta_1=2 and eta_2=1

## Context

This work extends the approach of Cabezon (2004) and Garcia-Senz (2003) by adding weak interaction processes to moderately-sized networks, which were previously absent from multidimensional hydrodynamic simulations of Type Ia supernovae. The network is verified against the much larger WinNet network of Reichert (2023), and improves upon the 14-species alpha networks used by Timmes (2000) and the 86-species network of Cabezon (2004) by providing better estimates of electron fraction and pressure in degenerate white dwarf matter. This enables more reliable multidimensional hydrodynamic models of Type Ia supernova explosions, particularly for Chandrasekhar-mass and sub-Chandrasekhar mass scenarios.
