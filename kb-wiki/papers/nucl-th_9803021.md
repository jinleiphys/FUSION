# Vector Meson Photoproduction with an Effective Lagrangian in the Quark Model II: $ω$ Photoproduction

> An investigation of $γp\to ωp$ is presented in a constituent quark model approach. The sparse data in the large $t$ region where the resonances dominate is well described within the model, while the diffractive behavior in the small $t$ region requires an additional t-channel exchange. Taking into account the t-channel $π^0$ exchange, we find a good overall agreement with the available data with only 3 free parameters. Our study shows that the differential cross section is not sensitive to s-channel resonances, however, the polarization observables are demonstrated to be very sensitive. Thus, measuring polarization observables is the crucial part of the vector meson photoproduction program in the search for "missing" resonances.

## Key claim

The differential cross section for γp → ωp is not sensitive to s-channel resonances, but polarization observables (target, recoil, and meson polarization) are very sensitive to their presence; therefore, measuring polarization observables is crucial for searching for "missing" resonances, though the authors note their calculations are limited to the SU(6)⊗O(3) symmetry limit which should be broken in more realistic quark model wavefunctions.

## Method

The authors used a constituent quark model with an effective Lagrangian to compute s- and u-channel resonance contributions to ω photoproduction, evaluating helicity amplitudes for all s-channel resonances below 2 GeV in the SU(6)⊗O(3) symmetry limit and treating resonances above 2 GeV as degenerate. They added a t-channel π⁰ exchange with coupling constants fixed from separate experiments (g²πNN/4π = 14, g²ωπγ = 3.315) to describe the diffractive behavior at small t, and introduced a Lorentz boost factor to partially remedy the inadequacy of nonrelativistic wavefunctions at higher energies.

## Key numbers

- Quark mass fixed at mq = 330 MeV and harmonic oscillator strength α = 410 MeV
- Coupling constants from separate experiments: g²πNN/4π = 14, g²ωπγ = 3.315
- Best-fit parameters: a = -2.2, b′ = 3.0, απ = 300 MeV
- Differential cross section calculated at average photon energies Eγ = 1.225, 1.45, 1.675, and 1.915 GeV
- The resonance F₁₅(2000) has a larger decay width into ωN than P₁₃(1900), with threshold energy around 1.675 GeV in the lab frame
- A small bump around 1.7 GeV in the total cross section comes from the F₁₅(2000) resonance

## Context

This work extends the quark model approach previously applied to pseudoscalar meson photoproduction (Zhao, Li, Bennhold, 1998, Ref. [vector]) to vector meson photoproduction, specifically ω photoproduction which shares features with η photoproduction due to both being isospin zero states. The study builds on the π⁰ exchange model of Friman and Soyeur (Ref. [FrimanSoyeur]) and addresses the puzzle that nonrelativistic quark models (NRQM, Capstick) predict a richer resonance spectrum than observed in πN scattering; the results suggest that polarization measurements at facilities like ELSA and TJNAF could help identify "missing" resonances such as F₁₅(2000), though the authors note that Pomeron exchange may play a more important role at higher energies than covered by their model.
