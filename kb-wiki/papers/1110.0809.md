# The Wake of a Quark Moving through Hot QCD Plasmas vs. N = 4 SYM Plasmas

> We present the energy density and flux distribution of a heavy quark moving through high temperature QCD plasmas and compare them with those in the strongly coupled N = 4 SYM plasma. The Boltzmann equation is reformulated as a Fokker-Planck equation at the leading log approximation and is solved numerically with nontrivial boundary conditions in momentum space. We use kinetic theory and perform a Fourier transform to calculate the energy and momentum density in position space. The angular distributions exhibit the transition to the ideal hydrodynamics and are analyzed with the first and second order hydrodynamic source. The AdS/CFT correspondence allows the same calculation at strong coupling. Compared to the kinetic theory results, the energy-momentum tensor is better described by hydrodynamics even after accounting for the differences in the shear viscosities. We argue that the difference between the Boltzmann equation and the AdS/CFT correspondence comes from the second order hydrodynamic coefficient $τ_π$ which is generically large compared to the shear length in a theory based on the Boltzmann equation.

## Key claim

The energy-momentum tensor of a heavy quark moving through hot QCD plasmas at weak coupling is significantly less well described by hydrodynamics at sub-asymptotic distances compared to the strongly coupled N=4 SYM plasma, even after accounting for differences in shear viscosities; the authors argue this difference arises because the second order hydrodynamic coefficient τ_π is generically large compared to the shear length in a theory based on the Boltzmann equation.

## Method

The linearized Boltzmann equation was reformulated as a Fokker-Planck equation at the leading log approximation and solved numerically with absorptive boundary conditions in momentum space. The stress-energy tensor was calculated in Fourier space using kinetic theory and then Fourier-transformed back to position space to obtain the energy density and flux distributions. The resulting distributions were compared with those from the AdS/CFT correspondence at strong coupling and analyzed using first and second order hydrodynamic source terms.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity from the Boltzmann equation: η/(sT) = 0.4613 T/μ_A
- Second order hydrodynamic coefficient from the Boltzmann equation: τ_π = 6.32 η/(sT)
- Shear viscosity from AdS/CFT: η/(sT) = 1/(4π T)
- Second order hydrodynamic coefficient from AdS/CFT: τ_π = (2 - ln 2)/(2π T)
- τ_π in units of the shear length [4η/3]/(c_s^2 sT): τ_π ≈ 1.58 (Boltzmann) versus τ_π ≈ 0.65 (AdS/CFT), making τ_π approximately 2.4 times larger in the Boltzmann equation than in gauge/gravity duality
- The correction coefficient φ_2(0,0) for the Boltzmann equation: 0.48 [R] where [R] = [(4η/3)/(c_s^2 sT)]

## Context

This work extends the study of Mach cone structures in hot QCD plasmas, originally motivated by two-particle correlation functions in heavy ion collisions, and directly compares weak-coupling kinetic theory results with strong-coupling results from the AdS/CFT correspondence previously calculated by Gubser (2007) and Chesler (2007). The analysis uses transport coefficients including shear viscosity η and the second order hydrodynamic coefficient τ_π, previously determined by Hong (2010) for the Boltzmann equation and by Kovtun (2004) and Baier (2007) for AdS/CFT. The findings enable a quantitative understanding of how the approach to the hydrodynamic regime differs between weakly coupled QCD plasmas and strongly coupled plasmas, with implications for interpreting heavy ion collision data.
