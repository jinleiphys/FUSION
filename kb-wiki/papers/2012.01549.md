# Intrinsic Correlations Among Characteristics of Neutron-rich Matter Imposed by the Unbound Nature of Pure Neutron Matter

> The unbound nature of pure neutron matter (PNM) requires intrinsic correlations between the symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) EOS parameters (incompressibility $K_0$, skewness $J_0$ and kurtosis $I_0$) and those (slope $L$, curvature $K_{\rm{sym}}$ and skewness $J_{\rm{sym}}$) characterizing the symmetry energy independent of any nuclear many-body theory. We investigate these intrinsic correlations and their applications in better constraining the poorly known high-density behavior of nuclear symmetry energy. Several novel correlations connecting the characteristics of SNM EOS with those of nuclear symmetry energy are found. In particular, at the lowest-order of approximations, the bulk parts of the slope $L$, curvature $K_{\rm{sym}}$ and skewness $J_{\rm{sym}}$ of the symmetry energy are found to be $L\approx K_0/3, K_{\rm{sym}}\approx LJ_0/2K_0$ and $J_{\rm{sym}}\approx I_0L/3K_0$, respectively. High-order corrections to these simple relations can be written in terms of the small ratios of high-order EOS parameters. The resulting intrinsic correlations among some of the EOS parameters reproduce very nicely their relations predicted by various microscopic nuclear many-body theories and phenomenological models constrained by available data of terrestrial experiments and astrophysical observations in the literature. The unbound nature of PNM is fundamental and the required intrinsic correlations among the EOS parameters characterizing both the SNM EOS and symmetry energy are universal. These intrinsic correlations provide a novel and model-independent tool not only for consistency checks but also for investigating the poorly known high-density properties of neutron-rich matter by using those with smaller uncertainties.

## Key claim

The unbound nature of pure neutron matter (PNM) imposes intrinsic correlations between symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) equation-of-state (EOS) parameters and symmetry energy parameters, independent of any nuclear many-body theory. At lowest order, the slope \(L\), curvature \(K_{\rm{sym}}\), and skewness \(J_{\rm{sym}}\) of the symmetry energy are approximately \(L \approx K_0/3\), \(K_{\rm{sym}} \approx L J_0 / 2K_0\), and \(J_{\rm{sym}} \approx I_0 L / 3K_0\), with high-order corrections expressible in terms of small ratios of higher-order EOS parameters.

## Method

The authors derived an expression for the saturation density \(\rho_{\rm{sat}}(\delta)\) of asymmetric nucleonic matter (ANM) as a power series in \(\delta^2\) up to order \(\delta^6\), using expansions of the EOS around the saturation density \(\rho_0\) of SNM. Setting \(\rho_{\rm{sat}}(\delta) = 0\) for PNM (i.e., \(\delta = 1\)) as required by its unbound nature yields a sum rule linking the characteristic EOS parameters of SNM (incompressibility \(K_0\), skewness \(J_0\), kurtosis \(I_0\)) with those of the symmetry energy (slope \(L\), curvature \(K_{\rm{sym}}\), skewness \(J_{\rm{sym}}\)). This sum rule was analyzed at different orders in \(\delta^2\) to extract approximate relations among the parameters, and Monte Carlo sampling was used to quantify correlations.

## Key numbers

* The incompressibility of SNM is \(K_0 \approx 240 \pm 20\) MeV (Garg 2018, You 1999, Shl 2006, Che 2012, Col 2014).
* The skewness of SNM is \(J_0 \approx -300\) MeV (Cai 2017).
* The kurtosis of SNM is \(I_0 \approx -146 \pm 1728\) MeV (Xie and Li).
* The slope of the symmetry energy is \(L \approx 60 \pm 30\) MeV (Li 2013).
* The curvature of the symmetry energy is \(K_{\rm{sym}} \approx -80\) MeV (Xie 2020).
* The skewness of the symmetry energy is \(J_{\rm{sym}} \approx 300\) MeV (Mon 2017).
* Using the approximate relation \(L \approx K_0/3\) with \(K_0 = 240\) MeV gives \(L \approx 80\) MeV; including high-order corrections yields \(L \approx 53.3\) MeV.
* From the relation \(K_{\rm{sym}} \approx K_0 (1 - K_0/3L + J_0 L / 2K_0^2 + L_{\rm{sym,4}}/L)\), using \(K_0 = 240\) MeV, \(J_0 = -300\) MeV, \(L = 60\) MeV, and \(L_{\rm{sym,4}} = 6\) MeV, one obtains \(K_{\rm{sym}} \approx -93\) MeV.
* Monte Carlo sampling at \(\delta^4\) order gave the linear correlation \(K_{\rm{sym}} \approx a_1 L + b_1\) with \(\overline{a}_1 \approx 5.25 \pm 0.40\) and \(\overline{b}_1 \approx -441 \pm 28\) MeV.
* At \(\delta^6\) order, the correlation \(K_{\rm{sym}} \approx a_1' L + b_1'\) gave \(\overline{a}_1' \approx 3.61 \pm 0.43\) and \(\overline{b}_1' \approx -268 \pm 21\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Margueron et al. (2019), who used the condition that the PNM EOS vanishes at zero density to derive a linear correlation between \(K_{\rm{sym}}\) and \(3S - L\). The present analysis imposes the more stringent condition of vanishing pressure in PNM, leading to a sum rule that couples SNM and symmetry energy parameters at multiple orders. The resulting intrinsic correlations provide a model-independent tool for consistency checks and for constraining poorly known high-density properties of neutron-rich matter using better-determined low-density parameters. The correlations reproduce relations predicted by microscopic many-body theories and phenomenological models, and can be applied to estimate the slope \(L\), curvature \(K_{\rm{sym}}\), and skewness \(J_{\rm{sym}}\) of the symmetry energy.
