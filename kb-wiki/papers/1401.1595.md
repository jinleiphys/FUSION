# Higher bottom and bottom-strange mesons

> Motivated by the recent observation of the orbital excitation $B(5970)$ by CDF collaboration, we have performed a systematical study of the mass spectrum and strong decay patterns of the higher $B$ and $B_s$ mesons. Hopefully the present investigation may provide valuable clues to further experimental exploration of these intriguing excited heavy mesons.

## Key claim

The authors systematically calculate the mass spectrum and strong decay patterns of higher bottom and bottom-strange mesons using a relativistic quark model and the quark pair creation (QPC) model, motivated by the CDF observation of the orbital excitation \(B(5970)\). The study provides predictions for radially and orbitally excited states, but the authors note that discrepancies between different theoretical models for masses of \(1F\), \(2P\), and \(2D\) states can reach up to \(100\sim 200\) MeV, so they consider the mass dependence of strong decay widths.

## Method

The mass spectrum was calculated using a relativistic quark model with a Hamiltonian including confinement, spin-orbit, and hyperfine terms, diagonalized in simple harmonic oscillator bases. The two-body strong decays were studied using the OZI-allowed quark pair creation (QPC) model, where the spatial wave functions were represented by simple harmonic oscillator wave functions with \(\beta\) values determined by reproducing the root-mean-square radius from the relativistic quark model. The helicity amplitudes were converted to partial wave amplitudes via the Jacob-Wick formula to compute decay widths.

## Key numbers

- Quark masses used: \(m_u = m_d = 220\) MeV, \(m_b = 4977\) MeV, \(m_s = 419\) MeV.
- Predicted mass of \(1^1S_0\) bottom meson: 5309 MeV; \(1^3S_1\): 5369 MeV.
- Predicted mass of \(2^1S_0\) bottom meson: 5904 MeV; \(2^3S_1\): 5934 MeV.
- Predicted mass of \(3^1S_0\) bottom meson: 6334 MeV; \(3^3S_1\): 6355 MeV.
- Predicted mass of \(1^3P_2\) bottom meson: 5796 MeV; \(1^3P_0\): 5756 MeV; \(1^3P_1\): 5782 MeV; \(1^1P_1\): 5779 MeV.
- Predicted mass of \(1^3D_3\) bottom meson: 6105 MeV; \(1^3D_1\): 6110 MeV; \(1^3D_2\): 6113 MeV; \(1^1D_2\): 6108 MeV.
- Predicted mass of \(1^3F_4\) bottom meson: 6364 MeV; \(1^3F_2\): 6387 MeV; \(1^3F_3\): 6380 MeV; \(1^1F_3\): 6375 MeV.
- For bottom-strange mesons: \(1^1S_0\) mass: 5390 MeV; \(1^3S_1\): 5447 MeV; \(2^1S_0\): 5985 MeV; \(2^3S_1\): 6013 MeV; \(1^3P_2\): 5875 MeV; \(1^3P_0\): 5830 MeV; \(1^3P_1\): 5859 MeV; \(1^1P_1\): 5858 MeV.
- Partial decay width of \(B(1^3P_0) \to B\pi\): 225 MeV.
- Total decay width of \(B(2^3S_1)\): 47 MeV, consistent with the experimental width of \(B(5970)\) within errors.
- Total decay width of \(B(1^3D_1)\): 294 MeV.
- Ratio \(\frac{B(B_2^*(5747)\rightarrow B^{*+}\pi^-)}{B(B_2^*(5747)\rightarrow B^{+}\pi^-)} = 0.95\), consistent with experimental value \(1.10 \pm 0.42 \pm 0.31\).
- For \(B_1(5721)^0\), the calculated width at ideal mixing angle \(\theta_{1P} = -54.7^\circ\) is 1.8 MeV; the experimental width is \(20 \pm 2 \pm 5\) MeV (neutral) and \(42 \pm 11 \pm 13\) MeV (charged).

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies of bottom and bottom-strange mesons using the relativistic quark model by Godfrey (1985) and the quasipotential approach by Ebert (2009), as well as the Dirac Hamiltonian potential model by Di Pierro (2001). It also builds on experimental observations by the CDF and D0 collaborations, including \(B_1(5721)\), \(B_2^*(5747)\), and the newly reported \(B(5970)\). The results provide predictions for the masses and decay widths of higher excited states (e.g., \(2P\), \(3S\), \(1D\), \(2D\), \(1F\)) that can guide future experimental searches at facilities such as LHCb and future bottom-factories.
