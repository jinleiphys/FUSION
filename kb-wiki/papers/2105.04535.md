# Insights on the peak in the speed of sound of ultradense matter

> In this work we investigate the minimal physical requirements needed for generating a speed of sound that surpasses its asymptotic conformal limit. It is shown that a peak in the speed of sound of homogeneous matter naturally emerges in the transition from a phase with broken chiral symmetry to one with a gapped Fermi surface. We argue that this could be relevant for understanding the peak in the speed of sound displayed by some of the current models for cold ultradense matter. A minimal model implementation of this mechanism is presented, based on the spontaneous breakdown of an approximate particle-antiparticle symmetry, and its thermodynamic properties are determined.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a peak in the speed of sound above the conformal limit (c_s^2 = 1/3) necessarily requires a large, nearly constant nonperturbative energy scale contributing to the baryon-number susceptibility, and that such a scale naturally emerges from the transition between a phase with broken chiral symmetry and one with a gapped Fermi surface due to diquark or dibaryon condensation. The authors hedge that this mechanism is presented as a minimal model and that the mean-field treatment becomes unreliable very close to the transition where fluctuations dominate.

## Method

The authors construct a Ginzburg-Landau theory with two order parameters: a neutral chiral condensate sigma and a baryon-number-charged condensate Phi (diquark/dibaryon). They then build a low-energy effective model based on an approximate SU(2)_GMM symmetry (Gell-Mann-Majorana symmetry) that rotates between particle and antiparticle states, with the constraint sigma^2 + |Phi|^2 = F^2. The thermodynamic properties are computed in mean-field approximation at zero temperature by evaluating the fermionic determinant and minimizing the renormalized effective potential to find the condensate angle phi as a function of chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- The conformal limit for the speed of sound squared is c_s^2 = 1/3 for three spatial dimensions.
- The condensate contribution to the susceptibility is given by chi_2^cond = 12 F^2 (m_Phi / (2 mu))^4 + 4 F^2 (plus fermionic contribution).
- The critical chemical potential for the onset of the superfluid phase satisfies 2|mu| >= m_Phi in the noninteracting limit g -> 0.
- The fermion mass is m_f = g F, where g is the coupling constant and F is the condensate scale.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of the conformal speed-of-sound bound from Bedaque (2014) and provides a mechanism alternative to quarkyonic matter (McLerran 2007, 2018) for generating a peak in the speed of sound. The mechanism is inspired by QCD with two colors and two flavors (Hands 2001, 2006) and by results from functional renormalization group calculations (Leonhardt 2019) and Bayesian analyses (Ayriyan 2021) that connect diquark gaps to speed-of-sound maxima. The work enables future studies of neutron star equations of state that incorporate a chiral-superfluid transition, and predicts a unique dip in the speed of sound before the peak that could be tested against observational data from NICER and gravitational-wave events.
