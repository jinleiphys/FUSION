# Non-relativistic expansion of Dirac equation with spherical scalar and vector potentials by similarity renormalization group

> By following the conventional similarity renormalization group (SRG) expansion of the Dirac equation developed in [J.-Y. Guo, Phys. Rev. C \textbf{85}, 021302 (2012)], we work out the analytic expression of the ${1}/{M^4}$ order and verify the convergence of this method. As a step further, the reconstituted SRG method is proposed by using the re-summation technique. The speed of convergence of the reconstituted SRG becomes much faster than the conventional one, and the single-particle densities with the reconstituted SRG are also almost identical to the exact values.

## Key claim

The conventional similarity renormalization group (SRG) expansion of the Dirac equation converges slowly because the series in powers of \(S/M\) (where \(S\) is the scalar potential and \(M\) the nucleon mass) has a ratio of approximately 1/3; the authors propose a reconstituted SRG method using re-summation in terms of the Dirac effective mass \(M^\ast(r) = M + S(r)\) to achieve much faster convergence, though the truncated Hamiltonian must include specific higher-order terms to remain Hermitian.

## Method

The authors performed the non-relativistic expansion of the Dirac Hamiltonian with spherical scalar and vector potentials using the similarity renormalization group (SRG) method, deriving analytic expressions up to the \(1/M^4\) order for the first time. They then proposed a reconstituted SRG method that replaces the bare nucleon mass \(M\) with the Dirac effective mass \(M^\ast(r) = M + S(r)\) in the expansion, effectively summing infinite series of terms belonging to the same operator families. The non-relativistic Schrodinger-like equations for both methods were solved in coordinate space by the shooting method with Woods-Saxon potentials typical for \(^{208}\text{Pb}\), and higher-order terms were treated by perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

* The scalar potential depth is \(\Sigma_0 = -66.0\) MeV and the vector potential depth is \(\Delta_0 = 650.0\) MeV, with radius \(r_0 = 7.0\) fm and diffuseness \(a_0 = 0.6\) fm, typical for \(^{208}\text{Pb}\).
* The nucleon mass is taken as \(M = 939.0\) MeV; the ratio \(S/M\) is approximately \(1/3\).
* For the conventional SRG at \(1/M^2\) order, the single-particle energies for eight states are over bound by around 3 MeV (e.g., \(3p_{3/2}\) exact \(-7.6933\) MeV vs. \(-10.7831\) MeV, difference \(-3.2632\) MeV).
* At \(1/M^3\) order, the largest correction comes from the operator \(\frac{S^2}{2M^3}p^2\) (dynamical term), contributing about \(+3\) MeV (e.g., \(+3.1763\) MeV for \(2f_{7/2}\)).
* After including \(1/M^3\) corrections, differences between conventional SRG and exact energies are between 0.5 and 1 MeV (e.g., \(3p_{3/2}\) exact \(-7.6933\) MeV vs. \(-8.1468\) MeV, difference \(-0.4536\) MeV).
* After including \(1/M^4\) corrections, differences are less than 0.2 MeV for all states (e.g., \(3p_{3/2}\) exact \(-7.6933\) MeV vs. \(-7.5200\) MeV, difference \(+0.1733\) MeV; \(1h_{9/2}\) exact \(-14.1161\) MeV vs. \(-14.1119\) MeV, difference \(+0.0042\) MeV).

## Context

This work extends the conventional SRG expansion of the Dirac equation developed by Guo (2012) by deriving the analytic \(1/M^4\) order terms and verifying convergence. The reconstituted SRG method using re-summation with the Dirac effective mass \(M^\ast\) is a novel proposal that dramatically accelerates convergence compared to the conventional expansion. The results enable more accurate non-relativistic reductions of relativistic nuclear density functional theory, and support earlier findings by Guo (2012) on the pseudospin symmetry breaking mechanism, where spin-orbit and relativistic kinetic corrections reduce pseudospin-orbit splittings while dynamical terms increase them.
