# Measurements of correlations of anisotropic flow harmonics in Pb--Pb Collisions with ALICE

> We report the first measurements of the correlation strength between various anisotropic flow harmonics, using ALICE data. This correlation strength is characterized with multi-particle cumulants of mixed harmonics, which by construction depend only on the fluctuations of magnitudes of the anisotropic flow vectors. A detailed comparison to theoretical model calculations, including HIJING, Monte Carlo Glauber and hydrodynamics is also presented. These studies further constrain initial conditions, the properties and the evolution of the system to be used in theoretical simulations of heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first measurements of the correlation strength between anisotropic flow harmonics using multi-particle cumulants of mixed harmonics, which by construction depend only on fluctuations of the magnitudes of the anisotropic flow vectors and are insensitive to non-flow effects and inter-correlations of symmetry planes.

## Method

The analysis used approximately 16 million minimum-bias Pb-Pb events at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV recorded by ALICE, with charged particles reconstructed using the Inner Tracking System and Time Projection Chamber in the pseudorapidity range |eta| < 0.8. The Symmetric 2-harmonic 4-particle Cumulants SC(4,2) and SC(3,2) were measured using the generic framework for multi-particle correlations, and compared to calculations from the HIJING model, Monte Carlo Glauber simulations with Wounded Nucleon and Binary Collisions weighting, and hydrodynamic calculations with various eta/s(T) parameterizations.

## Key numbers

- SC(4,2) is positive and SC(3,2) is negative for all centralities in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV
- The correlation strength of SC increases non-linearly up to 60% centrality
- HIJING calculations show both SC(4,2) and SC(3,2) consistent with zero, confirming the observable is nearly insensitive to non-flow effects
- MC-Glauber calculations using Binary Collisions parameterization are favored by the data in 0-10% central collisions

## Context

This work extends the study of anisotropic flow harmonics v_n (for n=1 to 6) previously measured at the LHC by ALICE (2011), and builds on the proposal by Bilandzic (2013) to use Symmetric 2-harmonic 4-particle Cumulants to study relationships between different flow harmonics. The measurements provide new constraints on initial conditions and the temperature dependence of eta/s in hydrodynamic calculations, going beyond what standard v_n measurements alone can achieve, as demonstrated by comparisons to hydrodynamic calculations from Niemi (2015).
