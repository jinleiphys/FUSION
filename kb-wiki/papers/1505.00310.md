# Progress in vacuum susceptibilities and their applications to the chiral phase transition of QCD

> The QCD vacuum condensates and various vacuum susceptibilities are all important parameters which characterize the nonperturbative properties of the QCD vacuum. In the QCD sum rules external field formula, various QCD vacuum susceptibilities play important roles in determining the properties of hadrons. In this paper, we review the recent progress in studies of vacuum susceptibilities together with their applications to the chiral phase transition of QCD. The results of the tensor, the vector, the axial-vector, the scalar, and the pseudo-scalar vacuum susceptibilities are shown in detail in the framework of Dyson-Schwinger equations.

## Key claim

This review paper argues that various QCD vacuum susceptibilities (tensor, vector, axial-vector, scalar, and pseudo-scalar) can be systematically calculated within the Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) framework, and that these quantities serve as effective probes of the chiral phase transition. The authors note that only the scalar and tensor vacuum susceptibilities remain model-dependent, and that the rainbow-ladder approximation is insufficient for the scalar case, requiring calculations beyond that truncation.

## Method

The authors derive a model-independent expression for the vector vacuum susceptibility using the QCD sum rules external field formula, based on the linear response of the full quark propagator to a constant external vector field. They then extend this formalism to the tensor, axial-vector, scalar, and pseudo-scalar vacuum susceptibilities, computing them within the DSEs framework in Euclidean space and Landau gauge. For the scalar vacuum susceptibility, they go beyond the rainbow-ladder approximation by exploiting the fact that only the scalar vertex with zero momentum exchange is needed, combined with the Ward-Takahashi identity.

## Key numbers

* The tensor vacuum susceptibility obtained with the Ball-Chiu (BC) vertex is reduced by about 10% compared to its rainbow-ladder approximation value (no absolute numerical value given for the susceptibility itself).
* The scalar vacuum susceptibility is calculated beyond the rainbow-ladder approximation (no specific numerical result quoted in the truncated text).
* The vector vacuum susceptibility is expressed via the difference between the full and perturbative vector vacuum polarizations at zero momentum, as given in Eq. (sus-ge) of the paper.

## Context

This work reviews and extends a line of research initiated by the model-independent expression for the vector vacuum susceptibility proposed in Ref. (PRC.72.035202), which was based on the QCD sum rules external field formula. It builds upon prior DSEs calculations of the axial-vector and tensor vacuum susceptibilities under rainbow-ladder approximation (PRC.73.035206, PLB.639.248-257) and the scalar vacuum susceptibility beyond rainbow-ladder (PRC.79.035209). The paper enables future studies of the chiral phase transition at finite temperature and chemical potential by providing a unified framework for all five vacuum susceptibilities, and it notes that lattice QCD calculations for three-point Green functions remain challenging, making effective approaches like DSEs necessary.
