# Diffractive hadron production in DIS off heavy nuclei and gluon saturation

> We calculate the cross section for diffractive hadron production in deep inelastic scattering off heavy nuclei in the framework of gluon saturation/color glass condensate. We analyze the kinematic region of the future Electron-Ion Collider. We argue that coherent and incoherent diffractive channels are very sensitive to the structure of the nuclear matter at low $x$. This expresses itself in a characteristic dependence of the cross sections on rapidity and transverse momentum of the produced hadron and on the nuclear weight. We also discuss dependence on the scattering angle and argue that both coherent and incoherent cross sections may be within experimental reach at EIC.

## Key claim

The authors argue that coherent and incoherent diffractive hadron production in deep inelastic scattering off heavy nuclei are very sensitive to the structure of nuclear matter at low Bjorken x, and that both cross sections may be within experimental reach at the future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC), provided the scattering angle can be resolved down to approximately 0.13 degrees.

## Method

The cross sections for diffractive gluon production are calculated using the dipole model, where the virtual photon wave function is given by perturbative QED and the dipole-nucleus interaction is expressed as a product of the dipole density (satisfying the BFKL equation) and the dipole-nucleus forward elastic scattering amplitude. The authors employ the b-CGC (b-dependent Color Glass Condensate) model for the dipole scattering amplitude, with parameters fitted to low-x DIS data, and treat nuclear and proton profiles as step functions with saturation scales scaling as Q_s^2 ∝ A^{1/3}. The cross sections are convoluted with a leading-order pion fragmentation function to obtain hadron-level predictions.

## Key numbers

- The center-of-mass energy of the γ*A collision is taken as W = 100 GeV per proton, corresponding to a total rapidity interval Y = 9.2.
- The saturation scale is parameterized as Q_s^2 = A^{1/3} x_0^λ e^{λ y} GeV^2, with x_0 = 3 × 10^{-4} and λ = 0.28.
- The anomalous dimension parameters are γ_s = 0.628 and κ = 9.9, with N_0 = 0.7.
- The scattering angle at which coherent and incoherent contributions become comparable is θ' ≈ 0.13° for W = 100 GeV.
- The coherent cross section decreases as 1/|t|^3 at |t| ≫ 1/R_A^2, while the incoherent cross section decreases exponentially as e^{-|t| R_p^2/4}.
- The nuclear modification factor is studied for photon virtualities in the range 1 < Q^2 < 25 GeV^2.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of diffractive gluon production in pA collisions (Li 2008, Tuchin 2008) to the nuclear DIS case at EIC energies. It builds on the dipole model framework introduced by Mueller (1989) and the b-CGC model of Kowalski (2006). The paper contrasts with earlier HERA studies of diffractive processes on protons (Golec-Biernat 1998, Marquet 2004) and semi-inclusive DIS on nuclei (Kowalski 2007). The results enable experimental separation of coherent and incoherent diffractive channels at EIC, which would provide a direct probe of gluon saturation and the structure of nuclear matter at low x.
