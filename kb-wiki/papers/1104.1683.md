# Stellar electron-capture rates calculated with the finite-temperature relativistic random-phase approximation

> We introduce a self-consistent microscopic theoretical framework for modelling the process of electron capture on nuclei in stellar environment, based on relativistic energy density functionals. The finite-temperature relativistic mean-field model is used to calculate the single-nucleon basis and the occupation factors in a target nucleus, and $J^π = 0^{\pm}$, $1^{\pm}$, $2^{\pm}$ charge-exchange transitions are described by the self-consistent finite-temperature relativistic random-phase approximation. Cross sections and rates are calculated for electron capture on 54,56Fe and 76,78Ge in stellar environment, and results compared with predictions of similar and complementary model calculations.

# Stellar electron-capture rates calculated with the finite-temperature relativistic random-phase approximation

## Key claim

The finite-temperature relativistic random-phase approximation (FTRRPA) provides a fully self-consistent microscopic framework for calculating stellar electron-capture rates on medium-mass nuclei, but the model is limited to one-particle–one-hole correlations and therefore cannot reproduce the detailed fragmentation of Gamow-Teller strength distributions, focusing instead on centroid energies.

## Method

The single-nucleon basis and thermal occupation factors for the target nucleus are obtained from the finite-temperature relativistic mean-field model using the DD-ME2 density-dependent effective interaction. Charge-exchange transitions with multipolarities Jπ = 0±, 1±, and 2± are computed using the self-consistent finite-temperature relativistic random-phase approximation, with the same energy density functional used consistently in both the mean-field and RPA equations. Electron-capture cross sections are derived from Fermi's golden rule using the weak-interaction Hamiltonian in current-current form, and stellar rates are obtained by integrating the cross section over the Fermi-Dirac electron distribution at given temperature and density.

## Key numbers

- For 54Fe at T=0 MeV, the RQRPA GT+ centroid energy is approximately 1 MeV lower than the large-scale shell model (LSSM) calculation; at T=1 MeV the transition energy decreases by about 1.1 MeV relative to the RQRPA result.
- For 56Fe at T=0 MeV, the RQRPA GT+ centroid energy is approximately 1 MeV lower than LSSM; at T=1 MeV the transition energy decreases by about 1.6 MeV, and at T=2 MeV it decreases further by about 0.5 MeV.
- For 76Ge at T=0 MeV with pairing, two major GT+ peaks appear at E=15.8 MeV and E=16.9 MeV; at T=1 MeV these shift to E=2.8 MeV and E=4.3 MeV, a decrease of about 13 MeV.
- For 76Ge at T=2 MeV, the excitation energies are only slightly lowered by 0.1 MeV compared to T=1 MeV, but the transition strengths are considerably enhanced.
- The mass difference between neutron and proton Δnp = 1.294 MeV is used in the neutrino energy conservation relation.
- The axial-vector coupling constant is quenched to gA = -1.0 from its free-nucleon value of gA = -1.26 for all multipole excitations.

## Context

This work extends the finite-temperature relativistic RPA framework previously developed for low-energy monopole and dipole response (Niu 2009) into the charge-exchange channel for electron-capture studies. It provides a fully self-consistent alternative to the hybrid SMMC+RPA approach (Langanke 2001) and the thermal QRPA based on Woods-Saxon potentials (Dzhioev 2010), with the advantage that calculations are completely determined by a given relativistic energy density functional and can be extended over arbitrary mass regions. The framework enables systematic calculations of electron-capture rates for the many neutron-rich nuclei involved in presupernova collapse networks, where forbidden transitions beyond the Gamow-Teller channel become important for heavier nuclei.
