# Inelastic Neutrino-Nucleus Interactions within the Spectral Function Formalism

> We report the results of a study of neutrino-carbon interactions at beam energies ranging between few hundreds MeV and few tens of GeV, carried out within the framework of the impulse approximation using a realistic spectral function. The contributions of quasi elastic scattering, resonance production and deep inelastic scattering---consistently obtained, for first time, from a model based on a realistic description of the nuclear ground state---are compared and analyzed.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first comprehensive study of neutrino-carbon cross sections, including quasi-elastic scattering, resonance production, and deep inelastic scattering, consistently obtained within the impulse approximation using a realistic spectral function, but the authors note that the full calculation sizably exceeds the measured NOMAD total cross section at energies above about 10 GeV, likely due to double counting between resonance and DIS contributions.

## Method

The authors employed the impulse approximation (IA) with a realistic nuclear spectral function for carbon-12, factorizing the nuclear cross section into a convolution of the spectral function (describing the probability of removing a nucleon from the ground state) and the elementary neutrino-nucleon cross section. The CCQE channel used vector form factors from electron scattering data and a dipole axial form factor with axial mass M_A = 1.03 GeV; resonance production included the P33(1232) state and three isospin-1/2 resonances (D13(1520), P11(1440), S11(1535)) with structure functions constrained by electroproduction data; DIS structure functions were obtained from parton distributions of GRV98 via relations connecting electromagnetic and weak structure functions.

## Key numbers

- Axial-vector coupling constant: g_A = -1.2761 (+0.0014 / -0.0017), from neutron beta-decay
- Axial mass used: M_A = 1.03 GeV, from elastic neutrino-nucleon scattering and other data
- At E_nu ≈ 1.3 GeV, the contributions from CCQE, resonance production, and DIS are about equal
- At E_nu = 1 GeV and 1.5 GeV, the full dσ/dQ² exhibits a pronounced maximum at Q² ≲ 0.2 GeV²
- Minimum Q² for parton distributions: Q²_min = 0.8 GeV² (from GRV98)
- Muon emission angle for differential cross sections: θ_μ = 30 degrees

## Context

This work extends the spectral function formalism, previously validated against electron-nucleus scattering data (Benhar 2005, Benhar 2006), to consistently describe all inelastic neutrino-nucleus channels for the first time. The approach contrasts with the Relativistic Fermi Gas Model (RFGM) used by MiniBooNE and others, and the results are compared to NOMAD total cross section data (NOMAD 2008) and NOMAD CCQE data (NOMAD 2003). The framework enables future inclusion of meson-exchange currents (MEC) and final state interactions (FSI), which are needed to explain MiniBooNE flux-integrated double-differential cross sections and to determine nuclear transparency.
