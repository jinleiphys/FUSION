# Aspects of meson-baryon scattering in three- and two-flavor chiral perturbation theory

> We analyze meson-baryon scattering lengths in the framework of covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory at leading one-loop order. We compute the complete set of matching relations between the dimension-two low-energy constants in the two- and three-flavor formulations of the theory. We derive new two-flavor low-energy theorems for pion-hyperon and pion-cascade scattering that can be tested in lattice simulations.

## Key claim

The paper computes meson-baryon scattering lengths in covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory at leading one-loop order, finding that chiral corrections in the covariant (full) approach come out mostly larger than in the heavy-baryon scheme, and that the reordering prescription does not improve convergence for scattering lengths in contrast to its success for baryon masses and magnetic moments.

## Method

The authors calculated all elastic meson-baryon scattering lengths in SU(3) covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory to third chiral order using infrared regularization, fitting the dimension-two low-energy constants to baryon masses, the pion-nucleon sigma term, and experimental scattering data for pion-nucleon and kaon-nucleon scattering. They then performed the matching of two- and three-flavor pion-nucleon scattering amplitudes to derive constraints between the dimension-two low-energy constants, and derived the complete effective Lagrangians for pion-hyperon interactions to second order.

## Key numbers

- Pion-nucleon scattering length \(a_{\pi N}^{+} = -0.002 \pm 0.007\) fm (experimental input used for fitting)
- Kaon-nucleon scattering lengths: \(a_{K N}^{(0)} = +0.02\) fm and \(a_{K N}^{(1)} = -0.33\) fm (experimental input)
- Baryon masses used: \(m_N = 0.938\) GeV, \(m_\Lambda = 1.115\) GeV, \(m_\Sigma = 1.192\) GeV, \(m_\Xi = 1.314\) GeV
- Pion-nucleon sigma term: \(\sigma_{\pi N} = 45 \pm 8\) MeV
- Fitted symmetry-breaking LECs in IR: \(b_0^{\rm IR} = -0.45_{-0.01}^{+0.01}\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(b_D^{\rm IR} = +0.05_{-0.00}^{+0.00}\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(b_F^{\rm IR} = -0.45_{-0.01}^{+0.01}\) GeV\(^{-1}\)
- Fitted dynamical LEC combination \(B_1^{\rm IR} = +0.43_{-0.12}^{+0.08}\) GeV\(^{-1}\) in the full (IR) approach
- Full result for \(a_{\pi N}^{(3/2)} = -0.04_{-0.07}^{+0.07}\) fm summing all orders
- Full result for \(a_{\bar{K} N}^{(0)} = +1.11_{-0.59}^{+0.47} + 0.22 i\) fm (wrong sign compared to experimental value \((-1.70+0.68i)\) fm)

## Context

This work extends the heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory analysis of meson-baryon scattering lengths by Liu (2006) to a covariant formulation using infrared regularization as developed by Becher (1999). The matching relations between SU(2) and SU(3) low-energy constants enable constraints for unitarized coupled-channel analyses of photo-kaon threshold production data from ELSA, JLab, and MAMI, as discussed by Borasoy (2007). The new two-flavor low-energy theorems for pion-hyperon scattering provide formulas useful for chiral extrapolation of lattice QCD simulations, extending earlier work on two-flavor ChPT for hyperons by Beane (2003), Tiburzi (2008), and Jiang (2009).
