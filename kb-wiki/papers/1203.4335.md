# Comparing non-perturbative models of the breakup of neutron-halo nuclei

> Breakup reactions of loosely-bound nuclei are often used to extract structure and/or astrophysical information. Here we compare three non-perturbative reaction theories often used when analyzing breakup experiments, namely the continuum discretized coupled channel model, the time-dependent approach relying on a semiclassical approximation, and the dynamical eikonal approximation. Our test case consists of the breakup of 15C on Pb at 68 MeV/nucleon and 20 MeV/nucleon.

# Comparing non-perturbative models of the breakup of neutron-halo nuclei

## Key claim
The three non-perturbative models (CDCC, TD, and DEA) agree well for angle-integrated observables at 68 MeV/nucleon, but at 20 MeV/nucleon the DEA overestimates the energy distribution by about 10% and shifts the angular distribution to more forward angles by about 10% in theta, primarily due to the absence of Coulomb deflection in the eikonal approximation.

## Method
The authors compare three non-perturbative reaction theories for the breakup of a one-neutron halo nucleus approximated as a two-body cluster (core plus valence neutron): the continuum discretized coupled channel method (CDCC), the time-dependent semiclassical approach (TD), and the dynamical eikonal approximation (DEA). All three models start from the same three-body Hamiltonian with identical optical potentials and projectile structure inputs, and are applied to the breakup of 15C on a 208Pb target at two beam energies (68 and 20 MeV/nucleon). Breakup cross sections as functions of the core-neutron relative energy and the scattering angle of the center of mass are computed and compared.

## Key numbers
- At 68 MeV/nucleon, the three models predict energy distributions differing by only 1-3% at the peak.
- At 20 MeV/nucleon, the DEA energy distribution is about 10% too high at the peak compared to CDCC and TD.
- At 68 MeV/nucleon, the DEA angular distribution shows a shift of about 3% in theta relative to CDCC.
- At 20 MeV/nucleon, the DEA angular distribution is shifted by about 10% in theta relative to CDCC.
- At 40 MeV/nucleon, the energy distributions differ by a mere 5% at the maximum and the shift in angular distributions is about 5% in theta.
- The 14C-n potential reproduces the 15C ground state at -1.218 MeV (1s1/2) and the excited 5/2+ state at -0.478 MeV (0d5/2).
- Convergence tests ensure an accuracy of at least 4% in the energy and angular distributions for all three models.

## Context
This work provides the first quantitative comparison of non-perturbative breakup models at intermediate energies, extending the analysis of Alder and Winther (1975) on the semiclassical approximation. The test case of 15C on Pb at 68 MeV/nucleon had been previously measured at RIKEN (2011), and the present results validate the reaction theory and the single-particle nature of 15C. The study shows that the TD model works well for angle-integrated observables over a wider energy range than originally suggested, while the DEA provides a computationally efficient alternative to CDCC at higher energies but requires correction for Coulomb deflection at lower energies. The results cannot be readily extended to charged (proton-halo) systems due to different breakup mechanisms involving more significant E2 transitions and stronger higher-order dynamical effects, as discussed in previous work by Esbensen et al. (2005) and Ogata et al. (2006).
