# Role of Multi-Parton Interactions on $J/ψ$ production in $p+p$ collisions at LHC Energies

> The production mechanism of quarkonia states in hadronic collisions is still to be understood by the scientific community. In high-multiplicity $p+p$ collisions, Underlying Event (UE) observables are of major interest. The Multi-Parton Interactions (MPI) is a UE observable, where several interactions occur at the partonic level in a single $p+p$ event. This leads to dependence of particle production on event multiplicity. If the MPI occurs in a harder scale, there will be a correlation between the yield of quarkonia and total charged particle multiplicity. The ALICE experiment at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) in $p+p$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 7 and 13 TeV has observed an approximate linear increase of relative $J/ψ$ yield ($\frac{dN_{J/ψ}/dy}{<dN_{J/ψ}/dy>}$) with relative charged particle multiplicity density ($\frac{dN_{ch}/dy}{<dN_{ch}/dy>}$). In our present work we have performed a comprehensive study of the production of charmonia as a function of charged particle multiplicity in $p+p$ collisions at LHC energies using pQCD-inspired multiparton interaction model, PYTHIA8 tune 4C, with and without Color Reconnection (CR) scheme. A detail multiplicity and energy dependent study is performed to understand the effects of MPI on $J/ψ$ production. The ratio of $ψ(2S)$ to $J/ψ$ is also studied as a function of charged particle multiplicity at LHC energies.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the observed approximate linear increase of relative $J/\psi$ yield with relative charged particle multiplicity in $p+p$ collisions at LHC energies can be qualitatively reproduced by the PYTHIA8 event generator with Multi-Parton Interactions (MPI), but the Color Reconnection (CR) scheme has only a small effect on $J/\psi$ production, becoming noticeable only in the highest multiplicity bins at the highest energies.

## Method

The authors used the PYTHIA8.2 event generator with tune 4C to simulate inelastic, non-diffractive $p+p$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 0.9, 2.76, 5.02, 7, and 13 TeV, generating 100 million events per energy. They reconstructed $J/\psi$ mesons via the dimuon decay channel at forward rapidity ($2.5 < y < 4.0$) and measured charged particle multiplicity at midrapidity ($|y| < 1.0$), comparing simulations both with and without the MPI-based Color Reconnection scheme.

## Key numbers

- Relative $J/\psi$ yield increases approximately linearly with relative charged particle multiplicity at all five center-of-mass energies studied (0.9, 2.76, 5.02, 7, and 13 TeV).
- The difference in relative $J/\psi$ yield between with-CR and without-CR simulations at $\sqrt{s}$ = 13 TeV ranges from 0.020 $\pm$ 0.011 in the 0-5 multiplicity bin to 1.017 $\pm$ 0.161 in the 100-150 multiplicity bin.
- At $\sqrt{s}$ = 7 TeV, the difference between with-CR and without-CR ranges from 0.025 $\pm$ 0.013 (0-5 bin) to 1.001 $\pm$ 0.246 (100-150 bin).
- The fitting parameter $n$ (rate of increase of relative $J/\psi$ yield with center-of-mass energy) becomes positive for multiplicity bins with $N_{ch} >$ 20, indicating a threshold for substantial MPI effects.
- PYTHIA8 reproduces ALICE $J/\psi$ $p_T$ spectra at $\sqrt{s}$ = 5.02 TeV with a maximum deviation of about 1% for most bins, and rapidity spectra with less than 1% deviation.

## Context

This work extends studies by the ALICE experiment (Abelev 2012) which observed an approximate linear increase of relative $J/\psi$ yield with multiplicity at $\sqrt{s}$ = 7 TeV, and by the ALICE collaboration (Adam 2015) for open charm and non-prompt $J/\psi$ at 7 and 13 TeV. The authors note that PYTHIA6 could not describe the observed trend, showing an opposite behavior, while PYTHIA8 with MPI can. The study enables future comparisons with experimental data at 13 TeV and provides a threshold multiplicity ($N_{ch} >$ 20) above which MPI effects on charmonia become substantial, informing both event generator tuning and percolation theory models (Ferreiro).
