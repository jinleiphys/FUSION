# Neutron star deformability with hyperonization in density-dependent relativistic mean-field models

> Neutron star tidal deformability extracted from gravitational wave data provides a novel probe to the interior neutron star structures and the associated nuclear equation of state (EOS). Instead of the popular composition of nucleons and leptons in neutron stars, we include hyperons and examine the role of hyperons in the tidal deformability and its impact on the symmetry energy in a relativistic mean-field approach with the density-dependent parametrizations. The hyperons are found to have significant impact on the deformability, correlated sensitively with the onset density and fraction of hyperons in neutron star matter. Moderately lower onset density of hyperons can yield considerable modification to the tidal deformability and shift its inference on the nuclear EOS. The future measurements of the tidal deformability at multi-fiducial star masses are anticipated to lift the degeneracy between the contributions from the hyperon component and symmetry energy.

## Key claim

Hyperons can significantly modify the tidal deformability of neutron stars, and this modification is sensitively correlated with the onset density and fraction of hyperons; a moderately lower onset density of hyperons can shift the inference of the nuclear equation of state (EOS) from gravitational wave data, potentially smearing out constraints on the symmetry energy.

## Method

The authors used density-dependent relativistic mean-field (RMF) models (parameter sets SLC and SLCd) that include baryons (nucleons, Λ, Σ, Ξ) and leptons (e, μ), with density-dependent meson-baryon coupling constants governed by Brown-Rho scaling functions. A small parameter α was introduced to tune the density dependence of vector meson couplings to hyperons, thereby adjusting the hyperon onset density and fraction. The tidal deformability was computed by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations together with the first-order differential equation for the metric perturbation, using a piecewise EOS that matches the core to inner and outer crusts.

## Key numbers

- For the 1.4 M⊙ star with the SLC model and no hyperons, the tidal deformability Λ_g is 394.6; with hyperons (f_σΛ = 0.8, α = -0.05, 3.06% hyperon fraction) it reduces to 310.6 (a 21.2% reduction), and with α = 0.05 (10.7% hyperon fraction) it reduces to 227.4 (a 42.4% reduction).
- For the SLCd model at 1.4 M⊙, Λ_g without hyperons is 229.5; with hyperons (f_σΛ = 0.8, α = -0.05, 1.90% hyperon fraction) it is 215.0, and with α = 0.05 (7.72% hyperon fraction) it is 163.5.
- The onset densities of Λ hyperons range from 2.57 ρ₀ to 3.24 ρ₀ across the parameter choices, where ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ is the saturation density.
- The slope parameter L of the symmetry energy at saturation density is 92.3 MeV for SLC and 61.5 MeV for SLCd.
- The core-crust transition density is 0.0912 fm⁻³ for SLC and 0.0928 fm⁻³ for SLCd.

## Context

This work extends previous density-dependent RMF models (Jiang 2007, 2012) that can reproduce 2 M⊙ neutron stars with hyperons, and examines their compatibility with the tidal deformability constraint from the GW170817 event (Abbott 2018). The results show that hyperon inclusion reduces the difference in tidal deformability between models with different symmetry energy stiffness (SLC vs. SLCd), which complicates the extraction of symmetry energy constraints from gravitational wave data. The authors propose that future measurements of tidal deformability at multiple fiducial star masses are needed to disentangle the effects of hyperons and symmetry energy, building on earlier work by Fattoyev (2013) and Zhang (2018).
