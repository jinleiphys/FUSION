# Exploring $X(5568)$ as a meson molecule

> The parameters, i.e. the mass and current coupling of the exotic $X(5568)$ state observed by the D0 Collaboration as well as the decay width of the process $X \to B_s^{0}π^{+}$ are explored using $B\bar{K}$ molecule assumption on its structure. Employed computational methods include QCD two-point and light-cone sum rules, latter being considered in the soft-meson approximation. The obtained results are compared with the data of the D0 Collaboration as well as with the predictions of the diquark-antidiquark model. This comparison strengthens a diquark-antidiquark picture for the $X(5568)$ state rather than a meson molecule structure.

## Key claim

The paper tests whether the exotic $X(5568)$ state observed by the D0 Collaboration can be described as a $B\overline{K}$ meson molecule using QCD sum rules, and finds that the molecule model yields a mass and decay width that overshoot the experimental data, strengthening the case for a diquark-antidiquark interpretation instead.

## Method

The authors used QCD two-point sum rules with a molecule-type interpolating current $J^{X_b}(x)=[\overline{d}^a(x)\gamma_5 s^a(x)][\overline{b}^b(x)\gamma_5 u^b(x)]$ to compute the mass and current coupling of $X(5568)$. They then employed light-cone QCD sum rules in the soft-meson approximation to extract the strong coupling $g_{X_b B_s \pi}$ and the width of the decay $X_b \to B_s^0 \pi^+$. The spectral density was calculated including quark, gluon, and mixed condensates up to dimension eight, with continuum subtraction and Borel transformation applied.

## Key numbers

* Mass of $X_b$ as a molecule: $m_{X_b} = (5757 \pm 145)\ \mathrm{MeV}$, compared to the D0 experimental value $5567.8 \pm 2.9\ (\mathrm{stat})\ _{-1.9}^{+0.9}\ (\mathrm{syst})\ \mathrm{MeV}$.
* Decay constant of $X_b$ as a molecule: $f_{X_b} = (0.17 \pm 0.04) \cdot 10^{-2}\ \mathrm{GeV}^4$.
* Strong coupling: $g_{X_b B_s \pi} = (0.50 \pm 0.14)\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}$.
* Width of $X_b \to B_s \pi$ as a molecule: $\Gamma = (33.6 \pm 12.1)\ \mathrm{MeV}$, compared to the D0 experimental value $21.9 \pm 6.4\ (\mathrm{stat})\ _{-2.5}^{+5.0}\ (\mathrm{syst})\ \mathrm{MeV}$.
* Borel parameter window for mass and coupling calculations: $4\ \mathrm{GeV}^2 \leq M^2 \leq 6\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$.
* Continuum threshold range: $34.5\ \mathrm{GeV}^2 \leq s_0 \leq 37\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of $X(5568)$ within the diquark-antidiquark picture (Agaev 2016) and applies the same QCD sum rule methodology to the molecule hypothesis. The results contradict the molecule interpretation and instead support the diquark-antidiquark model predictions, which agreed well with the D0 Collaboration data. The paper highlights the unresolved experimental tension with the LHCb Collaboration's failure to confirm the state, and calls for further theoretical and experimental work to clarify the nature of $X(5568)$.
