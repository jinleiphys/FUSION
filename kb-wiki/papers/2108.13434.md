# Ratios of jet and hadron spectra at LHC energies: measuring high-$p_T$ suppression without a $pp$ reference

> We analyze the reliability of several techniques for computing jet and hadron spectra at different collision energies. This is relevant for discovering energy loss in the upcoming oxygen-oxygen (OO) run at the LHC, for which a reference pp run is currently not planned. For hadrons and jets we compute the ratio of spectra between different pp collision energies in perturbative QCD, which can be used to construct a reference spectrum. Alternatively, it can be interpolated from measured spectra at nearby energies. We estimate the precision of both strategies for the spectra ratio relevant to the oxygen run, and conclude that the central values agree to 4% accuracy for hadrons and 2% accuracy for jets. Finally we propose taking the ratio of OO and pp spectra at different collision energies, which cleanly separates the experimental measurement and theoretical computation.

## Key claim

The central values of the ratio of hadron and jet spectra between 6.37 and 5.02 TeV computed using perturbative QCD and data-driven interpolation agree to 4% accuracy for hadrons and 2% accuracy for jets, but the interpolation method is sensitive to data selection and relies on using three collision energies with significant cancellation of systematic uncertainties.

## Method

The authors computed the ratio of inclusive hadron and jet spectra in pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 6.37 and 5.02 TeV using collinear perturbative QCD at leading order (LO), next-to-leading order (NLO), and next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) with the NNLOjet framework, using the anti-kT algorithm with jet radius R=0.4. They also performed global fits to existing ALICE hadron data at 2.76, 5.02, and 7 TeV and ATLAS jet data at the same energies using nine different functional forms, and used Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) fits to propagate experimental uncertainties into uncertainties on the interpolated spectra ratio. Finally, they proposed a mixed-energy nuclear modification factor comparing OO spectra at 6.37 TeV to pp spectra at 5.02 TeV, computed with nuclear PDF sets EPPS16, nNNPDF2.0, and nCTEQ15(WZSIH).

## Key numbers

- The NLO scale envelope for hadrons is less than 2% in the momentum range pT between 7 and 50 GeV
- The NLO scale envelope for jets is below 1% for pT between 40 and 200 GeV
- The central values of pQCD and interpolated ratios deviate from each other by up to 4% for hadrons and 2% for jets
- For hadrons, statistical and luminosity uncertainties give few-percent uncertainties for pT less than 30 GeV, growing to about 5% at 50 GeV
- For jets, the interpolated ratio has about 2% uncertainties for all pT shown
- The nCTEQ15 nPDF uncertainty bands are below 3% for hadrons and 2% for jets
- Model studies indicate energy loss effects can lead to suppression of charged hadron spectra up to about 20% for hadrons with momentum about 20 GeV

## Context

This work extends the methodology used for constructing references for the 5.02 TeV proton-lead run at the LHC, where interpolation was used by ALICE (2012), ATLAS (2014, 2016), and CMS (2015, 2016). The paper addresses the specific challenge that a measured pp reference at the same energy is not planned for the upcoming oxygen-oxygen run at the LHC in Run 3, as discussed by Citron (2018) and Brewer (2021). The mixed-energy nuclear modification factor approach enables future searches for high-pT partonic rescatterings in small collision systems, where previous searches in proton-lead collisions by CMS (2016), ALICE (2018), and ATLAS (2016) have been unsuccessful despite evidence for collective effects.
