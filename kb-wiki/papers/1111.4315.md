# From confinement to new states of dense QCD matter

> Transitions between centre sectors are related to confinement in pure Yang-Mills theories. We study the impact of these transitions in QCD-like theories for which centre symmetry is explicitly broken by the presence of matter. For low temperatures, we provide numerical evidence that centre transitions do occur with matter merely providing a bias towards the trivial centre sector until centre symmetry is spontaneously broken at high temperatures. The phenomenological consequences of these transitions for dense hadron matter are illustrated in an SU(3) effective quark theory: centre dressed quarks undergo condensation due to Bose-type statistics forming a hitherto unknown state of dense but cold quark matter.

## Key claim

Centre-sector transitions, which are responsible for confinement in pure Yang-Mills theories, persist in QCD-like theories even when centre symmetry is explicitly broken by dynamical matter; these transitions enable a new condensation mechanism for quarks (Fermi-Einstein condensation) that, for SU(3) with an odd number of colours, requires finite spatial volume (pressure) to manifest.

## Method

The authors study centre-sector transitions using lattice simulations of SU(2) pure gauge theory and SU(2) Higgs theory, measuring a "tunneling coefficient" p that quantifies the probability of different spatial regions belonging to different centre sectors. They then construct an exactly solvable SU(3) effective quark model where the gluonic background is approximated by centre-sector gauge fields (setting the trivial-sector field to zero), and compute the baryon number and centre-sector weights as functions of temperature, chemical potential, and spatial volume.

## Key numbers

- For pure SU(2) gauge theory at low temperatures, the tunneling coefficient p ≈ 1/2, indicating frequent centre-sector transitions; at high temperatures p → 0, indicating deconfinement.
- For SU(2) Higgs theory, the same qualitative behaviour is observed, with the transition occurring at a smaller value of the Wilson β coefficient, indicating a shift of the critical temperature to smaller values.
- In the SU(3) quark model, for small temperatures (T ≪ 0.2m), the three centre sectors contribute with roughly equal weights ω₁ ≈ ω₂ ≈ ω₃ ≈ 1/3; centre symmetry breaks spontaneously for T ≳ 0.2m, where ω₃ ≈ 1.
- For small spatial volumes (mL < 5), a region of the phase diagram at low temperatures and intermediate chemical potential shows |ω₃| ≫ 1, indicating the new condensed state; for large volumes (mL > 15), a hadronic phase is separated from a deconfinement phase.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of Fermi-Einstein condensation in QCD-like theories with an even number of colours (Langfeld 2009) and in the exactly solvable Schwinger model (Langfeld 2011). It builds on the centre-vortex picture of confinement reviewed by Greensite (2003) and on the understanding of centre symmetry and Polyakov loop order parameters from Svetitsky (1982, 1985). The results suggest that a new state of cold, dense quark matter may exist in the hadronic phase of QCD, and the authors propose extending the model by adding NJL-type four-fermion interactions or by coupling quarks to a centre-vortex model to make it more realistic.
