# Towards a data-driven analysis of hadronic light-by-light scattering

> The hadronic light-by-light contribution to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon was recently analyzed in the framework of dispersion theory, providing a systematic formalism where all input quantities are expressed in terms of on-shell form factors and scattering amplitudes that are in principle accessible in experiment. We briefly review the main ideas behind this framework and discuss the various experimental ingredients needed for the evaluation of one- and two-pion intermediate states. In particular, we identify processes that in the absence of data for doubly-virtual pion-photon interactions can help constrain parameters in the dispersive reconstruction of the relevant input quantities, the pion transition form factor and the helicity partial waves for $γ^*γ^*\toππ$.

## Key claim

The dispersive framework for hadronic light-by-light scattering can be implemented using only on-shell form factors and scattering amplitudes accessible in experiment, but in the absence of direct doubly-virtual pion-photon data, other processes such as \(e^+e^-\to 3\pi\), \(\gamma\pi\to\pi\pi\), and \(\omega,\phi\to 3\pi\) can constrain the required input quantities.

## Method

The authors decompose the hadronic light-by-light tensor into contributions from the \(\pi^0\) pole, a scalar-QED amplitude dressed by the pion vector form factor, and \(\pi\pi\) intermediate states, each defined by its analytic structure. For the \(\pi^0\) pole, the key input is the doubly-virtual pion transition form factor \(\mathcal{F}_{\pi^0\gamma^*\gamma^*}(q_1^2,q_2^2)\); for the \(\pi\pi\) contribution, the helicity partial waves for \(\gamma^*\gamma^*\to\pi\pi\) are reconstructed via dispersion relations using unitarity, with the left-hand cut built from pion-pole, \(\rho\)-meson, and \(\omega/\phi\) contributions. The formalism relates these quantities to measurable processes including \(e^+e^-\to 3\pi\), \(\gamma\pi\to\pi\pi\), \(\omega,\phi\to 3\pi\), and \(e^+e^-\to\pi^0\gamma\), allowing dispersive reconstruction even without direct doubly-virtual data.

## Key numbers

- The \(\pi^0\) pole contribution to \(a_\mu\) is given by an integral over \(q_1^2\), \(q_2^2\), and \(s=(q_1+q_2)^2\) involving the pion transition form factor \(\mathcal{F}_{\pi^0\gamma^*\gamma^*}\) and known kinematic functions \(T^{\pi^0}_1, T^{\pi^0}_2\).
- The \(\pi\pi\) contribution to \(a_\mu\) is expressed as an integral over \(q_1^2\), \(q_2^2\), and \(s\) with dispersive integrals \(I_i(s,q_1^2,q_2^2)\) that involve helicity partial-wave amplitudes \(h^J_{\lambda_1\lambda_2,\lambda_3\lambda_4}\) for \(\gamma^*\gamma^*\to\pi\pi\).
- Sum rules for transition form factor normalizations (e.g., \(\omega,\phi\to\pi^0\gamma\) and \(\pi^0\to\gamma\gamma\)) are found to be saturated by two-pion intermediate states at the 90% level.
- Data in the low-energy region below \(1-1.5\ \text{GeV}\) center-of-mass energy are expected to have the largest potential impact on the HLbL contribution.
- The singly-subtracted representation for \(\gamma\pi\to\pi\pi\) requires the chiral anomaly \(F_{3\pi}\) as a subtraction constant; a twice-subtracted representation uses the cross section \(\sigma(\gamma\pi\to\pi\pi)\) as input.

## Context

This work extends the dispersive framework for hadronic light-by-light scattering introduced by Colangelo, Hoferichter, Procura, and Stoffer (2014) to identify specific experimental measurements that can constrain the required input. It builds on prior dispersive analyses of \(\gamma\gamma\to\pi\pi\) by Garcia-Martin and Moussallam (2010) and of \(\gamma^*\gamma\to\pi\pi\) by Moussallam (2013), as well as on studies of the pion transition form factor via \(\omega,\phi\to 3\pi\) by Niecknig, Kubis, and Schneider (2012) and \(\gamma\pi\to\pi\pi\) by Hoferichter, Kubis, and Sakai (2012). The approach enables a data-driven evaluation of the HLbL contribution to \(a_\mu\) that is less model-dependent than previous vector-meson-dominance models, and it identifies the need for future measurements of doubly-virtual pion-photon interactions and improved data on \(e^+e^-\to e^+e^-\pi\pi\) and \(e^+e^-\to e^+e^-\pi^0\).
