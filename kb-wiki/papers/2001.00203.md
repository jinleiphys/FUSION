# Ernest Henley and the shape of baryons

> Calculations of pion-baryon couplings, baryon quadrupole and octupole moments, baryon spin and orbital angular momentum done in collaboration with Ernest Henley are reviewed. A common theme of this work is the shape of baryons. Also, a personal account of my work with Ernest Henley during the period 1999-2013 is given.

## Key claim

The paper reviews calculations of baryon properties performed in collaboration with Ernest Henley, arguing that the nucleon and Delta baryon have nonzero intrinsic quadrupole deformations (prolate for the proton, oblate for the Delta) even though the spectroscopic quadrupole moment of the spin-1/2 nucleon vanishes due to angular momentum coupling selection rules.

## Method

The authors applied Morpurgo's general parameterization method, which expresses baryon observables in terms of one-, two-, and three-quark spin-flavor operators constrained by QCD symmetries, to calculate pion-baryon couplings, quadrupole moments, and octupole moments. For the intrinsic quadrupole moment, they used both a quark model (with spin-renormalized wave functions that undo the Clebsch-Gordan averaging) and a pion cloud model (where the nucleon is a superposition of a spherical quark core and a p-wave pion cloud). The parameters were fixed using experimental data on the N-to-Delta transition quadrupole moment and nucleon charge radii.

## Key numbers

- The intrinsic quadrupole moment of the proton is \(Q_0^p = -r_n^2\), where \(r_n^2\) is the neutron charge radius (negative), making \(Q_0^p\) positive (prolate shape)
- The intrinsic quadrupole moment of the \(\Delta^+\) is \(Q_0^{\Delta^+} = r_n^2\), equal in magnitude but opposite in sign to the proton's intrinsic quadrupole moment
- The \(\pi^0 pp\) coupling constant is normalized to \(f = 1\) (input), with the \(\pi\Delta\Delta\) coupling constant found to be \(f_{\pi^0 \Delta^+\Delta^+} = 0.23\) in the SU(3) symmetric case (\(r=1\))
- The ratio of transition couplings for \(\pi^+\) emission gives \(f_{\Delta^{++} p} / f_{\Sigma^{*+} \Sigma^0} = -\sqrt{6} \approx -2.45\) in the SU(3) symmetric case, with the number in parentheses including SU(3) breaking giving \(-3.06\)
- The spectroscopic quadrupole moment of the \(\Delta^+\) satisfies \(Q_{\Delta^+} = r_n^2 = 4B\), where \(B\) is the two-body quadrupole operator constant with dimension fm\(^2\)

## Context

This work extends the general parameterization method of Morpurgo (1989) and Dillon and Morpurgo (1999) from baryon charge radii and magnetic moments to pion-baryon couplings and intrinsic quadrupole deformations. The results contradict earlier quark model studies by Vento (1981) that concluded the nucleon is oblate, because those studies ignored the deformation of the pion cloud itself. The findings enable future calculations of baryon octupole moments and provide a connection between the nucleon shape problem and the proton spin problem, which the authors subsequently addressed in Henley and Buchmann (2011, 2014).
