# Is the Time-Delay Signal in Bose-Einstein Correlations a Signature for the Quark-Gluon Plasma at RHIC ?

> In 1996, D.H. Rischke and M. Gyulassy proposed to use the time-delay signal in Bose-Einstein correlations (BEC) as a signature for the possible formation of a quark-gluon plasma (QGP) in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. In particular, they suggested to measure the ratio of the transversal interferometry radii, $R_{out}/R_{side}$, which can be obtained by fitting experimental BEC data with a parametrization introduced by G. Bertsch et al. in 1988. The transverse radius parameter $R_{out}$ has compared to the transverse radius parameter $R_{side}$ an additional temporal dependence which should be sensitive to a prolonged lifetime of a fireball, in case a QGP was formed in a relativistic heavy-ion collision. In my presentation, I am going to revisit the above made considerations by addressing various possible scenarios for hadron spectra (single and double inclusives) for Pb+Pb collisions at CERN/SPS beam energies and for Au+Au collisions at BNL/RHIC beam energies, respectively, within a relativistic {\it true} hydrodynamic framework (i.e., HYLANDER-C). I claim, that the time-delay signal in Bose-Einstein correlations {\it is not} a signature for the possible formation of a QGP, and I am going to explain why this is the case.

## Key claim

The time-delay signal in Bose-Einstein correlations, specifically the ratio of transverse interferometry radii R_out/R_side, is not a signature for quark-gluon plasma formation in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, contrary to the proposal by Rischke and Gyulassy (1996). The author claims this based on simulations within a relativistic true hydrodynamic framework (HYLANDER-C) for both CERN/SPS and BNL/RHIC beam energies.

## Method

The author used the relativistic hydrodynamic code HYLANDER-C to simulate various scenarios for single and double inclusive hadron spectra in Pb+Pb collisions at CERN/SPS beam energies and Au+Au collisions at BNL/RHIC beam energies. The transverse interferometry radii R_out and R_side were extracted from the simulated Bose-Einstein correlation data using the parametrization introduced by Bertsch et al. (1988), and the ratio R_out/R_side was examined for sensitivity to fireball lifetime.

## Context

This work directly challenges the proposal by Rischke and Gyulassy (1996) that the R_out/R_side ratio from Bose-Einstein correlations could serve as a signature for quark-gluon plasma formation. The author uses a relativistic hydrodynamic framework (HYLANDER-C) to test this proposal for both CERN/SPS Pb+Pb collisions and BNL/RHIC Au+Au collisions. The findings suggest that alternative interpretations of the time-delay signal in Bose-Einstein correlations are needed, and the work enables further investigation into what physical mechanisms actually produce the observed interferometry radii in heavy-ion collisions.
