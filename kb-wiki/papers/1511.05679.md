# Pion photo- and electroproduction and the chiral MAID interface

> We discuss the extended on-mass-shell scheme for manifestly Lorentz-invariant baryon chiral perturbation theory. We present a calculation of pion photo- and electroproduction up to and including order $q^4$. The low-energy constants have been fixed by fitting experimental data in all available reaction channels. Our results can be accessed via a web interface, the so-called chiral MAID (http://www.kph.uni-mainz.de/MAID/chiralmaid/). We explain how our program works and how it can be used for further analysis.

## Key claim

The authors present a manifestly Lorentz-invariant chiral perturbation theory (RChPT) calculation of pion photo- and electroproduction up to order q^4, using the extended on-mass-shell (EOMS) scheme to restore power counting. The claim is that this is the first ChPT approach that can consistently describe all pion photo- and electroproduction channels in the threshold region, but the applicability is limited to energies below approximately E_gamma^lab = 170 MeV for neutral pion photoproduction, above which deviations from data become visible.

## Method

The authors performed a manifestly Lorentz-invariant baryon chiral perturbation theory calculation using the extended on-mass-shell (EOMS) renormalization scheme, computing all tree-level and one-loop diagrams up to and including order q^4 (15 tree-level and 50 one-loop diagrams at O(q^3); 20 tree-level and 85 one-loop diagrams at O(q^4)). The low-energy constants (LECs) were determined by fitting experimental data across all available reaction channels, and the numerical results were implemented in a web interface called chiral MAID (chiMAID) that allows users to modify the LECs arbitrarily.

## Key numbers

- The calculation includes 19 LECs at O(q^3) and O(q^4): 8 in the isospin (0) channel, 8 in the isospin (+) channel, and 3 in the isospin (-) channel
- For neutral pion photoproduction, the theory starts to deviate from experimental data for laboratory photon energies above E_gamma^lab approximately 170 MeV
- The reduced chi-squared (chi^2_red) is shown as a function of fitted energy range up to E_gamma^lab = 165.8 MeV (first nine panels of data)
- For electroproduction, total cross sections are shown as a function of Q^2 for different c.m. energies above threshold Delta W in MeV
- The LEC d_9 in the isospin (0) channel has value -1.22 +/- 0.12 GeV^{-2}
- The LEC d_8 in the isospin (+) channel has value -1.09 +/- 0.12 GeV^{-2}
- The LEC d_20 in the isospin (-) channel has value 4.34 +/- 0.08 GeV^{-2}
- The LEC d_21 in the isospin (-) channel has value -3.1 +/- 0.1 GeV^{-2}
- For electroproduction, the combined constants tilde_e_52 = e_52 + e_72 = 6.4 +/- 0.7 and tilde_e_53 = e_53 + e_73 = -0.5 +/- 0.2

## Context

This work extends the earlier heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) calculations by Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner (1992) by using a manifestly Lorentz-invariant formulation with the EOMS scheme, which solves the power-counting problem that had previously plagued relativistic ChPT. The results are compared with the Dubna-Mainz-Taipei (DMT) model by Kamalov et al. (2001) and the covariant unitary chiral approach of Gasparyan and Lutz (2010). The web interface chiral MAID enables future studies of the range of validity of ChPT and allows new experimental data to be used for re-determining the LECs.
