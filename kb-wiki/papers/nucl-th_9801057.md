# Comment on ``Evidence for Narrow Baryon Resonances in Inelastic pp Scattering''

> Compton scattering data are sensitive to the existence of low-mass resonances reported by Tatischeff et al. We show that such states, with their reported properties, are excluded by previous Compton scattering experiments.

# Comment on "Evidence for Narrow Baryon Resonances in Inelastic pp Scattering"

## Key claim

Compton scattering data exclude the three narrow neutral baryon resonances claimed by Tatischeff et al. (1997) at masses 1004, 1044, and 1094 MeV, provided the resonances have radiative branching ratios of order unity as reported; the 1094 MeV state could survive only if its radiative branching is anomalously suppressed compared to the typical value Br_γ ∼ α.

## Method

The authors computed the expected contribution of each claimed resonance to the differential cross section of Compton scattering on protons and neutrons, using the resonance masses, widths (4–15 MeV), and radiative branching ratios reported by Tatischeff et al. (1997). They compared these predictions to existing experimental data on γp scattering at 68, 112, and 169 MeV from Federspiel et al. (1991), MacGibbon et al. (1995), and Hall et al. (1993), and to γn and γd data from Rose et al. (1990) and Lucas (1994). Upper limits on the product of radiative branching and total width were extracted by requiring that the resonance signal not exceed the observed cross section variations.

## Key numbers

- For the claimed N*(1004) resonance on protons, the data of Federspiel et al. (1991) near E_γ = 68 MeV give an upper limit X < 4×10⁻⁸, implying total width Γ < 0.2 eV, seven orders of magnitude smaller than the reported width of 4–15 MeV.
- For the claimed p*(1044) resonance, data of MacGibbon et al. (1995) give an upper limit Γ < 1.6 eV.
- For the claimed p*(1094) resonance, data of Hall et al. (1993) give an upper limit Br²_γ Γ < 7 eV.
- For the claimed n*(1044) state, data from Rose et al. (1990) at E_γ = 110 MeV yield X < 1.5×10⁻⁷ and Γ < 6 eV.
- From elastic γd scattering at 69 MeV (Lucas 1994), the combined total width of p*(1004) and n*(1004) is bounded by Γ_p + Γ_n < 1.5 eV.
- The transition dipole moment of N*(1004) is constrained to D < 1.0×10⁻³ fm, at least three orders of magnitude smaller than the nucleon size.

## Context

This comment directly contradicts the claim of narrow baryon resonances below the pion production threshold by Tatischeff et al. (1997), using previously published Compton scattering data that the original Letter did not analyze. The results are consistent with a null search for isospin 3/2 resonances in this mass region by Ramavataram et al. (1994). The analysis demonstrates that Compton scattering provides a sensitive and model-independent constraint on any narrow, radiatively-decaying baryon state, and that the claimed resonances would require unphysically small transition moments to evade detection.
