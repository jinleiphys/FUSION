# Possibility to disentangle anisotropic flow, flow fluctuation, and nonflow assuming Gaussian fluctuations

> We suggest the possibility to disentangle anisotropic flow, flow fluctuation, and nonflow using two-, four-, and six-particle azimuthal moments assuming Gaussian fluctuations. We show that such disentanglement is possible when the flow fluctuations are large, comparable to the average flow magnitude. When fluctuations are small, the disentanglement becomes difficult. We verify our results with a toy-model Monte Carlo simulation.

## Key claim

Under the assumption of Gaussian flow fluctuations, it may be possible to disentangle the average anisotropic flow, flow fluctuations, and nonflow using two-, four-, and six-particle azimuthal moments, but only when the flow fluctuations are large (comparable to the average flow magnitude); when fluctuations are small, the disentanglement becomes difficult because higher-order moments do not add new information.

## Method

The authors derive analytic expressions for the two-, four-, and six-particle azimuthal moments under the assumption that event-by-event flow fluctuations follow a Gaussian distribution with mean ⟨v⟩ and width σ. They then solve the system of three equations (Eqs. v{2}², v{4}⁴, and v{6}⁶ expressed in terms of ⟨v⟩, σ, and nonflow δ) to extract all three quantities. The method is verified using a toy-model Monte Carlo simulation where particles are generated with flow modulation, Gaussian flow fluctuations, and a simple nonflow contribution from correlated particle pairs.

## Key numbers

- For large fluctuations (σ²/⟨v⟩² ≳ 0.2), a measurement of the ratio v{6}/v{4} can determine the flow fluctuations; the ratio is given by v{6}/v{4} = (1 − 3σ²/⟨v⟩²)^{1/6} / (1 − 2σ²/⟨v⟩² − σ⁴/⟨v⟩⁴)^{1/4}.
- When fluctuations are small (σ²/⟨v⟩² ≪ 1), v{4}/⟨v⟩ ≈ v{6}/⟨v⟩ ≈ 1 − σ²/(2⟨v⟩²), and v{6}/v{4} ≈ 1 − σ⁶/(3⟨v⟩⁶).
- In the toy-model simulation with N = 400, N_cl2 = 72, ⟨v₂⟩ = 0.07, σ₂ = 0.03, and δ₂ = 9×10⁻⁴ (Case i), the extracted values were ⟨v⟩ = 0.070 ± 0.003, σ = 0.030 ± 0.004, and δ = (8.4 ± 6.1)×10⁻⁴.
- In Case (iii) with N = 1200, N_cl2 = 288, ⟨v₂⟩ = 0.03, σ₂ = 0.02, and δ₂ = 4×10⁻⁴, the extracted values were ⟨v⟩ = 0.0300 ± 0.0009, σ = 0.0200 ± 0.0005, and δ = (4.0 ± 0.8)×10⁻⁴.
- For triangular flow with ⟨v₃⟩ = 0.03, σ₃ = 0.03, and δ₃ = 4×10⁻⁴ (Case iv), the extracted values were ⟨v⟩ = 0.0300 ± 0.0006, σ = 0.0300 ± 0.0001, and δ = (4.0 ± 0.4)×10⁻⁴.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Voloshin (2008) on extracting flow fluctuations under Gaussian assumptions via higher cumulants, and by Borghini et al. (2001) on multi-particle correlation methods for flow measurements. The paper addresses the long-standing problem that two- and four-particle moments alone cannot determine average flow, flow fluctuations, and nonflow simultaneously, as noted by Voloshin (2008). The method is particularly relevant for odd harmonic flows (v₁ and v₃) where fluctuations are expected to be large, and enables experimental tests of different fluctuation models; the authors note that preliminary STAR data appear to favor their Gaussian fluctuation model over the Bessel-Gaussian model proposed by Voloshin (2008).
