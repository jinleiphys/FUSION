# Charmed and beautiful baryonic systems (hypernuclei) in chiral soliton models

## Key claim
The binding energies of baryonic systems with charm or beauty quantum numbers and neutron excess are estimated within the bound state version of the chiral topological soliton model, but the results for binding energies turned out to be partly negative. The inclusion of flavor excitation energies into the minimization of the total mass of the baryonic state leads to a decrease in mass, with this effect increasing for heavier flavors, yet the procedure yields negative binding energies in several cases.

## Method
The authors apply the bound state version of the chiral topological soliton model, extending the basic SU(2) skyrmion to SU(3) with (u,d,c) or (u,d,b) flavor sectors. They use the rational map approximation for multiskyrmions with baryon numbers up to B=13, and implement a skyrmion rescaling (squeezing) procedure that includes flavor excitation energies into the minimization of the total mass of the baryonic state. The total binding energies are estimated using a double subtraction procedure that compares the energy of the flavored state with the sum of the non-flavored baryonic system mass plus the mass difference between the heavy-flavor hyperon and the nucleon.

## Key numbers
- Flavor excitation energies for charm (omega_c) in the SK4 variant range from 1.50 to 1.54 GeV for B=1 to B=13; for beauty (omega_b) they range from 4.74 to 4.80 GeV over the same baryon numbers.
- Flavor excitation energies for charm in the SK6 variant range from 1.61 to 1.64 GeV; for beauty from 4.92 to 4.98 GeV.
- The sigma-term Gamma for B=1 in the SK4 variant is 4.80 GeV^-1; for B=13 it is 70.2 GeV^-1.
- The isotopic moment of inertia Theta_I(SK4) for B=1 is 2.64 GeV^-1; for B=13 it is 34.54 GeV^-1.
- The flavor moment of inertia Theta_F(SK4) for B=1 is 0.85 GeV^-1; for B=13 it is 12.95 GeV^-1.
- The ratio F_D/F_pi used in calculations is 1.576; F_B/F_pi is approximately 1.44.

## Context
This work extends earlier chiral soliton approach estimates by Kopeliovich (2010) and Kopeliovich and Zakharova (2011) for baryon numbers up to B=4 and B=8 respectively, now including charm and beauty flavors with baryon numbers up to B=13. The rescaling procedure follows the approach used by Schwesinger et al. for strange dibaryon configurations. The study contrasts with potential model approaches to charmed hypernuclei by Tyapkin (1975), Dover and Kahana (1977), and others, and addresses the open question of whether heavy-flavor hypernuclei can be bound given that the chiral soliton model yields partly negative binding energies. The results suggest that the rescaling procedure is important for moderate baryon numbers but becomes less significant with increasing B.
