# Production of ϕMesons in Near-Threshold πN and N N Reactions

> We analyze the production of $ϕ$ mesons in $πN$ and NN reactions in the near-threshold region, using throughout the conventional ``non-strange'' dynamics based on such processes which are allowed by the non-ideal $ω-ϕ$ mixing. We show that the occurrence of the direct $ϕNN$ interaction may show up in different unpolarized and polarization observables in $πN\to Nϕ$ reactions. We find a strong non-trivial difference between observables in the reactions $pp\to ppϕ$ and $pn\to pnϕ$ caused by the different role of the spin singlet and triplet states in the entrance channel. A series of predictions for the experimental study of this effect is presented.

## Key claim

The conventional "non-strange" dynamics based on ω-φ mixing can explain φ meson production in near-threshold πN and NN reactions without invoking intrinsic strangeness in the nucleon, but the authors hedge that two distinct parameter sets (one where meson exchange dominates and one where direct φ emission dominates) both fit existing data equally well, so additional polarization observables are needed to distinguish them.

## Method

The authors constructed a tree-level hadronic model with two competing mechanisms: (a) meson exchange via the φρπ coupling and (b) direct φ radiation from nucleon legs via the φNN interaction, both dressed by monopole form factors. They fitted the free parameters (gφNN, cut-off parameters Λρφρπ and ΛN) to existing data on π⁻p → nφ and pp → ppφ total cross sections and angular distributions at excess energies of 20-100 MeV, then calculated predictions for spin density matrix elements, decay anisotropies, and beam-target asymmetries for pp and pn reactions including final-state interactions via Jost functions.

## Key numbers

- gφρπ = -1.10 GeV⁻¹, determined from Γ(φ → ρπ) = 0.69 MeV
- Three parameter sets used: Set A (gφNN = -0.24, Λρφρπ = 1.34 GeV, ΛN = 1.065 GeV); Set B (gφNN = -0.8, Λρφρπ = 1.34 GeV, ΛN = 0.715 GeV); Set C (gφNN = -0.8, Λρφρπ = 1.60 GeV, ΛN = 1.99 GeV)
- Minimum |gφNN| values from fits: 0.07 for the meson-exchange-dominant solution and 0.60 for the direct-emission-dominant solution
- The ratio σφ/σω = (3.7 ± 0.5) × 10⁻³ at Tlab = 2.85 GeV from DISTO data
- Excess energy for pp → ppφ analysis: Δs¹/² = 82 MeV
- Theoretical estimate κφ = 0.2 used as upper limit for tensor coupling

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Nakayama (1999) on pp → ppφ by performing a combined study of π⁻p → nφ and pp → ppφ at the same excess energies (20-100 MeV) to reduce parameter uncertainties. It contrasts with the approach of Ellis (1999) who argued that hidden strangeness might be seen in hadronic reactions, and with earlier work by Titov, Kämpfer, and Reznik (1997-1998) on φ photoproduction. The results enable experimental tests with the HADES detector at SIS/GSI, which can identify φ via both e⁺e⁻ and K⁺K⁻ decay channels, to distinguish between competing reaction mechanisms and ultimately clarify the role of strangeness in the nucleon.
