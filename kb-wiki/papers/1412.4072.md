# Beam Energy Scan a Case for the Chiral Magnetic Effect in Au-Au Collisions

> The Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) is predicted for Au-Au collisions at RHIC. However many backgrounds can give signals that make the measurement hard to interpret. The STAR experiment has made measurements at different collisions energy ranging from $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=7.7 GeV to 62.4 GeV. In the analysis that is presented we show that the CME turns on with energy and is not present in central collisions where the induced magnetic is small.

# Beam Energy Scan a Case for the Chiral Magnetic Effect in Au-Au Collisions

## Key claim

The Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) turns on with collision energy and is not present in central collisions where the induced magnetic field is small, but the analysis assumes the CME is the only charge-dependent, reaction-plane-dependent effect at work when isolating the HCME amplitude.

## Method

The analysis uses STAR data from Au-Au collisions at six beam energies (7.7 to 62.4 GeV) to extract the two-particle azimuthal correlations gamma and delta for same-sign and opposite-sign charged pairs as a function of centrality. These correlations are algebraically separated into reaction-plane-dependent (H) and reaction-plane-independent (F) components, which are further decomposed into charge-dependent and charge-independent amplitudes. The charge-dependent, reaction-plane-dependent amplitude (HCME) is attributed to the CME, and a quenching factor is introduced to account for particles that lose direction when re-entering the fireball.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies analyzed: sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27.0, 39.0, and 62.4 GeV, with event statistics ranging from 4M (7.7 GeV) to 100M (39.0 GeV)
- Acceptance cuts: 0.15 < p_t < 2 GeV/c and |eta| < 1.0
- HCME amplitude reaches approximately 0.0003 at top energies, with an isolated highest value of approximately 0.0005 (units of 10^-4)
- Under maximum quenching, the HCME amplitude becomes approximately 0.0006, with an isolated highest value of approximately 0.0010
- FCI amplitude reaches approximately -0.0035 at the lowest beam energy and most peripheral collisions
- HCI amplitude reaches approximately -0.0025 at low beam energy and most peripheral collisions
- FCD amplitude reaches approximately -0.0018 at the lowest beam energy and 55% centrality
- The ratio RF for reaction-plane-independent amplitudes drops from 1.0 at high beam energy to 0.3 at the lowest energy and most peripheral collisions

## Context

This work extends the STAR collaboration's published measurements of charged particle azimuthal correlations consistent with CME expectations, using the formalism from Voloshin (2004) and the background treatment from Koch (2010) which describes Transverse Momentum Conservation and Local Charge Conservation as important backgrounds. The analysis also incorporates the quenching mechanism proposed by Warringa (2013), where P-odd domains on the fireball surface emit same-sign particles along the magnetic field while opposite-sign particles are thermalized. The results enable future studies of the CME signal as a function of energy and centrality, with the ratio RH showing a gaussian shape consistent with quenching in the reacting systems.
