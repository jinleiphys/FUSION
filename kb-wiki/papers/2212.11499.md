# Theory of non-linear diffusion with a physical gapped mode

> In a system with one conserved charge the charge diffusion is modified by non-linear self-interactions within an effective field theory (EFT) of diffusive fluctuations. We include the slowest ultraviolet (UV) mode, constructing a UV-regulated EFT. The relaxation time of this UV mode is protected from renormalization, as supported by experimental data in a bad metal system. Furthermore, the retarded density-density Green's function acquires four branch points, eventually increasing the range of applicability. We discuss the fate of long-time-tails as well as implications for the quark gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a UV-regulated effective field theory (EFT) of non-linear diffusion by including the slowest ultraviolet mode, and finds that the relaxation time of this UV mode is protected from renormalization by fluctuations, while the diffusion constant receives significant renormalization.

## Method

The authors start from a phenomenological linear diffusion equation with a finite relaxation time tau (the Maxwell-Cattaneo equation) and promote the diffusion constant D to a function of the density fluctuations n, yielding a non-linear equation of motion. They construct the effective action using the Martin-Siggia-Rose formalism, then compute the one-loop retarded density-density Green's function to study renormalization of the transport coefficients and the long-time behavior of correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- The relaxation time tau is protected from renormalization: delta tau(omega, k) = 0 (exact result from one-loop calculation)
- The long-time tail in one spatial dimension behaves as t^{-1/2} times an exponential factor, identical to the known t^{-d/2} behavior without UV regulator
- Four branch points appear in the retarded Green's function at frequencies: omega_{11,22} = -i/tau (1 ∓ sqrt(1 - D k^2 tau)) and omega_{12,21} = -i/tau ± |k| sqrt(D/tau)
- For the Bjorken expanding QGP, the non-linear correction to the charge density is Delta⟨n(tau_p)⟩ = a T chi^2 mu (lambda_D^2/D^2) (1/(D tau_p)^{3/2}) (1 - (11/8)(tau/tau_p) + ...)

## Context

This work extends the non-linear diffusive fluctuation EFT of Chen-Lin (2018) and Chao (2020) by including a finite relaxation time tau as a UV regulator, which was previously taken to be zero. The theory is motivated by experimental data from a bad metal system (Brown 2018) where the relaxation time was measured in a Fermi-Hubbard model using ultracold lithium-6 atoms. The results enable studies of fluctuation effects in expanding quark-gluon plasma under Bjorken flow and near the QCD critical point, where the relaxation time becomes large and must be included in the long-wavelength description.
