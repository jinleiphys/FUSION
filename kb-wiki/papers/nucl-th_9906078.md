# Vacuum Condensates in the Global Color Symmetry Model

> Based on the quark propagator in the instanton dilute liquid approximation, we calculate analytically the quark condensate $<\bar{q}q>$, the mixed quark gluon condensate $g_{s}<\bar{q}G_{μν}σ^{μν}q>$ and the four quark condensate $<\bar{q} Γq\bar{q} Γq>$ at the mean field level in the framework of global color symmetry model. The numerical calculation shows that the values of these condensates are compatible with the ranges determined by other nonperturbative approaches. Moreover, we find that for nonlocal four quark condensate the previous vacuum saturation assumption is not a good approximation even at the mean field level.

## Key claim

The quark condensate, mixed quark-gluon condensate, and local four-quark condensate calculated at mean-field level in the global color symmetry model using the instanton dilute liquid approximation are compatible with ranges from other nonperturbative approaches; however, for the nonlocal four-quark condensate the vacuum saturation assumption is not a good approximation even at this mean-field level.

## Method

The authors use the quark propagator from the instanton dilute liquid approximation as input to the global color symmetry model (GCM), rather than using a model gluon propagator. They compute vacuum condensates via a saddle-point expansion of the GCM generating functional at mean-field level, evaluating the quark self-energy functions A(p^2) and B(p^2) from the instanton dilute liquid approximation. The condensates are then obtained from functional derivatives of the generating functional with respect to external sources.

## Key numbers

- Quark condensate: (-)<q̅q>^(1/3) = 207 MeV (at renormalization scale μ = 1 GeV^2)
- Mixed quark-gluon condensate: (-)<g_s q̅ σ G q>^(1/5) = 415 MeV
- Local four-quark condensate: <:q̅ γ_μ (λ^a_C/2) q q̅ γ_μ (λ^a_C/2) q:>_μ = (-4/9) <:q̅q:>^2, consistent with vacuum saturation
- Nonlocal quark condensate g(x^2) = <:q̅(x)q(0):>/<:q̅(0)q(0):> ranges from 1.0 at x^2=0 GeV^2 to 0.31 at x^2=20 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the global color symmetry model approach of Cahill and Roberts (1985) and Tandy (1997) by using the instanton dilute liquid approximation of Shuryak (1984) and Dyakonov and Petrov (1986) as input instead of model gluon propagators. The results are compared with QCD sum rules of Narison (1989), quenched lattice QCD of Kremer and Schierholz (1987), the instanton liquid model of Polyakov and Weiss (1996), and the effective quark-quark interaction model of Meissner (1997). The finding that vacuum saturation fails for nonlocal four-quark condensates even at mean-field level provides guidance for improving QCD sum rule analyses that rely on this assumption.
