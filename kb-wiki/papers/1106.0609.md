# Examination of the Gunion-Bertsch formula for soft gluon radiation

> The spectrum of emitted gluons from the process g+g -> g+g+g has been evaluated by relaxing some of the approximations used in earlier works. The difference in the results from earlier calculations have been pointed out. The formula obtained in the present work has been applied to estimate physical quantities like equilibration rate of gluons and the energy loss of fast gluon in the gluonic plasma.

# Examination of the Gunion-Bertsch formula for soft gluon radiation

## Key claim

The Gunion-Bertsch formula for soft gluon radiation from the process g+g → g+g+g is missing terms of order O(k⊥⁻¹) and O(k⊥⁰) in the matrix element expansion; when these correction terms are included, the equilibration rate and energy loss of fast gluons in gluonic plasma differ significantly from earlier calculations, with the energy loss enhanced by about 40% at T=300 MeV and 20% at T=400 MeV compared to the spectra of Das & Alam (2010) and Ghosh & Roy (2010).

## Method

The authors start from the full invariant amplitude squared for gg → ggg given by Berends et al. (1981) and expand it in the soft-gluon limit (k₅ → 0) keeping terms up to O(t³/s³) in the Mandelstam variables and up to O(k⊥²) in the transverse momentum of the radiated gluon, using a binomial expansion of 1/u in powers of (k⊥/√s − t/s). They then apply the resulting gluon spectrum to compute the equilibration rate (with s = 18T² following Biro et al. (1993)) and the energy loss of a 15 GeV gluon traversing a gluonic heat bath of length 4 fm, using the formalism of Wiedemann & Gyulassy (1999) with a Debye-screened elastic cross section and a theta-function implementation of the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect.

## Key numbers

- The matrix element squared for gg → ggg is expressed as: |M|² = 12 g² |M_gg→gg|² (1/k⊥²) × [(1 + t/(2s) + 5t²/(2s²) − t³/s³) − (3/(2√s) + 4t/(s√s) − 3t²/(2s²√s)) k⊥ + (5/(2s) + t/(2s²) + 5t²/s³) k⊥²]
- The equilibration rate obtained with the correction terms is smaller than when corrections are neglected for the entire temperature range considered (approximately 200–500 MeV from Fig. 1).
- For a 15 GeV gluon moving through a gluonic fluid of dimension 4 fm, the energy loss ΔE is enhanced by about 40% at T = 300 MeV and about 20% at T = 400 MeV compared to the spectra of Das & Alam (2010) and Ghosh & Roy (2010).
- The average transverse momentum transfer squared is ⟨q⊥²⟩ = (s m_D²)/(s − 4m_D²) ln(s/(4m_D²)), with s ∼ 6ET for an out-of-equilibrium incident gluon.
- The Debye mass is m_D = √(2π α_s(T) (C_A + N_F/2) T/3), with C_A = 3 for SU(3).

## Context

This work extends the original Gunion-Bertsch (1982) formula for soft gluon radiation by including previously neglected O(k⊥⁻¹) and O(k⊥⁰) terms in the matrix element expansion, correcting the approximations used in recent revisitations by Das & Alam (2010) and Ghosh & Roy (2010). The corrected gluon spectrum can be applied to other 2 → 2 + g partonic processes such as q+q → q+q+g and q+g → q+g+g, and has implications for chemical equilibration, energy loss phenomenology at RHIC and LHC, and transport coefficient calculations in gluonic plasma.
