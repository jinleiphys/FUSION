# Coupled-cluster studies of infinite nuclear matter

> The aim of this work is to develop the relevant formalism for performing coupled-cluster (CC) calculations in nuclear matter and neutron star matter, including thereby important correlations to infinite order in the interaction and testing modern nuclear forces based on chiral effective field theory. Our formalism includes the exact treatment of the so-called Pauli operator in a partial wave expansion of the equation of state. Nuclear and neutron matter calculations are done using a coupled particle-particle and hole-hole ladder approximation. The coupled ladder equations are derived as an approximation of CC theory, leaving out particle-hole and non-linear diagrams from the CC doubles amplitude equation. This study is a first step toward CC calculations for nuclear and neutron matter. We present results for both symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter employing state-of-the-art nucleon-nucleon interactions based on chiral effective field theory. We employ also the newly optimized chiral interaction [A. Ekström et al., Phys. Rev. Lett. 110, 192502 (2013)] to study infinite nuclear matter. The ladder approximation method and corresponding results are compared with conventional Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory.

## Key claim

The paper develops a coupled-cluster (CC) formalism for infinite nuclear matter that is a first step toward full CC calculations, using a coupled particle-particle and hole-hole ladder approximation that omits particle-hole and non-linear diagrams from the CC doubles amplitude equation, and the results show at most almost a 6% difference from standard Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory.

## Method

The authors derived coupled ladder equations as an approximation of CC doubles theory, keeping only the linear particle-particle and hole-hole ladder diagrams and neglecting particle-hole and non-linear terms. They solved these equations using a fixed-point iteration scheme in a plane-wave momentum basis with angular-averaged single-particle potentials, employing both exact and angular-averaged Pauli exclusion operators in a partial wave expansion. Calculations were performed for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter using chiral nucleon-nucleon interactions at N3LO and the newly optimized NNLO_opt interaction.

## Key numbers

- The exact treatment of the Pauli operator yields corrections of order 1.7-2% of the total energy in symmetric nuclear matter
- Inclusion of both hole-hole and particle-particle ladders gives corrections of order 0.7-2% compared to the approximation with only particle-particle ladders
- The maximum difference between the CC ladder calculation and standard Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach is almost 6%
- The NNLO_opt interaction gives binding energies in A=3 and A=4 systems that differ by 20 keV and 45 keV from experimental values, respectively

## Context

This work extends the early CC calculations of Day and Zabolitzky (1980s) for nuclear matter, which used the Bochum truncation scheme and older interactions, by applying modern chiral effective field theory interactions and avoiding the angular-average approximation for the Pauli operator. The coupled ladder approximation is similar to the self-consistent Green's function ladder approximation used by Dickhoff and collaborators, but with single-particle potentials only at the Hartree-Fock level rather than fully self-consistent off-shell self-energies. This study enables future full CC calculations at the T2 level that would include all particle-hole and non-linear diagrams, and provides a benchmark for comparing CC methods with Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory and hole-line expansion approaches.
