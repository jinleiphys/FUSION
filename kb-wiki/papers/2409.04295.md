# Sensitivity of jet quenching to the initial state in heavy-ion collisions

> In heavy-ion collisions, nuclear matter is subjected to extreme conditions in a highly dynamical, rapidly evolving environment. This poses a tremendous challenge for calculating jet quenching observables. Current approaches rely on analytical results for static cases, introducing theoretical uncertainties and biases in our understanding of the pre-equilibrated medium. To address this issue, we employ resummation schemes to derive analytical rates for radiative energy loss in generic, evolving backgrounds. We investigate regimes where rare scattering and multiple scattering with the dynamical medium occurs, and extract relevant scales governing the in-medium emission rate of soft gluons. Our analysis indicates that strong jet quenching is only possible when the equilibration time of the medium is longer than its mean free path, highlighting the importance of medium modifications of jets in the earliest stages of heavy-ion collisions. We also demonstrate analytically that a medium evolution, which initially has a small coupling to jets, typically leads to a stronger jet azimuthal asymmetry at the same jet suppression factor.

## Key claim

Strong jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions is only possible when the equilibration time of the medium is longer than its mean free path, and a medium evolution that initially has a small coupling to jets typically leads to a stronger jet azimuthal asymmetry at the same jet suppression factor.

## Method

The authors derive analytical rates for medium-induced radiative energy loss using resummation schemes (opacity expansion, resummed opacity expansion, and improved opacity expansion) that are valid for generic, evolving backgrounds rather than static media. They apply these rates to two phenomenological models of medium evolution: model (i) representing an initially over-occupied system with a large initial jet quenching parameter that decays, and model (ii) representing an initially under-occupied system with a delayed onset of quenching up to a thermalization time t_m. The rates are matched through time-dependent kinematic scales to cover the full emission phase space in gluon energy and time.

## Key numbers

- The jet quenching parameter in the early stages of a collision can be approximately 6 GeV^2/fm at tau ~ 0.06 fm, based on prior work by Carrington et al. (2021)
- For numerical studies, the authors choose hat q_0 = 0.3 GeV^3, mu^2 = 0.09 GeV^2, and t_m = 1 fm
- In the Bjorken scenario with alpha = 1, the medium hydrodynamization time must satisfy t_m >> lambda (the mean free path) to obtain contributions from the multiple-scattering regime
- The characteristic energy scale in the opacity expansion is bar omega_c(t) = mu^2 t/2
- The Bethe-Heitler frequency is omega_BH(t) = mu^2 lambda(t)/2
- The characteristic scale in the improved opacity expansion is omega_c(t) = hat q(t) t^2/2

## Context

This work extends prior analytical treatments of jet quenching that relied on static medium assumptions, introducing theoretical uncertainties in understanding the pre-equilibrated medium. The authors build on the improved opacity expansion approach developed by Mehtar-Tani et al. (2019) and Barata et al. (2020-2021), and on the work of Isaksen et al. (2022) for scattering on background media. The two medium evolution models are inspired by the "bottom-up" equilibration scenario for over-occupied systems (Mueller, 1999; Baier, 2000) and by under-occupied systems studied by Andres et al. (2020, 2022). The results enable more realistic calculations of jet quenching observables that account for the full dynamical evolution of the quark-gluon plasma, including its earliest stages.
