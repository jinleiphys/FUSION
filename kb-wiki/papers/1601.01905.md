# Magnetic dipole excitations of $^{50}$Cr

> The low-lying $M1$-strength of the open-shell nucleus $^{50}$Cr has been studied with the method of nuclear resonance fluorescence up to 9.7 MeV, using bremsstrahlung at the superconducting Darmstadt linear electron accelerator S-DALINAC and Compton backscattered photons at the High Intensity $γ$-ray Source (HI$γ$S) facility between 6 and 9.7 MeV of the initial photon energy. Fifteen $1^{+}$ states have been observed between 3.6 and 9.7 MeV. Following our analysis, the lowest $1^{+}$ state at 3.6 MeV can be considered as an isovector orbital mode with some spin admixture. The obtained results generally match the estimations and trends typical for the scissors-like mode. Detailed calculations within the Skyrme Quasiparticle Random-Phase-Approximation method and the Large-Scale Shell Model justify our conclusions. The calculated distributions of the orbital current for the lowest $1^{+}$-state suggest the schematic view of Lipparini and Stringari (isovector rotation-like oscillations inside the rigid surface) rather than the scissors-like picture of Lo Iudice and Palumbo. The spin M1 resonance is shown to be mainly generated by spin-flip transitions between the orbitals of the $fp$-shell.

## Key claim

The lowest 1+ state at 3628.2 keV in 50Cr is identified as an isovector orbital mode with some spin admixture, consistent with the scissors-like mode systematics; however, the calculated orbital current distributions suggest the Lipparini-Stringari picture of isovector rotation-like oscillations inside a rigid surface rather than the Lo Iudice-Palumbo scissors-like picture.

## Method

Nuclear resonance fluorescence (NRF) experiments were performed using unpolarized bremsstrahlung at the S-DALINAC (endpoint energies 7.5 and 9.7 MeV) and linearly polarized quasi-monoenergetic photons from laser Compton backscattering at the HIγS facility (6-9.7 MeV). Parity assignments for spin-1 states were determined from measured asymmetries in the polarized photon beam. The experimental results were interpreted using self-consistent Skyrme Quasiparticle Random-Phase-Approximation (QRPA) with the SGII force and Large-Scale Shell Model (LSSM) calculations.

## Key numbers

- Fifteen 1+ states observed between 3.6 and 9.7 MeV.
- Lowest 1+ state at 3628.2 keV has B(M1)↑ = 1.113(49) μN².
- Experimental quadrupole deformation parameter βexp = 0.2897(44).
- Ratio B(M1;1+1 → 2+1)/B(M1;1+1 → 0+1) = 1.02(2).
- Skyrme QRPA predicts a 1+ state at 2.71 MeV with B(M1)↑ = 1.52 μN², corresponding to the experimental 3.628 MeV state.
- Twelve 1+ states had their B(M1) values measured for the first time.
- Three new M1 transitions identified at 5930.8, 8045.1, and 8120.8 keV.

## Context

This work extends the systematics of the low-energy orbital scissors-like M1 mode in fp-shell nuclei, previously studied in 46,48Ti (Richter et al., 1990) and 56Fe (Beller et al., 2015), to the deformed Cr isotope 50Cr. The new data fill the gap between Ti and Fe chains and enable improved comparisons with phenomenological estimates (Lipparini and Stringari, 1983; Pietralla et al., 1998) and self-consistent QRPA calculations for medium-mass deformed nuclei. The results also provide input for understanding the interplay between orbital and spin M1 strength and the nature of the spin M1 resonance generated by f7/2 → f5/2 spin-flip transitions.
