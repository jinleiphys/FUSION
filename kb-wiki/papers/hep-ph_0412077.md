# Role of a_1(1260) resonance in multipion decays of light vector mesons

> The contribution of the a_1(1260) meson to the amplitudes of the decays rho(770) to 4 pi, omega(782) to 5 pi, and phi(1020) to 5 pi is analyzed in the chiral model of pseudoscalar, vector, and axial vector mesons based on the generalized hidden local symmetry added with the anomalous terms. The analysis shows that inclusion of a_1 meson in the intermediate states results in enhancement of the branching ratios of the above decays by the factor ranging from 1.3 to 1.9 depending on the mass of a_1 meson ranging from 1.23 GeV to m_{a_1}=m_rho sqrt{2}=1.09 GeV, the greater factor standing in case of lower mass of the a_1.

## Key claim

Inclusion of the $a_1(1260)$ meson in intermediate states enhances the branching ratios of the multipion decays rho(770) to 4 pi, omega(782) to 5 pi, and phi(1020) to 5 pi by a factor ranging from 1.3 to 1.9, with the larger enhancement corresponding to the lower a_1 mass of 1.09 GeV (the Weinberg relation value) rather than the PDG value of 1.23 GeV.

## Method

The authors used the generalized hidden local symmetry (GHLS) model, which incorporates pseudoscalar, vector, and axial vector mesons, to compute the a_1(1260) contribution to the decay amplitudes. They derived the effective lagrangian for pi, rho, omega, and a_1 mesons at the lowest number of derivatives, computed the a_1 to 3 pi decay width, and then evaluated the a_1 contribution to the rho to 4 pi amplitude, which enters as a subprocess in the omega and phi to 5 pi decays via the dominant omega,phi to rho pi to 5 pi diagram.

## Key numbers

- a_1 to 3 pi decay width: 320 MeV at m = 1.09 GeV, 860 MeV at m = 1.23 GeV, 1024 MeV at m = 1.26 GeV
- For rho^0 to 2 pi^+ 2 pi^- at m_rho^2: width = 1.84 keV (m_a1 = 1.09 GeV), 1.59 keV (m_a1 = 1.23 GeV), 0.94 keV (no a_1)
- For rho^0 to pi^+ pi^- 2 pi^0 at m_rho^2: width = 0.81 keV (m_a1 = 1.09 GeV), 0.75 keV (m_a1 = 1.23 GeV), 0.59 keV (no a_1)
- For rho^pm to 2 pi^pm pi^mp pi^0 at m_rho^2: width = 1.53 keV (m_a1 = 1.09 GeV), 1.38 keV (m_a1 = 1.23 GeV), 0.99 keV (no a_1)
- For rho^pm to pi^pm 3 pi^0 at m_rho^2: width = 1.17 keV (m_a1 = 1.09 GeV), 1.00 keV (m_a1 = 1.23 GeV), 0.59 keV (no a_1)
- Enhancement factor for branching ratios ranges from 1.3 to 1.9 depending on a_1 mass
- Weinberg relation mass: m_a1 = sqrt(2) * m_rho = 1.09 GeV
- a_1 width is less than 1.7 MeV for invariant masses below 740 MeV (the kinematic limit for phi decay)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations in Achasov and Kozhevnikov (2000, 2003) which evaluated multipion decay branching ratios of rho, omega, and phi mesons using hidden local symmetry (HLS) while neglecting the a_1(1260) contribution. The present paper uses the generalized hidden local symmetry model of Bando et al. (1988) to include axial vector mesons. The results enable more precise predictions for these rare multipion decays, which can serve as tests of chiral models of vector and pseudoscalar mesons, particularly because the final pions in these decays are soft enough to rely on lowest-order Born amplitudes.
