# Impact of QCD sum rules coupling constants on neutron stars structure

> We present a detailed investigation on the structure of neutron stars, incorporating the presence of hyperons within a relativistic model under the mean-field approximation. Employing coupling constants derived from QCD sum rules, we explore the particle fraction in beta equilibrium and establish the mass-radius relationship for neutron stars with hyperonic matter. Additionally, we compute the stellar Love number ($\mathcal{K}_{2}$) and the tidal deformability parameter ($\varLambda$), providing valuable insights into the dynamical properties of these celestial objects. Through comparison with theoretical predictions and observational data, our results exhibit good agreement, affirming the validity of our approach. These findings contribute significantly to refining the understanding of neutron star physics, particularly in environments containing hyperons, and offer essential constraints on the equation of state governing such extreme astrophysical conditions.

## Key claim

The authors show that coupling constants derived from QCD sum rules can produce a sufficiently stiff equation of state for neutron stars containing hyperons, thereby potentially resolving the "hyperon puzzle", the contradiction between the softening of the equation of state expected from hyperon presence and the observational requirement of neutron stars with masses near 2 solar masses. The claim is conditional: only Set 2 of the QCD sum rules coupling constants yields a maximum mass above 2 solar masses, while Sets 1, 3, and 4 produce lower maximum masses.

## Method

The authors construct a relativistic mean-field model with sigma, omega, and rho mesons including nucleons (neutrons, protons), hyperons (Sigma-minus and Lambda), and leptons (electrons, muons) under beta equilibrium and charge neutrality. They use four sets of baryon-meson coupling constants: one from a conventional sigma-omega-rho model parameterization and three sets derived from QCD sum rules calculations of meson-baryon coupling constants from the literature. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are solved with the resulting equations of state to obtain mass-radius relations, tidal Love numbers, and tidal deformability parameters.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass for nucleonic matter (nep): 2.26 solar masses at central density 0.92 fm^-3, radius 11.65 km
- Maximum mass for hyperonic matter with Set 2 (QCD sum rules): 2.07 solar masses at central density 1.11 fm^-3, radius 10.72 km
- Maximum mass for hyperonic matter with Set 1 (conventional): 1.56 solar masses at central density 1.14 fm^-3, radius 10.89 km
- Maximum mass for hyperonic matter with Set 3 (QCD sum rules): 1.74 solar masses at central density 1.01 fm^-3, radius 11.64 km
- Threshold baryon density for muon appearance: 0.13 fm^-3 (Set 1), 0.23 fm^-3 (Sets 2 and 3), 0.22 fm^-3 (Set 4)
- Threshold baryon density for Sigma-minus hyperon appearance: 0.27 fm^-3 (Set 1), 0.29 fm^-3 (Set 2), 0.32 fm^-3 (Set 3), 0.53 fm^-3 (Set 4)
- Threshold baryon density for Lambda hyperon appearance: 0.39 fm^-3 (Sets 1 and 2), 0.42 fm^-3 (Set 3), 0.76 fm^-3 (Set 4)

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean-field approach of Glendenning (1984) and Karimi (2022) by incorporating coupling constants derived from QCD sum rules, a method introduced by Shifman (1978) for mesons and Ioffe (1981) for baryons. The results address the "hyperon puzzle" identified in the literature, where the presence of hyperons typically softens the equation of state and reduces maximum neutron star masses below the approximately 2 solar masses observed in pulsars such as PSR J0740+6620 and PSR J1614-2230. The findings enable future comparisons with gravitational wave observations from GW170817 and GW190425, and provide constraints on the equation of state for dense matter containing hyperons.
