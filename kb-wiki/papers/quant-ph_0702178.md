# Resonances of Multichannel Systems

> We describe structure of the T-matrices, scattering matrices, and Green functions on unphysical energy sheets in multichannel scattering problems with binary channels and in the three-body problem. Based on the explicit representations obtained for the values of T- and S-matrices on the unphysical sheets, we prove that the resonances belonging to an unphysical sheet are just those energies where the correspondingly truncated scattering matrix, taken in the physical sheet, has eigenvalue zero. We show, in addition, that eigenvectors of the truncated scattering matrix associated with its zero eigenvalue are formed of the breakup amplitudes for the respective resonant states.

## Key claim

The paper proves that resonances on a given unphysical energy sheet of a multichannel scattering system correspond exactly to energies where the correspondingly truncated scattering matrix, evaluated on the physical sheet, has eigenvalue zero; this equivalence holds under the condition that the interaction potentials are holomorphic functions of the momenta on the whole complex spaces and fall off sufficiently rapidly.

## Method

The author derives explicit analytic continuation formulas for the T-matrices, scattering matrices, and Green functions from the physical energy sheet onto arbitrary unphysical sheets for multichannel systems with binary channels and for the three-body problem. These representations are obtained by applying the residue theorem to Cauchy-type integrals that appear in the Lippmann-Schwinger equations, yielding expressions that involve only quantities evaluated on the physical sheet. The key step involves introducing truncated scattering matrices \(s_\ell(z)\) that retain only those channel indices corresponding to non-zero branch numbers on the target unphysical sheet.

## Key numbers

- For the two-body case, the continuation of the free Green function onto the unphysical sheet \(\Pi_1\) is given by \(g_0(z|_{\Pi_1}) = g_0(z) + \mathrm{a}_0(z) j^\dagger(z) j(z)\) where \(\mathrm{a}_0(z) = -\pi\mathrm{i}\sqrt{z}\).
- The continued two-body T-matrix on the unphysical sheet is \(t(z|_{\Pi_1}) = t(z) - \mathrm{a}_0(z) t(z) j^\dagger(z) [s(z)]^{-1} j(z) t(z)\), with \(\mathrm{a}_0(z) = -\pi\mathrm{i}\sqrt{z}\).
- For multichannel systems with \(m\) binary channels, the Riemann surface has \(2^m\) sheets when all channel dimensions \(n_\alpha\) are odd, and infinitely many sheets when any \(n_\alpha\) is even.
- The continued scattering matrix on sheet \(\Pi_\ell\) is expressed as \(s(z|_{\Pi_\ell}) = \mathcal{E} [\widehat{I} + {{^{\ulcorner}}t^{\urcorner}} A\mathrm{e} - {{^{\ulcorner}}t^{\urcorner}} LA s_\ell^{-1} {{^{\ulcorner}}t^{\urcorner}} A\mathrm{e}] \mathcal{E}\), where \(\mathcal{E}\) is the inversion operator for odd \(\ell_\alpha\) and identity for even \(\ell_\alpha\).

## Context

This work extends the author's prior results from Motovilov (1993, 1997) on explicit representations for T-matrices and Green functions on unphysical sheets, providing a unified framework for two-body, multichannel binary, and three-body systems. The paper builds on the Gamow (1928) interpretation of resonances as complex poles of the scattering amplitude and the Jost function approach, while offering a practical alternative to the complex scaling method of Balslev and Combes (1971) by reducing resonance calculations entirely to the physical sheet. The results enable new computational methods for finding resonances in multichannel systems by searching for zeros of the truncated scattering matrix on the physical sheet, as demonstrated in subsequent work by the author and collaborators (2000, 1997, 1999).
