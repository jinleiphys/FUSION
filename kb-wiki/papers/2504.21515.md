# Double nuclear modification factor in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> The hypothesis of the factorization of the double nuclear modification factor in relativistic heavy ion collisions is tested for different types of final-state hadrons within the HYDJET++ model. The results demonstrate that this factorization holds reasonably well at moderately high transverse momenta, provided that the effects of double parton scattering can be small or neglected.

## Key claim

The factorization of the double nuclear modification factor into the product of two single nuclear modification factors holds reasonably well at moderately high transverse momenta, provided that double parton scattering effects are small or negligible; however, factorization violation becomes significant at very high transverse momenta, where the prefactor \(F\) can reach values around 2.2.

## Method

The authors used the HYDJET++ event generator to simulate central (0–5% centrality) Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 5.02\) TeV, including both jet quenching (via the PYQUEN partonic energy loss model) and nuclear shadowing. They computed single and double nuclear modification factors for pions, kaons, and (anti-)protons in the mid-rapidity region \(|\eta| < 1\) for two transverse momentum thresholds (5 and 12 GeV/\(c\)), and extracted the prefactor \(F\) defined as the ratio of the double nuclear modification factor to the product of the two single nuclear modification factors.

## Key numbers

* Single nuclear modification factor for pions with jet quenching only (Q) at \(p_{\mathrm{T}} > 5\) GeV/\(c\): \(R_{\mathrm{AA}}^{\pi} = 0.34\); with shadowing only (S): 0.71; with both effects (QS): 0.25.
* Single nuclear modification factor for pions with both effects (QS) at \(p_{\mathrm{T}} > 12\) GeV/\(c\): \(R_{\mathrm{AA}}^{\pi} = 0.24\).
* Double nuclear modification factor for \(\pi K\) pairs with both effects (QS) at \(p_{\mathrm{T}} > 5\) GeV/\(c\): \(R_{\mathrm{AA}}^{\pi K} = 0.07\); at \(p_{\mathrm{T}} > 12\) GeV/\(c\): 0.12.
* Prefactor \(F^{\pi K}\) with both effects (QS) at \(p_{\mathrm{T}} > 5\) GeV/\(c\): 1.11; at \(p_{\mathrm{T}} > 12\) GeV/\(c\): 2.15.
* Prefactor \(F^{K p}\) with both effects (QS) at \(p_{\mathrm{T}} > 5\) GeV/\(c\): 1.09; at \(p_{\mathrm{T}} > 12\) GeV/\(c\): 2.16.

## Context

This work extends the proposal of Baranov (2023) for a double nuclear modification factor as a probe of initial- and final-state effects, moving from a simplified scenario where only one particle loses energy to a more general case where both hard particles experience energy loss. The results support the factorization hypothesis at moderate transverse momenta but show clear violation at high \(p_{\mathrm{T}}\), suggesting that measurements of the double nuclear modification factor can provide additional constraints on jet quenching and parton energy-loss mechanisms in future LHC analyses.
