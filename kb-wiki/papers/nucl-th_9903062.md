# Low energy n+t scattering and the NN forces

> The Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations have been solved in configuration space for the four nucleon system. The n+t elastic cross section has been calculated using several realistic interactions and a phenomenological three-nucleon force. Special attention is devoted to the description of the observed resonant structure at $T_{lab}\approx 4$ MeV. In the present state of the calculations, the realistic NN interactions are unable to describe the energy region considered, i.e. n+t S- and P- waves. Whereas the inclusion of a three-nucleon force improves the zero energy region, the disagreement in the resonant peak remains. This failure contrasts with the results of a pure phenomenological model. The origin of this disagreement as well as the possible issues are discussed.

## Key claim

Modern realistic NN interactions (AV14, NIJM-II, REID-93), even when supplemented with a phenomenological three-nucleon force, fail to describe the n+t resonant structure near T_lab ≈ 4 MeV; the three-nucleon force improves only the zero-energy S-wave region but leaves the P-wave resonant peak essentially unchanged.

## Method

The Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations were solved in configuration space for the four-nucleon system, with the wavefunction expanded in tripolar harmonics using j-j coupling. The n+t elastic cross section was computed for S- and P-waves using the AV14, NIJM-II, and REID-93 NN potentials, and a phenomenological two-range three-nucleon force (W_r=500 MeV, W_a=174 MeV, μ=2.0 fm) was added to reproduce the experimental triton and ⁴He binding energies.

## Key numbers

- ⁴He binding energies (no Coulomb) with AV14: 23.34 MeV (N_c=5+5) and 23.81 MeV (N_c=15+9), compared to the Coulomb-corrected experimental value 29.0 MeV.
- n+t zero-energy cross section with AV14 alone: 193.7 fm²; with AV14+TNI: 167.5 fm² (experimental value 1.70±0.03 b = 170±3 fm²).
- n+t scattering lengths with AV14+TNI: a(0⁺)=3.99 fm, a(1⁺)=3.53 fm, coherent a_c=3.65 fm.
- At T_cm=3 MeV, AV14 P-wave phase shifts (degrees): 0⁻=21.2, 1⁻ (two eigenphases)=26.6 and 47.8, 2⁻=35.4.
- n+t elastic cross section at T_cm=3 MeV: experimental 2.40 b; MT I-III 2.40 b; AV14 (no TNI, no P-waves) 2.12 b; AV14 (TNI, no P-waves) 2.06 b; AV14 (TNI, all P-waves) 2.03 b.

## Context

This work extends the configuration-space Faddeev-Yakubovsky approach from the ⁴He bound state (previously studied by Kievsky et al. (1992) and others) to n+t continuum scattering. It contrasts with the success of the simple S-wave spin-dependent MT I-III potential (Ciesielski and Carbonell (1998)), which reproduces the resonant region almost perfectly. The results suggest that modern realistic NN potentials, even with three-nucleon forces, are fundamentally inadequate for the n+t resonant states, a conclusion independently reached by Fonseca (1998) for the nonlocal Bonn B potential. The work calls for further convergence checks and indicates that the effective interaction emerging from V_NN + V_NNN may need to be substantially different from current OBEP models.
