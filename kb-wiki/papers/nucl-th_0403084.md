# Predicted cross sections for photon-induced particle emission

> Cross sections for the photon-induced particle-emission reactions (gamma,n), (gamma,p), and (gamma,alpha) are given for all natural isotopes from Ti to Bi. The target nuclei are assumed to be in their ground states, except for 180Ta which is naturally occurring as the isomer 180mTa. The cross sections are calculated in a statistical model (Hauser-Feshbach) approach and covering an energy range from threshold up to 7.35 MeV above the threshold (14.7 MeV above threshold for (gamma,alpha) reactions). The results are intended to aid conception and analysis of experiments which can also be used to test the methods involved in predicting astrophysical reaction rates for nucleosynthesis.

# Predicted cross sections for photon-induced particle emission

## Key claim

The authors present calculated cross sections for (γ,n), (γ,p), and (γ,α) reactions on all natural isotopes from Ti to Bi using a global statistical model (Hauser-Feshbach) approach, but caution that these results are obtained with a global parametrization and that slightly higher accuracy could have been achieved by including more experimental information and fine-tuning to each nucleus individually. The cross sections are intended for targets in their ground states (except for 180mTa), meaning they represent laboratory cross sections rather than the stellar cross sections needed for astrophysical applications.

## Method

The cross sections were calculated using the global statistical model NON-SMOKER (rate set FRDM) as described in Rauscher and Thielemann (2000, 2001), employing a Hauser-Feshbach statistical model approach. For (γ,n) reactions, a fine energy grid with 5 keV step width was used from threshold up to 0.245 MeV above threshold, followed by 150 keV steps up to 7.35 MeV above threshold; for (γ,p) reactions, 150 keV steps were used up to 7.35 MeV above threshold; and for (γ,α) reactions, 300 keV steps were used up to 14.7 MeV above threshold. All targets were taken in their ground states except for 180Ta, which naturally occurs as the isomer 180mTa.

## Key numbers

- Energy range covered: from particle emission threshold up to 7.35 MeV above threshold for (γ,n) and (γ,p) reactions, and up to 14.7 MeV above threshold for (γ,α) reactions
- For (γ,n) cross sections: fine energy grid with step width of 5 keV from E0+0.005 MeV to E0+0.245 MeV, then 150 keV steps from E0+0.3 MeV to E0+7.35 MeV
- For (γ,p) cross sections: step width of 150 keV from E0+0.15 MeV to E0+7.35 MeV
- For (γ,α) cross sections: step width of 300 keV from E0+0.3 MeV to E0+14.7 MeV
- Example cross section for 56Fe(γ,n): 7.839×10−3 barn at 5 keV above the neutron emission threshold (E0 = 11.198 MeV)
- Example cross section for 48Ti(γ,n): 6.941×10−5 barn at 5 keV above threshold (E0 = 11.627 MeV)
- Example cross section for 54Fe(γ,n): 4.228×10−7 barn at 5 keV above threshold (E0 = 13.378 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous publications (Rauscher and Thielemann, 2000, 2001) which provided astrophysical reaction rates and stellar cross sections, by now presenting laboratory cross sections with targets in their ground states that can be directly compared to experiments using Bremsstrahlung radiation (Mohr et al., 2000; Babilon et al., 2001) or Compton back-scattered laser photons (Utsunomiya et al., 2003). The results enable testing of statistical model predictions for photon-induced reactions relevant to s-process branchings (Sonnabend et al., 2003) and hot nucleosynthesis scenarios including the r-, p-, and rp-processes, and can be used to derive capture cross sections for unstable, experimentally inaccessible targets.
