# Mirror Neutron Stars

> The fundamental nature of dark matter is entirely unknown. A compelling candidate is Twin Higgs mirror matter, invisible hidden-sector cousins of the Standard Model particles and forces. This predicts mirror neutron stars made entirely of mirror nuclear matter. We find their structure using realistic equations of state, robustly modified based on first-principle quantum chromodynamic calculations, for the first time. This allows us to predict their gravitational wave signals, demonstrating an impressive discovery potential and ability to probe dark sectors connected to the hierarchy problem.

## Key claim

Mirror neutron stars, composed entirely of mirror nuclear matter as predicted by the Twin Higgs mechanism, can have their structure determined using realistic equations of state modified from first-principle quantum chromodynamic calculations, but only within the specific benchmark where the mirror Higgs vacuum expectation value ratio f/v is between approximately 3 and 7, and the results depend mainly on the dark confinement scale.

## Method

The authors constructed a realistic equation of state for standard model neutron stars using a mean-field core model with neutrons, protons, electrons, and muons interacting via sigma, omega, and rho mesons, combined with a Baym-Pethick-Sutherland crust model. They then rescaled the particle masses and nucleon-meson couplings according to the Twin Higgs mirror matter benchmark, where mirror quarks and leptons are heavier than standard model fermions by a factor f/v between 3 and 7, and the mirror QCD confinement scale is given by a numerical fit to renormalization group running. The mirror neutron star equation of state was derived by applying these scalings to lattice QCD data at large pion masses, treating the ratio m_pi'^2 / Lambda_QCD'^2 as the relevant parameter.

## Key numbers

* For f/v = 3, the mirror confinement scale ratio Lambda_QCD'/Lambda_QCD is approximately 1.3; for f/v = 7, it is approximately 1.6
* The mirror pion mass is 2 times heavier than the standard model pion mass (140 MeV) for f/v = 3, and 4 times heavier for f/v = 10
* The standard model equation of state yields a maximum neutron star mass of M_max approximately 2.1 +/- 0.1 solar masses, consistent with millisecond pulsar observations
* The mirror Higgs vacuum expectation value ratio f/v is constrained to be greater than or approximately 3 from LHC Higgs coupling measurements, and less than approximately 10 to maintain the solution to the hierarchy problem

## Context

This work extends previous studies of exotic compact objects (reviewed by Cardoso 2019) and dark neutron stars (Maedan 2019, Narain 2006, Kouvaris 2015, Gresham 2018) by providing the first realistic treatment of mirror neutron stars arising from a dark QCD sector with different properties than standard model neutron stars. Unlike earlier work by Beradze (2019) which considered only exact Z2-symmetric mirror matter with identical properties to standard model neutron stars, this study uses the Twin Higgs benchmark model (Chacko 2005, 2016, 2018) to make concrete predictions for gravitational wave observables that could be detected by LIGO/Virgo using standard analysis techniques.
