# Neutron beta decay and the current determination of Vud

> Measurements of neutron beta decay observables required to determine $|V_{ud}|$ are reaching the 0.1% accuracy. In this paper we review the calculation of the decay rate of this process, discuss its relevant uncertainties, and obtain an expression that is precise at the $10^{-4}$ level. Our analysis clearly shows the necessity of measurements of $λ$, the ratio of axial/vector couplings. The current situation in neutron beta decay is that one cannot yet quote a single consistent value for $|V_{ud}|$ from it. We also discuss the region of parameter space in the $|V_{ud}|$--$λ$ plane where new physics effects should lie, if they contribute to neutron beta decay.

# Neutron beta decay and the current determination of Vud

## Key claim

The authors review the calculation of the neutron beta decay rate at the 10^{-4} level and conclude that, due to inconsistencies among the most precise measurements of λ (the ratio of axial-vector to vector couplings), one cannot yet quote a single consistent value for |V_{ud}| from neutron beta decay. The paper shows that the two subsets of λ measurements yield values of |V_{ud}| that differ by almost 4σ, and that neither set alone is fully consistent with CKM unitarity.

## Method

The authors compute the neutron beta decay rate including tree-level contributions from the leading form factors f₁(0) and g₁(0), and incorporate radiative corrections up to order α² in an additive (non-factorized) form. They evaluate the model-independent (Coulombic and bremsstrahlung) and model-dependent (short-distance electroweak) radiative corrections, using the renormalization group to resum leading logarithmic corrections. The resulting expression for the decay rate is then combined with experimental values of the neutron lifetime τ_n and the ratio λ = g₁(0)/f₁(0) to extract |V_{ud}|.

## Key numbers

- Neutron lifetime world average (1998 PDG): τ_n = (886.7 ± 1.9) s, with scale factor S = 1.2
- Updated neutron lifetime including a more recent measurement: τ_n = (885.9 ± 0.9) s
- PDG 1998 weighted average of λ from four most precise measurements: λ = 1.2670 ± 0.0035, with scale factor S = 1.9
- Updated average (PDG 1999) of the same four measurements: λ = 1.2687 ± 0.0016, but the two most precise values (λ_Y and λ_R) differ by 4.7 standard deviations from each other
- Radiative correction uncertainty from axial cutoff parameter: Δ = 0.0022 ± 0.0008
- Decay rate expression: R = 0.1897 |V_{ud}|² (1 + 3λ²) (1 + 0.0739 ± 0.0008) × 10⁻³ s⁻¹
- |V_{ud}| from λ_R (one subset): 0.9719 ± 0.0019 ± 0.0004
- |V_{ud}| from λ_{LYB} (other subset): 0.9790 ± 0.0016 ± 0.0004
- |V_{ud}| from superallowed Fermi transitions (SFT): 0.9740 ± 0.0010
- |V_{ud}| from unitarity of CKM matrix: 0.9756 ± 0.0005
- CKM unitarity check for λ_R: |V_{ud}|² + |V_{us}|² + |V_{ub}|² = 0.9928 ± 0.0031
- CKM unitarity check for λ_{LYB}: 1.0066 ± 0.0035
- CKM unitarity check for SFT: 0.9969 ± 0.0022

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of neutron beta decay by Marciano and Sirlin (1993) and Wilkinson (1982), providing an updated expression for the decay rate at 10⁻⁴ precision with radiative corrections taken in additive form rather than the factorized form commonly used in the literature (e.g., Wilkinson 1982, Towner 1992). The paper highlights that the current experimental situation for λ is inconsistent, with measurements clustering into two separate Gaussian distributions as shown in the PDG 1998 ideogram. The authors identify that resolving this discrepancy requires new, independent measurements of λ, and that once λ is better determined, neutron beta decay can provide a determination of |V_{ud}| competitive with the superallowed Fermi transition result from Towner (1992) and Marciano and Sirlin (1993). The analysis also enables searches for new physics beyond the Standard Model by defining the allowed region in the |V_{ud}|–λ plane.
