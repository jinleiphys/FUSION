# Continuum limit of a qubit-regularized SU(3) lattice gauge theory with glueballs

> We show that a simple qubit-regularized $\mathrm{SU}(3)$ lattice gauge theory (LGT) on a plaquette chain admits a continuum limit with massive glueball excitations, providing a minimal toy model of strong interactions without quarks. By mapping the plaquette-chain Hamiltonian to the three-state quantum clock model in a magnetic field, we demonstrate that the theory can be tuned to a continuum limit governed at short distances by the $\mathbb{Z}_3$ parafermion conformal field theory (CFT), which serves as the ultraviolet (UV) fixed point. A small relevant magnetic perturbation then drives the system to a massive continuum quantum field theory in the infrared (IR). The resulting relativistic massive particles can be interpreted as quasi one-dimensional analogues of glueballs. In the continuum theory we compute the ratio of the lowest glueball masses with opposite charge conjugation to be $m^{-}/m^{+} = \,1.459(2)$ and find $\sqrtσ/m^{+}\,= 0.2648(2)$, where $σ$ is the string tension between a static quark and antiquark.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a qubit-regularized SU(3) lattice gauge theory on a plaquette chain can be tuned to a continuum limit, but only in the specific sector where the magnetic perturbation parameter \(h\) (equivalently \(\kappa_c\)) is small and positive, and the system is at the critical point \(J=g=1\) of the equivalent three-state quantum clock model.

## Method

The authors map the SU(3) plaquette-chain Hamiltonian, restricted to the charge-conjugation-even vacuum sector, onto a one-dimensional three-state quantum clock model with nearest-neighbor coupling \(J\), transverse field \(g\), and magnetic field \(h\). Using density matrix renormalization group (DMRG) calculations on lattices of size \(L=32,48,64,80,96\), they compute the low-energy spectrum at the critical point \(J=g=1\) for various values of the scaling variable \(\mu = h^{15/28} L\). They extract the masses of the lightest charge-conjugation-even and charge-conjugation-odd particles, and compute the string tension from the static quark-antiquark potential by modifying the Hilbert space sector for plaquettes between the quark and antiquark.

## Key numbers

* Mass ratio of the lowest glueball masses with opposite charge conjugation: \(m^{-}/m^{+} = 1.459(2)\), extracted from the infrared regime at \(\mu = 12.0\).
* Ratio of the square root of the string tension to the lightest even-parity glueball mass: \(\sqrt{\sigma}/m^{+} = 0.2648(2)\), obtained at \(\mu = 12.0\).
* The dispersion relation constant \(\zeta\) (playing the role of \(\hbar c\) converting inverse lattice spacing to energy units) is \(\zeta = 2.5907(2)\), extracted from a linear fit to 87 data points with \(48 \leq L \leq 96\) and \(10.0 \leq \mu \leq 14.0\).
* The string tension extracted from the static quark potential at \(L=96\) and \(\mu=14.0\) is \(\bar{\sigma} = 0.009500(3)\) (energy per unit length in lattice units).

## Context

This work extends the approach of qubit-regularized lattice gauge theories proposed by Chandrasekharan (2025) and applies it to SU(3) for the first time, following a similar massive continuum limit recently identified in a qubit-regularized SU(2) theory by Siew (2025). The plaquette-chain geometry was previously used by Pradhan (2022), Yao (2023), and others for quantum computing studies of lattice gauge theories, while the specific SU(3) Hilbert space was explored by earlier works (2021, 2025, Chen 2026). The mapping to the three-state quantum clock model and the identification of the \(\mathbb{Z}_3\) parafermion conformal field theory as the ultraviolet fixed point builds on Fateev (1985) and Lepori (2009). The results enable future searches for analogous confinement-deconfinement critical points in higher-dimensional qubit-regularized SU(3) gauge theories, which could potentially reproduce conventional Yang-Mills theory or an exotic variant.
