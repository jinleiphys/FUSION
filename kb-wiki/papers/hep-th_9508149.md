# Chaos Suppression in the SU(2) Yang--Mills--Higgs System

> We study the classical chaos--order transition in the spatially homogenous SU(2) Yang--Mills--Higgs system by using a quantal analog of Chirikov's resonance overlap criterion. We obtain an analytical estimation of the range of parameters for which there is chaos suppression.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the classical chaos–order transition in the spatially homogeneous SU(2) Yang–Mills–Higgs system can be predicted analytically using a quantal analog of Chirikov's resonance overlap criterion, yielding a specific threshold parameter for chaos suppression. The hedge is that this result applies only to the constant-field (spatially homogeneous) toy model, not to more realistic field configurations such as monopole or sphaleron solutions.

## Method

The author starts from the SU(2) Yang–Mills–Higgs Lagrangian in (2+1)-dimensional Minkowski space, imposes spatial homogeneity, and works in the gauge \(A_0^a=0\) with a real triplet Higgs field. After spontaneous symmetry breaking to a nonzero Higgs vacuum \(v\), the Hamiltonian reduces to two coupled anharmonic oscillators. The author introduces action–angle variables, performs resonant canonical perturbation theory to average over fast angles, and identifies a separatrix in the reduced one-degree-of-freedom system. Semiclassical Bohr–Sommerfeld quantization is then applied to obtain quantum multiplets, and the condition for overlap of separatrices between neighboring multiplets is solved analytically to yield the critical coupling for the onset of widespread chaos.

## Key numbers

- The chaos–order transition is governed by the parameter \(\lambda = v^3 g / E\), where \(v\) is the Higgs vacuum expectation value, \(g\) is the Yang–Mills coupling constant, and \(E\) is the energy of the system.
- If \(0 < \lambda < \sqrt{2}/32\) (approximately 0.0442), a relevant region of phase space is chaotic.
- If \(\lambda > \sqrt{2}/32\), the system becomes regular.
- The analytical result for the critical inverse coupling is \(1/v^2 = 16\omega / (m_1 \hbar)\), where \(\omega = (2 v^2 g^2)^{1/2}\) and \(m_1 \hbar \simeq E\).

## Context

This work extends earlier numerical studies of chaos in Yang–Mills–Higgs systems, particularly those by Matinyan et al. (1990) and Kawabe et al. (1993), which used Lyapunov exponents and Poincaré sections to demonstrate chaos. It also contrasts with the curvature criterion of potential energy used by some authors (e.g., Kawabe et al., 1993), which the paper notes can give incorrect results. The analytical criterion derived here enables future work on more realistic SU(2) Yang–Mills–Higgs solutions, such as the monopole and sphaleron configurations, where only numerical evidence of chaos–order transitions currently exists.
