# Dense Matter and Neutron Stars in Parity Doublet Models

> We investigate the properties of dense matter and neutron stars. In particular we discuss model calculations based on the parity doublet picture of hadronic chiral symmetry. In this ansatz the onset of chiral symmetry restoration is reflected by the degeneracy of baryons and their parity partners. In this approach we also incorporate quarks as degrees of freedom to be able to study hybrid stars.

## Key claim

The parity doublet model, which incorporates both baryons and their opposite-parity partners, can reproduce nuclear matter saturation properties and yields neutron star maximum masses around 2 solar masses when quark degrees of freedom are included, but the results depend strongly on the choice of the mass parameter \(m_0\) and the mass of the nucleonic parity partner \(M_{N-}\).

## Method

The authors construct SU(2) and SU(3) parity doublet models where baryons and their parity partners are arranged in doublets, allowing a chirally invariant mass term \(m_0\). They perform an exhaustive parameter scan constrained by nuclear saturation density \(\rho_0 \approx 0.15/\text{fm}^3\) and binding energy per nucleon \(B/A \approx 16\) MeV, then solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for neutron star sequences. For the SU(3) extension, they include quark degrees of freedom coupled to scalar condensates and the Polyakov loop field \(\Phi\) as an order parameter for deconfinement, with an excluded volume correction for hadrons.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density \(\rho_0\) constrained to about \(0.15 / \text{fm}^3\) and binding energy per nucleon \(B/A\) to around 16 MeV.
- Compressibility \(\kappa\) below 300 MeV requires a mass parameter \(m_0\) around 840 MeV (given \(M_{N-} = 1535\) MeV).
- For \(M_{N-} = 1200\) MeV, parity partners start to appear at about \(2\rho_0\) for n\(_-\) and \(3\rho_0\) for p\(_-\).
- Critical end-point values for the chiral transition: \((T, \mu) = (17, 903)\) MeV and \((58, 1200)\) MeV for the liquid-gas and chiral transitions, respectively.
- Purely hadronic model yields a maximum neutron star mass of about 1.6 solar masses with a radius of 12 km.
- Including quarks shifts the maximum mass to 1.96 solar masses with a reduced radius of 10.7 km, consistent with the \(1.97 \pm 0.4\) solar mass pulsar PSR J1614-2230.

## Context

This work extends earlier parity doublet formulations by Montvay (1987) and DeTar and Kunihiro (1989), and builds on prior SU(2) and SU(3) hadronic models by Dexheimer et al. (2008, 2008a) and Zschiesche et al. (2006). It enables the study of hybrid stars with quark cores and connects chiral symmetry restoration to the degeneracy of baryon parity partners, providing a framework to interpret neutron star observations such as the massive pulsar PSR J1614-2230.
