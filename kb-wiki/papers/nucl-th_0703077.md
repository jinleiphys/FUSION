# Phase transitions in isolated strongly interacting systems

> Thermodynamical properties of the nuclear matter at sub-saturation densities were investigated using a simple van der Waals-like equation of state with an additional term representing the symmetry energy. First-order isospin-asymmetric liquid-gas phase transition appears restricted to isolated isospin-asymmetric systems while the symmetric systems will undergo fragmentation decay resembling the second-order phase transition. The density dependence of the symmetry energy scaling with the Fermi energy satisfactorily describes the symmetry energy at sub-saturation nuclear densities. The deconfinement-confinement phase transition from the quark-gluon plasma to the confined quark matter appears in the isolated systems continuous in energy density while discontinuous in quark density. A transitional state of the confined quark matter has a negative pressure and after hadronization an explosion scenario can take place which can offer explanation for the HBT puzzle as a signature of the phase transition.

# Phase transitions in isolated strongly interacting systems

## Key claim

First-order isospin-asymmetric liquid-gas phase transitions are restricted to isolated systems with large isospin asymmetry, while symmetric systems undergo fragmentation decay resembling a second-order phase transition; the deconfinement-confinement transition from quark-gluon plasma to confined quark matter can be continuous in energy density but discontinuous in quark density, with a negative-pressure transitional state that may explain the HBT puzzle.

## Method

The author adopts a modified van der Waals equation of state for nuclear matter at sub-saturation densities, with parameters chosen to reproduce saturation density (0.16 fm^-3), critical temperature (~14 MeV), and ground state energy (~-21 MeV), and adds a symmetry energy term with a ρ^(2/3) density dependence. For isolated systems, an enthalpy conservation rule is applied to model spinodal decomposition under adiabatic expansion, determining the vapor fraction and proton concentrations of coexisting liquid and gas phases. For the deconfinement-confinement transition, a confinement potential (string tension κ = 1 GeV/fm) is introduced into an ultrarelativistic quark gas equation of state, and isoenthalpic solutions are found numerically.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Critical temperature of symmetric nuclear matter: T_c ≈ 14 MeV
- Ground state energy: U_gs ≈ -21 MeV
- Equation of state parameters: a = -263.7 MeV fm³, b = 1674.94 MeV fm⁶
- Under adiabatic expansion, the critical temperature drops to slightly above 8 MeV
- Symmetry energy coefficient: a_a = 25 MeV
- For the deconfinement-confinement transition, initial QGP temperature T₀ = 170 MeV, string tension κ = 1000 MeV/fm
- Maximum value of the interaction parameter w = 1.862, occurring at quark density ρ = 0.921 fm⁻³
- Critical energy density for the deconfinement-confinement transition: approximately 1 GeV/fm³

## Context

This work extends the study of liquid-gas phase transitions in nuclear matter (Sauer, Müller) by explicitly treating the constraints of isolated (adiabatic) systems, in contrast to the standard Maxwell construction which assumes heat exchange. The results support the assumptions of the Statistical Model of Multifragmentation (SMM) and are consistent with experimental observations of correlated signals of isospin-asymmetric phase transitions (Veselsky, 2006) and limiting temperatures (JBNLimTmp). For the deconfinement-confinement transition, the work builds on lattice QCD predictions (QCDLatt) and interpolated equations of state (RandQGP), and proposes a "mini-inflation" scenario analogous to cosmological inflation to address the HBT puzzle observed at RHIC (HeinzRev, HBT). The approach enables a unified framework connecting sub-saturation nuclear matter phase transitions with the QCD phase transition in ultrarelativistic collisions.
