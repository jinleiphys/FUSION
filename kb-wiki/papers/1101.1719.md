# The helium trimer with soft-core potentials

> The helium trimer is studied using two- and three-body soft-core potentials. Realistic helium-helium potentials present an extremely strong short-range repulsion and support a single, very shallow, bound state. The description of systems with more than two helium atoms is difficult due to the very large cancellation between kinetic and potential energy. We analyze the possibility of describing the three helium system in the ultracold regime using a gaussian representation of a widely used realistic potential, the LM2M2 interaction. However, in order to describe correctly the trimer ground state a three-body force has to be added to the gaussian interaction. With this potential model the two bound states of the trimer and the low energy scattering helium-dimer phase shifts obtained with the LM2M2 potential are well reproduced.

# The helium trimer with soft-core potentials

## Key claim

The authors show that a simple two-body gaussian potential, which reproduces the low-energy He-He scattering properties of the realistic LM2M2 potential, can describe the helium trimer only after a repulsive short-range three-body force is added to correct the ground state binding energy; without this three-body force the gaussian potential overbinds the trimer ground state by about 24 mK.

## Method

The authors use the hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method to construct three-body wave functions for the helium trimer, solving the coupled hyperradial equations with box boundary conditions. They extract atom-dimer phase shifts using integral relations derived from the Kohn Variational Principle, which require only the internal part of the wave function and avoid computing the asymptotic behavior. A two-parameter gaussian three-body force is added to the two-body gaussian potential, with its strength adjusted to reproduce the LM2M2 trimer ground state energy for each of four chosen ranges.

## Key numbers

- The gaussian two-body potential (Eq. 5) gives a dimer binding energy of -1.2959 mK, scattering length 189.947 a.u., and effective range 13.846 a.u.; the LM2M2 values are -1.302 mK, 189.054 a.u., and 13.843 a.u.
- The trimer ground state energy with the gaussian potential alone is -150.0 mK, compared to -126.4 mK with the LM2M2 potential; the excited Efimov state is -2.467 mK with the gaussian potential versus -2.265 mK with LM2M2.
- The atom-dimer scattering length with the gaussian potential alone is 165.9 a.u., versus 217.3 a.u. with LM2M2; after adding the three-body force, the scattering length becomes 209-212 a.u. depending on the range chosen.
- The atom-dimer phase shift at an incident energy of 0.5 mK converges to -40.54 degrees when using a sufficiently large box (rho_max >= 2916.698 a.u.).
- The equivalence of the gaussian and LM2M2 two-body phase shifts holds up to approximately 0.2 K; above 0.4 K the phase shifts begin to differ.

## Context

This work extends the approach of using soft-core potentials for few-body systems, following the gaussian representation of the He-He interaction introduced by Nielsen et al. (1998). It directly compares with calculations using the realistic LM2M2 potential by Aziz et al. (1991) and with atom-dimer phase shift calculations by Motovilov et al. (2001), Roudnev et al. (2003), and Suno et al. (2008). The method enables future studies of larger helium clusters (up to 10 atoms, as in Lewerenz 1997) using soft-core potentials, which are computationally more tractable than hard-core potentials for systems with more than three atoms.
