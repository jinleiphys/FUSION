# $α$-$α$ Double Folding Cluster Potential Description of the $^{12}$C+$^{24}$Mg System

> We present a simultaneous analysis of the elastic scattering and fusion cross-section data of the $^{12}$C+$^{24}$Mg system around the Coulomb barrier and over energies by using the microscopic $α$-$α$ double folding cluster potential within the framework of the optical model and the coupled-channels formalism. The $α$-$α$ double folding cluster potential is obtained by using the $α$-cluster distribution densities of the nuclei in the usual double folding procedure. The microscopic potential results are compared with the findings of the phenomenological deep and shallow potentials. It is subsequently shown that only phenomenological deep real, microscopic nucleon-nucleon and $α$-$α$ double folding cluster potentials provide a consistent description of the angular distributions and fusion cross-section data simultaneously. The effect of the inclusion of the excited states of the target nucleus $^{24}$Mg on the fusion cross-section predictions is also determined by the coupled-channels calculations, which are shown to improve the agreement.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that only deep real potentials (phenomenological deep, microscopic nucleon-nucleon double folding, and α-α double folding cluster potentials) can simultaneously describe both elastic scattering angular distributions and fusion cross-section data for the ¹²C+²⁴Mg system, while shallow potentials fail to reproduce fusion data despite fitting elastic scattering well.

## Method

The authors performed optical model and coupled-channels calculations for the ¹²C+²⁴Mg system using four different real potentials: two microscopic (nucleon-nucleon double folding and α-α double folding cluster) and two phenomenological (deep Woods-Saxon squared and shallow Woods-Saxon). The α-α double folding cluster potential was constructed by folding an effective α-α interaction with α-cluster distribution densities derived from nuclear matter densities. Coupled-channels calculations included the 2⁺ and 4⁺ excited states of ²⁴Mg to improve fusion cross-section predictions.

## Key numbers

- The normalization factor for the α-α double folding cluster potential was set to N_R(α-α)=0.72, providing consistent description for all energies from 16.0 to 24.0 MeV.
- For the nucleon-nucleon double folding potential, the normalization factor was N_R(NN)=0.84.
- At E_Lab=20.0 MeV, the experimental fusion cross-section is 198.82 mb; the α-α DFC potential predicts 273.66 mb, NN-DF predicts 243.23 mb, deep Woods-Saxon squared predicts 236.11 mb, and shallow Woods-Saxon predicts 432.50 mb.
- At E_Lab=24.0 MeV, the experimental fusion cross-section is 435.01 mb; the α-α DFC potential predicts 553.70 mb, NN-DF predicts 518.43 mb, deep Woods-Saxon squared predicts 493.81 mb, and shallow Woods-Saxon predicts 791.99 mb.
- Including the 2⁺ and 4⁺ excited states of ²⁴Mg in coupled-channels calculations reduced the deep potential fusion prediction at E_Lab=20.0 MeV from 236.11 mb to 215.08 mb, an effect of 22.85 mb.
- The matter density root-mean-square radii used were 2.407 fm for ¹²C, 3.050 fm for ²⁴Mg, and 1.460 fm for ⁴He.

## Context

This work extends previous phenomenological analyses of the ¹²C+²⁴Mg system by Sciani (1997), Lepine-Szily (1993), and others, which failed to simultaneously explain elastic scattering and fusion data using shallow or deep optical potentials. It provides the first detailed microscopic folding model study of this system, demonstrating that deep potentials (including the α-α double folding cluster approach) are necessary for consistent description, while shallow potentials are inadequate. The results enable future applications of the α-α double folding cluster method to other 4n-type nuclear systems and validate the use of microscopic folding potentials over phenomenological shallow potentials for reactions around the Coulomb barrier.
