# Baryons as Relativistic Three-Quark Systems

> We discuss some recent developments in the description of baryons as three-quark systems within relativistic constituent quark models. In particular we address the issues of excitation spectra, electroweak structure, and mesonic resonance decays. The necessities of implementing simultaneously the symmetries of low-energy quantum chromodynamics and of relativistic invariance are emphasized.

## Key claim

The paper constructs the chiral effective Lagrangian for lattice QCD with Wilson fermions and for a mixed theory with Wilson sea fermions and Ginsparg-Wilson valence fermions, including terms of order \(a^2\) that had not been calculated before. The authors find that despite many operators entering the Symanzik action at this order, only three new terms appear in the chiral Lagrangian for the Wilson action and four for the mixed fermion theory, which is important for practical applications because every new operator comes with an undetermined low-energy constant.

## Method

The authors use Symanzik's effective theory to describe lattice QCD near the continuum limit, then extend the derivation of chiral perturbation theory from QCD to this effective theory with additional symmetry-breaking parameters. They perform a spurion analysis to construct the chiral Lagrangian, organizing the expansion in powers of the quark mass \(m\), the lattice spacing \(a\), and momentum \(p^2\), with the leading order containing terms of \(\mathcal{O}(p^2, m, a)\) and next-to-leading order containing terms of \(\mathcal{O}(p^4, p^2 m, p^2 a, m^2, m a, a^2)\). The calculation is performed for both the Wilson action and the mixed action theory, including the partially quenched extension.

## Key numbers

- The chiral Lagrangian at \(\mathcal{O}(a^2)\) for the Wilson action contains three new terms with coefficients \(W_6'\), \(W_7'\), and \(W_8'\), each proportional to \(\hat a^2\) where \(\hat a \equiv 2 W_0 a\)
- The mixed theory with Wilson sea and Ginsparg-Wilson valence fermions has four new \(\mathcal{O}(a^2)\) terms in the chiral Lagrangian
- At next-to-leading order, there are no \(\mathcal{O}(a^2)\) corrections to the valence-valence meson mass in the mixed theory due to the enhanced chiral symmetry of the valence sector
- O(4) symmetry breaking terms enter the Symanzik action at \(\mathcal{O}(a^2)\) but contribute to the chiral Lagrangian only at \(\mathcal{O}(a^2 p^4)\) or higher

## Context

This work extends the results of Rupak (2002) and Bar (2002) by calculating the chiral Lagrangian including \(\mathcal{O}(a^2)\) lattice effects, which were not computed in those earlier studies. The calculation is motivated by the fact that current unquenched simulations use lattice spacings that are not very small, so neglecting \(\mathcal{O}(a^2)\) contributions may not be justified, and because non-perturbatively improved Wilson fermions (where leading corrections are \(\mathcal{O}(a^2)\)) are becoming more common. The results enable more accurate chiral extrapolations of lattice QCD data by including discretization effects systematically, and the small number of new low-energy constants (three for Wilson, four for mixed action) makes practical applications feasible.
