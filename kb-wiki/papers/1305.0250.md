# Level densities of nickel isotopes: microscopic theory versus experiment

> We apply a spin-projection method to calculate microscopically the level densities of a family of nickel isotopes $^{59-64}$Ni using the shell model Monte Carlo approach in the complete $pfg_{9/2}$ shell. Accurate ground-state energies of the odd-mass nickel isotopes, required for the determination of excitation energies, are determined using the Green's function method recently introduced to circumvent the odd particle-number sign problem. Our results are in excellent agreement with recent measurements based on proton evaporation spectra and with level counting data at low excitation energies. We also compare our results with neutron resonance data, assuming equilibration of parity and a spin-cutoff model for the spin distribution at the neutron binding energy, and find good agreement with the exception of $^{63}$Ni.

# Level densities of nickel isotopes: microscopic theory versus experiment

## Key claim
The shell model Monte Carlo (SMMC) level densities for ⁵⁹⁻⁶⁴Ni agree well with experimental data from proton evaporation spectra and level counting, but the agreement with neutron resonance data depends on the assumption of parity equilibration and a spin-cutoff model; for ⁶³Ni the SMMC results disagree with neutron resonance data, though the proton evaporation data also differ from the neutron resonance data for this isotope.

## Method
The authors used the shell model Monte Carlo (SMMC) approach in the complete pfg₉/₂ shell to calculate level densities of ⁵⁹⁻⁶⁴Ni. For odd-mass isotopes, ground-state energies were determined using a Green's function method applied to the neighboring even-even nuclei to circumvent the odd particle-number sign problem, and level densities were obtained directly via spin projection onto M=0 (even-mass) or M=1/2 (odd-mass) states using the saddle-point approximation for the M-projected entropy and heat capacity.

## Context
This work extends the spin-projection SMMC method of Alhassid (2013), previously applied only to even-mass nuclei, to odd-mass nickel isotopes using the Green's function technique of Mukherjee (2012) for ground-state energies. The results are compared with experimental level densities from Voinov (2012) extracted from proton evaporation spectra, with level counting data from the RIPL-3 compilation, and with neutron resonance data from RIPL-3. The calculations enable microscopic predictions of level densities in mass and energy regimes relevant to stellar nucleosynthesis that are not yet experimentally accessible.
