# Constraining the equation of state with heavy quarks in the quasi-particle model of QCD matter

> In a quasi-particle model of QCD matter at finite temperature with thermal masses for quarks and gluons from hard thermal loops, the equation of state (EOS) can be described by an effective temperature dependence of the strong coupling $g(T)$. Assuming the same effective coupling between the exchanged gluon and thermal partons, the EOS can also be related to parton energy loss.} Based on the quasi-particle linear Boltzmann transport (QLBT) model coupled to a (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic model of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) evolution and a hybrid fragmentation-coalescence model for heavy quark hadronization, we perform a Bayesian analysis of the experimental data on $D$ meson suppression $R_{\rm AA}$ and anisotropy $v_2$ at RHIC and the LHC. We achieve a simultaneous constraint on the QGP EOS and the heavy quark transport coefficient, both consistent with the lattice QCD results.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that heavy flavor observables (D meson suppression and elliptic flow) can simultaneously constrain both the QGP equation of state and the heavy quark transport coefficient, with the extracted EOS consistent with lattice QCD results, but only under the assumption that the same effective coupling governs both the quasi-particle thermal masses and the parton energy loss.

## Method

The authors use the quasi-particle linear Boltzmann transport (QLBT) model coupled to (3+1)-dimensional CLVisc viscous hydrodynamics for QGP evolution and a hybrid fragmentation-coalescence model for heavy quark hadronization. They perform a Bayesian analysis with a Gaussian process emulator trained on over 800 parameter sets, using Markov Chain Monte Carlo to constrain six model parameters (a, b, c, d, A, B) that determine the temperature-dependent strong coupling g(T) and the heavy-quark-energy-dependent coupling g(E). The experimental data used are D meson R_AA and v_2 from Au-Au collisions at 200 AGeV (RHIC) and Pb-Pb collisions at 5.02 ATeV (LHC).

## Key numbers

* The transition temperature T_c is set to 150 MeV for comparison with Wuppertal-Budapest lattice data and 154 MeV for HotQCD lattice data.
* The specific shear viscosity used in the hydrodynamic evolution is eta/s = 0.08, with hydrodynamic starting time tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c.
* The Bayesian analysis uses 320,000 steps of random walks to approach equilibrium, then another 320,000 steps from which 32,000 parameter sets are drawn (every 10 steps).
* The integrated autocorrelation time tau_f for the six parameters ranges from 1.329 to 310.174 for T_c = 150 MeV and from 0.967 to 40.206 for T_c = 154 MeV.
* The spatial diffusion coefficient D_s of charm quarks is presented for heavy quark energy E = 10 GeV as a function of temperature.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Liu (2021) where g^2(T) was directly fitted from the lattice EOS, by instead constraining the QCD EOS from heavy flavor observables. It builds on prior Bayesian extractions of transport coefficients such as the JET Collaboration study (2013) for the jet quenching parameter and the Duke group Bayesian analysis (Bernhard, 2019) for shear viscosity. The results enable future combined Bayesian analyses of both hard and soft probes, potentially incorporating the phase transition temperature T_c as an additional parameter and including a broader range of jet observables and soft hadron spectra.
