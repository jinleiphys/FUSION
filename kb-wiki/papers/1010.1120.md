# Geometry of vanishing flow: a new probe to determine the in-medium nucleon nucleon cross-section

> We study the transverse flow throughout the mass range from $^{20}Ne+^{20}Ne$ to $^{131}Xe+^{131}Xe$ as a function of the impact parameter. We find that at smaller impact parameters the flow is negative while going through the impact parameter, transverse flow vanishes at a particular colliding geometry named GVF. We find that the mass dependence of GVF is insensitive to the equation of state and momentum dependent interactions whereas it is quite sensitive to the cross section. So it can act as a useful tool to pin down the nucleon nucleon cross section.

# Geometry of vanishing flow: a new probe to determine the in-medium nucleon nucleon cross-section

## Key claim

The mass dependence of the geometry of vanishing flow (GVF) follows a power law and is insensitive to the equation of state and momentum dependent interactions, but is quite sensitive to the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross-section; this sensitivity is demonstrated by comparing a constant 40 mb cross-section with the Cugnon energy-dependent parametrization (average strength about 32 mb in the Fermi energy range), where the power-law exponent nearly doubles.

## Method

The authors used the quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) model to simulate collisions of five symmetric systems from 20Ne+20Ne to 131Xe+131Xe at a fixed incident energy of 150 MeV/nucleon across the full range of impact parameters. They calculated the directed transverse momentum <p_x^dir> as a function of reduced impact parameter b/b_max and identified the colliding geometry at which this flow vanishes (GVF). The simulations were performed with hard, hard with momentum dependent interactions (HMD), and soft with MDI (SMD) equations of state, using both a constant isotropic cross-section of 40 mb and the Cugnon energy-dependent parametrization.

## Key numbers

- Power-law exponent tau for constant 40 mb cross-section: tau^Hard^40 = 0.24 ± 0.01, tau^HMD^40 = 0.23 ± 0.02, tau^SMD^40 = 0.25 ± 0.01
- Power-law exponent for Cugnon energy-dependent cross-section: tau^SMD^Cug = 0.44 ± 0.02
- Average strength of the Cugnon cross-section in the Fermi energy range is about 32 mb
- Incident energy for all simulations: 150 MeV/nucleon
- Mass range studied: from 20Ne+20Ne to 131Xe+131Xe (combined mass A from 40 to 262)
- GVF values range from approximately 0.5 to 0.8 in reduced impact parameter (b/b_max) across the mass range

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the energy of vanishing flow (EVF) by Krofcheck et al. (1989) and others, which used the disappearance of transverse flow as a function of beam energy to probe the equation of state. The authors introduce the geometry of vanishing flow (GVF) as a complementary observable that is sensitive to the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross-section rather than the equation of state, following earlier work by Sood and collaborators on the mass dependence of EVF. This enables future experimental determination of the in-medium cross-section by measuring the impact parameter dependence of transverse flow disappearance across different system masses.
