# Thermodynamic Properties of Strongly Interacting Matter in a Finite Volume using the Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model

> We present the thermodynamic properties of strongly interacting matter in finite volume in the framework of Polyakov loop enhanced Nambu$-$Jona-lasinio model within mean field approximation. We considered both the 2 flavor and 2+1 flavor matter. Our primary observation was a qualitative change in the phase transition properties that resulted in the lowering of the temperature corresponding to the critical end point. This would make it favorable for detection in heavy-ion experiments that intend to create high density matter with considerably small temperatures. We further demonstrate the possibility of obtaining chiral symmetry restoration even within the confined phase in finite volumes.

## Key claim

The authors find that reducing the system volume in the Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model lowers the temperature of the critical end point (CEP) and can even make the CEP disappear entirely for sufficiently small volumes, which they argue could make the CEP more accessible in heavy-ion collision experiments that produce small fireballs at low beam energies.

## Method

The authors study strongly interacting matter in a finite cubic volume using the PNJL model in the mean-field approximation for both 2-flavor and 2+1-flavor systems. Finite volume is implemented by introducing an infrared momentum cutoff λ = π/R (where R is the lateral size of the cube) in the momentum integrals, while neglecting surface and curvature effects and keeping the model parameters fixed at their infinite-volume values. The thermodynamic potential is minimized to obtain the temperature and chemical potential dependence of the chiral condensates and the Polyakov loop, and meson masses are computed within the random phase approximation.

## Key numbers

- For 2-flavor matter, the crossover temperature Tc varies from 167 MeV (R = 2 fm) to 186 MeV (R = ∞).
- For 2+1-flavor matter, Tc varies from 160 MeV (R = 2 fm) to 181 MeV (R = ∞).
- For 2-flavor matter at R = 2 fm, no critical end point exists; at R = 2.5 fm the CEP is at (Tc = 32 MeV, μq_c = 339 MeV); at R = ∞ the CEP is at (Tc = 72 MeV, μq_c = 329 MeV).
- For 2+1-flavor matter at R = 2 fm, no CEP exists; at R = 2.5 fm the CEP is at (Tc = 32 MeV, μq_c = 328 MeV); at R = ∞ the CEP is at (Tc = 93 MeV, μq_c = 313 MeV).
- At Tc for R = 2 fm, the scaled pressure P/T⁴ is approximately half that of the infinite-volume system.
- For R = 2 fm, the difference between the correct pressure (from volume derivative) and the approximation P = -Ω is about 10%.

## Context

This work extends earlier finite-volume studies using the NJL model (Kiriyama and collaborators, Shao and collaborators) and the PNJL model on the lattice (Cristo and collaborators) by providing a systematic mean-field treatment of thermodynamic quantities and the phase diagram. The results enable finite-size scaling analyses to extrapolate CEP locations from heavy-ion collision data to infinite-volume QCD, and suggest that the chiral symmetry restoration observed in finite volumes may occur even within the confined phase, a possibility the authors highlight for experimental detection.
