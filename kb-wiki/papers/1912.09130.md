# Systematical study of optical potential strengths in reactions involving strongly, weakly bound and exotic nuclei on $^{120}$Sn

> We present new experimental angular distributions for the elastic scattering of $^6$Li$+^{120}$Sn at three bombarding energies. We include these data in a wide systematic involving the elastic scattering of $^{4,6}$He, $^7$Li, $^9$Be, $^{10}$B and $^{16,18}$O projectiles on the same target at energies around the respective Coulomb barriers. Considering this data set, we report on optical model analyses based on the double-folding Sao Paulo Potential. Within this approach, we study the sensitivity of the data fit to different models for the nuclear matter densities and to variations in the optical potential strengths.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the double-folding São Paulo Potential (SPP), with a standard strong surface absorption (SSA) model using fixed parameters \(N_R=1\) and \(N_I=0.78\), provides an overall reasonable description of elastic scattering data for a wide range of projectiles on \(^{120}\)Sn, but the authors caution that the strong correlation between the real and imaginary strength parameters (\(N_R\) and \(N_I\)) introduces large uncertainties that make precise determination of these parameters ambiguous.

## Method

The authors performed new measurements of elastic scattering angular distributions for \(^{6}\)Li + \(^{120}\)Sn at three bombarding energies (19, 24, and 27 MeV) using the SATURN detector array at the University of São Paulo. They combined these with existing data for \(^{4,6}\)He, \(^{7}\)Li, \(^{9}\)Be, \(^{10}\)B, and \(^{16,18}\)O projectiles on the same target at energies around the respective Coulomb barriers. All data were analyzed within the optical model using the double-folding São Paulo Potential for the real part, with two models for the imaginary part: a strong surface absorption (SSA) model where the imaginary potential is proportional to the real potential with \(N_I=0.78\), and an only internal absorption (OIA) model using a Woods-Saxon shape with small diffuseness to simulate fusion absorption only.

## Key numbers

- New \(^{6}\)Li + \(^{120}\)Sn elastic scattering data at \(E_{\text{LAB}} = 19\), 24, and 27 MeV (three bombarding energies)
- S-wave barrier heights from SPP: \(^{4}\)He: 14.22 MeV, \(^{6}\)He: 12.78 MeV, \(^{6}\)Li: 19.76 MeV, \(^{7}\)Li: 19.45 MeV, \(^{9}\)Be: 25.78 MeV, \(^{10}\)B: 32.38 MeV, \(^{16}\)O: 50.79 MeV, \(^{18}\)O: 50.05 MeV
- Best fit for \(^{18}\)O at \(E_{\text{Red}} = 2.1\) MeV: \(N_R = 0.739\), \(N_I = 0.877\) with reduced chi-square \(\chi^2 = 5.80\)
- For the same \(^{18}\)O data, fixing \(N_R = 1\) gave optimum \(N_I = 0.616\) with \(\chi^2 = 15.54\)
- Standard SSA parameters from prior work: \(N_R = 1\) and \(N_I = 0.78\) (Alvarez 2003)
- \(^{6}\)He 2-neutron separation energy: \(S_{2n} = 0.98\) MeV
- \(^{6}\)Li break-up Q-value for \(\alpha + d\): \(-1.47\) MeV

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of elastic scattering on \(^{120}\)Sn previously reported by the same collaboration for \(^{7}\)Li (Zagatto 2017), \(^{9}\)Be (Arazi 2018), and \(^{10}\)B (Alvarez 2018, Gasques 2018), now including new \(^{6}\)Li data and a broader comparison across projectiles ranging from strongly bound (\(^{4}\)He, \(^{16,18}\)O) to weakly bound (\(^{6,7}\)Li, \(^{9}\)Be) and exotic (\(^{6}\)He) nuclei. The analysis enables future coupled-channel calculations to incorporate the effects of break-up and transfer channels, particularly for systems where the SSA model shows deviations, such as \(^{6}\)He at low energies where transfer/break-up channels are important (Appannababu 2019).
