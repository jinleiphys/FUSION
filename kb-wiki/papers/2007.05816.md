# Recoil Momentum Effects in Quantum Processes Induced by Twisted Photons

> We consider physical processes caused by the twisted photons for a wide range of energy scales, from optical (eV) to nuclear (MeV) to high-energy gamma-rays (TeV). We demonstrate that in order to satisfy angular momentum conservation, absorption of a twisted photon leads to a transverse recoil of the final particle or a system of particles leading to increased threshold energy required for the reaction to proceed. Modification of the threshold energy is predicted for (a) Photo-absorption on colds trapped ions of $^{40}$Ca, along with emerging new transverse-motion sidebands, (b) photo-disintegration of deuterium and (c) photo-production of electron-positron pairs in astrophysics environment.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that absorption of a twisted photon by a target located at a nonzero impact parameter from the vortex line imparts a transverse recoil momentum that increases the threshold energy required for the reaction, with the magnitude of the effect depending on the target's distance from the vortex center and on how much of the photon's angular momentum is absorbed into internal degrees of freedom versus center-of-mass motion.

## Method

The authors analyze the kinematics of twisted-photon absorption using angular momentum conservation, relating the transverse recoil momentum \(p_T\) to the angular momentum transferred to the target's center of mass via \(p_T = \langle \ell_z \rangle_{c.m.}/b\). They apply this formalism to three distinct physical systems: photo-absorption on cold trapped \(^{40}\)Ca\(^+\) ions, photo-disintegration of deuterium, and electron-positron pair production from high-energy gamma-ray collisions with optical background photons. For each case, they compute the modified threshold energy as a function of impact parameter and compare with plane-wave photon results.

## Key numbers

* For \(^{40}\)Ca\(^+\) absorbing a 397 nm photon in an \(S \to P\) transition, the longitudinal recoil energy is 0.13 neV; the transverse recoil energy equals the trap's 6.2 neV level spacing at an impact parameter \(b = 10\) nm.
* For deuteron photo-disintegration with binding energy \(E_B = 2224.52 \pm 0.2\) keV, the plane-wave recoil energy at threshold is \(E_B^2/2Mc^2 = 1.3\) keV; twisted photons with one extra unit of angular momentum double this recoil to 2.6 keV at \(b = \lambda/2\pi = 89\) fm.
* For \(\gamma\gamma \to e^+e^-\) with a 2.5 eV optical photon, the plane-wave threshold is \(\omega_1 \ge m_e^2/\omega_2 \approx 100\) GeV; the twisted-photon threshold increases by \(p_T^2/(4\omega_2)\) where \(p_T = \hbar(m_\gamma - \Lambda)/b\).
* For the \(S_{1/2} \to D_{5/2}\) transition in \(^{40}\)Ca\(^+\) with \(m_\gamma = -2\), approximately 1 in 15 transitions results in a jump to an excited trap state due to the superkick.

## Context

This work extends the concept of "superkicks" introduced by Barnett and Berry (2013) for twisted-light absorption, providing specific predictions for non-dipole transitions and for nuclear and astrophysical processes. It builds on earlier demonstrations of enhanced angular momentum selection rules in twisted-photon absorption by Afanasev et al. (2013) and experimental confirmations with cold trapped \(^{40}\)Ca ions by Schmiegelow et al. (2016) and Afanasev et al. (2018). The results suggest that twisted photons could serve as a new tool for nuclear structure studies, particularly for precision determination of the deuteron's \(D\)-state admixture, and offer an alternative explanation to beyond-Standard-Model physics for the observed transparency of the universe to very high energy gamma rays.
