# Low-energy enhancement in the magnetic dipole radiation of actinide nuclei

> We present the first theoretical results of the magnetic dipole (M1) $γ$-ray strength function ($γ$SF) for actinide nuclei within the shell-model Monte Carlo (SMMC) method. We observe a low-energy enhancement (LEE) in the M1 $γ$SFs of the six nuclei studied here, which serves as the first evidence, theoretical or experimental, that the LEE persists in the actinides. We also identify a scissors mode resonance in all six nuclei, which we compare with recent Oslo-method experiments.

# Low-energy enhancement in the magnetic dipole radiation of actinide nuclei

## Key claim
The authors present the first theoretical evidence that a low-energy enhancement (LEE) in the magnetic dipole gamma-ray strength function persists in actinide nuclei, based on shell-model Monte Carlo calculations. The claim is conditional on the use of the maximum entropy method for analytic continuation and the static-phase approximation as a prior, and the authors note that their calculations under-predict nuclear deformation, which may affect the scissors mode energy but not the LEE.

## Method
The authors used the shell-model Monte Carlo (SMMC) method to compute the imaginary-time M1 response function for six actinide nuclei at temperatures near their neutron separation energy. They then performed analytic continuation via the maximum entropy method (MEM) with a static-phase approximation (SPA) prior to extract the M1 strength function, which was converted into a de-excitation gamma-ray strength function using SMMC-calculated level densities. The valence space included the 82-126 shell plus the 1g9/2 orbital for protons and the 126-184 shell plus the 1h11/2 orbital for neutrons, with effective residual interactions including monopole pairing and multipole terms.

## Key numbers
- Six actinide nuclei were studied: 232Th, 237U, 238U, 239U, 240Pu, and 243Pu.
- The LEE was fitted to an exponential form f^{LEE}_{M1}(E_gamma) = C0 * exp(-kappa * E_gamma); C0 values ranged from 1.19(7) x 10^-7 MeV^-3 for 243Pu to 1.48(1) x 10^-7 MeV^-3 for 232Th.
- The slope parameter kappa ranged from 1.66(18) MeV^-1 for 237U to 2.38(29) MeV^-1 for 232Th.
- The scissors mode resonance was fitted to a Lorentzian; the resonance center omega ranged from 1.02(7) MeV for 232Th to 1.32(5) MeV for 239U.
- Integrated B(M1) strengths for the scissors mode ranged from 10.7(25) mu_N^2 for 232Th to 22.2(30) mu_N^2 for 243Pu; experimental comparisons gave 9.5(26) mu_N^2 for 232Th and 10.1(15) mu_N^2 for 243Pu.
- A spin-flip mode was observed at approximately 3.5 MeV in all six nuclei.

## Context
This work extends previous SMMC calculations of M1 strength functions in lanthanide nuclei (Fanto 2024, Mercenne 2024, DeMartini 2025) to the actinide region for the first time. It provides theoretical predictions that can be compared with Oslo-method experiments on actinides (Guttormsen 2014, Laplace 2016, Zeiser 2019), which have been limited to gamma-ray energies above approximately 1 MeV and thus cannot directly observe the LEE. The results suggest that the LEE may significantly affect neutron-capture rates in r-process nucleosynthesis and nuclear reactor applications, as previously proposed by Larsen (2010). The authors note that their calculations show a single scissors mode peak rather than the double peak observed experimentally, which they attribute to under-predicted deformation in their model space.
