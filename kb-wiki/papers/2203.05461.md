# Binary neutron star mergers as a probe of quark-hadron crossover equations of state

> It is anticipated that the gravitational radiation detected in future gravitational wave (GW) detectors from binary neutron star (NS) mergers can probe the high-density equation of state (EOS). We perform the first simulations of binary NS mergers which adopt various parametrizations of the quark-hadron crossover (QHC) EOS. These are constructed from combinations of a hadronic EOS ($n_{b} < 2~n_0$) and a quark-matter EOS ($n_{b} >~5~n_0$), where $n_{b}$ and $n_0$ are the baryon number density and the nuclear saturation density, respectively. At the crossover densities ($2~ n_0 < n_{b} < 5~ n_0$) the QHC EOSs continuously soften, while remaining stiffer than hadronic and first-order phase transition EOSs, achieving the stiffness of strongly correlated quark matter. This enhanced stiffness leads to significantly longer lifetimes of the postmerger NS than that for a pure hadronic EOS. We find a dual nature of these EOSs such that their maximum chirp GW frequencies $f_{max}$ fall into the category of a soft EOS while the dominant peak frequencies ($f_{peak}$) of the postmerger stage fall in between that of a soft and stiff hadronic EOS. An observation of this kind of dual nature in the characteristic GW frequencies will provide crucial evidence for the existence of strongly interacting quark matter at the crossover densities for QCD.

# Binary neutron star mergers as a probe of quark-hadron crossover equations of state

## Key claim

The quark-hadron crossover (QHC) equations of state exhibit a dual nature in gravitational wave signals: the maximum chirp frequency $f_{max}$ falls into the category of a soft hadronic EOS, while the dominant postmerger peak frequency $f_{peak}$ falls between that of soft and stiff hadronic EOSs. This dual nature, if observed by next-generation gravitational wave detectors, could provide evidence for strongly interacting quark matter at crossover densities, but the conclusion depends on the specific hadronic EOS used for comparison and the numerical resolution employed.

## Method

The authors performed the first numerical relativity simulations of binary neutron star mergers using piecewise-polytropic parametrizations of three QHC19 equations of state (QHC19B, QHC19C, QHC19D), constructed from a hadronic EOS below $2~n_0$ and a quark-matter EOS above $5~n_0$ with continuous softening in the crossover region. The simulations were evolved using the Einstein Toolkit with the GRHydro code under the BSSN-NOK formalism, with initial data generated via LORENE for irrotational binaries at 45 km separation, using baryonic masses of $1.45$, $1.50$, and $1.55~\rm{M}_\odot$. Gravitational waves were extracted via the Newman-Penrose formalism, and the results were compared against the soft hadronic SLy EOS and the stiff hadronic GNH3 EOS.

## Key numbers

- QHC19 EOSs have tidal deformability $\Lambda < 800$ for $M_0 = 1.4~\rm{M}_\odot$, satisfying the LIGO-Virgo observational bound from GW170817
- The piecewise-polytropic fits to QHC19 EOSs have residuals $< 0.02$ and reproduce mass-radius relations within 2% deviation (up to 3% for QHC19D)
- Postmerger lifetimes for QHC19B with $M_B = 1.55~\rm{M}_\odot$ ($t_{BH} = 14.65$ ms) are longer than SLy with the same mass ($t_{BH} = 13.31$ ms) but shorter than GNH3 ($t_{BH} = 14.44$ ms)
- QHC19C with $M_B = 1.45~\rm{M}_\odot$ and $1.50~\rm{M}_\odot$ do not collapse to a black hole within the simulation time (marked with *), while QHC19D with $M_B = 1.55~\rm{M}_\odot$ also does not collapse
- $f_{max}$ values for QHC19B range from 1796 Hz ($M_B = 1.55~\rm{M}_\odot$) to 1898 Hz ($M_B = 1.45~\rm{M}_\odot$), closely aligned with SLy values (1849-1915 Hz) and significantly higher than GNH3 values (1504-1595 Hz)
- $f_{peak}$ values for QHC19B range from 3150 Hz ($M_B = 1.45~\rm{M}_\odot$) to 3336 Hz ($M_B = 1.50~\rm{M}_\odot$), falling between SLy (3332-3727 Hz) and GNH3 (2534-2736 Hz)
- Characteristic frequencies $f_{max}$ and $f_{peak}$ vary within $0.05$ kHz with resolution changes
- Initial central densities of QHC neutron stars lie in the range $2.95-3.15~n_0$

## Context

This work extends previous studies of quark matter in neutron star mergers that focused primarily on first-order phase transitions (Bauswein 2019, Blacker 2020, Weih 2020, Liebling 2021, Prakash 2021), which soften the EOS and produce an upward shift in $f_{peak}$. In contrast, the QHC EOSs produce a downward shift in $f_{peak}$ relative to soft hadronic EOSs, providing an opposite observational signature. The study uses the QHC19 EOS family developed by Baym (2019) and compares with the soft SLy EOS and stiff GNH3 EOS. A concurrent study by Huang (2022) using QHC19D and QHC19B with the Togashi hadronic EOS corroborates the lower $f_{peak}$ for QHC19D but finds higher $f_{peak}$ for QHC19B, a discrepancy attributed to the different stiffness of the hadronic EOS used. The results enable next-generation gravitational wave detectors such as the Einstein Telescope and Cosmic Explorer to potentially distinguish crossover transitions from first-order phase transitions and constrain nonperturbative QCD interactions at supranuclear densities.
