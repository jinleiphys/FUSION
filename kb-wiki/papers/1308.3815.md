# New determination of double-beta-decay properties in 48Ca: high-precision Q-value measurement and improved nuclear matrix element calculations

> We report a direct measurement of the Q-value of the neutrinoless double-beta-decay candidate 48Ca at the TITAN Penning-trap mass spectrometer, with the result that Q = 4267.98(32) keV. We measured the masses of both the mother and daughter nuclides, and in the latter case found a 1 keV deviation from the literature value. In addition to the Q-value, we also present results of a new calculation of the neutrinoless double-beta-decay nuclear matrix element of 48Ca. Using diagrammatic many-body perturbation theory to second order to account for physics outside the valence space, we constructed an effective shell-model double-beta-decay operator, which increased the nuclear matrix element by about 75% compared with that produced by the bare operator. The new Q-value and matrix element strengthen the case for a 48Ca double-beta-decay experiment.

## Key claim

The paper reports a direct measurement of the 48Ca neutrinoless double-beta-decay Q-value of 4267.98(32) keV, which resolves a discrepancy in the previously accepted 48Ti mass value; the nuclear matrix element calculation using diagrammatic many-body perturbation theory to second order yields a value about 75% larger than the bare operator, though the authors caution that large second-order contributions mean higher-order contributions could also be significant.

## Method

The Q-value was measured at the TITAN Penning-trap mass spectrometer using time-of-flight ion-cyclotron-resonance and Ramsey excitation techniques on 48Ca+, 48Ti+, and N18OO+ ions, with interleaved cyclotron-frequency measurements to obtain frequency ratios. For the nuclear matrix element, the authors applied diagrammatic many-body perturbation theory to second order in the chiral N3LO nucleon-nucleon interaction evolved to low momentum (Vlowk), constructing an effective shell-model double-beta-decay operator for the pf-shell valence space above a 40Ca core, then combined this operator with wave functions from the GXPF1A interaction.

## Key numbers

* Q-value of 48Ca neutrinoless double-beta decay: 4267.98(32) keV, measured directly at TITAN
* Mass excess of 48Ti: -48492.71(21) keV, a 2.2-sigma deviation from the Atomic Mass Evaluation 2012 value
* Mass excess of 48Ca: -44224.45(27) keV, confirming the LEBIT measurement
* Frequency ratio precision: 5 x 10^-9 for 48Ca+-48Ti+, 6 x 10^-9 for 48Ca+-N18OO+, and 5 x 10^-9 for 48Ti+-N18OO+
* Nuclear matrix element increase: approximately 75% larger than the bare operator, with an estimated additional 8% increase from moving beyond the closure approximation
* Final summed nuclear matrix element: 1.301 (dimensionless), from the full second-order calculation including folding

## Context

This work extends the approach previously applied to 76Ge and 82Se by Engel, Lin, and Holt (2009, 2013) to calculate effective shell-model double-beta-decay operators using many-body perturbation theory. The Q-value measurement resolves a discrepancy in the 48Ti mass value that was uncovered by the LEBIT collaboration, and the improved nuclear matrix element calculation represents progress toward fully ab-initio calculations for 48Ca, which occupies a unique position among double-beta-decay candidates due to its doubly magic nature and suitability for ab-initio methods. The results enable more reliable extraction of the effective Majorana neutrino mass from a future 48Ca double-beta-decay experiment.
