# Gross, intermediate and fine structure of nuclear giant resonances: Evidence for doorway states

> We review the phenomenon of fine structure of nuclear giant resonances and its relation to different resonance decay mechanisms. Wavelet analysis of the experimental spectra provides quantitative information on the fine structure in terms of characteristic scales. A comparable analysis of resonance strength distributions from microscopic approaches incorporating one or several of the resonance decay mechanisms allows conclusions on the source of the fine structure. For the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance (ISGQR), spreading through the first step of the doorway mechanism, i.e.\ coupling between one particle-one hole ($1p1h$) and two particle-two hole ($2p2h$) states is identified as the relevant mechanism. In heavy nuclei it is dominated by coupling to low-lying surface vibrations, while in lighter nuclei stochastic coupling becomes increasingly important. The fine structure observed for the isovector giant dipole resonance (IVGDR) arises mainly from the fragmentation of the $1p1h$ strength (Landau damping), although some indications for the relevance of the spreading width are also found.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the fine structure observed in nuclear giant resonances provides evidence for the doorway state mechanism of resonance decay, where the first coupling step between one particle-one hole (1p1h) and two particle-two hole (2p2h) states is the dominant source of the observed fragmentation, though the relative importance of different decay mechanisms varies between resonance types and across the nuclear chart.

## Method

The authors review experimental spectra of giant resonances measured with high energy resolution (tens of keV) using inelastic proton and electron scattering, then apply continuous wavelet transform analysis to extract quantitative characteristic energy scales from the fine structure fluctuations. They compare these experimental power spectra with wavelet analyses of theoretical strength distributions from microscopic models that incorporate different combinations of the resonance decay mechanisms (Landau damping, escape width, spreading width) to identify which mechanism dominates the fine structure for each resonance type.

## Key numbers

- Energy resolution of approximately 40 keV (FWHM) achieved at iThemba LABS for ISGQR measurements in 90Zr, compared to earlier resolution of about 1 MeV (FWHM) at TRIUMF
- ISGQR in 208Pb located in excitation energy region Ex = 8-12 MeV
- IVGDR centroid energy follows systematics E_C = 31.2 A^{-1/3} + 20.6 A^{-1/6} MeV
- Proton beam energy of 295 MeV used for 208Pb(p,p') IVGDR measurements at RCNP
- Proton beam energy of 200 MeV used for IVGDR survey across multiple nuclei at iThemba LABS

## Context

This work extends the long-standing theoretical doorway state picture of giant resonance damping developed by Bortignon, Bracco, and Broglia (1998) and earlier work by Mahaux and Weidenmuller (1969). It provides the first systematic experimental evidence across the nuclear chart that the fine structure of giant resonances is a global phenomenon, confirming predictions from transport theories by Cassing and Mosel (1990), Reinhard and Toepffer (1994), and Abe et al. (1996). The wavelet analysis method, identified as the most promising among several approaches compared by Shevchenko et al. (2008), enables quantitative comparison between experiment and microscopic models, opening the way to identify specific coupling mechanisms responsible for the spreading width in different nuclear systems.
