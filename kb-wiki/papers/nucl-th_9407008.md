# A transport theory of relativistic nucleon-nucleon collisions with confinement

> A transport theory is developed on the quark level to describe nucleon--nucleon collisions. We treat the strong interaction effectively by the Friedberg-Lee model both in its original and in its modified confining version. First we study the stability of the static three-dimensional semiclassical configuration, then we present results of the time evolution given by a Vlasov equation for the quarks coupled to a Klein-Gordon equation for the mean field. We find at higher energies that the nucleons are almost transparent, whereas at lower energies we observe a substantial interaction. At very low energies we see a fusion of our bags, which is due to the purely attractive nature of the mean field and hence is an artifact of our model. We test the confinement mechanism and find that at higher energies the nucleons are restored shortly after the collision.

## Key claim

The authors develop a semiclassical transport theory on the quark level using the Friedberg-Lee model to describe nucleon-nucleon collisions, but they acknowledge that the observed fusion of bags at very low bombarding energies (90 MeV in the laboratory frame) is an artifact of the purely attractive mean field and the neglect of repulsive forces in the model.

## Method

The authors couple a Vlasov equation for the quark phase-space distribution to a Klein-Gordon equation for the scalar mean field, using the test particle method to solve the equations of motion numerically. They initialize stable nucleon solitons via a local Thomas-Fermi distribution in the static limit, then boost two such nucleons toward each other in the center-of-mass frame and evolve the system in three spatial dimensions using a staggered leapfrog integration scheme.

## Key numbers

- The static soliton solution yields a nucleon mass of 1101 MeV for the original model (L) and 1140 MeV for the confining model (C), compared to the experimental value of 1087 MeV
- The root-mean-square radius is 0.67 fm for model L and 0.674 fm for model C, compared to the experimental value of 0.83 fm
- The gluon mass (energy of the sigma field) is 1434 MeV for model L and 1129 MeV for model C, compared to approximately 1500 MeV experimentally
- The monopole oscillation frequency of the compressed soliton corresponds to an excitation energy of 377 MeV, associated with the 1440 MeV Roper resonance
- In a central collision at 3.98 GeV bombarding energy, the quark density in the overlap zone reaches 2.5 per fm^3 compared to the initial 1.6 per fm^3 of a single nucleon
- The total energy loss after 10 fm/c in a 2.88 GeV collision amounts to 7 percent, attributed to numerical viscosity
- For low bombarding energies up to 0.2 GeV, bag fusion is observed; at 90 MeV laboratory energy the fused bag oscillates from 2.4 to 14.4 fm/c

## Context

This work extends the one-dimensional transport theory previously presented by the same group (Kaviani, Vetter, Biro, Mosel 1993) to three spatial dimensions, and is conceptually close to the transport equations derived by Zhang and Wilets based on the Nambu Jona-Lasinio model, though that work did not include confinement or perform dynamical simulations. The approach enables future extensions to include direct quark-quark collisions and pair production on top of the stable colliding nucleons described here, and the effective potential method allows determination of parameter regions where soliton solutions can exist, which Shenghua and Jiarong (1993) used to model phase transitions in a quark-gluon plasma background.
