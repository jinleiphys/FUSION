# On the absence of the Chiral Magnetic Effect in equilibrium QCD

> In this paper we investigate the chiral magnetic effect (CME): the generation of an electric current due to a homogeneous background magnetic field and a homogeneous chiral imbalance in QCD. We demonstrate that the leading coefficient describing the CME vanishes in equilibrium, both for free fermions as well as in full QCD. Our full QCD results are based on continuum extrapolated lattice simulations using dynamical staggered quarks with physical masses as well as quenched Wilson quarks. We show that it is crucial that a gauge invariant ultraviolet regularization is used to compute the CME and elaborate on why some of the existing in-equilibrium calculations of this effect gave a nonzero result. We stress that our findings imply the absence of a time-independent CME current flowing in equilibrium QCD, but do not concern the CME as an out-of-equilibrium, time-dependent effect.

## Key claim
The leading coefficient describing the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) vanishes in equilibrium QCD, both for free fermions and in full QCD, but this finding does not concern the CME as an out-of-equilibrium, time-dependent effect.

## Method
The authors used continuum-extrapolated lattice QCD simulations with dynamical staggered quarks at physical masses and quenched Wilson quarks, computing the first derivative of the vector current with respect to the chiral chemical potential at zero chemical potential and nonzero magnetic field. They also performed analytic calculations for free fermions using Pauli-Villars regularization, and compared results obtained with conserved versus non-conserved (local) vector currents on the lattice.

## Key numbers
- The CME conductivity coefficient \(C_{\text{CME}}\) is found to be zero in equilibrium, both analytically for free fermions and numerically in full QCD.
- For free fermions, the unregularized integral gave \(C_{\text{CME}} = 1/(2\pi^2)\), but Pauli-Villars regularization yields \(C_{\text{CME}} = 0\) due to cancellation among regulator fields.
- The renormalized axial susceptibility for non-interacting fermions in the massless limit is \(\chi_5/T^2 = 1/3\).
- Lattice simulations used magnetic field strengths quantized as \(eB = 6\pi N_b/(aN_s)^2\) with integer flux quantum \(N_b\).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous method for the chiral separation effect (CSE) conductivity (Brandt 2023) to the CME, and contradicts earlier equilibrium lattice studies (Yamamoto 2011) that reported a nonzero CME current using non-conserved local currents. The results confirm Bloch's theorem (Yamamoto 2015) which forbids conserved currents in equilibrium, and clarify that gauge-invariant regularization is essential for correct results. The findings enable future studies to focus on the out-of-equilibrium, time-dependent CME relevant for heavy-ion collisions and astrophysics.
