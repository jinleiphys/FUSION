# $Λ$-hypernuclear production in ($K^-_{\rm stop}, π$) reactions

> We report on calculation of the $Λ$ - hypernuclear production induced by the stopped $K^-$. The calculation was performed within the framework of the distorted wave impulse approximation and employed chirally motivated model for the microscopic description of the elementary $K^-$-nucleon process. The sensitivity of the calculation was tested with various wave functions of both the kaon in the initial state and the pion in the final state. Our results are closer to the experimental values then the results of previous calculations.

## Key claim

The authors report that their calculated capture rates for Λ-hypernuclear production in stopped kaon reactions are closer to experimental values than previous calculations, but they hedge that the ambiguities in input wave functions and other theoretical factors make it impossible to convincingly decide whether a deep or shallow kaon-nucleus potential is better in general.

## Method

The calculation was performed within the distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) using a chirally motivated model for the elementary K⁻-nucleon process. The T-matrix was evaluated as a product of the elementary t-matrix and an integral over the K⁻ wave function, pion wave function, and nucleus-to-hypernucleus transition density. The sensitivity of the capture rates was tested with various wave functions for both the kaon in the initial state and the pion in the final state, using three different kaon optical potentials ([Kχ], [Keff], [KDD]) and three pion potentials (π₀, πb, πc).

## Key numbers

- Elementary branching ratios at K⁻N threshold: R(K⁻n → π⁻Λ) = 10.72 and R(K⁻p → π⁰Λ) = 5.36
- Kaon optical potential depths at nuclear matter density: [Kχ] = 50 MeV, [Keff] = 80 MeV, [KDD] = 190 MeV
- Best χ² per data point (7.7) achieved with combination of [Kχ] kaon potential and (πb) pion potential
- χ² per data point for (π₀) with [KDD] was 79.3, while for (πb) with [KDD] it was 20.0
- χ² per data point for (π₀) with [KEM] was 206.7, while for (πb) with [KEM] it was 7.3

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by Gal and Klieb (1980s), Matsuyama and Yazaki (1980s), and Cieply et al. (2000s), which gave capture rates at least three times smaller than experimental values. The novelty is the microscopic description of the elementary process using a chirally motivated model rather than phenomenological approaches. The results are compared with experimental data for ¹²ΛC (Agnello, 2005), ¹²ΛB (Ahmed, 2004), and ¹⁶ΛO (Tamura, 1990), and the authors find that the shallow potential [Kχ] gives the best description, though ambiguities prevent a definitive conclusion about the depth of the K⁻-nucleus potential.
