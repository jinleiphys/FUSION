# Jet broadening and radiation in the early anisotropic plasma in heavy-ion collisions

> Measuring the energy loss of energetic jet partons may provide experimental opportunities to constrain the initial nonequilibrium stages in heavy-ion collisions, which requires theoretical predictions for the jet-medium potential. In this letter, we go beyond the usual harmonic approximation, extract this potential for the first time using QCD kinetic theory simulations, and provide a simple cutoff-independent small-distance form. We find significant differences to thermal media at early times in momentum and position space, as well as substantial angular dependence. Applying our results to the gluon splitting rates using a novel computational method reveals tensions in the current kinetic theory implementations for early times.

## Key claim

The authors extract the full anisotropic jet-medium interaction potential (collision kernel) from QCD kinetic theory simulations for the first time, going beyond the usual harmonic approximation that only uses the jet quenching parameter $\hat q$. They find that the commonly used isotropic approximation for the collision kernel in kinetic theory simulations leads to gluon splitting rates that differ by up to 300% from the actual nonequilibrium rates at early times, questioning the current treatment of inelastic splittings in these simulations.

## Method

The authors solve the Boltzmann equation for a gluon plasma using QCD kinetic theory with an anisotropic initial condition inspired by the Glasma stage, evolving the distribution function numerically. They compute the full elastic collision kernel $C(\mathbf{q}_\perp)$ from the elastic scattering rate using matrix elements with isotropic hard-thermal loop resummed propagators, then obtain the dipole cross section $C(\mathbf{b})$ via Fourier transform. The gluon splitting rate $\gamma$ for the process $g \to gg$ is calculated by solving an integral equation that incorporates the anisotropic collision kernel, using a novel computational method generalized from Aurenche (2002).

## Key numbers

- For the more realistic coupling $\lambda=10$, the dipole cross section at small impact parameter $b T_\varepsilon = 0.01$ remains smaller than its equilibrium value during the entire pre-hydrodynamic evolution, leading to a reduction of jet quenching compared to thermal equilibrium.
- The splitting rate from the angular-averaged kernel agrees with the full anisotropic rate within just 2% throughout the entire evolution.
- The splitting rate in the nonequilibrium medium is enhanced by up to 100% at low parton energy $p$ compared to the thermal rate.
- The approximated rate $\gamma^{T_\ast}$ using the isotropic collision kernel differs from the actual nonequilibrium rate by up to 300% at early times.
- The approximated equilibrium form $C^{\mathrm{eq}}_{\mathrm{appr}}$ overestimates the true thermal dipole cross section by more than 25% for the small $b$ region relevant for jet quenching.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of jet-medium interactions that focused on thermal or hydrodynamic media (Aurenche 2002, Arnold 2008, Caron-Huot 2010) or applied thermal forms for the collision kernel even in nonequilibrium plasmas (Andres 2020, Moore 2021, Schlichting 2021). It builds on recent computations of the time evolution of the jet quenching parameter $\hat q(\tau)$ in the early Glasma and kinetic theory stages (Ipp 2020, Boguslavski 2023, Boguslavski 2024) by providing the full momentum-dependent kernel beyond the harmonic approximation. The results enable improved modeling of jet energy loss during the initial pre-hydrodynamic stages of heavy-ion collisions and question the current treatment of inelastic splittings in QCD kinetic theory simulations (Kurkela 2015, Abraao York 2014), with implications for understanding jet quenching in small collision systems.
