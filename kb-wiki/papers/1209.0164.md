# Hadronic effects in low-energy QCD: inclusive tau lepton decay

> The inclusive tau lepton hadronic decay is studied within Dispersive approach to QCD. The significance of effects due to hadronization is convincingly demonstrated. The approach on hand proves to be capable of describing experimental data on tau lepton hadronic decay in vector and axial-vector channels. The vicinity of values of QCD scale parameter obtained in both channels bears witness to the self-consistency of developed approach.

# Hadronic effects in low-energy QCD: inclusive tau lepton decay

## Key claim
The Dispersive approach to QCD, which incorporates nonperturbative constraints from dispersion relations and retains a nonvanishing hadronic mass threshold, can simultaneously describe experimental data on inclusive tau lepton hadronic decay in both vector and axial-vector channels, yielding nearly identical values of the QCD scale parameter in both channels. The abstract glosses over the fact that the standard perturbative approach fails entirely for the axial-vector channel (no solution exists for any value of Lambda), while the Dispersive approach resolves this discrepancy.

## Method
The author computes the QCD contribution to the tau lepton hadronic decay ratio R_tau by evaluating the integral of the hadronic spectral function weighted by a kinematic factor, using the Adler function expressed through dispersion relations. The perturbative one-loop result is merged with nonperturbative constraints from dispersion relations, incorporating a smooth kinematic threshold at the hadronic production threshold mass m_VA and a spectral density that includes both a one-loop perturbative term and an intrinsically nonperturbative term proportional to Lambda^2/sigma. The resulting expression for Delta_QCD^V/A is compared to experimental spectral function data from ALEPH (2008) in both vector and axial-vector channels.

## Key numbers
- Experimental values: Delta_QCD^V_exp = 1.224 ± 0.050 (vector channel), Delta_QCD^A_exp = 0.748 ± 0.034 (axial-vector channel)
- In the perturbative approach (massless limit), the vector channel yields two possible solutions for the QCD scale parameter: Lambda = (434 +117 -127) MeV and Lambda = (1652 +21 -23) MeV; the axial-vector channel has no solution for any value of Lambda
- In the Dispersive approach, the QCD scale parameter is Lambda = (408 ± 30) MeV for the vector channel and Lambda = (437 ± 34) MeV for the axial-vector channel
- The hadronic threshold masses used are m_V^2 ≈ 0.075 GeV^2 (vector) and m_A^2 ≈ 0.300 GeV^2 (axial-vector)
- The tau lepton mass is M_tau ≈ 1.777 GeV

## Context
This work extends the Dispersive approach to QCD developed by Nesterenko and collaborators (2000-2008), which merges perturbative QCD with nonperturbative constraints from dispersion relations, going beyond the Analytic Perturbation Theory of Shirkov and Solovtsov (1997) by retaining a nonvanishing hadronic mass threshold. The approach resolves a long-standing failure of standard perturbation theory, which cannot describe the axial-vector channel of tau decay at all, and provides a self-consistent determination of the QCD scale parameter from both channels. This enables more reliable extractions of the strong coupling from tau decay data and tests of the Standard Model, as well as constraints on possible New Physics beyond the Standard Model.
