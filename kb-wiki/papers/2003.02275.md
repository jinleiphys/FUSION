# Soft Fragmentation on the Celestial Sphere

> We develop two approaches to the problem of soft fragmentation of hadrons in a gauge theory for high energy processes. The first approach directly adapts the standard resummation of the parton distribution function's anomalous dimension (that of twist-two local operators) in the forward scattering regime, using $k_T$-factorization and BFKL theory, to the case of the fragmentation function by exploiting the mapping between the dynamics of eikonal lines on transverse-plane to the celestial-sphere. Critically, to correctly resum the anomalous dimension of the fragmentation function under this mapping, one must pay careful attention to the role of regularization, despite the manifest collinear or infra-red finiteness of the BFKL equation. The anomalous dependence on energy in the celestial case, arising due to the mismatch of dimensionality between positions and angles, drives the differences between the space-like and time-like anomalous dimension of parton densities, even in a conformal theory. The second approach adapts an angular-ordered evolution equation, but working in 4-2$ε$ dimensions at all angles. The two approaches are united by demanding that the anomalous dimension in 4-2$ε$ dimensions for the parton distribution function determines the kernel for the angular-ordered evolution to all orders.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a direct connection between the theory governing soft fragmentation (time-like DGLAP anomalous dimension) and the BFKL theory used to resum the space-like anomalous dimension of parton distribution functions, by working consistently in 4-2ε dimensions and recognizing that regularization is critical despite the manifest finiteness of the BFKL equation.

## Method

The authors develop two approaches to soft fragmentation in gauge theories. The first adapts the standard BFKL resummation of the PDF anomalous dimension to fragmentation functions by exploiting a conformal mapping between eikonal line dynamics on the transverse plane and on the celestial sphere, paying careful attention to regularization in 4-2ε dimensions. The second approach constructs an angular-ordered evolution equation working in 4-2ε dimensions at all angles. The two approaches are united by demanding that the anomalous dimension in 4-2ε dimensions for the PDF determines the kernel for the angular-ordered evolution to all orders.

## Key numbers

- The leading-order beta function coefficient β₀ = 11/12 and next-to-leading β₁ = 17/24 for pure Yang-Mills theory (with C_A normalization).
- The space-like anomalous dimension has single logarithmic behavior: each additional power of α_s comes with at most one additional logarithm (pole in n one order higher).
- The time-like anomalous dimension has double logarithmic behavior: each additional power of α_s comes with two additional logarithms (pole two orders higher).
- The resummation power counting is α_s ∼ n ≪ 1 for space-like and α_s ∼ n² ≪ 1 for time-like anomalous dimensions.
- The angular-ordered DGLAP approach recovers results for the time-like anomalous dimension to N³LL order and the coefficient function to N²LL order in the minimal subtraction scheme.

## Context

This work extends the space-time reciprocity relations between space-like and time-like DGLAP anomalous dimensions established by Moch, Vogt, and Dokshitzer (2004-2006) to include resummation of coefficient functions. It builds on the conformal mapping between the BFKL/Balitsky-Kovchegov/JIMWLK equations for forward scattering and the Banfi-Marchesini-Smye equation for jet physics proposed by Hatta (2008) and Avsar (2009), with confirmation up to 2-loops in QCD and 3-loops in N=4 super Yang-Mills by Caron-Huot (2015-2016). The work enables a unified treatment of soft fragmentation and forward scattering physics, connecting the resummation techniques of Mueller (1981-1982) for fragmentation to the BFKL resummation of Catani (1993-1994) for DIS.
