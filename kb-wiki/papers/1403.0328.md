# Azimuthal anisotropies of reconstructed jets in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}$ = 2.76 TeV in a multiphase transport model

> Azimuthal anisotropies of reconstructed jets [$v_{n}^{jet} (n=2, 3)$] have been investigated in Pb+Pb collisions at the center of mass energy $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}$ = 2.76 TeV within a framework of a multiphase transport (AMPT) model. The $v_{2}^{jet}$ is in good agreement with the recent ATLAS data. However, the $v_{3}^{jet}$ shows a smaller magnitude than $v_{2}^{jet}$, and approaches zero at a larger transverse momentum. It is attributed to the path-length dependence in which the jet energy loss fraction depends on the azimuthal angles with respect to different orders of event planes. The ratio $v_{n}^{jet}/\varepsilon_{n}$ increases from peripheral to noncentral collisions, and $v_{n}^{jet}$ increases with the initial spatial asymmetry ($\varepsilon_{n}$) for a given centrality bin. These behaviors indicate that the $v_{n}^{jet}$ is produced by the strong interactions between jet and the partonic medium with different initial geometry shapes. Therefore, azimuthal anisotropies of reconstructed jet are proposed as a good probe to study the initial spatial fluctuations, which are expected to provide constraints on the path-length dependence of jet quenching models.

## Key claim

The AMPT model qualitatively reproduces the measured elliptic anisotropy \(v_{2}^{jet}\) of reconstructed jets in Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, but slightly overestimates its magnitude; the triangular anisotropy \(v_{3}^{jet}\) is predicted to be smaller than \(v_{2}^{jet}\) and to approach zero at higher jet transverse momentum.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT model with string melting and a partonic cross section of 1.5 mb to simulate Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}=2.76\) TeV, triggering a dijet of \(p_T\sim 40\) GeV/\(c\) in the initial HIJING-based condition. Jets were reconstructed with the anti-\(k_t\) algorithm (cone size \(R=0.2\)) and background subtraction matching ATLAS cuts, and the harmonic coefficients \(v_{2}^{jet}\) and \(v_{3}^{jet}\) were computed relative to the second- and third-order event planes defined from the initial parton spatial distribution. The evolution of jet \(v_n\) was tracked through the parton cascade, hadronization via coalescence, and hadronic rescattering stages.

## Key numbers

- \(v_{2}^{jet}\) from AMPT is compared to ATLAS data for jet \(p_T\) bins covering 45–160 GeV/\(c\) across centrality bins; the model slightly overestimates the experimental magnitudes.
- For the \(45 < p_T < 60\) GeV/\(c\) bin, \(v_{3}^{jet}\) is smaller than \(v_{2}^{jet}\) and tends to vanish with increasing jet \(p_T\).
- The jet energy loss fraction \(\Delta p_T / p_T\) is maximal at \(\Delta\phi \sim \pi/2\) relative to the second-order event plane and at \(\Delta\phi \sim \pi/3\) relative to the third-order event plane for the 20–30% centrality bin.
- The ratio \(v_{n}^{jet}/\varepsilon_n\) increases with \(N_{part}\) (number of participant nucleons) from peripheral to noncentral collisions for the \(45 < p_T < 60\) GeV/\(c\) bin.
- For a fixed centrality bin of 20–30%, \(v_{n}^{jet}\) increases with the initial spatial eccentricity \(\varepsilon_n\) for \(n=2\) and \(n=3\).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of jet quenching and elliptic flow of jets (ATLAS Collaboration, 2013; ATLAS Collaboration, 2012) by predicting the triangular anisotropy \(v_{3}^{jet}\) using the AMPT model, which had previously described \(\gamma\)-jet imbalance (Ma, 2013), dijet asymmetry (Ma, 2013), and jet fragmentation (Ma, 2013). The results show that jet \(v_n\) arises primarily from parton cascade interactions with the medium, with little contribution from hadronization or hadronic rescatterings, and that the conversion efficiency \(v_{n}^{jet}/\varepsilon_n\) increases with centrality, suggesting that jet azimuthal anisotropies can constrain the path-length dependence of jet quenching models.
