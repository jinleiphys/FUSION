# The balance functions in azimuthal angle is a measure of the transverse flow

> The charge or barion number balance function in the relative azimuthal angle of a pair of particles emitted in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions is studied. The pi+pi- and ppbar balance functions are computed using thermal models with two different set of parameters, corresponding to a large freeze-out temperature and a moderate transverse flow or a small temperature and a large transverse flow. The single particle spectra including pions from resonance decays are similar for the two scenarios, on the other hand the azimuthal balance function is very different and could serve as an independent measure of the transverse flow at the freeze-out.

## Key claim

The azimuthal balance function \(B^\phi(\delta\phi)\) for \(\pi^+\pi^-\) and \(p\bar{p}\) pairs is proposed as an observable that can distinguish between two different freeze-out scenarios (high temperature with moderate transverse flow vs. low temperature with large transverse flow) even when single-particle transverse mass spectra are nearly identical.

## Method

The \(\phi\)-balance function was computed using two thermal models: a single freeze-out model with \(T_f = 165\) MeV and average transverse flow \(\langle\beta\rangle = 0.5\), and a blast-wave parameterization with \(T_f = 90\) MeV and \(\langle\beta\rangle = 0.6\). For \(\pi^+\pi^-\) pairs, contributions from neutral resonance decays (\(K_S, \rho^0, \sigma, f_0\)) were included explicitly, while nonresonant pions were emitted isotropically in the local source frame; for \(p\bar{p}\) pairs only the nonresonant thermal emission mechanism was used. The particle momenta were boosted by the collective flow velocity of each source element.

## Key numbers

- Average transverse flow in the single freeze-out model: \(\langle\beta\rangle = 0.5\) at \(T_f = 165\) MeV.
- Average transverse flow in the blast-wave model: \(\langle\beta\rangle = 0.6\) at \(T_f = 90\) MeV.
- For pions at \(T_f = 90\) MeV, the average angle between a \(\pi^+\) and a \(\pi^-\) is \(54^\circ\) for pairs of 1 GeV total transverse momentum and \(24^\circ\) for 2 GeV total transverse momentum.
- For pions at \(T_f = 165\) MeV, the average angle between a \(\pi^+\) and a \(\pi^-\) is \(80^\circ\) for pairs of 1 GeV total transverse momentum and \(50^\circ\) for 2 GeV total transverse momentum.
- At \(T_f = 165\) MeV, 30% of pions come from resonance decays.

## Context

This work extends the concept of the balance function in rapidity, previously studied by Bass (2000) and Jeon and Koch (2000), to the azimuthal angle as a new probe of freeze-out conditions. The STAR Collaboration (2003) had observed narrow balance functions in rapidity for Au-Au collisions at RHIC, which thermal models could reproduce by varying transverse flow. The paper shows that the \(\phi\)-balance function can break the degeneracy between temperature and transverse flow that exists in single-particle spectra and HBT radii, enabling independent determination of freeze-out parameters.
