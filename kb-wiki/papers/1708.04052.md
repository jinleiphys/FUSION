# Quasi-elastic neutrino charged-current scattering off $^{12}$C: effects of the meson exchange currents and large nucleon axial mass

> The quasi-elastic scattering of muon neutrino and electrons on a carbon target are analyzed using the relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (RDWIA). We also evaluate the contribution of the two-particle and two-hole meson exchange current ($2p-2h$ MEC) to electroweak response functions. The nuclear model dependence of the (anti)neutrino cross sections is studied within the RDWIA+MEC approach and RDWIA model with the large nucleon axial mass. It is shown that the results for the squared momentum transfer distribution $dσ/dQ^2$ and for invariant mass of the final hadronic system distribution $dσ/dW$ obtained within these models are substantially different.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the enhanced cross sections observed in MiniBooNE CCQE data can be described either by including two-particle two-hole meson exchange current (2p-2h MEC) contributions with a standard axial mass of M_A = 1.03 GeV, or by using a large axial mass of M_A = 1.35 GeV in the impulse approximation alone, but these two approaches produce substantially different predictions for the distributions in squared momentum transfer (dσ/dQ²) and invariant mass of the final hadronic system (dσ/dW).

## Method

The authors performed relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) calculations for charged-current quasielastic scattering of muon neutrinos and electrons on ¹²C, using a dipole parametrization of the axial form factor. They evaluated the 2p-2h MEC contribution to electroweak response functions using parameterizations of exact MEC calculations performed within the relativistic Fermi gas model, and added these to the RDWIA results. They then compared the RDWIA+MEC approach (with M_A = 1.03 GeV) against the RDWIA model with a large axial mass (M_A = 1.35 GeV) for neutrino and antineutrino cross sections at an incoming neutrino energy of 2 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Axial mass values compared: M_A = 1.03 GeV (standard, used in RDWIA+MEC approach) versus M_A = 1.35 GeV (large axial mass, used in RDWIA-only approach)
- For dσ/dQ² at Q² < 0.2 (GeV/c)², the RDWIA+MEC model results are about two times larger than the RDWIA cross sections with M_A = 1.03 GeV
- For dσ/dQ² in the range 0.2 < Q² < 1 (GeV/c)², the ratio R(MEC) ≈ 1.4 for both neutrino and antineutrino scattering
- For dσ/dW, the ratio R(MEC) increases from R ≈ 1.1 in the region of the QE peak up to 2.6 for neutrino and 4.5 for antineutrino scattering at W = 1.15 GeV
- The R(M_A = 1.35) ratios for dσ/dQ² increase with Q² from R ≈ 1 at Q² ≈ 0.1 (GeV/c)² to 1.7 at Q² ≈ 1 (GeV/c)²
- The occupancy of independent particle shell-model orbitals of ¹²C equals on average 89%, with 11% attributed to short-range NN-correlations

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for understanding the apparent discrepancy between MiniBooNE CCQE data and predictions using the standard axial mass, following studies by Martini et al. (2010), Nieves et al. (2011), and Megias et al. (2015) that showed CCQE-like data can be explained by including 2p-2h MEC contributions. The RDWIA approach used here was previously developed for QE electron-nucleus scattering and tested against data by Kelly (1996), Fissum et al. (1997), and others, and was applied to neutrino-nucleus interactions by Meucci et al. (2004) and Butkevich et al. (2012-2015). The results enable neutrino oscillation experiments like NOvA, T2K, DUNE, and Hyper-Kamiokande to better understand how the two different effects (MEC contributions versus large axial mass) affect the energy dependence of CCQE cross sections and neutrino energy reconstruction.
