# Enhanced nuclear Schiff moment in stable and metastable nuclei

> Nuclei with static intrinsic octupole deformation or a soft octupole vibrational mode lead to strongly enhanced collective nuclear Schiff? moments. Interaction between electrons and these Schiff moments produce enhanced time reversal (T) and parity (P) violating electric dipole moments (EDM) in atoms and molecules. Corresponding experiments may be used to test CP-violation theories predicting T,P-violating nuclear forces and to search for axions. Nuclear octupole deformations are predicted in many short lived isotopes. This paper investigates octupole deformations in stable and very long lifetime nuclei such as 153Eu, 235U, 237Np and 227Ac, which can ease atomic experiments substantially. The estimates of the enhanced Schiff? moments, atomic electric dipole momentsand T, P-odd interaction constants in molecules containing these nuclei are presented.

# Enhanced nuclear Schiff moment in stable and metastable nuclei

## Key claim

Nuclei with static intrinsic octupole deformation or a soft octupole vibrational mode produce strongly enhanced collective nuclear Schiff moments, and this paper identifies stable and very long-lived nuclei (153Eu, 235U, 237Np, 227Ac) where such enhancement could enable practical atomic and molecular EDM experiments, though the estimates for 235U are less reliable because the soft octupole mode contribution has not yet been calculated using a microscopic approach.

## Method

The authors use an analytical formula for the Schiff moment derived from the collective octupole deformation model, scaling the numerical result for 225Ra (which has known deformation parameters and level spacing) to other nuclei using scaling factors that account for angular momentum, deformation parameters β₂ and β₃, atomic mass number Z and A, and the energy difference between opposite-parity levels. Experimental nuclear excitation spectra are examined to identify nuclei with parity doublets and rotational bands indicating octupole deformation or soft octupole vibrational modes, and the Schiff moments are then converted to atomic EDM estimates and molecular interaction constants using electron scaling factors.

## Key numbers

- 225Ra Schiff moment from numerical calculation: S = 300 × 10⁻⁸ e η fm³ (compared to analytical estimate of 280 × 10⁻⁸ e η fm³)
- 225Ra Schiff moment coefficients: a₀ = -2.6, a₁ = 12.9, a₂ = -6.9 (average of four model calculations)
- 225Ra level spacing: E(1/2⁻) - E(1/2⁺) = 55.2 keV; deformation parameters β₃ = 0.099, β₂ = 0.129
- 237Np Schiff moment: S(237Np, θ̄) ≈ 6 θ̄ e fm³, with K_S = 6.5 (6.5 times larger than 225Ra)
- 237Np level spacing: E(5/2⁻) - E(5/2⁺) = 59.5 keV; deformation parameters β₃ = 0.12, β₂ = 0.26
- 237Np atomic EDM: d_a ≈ -0.8 × 10⁻¹⁵ θ̄ |e| cm
- 153Eu Schiff moment: S(153Eu, θ̄) ≈ 3.7 θ̄ e fm³, with K_S = 3.7
- 153Eu level spacing: E(5/2⁻) - E(5/2⁺) = 97.4 keV; deformation parameters β₃ = 0.15, β₂ = 0.31
- 153Eu atomic EDM: d_a ≈ -0.8 × 10⁻¹⁶ θ̄ |e| cm
- 235U Schiff moment: S(235U, θ̄) ≈ 3 θ̄ e fm³, with K_S = 3
- 235U level spacing: E(7/2⁺) - E(7/2⁻) = 81.7 keV; deformation parameters β₃ = 0.1, β₂ = 0.25
- 235U atomic EDM: d_a ≈ -0.4 × 10⁻¹⁵ θ̄ |e| cm
- 227Ac Schiff moment: S(227Ac, θ̄) ≈ 10 θ̄ e fm³, with K_S = 10
- 227Ac level spacing: E(3/2⁺) - E(3/2⁻) = 27.37 keV; deformation parameters β₃ = 0.134, β₂ = 0.197
- 227Ac atomic EDM: d_a ≈ -1.0 × 10⁻¹⁵ θ̄ |e| cm
- Intrinsic dipole moments from half-lives: D(153Eu) = 0.12 e fm, D(237Np) = 0.013 e fm, D(227Ac) = 0.061 e fm, D(161Dy) = 0.072 e fm, D(155Gd) = 0.047 e fm
- Molecular energy shift for 237Np: 2 W_S S = 5 × 10⁷ θ̄ h Hz
- Molecular energy shift for 153Eu: 2 W_S S = 0.4 × 10⁷ θ̄ h Hz
- Molecular energy shift for 235U: 2 W_S S = 2 × 10⁷ θ̄ h Hz
- Molecular energy shift for 227Ac: 2 W_S S = 0.5 × 10⁷ θ̄ h Hz (from RaO scaling)

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Auerbach, Spevak, and collaborators who first calculated enhanced Schiff moments in octupole-deformed nuclei like 223Ra, 225Ra, 223Rn, 221Fr, 223Fr, 225Ac, and 229Pa, and builds on the Schiff moment calculations for spherical nuclei (199Hg, 129Xe, 203Tl, 205Tl) by Sushkov, Flambaum, and Khriplovich (1984-1986). The paper identifies stable and long-lived alternatives to the short-lived 225Ra (15-day half-life) currently used in EDM experiments at Argonne and elsewhere, enabling experiments with macroscopic quantities of material rather than limited numbers of unstable atoms. The results enable improved searches for CP-violation through the QCD θ-term, quark chromo-EDMs, and axion dark matter, with the molecular energy shifts already within reach of existing experimental sensitivity demonstrated in the 1991 TlF experiment.
