# Efficient computation of the N-th rank QED polarization tensor: Universal worldline structure of form factors

> We derived in arXiv:2206.04188 arXiv:2211.15712 a compact expression for the $N$-th rank QED polarization tensor $Π_{μ_1\cdots μ_N}(k_1,\cdots,k_N)$ in a $(0+1)$-dimensional worldline framework. This fully off-shell object, a function of $N$ external photon four-momenta, is a key ingredient in high-order computations of cusp anomalous dimensions and lepton anomalous magnetic moments. We demonstrate here that $Π_{μ_1\cdots μ_N}$ can further be expressed simply in terms of a small number of independent ``head" form factors (each representing $(N-1)!/2$ Feynman diagrams) which have a universal structure in terms of sums over fermion Green functions and (propertime derivative of) their boson worldline superpartners. This worldline representation bypasses explicit Wick contractions and avoids tensor reductions to scalar loop integrals à la Passarino and Veltman, order by order in perturbation theory. We give explicit expressions for the $4$-th and $6$-th rank head form factors and provide a computer script generalizing these results to arbitrary $N$ external photons. The multiplicity of heads, and their growth with $N$, can be understood in terms of orbits of the permutation group. We employ the Burnside-Cauchy-Frobenius lemma to show that it scales as $e^{N-1}/\sqrt{N}$ terms as opposed to the $e^{N-1} N!/\sqrt{N}$ terms in conventional perturbation theory. We reexpress worldline parameter integrals that define the $4$-th rank heads as Feynman parameter integrals to reproduce the seminal results by Karplus and Neuman for the on-shell light-by-light amplitude and extend these to the fully off-shell case in massless QED employing a tailored integration-by-parts procedure. In a follow-up paper, we will discuss the direct computation of worldline integrals, potentially providing a further $N!$ advantage relative to Feynman diagram computations at high orders in perturbation theory.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the N-th rank QED polarization tensor can be expressed in terms of a small number of independent "head" form factors with universal structure, reducing the factorial growth of Feynman diagrams from \(e^{N-1} N!/\sqrt{N}\) terms to \(e^{N-1}/\sqrt{N}\) terms, though this advantage is partially lost when matching to conventional Feynman parameter integrals for the on-shell light-by-light amplitude.

## Method

The authors start from a previously derived compact expression for the N-th rank QED polarization tensor in a (0+1)-dimensional worldline framework, where the tensor is expressed as a normalized worldline expectation value of a product of N charged electromagnetic worldline currents. They classify the tensor structure into head, shoulder, and tail form factors using Ward identities and permutation symmetries, with the latter two determined by the head contributions. Explicit expressions are given for the 4th and 6th rank head form factors, and a computer script is provided to generalize to arbitrary N, with the multiplicity of heads understood in terms of orbits of the permutation group using the Burnside-Cauchy-Frobenius lemma.

## Key numbers

- The number of independent head form factors scales as \(e^{N-1}/\sqrt{N}\) terms, compared to \(e^{N-1} N!/\sqrt{N}\) terms in conventional perturbation theory
- For N=4, the tensor reduces to 6 independent gauge invariant head form factors
- For N=6, there are 40 irreducible form factors
- Each head form factor represents \((N-1)!/2\) Feynman diagrams (for N>2)
- The four-loop QED cusp anomalous dimension computation involving the fourth-rank light-by-light tensor consists of roughly 500 master integrals (Bruser 2020)
- The QCD four-loop cusp anomalous dimension computation reduces to 294 master integrals (Agarwal 2021)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous worldline reformulation of QED (Feal, Tarasov, Venugopalan 2022) and provides a computational framework for high-order perturbative calculations that bypasses explicit Wick contractions and tensor reductions a la Passarino and Veltman. The results reproduce the seminal on-shell light-by-light amplitude by Karplus and Neuman (1950) and extend it to the fully off-shell case in massless QED. The formalism enables computation of high-order cusp anomalous dimensions and lepton anomalous magnetic moments, with a follow-up paper planned to discuss direct computation of worldline integrals that could provide a further N! advantage relative to Feynman diagram computations.
