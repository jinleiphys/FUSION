# Time Reversal Odd Fragmentation Functions in Semi-Inclusive Scattering of Polarized Leptons from Unpolarized Hadrons

> Semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering of polarized leptons off hadrons enables one to measure the antisymmetric part of the hadron tensor. For unpolarized hadrons this piece is odd under time reversal. In deep inelastic scattering it shows up as a $\langle \sin ϕ\rangle$ asymmetry for the produced hadrons. This asymmetry can be expressed as the product of a twist-three "hadron $\rightarrow$ quark" distribution function and a time reversal odd twist-two "quark $\rightarrow$ hadron" fragmentation function. This fragmentation function can only be measured for nonzero transverse momenta of the produced hadron.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a time-reversal-odd twist-two fragmentation function \(H_1^\perp\) can be measured in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering of polarized leptons from unpolarized hadrons, but only when the transverse momentum of the produced hadron is nonzero; the asymmetry appears at order \(1/Q\) as a \(\langle \sin\phi\rangle\) modulation proportional to the lepton helicity.

## Method

The authors perform a diagrammatic expansion in powers of \(1/Q\) (the twist expansion) for the process \(\ell + H \to \ell' + h + X\), using the Ellis-Furmanski-Petronzio approach. They separate the hadronic tensor into soft quark correlation functions \(\Phi\) (for the target-to-quark part) and \(\Delta\) (for the quark-to-hadron part), keeping all contributions up to order \(1/Q\). The Dirac structure of \(\Delta\) is analyzed without imposing time-reversal invariance because the final states are out-states, which allows the appearance of the time-reversal-odd function \(H_1^\perp\) at twist-two. The cross section is then expressed in terms of convolutions of distribution and fragmentation functions over transverse momenta, and the azimuthal asymmetries are extracted by contracting with the polarized lepton tensor.

## Key numbers

- The time-reversal-odd fragmentation function \(H_1^\perp\) contributes at order \(1/Q\) to the cross section, specifically in the term proportional to \(2y\sqrt{1-y}\,\lambda \sin\phi_h\, |\boldsymbol{P}_{h\perp}|/(zQ)\).
- The leading twist-two unpolarized fragmentation function \(D_1\) and distribution function \(f_1\) appear in the cross section multiplied by the factor \((y^2/2 + 1 - y)\).
- The \(\cos\phi_h\) asymmetry at order \(1/Q\) involves the combination \(2(2-y)\sqrt{1-y}\,\cos\phi_h\, |\boldsymbol{P}_{h\perp}|/(zQ)\) multiplied by terms containing \(f_1\), \(D_1\), \(D^\perp\), and \(f^\perp\).
- The Gaussian transverse momentum widths used in the illustrative model are \(R_H\) (for the target) and \(R_h\) (for the produced hadron), with the convolution integral yielding a factor \(\pi/(R_H^2 + R_h^2)\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of twist-three distribution functions by Jaffe and Ji (1992) and the diagrammatic expansion of Ellis, Furmanski, and Petronzio (1983) to include time-reversal-odd fragmentation functions. It enables the extraction of the chiral-odd function \(H_1^\perp\) from azimuthal asymmetries in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering without requiring a polarized target, using only the lepton beam helicity. The paper also connects to the earlier proposal by Collins (1993) for probing transversely polarized quarks via such fragmentation functions, and it provides a framework for future measurements at moderate \(Q^2\) with good particle identification and azimuthal resolution.
