# Investigation of the elliptic flow fluctuations of the identified particles using the A Multi-Phase Transport model

> A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model is used to study the elliptic flow fluctuations of identified particles using participant and spectator event planes. The elliptic flow measured using the first order spectator event plane is expected to give the elliptic flow relative to the true reaction plane which suppresses the flow fluctuations. However, the elliptic flow measured using the second-order participant plane is expected to capture the elliptic flow fluctuations. Our study shows that the first order spectator event plane could be used to study the elliptic flow fluctuations of the identified particles in the AMPT model. The elliptic flow fluctuations magnitude shows weak particle species dependence and transverse momentum dependence. Such observation will have important implications for understanding the source of the elliptic flow fluctuations.

## Key claim

The ratio of elliptic flow measured with the first-order spectator event plane to that measured with the second-order participant event plane (\(v^{\rm SP}_{2}/v^{\rm EP}_{2}\)) can serve as a proxy for elliptic flow fluctuations in the AMPT model, showing weak particle species dependence and weak transverse momentum dependence, which suggests that initial-state eccentricity fluctuations dominate the observed fluctuations.

## Method

The study uses the AMPT model with string-melting mechanism and hadronic cascade to simulate Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV. Elliptic flow is measured via two methods: (i) the event plane method using the second-order participant event plane (\(\Psi^{\rm EP}_{2}\)) reconstructed from particles at \(2.5 < |\eta| < 4.5\) and the first-order spectator event plane (\(\Psi^{\rm SP}_{1}\)) from spectator nucleon positions, and (ii) the multi-particle cumulant technique to extract \(v_2\{4\}\). Particles of interest are taken from \(|\eta| < 1\) and \(0.1 < p_T < 4.0\) GeV/\(c\), and the ratio \(v^{\rm SP}_{2}/v^{\rm EP}_{2}\) is compared to the fluctuation metric \(v_2\{4\}/v^{\rm EP}_{2}\).

## Key numbers

- For charged particles in central to peripheral collisions, the ratio \(v^{\rm SP}_{2}/v^{\rm EP}_{2}\) decreases from central to peripheral collisions, consistent with patterns expected from initial-state eccentricity fluctuations.
- The ratio \(v^{\rm SP}_{2}/v^{\rm EP}_{2}\) shows no \(p_T\) dependence for charged particles in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV from the AMPT model.
- For identified particles in 0–40% centrality, the ratio \(v^{\rm SP}_{2}/v^{\rm EP}_{2}\) shows weak sensitivity to increasing \(p_T\) and no particle species dependence.
- The elliptic flow fluctuations magnitude increases from central to mid-central collisions.

## Context

This work extends studies of elliptic flow fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, which are typically probed via the ratio \(v_2\{4\}/v_2\{2\}\) (Giacalone 2017, Alba 2017), but that approach suffers from limited statistical power for multi-strange hadrons. The proposed spectator-plane method offers an alternative validation scheme, and the authors suggest that similar experimental studies using the STAR detector’s ZDC and EPD could test the ICCING scenario (Martinez 2019), which predicts larger elliptic flow fluctuations for strange and multi-strange hadrons due to initial strangeness eccentricity.
