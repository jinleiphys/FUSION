# Total Born cross section of $e^+e^-$-pair production in relativistic ion collisions from differential equations

> We apply the differential equation method to the calculation of the total Born cross section of the process $Z_1Z_2\to Z_1Z_2e^+e^-$. We obtain explicit expression for the cross section exact in the relative velocity of the nuclei.

## Key claim

The authors obtain an explicit expression for the total Born cross section of electron-positron pair production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions that is exact in the relative velocity β of the nuclei, not just in the high-energy limit. The abstract's claim of "exact in the relative velocity" is qualified by the fact that the result is obtained within the Born approximation (assuming Z₁α, Z₂α ≪ 1) and in the limit of infinite nuclear masses.

## Method

Using the optical theorem, the total cross section is expressed as the sum of two cut three-loop Feynman diagrams. The authors perform integration-by-parts (IBP) reduction using the LiteRed package to obtain 8 master integrals in d = 4 − 2ε dimensions, then derive a differential equation system in the Lorentz factor γ. The system is transformed to ε-form using the algorithm of Lee (2014) and solved recursively in ε via harmonic polylogarithms (HPLs), with boundary conditions fixed from small-β asymptotics obtained by expansion by regions.

## Key numbers

- The total Born cross section for spinor particles is given by Eq. (4.1) in the paper, expressed in terms of polylogarithms Li₂, Li₃, and logarithms of β = v/c, with overall factor (Z₁α)²(Z₂α)²/(π m²).
- For γ ≫ 1 (high energy), the leading asymptotic cross section is σ = (Z₁α)²(Z₂α)²/(π m²) × { (28/27) L₀³ − (178/27) L₀² + (370/27 + 7π²/27) L₀ + 7ζ₃/9 − 13π²/54 − 116/9 + … }, where L₀ = ln(2γ). This reproduces the result of Racah (1937).
- For β ≪ 1 (low energy), the cross section scales as σ = 296 (Z₁α)²(Z₂α)² β⁸ / (55125 π m²) × (1 + 7708 β²/3663 + …), showing β⁸ suppression.
- For scalar charged particles, the low-energy asymptotics scales as σ_s ∝ β⁴, differently from the spinor case.
- The cross section differential in the energies of the produced electron and positron at β ≪ 1 is given by Eq. (4.4), with explicit polynomial dependence on ε₊, ε₋, and m.

## Context

This work extends the classic calculation of Racah (1937), who obtained the high-energy asymptotics of the Born cross section up to power-suppressed terms in 1/γ, to an exact expression valid at all relative velocities β. It contradicts the estimate σ ∝ β⁵ given by Khriplovich (2014) for the low-energy regime, finding instead β⁸ scaling. The result enables precise comparisons with experimental data from heavy-ion colliders such as RHIC and LHC, and provides a benchmark for assessing Coulomb corrections studied by Baltz and McLerran (1998), Ivanov, Schiller, and Serbo (1999), and others.
