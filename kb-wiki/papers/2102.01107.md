# Deuterium scattering experiments in CTEQ global QCD analyses: a comparative investigation

> Experimental measurements in deep-inelastic scattering and lepton-pair production on deuterium targets play an important role in the flavor separation of $u$ and $d$ (anti)quarks in global QCD analyses of the parton distribution functions (PDFs) of the nucleon. We investigate the impact of theoretical corrections accounting for the light-nuclear structure of the deuteron upon the fitted $u, d$-quark, gluon, and other PDFs in the CJ15 and CT18 families of next-to-leading order CTEQ global analyses. The investigation is done using the $L_2$ sensitivity statistical method, which provides a common metric to quantify the strength of experimental constraints on various PDFs and ratios of PDFs in the two distinct fitting frameworks. Using the $L_2$ sensitivity and other approaches, we examine the compatibility of deuteron data sets with other fitted experiments under varied implementations of the deuteron corrections. We find that freely-fitted deuteron corrections modify the PDF uncertainty at large momentum fractions and will be relevant for future PDFs affecting electroweak precision measurements.

## Key claim

The paper finds that theoretical corrections for the light-nuclear structure of the deuteron, when freely fitted, modify parton distribution function (PDF) uncertainties at large momentum fractions and will be relevant for future PDFs affecting electroweak precision measurements, though the specific impact depends on whether the corrections are fixed, free, or omitted in the CJ15 and CT18 global analysis frameworks.

## Method

The authors used the L2 sensitivity statistical method to quantify the strength of experimental constraints on PDFs and PDF ratios in the CJ15 and CT18 next-to-leading order CTEQ global analyses, comparing three scenarios for deuteron corrections: uncorrected (no nuclear effects), fixed (using the CJ15 central fit off-shell correction with the AV18 potential), and free (allowing off-shell parameters to vary). They examined the compatibility of deuteron data sets with other fitted experiments by analyzing the pulls and sensitivities for u, d, and gluon PDFs across a wide range of x and Q, and explored implications for weak mixing angle measurements.

- The CT18 analysis uses a kinematic cut of W^2 > 12.25 GeV^2, while the CJ15 analysis uses W^2 > 3 GeV^2, allowing CJ to include additional fixed-target DIS data at large x and low Q.

- The off-shell correction function is parametrized with a 3-parameter shape: delta f^N(x) = C (x - x0)(x - x1)(1 + x0 - x), with x1 fixed by the quark-number sum rule.
- The CT18Z NNLO analysis includes 1120 data points from HERA Run I+II DIS, while the CJ15 fit includes 1185 points from the same experiment.

## Context

This work extends the CTEQ-JLab (CJ) and CTEQ-TEA (CT) global QCD analyses by systematically comparing the impact of deuteron corrections on PDF extraction, building on prior studies by Accardi (2016) for CJ15 and Hou (2019) for CT18. The investigation uses the L2 sensitivity method developed by Hobbs (2019) as a fast approximation to Lagrange Multiplier scans, and it addresses tensions between proton and deuteron data sets previously noted in studies of valence PDF falloff exponents by Courtoy (2020). The results enable improved understanding of how deuteron corrections affect electroweak precision measurements such as the weak mixing angle extracted from Z boson production at the LHC.
