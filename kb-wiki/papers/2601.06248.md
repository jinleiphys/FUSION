# Hadronic Probes of Non-Standard Neutrino Interactions

> In this work, we study leptonic decays of hadrons as probes of light neutrinophilic scalars that mediate enhanced neutrino self-interactions. Such scalars can be emitted in processes involving neutrinos, turning two-body decays into three-body final states and producing characteristic spectral distortions. We compute these effects for charged pion decay and nuclear electron capture decay, including both on-shell and off-shell scalar emission, as well as the loop-induced renormalization required to cancel divergences. Using these results, we derive the projected sensitivity of PIONEER and assess the current and future reach of BeEST. The resulting low-energy spectral tails provide a characteristic signal for light neutrinophilic scalars, making upcoming hadron decay experiments powerful probes of light mediators of non-standard neutrino self-interactions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that light neutrinophilic scalars emitted in hadron decays produce low-energy spectral tails rather than sharp peaks, and that upcoming experiments like PIONEER and BeEST can probe couplings as small as λ ∼ 10⁻³ for scalar masses below about 100 MeV, provided that the apparent infrared divergences in the massless scalar limit are canceled by one-loop renormalization in a UV-complete model.

## Method

The authors computed the differential decay rates for charged pion decay π⁺ → ℓ⁺ ν φ and nuclear electron capture decay of ⁷Be, including both on-shell and off-shell scalar emission, and incorporated the one-loop renormalization of the weak charged current coupling required to cancel logarithmic divergences as m_φ → 0. They then performed a log-likelihood ratio test for the PIONEER experiment, assuming 10⁸ π → e events with 1 MeV binning, Gaussian energy resolution varying from 10% at 10 MeV to 1.5% at 70 MeV, and two benchmark levels of muon decay-in-flight background. For BeEST, they assessed current and future sensitivity using the measured kinetic energy spectrum of the recoiling nucleus in ⁷Be electron capture.

## Key numbers

- PIONEER projected 95% CL sensitivity reaches λ_{eℓ} ∼ 10⁻³ for m_φ ≲ 100 MeV (on-shell scalar production), and λ_{eℓ} ∼ 0.3 for m_φ ≳ 200 MeV (off-shell scalar production)
- The SM ratio R_{e/μ}^{SM} = (1.2352 ± 0.0001) × 10⁻⁴ for π⁺ → e⁺ν_e(γ) relative to π⁺ → μ⁺ν_μ(γ)
- PIENU measured R_{e/μ} = (1.2327 ± 0.0023) × 10⁻⁴, providing a current constraint at 2σ
- Current Higgs invisible branching ratio upper limit BR_h_inv < 0.107 translates to |λ_{αβ}| ≲ 0.3
- Z invisible decay width Γ(Z → inv.) = 499.3 ± 1.5 MeV constrains λ_{eℓ} ≲ 0.5 (flavor off-diagonal)
- PIENU upper bounds: Br(π⁺ → μ⁺ ν_μ ν ν̄) < 8.6 × 10⁻⁶ and Br(π⁺ → e⁺ ν_e ν ν̄) < 1.7 × 10⁻⁷
- SM irreducible backgrounds: Br(π⁺ → μ⁺ ν_μ ν ν̄) = 4 × 10⁻²⁰ and Br(π⁺ → e⁺ ν_e ν ν̄) = 1.7 × 10⁻¹⁸
- The one-loop correction to the charged current coupling depends on log(Λ²/m_φ²) with Λ = 500 GeV and ξ_UV = 1 in the triplet model

## Context

This work extends the search for non-standard neutrino self-interactions beyond sterile neutrino searches, where the two-body decay produces a peak; here the three-body scalar emission produces a low-energy tail that remains distinguishable even for very light scalars, unlike sterile neutrino searches that lose sensitivity at low masses. The authors reinterpret PIENU bounds (2021, 2020) on π → e ν X and π → e 3ν, and compare projected PIONEER sensitivity to existing constraints from invisible Higgs decays (ATLAS 2023), invisible Z decays, kaon decays (NA62 2021), tau lepton decays (Brdar 2020), and astrophysical neutrino constraints from IceCube (Esteban 2021). The results enable future searches at DUNE and the Forward Physics Facility to probe the parameter space favored by CMB analyses of enhanced neutrino self-interactions.
