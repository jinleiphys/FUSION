# Analytically solvable potentials for $γ$-unstable nuclei

> An analytical solution of the collective Bohr equation with a Coulomb-like and a Kratzer-like $γ-$unstable potential in quadrupole deformation space is presented. Eigenvalues and eigenfunctions are given in closed form and transition rates are calculated for the two cases. The corresponding SO(2,1)$\times$SO(5) algebraic structure is discussed.

## Key claim
The paper presents two analytically solvable potentials for the collective Bohr Hamiltonian under the condition of γ-instability: a Coulomb-like potential (−A/β) and a Kratzer-like potential (−2D(β₀/β − ½β₀²/β²)). The authors show that these potentials yield closed-form eigenvalues and eigenfunctions, but the solutions are restricted to negative energies and require the hypergeometric function to reduce to an associated Laguerre polynomial, which imposes the quantization condition that μ+1/2−k must be a negative integer.

## Method
The authors start from the Bohr Hamiltonian with a potential depending only on β (γ-unstable condition), separate variables following Wilets and Jean, and reduce the β-equation to Whittaker's standard form through appropriate substitutions. For both potentials, they obtain regular solutions as Whittaker's functions M_{k,μ}(x), which become associated Laguerre polynomials when the quantization condition is satisfied, yielding discrete eigenvalues. The algebraic structure is identified as SO(2,1)×SO(5), with the spectrum-generating algebra constructed from operators that satisfy su(1,1) commutation relations.

## Key numbers
- Coulomb-like spectrum: ε_{τ,ξ} = A²/4 / (τ+ξ+2)², where τ is the SO(5) quantum number and ξ = 0,1,2,...
- Kratzer-like spectrum: ε_{τ,ξ} = D²β₀² / (√((τ+3/2)² + Dβ₀²) + 1/2 + ξ)²
- For the Coulomb-like potential, the (4⁺,2⁺) doublet with n=2 has energy 1.35 on a scale where the ground state is 0 and the first excited state is 1
- The Coulomb-like spectrum has a threshold at 1.8 corresponding to infinite quantum number
- In the Kratzer-like limit D→∞ with fixed β₀=0.5, the ξ=0 band follows ε_τ = τ(τ+3)/4
- Selected B(E2) transition rates are normalized to B(E2;2₁,₀⁺→0₀,₀⁺)=100; for the Coulomb-like case, the 2₁,₁⁺→0₀,₁⁺ transition is very small compared to this reference

## Context
This work extends the class of analytically solvable γ-unstable potentials beyond the infinite square well used in the E(5) model of Iachello (2001) and the finite square well studied by Caprio. The authors note that their approach parallels the Davidson potential studied by Elliott and later by Rowe, which also displays the same SO(2,1)×SO(5) algebraic structure. The Kratzer-like potential interpolates between the Coulomb-like limit (B→0, corresponding to spherical shapes) and a β-rigid, γ-soft limit (D→∞ or β₀→∞), enabling description of shape evolution from spherical to quadrupole-deformed γ-unstable nuclei. The authors indicate that forthcoming work will extend these potentials to include γ-dependence favoring axial symmetry, analogous to the X(5) case of Iachello (2001).
