# Transport and hydrodynamic calculations of direct photons at FAIR

> The microscopic transport model UrQMD and a micro+macro hybrid model are used to calculate direct photon spectra from U+U-collisions at Elab = 35 AGeV as will be measured by the CBM-collaboration at FAIR. In the hybrid model, the intermediate high-density part of the nuclear interaction is described with ideal 3+1-dimensional hydrodynamics. Different equations of state of the matter created in the heavy-ion collisions are investigated and the resulting spectra of direct photons are predicted. The emission patterns of direct photons in space and time are discussed.

## Key claim
The microscopic transport model UrQMD and a hybrid model combining UrQMD with ideal 3+1-dimensional hydrodynamics predict direct photon spectra from U+U collisions at FAIR energies, with the caveat that the results depend strongly on the chosen equation of state and that the hybrid model forces local thermal equilibrium at the transition from transport to hydrodynamics regardless of the actual equilibration state.

## Method
The authors used the UrQMD transport model in pure cascade mode and in a hybrid mode where the high-density intermediate stage (3.7 to 11 fm after first scatterings) is replaced by ideal 3+1-dimensional hydrodynamics with different equations of state: a hadron-gas EoS, a chiral EoS with a rapid cross-over to deconfined matter, and a MIT bag model EoS with a first-order phase transition. Photon emission was treated perturbatively using hadronic channels (ππ→γρ and πρ→γπ) with cross-sections from Kapusta et al. (1991) and rates from Turbide et al. (2004), while QGP photon emission was included only in the hydrodynamic phase.

## Key numbers
- U+U collisions at Elab = 35 AGeV with impact parameter b < 5 fm and rapidity cut |y_c.m.| < 0.5
- Transition from transport to hydrodynamics occurs at t_start = 3.7 fm after first scatterings
- Critical energy densities for mapping from hydrodynamics back to transport: 5 ε0 for HG-EoS and BM-EoS, 7 ε0 for χ-EoS, where ε0 = 146 MeV/fm³ is nuclear ground state energy density
- The intermediate stage is defined as 3.7 < t < 11 fm, with the late stage after t = 11 fm
- Direct photon spectra show differences of up to an order of magnitude between different equations of state at transverse momenta around 1 GeV

## Context
This work extends previous direct photon calculations using UrQMD by Dumitru et al. (1998) and Bauchle and Bleicher (2010), and with HSD by Bratkovskaya et al. (2008). It provides predictions for the CBM experiment at FAIR, which will measure U+U collisions at 35 AGeV, and enables comparison between hadronic-only evolution and scenarios with a QGP phase transition, complementing hydrodynamic calculations by Kapusta et al. (1991), Srivastava and Sinha (2001), and Turbide et al. (2004).
