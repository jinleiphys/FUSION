# The Thomas-Fermi approximation for gauge theories

> An effective field approximation, similar to the atomic Thomas-Fermi approach, is proposed for studying non-Abelian gauge theories which includes finite-volume effects. As applications of the formalism the equation of state for an SU(2) gauge theory with massless fermions is obtained. The extensions to realistic situations are briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a Thomas-Fermi-like effective field approximation for non-Abelian gauge theories that includes finite-volume effects, but the approach is limited to static, spherically symmetric configurations and relies on lowest-order perturbation theory for the thermally-averaged non-Abelian charge densities, with systematic improvement described as straightforward but not carried out.

## Method

The authors partition a finite volume of quark-gluon plasma into subvolumes large enough for statistical mechanics but small enough that a background gauge field varies slowly across them. They compute the partition function for massless fermions and gluons inside each subvolume using perturbation theory, treating the background field as constant within each subvolume. The background field is then determined self-consistently by solving the spherically symmetric Yang-Mills equations with sources given by the thermally-averaged non-Abelian charge densities, subject to bag-model-like boundary conditions at the container surface.

## Key numbers

- The thermal wavelength is defined as \(\lambda = 1/(2\sqrt{3\mu^2 + (\pi k T)^2})\), setting the subvolume scale as \(dV \sim \lambda^3\).
- The fermionic pressure per unit volume is \(p_\psi = \frac{1}{6\pi^2} \left[ \frac{g^4 a_0^4}{16} + \frac{g^2 a_0^2}{8\lambda^2} + \mu^4 + 2(\pi k T \mu)^2 + \frac{7}{15}(\pi k T)^4 \right]\).
- The gluonic pressure per unit volume is approximately \(p_{\text{gluons}} \simeq (\pi k T)^4/(15\pi^2)\), with deviations exponentially suppressed because \(\beta p_0 \ge 4\pi^2\).
- The surface charge density at the boundary is \(\vartheta \simeq g^2 a_0^2/(16\pi)\) in the limit of large \(a_0\) and small \(\beta\) or \(\mu\).
- The boundary condition at the container radius \(R\) gives \(f(R) = (3\pi/2)(R/\lambda)\).

## Context

This work extends the Thomas-Fermi model of the atom (Thomas 1927, Fermi 1928) to non-Abelian gauge theories, providing an alternative to perturbative approaches that suffer from infrared singularities beyond fifth order (Linde 1979) and to hard-thermal-loop resummations (Braaten and Pisarski 1990). The formalism incorporates bag-model boundary conditions (Chodos et al. 1974) and chiral bag conditions (Nielsen and Wirzba 1987) to confine color and fermion number. The approach enables the study of finite-volume effects on the equation of state of a quark-gluon plasma, with potential extensions to SU(3) gauge theory with three massive fermion flavors.
