# Role of U$_{A}$(1) breaking term in dynamical chiral symmetry breaking of chiral effective theories

> The spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry is examined by chiral effective theories, such as the linear sigma model and the Nambu Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model. Indicating that sufficiently large contribution of the UA(1) anomaly can break chiral symmetry spontaneously, we discuss such anomaly driven symmetry breaking and its implication. We derive a mass relation among the SU(3) flavor singlet mesons, eta0 and sigma0, in the linear sigma model to be satisfied for the anomaly driven symmetry breaking in the chiral limit, and find that it is also supported in the NJL model. With the explicit breaking of chiral symmetry, we find that the chiral effective models reproducing the observed physical quantities suggest that the sigma0 meson regarded as the quantum fluctuation of the chiral condensate should have a mass smaller than an order of 800 MeV when the anomaly driven symmetry breaking takes place.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a sufficiently large contribution from the U_A(1) anomaly can drive spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking even when the conventional quadratic mass parameter μ² is positive, a scenario the abstract describes as "anomaly driven symmetry breaking", a hedge glossing over the fact that this requires the anomaly term to dominate over the standard quadratic term.

## Method

The authors analyze the linear sigma model and the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model for three quark flavors, both including a determinant-type U_A(1) breaking term. In the linear sigma model, they derive vacuum conditions and mass relations at tree level in the mean field approximation; in the NJL model, they solve gap equations for dynamical quark masses and compute meson masses via the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the ladder approximation, using a three-momentum cutoff regularization.

## Key numbers

- In the linear sigma model with explicit chiral symmetry breaking, using empirical meson masses (m_η′ = 958 MeV, m_η = 550 MeV, m_π = 140 MeV) and decay constants (f_π = 92.2 MeV, f_K = 110.4 MeV), the σ_0 meson mass must satisfy m_σ_0 < 840 MeV for μ² > 0 (anomaly driven case) and m_σ_0 > 840 MeV for μ² < 0 (standard case).
- In the chiral limit of the linear sigma model, the anomaly driven solution requires the inequality (1/9) m_η_0² < m_σ_0² < (1/3) m_η_0².
- In the NJL model, the cutoff is fixed at Λ = 602.3 MeV; the critical four-point coupling for standard chiral symmetry breaking is g_S^crit = 2π²/(3Λ²).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Lenaghan (2000) and Schaefer (2008) on the role of the U_A(1) anomaly in chiral symmetry breaking within effective theories. The authors clarify that Schaefer (2008) incorrectly concluded that spontaneous symmetry breaking would be lost for positive μ² with the anomaly term. The results connect to the ongoing debate about the nature of the light scalar meson σ (around 500 MeV) and its interpretation as a chiral partner of the pion, suggesting that if this resonance has a large σ_0 component, the anomaly driven breaking scenario would be realized.
