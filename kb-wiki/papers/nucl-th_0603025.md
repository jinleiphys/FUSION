# Relativistic interactions for the meson-two-nucleon system in the clothed-particle unitary representation

> The method of unitary clothing transformations put forward in relativistic quantum field theory (QFT) by Greenberg and Schweber and developed by Shirokov is applied to construct a new family of interactions in the meson-two-nucleon system. Along with a brief exposition of its basic elements we show a specific transition from the initial ``bare'' one-meson and one-nucleon operators and states to their physical ``clothed'' counterparts. We emphasize that the clothing transformations in question do not alter the original total Hamiltonian, but provides a conceptually more transparent representation of the same Hamiltonian in terms of a new set of operators for particles with physical properties and their relativistic interactions. The Hermitian and energy-independent interaction operators for the processes piN -> piN, NN -> NN and NN -> pi NN are derived starting from the Yukawa-type couplings between fermions (nucleons and antinucleons) and bosons (pi-, eta-, rho-, omega-mesons, etc.). These types of interaction have a distinctive off-energy-shell structure which is naturally generated by the unitary transformation that removes from the Hamiltonian the (three-leg) piNN vertex coupling.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the method of unitary clothing transformations, originally developed by Greenberg and Schweber and extended by Shirokov, can be applied to construct Hermitian, energy-independent interaction operators for the meson-two-nucleon system that are derived from Yukawa-type couplings, with the crucial condition that these transformations do not alter the original total Hamiltonian but instead provide a representation in terms of physical ("clothed") particles rather than bare ones.

## Method

The authors start from a field-theoretic Hamiltonian expressed in terms of creation and destruction operators for bare particles with unphysical masses, then apply a two-step unitary clothing transformation: first a Bogoliubov-type transformation that converts bare particles to bare particles with physical masses, followed by a second transformation that removes the three-leg piNN vertex coupling and produces clothed-particle operators. The interaction operators for piN -> piN, NN -> NN, and NN <-> piNN processes are derived algebraically using an iterative technique within the instant form of relativistic dynamics, working entirely in momentum space with the Fock-space Hamiltonian expressed as sums of products of creation and destruction operators.

## Key numbers

- The bare fermion energies are defined as E_p^circ = sqrt(p^2 + m_0^2) and bare meson energies as omega_k^circ = sqrt(k^2 + mu_0^2), where m_0 and mu_0 are the unrenormalized (bare) masses of nucleons and mesons respectively
- The physical fermion energies are E_p = sqrt(p^2 + m^2) and physical meson energies are omega_k = sqrt(k^2 + mu^2), where m and mu are the physical masses
- The unitary transformation for mesons involves coefficients cosh(chi_k) and sinh(chi_k) satisfying cosh^2(chi_k) - sinh^2(chi_k) = 1, with exp(chi_k) = sqrt(omega_k^circ / omega_k)
- The fermion transformation matrix O(p) is a 4x4 unitary matrix with diagonal elements A(p) and off-diagonal elements involving B(p), satisfying A^2(p) + p^2 B^2(p) = 1

## Context

This work extends the unitary transformation methods previously applied by Gloeckle and Mueller (1981) for spinless nucleons and by Korchin and Shebeko (1993) for nucleon-nucleon and nucleon-antinucleon interactions, now incorporating pions, eta, rho, and omega mesons with full spinor structure. The approach differs from the Okubo block-diagonalization method used by Fuda and co-workers (1994-1999) and from the Osaka group's work (Kobayashi, Sato, Ohtsubo, 1997) by not aiming to block-diagonalize the Hamiltonian a priori, and by naturally incorporating mass and vertex renormalization alongside the interaction construction. The resulting Hermitian, energy-independent quasipotentials enable consistent relativistic treatment of pion production and absorption processes such as NN -> NNpi, which is needed to resolve ambiguities in the mechanisms contributing to threshold cross sections and to extend phenomenological studies of reactions like pd -> pi^0 He^3.
