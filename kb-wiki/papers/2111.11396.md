# Stability of Classical Chromodynamic Fields

> A system of gluon fields generated at the earliest phase of relativistic heavy-ion collisions can be described in terms of classical fields. Numerical simulations show that the system is unstable but a character of the instability is not well understood. With the intention to systematically study the problem, we analyze a stability of classical chromomagnetic and chromoelectric fields which are constant and uniform. We consider the Abelian configurations discussed in the past where the fields are due to the single-color potentials linearly depending on coordinates. However, we mostly focus on the nonAbelian configurations where the fields are generated by the multi-color non-commuting constant uniform potentials. We derive a complete spectrum of small fluctuations around the background fields which obey the linearized Yang-Mills equations. The spectra of Abelian and nonAbelian configurations are similar but different and they both include unstable modes. We briefly discuss the relevance of our results for fields which are uniform only in a limited spatial domain.

## Key claim

The paper systematically derives the complete spectrum of small fluctuations around constant, uniform chromomagnetic and chromoelectric fields in SU(2) Yang-Mills theory, showing that both Abelian and nonAbelian configurations contain unstable modes, but the nonAbelian instability domain is bounded in momentum space while the Abelian one extends to infinity for wave vectors perpendicular to the field.

## Method

The authors linearize the Yang-Mills equations around constant uniform background fields using the background gauge condition, then solve the resulting eigenvalue equations for small fluctuations. For Abelian configurations (single-color potentials linear in coordinates), the equations reduce to harmonic oscillator or inverted harmonic oscillator forms in one spatial coordinate. For nonAbelian configurations (multi-color non-commuting constant potentials), the equations become algebraic after Fourier transformation, and the dispersion relations are obtained from determinants of 3×3 and 6×6 matrices, yielding cubic equations solved via Viète's trigonometric form.

## Key numbers

- For the Abelian magnetic field configuration, the unstable mode growth rate is γ = √(gB − k_x²) for k_x² < gB (Nielsen-Olesen instability)
- For the nonAbelian magnetic field configuration, the dispersion relation yields three branches ω_n²(k); ω₁² and ω₂² are always positive, while ω₃² becomes negative in a bounded region of momentum space
- The discriminant for the nonAbelian magnetic cubic equation is Δ/(16g³B³) = 9g³B³ + 68g²B²k_T² + 49gBk_T⁴ + 16k_T⁶, which is always positive
- For the nonAbelian electric field configuration, the discriminant is Δ/(4g⁶A⁶) = 9g⁶A⁶ + 4g⁴A⁴(13k² − 7k_x²) + 4g²A²(25k⁴ + 14k²k_x² − 119k_x⁴) + 64(k² + k_x²)³, also always positive

## Context

This work extends the stability analyses of classical chromodynamic fields initiated by Mandula (1976), Chang (1979), and Sikivie (1979), who studied Abelian configurations, and by Tudron (1980), who briefly discussed nonAbelian magnetic configurations without deriving the full spectrum. The paper is motivated by the glasma picture from the Color Glass Condensate approach (Iancu 2003, Gelis 2012), where numerical simulations by Romatschke (2005, 2006) showed unstable modes whose character (Weibel vs. Nielsen-Olesen) remained debated. The results provide the complete linear stability spectrum needed to interpret these simulations and enable future studies of fields uniform only in a limited spatial domain.
