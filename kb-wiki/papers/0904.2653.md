# Microscopic description of heavy ion collisions around the barrier

> A microscopic mean-field description of heavy ion collisions is performed in the framework of the time dependent Hartree-Fock theory using a Skyrme energy density functional. A good agreement with experiments is obtained on the position of the fusion barriers for various total masses and mass asymmetries. The excitation function of the 16O+208Pb is overestimated by about 16% above the barrier. Transfer below the barrier is studied in 16O+208Pb central collisions. Heavier systems are considered to study their fusion hindrance. We also compute collision times of the 238U+238U system. The latter has been used to produce super strong electric fields and to test non perturbative quantum electrodynamics theory. Indeed, if the life time of such giant system is of the order of few 10^-21 s, its electric field should induce spontaneous electron-positron pair emissions from vacuum. In our calculations, highest collision times are reached in the 238U+238U reaction for center of mass energies between 1000 and 1300 MeV.

## Key claim

The time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory, using a Skyrme energy density functional with no adjustable parameters for reaction mechanisms, reproduces experimental fusion barriers for a wide range of systems but overestimates the fusion excitation function for 16O+208Pb by about 16% above the barrier, and cannot describe sub-barrier fusion due to its classical treatment of the trajectory.

## Method

The authors performed fully three-dimensional TDHF calculations using the SLy4d Skyrme parametrization with a lattice spacing of 0.8 fm and a time step of 1.5e-24 s. Initial nuclei were placed at a distance of 44.8 fm (or 51.2 fm for the 238U+238U system) and given velocities determined by a Rutherford trajectory before the start of the TDHF evolution. Fusion barriers were determined by bracketing the energy between the highest non-fusing and lowest fusing head-on collisions, and collision times for 238U+238U were defined as the duration during which the neck density exceeded one-tenth of saturation density.

## Key numbers

* Fusion barrier for 16O+208Pb: V_B^TDHF = 74.445 ± 0.005 MeV, in excellent agreement with the experimental value of approximately 74.5 MeV (Mortensen 1999).
* The frozen-density nucleus-nucleus potential gives a barrier of 76 MeV for 16O+208Pb, slightly higher than the TDHF value.
* Fusion cross sections for 16O+208Pb above the barrier are overestimated by about 16% compared to experimental data (Mortensen 1999).
* For 90Zr+124Sn, the TDHF fusion threshold is V_B^TDHF = 238.75 MeV, while the frozen-density approximation gives 218 MeV.
* Maximum collision times for 238U+238U central collisions reach approximately 1000 fm/c (about 3.3e-21 s) for center-of-mass energies between 1000 and 1300 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier TDHF studies of heavy-ion fusion (Simenel 2001, 2004, 2007, 2008; Washiyama 2008) and provides a systematic comparison with experimental fusion barriers and the Bass barrier (Bass 1977). The study of fusion hindrance in heavy quasisymmetric systems (with charge product Z1 x Z2 >= 1600) is compared to the extra-push model (Swiatecki 1982) and the proximity model (Blocki 1977). The 238U+238U collision time calculations are compared to Quantum Molecular Dynamics results (Tian 2008) and are relevant for testing non-perturbative quantum electrodynamics predictions of spontaneous electron-positron pair emission from vacuum (Reinhardt 1981, Greiner 1983).
