# Implementing chiral three-body forces in terms of medium-dependent two-body forces

> Three-nucleon (3N) forces are an indispensable ingredient for accurate few-body and many-body nuclear structure and reaction theory calculations. While the direct implementation of chiral 3N forces can be technically very challenging, a simpler approach is given by employing instead a medium-dependent NN interaction V_med that reflects the physics of three-body forces at the two-body normal-ordered approximation. We review the derivation and construction of V_med from the chiral 3N interaction at next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO), consisting of a long-range $2π$-exchange term, a mid-range $1π$-exchange component and a short-range contact term. Several applications of V_med to the equation of state of cold nuclear and neutron matter, the nucleon single-particle potential in nuclear matter, and the nuclear quasiparticle interaction are discussed. We also explore differences in using local vs. nonlocal regulating functions on 3N forces and make direct comparisons to exact results at low order in perturbation theory expansions for the equation of state and single-particle potential. We end with a discussion and numerical calculation of the in-medium NN potential V_med from the next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) chiral 3N force, which consists of a series of long-range and short-range terms.

## Key claim

The paper reviews how chiral three-body forces can be approximated by medium-dependent two-body interactions (V_med) through normal ordering with respect to the noninteracting Fermi sea, and demonstrates that this approach provides an excellent approximation at first order in many-body perturbation theory, though at higher orders it fails to reproduce all topologies and residual three-body interactions contribute about 1 MeV to the nuclear equation of state at saturation density.

## Method

The authors derive the medium-dependent two-body interaction V_med from the chiral three-nucleon force at next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO) by summing one particle line over filled states in the noninteracting Fermi sea, involving spin and isospin summations and momentum integration over the Fermi sphere. They compute the resulting density-dependent NN interaction for the three N2LO topologies (two-pion-exchange, one-pion-exchange, and contact term) and extend the derivation to the N3LO chiral three-body force. They compare results using local versus nonlocal regulating functions and test the approximation against exact three-body force calculations at low order in perturbation theory for the equation of state and single-particle potential.

## Key numbers

- The average pion mass used is m_pi = 138 MeV, the pion decay constant is f_pi = 92.2 MeV, and the axial coupling constant is g_A = 1.29
- The chiral symmetry breaking scale is taken as Lambda_chi = 700 MeV
- The cutoff scale for nucleon-nucleon potentials is typically chosen Lambda less than or about 700 MeV, with Lambda greater than or about 414 MeV needed to fit scattering data up to E_lab = 350 MeV
- The nucleon number density in saturated interior of heavy nuclei is n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Typical neutron stars with mass M about 1.4 M_sun reach central densities n about 3 n_0
- The low-energy constants used in representative calculations are c_1 = -0.81 GeV^{-1}, c_3 = -3.4 GeV^{-1}, c_4 = 3.4 GeV^{-1}, c_D = -0.24, and c_E = -0.106, associated with an N3LO NN chiral interaction with cutoff Lambda = 450 MeV
- Residual three-body interactions contribute about 1 MeV to the nuclear equation of state at saturation density up to second order in perturbation theory

## Context

This work extends the approach of implementing three-body forces via medium-dependent two-body interactions previously developed by Baldo (1997), Bogner (2005), and Hagen (2007), and applies it within chiral effective field theory as formulated by Weinberg (1979), Epelbaum (2009), and Machleidt (2011). The paper enables systematic inclusion of chiral three-body forces in many-body calculations of nuclear matter equation of state, single-particle potentials, and quasiparticle interactions, while identifying limitations of the normal-ordered approximation at higher orders in perturbation theory as studied by Drischler (2019).
