# Renormalization of a Contact Interaction on a Lattice

> Contact interactions can be used to describe a system of particles at unitarity, contribute to the leading part of nuclear interactions and are numerically non-trivial because they require a proper regularization and renormalization scheme. We explain how to tune the coefficient of a contact interaction between non-relativistic particles on a discretized space in 1, 2, and 3 spatial dimensions such that we can remove all discretization artifacts. By taking advantage of a latticized Lüscher zeta function, we can achieve a momentum-independent scattering amplitude at any finite lattice spacing.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that by using a latticized Luscher zeta function derived from the discretized kinetic operator, one can tune the contact interaction coefficient to achieve momentum-independent scattering amplitudes at any finite lattice spacing, thereby removing all discretization artifacts without requiring a continuum extrapolation.

## Method

The authors implement a contact interaction on a cubic lattice in 1, 2, and 3 spatial dimensions using finite-difference kinetic operators that reach up to n_s nearest neighbors. They derive a dispersion-aware zeta function by replacing the continuum momentum dispersion in the scattering integrals with the discretized kinetic operator, then match the finite-volume lattice spectra directly to continuum infinite-volume phase shifts through a renormalization condition that absorbs the discretization-dependent counterterms.

## Key numbers

* In 3 dimensions, the dispersion counterterm L^dispersion_3 converges to 15.34824844606382 in the n_s to infinity limit, with values ranging from 19.95484069754250 (n_s=1) to 15.39675309099405 (n_s=50).
* In 2 dimensions, the dispersion counterterm L^dispersion_2 equals 1.116306393581637659468497, derived from Catalan's constant G as exp(log(2) - 2G/pi).
* The renormalized contact strength in 2 dimensions is given by C_R^{(n_s)}(Lambda) = -pi / [mu log(tilde_a_2 L^dispersion_2 Lambda)], where the coefficient L^dispersion_2 carries n_s dependence.
* In 3 dimensions, the renormalized contact strength satisfies -1/a_3 = (2 pi/mu)[1/C_R(Lambda) + 2 Lambda/pi], where a_3 is the scattering length.

## Context

This work extends the Luscher formalism (Luscher, 1986) to finite lattice spacings by constructing a discretization-aware zeta function, building on prior renormalization approaches for contact interactions by Seki (2005) and Epelbaum (2018). The method enables direct extraction of continuum infinite-volume phase shifts from finite-volume lattice data without continuum extrapolation, and the authors expect their tuning prescription to generalize to many-body computations such as calculations of the Bertsch parameter.
