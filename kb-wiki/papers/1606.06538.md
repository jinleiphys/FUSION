# Correlations of conserved number mixed susceptibilities in a hadron resonance gas model

> The ratios of off-diagonal and diagonal susceptibilities of conserved charges are studied using a hadron resonance gas (HRG) model with an emphasis towards providing a proper baseline for comparison to the corresponding future experimental measurements. We have studied the effect of kinematic acceptances, transverse momentum ($p_T$) and pseudorapidity ($η$), and different charged states on the ratios of the calculated susceptibilities. We find that the effect of $p_T$ and $η$ acceptance on the ratio of the susceptibilities are small relative to their dependence on the beam energy or the charged states of the used particles. We also present a HRG based calculation for various combinations of cumulant ratios of protons and pions, recently proposed as robust observables (with no theoretical uncertainties) for critical point search in the experiments. These results which increase as a function of collision energy will provide a better baseline for non-critical point physics compared to Poisson expectation.

## Key claim

The ratios of off-diagonal to diagonal susceptibilities of conserved charges (baryon number, electric charge, strangeness) calculated in a hadron resonance gas (HRG) model show only a small dependence on kinematic acceptances (transverse momentum and pseudorapidity) but a strong dependence on beam energy and on which charged states are included, meaning that the HRG model can provide a more realistic baseline for experimental critical-point searches than the Poisson expectation.

## Method

The authors compute generalized susceptibilities and mixed susceptibilities (e.g., χ_{BQ}, χ_{QS}, χ_{BS}) from the HRG partition function, which sums over all known hadron resonances. They apply experimental kinematic cuts in transverse momentum (p_T) and pseudorapidity (η) to the momentum-space integrals, and also vary which charged or strange particle species are included in the numerators and denominators of the susceptibility ratios. For the critical-point baseline, they calculate five specific cumulant ratios of protons and pions (e.g., skewness and kurtosis ratios) from the HRG model, both for primordial particles and after including resonance decay contributions.

## Key numbers

- The ratios χ_{BQ}^{11}/χ_B^2, χ_{BQ}^{11}/χ_Q^2, χ_{QS}^{11}/χ_Q^2, χ_{QS}^{11}/χ_S^2, χ_{BS}^{11}/χ_B^2, and χ_{BS}^{11}/χ_S^2 are studied as functions of collision energy √s_{NN} for p_T acceptances ranging from full acceptance to 0.2–2.0 GeV/c and η acceptances from full to |η| ≤ 0.5.
- The off-diagonal ratios χ_{BQ}^{13}/χ_Q^2, χ_{QS}^{13}/χ_Q^2, and χ_{BS}^{13}/χ_S^2 show a visibly stronger p_T dependence than the χ^{11}/χ^2 ratios across all collision energies.
- For the baryon-charge correlation χ_{BQ}^{11}/χ_p^2 and χ_{BQ}^{13}/χ_p^2, inclusion of higher charge states like Δ^{++} produces a significant difference compared to using only protons.
- The cumulant ratios of protons and pions (e.g., (κ_{3p}κ_{2π}^{3/2})/(κ_{3π}κ_{2p}^{3/2})) are calculated within 0.2 ≤ p_T ≤ 2.0 GeV/c and |η| ≤ 0.5, and are observed to increase as a function of √s_{NN}.

## Context

This work extends the HRG-based baseline calculations of conserved-charge susceptibilities (e.g., Bazavov (2012), Borsanyi (2011)) by systematically studying the effect of experimental kinematic acceptances and charge-state selection, which is directly relevant to the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program. The authors also provide HRG baselines for the mixed pion-proton cumulant ratios proposed by Athanasiou (2010) as robust observables for the QCD critical point, enabling experimental comparisons that can replace the simpler Poisson expectation with a more realistic hadronic baseline.
