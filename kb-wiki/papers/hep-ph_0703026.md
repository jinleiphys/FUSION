# Electromagnetic Radiation from Broken Symmetries in Relativistic Nuclear Collisions

> A new channel of direct photon production from a quark gluon plasma (QGP) is explored in the framework of high-temperature QCD. This process appears at next-to-leading order, in the presence of a charge asymmetry in the excited matter. The photon production rate from this new mechanism is suppressed compared to the QCD annihilation and Compton scattering at low baryon density but assumes importance in baryon-rich matter.

## Key claim

A new channel of direct photon production from a quark-gluon plasma (QGP) via a gluon-gluon-photon vertex becomes possible only when the medium has a finite baryon chemical potential, breaking charge conjugation invariance; this rate is suppressed compared to leading-order QCD annihilation and Compton scattering at low baryon density but becomes significant in baryon-rich matter.

## Method

The authors compute the hard photon production rate from a QGP at finite temperature and chemical potential using the Hard-Thermal-Loops (HTL) effective field theory of high-temperature QCD. They evaluate the imaginary part of the retarded photon self-energy from diagrams containing the effective two-gluon-photon vertex, summing over Matsubara frequencies and using spectral functions for longitudinal and transverse gluon propagators. The calculation is performed for two massless quark flavors with equal chemical potentials, fixing the strong coupling constant at α_s = 0.4.

## Key numbers

- Temperature T = 200 MeV is used for the comparison plot of photon emission rates.
- The strong coupling constant is fixed at α_s = 0.4 throughout the calculation.
- The quark chemical potential is set as μ_u = μ_d = μ = μ_B/3 for two massless flavors.
- The critical temperature for the QCD phase transition at vanishing chemical potential is cited as T_c ~ 170 MeV from lattice QCD (Karsch, 2003).
- The lower bound on energy density in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC is about 5 GeV/fm³ at τ = 1 fm/c, placing temperatures above 300 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier proposal of photon production from broken charge conjugation symmetry in a dense medium (Majumder, 2000; Majumder, 2003) by providing the first numerical calculation of the hard photon rate from the gluon-gluon-photon vertex using HTL effective theory. It contrasts with prior calculations of photon rates at finite chemical potential (Kapusta, 1991; Dumitru, 1993; Vija, 1994) which focused on processes already present at zero baryon density. The new channel offers a potential probe of the pure gluon sector of QGP, especially in baryon-rich matter from low-energy heavy-ion collisions, and can be extended to non-equilibrium scenarios such as the early stages of relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions where gluons dominate.
