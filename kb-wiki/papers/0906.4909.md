# Symmetry coefficients and incompressibility of clusterized supernova matter

> The symmetry energy coefficients, incompressibility, and single-particle and isovector potentials of clusterized dilute nuclear matter are calculated at different temperatures employing the $S$-matrix approach to the evaluation of the equation of state. Calculations have been extended to understand the aforesaid properties of homogeneous and clusterized supernova matter in the subnuclear density region. Comparison of the results in the $S$-matrix and mean-field approach reveals some subtle differences in the density and temperature region we explore.

## Key claim

The symmetry energy coefficients, incompressibility, and single-particle potentials of clusterized supernova matter are calculated using the S-matrix approach, revealing that the symmetry energy in clusterized matter is anharmonic in the asymmetry parameter (particularly at lower temperatures) and that the symmetry coefficients show marked differences from mean-field predictions, with the caveat that the S-matrix approach is restricted to baryon densities up to 0.02 fm⁻³ where asymptotic wave functions remain meaningful.

## Method

The authors employed the S-matrix formalism of statistical mechanics (Dashen-Ma-Bernstein approach) to compute the grand-canonical partition function for dilute nuclear and supernova matter, including contributions from stable ground states, excited states below particle emission threshold, and two-particle scattering channels with experimentally known phase shifts. For supernova matter, the Coulomb interaction was included in fragment binding energies using the Wigner-Seitz approximation, and only nuclei within the drip lines (approximately 9000 isotopes from the Myers mass formula) were considered. The results were compared with mean-field calculations using the SkM* interaction.

## Key numbers

- Maximum baryon density considered: 0.02 fm⁻³ (limit of applicability of S-matrix approach)
- Temperature range explored: up to approximately 20 MeV (with detailed results shown at T = 4 MeV and T = 8 MeV)
- Maximum fragment mass used in nuclear matter calculations: A_max = 1000
- Effective single-particle potential in clusterized symmetric nuclear matter at saturation: approximately -34 MeV (compared to approximately -60 MeV for saturated uniform nuclear matter)
- Level density parameter: a = A/8 MeV⁻¹ (empirical value)
- Upper limit for resonance domination (E_r): 12 MeV
- Particle emission threshold (E_s): 8 MeV
- Lower limit for excited state integration (E_0): 2 MeV
- Fragment radius parameter: R_0i = 1.16 A_i^(1/3) fm (0.8 fm for protons)

## Context

This work extends the S-matrix approach previously applied to nuclear matter by Malatesta et al. to supernova matter (charge-neutral matter in beta equilibrium with electrons), incorporating Coulomb effects that constrain fragment sizes within drip lines. It contrasts with the virial expansion approach of Horowitz and Schwenk (2006), which included only neutrons, protons, and alpha particles, and with mean-field calculations using the SkM* interaction. The results enable improved modeling of supernova dynamics by providing temperature- and density-dependent symmetry coefficients and incompressibilities for clusterized matter in the subnuclear density region.
