# Optimized basis of covariant density functional theory: point coupling functionals and excited states

> The present investigation focuses on the improvement of the accuracy of the description of physical observables of interest in moderately sized fermionic basis within the framework of covariant density functional theory. It extends previous study of Ref. [1] to point coupling (PC) covariant energy density functionals (CEDFs) and to excited states. Using as a benchmark the solutions corresponding either to infinite fermionic basis or those extrapolated to such a basis it is shown that the optimization of oscillator frequency $\hbarω_0$ of the harmonic oscillator (HO) basis leads to a substantial improvement in the description of different physical observables in the fermionic basis truncated at $N_F$. Globally optimized scaling factors $f_{opt}(A)$ of the oscillator frequency and the sizes $N_F^{\varepsilon}$ of the HO bases providing the required accuracy $\varepsilon$ in the calculations of the binding energies are generated for the PC functionals. The optimization of the basis also significantly improves the accuracy of the description of potential energy curves, defining the fission barriers and fission isomers in actinides and superheavy nuclei, provided that the size of the basis is at least equal to $N_F=20$. The optimization of the HO basis improves the accuracy of the description of the energies of bound single-particle states: the only exceptions are weakly bound neutron states with low orbital momenta $l=0$, 1 and 2. It is demonstrated for the first time that the halo densities of neutron halo nuclei generated in the coordinate space calculations are well reproduced in the calculations with very large fermionic HO bases.

## Key claim

The optimization of the harmonic oscillator basis scaling factor \(f\) (and thus the oscillator frequency \(\hbar\omega_0\)) substantially improves the accuracy of binding energies, charge radii, and excited-state properties in truncated fermionic bases for point-coupling covariant density functional theory, provided the basis size is at least \(N_F=20\); however, the convergence of charge radii is slower than that of binding energies, and weakly bound neutron states with low orbital momenta \(l=0,1,2\) remain exceptions to the improved single-particle energy description.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations using spherical and axially deformed computer codes, employing the point-coupling functional PC-Z (and, for excited states, the meson-exchange functional DD-MEZ) with separable pairing. They systematically varied the scaling factor \(f\) of the harmonic oscillator frequency \(\hbar\omega_0 = f \times 41 A^{-1/3}\) MeV and compared results in truncated fermionic bases (up to \(N_F=40\) for deformed, up to \(N_F>70\) for spherical nuclei) against either exact infinite-basis solutions (for spherical nuclei) or extrapolated infinite-basis values (for deformed nuclei, using Shanks transformations and Richardson extrapolation). The analysis covered binding energies, charge radii, potential energy curves for fission, single-particle energies, and neutron halo densities across a set of spherical and deformed even-even nuclei distributed across the nuclear chart.

## Key numbers

* Global rms differences \(\delta B_{rms}\) between binding energies in infinite and truncated (\(N_F=20\)) bases for meson-exchange functionals are only 0.025 MeV (NL5(Z)) and 0.031 MeV (DD-MEZ), as reported in prior work (OAD.25).
* For spherical nuclei with the PC-Z functional, the required basis size \(N_F^{\varepsilon=1\,\text{keV}}\) to reach 1 keV accuracy varies with scaling factor; for example, \(^{16}\text{O}\) reaches minimum \(N_F^{\varepsilon=1\,\text{keV}}\) at \(f=1.4\), while \(^{304}120\) requires \(N_F=30\) at \(f=1.9\) for the same accuracy.
* For a numerical error of \(\varepsilon=30\) keV, the required basis size \(N_F^{\varepsilon=30\,\text{keV}}\) is drastically reduced (by almost 20 fermionic shells) compared to \(N_F^{\varepsilon=1\,\text{keV}}\); for \(^{208}\text{Pb}\) with \(f=1.7\), \(N_F^{\varepsilon=30\,\text{keV}}=30\).
* For deformed nuclei, optimal scaling factors \(f_{opt}\) increase from 1.4 (light nuclei like \(^{16}\text{O}\)) to 1.9 (superheavy \(^{304}120\)); for \(^{290}\text{Lv}\) at \(f_{opt}=1.8\), the basis size required for 0.1 MeV accuracy is \(N_F=30\) and for 0.03 MeV accuracy is \(N_F=34\).
* The variation of charge radii \(r_{ch}\) for scaling factors \(f=1.0-2.0\) in truncated bases at \(N_F^{\varepsilon=1\,\text{keV}}\) is typically below \(8\times 10^{-5}\) fm, except for \(^{16}\text{O}\) and \(^{298}\text{Fl}\).
* For the spherical \(^{208}\text{Pb}\) nucleus, extrapolation errors with 3-point Shanks transformation at \(f=1.0\) exceed 100 keV for basis sizes \(N_F \leq 40\), while at \(f=1.7\) the errors become smaller than 40 keV at \(N_F \approx 30\).

## Context

This work extends the prior optimization study of OAD.25 (which focused on meson-exchange functionals) to point-coupling functionals and to excited states, including fission barriers and isomers. It demonstrates that the same basis optimization strategy works for PC functionals despite their different convergence behavior (binding energies converge from above, unlike the mixed convergence of meson-exchange functionals). The results enable accurate CDFT calculations in moderately sized bases (\(N_F=20-34\)) with controlled numerical errors, and show for the first time that neutron halo densities from coordinate-space calculations can be reproduced in very large harmonic oscillator bases, opening the possibility of studying halo nuclei within the HO-basis RHB framework.
