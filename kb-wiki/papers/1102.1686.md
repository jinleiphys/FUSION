# Microscopic Calculation of IBM Parameters by Potential Energy Surface Mapping

> A coherent state technique is used to generate an Interacting Boson Model (IBM) Hamiltonian energy surface that simulates a mean field energy surface. The method presented here has some significant advantages over previous work. Specifically, that this can be a completely predictive requiring no a priori knowledge of the IBM parameters. The technique allows for the prediction of the low lying energy spectra and electromagnetic transition rates which are of astrophysical interest. Results and comparison with experiment are included for krypton, molybdenum, palladium, cadmium, gadolinium, dysprosium and erbium nuclei.

## Key claim

The authors present a method to determine Interacting Boson Model (IBM-1) Hamiltonian parameters by mapping the bosonic potential energy surface (PES) onto a fermionic PES generated by a micro-macro method, which can be completely predictive if the energy scale is fixed by a cranking calculation rather than experimental input, but the mapping only reproduces the low-energy portion of the fermionic PES (below 1 MeV) and the overall energy scale remains a free parameter fixed by the energy of the first 2+ state.

## Method

The fermionic PES was generated using the Tilted-Axis Cranking (TAC) micro-macro method with a Nilsson potential and BCS pairing, calculating energy as a function of quadrupole deformation parameters ε2 and γ. The bosonic PES was obtained from the expectation value of the IBM-1 Hamiltonian in a coherent state, and the IBM parameters ζ and χ were determined by minimizing the mean squared deviation between the two surfaces for grid points where the TAC PES lies below 1 MeV. The energy scale cE was fixed by the experimental or TAC-calculated energy of the first 2+ state, and the deformation scale cβ was fixed by requiring the IBM PES minimum to match the TAC PES minimum in ε2.

## Key numbers

- BCS pairing gaps used: Δp = 13.4/A1/2 MeV and Δn = 12.8/A1/2 MeV
- For 76Kr: ε2 = -0.220, ζ = 0.62, χ = 0.74, cE = 3.07, cβ = 2.50, eB = 0.081 e·b
- For 154Gd: ε2 = 0.202, ζ = 1.00, χ = -0.30, cE = 2.12, cβ = 5.00, eB = 0.153 e·b
- For 168Er: ε2 = 0.261, ζ = 1.00, χ = -0.36, cE = 2.55, cβ = 4.00, eB = 0.116 e·b
- For 98Mo with calculated scale: E(2+1)TAC = 238 keV, B(E2)TAC = 0.131 e2b2, ζ = 0.68, χ = -0.06
- The mapping procedure uses only regions of the TAC energy surface below 1 MeV
- TAC-calculated E(2+1) and B(E2) values typically agree with experiment within 20%

## Context

This work extends the approach of Nomura et al. (2008), who matched a Skyrme Hartree-Fock PES with an IBM-2 Hamiltonian, by adopting IBM-1 and using a micro-macro method (TAC) instead of Skyrme energy density functional, which provides smoother potentials suitable for automated fitting. The method enables prediction of low-lying spectra and γ-absorption strength functions for nuclei far from stability via the Instantaneous Shape Sampling technique, which is needed for studies of r- and p-process nucleosynthesis. The results are compared with previous IBM parameter fits by McCutchan et al. (2004) for gadolinium, dysprosium, and erbium isotopes, revealing an ambiguity where different parameter sets can produce similar low-energy PES.
