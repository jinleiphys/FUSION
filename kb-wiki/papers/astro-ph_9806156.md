# Damping of differential rotation in neutron stars

> We derive the transport relaxation times for quasiparticle-vortex scattering processes via nuclear force, relevant for the damping of differential rotation of superfluids in the quantum liquid core of a neutron star. The proton scattering off the neutron vortices provides the dominant resistive force on the vortex lattice at all relevant temperatures in the phase where neutrons only are in the paired state. If protons are superconducting, a small fraction of hyperons and resonances in the normal state would be the dominant source of friction on neutron and proton vortex lattices at the core temperatures $T\ge 10^{7}$ K.

# Damping of differential rotation in neutron stars

## Key claim

Nuclear-force-mediated scattering of baryons off vortex core quasiparticles provides the dominant resistive force on vortex lattices in neutron star superfluid interiors, exceeding electromagnetic coupling channels in most phases. This dominance holds when any baryons are in the normal state, but the specific implementation depends on uncertain microphysical inputs such as pairing gaps and baryon abundances at high densities.

## Method

The author derives transport relaxation times for quasiparticle-vortex scattering using a quasiclassical Landau-Boltzmann kinetic equation for the proton distribution function, with the scattering vertex computed via the Brueckner G-matrix approximation. The transition probability for proton scattering off neutron vortex core quasiparticles is expressed through the structure factor of vortex core states (using BCS coherence factors in cylindrical coordinates) and the in-medium neutron-proton differential cross section. The relaxation time is obtained by linearizing the kinetic equation and using a variational method with a trial function proportional to the vortex velocity, then evaluating the spectral functions for protons and neutrons in the low-temperature limit.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of electron–neutron-vortex to proton–neutron-vortex relaxation times τ_en/τ_pn is of order 10^6, reflecting the difference between spin-magnetic-moment and nuclear interaction matrix elements.
- The crossover temperature where τ_eM (electron scattering off P-wave vortex magnetization) exceeds τ_pn is approximately 3 × 10^6 K.
- At density n = 0.4 fm^{-3} with proton fraction x_p = 0.060 and neutron gap Δ = 0.7 MeV, the proton–neutron-vortex relaxation time τ_pn is 0.001 s at T ≈ 10^8 K, 0.004 s at T ≈ 10^7 K, and 3.670 s at T ≈ 3 × 10^6 K (for Vela pulsar spin period P = 0.089 s).
- At n = 0.4 fm^{-3} with hyperon concentration x_Y = 0.01, the hyperon–neutron-vortex relaxation time τ_Yn is 0.0002 s at T ≈ 10^8 K, while the electron–vortex-flux relaxation time τ_eΦ is 0.167 s.
- At n = 0.5 fm^{-3} with x_Y = 0.1, τ_Yn is 0.0002 s at T ≈ 10^8 K and τ_eΦ is 0.195 s.
- The effective neutron–proton scattering cross section σ_pn* ranges from 51.7 fm^2 to 87.2 fm^2 across densities 0.4–1.0 fm^{-3}.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of electromagnetic coupling channels between electrons and nucleonic superfluids in neutron star cores, specifically the electron coupling to neutron vortex magnetic moments (Feibelman, 1971), to P-wave vortex magnetization (Sauls, 1989), and to entrainment magnetization in proton superconductors (Alpar et al., 1984; Sedrakian, 1995). The paper shows that nuclear interaction channels dominate these electromagnetic mechanisms when normal baryons are present. It enables more realistic modeling of the coupled crust-core dynamics in pulsar spin glitches and timing noise, though implementation requires resolving uncertainties in high-density pairing gaps and hyperon thresholds from many-body calculations (Baldo et al., 1992; Elgarøy et al., 1996; Wiringa et al., 1988; Glendenning, 1985; Glendenning, 1987).
