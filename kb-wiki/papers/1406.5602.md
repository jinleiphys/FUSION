# Chiral dynamics in the low-temperature phase of QCD

> We investigate the low-temperature phase of QCD and the crossover region with two light flavors of quarks. The chiral expansion around the point $(T,m=0)$ in the temperature vs. quark-mass plane indicates that a sharp real-time excitation exists with the quantum numbers of the pion. An exact sum rule is derived for the thermal modification of the spectral function associated with the axial charge density; the (dominant) pion pole contribution obeys the sum rule. We determine the two parameters of the pion dispersion relation using lattice QCD simulations and test the applicability of the chiral expansion. The time-dependent correlators are also analyzed using the Maximum Entropy Method, yielding consistent results. Finally, we test the predictions of the chiral expansion around the point $(T=0,m=0)$ for the temperature dependence of static observables.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a sharp pion quasiparticle excitation persists in the low-temperature phase of QCD up to temperatures near the crossover, with a dispersion relation of the form ω² = u²(mπ² + k²) where the pion velocity u deviates significantly from unity (u ≈ 0.88 at T ≈ 150 MeV), indicating a substantial violation of boost invariance by the thermal medium. The validity of the chiral expansion around the point (T, m=0) is tested and found to hold up to T ≈ 190 MeV, but breaks down at higher temperatures where the system enters the crossover region.

## Method

The authors perform lattice QCD simulations with two flavors of O(a) improved Wilson quarks at renormalized masses of approximately 8 MeV and 15 MeV, using lattices of size 16×32³ with temperatures varied by changing the bare coupling at fixed aspect ratio LT=2. They extract the screening pion mass mπ from spatial pseudoscalar correlators, the pion decay constant fπ from axial current correlators, and determine the pion velocity u from the midpoint of the axial charge correlator using a relation derived from chiral Ward identities. The spectral function is also analyzed using the Maximum Entropy Method (MEM) to test the dominance of the pion quasiparticle contribution.

## Key numbers

- Pion velocity u = 0.88(2) at T ≈ 150 MeV for quark mass m = 15 MeV (in the MSbar scheme at μ=2 GeV)
- Pseudocritical temperatures: Tc = 211(5) MeV at m ≈ 15 MeV and Tc = 193(7) MeV at m ≈ 8 MeV
- Extrapolated critical temperature in the chiral limit: between 160 MeV and 175 MeV
- At T ≈ 150 MeV, the screening pion mass and decay constant fπ change by only about 5% from their T=0 values
- Renormalized quark masses in the range 8 MeV ≤ m ≤ 15 MeV (MSbar scheme at μ=2 GeV)
- The screening pion mass increases as quark mass is reduced from 15 MeV to 8 MeV at T ≈ 180 MeV, indicating breakdown of chiral expansion

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Son and Stephanov (2002) who derived the pion dispersion relation in the low-temperature phase using chiral perturbation theory around (T, m=0), and tests it with lattice QCD for the first time. The results complement the hadron resonance gas model (HRG) which successfully describes static thermodynamic quantities (Borsanyi 2013, Bazavov 2012) but does not predict real-time excitation properties. The paper enables future studies of in-medium modifications of hadron properties and tests of critical scaling predictions near the chiral phase transition, though the authors note that at the simulated quark masses the system still exhibits a smooth crossover rather than critical behavior.
