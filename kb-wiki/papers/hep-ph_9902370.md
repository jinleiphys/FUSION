# Wigner symmetry in the limit of large scattering lengths

> We note that in the limit where the NN 1S0 and 3S1 scattering lengths, a^(1S0) and a^(3S1), go to infinity, the leading terms in the effective field theory for strong NN interactions are invariant under Wigner's SU(4) spin-isospin symmetry. This explains why the leading effects of radiation pions on the S-wave NN scattering amplitudes vanish as a^(1S0) and a^(3S1) go to infinity. The implications of Wigner symmetry for NN to NN axion and gamma d to n p are also considered.

## Key claim

In the limit where both the NN 1S0 and 3S1 scattering lengths go to infinity, the leading terms in the effective field theory for strong NN interactions become invariant under Wigner's SU(4) spin-isospin symmetry, even though the physical scattering lengths are finite and very different in the two channels. The symmetry is useful for momenta satisfying 1/a^(s) << p << Lambda, where corrections scale as (1/a^(1S0) - 1/a^(3S1)) rather than as (a^(1S0) - a^(3S1)).

## Method

The authors analyze the effective field theory for nucleon strong interactions, first with pions integrated out, using dimensional regularization and comparing minimal subtraction with PDS (power divergence subtraction) and OS (on-shell) momentum subtraction schemes. They show that in PDS or OS schemes, as the scattering lengths go to infinity, the two-body contact couplings C0^(s)(mu) flow to the same value -4pi/(M mu) in both channels, making the leading Lagrangian term proportional to (N-dagger N)^2 invariant under Wigner SU(4) transformations. They then apply this symmetry to compute amplitudes for NN to NN axion and gamma d to n p, and discuss radiation pion contributions.

## Key numbers

- a^(1S0) = -23.714 +/- 0.013 fm (physical NN 1S0 scattering length)
- a^(3S1) = 5.425 +/- 0.001 fm (physical NN 3S1 scattering length)
- 1/a^(1S0) = -8.3 MeV and 1/a^(3S1) = 36 MeV (inverse scattering lengths in energy units)
- r0^(1S0) = 2.73 +/- 0.03 fm (effective range in 1S0 channel)
- r0^(3S1) = 1.749 +/- 0.008 fm (effective range in 3S1 channel)
- Estimated symmetry-breaking scale Lambda ~ 400 MeV from (r0^(1S0) - r0^(3S1))/2 = 0.49 fm
- g_A ~ 1.25 (axial coupling) and f ~ 131 MeV (pion decay constant)

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to nuclear physics developed by Weinberg (1990, 1991) and the power counting scheme of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998) for large scattering lengths. It explains the numerical cancellations observed in explicit calculations of radiation pion contributions by Mehen and Stewart (1999) as a consequence of Wigner symmetry. The symmetry is analogous to heavy quark spin-flavor symmetry in QCD (Isgur and Wise, 1989, 1990) and also arises in the large number of colors limit of QCD (Kaplan and Savage, 1996). The results enable systematic treatment of three-body forces, where recent work by Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (1998, 1999) suggests the (N-dagger N)^3 contact interaction may not be subleading compared to two-body effects.
