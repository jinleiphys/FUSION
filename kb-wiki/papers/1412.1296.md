# Extracting meson-baryon contributions to the electroexcitation of the $N(1675){\frac{5}{2}}^-$ nucleon resonance

> We report on the determination of the electrocouplings for the transition from the proton to the $N(1675){\frac{5}{2}}^-$ resonance state using recent differential cross section data on $e p \rightarrow eπ^+ n$ by the CLAS collaboration at $1.8 \le Q^2 < 4.5$GeV$^2$. The data have been analyzed using two different approaches, the unitary isobar model and fixed-t dispersion relations. The extracted $γ^* p\rightarrow N(1675){\frac{5}{2}}^-$ helicity amplitudes show considerable coupling through the $A^p_{1/2}$ amplitude, that is significantly larger than predicted three-quark contribution to this amplitude. The amplitude $A^p_{3/2}$ is much smaller. Both results are consistent with the predicted sizes of the meson-baryon contributions at $Q^2 \geq 1.8 $GeV$^2$ from the dynamical coupled-channel model.

## Key claim

The electroexcitation of the \(N(1675)\frac{5}{2}^-\) resonance from the proton is dominated by meson-baryon contributions rather than quark transitions, because the measured transverse helicity amplitude \(A^p_{1/2}\) is significantly larger than the three-quark contribution predicted by quark models, while \(A^p_{3/2}\) is much smaller and consistent with zero within uncertainties.

## Method

The authors analyzed differential cross section data for the \(ep \rightarrow e\pi^+ n\) reaction from the CLAS collaboration at \(1.8 \le Q^2 < 4.5\) GeV\(^2\) and \(1.60 < W < 2.0\) GeV, combined with earlier CLAS data at lower \(W\) and similar \(Q^2\). Two independent theoretical frameworks were used to extract the resonance helicity amplitudes: the unitary isobar model (UIM) and fixed-t dispersion relations (DR), both of which model non-resonant backgrounds and separate them from the s-channel resonance contributions. The extracted amplitudes were averaged between the two methods, and model uncertainties from resonance parameters, background modeling, and the \(\rho \rightarrow \pi \gamma\) form factor were added in quadrature.

## Key numbers

- The analysis used over 37,000 cross section data points from CLAS on \(ep \rightarrow e n\pi^+\) at \(1.8 \le Q^2 < 4.5\) GeV\(^2\) and \(1.60 < W < 2.0\) GeV (Park 2014), combined with earlier CLAS data at \(1.15 < W < 1.69\) GeV at close \(Q^2\) values (Park 2011).
- The \(\chi^2/N\) values for the UIM and DR fits ranged from 1.2 to 2.9 across the different \(Q^2\) bins, with the best fits at \(Q^2 = 4.16\) GeV\(^2\) (\(\chi^2/N = 1.2\) for UIM, 1.3 for DR) and the worst at \(Q^2 = 1.72\) GeV\(^2\) (\(\chi^2/N = 2.7\) for UIM, 2.9 for DR).
- The resonance parameters used were mass \(M = 1.675\) GeV, width \(\Gamma = 0.15\) GeV, and \(\pi N\) branching ratio \(\beta_{\pi N} = 0.4\).
- The extracted \(A^p_{1/2}\) amplitude is significantly larger than the three-quark predictions from quark models (Giannini 2001, Merten 2002, Aznauryan 2007), which are smaller than the statistical and model uncertainties of the data.
- The \(A^p_{3/2}\) amplitude is consistent with zero within uncertainties and drops much faster than \(A^p_{1/2}\) when going from \(Q^2 = 0\) (RPP estimates) to the measured \(Q^2\) range.

## Context

This work extends the study of meson-baryon contributions to nucleon resonance electroexcitation, previously observed in the \(\Delta(1232)\frac{3}{2}^+\) transition (Thomas 1984, Faessler 1985, Kamalov 1999, Sato 2001), to the \(N(1675)\frac{5}{2}^-\) state. The \(N(1675)\frac{5}{2}^-\) is uniquely suited because the Moorhouse selection rule (Moorhouse 1966) suppresses its transverse quark-transition amplitudes from the proton, making it a clean probe of non-quark contributions. The results are consistent with the meson-baryon contributions predicted by the dynamical coupled-channel model of the EBAC group (Sato 2008) and provide an experimental test for models of nucleon resonance structure. Future measurements at lower \(Q^2\) and on the neutron are needed to fully map the transition to the real photon point and to test the expected significant quark-transition strength from the neutron.
