# Improved modeling of $γγ$ processes in ultraperipheral collisions at hadron colliders

> The CERN LHC is not only the current energy-frontier collider for parton-parton collisions, but has proven a powerful photon collider providing photon-photon ($γγ$) collisions at center-of-mass energies and luminosities never reached before. The latest theoretical developments implemented in the gamma-UPC Monte Carlo (MC) event generator, which can calculate arbitrary exclusive final state produced via $γγ$ fusion in ultraperipheral collisions (UPCs) of protons and/or nuclei at the LHC, are presented. These include azimuthal modulations of dilepton pairs produced in the $γγ\to\ell^+\ell^-$ process, and neutron emission probabilities for photoexcited lead ions in PbPb UPCs. A few comparisons of the results of the updated gamma-UPC v.1.6 code to relevant RHIC and LHC data are presented.

## Key claim
The gamma-UPC Monte Carlo event generator has been extended to include two previously missing physical effects: azimuthal modulations of dilepton pairs from linearly polarized photons, and neutron emission probabilities from photoexcited lead ions. The authors show that their implementation reproduces existing experimental data from STAR and CMS within uncertainties, but the azimuthal coefficient comparisons suggest possible need for further refinement.

## Method
The authors implemented transverse-momentum (kt) and azimuthal-angle (phi) smearing of initial photons in gamma-UPC by processing MadGraph5 Les Houches event files with a Python script that incorporates photon transverse-momentum distribution (TMD) coefficients. For neutron emission, they fitted experimental photoabsorption cross sections for lead ions from threshold (few MeV) up to 16.4 GeV, and used a Regge-based parameterization scaled by nuclear mass number A=208 with a shadowing factor of 0.65 for higher energies, then multiplied the neutron emission probability by the no-inelastic-interaction probability in the photon flux convolution integral.

## Key numbers
- Au-Au UPCs at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV: dielectron invariant mass range 0.45–2.6 GeV, electron transverse momentum p_T > 0.2 GeV, pseudorapidity |eta| < 1, pair transverse momentum p_T^ee < 0.1 GeV
- PbPb UPCs at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV: dielectron invariant mass m_ee > 5 GeV
- Photoabsorption cross section fit covers photon energies from threshold (few MeV) up to 16.4 GeV using data from Lepretre (1981), Carlos (1984), Muccifora (1998), Caldwell (1973), Michalowski (1977), and Gheorghe (2024)
- High-energy photoabsorption uses Regge parameterization scaled by A=208 with shadowing factor 0.65, matching Caldwell (1978) data
- ChFF-based gamma-UPC predictions for neutron emission categories show data/gamma-UPC ratio around unity for CMS PbPb UPC data at 5.02 TeV

## Context
This work extends the gamma-UPC generator (Shao, 2022) which enabled automated computation of arbitrary gamma-gamma processes in ultraperipheral collisions. The azimuthal modulation implementation addresses the linear polarization of quasireal photons predicted by the equivalent photon approximation (von Weizsacker, 1934; Williams, 1934) and measured by STAR (2019), providing an alternative to the Superchic model (Harland-Lang, 2023). The neutron emission implementation follows approaches used by other generators such as nOOn (Broz, 2019) and Superchic (Harland-Lang, 2023), enabling comparisons with CMS data (2024) and enabling more precise trigger simulations for photon-photon physics at the LHC.
