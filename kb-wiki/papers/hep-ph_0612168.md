# Wilson loops in heavy ion collisions and their calculation in AdS/CFT

> Expectation values of Wilson loops define the nonperturbative properties of the hot medium produced in heavy ion collisions that arise in the analysis of both radiative parton energy loss and quarkonium suppression. We use the AdS/CFT correspondence to calculate the expectation values of such Wilson loops in the strongly coupled plasma of N=4 super Yang-Mills (SYM) theory, allowing for the possibility that the plasma may be moving with some collective flow velocity as is the case in heavy ion collisions. We obtain the N=4 SYM values of the jet quenching parameter $\hat q$, which describes the energy loss of a hard parton in QCD, and of the velocity-dependence of the quark-antiquark screening length for a moving dipole as a function of the angle between its velocity and its orientation. We show that if the quark-gluon plasma is flowing with velocity v_f at an angle theta with respect to the trajectory of a hard parton, the jet quenching parameter $\hat q$ is modified by a factor gamma_f(1-v_f cos theta), and show that this result applies in QCD as in N=4 SYM. We discuss the relevance of the lessons we are learning from all these calculations to heavy ion collisions at RHIC and at the LHC. Furthermore, we discuss the relation between our results and those obtained in other theories with gravity duals, showing in particular that the ratio between $\hat q$ in any two conformal theories with gravity duals is the square root of the ratio of their central charges. This leads us to conjecture that in nonconformal theories $\hat q$ defines a quantity that always decreases along renormalization group trajectories and allows us to use our calculation of $\hat q$ in N=4 SYM to make a conjecture for its value in QCD.

## Key claim

The paper uses the AdS/CFT correspondence to calculate Wilson loop expectation values in strongly coupled N=4 super Yang-Mills (SYM) plasma, obtaining results for the jet quenching parameter and the velocity-dependent quark-antiquark screening length that are conjectured to apply to QCD despite the differences between the two theories, with the caveat that the ratio of jet quenching parameters between any two conformal theories with gravity duals equals the square root of the ratio of their central charges.

## Method

The authors compute expectation values of Wilson loops in hot N=4 SYM theory using the AdS/CFT correspondence, mapping the problem onto a Nambu-Goto action in a five-dimensional anti-deSitter spacetime containing a black hole. They evaluate the action for two distinct limits: taking the dipole rapidity to infinity first yields a space-like world sheet bounded by a light-like Wilson loop defining the jet quenching parameter, while taking the boundary cutoff to infinity first yields a time-like world sheet defining the quark-antiquark potential for a moving dipole. The calculation is extended to include a flowing plasma with collective velocity, and the results are compared across different conformal theories with gravity duals.

## Key numbers

- The jet quenching parameter in N=4 SYM is modified by a factor gamma_f (1 - v_f cos theta) when the quark-gluon plasma flows with velocity v_f at an angle theta relative to the hard parton trajectory.
- The ratio of jet quenching parameters between any two conformal theories with gravity duals is the square root of the ratio of their central charges.
- The conjectured value of the jet quenching parameter in QCD is smaller than that in N=4 SYM by a factor of order sqrt(120/47.5) approximately 1.6, based on the ratio of central charges.
- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio takes the universal value 1/(4 pi) in the large N_c and large 't Hooft coupling limit of any gauge theory with a holographically dual supergravity description.

## Context

This work extends the AdS/CFT program for heavy ion phenomenology that began with calculations of shear viscosity by Policastro (2001), Kovtun (2003), and others, and builds on earlier AdS/CFT calculations of the jet quenching parameter by Liu (2006) and of heavy quark bound states by Rey (1998) and Mateos (2006). The results enable quantitative predictions for jet quenching and quarkonium suppression at RHIC and the LHC, and establish a connection between the jet quenching parameter and the central charge of the gauge theory, suggesting a universal scaling that can guide calculations in nonconformal theories closer to QCD.
