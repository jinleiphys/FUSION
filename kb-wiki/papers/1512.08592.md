# Phenomenological QCD equations of state for neutron stars

> We delineate the properties of QCD matter at baryon density n_B=1-10n_0 (n_0: nuclear saturation density), through the construction of neutron star equations of state that satisfy the neutron star mass-radius constraints as well as physical conditions on the speed of sound. The QCD matter is described in the 3-window modeling: at n_B < 2n_0 purely nuclear matter; at n_B > 5n_0 percolated quark matter; and at 2n_0 < n_B < 5n_0 matter intermediate between these two which are constructed by interpolation. Using a schematic quark model with effective interactions inspired from hadron and nuclear physics, we analyze the strength of interactions necessary to describe observed neutron star properties. Our finding is that the interactions should remain as strong as in the QCD vacuum, indicating that gluons at n_B =1-10 n_0 remain non-perturbative even after quark matter formation.

## Key claim

The interactions in QCD matter at baryon densities of 1-10 times nuclear saturation density must remain as strong as in the QCD vacuum, indicating that gluons remain non-perturbative even after quark matter formation, based on constructing neutron star equations of state that satisfy mass-radius constraints and speed-of-sound conditions.

## Method

The authors use a 3-window modeling approach: purely nuclear matter below 2 times nuclear saturation density (using the SLy equation of state for 0-0.5n0 and the Akmal-Pandharipande-Ravenhall equation of state for 0.5-2.0n0), percolated quark matter above 5n0 described by a schematic quark model with effective interactions, and an interpolated region between 2n0 and 5n0. The quark model includes NJL-type interactions, diquark couplings, and repulsive vector interactions, treated in mean-field approximation with charge neutrality, beta-equilibrium, and color neutrality constraints.

## Key numbers

- Baryon density range studied: n_B = 1-10 n_0, where n_0 = 0.16 fm^-3 (nuclear saturation density)
- Three-window boundaries: purely nuclear at n_B < 2n_0, percolated quark matter at n_B > 5n_0, intermediate region at 2n_0 < n_B < 5n_0
- Neutron star radius from the model: approximately 11.3 km
- Vector coupling required to satisfy the 2 solar mass constraint: G_V > 0.5 G_s
- Diquark coupling required to remove unphysical inflection points: H approximately 1.5 G_s
- Two observed neutron stars of approximately 2 solar masses require stiff equations of state at n_B > 4n_0
- Observed neutron star radii constraints: 10-13 km

## Context

This work extends the 3-window modeling approach of Masuda, Hatsuda, and Takatsuka (2012) for QCD matter in neutron stars, and contrasts with conventional hybrid modeling using Maxwell constructions which cannot simultaneously satisfy soft low-density and stiff high-density constraints. The finding that quark model couplings at high density remain as large as vacuum values supports the concept of non-perturbative quark matter originally proposed by McLerran and Pisarski (2007). The results enable future work connecting precise neutron star radius measurements to the nature of the QCD phase diagram, particularly whether the hadron-to-quark matter transition is a crossover or first-order phase transition.
