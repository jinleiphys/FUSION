# Meson-baryon scattering up to the next-to-next-to-leading order in covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory

> We study the scattering of a pseudoscalar meson off one ground state octet baryon in covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory (BChPT) up to the next-to-next-to-leading order. The inherent power counting breaking terms are removed within extended-on-mass-shell scheme. We perform the first combined study of the pion-nucleon and kaon-nucleon scattering data in covariant BChPT and show that it can provide a reasonable description of the experimental data. In addition, we find that it is possible to fit the experimental baryon masses and the pion-nucleon and kaon-nucleon scattering data simultaneously at this order, thus providing a consistent check on covariant BChPT. We compare the scattering lengths of all the pertinent channels with available experimental data and those of other approaches. In addition, we have studied the leading order contributions of the virtual decuplet and found that they can improve the description of the $πN$ phase shifts near the $Δ(1232)$ peak, while they have negligible effects on the description of the $K N$ phase shifts.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory (BChPT) with the extended-on-mass-shell (EOMS) scheme can provide a reasonable simultaneous description of pion-nucleon and kaon-nucleon scattering data, as well as baryon masses, up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO), but only after the inherent power counting breaking terms are removed via the EOMS scheme and the description of πN phase shifts near the Δ(1232) peak requires the leading-order contributions of the virtual decuplet.

## Method

The authors calculated meson-baryon scattering amplitudes in covariant BChPT up to 𝒪(p³) using the EOMS scheme to remove power counting breaking terms, employing chiral Lagrangians up to 𝒪(p³) with 24 independent low-energy constants (LECs) contributing to πN and KN scattering. They performed a combined fit to experimental baryon masses and πN and KN scattering data, using partial wave phase shifts from the George Washington University group to fix the relevant LECs, and included leading-order virtual decuplet contributions to study their effect on phase shifts near the Δ(1232) resonance.

## Key numbers

- The chiral order ν for any Feynman diagram is given by ν = 4L + Σ n V_n - 2N_M - N_B, where L is the number of loops, V_n the number of n-th order vertices, N_M internal meson lines, and N_B internal baryon lines.
- The expansion parameters are: s - m̃² ∼ 𝒪(p), t ∼ 𝒪(p²), m_π, m_K, m_η ∼ 𝒪(p), and m_N,Λ,Σ,Ξ - m̃ ∼ 𝒪(p²), where m̃ is the chiral limit baryon mass (set to m_N for πN and KN scattering).
- For πN scattering, 8 independent combinations of LECs (α₁ through α₈) contribute, with units ranging from GeV⁻¹ to GeV⁻⁴; for KN scattering in I=0, 8 combinations (β₁ through β₈) contribute; for KN scattering in I=1, 8 combinations (γ₁ through γ₈) contribute.
- The baryon mass insertions at 𝒪(p²) are: Δ_N = 4m_K²(b₀+b_D-b_F) + 2m_π²(b₀+2b_F), Δ_Σ = 2m_π²(b₀+2b_D) + 4b₀m_K², Δ_Λ = (2/3)(m_K²(6b₀+8b_D) + m_π²(3b₀-2b_D)), Δ_Ξ = 4m_K²(b₀+b_D+b_F) + 2m_π²(b₀-2b_F).

## Context

This work extends the application of EOMS BChPT from SU(2) pion-nucleon scattering (previously studied by Alarcon (2011, 2012), Chen (2012), Yao (2016), and Siemens (2016, 2017)) to SU(3) meson-baryon scattering, filling the gap for kaon-nucleon scattering in this framework. It builds on prior studies of baryon masses in covariant BChPT by Ren (2012-2014) and Ling (2017), and on recent attempts to construct baryon-baryon interactions using covariant BChPT by Ren (2016), Li (2016, 2018), Song (2018), and Xiao (2018). The results enable consistent checks of covariant BChPT across multiple sectors and provide a foundation for studying hyperon-nucleon interactions and the equation of state of dense matter relevant to the hyperon puzzle in neutron stars.
