# Density Functional Theory studies of cluster states in nuclei

> The framework of nuclear energy density functionals is applied to a study of the formation and evolution of cluster states in nuclei. The relativistic functional DD-ME2 is used in triaxial and reflection-asymmetric relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov calculations of relatively light $N = Z$ and neutron-rich nuclei. The role of deformation and degeneracy of single-nucleon states in the formation of clusters is analysed, and interesting cluster structures are predicted in excited configurations of Be, C, O, Ne, Mg, Si, S, Ar and Ca $N = Z$ nuclei. Cluster phenomena in neutron-rich nuclei are discussed, and it is shown that in neutron-rich Be and C nuclei cluster states occur as a result of molecular bonding of $α$-particles by the excess neutrons, and also that proton covalent bonding can occur in $^{10}$C.

## Key claim

The paper claims that nuclear energy density functional (EDF) calculations, particularly with the relativistic functional DD-ME2, can predict cluster structures in excited configurations of light N=Z and neutron-rich nuclei, but this result is conditional on the depth of the self-consistent mean-field potential: deeper potentials produce more pronounced spatial localization of single-nucleon wave functions, and the relativistic functional DD-ME2 yields the deepest potential among the three functionals tested (SLy4, D1S, DD-ME2).

## Method

The authors performed deformation-constrained relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations using the relativistic functional DD-ME2, imposing constraints on axial quadrupole and octupole moments to map energy surfaces and identify local minima corresponding to cluster configurations. They also compared results with non-relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations using the Skyrme functional SLy4 and the Gogny interaction D1S, and analyzed the mean energy gap between consecutive occupied neutron single-particle levels as a function of quadrupole deformation to correlate level degeneracy with cluster formation.

## Key numbers

- The relativistic functional DD-ME2 produces a self-consistent mean-field potential depth of -82.4 MeV for $^{36}$Ar, compared to -72.4 MeV for the Skyrme functional SLy4
- The equilibrium shape of $^{36}$Ar is a slightly oblate axially symmetric quadrupole ellipsoid with $\beta_2 \approx -0.2$ for all three functionals
- The mean value of the energy gap between consecutive occupied neutron levels in $^{12}$C and $^{20}$Ne shows sharp increases at deformations where alpha-cluster formation becomes pronounced, with the plot limited to a maximum mean gap of 5 MeV
- The $^8$Be parity-projected energy surface displays a deep minimum at very large quadrupole deformation corresponding to a two-alpha configuration

## Context

This work extends the Ikeda diagram (Ikeda 1968) concept of cluster-state coexistence with mean-field structures by providing a microscopic EDF-based analogue, and builds on previous studies by Ebran et al. (2013, 2014) and Natowitz et al. on the localization parameter and cluster formation. The results enable systematic exploration of cluster structures across the nuclear chart using EDF methods, and the predictions for neutron-rich Be and C isotopes (molecular bonding of alpha particles by excess neutrons) are consistent with Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics calculations by Yoshida et al. (2012) and molecular-orbit model studies by Itagaki et al. (2001).
