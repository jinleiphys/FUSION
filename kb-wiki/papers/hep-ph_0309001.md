# Are $Θ^+$ and the Roper resonance diquark-diquark-antiquark states?

> We consider a $[ud]^2\bar{s}$ current in the QCD sum rule framework to study the mass of the recently observed pentaquark state $Θ^+(1540)$, obtaining good agreement with the experimental value. We also study the mass of the pentaquark $[ud]^2\bar{d}$. Our results are compatible with the interpretation of the $[ud]^2\bar{d}$ state as being the Roper resonance N(1440), as suggested by Jaffe and Wilczek.

## Key claim

Using a diquark-diquark-antiquark interpolating current in the QCD sum rule framework, the authors reproduce the experimental mass of the Θ⁺(1540) and obtain a mass for the [ud]²\bar{d} state compatible with the Roper resonance N(1440), supporting the Jaffe-Wilczek interpretation. The results depend on the choice of continuum threshold and current parameter, and the mass difference between the two states is attributed entirely to the strange quark mass.

## Method

The authors construct two independent interpolating currents for the Θ⁺ with diquark-diquark-antiquark structure following Jaffe and Wilczek, then take a linear combination with a mixing parameter t. They compute the two-point correlation function in the QCD sum rule framework, performing an operator product expansion (OPE) up to dimension-6 condensates including quark and gluon condensates, and apply a Borel transform to suppress higher-dimension contributions. The sum rule for the N⁺ (Roper candidate) is obtained from the same formalism by setting the strange quark mass to zero.

## Key numbers

- Θ⁺ mass: m_Θ = 1.55 ± 0.10 GeV, obtained with continuum threshold s_{0Θ} in the range 3.6–4.4 GeV² and current parameter t in the range –1.1 to –0.8, at Borel mass M² = 2.5 GeV².
- N⁺ (Roper candidate) mass: m_N = 1.44 ± 0.11 GeV, obtained with continuum threshold s_{0N} in the range 3.2–4.0 GeV² and same t range, at M² = 2.5 GeV².
- Mass difference: m_Θ – m_N = 110 ± 10 MeV, consistent with the experimental difference between Θ⁺(1540) and N(1440).
- Input parameters: m_s = 0.15 GeV, ⟨\bar{q}q⟩ = –(0.23)³ GeV³, ⟨\bar{s}s⟩ = 0.8 ⟨\bar{q}q⟩, m₀² = 0.8 GeV², ⟨g²G²⟩ = 0.5 GeV⁴.

## Context

This work extends the semi-qualitative diquark-diquark-antiquark model proposed by Jaffe and Wilczek (2003) by providing a quantitative QCD sum rule calculation. It contrasts with the triquark–q\bar{q} configuration used by Zhu (2003), which found a similar Θ⁺ mass but predicted a much smaller mass difference between Θ⁺ and the Roper because that sum rule did not depend on the strange quark mass. The results enable future QCD sum rule calculations for the other antidecuplet members (Ξ⁺ and Ξ⁻⁻), whose masses were estimated as ~2070 MeV by Diakonov, Petrov and Polyakov (1997) and ~1750 MeV by Jaffe and Wilczek (2003).
