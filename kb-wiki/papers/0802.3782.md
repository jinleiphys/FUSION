# Saturation of nuclear matter in effective field theory

> The two- and three-nucleon interaction derived in chiral effective field theory at next-to-next-to-leading order is used to obtain the binding energy of nuclear matter. Saturation is found at a binding energy per particle E/A = -16.2 \pm 0.3 MeV and a Fermi momentum k_F = 1.30 \pm 0.03 fm^{-1}, where the uncertainty is due to the cut-off dependence of the two-nucleon interaction. The sensitivity of these values to the three-nucleon force is also studied.

## Key claim

Using the two- and three-nucleon interactions derived from chiral effective field theory at next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO), nuclear matter saturates at a binding energy per particle and Fermi momentum close to the empirical values, with the quoted uncertainties arising only from the cut-off dependence of the two-nucleon interaction, while the sensitivity to the three-nucleon force constants is explored but not included in the final error bar.

## Method

The authors solve the Lippmann-Schwinger equation in partial-wave representation using the matrix inversion method of Haftel and Tabakin to obtain the R-matrix for the chiral N2LO two-nucleon interaction, incorporating an angle-averaged Pauli operator. The contribution of the three-nucleon force at N2LO is computed using a regulator function and the pion-nucleon low-energy constants c1, c3, c4, plus the short-range constants cD and cE, with the total energy per particle being the sum of kinetic energy and the two- and three-body contributions.

## Key numbers

- Saturation binding energy per particle: E/A = -16.2 ± 0.3 MeV (from abstract) or -16.2 ± 0.4 MeV (from conclusions), with Fermi momentum k_F = 1.30 ± 0.03 fm^{-1}, where the uncertainty is due to cut-off dependence of the two-nucleon interaction.
- For the central choice c_E = -c_D = 0.2, the saturation point is at k_F = 1.29 fm^{-1} with E/A = -16.11 MeV.
- Binding energies per particle for the five regulator combinations scatter between -15.8 MeV and -16.6 MeV, with Fermi momenta between 1.27 fm^{-1} and 1.33 fm^{-1}.
- Compression modulus K = 197 MeV, with spread K_max - K_min = 37 MeV (range 179 to 216 MeV).
- Skewness parameter S = 914 ± 217 MeV.
- Critical Fermi momentum for transition to a deuteron gas: (k_F)_critical = 0.51 fm^{-1}.
- Using only the longest-range part of the three-nucleon force (c_D = 0, c_E = 0): E/A = -15.36 MeV, k_F = 1.27 fm^{-1}.

## Context

This work extends chiral effective field theory, previously successful for few-nucleon systems and nucleon-nucleon phase shifts, to the nuclear matter problem. It contrasts with earlier approaches such as the Munich group (Kaiser, 2001; Kaiser, 2006) which developed a chiral interaction for the medium without a vacuum two-nucleon link, and with the V_low-k approach of Bogner (2005) which used renormalization-group-evolved potentials. The results also differ from the LO calculations of Lacour (2010) and Machleidt (2010), which found saturation at lower Fermi momenta or underbinding, respectively. The present N2LO results overlap with the empirical saturation region from the finite-range droplet model of Moller, Myers, Swiatecki, and Treiner (1988) and with microscopic mean-field approaches reviewed by Bender, Heenen, and Reinhard (2003), enabling further study of the role of the delta-isobar and improved determination of the three-nucleon force constants.
