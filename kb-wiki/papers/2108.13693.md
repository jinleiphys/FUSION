# Charm and beauty in the deconfined plasma from quenched lattice QCD

> We present continuum extrapolated results of charmonium and bottomonium correlators in the vector channel at several temperatures below and above $T_c$. The continuum extrapolation jointly performed with the interpolations to have physical values of $J/ψ$ and $Υ$ masses in the confined phase is based on calculations on several large quenched isotropic lattices using clover-improved Wilson valence fermions carrying different quark masses. The extrapolated lattice correlators are confronted with perturbation theory results incorporating resummed thermal effects around the threshold from pNRQCD and vacuum asymptotics above the threshold. An additional transport peak is modelled below the threshold allowing for an estimate of the diffusion coefficients for charm and bottom quarks. We find that charmonium correlators in the vector channel can be well reproduced by perturbative spectral functions above $T_c$ where no resonance peaks for $J/ψ$ are needed at and above 1.1 $T_c$, while for bottomonium correlators a resonance peak for $Υ$ is still needed up to 1.5 $T_c$. By analyzing the transport contribution to the correlators we find that the drag coefficient of a charm quark is larger than that of a bottom quark.

## Key claim

The paper claims that charmonium vector correlators above the deconfining temperature can be reproduced by perturbative spectral functions without any resonance peak for J/ψ at and above 1.1 Tc, while bottomonium correlators require a resonance peak for Υ up to 1.5 Tc; the drag coefficient of a charm quark is larger than that of a bottom quark, but the analysis is limited by the fact that the curvature of the transport correlator provides no additional information to determine the absolute value of the heavy quark diffusion coefficient.

## Method

The authors performed quenched lattice QCD calculations on several large isotropic lattices using clover-improved Wilson valence fermions at four different lattice spacings and five to six different quark masses per spacing. They carried out a combined mass interpolation and continuum extrapolation of the vector channel Euclidean correlators for charmonium and bottomonium at five temperatures from 0.75 to 2.25 Tc. The continuum-extrapolated correlators were compared to perturbative spectral functions constructed from pNRQCD near threshold and vacuum asymptotics at high frequencies, with a Lorentzian transport peak modeled at low frequencies to estimate diffusion coefficients.

## Key numbers

- Charmonium correlators require no J/ψ resonance peak at and above 1.1 Tc; bottomonium correlators require a Υ resonance peak up to 1.5 Tc.
- At 1.5 Tc, the fit parameter A for charmonium is 1.03(2) and B/T is 0.01(6); for bottomonium A is 1.00(2) and B/T is -0.12(2).
- At 2.25 Tc, the fit parameter A for charmonium is 0.99(3) and B/T is -0.27(9); for bottomonium A is 0.99(2) and B/T is -0.23(4).
- The lattice spacings used range from 0.018 fm (a^{-1}=11.19 GeV) at β=7.192 to 0.009 fm (a^{-1}=22.78 GeV) at β=7.793.
- The quark masses used for the transport analysis are Mc=1.28 GeV and Mb=4.18 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Burnier (2017) for pseudoscalar correlators to the vector channel for both charm and bottom quarks, using continuum-extrapolated quenched lattice QCD results. It enables future comparisons with unquenched calculations and provides a framework for extracting heavy quark diffusion coefficients from lattice correlators, though the current precision does not allow a determination of the absolute diffusion coefficient D. The results are consistent with the earlier heavy quark momentum diffusion coefficient estimates from Francis (2015) and Brambilla (2020), which gave a range of 2πTD in [3.71, 6.91] at 1.5 Tc.
