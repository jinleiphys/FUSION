# Detection of the 4.4-MeV gamma rays from $^{16}$O($Î―, Î―^{\prime}$)$^{16}$O(12.97 ${\rm MeV}, 2^-)$ with a water-Cherenkov detector in the supernova neutrino bursts

> We first discuss and determine the isospin mixing of the two $2^-$ states (12.53 MeV and 12.97 MeV) of $^{16}$O nucleus using the inelastic electron scattering data. We then evaluate the cross section of 4.4-MeV $Îģ$ rays produced in the neutrino neutral-current (NC) reaction $^{16}$O($Î―, Î―^{\prime}$)$^{16}$O$(12.97 {\rm MeV}, 2^-$) in a water Cherenkov detector at the low energy below 100 MeV. The detection of $Îģ$ rays for $E_Îģ>5$ MeV from the NC reaction $^{16}$O($Î―, Î―^{\prime}$)$^{16}$O$(E_x>16\ {\rm MeV}, T=1$) with a water Cherenkov detector in the supernova neutrino bursts has been proposed and discussed by several authors previously. In this article, we discuss a new NC reaction channel from $^{16}$O(12.97 ${\rm MeV}, 2^-$) producing a 4.4-MeV $Îģ$ ray, the cross section of which is more robust and even larger at the low energy ($E_Î―<25$ MeV) than the NC cross section from $^{16}$O$(E_x>16\ {\rm MeV}, T=1$). We also evaluate the number of such events induced by neutrinos from supernova explosion which can be observed by the Super-Kamiokande, a 32 kton water Cherenkov detector in the Earth.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the 4.4-MeV gamma rays from the neutral-current reaction $^{16}$O($\nu, \nu^{\prime}$)$^{16}$O(12.97 MeV, $2^-$) provide a new detection channel for supernova neutrinos, with a cross section that is more robust and even larger at low neutrino energies ($E_{\nu}<25$ MeV) than the previously proposed NC channel from $^{16}$O($E_x>16$ MeV, $T=1$), but the prediction depends on an uncertain branching ratio Br($\alpha_1$) for the 12.97-MeV state decaying to $\alpha + ^{12}$C(4.4 MeV), which the authors take as a simple mean of three inconsistent measurements (0.35).

## Method

The authors determined the isospin mixing parameter $\beta$ between the two $2^-$ states (12.53 MeV and 12.97 MeV) of $^{16}$O by fitting published inelastic electron scattering data ($e, e^{\prime}$) from Stroetzel (1970) and Kim et al. (1975) for the reduced transition probability $\sqrt{B(M2,q)}$. They then calculated the neutral-current neutrino-oxygen cross section for the 12.97-MeV state using shell-model calculations (SFO-tls for $T=1$ and YSOX for $T=0$ Hamiltonians) and folded this cross section with supernova neutrino flux spectra from the Keil-Raffelt-Janka parametrization and the Nakazato et al. (2013) database to estimate the number of 4.4-MeV gamma-ray events observable in a 32 kton water Cherenkov detector (Super-Kamiokande) at 10 kpc distance.

## Key numbers

* Isospin mixing parameter $\beta = 0.25 \pm 0.05$ between the 12.97 MeV ($T=1$) and 12.53 MeV ($T=0$) $2^-$ states of $^{16}$O, determined from ($e, e^{\prime}$) data.
* Branching ratio Br($\alpha_1$) = $\Gamma_{\alpha_1}/\Gamma = 0.35$ for the 12.97-MeV state decaying to $\alpha + ^{12}$C(4.4 MeV), taken as the simple mean of three measurements: 0.37$\pm$0.06 (Leavitt et al.), 0.22$\pm$0.04 (Zijderhand and van der Leun, 1987), and 0.46$\pm$0.08 (Charity et al.).
* The NC cross section of the 12.97-MeV state is larger than that of the excited states ($E_x>16$ MeV) for $E_{\nu} < 25$ MeV; the latter becomes larger for $E_{\nu} > 25$ MeV.
* The ratio of the 4.4-MeV production cross section from the 12.53-MeV state to that from the 12.97-MeV state is 0.16, adding about 16% to the total.
* For the KRJ fit with $\alpha=3$ and $\langle E_\nu\rangle=12$ MeV, the number of 4.4-MeV gamma-ray events from the 12.97-MeV state is shown in Fig. 6 as a function of average neutrino energy (specific numerical event counts are not given in the truncated text).

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Langanke, Kolbe and Vogel (1996) to detect gamma rays above 5 MeV from the NC reaction $^{16}$O($\nu, \nu^{\prime}$)$^{16}$O($E_x>16$ MeV, $T=1$) in water Cherenkov detectors for supernova neutrino bursts, and the subsequent estimates by Beacom and Vogel (1999). The authors introduce a new channel producing 4.4-MeV gamma rays from the 12.97-MeV $2^-$ state, which has a lower energy threshold (12.97 MeV) and a more robust cross section calculation based on measured electromagnetic form factors and weak reaction rates, unlike the higher-energy states ($E_x>16$ MeV) whose form factors and gamma-ray emission probabilities are less well measured. This work enables Super-Kamiokande (now SK-Gd) to potentially detect 4.4-MeV gamma rays from supernova neutrinos, taking advantage of the lowered analysis threshold to 3.5 MeV in SK-IV.
