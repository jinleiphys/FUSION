# Effectiveness of parton cascade in solving the relativistic Boltzmann equation in a box

> We benchmark the ZPC parton cascade with an exact analytical solution of the relativistic Boltzmann equation for a homogeneous and massless gas with a constant and isotropic elastic cross section. We measure the accuracy of ZPC with the relative mean deviation between its momentum distribution and the exact solution. We use two generalized collision schemes to further improve the accuracy of ZPC over the recent $t$-minimum collision scheme. We find that ZPC can reproduce very well the time evolution of the single-particle distribution function for the exact solution's initial condition, with one generalized collision scheme giving an accuracy better than $1\%$ for the momentum distribution at any time in all studied cases, including very high opacities where naively the parton cascade approach is expected to fail.

# Effectiveness of parton cascade in solving the relativistic Boltzmann equation in a box

## Key claim

The ZPC parton cascade can reproduce the time evolution of the single-particle distribution function with a relative mean deviation better than 1% for all studied opacities up to χ=40, including regimes where parton cascades are naively expected to fail due to causality violation, but only when using a generalized opacity-dependent collision scheme rather than the simpler t-minimum scheme.

## Method

The authors benchmarked the ZPC parton cascade against an exact analytical solution of the relativistic Boltzmann equation for a homogeneous, massless gas with constant and isotropic elastic cross section in a box with periodic boundary conditions (non-expanding case with scale factor a(t)=1). They introduced two generalized collision schemes parameterized by a variable r that interpolates between the t-minimum scheme (r=0) and the t-average scheme (r=1/2), where the collision time is tc = min(tc1, tc2) + r|tc1 - tc2|, and determined optimal r values as functions of opacity χ by minimizing either the equilibrium or maximum relative mean deviation. The accuracy was quantified using the relative mean deviation (RMD) between the ZPC momentum distribution and the exact analytical solution across momentum bins.

## Key numbers

- The t-minimum collision scheme yields a relative mean deviation of ≤ 2% in equilibrium for all opacities studied.
- The peak generalized collision scheme achieves a relative mean deviation of ≤ 1% for the momentum distribution at any time for all opacities up to χ=40.
- The equilibrium scheme reduces the equilibrium RMD by a factor of 3 to 6 compared to the t-minimum scheme for moderate to high opacities (χ ≥ 1).
- The optimal r values are parameterized as r_optimal(χ) = 873/(15.8+χ)^3.40 for the equilibrium scheme and r_optimal(χ) = 518/(15.8+χ)^3.40 for the peak scheme.
- Simulations used 1000 events with 16000 gluons per event, run until τ=60.
- The opacity is given by χ = (16/π²)T³√(σ³/π), and the time variable τ relates to global time t as τ = t T (16χ²/π)^(1/3).

## Context

This work extends the benchmarking of transport models against exact solutions of the relativistic Boltzmann equation, following the recent analytical solution by Bazow et al. (2015, 2016) for a massless gas and the comparison of the SMASH hadron transport with this solution by Tindall et al. (2016). It improves upon the earlier ZPC study by Zhao et al. (2020) that introduced the t-minimum collision scheme and used parton subdivision as a reference, by providing direct comparison to an exact analytical solution rather than relying on subdivision results. The work enables application of ZPC to realistic 3-dimensional expansion cases without needing computationally expensive parton subdivision, which alters event-by-event correlations and fluctuations important for nuclear collision studies.
