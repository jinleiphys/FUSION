# Propagation of heavy baryons in heavy-ion collisions

> The drag and diffusion coefficients of heavy baryons ($Λ_c$ and $Λ_b$) in the hadronic phase created in the latter stage of the heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies have been evaluated recently. In this work we compute some experimental observables, such as the nuclear suppression factor $R_{AA}$ and the elliptic flow $v_2$ of heavy baryons at RHIC and LHC energies, highlighting the role of the hadronic phase contribution to these observables, which are going to be measured at Run 3 of LHC. For the time evolution of the heavy quarks in the QGP and heavy baryons in the hadronic phase we use the Langevin dynamics. For the hadronization of the heavy quarks to heavy baryons we employ Peterson fragmentation functions. We observe a strong suppression of both the $Λ_c$ and $Λ_b$. We find that the hadronic medium has a sizable impact on the heavy-baryon elliptic flow whereas the impact of hadronic medium rescattering is almost unnoticeable on the nuclear suppression factor. We evaluate the $Λ_c/D$ ratio at RHIC and LHC. We find that $Λ_c/D$ ratio remain unaffected due to the hadronic phase rescattering which enable it as a nobel probe of QGP phase dynamics along with its hadronization.

## Key claim

The hadronic phase has a sizable impact on the heavy-baryon elliptic flow ($v_2$) but almost no effect on the nuclear suppression factor ($R_{AA}$) for both $\Lambda_c$ and $\Lambda_b$ baryons at RHIC and LHC energies, with the $\Lambda_c/D$ ratio remaining unaffected by hadronic rescattering and thus serving as a probe of QGP phase dynamics.

## Method

The authors used Langevin dynamics to simulate the time evolution of heavy quarks in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) and heavy baryons in the hadronic phase, with drag and diffusion coefficients for the QGP taken from a quasiparticle model and for the hadronic phase from unitarized effective field theories respecting chiral and heavy-quark symmetries. Hadronization of heavy quarks to heavy baryons was performed using Peterson fragmentation functions, with $\epsilon_c=0.02$ for $\Lambda_c$ and $\epsilon_c=0.006$ for $\Lambda_b$, and the background medium evolution was described by a 3D+1 relativistic transport code with Glauber initial conditions.

## Key numbers

- For RHIC ($Au+Au$ at $\sqrt{s}=200$ AGeV): maximum initial temperature $T_0=340$ MeV, initial time $\tau_0=0.6$ fm/c, critical temperature $T_c=160$ MeV, kinetic freeze-out temperature $T_{kin}=120$ MeV
- For LHC ($Pb+Pb$ at $\sqrt{s}=5.5$ ATeV): maximum initial temperature $T_0=610$ MeV, initial time $\tau_0=0.25$ fm/c
- The $\Lambda_c$ fragmentation function uses $\epsilon_c=0.02$, a factor two larger than the $D$ meson value of $\epsilon_c=0.01$
- The $\Lambda_b$ fragmentation function uses $\epsilon_c=0.006$, a factor two larger than the $B$-meson fragmentation function
- At LHC energy, the hadronic phase enhances $v_2$ by up to 15%

## Context

This work extends the microscopic study of $\Lambda_c$ and $\Lambda_b$ interactions with light mesons ($\pi$, $K$, $\bar K$, $\eta$) from Tolos et al. (2016) to compute experimental observables for upcoming ALICE Run 3 measurements. It contrasts with previous work on heavy mesons ($D$ and $B$) by focusing on heavy baryons, and shows that the $\Lambda_c/D$ ratio remains unaffected by hadronic rescattering, making it a clean probe of QGP dynamics and hadronization, similar to how the light-flavor baryon-to-meson ratio has been studied by Greco et al. (2003).
