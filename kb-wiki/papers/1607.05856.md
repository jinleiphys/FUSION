# Structure of the $\mathbf{Λ(1405)}$ from Hamiltonian effective field theory

> The pole structure of the $Λ(1405)$ is examined by fitting the couplings of an underlying Hamiltonian effective field theory to cross sections of $K^- p$ scattering in the infinite-volume limit. Finite-volume spectra are then obtained from the theory, and compared to lattice QCD results for the mass of the $Λ(1405)$. Momentum-dependent, non-separable potentials motivated by the well-known Weinberg-Tomozawa terms are used, with SU(3) flavour symmetry broken in the couplings and masses. In addition, we examine the effect on the behaviour of the spectra from the inclusion of a bare triquark-like isospin-zero basis state. It is found that the cross sections are consistent with the experimental data with two complex poles for the $Λ(1405)$, regardless of whether a bare baryon basis state is introduced or not. However, it is apparent that the bare baryon is important for describing the results of lattice QCD at high pion masses.

## Key claim

The cross sections of K^- p scattering are consistent with experimental data with two complex poles for the Λ(1405) regardless of whether a bare baryon basis state is introduced, but the bare baryon is important for describing lattice QCD results at high pion masses where the Λ(1405) becomes a stable state below the πΣ decay threshold.

## Method

The authors constructed a Hamiltonian effective field theory with momentum-dependent, non-separable potentials derived from Weinberg-Tomozawa terms, including πΣ, K̅N, ηΛ, and KΞ channels for isospin-0 and πΣ, K̅N, and πΛ channels for isospin-1. They solved the coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation to obtain T-matrices and cross sections in infinite volume, then discretized the Hamiltonian in a finite box to compare with lattice QCD results from the CSSM group. Two scenarios were examined: one with the Λ(1405) dynamically generated purely from meson-baryon interactions, and one also including a bare triquark-like isospin-zero basis state.

## Key numbers

- Pole 1 (without bare state): 1428 − 23i MeV; Pole 2 (without bare state): 1333 − 85i MeV
- Pole 1 (with bare state): 1429 − 22i MeV; Pole 2 (with bare state): 1338 − 89i MeV
- Bare baryon mass m_B^0 = 1740 MeV (with regulator Λ = 1 GeV)
- χ² = 166 for 120 data points (no bare state); χ² = 177 (with bare state)
- Lattice QCD masses from CSSM: m_1 ranges from 1.446(46) GeV at m_π = 0.6233(7) GeV to 1.863(29) GeV at m_π = 0.1742(26) GeV; m_2 values are 1.686(33) GeV and 1.607(37) GeV at the two lightest pion masses
- α_B^0 = 0.55 GeV⁻¹ for the bare mass slope, approximately 2/3 of α_N(1535)^0 = 0.77 GeV⁻¹

## Context

This work extends the Hamiltonian effective field theory approach previously applied to the Λ(1405) by Hall (2015) and Hall (2014), which focused on flavour singlet couplings and identified the Λ(1405) as a molecular K̅N bound state. The present study uses momentum-dependent non-separable potentials rather than the on-shell approximation common in K-matrix approaches by Oset (1998) and Ikeda (2011), and examines the finite-volume spectrum without distinguishing flavour symmetry of the isospin-zero state. The results enable interpretation of which states are observed in lattice QCD calculations with local three-quark operators by Menadue (2012), showing that a bare baryon component resolves discrepancies at high pion masses where the model without a bare state fails to reproduce lattice results.
