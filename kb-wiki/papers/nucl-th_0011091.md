# Momentum distributions and reaction mechanisms for breakup of two--neutron halos

> A theoretical model able to describe fragmentation reactions of three--body halo nuclei on different targets, from light to heavy, is used to compute neutron and core momentum distributions. Both Coulomb and nuclear interactions are simultaneously included. We specify the different reaction mechanisms related to various processes. The method is applied to fragmentation of $^6$He and $^{11}$Li on C and Pb. We find good agreement with the available experimental results.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the similar shapes of momentum distributions from light and heavy targets arise from fundamentally different reaction mechanisms, nuclear neutron-target interaction for light targets and Coulomb core-target interaction for heavy targets, rather than from a single universal process, and that this similarity is a coincidence explained by the fact that in both cases the core's initial momentum distribution is left relatively unchanged.

## Method

The authors model fragmentation of two-neutron halo nuclei as a superposition of three independent reactions, each where only one constituent (participant) interacts with the target via an optical potential, while the other two are spectators. They simultaneously include Coulomb and nuclear interactions, and compute differential cross sections for absorption and elastic scattering of the participant, then integrate over remaining variables to obtain momentum distributions. The model uses a black-disk approximation with cutoff radii derived from experimental spectator-target absorption cross sections, and applies it to fragmentation of 6He and 11Li on carbon and lead targets at beam energies around 300 MeV/nucleon.

## Key numbers

- Cutoff radii for the black-disk cylinder: for neutrons, R_cut = 3.5 fm on C and 7.4 fm on Pb; for 4He core, R_cut = 4.0 fm on C and 7.6 fm on Pb; for 9Li core, R_cut = 4.8 fm on C and 7.9 fm on Pb.
- The momentum transfer dividing low and large impact parameters is given by q_g = Z_0 Z_c e^2 (gamma+1)/(c gamma beta (R_0+R_c+ pi a/2)).
- The neutron-neutron and neutron-9Li final state interactions both involve a low-lying virtual s-state at an energy of around 100 keV.
- Experimental neutron acceptance was 50 MeV/c in both horizontal and vertical directions.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previously developed consistent model (Garrido 2000, Garrido 2000b) that simultaneously treats Coulomb and nuclear breakup for two-neutron halos on any target, which had previously been treated separately for light targets (Garrido 1999, Bertsch 1998) and heavy targets (Suzuki 1990, Bertsch 1992, Baur 1998, Cobis 1998). The paper resolves the "almost forgotten problem" noted by Humbert (1995) that momentum distributions from light and heavy targets are surprisingly similar despite different reaction mechanisms. The results enable future work to include full three-body continuum final state wave functions for heavy targets where the neutron-core final state interaction is essential.
