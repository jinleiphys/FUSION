# Structure and rotations of the Hoyle state

> The excited state of the 12C nucleus known as the "Hoyle state" constitutes one of the most interesting, difficult and timely challenges in nuclear physics, as it plays a key role in the production of carbon via fusion of three alpha particles in red giant stars. In this letter, we present ab initio lattice calculations which unravel the structure of the Hoyle state, along with evidence for a low-lying spin-2 rotational excitation. For the 12C ground state and the first excited spin-2 state, we find a compact triangular configuration of alpha clusters. For the Hoyle state and the second excited spin-2 state, we find a "bent-arm" or obtuse triangular configuration of alpha clusters. We also calculate the electromagnetic transition rates between the low-lying states of 12C.

## Key claim

The Hoyle state and its spin-2 rotational excitation have strong overlap with a "bent-arm" or obtuse triangular configuration of alpha clusters, while the ground state and first spin-2 state have strong overlap with a compact triangular configuration; these conclusions are based on ab initio lattice calculations at leading order (LO) through next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in chiral effective field theory, with the caveat that the electromagnetic moments and transition rates were computed only at LO.

## Method

The authors performed ab initio lattice Monte Carlo calculations using chiral effective field theory interactions up to NNLO (O(Q^3)), on a periodic cubic lattice with spacing a = 1.97 fm and length L = 12 fm, with Euclidean time step a_t = 1.32 fm. They used a multi-channel method with diverse initial and final states (including delocalized nucleon standing waves and explicit alpha-cluster wave functions) to project onto low-energy states, and measured four-nucleon correlations via the expectation value of the total nucleon density to identify alpha clustering.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy of 12C at NNLO: -92(3) MeV (experimental: -92.16 MeV)
- Hoyle state (0_2^+) energy at NNLO: -85(3) MeV (experimental: -84.51 MeV)
- First spin-2 state (2_1^+) energy at NNLO: -89(3) MeV (experimental: -87.72 MeV)
- Second spin-2 state (2_2^+) energy at NNLO: -83(3) MeV (experimental values range from -82.6(1) to -81.1(3) to -82.32(6) MeV)
- Ground state charge radius at LO: 2.2(2) fm (experimental: 2.47(2) fm)
- Hoyle state charge radius at LO: 2.4(2) fm (no experimental value given)
- Quadrupole moment of 2_1^+ state at LO: 6(2) e fm^2 (experimental: 6(3) e fm^2)
- Quadrupole moment of 2_2^+ state at LO: -7(2) e fm^2 (no experimental value given)
- B(E2, 2_1^+ -> 0_1^+) at LO: 5(2) e^2 fm^4 (experimental: 7.6(4) e^2 fm^4)
- B(E2, 2_2^+ -> 0_2^+) at LO: 6(2) e^2 fm^4 (no experimental value given)
- Monopole matrix element m(E0, 0_2^+ -> 0_1^+) at LO: 3(1) e fm^2 (experimental: 5.5(1) e fm^2)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous ab initio lattice calculation of the Hoyle state (Epelbaum 2011) by introducing a major innovation that constructs and tests a wide class of nuclear wave functions explicitly, resolving the alpha cluster structure. The results can be compared with other recent theoretical calculations using the no-core shell model (Roth 2011, Forssen 2011) and variational calculations with Fermionic Molecular Dynamics (Chernykh 2007, 2010). The findings confirm Morinaga's 1956 conjecture that excited alpha-nuclei may be non-spherical with low-lying rotational excitations, and provide a first-principles understanding that enables future calculations with smaller lattice spacings and detailed studies of the resonances as a function of lattice volume.
