# Multiple parton interactions in high-density QCD matter

> Multiple interactions of quarks and gluons in high-energy heavy-ion collisions may give rise to interesting phemomena of color charges propagating in high-density QCD matter. We study the dynamics of multi-parton systems produced in nucleus-nucleus collisions at energies corresponding the the CERN SPS and the future BNL RHIC experiments. Due to the complexity of the multi-particle dynamics we choose to employ the parton cascade model in order to simulate the development of multiple parton scatterings and associated stimulated emision processes. Our results indicate a non-linear increase with nuclear mass A of, e.g., parton multiplicity, energy density, strangeness, and contrast a linear A-scaling as in Glauber-type approaches. If multiple interactions are suppressed and only single parton scatterings (no re-interactions) are considered, we recover such a linear behavior. It remains to be studied whether these results on the parton level can be experimentally seen in final-state observables, such as the charged particle multiplicity, the magnitude of produced transverse energy, or the number of produced strange hadrons.

## Key claim

Multiple parton interactions in high-energy heavy-ion collisions produce a non-linear increase with nuclear mass number A of parton multiplicity, energy density, and strangeness, contrasting with the linear A-scaling predicted by Glauber-type approaches; however, the authors hedge that it remains to be studied whether these parton-level results can be experimentally seen in final-state observables such as charged particle multiplicity, transverse energy, or strange hadron yields.

## Method

The authors employed the Parton Cascade Model (PCM) implemented in the Monte Carlo program VNI to simulate the space-time evolution of internetted parton cascades in central nucleus-nucleus collisions at CERN SPS (sqrt(s) = 20 A GeV) and BNL RHIC (sqrt(s) = 200 A GeV) energies. The model uses renormalization-group improved perturbative QCD with a minimum momentum transfer cutoff p0 = 1.1 GeV/c for SPS and p0 = 2.1 GeV/c for RHIC, including only (semi)hard parton-parton scatterings with q^2 >= p0^2, and incorporates multiple scatterings, gluon bremsstrahlung, and absorption processes while neglecting non-perturbative soft scatterings to avoid model dependence.

## Key numbers

- Minimum momentum transfer cutoff: p0 = 1.1 GeV/c for sqrt(s) = 20 A GeV and p0 = 2.1 GeV/c for sqrt(s) = 200 A GeV
- Maximum number of produced partons around z=0 in central gold-gold collisions: approximately 600 at 20 A GeV and close to 4000 at 200 A GeV, an increase factor of 6.5
- Energy density at z=0: approximately 8 GeV/fm^3 at 20 A GeV and approximately 50 GeV/fm^3 at 200 A GeV
- Time to reach maximum parton materialization: around 0.5 fm/c at 20 A GeV and 0.3 fm/c at 200 A GeV
- Enhancement of materialized parton numbers due to multiple scatterings versus single scatterings: up to 25-30% at 20 A GeV and up to 40% at 200 A GeV
- Radiative emissions contribute up to 30% of multiplicity at 20 A GeV and about 50% at 200 A GeV
- Non-linear departure from linear A-scaling sets in for systems A+A above 220, corresponding to collisions of Silver nuclei

## Context

This work extends the Parton Cascade Model approach developed by Geiger and collaborators (1990s) to study multiple parton interactions in high-density QCD matter, contrasting with Glauber-type models that predict linear A-scaling. The results provide initial conditions for subsequent hydrodynamic evolution and hadronization models, and the authors note that establishing connections to final-state hadronic and electromagnetic particles (photons, dileptons, strange hadrons) requires a suitable parton-hadron conversion model, which they postpone to future work.
