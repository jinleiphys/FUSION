# Exact interpolation between Fick and Cattaneo diffusion in relativistic kinetic theory

> We construct a family of exactly solvable relativistic kinetic theories in $1+1$ dimensions whose hydrodynamic sector continuously interpolates between Fick's and Cattaneo's laws of diffusion. The interpolation is controlled by a single parameter $a\in[0,1]$, which tunes the microscopic scattering dynamics from infinitely soft but infinitely frequent scatterings ($a=0$), reproducing standard diffusion, to maximally hard but finite-rate scatterings ($a=1$), yielding hyperbolic Cattaneo-type transport. For intermediate values of $a$, the dynamics combines frequent weak scatterings with rare strong randomizing events, providing a concrete microscopic realization of mixed diffusive-telegraphic behavior. Remarkably, the full quasinormal mode spectrum can be obtained analytically for all $a$. This allows us to track explicitly how purely diffusive modes continuously deform into damped propagating modes as the collision structure is varied.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a family of exactly solvable relativistic kinetic theories in 1+1 dimensions whose hydrodynamic sector continuously interpolates between Fick's and Cattaneo's laws of diffusion, controlled by a single parameter \(a\in[0,1]\). The interpolation is exact for the hydrodynamic mode, not merely an effective low-wavenumber description, and the full quasinormal mode spectrum is obtained analytically for all \(a\).

## Method

The author constructs a Boltzmann equation whose collision term is a weighted sum of a Fokker-Planck operator (infinitely soft, infinitely frequent scatterings) and an Anderson-Witting relaxation-time operator (maximally hard, finite-rate scatterings). The relative weight is parameterized as \(\nu = 4(1-a)/\tau\) and \(\Gamma = a/\tau\), where \(\tau\) is the microscopic relaxation time. By transforming the Boltzmann equation into a one-dimensional Schrodinger-like equation with a rank-one projector, the hydrodynamic mode is obtained as a bound-state solution, and the dispersion relation is derived analytically from the resulting polynomial constraint.

## Key numbers

* The diffusivity coefficient interpolates as \(\mathfrak{D}(a) = \tau / (2 - a + 2\sqrt{1-a})\), yielding \(\mathfrak{D}(0) = \tau/4\) (Fick limit) and \(\mathfrak{D}(1) = \tau\) (Cattaneo limit).
* For \(a < 2/3\), the hydrodynamic branch merges with the continuous spectrum at a finite imaginary wavenumber \((ik\tau)_{\text{max}}\), which diverges as \(a \to 2/3\).
* The continuous spectrum occupies the region \(i\omega \in \pm ik + [1/\tau, \infty)\) for all \(a < 1\), collapsing to \(i\omega = \pm ik + 1/\tau\) at \(a = 1\).
* The non-propagating branch at real wavenumber exists for \(1/2 < r \leq (2\sqrt{1-a})^{-1}\), where \(r\) is the real part of the bound-state exponents.

## Context

This work extends the author's prior derivations of exact Fick diffusion from Fokker-Planck kinetics (Gavassino, 2026) and exact Cattaneo diffusion from Anderson-Witting kinetics (Basar, 2024), showing they are opposite limits of a single family. The interpolating theory provides a concrete microscopic realization of mixed diffusive-telegraphic behavior and enables analytic tracking of how purely diffusive modes continuously deform into damped propagating modes as the collision structure varies. This addresses fundamental questions about causality and well-posedness of Fick's law versus Cattaneo's theory, and opens the door to studying stochastic fluctuations in the interpolating regime.
