# Neutrino-induced coherent pion production

> We have investigated the neutrino induced coherent pion production reaction at the energies of interest for recent experiments like K2K and MiniBooNE. The model includes pion, nucleon and the Delta(1232) resonance. Medium effects in the production mechanism and the distortion of the pion wave function are taken into account. We find a strong reduction of the cross section due to these effects and also substantial modifications in the energy distributions of the final pion. The sensitivity of the results on the axial N-Delta coupling C5A(0) and the coherent fraction in neutral-current pi0 production are discussed.

## Key claim

The authors find that nuclear medium effects (modification of the Delta(1232) resonance properties and pion distortion) cause a strong reduction of the neutrino-induced coherent pion production cross section, with the magnitude of the reduction depending sensitively on the value of the axial N-Delta coupling constant C5A(0), which remains poorly constrained.

## Method

The model includes pion, nucleon, and Delta(1232) resonance degrees of freedom, with the relativistic amplitude obtained as a coherent sum over all nucleons in the nucleus. Medium effects are incorporated by adding a density-dependent selfenergy to the Delta propagator, and pion distortion is treated by solving the Klein-Gordon equation with a microscopic optical potential based on the Delta-hole model. The authors compare two parametrizations of the axial N-Delta form factor C5A: set I with C5A(0)=1.2 (consistent with the Goldberger-Treiman relation) and set II with C5A(0)=0.867 (fitted to ANL data).

## Key numbers

* Cross section for CC coherent pi+ production on 12C averaged over the K2K flux: 10.8 x 10^-40 cm^2 (set I) and 5.7 x 10^-40 cm^2 (set II), compared to the experimental upper limit of 7.7 x 10^-40 cm^2
* Cross section for NC coherent pi0 production on 12C averaged over the MiniBooNE flux: 5.0 x 10^-40 cm^2 (set I) and 2.6 x 10^-40 cm^2 (set II)
* Cross section for NC coherent pi0 production on 27Al averaged over the Aachen-Padova flux: 19.9 x 10^-40 cm^2 (set I) and 10.1 x 10^-40 cm^2 (set II), compared to the experimental value of 29 +/- 10 x 10^-40 cm^2
* Coherent fraction in NC pi0 production at MiniBooNE: 0.14 (ratio of coherent to total coherent plus incoherent cross section)
* Medium effects reduce the cross section by approximately 35 percent due to Delta selfenergy modifications alone

## Context

This work extends and improves earlier calculations by Kelkar (1996) and Singh (2006) by including a more complete set of hadronic mechanisms (direct and crossed Delta, nucleon-pole terms) and a realistic treatment of pion distortion via the Klein-Gordon equation with a microscopic optical potential. The results contradict the earlier Rein and Sehgal model (1982), which overpredicted the coherent fraction compared to the K2K upper limit. The calculations enable comparison with ongoing experiments at MiniBooNE and future measurements at SciBooNE and MINERvA, and highlight the need for improved constraints on the axial N-Delta coupling C5A(0).
