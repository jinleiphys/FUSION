# Constraints on jet quenching from a multi-stage energy-loss approach

> We present a multi-stage model for jet evolution through a quark-gluon plasma within the JETSCAPE framework. The multi-stage approach in JETSCAPE provides a unified description of distinct phases in jet shower contingent on the virtuality. We demonstrate a simultaneous description of leading hadron and integrated jet observables as well as jet $v_n$ using tuned parameters. Medium response to the jet quenching is implemented based on a weakly-coupled recoil prescription. We also explore the cone-size dependence of jet energy loss inside the plasma.

## Key claim

The multi-stage JETSCAPE framework, combining MATTER for high-virtuality and LBT for low-virtuality parton evolution, can simultaneously describe leading hadron and integrated jet observables as well as jet anisotropic flow coefficients, but the agreement depends on tuning the switching virtuality \(Q_0\) and the strong coupling \(\alpha_s = 0.25\) to constrain the jet quenching parameter \(\hat{q}\).

## Method

The authors simulated Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02\) TeV using (2+1)-D VISHNU hydrodynamics with TRENTo initial conditions and free-streaming. Hard partons from PYTHIA with initial-state radiation and multi-parton interactions were evolved through the medium using MATTER for the high-virtuality stage (virtuality \(Q > Q_0\)) and LBT for the low-virtuality stage, with switching virtualities \(Q_0 = 1, 2, 3\) GeV. Medium response was implemented via a weakly-coupled recoil prescription, and the quenching parameter \(\hat{q}\) was formulated using a hard thermal loop technique within the Higher-Twist energy loss model.

## Key numbers

- Jet cross-section in p+p collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02\) TeV is consistent with data for jet \(p_T > 200\) GeV at mid-rapidity.
- Switching virtuality \(Q_0\) was set to 1, 2, and 3 GeV with \(\alpha_s = 0.25\) to determine \(\hat{q}\).
- Jet \(R_{AA}\) shows stronger suppression with larger \(Q_0\) values in both central and peripheral Pb-Pb collisions.
- Ratio of jet \(R_{AA}\) with respect to \(R = 0.2\) is consistent with unity, independent of centrality and jet \(p_T\).
- Jet \(v_2\) is non-zero for high-energy jets in peripheral collisions, while jet \(v_3\) vanishes within statistical uncertainties.

## Context

This work extends the JETSCAPE framework's multi-stage approach (Kumar 2020) by simultaneously describing integrated jet observables and jet anisotropic flow coefficients, building on prior MATTER (Majumder 2013, Abir 2015) and LBT (Wang 2013, He 2015) formalisms. The results constrain the jet quenching parameter \(\hat{q}\) through model-to-data comparison and enable future investigations of recoil effects on jet shapes at large cone radii, which are expected to be significant for understanding the detailed jet quenching mechanism.
