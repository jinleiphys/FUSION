# Coupled-channel analysis of collisional effects on HFS transitions in antiprotonic helium atoms

> Collisions of metastable antiprotonic helium with atoms of medium induce transitions between hyperfine structure sublevels as well as shifts and broadenings of the microwave M1 spectral lines. We consider these phenomena in the framework of a model with scalar and tensor interactions between antiprotonic and ordinary helium atoms. S-matrix is obtained by solving coupled-channels equations involving 4 HFS sublevels (F=L\pm 1/2, J=F\pm 1/2) of the nL level and relative angular momenta up to l=5 at the kinetic energy E<25 K. The calculated spin-flip cross sections are less than elastic ones by four orders of value in the case ΔJ=\pm 1, and by seven orders of value in the case ΔF=\pm 1, ΔJ=0 or ΔJ=\pm 2. The considered cross sections reveal a resonance behaviour at very low energy (E about 1 - 4 K depending on the model parameters). At the density N=3\times 10^{20} cm^{-3} and T=6 K we obtain the relaxation time =160 ns, the frequency shift Δν = 80 kHz and the frequency broadening γ\lesssim 5.9 MHz for M1 spectral lines of the favored transitions (ΔF=\pm 1, ΔJ=\pm 1). The results are compatible with the recent experimental data obtained by a laser-microwave-laser resonance method. With the temperature rising up to 25 K the rate of relaxation λ=1/τas well as shift and broadening of the M1 microwave lines are lowered by a factor 1.5 - 2 reflecting the displacement of a mean kinetic energy from the region of resonance scattering.

## Key claim

Collisional effects on hyperfine-structure (HFS) transitions in antiprotonic helium atoms are calculated using a coupled-channels model with scalar and tensor interactions, and the results are compatible with experimental data from a laser-microwave-laser resonance method, but the model's predictions depend on two parameter sets (A and B) that yield different quantitative values for relaxation times and line broadenings.

## Method

The authors solved coupled-channels equations for the \((\bar{p}\mathrm{He}^+)_{nL} - \mathrm{He}\) system, including four HFS sublevels (with quantum numbers \(F = L \pm 1/2\), \(J = F \pm 1/2\)) and relative angular momenta up to \(l = 5\) at kinetic energies \(E \lesssim 25\) K. The interaction potential was modeled with scalar and tensor Van der Waals terms, using two parameter sets: set A from fitting density shifts of E1 transitions, and set B from ab initio potential surface data. The S-matrix was obtained from the coupled equations, and cross sections, transition rates, and line shifts and broadenings were computed with thermal averaging over a Maxwell velocity distribution.

## Key numbers

- Spin-flip cross sections for \(\Delta J = \pm 1\) are less than elastic cross sections by **four orders of magnitude**; for \(\Delta F = \pm 1, \Delta J = 0\) or \(\Delta J = \pm 2\), they are less by **seven orders of magnitude**.
- At density \(N = 3 \times 10^{20}\) cm\(^{-3}\) and temperature \(T = 6\) K, the relaxation time \(\tau(FJ \rightarrow F'J') \geq 160\) ns (set A gives 160.5 ns; set B gives 325 ns).
- At the same conditions, the frequency shift \(\Delta\nu \simeq 80\) kHz (79.5 kHz for set A, 86.6 kHz for set B) for favored transitions (\(\Delta F = \pm 1, \Delta J = \pm 1\)).
- At the same conditions, the frequency broadening \(\gamma \lesssim 5.9\) MHz (5.92 MHz for set A; 2.66 MHz for set B), compatible with the experimental value \(5.3 \pm 0.7\) MHz.
- With temperature rising to 25 K, the relaxation rate \(\lambda = 1/\tau\), shift, and broadening are lowered by a factor of **1.5 to 2**.
- Resonances in cross sections appear at kinetic energies \(E \sim 1\) to 4 K, depending on model parameters.

## Context

This work extends earlier model calculations by Korenman and Yudin (2004) on collisional effects in antiprotonic helium, which previously focused on E1 transitions, to the hyperfine structure and M1 microwave transitions. It provides the first theoretical analysis of collisional shifts, broadenings, and relaxation times for HFS sublevels, complementing experimental data from a laser-microwave-laser resonance method (Yamazaki, Hayano, Widmann, 2004). The results enable interpretation of existing measurements and guide future experiments on temperature-dependent collisional effects in antiprotonic helium.
