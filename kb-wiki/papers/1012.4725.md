# Parity violation in $n + ^3He \rightarrow ^3H + p$ reaction: resonance approach

> The method based on microscopic theory of nuclear reactions has been applied for the analysis of parity violating effects in a few-body systems. Different parity violating and parity conserving asymmetries and their dependence on neutron energy have been estimated for $n + ^3He \rightarrow ^3H + p$ reaction. The estimated effects are in a good agreement with available exact calculations.

## Key claim
The resonance approach, previously validated for medium and heavy nuclei, can be applied to estimate parity-violating (PV) effects in the few-body \(n + ^3He \rightarrow ^3H + p\) reaction, yielding values in good agreement with exact calculations for zero-energy neutrons despite an estimated uncertainty of about one order of magnitude.

## Method
The author uses a microscopic theory of nuclear reactions with distorted wave Born approximation to express PV and parity-conserving (PC) asymmetries in terms of \(\hat{R}\)-matrix elements, which are then parameterized using known resonance structure of the \(^4He\) system. Nine low-energy resonances with total spin \(J=0,1\) are included for PV effects, and resonances with \(J=0,1,2\) for PC effects, with weak matrix elements estimated from a phenomenological formula \(w = 2 \times 10^{-4} \, \text{eV} \sqrt{\bar{D}(\text{eV})}\) using an average level spacing \(\bar{D} \simeq 6 \, \text{MeV}\). Two sets of resonance parameters from Mughabghab and Tilley (1992) are used to account for discrepancies in the first positive resonance at \(E_n = 0.430 \, \text{MeV}\).

## Key numbers
- PV asymmetry for thermal neutrons: \(\alpha_{PV} = -(1-4) \times 10^{-7}\) (Set 1) and \(\alpha_{PV} = -(4-8) \times 10^{-7}\) (Set 2).
- Left-right PC asymmetry at thermal energy: \(\alpha_{LR} = -(2-8) \times 10^{-4}\).
- PV transmission parameter for thermal neutrons: \(P = -(2-4) \times 10^{-10}\) (Set 1) and \(P = -(0.8-1.6) \times 10^{-10}\) (Set 2).
- Weak matrix element estimate: \(w = 0.5 \, \text{eV}\), derived from \(\bar{D} \simeq 6 \, \text{MeV}\).
- Neutron wave vector at \(E_n \sim 0.01 \, \text{eV}\): \(k_n \sim 2.19 \times 10^{-5} \, \text{fm}^{-1}\); proton wave vector: \(k_p = 0.19 \, \text{fm}^{-1}\).
- Characteristic \(^3He\) radius: \(R = 1.97 \, \text{fm}\), giving \((k_n R) \sim 4 \times 10^{-4}\) and \((k_p R) \sim 0.4\).

## Context
This work extends the resonance-based method of Bunakov and Gudkov (1982) for calculating PV effects in nuclear reactions to the few-body \(n + ^3He \rightarrow ^3H + p\) system, motivated by a proposed measurement at the Spallation Neutron Source. The estimated thermal-neutron PV asymmetry agrees well with exact calculations by Viviani (2010) for zero-energy neutrons, suggesting the approach can predict PV effects across a wide range of neutron energies. The results highlight the need for improved measurements of key resonance parameters (e.g., the \(0^-\) resonance at \(0.430 \, \text{MeV}\)) to reduce uncertainties and enable selection of optimal observables for future experiments.
