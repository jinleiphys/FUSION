# Anomalous Lagrangians and the radiative muon capture in hydrogen

> The structure of an anomalous Lagrangian of the pi-rho-omega-a_1 system is investigated within the hidden local SU(2)_R x SU(2)_L symmetry approach. The interaction of the external electromagnetic and weak vector and axial-vector fields with the above hadron system is included. The Lagrangian of interest contains the anomalous Wess-Zumino term following from the well known Wess-Zumino-Witten action and six independent homogenous terms. It is characterized by four constants that are to be determined from a fit to the data on various elementary reactions. Present data allows one to extract the constants with a good accuracy. The homogenous part of the Lagrangian has been applied in the study of anomalous processes that could enhance the high energy tail of the spectrum of photons, produced in the radiative muon capture in hydrogen. It should be noted that recently, an intensive search for such enhancement processes has been carried in the literature, in an attempt to resolve the so called "g_P puzzle": an about 50 % difference between the theoretical prediction of the value of the induced pseudoscalar constant g_P and its value extracted from the high energy tail of the photon spectrum, measured in the precision TRIUMF experiment. Here, more details on the studied material are presented and new results, obtained by using the Wess-Zumino term, are provided.

## Key claim

The paper constructs an anomalous Lagrangian for the π-ρ-ω-a₁ system within the hidden local SU(2)ₓ × SU(2)ₗ symmetry approach and applies it to the radiative muon capture in hydrogen, aiming to resolve the "gₚ puzzle", an approximately 50% discrepancy between the theoretical PCAC prediction for the induced pseudoscalar constant gₚ and the value extracted from the high-energy tail of the photon spectrum measured in the TRIUMF experiment. The authors show that the anomalous contributions from the homogeneous part of the Lagrangian and the Wess-Zumino term can be constructed to satisfy the PCAC constraint, but the Wess-Zumino term contribution to the photon spectrum was found to be negligible in earlier calculations.

## Method

The authors construct the most general anomalous Lagrangian for the π-ρ-ω-a₁ system using the hidden local symmetry approach, combining the covariant Wess-Zumino-Witten action with six independent homogeneous terms characterized by four constants (c̃₇, c̃₈, c̃₉, c̃₁₀) determined from fits to data on elementary reactions. They then derive the Feynman amplitudes for the radiative muon capture process from each term of the Lagrangian, explicitly calculating the divergences to verify that the amplitudes satisfy the conservation laws (CVC and PCAC) and identifying which terms contribute to the induced pseudoscalar form factor gₚ with the enhancement factor of approximately 3.

## Key numbers

- The induced pseudoscalar constant predicted by PCAC for ordinary muon capture in hydrogen is gₚ^PCAC = 6.44 gₐ = -8.29, where gₐ = -1.2695 ± 0.0029
- The value of gₚ extracted from the TRIUMF experiment is about 50% smaller than the PCAC prediction
- The constants determined from fits to data are: c̃₇ = 8.72 × 10⁻³, c̃₈ = -1.07 × 10⁻¹, c̃₉ = 9.76 × 10⁻³, c̃₁₀ = 7.59 × 10⁻²
- The Goldberger-Treiman relation M|gₐ| = g_πNN f_π is satisfied to within 1.1% using M = 938.92 MeV, f_π⁺ = 92.7 ± 0.3 MeV, and g_πNN ≈ 13.0
- The nucleon axial radius is r²ₐ = 0.42 ± 0.04 fm² from quasi-elastic neutrino scattering and r²ₐ = 0.403 ± 0.030 fm² from charged pion electroproduction, corresponding to an axial mass mₐ = 1.077 GeV

## Context

This work extends the earlier construction of anomalous Lagrangians for vector and axial-vector mesons by Kaymakcalan and Schechter (1984) and applies it to the weak sector following the approach of Truhlik, Smejkal, and Khanna (2003, 2004). The paper aims to address the "gₚ puzzle" identified in the TRIUMF experiment (Wright et al., 1998; Armstrong et al., 1999), where the measured photon spectrum from radiative muon capture in hydrogen gave a value of gₚ about 50% below the PCAC prediction. The results enable further calculations of the full anomalous contribution to the radiative muon capture amplitude, which can be combined with non-anomalous amplitudes from earlier work by Truhlik, Smejkal, and Khanna (2003) to test whether anomalous processes provide the missing enhancement needed to resolve the discrepancy.
