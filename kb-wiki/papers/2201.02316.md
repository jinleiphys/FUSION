# Practical considerations for the effect of finite coverage on the azimuthal dependence of global spin alignment

> The global spin alignment of vector mesons is a powerful probe to study the vorticity field of the system produced in non-central relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Since the experimental observables of global spin alignment are sensitive to many factors, proper corrections are need to be taken care of when measuring the global spin alignment of vector mesons. In this paper, we propose a method to correct the effect of finite pseudo-rapidity coverage when extract the azimuthal dependence of spin alignment parameter $ρ_{00}$. The effects of the finite pseudo-rapidity coverage on the azimuthal dependence of $ϕ$ meson spin alignment and the potentially exist intrinsic spin alignment effects are taken into consideration. The method presented in this paper is verified in a Monte-Carlo simulation, it allows the measurements of azimuthal dependence of global spin alignment to be conducted properly and accurately.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a correction method for the effect of finite pseudorapidity coverage on the azimuthal dependence of the vector meson spin alignment parameter ρ₀₀, but the method requires that the intrinsic spin alignment in the helix frame be extracted separately via a 3D rotation procedure, and the correction is verified only in a toy Monte-Carlo simulation rather than with real experimental data.

## Method

The authors construct a toy Monte-Carlo model sampling φ meson distributions based on STAR-measured pT spectra, dN/dy, and elliptic flow v₂, with PYTHIA6 used for the φ → K⁺K⁻ decay. They apply a finite pseudorapidity cut and a 3D rotation of the quantization axis to separate the contributions from acceptance effects (ρ₀₀,acc), intrinsic spin alignment in the helix frame (ρ^{HX}₀₀,in), and the real spin alignment (ρ₀₀,real). The correction is performed by fitting cosθ* distributions in four Φ−Ψ bins using the angular distribution formula dN/d(cosθ*) ∝ (1−ρ₀₀) + (3ρ₀₀−1)cos²θ*, with the acceptance and intrinsic contributions factored out sequentially.

## Key numbers

- Input ρ₀₀,real values for the four Φ−Ψ bins were set to (0.38, 0.36, 0.34, 0.32) in the toy simulation.
- The intrinsic spin alignment parameter in the φ meson helix frame was set to 0.35.
- The extracted ρ^{HX}₀₀,in from the 3D rotation method was found to be less than 1/3 for Φ ∈ [0, π/8) and greater than 1/3 for Φ ∈ [3π/8, π/2), consistent with the input intrinsic value of 0.35.
- The corrected ρ₀₀,real values were consistent with the input ρ₀₀,input within uncertainties for all four Φ−Ψ bins.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of finite-acceptance effects on global spin alignment first raised by Lan et al. (2017) and the correction method proposed by Tang et al. (2018), which addressed the integrated (azimuthally averaged) ρ₀₀. The paper specifically addresses the azimuthal dependence of ρ₀₀, which had not been fully explored in previous studies, and enables experimental measurements of the azimuthal modulation of vector meson spin alignment at RHIC and LHC experiments. The method also accounts for possible intrinsic spin alignment in the helix frame, which was not considered in earlier acceptance-correction approaches.
