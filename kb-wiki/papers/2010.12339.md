# Towards a power counting in nuclear energy-density-functional theories through a perturbative analysis

> We illustrate a step towards the construction of a power counting in energy-density-functional (EDF) theories, by analyzing the equations of state (EOSs) of both symmetric and neutron matter. Within the adopted strategy, next-to-leading order (NLO) EOSs are introduced which contain renormalized first-order-type terms and an explicit second-order finite part. Employing as a guide the asymptotic behavior of the introduced renormalized parameters, we focus our analysis on two aspects: (i) With a minimum number of counterterms introduced at NLO, we show that each energy contribution entering in the EOS has a regular evolution with respect to the momentum cutoff (introduced in the adopted regularization procedure) and is found to converge to a cutoff-independent curve. The convergence features of each term are related to its Fermi-momentum dependence. (ii) We find that the asymptotic evolution of the second-order finite-part coefficients is a strong indication of a perturbative behavior, which in turns confirms that the adopted strategy is coherent with a possible underlying power counting in the chosen Skyrme-inspired EDF framework.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a perturbative expansion based on the Dyson many-body series, using a minimal Skyrme-type leading-order interaction (the \(t_0-t_3\) model), produces a convergent pattern where the second-order finite-part contributions are progressively suppressed as the momentum cutoff increases, indicating that the scheme is coherent with a possible underlying power counting in energy-density-functional theories. However, they do not claim to have established a full power counting, as the breakdown scale cannot yet be extracted without next-to-next-to-leading order calculations.

## Method

The authors construct next-to-leading order (NLO) equations of state for symmetric matter and neutron matter using a zero-range Skyrme-type interaction at leading order (the \(t_0-t_3\) model) and include second-order diagrams from the once-iterated leading-order interaction plus a minimal set of counterterms (one type) to absorb ultraviolet divergences. The renormalized parameters are adjusted to reproduce the SLy5 mean-field equations of state for both symmetric and neutron matter, using cutoff values ranging from 2 to 30 fm\(^{-1}\), and the asymptotic behavior of the parameters and energy contributions is analyzed as a function of the cutoff.

## Key numbers

* The renormalized parameters at \(\Lambda = 20\) and 30 fm\(^{-1}\) differ by only 0.6, 0.4, 0.4, 0.1, 0.5, and 4.4 percent for \(t_0^{\Lambda}\), \(t_{3,\alpha}^{\Lambda}\), \(C^{\Lambda}\), \(t_0^{\Lambda*}\), \(t_{3,\alpha}^{\Lambda*}\), and \(C^{\Lambda*}\), respectively.
* The second-order finite-part contribution to the total energy at \(\Lambda = 30\) fm\(^{-1}\) is 0.64 MeV at a density of 0.05 fm\(^{-3}\) and 6.92 MeV at a density of 0.29 fm\(^{-3}\), compared to total energies of -8.97 MeV and -8.55 MeV, respectively.
* The parameter \(\alpha\) increases monotonically with cutoff and saturates to a value of 0.243 at \(\Lambda = 30\) fm\(^{-1}\), remaining smaller than 1/3.
* The \(\chi^2\) values for the fits range from 0.029 at \(\Lambda = 2\) fm\(^{-1}\) to 0.117 at \(\Lambda = 30\) fm\(^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the perturbative analysis of Yang et al. (2017) by employing a more systematic optimization protocol and focusing on the asymptotic behavior of renormalized parameters to assess perturbativity. The approach is guided by the Dyson many-body expansion, in contrast to the phenomenological methodology of traditional Skyrme energy-density-functional theories, and aims to establish a foundation for a power counting similar to that in effective field theories such as chiral effective field theory. The results open perspectives for applications to exotic nuclei with dilute surfaces and neutron-star crusts, where the perturbative behavior is even more pronounced at lower densities.
