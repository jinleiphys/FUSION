# Nucleon Charges and Electromagnetic Form Factors from 2+1+1-Flavor Lattice QCD

> We present lattice-QCD results on the nucleon isovector axial, scalar and tensor charges, the isovector electromagnetic Dirac and Pauli form factors, and the connected parts of the isoscalar charges. The calculations have been done using two ensembles of HISQ lattices generated by the MILC Collaboration with 2+1+1 dynamical flavors at a lattice spacing of 0.12 fm and with light-quark masses corresponding to pions with masses 310 and 220 MeV. We perform a systematic study including excited-state degrees of freedom and examine the dependence of the extracted nucleon matrix elements on source-sink separation. This study demonstrates with high-statistics data that including excited-state contributions and generating data at multiple separations is necessary to remove contamination that would otherwise lead to systematic error. We also determine the renormalization constants of the associated quark bilinear operators in the RI-sMOM scheme and make comparisons of our renormalized results with previous dynamical-lattice calculations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that nucleon isovector charges and electromagnetic form factors can be calculated using 2+1+1-flavor lattice QCD, but the results at a single lattice spacing of 0.12 fm show significant discretization effects and require careful treatment of excited-state contamination, with the final estimates lacking continuum extrapolation and thus carrying substantial systematic uncertainties.

## Method

The authors used two ensembles of HISQ lattices generated by the MILC Collaboration with 2+1+1 dynamical flavors at a lattice spacing of 0.12 fm and pion masses of 310 and 220 MeV. They calculated nucleon three-point correlation functions at multiple source-sink separations and performed simultaneous fits including excited-state contributions (two-simRR method) to extract ground-state matrix elements. The renormalization constants for the quark bilinear operators were determined in the RI-sMOM scheme and converted to the MS-bar scheme at 2 GeV using one-loop conversion factors.

## Key numbers

- Axial charge: extrapolated to physical pion mass gives g_A = 1.193(68)
- Scalar charge: extrapolated to physical pion mass gives g_S = 0.72(32)
- Tensor charge: extrapolated to physical pion mass gives g_T = 1.047(61)
- Isovector Dirac radius: extrapolated to physical pion mass gives ⟨r_1²⟩ = 0.421(88)(25) fm²
- Isovector Pauli radius: extrapolated to physical pion mass gives ⟨r_2²⟩ = 0.368(175)(65) fm²
- Anomalous magnetic moment: extrapolated to physical pion mass gives κ^v = 2.89(35)(10)
- Renormalization constants in MS-bar at 2 GeV: Z_A/Z_V = 1.03(1), Z_S/Z_V = 0.95(3), Z_T/Z_V = 1.01(2)
- Unrenormalized bare charges at 310 MeV: g_A^bare = 1.269(45), g_S^bare = 0.73(12), g_T^bare = 1.104(23)
- Unrenormalized bare charges at 220 MeV: g_A^bare = 1.264(35), g_S^bare = 0.79(22), g_T^bare = 1.122(29)

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations of nucleon charges by the LHPC (Green 2012), ETMC (Alexandrou 2013), and QCDSF (Horsley 2013) collaborations, using the first 2+1+1-flavor ensembles from MILC with HISQ fermions. The results enable improved constraints on novel scalar and tensor interactions at the TeV scale when combined with experimental measurements of neutron beta decay parameters, though the authors note that future calculations at multiple lattice spacings (0.09 and 0.06 fm) and higher statistics are needed for controlled continuum and chiral extrapolations.
