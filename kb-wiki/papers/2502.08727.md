# Revisiting Gauge Ambiguities for DUNE Precision

> The de Forest prescription for handling off-shell initial states in the impulse approximation for lepton-nucleus scattering breaks gauge invariance. We discuss existing methods to address this problem and handle the form factor scale ambiguity. We demonstrate that the irreducible differences between the prescriptions are significant compared to the precision expected of next-generation accelerator neutrino experiments. A novel approach directly using the off-shell currents is proposed as a systematically improvable alternative.

## Key claim

The irreducible differences between existing gauge-restoration prescriptions (Coulomb, Landau, Weyl) for handling off-shell initial-state nucleons in the plane-wave impulse approximation produce uncertainties of 2–5% from the gauge choice alone and an additional 5–10% from the form-factor scale ambiguity, both of which exceed the few-percent precision required by DUNE.

## Method

The paper systematically compares three existing gauge-restoration prescriptions, Coulomb, Weyl, and Landau, applied to the de Forest energy-shift method for off-shell nucleons in quasielastic lepton-nucleus scattering within the plane-wave impulse approximation. It also examines the ambiguity in choosing either the original gauge-boson momentum or the shifted gauge-boson momentum as the scale for the form factors. A novel alternative is proposed that directly uses the full off-shell current (Eq. 7), introducing ten form factors instead of four and replacing the ad hoc energy shift with a systematically improvable spinor-based treatment of off-shellness.

## Key numbers

- Gauge-restoration prescription differences (Coulomb vs. Landau) produce uncertainties of **2–5%** for electron-carbon scattering at beam energies of 680 MeV, 1108 MeV, and 1930 MeV (Figs. 1–3).
- Form-factor scale ambiguity (original vs. shifted gauge-boson momentum) adds an additional **5–10%** uncertainty for neutrino-carbon scattering at 1 GeV beam energy (Figs. 4–5).
- DUNE requires uncertainties constrained to the level of **a few percent** for neutrino-nucleus interaction modeling.

## Context

This work extends the de Forest prescription (DeForest, 1983) for off-shell nucleons in the impulse approximation, which is widely used in event generators such as Achilles, GENIE, and NuWro. It contradicts the implicit assumption that gauge-restoration ambiguities are negligible at the precision needed by next-generation experiments like DUNE and Hyper-Kamiokande. The proposed off-shell current approach enables a systematically improvable framework where uncertainties can be reduced through future lattice QCD calculations, vector-meson dominance models, or chiral perturbation theory (e.g., Tiemeijer, 1990; Bos, 1993), rather than relying on ad hoc energy shifts.
