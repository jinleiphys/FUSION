# Modeling net-charge fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC

> The analysis of Pb+Pb data for net-charge fluctuations at LHC energies within the HYDJET++ model is presented. The strongly intensive quantities $D$ and $Σ$ are used to get rid of the effects related to volume fluctuations of the system. We employ two versions of the HYDJET++ for the analysis. The first one is the standard or default version, whereas the second one represents its further modification, which takes into account explicit event-by-event conservation of the electric net-charge of produced particles. Inclusion of the canonical net-charge conservation in the model allows one for better description of the experimental data obtained by the ALICE and the CMS Collaborations. Comparison with calculations of other models is also presented.

## Key claim
The HYDJET++ model, when modified to include explicit event-by-event canonical conservation of net electric charge, provides a better description of ALICE and CMS net-charge fluctuation data than the default grand-canonical version, but only after tuning the strength of unlike-sign pair correlations (the Gaussian width parameters sigma_phi and sigma_eta) to match balance-function widths.

## Method
The authors used the HYDJET++ Monte Carlo event generator in two versions: the default (grand-canonical) version and a modified version that enforces exact event-by-event conservation of net electric charge by randomly discarding half of the charged hadrons from the soft component and generating opposite-charge partners with Gaussian-distributed azimuthal angle and rapidity around the original hadron. They computed the strongly intensive fluctuation measures D and Sigma for Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies of 2.76 TeV and 5.02 TeV per nucleon pair, comparing results with ALICE and CMS data and with predictions from the HIJING and AMPT models.

## Key numbers
- For central 0-5% Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, the modified HYDJET++ with newly tuned correlation strength (weta = 0.5 for 0-5% centrality) reproduces the experimental D^corr and Sigma-1 values as functions of Delta-eta window, while the default HYDJET++ (weta = 0.35 for 0-5%) does not.
- The Gaussian width parameter weta (sigma_eta) was tuned to values of 0.5 (0-5% centrality), 0.7 (20-30%), and 1.1 (50-60%) for the new parametrization, compared to previous values of 0.35, 0.5, and 1.0 respectively.
- Deviations of model calculations with newly tuned parameters from those in Chernyshov (2022) lie within a 7% accuracy limit for balance-function widths at 2.76 TeV.
- The transverse momentum range used in calculations was 0.2 < p_T < 5 GeV/c, matching experimental data.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of net-charge fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions beyond earlier studies by the NA49 Collaboration at SPS, STAR at RHIC, and ALICE and CMS at LHC, which were poorly described by existing theoretical models. The modification of HYDJET++ builds on the approach of Chernyshov (2022) for charge balance functions, and the results are compared with predictions from the HIJING model of Gyulassy (1994) and the AMPT model of Zhang (1999). The findings enable improved modeling of charge conservation effects in heavy-ion event generators, with the tuned parameters providing a benchmark for future studies of fluctuation observables at LHC energies.
