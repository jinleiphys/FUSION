# The nature of Lambda(1405) hyperon resonance in chiral dynamics

> The nature of the Lambda(1405) is discussed based on the unitarised coupled-channels approach with chiral dynamics (chiral unitary model). This approach describes the Kbar N scattering cross sections and the Lambda(1405) spectra phenomenologically very well. With this successful description of Lambda(1405), it is found that the Lambda(1405) is composed by two resonance states having different coupling nature to the meson-baryon states. As a consequence, the resonance position in the pi Sigma invariant mass spectrum depends on the initial channel of the Lambda(1405) production. To observe the Lambda(1405) initiated by the Kbar N channel, K^- d to Lambda(1405) n is one of the most favorable reactions. Hadronic molecule states with kaons are also discussed by emphasizing an important role of Lambda(1405) as a quasibound state of Kbar N.

## Key claim

The Lambda(1405) resonance is not a single state but is composed of two distinct resonance poles with different coupling properties to meson-baryon channels; consequently, the observed peak position in the pi Sigma invariant mass spectrum depends on the initial production channel, with the Kbar N initiated channel producing a peak near 1420 MeV rather than 1405 MeV.

## Method

The authors employed a unitarized coupled-channels approach based on chiral dynamics (the chiral unitary model), which solves the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for s-wave meson-baryon scattering in the S=-1 sector using vertices from chiral perturbation theory. They performed analytic continuation of the scattering amplitude into the complex energy plane to locate resonance poles, and calculated the pi Sigma invariant mass spectra for the K- d -> pi Sigma n reaction at 800 MeV/c incident kaon momentum, comparing with bubble chamber data from Braun (1977).

## Key numbers

- Pole 1 (lower pole): located at 1390 - 66i MeV, with larger width and strong coupling to pi Sigma
- Pole 2 (higher pole): located at 1426 - 16i MeV, with narrower width and dominant coupling to Kbar N
- The Kbar N initiated spectrum shows a peak at approximately 1420 MeV, not 1405 MeV
- The pi Sigma scattering length with I=0 varies across models: 0.517 fm (BNW), 0.789 fm (ORB), 0.692 fm (HNJH), 0.770 fm (BMN); a virtual state scenario gives approximately 5 fm
- The K Kbar N quasibound state is found around 1910 MeV, below all meson-baryon decay thresholds
- The electric radius of Lambda(1405) is about three times larger than that of the neutron
- Inter-hadron distances in the K Kbar N state are larger than 2 fm

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary model studies of Kaiser (1995), Oset (1998), Oller (2000), and Jido (2003) that first identified the double-pole nature of Lambda(1405). It directly addresses the experimental finding of Hemingway (1984) that the Lambda(1405) spectrum appears at 1420 MeV in certain reactions, and provides a theoretical explanation through channel-dependent production. The results enable future experimental searches for the higher pole using K- d -> Lambda(1405) n reactions, and motivate measurements of pi Sigma scattering lengths to constrain the lower pole position, which is important for understanding Kbar few-body systems such as Kbar NN and K Kbar N.
