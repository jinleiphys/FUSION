# A Non-Perturbative Treatment of the Pion in the Linear Sigma-Model

> Using a non-perturbative method based on the selfconsistent Quasi-particle Random-Phase Approximation (QRPA) we describe the properties of the pion in the linear $σ$-model. It is found that the pion is massless in the chiral limit, both at zero- and finite temperature, in accordance with Goldstone's theorem.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a selfconsistent Quasi-particle Random-Phase Approximation (QRPA) treatment of the linear sigma model yields a massless pion in the chiral limit at both zero and finite temperature, thereby preserving Goldstone's theorem non-perturbatively. This result holds despite the fact that the mean-field (BCS) level alone produces a non-zero quasipion mass in the chiral limit, which would violate the theorem.

## Method
The authors apply a bosonic Bogoliubov transformation to the pion and sigma fields of the linear sigma model, defining a selfconsistent quasiparticle basis via minimization of the vacuum expectation value of the Hamiltonian. They then construct the single-pion RPA excitation operator, solving the equation of motion to obtain the physical pion mass, which includes contributions from quasipion-quasisigma scattering. The formalism is extended to finite temperature using the finite-temperature Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (FTHFB) method and the finite-temperature RPA.

## Key numbers
- The quasipion mass at mean-field level in the chiral limit is given by \( {\cal E}_{\pi}^2(0) = 2\lambda_0^2 [I_0 - J_0] \), which is non-zero due to the difference of the divergent integrals \(I_0\) and \(J_0\), violating Goldstone's theorem.
- After including RPA correlations, the physical pion mass squared in the chiral limit is \( \Omega^2_{\nu} = c/s \), which vanishes when the explicit symmetry breaking parameter \(c = 0\).
- The RPA dispersion relation for the pion at finite momentum is \( \Omega^2_{\nu}(\vec{p}) = \Omega^2_{\nu}(0) + \vec{p}^2 \), valid both in and away from the chiral limit.
- At finite temperature, the condition for a zero-energy spurious mode is \( c/s_T = 0 \), which holds only in the Goldstone phase with finite condensate \(s_T\).

## Context
This work extends the selfconsistent QRPA method, originally developed in many-body physics for fermionic systems (Marsh, year not given), to the bosonic sector of the linear sigma model. It directly addresses the problem of preserving chiral symmetry in non-perturbative calculations, which is known to be delicate (Aouissat et al., year not given). The approach contrasts with chiral perturbation theory (Gasser and Leutwyler, 1984), which is perturbative, and aims to provide a framework for studying the chiral phase transition non-perturbatively, with future applications to SU(3) and inclusion of fermions.
