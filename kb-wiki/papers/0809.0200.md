# Low-momentum effective interaction in the three-dimensional approach

> The formulation of the low-momentum effective interaction in the model space Lee-Suzuki and the renormalization group methods is implemented in the three-dimensional approach. In this approach the low-momentum effective interaction V_{low k} has been formulated as a function of the magnitude of momentum vectors and the angle between them. As an application the spin-isospin independent Malfliet-Tjon potential has been used into the model space Lee-Suzuki method and it has been shown that the low-momentum effective interaction V_{low k} reproduces the same two-body observables obtained by the bare potential V_{NN}.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the low-momentum effective interaction \(V_{low\,k}\) can be formulated directly in a three-dimensional (3D) momentum representation, without partial-wave decomposition, and that this 3D formulation reproduces the same two-body observables as the bare potential \(V_{NN}\) when applied to the spin-isospin independent Malfliet-Tjon potential. The authors note that spin and isospin degrees of freedom have been neglected in this first step, and that including them is a major additional task currently underway.

## Method

The authors implemented the model-space Lee-Suzuki method and the renormalization group (RG) decimation method in a three-dimensional momentum representation, expressing the low-momentum effective interaction \(V_{low\,k}\) as a function of the magnitudes of momentum vectors and the angle between them. They applied the spin-isospin independent Malfliet-Tjon potential, which is a superposition of short-range repulsive and long-range attractive Yukawa interactions, and solved the Lippmann-Schwinger equation in 3D representation to obtain the half-on-shell two-body \(T\) matrix. The \(\omega\) operator was computed using the wave function components in the \(P\) and \(Q\) spaces, and then used to construct \(V_{low\,k}\) via equation (13). The numerical calculations used Gaussian quadrature grids with 40 points for the \(P\) space momentum interval \([0,\Lambda]\) and 30 points for the \(Q\) space interval \([\Lambda,\infty)\), with 20 and 14 grid points for the spherical and polar angle variables respectively.

## Key numbers

- The cutoff momentum used in the calculations is \(\Lambda = 2.1\,\text{fm}^{-1}\).
- The Malfliet-Tjon potential parameters are: repulsive strength \(V_R = 7.291\), attractive strength \(V_A = 3.177\), repulsive meson mass \(\mu_R = 613.7\,\text{MeV}\), attractive meson mass \(\mu_A = 305.9\,\text{MeV}\).
- The Malfliet-Tjon potential supports one bound state at energy \(E = -2.23\,\text{MeV}\).
- The mapping parameter for the \(Q\) space integration is \(b = 5\,\text{fm}^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the low-momentum effective interaction methods previously developed in partial-wave representation by Bogner et al. (2003, 2004) to a three-dimensional formalism that avoids the angular momentum algebra required for partial-wave decomposition. The 3D approach builds on recent developments in few-body bound and scattering problems by Elster et al. (1998) and others, and is intended to simplify future calculations involving three-body forces and spin-isospin degrees of freedom. The authors state that a formulation including spin and isospin in a helicity basis is currently underway, and that calculations with realistic potentials such as Bonn-B, AV18, and Chiral potentials are in progress.
