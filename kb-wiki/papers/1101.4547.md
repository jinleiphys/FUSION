# Novel structure for magnetic rotation bands in 60Ni

> The self-consistent tilted axis cranking relativistic mean-field theory based on a point-coupling interaction has been established and applied to investigate systematically the newly observed shears bands in 60Ni. The tilted angles, deformation parameters, energy spectra, and reduced M1 and $E2$ transition probabilities have been studied in a fully microscopic and self-consistent way for various configurations and rotational frequencies. It is found the competition between the configurations and the transitions from the magnetic to the electric rotations have to be considered in order to reproduce the energy spectra as well as the band crossing phenomena. The tendency of the experimental electromagnetic transition ratios B(M1)/B(E2) is in a good agreement with the data, in particular, the B(M1) values decrease with increasing spin as expected for the shears mechanism, whose characteristics are discussed in detail by investigating the various contributions to the total angular momentum as well.

## Key claim

The self-consistent tilted axis cranking relativistic mean-field theory based on a point-coupling interaction can reproduce the experimental energy spectra and electromagnetic transition ratios of the shears bands in 60Ni, but only if competition between configurations and transitions from magnetic to electric rotation are considered; the calculations converge only up to approximately 14-16 hbar depending on the configuration, not up to the largest observed spin values.

## Method

The authors developed a self-consistent tilted axis cranking relativistic mean-field (TAC-RMF) model using a point-coupling interaction with the PC-PK1 parameter set, solving the Dirac equation in a three-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis with 10 major shells. They applied this model to three configurations (Config1, Config2, Config3) for the four experimentally observed shears bands (M-1, M-2, M-3, M-4) in 60Ni, determining the tilted angle self-consistently by minimizing the total Routhian. The calculations included energy spectra, deformation parameters, angular momenta, and reduced M1 and E2 transition probabilities for various rotational frequencies.

## Key numbers

- The deformation parameters at the bandhead were (beta, gamma) = (0.27, 19 degrees) for M-1, (0.30, 28 degrees) for M-2, and (0.30, 6 degrees) for M-3.
- The beta values for Config1, Config2, and Config3 typically lie between 0.2 and 0.3 and decrease smoothly with rotational frequency, while gamma values increase up to about 40 degrees.
- For Config3*, the beta value jumped from 0.24 to 0.40 with a gamma value reducing from 40 degrees to nearly zero, indicating a large prolate deformation (beta approximately 0.4) with axial symmetry.
- The B(E2) values for all three bands were less than 0.1 (e b)^2, with specific predicted values of 0.006 (e b)^2 for Config1* and 0.1 (e b)^2 for Config3* at configuration changes.
- Convergent results were obtained only up to approximately 14 hbar for Config1, approximately 15 hbar for Config2, and approximately 16 hbar for Config3.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent tilted axis cranking approach to the point-coupling version of covariant density functional theory, following earlier relativistic TAC calculations for 84Rb by Madokoro (2000) and for 142Gd by Peng (2008). The study applies the method to 60Ni, which Torres (2008) identified as the lightest system with magnetic rotation observed so far, and uses the PC-PK1 parameter set proposed by Zhao (2010). The results enable future investigations of chiral partner bands in 60Ni and systematic studies of magnetic rotation in lighter nuclei, while also highlighting the need to include pairing correlations and particle-vibration coupling for absolute B(M1) values.
