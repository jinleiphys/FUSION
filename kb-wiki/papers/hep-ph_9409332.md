# Higgs signals modified by singlet scalars

> Presented by T. Binoth at "Quarks 94", Vladimir, May 1994. 12 pages latex, 4 ps files. Unprocessable pictures removed, missing pictures upon request. Article appeared in the Proceedings of the 8th International Seminar: Quarks 94, eds. D.Yu. Grigoriev et. al., World Scientific (1995)

## Key claim
The paper shows that if the Standard Model Higgs boson couples strongly to additional scalar singlet fields (called "phions") that are invisible to detectors, the Higgs resonance can become so broad and its branching ratio to visible channels so suppressed that detection at hadron colliders like the LHC may become impossible, even for Higgs masses above the ZZ threshold where Standard Model signals are expected to be clean.

## Method
The authors extend the Standard Model scalar sector by adding an N-plet of real scalar singlet fields (phions) that interact only with the Higgs boson via a renormalizable coupling. They treat the strong interactions in the extended scalar sector using the 1/N expansion, compute the Higgs self-energy from the phion bubble sum in the large-N limit, and derive the modified Higgs propagator. They then calculate the cross section for the gluon-fusion process gg → H → ZZ → 4μ at LHC energies, subtracting the qq̅ → ZZ and gg → ZZ box backgrounds, for various values of the Higgs-phion coupling α and the quartic phion coupling β.

## Key numbers
- For the Standard Model Higgs at m_H = 300 GeV (λ = 0.744) with α = 0 (no phion coupling), the signal cross section minus background is 3.10 fb at LHC (√s = 16 TeV) with rapidity cut |y_l| < 3 and p_Tl > 20 GeV.
- For the same λ = 0.744, increasing the phion coupling to α = 0.2 reduces the signal to 0.43 fb; at α = 1.0 the signal is only 0.02 fb.
- For a lighter Higgs (λ = 0.33, corresponding to m_H ≈ 200 GeV), the signal drops from 2.98 fb at α = 0 to 0.08 fb at α = 0.2 and below 0.01 fb at α = 1.0.
- In the limit β = 0 (no quartic phion self-coupling) and large α, the width-to-mass ratio Γ/M asymptotically approaches 2, making the resonance unobservably broad.
- The vacuum expectation value is set to v = 246 GeV, used as the unit for all masses and energies.

## Context
This work extends the Standard Model by adding a hidden sector of scalar singlets, which appear in non-minimal supersymmetric extensions. The authors show that strong Higgs-phion couplings can completely suppress the golden-channel Higgs signal (H → ZZ → 4l) that was considered the most promising discovery mode at the LHC for masses above 2m_Z, as studied in earlier work by van der Bij and collaborators (1994). The paper enables the interpretation that non-observation of the Higgs at hadron colliders could indicate a more complicated scalar sector, and argues that e⁺e⁻ colliders with Higgs bremsstrahlung and missing-p_T measurements would be more robust for discovery in such scenarios.
