# Omega photoproduction beyond the resonance region at small u

> Within the meson exchange model we study $ω$ meson photoproduction at energies above the s channel resonance region. Different model prescriptions for the $ω{NN}$ vertex function are investigated imposing gauge invariance as well as crossing symmetry. The calculations reproduce the energy dependence of the differential $ω$ photoproduction cross sections at moderate $|u|$ for $E_γ{\le}4.7$ GeV, which previously were discussed as an indication of the hard interaction between the photon and quarks of the nucleon.

## Key claim

Within a meson exchange model that includes π, η, σ, and nucleon exchanges with gauge-invariant ωNN vertex functions, the energy dependence of the differential ω photoproduction cross section at moderate |u| for Eγ ≤ 4.7 GeV can be reproduced without invoking hard partonic interactions, although the model cannot reproduce the dip around u ≈ −0.15 GeV² that was previously interpreted as evidence for such hard processes.

## Method

The authors computed ω meson photoproduction amplitudes using effective Lagrangians for π, η, and σ meson exchanges in the t-channel and nucleon exchange in the s- and u-channels, with monopole form factors at meson-nucleon vertices and four different prescriptions for the ωNN vertex function (models A–D). Model parameters including coupling constants and cutoff parameters were fitted globally to both dσ/du and dσ/dt data from the Daresbury and other experiments at photon energies 2.8–4.7 GeV, using the Minuit minimization package.

## Key numbers

- The global fit included 120 experimental points for photon energies 2.8 ≤ Eγ ≤ 4.7 GeV.
- Model D (product form factor F(u,s)=F(u,Λ)F(s,Λ) with crossing symmetry) achieved total χ² = 598, compared to χ² = 1796 for model A (no ωNN form factor) and χ² = 614 for model C (Davidson-Workman form factor).
- The fitted ωNN coupling constant in model D was gωNN = 1.7 ± 0.1, with cutoff ΛωNN = 1.85 ± 0.03 GeV.
- The πNN coupling constant was fixed at gπNN = 13.26, with cutoff ΛπNN = 0.7 GeV; the ηNN coupling was gηNN = 3.53 with cutoff ΛηNN = 1.0 GeV.
- The ωγπ coupling constant was gωγπ = 1.82, and ωγη was gωγη = 0.42, evaluated from partial decay widths.
- The data at −0.1 < u < 0 GeV² were fitted by an s⁻³·⁹±⁰·³ dependence, while data at −1.8 < u < −1.6 GeV² gave s⁻⁹·³±²·¹; model D calculations gave s⁴·⁵⁷ and s⁶·⁷³ respectively (note: these exponents appear with positive sign in the text, likely indicating the power-law form used differs from the experimental fit convention).

## Context

This work extends the meson exchange approach of Joos and Kramer (1964) and Friman and Soyeur (1990) to backward-angle ω photoproduction, where previous models accounting only for t-channel contributions could not reproduce the Daresbury data of Clifft et al. (1977). The calculations contradict the interpretation by Clifft et al. that the steep s⁻⁸ dependence of the cross section indicates hard photon-quark interactions, as proposed by Brodsky (1974); instead, the authors show that a standard hadronic model with appropriate ωNN vertex functions can reproduce the energy dependence. The results enable further experimental investigation of whether the dip at u ≈ −0.15 GeV², which is not seen in π, ρ, or f meson photoproduction, requires additional confirmation.
