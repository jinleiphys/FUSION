# Two, three, many body systems involving mesons. Multimeson condensates

> In this talk we review results from studies with unconventional many hadron systems containing mesons: systems with two mesons and one baryon, three mesons, some novel systems with two baryons and one meson, and finally systems with many vector mesons, up to six, with their spins aligned forming states of increasing spin. We show that in many cases one has experimental counterparts for the states found, while in some other cases they remain as predictions, which we suggest to be searched in BESIII, Belle, LHCb, FAIR and other facilities.

## Key claim

The paper reviews theoretical predictions for unconventional many-hadron systems containing mesons, showing that many predicted multimeson states can be associated with known experimental resonances, while others remain predictions that should be searched for at facilities like BESIII, Belle, LHCb, and FAIR.

## Method

The authors use the chiral unitary approach to describe meson-meson and meson-baryon interactions, implementing unitarity in coupled channels. For three-body systems, they employ the Fixed Center Approximation (FCA) to the Faddeev equations, where a third particle interacts multiply with a bound two-particle cluster. For multimeson systems, they iteratively add vector mesons with aligned spins, allowing each new meson to interact with the existing cluster to produce states of increasing total spin.

## Key numbers

- The $f_2(1270)$ resonance is understood as a $\rho\rho$ molecular state in the spin $J=2$ channel.
- Multirho states up to six $\rho$ mesons were found and associated with known resonances: $f_2(1270)$, $\rho_3(1690)$, $f_4(2050)$, $\rho_5(2350)$, and $f_6(2510)$.
- $K^*$ multirho states were associated with $K^*_2(1430)$, $K^*_3(1780)$, $K^*_4(2045)$, and $K^*_5(2380)$.
- Predicted $D^*$ multirho states have masses around $2800-2850$ MeV ($D^*_3$), $3075-3200$ MeV ($D^*_4$), $3360-3375$ MeV ($D^*_5$), and $3775$ MeV ($D^*_6$), with widths of about $60-100$ MeV, $200-400$ MeV, $200-400$ MeV, and $400$ MeV respectively.
- The $\bar{K}DN$, $NDK$, and $ND\bar{D}$ systems produce quasibound states with energies $3150$ MeV, $3050$ MeV, and $4400$ MeV respectively, all with $J^P=1/2^+$ and isospin $I=1/2$.
- The $\rho D^*\bar{D}^*$ system with spins aligned gives an $I=1$ state with mass around $4340$ MeV and width about $50$ MeV.
- The $DNN$ system has a quasibound state at $\sqrt{s} \sim 3500$ MeV, bound by about $250$ MeV from the $DNN$ threshold, with width estimated at $20-40$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach for meson-meson interaction (Gasser 1983) and meson-baryon interaction (Ecker 1994, Bernard 1995) to many-body systems, building on prior studies of dynamically generated states from vector-vector interactions (Roca 2005, Geng 2007). The multirho predictions follow from the observation that the $\rho\rho$ interaction in $J=2$ is strong enough to produce the $f_2(1270)$ (Roca 2005), and the iterative procedure for adding more $\rho$ mesons was developed in Roca (2010) and Roca (2011). The results enable experimental searches for predicted high-spin states containing $D^*$ mesons and other exotic multihadron configurations at current and future facilities.
