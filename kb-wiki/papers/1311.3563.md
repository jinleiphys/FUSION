# Evolved Chiral NN+3N Hamiltonians for Ab Initio Nuclear Structure Calculations

> We discuss the building blocks for a consistent inclusion of chiral three-nucleon (3N) interactions into ab initio nuclear structure calculations beyond the lower p-shell. We highlight important technical developments, such as the similarity renormalization group (SRG) evolution in the 3N sector, a JT-coupled storage scheme for 3N matrix elements with efficient on-the-fly decoupling, and the importance truncated no-core shell model with 3N interactions. Together, these developments make converged ab initio calculations with explicit 3N interactions possible also beyond the lower p-shell. We analyze in detail the impact of various truncations of the SRG-evolved Hamiltonian, in particular the truncation of the harmonic-oscillator model space used for solving the SRG flow equations and the omission of the induced beyond-3N contributions of the evolved Hamiltonian. Both truncations lead to sizable effects in the upper p-shell and beyond and we present options to remedy these truncation effects. The analysis of the different truncations is a first step towards a systematic uncertainty quantification of all stages of the calculation.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that converged ab initio calculations with explicit chiral three-nucleon (3N) interactions can be extended beyond the lower p-shell, but only if truncations from the similarity renormalization group (SRG) evolution, specifically the harmonic-oscillator model-space truncation and the omission of induced beyond-3N contributions, are carefully controlled and remedied, as these truncations produce sizable effects in the upper p-shell and beyond.

## Method

The authors compute three-nucleon (3N) matrix elements starting from a harmonic-oscillator (HO) basis in three-body Jacobi coordinates, then transform them to a JT-coupled storage scheme for efficient on-the-fly decoupling to the m-scheme during many-body calculations. They perform similarity renormalization group (SRG) evolution of the chiral NN+3N Hamiltonian at the three-body level using an HO representation, introducing a frequency conversion tool to overcome HO model-space limitations. The importance-truncated no-core shell model (IT-NCSM) is employed with explicit 3N interactions to perform converged calculations, and the impact of truncations in the SRG model space and the omission of induced beyond-3N contributions is analyzed.

## Key numbers

- For a mid p-shell nucleus at N_max=8 (corresponding to E_3max=11), storing m-scheme 3N matrix elements requires about 33 GB in single precision exploiting all symmetries.
- For the same N_max=8 p-shell calculation, storing JT-coupled 3N matrix elements requires only 0.4 GB in single precision, a reduction of up to three orders of magnitude.
- The memory required to store T-coefficients, antisymmetrized-Jacobi, JT-coupled, and m-scheme 3N matrix elements is shown as a function of the maximum three-body energy quantum number E_3max, with the m-scheme storage growing dramatically beyond E_3max=11.

## Context

This work extends the domain of ab initio no-core shell model calculations beyond the lower p-shell, which was previously limited by the huge number of m-scheme 3N matrix elements that needed to be stored (as in earlier NCSM calculations by Maris et al. (2013), Jurgenson et al. (2013, 2011, 2009)). The JT-coupled storage scheme with on-the-fly decoupling, first introduced by Roth and Langhammer (2011), enables calculations with explicit 3N interactions in larger model spaces. The analysis of SRG truncation effects is a first step toward systematic uncertainty quantification, and the resulting evolved Hamiltonians have already been adopted in applications to medium-mass nuclei using coupled-cluster theory (Binder et al. 2013, 2013; Roth et al. 2012) and self-consistent Green's function methods (Somà et al. 2013, Cipollone et al. 2013).
