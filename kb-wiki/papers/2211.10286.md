# A new avenue in the search for CP violation: Moessbauer spectroscopy of 227-Ac

> This work proposes a new avenue in the search for CP-violating odd-electric and even-magnetic nuclear moments. A promising candidate to find such moments in the ground state is the quadrupole-deformed and octupole-correlated nucleus 227-actinium. In this nucleus, the 27.4-keV $E1$~transition that connects the $3/2^+$ parity-doublet partner and the $3/2^-$ ground state is perfectly suited to apply the sensitive technique of recoil-free selfabsorption, commonly known as Mössbauer spectroscopy. In this experimental approach, the lifetime of the $3/2^+$ upper parity-doublet partner allows an estimate of the lower limit of $ΔE = 2\cdot Γ_γ$= $23.7(1) \times 10^{-9}$ eV for the achievable energy resolution to be made. This resolution must be exceeded by the interaction of a CP-violating moment and the corresponding multipole moment of the field distribution in the lattice. This work presents the first ideas for patterns caused by CP-violating moments on the expected quadrupole splitting and nuclear Zeeman effect.

## Key claim

Mossbauer spectroscopy of the 27.4-keV E1 transition in 227-Ac could detect CP-violating odd-electric and even-magnetic nuclear moments if the interaction energy of such a moment with the corresponding multipole moment of the lattice field distribution exceeds the natural-linewidth-limited energy resolution of Delta E = 23.7(1) x 10^{-9} eV, and the paper presents only the first conceptual ideas for the expected spectral patterns rather than experimental results.

## Method

The authors propose applying recoil-free resonant absorption (Mossbauer spectroscopy) to the 27.4-keV E1 transition connecting the 3/2+ parity-doublet partner and the 3/2- ground state in 227-Ac. They analyze how the presence of CP-violating odd-electric moments (E1, E3) and even-magnetic moments (M2) would perturb the standard quadrupole splitting and nuclear Zeeman effect patterns observable in Mossbauer spectra. The required electric and magnetic field distributions at the nuclear position in candidate lattice materials would be calculated using density functional theory.

## Key numbers

- The 27.4-keV E1 transition connects the 3/2+ upper parity-doublet partner and the 3/2- ground state of 227-Ac, with a parity-doublet energy difference Delta E_PD = 27.369(11) keV
- The natural line width of the upper level is Gamma_gamma = 11.8(1) x 10^{-9} eV, giving an achievable energy resolution of Delta E = 2 * Gamma_gamma = 23.7(1) x 10^{-9} eV
- The conversion coefficient for the 27.4-keV E1 transition is alpha_C = 3.54(5), allowing approximately 22% of decays to proceed via gamma-ray emission
- The upper parity-doublet partner has a half-life of T_1/2,ul = 38.52(19) ns
- An energy shift of 11.74 x 10^{-9} eV corresponds to a sample velocity of 1 cm/s in the Mossbauer drive
- The ground-state half-life of 227-Ac is 21.772(3) years

## Context

This work extends the long-standing search for CP-violating nuclear moments, which are predicted to be enhanced in nuclei with quadrupole-octupole deformed pear shapes as discussed by Spevak, Auerbach, Naftali, Zelevinsky, Dobaczewski, and others. The proposal builds on systematics of E1 strength in quadrupole-octupole coupled 1- levels established by Kneissl, Pitz, and coworkers (1996, 2007), and on observations of enhanced B(E3) strength in radium isotopes by Butler, Gaffney, and collaborators. 227-Ac is identified as the nucleus with the lowest established parity-doublet energy difference (27.4 keV) among candidates in the A approximately 224 mass region, making it the most promising system for this technique. The work enables a new experimental pathway to constrain CP-violating Schiff moments and magnetic quadrupole moments, complementing existing approaches such as atomic and molecular electric dipole moment searches.
