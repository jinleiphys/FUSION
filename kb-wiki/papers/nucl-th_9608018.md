# The Chromo-Dielectric Soliton Model: Quark Self Energy and Hadron Bags

> The chromo-dielectric soliton model (CDM) is Lorentz- and chirally-invariant. It has been demonstrated to exhibit dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and spatial confinement in the locally uniform approximation. We here study the full nonlocal quark self energy in a color-dielectric medium modeled by a two parameter Fermi function. Here color confinement is manifest. The self energy thus obtained is used to calculate quark wave functions in the medium which, in turn, are used to calculate the nucleon and pion masses in the one gluon exchange approximation. The nucleon mass is fixed to its empirical value using scaling arguments; the pion mass (for massless current quarks) turns out to be small but non-zero, depending on the model parameters.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the full nonlocal quark self-energy in the chromo-dielectric soliton model (CDM) yields a small but non-zero pion mass for massless current quarks, rather than the exactly zero mass demanded by Goldstone's theorem, indicating that the approximation schemes used (including the one gluon exchange approximation and the treatment of center-of-mass motion) are imperfect.

## Method

The authors solved the linearized (Abelian) gluon propagator in an inhomogeneous color-dielectric medium modeled by a two-parameter Fermi function, then solved the Schwinger-Dyson equation for the quark self-energy along the imaginary energy axis to avoid mass poles. The self-energy was analytically continued to the real energy axis and used as a nonlocal scalar potential in the Dirac equation to obtain quark wave functions. Hadron masses for the nucleon and pion were calculated in the one gluon exchange approximation with approximate center-of-mass corrections, and the nucleon mass was fixed to its empirical value using scaling arguments.

## Key numbers

- The ultraviolet momentum cutoff was chosen as Lambda_CDM = 5.0 fm^{-1}
- The angular momentum summation was limited by l_max = R * omega_max, where R is the radius parameter from the Fermi function
- The single-quark energy epsilon was calculated down to kappa_v = 0.05 without showing signs of divergence
- The color matrix element <lambda_1 * lambda_2> has the value -16/3 for the pion and -8/3 for the nucleon
- The nucleon mass was fixed to its empirical value m_N = 938 MeV using scaling relations
- The pion mass (for massless current quarks) was found to be small but non-zero, depending on the model parameters R and A

## Context

This work extends the chromo-dielectric soliton model previously studied by Krein et al. (1990s) in the locally uniform approximation, which demonstrated dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and spatial confinement but did not address color confinement or the nonlocal nature of the self-energy. The paper also builds on the Friedberg-Lee nontopological soliton model (1970s) by removing the direct quark-sigma coupling term to preserve chiral invariance. The results enable future fully self-consistent calculations of hadron properties within the CDM framework, with the non-zero pion mass serving as a diagnostic for improving the approximation schemes.
