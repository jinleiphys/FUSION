# Probing exotic multi-proton emitters: A Gamow shell model study of proton-rich fluorine and neon isotopes beyond the drip line

> We investigate proton-rich systems beyond the proton drip line, focusing on the notably poorly known 13F and 15Ne and the yet unobserved 14Ne, whose structure properties remain weakly constrained. Using the Gamow shell model (GSM), which consistently incorporates both inter-nucleon correlations and couplings to the particle continuum, we study oxygen, fluorine, and neon isotopes with mass A=12-16. Taking 8C as an inert core, the GSM Hamiltonian based on an effective field theory nucleon-nucleon interaction is optimized for this proton-rich region. The constructed Hamiltonian reproduces the low-lying spectra and decay properties of fluorine and neon isotopes beyond the proton drip line. We quantify many-body configuration and average partial-wave occupancies to elucidate the structural evolution of the drip line nuclei 12-14O, 13-15F, and 14-16Ne. In particular, multi-proton separation energies and spectroscopic factors are analyzed in detail, leading to a prediction for the unresolved ground state of 13F. Furthermore, the candidate 4p emitter 14Ne is theoretically predicted for the first time, providing valuable guidance for future experimental investigations.

## Key claim
The Gamow shell model predicts that the yet-unobserved nucleus ¹⁴Ne is a candidate 4-proton emitter, providing the first theoretical prediction of its existence and decay properties, though the ground-state energy and width are calculated with truncation uncertainties below 300 keV.

## Method
The authors employed the Gamow shell model (GSM) with a ⁸C inert core, using a Berggren basis that includes bound, resonant, and scattering states to treat continuum coupling. The Hamiltonian was based on a pionless effective field theory nucleon-nucleon interaction at leading and next-to-leading order, with parameters optimized by fitting to experimental binding energies of low-lying states in nitrogen, oxygen, fluorine, and neon isotopes. The valence space included proton partial waves 0p₁/₂, 1s₁/₂, and 0d₅/₂ as pole states plus complex contours, while neutron valence space was restricted to harmonic oscillator p partial waves; truncation schemes of 3p–3h or 4p–4h were applied depending on the nucleus.

## Key numbers
- The optimized GSM Hamiltonian achieves a root mean square deviation of 0.157 MeV between theoretical and experimental energies for the fitted states.
- For ¹⁵F, the ground state (1/2⁺) is unbound by 1.53 MeV relative to the ¹⁴O+p threshold, with a spectroscopic factor C²S(s₁/₂)=0.92; the 5/2⁺ state is unbound by 3.28 MeV with C²S(d₅/₂)=0.94.
- For ¹⁶Ne, the ground state is unbound by 1.627 MeV relative to the ¹⁴O+2p threshold, close to the experimental value of 1.466(20) MeV.
- For ¹⁴F, the single-proton separation energy is 1.66 MeV, consistent with the experimental value of 1.56 MeV.
- For ¹³F, the predicted ground state (1/2⁺) lies 3.59 MeV above the ³p decay threshold to ¹⁰C with width 1.225 MeV; the 5/2⁺ excited state is at 4.91 MeV with width 0.732 MeV.
- For ¹⁴Ne, the ground state (0⁺) is predicted with a dominant configuration of π{(1s₁/₂)²(0p₁/₂)²}⊗ν(0p₃/₂)².

## Context
This work extends previous GSM calculations for ¹⁵F and ¹⁶Ne (PhysRevC.103.044319, 2021) and provides the first microscopic prediction for ¹⁴Ne, which has not been observed experimentally. The results build on experimental studies of ¹³F via charge-exchange reactions (PhysRevLett.126.132501, 2021) and ¹⁵Ne via two-neutron knockout (PhysRevLett.112.132502, 2014). The predictions for ¹⁴Ne as a 4-proton emitter offer guidance for future experimental searches using invariant-mass or resonance-scattering techniques.
