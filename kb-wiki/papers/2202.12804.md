# Hadronization and Color Transparency

> In this article we review our work on the production of hadrons in the nuclear environment. We work with a string-breaking model for the initial production of hadrons and with a quantum-kinetic transport model (GiBUU) to describe the final state interactions of the newly formed (pre)hadrons. The latter are determined both by the formation times and by the time-development of the hadron-hadron cross section. We first show that only a linear time-dependence is able to describe the available hadronization data. We then compare with detailed data from HERMES and JLAB; very good agreement is reached in all reactions studied without any tuning of parameters. We also repeat predictions of spectra for pions and kaons at JLAB@12GeV. We finally explain the absence of CT effects in the recent experiment on proton transparencies in quasielastic (QE) events on nuclei. We propose to look instead for CT effects on protons in semi-inclusive DIS (SIDIS) events.

# Hadronization and Color Transparency

## Key claim

The absence of color transparency (CT) effects in recent quasielastic (e,e'p) proton transparency experiments is explained because in QE events the proton's production and formation times are both zero, no string is stretched, so the cross section never evolves from a point-like configuration; the authors propose that CT should instead be observable for protons produced in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) events, where formation times of 3–5 fm are comparable to nuclear radii.

## Method

The authors use the GiBUU quantum-kinetic transport model, based on the Kadanoff-Baym equations, to describe final-state interactions of hadrons produced in hard photonuclear reactions. The initial hadron production is modeled via the LUND string fragmentation model as implemented in PYTHIA, from which production and formation times are extracted event-by-event without free parameters. The time-development of the prehadronic cross section is tested with constant, linear, and quadratic time-dependences, with the linear dependence (including a 1/Q²-dependent pedestal term) found to uniquely describe data across different kinematical regimes.

## Key numbers

- Formation time in the hadron restframe: τ_f ≈ (1 … 2) m_h, where m_h is the hadron mass in GeV and τ_f is obtained in fm.
- Formation time in the lab frame: t_f = γ τ_f = (0.7 … 1.4) ν, with numerical factors in fm/GeV weakly dependent on ν.
- For protons at JLAB energies, the lab formation time is about 0.8/ν fm, translating to 3–5 fm for t_f.
- At Q² up to ≈ 4 GeV², the transverse cross section for pion production is larger than the longitudinal one by about a factor of 2, contrary to pQCD expectation.
- The pedestal value X_0 in the time-dependent cross section formula is X_0 = n_lead * k / Q², with k = 1 GeV² and n_lead = 2 for mesons, 3 for baryons.
- For the EMC experiment at 100 and 280 GeV beam energy, the nuclear modification factor stays close to R = 1 because formation distances exceed nuclear radii.
- Proton transparency in quasielastic ¹²C(e,e'p) reactions was observed to be constant for Q² up to 14 GeV².

## Context

This work reviews and extends the authors' program of using string fragmentation and quantum-kinetic transport (GiBUU) to describe hadronization in nuclei, building on earlier studies by Dokshitzer et al. (1991) who proposed linear versus quadratic time-dependence for prehadronic cross sections. It contrasts with Glauber-based approaches by Larson (2006) and Cosyn (2007) that applied CT to both longitudinal and transverse amplitudes without modeling the elementary cross section, and with the Feynman-mechanism explanation by Miller and collaborators (2021) for the absence of CT in QE events. The results enable predictions for the upcoming JLAB 12 GeV program, particularly for kaon spectra where the interplay of prehadronic interactions and non-leading particle production can be tested.
