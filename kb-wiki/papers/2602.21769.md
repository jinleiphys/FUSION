# A Consistent Holographic Analysis of Anomaly-induced Charge Transport in the D3/D7 Model

> We propose a scheme to correctly incorporate the contribution of the chiral anomaly in the D3/D7 model to calculate chiral transport phenomena. To ensure the D7-brane wraps S^5 appropriately and the Wess-Zumino term is switched on, we allow the D7-brane to rotate in the compactified extra directions and perform the analysis accordingly. To demonstrate that this calculation procedure works well, we specifically compute the magnetoresistance in the D3/D7 model. We find that a finite axial chemical potential is realized and the negative magnetoresistance is enhanced by the anomaly contribution.

## Key claim

The authors propose a scheme to correctly incorporate the chiral anomaly contribution in the D3/D7 holographic model by allowing the D7-brane to rotate in the compactified extra directions, which switches on the Wess-Zumino term; they demonstrate that this procedure yields a finite axial chemical potential and enhances the negative magnetoresistance compared to calculations without the anomaly contribution.

## Method

The authors relax the previous ansatz where the D7-brane angle Psi was held constant, instead employing a rotating configuration Psi = omega t + Phi(u) to introduce a finite axial chemical potential mu_5 = omega/2. They compute the nonlinear electric conductivity in the presence of parallel electric and magnetic fields by solving the D7-brane action including both the Dirac-Born-Infeld term and the now-nonvanishing Wess-Zumino term, using a Legendre transformation to obtain conserved quantities related to the electric currents and the axial current divergence.

## Key numbers

* The axial chemical potential is given by mu_5 = omega/2, where omega is the angular velocity of the D7-brane rotation in the X^8-X^9 plane.
* The Hawking temperature of the dual gauge theory is T = sqrt(2)/(pi u_H), where u_H is the horizon location.
* The electric current j_x in the absence of anomaly (constant Psi) is given by j_x = (N E_x L^3 cos^3(theta(u_*)) sqrt(4 L^4 + (B_x^2 + E_x^2) u_H^4)) / (u_H (4 L^4 + E_x^2 u_H^4)^(1/4)), where u_* is the effective horizon defined by (E_x^2 - |g_tt| g_xx)|_(u=u_*) = 0.
* The resistivity rho in the absence of anomaly is rho = (u_H (4 L^4 + E_x^2 u_H^4)^(1/4)) / (N L^3 cos^3(theta(u_*)) sqrt(4 L^4 + (B_x^2 + E_x^2) u_H^4)).

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of nonlinear conductivity in the D3/D7 model by Ammon (2009) and Baumgartner (2017), which obtained negative magnetoresistance but without the chiral anomaly contribution because the Wess-Zumino term was not switched on due to insufficient winding of the D7-brane around the S^5. The rotating D7-brane configuration follows the approach of Hoyos (2011), Das (2010), and Guo (2016) for introducing axial chemical potential, and the analysis enables proper incorporation of anomaly-induced charge transport in holographic models of strongly coupled fermion systems, relevant for understanding negative magnetoresistance in Dirac and Weyl semimetals.
