# Deconfinement transition in a one-dimensional model at finite temperature

> We present a model for quark matter with a density dependent quark-quark (confining) potential, which allows to describe a deconfinement phase transition as the system evolves from a low density assembly of bound structures to a high density free Fermi gas of quarks. A proper account of the many-body correlations induced by the medium is crucial in order to disentangle this behaviour, which does not uniquely stem from the the naive density dependence of the interaction. We focus here on the effects of finite (non-zero) temperatures on the dynamical behaviour of the system. In particular we investigate the ground state energy per particle and the pair correlation function, from which one can extract the relevant information about the quarks being confined or not; the temperature dependence of the transition density is also derived.

## Key claim

The paper presents a one-dimensional, non-relativistic model of quark matter with a density-dependent confining potential that can describe a deconfinement phase transition, but the authors explicitly note that the model cannot identify a specific order parameter for a thermodynamic phase transition and instead uses an arbitrary definition of transition density based on the vanishing of medium-induced correlations.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Goldstone equation for the relative motion of two interacting quarks within the independent pair approximation, using a density-dependent potential V(x) = (1/2)x² exp(-cρ|x|) where ρ is the uniform density. At finite temperature, they modify the Pauli operator by replacing the zero-temperature step function with the Fermi-Dirac distribution and also introduce a temperature dependence into the interaction strength following the lattice QCD-inspired form σ(T) = σ₀ √(1 - (T/T_C)²) for T < T_C. The ground state energy per particle and pair correlation function are evaluated from the Bethe-Goldstone wavefunctions, and the transition density is defined as the density where the quantity ΔU(ρ) = 0, representing the average difference between bare potential and G-matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- The transition density at T=0 occurs at ρ ≈ 0.19 (in the model's dimensionless units), where the pair correlation function starts developing a Fermi gas component at large distances.
- At low densities (ρ ≤ 0.1) and T=0, the Bethe-Goldstone wavefunctions are close to bound states from the pure Schrödinger equation.
- The critical temperature T_C is arbitrarily fixed to equal twice the Fermi energy at ρ = 0.5, giving T_C = π²/4 in the model's units.
- The ground state energy per particle at T=0 coincides with the free Fermi gas kinetic energy for densities ρ > 1.0.
- At T/T_C = 0.1, the transition density is somewhat larger than at T=0, while at T/T_C = 0.995 the transition density drops to near zero.

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature results of Alberico, Nardi, and Quattrocolo (1995) to finite temperature, building on earlier non-relativistic constituent quark models by Horowitz, Horowitz, and Horowitz (1980s) and the density-dependent potential approach of Alberico, Boffi, Maieron, and Pizzochero (1994). The temperature dependence of the confining potential is motivated by lattice QCD calculations of the string tension by Pisarski (1980s). The model enables a microscopic description of the hadron-to-quark-gluon plasma phase transition, though the authors note it is preliminary due to being one-dimensional, non-relativistic, and lacking spin, colour, and gluonic degrees of freedom.
