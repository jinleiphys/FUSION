# Chiral extrapolations of baryon masses for unquenched three-flavor lattice simulations

> We have analyzed the chiral expansion of the baryon masses to second order in the quark masses, including strong isospin violation. The calculations are performed in Lorentz-invariant baryon chiral perturbation theory. Chiral extrapolation functions for three-flavor unquenched lattice simulations are derived. The matching to the two-flavor case and the sigma terms are also considered.

## Key claim

The authors derive chiral extrapolation functions for octet baryon masses to second order in quark masses (i.e., up to and including order \(p^4\)) using Lorentz-invariant baryon chiral perturbation theory with infrared regularization, including strong isospin violation effects \(\sim m_u - m_d\); however, they acknowledge that present-day lattice simulations lack the accuracy to resolve these fine isospin-breaking effects, so the expressions are provided for future use.

## Method

The calculation uses the effective chiral Lagrangian up to dimension four for mesons and meson-baryon interactions, with the spin-3/2 decuplet effects absorbed into low-energy constants. Baryon masses are computed from the two-point function self-energy at the baryon pole, expanded to chiral dimension four, using infrared regularization to separate soft chiral loop contributions from hard ones. The results are expressed in terms of quark masses \(\hat m = (m_u+m_d)/2\), \(m_s\), and the isospin-breaking parameter \(\varepsilon\) (the \(\pi^0-\eta\) mixing angle), retaining only linear terms in \(\varepsilon\).

## Key numbers

- The \(\pi^0-\eta\) mixing angle is numerically \(\varepsilon = 1 \times 10^{-2}\) when using leading isospin-violating corrections and electromagnetic effects.
- The axial-vector couplings used are \(D \simeq 0.81\) and \(F \simeq 0.46\) (from Ratcliffe).
- The chiral limit octet mass \(m_0\) is of order the scale of chiral symmetry breaking, \(\Lambda_\chi \simeq 4\pi F_\pi\).
- The second-order coefficients \(\gamma_{i,B}\) for the nucleon are: \(\gamma_{1,N} = -8b_0 - 4(b_D + b_F)\), \(\gamma_{2,N} = 4(-b_0 - b_D + b_F)\).
- The third-order coefficient for the nucleon from pion loops is \(\delta_{1,N} = -\frac{3}{4}D^2 - \frac{3}{2}DF - \frac{3}{4}F^2\).

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Borasoy and Meißner (1996) by using Lorentz-invariant baryon chiral perturbation theory with infrared regularization (Becher and Leutwyler, 1999) rather than heavy-baryon methods, and includes all strong isospin-breaking effects up to fourth order. It provides the necessary chiral extrapolation functions for three-flavor unquenched lattice QCD simulations, enabling future lattice studies to extrapolate baryon masses from unphysically large quark masses to the physical limit, and also discusses matching to the two-flavor case and sigma terms.
