# Flavor-changing hyperon decays with light invisible bosons

> We consider the strangeness-changing decays of hyperons into another baryon and missing energy which is carried away by a pair of invisible spinless bosons. Pursuing a model-independent approach and taking into account constraints from the kaon sector, we find that these hyperon modes can have large rates, especially if the bosons have parity-odd effective couplings to light quarks. In that case the rates could attain values which are well within the expected reach of the currently running BESIII experiment. Since the branching fractions of hyperon decays with missing energy are tiny in the standard model, observing them at ongoing or upcoming facilities would likely be indicative of new physics. In addition, we show that the kaon restrictions may be weakened or absent if the bosons have nonnegligible mass. Specifically, if the bosons interact with the quarks solely via an axial-vector current, in the mass region from about 115 to 175 MeV the constraints from rare kaon decays no longer apply and a couple of the hyperon modes become the only direct probes of such interactions. We also make comparisons with the previously explored new-physics scenario where the invisible particles are spin-1/2 fermions.

## Key claim

The authors show that strangeness-changing hyperon decays into another baryon plus missing energy carried by a pair of invisible spinless bosons can have rates well within the expected reach of the BESIII experiment, particularly if the bosons have parity-odd effective couplings to light quarks; this conclusion depends on the fact that kaon constraints are far weaker for parity-odd operators than for parity-even ones, and in the mass region from about 115 to 175 MeV the constraints from rare kaon decays no longer apply if the bosons interact solely via an axial-vector current.

## Method

The authors adopt a model-independent effective Lagrangian approach, starting from the most general SM-gauge-invariant dimension-six operators describing interactions of a complex spin-0 boson field with down-type quarks via dsφφ couplings. They compute the differential decay rates for hyperon modes (Λ→n, Σ⁺→p, Ξ⁰→Λ, Ξ⁰→Σ⁰, Ξ⁻→Σ⁻, and Ω⁻→Ξ⁻) and for the corresponding kaon modes (K→φφ, K→πφφ, K→ππ'φφ) using hadronic matrix elements estimated from flavor-SU(3) chiral perturbation theory at leading order, incorporating form-factor effects from hyperon semileptonic decay data.

## Key numbers

- SM branching fractions for hyperon decays with missing energy are all below 10⁻¹¹: Λ→nνν̅ = 7.1×10⁻¹³, Σ⁺→pνν̅ = 4.3×10⁻¹³, Ξ⁰→Λνν̅ = 6.3×10⁻¹³, Ξ⁰→Σ⁰νν̅ = 1.0×10⁻¹³, Ξ⁻→Σ⁻νν̅ = 1.3×10⁻¹³, Ω⁻→Ξ⁻νν̅ = 4.9×10⁻¹²
- Expected BESIII sensitivities: Λ→n = 3×10⁻⁷, Σ⁺→p = 4×10⁻⁷, Ξ⁰→Λ = 8×10⁻⁷, Ξ⁰→Σ⁰ = 9×10⁻⁷, Ω⁻→Ξ⁻ = 2.6×10⁻⁵
- For the case with only c_φ^P nonzero and m_φ=0, the K→φφ constraints give |c_φ^P|² < 1.1×10⁻¹⁶ GeV⁻², leading to upper limits: B(Λ→nφφ̅) < 1.3×10⁻⁷, B(Σ⁺→pφφ̅) < 3.7×10⁻⁸, B(Ξ⁰→Λφφ̅) < 2.0×10⁻⁸, B(Ξ⁰→Σ⁰φφ̅) < 2.5×10⁻⁷, B(Ξ⁻→Σ⁻φφ̅) < 3.1×10⁻⁷, B(Ω⁻→Ξ⁻φφ̅) < 1.7×10⁻⁶
- For the case with only c_φ^A nonzero and m_φ=0, the K→ππ'φφ constraints give |c_φ^A|² < 2.5×10⁻⁷, leading to upper limits: B(Λ→nφφ̅) < 4.5×10⁻⁸, B(Σ⁺→pφφ̅) < 1.2×10⁻⁸, B(Ξ⁰→Λφφ̅) < 6.3×10⁻⁹, B(Ξ⁰→Σ⁰φφ̅) < 8.5×10⁻⁸, B(Ξ⁻→Σ⁻φφ̅) < 1.1×10⁻⁷, B(Ω⁻→Ξ⁻φφ̅) < 5.0×10⁻⁷
- If the boson mass lies between about 115 and 175 MeV and couplings are purely axial-vector, the K→ππ'φφ constraints no longer apply, and Σ⁺→pφφ̅ and Ω⁻→Ξ⁻φφ̅ become the only direct probes

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Tandean (2019) which explored the scenario where invisible particles are spin-1/2 fermions, now considering spin-0 bosons instead. It builds on earlier studies of hyperon decays with missing energy by Li (2016) and Hu (2018) that focused on SM-like chiral structures, and on prior work by Bird (2004, 2006), He (2010), and Gninenko (2015) that addressed nonstandard bosons with ds couplings in specific new-physics contexts. The results show that hyperon modes can complement kaon measurements in probing new physics, with some hyperon modes covering broader boson mass ranges than the kaon modes.
