# Computation of Neutron Star Structure Using Modern Equation of State

> Using the modern equations of state derived from microscopic calculations, we have calculated the neutron star structure. For the neutron star, we have obtained a minimum mass about $0.1 {\rm M_{\odot}}$ which is nearly independent of the equation of state, and a maximum mass between $1.47 {\rm M_{\odot}}$ and $1.98 {\rm M_{\odot}}$ which is strongly dependent on the equation of state. It is shown that among the equations of state of neutron star matter which we have used, the stiffest one leads to higher maximum mass and radius and lower central density. It is seen that the given maximum mass for the Reid-93 equation of state shows a good consistency with the accurate observations of radio pulsars. We have indicated that the thickness of neutron star crust is very small compared to the predicted neutron star radius.

## Key claim
The paper calculates neutron star structure using modern equations of state derived from microscopic constrained variational calculations, finding a minimum mass near 0.1 solar masses that is nearly independent of the equation of state and a maximum mass between 1.47 and 1.98 solar masses that depends strongly on the equation of state. The authors hedge that the Reid-93 equation of state gives a maximum mass consistent with accurate radio pulsar observations, but only for that specific potential.

## Method
The authors numerically integrated the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation of hydrostatic equilibrium using equations of state for neutron star matter obtained from microscopic constrained variational calculations with cluster expansion of the energy functional. They employed the modern two-nucleon potentials AV18, Reid-93, and AV14, as well as the UV14+TNI potential which includes a three-nucleon interaction via two density-dependent terms. For densities below 0.05 fm^-3, they used the equation of state from Baym et al. (1971).

## Key numbers
- Minimum neutron star gravitational mass: about 0.1 M_sun, nearly independent of the equation of state
- Maximum neutron star gravitational mass range: between 1.47 M_sun and 1.98 M_sun, depending on the equation of state
- For the UV14+TNI equation of state, the speed of sound exceeds the speed of light above a density of 1.28 fm^-3 (mass density 29.61 x 10^14 g/cm^3)
- For a neutron star with mass 1.47 M_sun using the Reid-93 equation of state: crust thickness about 0.43 km, which is 5.2% of the predicted neutron star radius; core radius approximately 7.8 km
- For central densities above 7 x 10^14 g/cm^3, the neutron star radius becomes nearly constant
- For the noninteracting beta-stable matter equation of state, the maximum mass is 0.7 M_sun

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous microscopic constrained variational calculations (Bordbar 2002, Bordbar 2004) of the equation of state of neutron star matter using modern nucleon-nucleon potentials. The results are compared with observational mass determinations from binary radio pulsars (Weisberg 1984, Taylor 1989, Thorsett 1999) and with other theoretical calculations (Wiringa 1988, Baldo 1997, Lattimer 2001, Haensel 2003). The paper enables future comparisons between microscopic equations of state and neutron star observations, particularly for constraining the high-density behavior of nuclear matter.
