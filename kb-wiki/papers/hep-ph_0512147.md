# Comparison of Boltzmann Equations with Quantum Dynamics for Scalar Fields

> Boltzmann equations are often used to study the thermal evolution of particle reaction networks. Prominent examples are the computation of the baryon asymmetry of the universe and the evolution of the quark-gluon plasma after relativistic heavy ion collisions. However, Boltzmann equations are only a classical approximation of the quantum thermalization process which is described by the so-called Kadanoff-Baym equations. This raises the question how reliable Boltzmann equations are as approximations to the full Kadanoff-Baym equations. Therefore, we present in this paper a detailed comparison between the Kadanoff-Baym and Boltzmann equations in the framework of a scalar Phi^4 quantum field theory in 3+1 space-time dimensions. The obtained numerical solutions reveal significant discrepancies in the results predicted by both types of equations. Apart from quantitative discrepancies, on a qualitative level the universality respected by the Kadanoff-Baym equations is severely restricted in the case of Boltzmann equations. Furthermore, the Kadanoff-Baym equations strongly separate the time scales between kinetic and chemical equilibration. This separation of time scales is absent for the Boltzmann equation.

## Key claim

Boltzmann equations, which are derived from the full Kadanoff-Baym equations through a series of uncontrolled approximations including a gradient expansion, the Kadanoff-Baym ansatz, and the quasi-particle (on-shell) approximation, produce significant quantitative and qualitative discrepancies compared to the quantum dynamics, particularly in the separation of time scales between kinetic and chemical equilibration.

## Method

The authors numerically solved the Kadanoff-Baym equations derived from a 2PI effective action truncated at three-loop order for a real scalar Phi^4 quantum field theory in 3+1 dimensions, using a lattice discretization with N_s=32 sites and a_t m_R=0.06. They separately solved the corresponding Boltzmann equation derived from the same theory after applying the gradient expansion, Kadanoff-Baym ansatz, and quasi-particle approximation, using N_s=500 momentum modes and cubic spline interpolation for the collision integral. Both sets of equations were evolved for three different initial particle number distributions with the same average energy density, and the evolution of the effective particle number density was compared.

## Key numbers

- Lattice parameters for Kadanoff-Baym simulations: N_t = 500, N_s = 32, a_t m_R = 0.06, a_s m_R = 0.5, coupling lambda = 18
- Lattice parameters for Boltzmann simulations: N_s = 500, a_s m_R = 0.5, lambda = 18
- The coupling constant used in both simulations was lambda = 18

## Context

This work extends the comparison between Kadanoff-Baym and Boltzmann equations previously studied by Danielewicz (1982), Kohler (1995), Morawetz (1998), and Juchem (2003), but in the simpler framework of scalar Phi^4 theory rather than more realistic theories of leptogenesis or heavy ion collisions. The authors note that Berges (2001) had already shown that Kadanoff-Baym equations can describe thermalization in 1+1 dimensions where Boltzmann equations cannot, due to off-shell effects. This comparison enables future work to assess how large the quantum mechanical corrections are in realistic leptogenesis scenarios and quark-gluon plasma evolution, where Boltzmann equations are commonly used despite their known shortcomings.
