# Alternative Scenarios of Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions: I. Baryon Stopping

> Simulations of relativistic heavy-ion collisions within the three-fluid model employing a purely hadronic equation of state (EoS) and two versions of the EoS involving deconfinement transition are presented. The latter are an EoS with the first-order phase transition and that with a smooth crossover transition. The model setup is described in detail. The analysis is performed in a wide range of incident energies 2.7 GeV $< \sqrt{s_{NN}} <$ 39 GeV in terms of the center-of-mass energy. Results on proton and net-proton rapidity distributions are reported. Comparison with available data indicate certain preference of the crossover EoS. It is found that predictions within deconfinement-transition scenarios exhibit a "peak-dip-peak-dip" irregularity in the incident energy dependence of the form of the net-proton rapidity distributions in central collisions. This irregularity is a signal of deconfinement onset occurring in the hot and dense stage of the nuclear collision.

## Key claim

Simulations within the three-fluid model show that a "peak-dip-peak-dip" irregularity in the incident-energy dependence of net-proton rapidity distributions in central collisions is a robust signal of deconfinement onset, but the specific form of this irregularity depends on the equation of state used, with the crossover EoS showing only a weak wiggle that is preferred by available data.

## Method

The three-fluid dynamics (3FD) model was used to simulate relativistic heavy-ion collisions across a range of incident energies, employing three different equations of state: a purely hadronic EoS, an EoS with a first-order phase transition (2-phase EoS), and an EoS with a smooth crossover transition. The friction forces between fluids were tuned separately for each EoS to reproduce the observed stopping power, with the hadronic scenario requiring an enhanced friction factor that grows with energy while the deconfinement scenarios required no modification of hadronic friction. Proton and net-proton rapidity distributions were calculated and compared with experimental data from AGS and SPS collaborations.

## Key numbers

- Incident energy range: 2.7 GeV ≤ √sNN ≤ 39 GeV (center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair)
- Hadronic EoS friction enhancement factor: ξh² = 2.2 at √sNN = 17.3 GeV (top SPS energy)
- Formation time τ = 2 fm/c for hadronic EoS; τ = 0.17 fm/c for both 2-phase and crossover EoS
- Freeze-out energy density εfrz = 0.4 GeV/fm³ for all three scenarios
- Quark-gluon modification factor for 2-phase EoS: ξq² = 60 × (4mN²/s)
- Quark-gluon modification factor for crossover EoS: ξq² = 200 × (4mN²/s)
- At top AGS energy of 10A GeV, the antiproton-to-net-proton ratio at midrapidity is 0.03%
- Computations extended up to √sNN = 62.4 GeV, though results at this top energy are not fully accurate

## Context

This work extends previous 3FD simulations (Ivanov, 2013) that used only a hadronic EoS by incorporating deconfinement transitions via the EoS of Toneev (2006). It contrasts with conventional one-fluid hydrodynamics (Merdeev, 2011; Mishustin, 2010) which assumes immediate stopping and cannot treat incomplete baryon stopping at the initial stage, and with hybrid approaches (Bleicher, 2008; Nonaka, 2012) that use kinetic codes to prepare the initial fireball before hydrodynamic evolution. The results enable identification of the "peak-dip-peak-dip" pattern as a potential experimental signature of deconfinement onset in the beam-energy-scan programs at RHIC and SPS, as well as at future FAIR and NICA facilities.
