# Vorticity-induced modifications of chemical freeze-out in heavy-ion collisions

> We investigate the influence of global rotation on the chemical freeze-out parameters in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Within the framework of the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model, the freeze-out parameters are determined using commonly employed freeze-out criteria, namely the fixed energy per particle and the scaled entropy density, extended here to include rotational effects. We find that the presence of rotation leads to a systematic shift of the chemical freeze-out curve toward lower temperatures in the $T\text{--}μ_B$ phase diagram. The behavior of the electric charge and strangeness chemical potentials in the presence of rotation is also analyzed, providing the first systematic study of their rotational dependence within the HRG framework. Furthermore, we examine the impact of rotation on experimentally relevant observables, including hadron yield ratios and susceptibility ratios of conserved charges. Our results show that while particle yield ratios exhibit noticeable sensitivity to rotation, the conventional cumulant ratios remain comparatively less affected. This indicates that hadronic yield ratios may provide a more suitable observable for estimating the magnitude of rotational effects generated in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim
The paper claims that global rotation systematically shifts the chemical freeze-out curve toward lower temperatures in the \(T-\mu_B\) phase diagram, but this shift is conditional on the rotational strength and baryon chemical potential, with the effect becoming more pronounced at higher \(\omega\) and higher \(\mu_B\).

## Method
The authors extended the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model to include global rotation by modifying the single-particle energy spectrum to \(\varepsilon_{\ell,i} = \sqrt{k_r^2 + k_z^2 + m_i^2} - (\ell + s)\omega\), where \(\ell\) is the orbital angular momentum quantum number and \(s\) is the spin projection along the rotation axis. They imposed freeze-out conditions of constant average energy per particle (\(\varepsilon/n = 1.08\) GeV) and constant scaled entropy density (\(s/T^3 = 7\)), solving for the freeze-out parameters while enforcing strangeness neutrality and a fixed electric charge-to-baryon ratio of 0.4. All thermodynamic quantities were evaluated at the boundary of the system (\(r = R = 30\) GeV\(^{-1}\)) to probe maximal rotational effects.

## Key numbers
- Freeze-out temperature decreases by approximately 5 MeV for \(\omega = 0.005\) GeV and by approximately 40–50 MeV for \(\omega = 0.015\) GeV, using the \(\varepsilon/n\) criterion at \(\mu_B = 0.2\) and 0.6 GeV.
- For the \(s/T^3\) criterion, the freeze-out temperature decreases by approximately 3 MeV for \(\omega = 0.005\) GeV.
- The freeze-out curve at \(\omega = 0\) reproduces the non-rotating result from Cleymans et al. (2005) and is consistent with Lysenko et al. (2024) for \(\varepsilon/n = 0.951\) GeV.
- The leading-order coefficient \(q_1 = (\mu_Q/\mu_B)_{\mathrm{LO}}\) becomes more negative with increasing \(\omega\), while \(s_1 = (\mu_S/\mu_B)_{\mathrm{LO}}\) increases with \(\omega\).
- At \(\mu_B = 0\) and \(T = 155\) MeV, particle number densities normalized to their zero-rotation values show sensitivity to \(\omega\) up to 0.015 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the study of external fields on freeze-out parameters, previously investigated for magnetic fields by Fukushima et al. (2016), who found that magnetic fields shift the freeze-out curve toward lower temperatures (inverse magnetic catalysis). The authors also build on the freeze-out criteria established by Cleymans et al. (1998, 2005) and the rotating HRG formalism developed by Fujimoto et al. (2021) and Mukherjee et al. (2023). The results suggest that hadron yield ratios may serve as more sensitive observables than cumulant ratios for estimating the magnitude of vorticity in heavy-ion collisions, enabling future experimental extraction of rotational effects from peripheral collision data.
