# Re-analysis of the nucleon space- and time-like electromagnetic form factors in a two-component model

> Recent experimental data on space-like and time-like form factors of the nucleon are analyzed in terms of a two-component model with a quark-like intrinsic three-quark structure and quark-antiquark pairs.

# Re-analysis of the nucleon space- and time-like electromagnetic form factors in a two-component model

## Key claim
The two-component model with a quark-like intrinsic three-quark structure and quark-antiquark pairs can fit recent neutron space-like form factor data, but only at the expense of a slight deterioration of the fit for proton space-like data and a major failure to describe neutron time-like data, unlike the 1973 parametrization which agreed with both proton and neutron time-like form factors.

## Method
The authors extend the 1973 two-component model (Iachello, Jackson, Lande, 1973) by introducing a direct coupling of the intrinsic three-quark structure to the Pauli form factor \(F_2^V\), parametrized as \(g(Q^2)/(1+\gamma Q^2)\), while maintaining the perturbative QCD asymptotic behavior \(F_1 \rightarrow 1/Q^4\) and \(F_2 \rightarrow 1/Q^6\). They fit the five coefficients \(\beta_\rho, \beta_\omega, \beta_\varphi, \alpha_\rho, \alpha_\varphi\) and the parameter \(\gamma\) to recoil polarization JLab data for the ratios \(R_p = \mu_p G_{E_p}/G_{M_p}\) and \(R_n = \mu_n G_{E_n}/G_{M_n}\), combined with Rosenbluth separation data from SLAC for \(G_{M_p}\) and \(G_{M_n}\). For time-like form factors, the intrinsic structure is analytically continued with a phase \(\theta\), and the \(\rho\) meson propagator is continued using a Frazer-Fulco prescription.

## Key numbers
- Fitted parameters: \(\beta_\rho = 0.512\), \(\beta_\omega = 1.129\), \(\beta_\varphi = -0.263\), \(\alpha_\rho = 2.675\), \(\alpha_\varphi = -0.200\), \(\gamma = 0.515\) (GeV/c)\(^{-2}\)
- Spatial extent of intrinsic structure: \(\langle r^2 \rangle^{1/2} \simeq 0.49\) fm (compared to 0.34 fm in Iachello (2001))
- Phase for time-like analytic continuation: \(\theta = 0.397\) rad \(\simeq 22.7^\circ\) (compared to \(\simeq 53^\circ\) in Wan (2003))
- Meson cloud coupling strength: \(\alpha_\rho = 2.675\) (compared to 3.706 in Iachello (2001))
- Vector meson masses used: \(m_\rho = 0.776\) GeV, \(m_\omega = 0.783\) GeV, \(m_\varphi = 1.019\) GeV
- Effective \(\rho\) width: \(\Gamma_\rho = 0.112\) GeV
- In the 1973 model, the zero in \(R_p\) occurs at \(\simeq 8\) (GeV/c)\(^2\)

## Context
This work extends the two-component model of Iachello, Jackson, and Lande (1973) by identifying the intrinsic structure with three valence quarks and adding a direct quark coupling to the Pauli form factor, motivated by relativistic constituent quark models (Miller, 2002; Salme, 2002) and perturbative QCD constraints (Brodsky, 1973; Belitsky, 2003). The analysis addresses inconsistencies between recoil polarization and Rosenbluth separation data, and between neutron space-like and time-like data previously noted by Hammer et al. (2003) and Wan (2003). The results enable future measurements at JLab (experiments E01-109, PR04-003) and FRASCATI-DAFNE to resolve the remaining discrepancies between proton and neutron data and between space-like and time-like regimes.
