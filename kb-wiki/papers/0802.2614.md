# Fission barriers in neutron-proton isospin plane for heavy neutron-rich nuclei

> We discuss the sensitivity of fission barrier for heavy neutron-rich nuclei to fission paths in the two dimensional neutron-proton quadrupole plane. To this end, we use the constrained Skyrme-Hartree-Fock + BCS method, and examine the difference of fission barriers obtained with three constraining operators, that is, the neutron, proton, and mass quadrupole operators. We investigate $^{220}$U, $^{236}$U, and $^{266}$U, %from proton-rich to neutron-rich uranium isotopes, that is relevant to r-process nucleosynthesis. We find that the fission barrier heights are almost the same among the three constraining operators even for neutron-rich nuclei, indicating that the usual way to calculate fission barriers with the mass quadrupole operator is well justified. We also discuss the difference between proton and neutron deformation parameters along the fission paths.

# Fission barriers in neutron-proton isospin plane for heavy neutron-rich nuclei

## Key claim

The fission barrier heights for neutron-rich uranium isotopes are almost the same whether one constrains the mass quadrupole operator, the proton quadrupole operator, or the neutron quadrupole operator, indicating that the standard practice of using the mass quadrupole operator is justified even for neutron-rich nuclei. This conclusion holds for the three nuclei studied ($^{220}$U, $^{236}$U, and $^{266}$U), but the authors note that the potential energy surface is steep along the isovector degree of freedom, which explains the insensitivity.

## Method

The authors used the constrained Skyrme-Hartree-Fock + BCS method with the SLy4 Skyrme functional and a delta pairing force, implemented in the code SKYAX assuming reflection and axially symmetric nuclear shapes. They calculated fission barriers for three uranium isotopes ($^{220}$U, $^{236}$U, and $^{266}$U) using three different constraining operators: the mass quadrupole operator (total constraint), the proton quadrupole operator (proton constraint), and the neutron quadrupole operator (neutron constraint), and compared the resulting barrier heights and deformation parameters along the fission paths.

## Context

This work extends the study of fission barriers in the neutron-proton isospin plane, which had previously been applied to light neutron-rich nuclei such as $^{16}$C and $^{20}$O (see the thesis of N. W. L. and related work by the same group), and to actinide nuclei in Refs. Dobrowolski and GHFB. The results justify the standard mean-field approach of using mass multipole moments as constraining operators even for neutron-rich nuclei relevant to r-process nucleosynthesis, where one might expect separate proton and neutron constraints to be necessary. This enables systematic calculations of fission barriers for many neutron-rich nuclei without needing to treat proton and neutron deformations separately, which would be computationally demanding when including higher multipole moments.
