# Structure of $λ^*$(1405) and the $λ^*$-Meson-Baryon Coupling Constants

> Within an extended chiral constituent quark model, three- and five-quark structure of the $S_{01}$ resonance $Λ(1405)$ is investigated with respect to the coupling constants $g^2_{Λ^* πΣ}$ and $g^2_{Λ^* \bar{K} N}$. Our findings corroborate with about 50% of five-quark admixture in the $Λ(1405)$ needed in reproducing the strong decay width, $Γ_{Λ(1405)\to (Σπ)^\circ}$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Λ(1405) resonance contains approximately 50% five-quark admixture, but this conclusion depends on which observable is used: the coupling constant g²_Λ*K̅N gives P_5q = (55 ± 1)%, while the ratio R = g²_Λ*K̅N / g²_Λ*πΣ gives P_5q = (48 ± 3)%, both consistent with the earlier result from the strong decay width.

## Method

Within an extended chiral constituent quark model, the authors calculated the transition amplitudes for Λ(1405) decays to πΣ and K̅N using a Fock space that includes three-quark (qqq) and five-quark (qqqqq̅) components with configuration mixing. They extracted the coupling constants g²_Λ*πΣ and g²_Λ*K̅N as functions of the five-quark admixture percentage P_5q by evaluating diagonal and non-diagonal transition amplitudes in the nonrelativistic chiral constituent quark model.

## Key numbers

- g²_Λ*πΣ / 4π = 0.047 ± 0.007, extracted from T-matrix effective-range expansion (Kim, year not given)
- g²_Λ*K̅N / 4π = 0.32 ± 0.02, extracted from T-matrix effective-range expansion (Kim, year not given)
- Ratio R = g²_Λ*K̅N / g²_Λ*πΣ = 6.8 ± 1.0, about one order of magnitude larger than the pure SU(3) singlet value of 2/3
- From g²_Λ*K̅N data, the five-quark admixture is P_5q = (55 ± 1)%
- From the ratio R, the five-quark admixture is P_5q = (48 ± 3)%
- The predicted g²_Λ*πΣ varies from 0.031 at 0% five-quark admixture to a maximum of about 0.065 at P_5q ≈ 46%, then returns to 0.031
- The strong decay width Γ_Λ(1405)→(Σπ)° = 50 ± 2 MeV is reproduced with P_5q ≈ 50%

## Context

This work extends the chiral constituent quark model approach previously applied to non-strange baryons (nucleon, Δ, P_11(1440), S_11(1535)) into the strangeness sector, building on the authors' earlier study of Λ(1405) electromagnetic and strong decays (An, year not given). The results differ significantly from prior theoretical approaches: current algebra (Weil, year not given; G-M, year not given) gave R = 3.2, potential models (Weil, year not given; Dalitz, year not given) gave R = 4.8, dispersion relations (Martin, year not given) gave R = 4.0, and asymptotic SU(3) symmetry (Oneda, year not given) gave R = 4.8. The findings enable further constraints on the internal quark structure of Λ(1405) and will benefit from ongoing experimental programs at DAΦNE and Jefferson Lab.
