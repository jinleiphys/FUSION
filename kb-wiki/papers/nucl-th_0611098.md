# Critical review of deeply bound kaonic nuclear states

> We critically revise the recent claims of narrow deeply bound kaonic states and show that at present there is no convincing experimental evidence for their existence. In particular, we discuss in details the claim of K- pp deeply bound state associated to a peak seen in the Lambda p invariant mass spectrum from K- nuclear absorption reactions by the FINUDA collaboration. An explicit theoretical simulation shows that the peak is simply generated from a two-nucleon absorption process, like K- pp --> Lambda p, followed by final-state interactions of the produced particles with the residual nucleus.

# Critical review of deeply bound kaonic nuclear states

## Key claim
There is no convincing experimental evidence for the existence of narrow deeply bound kaonic states; the peak in the Λp invariant mass spectrum at 2255 MeV reported by the FINUDA collaboration as a K⁻pp bound state is instead generated by two-nucleon absorption (K⁻pp → Λp) followed by final-state interactions of the produced particles with the residual nucleus, without requiring any exotic bound-state mechanism.

## Method
The authors performed a Monte Carlo simulation of stopped K⁻ absorption on nuclei (⁶Li, ⁷Li, ¹²C, ²⁷Al, ⁵¹V) using a local density approximation, where the kaon is absorbed from its atomic orbit (2p for light nuclei, 3d for ²⁷Al, 4f for ⁵¹V) by two nucleons within the local Fermi sea. The primary nucleon and Λ are then allowed to re-scatter with nucleons in the nucleus according to experimental NN and ΛN cross sections, with the ΛN cross section parameterized as σ_Λ = (39.66 − 100.45x + 92.44x² − 21.40x³)/p_LAB [mb] where x = Min(2.1 GeV, p_LAB). Events are filtered by the same experimental cuts (P_Λ > 300 MeV/c and cosθ < −0.8) and the Λp invariant mass distribution is constructed from events requiring at least one secondary collision.

## Key numbers
- The claimed K⁻pp bound state binding energy was B_{K⁻pp} = 115^{+6}_{-5}(stat)^{+2}_{-3}(syst) MeV with width Γ = 67^{+14}_{-11}(stat)^{+2}_{-3}(syst) MeV (FINUDA collaboration).
- The simulated Λp invariant mass peak appears at 2260–2270 MeV for all nuclei studied, matching the position of the main peak in the FINUDA data.
- The maximum number of allowed secondary collisions ranges from 2 for ⁶Li to 5 for ⁵¹V.
- The fraction of events corresponding to ground-state-to-ground-state transitions (removed from the simulation) is estimated to be smaller than 15% in ⁷Li.
- The experimental mixture used for comparison was 51% ¹²C, 35% ⁶Li, and 14% ⁷Li.

## Context
This work extends the earlier criticism by Oset and Toki (2006) of the deeply bound kaonic states predicted by Akaishi and collaborators (2002, 2005) from variational calculations, which claimed densities up to ten times normal nuclear density. The authors had previously reinterpreted the KEK-E471 proton peak from ⁴He(stopped K⁻,p) as two-nucleon absorption (Magas et al., 2006). The present paper provides a more realistic treatment using experimental ΛN cross sections rather than the earlier approximation σ_Λ = 2σ_N/3, and shows that the FINUDA Λp invariant mass spectrum is naturally explained without exotic bound states. The authors note that recent Faddeev and variational calculations using realistic K⁻N interactions predict few-nucleon kaonic states bound by 50–70 MeV with large widths of order 100 MeV, contradicting the earlier claims of extremely deep states.
