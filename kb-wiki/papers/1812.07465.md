# Jet structure in integrated EPOS3-HQ approach

> We report on the first results for jet observables from a time-like parton cascade integrated with hydrodynamic evolution within the EPOS3-HQ framework. The hard (jet) partons are produced along with soft partons in the initial state EPOS approach. The soft partons, represented by strings, melt into a thermalized medium which is described by a 3 dimensional event-by-event viscous hydrodynamic approach. The jet partons then propagate in the hydrodynamically expanding medium. The total jet energy gets progressively `degraded' when the partons reaching a thermal energy scale are melted into the hydrodynamic medium via the source terms. The full evolution proceeds in a parallel mode, without separating the thermalized and jet parts. We demonstrate how the transverse expansion of the medium affects the jet structure, and how the medium itself is affected by the jet recoil.

# Jet structure in integrated EPOS3-HQ approach

## Key claim
The authors present first results for jet observables from a time-like parton cascade integrated with hydrodynamic evolution within the EPOS3-HQ framework, showing that the transverse expansion of the medium significantly affects the angular structure of jets, but the results are limited to parton-level distributions without hadronization or jet finding algorithms.

## Method
Hard (jet) partons and soft partons are produced together in the EPOS3 initial state; the soft partons melt into a thermalized medium described by 3-dimensional event-by-event viscous hydrodynamics using the vHLLE code. Jet partons propagate through the hydrodynamically expanding medium, undergoing radiative energy loss via increased virtuality (modeled after YaJEM with transport coefficient \(\hat{q}_R = 210 \cdot T^3 / (1 + 53 \cdot T)\)) and collisional energy loss via a longitudinal drag force, with partons that reach thermal energies being melted into the medium via source terms that conserve total energy and momentum.

## Key numbers
- Minimal virtuality scale for parton cascade evolution: \(Q_\downarrow = 0.6\) GeV
- Transport coefficient parametrization: \(\hat{q}_R = 210 \cdot T^3 / (1 + 53 \cdot T)\)
- Ratio of transport coefficient to drag coefficient: \(\hat{q}_R / A = (0.09 + 0.715 \cdot T / 0.16)\) GeV
- Collision energy for central PbPb events: \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2.76\) TeV
- Centrality class for hydrodynamic calculation: 0-5% central PbPb collisions
- Initial virtuality threshold for selected jets in trajectory plots: \(Q_\uparrow > 10\) GeV

## Context
This work extends the EPOS3-HQ framework (Werner, 2013) by coupling a time-like parton cascade (Rohrmoser, 2018) with hydrodynamic evolution, building on earlier studies that modeled the QGP medium with varying complexity from constant-temperature blobs (Zigic, 2018) to event-by-event hydrodynamics (He, 2018). The results demonstrate that precise modeling of the background medium evolution, particularly radial flow, is essential for quantitative jet structure description, as emphasized by recent community studies (Cao, 2018). The approach enables future inclusion of hadronization and jet finding algorithms for comparison with experimental data.
