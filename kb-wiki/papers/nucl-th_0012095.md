# Mean-field models and superheavy elements

> We discuss the performance of two widely used nuclear mean-field models, the relativistic mean-field theory (RMF) and the non-relativistic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach (SHF), with particular emphasis on the description of superheavy elements (SHE). We provide a short introduction to the SHF and RMF, the relations between these two approaches and the relations to other nuclear structure models, briefly review the basic properties with respect to normal nuclear observables, and finally present and discuss recent results on the binding properties of SHE computed with a broad selection of SHF and RMF parametrisations.

# Mean-field models and superheavy elements

## Key claim

The paper argues that both the relativistic mean-field (RMF) and non-relativistic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) models can describe superheavy elements (SHE) with reasonable accuracy, but the predictive power is limited by systematic differences between the two approaches, particularly in nuclear matter properties such as symmetry energy and incompressibility, that become amplified when extrapolating to the superheavy region.

## Method

The authors compare ten parametrisations (seven SHF: SkM*, SkT6, SkP, Zσ, SLy6, SkI3, SkI4; three RMF: NL-Z, NL3, TM1) by computing binding energies, charge radii, surface thicknesses, and nuclear matter properties for normal nuclei and known superheavy elements. Each parametrisation is supplemented with a zero-range delta pairing force (with separately adjusted proton and neutron strengths) and a center-of-mass correction, and the calculations are performed at the strict mean-field level without beyond-mean-field correlations.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy errors for normal nuclei are 0.4% or below for all chosen forces.
- The experimental isotopic shift in heavy Pb isotopes is δ⟨r²⟩ = r²(²¹⁴Pb) − r²(²⁰⁸Pb) = 0.6 fm²; all RMF forces reproduce this value well, while conventional SHF forces (with b₄ = b̃₄) fall short.
- Nuclear matter incompressibility K∞ for SHF forces clusters around 230 MeV, with SkP as an exception at a lower value; RMF forces give K∞ = 202 MeV (NL-Z), 271 MeV (NL3), and 281 MeV (TM1).
- Symmetry energy coefficient asym is approximately 30 MeV for most SHF forces, but RMF forces yield asym ≈ 40 MeV.
- The RMF forces and the two SHF forces with extended spin-orbit splitting (SkI3, SkI4) stay within the 0.35% error band for binding energies of known superheavy nuclei, while conventional SHF forces fall below this bound.

## Context

This review extends earlier work on mean-field descriptions of exotic nuclei and superheavy elements, building on the liquid-drop model (LDM) and macroscopic-microscopic (mac-mic) approaches of Möller and collaborators. The paper relates SHF and RMF to each other through a non-relativistic and zero-range expansion, and connects to the ETFSI approach (which derives a mac-mic model from SHF) and the Thomas-Fermi approach of Myers. The results enable systematic comparison of different mean-field parametrisations for predicting properties of yet-unobserved superheavy nuclei, particularly the location of the next spherical shell closures.
