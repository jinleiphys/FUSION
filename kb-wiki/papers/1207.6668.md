# Cooper pair correlations and energetic knock-out reactions

> Two-nucleon removal (or knock-out) reactions at intermediate energies are a developing tool for both nuclear spectroscopy and for the study of certain nucleon correlations in very exotic and some stable nuclei. We present an overview of these reactions with specific emphasis on the nature of the two-nucleon correlations that can be probed. We outline future possibilities and tests needed to fully establish these sensitivities.

## Key claim

Two-nucleon removal reactions at intermediate energies can probe the spin structure of nuclear wave functions, but the sensitivity to spin-singlet (S=0) nucleon pairs is not a generic feature and must be evaluated on a case-by-case basis, as the reaction mechanism makes no intrinsic selection of the spin S of the removed pair.

## Method

The authors develop a theoretical framework for two-nucleon removal reactions using eikonal reaction dynamics, where the projectile grazes a light target nucleus (e.g., beryllium or carbon) at incident energies above 80 MeV per nucleon. The structure input comes from truncated-basis shell-model calculations that provide two-nucleon amplitudes (TNA), which are then re-expressed in LS-coupling to analyze the role of spin S. The observables are computed from the joint-position probability ρ_f(r₁,r₂) of the two nucleons in the projectile ground state, integrated over the beam-direction coordinates to obtain the projected densities seen by the target.

## Key numbers

- Incident beam energies are typically >80 MeV per nucleon with v/c ≥ 0.35
- The probed volume for Cooper pairs at the nuclear surface has dimensions of 2–3 fm
- For 2p removal from ²⁸Mg at 82 MeV per nucleon: the inclusive experimental cross section is 1.50(10) mb, with the theoretical inclusive cross section being 3.02 mb
- The S=0 component contributes 64% of the computed inclusive cross section for ²⁸Mg(–2p)
- For the 0⁺ ground-state transition in ²⁸Mg(–2p): the S=0 fraction of the overlap is 86%, and the S=0 contribution to the cross section is 90% (1.083 mb out of 1.190 mb)
- For the 2⁺ state at 2.02 MeV: the S=0 fraction is 18%, contributing 22% of the partial cross section (0.071 mb out of 0.327 mb)
- For the 4⁺ state at 3.50 MeV: the S=0 fraction is 38%, contributing 49% of the partial cross section (0.523 mb out of 1.046 mb)
- For the 2₂⁺ state at 3.70 MeV: the S=0 fraction is 50%, contributing 54% of the partial cross section (0.250 mb out of 0.458 mb)

## Context

This work extends earlier investigations of one-neutron removal from halo nuclei (OAA92; Han96; HaT03) and builds on the theoretical framework for two-nucleon removal developed in Refs. TPB04, ToB06, ecs1, ecs2, and ecs3. The authors contrast this approach with low-energy two-nucleon transfer reactions like (p,t), which predominantly select S=0 pairs through the light-ion transfer vertex. The paper enables future studies of pairing correlations in exotic nuclei, including cross-shell excitations near closed shells (e.g., ⁴⁸Ca), and opens the possibility of connecting final-state two-nucleon correlations to those in the projectile entrance channel.
