# Light antiproton-nucleus systems at low energies with the ab initio NCSM/RGM method

> The availability of low-energy antiproton beams at the CERN Antiproton Decelerator has renewed interest in using antimatter as a probe of nuclear structure and in forming exotic antiprotonic few-body systems. In this work, we extend the ab initio No-Core Shell Model combined with the Resonating Group Method (NCSM/RGM), which was successfully applied to light-nucleus structure and reactions, to antiproton-nucleus dynamics at low energies. The NCSM/RGM formalism is adapted to antiproton projectiles by removing the requirement of antisymmetrization under exchange of target and projectile constituents, while retaining a fully microscopic description of the nuclear target and the relative motion. We focus on the lightest systems, ${\bar p}+d$, ${\bar p}+{}^3 \mathrm{H}$, and ${\bar p}+{}^3\mathrm{He}$, for which benchmarking against exact solutions of the Schrödinger equation enables stringent validation and helps disentangle methodological uncertainties -- e.g., those associated with the choice of configurations included in the NCSM/RGM expansion -- so that the dominant residual uncertainty can be attributed to the $N\bar{N}$ interaction. We compute phase shifts, scattering lengths, cross sections, antiprotonic-atom level shifts and widths, nuclear quasi-bound energies, and annihilation densities. We find that the hard short-range components of the meson-exchange-based $N\bar{N}$ interaction lead to slow convergence of the NCSM/RGM kernels expanded in a harmonic-oscillator basis, requiring exceptionally large model spaces and posing significant numerical challenges. We discuss practical strategies to mitigate these limitations and assess the impact of missing closed-channel configurations, which is a significant source of uncertainties in very light systems.

## Key claim

The authors extend the ab initio No-Core Shell Model combined with the Resonating Group Method (NCSM/RGM) to antiproton-nucleus dynamics by removing the requirement of antisymmetrization between target and projectile, while retaining a fully microscopic description of the nuclear target. The dominant residual uncertainty in the calculations can be attributed to the N\(\bar{N}\) interaction, after methodological uncertainties from the choice of configurations in the NCSM/RGM expansion are disentangled through benchmarking against exact solutions.

## Method

The authors adapt the NCSM/RGM formalism for antiproton projectiles by omitting the inter-cluster antisymmetrizer, since no Pauli principle applies between target nucleons and the antiproton. They compute the Hamiltonian kernels using a harmonic-oscillator basis expansion for the relative motion, with target wave functions obtained from NCSM calculations in Jacobi coordinates. The Kohno-Weise (KW) N\(\bar{N}\) optical potential is used, which includes a real meson-exchange component (from \(\pi\), \(\rho\), \(\omega\), and \(\sigma\) exchanges via G-parity transformation) and a complex Woods-Saxon annihilation term. The resulting integro-differential equation is solved for the lightest systems (\(\bar{p}+d\), \(\bar{p}+{}^3\)H, and \(\bar{p}+{}^3\)He) to extract phase shifts, scattering lengths, cross sections, antiprotonic-atom level shifts and widths, nuclear quasi-bound energies, and annihilation densities.

## Key numbers

- The annihilation term of the KW potential uses a Woods-Saxon form with parameters \(W_0 = 1.2i\) GeV, \(R = 0.55\) fm, and \(a = 0.2\) fm, obtained by fitting to \(p\bar{p}\) total, elastic, and charge exchange cross sections.
- The HO basis truncation for the relative motion is defined by \(N_{\text{max}} = \max(2n+\ell)\), with the target NCSM truncation parameter \(N^{\rm cluster}_{\text{max}}\) coinciding with \(N_{\text{max}}\) for negative total-parity \(\bar{p}\)-nucleus states (s- and d-waves), while for positive total-parity states \(N_{\text{max}} = N^{\rm cluster}_{\text{max}} + 1\).
- The Trueman formula for antiprotonic-atom level shifts and widths is given up to second order as \(z_{n,\lambda} = -\frac{4}{n} x_{\lambda} \alpha_{n,\ell} (1 - x_{\lambda} \beta_{n,\ell})\), where \(x_{\lambda} = a^{\lambda}_{sc} / B^{2\ell+1}\) and \(B\) is the Bohr radius.

## Context

This work extends the NCSM/RGM method, previously applied to light-nucleus structure and reactions by Quaglioni (2008) and others, to antiproton-nucleus systems for the first time. It builds on renewed experimental interest from the CERN Antiproton Decelerator and the PUMA experiment, and enables benchmarking against exact few-body solutions for the lightest systems (\(\bar{p}+d\), \(\bar{p}+{}^3\)H, \(\bar{p}+{}^3\)He) to validate the many-body approach. The ultimate goal is to lay the foundation for extending calculations to systems with \(A \sim 16\), which are beyond the reach of few-body methods and where the many-body method is expected to work much better.
