# Halo structure of $^8$B determined from intermediate energy proton elastic scattering in inverse kinematics

> The absolute differential cross section for small-angle proton elastic scattering on the proton-rich $^8$B nucleus has been measured in inverse kinematics for the first time. The experiment was performed using a secondary radioactive beam with an energy of 0.7 GeV/u at GSI, Darmstadt. The active target, namely hydrogen-filled time projection ionization chamber IKAR, was used to measure the energy, angle and vertex point of the recoil protons. The scattering angle of the projectiles was simultaneously determined by the tracking detectors. The measured differential cross section is analyzed on the basis of the Glauber multiple scattering theory using phenomenological nuclear-density distributions with two free parameters. The radial density distribution deduced for $^8$B exhibits a halo structure with the root-mean-square (rms) matter radius $R_{\rm m} = 2.58 (6)$ fm and the rms halo radius $R_{\rm h} = 4.24 (25)$ fm. The results on $^8$B are compared to those on the mirror nucleus $^8$Li investigated earlier by the same method. A comparison is also made with previous experimental results and theoretical predictions for both nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the proton-rich nucleus \(^8\)B exhibits a halo structure, based on the first measurement of absolute differential cross sections for small-angle proton elastic scattering in inverse kinematics at 0.7 GeV/u. The claim is hedged by the fact that the halo structure is inferred from the positive curvature in the \(t\)-dependence of \(\ln(\mathrm{d}\sigma/\mathrm{d}t)\) and from model-dependent fits using phenomenological nuclear-density distributions with two free parameters, rather than from a direct model-independent extraction.

## Method

The absolute differential cross section for proton elastic scattering on \(^8\)B was measured in inverse kinematics using a secondary radioactive beam at 0.7 GeV/u at GSI, Darmstadt. The active hydrogen target IKAR, a time projection ionization chamber, recorded the energy, angle, and vertex of recoil protons, while tracking detectors determined the projectile scattering angle. The measured cross section in the momentum-transfer range \(0.001 < |t| < 0.06\) (GeV/\(c\))\(^2\) was analyzed using the Glauber multiple scattering theory with four phenomenological nuclear-density parameterizations (SF, GH, GG, GO), each having two free parameters, assuming \(^8\)B consists of a \(^7\)Be core and a valence proton.

## Key numbers

- Root-mean-square (rms) matter radius of \(^8\)B: \(R_{\rm m} = 2.58 \pm 0.06\) fm (averaged over all density parameterizations, including statistical and systematic uncertainties).
- rms halo radius of \(^8\)B: \(R_{\rm h} = 4.24 \pm 0.25\) fm (deduced from GG and GO parameterizations).
- rms core radius of \(^8\)B: \(R_{\rm c} = 2.25 \pm 0.03\) fm.
- Ratio of valence to core nucleon radii for \(^8\)B: \(\kappa = R_{\rm v} / R_{\rm c} = 1.88 \pm 0.14\).
- rms proton radius of \(^8\)B: \(R_{\rm p} = 2.76 \pm 0.09\) fm.
- Charge radius of \(^8\)B: \(R_{\rm ch} = 2.89 \pm 0.09\) fm.
- Proton skin thickness of \(^8\)B: \(\delta_{\rm pn} = R_{\rm p} - R_{\rm n} = 0.51 \pm 0.09\) fm.
- For comparison, the rms matter radius of the mirror nucleus \(^8\)Li from earlier work (Dobrovsky 2006) is \(R_{\rm m} = 2.50 \pm 0.06\) fm, with \(\kappa = 1.04 \pm 0.22\) (nonhalo).

## Context

This work extends the method of intermediate-energy proton elastic scattering in inverse kinematics, previously applied to neutron-rich halo nuclei such as \(^6\)He, \(^8\)He, \(^8\)Li, \(^9\)Li, \(^{11}\)Li, \(^{12}\)Be, and \(^{14}\)Be (Neumaier 2002, Alk 2002, Egelhof 2002, Dobrovsky 2006, Ilieva 2012), to the proton-rich nucleus \(^8\)B for the first time. The results are compared to the mirror nucleus \(^8\)Li studied earlier by the same method (Dobrovsky 2006) and to previous experimental determinations of the \(^8\)B matter radius from reaction cross sections (Tanihata 1988, Khali 1996, Obuti 1996, Negoita 1996, Fukuda 1999, Fan 2015), which were widely scattered (2.38 to 2.61 fm). The deduced matter radius agrees with the reanalysis by Fan (2015) and with theoretical predictions from cluster models (Grigorenko 1998) and other calculations (Csoto 1993, Varga 1995, Kitagawa 1999, Chandel 2003). The work enables a direct experimental determination of the charge radius of \(^8\)B for the first time and supports the concept of a proton halo in \(^8\)B, with implications for the \(^7\)Be(\(p,\gamma\))\(^8\)B reaction rate relevant to solar neutrino production.
