# Anomalous enhancement of dilepton production as a precursor of color superconductivity

> We compute the modification of the photon self-energy due to dynamical diquark fluctuations developed near the critical temperature of the color superconductivity through the Aslamasov-Larkin, Maki-Thompson and density of states terms, which are responsible for the paraconductivity in metals at vanishing energy and momentum. It is shown that the rate has a significant enhancement at low invariant-mass region over a rather wide range of temperature in the normal phase. This enhancement is worth exploration in the relativistic heavy-ion collisions, which may thereby reveal the significance of the diquark fluctuations in dense quark matter.

## Key claim

The paper claims that dynamical diquark fluctuations near the critical temperature of color superconductivity cause a significant enhancement of dilepton production rates at low invariant mass, which could serve as an observable precursor of color superconductivity in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, even though the temperatures achieved in such collisions may be much higher than the critical temperature itself.

## Method

The authors compute the modification of the photon self-energy due to diquark fluctuations using the Aslamasov-Larkin, Maki-Thompson, and density of states terms in the imaginary-time formalism, then perform analytic continuation to obtain the retarded self-energy. The diquark propagator is calculated in the random-phase approximation using a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, and the T-matrix is approximated using the time-dependent Ginzburg-Landau approximation for small energies and momenta near the critical temperature.

## Key numbers

* The enhancement of the dilepton production rate is significant at low invariant-mass region M less than about 200 MeV, observed up to temperatures T approximately 1.5 times the critical temperature T_c.
* The scalar coupling constant G_S = 5.01 GeV^{-2} and three-momentum cutoff Lambda = 650 MeV are used, determined to reproduce the pion decay constant f_pi = 93 MeV and chiral condensate of (-250 MeV)^3 in vacuum.
* The enhancement is shown for chemical potentials mu = 350 MeV, 400 MeV, and 500 MeV, with the diquark coupling G_C = 0.7 G_S as the default value.
* The enhancement in the low energy region is observed for omega less than about 300 MeV at zero momentum, with the effect more pronounced as temperature approaches T_c.

## Context

This work extends the study of precursory phenomena of color superconductivity previously investigated by Kitazawa (2001, 2003, 2005) and Kunihiro (2007), who showed that diquark fluctuations develop a soft collective mode affecting various observables including the pseudogap region. The calculation parallels the theory of paraconductivity in metallic superconductors developed by Aslamasov and Larkin (1968), Maki (1968), and Thompson (1968), where analogous fluctuation terms explain anomalous enhancement of electric conductivity above T_c. The results suggest that dilepton measurements in heavy-ion collision experiments at RHIC, HADES, NA61/SHINE, and future facilities at GSI-FAIR, NICA-MPD, and J-PARC-HI could reveal strong diquark correlations as a precursor to color superconductivity in dense quark matter.
