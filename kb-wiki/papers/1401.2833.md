# Laser-Nucleus Reactions: Population of States far above Yrast and far from Stability

> Nuclear reactions induced by a strong zeptosecond laser pulse are studied theoretically in the quasiadiabatic regime where the photon absorption rate is comparable to the nuclear equilibration rate. We find that multiple photon absorption leads to the formation of a compound nucleus in the so-far unexplored regime of excitation energies several hundred MeV above the yrast line. At these energies, further photon absorption is limited by neutron decay and/or induced nucleon emission. With a laser pulse of $\approx 50$ zs duration, proton-rich nuclei far off the line of stability are produced.

## Key claim
Multiple coherent photon absorption in the quasiadiabatic regime (where the effective dipole width \(N \Gamma_{\rm dip}\) is comparable to the nuclear spreading width \(\Gamma_{\rm sp}\)) can produce compound nuclei at excitation energies several hundred MeV above the yrast line, but this excitation is always terminated by neutron evaporation or induced nucleon emission before reaching the maximum of the nuclear level density.

## Method
The authors combined a newly developed method for calculating nuclear level densities at high excitation energies and large particle numbers (Palffy and Weidenmüller, 2013a,b) with nuclear reaction theory. They used a simplified master-equation approach treating consecutive dipole photon absorption by an equilibrated compound nucleus, comparing the effective absorption rate \((N \Gamma)_{\rm eff}(E)\) against four competing widths: induced dipole emission, neutron evaporation via the Weisskopf formula, induced nucleon emission into the continuum, and induced fission via the Bohr-Wheeler formula. Level densities were computed using a Fermi-gas model with two forms for the single-particle level density \(\rho_1(\ve)\): linear for \(A=100\) and quadratic for \(A=200\).

## Key numbers
- Laser pulse parameters: \(N = 10^3 - 10^4\) coherent photons, mean photon energy \(E_L \approx 1 - 5\) MeV, energy spread \(\sigma \approx 50\) keV, pulse duration \(\approx 50\) zeptoseconds.
- Effective dipole width: \(N \Gamma_{\rm dip} \approx 1 - 5\) MeV; up to \(N_0 \approx 5 \times 10^2\) photons may be absorbed.
- Spreading width \(\Gamma_{\rm sp} \approx 5\) MeV for excitation energies up to several 10 MeV.
- Maximum level density occurs at \(E_{\rm max} = 533\) MeV for \(A=100\) and \(E_{\rm max} = 1200\) MeV for \(A=200\).
- For \(A=200\), the intersection energy where \((N \Gamma)_{\rm eff}\) equals \((N \Gamma)_{\rm cnt}\) is fixed at \(E_{\rm x} = 901\) MeV; at \(N \Gamma_{\rm dip} = 2.73\) MeV, neutron evaporation and induced nucleon emission exchange roles in limiting excitation.
- For \(A=100\) (\(A=200\)), less than 10% of evaporated neutrons have energies exceeding 20 MeV (25 MeV, respectively).
- Average nuclear spin after \(N_0\) absorptions: \(J = \hbar \sqrt{N_0}\), of order \(10\hbar\) for several hundred absorbed photons.

## Context
This work extends the study of laser-nucleus interactions beyond the perturbative regime (Weidenmüller, 2011) and the sudden regime, into the quasiadiabatic regime where nuclear equilibration competes with photon absorption. It contrasts with heavy-ion reactions, which populate states near the yrast line, by showing that dipole photon absorption produces states far above yrast with low angular momentum. The results enable predictions for future experiments at ELI and IZEST facilities, suggesting that laser-induced reactions can produce proton-rich nuclei far from stability and provide insight into the structure of such nuclei and the time scales and level densities involved.
