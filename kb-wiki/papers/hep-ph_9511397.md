# Elastic $ππ$ scattering to two loops

> We evaluate analytically the elastic $ππ$ scattering amplitude to two loops in chiral perturbation theory and give numerical values for the two $S$--wave scattering lengths and for the phase shift difference $δ_0^0-δ_1^1$.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first full analytic two-loop calculation of the elastic ππ scattering amplitude in chiral perturbation theory (CHPT), showing that the two-loop corrections to the S-wave scattering lengths are dominated by chiral logarithms and that the new O(p⁶) low-energy couplings contribute negligibly when estimated via resonance saturation.

## Method

The authors evaluate the elastic ππ scattering amplitude A(s,t,u) to O(p⁶) by expanding in powers of external momenta and light quark masses, using an effective Lagrangian with terms L₂, L₄, and L₆. They compute two-loop graphs from L₂, one-loop graphs with one vertex from L₄, and tree graphs from L₂+L₄+L₆, expressing the result in terms of the physical pion mass M_π, the physical pion decay constant F_π, and the low-energy couplings l_i^r (from L₄) and r_i^r (from L₆). The new couplings r_i^r are estimated by saturating them with contributions from vector and scalar exchange and from kaon and eta loops.

## Key numbers

- a₀⁰ = 0.217 (two-loop result), compared to 0.201 (one-loop result) and 0.156 (tree-level result)
- a₀⁰ − a₀² = 0.258 (two-loop result), compared to 0.242 (one-loop result) and 0.201 (tree-level result)
- With r_i^r(1 GeV) = 0, the values are a₀⁰ = 0.215 and a₀⁰ − a₀² = 0.256
- Using F_π = 92.4 MeV instead of 93.2 MeV increases a₀⁰ by 0.005 and a₀⁰ − a₀² by 0.006
- The two-loop correction to the phase shift difference δ₀⁰ − δ₁¹ at 380 MeV increases by 0.4 degrees when using estimated r_i^r couplings

## Context

This work extends the one-loop CHPT calculation of Gasser and Leutwyler (1984) for ππ scattering to two-loop order, and provides the full analytic amplitude that was only partially known from the unitarity-based approach of Knecht et al. (1995). The result enables precise theoretical predictions to confront forthcoming experimental data from Kₑ₄ decays at DAΦNE and Brookhaven, and from π⁺π⁻ atom lifetime measurements, which will allow determination of the quark-antiquark condensate in QCD.
