# Fluctuations in Statistical Models

> The multiplicity fluctuations of hadrons are studied within the statistical hadron-resonance gas model in the large volume limit. The role of quantum statistics and resonance decay effects are discussed. The microscopic correlator method is used to enforce conservation of three charges -- baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness -- in the canonical ensemble. In addition, in the micro-canonical ensemble energy conservation is included. An analytical method is used to account for resonance decays. The multiplicity distributions and the scaled variances for negatively and positively charged hadrons are calculated for the sets of thermodynamical parameters along the chemical freeze-out line of central Pb+Pb (Au+Au) collisions from SIS to LHC energies. Predictions obtained within different statistical ensembles are compared with the preliminary NA49 experimental results on central Pb+Pb collisions in the SPS energy range. The measured fluctuations are significantly narrower than the Poisson ones and clearly favor expectations for the micro-canonical ensemble. Thus, this is a first observation of the recently predicted suppression of the multiplicity fluctuations in relativistic gases in the thermodynamical limit due to conservation laws.

## Key claim

Multiplicity fluctuations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, as measured by the NA49 collaboration at SPS energies, are significantly narrower than Poisson expectations and are best described by the micro-canonical ensemble (MCE) of the hadron-resonance gas model, representing the first experimental observation of the predicted suppression of fluctuations due to conservation laws in the thermodynamic limit.

## Method

The author calculates multiplicity distributions and scaled variances for charged hadrons within the hadron-resonance gas model using the grand canonical ensemble (GCE), canonical ensemble (CE), and micro-canonical ensemble (MCE) in the large volume limit. The microscopic correlator method enforces conservation of baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness in the CE, with additional energy conservation in the MCE, while an analytical method accounts for resonance decay contributions. Model predictions are compared with preliminary NA49 data on central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies, with the limited experimental acceptance accounted for via an uncorrelated particle approximation.

## Key numbers

- Scaled variances in the GCE at SPS energies: \(\omega^-_{g.c.e.} \cong 1.1\) and \(\omega^+_{g.c.e.} \cong 1.2\)
- NA49 acceptance probabilities for charged hadrons at SPS energies: \(q^{\pm}=0.038, 0.063, 0.085, 0.131, 0.163\) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=6.27, 7.62, 8.77, 12.3, 17.3\) GeV, respectively
- The scaled variances \(\omega_{m.c.e.}^-\) and \(\omega_{m.c.e.}^+\) calculated in full phase space within the MCE vary by less than 1% when changing the chemical freeze-out parameter set
- Data from 1% of the most central Pb+Pb collisions at all SPS energies are best described by MCE results

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadron-resonance gas model approach from mean multiplicities (Fermi, 1950; Landau, 1953; Hagedorn, 1965) to multiplicity fluctuations, following the recent finding by Begun et al. (2004) and the author's group that scaled variances differ between ensembles even in the thermodynamic limit. The results contradict GCE predictions and favor the MCE, enabling future tests of phase transition signals (such as the critical point) and motivating more differential fluctuation measurements at higher acceptance.
