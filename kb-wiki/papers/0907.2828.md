# On the multistep processes in the nuclei

> The intermediate-state interaction and structure of amplitudes of complicated processes in medium (decays, reactions and the $n\bar{n}$ transitions) are studied. It is proposed to use the branching ratio of channels of free-space hadron-nucleon interaction as a test in the construction and verification of the models. The corresponding formulas for the processes in the medium are obtained. The connection between particle self-energy and amplitudes of subprocesses are analyzed as well.

## Key claim

The paper argues that models of multistep processes in nuclei (decays, reactions, and $n\bar{n}$ transitions) must reproduce the free-space branching ratio of hadron-nucleon interaction channels as a necessary condition for correctness; the intermediate-state particle propagator should be bare when the annihilation amplitude is defined through observable values, and including an additional self-energy via a non-Hermitian optical potential violates unitarity and can suppress the process in the wrong direction.

## Method

The author studies the structure of amplitudes for decay processes in nuclear matter (e.g., $a\rightarrow \pi^0\bar{n}$) using the low-density approximation for the $\bar{n}$-medium interaction, calculating the widths of annihilation and scattering channels from the unitarity condition. The free-space $\bar{n}N$ annihilation amplitude is defined via the $T$-matrix, and the in-medium amplitude is constructed with a bare antineutron propagator, avoiding the dressed propagator approach that leads to non-unitary $S$-matrices. The branching ratios of channels in the medium are then expressed in terms of free-space cross sections $\sigma_a$ and $\sigma_t$.

## Key numbers

- For free-space $\bar{n}N$ interaction at low energies: $\sigma_a / \sigma_t > 0.7$ (where $\sigma_t = \sigma_a + \sigma_s$), meaning annihilation dominates over scattering.
- The differential branching ratio in the medium satisfies $d\Gamma_a/dq \big/ d\Gamma_t/dq = \sigma_a / \sigma_t > 0.7$, and $d\Gamma_s/dq \big/ d\Gamma_t/dq = \sigma_s / \sigma_t < 0.3$.
- For reactions of type $a_1+N\rightarrow b+c$ in the medium, the ratio of differential cross sections obeys $d\sigma_r^m/dq \big/ d\sigma_s^m/dq = \sigma_r / \sigma_s$.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of unitarity constraints on multistep processes in nuclei, previously discussed in the context of $n\bar{n}$ transitions (Nazaruk, references [1,2]) where the optical model with a non-Hermitian potential was shown to give the wrong sign for absorption effects. The paper contrasts its approach with standard calculations of $n\bar{n}$ transitions (references [8,9]) that rely on the flawed optical-model method, and it provides a necessary condition for model construction that can be tested against free-space hadron-nucleon data. The results enable verification and correction of models for decays and reactions in nuclear matter, particularly for processes involving antineutron intermediate states.
