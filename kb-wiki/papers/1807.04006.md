# Better insight into the Strutinsky method (published version)

> Strutinsky's method is reviewed through a new understanding. This method depends on two free parameters: The smoothing parameter and the order of the curvature correction. It turns out that this method is nothing but a compromise between two fundamental conditions which are the so-called asymptotic limit which comes from the so-called remainder which imposes a small as possible smoothing parameter and the smoothing condition which forces that parameter to be, at least, slightly larger than the inter shell spacing. In this paper, to find the best value of the smoothing parameter, a new criterion is proposed instead of the plateau condition . This new criterion is much more clear and free from ambiguities of the usual plateau condition. It is also found, that the second free parameter, i.e., the order of the curvature correction, plays an accessory role since, it is connected intimately to the smoothing parameter, when the smoothing is realized. This paper provides a new and definitive insight into Strutinsky's method and its relationship with semi-classical methods.

## Key claim

The Strutinsky method is only an approximation of the semi-classical (Wigner-Kirkwood) method, and its accuracy depends on a compromise between the smoothing condition (gamma at least slightly larger than the inter-shell spacing) and the asymptotic limit (gamma much smaller than the Fermi energy measured from the bottom of the well), with a new criterion proposed to replace the ambiguous plateau condition.

## Method

The author analytically derived the relationship between the Strutinsky level density and the semi-classical level density using a folding procedure, showing that the Strutinsky density equals the semi-classical density multiplied by a factor of (1 + Remainder). The remainder was expressed as an infinite sum of terms involving derivatives of the semi-classical density and powers of the smoothing parameter gamma, with coefficients determined by Hermite polynomial orthogonality. Numerical calculations using FORTRAN programs were performed to test the new criterion for selecting the optimal smoothing parameter.

## Key numbers

- The inter-shell spacing for realistic nuclei is given by hbar-omega approximately equal to 41 A^{-1/3} MeV, where A is the mass number.
- For the three-dimensional harmonic oscillator, the semi-classical level density is g_sc(lambda) approximately equal to (1/2 hbar-omega)[(lambda/hbar-omega)^2 - 1/4].
- For the cubic box example with M=4, the condition for good accuracy is (gamma/lambda)^4 much less than 512/15, meaning gamma/lambda much less than 2.4.
- The coefficient C_{M+2} in the remainder is given by (-1)^{M/2} / [2^{(M+1)} (M/2)! (M+2)].

## Context

This work extends the analytical connection between Strutinsky's method and semi-classical methods established by Mohammed-Azizi (2006), which showed that the Strutinsky density is an approximation of the Wigner-Kirkwood expansion. The paper addresses the long-standing ambiguity of the plateau condition, which was noted by Brack (1972) and others to be seldom satisfied in practice. The new criterion enables more precise determination of the optimal smoothing parameter and clarifies that the curvature correction order M plays an accessory role when smoothing is properly realized, potentially allowing the Strutinsky method to be replaced by the parameter-free semi-classical method of Wigner-Kirkwood as done by Bhagwat (2010).
