# Transverse hydrodynamics in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> General features of the formalism describing hydrodynamic evolution of transversally thermalized matter possibly produced at the very early stages of ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions are presented. Thermodynamical consistency of the model is emphasized. The covariant formulas for the moments of the phase-space distribution function are derived. The simple model for the transition from purely transverse to standard perfect-fluid hydrodynamics is proposed.

## Key claim
The paper presents a formalism for transverse hydrodynamics where matter created in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions is thermalized only in the transverse degrees of freedom at very early stages, with the longitudinal direction treated as free streaming; the model is thermodynamically consistent and includes a proposed sudden transition to standard three-dimensional perfect-fluid hydrodynamics via Landau matching conditions, but the abstract glosses over the fact that the transition necessarily produces entropy and that the model's applicability depends on the assumption that clusters evolve independently without longitudinal interactions.

## Method
The author derives the energy-momentum tensor for a transversally thermalized system, incorporating two-dimensional thermodynamic variables (energy density ε₂, pressure P₂, entropy density s₂) that satisfy standard thermodynamic identities with vanishing chemical potential. The hydrodynamic equations are obtained from energy-momentum conservation and rewritten in cylindrical coordinates for numerical implementation, using a parameterization of the transverse fluid velocity. The phase-space distribution function is factorized into a longitudinal part (with a Dirac delta enforcing y = η for free streaming) and a transverse part (local equilibrium), and its moments are computed via covariant tensor decomposition.

## Key numbers
- For massless fermions: n₂ = ν_g π T² / 24, ε₂ = 3 ν_g ζ(3) T³ / (4π)
- For massless bosons: n₂ = ν_g π T² / 12, ε₂ = ν_g ζ(3) T³ / π
- Sound velocity in the 2D case: c_s² = 1/2 (constant), from P₂ = (1/2) ε₂
- For Boltzmann statistics with finite mass m: n₂ = (ν_g T / (2π)) (m + T) e^{-m/T}, P₂ = (ν_g T² / (2π)) (m + T) e^{-m/T}, s₂ = (ν_g / (2π)) [m² + 3mT + 3T²] e^{-m/T}, ε₂ = (ν_g T / (2π)) [T² + (m + T)²] e^{-m/T}
- Sound velocity for massive classical particles: c_s² = T (m² + 3mT + 3T²) / (m³ + 3m²T + 6mT² + 6T³)
- Internal degrees of freedom for gluons: ν_g = 16

## Context
This work extends the transverse-hydrodynamics concept originally introduced by Heinz and Wong (2002), who concluded such a model could not describe RHIC data, but reformulated by Bialas et al. (2007) and Chojnacki et al. (2007) to show consistency with experimental results. The paper builds on formal results from Ryblewski (2008) by including finite parton masses and quantum statistics, and proposes a transition to 3D hydrodynamics via Landau matching conditions, similar to approaches used by Broniowski et al. (2008) for parton free-streaming transitions. The formalism enables numerical calculations of physical observables at RHIC, with the transition to 3D hydrodynamics also addressed in dissipative hydrodynamics by Bozek (2007) and in entropy-conserving anisotropic hydrodynamics by Florkowski et al. (2008, 2009).
