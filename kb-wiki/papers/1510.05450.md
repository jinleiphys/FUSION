# Green's Function Monte Carlo Calculations with Two- and Three-Nucleon Interactions from Chiral Effective Field Theory

> I discuss our recent work on Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) calculations of light nuclei using local nucleon-nucleon interactions derived from chiral effective field theory (EFT) up to next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^2$LO). I present the natural extension of this work to include the consistent three-nucleon (3N) forces at the same order in the chiral expansion. I discuss our choice of observables to fit the two low-energy constants which enter in the 3N sector at N$^2$LO and present some results for light nuclei.

## Key claim
Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) calculations of light nuclei can now incorporate consistent three-nucleon (3N) forces from chiral effective field theory at next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO), but the two low-energy constants entering at this order must be fitted to the 4He binding energy and to n-alpha P-wave elastic scattering phase shifts, and not all operator choices for these constants yield a simultaneous fit to both observables.

## Method
Local nucleon-nucleon interactions derived from chiral EFT up to N2LO were combined with consistent 3N forces at the same order in the chiral expansion. The two low-energy constants c_D and c_E were fitted to the 4He binding energy and to n-alpha P-wave elastic scattering phase shifts, using several different operator choices for the terms proportional to these constants. GFMC calculations were then performed for A=3 nuclei (3H and 3He) to compare binding energies and point-proton radii with experimental values.

## Key numbers
- 3H binding energy: -8.33(1) MeV (GFMC, 3N cutoff R_3N=1.0 fm) compared to experimental -8.48 MeV
- 3H binding energy: -8.35(4) MeV (GFMC, 3N cutoff R_3N=1.2 fm) compared to experimental -8.48 MeV
- 3He binding energy: -7.66(1) MeV (GFMC, 3N cutoff R_3N=1.0 fm) compared to experimental -7.72 MeV
- 3He binding energy: -7.63(4) MeV (GFMC, 3N cutoff R_3N=1.2 fm) compared to experimental -7.72 MeV
- 3H point-proton radius: 1.55(3) fm (GFMC, R_3N=1.0 fm) compared to experimental 1.59 fm
- 3H point-proton radius: 1.55(4) fm (GFMC, R_3N=1.2 fm) compared to experimental 1.59 fm
- 3He point-proton radius: 1.77(2) fm (GFMC, R_3N=1.0 fm) compared to experimental 1.76 fm
- 3He point-proton radius: 1.77(1) fm (GFMC, R_3N=1.2 fm) compared to experimental 1.76 fm

## Context
This work extends the author's previous GFMC calculations of A=3,4 nuclei using chiral interactions up to N2LO (Lynn 2014) by now including the consistent 3N forces that enter at the same order. The ability to simultaneously fit the 4He binding energy and n-alpha P-wave phase shifts demonstrates that chiral interactions at N2LO have sufficient freedom to describe both bound-state and scattering observables in light nuclei. This enables future GFMC calculations of larger light nuclei with consistent two- and three-nucleon forces from chiral EFT.
