# The structure of the nucleon

> These lectures give an introduction to the structure of the nucleon as seen with the electromagnetic probe. Particular emphasis is put on the form factors, the strangeness content, Compton scattering and polarizabilities, pion photo- and electroproduction, the spin structure and sum rules. The existing data are compared to predictions obtained from chiral perturbation theory, dispersion theory and effective Lagrangians.

# The structure of the nucleon

## Key claim
The paper presents a pedagogical review of nucleon structure as probed by electromagnetic interactions, with the caveat that no ab-initio calculation can yet fully describe the confinement region; lattice gauge theory remains restricted to the quenched approximation with massive quarks extrapolated downward, and chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) requires a growing number of low-energy constants that must be fitted to data, limiting predictive power at higher orders.

## Method
The author reviews experimental and theoretical results on nucleon form factors, Compton scattering, polarizabilities, pion photo- and electroproduction, and spin structure, comparing data to predictions from chiral perturbation theory (ChPT), dispersion theory, and effective Lagrangians. The formalism of electron-nucleon scattering kinematics is developed using Mandelstam variables, and the Sachs form factors G_E and G_M are introduced via the Breit frame. The Rosenbluth separation technique and double-polarization experiments for extracting form factor ratios are described.

## Key numbers
- Proton magnetic form factor G_M^p/(μ_p G_D) falls to about 0.65 at Q^2 = 20 (GeV/c)^2 (SLAC data)
- At Q^2 = 3.5 (GeV/c)^2, G_E^p/G_M^p reaches about 0.55 (Jefferson Lab polarized experiment)
- Neutron electric radius ⟨r^2⟩_E^n ≈ -0.11 fm^2 (from low-energy neutron scattering on ^208Pb)
- Dipole fit mass parameter M_V = 840 MeV, M_{V'} = 790 MeV for the Sachs form factor parameterization
- Pion decay constant f_π ≈ 93 MeV
- Quark condensate ⟨q̄q⟩ ≈ -(225 MeV)^3
- Quark masses: m_u ≈ 5 MeV, m_d ≈ 9 MeV, m_s ≈ 175 MeV
- Charged pion lifetime 2.6×10^{-8} sec, neutral pion lifetime 8.4×10^{-17} sec
- QCD scale Λ_QCD ≈ 200 MeV/c

## Context
This review extends the pedagogical tradition of nucleon structure lectures, summarizing work by Gasser and Leutwyler (1984) on chiral perturbation theory, Bernard, Kaiser, and Meißner (1995) on photoinduced reactions, and the Sachs form factor formalism (Sach, 1962). It discusses recent Jefferson Lab results on G_E^p/G_M^p and Mainz double-polarization measurements of G_E^n that contradict earlier Saclay fits from elastic ed scattering (Platchkov, 1990). The work enables comparison of new polarization data with dispersion-theoretic calculations (Hammer, 1996) and highlights open questions about final-state interactions in deuteron and ^3He experiments.
