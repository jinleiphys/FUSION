# Spectral function of fermions in a highly occupied non-Abelian plasma

> We develop a method to obtain fermion spectral functions non-perturbatively in a non-Abelian gauge theory with high occupation numbers of gauge fields. After recovering the free field case, we extract the spectral function of fermions in a highly occupied non-Abelian plasma close to its non-thermal fixed point, i.e., in a self-similar regime of the non-equilibrium dynamics. We find good agreement with hard loop perturbation theory for medium-induced masses, dispersion relations and quasiparticle residues. We also extract the full momentum dependence of the damping rate of the collective excitations.

## Key claim

The authors develop a non-perturbative method to compute fermion spectral functions in a highly occupied non-Abelian plasma and find that, while dispersion relations and quasiparticle residues agree well with leading-order hard thermal loop (HTL) perturbation theory, the damping rate at zero momentum decreases much slower than HTL predicts and remains of the same order as the fermion screening mass, a feature not captured by the perturbative framework.

## Method

The authors perform classical-statistical lattice simulations of SU(2) gauge fields in 3+1 dimensions, treating the gauge fields as classical while solving linearized Dirac equations for fermion mode functions in the background of these dynamical bosonic fields. They initialize fermion wave-functions as plane waves at a reference time, evolve them forward using a tree-level improved Wilson Dirac operator alongside the Yang-Mills equations, and extract the spectral function by projecting onto the appropriate momentum modes. Only individual momentum modes need to be simulated independently, making the calculation computationally much less demanding than full fermion simulations.

## Key numbers

- The fermion screening mass \(m_f\) is related to the gluon asymptotic mass via \(m_f^2 = (C_F / 2 N_c) m_g^2\), with \(C_F = (N_c^2 - 1)/(2 N_c)\) and \(N_c = 2\) in this work.
- For zero momentum, the HTL retarded propagator has poles at \(\omega_\pm(p=0) = m_f\) with residues \(Z_\pm(p=0) = 1/2\).
- For small momenta \(p \ll m_f\), the HTL dispersion relations are \(\omega_\pm(p) \simeq m_f \pm (1/3) p\).
- For large momenta \(p \gg m_f\), the HTL dispersion relation is \(\omega_+(p) \simeq p + m_l^2/(2p)\) with \(m_l = \sqrt{2} m_f\).
- The damping rate at zero momentum \(\gamma^{+}(t,p=0)\) remains of the same order as the mass, \(\gamma^{+}(t,p=0) \sim m_f(t)\), which is unexpectedly larger than HTL predictions.

## Context

This work extends previous non-perturbative calculations of gluon spectral functions (Boguslavski et al., 2021) to the fermion sector, building on real-time lattice fermion methods developed by Mace et al. (2016, 2019). The results enable benchmarking and potential improvement of perturbative calculations in the presence of strong gauge fields, and open the door to studying quark spectral functions in expanding QCD plasmas or the behavior of highly-energetic and heavy-flavor quarks.
