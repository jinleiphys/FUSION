# Relativistic magnetohydrodynamics with spin

> In this work, we present a novel framework of relativistic non-resistive dissipative magnetohydrodynamics for spin-polarized particles. Utilizing a classical relativistic kinetic equation for the distribution function in an extended phase-space of position, momentum, and spin, we derive equations of motion for dissipative currents at first-order in spacetime gradients. Our findings reveal a coupling between fluid vorticity and magnetization via an electromagnetic field, leading to relativistic analogs of the Einstein-de Haas and Barnett effects. Our study provides a tool for a better understanding of the polarization phenomena observed in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper presents a first-order dissipative relativistic magnetohydrodynamic framework for spin-polarized particles, derived from a classical kinetic equation in an extended phase space of position, momentum, and spin. The key finding is that the coupling between fluid vorticity and magnetization occurs specifically via the electromagnetic field, producing relativistic analogs of the Einstein-de Haas and Barnett effects, but this coupling vanishes when the electromagnetic field is absent.

## Method

The authors start from a classical relativistic Boltzmann-Vlasov-type kinetic equation for the distribution function \(f(x,p,s)\) in an extended phase space, including force and torque terms from the electromagnetic field (Lorentz and Mathisson forces, but neglecting the torque term). Using the relaxation-time approximation (RTA) for the collision kernel and assuming the non-resistive (ideal conductor) limit where the electric field vanishes in the fluid rest frame, they derive constitutive relations for dissipative currents (particle diffusion, bulk viscosity, shear viscosity, and spin current) at first order in spacetime gradients. The equilibrium distribution is taken as a Fermi-Dirac form with a spin-dependent term linear in the spin polarization tensor \(\omega^{\mu\nu}\), and the matching condition \(u_\lambda \delta S^{\lambda,\mu\nu}=0\) is imposed to ensure the spin moment of the collision kernel vanishes.

## Key numbers

* The spin length is fixed by the Casimir operator eigenvalue: \(\spin^2 = \frac{3}{4}\) (corresponding to spin-1/2 particles).
* The metric convention is \(g_{\mu\nu} = \mathrm{diag}(+1,-1,-1,-1)\) and the Levi-Civita symbol normalization is \(\epsilon^{0123} = -\epsilon_{0123}=1\).
* The equilibrium magnetization tensor is given by \(M^{\mu\nu}_{\rm eq}=a_1\,\omega^{\mu\nu} + a_2\, u^{[\mu} u_\gamma \omega^{\nu]\gamma}\), where the coefficients \(a_1, a_2\) are not numerically specified in the text.
* The dissipative transport coefficients are expressed in terms of the relaxation time \(\tau_{\rm eq}\) and thermodynamic integrals: bulk viscosity \(\zeta=-\tau_{\mathrm{eq}} \beta_{\Pi\Pi}\), shear viscosity \(\eta^{\mu\nu\alpha\beta}=\tau_{\mathrm{eq}}\beta_{\pi\pi}^{\langle\mu\nu\rangle\alpha\beta}\), particle diffusion \(\kappa^{\mu\alpha}=\tau_{\mathrm{eq}}\beta_{nn}^{\langle\mu\rangle\alpha}\), and spin diffusion \(\Sigma^{\lambda\mu\nu\alpha\beta\gamma}=\tau_{\mathrm{eq}}B_{\Sigma}^{\lambda,[\mu\nu]\alpha\beta\gamma}\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous formulation of dissipative spin hydrodynamics (Bhadury 2020, Bhadury 2022) by incorporating electromagnetic fields, building on the relativistic spin hydrodynamics framework initiated by Florkowski (2017) and the kinetic theory approach of Weickgenannt (2019). It provides a theoretical tool to interpret spin polarization measurements in relativistic heavy-ion collisions observed by STAR (2017, 2019, 2021) and ALICE (2019), where large angular momentum and strong magnetic fields are expected in non-central collisions. The framework enables future studies of how vorticity and magnetic fields jointly generate spin polarization, and how the relativistic analogs of the Barnett and Einstein-de Haas effects manifest in the quark-gluon plasma.
