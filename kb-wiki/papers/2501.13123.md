# Strongly Interacting Matter Under Extreme Conditions

> Different variants of the Hadron Resonance Gas model have been used to describe the hadronic phase of strongly interacting matter. HRG model is improved by including repulsive interaction through the inclusion of excluded volume in Excluded Volume Hadron Resonance Gas (EVHRG) model. Here, the EVHRG model is further improved by incorporating unequal radii of hadrons which has been named as Modified Excluded Volume Hadron Resonance Gas (MEVHRG) model and also by taking their Lorentz contraction in Lorentz contracted MEVHRG model, namely, LMEVHRG model. Another way of incorporating the repulsive interactions in the HRG model is through the introduction of a mean-field potential. This is done in the HRG mean-field (HRGMF) model where the single particle energies are modified by a density dependent term. Both the inclusion of unequal radii and the Lorentz contraction have significant effects on the thermodynamic quantities and susceptibilities of conserved charges. The centre of mass energy dependence of some thermodynamic quantities in presence of repulsive interaction has been studied. Our study clearly indicates that proper modelling of repulsive interaction among hadrons is very important to explain the thermodynamic quantities and susceptibilities of conserved charges. The effect of static magnetic field in HRG and EVHRG models by the means of Landau levels has also been investigated. The vacuum part of the pressure, in presence of magnetic field, has been properly renormalised. The magnetic field is found to effect the thermodynamic quantities and the susceptibilities significantly. The electric charge susceptibility is influenced more strongly by the magnetic field than the baryon susceptibility. The total magnetization of hadronic matter is found to be positive.

## Key claim

The thesis systematically demonstrates that properly modeling repulsive interactions among hadrons, through excluded volume effects (with unequal radii and Lorentz contraction) or mean-field potentials, is essential for reproducing lattice QCD results for thermodynamic quantities and susceptibilities of conserved charges, whereas the ideal Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model, while adequate for pressure, fails for susceptibilities.

## Method

The author develops and compares several variants of the Hadron Resonance Gas model: the standard HRG model with point-like particles; the Excluded Volume HRG (EVHRG) model with hard-core repulsion; the Modified EVHRG (MEVHRG) model incorporating unequal hadron radii; the Lorentz-contracted MEVHRG (LMEVHRG) model; and the HRG mean-field (HRGMF) model where single-particle energies are shifted by a density-dependent term. Thermodynamic quantities (pressure, energy density, specific heat, speed of sound) and susceptibilities of conserved charges (baryon number, strangeness, electric charge) are computed as functions of temperature and baryon chemical potential, and compared with lattice QCD data. The effect of a static magnetic field is also investigated by incorporating Landau levels for charged hadrons, with proper renormalization of the vacuum pressure.

## Key numbers

- Second order strangeness susceptibility in pure HRG matches lattice data up to 0.165 GeV of temperature; at higher temperatures, LMEVHRG results show good agreement with lattice QCD.
- A representative magnetic field value of eB = 0.2 GeV² was chosen to study the effect of magnetic fields on thermodynamic quantities.
- The total magnetization of hadronic matter is found to be positive.
- Electric charge susceptibility is influenced more strongly by the magnetic field than baryon susceptibility.

## Context

This work extends the standard HRG model framework (Braun-Munzinger, Redlich, Stachel, 2004; Cleymans and Redlich, 1999) by incorporating repulsive interactions through multiple approaches. It builds on prior excluded volume studies (Alba et al., 2017; Vovchenko et al., 2016) and mean-field approaches (Huovinen and Petreczky, 2018; Kadam and Mishra, 2019), while introducing novel treatments of unequal hadron radii and Lorentz contraction. The results enable improved modeling of the hadronic phase for comparison with lattice QCD data and heavy-ion collision experiments, particularly for understanding fluctuations of conserved charges and the behavior of strongly interacting matter under magnetic fields relevant to magnetars and non-central heavy-ion collisions.
