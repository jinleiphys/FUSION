# On three topical aspects of the N=28 isotonic chain

> The evolution of single-particle orbits along the N=28 isotonic chain is studied within the framework of a relativistic mean-field approximation. We focus on three topical aspects of the N=28 chain: (a) the emergence of a new magic number at Z=14; (b) the possible erosion of the N=28 shell; and (c) the weakening of the spin-orbit splitting among low-j neutron orbits. The present model supports the emergence of a robust Z=14 subshell gap in 48Ca, that persists as one reaches the neutron-rich isotone 42Si. Yet the proton removal from 48Ca results in a significant erosion of the N=28 shell in 42Si. Finally, the removal of s1/2 protons from 48Ca causes a ~50% reduction of the spin-orbit splitting among neutron p-orbitals in 42Si.

## Key claim

The relativistic mean-field model supports the emergence of a robust Z=14 subshell gap in 48Ca that persists in 42Si, but the removal of protons from 48Ca causes a significant erosion of the N=28 shell in 42Si and a roughly 50% reduction in the spin-orbit splitting among neutron p-orbitals. The abstract's claim of a "robust" Z=14 gap is qualified by the text noting that the gap weakens slightly (by about 1 MeV) when moving from 48Ca to 42Si.

## Method

The author used a relativistic mean-field approximation with a slightly modified NL3 parameter set, adjusting the scalar meson mass to reproduce the 1d3/2-2s1/2 proton gap in 40Ca while keeping the binding energy and charge radius of 40Ca close to experimental values. Ground-state properties and single-particle spectra were computed at the mean-field level for the N=28 isotones 48Ca, 46Ar, 44S, and 42Si, with the evolution of proton and neutron single-particle orbits studied as a function of proton removal.

## Key numbers

- The 1d3/2-2s1/2 proton gap in 48Ca is reduced to 110 keV, compared to about 2 MeV in 40Ca
- The Z=14 proton gap in 48Ca is about 6 MeV, weakening to about 5 MeV in 42Si
- The N=28 neutron gap is reduced from 3.4 MeV in 48Ca to 1.4 MeV in 42Si
- The spin-orbit splitting of neutron 2p orbitals in 42Si is quenched by about 50% relative to 48Ca
- The 1d3/2-2s1/2 proton gap in 40Ca is 2.8±0.6 MeV experimentally, while the original NL3 parameter set predicts 0.83 MeV
- A preliminary RPA calculation reveals a low-lying 2+ excitation at about 0.5 MeV in 42Si

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean-field study of the N=28 isotonic chain, building on earlier predictions by Todd-Rutel (2004) of spin-orbit weakening in 46Ar and 206Hg, which was later confirmed experimentally by Gaudefroy and Sorlin (2006). The results contradict the conclusions of Retamosa (1996) and Caurier (2004) who argued for a spherical doubly-magic 42Si, instead supporting the findings of Werner (1994, 1996), Terasaki (1996), Lalazissis (1998), and Peru (2000) who predicted strong deformation in 42Si. The work also engages with the tensor-force approach of Otsuka (2001, 2005) and the pseudospin symmetry framework of Ginnochio, showing that relativistic mean-field models can capture similar physics through renormalization effects.
