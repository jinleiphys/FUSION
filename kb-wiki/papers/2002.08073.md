# Continuity from neutron matter to color-superconducting quark matter with $^3 P_2$ superfluidity

> I clarify how the concept of quark-hadron continuity, which was previously considered in the context of the asymptotic color-flavor locked phase with ideal three-flavor symmetry, is applied to two-flavor matter. Our observation is that neutron star matter can continuously be connected to two-flavor color-superconducting (2SC) phase with an additional condensate of $d$-quarks in the $^3P_2$ channel. I discuss here two aspects of this novel phase. First, I introduce the notion of continuity based on the patterns of symmetry breaking and the corresponding order parameters, and then explain qualitatively the physics mechanism of $d$-quark $^3P_2$ pairing in analogy to nuclear physics. Our finding serves as the theoretical underpinning for the phenomenological construction of the equation of state in the neutron star environment.

## Key claim

The paper argues that neutron star matter can be continuously connected to a two-flavor color-superconducting (2SC) phase with an additional condensate of down quarks in the \(^3P_2\) channel, extending the concept of quark-hadron continuity beyond the previously considered three-flavor color-flavor locked (CFL) case. The continuity holds only if this additional \(d\)-quark \(^3P_2\) condensate is present, because the residual symmetries of the 2SC phase alone differ from those of hadronic matter.

## Method

The author analyzes the symmetry breaking patterns of QCD for two-flavor matter, comparing the residual global symmetries of the hadronic phase and the color-superconducting quark phase. The paper identifies a novel quark phase called 2SC+\(\langle dd \rangle\) (2SC plus a \(d\)-quark condensate in the \(^3P_2\) channel) by constructing a gauge-invariant order parameter for neutron superfluidity and factorizing it into a 2SC diquark condensate and a \(d\)-quark pairing term. The dynamical mechanism favoring \(^3P_2\) pairing between \(d\)-quarks is justified by analogy to nuclear physics, using the spin-orbit interaction and the repulsive nature of one-gluon exchange in the color \(\boldsymbol{6}\) channel, and is connected to the equation of state via a four-fermion coupling term that couples to the energy-momentum tensor.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear density \(n_0 \simeq 0.16 \,\text{fm}^{-3}\), used as the reference density for neutron pairing regimes.
- Neutrons undergo \(^1S_0\) pairing at baryon densities \(n_{\rm B} < 0.5\,n_0\) and \(^3P_2\) pairing at \(n_{\rm B} > n_0\).
- The three-window modeling of the equation of state uses nuclear EoS at \(n_{\rm B} < 2\,n_0\) and quark EoS at \(n_{\rm B} > 5\,n_0\), with an interpolated intermediate region.

## Context

This work extends the concept of quark-hadron continuity, previously established for three-flavor matter in the color-flavor locked phase by Schafer (1998), to the more realistic two-flavor case relevant for neutron stars. The finding provides a theoretical basis for the three-window modeling of the dense matter equation of state as described by Baym (2017), where the hadronic and quark phases are connected by a smooth crossover. The paper enables future work on constructing neutron star equations of state and testing the continuity hypothesis against astrophysical observations, including methods using deep neural networks as explored by Fujimoto (2017, 2019).
