# Discovery potential of hidden charm baryon resonances via photoproduction

> In this work, we study the possibility to find $N^*_{c\bar{c}}$ and $Λ^*_{c\bar{c}}$ resonances with hidden charm with mass above 4 GeV in the photon-induced production. The cross sections for the photoproductions of hidden charmed baryons are predicted in the effective Lagrangian approach with the vector meson dominance mechanism. The $N^*(4412)$ can be produced directly by photon excitation with total cross section about 1 nb. The $N^*(4412)$ provides a obvious peak near threshold for $J/ψ$ photoproduction, which is promising to be checked by the future high precision experiment at JLab 12 GeV. The results will be helpful to the experimental search for the hidden-charmed baryon resonances in the coming experiment at JLab 12 GeV, such as SoLID, and the proposed electron-ion colliders at FAIR and HIAF.

## Key claim

The hidden-charm nucleon resonance \(N^*(4412)\) can be observed in photoproduction via a peak near the \(J/\psi\) threshold, provided future experiments at JLab 12 GeV reach a precision of about 0.1 nb; the claim is conditional on the resonance existing as predicted by Wu et al. (2010) and on the effective Lagrangian approach with vector meson dominance being valid.

## Method

The authors used an effective Lagrangian approach with the vector meson dominance (VMD) mechanism to compute cross sections for photoproduction of hidden-charm baryon resonances (\(N^*_{c\bar{c}}\) and \(\Lambda^*_{c\bar{c}}\)) with masses above 4 GeV. Coupling constants were extracted from the partial decay widths predicted by Wu et al. (2010) for S-wave decays, and form factors with a cutoff \(\Lambda_M = m_M + \alpha \Lambda_{\rm QCD}\) (\(\alpha = 1.3\), \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD} = 0.22\) GeV) were introduced to account for off-shell effects. Background contributions for \(\gamma p \to J/\psi p\) were modeled using two-gluon exchange, while for other channels (e.g., \(K^*\Sigma\)) t-channel meson exchanges were used.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section for \(N^*(4412)\) production via \(\gamma p \to J/\psi p\) at \(W = 4.412\) GeV is about 1 nb.
- Total cross section for \(N^*(4412)\) in the \(K^{*0}\Sigma^+\) channel at \(W = 4.412\) GeV is 0.4 nb (with \(\alpha = 1.3\)); for \(K^{*+}\Sigma^0\) it is 0.2 nb.
- Background cross sections at \(W = 4.412\) GeV are about 20 nb for \(\gamma p \to K^{*0}\Sigma^+\) and 6 nb for \(\gamma p \to K^{*+}\Sigma^0\).
- Total cross section for \(N^*(4261)\) production via \(\gamma p \to \pi^+ N^*(4261)\) is in the order 0.01–0.1 nb; for the specific channel \(\gamma p \to \pi^+ K^- \Sigma^+\) it is about 0.001 nb.
- Total cross sections for \(\Lambda^*(4209)\) and \(\Lambda^*(4368)\) productions are of the order \(10^{-3}\) nb.
- Coupling constants for \(N^*(4412)\) decays: \(g_{J/\psi N} = 0.416\), \(g_{\rho N} = 0.030\), \(g_{\omega N} = 0.096\), \(g_{K^*\Sigma} = 0.061\) (dimensionless).

## Context

This work extends the predictions of hidden-charm baryon resonances by Wu et al. (2010), who suggested masses around 4.2–4.6 GeV and widths below 100 MeV from coupled-channel calculations, and proposes photoproduction as a search channel complementary to the proton-antiproton collisions suggested for PANDA. The results are directly relevant to the JLab 12 GeV upgrade (including the SoLID detector) and to proposed electron-ion colliders at FAIR and HIAF, where the \(J/\psi\) photoproduction channel near threshold offers the most promising signal for discovering the \(N^*(4412)\) resonance.
