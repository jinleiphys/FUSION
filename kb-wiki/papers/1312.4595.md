# Nuclear shape coexistence: A study of the even-even Hg isotopes using the interacting boson model with configuration mixing

> Background: The Po, Pb, Hg, and Pt region is known for the presence of coexisting structures that correspond to different particle-hole configurations in the Shell Model language or equivalently to nuclear shapes with different deformation. Purpose: We intend to study the configuration mixing phenomenon in the Hg isotopes and to understand how different observables are influenced by it. Method: We study in detail a long chain of mercury isotopes, $^{172-200}$Hg, using the interacting boson model with configuration mixing. The parameters of the Hamiltonians are fixed through a least square fit to the known energies and absolute B(E2) transition rates of states up to $3$ MeV. Results: We obtained the IBM-CM Hamiltonians and we calculate excitation energies, B(E2)'s, quadrupole shape invariants, wave functions, isotopic shifts, and mean field energy surfaces. Conclusions: We obtain a fairly good agreement with the experimental data for all the studied observables and we conclude that the Hamiltonian and the states we obtain constitute a good approximation to the Hg isotopes.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the interacting boson model with configuration mixing (IBM-CM) provides a good approximation to the even-even Hg isotopes across the range 172-200, but this agreement is conditional on fixing the energy offset parameter Delta^{N+2} to 3480 keV and the mixing strengths to 20 keV, with the fit constrained to states below 3 MeV and using a weighted chi-squared minimization that assigns different sigma values to different classes of states.

## Method

The authors performed a least-squares fit of the IBM-CM Hamiltonian parameters to experimental excitation energies and absolute B(E2) transition rates for states up to 3 MeV in the even-even Hg isotopes from 172Hg to 200Hg. The model space includes two boson configurations: a regular [N]-boson subspace (proton 2-hole configurations) and an intruder [N+2]-boson subspace (proton 4-hole-2-particle configurations), with mixing described by a Hamiltonian containing an extended consistent-Q (ECQF) term in each subspace plus a mixing term. Seven parameters per isotope were varied (epsilon_N, kappa_N, chi_N, kappa_{N+2}, chi_{N+2}, e_N, e_{N+2}) while keeping Delta^{N+2}=3480 keV, w_0=w_2=20 keV, and epsilon_{N+2}=kappa'_N=kappa'_{N+2}=0 fixed.

## Key numbers

- The energy offset parameter Delta^{N+2} was fixed at 3480 keV for all isotopes, chosen to produce degenerate 2_1^+ and 0_2^+ states at A=182.
- The mixing strengths were fixed at w_0^{N,N+2}=w_2^{N,N+2}=20 keV for all Hg isotopes, compared to 50 keV for Pt and 18 keV for Pb from prior work.
- The Hamiltonian parameters for each isotope are tabulated; for example, in 182Hg: epsilon_N=1253.4 keV, kappa_N=-58.46 keV, chi_N=0.39, kappa_{N+2}=-39.91 keV, chi_{N+2}=-0.17, e_N=1.11 sqrt(W.u.), e_{N+2}=2.24 sqrt(W.u.).
- The effective charges for 188Hg were e_N=1.42 sqrt(W.u.) and e_{N+2}=2.13 sqrt(W.u.), used as defaults for 190-194Hg.
- The fit used sigma=0.1 keV for the 2_1^+ state, sigma=1 keV for 4_1^+, 0_2^+, 2_2^+ states, sigma=10 keV for 2_3^+, 3_1^+, 4_2^+, 6_1^+, 8_1^+ states, and sigma=100 keV for higher-lying states.

## Context

This work extends earlier IBM-CM studies of the Pb region by Heyde and collaborators (Heyde 1987, 1991; Fossion 2003; Hellemans 2005, 2008) and of Pt isotopes by Garcia-Ramos and Heyde (2009, 2011), applying the same configuration-mixing formalism to the full chain of Hg isotopes from 172 to 200. The results enable systematic comparison with experimental data on excitation energies, B(E2) values, isotopic shifts, and quadrupole shape invariants, and complement recent mean-field calculations using Skyrme functionals (Duguet 2003; Bender 2004; Yao 2013) and Gogny D1S forces (Rodriguez 2004, 2010) as well as the mapping approach of Nomura (2013) for Hg isotopes. The study provides a unified parameter set that can be used to predict properties of unmeasured states and to test the evolution of shape coexistence across the neutron shell.
