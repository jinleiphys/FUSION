# Global analysis of fragmentation functions to light neutral hadrons

> Fragmentation functions (FFs) are crucial non-perturbative components in quantum chromodynamics (QCD), playing a vital role in predictions and understanding of the hadronization process. In this paper, we present the FFs for $K_S^0$, $η$, $π^0$ mesons, and $Λ$ baryons in the context of global QCD analysis. The data included in the fit are from single inclusive $e^+ e^-$ annihilation (SIA), semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (SIDIS) and proton-proton collisions, with kinematic cuts carefully applied to ensure validity of collinear factorization and perturbative QCD expansion. For the first time, data from SIDIS and hadron-in-jet production in SIA have been incorporated into the extraction of FFs for light-flavor neutral hadrons. Our analysis reveals that these data play a critical role in constraining the gluon distribution, and in distinguishing between different quark flavors. Pulls from different datasets are also studied by performing alternative fits with systematically subtracting groups of data from the nominal fit. For the quality of the fit, good $χ^2$ values are achieved for most of the datasets, and FFs are generally well constrained within the momentum fraction region $\pqty{0.1, 0.5}$. The extracted $K_S^0$ fragmentation functions, together with the $K_S^0$ FFs constructed from $K^{\pm}$ FFs via isospin symmetry, are used to test isospin symmetry in kaon fragmentation. Although a definitive conclusion cannot be reached yet, these studies have identified several potential measurements that can be performed at existing facilities, which may ultimately help us to arrive at a conclusive answer. With the comprehensive species of FFs extracted within the NPC framework, we are able to perform a test on the momentum sum rule with the light-flavor charged and neutral hadrons.

## Key claim

The authors present the first global QCD analysis of fragmentation functions (FFs) for light neutral hadrons ($K_S^0$, $\eta$, $\pi^0$, and $\Lambda$) that incorporates semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (SIDIS) data and hadron-in-jet production data from $e^+e^-$ annihilation, finding that these new data critically constrain the gluon distribution and distinguish quark flavors, though a definitive conclusion on isospin symmetry in kaon fragmentation cannot yet be reached.

## Method

The authors performed a next-to-leading-order (NLO) global QCD fit of fragmentation functions for $K_S^0$, $\eta$, $\pi^0$, and $\Lambda$ hadrons using data from single inclusive $e^+e^-$ annihilation (SIA), semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (SIDIS), and proton-proton collisions, with kinematic cuts ($x_h > 0.05$ for SIA, $z_p > 0.05$ for SIDIS, $p_T > 4$ GeV for $pp$) applied to ensure collinear factorization validity. The $\pi^0$ FFs were derived from $\pi^\pm$ FFs via isospin symmetry rather than fitted independently. The fit included for the first time SIDIS data from ZEUS (2011) and hadron-in-jet data from ALEPH three-jet events, enabled by the FMNLO program, and the quality was assessed via $\chi^2$ values and alternative fits with systematic data subtraction.

## Key numbers

* The fragmentation functions are generally well constrained within the momentum fraction region (0.1, 0.5).
* For SIA data, kinematic cuts of $x_h > 0.05$ were applied; for SIDIS, $z_p > 0.05$; for $pp$ collisions, $p_T > 4$ GeV.
* The ZEUS SIDIS data were included in three $Q^2$ bins: (160, 640) GeV$^2$, (640, 2560) GeV$^2$, and (2560, 20140) GeV$^2$, with the two lowest $Q^2$ bins (10, 40) GeV$^2$ and (40, 160) GeV$^2$ excluded.
* The DELPHI data at $\sqrt{s} = 183$ GeV and 189 GeV were excluded due to known tension with other world data.
* The BESIII measurement on $K_S^0$ production was not included due to mass correction treatment limitations.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous NPC23 analysis of light charged hadron FFs (Gao et al., 2024, 2025) to neutral hadrons, enabling a test of the momentum sum rule for light-flavor charged and neutral hadrons. It builds upon prior extractions of $K_S^0$ FFs by BKK96, AKK05, AKK08, SAK20, LAXZ, and FF24 (which used only SIA data except AKK08), and $\Lambda$ FFs by DSV, AKK05, AKK08, and SAK20, while incorporating SIDIS and hadron-in-jet data for the first time for neutral hadrons. The results enable predictions for hadron production in various high-energy processes and identify potential measurements at existing facilities to resolve the isospin symmetry question in kaon fragmentation.
