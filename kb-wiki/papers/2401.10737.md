# Neutrino-Neutron Scattering Opacities in Supernova Matter

> We compute the static density and spin structure factors in the long wavelength limit for pure neutron matter at subsaturation densities relevant to core-collapse supernovae within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach. The BHF results are reliable at high densities, extending beyond the validity of the virial expansion. Motivated by the similarities between the dilute neutron gas and a unitary gas, we propose a phenomenological approach to derive the static structures with finite momentum transfer as well as the dynamic ones with simple analytical expressions, based on the computed static structures in the long wavelength limit. We also compare the in-medium neutrino-neutron scattering cross sections using different structure factors. Our study emphasizes the importance of accurately computing the static structure factors theoretically and utilizing the full dynamic structure factors in core-collapse supernova simulations.

## Key claim

The Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach provides reliable static structure factors for pure neutron matter at subsaturation densities where the virial expansion fails (fugacity \(z \gtrsim 0.5\)), but the BHF method itself is thermodynamically inconsistent at higher densities due to missing hole-hole correlations, requiring macroscopic chemical potentials for improved accuracy.

## Method

The authors computed static density and spin structure factors in the long wavelength limit for pure neutron matter using the BHF formalism with a chiral effective field theory two-body potential up to N\(^4\)LO, solving the Bethe-Goldstone equation self-consistently at finite temperature. They derived both microscopic chemical potentials (from the quasiparticle spectrum) and macroscopic chemical potentials (from thermodynamic derivatives of the free energy) to obtain \(S_V(0)\) and \(S_A(0)\). To extend to finite momentum transfer and dynamic structure factors, they proposed a phenomenological approach based on the similarity between dilute neutron gas and unitary gas, using Tan contact parameters and Gaussian fits constrained by \(f\)-sum rules.

## Key numbers

- Fugacity \(z\) reaches approximately 7 at \(T = 5\) MeV and density \(n = 0.063\) fm\(^{-3}\), the highest value considered.
- The virial expansion is valid for \(z \le 0.25\); BHF results are recommended for \(z \ge 0.5\), with numerical interpolation in between.
- Fitting functions for \(S_V(0)\) and \(S_A(0)\) are accurate within 2% for most conditions, with errors up to 5% at \(z \gtrsim 5\).
- The Tan contact for neutron gas is taken as \(I = 3\pi^2 (T/T_F)^2 (z^2/\pi - 0.14 z^3)\) for \(z \le 0.5\), and \(I = 2.22 + 0.4(1.1 - T/T_F)\) for \(z > 0.5\).
- At \(T = 10\) MeV and \(n = 0.004\) fm\(^{-3}\) (\(z \approx 0.23\)), the virial expansion gives \(S_V(0) \approx 1.13\) and \(S_A(0) \approx 0.75\).

## Context

This work extends the virial expansion studies of Horowitz (2005, 2006) and Bedaque (2018) for neutrino response in supernova matter to higher densities using microscopic BHF calculations, which are valid beyond the fugacity limit \(z \ll 1\). The results enable more accurate neutrino-neutron scattering opacities for core-collapse supernova simulations, particularly in regimes where the virial expansion fails and RPA calculations (Burrows 1998) overestimate \(S_A(0)\) at intermediate and high densities. The phenomenological mapping to unitary gas structure factors provides a practical method to obtain full dynamic structure factors for use in neutrino transport.
