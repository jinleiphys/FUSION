# Accessing the quark orbital angular momentum with Wigner distributions

> The quark orbital angular momentum (OAM) has been recognized as an important piece of the proton spin puzzle. A lot of effort has been invested in trying to extract it quantitatively from the generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and the transverse-momentum dependent parton distributions (TMDs), which are accessed in high-energy processes and provide three-dimensional pictures of the nucleon. Recently, we have shown that it is more natural to access the quark OAM from the phase-space or Wigner distributions. We discuss the concept of Wigner distributions in the context of quantum field theory and show how they are related to the GPDs and the TMDs. We summarize the different definitions discussed in the literature for the quark OAM and show how they can in principle be extracted from the Wigner distributions.

## Key claim

The quark orbital angular momentum (OAM) can be accessed more naturally from phase-space Wigner distributions than from generalized parton distributions (GPDs) or transverse-momentum dependent parton distributions (TMDs), but the definition of OAM in terms of Wigner distributions always corresponds to a gauge-invariant extension of the canonical OAM, not the kinetic OAM, contrary to claims by Ji, Xiong, and Yuan (2012).

## Method

The authors constructed quark Wigner distributions via a two-dimensional Fourier transform of generalized transverse-momentum dependent parton distributions (GTMDs) in the impact-parameter representation, using light-front coordinates. They computed these distributions within the light-front constituent quark model (LFCQM) and compared with results from the light-front version of the chiral quark-soliton model (LFchiQSM), focusing on the distribution of unpolarized quarks in a longitudinally polarized nucleon. The longitudinal component of quark OAM was then expressed as a phase-space integral of the cross product of impact parameter and transverse momentum weighted by the Wigner distribution.

## Key numbers

- The d-quark OAM changes sign around 0.25 fm away from the transverse center of momentum, as observed in the impact-parameter distribution of average quark transverse momentum in a longitudinally polarized nucleon.
- At twist-two level, there exist 16 Wigner distributions for quarks.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Lorce and Pasquini, 2011) on quark Wigner distributions and their relation to OAM, building on the GTMD framework of Meissner, Metz, and Schlegel (2009). It contradicts the claim by Ji, Xiong, and Yuan (2012) that a straight gauge link in the Wigner distribution yields the kinetic OAM, instead arguing it gives the gauge-invariant extension of the canonical OAM. The results enable future experimental access to quark OAM through Wigner distributions, though no direct experimental process is currently known.
