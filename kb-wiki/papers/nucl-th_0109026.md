# In-medium chiral perturbation theory beyond the mean-field approximation

> An explicit expression of the generating functional of two-flavor low-energy QCD with external sources in the presence of non-vanishing nucleon densities has been derived recently [1]. Within this approach we derive power counting rules for the calculation of in-medium pion properties. We develop the so-called standard rules for residual nucleon energies of the order of the pion mass and a modified scheme (non-standard counting) for vanishing residual nucleon energies. We also establish the different scales for the range of applicability of this perturbative expansion, which are \sqrt{6}πf_π\simeq 0.7 GeV for the standard and 6π^2 f_π^2/2m_N\simeq 0.27 GeV for non-standard counting, respectively. We have performed a systematic analysis of n-point in-medium Green functions up to and including next-to-leading order when the standard rules apply. These include the in-medium contributions to quark condensates, pion propagators, pion masses and couplings of the axial-vector, vector and pseudoscalar currents to pions. In particular, we find a mass shift for negatively charged pions in heavy nuclei that agrees with recent determinations from deeply bound pionic Pb-207. We have also established the absence of in-medium renormalization in the π^0 \to γγdecay amplitude up to the same order. The study of ππscattering requires the use of the non-standard counting and the calculation is done at leading order. Even at that order we establish new contributions not considered so far. We also point towards further possible improvements of this scheme and touch upon its relation to more conventional many-body approaches.

## Key claim
The paper derives a systematic chiral perturbation theory for pions in nuclear matter that goes beyond the simple mean-field approximation by including nucleon propagator effects, but only for the case where multi-nucleon local interactions are neglected. The validity of the expansion depends on two distinct counting schemes: standard counting (for residual nucleon energies of order the pion mass) with a breakdown scale of about 0.7 GeV, and non-standard counting (for vanishing residual nucleon energies) with a lower breakdown scale of about 0.27 GeV.

## Method
The authors start from the in-medium generating functional of two-flavor chiral perturbation theory derived by integrating out nucleon fields at bilinear order, then establish power counting rules for diagrams involving generalized in-medium vertices that include Fermi-sea insertions and free baryon propagators. They systematically compute n-point in-medium Green functions up to next-to-leading order (O(p^5)) for the standard counting case, including quark condensates, pion propagators, pion masses, and couplings to external currents, and perform a leading-order (O(p^4)) calculation of ππ scattering under the non-standard counting scheme.

## Key numbers
- The standard counting breakdown scale is √(6)πf_π ≃ 0.7 GeV
- The non-standard counting breakdown scale is 6π²f_π²/(2m_N) ≃ 0.27 GeV
- The mass shift for negatively charged pions in heavy nuclei is ΔM_{π^-} = (18 ± 5) MeV, agreeing with determinations from deeply bound pionic ²⁰⁷Pb
- For symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³, the quark condensate shows a relative reduction of (35 ± 9)% from its vacuum value, using σ = 44 ± 8 ± 7 MeV
- The isospin-breaking difference in quark condensates is ⟨Ω|ūu - d̄d|Ω⟩ = ⟨q̄q⟩_{vac} × 0.028 × (ρ_n - ρ_p)/ρ₀
- The isoscalar sum of quark condensates follows ⟨Ω|ūu + d̄d|Ω⟩ ≃ ⟨q̄q⟩_{vac}[1 - 0.35(ρ_n + ρ_p)/ρ₀]

## Context
This work extends the in-medium chiral perturbation theory formalism of Meißner et al. (2001) beyond the mean-field approach used in earlier works by Kaplan, other authors, and by A. Wirzba (1998), where nucleon bilinears were simply replaced by density-dependent traces. The approach also exceeds the low-density theorems formulated in references LDT (1995). The results enable systematic calculations of pion properties in nuclear matter with controlled error estimates, and the predicted pion mass shift provides a theoretical foundation for interpreting deeply bound pionic state measurements at GSI.
