# Role of Matter Inhomogeneity on Fast Flavor Conversion of Supernova Neutrinos

> We investigated the impact of a spatially varying matter potential $λ$, coming from neutrino-electron forward scattering, on the emergence of fast neutrino flavor conversion (FFC) triggered by the presence of zero crossings in the angular distribution of the neutrino electron lepton number (ELN). We find that FFC can be significantly affected as the spatial variation rate of $λ$ increases, and strong spatial variations can completely stabilize initially unstable systems. Using stability analysis based solely on initial conditions, we identified for the first time a critical variation rate above which no FFC occurs even if the flavor instability exists. By analyzing several representative matter profiles based on an 18 $M_{\odot}$ SN model, we show that spatially inhomogeneous $λ$ can suppress the occurrence of FFC associated with shallow ELN zero crossings in most of the SN's radial region, especially during the accretion phase. Our finding highlights the need to consider the impact of matter inhomogeneity in the development of improved SN models that aim to include the effect of neutrino flavor conversions.

## Key claim
Spatially varying matter potential from neutrino-electron forward scattering can suppress or completely stabilize fast flavor conversion (FFC) even when the angular distribution of the neutrino electron lepton number (ELN) contains zero crossings, provided the spatial variation rate exceeds a critical value determined by the initial neutrino angular distribution.

## Method
The authors used a simplified two-beam model with radial velocities v = +1 and v = -1, neglecting vacuum oscillations and collisions, and solved the flavor evolution equations using the finite-volume version of the COSEν solver over a 60 cm spatial domain with 6,000 uniform grids. They performed linear stability analysis by transforming the equations into a time-dependent ordinary differential equation for each Fourier mode, introducing a dimensionless parameter γ = β/r_s (the ratio of maximum instability growth rate to the shift rate of unstable Fourier modes in k-space). They applied their analytical criteria to radial matter profiles from an 18 M⊙ supernova model at four post-bounce snapshots.

## Key numbers
- For the two-beam model with α = 1/3 and μ = 1.5 cm⁻¹, the maximum instability growth rate β = 2μ√α ≈ 58 ns⁻¹ at k₀ = μ(1+α) ≈ 2 cm⁻¹
- The range of unstable Fourier modes without matter effect is k ∈ (0.3, 3.6) cm⁻¹, with k_min = μ(1-√α)² ≈ 0.3 cm⁻¹ and k_max = μ(1+√α)² ≈ 3.6 cm⁻¹
- The critical spatial variation rate m_crit ≈ 3 cm⁻², corresponding to γ ≈ 1, above which no FFC occurs
- For m = 20 cm⁻² (γ ≈ 0.15), all initially unstable Fourier modes decay exponentially at rate r_s ≈ 385 ns⁻¹
- For the 18 M⊙ SN model, α_crit(r) ranges from 10⁻⁵ to 10⁻³ in regions above the proto-neutron star during accretion phases (t = 0.05 s, 0.1 s, 0.3 s)
- Simulations were run up to t = 1.8 ns with spatial variation rates m = {10⁻⁶, 10⁻⁴, 10⁻², 0.5, 1, 1.5, 2, 5, 10, 20} cm⁻²

## Context
This work extends studies of fast flavor conversion in core-collapse supernovae that previously ignored spatial variation of the matter potential (Bhattacharyya 2021, Wu 2021, Richers 2021, Xiong 2023, Nagakura 2023). It contradicts the implicit assumption from earlier work (Izaguirre 2017, Chakraborty 2016) that matter inhomogeneity does not qualitatively affect FFC development. The finding enables improved supernova models that incorporate neutrino flavor conversions by providing a quantitative criterion (γ < 1) for when matter inhomogeneity must be included, particularly relevant for shallow ELN zero crossings identified in recent studies (Johns 2024, Xiong 2024, Liu 2024).
