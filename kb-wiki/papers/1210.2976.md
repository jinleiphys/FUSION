# Electromagnetic Observables in Few-Nucleon Systems

> The electromagnetic probe is a very valuable tool to study the dynamics of few nucleons. It can be very helpful in shedding light on the not yet fully understood three-nucleon forces. We present an update on the theoretical studies of electromagnetic induced reactions, such as photo-disintegration and electron scattering off 4He. We will show that they potentially represent a tool to discriminate among three-nucleon forces. Then, we will discuss the charge radius and the nuclear electric polarizability of the 6He halo nucleus.

## Key claim

Electromagnetic observables in few-nucleon systems show sensitivity to nuclear Hamiltonians and could potentially discriminate among different three-nucleon forces, but for the photo-disintegration of 4He the theoretical sensitivity to changes in Hamiltonians is smaller than the differences among experimental data sets, making discrimination not yet possible.

## Method

The author uses the hyper-spherical harmonics (HH) expansion to solve the Schrodinger equation for bound states in few-body systems with up to six constituents. For electromagnetic break-up reactions, the Lorentz integral transform (LIT) method is employed to circumvent the need to know all possible final continuum states, reducing the problem to solving an equation with bound-state-like asymptotics. Calculations are performed with realistic potentials including the AV18+UIX force, chiral effective field theory forces, and low-momentum interactions (Vlowk).

## Key numbers

- The theoretical sensitivity of the 4He photo-absorption cross section at the peak to different Hamiltonians is of the order of 10%, which is much less than the difference in experimental data (data from Shima et al. are a factor of 2 smaller than all other measurements)
- At low momentum transfer q=50 MeV/c, the difference between calculations of the longitudinal response function RL for 4He with NN only and with 3NFs reaches 50%
- The charge radius of 6He was measured as r_ch = 2.060 ± 0.008 fm, which can be converted into a point-proton radius
- The theoretical nuclear electric polarizability of 6He is estimated as α_E = 1.00(13) fm^3, about a factor of two smaller than the experimental value of α_E^exp = 1.99(40) fm^3
- The extrapolated neutron skin radius of 6He is r_skin(∞) = 0.87(5) fm

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of electromagnetic observables in few-nucleon systems, including calculations of the 4He photo-absorption cross section with the AV18+UIX potential from Bacca (2009), with chiral EFT forces from Bacca (2010), and with a central 3NF from Wataru (2012). The longitudinal response function calculations at different momentum transfer values extend the work of Bacca (2009, 2010). For 6He, the study of the charge radius and polarizability builds on prior ab-initio calculations using NCSM, FMD, MCM, and GFMC methods, with only the GFMC calculations including three-nucleon forces. The results enable future investigations of the sensitivity of electromagnetic observables to different nuclear Hamiltonians, particularly for discriminating among three-nucleon force models when precise experimental data become available.
