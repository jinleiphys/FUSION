# Density slope of the nuclear symmetry energy from the neutron skin thickness of heavy nuclei

> Expressing explicitly the parameters of the standard Skyrme interaction in terms of the macroscopic properties of asymmetric nuclear matter, we show in the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach that unambiguous correlations exist between observables of finite nuclei and nuclear matter properties. We find that existing data on neutron skin thickness $Δr_{np}$ of Sn isotopes give an important constraint on the symmetry energy $E_{sym}({ρ_{0}})$ and its density slope $L$ at saturation density ${ρ_{0}}$. Combining these constraints with those from recent analyses of isospin diffusion and double neutron/proton ratio in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies leads to a more stringent limit on $L$ approximately independent of $E_{sym}({ρ_{0}})$. The implication of these new constraints on the $Δr_{np}$ of $^{208}$Pb as well as the core-crust transition density and pressure in neutron stars is discussed.

# Density slope of the nuclear symmetry energy from the neutron skin thickness of heavy nuclei

## Key claim
The authors show that existing neutron skin thickness data on Sn isotopes, combined with constraints from heavy-ion collision analyses, yield a value of the density slope L = 58 ± 18 MeV that is approximately independent of the symmetry energy at saturation density, but this result depends on the specific choice of the symmetry-gradient coefficient G_V = 5 MeV·fm⁵ and the use of the standard Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach with the MSL0 parameter set.

## Method
The authors express the 9 parameters of the standard Skyrme interaction in terms of 9 macroscopic properties of asymmetric nuclear matter (saturation density, binding energy, incompressibility, isoscalar and isovector effective masses, symmetry energy and its slope, and gradient coefficients) using the modified Skyrme-like (MSL) model. They then perform Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations for finite nuclei, varying one macroscopic quantity at a time while keeping others fixed at their MSL0 default values, to isolate correlations between neutron skin thickness and individual nuclear matter properties. A χ² analysis is carried out comparing calculated neutron skin thicknesses of Sn isotopes against experimental data.

## Key numbers
- L = 54 ± 13 MeV: extracted from Sn isotope neutron skin data alone (2σ uncertainty)
- L = 58 ± 18 MeV: combined constraint from Sn skin data and heavy-ion collision analyses (isospin diffusion and double n/p ratio), approximately independent of E_sym(ρ₀)
- Δr_np of ²⁰⁸Pb = 0.175 ± 0.02 fm: predicted from the combined L–E_sym(ρ₀) constraints
- Core-crust transition density ρ_t = 0.069 ± 0.018 fm⁻³ in neutron stars
- Core-crust transition pressure P_t = 0.33 ± 0.21 MeV/fm³ in neutron stars
- G_V = 5 MeV·fm⁵: chosen value leading to Landau parameter G₀' = 0.42, with G_S = 132 MeV·fm⁵ and W₀ = 133.3 MeV·fm⁵
- Default MSL0 values: ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³, E₀(ρ₀) = -16 MeV, K₀ = 230 MeV, m_s,0* = 0.8m, m_v,0* = 0.7m, E_sym(ρ₀) = 30 MeV, L = 60 MeV

## Context
This work extends previous mean-field studies (Bro00, Hor01, Fur02, Yosh04, Che05b, Tod05, Rei10) that found correlations between neutron skin thickness and symmetry energy, but were limited by the highly correlated nature of Skyrme parameter variations. The authors' MSL approach allows transparent isolation of individual macroscopic quantities. The combined constraint L = 58 ± 18 MeV agrees with the value L = 66.5 MeV from microscopic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations using the Argonne V18 potential plus three-body force (Vid09) and with L = 52.7 MeV from global nucleon optical potential analyses (XuC10). The predicted Δr_np for ²⁰⁸Pb of 0.175 ± 0.02 fm provides a target for the PREX experiment at Jefferson Lab, which aims to measure the neutron radius of ²⁰⁸Pb to 1% accuracy. The core-crust transition results improve upon earlier work (XuJ09) that used E_sym(ρ₀) = 30.5 MeV and L = 86 ± 25 MeV from isospin diffusion data alone.
