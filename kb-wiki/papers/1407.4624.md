# Semirelativistic Bound-State Equations: Trivial Considerations

> Observing renewed interest in long-standing (semi-) relativistic descriptions of bound states, we would like to make a few comments on the eigenvalue problem posed by the spinless Salpeter equation and, illustrated by the examples of the nonsingular Woods-Saxon potential and the singular Hulthén potential, recall elementary tools that practitioners looking for analytic albeit approximate solutions might find useful in their quest.

## Key claim
The spinless Salpeter equation, a semirelativistic bound-state equation, cannot be solved analytically in general, but elementary tools such as upper bounds on the number of bound states, variational bounds on eigenvalues, and a relativistic virial theorem can provide a clear picture of expected solutions, though not all published solutions respect these constraints.

## Method
The authors apply the spinless Salpeter equation (with Hamiltonian \( H = 2\sqrt{\mathbf{p}^2 + m^2} + V(r) \)) to two central potentials: the nonsingular Woods–Saxon potential and the singular Hulthén potential. They compute variational upper bounds to binding energies using a trial basis of generalized-Laguerre polynomials with two variational parameters, and compare these to nonrelativistic Schrödinger equation results. They also derive constraints on potential parameters from boundedness-from-below conditions and from the relativistic generalization of the virial theorem.

## Key numbers
- For the Woods–Saxon potential with parameters \( m = 940.271\ \text{MeV} \), \( V_0 = 67.70352\ \text{MeV} \), \( R = 7.6136\ \text{fm} \), \( a = 0.65\ \text{fm} \): the lower bound on the energy spectrum is \( E_0 \ge V(0) = -67.70296\ \text{MeV} \).
- For the same Woods–Saxon system, the maximum number of bound states is \( N \le 850 \) for relativistic kinematics and \( N \le 1201 \) for nonrelativistic kinematics.
- Variational upper bounds to semirelativistic Woods–Saxon binding energies (in GeV) for the ground state (\( n_r=0, \ell=0 \)): \( -0.06032 \) (spinless Salpeter) vs. \( -0.06030 \) (Schrödinger).
- For the Hulthén potential with parameters \( m = 1 \), \( b = 0.15 \), \( v = 0.11 \) (arbitrary units): boundedness from below requires \( v/b < 16/(3\pi) \).
- Variational upper bounds to semirelativistic Hulthén binding energies (arbitrary units) for the ground state (\( n_r=0, \ell=0 \)): \( -0.10577 \) (spinless Salpeter) vs. \( -0.085069\dot4 \) (Schrödinger).
- For the Woods–Saxon system, the expectation value of \( \mathbf{p}^2/m^2 \) over the first trial state is approximately \( 6 \times 10^{-3} \), indicating a highly nonrelativistic regime.

## Context
This work extends prior analyses of the spinless Salpeter equation by Lucha and Schöberl (multiple references, e.g., Lucha:SSEa, Lucha:SSEb) and applies known rigorous bounds, such as the Bargmann bound for nonrelativistic operators and the Daubechies bound for semirelativistic operators, to specific potentials. It directly critiques recent approximate solutions by Hamzavi (2012) for the Woods–Saxon potential and by Zarrinkamar (2013) for the Hulthén potential, showing that some published results violate elementary constraints. The paper enables practitioners to validate approximate solutions by checking against variational upper bounds, the relativistic virial theorem, and boundedness conditions.
