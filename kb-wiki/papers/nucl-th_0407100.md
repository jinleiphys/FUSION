# The meaning of S-D dominance

> The dominance of S and D pairs in the description of deformed nuclei is one of the facts that provided sustain to the Interacting Boson Approximation. In Ref.(J. Dukelsky and S. Pittel, Phys. Rev. Lett. 86, 4791, 2001.), using an exactly solvable model with a repulsive pairing interaction between bosons it has been shown that the ground state is described almost completely in terms of S and D bosons. In the present paper we study the excited states obtained within this exactly solvable hamiltonian and show that in order to obtain a rotational spectra all the other degrees of freedom are needed.

## Key claim

The paper shows that while S and D bosons dominate the occupation probabilities (more than 85%) in the ground state and low-lying excited states of a solvable boson model with repulsive pairing, the high-angular-momentum bosons (with small occupation probabilities) are essential for reproducing a rotational spectrum; the S-D dominance alone is insufficient.

## Method

The authors use an exactly solvable Richardson-Gaudin integrable model with a repulsive pairing interaction between bosons, where the bosons represent correlated pairs of nucleons with angular momenta up to λ=12. They solve the eigenvalue equations for a system of 40 bosons, tuning the single-boson energies to reproduce rigid-rotor energy ratios at a coupling strength G/hν₂=0.72, and compute occupation probabilities for each boson type across different seniority states.

## Key numbers

- For the calculation including all even λ up to 12 at G/hν₂=0.72, the S (λ=0) and D (λ=2) bosons together share more than 85% of the occupation probability in the ground state (J=0, ν_i=0): P(J=0)=0.70384, P(J=2)=0.17515.
- The asymptotic value of the 2₁ state excitation energy is approximately 0.124 in units of hν₂ (the λ=2 boson energy).
- For the calculation including only bosons up to λ=8, the asymptotic 2⁺ energy is 0.101 in units of hν₂, and the 8⁺ state cannot be properly reproduced.
- The single-boson energy differences (relative to the J=0 boson) used for the full calculation are: E₄=1.25, E₆=1.50, E₈=2.12, E₁₀=2.28, E₁₂=2.42 (in units of hν₂).

## Context

This work extends the exactly solvable model of Dukelsky and Pittel (2001), who showed S-D dominance in the ground state using a repulsive pairing interaction, by examining excited states and the conditions for rotational spectra. The results contradict the assumption that S-D dominance alone justifies the Interacting Boson Approximation (IBA) of Arima and Iachello, showing instead that high-angular-momentum bosons (G, I, etc.) are necessary for rotational bands even when their occupation probabilities are small. The paper enables future work on whether the IBA bosons can be directly related to those from Nilsson+BCS calculations.
