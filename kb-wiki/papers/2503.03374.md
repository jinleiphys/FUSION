# Holographic derivation of effective action for a dissipative neutral fluid

> Using a holographic prescription for the Schwinger-Keldysh closed time path, we derive the effective action for a dissipative neutral fluid holographically described by the Einstein gravity in an asymptotic AdS spacetime. In the saddle point approximation for the dual gravity, the goal is achieved by solving the double Dirichlet problem for the linearized gravitational field living in a complexified static AdS black brane background. We adopt a partially on-shell scheme for solving the bulk dynamics, which is equivalent to ``integrating out'' the gapped modes in the boundary field theory. The boundary effective action in the fluid spacetime, identified as the partially on-shell bulk action, is computed to first order in boundary derivative and to cubic order in AdS boundary data. The boundary effective action, rewritten in the physical spacetime, successfully reproduces various results known in the framework of classical hydrodynamics, confirming our holographic derivation.

## Key claim

Using a holographic prescription for the Schwinger-Keldysh closed time path, the authors derive the effective action for a dissipative neutral fluid to first order in boundary derivative and to cubic order in AdS boundary data, but only in the saddle point approximation for the dual gravity and for linearized gravitational perturbations on a complexified static AdS black brane background.

## Method

The authors solve the double Dirichlet problem for linearized gravitational fields living in a complexified static AdS black brane background, adopting a partially on-shell scheme that is equivalent to "integrating out" the gapped modes in the boundary field theory. The boundary effective action in the fluid spacetime is identified as the partially on-shell bulk action, computed to first order in boundary derivative and to cubic order in AdS boundary data. The bulk metric perturbation on top of the static AdS black brane introduces variations and fluctuations for the fluid velocity and temperature on the boundary.

## Key numbers

* The boundary effective action is computed to first order in boundary derivative and to cubic order in AdS boundary data.
* The shear viscosity over entropy density ratio predicted by the holographic duality is described as universal for a large class of strongly coupled field theories, with the holographic prediction being close to those of the quark-gluon plasma and the unitary Fermi gas (Adams 2012).
* The paper reproduces the hydrodynamic constitutive relation including the shear viscosity term $\eta_0$ in $T_{\rm hydro}^{\mu\nu} = (\epsilon + P) u^\mu u^\nu + P g_{\r}^{\mu\nu} - \eta_0 \sigma^{\mu\nu} + \cdots$.

## Context

This work extends the holographic Schwinger-Keldysh technique of Glorioso (2018) to the dynamics of bulk gravity itself, rather than focusing only on matter fields in a complexified AdS black brane background as in previous studies (Glorioso 2018, deBoer 2018, Chakrabarty 2019, Bu 2020-2024). Previous attempts to derive hydrodynamic effective actions from AdS gravity (Crossley 2015, deBoer 2015) encountered problems with the gravity metric near the horizon, which were avoided by a non-dissipative horizon condition. The present work successfully reproduces various results from classical hydrodynamics, including hydrodynamic modes, the hydrodynamic constitutive relation, the stochastic version of the hydrodynamic equation, and the full set of two-point correlators of the stress tensor, enabling a systematic holographic derivation of the hydrodynamic effective field theory.
