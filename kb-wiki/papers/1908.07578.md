# Renormalization of pionless effective field theory in the A-body sector

> Current models of inter-nucleon interactions are built within the frame of Effective Field Theories (EFTs). Contrary to traditional nuclear potentials, EFT interactions require a renormalization of their parameters in order to derive meaningful estimations of observable. In this paper, a renormalization procedure is designed in connection with many-body approximations applicable to large-A systems and formulated within the frame of many-body perturbation theory. The procedure is shown to generate counterterms that are independent of the targeted A-body sector. As an example, the procedure is applied to the random phase approximation. This work constitutes one step towards the design of a practical EFT for many-body systems.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a renormalization procedure for pionless effective field theory can be designed specifically for a given many-body approximation (formulated within many-body perturbation theory) such that the necessary counterterms are independent of the A-body sector, rather than requiring renormalization to be performed separately for each A.

## Method

The authors formulate the renormalization problem within many-body perturbation theory using a kinetic-energy unperturbed Hamiltonian and a contact two-body interaction C0, regularized by a momentum cutoff Λ. They employ Weinberg's asymptotic theorem and the Bogoliubov-Parasiuk-Hepp-Zimmermann (BPHZ) theorem to analyze ultraviolet divergences in time-ordered diagrams built from particle and hole propagators that depend on the A-body reference state. The procedure is then applied to the Random Phase Approximation (RPA) as a concrete example of a many-body approximation.

## Key numbers

* The superficial degree of divergence for an in-vacuum diagram with n vertices and k external lines is D = 5 - 3k + n (Equation in Section 2.2.2).
* The in-vacuum propagator has asymptotic coefficient α = -1 for the subspace spanned by the energy direction, α = -2 for any single non-energy direction, and α = -2 for subspaces of dimension ≥ 2 (Section 2.1.1).

## Context

This work extends the renormalization program of pionless effective field theory (Bedaque 1998, Bedaque 2000, Platter 2004) from few-body exact solutions to many-body approximations applicable to large-A systems. It addresses the challenge that exact solution of the leading-order Schrödinger equation is numerically intractable for A ≫ 10, requiring methods such as self-consistent Green's functions (Dickhoff 2004, Carbone 2013, Soma 2014), coupled cluster (Hagen 2014, Binder 2014), in-medium similarity renormalization group (Tsukiyama 2011, Hergert 2016), or many-body perturbation theory (Goldstone 1957, Tichai 2018). The paper enables future construction of EFTs where the renormalization prescription is built into the many-body approximation itself, rather than relying on exact few-body renormalization that may not carry over to approximate large-A calculations.
