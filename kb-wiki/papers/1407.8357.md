# Multiplicity fluctuation from hydrodynamic noise

> We discuss multiplicity fluctuation caused by noises during hydrodynamic evolution of the quark-gluon fluid created in high-energy nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

Multiplicity fluctuations in high-energy nuclear collisions can arise from hydrodynamic noise (stochastic terms in the constitutive equations for dissipative currents) even when the initial state is macroscopically identical, and these fluctuations are bounded by a value determined solely from the initial entropy.

## Method

The authors employed relativistic fluctuating hydrodynamics in a (1+1)-dimensional Bjorken coordinate system with a boost-invariant scaling ansatz. They introduced stochastic constitutive equations for shear stress and bulk pressure, each containing a Gaussian white noise term, coupled to the energy density evolution equation. They then derived the entropy production rate and applied the fluctuation theorem from non-equilibrium statistical mechanics to quantify the probability of negative entropy production in individual events.

## Key numbers

- The relative width of entropy distribution per local thermal system at time τ is given by ΔS/⟨S⟩(τ) = √(2⟨Δ(τs)⟩) / (τ₀s₀ + ⟨Δ(τs)⟩) × 1/√(Δηₛ Δx Δy), where τ₀ is initial time and s₀ is initial entropy density.
- The relative fluctuation for total entropy per space-time rapidity window Δηₛ is bounded by 1/√(2S_ini), where S_ini is the initial entropy.
- For p+p collisions at RHIC and the LHC, 1/√(σ_in) ≈ 0.4–0.5 fm⁻¹, which is comparable to values in peripheral A+A collisions.
- The factor 1/√A (controlling entropy fluctuation) rapidly enhances in peripheral collisions at impact parameters b ∼ 10–12 fm for nuclei with radius 6 fm.

## Context

This work extends the conventional hydrodynamic framework by incorporating causal hydrodynamic fluctuations, building on the authors' prior development of relativistic fluctuating hydrodynamics (Murase 2013). It contrasts with standard dissipative hydrodynamics where constitutive equations are designed to obey the second law of thermodynamics, showing instead that stochastic equations obey the fluctuation-dissipation relation on average. The results suggest that hydrodynamic noise effects on multiplicity fluctuations become important in small systems such as peripheral A+A collisions and high-multiplicity p+A and p+p events, where conventional hydrodynamics' validity has been questioned (CMS 2012, Abelev 2012).
