# Low Energy Nuclear Structure from Ultra-relativistic Heavy-Light Ion collisions

> The search for specific signals in ultra-relativistic heavy-light ion collisions addressing intrinsic geometric features of nuclei may open a new window to low energy nuclear structure. We discuss specifically the phenomenon of α-clustering in $^{12}$C when colliding with $^{208}$Pb at almost the speed of light.

## Key claim

The paper argues that specific geometric features of low-energy nuclear structure, such as α-clustering in ¹²C, can be probed through ultra-relativistic heavy-light ion collisions (¹²C+²⁰⁸Pb at SPS energies), provided that the analysis focuses on multiparticle correlations like harmonic flow coefficients rather than one-body observables.

## Method

The authors use the Glauber formalism with Monte Carlo techniques (GLISSANDO code) to simulate ¹²C+²⁰⁸Pb collisions at √sNN = 17 GeV, comparing clustered (three α-particles in an equilateral triangle) and uniform (unclustered) wave functions for ¹²C. The α-clustered wave function is constructed by randomly generating Gaussian nucleon positions within each α-cluster, placing cluster centers at triangle vertices with side length d ≈ 3 fm, and incorporating short-distance NN repulsion. The resulting fireball density in the transverse plane is analyzed via eccentricity parameters (ε₂ for ellipticity, ε₃ for triangularity) and their cumulant moments.

## Key numbers

- α-particle charge mean squared radius: ⟨r²⟩_{em,α} = (1.668(5) fm)²
- α-particle baryon radius: ⟨r²⟩_α = (1.42 fm)²
- α-α effective elementarity radius: r_c = 2.88(3) fm
- ⁸Be resonance energy: Q = 92 KeV, width Γ = 3.4(2) eV
- ⁸Be binding energy without Coulomb above r_c: B_{αα} = 1.5229 MeV
- ¹²C triangle side length from form factor fit: d = 3.05 fm
- Bonding model fit: V_{αα} = -2.29 MeV for A=4n nuclei with n ≥ 3
- Many-body model fit: V_{2α} = -2.39 MeV, V_{3α} = 0.47 MeV, V_{4α} = -0.04 MeV, mean standard deviation 0.1 MeV per nucleon
- NN scattering analyzed at √s = 23.5 GeV

## Context

This work extends the connection between low-energy nuclear structure and high-energy heavy-ion collisions previously explored by Broniowski (2013) and Bozek (2014), specifically applying it to α-clustering in ¹²C. The method contrasts with traditional one-body probes (electron scattering form factors, excitation spectra) which cannot directly prove clusterization, and instead uses multiparticle correlations accessible through harmonic flow. The approach enables future experimental searches for clusterization signals in light-heavy ion collisions at SPS energies, and conversely allows clustered wave functions to constrain fireball evolution models (hydrodynamics, transport) for studying hot dense matter properties.
