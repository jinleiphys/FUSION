# Quarks, diquarks and QCD mixing in the $N^*$ resonance spectrum

> We identify a "$Λ$ selection rule" for $N^*$ resonances in the presence of QCD mixing effects. We quantify these mixing effects from existing data and predict amplitudes for exciting {\bf 20} representations in SU(6), which are forbidden in strict diquark models. By classifying Particle-Data-Group (PDG) states at N=2, we show that $γN\to KΛ$, $K^*Λ$, $KΣ$, $K^*Σ$, and $J/ψ\to \bar{p}N^*$ are ideal probes of baryon dynamics and for establishing whether strongly correlated diquarks survive for $L > 0$.

# Quarks, diquarks and QCD mixing in the $N^*$ resonance spectrum

## Key claim
The authors identify a "Λ selection rule" for $N^*$ resonances that has been overlooked in the literature: $N^*$ states in the $[{\bf 70}, ^4 8]$ representation decouple from $K\Lambda$ and $K^*\Lambda$ channels in the spectator approximation. This rule remains robust even when QCD mixing effects are included, with violations expected at most at the 20% level in amplitude, comparable to the Moorhouse selection rule violations already observed.

## Method
The authors classify Particle Data Group (PDG) baryon resonances at the $N=2$ oscillator level using SU(6)$\otimes$O(3) symmetry and quantify QCD mixing effects from single-gluon-exchange interactions that induce ${\bf 70}$-plet admixtures into the nucleon wavefunction. They compute transition amplitudes for exciting $[{\bf 20}, 1^+]$ representations (forbidden in strict diquark models) via the ${\bf 70}$ component of the nucleon, and apply the Λ selection rule to distinguish between $[{\bf 56}, ^2 8]$, $[{\bf 70}, ^2 8]$, $[{\bf 70}, ^4 8]$, and $[{\bf 20}, ^2 8]$ assignments for observed $P_{11}$ and $P_{13}$ states.

## Key numbers
- The nucleon wavefunction mixing coefficients from QCD: $|N\rangle = 0.90|^2S_S\rangle -0.34|^2S_{S'}\rangle -0.27|^2S_M\rangle -0.06|^4 D_M\rangle$, giving 34% amplitude in excited ${\bf 56}$, 27% in ${\bf 70}$ with $L=0$, and 6% in ${\bf 70}$ with $L=2$.
- The $N$-$\Delta$ mass gap of 300 MeV is cited as the scale of $\Lambda_{QCD}$.
- Photoproduction amplitudes for $P_{11}(1440)$: $A^p_{1/2} = -0.065 \pm 0.004$ GeV$^{-1/2}$ and $A^n_{1/2} = +0.040 \pm 0.010$ GeV$^{-1/2}$, consistent with M1 transitions in ratio $-3:2$.
- Total widths of $P_{11}(1710)$ and $P_{13}(1720)$ are approximately 100 MeV, compared to $\Gamma_T \sim 350$ MeV for $P_{11}(1440)$.
- The $P_{13}(1720)$ branching ratio to $N\pi\pi$ is approximately 70%.
- An upper limit of $B.R. < 1\%$ is set by PDG for $D_{15}(1675) \to K\Lambda$.
- The $F_{17}(1990)$ is identified as the only $F_{17}$ state at $N=2$.

## Context
This work extends the quark model analysis of baryon resonances by Close (1990s) and the QCD mixing formalism of Isgur and Karl (1978, 1979), who computed the ${\bf 70}$ admixture in the nucleon. The Λ selection rule complements the Moorhouse selection rule (1966) for $\gamma p$ transitions and the Hey rule (1970s) for $\Lambda^*$ decays. The paper addresses the debate between three-quark dynamics and quark-diquark models, where diquark models (Wilcox, 2004; Jaffe and Wilczek, 2003) forbid $[{\bf 20}, L^P]$ states. The results enable experimental searches at Jefferson Laboratory and BEPC for ${\bf 20}$-plet resonances through $\gamma N \to K\Lambda$, $K^*\Lambda$, $K\Sigma$, $K^*\Sigma$, and $J/\psi \to \bar{p}N^*$ reactions, and motivate partial wave analyses to include $N^*({\bf 70})\pi$ intermediate states.
