# Effective Helium Burning Rates and the Production of the Neutrino Nuclei

> Effective values for the key helium burning reaction rates, triple-alpha and 12C(alpha,gamma)16O, are obtained by adjusting their strengths so as to obtain the best match with the solar abundance pattern of isotopes uniquely or predominately made in core collapse supernovae. These effective rates are then used to determine the production of the neutrino isotopes. The use of effective rates considerably reduces the uncertainties in the production factors arising from uncertainties in the helium burning rates, and improves our ability to use the production of 11B to constrain the neutrino emission from supernovae.

## Key claim
Effective reaction rates (ERR) for the triple-alpha and 12C(alpha,gamma)16O reactions are obtained by fitting to the solar abundance pattern of intermediate-mass and s-only isotopes, revealing a correlated band rather than individually constrained rates; this reduces uncertainties in neutrino isotope production to typically ±10% or less, compared to much larger spreads from uncorrelated rate uncertainties.

## Method
The authors used the Kepler stellar evolution code to model 12 stars (12-30 solar masses) from hydrogen burning to core collapse, with a piston-driven explosion yielding 1.2 × 10^51 erg kinetic energy. They computed 176 rate pairs covering ±2σ for both the triple-alpha and 12C(alpha,gamma)16O rates, totaling 2112 simulations, then averaged over a Salpeter initial mass function and filtered models with compactness factor ξ2.5 < 0.25. The effective rates were determined by minimizing the standard deviation of IMF-averaged production factors for intermediate-mass isotopes (16O, 18O, 20Ne, 23Na, 24Mg, 27Al, 28Si, 32S, 36Ar, 40Ca) and s-only isotopes (70Ge, 76Se, 80Kr, 82Kr, 86Sr, 87Sr), giving equal weight to both groups.

## Key numbers
- The best-fit ERR line follows the relation r_alpha,gamma = r_3alpha + 0.35, with a range of 35% in the allowed values
- Production factors for neutrino isotopes (7Li, 11B, 19F, 138La, 180Ta) vary by typically ±10% or less along the entire ERR line
- For larger r_3alpha, the average s-only production factor normalized to 16O decreases from 0.85 to a plateau of 0.55 for r_3alpha ≥ 1.0
- The explosion energy used was 1.2 × 10^51 erg
- Neutrino spectra used Fermi-Dirac distributions with temperatures of 4 MeV (nu_e), 5 MeV (anti-nu_e), and 6 MeV (nu_x, where x = mu, tau)
- Moving the ERR line by changing r_alpha,gamma by -0.1 changed neutrino isotope production by between 5% and 12%

## Context
This work extends earlier efforts by Weaver and Woosley (1993) and Boyes (2001, unpublished) who varied only the 12C(alpha,gamma)16O rate, and by Tur et al. (2007) who improved the procedure with a larger set of stars and explosive nucleosynthesis. The key advance is treating both helium burning rates simultaneously as effective parameters, analogous to effective interactions in nuclear shell model calculations. The results enable using 11B abundance to constrain average supernova neutrino emission, as proposed by Austin et al. (2011), by removing the dominant uncertainty from helium burning rates.
