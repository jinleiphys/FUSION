# Constraints on the nuclear equation of state and the neutron star structure from crustal torsional oscillations

> We systematically examine torsional shear oscillations of neutron star crusts by newly taking into account the possible presence of the phase of cylindrical nuclei. In this study, we neglect an effect of magnetic fields, under which the shear oscillations can be damped by the magnetic interaction. First, by identifying the low frequency quasi-periodic oscillations (QPOs) observed in the soft-gamma repeaters (SGRs) as the fundamental torsional oscillations, we constrain the slope parameter of the nuclear symmetry energy, $L$, for reasonable values of the star's mass $M$ and radius $R$. Meanwhile, we find that the 1st overtone of torsional oscillations obtained for given $M$ and $R$ can be expressed well as a function of a new parameter $ς\equiv (K_0^4 L^5)^{1/9}$, where $K_0$ is the incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter. Assuming that the lowest of the QPO frequencies above 500 Hz observed in SGR 1806-20 comes from the 1st overtone, we can constrain the value of $ς$. Then, for each neutron star model, such a value of $L$ as can be obtained from the observed low frequency QPOs translates to the optimal value of $K_0$ via the above constraint on $ς$. Finally, its consistency with allowed values of $K_0$ from empirical giant monopole resonances leads to neutron star models with relatively low mass and large radius, which are qualitatively similar to the prediction in earlier investigations. This result suggests that $L\simeq 58-73$ MeV, even when uncertainties in the neutron superfluid density inside the phase of cylindrical nuclei are allowed for.

## Key claim

The authors constrain the slope parameter of the nuclear symmetry energy, \(L\), to approximately 58-73 MeV by identifying observed quasi-periodic oscillations (QPOs) from soft-gamma repeaters as crustal torsional oscillations, but this result depends on neglecting magnetic field effects and on assuming that the lowest QPO frequency above 500 Hz in SGR 1806-20 corresponds to the first overtone.

## Method

The authors constructed equilibrium neutron star crust models using the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations with a phenomenological equation of state (OI-EOS) that includes the phase of cylindrical nuclei (pasta phase). They calculated the shear modulus for spherical and cylindrical nuclear phases, then solved the relativistic perturbation equation for torsional shear oscillations to obtain eigenfrequencies for fundamental and first overtone modes. The calculated frequencies were compared with observed QPO frequencies from SGR 1806-20 and SGR 1900+14 to constrain the nuclear matter parameters \(L\) and \(K_0\).

## Key numbers

* Lower bound on the slope parameter \(L \gsim 51.9\) MeV for \(N_s/N_d=0\) and \(L \gsim 56.1\) MeV for \(N_s/N_d=1\) in the phase of cylindrical nuclei, derived from requiring the 18 Hz QPO in SGR 1806-20 to be higher than the \(\ell=2\) fundamental torsional frequency for stellar models with \(M=1.4-1.8 M_\odot\) and \(R=10-14\) km.
* Final suggested range \(L \simeq 58-73\) MeV, obtained after combining constraints from low-frequency QPOs, the first overtone constraint via the parameter \(\varsigma \equiv (K_0^4 L^5)^{1/9}\), and consistency with empirical giant monopole resonance data.
* Observed QPO frequencies used: 18, 26, 29, 92.5, 150, 626.5, and 1837 Hz in SGR 1806-20; 28, 54, 84, and 155 Hz in SGR 1900+14; a 57 Hz QPO from less energetic bursts in SGR 1806-20; and possible 9.2 Hz (SGR 1806-20) and 7.7 Hz (SGR 1900+14) QPOs from Bayesian analysis.

## Context

This work extends earlier investigations by Sotani, Iida, and Oyamatsu (2012, 2013a, 2013b, 2017a) that constrained the nuclear equation of state using crustal torsional oscillations but neglected the phase of cylindrical nuclei. The inclusion of cylindrical nuclei is motivated by the work of Lorenz, Ravenhall, and Pethick (1993) and Oyamatsu (1993) on pasta phases, and by the finding of de Groot, Pethick, and Pines (1993) and Pethick and Potekhin (1998) that slab-like nuclei behave as a fluid. The results enable future studies to incorporate magnetic field effects and more realistic neutron superfluid entrainment, as discussed by Chamel (2005, 2012) and Watanabe and Pethick (2017), to refine constraints on the neutron star equation of state.
