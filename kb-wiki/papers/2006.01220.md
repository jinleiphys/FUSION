# Effects of the QCD phase transition on hadronic observables in relativistic hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion reactions in the FAIR/NICA energy regime

> We investigate hadronic particle spectra and flow characteristics of heavy-ion reactions in the FAIR/NICA energy range of $1\ A{\rm GeV} \le E_{\rm lab} \le 10\ A{\rm GeV}$ within a relativistic ideal hydrodynamic one-fluid approach. The particlization is realized by sampling the Cooper-Frye distribution for a grand canonical hadron gas on a hypersurface of constant energy density. Results of the hydrodynamic calculations for different underlying equations of state are presented and compared with experimental data and microscopic transport simulations. The sensitivity of the approach to physical model inputs concerning the initial state and the particlization is studied.

# Effects of the QCD phase transition on hadronic observables in relativistic hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion reactions in the FAIR/NICA energy regime

## Key claim

The relativistic one-fluid hydrodynamic approach with particlization can describe hadronic observables in the FAIR/NICA energy range (1 to 10 AGeV), but the sensitivity to the equation of state is much stronger for flow harmonics than for inclusive particle spectra; the chiral model equation of state with a QGP phase transition provides the best description of elliptic flow data, while the hadron gas equation of state yields the best agreement with transverse mass spectra at higher beam energies (6 to 10 AGeV).

## Method

The authors performed ideal relativistic one-fluid hydrodynamic simulations of central and mid-central Au+Au collisions using the SHASTA algorithm, with initialization from either averaged nuclear profiles or Monte Carlo event-by-event fluctuating nucleon distributions. Particlization was implemented via Cooper-Frye sampling on a hypersurface of constant energy density (set to nuclear ground state energy density) into a grand canonical hadron gas, followed by final-state interactions using the UrQMD cascade. Three different equations of state were compared: a hadron gas of known resonances, a MIT bag model with first-order phase transition, and a chiral model with a crossover transition to deconfined matter.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy range studied: 1 AGeV ≤ E_lab ≤ 10 AGeV
- Cell size for hydrodynamic grid: (0.2 fm)^3
- Width of Lorentz-contracted Gaussians for nucleon initialization: σ = 1 fm
- Particlization energy density: ε_fr = ε_0 (nuclear ground state energy density)
- Baryon number conservation level for hadron gas EoS with averaged initial state: 1% level
- Baryon number conservation level with initial state fluctuations: 5% level
- Sensitivity test range for particlization energy density: ε_fr = 0.75 ε_0 to 1.25 ε_0
- Impact parameter for central collisions: b = 2 fm
- Impact parameter for mid-central flow studies: b = 7 fm
- Bag model EoS shows minimum in directed flow at around 6 AGeV, coinciding with results of Rischke (1995)
- Chiral model EoS shows maximum in directed flow at around 4 AGeV

## Context

This work extends the one-fluid hydrodynamic approach of Rischke (1995) by incorporating modern particlization schemes and comparing multiple equations of state, including a chiral model from Steinheimer (2011) and a bag model from Rischke (1995). It contrasts with multi-fluid and hybrid approaches such as those of Ivanov (2005) and Batyuk (2016), which rely on separation of baryon currents that may not be valid at these low energies. The results enable systematic predictions for the upcoming FAIR and NICA experimental programs and demonstrate that fluctuating initial conditions are essential for realistic flow descriptions, consistent with findings of Petersen (2010).
