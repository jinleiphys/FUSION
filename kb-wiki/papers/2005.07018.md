# $Z^0$ boson associated b-jet production in high-energy nuclear collisions

> The production of vector boson tagged heavy quark jets potentially provides new tools to probe the jet quenching effect. In this paper, we present the first theoretical study on the angular correlations ($Δϕ_{bZ}$), transverse momentum imbalance ($x_{bZ}$), and nuclear modification factor ($I_{AA}$) of $Z^0$ boson tagged b-jets in heavy-ion collisions, which was performed using a Monte Carlo transport model. We find that the medium modification of the $Δϕ_{bZ}$ for $Z^0\,+\,$b-jet has a weaker dependence on $Δϕ_{bZ}$ than that for $Z^0\,+\,$jet, and the modification patterns are sensitive to the initial jet $p_T$ distribution. Additionally, with the high purity of the quark jet in $Z^0\,+\,$(b-)jet production, we calculate the momentum imbalance $x_{bZ}$ and the nuclear modification factor $I_{AA}$ of $Z^0\,+\,$b-jet in Pb+Pb collisions. We observe a smaller $Δ\left\langle x_{jZ} \right\rangle$ and larger $I_{AA}$ of $Z^0\,+\,$b-jet in Pb+Pb collisions relative to those of $Z^0\,+\,$jet, which may be an indication of the mass effect of jet quenching and can be tested in future measurements.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first theoretical study of Z0 boson tagged b-jets in heavy-ion collisions, finding that the medium modification of the azimuthal angular correlation for Z0+b-jet has a weaker dependence on the azimuthal angle than for Z0+jet, and that the modification patterns are sensitive to the initial jet transverse momentum distribution rather than the azimuthal angle itself.

## Method

The authors used the SHERPA event generator with next-to-leading order (NLO) plus parton shower to obtain initial Z0+b-jet production in p+p collisions, then simulated in-medium jet evolution in Pb+Pb collisions using a Monte Carlo transport model. Heavy quarks were propagated via modified Langevin equations with both collisional and radiative energy loss, while light partons used a Hard-Thermal-Loop approximation for collisional energy loss and Higher-Twist formalism for medium-induced gluon radiation, with the bulk medium evolution provided by the VISHNU hydrodynamic code.

## Key numbers

- Jet transport coefficient q0 = 1.2 GeV2/fm at the LHC energy, determined via global extraction of single hadron production in Pb+Pb collisions
- Spatial diffusion coefficient Ds(2πT) ≈ 4, extracted via χ2 fitting to D meson RAA data, consistent with lattice QCD results of Ds(2πT) = 3.7–7
- QCD transition temperature Tc = 165 MeV used as cutoff for medium evolution
- Initial time τ0 = 0.6 fm/c for the bulk medium evolution
- Strong coupling constant fixed at αs = 0.3 in the calculations
- Jet reconstruction used anti-kT algorithm with cone size R = 0.5 for Z0+b-jet studies, and R = 0.3 for comparison with CMS Z0+jet data
- Kinematic cuts: jet pT > 30 GeV, |ηjet| < 2.4, Z0 boson pT > 60 GeV (for CMS comparison), lepton pT > 20 GeV, dilepton invariant mass 70 < Mll < 111 GeV
- Average b-jet transverse momentum ⟨pT⟩ as a function of ΔφbZ was approximately constant at 55 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of vector boson tagged jets in heavy-ion collisions (such as those by Neufeld (2010), Wang (1996), and Dai (2012)) to the specific case of b-jets associated with Z0 bosons, addressing the mass effect of jet quenching between light-quark jets and massive bottom jets. The study builds on the authors' prior framework for heavy-flavor jet production (Dai (2018), Wang (2019, 2020, 2021)) and enables future experimental tests at the LHC by ATLAS and CMS to directly probe the dead-cone effect and mass dependence of parton energy loss in quark-gluon plasma.
