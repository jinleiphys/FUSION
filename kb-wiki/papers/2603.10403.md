# Quantifying uncertainty in physics-based predictions of rare-isotope production cross sections via Bayesian-inspired model averaging across nuclear mass tables

> Accurate prediction of fragmentation cross sections is essential for rare-isotope beam production, planning new-isotope searches, and designing experiments to study the most exotic regions of the nuclear chart. However, existing reaction models and phenomenological cross-section parameterizations often exhibit significant deviations over broad regions of mass and charge. In this work, a Bayesian-inspired model-averaging framework is developed to combine abrasion--ablation (AA) calculations based on multiple nuclear mass tables into a single statistically weighted estimate. For the calibrated systems, the model weights are assigned empirically according to the relative quality of fit to measured cross sections, thereby reducing systematic model bias while preserving the underlying physics content of the AA description. The weights are constrained using proton-rich fragmentation data for the $^{78}$Kr and $^{124}$Xe projectiles. The resulting parameter trends are then propagated to the $^{92}$Mo and $^{144}$Sm systems through a controlled scaling procedure. In the present implementation, the excitation-energy prescription is fixed, while the averaging is performed across nuclear-mass inputs; the framework provides both weighted cross sections and associated uncertainty estimates. Applied to proton-rich fragmentation, the present approach provides a practical basis for interpolation and limited extrapolation in regions relevant to rare-isotope production. The resulting predictions are used to assess the production of very proton-rich nuclei, and candidate new isotopes are discussed.

# Quantifying uncertainty in physics-based predictions of rare-isotope production cross sections via Bayesian-inspired model averaging across nuclear mass tables

## Key claim

The Bayesian-inspired model-averaging framework combines abrasion-ablation calculations based on multiple nuclear mass tables into a single statistically weighted estimate, but only for the specific case where the excitation-energy prescription is fixed and the averaging is performed exclusively across nuclear-mass inputs. The weights are assigned empirically according to the relative quality of fit to measured cross sections, not through a full Bayesian posterior probability construction.

## Method

The author performed abrasion-ablation (AA) calculations using the LISE++ implementation for projectile fragmentation of 78Kr and 124Xe on 9Be targets, fitting six AA parameters (EDM parameters k1, k2, kNZ; evaporation odd-even parameter delta_oe; global scaling factor S_sigma; effective Coulomb-barrier parameter Delta R) for each of 12 nuclear mass models. Model weights were assigned inversely proportional to the objective function value J for each mass model, then normalized. The excitation-energy scale was propagated to 92Mo and 144Sm projectiles using a two-point calibration of a separable size-isospin scaling law, with model weights interpolated between the two calibrated systems in the (A,I) plane.

## Key numbers

- For 78Kr+9Be, the KTUY mass model gave the lowest objective-function value J=1.10 among the 12 mass models considered
- For 124Xe+9Be, the HFB-27 mass model gave the lowest J value among the models considered
- Weighted-average value of k1=13.1 MeV, close to values obtained previously with Gaussian-shape excitation-energy models
- Weighted-average value of S_sigma approximately 1.1 for 78Kr, indicating only a small residual normalization offset
- For 78Kr+9Be, 64% of BMA-inspired predictions were within a factor of 2 of measured cross sections; geometric-mean ratio sigma_BMA/sigma_exp = 1.9
- For 124Xe+9Be, 56% of BMA-inspired predictions were within a factor of 2; geometric-mean ratio sigma_BMA/sigma_exp = 0.58
- For 78Kr+9Be, 61% of predictions were within 1 sigma and 96% within 2 sigma
- For 124Xe+9Be, 58% of predictions were within 1 sigma and 76% within 2 sigma
- Delta R remained close to the default value of 6 fm, extracted by Benlliure et al. (1998) from fragmentation-corridor systematics
- Default delta_oe=12 MeV originates from the Ignatyuk pairing-gap parametrization adopted in Gaimard and Schmidt (1991)

## Context

This work extends previous AA-based studies of 78Kr fragmentation by Kubeila et al. (2021) and Suzuki et al. (2025), which used a single mass table and Gaussian-shaped excitation-energy distributions, by combining predictions from 12 different nuclear mass models into a weighted average. The framework enables interpolation and limited extrapolation to unmeasured proton-rich nuclei, with predictions used to assess production of very proton-rich nuclei and candidate new isotopes. The approach contrasts with purely phenomenological parameterizations such as EPAX3 (Summerer and Blank, 2000) and mass-based systematics (Delta BE and Qg methods), which have limited predictive power when extrapolated into regions with little or no data.
