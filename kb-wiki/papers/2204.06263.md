# Two particles interacting via a contact interaction on $S^2$

> We consider two particles interacting via a contact interaction that are constrained to a sphere, or $S^2$. We determine their spectrum to arbitrary precision and for arbitrary angular momentum. We show how the non-inertial frame leads to non-trivial solutions for different angular momenta. Our results represent an extension of the finite-volume Lüscher formulas but now to a non-trivial geometry. We apply our results to predict the spectrum of select two-nucleon halo nuclei and compare with experimental results.

## Key claim

The paper derives a quantization condition (Lüscher formula) for two particles interacting via a contact interaction on the surface of a sphere (S²), showing that the non-inertial geometry prevents separation of relative and center-of-mass coordinates, leading to distinct solutions for each total angular momentum L, a feature absent in flat or harmonic geometries.

## Method

The authors solve the two-body Schrödinger equation on S² by projecting the integral equation onto states of good total angular momentum L, using a contact interaction regularized with a momentum cutoff Λ. The matrix elements of the interaction are expressed in terms of Wigner 3‑j symbols, and the quantization condition is obtained by summing over angular momentum quantum numbers and taking the Λ → ∞ limit. Closed-form analytic expressions for the quantization condition are derived for L = 0, 1, and 2 using digamma functions.

## Key numbers

- For L = 0, the quantization condition is log(a/R) = −½ [ψ(½(1−√(2x+1))) + ψ(½(1+√(2x+1)))], where x = 2mER².
- For L = 1, the quantization condition is log(a/R) = −½ [ψ(1−√(x/2)) + ψ(1+√(x/2))].
- The zeros x₀ of the quantization curves at a/R = 1 (unitary limit) are given to 14 decimal places: for L = 0, x₀ = −2.69519416311127 ± 0.00000000000001; for L = 1, x₀ = −1.56227783993538 ± 0.00000000000001; for L = 2, x₀ = 0.57785048142503 ± 0.00000000000001.
- For the ⁶He halo nucleus, the spin-singlet scattering length extracted is \tilde a₀ = −5.58 ± 0.06.

## Context

This work extends the finite-volume Lüscher quantization formulas (Lüscher 1986, 1991) from flat geometries (cubic boxes, tori) to the curved geometry of S². It contrasts with the separable center-of-mass solutions available for the 2‑D harmonic oscillator (Busch 1998) and the S¹×S¹ torus (Beane 2010), where non-zero center-of-mass motion simply shifts the s‑wave curve. The results enable extraction of two-nucleon interaction parameters from halo nuclei such as ⁶He and ¹¹Li, treating the core as an infinitely massive point at the sphere’s center and the halo nucleons as interacting via a contact force on the sphere’s surface.
