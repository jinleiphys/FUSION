# Initial State Quantum Fluctuations in the Little Bang

> We review recent developments in the ab-initio theoretical description of the initial state in heavy-ion collisions. We emphasize the importance of fluctuations, both for the phenomenological description of experimental data from the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and the Large Hadron Collider (LHC), and the theoretical understanding of the non-equilibrium early time dynamics and thermalization of the medium.

## Key claim

The paper reviews how initial-state quantum fluctuations in the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective theory are essential for describing experimental data from RHIC and LHC heavy-ion collisions, but notes that the leading-order (LO) classical Yang-Mills description lacks a mechanism for thermalization and isotropization of the stress tensor, requiring higher-order quantum fluctuations to achieve equilibration.

## Method

The authors review the CGC framework where gluon fields at high energy are treated as classical Yang-Mills fields coupled to static color sources, with the McLerran-Venugopalan model providing a Gaussian distribution for the color charge density. The IP-Glasma model implements this by sampling nucleon positions from Woods-Saxon distributions, constraining the color charge density via the IP-Sat dipole cross section fitted to HERA deep inelastic scattering data, and solving the classical Yang-Mills equations on a lattice to obtain the gluon multiplicity and energy-momentum tensor. The resulting initial conditions are then matched to viscous relativistic hydrodynamics to compute flow harmonics and compare to experimental data.

## Key numbers

* Saturation momentum $Q_s(x)$ increases with energy, with the transverse correlation length of gluon fields being approximately $1/Q_s$; for gold nuclei at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV and $\sqrt{s}=5$ TeV, the correlator $(1/N_c) {\rm Re} [{\rm Tr}(V^\dag(0,0) V(x,y))]$ shows the fluctuation scale is smaller at the higher energy.
* The hydrodynamical evolution should start at times $\tau \lesssim 1$ fm/c after the collision.
* The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio $\eta/s$ is close to the conjectured strong coupling limit of $\eta/s=1/4\pi$ from the AdS/CFT correspondence.
* In the IP-Glasma model, switching from Yang-Mills dynamics to hydrodynamics occurs at a time of $0.2$ fm/c.
* Flow harmonics $v_n$ ($n=2,\dots,5$) are computed for Pb+Pb collisions at LHC with center-of-mass energy $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV and compared to ATLAS and ALICE data.
* For U+U collisions at $\sqrt{s}=193$ GeV in 0-1% central events, the IP-Glasma model predicts a weaker anti-correlation between $v_2$ and multiplicity than Glauber models, consistent with STAR data.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on ab-initio descriptions of the initial state in heavy-ion collisions, building on the CGC framework developed by McLerran and Venugopalan (1994) and the classical Yang-Mills simulations by Krasnitz, Nara, and Lappi (1998-2006). The IP-Glasma model (Schenke, 2012) improves on earlier approaches by using the IP-Sat dipole model constrained by HERA data to determine the color charge density. The paper contrasts with Glauber-based models that predict different multiplicity-flow correlations, as demonstrated in U+U collisions. The results enable further constraints on initial state fluctuations and transport properties of the quark-gluon plasma, and highlight the need for including higher-order quantum fluctuations to understand thermalization, which remains an open theoretical challenge.
