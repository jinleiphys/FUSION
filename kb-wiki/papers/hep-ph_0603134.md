# On the Strangeness -1 S-wave Meson-Baryon Scattering

> We consider meson-baryon interactions in S-wave with strangeness -1. This is a non-perturbative sector populated by plenty of resonances interacting in several two-body coupled channels.We study this sector combining a large set of experimental data. The recent experiments are remarkably accurate demanding a sound theoretical description to account for all the data. We employ unitary chiral perturbation theory up to and including \cal{O}(p^2) to accomplish this aim. The spectroscopy of our solutions is studied within this approach, discussing the rise from the pole content of the two Λ(1405) resonances and of the Λ(1670), Λ(1800), Σ(1480), Σ(1620) and Σ(1750). We finally argue about our preferred solution.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that unitary chiral perturbation theory (UCHPT) up to and including \({\cal O}(p^2)\) can simultaneously describe a large set of strangeness -1 S-wave meson-baryon scattering data, including the DEAR measurement of kaonic hydrogen, but only for a specific family of fits (the \(A_4^+\) type); other fits (\(B_4^+\) and \({\cal O}(p)\)) that reproduce the scattering data disagree with the DEAR result.

## Method

The author employs unitary chiral perturbation theory (UCHPT) up to and including next-to-leading order (\({\cal O}(p^2)\)) to calculate S-wave meson-baryon scattering amplitudes in ten coupled channels with strangeness -1. The chiral amplitudes from the \({\cal O}(p)\) and \({\cal O}(p^2)\) Lagrangians are used as input for a coupled-channel dispersion relation that resums unitarity bubbles, with the interaction kernel \({\cal T}(W)\) fixed by matching to the baryon CHPT amplitudes order by order. The free parameters (low-energy constants \(b_i\) and subtraction constants \(a_i\)) are fitted to a combined dataset including \(K^-p\) cross sections, threshold ratios, \(\pi\Sigma\) event distributions, and the DEAR kaonic hydrogen measurement, with additional constraints from \(\pi N\) observables.

## Key numbers

* The DEAR measurement of the kaonic hydrogen 1s level shift is \(\Delta E = 193 \pm 37 \text{ (stat)} \pm 6 \text{ (syst) eV}\) and width \(\Gamma = 249 \pm 111 \text{ (stat)} \pm 39 \text{ (syst) eV}\).
* The threshold ratio \(\gamma = \sigma(K^-p \rightarrow \pi^+\Sigma^-) / \sigma(K^-p \rightarrow \pi^-\Sigma^+) = 2.36 \pm 0.04\).
* The threshold ratio \(R_c = \sigma(K^-p \rightarrow \text{charged particles}) / \sigma(K^-p \rightarrow \text{all}) = 0.664 \pm 0.011\).
* The threshold ratio \(R_n = \sigma(K^-p \rightarrow \pi^0\Lambda) / \sigma(K^-p \rightarrow \text{all neutral states}) = 0.189 \pm 0.015\).
* For the preferred fit \(A_4^+\), the calculated kaonic hydrogen shift is \(\Delta E = 201\) eV and width \(\Gamma = 338\) eV, while for fit \(B_4^+\) they are \(\Delta E = 403\) eV and \(\Gamma = 477\) eV.
* The \(K^-p\) scattering length from fit \(A_4^+\) is \(a_{K^-p} = -0.51 + i\,0.42\) fm.
* The isospin I=0 scattering length from fit \(A_4^+\) is \(a_0 = -1.23 + i\,0.45\) fm, and the I=1 scattering length is \(a_1 = 0.98 + i\,0.35\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Oller (2006, ref. [opv]) by including additional experimental data from the Crystal-Ball Collaboration for the reactions \(K^-p \rightarrow \eta \Lambda\) (Nefkens, 2005) and \(K^-p \rightarrow \pi^0\pi^0\Sigma^0\) (Prakhov, 2004), which were not considered in previous UCHPT fits. The paper addresses the possible inconsistency between the DEAR measurement of kaonic hydrogen and \(K^-p\) scattering data that was pointed out by Meissner (2004) and Borasoy (2005), showing that a family of fits (\(A_4^+\)) can simultaneously satisfy both. The results enable improved predictions for kaonic atom spectroscopy, kaon condensation in neutron stars, and the pole content of resonances such as the two \(\Lambda(1405)\) states, \(\Lambda(1670)\), \(\Lambda(1800)\), \(\Sigma(1480)\), \(\Sigma(1620)\), and \(\Sigma(1750)\).
