# Short-range correlations and their implications for isospin-dependent modification of nuclear quark distributions

> The past decade has provided a much clearer picture of the structure of high-momentum components in nucleons, associated with hard, short-distance interactions between pairs of nucleons. Recent Jefferson Lab data on light nuclei suggest a connection between these so-called 'short-range correlations' and the modification of the quark structure of nucleons in the nuclear environment. In light of this discovery that the detailed nuclear structure is important in describing the nuclear quark distributions, we examine the potential impact of the isospin-dependent structure of nuclei to see at what level this might yield flavor-dependent effects in nuclear quark distributions.

## Key claim

The observed correlation between the EMC effect and short-range correlations (SRCs) implies that the EMC effect is likely flavor-dependent in non-isoscalar nuclei, but the exact size and nature of this flavor dependence depends on whether the EMC effect is driven by local-density effects (all NN pairs contribute) or by high-momentum/off-shell effects (dominated by np pairs), and existing data cannot yet distinguish between these scenarios.

## Method

The author calculates four scaling assumptions for the A-dependence of the EMC effect, high-momentum fraction (nucleons above 300 MeV/c), average kinetic energy, average density, and overlap probability (probability of a nucleon being within 1 fm of another), using Quantum Monte Carlo calculations from Wiringa (2014) for all particle-stable light nuclei. The scaling parameter is determined independently for protons and neutrons, then combined with cross-section and N/Z weighting to predict the unpolarized EMC effect and the fractional difference between proton and neutron EMC effects as a function of neutron excess.

## Key numbers

- SRCs are dominated at the ~90% level by neutron-proton pairs, based on measurements from Shneor (2007), Subedi (2008), Korover (2014), and Hen (2014).
- The high-momentum fraction scaling uses a cutoff of 300 MeV/c for the nucleon momentum distribution.
- The overlap probability scaling uses a distance threshold of 1 fm for two nucleons to be considered overlapping.
- The largest neutron excess shown in the isospin-dependence figure corresponds to nuclei with |(N-Z)/A| = 1/3 for ³H and ³He.

## Context

This work extends the observed correlation between the EMC effect and SRCs first noted by Weinstein (2011) and examined in detail by Arrington (2012) and Hen (2013), following precise measurements of the EMC effect in light nuclei by Seely (2009) and SRC measurements by Fomin (2012) that showed ⁹Be deviates from simple density dependence due to its cluster structure. The paper enables future experimental tests of flavor-dependent EMC effects through approved Jefferson Lab measurements of unpolarized EMC in light nuclei (including ⁴⁰Ca vs. ⁴⁸Ca) and through parity-violating deep-inelastic scattering from non-isoscalar nuclei as proposed by Cloet (2012), which provides the cleanest way to isolate isospin-dependent modifications of nuclear parton distributions.
