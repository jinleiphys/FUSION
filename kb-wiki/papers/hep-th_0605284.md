# Skyrmions and the alpha-particle model of nuclei

> We compute new solutions of the Skyrme model with massive pions. Concentrating on baryon numbers which are a multiple of four, we find low energy Skyrmion solutions which are composed of charge four sub-units, as in the alpha-particle model of nuclei. We summarize our current understanding of these solutions, and discuss their relationship to configurations in the alpha-particle model.

# Skyrmions and the alpha-particle model of nuclei

## Key claim

For baryon numbers that are multiples of four, the Skyrme model with massive pions (pion mass parameter m=1) admits low-energy Skyrmion solutions composed of charge-four sub-units that resemble alpha-particle clusters, but the energy differences between competing cluster arrangements are extremely small (often less than 0.1% of total energy), making it difficult to identify which configuration is the true global minimum.

## Method

The authors numerically relaxed field configurations to static solutions of the Skyrme model with pion mass parameter m=1, using grids containing 100^3 points with lattice spacing Δx=0.1 (and some checks with 200^3 points and Δx=0.05). Initial configurations were constructed using the product ansatz (placing B=4 cubic Skyrmions in various geometric arrangements) and the double rational map ansatz, then relaxed while preserving or breaking specified symmetries to find local energy minima and saddle points.

## Key numbers

- Energy per baryon of the B=4 cube at m=1: E/B = 1.307 (on 100^3 grid with Δx=0.1)
- For B=8 at m=1: both the D_{6d} and D_{4h} solutions have E/B = 1.294 (100^3 grid); on 200^3 grid, E/B = 1.2970 and 1.2967 respectively
- B=12 triangular C_3 symmetric solution: E/B = 1.288
- B=12 three-cubes-in-a-line solution: E/B = 1.285
- B=16 tetrahedral solution (saddle point): E/B = 1.288
- B=16 bent square (D_{2d}) solution: E/B = 1.284
- B=16 flat square solution: E/B = 1.293
- B=20 triangular bipyramid relaxation: E/B = 1.283
- B=24 solution (bent square plus B=8): E/B = 1.282
- B=28 solution (cubic B=32 with one cube removed): E/B = 1.279
- Numerical errors estimated at up to 0.5% for energy computations

## Context

This work extends the recent discovery by Battye and Sutcliffe (2005-2006) that for baryon numbers B ≥ 8, the hollow polyhedral Skyrmion solutions found at zero pion mass become unstable when a physically reasonable pion mass is introduced, and are replaced by cluster-like solutions. The paper systematically searches for B=4 sub-unit arrangements for B = 8, 12, 16, 20, 24, 28, and 32, making direct contact with the alpha-particle model of nuclei developed by Bethe and others in the 1930s. The results enable future work on quantizing these classical solutions to obtain spin/parity assignments for comparison with real nuclei such as ^8Be, ^12C, and ^16O, and suggest that the Skyrme model may reproduce alpha-cluster structures seen in nuclear physics.
