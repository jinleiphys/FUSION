# Collective motion in quantum diffusive environment

> The general problem of dissipation in macroscopic large-amplitude collective motion and its relation to energy diffusion of intrinsic degrees of freedom of a nucleus is studied. By applying the cranking approach to the nuclear many-body system, a set of coupled dynamical equations for the collective classical variable and the quantum mechanical occupancies of the intrinsic nuclear states is derived. Different dynamical regimes of the intrinsic nuclear motion and its consequences on time properties of collective dissipation are discussed.

# Collective motion in quantum diffusive environment

## Key claim

The paper derives a non-Markovian diffusion equation for the occupancies of intrinsic nuclear states during large-amplitude collective motion, showing that the dissipation of collective energy arises from energy diffusion in the space of highly excited many-body states, but only when the nuclear level density increases with energy (a constant level density would yield no collective damping).

## Method

The authors apply the cranking approach to a nuclear many-body system, introducing a single classical collective variable q(t) for the slow collective motion while treating the intrinsic nucleonic degrees of freedom quantum-mechanically via the Liouville equation for the density matrix. Using Zwanzig's projection method and ensemble averaging over randomly distributed energy spacings and squared off-diagonal matrix elements (modeled via random matrix theory with GOE or GUE statistics), they derive a non-Markovian diffusion equation for the occupancy of nuclear states. The resulting coupled equations are then applied to symmetric fission of 236U, using an inverted parabolic potential for the descent from saddle to scission.

## Key numbers

- The fission barrier height for 236U is taken as 8 MeV, with an inverted parabolic potential characterized by ℏω_f = 1.16 MeV
- The initial (saddle-point) deformation is q_0 = 1.6, measured in units of the nuclear radius R_0 = r_0 A^(1/3)
- The scission point is defined by E_pot(q_0) - E_pot(q_sc) = 20 MeV
- The initial collective kinetic energy is taken as 1 MeV
- The initial nuclear temperature is T = 2 MeV
- The strength σ^2 of the ensemble averaged squared matrix elements is given by σ^2 = (3 m^2 ω_0^3 A R_0^4)/(560π ℏ) with ℏω_0 = 41/A^(1/3) MeV
- The saddle-to-scission time t_sc lies in the range (6-12)×10^(-21) s, based on previous work by the authors
- The estimated width Γ of the Lorentzian distribution of ensemble averaged squared matrix elements lies in the interval 10 MeV ≤ Γ ≤ 20 MeV
- The pre-scission kinetic energy E_ps lies in the range 1 MeV ≤ E_ps ≤ 3 MeV for the estimated Γ interval

## Context

This work extends the linear response theory (LRS1, LRS2) and time-dependent shell-model theory (TDSMT) approaches to nuclear collective dissipation by providing a self-consistent description of both collective and intrinsic nucleonic degrees of freedom without assuming adiabaticity a priori. The authors apply their formalism to nuclear fission of 236U, building on the Kramers (1940) and Nix (1969) treatments of fission dynamics with an inverted parabolic potential, and their results for saddle-to-scission times are consistent with previous estimations by the same group (KRS). The estimated width Γ = 10-20 MeV is consistent with earlier work by Brink (1972) and Zelevinsky (1996).
