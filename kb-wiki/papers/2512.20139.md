# Challenge of nuclear transmutation in heavy-ion colliders

> We investigate the production of secondary nuclei in the hadronic fragmentation and electromagnetic dissociation (EMD) of $^{20}$Ne beams at the LHC and $^{124}$Xe beams at NICA. For light nuclei at LHC energies, our calculations show that hadronic interactions are the dominant channel for nuclear transmutation. This contrasts with the previously established dominance of EMD in $^{208}$Pb-$^{208}$Pb collisions. For $^{20}$Ne-$^{20}$Ne collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.36$ TeV, we provide calculated cross sections and momentum distributions of produced nuclei such as $^4$He, $^{12}$C, $^{14}$N, and $^{16}$O. These results are essential for assessing potential contamination of the $^{20}$Ne-$^{20}$Ne event sample by collisions involving other nuclear species. Secondary nuclei will be also produced in the EMD of $^{124}$Xe beams at NICA, but they will not contaminate $^{124}$Xe-$^{124}$Xe data.

## Key claim

For light nuclei at LHC energies, hadronic interactions are the dominant channel for nuclear transmutation, in contrast to the previously established dominance of electromagnetic dissociation (EMD) in $^{208}$Pb-$^{208}$Pb collisions; however, the total EMD cross sections for $^{16}$O and $^{20}$Ne nuclei are less reliable than those for $^{208}$Pb due to large uncertainties in the photoabsorption cross sections used as input.

## Method

The authors used the Abrasion-Ablation Monte Carlo for Colliders model with Minimum Spanning Tree clustering (AAMCC-MST) to simulate hadronic fragmentation of $^{20}$Ne beams at the LHC, combining a Glauber Monte Carlo model for spectator matter volume, MST-clustering for pre-equilibrium decays, and statistical models for excited prefragment decays. The RELDIS model, based on a photonuclear reaction cascade with pre-equilibrium emission, nucleon coalescence, and evaporation/fission, was used to simulate electromagnetic dissociation of $^{124}$Xe beams at NICA and $^{208}$Pb beams at the LHC.

## Key numbers

- Total hadronic cross section for $^{20}$Ne-$^{20}$Ne collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.36$ TeV is 1.7 b; total EMD cross section is 0.3 b.
- Total hadronic cross section for $^{208}$Pb-$^{208}$Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.36$ TeV is 7.6 b; total EMD cross section is 206.5 b.
- Total hadronic cross section for $^{124}$Xe-$^{124}$Xe collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=9.5$ GeV is 6 b; total EMD cross section is 5.3 b.
- Average inclusive cross sections for production of $^4$He, $^{12}$C, $^{14}$N, and $^{16}$O in hadronic $^{20}$Ne-$^{20}$Ne collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.36$ TeV are $1.04\pm 0.11$ b, $0.11\pm 0.01$ b, $0.17\pm 0.05$ b, and $0.08\pm 0.02$ b, respectively.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of nuclear transmutation in heavy-ion colliders, which previously focused on $^{208}$Pb beams at the LHC where EMD dominates (Pshenichnov 2011, Dmitrieva 2023, Acharya 2025). The finding that hadronic fragmentation dominates for light $^{20}$Ne beams contrasts with the heavy-ion case and provides essential cross sections and momentum distributions for assessing contamination of $^{20}$Ne-$^{20}$Ne event samples by collisions involving secondary nuclei with $Z/A=1/2$ such as $^4$He, $^{12}$C, $^{14}$N, and $^{16}$O. For $^{124}$Xe at NICA, the results show that while EMD produces secondary nuclei, none have the same $Z/A$ as the beam, preventing data contamination, though impact on collider components requires further study.
