# Phase structures of strong coupling lattice QCD with finite baryon and isospin density

> Quantum chromodynamics (QCD) at finite temperature (T), baryon chemical potential (\muB) and isospin chemical potential (\muI) is studied in the strong coupling limit on a lattice with staggered fermions. With the use of large dimensional expansion and the mean field approximation, we derive an effective action written in terms of the chiral condensate and pion condensate as a function of T, \muB and \muI. The phase structure in the space of T and \muB is elucidated, and simple analytical formulas for the critical line of the chiral phase transition and the tricritical point are derived. The effects of a finite quark mass (m) and finite \muI on the phase diagram are discussed. We also investigate the phase structure in the space of T, \muI and m, and clarify the correspondence between color SU(3) QCD with finite isospin density and color SU(2) QCD with finite baryon density. Comparisons of our results with those from recent Monte Carlo lattice simulations on finite density QCD are given.

## Key claim

The paper derives an effective free energy for strong coupling lattice QCD at finite temperature, baryon chemical potential, and isospin chemical potential using a large dimensional expansion and mean field approximation, and claims that the resulting phase structure is qualitatively consistent with other analytical approaches, though the slope of the chiral critical line near zero chemical potential is much steeper than in Monte Carlo lattice simulations, which the author attributes to the chiral limit and infinite coupling limit used.

## Method

Starting from the lattice action for staggered fermions in the strong coupling limit (gluonic action vanishes), the author performs the path integral over spatial gauge link variables using a 1/d expansion, linearizes the resulting four-fermion term via an auxiliary field, and introduces scalar (chiral condensate) and pseudoscalar (pion condensate) modes. After adopting the Polyakov gauge and performing a partial Fourier transformation, the Grassmann integration over quark fields yields a determinant that is evaluated analytically, leading to a closed-form effective free energy as a function of temperature T, baryon chemical potential μB, isospin chemical potential μI, and quark mass m.

## Key numbers

- Tricritical point for Nc=3, Nf=4 in the chiral limit: (T_tri, μ_tri) = (0.866, 1.73) (in lattice units)
- Critical temperature at zero chemical potential for Nc=3, Nf=4: T_c(0) = 5/3 (in lattice units)
- For Nc=3, Nf=4, the slope of the critical temperature near zero chemical potential is given by T_c(μ)/T_c(0) = 1 - 1.5 μ²/T_c(0)² - 3.375 μ⁴/T_c(0)⁴ + O((μ/T_c)⁶)
- For Nc=3, Nf=4 with quark mass m=0.1, the critical end point shifts to (T_end, μ_end) = (0.646, 1.96), a decrease of 25.5% in T and an increase of 12.9% in μ relative to the tricritical point
- At T=0, the chiral condensate in the broken phase is σ₀² = (2√(1+d²)-2)/d², which for d=3 gives σ₀ ≈ 0.745

## Context

This work extends the author's previous study of strong coupling SU(2) QCD (Nishida 2003) to SU(3) QCD with both baryon and isospin chemical potential, and provides analytical formulas for the chiral phase transition line and tricritical point that are consistent with results from the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Asakawa 1989), random matrix model (Halasz 1998), and Schwinger-Dyson method (Takagi 2002, Barducci 1990). The results enable comparison with Monte Carlo lattice simulations at finite density (Fodor 2002, Allton 2002, de Forcrand 2002, D'Elia 2003) and clarify the correspondence between color SU(3) QCD with finite isospin density and color SU(2) QCD with finite baryon density.
