# Rapidity evolution of the entanglement entropy in quarkonium: parton and string duality

> We investigate the quantum entanglement in rapidity space of the soft gluon wave function of a quarkonium, in theories with non-trivial rapidity evolutions. We found that the rapidity evolution drastically changes the behavior of the entanglement entropy, at any given order in perturbation theory. At large $N_c$, the reduced density matrices that "resum" the leading rapidity-logs can be explicitly constructed, and shown to satisfy Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK)-like evolution equations. We study their entanglement entropy in a simplified $1+1$ toy model, and in 3D QCD. The entanglement entropy in these cases, after re-summation, is shown to saturate the Kolmogorov-Sinai bound of 1. Remarkably, in 3D QCD the essential growth rate of the entanglement entropy is found to vanish at large rapidities, a result of kinematical "quenching" in transverse space. The one-body reduction of the entangled density matrix obeys a BFKL evolution equation, which can be recast as an evolution in an emergent AdS space, at large impact-parameter and large rapidity. This observation allows the extension of the perturbative wee parton evolution at low-x, to a dual non-perturbative evolution of string bits in curved AdS$_5$ space, with manifest entanglement entropy in the confining regime.

## Key claim

The rapidity evolution of the entanglement entropy in quarkonium, after resummation of leading rapidity logarithms in the large \(N_c\) limit, saturates the Kolmogorov-Sinai bound of 1 for the case with overall longitudinal momentum conservation, but is otherwise linear in rapidity; in 3D QCD the growth rate vanishes at large rapidities due to kinematical quenching in transverse space.

## Method

The authors constructed the reduced density matrix for soft gluons in a quarkonium state by tracing over quark longitudinal and transverse degrees of freedom, leaving gluon longitudinal momentum untraced. They derived BK-like evolution equations for these density matrices in the large \(N_c\) limit, resumming leading rapidity logarithms, and studied the resulting entanglement entropy in a simplified 1+1 toy model and in 3D QCD. The one-body reduction of the entangled density matrix was shown to obey a BFKL evolution equation, which was recast as an evolution in an emergent AdS space.

## Key numbers

- The entanglement entropy for the reduced density matrix \(\hat\rho_1\) (without overall momentum conservation) asymptotes to \(ay + 1 + \mathcal{O}(e^{-ay})\) for large rapidity \(y\), where \(a \sim \alpha_s C_F\).
- The entanglement entropy for the reduced density matrix \(\hat\rho\) (with momentum conservation) saturates the Kolmogorov-Sinai bound of 1.
- The mean number of soft gluons is \(\bar n = e^{ay} - 1\), and the probability of finding \(n\) soft gluons is \(p_n = e^{-ay}(1 - e^{-ay})^n\).
- At order \(\alpha_s\), the leading contribution to the entanglement entropy is enhanced by \(\frac{1}{2} \ln^2 \Lambda^- p_0\), where \(p_0 = \frac{2\alpha_s C_F}{\pi} \int d^2b_\perp \ln b_\perp^2 \mu^2 |\Psi^{(0)}(b_\perp)|^2\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous 2D QCD observations (Liu, 2022) on entanglement entropy in confined meson states to 4D QCD with non-trivial rapidity divergences. It builds on the dipole generating functional approach of Mueller (1993, 1994) and the BFKL/BK evolution equations (Kuraev, 1977; Balitsky, 1978; Lipatov, 1996; Balitsky, 1995; Kovchegov, 1999). The entanglement entropy analysis connects to earlier holographic string analysis (Stoffers, 2012) and weak-coupling evolution equation analysis (Kharzeev, 2017), and enables a dual description linking perturbative wee parton evolution at low-x to non-perturbative evolution of string bits in curved AdS\(_5\) space.
