# Real-time Dynamics of the Schwinger Model as an Open Quantum System with Neural Density Operators

> Ab-initio simulations of multiple heavy quarks propagating in a Quark-Gluon Plasma are computationally difficult to perform due to the large dimension of the space of density matrices. This work develops machine learning algorithms to overcome this difficulty by approximating exact quantum states with neural network parametrisations, specifically Neural Density Operators. As a proof of principle demonstration in a QCD-like theory, the approach is applied to solve the Lindblad master equation in the 1+1d lattice Schwinger Model as an open quantum system. Neural Density Operators enable the study of in-medium dynamics on large lattice volumes, where multiple-string interactions and their effects on string-breaking and recombination phenomena can be studied. Thermal properties of the system at equilibrium can also be probed with these methods by variationally constructing the steady state of the Lindblad master equation. Scaling of this approach with system size is studied, and numerical demonstrations on up to 32 spatial lattice sites and with up to 3 interacting strings are performed.

# Real-time Dynamics of the Schwinger Model as an Open Quantum System with Neural Density Operators

## Key claim

Neural Density Operators (NDOs) combined with the time-dependent Variational Monte Carlo (tVMC) algorithm can simulate Lindbladian dynamics of the 1+1d lattice Schwinger Model on up to 32 spatial lattice sites with up to 3 interacting strings, but the method introduces systematic errors from finite neural network expressivity and statistical uncertainties from Monte Carlo sampling that only vanish in the infinite-sample limit.

## Method

The authors parametrize density matrices using Neural Density Operators, a neural network architecture with hidden and ancillary bits that represents density matrices as traces over ancillary qubits of pure-state wavefunctions. They simulate real-time Lindbladian evolution by applying the Time-Dependent Variational Principle, estimating the Quantum Geometric Tensor and forces via Monte Carlo Markov Chains (tVMC). Initial states are prepared by explicit weight setting, and a transfer-learning procedure stitches together NDOs trained on size L lattices to initialize NDOs on size 2L lattices, exploiting approximate translational symmetry.

## Key numbers

- Numerical demonstrations performed on up to 32 spatial lattice sites and with up to 3 interacting strings
- Lindbladian evolution of string states simulated on an L = 20 lattice, enabling study of multiple-string interactions not possible on smaller lattices
- Chiral condensate phase transition investigated on an L = 32 lattice via successive transfer learning and retraining
- Exact diagonalization comparison performed on L = 4 lattice for chiral symmetry phase transition validation
- The total number of complex parameters in the NDO grows quadratically as L increases for fixed hyperparameters M_h, M_a

## Context

This work extends neural network methods for open quantum systems, which have previously focused on spin systems with local interactions (Nagy 2019, Hartmann 2019, Vicentini 2019, Yoshioka 2019), to quantum field theories using the Schwinger Model as a testbed. The approach contrasts with Master Equation Unraveling methods applied to single heavy quark or quarkonium systems (Akamatsu 2018, Miura 2020, Sharma 2020, Brambilla 2020) and tensor-network parametrizations (Sulz 2023). The results enable future extension to realistic simulations of heavy quarkonia propagating through the Quark-Gluon Plasma, though challenges remain including the nonlocalities introduced by Jordan-Wigner transformations in 3d and the different Hilbert space structure of Non-Relativistic QCD.
