# Unwinding the rare $Ω$ sector: Fragmentation of fully charmed baryons from HL-LHC to FCC

> By adopting a hadron-structure-oriented approach, we present and discuss the release of the novel OMG3Q1.0 set of collinear fragmentation functions for fully charmed, rare $Ω$ baryons. Our methodology combines diquark-like proxy model inputs for both charm-quark and gluon channels, calculated at the initial energy scales, with a DGLAP evolution that ensures a consistent treatment of heavy-quark thresholds, following directly from the HF-NRevo scheme. We complement our work with a phenomenological study of NLL/NLO$^+$ resummed $Ω_{3c}$ plus jet distributions using (sym)JETHAD at the HL-LHC and the future FCC. Unraveling the production mechanisms of rare, yet-unobserved hadrons, as provided by the OMG3Q1.0 functions, stands as a key asset for deepening our understanding of QCD at future high-energy hadron colliders.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first public release of collinear fragmentation functions (FFs) for fully charmed, rare Omega baryons (Omega_{3c}), but the initial-scale inputs rely on a scalar diquark approximation that does not fully capture the symmetrization structure expected for a J=1/2 baryon composed of three identical fermions, with the full spin structure recovered only through nonperturbative hadronization effects.

## Method

The OMG3Q1.0 fragmentation functions are constructed by combining diquark-like proxy model NLO inputs for both charm-quark and gluon channels at the lowest factorization scale, using existing calculations from MoosaviNejad (2017) and Delpasand (2019). These initial-scale inputs are then evolved to higher energy scales using DGLAP timelike evolution equations in a variable-flavor-number scheme (VFNS) implemented via the Heavy-Flavor NonRelativistic evolution (HF-NRevo) scheme. The evolved FFs are provided in LHAPDF6 format and interfaced with the (sym)JETHAD code to compute NLL/NLO+ resummed distributions for Omega_{3c} plus jet production at HL-LHC and FCC energies.

## Key numbers

* The Omega_{3c} baryon is predicted to have a mass around 4.8 GeV, based on prior work by Shah (2017)
* Fragmentation probabilities for triply heavy baryons are estimated in the range [10^{-5} to 10^{-7}], with total cross sections in the nanobarn regime, based on prior work by Chen (2011) and GomshiNobary (2005)
* The relative production rate of B_c mesons compared to singly bottomed B mesons remains below 0.1%, based on prior LHCb measurements and the ZCFW22 FF framework

## Context

This work extends recent efforts to characterize rare and exotic hadron production via fragmentation, including prior releases of FFs for fully heavy tetraquarks (TQ4Q1.0, TQ4Q1.1 families by Celiberto 2024-2025) and fully heavy pentaquarks (PQ5Q1.0 by Celiberto 2025). The OMG3Q1.0 sets provide a benchmark for future studies of triply heavy states at HL-LHC and FCC, and lay groundwork for comparisons with lattice QCD, potential models, and heavy-flavor QCD phenomenology, while also enabling searches for Omega_{3c} as possible decay products of multiquark exotics such as pentaquarks.
