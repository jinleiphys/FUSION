# Electromagnetic processes in a $χ$EFT framework

> Recently, we have derived a two--nucleon potential and consistent nuclear electromagnetic currents in chiral effective field theory with pions and nucleons as explicit degrees of freedom. The calculation of the currents has been carried out to include N$^3$LO corrections, consisting of two--pion exchange and contact contributions. The latter involve unknown low-energy constants (LECs), some of which have been fixed by fitting the $np$ S- and P-wave phase shifts up to 100 MeV lab energies. The remaining LECs entering the current operator are determined so as to reproduce the experimental deuteron and trinucleon magnetic moments, as well as the $np$ cross section. This electromagnetic current operator is utilized to study the $nd$ and $n^3$He radiative captures at thermal neutron energies. Here we discuss our results stressing on the important role played by the LECs in reproducing the experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a chiral effective field theory (χEFT) electromagnetic current operator, including N³LO corrections with low-energy constants (LECs) fitted to selected observables, can reproduce experimental data for thermal neutron radiative captures on deuterons and ³He, but the agreement depends on the specific LECs and the cutoff regularization, with the N³LO(LECs) contributions being crucial for bringing theory into agreement with experiment.

## Method

The authors derived nuclear electromagnetic currents in χEFT up to N³LO (order e Q) using time-ordered perturbation theory with non-relativistic Hamiltonians from chiral Lagrangians, retaining pions and nucleons as degrees of freedom. The currents include one-loop two-pion exchange terms, tree-level terms involving a nuclear-electromagnetic Hamiltonian of order e Q², and contact currents of minimal and non-minimal nature. The unknown LECs were fixed by fitting the np S- and P-wave phase shifts up to 100 MeV lab energies, and then the remaining four LECs were determined by reproducing the experimental deuteron and trinucleon magnetic moments and the np cross section at thermal energies. The resulting current operator was used to study nd and n³He radiative captures at thermal neutron energies within a hybrid approach, using nuclear wave functions from realistic Hamiltonians (AV18/UIX and N3LO/N2LO chiral potentials).

## Key numbers

- The np S- and P-wave phase shifts were fitted up to 100 MeV lab energies to fix LECs entering the contact two-nucleon chiral potential.
- The cutoff Λ used to regularize the EM current operator was varied from 500 to 700 MeV, corresponding to removing short-range physics at distance scales less than 1/(3 m_π).
- The LO contribution provides only about 46% of the total calculated nd cross section and 18% of the total calculated n³He cross section.
- The calculated n³He cross section undergoes a 5% variation when the cutoff changes from 500 to 700 MeV, but remains within the experimental error band.

## Context

This work extends the χEFT approach to nuclear electromagnetic currents beyond previous derivations by Park et al. (1996) in covariant perturbation theory, which included only irreducible contributions, and agrees with the results of Kolling et al. (2009) obtained by unitary transformations. The hybrid calculations use wave functions from the Argonne v18 potential with Urbana-IX three-nucleon potential (AV18/UIX) and N³LO/N²LO chiral potentials, following earlier studies by Carlson et al. (1998) that showed these reactions are dominated by many-body current components. The paper enables future fully consistent χEFT calculations using the N²LO chiral potential derived in Pastore et al. (2009) together with the presented EM currents.
