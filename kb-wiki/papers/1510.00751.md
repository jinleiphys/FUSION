# Uncovering the Matter-Neutrino Resonance

> Matter Neutrino Resonances (MNRs) can drastically modify neutrino flavor evolution in astrophysical environments and may significantly impact nucleosynthesis. Here we further investigate the underlying physics of MNR type flavor transitions. We provide generalized resonance conditions and make analytical predictions for the behavior of the system. We discuss the adiabatic evolution of these transitions, considering both Symmetric and Standard scenarios. Symmetric MNR transitions differ from Standard MNR transitions in that both neutrinos and antineutrinos can completely transform to other flavors simultaneously. We provide an example of the simplest system in which such transitions can occur with a neutrino and an antineutrino having a single energy and emission angle. We further apply linearized stability analysis to predict the location of self-induced nutation type (or bipolar) oscillations due to neutrino-neutrino interactions in the regions where MNR is ineffective. In all cases, we compare our analytical predictions to numerical calculations.

## Key claim

The paper provides generalized resonance conditions and analytical predictions for Matter-Neutrino Resonance (MNR) flavor transitions, showing that these transitions occur when the neutrino-neutrino potential actively cancels the background matter contribution, and that the system can maintain resonance over an extended period by suitably transforming neutrinos to other flavors.

## Method

The authors derived analytical resonance conditions by diagonalizing the flavor-basis Hamiltonian and minimizing the in-medium mass splitting gap between instantaneous eigenstates. They applied linearized stability analysis to predict the onset of self-induced nutation-type (bipolar) oscillations in regions where MNR is ineffective, and compared all analytical predictions to numerical solutions of the neutrino and antineutrino density matrix evolution equations for two-flavor monoenergetic systems with single emission angles.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum mixing angle used for MNR transitions: θ_V = 0.154 (consistent with θ_13 measurements)
- Reduced vacuum mixing angle used to suppress MNR transitions: θ_V = 0.001
- Matter potential kept constant at V_e = 1000 |Δ_V| for both Model A and Model B
- Neutrino-neutrino interaction strength for Model A: μ_ν = 10,000 |Δ_V| (constant)
- Neutrino-neutrino interaction strength for Model B: μ_ν(r) = 10,000 e^(-r/10) |Δ_V|
- Initial ν_e and ν̄_e asymmetry factor for Model A: α(r) = 1.3 - 0.048 r
- Initial ν_e and ν̄_e asymmetry factor for Model B: α = 4/3
- Vacuum scale Δ_V = +1 for normal hierarchy, -1 for inverted hierarchy

## Context

This work extends the numerical observations of MNR transitions first reported in Malkus (2012) for neutrino flavor evolution above black hole accretion disks, and further investigated in Malkus (2014) and Malkus (2015). The paper provides the first generalized analytical framework for both Symmetric and Standard MNR transitions, and applies linearized stability analysis methods from Vaananen (2013) to predict bipolar oscillation regions when MNR is suppressed by small mixing angles. This enables predictions of flavor evolution in compact object mergers and core-collapse supernovae without requiring full numerical simulations.
