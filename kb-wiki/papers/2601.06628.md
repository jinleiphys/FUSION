# Distribution Functions of Radially Excited Pion using the Light-Front Quark Model

> We investigate the internal structure of the ground ($1S$) and the first two radially excited ($2S,3S$) states of the pion within the light-front quark model. The valence Fock sector is described using pure harmonic-oscillator eigenstates and mixed states formed as orthogonal linear combinations of these eigenfunctions. The optimal wavefunction parameters are determined through a variational procedure based on a QCD-motivated effective Hamiltonian. Using the resulting light-front wavefunctions, we study the pion distribution amplitude, parton distribution function, and electromagnetic form factor. After QCD evolution, the ground state distribution amplitude and parton distribution function are found to be in good agreement with available experimental data. At the model scale, the parton distribution functions of the $1S$ and $2S$ states show clear sensitivity to state mixing, while the distribution amplitudes and electromagnetic form factors are weakly sensitive. In contrast, for the $3S$ state, all three observables exhibit a pronounced sensitivity to mixing. The decay constants of the mixed states are also found to decrease sequentially with increasing radial excitation.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the internal structure of the ground and first two radially excited pion states can be described within a light-front quark model using mixed harmonic-oscillator wavefunctions, and that the sensitivity to state mixing increases with radial excitation, for the 3S state, all observables show pronounced sensitivity, whereas for 1S and 2S states only the parton distribution functions are clearly sensitive.

## Method
The authors construct light-front wavefunctions for the pion 1S, 2S, and 3S states using pure harmonic-oscillator eigenfunctions and mixed states formed as orthogonal linear combinations of these eigenfunctions. The optimal wavefunction parameters, including mixing angles and the harmonic parameter, are determined through a variational procedure based on a QCD-motivated effective Hamiltonian containing kinetic, confining, Coulomb, and hyperfine interaction terms. The resulting light-front wavefunctions are then used to compute the distribution amplitude, parton distribution function, and electromagnetic form factor for each state.

## Key numbers
- Best-fit mixing angles: θ = 0.339 rad (19.42°) and φ = 0.029 rad (1.64°)
- Potential parameters from mixing: a = -0.647 GeV, α_s = 0.410
- Harmonic parameter β for pion with mixing: 0.550 GeV; without mixing: 0.634 GeV
- Calculated masses with mixing: π(2S) = 1.365 GeV, π(3S) = 1.783 GeV (PDG values: 1.300 GeV and 1.810 GeV)
- χ² for mixing case: 6.51 MeV; for no-mixing case: 84.153 MeV
- Decay constants with mixing: π(1S) = 156.4 MeV, π(2S) = 28.6 MeV, π(3S) = 6.5 MeV
- Decay constants without mixing: π(1S) = 146.3 MeV, π(2S) = 25.9 MeV, π(3S) = 69.9 MeV
- Ground state DA and PDF evolved from model scale Q₀ = 0.49 GeV to Q = 2 GeV for DA and Q² = 16 GeV² for PDF for comparison with E791 and E615 data

## Context
This work extends the light-front quark model approach of Choi (2015) to radially excited pion states, using a variational method with mixed harmonic-oscillator wavefunctions. It provides predictions for the distribution amplitude, parton distribution function, and electromagnetic form factor of π(2S) and π(3S) states, complementing Bethe-Salpeter calculations by Xu (2025) and Wang (2025) and lattice QCD results by Gao (2021). The results enable future comparisons with upcoming electron-ion collider data via the Sullivan process, and provide benchmarks for first-principles lattice QCD simulations of excited pion structure.
