# New physics from COHERENT data with improved Quenching Factor

> A recent new measurement and re-analysis of past measurements suggested an improved quenching factor value and uncertainty for CsI[Na]. This implies a measurement of the COHERENT experiment of coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering that is closer to the Standard Model prediction and has less uncertainty. We illustrate the impact of this improvement by revisiting fits to the Weinberg angle, neutrino magnetic moments, neutron rms and neutrino charge radii, neutrino non-standard interactions (in particular those relevant for LMA-Dark) and new scalar as well as vector bosons. Significant improvement is observed, particularly for those scenarios coherently affecting the electroweak SM process.

## Key claim

The new quenching factor measurement and re-analysis for CsI[Na] brings the COHERENT experiment's measured CEνNS rate closer to the Standard Model prediction and reduces the systematic uncertainty, leading to significantly improved constraints on electroweak and new physics parameters, particularly for scenarios that coherently affect the Standard Model process.

## Method

The authors performed a chi-squared fit to COHERENT data using 17 energy bins, incorporating the new energy-dependent quenching factor from Collar (2019) and its reduced systematic uncertainty of 13.5% (down from 28%). They used the Klein-Nystrand nuclear form factor, the acceptance function recommended by the COHERENT collaboration, and included pull parameters for signal and background systematic uncertainties to extract constraints on multiple physics parameters.

## Key numbers

- New total systematic uncertainty: 13.5% (previously 28%)
- New expected SM event rate: 138 ± 19 events (previously 173 ± 48), compared to measured 134 ± 22 events
- Weinberg angle: sin²θ_W = 0.248 ± 0.045 (1σ), compared to previous 0.217_{-0.051}^{+0.068} (1σ)
- Neutron rms charge radius: R_n = 4.6_{-0.8}^{+0.9} fm (1σ), compared to previous 4.9_{-1.3}^{+1.1} fm (1σ)
- Weak nuclear charge of Cs: Q_W^Cs = -72.2_{-1.2}^{+1.4} (1σ), compared to previous -72.6_{-2.0}^{+1.9} (1σ)
- Neutrino magnetic moment constraints at 90% C.L.: -76×10^{-10} < μ_{eα}/μ_B < 76×10^{-10} (improved from ±86×10^{-10}); -48×10^{-10} < μ_{μβ}/μ_B < 48×10^{-10} (improved from ±57×10^{-10})
- Neutrino charge radius constraints at 90% C.L.: -48×10^{-32} < ⟨r²_ee⟩/cm² < 12×10^{-32}; -44×10^{-32} < ⟨r²_μμ⟩/cm² < 6×10^{-32}
- LMA-Dark NSI parameter region excluded at >99% C.L. (3.1σ) with new QF, compared to 90% C.L. (2.1σ) previously

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the COHERENT experiment's first CEνNS observation (Akimov 2017) by incorporating the improved quenching factor measurement and re-analysis from Collar (2019). The results improve upon previous constraints on the Weinberg angle, neutrino magnetic moments, and neutrino charge radii, and show that the LMA-Dark solution for solar neutrino oscillations is now excluded at more than 99% C.L. by COHERENT data alone. The improved neutron rms charge radius measurement (4.6 fm) is now in better agreement with predictions from nuclear models (0.1-0.2 fm neutron skin), though uncertainties remain large. This work enables more precise future tests of Standard Model parameters at low momentum transfer and tighter constraints on new physics scenarios involving vector and scalar mediators.
