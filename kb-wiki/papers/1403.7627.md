# Population of dipole states via isoscalar probes: the splitting of pygmy dipole resonance in $^{124}$Sn}

> Inelastic $α$-scattering excitation cross sections are calculated for electric dipole excitations in $^{124}$Sn based on the transition densities obtained from the relativistic quasiparticle time-blocking approximation (RQTBA) in the framework of a semiclassical model. The calculation provides the missing link to directly compare the results from the microscopic RQTBA calculations to recent experimental data measured via the $(α,α'γ)$ reaction, which show a structural splitting of the low-lying E1 strength often denoted as pygmy dipole resonance (PDR). The experimentally observed splitting is reproduced by the cross section calculations, which allows to draw conclusion on the structure of the PDR.

## Key claim

The experimentally observed splitting of the low-lying E1 strength (pygmy dipole resonance) in $^{124}$Sn into two groups is reproduced by the calculated inelastic $\alpha$-scattering cross sections based on RQTBA transition densities, but the RQTBA calculations overpredict the strength of individual states by about a factor of ten due to insufficient fragmentation, and the overall strength distributions show an approximately 800 keV energy shift relative to experiment.

## Method

Inelastic $\alpha$-scattering cross sections for $J^{\pi}=1^{-}$ states in $^{124}$Sn at $E_\alpha=136$ MeV were calculated using a semiclassical model where the colliding nuclei follow classical trajectories determined by the real part of the optical potential, while the internal degrees of freedom are treated quantum mechanically. The transition densities for the target nucleus were obtained from the relativistic quasiparticle time-blocking approximation (RQTBA) based on the covariant density functional with NL3 parametrization, and the nuclear formfactors were constructed via double folding of these transition densities with the M3Y-Reid nucleon-nucleon interaction. The cross sections were integrated over impact parameter ranges (8.2-9.0 fm and 13.3-52.0 fm) corresponding to the experimental scattering angle range of approximately $1.53^\circ$ to $5.94^\circ$ in the center-of-mass system.

## Key numbers

- The integrated total cross section up to 8.7 MeV is 43.6 mb/sr for the RQTBA calculation and 44.1(5) mb/sr for the experiment (including continuum contribution)
- The RQTBA strength distributions show an overall shift of approximately 800 keV relative to the experimental data
- The strongest states in both the calculated cross sections and B$_{em}$(E1) distributions are about a factor of ten stronger in the RQTBA compared to experiment
- The bunching procedure groups states into approximately 40 keV bins
- The phonon space in the RQTBA calculation was truncated by angular momentum at $J^{\pi} = 6^+$ and by frequencies at 15 MeV

## Context

This work provides the missing link for direct comparison between microscopic RQTBA calculations and experimental data from the $(\alpha,\alpha'\gamma)$ reaction, which previously had only been compared qualitatively as reported in Endres et al. (2010). The calculations confirm the splitting of the PDR into two groups observed in experiments by Savran et al. (2006) and Endres et al. (2010): a lower group excited by both isoscalar and isovector probes and a higher group excited only by photons. The approach extends earlier semiclassical model calculations by Vitturi et al. (2010, 2011) and Lanza et al. (2010, 2011) and enables future work on the connection between the neutron skin and the isoscalar parameters in the nuclear equation of state, though the authors note that the NL3 interaction used produces a neutron skin larger than experimental data indicate.
