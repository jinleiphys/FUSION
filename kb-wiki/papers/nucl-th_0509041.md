# Hadron attenuation by (pre)hadronic FSI at HERMES

> We investigate hadron production in deep inelastic lepton-nucleus scattering in the kinematic regime of the HERMES experiment. Our calculations are carried out in the framework of a BUU transport model which contains the Lund event generators PYTHIA and FRITIOF for the simulation of high-energy elementary interactions. For the first time we consistently use the complete four-dimensional information of the Lund string break up vertices as input for our transport theoretical studies of (pre)hadronic final state interactions. We compare our results with experimental HERMES data on charged hadron attenuation.

## Key claim
The observed hadron attenuation in deep inelastic lepton-nucleus scattering at HERMES can be described by assuming that the struck quark forms a color-neutral prehadron early after the interaction, which then scatters with hadronic cross sections, rather than requiring partonic energy loss from induced gluon radiation.

## Method
The authors used a semi-classical Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model that incorporates the Lund event generators PYTHIA and FRITIOF for simulating the elementary deep inelastic scattering process and (pre)hadronic final state interactions. For the first time, they extracted the complete four-dimensional space-time information of the Lund string break-up vertices to assign production and formation points to each hadron, then propagated prehadrons through the nucleus with their interaction cross sections set to the corresponding hadronic values.

## Key numbers
- Multiplicity ratio \(R_M^h\) for charged hadrons was calculated for a 27.6 GeV positron beam on \(^{14}\)N and \(^{84}\)Kr targets, using kinematic cuts and detector acceptance matching the HERMES experiment.
- The production and formation points for a typical HERMES event were shown for photon energy \(\nu = 14\) GeV and virtuality \(Q^2 = 2.5\) GeV\(^2\), with a large fraction of these points falling within a distance behind the struck nucleon comparable to nuclear radii.
- Two PYTHIA parameter sets were compared: the default set and a set fitted to hydrogen data by the HERMES collaboration; both yielded comparable results in good agreement with experimental data.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous BUU transport model studies (Falter 2004) and contrasts with interpretations by other groups (partonic energy loss models) that attribute attenuation to induced gluon radiation from the struck quark. The results support the prehadron formation picture and enable future investigations of string fragmentation in nuclei, induced gluon radiation prior to prehadron formation, and applications to jet quenching in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC.
