# Degeneracy in Studying the Supranuclear Equation of State and Modified Gravity with Neutron Stars

> It is generally acknowledged that an extrapolation in physics from a well-known scale to an unknown scale is perilous. This prevents us from using laboratory experience to gain precise information for the supranuclear matter inside neutron stars (NSs). With operating and upcoming astronomical facilities, NSs' equation of state (EOS) is expected to be determined at a new level in the near future, under the assumption that general relativity (GR) is the correct theory for gravitation. While GR is a reasonable working assumption yet still an extrapolation, there could be a large uncertainty due to the not-so-well-tested strong gravitational field inside NSs. Here we review some recent theoretical efforts towards a better understanding of the degeneracy between the supranuclear EOS and alternative gravity theories.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the determination of the neutron star equation of state (EOS) from astronomical observations is degenerate with the choice of gravitational theory, because general relativity (GR) has not been fully tested in the strong-field regime inside neutron stars, and this degeneracy must be accounted for before claiming precise EOS measurements.

## Method

The author reviews two parameterized frameworks for studying neutron star structure beyond GR: the post-TOV formalism, which adds perturbative 1PN and 2PN corrections to the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations, and the non-perturbative Damour-Esposito-Farese (DEF) scalar-tensor gravity, where a scalar field with non-minimal coupling can trigger spontaneous scalarization. For each framework, the modified TOV equations are integrated for a set of nine candidate EOSs that support neutron stars above 2 solar masses, and the resulting mass-radius relations are compared to GR predictions and to observations from PSR J0348+0432 and GW170817.

## Key numbers

- Nine EOSs from Lattimer (2000) are used, all with maximum neutron star mass larger than 2 solar masses.
- The 1PN post-TOV parameter δ3 = 0.5 is used as an illustrative example, though the text notes this value is already excluded by Solar System and radio pulsar observations.
- The 2PN post-TOV parameter μ1 = -0.5 is used as an illustrative example.
- In the DEF scalar-tensor gravity, spontaneous scalarization occurs when β0 ≲ -4.5, with |α0| = 10^{-5} and β0 = -4.5 used as the example parameter set.
- The effective scalar coupling αeff shows a sudden increase by orders of magnitude at a critical compactness, with the threshold mass depending on the EOS (e.g., WFF1 scalarizes at relatively low mass, PAL1 at quite high mass).

## Context

This work extends the parameterized post-Newtonian (PPN) framework of Will (1993) and the post-TOV formalism of Glampedakis (2015, 2016) to systematically study degeneracy between the supranuclear EOS and modified gravity. It also builds on the spontaneous scalarization phenomenon discovered by Damour and Esposito-Farese (1993, 1996) in scalar-tensor gravity. The paper enables future joint fits of EOS and gravity parameters using upcoming facilities like the Square Kilometre Array (SKA), the enhanced X-ray Timing and Polarimetry mission (eXTP), and gravitational-wave detectors.
