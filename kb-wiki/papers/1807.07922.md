# Coexistence of chiral symmetry and pseudospin symmetry in one nucleus: triplet bands in $^{105}$Ag

> The nearly degenerate triplet bands with the $πg_{9/2}^{-1}\otimes νh_{11/2} (g_{7/2},d_{5/2})$ configuration in $^{105}$Ag are studied via the relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory and the multiparticle plus rotor model (MPRM), which indicates that these bands are associated with chiral symmetry and pseudospin symmetry. The configuration-fixed constrained triaxial RMF calculations exhibit the pseudospin symmetry in single particle spectra and the triaxial shape coexistence. The experimental excitation energies and the electromagnetic transition probabilities for the triplet bands are reproduced very well by the MPRM calculaitons. The chiral doublet bands show the same phase in the $B(M1)/B(E2)$ staggering, while the pseudospin doublet bands hold the opposite phase. The coexistence of chiral symmetry and pseudospin symmetry in one nucleus and its corresponding characteristic of the rotational structure are discussed for the first time.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the three nearly degenerate negative-parity bands in \(^{105}\)Ag with the \(\pi g_{9/2}^{-1}\otimes \nu h_{11/2} (g_{7/2},d_{5/2})\) configuration provide the first example of the coexistence of chiral symmetry and pseudospin symmetry in one single nucleus, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific interpretation that the second and third bands (G and D) are chiral doublet bands while the first and second bands (C and G) are pseudospin doublet bands, as supported by the multiparticle plus rotor model (MPRM) calculations.

## Method

The authors performed configuration-fixed constrained triaxial relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations using the NL3 effective interaction to obtain the triaxial deformations and single-particle configurations for \(^{105}\)Ag. They then used the multiparticle plus rotor model (MPRM) to calculate the excitation energies and in-band \(B(M1)/B(E2)\) ratios for the triplet bands, adopting deformation parameters \((\beta,\gamma) = (0.23, 30^\circ)\), moment of inertia \(\Im = 32\hbar^2/\)MeV, and a Coriolis attenuation factor \(\xi = 0.85\). The calculated results were compared with experimental data from Timar (2007).

## Key numbers

- The ground state \(a\) has unpaired nucleon configuration \(\pi g_{9/2}\) with total energy \(-894.16\) MeV and deformation \((\beta,\gamma) = (0.18, 0.02^\circ)\).
- State \(e\) (configuration \(\pi g_{9/2}^{-1}\otimes \nu h_{11/2}g_{7/2}\)) has excitation energy 2.46 MeV relative to the ground state, compared to the experimental band head energy of 2.47 MeV for band C.
- State \(f\) (configuration \(\pi g_{9/2}^{-1}\otimes \nu h_{11/2}d_{5/2}\)) has excitation energy 3.10 MeV, compared to the experimental band head energy of 2.62 MeV for band D.
- State \(g\) (configuration \(\pi g_{9/2}^{-1}\otimes \nu h^{2}_{11/2}\)) has excitation energy 3.94 MeV, compared to the experimental band head energy of 3.91 MeV for band E.
- The triaxial deformation parameters for states \(e\), \(f\), and \(g\) are \(\gamma = 34.9^\circ\), \(26.5^\circ\), and \(38.5^\circ\), respectively, all with \(\beta \approx 0.23\) to 0.25.
- The MPRM calculations used a moment of inertia \(\Im = 32\hbar^2/\)MeV and a Coriolis attenuation factor \(\xi = 0.85\).
- The gyromagnetic ratios used were \(g_R = 0.44\), \(g_p(g_{9/2}) = 1.26\), \(g_n(h_{11/2}) = -0.21\), \(g_n(g_{7/2}) = 0.26\), and \(g_n(d_{5/2}) = -0.46\).

## Context

This work extends the study of multiple chiral doublet bands (M\(\chi\)D) in nuclei such as \(^{103}\)Rh (Kuti, 2014) and \(^{78}\)Br (Liu, 2016) by demonstrating that chiral symmetry can coexist with pseudospin symmetry in a single nucleus, a phenomenon not previously discussed. The pseudospin doublet interpretation builds on earlier observations of pseudospin doublet bands in nuclei like \(^{108}\)Tc (Xu, 2008) and \(^{118}\)Sb (Wang, 2010). The results enable future searches for "chirality-pseudospin quartet bands" in \(^{105}\)Ag and other candidate chiral nuclei, and suggest that similar triplet bands in the neighboring nucleus \(^{105}\)Rh (Alcantara, 2004) could be examined with electromagnetic transition measurements.
