# Susceptibilities and screening masses in two flavor QCD

> We studied QCD with two flavors of dynamical staggered quarks at finite temperature, with a bare sea quark mass of about 17 MeV. We report investigations of baryon, isospin, charge and strangeness susceptibilities, as well as screening masses obtained from correlators of local and one-link separated meson operators. These were studied as functions of valence quark mass at several temperatures. Our results for susceptibilities deviate significantly from ideal gas values, and even more from the weak coupling series. We also report the first measurement of off-diagonal quark number susceptibilities below the transition temperature, Tc, where they are the main contribution to charge fluctuations. We present evidence for a close connection between the susceptibilities and the screening masses.

## Key claim

The authors report that at temperatures above the transition temperature \(T_c\), the inclusion of two light dynamical quark flavors produces only small changes in quark number susceptibilities relative to the quenched approximation, and they present the first measurement of off-diagonal quark number susceptibilities below \(T_c\), where these off-diagonal terms dominate charge fluctuations.

## Method

The study used lattice QCD with two flavors of dynamical staggered quarks (\(N_f=2\)) on \(N_t=4\) lattices, with a bare sea quark mass fixed at \(m/T_c=0.1\) (approximately 17 MeV) as temperature was varied. Screening masses were extracted from spatial correlators of local and one-link separated meson operators, classified by irreducible representations of the dihedral group \(D_4^h\), while quark number susceptibilities (diagonal and off-diagonal) were computed using stochastic estimators for traces of Dirac operator inverses at zero chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- Bare sea quark mass: \(m = 17 \pm 2\) MeV (corresponding to \(m/T_c = 0.1\)); a second set used \(m = 12 \pm 1\) MeV (\(m/T_c = 0.075\)).
- Transition temperature in physical units: \(T_c = 167 \pm 17\) MeV.
- At \(T = 1.5 T_c\) on a \(4 \times 16^3\) lattice with \(m_v/T_c = 0.1\): \(\chi_3/T^2 = 0.98 \pm 0.03\) and \(\chi_{ud}/T^2 = (0.02 \pm 0.02) \times 10^{-4}\).
- Screening mass in the \(A_1^+\) (S/PS) channel at \(T = 1.5 T_c\) for \(N_f=2\): \(M/T = 3.67 \pm 0.02\); for \(N_f=0\): \(M/T = 4.21 \pm 0.01\).
- Screening mass in the \(A_1^+\) (V/AV) channel at \(T = 1.5 T_c\) for \(N_f=2\): \(M/T = 5.44 \pm 0.08\); for \(N_f=0\): \(M/T = 5.32 \pm 0.08\).

## Context

This work extends earlier quenched QCD studies of quark number susceptibilities by Gavai, Gupta, and Majumdar (2001) and screening mass measurements in the \(N_f=4\) theory by the same group, moving to the more realistic case of two dynamical light flavors. The results enable future lattice simulations with lighter u and d quarks and inclusion of dynamical strange quarks, and they provide non-perturbative data that challenge weak-coupling resummation approaches (e.g., those cited as "resum" in the text).
