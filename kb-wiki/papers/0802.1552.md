# Covariant spectator theory of np scattering: Phase shifts obtained from precision fits to data below 350 MeV

> Using the covariant spectator theory (CST), we present two one boson exchange kernels that have been successfully adjusted to fit the 2007 world np data (containing 3788 data) below 350 MeV. One model (which we designate WJC-1) has 27 parameters and fits with a chi2/N = 1.06. The other model (designated WJC-2) has only 15 parameters and fits with a chi2/N = 1.12. Both of these models also reproduce the experimental triton binding energy without introducing additional irreducible three-nucleon forces. One result of this work is a new phase shift analysis, updated for all data until 2006, which is useful even if one does not work within the CST. In carrying out these fits we have reviewed the entire data base, adding new data not previously used in other high precision fits and restoring some data omitted in previous fits. A full discussion and evaluation of the 2007 data base is presented.

## Key claim

Using the covariant spectator theory (CST), two one-boson-exchange kernels (WJC-1 with 27 parameters and WJC-2 with 15 parameters) fit the 2007 world np scattering data below 350 MeV with chi-squared per data point of 1.06 and 1.12 respectively, and both reproduce the experimental triton binding energy without additional three-nucleon forces, though the fits are limited to the specific CST framework and the chosen data set.

## Method

The authors constructed two one-boson-exchange kernels within the covariant spectator theory and performed chi-squared minimization fits to a database of 3788 np scattering data points below 350 MeV laboratory energy, including total cross sections, differential cross sections, polarization observables, and spin correlation coefficients. They reviewed and updated the entire data base, adding new data not used in previous high-precision fits and restoring some data omitted earlier, and produced a new phase shift analysis updated through 2006.

## Key numbers

* WJC-1 model: 27 parameters, chi-squared per data point = 1.06 for 3788 np data below 350 MeV.
* WJC-2 model: 15 parameters, chi-squared per data point = 1.12 for the same data set.
* Both models reproduce the experimental triton binding energy without irreducible three-nucleon forces.
* The database contains 3788 data points from the 2007 world np data compilation.

## Context

This work extends the covariant spectator theory approach to nucleon-nucleon scattering, previously applied by Gross and collaborators, by producing high-precision fits comparable to those from non-relativistic potential models such as the Nijmegen potentials (Nijmegen group, 1990s) and the CD-Bonn potential (Machleidt, 2001). The new phase shift analysis and the evaluated 2007 database provide a benchmark for future relativistic and non-relativistic NN interaction models, and the ability to reproduce the triton binding energy without three-nucleon forces suggests that relativistic effects in the CST framework may reduce the need for such forces.
