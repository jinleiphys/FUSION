# Analysis of polarization observables and radiative effects for the reaction $\bar p+p\rightarrow e^++e^-$

> The expressions for the differential cross section and of the polarization observables for the reaction $\bar p+p\rightarrow e^++e^-$ are given in terms of the nucleon electromagnetic form factors in the laboratory system. Radiative corrections due to the emission of virtual and real soft photons from the leptons are also calculated. Unlike the center-of- mass system case, they depend on the scattering angle. Polarization effects are derived in the case when the antiproton beam, the target and the electron in the final state are polarized. Numerical estimations have been done for all observables for the PANDA experimental conditions using models for the nucleon electromagnetic form factors in the time-like region. The radiative corrections to the differential cross section are calculated as function of the beam energy and of the electron angle.

# Analysis of polarization observables and radiative effects for the reaction \(\bar p+p\rightarrow e^++e^-\)

## Key claim

The differential cross section and polarization observables for antiproton-proton annihilation into an electron-positron pair are derived in the laboratory system in terms of nucleon electromagnetic form factors, and radiative corrections from lepton photon emission are shown to depend on the scattering angle (unlike the center-of-mass case). The numerical predictions for PANDA conditions depend strongly on which model for the time-like form factors is chosen, with the two parametrizations giving opposite signs for some spin correlation coefficients.

## Method

The authors compute the spin structure of the one-photon-exchange matrix element for \(\bar p + p \to e^+ + e^-\) in the laboratory frame, expressing the differential cross section and all polarization observables (single-spin asymmetry, spin correlation coefficients, polarization transfer) in terms of the complex Sachs electric and magnetic form factors \(G_E\) and \(G_M\). Radiative corrections from virtual and real soft photon emission from the leptons are calculated analytically using the t'Hooft-Veltman method, yielding a multiplicative correction factor \(\delta\) that depends on the electron scattering angle. Numerical evaluations are performed for antiproton beam energies of 2 GeV and 5 GeV using two parametrizations of the time-like form factors: a vector dominance model (VDM) and a pQCD-inspired dipole extension.

## Key numbers

- The single-spin asymmetry \(A_y\) for a polarized antiproton beam is proportional to \(\sin\theta \, \text{Im}(G_M G_E^*)\) and vanishes at \(\theta = 0^\circ\) and \(\theta = 180^\circ\).
- The spin correlation coefficient ratio \(R = C_{xx}/C_{yy}\) determines the form factor ratio via \(|G_E|/|G_M| = \sqrt{(R+1)/(R-1)\,\tau}\).
- The radiative correction factor \(\delta\) is of order 10% for the kinematical conditions considered; at \(E = 5\) GeV with \(\Delta E = 0.03E\) it is larger at forward angles than at backward angles.
- The pQCD-inspired parametrization uses \(A = 96.21\) (GeV/c)\(^4\) and \(\Lambda = 0.3\) GeV in \(|G_{E,M}^{QCD}| = A / [s^2 (\log^2(s/\Lambda^2) + \pi^2)]\).
- The total cross section in the laboratory system is \(\sigma_{tot} = (2\pi\alpha^2)/(3p(E+M)) [|G_E|^2 + 2\tau |G_M|^2]\).

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses of the annihilation reaction \(\bar p + p \to \ell^+ + \ell^-\) that were performed primarily in the center-of-mass system, such as the unpolarized cross section by Zichichi (1962) and the polarized case by Bilenky (1993). It provides laboratory-frame formulas needed for the PANDA fixed-target experiment at FAIR, where antiproton beams up to 15 GeV/c will be available. The radiative corrections calculated here complement the CMS analysis by Kuraev (2010) and show that in the laboratory frame the correction factor acquires an angular dependence. The results enable experimental extraction of the relative phase \(\phi = \phi_M - \phi_E\) of the time-like form factors and the ratio \(|G_E|/|G_M|\) from polarization measurements, which are less affected by systematic uncertainties than unpolarized cross section measurements.
