# Assessing background effects in search of the chiral vortical effect in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The search for the Chiral Vortical Effect (CVE) in relativistic heavy-ion collisions is carried out by measuring azimuthal correlators for baryon pairs such as $Λ$ and protons. Experimental results from the ALICE collaboration show significant separations in these observables, however, the interpretation remains unclear. It is believed that background contributions from baryon production mechanisms may play an important role. Using three phenomenological models, the Blast Wave, AMPT, and AVFD+UrQMD, we systematically investigate the background effects in Pb--Pb collisions at \snn = 5.02 TeV. We demonstrate that local baryon conservation, as well as hadronic annihilation processes, can significantly influence the correlators. The feed-down contribution from secondary protons is also estimated. Our study provides a foundation for disentangling background mechanisms and further facilitates the search for the CVE.

# Assessing background effects in search of the chiral vortical effect in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The observed azimuthal correlator signals for Lambda-proton pairs in Pb--Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, which have been interpreted as possible evidence for the chiral vortical effect, can be reproduced by background mechanisms alone, specifically local baryon conservation, hadronic annihilation, and feed-down from weak decays, without invoking any chiral anomalous effects.

## Method

Three phenomenological models were employed: a Blast Wave model with tunable local baryon conservation (LBC) strength, the AMPT model (string melting version with partonic coalescence), and AVFD+UrQMD with LBC and hadronic cascade enabled or disabled. All models simulated Pb--Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV and were tuned to reproduce measured hadronic spectra and anisotropic flow. The azimuthal correlators Delta-gamma and Delta-delta for Lambda-proton pairs were computed across centrality classes and kinematic selections (sum pT and pseudorapidity difference), and compared to ALICE experimental data.

## Key numbers

- LBC pair fraction f_LBC in the Blast Wave model rises from approximately 3.4% in 10–20% centrality to approximately 5% in 50–60% centrality.
- In AVFD+UrQMD, a configuration with P_LBC = 30% (30% of particles created in baryon-antibaryon pairs) was used alongside a P_LBC = 0 configuration.
- Feed-down from Lambda decays to secondary protons yields a ratio of approximately 97% relative to the Lambda–Lambda correlation across all centralities, based on a constant fit.
- The Blast Wave model reproduces the measured Delta-delta and Delta-gamma values across centralities from 10–20% to 50–60% at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV.

## Context

This work extends the systematic background characterization approach previously applied to the chiral magnetic effect (CME), where local charge conservation (LCC) was studied by Bzdak (2011), to the chiral vortical effect (CVE) search. The study directly addresses the open question from ALICE measurements by Shou (2024) and Acharya (2024) and STAR measurements by Chen (2024), which showed significant Lambda-proton correlator signals but left interpretation unclear. The results enable future extraction of a potential CVE signal by providing quantitative background templates that can be subtracted, analogous to the event-shape engineering method used for CME by Acharya (2018). The work also extends the Blast Wave approach of Wu (2023) from charge conservation to baryon number conservation.
