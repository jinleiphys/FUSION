# An improved single particle potential for transport model simulations of nuclear reactions induced by rare isotope beams

> Taking into account more accurately the isospin dependence of nucleon-nucleon interactions in the in-medium many-body force term of the Gogny effective interaction, new expressions for the single nucleon potential and the symmetry energy are derived. Effects of both the spin(isospin) and the density dependence of nuclear effective interactions on the symmetry potential and the symmetry energy are examined. It is shown that they both play a crucial role in determining the symmetry potential and the symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities. The improved single nucleon potential will be useful for simulating more accurately nuclear reactions induced by rare isotope beams within transport models.

# An improved single particle potential for transport model simulations of nuclear reactions induced by rare isotope beams

## Key claim

The authors derive new expressions for the single nucleon potential and symmetry energy by replacing the total-density-dependent term in the Gogny effective interaction with separate density dependences for neutron-neutron, proton-proton, and neutron-proton pairs, but the resulting symmetry potential and symmetry energy differ significantly from the original MDI (momentum-dependent interaction) only at supra-saturation densities and only for certain values of the spin(isospin)-dependence parameter x (x=0 and x=-1); for x=1 the two formulations are identical.

## Method

The authors modify the density-dependent term of the Gogny effective interaction so that the interaction between two nucleons depends on the local density of each nucleon type (neutron or proton) rather than the total density, following earlier ideas from nuclear structure studies by Sprung and Banerjee, Brueckner and Dabrowski, and Negele. They then derive the corresponding single nucleon potential (called IMDI, improved momentum-dependent interaction) and symmetry energy using the Hartree-Fock approximation, and compare the results with the original MDI potential of Das03 used in the IBUU04 transport model. The parameters are re-adjusted to fit the saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter and a symmetry energy of 30 MeV at normal nuclear matter density.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear matter density: ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Symmetry energy at saturation: E_sym(ρ₀) = 30 MeV
- Interaction strength parameters: C_unlike = -103.4 MeV, C_like = -11.7 MeV
- Parameter B = 106.35 MeV
- Density-dependence parameter σ = 4/3 (with variations of ±1/30 examined)
- Incompressibility constraint used: K₀ = 210 ± 20 MeV
- Slope parameter constraint used: L = 88 ± 25 MeV (extracted from isospin diffusion data using the IBUU04 transport model)
- Symmetry potentials are compared at densities ρ = 0.16, 0.32, and 0.48 fm⁻³

## Context

This work extends the MDI single nucleon potential derived by Das03 from the original Gogny effective interaction, which was used in the IBUU04 transport model for simulating heavy-ion collisions. The authors adopt the idea of separate density dependences for nn, pp, and np pairs that was proposed earlier by Sprung and Banerjee, Brueckner and Dabrowski (1964), and Negele for nuclear structure studies, but had not previously been applied to transport model simulations of reactions induced by rare isotope beams. The improved potential (IMDI) is intended to enable more accurate simulations of nuclear reactions with rare isotope beams, particularly for exploring the isospin dependence of the nuclear equation of state at supra-saturation densities relevant for astrophysical phenomena.
