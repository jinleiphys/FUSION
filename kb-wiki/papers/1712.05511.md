# Hauser-Feshbach fission fragment de-excitation with calculated macroscopic-microscopic mass yields

> The Hauser-Feshbach statistical model is applied to the de-excitation of primary fission fragments using input mass yields calculated with macroscopic-microscopic models of the potential energy surface. We test the sensitivity of the prompt fission observables to the input mass yields for two important reactions, $^{235}$U$(n_\mathrm{th},f)$ and $^{239}$Pu$(n_\mathrm{th},f)$, for which good experimental data exist. General traits of the mass yields, such as the location of the peaks and their widths, can impact both the prompt neutron and $γ$-ray multiplicities, as well as their spectra. Specifically, we use several mass yields to determine a linear correlation between the calculated prompt neutron multiplicity $\barν$ and the average heavy-fragment mass $\langle A_h\rangle$ of the input mass yields $\partial\barν/\partial\langle A_h\rangle = \pm 0.1\,n/f/\mathrm{u}$. The mass peak width influences the correlation between the total kinetic energy of the fission fragments and the total number of prompt neutrons emitted $\barν_T(\mathrm{TKE})$. Typical biases on prompt particle observables from using calculated mass yields instead of experimental ones are: $δ\barν = 4\%$ for the average prompt neutron multiplicity, $δ\bar{M}_γ= 1\%$ for the average prompt $γ$-ray multiplicity, $δ\barε_n^\mathrm{LAB} = 1\%$ for the average outgoing neutron energy, $δ\barε_γ= 1\%$ for the average $γ$-ray energy, and $δ\langle\mathrm{TKE}\rangle = 0.4\%$ for the average total kinetic energy of the fission fragments.

## Key claim

Using calculated macroscopic-microscopic mass yields in Hauser-Feshbach de-excitation calculations introduces typical biases on prompt fission observables of 4% for average prompt neutron multiplicity and 1% or less for gamma-ray observables, but these biases depend on the specific mass yield characteristics such as peak location and width.

## Method

The authors combined mass yields from macroscopic-microscopic potential energy surface models (Brownian shape-motion and Langevin approaches) with the CGMF Monte Carlo implementation of Hauser-Feshbach statistical decay theory. They performed calculations for thermal neutron-induced fission of 235U and 239Pu using both experimental mass yields from multiple sources and calculated yields from Moller (2015) and Sierk (2017), fitting all yields to a three-Gaussian parameterization. The fragment de-excitation was simulated with fixed average total kinetic energy (TKE) values of 171.40 MeV for 235U and 177.93 MeV for 239Pu, using Wahl systematics for charge distributions and adjusting the spin-scaling factor and TKE variance scaling to match experimental gamma-ray and neutron multiplicity data.

## Context

This work extends the approach of combining fission fragment yield calculations with de-excitation models, building on the Brownian shape-motion model of Randrup (2011) and Moller (2015) and the Langevin approach of Sierk (2017). It connects these yield calculations to the Hauser-Feshbach statistical decay framework implemented in CGMF by Becker (2013) and Kawano (2010). The results demonstrate that calculated mass yields can reproduce gamma-ray observables reasonably well but produce larger discrepancies in neutron observables, suggesting that theoretical yields may be satisfactory for astrophysical r-process applications where experimental data are unavailable, while also showing that precise ν̄ measurements could constrain ⟨TKE⟩ uncertainties by about a factor of three.
