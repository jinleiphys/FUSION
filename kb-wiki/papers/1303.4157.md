# A finite-volume matrix Hamiltonian model for a Delta -> nucleon-pion system

> A matrix Hamiltonian model is developed to address the finite-volume effects appearing in studies of baryon resonances in lattice QCD. The Hamiltonian model includes interaction terms in a transparent way, and can be readily generalized to address multi-channel problems. The eigenvalue equation of the model is exactly solvable, and can be matched onto chiral effective field theory. The model is investigated in the case of Delta -> nucleon-pion scattering. A robust method for determining the resonance parameters from lattice QCD is developed. It involves constraining the free parameters of the model based on the lattice spectrum in question. The method is tested in the context of a set of pseudodata, and a picture of the model-dependence is obtained by examining a variety of regularization schemes in the model. A comparison is made with the Luescher method, and it is found that the matrix Hamiltonian method is equally robust. Both methods are tested in a more realistic scenario, where a background interaction corresponding to direct nucleon-pion scattering is incorporated into the pseudodata. The resulting extraction of the resonance parameters associated with the Delta baryon resonance provides evidence that an effective field theory style of approach yields a successful realization of finite-volume effects in the context of baryon resonances.

## Key claim

The matrix Hamiltonian model provides a method for extracting resonance parameters from lattice QCD finite-volume spectra that is equally robust as the Lüscher method, and can improve upon it when the regularization scale is treated as a fit parameter, even when nonresonant background interactions are present in the data.

## Method

A finite-volume matrix Hamiltonian is constructed with diagonal entries representing the bare Delta resonance energy and discrete pion kinetic energies in a cubic box, and off-diagonal couplings between the bare Delta and N-pi states derived from chiral effective field theory. The eigenvalue equation is solved exactly, yielding a one-loop renormalization formula that matches the continuum chiral EFT result when the sum over discrete momenta is replaced by an integral. The free parameters (bare resonance energy Delta_0 and coupling coefficient chi_Delta) are constrained by fitting the model's energy eigenvalues to lattice QCD spectra (or pseudodata), and the resonance parameters are then extracted from the infinite-volume t-matrix.

## Key numbers

* The phenomenological Delta resonance energy is E_res^phys = 292 MeV, used as the target value for the infinite-volume model.
* The pion decay constant is f_pi = 92.4 MeV, and the SU(6) flavour-symmetry parameter is C = -1.52, used to define the coupling coefficient chi_Delta.
* A dipole regulator with scale Lambda = 0.8 GeV is used as the default regularization scheme.
* Finite-volume corrections from applying the Lüscher method to the Hamiltonian model are insignificant for box sizes L >= 3 fm at the physical pion mass.
* For box sizes where a discrete energy eigenvalue lies near the resonance position, the experimental value of E_res is attained within a few MeV.
* Fitted values of the Gaussian regulator scale Lambda are typically close to 0.6 GeV for the simple model, and closer to 0.5 GeV when background interactions are present.

## Context

This work extends the finite-volume formalism of Lüscher (1990) by providing an alternative Hamiltonian-based approach that can be matched onto chiral effective field theory, as developed by Jenkins and collaborators (1991) and others. The method enables extraction of resonance parameters from lattice QCD calculations of baryon resonances, such as those by Edwards (2011), Engel (2010), and Mahbub (2010), and can be generalized to multichannel problems. The analysis of eigenvectors demonstrates that in the light-quark regime, no single eigenstate can be identified as purely single-particle or multiparticle, contradicting earlier heavy-quark-mass domain assumptions.
