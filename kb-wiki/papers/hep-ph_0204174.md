# Polyakov Loops versus Hadronic States

> The order parameter for the pure Yang-Mills phase transition is the Polyakov loop which encodes the symmetries of the Z_N center of the SU(N) gauge group. On the other side the physical degrees of freedom of any asymptotically free gauge theory are hadronic states. Using the Yang-Mills trace anomaly and the exact Z_N symmetry we construct a model able to communicate to the hadrons the information carried by the order parameter.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a model that couples the Polyakov loop (the order parameter for the deconfinement phase transition in pure Yang-Mills theory) to the glueball field (the hadronic state), using the Yang-Mills trace anomaly and exact Z_N symmetry, to show that the information about the phase transition is communicated to the physical hadronic states. The model is presented as a tree-level effective theory with unknown temperature-dependent coefficients that should be fit to lattice data, and the author explicitly notes it "cannot be the full story" since it neglects the tower of excited glueballs, other gauge-invariant operators, and thermal corrections.

## Method

The author constructs an effective potential V[H, ℓ] for the glueball field H (mass dimension 4) and the Polyakov loop ℓ (dimensionless), combining the zero-temperature glueball potential from the trace anomaly with a Z_N-invariant polynomial potential for ℓ and an interaction term H P[ℓ]. The equations of motion are solved for SU(2) and SU(3) Yang-Mills theories using mean-field approximations with simplified temperature-dependent coefficients (a₁ = α(T_* - T)/T, with a₂, a₃, b₁ taken as positive constants), and the resulting temperature dependence of ⟨ℓ⟩ and ⟨H⟩ is analyzed near the critical temperature.

## Key numbers

- For SU(2): the zero-temperature glueball vacuum expectation value is ⟨H⟩ = Λ⁴/e, where Λ is the confining scale.
- For SU(2): the critical temperature satisfies T_c = T_* + (b₁/(eα))(Λ⁴/T_c³).
- For SU(2) near T_c: ⟨ℓ⟩² = (α/(2a₂)) × (1 + 3(b₁/(eα))(Λ⁴/T_c⁴))/(1 - (b₁²/(e a₂))(Λ⁴/T_c⁴)) × (ΔT/T_c).
- For SU(3): the critical temperature is T_c = [T_* + (b₁/(eα))(Λ⁴/T_c³)] × (α a₂)/(α a₂ + a₃²).
- For SU(3) just above T_c: ⟨|ℓ|⟩ = a₃/a₂, and ⟨H⟩ = (Λ⁴/e) exp[-2b₁(a₃/a₂)²].

## Context

This work extends the Polyakov Loop Model (PLM) of Pisarski (2000, 2001) by coupling the order parameter to the glueball field via the trace anomaly, and it supports the phenomenological investigations of Scavenius, Dumitru, and Jackson (2001) and Scavenius, Dumitru, and Lenaghan (2002) aimed at understanding RHIC physics. The model enables lattice tests of the predicted exponential drop of the gluon condensate at the deconfinement transition and the transfer of information about the order of the phase transition (second order for SU(2), weak first order for SU(3)) to the hadronic sector.
