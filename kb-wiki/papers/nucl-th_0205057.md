# Evaluation of QCD sum rules for light vector mesons at finite density and temperature

> QCD sum rules are evaluated at finite nucleon densities and temperatures to determine the change of mass parameters for the lightest vector mesons $ρ$, $ω$ and $ϕ$ in a strongly interacting medium. For conditions relevant for the starting experiments at HADES we find that the in-medium mass shifts of the $ρ$ and $ω$ mesons are governed, within the Borel QCD sum rule approach, by the density and temperature dependence of the four-quark condensate. In particular, the variation of the strength of the density dependence of the four-quark condensate reflects directly the decreasing mass of the $ρ$ meson and can lead to a change of the sign of the $ω$ meson mass shift as a function of the density. In contrast, the in-medium mass of the $ϕ$ meson is directly related to the chiral strange quark condensate which seems correspondingly accessible.

## Key claim

Within the Borel QCD sum rule approach, the in-medium mass shifts of the ρ and ω mesons at finite nucleon density and temperature are governed by the density and temperature dependence of the four-quark condensate, while the φ meson mass shift is directly related to the chiral strange quark condensate; the sign of the ω meson mass shift can change as a function of density depending on the strength of the four-quark condensate's density dependence.

## Method

The authors evaluate QCD sum rules at finite nucleon chemical potential μ_N and temperature T using the retarded current-current correlation function in the limit of vanishing three-momentum. The operator product expansion (OPE) is performed up to dimension-6 operators, with condensates evaluated via a dilute gas approximation of non-interacting nucleons and pions using the grand canonical ensemble average. The hadronic spectral function is modeled as a sum of a Landau damping term, a resonance contribution, and a perturbative QCD continuum, and the mass parameter m_V is extracted from the Borel-transformed sum rule using a sliding Borel window determined by the "10% + 50%" rule.

## Key numbers

- The density dependence of m_ρ can be approximated by m_ρ = m_ρ^0 (1 - a_ρ n_N / n_0) with a_ρ ≈ 0.16, remaining constant within 30% accuracy for temperatures 0 ≤ T ≤ 140 MeV (using κ_N = 1.4, κ_0 = 3.3).
- The vacuum pion decay constant f_π = 93 MeV is used in the soft pion theorem.
- Normal nuclear matter density is taken as n_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}.
- The scalar quark condensate at small temperatures can be approximated as 1 - α n/n_0 with α ≈ 0.32 for light quarks, α ≈ 0.09 for strange quarks, and α ≈ 0.08 for the gluon condensate.
- The Borel window for ρ and ω mesons uses M_min = 0.8 GeV^2 and M_max = 1.5 GeV^2; for the φ meson M_max = 2.5 GeV^2.
- For the ω meson, the sign of the mass shift changes at κ_N ≈ 2 (with κ_0 = 3.0): for κ_N < 2 the mass increases with density, for κ_N > 2 it drops.

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule evaluations of Hatsuda and Lee (1992) and Hatsuda, Lee, and Shiomi (1995) from cold nucleon matter or hot pion gas to the combined case of finite baryon density and temperature relevant for the HADES experiment at GSI. The results are consistent with the Eletsky-Ioffe mixing theorem (1995) for the ρ meson at finite temperature, and the ω meson behavior at T = 0 agrees with the findings of Abee (unreferenced in text). The study enables predictions for dilepton measurements in heavy-ion collisions at SIS energies, where both density and temperature effects must be considered simultaneously.
