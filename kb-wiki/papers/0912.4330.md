# Comparative study of hyperon-nucleon interactions of quark model and chiral effective field theory by low-momentum equivalent interactions and $G$ matrices

> Hyperon-nucleons interactions constructed by two frameworks, the Kyoto-Niigata SU$_6$ quark model and the chiral effective field theory, are compared by investigating equivalent interactions in a low-momentum space and in addition by calculating hyperon single-particle potentials in the lowest-order Brueckner theory in symmetric nuclear matter. Two descriptions are shown to give similar matrix elements in most channels after renormalizing high momentum components. Although the range of the $ΛN$ interaction is different in two potentials, the $Λ$ single-particle potential in nuclear matter is very similar. The $Σ$-nucleus and $Ξ$-nucleus potentials are also found to be similar. These predictions are to be confronted with forthcoming experimental data.

# Comparative study of hyperon-nucleon interactions of quark model and chiral effective field theory by low-momentum equivalent interactions and $G$ matrices

## Key claim
The Kyoto-Niigata SU$_6$ quark-model potential fss2 and the leading-order chiral effective field theory potential produce similar hyperon single-particle potentials in symmetric nuclear matter for $\Lambda$, $\Sigma$, and $\Xi$ hyperons, despite differences in the short-range description and in the momentum dependence of the low-momentum equivalent interactions in some channels. The similarity holds after renormalizing high-momentum components, but the chiral EFT potential predicts an opposite sign for the $\Lambda$ spin-orbit Scheerbaum factor compared to the quark model.

## Method
The author constructed low-momentum equivalent interactions with a cutoff $\Lambda = 2.0$ fm$^{-1}$ for the $^1S_0$ and $^3S_1$ partial waves of $\Lambda N$, $\Sigma N$, and $\Xi N$ systems, using the energy-independent version of the quark-model potential fss2 and the chiral EFT potential with a cutoff mass of 600 MeV. Hyperon single-particle potentials in symmetric nuclear matter were calculated in the lowest-order Brueckner theory with the continuous prescription for intermediate spectra, using the nucleon single-particle potential from fss2 for the chiral EFT calculations to focus on the hyperon-nucleon interaction properties.

## Key numbers
- Cutoff for low-momentum equivalent interactions: $\Lambda = 2.0$ fm$^{-1}$
- Chiral EFT regularization cutoff mass: 600 MeV
- Scheerbaum factor $S_\Lambda$ for $\Lambda$ spin-orbit potential in normal symmetric nuclear matter ($k_F = 1.35$ fm$^{-1}$): $-15.4$ MeV$\cdot$fm$^5$ for NSC97f, $-12.2$ MeV$\cdot$fm$^5$ for fss2, and $+4.8$ MeV$\cdot$fm$^5$ for chiral EFT
- Empirical value of $S_\Lambda$ needed to explain experimental data: approximately $-3.2$ MeV$\cdot$fm$^5$
- Hypertriton binding energies: $-2.30$ MeV for NSC97f, $-2.487$ MeV for fss2, and $-2.34$ MeV for chiral EFT, compared with empirical value $-2.354 \pm 0.050$ MeV
- Fermi momenta considered for single-particle potentials: $k_F = 0.75$ to $1.45$ fm$^{-1}$

## Context
This work extends a previous comparison by Kohno and collaborators (2006) of low-momentum equivalent interactions for the Nijmegen OBEP nsc97f and the Kyoto-Niigata quark-model potential fss2 for $\Lambda N$ and $\Sigma N$ interactions, now including the chiral EFT potential developed by the Julich group (Polinder, Haidenbauer, and Meissner, 2005-2007). The study enables future confrontation with experimental data from J-PARC, particularly for $\Sigma$ and $\Xi$ hypernuclear bound states and production spectra. The unresolved problem of the small $\Lambda$ spin-orbit splitting in nuclei is highlighted, where the quark model's antisymmetric spin-orbit component does not provide sufficient cancellation, while the leading-order chiral EFT gives the opposite sign.
