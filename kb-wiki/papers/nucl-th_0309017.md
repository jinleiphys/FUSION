# Role of in-medium hadrons in photon-nucleus reactions : shadowing and dilepton spectrum

> We study the effect of in-medium hadronic properties in photon nucleus interactions in the context of shadowing as well as the dilepton spectrum for incident photon energies in the range 1.1- 3 GeV. A reasonable agreement with the experimental data for shadowing is obtained in a scenario of downward spectral shift of the hadrons. We show that distinguishable features for in-medium changes of the hadronic properties can be observed experimentally through the dilepton spectrum by judicious choice of target nuclei and incident energy of photons.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a downward spectral shift of hadron masses (universal scaling scenario) provides a reasonable description of photonuclear shadowing data for incident photon energies between 1.1 and 3 GeV, and that the dilepton spectrum from photon-nucleus reactions can serve as a less ambiguous probe of in-medium hadron modifications than heavy-ion collisions, provided one makes a judicious choice of target nucleus and incident photon energy.

## Method

The authors use the Vector Meson Dominance (VMD) model combined with a Glauber multiple-scattering formalism to compute the nuclear photoabsorption cross section (shadowing) for carbon and lead targets. They then evaluate the dilepton invariant mass spectrum from vector meson decay using t-channel exchange diagrams (π and σ exchange) for ρ, ω, and φ photoproduction, incorporating density-dependent hadron masses via two models: universal scaling (USS) and Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD). The propagation of vector mesons inside the nucleus is treated by tracking the production point and decay point separately, accounting for collisional broadening and the density-dependent mass and width of the mesons.

## Key numbers

- Incident photon energy range considered: 1.1–3 GeV.
- Universal scaling relation: m_H*/m_H = 1 – 0.2x, where x = n(r)/n_0 and n_0 ≈ 0.15 fm⁻³.
- QHD parametrization coefficients for ρ meson: a₁ = –1.30966, a₂ = 1.78784, a₃ = –1.17524, a₄ = 0.294456.
- QHD parametrization coefficients for ω meson: a₁ = –0.470454, a₂ = 0.313825, a₃ = –0.0731274.
- QHD parametrization coefficients for nucleon: a₁ = –0.351277, a₂ = 0.0766239.
- Coupling constants: g_ω = 5.03, g_φ = 13.25, g_ρ = 17.05 (from e⁺e⁻ decay widths).
- g_{ωρπ} = 1.92 (from Γ_{ω→π⁰γ} = 715 keV).
- g_{φρπ} = 0.097 (from φ radiative decay).
- g_{σρρ} = 4.6 (from ρ→π⁺π⁻γ decay).
- g_{σππ} = 17.6 (with m_σ = 500 MeV, Γ_{σ→ππ} = 300 MeV).
- Cut-off parameters: Λ_π = 0.7 GeV, Λ_σ = 1 GeV, Λ_{σρρ} = 0.9 GeV.
- Threshold photon energy for ρ production: E₀ = 1.1 GeV (for m_ρ = 770 MeV).
- Average number of collisions for vector mesons in Pb: 3; in C: 1.6 (at E_γ = 1.1 GeV and 2 GeV).
- Two-body correlation parameter: q_c = 780 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of in-medium hadron modifications in heavy-ion collisions (CERES, HELIOS-3, NA50 collaborations) to the cleaner environment of photon-nucleus reactions, where initial-state interactions are absent. It contrasts with the interpretation of shadowing by Mosel (2000) using Glauber theory with a negative real part of the ρ-N amplitude, and with the broadening-only scenario of Rapp (1997), Cassing (1998), and Friman (1999). The results enable experimental tests at CEBAF (G7 collaboration) using deuterium, carbon, iron, and lead targets, where the dilepton spectrum can distinguish between mass-shift and broadening scenarios.
