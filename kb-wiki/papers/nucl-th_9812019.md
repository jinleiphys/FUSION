# Cascade in Muonic and Pionic Atoms with Z=1

## Key claim

Recent theoretical and experimental studies of exotic atoms with Z=1 are reviewed, with the central claim that an interplay between atomic internal and external degrees of freedom is essential for a good description of the atomic cascade. The paper outlines the perspective of *ab initio* cascade calculations but notes that current models still require tuning parameters and ad hoc assumptions, and that some mechanism may still be missing in the cascade model (e.g., the resonant molecular formation in excited states).

## Method

The paper reviews the cascade mechanisms in muonic and pionic hydrogen atoms, including radiative transitions, external Auger effect, Stark mixing, elastic scattering, Coulomb transitions, and transfer (isotope exchange). It compares various cascade models (e.g., Leon and Bethe 1962, Borie and Leon 1980, Markushin 1981, 1994, Aschenauer et al. 1995, Aschenauer and Markushin 1997) that differ in whether they include Coulomb de-excitation, elastic scattering, and explicit energy evolution. The review analyzes X-ray yield measurements, kinetic energy distributions from Doppler broadening, and the Q1S factor in hydrogen-deuterium mixtures to test the models against experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The hydrogen density at liquid hydrogen density (LHD) is N0 = 4.3 x 10^22 cm^-3.
- The ratio Y(K_alpha)/Y(K_beta) in muonic hydrogen at phi=1 (LHD) is calculated as 15 without Coulomb de-excitation, compared to the experimental value 19.9 +/- 2.5 (La98).
- At phi=0.08, the theoretical ratio without Coulomb transitions is 2.0, while the experimental ratio is 6.4 +/- 1.3 (La98).
- The effective radiative transition rate ratio lambda^gamma_(3->2)/lambda^gamma_(3->1) = 0.79.
- The Auger and Coulomb de-excitation rates from n=3 to n=2, when combined, give the factor 14.6 phi (1 + lambda^Coulomb/lambda^Auger) in the ratio formula.
- A kinetic energy T=0.5 eV corresponds to a Doppler broadening of the K_beta line delta_Gamma = 0.1 eV, which is about 10% of the nuclear width Gamma_1S = 1 eV for pionic hydrogen.
- The Coulomb de-excitation produces atoms with kinetic energy E >= 50 eV that are not significantly decelerated before nuclear reaction; atoms with E <= 20 eV experience important deceleration.
- The Q1S factor (probability that a muon captured by hydrogen in HD mixture reaches the mu p ground state) shows a dependence on deuterium fraction that requires scaling the transfer rates by a factor of about 0.5 to match experiment (Aschenauer and Markushin 1997).

## Context

This review extends earlier reviews by Leon and Bethe (1962), Markushin (1990, 1994), and Kottmann (1992), focusing on recent progress in theoretical studies of the atomic cascade in light muonic and pionic atoms. The work enables precision X-ray spectroscopy of the pi-p atom, where Doppler-broadening corrections must be accounted for to measure the nuclear width of the 1S state, and supports new generation experiments with mu p and pi-p atoms (Simons 1998) that will test theoretical methods for reactions with excited exotic atoms. The results are also applicable to other systems such as K-p and antiproton-proton atoms.
