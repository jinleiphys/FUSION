# Spin measurements for 147Sm+n resonances: Further evidence for non-statistical effects

> We have determined the spins J of resonances in the 147Sm(n,gamma) reaction by measuring multiplicities of gamma-ray cascades following neutron capture. Using this technique, we were able to determine J values for all but 14 of the 140 known resonances below En = 1 keV, including 41 firm J assignments for resonances whose spins previously were either unknown or tentative. These new spin assignments, together with previously determined resonance parameters, allowed us to extract separate level spacings and neutron strength functions for J = 3 and 4 resonances. Furthermore, several statistical test of the data indicate that very few resonances of either spin have been missed below En = 700eV. Because a non-statistical effect recently was reported near En = 350 eV from an analysis of 147Sm(n,alpha) data, we divided the data into two regions; 0 < En < 350 eV and 350 < En < 700 eV. Using neutron widths from a previous measurement and published techniques for correcting for missed resonances and for testing whether data are consistent with a Porter-Thomas distribution, we found that the reduced-neutron-width distribution for resonances below 350 eV is consistent with the expected Porter-Thomas distribution. On the other hand, we found that reduced-neutron-width data in the 350 < En < 700 eV region are inconsistent with a Porter-Thomas distribution, but in good agreement with a chi-squared distribution having two or more degrees of freedom. We discuss possible explanations for these observed non-statistical effects and their possible relation to similar effects previously observed in other nuclides.

## Key claim

The reduced-neutron-width distribution for 147Sm+n resonances below 350 eV is consistent with the expected Porter-Thomas distribution, but the distribution in the 350 to 700 eV region is inconsistent with Porter-Thomas and instead agrees with a chi-squared distribution having two or more degrees of freedom, indicating a non-statistical effect near 350 eV.

## Method

The spins J of resonances in the 147Sm(n,gamma) reaction were determined by measuring gamma-ray cascade multiplicities using the DANCE detector array at LANSCE, a 4pi array of 160 BaF2 crystals. A new technique using linear combinations of multiplicity distributions was employed to assign spins even for partially-resolved resonances, yielding firm J assignments for 126 of the 140 known resonances below En = 1 keV. Using these spin assignments together with previously determined resonance parameters, the authors extracted separate level spacings and neutron strength functions for J = 3 and J = 4 resonances, and tested the reduced-neutron-width distributions against Porter-Thomas and chi-squared distributions in two energy regions divided at 350 eV.

## Key numbers

- Level spacing for J = 3 resonances: D0,3 = 12.99 ± 0.93 eV (from linear fit to cumulative number below 700 eV)
- Level spacing for J = 4 resonances: D0,4 = 12.38 ± 0.85 eV (from linear fit to cumulative number below 700 eV)
- Neutron strength function for J = 3: 10^4 S0,3 = 4.70 ± 0.91 (from cumulative reduced neutron widths to 1 keV)
- Neutron strength function for J = 4: 10^4 S0,4 = 4.93 ± 0.92 (from cumulative reduced neutron widths to 1 keV)
- Delta3 values for both spin states below 700 eV: 0.40, in agreement with expected value of 0.40 ± 0.11
- 41 firm J assignments were made for resonances whose spins were previously unknown or tentative
- Only 14 of 140 known resonances below 1 keV remain without firm J assignments (9 below 700 eV)

## Context

This work extends the spin measurements of Gersch (1993) for 147Sm+n resonances, using the higher flux and improved detector capabilities of DANCE to assign spins to 41 previously unknown or tentative resonances. The results directly address the surprising non-statistical effects observed in the alpha-width distributions and the abrupt decrease in the 3- to 4- strength function ratio near 300 eV reported by Koehler (2004) from analysis of 147Sm(n,alpha) data. The finding that reduced-neutron-width distributions deviate from Porter-Thomas above 350 eV provides further evidence for non-statistical effects in this nuclide and enables more reliable tests of nuclear models used to calculate astrophysical reaction rates.
