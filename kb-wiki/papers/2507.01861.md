# Pentaquarks made of light quarks and their admixture to baryons

> This paper is a continuation of our studies of multiquark hadrons. The anti-symmetrization of their wavefunctions required by Fermi statistics is nontivial, as it mixes orbital, color, spin and flavor structures. In our previous papers we developed a method to find them based on the representations of the permutation group, and derived the explicit wave functions for baryons excited to the first and second shells $(L=1,2)$, tetraquarks $qq\bar q\bar q$ and hexaquarks ($6q$). Now we apply it to light pentaquarks ($qqqq\bar q$), in the S- and P-shells ($L=0,1$). Using Jacobi coordinates, one can use the hyperdistance approximation in 12-dimensional space. We further address the issue of ``unquenching" of baryons, by considering their mixing with pentaquarks, via two channels, through the addition of $σ$-like or $π$-like $\bar q q$ pairs. This mixing is central for understanding of the observed flavor asymmetry of the antiquark sea, the amount of orbital motion issue as well as other nucleon properties.

## Key claim

The paper derives explicit wave functions for light pentaquarks (qqqqq̅) in the S-shell (L=0) and P-shell (L=1) using permutation group methods, and argues that mixing between three-quark baryons and these pentaquark states, via addition of σ-like or π-like q̅q pairs, is central to understanding the flavor asymmetry of the antiquark sea and orbital motion in nucleons, with the caveat that no firm association of the derived states with identified experimental resonances is yet established.

## Method

The authors construct antisymmetric wave functions for pentaquarks by enforcing Fermi statistics through representations of the permutation group S₄ for the four-quark core, using Jacobi coordinates to eliminate center-of-mass motion and applying a hyperdistance approximation in 12-dimensional space. The radial wave functions are obtained by solving the Schrödinger equation with a Cornell-type potential averaged over angles, using a light quark mass of 0.35 GeV. The mixing between three-quark baryons and pentaquarks is treated via two channels corresponding to addition of σ-like and π-like q̅q pairs, enforcing chiral symmetry.

## Key numbers

- The five lowest L=0 pentaquark energy splittings relative to the ground state: 0.32, 0.62, 0.89, 1.16 GeV
- The L=1 radial equation includes a centrifugal term 10/Y², giving splitting E₂(L=1) − E₁(L=1) = 0.30 GeV
- The ground state pentaquark wave function peaks at Y ≈ 10 GeV⁻¹ ≈ 2 fm
- For the maximal spin S=5/2, I=1/2 pentaquark state: ⟨∑_{i>j} λᵢ·λⱼ⟩ = −40/3 and ⟨∑_{i>j} (λᵢ·λⱼ)(Sᵢ·Sⱼ)⟩ = −10/3
- For the S=5/2, I=1/2 state: ⟨I_z(q)⟩ = 1/4, ⟨I_z(q̅)⟩ = −1/2
- The color-spin Hamiltonian eigenvalues H_{λσ}/C_{λσ} for I=1/2 states: −4.66, −1.44, 2.77 (S=1/2 triplet); −3, 1/3, 10/3 (S=3/2 triplet); 10/3 (S=5/2 singlet)
- The S=5/2, I=1/2 pentaquark state has 708 nonzero terms in monom representation; the S=5/2, I=3/2 state has 252 nonzero terms
- The 13 L=1, S=1/2, I=1/2 states used for nucleon mixing have 187200 nonzero components together

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of multiquark hadrons (Miesch, Shuryak, Zahed, 2024) where they developed permutation group methods for baryons excited to L=1,2 shells, tetraquarks, and hexaquarks. It contrasts with the "unquenched constituent quark model" of Santopinto (2007) and Bijker (2009) by deriving pentaquark wave functions solely through Fermi statistics rather than combinations of color-spin-flavor group representations, and differs from the approach of An (2019) by enforcing chiral symmetry in the mixing of nucleon and pentaquark states. The results enable bridging between hadronic spectroscopy and partonic observables such as PDFs and GPDs, with the intermediate description at Q² ∼ 1 GeV² intended to connect chiral evolution from the left and pQCD evolution from the right.
