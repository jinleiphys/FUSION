# Comment on B. Davids et al.; Proton-Decaying States in 22Mg and the Nucleosynthesis of 22Na in Novae

> B.S. Davids {\em et al.} make the explicit assumption that radiative widths of analog states in \ne22 and \mg22 are equal. We demonstrate that this misapplication of iso-spin symmetry leads to very wrong results. Considerations of elementary nuclear structure suggests that such an assumption can be inaccurate by a large factor (in \mg22), as is evident from a comparison with recent measurements of radiative width in \mg22. Estimates of radiative widths from analog transitions are common but often wrong (e.g. in \mg22) and should not be considered a useful tool in nuclear astrophysics.

# Comment on B. Davids et al.; Proton-Decaying States in 22Mg and the Nucleosynthesis of 22Na in Novae

## Key claim
The assumption by Davids et al. that radiative widths of analog states in 22Ne and 22Mg are equal is a misapplication of isospin symmetry that leads to very wrong results. Even in the limit of exact isospin symmetry, only reduced widths of specific transition types (ΔT=±1 transitions, or E1 ΔT=0 transitions) are predicted to be equal, not total radiative widths; for weak ΔT=0 E2 or M1 transitions, isospin symmetry does not predict equality of reduced widths, and Coulomb corrections can cause large deviations.

## Method
The author examines the theoretical basis for equating radiative widths of analog states, reviewing the isospin symmetry rules from Warburton (Ref. [War]) that specify which transition types have predicted equal reduced widths. The analysis compares the specific case of the 0⁺₂ state at 5.962 MeV and tentative 1⁻ states at 6.046 MeV in 22Mg with their proposed analogs in 22Ne, evaluating the multipolarities and strengths of the transitions involved. The author also cites recent measurements of resonance strengths in 22Mg from TRIUMF to demonstrate the discrepancy between assumed and measured values.

## Key numbers
- The B(E2↑: 2⁺ → 0⁺₂) in 22Ne is estimated as small as 0.001 Weisskopf units (W.u.), while a reasonable assumption of average strength (1 W.u.) for the analog transition in 22Mg would differ by a factor of 1000, with an upper limit of 10,000.
- Preliminary measurement of B(E2↑: 2⁺ → 0⁺₂) in 22Mg yields approximately 0.3 W.u., making the Davids assumption wrong by a factor of approximately 300.
- For the 1⁻ states in 22Ne, weak B(E1) transitions range between 0.008 and 0.0002 W.u.
- The measured central value of ωγ for the 5.962 MeV state in 22Mg differs from the assumed value by a factor of 4.3.
- The measured central value of ωγ for the 6.046 MeV state in 22Mg differs from the assumed value by a factor of 16.4.

## Context
This comment directly critiques the approach of Davids et al. (2003) and their earlier work (Davids et al., prior papers) which assumed equality of radiative widths between mirror nuclei for estimating resonance strengths in 22Mg. The author extends the known issue of isospin symmetry breaking in analog transitions, citing the Thomas-Ehrman shift and the work of Barker on Coulomb corrections in the 13C-13N isodoublet, where B(E1) differs by more than a factor of 3. The paper also references the textbook case of anomalous beta-decay lifetime of 14C (log ft = 9.04) versus its analog in 14O (log ft = 7.2) as an example of fine-tuned cancellations in analog matrix elements. The recent TRIUMF measurements of resonance strengths in 22Mg demonstrate that such assumptions are unreliable for nuclear astrophysics applications, particularly for nova and x-ray burst nucleosynthesis calculations.
