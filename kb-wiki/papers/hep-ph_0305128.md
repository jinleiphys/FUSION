# Upward Tau Air Showers from Earth

> We estimate the rate of observable Horizontal and Upward Tau Air-Showers (HORTAUs, UPTAUS) considering both the Earth opacity and the finite size of the terrestrial atmosphere. We calculate the effective target volumes and masses for Tau air-showers emerging from the Earth. The resulting model-independent masses for satellite experiments such as EUSO may encompass at E_nu_tau = 10^19 eV a very large volume, V= 1020 km^3. Adopting simple power law neutrino fluxes, E^-2 and E^-1, calibrated to GZK-like and Z-Burst-like models, we estimate that at E= 10^19 eV nearly half a dozen horizontal shower events should be detected by EUSO in three years of data collection by the "guaranteed" GZK neutrino flux. We also find that the equivalent mass for an Earth outer layer made of rock is dominant compared to the water, contrary to simplified all-rock/all-water Earth models and previous Montecarlo simulations. Therefore we expect an enhancement of neutrino detection along continental shelves nearby the highest mountain chains, also given the better geometrical acceptance for Earth skimming neutrinos. The Auger experiment might reveal such a signature at E_nu= 10^{18} eV (with 26 events in 3 yr) towards the Andes, if the angular resolution at the horizon (both in azimuth and zenith) would reach an accuracy of nearly one degree needed to disentangle tau air showers from common UHECR. The number of events increases at lower energies; therefore we suggest an extension of the EUSO and Auger sensitivity down to (or even below) E_nu = 10^19 eV and E_nu = 10^18 eV respectively.

## Key claim

The authors estimate that, for a "guaranteed" GZK neutrino flux, nearly half a dozen horizontal tau air-shower events should be detected by the EUSO space observatory in three years of data collection, assuming a 10% duty cycle efficiency and a minimal tau neutrino flux of 50 eV cm^-2 s^-1 sr^-1. This result depends on the assumption of a power-law neutrino flux calibrated to GZK-like models and on the specific effective volume calculation that includes the finite extension of the terrestrial atmosphere.

## Method

The authors derived an analytical formula for the effective target volume and mass for tau air-showers emerging from the Earth, considering both Earth opacity and the finite size of the terrestrial atmosphere. They calculated the probability for a tau neutrino to convert into a tau lepton within the Earth crust and for that tau to emerge and decay in the atmosphere, using column depth integrals based on the Preliminary Earth Model density profile. The effective volume was then convolved with power-law neutrino fluxes (E^-2 and E^-1) calibrated to GZK-like and Z-burst-like models to estimate event rates for the EUSO and Auger experiments.

## Key numbers

* At E_nu_tau = 10^19 eV, the model-independent effective volume for EUSO is approximately 1020 km^3.
* For the EUSO experiment, nearly half a dozen (approximately 6) horizontal tau air-shower events are expected in three years at E = 10^19 eV, assuming a minimal tau neutrino flux of 50 eV cm^-2 s^-1 sr^-1 and a 10% duty cycle.
* For the Auger experiment, 26 events in 3 years are expected at E_nu = 10^18 eV, provided the angular resolution at the horizon reaches an accuracy of nearly one degree.
* The equivalent mass for an Earth outer layer made of rock is dominant compared to water, contrary to simplified all-rock/all-water Earth models.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of tau air-shower production from Earth-skimming neutrinos (Fargion, Aiello, and Conversano 1999; Fargion 2002a; Feng et al. 2002; Bottai and Giurgola 2003; Bugaev, Montaruli, and Sokalski 2003) by introducing a compact analytical formula for effective volume that is independent of any incoming neutrino flux model. The results enable predictions for the EUSO space observatory and the Auger ground-based experiment, and suggest that neutrino detection may be enhanced along continental shelves near high mountain chains due to the dominance of rock over water in the Earth's outer layer.
