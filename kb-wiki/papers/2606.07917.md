# First determination of vector and tensor couplings from polarized $πΔ$ photoproduction

> The couplings between hadrons encode the dynamics of quantum chromodynamics. While many couplings can be calculated from decay widths, in some cases the decays are kinematically forbidden and hence are not directly accessible. We use a Regge framework to determine these couplings from high-energy polarized scattering processes. We apply this to the $πΔ$ photoproduction that was recently studied at GlueX and provide the first determination of the complete set of $NΔ$ couplings to $ρ$, $b_1$, and $a_2$.

# First determination of vector and tensor couplings from polarized πΔ photoproduction

## Key claim
The authors provide the first direct extraction of the complete set of NΔ couplings to ρ, b₁, and a₂ mesons from high-energy scattering data, using a Regge framework applied to polarized πΔ photoproduction measured at GlueX. The method is validated on the π-exchange coupling, but the extracted ρNΔ couplings differ significantly from model values used in earlier works on single pion production in NN collisions and dibaryon predictions.

## Method
The authors model s-channel helicity amplitudes for γp → πΔ as a sum of π, ρ, b₁, and a₂ Regge exchanges with factorized vertices, using generic polynomials in t for the pΔ vertex supplemented with exponential form factors. They fit the 20 free parameters simultaneously to spin density matrix elements (SDMEs) from GlueX in the Gottfried-Jackson frame and to cross-section data from SLAC, achieving χ²/dof ≈ 1.1. The s-channel amplitudes are then crossed to the t-channel using crossing relations, partial waves of definite spin and parity are constructed, kinematical singularities are removed, and the residues are extracted by continuing to the pole.

## Key numbers
- πNΔ coupling constant extracted as f_{πNΔ} = -2.18 ± 0.08, yielding Δ(1232) decay width Γ_{Δ→pπ} = 129 ± 9 MeV, consistent with the PDG value.
- ρNΔ couplings: g^{(1)}_{ρNΔ} = 33 ± 10, g^{(2)}_{ρNΔ} = -522 ± 82, g^{(3)}_{ρNΔ} = 53 ± 19.
- b₁NΔ couplings: g^{(1)}_{b₁NΔ} = -1.1 ± 1.9, g^{(2)}_{b₁NΔ} = -199 ± 98, g^{(3)}_{b₁NΔ} = 66 ± 37.
- a₂NΔ couplings: g^{(1)}_{a₂NΔ} = -69 ± 27, g^{(2)}_{a₂NΔ} = 627 ± 151, g^{(3)}_{a₂NΔ} = 53 ± 40, g^{(4)}_{a₂NΔ} = 6.6 ± 5.0.
- The fit to GlueX SDMEs and SLAC cross section gave χ²/dof ≈ 1.1 with 20 free parameters.

## Context
This work extends the program of extracting hadronic couplings from high-energy scattering, which previously relied on quark models (Arenhövel, 1975) or effective Lagrangian approaches (Gotsman, 1969; Barbour, 1974; Clark, 1977; Nam, 2011; Yu, 2016) in the absence of polarized data. The recent availability of high-precision SDMEs from GlueX (2024) enables the first direct extraction of t-channel Regge residues from data. The ρNΔ couplings obtained differ significantly from the assumptions g^{(1)}_{ρNΔ} = g^{(3)}_{ρNΔ} = 0 used in earlier works on single pion production in NN collisions (Engel, 1996; Janssen, 1996; Ronchen, 2012) and in dibaryon predictions (Mulders, 1980; Li, 2000; Valcarce, 2005; Lu, 2020). This method establishes high-energy production reactions as a quantitative source for resonance couplings that are kinematically forbidden in decays and inaccessible to conventional low-energy partial wave analyses.
