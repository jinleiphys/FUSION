# The Pion-Nucleon Coupling Constant in QCD Sum Rules

> The pion-nucleon coupling constant $g_{πN}$ is studied on the basis of the QCD sum rules. Both the Borel sum rules and the finite energy sum rules for $g_{πN}$ are used to examine the effects of higher dimensional operators (up to dim. 7) and $α_s$ corrections in the operator product expansion. Agreement with the experimental number is reached only when $S_π/S_N$ is greater than one, where $S_π$ ($S_N$) is the continuum threshold for the $g_{πN}$ (nucleon) sum rule.

## Key claim

The pion-nucleon coupling constant $g_{\pi N}$ can be reproduced from QCD sum rules only when the continuum threshold for the $g_{\pi N}$ sum rule ($S_\pi$) is greater than the continuum threshold for the nucleon sum rule ($S_N$), i.e., $S_\pi/S_N > 1$, even after including higher-dimensional operators up to dimension 7 and $\alpha_s$ corrections.

## Method

The authors used both Borel sum rules and finite energy sum rules (FESR) for the two-point correlation function of the Ioffe nucleon current with a soft pion, evaluating the operator product expansion (OPE) up to dimension 7 operators with $\alpha_s$ corrections. They determined the continuum thresholds $S_N$ and $S_\pi$ from FESR analyses of the nucleon and pion-nucleon systems respectively, then examined the Borel stability of two sum rule expressions for $g_{\pi N}$ (denoted $g_{\pi N}^{even}$ and $g_{\pi N}^{odd}$) as functions of the Borel mass $M^2$.

## Key numbers

- The experimental value $g_{\pi N} = 13.4 \pm 0.1$ is obtained empirically from N-N scattering, $\pi$-N scattering, and deuteron properties.
- For the nucleon sum rule, using $\langle \bar{q}q \rangle = -(0.2402\ \text{GeV})^3$ and $\langle \frac{\alpha_s}{\pi} G^2 \rangle = 0.012\ \text{GeV}^4$, the FESR gives $S_N = 1.60\ \text{GeV}^2$, $\lambda_N = 0.0235\ \text{GeV}^3$, and $M_N = 0.940\ \text{GeV}$.
- The physical range for $S_N$ is $1.34 - 1.77\ \text{GeV}^2$, corresponding to $\langle \bar{q}q \rangle$ values from $-(0.225\ \text{GeV})^3$ to $-(0.250\ \text{GeV})^3$.
- For the $g_{\pi N}$ sum rule, the FESR yields $S_\pi = 1.85\ \text{GeV}^2$ with $\langle \bar{q}q \rangle = -(0.240\ \text{GeV})^3$, and $S_\pi = 1.98\ \text{GeV}^2$ with $\langle \bar{q}q \rangle = -(0.250\ \text{GeV})^3$.
- The ratio of dimension 7 to dimension 3 terms in the $g_{\pi N}$ sum rule is about 4% at $M^2 \sim 1\ \text{GeV}^2$.
- At $M^2 = 1\ \text{GeV}^2$, $g_{\pi N}^{odd}$ gives values of 11.3 to 12.8, while $g_{\pi N}^{even}$ gives 9.02 to 9.10.
- The experimental value $g_{\pi N} = 13.4$ is obtained at $M^2 = 1.6\ \text{GeV}^2$ for the parameter set with $\langle \bar{q}q \rangle = -(0.225\ \text{GeV})^3$.

## Context

This work extends the earlier QCD sum rule studies of $g_{\pi N}$ by Reznikov, Radyushkin, and Yazaki (1980s), which only considered the lowest-dimensional operator and neglected continuum thresholds and $\alpha_s$ corrections, yielding $g_{\pi N} = M_N/f_\pi$. The authors show that higher-dimensional operators and $\alpha_s$ corrections play no crucial role for Borel stability, but the ratio $S_\pi/S_N > 1$ is essential. This finding connects to the Adler-Weisberger sum rule, which requires continuum contributions in the $\pi$-N channel to obtain $g_A > 1$ (equivalently $g_{\pi N} > M_N/f_\pi$). The authors suggest that future work should include instanton contributions, which have been shown to improve nucleon Borel sum rules by Forkel and collaborators.
