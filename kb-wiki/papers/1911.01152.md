# Quark number scaling of $p_{T}$ spectra for $Ω$ and $ϕ$ in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We show that the experimental data of transverse momentum ($p_{T}$) spectra of $Ω$ baryon and $ϕ$ meson at mid-rapidity in heavy-ion collisions exhibit the constituent quark number scaling in a wide energy range from RHIC to LHC. Such a scaling behavior is a direct consequence of quark combination mechanism via equal velocity combination and provides a very convenient way to extract the $p_{T}$ spectrum of strange quarks at hadronization. We present the results of strange quarks obtained from the available data and study the properties in particular the energy dependence of the averaged transverse momentum $\langle p_{T}\rangle$ and the transverse radial flow velocity $\langleβ\rangle$ with a hydrodynamics-motivated blast-wave model.

## Key claim

The experimental data for transverse momentum spectra of $\Omega$ baryons and $\phi$ mesons in heavy-ion collisions exhibit constituent quark number scaling across a wide energy range from RHIC to LHC, but only after adjusting a constant normalization factor $\kappa_{\phi,\Omega}$ that is independent of $p_T$ but can depend on collision energy and centrality.

## Method

The authors tested quark number scaling by comparing $f_{\phi}^{1/2}(2p_T)$ with $\kappa_{\phi,\Omega} f_{\Omega}^{1/3}(3p_T)$ using published experimental data for $\Omega$ ($\Omega^- + \bar{\Omega}^+$) and $\phi$ in Au+Au collisions at RHIC and Pb+Pb collisions at LHC. They extracted the strange quark $p_T$ spectrum from the scaled hadron data and analyzed its properties using a Tsallis-Levy function fit and a hydrodynamics-motivated blast-wave model.

## Key numbers

- The average transverse momentum of strange quarks in the soft region $0 < p_T < 2$ GeV/c follows a logarithmic fit: $\langle p_T\rangle = 0.476 + 0.159 \ln[(dN_{ch}/d\eta)/(0.5N_{part})]$ GeV
- The parameter $a$ describing baryon to meson production ratio was determined as $a = 4.86$ in the light-flavor sector
- The temperature at vanishing baryon chemical potential was taken as $T_0 = 164 \pm 5$ MeV
- The curvature parameter for temperature dependence on baryon chemical potential was $c_2 = 0.0145$ from Lattice QCD calculations
- The baryon number chemical potential was parameterized as $\mu_B = 1.3075/(1 + 0.288\sqrt{s_{NN}})$ GeV

## Context

This work extends the quark number scaling previously observed in high-multiplicity $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=7$ and 13 TeV (Gou 2017, Zhang 2018) and in $p$-Pb collisions at LHC energies (Song 2017) to heavy-ion collisions across a broad energy range from $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=11.5$ to 2760 GeV. The scaling provides a method to extract strange quark $p_T$ spectra before hadronization and enables study of radial flow properties of quarks, which can be compared with blast-wave model fits to pions, kaons, and protons (Abelev 2013, Adamczyk 2017). The authors note that event generators using fragmentation mechanisms such as PYTHIA, Herwig, AMPT, and HIJING show significant deviations from this scaling behavior.
