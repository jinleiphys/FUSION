# Thermal photon production in a strongly coupled anisotropic plasma

> Photons produced in heavy ion collisions escape virtually unperturbed from the surrounding medium, thus representing an excellent probe of the conditions at the emission point. Using the gauge/gravity duality, we calculate the rate of photon production in an anisotropic, strongly coupled N=4 plasma with Nf<<Nc quark flavors. We consider arbitrary orientations of the photon momentum with respect to the anisotropic direction, as well as arbitrary values of the anisotropy. We present results for the correlation functions of two electromagnetic currents and for the electric conductivity. These quantities can be larger or smaller than the isotropic ones, depending on the direction of propagation and polarization of the photons. The total production rate is however always larger than the isotropic one, independently of the frequency, direction of propagation, and value of the anisotropy.

## Key claim
Using gauge/gravity duality, the total photon production rate in a strongly coupled anisotropic N=4 plasma is always larger than the isotropic rate, regardless of frequency, direction of propagation, and value of the anisotropy, even though individual polarization components can be either larger or smaller than their isotropic counterparts.

## Method
The authors introduce Nf << Nc massless quark flavors as probe D7-branes in the type IIB supergravity background of Mateos and Trancanelli (2011), which describes an anisotropic plasma in thermal equilibrium via a position-dependent theta-angle. They solve the linearized equations of motion for the U(1) gauge field on the D7-branes with infalling boundary conditions at the horizon, computing the retarded correlators of electromagnetic currents for arbitrary photon momentum orientations relative to the anisotropic direction. The spectral densities are obtained numerically for various values of the anisotropy parameter a/T and photon frequency.

## Key numbers
- The transition between isotropic and anisotropic scaling of entropy density occurs at approximately a/T ≈ 3.7.
- For large a/T, the entropy density scales as s ≈ 3.21 Nc^2 T^3 (a/T)^(1/3).
- The isotropic spectral density is given by χ_iso = (8 𝒩̃_D7 w) / [2^w |₂F₁(1-(1+i)w/2, -(1+i)w/2, 1-iw; 1/2)|²], where w = k₀/(2πT) and 𝒩̃_D7 = (1/4) Nc Nf T².
- The isotropic temperature is T_iso = 1/(π u_H) and isotropic entropy density is s_iso = (π²/2) Nc² T³.

## Context
This work extends the holographic calculation of photon production in isotropic N=4 plasma initiated by Caron-Huot et al. (2006) and continued by Mateos and Patino (2007) to the anisotropic case, using the regular geometry of Mateos and Trancanelli (2011) rather than the singular geometry of Janik and Witaszczyk (2008) used by Rebhan and Steineder (2011). The results enable comparison of electromagnetic signatures from anisotropic plasmas with those from isotropic plasmas at either the same temperature or the same entropy density, relevant for understanding early-time dynamics in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
