# $B_c, B^{*}_c, B_s, B^{*}_s, D_s$ and $D^{*}_s$ mass shift in a nuclear medium

> For the first time, we estimate the in-medium mass shift of the two-flavored heavy mesons $B_c, B_c^*, B_s, B_s^*, D_s$ and $D_s^*$ in symmetric nuclear matter. The estimates are made by evaluating the lowest order one-loop self-energies. The enhanced excitations of intermediate state heavy-light quark mesons in symmetric nuclear matter are the origin of their negative mass shift. Our results show that the magnitude of the mass shift for the $B_c$ meson ($\bar{b} c$ or $b \bar{c}$) is larger than those of the $η_c (\bar{c} c)$ and $η_b (\bar{b} b)$, different from a naive expectation that it would be in-between of them. While, that of the $B_c^*$ shows the in-between of the $J/ψ$ and $Υ$. We observe that the lighter vector meson excitation in each meson self-energy gives a dominant contribution for the corresponding meson mass shift, $B_c, B_s,$ and $D_s$.

## Key claim

For the first time, the in-medium mass shift of the two-flavored heavy mesons B_c, B_c^*, B_s, B_s^*, D_s and D_s^* in symmetric nuclear matter is estimated, with the origin attributed to enhanced excitations of intermediate state heavy-light quark mesons. The magnitude of the mass shift for the B_c meson is found to be larger than those of the eta_c and eta_b, contrary to the naive expectation that it would lie between them.

## Method

The estimates are made by evaluating the lowest order one-loop self-energies for each meson in symmetric nuclear matter. The in-medium mass shift is computed from the real part of the self-energy, where the intermediate state meson excitations are modified by the nuclear medium. The dominant contribution to each meson's mass shift comes from the lighter vector meson excitation in the corresponding self-energy loop.

## Key numbers

* B_c meson mass shift magnitude is larger than those of eta_c (charmonium) and eta_b (bottomonium), contrary to naive expectation
* B_c^* meson mass shift magnitude lies between those of J/psi and Upsilon
* The lighter vector meson excitation in each meson self-energy gives the dominant contribution for the corresponding meson mass shift for B_c, B_s, and D_s

## Context

This work extends previous studies of in-medium mass shifts for hidden heavy-flavor mesons such as J/psi, eta_c, Upsilon, and eta_b by Krein, Thomas, Tsushima, and collaborators (2011-2021). It also builds on the approach used by Zeminiani, Cobos-Martinez, and Tsushima (2021) for Upsilon and eta_b mass shifts. The results enable predictions for the behavior of two-flavored heavy mesons in nuclear matter, which can be tested in future heavy-ion collision experiments at facilities such as the LHC and the proposed NA60+ experiment at CERN SPS.
