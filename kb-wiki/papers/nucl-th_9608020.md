# Shell Structure of the Superheavy Elements

> Ground state properties of the superheavy elements (SHE) with Z from 108 to 128 and N from 150 to 192 are investigated using both the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock method with a density-independent contact pairing interaction and the macroscopic-microscopic approach with an average Woods-Saxon potential and a monopole pairing interaction. Detailed analysis of binding energies, separation energies, shell effects, single proton and neutron states, equilibrium deformations, alpha-decay energies, and other observables is given.

## Key claim

The paper claims that self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations favor the Z=126 spherical shell closure over the Z=114 gap for superheavy elements, but this conclusion is qualified by strong model dependence: the WS macroscopic-microscopic approach predicts Z=114 as the dominant gap, and the relative size of these gaps is sensitive to the surface diffuseness of the proton potential and the effective mass of the interaction.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale Skyrme-Hartree-Fock+BCS calculations using the SkP and SLy7 parametrizations with a density-independent contact pairing interaction, solving the equations on a three-dimensional mesh with 15^3 points and 1.0 fm spacing. For comparison, they also carried out macroscopic-microscopic calculations using a deformed Woods-Saxon single-particle potential with monopole pairing and the Yukawa-plus-exponential mass formula. Both approaches employed the Lipkin-Nogami method for approximate particle number projection, and the calculations covered nuclei with Z from 108 to 128 and N from 150 to 192, allowing for reflection-symmetric but possibly triaxial shapes.

## Key numbers

- The experimental pairing gaps in even-even nuclei around 254Fm were used to calibrate pairing strengths, yielding average pairing gap parameters δ_n = 10.304 MeV and δ_p = 13.072 MeV in the formula Δ̄_τ = δ_τ/A^{1/2}
- The SkP interaction has effective mass m*/m = 1.0, while SLy7 has m*/m = 0.67
- The semiclassical surface energy for both SkP and SLy7 is a_surf = 21.8 MeV, identical to the SkM* interaction
- In the HF+SLy7 calculation for 288112, the ground state has Q_20 = 15 b (β_2 ≈ 0.11) with a prolate shape
- The largest ground-state deformations reach β_2 = 0.26 for nuclei with Z ≤ 114 and N ≤ 162
- The largest hexadecapole distortions are β_4 ≈ -0.10 for nuclei around Z=112 and N=168
- The Z=114 gap in the HFB+Gogny calculations of Berger et al. (1996) is approximately 1.4 MeV
- The smoothing parameter for shell corrections in the WS model is 49.2/A^{1/3} MeV with a sixth-order curvature correction

## Context

This work extends previous macroscopic-microscopic calculations using Woods-Saxon potentials (Ćwiok et al., 1983-1995) and folded-Yukawa potentials (Möller et al., 1981-1995) by providing the first large-scale self-consistent description of superheavy elements including non-spherical degrees of freedom. The results contradict earlier predictions favoring Z=114 as the dominant proton magic number (Sobiczewski, 1966; Meldner, 1967; Gustafson, 1967) and instead support Z=126 as the preferred spherical shell closure, consistent with recent HFB+Gogny calculations (Berger et al., 1996) and experimental B(E2) systematics (Zamick, 1995). The work enables more reliable predictions of alpha-decay half-lives and fission barriers for superheavy elements, while highlighting the need for dynamical calculations including triaxial degrees of freedom for nuclei like 310126.
