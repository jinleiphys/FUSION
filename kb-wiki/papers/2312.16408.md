# The Transverse Energy-Energy Correlator at Next-to-Next-to-Next-to-Leading Logarithm

> We present an operator based factorization formula for the transverse energy-energy correlator in the back-to-back (dijet) region, and uncover its remarkable perturbative simplicity and relation to transverse momentum dynamics. This simplicity enables us to achieve next-to-next-to-next-to leading logarithmic (N$^3$LL) accuracy for a hadron collider dijet event shape for the first time. Our factorization formula applies to color singlet, $W/Z/γ$ + jet, and dijet production, providing a natural generalization of transverse momentum observables to one- and two-jet final states. This provides a laboratory for precision studies of QCD and transverse momentum dynamics at hadron colliders, as well as an opportunity for understanding factorization and its violation in a perturbatively well controlled setting.

## Key claim
The paper presents an operator-based factorization formula for the transverse energy-energy correlator (TEEC) in the back-to-back (dijet) region, achieving next-to-next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic (N$^3$LL) accuracy for a hadron collider dijet event shape for the first time, though the authors note that factorization violation from Glauber effects is expected at higher perturbative orders and that the factorization formula may not hold for all color configurations.

## Method
The authors derived the factorization formula using soft collinear effective theory (SCET), expressing the TEEC in the back-to-back limit as a projection of transverse momentum dependent (TMD) factorization onto a scattering plane. They incorporated the three-loop quadrupole soft anomalous dimension, three-loop rapidity anomalous dimension, four-loop cusp anomalous dimension, NNLO TMD parton distribution functions, NNLO TMD fragmentation functions, NNLO TEEC soft function, and NNLO 2-to-2 scattering amplitudes to achieve N$^3$LL resummation.

## Key numbers
- N$^3$LL accuracy achieved for a hadron collider dijet event shape for the first time
- NNLO fixed-order calculations used for TMD PDFs, TMD fragmentation functions, TEEC soft function, and 2-to-2 scattering amplitudes
- Three-loop quadrupole soft anomalous dimension from Almelid (2015, 2017)
- Three-loop rapidity anomalous dimension from Li (2016)
- Four-loop cusp anomalous dimension from Moch (2017, 2018), Davies (2016), and Henn (2019)
- ATLAS measurement imposes average $p_T \geq 250$ GeV for two leading jets and rapidity $|Y| \leq 2.5$
- The variable $\tau \equiv 1-z_\phi$ characterizes the back-to-back limit, where $z_\phi = (1-\cos\phi)/2$

## Context
This work extends the NNLL results for the TEEC presented by Gao (2019) and builds on the factorization framework for the energy-energy correlator in $e^+e^-$ collisions developed by Moult (2018) and Ebert (2020). The TEEC provides a generalization of transverse momentum ($q_T$) observables to final states with one and two jets, enabling precision studies of QCD and transverse momentum dynamics at hadron colliders. The authors note that recent NNLO calculations of hadron collider event shapes by Alvarez (2023) provide the necessary order to match to their N$^3$LL resummed result, and that the TEEC can be defined for Drell-Yan, $W/Z/\gamma$+jet, and dijet production, allowing systematic study of color flow and factorization violation across different final states.
