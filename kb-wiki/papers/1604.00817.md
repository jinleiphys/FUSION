# Liquid gas phase transition in hypernuclei

> The fragmentation of excited hypernuclear system formed in heavy ion collisions has been described by the canonical thermodynamical model extended to three component systems. The multiplicity distribution of the fragments has been analyzed in detail and it has been observed that the hyperons have the tendency to get attached to the heavier fragments. Another important observation is the phase coexistence of the hyperons, a phenomenon which is linked to liquid gas phase transition in strange matter.

# Liquid gas phase transition in hypernuclei

## Key claim
The canonical thermodynamical model extended to three components (neutrons, protons, and Λ hyperons) predicts that the "U-shaped" multiplicity distribution characteristic of liquid-gas phase coexistence in normal nuclear fragmentation persists when up to eight hyperons are attached to the projectile-like fragment, indicating that first-order phase transition also occurs in strange matter.

## Method
The authors extended the canonical thermodynamical model (CTM) to three components by including Λ hyperons alongside neutrons and protons, allowing up to eight hyperons in the fragmenting system. They computed the average multiplicities of fragments with varying baryon number, charge, and strangeness using recursion relations derived from the canonical partition function, with binding energies from experimental data for light nuclei and a liquid-drop formula for heavier species. The fragmenting source was taken as a projectile-like fragment with A₀ = 128, Z₀ = 50, and H₀ = 8 at temperatures between 3 and 10 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Fragmenting source parameters: A₀ = 128 baryons, Z₀ = 50 protons, H₀ = 8 hyperons.
- Temperatures studied: 3 MeV, 5 MeV, 7 MeV, and 10 MeV for the strangeness distribution of hyperfragments.
- At T = 3 MeV, the average mass ⟨a_h⟩ for fragments with h = 8 hyperons is approximately 5 times larger than at T = 7 MeV.
- The liquid-drop surface tension uses σ₀ = 18.0 MeV and critical temperature T_c = 18.0 MeV.
- The Λ hyperon mass used in the partition function is 1116 MeV; the nucleon mass is 938 MeV.
- The freeze-out volume is V_f = 3V₀, where V₀ is normal nuclear volume.

## Context
This work extends the canonical thermodynamical model previously applied to two-component (neutron-proton) systems by Dasgupta et al. (2014, 2015) to include multiple hyperons, going beyond the earlier restriction to only two strange particles. The "U-shaped" mass distribution and the "rise and fall" of intermediate mass fragment multiplicities are established signatures of liquid-gas phase transition in normal nuclear fragmentation, as studied by Pochodzalla (1995), Dasgupta et al. (2012), and Chaudhuri et al. (2012). The results suggest that phase coexistence persists in hypernuclear systems, which has implications for understanding strange matter in neutron star cores and for extending the nuclear chart into the strangeness sector as discussed by Schaffner et al. (1994) and Botvina et al. (2015).
