# Viscosity coefficients for hadron and quark-gluon phases

> The shear ($η$) and bulk ($ζ$) viscosities are calculated in a quasiparticle relaxation time approximation. The hadron phase is described within the relativistic mean field based model with scaled hadron masses and couplings. The quark phase is treated in terms of the heavy quark bag model fitted to the lattice data. A two-phase model allowing for the first order phase transition from the hadron phase to the strongly coupled quark gluon plasma is constructed by means of the Gibbs conditions. Temperature and baryon density dependence of the calculated viscosity-to-entropy ratios ($η/s$, $ζ/s$) are analyzed and compared with those obtained in other models. Special attention is paid to the behavior of viscosity coefficients near the critical temperature, from both hadron and quark-gluon side. Effects of resonance widths on viscosities and viscosity-to-entropy ratios are estimated.

## Key claim

The paper calculates shear and bulk viscosity coefficients for both hadronic and quark-gluon phases using a quasiparticle relaxation time approximation, with the specific condition that the two-phase model exhibits a first-order phase transition at the critical temperature, whereas lattice QCD data at zero baryon chemical potential indicate a crossover transition rather than a first-order transition.

## Method

The hadron phase is described using the relativistic mean-field based SHMC (Scaled Hadron Masses and Couplings) model with suppressed coupling constants for non-nucleon baryons, while the quark-gluon phase is treated with the heavy quark bag (HQB) model fitted to lattice QCD data for Nf=2+1 systems. A two-phase model is constructed by matching the equations of state via Gibbs conditions for thermal, mechanical, and chemical equilibrium, allowing for a first-order phase transition with a mixed phase region. Shear and bulk viscosities are then computed in the relaxation time approximation for quasiparticle collisions, including effects of resonance widths.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for the phase transition: T_c ≈ 180 MeV at zero baryon chemical potential
- Latent heat jump at T_c: Δε ∼ 0.85 GeV/fm³ (Δε/T⁴ ∼ 6)
- Quark masses used in the HQB model: m_u = m_d = 100 MeV, m_s = 450 MeV; gluon mass: m_g = 600 MeV; bag constant B = (215 MeV)⁴
- The SHMC model with suppressed couplings agrees with lattice data for pressure up to T ∼ 220–230 MeV
- The speed of sound squared c_s² exhibits a deep minimum at T ≈ T_c ≈ 180 MeV in the SHMC model

## Context

This work extends previous quasiparticle approaches to shear and bulk viscosities by Sasaki and Redlich (2008) and others, applying them to a two-phase model that combines the SHMC hadronic model (Khvorostukhin, Toneev, Voskresensky 2007, 2008) with the HQB quark model fitted to lattice data. The results enable comparison with the conjectured lower bound η/s = 1/(4π) from AdS/CFT duality (Kovtun, Son, Starinets 2003) and with lattice QCD calculations of transport coefficients near the deconfinement transition, while highlighting the need for better treatment of dynamical effects in the near-critical region.
