# Polyakov loop potential at finite density

> The Polyakov loop potential serves to distinguish between the confined hadronic and the deconfined quark-gluon plasma phases of QCD. For Nf=2+1 quark flavors with physical masses we determine the Polyakov loop potential at finite temperature and density and extract the location of the deconfinement transition. We find a cross-over at small values of the chemical potential running into a critical end-point at mu/T > 1.

## Key claim
For Nf=2+1 quark flavors with physical masses, the Polyakov loop potential at finite temperature and density shows a cross-over at small chemical potential that runs into a critical end-point at mu/T > 1, specifically at (T*, mu*) = (101 MeV, 174 MeV). The abstract's claim of a critical end-point at mu/T > 1 is qualified by the full text specifying that the cross-over sharpens with increasing chemical potential and finally turns into a first order transition at that specific point.

## Method
The Polyakov loop potential was computed from the functional Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) for the background gluon one-point function, using input from quenched lattice gluon propagators and a model ansatz for the quark-gluon vertex constrained by Slavnov-Taylor identities. The quark and gluon propagators for Nf=2+1 QCD at finite temperature and chemical potential were solved from their respective DSEs, with the gluon DSE approximated by replacing the Yang-Mills part with the inverse quenched propagator. The A0-dependence enters solely through shifted Matsubara frequencies, and the potential separates into glue and quark contributions, V(phi) = V_glue(phi) + V_quark(phi).

## Key numbers
- Critical endpoint at (T*, mu*) = (101 MeV, 174 MeV) for Nf=2+1 QCD with physical masses
- Deconfinement cross-over temperature T_conf defined by inflection point of the Polyakov loop; chiral transition temperature T_chi agrees with T_conf within the width of the cross-over for the whole phase diagram
- Polyakov loop potential shown at T=115 MeV, demonstrating sharper cross-over as a function of chemical potential due to proximity of the critical endpoint
- Dimensionless potential V(phi)/p_SB normalized with p_SB = (19*pi^2/36)*T^4 + (3/2)*T^2*mu^2 + (3/(4*pi^2))*mu^4

## Context
This work extends functional continuum approaches to QCD phase structure at finite density, where lattice QCD faces the sign problem preventing access to mu/T > 1 (Endrodi 2011, Kaczmarek 2011). The method builds on prior FRG computations of the Polyakov loop potential in Yang-Mills theory (Braun 2007, Marhauser 2008, Braun 2010, Fister 2013) and in QCD at imaginary chemical potential (Braun 2009), as well as DSE studies of the QCD phase diagram (Fischer 2011, Fischer 2012). The results provide input for Polyakov-loop extended model applications (Pisarski 2000, Dumitru 2003, Herbst 2010, Skokov 2010, Weise 2012, Bratovic 2012, Fukushima 2012, Haas 2013) and serve as benchmark predictions for future evaluations with different methods.
