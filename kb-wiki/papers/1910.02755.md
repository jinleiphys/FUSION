# Pion-induced production of $Λ(1520)$ hyperons on nuclei near threshold

> We study the pion-induced inclusive $Λ(1520)$ hyperon production from nuclei near threshold within a nuclear spectral function approach accounting for incoherent primary $π^-$ meson--proton ${π^-}p \to K^0Λ(1520)$ production processes. We calculate the absolute differential and total cross sections for the production of $Λ(1520)$ hyperons off carbon and tungsten nuclei at laboratory angles of 0$^{\circ}$--10$^{\circ}$, 10$^{\circ}$--45$^{\circ}$ and 45$^{\circ}$--85$^{\circ}$ by $π^-$ mesons with momentum of 1.7 GeV/c as well as their relative (transparency ratio) differential and integral yields from these nuclei within four scenarios for their total in-medium width. We demonstrate that these absolute observables, contrary to the relative ones, reveal some sensitivity to the $Λ(1520)$ in-medium width. Therefore, their measurement in a dedicated experiment at the GSI pion beam facility will allow to shed light on this width.

## Key claim

The absolute differential and total cross sections for $\Lambda(1520)$ production in pion-nucleus reactions near threshold show sensitivity to the in-medium width of the hyperon, while the relative yields (transparency ratios) do not provide a reliable tool for determining this width. The claim is conditional on the specific nuclear spectral function approach and the four scenarios for the in-medium width considered, and the authors state that measurement of the absolute observables in a dedicated experiment at the GSI pion beam facility "will allow to shed light on this width."

## Method

The authors employ a nuclear spectral function approach based on a first-collision model, accounting only for incoherent primary $\pi^- p \to K^0 \Lambda(1520)$ production processes. They calculate the inclusive differential cross section for $\Lambda(1520)$ production on $^{12}$C and $^{184}$W targets by 1.7 GeV/c $\pi^-$ mesons, incorporating the absorption of the incident pion and the $\Lambda(1520)$ hyperon in nuclear matter, using a local nucleon density and a nuclear spectral function. The $\Lambda(1520)$ total in-medium width is modeled as the sum of its free width (15.6 MeV) and a collisional width of the form $55(\rho_N/\rho_0)$ MeV, with the collisional width multiplied by factors $f = 0, 0.5, 1, 2$ to test sensitivity.

## Key numbers

- The free production threshold momentum for the $\pi^- p \to K^0 \Lambda(1520)$ reaction is 1.68 GeV/c; the incident pion momentum used is 1.7 GeV/c.
- The free $\Lambda(1520)$ width is 15.6 MeV; the collisional width at normal nuclear density $\rho_0$ is 55 MeV, giving a total width of about 70 MeV at $\rho_0$ (a factor of ~5 larger than the free width).
- The on-shell total cross section for $\pi^- p \to K^0 \Lambda(1520)$ at 1.7 GeV/c with a free target proton at rest is approximately 13 $\mu$b; the off-shell cross section for a proton bound in $^{12}$C by 16 MeV with momentum 250 MeV/c is about 53 $\mu$b.
- The absolute differential cross sections in the high-momentum region 0.7–1.4 GeV/c at angles $\le 45^\circ$ have a strength of ~1–100 $\mu$b/(GeV/c).
- Differences in absolute differential cross sections between different in-medium width scenarios are ~20–30% for $^{12}$C and ~30–50% for $^{184}$W.
- At momentum 1.0 GeV/c and angular range 0$^{\circ}$–10$^{\circ}$, the $\Lambda(1520)$ momentum distributions are reduced at collisional width $0.5\cdot\Gamma_{\rm coll}$ by factors of about 1.3 ($^{12}$C) and 2.0 ($^{184}$W) compared to the free-width scenario; going from $0.5\cdot\Gamma_{\rm coll}$ to $1.0\cdot\Gamma_{\rm coll}$ gives reduction factors of about 1.2 and 1.4; going from $1.0\cdot\Gamma_{\rm coll}$ to $2.0\cdot\Gamma_{\rm coll}$ gives factors of about 1.4 and 1.5.
- The total cross sections in the angular domain 0$^{\circ}$–45$^{\circ}$ are reduced at $0.5\cdot\Gamma_{\rm coll}$ by factors of about 1.3 ($^{12}$C) and 1.9 ($^{184}$W) compared to the free-width scenario; going from $0.5\cdot\Gamma_{\rm coll}$ to $1.0\cdot\Gamma_{\rm coll}$ gives factors of about 1.2 and 1.4; going from $1.0\cdot\Gamma_{\rm coll}$ to $2.0\cdot\Gamma_{\rm coll}$ gives factors of about 1.3 and 1.5.
- The transparency ratio $T_A$ for W/C shows changes of ~30% only between the free-width and $0.5\cdot\Gamma_{\rm coll}$ scenarios at momenta ~0.7–1.4 GeV/c; differences between other scenarios are ~10–15%.

## Context

This work extends the study of in-medium properties of hyperons, specifically $\Lambda(1520)$, from proton-nucleus and photon-nucleus reactions (studied in collisional models by Paryev and Kiselev in earlier works, e.g., 2015 and 2017) to pion-nucleus reactions near threshold. It builds on a first-collision model previously developed for $\phi$ meson production (Paryev and Kiselev, 2018) and uses the same nuclear targets and beam momentum as recent GSI/HADES measurements of $\phi$ meson production. The predictions are intended to guide a dedicated experiment at the GSI pion beam facility to determine the $\Lambda(1520)$ in-medium width, complementing information from chiral unitary hadronic theory predictions (e.g., Oset and Ramos, 2002) that suggest a moderate increase in width at normal nuclear density.
