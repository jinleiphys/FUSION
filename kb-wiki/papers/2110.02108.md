# Spectral density reconstruction with Chebyshev polynomials

> Accurate calculations of the spectral density in a strongly correlated quantum many-body system are of fundamental importance to study its dynamics in the linear response regime. Typical examples are the calculation of inclusive and semi-exclusive scattering cross sections in atomic nuclei and transport properties of nuclear and neutron star matter. Integral transform techniques play an important role in accessing the spectral density in a variety of nuclear systems. However, their accuracy is in practice limited by the need to perform a numerical inversion which is often ill-conditioned. In the present work we extend a recently proposed quantum algorithm which circumvents this problem. We show how to perform controllable reconstructions of the spectral density over a finite energy resolution with rigorous error estimates. An appropriate expansion in Chebyshev polynomials allows for efficient simulations also on classical computers. We apply our idea to reconstruct a simple model -- response function as a proof of principle. This paves the way for future applications in nuclear and condensed matter physics.

## Key claim

The paper extends a previously proposed quantum algorithm (Roggero 2020) to enable controllable reconstructions of the spectral density over a finite energy resolution with rigorous error estimates, using an expansion in Chebyshev polynomials that also allows efficient classical simulations; the key condition is that the method provides error bounds only for coarse-grained (histogram) representations of the spectrum, not for pointwise reconstruction.

## Method

The authors expand integral kernels (Lorentzian and Gaussian) in a basis of Chebyshev polynomials of the first kind, then compute the integral transform of the spectral density as a linear combination of Chebyshev moments obtained from repeated application of the Hamiltonian to the excited state. They introduce a histogram-based coarse-graining procedure where the spectral density is integrated over bins of width 2Δ, and derive rigorous upper and lower bounds on the histogram values using the kernel's resolution Λ and truncation error β from the finite Chebyshev expansion.

## Key numbers

- For a Gaussian signal of width Γ=0.1, the CheET method with Gaussian kernel (λ=0.01) reaches a plateau accuracy of approximately e⁻⁸ with N≈60 moments, while for Γ=0.01 the same λ=0.001 gives plateau accuracy of approximately e⁻¹.
- To achieve a truncation error β of order 0.1 with λ=0.01, approximately N≈900 moments are needed for the Gaussian kernel; for λ=0.001, approximately N≈10000 moments are required.
- For the histogram reconstruction with bin width 2Δ=0.2 and kernel resolution Λ=0.01, using λ=0.0025 and N=4000 moments, the Gaussian kernel yields negligible tail bound Σ^(G) and truncation error, while the Lorentzian kernel gives Σ^(L) ≤ 2λ/(πΛ) which dominates the uncertainty.
- The tail bound for the Gaussian kernel is Σ^(G) ≤ exp(-Λ²/(2λ²)), and for the Lorentzian kernel Σ^(L) ≤ 2λ/(πΛ).

## Context

This work extends the quantum algorithm approach of Roggero (2020) for spectral density reconstruction and is similar in spirit to both the Kernel Polynomial Method (KPM) popular in condensed matter physics (Weisse 2006) and the Lorentz Integral Transform (LIT) method used in nuclear physics (Efros 1994). The method enables future applications to ab-initio calculations of dynamical response functions in nuclear many-body systems, particularly extending LIT-CC calculations (Bacca 2013, Bacca 2014, Sobczyk 2021) to observables where numerical inversion is unstable, and can also benefit tensor network applications (Yang 2020, Papaefstathiou 2021).
