# Effective Baryon-Baryon Potentials in the Quark Delocalization and Color Screening Model

> The quark delocalization and color screening model is used for a systematic study of the effective potential between baryons in the u,d and s sector. The model is constrained by the properties of baryons and N-N scattering. The effective potentials for the N-N (IJ=01,10,11,00) channels and the N-Lambda and N-Sigma (IJ = (1/2)1, (1/2)0, (3/2)1, (3/2)0) channels fit the N-N, N-Lambda and N-Sigma scattering data reasonably well. This model predicts: There are rather strong effective attractions between decuplet-baryons; the effective attractions between octet-baryons are weak or even repulsive; and the attractions between decuplet- and octet-baryons lie in between.

## Key claim

The quark delocalization and color screening model (QDCSM) predicts that effective attractions between decuplet-baryons are rather strong, between octet-baryons are weak or even repulsive, and between decuplet- and octet-baryons lie in between; however, these predictions are qualified by the fact that the model parameters for quadratic confinement require adjustment of the screening parameter from the lattice QCD value to fit N-N scattering data, while linear confinement yields parameter-free agreement with phase shift analyses.

## Method

The authors used the quark delocalization and color screening model (QDCSM) with a six-quark Hamiltonian including confinement and one-gluon exchange potentials, where single-quark orbits are delocalized between two baryon clusters via a variational parameter epsilon(s). Effective baryon-baryon potentials were computed by evaluating the energy of the six-quark system at finite separation s and subtracting the energy at infinite separation, using the resonating group method with a group-theory classification basis to handle the 168 channels of octet and decuplet baryons in the u,d,s sector.

## Key numbers

- Quark mass m = 313 MeV, strange quark mass m_s = 618.45 MeV (linear confinement) or 633.76 MeV (quadratic confinement)
- Baryon size parameter b = 0.625 fm (linear) or 0.603 fm (quadratic)
- Strong coupling constant alpha_s = 1.71 (linear) or 1.54 (quadratic)
- Confinement strength a = 39.14 MeV/fm^2 (linear) or 25.13 MeV/fm^2 (quadratic)
- Screening parameter mu_n = 1.1 (linear) or 1.6 (quadratic)
- Lattice QCD screening parameter mu_1^{-1} = 0.90 +/- 0.20 fm, sqrt(sigma) = 400 MeV, alpha_s = 0.21 +/- 0.01
- N-N channels (IJ=01,10) show effective attraction, with (01) slightly stronger than (10); channels (11) and (00) are repulsive
- N-Sigma channel IJ=1/2 1 shows strongest attraction among N-Sigma channels; IJ=3/2 1 channel is repulsive
- Omega-Omega (00) state shows strong enough attraction to form a strong interaction bound state
- Minima of Sigma-Sigma (20) and Xi-Xi (00) potentials found at separations greater than 1 fm

## Context

This work extends the quark delocalization and color screening model previously applied to N-N scattering (Wang et al., 1992; Wu et al., 1996) and dibaryon searches (Wang et al., 1995) to a systematic calculation of effective potentials across all octet and decuplet baryon combinations in the u,d,s sector. It contrasts with one-boson-exchange models (Nijmegen and Julich models, 1973-1989) which require phenomenological repulsive cores, and with conventional quark cluster models (Oka, Shimizu, Yazaki, 1987; Straub et al., 1988) that need meson exchange for medium-range attraction. The results enable predictions for dibaryon candidates, multiquark states, strangelets, and the equation of state of strongly interacting matter, with specific candidates including the d* (Delta-Delta 03) narrow resonance and the H particle (YIJ=000).
