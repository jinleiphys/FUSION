# Low-momentum pion enhancement from schematic hadronization of a gluon-saturated initial state

> We study the particle production in the early stage of the ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. To this end the Boltzmann kinetic equations for gluons and pions with elastic rescattering are considered together with a simple model for the parton-hadron conversion process (hadronisation). It is shown that the overpopulation of the gluon phase space in the initial state leads to an intermediate stage of Bose enhancement in the low-momentum gluon sector which due to the gluon-pion conversion process is then reflected in the final distribution function of pions. This pattern is very similar to the experimental finding of a low-momentum pion enhancement in the ALICE experiment at CERN LHC. Relations to the thermal statistical model of hadron production and the phenomenon of thermal and chemical freeze-out are discussed in this context.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that an overpopulated initial gluon phase space in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions can produce a low-momentum enhancement in the final pion distribution through Bose enhancement in the gluon sector, which is then transferred to pions via a schematic hadronization process. This pattern is claimed to be "very similar" to the experimental finding of low-momentum pion enhancement observed by the ALICE experiment at CERN LHC, though the model uses a simplified, schematic hadronization conversion rather than a full QCD treatment.

## Method
The authors solve Boltzmann kinetic equations for gluons and pions including elastic rescattering, coupled with a simple parton-hadron conversion (hadronization) model. The initial state is prepared with a gluon-saturated (overpopulated) phase space, and the time evolution of the gluon and pion distribution functions is computed. The gluon-pion conversion process transfers the Bose-enhanced low-momentum gluon population into the final pion distribution.

## Key numbers
- No specific numerical results (e.g., cross sections, yields, or momenta in GeV/c) are reported in the available text; the paper presents a qualitative demonstration of the mechanism.

## Context
This work extends the line of research on gluon saturation and its observable consequences in heavy-ion collisions, particularly the phenomenon of low-momentum pion enhancement reported by the ALICE collaboration at the LHC. The authors relate their findings to the thermal statistical model of hadron production and the concepts of thermal and chemical freeze-out. The schematic approach enables a qualitative connection between initial-state gluon overpopulation and final-state pion distributions, but a quantitative comparison to data would require a more realistic hadronization model.
