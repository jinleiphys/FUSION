# Is the centrality dependence of the elliptic flow $v_2$ and of the average $<p_T>$ in RHIC experiments more than a Core-Corona Effect?

> Recently we have shown that the centrality dependence of the multiplicity of different hadron species observed in RHIC and SPS experiments can be well understood in a simple model, dubbed core-corona model. There it is assumed that those incoming nucleons which scatter only once produce hadrons as in pp collisions whereas those which scatter more often form an equilibrated source which decays according to phase space. In this article we show that also kinematical variables like $v_2/ε_{part} (N_{part})$ as well as $v_2^i/ε_{part} (N_{part})$ and $<p_T^i (N_{part})>$ of identified particles are well described in this model. The correlation of $<p_T^i>$ between peripheral heavy ion collisions and pp collisions for different hadrons, reproduced in this model, questions whether hydrodynamical calculations are the proper tool to describe non-central heavy ion collision. The model explains as well the centrality dependence of $v_2/ε_{part}$ of charged particles, considered up to now as an observable which allows to determine the viscosity of the quark gluon plasma. The observed dependence of $v_2^i/ε_{part}(N_{part})$ on the particle species is a simple consequence of the different ratios of core to corona particles.

# Core-Corona Model for Elliptic Flow and Average Transverse Momentum in Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim

The centrality dependence of elliptic flow ($v_2/\epsilon_{part}$) and average transverse momentum ($\langle p_T \rangle$) for identified hadrons in RHIC experiments can be fully explained by a simple core-corona superposition model, without requiring viscous hydrodynamics or a finite quark-gluon plasma viscosity. The model questions whether hydrodynamical calculations are the proper tool to describe non-central heavy ion collisions, because the observed correlation between peripheral heavy ion data and pp data is naturally reproduced by the core-corona approach but is alien to hydrodynamics.

## Method

The core-corona model classifies participant nucleons as either "core" particles (those undergoing more than one initial collision, forming an equilibrated source that decays according to phase space) or "corona" particles (those scattering only once, producing hadrons as in pp collisions). The fraction of core nucleons $f_{core}$ is calculated in a Glauber model as a function of participant number $N_{part}$, system size, and beam energy. For each hadron species $i$, the average transverse momentum $\langle p_T^i \rangle(N_{part})$ is computed as a weighted average of the core value (taken from central heavy ion data) and the corona value (taken from pp data), using the species-dependent core fraction $f_{core}^i$. The elliptic flow $v_2/\epsilon_{part}$ is taken proportional to $f_{core}$ for charged hadrons, with the proportionality constant $(v_2/\epsilon_{part})^{hydro}$ determined from central collisions; for identified hadrons, the flow is weighted by the species-dependent core fraction.

## Key numbers

- For charged hadrons at $\sqrt{s}=200$ AGeV, $(v_2/\epsilon_{part})^{hydro}$ is taken as 24%; at 62 AGeV, 19.7%; at 17.2 AGeV, 12.6%.
- The ratio $\langle p_T^i \rangle_{corona} / \langle p_T^i \rangle_{core}$ varies from 0.81 for pions to 0.62 for protons (STAR data).
- For pions: $\langle p_T \rangle_{corona} = 0.348 \pm 0.018$ GeV/c (STAR pp), $\langle p_T \rangle_{core} = 0.429 \pm 0.022$ GeV/c (STAR central AA).
- For protons: $\langle p_T \rangle_{corona} = 0.686 \pm 0.041$ GeV/c (STAR pp), $\langle p_T \rangle_{core} = 1.104 \pm 0.11$ GeV/c (STAR central AA).
- For kaons ($K^-$): $\langle p_T \rangle_{corona} = 0.605 \pm 0.073$ GeV/c, $\langle p_T \rangle_{core} = 0.717 \pm 0.050$ GeV/c.
- For $\phi$ mesons: $\langle p_T \rangle_{corona} = 0.820 \pm 0.050$ GeV/c, $\langle p_T \rangle_{core} = 0.970 \pm 0.097$ GeV/c.
- For $\Lambda$ baryons: $\langle p_T \rangle_{corona} = 0.780 \pm 0.040$ GeV/c, $\langle p_T \rangle_{core} = 1.050 \pm 0.090$ GeV/c.
- For $\Xi$ baryons: $\langle p_T \rangle_{corona} = 0.924 \pm 0.130$ GeV/c, $\langle p_T \rangle_{core} = 1.110 \pm 0.090$ GeV/c.
- The experimental $v_2/(\epsilon_{part} f_{core})$ is independent of $N_{part}$ for AuAu and CuCu at 200 and 62 AGeV (PHOBOS data).

## Context

This work extends the core-corona model previously applied by the same authors (Aichelin and Werner, 2008) to hadron multiplicities from SPS to RHIC energies. It directly challenges the interpretation of Drescher et al. (2007) and Luzum et al. (2008), who argued that the centrality dependence of $v_2/\epsilon_{part}$ signals finite viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma and requires viscous hydrodynamics. The model also contradicts the expectation from ideal hydrodynamics that $v_2/\epsilon_{part}$ should be centrality-independent. The success of the core-corona approach suggests that the centrality dependence of these observables reflects the changing ratio of core to corona particles rather than plasma properties, and enables a simpler interpretation where the core source properties are independent of centrality. The model predicts that for the same $N_{part}$, CuCu collisions show higher $v_2/\epsilon_{part}$ than AuAu collisions, opposite to hydrodynamical predictions, offering a clear experimental test once error bars are reduced.
