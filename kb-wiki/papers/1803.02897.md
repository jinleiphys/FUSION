# Extraction of isoscalar $ππ$ phase-shifts from lattice QCD

> We conduct a two-flavor ($N_f=2$) lattice QCD calculation of the elastic phase-shifts for pion-pion scattering in the scalar, isoscalar channel (the $σ$-meson). The calculation is performed for two quark masses corresponding to a pion mass of $315\text{ MeV}$ and $227\text{ MeV}$. The $σ$-meson parameters are extracted using various parametrizations of the scattering amplitude. The results obtained from a chiral unitary parametrization are extrapolated to the physical point and read $M_σ= (440^{+10}_{-16}(50) - i\,240(20)(25))\text{ MeV}$, where the uncertainties in the parentheses denote the stochastic and systematic ones. The behavior of the $σ$-meson parameters with increasing pion mass is discussed as well.

## Key claim
The paper reports a lattice QCD calculation of isoscalar pion-pion phase shifts and extracts the sigma meson mass and width, but the final result of M_sigma = (440+10-16 - i 240(20)(25)) MeV is obtained only after extrapolating a chiral unitary parametrization to the physical point, with the uncertainties in parentheses denoting stochastic and systematic errors respectively.

## Method
The authors performed N_f=2 lattice QCD calculations at two pion masses (227 MeV and 315 MeV) using three lattice volumes with different elongation factors and two total-momentum frames (rest frame and boosted frame). They constructed a 6x6 variational basis including both quark-antiquark and two-pion interpolating fields, extracted energy eigenvalues via the generalized eigenvalue problem, and used the Luscher formalism to relate finite-volume energies to infinite-volume phase shifts. The scattering amplitude was parametrized using conformal mapping and chiral unitary approaches, with parameters fitted directly to the lattice energy eigenvalues.

## Key numbers
- Pion masses used: M_pi = 227 MeV and M_pi = 315 MeV
- Final extrapolated sigma meson parameters: M_sigma = (440+10-16 - i 240(20)(25)) MeV, where the first parentheses give stochastic uncertainty and the second systematic uncertainty
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.1210(2)(24) fm for the 227 MeV ensemble and a = 0.1215(3)(24) fm for the 315 MeV ensemble
- Number of gauge configurations: 300 for the 227 MeV ensemble and 400 for the 315 MeV ensemble
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom values: 0.7-1.1 for various fits

## Context
This work extends earlier lattice QCD studies of pion-pion scattering, particularly the isovector channel calculations by the same group (Guo 2016) and the isoscalar results from the Hadron Spectrum Collaboration (Briceno 2016). The results enable improved determination of the sigma meson properties from first-principles QCD and allow study of how the resonance parameters evolve with pion mass, complementing analyses using the Inverse Amplitude Method (Doring 2016) and experimental data analyses (Pelaez 2015).
