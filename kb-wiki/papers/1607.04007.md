# $Δ$ (1232) effects in density-dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock theory and neutron stars

> The density-dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock (DDRHF) theory is extended to include $Δ$-isobars for the study of dense nuclear matter and neutron stars. To this end, we solve the Rarita-Schwinger equation for spin-3/2 particle. Both the direct and exchange terms of the $Δ$-isobars' self-energies are evaluated in details. In comparison with the relativistic mean field theory (Hartree approximation), a weaker parameter dependence is found for DDRHF. An early appearance of $Δ$-isobars is recognized at $ρ_B\sim0.27$fm$^{-3}$, comparable with that of hyperons. Also, we find that the $Δ$-isobars' softening of the equation of state is found to be mainly due to the reduced Fock contributions from the coupling of the isoscalar mesons, while the pion contributions are found negligibly small. We finally conclude that with typical parameter sets, neutron stars with $Δ$-isobars in their interiors could be as heavy as the two massive pulsars whose masses are precisely measured, with slightly smaller radii than normal neutron stars.

## Key claim

The density-dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock (DDRHF) theory, when extended to include Delta-isobars, predicts that neutron stars with Delta-isobars in their interiors can be as heavy as the two precisely measured two-solar-mass pulsars, but with slightly smaller radii than normal neutron stars; this conclusion depends on the specific choice of meson-Delta coupling constants and parameter sets, and the softening of the equation of state is mainly due to reduced Fock contributions from isoscalar mesons rather than pion contributions.

## Method

The authors extended the DDRHF theory to include Delta-isobars by solving the Rarita-Schwinger equation for spin-3/2 particles, evaluating both direct (Hartree) and exchange (Fock) terms of the Delta-isobars' self-energies self-consistently. Four mesons (isoscalar sigma and omega, isovector rho and pi) were included for baryon interactions, and four available DDRHF parameter sets (PKO1, PKO2, PKO3, PKA1) were employed with various choices of the uncertain meson-Delta coupling constants. The equation of state was then used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for neutron star structure.

## Key numbers

- Delta-isobars appear at a baryon density of approximately 0.28 fm^-3, comparable with that of hyperons.
- For the PKO1 parameter set with meson-Delta couplings (x_sigma, x_omega, x_rho) = (0.8, 1.0, 0.5), the Delta critical density is 1.45 times the saturation density (rho_0), compared to 2.1 rho_0 in relativistic mean field theory for the same meson coupling of (1.0, 1.0, 1.0).
- The Delta threshold densities for different parameter sets are: 0.2805 fm^-3 (PKO1), 0.2775 fm^-3 (PKO2), 0.2715 fm^-3 (PKO3), and 0.2640 fm^-3 (PKA1), showing a variation of at most 5.99%.
- For PKO3 (PKA1) with (x_sigma, x_omega) = (0.8, 1.0), the maximum neutron star mass is 2.24 solar masses (2.14 solar masses) with Delta-isobars, compared to 2.50 solar masses (2.43 solar masses) without Delta-isobars.
- For PKO1 with (x_sigma, x_omega) = (0.8, 1.0), the maximum mass is 2.19 solar masses with a radius of 11.62 km, compared to 2.45 solar masses and 12.38 km without Delta-isobars.
- The symmetry energy parameters for PKO3 are (E_sym, L) = (32.99 MeV, 83.00 MeV) and for PKA1 are (36.02 MeV, 103.50 MeV).

## Context

This work extends previous relativistic mean field (RMF) studies of Delta-isobars in neutron stars, such as Li (2015), by including exchange (Fock) terms and pseudo-vector pion couplings that are only effective through exchange terms. The DDRHF approach shows a weaker parameter dependence than RMF calculations, where earlier work by Guo (2003) found much larger variations in Delta threshold densities (e.g., 37.6% between NL1 and TM1 parameter sets in the nonlinear Walecka model). The results enable future studies of neutron star structure with Delta-isobars that can simultaneously satisfy the two-solar-mass pulsar constraints, and suggest that new DDRHF parameter sets with lower symmetry energy slope L values should be proposed.
