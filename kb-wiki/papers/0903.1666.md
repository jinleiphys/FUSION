# Lattice chiral effective field theory with three-body interactions at next-to-next-to-leading order

> We consider low-energy nucleons at next-to-next-to-leading order in lattice chiral effective field theory. Three-body interactions first appear at this order, and we discuss several methods for determining three-body interaction coefficients on the lattice. We compute the energy of the triton and low-energy neutron-deuteron scattering phase shifts in the spin-doublet and spin-quartet channels using Luescher's finite volume method. In the four-nucleon system we calculate the energy of the alpha particle using auxiliary fields and projection Monte Carlo.

## Key claim
Three-body interactions in chiral effective field theory first appear at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO), and at low cutoff momentum (Λ = 314 MeV) the only new contributions at this order are due to three-nucleon interactions. The authors demonstrate that with a suitable choice of temporal lattice spacing, the three-nucleon contact interaction is numerically small enough to be treated using perturbation theory rather than requiring non-perturbative methods.

## Method
The authors used lattice chiral effective field theory with Weinberg power counting, employing a spatial lattice spacing a = (100 MeV)^{-1} and temporal lattice spacing a_t = (150 MeV)^{-1}. Two-nucleon interactions at leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO) were determined by fitting to nucleon-nucleon phase shifts using a spherical wall method, while three-nucleon interactions at NNLO were included perturbatively. The triton energy was computed using iterative sparse-matrix eigenvector methods on cubic periodic lattices with side lengths L = 3 through 8, with infinite volume extrapolation using Luescher's finite volume method, and the alpha particle energy was calculated using auxiliary fields and projection Monte Carlo.

## Key numbers
- Spatial lattice spacing: a = (100 MeV)^{-1}, corresponding to cutoff momentum Λ = 314 MeV ≈ 2.3 m_π
- Temporal lattice spacing: a_t = (150 MeV)^{-1}
- Physical constants used: nucleon mass m = 938.92 MeV, pion mass m_π = 138.08 MeV, pion decay constant f_π = 93 MeV, nucleon axial charge g_A = 1.26
- Low-energy constants for pion-nucleon coupling: c_1 = -0.81 GeV^{-1}, c_3 = -4.7 GeV^{-1}, c_4 = 3.4 GeV^{-1}
- Leading-order coefficients: C^{I=0} = -5.105 × 10^{-5} MeV^{-2}, C^{I=1} = -3.507 × 10^{-5} MeV^{-2}
- Physical triton energy used for constraint: -8.48 MeV; pseudo triton energy (nn replaced by np): -8.68 MeV
- NLO operator coefficients include ΔC = 4.08 × 10^{-6} MeV^{-2}, ΔC_{I^2} = 5.92 × 10^{-6} MeV^{-2}, and seven other coefficients ranging from -1.31 × 10^{-9} to 1.11 × 10^{-10} MeV^{-4}

## Context
This work extends a series of lattice chiral effective field theory studies by the same group, which previously considered dilute neutron matter and light nuclei at leading order (Lee 2004, Borasoy 2006) and next-to-leading order (Borasoy 2007, Epelbaum 2008). The three-nucleon interactions at NNLO follow the formalism of Weinberg (1991) and van Kolck (1994), with the c_E versus c_D constraint curve showing similarity to results from Epelbaum (2002) and Navratil (2007). The results enable future lattice calculations of larger nuclear systems including three-body forces at this order, and the authors note that alternative power counting schemes (Beane 2001, Nogga 2005, Birse 2005) would give numerically small differences at this low cutoff momentum.
