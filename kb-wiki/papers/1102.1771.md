# Evidence for strong refraction of $^3$He in an alpha-particle condensate

> We have analyzed $^{3}$He scattering from $^{12}$C at 34.7 and 72 MeV in a coupled channel method with a double folding potential derived from the precise wave functions for the ground 0$^+$ state and $0_2^+$ (7.65 MeV) Hoyle state, which has been suggested to be an $α$ particle condensate. It is found that strong refraction of $^3$He in the Hoyle state can be clearly seen in the experimental angular distribution at {\it low} incident energy region as an Airy minimum of the {\it pre-rainbow oscillations}.

## Key claim

Strong refraction of $^3$He in the Hoyle state (0₂⁺, 7.65 MeV) of ¹²C, suggested to be an α-particle condensate, can be clearly observed as an Airy minimum of the pre-rainbow oscillations in the experimental angular distribution at low incident energy (34.7 MeV), where a pocket exists in the effective potential, rather than only at high incident energies where typical nuclear rainbow scattering appears.

## Method

The authors analyzed elastic and inelastic $^3$He scattering from ¹²C at 34.7 and 72 MeV using a coupled channel method with double folding potentials derived from precise resonating-group-method wave functions for the ground 0⁺ state, the 2⁺ (4.44 MeV), the 0₂⁺ Hoyle state, and the 3⁻ (9.63 MeV) state. The folding potential used the density-dependent M3Y effective interaction (DDM3Y) and a normalization factor N_R = 1.28 for the real part, with phenomenological Woods-Saxon imaginary potentials (volume and surface absorption) for each channel. Angular distributions were decomposed into farside and nearside components following Fuller's prescription to identify Airy minima.

## Key numbers

- At E_L = 72 MeV, the first Airy minimum A1 appears at 30° for elastic scattering and at 35° for the 0₂⁺ state.
- At E_L = 34.7 MeV, the Airy minimum A1 appears at 60° for elastic scattering and at 75° for the 0₂⁺ state, a three-times larger shift to larger angle than at 72 MeV.
- The volume integral per nucleon pair J_V for the 0₂⁺ state is 529 MeV fm³ at 72 MeV and 586 MeV fm³ at 34.7 MeV, compared to 408 and 445 MeV fm³ for the ground state at the same energies.
- The root mean square radius of the real folding potential is 4.385 fm for the 0₂⁺ state and 3.574 fm for the ground state at both energies.
- The volume integral for elastic scattering at E_L = 119 MeV is 365 MeV fm³, consistent with 352 MeV fm³ from Hyakutake et al.

## Context

This work extends the study of refractive effects in nuclear scattering to the Hoyle state of ¹²C, which was previously investigated for α-particle scattering at high energies by Ohkubo (2004). The analysis uses wave functions from the microscopic cluster model of Uegaki et al. (1979) and Kamimura and Fukushima (1981), which are equivalent to the Bose-Einstein condensate wave function of Tohsaki et al. (2001) and Funaki et al. (2003). The findings enable a new method to probe the dilute density distribution of α-condensate states in heavier 4N-nuclei such as ¹⁶O and ⁴⁰Ca, as well as analogue states in neutron-rich nuclei like ¹³C, ¹⁴C, and ¹⁶C, by measuring the shift of the pre-rainbow Airy minimum in low-energy inelastic scattering.
