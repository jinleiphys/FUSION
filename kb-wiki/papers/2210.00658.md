# Far-from-equilibrium attractors for massive kinetic theory in the relaxation time approximation

> We investigate whether early and late time attractors for non-conformal kinetic theories exist by computing the time-evolution of a large set of moments of the one-particle distribution function. For this purpose we make use of a previously obtained exact solution of the 0+1D boost-invariant massive Boltzmann equation in relaxation time approximation. We extend prior attractor studies of non-conformal systems by using a realistic mass- and temperature-dependent relaxation time and explicitly computing the effect of varying both the initial momentum-space anisotropy and initialization time on the time evolution of a large set of integral moments. Our findings are consistent with prior studies, which found that there is an attractor for the scaled longitudinal pressure, but not for the shear and bulk viscous corrections separately. We further present evidence that both late- and early-time attractors exist for all moments of the one-particle distribution function that contain greater than one power of the longitudinal momentum squared.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that for massive kinetic theory in the relaxation time approximation, both early-time and late-time attractors exist for all moments of the one-particle distribution function containing greater than one power of the longitudinal momentum squared (l >= 1), but not for the shear and bulk viscous corrections separately; the existence of an attractor for the scaled longitudinal pressure is consistent with prior studies.

## Method

The authors use a previously obtained exact solution of the 0+1D boost-invariant massive Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation (RTA) for a gas with Boltzmann statistics, extending it to compute all moments M^{nl} of the one-particle distribution function. They employ a self-consistently determined temperature- and mass-dependent relaxation time tau_eq(T,m) derived from fixed specific shear viscosity eta/s, and solve the integral equation for the temperature T(tau) iteratively on a logarithmic grid with 4096 points. They then assess the existence of forward attractors by holding initialization time and energy density fixed while varying the initial momentum-space anisotropy parameter xi_0, and assess early-time (pull-back) attractors by holding initial anisotropy and energy density fixed while varying initialization time tau_0.

## Key numbers

* For masses m = 0.2 GeV, 1 GeV, and 5 GeV, the initial energy density corresponds to a temperature T_0 = 1 GeV, with final evolution time tau_f = 100 fm/c.
* For forward attractor studies, initialization time tau_0 = 0.1 fm/c was used; for pull-back attractor studies, initial anisotropy xi_0 = 0 was used.
* The integral equation was iterated 200 times, achieving convergence to 8 digits at all proper times.
* Initial anisotropy parameter alpha_0 = 1/sqrt(1+xi_0) was varied over the set {0.12, 0.25, 0.5, 1, 2} for forward attractor studies.
* Initialization times tau_0 in {0.01, 0.02, 0.04} fm/c were used for pull-back attractor studies.

## Context

This work extends prior attractor studies of non-conformal systems by Romatschke (2017), Florkowski (2017), Chattopadhyay (2021), and Jaiswal (2021, 2022) that used either constant relaxation time or conformal relaxation time proportional to inverse temperature, by instead using a realistic mass- and temperature-dependent relaxation time. The findings are fully compatible with and extend those reported by Chattopadhyay (2021) and Jaiswal (2021), and the exact solution framework builds on the original work of Florkowski (2014). The results enable systematic assessment of the quantitative reliability of different dissipative hydrodynamical frameworks for massive systems and clarify which moments of the distribution function possess attractor behavior in non-conformal kinetic theory.
