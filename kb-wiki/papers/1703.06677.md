# Comparison of the pp -> pi+ pn and pp -> pi+ d production rates

> Fully constrained bubble chamber data on the pp -> pi+ pn and pp -> pi+ d reactions are used to investigate the ratio of the counting rates for the two processes at low pn excitation energies. Whereas the ratio is in tolerable agreement with that found in a high resolution spectrometer experiment, the angular distribution in the final pn rest frame shows that the deviation from the predictions of final state interaction theory must originate primarily from higher partial waves in the pn system. These considerations might also be significant for the determination of the S-wave Lambda:p scattering length from data on the pp -> K+ Lambda p reaction.

# Comparison of the pp -> pi+ pn and pp -> pi+ d production rates

## Key claim
The ratio of counting rates for the two reactions at low pn excitation energies is in tolerable agreement with a high-resolution spectrometer experiment, but the angular distribution in the final pn rest frame shows that deviations from final state interaction theory originate primarily from higher partial waves in the pn system, not from modifications of the S-wave intensity.

## Method
Fully constrained bubble chamber data from the Petersburg Nuclear Physics Institute (PNPI) at three beam energies (900.2, 940.7, and 988.6 MeV) were used to select pp -> pi+ pn events with pn excitation energy below 20 MeV. The numbers of such events were compared to the numbers of pp -> pi+ d events to extract the normalization factor F in the final state interaction relation. The angular distribution of the final proton in the pn rest frame was fitted with a quadratic function in cos(theta_p) to test for isotropy.

## Key numbers
- Normalization factor F = 2.6 ± 0.2 (weighted average over three energies from PNPI data), compared to COSY-GEM value of 2.2 ± 0.1 at 951 MeV.
- At 900.2 MeV: N(pp -> pi+ pn) = 188, N(pp -> pi+ d) = 136, F = 2.6 ± 0.3.
- At 940.7 MeV: N(pp -> pi+ pn) = 101, N(pp -> pi+ d) = 77, F = 2.4 ± 0.3.
- At 988.6 MeV: N(pp -> pi+ pn) = 97, N(pp -> pi+ d) = 57, F = 3.1 ± 0.5.
- Quadratic fit coefficients for angular distribution: a0 = 27.3 ± 2.7, a1 = -1.5 ± 3.7, a2 = 33.8 ± 7.1, with chi^2/NDF = 0.27.
- COSY-GEM missing-mass resolution was approximately 100 keV/c^2.

## Context
This work extends the COSY-GEM measurements (ABD2005, BUD2009) and TRIUMF results (PLE1999) by using fully constrained PNPI bubble chamber data (ERM2014, ERM2011, ERM2017) to investigate the origin of deviations from the final state interaction theorem of Faeldt and Wilkin (1997) and Boudard (1996). The findings suggest that similar higher partial wave effects may complicate the determination of the S-wave Lambda:p scattering length from pp -> K+ Lambda p data, as attempted by the COSY-HIRES group (BUD2010) and studied by the COSY-TOF experiment (HAU2017, HAU2014, JOW2016). The determination of the Lambda p virtual state pole position (Faeldt 2017) is less affected because it is sensitive to very low excitation energies where the S-wave assumption is more reliable.
