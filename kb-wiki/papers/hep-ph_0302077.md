# Aspects of the Color Flavor Locking phase of QCD in the Nambu-Jona Lasinio approximation

> We study two aspects of the CFL phase of QCD in the NJL approximation. The first one is the issue of the dependence on μof the ultraviolet cutoff in the gap equation, which is solved allowing a running coupling constant. The second one is the dependence of the gap on the strange quark mass; using the high density effective theory we perform an expansion in the parameter (m_s/μ)^2 after checking that its numerical validity is very good already at first order.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in the Nambu-Jona Lasinio (NJL) model of the Color Flavor Locking (CFL) phase of QCD, the ultraviolet cutoff must increase with the chemical potential (as \(\delta = c\mu\)) and the coupling constant must be made cutoff-dependent to avoid a non-physical decreasing gap at high densities; the authors also show that an expansion in \((m_s/\mu)^2\) provides a very good semi-analytical description of the gap dependence on the strange quark mass, already at first order.

## Method

The authors solve the mean-field gap equations for the CFL phase using a NJL four-fermion interaction, first for massless quarks and then including a finite strange quark mass. For the massless case, they propose a running NJL coupling constant \(G(\Lambda)\) fixed by requiring that the pion decay constant \(f_\pi\) and the chiral gap equation are satisfied for any cutoff \(\Lambda\), and they adopt a cutoff \(\delta = c\mu\) (with \(c=0.35\pm0.10\)) for momenta measured from the Fermi surface. For the massive case, they use the high density effective theory (HDET) to derive a system of five gap equations for the antitriplet and sextet gap parameters, and perform a perturbative expansion in \(x_s^2 = (m_s/\mu)^2\) to obtain semi-analytical expressions for the gaps.

## Key numbers

*   For massless quarks, the cutoff is chosen as \(\delta = c\mu\) with \(c = 0.35 \pm 0.10\), a range where the gap parameters are less dependent on \(c\).
*   For a strange quark mass \(m_s = 250\) MeV and \(\delta = 0.35\mu\), the ratio \(\Delta_9/\Delta_1\) is \(-2.365\) at \(\mu = 500\) MeV and \(-2.33\) at \(\mu = 1000\) MeV.
*   The expansion parameters \(\xi\) (in MeV) for the five gaps are given for \(\mu = 500, 700, 1000\) MeV; for example, at \(\mu = 500\) MeV, \(\epsilon_1 = -35.29\), \(\epsilon_9 = 90.48\), \(\xi_1 = 33.63\), \(\xi_2 = -2.06\), \(\xi_3 = -6.20\).
*   The perturbative expansion in \((m_s/\mu)^2\) is shown to differ from the exact numerical solution for \(\Delta_1\) by only a few percent for \(m_s = 250\) MeV and \(\mu\) in the range 500-1000 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the CFL phase using NJL models, particularly addressing the problematic behavior of the gap when a fixed ultraviolet cutoff is used, as noted by Steiner et al. (2002). The authors build on the neutrality result of Rajagopal and Wilczek (2000) and the HDET formalism applied to the 2SC case by Casalbuoni et al. (2002). The paper provides semi-analytical results for the mass dependence of the CFL gaps, including both antitriplet and sextet channels, which enables more reliable modeling of quark matter in compact stellar objects where the chemical potential is around 400-500 MeV.
