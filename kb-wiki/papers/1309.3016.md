# Reaction cross sections of hypernuclei and the shrinkage effect

> We calculate the reaction cross sections for $^6{\rm Li}$ and $^7_Λ{\rm Li}$ on a $^{12}{\rm C}$ target at $100\,{\rm MeV/nucleon}$ using the Glauber theory. To this end, we assume a two-body cluster structure for $^6$Li and $^7_Λ{\rm Li}$, and employ the few-body treatment of the Glauber theory, that is beyond the well known optical limit approximation. We show that the reaction cross section for $^7_Λ{\rm Li}$ is smaller than that for $^6$Li by about 4\%, reflecting the shrinkage effect of the $Λ$ particle.

## Key claim

The reaction cross section for \(^7_\Lambda\)Li on a \(^{12}\)C target at 100 MeV/nucleon is smaller than that for \(^6\)Li by about 4%, reflecting the shrinkage effect of the \(\Lambda\) particle; however, the reduction is somewhat smaller than the reduction in the root-mean-square radius, and the exact percentage depends on the assumed value of the \(\Lambda\)-nucleon scattering cross section and on whether the few-body treatment or the optical limit approximation is used.

## Method

The authors assumed a two-body cluster structure (\(\alpha\)+deuteron for \(^6\)Li and \(^5_\Lambda\)He+deuteron for \(^7_\Lambda\)Li) and obtained ground-state wave functions using a semi-microscopic cluster model with Gaussian-type potentials. They then calculated reaction cross sections for both projectiles on a \(^{12}\)C target at 100 MeV/nucleon using the Glauber theory, employing both the optical limit approximation (OLA) and a few-body (FB) treatment that goes beyond the OLA to account for cluster correlations.

## Key numbers

- The root-mean-square (rms) radii for \(^6\)Li and \(^7_\Lambda\)Li are 4.342 fm and 3.527 fm, respectively, corresponding to a shrinkage of about 19%.
- The reaction cross section for \(^6\)Li on \(^{12}\)C at 100 MeV/nucleon is 825.9 mb in the FB treatment and 880.6 mb in the OLA.
- For \(^7_\Lambda\)Li with \(\sigma_{\Lambda N}=0\) mb, the reaction cross section is 781.6 mb (FB) and 815.3 mb (OLA); with \(\sigma_{\Lambda N}=30\) mb, it is 796.5 mb (FB) and 828.8 mb (OLA).
- The reduction in reaction cross section from \(^6\)Li to \(^7_\Lambda\)Li ranges from 3.6% to 5.4% in the FB treatment and from 5.9% to 7.4% in the OLA, depending on the \(\sigma_{\Lambda N}\) value.

## Context

This work extends the study of the shrinkage effect in hypernuclei, previously investigated via \(\gamma\)-ray spectroscopy of \(^7_\Lambda\)Li by Tanida (2001) which measured a 33% reduction in \(B(E2)\) corresponding to about 19% shrinkage of the intercluster distance. The authors propose reaction cross sections as a complementary tool to electromagnetic transitions for probing nuclear size changes due to a \(\Lambda\) particle, and their results agree with the earlier experimental shrinkage and with more microscopic calculations by Motoba (1983) and Hiyama (2006). This enables future experimental measurements of reaction cross sections for hypernuclei as an alternative to \(\gamma\)-ray spectroscopy.
