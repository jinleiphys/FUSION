# Universal bosonic tetramers of dimer-atom-atom structure

> Unstable four-boson states having an approximate dimer-atom-atom structure are studied using momentum-space integral equations for the four-particle transition operators. For a given Efimov trimer the universal properties of the lowest associated tetramer are determined. The impact of this tetramer on the atom-trimer and dimer-dimer collisions is analyzed. The reliability of the three-body dimer-atom-atom model is studied.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for a given Efimov trimer, the lowest associated tetramer of dimer-atom-atom structure exists as an unstable bound state (UBS) in a narrow range of two-boson scattering lengths, but the abstract glosses over the fact that the tetramer lies extremely close to the atom-trimer threshold (deviation of only about 2.26 × 10⁻⁴ times the trimer binding energy at most) and that experimental observation would be extremely difficult due to trimer widths greatly exceeding the tetramer-trimer energy separation.

## Method

The author solved exact Alt, Grassberger and Sandhas (AGS) integral equations for four-particle transition operators in momentum space using partial-wave decomposition with zero total angular momentum and positive parity states. The atom-atom interaction was modeled by a separable S-wave potential with one- or two-term gaussian form factors. The tetramer properties were extracted from poles of the transition operators in the complex energy plane, and results were compared with a three-body dimer-atom-atom (3BDAA) model where the dimer is treated as a pointlike particle.

## Key numbers

- Universal ratio of tetramer intersection with atom-trimer threshold: a_{n,0}^t / a_n^d = 1.608(1), corresponding to A_d / a_{n,0}^t = 5.491(3)
- Tetramer intersection with dimer-atom-atom threshold: a_{n,0}^d / a_n^d > 0.9999, with 1 - a_{n,0}^d / a_n^d = 1.2(1) × 10⁻⁶
- Maximum deviation of tetramer from atom-trimer threshold: (b_n - B_{n,0}^d) / b_n ≈ 2.26 × 10⁻⁴ at a_n^d / a = 0.8
- Difference between atom-trimer and dimer-atom-atom thresholds at a_n^d / a = 0.8: (b_n - b_d) / b_n ≈ 8.86 × 10⁻³
- For the (n,1)th tetramer: a_{n,1}^t / a_n^d - 1 = 1.3(1) × 10⁻⁶, corresponding to A_d / a_{n,1}^t = 1.7(1) × 10⁶ and (b_n - b_d) / b_d = 2.6(2) × 10⁻¹³
- The tetramer exists as UBS in the range 0.6219 < a_n^d / a < 1.0000012

## Context

This work extends previous studies of four-boson systems by Hammer (2007), Stecher (2009), and Deltuva (2010, 2011) that established universal properties of tetramers associated with Efimov trimers. The paper specifically addresses tetramers of dimer-atom-atom structure predicted by Braaten (2006) and Dincao (2009) as a consequence of the three-body Efimov effect in a system of a dimer and two atoms. The results enable predictions for resonant effects in ultracold atom-trimer and dimer-dimer collisions, though the author notes that experimental observation would be extremely difficult due to trimer widths (e.g., η* ≈ 0.03 in Zaccanti (2009) and η* ≈ 0.1 in Ferlaino (2009)) greatly exceeding the tetramer-trimer energy separation.
