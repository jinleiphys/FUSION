# Delta(1232) and Nucleon Spectral Functions in Hot Hadronic Matter

> Modifications of Delta(1232) and nucleon spectral functions at finite temperature and baryon density are evaluated in terms of resonant scattering off thermal pions, as well as a renormalization of the vacuum Delta-width including vertex corrections. The interactions are based on effective Lagrangians of pions and baryon resonances, with underlying parameters (coupling constants and form factors) determined by the elastic pi N scattering phase shift in the isobar channel, and by empirical decay branchings of excited resonances. Pion modifications are included via interactions in a pion gas, as well as standard Delta- and nucleon-hole excitations in nuclear matter. In hot hadronic matter, under conditions resembling thermal freezeout at the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC), the Delta exhibits a significant broadening of ~65 MeV together with a slight upward peak shift of 5-10 MeV, in qualitative agreement with preliminary data from the STAR collaboration.

## Key claim

The Delta(1232) resonance in hot hadronic matter under conditions resembling thermal freezeout at RHIC exhibits a significant broadening of approximately 65 MeV together with a slight upward peak shift of 5-10 MeV, in qualitative agreement with preliminary data from the STAR collaboration.

## Method

The authors evaluate modifications of Delta(1232) and nucleon spectral functions at finite temperature and baryon density using effective Lagrangians of pions and baryon resonances, with parameters determined by elastic pi N scattering phase shifts in the isobar channel and empirical decay branchings of excited resonances. They compute in-medium self-energies using the imaginary-time (Matsubara) formalism, including resonant scattering off thermal pions, renormalization of the vacuum Delta-width with vertex corrections, and pion modifications via interactions in a pion gas plus standard Delta- and nucleon-hole excitations in nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- Delta(1232) broadening of approximately 65 MeV under RHIC thermal freezeout conditions (T=100 MeV, baryon density 0.12 times nuclear saturation density)
- Delta(1232) upward peak shift of 5-10 MeV under the same conditions
- At thermal freezeout, the Delta peak position is approximately 1.226 GeV with width approximately 177 MeV, compared to vacuum values of approximately 1.219 GeV and approximately 110 MeV
- Nucleon spectral function broadening exceeding 200 MeV at chemical freezeout (T=180 MeV, baryon density 0.68 times nuclear saturation density), with approximately 80% due to Delta excitation
- Bare mass of Delta(1232) set to 1302 MeV with pi N Delta coupling constant 3.2 and cutoff 290 MeV
- Coupling constant for pi N N vertex set to 1.0

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of in-medium Delta spectral functions for cold nuclear matter by Horiuchi (1980), Oset and collaborators (1987), and others, with few previous finite-temperature treatments by Ko (1989) and Korpa (1995). The authors go beyond earlier works by performing a more detailed treatment of the pion-nucleon cloud at finite temperature including vertex corrections, as well as resonance excitations induced by thermal pions. The results enable comparison with pi N invariant-mass spectra from heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and the future GSI facility, and contribute to understanding baryon spectrum changes toward chiral restoration in the QCD phase diagram.
