# On the size of isospin violation in low-energy pion-nucleon scattering

> We present an analysis of isospin-breaking effects in threshold pion-nucleon scattering due to the light quark mass difference and the dominant virtual photon effects. We discuss the deviation from various relations, which are exact in the isospin limit. The size of the isospin-violating effects in the relations involving the isovector $πN$ amplitudes is typically of the order of one percent. We also find a new remarkably large effect $(\sim 40%)$ in an isoscalar triangle relation connecting the charged and neutral pion scattering off protons.

# On the size of isospin violation in low-energy pion-nucleon scattering

## Key claim
Isospin-violating effects in the isovector pion-nucleon amplitudes at threshold are typically of order one percent, but a new remarkably large effect of approximately 40% is found in the isoscalar triangle relation R1 connecting charged and neutral pion scattering off protons. This large effect arises because the isoscalar amplitude is strongly suppressed, making the symmetry-breaking terms comparable in size.

## Method
The authors use heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory to third order in small momenta, including the leading isospin-breaking terms from the light quark mass difference (m_d - m_u) and the dominant virtual photon effects that produce pion and nucleon mass differences. They calculate six ratios R_i that vanish exactly in the isospin limit, evaluating the real parts of the threshold amplitudes using tree graphs, one-loop graphs, and low-energy constants fitted to three different partial wave analyses (Karlsruhe, Matsinos, and VPI/SAID).

## Key numbers
- Isospin violation in the isovector ratios R2, R3, R4, R5 is typically 0.5% to 1.1% across all three parameter fits (Fit 1: R2 = 0.9%, R3 = -0.5%, R4 = -0.7%, R5 = 1.1%)
- The isoscalar ratio R1 is 36.7% for Fit 1, representing a large isospin-violating effect in an isoscalar quantity
- The isoscalar ratio R6 (Weinberg's relation for neutral pion scattering off nucleons) is 19% for Fit 1, smaller than the 25% reported in earlier work by Meissner and Steininger (1998)
- Scattering lengths for Fit 1: a(pi+ p -> pi+ p) = -108.7 x 10^{-3}/M_pi+, a(pi- p -> pi- p) = (70.2 + i 3.65) x 10^{-3}/M_pi+, a(pi0 p -> pi0 p) = -13.4 x 10^{-3}/M_pi+, a(pi- p -> pi0 n) = -125.5 x 10^{-3}/M_pi+, a(pi0 p -> pi+ n) = (-124.7 - i 0.63) x 10^{-3}/M_pi+

## Context
This work extends the effective field theory framework for pion-nucleon scattering developed by Meissner and Steininger (1998) and by Muller, Meissner, and Steininger (1997), incorporating both strong isospin breaking from the quark mass difference and electromagnetic effects from virtual photons. It provides a systematic calculation of isospin-violating ratios that were previously estimated only partially, such as the 7% effect reported by Gibbs (1987) and Matsinos (1997) in the triangle relation R2. The results motivate precise measurements of the isoscalar scattering length a^+ and the pi0 p scattering length, potentially from threshold pion photoproduction experiments as proposed by Bernstein (1998). The framework enables future extension to include Coulomb and soft photon effects for direct comparison with cross section data.
