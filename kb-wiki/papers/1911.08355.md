# Equilibrium constants of Hydrogen and Helium isotopes at low nuclear densities

> Equilibrium constants for Hydrogen and Helium isotopes as a function of density and temperature are measured in the framework of the study made by Qin et al. [Qin L et al 2012 Phys. Rev. Lett. 108 172701]. We review and comment on all stages of the analysis and conclude that our measurements are not inconsistent with Qin et al. results. Improvements are being made to the initial analysis and we raise the issue of the binding energies which has to be clarified.

## Key claim

The authors measure equilibrium constants for Hydrogen and Helium isotopes at low nuclear densities and conclude that their results are "not inconsistent" with those of Qin et al. (2012), but they raise the issue that cluster binding energies are assumed to be vacuum values for density determination, which they consider inconsistent.

## Method

The INDRA 4π multi-detector was used to study four reactions: 136Xe and 124Xe beams at 32 MeV/nucleon on 124Sn and 112Sn targets. Central collisions (reduced impact parameter below 0.15) were selected, and particles emitted in the 60°–90° center-of-mass polar angular range were analyzed to isolate an intermediate-velocity gas source. The surface velocity of particles was reconstructed after subtracting a Coulomb barrier of 10 MeV per unit charge, and the temperature was derived from the relative yields of 2H, 3H, 3He, and 4He using the Albergo thermometer formula. The volume of the gas source was extracted from a thermal equilibrium model, and the density was obtained by adding excluded volumes for the clusters.

## Key numbers

- Bombarding energy: 32 MeV/nucleon for all four Xe+Sn reactions
- Coulomb barrier per unit charge: 10 MeV, with an estimated error of ±2 MeV
- Gas source sizes (Asource): 51±2.2 (124Xe+124Sn), 53±1.6 (136Xe+124Sn), 49±1.6 (124Xe+112Sn), and 51±1.5 (136Xe+112Sn)
- Temperature range: 5 to 9 MeV over the confidence interval of surface velocities between 3 cm/ns and 6.5 cm/ns

## Context

This work extends the study of Qin et al. (2012) on equilibrium constants in low-density nuclear matter, using the INDRA detector with four different entrance channel systems (136,124Xe+124,112Sn) to test assumptions about neutron-to-proton ratios. The results are compared to the thermodynamical path of Qin et al. (2012), obtained at higher bombarding energy (47A MeV) with asymmetric systems (Ar+Sn and Zn+Sn). The paper enables further investigation of the density dependence of the nuclear equation of state relevant for core-collapse supernovae, compact star mergers, and proto-neutron star cooling, while raising the open question of whether vacuum binding energies are appropriate for density determination in such low-density environments.
