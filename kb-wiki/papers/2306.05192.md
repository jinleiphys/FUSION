# Description of the proton-decaying 0$^+_2$ resonance of the $α$ particle

> The recent precise experimental determination of the monopole transition form factor from the ground state of $^4$He to its $0^+_2$ resonance via electron scattering has reinvigorated discussions about the nature of this first excited state of the $α$ particle. The $0^+_2$ state has been traditionally interpreted in the literature as the isoscalar monopole resonance (breathing mode) or, alternatively, as a particle-hole shell-model excitation. To better understand the nature of this state, which lies only $\sim$ 410 keV above the proton emission threshold, we employ the coupled-channel representation of the no-core Gamow shell model. By considering the $[^3$H$ + p]$, $[^3$He$ + n]$, and $[^2$H+$^2$H] reaction channels, we explain the excitation energy and monopole form-factor of the $0^+_2$ state. We argue that the continuum coupling strongly impacts the nature of this state, which carries characteristics of the proton decay threshold.

# Description of the proton-decaying 0⁺₂ resonance of the α particle

## Key claim

The 0⁺₂ state of ⁴He should not be viewed as a breathing mode or particle-hole excitation, but rather as a threshold-aligned broad resonance whose structure is dominated by the interplay of the open [³H + p] channel and closed [³He + n] and [²H+²H] channels, with the monopole form factor showing a strong dependence on the energy distance from the proton threshold.

## Method

The authors employed the no-core Gamow shell model in the coupled-channel representation (NCGSM-CC), decomposing the four-body state into binary reaction channels [³H + p], [³He + n], and [²H+²H]. The Hamiltonian was based on the V_low-k N³LO interaction with Λ = 1.9 fm⁻¹, using a model space of 12 ħω for the harmonic oscillator expansion of channel functions, with the Berggren basis discretized with 90 points and resonant poles added to ensure proper asymptotic description of the 0⁺₂ resonance.

## Key numbers

- The 0⁺₂ state lies at E = 20.1 MeV excitation energy, approximately 410 keV above the proton emission threshold
- The [³He + n] decay threshold lies only 0.37 keV above the proton threshold, while the [²H+²H] threshold lies 3.64 MeV higher
- At ΔE_thr ≈ 150 keV, the [³H + p] channel occupation is ~95%; at the experimental value ΔE_thr ≈ 400 keV, it drops to 32% and the [²H+²H] channel becomes dominant
- The NCGSM-CC predicts a large decay width Γ ≈ 1200 keV at ΔE_thr ≈ 400 keV
- The neutron decay channel opens at ΔE_thr ≈ 0.78 MeV
- The ground state 0⁺₁ energy is about -25 MeV with the chosen parameters (HO length b_HO = 1.8 fm)
- Three-body forces contribute about 1.5 to 2 MeV to the binding energy but have very small effect on the spectrum above the proton decay threshold

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the 0⁺₂ state in ⁴He that interpreted it as a breathing mode (Werntz 1966, Blomqvist 1967) or particle-hole excitation (Szydlik 1970, Flocard 1975), and addresses the recent precise measurement by the A1 Collaboration at MAMI (Kegel 2023) which showed that modern nuclear forces fail to reproduce the monopole form factor. The authors answer affirmatively the question raised by Epelbaum (2023) regarding the relationship between the form factor and the resonance location, and demonstrate that the 0⁺₂ state exhibits characteristics of a threshold-aligned state as described in the framework of Okolowicz (2012, 2013). The approach enables future microscopic descriptions of near-threshold resonances in light nuclei where continuum coupling is essential.
