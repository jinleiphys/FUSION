# Critical lensing and kurtosis near a critical point in the QCD phase diagram in and out-of-equilibrium

> In this work, we study the lensing effect of the QCD critical point on hydrodynamic trajectories, and its consequences on the net-proton kurtosis $κ_4$. Including critical behavior by means of the BEST Collaboration equation of state (EoS), we first consider a scenario in equilibrium, then compare with hydrodynamic 0+1D simulations with Bjorken expansion, including both shear and bulk viscous terms. We find that, both in and out-of-equilibrium, the size and shape of the critical region directly affect if the signal will survive through the dynamical evolution.

## Key claim
The size and shape of the critical region in the QCD phase diagram, determined by non-universal parameters of the Ising-to-QCD mapping, directly control whether a critical lensing signal survives through dynamical evolution, both in equilibrium and in out-of-equilibrium viscous hydrodynamic simulations.

## Method
The authors incorporated critical behavior via the BEST Collaboration equation of state, which maps the 3D Ising model onto QCD variables using a linear map with parameters controlling the critical region's shape. They first studied equilibrium kurtosis across the phase diagram, then performed 0+1D relativistic viscous hydrodynamic simulations with Bjorken expansion, including both shear and bulk viscosities with critical scaling, to examine how hydrodynamic trajectories are deformed by the critical point.

## Key numbers
- Critical point location fixed at T_C = 138 MeV and mu_BC = 420 MeV for all parameter sets studied
- Mapping parameters varied: w = 0.5, 1.0, 2.0 and rho = 0.5, 1.0, 2.0, with alpha_1 = 4.6 degrees and alpha_2 = 94.6 degrees
- Hydrodynamic trajectories selected within a total width of 7 MeV around the isentropic trajectory passing through the critical point
- Three freeze-out scenarios considered: Delta T = T_C - T_FO = 1, 3, 5 MeV
- Bulk viscosity scaling: (zeta T/w)_CS = (zeta T/w) [1 + (xi/xi_0)^3], with bulk viscosity scaling as zeta proportional to xi^3
- Kurtosis kappa_4 sigma^2 = chi_4/chi_2, with kappa_4 proportional to xi^7 at the critical point

## Context
This work extends previous equilibrium studies of kurtosis near the QCD critical point by Stephanov (2011) and Mroczek (2020), which showed that the Ising-to-QCD mapping strongly influences the critical region shape and resulting kurtosis at freeze-out. The authors incorporate out-of-equilibrium effects using the same hydrodynamic framework as Dore (2020), which found that far-from-equilibrium effects can dramatically alter paths through the phase diagram. This study enables future work in higher dimensions with BSQ (baryon number, strangeness, electric charge) diffusion, and provides qualitative guidance for experimental searches at RHIC and FAIR.
