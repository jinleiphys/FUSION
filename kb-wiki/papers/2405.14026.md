# Pion gravitational form factors in the QCD instanton vacuum I

> The pion form factors of the QCD energy-momentum tensor (EMT) are studied in the instanton liquid model (ILM) of the QCD vacuum. In this approach the breaking of conformal symmetry is encoded in the form of stronger-than-Poisson fluctuations in the number of instantons. For the trace of the EMT, it is shown that the gluonic trace anomaly term contributes half the pion mass, with the other half coming from the quark-mass-dependent sigma term. The $Q^2$ dependence of the form factors is governed by glueball and scalar meson exchanges. For the traceless EMT, the spin-0 and 2 form factors are computed at next-to-leading order in the instanton density using effective quark operators. Relations between the gluon and quark contributions to the EMT form factors are derived. The form factors are also expressed in terms of the pion light-front wave functions in the ILM. The results at the low resolution scale of the inverse instanton size are evolved to higher scales using the renormalization group equation. The ILM results compare well with those of recent lattice QCD calculations.

## Key claim
The pion mass is exactly equally split between the gluonic trace anomaly contribution (half) and the quark-mass-dependent sigma term (the other half), a specific consequence of the pion's Goldstone boson nature that differs from other hadrons like the nucleon. This equal splitting holds at leading order in the current quark mass and 1/N_c expansion.

## Method
The authors compute pion gravitational form factors (GFFs) of the QCD energy-momentum tensor using the instanton liquid model (ILM) of the QCD vacuum, where conformal symmetry breaking is encoded in stronger-than-Poisson fluctuations of the instanton number. The trace part of the EMT is obtained from the topological compressibility of the instanton ensemble combined with the pion scalar form factor, while the traceless part is evaluated at next-to-leading order in the instanton density using effective quark operators derived from instanton-antiinstanton molecules. Results at the low resolution scale of the inverse instanton size (mu = 1/rho) are evolved to higher scales using renormalization group equations.

## Key numbers
- The instanton plus anti-instanton density is n_{I+A} = 1/R^4 with R = 1 fm, and the packing fraction is kappa = pi^2 * rho^4 * n_{I+A} = 0.1
- The vacuum topological compressibility is sigma_T / N_bar = 4/b, approximately 1/3 for N_c = 3
- The vacuum expectation value of the trace of the EMT is approximately -10 fm^{-4}
- The pion D-form factor at zero momentum transfer satisfies D(0) = -1 from chiral constraints
- At the resolution mu = 1/rho, the quark and gluon contributions to the gravitational charges are: A_q(0) = 0.9924, A_g(0) = 0.0076, D_q(0) = -0.7539, D_g(0) = 0.03556
- The scalar glueball mass used for the soft regime is m_{0++} = 1.25 GeV

## Context
This work extends the instanton liquid model approach previously applied to nucleon gravitational form factors by Liu, Shuryak, and Zahed (2024) to the pion, which is of special interest as the Goldstone boson of spontaneously broken chiral symmetry. The results are compared to recent lattice QCD calculations by Hackett et al. (2023) and Wang et al. (2024), as well as to chiral perturbation theory predictions. The paper also builds on earlier instanton vacuum studies of the topological compressibility by Diakonov et al. (1995) and Kacir et al. (1996). The derived relations between gluon and quark contributions to the EMT form factors and the connection to pion light-front wave functions enable future studies of the mechanical properties of the pion and comparisons with experimental measurements.
