# Ab initio framework for nuclear scattering and reactions induced by light projectiles

> A quantitative and predictive microscopic theoretical framework that can describe reactions induced by $α$ particles ($^4$He nuclei) and heavier projectiles is currently lacking. Such a framework would contribute to reducing uncertainty in the modeling of stellar evolution and nucleosynthesis and provide the basis for achieving a comprehensive understanding of the phenomenon of nuclear clustering (the organization of protons and neutrons into distinct substructures within a nucleus). We have developed an efficient and general configuration-interaction framework for the description of low-energy reactions and clustering in light nuclei. The new formalism takes full advantage of powerful second-quantization techniques, enabling the description of $α$-$α$ scattering and an exploration of clustering in the exotic $^{12}$Be nucleus. We find that the $^4$He($α$, $α$)$^4$He differential cross section computed with non-locally regulated chiral interactions is in good agreement with experimental data. Our results for $^{12}$Be indicate the presence of strongly mixed helium-cluster states consistent with a molecular-like picture surviving far above the $^6$He+$^6$He threshold, and reveal the strong influence of neutron decay in both the $^{12}$Be spectrum and in the $^6$He($^6$He,$α$)$^8$He cross section. We expect that this approach will enable the description of helium burning cross sections and provide insight on how three-nucleon forces influence the emergence of clustering in nuclei.

## Key claim

The authors developed a new configuration-interaction implementation of the ab initio no-core shell model combined with the resonating group method (NCSM/RGM) that enables, for the first time, the calculation of reactions induced by alpha particles and heavier p-shell projectiles, with the caveat that the formalism loses track of particle identity between projectile and target and becomes equivalent to previous methods only at convergence in the harmonic oscillator expansion.

## Method

The approach uses second-quantization techniques to construct reaction channel states as linear combinations of Slater determinants for the combined target-plus-projectile system, from which the localized components of the non-local integration kernel of the NCSM/RGM coupled-channel equations are computed. The internal structure of both the target and projectile nuclei is obtained from no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations, and the relative motion between the two clusters is expanded in harmonic oscillator radial wave functions. The method is applied to alpha-alpha elastic scattering and to the study of clustering in the 12Be system, including the 6He(6He,alpha)8He transfer reaction.

## Key numbers

* The 4He(alpha,alpha)4He differential cross section computed with non-locally regulated chiral interactions shows good agreement with experimental data (no specific numerical deviation given).
* For n-alpha elastic scattering, differences between the new method and the previous approach of Quaglioni (2009) produce a shift of approximately 50 keV at N_max=11 and 3 keV at N_max=15 in predicted resonance positions.
* The alpha-alpha scattering phase shifts obtained with the NN+3N_loc interaction reach convergence at N_rel approximately 8.

## Context

This work extends the NCSM/RGM framework of Quaglioni (2009) and Navratil (2016) by replacing the previous hybrid approach (which used transition densities for the heavier nucleus) with a fully second-quantized formalism that treats both projectile and target on equal footing. The method enables ab initio calculations of reactions induced by alpha particles and p-shell nuclei, which were previously inaccessible, and opens the door to describing helium burning cross sections and investigating how three-nucleon forces influence nuclear clustering, building on prior microscopic studies of clustering by Freer (2018) and lattice EFT calculations by Elhatisari (2015).
