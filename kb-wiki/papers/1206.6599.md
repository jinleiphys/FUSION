# Baryon properties from the covariant Faddeev equation

> A calculation of the masses and electromagnetic properties of the Delta and Omega baryon together with their evolution with the current quark mass is presented. Hereby a generalized Bethe-Salpeter approach with the interaction truncated to a dressed one-gluon exchange is employed. The model dependence is explored by investigating two forms for the dressed gluon exchange.

## Key claim

The masses and electromagnetic properties of the Delta and Omega baryons are calculated using a covariant three-body Faddeev approach with two different effective interactions (Maris-Tandy and AFW), showing qualitative model independence within the Rainbow-Ladder truncation, but with deviations between models growing as quark masses increase.

## Method

The authors solve the covariant Faddeev equation for three-quark bound states, truncating the interaction to a dressed one-gluon exchange in the Rainbow-Ladder (RL) approximation. Two effective interactions are compared: the Maris-Tandy (MT) model and the AFW model, which combines a gluon dressing function with a model for the quark-gluon vertex. Electromagnetic form factors of the Delta baryon are computed using the "gauging of equations" technique to preserve gauge symmetry.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass: 0.94 GeV (MT), 0.97 GeV (AFW), compared to experimental 0.94 GeV
- Delta mass: 1.26 GeV (MT), 1.22 GeV (AFW), compared to experimental 1.23 GeV
- Omega mass: 1.72 GeV (MT), 1.80 GeV (AFW), compared to experimental 1.67 GeV
- Omega_ccc mass: 4.4 GeV (MT), 4.9 GeV (AFW), compared to lattice 4.7 GeV and pNRQCD 4.9(0.25) GeV
- Omega_bbb mass: 13.7 GeV (MT), 13.8 GeV (AFW), compared to lattice 14.4 GeV and pNRQCD 14.5(0.25) GeV
- Delta+ charge radius <r_E0^2>: 0.67 fm^2 (MT), 0.60 fm^2 (AFW), compared to lattice values ranging from 0.279(6) to 0.373(21) fm^2
- Delta+ magnetic dipole G_M1(0): 2.22 (MT), 2.33 (AFW), compared to lattice values 2.35(16) to 2.68(13) and experimental 3.54(+4.50/-4.72)

## Context

This work extends previous quark-diquark baryon calculations (e.g., hep-ph/9705267, nucl-th/9805054) to a full three-body covariant description, building on recent progress in three-body Bethe-Salpeter studies (arXiv:0912.2246, arXiv:1104.4505). The results enable future calculations of Omega baryon electromagnetic form factors and refinements in numerical techniques, while also highlighting the need to study beyond-RL corrections for heavy quark systems where three-body interactions may become relevant.
