# Centrality dependence of the direct photon yield and elliptic flow in heavy-ion collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV

> We calculate the centrality dependence of direct photons produced in Au+Au collisions at sqrt{s}=200GeV and their transverse momentum spectra within the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach. As sources for 'direct' photons, we incorporate the interactions of quarks and gluons as well as hadronic interactions (pi+pi->rho+gamma, rho+pi->pi+gamma, meson-meson bremsstrahlung m+m->m+m+gamma, meson-baryon bremsstrahlung m+B->m+B+gamma), the decays of phi and a_1 mesons and the photons produced in the initial hard collisions ('pQCD'). We find that the pT spectra of the 'thermal' photons (i.e. the direct photons after the pQCD contribution is subtracted) deviate from exponential distributions and, consequently, observe a strong dependence of the inverse slope parameter T_eff on the fitting range in pT. On the other hand, all the obtained 'effective temperatures' are well above the critical temperature for the deconfinement phase transition even for peripheral collisions, reflecting primarily a 'blue shift' due to radial collective motion of hadrons. Our calculations suggest that the channel decomposition of the observed spectrum changes with centrality with an increasing (dominant) contribution of hadronic sources for more peripheral reactions. Furthermore, the thermal photon yield is found to scale roughly with the number of participant nucleons as N_part^alpha with alpha=1.5, whereas the partonic contribution scales with an exponent alpha_p=1.75. Additionally, we provide predictions for the centrality dependence of the direct photon elliptic flow v_2(pT). The direct photon $v_2$ is seen to be larger in peripheral collisions compared to the most central ones since the photons from the hot deconfined matter in the early stages of the collision carry a much smaller elliptic flow than the final hadrons.

## Key claim

The direct photon elliptic flow \(v_2\) is larger in peripheral collisions than in central ones because the channel decomposition of the direct photon yield changes with centrality, with hadronic sources (which carry a large elliptic flow) dominating in peripheral collisions, while the partonic channels (which produce photons with a small \(v_2\) of about 2%) contribute more in central collisions.

## Method

The authors used the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach to calculate direct photon production in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV for four centrality bins (0-20%, 20-40%, 40-60%, 60-92%). They included partonic sources (quark-gluon plasma interactions and initial hard pQCD collisions) and hadronic sources (meson-meson and meson-baryon bremsstrahlung, \(\pi+\pi\to\rho+\gamma\), \(\rho+\pi\to\pi+\gamma\), and decays of \(\phi\) and \(a_1\) mesons), with the soft-photon approximation used for bremsstrahlung processes.

## Key numbers

* The effective slope parameter \(T_{eff}\) of thermal photon spectra (fit range 0.6–2 GeV) is \(265\pm20\) MeV for 0-20% central collisions, \(260\pm20\) MeV for 20-40%, \(250\pm20\) MeV for 40-60%, and \(260\pm20\) MeV for 60-92% central collisions; all values are well above the critical temperature \(T_c \approx 160\) MeV for deconfinement.
* The thermal photon yield scales with the number of participant nucleons as \(N_{part}^\alpha\) with \(\alpha \approx 1.5\); the partonic contribution scales with exponent \(\alpha_p \approx 1.75\).
* The ratio of QGP photons to all direct photons reaches slightly more than 30% for the most central bin (0-20%) and falls below 10% for the most peripheral bin (60-92%).
* The elliptic flow \(v_2\) of photons from partonic channels is of the order of 2%, while photons from hadronic sources show elliptic flow up to 10%.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous PHSD study of minimum-bias collisions (Linnyk 2013) by providing a centrality-dependent analysis of direct photon spectra and elliptic flow. It addresses the "photon \(v_2\) puzzle" raised by PHENIX measurements (Adare 2008), where direct photon elliptic flow was found to be comparable to that of pions, contradicting earlier expectations that direct photons should carry little flow. The results contrast with event-by-event hydrodynamical calculations (Chatterjee 2013, Shen 2013) that underestimated the direct photon \(v_2\), and suggest that hadronic bremsstrahlung channels, rather than unconventional sources such as the conformal anomaly (Basar 2012), are responsible for the large observed elliptic flow. Future measurements of photon spectra and flow as a function of centrality by the ALICE Collaboration at the LHC (Wilde 2012, Lohner 2012) will help clarify the physical sources.
