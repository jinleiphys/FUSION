# Transport coefficients and quark-hadron phase transition(s) from PLSM in vanishing and finite magnetic field

> In peripheral heavy-ion collisions, localized ,short-lived an extremely huge magnetic field can be generated. Its possible influences on the quark-hadron phase transition(s) and the transport properties of the hadronic and partonic matter shall be analysed from the Polyakov linear-sigma model. Our calculations are compared with recent lattice QCD calculations.

# Transport coefficients and quark-hadron phase transition(s) from PLSM in vanishing and finite magnetic field

## Key claim
The Polyakov linear-sigma model (PLSM) predicts that a finite magnetic field enhances the characteristic peaks in bulk viscosity at the critical temperature and causes the critical temperature to decrease with increasing magnetic field strength (inverse magnetic catalysis), in agreement with lattice QCD calculations at small magnetic fields.

## Method
The author computes bulk and shear viscosities from the PLSM grand potential using the Green-Kubo formalism and relaxation time approximation, with quark dispersion relations modified by Landau quantization in a magnetic field directed along the z-axis. The model incorporates both the Polyakov loop potential for confinement and the linear-sigma model for chiral symmetry, with the quark contribution to the potential summed over light and strange flavors and over Landau levels. Two methods are used to determine the quasi-critical temperature: normalized quark susceptibility and the intersection of the light-quark chiral condensate with deconfinement order parameters.

## Key numbers
- Bulk viscosity to entropy ratio ζ/s shows a sharp peak at T_c, with values at eB = 0.0, 0.2, and 0.4 GeV² compared as functions of temperature at vanishing chemical potential
- Shear viscosity to entropy ratio η/s at eB = 0.0, 0.15, 0.2, and 0.4 GeV² is compared with the Kovtun-Son-Starinets (KSS) bound
- Magnetic susceptibility χ_B is computed at eB = 0.2 GeV² and vanishing baryon chemical potential as a function of temperature
- The critical temperature decreases with increasing eB, with excellent agreement between PLSM and lattice QCD for the range 0 ≤ eB ≤ 0.2 GeV² using the chiral condensate method, and for 0.13 ≤ eB ≤ 0.55 GeV² using the susceptibility method
- The magnetic field at RHIC is approximately m_π² and at LHC is approximately 10-15 m_π²

## Context
This work extends previous PLSM studies by Tawfik and collaborators on thermodynamic quantities, meson masses, and QGP properties in nonzero magnetic fields (Tawfik 2014, 2015). The results for bulk and shear viscosities at vanishing magnetic field are compared with lattice QCD simulations by Meyer (2007), Sakai (2005, 2007), and Karsch (2008), as well as with NJL and DQPM models by Bratkovskaya. The magnetic field dependence of the chiral phase diagram is compared with lattice QCD results from 2014. The work enables improved modeling of hydrodynamic flow in peripheral heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where the generated magnetic field influences transport properties and the nature of the quark-hadron phase transition.
