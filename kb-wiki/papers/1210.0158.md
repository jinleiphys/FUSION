# Probing in-medium spin-orbit potential with intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions

> Incorporating for the first time both the spin and isospin degrees of freedom explicitly in transport model simulations of intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions, we observe that a local spin polarization appears during collision process. Most interestingly, it is found that the nucleon spin up-down differential transverse flow is a sensitive probe of the spin-orbit interaction, providing a novel approach to probe both the density and isospin dependence of the in-medium spin-orbit coupling that is important for understanding the structure of rare isotopes and synthesis of superheavy elements.

## Key claim

The nucleon spin up-down differential transverse flow in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions is a sensitive probe of the in-medium spin-orbit interaction, but the observable's magnitude depends on the still-unknown spin-flip probability after nucleon-nucleon scatterings, and the approach requires future experimental confirmation of spin-related observables at these energies.

## Method

The authors developed a spin- and isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model (SI-BUU12) that incorporates the spin degree of freedom and spin-orbit potentials explicitly for the first time in intermediate-energy heavy-ion simulations. They simulated Au+Au collisions at 50 MeV/nucleon with an impact parameter of 8 fm, calculating the spin up-down differential transverse flow as a function of rapidity for different values of the spin-orbit coupling strength \(W_0\), its density dependence parameter \(\gamma\), and the isospin coupling parameters \(a\) and \(b\). The model also tested three different treatments of nucleon spin after each scattering (flipped, randomized, or unchanged) to study the effect of spin-flip probability.

## Key numbers

- Spin-orbit coupling constant \(W_0\) ranges from about 80 MeV fm\(^5\) to 150 MeV fm\(^5\) in the literature; a 47% increase in \(W_0\) (from the lower to the upper limit) leads to an approximately 40% higher spin up-down differential flow.
- The nuclear matter incompressibility used is \(K_0 = 230\) MeV for symmetric nuclear matter, with symmetry energy \(E_{sym} = 30\) MeV and its density slope \(L = 60\) MeV at saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The spin up-down differential transverse flow is larger for a weaker density dependence of the spin-orbit coupling (smaller \(\gamma\)) when the strength at saturation density is fixed.
- The spin up-down differential flow decreases with increasing spin-flip probability, but remains considerable even with a 100% spin-flip probability assumed.

## Context

This work extends time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations by Maruhn (2006) and Iwata (2011) that first observed local spin polarization in heavy-ion collisions, and contrasts with the lack of spin-orbit studies in intermediate-energy transport models that have focused on the nuclear equation of state and symmetry energy. The approach enables future experiments at facilities like GSI and RIKEN to determine the density and isospin dependence of the in-medium spin-orbit coupling, which is important for understanding the structure of rare isotopes and the synthesis of superheavy elements, as discussed by Sorlin (2012) and others.
