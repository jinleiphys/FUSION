# A new two-component model for hadron production in heavy-ion collisions

> Using the experimental data from the ALICE program on the centrality dependence of the transverse momentum ($p_T$) spectra in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV, we show that the double-Tsallis distribution and the generalized Fokker-Plank (FP) solution can not describe the spectra of pions, kaons and protons from central to peripheral collisions in the entire $p_T$ region, simultaneously. Hence, a new two-component distribution, which is a hydrodynamic extension of the generalized FP solution accounting for the collective motion effect in heavy-ion collisions, is proposed in order to reproduce all the particle spectra. Our results suggest that the particle production dynamics may be different for different particles, especially at very low $p_T$ region.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new two-component distribution, combining a hydrodynamic term with a generalized Fokker-Planck solution, to describe the transverse momentum spectra of pions, kaons, and protons in Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV across all centralities, because the double-Tsallis distribution and the generalized Fokker-Planck solution alone fail to simultaneously fit all particle species over the entire \(p_T\) region.

## Method

The authors fit three models to ALICE data for \(\pi^++\pi^-\), \(K^++K^-\), and \(p+\bar{p}\) spectra in six centrality classes: a double-Tsallis distribution, a generalized Fokker-Planck solution with five free parameters, and a new two-component distribution that adds a hydrodynamic blast-wave term to the generalized Fokker-Planck solution. The quality of each fit is evaluated by the relative discrepancy \(R = (\text{data} - \text{fitted})/\text{data}\) as a function of \(p_T\).

## Key numbers

- The double-Tsallis distribution fits pions and kaons well but shows a "much larger" deviation for protons at \(p_T \leq 2\) GeV/c in central and less central collisions (Figure 1c).
- The generalized Fokker-Planck solution fits kaons and protons well but yields lines "a little bit lower than the data at \(p_T < 1\) GeV/c for pions in central collisions" (Figure 2a).
- For the new two-component distribution applied to pions at 0-5% centrality, the relative discrepancies are all within the range \(-0.1\) to \(0.1\) (Figure 4a).
- For kaons at 0-5% centrality, the double-Tsallis and generalized Fokker-Planck fits show deviations "less than 10%" from data (Figure 4b).
- For the 60-80% peripheral centrality, all three distributions yield deviations "less than 10%" for pions, kaons, and protons (Figure 5).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of hadron production in heavy-ion collisions, where single Tsallis distributions (Tsallis, 1988) and double-Tsallis distributions (Rybczynski, 2014) were applied to charged particles in central Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV. It also builds on a generalized Fokker-Planck solution previously used for central collisions (Zheng, 2015). The new two-component model improves upon the two-component model of Bylinkin (2014, 2015), which used an exponential plus two power-law terms but could not describe very high \(p_T\) charged particles. The results enable better understanding of particle production mechanisms, particularly the different dynamics for pions, kaons, and protons at low \(p_T\), and suggest further studies at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02\) TeV.
