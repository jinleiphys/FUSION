# Comment on "Chiral Corrections in Hadron Spectroscopy"

## Key claim
The principal pattern in baryon spectroscopy associated with the flavor-spin dependent hyperfine interaction arises from the spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry in QCD, and therefore chiral perturbation theory (ChPT), which is based on explicit chiral symmetry breaking, cannot address this pattern. The author argues that the splitting pattern implied by the operator $-\vec{\tau}_i \cdot \vec{\tau}_j \vec{\sigma}_i \cdot \vec{\sigma}_j$ is not inconsistent with chiral symmetry, contrary to the claim by Thomas and Krein (1999).

## Method
The author distinguishes between two aspects of chiral symmetry in QCD: spontaneous breaking (which produces constituent quark masses, massless Goldstone bosons, and their couplings to constituent quarks) and explicit breaking (due to nonzero current quark masses). The author derives the Goldstone boson exchange interaction in the chiral limit as a contact interaction, and contrasts it with the long-range Yukawa potential that appears when explicit chiral symmetry breaking gives the pion a finite mass. The author then shows that the N and Delta mass splitting arises from the contact interaction in the chiral limit, not from the Yukawa tail that ChPT addresses.

## Key numbers
- The N and Delta masses are given by $M_N = M_0 - 15 P_{00}^{\pi}$ and $M_\Delta = M_0 - 3 P_{00}^{\pi}$, where $P_{00}^{\pi}$ is positive, arising from the contact interaction in the chiral limit.
- The long-range Yukawa tail contribution is stated to be "much smaller" than the contact term contribution for the N and Delta, based on nonperturbative calculations by Glozman, Plessas, Varga, and Wagenbrunn (1998).
- For highly excited states, the role of the Yukawa tail increases because of a bigger baryon size.

## Context
This comment directly responds to Thomas and Krein (1999), who questioned the foundations of the chiral constituent quark model developed by Glozman and Riska (1996) and Glozman, Plessas, Varga, and Wagenbrunn (1998). The author argues that ChPT cannot be used to derive or constrain the contact interaction that arises from spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking. The work clarifies that the systematic corrections from finite meson mass should be addressed by a constituent quark chiral perturbation theory, which the author states is awaiting practical implementation.
