# Random matrix analysis of the monopole strength distribution in $^{208}$Pb

> We study statistical properties of the $0^+$ spectrum of $^{208}$Pb in the energy region $E_x\leq20$ MeV. We use the Skyrme interaction SLy4 as our model Hamiltonian to create a single-particle spectrum and to analyze excited states. The finite-rank separable approximation for the particle-hole interaction enables us to perform the calculations in large configuration spaces. We show that while the position of the monopole resonance centroid is determined by one phonon excitations of $0^+$, the phonon-phonon coupling is crucial for the description of a strength distribution of the $0^+$ spectrum. In fact, this coupling has an impact on the spectral rigidity $Δ_3(L)$ which is shifted towards the random matrix limit of the Gaussian orthogonal ensembles.

## Key claim

The phonon-phonon coupling (PPC) is crucial not only for describing the strength distribution of the $0^+$ spectrum in $^{208}$Pb, but specifically for shifting the spectral rigidity $\Delta_3(L)\) toward the Gaussian orthogonal ensemble (GOE) limit of random matrix theory, even though the coupling does not produce visible changes in the spectrum itself.

## Method

The authors used the Skyrme interaction SLy4 to generate a single-particle spectrum via Hartree-Fock calculations, then applied the finite-rank separable approximation (FRSA) to solve the random phase approximation (RPA) equations in a large configuration space. They constructed wave functions as linear combinations of one-phonon and two-phonon RPA configurations (including $0^+$, $1^-$, $2^+$, $3^-$, and $4^+$ phonons with energies below 25 MeV) and diagonalized the Hamiltonian including the coupling matrix elements between one- and two-phonon states. The spectral statistics of the resulting $0^+$ spectrum up to 20 MeV were analyzed using the nearest-neighbor spacing distribution (NNSD) and the Dyson-Metha $\Delta_3$ spectral rigidity.

## Key numbers

- The RPA centroid of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) is found at $E \sim 14.7$ MeV.
- 96% of the non energy-weighted sum rule (NEWSR) is located in the energy region $E_x = 10.5-18$ MeV within the RPA.
- The phonon-phonon coupling decreases the NEWSR to 78% in the ISGMR region ($E_x = 10.5-18$ MeV).
- The PPC induces a 1 MeV downward shift of the main peak.
- The coupling produces a shift of 11% of the NEWSR from the ISGMR region to high energy and 7% to lower energy.
- The lowest $0^+$ state appears at 6.5 MeV, close to the value $\sim 2\hbar\Omega$ with $\hbar\Omega = E_{3_1^-}^{RPA}$.
- The wave function of the $0_1^+$ state contains 4% of the $[0_4^+]_{RPA}$ component.

## Context

This work extends the quasiparticle-phonon model (QPM) approach, previously applied to statistical properties of the pygmy dipole resonance by Volya and Zelevinsky (2006), by using a unified Skyrme interaction (SLy4) for both mean field and residual interaction rather than a Woods-Saxon potential. The finite-rank separable approximation (FRSA) developed by Severyukhin and collaborators (1998, 2002, 2008) enables calculations in large configuration spaces. The results show that two-phonon components of octupole nature ($3^- \otimes 3^-$) provide the dominant contribution driving the system toward GOE statistics, and the authors indicate that further systematic statistical studies of phonon-phonon coupling effects on vibrational spectra and transition strengths are in progress.
