# Investigating the scaling of higher-order flows in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The modified number of constituent quark (NCQ) scaling $v_{n}/n_{q}^{n/2} \sim KE_{T}/n_{q}$ for mesons and baryons and the scaling relation $v_{n} \sim v_{2}^{n/2}$ for higher-order anisotropic flows, which were observed experimentally, have been investigated at top RHIC energy. It has been found that the modified NCQ scaling can not be obtained from the naive coalescence even by taking into account event-by-event fluctuations but may be due to hadronic afterburner or thermal freeze-out. In addition, we observed that the behavior of the $v_{n}/v_{2}^{n/2}$ ratio is sensitive to the partonic interaction, and it is different for mesons and baryons from the naive coalescence but is expected to be almost the same experimentally.

## Key claim

The modified number-of-constituent-quark (NCQ) scaling \(v_{n}/n_{q}^{n/2} \sim KE_{T}/n_{q}\) for higher-order flows cannot be obtained from the naive coalescence model even when event-by-event fluctuations are included, but may instead originate from the hadronic afterburner effect or thermal freeze-out; the scaling relation \(v_{n} \sim v_{2}^{n/2}\) yields different coefficients for mesons and baryons in the naive coalescence picture, whereas experimentally they are expected to be nearly the same.

## Method

The authors used the string-melting version of the AMPT (a multiphase transport) model to generate parton phase-space distributions for Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV, with partonic scattering cross sections of 1.5 mb and 10 mb. They applied an analytical quark coalescence formalism extended to include event-by-event fluctuations, and also employed a generalized blast wave model to simulate thermal freeze-out. The hadronic afterburner effect was isolated by modifying the AMPT model to reject hadrons with large momentum distance between valence quarks (\(\Delta p > 0.2\) GeV/c) and by comparing results before and after hadronic rescatterings.

## Key numbers

- Partonic scattering cross sections of 1.5 mb and 10 mb were used in the AMPT model for Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV.
- In the modified AMPT model, hadrons with valence quark momentum distance \(\Delta p > 0.2\) GeV/c were discarded to study the hadronic afterburner effect.
- The generalized blast wave model used parameters: freeze-out temperature \(T_f = 175\) MeV, source radius \(R = 5.0\) fm, radial flow \(\rho_0 = 0.55\), velocity anisotropy \(\rho_n = 0.43\), and spatial anisotropy \(s_n = -0.05\) fm.
- From the analytical coalescence, the ratio \(C_n^m / C_n^b\) for mesons versus baryons is approximately \(\sqrt{3/2}\) for \(n=3\) and \(3/2\) for \(n=4\).
- The \(v_n/v_2^{n/2}\) ratio for partons from the analytical coalescence is about \(1/\sqrt{2}\) that for mesons and about \(1/\sqrt{3}\) that for baryons for \(n=3\); for \(n=4\) it is about \(1/2\) that for mesons and about \(1/3\) that for baryons.

## Context

This work extends the study of NCQ scaling for elliptic flow (STAR Collaboration, 2004-2008; PHENIX Collaboration, 2007) to higher-order harmonic flows, and investigates the scaling relation \(v_n \sim v_2^{n/2}\) observed by the ATLAS Collaboration (2012). It contradicts the expectation that event-by-event fluctuations in the naive coalescence model (Kolb et al., 2004) could produce the modified NCQ scaling, and instead points to hadronic afterburner effects or thermal freeze-out as the origin. The results enable future work to disentangle hadronization mechanisms from initial-state and viscous effects in the quark-gluon plasma, and clarify that coalescence corrections must be accounted for when using the \(v_n/v_2^{n/2}\) ratio to study initial eccentricities (Lacey et al., 2010, 2011) and QGP viscosity (Gardim et al., 2012).
