# Production of phi and Omega at RHIC in the Recombination Model

> The recombination model is applied to the production of phi and Omega at all pt in central Au+Au collisions. Since no light quarks are involved in the hadronization, those hidden-strange particles present a clean slate for the study of the role of strange quarks in large-pt physics. We find that shower s quarks have negligible effect for pt<6 GeV/c, in which range the thermal s quarks make the dominant contributions to the formation of phi and Omega. We show that the same effective temperature of the s quarks is responsible for the shape of the spectra of both phi and Omega. We predict that the ratio of Omega to phi will show a peak at pt \approx 6 GeV/c due to the effect of the hard partons. We also give reasons on the basis of the pt dependence that phi cannot be formed by means of K+K- coalescence.

## Key claim
The recombination model, applied to central Au+Au collisions at RHIC, predicts that thermal strange quarks dominate the production of phi and Omega mesons for pT < 6 GeV/c, with shower s quarks having negligible effect in that range; the ratio of Omega to phi is predicted to show a peak at pT ≈ 6 GeV/c due to the onset of thermal-shower recombination.

## Method
The authors apply the recombination model to compute the invariant transverse momentum distributions of phi (s sbar) and Omega (s s s) mesons in central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV. The joint parton distributions are decomposed into thermal (T) and shower (S) contributions, with thermal s quarks parameterized by an exponential distribution with parameters C_s and T_s, and shower s quarks derived from hard parton fragmentation functions fixed by prior studies. The phi spectrum is fit to data up to pT=3 GeV/c using only thermal-thermal recombination, and the Omega spectrum is then predicted using the same T_s with an additional normalization factor kappa.

## Key numbers
- Thermal s quark parameters from fitting phi data: C_s = 8.64 (GeV/c)^{-1}, T_s = 0.385 GeV/c.
- Normalization factor for Omega production: kappa = 0.037.
- For comparison, non-strange quark parameters from prior work: C = 23.2 (GeV/c)^{-1}, T = 0.317 GeV/c.
- Shower s quarks become important for phi only above pT ≈ 7 GeV/c; thermal-shower recombination for phi becomes substantial above pT = 5 GeV/c.
- The Omega/phi ratio is predicted to peak at pT ≈ 6 GeV/c with a value less than 0.4, in contrast to the p/pi ratio which peaks at pT ≈ 3 GeV/c with a value exceeding 1.

## Context
This work extends the recombination model previously applied to pions, kaons, and protons (Hwa and Yang, 2004) to the hidden-strange sector, where no light quarks participate. It contradicts models that propose phi formation via K+K- coalescence (e.g., Shor, 1985; Koch et al., 1990) by showing that the pT dependence of the phi spectrum is incompatible with that mechanism. The results enable experimental tests of the recombination model through measurement of the Omega/phi ratio at RHIC, and provide a clean probe of strange quark thermalization and the transition from thermal to shower parton dominance at intermediate pT.
