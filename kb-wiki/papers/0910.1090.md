# Jet tomography of high-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions at next-to-leading order

> We demonstrate that jet observables are highly sensitive to the characteristics of the vacuum and the in-medium QCD parton showers and propose techniques that exploit this sensitivity to constrain the mechanism of quark and gluon energy loss in strongly-interacting plasmas. As a first example, we calculate the inclusive jet cross section in high-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions to ${\cal O}(α_s^3)$. Theoretical predictions for the medium-induced jet broadening and the suppression of the jet production rate due to cold and hot nuclear matter effects in Au+Au and Cu+Cu reactions at RHIC are presented.

## Key claim

Jet observables are highly sensitive to the characteristics of both vacuum and in-medium QCD parton showers, and this sensitivity can be exploited to constrain the mechanism of quark and gluon energy loss in quark-gluon plasmas. The authors present the first calculation of jet cross sections and jet sub-structure for Au+Au and Cu+Cu reactions at RHIC that includes both NLO perturbative effects and effects of the nuclear medium.

## Method

The authors calculate the inclusive jet cross section in high-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions to O(α_s^3) using collinear factorization, with phase space constraints S_2 and S_3 that encode the jet cone radius R and the jet finding algorithm (midpoint cone or k_T). They evaluate the probability distribution P_{q,g}(ε,E) for quarks and gluons to lose a fraction of their energy due to medium-induced bremsstrahlung using the reaction operator approach to non-Abelian energy loss, then determine the fraction f_{q,g} of that energy redistributed inside the jet cone. Cold nuclear matter effects (EMC effect and initial-state energy loss) are included, and the medium-modified jet cross section per binary nucleon-nucleon scattering is computed as a probabilistic superposition of protojets of initially larger energy.

## Key numbers

- For p+p collisions at √s = 200 GeV, variation of renormalization and factorization scales within (E_T/2, 2E_T) leads to less than (+10%, -20%) variation of the jet cross section.
- For Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV with b = 3 fm, the nuclear modification factor R_AA^jet varies continuously with cone radius R; for R ≤ 0.2 the quenching approximates the suppression of inclusive high-p_T particles.
- Cold nuclear matter effects contribute close to 1/2 of the observed attenuation for E_T ≥ 30 GeV in Au+Au collisions.
- For a 30 GeV quark jet with R = 0.4 in Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV, the mean relative jet radius ⟨r/R⟩ in vacuum is 0.271, in the medium is 0.601, and the total is 0.283, giving a QGP-induced broadening Δ⟨r/R⟩ of 4%.
- For the same conditions in Cu+Cu collisions, the vacuum ⟨r/R⟩ is 0.271, medium is 0.640, total is 0.272, giving Δ⟨r/R⟩ of 0.4%.

## Context

This work extends the theory of jets to energetic reactions with large nuclei (A+A), building on NLO calculations for p+p collisions by Ellis, Kunszt, and Soper (1990) and on the reaction operator approach to non-Abelian energy loss by Gyulassy, Levai, and Vitev (2000). The authors demonstrate that jet observables are much more differential than leading particle quenching measurements, which cannot discriminate between competing energy loss mechanisms. The results are of immediate relevance to upcoming PHENIX and STAR experimental measurements at RHIC and will enable first-principles insights into many-body QCD parton dynamics, including the relative importance of collisional versus radiative energy loss and the applicability of weak versus strong jet-medium coupling regimes.
