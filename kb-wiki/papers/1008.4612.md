# Temporal evolution of tubular initial conditions and their influence on two-particle correlations in relativistic nuclear collisions

> Relativistic nuclear collisions data on two-particle correlations exhibit structures as function of relative azimuthal angle and rapidity. A unified description of these near-side and away-side structures is proposed for low to moderate transverse momentum. It is based on the combined effect of tubular initial conditions and hydrodynamical expansion. Contrary to expectations, the hydrodynamics solution shows that the high energy density tubes (leftover from the initial particle interactions) give rise to particle emission in two directions and this is what leads to the various structures. This description is sensitive to some of the initial tube parameters and may provide a probe of the strong interaction. This explanation is compared with an alternative one where some triangularity in the initial conditions is assumed. A possible experimental test is suggested.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a unified description of both near-side and away-side two-particle correlation structures in relativistic nuclear collisions can be achieved through the combined effect of tubular initial conditions and hydrodynamic expansion, but contrary to earlier expectations the high energy density tubes give rise to particle emission in two directions rather than one, and this description is sensitive to the tube's total energy per unit length rather than its radius or energy density separately.

## Method

The authors use a simplified one-tube model based on a parametrization of initial energy density extracted from NeXus event generator output for central Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV per nucleon pair, with transverse expansion computed numerically assuming boost-invariant longitudinal expansion and sudden freeze-out at constant temperature. They then extend the analysis to more realistic NeXus events with multiple tubes, using the same two-dimensional hydrodynamic model, and compare the resulting two-particle correlations with an alternative triangularity-based explanation.

## Key numbers

- Tube radius extracted from NeXus initial conditions is of order 0.9 fm, with maximum energy density of order 12 GeV fm^-3 at proper time 1 fm
- The parametrization places the tube at r_0 = 5.4 fm from the center
- The two peaks in the single-particle angular distribution are separated by approximately 2 radians, more or less independently of transverse momentum
- The height of the two-particle correlation at Delta phi = 0 saturates for tube positions between 5.5 and 8 fm from the center and decreases strongly for smaller distances
- Freeze-out temperature was varied between 130 and 150 MeV without qualitatively affecting the result
- For a single tube in a central collision, the highest peak is approximately twice higher than the two smaller peaks

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous NeXSPheRIO hydrodynamic calculations (Andrade et al., 2008) by providing a detailed mechanistic explanation of how tubular initial conditions generate the observed correlation structures, in contrast to earlier expectations that hydrodynamics would cause tubes to disappear (Shuryak, 2009). The paper compares its local tube-based explanation with the alternative global triangularity approach proposed by Alver and Roland (2010), and suggests that 2+1 particle correlations could experimentally distinguish between these two scenarios, which correspond to different schemes of initial state energy deposition in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
