# Transport model study of nuclear stopping in heavy ion collisions over an energy range from 0.09A GeV to 160A GeV

> Nuclear stopping in the heavy ion collisions over a beam energy range from SIS, AGS up to SPS is studied in the framework of the modified UrQMD transport model, in which mean field potentials of both formed and "pre-formed" hadrons (from string fragmentation) and medium modified nucleon-nucleon elastic cross sections are considered. It is found that the nuclear stopping is influenced by both the stiffness of the equation of state and the medium modifications of nucleon-nucleon cross sections at SIS energies. At the high SPS energies, the two-bump structure is shown in the experimental rapidity distribution of free protons, which can be understood with the consideration of the "pre-formed" hadron potentials.

# Transport model study of nuclear stopping in heavy ion collisions over an energy range from 0.09A GeV to 160A GeV

## Key claim

Nuclear stopping in heavy ion collisions is influenced by both the stiffness of the equation of state and medium modifications of nucleon-nucleon elastic cross sections at SIS energies, while at high SPS energies the two-bump structure in the experimental rapidity distribution of free protons can be understood only when mean-field potentials of both formed and "pre-formed" hadrons (from string fragmentation) are included in the UrQMD transport model.

## Method

The authors used a modified version of the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport model (version 2.0) to simulate central Au+Au collisions at SIS energies (0.09A to 1.5A GeV) and central Au+Au/Pb+Pb collisions at AGS (2A to 11A GeV) and SPS (20A to 160A GeV) energies. The model incorporated mean-field potentials including Skyrme, momentum-dependent, symmetry, Yukawa, Coulomb, and Pauli terms, plus newly added potentials for "pre-formed" hadrons from string fragmentation, and tested three forms of nucleon-nucleon elastic cross sections: free-space cross sections, a density- and momentum-dependent in-medium cross section from extended QHD theory, and a simpler density-dependent reduction.

## Key numbers

- The excitation function of the stopping observable vartl was calculated for central Au+Au collisions over beam energies from 0.09A GeV to 1.5A GeV, with FOPI data used for comparison.
- At SIS energies, nuclear stopping reaches a well-defined plateau of maximal stopping centered around (0.5 ± 0.3)A GeV with a fast drop on both sides.
- For the coalescence model at SIS energies, the parameter set (R0 = 3.0 fm, P0 = 0.2 GeV/c) gave a better description of FOPI data for fragments with proton number Z = 1, 3, and 8 at 0.15A GeV than the set (3.5 fm, 0.2 GeV/c).
- At SPS energy 160A GeV, the rapidity distribution of free protons shows clearly two peaks at y ~ 1.5.
- Rapidity distributions of protons were calculated at AGS energies 2A, 4A, 6A, 8A, and 11A GeV for central Au+Au collisions and at SPS energies 20A, 30A, 40A, 80A, and 160A GeV for central Pb+Pb collisions.
- Rapidity distributions of Λs (sum Λ + Σ0) were calculated for central Pb+Pb collisions at 40A GeV and 160A GeV and compared to NA49 data.

## Context

This work extends earlier UrQMD studies by Bass (1998) and Bleicher (1999) that did not include interactions of newly produced "pre-formed" particles, and by Li (2009) who first showed the two-bump structure in net-proton rapidity distributions at 158A GeV. The study directly compares with FOPI data from Reisdorf (2004) for the vartl observable at SIS energies, with E895 and E802 data at AGS energies, and with NA49 data at SPS energies. The results enable further investigation of the effects of mean-field potentials versus high-mass resonance decays on particle production and collective flows, which the authors note is in progress.
