# Mass spectra and radiative transitions of doubly heavy baryons in a relativized quark model

> We study the mass spectra and radiative decays of doubly heavy baryons within the diquark picture in a relativized quark model. The mass of the $J^P=1/2^+$ $Ξ_{cc}$ ground state is predicted to be 3606 MeV, which is consistent with the mass of $Ξ_{cc}^{++}(3621)$ newly observed by the LHCb collaboration. The predicted mass gap between two $S$ wave states, $Ξ_{cc}^*$ ($J^P=3/2^+$) and $Ξ_{cc}$ ($J^P=1/2^+$), is 69 MeV. Furthermore, the radiative transitions of doubly heavy baryons are also estimated by using the realistic wave functions obtained from relativized quark model. The radiative decay widths of $Ξ_{cc}^{*++} \to Ξ_{cc}^{++}γ$ and $Ξ_{cc}^{*+} \to Ξ_{cc}^+γ$ are predicted to be about 7 and 4 keV, respectively. These predictions of doubly heavy baryons can provide helpful information for future experimental searches.

## Key claim
The paper predicts the mass of the $J^P=1/2^+$ $\Xi_{cc}$ ground state as 3606 MeV, which is consistent with the LHCb observation of $\Xi_{cc}^{++}(3621)$, but the authors note that the predicted mass is 15 MeV lower than the experimental value, leaving open the possibility that the assignment is not definitive.

## Method
The authors use a relativized quark model within a diquark picture, where the two heavy quarks (cc or bb) are treated as a diquark with a size. They first calculate diquark masses and wave functions by solving the Schrodinger-type equation with a relativized potential, then obtain doubly heavy baryon spectra by solving the diquark-quark system, including form factors to account for diquark size effects. Radiative transition widths are computed using a nonrelativistic quark-photon EM coupling operator applied to the realistic wave functions.

## Key numbers
- Ground state $\Xi_{cc}$ ($J^P=1/2^+$) mass: 3606 MeV, compared to the LHCb observed $\Xi_{cc}^{++}(3621)$.
- Mass gap between $S$-wave states $\Xi_{cc}^*$ ($J^P=3/2^+$) and $\Xi_{cc}$ ($J^P=1/2^+$): 69 MeV.
- Radiative decay width for $\Xi_{cc}^{*++} \to \Xi_{cc}^{++}\gamma$: 7.21 keV.
- Radiative decay width for $\Xi_{cc}^{*+} \to \Xi_{cc}^+\gamma$: 3.90 keV.
- Ground state $\Xi_{bb}$ ($J^P=1/2^+$) mass: 10138 MeV.
- Ground state $\Omega_{cc}$ ($J^P=1/2^+$) mass: 3715 MeV.
- Ground state $\Omega_{bb}$ ($J^P=1/2^+$) mass: 10230 MeV.
- Ratio of radiative widths $\Gamma[\Xi_{cc}^{*++} \to \Xi_{cc}^{++} \gamma] / \Gamma[\Xi_{cc}^{*+} \to \Xi_{cc}^+ \gamma]$: 1.85.

## Context
This work extends the relativized quark model of Godfrey, Capstick, and Isgur (1985, 1986) to doubly heavy baryons, which was previously applied to conventional hadrons but not to these systems. The predictions are compared with other theoretical approaches including the relativized quark model of Ebert (2002), nonrelativistic quark models of Gershtein (2000) and Roberts (2007), the Feynman-Hellmann approach of Roncaglia (1995), and lattice QCD of Brown (2014). The results support the assignment of the LHCb $\Xi_{cc}^{++}(3621)$ as the ground state, contradicting the SELEX $\Xi_{cc}^+(3519)$ interpretation. The radiative decay predictions enable future experimental searches for $\Xi_{cc}^{*++}$ via the decay chain $\Xi_{cc}^{*++}\to \Xi_{cc}^{++}\gamma \to \Lambda_c^+ K^- \pi^+ \pi^-\gamma$.
