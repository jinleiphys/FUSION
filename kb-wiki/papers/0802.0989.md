# Modification of the $ω$-Meson Lifetime in Nuclear Matter

> The photo production of $ω$ mesons on the nuclei C, Ca, Nb and Pb has been measured using the Crystal Barrel/TAPS detector at the ELSA tagged photon facility in Bonn. The dependence of the $ω$ meson cross section on the nuclear mass number has been compared with three different types of models, a Glauber analysis, a BUU analysis of the Giessen theory group and a calculation by the Valencia theory group. In all three cases, the inelastic $ω$ width is found to be $130-150 \rm{MeV/c^2}$ at normal nuclear matter density for an average 3-momentum of 1.1 GeV/c. In the restframe of the $ω$ meson, this inelastic $ω$ width corresponds to a reduction of the $ω$ lifetime by a factor $\approx 30$. For the first time, the momentum dependent $ω$N cross section has been extracted from the experiment and is in the range of 70 mb.

# Modification of the ω-Meson Lifetime in Nuclear Matter

## Key claim

The inelastic width of the ω meson in nuclear matter is found to be 130–150 MeV/c² at normal nuclear matter density for an average 3-momentum of 1.1 GeV/c, corresponding to a reduction of the ω lifetime by a factor of approximately 30 in its rest frame. This result is conditional on the comparison of experimental transparency ratios with three different theoretical models (Glauber analysis, BUU transport calculation, and Valencia Monte Carlo simulation), all of which yield consistent width values.

## Method

The CBELSA/TAPS collaboration measured inclusive ω meson photoproduction on C, Ca, Nb, and Pb targets using a 2.8 GeV electron beam at the ELSA tagged photon facility in Bonn, with photon energies from 0.64 to 2.53 GeV. The ω mesons were reconstructed via the ω → π⁰γ decay channel, requiring three neutral hits in the combined Crystal Barrel (CsI(Tl)) and TAPS (BaF₂) detector system covering 99% of 4π solid angle. The transparency ratio, defined as the nuclear cross section divided by A times the free nucleon cross section and normalized to carbon, was compared to three theoretical models to extract the in-medium ω width and, for the first time, the momentum-dependent ωN inelastic cross section via a Glauber eikonal analysis.

## Key numbers

- Inelastic ω width at normal nuclear matter density: 130–150 MeV/c² (nuclear rest frame) for ω mesons with average 3-momentum of 1.1 GeV/c
- Lifetime reduction factor: approximately 30 in the ω rest frame compared to vacuum
- Inelastic ωN cross section extracted from experiment: approximately 70 mb (range given in abstract)
- α values from A^α scaling of cross sections: 0.54–0.74, varying from lower to higher ω 3-momenta
- Systematic uncertainty on transparency ratios: 3.5%
- Photon beam energy range used in analysis: 1.2 GeV to 2.2 GeV
- Target thicknesses: C (20 mm), Ca (10 mm), Nb (1 mm), Pb (0.64 mm), all 30 mm diameter
- Normal nuclear matter density used: ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of vector meson in-medium properties, particularly following measurements of ρ meson broadening in cold nuclear matter (JLab, 2007) and heated matter (NA60, 2006), as well as conflicting reports of ρ mass shifts without broadening (KEK, 2006). The transparency ratio method was previously applied to φ mesons by the LEPS collaboration at Spring8, who reported an unexpectedly large in-medium inelastic φN cross section. The present results supersede an earlier ω meson analysis (Trnka, 2005) that was insensitive to the in-medium decay width due to detector resolution. The extracted momentum-dependent ωN cross section exceeds by a factor of approximately 3 the inelastic ωN cross section used as input in prior BUU calculations (Muehlich, 2005; Muehlich, 2006), indicating room for improvement in theoretical parameterizations.
