# Chiral Cherenkov and chiral transition radiation in anisotropic matter

> A significant contribution to the electromagnetic radiation by a fast electric charge moving in anisotropic chiral matter arises from spontaneous photon radiation due to the chiral anomaly. While such a process, also known as the "vacuum Cherenkov radiation", is forbidden in the QED vacuum, it can occur in chiral matter, where it is more appropriate to call it the "chiral Cherenkov radiation". Its contribution to the radiation spectrum is of order $α^2$ compared to $α^3$ of the bremsstrahlung. I derive the frequency spectrum and the angular distribution of this radiation in the high energy limit. The quantum effects due to the hard photon emission and the fermion mass are taken into account. The obtained spectra are analyzed in the case the quark-gluon plasma and a Weyl semimetal.

## Key claim

Chiral Cherenkov radiation, which is forbidden in the QED vacuum, can occur in anisotropic chiral matter due to the chiral anomaly; its contribution to the radiation spectrum is of order α² compared to α³ for bremsstrahlung, but the process is only allowed when the photon polarization and the angle between the photon direction and the material's anisotropy axis satisfy specific conditions (λ cosβ > 0 and x < x_max).

## Method

The author derives the photon radiation rate from the Maxwell-Chern-Simons effective Lagrangian, which encodes the chiral anomaly through a pseudoscalar field θ with constant spatial gradient b = c_A ∇θ. The electromagnetic field is quantized in the anisotropic dispersive medium described by a dielectric tensor that depends on b, and the radiation rate is computed in leading-order perturbation theory using the high-energy (ultra-relativistic) approximation to obtain closed-form expressions for the frequency spectrum and angular distribution.

## Key numbers

- The chiral anomaly coefficient is c_A = N_c Σ_f q_f² e²/2π²
- For a sample Weyl semimetal, b = (α/π) × 80 eV ≈ 0.19 eV
- For an electron of energy ε = 10 GeV moving parallel to b (β=0), the maximum possible photon energy fraction is x_max = 7.4 × 10⁻³
- The photon emission cone opening angle satisfies ϑ < √(b ε λ cosβ + m²)/ε, which for the benchmark Weyl semimetal gives ϑ < m/ε
- For a u-quark of energy 10 GeV and thermal mass 0.2 GeV in quark-gluon plasma, the spatial gradients of θ are of order π/R ~ 1 GeV
- The total radiation rate W = (1/2)α Q² b |λ cosβ| ln( (m²/ε²) + (b|λ cosβ|/ε) )⁻¹

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of "vacuum Cherenkov radiation" by Lehnert and Potting (2004) as a test of Lorentz symmetry violation, and recent work on chiral transition radiation by Tuchin (2018) and Huang (2018). The author re-derives the photon spectrum using a form of the θ-field appropriate for condensed matter and nuclear physics applications, taking into account quantum corrections from hard photon emission and fermion mass. The results enable experimental detection of CP-odd domains in quark-gluon plasma and provide quantitative predictions for electromagnetic radiation measurements in Weyl semimetals.
