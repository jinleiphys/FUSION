# Electromagnetic field from asymmetric to symmetric heavy ion collision at 200 GeV/c

> Electromagnetic fields produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions are affected by the asymmetry of the projectile-target combination as well as the different initial configurations of the nucleus. In this study, the results of the electric and magnetic fields produced for different combinations of ions, namely $^{12}$C + $^{197}$Au, $^{24}$Mg + $^{197}$Au, $^{64}$Cu + $^{197}$Au, and $^{197}$Au + $^{197}$Au at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV are demonstrated with a multi-phase transport model (AMPT). The configuration of the distribution of nucleons of $^{12}$C is initialized by a Woods-Saxon spherical structure, a three-$α$-clustering triangular structure or a three-$α$-clustering chain structure. It was observed that the electric and magnetic fields display different behavioral patterns for asymmetric combinations of the projectile and target nuclei as well as for different initial configurations of the carbon nucleus. The major features of the process are discussed.

## Key claim

The electric and magnetic fields produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV depend systematically on the asymmetry of the projectile-target combination and on the initial nucleon configuration of the projectile, with the electric field being stronger in asymmetric systems and the magnetic field being stronger in symmetric systems, but only when the fields are calculated at the collision center with a cutoff radius of 0.6 fm to avoid divergence.

## Method

The authors used the multi-phase transport model (AMPT) to simulate collisions of \(^{12}\mathrm{C} + ^{197}\mathrm{Au}\), \(^{24}\mathrm{Mg} + ^{197}\mathrm{Au}\), \(^{64}\mathrm{Cu} + ^{197}\mathrm{Au}\), and \(^{197}\mathrm{Au} + ^{197}\mathrm{Au}\) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV. For the \(^{12}\mathrm{C}\) projectile, three initial nucleon configurations were implemented: a Woods-Saxon spherical distribution, a three-\(\alpha\)-clustering triangular structure, and a three-\(\alpha\)-clustering chain structure. The electromagnetic fields at the collision center (\(\vec{r}=0\), \(t=0\)) were computed using the Lienard-Wiechert potentials with a cutoff of \(r > 0.6\) fm to avoid divergence, and each event was rotated to the participant plane (second-order) before averaging.

## Key numbers

- For the \(^{12}\mathrm{C} + ^{197}\mathrm{Au}\) system, the triangular \(\alpha\)-cluster configuration used \(r_c = 1.8\) fm and \(\sigma_{r_c} = 0.1\) fm; the chain configuration used \(r_c = 2.5\) fm and \(\sigma_{r_c} = 0.1\) fm for two \(\alpha\) clusters, with the third at the center.
- A cutoff radius of \(r > 0.6\) fm was applied to avoid divergence of the electromagnetic fields; tuning the cutoff from 0.3 fm to 0.9 fm produced no appreciable change in results.
- The electric field \(\langle E_x\rangle/m_\pi^2\) peaked at a certain impact parameter and increased with increasing asymmetry from Au+Au to C+Au; the magnetic field \(\langle -B_y\rangle/m_\pi^2\) showed the opposite trend, decreasing with increasing asymmetry.
- For the \(^{12}\mathrm{C} + ^{197}\mathrm{Au}\) system in semi-peripheral collisions, \(\langle E^2\rangle/m_\pi^4\) was largest for the triangular configuration, followed by Woods-Saxon, and smallest for the chain configuration; \(\langle B^2\rangle/m_\pi^4\) was largest for the chain configuration.
- The \(y\)-component of the electric field \(\langle E_y\rangle/m_\pi^2\) and the \(x\)-component of the magnetic field \(\langle B_x\rangle/m_\pi^2\) were zero for all impact parameters and all configurations.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of electromagnetic fields in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, which had focused primarily on symmetric systems such as Au+Au and Pb+Pb, by systematically comparing asymmetric projectile-target combinations. The results are relevant to studies of the chiral magnetic effect (CME) and charge separation over the reaction plane, as discussed in reviews by the authors (2016) and in experimental work by STAR, PHENIX, and ALICE. The inclusion of \(\alpha\)-clustering configurations for \(^{12}\mathrm{C}\) connects to theoretical predictions from fermionic molecular dynamics (Chernykh, 2007) and antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (Kanada-Enyo, 2012), and suggests that system scans of electromagnetic observables could complement collective flow measurements (Cao, 2019; Zhang, 2017) as probes of nuclear structure in ultra-relativistic collisions.
