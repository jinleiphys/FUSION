# Chiral Vortical Effect For An Arbitrary Spin

> The spin Hall effect of light attracted enormous attention in the literature due to the ongoing progress in developing of new optically active materials and metamaterials with non-trivial spin-orbit interaction. Recently, it was shown that rotating fermionic systems with relativistic massless spectrum may exhibit a 3d analogue of the spin Hall current -- the chiral vortical effect (CVE). Here we show that CVE is a general feature of massless particles with an arbitrary spin. We derive the semi-classical equations of motion in rotating frame from the first principles and show how by coordinate transformation in the phase space it can be brought to the intuitive form proposed in [1]. Our finding clarifies the superficial discrepancies in different formulations of the chiral kinetic theory for rotating systems. We then generalize the chiral kinetic theory, originally introduced for fermions, to an arbitrary spin and study chirality current in a general rotating chiral medium. We stress that the higher-spin realizations of CVE can be in principle observed in various setups including table-top experiments on quantum optics.

## Key claim

The chiral vortical effect (CVE) is a general feature of massless particles with arbitrary spin, not only fermions, and the authors derive the semi-classical equations of motion in a rotating frame from first principles, showing that different formulations in the literature are related by a phase space coordinate transformation.

## Method

The authors derive semi-classical equations of motion for massless particles of arbitrary spin in a rotating frame using a path integral formalism with Berry phase, then generalize the chiral kinetic theory originally developed for fermions. They compare two approaches: an intuitive picture where the Coriolis force replaces the Lorentz force, and a first-principles derivation starting from a microscopic Hamiltonian that includes spin-orbit coupling and angular momentum coupling to rotation. They show that the two sets of equations are related by a phase space coordinate transformation at leading order in ℏ.

## Key numbers

- The Berry curvature for a particle of spin s is given by **b = ± s p̂ / |p|²**, where the sign corresponds to the two helicities.
- The CVE chirality current for arbitrary spin s is **J_χ = ℏ (s Ω / π²) Σ_± ∫₀^∞ f_±(|p|) |p| d|p|**, where the sum is over left- and right-handed particles.
- For fermions (s = 1/2), the CVE current reduces to **J_χ = ℏ ( (μ₊² + μ₋²) / (4π²) + T²/6 ) Ω**, where μ₊ and μ₋ are chemical potentials for right- and left-handed fermions.
- The velocity along the trajectory, expanded to linear order in Ω, contains a term **s ℏ Ω_⟂ / |p|** arising from non-inertial effects and Berry phase.

## Context

This work extends the chiral kinetic theory, originally developed for fermions by Stephanov and Son (2012) and others, to massless particles of arbitrary spin, including photons (spin 1). It builds on the photonic CVE derived by Avkhadiev, Sadofyev, and Zakharov (2017) and the semi-classical treatment by Yamamoto (2017), and clarifies discrepancies between different formulations of chiral kinetic theory in rotating systems. The results enable experimental study of higher-spin CVE in quantum optics and table-top experiments, and connect the spin Hall effect of light to the chiral vortical effect at the single-particle level.
