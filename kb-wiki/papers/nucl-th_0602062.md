# A scalar two-level boson model to study the IBM phase diagram in the Casten triangle

> We introduce a simple two-level boson model with the same energy surface as the Q-consistent Interacting Boson Model Hamiltonian. The model can be diagonalized for large number of bosons and the results used to check analytical finite-size corrections to the energy gap and the order parameter in the critical region. \

## Key claim

The paper introduces a scalar two-level boson model (the $st$ model) whose energy surface in the thermodynamic limit coincides with that of the Q-consistent Interacting Boson Model (IBM) Hamiltonian when axial symmetry is imposed (the $\gamma$ degree of freedom is frozen at $\gamma=0$), but the model is simpler and can be diagonalized for much larger boson numbers than standard IBM codes allow.

## Method

The authors constructed a Hamiltonian $H = x n_t - \frac{1-x}{N} Q^y Q^y$ with two species of scalar bosons $s$ and $t$, where $x$ and $y$ are control parameters. They derived the energy surface in the large-$N$ limit using coherent states and showed its equivalence to the IBM energy surface by setting $y = -\sqrt{2/7} \chi$. Finite-size corrections were computed in the symmetric phase using a Holstein-Primakoff boson expansion followed by a canonical transformation to diagonalize the Hamiltonian order by order in $1/N$, up to order $(1/N)^1$.

## Key numbers

* The critical point for the second-order transition at $y=0$ is $x_c = 4/5$.
* For $y \neq 0$, the critical point is given by $x_c = (4 + y^2)/(5 + y^2)$; for $y = 1/\sqrt{2}$, $x_c = 9/11$.
* The jump of the order parameter at the first-order transition is $y^2/(4 + y^2)$, which vanishes for $y=0$.
* At the critical point $x = x_c$, the equilibrium deformation parameter in the broken phase is $\beta_0 = y/2$.
* The gap at the critical point decays exponentially as $\Delta \sim e^{-A N}$, in contrast to the $y=0$ case where $\Delta \sim N^{-1/3}$.

## Context

This work extends the study of quantum phase transitions in the Interacting Boson Model (IBM) of nuclear structure, specifically within the Casten triangle. The integrable line corresponding to the $U(5)$ to $O(6)$ transition had been solved exactly using Richardson's boson pairing model (Richardson, 1968; Pan, 1998; Dukelsky, 2001; Arias, 2003), but the interior of the triangle and the other two sides were inaccessible to large-scale numerical studies. The present $st$ model enables numerical calculations for large boson numbers along any trajectory in the Casten triangle, allowing tests of analytical finite-size corrections. The authors note that beyond mean-field level, the full IBM may have different properties due to dynamics in the full ($\beta$, $\gamma$) space, and that extension of this formalism to the IBM is planned for a future publication.
