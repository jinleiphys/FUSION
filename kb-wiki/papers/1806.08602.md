# A framework for the chiral extrapolation of the charmed baryon ground-state masses

> We consider the chiral Lagrangian for charmed baryon fields with $J^P =\frac{1}{2}^+$ or $J^P =\frac{3}{2}^+$ quantum numbers. A chiral expansion framework for the baryon ground state masses is worked out to N$^3$LO as to compute their dependence on the up, down and strange quark masses for finite box QCD lattice simulations. It is formulated in terms of on-shell meson and baryon masses. The convergence of such a scheme is illustrated with physical masses as taken from the PDG. The counter terms relevant at N$^3$LO are correlated systematically by large-$N_c$ sum rules to leading and subleading order in a manner that keeps the renormalization scale invariance of the approach.

## Key claim

The paper develops a chiral expansion framework for charmed baryon ground-state masses up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^3$LO) that is formulated in terms of on-shell meson and baryon masses rather than bare masses, which the authors argue is necessary because a conventional chiral expansion around the flavor SU(3) limit using bare masses "is not convergent for the physical up, down and strange quark masses" and "should be abandoned."

## Method

The authors construct the chiral Lagrangian for charmed baryon fields with $J^P = \frac{1}{2}^+$ (flavor anti-triplet and sextet) and $J^P = \frac{3}{2}^+$ (flavor sextet) quantum numbers, including symmetry-breaking counter terms at NLO and N$^3$LO. They compute baryon self-energies from tree-level, tadpole, and one-loop bubble contributions, and correlate the counter terms at N$^3$LO using large-$N_c$ sum rules to leading and subleading order while maintaining renormalization scale invariance. The framework is designed for application to finite-box QCD lattice simulations.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous chiral extrapolation frameworks for baryon octet and decuplet masses (Lutz 2018, Guo 2018, Bavontaweepanya 2018) to the charmed baryon sector. It complements earlier derivations of counter terms constrained by heavy-quark spin symmetry and large-$N_c$ sum rules (Lutz 2014, Heo 2018) by providing explicit N$^3$LO expressions. The framework enables future fits to lattice QCD data on charmed baryon masses from groups including Liu (2009), Bali (2012), Briceno (2012), Alexandrou (2012), Namekawa (2013), and Perez-Rubio (2013), which the authors note is a "rather scarce data set."
