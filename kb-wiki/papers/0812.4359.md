# Formation spectra of light kaonic nuclei by in-flight (${\bar K},N$) reactions with chiral unitary amplitude

> We study theoretically the in-flight ($K^-,N$) reactions for the formation of light kaonic nuclear systems to get deeper physical insights on the expected spectra, and to investigate the experimental feasibility of the reaction at new facilities like J-PARC. We show the expected spectra for the formation of the $K^-pp, K^-pn$, $K^-nn$ and $K^-$-$^{11}$B systems which are accessible by the ($K^-,N$) experiments. By considering the conversion part of the Green's function, we can show the missing mass spectra of the ($K^-,N$) reactions coincidence with the particle emissions due to ${\bar K}$ absorption in ${\bar K}N\to πY$ processes. To calculate the cross sections, we use the so-called $Tρ$ approximation to evaluate the optical potential. As for the amplitude $T$, we adopt the chiral unitary amplitude of ${\bar K}N$ channel in vacuum for simplicity, and we also check the medium effects by applying the chiral amplitude at finite density. The effects of the p-wave optical potential of $Σ$(1385) channel and the contribution from ${\bar K^0}$ mixing in $^3$He($K^-,n$) reaction are also evaluated numerically. To understand the meanings of the spectrum shape, we also study the behavior of the poles of kaon Green's function in nuclear matter. We conclude that $^3$He($K^-,n$) and $^3$He($K^-,p$) reactions coincident with the $πΣ$ emission due to ${\bar K}$ absorption may show the certain structure in the bound region spectra indicating the existence of the unstable kaonic nuclear bound states. As for the $^{12}$C($K^-,p$) spectra with the $πΣ$ emission, we may also observe the structure in the bound region, however, we need to evaluate the medium effects carefully for larger nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper claims that semi-exclusive ($K^-,N$) spectra measured in coincidence with $\pi\Sigma$ emission from $\bar K$ absorption may show structure in the bound region indicating unstable kaonic nuclear bound states, but the authors hedge that for larger nuclei like $^{12}$C, medium effects must be evaluated carefully before drawing conclusions.

## Method

The authors used the Green's function method with a $T\rho$ approximation to construct the $\bar K$-nucleus optical potential, adopting the chiral unitary $\bar K N$ scattering amplitude in vacuum. They solved the Klein-Gordon equation self-consistently for the complex kaon energy in finite nuclear systems, and decomposed the nuclear response function into escape and conversion parts to calculate semi-exclusive spectra for the $^3$He($K^-,n$), $^3$He($K^-,p$), $t(K^-,p$), $t(K^-,n$), and $^{12}$C($K^-,p$) reactions at an incident kaon kinetic energy of 600 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of $K^-pp$ $1s$ state: 23.0 MeV with width 41.3 MeV
- Binding energy of $K^-pn$ $1s$ state: 12.9 MeV with width 36.4 MeV
- Binding energy of $K^-pp$ $2s$ state: 9.0 MeV with width 36.8 MeV
- Binding energy of $K^-pp$ $2p$ state: 11.5 MeV with width 38.5 MeV
- Binding energy of $K^-pn$ $2p$ state: 8.6 MeV with width 35.4 MeV
- Transferred momentum in the ($K^-,N$) reaction: about 200 MeV/c in the lab frame
- Nuclear density parameters for $pp$ and $nn$ systems: radius $R=1.01$ fm, diffuseness $a=0.50$ fm, giving central density 0.117 fm$^{-3}$
- Nuclear density parameters for $pn$ system: radius $R=1.40$ fm, diffuseness $a=0.51$ fm
- $^{11}$B density parameters: $R=2.23$ fm, $a=0.44$ fm
- Nucleon separation energies used: 7.72 MeV for $^3$He and 8.48 MeV for $t$

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies of kaonic nuclear states using the Green's function method (Gata et al., 2006; Koike et al., 2007) and the effective number approach (Gata et al., 2005), which found no clear peak structure in inclusive missing-mass spectra due to large $\bar K$ absorption width. The novelty is the calculation of semi-exclusive spectra coincident with $\pi\Sigma$ emission, which may reveal bound-state structure. The results are intended to guide upcoming experiments at J-PARC, particularly the proposed $^3$He($K^-,n$) reaction for $K^-pp$ formation, and to address controversies between theoretical predictions and the FINUDA experiment (2005) regarding the existence of kaonic nuclear bound states.
