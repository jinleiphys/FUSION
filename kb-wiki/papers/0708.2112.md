# In-Medium Hadronic Interactions and the Nuclear Equation of State

> Microscopic studies of nuclear matter under diverse conditions of density and asymmetry are of great contemporary interest. Concerning terrestrial applications, they relate to future experimental facilities that will make it possible to study systems with extreme neutron-to-proton ratio. In this talk, I will review recent efforts of my group aimed at exploring nuclear interactions in the medium through the nuclear equation of state (EoS). The approach we take is microscopic and relativistic, with the predicted EoS properties derived from realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials. I will also discuss work in progress. Most recently, we completed a DBHF calculation of the $Λ$ hyperon binding energy in nuclear matter.

# In-Medium Hadronic Interactions and the Nuclear Equation of State

## Key claim
The Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach, using the Bonn B potential as the starting free-space interaction, can predict the nuclear equation of state (EoS) properties of symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter, including spin-polarized neutron matter and hyperon binding energies, with results that compare reasonably well with empirical constraints, though the author notes that major model dependence remains for parameters such as the skewness K' and the symmetry pressure L, and that no reliable constraints on the spin Landau parameter G0 are yet available.

## Method
The author applies the DBHF approach to asymmetric nuclear matter using the Bonn B nucleon-nucleon potential, following methods previously developed by the Oslo group. For spin-polarized neutron matter, a coupled self-consistency scheme is developed to calculate the EoS, where single-particle potentials for upward and downward polarized neutrons are obtained self-consistently with the effective interaction. For hyperon calculations, the latest Jülich nucleon-hyperon potential (2005) is used in a Brueckner calculation with angle-averaged Pauli blocking and dispersive effects, with the Λ binding energy extracted from the single-particle potential at zero momentum.

## Key numbers
- Saturation energy of symmetric nuclear matter: -16.14 MeV
- Saturation density: 0.185 fm⁻³
- Incompressibility K: 259 MeV
- Skewness parameter K': 506 MeV
- Symmetry energy at saturation density: 33.7 MeV
- Symmetry pressure L at saturation density: 70.1 MeV
- Λ hyperon binding energy in nuclear matter: approximately 49 MeV at k_F^N = 1.35 fm⁻¹ (using the new Jülich NY potential)
- DBHF effect reduces the Λ binding energy by 3-4 MeV
- Ratio χ_F/χ (free Fermi gas susceptibility over calculated susceptibility) ranges from 2.1455 at ρ/ρ₀ = 0.5383 to 2.6548 at ρ/ρ₀ = 3.7457
- Empirical constraint on symmetry pressure L: 62 MeV < L < 107 MeV (from neutron skin thickness and isospin diffusion data)

## Context
This work extends the DBHF approach of the Oslo group (1990s) and earlier Brueckner calculations of hyperon binding by Reuber et al. (1994), using the updated Jülich nucleon-hyperon potential of 2005 which incorporates correlated ππ and K̄K exchange. The results are compared with constraints from heavy-ion collision data and compact star phenomenology as compiled by Klähn et al. (2006). The work enables future self-consistent DBHF calculations of nucleon, Λ, and Σ potentials for diverse hyperon concentrations, and aims to inform understanding of neutron star maximum masses and the behavior of the nuclear force in the medium under extreme isospin and spin asymmetry.
