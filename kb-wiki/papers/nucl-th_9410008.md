# Chaos {\sl vs} Thermalization in the Nuclear Shell Model

> Generic signatures of quantum chaos found in realistic shell model calculations are compared with thermal statistical equilibrium. We show the similarity of the informational entropy of individual eigenfunctions in the mean field basis to the thermodynamical entropy found from the level density. Mean occupation numbers of single-particle orbitals agree with the Fermi-Dirac distribution despite the strong nucleon interaction.

# Chaos vs Thermalization in the Nuclear Shell Model

## Key claim
The informational entropy of individual eigenfunctions in the mean field basis closely matches the thermodynamic entropy derived from the level density, and mean occupation numbers of single-particle orbitals follow a Fermi-Dirac distribution, but only when the self-consistent mean field and residual interactions are both present at realistic strengths; with artificially suppressed or enhanced diagonal matrix elements, the entropies diverge.

## Method
The authors performed nuclear shell model calculations for 12 particles in the sd-shell using the Wildenthal interaction, constructing many-body basis states with good quantum numbers (J, M, parity, isospin). They diagonalized the Hamiltonian for states with JπT = 0+0 (model space dimension N=839) and 2+0 (N=3273), and computed eigenfunction amplitudes, informational entropy, thermodynamic entropy from level density, and single-particle occupation numbers. They compared these quantities across three regimes: enhanced diagonal matrix elements (factor 10), realistic interaction, and reduced diagonal matrix elements (factor 10).

## Key numbers
- For the 0+0 states (N=839), the level density follows a Gaussian fit with centroid at E0 = -90 MeV and variance σE = 13 MeV.
- In the middle of the spectrum for realistic interaction, the localization length Nα ≈ 0.9N, indicating near-total mixing.
- For the 0+0 class, the chaotic maximum of exp(Sα) is 0.48N = 404.
- Effective single-particle energies extracted from occupation numbers: e(d5/2) - μ = -3.4 MeV and e(d3/2) - μ = 4.7 MeV, compared to the shell model spin-orbit splitting of 7.2 MeV near the ground state.
- The Hamiltonian matrix has diagonal elements spread from -120 to -60 MeV and off-diagonal elements with average value about 0.5 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the study of quantum chaos in nuclear shell model calculations by Horoi, Zelevinsky, and Brown (1994) and connects it to statistical thermodynamics, building on earlier work by French and Ratcliff on statistical spectroscopy. The results show that for realistic interactions, the system reaches thermal equilibrium with quasiparticle behavior, contradicting the expectation that strong interactions would destroy the quasiparticle picture. This enables future calculations of matrix elements between compound states using effective single-particle energies, as suggested by Fvorov.
