# The electric form factor of the neutron and its chiral content

> Considering the nucleon as a system of confined valence quarks surrounded by pions we derive a Galster-like parameterization of the neutron electric form factor $G_E^n$. Furthermore, we show that the proposed parameterization can be linked to properties of the pion cloud. By this, the high quality data for the pion form factor can be used in predictions of $G_E^n$ in the low $Q^2$ region, where the direct double polarization measurements are not available.

## Key claim

The paper derives a theoretical justification for the empirical Galster parameterization of the neutron electric form factor \(G_E^n\) by linking its parameters to the pion cloud content of the nucleon, specifically showing that the shape parameter \(b\) can be related to the pion electromagnetic form factor and the magnitude parameter \(a'\) to the spectroscopic strength of pions in the nucleon.

## Method

The authors start from a chiral quark model (\(\chi\)QM) with an effective \(SU(2)_R \times SU(2)_L\) Lagrangian, treating the nucleon as a system of confined valence quarks surrounded by a pion cloud. They compute the one-pion loop contributions to the neutron electric form factor in the Breit frame, then simplify the resulting expressions in the low \(Q^2\) limit to obtain a Galster-like parameterization where the quark core electric form factor is approximated by the dipole form \(G_D(Q^2)\) and the pion form factor \(F_\pi(Q^2)\) is taken from electroproduction data as a monopole term.

## Key numbers

- The neutron charge radius \(\langle r^2 \rangle_n = -0.115 \pm 0.003\) fm\(^2\) from Kopecky (1997) is used as an experimental constraint.
- The pion form factor cutoff mass squared is \(\Lambda_\pi^2 = 0.53\) GeV\(^2\) from Bebek (1978) and Volmer (2001).
- Fits to \(G_E^n\) polarization data from FriedrichW and Becker yield \(a' = 0.369 \pm 0.008\) and \(b = 4.69 \pm 0.11\) with \(\Lambda^2 = 0.71\) GeV\(^2\) fixed (chi-squared/ndof = 9.9/14).
- Fits to pion data from Bebek yield \(a' = 0.221 \pm 0.002\) and \(b = 8.21 \pm 0.16\) with \(\Lambda^2 = 0.71\) GeV\(^2\) fixed (chi-squared/ndof = 34.2/19).
- A fit to polarization data with \(a'\) fixed to 0.26 and \(b\) fixed to 6.65 gives \(\Lambda^2 = 0.86 \pm 0.04\) GeV\(^2\) (chi-squared/ndof = 9.7/15), corresponding to a core radius \(\sqrt{\langle r^2 \rangle} = 0.74\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological Galster parameterization (Galster, 1971) by providing a theoretical foundation based on the chiral quark model of Thomas and Weise (2001), where the pion cloud is treated as a dynamical degree of freedom. The paper enables precise predictions of \(G_E^n\) at low \(Q^2\) (below about 0.3 GeV\(^2\)) using high-quality pion form factor data from electroproduction experiments (Bebek, 1978; Volmer, 2001), a region where direct double polarization measurements are difficult due to final state interaction corrections. The authors also note that an analogous prescription could be applied to the strange electric form factor of the nucleon.
