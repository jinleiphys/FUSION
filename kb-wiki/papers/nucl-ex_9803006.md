# Nuclear Matter Expansion Parameters from the Measurement of Differential Multiplicities for Lambda Production in Central Au+Au Collisions at AGS

> The double differential multiplicities and rapidity distributions for Lambda hyperon production in central Au+Au interactions at AGS in the range of rapidities from 1.7 to 3.2 and the range of transverse kinetic energies from 0.0 to 0.7 GeV are parametrized in terms of the the Blast Wave approximation. The longitudinal and transverse radial expansion parameters and the mean temperature of Lambda hyperons after the freeze-out of the nuclear matter are presented. The predictions of the RQMD model with and without mean field potentials are compared to our data. Both variants of RQMD are parameterized in terms of the Blast Wave model and the results of such parameterizations are compared to the experimental ones. It is found that inclusion of the mean field potentials in RQMD is essential to account for the strong expansion observed in the data.

## Key claim

The double differential multiplicities for Lambda hyperon production in central Au+Au collisions at AGS can be described by an aspherical blast wave with a single temperature, contrary to earlier claims that a single temperature and transverse expansion velocity could not describe such spectra. The inclusion of mean field potentials in the RQMD model is essential to account for the strong expansion observed in the data.

## Method

The E891 collaboration measured Lambda hyperon production in central Au+Au collisions at 11.6 x A GeV/c using three Time Projection Chambers in the MPS magnet at BNL AGS, covering rapidities from 1.7 to 3.2. The double differential multiplicities were parametrized using a Blast Wave approximation with a continuous superposition of flowing Boltzmann sources, where the transverse velocity profile and emission power were described by functions of the longitudinal boost angle. The RQMD model predictions with and without mean field potentials were compared to the experimental data, with both variants also parametrized in terms of the Blast Wave model.

## Key numbers

- Beam momentum: 11.6 x A GeV/c Au beam on a 0.025 cm Au target (7.5% radiation lengths)
- Central event selection: total charged track multiplicities >220, corresponding to a measured cross section of 270 mb (approximately 4.4% of the geometric Au+Au cross section)
- Final data sample: 3932 Lambda hyperons above background for rapidity range 1.4 < y < 3.2 and transverse kinetic energy m_t - m_0 < 0.7 GeV/c^2
- Lambda acceptance: 2-3% including correction for neutral decays
- Effective mass selection window: 1.104-1.128 GeV/c^2 for Lambda candidates
- Efficiency corrections for rapidity bins integrated over m_t: 1.03 for 1.7 < y < 2.0, 1.10 for 2.0 < y < 2.3, 1.21 for 2.3 < y < 2.6, and 1.36 for 2.6 < y < 2.9

## Context

This work extends the study of collective nuclear flow in heavy-ion collisions, which directly probes the equation of state of nuclear matter and can provide information about possible phase transitions as discussed by Hung and Rischke. The authors build on the Bjorken scenario of one-dimensional longitudinal expansion and the Siemens-Rasmussen spherical blast wave model used for Bevalac energies, as well as the work of Braun-Munzinger et al. (Stachel) who used a similar approach for Si+Al interactions at AGS with temperature T=120 MeV and mean longitudinal expansion velocity <v_l>=0.52. The results enable more precise determination of nuclear matter expansion parameters and constrain the compressibility of hadronic matter at baryon densities far above normal.
