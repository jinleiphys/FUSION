# Mechanical Analogy for the Wave of Nuclear Burning

> We consider a model of neutron-nuclear wave burning. The wave of nuclear burning of the medium is initiated by an external neutron source and is the basis for the new generation reactors -- the so-called "traveling-wave reactors". We develop a model of nuclear wave burning, for which it is possible to draw an analogy with a mechanical dissipative system. Within the framework of the new model, we show that two burning modes are possible depending on the control parameters: a traveling autowave and a wave driven by an external neutron source. We find the autowave to be possible for certain neutron energies only, and the wave velocity has a continuous spectrum bounded below.

## Key claim
The paper shows that a traveling autowave mode of nuclear burning is possible only for certain neutron energies, and the wave velocity has a continuous spectrum bounded below, contrary to the abstract's implication that both modes are equally accessible.

## Method
The authors develop a system of kinetic equations for neutron and nuclide concentrations (²³⁸U, ²³⁹U, ²³⁹Pu) in the diffusion approximation, simplified by assuming equal mean free times for all relevant nuclear reactions. They transform the system into an autowave form using the substitution z = y + vt, then derive an integro-differential equation that is mapped onto Newton's second law by treating the integrated neutron concentration as a mechanical coordinate. The resulting mechanical analogy yields an energy conservation equation with a potential energy function U(N) and a viscous dissipation term, allowing analysis of equilibrium conditions for autowave solutions.

## Key numbers
- The ratio of neutron mean free time τ to β-decay time τ_β is ε ≈ 10⁻¹⁴ (dimensionless), where τ ≈ 10⁻⁷ s and τ_β ≈ 3 days.
- The characteristic β-decay time is τ_β = 2.3/ln(2) ≈ 3.3 days for the ²³⁸U → ²³⁹Pu chain.
- The wave velocity v must satisfy (v/ε)² exp(-2N₀) - 4 ≥ 0 to avoid non-physical negative neutron concentrations, where N₀ is the equilibrium value of the integrated neutron concentration.

## Context
This work extends the kinetic model of L.P. Feoktistov (1998) for traveling-wave reactors (also called Feoktistov reactors, Traveling-wave reactors in the USA, or CANDLE reactors in Japan) by introducing a mechanical analogy that clarifies the conditions for autowave existence. The paper addresses the lack of information about how burning modes depend on external neutron source characteristics, which was noted as an open problem in prior studies (e.g., Feoktistov 1998, and numerical simulations by various authors cited as references 3, 16, 29, 32, 33, 34, 41). The mechanical analogy enables future determination of wave velocity criteria and reactor heat power, which were previously undefined.
