# Pion-nucleon sigma term $σ_{πN}$ and deeply bound pionic atoms

> We investigate the possibility to determine the value of the pion-nucleon sigma term $σ_{πN}$ precisely by the experimental observables of the deeply bound pionic atoms. We discuss the sensitivity of the observables to $σ_{πN}$ and take into account the typical errors of the up-to-date experiments of the deeply bound pionic atoms. We find that the gap of the binding energies and the width of the deeply bound pionic states are good observables for the $σ_{πN}$ value determination by the experimental data. We also discuss the expected difficulties for the accurate determination of the value of $σ_{πN}$ due to the correlation between the $σ$ term and the potential parameter in the pion-nucleus optical potential.

## Key claim

The authors investigate whether the pion-nucleon sigma term σπN can be determined precisely from deeply bound pionic atom observables, but they find that a strong correlation between σπN and the potential parameter ReB0 in the pion-nucleus optical potential creates difficulties for accurate simultaneous determination of both parameters.

## Method

The authors solve the Klein-Gordon equation with an Ericson-Ericson type optical potential for pionic atoms in Sn isotopes, embedding the σπN term into the density-dependent s-wave isovector parameter b1(ρ) via the Tomozawa-Weinberg and Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relations, and into the isoscalar parameter b0(ρ) via double scattering effects. They calculate binding energies, widths, and formation spectra for the (d,3He) reaction using the effective number approach, varying σπN between 25 and 60 MeV to study sensitivities.

## Key numbers

- For 111Sn, the shift of the 1s binding energy |ΔBπ(1s)| = 7.5 keV per 1 MeV change in σπN
- For 111Sn, the shift of the 1s width |ΔΓπ(1s)| = 12.9 keV per 1 MeV change in σπN
- For 111Sn, the sensitivity of the energy gap |Δ(Bπ(1s)-Bπ(2p))| = 5.8 keV per 1 MeV change in σπN
- For 123Sn, the sensitivity of the energy gap |Δ(Bπ(1s)-Bπ(2p))| = 4.5 keV per 1 MeV change in σπN
- The expected uncertainty of σπN from the energy gap Bπ(1s)-Bπ(2p) is estimated to be around 2.5 MeV, based on experimental errors of 10-15 keV
- The expected uncertainty of σπN from the 1s width in 111Sn is estimated to be 3.1 MeV, based on experimental error of 40 keV
- The expected uncertainty of σπN from the absolute 1s binding energy in 111Sn is estimated to be 11 MeV, based on experimental error of 80 keV
- The free isovector scattering length b1free = -0.0861 mπ-1 and free isoscalar scattering length b0free = 0.0076 mπ-1 are used as input

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of pionic atoms for σπN determination, particularly the work of Friedman (2019, 2020) who reported σπN = 57 ± 7 MeV from existing pionic atom data, and the analysis of deeply bound pionic atoms in Sn by Suzuki et al. which indicated σπN ~ 45 MeV. The authors improve upon earlier approaches by implementing explicit density-dependent potential parameters rather than using the effective density approximation. The results enable future combined analyses of pionic atom data from different nuclei with independently determined neutron densities, which the authors argue is necessary to overcome the parameter correlation between σπN and ReB0.
