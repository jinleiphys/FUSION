# Revisiting the isospin relaxation time in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions

> Isospin relaxation times characterizing isospin transport processes between the projectile and the target with different $N/Z$ ratios and that between the neck and the spectator with different isospin asymmetries and densities in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions are studied within an isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport model using the lattice Hamiltonian approach. The respective roles and time scales of the isospin diffusion and drift as the major mechanisms of isospin transport in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions are discussed. Effects of nuclear symmetry energy and neutron-proton effective mass splitting on the isospin relaxation times are examined.

## Key claim

The isospin relaxation time in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions is measurably affected by both the nuclear symmetry energy and the neutron-proton effective mass splitting, but these effects are much smaller than the current experimental uncertainty range of the isospin relaxation time extracted by the Texas A&M University group.

## Method

The authors employed an improved isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model using the lattice Hamiltonian method for calculating the mean-field potential from an improved isospin- and momentum-dependent interaction (ImMDI). They simulated two types of isospin transport processes: isospin diffusion between projectile and target with different N/Z ratios in central ⁴⁰Ca+¹²⁴Sn collisions at beam energies from 25 to 300 AMeV, and isospin transport between neck and spectator in non-central ⁷⁰Zn+⁷⁰Zn collisions at 35 AMeV. The isospin relaxation time was extracted from the time evolution of the relative neutron/proton ratio in the first case and from the isovector dipole moment of the projectile-like fragment in the second case.

## Key numbers

- In ⁴⁰Ca+¹²⁴Sn collisions, the particle emission time scale changes from approximately 150 fm/c at 25 AMeV to about 75 fm/c at 300 AMeV
- For the isospin transport between neck and spectator in non-central ⁷⁰Zn+⁷⁰Zn collisions at 35 AMeV with impact parameter 4 fm, the isospin relaxation time τ = 68.00 ± 1.22 fm/c for L=60 MeV with mₙ₋ₚ* = 0.426δ (mₙ* > mₚ*)
- For the same system with L=60 MeV but mₙ₋ₚ* = -0.251δ (mₙ* < mₚ*), the isospin relaxation time τ = 73.52 ± 3.45 fm/c
- For L=90 MeV with mₙ₋ₚ* = 0.426δ, the isospin relaxation time τ = 60.02 ± 1.05 fm/c
- The symmetry energy slope parameters used were L=10 MeV (softer) and L=60 MeV and L=90 MeV (stiffer), with the symmetry energy at saturation density fixed at E_sym(ρ₀) = 32.5 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of isospin transport in heavy-ion collisions, particularly the isospin transport ratio method proposed by Tsang et al. (2004, 2009) and the experimental extraction of isospin relaxation time by the Texas A&M University group (Je17). The study connects to earlier work by Li et al. (1998) on isospin diffusion between projectile and target, and to investigations of the neutron-proton effective mass splitting by Zhang et al. (2014) and Kong et al. (2015). The results enable future experimental constraints on the symmetry energy and effective mass splitting, provided that experimental uncertainties on the isospin relaxation time can be reduced below the current level.
