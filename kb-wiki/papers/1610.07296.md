# Non-perturbative production rate of photons with a lattice quark propagator: effect of vertex correction

> We analyze the production rate of photons from the thermal medium above the deconfinement temperature with a quark propagator obtained from a lattice QCD numerical simulation. The photon-quark vertex is determined gauge-invariantly, so as to satisfy the Ward-Takahashi identity. The obtained photon production rate shows a suppression compared to perturbative results.

## Key claim

The photon production rate from a thermal medium above the deconfinement temperature, calculated using a lattice QCD quark propagator with a gauge-invariant vertex satisfying the Ward-Takahashi identity, shows suppression compared to perturbative results, but the rate is comparable to perturbative ones despite relying on different production mechanisms (Landau damping and pair annihilation of quasi-quark excitations rather than bremsstrahlung and inelastic pair annihilation).

## Method

The authors used a quark propagator obtained from lattice QCD numerical simulations in the Landau gauge with the quenched approximation, where the quark spectral function was deduced using a two-pole ansatz representing normal and plasmino quasi-particle modes. They constructed a gauge-invariant photon-quark vertex function that satisfies the Ward-Takahashi identity, and then evaluated the real photon production rate using the full photon self-energy in Matsubara formalism. The numerical results were computed for temperatures of 1.5 T_c and 3 T_c.

## Key numbers

- Photon production rate calculated at T = 1.5 T_c shows a discontinuity at omega approximately 0.63 GeV, corresponding to the threshold energy of the pair annihilation process
- Results are presented for two temperatures: T = 1.5 T_c and T = 3 T_c
- The production rate is compared to leading order HTL results from AMY (2001) for three values of the strong coupling constant alpha_s

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous non-perturbative analysis of virtual photon (dilepton) production rates (Kim, Asakawa, Kitazawa, 2015) to real photon production, using the same lattice quark propagator from Kaczmarek et al. (2011). It contrasts with perturbative calculations at leading order (AMY, 2001) and next-to-leading order (Ghiglieri et al., 2013) based on hard thermal loop resummation, showing that non-perturbative effects yield comparable rates through different microscopic mechanisms. This enables more reliable predictions for photon observables in relativistic heavy ion collisions near the critical temperature where the medium is strongly coupled.
