# Effective Action of QED in Electric Field Backgrounds II: Spatially Localized Fields

> We find the Bogoliubov coefficient from the tunneling boundary condition on a charged particle coupled to a static electric field $E_0 sech^2 (z/L)$ and, using the regularization scheme in Phys. Rev. D 78, 105013 (2008), obtain the exact one-loop effective action in scalar and spinor QED. It is shown that the effective action satisfies the general relation between the vacuum persistence and the mean number of produced pairs. We advance an approximation method for general electric fields and show the duality between the space-dependent and time-dependent electric fields of the same form at the leading order of the effective actions.

# Effective Action of QED in Electric Field Backgrounds II: Spatially Localized Fields

## Key claim

The authors obtain the exact one-loop effective action for scalar and spinor QED in the spatially localized electric field \(E_0 \operatorname{sech}^2(z/L)\), and show that the leading-order effective action for a space-dependent field of a given functional form is dual to that for a time-dependent field of the same form, though the exact duality remains an open question.

## Method

The authors solve the Klein-Gordon and Dirac equations for a charged particle in the Coulomb-gauge potential \(A_0(z) = -E_0 L \tanh(z/L)\), obtaining the Bogoliubov coefficient from the ratio of the incident coefficient to the reflection coefficient of the flux, consistent with the causality requirement for the Klein paradox. Using the regularization scheme of Kim, Lee, and Yoon (2008) (Phys. Rev. D 78, 105013), they compute the renormalized one-loop effective action by summing over modes and subtracting divergent terms. They also develop an approximation method based on the Liouville-Green transformation, which reduces the field equation to a parabolic cylinder equation whose instanton action determines the leading contribution to the effective action.

## Key numbers

- The electric field is \(E(z) = E_0 \operatorname{sech}^2(z/L)\), with length scale \(L\).
- The gauge potential is \(A_0(z) = -E_0 L \tanh(z/L)\).
- The asymptotic momenta at \(z = \pm \infty\) are \(k_{z(\pm)} = \sqrt{(\omega \mp qE_0 L)^2 - m^2 - \mathbf{k}_\perp^2}\).
- The mean number of produced pairs is \(\mathcal{N}_{\omega \mathbf{k}_\perp \sigma} = \frac{2 \sinh(\pi L k_{z(+)}) \sinh(\pi L k_{z(-)})}{\cosh(2\pi \lambda_\sigma) + (-1)^{2\sigma} \cosh(\pi L k_{z(+)} - \pi L k_{z(-)})}\), where \(\lambda_\sigma = \sqrt{(qE_0 L^2)^2 - \left(\frac{1 - 2|\sigma|}{2}\right)^2}\).
- The leading term of the mean number is \(\mathcal{N}_{\omega \mathbf{k}_\perp \sigma} \approx e^{-\pi (2\lambda_\sigma - L k_{z(+)} - L k_{z(-)})}\), agreeing with the instanton action of Kim and Page (2007).
- The integration limits are \(\int d\omega = \int_{-(qE_0 L - m)}^{qE_0 L - m} d\omega\) and \(\int d^2\mathbf{k}_\perp = 2\pi \int_0^{k_{\perp \text{max}}} k_{\perp \text{max}} dk_{\perp \text{max}}\).

## Context

This work extends the in- and out-state formalism based on Bogoliubov transformations developed by Kim, Lee, and Yoon (2008) for pulsed electric fields to the case of spatially localized fields, and builds on the scattering formalism of Nikishov (1970) and the causality resolution of the Klein paradox by Hansen and Ravndal. The exact effective actions for the Sauter-type field \(E_0 \operatorname{sech}^2(z/L)\) are the first such results for this configuration, and the approximation method using the Liouville-Green transformation generalizes the uniform semiclassical approximation of Dunne and Hall to space-dependent fields. The duality between space-dependent and time-dependent fields of the same form at leading order suggests a generic relation, but whether exact duality exists remains an open question.
