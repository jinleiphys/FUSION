# Electrical conductivity of quark matter at finite T under external magnetic field

> We investigate the electrical conductivity (sigma) of quark matter via the Kubo formula at finite temperature and zero quark density (T>0, mu=0) in the presence of an external strong magnetic field. For this purpose, we employ the dilute instanton-liquid model, taking into account its temperature modification with the trivial-holonomy caloron distribution. By doing that, the momentum and temperature dependences for the effective quark mass and model renormalization scale are carefully evaluated. From the numerical results, it turns out that sigma\approx(0.02 ~ 0.15)/fm for T=(0 ~ 400) MeV with the relaxation time tau=(0.3 ~ 0.9) fm. In addition, we also parameterize the electrical conductivity as sigma/T ~ (0.46,0.77,1.08,1.39)C_EM for tau=(0.3,0.5,0.7,0.9) fm, respectively. These results are well compatible with other theoretical estimations, including those from the lattice QCD simulations. It also turns out that the external magnetic field plays only a minor role for sigma even for the very strong one B_0 ~ m^2_pi*10 and becomes relatively effective for T\lesssim200 MeV. Moreover, we compute the soft photon emission rate from the quark-gluon plasma, using the electrical conductivity calculated.

## Key claim

The electrical conductivity of quark matter is computed via the Kubo formula in the dilute instanton-liquid model, yielding values that are well compatible with lattice QCD simulations, but the external magnetic field plays only a minor role even for very strong fields \(B_0 \sim m_\pi^2 \times 10\), becoming relatively effective only for temperatures \(T \lesssim 200\) MeV.

## Method

The electrical conductivity \(\sigma\) was computed using the Kubo formula for the vector-current correlation function in Euclidean space, employing the dilute instanton-liquid model with temperature-dependent instanton parameters (average instanton size \(\bar{\rho}\) and inter-instanton distance \(\bar{R}\)) modified via the trivial-holonomy caloron distribution. The momentum- and temperature-dependent effective quark mass was evaluated, and the fermionic Matsubara formula was used to incorporate finite temperature, while the external magnetic field was introduced via the Schwinger method. The calculation was performed for the SU(2) light-flavor sector in the chiral limit at zero quark density, with the relaxation time \(\tau\) treated as a parameter.

## Key numbers

*   \(\sigma \approx (0.02 \sim 0.15)\,\mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) for temperature range \(T = (0 \sim 400)\) MeV with relaxation time \(\tau = (0.3 \sim 0.9)\) fm.
*   Parameterized conductivity: \(\sigma/T \approx (0.46, 0.77, 1.08, 1.39)\,C_\mathrm{EM}\) for \(\tau = (0.3, 0.5, 0.7, 0.9)\) fm respectively, where \(C_\mathrm{EM} \approx 0.051\) for SU(2) light flavors.
*   At \(T_c \approx 180\) MeV, \(\sigma = (0.023, 0.039, 0.054, 0.070)\,\mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) for \(\tau = (0.1, 0.3, 0.5, 0.9)\) fm.
*   At \(T_c \approx 155\) MeV, \(\sigma = (0.022, 0.037, 0.052, 0.067)\,\mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) for \(\tau = (0.1, 0.3, 0.5, 0.9)\) fm.
*   External magnetic field strength used: \(B_0 = m_\pi^2 \times 10\).

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of electrical conductivity in hot QCD matter, comparing with quenched SU(3) lattice QCD results from Gupta (2003) and Aarts (2007), as well as SU(2) lattice QCD from Buividovich (2010) and the maximum entropy method analysis by Ding (2010). The results are also consistent with the Green-function method approach of Kerbikov (2012) at finite density. The calculation enables estimation of the soft photon emission rate from quark-gluon plasma, and the framework can be extended by including the meson-loop correction and the UA(1) anomaly to study magnetic field effects more fully.
