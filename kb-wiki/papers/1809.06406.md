# Impact of QCD jets and heavy-quark production in cosmic-ray proton atmospheric showers up to 10$^{20}$ eV

> The PYTHIA 6 Monte Carlo (MC) event generator, commonly used in collider physics, is interfaced for the first time with a fast transport simulation of a hydrogen atmosphere, with the same density as air, in order to study the properties of extended atmospheric showers (EAS) produced by cosmic ray protons with energies E$_{CR}\approx 10^{14}$--$10^{20}$ eV. At variance with the hadronic MC generators (EPOS-LHC, QGSJET, and SIBYLL) commonly used in cosmic-rays physics, PYTHIA includes the generation of harder hadronic jets and heavy (charm and bottom) quarks, thereby producing higher transverse momentum final particles, that could explain several anomalies observed in the data. The electromagnetic, hadronic, and muonic properties of EAS generated with various settings of PYTHIA 6, tuned to proton-proton data measured at the LHC, are compared to those from EPOS-LHC, QGSJET 01, QGSJET II, and SIBYLL 2.1. Despite their different underlying parton dynamics, the characteristics of the EAS generated with PYTHIA 6 are in between those predicted by the rest of MC generators. The only exceptions are the muonic components at large transverse distances from the shower axis, where PYTHIA predicts more activity than the rest of the models. Heavy-quark production, as implemented in this study for a hydrogen atmosphere, does not seem to play a key role in the EAS muon properties, pointing to nuclear effects as responsible of the muon anomalies observed in the air-shower data.

## Key claim

The PYTHIA 6 Monte Carlo event generator, when used to simulate cosmic-ray proton showers in a hydrogen atmosphere (with the same density as air), predicts shower characteristics that are generally between those of standard cosmic-ray hadronic models (EPOS-LHC, QGSJET, SIBYLL), except for muon activity at large transverse distances from the shower axis where PYTHIA shows more activity; however, heavy-quark (charm and bottom) production does not appear to play a key role in the muon properties of extended atmospheric showers, pointing instead to nuclear effects as responsible for the observed muon anomalies in air-shower data.

## Method

The authors interfaced the PYTHIA 6 Monte Carlo event generator with the CONEX fast transport simulation code, using a hydrogen atmosphere with density matching that of air, to simulate extended atmospheric showers from cosmic-ray protons with energies from 10^14 to 10^20 eV. Seven different PYTHIA 6 parameter tunes (Perugia 350, 350 without heavy quarks, 371, 372, 380, 381, 382) were compared against four standard cosmic-ray hadronic interaction models (EPOS-LHC, QGSJET 01, QGSJET II, and SIBYLL 2.1), with 1000 showers generated per energy point per model for a total of 154,000 simulated showers. The electromagnetic, hadronic, and muonic properties of the resulting showers were analyzed for inclined showers at a zenith angle of 60 degrees.

## Key numbers

- Cosmic-ray proton energies studied: 10^14 to 10^20 eV, with 14 energy points at intervals of 10^0.5 eV
- At E_CR approximately 10^20 eV, PYTHIA 6 predicts a shower maximum depth about 80 g/cm^2 larger than QGSJET 01 and about 50 g/cm^2 larger than EPOS-LHC
- A vertically-incident 10^20 eV proton produces about 10^11 secondaries at sea-level with energies above 90 keV in the annular region from 8 m to 8 km off the shower axis, of which 90% are gamma rays, 9% e+/e-, and 1% muons plus charged hadrons
- The mean energy of electromagnetic particles at ground level is around 10 MeV, transporting 85% of the total energy at ground
- The inelasticity (fraction of primary energy transferred to secondary particles) for PYTHIA 6 shows an almost flat behavior with proton energy, compared to a 25-30% increase observed for EPOS-LHC, QGSJET II, and QGSJET 01 between 10^14 eV and 10^20 eV
- The difference in Xmax between EPOS-LHC and QGSJET II is reduced from about 20 g/cm^2 for proton-air to less than 10 g/cm^2 for proton-proton

## Context

This work extends the comparison of hadronic interaction models for cosmic-ray air showers, previously studied by d'Enterria (2011) and Pierog (2015), by introducing PYTHIA 6 as an alternative to the standard Reggeon Field Theory-based models (EPOS-LHC, QGSJET, SIBYLL) that have been used with the CORSIKA transport code. The study addresses the persistent "muon anomaly" observed by the Pierre Auger Observatory (2014, 2016) and earlier by HiRes-MIA (1999), where 30-60% more muons are measured on ground than predicted by any standard model. The finding that heavy-quark production in a hydrogen atmosphere does not explain the muon excess indicates that future work should focus on nuclear effects in proton-air collisions, and the authors note that the hydrogen atmosphere used here produces different elongation rates and Xmax values compared to standard air showers as shown by Pierog (2017).
