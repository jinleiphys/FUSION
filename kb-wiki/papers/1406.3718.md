# Medium effect in high-density region probed by nucleus-nucleus elastic scattering

> We investigate the sensitivity of the medium effect in the high-density region on the nucleus-nucleus elastic scattering in the framework of the double-folding (DF) model with the complex $G$-matrix interaction. The medium effect including three-body-force (TBF) effect is investigated with two methods. In the both methods, the medium effect is clearly seen on the potential and the elastic cross section. Finally, we make clear the crucial role of the TBF effect up to $k_F =$ 1.6 fm$^{-1}$ in the nucleus-nucleus elastic scattering.

## Key claim

The medium effect, including the three-body-force (TBF) effect, in the high-density region up to a Fermi momentum of 1.6 fm$^{-1}$ has a critical and decisive role in determining the elastic angular distribution of nucleus-nucleus scattering, as probed through the double-folding model with a complex $G$-matrix interaction; the abstract's claim of a "crucial role" is specifically qualified by the finding that the elastic cross section is sensitive to the medium effect up to $k_F =$ 1.6 fm$^{-1}$, while the potential itself shows sensitivity up to $k_F =$ 1.8 fm$^{-1}$.

## Method

The authors constructed a nucleus-nucleus optical potential using the double-folding (DF) model with a complex $G$-matrix interaction based on the ESC08 interaction and the multi-pomeron exchange potential (MPP) model (MPa version). The local density was evaluated using the frozen-density approximation (FDA), where the density is the sum of the undisturbed ground-state densities of the colliding nuclei. To isolate the medium effect in the high-density region, the local density was artificially cut off at various values of $k_{\rm cut}$ (corresponding to a maximum density $\rho_{\rm cut}$), and the TBF effect was separately investigated by replacing the MPa interaction (with TBF) with the ESC interaction (without TBF) for densities above a threshold $k_{\rm rep.}$.

## Key numbers

* The medium effect in the elastic cross section is clearly seen up to $k_F =$ 1.6 fm$^{-1}$ for the $^{16}$O + $^{16}$O system at $E/A =$ 70 MeV, while the effect in the potential is seen up to $k_F =$ 1.8 fm$^{-1}$.
* The TBF effect in the elastic cross section is clearly seen up to $k_F =$ 1.6 fm$^{-1}$, while the TBF effect in the DF potential is seen up to $k_F =$ 1.8 fm$^{-1}$.
* The single-particle potential in nuclear matter for the MPa interaction (with TBF) is Re $U_{\rm MPa} (k_F = 1.7$ fm$^{-1}) = -30.60$ MeV and Re $U_{\rm MPa} (k_F = 1.8$ fm$^{-1}) = -16.37$ MeV; for the ESC interaction (without TBF) it is Re $U_{\rm ESC} (k_F = 1.7$ fm$^{-1}) = -64.58$ MeV and Re $U_{\rm ESC} (k_F = 1.8$ fm$^{-1}) = -67.76$ MeV.
* The local density in the DF model based on FDA reaches up to twice the normal density, corresponding to $k_F =$ 1.8 fm$^{-1}$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analyses using the CEG07 complex $G$-matrix interaction (Furumoto et al., 2009) and the MPP model (Yamamoto et al., 2013, 2014), which demonstrated the importance of the TBF repulsive effect for reproducing $^{16}$O + $^{16}$O elastic scattering data. The present study clarifies the specific density range over which the medium effect is probed, showing that the elastic scattering is sensitive to the TBF effect up to $k_F =$ 1.6 fm$^{-1}$. This result enables a more precise connection between nucleus-nucleus scattering and the equation of state (EOS) of nuclear matter at high density, relevant for neutron-star physics, and raises a caution against using complex $G$-matrix interactions (such as JLM, original CEG, and Melbourne-$G$) that do not include the medium effect up to twice the normal density.
