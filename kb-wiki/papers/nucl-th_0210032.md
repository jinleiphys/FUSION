# Dileptons and Photons from Coarse-Grained Microscopic Dynamics and Hydrodynamics Compared to Experimental Data

> We compute the radiation of dileptons and photons using relativistic hydrodynamics and a coarse-grained version of the microscopic event generator UrQMD, both of which provide a good description of the hadron spectra. The currently most accurate dilepton and photon emission rates from perturbative QCD and from experimentally-based hadronic calculations are used. Comparisons are made to data on central Pb-Pb and Pb-Au collisions taken at the CERN SPS at a beam energy of 158 A GeV. Both hydrodynamics and UrQMD provide very good descriptions of the photon transverse momentum spectrum measured between 1 and 4 GeV, but very slightly underestimate the low mass spectrum of e+e- pairs, even with greatly broadened rho and omega vector mesons.

# Fusion Cycles in Stars and Stellar Neutrinos

## Key claim

This paper provides a historical and pedagogical survey of thermonuclear fusion cycles in stars, from the early works of Weizsäcker and Bethe through contemporary solar neutrino measurements, arguing that the combined evidence from solar models and neutrino oscillation experiments has resolved the long-standing solar neutrino deficit in favor of particle-physics explanations rather than deficiencies in stellar models.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical development of the proton-proton chain and CNO-cycle, including the quantum-mechanical tunneling mechanism (Gamow factor) that enables thermonuclear reactions at stellar temperatures far below the Coulomb barrier. It then presents the experimental history of solar neutrino detection, from the radiochemical chlorine experiment of Davis et al. (1968) through the gallium experiments (Gallex, Sage) and the water-Cherenkov detectors (Kamiokande, SuperKamiokande, SNO), comparing measured neutrino fluxes to predictions from standard solar models.

## Key numbers

- Central solar temperature from numerical solutions: Tc = 1.57 × 10^7 K, with central pressure Pc = 2.34 × 10^16 Pa and central density ρc = 1.53 × 10^5 kg/m^3
- Solar luminosity: 3.842 × 10^26 W, corresponding to a mass loss rate of 4.25 × 10^9 kg/s from hydrogen fusion
- S-factor for the 3He(3He,2p)4He reaction at the Gamow peak: 5.3 MeV·b, with LUNA data extending down to 21 keV
- Davis chlorine experiment result: (2.3 ± 0.3) SNU observed versus approximately 8 SNU predicted from solar models
- Gallex result: (69.7 +7.8/−8.1) SNU, confirmed by Sage at (69 ± 12) SNU, versus standard solar model predictions of 120–140 SNU
- SuperKamiokande boron-8 flux ratio (Observed/Predicted): 0.47 ± 0.02
- SNO neutral current total flux: (5.09 +0.64/−0.61) × 10^6 neutrinos per (cm^2·s), in agreement with solar model value of (5.05 +1.01/−0.81)
- Atmospheric neutrino oscillation parameters: Δm²_atm ≈ 2.5 × 10^−3 eV² with maximal mixing sin²(2θ_atm) ≈ 1
- Upper limit on antielectron-neutrino mass from tritium beta decay: 2.2 eV/c²; lower limit from neutrinoless double beta decay: 0.05–0.2 eV/c² (conditional on neutrino being its own antiparticle)

## Context

This review extends the foundational works of Weizsäcker (1937/8) and Bethe (1938/9) on the CNO-cycle and the Bethe-Critchfield (1938) outline of the proton-proton chain, incorporating the later development of the pp-chain branches by Salpeter (1950s) and the systematic nucleosynthesis framework of Burbidge, Burbidge, Fowler and Hoyle (1957). The paper connects these theoretical developments to the experimental program of solar neutrino detection initiated by Davis et al. (1968), culminating in the resolution of the solar neutrino deficit through the oscillation measurements of SuperKamiokande (1998) and SNO (2001), which confirmed that neutrinos have finite mass and can oscillate between flavors. The work enables future precision measurements of the full solar neutrino spectrum by detectors such as Borexino and LENS, which will determine all mixing parameters in a three-family scheme.
