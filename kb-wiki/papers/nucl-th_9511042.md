# Nucleation Rate of Hadron Bubbles in Baryon-Free Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We evaluate the factor $κ$ appearing in Langer's expression for the nucleation rate extended to the case of hadron bubbles forming in zero baryon number cooled quark-gluon plasma. We consider both the absence and presence of viscosity and show that viscous effects introduce only small changes in the value of $κ$

## Key claim

The factor κ in Langer's nucleation rate for hadron bubbles in baryon-free quark-gluon plasma does not require viscosity to be nonzero; contrary to a prior claim by Csernai and Kapusta (1992), energy flow via convection can drive bubble growth even when viscosity coefficients vanish, and viscous effects introduce only small corrections to κ.

## Method

The authors solved the linearized relativistic energy and momentum transport equations (equations for energy density and velocity) in the quark vapor region for the case of zero baryon number and zero viscosity, using spherical symmetry and the Kotchine boundary conditions across the bubble interface. They derived a wave equation for the energy density perturbation and solved for the growing mode to obtain κ, then compared their result to the nonrelativistic solution of Langer and Turski (1973) by mapping enthalpy density w to particle density n.

## Key numbers

- The bag constant B = 235⁴ MeV⁴ (approximately 3.05 × 10⁹ MeV⁴) sets the scale for the quark matter equation of state.
- The quark vapor pressure constant a_v = 37π²/90 and the hadron pressure constant a_h = 3π²/90 define the equation of state p = a T⁴ − B for quark matter and p = a_h T⁴ for hadron matter.
- In the limit of small y (where y = 2σ w_v / [c² R_o² (Δw)²]), the nucleation rate factor becomes κ = √[2σ w_v / (R_o³ (Δw)²)].
- The critical bubble radius R_o and surface tension σ appear in the Laplace pressure condition p_h − p_v = 2σ/R.

## Context

This work extends the Langer-Turski nucleation formalism (1973) to relativistic baryon-free quark-gluon plasma, correcting the earlier treatment by Csernai and Kapusta (1992) who claimed that viscosity was necessary for hadron bubble growth. The authors show that energy flow (convection) replaces heat conduction in systems with zero chemical potential, and that the resulting κ has the same functional form as the nonrelativistic result with enthalpy density replacing particle density. This enables more accurate modeling of the quark-hadron phase transition in heavy-ion collisions and early-universe cosmology, where supercooled quark matter nucleates hadron bubbles.
