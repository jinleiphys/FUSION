# Evidence of double magicity of N=Z nuclei near the rp-process path

> N=Z nuclei above Ni are understood to be waiting-point nuclei in the rp-process nucleosynthesis. Investigating the experimental isotope shifts in Kr isotopes near the proton drip-line, we have discovered that N=Z rp-process nuclei $^{68}$Se, $^{72}$Kr, $^{76}$Sr and $^{80}$Zr exhibit a significant shell gap both at the proton and neutron numbers in the deformed space with the consequence that pairing correlations for protons and neutrons vanish, thus lending a double-magic character to these nuclei. A significant number of nuclei in this region are also shown to exhibit neutron magicity at N = 34, 36, 38, and 40 in the deformed space.

## Key claim

Based on experimental isotope shifts in Kr isotopes and relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov calculations, the N=Z rp-process waiting-point nuclei ⁶⁸Se, ⁷²Kr, ⁷⁶Sr, and ⁸⁰Zr exhibit a significant shell gap at both proton and neutron numbers in deformed space, causing pairing correlations to vanish and lending these nuclei a double-magic character. The authors qualify that this double magicity exists specifically in the deformed space, not in the spherical shell-model sense.

## Method

The authors performed deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations using the NL-SV1 Lagrangian with vector self-coupling of the omega meson, expanding Dirac spinors and meson fields into 20 shells of an axially deformed oscillator potential. The Gogny force D1S was used for the pairing channel, with its strength calibrated against binding energies of Sn isotopes. The RHB equations were solved self-consistently to obtain single-particle levels, pairing correlations, and charge radii for Kr isotopes and other N=Z nuclei near the rp-process path.

## Key numbers

- ⁷²Kr (N=36, Z=36): pairing correlations vanish completely for both protons and neutrons, with a negative experimental isotope shift relative to N=50; theoretical deformation β₂ = -0.34
- ⁶⁸Se (N=Z=34): exhibits double magicity in deformed space, with a second minimum (prolate shape) within 0.5 MeV of the oblate ground state
- ⁷⁶Sr and ⁸⁰Zr: exhibit large prolate deformation in the ground state, with vanishing pairing correlations for neutrons and protons
- ⁶⁴Ge (N=Z=32): shows shell gaps that cannot be construed as magic numbers, with non-zero but significantly reduced pairing energy compared to a normal superfluid nucleus
- Theoretical binding energies for Kr isotopes reproduced within 0.20% divergence using NL-SV1 with deformed RHB

## Context

This work extends prior studies of waiting-point nuclei in the rp-process (Schatz 1998, 2001; Wiescher 2001) and precise mass measurements that established the waiting-point character of these N=Z nuclei (Clark 2004; Rodriguez 2004; Gomez 2008). The authors note that while deformed shell gaps at some of these N and Z numbers were indicated in earlier literature (Nazarewicz 1985), those shell gaps had never been shown to qualify for magicity. The finding of double magicity in deformed space provides a structural explanation for the waiting-point nature of these nuclei and may influence predictions of beta-decay half-lives and x-ray flux from x-ray binaries.
