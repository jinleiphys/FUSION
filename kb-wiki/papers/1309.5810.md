# Microscopic description of translationally-invariant core+N+N overlap functions

> We derive expressions for core + N + N overlap integrals starting from microscopic wave functions obtained in the ab initio no-core shell model. These overlap integrals correspond to three-body channel form factors and can be used to investigate the clustering of many-body systems into a core plus two nucleons. We consider the case when the composite system and the core are described in Slater determinant, harmonic oscillator bases, and we show how to remove spurious center of mass components exactly in order to derive translationally-invariant overlap integrals. We study in particular the Borromean 6He nucleus using realistic chiral nuclear interactions, and we demonstrate that the observed clusterization in this system is a Pauli focusing effect. The inclusion of three-body forces has a small effect on this structure. In addition, we discuss the issue of absolute normalization for spectroscopic factors, which we show is larger than one. As part of this study we also perform extrapolations of ground-state observables and investigate the dependence of these results on the resolution scale of the interaction.

## Key claim

The observed clusterization in the Borromean 6He nucleus into a 4He core plus two neutrons is a Pauli focusing effect driven by antisymmetrization, not by dynamical correlations from the nuclear interaction; the inclusion of three-body forces has only a small effect on this structure.

## Method

The authors derived algebraic expressions for translationally-invariant core+N+N overlap integrals (three-body channel form factors) starting from ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) wave functions expressed in Slater determinant harmonic oscillator bases. They removed spurious center-of-mass components exactly using Talmi-Moshinsky transformations and computed the overlap integrals via double-reduced matrix elements between SD eigenstates. Calculations were performed for 6He in model spaces up to Nmax=16 using SRG-evolved chiral NN interactions at N3LO with a 500 MeV regularization cutoff, with flow parameters Lambda_SRG = 1.8-2.2 fm^-1.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state binding energy of 6He: 29.20(11) MeV at Lambda_SRG = 2.0 fm^-1 (experimental: 29.269 MeV)
- Two-neutron separation energy S_2n: 0.95(10) MeV at Lambda_SRG = 2.0 fm^-1 (experimental: 0.975 MeV)
- Point-proton radius of 6He: 1.820(4) fm at Lambda_SRG = 2.0 fm^-1 (experimental: 1.938(23) fm)
- Total spectroscopic factor for <6He(0+) | 4He(0+) + n + n> overlap: 1.3340 at Lambda_SRG = 2.0 fm^-1 and hbar-Omega = 20 MeV (Nmax=14)
- Spectroscopic factor for overlap with excited core <6He(0+) | 4He(2+) + n + n>: 0.30(4) at Lambda_SRG = 2.0 fm^-1
- Relative weight of K=2, L=S=0 hyperspherical harmonics channel: 91.9% at Lambda_SRG = 2.0 fm^-1 and hbar-Omega = 20 MeV
- Maximum model space dimension: 3.6 x 10^8 at Nmax=16

## Context

This work extends the translation-invariant shell-model approach of Timofeyuk (2001) and the microscopic Monte Carlo study of Brida (2010) by using realistic chiral nuclear interactions in the ab initio NCSM framework. The derived translationally-invariant overlap integrals provide a natural interface with reaction calculations such as the NCSM/RGM approach of Quaglioni (2013), enabling future investigations of three-body clustering and direct reactions in light nuclei. The formalism also allows detailed study of cluster structures when combined with the ongoing development of the no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC).
