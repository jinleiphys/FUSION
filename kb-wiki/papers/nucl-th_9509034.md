# The role of nucleon structure in finite nuclei

> The quark-meson coupling model, based on a mean field description of non-overlapping nucleon bags bound by the self-consistent exchange of $σ$, $ω$ and $ρ$ mesons, is extended to investigate the properties of finite nuclei. Using the Born-Oppenheimer approximation to describe the interacting quark-meson system, we derive the effective equation of motion for the nucleon, as well as the self-consistent equations for the meson mean fields. The model is first applied to nuclear matter, after which we show some initial results for finite nuclei.

## Key claim

The quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, which incorporates internal quark degrees of freedom via the MIT bag model, can be extended from infinite nuclear matter to finite nuclei using the Born-Oppenheimer approximation, but the results presented are only "initial" and the non-relativistic quantization is postponed to future work, with a relativistic formulation used instead for the finite-nuclei calculations shown.

## Method

The authors treat nucleons as non-overlapping MIT bags containing three quarks, coupled self-consistently to scalar (sigma), vector (omega), and rho meson mean fields that vary with position in the finite nucleus. Using the Born-Oppenheimer approximation, they solve for the quark wave functions in the nucleon's instantaneous rest frame, treating the nucleon position as a fixed parameter, then derive an effective Hamiltonian for the nucleon motion including a spin-orbit interaction from both the magnetic moment of the nucleon and Thomas precession. The meson field equations are obtained from the expectation values of the quark scalar and vector currents, and the system is solved self-consistently for spherical, spin-saturated nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Free bag radius \(R^0_B\) is treated as a free parameter with values 0.6, 0.8, and 1.0 fm; for \(m_q = 5\) MeV, the corresponding bag constant \(B^{1/4}\) is 210.9, 170.0, and 143.8 MeV, and the zero-point parameter \(z_0\) is 4.003, 3.295, and 2.587.
- The maximum acceleration of a nucleon in a Woods-Saxon potential with depth \(V_0 \sim -50\) MeV, surface thickness \(a \sim 2\) fm, and radius \(R_A \sim 1.2 A^{1/3}\) fm is estimated as \(|\vec{\alpha}_{max}| = V_0/(a M_N)\), leading to a fractional change in the nucleon position relative to bag radius of \(|R'(t)-R'_0|/R_B \sim t^2/80\) with \(t\) in fm, which is small for times under about 9 fm.
- The relative change in the sigma field due to nucleon motion is estimated as \(\Delta\sigma/\sigma \sim 3\%\), using a Fermi momentum \(k_F = 1.7\) fm\(^{-1}\) and a surface thickness \(d \sim 2\) fm.
- The free nucleon mass is taken as \(M_N = 939\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the QMC model, originally proposed by Guichon (1988) and developed for infinite nuclear matter, to finite nuclei, building on earlier studies of nuclear matter saturation and the EMC effect by the same group (Saito and Thomas, 1994; 1995). The approach contrasts with Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) of Serot and Walecka, which treats nucleons as point-like particles, and with Dirac-Brueckner methods; the QMC model yields a lower nuclear incompressibility than QHD due to the internal quark structure. The results enable future self-consistent calculations of finite-nucleus properties such as charge densities and binding energies, and may be applied to the Okamoto-Nolen-Schiffer anomaly (Okamoto, 1964; Nolen and Schiffer, 1969) and corrections to super-allowed Fermi beta-decay.
