# O(12) limit and complete classification of symmetry schemes in proton-neutron interacting boson model

> It is shown that the proton-neutron interacting boson model (pnIBM) admits new symmetry limits with O(12) algebra which break F-spin but preserves the quantum number M_F. The generators of O(12) are derived and the quantum number `v' of O(12) for a given boson number N is determined by identifying the corresponding quasi-spin algebra. The O(12) algebra generates two symmetry schemes and for both of them, complete classification of the basis states and typical spectra are given. With the O(12) algebra identified, complete classification of pnIBM symmetry limits with good M_F is established.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the proton-neutron interacting boson model (pnIBM) admits new symmetry limits based on an O(12) algebra that breaks F-spin but preserves the quantum number M_F (the z-component of F-spin), providing a complete classification of pnIBM symmetry schemes with good M_F.

## Method

The generators of the O(12) algebra were derived by combining O(10) generators in d-boson space with O(2) generators in s-boson space, then identifying the remaining 20 generators through commutation relations. The corresponding quasi-spin algebra was constructed to determine the quantum number v of O(12) for a given boson number N. Two group chains were analyzed: O(12) ⊃ O(6) ⊗ O(2) and O(12) ⊃ O(2) ⊕ O(10), with complete classification of basis states and typical spectra given for both.

## Key numbers

- For the O(12) algebra, the quadratic Casimir operator eigenvalue is v(v+10), where v = N, N-2, ..., 0 or 1.
- For the O(10) subalgebra in d-boson space, the quadratic Casimir eigenvalue is v_d(v_d+8), where v_d = N_d, N_d-2, ..., 0 or 1.
- In the O(12) ⊃ O(6) ⊗ O(2) limit, the energy formula includes terms a_1 v(v+10) + a_2 [σ_1(σ_1+4) + σ_2(σ_2+2)] + a_3 [v_1(v_1+3) + v_2(v_2+1)] + a_4 L(L+1).
- For N=6 and M_F=-1 (N_π=2, N_ν=4), the [51] O(6) states appear around 1.5 MeV excitation, while v=N-2 states appear around 2.2-2.5 MeV excitation.

## Context

This work extends the known dynamical symmetries of pnIBM beyond the established U(6) ⊗ SU_F(2) and U_π(6) ⊕ U_ν(6) limits studied by Iachello (1987), Van Isacker (1985), and Frank (1994). The O(12) limit is closely related to O(6) nuclei and follows the O(18) and O(36) symmetry limits previously identified in IBM-3 and IBM-4 models by Kota (1998, 2000). The classification enables future studies of F-spin mixing and electromagnetic transition rates in nuclei where mixed-symmetry states appear at lower energies than in the standard U(6) ⊗ SU_F(2) limit.
