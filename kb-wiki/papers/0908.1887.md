# Spin susceptibility of degenerate quark matter

> The expression for the spin susceptibility $χ$ of degenerate quark matter is derived with corrections upto $ {\cal O}(g^4\ln g^2)$. It is shown that at low density, $χ^{-1}$ changes sign and turns negative indicating a ferromagnetic phase transition. To this order, we also calculate sound velocity $c_1$ and incompressibility $K$ with arbitrary spin polarization. The estimated values of $c_1$ and $K$ show that the equation of state of the polarized matter is stiffer than the unpolarized one. Finally we determine the finite temperature corrections to the exchange energy and derive corresponding results for the spin susceptibility.

## Key claim

The paper derives the spin susceptibility of degenerate quark matter including correlation corrections up to \( \mathcal{O}(g^4 \ln g^2) \), and shows that the inverse susceptibility changes sign at a density of approximately \( 0.1 \, \text{fm}^{-3} \) (with correlations included), indicating a ferromagnetic phase transition; the abstract does not specify the exact density at which the sign change occurs.

## Method

The authors compute the ground state energy of spin-polarized quark matter as a sum of kinetic, exchange, and correlation energy densities, where the correlation energy is obtained by resumming bubble diagrams to handle infrared divergences from massless gluon exchange. The spin susceptibility is then derived from the second derivative of the total energy with respect to the polarization parameter \(\xi\) in the small-\(\xi\) limit, and analytic expressions are given in both the ultra-relativistic and non-relativistic limits. Additionally, the incompressibility and sound velocity are calculated from the total energy density and chemical potentials, and finite-temperature corrections to the exchange energy and susceptibility are evaluated using Fermi distribution functions.

## Key numbers

* Inverse spin susceptibility \(\chi^{-1}\) changes sign at density \(\sim 0.12 \, \text{fm}^{-3}\) without correlation correction, and at \(\sim 0.1 \, \text{fm}^{-3}\) with correlation correction (Fig. 1, for degenerate quark matter).
* Sound velocity for unpolarized matter: \(c_1 = 0.46\); for completely polarized matter: \(c_1 = 0.54\) (ultra-relativistic limit, below the causal value \(1/\sqrt{3} \approx 0.57\)).
* In the ultra-relativistic limit, the total spin susceptibility is \(\chi^{ur} = \chi_P \left[1 - \frac{g^2}{6\pi^2} - \frac{g^4}{384\pi^4}(\ln r_s - 0.286)\right]^{-1}\), where \(r_s = g^2 (3\pi/4)^{1/3}\).
* In the non-relativistic limit, the total spin susceptibility is \(\chi^{nr} = \chi_P \left[1 - \frac{g^2}{3\pi^2} \frac{m_q}{p_f} - \frac{g^4 \ln g^2}{384\pi^4} \frac{m_q^2}{p_f^2}\right]^{-1}\).
* At non-zero temperature in the ultra-relativistic limit: \(\chi^{ur} = \chi_P \left[1 - \frac{g^2}{6\pi^2} \left(1 + \frac{4\pi^2 T^2}{3p_f^2}\right)\right]^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier Hartree-Fock calculations of spin-polarized quark matter by Tatsumi (2000) and Pal (2009) to include correlation (bubble diagram) corrections, which are necessary to obtain a finite perturbative result beyond exchange. The method is analogous to the Gell-Mann and Brueckner (1957) treatment of the degenerate electron gas and to the work of Brueckner and Sawada (1958) and Shastry (1977, 1978) applied to QCD matter. The results enable more accurate predictions for the possibility of ferromagnetic phase transitions in dense quark matter, relevant for neutron star and quark star astrophysics, and provide the equation of state for polarized matter including correlation effects.
