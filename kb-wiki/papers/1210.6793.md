# Extracting the chiral anomaly from gamma pi --> pi pi

> We derive dispersive representations for the anomalous process gamma pi --> pi pi with the pi pi P-wave phase shift as input. We investigate how in this framework the chiral anomaly can be extracted from a cross-section measurement using all data up to 1 GeV, and discuss the importance of a precise representation of the gamma pi --> pi pi amplitude for the hadronic light-by-light contribution to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the chiral anomaly can be extracted from a fit of dispersive representations to all gamma pi -> pi pi cross-section data up to about 1 GeV, including the rho(770) resonance region, rather than only from the low-energy threshold region; however, the extraction requires matching to chiral perturbation theory and relies on a resonance-saturation estimate for a low-energy constant, introducing a systematic uncertainty at the percent level.

## Method

The authors derived dispersive representations for the gamma pi -> pi pi amplitude using the pi pi P-wave phase shift as input, implementing both an infinite-matching-point formulation with two subtraction constants and a finite-matching-point formulation with one subtraction constant. They constructed Omnès functions from the phase shift and solved the resulting integral equations iteratively to obtain the partial wave f_1(s) and the full amplitude. The subtraction constants were then related to the chiral anomaly by matching the dispersive representation to one-loop and two-loop chiral perturbation theory expressions.

## Key numbers

- The chiral anomaly prediction at leading order is F_{3pi} = (9.78 +/- 0.05) GeV^{-3}, derived from F_pi = (92.21 +/- 0.14) MeV and the number of colors N_c.
- The Serpukhov Primakoff experiment extracted F_{3pi} = (12.9 +/- 0.9 +/- 0.5) GeV^{-3} assuming the amplitude were given solely by the low-energy theorem.
- After including electromagnetic corrections and higher-order chiral corrections, the extracted value was reduced to F_{3pi} = (10.7 +/- 1.2) GeV^{-3}.
- The pi- e- -> pi- e- pi0 experiment extracted F_{3pi} = (9.6 +/- 1.1) GeV^{-3}.
- The resonance-saturation estimate for the Order(p^6) low-energy constant C_2^r(mu) amounts to a 4.9% effect on the anomaly extraction, and the chiral logarithm at mu = M_rho generates another 1.8% correction.
- The maximum matching point for rigorous validity of the dispersive representation is s_max = (1.2 GeV)^2.

## Context

This work extends earlier dispersive treatments of gamma pi -> pi pi that suffered from unknown free parameters (Truong) and addresses the long-standing tension between the chiral anomaly prediction and experimental data from the Serpukhov experiment (Antipov). The approach enables using the full data set from the ongoing COMPASS Primakoff measurement (Nagel) rather than only threshold data, and provides vital input for dispersive analyses of the pi0 transition form factor needed for hadronic light-by-light scattering contributions to the muon anomalous magnetic moment (g-2 review).
