# Investigating the dependence of collective dynamics on n/p asymmetry for light nuclei

> The dynamics present in the fusion of neutron-rich nuclei is explored through the comparison of experimental cross-sections at above-barrier energies with measurements of the interaction cross-section at relativistic energies. The increase of fusion dynamics with increasing neutron excess is clearly demonstrated. Experimental cross-sections are compared with the predictions of a Sao Paulo model using relativistic mean field density distributions and the impact of different interactions is explored.

## Key claim

By comparing experimental fusion cross-sections at above-barrier energies with interaction cross-sections at relativistic energies, the paper demonstrates that fusion dynamics for light carbon isotopes increase with neutron excess, but this conclusion is conditional on the interpretation that the difference between the average fusion cross-section and the interaction cross-section reflects the role of dynamics.

## Method

The authors measured fusion excitation functions for 10-15C + 12C at near-barrier energies using an active target approach, with beam intensities as low as 500 ions/s. They compared these experimental cross-sections with predictions from the Sao Paulo model using relativistic mean field density distributions calculated with two interactions (FSUGOLD and NL3). They also compared the average fusion cross-section over 14-17 MeV center-of-mass energy with interaction cross-sections measured at relativistic energies (E/A ~ 900 MeV) for carbon isotopes.

## Key numbers

- Interaction cross-section for 14C is approximately 850 mb, while for 16C it jumps to approximately 1050 mb, reflecting the completion of the 1p1/2 shell at N=8 and population of the 1d5/2 shell.
- The average fusion cross-section was calculated over the interval 14 MeV <= ECM <= 17 MeV, designated as <sigma_fusion>_14^17.
- For 12C, the average fusion cross-section and interaction cross-section are essentially the same value.
- For N>Z, the fusion cross-section depends more strongly on neutron excess than the interaction cross-section does, as shown by the larger slope for fusion data.

## Context

This work extends previous measurements of fusion cross-sections for carbon isotopes by the ANL group (Carnelli 2014, 2015; Calderon 2015) and interaction cross-section measurements at GSI-Darmstadt (Ozawa 2001a, 2001b) and LBL Bevalac (Ozawa 1996). The paper introduces a novel method of comparing fusion cross-sections with interaction cross-sections to isolate the role of dynamics, enabling future measurements at FRIB for more neutron-rich carbon isotopes (16-19C) and oxygen isotopes near the neutron drip line.
