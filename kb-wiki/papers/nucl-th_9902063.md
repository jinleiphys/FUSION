# Event by Event fluctuations and Inclusive Distribution

> Event-by-event observables are compared with conventional inclusive measurements. We find that moments of event-by-event fluctuations are closely related to inclusive correlation functions. Implications for upcomming heavy ion experiments are discussed.

# Event by Event fluctuations and Inclusive Distribution

## Key claim
Moments of event-by-event fluctuations of any (multi-particle) observable can be expressed in terms of inclusive distributions, provided the inclusive distributions are known up to twice the order of the observable under consideration; however, fluctuations of ratios of observables cannot simply be expressed in terms of ratios of inclusive measurements and require knowledge of inclusive distributions in narrow multiplicity intervals, unless the central limit theorem applies.

## Method
The authors derive explicit algebraic relations between event-averaged moments of event-by-event observables (such as sums over particles in an event) and integrals over n-particle inclusive distribution functions. For a single-particle observable S(x) = sum_i x(p_i), they show that <S^k> can be written as sums of integrals of inclusive distributions rho_n up to order n = k, with combinatorial factors. For two-particle observables T = sum_{i != j} y(p_i,p_j), analogous relations involve inclusive distributions up to order 4 for the second moment. They then analyze the case of intensive variables (ratios s = S/N and t = T/[N(N-1)]) and show that these require either multiplicity-binned inclusive data or, under the central limit theorem, can be approximated from inclusive spectra plus knowledge of <N> and <N^2>.

## Key numbers
- For 200 AGeV Pb+Pb collisions, there are about 200 negatively charged particles per unit rapidity at mid rapidity.
- Assuming a two-arm spectrometer covering about half a unit of rapidity, the relative fluctuation in particle number is about <(N - <N>)^2>/<N>^2 ~ 1/<N> ~ 1 percent.
- The dispersion of S at fixed N follows D^2_N ~ N delta^2 under the central limit theorem, where delta is a constant independent of N.

## Context
This work extends earlier proposals by Stodolsky (1995) and Shuryak (1998) to use event-by-event fluctuations of temperature or transverse momentum to extract thermodynamic properties like heat capacity, and by the authors themselves (Bialas and Koch, prior work) on fluctuation analysis. It provides the formal connection between event-by-event analysis and standard inclusive correlation measurements, enabling experiments such as NA49 to compare both approaches. The paper also notes that the formalism applies to any phase-space region, not requiring a 4pi detector, and that it cannot be used to investigate tails of event-by-event distributions for rare exotic events.
