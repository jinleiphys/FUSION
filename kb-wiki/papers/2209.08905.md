# Shape evolution in the rapidly rotating $^{140}$Gd nucleus

> Ground state band of $^{140}$Gd has been investigated following their population in the $^{112}$Sn($^{35}$Cl,~$α$p2n)$^{140}$Gd reaction at 195 MeV of beam energy using a large array of Compton suppressed HPGe clovers as the detection setup. Apart from other spectroscopic measurements, level lifetimes of the states have been extracted using the Doppler Shift Attenuation Method. Extracted quadrupole moment along with the pairing independent cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky model calculations for the quadrupole band reveal that the nucleus preferably attains triaxiality with $γ$ = -30$^\circ$. The calculation though shows a slight possibility of rotation around the longest possible principal axis at high spin $\sim$ 30$\hbar$ which is beyond the scope of the present experiment.

## Key claim

The ground-state band of $^{140}$Gd preferentially attains triaxiality with $\gamma = -30^\circ$ (intermediate-axis rotation) at low spin, and the cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky calculations suggest a slight possibility of rotation around the longest principal axis ($\gamma \sim -75^\circ$) at high spin $\sim 30\hbar$, but this is beyond the scope of the present experiment.

## Method

The excited states of $^{140}$Gd were populated via the $^{112}$Sn($^{35}$Cl, $\alpha$p2n)$^{140}$Gd fusion-evaporation reaction at 195 MeV beam energy, using a 2.44 mg/cm$^2$ thick $^{112}$Sn target with a 8.8 mg/cm$^2$ $^{208}$Pb backing. Gamma-ray coincidence data were recorded with eleven Compton-suppressed HPGe clover detectors arranged at four angles, and level lifetimes were extracted using the Doppler Shift Attenuation Method with the LINESHAPE package. The experimental quadrupole moments were compared with pairing-independent cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky (CNS) model calculations performed over a mesh of deformation parameters $\varepsilon_2$, $\varepsilon_4$, and $\gamma$.

## Key numbers

- Level lifetime of the $8^+$ state: $1.2(2)$ ps, corresponding to $B(E2) = 0.5(1)$ $e^2b^2$ and $Q_t = 3.9(3)$ $eb$.
- Level lifetime of the $10^+$ state: $0.9(1)$ ps, corresponding to $B(E2) = 0.7(1)$ $e^2b^2$ and $Q_t = 4.6(3)$ $eb$.
- Level lifetime of the $12^+$ state: $3.5(4)$ ps, corresponding to $B(E2) = 0.8(1)$ $e^2b^2$ and $Q_t = 4.8(3)$ $eb$.
- Level lifetime of the $14^+$ state: $1.5(1)$ ps, corresponding to $B(E2) = 0.8(1)$ $e^2b^2$ and $Q_t = 4.8(3)$ $eb$.
- Level lifetime of the $16^+$ state: $0.6(1)$ ps, corresponding to $B(E2) = 0.6(1)$ $e^2b^2$ and $Q_t = 4.1(3)$ $eb$.
- Effective lifetime of the $18^+$ state: $0.6$ ps (lower limit), with $B(E2) = 0.3$ $e^2b^2$ (upper limit) and $Q_t = 2.9$ $eb$ (upper limit).
- The CNS calculations identify the configuration [4,8] (4 protons and 8 neutrons in $h_{11/2}$ orbitals) as yrast, with total energy surfaces scanned for spins from $4\hbar$ to $30\hbar$.

## Context

This work extends the study of triaxial rotation in the $A \sim 140$ mass region, following prior investigations of $^{142}$Gd by Carlsson et al. (1995) where long-axis rotation was observed only for a single transition. The measured quadrupole moments for $^{140}$Gd provide the first systematic lifetime information for the ground-state band, enabling comparison with CNS calculations that predict evolution from intermediate-axis rotation ($\gamma \sim -30^\circ$) at low spin toward possible long-axis rotation ($\gamma \sim -75^\circ$) at higher spin. This enables future experiments at higher angular momentum to confirm the predicted rotation around the longest principal axis in a single nucleus.
