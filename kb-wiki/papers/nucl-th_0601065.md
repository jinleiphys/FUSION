# Neutron star properties and the equation of state of neutron-rich matter

> We calculate total masses and radii of neutron stars (NS) for pure neutron matter and nuclear matter in beta-equilibrium. We apply a relativistic nuclear matter equation of state (EOS) derived from Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) calculations. We use realistic nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions defined in the framework of the meson exchange potential models. Our results are compared with other theoretical predictions and recent observational data. Suggestions for further study are discussed.

# Neutron star properties and the equation of state of neutron-rich matter

## Key claim

The authors calculate neutron star masses and radii using a relativistic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) equation of state, finding that for pure neutron matter the three Bonn potentials (A, B, C) produce essentially identical results, but this agreement is specific to these potentials and does not imply general model independence. The predictions are compared with observational data, but the paper emphasizes that the high-density EOS remains uncertain above approximately 3 × 10¹⁴ g/cm³.

## Method

The authors apply a relativistic DBHF approach to derive the equation of state for both pure neutron matter and nuclear matter in beta-equilibrium (including electrons and muons). They use the Bonn A, B, and C one-boson-exchange potentials as the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction, solve the relativistic Bethe-Goldstone equation self-consistently for the G-matrix and single-particle potentials, and then integrate the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations using a fifth-order Runge-Kutta scheme to obtain neutron star masses and radii for a range of central energy densities.

## Key numbers

- Observed neutron star masses in six known binaries fall in the range 1.36 ± 0.08 solar masses (M☉), cited from Thorsett and Chakrabarty.
- Normal nuclear density is ρ₀ ≈ 0.17 fm⁻³.
- The high-density EOS is uncertain above ρₙ ≈ 3 × 10¹⁴ g/cm³.
- The maximum proton fraction in beta-stable matter increases from 0.11 to about 0.13 when muons are included (with Bonn B potential).
- The Bonn A, B, and C potentials predict deuteron D-state probabilities of 4.5%, 5.1%, and 5.5%, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous DBHF studies of isospin-asymmetric matter (Alonso and Sammarruca, 2005) and neutron skins (Alonso and Sammarruca, 2005) to higher densities relevant for neutron star structure. It builds on earlier neutron star calculations using DBHF by Ostgaard (1994) and Sumiyoshi (1995), as well as non-relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approaches with three-body forces by Baldo and Burgio (1998). The results enable future comparisons with observational constraints from neutron star mass and radius measurements, and the authors suggest further study of hyperon contributions and phase transitions to quark matter.
