# Perturbative matching of continuum and lattice quasi-distributions

> Matching of the quasi parton distribution functions between continuum and lattice is addressed using lattice perturbation theory specifically with Wilson-type fermions. The matching is done for nonlocal quark bilinear operators with a straight Wilson line in a spatial direction. We also investigate operator mixing in the renormalization and possible O(a) operators for the nonlocal operators based on a symmetry argument on lattice.

## Key claim

The one-loop perturbative matching between continuum and lattice quasi-PDFs for Wilson-type fermions shows that the nonlocal quark bilinear operator can mix with a different Dirac structure, and this mixing is proportional to the Wilson parameter \(r\); the mixing becomes negligible at large quark-antiquark separations when HYP2 smearing is applied to the Wilson line.

## Method

The author performed one-loop lattice perturbation theory for nonlocal quark bilinear operators with a straight Wilson line in the spatial \(z\)-direction, using Wilson fermions with a plaquette gluon action and Feynman gauge. The matching coefficients between continuum and lattice were computed for the operators \(O_{\Gamma}(\delta z)\) with \(\Gamma=\gamma_3\) and \(\Gamma'=\mathbb{1}\), both for unsmeared and HYP2-smeared Wilson lines. Separately, a symmetry analysis using parity, time reversal, charge conjugation, and chiral symmetry was carried out to determine the operator mixing pattern and possible \({\cal O}(a)\) operators at nonperturbative level.

## Key numbers

* The one-loop matching coefficients \(c_{\Gamma\Gamma}(\delta z, r)\) and \(c_{\Gamma\Gamma'}(\delta z, r)\) were computed for \(\Gamma=\gamma_3\) and \(\Gamma'=\mathbb{1}\) as functions of the separation \(\delta z\) and the Wilson parameter \(r\); the mixing coefficient \(c_{\gamma_3\mathbb{1}}(\delta z)\) is proportional to \(r\) and becomes negligible for larger \(\delta z\) when HYP2 smearing is applied.
* The mixing part of the one-loop amplitude is always proportional to \(r\), so the \(r=0\) case (chiral fermions) does not cause operator mixing.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative matching calculations for quasi-PDFs initiated by Ji (2013) and the large-momentum effective theory (LaMET) framework of Ji (2014). It confirms and generalizes the operator mixing pattern first found in one-loop lattice perturbation by Constantinou (2017) for clover-Wilson fermions, and supports the nonperturbative renormalization studies using RI/MOM scheme by Alexandrou (2017), Chen (2017), Green (2017), and Stewart (2017). The symmetry analysis provides a nonperturbative foundation for the mixing and identifies \({\cal O}(a)\) operators that survive even with chiral fermions, motivating future \({\cal O}(a)\)-improvement programs for nonlocal operators in lattice QCD simulations of quasi-PDFs.
