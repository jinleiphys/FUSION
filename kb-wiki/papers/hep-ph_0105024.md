# Chiral Transition and the Scalar and Vector Correlations

> The properties of the scalar and vector correlations in the hot and/or dense hadronic matter close to chiral transition are discussed. Presuming that the linear realization of chiral symmetry will become appropriate at least near the critical point, we argue that the strength function in the $I=J=0$ channel will soften near the critical point, and the sigma meson will accordingly become a clearer resonance in hot and/or dense medium than in the free space. It is shown that the steep rise of the baryon-number susceptibility $χ_B$ around the critical point seen in the lattice simulations may suggest that the interactions between quarks in the vector channel become week near the critical point; this implies that the peculiar behavior of the $χ_B$ does not necessarily imply a proliferation of baryons and antibaryons (Skyrmions) which is though a natural scenario of chiral transition in the non-linear realization of chiral symmetry.

## Key claim

The steep rise of the baryon-number susceptibility near the chiral critical point, observed in lattice simulations, may be explained by weakening vector-channel interactions between quarks rather than requiring a proliferation of baryons and antibaryons (Skyrmions) as would be natural in the non-linear realization of chiral symmetry.

## Method

The author analyzes the relationship between the baryon-number susceptibility and the vector-vector correlation function, showing that the susceptibility is the zero-momentum, zero-frequency limit of the longitudinal component of the retarded Green's function in the vector channel. Using the fluctuation-dissipation theorem and the connection between the susceptibility and the isothermal compressibility, the author argues that strong vector interactions suppress the susceptibility, so its observed rise near the critical point implies weak vector coupling at high temperature.

## Key numbers

- The real part of the sigma meson pole in the pi-pi scattering matrix ranges from 400 MeV to 800 MeV, as determined from phase-shift analysis in the I=J=0 channel.
- The spectral enhancement in the sigma channel near the 2m_pi threshold (approximately 280 MeV) is predicted to occur in hot and/or dense medium due to partial restoration of chiral symmetry.
- The CHAOS collaboration observed an enhancement in the reaction A(pi+, (pi+ pi-)_(I=J=0))A' near the 2m_pi threshold, which the author interprets as experimental evidence of partial chiral symmetry restoration in heavy nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies on the sigma meson as the soft mode of chiral transition (Kunihiro 1991, Hatsuda and Kunihiro 1994) and the baryon-number susceptibility as a probe of chiral and deconfinement transitions (Kunihiro 2000). It contrasts with the Skyrmion proliferation scenario proposed by DeTar (1988) for the non-linear realization of chiral symmetry, and with the vector-realization approach based on hidden-local symmetry (Bando 1985, Harada 1996). The analysis enables experimental searches for the sigma meson and partial chiral symmetry restoration using nuclear reactions, photo-production, and heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
