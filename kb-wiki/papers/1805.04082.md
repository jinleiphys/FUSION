# Dissociation of heavy quarkonium states in rapidly varying strong magnetic field

> In a transient magnetic field, heavy quarkonium bound states evolve non adiabatically. In presence of a strong magnetic field, $J/Ψ$ and $Υ(1S)$ become more tightly bound than we expected earlier for a pure thermal medium. We have shown that in a time varying magnetic field, there is a possibility of moderate suppression of $J/Ψ$ through the non adiabatic transition to continuum where as the $Υ(1S)$ is so tightly bound that can not be dissociated through this process. We have calculated the dissociation probabilities up to the first order in the time dependent perturbation theory for different values of initial magnetic field intensity.

# Dissociation of heavy quarkonium states in rapidly varying strong magnetic field

## Key claim
In a transient magnetic field that decays rapidly with time, the $J/\Psi$ charmonium state can undergo moderate dissociation (12-50%) through non-adiabatic transitions to the continuum, while the $\Upsilon(1S)$ bottomonium state remains so tightly bound that it cannot be dissociated by this mechanism. This result is obtained within first-order time-dependent perturbation theory and depends on the initial magnetic field intensity, which is not precisely known in heavy-ion collisions.

## Method
The authors computed the time-dependent heavy quark potential in a strongly magnetized medium by incorporating the magnetic-field-dependent Debye screening mass, which includes contributions from gluon loops and fermion loops with Landau-level quantization in the strong-field limit ($eB/m^2 \gg 1$, $eB/T^2 \gg 1$). They modeled the magnetic field decay as $B = B_0/(1+at)$ with $a=0.5$, and the medium temperature evolution as $T(t) = T_0(\tau_0/(\tau_0+t))^{1/3}$ with $\tau_0 = 5$ fm/c. Using first-order time-dependent perturbation theory, they calculated transition probabilities from the ground states of charmonia ($J/\Psi$) and bottomonia ($\Upsilon(1S)$) to unbound plane-wave continuum states, treating the time-dependent part of the potential as the perturbation.

## Key numbers
- The magnetic field strength at production can be as large as $B \simeq 50 m_\pi^2$, where $m_\pi^2 = 10^{18}$ Gauss.
- The magnetic field decays such that at $t \simeq 0.4$ fm it is practically negligible, unless QGP formation increases its persistence time.
- The dissociation probability of $J/\Psi$ ranges from 12% to 50% over an initial magnetic field intensity range of $1-15 m_\pi^2$.
- The dissociation probability of $\Upsilon(1S)$ is approximately zero over the same range of initial magnetic field intensity ($1-15 m_\pi^2$).
- In the presence of magnetic field, the dissociation temperature of $J/\Psi$ becomes $2.73-2.94 T_c$ and of $\Upsilon(1S)$ becomes $8.12-8.89 T_c$, compared to approximately $2.2 T_c$ and $4 T_c$ respectively in a pure thermal medium.
- The Debye mass calculation uses parameters: $M_B = 1$ GeV, string tension $\sigma = 0.18$ GeV$^2$, $\mu_0 = 1.1$ GeV, and $\Lambda_V = 0.385$ GeV.

## Context
This work extends studies of heavy quarkonium suppression in the Quark-Gluon Plasma by incorporating the effect of a time-dependent magnetic field, which modifies the heavy quark potential through magnetic-field-dependent Debye screening. Prior work by Tuchin (2011) examined ionization of bound states due to tunneling in a magnetic field, while studies by Bonati et al. (2015), Guo et al. (2015), and Yang et al. (2011) investigated quarkonium suppression in magnetized media. The non-adiabatic evolution approach was previously applied in the context of evolving QGP by Dutta (2012) and rapid thermalization by Bagchi (2014). The results suggest that while $J/\Psi$ suppression may have a contribution from non-adiabatic transitions in transient magnetic fields, $\Upsilon(1S)$ remains robust against this mechanism, motivating further work with exact solution of the time-dependent Schrodinger equation for more precise predictions.
