# First application of dispersive optical model to ($p$,$2p$) analysis within distorted wave impulse approximation framework

> Both ($e$,$e'p$) and ($p$,$2p$) reactions have been performed to study the proton single-particle character of nuclear states with its related spectroscopic factor. Recently, the dispersive optical model (DOM) was applied to the ($e$,$e'p$) analysis revealing that the traditional treatment of the single-particle overlap function, distorted waves, and nonlocality must be further improved to achieve quantitative nuclear spectroscopy. We apply the DOM wave functions to the traditional ($p$,$2p$) analysis and investigate the consistency of the DOM spectroscopic factor that describes the ($e$,$e'p$) cross section with the result of the ($p$,$2p$) analysis. Additionally, we make a comparison with a phenomenological single-particle wave function and optical potential. Uncertainty arising from a choice of $p$-$p$ interaction is also investigated. We implement the DOM wave functions to the distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) framework for ($p$,$2p$) reactions. DOM + DWIA analysis on $^{40}$Ca($p$,$2p$)$^{39}$K data generates a proton $0d_{3/2}$ spectroscopic factor of 0.560, which is meaningfully smaller than the DOM value of 0.71 shown to be consistent with the ($e$,$e'p$) analysis. Uncertainties arising from choices of single-particle wave function, optical potential, and $p$-$p$ interaction do not explain this inconsistency. The inconsistency in the spectroscopic factor suggests there is urgent need for improving the description of $p$-$p$ scattering in a nucleus and the resulting in-medium interaction with corresponding implications for the analysis of this reaction in inverse kinematics.

## Key claim

The dispersive optical model (DOM) wave functions, when applied to the distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) analysis of the $^{40}$Ca($p$,$2p$)$^{39}$K reaction, yield a spectroscopic factor that is meaningfully smaller than the DOM value that successfully describes the ($e$,$e'p$) data, and this inconsistency cannot be explained by uncertainties from choices of single-particle wave function, optical potential, or $p$-$p$ interaction.

## Method

The authors implemented the nonlocal DOM single-particle wave functions and distorted waves into the nonrelativistic factorized DWIA framework for ($p$,$2p$) reactions. They analyzed the $^{40}$Ca($p$,$2p$)$^{39}$K reaction at 197 MeV in coplanar kinematics with equal emission angles of 42.0 degrees, extracting the proton $0d_{3/2}$ spectroscopic factor by fitting the theoretical triple differential cross section to experimental data from the E258 experiment at RCNP. They compared results using DOM inputs with those using the phenomenological Kramer single-particle wave function and Koning-Delaroche or Dirac phenomenology optical potentials, and also tested three different $p$-$p$ effective interactions: the Franey-Love interaction, the Melbourne $g$-matrix at mean density, and the Melbourne $g$-matrix at zero density.

## Key numbers

- DOM + DWIA analysis of $^{40}$Ca($p$,$2p$)$^{39}$K yields a proton $0d_{3/2}$ spectroscopic factor of $0.560 \pm 0.005$ using the Franey-Love $p$-$p$ interaction.
- The DOM-calculated spectroscopic factor that reproduces $^{40}$Ca($e$,$e'p$)$^{39}$K data is $0.71 \pm 0.04$, representing a discrepancy of at least 21%.
- Using the Kramer phenomenological single-particle wave function with the Koning-Delaroche optical potential gives a spectroscopic factor of $0.623 \pm 0.006$, and with Dirac phenomenology gives $0.672 \pm 0.006$.
- Using DOM wave functions with the Melbourne $g$-matrix at mean density gives $0.489 \pm 0.005$, and with the Melbourne $g$-matrix at zero density gives $0.515 \pm 0.005$.
- The experimental data were taken at an incident proton energy of 197 MeV, with one outgoing proton energy as low as 36 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the application of the dispersive optical model, previously applied to ($e$,$e'p$) reactions by Mack (2018, 2019), to the ($p$,$2p$) reaction for the first time. The DOM framework, developed from the original work by Mahaux and Sartor (1991) and extended by Mahzoon (2014) and Dickhoff (2017), provides a unified description of bound and scattering nucleon wave functions. The inconsistency found between the ($p$,$2p$) and ($e$,$e'p$) spectroscopic factors suggests the need for improving the treatment of $p$-$p$ scattering inside a nucleus beyond the standard $t$- or $g$-matrix approach, with implications for analyzing ($p$,$2p$) reactions in inverse kinematics for exotic nuclei.
