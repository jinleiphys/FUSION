# Determining the Ξ-Nucleus Potential from the Measured Binding Energies of ^15_Ξ^C

> The recent observation of the deeply bound Ξ- hypernucleus ^15_Ξ^-C through the IRRAWADDY and KINKA events provided a crucial benchmark for determining the Ξ-nucleus interaction. This work aims to constrain the depth of this potential by calculating the binding energy B_Ξof the ^15_ΞC system, which forms a Ξ^- -^14 N bound state. We achieve this by numerically solving the Schroedinger equation for a Ξhyperon within a phenomenological Woods-Saxon potential, using the stable Numerov method, incorporating the Coulomb interaction. For a potential well depth V_0 = 12 MeV, our calculations yield a 0^+_1 state binding energy of 6.35 MeV and a 1^-_1 state energy of 0.87 MeV. These results are in excellent agreement with the IRRAWADDY event (B_Ξ= 6.27 \pm 0.27 MeV) and the shallower 1^-_1 states (KISO/IBUKI events, B_Ξ\approx 1 MeV), respectively. Assuming Ξ^0 instead of Ξ^-, we predict the ground state 0^+ of _{Ξ^0}^{15}N with (B_{Ξ^0} = 2.636 MeV) by omitting the Coulomb interaction as a first approximation.

## Key claim

The authors claim that a Woods-Saxon potential depth of exactly 12 MeV simultaneously reproduces both the deeply bound ground state (IRRAWADDY event) and the shallower excited state (KISO/IBUKI events) of the Ξ⁻-¹⁴N system, contradicting earlier estimates of 14–16 MeV that overbind the ground state relative to the new experimental data.

## Method

The authors numerically solved the radial Schrödinger equation for a Ξ⁻ hyperon bound to a ¹⁴N core using the Numerov method with a phenomenological Woods-Saxon nuclear potential plus a finite-size Coulomb potential. They varied the potential depth V₀ from 10 to 20 MeV while fixing the diffuseness parameter a = 0.65 fm and nuclear radius parameter r₀ = 1.2 fm, then compared the calculated binding energies of the 0⁺₁ and 1⁻₁ states to experimental values from the J-PARC E07 (IRRAWADDY) and earlier KEK (KISO/IBUKI) emulsion experiments.

## Key numbers

- For V₀ = 12 MeV, the calculated 0⁺₁ state binding energy is 6.352 MeV, in agreement with the IRRAWADDY event value of 6.27 ± 0.27 MeV
- For V₀ = 12 MeV, the calculated 1⁻₁ state binding energy is 0.868 MeV, consistent with the KISO/IBUKI events value of approximately 1 MeV
- For V₀ = 14 MeV, the calculated 0⁺₁ state binding energy is 7.586 MeV, which overestimates the IRRAWADDY value
- The root-mean-square radius of the 0⁺₁ state at V₀ = 12 MeV is 2.852 fm, smaller than the nuclear radius of approximately 2.92 fm
- For the Ξ⁰-¹⁴N system (switching off Coulomb), the predicted ground state binding energy of ¹⁵_Ξ⁰N is 2.636 MeV at V₀ = 12 MeV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the Ξ-nucleus interaction following the first observation of a deeply bound Ξ⁻ hypernucleus in the IRRAWADDY event by Hayakawa (2021), which challenged earlier shallower potential models. The authors compare their results to the Ehime one-boson-exchange potential predictions by Yamaguchi (2001) and Sasaki (2020), and to the BNL E885 missing-mass measurement by Khaustov (2000) which suggested a potential depth of 14–16 MeV. The finding that V₀ = 12 MeV best fits the combined data enables more precise constraints on theoretical models of the S = -2 sector of the strong interaction and provides a benchmark for future calculations of Ξ hypernuclei and neutron star equations of state.
