# $Θ$ vacua states in heavy ion collisions in presence of dissipation and noise

> We have studied possible formation of $Θ$ vacua states in heavy ion collisions. Random phases of the chiral fields were evolved in a finite temperature potential, incorporating the breaking of $U_A(1)$ symmetry. Initial random phases very quickly settle into oscillation around the values dictated by the potential. The simulation study indicate that an initial $Θ$=0 state do not evolve into a $Θ$ $\neq$ 0 state. However, an initial $Θ$ $\neq$ 0 state, if formed in heavy ion collision, can survive, as a coherent superposition of a number of modes.

## Key claim

The simulation shows that an initial $\Theta=0$ state does not evolve into a $\Theta \neq 0$ state even in the presence of dissipation and noise, but an initial $\Theta \neq 0$ state, if formed, can survive as a coherent superposition of multiple modes. The author notes that the sample of 1000 events may be too small to detect a $\Theta$ vacua state, which is expected to be a rare event.

## Method

The author solved the equation of motion for chiral phases $\phi_i$ ($i=u,d,s$) derived from Witten's effective non-linear sigma model on a $64^2$ lattice with 1 fm spacing, using boost-invariant coordinates. The potential included temperature-dependent anomaly and Goldstone boson mass terms, with a dissipative term (friction coefficient $\eta_0=200$ MeV) and a Gaussian white noise term satisfying the fluctuation-dissipation theorem. Initial random phases were evolved for 20 fm under a cooling law $T(\tau)=T_d \tau_i/\tau$ with decoupling temperature $T_d=160$ MeV, and 1000 events were classified by a space-time integrated phase parameter $G$ to identify the most favorable event for $\Theta$ vacua formation.

## Key numbers

- Decoupling temperature: $T_d = 160$ MeV
- Friction coefficient: $\eta_0 = 200$ MeV
- Lattice size: $64^2$ with lattice spacing $a = 1$ fm and time interval $a/10$ fm
- Initial time: $\tau_i = 1$ fm; evolution time: 20 fm
- For initial $\Theta=0$, the classification parameter $G$ ranges between [0.02, 0.75]
- For initial $\Theta=4\pi/16$, $G$ ranges between [154.1, 154.9]
- Zero-temperature parameters: $\mu^2_{u0}=(114\ \mathrm{MeV})^2$, $\mu^2_{d0}=(161\ \mathrm{MeV})^2$, $\mu^2_{s0}=(687\ \mathrm{MeV})^2$, $a_0=(492\ \mathrm{MeV})^2$
- For $\Theta=4\pi/16$, minimization gives $\phi_u=0.502$, $\phi_d=0.243$, $\phi_s=0.013$

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier study (Chaudhuri, 2001) by including temperature dependence of the potential, dissipation, and noise, and contrasts with Buckley et al. (2000) who observed $\Theta$ vacua formation within $10^{-23}$ sec in a quench scenario without fluctuations. The study was motivated by the suggestion of Kharzeev, Pisarski, and Tytgat (1998) and Kharzeev and Pisarski (1999) that nontrivial $\Theta$ vacua states may be created in heavy ion collisions via restoration of $U_A(1)$ symmetry at the deconfining phase transition. The results leave open the possibility of $\Theta$ vacua formation in heavy ion collisions, with potential signals including $\eta \rightarrow \pi^+ \pi^-$ decay (suggested by Buckley et al., 2000) or P-odd observables of order $10^{-3}$ (estimated by Kharzeev and Pisarski, 1999).
