# Analysis of $Λ_c(2595)$, $Λ_c(2625)$, $Λ_b(5912)$, $Λ_b(5920)$ based on a chiral partner structure

> We construct an effective hadronic model including $Λ_c(2595)$, $Λ_c(2625)$, $Λ_b(5912)$ and $Λ_b(5920)$ regarding them as chiral partners to $Σ_c(2455)$, $Σ_c(2520)$, $Σ_b$ and $Σ_b^\ast$, respectively, with respecting the chiral symmetry and heavy-quark spin-flavor symmetry. We determine the model parameters from the experimental data for relevant masses and decay widths of $Σ_c^{(\ast)}$ and $Λ_c(2595)$. Then, we study the decay widths of $Λ_c(2625)$, $Λ_b(5912)$ and $Λ_b(5920)$. We find that, although the decay of $Λ_c(2595)$ is dominated by the resonant contribution through $Σ_c(2455)$, non-resonant contributions are important for $Λ_c(2625)$, $Λ_b(5912)$ and $Λ_b(5920)$, which reflects the chiral partner structure. We also study the radiative decays of the baryons, and show that each of their widths is determined from the radiative decay width of their chiral partners.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the negative-parity baryons \(\Lambda_c(2595)\), \(\Lambda_c(2625)\), \(\Lambda_b(5912)\), and \(\Lambda_b(5920)\) are chiral partners to the positive-parity baryons \(\Sigma_c(2455)\), \(\Sigma_c(2520)\), \(\Sigma_b\), and \(\Sigma_b^\ast\), respectively, and constructs an effective model to test this assignment; the abstract's claim that non-resonant contributions are important for the decays of \(\Lambda_c(2625)\), \(\Lambda_b(5912)\), and \(\Lambda_b(5920)\) is conditional on the specific chiral partner structure and the model parameters being fixed from \(\Sigma_c^{(\ast)}\) and \(\Lambda_c(2595)\) data.

## Method

The authors construct an effective Lagrangian for single heavy baryons (SHBs) respecting chiral symmetry and heavy-quark spin-flavor symmetry, embedding the \(\Sigma_Q\) (positive-parity, iso-triplet) and \(\Lambda_{Q1}\) (negative-parity, iso-singlet) fields into a single chiral multiplet \(S_Q^\mu\). Model parameters \(g_2^Q\) (for charm and bottom) are determined from the mass differences of the chiral partner pairs, and the coupling \(g_3\) from the \(\Sigma_c \to \Lambda_c \pi\) decay widths. The two-pion decay widths of \(\Lambda_{Q1}^{(\ast)} \to \Lambda_Q \pi\pi\) are then calculated, including both resonant contributions (via intermediate \(\Sigma_Q^{(\ast)}\)) and non-resonant contact terms, with the parameters \(h_1^I\) and \(h_2\) constrained by the total width of \(\Lambda_c(2595)\).

## Key numbers

- Mass difference for charm chiral partners: \(\Delta M_c^{(1/2,\ \mathrm{exp})}/f_\pi = 1.19\) and \(\Delta M_c^{(3/2,\ \mathrm{exp})}/f_\pi = 1.53\), yielding \(g_2^c = 1.30^{+0.23}_{-0.11}\).
- Mass difference for bottom chiral partners yields \(g_2^b = 0.980^{+0.090}_{-0.046}\).
- Coupling constant \(g_3 = 0.688^{+0.013}_{-0.025}\), determined from \(\Sigma_c \to \Lambda_c \pi\) decays.
- Predicted decay width of \(\Sigma_c^{++} \to \Lambda_c^+ \pi^+\): \(1.96^{+0.07}_{-0.14}\) MeV (experiment: \(1.89^{+0.09}_{-0.18}\) MeV).
- Predicted decay width of \(\Sigma_c^{*++} \to \Lambda_c^+ \pi^+\): \(14.7^{+0.6}_{-1.1}\) MeV (experiment: \(14.78^{+0.30}_{-0.40}\) MeV).
- Predicted total two-pion decay width of \(\Lambda_c(2625)\): \(0.106\)–\(0.733\) MeV (experimental upper limit: \(<0.97\) MeV).
- Predicted total two-pion decay width of \(\Lambda_b(5912)\): \((2.1\)–\(10) \times 10^{-3}\) MeV (experimental upper limit: \(<0.66\) MeV).
- Predicted total two-pion decay width of \(\Lambda_b(5920)\): \((3.0\)–\(25) \times 10^{-3}\) MeV (experimental upper limit: \(<0.63\) MeV).

## Context

This work extends the chiral partner concept for heavy-light mesons (Nowak 1993, Bardeen 2003) to single heavy baryons, proposing a new assignment where \(\Lambda_Q\) baryons with negative parity are chiral partners to \(\Sigma_Q\) baryons with positive parity, in contrast to the earlier study by Liu (2011) which considered negative-parity \(\Sigma_c\) as chiral partners. The model enables predictions for the hadronic and radiative decays of \(\Lambda_c(2625)\), \(\Lambda_b(5912)\), and \(\Lambda_b(5920)\), and the authors note that the predicted tiny hadronic widths for the bottom-sector baryons make their radiative decays potentially competitive, providing a test for future experiments.
