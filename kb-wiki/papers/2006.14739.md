# Hadron production in elementary nucleon-nucleon reactions from low to ultra-relativistic energies

> We study the hadron production in $p+p$, $p+n$ and $n+n$ reactions within the microscopic Parton-Hadron-Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach in comparison to PYTHIA 8.2. We discuss the details of the "PHSD tune" of the Lund string model (realized by event generators FRITIOF and PYTHIA) in the vacuum (as in $N+N$ collisions) as well as its in-medium modifications relevant for heavy-ion collisions where a hot and dense matter is produced. We compare the results of PHSD and PYTHIA 8.2 (default version) for the excitation function of hadron multiplicities as well as differential rapidity $y$, transverse momentum $p_T$ and $x_F$ distributions in $p+p$, $p+n$ and $n+n$ reactions with the existing experimental data in the energy range $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.7 - 7000$ GeV. We discuss the production mechanisms of hadrons and the role of final state interactions (FSI) due to the hadronic rescattering. We also show the influence of the possible quark-gluon plasma (QGP) formation on hadronic observables in $p+p$ collisions at LHC energies. We stress the importance of developing a reliable event generator for elementary reactions from low to ultra-relativistic energies in view of actual and upcoming heavy-ion experiments.

## Key claim

The PHSD transport approach, using a "PHSD tune" of the Lund string model (FRITIOF 7.02 and PYTHIA 6.4), provides a reliable description of hadron production in elementary nucleon-nucleon reactions from low to ultra-relativistic energies, but only when final state interactions (FSI) and isospin-dependent tuning are included; the default PYTHIA 8.2 systematically overestimates multiplicities for pions, protons, and antiprotons.

## Method

The authors used the Parton-Hadron-Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, which solves Kadanoff-Baym equations with a collision term, to simulate p+p, p+n, and n+n collisions from sqrt(s_NN) = 2.7 to 7000 GeV. The Lund string model (FRITIOF 7.02 and PYTHIA 6.4) was tuned by lowering the string threshold to sqrt(s_BB)=2.65 GeV for baryon-baryon collisions, adjusting strangeness suppression factors (gamma_s=0.3 at SPS to RHIC, 0.4 at AGS energies), modifying flavour decomposition for mesonic and baryonic states, and incorporating fully relativistic Breit-Wigner spectral functions with mass-dependent widths. The PHSD results were compared to default PYTHIA 8.2 (in 'Soft QCD' mode) and experimental data.

## Key numbers

*   The PHSD calculations cover the energy range sqrt(s_NN) = 2.7 - 7000 GeV for p+p, p+n, and n+n reactions.
*   The string threshold was lowered from the default 10 GeV to sqrt(s_BB)=2.65 GeV for BB collisions, sqrt(s_mB)=2.4 GeV for mB collisions, and sqrt(s_mm)=1.3 GeV for mm collisions.
*   The strangeness suppression factor gamma_s is 0.3 at SPS to RHIC energies and 0.4 at AGS energies, with a linear transition as a function of sqrt(s_NN).
*   The fragmentation function parameters used in PHSD are a=0.23 and b=0.34 GeV^{-2}.
*   The formation time for prehadronic states is tau_F ~ 0.8 fm/c in the rest-frame of the string.
*   The critical energy density for hadronization in PHSD is epsilon_c = 0.5 GeV/fm^3.
*   The string tension kappa is approximately 0.176 GeV^2.
*   The constituent quark masses adopted are m_u ~ 0.35 GeV and m_s ~ 0.5 GeV.
*   The role of final state interactions (FSI) reaches a few percent (<5%) at LHC energies.

## Context

This work extends the PHSD transport approach (Cassing 2008, Bratkovskaya 2011) and its earlier version HSD (Cassing 1999) by systematically comparing the "PHSD tune" of the Lund string model (FRITIOF and PYTHIA) to default PYTHIA 8.2 (Sjostrand 2006) across a wide energy range. It enables reliable event generation for upcoming heavy-ion experiments at FAIR and NICA, and for the RHIC BES-II fixed target program, by providing a validated reference for elementary nucleon-nucleon collisions that accounts for isospin dependence and final state interactions.
