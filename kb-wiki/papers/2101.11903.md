# Low-density neutron matter and the unitary limit

> We review the properties of neutron matter in the low-density regime. In particular, we revise its ground state energy and the superfluid neutron pairing gap, and analyze their evolution from the weak to the strong coupling regime. The calculations of the energy and the pairing gap are performed, respectively, within the Brueckner--Hartree--Fock approach of nuclear matter and the BCS theory using the chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction of Entem and Machleidt at N$^3$LO and the Argonne V18 phenomenological potential. Results for the energy are also shown for a simple Gaussian potential with a strength and range adjusted to reproduce the $^1S_0$ neutron-neutron scattering length and effective range. Our results are compared with those of quantum Monte Carlo calculations for neutron matter and cold atoms. The Tan contact parameter in neutron matter is also calculated finding a reasonable agreement with experimental data with ultra-cold atoms only at very low densities. We find that low-density neutron matter exhibits a behavior close to that of a Fermi gas at the unitary limit, although, this limit is actually never reached. We also review the properties (energy, effective mass and quasiparticle residue) of a spin-down neutron impurity immersed in a low-density free Fermi gas of spin-up neutrons already studied by the author in a recent work where it was shown that these properties are very close to those of an attractive Fermi polaron in the unitary limit.

# Low-density neutron matter and the unitary limit

## Key claim

Low-density neutron matter exhibits behavior close to that of a Fermi gas at the unitary limit, but this limit is actually never reached; the system approaches but does not achieve the unitary regime because the neutron-neutron scattering length, while large at -18.9(4) fm, remains finite.

## Method

The ground state energy of low-density neutron matter was calculated using the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach, and the superfluid neutron pairing gap was computed using BCS theory. Both calculations employed the chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction of Entem and Machleidt at N$^3$LO with a 500 MeV cutoff (EM500) and the Argonne V18 (AV18) phenomenological potential, with additional energy results from a simple Gaussian potential tuned to reproduce the $^1S_0$ neutron-neutron scattering length and effective range. The Tan contact parameter was derived from fits of the BHF energy results to the Lacroix density functional, and the properties of a spin-down neutron impurity in a free Fermi gas of spin-up neutrons were analyzed within the BHF approach.

## Key numbers

- Neutron-neutron $^1S_0$ scattering length: $a_s = -18.9(4)$ fm; effective range: $r_e = 2.75(11)$ fm
- Bertsch parameter from most precise experimental measurement with ultra-cold atoms: $\xi = 0.376(4)$ (Ku et al., 2012)
- BHF results for $E/E_{FG}$ in the range $6.61 < -k_F a_s < 9.45$ ($0.35 < k_F < 0.5$ fm$^{-1}$): 0.561 (EM500), 0.563 (AV18), 0.566 (Gaussian potential)
- Maximum BCS pairing gap: $\sim 0.50 E_F$ at $-k_F a_s = 4.4$ (EM500) and $\sim 0.53 E_F$ at $-k_F a_s \sim 4.1$ (AV18)
- QMC maximum pairing gap for neutron matter: $\sim 0.3 E_F$ at $k_F \sim 0.27$ fm$^{-1}$ (Gezerlis and Carlson, 2008)
- Energy of spin-down neutron impurity: $E_{pol} = \eta E_F$ with $\eta = -0.63$ (EM500) and $\eta = -0.64$ (AV18)
- Average effective mass of neutron impurity over $k_F = 0.2-0.5$ fm$^{-1}$: $m^*_\downarrow = 1.18 m$ (EM500) and $1.19 m$ (AV18)
- Average quasiparticle residue over $k_F = 0.2-0.5$ fm$^{-1}$: $Z_\downarrow = 0.78$ (EM500) and $0.79$ (AV18)
- P-wave contributions increase ground state energy by $\sim 7\%$ at $-k_F a_s = 10$ and $(2-3)\%$ at $-k_F a_s = 5$

## Context

This review extends previous work on low-density neutron matter, including BHF calculations by Baldo and Maieron (2008), variational calculations by Friedman and Pandharipande (1981), and finite volume Green's function Monte Carlo calculations by Carlson et al. (2003). The results are compared with quantum Monte Carlo calculations for neutron matter and cold atoms by Gezerlis and Carlson (2008), and with experimental data from ultra-cold atomic gases of $^6$Li and $^{40}$K. The analysis of the neutron impurity builds on the author's recent work (Vidana, 2021) and connects to the Fermi polaron studies by Chevy (2006), Prokofev and Svistunov (2008), and experimental measurements with spin-polarized $^6$Li atoms by Shin et al. (2008) and Schirotzek et al. (2009). The work enables further understanding of neutron star crust physics and the BCS-BEC crossover in nuclear systems, with open questions remaining about the role of medium polarization effects on the pairing gap and the exact density at which unitary-like behavior is maximized.
