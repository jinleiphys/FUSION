# Emergence of hyperons in failed supernovae: trigger of the black hole formation

> We investigate the emergence of strange baryons in the dynamical collapse of a non-rotating massive star to a black hole by the neutrino-radiation hydrodynamical simulations in general relativity. By following the dynamical formation and collapse of nascent proto-neutron star from the gravitational collapse of a 40Msun star adopting a new hyperonic EOS table, we show that the hyperons do not appear at the core bounce but populate quickly at ~0.5-0.7 s after the bounce to trigger the re-collapse to a black hole. They start to show up off center owing to high temperatures and later prevail at center when the central density becomes high enough. The neutrino emission from the accreting proto-neutron star with the hyperonic EOS stops much earlier than the corresponding case with a nucleonic EOS while the average energies and luminosities are quite similar between them. These features of neutrino signal are a potential probe of the emergence of new degrees of freedom inside the black hole forming collapse.

## Key claim

Hyperons do not appear at core bounce but populate quickly at ~0.5–0.7 s after bounce, initially off-center due to high temperatures, and then trigger the re-collapse to a black hole; the neutrino burst terminates much earlier than in the purely nucleonic case, while average energies and luminosities remain quite similar.

## Method

The authors performed neutrino-radiation hydrodynamical simulations in general relativity under spherical symmetry, following the collapse of a non-rotating 40 M☉ progenitor star (Woosley 1995) from onset through core bounce, post-bounce accretion, and black hole formation. They used a new hyperonic equation of state (Ishizuka 2008) extending the Shen EOS to include the full baryon octet, and compared results with the purely nucleonic Shen EOS (model SH) and the softer Lattimer-Swesty EOS (model LS). Neutrino-hyperon interactions were ignored because hyperons appear only ~200 ms before black hole formation, too short to affect diffusion rates.

## Key numbers

- Hyperons first appear off-center at t_pb ~ 500 ms after bounce, with mass fraction of a few percent (mostly Λ and Σ⁻) around 10 km (0.7 M☉ mass coordinate).
- Hyperons become major compositions by t_pb = 680 ms, when central density exceeds three times nuclear matter density.
- Black hole formation occurs at t_pb = 682 ms in model IS (hyperonic EOS), compared to t_pb = 1345 ms in model SH (nucleonic Shen EOS).
- Critical gravitational mass for the hot proto-neutron star is 2.1 M☉ in model IS versus 2.4 M☉ in model SH.
- Maximum masses of cold neutron stars are 2.2 M☉ for the Shen EOS and 1.6 M☉ for the hyperonic EOS.
- Temperature at the off-center peak exceeds 50 MeV at t_pb = 500 ms.
- Central electron fraction remains high (~0.3) up to black hole formation.

## Context

This work extends the previous studies of black hole forming collapses by Sumiyoshi (2006, 2007), which considered only nucleonic degrees of freedom, by incorporating a hyperonic EOS developed by Ishizuka (2008). The rapid dynamical appearance of hyperons in accreting proto-neutron stars is entirely different from the quasi-static cooling scenario of proto-neutron stars studied by Pons (1999, 2001), where hyperons appear gradually over >10 s through deleptonization. The results enable future neutrino observations to potentially distinguish hyperon emergence from other softening mechanisms such as softer nucleonic EOS (Lattimer-Swesty 1991) or quark matter, and motivate systematic studies with neutrino oscillations and progenitor dependence.
