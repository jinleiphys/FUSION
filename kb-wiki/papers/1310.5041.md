# Spin constraints on nuclear energy density functionals

> The Gallagher-Moszkowski rule in the spectroscopy of odd-odd nuclei imposes a new spin constraint on the energy functionals for self-consistent mean field theory. The commonly used parameterization of the effective three-body interaction in the Gogny and Skyrme families of energy functionals is ill-suited to satisfy the spin constraint. In particular, the Gogny parameterization of the three-body interaction has the opposite spin dependence to that required by the observed spectra. The two-body part has a correct sign, but in combination the rule is violated as often as not. We conclude that a new functional form is needed for the effective three-body interaction that can take into better account the different spin-isospin channels of the interaction.

# Spin constraints on nuclear energy density functionals

## Key claim
The commonly used parameterization of the effective three-body interaction in both Gogny and Skyrme families of nuclear energy density functionals has the wrong sign for the spin dependence required by the Gallagher-Moszkowski rule for odd-odd nuclei; the Gogny three-body interaction in particular gives the opposite spin dependence to that required by observed spectra, and when combined with the two-body part the rule is violated as often as it is satisfied.

## Method
The authors developed a new Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) code for axially symmetric nuclei that treats time-odd fields including spin-dependent ones, and applied it to compute spin splittings of neutron-proton two-quasiparticle configurations in deformed odd-odd rare-earth nuclei. They separated the contributions into three parts: the spin dependence of the two-body interaction treated in first-order perturbation theory, the spin dependence of the density-dependent three-body interaction treated perturbatively, and the many-body rearrangement effects from wave function modifications in the two-quasiparticle state.

## Key numbers
- In 174Lu, the perturbative two-body contribution to the GM splitting is +188 keV, the three-body contribution is -291 keV, and the rearrangement contribution is +44 keV, giving a total splitting of -61 keV compared to an empirical value of +114 keV (after removing rotational effects from the observed 171 keV splitting).
- For the D1S Gogny interaction in 164-168Ho, the two-body interaction alone agrees with the GM rule in 93% of cases, the three-body alone in 8%, the full perturbative treatment in 28%, and the fully self-consistent HFB calculation in 45%.
- For 168-172Tm: two-body 97%, three-body 4%, full perturbative 26%, self-consistent 41%.
- For 172-176Lu: two-body 97%, three-body 4%, full perturbative 28%, self-consistent 40%.
- For 180-184Ta: two-body 97%, three-body 5%, full perturbative 37%, self-consistent 30%.
- For 184-188Lu: two-body 97%, three-body 3%, full perturbative 36%, self-consistent 28%.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of time-odd fields in nuclear energy density functionals (Dobaczewski 1995, Bender 2002, Potel 2012) by applying the Gallagher-Moszkowski rule from odd-odd nuclear spectroscopy (Gallagher and Moszkowski 1958, Bohr and Mottelson 1976) as a new constraint. The paper demonstrates that the contact form of the effective three-body interaction used in both Gogny and Skyrme functionals is fundamentally unable to reproduce the empirical spin dependence of the neutron-proton interaction, and that a finite-range three-body interaction or derivative terms (Raimondi 2011, Sadoudi 2013) will be needed. The results enable future work on developing new functional forms that can properly account for the different spin-isospin channels of the interaction.
