# The 2020 Skyrmionics Roadmap

> The notion of non-trivial topological winding in condensed matter systems represents a major area of present-day theoretical and experimental research. Magnetic materials offer a versatile platform that is particularly amenable for the exploration of topological spin solitons in real space such as skyrmions. First identified in non-centrosymmetric bulk materials, the rapidly growing zoology of materials systems hosting skyrmions and related topological spin solitons includes bulk compounds, surfaces, thin films, heterostructures, nano-wires and nano-dots. This underscores an exceptional potential for major breakthroughs ranging from fundamental questions to applications as driven by an interdisciplinary exchange of ideas between areas in magnetism which traditionally have been pursued rather independently. The skyrmionics roadmap provides a review of the present state of the art and the wide range of research directions and strategies currently under way. These are, for instance, motivated by the identification of the fundamental structural properties of skyrmions and related textures, processes of nucleation and annihilation in the presence of non-trivial topological winding, an exceptionally efficient coupling to spin currents generating spin transfer torques at tiny current densities, as well as the capability to purpose-design broad-band spin dynamic and logic devices.

## Key claim

This roadmap reviews the present state of skyrmionics research, covering both equilibrium and non-equilibrium properties of skyrmions and related topological spin textures, but it is explicitly a review of ongoing research directions rather than a presentation of a single new experimental result or theoretical breakthrough.

## Method

The paper is a multi-author roadmap that compiles review sections on different aspects of skyrmion physics, including non-centrosymmetric bulk materials hosting skyrmions, skyrmions in achiral magnets with competing interactions, and skyrmions far from equilibrium in bulk materials. Each section summarizes the status, current challenges, and advances needed, drawing on published experimental and theoretical results from the literature.

## Key numbers

- Co₉Zn₉Mn₂ exhibits equilibrium skyrmion crystal state at 390 K and 0.04 T, with metastable skyrmions observed at 290 K and 0 T (Karube 2017).
- SrFeO₃ undergoes an antiferromagnetic phase transition at T_N ∼ 130 K, ascribed to the onset of helical spiral order (Ishiwata 2018).
- In frustrated magnets, the period of magnetic modulations often does not exceed a few lattice constants, making observation by Lorentz transmission electron microscopy and magnetic force microscopy difficult.

## Context

This roadmap extends the review tradition of skyrmionics by covering both chiral and achiral magnet systems, including frustrated magnets and non-equilibrium states. It builds on foundational work such as the discovery of skyrmion crystals in MnSi (Mühlbauer 2009) and the identification of skyrmions in B20 compounds (Yu 2010), and it points toward future challenges including achieving smaller skyrmion sizes at higher temperatures, understanding three-dimensional skyrmion string dynamics, and developing electrical detection methods such as topological Hall and planar Hall effects.
