# The $a_0(980)$ and $О›(1670)$ in the $О›^+_c \to ПЂ^+ О·О›$ decay

> We propose to study the $a_0(980)$ and the $О›(1670)$ resonances in the $О›^+_c \to ПЂ^+ О·О›$ decay via the final state interactions of the $ПЂ^+ О·$ and $О·О›$ pairs. The weak interaction part proceeds through the $c$ quark decay process: $c(ud) \to (s + u + \bar d)(ud)$, while the hadronization part takes place in two different mechanisms. In the first mechanism, the $sud$ cluster picks up a $q\bar{q}$ pair from the vacuum to form the $О·О›$ meson-baryon pair while the $u\bar{d}$ pair from the weak decay hadronizes into a $ПЂ^+$. In the second, the $sud$ cluster turns into a $О›$, while the $u\bar{d}$ pair from the $c$ decay picks up a $q\bar{q}$ pair and hadronizes into a meson-meson pair ($ПЂО·$ or $K\bar{K}$). Because the final $ПЂ^+ О·$ and $О·О›$ states are in pure isospin $I = 1$ and $I=0$ combinations, the $О›^+_c \to ПЂ^+ О·О›$ decay can be an ideal process to study the $a_0(980)$ and $О›(1670)$ resonances. Describing the final state interaction in the chiral unitary approach, we find that the $ПЂ^+ О·$ and $О·О›$ invariant mass distributions, up to an arbitrary normalization, show clear cusp and peak structures, which can be associated with the $a_0(980)$ and $О›(1670)$ resonances, respectively. The proposed mechanism can provide valuable information on the nature of these resonances and can in principle be test by facilities such as BEPCII.

## Key claim

The $\Lambda^+_c \to \pi^+ \eta \Lambda$ decay can serve as an ideal process to study the $a_0(980)$ and $\Lambda(1670)$ resonances because the final $\pi^+ \eta$ and $\eta \Lambda$ states are in pure isospin $I=1$ and $I=0$ combinations, respectively, but the visibility of the $a_0(980)$ structure in the $\pi^+ \eta$ invariant mass distribution depends on the unknown relative strength and phase between the two hadronization mechanisms.

## Method

The weak decay proceeds via $c(ud) \to (s+u+\bar d)(ud)$, followed by hadronization in two mechanisms: (1) the $sud$ cluster picks up a $q\bar q$ pair from the vacuum to form an $\eta\Lambda$ meson-baryon pair while the $u\bar d$ pair hadronizes into a $\pi^+$; (2) the $sud$ cluster turns into a $\Lambda$ while the $u\bar d$ pair picks up a $q\bar q$ pair and hadronizes into a $\pi^+\eta$ or $K^+\bar K^0$ meson-meson pair. Final state interactions of the $\eta\Lambda$ and $\pi^+\eta$ pairs are described using the chiral unitary approach, with meson-baryon scattering amplitudes from Oset (2001) and meson-meson scattering amplitudes from Oller (1997).

## Key numbers

- The branching ratio $\mathrm{Br}(\Lambda^+_c \to \pi^+ \eta \Lambda)$ is $(2.4 \pm 0.5)\%$, one of the dominant decay modes of the $\Lambda^+_c$ state.
- For $M_{\pi^+ \eta} = 980$ MeV, the allowed $\eta\Lambda$ invariant mass range is $1680$ MeV to $2043$ MeV.
- For $M_{\eta \Lambda} = 1670$ MeV, the allowed $\pi^+ \eta$ invariant mass range is $1011$ MeV to $1104$ MeV.
- The $a_0(980)$ total decay width is around $170$ MeV in the chiral unitary approach.
- The relative normalization between the two hadronization mechanisms yields $V'_P = 0.38 V_P$ under the constraint of equal partial widths.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Miyahara (2015) on $\Lambda^+_c \to \pi^+ MB$ weak decays, which studied $\Lambda(1405)$ and $\Lambda(1670)$ resonances, by additionally including the $\pi^+ \eta$ final state interaction to study the $a_0(980)$ resonance. The $a_0(980)$ has been suggested as a $q\bar q$ state, tetraquark, meson-meson molecule, glueball, or dynamically generated state (Klempt 2007), while the $\Lambda(1670)$ has been argued to be either a three-quark state (Starostin 2001) or dynamically generated from meson-baryon interactions (Oset 2001). The proposed mechanism can be tested at facilities such as BEPCII/BESIII.
