# Role of ($őĪ$,n) reactions under $r$-process conditions in neutrino-driven winds revisited

> Background: The astrophysical $r$-process occurs in an explosive astrophysical event under extremely neutron-rich conditions, leading to (n,$ő≥$)-($ő≥$,n) equilibrium along isotopic chains which peaks around neutron separation energies of a few MeV. Nuclei with larger $Z$ are usually produced by $ő≤^-$-decay, but under certain conditions also $őĪ$-induced reactions may become relevant for the production of nuclei with $Z+2$. Purpose: The uncertainties of the reaction rates of these $őĪ$-induced reactions are discussed within the statistical model. As an example, $őĪ$-induced ($őĪ$,n) and $(őĪ$,$x$n) reaction cross sections for the neutron-rich $^{86}$Se nucleus are studied in detail. Method: In a first step, the relevance of ($őĪ$,n) and $(őĪ$,$x$n) reactions is analyzed. Next the uncertainties are determined from a variation of the $őĪ$-nucleus potential which is the all-dominant parameter for the astrophysical $Z \rightarrow Z+2$ reaction rate. Results: It is found that the $r$-process flow towards nuclei with larger $Z$ is essentially influenced only by the $őĪ$-nucleus potential whereas the other ingredients of the statistical model play a very minor role. This finding is based on the fact that the flow towards larger $Z$ depends on the sum over all ($őĪ$,$x$n) cross sections which is practically identical to the total $őĪ$-induced reaction cross section. Conclusions: $őĪ$-nucleus potentials play an important role under certain $r$-process conditions because the flow towards larger $Z$ depends sensitively on the total $őĪ$-induced reaction cross section. The uncertainty of the reaction rate is about a factor of two to three at higher temperatures and exceeds one order of magnitude at very low temperatures.

## Key claim

Under r-process conditions in neutrino-driven winds, the astrophysically relevant quantity for producing nuclei with Z+2 is the sum over all (őĪ,xn) cross sections, which is practically identical to the total őĪ-induced reaction cross section; this means the reaction rate depends only on the őĪ-nucleus potential and is insensitive to other ingredients of the statistical model, contrary to the impression from the abstract that the uncertainty arises from multiple sources.

## Method

The author analyzed the relevance of (őĪ,n) and (őĪ,xn) reactions for the r-process flow towards larger Z, using the neutron-rich nucleus ‚Āł‚Ā∂Se as a detailed example. The total őĪ-induced reaction cross section ŌÉ_reac was calculated from the reflexion coefficients ő∑_L by solving the Schr√∂dinger equation for eight different őĪ-nucleus potentials (McFadden/Satchler, Sauerwein et al., ATOMKI-V1, Avrigeanu et al., Watanabe/TALYS default, and three versions from Demetriou et al.). The uncertainty of the reaction rate was determined from the variation of ŌÉ_reac across these potentials, with the Gamow window for temperatures T‚āČ = 2‚Äď5 used to identify the astrophysically relevant energy range.

## Context

This work extends the sensitivity study of Pereira and Montes (2016), who found that (őĪ,n) reaction rates on ‚Āł‚Ā∂Se are uncertain by at least an order of magnitude at low temperatures due to the őĪ-nucleus potential choice. The present study adds three additional őĪ-nucleus potentials (Avrigeanu et al. 2014, Sauerwein et al. 2011, and the ATOMKI-V1 potential from Mohr 2013) and shows that the astrophysically relevant quantity is the total őĪ-induced reaction cross section rather than the exclusive (őĪ,n) channel. This finding enables more reliable r-process network calculations by clarifying that inclusive (őĪ,xn) rates from codes like NON-SMOKER should be used rather than exclusive (őĪ,n) rates from TALYS, and it highlights the need for experimental tests of őĪ-nucleus potentials on neutron-rich nuclei at future radioactive ion beam facilities.
