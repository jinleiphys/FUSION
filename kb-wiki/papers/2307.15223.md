# Central Speed of Sound, Trace Anomaly and Observables of Neutron Stars from Perturbative Analyses of Scaled TOV Equations

> The central speed of sound (SS) measures the stiffness of the Equation of State (EOS) of superdense neutron star (NS) matter. Its variations with density and radial coordinate in NSs in conventional analyses often suffer from uncertainties of the specific nuclear EOSs used. Using the central SS and NS mass/radius scaling obtained from solving perturbatively the scaled Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations, we study the variations of SS, trace anomaly and several closely related properties of NSs in an EOS-model independent manner. We find that the SS increases with the reduced central pressure $\widehat{P}_{\rm{c}}\equiv P_{\rm{c}}/\varepsilon_{\rm{c}}$ (scaled by the central energy density $\varepsilon_{\rm{c}}$), and the conformal bound for SS tends to break down for NSs with masses higher than about 1.9$M_{\odot}$. The ratio $P/\varepsilon$ is upper bounded as $P/\varepsilon\lesssim0.374$ around the centers of stable NSs. We demonstrate that it is an intrinsic property of strong-field gravity and is more relevant than the perturbative QCD bound on it. While a sharp phase transition at high densities characterized by a sudden vanishing of SS in cores of massive NSs are basically excluded, the probability for a continuous crossover signaled by a peaked radial profile of SS is found to be enhanced as $\widehat{P}_{\rm{c}}$ decreases, implying it likely happens near the centers of massive NSs. Moreover, a new and more stringent causality boundary as $R_{\max}/\rm{km}\gtrsim 4.73M_{\rm{NS}}^{\max}/M_{\odot}+1.14$ for NS M-R curve is found to be excellently consistent with observational data on NS masses and radii. Furthermore, new constraints on the ultimate energy density and pressure allowed in NSs before collapsing into black holes are obtained and compared with earlier predictions in the literature.

## Key claim

Using a perturbative analysis of the scaled Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations that avoids reliance on specific nuclear equation-of-state models, the authors find that the conformal bound for the speed of sound (squared less than 1/3) tends to break down for neutron stars with masses higher than about 1.9 solar masses, and that the ratio P/ε is upper bounded at about 0.374 near the centers of stable neutron stars as an intrinsic property of strong-field gravity rather than a perturbative QCD bound.

## Method

The authors solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations perturbatively by scaling the neutron star mass and radius by the central energy density, expanding the reduced energy density, pressure, and mass as polynomial series in the reduced radial coordinate. They then used the resulting scaling relations for the maximum-mass configuration to derive the central speed of sound, trace anomaly, and causality boundaries without inputting any specific dense-matter equation of state, and compared their results against 87 phenomenological and 17 microscopic equation-of-state models.

## Key numbers

- The conformal bound for the speed of sound (s² ≤ 1/3) tends to break down for neutron stars with maximum masses M_NS^max/M_⊙ ≳ 1.89.
- The ratio P/ε near the centers of stable neutron stars is upper bounded as P/ε ≲ 0.374.
- A new causality boundary for the neutron star mass-radius curve is given as R_max/km ≳ 4.73 M_NS^max/M_⊙ + 1.14.
- For a 2.08 solar mass neutron star (PSR J0740+6620), the central speed of sound squared is s_c² ≈ 0.45_{-0.18}^{+0.14} using a reduced central pressure P_c/ε_c ≈ 0.24_{-0.07}^{+0.05}.
- The existence of a 2.08 solar mass neutron star implies an ultimate energy density ε_ult ≲ 1.32 GeV/fm³ and an ultimate pressure P_ult ≲ 494 MeV/fm³.
- The trace anomaly Δ ≡ 1/3 - P/ε is bounded from below as Δ ≳ -0.041 near the centers of massive neutron stars.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier perturbative scaling approach (Cai, Li, Zhang 2023) for extracting the central equation of state of neutron stars directly from observational data. The results contradict the expectation that the conformal bound on the speed of sound holds in massive neutron stars, consistent with previous studies by McLerran and Reddy (2019), Tews et al. (2018), and Altiparmak et al. (2022). The new causality boundary is more stringent than earlier constraints and is consistent with NICER observations of PSR J0740+6620 (Riley et al. 2021) and PSR J0030+0451 (Riley et al. 2019), while the ultimate energy density and pressure limits agree with several recent microscopic model analyses. The work enables equation-of-state independent constraints on the central properties of neutron stars, including the possibility of continuous crossovers near the centers of massive neutron stars.
