# Pseudoscalar mesons in nuclei and partial restoration of chiral symmetry

> Recent theoretical developments of hadrons in nuclei are briefly reviewed in the aspect of chiral symmetry in nuclei. We show a general sum rule connecting in-medium hadronic quantities and quark condensate, emphasizing that both pionic mode and particle-hole excitations in the nuclear medium are responsible for reduction of the chiral condensate in nuclear matter. We also discuss that few-body nuclear systems with kaons are good objects to investigate basic features of kaon-nucleus interactions, showing recent understandings of the structure of Lambda(1405) and possible bound states of KbarNN and KKbarN. We also mention eta mesonic nuclei in connection with chiral symmetry of baryons.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the reduction of the quark condensate in nuclear matter, a signal of partial chiral symmetry restoration, can be connected to experimental observables via an exact sum rule; however, this sum rule requires summing over all zero modes (including both the pion mode and particle-hole excitations) in the nuclear medium, not just the pion mode alone.

## Method

The author derives an exact sum rule in the chiral limit that relates the in-medium quark condensate to matrix elements of the axial current and pseudoscalar density for all zero-mode excitations in symmetric nuclear matter. At linear density, the sum rule simplifies to a scaling law connecting the in-medium quark condensate to the in-medium pion decay constant and pion wavefunction renormalization. The paper also reviews chiral unitary calculations for the Lambda(1405) resonance as a quasibound state of anti-kaon-nucleon, and nonrelativistic potential model and Faddeev calculations for three-body kaonic systems (anti-kaon-nucleon-nucleon and kaon-anti-kaon-nucleon).

## Key numbers

* The isovector pi-minus-nucleus interaction parameter was extracted as b1_free/b1 = 0.78 ± 0.05 at around ρ ~ 0.6 ρ0 from deeply bound pionic atoms (Suzuki, 2002).
* The Lambda(1405) resonance has a state relevant for anti-kaon-nucleon interaction located around 1420 MeV, with a dominant coupling to anti-kaon-nucleon, as opposed to the nominal position of 1405 MeV.
* A possible quasibound state of kaon-anti-kaon-nucleon with I=1/2 and J^P=1/2+ was found with a mass of 1910 MeV and a width of 90 MeV.
* The kaon-anti-kaon-nucleon quasibound state has a binding energy of 20 MeV and a root mean squared radius of 1.7 fm.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework connecting in-medium hadron properties to chiral symmetry restoration, building on the Glashow-Weinberg relation (Glashow, 1967) and prior work on in-medium chiral effective theory (Kolomeitsev, 2002; Jido, 2008). The sum rule approach enables experimental confirmation of partial chiral symmetry restoration using data from deeply bound pionic atoms (Suzuki, 2002) and low-energy pion-nucleus scatterings (Friedman, 2004). The discussion of few-body kaonic systems enables future experimental searches at J-PARC and DAFNE, and the eta mesonic nuclei discussion connects to probing chiral symmetry for baryons through the N(1535) resonance as a chiral partner of the nucleon.
