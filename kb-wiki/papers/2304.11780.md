# Shape transition of Nd and Sm isotopes and neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix element of $^{150}$Nd

> Neutron-rich Nd and Sm isotopes are known to exhibit shape phase transition as a function of neutron number. Among them, $^{150}$Nd and $^{150}$Sm are important not only because they are transitional nuclei, but also the parent and daughter nuclei of double-beta decay. We performed large-scale shell-model calculations of even-even Nd and Sm isotopes including the spherical-deformed shape transition. The quasi-particle vacua shell model enables us to perform shell-model calculations with sufficiently large model space with the $^{110}$Zr inert core. The shell-model result well reproduces the experimental excitation energies and quadrupole properties of the yrast and non-yrast states. The nuclear matrix element of neutrinoless double-beta decay of $^{150}$Nd is evaluated showing its modest enhancement by shape mixing.

## Key claim

The neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix element (NME) of \(^{150}\)Nd is evaluated as 4.00 (without short-range correlation) and shows a modest enhancement of about 20% due to shape mixing between two prolate-deformed components in the ground states of \(^{150}\)Nd and \(^{150}\)Sm, rather than a simple spherical-to-deformed transition.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell-model calculations using the quasi-particle vacua shell model (QVSM) with a \(^{110}\)Zr inert core, including the \(sdg\) shell, \(0h_{11/2}\), \(1f_{7/2}\), and \(2p_{3/2}\) orbits for protons and the \(pfh\) shell, \(0i_{13/2}\), \(1g_{9/2}\), \(2d_{5/2}\), and \(3s_{1/2}\) orbits for neutrons. The Hamiltonian combined the monopole-based universal interaction (with the isoscalar central part of the proton-neutron interaction multiplied by 0.94) and a \(G\)-matrix-based interaction, with pairing matrix elements scaled by 0.9 for protons and 0.7 for neutrons. For \(^{150}\)Nd and \(^{150}\)Sm, 24 QVSM basis states were optimized for the two lowest \(0^+\), \(2^+\), and \(4^+\) states, and the NME was computed in the closure approximation with an intermediate energy of \(E_c = 1.12 A^{1/2}\) MeV and \(g_A/g_V = 1.27\).

## Key numbers

- NME of \(^{150}\)Nd without short-range correlation: \(M^{0\nu} = 4.00\) (Gamow-Teller contribution 3.32, Fermi contribution -1.11, tensor contribution -0.01).
- NME with Miller-Spencer short-range correlation: \(M^{0\nu} = 3.21\) (quenching by \(\sim\)20%).
- NME with CD-Bonn short-range correlation: \(M^{0\nu} = 4.11\).
- NME with Argonne short-range correlation: \(M^{0\nu} = 3.88\).
- NME from the ground state of \(^{150}\)Nd to the \(0^+_2\) state of \(^{150}\)Sm: 1.1.
- Configuration mixing increases the NME by 20% compared to the \(N_b=1\) (single quasiparticle vacuum) value.
- The ground-state wave function of \(^{150}\)Nd is composed of a weaker-deformation component (amplitude 0.50) and a stronger-deformation component (amplitude 0.86); for \(^{150}\)Sm, the amplitudes are 0.65 and 0.76, respectively.
- The NME between the weaker-deformation components of the two nuclei is 3.87; between the stronger-deformation components it is 2.81; the cross terms are 2.70 and -0.28.

## Context

This work extends the Monte Carlo Shell Model (MCSM) study of Sm isotopes by SmEr_PRL_2019 to include Nd isotopes and to evaluate the \(0\nu\beta\beta\)-decay NME of \(^{150}\)Nd using the QVSM, which handles pairing correlations more efficiently than the MCSM with Slater determinants. The results are compared with prior calculations using the Interacting Boson Model (Barea_IBM_PhysRevC.79.044301), quasiparticle random-phase approximation (FangFaessler2010), and generator coordinate method (Yao_150Nd_octupole, Lopez-PRL111), which gave NME values ranging from 2.71 to 5.6. The present work enables more precise extraction of the neutrino mass from future \(0\nu\beta\beta\)-decay experiments on \(^{150}\)Nd and demonstrates the importance of shape mixing in transitional nuclei for NME evaluation.
