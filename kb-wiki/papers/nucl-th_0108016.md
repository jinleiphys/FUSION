# Consequences of a Relativistic Pseudospin Symmetry for Radial Nodes and Intruder Levels in Nuclei

> The identification of pseudospin symmetry as a relativistic symmetry of the Dirac Hamiltonian is used to explain the structure of radial nodes occurring in pseudospin doublets and to illuminate the special status of nodeless intruder states in nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the relativistic origin of pseudospin symmetry, specifically the equality of the lower-component radial wave functions for the two states in a pseudospin doublet, explains the observed relation between radial nodes (n_r vs. n_r-1) in the upper components of pseudospin partners, and also accounts for why nodeless intruder states (j = ℓ + 1/2) have no pseudospin partner eigenstate.

## Method

The authors analyze the radial Dirac equations for a single nucleon in spherically symmetric scalar and vector potentials, deriving general properties of the radial wave functions G (upper component) and F (lower component). They establish the relation between the number of internal nodes of G and F for states with κ < 0 (aligned spin, j = ℓ + 1/2) and κ > 0 (unaligned spin, j = ℓ - 1/2), then apply these results to the relativistic pseudospin SU(2) algebra generators to explain the node structure of pseudospin doublets and the absence of partners for nodeless intruder states.

## Key numbers

- For typical relativistic mean fields in nuclei: scalar potential V_S(0) ~ -400 MeV, vector potential V_V(0) ~ 350 MeV, total energy E ~ 900 MeV, nucleon mass M ~ 940 MeV.
- The quantity A(r) is positive definite with A(0) ~ 1090 MeV; B(0) ~ 10 MeV (small positive) at the origin, becoming negative (E - M < 0) at large r.
- For bound states with κ < 0 (j = ℓ + 1/2): n_F = n_G (number of internal nodes of F equals that of G).
- For bound states with κ > 0 (j = ℓ - 1/2): n_F = n_G + 1.
- Nodeless bound states (n_F = n_G = 0) can occur only for κ < 0 (j = ℓ + 1/2), not for κ > 0.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic pseudospin symmetry framework established by Ginocchio (1997) and Leviatan (1999), which identified pseudospin as a relativistic symmetry of the Dirac Hamiltonian when V_S + V_V = 0. The paper resolves two previously unexplained features of the non-relativistic pseudospin scheme: the specific radial node relation in pseudospin doublets (observed empirically in shell-model orbitals) and the exclusion of intruder abnormal-parity states. The results enable a unified relativistic treatment of both normal-parity and abnormal-parity states in nuclei, and clarify why pseudospin symmetry must be broken (since exact symmetry would preclude bound states) yet remains approximately valid in realistic mean-field calculations.
