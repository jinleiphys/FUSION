# Recent developments in SU(3) covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory

> Baryon chiral perturbation theory (BChPT), as an effective field theory of low-energy quantum chromodynamics (QCD), has played and is still playing an important role in our understanding of non-perturbative strong interaction phenomena. In the past two decades, inspired by the rapid progress in lattice QCD simulations and the new experimental campaign to study the strangeness sector of low-energy QCD, many efforts have been made to develop a fully covariant BChPT and to test its validity in all scenarios. These new endeavours have not only deepened our understanding of some long-standing problems, such as the power-counting-breaking problem and the convergence problem, but also resulted in theoretical tools that can be confidently applied to make robust predictions. Particularly, the manifestly covariant BChPT supplemented with the extended-on-mass-shell (EOMS) renormalization scheme has been shown to satisfy all analyticity and symmetry constraints and converge relatively faster compared to its non-relativistic and infrared counterparts. In this article, we provide a brief review of the fully covariant BChPT and its latest applications in the $u$, $d$, and $s$ three-flavor sector.

## Key claim

The extended-on-mass-shell (EOMS) renormalization scheme for covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory satisfies all analyticity and symmetry constraints and converges relatively faster than its non-relativistic (heavy-baryon) and infrared counterparts, but the paper reviews this claim across multiple applications rather than proving it definitively for all observables.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical foundations of covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory (BChPT) in the three-flavor (u, d, s) sector, comparing four renormalization schemes: the minimal subtraction (MS-bar) scheme, the heavy-baryon (HB) approach, the infrared (IR) scheme, and the extended-on-mass-shell (EOMS) scheme. It explains the power-counting-breaking problem using the nucleon mass as an explicit example, showing how loop diagrams at nominal order p^3 produce lower-order terms that violate the chiral power-counting rule. The EOMS scheme subtracts only the power-counting-breaking terms while preserving the full analytic structure of loop amplitudes, unlike the HB and IR schemes which also remove higher-order analytic terms and introduce unphysical cuts.

## Key numbers

- At next-to-leading order in the one-baryon sector, there are 16 low-energy constants (LECs) at O(p^2), compared to 12 LECs at O(p^4) in the mesonic sector
- At third order (O(p^3)) in the one-baryon sector, the number of independent LECs increases to 78
- The nucleon mass up to NNLO receives contributions from tree-level terms (order 0 and order 2) and one-loop contributions at order 3, with the loop contribution containing PCB terms proportional to 2m_0^3[1+log(mu^2/m_0^2)] and 2m_0 m_pi^2[2+log(mu^2/m_0^2)]
- The infrared scheme introduces an unphysical cut at m_pi = 2 m_N in the nucleon mass calculation

## Context

This review extends the line of work on baryon chiral perturbation theory that began with Weinberg (1978) and was systematically developed by Gasser and Leutwyler (1983, 1984) for mesons and Gasser, Sainio, and Svarc (1987) for baryons. It contrasts the EOMS approach, developed by Fuchs et al. (2003), with the heavy-baryon approach of Jenkins and Manohar (1990) and the infrared scheme of Becher and Leutwyler (1999). The paper enables future applications of covariant BChPT to lattice QCD extrapolations and studies of baryon magnetic moments, masses, sigma terms, and hyperon vector couplings in the three-flavor sector.
