# Variational Theory of Hot Nucleon Matter

> We develop a variational theory of hot nuclear matter in neutron stars and supernovae. It can also be used to study charged, hot nuclear matter which may be produced in heavy-ion collisions. This theory is a generalization of the variational theory of cold nuclear and neutron star matter based on realistic models of nuclear forces and pair correlation operators. The present approach uses microcanonical ensembles and the variational principle obeyed by the free energy. In this paper we show that the correlated states of the microcanonical ensemble at a given temperature $T$ and density $ρ$ can be orthonormalized preserving their diagonal matrix elements of the Hamiltonian. This allows for the minimization of the free energy without corrections from the nonorthogonality of the correlated basis states, similar to that of the ground state energy. Samples of the microcanonical ensemble can be used to study the response, and the neutrino luminosities and opacities of hot matter. We present methods to orthonormalize the correlated states that contribute to the response of hot matter.

## Key claim

The authors show that correlated basis states (CBS) in a microcanonical ensemble at finite temperature can be orthonormalized without changing their diagonal matrix elements of the Hamiltonian, thereby allowing variational free-energy minimization without corrections from nonorthogonality. This result holds only when the microcanonical ensemble is defined with a finite energy width (the set of Most Probable Distributions), not with a sharp energy-conserving constraint.

## Method

The authors generalize the zero-temperature variational theory of cold nuclear matter, based on realistic nuclear forces and pair correlation operators, to finite temperatures using microcanonical ensembles. They construct correlated basis states from a non-interacting Fermi gas via a many-body correlation operator (a symmetrized product of pair correlation operators), then apply a combination of Lowdin (democratic) and Gram-Schmidt (sequential) orthonormalization to the states in the microcanonical ensemble. They evaluate the change in diagonal Hamiltonian matrix elements due to orthonormalization using cluster expansions and diagrammatic techniques, showing that the leading two-body and three-body cluster contributions vanish in the thermodynamic limit when the microcanonical ensemble is defined with a finite energy width.

## Key numbers

* The error in variational calculations of pure neutron matter at densities less than or equal to rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3} is approximately 8 percent, based on comparison with Green's function Monte Carlo calculations.
* The error in variational calculations of symmetric nuclear matter is estimated to be less than 10 percent.
* The two-body cluster contribution to the change in energy per particle (delta e_I) from Lowdin orthonormalization scales as rho Omega^{-2/3}, which vanishes in the thermodynamic limit as N goes to infinity.
* The three-body cluster contribution to delta e_I scales as rho^2 Omega^{-2/3}, which also vanishes in the thermodynamic limit.

## Context

This work extends the variational theory of cold nuclear and neutron star matter developed by Akmal and Pandharipande (1998) and the orthonormalization scheme of Fantoni and Pandharipande (1988) to finite temperatures. It addresses concerns raised in early applications by Schmidt and Pandharipande (1979) and by Friedman and Pandharipande (1981) regarding nonorthogonality corrections to the free energy. The formalism enables ab initio calculations of the free energy, response functions, and neutrino luminosities and opacities of hot matter in neutron stars and supernovae, and can also be applied to charged hot nuclear matter produced in heavy-ion collisions.
