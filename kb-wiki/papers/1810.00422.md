# Ab-initio description of excited states of a one-dimensional nuclear matter with the Hohenberg-Kohn-theorem-inspired functional-renormalization-group method

> We demonstrate for the first time that a functional-renormalization-group aided density-functional theory (FRG-DFT) describes well the characteristic features of the excited states as well as the ground state of an interacting many-body system with infinite number of particles in a unified manner. The FRG-DFT is applied to a $(1+1)$-dimensional spinless nuclear matter. For the excited states, the density--density spectral function is calculated at the saturation point obtained in the framework of FRG-DFT, and it is found that our result reproduces a notable feature of the density--density spectral function of the non-linear Tomonaga-Luttinger liquid: The spectral function has a singularity at the edge of its support of the lower-energy side. These findings suggest that the FRG-DFT is a promising first-principle scheme to analyze the excited states as well as the ground states of quantum many-body systems starting from the inter-particle interaction.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate for the first time that the functional-renormalization-group aided density-functional theory (FRG-DFT) can describe excited states as well as the ground state of an infinite many-body system, specifically showing that the density-density spectral function of a (1+1)-dimensional spinless nuclear matter reproduces the singularity at the lower-energy edge of its support characteristic of a non-linear Tomonaga-Luttinger liquid.

## Method

The authors apply the FRG-DFT method to a (1+1)-dimensional spinless nuclear matter with infinite particle number, using a two-body Gaussian interaction consisting of a short-range repulsive core and long-range attraction. They solve the flow equations for the energy per particle and the two-point density correlation function under a truncation that neglects the flows of higher-order correlation functions but preserves Pauli blocking effects through a multiplicative factor. The density-density spectral function is obtained by performing analytic continuation directly in the flow equations, avoiding numerical methods such as the maximum entropy method or Padé approximation.

## Key numbers

- The saturation energy derived from FRG-DFT is -0.844 (in units where the nucleon mass is 1), with the Monte Carlo result being -0.867 ± 0.003, giving a discrepancy of 2.7%.
- The interaction parameters are chosen as g = 12, σ₁ = 0.2, and σ₂ = 0.8 (in units where the nucleon mass is 1).
- The Fermi momentum is defined as p_F = πn, where n is the ground-state density, and the Fermi energy is E_F = p_F²/2.
- The ground-state energy per particle of the free Fermi gas is E_F/3.

## Context

This work extends the FRG-DFT approach previously applied to zero-dimensional and one-dimensional quantum anharmonic vibrators (Kemler 2013) and to (1+1)-dimensional systems with finite particle numbers (Kemler 2017), where bound-state energies were underestimated by approximately 30% compared to exact solutions. The present calculation for infinite matter achieves much better agreement with Monte Carlo results (Alexander 1989) and demonstrates for the first time that FRG-DFT can describe excited states, enabling future applications to the spectral properties of quantum many-body systems starting from microscopic interactions.
