# Systematic study of $α$ decay for odd-$A$ nuclei within a two-potential approach

> $α$ decay is usually associated with both ground and low-lying isomeric states of heavy and superheavy nuclei, and the unpaired nucleon plays a key role on $α$ decay. In this work, we systematically studied the $α$ decay half-lives of odd-$A$ nuclei, including both favored and unfavored $α$ decay within the two-potential approach based on the isospin dependent nuclear potential. The $α$ preformation probabilities are estimated by using an analytic formula taking into account the shell structure and proton-neutron correlation, and the parameters are obtained through the $α$ decay half-lives data. The results indicate that in general the $α$ preformation probabilities of even-$Z$, odd-$N$ nuclei are slightly smaller than the odd-$Z$, even-$N$ ones. We found that the odd-even staggering effect may play a more important role on spontaneous fission than $α$ decay. The calculated half-lives can well reproduce the experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors systematically calculate alpha decay half-lives for 270 odd-A nuclei using a two-potential approach with an isospin-dependent nuclear potential, finding that the alpha preformation probabilities of even-Z, odd-N nuclei are generally slightly smaller than those of odd-Z, even-N nuclei, and that the odd-even staggering effect plays a more important role on spontaneous fission than on alpha decay.

## Method

The authors employ the two-potential approach (Gurvitz 1987) with a cosh-parametrized nuclear potential whose depth and diffuseness are isospin-dependent (V0 = 192.42 + 31.059(N-Z)/A MeV, a = 0.5958 fm). Alpha preformation probabilities are extracted from experimental half-lives using an analytic formula that accounts for shell structure and proton-neutron correlation, with parameters fitted separately for four nuclear regions defined by major shell closures. For unfavored alpha decays, the centrifugal potential is included with the minimal angular momentum quantum number carried by the emitted alpha particle.

## Key numbers

- 270 odd-A nuclei were studied, including both even-Z/odd-N and odd-Z/even-N nuclei
- Nuclear potential parameters: a = 0.5958 fm, V0 = 192.42 + 31.059(N-Z)/A MeV
- Nuclear sharp radius formula: R = 1.28A^(1/3) - 0.76 + 0.8A^(-1/3) fm
- Average alpha preformation factor P0 = 0.35 for odd-A nuclei (compared to 0.7 for even-even and 0.18 for doubly-odd nuclei)
- Region I (50<Z<82, 82<N<126): a = -1.8782, b = 0.0013, c = 0.0015, d = 0.0049, e = -0.0009
- Region II (82<Z<126, 82<N<126): a = -7.0561, b = 0.004, c = 0.0027, d = 0.0248, e = -0.0017
- Region III.1 (even-odd nuclei, 82<Z<126, 126<N<152): a = 51.2962, b = 0.0044, c = 0.0128, d = -0.2517, e = 0.0172
- Region III.2 (odd-even nuclei, 82<Z<126, 126<N<152): a = 10.6825, b = 0.0042, c = 0.0035, d = -0.0587, e = 0.0015
- Region IV (82<Z<126, 152<N<184): a = 5.3064, b = -0.0085, c = -0.003, d = -0.009, e = 0.0004
- Example half-life: 255Rf has a half-life of 2.3 seconds, while neighboring even-even 254,256Rf undergo spontaneous fission within 23 microseconds and 6.4 milliseconds respectively

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous systematic study of even-even nuclei (Sun 2016) to odd-A nuclei using the same two-potential approach with isospin-dependent nuclear potential. The results are consistent with findings from the Viola-Seaborg formula (Don 2005) regarding the relative alpha preformation probabilities of even-odd versus odd-even nuclei. The study enables improved predictions for alpha decay half-lives of odd-A superheavy nuclei and identifies that unfavored alpha decay, where the alpha particle carries nonzero orbital angular momentum, requires further theoretical development.
