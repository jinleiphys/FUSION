# A proposed solution for the lifetime puzzle of the 229mTh+ isomer

> With the example of the 229Th nucleus, which is the most likely candidate for the creation frequency standard of a future, the dynamics of the interplay and the relationship of various resonance conversion mechanisms is analyzed. As a result, a solution is proposed for the so-called thorium puzzle, which consisted of a contradiction between the experimental and theoretical lifetimes of Th+ ions. First, the solution demonstrates the dependence of the lifetime of the nuclear isomer on the ambient conditions. Second, it demonstrates the leveling role of the fragmentation of the single-electron levels, which makes the resonance amplification of the electron-nuclear interaction more likely. Both of these trends lead to a probable decrease of the theoretical lifetime towards agreement with experiment.

# Wiki Page: Proposed Solution for the Lifetime Puzzle of the 229mTh+ Isomer

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the discrepancy between experimental and theoretical lifetimes of singly charged 229mTh+ ions (the "thorium puzzle") can be resolved by two mechanisms acting together: fragmentation of single-electron levels due to configuration mixing, which increases the probability of resonant electron-nuclear interaction, and collisional broadening from the buffer gas environment, which shortens the observed nuclear lifetime. The authors claim a 24-41% probability that random resonance coincidence can explain the observed lifetime, depending on whether ambient conditions are considered.

## Method

The authors performed multiconfiguration Dirac-Fock (MCDF) calculations of the resonance conversion R factor for the 7s-8s-7p electronic bridge transitions in singly charged 229Th+ ions, considering two possible ground-state electronic configurations (7s(6d)² and (7s)²6d). They computed the R factor as a function of isomer energy over an interval of 0.4 eV around 8.2 eV, and calculated the probability that the R factor exceeds the threshold needed to match the experimental lifetime upper bound of 0.01 s. The analysis included the effect of collisional broadening from the buffer gas at 30-40 mbar pressure and the fragmentation of electronic levels due to configuration mixing.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical analysis of Karpeshin and Trzhaskovskaya (2018, ka18) which calculated a resonance conversion factor of R ≈ 5900 and a lifetime of ~1 s for Th+ ions, still two orders of magnitude above the experimental upper bound from Seiferle (2018, seifdiser) and Seiferle et al. (2017, seif). The paper contradicts the earlier calculation by Porsev et al. (porsev) which gave a much larger discrepancy, arguing that Porsev et al. used an incomplete transition amplitude and omitted the dominant 7s-8s-7p electronic channel. The solution builds on the authors' prior work on resonance conversion in 125Te45+ ions (TeZh, TeHars, Atalah) where fragmentation of electronic levels was shown to enable resonance conversion. The results enable a path toward resolving the thorium puzzle and inform the design of a nuclear-optical frequency standard based on 229Th, suggesting that triply charged ions with 5f valence electrons (Campbell et al., camp) may be less susceptible to environmental broadening than hydrogen-like configurations (Peik, peik; Zylicz, zylic).
