# Quantum computing for heavy-ion physics: near-term status and future prospects

> We discuss recent advances in applying Quantum Information Science to problems in high-energy nuclear physics. After outlining key developments, open challenges, and emerging connections between these disciplines, we highlight recent results on the study of matter states, hard probes, and spin correlations using novel quantum technologies. This work summarizes the corresponding presentation delivered at the Quark Matter 2025 conference in Frankfurt, Germany.

## Key claim

Quantum Information Science methods can address open questions in heavy-ion physics that are inaccessible to standard lattice or perturbative approaches, but the actual realization of a full first-principles simulation of bulk QCD matter evolution on a quantum computer remains a long-term goal, with current focus on simpler or smaller-scale problems.

## Method

The paper reviews recent applications of quantum computing and related classical methods (such as tensor networks) to gauge theories relevant for heavy-ion collisions, including (1+1)D QED and QCD-inspired models. It examines three specific areas: nuclear structure calculations of parton distribution functions using discretized Wilson line operators; real-time dynamics of heavy probes, quarkonia, and jets in thermal backgrounds; and tests of spin correlations and entanglement using quantum processors and classical emulators.

## Key numbers

- The first instances after a heavy-ion collision are characterized by formation of a far-from-equilibrium QCD state at τ ≤ 1 fm/c, with hadronization occurring at τ ≥ 5 fm/c.
- In a (1+1)D QED calculation of the parton distribution function for the first excited state, the expected peak at energy fraction x = 0.5 was found for the Schwinger boson state.
- Quantum simulation of a meson wavepacket in two-dimensional QED on an IBM quantum processor showed results quickly dominated by noise, while exact classical methods easily performed the same simulation.
- In a model calculation of ΛΛ̅ hadronization in a QCD string, spin correlations were extracted from an IBM quantum processor and compared with a classical emulator.
- CMS collaboration measurements of tt̅ production at the LHC indicate the presence of genuine quantum correlations in the measured pairs.

## Context

This work extends the application of Quantum Information Science methods developed for lattice gauge theories (Banuls 2013, Jordan 2012) to heavy-ion physics problems, building on prior theoretical frameworks for parton distribution functions (Ji 2013, Radyushkin 2017) and jet quenching (Baier 1996, Arnold 2019). It enables future first-principles real-time simulations of QCD matter evolution that could benchmark effective descriptions such as kinetic theory and relativistic hydrodynamics, while also opening new experimental tests of quantum correlations in hyperon pairs at the future Electron-Ion Collider.
