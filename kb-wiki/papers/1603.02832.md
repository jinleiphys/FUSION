# Hadron production within PHSD

> We study the production of hadrons in nucleus-nucleus collisions within the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach that is extended to incorporate essentials aspects of chiral symmetry restoration (CSR) in the hadronic sector (via the Schwinger mechanism) on top of the deconfinement phase transition as implemented in PHSD. The essential impact of CSR is found in the Schwinger mechanism (for string decay) which fixes the ratio of strange to light quark production in the hadronic medium. Our studies provide a microscopic explanation for the maximum in the $K^+/π^+$ ratio at about 30 A GeV which only shows up if in addition to CSR a deconfinement transition to partonic degrees-of-freedom is incorporated in the reaction dynamics.

## Key claim
The maximum in the \(K^+/\pi^+\) ratio at about 30 A GeV is explained microscopically by combining chiral symmetry restoration (CSR) in the hadronic sector via the Schwinger mechanism with a deconfinement phase transition to partonic degrees-of-freedom; neither effect alone reproduces the observed peak.

## Method
The Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach was extended to incorporate CSR by making the strange-to-light quark production ratio in string decay depend on the in-medium scalar quark condensate via the Schwinger formula. The in-medium quark condensate was estimated using the Friman et al. (1998) relation, which depends on the scalar nucleon density and meson sigma-commutators, and the resulting effective quark masses were inserted into the Schwinger probability for string fragmentation. The approach was applied to 5% central nucleus-nucleus collisions, and the \(K^+/\pi^+\) and \((\Lambda+\Sigma^0)/\pi^-\) ratios at midrapidity were computed as a function of invariant energy \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\).

## Key numbers
- The string tension \(\kappa \approx 0.176\) GeV\(^2\) is used in the Schwinger formula for the strange-to-light quark production ratio.
- Vacuum dressed quark masses: \(m_u \approx 0.33\) GeV, \(m_s \approx 0.5\) GeV; bare masses: \(m_q^0 \approx 7\) MeV, \(m_s^0 \approx 100\) MeV.
- The vacuum strange-to-light quark production ratio \(\gamma_s \approx 0.3\) is obtained from the Schwinger formula with vacuum masses.
- The pion-nucleon \(\Sigma\)-term is \(\Sigma_\pi \approx 45\) MeV.

## Context
This work extends earlier hadronic transport studies (Bratkovskaya 2004, Weber 2003) that could not describe the \(K^+/\pi^+\) ratio at low energies, and it provides a microscopic mechanism for the "horn" previously attributed only to deconfinement (Gazdzicki and Gorenstein 1999, Gazdzicki, Gorenstein, and Seyboth 2011). The results enable future studies of strangeness enhancement in heavy-ion collisions by separating the roles of CSR and deconfinement, and they motivate further measurements in the 5–40 A GeV energy range.
