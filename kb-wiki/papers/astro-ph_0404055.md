# Neutrino oscillations and supernovae

> In a 1996 JRO Fellowship Research Proposal (Los Alamos), the author suggested that neutrino oscillations may provide a powerful indirect energy transport mechanism to supernovae explosions. The principal aim of this addendum is to present the relevant unedited text of Section 1 of that proposal. We then briefly remind, (a) of an early suggestion of Mazurek on vacuum neutrino oscillations and their relevance to supernovae explosion, and (b) Wolfenstein's result on suppression of the effect by matter effects. We conclude that whether or not neutrino oscillations play a significant role in supernovae explosions shall depend if there are shells/regions of space in stellar collapse where matter effects play no essential role. Should such regions exist in actual astrophysical situations, the final outcome of neutrino oscillations on supernovae explosions shall depend, in part, on whether or not the LNSD signal is confirmed. Importantly, the reader is reminded that neutrino oscillations form a set of flavor-oscillation clocks and these clock suffer gravitational redshift which can be as large as 20 percent. This effect must be incorporated fully into any calculation of supernova explosion.

# Neutrino oscillations and supernovae

## Key claim
Neutrino oscillations may provide an indirect energy transport mechanism that could supply the missing energy (approximately five percent) needed for robust supernova explosions, but only if there exist shells or regions in stellar collapse where matter effects (which suppress vacuum oscillations) play no essential role. The final outcome also depends in part on whether the LSND signal is confirmed.

## Method
The author reproduces the unedited text of Section 1 of his 1996 J Robert Oppenheimer Fellowship Research Proposal, which qualitatively argues that neutrino oscillations between flavors can transfer energy from higher-energy muon and tau neutrinos to the more actively interacting electron neutrinos and antineutrinos. The paper then reviews Mazurek's earlier suggestion of vacuum neutrino oscillations in supernovae and Wolfenstein's result that matter effects suppress such oscillations in collapsing stellar cores, and discusses the additional complication of gravitational redshift of flavor-oscillation clocks.

## Key numbers
- The missing energy needed for a robust supernova explosion is about five percent of the total explosion energy.
- Energy flux in electron neutrinos and antineutrinos is about few × 10^52 ergs s^{-1}, with comparable fluxes of muon and tau neutrinos and their antiparticles.
- Average energy of electron neutrinos is about 10 MeV; average energy of muon neutrinos and antineutrinos is higher by a factor of 2, and of tau neutrinos and antineutrinos by a factor of 3.
- Assuming Δm^2 ≈ 0.4 eV^2 (LSND-suggested), the vacuum oscillation length ranges from about 10 meters for a 1 MeV neutrino to 300 meters for a 25 MeV neutrino.
- Gravitational redshift of flavor-oscillation clocks can be as large as 20 percent.

## Context
This work extends the author's 1996 proposal and earlier work with C. Burgard on terrestrial neutrino anomalies, and directly connects to Mazurek's early talk on vacuum neutrino oscillations in supernovae and Wolfenstein's (1979) demonstration that matter effects suppress oscillations in collapsing stars. The paper enables future quantitative calculations that must incorporate both matter effects (Mikheyev, Smirnov, Wolfenstein) and gravitational effects on neutrino oscillations, and notes that the matter remains unsettled with ongoing work by Goswami, Hix, Liebendoerfer, Ahriche, Fryer, and others.
