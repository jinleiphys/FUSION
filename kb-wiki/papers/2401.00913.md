# Exploring the partonic collectivity in small systems at the LHC

> Using the Hydro-Coal-Frag model that combines hydrodynamics at low $p_{\rm T}$, quark coalescence at intermediate $p_{\rm T}$, and the LBT transport model at high $p_{\rm T}$, we study the spectra and elliptic flow of identified hadrons in high multiplicity p--Pb and p--p collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). In p--Pb collisions, the Hydro-Coal-Frag model gives a good description of the differential elliptic flow over the $p_{\rm T}$ range from 0 to 6 GeV and the approximate number of constituent quark (NCQ) scaling at intermediate $p_{\rm T}$. Although Hydro-Coal-Frag model can also roughly describe the elliptic flow in high multiplicity p--p collisions with the quark coalescence process, the larger contribution from the string fragmentations leads to a notable violation of the NCQ scaling of $v_2$ at intermediate $p_{\rm T}$ as observed in the experiment. Comparison runs of the Hydro-Frag model without the coalescence process demonstrate that regardless the parameter adjustments, the Hydro-Frag model cannot simultaneously describe the $p_{\rm T}$ spectra and the elliptic flow of identified hadrons in either p--Pb collisions or p--p collisions. The calculations in this paper thus provide support for the existence of partonic degrees of freedom and the possible formation of the QGP in the small systems created at the LHC.

## Key claim

Using the Hydro-Coal-Frag model that combines hydrodynamics, quark coalescence, and LBT transport, the authors show that partonic collectivity (quark coalescence) is essential to describe the differential elliptic flow and its approximate number of constituent quark (NCQ) scaling in high-multiplicity p-Pb collisions, while in p-p collisions the larger contribution from string fragmentation leads to a notable violation of NCQ scaling as observed experimentally; the work thus supports the existence of partonic degrees of freedom and possible QGP formation in small systems.

## Method

The authors implement the Hydro-Coal-Frag model, which uses (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics (VISH2+1) with TRENTo initial conditions to produce low-pT hadrons, a quark coalescence model (recombining thermal-thermal, thermal-hard, and hard-hard partons) for intermediate-pT hadrons, and the LBT transport model with PYTHIA8 initial hard partons for high-pT hadrons via string fragmentation. The hadronic afterburner stage is simulated with UrQMD. For comparison, a Hydro-Frag model without the coalescence process is also run. The parameters are tuned to fit the pT spectra of pions, kaons, and protons in high-multiplicity p-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV and p-p collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=13 TeV, and then the differential elliptic flow v2(pT) and its NCQ scaling are predicted.

## Key numbers

- In p-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV (0-20% centrality), the Hydro-Coal-Frag model describes the differential elliptic flow over the pT range from 0 to 6 GeV.
- In p-p collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=13 TeV (105 < N_ch < 150), the model roughly describes the elliptic flow, but the NCQ scaling of v2 at intermediate pT is notably violated.
- The switching temperature for hydrodynamics is set to T_switch=160 MeV for p-Pb and T_switch=150 MeV for p-p collisions.
- The specific shear viscosity eta/s=0.12 and bulk viscosity zeta/s=0.6 for p-Pb; eta/s=0.03 and zeta/s=0.4 for p-p collisions.
- The pT cut-off parameters are p_T1=1.6 GeV and p_T2=2.6 GeV for p-Pb, and p_T1=1.4 GeV and p_T2=2.0 GeV for p-p collisions.
- The strong coupling constant in LBT is set to alpha_s=0.15.
- The gluon virtual mass parameter m_max=1.5 GeV is used to fit the relative spectra between kaon and pion at intermediate pT.
- The Hydro-Frag model underestimates v2(pT)/n for each hadron species by more than 20-50% in both p-Pb and p-p collisions compared to experimental data.

## Context

This work extends the Hydro-Coal-Frag model previously applied to p-Pb and Pb-Pb collisions (Zhao 2020, Zhao 2021) to perform the first systematic study of elliptic flow from 0 to 6 GeV in both high-multiplicity p-p and p-Pb collisions. It directly addresses the experimental observation of approximate NCQ scaling in p-Pb collisions by ALICE (Pacik 2018, CMS 2018) and the violation of that scaling in p-p collisions measured by CMS, ATLAS, and ALICE (ALICE 2020, CMS 2016, ATLAS 2016). The comparison with the Hydro-Frag model demonstrates that quark coalescence is necessary to simultaneously describe the pT spectra and elliptic flow, supporting the interpretation of partonic collectivity and possible QGP formation in small systems.
