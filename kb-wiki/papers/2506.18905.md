# Relativistic corrections to exclusive photoproduction of Quarkonia near-threshold

> Non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) is used to calculate the relativistic correction to the amplitude for exclusive photoproduction of vector Quarkonia in the near-threshold region within the generalized parton distribution (GPD) framework. The relativistic corrections are found to be large for $J/ψ$. Cross-sections for both $J/ψ$ and $Υ$ are calculated with the former being compared to the data. We also demonstrate the presence of endpoint divergences for the relativistic correction away from the near-threshold regime.

## Key claim

Relativistic corrections to exclusive photoproduction of vector quarkonia near threshold are calculated using NRQCD and the GPD framework; the corrections are found to be large for J/psi, but the calculation is restricted to the near-threshold region where a small-x expansion avoids endpoint divergences that appear away from that regime.

## Method

The amplitude for exclusive photoproduction of J/psi and Upsilon is computed at leading order in alpha_s using NRQCD to expand in the relative velocity v of the heavy quark-antiquark pair, with the proton's nonperturbative dynamics encoded in gluon GPDs. The relativistic correction (O(v^2)) is obtained by projecting the full amplitude onto the higher-order NRQCD LDME <P_1(^3S_1)>_V, and the convolution with the GPD is evaluated by expanding the hard matching coefficient about |x| << |xi|, keeping only the zeroth moment of the GPD. The cross-section is then expressed in terms of gravitational form factors A_g, B_g, C_g taken from lattice calculations (Pefkou 2021).

## Key numbers

- For J/psi: charm quark 1S mass m_c^{1S} = 1.3 GeV gives <v^2>_{J/psi} = 0.42 from the Gremm-Kapustin relation; the LDME <O_1(^3S_1)>_{J/psi} = 6.5712/(4pi) GeV^3 from leptonic decay.
- For Upsilon: bottom quark 1S mass m_b^{1S} = 4.7 GeV gives <v^2>_{Upsilon} = 0.013; the LDME <O_1(^3S_1)>_{Upsilon} = 35.1528/(4pi) GeV^3.
- Gravitational form factor parameters from lattice: A_g(0)=0.429, m_A=1.641 GeV; B_g(0)=0.097, m_B=4.0 GeV; C_g(0)=-1.93, m_C=1.07 GeV.
- The relativistic correction to the J/psi cross-section is estimated as (100% +/- 50%)^2 from the charm mass uncertainty, combined with (90% +/- 50%)^2 from missing power corrections (purple band) or (97% +/- 97%)^2 (orange band).
- The asymptotic GPD form implies that neglecting higher x-moments contributes approximately 25% uncertainty.

## Context

This work extends the leading-power (LP) calculation of exclusive photoproduction near threshold by Guo (2021, 2023), which related the amplitude to gluon gravitational form factors, by adding the first subleading power correction from NRQCD's v^2 expansion. The relativistic corrections are found to be as important as NLO perturbative contributions for J/psi, and including them improves agreement with GlueX data (GlueX 2019, 2023) at low W. The calculation also reveals endpoint divergences in the relativistic correction away from threshold, similar to those found in subleading-power SCET calculations by Beneke (2022) and Luke (2022), which must be resolved in future work to extend the formalism beyond the near-threshold regime.
