# Signatures of chiral dynamics in the Nucleon to Delta transition

> Utilizing the methods of chiral effective field theory we present an analysis of the electromagnetic $NΔ$-transition current in the framework of the non-relativistic "small scale expansion" (SSE) to leading-one-loop order. We discuss the momentum dependence of the magnetic dipole, electric quadrupole and coulomb quadrupole transition form factors up to a momentum transfer of $Q^2<0.3$ GeV$^2$. Particular emphasis is put on the identification of the role of chiral dynamics in this transition. Our analysis indicates that there is indeed non-trivial momentum dependence in the two quadrupole form factors at small $Q^2<0.15$ GeV$^2$ arising from long distance pion physics, leading for example to negative radii in the (real part of the) quadrupole transition form factors. We compare our results with the EMR($Q^2$) and CMR($Q^2$) multipole-ratios from pion-electroproduction experiments and find a remarkable agreement up to four-momentum transfer of $Q^2\approx 0.3$ GeV$^2$. Finally, we discuss the chiral extrapolation of the three transition form factors at $Q^2=0$, identifying rapid changes in the (real part of the) quark-mass dependence of the quadrupole transition moments for pion masses below 200 MeV, which arise again from long distance pion dynamics. Our findings indicate that dipole extrapolation methods currently used in lattice QCD analyses of baryon form factors are not applicable for the chiral extrapolation of $NΔ$ quadrupole transition form factors.

## Key claim

Chiral effective field theory calculations at leading-one-loop order show that the small electric and Coulomb quadrupole transition form factors in the nucleon-to-Delta transition acquire nontrivial momentum dependence at low momentum transfer (Q² < 0.15 GeV²) from long-distance pion dynamics, but the strict O(ε³) calculation fails to describe the Q²-dependence of the quadrupole ratios unless a formally higher-order short-distance radius term is included in the G₂ form factor.

## Method

The electromagnetic NΔ-transition current was calculated to O(ε³) in the non-relativistic "small scale expansion" (SSE) scheme of chiral effective field theory, which treats the pion mass, momentum transfer, and the Delta-nucleon mass splitting as small scales of the same order. The three transition form factors G₁, G₂, and G₃ were obtained from one-loop diagrams and local counterterms, then converted into the multipole basis (magnetic dipole 𝒢_M*, electric quadrupole 𝒢_E*, and Coulomb quadrupole 𝒢_C*) via Jones-Scadron relations. The two unknown low-energy constants A(λ) and B(λ) were fitted to experimental data for |𝒢_M*Ash(Q²)| and the electric multipole ratio EMR at the photon point, with an additional short-distance radius parameter Cₛ introduced in G₂ to improve the Q²-dependence of the quadrupole ratios.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic dipole form factor |𝒢_M*Ash(Q²)| is described up to Q² ≈ 0.2 GeV² in Fit 1 and up to Q² ≈ 0.3 GeV² in Fit 2, compared to experimental data from Stein, Baetzner, Bartel, and Beck.
- The electric multipole ratio EMR at the photon point is fixed to the experimental value EMR = -(2.5 ± 0.1_stat ± 0.2_sys)% from Beck (2000).
- The short-distance radius parameter Cₛ is fitted as -17.0 GeV⁻² in Fit 2, while A(1 GeV) = 10.5 and B(1 GeV) = 15.4 remain unchanged between fits.
- The chiral expansion of G₃(0) shows a leading logarithmic singularity ∼ log m_q near the chiral limit, while G₁(0) and G₂(0) have leading quark-mass dependence linear in m_q.
- Rapid changes in the real part of the quadrupole transition moments occur for pion masses below 200 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier O(ε³) SSE calculation of Gail, Hemmert, Kampf, and Phillips (2004) by updating input parameters and introducing a short-distance radius term in G₂ that was previously omitted. It contradicts the applicability of dipole extrapolation methods used in lattice QCD analyses of baryon form factors for the NΔ quadrupole transitions. The results agree with experimental EMR and CMR data from OOPS (2001), CLAS (2002), and MAMI (2003) up to Q² ≈ 0.3 GeV² when the radius term is included, and enable future O(ε⁴) calculations to separate the individual low-energy constants b₁, b₆, and D₁.
