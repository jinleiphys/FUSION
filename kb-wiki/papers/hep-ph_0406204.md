# Pentaquarks

> Since LEPS collaboration reported the first evidence of $Θ^+$ pentaquark in early 2003, eleven other experimental groups have confirmed this exotic state while many other groups didn't see any signal. If this state is further established by future high statistical experiments, its discovery shall be one of the most important events in hadron physics for the past three decades. This exotic baryon with such a low mass and so narrow a width imposes a big challenge to hadron theorists. Up to now, there have appeared more than two hundred theoretical papers trying to interpret this charming state. I will review some important theoretical developments on pentaquarks based on my biased personal views.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the theoretical landscape surrounding the $\Theta^+$ pentaquark following its reported discovery in 2003, emphasizing that while many experimental groups claimed confirmation, many others saw no signal, and the existence of the state remains not completely established despite being listed as a three-star resonance in the 2004 PDG.

## Method

The author provides a selective review of theoretical approaches to pentaquarks, including the chiral soliton model, Jaffe and Wilczek's diquark model, Karliner and Lipkin's diquark-triquark model, non-clustered quark models, QCD sum rules, and the heptaquark proposal. The review covers group-theoretical classification of pentaquark wave functions under SU(3) flavor and SU(2) spin, and discusses the parity puzzle, mass predictions, and the narrow width problem for each model.

## Key numbers

- $\Theta^+$ mass reported by LEPS: $1.54 \pm 0.01$ GeV with width smaller than 25 MeV and statistical significance of $4.6\sigma$
- $\Xi^{--}_5$ mass reported by NA49: $(1.862 \pm 0.002)$ GeV with width below detector resolution of about 0.018 GeV
- Heavy pentaquark candidate from H1: around 3099 MeV with quark content $udud\bar{c}$
- STAR collaboration peak: $1734 \pm 0.5 \pm 5$ MeV in $\Lambda K_s^0$ invariant mass
- QCD sum rule prediction for $\Theta^+$ mass: $M_0 = (1.56 \pm 0.15)$ GeV
- Chiral soliton model range for $\Theta^+$ mass: 1430 MeV $\le m_{\Theta^+} \le$ 1660 MeV
- Negative-parity octet pentaquark members $p_8, n_8$ lie 22 MeV below the $p\eta_0$ threshold and 228 MeV below the $\Sigma K$ threshold
- $\Lambda (1520)$ width: $15.6 \pm 1.0$ MeV (given for comparison with the narrow $\Theta^+$)

## Context

This review extends the theoretical discussion following the LEPS collaboration's 2003 report of the $\Theta^+$ pentaquark, which motivated over two hundred theoretical papers. The author discusses the chiral soliton model prediction by Diakonov et al. (1997) that partly motivated the experimental search, and contrasts it with Jaffe and Wilczek's diquark model (2003) and Karliner and Lipkin's diquark-triquark model (2003). The review also addresses Cohen's large $N_c$ argument (2003) challenging the chiral soliton model's predictions, and discusses the narrow width puzzle that remains the most challenging theoretical issue. The work enables future experimental searches to test the predicted negative-parity octet pentaquarks and to resolve the parity of the $\Theta^+$ through polarized scattering or photo-production experiments.
