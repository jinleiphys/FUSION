# Dynamical analysis on heavy-ion fusion reactions near Coulomb barrier

> The shell correction is proposed in the improved isospin dependent quantum molecular dynamics (ImIQMD) model, which plays an important role in heavy-ion fusion reactions near Coulomb barrier. By using the ImIQMD model, the static and dynamical fusion barriers, dynamical barrier distribution in the fusion reactions are analyzed systematically. The fusion and capture excitation functions for a series of reaction systems are calculated and compared with experimental data. It is found that the fusion cross sections for neutron-rich systems increase obviously, and the strong shell effects of two colliding nuclei result in a decrease of the fusion cross sections at the sub-barrier energies. The lowering of the dynamical fusion barriers favors the enhancement of the sub-barrier fusion cross sections, which is related to the nucleon transfer and the neck formation in the fusion reactions.

## Key claim

The shell correction incorporated into the improved isospin dependent quantum molecular dynamics (ImIQMD) model reduces fusion cross sections at sub-barrier energies for systems involving rigid, double-magic nuclei (e.g., \(^{48}\)Ca), while neutron-rich systems show increased fusion cross sections due to lower dynamical fusion barriers; the agreement with experimental data is achieved without adjustable parameters, but the model's reliability is limited by the need for longer stability of initial nuclei for very heavy systems.

## Method

The authors extended the ImIQMD model by adding a Woods-Saxon shell correction potential (Eq. 19) derived from the Droplet model's ground-state shell correction energies, and used a switch function to evolve the shell correction from separated projectile and target to the compound nucleus during the collision. Static and dynamical fusion barriers were computed from the total energy of the system as a function of the distance between centers, and fusion/capture excitation functions were calculated by simulating 200–300 events per energy and impact parameter, with fusion defined as a coalesced one-body density surviving at least one rotation or several radius oscillations.

## Key numbers

- Shell correction energies for single nuclei: \(-5.48\) MeV for \(^{48}\)Ca and \(-8.93\) MeV for \(^{208}\)Pb, matching the empirical Droplet model values.
- Static fusion barrier for \(^{36}\)S+\(^{90}\)Zr: \(V_b = 84.37\) MeV; dynamical barrier distributions at 80 MeV and 85 MeV shift to lower energies.
- Fusion cross sections for \(^{48}\)Ca+\(^{90}\)Zr are reduced after including shell correction, especially at sub-barrier energies, reproducing experimental data from St06.
- For \(^{46}\)Ti+\(^{46}\)Ti and \(^{40}\)Ca+\(^{112}\)Sn, the ImIQMD model matches experimental excitation functions within statistical error bars, while the one-dimensional Hill-Wheeler formula underestimates sub-barrier cross sections.
- Capture cross sections for \(^{48}\)Ca+\(^{244}\)Pu and \(^{48}\)Ca+\(^{248}\)Cm are calculated and compared with experimental data from It04; capture occurs in the evolution range 600–800 fm/c.

## Context

This work extends the ImIQMD model (previously developed by Wang et al. (2004) and Feng et al. (2005)) by incorporating shell corrections, addressing a limitation of earlier quantum molecular dynamics approaches that could not reproduce sub-barrier fusion suppression for magic nuclei. It contrasts with coupled-channel methods (e.g., Rowley et al. (1991)) by providing a fully dynamical, microscopic description without adjustable parameters, and it enables future studies of halo-nucleus reactions, quasi-fission, and superheavy element synthesis (e.g., reactions used by Oganessian et al. (2000) for Z=114 and Z=116).
