# Strong Coupling BCS Superconductivity and Holography

> We attempt to give a holographic description of the microscopic theory of a BCS superconductor. Exploiting the analogy with chiral symmetry breaking in QCD we use the Sakai-Sugimoto model of two D8 branes in a D4 brane background with finite baryon number. In this case there is a new tachyonic instability which is plausibly the bulk analog of the Cooper pairing instability. We analyze the Yang-Mills approximation to the non-Abelian Born-Infeld action. We give some exact solutions of the non-linear Yang-Mills equations in flat space and also give a stability analysis, showing that the instability disappears in the presence of an electric field. The holograhic picture also suggests a dependence of $T_c$ on the number density which is different from the usual (weak coupling) BCS. The flat space solutions are then generalized to curved space numerically and also, in an approximate way, analytically. This configuration should then correspond to the ground state of the boundary superconducting (superfluid) ground state. We also give some preliminary results on Green functions computations in the Sakai - Sugimoto model without any chemical potential

## Key claim

The authors propose that the Sakai-Sugimoto model, when modified to include a finite baryon number density and two D8 branes, provides a holographic description of strong-coupling BCS superconductivity; the tachyonic instability that appears when the branes intersect at an angle is identified as the bulk analog of the Cooper pairing instability, but the authors note this identification assumes there are no other unexpected boundary instabilities.

## Method

The authors use the Sakai-Sugimoto model of two D8 branes in a D4 brane background with finite baryon number, exploiting the analogy between chiral symmetry breaking in QCD and the Cooper pairing instability. They analyze the Yang-Mills approximation to the non-Abelian Dirac-Born-Infeld action, giving exact solutions of the non-linear Yang-Mills equations in flat space and performing a stability analysis showing the instability disappears in the presence of an electric field. The flat space solutions are then generalized to curved space numerically and, in an approximate way, analytically.

## Key numbers

* The critical coupling for chiral symmetry breaking in the NJL model is given as \(g_{cr} \approx {2 \pi^2 \over \Lambda^2}\).
* For weak coupling BCS, the gap is \(\Delta \approx \epsilon_c e^{ - {1 \over g \; {d n \over d \epsilon}}}\) when \(g \; {d n \over d \epsilon} << 1\).
* For strong coupling BCS, the gap is \(\Delta \approx \epsilon_c g \; {d n \over d \epsilon}\) when \(g \; {d n \over d \epsilon} >> 1\).
* The D4 brane action for wrapped branes is \(S_{D4} = {n_B ~\tilde T \over 3} u_c\).
* The Chern-Simons action gives \(S_{CS} = n_B \tilde T a_0(u_c)\).

## Context

This work extends the holographic approach to condensed matter systems by applying the Sakai-Sugimoto model (Sakai and Sugimoto, 2005) to BCS superconductivity, building on earlier studies of baryon number in this model by Bergman et al. (2006) and Chuang et al. (2009). The analogy between Cooper pairing and chiral symmetry breaking follows the classic work of Nambu and Jona-Lasinio (1961). The paper enables future calculations of the critical temperature dependence on number density in the strong-coupling regime, which differs from the exponential weak-coupling BCS result, and provides a framework for computing Green functions in the Sakai-Sugimoto model.
