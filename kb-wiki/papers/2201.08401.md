# Factorization connecting continuum and lattice TMDs

> Transverse-momentum-dependent parton distribution functions (TMDs) can be studied from first principles by a perturbative matching onto lattice-calculable quantities: so-called lattice TMDs, which are a class of equal-time correlators that includes quasi-TMDs and TMDs in the Lorentz-invariant approach. We introduce a general correlator that includes as special cases these two Lattice TMDs and continuum TMDs, like the Collins scheme. Then, to facilitate the derivation of a factorization relation between lattice and continuum TMDs, we construct a new scheme, the Large Rapidity (LR) scheme, intermediate between the Collins and quasi-TMDs. The LR and Collins schemes differ only by an order of limits, and can be matched onto one another by a multiplicative kernel. We show that this same matching also holds between quasi and Collins TMDs, which enables us to prove a factorization relation between these quantities to all orders in $α_s$. Our results imply that there is no mixing between various quark flavors or gluons when matching Collins and quasi TMDs, making the lattice calculation of individual flavors and gluon TMDs easier than anticipated. We cross-check these results explicitly at one loop and discuss implications for other physical-to-lattice scheme factorizations.

## Key claim

The paper proves a factorization relation between lattice-calculable quasi-TMDs and physical Collins-scheme TMDs to all orders in $\alpha_s$ for both quarks and gluons, with the key condition that the matching is flavor-diagonal and spin-independent, meaning there is no mixing between different quark flavors or between quarks and gluons.

## Method

The authors introduce a unified notational framework for TMDs based on a generic correlator with staple-shaped Wilson lines, then construct an intermediate "Large Rapidity (LR) scheme" that bridges the Collins scheme and quasi-TMDs. Using Lorentz invariance, they show equivalence between quasi-TMDs and LR-scheme TMDs up to power corrections, and derive a multiplicative perturbative matching kernel between the LR and Collins schemes, which together yield the factorization relation. The proof is cross-checked at one-loop order.

## Key numbers

* The factorization relation holds up to power corrections of order $\mathcal{O}[1/(x\tilde P^z b_T)^2, \Lambda_{\rm QCD}^2/(x\tilde P^z)^2]$, where $x\tilde P^z$ is the parton momentum and $b_T$ the transverse separation.
* The matching coefficient $C_i$ takes different values for quarks ($C_i = C_q$) and gluons ($C_i = C_g$), but is independent of quark flavor and spin.
* The soft function ratio $\sqrt{\tilde S^R_{\rm naive}(b_T, \mu) / S_C^R(b_T, \mu, 2y_n, 2y_B)}$ relates the naive quasi-TMD to the proper quasi-TMD, with $R=q$ for fundamental and $R=g$ for adjoint color representations.

## Context

This work extends previous one-loop matching results for non-singlet quark quasi-TMDs (Ebert 2018, Ji 2019) to all orders in $\alpha_s$ and to gluon TMDs, while also proving the absence of flavor mixing that had been observed in longitudinal quasi-PDF factorizations (Zhang 2018). The proof enables lattice QCD calculations of individual quark flavors and gluon TMDs without mixing complications, and provides a framework for deriving factorization relations between other lattice TMD schemes (such as the MHENS scheme) and continuum TMDs.
