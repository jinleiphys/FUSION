# Cascade particles, nuclear evaporation, and residual nuclei in high energy hadron-nucleus interactions

> Based on a Monte Carlo realization of the Dual Parton Model we study the production of target associated particles and of nuclear fragments in high energy hadron-nucleus interactions. A formation zone intranuclear cascade of low energy secondaries inside the target nucleus is discussed. We calculate excitation energies of residual nuclei left after the intranuclear cascade process and treat their further disintegration by introducing models for the evaporation of protons, neutrons, and light fragments, high energy fission, and by applying a Fermi Break-up model to light nuclear fragments. The results are compared to data on target associated particle production. We furthermore calculate cross sections for the production of nuclear fragments.

## Key claim

Based on a Monte Carlo realization of the Dual Parton Model, the authors study target-associated particle production and nuclear fragmentation in high-energy hadron-nucleus interactions, but the model's applicability to energies below 10-20 GeV is uncertain due to discrepancies with experimental data in the threshold region.

## Method

The authors use the DTUNUC and DPMJET-II Monte Carlo implementations of the Dual Parton Model, which include a formation zone intranuclear cascade to calculate excitation energies of residual nuclei after primary and secondary interactions. They then treat the further disintegration of these excited residual nuclei using a Weisskopf evaporation model for protons, neutrons, and light fragments, a high-energy fission model following Atchison's prescriptions, and a Fermi Break-up model for light nuclear fragments with mass number A ≤ 17. The results are compared to experimental data on grey and black particle production in emulsion experiments.

## Key numbers

- Average excitation energies of residual target nuclei are almost independent of projectile momentum at high energies, consistent with limiting fragmentation (Benecke 1969)
- The formation zone parameter τ₀ is fixed to 2 fm/c based on comparisons to experimental data
- Fermi momenta are modified by a correction factor α_mod^F = 0.75
- Nuclear radius parameter r₀ = 1.29 fm
- The level density parameter a uses a₀ = 0.142 MeV⁻¹ for undeformed nuclei and 0.12 MeV⁻¹ for deformed nuclei (Gilbert 1965)
- The level density at the saddle point uses a_F ≈ 1.08 a, with a smooth A dependence
- Fermi Break-up is activated for compound nuclei with mass number A ≤ 17
- Average multiplicities of grey, black, and heavy particles in proton-emulsion interactions are calculated for various projectile momenta, showing increasing multiplicity up to about 40 GeV/c and constant behavior at higher energies

## Context

This work extends the Dual Parton Model (Capella 1994) and its Monte Carlo implementations (Moehring 1991, Ranft 1994) by adding nuclear evaporation, fission, and Fermi Break-up models to describe the disintegration of residual nuclei after high-energy hadron-nucleus collisions. Previous intranuclear cascade models (Bertini 1963, 1969) were not applicable in the multi-GeV energy range, and older models introduced nuclear excitation energy only phenomenologically (Ranft 1972). The formation zone concept (Stodolski 1975, Ranft 1989) extends the validity range to higher energies. The model enables calculation of cross sections for nuclear fragment production and will be extended to peripheral high-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions in a forthcoming publication.
