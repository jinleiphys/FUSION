# Effect of the tensor force on the charge-exchange spin-dipole excitations of 208Pb

> The charge-exchange spin-dipole (SD) excitations of 208Pb are studied by using a fully self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock plus Random Phase Approximation (HF+RPA) formalism which includes the tensor interaction. It is found, for the first time, that the tensor correlations have a unique, multipole-dependent effect on the SD excitations, that is, they produce softening of 1- states, but hardening of 0- and 2- states. This paves the way to a clear assessment of the strength of the tensor terms. We compare our results with a recent measurement, showing that our choice of tensor terms improves the agreement with experiment. The robustness of our results is supported by the analytic form of the tensor matrix elements.

## Key claim

The tensor force produces a multipole-dependent effect on spin-dipole (SD) excitations in 208Pb: it softens (lowers the energy of) 1- states but hardens (raises the energy of) 0- and 2- states, and this signature is robust across different Skyrme parameter sets that include tensor terms.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock plus Random Phase Approximation (HF+RPA) calculations in coordinate space, including both the two-body spin-orbit and tensor interactions. They used several Skyrme parameter sets with tensor terms (the TIJ family and SLy5+T_w) and computed the charge-exchange SD strength distributions for the three multipoles 0-, 1-, and 2- separately, both with and without tensor correlations. The results were compared with experimental data from the (p,n) reaction on 208Pb measured by Wakasa and analyzed via Distorted Wave Impulse Approximation (DWIA) and multipole decomposition.

## Key numbers

- For the T43 interaction, the main peak of the 0- strength distribution is shifted upward by about 7.5 MeV due to tensor correlations, while the 1- peak is shifted downward by about 5 MeV.
- For the T43 interaction, the 2- peak at excitation energy approximately 17.7 MeV is moved upward by about 2 MeV when tensor is included, while another peak at approximately 3.9 MeV is shifted downward by about 0.6 MeV.
- For the T43 interaction, the main peak of the total SD strength shifts from 26 MeV to 21 MeV when tensor is included.
- For the SLy5+T_w interaction, the 0- strength peak is shifted upward by about 1.3 MeV by tensor correlations.
- The model-independent sum rule m0(t-)-m0(t+) = (2λ+1)/(4π) (N⟨r²⟩_n - Z⟨r²⟩_p) is fulfilled with 1% accuracy in the numerical calculations.
- For SLy5+T_w, the m1/m0 ratio changes from 29.7 to 29.9 for 0- (ΔE = 0.2 MeV), from 27.2 to 23.0 for 1- (ΔE = -4.2 MeV), and remains 21.3 for 2- (ΔE = 0 MeV).

## Context

This work extends previous self-consistent HF+RPA studies of Gamow-Teller and spin-quadrupole transitions in 90Zr and 208Pb by Bai et al. (2009, 2010) and the non-charge-exchange multipole responses by G. Colo et al. (2010), now focusing on the spin-dipole channel. The results provide a clear observable, the multipole-dependent softening/hardening of SD states, that can constrain the strength of the tensor terms in Skyrme effective interactions, which had remained ambiguous from ground-state properties and single-particle energies alone. The analytic formulas for the tensor matrix elements show that the effect originates from the multipole dependence of the triplet-even and triplet-odd tensor interactions, consistent with finite-range tensor interaction results from S. B. and S. C. (1984).
