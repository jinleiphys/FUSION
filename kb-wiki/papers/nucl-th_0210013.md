# Excitation of S11 resonances in pion scattering and pion photoproduction on the proton

> A self-consistent analysis of pion scattering and pion photoproduction within a coupled channels dynamical model is presented. In the case of pion photoproduction, we obtain background contributions to the imaginary part of the S-wave multipole which differ considerably from the result based on the K-matrix approximation. Within the dynamical model these background contributions become large and negative in the region of the S_{11}(1535) resonance. Due to this fact much larger resonance contributions are required in order to explain the results of the recent multipole analyses. For the first S_{11}(1535) resonance we obtain as a value of the dressed electromagnetic helicity amplitude: A_{1/2}=(72 +/- 2) * 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}. Similar values can be derived from eta photoproduction if one takes the same total width Gamma_R=95 +/- 5 MeV) as in pion scattering and pion photoproduction. The combined analysis yields considerable strength at invariant mass W>1750 MeV, which can be explained by a third and a fourth S_{11} resonance with the masses 1846 +/- 47 and 2113 +/- 70 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a self-consistent coupled-channels dynamical model of pion scattering and pion photoproduction requires much larger resonance contributions to explain the S-wave multipole data than models using the K-matrix approximation, because the dynamical model's background contributions become "large and negative" in the region of the S11(1535) resonance. This leads to a dressed helicity amplitude for the first S11 resonance that differs from values obtained in analyses that neglect off-shell rescattering effects.

## Method

The authors extended a meson-exchange (MEX) model for pion-nucleon scattering to include coupled pi, eta, and 2pi channels with explicit S11 resonances, solving three-dimensional coupled integral equations derived from the Bethe-Salpeter equation using a Cooper-Jennings reduction scheme. For pion photoproduction, they used the DMT (Dubna-Mainz-Taipei) dynamical model, which includes off-shell rescattering via a principal value integral, unlike the K-matrix approximation that neglects it. The resonance parameters were extracted by fitting the t-matrix for pion scattering and eta production in the S11 channel up to 2 GeV, and then the bare electromagnetic vertex parameters were determined from fits to the imaginary part of the proton E0+ multipole and to full data sets including differential cross sections and asymmetries.

## Key numbers

- Dressed helicity amplitude for the first S11(1535) resonance: A1/2(dressed) = (72 +/- 2) x 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}
- Bare helicity amplitude for the first S11(1535) resonance: A1/2(bare) = (116 +/- 3) x 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}
- Total width of the first S11 resonance: Gamma_R = 95 +/- 5 MeV
- Mass of the third S11 resonance: 1846 +/- 47 MeV
- Mass of the fourth S11 resonance: 2113 +/- 70 MeV
- Bare mass of the third S11 resonance from pion scattering fit: M3(0) = 1901 MeV
- Bare mass of the fourth S11 resonance from pion scattering fit: M4(0) = 2160 MeV
- Total width of the second S11 resonance: Gamma_R2 = 195 +/- 14 MeV
- Total width of the third S11 resonance: Gamma_R3 = 265 +/- 31 MeV
- Total width of the fourth S11 resonance: Gamma_R4 = 427 +/- 26 MeV
- Physical mass of the first S11 resonance: 1520 +/- 6 MeV
- Physical mass of the second S11 resonance: 1677 +/- 6 MeV
- Physical mass of the third S11 resonance: 1893 +/- 18 MeV
- Physical mass of the fourth S11 resonance: 2183 +/- 21 MeV

## Context

This work extends the meson-exchange model for pion-nucleon scattering developed by Hung, Yang, and Lee (2001) and the DMT dynamical model for pion photoproduction by Kamalov, Yang, Drechsel, Hanstein, and Tiator (2001) to the S11 channel up to 2 GeV. It contradicts analyses based on the K-matrix approximation (such as the MAID model by Drechsel et al., 1999) which neglect off-shell rescattering and therefore obtain smaller background contributions and different resonance parameters. The results enable improved extraction of resonance properties from combined pion scattering and photoproduction data, and provide evidence for a third and fourth S11 resonance that may correspond to predictions from the Hypercentral Constituent Quark Model by Giannini, Santopinto, and Vassallo (2002).
