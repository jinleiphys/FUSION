# The kinks in charge radii across $N$ = 82 and 126 revisited

> We revisit the studies of the isotopic shift in the charge radii of {\it even-even} isotopes of Sn and Pb nuclei at $N$ = 82, and 126, respectively, within the relativistic mean-field and Relativistic-Hartree-Bogoliubov approach. The shell model is also used to estimate isotopic shift in these nuclei, for the first time, to the best of our knowledge. The ground state single-particle energies ($spe$) are calculated for non-linear NL3 \& NL3$^*$ and density-dependent DD-ME2 parameter sets compared with the experimental data, wherever available. We establish a correlation between the filling of single-particle levels and the isotopic shift in occupation probabilities. The obtained $spe$ from the relativistic mean-field and Relativistic-Hartree-Bogoliubov approaches are in line with those used in the shell model and experimental data for both the Sn and Pb isotopic chains. The shell model calculated isotopic shift agrees with relativistic mean-field and Relativistic-Hartree-Bogoliubov approaches that explain the experimental data quite well.

## Key claim

The paper revisits the isotopic shift in charge radii of even-even Sn and Pb isotopes at N = 82 and 126, respectively, and establishes a correlation between the filling of single-particle levels and the isotopic shift in occupation probabilities; however, the shell model calculations fail to reproduce the isotopic shift for Pb isotopes beyond N = 126, suggesting an extra mechanism is needed beyond the shell model description.

## Method

The authors used relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory with NL3 and NL3* parameter sets and Relativistic-Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) theory with the DD-ME2 parameter set to calculate ground-state charge radii and single-particle energies for even-even Sn and Pb isotopes. They also performed shell model calculations using the Sn100PN and CWG interactions for Sn isotopes and the Kuo-Herling hole and particle interactions (KHHE and KHPE) for Pb isotopes, computing isotopic shifts from neutron occupation probabilities of specific orbitals using the Blomqvist-Molinari oscillator parameter prescription.

## Key numbers

- The isotopic shift for Sn isotopes fits a slope of 0.035 ± 0.015 for mass A ≤ 132 and 0.105 ± 0.018 for A ≥ 132
- The isotopic shift for Pb isotopes fits a slope of 0.047 ± 0.015 for mass A ≤ 208 and 0.116 ± 0.018 for A ≥ 208
- For $^{134}$Sn (N = 84), the partial occupation of the 3p$_{1/2}$ level is 0.236
- The oscillator size parameter b is given by b² = 0.90 A^{1/3} + 0.70 fm²
- In $^{210}$Pb, the 0+ ground state has approximately 66% contribution from the g$_{9/2}$² configuration and approximately 18% from the i$_{11/2}$² configuration
- The calculated 1+ state in $^{210}$Pb is found at 1.688 MeV, arising from the i$_{11/2}$¹ g$_{9/2}$¹ configuration

## Context

This work extends previous studies of charge radii kinks at magic neutron numbers, particularly the theoretical work of Goddard et al. (2013) for Pb isotopes and the experimental analysis of Gorgone et al. (2019) for Sn isotopes. The paper provides the first shell model estimation of isotopic shifts for these isotopic chains, complementing earlier relativistic mean-field and non-relativistic energy density functional approaches such as those using the Fayans functional by Reinhard et al. (2017). The results enable further investigation into the mechanism behind the large measured charge radii beyond N = 126 in Pb, which the shell model cannot reproduce.
