# Relationship between Quark-Meson Coupling Model and Quantum Hadrodynamics

> Using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, we study nuclear matter from the point of view of quark degrees of freedom. Performing a re-definition of the scalar field in matter, we transform QMC to a QHD-type model with a non-linear scalar potential. The potentials obtained from QMC are compared with those of the relativistic mean-field models.

## Key claim

By redefining the scalar field in nuclear matter, the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model can be transformed into a quantum hadrodynamics (QHD)-type model that contains a non-linear scalar potential, establishing a formal connection between quark-level and hadron-level descriptions of nuclear matter.

## Method

The author starts from the QMC model, which describes nuclear matter using quark degrees of freedom confined in bags, and performs a redefinition of the scalar field in the medium. This transformation converts the QMC Lagrangian into a form equivalent to QHD with a non-linear scalar potential, allowing direct comparison of the resulting scalar potentials with those used in relativistic mean-field models.

## Key numbers

- The empirical saturation density of nuclear matter is given as ρ₀ = 0.15 nucleons/fm³, corresponding to a mass density of ε = 2.5 × 10¹⁴ g/cm³.
- The normal nuclear matter energy density is ε₀ = 140 MeV/fm³.
- Binding energies per nucleon at saturation for various relativistic models range from −8.7 MeV (for the ehehfv model) to −16.3 MeV (for several models including G₃₀₀ and KM).
- Incompressibility values K for relativistic models range from 115 MeV (ehehfv) to 376 MeV (HFV).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework connecting quark-level descriptions of nuclear matter (QMC) to hadron-level effective field theories (QHD), following earlier developments in relativistic mean-field models by Serot and Wilets (1986) and the QMC approach. The transformation enables future studies to compare predictions from quark-based models directly with the extensive body of work using QHD-type Lagrangians, potentially constraining the non-linear scalar couplings that appear in phenomenological relativistic models.
