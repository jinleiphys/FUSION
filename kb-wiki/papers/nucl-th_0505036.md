# Anisotropy of flow and the order of phase transition in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> Using a hydrodynamical model we study how the order of phase transition in the equation of state of strongly interacting matter affects single particle spectra, elliptic flow and higher order anisotropies in Au+Au collisions at RHIC (sqrt{s_NN}=200 GeV energy). We find that the single particle spectra are independent of the order of phase transition and that the fourth harmonic v_4(p_T) shows only a weak dependence in the p_T region where hydrodynamics is expected to work. The differential elliptic flow, v_2(p_T), of baryons shows the strongest dependence on equation of state. Surprisingly the closest fit to data was obtained when the equation of state had a strong first order phase transition and a lattice inspired equation of state fits the data as badly as a purely hadronic equation of state.

## Key claim

Using a hydrodynamical model, the differential elliptic flow \(v_2(p_T)\) of baryons shows the strongest dependence on the equation of state, and surprisingly the closest fit to Au+Au collision data at RHIC was obtained with an equation of state having a strong first order phase transition, while a lattice-inspired equation of state fits the data as badly as a purely hadronic equation of state.

## Method

The author used a boost-invariant hydrodynamic code to simulate Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV, employing four different equations of state: a quasiparticle model tuned to lattice QCD results (EoS qp), a bag-model-inspired equation of state with a first order phase transition (EoS Q), a purely hadronic resonance gas (EoS H), and a parametrized crossover equation of state (EoS T). The initial entropy density was taken to scale with a linear combination of participant and binary collision densities (weights 0.75 and 0.25), and the freeze-out energy density was chosen to reproduce the \(p_T\) spectra of pions and net protons in most central collisions.

## Key numbers

- The latent heat of EoS Q (first order phase transition) is 1.15 GeV/fm\(^3\).
- The critical temperature for the phase transition is \(T_c = 170\) MeV for EoS qp and EoS T, and \(T_c = 165\) MeV for EoS Q.
- The freeze-out energy density for EoS qp is \(\varepsilon_{fo} = 0.14\) GeV/fm\(^3\) (average freeze-out temperature \(\langle T_{fo}\rangle = 141\) MeV); for EoS Q and EoS T it is \(\varepsilon_{fo} = 0.08\) GeV/fm\(^3\) (\(\langle T_{fo}\rangle = 130\) MeV); for EoS H it is \(\varepsilon_{fo} = 0.10\) GeV/fm\(^3\) (\(\langle T_{fo}\rangle = 135\) MeV).
- The initial time of the calculation is \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm/\(c\).
- For a test with larger latent heat (2 GeV/fm\(^3\) instead of 1.15 GeV/fm\(^3\)), the fit to antiproton anisotropy was no better than for EoS Q.

## Context

This work extends earlier hydrodynamic studies of elliptic flow at RHIC (Heinz, 2002; Kolb, 2003) by systematically comparing equations of state with different phase transition orders and plasma properties, including a lattice-inspired quasiparticle model from Schneider and Weise (2001). The finding that a strong first order phase transition fits the baryon \(v_2\) data better than a crossover or purely hadronic equation of state challenges the common assumption that the details of the phase transition have little dynamical effect (Blaizot, 1995). This result enables future studies to constrain the equation of state of strongly interacting matter using differential elliptic flow of heavy particles, and motivates more detailed investigations of higher harmonics and initial state fluctuations.
