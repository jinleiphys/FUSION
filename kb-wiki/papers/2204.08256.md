# Ab initio study of resonance states of exotic 10Li nucleus

> The spectrum, partial and total decay widths of the states of the exotic 10Li nucleus are studied in an ab initio approach. The spectrum is calculated using No-Core Shell Model and corresponding extrapolation procedure. The calculations use the well-proven Daejeon16 potential. The NCSM-based approach, which includes a method for constructing the basis of functions of cluster channels and a procedure for matching the cluster form factors obtained within this method with the asymptotic wave functions, is applied to compute the widths of the levels. The results obtained quite adequately reproduce the pattern given by the most advanced experiments. Detailed predictions of the spectral characteristics of 10Li nucleus states are given.

## Key claim
The first ab initio computation of the complete set of spectral characteristics of 10Li up to 4.5 MeV, including decay widths, finds that the spectrum consists of overlapping broad resonances; however, the authors apply a phenomenological shift of -0.50 MeV to the calculated level energies because the computed energy of the lowest 1+ state (0.95 MeV) differs from the experimental value (0.45 MeV), and they note that the 0.44 MeV difference between the 1+ and 2- states does not allow an unambiguous conclusion about parity inversion.

## Method
The authors perform No-Core Shell Model (NCSM) calculations using the Daejeon16 NN-potential with the Bigstick code, limiting the basis to N*max_tot=10 for positive parity and 9 for negative parity states. They apply the five-parameter "Extrapolation A5" method to obtain total binding energies in an infinite basis, then use the Cluster Channel Orthogonalized Functions Method (CCOFM) to project the 10Li wave functions into 9Li + n cluster channels. The resulting cluster form factors are matched with asymptotic channel functions using R-matrix theory to compute partial and total decay widths.

## Key numbers
- The lowest 1+ state has a total decay width of 547 keV, in good agreement with the experimental value from Cavallaro et al. (2014) of 0.68 ± 0.03 MeV
- The 1+ state decay width into the l=1, S=1 channel is 281 keV and into the l=1, S=2 channel is 266 keV
- The 2-1 state has a total width dominated by the l=0, S=2 channel at 1670 keV, with l=2 contributions of 6.7 and 7.0 keV
- The 2+1 state has widths of 542 keV (l=1, S=1) and 681 keV (l=1, S=2), plus a 14.7 eV contribution from l=3, S=2
- The 1-1 state has a width of 2560 keV from the l=0, S=1 channel and 19 keV from l=2, S=2
- The 0+1 state at 2.14 MeV decay energy has a width of 1388 keV from the l=1, S=1 channel
- The 4-1 state has a dominant width of 1410 keV from the l=2, S=2 channel, with other channels contributing less than 1 keV
- The 3+1 state has a width of 1754 keV from the l=1, S=2 channel

## Context
This work extends the CCOFM approach previously applied to 8Be, 7Li, and 7He (Rodkin and Tchuvil'sky, 2020-2021) to the exotic unbound nucleus 10Li. It goes beyond earlier ab initio studies that computed only level energies using NCSM (with SD-Bonn 2000 potential) or Gamow Shell Model, and beyond cluster-model calculations using Generator Coordinate Method or Microscopic Multicluster Model. The results correlate well with the high-statistics experiment of Cavallaro et al. (2014) and with the theoretical analysis of that data by Potel et al. (2015), while providing predictions for resonant excitation of the 1/2- state of 9Li at 2.69 MeV that could be observed in reactions such as d(9Li,pn)9Li.
