# QED Meson Description of the X17 and Other Anomalous Particles

> The X17 particle, the E38 particle, and the anomalous soft photons are anomalous particles because they do not appear to belong to any known Standard Model families. We propose a QED meson description of the anomalous particles as composite systems of a light quark and a light antiquark bound and confined by the compact QED interaction, by combining Polyakov's transverse confinement of opposite electric charges in compact QED in (2+1)D and Schwinger's longitudinal confinement for massless opposite electric charges in QED in (1+1)D. With predicted QED meson masses close to the observed X17 and E38 masses, QED mesons may be good candidates for the description of the anomalous particles.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the X17 particle, the E38 particle, and anomalous soft photons can be described as composite "QED mesons", bound states of a light quark and a light antiquark interacting via compact QED, but only for quark-antiquark pairs whose center-of-mass energy lies between the sum of the quark masses and the pion mass, where QCD interactions cannot dominate.

## Method

The author constructs a model combining Polyakov's transverse confinement of opposite electric charges in compact QED in (2+1)D with Schwinger's longitudinal confinement of massless opposite charges in QED in (1+1)D. The quark-antiquark pair is produced at an eigenenergy matching a QED meson state, with transverse confinement arising from self-interacting transverse gauge photons in compact QED, and longitudinal confinement described by Schwinger's exact solution for massless fermions in (1+1)D QED. The resulting flux-tube configuration is idealized as a longitudinal string, and bosonization methods yield the predicted masses.

## Key numbers

- Predicted masses of isoscalar QED mesons: approximately 13–17 MeV for the \(I(J^\pi)=0(0^-)\) state and 36–38 MeV for the \(I(J^\pi)=1(0^-)\) state, matching the observed X17 and E38 masses respectively.
- Estimated decay width of X17 into two photons: \(\Gamma(\text{X17} \to \gamma\gamma) \sim 0.4\) MeV, using a transverse size \(R_T \sim 0.4\) fm and longitudinal length \(L_z \sim 7.15\) fm.
- Estimated X17 width into \(e^+e^-\): 4.2 eV from \(^4\)He decay and 0.828 eV from \(^8\)Be decay, based on ATOMKI measurements.
- Width of the \(^{12}\)C(\(1^+\) 18.16 MeV) state: \(\Gamma = 240\) keV, compared to the \(^8\)Be\(^*\)(\(1^+\) 18.15 MeV) state width of \(\Gamma = 138\) keV.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier proposals (Wong 2010, 2020) that quark-antiquark pairs interacting via QED could produce new bound states, and it provides a theoretical framework using compact QED confinement mechanisms from Polyakov (1977, 1987) and Schwinger (1962, 1963). The paper contrasts with most theoretical discussions of X17 that treat it as an elementary particle, instead offering a composite interpretation. It suggests that future lattice gauge calculations of compact QED with dynamical quarks could test the model, and that searches for QED mesons could be conducted at RHIC and LHC via virtual diphoton decays.
