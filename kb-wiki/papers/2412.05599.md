# Superfluid fraction in the crystalline crust of a neutron star: role of BCS pairing

> The breaking of translational symmetry in the inner crust of a neutron star leads to the depletion of the neutron superfluid reservoir similarly to cold atomic condensates in optical lattices and in supersolids. This effect is studied in the general framework of the self-consistent time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory, treating the crust as a perfect crystal. The superfluid fraction is derived in the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer approximation for superfluid velocities much smaller than Landau's critical velocity within the linear-response theory. The different assumptions made in previous studies are clarified. Fully three-dimensional band-structure calculations of superfluid neutrons in a body-centered cubic lattice are carried out. Although the formation of Cooper pairs is essential for the occurrence of superfluidity, the superfluid fraction is found to be insensitive to the pairing gap, as in uniform neutron matter. In the intermediate region of the inner crust at the average baryon number density 0.03 fm$^{-3}$, only 8\% of the free neutrons are found to participate to the superflow. Such very low superfluid fraction challenges the classical interpretation of pulsar frequency glitches and calls for more systematic calculations within the full HFB approach.

## Key claim

The superfluid fraction in the inner crust of a neutron star is found to be insensitive to the BCS pairing gap, even though Cooper pairing is essential for superfluidity; this result holds for superfluid velocities much smaller than Landau's critical velocity within the linear-response approximation, and the paper clarifies that previous band-structure calculations that neglected pairing were therefore justified in doing so.

## Method

Fully three-dimensional band-structure calculations of superfluid neutrons in a body-centered cubic lattice were carried out within the self-consistent time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory, treating the crust as a perfect crystal. The superfluid fraction was derived in the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) approximation using linear-response theory, with the pairing gap treated as a constant parameter. The neutron single-particle energies and Bloch wavefunctions were computed from static band-structure calculations, and the superfluid density was obtained by integrating over the first Brillouin zone using Eq. (42), with the chemical potential determined self-consistently from the average neutron density.

## Key numbers

- At the average baryon number density 0.03 fm\(^{-3}\) in the intermediate region of the inner crust, only 8% of the free neutrons participate to the superflow.
- The superfluid fraction \(\rho_{n,s}/\bar\rho_n\) is approximately 6% at the same density when pairing is neglected, as found in earlier band-structure calculations (Chamel 2005).
- The correction to the superfluid fraction induced by the vector potential is small, about 3% for the crustal layer considered, since the effective neutron mass \(m_n^\oplus \lesssim m_n\) at the prevailing neutron densities.

## Context

This work extends the band-structure calculations of Chamel (2005, 2012) by explicitly including BCS pairing in three-dimensional calculations, confirming that the strong suppression of the superfluid fraction (to about 6-8%) is not an artifact of neglecting pairing. It contradicts the hydrodynamical approach of Martin and Urban (2016), which found a much milder reduction (80-90%), and the HFB slab calculations of Watanabe and Pethick (2017), which gave intermediate values (60-70%). The results challenge the classical interpretation of pulsar frequency glitches and call for more systematic calculations within the full HFB approach, as well as further studies of the role of the vector potential and self-consistency.
