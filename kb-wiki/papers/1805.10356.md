# Universal Fluctuations and Coherence Lengths in Chaotic Mesoscopic Systems and Nuclei

> We discuss the phenomenon of universal fluctuations in mesoscopic systems and nuclei. For this purpose we use Random Matrix Theory (RMT). The statistical $S$-matrix is used to obtain the physical observables in the case of Quantum Dots, both the Schrödinger and the Dirac types. To obtain analytical results, we use the Stub model. In all cases we concentrate our attention on the average density of maxima in the fluctuating observables, such as the electronic conductance. The case of neutron capture by a variety of nuclei at thermal energies is also considered. Here the average density of maxima in the cross section vs. the mass number is analysed and traced to astrophysical conditions.

# Universal Fluctuations and Coherence Lengths in Chaotic Mesoscopic Systems and Nuclei

## Key claim

The paper argues that universal fluctuations in chaotic systems, including Schrödinger and Dirac quantum dots as well as thermal neutron capture cross sections, can be characterized by the average density of maxima in the fluctuating observable, which is directly related to the correlation length; however, the specific form of this relation depends on the tunneling probability (openness parameter) and on the type of external parameter variation, with the parallel magnetic field case being notably independent of the openness parameter.

## Method

The authors employ Random Matrix Theory (RMT) and the statistical S-matrix, using the Stub model to obtain analytical expressions for correlation functions and the average density of maxima in fluctuating observables. For quantum dots, they analyze both Schrödinger-type (SQD) and Dirac-type (DQD) systems, deriving correlation functions for energy variation and for several external parameter variations (perpendicular magnetic field, Rashba-Dresselhaus field, and parallel magnetic field). For nuclei, they apply the same formalism to thermal neutron capture cross sections as a function of compound nucleus mass number, extracting the correlation width from empirical data using the Moldauer-Simonius theorem to relate the tunneling probability to the average transmission coefficient.

## Key numbers

- For Schrödinger quantum dots with energy variation: average density of maxima ⟨ρ_E⟩ = 1/(πΓ_E), where Γ_E is the correlation length
- For Schrödinger quantum dots with external parameter variation: ⟨ρ_X⟩ = √6/(πΓ_X)
- For the parallel magnetic field case in Schrödinger quantum dots: ⟨ρ_{H_∥}⟩ = √3/(πΓ_{H_∥}) ≈ 0.55/Γ_{H_∥}, independent of the openness parameter
- For almost closed systems (p << 1), the ratio D = Γ_(corr.,E)/Γ_W = √(3/5) = 0.81, where Γ_W is the Weisskopf estimate
- For thermal neutron capture cross sections, the average density of maxima ⟨ρ_A⟩ = 0.39, yielding a correlation width Γ_A = 1.94 (in mass number units)
- Exceptional cross sections excluded from analysis: n + ¹⁰B (3500 b), n + ¹³⁵Xe (2.5 × 10⁶ b), n + ¹⁵⁷Gd (2.5 × 10⁵ b)

## Context

This work extends the RMT-based analysis of universal fluctuations from mesoscopic systems (quantum dots) to nuclear physics, specifically to thermal neutron capture cross sections as a function of mass number. The authors build on prior work by Beenakker (1997) and Alhassid (2000) on conductance fluctuations in quantum dots, and on the correlation function formalism developed in earlier papers by the same group (RBHL2011, BHR2013, HR2014). The analysis of Dirac quantum dots follows from studies by Baranger, Beenakker, and Richter on graphene-based systems. For the nuclear application, the authors reference the Block-Feshbach doorway state concept (1963) and the Moldauer-Simonius theorem (1969, 1974) to connect the tunneling probability to the average S-matrix. The finding that the remnant coherence in the chaotic capture cross section is restricted to ΔA = 1 and 2 is interpreted as consistent with nucleosynthesis via s- and r-processes.
