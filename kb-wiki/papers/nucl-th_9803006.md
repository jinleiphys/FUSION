# Dependence of lepton pair emission on EoS and initial state

> We present results from a hydrodynamic calculation for thermal emission of lepton pairs in central lead-lead collisions at the CERN SPS energy. Dependence of the emission on the initial conditions and Equation of State (EoS) is considered and the spectra are compared with CERES data and calculated distribution of Drell--Yan pairs.

## Key claim

Thermal lepton pair emission from a hydrodynamically expanding fireball, constrained to reproduce observed hadron spectra, can account for a significant part of the excess over conventional sources reported by CERES and NA50, but only if medium modifications to the rho meson provide an enhancement factor of approximately 8–10 in the low-mass region (M ≈ 500 MeV), and the thermal contribution is sensitive to the equation of state (EoS) at low masses and to initial conditions at high masses.

## Method

The authors performed a hydrodynamic calculation for central lead-lead collisions at CERN SPS energy, using a parametrization of initial conditions based on local nuclear thickness in the transverse plane with energy and baryon number conservation. They employed two different equations of state with transition temperatures Tc = 165 MeV and 200 MeV, and used lowest-order perturbative rates for quark-gluon plasma emission and binary rates from Gale and Lichard for hadron gas emission, with vector meson dominance and quark-hadron duality applied for masses above 1.5 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out temperature favored by hadron spectra fits: Tf ≈ 120 MeV.
- Thermal lepton pair emission in the mass region M ≈ 500 MeV is equal to the background from final hadrons, but the total is below CERES data by a factor of 4–5, implying an enhancement factor of ≈ 8–10 needed if thermal emission is the source of the excess.
- For masses M ≥ 1.5 GeV, thermal emission can be of the same magnitude as Drell–Yan pairs, and the contribution is sensitive to initial conditions (average initial energy density ⟨εi⟩) but less sensitive to the EoS.
- Two EoS transition temperatures studied: Tc = 165 MeV and Tc = 200 MeV.

## Context

This work extends hydrodynamic modeling of heavy-ion collisions at CERN SPS energies (Huovinen, Ruuskanen, Sollfrank, 1998) by incorporating thermal lepton pair emission as an independent constraint on the expansion dynamics, complementing earlier fits to hadron spectra. It contrasts with approaches using simplified hydrodynamic descriptions (Wa) or RQMD simulations without a phase transition (Ko) to fit the CERES excess, and it enables future studies of how medium modifications to the rho meson parameters depend on the equation of state and transition temperature.
