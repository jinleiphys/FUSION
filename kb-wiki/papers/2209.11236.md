# Resolving the Scales of the Quark-Gluon Plasma with Energy Correlators

> Jets provide us with ideal probes of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) produced in heavy-ion collisions, since its dynamics at its different scales is imprinted into the multi-scale substructure of the final state jets. We present a new approach to jet substructure in heavy-ion collisions based on the study of correlation functions of energy flow operators. By analysing the two-point correlator of an in-medium quark jet, we demonstrate that the spectra of correlation functions robustly identify the scales defined by the properties of the QGP, particularly those associated with the onset of colour coherence.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the two-point energy correlator (EEC) computed on an in-medium quark jet can robustly identify the angular scales associated with the quark-gluon plasma (QGP), particularly the onset of colour coherence, but only within the specific model of a static medium of finite length treated with the BDMPS-Z formalism and harmonic oscillator approximation.

## Method

The authors compute the two-point energy-energy correlator (EEC) for a quark jet propagating through a static brick of QGP of length L and jet-quenching parameter hat q, using the BDMPS-Z formalism with the harmonic oscillator approximation at leading colour for a semi-hard splitting. They factorize the in-medium splitting cross section as the vacuum cross section multiplied by a medium modification factor F_med, and numerically evaluate the EEC for 242 parameter sets with jet energy E in [50,700] GeV, L in [0.2,10] fm, and hat q in [1,3] GeV^2 fm^{-1}. The results are compared with simulations from the JEWEL event generator for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV.

## Key numbers

- The onset angle theta_on scales as theta_on ~ theta_L^(1 +/- 0.1), where theta_L ~ (E L)^{-1/2}, robustly across both decoherent (DC) and partially coherent (PC) regimes for both n=1 and n=2 correlators.
- In the DC limit, the peak position scales as theta_peak^DC ~ E^{-0.86 +/- 0.1} L^{0.21 +/- 0.1} hat q^{0.36 +/- 0.1} for both n=1 and n=2.
- In the PC limit, the peak position scales as theta_peak^PC ~ E^{-0.54 +/- 0.1} L^{-0.31 +/- 0.1} hat q^{0.09 +/- 0.1} for both n=1 and n=2.
- The critical energy for the transition between DC and PC regimes is E_c ~ hat q L^2, which for hat q = 1.5 GeV^2 fm^{-1} and L = 5 fm gives E_c ~ 187.5 GeV.
- JEWEL simulations use anti-k_T R=0.4 jets recoiling off photons in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV with medium temperatures T = 0.34 GeV and T = 2.04 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the application of energy correlators, previously developed for vacuum jets in proton-proton collisions (Komiske 2022, Chen 2022, Holguin 2022), to heavy-ion collisions for the first time. It contrasts with traditional jet substructure approaches based on grooming and algorithmic de-clustering (Larkoski 2015, Larkoski 2017), which have struggled to introduce robust angular variables in heavy-ion environments due to background misidentification (Mulligan 2020). The approach enables future experimental measurements of the EEC at RHIC and LHC, and opens the door to studying higher-point correlators, heavy quark correlators, and medium modifications to the hadronisation transition.
