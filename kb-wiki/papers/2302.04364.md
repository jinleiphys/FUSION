# Exotic Baryons in Hot Neutron Stars

> We study the nuclear isentropic equation of state for a stellar matter composed of nucleons, hyperons, and $Δ$-resonances. We investigate different snapshots of the evolution of a neutron star, from its birth as a lepton-rich protoneutron star in the aftermath of a supernova explosion to a lepton-poor regime when the star starts cooling to a catalyzed configuration. We use a relativistic model within the mean-field approximation to describe the hot stellar matter and adopt density-dependent couplings adjusted by the DDME2 parameterization. We use baryon-meson couplings for the spin-$1/2$ baryonic octet and spin-$3/2$ decuplet determined in a unified manner relying on $\text{SU}(6)$ and $\text{SU}(3)$ symmetry arguments. We observe that $Λ$ is the dominant exotic particle in the star at different entropies for both neutrino-free and neutrino-trapped stellar matter. For a fixed entropy, the inclusion of new particles (hyperons and/or delta resonances) in the stellar matter decreases the temperature. Also, an increase in entropy per baryon ($1\;\text{to}\; 2$) with decreasing lepton number density ($0.4\;\text{to}\; 0.2$) leads to an increase in stellar radii and a decrease in its mass due to neutrino diffusion. In the neutrino transparent matter, the radii decrease from entropy per baryon $2$ to $T\,=\,0$ without a significant change in stellar mass.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a relativistic mean-field model with density-dependent couplings (DDME2) and baryon-meson couplings determined from SU(6) and SU(3) symmetry can describe the isentropic evolution of a protoneutron star from birth to cold catalyzed configuration, and that this model remains compatible with the 2 solar mass observational constraint even when hyperons and Delta-resonances are included.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic mean-field model with density-dependent couplings (DDME2 parameterization) to describe hot stellar matter composed of nucleons, the spin-1/2 baryon octet (hyperons), and the spin-3/2 decuplet (Delta-resonances). Baryon-meson couplings were determined in a unified manner using SU(6) and SU(3) symmetry arguments, with scalar couplings fixed by hyperon potentials (U_Lambda = -28 MeV, U_Sigma = 30 MeV, U_Xi = -4 MeV, U_Delta ≈ -98 MeV). The authors solved for particle abundances, equation of state, temperature profiles, and mass-radius relations at four evolutionary snapshots: neutrino-trapped with S/n_B = 1 and Y_L,e = 0.4 (birth), neutrino-trapped with S/n_B = 2 and Y_L,e = 0.2 (deleptonization), neutrino-free with S/n_B = 2 (maximally heated), and T = 0 (cold neutron star).

## Key numbers

- Saturation density n_0 = 0.152 fm^{-3} with binding energy B/A = -16.4 MeV, compressibility K_0 = 251.9 MeV, symmetry energy J = 32.3 MeV, and slope L = 51.3 MeV (DDME2 parameterization)
- For the cold (T = 0) NHDelta matter: maximum mass M_max = 2.26 M_sun with radius R = 11.91 km
- For the cold (T = 0) NH matter: maximum mass M_max = 2.26 M_sun with radius R = 11.96 km
- For the cold (T = 0) N matter: maximum mass M_max = 2.48 M_sun with radius R = 12.03 km
- For neutrino-trapped matter at S/n_B = 1, Y_L,e = 0.4 with NHDelta: M_max = 2.32 M_sun, R = 12.41 km
- For neutrino-trapped matter at S/n_B = 2, Y_L,e = 0.2 with NHDelta: M_max = 2.29 M_sun, R = 12.56 km
- For neutrino-free matter at S/n_B = 2 with NHDelta: M_max = 2.24 M_sun, R = 12.41 km
- Hyperon potentials used: U_Lambda = -28 MeV, U_Sigma = 30 MeV, U_Xi = -4 MeV, U_Delta ≈ -98 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of cold neutron stars with hyperons and Delta-resonances (Marquez, Schurhoff, Drago, Li, Raduta, Ribes, Zhu) and finite-temperature protoneutron star studies (Sedrakian, Malfatti) by using a unified coupling scheme from Lopes (2021) based on group theory. The model addresses the "hyperon puzzle" by showing that the DDME2 parameterization with SU(3)-determined couplings can produce maximum masses above 2 M_sun, consistent with the PSR J0740+6620 measurement by NICER (Cromartie 2019, Fonseca 2021, Riley 2021). The results enable more realistic simulations of protoneutron star evolution and binary neutron star mergers by providing isentropic equations of state across different evolutionary stages.
