# Elastic pion-nucleon scattering in chiral perturbation theory: A fresh look

> Elastic pion-nucleon scattering is analyzed in the framework of chiral perturbation theory up to fourth order within the heavy-baryon expansion and a covariant approach based on an extended on-mass-shell renormalization scheme. We discuss in detail the renormalization of the various low-energy constants and provide explicit expressions for the relevant $β$-functions and the finite subtractions of the power-counting breaking terms within the covariant formulation. To estimate the theoretical uncertainty from the truncation of the chiral expansion, we employ an approach which has been successfully applied in the most recent analysis of the nuclear forces. This allows us to reliably extract the relevant low-energy constants from the available scattering data at low energy. The obtained results provide a clear evidence that the breakdown scale of the chiral expansion for this reaction is related to the $Δ$-resonance. The explicit inclusion of the leading contributions of the $Δ$-isobar is demonstrated to substantially increase the range of applicability of the effective field theory. The resulting predictions for the phase shifts are in an excellent agreement with the ones from the recent Roy-Steiner-equation analysis of pion-nucleon scattering.

## Key claim
The breakdown scale of the chiral expansion for elastic pion-nucleon scattering is related to the Δ-resonance, and explicitly including the leading Δ-isobar contributions substantially increases the range of applicability of the effective field theory. The authors hedge that the theoretical uncertainty from truncation is estimated using an approach previously applied to nuclear forces, and that the resulting phase shifts agree with those from a recent Roy-Steiner-equation analysis.

## Method
The authors analyzed elastic πN scattering in chiral perturbation theory (χPT) up to fourth order (one-loop level) using both the heavy-baryon (HB) expansion and a covariant extended on-mass-shell (EOMS) renormalization scheme. They performed least-squares fits to available πN scattering data (differential cross sections and polarizations) for all three channels simultaneously, employing a theoretical uncertainty estimate based on the truncation of the chiral expansion. The fits were performed for incoming pion kinetic energies up to 100 MeV, and the extracted low-energy constants (LECs) were compared across different chiral orders and counting schemes.

## Key numbers
- Pion mass: M_π = 139.57 MeV; pion decay constant: F_π = 92.2 MeV; nucleon mass: m_N = 938.27 MeV (from Agashe (2014))
- Pion-nucleon coupling constant: g_πNN^2/4π = 13.7(2), leading to g_A = 1.289(1) via the Goldberger-Treiman relation
- Breakdown scale estimate: Λ_b ~ 600 MeV, adopted from Epelbaum (2014)
- Fits performed for T_π < {50, 75, 100, 125, 150} MeV, corresponding to {1035, 1368, 1704, 1854, 2176} data points respectively
- Reduced χ^2 decreases with increasing chiral order for fits with T_π < 100 MeV

## Context
This work extends previous χPT analyses of πN scattering by Bernard (1992), Fettes (1998, 2000), Becher (2001), and Chen (2012), and provides a systematic comparison between HB and covariant formulations at fourth order. The results are compared with the Roy-Steiner-equation analysis of Hoferichter (2015), which provided phase shifts with quantified uncertainties. The extracted LECs are relevant for the two-pion exchange contributions to nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon forces used in the state-of-the-art nuclear potentials of Epelbaum (2014). The explicit inclusion of the Δ(1232) resonance is shown to improve the convergence, enabling more reliable determinations of πN LECs for nuclear chiral EFT.
