# S_eff(E) and the 7Be(p,gamma)8B reaction

> We explore approximations to the effective $S$ factor for the $^7Be(p,γ)^8B$ reaction using different approximation for the integral over the Gamov peak. In the temperature range of interest for solar neutrino production, $S_{eff}(E)$ may be determined to within 0.5% from S(20) with no direct information on the derivatives of S(E).

# S_eff(E) and the 7Be(p,gamma)8B reaction

## Key claim

For the 7Be(p,gamma)8B reaction at solar neutrino production temperatures (1.3–1.6 × 10^7 K), the effective S-factor S_eff(E) can be determined to within 0.5% using only S(20), the S-factor evaluated at 20 keV, with no direct information on the derivatives of S(E). This approximation (Eq. 4 in the paper) is simpler and more reliable than using derivative-based approximations when the derivatives are poorly determined.

## Method

The authors compared four approximations to the exact effective S-factor integral over the Gamow peak for the 7Be(p,gamma)8B reaction. They used a known parameterization of S(E)/S(0) (a Padé-type form valid below 100 keV) with S(0) = 19.0 eVb, and computed S_eff for temperatures from 12 to 17 × 10^6 K. They also examined the model dependence of the first and second derivatives of S at threshold using hard-sphere and Woods-Saxon potential models from Jennings et al. (1998), and compared these to values from Adelberger et al. (1998) and Barker (1983).

## Key numbers

- S_eff values across the temperature range 12–17 × 10^6 K vary by less than 0.1 eVb, from 18.58 to 18.67 eVb
- The approximation using only S(20) (S_eff-S20) deviates from the exact S_eff by no more than 0.5% (0.1 eVb) across all temperatures studied
- The recommended value for the first derivative at threshold is (1/S)(dS/dE)|_{E=0} = -1.87 ± 0.1 MeV^{-1}, averaged over realistic models (excluding the unrealistic rc=4.1 fm hard-sphere model and the Adelberger et al. values)
- The recommended value for the second derivative at threshold is [1/(2S)](d^2S/dE^2)|_{E=0} = 15.7 MeV^{-2}
- Using the derivatives from Adelberger et al. (1998) in the Taylor-expansion approximation introduces an error of 0.3 eVb in S_eff, corresponding to a 1.5% error (30% of the allowed 5% total error in reaction rate)

## Context

This work extends the standard treatment of the 7Be(p,gamma)8B reaction rate for solar neutrino production, which conventionally requires the S-factor and its first two derivatives (Bahcall, 1966; Adelberger et al., 1998). The authors show that the derivative-based approximations are sensitive to the energy range used for fitting, explaining the discrepancies between derivative values reported by Adelberger et al. (1998) and Barker (1983). The key finding, that using S(20) alone is more accurate than using poorly-determined derivatives, enables simpler and more robust determinations of the reaction rate for solar neutrino physics. The authors recommend that future measurements quote S(20) alongside S(0).
