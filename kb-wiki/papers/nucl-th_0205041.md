# New features of collective motion of intrinsic degrees of freedom. Toward a possible way to classify the intrinsic states

> Three exactly solvable Hamiltonians of complex structure are studied in the framework of a semi-classical approach. The quantized trajectories for intrinsic coordinates correspond to energies which may be classified in collective bands. For two of the chosen Hamiltonians the symmetry SU2xSU2 is the appropriate one to classify the eigenvalues in the laboratory frame. Connections of results presented here with the molecular spectrum and Moszkowski model are pointed out. The present approach suggests that the intrinsic states, which in standard formalisms are heading rotational bands, are forming themselves "rotational" bands, the rotations being performed in a fictious boson space.

## Key claim

The paper claims that intrinsic collective degrees of freedom in nuclei, which in standard formalisms head rotational bands, can themselves form "rotational" bands in a fictitious boson space, with the classification of these intrinsic states possible through SU(2) or SU(2)⊗SU(2) symmetries, but this classification is conditional on the specific Hamiltonian structure and only holds exactly for two of the three Hamiltonians studied.

## Method

Three exactly solvable fourth-order quadrupole boson Hamiltonians were treated semi-classically by averaging them on coherent states to obtain classical energy functions, then solving the resulting equations of motion analytically using elliptic functions. The classical trajectories were quantized using a Bohr-Sommerfeld type quantization condition, and the resulting energy levels were classified by angular momentum quantum numbers arising from SU(2) algebras in a fictitious boson space. For the second and third Hamiltonians, the classical energy functions admitted both ℒ² and ℒ₃ as constants of motion, enabling classification by SU(2) irreducible representations.

## Key numbers

- For the first Hamiltonian, two parameter sets were used: Set I with A'=0.01 MeV, A=3 MeV, D=0.4 MeV (spherical/deformed potential); Set II with A'=0.01 MeV, A=3 MeV, D=-0.04 MeV (pocket potential)
- For the pocket potential (case II), the number of bound states varies from 18 for L=0 to 0 for L≥46
- For the second Hamiltonian, quantized energies are given by E_{LM}=2A√[L(L+1)]+4BL(L+1)+(C/2)M² and alternatively E_{n,M}=A(n+1)+B(n+1)²+(C/2)M²
- For the third Hamiltonian (quadrupole-quadrupole interaction), the quantized intrinsic energy is E_{LM}=2Ω√[L(L+1)]-(48/7)F[L(L+1)-M²]

## Context

This work extends the semi-classical treatment of collective nuclear motion beyond the liquid drop model of Bohr and the interacting boson model of Arima and Iachello, by showing that intrinsic degrees of freedom can themselves be organized into rotational bands classified by SU(2) symmetry. The results connect to the Moszkowski model (Moszkowski) for the interplay of individual and collective degrees of freedom, and to the work of Erb and Bromley on the ¹²C+¹²C molecular spectrum. The paper enables a new classification scheme for intrinsic nuclear states and suggests experimental tests of whether this fictitious SU(2) symmetry is observed in real nuclei.
