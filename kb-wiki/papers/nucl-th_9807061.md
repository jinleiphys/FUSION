# Benchmark calculations for polarization observables in 3N scattering

> High precision benchmark calculations for phase-shifts and mixing parameters as well as observables in elastic neutron-deuteron scattering below the deuteron breakup threshold are presented using a realistic nucleon-nucleon potential. Two totally different methods, one using a variational principle in configuration space and the other solving the Faddeev equations in momentum space are used and compared to each other. The agreement achieved in phase-shifts and mixing parameters as well as in the polarization observables is excellent. The extreme sensitivity of the vector analyzing power Ay to small changes of the phase shifts and mixing parameters is pointed out.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that two completely different theoretical methods, a variational principle in configuration space and Faddeev equations in momentum space, produce phase-shift and mixing parameters agreeing to within less than 0.1% (with few exceptions up to 0.7%) for elastic neutron-deuteron scattering below breakup, but the vector analyzing power A_y is so sensitive that even 1% changes in the \(^4P_J\) phase-shifts can produce changes of up to nearly 20% in the observable.

## Method

The Pisa group expanded the three-nucleon scattering wave function in a Pair correlated Hyperspherical Harmonic (PHH) basis and used the complex Kohn variational principle to determine the S-matrix in configuration space. The Bochum-Cracow group solved the Faddeev equations for the breakup operator in momentum space and obtained the elastic transition matrix by quadrature. Both groups used the realistic AV14 nucleon-nucleon potential at a fixed incident laboratory energy of 3.0 MeV, just below the deuteron breakup threshold.

## Key numbers

- Phase-shift and mixing parameters from the two methods agree to within less than 0.1%, with a few exceptions up to 0.7%.
- For the Bochum-Cracow method, convergence in phase-shifts was achieved with total two-body angular momentum \(j_{max}=6\); for \(J=1/2\) this corresponds to 50 channels.
- For the Pisa method, convergence required including channels with \(l_\alpha + L_\alpha \le 6\) for positive parity and \(\le 7\) for negative parity; for the \(J^\Pi = 1/2^+\) state this corresponds to \(N_c = 26\) channels.
- A 1% change in the \(^4P_J\) phase-shifts produces changes in A_y of up to nearly 20% (enlargement factor of up to 20).
- The changes in the four dominant parameters (\(^4P_{1/2}\), \(^4P_{3/2}\), \(^4P_{5/2}\), \(\epsilon^{3/2-}\)) from the older results of Hueber (1995) to the present work were: 0.24% (0.20%), 0.42% (0.42%), 0.30% (0.65%), and 0.55% (1.65%) for the Bochum-Cracow (Pisa) cases respectively.
- For observables with magnitude about 0.1 or greater, convergence in total angular momentum \(J\) is reached for \(J \le 11/2\); for observables about 10 times smaller, convergence requires \(J \le 13/2\); for observables another factor of 10 smaller, states up to \(J=25/2\) are needed.

## Context

This work extends the benchmark comparison of Hueber (1995) from phase-shifts and mixing parameters for states with \(J \le 7/2\) to a full set of polarization observables including higher \(J\) states, improving the numerical accuracy by one additional digit. The extreme sensitivity of A_y to the \(^4P_J\) phase-shifts explains why earlier calculations (e.g., Ay1, Ay2, Ay3, Ay4) found theoretical descriptions about 30% off experimental data in the low-energy region, and the paper establishes that both methods are now numerically reliable enough to serve as a basis for future investigations including three-nucleon interaction (TNI) terms.
