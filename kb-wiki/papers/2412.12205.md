# Radiative Capture Reaction $d(α,γ)^{6}\mathrm{Li} $ in Cluster Effective Field Theory

> In this study, we focus on the radiative capture process of the deuteron on alpha particle leading to the formation of $^6{\textrm{Li}}$ in the two-body formalism through the cluster effective field theory~(CEFT). It was the primitive nuclear reaction to produce ${^6 \textrm{Li}}$ in a few minutes after the Big Bang. In detail, we outline the calculation of the dominant $E1$ and $E2$ electromagnetic transition amplitudes of $d(α,γ){^6\textrm{Li}}$. Then, we obtain the astrophysical S-factor by fitting it to the experimental data. Finally, we compare the obtained CEFT results for the astrophysical S-factor with the other theoretical results.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the astrophysical S-factor for the radiative capture reaction \(d(\alpha,\gamma)^{6}\mathrm{Li}\) using cluster effective field theory (CEFT) up to next-to-leading order, but note that the expansion parameter is approximately \(1/5\) and the precision becomes questionable for center-of-mass energies above 3.3 MeV.

## Method

The authors treat the deuteron and alpha particle as point-like clusters and the \(^{6}\mathrm{Li}\) nucleus as a two-body bound state of these clusters with binding energy \(B = 1.47\) MeV. They construct a non-relativistic effective Lagrangian for elastic scattering and radiative capture, including Coulomb interactions non-perturbatively through Coulomb Green's functions. The \(E1\) and \(E2\) transition amplitudes are computed from Feynman diagrams involving one-body and two-body currents, and the astrophysical S-factor is obtained by fitting to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of \(^{6}\mathrm{Li}\): \(B = 1.47\) MeV
- Alpha particle mass: \(m_\alpha = 3727.38\) MeV
- Deuteron mass: \(m_d = 1875.61\) MeV
- Sommerfeld parameter \(\eta_p = k_C/p\) with \(k_C\) the inverse Bohr radius of the \(d-\alpha\) system; the characteristic momentum scale is \(k_C \sim 18\) MeV
- The EFT expansion parameter is estimated as \(1/5\) around \(p \sim k_C \sim 18\) MeV; the prediction is questionable for \(E_{CM} > 3.3\) MeV
- The experimental data cover the region \(100\ \mathrm{keV} \leq E_{CM} \leq 4\ \mathrm{MeV}\)

## Context

This work extends the application of cluster effective field theory (CEFT), previously formulated for shallow resonances such as the p-wave neutron-alpha resonance and s-wave alpha-alpha resonance (Hammer 2017, Ryberg 2016), to the \(d(\alpha,\gamma)^{6}\mathrm{Li}\) radiative capture reaction. The calculation builds on the authors' prior work on elastic \(d-\alpha\) scattering (Nazari 2023) and aims to provide a systematic EFT description of the astrophysical S-factor, complementing earlier theoretical approaches such as the microscopic resonating group method (Typel 1991), the quasi-microscopic potential model (Burkova 1990), and the multicluster dynamic model (Ryzhikh 1995). The results enable comparison with experimental data from direct measurements and Coulomb breakup studies (Kiener 1991, Trezzi 2017).
