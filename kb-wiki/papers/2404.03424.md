# Information entropy for central $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au collisions with the ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics model

> This study investigates the multiplicity information entropy of hadrons, anti-hadrons, baryons, and net-protons in central \(^{197}\)Au+\(^{197}\)Au collisions with impact parameters of 0$-$3 fm, using the ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics model (UrQMD) across various center-of-mass energies (\(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}\)) from 5.0 to 54.4 GeV. Our simulations employ hydrodynamic modes with different equations of state (EoS) and a default mode without hydrodynamics. The results reveal that the information entropies of baryons and net-protons are sensitive to the selected EoS. In particular, enhancements of the information entropies around \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} \sim 30\) GeV, especially with chiral hadron gas and Bag model EoS, indicate a phase transition or critical endpoint behavior. These findings highlight the importance of the EoS in understanding the thermodynamic properties of matter produced in high-energy collisions.

## Key claim

The information entropies of baryons and net-protons in central Au+Au collisions are sensitive to the chosen equation of state (EoS) in hydrodynamic simulations, with enhancements around \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} \sim 30\) GeV for EoS that include a first-order phase transition or critical endpoint, suggesting that information entropy may serve as an alternative observable for the QGP phase transition.

## Method

Central \(^{197}\)Au+\(^{197}\)Au collisions (impact parameter 0–3 fm) were simulated using the ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) model in both a default mode (without hydrodynamics) and hybrid modes that couple a 3D+1D ideal fluid dynamics stage to hadronic transport via the Cooper-Frye particlization. Three equations of state were employed in the hybrid modes: hadron gas (HG) EoS without deconfinement transition, chiral+hadron gas (CH) EoS with a first-order transition and critical endpoint, and bag model (BM) EoS with a strong first-order transition between QGP and hadronic phases. Multiplicity information entropies were computed from the normalized multiplicity probability distributions of hadrons, anti-hadrons, baryons, anti-baryons, net-baryons, and net-protons across center-of-mass energies from 5.0 to 54.4 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Information entropies of baryons for all hydrodynamic EoS cases exhibit valley shapes with a minimum at around \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 20\) GeV.
- An enhancement of baryon and net-baryon information entropy appears around \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 30\) GeV for both the EoS:CH and EoS:BM cases, which include first-order phase transitions.
- The experimental net-proton data (from STAR, Ref. STAR1_2021_netproton) shows an enhancement around \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 20\) GeV relative to the baseline without hydrodynamics, while the UrQMD simulations with EoS:BM and EoS:CH show enhancements at around 30 GeV.
- More than 90% of the total information entropies for all particle species are reached within 0–5 fm/c of the collision time.

## Context

This work extends the concept of multiplicity information entropy, first proposed by Ma (1999) for studying the liquid-gas phase transition in nuclear matter, to relativistic heavy-ion collisions and applies it for the first time to anti-hadrons, net-hadrons, and net-baryons. The results are consistent with a previous study using the AMPT model (PuJie 2023) that also found a valley in baryon information entropy around 20 GeV. The observed enhancement around 30 GeV aligns with recent experimental observations from the \(N_{t}N_{p}/N_{d}^{2}\) ratio (STAR 2023) and intermittency analysis (STAR) which give enhancements around \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 27\) GeV, potentially indicating the critical endpoint. The findings suggest that information entropy could complement traditional probes such as higher-order moments of net-proton distributions (STAR 2021) in searching for the QCD critical point.
