# Chaotic wave functions and exponential convergence of low-lying energy eigenvalues

> We suggest that low-lying eigenvalues of realistic quantum many-body hamiltonians, given, as in the nuclear shell model, by large matrices, can be calculated, instead of the full diagonalization, by the diagonalization of small truncated matrices with the exponential extrapolation of the results. We show numerical data confirming this conjecture. We argue that the exponential convergence in an appropriate basis may be a generic feature of complicated ("chaotic") systems where the wave functions are localized in this basis.

# Chaotic wave functions and exponential convergence of low-lying energy eigenvalues

## Key claim

The authors propose that low-lying eigenvalues of realistic nuclear shell-model Hamiltonians, represented by large matrices, can be obtained by diagonalizing small truncated matrices and then extrapolating the results exponentially, rather than performing full diagonalization. The exponential convergence is argued to be a generic feature of complicated ("chaotic") systems where wave functions are localized in the chosen basis, but the authors acknowledge that a rigorous mathematical theory of this convergence is absent and they limit themselves to qualitative arguments and plausible conjectures.

## Method

The authors truncate shell-model matrices by grouping states into partitions (sets of states belonging to the same particle configuration) and retaining only those partitions whose statistical centroids lie within 3σ of the studied state, where σ is the energy dispersion of a simple configuration computed from off-diagonal matrix elements without diagonalization. They then diagonalize the truncated matrices at several increasing dimensions n and fit the resulting low-lying eigenvalues to an exponential function E(n) = E∞ + A exp(-γ n) to extrapolate to the exact result. The method is tested on the nucleus ⁵¹Sc using the FPD6 interaction, and on random banded matrices, harmonic oscillators, and tight-binding models for comparison.

## Key numbers

- For ⁵¹Sc pf-shell model calculations, the dimensions of 1/2⁻ and 3/2⁻ states are 13016 and 24474, respectively.
- The exponential fit parameters for the shell model example are typically A ≈ 300 keV and γ ≈ 6/N, where N is the full dimension.
- The spreading width Γ is estimated as Γ ≈ 2σ̄, where σ̄ is the mean energy dispersion of simple configurations.
- For banded GOE matrices with width b = 0.293 N, exponential convergence is clearly demonstrated.
- For the Wigner-type banded matrix with equidistant diagonal spacing D and off-diagonal elements within band width b, the critical coupling strength for convergence is gc = 6/π².
- For the tight-binding model of a one-dimensional lattice, the convergence of eigenvalues in the site basis follows ∼ 1/n².
- The exponential convergence region for tridiagonal matrices requires λ² < 1/4, where λn = Vn²/(εn εn-1) in the asymptotic limit.

## Context

This work extends the statistical spectroscopy approach to nuclear shell-model calculations, building on prior studies of chaotic many-body dynamics in atoms (Gribakin, 1993) and nuclei (Zelevinsky, 1996) that demonstrated proximity to the random matrix theory limit while revealing deviations due to mean-field and two-body interaction effects. The truncation method was previously suggested by the same group (Horoi, 1997), and the present paper provides the exponential extrapolation framework that could make full large-scale diagonalization almost redundant for low-lying states. The results enable shell-model practitioners to calculate low-lying eigenvalues with high precision using tractable matrix dimensions, avoiding the dramatic growth of computational cost with valence nucleon number that limits standard diagonalization approaches such as the DUPSM code (Akiva, 1997) or the Monte Carlo shell model method (Koonin, 1997) with its sign problem.
