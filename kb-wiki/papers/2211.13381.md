# Supersolidity of $α$ cluster structure in $^{40}$Ca

> $α$ cluster structure in nuclei has been long understood based on the geometrical configuration picture. By using the spatially localized Brink $α$ cluster model in the generator coordinate method, it is shown that the $α$ cluster structure has the apparently opposing duality of crystallinity and condensation, a property of supersolids. To study the condensation aspects of the $α$ cluster structure a field theoretical superfluid cluster model (SCM) is introduced, in which the order parameter of condensation is incorporated by treating rigorously the Nambu-Goldstone mode due to spontaneous symmetry breaking of the global phase. The $α$ cluster structure of $^{40}$Ca, which has been understood in the crystallinity picture, is studied by the SCM with ten $α$ clusters. It is found that the $α$ cluster structure of $^{40}$Ca is reproduced by the SCM in addition to $^{12}$C reported in a previous paper, which gives support to the duality of the $α$ cluster structure. The emergence of the mysterious $0^+$ state at the lowest excitation energy near the $α$ threshold energy is understood to be a manifestation of the Nambu-Goldstone zero mode, a soft mode, due to the condensation aspect of the duality similar to the Hoyle state in $^{12}$C. The duality of $α$ cluster structure with incompatible crystallinity and coherent wave nature due to condensation is the consequence of the Pauli principle, which causes clustering.

## Key claim
The paper claims that α cluster structure in nuclei possesses a duality of crystallinity and condensation, a property of supersolids, and that this duality is a general consequence of the Pauli principle, not limited to the three-α system of ¹²C but extending to ⁴⁰Ca with ten α clusters. The hedge is that whether a state is actually superfluid depends on the superfluid density ρ_s, which encapsulates the structure and degree of clustering.

## Method
The author uses the Brink α cluster model within the generator coordinate method (GCM) to show that the GCM wave function can be rewritten via Laplace transformation into a nonlocalized cluster model (NCM) form, demonstrating simultaneous crystallinity and condensate nature. A field theoretical superfluid cluster model (SCM) is then introduced, which incorporates an order parameter for condensation by treating the Nambu-Goldstone mode from spontaneous symmetry breaking of the global phase; the SCM solves coupled Gross-Pitaevskii, Bogoliubov-de Gennes, and zero-mode equations to describe the condensate and its excitations.

## Key numbers
- The 0₂⁺ state in ⁴⁰Ca appears at 3.35 MeV excitation energy, which is the lowest excitation energy near the α threshold.
- The α spectroscopic factor S²_α = 0.30 extracted from α transfer reactions shows the ground state has significant α cluster correlation.
- The SCM calculation for ⁴⁰Ca uses a condensation rate of 6%, giving energy levels that reproduce the experimental low-lying states.
- The 0₃⁺ state in ⁴⁰Ca is at 5.21 MeV, and the 0₄⁺ state is at 7.30 MeV, as reported from shell model studies.
- The K=0⁺ band members include the 2⁺ state at 3.90 MeV, 4⁺ at 5.28 MeV, and 6⁺ at 6.93 MeV built on the 0₂⁺ state.

## Context
This work extends the concept of supersolidity in α cluster structure from the three-α system of ¹²C (Ohkubo 2020) to the ten-α system of ⁴⁰Ca, which has been understood in the geometrical crystallinity picture through the α+³⁶Ar cluster model (Ohkubo and Umehara 1988; Sakuda and Ohkubo 1994). The paper connects to shell model studies of the mysterious 0⁺ state (Brown and Green 1966; Gerace and Green 1967) and the collective model viewpoint (Marumori and Suzuki 1968), showing that the condensation aspect explains the emergence of this state as a Nambu-Goldstone zero mode. The SCM approach enables tractable calculations for many-α systems where full GCM calculations are computationally prohibitive, and has been previously applied to ¹²C, ¹⁶O, ²⁰Ne, ⁴⁸Cr, and ⁵²Fe (Katsuragi 2018).
