# Clustering structure of nuclei in deep inelastic processes

> A clustering aspect is explained for the $^9$Be nucleus in charged-lepton deep inelastic scattering. Nuclear modifications of the structure function $F_2$ are studied by the ratio $R_{\rm EMC} = F_2^A /F_2^D$, where $A$ and $D$ are a nucleus and the deuteron, respectively. In a JLab experiment, an unexpectedly large nuclear modification slope $|dR_{\rm EMC}/dx|$ was found for $^9$Be, which could be related to its clustering structure. We investigated a mean conventional part of a nuclear structure function $F_2^A$ by a convolution description with nucleon momentum distributions calculated by antisymmetrized (or fermionic) molecular dynamics (AMD) and also by a simple shell model. We found that clustering effects are small in the conventional part, so that the JLab result could be associated with an internal nucleon modification or a short-range nuclear correlation which is caused by high densities due to cluster formation.

# Clustering structure of nuclei in deep inelastic processes

## Key claim
Clustering effects in the mean conventional part of the nuclear structure function $F_2^A$ for $^9$Be are small, so the unexpectedly large nuclear modification slope $|dR_{\rm EMC}/dx|$ observed at JLab likely arises from internal nucleon modifications or short-range nuclear correlations caused by high local densities due to cluster formation, rather than from the mean-field momentum distribution itself.

## Method
The authors calculated the mean conventional part of the nuclear structure function $F_2^A$ using a convolution description with nucleon momentum distributions obtained from antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) and from a simple harmonic-oscillator shell model. They compared the resulting $F_2$ ratios $R_{\rm EMC} = F_2^A/F_2^D$ for $^9$Be and other light nuclei, and also computed maximum local densities from both models to re-plot the JLab slope data as a function of local density rather than average nuclear density.

## Key numbers
- The JLab experiment reported an unexpectedly large nuclear modification slope $|dR_{\rm EMC}/dx|$ for $^9$Be that is much larger than expected from its average nuclear density, while other nuclei lie on a smooth curve when plotted against scaled average density.
- The AMD calculation for $^9$Be shows a two-$\alpha$ cluster structure with surrounding neutron clouds, producing a radial density distribution shifted toward larger $r$ compared to the shell model.
- The momentum distribution from AMD for $^9$Be is shifted toward high-momentum components relative to the shell model, due to nucleon confinement in dense cluster regions.
- When the JLab slope data are plotted against maximum local density from AMD, all data including $^9$Be lie on a smooth curve; when plotted against shell-model maximum local density, the $^9$Be point lies far from the curve.

## Context
This work extends the study of the EMC effect (nuclear modifications of the structure function $F_2$) to the specific case of $^9$Be, which has a known cluster structure with two $\alpha$ particles. The JLab measurement (2009) found an anomalously large nuclear modification slope for $^9$Be that could not be explained by average nuclear density alone. The authors show that the mean conventional part calculated by convolution with AMD or shell-model momentum distributions cannot account for the anomaly, and instead attribute it to high local densities from cluster formation. This opens the possibility of using deep inelastic scattering to study cluster physics, traditionally investigated only in low-energy nuclear reactions. An approved JLab experiment will measure EMC effects in other light nuclei ($^6$Li, $^7$Li, $^{10}$B, $^{11}$B) to provide further information.
