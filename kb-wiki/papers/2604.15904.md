# Charge radii of Sn isotopes in the relativistic mean field approximation

> The kink observed in the nuclear charge radius of Sn isotopes around neutron number $N = 82$ is investigated within the relativistic mean-field (RMF) framework using the NL3$^*$ parameter set. It is shown that the small components of the Dirac spinors for the neutron single-particle states near the Fermi level play a crucial role in forming the kink through their contribution to the proton central potential. In particular, the significant differences between the radial parts of the small components of spin-orbit partner states make neutrons with $j = l - 1/2$ more efficient in increasing the nuclear charge radius than those with $j = l + 1/2$. However, the effect induced by the small components alone does not fully account for the magnitude of the kink observed in Sn isotopes.

# Charge radii of Sn isotopes in the relativistic mean field approximation

## Key claim

The small components of the Dirac spinors for neutron single-particle states near the Fermi level play a crucial role in forming the kink in Sn charge radii around N=82 through their contribution to the proton central potential, but this effect alone does not fully account for the magnitude of the observed kink.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations using the NL3* parameter set for spherical Sn isotopes, solving the Dirac equation for nucleons and the static meson field equations for sigma, omega, and rho mesons. Pairing correlations were treated with a zero-range pairing interaction (strength V0 = 110 MeV fm^3) using both BCS and particle-number-projected BCS (PBCS) approaches, and a modified density-dependent spin-orbit interaction was also tested.

## Key numbers

- Pairing strength V0 = 110 MeV fm^3 yields an average BCS pairing gap of about 1.5 MeV around the middle of the neutron major shell for Sn isotopes
- For 134Sn, ΔRc(134) = 0.0196 fm (PBCS with standard SO), 0.0220 fm (PBCS with modified SO), compared to experimental value 0.0240 fm
- Kink indicator ξ(132) = 1.53 (PBCS standard SO), 1.80 (PBCS modified SO), compared to experimental value 3.38
- Δ²Rc(132) = 0.0068 fm (PBCS standard SO), 0.0098 fm (PBCS modified SO), compared to experimental value 0.0169 fm
- Nuclear matter saturation density ρB0 = 0.15 fm^-3 for the NL3* functional

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of the kink mechanism in Pb isotopes (Marcos 2024) to the Sn isotopic chain, where the single-particle structure differs significantly. The study addresses the well-known difficulty of both relativistic and nonrelativistic mean-field models in reproducing the Sn kink at N=82, as documented by Nakada (2019) and Naito (2023). The results are compared with relativistic Hartree-Fock calculations using PKO1 and PKO3 parameter sets (Wang 2021, Naito 2023), and with experimental data from Gorges (2019). The analysis enables future work on whether the kink origin within the RMF framework constitutes an intrinsic feature of the relativistic approach.
