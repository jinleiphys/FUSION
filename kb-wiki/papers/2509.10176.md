# Effective Mass in Quantum Hadrodynamics-I and its Impact on the Equation of State of Neutron Matter

> The effective nucleon mass (M^*) plays a central role in Quantum Hadrodynamics-I (QHD-I), linking scalar meson interactions at the microscopic level to the macroscopic properties of dense nuclear matter. In this work, we re-derive the scalar density integral in detail and validate it numerically using Gaussian quadrature. The numerical and analytic results are found to be in excellent agreement, confirming the robustness of both approaches. We then investigate the sensitivity of M^* to different parameter sets, highlighting its strong influence on nuclear saturation, compressibility, and the resulting equation of state (EoS). The analysis shows that variations in meson-nucleon couplings propagate directly into differences in pressure and energy density, affecting the stiffness of the EoS. While QHD-I produces characteristically stiff EoS, the effective mass evaluation provides a transparent framework for connecting microscopic meson dynamics to macroscopic neutron star properties. These findings underline the relevance of M^* as a microscopic-macroscopic bridge and demonstrate the utility of numerical methods for extending relativistic mean-field models in nuclear astrophysics.

## Key claim
The effective nucleon mass \(M^*\) in the QHD-I (Walecka) model serves as a robust microscopic-macroscopic bridge connecting scalar meson dynamics to the equation of state of neutron matter, but the model's intrinsic simplicity yields characteristically stiff equations of state that require extensions for realistic neutron star modeling.

## Method
The authors re-derived the scalar density integral analytically and validated it numerically using 20-point Gaussian quadrature with Legendre polynomials. The self-consistent effective mass equation was solved using the Secant method with a convergence tolerance of \(10^{-3}\) and a maximum of 1000 iterations. The validated numerical framework was then applied to three parameter sets (Walecka, Serot-Walecka, and RHA0) to compute the effective mass, binding energy, and equation of state for pure neutron matter with spin-isospin degeneracy \(\gamma=2\).

## Key numbers
- Free nucleon mass \(M = 939\) MeV and vector meson mass \(m_v = 783\) MeV used for all parameter sets
- Walecka parameter set: scalar meson mass \(m_s = 550.0\) MeV, \(C_s^2 = 266.9\), \(C_v^2 = 195.7\)
- Serot-Walecka parameter set: \(m_s = 550.0\) MeV, \(C_s^2 = 357.4\), \(C_v^2 = 273.8\)
- RHA0 parameter set: \(m_s = 456.0\) MeV, \(C_s^2 = 228.0\), \(C_v^2 = 147.5\)
- Walecka set fitted to binding energy per nucleon of \(-15.75\) MeV at Fermi momentum \(k_F = 1.42\) fm\(^{-1}\)
- Serot-Walecka set shifts saturation point to \(k_F = 1.30\) fm\(^{-1}\)
- Scalar attraction \(g_s \phi_0 \cong 400\) MeV and vector repulsion \(g_v V_0 \cong 330\) MeV
- Equation of state spans approximately \(10^{13.4}\) to \(10^{14.5}\) g/cm\(^3\) in energy density

## Context
This work revisits the foundational QHD-I model developed by Walecka (1974) and extended by Serot and Walecka (1986), providing a detailed numerical validation of the scalar density integral that is often taken for granted. The study demonstrates how parameter fitting to saturation properties propagates into qualitative features of the equation of state, and compares with more advanced parameterizations such as NL3 (Lalazissis, 1997), PK1 (Long, 2004), and FSUGold (Piekarewicz, 2004) that include nonlinear scalar interactions and additional mesons. The numerical framework established here is designed to be extendable to more sophisticated relativistic mean-field models incorporating \(\rho\) mesons, nonlinear couplings, finite-temperature effects, and \(\beta\)-equilibrated matter for realistic neutron star modeling.
