# Off-shell effects in heavy particle production

> Off-shell propagation of nucleons is neglected in one-body transport models of heavy-ion collisions, but it could be significant in processes that are limited by phase space, such as the threshold production of heavy particles. We estimate the relative magnitude of off-shell production to on-shell production of the N$^*$(1535) resonance in heavy ion collisions. In the region where the on-shell production is dominated by a~two-step mechanism with an intermediate $Δ$, we find that the contribution of off-shell scattering between projectile and target nucleons is indeed small. Beyond the latter contribution, however, correlations in the initial wave function produce off-shell contributions which can exceed those of the on-shell $Δ$ mechanism.

# Off-shell effects in heavy particle production

## Key claim
Off-shell propagation of nucleons is neglected in one-body transport models like BUU, but initial-state correlations between nucleons in the same nucleus produce off-shell contributions to N*(1535) production that can exceed those of the on-shell delta mechanism, even though direct off-shell scattering between projectile and target nucleons is small.

## Method
The authors derive analytic expressions for the production rate of the N*(1535) resonance in heavy-ion collisions, comparing the on-shell two-step mechanism (with an intermediate delta resonance) to off-shell mechanisms. They evaluate the ratio of off-shell to on-shell production rates using a simplified kinematic model with a beam momentum of 1.9 GeV/c, assuming constant matrix elements and neglecting Fermi motion, and consider two cases: projectile-target nucleon scattering and same-nucleus (correlation) scattering.

## Key numbers
- Beam momentum chosen: 1.9 GeV/c, which is below the two-body threshold at 2.1 GeV/c but above three-body thresholds
- Delta mass: m_delta = 1.23 GeV; delta width: Gamma_delta = 0.12 GeV
- For the on-shell delta mechanism, the delta momentum in the N-Delta cm system is k_delta = 0.5 GeV/c, with solid angle integration extending only over Omega_delta ~ 0.2 x 4pi
- For the off-shell mechanism, the k1 integration range is kmax ~ 0.4 GeV/c in the 3-body cm frame, with Delta E ~ -0.5 GeV
- Ratio of off-shell to on-shell delta production for projectile-target scattering: W_off/W_delta ~ 15%
- For initial-state correlations (both particles from same nucleus), the phase-space average Delta E ~ -0.1 GeV, giving ratio W_corr/W_delta ~ 3

## Context
This work extends the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport theory (Bertsch and Das Gupta, 1988) by examining its assumption that off-shell propagation is negligible. Previous BUU-based studies (Batko et al., 1991; Wolf et al., 1993; Li et al., 1992; Hartnack et al., 1994) had claimed that near-threshold heavy-particle production is mediated by intermediate delta resonances. The finding that initial-state correlations dominate threshold production is consistent with the approach of Danielewicz (1990), which included two- and more-particle correlations. The results suggest that such off-shell effects could be treated as effective three-body interactions in transport calculations, as described by Batko, Randrup, and Vetter (1992).
