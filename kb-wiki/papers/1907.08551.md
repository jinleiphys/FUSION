# Parton distributions from nonlocal chiral SU(3) effective theory: Flavor asymmetries

> Using recently derived results for one-loop hadronic splitting functions from a nonlocal implementation of chiral effective theory, we study the contributions from pseudoscalar meson loops to flavor asymmetries in the proton. Constraining the parameters of the regulating functions by inclusive production of $n$, $Δ^{++}$, $Λ$ and $Σ^{*+}$ baryons in $pp$ collisions, we compute the shape of the light antiquark asymmetry $\bar{d}-\bar{u}$ in the proton and the strange asymmetry $s-\bar{s}$ in the nucleon sea. With these constraints, the magnitude of the $\bar{d}-\bar{u}$ asymmetry is found to be compatible with that extracted from the Fermilab E866 Drell-Yan measurement, with no indication of a sign change at large values of $x$, and an integrated value in the range $\langle \bar d-\bar u \rangle \approx 0.09-0.17$. The $s-\bar s$ asymmetry is predicted to be positive at $x > 0$, with compensating negative contributions at $x=0$, and an integrated $x$-weighted moment in the range $\langle x (s-\bar s) \rangle \approx (0.9-2.5) \times 10^{-3}$.

## Key claim

Using a nonlocal implementation of chiral effective theory with regulator parameters constrained by inclusive baryon production data in pp collisions, the authors compute the light antiquark asymmetry \(\bar{d}-\bar{u}\) and the strange asymmetry \(s-\bar{s}\) in the nucleon sea, finding that the \(\bar{d}-\bar{u}\) asymmetry is compatible with the Fermilab E866 Drell-Yan measurement with no sign change at large \(x\), and predicting a positive \(s-\bar{s}\) asymmetry at \(x>0\) with compensating negative contributions at \(x=0\).

## Method

The authors apply one-loop hadronic splitting functions from nonlocal chiral SU(3) effective theory, previously derived in Salamu et al. (nonlocal-I), to compute sea quark PDF asymmetries via convolution of splitting functions with intermediate state baryon and meson PDFs. The regulator mass parameters in the splitting functions are constrained by fitting inclusive production cross sections of \(n\), \(\Delta^{++}\), \(\Lambda\), and \(\Sigma^{*+}\) baryons in high-energy \(pp\) collisions. The bare baryon and meson PDFs are derived within the same chiral effective theory framework using SU(3) relations for octet and decuplet baryon moments and meson moments.

## Key numbers

- Integrated light antiquark asymmetry: \(\langle \bar d-\bar u \rangle \approx 0.09-0.17\)
- Integrated \(x\)-weighted strange asymmetry: \(\langle x (s-\bar s) \rangle \approx (0.9-2.5) \times 10^{-3}\)
- The \(n=1\) moment normalization for meson PDFs is fixed by \(a^{(1)} = 2\)
- The \(n=1\) moments for octet baryon coefficients: \(\alpha^{(1)} = 2\), \(\beta^{(1)} = 1\), \(\sigma^{(1)} = 0\)
- The \(n=1\) moments for decuplet baryon coefficients: \(\theta^{(1)} = 3\), \(\rho^{(1)} = 0\)

## Context

This work extends the nonlocal chiral effective theory approach of Salamu et al. (nonlocal-I) to compute sea quark asymmetries for the first time within that framework, building on the earlier prediction of the \(\bar{d}-\bar{u}\) asymmetry by Thomas (1983) based on chiral symmetry breaking. The results are compared with the Fermilab E866 Drell-Yan measurement and with semi-inclusive DIS data, and the predictions for \(s-\bar{s}\) are compared with recent PDF parametrizations; future measurements could further constrain these asymmetries experimentally.
