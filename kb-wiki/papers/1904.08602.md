# Mean-field potential effects in the cumulants of baryons from central Au+Au collision at $E_{lab}$= 1.23 GeV$/$nucleon

> The cumulants of baryon multiplicity distribution in relativistic heavy-ion collisions (HICs) have attracted considerable attention recently. It has been conjectured that they may serve as a promising observable to detect the critical end point in the QCD phase diagram, while the cumulants in HICs at intermediate energies have not been widely studied to date. How to interpret the cumulants data at intermediate energies and compare with the data at relativistic energies is now being actively discussed. Both meam-field potential and clustering are highly important to HICs at intermediate energies. In this talk, we discuss these effects on the cumulant ratios of baryon number distributions in Au+Au collisions at beam energies of 1.23 GeV$/$nucleon which have been currently performed by the HADES Collaboration at GSI. Within the newest version of the ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) model, calculations with different mean field potentials as well as without mean field potential are performed. It is found that the mean field potential enhances fluctuations in the momentum space during the expanding stage, especially in a small rapidity acceptance window. The enhancement of cumulant ratios for free protons is suppressed compared with that for all baryons.

## Key claim
Mean-field potential enhances fluctuations in momentum space during the expanding stage of central Au+Au collisions at 1.23 GeV/nucleon, especially in a small rapidity acceptance window, but the enhancement of cumulant ratios for free protons is suppressed compared with that for all baryons.

## Method
The authors used the ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) model to simulate central Au+Au collisions at beam energy of 1.23 GeV/nucleon with impact parameter b=0 fm, generating over 2,000,000 events for each case. They compared calculations with different mean-field potentials (soft momentum-dependent SM, hard H, hard momentum-dependent HM) and without any mean-field potential (cascade mode), analyzing cumulants up to fourth order for baryon multiplicity distributions in a small rapidity window around mid-rapidity.

## Key numbers
- Beam energy: 1.23 GeV/nucleon for Au+Au collisions
- Impact parameter: b = 0 fm (central collisions)
- Over 2,000,000 events simulated for each case
- Small rapidity cut: -0.1 < y_0 < 0.1 around mid-rapidity
- Total baryon number N = 394 used for binomial distribution baseline
- Incompressibility values: SM with K_0 = 200 MeV, H with K_0 = 300 MeV, HM with K_0 = 380 MeV
- None of the kurtosis times variance (κσ²) values exceed 1

## Context
This work extends the study of baryon cumulants from relativistic energies (where they are used to search for the QCD critical point in the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program) to intermediate energies around 1 GeV/nucleon, where nuclear liquid-gas phase transition may occur instead. The HADES Collaboration at GSI has recently shown preliminary results for proton cumulants at this energy (Caines 2017). The authors build on prior UrQMD studies of collective flow and fragment distributions (Li 2018, Wang 2018) and on their own earlier work (Ye 2018) that examined cumulants as a function of reaction time using both UrQMD and JAM models. The results enable comparison between cumulant measurements at intermediate and relativistic energies, while raising the open question of whether coordinate-space correlations can be fully translated to momentum-space correlations measurable in experiment.
