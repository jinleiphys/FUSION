# Coherent quadrupole-octupole states from a SUSY-QM Hamiltonian hierarchy and shape invariance

> We show that the potential in the radial equation in the model of coherent quadrupole-octupole motion (CQOM) in nuclei generates a sequence of superpotentials and subsequent series of effective potentials which satisfy the shape-invariance condition and correspond to a SUSY-QM hierarchy of Hamiltonians. On this basis we suggest that the original CQOM level scheme possesses a generic supersymmetric structure of the spectrum inherent for the coherent quadrupole-octupole mode. We outline the mechanism in which the real quadrupole-octupole spectra in even-even and odd-even nuclei deviate from the genuine symmetry. By using it we illustrate the possibilities to identify the signs of supersymmetry in the alternating-parity spectra of even-even nuclei and the quasi-parity-doublet levels of odd-mass nuclei described within the CQOM model approach.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the radial potential in the coherent quadrupole-octupole motion (CQOM) model satisfies the shape-invariance condition and generates a SUSY-QM hierarchy of Hamiltonians, but the genuine supersymmetry is necessarily and multilaterally broken by the involvement of additional angular and rotational modes as well as by parity conditions and odd-nucleon effects in real nuclei.

## Method

The authors applied the supersymmetric quantum mechanics (SUSY-QM) factorization procedure to the radial Schrodinger equation of the CQOM model, which describes quadrupole-octupole vibrations and rotations in nuclei using a two-dimensional potential in axial quadrupole and octupole deformation variables. They solved the Riccati equation to obtain the superpotential and demonstrated that the effective radial potential satisfies the shape-invariance condition, allowing construction of a hierarchy of partner Hamiltonians. The full CQOM spectrum was then derived through the SUSY-QM formalism, and the resulting level scheme was compared with experimental alternating-parity bands in even-even nuclei and quasi-parity-doublet levels in odd-mass nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The ground-state energy of the radial Hamiltonian is \(E_0(s) = \hbar \omega (1+s)\), where \(s = \sqrt{k^2 + bX(I)}\) and \(\omega\) is the coherent quadrupole-octupole frequency.
- The full CQOM energy spectrum is \(E_{nk}(I) = \hbar \omega [2n + 1 + \sqrt{k^2 + bX(I)}]\), with \(n = 0,1,2,\dots\) and \(k = 1,2,3,\dots\).
- The shape-invariance condition yields \(R(s) = 2\hbar \omega\), independent of the parameter \(s\).
- The superpotential parameters are \(a_1 = \omega \sqrt{B/2}\) and \(a_2 = (\hbar/\sqrt{2B})(s + 1/2)\), where \(B\) is the mass parameter.
- The radial ground-state wave function is \(f_0(\eta, s) = N_0 \exp(-c\eta^2/2) \eta^{(s+1/2)}\) with \(c = \sqrt{BC}/\hbar\) and normalization \(N_0 = \sqrt{2c^{s+1}/\Gamma(s+1)}\).

## Context

This work extends the SUSY-QM approach previously applied to Bohr-like Hamiltonians with deformation-dependent mass by Bugaru et al. (2010, 2011, 2013) to the CQOM model developed by Minkov et al. (2006-2013). The paper shows that the CQOM radial potential belongs to the same class of shape-invariant potentials classified by Dutt et al. (1988), and that the SUSY hierarchy provides a deeper understanding of the symmetry underlying the model. The results enable identification of supersymmetric signatures in experimental quadrupole-octupole spectra of nuclei such as \(^{152}\)Sm, \(^{154}\)Gd, \(^{100}\)Mo, \(^{223}\)Ra, and \(^{237}\)U, while also explaining deviations observed in \(^{154}\)Sm, \(^{156}\)Gd, \(^{158}\)Gd, \(^{236}\)U, \(^{161}\)Dy, and \(^{239}\)Pu as manifestations of dynamical SUSY breaking.
