# On the Color-Singlet States in Many-Quark Model with the su(4)-Algebraic Structure. I --- Color-Symmetric Form ---

> In the modified Bonn quark model in which quark-pairing and particle-hole interactions are included, color-singlet states are examined in addition to the color-neutral quark-triplet state by a method of boson realization. The region which consists of single-quarks, quark pairs and quark triplets is analyzed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the color-singlet condition (exact vanishing of all color-\(su(3)\) generators on a state) is too restrictive for the modified Bonn quark model, and instead proposes a relaxed "color-singlet" definition based on vanishing expectation values of those generators, with the additional requirement that the expectation value of the \(su(3)\) Casimir operator be minimized.

## Method

The authors use a Schwinger boson realization of the \(su(4)\) algebra that underlies the modified Bonn quark model, which includes quark-pairing and particle-hole interactions. They construct energy eigenstates as products of boson operators representing single-quarks (\({\hat q}^i\)), quark-pairs (\({\hat S}^i\)), and quark-triplets (\({\hat B}^*\)), and then examine six possible choices of \(su(3)\) subalgebra within the \(su(4)\) algebra. The color-singlet condition is imposed in the average sense (expectation values) rather than as an operator equation, and the state with minimal \(su(3)\) Casimir expectation value is sought.

## Key numbers

* The total quark number operator eigenvalue is \(N = 3(\Omega - w) + 2(s + 2l + 3r)\), where \(\Omega\) is the degeneracy parameter, and \(w, s, l, r\) are non-negative integers labeling the boson state \(\dket{lsrw}\).
* The \(su(3)\) Casimir operator eigenvalue for the state \(\dket{lsrw}\) is \(Q^2 = \frac{8}{3}(s^2 + sl + l^2) + 4(s + l)\).
* The state \(\dket{lsrw}\) is an exact color-singlet (satisfying the strict operator condition) only when \(l = s = 0\), i.e., states consisting purely of quark-triplets: \(\dket{l=0\ s=0\ rw} = ({\hat B}^*)^{2r}({\hat b}^*)^{2w}\ket{0}\).
* For the minimum weight state \(\ket{m_1}\), the quark numbers in color channels are \(N_1 = n\) and \(N_2 = N_3 = n_0\), with \(n \geq n_0\) and \(n_0 = \Omega - \sigma\), \(n = \Omega + \sigma - 2\sigma_0\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of the modified Bonn quark model (Tsue et al., Refs. (A), (B), (C) in the paper), which had only examined color-neutral quark-triplet states as color-singlets. The present paper introduces a broader definition of color-singlet states based on expectation values, enabling the treatment of states containing single-quarks and quark-pairs alongside quark-triplets. This approach enables future investigation of the coexistence of these three types of quark configurations in many-quark systems, and the parameter regions where each dominates.
