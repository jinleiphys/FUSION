# Analytical Solutions of Landau (1+1)-Dimensional Hydrodynamics

> To help guide our intuition, summarize important features, and point out essential elements, we review the analytical solutions of Landau (1+1)-dimensional hydrodynamics and exhibit the full evolution of the dynamics from the very beginning to subsequent times. Special emphasis is placed on the matching and the interplay between the Khalatnikov solution and the Riemann simple wave solution at the earliest times and in the edge regions at later times. These analytical solutions collected and developed here serve well as a useful guide and cross-check in the development of complicated numerically-intensive relativistic hydrodynamical Monte Carlo simulations.

## Key claim

The paper reviews and completes the analytical solutions of Landau (1+1)-dimensional hydrodynamics by explicitly addressing three gaps: the Khalatnikov solution is not applicable at the earliest times below a certain time coordinate, requiring a Riemann simple wave solution for the earliest history; the inversion of the Khalatnikov solution from space-time coordinates to energy-density-velocity coordinates requires an explicit numerical procedure; and the Khalatnikov solution must be matched to the Riemann simple wave solution at the edge boundaries to form a complete hydrodynamical description.

## Method

The authors present the Khalatnikov solution for the interior region and the Riemann simple wave solution for the edge boundary regions, using the equation of state p = epsilon/3 with speed of sound c_s = 1/sqrt(3). They provide explicit procedures for inverting the Khalatnikov solution from (zeta, y) coordinates to (z, t) coordinates using Newton's method, and describe the matching condition where the energy density logarithm zeta and flow rapidity y satisfy zeta = -c_s y at the transition between the two solution regimes.

## Key numbers

- Speed of sound: c_s = 1/sqrt(3) for the equation of state p = epsilon/3
- The rarefaction wave reaches the spatial origin z = 0 at time t = l/c_s = sqrt(3) * l
- At the matching point for different times t, the energy density ratio epsilon/epsilon_0 = exp{-4 c_s (tanh^{-1} c_s)} ~ 0.22
- The Khalatnikov solution covers a longitudinal region less than the Riemann waves for t less than approximately 5l, but a greater region for t greater than approximately 5l
- For tau/l = 70, epsilon/epsilon_0 decreases by a factor of three as y_s increases from 0 to 3

## Context

This work extends the foundational Landau hydrodynamics framework from Landau (1953) and Belenkij and Landau (1955, 1956, 1965) and the Khalatnikov analytical solution from Khalatnikov (1954). It addresses gaps in the existing literature where previous applications concentrated on the Khalatnikov solution alone without recognizing its inapplicability at earliest times, and without providing explicit inversion procedures or proper matching to Riemann simple wave solutions as described in Landau and Lifshitz (1958). The analytical solutions enable cross-checking and guidance for numerically-intensive relativistic hydrodynamical Monte Carlo simulations such as those by Sen (2014).
