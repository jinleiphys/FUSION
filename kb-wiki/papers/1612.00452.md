# $\boldsymbol{μ^-}$- $\boldsymbol{e^+}$ Conversion from Short-Range Operators

> We present a detailed discussion of the lepton flavour and number violating conversion of bound muons into positrons. This process is a viable alternative to neutrinoless double beta decay and, given that experiments on ordinary $μ^-$- $e^-$ conversion are expected to improve their sensitivities by several orders of magnitude in the coming years, we can also assume the limit on $μ^-$- $e^+$ conversion to improve by roughly the same factor. We discuss how new physics at a high scale can lead to short-range contributions to this conversion process and we present one explicit case in great detail (the single one for which the corresponding nuclear matrix element is presently known). The main goal of our discussion is to make the respective computation accessible to the particle physics community, so that promising models can be investigated while the nuclear physics community can simultaneously advance the computation of nuclear matrix elements. Given the progress to be expected on the experimental side, it may even be possible that lepton number violation in the $eμ$-sector is discovered by $μ^-$- $e^+$ conversion before neutrinoless double beta decay can show its existence in the $ee$-sector.

## Key claim

The paper presents a detailed computation of the lepton flavour and number violating conversion of bound muons into positrons via short-range operators, with the explicit example of the operator coefficient epsilon_3^{LLR,} but the authors note that this process could be discovered before neutrinoless double beta decay only if experimental sensitivities improve by roughly five orders of magnitude as expected for the related muon-to-electron conversion experiments.

## Method

The authors use an effective field theory approach with dimension-9 short-range operators to describe the conversion process, separating nuclear and particle physics contributions. They perform a detailed perturbative calculation for the specific operator epsilon_3^{LLR} using a doubly charged scalar model as an explicit example, integrating out the W-bosons and the scalar at energy scales much larger than the muon mass. The computation incorporates non-relativistic approximations for the bound muon and nucleus, dipole parametrization for nucleon form factors, and a Fermi function to account for the Coulomb potential affecting the outgoing positron.

## Key numbers

* The muon binding energy is approximately epsilon_b ~ (m_mu / m_e) * 13.6 eV * Z, which is much smaller than the muon mass m_mu ~ 100 MeV
* The positron energy is E_e ~ m_mu - epsilon_b - (E_f - E_i) ~ O(100 MeV), with the nuclear energy difference E_f - E_i ~ O(MeV)
* The coherent process constitutes about 40% of all nuclear transitions for this conversion
* The nuclear radius is approximated as R = 1.1 * A^(1/3) fm, where A is the mass number
* The Fermi function for the positron uses gamma_1 = sqrt(1 - (alpha Z)^2) and y = alpha Z E / |p_e|, with alpha ~ 1/137

## Context

This work extends the earlier computation by Domin (2004) for heavy neutrino exchange to a more general effective operator framework, making the calculation accessible to particle physicists and providing tools to compare different new physics models. The paper enables future work where nuclear physicists can compute additional nuclear matrix elements for other short-range operators, while particle physicists can investigate promising models; it builds on the authors' previous letter (Geib, 2016) that motivated the study of muon-to-positron conversion as an alternative to neutrinoless double beta decay.
