# Neutrino flavor evolution in dense environments and the r-process

> In dense environments, standard and non-standard neutrino interactions with the background particles trigger a variety of flavor mechanisms, which can impact r-process nucleosynthetic abundances. Future observations of a(n) (extra)galactic supernova will tell us about properties of neutrinos and of the astrophysical source that produce them. The upcoming measurement of the diffuse supernova neutrino background constitute a unique source of information. We highlight some recent developments.

# Neutrino flavor evolution in dense environments and the r-process

## Key claim
Standard and non-standard neutrino interactions with background particles in dense environments trigger flavor conversion mechanisms (such as the matter-neutrino resonance and the I-resonance) that can impact r-process nucleosynthetic abundances, but self-consistent multidimensional calculations of matter composition and neutrino flavor evolution are still missing.

## Method
The paper reviews theoretical investigations of neutrino flavor evolution in binary neutron star mergers (BNS) and core-collapse supernovae using the mean-field approximation to the neutrino evolution equations. It examines how cancellations between matter and neutrino self-interaction terms in the propagation Hamiltonian can trigger flavor conversion mechanisms like the matter-neutrino resonance (MNR) and the I-resonance, and discusses the role of fast flavor modes arising from angular distribution crossings at the neutrinosphere.

## Key numbers
- Core-collapse supernovae emit 99% of a few 10^53 erg as neutrinos, confirmed by the 24 SN1987A neutrino events detected in Kamiokande, IMB, and Baksan.
- The gravitational binding energy from a future supernova can be determined with 11% precision in Super-Kamiokande and 3% precision in Hyper-Kamiokande using likelihood analysis of neutrino fluences.
- Shock wave effects can change diffuse supernova neutrino background (DSNB) rates by about 10-20% depending on the neutrino mass ordering.
- The combined analysis (90% C.L.) of Super-Kamiokande IV and previous phases shows sensitivity on par with DSNB rates from four models, while more conservative predictions are lower by about a factor of 4.

## Context
This work extends investigations of the Mikheev-Smirnov-Wolfenstein (MSW) mechanism (Wolfenstein, 1977; Mikheev, 1986) to dense environments where neutrino self-interactions dominate, building on prior studies of the matter-neutrino resonance in BNS (Malkus, 2015) and core-collapse supernovae (Stapleford, 2016). The review highlights that future supernova neutrino observations and the upcoming measurement of the diffuse supernova neutrino background with Super-Kamiokande+Gd, JUNO, and Hyper-Kamiokande will provide unique information on neutrino properties, non-standard interactions, and r-process nucleosynthesis sites.
