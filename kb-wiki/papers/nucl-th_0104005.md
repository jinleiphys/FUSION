# Comment on `Two-body random ensembles: from nuclear spectra to random polynomials

> In PRL 85, 3773 (2000) it was suggested to use random polynomials to analyze and understand the properties of two-body random ensembles. In this comment we point out that for the vibron model the random polynomial is not quadratic, but has a more general form. We make a comparison for the percentage of ground states with L=0 in the vibron model with random interactions.

## Key claim

The random-polynomial method proposed by Kusnezov (2000) for analyzing two-body random ensembles in the vibron model is valid only for the special case of purely diagonal interactions; in the general case with off-diagonal terms the appropriate function is not a quadratic polynomial but a more general form that includes a square-root term.

## Method

The authors derived an analytic expression for the lowest eigenvalue of the vibron model Hamiltonian in the vibrational basis to leading order in 1/N, which includes both diagonal terms (quadratic in z and j) and an off-diagonal term proportional to sqrt((1-z)^2(z^2-j^2)). They compared the percentages of ground states with J=0, J=1, and J=N obtained from this function against exact numerical diagonalizations for N=20 with 100,000 runs, and also against the quadratic-polynomial approximation of Kusnezov (2000). A sampling method over the two-dimensional (z,j) space was developed to analyze the general function.

## Key numbers

- For purely diagonal interactions, the quadratic-polynomial method gives ground-state percentages of 64.6% (J=0), 5.2% (J=1), and 19.8% (J=N), in close agreement with exact values of 64.4%, 4.1%, and 24.2% for N=20 and 100,000 runs.
- When both diagonal and off-diagonal terms are present with equal widths (x=0.5), the exact percentages shift to 72.1% (J=0), 0.5% (J=1), and 23.7% (J=N).
- The percentage of J=0 ground states is shown as a function of x = sigma_1/(sigma_0+sigma_1), where sigma_i are the widths of Gaussian distributions for diagonal (i=0) and off-diagonal (i=1) matrix elements.

## Context

This comment extends the work of Kusnezov (2000) on random polynomials for tridiagonal Hamiltonian matrices, which was applied to the vibron model. The authors had previously studied random interactions in the interacting boson model (Bijker and Frank, 2000) and the nuclear shell model (Bijker, Frank, and Pittel, 1999), finding that ground states with J^P=0+ occur in 60-70% of cases. The corrected analytic form explains the enhancement of J=0 ground states when off-diagonal interactions are present, but the method remains difficult to apply to more complex Hamiltonians like the interacting boson model or shell model where no analytic form for the function f is available.
