# Low-energy $η$-nucleon interaction studied with $η$ photoproduction off the deuteron

> We develop a reaction model for $η$ photoproduction off the deuteron ($γd\toηpn$), and study the reaction at a special kinematics, where the photon beam energy is $\sim 0.94$ GeV and the scattered proton is detected at $\sim 0^\circ$, for the purpose of determining the $η$-nucleon scattering length ($a_{ηN}$) and effective range ($r_{ηN}$). In this kinematics, the $η$-nucleon elastic rescattering is significantly enhanced while other background mechanisms being suppressed. We show that a ratio $R$, the $γd\toηpn$ cross section divided by the $γp\toηp$ cross section convoluted with the proton momentum distribution in the deuteron, has a very good resolving power of $a_{ηN}$ and $r_{ηN}$. We conclude that the $R$ data with 5% error, binned in 1 MeV width of the $η$-neutron invariant mass, can determine ${\rm Re}[a_{ηN}]$ (${\rm Re}[r_{ηN}]$) at the precision of $\sim\pm$0.1 fm ($\sim\pm$0.5 fm), significantly narrowing down the previously estimated ranges of the parameters. To arrive at the conclusion, it is essential to use the $γd\toηpn$ reaction model equipped with elementary amplitudes that are well constrained by $πN$ and $γN$ reaction data through a sophisticated coupled-channels analysis. This result strongly motivates the Research Center for Electron Photon Science (ELPH) at Tohoku University to measure $R$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a ratio \(R\) of the \(\gamma d\to\eta pn\) cross section to the \(\gamma p\to\eta p\) cross section convoluted with the deuteron proton momentum distribution, measured at the specific kinematics of \(E_\gamma\sim 0.94\) GeV and \(\theta_p\sim 0^\circ\), can determine the real parts of the \(\eta\)-nucleon scattering length and effective range to precisions of \(\sim\pm 0.1\) fm and \(\sim\pm 0.5\) fm respectively, provided the data have 5% error per 1 MeV bin of the \(\eta\)-neutron invariant mass. This conclusion depends on using a reaction model whose elementary amplitudes are well constrained by \(\pi N\) and \(\gamma N\) data through a sophisticated coupled-channels analysis.

## Method

The authors developed a reaction model for \(\gamma d\to\eta pn\) based on impulse and first-order rescattering mechanisms (including \(\eta\)-exchange, \(\pi\)-exchange, and \(NN\) rescattering). They employed elementary amplitudes for \(\gamma N\to MN\) and \(MN\to M'N\) from a dynamical coupled-channels (DCC) model fitted to \(\sim 27,000\) data points, and used the CD-Bonn potential for the deuteron wave function and \(NN\) scattering. The model was used to calculate the threefold differential cross section and the ratio \(R\) at the ELPH kinematics (\(E_\gamma=0.94\) GeV, \(\theta_p=0^\circ\)), and the sensitivity of \(R\) to variations in \(a_{\eta N}\) and \(r_{\eta N}\) was studied.

## Key numbers

* The DCC model predicts \(\eta N\) scattering parameters: \(a_{\eta N} = 0.75 + 0.26i\) fm and \(r_{\eta N} = -1.6 - 0.6i\) fm.
* At the ELPH kinematics, the \(\eta\)-exchange mechanism changes the impulse result by \(-40\%\) to \(+20\%\).
* The \(\pi\)-exchange mechanism suppresses the cross section by \(\lesssim 9\%\).
* Varying \({\rm Re}[a_{\eta N}]\) over 0.2–1.0 fm changes \(R_{\rm th}\) by \(\sim 25\%\) at the quasi-free peak (\(M_{\eta n}\sim 1.488\) GeV); varying by \(\pm 0.1\) fm from 0.6 fm gives \(\sim 5\%\) change.
* Varying \({\rm Re}[r_{\eta N}]\) over \(-6\) to 0 fm changes \(R_{\rm th}\) by \(\sim 30\%\) at \(M_{\eta n}=1.5\) GeV; varying over \(-3.5\) to \(-2.5\) fm gives \(\sim 5\%\) change.
* The ratio \(R_{\rm th}\) is stable to \(\lesssim 0.1\%\) when the overall magnitude of \(\gamma p\to\eta p\) amplitudes is varied by \(\pm 3\%\), and to \(\lesssim 0.5\%\) at the quasi-free peak when using different deuteron wave functions (CD-Bonn, Nijmegen I, Reid93).

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts by Sibirtsev et al. (2002) and Fix et al. (2004) to extract \(a_{\eta N}\) from \(\gamma d\to\eta pn\) data, which were inconclusive due to insufficient sensitivity and uncontrolled background contributions. The present study uses a different kinematics (higher photon energy and forward proton detection) where \(\eta N\) elastic rescattering is enhanced and other mechanisms are suppressed, and proposes the use of a ratio \(R\) to cancel theoretical uncertainties. The results provide a strong motivation for the planned measurement at the Research Center for Electron Photon Science (ELPH) at Tohoku University, and would enable significantly improved constraints on the existence of \(\eta\)-mesic nuclei and the pole position of the \(N(1535)1/2^-\) resonance.
