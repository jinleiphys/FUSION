# BCS-BEC crossover in a relativistic superfluid and its significance to quark matter

> The character change of a superfluid state due to the variation of the attractive force is investigated in the relativistic framework with a massive fermion. Two crossovers are found. One is a crossover from the usual BCS state to the Bose-Einstein condensation (BEC) of bound fermion pairs. The other is from the BEC to the relativistic Bose-Einstein condensation (RBEC) of nearly massless bound pairs where antiparticles as well as particles dominate the thermodynamics. Possible realization of the BEC and RBEC states in the quark matter is also pointed out.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in a relativistic superfluid with a massive fermion, there are two crossovers as the attractive coupling increases: first from a BCS state to a BEC of bound fermion pairs, and then from that BEC to a "relativistic BEC" (RBEC) where antiparticles as well as particles dominate the thermodynamics. The abstract glosses over the condition that the BEC region shrinks as the fermion mass decreases, and that in the ultrarelativistic limit m→0 the BEC region disappears entirely.

## Method

The authors study a relativistic four-Fermi interaction model with a contact attraction in the J^P=0^+ channel, using a Dirac fermion field with mass m and chemical potential μ. They introduce Hubbard-Stratonovich fields for the pair condensate, integrate out the fermions, and evaluate the effective action in the Gaussian (ring-diagram resummation) approximation. The critical temperature Tc is obtained from the Thouless criterion, and the fermion number density is computed including both mean-field fermion contributions and pair-fluctuation contributions, with bound-state poles extracted to identify boson and antiboson densities.

## Key numbers

- For the characteristic parameter set (m/Λ=0.2, pF/Λ=0.1), the BCS region exists for G/G0 ≲ 0.86, the BEC region for 0.86 ≲ G/G0 ≲ 1.07, and the RBEC region for G/G0 ≳ 1.07, where G0 = π²/Λ².
- In the BEC region, the critical temperature is approximated by the nonrelativistic BEC formula T_BEC^NR = (2π/M_B) [N_B / (2 ζ(3/2))]^(2/3) with boson mass M_B(Tc) = 2μ.
- In the RBEC region, the critical temperature is approximated by the relativistic BEC formula T_BEC^RL = √[3(N_B - N_B̅) / (2 M_B)].
- The dissociation temperature T_diss (where bound bosons disappear) appears from G/G0 ≃ 0.92 and increases monotonically with coupling.

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic BCS-BEC crossover concept (Eagles 1969, Leggett 1980, Nozieres and Schmitt-Rink 1985) to relativistic systems, motivated by possible applications to color superconducting quark matter (Rajagopal 2001, Abuki 2002) and pion superfluids at finite isospin density (Son 2000). The key relativistic effect is that the boson mass M_B = 2μ decreases with increasing coupling, so the critical temperature in the BEC region does not plateau as in nonrelativistic treatments (Melo 1993) but instead rises. The paper enables future studies of the QCD phase diagram, suggesting a band of superfluid phases (BCS-BEC-RBEC) between the hadronic phase and the quark-gluon plasma, and raises the open question of whether qq̅ condensation might wash out the (R)BEC phases.
