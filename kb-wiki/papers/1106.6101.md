# Theoretical support for the $π(1300)$ and the recently claimed $f_0(1790)$ as molecular resonances

> A study of three-pseudoscalar $πK \bar{K}$ and $ππη$ coupled system is made by solving the Faddeev equations within an approach based on unitary chiral dynamics. A resonance with total isospin one and spin-parity $J^π= 0^-$ is found with mass $\sim$ 1400 MeV when the $K \bar{K}$ system gets reorganized as the $f_0(980)$. This resonance is identified with the $π(1300)$ listed by the Particle Data Group. Further, the two-body amplitude which describes the interaction between a $π$ and the $f_0(980)$ is extracted from the study of the $πK\bar K$ and $ππη$ system and is then employed to study the $f_0(980)ππ$ system. As a result, a scalar resonance is found near 1790 MeV which drives the two $ f_0 (980)π$ systems to resonate as the $π(1300)$ while the invariant mass of the two pions falls in the mass region of the scalar $σ(600)$. These findings support the existence of a new $f_0$ resonance near 1790 MeV, as found by the BES and Crystal Barrel collaborations. Our results show that this $f_0(1790)$ is definitely distinct to $f_0(1710)$, the latter of which seems to possess a glueball structure dominantly.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the π(1300) and the recently claimed f0(1790) can be understood as molecular resonances formed from three-pseudoscalar systems, but with the hedge that the π(1300) peak is found at about 1400 MeV (the upper limit of the experimental range 1300±100 MeV) and the f0(1790) is found at 1773 MeV, not exactly 1790 MeV.

## Method
The authors solved coupled-channel Faddeev equations for the πK\bar{K} and ππη systems using two-body t-matrices obtained from unitary chiral dynamics via Bethe-Salpeter equations. They extracted an effective π-f0(980) amplitude from the three-body π(K\bar{K}) results and used it as input to study the f0(980)ππ system as an effective three-body problem. All amplitudes were projected onto S-wave, giving Jπ=0− for the πK\bar{K} system and 0++ for the f0(980)ππ system.

## Key numbers
- π(1300) resonance: mass ~1400 MeV, width ~85 MeV, found in the πK\bar{K} system with total isospin I=1 and the K\bar{K} subsystem in isospin I=0
- The K\bar{K} invariant mass at the π(1300) peak is ~985 MeV, corresponding to the f0(980) resonance
- f0(1790) resonance: mass ~1773 MeV, width ~100 MeV, found in the f0(980)ππ system with total quantum numbers 0++
- The ππ invariant mass at the f0(1790) peak is ~450 MeV, in the region of the σ(600) resonance
- The f0(980) mass used in the extraction is 982 MeV with width 32 MeV

## Context
This work extends the approach previously applied to the K K\bar{K} system (Torres et al., 2010) and other three-hadron systems with a K\bar{K} pair, where the f0(980) configuration provides strong attraction. The results support the experimental claims by the BES and Crystal Barrel collaborations (2006-2008) for a distinct f0(1790) resonance, and contradict the identification of this state with the f0(1710), which is believed to have dominant glueball structure. The study enables future investigations of four-particle systems (particle+particle+resonance) using the extracted effective two-body amplitudes.
