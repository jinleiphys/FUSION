# The Effects of Deformation on Isovector Electromagnetic and Weak Transition Strengths

> The summed strength for transitions from the ground state of $^{12}C$ via the operators $\vec{s}t, \vec{\ell}t, rY't, r[Y's]^λt$ and $r[Y'\ell]^λt$ are calculated using the $ΔN = 0$ rotational model. If we choose the z component of the isospin operator $t_{z}$, the above operators are relevant to electromagnetic transitions; if we choose $t_{+}$ they are relevant to weak transitions such as neutrino capture. In going from the spherical limit to the asymptotic (oblate) limit the strength for the operator $\vec{s} t$ decreases steadily to zero; the strength for the operator $\vec{\ell}τ$ (scissors mode) increases by a factor of three. For the last three operators - isovector dipole, spin dipole and orbital dipole (including the twist mode) it is shown that the summed strength is independant of deformation. The main difference in the behavior is that for the first two operators we have in-shell transitions whereas for the last three operators the transitions are out of shell.

## Key claim

The summed strength for the isovector dipole, spin-dipole, and orbital-dipole operators (including the twist mode) is independent of deformation in $^{12}C$, while the strength for the spin operator $\vec{s}t$ decreases steadily to zero and the strength for the orbital operator $\vec{\ell}\tau$ (scissors mode) increases by a factor of three when going from the spherical limit to the asymptotic oblate limit. This independence holds only for transitions that are out of shell, whereas the deformation-sensitive operators involve in-shell transitions.

## Method

The authors used the $\Delta N = 0$ rotational model to calculate summed transition strengths from the ground state of $^{12}C$ for five isovector operators: $\vec{s}t$, $\vec{\ell}t$, $rY't$, $r[Y's]^\lambda t$, and $r[Y'\ell]^\lambda t$. They evaluated these strengths in both the spherical limit (pure $p_{3/2}$ closed shell) and the asymptotic oblate limit (deformation parameter $\beta \sim -0.6$), as well as for an intermediate deformation with $\delta = -0.3$ using wavefunctions from Irvine (1972). The operators with $t_z$ correspond to electromagnetic transitions and those with $t_+$ to weak transitions such as neutrino capture.

## Key numbers

- For the M1 transition in $^{12}C$, the spherical limit gives $B(M1) = 11.262\ \mu_N^2$, while the asymptotic oblate limit gives $B(M1) = 0.4775\ \mu_N^2$ (scissors mode only)
- For intermediate deformation $\delta = -0.3$ with wavefunctions from Irvine (1972), $B(M1) = 2.655\ \mu_N^2$, close to the experimental value of $2.61\ \mu_N^2$
- The operator $2st_+$ (Gamow-Teller) yields strength $16/3$ in the spherical limit, vanishes in the asymptotic oblate limit, and gives $0.987$ for $\delta = -0.3$
- Total summed dipole strength ($rY^1t$) is 27 in both spherical and asymptotic limits
- Total summed spin-dipole strength ($r[Y^1s]^\lambda t$) is 20.25 in both limits
- Total summed orbital-dipole strength ($r[Y^1\ell]^\lambda t$) is 48 in both limits
- The LSND experimental flux-averaged exclusive cross-section to $^{12}N$ ground state is $\sigma_{exc} = (0.66 \pm 0.1 \pm 0.1) \times 10^{-42}\ cm^2$
- The LSND flux-averaged total inclusive cross-section is $\sigma_{inc} = (12.2 \pm 0.3 + 1.8) \times 10^{-42}\ cm^2$

## Context

This work addresses the long-standing problem that Hartree-Fock and RPA calculations assuming a pure $p_{3/2}$ closed shell for $^{12}C$ overestimate the exclusive neutrino capture cross-section by a factor of 5-7 compared to LSND results (Kolbe et al., 1995; Auerbach et al., 1997). The authors show that deformation naturally reduces the allowed Gamow-Teller strength while preserving the total dipole strength, partially justifying the ad-hoc reduction factor used by Kolbe et al. (1995). The results extend earlier work by Cohen and Kurath (1965) on configuration mixing in $^{12}C$ and are consistent with the independent finding by Vogel (1999) that total strength does not depend on $p_{1/2}$ shell occupancy. This work enables more realistic calculations of neutrino-nucleus cross-sections for the LSND and KARMEN experiments by incorporating deformation effects explicitly rather than through phenomenological reduction factors.
