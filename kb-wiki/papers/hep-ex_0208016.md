# Nuclear shadowing at low Q^2

> We re-examine the role of vector meson dominance in nuclear shadowing at low Q^2. We find that models which incorporate both vector meson and partonic mechanisms are consistent with both the magnitude and the Q^2 slope of the shadowing data.

# Nuclear Shadowing at Low Q²

## Key claim

Models that combine vector meson dominance (VMD) at low Q² with partonic (Pomeron-exchange) mechanisms at high Q² are consistent with both the magnitude and the Q² slope of nuclear shadowing data from the New Muon Collaboration, provided the VMD component is included to describe the transition to the photoproduction region; a purely partonic mechanism alone is insufficient to describe the logarithmic slope in Q² at low x.

## Method

The authors compute nuclear structure function ratios F₂^A/F₂^D using a two-phase model: at high Q² (above ~2 GeV²), shadowing is described by diffractive scattering through double and triple Pomeron exchange, parameterized via a Pomeron flux and effective Pomeron structure function; at low Q², shadowing is described by vector meson dominance, where the photon couples to low-mass vector mesons (ρ⁰, ω, φ) whose multiple scattering is treated with Glauber theory. The model is compared to NMC data on Sn/C, C/D, and Ca/D ratios over a range of x from 10⁻⁵ to 0.1 and Q² from 0.03 to 100 GeV².

## Key numbers

- The VMD correction to F₂^A peaks at Q² ~ 1 GeV², with vector meson propagators producing a strong Q² dependence at low Q².
- For Q² > 10 GeV², the partonic (Pomeron) contribution dominates and the Q² dependence becomes very weak, as expected from a leading-twist mechanism.
- The logarithmic slope b = dR/d ln Q² of the Sn/C ratio decreases from approximately 0.04 at the smallest x value (x ≈ 0.0125) to zero at x > 0.06.
- The model calculations cover Q² from 0.03 to 100 GeV² and x from 10⁻⁵ to 0.1 for nuclei from A=2 to A=208 (D, Li, Be, C, Al, Ca, Fe, Sn, Xe, Pb).
- NMC data on Sn/C ratios extend down to Q² ≈ 1 GeV² at the smallest x bins, while C/D and Ca/D data extend down to Q² ≈ 0.05 GeV² at x > 0.0003.

## Context

This work extends the two-phase shadowing model previously developed by Melnitchouk and Thomas (1993, 1995), similar to the approach pioneered by Kwiecinski and Badelek (1988, 1992). It directly addresses the need to understand nuclear shadowing in charged lepton scattering before tackling corrections in neutrino scattering, motivated by the NuTeV Collaboration's analysis (2002) and proposals for high-intensity neutrino experiments at Fermilab. The model's successful description of NMC data over a wide Q² range provides a basis for extending the approach to neutrino-nucleus scattering, as pursued by Boros, Londergan, and Thomas (1998, 1999).
