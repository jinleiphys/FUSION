# Beam Energy Dependence of Higher Moments of Net-proton Multiplicity Distributions in Heavy-ion Collisions at RHIC

> We present various order moments (Variance ($σ^2$), Skewness($S$), Kurtosis($κ$)) of net-proton multiplicity distributions in Au+Au collisions from the first phase of RHIC beam energy scan ({\sNN}=7.7$-$200 GeV). The measurements are carried out by the STAR detector at mid-rapidity ($|y|<0.5$) and within transverse momentum range $0.4<p_{T}<0.8$ {\gevc}. The product of moments ({\SD} and \KV) are predicted to be sensitive to the correlation length and connected to ratios of baryon number susceptibility. We observe deviations below Poisson expectations in the most central collisions for all of the energies. These results are compared with a transport model to understand the effects not related to critical physics. We also discuss the error estimation methods and the techniques to suppress centrality resolution effect in the moment analysis.

## Key claim

The measured product of moments (κσ² and Sσ) of net-proton multiplicity distributions in Au+Au collisions deviate from Poisson expectations in the most central collisions at all beam energies, with the deviation becoming significant below √sNN = 39 GeV; however, these deviations may include contributions from non-critical physics such as centrality resolution effects and baryon number conservation, which must be disentangled before attributing them to the QCD critical point.

## Method

The STAR detector measured net-proton multiplicity distributions (protons minus antiprotons) at mid-rapidity (|y| < 0.5) and transverse momentum 0.4 < pT < 0.8 GeV/c in Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV. Protons and antiprotons were identified via ionization energy loss (dE/dx) in the Time Projection Chamber, and centrality was determined from charged particle multiplicity excluding protons and antiprotons within |η| < 0.5 to avoid autocorrelation. The cumulants C1–C4 were extracted from the net-proton distributions and corrected for finite centrality bin width, then formed into the moment products Sσ = C3/C2 and κσ² = C4/C2.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies studied: √sNN = 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV, corresponding to baryon chemical potential μB from about 20 to 450 MeV
- Kinematic acceptance: mid-rapidity |y| < 0.5 and transverse momentum range 0.4 < pT < 0.8 GeV/c
- For 0-5% central Au+Au collisions, κσ² and Sσ normalized to Poisson expectations show deviation below √sNN = 39 GeV, while at √sNN = 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV they are close to Poisson expectations
- In the AMPT string melting model study of centrality resolution effect, four different η ranges (|η| < 0.5, 1, 1.5, 2) were used for centrality definition, showing that κσ² is more sensitive to centrality resolution than Sσ
- For the Skellam distribution simulation of error estimation, mean values were set as Mp = 4.11 and Manti-p = 2.99, with sample sizes of 0.01, 0.1, and 1 million events

## Context

This work presents the first systematic beam energy scan measurements of higher moments of net-proton distributions from the RHIC BES program, extending prior moment analyses that were limited to single energies. The results are compared to UrQMD transport model calculations to identify non-critical physics contributions such as baryon number conservation and hadronic scattering, and to Lattice QCD predictions for baryon number susceptibilities (e.g., κσ² = χB(4)/χB(2) and Sσ = χB(3)/χB(2)). The study also addresses the centrality resolution effect previously identified in moment analysis (Skokov 2011) and compares three statistical error estimation methods (Delta theorem, Bootstrap, Sub-group) to establish reliable uncertainty quantification for future higher-statistics measurements in the second phase of the BES program.
