# Off-the-energy-shell pp scattering in the exclusive proton knockout 12C(p,2p) reaction at 392 MeV

> The triple differential cross section (TDX) for the 12C(p,2p) proton knockout reaction from the 1p3/2 single-particle state measured at 392 MeV is investigated by nonrelativistic distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) with accurate treatment of the kinematics of the colliding two protons and effects of in-medium modification to the matrix elements of the proton-proton (pp) effective interaction. Some simplifying approximations made in previous studies with DWIA are examined. The off-the-energy-shell matrix elements of the pp effective interaction are shown to play an essential role in describing the asymmetric two peaks of the measured TDX corresponding to the kinematics in which the momentum transfer is fixed.

## Key claim

Off-the-energy-shell matrix elements of the proton-proton effective interaction are essential to reproduce the asymmetric two peaks of the measured triple differential cross section for the 12C(p,2p) reaction at 392 MeV, under the specific kinematic condition where the momentum transfer is fixed at 2.5 fm^-1. The asymmetry cannot be reproduced by the on-shell approximation, while in-medium modifications due to the Pauli principle and refractive effects of distortion are found to be negligible for this observable.

## Method

The authors performed nonrelativistic distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) calculations for the 12C(p,2p) reaction at 392 MeV, using the Melbourne g matrix (based on the Bonn-B nucleon-nucleon potential) as the proton-proton effective interaction. They treated the kinematics of the colliding two protons accurately, including the local momenta of the incoming and outgoing protons via the local semi-classical approximation, and compared calculations with and without the off-shell property of the pp interaction. The spectroscopic factor was determined by chi-squared fit to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Spectroscopic factor S = 1.71 for the 1p3/2 state, determined by chi-squared fit of the full calculation to experimental data, agreeing with the (e,e'p) value of 1.72 ± 0.11
- Spectroscopic factor S = 1.84 obtained with the simplified on-shell approximation neglecting in-medium effects
- Momentum transfer q fixed at 2.5 fm^-1 for kinematics 1
- For kinematics 1, the off-shell pp cross section for the right peak (Q = 4.0 fm^-1) is twice as large as that for the left peak (Q = 3.0 fm^-1)
- For kinematics 3, spectroscopic factor S = 1.64 (full calculation) and S = 1.78 (on-shell approximation)
- Experimental angular widths used for smearing: Δθ1 = 20 mrad, Δφ1 = 30 mrad, Δθ2 = 50 mrad, Δφ2 = 45 mrad

## Context

This work extends previous nonrelativistic DWIA studies (Redish; Noro et al.; Hillhouse et al.) that had used simplifying approximations including on-shell approximation and neglect of refractive effects. The authors show that the off-shell property of the pp interaction, previously not treated properly in nonrelativistic DWIA, is essential for describing the asymmetric TDX in kinematics where momentum transfer is fixed. The successful description opens the door to analysis of spin observables such as the analyzing power Ay, which had shown discrepancies in earlier work (Noro et al.; Hillhouse et al.) and where in-medium meson mass reduction had been invoked but caused problems for other spin observables.
