# Effects of the equation of state on the bulk properties of maximally-rotating neutron stars

> Neutron stars are among the densest known objects in the universe and an ideal laboratory for the strange physics of super-condensed matter. While the simultaneously measurements of mass and radius of non-rotating neutron stars may impose constraints on the properties of the dense nuclear matter, the observation and study of maximally-rotating ones, close to the mass-shedding limit, may lead to significantly further constraints. Theoretical predictions allow neutron stars to rotate extremely fast (even more than $2000 \ {\rm Hz}$). However, until this moment, the fastest observed rotating pulsar has a frequency of $716 \ {\rm Hz}$, much lower compared to the theoretical predictions. There are many suggestions for the mechanism which lead to this situation. In any case, the theoretical study of uniformly rotating neutron stars, along with the accurate measurements, may offer rich information concerning the high density part of the equation of state. In addition, neutron stars through their evolution, may provide us with a criteria to determine the final fate of a rotating compact star. Sensitivity of bulk neutron stars properties on the equation of state at the mass-shedding limit are the main subject of the present study.

# Effects of the equation of state on the bulk properties of maximally-rotating neutron stars

## Key claim
The sensitivity of bulk neutron star properties (mass, radius, moment of inertia, eccentricity, braking index) to the nuclear equation of state is systematically studied at the mass-shedding (Keplerian) limit, using 23 hadronic EoSs plus one hyperonic and one quark-star EoS. The authors find that the Keplerian frequency depends almost linearly on the quantity x = (M/M_sun)^(1/2) * (10 km/R)^(3/2) for rotating configurations, with a coefficient C_rot = 1781.90 Hz and relative error under 1%, providing a more precise universal relation than earlier work.

## Method
The authors employed the public RNS code by Stergioulas and Friedman to solve the stationary axisymmetric Einstein equations for uniformly rotating neutron stars, using 23 hadronic equations of state (phenomenological, field-theoretical, and microscopic) plus one hyperonic EoS (FSU2H) and one quark-star EoS (QS57.6). They constructed two additional EoSs (APR-1 and APR-2) by fitting the MDI momentum-dependent interaction model to data from Akmal et al. (1998) for symmetric and pure neutron matter. The Keplerian (mass-shedding) frequency was determined via the turning-point method for secular instability, and bulk properties were computed along the Keplerian sequence for both normal and supramassive configurations.

## Key numbers
- The fastest observed rotating pulsar (J1748-244ad) has a spin frequency of 716 Hz, much lower than theoretical predictions exceeding 2000 Hz.
- The relation between Keplerian frequency and rotating-configuration properties gives C_rot = 1781.90 Hz with relative error ≤ 1% for 23 hadronic EoSs.
- For the static-configuration relation, C_st = 1266.68 Hz with 5.6% relative error, updating the earlier value of 1220 Hz from Haensel et al. (2009).
- The rest mass at maximum rotating configuration relates to gravitational mass as M_max^(rm;rot)/M_sun = 1.17 * M_max^(gm;rot)/M_sun, with maximum error less than 3.3% (17% percentage difference).
- The moment of inertia at maximum rotating configuration follows I_max^rot = -1.568 + 0.883 * exp[0.7 * (M_max^(gm;rot)/M_sun)] (in units of 10^45 g cm^2).
- The dimensionless moment of inertia at maximum mass relates to compactness as (I/MR^2)_max = -0.006 + 1.379 * beta_max.

## Context
This work extends the foundational studies of Cook, Shapiro, and Teukolsky (1994) and the more recent work of Cipolletta et al. (2015) by employing a larger set of modern EoSs that satisfy the observed 2 M_sun mass constraints. The authors update the empirical universal relation for Keplerian frequency from Haensel et al. (2009) and provide new universal relations for moment of inertia and compactness at the maximum mass configuration. The results enable tighter constraints on the high-density equation of state from future measurements of neutron star spin frequencies, moments of inertia, and radii, and inform the evolution of rotating neutron stars toward black hole formation.
