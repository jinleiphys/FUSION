# The influence of negative-energy states on proton-proton bremsstrahlung

> We investigate the effect of negative-energy states on proton-proton bremsstrahlung using a manifestly covariant amplitude based on a T-matrix constructed in a spectator model. We show that there is a large cancellation among the zeroth-order, single- and double-scattering diagrams involving negative-energy nucleonic currents. We thus conclude that it is essential to include all these diagrams when studying effects of negative-energy states.

## Key claim

The paper shows that while individual diagrams involving nucleon negative-energy states in proton-proton bremsstrahlung are large, their total contribution to the cross section nearly vanishes due to a large cancellation among the zeroth-order, single-scattering, and double-scattering (rescattering) diagrams; therefore, any study of negative-energy state effects must include all these diagrams, not just the zeroth-order scattering as done in prior work.

## Method

The authors used a manifestly covariant amplitude based on a T-matrix constructed in the spectator model (Gross et al., 1992), which retains both positive- and negative-energy parts of the off-shell nucleon propagator. The electromagnetic interaction was introduced consistently with the hadronic interaction using the Ward-Takahashi identity to satisfy current conservation, with the photon allowed to couple to both positive- and negative-energy nucleons. Calculations were performed for a selected set of kinematics from the coplanar TRIUMF experiment, comparing results with and without negative-energy nucleonic currents, and also comparing single-scattering diagrams alone versus the full amplitude including rescattering diagrams.

## Key numbers

* The negative-energy nucleonic current shows practically no influence on the cross section when all diagrams are included, despite individual diagrams being large.
* In the $++ \rightarrow +-$ spin transition channel, the matrix elements $M^{\mu=1}$ and $M^{\mu=2}$ from the single-scattering diagrams are very large, but are almost exactly cancelled by the sum of the negative-energy rescattering diagrams.
* The analyzing power is much more affected by the negative-energy contribution than the cross section; including negative-energy states fills up the minimum in the analyzing power at intermediate photon angles almost completely.
* The Bonn OBEPQ potential results (solid lines) and the spectator model results with only positive-energy nucleonic currents (long-dashed lines) are similar to each other, falling into the trend observed in earlier works that T-matrices fitting phase-shifts give similar results for $pp\gamma$ observables.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of reaction mechanisms in proton-proton bremsstrahlung, particularly the investigation of higher-order processes such as $\Delta$-decay diagrams (de Jong et al., 1994; Jetter and Fearing, 1995; Eden and Gari, 1995) and vector-meson-decay diagrams (de Jong and Nakayama, 1995). It directly addresses the limited attention given to negative-energy states in prior work by Mundel (1994), who used a $p/m$ expansion with only zeroth-order scattering, and Eden and Gari (1995), who included negative-energy contributions only in zeroth-order scattering. The paper forces a revision of claims by Liou et al. (1995) and Eden and Gari (1995) that the ps/pv content of the $\pi$NN vertex can be determined from negative-energy contributions to $pp\gamma$ observables, since those calculations missed the cancellation observed here. The results suggest that reproducing the TRIUMF analyzing power data (Michaelian et al., 1990) will be a severely restrictive test on potential models incorporating nucleon negative-energy states.
