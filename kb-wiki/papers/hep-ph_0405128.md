# Propagators of light scalar mesons

> For the first time, as far as we know, in field theory are found explicit forms of propagators satisfying the Källen -- Lehmann representation. To be exact, it is shown that scalar meson propagators, taking into account a virtual intermediate state contribution to the resonance self-energy, satisfy the Källen -- Lehmann representation in the wide domain of coupling constants of the light scalar mesons with the two-particle states. It is proposed to use these propagators in routine fitting data about light scalar mesons to reveal physics underlying the light scalar mesons.

# Propagators of light scalar mesons

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate for the first time that explicit propagators for light scalar mesons, which include virtual intermediate state contributions to the resonance self-energy, satisfy the Källen–Lehmann representation, but only within a restricted domain of coupling constants between the scalar resonance and its two-particle decay channels.

## Method
The authors construct scalar meson propagators of the form \(1/D_R(m^2)\) where the inverse propagator \(D_R(m^2) = m_R^2 - m^2 + \text{Re}(\Pi_R(m_R^2)) - \Pi_R(m^2)\) incorporates finite-width corrections from two-particle intermediate states. They then derive analytic conditions on the coupling constants \(g_{Rab}\) that ensure the propagator has no zeros in the complex plane, which is required for the Källen–Lehmann representation to hold. The analysis is performed separately for one-channel cases (sigma and kappa mesons) and two-channel cases (f0(980) and a0(980) mesons).

## Key numbers
- For the sigma meson: \(g^2_{\sigma\pi\pi}/16\pi^2 < 0.1\ \text{GeV}^2\), corresponding to \(\Gamma(\sigma\to\pi\pi, m_\sigma) < \Gamma_\sigma^c \approx 0.5\ \text{GeV}\)
- For the kappa meson (with \(m_\kappa = 0.8\ \text{GeV}\)): \(g^2_{\kappa\pi K}/16\pi^2 < 0.4\ \text{GeV}^2\), corresponding to \(\Gamma(\kappa\to\pi K, m_\kappa) < \Gamma_\kappa^c \approx 0.8\ \text{GeV}\)
- For f0(980): the Källen–Lehmann representation holds for any \(g_{f_0\pi\pi}^2\) when \((g_{f_0K\bar K}/g_{f_0\pi\pi})^2 \geq r_{f_0}^c \approx 2.5\)
- For a0(980): the Källen–Lehmann representation holds for any \(g_{a_0\pi\eta}^2\) when \((g_{a_0K\bar K}/g_{a_0\pi\eta})^2 \geq r_{a_0}^c \approx 0.8\)
- For a weak-coupling test case with \((g_{f_0K\bar K}/g_{f_0\pi\pi})^2 = 1/3\): \((g_{f_0\pi\pi})^2/16\pi^2 < 0.3\ \text{GeV}^2\), \(\Gamma(f_0\to\pi\pi, m_{f_0}) < \Gamma_{f_0}^c \approx 0.9\ \text{GeV}\)
- For the same weak-coupling test case for a0: \((g_{a_0\pi\eta})^2/16\pi^2 < 0.9\ \text{GeV}^2\), \(\Gamma(a_0\to\pi\eta, m_{a_0}) < \Gamma_{a_0}^c \approx 1.8\ \text{GeV}\)

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier development of scalar meson propagators (Achasov and collaborators, 1979-1984) by proving they satisfy the Källen–Lehmann representation, a fundamental requirement of quantum field theory. The propagators are contrasted with the Flatté formulas (Flatté, 1976), which have quantum mechanical origin and neglect virtual particle contributions to resonance self-energy; the Flatté formulas are shown to require a unitarity-violating normalization factor and to shift the renormalized mass in two-channel treatments. The propagators have been used by the Sobolev Institute for Mathematics Group, and by the SND, CMD-2, and KLOE Collaborations in analyzing \(\phi\to\gamma f_0(980)\to\gamma\pi\pi\) and \(\phi\to\gamma a_0(980)\to\gamma\pi\eta\) decays. The work enables routine extraction of coupling constants and masses from experimental mass spectra, distinguishing between compact states (such as \(q\bar q\) or \(q^2\bar q^2\)) strongly coupled to decay channels and weakly bound systems.
