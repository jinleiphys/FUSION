# Point-form quantum field theory

> We examine canonical quantization of relativistic field theories on the forward hyperboloid, a Lorentz-invariant surface of the form $x_μx^μ= τ^2$. This choice of quantization surface implies that all components of the 4-momentum operator are affected by interactions (if present), whereas rotation and boost generators remain interaction free -- a feature characteristic of Dirac's `` point-form\rq\rq of relativistic dynamics. Unlike previous attempts to quantize fields on space-time hyperboloids, we keep the usual plane-wave expansion of the field operators and consider evolution of the system generated by the 4-momentum operator. We verify that the Fock-space representations of the Poincaré generators for free scalar and spin-1/2 fields look the same as for equal-time quantization. Scattering is formulated for interacting fields in a covariant interaction picture and it is shown that the familiar perturbative expansion of the S-operator is recovered by our approach. An appendix analyzes special distributions, integrals over the forward hyperboloid, that are used repeatedly in the paper.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that canonical quantization of free scalar and spin-1/2 fields on the forward hyperboloid (a Lorentz-invariant surface of the form \(x_\mu x^\mu = \tau^2\)) yields the same Fock-space representations of the Poincaré generators as standard equal-time quantization, provided one retains the usual plane-wave expansion of field operators and considers evolution generated by the 4-momentum operator rather than the dilatation operator.

## Method

The authors quantize free scalar and spin-1/2 fields on the forward hyperboloid \(\sigma_\tau: x_\mu x^\mu = \tau^2\) using Lorentz-invariant canonical (anti)commutation relations adapted to that surface. They expand the field operators in the usual plane-wave basis (momentum eigenstates) rather than the Lorentz basis used in prior attempts, and compute the Fock-space representations of the Poincaré generators by evaluating integrals over the hyperboloid with the help of a Lorentz-invariant distribution \(W(P,Q)\). For interacting fields, they formulate scattering in a covariant interaction picture and show that the perturbative expansion of the S-operator matches the standard time-ordered perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

* The scalar product on the hyperboloid \(\sigma_\tau\) is shown to be independent of \(\tau\) and reduces to \(\int d^3p/(2\omega_{\vec{p}})\, \tilde{\psi}^\ast(\vec{p})\,\tilde{\chi}(\vec{p})\) for Klein-Gordon solutions.
* The commutation relations for creation and annihilation operators obtained from hyperboloid quantization are \([\hat{a}(\vec{p}),\hat{a}^\dagger(\vec{p}\,')] = 2\,\omega_{\vec{p}}\,\delta^3(\vec{p}-\vec{p}\,')\) (and similarly for \(\hat{b}\)), identical to the equal-time result.
* The charge operator for the complex scalar field takes the usual form \(\hat{Q} = \int d^3p/(2\omega_{\vec{p}})\,[\hat{a}^\dagger(\vec{p})\hat{a}(\vec{p}) - \hat{b}^\dagger(\vec{p})\hat{b}(\vec{p})]\).

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts at point-form quantum field theory by Fubini et al. (1973), Schwager (1974), and others, which used hyperbolic coordinates and a Lorentz basis that diagonalized boost generators but made the 4-momentum operator difficult to define as a self-adjoint operator. By instead using the standard momentum basis and focusing on evolution generated by the 4-momentum operator rather than the dilatation operator, the authors avoid the technical problems of those prior approaches. The results provide a solid field-theoretic foundation for point-form relativistic quantum mechanics, which has been used in constituent quark models for calculating masses, electroweak properties, and strong decays of hadrons (e.g., by Glozman et al. (1998, 2004) and Biernat et al. (2001, 2004)).
