# The Mass Gap Approach to QCD

> We convincingly argue that the true dynamical and gauge structures of the QCD ground state are much more complicated than its Lagrangian exact gauge symmetry supposes to be. The dynamical source of these complications has been identified with the tadpole/seagull term, which renormalized version called, {\sl the mass gap}. It is explicitly present in the full gluon self-energy. The true dynamical role of the mass gap was hidden in the QCD ground state. To disclose it the splintering between the transverse conditions for the full gluon self-energy and its subtracted counterpart has been derived. We have established the equation of motion for the full gluon propagator on account of the mass gap. It allows to fix the dynamical and gauge structures of the full gluon propagator with a newly-derived generalized gauge. It is uniquely given as a function of the gluon momentum. All this makes it possible to present novel non-perturbative analytical approach to QCD, which we call the mass gap approach. It extends the mass gap concept to be accounted for the QCD ground state as well. We have found the general non-perturbative solution for the full gluon propagator with exactly defined finite gluon pole mass, and it is different from the excitations with the effective gluon masses. Despite the exact gauge symmetry is broken in the ground state, the renormalizability of the theory is not affected due to the important role of the Slavnov-Taylor identity in the renormalization beyond the perturbation theory. For this the non-perturbative multiplicative renormalization program for the full massive gluon propagator has been formulated. Our approach does not allow the massive gluons to be the mass-shell objects, and thus prevents them to appear as physical states at large distances (confinement of massive gluons).

## Key claim

The paper argues that the true dynamical and gauge structures of the QCD ground state are more complicated than the Lagrangian's exact gauge symmetry suggests, and that the mass gap (the renormalized tadpole/seagull term) is the dynamical source of these complications, leading to a non-perturbative solution for the full gluon propagator with a finite gluon pole mass that differs from effective gluon mass excitations.

## Method

The authors analyze the Schwinger-Dyson equation for the full gluon propagator, focusing on the transversity conditions for the full gluon self-energy and its subtracted counterpart. They derive the splintering between these transverse conditions to disclose the role of the mass gap, establish an equation of motion for the full gluon propagator that accounts for the mass gap, and formulate a non-perturbative multiplicative renormalization program for the full massive gluon propagator within a newly-derived generalized gauge.

## Key numbers

- The tadpole term contributes to both transverse and longitudinal components of the full gluon propagator via the relation $\Pi_{\rho\sigma}^t(D) \sim \int d^4 k D_{\alpha\beta}(k) T^0_{\rho\sigma\alpha\beta} = g_{\rho\sigma} \Delta^2_t(D)$, where $\Delta^2_t(D)$ is a quadratically UV divergent but regularized constant.
- The full gluon self-energy at zero momentum is $\Pi_{\rho\sigma}(0; D) = g_{\rho\sigma} \Delta^2(D) = g_{\rho\sigma} [\Delta^2_q + \Delta^2_g(D) + \Delta^2_t(D)]$, where each term is a quadratically UV divergent but regularized constant.
- The transverse condition for the subtracted gluon self-energy yields $q_{\rho}q_{\sigma} \Pi^{(s)}_{\rho\sigma}(q; D) = - {( \xi_0 - \xi) \over \xi \xi_0 } (q^2)^2 - q^2 \Delta^2(D)$, where $\xi$ and $\xi_0$ are gauge-fixing parameters.
- In the perturbative case where the transverse conditions are satisfied, the equality $\xi = \xi_0$ is required, and the full gluon propagator becomes $D^{PT}_{\mu\nu}(q) = - i \left[ d^{PT}(q^2) T_{\mu\nu}(q) + \xi_0 L_{\mu\nu}(q) \right] {1 \over q^2}$ with $d^{PT}(q^2) = { 1 \over 1 + \Pi^{(s)}_t(q^2)}$, describing massless gluons singular only at $q^2=0$.

## Context

This work extends the mass gap concept, originally introduced by Jaffe and Witten (2000) within the Hamiltonian formalism, to be accounted for the QCD ground state (vacuum) as well. The approach contrasts with perturbative QCD where the gauge symmetry of the Lagrangian coincides with that of the ground state (analogous to QED), and instead shows that the exact gauge symmetry is dynamically broken in the ground state due to the tadpole term. The results enable a novel non-perturbative analytical approach to low-energy QCD that complements lattice QCD calculations, and the authors emphasize that their approach does not allow massive gluons to appear as physical states at large distances, thus providing a mechanism for gluon confinement.
