# Identical Bands Around the Isobaric Rare Earth Even-Even Nuclei with the Mass Number A = 164

> Eight pairs of rare earth normally deformed nuclei around the isobaric nuclei with A = 164 and have identical values of F-spin have been studied. These pairs of identical bands cover 16 mass units and are classified. We suggested a theoretical collective rotational formula containing three parameters (CRF3) as an extended version of Bohr-Mottelson model to calculate the ground state positive parity excitation energies. Also, the sd-version of the interacting boson model (IBM) has been used to describe the nuclear shapes by using the intrinsic coherent-state. The optimized models parameters for each nucleus are adjusted by using a simulation search program to minimize the root mean square deviation between the theoretical calculation and experimental excitation energies. The best adopted model parameters of the CRF3 are used to calculate the rotational frequencies, the kinematic and dynamic moments of inertia and the evolution of with increasing hw are systematically analyzed. A smooth gradual increase in both moments of inertia was seen. The calculated results agree excellently with the experimental ones which give strong support to the suggested CRF3. The adopted IBM parameters are used to calculate the potential energy surfaces which describe the nuclear deformation. The correlation quantities which identify the IB are extracted, exhibit identical excitation energies and energy ratios in their ground state rotational bands.

# Identical Bands Around the Isobaric Rare Earth Even-Even Nuclei with the Mass Number A = 164

## Key claim

Eight pairs of normally deformed rare-earth nuclei around A = 164 with identical F-spin values exhibit identical ground-state rotational bands, but the correlation is governed by the ratio NpNn/Δ (where Δ is the average pairing gap) rather than by the product NpNn alone; the pair (156Dy, 172W) has identical NpNn but is not truly identical, showing approximately 6.7% average percentage difference in transition energies.

## Method

The authors fitted a three-parameter collective rotational formula (CRF3), an extension of the Bohr-Mottelson model with a spin-dependent moment of inertia J(I) = J(1 + σ I(I+1)), to experimental ground-state excitation energies of 16 even-even nuclei (Dy, Er, Yb, Hf, W isotopes from A=156 to A=172). They also applied the sd-interacting boson model (IBM) with intrinsic coherent states to calculate potential energy surfaces. Model parameters were optimized by minimizing the root mean square deviation between calculated and experimental excitation energies using a simulation search program.

## Key numbers

- Eight pairs of identical bands covering 16 mass units were classified: 3 pairs separated by (2p,2n), 2 pairs by (4p,4n), 2 pairs by (6p,6n), and 1 pair by (8p,8n)
- The energy ratio R4/2 = E(4₁⁺)/E(2₁⁺) differences between IB pairs range from 0.4% to 2.5%, except for the two pairs containing 170W and 172W where the difference is about 5%
- Percentage differences in transition energies |δ| between IB pairs range from 0.10% (160Dy–168Yb) to 6.73% (156Dy–172W)
- The average pairing gap Δ is approximately 12/√A MeV, yielding values from 0.914 MeV (172W) to 0.960 MeV (156Dy)
- The ratio NpNn/Δ ranges from 117.4 MeV⁻¹ (158Er) to 240.601 MeV⁻¹ (166Er)
- The Grodzins deformation parameter βG ranges from 0.2173 (158Er) to 0.3256 (162Dy)
- The CRF3 parameters α range from 13.49 keV (162Dy, 166Er) to 32.76 keV (158Er); γ ranges from -0.00749×10⁻³ (170Hf) to 9.699×10⁻³ (158Er); σ ranges from 1.188×10⁻³ (166Er) to 23.52×10⁻³ (158Er)

## Context

This work extends the study of identical bands from superdeformed nuclei (first discovered by Byrski et al. and others in the early 1990s) to normally deformed rare-earth nuclei, following earlier observations by Baktash et al. (1992) and others that low-spin identical bands occur in pairs of even-even nuclei separated by up to 24 mass units. The authors build on Casten's P-factor systematics and the F-spin formalism of the interacting boson model, showing that the quantity NpNn/Δ is a better identifier of identical bands than NpNn alone. The results enable systematic classification of identical bands across the rare-earth region and provide a test of the CRF3 formula for predicting rotational energies in normally deformed nuclei.
