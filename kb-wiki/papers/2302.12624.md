# Bayesian Analysis of $χ$EFT at Leading Order in a Modified Weinberg Power Counting Approach

> We present a Bayesian analysis of renormalization-group invariant nucleon-nucleon interactions at leading order in chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT) with momentum cutoffs in the range 400--4000 MeV. We use history matching to identify relevant regions in the parameter space of low-energy constants (LECs) and subsequently infer the posterior probability density of their values using Markov chain Monte Carlo. All posteriors are conditioned on experimental data for neutron-proton scattering observables and we estimate the $χ$EFT truncation error in an uncorrelated limit. We do not detect any significant cutoff dependence in the posterior predictive distributions for two-nucleon observables. For all cutoff values we find a multimodal LEC posterior with an insignificant mode harboring a bound $^1{S}_0$ state. The $^3P_0$ and $^3P_2$ phase shifts emerging from the Bayesian analysis are less constrained and typically more repulsive compared to the results of a phase shift optimization. We expect that our inference will impact predictions for nuclei. This work demonstrates how to perform inference in the presence of limit-cycle-like behavior and spurious bound states, and lays the foundation for a Bayesian analysis of renormalization-group invariant $χ$EFT interactions beyond leading order.

# Bayesian Analysis of χEFT at Leading Order in a Modified Weinberg Power Counting Approach

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that Bayesian inference of low-energy constants (LECs) in renormalization-group invariant leading-order chiral effective field theory (χEFT) is feasible despite limit-cycle-like behavior and spurious bound states, but they find that the posterior predictive distributions for $^3P_0$ and $^3P_2$ phase shifts are less constrained and typically more repulsive than results from a standard phase shift optimization, and they do not detect any significant cutoff dependence in posterior predictive distributions for two-nucleon observables across momentum cutoffs from 400 to 4000 MeV.

## Method
The authors performed a Bayesian analysis of nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions at leading order in χEFT using a modified Weinberg power counting approach, with the LO potential consisting of one-pion exchange and contact terms in the $^1S_0$, $^3S_1$, $^3P_0$, and $^3P_2$ partial waves. They first used history matching to identify relevant regions in the four-dimensional LEC parameter space, then employed Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) with the emcee algorithm to sample the posterior probability density, conditioning on 1043 neutron-proton scattering observables (total cross sections, differential cross sections, and total cross sections for polarized beams) with laboratory kinetic energies up to 99 MeV, while estimating the χEFT truncation error in an uncorrelated limit using a Bayesian error model with a naturalness prior on the expansion coefficients.

## Key numbers
- Momentum cutoff range: 400–4000 MeV, with specific cutoffs analyzed at 450, 550, 700, and 1000 MeV
- Number of experimental data points used for inference: 1043 (324 total cross sections from $10^{-6}$ to 99.0 MeV, 698 differential cross sections from 2.72 to 99.0 MeV, 13 total cross sections for polarized beams from 3.65 to 17.1 MeV, and 8 total cross sections for polarized beams from 4.98 to 66.0 MeV)
- Breakdown scale assumed: $\Lambda_\chi = 600$ MeV
- Prior standard deviations for LECs: $4\pi/f_\pi^2 \approx 0.1 \times 10^4$ GeV$^{-2}$ for S-wave LECs and $4\pi/(f_\pi^2 \Lambda_\chi^2) \approx 0.4 \times 10^4$ GeV$^{-4}$ for P-wave LECs
- Limit-cycle-like behavior observed in $\tilde{C}_{^3S_1}$ at $\Lambda = 1150$ MeV and in $C_{^3P_0}$ at $\Lambda = 780$ MeV
- Spurious deeply-bound state appears in $^3P_0$ for $\Lambda \geq 770$ MeV and in the coupled $^3S_1$-$^3D_1$ channel for $\Lambda \geq 1140$ MeV
- History matching reduced the relevant parameter volume by a factor as small as $10^{-5}$ relative to the initial volume

## Context
This work extends previous Bayesian studies of χEFT interactions by Melendez (2017), Wesolowski (2021), and Svensson (2022), which were all grounded in the canonical Weinberg power counting, to a modified Weinberg power counting approach that aims for renormalization-group invariance. It directly addresses the shortcomings at leading order identified by Yang (2020) for finite nuclei, who found that the essential mechanism for nuclear binding tends to fail for selected $A>4$ nuclei when using available RG-invariant power counting schemes and who renormalized LECs by demanding exact reproduction of selected phase shifts at a single scattering energy. The work enables future Bayesian analyses of RG-invariant χEFT interactions beyond leading order and provides a framework for performing inference in the presence of limit-cycle-like behavior and spurious bound states, which will impact predictions for nuclei.
