# Analysis of kaon spectra at SIS energies - what remains from the KN potential

> We study the reaction Au+Au at 1.48 AGeV and analyze the influence of the KN optical potential on cm spectra and azimuthal distributions at mid-rapidity. We find a significant change of the yields but only slight changes in the shapes of the distributions when turning off the optical potential. However, the spectra show contributions from different reaction times, where early kaons contribute stronger to higher momenta and late kaons to lower momenta. Azimuthal distributions of the kaons at mid-rapidity show a strong centrality dependence. Their shape is influenced by the KN optical potential as well as by re-scattering.

# Analysis of kaon spectra at SIS energies - what remains from the KN potential

## Key claim

The KN optical potential significantly changes the absolute yield of kaons produced in Au+Au collisions at 1.48 AGeV, but the shapes of the center-of-mass momentum spectra and azimuthal distributions are only slightly affected; the spectral shapes are dominated by the time structure of the collision dynamics rather than by the KN potential itself, and the azimuthal squeeze signal arises from both the potential and re-scattering effects.

## Method

The authors used the Isospin Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) model to simulate Au+Au collisions at 1.48 AGeV, incorporating all relevant cross sections for kaon production and annihilation along with a density- and momentum-dependent KN optical potential parametrized from relativistic mean field calculations by Schaffner-Bielich. They compared calculations with and without the KN potential, and also decomposed the kaon spectra into contributions from different reaction time windows (0-4, 4-8, 8-16, and >16 fm/c) to isolate the effects of collision dynamics from medium effects.

## Key numbers

- Maximum baryonic density reached in central (b=0 fm) Au+Au collisions at 1.48 AGeV: about 2.5 times ground state nuclear density, occurring at approximately 8 fm/c after first contact.
- Total cross section assumed for minimum bias reactions: 5.3 barn.
- Transverse momentum cut applied for azimuthal distribution analysis: 0.2 GeV/c ≤ p_T ≤ 0.8 GeV/c.
- Centrality cuts: central collisions defined as impact parameter b < 5.9 fm; peripheral collisions defined as b > 7.8 fm.
- Mid-rapidity cut for azimuthal distributions: |y(cm)/y_Proj(cm)| < 0.4.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of sub-threshold kaon production and the KN optical potential by groups such as Cassing, Ko, Fuchs, and LiKo, and directly compares to experimental data from the KaoS collaboration (Foerster) and FOPI collaboration (Devismes) for Au+Au at 1.48 AGeV. The finding that spectral shapes reflect collision dynamics rather than the KN potential contradicts stronger claims by LiKo and Wang regarding the influence of the potential on azimuthal distributions, which the authors suggest may arise from different implementations of Lorentz-type forces. The results enable future work to focus on disentangling medium effects from dynamical evolution in heavy-ion collisions, and highlight that the time structure of kaon production (early kaons at high momenta, late kaons at low momenta) must be accounted for when interpreting experimental spectra.
