# Distribution Amplitudes of Heavy-Light Mesons

> A symmetry-preserving approach to the continuum bound-state problem in quantum field theory is used to calculate the masses, leptonic decay constants and light-front distribution amplitudes of empirically accessible heavy-light mesons. The inverse moment of the $B$-meson distribution is particularly important in treatments of exclusive $B$-decays using effective field theory and the factorisation formalism; and its value is therefore computed: $λ_B(ζ= 2\,{\rm GeV}) = 0.54(3)\,$GeV. As an example and in anticipation of precision measurements at new-generation $B$-factories, the branching fraction for the rare $B\to γ(E_γ) \ell ν_\ell$ radiative decay is also calculated, retaining $1/m_B^2$ and $1/E_γ^2$ corrections to the differential decay width, with the result $Γ_{B\to γ\ell ν_\ell}/Γ_B = 0.47(15)$ on $E_γ> 1.5\,$GeV.

## Key claim

Using a symmetry-preserving truncation of the continuum bound-state problem, the authors compute the masses, leptonic decay constants, and light-front distribution amplitudes of heavy-light mesons, but the rainbow-ladder truncation is only directly reliable for systems where the difference in current-quark masses is below a critical threshold; for lighter quarks, the authors must extrapolate using the Schlessinger point method.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations using a rainbow-ladder truncation with an interaction kernel constrained by analyses of QCD's gauge sector, varying the current-quark masses until the Bethe-Salpeter equation yields a solution at the meson mass. For systems where the mass difference between valence quarks exceeds a critical value (below approximately 0.4 GeV for the light quark in c-quark systems and below 1.3 GeV in b-quark systems), the authors extrapolate computed quantities using the Schlessinger point method. The distribution amplitudes are obtained by computing Mellin moments up to third order and fitting to a two-parameter functional form.

## Context

This work extends the continuum bound-state approach previously applied to light mesons by Chang (2013) and heavy-heavy systems by Ding (2015) to heavy-light mesons, providing parameter-free predictions for masses, decay constants, and distribution amplitudes. The computed inverse moment of the B-meson distribution amplitude, lambda_B = 0.54(3) GeV at 2 GeV, is consistent with the value 0.58(4) reported by Lee (2005) using similar methods. The results enable improved predictions for exclusive B-decays within the factorisation formalism, as demonstrated by the calculation of the B -> gamma ell nu_ell branching fraction, which shows marked sensitivity to the B-meson distribution amplitude as previously highlighted by Beneke (2011) and Braun (2012).
