# Mirror displacement energies and neutron skins

> A gross estimate of the neutron skin [0.80(5)$(N-Z)/A$ fm] is extracted from experimental proton radii, represented by a four parameter fit, and observed mirror displacement energies (CDE). The calculation of the latter relies on an accurately derived Coulomb energy and smooth averages of the charge symmetry breaking potentials constrained to state of the art values. The only free parameter is the neutron skin itself. The Nolen Schiffer anomaly is reduced to small deviations (rms=127 keV) that exhibit a secular trend. It is argued that with state of the art shell model calculations the anomaly should disappear. Highly accurate fits to proton radii emerge as a fringe benefit.

# Mirror displacement energies and neutron skins

## Key claim
A neutron skin of 0.80(5)(N-Z)/A fm is extracted from experimental proton radii and mirror displacement energies, reducing the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly to small deviations (rms=127 keV) that exhibit a secular trend; the authors argue that with state-of-the-art shell model calculations the anomaly should disappear, but they cannot definitively decide whether a residual anomaly remains until such calculations are performed.

## Method
The authors fit a four-parameter smooth Bethe-Weizsäcker-type expression for proton point radii to 82 experimental values at classical shell closures (N or Z = 6, 14, 28, 50, 82, 126). They then compare 63 experimental mirror displacement energies (CDE) with calculations that combine an exact oscillator-basis treatment of the Coulomb potential, smooth averages of charge symmetry breaking (CSB) potentials constrained to theoretical values (B_v=300 keV, B_s=200 keV at A=16), and the neutron skin as the only free parameter. The neutron skin is determined by finding which value of the skin parameter ζ yields the best rms deviation when the CSB potentials are held at their constrained values.

## Key numbers
- Neutron skin estimate: Δ_{rνπ} = 0.80(5) t/A fm (where t = N-Z)
- Root mean square deviation of calculated from experimental CDE: 127 keV (for ζ=0.8 with B_v=306 keV, B_s=199 keV)
- Proton radius fit parameters: ρ_0 = 0.943(2), g = 1.04(3)
- For ζ=0.8, the corresponding υ parameter is 0.62
- The Coulomb potential smooth average: ⟨V_{Cm}^d⟩ ≈ 0.864 (Z(Z-1)-Z)/R_π^c MeV
- The CSB potential form: V_B = -(t/2)[B_v - B_s (A_0/A)^{1/3}] keV, with A_0=16
- Standard CSB parameters: B_v=300 keV, B_s=200 keV
- Shell-corrected proton radius fit to 634 experimental values yields rmsd ≤ 11 mf (millifermi) with λ=5.6(2), μ=23(1)

## Context
This work extends the analysis of neutron skins from experimental data by Trzcinski et al. (2001) and Kratz et al. (1999), and addresses the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly first identified by Nolen and Schiffer (1969). The approach differs from mean-field calculations (e.g., Dobaczewski et al. 1996, Brown et al. 2000) by using smooth averages that depend only on N and Z, and by constraining the CSB potentials to state-of-the-art values from Machleidt (2001). The results enable future shell-model calculations of mirror energy differences (MED) as undertaken by Zuker et al. (2002), which achieve ≈10 keV accuracy and suggest the anomaly may disappear when proper configuration mixing is included.
