# Cluster properties of heavy nuclei predicted with the Barcelona-Catania-Paris-Madrid energy density functional

> We study the cluster emission properties of 224Ra and 238Pu employing the Barcelona-Catania-Paris-Madrid (BCPM) energy density functional (EDF). Starting from two-dimensional potential energy surfaces, coexisting fission paths are identified. A fission valley located at large octupole deformations, corresponding to a highly-asymmetric mass distribution, is found in both nuclei. As the corresponding fragments are dominated by the presence of 208Pb, we can relate this fission path to the emergence of cluster emission. Using the octupole moment as collective degree of freedom, we compute the cluster decay half-lives and study the impact of collective inertias, pairing strength and collective zero-point energy. The agreement with experimental data resembles the results obtained for spontaneous fission half-lives, indicating the capability of BCPM to consistently describe a large variety of fission phenomena, including cluster emission.

## Key claim

The Barcelona-Catania-Paris-Madrid (BCPM) energy density functional predicts cluster emission half-lives for 224Ra and 238Pu that agree with experimental data, but only when the Gaussian Overlap Approximation to the Generator Coordinate Method (GOA-GCM) collective inertias are used; the adiabatic time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (ATDHFB) scheme overestimates half-lives unless the pairing strength is artificially increased by 10%.

## Method

The authors performed constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the BCPM energy density functional on a two-dimensional grid of quadrupole (Q20) and octupole (Q30) moments for 224Ra and 238Pu. They identified fission paths by following the minimal energy valley on the potential energy surface, then computed cluster decay half-lives using the Wenzel-Kramers-Brillouin (WKB) method with the octupole moment as the collective coordinate, comparing ATDHFB and GOA-GCM prescriptions for collective inertias and zero-point energies.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state quadrupole moment of 224Ra: Q20 = 8 barns
- Ground-state quadrupole moment of 238Pu: Q20 = 14 barns
- Ground-state octupole moment of 224Ra: Q30 = 4 barns^(3/2)
- Inner barrier height for 224Ra: 6.73 MeV
- Inner barrier height for 238Pu: 6.79 MeV
- Fission barrier height for 224Ra along elongated path: 10.76 MeV
- Saddle point octupole moment for cluster decay of 224Ra: Q30 = 26 barns^(3/2)
- Saddle point octupole moment for cluster decay of 238Pu: Q30 = 52 barns^(3/2)
- Cluster decay channels: 224Ra -> 210Pb + 14C; 238Pu -> 208Pb + 30Mg
- Oblate deformation of 30Mg fragment: beta2 = -0.17
- Grid spacing: 2 barns in Q20, 2 barns^(3/2) in Q30

## Context

This work extends previous BCPM functional studies of spontaneous fission (Giuliani 2013, 2014, 2018) to the highly asymmetric fission regime of cluster radioactivity. The approach follows the methodology of Warda (2011) for identifying cluster paths via octupole deformation, and the results are compared to experimental data from Bonetti (2007). The study demonstrates that BCPM can describe both traditional fission and cluster emission within a unified framework, enabling future work on including pairing as a dynamical variable and using non-perturbative collective inertia schemes.
