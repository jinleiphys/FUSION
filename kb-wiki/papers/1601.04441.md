# A simple approach to $α$-decay fine structure

> We propose a simple method to evaluate $α$-transition rates to low-lying excited states in even-even nuclei. For this a realistic $α$-daughter double folding interaction is approximated by a parabola in the region where the decay process takes place. This allows us to evaluate the penetration probability analytically. The main experimental features of branching ratios to excited states are reproduced by this simple approach.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the main experimental features of alpha-decay fine structure (branching ratios to excited states) in even-even nuclei can be reproduced within one order of magnitude by a simple analytical semiclassical method, where the realistic double folding potential is approximated by a parabola in the region relevant for decay.

## Method

The authors approximated the realistic alpha-daughter double folding interaction (M3Y nucleon-nucleon interaction) by a parabola in the spatial region between the inner and outer turning points, allowing analytical evaluation of the action integrals. They applied a spherical semiclassical approximation to compute partial decay widths to yrast states (J=2, 4, 6) in even-even daughter nuclei, using fitted parameters for the parabolic potential that depend on the mass and charge of the daughter nucleus. The total decay width was obtained by summing partial widths, and spectroscopic factors were defined as the ratio of experimental to theoretical widths.

## Key numbers

- The parabolic potential parameters were fitted with standard errors: R0 = 3.24 A^0.234 (sigma = 0.0009), c = 0.97 (2Ze^2/R0) (sigma = 0.0054), a = 1.76 A^0.177 (sigma = 0.0198), where Z and A are the charge and mass number of the daughter nucleus.
- The matching radius Rm between the parabolic and Coulomb parts of the potential was found to be approximately Rm = R0 + 0.3 fm.
- The spherical semiclassical approximation used is known to be 1-2% accurate with respect to the exact solution (Delion 2010).
- The main experimental features of fine structure are reproduced within one order of magnitude, as determined from analysis of hindrance factors.

## Context

This work extends the coupled channels method using double-folding alpha-core potentials (Delion 2015) which reproduces alpha-decay intensities with good accuracy but is computationally involved. The simple analytical approach reproduces the linear increasing trend of intensity I2 with excitation energy E2, as predicted in Delion (2009). The analysis of hindrance factors reveals that for J=2 states the fine structure is well reproduced for neutron numbers N>126, while for N<126 additional effects from deformation and alpha-clustering are needed; for J=4 states a maximum in hindrance factor appears in the region of Pu isotopes, connected to a subshell effect (Buc12).
