# A phenomenological description on an incoherent Fermi liquid near optimal doping in high T_{c} cuprates

> Marginal Fermi-liquid physics near optimal doping in high T_{c} cuprates has been explained within two competing scenarios such as the spin-fluctuation theory based on an itinerant picture and the slave-particle approach based on a localized picture. In this study we propose an alternative scenario for the anomalous transport within the context of the slave-particle approach. Although the marginal Fermi-liquid phenomenology was interpreted previously within deconfinement of the compact gauge theory, referred to as the strange metal phase, we start from confinement, introducing the Polyakov-loop parameter into an SU(2) gauge theory formulation of the t-J model. The Polyakov-loop parameter gives rise to incoherent electrons through the confinement of spinons and holons, which result from huge imaginary parts of self-energy corrections for spinons and holons. This confinement scenario serves a novel mechanism for the marginal Fermi-liquid transport in the respect that the scattering source has nothing to do with symmetry breaking. Furthermore, the incoherent Fermi-liquid state evolves into the Fermi liquid phase through crossover instead of an artificial second-order transition as temperature is lowered, where the crossover phenomenon does not result from the Anderson-Higgs mechanism but originate from an energy scale in the holon sector. We fit an experimental data for the electrical resistivity around the optimal doping and find a reasonable match between our theory and the experiment.

# A phenomenological description on an incoherent Fermi liquid near optimal doping in high T\(_{c}\) cuprates

## Key claim

The authors propose that marginal Fermi-liquid transport near optimal doping in high-T\(_{c}\) cuprates arises from the *confinement* of spinons and holons (introduced via a Polyakov-loop parameter in an SU(2) gauge theory of the t-J model), rather than from the deconfinement-based strange metal phase or from spin-fluctuation theory. This confinement scenario produces incoherent electrons through large imaginary self-energy corrections for the fractionalized particles, and the crossover from non-Fermi liquid to Fermi liquid occurs without an artificial second-order transition, driven instead by an energy scale in the holon sector.

## Method

The authors construct a Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model for the strange metal phase of an SU(2) gauge theory formulation of the t-J model. They introduce the Polyakov-loop parameter \(\Phi = \cos\beta\phi\) as an effective fugacity for fractionalized excitations, with a gauge-sector free energy that drives confinement at low temperatures and deconfinement at high temperatures. Using a Luttinger-Ward (Eliashberg) self-consistent approach, they compute the self-energies of spinons, holons, and confined electrons, and then evaluate the electrical resistivity from the electron self-energy, fitting to experimental data.

## Key numbers

* The electrical resistivity shows a crossover from non-Fermi liquid to Fermi liquid behavior below a temperature scale set by the holon chemical potential \(\mu\); the imaginary part of the electron self-energy is proportional to \(T^{2/3}\) for \(T > |\mu|\) (from scattering with \(z=3\) dissipative holon modes), while Fermi-liquid \(\omega^{2}\) behavior is recovered for \(T < |\mu|\).
* The Polyakov-loop parameter \(\Phi\) vanishes for \(T < T_{CD}\) (confinement-deconfinement crossover temperature) and becomes condensed (\(\Phi \to 1\)) for \(T > T_{CD}\), where \(T_{CD} < T_{0}\) and \(T_{0}\) is the confinement-deconfinement transition temperature of the pure gauge sector.
* The holon chemical potential \(\mu\) is negative and much larger in magnitude in the confinement phase than in the deconfinement phase.
* The spinon band mass is \(m_{\psi} \propto 1/J\) and the holon band mass is \(m_{h} \propto 1/t\); the ratio \(\alpha\) of the electron band mass to the spinon band mass is approximately unity.

## Context

This work extends the slave-particle approach to the marginal Fermi-liquid phenomenology of high-T\(_{c}\) cuprates, which was previously interpreted within deconfinement of compact gauge theory (the strange metal phase) by Lee, Nagaosa, and Wen (2006) and within spin-fluctuation theory near an itinerant antiferromagnetic quantum critical point by Chubukov, Pines, and Schmalian (2003). The authors contrast their confinement mechanism with the earlier spinon-holon binding study by T. K. Ng (2004), noting that in their formulation the fractionalized particles are unstable for all energy scales due to the Polyakov-loop parameter, rather than being well-defined above a binding energy. The work enables a description of the crossover from non-Fermi liquid to Fermi liquid without the artificial finite-temperature transition that arises from the Anderson-Higgs mechanism in the deconfinement scenario, and it provides a framework for fitting experimental resistivity data near optimal doping.
