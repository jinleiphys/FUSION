# Understanding the energy dependence of $B_2$ in heavy ion collisions: Interplay of volume and space-momentum correlations

> The deuteron coalescence parameter $B_2$ in proton+proton and nucleus+nucleus collisions in the energy range of $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 900 - 7000 GeV for proton+proton and $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 2 - 2760 GeV for nucleus+nucleus collisions is analyzed with the Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport model, supplemented by an event-by-event phase space coalescence model for deuteron and anti-deuteron production. The results are compared to data by the E866, E877, PHENIX, STAR and ALICE experiments. The $B_2$ values are calculated from the final spectra of protons and deuterons. At lower energies, $\sqrt{s_{NN}}\leq 20$ GeV, $B_2$ drops drastically with increasing energy. The calculations confirm that this is due to the increasing freeze-out volume reflected in $B_2\sim 1/V$. At higher energies, $\sqrt{s_{NN}}\geq 20$ GeV, $B_2$ saturates at a constant level. This qualitative change and the vanishing of the volume suppression is shown to be due to the development of strong radial flow with increasing energy. The flow leads to strong space-momentum correlations which counteract the volume effect.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the energy dependence of the deuteron coalescence parameter \(B_2\) in nucleus-nucleus collisions results from two competing effects: at low energies (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \leq 20\) GeV) the decrease of \(B_2\) is driven by increasing freeze-out volume (\(B_2 \sim 1/V\)), while at higher energies (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \geq 20\) GeV) the development of strong radial flow creates space-momentum correlations that counteract the volume suppression, causing \(B_2\) to saturate at a constant level.

## Method

The authors used the Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport model (version 3.4) in both Boltzmann mode and hybrid mode (with an intermediate ideal hydrodynamic stage) to simulate proton+proton and nucleus+nucleus collisions. Deuteron and anti-deuteron production was modeled via an event-by-event phase space coalescence approach, where a proton and neutron form a deuteron if their relative distance in space and momentum space is sufficiently small (using \(\Delta p_{max} = 0.285\) GeV/c and \(\Delta r_{max} = 3.575\) fm). The coalescence parameter \(B_2\) was calculated from the final invariant momentum spectra of protons and deuterons, and results were compared to experimental data from E866, E877, PHENIX, STAR, and ALICE.

## Key numbers

- \(B_2\) drops drastically with increasing energy for \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \leq 20\) GeV in Au+Au collisions, then saturates at a constant level for \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \geq 20\) GeV.
- In proton+proton collisions, \(B_2\) was studied at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 0.9\) TeV, 2.76 TeV, and 7 TeV; at 0.9 TeV, \(B_2\) shows a strong increase with \(p_T/A\), while this increase is only mild at the two higher energies.
- For Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV, \(B_2\) at \(p_T/A = 1.05\) GeV/c decreases strongly with increasing charged-particle multiplicity (centrality).
- The coalescence parameters used in the full phase space coalescence model are \(\Delta p_{max} = 0.285\) GeV/c and \(\Delta r_{max} = 3.575\) fm.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of deuteron coalescence in heavy ion collisions by providing a systematic explanation of the non-monotonic energy dependence of \(B_2\) across the RHIC Beam Energy Scan and LHC energy regimes. The results confirm the volume scaling (\(B_2 \sim 1/V\)) expected from thermal models and earlier coalescence approaches (e.g., Scheibl (1998), Monreal (1999)), but show that this simple picture breaks down at high energies due to radial flow. The finding that space-momentum correlations from hydrodynamic flow create a plateau in \(B_2\) explains the difference between cascade-only and hybrid (hydrodynamic) simulations, and enables future studies to use \(B_2\) as a probe of both source volume and flow strength in heavy ion collisions.
