# Some recent progress on quark pairings in dense quark and nuclear matter

> We give a brief overview on some recent progress in quark pairings in dense quark/nuclear matter mostly developed in the past five years. We focus on following aspects in particular: the BCS-BEC crossover in the CSC phase, the baryon formation and dissociation in dense quark/nuclear matter, the Ginzburg-Landau theory for three-flavor dense matter with $U_{A}$(1) anomaly, and the collective and Nambu-Goldstone modes for the spin-one CSC.

## Key claim

This review paper surveys recent progress on quark pairings in dense quark and nuclear matter, focusing on four specific aspects developed mostly in the past five years: the BCS-BEC crossover in color superconductivity, baryon formation and dissociation, Ginzburg-Landau theory with the U_A(1) anomaly, and collective modes for spin-one color superconductivity. The paper is a review and does not present a single original claim with a hedge or qualifier beyond what is stated in the abstract.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical approaches including the boson-fermion model and NJL-type models to study BCS-BEC crossover in color superconducting phases, with random phase approximation and Dyson-Schwinger equations used to obtain propagating modes of diquark pairings. Baryon formation and dissociation are studied by regarding a baryon as composed of a quark and a diquark in the NJL-type model, while a nonlocal extended NJL model provides momentum-dependent constituent quark masses with automatically convergent bubble diagrams. Collective modes in spin-one color superconductivity are analyzed using the Ginzburg-Landau approach.

## Key numbers

- For the mean-field BCS-BEC crossover calculation at zero temperature: fermion mass m = 0.2 GeV, coupling g = 4, momentum cutoff Λ = 1 GeV; the crossover parameter x is varied from negative (BCS side) to positive (BEC side) values
- For the 2PI calculation at finite temperature: T = 0.14 GeV, m = 0.28 GeV, g = 1.8, Λ = 1 GeV; the bare boson mass m_b is varied to study the crossover
- For the magnetic field study: momentum cutoff Λ = 1 GeV, fermion mass m = 0.2Λ, bare boson mass m_b = 0.8 GeV; the magnetic field B is varied and results are shown as functions of ln(qB/m²)
- In the magnetic field study at weak field (BEC regime), the gap Δ is approximately 73 MeV

## Context

This review extends the line of work on color superconductivity first proposed by Barrois (1977) and Bailin and Love (1983), and further developed by many others including Rajagopal (2000), Alford (2001), and Rischke (2003). The BEC-BCS crossover in color superconductivity was first studied by Nishida (2005) and Abuki (2006) with the NJL model. The paper enables future studies of quark pairing phenomena in neutron star cores and heavy ion collision experiments, particularly under the extremely strong magnetic fields expected in those environments.
