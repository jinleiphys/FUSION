# RG generated fermion mass

> Chiral symmetry breaking in a purely fermionic theory is investigated by the help of the renormalization group method. The RG equation for the running mass $m_k$ admits a solution with vanishing bare mass and finite physical mass. The running Fermi coupling constant, $G_k$, converges to a finite (renormalized) physical value. It is also shown that the RG equation for $\tilde G$, the dimensionless Fermi coupling, has an UV fixed point $\tilde G_{UV}$. Contrary to a previous result however, it is proven that the chiral symmetry breaking point $\tilde G_c$ does not coincide with $\tilde G_{UV}$.

## Key claim

The renormalization group equation for the running mass in a purely fermionic theory admits a solution with vanishing bare mass and finite physical mass, breaking chiral symmetry dynamically; contrary to a previous result by Miransky, the chiral symmetry breaking point \(\tilde G_c\) does **not** coincide with the UV fixed point \(\tilde G_{UV}\).

## Method

The author applies the Wegner-Houghton renormalization group in the Local Potential Approximation (LPA) to a four-dimensional fermionic model with discrete \(\gamma_5\) symmetry, truncating the potential to the quadrifermionic interaction term. The resulting flow equations for the dimensionless running mass \(\tilde m_t\) and dimensionless Fermi coupling \(\tilde G_t\) are solved analytically in asymptotic UV and IR regions and numerically for the full system, with boundary conditions chosen at a UV scale.

## Key numbers

* The UV fixed point of the dimensionless Fermi coupling is \(\tilde G_{UV} = 8\pi^2\).
* The critical value \(\tilde G_c\) for chiral symmetry breaking lies in the range \([2\pi^2, \frac{8\pi^2}{3}]\); the author suggests \(\tilde G_c = \frac{8\pi^2}{3}\).
* For \(\tilde G = 8\pi^2\), the UV behavior of the running mass is \(m_k \sim 1/k^3\); for \(\tilde G = 4\pi^2\), \(m_k \sim 1/k^2\); for \(\tilde G = \frac{8\pi^2}{3}\), \(m_k \sim 1/k\).
* The UV scaling of the dimensionful Fermi coupling is \(G_k \sim 8\pi^2 / k^2\).
* Numerical boundary values used: \(m_k = 10^{-6}\) and \(G_k = 7.895 \times 10^{-5}\) at \(k = 10^3\).

## Context

This work extends the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) approach (Nambu and Jona-Lasinio, 1961) by using a renormalization group method rather than mean-field approximation, allowing a momentum-dependent mass function and a continuum limit. It directly contradicts the interpretation of Miransky (1985) that the chiral symmetry breaking point coincides with the UV fixed point in the quadrifermionic theory; the author argues that phase diagrams and models based on that interpretation must be reconsidered. The results enable non-perturbative renormalization group studies of fermionic mass generation in four dimensions.
