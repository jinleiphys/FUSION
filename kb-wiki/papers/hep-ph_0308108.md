# Fragmentation functions of mesons in the Field-Feynman model

> The fragmentation functions of the pion with distinction between $D_{u}^{π^{+}}$, $D_{d}^{π^{+}}$, and $D_{s}^{π^{+}}$ are studied in the Field-Feynman recursive model, by taking into account the flavor structure in the excitation of quark-antiquark pairs by the initial quarks. The obtained analytical results are compatible with available empirical results. The framework is also extended to predict the fragmentation functions of the kaon with distinction between $D_{\bar{s}}^{K^{+}}(z)$, $D_{u}^{K^{+}}(z)$, $D_{s}^{K^{+}}(z)$, and $D_{d}^{K^{+}}(z)$. This work gives a significant modification of the original model, and the predictions can be tested by future experiments on the fragmentation functions of the kaon.

## Key claim

By introducing flavor-dependent parameters for the excitation of quark-antiquark pairs by initial light-flavor (u or d) and strange (s) quarks, the authors obtain analytical fragmentation functions for pions and kaons that are compatible with empirical results, but only in the domain z > 0.3; the model does not describe the small-z region where sea quark effects inside the meson become important.

## Method

The authors modify the Field-Feynman recursive model by assigning distinct probabilities β_u and β_s for the excitation of u\bar{u} or d\bar{d} pairs by an initial light-flavor quark versus an initial strange quark, while maintaining SU(2) symmetry (β_{iu}=β_{id}=β_i). They fit β_u and the exponent d of the momentum-sharing function f(z)=(d+1)z^d to the Kretzer-Leader-Christova (KLC) parametrization of pion fragmentation functions extracted from e+e- and semi-inclusive DIS data, then fix β_s by fitting D_s^{π+}. The same three parameters are applied without modification to predict four distinct kaon fragmentation functions.

## Key numbers

- β_u = 0.46: probability that a u or d quark excites a u\bar{u} or d\bar{d} pair (fitted from D_d^{π+}/D_u^{π+} ratio in z ∈ (0.3,1))
- d = 1.45: exponent in the momentum-sharing function f(z) = (d+1)z^d (fitted from D_u^{π+} - D_d^{π+})
- β_s = 0.38: probability that an s quark excites a u\bar{u} or d\bar{d} pair (fitted from D_s^{π+})
- Analytical result for D_u^{π+}(z): 1.127 (0.54 + 0.46/z) (1-z)^{1.45}
- Analytical result for D_d^{π+}(z): 0.51842 z^{-1} (1-z)^{2.45}
- Analytical result for D_s^{π+}(z): 0.35378 z^{-1} (1-z)^{2.45}
- β_{ss} = 1 - 2β_s = 0.24: fraction of s\bar{s} pairs excited by an s quark
- β_{us} = 1 - 2β_u = 0.08: fraction of s\bar{s} pairs excited by a u or d quark

## Context

This work extends the original Field-Feynman model (Field and Feynman, 1978), which assumed β_u = β_s and therefore predicted D_d^{π+} = D_s^{π+}, by incorporating flavor-dependent sea-quark excitation probabilities. The results are compatible with the KLC parametrization (Kretzer, Leader, and Christova, 2001) that extracted a complete set of pion fragmentation functions from HERMES SIDIS data and e+e- data without assuming a relation between favored and unfavored transitions. The framework provides testable predictions for kaon fragmentation functions that distinguish four separate channels (D_{\bar{s}}^{K+}, D_u^{K+}, D_s^{K+}, D_d^{K+}), which can be checked by future semi-inclusive kaon production experiments at HERMES or CLAS.
