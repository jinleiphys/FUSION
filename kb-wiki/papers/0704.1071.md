# Bulk viscosity of superfluid neutron stars

> The hydrodynamics, describing dynamical effects in superfluid neutron stars, essentially differs from the standard one-fluid hydrodynamics. In particular, we have four bulk viscosity coefficients in the theory instead of one. In this paper we calculate these coefficients, for the first time, assuming they are due to non-equilibrium beta-processes (such as modified or direct Urca process). The results of our analysis are used to estimate characteristic damping times of sound waves in superfluid neutron stars. It is demonstrated that all four bulk viscosity coefficients lead to comparable dissipation of sound waves and should be considered on the same footing.

## Key claim

This paper calculates, for the first time, all four bulk viscosity coefficients that arise in the relativistic hydrodynamics of superfluid neutron-star matter, rather than the single coefficient assumed in prior work; the author demonstrates that all four coefficients lead to comparable dissipation of sound waves and must be considered together, but the calculation is restricted to the simplest npe (neutron-proton-electron) matter and to cases where the difference between superfluid and normal velocities is small.

## Method

The author extends the relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics of Son (2001) to superfluid mixtures, deriving the general form of dissipative corrections to the energy-momentum tensor and the superfluid four-velocity from the entropy generation equation. The four bulk viscosity coefficients are then calculated by assuming they arise from non-equilibrium beta-processes (direct or modified Urca processes) in npe-matter, using the linearized response function ΔΓ = λ δμ for the net electron generation rate, with the function λ taken from Haensel, Levenfish, and Yakovlev (2000, 2001). The quasineutrality condition n_e = n_p and the constraint ∂_μ[Y_{pk} w^μ_{(k)}] = 0 reduce the number of independent bulk viscosity coefficients that contribute to dissipation to four: ξ_{1n}, ξ_2, ξ_{3n}, and ξ_{4n}.

## Key numbers

- The condition for neglecting the dependence of ΔΓ on superfluid-normal velocity differences is |ΔV_n| ≪ 0.01 c (T/10^9 K)^{1/2}, where c is the speed of light and T is temperature.
- The critical velocity for breakdown of neutron superfluidity (Landau criterion) is |ΔV_cr| ~ Δ_n / p_Fn ≈ 0.0003 c (T_{cn}/10^9 K) (n_0/n_n)^{1/3}, where n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3} is the saturation density of nuclear matter.
- The condition for the validity of the calculation is that the superfluid-normal velocity difference satisfies |ΔV_n| ≪ |ΔV_cr|, which is justified at not very low temperatures when neutrons are superfluid.

## Context

This work extends the single-coefficient bulk viscosity calculations for non-superfluid neutron-star matter (e.g., Sawyer 1989, Haensel et al. 2000-2002) to the superfluid case, where four coefficients are required by the theory of Khalatnikov (1952, 1989) and Landau and Lifshitz (1987). It enables more accurate modeling of damping times for sound waves and gravitational-wave instability windows in superfluid neutron stars, and provides a framework that can be generalized to matter with muons or hyperons.
