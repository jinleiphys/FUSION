# Two flavor QCD and Confinement

> We argue that the order of the chiral transition for N_f=2 is a sensitive probe of the QCD vacuum, in particular of the mechanism of color confinement. A strategy is developed to investigate the order of the transition by use of finite size scaling analysis. An in-depth numerical investigation is performed with staggered fermions on lattices with N_t=4 and N_s=12,16,20,24,32 and quark masses am_q ranging from 0.01335 to 0.307036. The specific heat and a number of susceptibilities are measured and compared with the expectations of an O(4) second order and of a first order phase transition. A second order transition in the O(4) and O(2) universality classes are excluded. Substantial evidence emerges for a first order transition. A detailed comparison with previous works is performed.

## Key claim

The order of the chiral transition for two-flavor QCD is a sensitive probe of the confinement mechanism; the authors argue that if confinement is an absolute property of the QCD vacuum, the transition must be first order, and they present substantial evidence for a first order transition, excluding second order transitions in the O(4) and O(2) universality classes.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations using the standard staggered fermion action with the Hybrid R algorithm on lattices with temporal extent N_t=4 and spatial sizes N_s=12, 16, 20, 24, 32, at quark masses am_q ranging from 0.01335 to 0.307036. They measured the specific heat and multiple susceptibilities and applied finite size scaling analysis, including a novel strategy that fixes am_q L_s^{y_h} constant to reduce the two-scale problem to a one-scale test of O(4) or O(2) critical behavior.

## Key numbers

- For the first order transition fit, the critical coupling beta_0 = 5.2484(5) and the coefficient k_m = 1.82(8)
- For the O(4) fit, beta_0 = 5.2435(25) and k_m = 1.13(19)
- Lattice sizes used: N_s = 12, 16, 20, 24, 32 with N_t = 4
- Quark masses am_q ranged from 0.01335 to 0.307036
- The two fixed-parameter runs used am_q L_s^{y_h} = 74.7 (Run1) and 149.4 (Run2) with y_h = 2.49
- The product a L_s * m_pi ranged from 4.5 to 15.8 across the runs
- The connected component of the chiral susceptibility contributed less than about 15% of the disconnected component at the peak

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies by the JLQCD collaboration, the MILC collaboration, the Bielefeld group, and others (Fukugita et al., Karsch et al., Columbia group) that had found no clear sign of discontinuity but also no conclusive agreement with O(4) scaling. The authors' large-scale numerical effort with multiple lattice sizes and a novel fixed-scaling-variable strategy provides evidence against O(4) and O(2) second order transitions, supporting a first order transition that would imply no tricritical point in the T-mu plane, contrary to proposals for heavy ion collision experiments (Stephanov, 2004).
