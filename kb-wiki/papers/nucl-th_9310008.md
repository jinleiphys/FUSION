# The high-frequency finite-temperature quark dispersion relation

> I calculate the dispersion relation for quarks of mass $m$ and momentum $k$ in a quark gluon plasma at temperature $T$, in the limit $m^2+k^2 \gg (gT)^2$, where $g$ is the strong coupling constant. I find three contributions to the dispersion relation: one that depends on $T$ but not $m$ or $k$, one that depends on $m$ and $T$ but not $k$, and third contribution that depends on all three (and is opposite in sign to the other two).

## Key claim

The paper calculates the high-frequency quark dispersion relation in a quark-gluon plasma to order \(g^2\), valid in the limit \(m^2+k^2 \gg (gT)^2\), finding three contributions to the thermal mass correction: one depending only on \(T\), one depending on \(m\) and \(T\) but not \(k\), and a third depending on all three that is opposite in sign to the other two.

## Method

The author computes the one-loop quark self-energy \(\Sigma(K)\) in the plasma rest frame, using the decomposition \(\Sigma(K) = -a\not\!K - b\not\!u - cm\) and extracting the coefficients from traces of the self-energy. The dispersion relation is then derived from the poles of the quark propagator, with the self-energy projections taken from Petitgirard (1992) and simplified by setting \(K^2=m^2\) in the logarithms to obtain the leading-order thermal correction \(\delta\).

## Key numbers

- The thermal mass correction is \(\delta = g^2 C_F \left[ \left( \frac{1}{6} + q(\frac{m}{T}) \right) T^2 + r(\frac{m}{T}, \frac{k}{T}) m^2 \right]\), where \(q>0\) and \(r<0\).
- For \(m/T \ll 1\) and low quark density (\(-\mu/T \gg 1\)): \(q_\infty(\frac{m}{T}) = \frac{1}{\pi^2} \left(1 + \frac{m^2}{2T^2} \ln(\frac{m}{T}) + \mathcal{O}[\frac{m^2}{T^2}]\right)\).
- For \(m/T \ll 1\): \(\overline{r}_\infty(\frac{m}{T}) = \frac{-1}{2\pi^2} \left(1 - \frac{m^2}{T^2} \ln^2(\frac{m}{T}) + \mathcal{O}[\frac{m^2}{T^2} \ln(\frac{m}{T})]\right)\).
- For \(m/T \gg 1\): \(q_\infty(\frac{m}{T}) = \left(\frac{m}{2\pi^3 T}\right)^{1/2} e^{-m/T} \left(1 + \frac{3T}{8m} + \mathcal{O}[\frac{T^2}{m^2}]\right)\).
- For \(m/T \gg 1\): \(\overline{r}_\infty(\frac{m}{T}) = -\left(\frac{T}{8\pi^3 m}\right)^{1/2} e^{-m/T} \left(1 - \frac{19T}{8m} + \mathcal{O}[\frac{T^2}{m^2}]\right)\).

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of thermal dispersion relations for massive quarks by Levinson and Boal (1985), Toimela (1986), and Blaizot and Ollitrault (1993), which focused on the low-frequency regime. The high-frequency limit is motivated by regulating divergent production rates in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions, where the relevant quark momenta are of order \((T^2+mT)^{1/2}\) rather than \(gT\). The results enable a smooth interpolation between massless and massive quark production rates, particularly for the chemically under-saturated conditions typical of early-stage nuclear collisions.
