# The phonon-coupling model for Skyrme forces

> A short review on the self-consistent RPA based on a energy-density functional of the Skyrme type is given. We also present an extension of the RPA where the coupling of phonons to the single-particle states is considered. Within this approach we present numerical results which are compared with data. The self-consistent approach is compared with the Landau-Migdal theory. Here we derive from the self-consistent $ph$ interaction, the Landau-Migdal parameters as well as their density dependence. In the Appendix a new derivation of the reduced matrix elements of the $ph$ interaction is presented.

# The phonon-coupling model for Skyrme forces

## Key claim

The self-consistent time blocking approximation (TBA) that includes phonon coupling to single-particle states improves the description of giant resonance strength distributions compared to RPA alone, but a single Skyrme parameter set that simultaneously reproduces the giant isovector dipole resonance (GDR), isoscalar giant monopole resonance (GMR), and isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance (GQR) in 208Pb, the Ca isotopes, and 16O is still missing.

## Method

The authors start from a Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) energy-density functional and derive the residual particle-hole interaction fully self-consistently via functional derivatives of the density functional. They solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the response function at both the RPA level and the TBA level, where the TBA extends RPA by including 1p1h⊗phonon configurations through an induced interaction with a subtraction scheme to avoid double counting. The calculations use a discrete basis with box boundary conditions and a continuum representation for the free 1p1h propagator, and the phonons are taken from RPA solutions that exhaust more than 20% of the total B(EL) strength.

## Key numbers

- Seven Skyrme parametrizations are used, with incompressibility K ranging from 218 MeV (SV-K218) to 255 MeV (SV-m56k6), effective mass m*/m from 0.56 (SV-m56k6) to 0.90 (SV-bas, SV-kap00, SV-mas07, SV-sym34, SV-K218), symmetry energy a_sym from 27 MeV (SV-m64k6, SV-m56k6) to 34 MeV (SV-sym34), and TRK sum rule enhancement κ_TRK from 0.0 (SV-kap00) to 0.6 (SV-m64k6, SV-m56k6)
- The box sizes are 15 fm for 16O and 40,48Ca, and 18 fm for 208Pb
- The cutoff for the 1p1h space is 100 MeV for all nuclei
- Spectral smoothing parameter Δ = 400 keV is used for the photo-absorption strength in 208Pb
- The experimental GDR in 48Ca is centered at 20.2 MeV
- The standard Landau-Migdal scaling factor C0 = 150 MeV·fm³ (for effective mass m*/m = 1)

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent RPA approach based on Skyrme energy-density functionals (Bender 2003, Stone 2007, Erler 2011) by incorporating phonon coupling via the time blocking approximation (TBA) developed by Tselyaev (1989) and Kamerdzhiev (2004). The authors compare their self-consistent approach with the Landau-Migdal theory (Migdal 1967) and derive Landau-Migdal parameters from the Skyrme residual interaction. The paper uses the systematic variation of nuclear matter properties introduced by Kluepfel (2009) to explore sensitivity of phonon coupling to the functional, and the parametrizations SV-m64k6 and SV-m56k6 from Lyutorovich (2012) that were designed to describe the GDR in both 16O and 208Pb simultaneously. The subtraction method for TBA (Tselyaev 2013) is employed to eliminate double counting and ensure stability. The results enable future systematic studies of giant resonances across the nuclear chart with fully self-consistent beyond-RPA methods, though a universal parameter set remains to be found.
