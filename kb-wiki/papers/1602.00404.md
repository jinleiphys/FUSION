# Equations of motion of test particles for solving the spin-dependent Boltzmann-Vlasov equation

> A consistent derivation of the equations of motion (EOMs) of test particles for solving the spin-dependent Boltzmann-Vlasov equation is presented. The resulting EOMs in phase space are similar to the canonical equations in Hamiltonian dynamics, and the EOM of spin is the same as that in the Heisenburg picture of quantum mechanics. Considering further the quantum nature of spin and choosing the direction of total angular momentum in heavy-ion reactions as a reference of measuring nucleon spin, the EOMs of spin-up and spin-down nucleons are given separately. The key elements affecting the spin dynamics in heavy-ion collisions are identified. The resulting EOMs provide a solid foundation for using the test-particle approach in studying spin dynamics in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies. Future comparisons of model simulations with experimental data will help constrain the poorly known in-medium nucleon spin-orbit coupling relevant for understanding properties of rare isotopes and their astrophysical impacts.

## Key claim

The paper provides a consistent derivation of equations of motion (EOMs) for test particles to solve the spin-dependent Boltzmann-Vlasov equation, but the derivation assumes that the unit vector \(\vec{n}\) representing the spin direction is independent of \(\vec{r}\) and \(\vec{p}\), which is valid only if \(\vec{n}\) evolves much faster than the phase-space coordinates or if \(\vec{n}\) is a global constant.

## Method

The authors start from the spin-dependent Boltzmann-Vlasov equation expressed in terms of scalar and vector Wigner functions, then decompose the vector part \(\vec{g}(\vec{r},\vec{p})\) as \(\vec{n} f_1(\vec{r},\vec{p})\) under the assumption that \(\vec{n}\) is independent of position and momentum. By separating the equations for spin-up and spin-down distributions and applying the test-particle method, they derive separate EOMs for the phase-space coordinates \(\vec{R}\) and \(\vec{P}\) and for the spin direction \(\vec{n}\). For practical application to heavy-ion collisions, they fix \(\vec{n} = \hat{y}\) (the direction of total angular momentum perpendicular to the reaction plane) and obtain separate EOMs for spin-up and spin-down nucleons.

## Key numbers

- The spin-orbit coupling strength parameter \(W_0\) appears in the Skyrme-type effective two-body interaction used for the spin-dependent single-particle Hamiltonian, with terms proportional to \(-\frac{1}{2}W_0\) in Eqs. (h1) through (h4).
- The spin precession equation is \(\partial \vec{n}/\partial t = 2(\vec{h}_2 + \vec{h}_3) \times \vec{n} / \hbar\), where \(\hbar\) is the reduced Planck constant.
- The test-particle representation uses \(N_{TP}\) test particles per nucleon, with densities expressed as sums over test particles weighted by \(1/N_{TP}\).

## Context

This work extends the test-particle method (Wong, 1982; Bertsch and Das Gupta, 1984) from spin-independent Boltzmann-Vlasov equations to the spin-dependent case, providing a foundation for simulating spin transport in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies. The derivation is analogous to Method I used in solid-state physics for electron spin transport (Sinitsyn, 2004; Schliemann and Loss, 2006; Son and Niu, 2010), but with nuclear-specific interactions. The resulting EOMs enable future comparisons of model simulations with experimental data to constrain the poorly known in-medium nucleon spin-orbit coupling, which is relevant for understanding properties of rare isotopes and their astrophysical impacts.
