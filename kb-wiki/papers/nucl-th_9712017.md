# Strange Particles in Dense Matter and Kaon Condensates

> We discuss the role of strangeness in dense matter and especially in neutron stars. The early (in density) introduction of hyperons found in many calculations is probably delayed by the decrease in vector mean field acting on the neutron. The decrease results from both conventional many-body rescattering effects and from the movement towards asymptotic freedom at high densities. Subthreshold $K^-$-meson production by the KaoS collaboration at GSI shows that the $K^-$-mass must be substantially lowered, by $\gtrsim$ 200 MeV at $ρ\sim 2ρ_0$. It is shown that explicit chiral symmetry breaking through the kaon mass may be responsible for $Σ^-$-nucleon and $Ξ^-$-nucleon scalar attraction being weaker than obtained by simple quark scaling. The normal mode of the strangeness minus, charge $e^-$, excitation is constructed as a linear combination of $K^-$-meson and $Σ^-$, neutron-hole state. Except for zero momentum, where the terms are unmixed the "kaesobar" is a linear combination of these two components.

## Key claim

The early introduction of hyperons in dense neutron-star matter is probably delayed to higher densities than previously thought, because the vector mean field acting on the neutron is reduced by both conventional many-body rescattering effects and by the movement towards asymptotic freedom at high densities; the most favorable density for hyperons is therefore around three times nuclear saturation density.

## Method

The authors construct a model of dense matter using hadronic degrees of freedom, incorporating vector meson exchange (ω, ρ) with SU(3) coupling relations and scalar interactions from correlated ππ and K̄K exchange. They analyze the normal mode of strangeness-minus, charge e⁻ excitations by diagonalizing the coupled K⁻-meson and Σ⁻-particle–neutron-hole states (the "kaesobar"), and compare their results to subthreshold K⁻ production data from the KaoS collaboration at GSI and to heavy-ion flow data from Au+Au collisions.

## Key numbers

- The K⁻ mass must be lowered by ≥ 200 MeV at density ρ ∼ 2ρ₀, as shown by subthreshold K⁻ production in Ni+Ni collisions at 1.8 A GeV.
- At ρ ∼ 3ρ₀, the neutron Fermi momentum is k_Fn ∼ 500 MeV and the neutron effective mass is m_n^* ∼ 200–300 MeV.
- The vector mean field from Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations is reduced by a factor ∼ 0.6 at ρ ∼ 3ρ₀ compared to linear extrapolation from ρ₀.
- The vector mean field from heavy-ion flow analysis is V = 460 MeV at ρ ∼ 3ρ₀ (Sahu and Cassing), about 10% below the DBHF value.
- The electron chemical potential at ρ ∼ 3ρ₀ is μ_e ∼ 210 MeV.
- The effective σ coupling strengths from the Jülich group are: NN = 5.87, NΛ = 2.82, NΣ = 2.58, NΞ = 1.19 (full model); with K̄K exchange removed and kaon mass set to zero, the values become: NN = 6.52, NΛ = 3.33, NΣ = 3.34, NΞ = 1.61.
- The ratio of K⁺/K⁻ production changes by an order of magnitude due to medium effects in Ni+Ni collisions.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of strangeness in dense matter from earlier quark-matter approaches (Bethe, Brown and Cooperstein) and from the Kaplan-Nelson (1986) prediction of kaon condensation, while addressing the competition between hyperon introduction and kaon condensation raised by recent authors. The paper uses the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations of the Jülich group for nucleon-hyperon interactions and the KaoS collaboration data to constrain in-medium kaon masses. It enables future experimental tests via subthreshold K⁻ production in Au+Au collisions at GSI and provides a framework for understanding the kaesobar as the relevant normal mode in neutron stars at temperatures T ∼ 50 MeV.
