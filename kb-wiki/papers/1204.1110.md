# Moving Multi-Channel Systems in a Finite Volume with Application to Proton-Proton Fusion

> The spectrum of a system with multiple channels composed of two hadrons with nonzero total momentum is determined in a finite cubic volume with periodic boundary conditions using effective field theory methods. The results presented are accurate up to exponentially suppressed corrections in the volume due to the finite range of hadronic interactions. The formalism allows one to determine the phase shifts and mixing parameters of pipi-KK isosinglet coupled channels directly from Lattice Quantum Chromodynamics. We show that the extension to more than two channels is straightforward and present the result for three channels. From the energy quantization condition, the volume dependence of electroweak matrix elements of two-hadron processes is extracted. In the non-relativistic case, we pay close attention to processes that mix the 1S0-3S1 two-nucleon states, e.g. proton-proton fusion (pp -> d+ e^+ + nu_e), and show how to determine the transition amplitude of such processes directly from lattice QCD.

## Key claim

The paper presents a model-independent, fully relativistic framework for determining the finite-volume spectrum of multi-channel two-hadron systems with nonzero total momentum, accurate up to exponentially suppressed volume corrections, and extends the formalism to extract electroweak transition amplitudes such as those needed for proton-proton fusion directly from lattice QCD.

## Method

The authors derive the quantization condition for N arbitrarily strongly coupled two-body channels in a moving frame by solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation, upgrading the kernel and two-particle propagators to matrices in the space of open channels and partial waves. For the non-relativistic case, they apply degenerate perturbation theory to processes mixing the 1S0-3S1 two-nucleon states, using a pionless effective field theory (EFT(π̸)) to relate finite-volume weak matrix elements to infinite-volume low-energy coefficients parametrizing one-body and two-body weak axial-vector currents.

## Context

This work generalizes the single-channel finite-volume formalism of Lüscher (1991) and the moving-frame extension by Rummukainen and Gottlieb (1995) and Kim, Sachrajda, and Sharpe (2005) to multi-channel systems with arbitrary total momentum. It extends the coupled-channel quantum mechanical approach of Liu et al. (2005) and the effective field theory method of Lage, Meißner, and Rusetsky (2009) to a fully relativistic framework, and generalizes the Lellouch-Lüscher factor (2001) for electroweak matrix elements to moving frames and multi-channel systems. The formalism enables model-independent lattice QCD calculations of resonances such as f₀(980) and a₀(980) in the ππ-KK system, and of weak processes like proton-proton fusion (pp → d e⁺ ν_e) and hyperon decays, providing a path to determine scattering phase shifts, mixing parameters, and transition amplitudes directly from the underlying theory.
