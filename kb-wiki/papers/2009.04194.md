# Spectral functions from the real-time functional renormalization group

> We employ the functional renormalization group approach formulated on the Schwinger-Keldysh contour to calculate real-time correlation functions in scalar field theories. We provide a detailed description of the formalism, discuss suitable truncation schemes for real-time calculations as well as the numerical procedure to self-consistently solve the flow equations for the spectral function. Subsequently, we discuss the relations to other perturbative and non-perturbative approaches to calculate spectral functions, and present a detailed comparison and benchmark in $d=0+1$ dimensions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the real-time functional renormalization group (FRG) formulated on the Schwinger-Keldysh contour can calculate spectral functions in scalar field theories without requiring analytic continuation, but only if the truncation scheme goes beyond the local potential approximation (LPA) to include momentum-dependent four-point functions, because LPA alone cannot generate broadening of the spectral function in the absence of spontaneous symmetry breaking.

## Method

The authors formulate the FRG on the Schwinger-Keldysh contour for an O(N) scalar field theory in d spatial dimensions, introducing a regulator scheme that respects time-ordering and causality. They derive flow equations for the effective action and n-point correlation functions, then develop a truncation based on a vertex expansion that includes momentum-dependent four-point functions, making the propagators two-loop complete. The flow equations are solved self-consistently for the spectral function in d=0+1 dimensions, and results are benchmarked against non-perturbative classical-statistical real-time lattice simulations.

## Key numbers

- The free retarded propagator in momentum space is given by G^{R,ab}_0(ω,p) = -1/(ω² - E_p² + i(γ/β)ω) δ^{ab}, where E_p = √(p² + m²)
- The classical-statistical effective distribution is n_eff^cl(ω) = 1/(βω), corresponding to the Rayleigh-Jeans distribution
- The quantum effective distribution is n_eff^qu(ω) = (ħ/2) coth(ħβω/2)
- The regulator function for the 0+1 dimensional case study is chosen as μ_k(ω) = k² - ω² and γ_k(ω) = (γ/(β m²)) k(k - |ω|)

## Context

This work extends the analytically continued FRG approach previously applied to low-energy effective models of QCD by Tripolt (2013), Pawlowski (2015), and Jung (2016), but performs calculations directly in Minkowski space without analytic continuation. The method enables comparison with classical-statistical real-time lattice simulations by Aarts (2001) and Berges (2009), providing a benchmark for the quality of truncation approximations. The approach is applicable to both quantum and classical-statistical field theories, and the results in 0+1 dimensions serve as a test case before extension to higher-dimensional systems relevant for heavy ion collision phenomenology.
