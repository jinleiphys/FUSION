# Mass, spectroscopy and two-neutron decay of $^{16}$Be

> The structure and decay of the most neutron-rich beryllium isotope, $^{16}$Be, has been investigated following proton knockout from a high-energy $^{17}$B beam. Two relatively narrow resonances were observed for the first time, with energies of $0.84(3)$ and $2.15(5)$ MeV above the two-neutron decay threshold and widths of $0.32(8)$ and $0.95(15)$ MeV respectively. These were assigned to be the ground ($J^π=0^+$) and first excited ($2^+$) state, with $E_x=1.31(6)$ MeV. The mass excess of $^{16}$Be was thus deduced to be $56.93(13)$ MeV, some $0.5$ MeV more bound than the only previous measurement. Both states were observed to decay by direct two-neutron emission. Calculations incorporating the evolution of the wavefunction during the decay as a genuine three-body process reproduced the principal characteristics of the neutron-neutron energy spectra for both levels, indicating that the ground state exhibits a strong spatially compact dineutron component, while the 2$^+$ level presents a far more diffuse neutron-neutron distribution.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first clear identification of the ground and first excited states of the most neutron-rich beryllium isotope, 16Be, as two relatively narrow resonances, with the ground state mass excess determined to be 56.93(13) MeV, which is about 0.5 MeV more bound than the only previous measurement by Spyrou et al. (2012).

## Method

The experiment used a 277 MeV/nucleon 17B beam impinging on a 15 cm thick liquid hydrogen target at the RIKEN RIBF facility, with the 16Be nuclei produced via proton knockout in the (p,2p) reaction. The reaction vertex was reconstructed using the MINOS time projection chamber, and the forward-going 14Be fragments and neutrons were detected with the SAMURAI spectrometer and NEBULA neutron array, allowing reconstruction of the 14Be+n+n invariant mass. The observed energy spectrum was fit with two Breit-Wigner lineshapes incorporating energy-dependent widths and a complete simulation of the setup and reaction process.

## Key numbers

- Ground state resonance energy: 0.84(3) MeV above the two-neutron decay threshold, with width 0.32(8) MeV
- First excited state (2+) resonance energy: 2.15(5) MeV above the two-neutron decay threshold, with width 0.95(15) MeV
- Excitation energy of the 2+ state: Ex = 1.31(6) MeV
- Mass excess of 16Be: 56.93(13) MeV, which is 0.52 MeV more bound than the value of 57.45(17) MeV from Spyrou et al. (2012)
- Three-body model predicted widths: 0.10 MeV for the 0+ state and 0.42 MeV for the 2+ state

## Context

This work extends the only previous study of 16Be by Spyrou et al. (2012), which identified a single broader structure at 1.35(10) MeV with width 0.8 MeV, by resolving two separate resonances with greatly enhanced statistics, better resolution, and superior acceptances. The results enable more reliable mass-surface extrapolations for neighboring nuclei including 17,18Be, and the three-body modeling approach demonstrates the importance of incorporating the time evolution of the initial resonance wavefunction to describe two-neutron decay, going beyond the simplified comparisons used in earlier studies of systems such as 10He, 14Be, and 20,24O.
