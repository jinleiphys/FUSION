# Confinement, Crossing Symmetry, and Glueballs

> We suggest that the quark-confining force is related by crossing symmetry to a color-singlet glueball ${\cal G}$ which is well described as a loop of one quantum of color magnetic flux. Electron pair annihilation as high as $\approx 2 GeV$ above the $Υ$ mass could produce $Υ\rightarrow \ell^+\ell^-$ accompanied by ${\cal G}$ or one of its excited states.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that the quark-confining force is related by crossing symmetry to a color-singlet glueball described as a loop of one quantum of color magnetic flux, but the authors hedge that this identification is speculative and that the naive crossing-symmetric partner (a color electric flux loop) would be practically indistinguishable from ordinary meson systems.

## Method

The authors use crossing symmetry arguments to relate the long-range confining force between heavy quarks to a scalar, color-singlet glueball state. They develop a perturbative approach using the Richardson ansatz for the running coupling to show that two-gluon exchange and multi-gluon exchange produce a linearly rising potential. They then propose an experimental test via electron-positron annihilation at energies near or above the Upsilon mass, where heavy quark pair production could generate a coherent color magnetic flux loop that decays to an Upsilon plus a glueball.

## Key numbers

- Electron pair annihilation as high as approximately 2 GeV above the Upsilon mass could produce Upsilon to lepton pairs accompanied by a glueball or its excited states
- Current estimates for 0++ glueball masses lie above 1.5 GeV, and for 2++ above 2 GeV
- The characteristic glueball momentum width is approximately 200 MeV/c
- The inverse moment of inertia of a glueball loop of mass 1 GeV and radius 1 fm is 80 MeV, giving 80 MeV for the first excited rotational state energy
- The expected branching ratio for the muon pair signal after e+e- collision should lie between 10^-4 and 10^-2, compared to a conventional Upsilon cascade background at least 100 times smaller
- The 3S Upsilon state has an inclusive branching ratio less than or equal to 0.1 for producing a 1S Upsilon; the 4S state should have a branching ratio to Upsilon of less than or equal to 10^-4

## Context

This work extends the Yukawa concept of force-carrying particles to the confining force in QCD, proposing that the exchanged object is a scalar, color-singlet glueball rather than a colored object. It contrasts with the electric flux string picture of Isgur and collaborators, and with the QCD sum rule approach of Novikov et al. who studied scalar glueballs in psi to 2pi plus gamma decays. The paper enables experimental searches at e+e- colliders for glueball production accompanying Upsilon decays to lepton pairs, and proposes lattice tests of whether the linear confining potential affects color octet objects.
