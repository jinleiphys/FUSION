# The electro-disintegration of few body systems revisited

> Recent studies of the electro-disintegration of the few body systems at JLab have revived the field. Not only recoil momentum distributions have been determined in a single shot. But also they confirm that the diagrammatic approach, which I developed 25 years ago, is relevant to analyze them, provided that the Nucleon-Nucleon scattering amplitude, determined in the same energy range, is used. They provide us with a solid starting point to address the issue of the propagation of exotic components of hadrons in nuclear matter

## Key claim
The diagrammatic approach developed by the author 25 years prior is confirmed to be relevant for analyzing electro-disintegration of few-body systems at JLab, but only provided that the Nucleon-Nucleon scattering amplitude determined in the same energy range is used, and the method is founded on solid grounds specifically in quasi-elastic kinematics (X~1).

## Method
The author computes the D(e,e'p)n, ³He(e,e'p)d, ³He(e,e'p)np, and ⁴He(e,e'p)T reaction cross sections using a diagrammatic expansion in momentum space in the laboratory frame, with relativistic kinematics and no angular approximations in loop integrals. The elementary operators at each vertex are calibrated against the corresponding channels, and the NN scattering amplitude is parameterized using a global form with central, spin-orbit, and spin-spin terms, where the central part is determined from experimental NN total cross sections and slope parameters. The FSI integral runs over the momentum of the spectator nucleon, and the singular part corresponding to on-shell nucleon propagation is maximum when X=1.

## Key numbers
- At Q²=1.55 GeV² in ³He(e,e'p)d, the relative kinetic energy between outgoing proton and deuteron is T_L=830 MeV, where σ_NN≈45 mb.
- In D(e,e'p)n at Q²=5 GeV² and X=1, the Δ formation term contributes little up to p_n~800 MeV/c but dominates above.
- The slope parameter β and total cross section σ_NN are used from experiments at Los Alamos, Saturne, and COSY; the ratio ε between real and imaginary parts is kept constant at ε=-0.2 above 1 GeV and extrapolated to zero at pion threshold.
- The parameterization reproduces the modulus of the central part of the NN amplitude from the SAID database up to four-momentum transfer -t=0.4 GeV², i.e., p_m~0.63 GeV/c in quasi-elastic kinematics.
- FSI reduces the quasi-free contribution in ³He(e,e'p)d below 300 MeV/c and overwhelms it by more than a factor five around 500 MeV/c.

## Context
This work extends the diagrammatic approach developed by Laget (1978, 1981, 1994) for electro-disintegration of few-body systems, which was previously applied at lower Q² (~0.4 GeV²) at Saclay and Amsterdam. The method is contrasted with the classical Glauber approximation, which predicts the rescattering peak at 90° and is shown to be incorrect for analyzing (e,e'p) reactions at large angles and large recoil momentum; the Generalized Eikonal Approximation (GEA) of Sargsian (2000) corrects this by including higher-order recoil terms. The results enable future studies of exotic components of hadrons via color transparency or color screening, as proposed by Laget (1998, 2000), and provide a starting point for determining hadron structure at short distances.
