# Melting and freeze-out conditions of hadrons in a thermal medium

> We describe two independent frameworks which provide unambiguous determinations of the deconfinement and the decoupling conditions of a relativistic gas at finite temperature. First, we use the Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model to compute meson and baryon masses at finite temperature and determine their melting temperature as a function of their strangeness content. Second, we analyze a simple expanding gas within a Friedmann-Robertson-Walker metric, which admits a well-defined decoupling mechanism. We examine the decoupling time as a function of the particle mass and cross section. We find evidences of an inherent dependence of the hadronization and freeze-out conditions on flavor, and on mass and cross section, respectively.

# Melting and freeze-out conditions of hadrons in a thermal medium

## Key claim
The deconfinement (melting) temperature and the freeze-out (decoupling) temperature of hadrons depend on flavor, mass, and cross section, with multistrange hadrons melting and freezing out at higher temperatures than nonstrange hadrons. The authors provide qualitative evidence from two independent frameworks, but note that the freeze-out study addresses kinetic freeze-out (absence of interactions) rather than chemical freeze-out, and inelastic processes are absent.

## Method
The authors used the Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model with three massive quark flavors to compute meson and baryon masses as functions of temperature, identifying the Mott (melting) temperature where bound states dissolve. Separately, they employed the SMASH transport model with a Friedmann-Robertson-Walker metric to simulate an expanding relativistic gas of particles with constant cross sections, extracting decoupling times and temperatures by fitting the distribution function after freeze-out.

## Key numbers
- Mott temperature of the proton: 254 MeV
- Mott temperature of the Xi baryon: 287 MeV
- Mott temperature of the Omega baryon: 288 MeV
- Mott temperature of the phi meson: 382 MeV
- Mott temperature of the pion: 282 MeV
- Mott temperature of the kaon: 286 MeV
- Difference in freeze-out temperature between multistrange and nonstrange baryons from ALICE thermal fits: approximately 16 MeV (cited from prior work)
- Difference in inflection point of 2nd-order susceptibility between strange and light sectors from lattice QCD: approximately 15 MeV (cited from prior work)
- Decoupling time for the example case in the expanding system: 5.3 ± 0.6 fm

## Context
This work extends observations from statistical thermal model fits to ALICE data (ALICE collaboration, 2017) showing a tension between multistrange and nonstrange baryon freeze-out temperatures, and from lattice QCD susceptibility calculations (Rene, 2017) indicating a flavor hierarchy in the crossover transition temperature. The PNJL results support earlier findings of a flavor-dependent deconfinement temperature (Sandeep, 2017), while the SMASH expanding-spacetime study provides a dynamical mechanism for mass- and cross-section-dependent freeze-out. The work enables more refined thermal fits that account for flavor-dependent freeze-out conditions and motivates inclusion of inelastic processes in future transport studies.
