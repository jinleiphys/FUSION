# Aligned neutron--proton pairs in $N\sim Z$ nuclei

> It is argued that $N\sim Z$ nuclei with $90\leq A\leq100$ can be interpreted in terms of aligned neutron--proton pairs with angular momentum $J=2j$ and isospin T=0. Based on this observation, a version of the interacting boson model is formulated in terms of isoscalar high-spin bosons. To illustrate its possible use, the model is applied to the $21^+$ isomer in $^{94}$Ag.

## Key claim

The paper argues that $N\sim Z$ nuclei with $90\leq A\leq100$ can be interpreted in terms of aligned neutron-proton pairs with angular momentum $J=2j$ and isospin $T=0$, but this interpretation presupposes that the $1g_{9/2}$ orbit is dominant in this mass region and does not work for the $8^+$ yrast state in $^{96}$Cd, which cannot be written in terms of two such pairs.

## Method

The study analyzed shell-model wave functions of $^{96}$Cd in terms of aligned neutron-proton pairs (so-called $B$ pairs) and then mapped shell-model states onto corresponding boson states for $^{96}$Cd, $^{94}$Ag, and $^{92}$Pd using standard boson mapping techniques. This mapping produced an interacting boson model (IBM) containing a single type of boson with angular momentum $\ell=9$ (a $b$ boson, called $b$-IBM). The model was then applied to the $21^+$ isomer in $^{94}$Ag by deriving energy expressions from both the boson approximation and the exact fermionic calculation for three neutrons and three protons in a $j=9/2$ orbit.

## Key numbers

- The $21^+$ isomer in $^{94}$Ag lies at $6.7(5)$ MeV above the ground state (Mukha 2005).
- The shell-model energy of both the $7^+$ and $21^+$ states in $^{94}$Ag is reproduced with $b$-IBM to within less than 100 keV (Zerguine 2011).
- For the three-boson system with $\ell=9$, the multiplicity calculation yields $d^{(9)}_3(21)=2$ and $d^{(9)}_1(21)=0$, meaning two independent boson states with $J=21$ can be constructed, one of which is spurious.
- The exact fermionic energy expression for the $21^+$ state in the 3n-3p system is $E_{\rm f}(21^+)= \frac{21}{65}\nu^{\rm f}_5+ \frac{21}{10}\nu^{\rm f}_6+ \frac{645}{442}\nu^{\rm f}_7+ \frac{69}{10}\nu^{\rm f}_8+ \frac{717}{170}\nu^{\rm f}_9$, where $\nu^{\rm f}_\lambda$ are shell-model two-body interaction matrix elements.
- The boson approximation energy expression for the $21^+$ state contains small coefficients for $\nu^{\rm f}_3$ and $\nu^{\rm f}_4$ (which should not contribute in the exact fermionic calculation), indicating the boson approximation is reasonably accurate.

## Context

This work extends the aligned-pair scheme proposed by Blomqvist (described in Cederwall 2011) and the subsequent analysis by Zerguine (2011) of $^{96}$Cd, $^{94}$Ag, and $^{92}$Pd. The paper formulates an interacting boson model (IBM) in terms of isoscalar high-spin bosons, building on the standard IBM framework of Iachello (1987). The approach enables a simplified description of complicated shell-model states, such as the $7^+$ isomer in $^{94}$Ag which has 30 components in the $JT$ scheme but only three independent components in terms of $B$ pairs or $b$ bosons, allowing for better structural understanding of $N\sim Z$ nuclei approaching $^{100}$Sn.
