# Constraining Palatini gravity with GR-independent equations of state for neutron stars

> We demonstrate how to construct GR-independent equations of state. We emphasize the importance of using theory-based principles instead of relying solely on astrophysical observables and General Relativity (GR). We build a set of equations of state based on first principles, including chiral perturbation theory and perturbation theory in quantum chromodynamics. Interpolation methods are employed to assume thermodynamic stability and causality in the intermediate region. These equations of state are then used to constrain quadratic Palatini $f(\mathcal R)$ gravity, indicating that the parameter lies within the range $-6.47 \lesssim β\lesssim 1.99$ km$^2$. Additionally, we briefly discuss the problem of phase transitions and twin stars.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates how to construct equations of state for neutron stars that are independent of General Relativity, using only first-principles inputs from chiral effective field theory and perturbative quantum chromodynamics, and then uses these EoSs to constrain the free parameter of quadratic Palatini f(R) gravity to a range of approximately -6.47 to 1.99 km^2, though the authors acknowledge this is a first attempt and the constraint depends on the specific criterion adopted for the radius error bars.

## Method

The authors construct GR-independent equations of state by combining chiral effective field theory calculations at N3LO for low densities (0.05 to 0.34 fm^-3) with perturbative QCD results at very high densities (n greater than 40 times nuclear saturation density), using interpolation methods that enforce thermodynamic stability, causality (sound speed squared between 0 and 1), and monotonic behavior in the intermediate region. They generate a grid of candidate EoS points between these regimes, ensuring thermodynamic consistency through discrete versions of the first law of thermodynamics and the Euler equation, and select five representative EoSs (stiffest, softest, and three intermediate). These EoSs are then used to solve the modified Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations in Palatini f(R) gravity to constrain the quadratic parameter beta.

## Key numbers

- The quadratic Palatini f(R) gravity parameter beta is constrained to the range -6.47 to 1.99 km^2, based on the constructed GR-independent EoSs
- The chiral EFT calculations cover the density range n = 0.05 to 0.34 fm^-3, with theoretical uncertainties of about 10% at nuclear saturation density (n_s = 0.16 fm^-3) and about 25% at 2n_s
- The pQCD matching point uses a baryon chemical potential of approximately 2.6 GeV, with densities n greater than 40 n_s
- The maximum mass of a neutron star was initially estimated at approximately 0.6 solar masses using the free neutron gas EoS, while recent observations suggest masses up to approximately 2.4 solar masses
- The saturation density is approximately n_s = 0.16 fm^-3, corresponding to about 2.7 x 10^14 g cm^-3

## Context

This work extends the approach of constructing EoSs from first principles without astrophysical inputs, building on the interpolation methods developed by Lope-Oter (2019) and Komoltsev (2021), and differs from prior work by Annala (2019) and Altiparmak (2022) by also providing baryon density, binding energy per nucleon, sound speed, and baryon chemical potential. The resulting GR-independent EoSs enable consistent testing of modified gravity theories against neutron star observations, and the authors note this formalism can now be applied to study twin star phenomena, phase transitions, and latent heat in a more consistent way than previous approaches that assumed General Relativity.
