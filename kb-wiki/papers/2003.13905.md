# Connecting fluctuation measurements in heavy-ion collisions with the grand-canonical susceptibilities

> We derive the relation between cumulants of a conserved charge measured in a subvolume of a thermal system and the corresponding grand-canonical susceptibilities, taking into account exact global conservation of that charge. The derivation is presented for an arbitrary equation of state, with the assumption that the subvolume is sufficiently large to be close to the thermodynamic limit. Our framework -- the subensemble acceptance method (SAM) -- quantifies the effect of global conservation laws and is an important step toward a direct comparison between cumulants of conserved charges measured in central heavy ion collisions and theoretical calculations of grand-canonical susceptibilities, such as lattice QCD. As an example, we apply our formalism to net-baryon fluctuations at vanishing baryon chemical potentials as encountered in collisions at the LHC and RHIC.

## Key claim

The paper derives a relation between cumulants of a conserved charge measured in a subvolume of a thermal system and the corresponding grand-canonical susceptibilities, taking into account exact global conservation of that charge, under the assumption that the subvolume is sufficiently large to be close to the thermodynamic limit. The derivation works for an arbitrary equation of state, not just the ideal hadron resonance gas model.

## Method

The authors develop the subensemble acceptance method (SAM), which considers a spatially uniform thermal system at fixed temperature, volume, and total net baryon number, described by the canonical ensemble. They pick a subsystem of fixed volume fraction α = V₁/V and evaluate the cumulants of the charge distribution within that subsystem using the canonical partition functions of the two subsystems. In the thermodynamic limit (V → ∞), they express the canonical partition function through the volume-independent free energy density and derive relations between subvolume cumulants and grand-canonical susceptibilities by differentiating the cumulant generating function with respect to a fictitious parameter t.

## Key numbers

- For net baryon fluctuations at LHC conditions (T = 160 MeV, μB = 0), the kurtosis κ₄/κ₂ and hyperkurtosis κ₆/κ₂ are computed as functions of α using lattice QCD susceptibilities from Borsanyi (2018)
- At α = 0.5, the difference between QCD and ideal hadron resonance gas results for κ₆/κ₂ is smallest, making it difficult to distinguish QCD dynamics from ideal HRG in the region 0.2 < α < 0.8
- For α < 0.2, the ratio κ₆/κ₂ becomes sensitive to the equation of state; a negative κ₆/κ₂ for α ≲ 0.1 is consistent with χ₆^B/χ₂^B being negative or close to zero
- At LHC (√sNN = 5.02 TeV, ybeam ≈ 8.5), α ≲ 0.1 corresponds to measurements within approximately two units of rapidity; at RHIC (√sNN = 200 GeV, ybeam ≈ 5.4), α ≲ 0.1 corresponds to approximately one unit of rapidity
- The limiting α below which the formalism breaks down is estimated as αlim ≈ 10⁻³ for LHC (Vtot ≈ 80000 fm³) and αlim ≈ 7·10⁻³ for RHIC (dV/dy ≈ 1500 fm³)

## Context

This work extends prior studies of global charge conservation effects in the ideal hadron resonance gas model by Bzdak (2012) and Braun-Munzinger (2016) to arbitrary equations of state, enabling direct comparison between experimental fluctuation measurements and lattice QCD calculations of grand-canonical susceptibilities. The formalism is most suitable for central collisions at LHC and top RHIC energies where strong space-momentum correlations exist, and it enables experimental tests of the QCD chiral crossover at μB = 0 through measurements of net baryon hyperkurtosis κ₆/κ₂. The SAM has been extended to multiple conserved charges in a companion paper by Vovchenko (2020).
