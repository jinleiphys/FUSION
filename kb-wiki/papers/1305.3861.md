# Pion production in the T2K experiment

> Background: Pion production gives information on the axial form factors of nucleon resonances. It also introduces a noticeable background to quasi-elastic measurements on nuclear targets and thus has a significant impact on precision studies of neutrino oscillation parameters. Purpose: To clarify neutrino-induced pion production on nucleons and nuclei. Method: The Giessen Boltzmann--Uehling--Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) model is used for the description of neutrino-nucleus reactions. Results: Theoretical results for differential cross sections for the T2K neutrino flux at the ND280 detector and integrated cross sections as a function of neutrino energy are given. Two sets of pion production data on elementary targets are used as inputs to obtain limits for pion production in neutrino-nucleus reactions. Conclusions: Pion production in the T2K ND280 detector can help to narrow down the uncertainties in the elementary pion production cross sections. It can also give valuable information on the nucleon-Delta axial form factor.

# Pion production in the T2K experiment

## Key claim

Pion production measurements at the T2K ND280 detector can help narrow down the 30% uncertainty in elementary pion production cross sections on nucleons, but only if analyzed together with MiniBooNE data to disentangle the effects of flux uncertainties and the elementary cross section input.

## Method

The Giessen Boltzmann–Uehling–Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) transport model was used to calculate neutrino-nucleus reactions on carbon, incorporating quasielastic scattering, 2p-2h processes, resonance pion production, and deep inelastic scattering. Two sets of elementary pion production data, the BNL data (Kitagaki 1986) and the ANL data (Radecky 1981), were used as inputs to obtain upper and lower limits for pion production in neutrino-nucleus reactions, with no tuning of parameters.

## Key numbers

- Flux-averaged total inclusive cross section: 8.32 × 10⁻³⁹ cm² per nucleon at an average neutrino energy of 0.93 GeV (using the T2K flux from t2kflux, integrated up to 22 GeV)
- Partial cross sections per nucleon (in units of 10⁻³⁹ cm²): true QE 3.68, 2p-2h 0.95, Δ excitation 1.72, higher resonance excitation 0.25, single-pion background 0.44, DIS 1.29
- Total pion-production cross sections per nucleon (in units of 10⁻³⁹ cm²): 1π⁻ 0.65, 1π⁰ 1.53, 1π⁺ 2.53
- Coherent pion production cross section on ¹²C: about 0.03 × 10⁻³⁸ cm² at the peak T2K energy of 0.6 GeV (Nakamura 2009)
- Pion kinetic energy spectra peak at approximately Tπ = 80 MeV, extending to about 200 MeV
- Differential cross section for muon kinetic energy peaks at about 0.15 GeV
- In the energy range up to 0.8 GeV, the pion production cross section is expected at the level of 0.2 – 1.5 × 10⁻³⁸ cm²

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous GiBUU-based comparison with MiniBooNE data (Lalakulich 2012) to the T2K experiment, which has a narrower neutrino flux distribution peaked around 0.6 GeV compared to MiniBooNE. The calculations address the long-standing discrepancy between theoretical predictions and MiniBooNE pion data, which has also been noted by Hernandez (2013). The results enable T2K to provide independent constraints on the nucleon-Delta axial form factor, which remains largely unknown (Hernandez 2010), and can help resolve whether the MiniBooNE discrepancy arises from flux uncertainties, elementary cross section uncertainties, or 2p2h1π processes (Mariano 2013).
