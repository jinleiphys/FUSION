# CP violation and chiral symmetry breaking in hot and dense quark matter in presence of magnetic field

> We investigate chiral symmetry breaking and strong CP violation effects on the phase diagram of strongly interacting matter in presence of a constant magnetic field. The effect of magnetic field and strong CP violating term on the phase structure at finite temperature and density is studied within a three flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model including the Kobayashi-Maskawa-t'Hooft (KMT) determinant term. This is investigated using an explicit variational ansatz for ground state with quark anti-quark pairs leading to condensates both in scalar and pseudoscalar channels. Magnetic field enhances the condensate in both the channels. Inverse magnetic catalysis for CP transition at finite chemical potential is seen for zero temperature and for small magnetic fields.

## Key claim

The paper investigates how a constant magnetic field modifies both chiral symmetry breaking and strong CP violation in hot and dense quark matter, finding that while magnetic field enhances condensates in both scalar and pseudoscalar channels, it can also produce inverse magnetic catalysis for the CP transition at finite chemical potential for zero temperature and small magnetic fields.

## Method

The authors use a three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model including the Kobayashi-Maskawa-t'Hooft (KMT) determinant term to simulate the effects of the CP-violating theta term in QCD. They construct a variational ansatz for the ground state with quark-antiquark pairs that allows for condensates in both scalar and pseudoscalar channels, and then solve the coupled gap equations for the constituent quark masses at finite temperature, chemical potential, and magnetic field strength, using the formalism of thermo field dynamics to incorporate thermal and density effects.

## Key numbers

- The model parameters are: three-momentum cutoff Lambda = 0.6023 GeV, four-fermion coupling G_s * Lambda^2 = 1.835, six-fermion coupling K * Lambda^5 = 12.36, current quark masses m_u = m_d = 5.5 MeV, and m_s = 0.1407 GeV.
- At zero temperature and zero density, the constituent masses are M^{u,d} = 0.368 GeV for u-d quarks and M^s = 0.549 GeV for the strange quark.
- The experimental limit on the CP-violating parameter theta is theta < 0.7 x 10^{-11} from neutron dipole moment measurements.
- Magnetar magnetic fields are of order 10^{15} to 10^{16} gauss.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of strong CP violation in two-flavor NJL models (CPNJL, 2008; Boomsma and Boer, 2008) and the authors' own earlier three-flavor NJL investigation without magnetic fields (Chatterjee, Mishra, and Mishra, 2014) to include the effects of a constant magnetic background. It also builds on studies of magnetic catalysis in NJL models (Providencia et al., 2013; Boomsma and Boer, 2010) and addresses the tension between magnetic catalysis at low temperature and inverse magnetic catalysis near the critical temperature observed in lattice QCD calculations (Schaefer et al., 2012). The results enable future investigations of the chiral magnetic effect in heavy-ion collisions and the phase structure of strongly magnetized compact stars.
