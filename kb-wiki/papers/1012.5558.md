# Two-pion interferometry for granular sources

> A review on the two-pion Hanbury-Brown-Twiss (HBT) interferometry in the granular source model of quark-gluon plasma droplets is presented. The characteristic quantities of the granular source extracted by imaging analysis are presented and compared with the HBT radii obtained by the usual Gaussian formula fit. The signals of granular sources are presented.

## Key claim

This review argues that the granular source model of quark-gluon plasma droplets can explain the RHIC HBT puzzle (where \(R_{\rm out}/R_{\rm side} \approx 1\)) because for a granular source the HBT radius \(R_{\rm side}\) (proportional to the droplet distribution) is much larger than the source lifetime \(\tau\) (proportional to the droplet radius), unlike uniform hydrodynamic sources which always yield \(R_{\rm out} > R_{\rm side}\).

## Method

The paper reviews the granular source model of QGP droplets, where the system fragments into droplets at time \(t_0\) after thermalization, with droplets distributed within a short cylinder and evolving via relativistic hydrodynamics with a cross-over equation of state. The two-pion source function \(S(r)\) is extracted using the model-independent imaging technique of Brown, Danielewicz, and Pratt, and the resulting first-order moment quantities \(\widetilde{R}_i = \sqrt{\pi}\langle r_i \rangle/2\) are compared with Gaussian-fitted HBT radii. Single-event HBT correlation functions are analyzed using the relative fluctuation quantity \(f\) to study event-by-event fluctuations for fluctuating versus smoothed initial conditions.

## Key numbers

- The ratio \(R_{\rm out}/R_{\rm side} \approx 1\) is the RHIC HBT puzzle that the granular source model addresses.
- For 40 events generated by smoothed particle hydrodynamics with impact parameter \(b = 5\) fm, the distributions of \(f\) for fluctuating initial conditions (FIC) are much wider than for smoothed initial conditions (SIC) when the pion pair number \(N_{\pi\pi} = 5 \times 10^6\).
- Even for \(N_{\pi\pi} = 5 \times 10^5\), the widths of the \(f\) distributions for FIC are visibly larger than those for SIC.
- At LHC energy, the event multiplicity of identical pions is about two thousand, making the order of \(N_{\pi\pi}\) about \(10^6\).

## Context

This review extends the granular source model first proposed by Zhang (2004) and improved by Zhang (2006, 2009) to address the RHIC HBT puzzle, which could not be explained by standard hydrodynamic calculations. The work builds on the concept of QGP droplet formation during a first-order phase transition proposed by Witten (1984) and on earlier two- and multi-pion HBT studies of granular sources by Pratt (1992) and Zhang (1995). The imaging analysis results are consistent with RHIC experimental HBT radii, and the paper suggests that at the LHC, the large event multiplicity will allow observation of the event-by-event fluctuation signals of granular sources, which are smoothed out in mixed-event analyses at RHIC.
