# Large deformed structures in Ne-S nuclei near neutron drip-line

> The structure of Ne, Na, Mg, Al, Si and S nuclei near the neutron drip-line region is investigated in the frame-work of relativistic meannfield (RMF) and non-relativistic Skyrme Hartree-Fock formalisms. The drip-line of these nuclei are pointed out. We analysed the large deformation structures and many of these neutron rich nuclei are quite deformed. New magic number are seen for these deformed nuclei.

## Key claim

The neutron drip-line for Ne, Na, Mg, Al, Si, and S isotopes is predicted using both relativistic mean-field (RMF) and non-relativistic Skyrme Hartree-Fock (SHF) formalisms, with the caveat that only intrinsic state solutions are presented and angular momentum projection is needed for definitive spectroscopic predictions.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent mean-field calculations using the RMF formalism with the NL3 parameter set and the SHF formalism with the SkI4 parameter set, solving the field equations in an axially deformed harmonic oscillator basis. Constant-gap BCS pairing was included for open-shell nuclei, and the ground state was determined by comparing prolate, oblate, and spherical solutions to find the maximum binding energy for each isotope.

## Key numbers

- Predicted neutron drip-line for Ne: A=34 (RMF and SHF), compared to experimental limit of A=30 (extrapolated to A=34)
- Predicted neutron drip-line for Mg: A=40 (RMF and SHF), compared to experimental limit of A=34 (extrapolated to A=40)
- Predicted neutron drip-line for S: A=55 (RMF and SHF), compared to experimental limit of A=45 (extrapolated to A=49)
- Calculated quadrupole deformation parameter beta_2 for 34Mg: 0.59 (RMF), compared to experimental value of 0.58 +/- 0.06 from Iwasaki et al. (2001)
- Binding energy difference between prolate and oblate solutions in 24Ne: 0.179 MeV (RMF), with ground state at beta_2 = -0.259 (BE = 189.093 MeV) and excited state at beta_2 = 0.278 (BE = 188.914 MeV)
- Binding energy difference between prolate and oblate solutions in 30Mg: 0.186 MeV (SHF SkI4), with beta_2 = -0.183 and 0.202

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutron-rich light nuclei, including the discovery of large collectivity in 34Mg by Iwasaki et al. (2001) and the observation of 40Mg and 42Al by Baumann et al. (2007), which pushed the expected drip-line to heavier masses. The calculations predict the disappearance of the N=28 magic number in 44S and suggest a new shell closure at N=34, two units beyond the experimentally observed N=32 shell closure reported by Kanungo et al. (2002). The results enable future angular momentum projection calculations to obtain spectroscopic predictions for these deformed drip-line nuclei.
