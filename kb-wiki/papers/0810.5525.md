# Description of Superdeformed Nuclei in the A~190 Region by Generalized Deformed su_{q}(2)

> The generalised deformed su_{q}(2) model is applied to 79 superdeformed bands in the region A~190. The transition energies and the moments of inertia are calculated within the model, Its validity is investigated by comparing it with the experimental data. Both the standard su_{q}(2) and the generalized one fail to account for the uprising and the downturn of the dynamic moment of inertia. Both models, however, show remarkable agreement with the available experimental data at low angular frequancy (hw <= 0.25MeV).

## Key claim

The generalized deformed su_q(2) model, which replaces the angular momentum expression I(I+1) with I(I+c), provides a better fit to the transition energies of superdeformed bands in the A~190 region than the standard su_q(2) model, but both models fail to account for the uprising and downturn of the dynamic moment of inertia at high angular frequency, showing remarkable agreement only at low angular frequency (hbar*omega <= 0.25 MeV).

## Method

The authors applied both the standard su_q(2) model and a generalized version (with an anharmonicity parameter c replacing I+1 by I+c) to calculate transition energies, kinematic moments of inertia J^(1), and dynamic moments of inertia J^(2) for 79 superdeformed bands in the A~190 region (nuclei Au, Tl, Bi, Pb, Po). The three parameters (moment of inertia j^(0), deformation parameter tau, and anharmonicity parameter c) were fitted to experimental transition energies from Singh (2002) using a root-mean-square (rms) criterion.

## Key numbers

- 79 superdeformed bands in the A~190 region were analyzed, covering nuclei Au, Tl, Bi, Pb, and Po.
- For 20 of the 79 bands, the anharmonic parameter c=1 (no improvement over standard model); these include bands such as 197Bi SD, 190Hg SD3, 191Hg SD1 and SD4, and others.
- 12 bands had c>2, considered outside the rotational region (e.g., 191Au SD3, 189Hg SD1, 190Hg SD4, 194Hg SD1).
- For 191Au SD1, the standard su_q(2) model gave rms=0.42239% while the generalized model gave rms=0.41994% with c=1.06323.
- For 198Po SD, the standard model gave rms=0.37078% while the generalized model gave rms=0.10810% with c=1.52075.
- For 196Bi SD, the standard model gave rms=0.61989% while the generalized model gave rms=0.03724% with c=1.82055.
- The low angular frequency region where agreement holds is defined as hbar*omega <= 0.25 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the application of quantum algebraic methods (su_q(2) models) previously used by Bonatsos (1999) for deformed nuclei and by Raychev (1990) for rotational spectra, now applied to 79 superdeformed bands in the A~190 region. The study was motivated by questioning of the standard su_q(2) model by Meng (1998). The results show that while the generalized model improves the fit for many bands, neither model can reproduce the irregular behavior of the dynamic moment of inertia at high rotational frequencies, which remains an open problem for superdeformed nuclear structure theory. The work enables future studies to explore alternative approaches for describing the uprising and downturn of J^(2) at high angular frequency.
