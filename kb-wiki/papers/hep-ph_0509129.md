# The collective quantization of three-flavored Skyrmions revisited

> A self-consistent large $N_c$ approach is developed for the collective quantization of SU(3) flavor hedgehog solitons, such as the Skyrmion. The key to this analysis is the determination of all of the zero modes associated with small fluctuations around the hedgehog. These are used in the conventional way to construct collective coordinates. This approach differs from previous work in that it does not implicitly assume that each static zero mode is associated with a dynamical zero mode. It is demonstrated explicitly in the context of the Skyrmion that there are fewer dynamical zero modes than static ones due to the Witten-Wess-Zumino term in the action. Group-theoretic methods are employed to identify the physical states resulting from canonical quantization of the collectively rotating soliton. The collective states fall into representations of SU(3) flavor labeled by $(p,q)$ and are given by $(2J, \frac{Nc}{2} -J)$ where $J={1/2},{3/2},... $ is the spin of the collective state. States with strangeness $S > 0$ do not arise as collective states from this procedure; thus the $θ^{+}$ (pentaquark) resonance does not arise as a collective excitation in models of this type.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the standard rigid-rotator collective quantization of SU(3) flavor hedgehog solitons incorrectly assumes seven dynamical zero modes, whereas a self-consistent analysis reveals only three dynamical zero modes due to the Witten-Wess-Zumino term; consequently, exotic baryons with positive strangeness such as the theta-plus pentaquark do not arise as collective excitations in these models.

## Method

The authors identify all static and dynamical zero modes of small fluctuations around the SU(3) hedgehog Skyrmion by analyzing the equations of motion including the Witten-Wess-Zumino term. They show that while there are seven static zero modes corresponding to rotations in SU(3)/U(1), only the three rotations within the SU(2) isospin subspace yield dynamical zero modes; the four rotations into strange directions produce non-zero overlaps with static zero modes and therefore lack corresponding dynamical zero modes. Using this reduced set of ten zero modes (seven static, three dynamical), they construct a collective ansatz and determine the allowed SU(3) flavor representations via group-theoretic methods and Noether current analysis.

## Key numbers

- The overlap integrals between the Witten-Wess-Zumino term contributions and static zero modes yield values of plus or minus 9 pi squared for the strange-direction rotations, demonstrating the elimination of dynamical zero modes
- The maximum hypercharge for collective states is Y_max = N_c/3, independent of the collective motion parameters
- The allowed SU(3) representations are labeled by (p,q) = (2J, N_c/2 - J) where J = 1/2, 3/2, ... is the spin of the collective state

## Context

This work resolves a long-standing controversy in Skyrmion physics by showing that the rigid-rotator quantization approach of Guadagnini (1984) and the hedgehog quantization scheme implicitly assumed seven dynamical zero modes, leading to incorrect predictions of exotic baryons. The analysis extends the SU(2) collective quantization method of Adkins, Nappi and Witten (1983) to SU(3) while correcting the treatment of the Witten-Wess-Zumino term. The resulting collective spectrum matches the model-independent large N_c consistency rules of Dashen, Jenkins and Manohar (1994), and the paper explicitly contradicts the exotic baryon predictions of Diakonov, Petrov and Polyakov (1997) and Praszalowicz (2003). The approach enables consistent treatment of SU(3) soliton models within a proper 1/N_c expansion framework.
