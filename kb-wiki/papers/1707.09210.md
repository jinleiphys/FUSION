# The nucleon as a test case to calculate vector-isovector form factors at low energies

> Extending a recent suggestion for hyperon form factors to the nucleon case, dispersion theory is used to relate the low-energy vector-isovector form factors of the nucleon to the pion vector form factor. The additionally required input, i.e. the pion-nucleon scattering amplitudes are determined from relativistic next-to-leading-order (NLO) baryon chiral perturbation theory including the nucleons and optionally the Delta baryons. Two methods how to include pion rescattering are compared: (a) solving the Muskhelishvili-Omnes (MO) equation and (b) using an N/D approach. It turns out that the results differ strongly from each other. Furthermore the results are compared to a fully dispersive calculation of the (subthreshold) pion-nucleon amplitudes based on Roy-Steiner (RS) equations. In full agreement with the findings from the hyperon sector it turns out that the inclusion of Delta baryons is not an option but a necessity to obtain reasonable results. The magnetic isovector form factor depends strongly on a low-energy constant of the NLO Lagrangian. If it is adjusted such that the corresponding magnetic radius is reproduced, then the results for the corresponding pion-nucleon scattering amplitude (based on the MO equation) agree very well with the RS results. Also in the electric sector the Delta degrees of freedom are needed to obtain the correct order of magnitude for the isovector charge and the corresponding electric radius. Yet quantitative agreement is not achieved. If the subtraction constant that appears in the solution of the MO equation is not taken from nucleon+Delta chiral perturbation theory but adjusted such that the electric radius is reproduced, then one obtains also in this sector a pion-nucleon scattering amplitude that agrees well with the RS results.

## Key claim

Dispersion theory can relate the low-energy vector-isovector form factors of the nucleon to the pion vector form factor, but the results depend strongly on the method used to include pion rescattering: solving the Muskhelishvili-Omnes (MO) equation yields results that differ strongly from those obtained using an N/D approach, and the inclusion of Delta baryons is a necessity, not an option, to obtain reasonable results.

## Method

The author used dispersion theory to relate the nucleon's vector-isovector form factors to the pion vector form factor, with the required pion-nucleon scattering amplitudes determined from relativistic next-to-leading-order (NLO) baryon chiral perturbation theory (χPT) including nucleons and optionally Delta baryons. Two methods for including pion rescattering were compared: (a) solving the Muskhelishvili-Omnes (MO) equation and (b) using an N/D approach, and the results were compared to a fully dispersive calculation based on Roy-Steiner (RS) equations. The left-hand cut structure K was determined from tree-level nucleon and (optionally) Delta exchange diagrams, while the subtraction constant P0 was either taken from χPT or fitted to reproduce the electric or magnetic radius.

## Key numbers

- The low-energy constant c4 determined from the MO scheme with nucleon+Delta χPT at NLO, adjusted to reproduce the magnetic radius, is c4 = 2.99 GeV⁻¹ (with cutoff Λ = 1.8 GeV) and c4 = 3.07 GeV⁻¹ (with cutoff Λ = 1 GeV), which lies between the NLO value c4 ≈ 2.17 GeV⁻¹ and the NNLO value c4 ≈ 3.56 GeV⁻¹ from Roy-Steiner analyses.
- The parameter αV for the pion vector form factor is αV = 0.12 GeV⁻², determined from a fit to tau decay data.
- The pion-Delta-nucleon coupling constant hA is set to hA = 2.88, chosen to reproduce the Delta width.
- The axial coupling constant gA = F + D = 1.26.

## Context

This work extends a recent suggestion for hyperon form factors (Granados, 2017) to the nucleon case, using the same combination of dispersion theory and relativistic octet+decuplet chiral perturbation theory. It contrasts with the approach of Alarcon (2017), which used a variant of the N/D method with leading-order χPT, and finds that the MO version agrees very well with the fully dispersive Roy-Steiner analysis of Hoferichter (2016), while the N/D results deviate significantly in the rho-meson region. The analysis supports the idea that explicit inclusion of decuplet degrees of freedom (the Delta baryon for the nucleon) is mandatory, and enables predictions of form factor shapes (e.g., curvature) given value and slope at the photon point as input.
