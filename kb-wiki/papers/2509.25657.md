# Nonlocal effective field theory and its applications

> We review recent applications of nonlocal effective field theory, focusing in particular on nonlocal chiral effective theory and nonlocal quantum electrodynamics (QED), as well as an extension of nonlocal effective theory to curved spacetime. For the chiral effective theory, we discuss the calculation of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) of the nucleon at nonzero skewness, along with the corresponding gravitational (or mechanical) form factors, within the convolution framework. In the QED application, we extend the nonlocal formulation to construct the most general nonlocal QED interaction, in which both the propagator and fundamental QED vertex are modified due to the nonlocal Lagrangian, while preserving the Ward-Green-Takahashi identities. For consistency with the modified propagator, a solid quantization is proposed, and the nonlocal QED is applied to explain the lepton $g-2$ anomalies without the introduction of new particles or interactions. Finally, with an extension of the chiral effective action to curved spacetime, we investigate the nonlocal energy-momentum tensor and gravitational form factors of the nucleon with a nonlocal pion-nucleon interaction.

## Key claim

This review argues that nonlocal effective field theory, in which interaction vertices are smeared over a finite range, can simultaneously describe nucleon generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and gravitational form factors, explain the lepton g-2 anomaly without new particles, and be extended to curved spacetime, provided the nonlocal Lagrangian preserves the Ward-Green-Takahashi identities and a consistent quantization scheme is adopted.

## Method

The authors review three applications of nonlocal effective field theory. First, in nonlocal chiral effective theory, they compute nucleon GPDs at nonzero skewness and the corresponding gravitational form factors using a convolution framework. Second, they construct the most general nonlocal QED interaction by modifying both the propagator and the fundamental QED vertex while preserving gauge invariance through the Ward-Green-Takahashi identities, and apply it to the lepton g-2 anomaly. Third, they extend the chiral effective action to curved spacetime to derive the nonlocal energy-momentum tensor and gravitational form factors of the nucleon with a nonlocal pion-nucleon interaction.

## Context

This review extends the authors' prior work on nonlocal chiral effective theory (e.g., Salamu et al., previous GPD calculations) and nonlocal QED (e.g., Wang et al., earlier g-2 studies) by unifying these applications and adding the curved-spacetime extension. It enables future calculations of nucleon mechanical properties from first principles and provides a framework for addressing the muon g-2 discrepancy without invoking beyond-Standard-Model particles. The curved-spacetime extension opens the door to studying gravitational form factors in a nonlocal setting, which connects to lattice QCD and experimental programs at Jefferson Lab and the Electron-Ion Collider.
