# Mechanisms for production of hypernuclei beyond the neutron and proton drip lines

> We analyze hypernuclei coming from fragmentation and multifragmentation of spectator residues obtained in relativistic ion collisions. These hypernuclei have a broad distribution in masses and isospin. They reach beyond the neutron and proton drip lines, and they are expected to be stable with respect to neutron and proton emission. This gives us the opportunity to investigate the properties of exotic hypernuclei, as well as the properties of normal nuclei beyond the drip lines, which can be produced after weak decay of such hypernuclei.

# Mechanisms for production of hypernuclei beyond the neutron and proton drip lines

## Key claim
Hypernuclei produced via fragmentation and multifragmentation of spectator residues in relativistic ion collisions can extend beyond the neutron and proton drip lines and remain stable against strong decay, provided the hyperon-nucleon binding energy is sufficient to shift the separation energies above zero. The authors hedge that their liquid-drop approximation likely provides a *lower limit* for hyperon binding, meaning the actual stability of such exotic hypernuclei may be even greater than predicted.

## Method
The authors used the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) generalized to include Λ hyperons, with a liquid-drop parametrization for hypernucleus binding energies. They simulated the disintegration of excited hyperspectator residues (with up to four Λ hyperons) produced in peripheral relativistic heavy-ion collisions at beam energies around 20 A GeV, using input from transport models (DCM and UrQMD). The calculations were performed at temperatures of 3–5 MeV for systems with masses A₀ = 50, 100, and 200 and varying isospin content (Z₀/A₀ = 0.4 and 0.35), and the yields of fragments with different numbers of Λ hyperons were computed via grand-canonical statistics.

## Key numbers
- Adding one Λ hyperon to a molybdenum nucleus shifts its neutron drip line to the right by nearly **30 mass units** (based on S₂ₙ = 0 criterion).
- The liquid-drop hyperenergy term uses a volume coefficient of **−10.68 MeV** and a surface coefficient of **21.27 MeV** per hyperon fraction (H/A).
- The standard SMM symmetry energy coefficient is γ = **25 MeV**; a reduced value of γ = **14 MeV** (consistent with multifragmentation data) increases yields of neutron-rich isotopes.
- The expected neutron drip line for molybdenum lies at N ≈ **86–88**; this can be reached and overpassed after disintegration of a system with Z₀ = 70 and A₀ = 200.
- Calculations were performed for systems with initial hyperon numbers H₀ = **2** and **4**, at temperatures T = **3, 4, and 5 MeV**.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous SMM-based studies of hypernucleus production (Botvina et al., 2011; Botvina and Pochodzalla, 2013) to systems with up to four Λ hyperons and to nuclei far from stability. It builds on transport-model predictions (Dubna cascade model and UrQMD) that spectator residues in peripheral collisions at 20 A GeV can absorb multiple hyperons. The approach contrasts with traditional hypernucleus production via kaon beams or spectroscopy (Japan collaboration) and with structure calculations such as relativistic mean-field models (Greiner, 1990; Schaffner et al., 1994). The results suggest that hypernuclei can serve as a tool to produce normal nuclei beyond the drip lines after weak decay, enabling studies of neutron-rich islands of stability predicted by some mean-field calculations (Yilmaz et al., 2011).
