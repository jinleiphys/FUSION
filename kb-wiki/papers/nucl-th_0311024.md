# The NN-final-state-interaction in two-nucleon-knockout reactions

> The influence of the mutual interaction between the two outgoing nucleons (NN-FSI) in electro- and photoinduced two-nucleon knockout from $^{16}O$ has been investigated perturbatively. It turns out that the effect of NN-FSI depends on the kinematics and on the type of reaction considered. In the kinematics studied so far, the effect is larger in pp- than in pn-knockout and in electron induced than in photoinduced reactions.

## Key claim
The mutual interaction between the two outgoing nucleons (NN-FSI) in two-nucleon knockout from 16O is not negligible in general, but its magnitude depends strongly on kinematics, reaction type, and final state of the residual nucleus; the effect is larger in pp- than in pn-knockout and in electron-induced than in photoinduced reactions, but these trends are not universal and each situation must be evaluated individually.

## Method
The authors calculated differential cross sections for 16O(e,e'pp), 16O(e,e'pn), 16O(γ,pp), and 16O(γ,pn) reactions using an unfactorized model with a one-body current and two-body meson-exchange currents (pionic seagull, pion-in-flight, Δ-current). The final-state wave function was treated in four approximations: plane-wave (PW), distorted-wave with optical potential only (DW), plane-wave plus NN-FSI (PW-NN), and distorted-wave plus NN-FSI (DW-NN), where NN-FSI was included perturbatively via a partial-wave decomposition of the NN scattering amplitude up to orbital angular momentum 3.

## Key numbers
- In superparallel kinematics (E0=855 MeV, θe=18°, ω=215 MeV, q=316 MeV/c), the optical potential reduces the cross section at pB=100 MeV/c by a factor of ~0.2 in (e,e'pp) and ~0.3 in (e,e'pn) compared to PW.
- In (e,e'pp) at pB=300 MeV/c, NN-FSI enhances the cross section by roughly an order of magnitude relative to DW.
- In (e,e'pn) at pB=300 MeV/c, NN-FSI produces only a 50% enhancement relative to DW.
- In (γ,pp) at Eγ=215 MeV in superparallel kinematics, NN-FSI enhances the cross section by a factor of ~4 at pB=300 MeV/c.
- In (γ,pp) at Eγ=120 MeV with T1=45 MeV and γ1=45°, NN-FSI has almost no effect on the cross section.

## Context
This work extends earlier calculations by the same group (Gui97, Gui98, Gui99, Gui00, Gui01) that included only the nucleon-nucleus optical potential (DW) and neglected NN-FSI, based on the assumption that NN-FSI would be small in superparallel kinematics. It confirms the nuclear-matter result of Kno00 that NN-FSI cannot be neglected. The results are preliminary because multiscattering processes (diagrams c and d in the paper) requiring a full three-body approach are not yet included; the authors state that such a three-body treatment is needed for a more reliable description.
