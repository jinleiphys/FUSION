# Testing hydrodynamic response to initial-state geometry in Pb+$d^\uparrow$ collisions

> Deuterons with different polarization states have distinct shapes for their wavefunctions. This offers a unique opportunity to experimentally control the initial-state collision geometry with the polarization of the light-ion targets in relativistic heavy-ion experiments. We study the charged hadron elliptic flow coefficients with respect to the polarization angle of deuterons in Pb + polarized deuteron collisions using a hydrodynamics + hadronic transport model. Hydrodynamic response to initial-state geometry predicts a distinct sign of $v_2$ correlated with the deuteron's polarization states, providing a clean test case for elucidating the collective origin in small collision systems.

## Key claim

Hydrodynamic response to initial-state geometry predicts a distinct sign of the elliptic flow coefficient $v_2$ correlated with the deuteron's polarization state in Pb+$d^\uparrow$ collisions, providing a clean test case for elucidating the collective origin in small collision systems. The authors note that the PYTHIA model with string shoving predicts the opposite signs for $v_2$ in these collision systems, making experimental measurements a decisive test.

## Method

The authors used the 3D-Glauber + MUSIC + UrQMD hybrid model to simulate (3+1)D event-by-event hydrodynamic collisions of Pb nuclei with transversely polarized deuterons at fixed-target energies. They computed charged hadron elliptic flow coefficients with respect to the deuteron's polarization axis for particles with transverse momentum $p_T \in [0.2, 3]$ GeV and pseudo-rapidities $\eta \in [2, 5]$ in the lab frame, matching LHCb detector acceptances. Model parameters were taken from a recent Bayesian calibration incorporating RHIC Beam Energy Scan measurements from Jahan (2024) and Jahan (2025).

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 71$ GeV for Pb+$d^\uparrow$ collisions in fixed-target mode at LHCb SMOG.
- For events with charged hadron multiplicity $N_\mathrm{ch} > 10$, the sign of initial-state eccentricity $\varepsilon_2(J^\uparrow)$ matches expectations from deuteron spatial configurations.
- Final-state $v_2(J^\uparrow)$ magnitudes are significantly smaller than those of $\varepsilon_2(J^\uparrow)$, indicating limited hydrodynamic conversion of spatial eccentricity to momentum anisotropy in small systems.
- Positive $v_2(J^\uparrow)$ for deuterons with $J^z = 0$ state; negative $v_2(J^\uparrow)$ for deuterons with $J^z = \pm 1$ states.

## Context

This work extends the line of inquiry into collectivity in small collision systems, a central topic since Schenke (2021) and Noronha (2024), by proposing a controlled experimental test using polarized deuteron targets. Prior attempts to identify hydrodynamic response signatures, such as those by PHENIX (2018), were complicated by event-by-event fluctuations at nucleon and sub-nucleonic levels. The results provide a benchmark for upcoming LHCspin measurements with the LHCb SMOG setup, enabling a concrete test of whether hydrodynamic responses occur in small systems, as opposed to initial-state or non-hydrodynamic mechanisms.
