# Nucleon Isovector Axial Form Factors

> We present results for the isovector axial vector form factors obtained using thirteen 2+1+1-flavor highly improved staggered quark (HISQ) ensembles generated by the MILC collaboration. The calculation of nucleon two- and three-point correlation functions has been done using Wilson-clover fermions. In the analysis of these data, we quantify the sensitivity of the results to strategies used for removing excited state contamination and invoke the partially conserved axial current relation between the form factors to choose between them. Our data driven analysis includes removing contributions from multihadron $N π$ states that make significant contributions. Our final results are: $g_A = 1.292 (53)_\text{stat}\,(24)_\text{sys}$ for the axial charge; $g_S = 1.085 (50)_\text{stat}\, (103)_\text{sys}$ and $g_T = 0.991 (21)_\text{stat}\, (10)_\text{sys}$ for the scalar and tensor charges; $\langle r_A^2 \rangle = 0.439 (56)_\text{stat} (34)_\text{sys}$ fm${}^2$ for the mean squared axial charge radius, $g_P^\ast = 9.03(47)_\text{stat}(42)_\text{sys} $ for the induced pseudoscalar charge; and $g_{πNN} = 14.14(81)_\text{stat}(85)_\text{sys}$ for the pion-nucleon coupling. We also provide a parameterization of the axial form factor $G_A(Q^2)$ over the range $0 \le Q^2 \le 1$ GeV${}^2$ for use in phenomenology and a comparison with other lattice determinations. We find that the various lattice data agree within 10\% but are significantly different from the extraction of $G_A(Q^2)$ from the $ν$-deuterium scattering data.

## Key claim

The authors present lattice QCD results for the isovector axial form factors that include contributions from multihadron \(N\pi\) excited states, which had been missed in all prior lattice calculations, and find that these contributions are essential for satisfying the partially conserved axial current (PCAC) relation; the final results agree with other lattice determinations within 10% but differ significantly from extractions based on \(\nu\)-deuterium scattering data.

## Method

The calculation used thirteen 2+1+1-flavor highly improved staggered quark (HISQ) ensembles generated by the MILC collaboration, with nucleon two- and three-point correlation functions constructed using Wilson-clover fermions. Three strategies for removing excited state contamination were compared: a standard approach using excited-state parameters from fits to two-point functions only (S2pt), a strategy using the \(A_4\) correlator to determine the first excited state (SA4), and a simultaneous fit to all five three-point correlators (Ssim). The analysis included explicit removal of contributions from multihadron \(N\pi\) states, and the PCAC relation was used to evaluate how well excited state contamination had been controlled. Results were extrapolated to the physical point using simultaneous chiral-continuum-finite-volume (CCFV) fits.

## Key numbers

- Axial charge: \(g_A = 1.292 (53)_\text{stat} (24)_\text{sys}\)
- Scalar charge: \(g_S = 1.085 (50)_\text{stat} (103)_\text{sys}\)
- Tensor charge: \(g_T = 0.991 (21)_\text{stat} (10)_\text{sys}\)
- Mean squared axial charge radius: \(\langle r_A^2 \rangle = 0.439 (56)_\text{stat} (34)_\text{sys}\) fm\(^2\)
- Induced pseudoscalar charge: \(g_P^\ast = 9.03 (47)_\text{stat} (42)_\text{sys}\)
- Pion-nucleon coupling: \(g_{\pi NN} = 14.14 (81)_\text{stat} (85)_\text{sys}\)
- The axial form factor \(G_A(Q^2)\) was parameterized using the \(z\)-expansion over the range \(0 \le Q^2 \le 1\) GeV\(^2\)
- The contribution of the \(N\pi\) state to \(\widetilde{G}_P\) and \(G_P\) reaches \(\sim 45\%\) at the physical pion mass, with a roughly \(5\%\) effect observed in \(G_A\)

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD methodology developed by the PNDME Collaboration (Jang 2019) that first identified the need to include \(N\pi\) excited states, which had been missed in all prior lattice calculations. The results are compared with the FLAG averaging group reports (2019, 2021) and with the MINER\(\nu\)A experiment (2023), which recently extracted the axial form factor from charged current elastic scattering on hydrogen. The axial charge radius \(\sqrt{\langle r_A^2 \rangle} = 0.66 \pm 0.09\) fm (from the squared value) is consistent within one combined sigma with the MINER\(\nu\)A result of \(0.73 \pm 0.17\) fm, while the lattice data disagree significantly with extractions from \(\nu\)-deuterium scattering data. The parameterization of \(G_A(Q^2)\) over \(0 \le Q^2 \le 1\) GeV\(^2\) is provided for use in neutrino oscillation phenomenology and nuclear many-body calculations.
