# Dirac-Brueckner Hartree-Fock Approach: from Infinite Matter to Effective Lagrangians for Finite Systems

> One of the open problems in nuclear structure is how to predict properties of finite nuclei from the knowledge of a bare nucleon-nucleon interaction of the meson-exchange type. We point out that a promising starting point consists in Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) calculations us- ing realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions like the Bonn potentials, which are able to reproduce satisfactorily the properties of symmetric nuclear matter without the need for 3-body forces, as is necessary in non-relativistic BHF calculations. However, the DBHF formalism is still too com- plicated to be used directly for finite nuclei. We argue that a possible route is to define effective Lagrangians with density-dependent nucleon-meson coupling vertices, which can be used in the Relativistic Hartree (or Relativistic Mean Field (RMF)) or preferrably in the Relativistic Hartree- Fock (RHF) approach. The density-dependence is matched to the nuclear matter DBHF results. We review the present status of nuclear matter DBHF calculations and discuss the various schemes to construct the self-energy, which lead to differences in the predictions. We also discuss how effective Lagrangians have been constructed and are used in actual calculations. We point out that completely consistent calculations in this scheme still have to be performed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) calculations using realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions (like the Bonn potentials) can reproduce symmetric nuclear matter properties without three-body forces, but the DBHF formalism is still too complicated for direct application to finite nuclei; the proposed route is to define effective Lagrangians with density-dependent nucleon-meson coupling vertices matched to DBHF nuclear matter results, though completely consistent calculations in this scheme have not yet been performed.

## Method

The authors review the status of DBHF calculations for infinite nuclear matter, focusing on the problem of reconstructing the Lorentz structure of the self-energy (scalar, time-like vector, and space-like vector components) from the G-matrix, which is typically solved via a three-dimensional Thompson reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation. They then discuss how to construct effective Lagrangians with density-dependent meson-nucleon coupling vertices by matching the DBHF self-energies in either the Relativistic Hartree (RMF) or Relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) approximation, and examine the various projection schemes used to extract the Lorentz components of the self-energy.

## Key numbers

- Empirical saturation point of symmetric nuclear matter: E/A ≈ -16 MeV at k_F ≈ 1.36 fm^{-1}
- Binding energies near saturation in Brueckner calculations can vary by about 10% due to angle averaging of the Pauli blocking factor (Schiller, Muther and Czerski, 1999)
- The density range relevant for nuclear structure calculations is approximately 0.1ρ_0 to (1-2)ρ_0, where ρ_0 is the saturation density
- Phenomenological DDRMF or DDRHF calculations adjust density-dependent parameters with 7 to 10 global parameters to fit nuclear matter and finite nuclei data

## Context

This work extends the Brueckner theory lineage from the original non-relativistic BHF approach (Brueckner, 1954; Bethe, 1956), which required three-body forces to reproduce saturation (Baldo, 1999; Zuo, 2002), to the relativistic DBHF framework (Anastasio, 1983; Brockmann, 1984; Ter Haar and Malfliet, 1987). The paper reviews the status of constructing effective Lagrangians from DBHF, building on earlier attempts by Negele (1970) and the density-dependent relativistic mean field (DDRMF) work of Fuchs (1995) and Hofmann (2001). It identifies the next step as performing fully consistent RHF calculations of finite nuclei using DBHF-derived effective Lagrangians, which would enable parameter-free predictions for exotic nuclei, heavy ion collisions, and astrophysical objects like neutron stars.
