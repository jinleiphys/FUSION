# Neutrino-Nucleus Interactions around 1 GeV

> The extraction of neutrino oscillation parameters often requires the unambiguous identification of the reaction mechanism to be used for the energy determination. The use of nuclear targets and of beams with broad energy distributions complicates this task because it requires the reliable description of many different elementary processes and the use of event generators to isolate and identify them. In this talk I briefly summarize our present understanding of this task.

# Neutrino-Nucleus Interactions around 1 GeV

## Key claim

The excess in quasielastic-like cross sections observed by MiniBooNE can be explained either by modifying the axial or vector couplings within the impulse approximation, or by invoking two-particle-two-hole (2p-2h) contributions; the true reaction mechanism remains ambiguous because total inclusive cross sections alone cannot distinguish between these scenarios.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical calculations and event generator results (primarily GiBUU) for neutrino-nucleus interactions around 1 GeV, comparing impulse approximation models (1p-1h) with models including 2p-2h contributions. The analysis examines cross sections for quasielastic scattering, pion production, and multi-nucleon knock-out on nuclear targets, using comparisons to electron scattering data and photon-induced reactions as validation tests.

## Key numbers

- The MiniBooNE quasielastic cross section is significantly larger than predictions using the world-average axial mass M_A ≈ 1 GeV
- The 2p-2h contributions are believed to account for approximately 25% of the surplus events labeled as quasielastic in the MiniBooNE experiment
- Axial mass values extracted from experiments range from M_A ≈ 1.2 - 1.3 GeV up to M_A = 1.6 GeV in some analyses
- At 1 GeV neutrino energy, the probability to see 2 nucleons in the final state amounts to 20% of the 1-nucleon contribution, even when the initial interaction was on one nucleon only
- The average uncertainty in reconstructed neutrino energy is about 20% with a bias towards lower energies

## Context

This work extends the ongoing discussion triggered by the MiniBooNE results (Aguilar-Arevalo, 2010) showing excess quasielastic cross sections, and reviews competing explanations from Bodek et al. (2011) (enhanced vector strength) and from Nieves (2011), Martini (2011), and Amaro (2011) (2p-2h contributions). The paper enables future experimental verification by identifying nucleon knock-out measurements and comparisons between neutrino and antineutrino data as the most promising ways to distinguish between reaction mechanisms, and calls for incorporation of 2p-2h interactions into event generators.
