# Single spin asymmetry in high energy QCD

> We present the first steps in an effort to incorporate the physics of transverse spin asymmetries into the saturation formalism of high energy QCD. We consider a simple model in which a transversely polarized quark scatters on a proton or nuclear target. Using the light-cone perturbation theory the hadron production cross section can be written as a convolution of the light-cone wave function squared and the interaction with the target. To generate the single transverse spin asymmetry (STSA) either the wave function squared or the interaction with the target has to be T-odd. In this work we use the lowest-order q->qG wave function squared, which is T-even, generating the STSA from the T-odd interaction with the target mediated by an odderon exchange. We study the properties of the obtained STSA, some of which are in qualitative agreement with experiment: STSA increases with increasing projectile x_F and is a non-monotonic function of the transverse momentum k_T. Our mechanism predicts that the quark STSA in polarized proton--nucleus collisions should be much smaller than in polarized proton--proton collisions. We also observe that the STSA for prompt photons due to our mechanism is zero within the accuracy of the approximation.

## Key claim

The authors present a new mechanism for generating single transverse spin asymmetry (STSA) in high-energy QCD, where the spin dependence comes from the polarized projectile quark's light-cone wave function while the required T-odd phase arises from the interaction with the unpolarized target via odderon exchange, rather than from the wave function squared or fragmentation functions as in the Sivers or Collins mechanisms.

## Method

The authors consider a simple model of a transversely polarized quark scattering on an unpolarized proton or nuclear target. Using light-cone perturbation theory (LCPT), they write the hadron production cross section as a convolution of the lowest-order q→qG light-cone wave function squared (which is T-even) with the target interaction. The STSA is generated from the T-odd part of the interaction with the target, which is isolated by decomposing the dipole S-matrix into C-even Pomeron and C-odd Odderon components. The resulting expressions for the spin-dependent and spin-averaged cross sections are evaluated in the Glauber-Mueller multiple-rescattering approximation in the large-Nc limit.

## Key numbers

- At large transverse momentum, the STSA falls as A_N^(q) ∝ 1/k_T^5 for k_T ≫ Q_s
- For k_T ≈ Q_s, the STSA scales with atomic number as A_N^(q) ∼ A^(-7/6), assuming Q_s^2 ∼ A^(1/3)
- The STSA for prompt photons is exactly zero, A_N^(γ) = 0, within the order of approximation considered

## Context

This work extends the saturation/Color Glass Condensate formalism (reviewed by Iancu (2003), Weigert (2005), and Jalilian-Marian (2005)) to incorporate transverse spin asymmetries for the first time. The mechanism differs from the Sivers effect (Sivers, 1989) where both spin dependence and phase originate in the wave function squared, and from the Collins mechanism (Collins, 1992) where the phase is generated in the fragmentation function. The authors note that the STSA increases with increasing projectile x_F and is a non-monotonic function of k_T, in qualitative agreement with experimental data. Future work should explore STSA generation from the wave function squared (the Sivers-type mechanism) and determine whether the saturation scale in peripheral collisions is large enough for perturbative validity.
