# Nuclear bound state of eta'(958) and partial restoration of chiral symmetry in the eta' mass

> The in-medium mass of the $η^{\prime}$ meson is discussed in a context of partial restoration of chiral symmetry in nuclear medium. The $η^{\prime}$ mass is expected to be reduced by order of 100 MeV at the saturation density. The reduction is a consequence of the suppression of the anomaly effect on the $η^{\prime}$ mass induced by partial restoration of chiral symmetry. This strong attraction in $η^{\prime}$ nuclear systems does not accompany large absorption of $η^{\prime}$ into nuclear matter. This leads to the possibility of so narrow bound states of the $η^{\prime}$ meson in nuclei to be seen in hadronic reactions with light nuclear targets.

## Key claim
The authors argue that partial restoration of chiral symmetry in nuclear matter suppresses the U_A(1) anomaly effect on the eta' meson mass, leading to a mass reduction of order 100 MeV at saturation density without accompanying large absorption. This attraction is specifically a consequence of the interplay between chiral symmetry breaking and the anomaly, meaning the mass reduction occurs only insofar as chiral symmetry is partially restored.

## Method
The authors use a symmetry argument based on SU(3)_L x SU(3)_R chiral multiplets to show that the eta-eta' mass splitting requires chiral symmetry breaking, and that the U_A(1) anomaly effect on the eta' mass is necessarily tied to the chiral condensate. They construct a simple phenomenological optical potential V_eta'(r) = V_0 * rho(r)/rho_0 with Woods-Saxon density distributions, and solve the Klein-Gordon equation to obtain bound state spectra for eta' in C-11. Formation spectra for the C-12(pi+,p)C-11⊗eta' reaction at 1.8 GeV/c incident pion momentum are calculated using the approach developed in earlier work by the same group.

## Key numbers
- The eta' mass is expected to be reduced by order of 100 MeV at the nuclear saturation density (rho_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3})
- Assuming 30% reduction of the quark condensate in nuclear medium, one could expect an order of 150 MeV attraction for the eta' meson
- The absorption width of the eta' meson at saturation density is as small as around 30 MeV, based on transparency ratio measurements by Nanova (2011)
- The elementary pi+ n -> eta' p forward differential cross section is estimated to be 100 μb/sr in the laboratory frame
- The level spacing of eta' bound states in a nucleus with mass number A is characterized as hbar omega ~ 40 A^{-1/3} MeV
- For the C-12 target, the upper limit for the level width is Gamma = -2 Im V ~ 50 MeV, compared to Gamma = 25-30 MeV extracted from transparency ratio measurements

## Context
This work extends theoretical studies of the eta' meson in finite density hadronic matter by Pisarski (1983), Kunihiro (1989), and Kapusta (1995), and builds on Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model calculations by Costa (2002) suggesting 150 MeV mass reduction and Nagahiro (2006) reporting 250 MeV reduction. The authors connect their mechanism to experimental observations of deeply bound pionic atoms by Suzuki (2002) and Friedman (2003, 2004, 2005), which established partial restoration of chiral symmetry with ~30% reduction of the quark condensate. The work enables experimental searches for eta'-nucleus bound states in hadronic reactions with light nuclear targets (A ~ 10 to 20), particularly using the (pi+,p) reaction, and suggests that observing such bound states would provide insight into the U_A(1) anomaly effects on hadrons.
