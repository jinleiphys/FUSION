# A Microscopic Cluster Description of 12C

> We investigate both bound and resonance states in 12C embedded in a three-α-cluster continuum using two distinct three-cluster microscopic models. The first one relies on the Hyperspherical Harmonics basis to enumerate the channels describing the three-cluster continuum. The second model incorporates both Gaussian and Oscillator basis functions, and reduces the three-cluster problem to a two-cluster one, in which a two-cluster subsystem is described by a set of pseudo-bound state states. It is shown that the results agree well with comparable calculations from the literature.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the AMHHB (Algebraic Model in a Hyperspherical Harmonics Basis) provides a fully microscopic description of bound and resonance states in 12C as a three-alpha cluster system, but the results depend on the choice of nucleon-nucleon interaction parameters (Minnesota potential with u=0.94) and the maximal Hypermomentum Kmax=14 for even parity states, with convergence achieved only at Kmax=12 for resonances.

## Method

The authors applied the AMHHB model to 12C as a three-alpha cluster system, using a Hyperspherical Harmonics basis to enumerate three-cluster channels and an oscillator basis for radial behavior. They solved the Schrodinger equation via the Algebraic Method (Modified J-Matrix method) for both bound and scattering states, implementing correct asymptotic boundary conditions through effective charges. The wave functions were used to compute correlation functions and density distributions in both coordinate and momentum space to analyze the spatial configuration of the three alpha particles.

## Key numbers

- The ground state of 12C displays an almost equilateral triangle configuration with inter-alpha distance approximately 3 fm
- The first 0+ resonance state (Hoyle state analog) has energy 0.68 MeV above the three-alpha threshold with total width 2.79 keV, decaying predominantly through the K=0 channel (partial width 2.79 keV)
- The first 2+ resonance appears at E=2.73 MeV with total width 8.75 keV, with partial widths of 6.11 keV (K=2 channel) and 3.84 keV (K=4 channel)
- A second 2+ resonance at E=3.11 MeV has total width 246.78 keV, decaying mainly through the K=4 channel (partial width 278.89 keV)
- The 4+ resonance at E=5.60 MeV has total width 0.55 keV, distributed across K=4 (0.23 keV), K=6 (0.15 keV), and K=8 (0.16 keV) channels
- The 1- resonance at E=3.52 MeV has total width 0.21 keV, decaying almost entirely through the K=3 channel (0.206 keV)
- The 3- resonance at E=0.67 MeV has total width 8.34 keV, decaying exclusively through the K=3 channel
- For the first 0+ resonance, the prolate triangular configuration (two alphas at ~3.5 fm, third at ~5 fm) has approximately 6 times more weight than the linear chain configuration

## Context

This work extends the AMHHB approach previously applied to 6He, 6Be, and 5H (Vasilevsky 2001, 2007) to the Borromean nucleus 12C, which has no bound two-cluster subsystems. The results are compared to calculations by Arai (2006) using Complex Scaling and the analytical continuation of the S-matrix, showing reasonable agreement for the 8Be subsystem. The paper contradicts earlier suggestions by Alvarez-Rodriguez (1995), Maruyama (1992), and others that some 12C resonances have a dominant linear three-alpha chain structure, finding instead that the first 0+ resonance favors a prolate triangular configuration. The method enables extraction of partial decay widths and spatial correlation functions for three-cluster resonances, which can be applied to other Borromean systems.
