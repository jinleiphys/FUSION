# On the analytic solution of the pairing problem: one pair in many levels

> We search for approximate, but analytic solutions of the pairing problem for one pair of nucleons in many levels of a potential well. For the collective energy a general formula, independent of the details of the single particle spectrum, is given in both the strong and weak coupling regimes. Next the displacements of the solutions trapped in between the single particle levels with respect to the unperturbed energies are explored: their dependence upon a suitably defined quantum number is found to undergo a transition between two different regimes.

# On the analytic solution of the pairing problem: one pair in many levels

## Key claim

The paper derives approximate but analytic formulas for the pairing Hamiltonian eigenvalues for one pair of nucleons in many levels, claiming that the collective energy formula is independent of the details of the single-particle spectrum in both strong and weak coupling regimes, and that the trapped solutions' dependence on a quantum number undergoes a transition between two regimes, but these results are limited to the one-pair case and rely on expansions that converge only for specific ranges of the coupling parameter α.

## Method

The authors start from the pairing Hamiltonian in the Grassmann (Bargmann-Fock) representation, reduce the problem to a secular equation for one pair, and then expand the collective energy in terms of the moments (variance, skewness, kurtosis) of the single-particle level distribution using a parameter α = 2σ/(GΩ). For the trapped solutions between single-particle levels, they isolate the two nearest poles in the secular equation, apply the Euler-McLaurin formula to replace sums by integrals, and solve the resulting analytic equation for the displacement variable z.

- Collective energy expansion: E ≈ −GΩ + 2ē − 4σ²/(GΩ) + 8γσ³/(G²Ω²) − 16(1+c)σ⁴/(G³Ω³) (Equation n6.17), where σ is the variance, γ the skewness, and c the kurtosis of the single-particle level distribution
- For the harmonic oscillator with 5 levels and G̃ = 1/2 (strong coupling), the collective state lies below the lowest single-particle level; for G̃ = 1/15 (weak coupling), the force is too weak to organize collective motion
- For ²¹⁰Pb with G ≈ 0.1 MeV, the parameters are α = 0.53 and β = 0.89, placing the system in the strong coupling regime
- For the harmonic oscillator with G̃ = 0.1 and N = 12 levels, the exact collective energy is −17.68 ħω₀, while the O(α⁴) approximation gives −17.68 ħω₀ and the Euler-McLaurin second-order approximation gives −17.68 ħω₀
- For the harmonic oscillator with G̃ = 0.2 and N = 12 levels, the exact collective energy is −53.66 ħω₀, with the O(α⁴) approximation yielding −53.66 ħω₀
- For ²¹⁰Pb trapped levels, the exact energies range from −6.903 MeV (ν=2) to −3.043 MeV (ν=7), with the first-order approximation z₁ agreeing to within 0.001 for several levels

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier study (Barbaro, Fomenko, Molinari, Quaglia, 2001) which first noted the parabolic pattern of trapped solutions in a harmonic oscillator well. It provides analytic formulas that connect the collective energy to global features of the single-particle spectrum (variance, skewness, kurtosis), going beyond the standard numerical diagonalization approach of Richardson (1964) and Rowe (1970). The results enable quick estimates of pairing energies for one pair in any potential well without full numerical solution, and the Euler-McLaurin treatment opens the way to studying the infinite-level limit and the transition between coupling regimes.
