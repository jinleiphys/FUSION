# Eigenstate Estimation for the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) Hamiltonian

> We show how multi-level BCS Hamiltonians of finite systems in the strong pairing interaction regime can be accurately approximated using multi-dimensional shifted harmonic oscillator Hamiltonians. In the Shifted Harmonic Approximation (SHA), discrete quantum state variables are approximated as continuous ones and algebraic Hamiltonians are replaced by differential operators. Using the SHA, the results of the BCS theory, such as the gap equations, can be easily derived without the BCS approximation. In addition, the SHA preserves the symmetries associated with the BCS Hamiltonians. Lastly, for all interaction strengths, the SHA can be used to identify the most important basis states -- allowing accurate computation of low-lying eigenstates by diagonalizing BCS Hamiltonians in small subspaces of what may otherwise be vastly larger Hilbert spaces.

## Key claim

The Shifted Harmonic Approximation (SHA) can accurately approximate multi-level BCS Hamiltonians of finite systems in the strong pairing interaction regime by mapping them to multi-dimensional shifted harmonic oscillator Hamiltonians, while also providing useful basis state selection for weaker interactions.

## Method

The authors replace discrete quantum state variables (quasi-spin projection quantum numbers \(m_p\)) with continuous variables \(x_p = m_p/j_p\), converting the algebraic BCS Hamiltonian into a differential operator. They then expand this operator about the minimum of the resulting harmonic oscillator potential up to bilinear terms in \((x_p - x_{op})\) and \(\partial/\partial x_p\), yielding a \((k-1)\)-dimensional shifted harmonic oscillator Hamiltonian for a \(k\)-level system. The SHA eigenfunctions are evaluated at the discrete grid points corresponding to the original basis states to approximate the exact eigenstates.

## Key numbers

- For a 4-level model with \(N=28\) pairs, \(j=[7,8,9,10]\), and \(\epsilon=[0.5,2.3,6.1,7.3]\), the exact Hamiltonian matrix is \(3231 \times 3231\).
- At interaction strength \(g=0.150\), the SHA predicts the first excitation energy \(\{1,0,0\}\) as 15.06 (arbitrary units), while diagonalization in the SHA-selected subspace gives 15.03.
- At \(g=0.010\), the SHA predicts the \(\{1,0,0\}\) excitation energy as 3.61, while diagonalization in the 50 most important \(\mathfrak{su}(2)\) basis states gives 3.650313.
- At \(g=0.045\), the SHA predicts the \(\{1,0,0\}\) excitation energy as 3.07, while diagonalization in the 300 most important \(\mathfrak{su}(2)\) basis states gives 3.60.

## Context

This work extends the Shifted Harmonic Approximation introduced by Chen et al. (SHA) to multi-level systems, providing a number-conserving alternative to the traditional BCS approximation (BCS) which violates particle-number conservation for finite systems. The approach contrasts with the Richardson-Gaudin method (Richardson, Gaudin) which is limited to special integrable cases of BCS Hamiltonians and produces numerically challenging solutions. The SHA enables accurate computation of low-lying eigenstates by diagonalizing in small subspaces, and its predictions of mean level occupancies match BCS theory while additionally providing number-conserving fluctuations.
