# Atomic parity violation in cesium and implications for new physics

> Recent high-precision measurements of atomic parity-nonconserving transitions between the 6S and 7S states of cesium allow for a determination of the weak nuclear charge with a precision of 1.3%, providing an improved test of the standard model at low energy. Implications for new physics are examined in terms of low energy effects on the weak charge, in particular contact interactions and scalar leptoquark limits. Prospects for further improvements are described.

## Key claim

The 1.3% precision measurement of the weak nuclear charge in cesium provides an improved low-energy test of the standard model, but the atomic theory uncertainty (0.89) now dominates the total error, limiting the sensitivity to new physics until theoretical calculations improve.

## Method

The author uses the measured parity-nonconserving transitions between the 6S(F=3) to 7S(F=4) and 6S(F=4) to 7S(F=3) states of 133Cs from Wood et al. (1997), taking a linear combination that eliminates nuclear spin-dependent contributions to extract the weak charge Q_W. The shift in Q_W from the standard model prediction is then used to set 95% C.L. lower bounds on the mass scale of four-fermion contact interactions and on the mass-to-coupling ratio M/g for scalar leptoquarks. For leptoquarks, the cesium bounds are combined with Tevatron direct search data from the D0 collaboration and with the high-Q^2 event excess at HERA to produce combined limits in the (M, g) plane.

## Key numbers

- Weak charge of cesium: Q_W = -72.11 ± 0.27 (experimental) ± 0.89 (atomic theory)
- Standard model prediction for Q_W: -72.88 ± 0.06
- Weinberg angle extracted from cesium data: sin^2 θ_W(m_Z)_MSbar = 0.2267 ± 0.0040 (for Higgs mass 300 GeV)
- 95% C.L. lower bounds on contact interaction scales Λ^q_ij: range from 7.4 TeV to 12.3 TeV depending on quark type and chiral structure
- 95% C.L. lower bounds on scalar leptoquark mass-to-coupling ratio M/g: 1500 GeV (S_L), 2300 GeV (S_R), 2200 GeV (D_L), 2300 GeV (D_R), 2700 GeV (T), 2500 GeV (tilde S), 2500 GeV (tilde D)
- For a leptoquark mass of 200 GeV, 95% C.L. coupling bounds: g_L,R < 0.09, g_tilde D < 0.08
- Preferred coupling for a 200 GeV tilde D leptoquark from HERA fit: g = 0.034; for D_R: g = 0.017

## Context

This work extends the atomic parity violation program in cesium, which previously had a factor of 7 worse precision (Noecker et al., 1988), by using the improved measurement from Wood et al. (1997). The derived bounds on contact interactions and scalar leptoquarks complement constraints from high-energy colliders such as LEP and the Tevatron, and are directly relevant to interpreting the excess of high-Q^2 events observed at HERA. The author notes that future improvements in atomic theory calculations (Blundell et al., 1992) could reduce the error on sin^2 θ_W from cesium to 0.0013, making it a more significant low-energy test of the standard model.
