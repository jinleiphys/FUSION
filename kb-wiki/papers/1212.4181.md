# Viability of carbon-based life as a function of the light quark mass

> The Hoyle state plays a crucial role in the helium burning of stars that have reached the red giant stage. The close proximity of this state to the triple-alpha threshold is needed for the production of carbon, oxygen, and other elements necessary for life. We investigate whether this life-essential condition is robust or delicately fine-tuned by measuring its dependence on the fundamental constants of nature, specifically the light quark mass and the strength of the electromagnetic interaction. We show that there exist strong correlations between the alpha-particle binding energy and the various energies relevant to the triple-alpha process. We derive limits on the variation of these fundamental parameters from the requirement that sufficient amounts of carbon and oxygen be generated in stars. We also discuss the implications of these results for an anthropic view of the universe.

## Key claim

The Hoyle state's proximity to the triple-alpha threshold is not as finely tuned as previously thought; variations of the light quark mass of 2-3% are unlikely to be catastrophic to the formation of life-essential carbon and oxygen, and the central values of the relevant parameters are even compatible with a complete lack of fine-tuning (a vanishing derivative of the triple-alpha energy with respect to pion mass).

## Method

The authors performed first-principles nuclear lattice simulations using chiral effective field theory and Monte Carlo methods on a periodic cubic lattice with spacing 1.97 fm and length 11.82 fm. They computed the derivatives of the energies of the Hoyle state, ⁸Be ground state, and ⁴He ground state with respect to the pion mass (which encodes the light quark mass dependence) and the electromagnetic fine-structure constant, treating higher-order terms in perturbation theory and generating approximately 10⁷ independent configurations on the JUQUEEN supercomputer.

## Key numbers

- The measured energy of the Hoyle state is ε = 379.47(18) keV above the triple-alpha threshold, with total width Γ_tot = 8.3(1.0) eV and radiative width Γ_γ = 3.7(5) meV.
- The derivative of the triple-alpha energy ε with respect to pion mass is ∂ε/∂M_π = -0.572(19)Ā_s - 0.933(15)Ā_t + 0.064(16), where Ā_s and Ā_t parameterize the pion-mass dependence of the inverse spin-singlet and spin-triplet NN scattering lengths.
- The electromagnetic shifts are Q(ΔE_b) = 1.19(8) MeV, Q(ΔE_h) = 2.80(10) MeV, and Q(ε) = 3.99(9) MeV.
- A variation of α_em by ±100 keV / Q(ε) ≈ 2.5% is compatible with carbon and oxygen formation.
- The most up-to-date NNLO analysis gives Ā_s = 0.29^{+0.25}_{-0.23} and Ā_t = -0.18^{+0.10}_{-0.10}, with ratio Ā_s/Ā_t = -1.6^{+1.0}_{-1.7}.
- Carbon-based life survives at least a 0.7% shift in the light quark mass m_q, and variations of 2-3% are unlikely to be catastrophic.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Livio et al., who manually modified ε and found a tolerance of about 60 keV, and by Oberhummer et al. (2000), who used a nuclear cluster model to find a 0.5% window for the strong force and 4% for the Coulomb force. The present results provide the first ab initio connection to QCD's fundamental parameters (the light quark masses) and show strong correlations between the alpha-particle binding energy and the triple-alpha energy differences, as speculated by Livio et al. and Weinberg. Future lattice QCD calculations of NN systems are needed to reduce the uncertainty in Ā_s and Ā_t and make more definitive statements about larger parameter variations.
