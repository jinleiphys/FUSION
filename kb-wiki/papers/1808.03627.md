# Black Hole Accretion Disk Diffuse Neutrino Background

> We study the cosmic MeV neutrino background from accretion disks formed during collapsars and the coalescence of compact-object mergers. We provide updated estimates, including detection rates, of relic neutrinos from collapsars, as well as estimates for neutrinos that are produced in mergers. Our results show that diffuse neutrinos detected at HyperK would likely include some that were emitted from binary neutron-star mergers. The collapsar rate is uncertain, but at its upper limit relic neutrinos from these sources would provide a significant contribution to the Cosmic Diffuse Neutrino Background.

## Key claim

The cosmic MeV neutrino background from accretion disks formed in collapsars and compact-object mergers could contribute significantly to the total diffuse neutrino flux, but this conclusion depends critically on the uncertain collapsar rate; at its upper limit, collapsar relic neutrinos would provide a contribution comparable to the supernova relic neutrino background, while binary neutron-star mergers are likely to contribute some detectable neutrinos at Hyper-Kamiokande even under conservative assumptions.

## Method

The authors convolved neutrino spectra from two accretion disk models, a fully relativistic steady-state model (Chen and Beloborodov) and a pseudo-relativistic hydrodynamical simulation (Just et al.), with cosmological event rates for collapsars (failed supernovae) and compact-object mergers (NS-NS and BH-NS). They calculated the diffuse electron antineutrino flux by integrating over cosmological redshift, accounting for gravitational redshift and bending from the black hole, and then computed detection rates at Super-Kamiokande and Hyper-Kamiokande via the inverse beta decay reaction.

## Key numbers

* For the steady-state model with black hole spin a=0.95 and accretion rate 3 solar masses per second, the disk neutrino spectra are larger than the supernova spectrum except for the a=0 model at energies below 14 MeV.
* The optimistic collapsar (failed supernova) rate at z=0 is approximately 0.0074 per solar mass per year per cubic megaparsec, scaled by (1+z)/10 for redshift evolution.
* NS-NS merger rates at z=0 range from approximately 10^-6 to 10^-3 per year per Milky Way Equivalent Galaxy, with a recent gravitational-wave-based estimate of 1540^{+3200}_{-1220} Gpc^-3 yr^-1.
* The total detection rate at Super-Kamiokande (32 kton fiducial volume) for collapsar neutrinos ranges from the pessimistic case (0.1x unnova rate with C0 model at 3 solar masses per second) to the optimistic case (unnova rate with Ca model at 9 solar masses per second), with corresponding rates at Hyper-Kamiokande (560 kton) scaled by a factor of 17.5.
* For NS-NS mergers, the optimistic detection rate uses the Ca model with 9 solar masses per second and low-metallicity common-envelope rates, while the pessimistic case uses the C0 model with 3 solar masses per second and standard merger rates.
* The hydrodynamical model Ja (spin a=0.8) with an estimated emission time of 2 seconds, assuming a NS-NS merger in a high-metallicity galaxy with standard evolution, yields a detection rate shown in the figures.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the cosmic MeV neutrino background, particularly the supernova relic neutrino background studied by Kaplinghat (1999), Beacom (2003), Ando (2003-2004), and Lunardini (2006-2010), and the failed supernova neutrino background studied by Lunardini (2009), Keehn (2010), and Nakazato (2015). It updates the collapsar neutrino background estimates of Nagataki (2002) using improved disk models and modern event rates, and provides the first estimates of the diffuse neutrino background from compact-object mergers. The results enable future comparisons with observations at Hyper-Kamiokande, DUNE, JUNO, and other next-generation detectors, and motivate further study of neutrino oscillations in accretion disks, which the authors note will be addressed in future work.
