# Finite hadronization time and unitarity in quark recombination model

> The effect of finite hadronization time is considered in the recombination model, and it is shown that the hadron multiplicity turns out to be proportional to the initial quark density and unitarity is conserved in the model. The baryon to meson ratio increases rapidly with the initial quark density due to competition among different channels.

## Key claim

The paper shows that introducing a finite hadronization time into the quark recombination model ensures unitarity (total hadron multiplicity becomes proportional to initial quark number), and that the baryon-to-meson ratio increases monotonically with the initial constituent quark density due to competition between meson and baryon production channels.

## Method

The author models hadronization as a time-dependent process where constituent quarks recombine into mesons and baryons at finite rates, with the quark density decreasing over time as hadrons are produced. Two limiting cases are considered: (1) fixed volume (rapid expansion compensates for hadronization shrinkage) and (2) shrinking volume (no expansion, constant parton density). The coupled rate equations for meson and baryon production are solved analytically or numerically, introducing a scaled initial density parameter \( u = 3A_2\rho_0/A_1 \) that encodes the competition between channels.

## Key numbers

- For the fixed-volume case with only one meson species, the total meson multiplicity equals the initial number of constituent quarks: \( N^M = V\rho_0 = N_q \).
- For a single hadron species with \( n \) constituent quarks in the fixed-volume case: \( N^n = 2V\rho_0/n = 2N_q/n \).
- In the fixed-volume case with both mesons and baryons, the baryon-to-meson ratio increases almost linearly with the scaled density \( u \); at \( u \approx 8 \) the baryon yield equals the meson yield, and at \( u = 20 \) the ratio exceeds 2.
- For the shrinking-volume (no expansion) case, the baryon-to-meson ratio is \( N^B/N^M = u/3 \), which is larger than the fixed-volume case for the same \( u \).

## Context

This work extends the quark recombination model (also called coalescence model) used to explain RHIC phenomena such as the high proton-to-pion ratio at \( p_T \approx 3 \) GeV/\(c\) and constituent quark number scaling of elliptic flow. It directly addresses the unitarity violation pointed out by earlier authors (e.g., in works cited as mv, mol, lm) where hadron yields scaled quadratically or cubically with quark density. The paper enables predictions for baryon-to-meson ratios at LHC energies, where initial quark densities will be higher, and suggests that the measured \( p/\pi \) ratio can be used to infer the initial parton density just before hadronization.
