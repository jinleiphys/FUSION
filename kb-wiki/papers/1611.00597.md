# Uncertainty propagation within the UNEDF models

> The parameters of the nuclear energy density have to be adjusted to experimental data. As a result they carry certain uncertainty which then propagates to calculated values of observables. In the present work we quantify the statistical uncertainties of binding energies, proton quadrupole moments, and proton matter radius for three UNEDF Skyrme energy density functionals by taking advantage of the knowledge of the model parameter uncertainties. We find that the uncertainty of UNEDF models increases rapidly when going towards proton or neutron rich nuclei. We also investigate the impact of each model parameter on the total error budget.

## Key claim

The statistical uncertainties of UNEDF Skyrme energy density functionals increase rapidly when going towards proton- or neutron-rich nuclei, but the reported standard deviations (0.3–3.0 MeV for binding energies) are still far larger than experimental precision (few keV), and most theoretical binding energies do not overlap with experimental values even when including error bars, indicating underlying model deficiency rather than parameter optimization issues.

## Method

The authors used the HFBTHO code to solve Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations for Skyrme energy density functionals in an axially symmetric harmonic oscillator basis with 20 oscillator shells, applying the Lipkin-Nogami method for approximate particle-number restoration. Statistical uncertainties were propagated from the covariance matrices of the UNEDF0, UNEDF1, and UNEDF2 parameter sets using finite-difference derivatives of observables with respect to model parameters. Binding energies, two-neutron separation energies, proton quadrupole moments, and proton matter root-mean-square radii were calculated for isotopic and isotonic chains of even-even and odd-even nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy standard deviations: 0.5–3.0 MeV for UNEDF0, 0.4–1.7 MeV for UNEDF1, and 0.3–1.5 MeV for UNEDF2
- Experimental binding energy precision is of the order of few keV (reference to Audi (2012) and Wang (2012))
- Electron binding energy correction used: B_E ≈ -1.433 × 10^{-5} Z^{2.39} MeV
- Equilibrium density ρ_c set to 0.16 fm^{-3} for pairing energy density
- Convergence criterion for HFB equations: norm of matrix difference less than 10^{-7}
- Basis size: 20 oscillator shells

## Context

This work extends the uncertainty quantification efforts in nuclear density functional theory, following earlier statistical analyses by Gao (2013), Schunck (2015), and Kortelainen (2015), and applies the eigenmode decomposition method previously used by Niksic (2015) for the DD-PC1 functional. The results enable identification of which model parameters dominate error budgets in neutron-rich regions, particularly symmetry energy parameters a_sym^NM and L_sym^NM for UNEDF0, and more distributed contributions across parameters for UNEDF2, providing guidance for future optimization of Skyrme energy density functionals toward spectroscopic-quality predictions.
