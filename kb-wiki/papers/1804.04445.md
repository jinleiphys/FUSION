# Giant Dipole Resonance Parameters of Ground-State Photoabsorption: Experimental Values with Uncertainties

> Updated values and corresponding uncertainties of Isovector Giant Dipole Resonance (GDR) parameters which are obtained by the least-squares fitting of theoretical photoabsorption cross sections to experimental data are presented. The theoretical photoabsorption cross sections are taken as a sum of the components corresponding to the excitation of the GDR and quasideuteron photodisintegration. The current compilation is an extension and improvement of the earlier compilations of Lorentzian parameters for ground-state photoneutron and photoabsorption cross sections and covers experimental data made available up to June 2017.

## Key claim

Updated values and uncertainties of Isovector Giant Dipole Resonance (GDR) parameters are presented, obtained by least-squares fitting of theoretical photoabsorption cross sections (sum of GDR and quasideuteron components) to experimental data, but the parameters are strictly Lorentzian fit parameters valid only in energy intervals near the GDR peak, and the assumption that these Lorentzian parameters equal the true GDR parameters is poor for light nuclei where photoproton reactions are important.

## Method

The theoretical photoabsorption cross section was taken as a sum of a Standard Lorentzian (SLO) or Simplified Modified Lorentzian (SMLO) GDR component plus a quasideuteron photodisintegration component. For each nuclide, the parameters (resonance energy, width, and weight in units of the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn sum rule) were determined by minimizing the chi-squared deviation between the theoretical cross section and experimental data from the EXFOR database, using the CERN MINUIT package with the MINOS procedure for uncertainty estimation. The fitting energy interval was chosen near the GDR peak, typically between the one-neutron and three-neutron separation energies, and data were fitted with either one Lorentzian (spherical nuclei) or two Lorentzians (axially deformed nuclei).

## Key numbers

- Parameters are given for 144 isotopes from Li-6 to Pu-239 and for 19 elements of natural isotopic composition, totaling 475 entries.
- GDR parameters were determined for 164 datasets, including 14 new isotopes and 13 natural elements not in the previous compilation (Plujko et al., 2011).
- GDR parameters for 7 datasets (C-12, N-15, natural Ar, natural Fe, Co-59, natural Zr) were excluded due to poor fit quality.
- The ratio of mean chi-squared values for SMLO to SLO models over all nuclei is 1.09 in the fitting interval, 0.76 on the low-energy tail, and 0.74 over the full experimental energy range (up to 30 MeV).
- The quasideuteron cross section formula uses a constant factor of 397.8 mb times NZ/A, with the photon energy in MeV.
- The Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn sum rule is given as sigma_TRK = 60 * NZ/A = 15A(1-I^2) mb*MeV, where I = (N-Z)/A.
- The GDR energy uncertainties typically do not exceed about 10%.

## Context

This work extends and improves the earlier compilations of Lorentzian parameters for ground-state photoneutron and photoabsorption cross sections by Berman and Fultz (1975), Berman et al. (1987), Dietrich and Berman (1988), and the previous update by Plujko, Capote, and Gorbachenko (2011). It also adds parameters for nuclides not present in the RIPL-3 database (Capote et al., 2009). The new database enables more reliable modeling of E1 gamma-ray cascades in highly excited nuclei, verification of theoretical approaches for nuclear structure (deformations, velocity-dependent forces, shape transitions), and provides input for modern nuclear reaction codes such as EMPIRE (Herman et al., 2007) and TALYS (Koning et al., 2007).
