# The ${ϕNN,J/ψNN,η_c NN}$ systems based on HAL QCD interactions

> We investigate the existence of bound states and resonances in the ${ϕNN, J/ψNN, η_c NN}$ systems using HAL QCD interactions for ${ϕN, J/ψN}$, and ${η_c N}$. We employ the Gaussian expansion method to solve the complex-scaled Schrödinger equation and find no resonances or bound states in the ${J/ψNN}$ and ${η_c NN}$ systems. We estimate the interaction between charmonium and nuclei, concluding that the $J/ψ$ or $η_c$ is likely to bind with ${}^3\mathrm{H}$, ${}^3\mathrm{He}$, ${}^4\mathrm{He}$, and heavier nuclei. For the $ϕNN$ system, the lattice QCD $ϕN\left({ }^2 S_{1 / 2}\right)$ interaction is absent. We combine the $ϕp$ correlation function analysis and HAL QCD results in Model A. We assume the spin-spin interactions for $J/ψN$ and $ϕN$ systems are inversely proportional to their masses in Model B. Model A predicts a stronger $ϕN({}^2 S_{1/2})$ interaction and permits a two-body bound state, whereas Model B suggests the interaction is attractive but too weak to form a bound state. Both models predict bound states for the $I(J^P) = 0(0^-)$ and $0(1^-)$ $ϕNN$ systems. In Model A, these states are deeply bound with binding energies exceeding 15 MeV and remain existent when considering parameter uncertainties. In contrast, these states are very loosely bound in Model B, with binding energies below 1 MeV and an existent probability of about 60\% when parameter uncertainties are considered. In both models, there exist very loosely bound $I(J^P) = 0(2^-)$ three-body states which resemble a $ϕ$-d atom with the $ϕ$ meson surrounding the deuteron, but their existences are sensitive to parameter uncertainties. No bound states or resonances are found in the isovector $I(J^P) = 1(1^-)$ $ϕNN$ system.

## Key claim
The paper investigates whether bound states or resonances exist in the ϕNN, J/ψNN, and ηcNN systems using HAL QCD lattice interactions, finding that while J/ψNN and ηcNN show no such states, the ϕNN system can host bound states depending on the model used for the ϕN(²S₁/₂) interaction, with Model A predicting deeply bound states and Model B predicting very loosely bound states that are sensitive to parameter uncertainties.

## Method
The authors solved the complex-scaled Schrödinger equation using the Gaussian expansion method (GEM) with S-wave projected potentials from HAL QCD for ϕN(⁴S₃/₂), J/ψN, and ηcN interactions, combined with a modified Malfliet-Tjon potential for nucleon-nucleon interactions. For the ϕN(²S₁/₂) channel where lattice QCD cannot extract the potential directly, they constructed two models: Model A fitted the ϕp correlation function data, while Model B assumed spin-spin interactions inversely proportional to hadron masses based on J/ψN potentials. The complex scaling method was employed to distinguish bound states from scattering states and to search for resonances.

## Key numbers
- ϕN(⁴S₃/₂) scattering length: a₀ = -1.44 fm, effective range: r_eff = 2.36 fm
- ϕN(²S₁/₂) Model A: bound state with binding energy 18.4 MeV, rms radius 1.27 fm, scattering length a₀ = 1.76 fm
- ϕN(²S₁/₂) Model B: no bound state, scattering length a₀ = -2.19 fm, effective range r_eff = 1.88 fm
- J/ψN(⁴S₃/₂) scattering length: a₀ = -0.30 fm, effective range: r_eff = 3.28 fm
- J/ψN(²S₁/₂) scattering length: a₀ = -0.38 fm, effective range: r_eff = 2.67 fm
- ηcN(²S₁/₂) scattering length: a₀ = -0.22 fm, effective range: r_eff = 3.61 fm
- NN(³S₁) deuteron binding energy: 2.41 MeV, rms radius: 3.84 fm
- Model A predicts ϕNN I(JP) = 0(0⁻) and 0(1⁻) bound states with binding energies exceeding 15 MeV
- Model B predicts ϕNN I(JP) = 0(0⁻) and 0(1⁻) bound states with binding energies below 1 MeV and about 60% existence probability under parameter uncertainties
- Both models predict very loosely bound I(JP) = 0(2⁻) ϕNN states resembling a ϕ-d atom, sensitive to parameter uncertainties
- No bound states or resonances found in the isovector I(JP) = 1(1⁻) ϕNN system
- No bound states or resonances found in J/ψNN or ηcNN systems

## Context
This work extends previous studies on charmonium-nucleon and ϕ-nucleon interactions using the most recent HAL QCD lattice potentials (Lyu 2022, Lyu 2024), which are more reliable than earlier phenomenological potentials such as those used by Gao (2000), Belyaev (2007, 2009), and Sofianos (2010). The authors address the unresolved ϕN(²S₁/₂) interaction by proposing two alternative models, contrasting with the single-channel spin-averaged approach used by Filikhin (2024). The finding that J/ψ and ηc do not bind with single nucleons but likely bind with ³H, ³He, ⁴He, and heavier nuclei provides guidance for future experimental searches for charmonium-nucleus bound states.
