# Origin of a sensitive dependence of calculated $ββ$-decay amplitudes on the particle-particle residual interaction

> In the present work the sensitivity of the QRPA calculation results to a realistic residual interaction is analyzed in the framework of the approach of Refs. \cite{Rum98,Rodin05}. Both Gamow-Teller (GT) and Fermi (F) \bb-decay amplitudes $M^{2ν}$, along with the corresponding energy-weighted sum rules $S$, are calculated. General expressions relating $S$ to a realistic residual particle-particle interaction are derived, which show a pronounced sensitivity of $S$ to the singlet-channel interaction in the case of F transitions, and to the triplet-channel interaction in the case of GT transitions. Decompositions of $M^{2ν}$, as well as the monopole transition contributions to $M^{0ν}$, are obtained by the method of Refs. \cite{Rum98,Rodin05}. It is shown that in most of the cases almost the whole dependence of $M^{2ν}$ and $M^{0ν}$ on the particle-particle renormalization parameter $g_{pp}$ is accounted for by the $g_{pp}$-dependence of the corresponding sum rules $S$. Thus, the $g_{pp}$-sensitivity of calculated $M^{2ν}$ and $M^{0ν}$ is unavoidable since it is dictated by the generic structure of the $ββ$ amplitudes. Finally, a better isospin-consistent way of a renormalization of the realistic residual particle-particle interaction to use in QRPA calculations is suggested.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the well-known sensitivity of calculated double-beta decay matrix elements to the particle-particle renormalization parameter \(g_{pp}\) is unavoidable because it originates from the generic structure of the decay amplitudes themselves, specifically from the energy-weighted sum rules \(S\) that depend exclusively on the residual particle-particle interaction; the authors show this sensitivity is not an artifact of the QRPA but is dictated by the SU(4)-symmetry-breaking part of the interaction.

## Method

The authors apply the identity transformation approach of Rumyantsev and Rodin (1998) and Rodin (2005) to partition both the two-neutrino (\(2\nu\beta\beta\)) and neutrinoless (\(0\nu\beta\beta\)) double-beta decay matrix elements into a smooth part and a term proportional to an energy-weighted sum rule \(S\). They derive general analytic expressions for \(S\) in terms of a realistic residual interaction (the Brueckner \(G\)-matrix from Bonn-CD, Argonne V18, Nijmegen I, and Bonn-C potentials) and compute the sum rules and matrix elements within the QRPA for the decays \({}^{76}\)Ge\(\rightarrow{}^{76}\)Se, \({}^{100}\)Mo\(\rightarrow{}^{100}\)Ru, and \({}^{130}\)Te\(\rightarrow{}^{130}\)Xe, using both small and large single-particle bases.

## Key numbers

* For the Fermi sum rule \(S^{2\nu}_0\), the zero crossing occurs very close to \(g'_{pp}=1\) (where \(g'_{pp}\equiv g_{pp}/g_{pair}\)), in agreement with the derived analytic expression.
* The ratio \(S_1 = 3 S_F\) at \(g'_{pp}=0\) comes out almost exact in the calculation.
* The parameter \(\gamma_{1}\) (the ratio \(S_{1}^{(pp)}/S_{1}^{(pair)}\)) ranges from approximately 1.3 (Bonn-C potential) to 1.5 (Bonn-CD potential) across all nuclei and basis sizes studied.
* For the \({}^{76}\)Ge system in the shell-model single-particle basis, the Gamow-Teller sum rules \(S_{1}^{(pair)}\) and \(S_{1}^{(pp)}\) are strongly underestimated, and \(\gamma_{1}\) is almost three times too small compared to the full QRPA basis.

## Context

This work extends the earlier identity-transformation approach of Rumyantsev (1998) and Rodin (2005), which used a schematic separable interaction, to realistic residual interactions derived from nucleon-nucleon potentials. The paper directly addresses the "\(g_{pp}\) problem" first identified by Vogel and Zirnbauer (1986), who showed that the two-neutrino double-beta decay matrix element is highly sensitive to the particle-particle interaction strength. The results enable a more isospin-consistent renormalization scheme for QRPA calculations, where different isospin channels of the residual interaction are renormalized separately, allowing simultaneous fitting of pairing gaps and double-beta decay matrix elements without spurious isospin breaking.
