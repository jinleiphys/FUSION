# Linear realization of SU(3) parity doublet model for octet baryons with bad diquark

> We construct a linear $SU(3)_L \times SU(3)_R$ parity doublet model for octet baryons. Our model employs the $(3,\bar{3}) + (\bar{3},3)$ and $(3,6) + (6,3)$ chiral representations while excluding the $(8,1) + (1,8)$ representation. Through systematic analysis, we demonstrate that the $(3,6) + (6,3)$ representation containing symmetric ``bad'' diquarks, despite being energetically disfavored, is essential for reproducing the correct baryon mass hierarchy, particularly the $Σ$$Ξ$ mass ordering. The model incorporates both spontaneous and explicit chiral symmetry breaking, with the latter implemented through bare quark mass terms that properly account for $SU(3)$ flavor breaking effects. Our numerical analysis successfully reproduces the ground-state octet baryon masses and predicts the spectrum of excited states up to 2.5 GeV. For the experimentally challenging $Ξ$ sector, we provide specific predictions for spin-parity assignments: identifying $Ξ(1950)$ as the first positive-parity excitation. The analysis reveals that ground states are dominated by the $(3,\bar{3}) + (\bar{3},3)$ representation, consistent with the preference for ``good'' diquark configurations, while the $(3,6) + (6,3)$ contribution remains crucial for the mass spectrum.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the $(3,6) + (6,3)$ chiral representation containing flavor-symmetric "bad" diquarks, despite being energetically disfavored, is essential for reproducing the correct baryon mass hierarchy (particularly the $\Sigma$-$\Xi$ mass ordering) in a linear $SU(3)_L \times SU(3)_R$ parity doublet model, but only when the mixing between the $(3,\bar{3}) + (\bar{3},3)$ and $(3,6) + (6,3)$ representations is not large.

## Method

The authors construct a linear $SU(3)_L \times SU(3)_R$ parity doublet model for octet baryons using the $(3,\bar{3}) + (\bar{3},3)$ and $(3,6) + (6,3)$ chiral representations, deliberately excluding the $(8,1) + (1,8)$ representation. They incorporate both spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking through meson field vacuum expectation values (with $\alpha = \beta = f_\pi = 93$ MeV and $\gamma = 2f_K - f_\pi = 127$ MeV) and explicit chiral symmetry breaking through bare quark mass terms. The model parameters are fitted to experimental ground-state octet baryon masses, and the resulting mass matrices are diagonalized to obtain predictions for excited states up to 2.5 GeV.

## Key numbers

- $f_\pi = 93$ MeV and $f_K = 110$ MeV used as inputs for the meson field vacuum expectation values $\alpha = \beta = f_\pi$ and $\gamma = 2f_K - f_\pi = 127$ MeV
- Ground-state masses used for fitting: $m_N = 939$ MeV, $m_\Lambda = 1116$ MeV, $m_\Sigma = 1193$ MeV, $m_\Xi = 1318$ MeV
- The relation $m_N + m_\Lambda - m_\Sigma - m_\Xi = -456$ MeV is derived from experimental masses
- The model predicts the spectrum of excited states up to 2.5 GeV
- $\Xi(1950)$ is identified as the first positive-parity excitation in the $\Xi$ sector
- The axial charge for the $(3,\bar{3})$ representation is $g_A^{(3,\bar{3})} = 1$, while for the $(3,6)$ representation it is $g_A^{(3,6)} = 5/3$, compared to the experimental value $g_A^{\mathrm{exp}} \approx 1.27$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous attempts in Minamikawa (2023) and Gao (2024) to construct linear $SU(3)_L \times SU(3)_R$ parity doublet models for octet baryons. The earlier model using only $(3,\bar{3}) + (\bar{3},3)$ and $(8,1) + (1,8)$ representations failed to reproduce the correct $\Sigma$-$\Xi$ mass ordering, while the comprehensive model including all representations required ten coupling parameters with substantial ambiguities. The present simplified model with only $(3,\bar{3}) + (\bar{3},3)$ and $(3,6) + (6,3)$ representations reduces the parameter space while incorporating explicit chiral symmetry breaking through quark mass terms, which was absent in the previous comprehensive analysis. This framework enables systematic exploration of baryon properties across different density regimes, from vacuum to dense matter relevant for neutron stars, where the linear realization becomes increasingly important as the system approaches chiral restoration.
