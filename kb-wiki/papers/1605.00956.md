# Multidimensionally-constrained covariant density functional theories --- nuclear shapes and potential energy surfaces

> The intrinsic nuclear shapes deviating from a sphere not only manifest themselves in nuclear collective states but also play important roles in determining nuclear potential energy surfaces (PES's) and fission barriers. In order to describe microscopically and self-consistently nuclear shapes and PES's with as many shape degrees of freedom as possible included, we developed multidimensionally-constrained covariant density functional theories (MDC-CDFTs). In MDC-CDFTs, the axial symmetry and the reflection symmetry are both broken and all deformations characterized by $β_{λμ}$ with even $μ$ are considered. We have used the MDC-CDFTs to study PES's and fission barriers of actinides, the non-axial octupole $Y_{32}$ correlations in $N = 150$ isotones and shapes of hypernuclei. In this Review we will give briefly the formalism of MDC-CDFTs and present the applications to normal nuclei.

## Key claim

Multidimensionally-constrained covariant density functional theories (MDC-CDFTs) allow the simultaneous breaking of both axial and reflection symmetries, enabling the self-consistent inclusion of all deformation parameters βλμ with even μ; the formalism has been applied to study potential energy surfaces, fission barriers, and non-axial octupole correlations in actinide and trans-actinide nuclei, but the quantitative agreement between different models remains rather large for some nuclei.

## Method

The authors developed MDC-CDFTs by expanding the Dirac spinors in an axially deformed harmonic oscillator basis and imposing the V4 symmetry group (invariance under reflections in the y-z and x-z planes and 180° rotation about the z axis). Four types of relativistic density functionals were implemented: meson exchange or point-coupling interactions combined with either non-linear or density-dependent couplings, with pairing correlations treated by either the Bogoliubov approach or the BCS method. The densities and potentials were expanded in Fourier series in the azimuthal angle, and the single-particle Hamiltonian matrix was block-diagonalized using the simplex quantum number.

## Key numbers

- The truncation for the upper (large) component of the Dirac spinor uses basis states with [nz/fz + (2nρ+|ml|)/fρ] ≤ Nf, where fz = max(bz/b0,1) and fρ = max(bρ/b0,1); for the lower (small) component, the truncation is Ng = Nf + 1.
- The oscillator basis deformation βbasis is defined through ωz = ω0 exp(−√(5/4π) βbasis) and ωρ = ω0 exp(√(5/16π) βbasis), with ω0 = (ωz ωρ²)^(1/3).
- The non-axial octupole Y32 effects were investigated in four N = 150 isotones.
- Third minima and triple-humped barriers were explored in light actinides, with the appearance of the third minima dependent on the functional used in the MDC-RMF calculations.

## Context

This work extends the covariant density functional theory (CDFT) framework, which has been used extensively for ground and excited states across the nuclear chart (Serot 1986, Ring 1996, Bender 2003, Vretenar 2005, Meng 2006), by implementing multidimensional constraints that break both axial and reflection symmetries. The MDC-CDFTs build on prior non-relativistic mean field calculations (Girod 1983) and earlier relativistic calculations (Rutz 1995, Abusara 2010) that showed the importance of triaxial and octupole distortions for fission barriers, and enable systematic studies of potential energy surfaces with all even-μ deformations included self-consistently. The approach enables future investigations of nuclear shapes in unknown regions including superheavy nuclei and nuclei near drip lines.
