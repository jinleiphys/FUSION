# Microscopic Study of Wobbling Motions in Hf and Lu Nuclei

> One of the most striking findings in the recent high-spin spectroscopy is the discovery of one-phonon, and possibly double-phonon, excitation of the nuclear wobbling rotational bands. In this talk, we first review the properties of observed wobbling motions, and discuss the failure and success of the possible interpretation in terms of the simple rotor model. Then, we further present results of our microscopic study in Hf and Lu nuclei by means of the theoretical framework, the cranked mean-field and the random phase approximation.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the simple rotor model fails to explain the observed wobbling motion in Lu and Hf nuclei because it cannot reproduce the decreasing wobbling energy with increasing rotational frequency, and that a microscopic cranked mean-field plus random phase approximation (RPA) framework is necessary, though even this approach underestimates the out-of-band B(E2) transition strengths by a factor of 2-3.

## Method

The authors apply the random phase approximation (RPA) on top of a cranked mean-field to describe the uniform rotation of the yrast band and the small-amplitude fluctuations of the wobbling phonon. The residual interaction is a QQ-type force with strengths fixed by the requirement of decoupling Nambu-Goldstone modes, leaving no adjustable parameters once the mean-field parameters are set. The RPA eigen-mode equation yields the wobbling phonon energy and the three moments of inertia, which are then compared to the simple rotor model formulas.

## Key numbers

- The triaxial superdeformed (TSD) bands in Lu and Hf nuclei have deformation parameters typically epsilon_2 approximately 0.4 and gamma approximately +20 degrees (Lund convention), compared to normal deformed states with epsilon_2 approximately 0.2 and gamma approximately 0 degrees.
- The out-of-band B(E2) values for the wobbling transitions in Lu isotopes are 100 or more Weisskopf units, while the in-band B(E2) values are about 500 or more Weisskopf units; the out-of-band transitions are about 20% of the in-band transitions.
- The calculated wobbling energy in 168Hf at hbar*omega_rot = 0.3 MeV is shown as a function of deformation parameters epsilon_2 and gamma, and pairing gap Delta_n,p up to about 0.5 MeV.
- In 163Lu, the calculated wobbling energy is smaller than the experimental data and stays almost constant with rotational frequency, while the experimental wobbling energy decreases with omega_rot.
- The calculated out-of-band B(E2) values in 163Lu are about 2-3 times smaller than the experimentally measured values.

## Context

This work extends the earlier rotor-model description of wobbling motion by Bohr and Mottelson (1975) and the particle-rotor coupling model of Hamamoto (2002, 2003), which could not explain the decreasing wobbling energy with spin observed in experiments on 163Lu by Odegard et al. (2001) and Jensen et al. (2002). The microscopic RPA framework was originally proposed by Marshalek (1979) and examined in realistic cases by Matsuzaki (1990) and Shimizu and Matsuzaki (1995). The present calculations show that the alignment of proton i13/2 quasiparticles is crucial for the appearance of the wobbling mode, and that the mean-field parameters must be treated as functions of rotational frequency to improve agreement with experiment. The work identifies the need for a microscopic theory that can reproduce the extremely collective out-of-band E2 transitions, which approach the macroscopic rotor limit.
