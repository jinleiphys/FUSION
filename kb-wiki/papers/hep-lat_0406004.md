# Recent Developments in Quark Nuclear Physics

> We provide an overview of recent work exploring the quark-mass dependence of hadronic observables and the associated role of chiral nonanalytic behavior due to the meson-cloud of hadrons. In particular, we address an issue of great current interest, namely the degree of model independence of results obtained through a controlled extrapolation of lattice QCD simulation results. Physical insights gained from this research are highlighted. We emphasize how chiral effective field theory formulated with a finite-range regulator provides a reliable and model-independent extrapolation to the physical world.

## Key claim

Chiral effective field theory formulated with a finite-range regulator provides a reliable and model-independent extrapolation of lattice QCD results to the physical quark mass, but this claim is conditional on having accurate lattice data for pion masses of approximately 0.3 GeV or less, and the extrapolation shows minimal model-dependence only when the self-energy integrals are evaluated with a finite ultraviolet regulator rather than dimensional regularisation.

## Method

The authors fit lattice QCD data for hadron masses using the general effective field theory expansion of the nucleon mass about the SU(2) chiral limit, evaluating the baryon-pion self-energy integrals with five different ultraviolet regulators: dimensional regularisation (truncated power series), sharp cut-off, monopole, dipole, and Gaussian. The coefficients of the non-analytic terms are constrained to their phenomenological values, while the analytic parameters and the regulator scale are determined by fitting lattice data, allowing a test of regulator dependence in the extrapolation to the physical pion mass.

## Key numbers

- The leading non-analytic coefficient for the nucleon mass, \(c_{\rm LNA}\), when allowed to vary as a free fit parameter in a truncated power series fit to lattice data, gave a value of approximately \(-0.76\ {\rm GeV}^{-2}\), compared to the physical value of \(-5.6\ {\rm GeV}^{-2}\), a factor of 8 smaller.
- Using a dipole regulator and neglecting the \(\Delta\pi\) contribution, the power series expansion of the nucleon mass to order \(m_\pi^6\) breaks down above \(m_\pi \sim 250\ {\rm MeV}\).
- The transition between smooth constituent-quark-like behaviour and rapid chiral non-analytic variation occurs for quark masses below about 60 MeV, corresponding to pion masses greater than typically 400–500 MeV.
- The mass splitting \(\Delta = m_\Delta - m_N\) is 292 MeV, which sets the scale for the \(\Delta\pi\) branch cut.

## Context

This work extends the approach of chiral perturbation theory (Gasser and Leinweber, 1980s) by addressing the convergence problems of the dimensionally regulated expansion, which Wright (2001) showed diverges for \(m_\pi > 0.4\) GeV with a sharp cut-off and around 0.25 GeV with a dipole regulator. The finite-range regulator method builds on earlier studies by Leinweber et al. (1999) and provides a framework that enables accurate extrapolation of lattice QCD results for hadron masses, magnetic moments, charge radii, and parton distribution moments to the physical quark mass, while also suggesting a novel approach to modelling hadron structure (Cloet et al., 2002).
