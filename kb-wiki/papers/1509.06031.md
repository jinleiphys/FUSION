# Nuclear annihilation by antinucleons

> We examine the momentum dependence of $\bar p$$p$ and $\bar n$$p$ annihilation cross sections by considering the transmission through a nuclear potential and the $\bar p p$ Coulomb interaction. Compared to the $\bar n p$ annihilation cross section, the $\bar p p$ annihilation cross section is significantly enhanced by the Coulomb interaction for projectile momenta below $p_{\rm lab} <$ 500 MeV/$c$, and the two annihilation cross sections approach the Pomeranchuk's equality limit [JETP Lett. {\bb 30}, 423 (1956)] at $p_{\rm lab}\sim 500$ MeV/$c$. Using these elementary cross sections as the basic input data, the extended Glauber model is employed to evaluate the annihilation cross sections for $\bar n$ and $\bar p$ interaction with nuclei and the results compare well with experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Coulomb interaction significantly enhances the $\bar p p$ annihilation cross section relative to the $\bar n p$ cross section for projectile momenta below 500 MeV/$c$, and that the two cross sections become equal above this momentum, consistent with Pomeranchuk's equality prediction; however, the authors note that a broad enhancement in the experimental $\bar n p$ annihilation cross section around 200-300 MeV/$c$ remains an unresolved puzzle.

## Method

The authors model antinucleon-nucleon annihilation using a partial-wave decomposition with an ingoing-wave strong absorption model, assuming a square well nuclear potential of depth 85 MeV and contact radius 0.97 fm, and include the Coulomb Gamow factor for $\bar p p$ interactions. They then use these elementary cross sections as input to an extended Glauber model that incorporates initial-state Coulomb corrections and in-medium momentum changes to compute annihilation cross sections for $\bar p$ and $\bar n$ on nuclei with $A > 1$.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear contact radius $R = 0.97$ fm and strong interaction potential $V_0 = 85$ MeV used for the square well potential.
- For $\bar p p$ annihilation, the power-law exponent $x = -1.544$ in the momentum range 30-50 MeV/$c$, approaching the expected $1/v^2$ limit ($x = -2.0$).
- For $\bar n p$ annihilation, the power-law exponent $x = -1.080$ in the momentum range 30-95 MeV/$c$, close to the expected $1/v$ behavior ($x = -1.0$).
- The $\bar p$Pt annihilation cross section at $p_{\rm lab}=100$ MeV/$c$ reaches approximately 9000 mb, corresponding to a black disk radius of about 17 fm, compared to a geometrical touching radius of about 8 fm.
- The Coulomb enhancement factor $(1-V_c(R_c)/E)$ for $\bar p$Pt at 100 MeV/$c$ is about 4-5, enhancing the cross section from about 2000 mb to about 9000 mb.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Glauber model study (Lee 2014) by replacing a semiempirical $1/v$ parametrization with a proper partial-wave treatment of nuclear and Coulomb interactions, and by incorporating energy-dependent $\bar p n$ cross sections rather than a fixed ratio. The results confirm Pomeranchuk's equality prediction (Pomeranchuk 1956) at high momenta and support a $q$-$\bar q$ pairing annihilation model over a same-flavor-only model. The improved elementary cross sections enable more accurate predictions for antinucleon-nucleus annihilation at FAIR and CERN AD facilities, though discrepancies for $\bar n$ on light nuclei (C, Al) below 400 MeV/$c$ remain to be resolved.
