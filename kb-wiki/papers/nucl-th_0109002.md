# A dynamical chiral bag model

> We study a dynamical chiral bag model, in which massless fermions are confined within an impenetrable but movable bag coupled to meson fields. The self-consistent motion of the bag is obtained by solving the equations of motion exactly assuming spherical symmetry. When the bag interacts with an external meson wave we find three different kinds of resonances: {\it fermionic}, {\it geometric}, and $σ$-resonances. We discuss the phenomenological implications of our results.

# A dynamical chiral bag model

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a spherically symmetric chiral bag model with massless fermions confined by an impenetrable but movable bag coupled to meson fields admits self-consistent classical solutions beyond the static case, provided the bag motion is determined by conservation of total energy and momentum. The abstract's claim of "solving the equations of motion exactly" is qualified by the assumption of spherical symmetry and the use of hedgehog solutions, and the resonances found depend on specific parameter choices (bag radius R₀ = 1 fm and f_π = 1 fm⁻¹) that are described as "representative" for showing qualitative features rather than quantitatively predictive.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled Dirac and Klein-Gordon equations for massless fermions inside a spherical bag with surface delta-function coupling to σ and π meson fields, using hedgehog ansätze that enforce spherical symmetry. They employ the null-lines method to determine the arbitrary functions Q(z) describing the fermion field and the meson field functions Σ(z) and Π(z) from the boundary conditions at the moving bag surface, using a fourth-order Runge-Kutta algorithm. For self-consistent motion, the bag radius evolution is derived from the continuity of the energy-momentum tensor across the surface, yielding an equation for ȧ that conserves total energy.

## Key numbers

- Bag radius R₀ = 1 fm and pion decay constant f_π = 1 fm⁻¹ are used as representative parameters for all numerical results
- Fermionic resonances occur at driving frequencies ν ≈ E_n − E_k, with an example shown at ν = 3.4/R₀ ≈ E₂ − E₁
- Geometric σ-resonances occur at ν = nπ/R₀ for integer n
- Parametric σ-resonances occur at ν = (2n+1)π/(2R₀) for integer n
- For parametric resonances, oscillation amplitudes ε > 0.005R₀ cause the frequency 2ν to become dominant in σ(R)
- Bag constant B = 1 fm⁻⁴ is used for self-consistent motion calculations (noted as unrealistically high but used for numerical stability)
- For three-quark calculations, the fermion source terms in the meson equations are multiplied by a factor of 3

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous proof (Colanero and Chu, 2000) that the original MIT bag model with massless quarks admits only static or constantly-expanding solutions, showing that adding chiral meson fields enables nontrivial dynamical bag behavior. It builds on the Chiral Bag model of Chodos and Thorn (1975) and the hedgehog solution approach of Chodos and Bhaduri, while addressing the fundamental consistency question left open by earlier dynamical bag studies such as Rebbi and DeGrand (1981) who used perturbation theory for small oscillations, and Kuti, Brown, and Meissner who employed adiabatic approximations. The results support the interpretation of radial baryon excitations (such as the Roper resonance) as collective breathing modes of the bag surface, as proposed by those earlier authors, while demonstrating that the σ-field excitation is the primary mechanism for bag expansion rather than direct fermion excitation.
