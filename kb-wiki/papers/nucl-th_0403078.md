# Pion entropy and phase-space densities in A+A collisions

> We propose a method to estimate the entropy of thermal pions in A+A collisions irrespective of unknown form of freeze-out (isothermal) hypersurface and transverse flows developed. We analyse the average phase-space densities and entropies of the thermal pions vs their multiplicities and collision energies. The behaviour of these values apparently indicates the deconfinement and chiral phase transitions in relativistic nuclear-nucleus collisions

## Key claim

The paper proposes a method to estimate the entropy of thermal pions in A+A collisions that does not require knowledge of the freeze-out hypersurface shape or transverse flow, but the method relies on the approximation of a uniform freeze-out temperature and density and uses a phenomenological factor $\kappa$ (0.65 for SPS, 0.7 for RHIC) to eliminate contributions from short-lived resonances.

## Method

The authors extract the average phase-space density (APSD) of thermal pions from experimental single-particle spectra and two-particle correlation functions using the Bertsch-Pratt parameterization, then use the factorization property of the locally-equilibrium distribution to relate the APSD to the pion chemical potential at a fixed freeze-out temperature of 120 MeV. With the chemical potential determined, the specific entropy per thermal pion is calculated from the equilibrium Bose-Einstein distribution function integrated over momentum. The analysis uses transverse spectra and interferometry radii from NA49 (SPS), CERES (SPS), STAR (RHIC), and PHENIX (RHIC) collaborations.

## Key numbers

- For $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 8.8$ GeV (SPS 40 AGeV): thermal pion APSD $(2\pi)^3\langle f(\sigma,y)\rangle^{\pi^{-}}_{th} = 0.125$, chemical potential $\mu_\pi = 30.5$ MeV, specific entropy $dS^{\pi^{-}}_{th}/dy \div dN^{\pi^{-}}_{th}/dy = 4.03$
- For $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 12.3$ GeV (SPS 80 AGeV): thermal pion APSD = 0.127, $\mu_\pi = 31.5$ MeV, specific entropy = 4.02
- For $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 17.3$ GeV (SPS 158 AGeV): thermal pion APSD = 0.137, $\mu_\pi = 37.7$ MeV, specific entropy = 3.96
- For $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 130$ GeV (RHIC): thermal pion APSD = 0.168, $\mu_\pi = 54$ MeV, specific entropy = 3.78
- The entropy rapidity density of thermal negative pions follows approximately $dS_{th}/dy = 28 + 3.6 \times dN_{th}/dy$
- The freeze-out temperature used throughout is $T_{th} = 120$ MeV
- The factor $\kappa$ is 0.65 for SPS and 0.7 for RHIC

## Context

This work extends the approach of Akkelin and Sinyukov (2002) for estimating phase-space overpopulation to the calculation of pion entropy, and builds on the factorization property first found in Nukleonika (1994) and used for particle number ratios by Braun-Munzinger, Stachel, and others. The results indicate that the specific entropy of thermal pions decreases with collision energy, approaching the massless limit $S/N \rightarrow 3.6$, which the authors interpret as possible evidence for deconfinement and chiral phase transitions. The method enables extraction of thermodynamic information from experimental data without assuming a specific freeze-out geometry, and the observed behavior of APSD and entropy could be used to constrain the temperature gap between hadronization and chemical freeze-out as discussed by P. Braun-Munzinger et al. (1999).
