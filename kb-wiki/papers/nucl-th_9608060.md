# Variation of hadron masses in finite nuclei

> Using a self-consistent, Hartree description for both infinite nuclear matter and finite nuclei based on a relativistic quark model (the quark-meson coupling model), we investigate the variation of the masses of the non-strange vector mesons, the hyperons and the nucleon in infinite nuclear matter and in finite nuclei.

## Key claim

Using a self-consistent Hartree description within the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, the authors find that hadron masses in finite nuclei decrease approximately in proportion to the number of non-strange quarks they contain, with the reduction scaling linearly with the scalar mean-field strength at low density.

## Method

The authors extended the QMC model to finite nuclei using a Born-Oppenheimer approximation and a mean-field effective Lagrangian density where the nucleon, vector meson, and hyperon masses depend only on the local scalar (sigma) field. They performed self-consistent Hartree calculations for both infinite nuclear matter and finite nuclei, treating the meson fields as classical mean fields that couple to confined quarks inside the hadrons rather than to point-like nucleons.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon effective mass in nuclear matter scales as \(M_N^{\star}/M_N \simeq 1 - 0.2 (\rho_B/\rho_0)\) at low density.
- The omega meson effective mass scales as \(m_{\omega}^{\star}/m_{\omega} \simeq 1 - 0.16 (\rho_B/\rho_0)\) at low density.
- The Lambda and Sigma hyperon effective masses scale as \(M_{\Lambda,\Sigma}^{\star}/M_{\Lambda,\Sigma} \simeq 1 - 0.11 (\rho_B/\rho_0)\) at low density.
- The Xi hyperon effective mass scales as \(M_{\Xi}^{\star}/M_{\Xi} \simeq 1 - 0.05 (\rho_B/\rho_0)\) at low density.
- In the interior of lead-208, the omega meson mass is reduced by about 15 percent, while the nucleon and sigma meson masses decrease by about 20 percent.
- The scaling relation \(\delta M_{\omega,\rho}^{\star} / \delta M_N^{\star} \approx \delta M_{\Lambda}^{\star} / \delta M_N^{\star} \approx 2/3\) and \(\delta M_{\Xi}^{\star} / \delta M_N^{\star} \approx 1/3\) holds for baryon densities up to about 3 times nuclear saturation density.

## Context

This work extends the QMC model, previously applied to infinite nuclear matter and finite nuclei using the local-density approximation, to self-consistent calculations of hadron mass variations in finite nuclei. The results are consistent with earlier studies using vector dominance models, QCD sum rules, and the Walecka model (QHD) that predicted decreasing vector meson masses in nuclear medium (Hatsuda and Lee, 1992). The scaling relation found here enables predictions for how different hadron species will shift in mass in heavy-ion collision experiments, providing a systematic way to interpret future ultra-relativistic heavy-ion data.
