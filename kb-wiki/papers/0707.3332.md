# Elliptic flow of deuterons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Using a dynamical model based on the $NN \to dπ$, $NNN \to dN$, and $NNπ\to dπ$ reactions and measured proton and pion transverse momentum spectra and elliptic flows, we study the production of deuterons and their elliptic flow in heavy ion collisions at RHIC. The results are compared with those from the coalescence model. The deviation of deuteron elliptic flow from the constituent nucleon number scaling expected from the coalescence model and the comparison with the experimental data are discussed in connection to the allowed nucleon phase space in these reactions.

## Key claim

Using a dynamical model based on the \(NN \to d\pi\), \(NNN \to dN\), and \(NN\pi \to d\pi\) reactions that conserve both energy and momentum, the authors find that the deuteron elliptic flow approximately follows the constituent nucleon number scaling predicted by the coalescence model at intermediate and large transverse momentum, but the scaling is not guaranteed at very low transverse momentum because the nucleon phase space allowed by energy-momentum conservation in the dynamical model excludes the region assumed in the coalescence model.

## Method

The authors calculated deuteron production rates from the two-body reaction \(NN \to d\pi\) and the three-body reactions \(NNN \to dN\) and \(NN\pi \to d\pi\) using hadronic cross sections based on empirically determined parameters. They used parameterized proton and pion transverse momentum spectra and elliptic flows from PHENIX experimental data as input, then multiplied the production rates by the hadronic matter volume at freeze-out (about 3,700 fm³) and a time interval of about 4 fm/c to obtain deuteron yields and elliptic flows, comparing results to those from the coalescence model using the Hulthén deuteron wave function.

## Key numbers

- The effective inverse slope parameter for the nucleon \(p_T\) spectrum was determined to be \(T_{\rm eff} = 295\) MeV from PHENIX minimum-bias proton data.
- The hadronic matter volume at freeze-out was taken as \(V \approx 3,700\) fm³, about 1/3 of the estimated volume in central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV.
- The time interval for deuteron production was chosen as \(\Delta\tau \approx 4\) fm/c to reproduce the measured deuteron spectrum.
- The nucleon elliptic flow parametrization used \(\alpha_N = 0.258\), \(\beta_N = 0.683\) GeV, and \(\lambda_N = 1.128\) GeV from PHENIX proton \(v_2\) data for 20-60% centrality.
- The pion elliptic flow parametrization used \(\alpha_\pi = 0.184\), \(\beta_\pi = 0.461\) GeV, and \(\lambda_\pi = 0.547\) GeV from PHENIX pion \(v_2\) data.
- The deuteron wave function parameters were \(\alpha_d = 0.23\) fm\(^{-1}\) and \(\beta_d = 1.61\) fm\(^{-1}\) for the Hulthén form.
- Contributions from three-body reactions were suppressed by several orders of magnitude compared to the two-body \(NN \to d\pi\) reaction.

## Context

This work extends the study of elliptic flow scaling from hadrons to deuterons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, building on the quark coalescence model predictions of constituent quark number scaling by Greco, Ko, and Levai (2003-2004) and the nucleon coalescence model for deuterons by Mattiello (1995) and Chen, Ko, and Lin (2003). The dynamical approach using \(NN \to d\pi\) reactions addresses concerns raised by McLerran (2007) and Rapp (2007) about the applicability of the coalescence model at low transverse momentum due to its neglect of energy conservation. The results enable future studies using non-equilibrium transport models such as the ART model to include radial flow effects and final-state interactions of deuterons, and they motivate precise measurements of both nucleon and deuteron elliptic flow at very low transverse momentum to resolve the sign discrepancy with preliminary STAR data.
