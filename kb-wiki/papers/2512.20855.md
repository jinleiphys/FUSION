# Formulation of Relativistic Dissipative Spin Hydrodynamics

> The primary objective of this thesis is to develop a consistent theoretical framework of dissipative hydrodynamics for a relativistic fluid with spin - hereafter referred to as relativistic dissipative spin hydrodynamics. In this framework, the dynamical description of a relativistic fluid requires a new macroscopic variable, the spin density, which is associated with a spin tensor. This tensor, defined as the expectation value of a rank-3 tensor operator in quantum field theory, contributes to the system's total angular momentum. The need for such a theory is motivated by recent measurements of spin polarization of hadrons produced in non-central relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Two distinct formulation methods are employed. The first is grounded in covariant thermodynamics and extends the conventional Navier-Stokes and Müller-Israel-Stewart theories of relativistic hydrodynamics by incorporating a spin tensor. The second is based on principles of relativistic quantum statistical mechanics, building upon and generalizing the foundational Zubarev approach. Both formulations aim to construct a closed system of evolution equations for the macroscopic variables and, via an entropy-current analysis, to identify the dissipative currents and their associated transport coefficients. These two approaches provide different perspectives for future applications focused on spin polarization measurements. Beyond its phenomenological relevance, the theory also opens several avenues for further theoretical developments. These include the verification of the thermodynamic relations employed in the first formulation method using microscopic frameworks, as well as a deeper understanding of the emerging transport coefficients - particularly those associated with the spin tensor - through microscopic modeling or data-driven parameter extraction.

# Formulation of Relativistic Dissipative Spin Hydrodynamics

## Key claim
This thesis develops a consistent theoretical framework for dissipative hydrodynamics of a relativistic fluid with spin, using two distinct formulation methods. The framework is limited to the case of zero chemical potential and assumes that gradients of the spin potential are of higher order in the hydrodynamic gradient expansion, meaning the entropy production is driven only by gradients of the flow four-velocity.

## Method
The first formulation extends conventional Navier-Stokes and Muller-Israel-Stewart theories by incorporating a spin tensor into covariant thermodynamics, performing an entropy-current analysis to identify dissipative currents and transport coefficients. The second formulation builds upon the Zubarev approach from relativistic quantum statistical mechanics, using irreducible decompositions of rank-2 and rank-3 tensors under SO(3,R) rotations to construct closed evolution equations for macroscopic variables including the spin density.

## Key numbers
- The energy-momentum tensor decomposition yields 24 independent components for a rank-3 tensor antisymmetric in its last two indices: 6 from the antisymmetric tensor S^{mu nu}, 1 from the scalar Sigma, 5 from the symmetric traceless Sigma_{(s)}^{<mu nu>}, 3 from the antisymmetric transverse Sigma_{(a)}^{mu nu}, and 9 from the fully orthogonal phi^{<lambda><mu><nu>}
- The sixteen-dimensional representation of rank-2 tensors under SO(3,R) decomposes into irreducible representations as 16 = (1 + 1 + 3 + 5) + (3 + 3), or equivalently (0 + 0 + 1 + 2) + (1 + 1) in angular momentum notation
- The four-dimensional vector representation decomposes as 4 = 1 + 3 (or 0 + 1 in j-notation)

## Context
This work extends the conventional relativistic hydrodynamics of Navier-Stokes and Muller-Israel-Stewart by adding spin as a macroscopic variable, motivated by spin polarization measurements of hadrons in non-central relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The decomposition methods build upon representation theory of SO(3,R) as described in references Gilmore (2006) and Tung (1985), and the irreducible tensor decomposition method is based exclusively on a reference by Daher (2025). The framework enables future applications to spin polarization measurements and opens avenues for verifying thermodynamic relations through microscopic modeling or data-driven parameter extraction.
