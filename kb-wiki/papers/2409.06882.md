# Unified and optimal frame choice for generalized parton distributions

> Reconstructing the internal three-dimensional quark and gluon structures of hadrons through generalized parton distributions (GPDs) from hard exclusive scattering processes is one of the most challenging tasks in nuclear and particle physics. In this paper, we introduce a new optimized reference frame that, for the first time, enables a unified view of all the reactions sensitive to GPDs and facilitates the interpretation of a variety of phase-space patterns that were previously hardly accessible and interpretable. Similarly to how the heliocentric description advanced our understanding of the solar system and gravitation, our new frame centers around a quasireal state, allows for a consistent separation of physical scales, and reveals a novel quantum interference mechanism.

# Unified and optimal frame choice for generalized parton distributions

## Key claim

The paper introduces a new "SDHEP frame" that centers kinematics around the intermediate quasireal state A* in single-diffractive hard exclusive processes, enabling a unified description of all 2→3 GPD-sensitive reactions with regular azimuthal modulations that cleanly separate different GPD contributions, but this separation is achieved only up to next-to-leading power in √(-t)/q_T, with higher-twist contributions generating additional harmonic modulations.

## Method

The authors define the SDHEP frame as the center-of-mass frame of the 2→2 hard scattering subprocess, with the A* state moving along the z-axis and the x-axis lying in the diffractive plane of the hadron h→h' transition. They decompose the total scattering amplitude into a diffractive structure function F and hard scattering coefficient H, each carrying distinct azimuthal phases (φ_S from the diffractive frame and φ from the SDHEP frame), then square the amplitude and trace over spin density matrices to obtain cross sections with explicit azimuthal modulation patterns. The formalism is applied to electroproduction (DVCS), photoproduction (dilepton production), and mesoproduction processes, with the BH subprocess (A* = γ*) contributing at leading power and GPD-sensitive channels (A* = [q\bar{q}] or [gg]) entering at next-to-leading power.

## Key numbers

- The hard scale q_T and soft scale √(-t) satisfy q_T ≫ √(-t), with the simulation using q_T ≥ 1 GeV and |t| ≤ 0.2 GeV²
- The electron beam energy used in the simulation is E_e = 12 GeV
- The BH subprocess with A* = γ* (j=1) contributes at leading power; amplitudes with (j+1) particles in A* are suppressed by a factor of √(-t)/q_T relative to amplitudes with j particles
- The photon electroproduction cross section at NLP contains eight polarization asymmetries, corresponding to the eight real degrees of freedom of the GPD moments
- In photoproduction, linear photon polarization ζ_γ introduces cos(4φ - 2φ_γ) modulation at LP and cos(3φ - 2φ_γ) modulation at NLP, plus four additional asymmetries (A_UT^NLP, A_LT^NLP, A_TT,1^NLP, A_TT,2^NLP)

## Context

This work extends the QCD factorization formalism for exclusive processes developed by Collins (1996), Ji (1998), and Qiu (2022) by providing a unified kinematic framework that resolves long-standing difficulties in separating GPD contributions from Bethe-Heitler backgrounds, a problem recognized since Belitsky (2001) and Guidal (2008). The SDHEP frame contrasts with the traditional Breit-frame approach used in DVCS analyses, where azimuthal modulations require harmonic fits up to N=4 and introduce additional systematic uncertainties. The formalism enables a complete extraction of GPD moments from existing Jefferson Lab data and future Electron-Ion Collider measurements, with the key advance being that the azimuthal modulations in φ and φ_S directly reflect the spin and parity of the exchange state A*, making the separation of different GPD channels unambiguous for the first time.
