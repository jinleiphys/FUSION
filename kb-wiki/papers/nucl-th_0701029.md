# Nuclear transparencies from photoinduced pion production

> We present a relativistic and cross-section factorized framework for computing nuclear transparencies extracted from A(γ,πN) reactions at intermediate energies. The proposed quantummechanical model adopts a relativistic extension to the multiple-scattering Glauber approximation to account for the final state interactions of the ejected nucleon and pion. The theoretical predictions are compared against the experimental ^4He(γ,p π^-) data from Jefferson Lab. For those data, our results show no conclusive evidence for the onset of mechanisms related to color transparency.

## Key claim
The authors present a relativistic, quantum-mechanical Glauber model for computing nuclear transparencies from A(γ,πN) reactions, and find that for the Jefferson Lab ^4He(γ,p π^-) data their results show no conclusive evidence for color transparency, in contrast to earlier semi-classical models that favored CT effects.

## Method
The model adopts a relativistic extension of the multiple-scattering Glauber approximation (RMSGA) to treat final-state interactions of the ejected nucleon and pion at the amplitude level, using relativistic mean-field wave functions from the σω-model (W1 parameterization). The cross section is factorized into an elementary γn→π^-p part and a distorted momentum distribution that incorporates the Glauber phase operator, with profile functions parameterized using interpolated NN and πN scattering data from the Particle Data Group and analyses by Arndt (2003) and Workman. Color transparency is implemented by replacing the total hadron-nucleon cross sections with effective ones that depend on the hadron formation length and the distance traveled since creation.

## Key numbers
- The RMSGA nuclear transparencies are systematically about 10% larger than those obtained in the semi-classical model of Gao, Holt and Pandharipande (1996).
- The RMSGA model predicts a rise in transparency for |t| values below 1.2 GeV^2, attributed to a minimum in the total proton-nucleon cross section.
- For the CT implementation, the hadronic expansion length uses ΔM^2 = 1 (GeV)^2 for the proton and ΔM^2 = 0.7 (GeV)^2 for the pion, with average transverse quark momentum k_t = 0.350 GeV/c.
- The A-dependence calculation was performed for ^4He, ^12C, ^16O, ^56Fe, and ^208Pb at |t| = 3.5 GeV^2 for θ^π_c.m. = 90° without color transparency.

## Context
This work extends the RMSGA framework previously applied to A(e,e'p) reactions (Ryckebusch 2003, Lava 2004) and A(p,2p) reactions (Van Overmeire 2006) to the photoinduced pion production channel. It provides the first quantum-mechanical model for computing transparencies from A(γ,πN) processes, contrasting with the semi-classical approach of Gao, Holt and Pandharipande (1996) and the later semi-classical approximation by Larson (2006). The model can be readily extended to electroproduction processes for comparison with forthcoming Jefferson Lab data.
