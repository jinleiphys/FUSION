# Dense QC$_2$D. What's up with that?!?

> We present recent updates and results from QC$_2$D (Two Colour QCD) simulations at non-zero baryon density, including progress toward determining the speed of sound.

## Key claim

The paper reports progress toward determining the speed of sound in dense QC\(_2\)D (two-colour QCD) using finer lattices and smaller diquark sources than previous studies, but the results are preliminary and the error analysis remains crude, with autocorrelations possibly present.

## Method

The authors performed lattice simulations of QC\(_2\)D with unimproved Wilson fermions and gauge action, using a diquark source term \(aj = 0.01, 0.015, 0.02, 0.03\) to lift low-lying eigenmodes in the superfluid phase, then extrapolating to \(j=0\). The pressure was obtained by integrating the quark number density with respect to chemical potential, corrected using the non-interacting lattice Stefan–Boltzmann form (Scheme II). The energy density was derived from the trace anomaly, which required evaluating the gluonic and fermionic components using beta functions determined from scale setting on the line of constant physics \(m_\pi/m_\rho \sim 0.81\). The speed of sound was then computed as the symmetric derivative of pressure with respect to energy density.

## Key numbers

* Lattice spacing \(a = 0.130\ \text{fm}\) for the fine lattice at \(\beta = 2.1\) and \(\kappa = 0.1577\).
* Onset chemical potential \(\mu_0 = m_\pi/2 = 0.223\) in lattice units.
* Diquark source values used: \(aj = 0.01, 0.015, 0.02, 0.03\).
* Beta functions for the fine lattice: \(\partial\beta/\partial a = -2.85 \pm 0.10\) and \(\partial\kappa/\partial a = 0.152 \pm 0.32\).
* The speed of sound squared \(C_s^2\) clearly exceeds the conformal limit of \(1/3\) above the onset chemical potential.
* The results are consistent with other recent QC\(_2\)D works (Iida et al., 2024; Itou et al., 2023; Iida et al., 2022).

## Context

This work extends previous QC\(_2\)D studies by the same group (Hands et al., 2006, 2010; Boz et al., 2013; Cotter et al., 2013) and by the Japanese group (Iida et al., 2024; Itou et al., 2023; Iida et al., 2022) by using a finer lattice and a larger range of diquark sources at smaller values. The observed behaviour of \(C_s^2\) exceeding the conformal limit is also seen in isospin QCD simulations (Brandt et al., 2022) and contrasts with perturbative predictions that \(C_s^2\) approaches \(1/3\) from below. The results enable future work with larger volumes, more configurations, and improved actions to reduce systematic uncertainties.
