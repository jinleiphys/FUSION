# Spectroscopy of Charmed and Bottom Hadrons using Lattice QCD

> We present preliminary results on the light, charmed and bottom baryon spectra using overlap valence quarks on the background of 2+1+1 flavours HISQ gauge configurations of the MILC collaboration. These calculations are performed on three different gauge ensembles at three lattice spacings (a ~ 0.12 fm, 0.09 fm and 0.06 fm) and for physical strange, charm and bottom quark masses. The SU(2) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory is used to extrapolate baryon masses to the physical pion mass and the continuum limit extrapolations are also performed. Our results are consistent with the well measured charmed baryons. We predict the masses of many other states which are yet to be discovered.

# Spectroscopy of Charmed and Bottom Hadrons using Lattice QCD

## Key claim

The authors present preliminary lattice QCD results for light, charmed, and bottom baryon spectra that are consistent with well-measured charmed baryons, and they predict masses for many undiscovered states; however, the results are preliminary, with chiral and continuum extrapolations still ongoing for several sectors, and the bottom baryon results use a non-relativistic (NRQCD) formulation rather than the fully relativistic overlap action used for lighter quarks.

## Method

Overlap valence quarks were used on three 2+1+1 flavor HISQ gauge ensembles from the MILC collaboration at lattice spacings of approximately 0.12 fm, 0.09 fm, and 0.06 fm. Light and charm quarks were treated with a unified overlap fermion action, while bottom quarks were treated with a non-relativistic NRQCD Hamiltonian improved through O(v^4). Chiral extrapolations to the physical pion mass were performed using SU(2) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) for singly charmed baryons, and continuum limit extrapolations were carried out using terms up to O((m_q a)^3) for mesons and O(a^2) for baryons.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacings: 0.1207(11) fm, 0.0888(8) fm, and 0.0582(5) fm, determined using the Omega_sss baryon mass and consistent with MILC r1 parameter measurements
- Tuned bare charm quark masses (am_c): 0.528, 0.425, and 0.29 on coarser to fine lattices respectively
- Observed hyperfine splitting in 1S bottomonium: 64 +/- 3 MeV, in good agreement with the experimental value of 62.3 +/- 3 MeV
- Extracted hyperfine splitting of 1S charmonia: 115 +/- 3 MeV, agreeing with the physical value of 113.5 +/- 0.5 MeV
- Predicted hyperfine splitting of B_c meson: 55 +/- 4 MeV
- Pseudoscalar meson mass range covered: from physical pion mass to about 5.5 GeV on coarser lattices, and from about 300 MeV to around 6 GeV on fine lattices

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD studies of heavy hadron spectroscopy by using overlap fermions for light through charm quarks combined with NRQCD for bottom quarks, building on prior lattice calculations by Alexandrou (2017), Brown (2014), Bali (2015), Briceno (2012), and Namekawa (2013). The authors note that their predictions for doubly charmed baryons predated the experimental discovery of Xi_cc++ by the LHCb collaboration (Aaij 2017), and their earlier work on Omega_c excited states (Padmanath 2013, 2014, 2015) successfully matched the five Omega_c0 resonances discovered by LHCb (Aaij 2017), identifying spin-parity assignments for these states. The results enable future experimental searches for undiscovered charmed-bottom baryons such as Omega_ccb and Omega_cbb states.
