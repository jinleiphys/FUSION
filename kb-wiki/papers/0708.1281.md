# Anomaly mediated neutrino-photon interactions at finite baryon density

> We propose new physical processes based on the axial vector anomaly and described by the Wess-Zumino-Witten term that couples the photon, Z-boson, and the omega-meson. The interaction takes the form of a pseudo-Chern-Simons term, $\sim ε_{μνρσ}ω^μZ^νF^{ρσ}$. This term induces neutrino-photon interactions at finite baryon density via the coupling of the Z-boson to neutrinos. These interactions may be detectable in various laboratory and astrophysical arenas. The new interactions may account for the MiniBooNE excess. They also produce a competitive contribution to neutron star cooling at temperatures >10^9 K. These processes and related axion--photon interactions at finite baryon density appear to be relevant in many astrophysical regimes.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a pseudo-Chern-Simons term coupling the photon, Z-boson, and omega-meson, derived from the Wess-Zumino-Witten term and the axial vector anomaly, induces new neutrino-photon interactions at finite baryon density; these interactions may account for the MiniBooNE low-energy excess and produce a competitive contribution to neutron star cooling at temperatures above about 10^9 K, but the authors note that the estimate for MiniBooNE is "extremely naive" and requires a more refined analysis including form-factor and coherence effects.

## Method

The authors derive a pseudo-Chern-Simons term of the form ∼ ε_{μνρσ} ω^μ Z^ν F^{ρσ} from the Wess-Zumino-Witten effective action, treating the ω meson as a gauge field coupled to baryon number. They integrate out the ω and Z bosons to obtain an effective interaction between the baryon current, the neutrino current, and the electromagnetic field strength, then compute the cross section for ν + N → ν + γ + N and the emissivity for γ → ν ν̅ in a neutron star core with superconducting protons.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section for neutrino-nucleon scattering: σ = 2.6 × 10^{-41} (E_ν/GeV)^6 (g_ω/10.0)^4 cm^2
- Estimated number of events at MiniBooNE from the anomaly process: ∼ 140 (g_ω/10)^4 events, based on ∼ 2 × 10^5 CCQE ν_μ events with σ_QE ∼ 0.9 × 10^{-38} cm^2 at E_ν ∼ 700 MeV
- Neutron star emissivity from the anomaly process: Q_ν^{anom} = 2.31 × 10^{22} erg s^{-1} cm^{-3} × m^{9/2} (g_ω/10)^4 e^{(-11.6 m/T_9)} (T_9)^{5/2}, where m = m_γ/(1 MeV) and T = T_9 × 10^9 K
- Standard modified Urca emissivity range for comparison: Q_ν^{mUrca} = (10^{18} – 10^{21}) × (T_9)^8 erg s^{-1} cm^{-3}
- Assumed typical neutron star density: 2ρ_0 = 5.6 × 10^{14} g cm^{-3}
- Assumed photon mass in superconducting neutron star core: m_γ ∼ 1 MeV
- Critical temperature for superfluid phase: T_c ∼ 10^{10} K

## Context

This work extends the application of the Wess-Zumino-Witten term, previously developed by Kaymakcalan, Rajeev and Schechter (1984) and Witten (1983), to include interactions between the ω meson, Z boson, and photon at finite baryon density. The proposed neutrino-photon interaction offers a potential explanation for the MiniBooNE excess reported by the MiniBooNE collaboration (2007) and provides a new cooling mechanism for neutron stars that is competitive with the modified Urca processes reviewed by Yakovlev et al. (2001). The authors note that similar interactions involving axions could be relevant for supernova bounds from SN1987A, and that the work enables future detailed studies of neutrino scattering on nuclei and early-stage neutron star evolution.
