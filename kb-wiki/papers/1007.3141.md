# The role of the $N^*(2080)$ resonance in the $\vecγ p \to K^+ Λ(1520)$ reaction

> We investigate the $Λ(1520)$ photo-production in the $\vecγ p \to K^+ Λ(1520)$ reaction within the effective Lagrangian method near threshold. In addition to the "background" contributions from the contact, $t-$channel $K$ exchange, and $s-$channel nucleon pole terms, which were already considered in previous works, the contribution from the nucleon resonance $N^*(2080)$ (spin-parity $J^P = 3/2^-$) is also considered. We show that the inclusion of the nucleon resonance $N^*(2080)$ leads to a fairly good description of the new LEPS differential cross section data, and that these measurements can be used to determine some of the properties of this latter resonance. However, serious discrepancies appear when the predictions of the model are compared to the photon-beam asymmetry also measured by the LEPS Collaboration.

## Key claim

The inclusion of the $N^*(2080)$ resonance (spin-parity $J^P = 3/2^-$) in an effective Lagrangian model provides a fairly good description of the LEPS differential cross section data for the $\vec{\gamma} p \to K^+ \Lambda(1520)$ reaction near threshold, but serious discrepancies appear when the model predictions are compared to the photon-beam asymmetry also measured by the LEPS Collaboration.

## Method

The authors constructed a tree-level effective Lagrangian model for the $\vec{\gamma} p \to K^+ \Lambda(1520)$ reaction, including background contributions from contact, $t$-channel $K$ exchange, and $s$-channel nucleon pole terms, plus a resonant $s$-channel $N^*(2080)$ pole term. They performed $\chi^2$-fits to the LEPS differential cross section data at forward $K^+$ angles (59 data points) to determine the unknown hadronic couplings $g_1$, $g_2$, cutoff parameters, and in some fits also the resonance mass, width, and electromagnetic couplings $f_1$, $f_2$.

## Key numbers

- The $N^*(2080)$ resonance mass was fitted to $2138 \pm 4$ MeV (fit B) or $2115 \pm 8$ MeV (fit C), and its total width to $168 \pm 10$ MeV (fit B) or $254 \pm 24$ MeV (fit C).
- The $\chi^2/dof$ values for fits including the $N^*(2080)$ resonance were 2.4 (fit A, 4 parameters), 1.4 (fit B, 6 parameters), and 1.2 (fit C, 8 parameters); a fit with only background contributions gave an unacceptable $\chi^2/dof \sim 24$.
- The predicted branching ratio $\Gamma_{N^* \to \Lambda^*K} / \Gamma_{N^*}$ ranged from $36 \pm 3\%$ (fit A) down to $7.5 \pm 2.8\%$ (fit C).
- The background cutoff parameter $\Lambda_s = \Lambda_t$ was fitted to $613 \pm 2$ MeV (fits A and B) or $604 \pm 2$ MeV (fit C).
- The photon-beam asymmetry $\langle \Sigma \rangle$ showed discrepancies of about two standard deviations with LEPS data for photon energies above 2 GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous effective Lagrangian studies of $\Lambda(1520)$ photo-production by Titov et al. (2005), Nam et al. (2005, 2006), and Toki et al. (2006), which failed to describe the forward-angle bump structure in the new LEPS data. The authors contradict the findings of Nam et al. (2006, 2007), who claimed that the $N^*(2080)$ resonance has negligible contribution to this reaction. The results enable improved determination of the poorly known properties of the $N^*(2080)$ resonance, which is listed as a two-star resonance in the Particle Data Group (2008) with only fair evidence for its existence.
