# Time Reversal Invariance Violation in Neutron Deuteron Scattering

> Time reversal invariance violating (TRIV) effects for low energy elastic neutron deuteron scattering are calculated for meson exchange and EFT-type of TRIV potentials in a Distorted Wave Born Approximation, using realistic hadronic strong interaction wave functions, obtained by solving three-body Faddeev equations in configuration space. The relation between TRIV and parity violating observables are discussed.

## Key claim
The paper calculates time reversal invariance violating (TRIV) effects in low-energy elastic neutron-deuteron scattering using both meson exchange and effective field theory (EFT) potentials, finding that the leading-order TRIV operators (n=1-5) dominate while higher-order operators (n>=6) contribute at least one order of magnitude less under naturalness assumptions. The authors note that existing estimates of CP-violating effects in nuclear reactions have "at least one order of magnitude of accuracy, or even worse."

## Method
TRIV observables were computed using Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) with non-perturbed three-body wave functions obtained by solving Faddeev equations in configuration space for the AV18+UIX strong interaction Hamiltonian. The TRIV nucleon-nucleon potentials were treated as perturbations, using both meson exchange models (pion, eta, rho, omega exchanges) and EFT approaches (pionless and pionful) with the same operator structure but different scalar functions. Matrix elements were calculated in jj-coupling scheme and transformed to lS coupling, considering only s-wave and p-wave contributions for low-energy neutron scattering.

## Context
This work extends previous calculations of parity-violating effects in neutron-deuteron scattering by Schiavilla (2008) and Song (2010) to the time-reversal violating case, using the same Faddeev methodology with AV18+UIX strong potentials. The results provide a benchmark for comparing TRIV effects in simple few-body systems versus complex nuclei, where enhancement factors of up to 10^6 have been predicted by Bunakov (1982). The calculations enable interpretation of future TRIV measurements at spallation neutron facilities (SNS at Oak Ridge, J-SNS at J-PARC) and can help distinguish between different CP-violation models (such as QCD theta-term versus CKM phase) based on the pattern of observed TRIV parameters.
