# On the sign of the pi-rho-omega coupling constant

> It is shown that the relative sign between the $NNω$ and $πρω$ coupling constants can be determined most sensitively from $ω$ production processes in $NN$ collisions. Recent data on these reactions clearly favor the sign of the $πρω$ coupling constant which is opposite to that inferred from studies of the photoproduction reaction in combination with the vector meson dominance assumption and used by many authors. Implication of this finding in the description of other reactions is discussed.

# On the sign of the pi-rho-omega coupling constant

## Key claim
The relative sign between the $NN\omega$ and $\pi\rho\omega$ coupling constants can be determined most sensitively from $\omega$ production processes in $NN$ collisions, and recent data favor a negative sign for $g_{\pi\rho\omega}$, opposite to the positive sign inferred from photoproduction studies combined with the vector meson dominance assumption. The authors acknowledge that their conclusion is model dependent, but argue it is robust given the strong interference effects observed.

## Method
The authors employed a Distorted Wave Born Approximation approach to describe $\omega$-meson production in $NN$ collisions, including nucleonic and mesonic currents with $NN$ initial and final state interactions. They calculated total and differential cross sections for $pp \to pp\omega$ and $pn \to d\omega$ using both positive ($g_{\pi\rho\omega}=+10$) and negative ($g_{\pi\rho\omega}=-10$) coupling constants, adjusting cutoff masses and the tensor-to-vector coupling ratio $\kappa_\omega$ to reproduce data at $Q=173$ MeV. The finite $\omega$ meson width was included, and the $NN$ final state interaction was treated exactly while the initial state interaction was treated within the on-shell approximation.

## Key numbers
- $g_{\pi\rho\omega}=+10$ (positive sign) and $g_{\pi\rho\omega}=-10$ (negative sign) were used as the two test values for the coupling constant
- With $g_{\pi\rho\omega}=+10$, the model underestimated the $pp \to pp\omega$ cross section near threshold by more than an order of magnitude
- With $g_{\pi\rho\omega}=-10$, the underestimation was reduced to only a factor of about 1.5
- Model parameters for the negative sign case: $\Lambda_N=1100$ MeV, $\Lambda_M=1000$ MeV, $\kappa_\omega=-2$
- With negative $g_{\pi\rho\omega}$ and $\kappa_\omega=0$, parameters were $\Lambda_N=990$ MeV, $\Lambda_M=950$ MeV
- The $\omega$ meson production threshold in $NN$ collisions is at $T_{\rm lab}=1.89$ GeV
- The $NN$ phase-shift analysis is unavailable above $T_{\rm lab}=1.3$ GeV
- The reduction factor due to initial state interaction for $pp \to pp\omega$ at $T_{\rm lab}=1.89$ GeV is about 0.45
- The $\omega$-exchange mechanism provides about 5% or less of the $\pi^0$ photoproduction total cross section at $E_\gamma = 300$ MeV
- Changing the sign in the $\omega$-exchange contribution would increase the total $\pi^0$ photoproduction cross section by a factor of $\sim 1.5$ at $E_\gamma = 800$ MeV

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of $\omega$ production in $NN$ collisions by the same group (Nakayama et al., 1998; Nakayama et al., 1999; Tsushima and Nakayama, 2003) and directly addresses the sign ambiguity in the $\pi\rho\omega$ coupling constant. The positive sign used by the Julich group (Gasparyan et al., 2003) and the Giessen group (Feuster and Mosel, 1998; Shklyar et al., 2004) in their coupled-channel models is contradicted by the $pp \to pp\omega$ data. The negative sign favored here is consistent with the effective chiral Lagrangian approach reviewed by Meissner (1988), though that derivation assumes a particular realization of vector meson dominance. The result enables more reliable extraction of nucleon resonance parameters from photoproduction data, particularly in the higher-mass resonance region where $\omega$-exchange becomes significant.
