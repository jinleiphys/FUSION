# Photons from Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions at Ultra-Relativistic Energies

> We compare the photon emission rates from hot hadronic matter with in-medium mass shift and Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP). It is observed that the WA98 data can be well reproduced by hadronic initial state with initial temperature $\sim 200$ MeV if the universal scaling of temperature dependent hadronic masses are assumed and the evolution of temperature with time is taken from transport model or (3+1) dimensional hydrodynamics. The data can also be reproduced by QGP initial state with similar initial temperature and non-zero initial radial velocity.

## Key claim

The WA98 photon data can be reproduced either by a hadronic initial state with initial temperature ~200 MeV assuming universal scaling of temperature-dependent hadronic masses, or by a QGP initial state with similar initial temperature and non-zero initial radial velocity; the paper explicitly states that it is difficult to distinguish between these two scenarios at present.

## Method

The authors calculated photon emission rates from hot hadronic matter (including π, ρ, ω, η, a₁ mesons) and from Quark Gluon Plasma using QCD Compton and annihilation processes. They incorporated in-medium mass modifications via a universal scaling hypothesis (m_V*/m_V = (1 - T²/T_c²)^λ with λ=1/2) and modeled space-time evolution using both (3+1) dimensional hydrodynamics and a transport model cooling law, comparing the resulting photon p_T spectra to WA98 Pb-Pb collision data at 158 A GeV.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature T_i ~ 196 MeV for the QGP initial state scenario
- Initial temperature T_i = 200 MeV for the hadronic initial state scenario with scaling hypothesis
- Critical temperature T_c = 160 MeV and freeze-out temperature T_f = 120 MeV used in the QGP scenario
- Hadronic initial state with T_i = 200 MeV and zero initial radial velocity reproduces WA98 data
- QGP initial state with initial radial velocity v_0 = 0.3 reproduces WA98 data
- dN/dy ~ 700 for impact parameter b ~ 3 fm
- n(b ~ 3 fm) ~ 660 effective nucleon-nucleon collisions
- σ_in ~ 30 mb for p-p inelastic cross section
- √s = 17.3 GeV for WA98 experiment
- τ_i = 1 fm/c assumed for thermalization time
- Transport model parameters: T_i = 200 MeV, T_∞ = 120 MeV, τ = 8 fm/c

## Context

This work extends the analysis of photon emission from heavy-ion collisions at CERN SPS energies, building on the WA98 Collaboration (1999) data and prior theoretical work on electromagnetic probes. The paper specifically addresses the in-medium modification of hadron masses as proposed by Brown and Rho (1991) and contrasts with the scenario of large collisional broadening of vector mesons (Rapp, 1999), finding that the broadening scenario (case iii) does not reproduce the observed photon excess in the region 1.5 ≤ p_T (GeV) ≤ 2.5. The results enable future discrimination between hadronic and QGP initial states at RHIC and LHC energies, though the authors note uncertainties in hard photon scaling, chemical equilibrium assumptions, and the validity of hard thermal loop approximation for strong coupling g ~ 2.
