# Eccentricity and elliptic flow in pp collisions at the LHC

> High-multiplicity proton-proton collisions at the LHC may exhibit collective phenomena such as elliptic flow. We study this issue using DIPSY, a brand-new Monte Carlo event generator which features almost-NLO BFKL dynamics and describes the transverse shape of the proton including all fluctuations. We predict the eccentricity of the collision as a function of the multiplicity and estimate the magnitude of elliptic flow. We suggest that flow can be signaled by a sign change in the four-particle azimuthal correlation.

## Key claim

High-multiplicity proton-proton collisions at the LHC may exhibit elliptic flow of about 6%, comparable to that observed in nucleus-nucleus collisions, but only if the participant eccentricity generated by QCD evolution fluctuations is large enough; the authors suggest that a sign change in the four-particle azimuthal correlation from negative to positive would be a clean signal of genuine collective flow.

## Method

The authors used the DIPSY Monte Carlo event generator, which implements the QCD dipole model (coordinate-space BFKL evolution) with almost-NLO corrections, energy conservation, and saturation effects, to simulate proton-proton collisions at 7 TeV. They computed the participant eccentricity from the transverse distribution of liberated gluons in each event, then estimated elliptic flow using an empirical hydrodynamic scaling formula (Equation 3) that relates \(v_2\) to eccentricity, system size, and multiplicity, previously validated for Au-Au and Cu-Cu collisions at RHIC and the LHC.

## Key numbers

- Eccentricity \(\epsilon\{2\}\) and \(\epsilon\{4\}\) in the highest multiplicity region at \(\sqrt{s}=7\) TeV are about 30–40%, similar to semi-central nucleus collisions at RHIC and the LHC.
- Elliptic flow \(v_2\) is estimated to be about 6% in high-multiplicity events at \(\sqrt{s}=7\) TeV.
- In the highest multiplicity bins, the measured two-particle correlation \(v_2\{2\} \approx 0.13\) is twice as large as the flow contribution, indicating dominance by nonflow effects.
- The fourth-order cumulant \((v_2\{4\})^4\) is negative in DIPSY without flow and in PYTHIA, while in the flow scenario it is positive; the preliminary ALICE data show a negative value with statistical error bars an order of magnitude larger than the predicted flow signal.

## Context

This work extends the study of collective flow from heavy-ion collisions (Au-Au and Cu-Cu at RHIC, Pb-Pb at the LHC) to the much smaller system of proton-proton collisions, motivated by the observation of high-multiplicity events at the LHC with charged particle densities comparable to semi-central Cu-Cu collisions. It builds on prior work by Casalderrey-Solana (2009) on hot-spot fluctuations and Pierog (2010) on flux-tube models, but uses the QCD dipole model to generate the transverse gluon distribution from first-principles evolution rather than assuming random fluctuations. The results enable experimental searches for flow in pp collisions using the sign of the fourth-order cumulant as a discriminant, and suggest that hydrodynamic behavior may be controlled by the dimensionless parameter \(\alpha = (\lambda/K_0) S / (dN/d\eta)\) rather than absolute system size.
