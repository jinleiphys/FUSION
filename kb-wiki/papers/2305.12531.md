# Singlet cross section and the tensor interaction in $\overline{\mathrm{p}} \mathrm{p} \rightarrow \barΛ Λ$

> In this short paper, we demonstrate using irreducible tensorial techniques and in a model-independent way, why tensor interactions and also orbital-angular momentum-changing vector interactions are absent in the singlet unpolarized differential cross section for the reaction $\overline{\mathrm{p}} \mathrm{p} \rightarrow \barΛ Λ$.

# Singlet cross section and the tensor interaction in $\overline{\mathrm{p}} \mathrm{p} \rightarrow \bar{\Lambda} \Lambda$

## Key claim
Using irreducible tensorial techniques in a model-independent way, the author demonstrates that the unpolarized singlet differential cross section for $\overline{\mathrm{p}} \mathrm{p} \rightarrow \bar{\Lambda} \Lambda$ receives no contribution from tensor interactions (rank $\lambda=2$) nor from orbital angular momentum-changing vector interactions, despite the tensor interaction being expected to dominate the $I=0$ channel. The result holds specifically for the singlet final state ($s_f=0$) with unpolarized beam and target.

## Method
The author applies irreducible tensorial techniques previously developed for pion production in $NN$ collisions (Pachattu, earlier work) to the $\overline{\mathrm{p}} \mathrm{p} \rightarrow \bar{\Lambda} \Lambda$ reaction, expressing the spin-space matrix $\mathbf{M}$ in terms of channel-spin operators and partial-wave amplitudes $M_{l_f s_f; l_i s_i}^j(E)$. The singlet unpolarized differential cross section $t^0_0(0,0)$ is derived from the Fano statistical tensors by setting $s_f=s_f'=0$ with unpolarized initial state, and the resulting expression is simplified using parity conservation ($(-1)^{l_f}=(-1)^{l_i}$) and a coordinate choice with the incident momentum along the $z$-axis.

## Key numbers
- The singlet unpolarized differential cross section reduces to $t^0_0(0,0) = \frac{1}{4}\left[|M^0_0(0,0)|^2 + 2|M^1_1(0,1)|^2\right]$, showing only contributions from $\lambda=0$ and $\lambda=1$ amplitudes.
- The highest contributing rank $\lambda$ to the singlet cross section is $\lambda=1$, while tensor interactions correspond to $\lambda=2$ and are therefore absent.
- Parity conservation imposes $(-1)^{l_f}=(-1)^{l_i}$, which together with angular momentum constraints from the Racah coefficient $W(l_f l_i s_f s_i; \lambda j)$ forces $l_i = l_f$ for $\lambda=s_i=1$, preventing orbital angular momentum-changing vector interactions.

## Context
This work extends the irreducible tensorial formalism developed by the author (Pachattu, earlier work) for pion production to the $\overline{\mathrm{p}} \mathrm{p} \rightarrow \bar{\Lambda} \Lambda$ reaction, which was previously identified as a promising channel to study tensor forces in the $I=0$ channel (Tabakin, earlier work). The result clarifies that singlet observables are insensitive to tensor interactions, redirecting experimental attention to triplet observables for studying such forces. The reaction is relevant for future experimental setups like PANDA, and the model-independent conclusion guides the design of polarization measurements to access tensor interaction effects.
