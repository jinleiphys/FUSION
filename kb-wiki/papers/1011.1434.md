# Dynamical generation of pseudoscalar resonances

> We study the interactions between the f_0(980) and a_0(980) scalar resonances and the lightest pseudoscalar mesons. We first obtain the elementary interaction amplitudes, or interacting kernels, without including any ad hoc free parameter. This is achieved by using previous results on the nature of the lightest scalar resonances as dynamically generated from the rescattering of S-wave two-meson pairs. Afterwards, the interaction kernels are unitarized and the final S-wave amplitudes result. We find that these interactions are very rich and generate a large amount of pseudoscalar resonances that could be associated with the K(1460), π(1300), π(1800), η(1475) and X(1835). We also consider the exotic channels with isospin 3/2 and 1, having the latter positive G-parity. The former could be also resonant in agreement with a previous prediction.

## Key claim

The paper claims that S-wave interactions between the scalar resonances $f_0(980)$ and $a_0(980)$ and the lightest pseudoscalar mesons ($\pi$, $K$, $\eta$, $\eta'$) dynamically generate several pseudoscalar resonances above 1 GeV, but the association with known states like $K(1460)$, $\pi(1300)$, $\pi(1800)$, $\eta(1475)$, and $X(1835)$ is qualified as a possible contribution to the physical signal rather than a definitive identification, and the results depend on the choice of a subtraction constant $a_1$ that is constrained but not uniquely fixed.

## Method

The authors first derived elementary interaction kernels for scalar-pseudoscalar scattering using a triangle-loop diagram where the scalar resonance ($f_0(980)$ or $a_0(980)$) couples to a virtual $K\bar{K}$ pair, exploiting the fact that these scalars are dynamically generated from pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar interactions. The kernels were then unitarized using an N/D method (analogous to the Bethe-Salpeter equation) to obtain final S-wave scattering amplitudes, with the unitarity loop function $g_{IG}(s)$ evaluated via a once-subtracted dispersion relation. The subtraction constant $a_1$ was varied within the range $|a_1| \lesssim 3$ with negative sign to ensure resonance generation from attractive interactions.

## Key numbers

* $K(1460)$: resonant peak at 1460 MeV in $I=1/2$ $f_0 K$ and $a_0 K$ amplitudes for $a_1 \approx -0.5$, with visual width around 100 MeV and coupling ratio $|g_{f_0K}/g_{a_0K}| \simeq 1.4$.
* $\pi(1800)$: resonant peak in $I=1$, $G=-1$ $a_0\eta'$ elastic amplitude at around 1.8 GeV for $a_1 \approx -1.3$, with visual width around 200 MeV (PDG width $208 \pm 12$ MeV).
* $X(1835)$: resonant peak in $I=0$, $G=+1$ $f_0\eta'$ elastic amplitude at 1835 MeV for $a_1 \approx -1.25$, with width around 70 MeV (PDG width $67.7 \pm 20.3 \pm 7.7$ MeV).
* $\eta(1475)$: resonant peak in $I=0$, $G=+1$ $f_0\eta$ elastic amplitude around 1.45 GeV for $a_1 = -0.8$, with width around 150 MeV (PDG width $85 \pm 9$ MeV); coupling to $a_0\pi$ is more than 14 times smaller than to $f_0\eta$.
* Exotic $I=3/2$ $a_0 K$ channel: resonant structure around 1.4 GeV for $a_1 = -0.5$, with width around 200 MeV, confirming a prediction by Longacre (1990).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Albaladejo et al. (2008) and Albaladejo et al. (2009), which studied $\phi(1020)f_0(980)$ and $\phi(1020)a_0(980)$ S-wave interactions and dynamically generated the $Y(2175)$ resonance, to the scalar-pseudoscalar sector. The results challenge the interpretation of the $\eta(1405)$ as a pseudoscalar glueball (since no signal for $\eta(1405)$ is found) and support the classification of $\eta(1295)$, $\eta(1475)$, $\pi(1300)$, and $K(1460)$ as a nonet of first radial excitations (Barnes et al., 1997), while also providing a dynamical origin for the $X(1835)$ observed by BES (Ablikim et al., 2003, 2005). The study enables future work that simultaneously includes vector-pseudoscalar interactions in P-wave to study both pseudoscalar and axial resonances together.
