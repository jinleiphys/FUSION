# Neutrino-induced nucleosynthesis of A>64 nuclei: The nu p-process

> We present a new nucleosynthesis process, that we denote nu p-process, which occurs in supernovae (and possibly gamma-ray bursts) when strong neutrino fluxes create proton-rich ejecta. In this process, antineutrino absorptions in the proton-rich environment produce neutrons that are immediately captured by neutron-deficient nuclei. This allows for the nucleosynthesis of nuclei with mass numbers A >64. Making this process a possible candidate to explain the origin of the solar abundances of 92,94Mo and 96,98Ru. This process also offers a natural explanation for the large abundance of Sr seen in an hyper-metal-poor star.

## Key claim
The paper proposes a new nucleosynthesis process, the nu p-process, that occurs in core-collapse supernovae when strong neutrino fluxes create proton-rich ejecta; antineutrino absorptions on free protons produce neutrons that are captured by neutron-deficient nuclei, enabling synthesis of nuclei with mass numbers A > 64. The process is presented as a candidate for the "lighter element primary process" (LEPP) and may explain the solar abundances of 92,94Mo and 96,98Ru, as well as the large Sr abundance in the hyper-metal-poor star HE 1327-2326, but the authors note that additional work is necessary to decide whether solar system abundances of light p-nuclei are due to this process.

## Method
The authors performed nucleosynthesis network calculations following 1435 isotopes between Z=1 and Z=54, using detailed neutrino spectral information from neutrino radiation hydrodynamical calculations (model B07) to determine neutrino and antineutrino absorption rates at each point of the nucleosynthesis trajectory. They also carried out parametric calculations based on adiabatic expansions with constant entropy per nucleon s = 50 k_B to test the sensitivity of nucleosynthesis to the electron fraction Y_e, varying Y_e by changing the temperatures of neutrino and antineutrino spectra assuming Fermi-Dirac distributions.

## Key numbers
- Neutron density of 10^14 to 10^15 cm^-3 is maintained for several seconds when temperatures are in the range 1 to 3 GK.
- The (n,p) reaction on 64Ge has a lifetime of 0.25 s at a temperature of 2 GK, permitting matter flow to continue to heavier nuclei.
- Entropy per nucleon in the simulations is s ≈ 50–75 k_B, in contrast to the s ≈ 150–170 k_B required in earlier work (Jordan and Meyer, 2004) for synthesis of nuclei heavier than A=64.
- Nuclei heavier than A=64 are only produced for Y_e > 0.5, showing a very strong dependence on Y_e in the range 0.5–0.6.
- The beta-decay half-life of 64Ge is 64 s, much longer than the expansion time scale of about 10 s.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of proton-rich freeze-out in supernovae (Froehlich et al., 2005; Pruet et al., 2005) by including the previously neglected effect of neutrino and antineutrino absorption on nucleosynthesis after nuclei form, which allows the matter flow to bypass the waiting point at 64Ge. The nu p-process is distinct from earlier suggestions for p-nuclei production in the neutrino wind (Fuller and Meyer, 1995; Hoffman, Woosley, and Fuller, 1996) because neutrons are delivered directly by antineutrino absorption on free protons in a proton-rich environment. The process is proposed as a candidate for the lighter element primary process (LEPP) inferred from galactic chemical evolution studies (Travaglio et al., 2004) and offers a natural explanation for Sr in the hyper-metal-poor star HE 1327-2326 (Frebel et al., 2005). The findings were independently confirmed by Pruet et al. (2005), who considered the early proton-rich neutrino wind and found the process extends to even heavier nuclei under those conditions.
