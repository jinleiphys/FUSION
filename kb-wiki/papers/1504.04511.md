# Limit Cycles from the Similarity Renormalization Group

> We investigate renormalization group limit cycles within the similarity renormalization group (SRG) and discuss their signatures in the evolved interaction. A quantitative method to detect limit cycles in the interaction and to extract their period is proposed. Several SRG generators are compared regarding their suitability for this purpose. As a test case, we consider the limit cycle of the inverse square potential.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that renormalization group limit cycles can be detected in the similarity renormalization group (SRG) evolved interaction, but only when using alternative SRG generators (exponential or inverse) with a dimensionful parameter sigma; the standard kinetic-energy generator does not produce a clear signal.

## Method

The authors evolve the attractive inverse square potential (1/R^2) using the SRG with three different generators: the standard kinetic-energy generator T, an exponential generator G_e = -sigma^2 exp(-T/sigma^2), and an inverse generator G_i = -sigma^2/(1+T/sigma^2). They examine the diagonal matrix elements V(p,p,lambda_a) of the evolved S-wave potential as a function of the effective momentum cutoff lambda_a, extracting the discrete scaling factor from the log-periodic oscillations observed for momenta p approximately equal to the generator parameter sigma.

## Key numbers

- For the inverse square potential with nu=11 (where nu = sqrt(-c-1/4) and c is the coupling), the extracted ratios of successive maxima in V(p,p,lambda_a) converge to approximately 1.33, compared to the exact scaling factor exp(pi/11) ≈ 1.33.
- For nu=5, the extracted ratios converge to approximately 1.87, compared to the exact scaling factor exp(pi/5) ≈ 1.87.
- The effective momentum cutoff for the T generator is lambda = s^{-1/4}, while for the exponential and inverse generators it is lambda_a = s^{-1/2} (with sigma set to 1).
- The initial cutoff used for the Lippmann-Schwinger equation is Lambda = 20 l_0^{-1} (or 30 l_0^{-1} in some calculations), where l_0 is a free length scale.

## Context

This work extends the study of renormalization group limit cycles, previously analyzed in effective field theory frameworks for the Efimov effect (Braaten 2004, Bedaque 1998) and for the inverse square potential (Hammer 2005), into the SRG framework. The SRG is a standard tool for softening nuclear interactions in many-body calculations (Bogner 2009, Furnstahl 2013), and the authors aim toward deriving the limit cycle in pionless effective field theory from a more fundamental chiral interaction with explicit pions. The alternative generators used were introduced by Li, Anderson, and Furnstahl (2011) for more efficient SRG evolution.
