# Spin degeneracy in multi-hadron systems with a heavy quark

> We study multi-hadron systems with a single heavy quark (charm or bottom) in the limit of heavy quark mass. The spin degeneracy of the states with quantum numbers $(j+1/2)^{P}$ and $(j-1/2)^{P}$ for $j \neq 0$, known in a normal hadron, can be generalized to multi-hadron systems. The spin degeneracy is the universal phenomena for any multi-hadron systems with a single heavy quark, irrespective of their internal structures, including compact multi-quarks, hadronic molecules and exotic nuclei. We demonstrate the spin degeneracy in the hadronic systems formed by a heavy hadron effective theory; $P^{(\ast)}N$ states with a $P^{(\ast)}=\bar{D}^{(\ast)}$, $B^{(\ast)}$ meson and a nucleon $N$, and a $P^{(\ast)}$ meson in nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The spin degeneracy of states with quantum numbers \((j+1/2)^{P}\) and \((j-1/2)^{P}\) for \(j \neq 0\), previously known for single hadrons, is shown to be a universal phenomenon in any multi-hadron system containing a single heavy quark (charm or bottom) in the heavy quark mass limit, regardless of whether the system is a compact multi-quark, a hadronic molecule, or an exotic nucleus.

## Method

The authors use heavy quark effective theory (HQET) to argue that the heavy quark spin is conserved in the \(m_Q \to \infty\) limit, making the total angular momentum of the light degrees of freedom (the "spin-complex") a good quantum number. They then demonstrate the spin degeneracy explicitly in two concrete systems using a heavy meson effective theory with one-pion exchange potentials: (1) \(P^{(\ast)}N\) hadronic molecules (where \(P^{(\ast)} = \bar{D}^{(\ast)}\) or \(B^{(\ast)}\) meson and \(N\) is a nucleon), and (2) a \(P^{(\ast)}\) meson embedded in uniform nuclear matter, where self-energy diagrams are computed at \(\mathcal{O}(g_\pi^2)\).

* The self-energy of the \(P\) meson in nuclear matter is found to equal the self-energy of the \(P^{\ast}\) meson: \(-i\Sigma_{P} = -i\Sigma_{P^{\ast}}\), confirming the HQS doublet nature.

## Context

This work extends the heavy quark spin symmetry, previously applied to single hadrons (Isgur and Wise, 1991; Rosner, 1985; Eichten and Hill, 1993; Manohar and Wise, 2000), to multi-hadron systems including exotic hadrons and nuclei. It generalizes the concept of "brown muck" to a "spin-complex" that can include composite hadronic degrees of freedom. The explicit demonstration using one-pion exchange potentials builds on prior work on \(P^{(\ast)}N\) systems (Yasui and Sudoh, 2009; Yamaguchi et al., 2011) and on \(\bar{D}^{(\ast)}\) mesons in nuclear matter (Yasui et al., 2012). The results provide a guiding principle for experimental searches at facilities such as J-PARC, GSI-FAIR, RHIC, and LHC, where partner states of observed exotic hadrons should be found nearby in mass.
