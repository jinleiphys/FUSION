# Rotation Effects on Mesonic Condensations in Isospin Matter

> We investigate the rotation effects on the mesonic condensation in isospin matter . Using the two-flavor NJL effective model with a global rotation, we demonstrate two important effects of the rotation on its phase structure: a rotational suppression of the scalar-channel condensates, in particular the pion condensation region; and a rotational enhancement of the rho condensation region with vector-channel condensate. A new phase diagram for isospin matter under rotation is mapped out on the $ω-μ_I$ plane where three distinctive phases, corresponding to $σ$, $π$, $ρ$ dominated regions respectively, are separated by a second-order line at low isospin chemical potential and a first-order line at high rotation which are further connected at a tri-critical point.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that global rotation has two opposing effects on mesonic condensation in isospin matter: it suppresses scalar-channel condensates (particularly pion condensation) while enhancing vector-channel condensates (rho condensation), leading to a new phase diagram on the rotation-isospin chemical potential plane. The authors note that while quantitative details may depend on model specifics, the three-region phase structure is expected to be generic.

## Method

The authors use a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) effective model with four-fermion interactions in scalar, pseudoscalar, and vector channels, incorporating global rotation via a polarization term in the Lagrangian density. Within mean-field approximation and assuming ωr ≪ 1, they compute the thermodynamic potential and solve coupled gap equations for σ, π, and ρ condensates simultaneously. The model parameters are standard: quark mass m₀ = 5 MeV, scalar and vector coupling constants G_s = G_v = 5.03 GeV⁻², momentum cutoff Λ = 650 MeV, and a radial distance r = 0.1 GeV⁻¹.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum chiral condensate value: σ₀ = 2 × (250 MeV)³
- Critical isospin chemical potential for pion/rho condensation onset without rotation: approximately 140 MeV (second-order transition)
- Tri-critical point (TCP) location on the ω-μ_I plane at T = 10 MeV and μ_B = 250 MeV: (μ_I^c = 165 MeV, ω^c = 548 MeV)
- At ω = 500 MeV and T = 10 MeV, a first-order transition occurs at high isospin density where pion condensate drops to zero and rho condensate becomes dominant
- At ω = 600 MeV and T = 10 MeV, pion condensate disappears entirely, with a smooth crossover from σ-dominated to ρ-dominated phase

## Context

This work extends previous studies of rotation effects on fermion pairing, such as the rotational suppression of chiral condensate and color superconductivity demonstrated by Jiang (2016), to the specific case of mesonic condensation in isospin matter. The emergence of rho condensation at high isospin density had been previously studied by Brauner (2016), but the interplay between rho condensate and rotation is analyzed here for the first time. The results enable mapping of a new phase diagram on the ω-μ_I plane, suggesting that the spontaneously broken isospin symmetry in the pion condensation phase can be restored under rapid rotation, and that a rho-condensation phase emerges at high isospin density under strong rotation.
