# Nucleon resonance structure in the finite volume of lattice QCD

> An approach for relating the nucleon resonances extracted from $πN$ reaction data to lattice QCD calculations has been developed by using the finite-volume Hamiltonian method. Within models of $πN$ reactions, bare states are introduced to parametrize the intrinsic excitations of the nucleon. We show that the resonance pole positions can be related to the probability $P_{N^*}(E)$ of finding the bare state, $N^*$, in the $πN$ scattering states in infinite volume. We further demonstrate that the probability $P_{N^*}^V(E)$ of finding the same bare states in the eigenfunctions of the underlying Hamiltonian in finite volume approaches $P_{N^*}(E)$ as the volume increases. Our findings suggest that the comparison of $P_{N^*}(E)$ and $P_{N^*}^V(E)$ can be used to examine whether the nucleon resonances extracted from the $πN$ reaction data within the dynamical models are consistent with lattice QCD calculation. We also discuss the measurement of $P_{N^*}^V(E)$ directly from lattice QCD. The practical differences between our approach and the approach using the Lüscher formalism to relate LQCD calculations to the nucleon resonance poles embedded in the data are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the probability of finding a bare nucleon resonance state in the finite-volume eigenfunctions of the Hamiltonian approaches the corresponding probability in infinite volume as the volume increases, suggesting that comparison of these probabilities can test whether nucleon resonances extracted from pi N reaction data are consistent with lattice QCD calculations, rather than directly comparing resonance pole positions.

## Method

The authors apply the finite-volume Hamiltonian method using dynamical pi N reaction models with meson-exchange mechanisms, first with a one-channel Sato-Lee model for the Delta(1232) resonance and then with a three-channel model including pi N, pi Delta, and sigma N channels. They solve the Schrodinger equation in a periodic finite volume to obtain the spectrum and eigenfunctions, from which they calculate the probability of finding the bare state in the finite-volume wave functions. They then compare this finite-volume probability, after energy averaging, with the infinite-volume probability derived from the scattering wave functions.

## Key numbers

- The one-channel Sato-Lee model fits empirical S and P partial-wave amplitudes up to invariant mass W = 1.3 GeV
- The three-channel model fits S and P partial-wave pi N empirical amplitudes up to invariant mass W = 1.6 GeV
- The energy-averaged probability P_N*^V(E, L) approaches the infinite-volume probability P_N*(E) as the lattice size L increases, with the condition L x Delta E ~ 4 yielding a well-defined function
- The finite-volume probability P_Delta^V(E, L) qualitatively reproduces the shape of P_Delta(E) even for L = 3 fm
- Phase shifts calculated from finite-volume spectra at L = 5 fm and L = 6 fm agree with the experimental values through the Luscher formalism

## Context

This work extends the finite-volume Hamiltonian method previously developed with separable potentials (Adelaide group, 2012-2015) to dynamical pi N reaction models based on meson-exchange mechanisms, specifically the Sato-Lee model and the ANL-Osaka Hamiltonian. The approach differs from the Luscher formalism used by the JLab group (2013-2015) and the Bonn group's unitarized chiral perturbation theory method (2014-2016), as it provides a direct comparison of bare-state probabilities rather than requiring extraction of resonance poles from the finite-volume spectrum. The findings enable next-generation lattice QCD calculations to extract resonance properties through measurement of P_N*^V(E, L) using non-local meson-baryon interpolating fields.
