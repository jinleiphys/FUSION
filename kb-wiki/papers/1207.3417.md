# Richardson-Gaudin integrability in the contraction limit of the quasispin

> Background: The reduced, level-independent, Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer Hamiltonian is exactly diagonalizable by means of a Bethe Ansatz wavefunction, provided the free variables in the Ansatz are the solutions of the set of Richardson-Gaudin equations. On the one side, the Bethe Ansatz is a simple product state of generalised pair operators. On the other hand, the Richardson-Gaudin equations are strongly coupled in a non-linear way, making them prone to singularities. Unfortunately, it is non-trivial to give a clear physical interpretation to the Richardson-Gaudin variables because no physical operator is directly related to the individual variables. Purpose: The purpose of this paper is to shed more light on the critical behavior of the Richardson-Gaudin equations, and how this is related to the product wave structure of the Bethe Ansatz. Method: A pseudo-deformation of the quasi-spin algebra is introduced, leading towards a Heisenberg-Weyl algebra in the contraction limit of the deformation parameter. This enables an adiabatic connection of the exact Bethe Ansatz eigenstates with pure bosonic multiphonon states. The physical interpretation of this approach is an adiabatic suppression of the Pauli exclusion principle. Results: The method is applied to a so-called "picket-fence" model for the BCS Hamiltonian, displaying a typical critical behavior in the Richardson-Gaudin variables. It was observed that the associated bosonic multiphonon states change collective nature at the critical interaction strengths of the Richardson-Gaudin equations. Conclusions: The Pauli exclusion principle is the main responsible for the critical behavior of the Richardson-Gaudin equations, which can be suppressed by means of a pseudo deformation of the quasispin algebra.

# Richardson-Gaudin integrability in the contraction limit of the quasispin

## Key claim

The critical behavior of the Richardson-Gaudin equations, where the rapidities recombine into complex conjugate pairs at specific interaction strengths, is driven by the Pauli exclusion principle acting on fermion pairs, and can be understood by adiabatically suppressing the Pauli principle via a pseudo-deformation of the quasispin algebra, connecting exact eigenstates to pure bosonic multiphonon states in the contraction limit.

## Method

A pseudo-deformation of the su(2) quasispin algebra was introduced with a deformation parameter ξ ∈ [0,1], where ξ=1 recovers the standard fermionic quasispin and ξ=0 yields a Heisenberg-Weyl bosonic algebra. The reduced BCS Hamiltonian was re-expressed in terms of the deformed generators, and the corresponding deformed Richardson-Gaudin equations were derived. By adiabatically tuning ξ from 1 to 0, the exact Bethe Ansatz eigenstates of the pairing Hamiltonian were continuously connected to multiphonon states of a one-body bosonic Hamiltonian. The method was applied to a "picket-fence" model of 12 uniformly spaced two-fold degenerate single-particle levels with 6 paired fermions.

## Key numbers

- The picket-fence model used 12 uniformly spaced two-fold degenerate single-particle levels with N=6 paired fermions (12 paired fermions total).
- All RG variables are real for |g| < 0.393; variables recombine into complex conjugate pairs at critical interaction strengths, with all variables complex for |g| > 0.891.
- The maximum ground-state overlap with TDA multiphonon states changes from (1,1,1,1,1,1,...) to (1,1,1,1,2,0,...) at |g| = 0.385 in the weak-coupling regime.
- The maximum ground-state overlap changes from (5,1,0,0,0,0,...) to (6,0,0,0,0,0,...) at |g| = 0.872 in the strong-coupling regime.
- The deformed RG variables near the contraction limit (ξ ≪ 1) scale as x_α(ξ) = ℏω_{k(α)} + i√(2ξ/a_{k(α)}) z_{ν_{k(α)},l(α)} + O(ξ), where z_{ν,l} are roots of Hermite polynomials H_ν(z) and a_k = (1/2) Σ_i Ω_i/(2ε_i − ℏω_k)².

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Sambataro (2007), who calculated overlaps between exact Richardson-Gaudin eigenstates and Tamm-Dancoff multiphonon states for the picket-fence model, finding three distinct coupling regimes but ambiguous structure in the intermediate regime. The pseudo-deformation approach provides a continuous adiabatic connection that resolves this ambiguity by showing how the Pauli principle forces RG variables into the complex plane. The method builds on the algebraic framework of Gaudin (1976) and the quasispin formulation of the reduced BCS Hamiltonian, and enables a clear physical interpretation of the Richardson-Gaudin variables in terms of collective pair behavior. The connection to Hermite polynomials in the near-contraction limit parallels results from Gaudin (1971) for the Lieb-Liniger Bose gas.
