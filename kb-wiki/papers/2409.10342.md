# Ab initio calculations of anomalous seniority breaking in the $πg_{9/2}$ shell for the $N=50$ isotones

> We performed \textit{ab initio} valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) calculations based on chiral two-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions to investigate the anomalous seniority breaking in the neutron number $N=50$ isotones: $^{92}$Mo, $^{94}$Ru, $^{96}$Pd, and $^{98}$Cd. Our calculations well reproduced the measured low-lying spectra and electromagnetic $E2$ transitions in these nuclei, supporting partial seniority conservation in the first $πg_{9/2}$ shell. Recent experiments have revealed that, compared to the symmetric patterns predicted under the conserved seniority symmetry, the $4^+_1\to2^+_1$ $E2$ transition strength in $^{94}$Ru is significantly enhanced and that in $^{96}$Pd is suppressed. In contrast, the $6^+_1\to 4^+_1$ and $8^+_1\to6^+_1$ transitions exhibit the opposite trend. We found that this anomalous asymmetry is sensitive to subtle seniority breaking effects, providing a stringent test for state-of-the-art nucleon-nucleon interactions and nuclear models. We analyzed the anomalous asymmetry using VS-IMSRG calculations across various valence spaces. Our \textit{ab initio} results suggest that core excitations of both proton and neutron across the $Z=50$ shell are ascribed to the observed anomalous seniority breaking in the $N=50$ isotones.

## Key claim

The paper claims that core excitations of both protons and neutrons across the Z=50 shell gap, rather than neutron-only excitations across N=50, are responsible for the observed anomalous seniority breaking in the N=50 isotones, based on ab initio VS-IMSRG calculations that reproduce the experimental E2 transition trends.

## Method

The authors performed valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) calculations using chiral two-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions (EM1.8/2.0 and ΔNNLOGO) to derive effective Hamiltonians and E2 operators for the gsd valence space. They diagonalized these effective Hamiltonians using the KSHELL shell-model code, including up to four-particle-four-hole excitations across the N=Z=50 major shell, and calculated excitation energies and E2 transition strengths for the N=50 isotones 92Mo, 94Ru, 96Pd, and 98Cd.

## Key numbers

* B(E2:4+1→2+1) for 94Ru: experimental value of 38(3) e²fm⁴ reported by Pérez-Vidal et al. (2022), with earlier measurements of ≥46 e²fm⁴ (Mach et al., 2017) and 103(24) e²fm⁴ (Das et al., 2021)
* The 4+2 and 4+1 states in 94Ru and 96Pd are separated by less than 500 keV, while in 92Mo and 98Cd the 4+2 state is more than 3.5 MeV higher than the 4+1 state
* Convergence of calculations achieved with E3max=28, emax=12, and ℏω=12 MeV for the model space parameters

## Context

This work extends previous large-scale shell model (LSSM) calculations on N=50 isotones that used various interactions and model spaces, including those by Mach et al. (2017), Das et al. (2021), and Pérez-Vidal et al. (2022). While LSSM calculations within the fpg model space achieved good overall agreement with experimental data but failed to reproduce anomalous E2 trends, and LSSM within the gsd space reproduced the trends but gave poor absolute B(E2) values, the present ab initio calculations successfully capture both the trends and overall values. This enables future investigations of seniority breaking effects in other semimagic nuclei across the nuclear chart.
