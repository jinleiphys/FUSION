# Polarization Observables in Hard Rescattering Mechanism of Deuteron Photodisintegration

> Polarization properties of high energy photodisintegration of the deuteron are studied within the framework of the hard rescattering mechanism~(HRM). In HRM, a quark of one nucleon knocked-out by the incoming photon rescatters with a quark of the other nucleon leading to the production of two nucleons with high relative momentum. Summation of all relevant quark rescattering amplitudes allows us to express the scattering amplitude of the reaction through the convolution of a hard photon-quark interaction vertex, the large angle p-n scattering amplitude and the low momentum deuteron wave function. Within HRM, it is demonstrated that the polarization observables in hard photodisintegration of the deuteron can be expressed through the five helicity amplitudes of NN scattering at high momentum transfer. At 90$^\circ$ CM scattering HRM predicts the dominance of the isovector channel of hard $pn$ rescattering, and it explains the observed smallness of induced, $P_y$ and transfered, $C_x$ polarizations without invoking the argument of helicity conservation. Namely, HRM predicts that $P_y$ and $C_x$ are proportional to the $ϕ_5$ helicity amplitude which vanishes at $θ_{cm}=90^\circ$ due to symmetry reasons. HRM predicts also a nonzero value for $C_z$ in the helicity-conserving regime and a positive $Σ$ asymmetry which is related to the dominance of the isovector channel in the hard reinteraction. We extend our calculations to the region where large polarization effects are observed in $pp$ scattering as well as give predictions for angular dependences.

## Key claim

Within the Hard Rescattering Mechanism (HRM), the observed smallness of the induced polarization \(P_y\) and transferred polarization \(C_x\) in deuteron photodisintegration at \(90^\circ\) center-of-mass (CM) scattering does not necessarily indicate the onset of helicity conservation; instead, it can be explained by the vanishing of the \(\phi_5\) helicity amplitude of nucleon-nucleon (NN) scattering at that angle due to symmetry reasons, without invoking helicity conservation.

## Method

The scattering amplitude for \(\gamma + d \rightarrow p + n\) was expressed within HRM as a convolution of the deuteron wave function, a hard photon-quark interaction vertex, and the large-angle pn scattering amplitude, summing all relevant quark rescattering amplitudes. The polarization observables \(P_y\), \(C_{x'}\), \(C_{z'}\), and \(\Sigma\) were then derived in terms of the five helicity amplitudes (\(\phi_1\) through \(\phi_5\)) of hard pn scattering, using the on-mass-shell approximation for the struck quark propagator and restricting to the s-wave contribution of the deuteron wave function. Numerical estimates were made by modeling the pn helicity amplitudes using a quark-interchange framework parameterized from existing pp scattering data, with two choices for the parameter \(\epsilon\) in the \(\phi_5\) amplitude to represent different assumptions about helicity conservation.

## Key numbers

- The hard scattering kinematics is defined by the requirement that \(-t, -u \ge 2\) GeV\(^2\).
- The momenta relevant in the deuteron wave function are \(\le 300\) MeV/c.
- The counting rule for \(\gamma+d\rightarrow p+n\) predicts an energy dependence \(\sim s^{-11}\), confirmed experimentally for photon energies starting at 1 GeV.
- At \(\theta_{cm}=90^\circ\) and \(P_{Lab}=11.75\) GeV, elastic pp scattering data show that protons polarized transverse to the scattering plane have four times larger probability to scatter with spins parallel than antiparallel, compared to the helicity conservation prediction of two.
- The parameterization of \(\phi_5\) uses \(\epsilon = \sqrt{s-4m^2}/2\) for the assumption that smallness at large angles is due only to symmetry, and \(\epsilon \approx 0.1\) for the case with additional suppression from helicity conservation.
- The angular function \(F(z)\) is defined as \(F(z) = 1/[(1+z)(1-z)^3]\).
- The helicity amplitudes \(\phi_{\pm,3}(0)\) are parameterized as \(\phi_{\pm,3}(0) = N/(s/\text{GeV}^2)^4 (B_{\pm,3} + C_{\pm,3} e^{i[\Psi_{\pm,3}(s) + \delta_{\pm,3}]})\), with \(\Lambda \equiv \Lambda_{QCD} = 0.2\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the Hard Rescattering Mechanism (HRM) previously developed by Sargsian (2003) for deuteron photodisintegration to polarization observables, building on earlier QCD-based predictions such as the minimal Fock component approximation by Brodsky and Farrar (1975) and the counting rule. It contrasts with the helicity conservation predictions of perturbative QCD (pQCD), which have been challenged by data on pp scattering (e.g., Crabb et al., 1990) showing large polarization effects. The paper enables future experimental tests at Jefferson Lab (JLAB) to distinguish between helicity conservation and the HRM explanation for the smallness of \(P_y\) and \(C_x\) at \(90^\circ\), and to verify the predicted positive \(\Sigma\) asymmetry and sizable \(C_{z'}\) values.
