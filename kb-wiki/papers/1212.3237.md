# Upper Energy Limit of Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory in Neutral Pion Photoproduction

> With the availability of the new neutral pion photoproduction from the proton data from the A2 and CB-TAPS Collaborations at Mainz it is mandatory to revisit Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (HBChPT) and address the extraction of the partial waves as well as other issues such as the value of the low-energy constants, the energy range where the calculation provides a good agreement with the data and the impact of unitarity. We find that, within the current experimental status, HBChPT with the fitted LECs gives a good agreement with the existing neutral pion photoproduction data up to $\sim$170 MeV and that imposing unitarity does not improve this picture. Above this energy the data call for further improvement in the theory such as the explicit inclusion of the Δ(1232). We also find that data and multipoles can be well described up to $\sim$185 MeV with Taylor expansions in the partial waves up to first order in pion energy.

## Key claim

Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (HBChPT) with fitted low-energy constants (LECs) provides a good description of neutral pion photoproduction data only up to approximately 170 MeV photon energy, and imposing unitarity on the S-wave amplitude does not improve this energy range.

## Method

The authors performed fits of HBChPT, unitary HBChPT (U-HBChPT), and an empirical Taylor expansion to differential cross section and photon beam asymmetry data for the reaction gamma p -> pi0 p from the A2 and CB-TAPS Collaborations at Mainz. They varied the maximum photon energy of the fit from 158.72 to 191.94 MeV and evaluated the chi-squared per degree of freedom and the stability of the extracted LECs as functions of the fit energy range.

## Key numbers

- HBChPT with fitted LECs gives good agreement with data up to approximately 170 MeV photon energy
- The empirical Taylor expansion describes data and multipoles up to approximately 185 MeV
- The unitary cusp parameter beta from unitarity is (3.35 +/- 0.08) x 10^(-3)/m_pi+, while the HBChPT value is 2.71 x 10^(-3)/m_pi+
- The real part of E0+(gamma p -> pi+ n) is (28.06 +/- 0.27 +/- 0.45) x 10^(-3)/m_pi+
- The scattering length a(pi+ n -> pi0 p) is (0.1195 +/- 0.0016)/m_pi+
- The lowest-energy fit (E_gamma_max = 158.72 MeV) used 100 experimental data points; the highest-energy fit (E_gamma_max = 191.94 MeV) used 514 data points
- The correlation coefficient between a_+ and a_- LECs is approximately 0.78 to 0.88
- The correlation coefficient between xi_2 and b_p LECs is approximately 0.45 to 0.6

## Context

This work revisits HBChPT calculations from Bernard et al. (1996, 2001) using the new high-precision data from the A2 and CB-TAPS Collaborations at Mainz (Hornidge et al.). The study extends previous analyses by determining the energy range where HBChPT remains valid and by testing whether imposing unitarity (as done in earlier work by Fernandez-Ramirez et al. 2009) resolves the disagreement at higher energies. The results show that the Delta(1232) resonance must be explicitly included to extend the theory beyond 170 MeV, motivating approaches such as dispersive chiral effective theory (Gasparyan and Lutz) and relativistic chiral perturbation theory (Hilt et al. 2012).
