# Time-reversal invariance violation effect in $\bm{dd}$ scattering

> A formalism has been developed for calculating the signal of violation of $T$ invariance, provided that $P$ invariance is preserved during the scattering of tensor-polarized deuterons on vector-polarized ones based on the Glauber theory with the full consideration of spin dependence of $NN$ elastic scattering amplitudes and spin structure of colliding deuterons. The numerical calculations have been carried out in the range of laboratory proton energies $T_p=0.1$--$1.2$ GeV using the SAID database for spin amplitudes and in the energy region of the SPD NICA experiment corresponding to the invariant mass of the interacting nucleon pairs $\sqrt{s_{NN}}= 2.5$--$25$ GeV, using two phenomenological models of $pN$ elastic scattering. It is found that only one type of the $T$ non-invariant $P$-invariant $NN$ interaction gives a non-zero contribution to the signal in question, that is important for isolating an unknown constant of this interaction from the corresponding data.

## Key claim
Only one type of time-reversal non-invariant parity-conserving (TVPC) nucleon-nucleon interaction, the \(h\)-type amplitude, gives a nonzero contribution to the TVPC null-test signal in deuteron-deuteron scattering; the \(g\)- and \(g'\)-type TVPC interactions cancel out due to isospin factors and charge-exchange symmetry, which is essential for isolating the unknown coupling constant of the \(h\)-type interaction from experimental data.

## Method
The authors developed a formalism within Glauber diffraction theory, incorporating the full spin dependence of \(NN\) elastic scattering amplitudes and the spin structure (including both \(S\)- and \(D\)-wave components) of the colliding deuterons. They computed the TVPC null-test signal in \(dd\) scattering at zero angle using the optical theorem, considering double-scattering mechanisms (normal and abnormal) while neglecting triple and quadruple collisions. Numerical calculations were performed using the SAID database for spin amplitudes at laboratory proton energies \(T_p = 0.1\)–\(1.2\) GeV and two phenomenological models (Regge parametrization and the HEGS model) for the higher-energy region \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.5\)–\(25\) GeV relevant to the SPD NICA experiment.

## Key numbers
- The TVPC signal was calculated in the range of laboratory proton energies \(T_p = 0.1\)–\(1.2\) GeV (corresponding to invariant mass \(\sqrt{s_{pN}} = 1.9\)–\(2.4\) GeV) using the SAID database.
- At higher energies, calculations covered the invariant mass range \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.5\)–\(25\) GeV for the SPD NICA experiment.
- Using the SAID database, the maximum of the TVPC signal occurs in the energy range \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 1.95\)–\(2.05\) GeV.
- With the HEGS parametrization at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \sim 5\) GeV, the TVPC signal is about an order of magnitude smaller than with the Regge parametrization from Sibirtsev (2010).
- The \(h_1(1170)\) meson parameters used are: mass \(m_h = 1.17\) GeV, coupling constant \(G_h = 4\pi \times 1.56\), and cutoff \(\Lambda = 2\) GeV.

## Context
This work extends previous Glauber-theory calculations of the TVPC null-test signal in \(pd\) scattering (Uzikov, 2015; Uzikov, 2016) and \(^3\mathrm{He}d\) scattering (Uzikov, 2023) to the \(dd\) system for the first time, including the \(D\)-wave component of the deuteron wave function which was omitted in an earlier treatment (Uzikov, 2024). The results enable experimental searches for TVPC effects at the SPD NICA facility and at COSY, providing a theoretical framework to extract the unknown \(h\)-type TVPC coupling constant from measured asymmetries in polarized \(dd\) scattering.
