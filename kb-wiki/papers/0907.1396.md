# Estimate of the magnetic field strength in heavy-ion collisions

> Magnetic fields created in the noncentral heavy-ion collision are studied within a microscopic transport model, namely the Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics model (UrQMD). Simulations were carried out for different impact parameters within the SPS energy range ($E_{lab} = 10 - 158 A$ GeV) and for highest energies accessible for RHIC. We show that the magnetic field emerging in heavy-ion collisions has the magnitude of the order of $eB_y \sim 10^{-1} m_π^2$ for the SPS energy range and $eB_y \sim m_π^2$ for the RHIC energies. The estimated value of the magnetic field strength for the LHC energy amounts to $eB_y \sim 15 m_π^2$.

## Key claim
The magnetic field created in noncentral heavy-ion collisions reaches magnitudes of order \(eB_y \sim 10^{-1} m_\pi^2\) at SPS energies and \(eB_y \sim m_\pi^2\) at RHIC energies, with an estimated value of \(eB_y \sim 15 m_\pi^2\) for LHC; these values are obtained from a microscopic transport model and are conditional on the impact parameter and collision energy, with the field being strongest for peripheral collisions.

## Method
The authors used the Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) model to simulate Au-Au collisions at various impact parameters and bombarding energies from \(E_{lab} = 10\) to \(158 A\) GeV (SPS range) and at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 130\) and \(200\) GeV (RHIC). The magnetic field at the center of the fireball was computed via the Lienard-Wiechert potentials, summing only over spectator nucleons to avoid participant uncertainties, and averaging over 100 events to reduce statistical fluctuations. Results were compared with a semianalytical model of two Lorentz-contracted uniformly charged noninteracting spheres.

## Key numbers
- For Au-Au collisions at SPS energy \(E_{lab} = 60A\) GeV with impact parameter \(b = 4\) fm, the peak magnetic field strength is \(eB_y \sim 10^{-1} m_\pi^2\).
- For Au-Au collisions at RHIC energy \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV with \(b = 4\) fm, the peak magnetic field strength is \(eB_y \sim m_\pi^2\) (close to the earlier estimate of \(eB \approx 1.3 m_\pi^2\) from Kharzeev et al. (2008)).
- For Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energy \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 4.5\) TeV with \(b = 4\) fm, the estimated maximal magnetic field strength is \(eB_y \sim 15 m_\pi^2\).
- The magnetic field is approximately homogeneous in the central region up to \(y \sim 5\) fm.
- The magnetic field strength in units of Gauss: \(m_\pi^2 \approx 10^{18}\) Gauss (using \(m_\pi = 140\) MeV).

## Context
This work extends the earlier qualitative estimate by Kharzeev et al. (2008) for RHIC energies by providing quantitative, event-averaged calculations from a microscopic transport model (UrQMD) across a range of SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies. The results confirm that the magnetic fields are strong enough to enable the chiral magnetic effect and other phenomena such as induced chiral symmetry breaking and modification of the chiral phase transition, provided the system reaches the deconfinement and chiral restored phase. The study enables more reliable predictions for observables in heavy-ion collisions at current and future facilities (FAIR, NICA, LHC) and can be compared with the semianalytical model of Fraga et al. (2008).
