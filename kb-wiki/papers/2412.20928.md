# The reduced-width amplitude in nuclear cluster physics

> The reduced-width amplitude, as a cluster overlap amplitude, is one important physical quantity for analyzing clustering in the nucleus depending on specified channels and has been calculated and applied widely in nuclear cluster physics. In this review, we briefly revisit the theoretical framework for calculating the reduced-width amplitude, as well as the outlines of cluster models to obtain the microscopic or semi-microscopic cluster wave functions. Besides, we also introduce recent progress related to the cluster overlap amplitudes, such as implementation for cross-section estimation and extension to three-body clustering analysis. Comprehensive examples are provided to show the applications of the reduced-width amplitude in analyzing cluster structures.

## Key claim

This review argues that the reduced-width amplitude (RWA), defined as the overlap between a nucleus's wave function and a cluster-coupling wave function at a specified inter-cluster distance, is a central quantity for analyzing nuclear clustering, but its calculation and interpretation depend critically on the choice of cluster model (microscopic, semi-microscopic, or phenomenological) and on the treatment of antisymmetrization, which suppresses the inner-region amplitude and determines the asymptotic tail.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical framework for computing the RWA from several cluster-model wave functions: the resonating group method (RGM), the generator coordinate method (GCM) with Brink wave functions, the orthogonality condition model (OCM), and the Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Röpke (THSR) wave function. It describes how the RWA is obtained from RGM via expansion in harmonic-oscillator states with norm-kernel eigenvalues, from GCM-Brink via a Laplace expansion method (Chiba and Kimura, 2017), and how the asymptotic tail of the RWA is matched to a Whittaker function to extract the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC). The review also shows how the RWA enters reaction-theory calculations, specifically in distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) for transfer reactions and in distort-wave impulsive approximation (DWIA) for knockout reactions.

## Key numbers

* The RWA for the Hoyle state of \(^{12}\mathrm{C}\) obtained from the THSR wave function has an overlap with the RGM/GCM \(3\alpha\) wave function of \(\left|\left<\Psi^\mathrm{THSR}_{3\alpha}\middle|\Psi^\mathrm{RGM/GCM}_{3\alpha}\right>\right|^2\approx100\%\).
* The spectroscopic factor \(S_c^2\) is defined as the integral of the squared norm of the RWA: \(S_c^2=\int_0^\infty \left|a y_c^{J\pi}(a)\right|^2 da\).
* The decay width for a narrow resonance is \(\Gamma_c=2P_l(a)\gamma_c^2(a)\), where the reduced width \(\gamma_c^2(a)=\frac{\hbar^2}{2\mu a}\left[ay_c^{J\pi}(a)\right]^2\) and the penetrability \(P_l(a)=\frac{ka}{F_l^2(ka)+G_l^2(ka)}\) uses the regular and irregular Coulomb functions \(F_l\) and \(G_l\).
* The dimensionless reduced width is \(\theta_c^2(a)\equiv\frac{\gamma_c^2(a)}{\gamma_W^2(a)}=\frac{a}3\left[ay_c^{J\pi}(a)\right]^2\), with the Wigner limit \(\gamma_W(a)=3\hbar^2/2\mu a\).
* The asymptotic tail of the RWA for bound systems and narrow resonances behaves as \(ay_c^{J\pi}(a) \to C_c^{J\pi}W_{-\eta,l+1/2}(2\kappa a)\), where \(C_c^{J\pi}\) is the ANC, \(\eta=Z_1Z_2e^2\mu/\hbar^2\kappa\) is the Sommerfeld parameter, and \(\kappa=\sqrt{-2\mu E}/\hbar\) is the wave number.

## Context

This review systematizes and extends the theoretical treatment of the RWA, a quantity with roots in the RGM of Wheeler (1937) and the GCM of Hill and Wheeler (1953) and Griffin (1957). It connects the RWA to the cluster form factor used in no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations (Navratil, 2004) and to the overlap function in reaction theories (Timofeyuk, 2014). The paper shows how the RWA enables more precise and self-consistent cross-section calculations in DWBA and DWIA, replacing the adjustable spectroscopic factors of traditional optical-potential models (Descouvemont, 2022). It also discusses the extension to three-body clustering analysis and the use of the Laplace expansion method (Chiba and Kimura, 2017) to compute the RWA within the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) framework.
