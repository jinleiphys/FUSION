# Neutron drop trapped in axially deformed external fields

> The neutron drop is firstly investigated in an axially symmetric harmonic oscillator (ASHO) field, whose potential strengths of different directions can be controlled artificially. The shape of the neutron drop will change from spherical to oblate or prolate according to the anisotropy of the external field. With the potential strength increasing in the axial direction, the neutron prefers to occupy the orbital perpendicular to the symmetry axis. On the contrary, the neutron likes to stay in the orbital parallel to the symmetry axis when the potential strength increases in the radial direction. Meanwhile, when the potential strength of one direction disappears, the neutron drop cannot bind together. These investigations are not only helpful to simulate the properties of neutrons in finite nuclei but also provide the theoretical predictions to the future artificial operations on the nuclei like the ultracold atom system, for a deeper realization of quantum many-body systems.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the shape and internal structure of a neutron drop can be controlled by tuning the anisotropy of an externally applied axially symmetric harmonic oscillator (ASHO) field, but this control is limited: when the potential strength in one direction vanishes entirely (deformation parameter delta = -1.5 or 0.75), the neutron drop cannot bind together and the calculation fails to converge.

## Method

The authors used the axially deformed Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approach with the SLy4 parameter set for the particle-hole nucleon-nucleon interaction and a mixed density-dependent delta interaction for the pairing channel. The quasiparticle wave functions were expanded in a basis of eigenfunctions of an ASHO potential, and the HFB equation was solved by diagonalization in each Omega-pi block. Neutron drops with N_D = 28 and N_D = 40 were studied for deformation parameters delta ranging from -1.45 to 0.70.

## Key numbers

* For N_D = 28 at delta = 0 (spherical external field), the ratio of total energy to the non-interacting harmonic oscillator energy E_tot/E_HO = 0.71, the total rms radius R_tot = 3.17 fm, and the average pairing gap Delta = 0.00 MeV.
* For N_D = 28 at delta = -1.45 (most oblate case studied), the total rms radius R_tot = 7.12 fm, the axial rms radius R_z = 1.03 fm, the radial rms radius R_perp = 6.85 fm, and the quadrupole deformation beta = -0.74.
* For N_D = 28 at delta = 0.70 (most prolate case studied), the total rms radius R_tot = 5.73 fm, the axial rms radius R_z = 5.24 fm, the radial rms radius R_perp = 1.95 fm, and the quadrupole deformation beta = 1.31.
* The spherical potential frequency was fixed at hbar*omega_0 = 10 MeV for all calculations.
* The lower and upper limits of the ASHO deformation parameter that can confine the N_D = 28 neutron drop with the maximum basis size N_sh = 50 are delta = -1.46 and delta = 0.72, respectively.
* At delta = -1.45, the central density of the neutron drop is around 0.05 fm^{-3}.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutron drops trapped in spherical harmonic oscillator potentials, such as those by Pudliner (1996), Gandolfi (2011), and Ge (2020), by introducing an axially deformed external field. The results are compared to the non-interacting neutron case in the same ASHO potential and to the Nilsson model for deformed nuclei (Nilsson, 1955). The findings enable future theoretical predictions for artificial manipulation of nuclear systems analogous to ultracold atom experiments, and can help calibrate effective Hamiltonians in nuclear density functional theory.
