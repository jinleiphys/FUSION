# Leading twist shadowing, black disk regime and forward hadron production

> We review theory of the leading twist nuclear shadowing, and describe phenomenon of post-selection suppression of leading parton spectrum (effective fractional energy losses) in the proximity of the black disk regime. We argue that $2 \to 2$ mechanism dominates in the inclusive leading pion production in d-Au collisions and explain that the post-selection naturally explains both the magnitude of the suppression of the forward pion production in d-Au collisions and the pattern of the forward - central correlations. At the same time this pattern of correlations rules out $2\to 1$ mechanism as the main source of the inclusive leading pion yield. It is demonstrated that the mechanism of the double parton interactions gives an important contribution to the production of two leading pions in $pp$ scattering opening a new way to study correlations of leading quarks in the nucleon. The same mechanism is enhanced in $dAu \to π^0π^0 +X$ collisions and explains the dominance of $Δϕ$ independent component and suppression of the away side peak.

## Key claim

Leading twist nuclear shadowing can be estimated with small uncertainty using correspondence with hard diffraction, but the post-selection mechanism (effective fractional energy losses) in the proximity of the black disk regime is the only effect that currently explains the suppression of forward pion production in d-Au collisions, while the double-parton mechanism explains the pedestal observed in two-pion production.

## Method

The author reviews the theory of leading twist nuclear shadowing based on the Gribov approach and AGK cutting rules, combining unitarity relations with QCD factorization for hard diffraction. The analysis uses numerical studies of dipole-nucleon interaction amplitudes in impact parameter space, comparing predictions for proton and nuclear targets. For forward hadron production, the author evaluates the contributions of 2→2 and 2→1 mechanisms to inclusive pion production in d-Au collisions at RHIC, and calculates the double-parton mechanism contribution to two-pion production using generalized parton densities.

## Key numbers

- For A∼200, the uncertainty between two extreme scenarios of color fluctuations is ≤20% for nuclear parton distribution functions
- The effective Pomeron trajectory from HERA experiments has α_Pomeron ∼ 1.11, while BFKL approaches give α_Pomeron ∼ 1.25
- For quarks near the black disk regime, effective energy losses are estimated to be 10–15%
- The median gluon x in the kinematics studied at RHIC is in the range x_g ∼ 0.01–0.03
- For central d-Au collisions, T_A ≈ 2.2 fm^{-2}, giving r_b+r_c ∼ 4.4 for the ratio of double-parton to leading-twist contributions
- The ratio r_{dAu} for small impact parameter changes from ∼9 to ∼12 for πR_int^2 = 15–20 mb
- The ratio of pedestals in dAu and pp is R_pedestal ∼ 2.5–4, compared to the experimental value ∼3
- The probability of "punch through" at b∼0 for Au nucleus is of order 5–10%

## Context

This paper reviews and extends the theory of leading twist nuclear shadowing developed by Gribov (1968) and Frankfurt (1998), and the post-selection mechanism proposed by Frankfurt (2001). The analysis contradicts the 2→1 mechanism of Kharzeev inspired by the color glass condensate model, and the models of Dumitru (2005) that neglect the 2→2 contribution. The work enables future studies of double quark distributions in nucleons at RHIC and provides a framework for understanding forward-central correlations in d-Au collisions.
