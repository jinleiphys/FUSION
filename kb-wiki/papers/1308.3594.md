# Chemical Freeze-out of Strange Particles and Possible Root of Strangeness Suppression

> Two approaches to treat the chemical freeze-out of strange particles in hadron resonance gas model are analyzed. The first one employs their non-equillibration via the usual γ_s factor and such a model describes the hadron multiplicities measured in nucleus-nucleus collisions at AGS, SPS and RHIC energies with χ^2/dof = 1.15. Surprisingly, at low energies we find not the strangeness suppression, but its enhancement. Also we suggest an alternative approach to treat the strange particle freeze-out separately, but with the full chemical equilibration. This approach is based on the conservation laws which allow us to connect the freeze-outs of strange and non-strange hadrons. Within the suggested approach the same set of hadron multiplicities can be described better than within the conventional approach with χ^2/dof = 1.06. Remarkably, the fully equilibrated approach describes the strange hyperons and antihyperons much better than the conventional one.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the apparent suppression of strangeness in heavy-ion collisions, traditionally modeled by a strangeness suppression factor γ_s < 1, is actually an enhancement (γ_s > 1) at low collision energies when using a high-quality hadron resonance gas model with multicomponent hard-core repulsion; this enhancement can be explained by a separate chemical freeze-out of all strange hadrons rather than by chemical non-equilibration.

## Method

The authors fit hadronic multiplicity ratios measured in nucleus-nucleus collisions at AGS, SPS, and RHIC energies using a multicomponent hadron resonance gas model with hard-core radii (R_b = 0.2 fm, R_m = 0.4 fm, R_π = 0.1 fm, R_K = 0.38 fm). They compare three approaches: (i) a single chemical freeze-out with γ_s = 1, (ii) a single freeze-out with γ_s as a free fitting parameter, and (iii) two separate freeze-outs, one for strange hadrons (SFO) and one for non-strange hadrons (FO), connected by conservation laws (entropy, baryon charge, isospin projection) such that only the SFO temperature is a free parameter.

## Key numbers

* Global fit quality for the γ_s approach: χ²/dof = 63.4/55 = 1.15 for 14 collision energies (√s_NN = 2.7, 3.3, 3.8, 4.3, 4.9, 6.3, 7.6, 8.8, 9.2, 12, 17, 62.4, 130, 200 GeV).
* Global fit quality for the single freeze-out with γ_s = 1: χ²/dof = 80.5/69 = 1.16.
* Global fit quality for the separate strangeness freeze-out (SFO) approach: χ²/dof = 58.5/55 = 1.06.
* At low energies (√s_NN = 2.7, 3.3, 3.8, 4.9, 6.3, 9.2 GeV) the γ_s fit yields γ_s > 1, indicating strangeness enhancement rather than suppression; for example, at √s_NN = 3.8 GeV the deepest minimum gives γ_s ≈ 1.6.
* The SFO approach improves the K⁺/π⁺ ratio (Strangeness Horn) description to χ²/dof = 6.3/14, compared to χ²/dof = 7.5/14 in the authors' prior work (Bugaev et al. 2013) and χ²/dof = 3.3/14 for the γ_s fit.

## Context

This work extends the multicomponent hadron resonance gas model with hard-core repulsion previously developed by Bugaev et al. (2013) and applied to the Strangeness Horn. It contradicts the findings of Becattini et al. (2004-2006) who reported γ_s < 1 (strangeness suppression) at all AGS and SPS energies using a statistical hadronization model with lower fit quality. The separate strangeness freeze-out idea was independently suggested by another group (StrangeFO:13) shortly after the present authors presented their results at a workshop (NICAWP), but the present approach is more elaborate, incorporating conservation laws and multicomponent repulsion rather than an ideal gas treatment. The SFO approach enables a unified description of strange antibaryon ratios and antiproton-to-pion ratios that were problematic for the γ_s approach, particularly at √s_NN = 6.3, 12, and 17 GeV.
