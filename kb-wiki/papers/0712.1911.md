# Three-body resonances Lambda-n-n and Lambda-Lambda-n

> Possible bound and resonant states of the hypernuclear systems $Œõnn$ and $ŒõŒõn$ are sought as zeros of the corresponding three-body Jost functions calculated within the framework of the hyperspherical approach with local two-body S-wave potentials describing the $nn$, $Œõn$, and $ŒõŒõ$ interactions. Very wide near-threshold resonances are found for both three-body systems. The positions of these resonances turned out to be sensitive to the choice of the $Œõn$-potential. Bound $Œõnn$ and $ŒõŒõn$ states only appear if the two-body potentials are multiplied by a factor of $\sim 1.5$.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the three-body hypernuclear systems Œõnn and ŒõŒõn do not possess bound states, but do exhibit very wide near-threshold resonances whose positions are sensitive to the choice of the Œõn potential; bound states only appear if the two-body potentials are artificially multiplied by a factor of approximately 1.5.

## Method

The authors locate S-matrix poles on the unphysical sheet of the complex energy surface by calculating zeros of the three-body Jost function within the hyperspherical harmonics approach, using the minimal (hypercentral) approximation with grand orbital quantum number L = 0. Local S-wave two-body Gaussian potentials for nn, Œõn, and ŒõŒõ interactions are employed, with three different parameter sets for the Œõn potential to test sensitivity.

## Key numbers

- For Œõnn, the resonance pole positions (energy and width) depend on the Œõn potential choice; for potential set "A" the resonance is at approximately (0.5 ‚àí i1.5) MeV, for set "B" at approximately (0.3 ‚àí i1.0) MeV, and for set "C" the resonance becomes sub-threshold (negative real part).
- For ŒõŒõn, the resonance pole positions similarly vary: for potential set "A" at approximately (0.4 ‚àí i1.2) MeV, for set "B" at approximately (0.2 ‚àí i0.8) MeV, and for set "C" sub-threshold.
- Bound states appear only when all two-body potentials are multiplied by a scaling factor of approximately 1.5, at which point the Jost function zeros cross the threshold onto the real negative axis.
