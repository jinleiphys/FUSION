# Microscopic study of $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O fusion

> We perform a study of $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O fusion at above and below the interaction barrier energies using three-dimensional time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations at above barrier energies and density-constrained TDHF calculations for the entire energy range. We discuss the variations of the experimental data at above the barrier energies. Calculations reasonably reproduce the observed energy-dependent broad oscillations in the fusion excitation functions. These oscillations result from overcoming $L$-dependent fusion barriers. The role of the coupling to low-lying octupole states is also discussed.

# Microscopic study of $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O fusion

## Key claim

The TDHF and DC-TDHF calculations reproduce the observed energy-dependent broad oscillations in the fusion excitation function, but the agreement with experimental data above the barrier is qualified by the fact that available experimental data sets disagree with each other; the calculations are in better agreement with the data exhibiting the largest cross-sections, overestimating the lowest data points by as much as 25% at 30 MeV center-of-mass energy.

## Method

Three-dimensional time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations using the SLy4d Skyrme energy density functional were performed for above-barrier energies, with fusion cross-sections determined via the sharp cut-off formula and a Hill-Wheeler barrier penetration approximation. Density-constrained TDHF (DC-TDHF) calculations were used for the entire energy range, extracting ion-ion potentials from TDHF time-evolution at zero impact parameter and solving the Schrodinger equation with the Incoming Wave Boundary Condition method to obtain barrier penetrabilities. The role of transfer channels was investigated using particle number projection techniques, and coupling to low-lying octupole states was studied via TDHF linear response and coupled-channel calculations with the ccfull code.

## Key numbers

- The DC-TDHF ion-ion potential barrier height is 10.05 MeV for the $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O system
- TDHF calculations overestimate the experimental data of Fernandez et al. by approximately 16%
- At sub-barrier energy $E_{c.m.}=10$ MeV, transfer probabilities are extremely small: $p_{1n}\sim p_{1p}\sim 3\times10^{-6}$
- The octupole vibration period extracted from TDHF dynamics is $T_{3^-}\simeq 0.56$ zs, corresponding to $E_{3^-}\simeq7.4$ MeV
- The RPA octupole strength distribution shows a peak at $E_{3^-}\simeq7.67$ MeV exhausting approximately 11.2% of the energy weighted sum rule
- The experimental $3^-_1$ state in $^{16}$O is at $E_{3^-_1}=6.129$ MeV with $13.1\pm0.6\%$ of the EWSR
- Coupled-channel calculations show the coupling to the $3^-_1$ state lowers the barrier by approximately 0.3 MeV per phonon, enhancing sub-barrier fusion by about one order of magnitude when both nuclei are coupled
- The DC-TDHF calculations are in excellent agreement with the low-energy sub-barrier data of Thomas and coworkers

## Context

This work extends earlier TDHF studies of $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O fusion from the 1970s (Ko76, CM76, KD77) that suffered from approximations including axial symmetry, omission of the spin-orbit term, and the "fusion window anomaly." The present calculations use modern three-dimensional codes with no symmetry assumptions and full time-odd terms in the Skyrme functional. The DC-TDHF method, previously developed by the same group (UO06a, UO06c, UO09b, UO10a, KU12), is applied to obtain microscopic ion-ion potentials. The work also builds on the comprehensive coupled-channels study by Esbensen (2008) and addresses the longstanding discrepancies among experimental data sets from Fernandez (1978), Tserruya (1978), Kolata (1979), Wu and Barnes (1984), and Thomas (1985, 1986). The results enable future microscopic determination of parameters for coupled-channel calculations and provide a benchmark for understanding fusion in light symmetric systems up to $^{28}$Si+$^{28}$Si.
