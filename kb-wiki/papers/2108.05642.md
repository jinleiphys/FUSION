# Magic mixing angles for doubly heavy baryons

> We study the so-called magic mixing angles for doubly heavy baryons. Defining that a magic mixing angle rotates states with definite $^{2S+1}(l_λ)_J$ to make them heavy-quark symmetric states, we derive the magic mixing angle only in the case $L_ρ=0$ between the heavy quark symmetric states with quantum numbers $\left(J, j_\ell\right)$ and the states with $\left(J, s_q+j_ρ\right)=\left(J, \{^4l_λ/^2l_λ\}\right)$ for a doubly heavy baryon in the standard $ρ-λ$ configuration, where $\vec j_\ell=\vec l_λ+\vec s_q$, $\vec s_ρ=\vec s_{Q1}+\vec s_{Q2}$, and $\vec j_ρ=\vec s_ρ+\vec L_ρ$. We discuss how this mixing angle affects model building for a doubly heavy baryon.

# Magic mixing angles for doubly heavy baryons

## Key claim
The paper derives "magic mixing angles" that rotate nonrelativistic doubly heavy baryon states with definite $^{2S+1}l_\lambda$ quantum numbers into heavy-quark symmetric (physical) states, but only for the case $L_\rho=0$ (where the two heavy quarks have zero relative orbital angular momentum). The authors note that these mixing angles affect how model builders should treat strong decays of $\lambda$-mode excited doubly heavy baryons.

## Method
The authors use the standard $\rho$-$\lambda$ configuration for doubly heavy baryons, where $\rho$ describes the separation between the two heavy quarks and $\lambda$ connects the heavy diquark to the light quark. They employ $6J$ symbols to derive the unitary transformation matrices that relate heavy-quark symmetric states (labeled by total angular momentum $J$ and light-quark total angular momentum $j_\ell$) to nonrelativistic states (labeled by $^{2S+1}l_\lambda$ with total spin $S = j_\rho + s_q$). The derivation assumes $L_\rho=0$ and $s_\rho=1$ (symmetric spin configuration for identical heavy quarks), and considers mixing between states with the same $J^P$ but different $j_\ell$ values.

## Key numbers
- For $l_\lambda=1$, $J^P=1/2^-$: magic mixing angle $\theta_m = 15.9^\circ$ (from $\tan\theta_m = 1/(2\sqrt{2})$)
- For $l_\lambda=1$, $J^P=3/2^-$: magic mixing angle $\theta_m = 41.8^\circ$ (from $\tan\theta_m = 2/\sqrt{5}$)
- For general $l_\lambda$, $J=l_\lambda-1/2$: $\tan\theta_m = \sqrt{2l_\lambda-1}/(2\sqrt{l_\lambda+1})$
- For general $l_\lambda$, $J=l_\lambda+1/2$: $\tan\theta_m = 2\sqrt{l_\lambda}/\sqrt{2l_\lambda+3}$
- For comparison, the heavy-light meson magic mixing angle for $L=1$ is $\theta = 35.3^\circ$ (from $\tan\theta = 1/\sqrt{2}$)

## Context
This work extends the concept of "magic mixing angles" previously established for heavy-light mesons by Rosner (1986) and later generalized by Matsuki (1997) and others, to the case of doubly heavy baryons. The authors note that prior calculations of strong decays for doubly heavy baryons, such as those by Lu (2017) and Xiao (2017), did not properly account for this mixing between $^2l_\lambda$ and $^4l_\lambda$ states, leading to confusion in identifying heavy-quark symmetric states. The results enable more accurate model building for doubly heavy baryon spectra and decays, particularly for $\Xi_{cc/bb}$ and $\Omega_{cc/bb}$ systems.
