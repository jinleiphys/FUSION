# Microcanonical Thermostatistics, the basis for a New Thermodynamics, "heat can flow from cold to hot", and nuclear multifragmentation. The correct treatment of Phase Separation after 150 years of statistical mechanics

> Equilibrium statistics of finite Hamiltonian systems is fundamentally described by the microcanonical ensemble (ME). Canonical, or grand-canonical partition functions are deduced from this by Laplace transform. Only in the thermodynamic limit are they equivalent to ME for homogeneous systems. Therefore ME is the only ensemble for non-extensive/inhomogeneous systems like nuclei or stars where the $\lim_{N\to \infty,ρ=N/V=const}$ does not exist. Conventional canonical thermo-statistic is inapplicable for non-extensive systems. This has far reaching fundamental and quite counter-intuitive consequences for thermo-statistics in general: Phase transitions of first order are signaled by convexities of $S(E,N,Z,...)$ \cite{gross174}. Here the heat capacity is {\em negative}. In these cases heat can flow from cold to hot! The original task of thermodynamics, the description of boiling water in heat engines can now be treated. Consequences of this basic peculiarity for nuclear statistics as well for the fundamental understanding of Statistical Mechanics in general are discussed. Experiments on hot nuclei show all these novel phenomena in a rich variety. The close similarity to inhomogeneous astro physical systems will be pointed out. \keyword{Microcanonical statistics, first order transitions, phase separation, steam engines, nuclear multifragmentation, negative heat capacity}

# Microcanonical Thermostatistics, the basis for a New Thermodynamics

## Key claim
First-order phase transitions in finite, non-extensive systems (such as boiling water, hot nuclei, or self-gravitating systems) are signaled by convexities of the microcanonical entropy S(E), which produce negative heat capacity and allow heat to flow from cold to hot. The canonical ensemble, which jumps over the convex intruder region, is fundamentally inapplicable to such inhomogeneous systems because the thermodynamic limit does not exist for them.

## Method
The author analyzes the microcanonical entropy S(E) as a function of energy for finite systems, using the Boltzmann definition S = k ln W where W counts the number of microstates consistent with macroscopic control parameters. For a system of 3000 sodium atoms with realistic interactions, the caloric curve and fragment distributions are computed microcanonically across the liquid-gas phase transition. The convex intruder in S(E) is identified as the generic signal of first-order phase separation, and the canonical partition sum Z(T) = ∫ e^{S(E)-E/T} dE is shown to become bimodal at the transition temperature, with the convex region inaccessible to the canonical ensemble.

## Key numbers
- For 3000 Na atoms at 1 atm: the convex intruder in S(E) lies between e ~ 0.35 and 1.25 eV/atom; below e ~ 0.33 eV, 2999 atoms are condensed in a single liquid droplet; above e ~ 1.3 eV, nearly all atoms are free (ideal gas).
- At e ~ 1.1 eV, the single cluster explodes into on average 2726 monomers, 80 dimers, about 20 quadrimers, and a few heavier fragments.
- The canonical transition temperature T_tr is lower than the microcanonical temperature in the compound-nucleus region (e ~ 0.35 to 1.0 eV), where the single cluster evaporates up to 2500 atoms.

## Context
This work extends the microcanonical treatment of first-order phase transitions developed by Gross (2001) and challenges the conventional canonical Boltzmann-Gibbs approach that has dominated statistical mechanics since Boltzmann and Gibbs. The paper explicitly contrasts the microcanonical picture with the canonical treatment emphasized by Moretto (1987) for compound nuclei, and with the Yang-Lee singularity description of phase transitions. The analysis enables a unified treatment of nuclear multifragmentation, boiling water in steam engines, and astrophysical systems such as rotating self-gravitating systems (Chavanis, 2003), where the microcanonical phase diagram in the energy-angular-momentum plane reveals regions of double stars, single stars, and mixed phases with negative heat capacity.
