# Interacting Ensemble of the Instanton-dyons and Deconfinement Phase Transition in the SU(2) Gauge Theory

> Instanton-dyons, also known as instanton-monopoles or instanton-quarks, are topological constituents of the instantons at nonzero temperature and holonomy. We perform numerical simulations of the ensemble of interacting dyons for the SU(2) pure gauge theory. Unlike previous studies, we focus on back reaction on the holonomy and the issue of confinement. We calculate the free energy as a function of the holonomy and the dyon densities, using standard Metropolis Monte Carlo and integration over parameter methods. We observe that as the temperature decreases and the dyon density grows, its minimum indeed moves from small holonomy to the value corresponding to confinement. We then report various parameters of the self-consistent ensembles as a function of temperature, and investigate the role of inter-particle correlations.

## Key claim

The authors perform numerical Monte Carlo simulations of an interacting ensemble of instanton-dyons for SU(2) pure gauge theory and show that the back reaction of the dyon ensemble on the holonomy can drive the deconfinement phase transition, with the minimum of the free energy shifting from small holonomy to the confining value ν=1/2 as temperature decreases and dyon density grows, but the transition may involve a small jump in holonomy rather than a completely smooth crossover due to the repulsive Coulomb interaction between dyons and antidyons of different types.

## Method

The authors simulate an ensemble of M and L instanton-dyons (and their antidyons) on a three-sphere S³ using Metropolis Monte Carlo, including the classical dyon-antidyon "streamline" interaction derived from gradient flow in previous work (Larsen and Shuryak, 2014), the Diakonov determinant for the metric on the collective coordinate space, and a Debye mass screening regularization. The free energy as a function of holonomy ν and dyon densities is obtained by integrating the average action over an auxiliary coupling parameter λ, and the self-consistent ensemble is found by requiring that the Debye mass extracted from the curvature of the free energy matches the input Debye mass used in the simulations.

## Key numbers

- The total number of dyons in each simulation is fixed to 64.
- The classical dyon-antidyon interaction is parameterized as ΔS_DD̄ = -2(8π²ν/g²)(1/x - 1.632e^{-0.704x}) for distances x > 4, where x = 2πν r T.
- The action parameter S = 8π²/g² is varied; the phase transition occurs around S ≈ 6, with the holonomy ν moving from near 0 to the confining value ν = 1/2.
- The Debye mass M_D is found to decrease toward zero near the transition, with self-consistent values shown as a function of S (Figure 6); lattice data from Bornyakov et al. (2010) are shown for comparison.
- The density n of individual dyon types (M and L) is plotted as a function of S; at S > 6 the densities of M and L dyons become different, with M dyons more abundant than L dyons in the deconfined phase.
- The free energy density f is shown for S = 6, 7, 9 as a function of the Polyakov loop P (Figure 8), with the minimum shifting from P ≈ 0 (confinement) toward P ≈ 1 (deconfinement) as S increases.
- The correlation function C_ML(r) shows attraction at small distances, tripling the density at r = 0; the integrated number of particles in the region where C_ML(r) > 1 is 0.50 particles, while for C_MM it is 0.34 particles.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of instanton-dyon ensembles by including the classical streamline interaction between dyons and antidyons, which was missing in the first Monte Carlo simulations by Faccioli and Shuryak (2013) that focused on chiral symmetry breaking rather than confinement. It is complementary to the mean-field treatment by Liu, Shuryak, and Zahed (2015), which applies only for T < T_c, whereas this work covers both dilute and dense regimes at T ≥ T_c. The results enable a first-principles derivation of the effective holonomy potential (analogous to that used in Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio models) from the dyon ensemble, and provide a microscopic mechanism for the deconfinement phase transition in pure gauge theory.
