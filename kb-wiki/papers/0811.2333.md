# Configuration mixing within the energy density functional formalism: pathologies and cures

> Configuration mixing calculations performed in terms of the Skyrme/Gogny Energy Density Functional (EDF) rely on extending the Single-Reference energy functional into non-diagonal EDF kernels. The standard way to do so, based on an analogy with the pure Hamiltonian case and the use of the generalized Wick theorem, is responsible for the recently observed divergences and steps in Multi-Reference calculations. We summarize here the minimal solution to this problem recently proposed [Lacroix et al, arXiv:0809.2041] and applied with success to particle number restoration[Bender et al, arXiv:0809.2045]. Such a regularization method provides suitable corrections of pathologies for EDF depending on integer powers of the density. The specific case of fractional powers of the density[Duguet et al, arXiv:0809.2049] is also discussed.

## Key claim

Configuration mixing calculations using the Skyrme/Gogny Energy Density Functional (EDF) suffer from divergences and non-physical steps because the standard extension of the Single-Reference functional to non-diagonal kernels via the generalized Wick theorem introduces spurious self-interaction and self-pairing contributions; the minimal solution removes the dangerous terms in the energy kernel that cause these pathologies, but only works for EDFs depending on integer powers of the density.

## Method

The authors trace the origin of divergences and steps in Multi-Reference (MR) EDF calculations to the use of the generalized Wick theorem for constructing energy kernels. They introduce a quasi-particle basis transformation that connects two arbitrary product states via a BCS-like expression, allowing them to separate the energy kernel into contributions that survive in the single-reference limit and those that cause pathologies. By comparing with the pure Hamiltonian case, they prove that the terms in the kernel proportional to products of the overlap factor \(\bar Z_{\nu\bar\nu}\,\bar Z_{\mu\bar\mu}\) with \(\nu = \mu\) or \(\nu = \bar \mu\) must be removed entirely to eliminate divergences and steps. This regularization is applied to particle-number restoration (PNR) calculations for \(^{18}\)O using the SIII Skyrme EDF.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{18}\)O with the SIII Skyrme EDF, uncorrected PNR calculations using \(L=5\) and 199 discretization points in the Fomenko procedure produce non-converging energy landscapes with non-physical steps at particular deformations; after regularization, the corrected curves for both discretizations are superimposed, removing the dependence on mesh points and the steps.
- The regularization fails for EDFs with fractional powers of the density, as demonstrated for SLy4 (which contains a non-integer power of the local normal density), where the left-over fractional power \(\rho^{\gamma}\) with \(0<\gamma<1\) generates cusps in the PNR energy landscape.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of pathologies in MR-EDF calculations first observed by Anguiano et al. (2001) for particle-number restoration and by Zduńczuk et al. (2007) for angular-momentum restoration, where divergences were linked to single-particle levels crossing the Fermi energy. The authors provide a general solution applicable to any type of configuration mixing, building on their earlier proposal (Lacroix et al., 2008) and its successful application to particle-number restoration (Bender et al., 2008). The regularization enables reliable beyond-mean-field calculations for EDFs with integer powers of the density, while the analysis of fractional-power EDFs (Duguet et al., 2008) reveals a fundamental limitation that must be addressed in future functional development.
