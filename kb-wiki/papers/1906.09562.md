# Nuclear modification of jet shape for inclusive jets and $γ$-jets at the LHC energies

> With our coupled jet-fluid model, we study the nuclear modifications of full jets and jet structures for single inclusive jets and $γ$-jets in Pb+Pb collisions at $5.02$~ATeV and $2.76$~ATeV. The in-medium evolution of full jet shower is described by a set of coupled transport equations including the effects of collisional energy loss, transverse momentum broadening and medium-induced splitting process. The dynamical evolution of bulk medium is simulated by solving relativistic hydrodynamic equation with source term which accounts for the energy and momentum deposited by hard jet shower to soft medium. Our study demonstrates that the hydrodynamic medium response to jet propagation significantly enhances the broadening of jet shape at large angles and is essential for the cone-size dependence of jet energy loss and nuclear modification factor of inclusive jet production. It is also found that the nuclear modification pattern of jet shape is sensitive to jet energy but has weak dependence on the flavor of the parton that initiates the jet. Our result can naturally explain the different nuclear modification patterns of jet shape functions for single inclusive jet and $γ$-jet events as observed by the CMS Collaboration, and can be tested in the future by measuring the jet shape function over a wider range of jet energies in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The different nuclear modification patterns of jet shape functions for single inclusive jets and γ-jets observed by CMS are primarily due to different jet transverse momenta in the two measurements, not to different parton flavor compositions as previously argued; the pattern depends strongly on jet energy but weakly on the flavor of the initiating parton.

## Method

The authors used a coupled jet-fluid model that solves transport equations for the three-dimensional momentum distributions of quarks and gluons in the jet shower, including collisional energy loss, transverse momentum broadening, and medium-induced radiative processes via higher-twist splitting kernels. The dynamical evolution of the bulk QGP medium was simulated with (3+1)-dimensional relativistic ideal hydrodynamics including a source term for energy and momentum deposited by the jet shower. The initial vacuum jet shower was generated with PYTHIA, and final jets were reconstructed by combining the hard shower partons with the hydrodynamic medium response obtained via the Cooper-Frye formula after subtracting the background medium without jets.

## Key numbers

- Jet transport parameter for quarks set to \(\hat{q}_0 = 1.8\ \mathrm{GeV^2/fm}\) for Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 ATeV, and \(\hat{q}_0 = 1.7\ \mathrm{GeV^2/fm}\) for Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 ATeV.
- For single inclusive jets with \(p_{\mathrm{T}}^{\mathrm{jet}} > 120\ \mathrm{GeV}\) in central Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 ATeV, the nuclear modification factor of jet shape shows a dip at \(r \sim 0.1\) and enhancement at large \(r\).
- For γ-jets with \(p_{\mathrm{T}}^{\mathrm{jet}} > 30\ \mathrm{GeV}\) in central Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 ATeV, the nuclear modification factor of jet shape shows monotonic increase as a function of \(r\).
- For γ-jets with \(p_{\mathrm{T}}^{\mathrm{jet}} > 120\ \mathrm{GeV}\) in central Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 ATeV, the nuclear modification factor of jet shape shows a clear dip structure similar to inclusive jets.
- The freeze-out temperature was set to \(T_{\mathrm{FO}} = 140\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and the critical temperature for QGP to hadronic matter transition to \(T_{\mathrm{c}} = 160\ \mathrm{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous coupled jet-fluid model studies (Chang 2016, Tachibana 2017) to systematically compare jet shape modifications for inclusive jets and γ-jets at LHC energies. It directly addresses the CMS measurements of jet shape functions (Chatrchyan 2013, Sirunyan 2018) and provides an alternative explanation to the flavor-composition argument of Chien (2015). The results enable future experimental tests by measuring jet shape functions over a wider range of jet energies in heavy-ion collisions, particularly for single inclusive jets with lower transverse momenta.
