# Structure of the two-neutrino double-$β$ decay matrix elements within perturbation theory

> The two-neutrino double-$β$ Gamow-Teller and Fermi transitions are studied within an exactly solvable model, which allows a violation of both spin-isospin SU(4) and isospin SU(2) symmetries, and is expressed with generators of the SO(8) group. It is found that this model reproduces the main features of realistic calculation within the quasiparticle random-phase approximation with isospin symmetry restoration concerning the dependence of the two-neutrino double-$β$ decay matrix elements on isovector and isoscalar particle-particle interactions. By using perturbation theory an explicit dependence of the two-neutrino double-$β$ decay matrix elements on the like-nucleon pairing, particle-particle T=0 and T=1, and particle-hole proton-neutron interactions is obtained. It is found that double-$β$ decay matrix elements do not depend on the mean field part of Hamiltonian and that they are governed by a weak violation of both SU(2) and SU(4) symmetries by the particle-particle interaction of Hamiltonian. It is pointed out that there is a dominance of two-neutrino double-$β$ decay transition through a single state of intermediate nucleus. The energy position of this state relative to energies of initial and final ground states is given by a combination of strengths of residual interactions. Further, energy-weighted Fermi and Gamow-Teller sum rules connecting Delta Z = 2 nuclei are discussed. It is proposed that these sum rules can be used to study the residual interactions of the nuclear Hamiltonian, which are relevant for charge-changing nuclear transitions.

# Structure of the two-neutrino double-β decay matrix elements within perturbation theory

## Key claim

The two-neutrino double-beta decay matrix elements do not depend on the mean field part of the nuclear Hamiltonian and are governed specifically by a weak violation of both SU(2) isospin and SU(4) spin-isospin symmetries by the particle-particle interaction, with the dominance of a single intermediate nuclear state; this conclusion holds within an exactly solvable SO(8) schematic model under the assumption that the symmetry-breaking parameters are small enough for perturbation theory to be valid.

## Method

The authors constructed an exactly solvable schematic model using generators of the SO(8) group, with degenerate single-particle orbitals and only L=0 pairing operators (spin-0 isovector and spin-1 isoscalar pairs) plus a particle-hole force in the T=1, S=1 channel. They decomposed the Hamiltonian into an unperturbed part H0 (which includes single-particle energies, like-nucleon pairing, and particle-hole interaction) and a perturbing part HI (which contains the differences between the pairing strength and the isoscalar and isovector particle-particle strengths). Using perturbation theory up to second order, they derived explicit analytic expressions for the double Fermi and double Gamow-Teller matrix elements as functions of the symmetry-breaking parameters (g_pair - g_pp^T=0) and (g_pair - g_pp^T=1), and compared these perturbative results with exact diagonalization of the Hamiltonian for a specific parameter set.

## Key numbers

- The model parameters used for numerical application: e_p = 1.2 MeV, e_n = 1.1 MeV, Ω = 12, N = 20, g_pair = 0.5 MeV, g_ph = 1.5 × g_pair (i.e., 0.75 MeV)
- For the transition from initial |0,4,4⟩ to final |0,2,2⟩ with isospin symmetry (g_pair = g_pp^T=1), the first-order perturbative result for the GT matrix element is: M^{2ν}_GT = [144√(231/35) × (g_pair - g_pp^T=0)] / [(5g_pair + 9g_ph)(10g_pair + 20g_ph)]
- The energy denominator for the dominant intermediate state (1,3,4) is: (2E'_134 - E'_022 - E'_044)/2 = 5g_pair + 9g_ph + (g_pair - g_pp^T=0)×(39/5) + higher-order terms
- For the case of broken SU(2) and SU(4) symmetries, the first-order Fermi matrix element is: M^{2ν}_F = -48√(33/5)×(g_pair - g_pp^T=1) / [(5g_pair + 3g_ph)(10g_pair + 6g_ph)]
- The first-order GT matrix element with both symmetries broken includes a term proportional to (g_pair - g_pp^T=1) with coefficient involving 2g_ph in the numerator
- The first-order perturbation theory is sufficient when g_pp^T=0/g_pair is within the range (0.8, 1.2)
- For fixed symmetry breaking, the absolute value of M^{2ν}_GT decreases with increasing isospin T of the initial nucleus (shown for T = 2, 4, 6, 8, and 10)

## Context

This work extends the schematic SO(8) model previously used by Vogel (1986) to study the vanishing of M^{2ν}_GT at dynamical SU(4) symmetry, and by Engel to examine isovector and isoscalar proton-neutron correlations. It also generalizes an earlier study by Stefanik (2013) that analyzed M^{2ν}_F using violation of isospin symmetry within an SO(5) group model. The results qualitatively agree with realistic proton-neutron QRPA calculations that include isospin symmetry restoration (Simkovic, 2013; Faessler, 2009), where the isoscalar particle-particle strength is fitted to 2νββ decay rates while the isovector strength is fixed by the requirement that M^{2ν}_F = 0. The explicit perturbative expressions derived here enable a clearer understanding of how different components of the residual interaction contribute to double-beta decay matrix elements, and the proposed energy-weighted sum rules connecting ΔZ = 2 nuclei could provide new constraints on the residual interactions relevant for charge-changing nuclear transitions.
