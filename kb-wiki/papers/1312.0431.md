# Effect of pairing correlations on nuclear low-energy structure: BCS and general Bogoliubov transformation

> Low-lying nuclear states of Sm isotopes are studied in the framework of a collective Hamiltonian based on covariant energy density functional theory. Pairing correlation are treated by both BCS and Bogoliubov methods. It is found that the pairing correlations deduced from relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations are generally stronger than those by relativistic mean-field plus BCS (RMF+BCS) with same pairing force. By simply renormalizing the pairing strength, the diagonal part of the pairing field is changed in such a way that the essential effects of the off-diagonal parts of the pairing field neglected in the RMF+BCS calculations can be recovered, and consequently the low-energy structure is in a good agreement with the predictions of the RHB model.

## Key claim

By simply renormalizing the pairing strength by a factor of about 1.06 in the BCS ansatz, the diagonal part of the pairing field is changed such that the essential effects of the off-diagonal parts of the pairing field neglected in the RMF+BCS calculations can be recovered, and consequently the low-energy structure predicted by the RMF+BCS method agrees well with the predictions of the full RHB model.

## Method

The authors performed five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian (5DCH) calculations based on covariant density functional theory, using the point-coupling energy functional PC-PK1 and a separable pairing force. They compared results from two treatments of pairing correlations: relativistic mean-field plus BCS (RMF+BCS) and relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB), for even-even Sm isotopes with mass numbers 134 to 154. The collective inertia parameters and moments of inertia were determined using the cranking approximation from triaxially deformed mean-field states obtained by solving the Dirac or RHB equations on a three-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis with 14 major shells.

## Key numbers

- The average neutron and proton pairing gaps from RHB calculations are generally larger than those from RMF+BCS with the same pairing force; the RMF+BCS gaps cross the RHB results at a pairing strength factor of approximately 1.06.
- With a 6% enhancement of the pairing strength in RMF+BCS, the relative deviation in average pairing gaps between RHB and RMF+BCS is less than 5% for the selected even-even Sm isotopes.
- For the intraband B(E2) transitions in 152Sm, the relative deviations between RHB and RMF+BCS predictions are reduced to less than 4% after the 6% pairing strength enhancement.
- The main interband transitions in 152Sm agree between the two methods within approximately 2 Weisskopf units (W.u.) after the enhancement.
- The necessary renormalization factor stays roughly constant at 1.06 in the Pu region and increases slightly to 1.10 in the Mg region.

## Context

This work extends earlier comparisons between Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) and Hartree-Fock plus BCS calculations by Girod et al. (1983), which showed that BCS gives slightly weaker pairing correlations. The study uses the 5DCH model previously developed by Niksic et al. (2009, 2011) and Li et al. (2009, 2010), and applies it to Sm isotopes which exhibit an abrupt shape-phase-transition as noted by Li et al. (2009). The results enable systematic studies of nuclear low-lying states using the computationally cheaper RMF+BCS method while recovering the accuracy of the more demanding RHB approach, with the renormalization factor providing a simple correction that remains approximately constant across heavy nuclei.
