# Systematics of proton emission

> A very simple formula is presented that relates the logarithm of the half-life, corrected by the centrifugal barrier, with the Coulomb parameter in proton decay processes. The corresponding experimental data lie on two straight lines which appear as a result of a sudden change in the nuclear shape marking two regions of deformation independently of the angular momentum of the outgoing proton. This feature provides a powerful tool to assign experimentally quantum numbers in proton emitters.

# Systematics of proton emission

## Key claim

The logarithm of the half-life corrected for the centrifugal barrier (the reduced half-life) is a linear function of the Coulomb parameter for proton emitters with Z>50, and the experimental data fall on two distinct straight lines corresponding to two regions of nuclear deformation. The authors assert that this pattern provides a tool for assigning quantum numbers, but note that the analysis depends on using the correct angular momentum value; otherwise the straight-line pattern is completely spoiled.

## Method

The authors define a reduced half-life T_red = T_1/2 / C_l^2, where C_l is a centrifugal barrier correction factor derived from the WKB approximation of the Coulomb-Hankel function. They plot log10(T_red) against the dimensionless Coulomb parameter χ for 24 proton emitters with Z>50, using experimental half-lives and Q-values from the literature, and fit two linear functions to the data. They then correlate the two groups of emitters with quadrupole deformation parameters β from the literature, showing a sharp transition between prolate and oblate shapes at Z=68.

## Key numbers

- Fitted line for Z<68: a_1 = 1.31, b_1 = -2.44, with standard error σ_1 = 0.26
- Fitted line for Z>68: a_2 = 1.25, b_2 = -4.71, with standard error σ_2 = 0.23
- The standard errors correspond to a mean factor less than two in half-life reproduction
- Deformation changes abruptly between Z=67 and Z=69: from large prolate shape with β ≈ 0.3 to oblate shape with β ≈ -0.2
- For the nucleus 109I, the authors changed the angular momentum assignment from l=0 (from Son02) to l=2, based on a coupled channels calculation showing a larger component with l=2 (0.37) compared to l=0 (0.02)

## Context

This work extends the Geiger-Nuttall rule, originally formulated for alpha decay by Gamow (1928), to proton emission by introducing a centrifugal barrier correction. The authors show that unlike a simple plot of log half-life versus Q-value (which Son02 showed yields no clear trend), their corrected plot reveals a systematic pattern. The two straight lines correlate with a sharp transition in nuclear deformation between prolate and oblate shapes at Z=68, as calculated by Moller (1995). This systematics enables assignment of spin and parity for proton decaying states, and the authors note that only 140Ho and 141Ho* deviate conspicuously from the pattern.
