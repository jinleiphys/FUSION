# Isospin compositions of correlated sources in the Fermi energy domain

> Isotopic yield distributions of nuclei produced in peripheral collisions of $^{80}$Kr+$^{40,48}$Ca at 35 MeV/nucleon are studied. Experimental results obtained by the FAZIA Collaboration at the LNS facility in Catania are compared with calculations performed with the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM). The fragments with atomic number $Z=19-24$ observed at forward angles are successfully described with the ensemble method previously established for reactions at higher energy. Using the SMM results, the isotopic compositions of the projectile residues are reconstructed. The results indicate a significant isospin exchange between the projectile and target nuclei, not far from isospin equilibrium, during the initial phase of the reaction. The two groups of light fragments with $Z=1-4$, experimentally distinguished by their velocities relative to coincident heavy projectile fragments, are found to originate from different sources. The isotopic composition of the slower group is consistent with emission from a low-density neck, enriched in neutrons, and satisfactorily reproduced with SMM calculations for a corresponding neck source of small mass.

## Key claim

The isotopic compositions of projectile residues in peripheral collisions of 80Kr+40,48Ca at 35 MeV/nucleon indicate a significant isospin exchange between projectile and target, not far from isospin equilibrium, during the initial reaction phase, with the authors noting that the subsequent statistical decay of excited sources is responsible for the minor target dependence of the final fragment distributions.

## Method

The authors compared experimental isotopic yield distributions from the FAZIA Collaboration with calculations using the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM). They constructed an ensemble of excited sources with varying isotopic compositions, defined by the maximum mass parameter A0 while keeping Z0=36 fixed, and varied the symmetry-term coefficient gamma to reproduce the measured isotope distributions for fragments with Z=19-24. For light fragments with Z=1-4, the authors compared the measured neutron-over-proton ratios to SMM calculations assuming a low-density neck source of small mass.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 35 MeV/nucleon for 80Kr projectiles on 40,48Ca targets
- For the 80Kr+48Ca reaction, the best description used A0=81 (average composition ⟨N/Z⟩=1.25), indicating the projectile picked up one neutron from the target
- For the 80Kr+40Ca reaction, the best description used A0=78 (average composition ⟨N/Z⟩=1.17), indicating the projectile lost two neutrons
- Symmetry-term coefficient gamma values: 18 MeV for Z=19-20, 19 MeV for Z=21-22, and 20 MeV for Z=23-24 for 80Kr+48Ca; values larger by 1 MeV for 80Kr+40Ca
- The uncertainty in gamma determination for Z=19 was approximately ±1 MeV
- The freeze-out density adopted for projectile sources was 0.3ρ0, where ρ0 ≈ 0.16 fm−3 is normal nuclear density
- Maximum excitation energy of sources was set to 8 MeV/nucleon, consistent with the physical limit from binding energy of finite nuclei

## Context

This work extends the ensemble method previously established for projectile fragmentation at relativistic energies up to 1 GeV/nucleon by Botvina (1995) and Ogul (2011) to the Fermi energy domain. It provides an alternative analysis to the FAZIA Collaboration's (2021) study of the same data, which used AMD transport model and GEMINI statistical decay code and reported a weak indication in favor of a stiff symmetry energy. The results enable reconstruction of the primary isotopic compositions of projectile residues through backtracing from detected fragmentation patterns, and demonstrate that the slower group of light fragments originates from a low-density neck source enriched in neutrons, consistent with predictions of neutron drift toward regions of lower density caused by the density dependence of the symmetry energy.
