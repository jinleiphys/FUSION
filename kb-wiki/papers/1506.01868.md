# Compact stars in a SU(3) Quark-Meson Model

> The recent observations of the massive pulsars PSR J1614-2230 and of PSR J0348+0432 with about two solar masses implies strong constraints on the properties of dense matter in the core of compact stars. Effective models of QCD aiming to describe neutron star matter can thereby be considerably constrained. In this context, a chiral quark-meson model based on a SU(3) linear $σ$-model with a vacuum pressure and vector meson exchange is discussed in this work. The impact of its various terms and parameters on the equation of state and the maximum mass of compact stars are delineated to check whether pure quark stars with two solar masses are feasible within this approach. Large vector meson coupling constant and a small vacuum pressure allow for maximum masses of two or more solar masses. However, pure quark stars made of absolutely stable strange quark matter, so called strange stars, turn out to be restricted to a quite small parameter range.

## Key claim

Pure quark stars made of absolutely stable strange quark matter can reach two solar masses only in a quite small parameter range, requiring a large vector meson coupling constant and a small vacuum pressure; however, for the canonical parameter choices the model does not simultaneously satisfy the stability conditions for strange quark matter and the two-solar-mass constraint.

## Method

The authors construct a chiral SU(3) quark-meson model in the mean-field approximation, coupling quarks to scalar and vector meson nonets via Yukawa interactions, and adding a vacuum pressure term. They solve the gap equations for the meson fields self-consistently at zero temperature, compute the equation of state, and integrate the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations for pure quark stars. The vector coupling constant, constituent quark mass, sigma meson mass, and vacuum pressure are varied independently while holding other parameters at canonical values to map the parameter space for maximum masses and absolute stability of strange quark matter.

## Key numbers

* Maximum mass of ~2.1 solar masses at a radius of 10 km for vector coupling g_omega = 4.0, with m_q = 300 MeV, m_sigma = 600 MeV, and B^(1/4) = 120 MeV held fixed.
* For the canonical parameter set (m_q = 300 MeV, m_sigma = 600 MeV, g_omega = 2.0, B^(1/4) = 120 MeV), the maximum mass is about 1.6 solar masses at a radius of about 9 km.
* With B^(1/4) = 40 MeV and fixed m_sigma = 600 MeV, g_omega = 2.0, m_q = 300 MeV, the maximum mass reaches ~2.0 solar masses at a radius of 12 km.
* The two-flavor stability condition requires energy per baryon E/3A >= 311 MeV at zero pressure; the three-flavor condition for absolutely stable strange quark matter requires E/3A <= 310 MeV.
* For g_omega = 2.0 and m_q = 300 MeV, pure quark star configurations appear only for B^(1/4) ~ 26 MeV and m_sigma ~ 725 MeV, with a maximum mass of ~1.8 solar masses.
* For g_omega = 3.0, the intersecting point for pure quark star stability occurs at B^(1/4) ~ 32 MeV and m_sigma ~ 760 MeV, yielding a maximum mass of ~2.7 solar masses.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of quark matter in compact stars using the MIT bag model (Weber, 2004) and Nambu-Jona-Lasinio models (Schertler, 1999; Steiner, 2000; Hanauske, 2001) by applying the chiral SU(3) quark-meson model, which had not previously been used for hybrid or quark star calculations. The results are constrained by the two-solar-mass measurements of PSR J1614-2230 (Demorest, 2010) and PSR J0348+0432 (Antoniadis, 2013). The model enables future systematic comparison with Polyakov-loop extended versions and with the Schwinger-Dyson approach (Klahn, 2015; Chen, 2015), and the twin-star solutions found for low vacuum pressure are noted for further investigation (Zacchi, 2015).
