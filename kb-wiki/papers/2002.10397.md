# Chiral Magnetic Effects in Nuclear Collisions

> The interplay of quantum anomalies with strong magnetic field and vorticity in chiral systems could lead to novel transport phenomena, such as the chiral magnetic effect (CME), the chiral magnetic wave (CMW) and the chiral vortical effect (CVE). In high-energy nuclear collisions, these chiral effects may survive the expansion of a quark-gluon plasma fireball and be detected in experiments. The experimental searches for the CME, the CMW and the CVE, have aroused extensive interest over the past couple of decades. The main goal of this article is to review latest experimental progress in search for these novel chiral transport phenomena at Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider at BNL and the Large Hadron Collider at CERN. Future programs to help reduce uncertainties and facilitate the interpretation of the data are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper reviews experimental evidence for chiral magnetic effects (CME) in nuclear collisions, concluding that while early data showed charge-dependent correlations consistent with CME expectations, more recent data-driven analyses at LHC energies indicate the observed signals are consistent with 100% flow-driven background contributions, with any possible CME signal contributing at most a few percent to the measured correlator.

## Method

The review examines measurements of the three-point gamma correlator (γ112) and its charge-dependent difference Δγ112 in Au+Au, Pb+Pb, U+U, p+Au, d+Au, and p+Pb collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. Various data-driven approaches to disentangle CME signals from backgrounds are evaluated, including comparisons between large and small collision systems, correlators relative to the third-order event plane (γ123), event-shape engineering techniques, and comparisons between participant plane and spectator plane measurements.

## Key numbers

- In Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV, κ112 values from STAR data are typically ≥ 2, while background-only AMPT simulations give κ112 ≈ 1.3, implying roughly one third of Δγ112 could arise from CME
- CMS data in Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV set an upper limit on possible CME signal fraction in Δγ112 of less than 7% at 95% confidence level
- The relative difference in magnetic field magnitudes between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions is approximately 13% for central events, increasing to 15-18% for peripheral events
- If CME contributes to more than 20% of Δγ112 in the 20-60% centrality range, a more than 7σ difference between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr data is expected
- κ112 values from CMS in Pb+Pb at 5.02 TeV and κ123 values are close to each other in most centrality intervals, indicating little CME contribution
- The κ112 data display a rise-and-fall trend with beam energy, peaking around 27 GeV Au+Au collisions and dropping to background level at 7.7 GeV

## Context

This review extends the experimental search program for chiral magnetic effects initiated by Voloshin (2004) and carried out by the STAR, PHENIX, ALICE, and CMS collaborations over the past two decades. The paper synthesizes results from large systems (Au+Au, Pb+Pb, U+U) and small systems (p+Au, d+Au, p+Pb) to evaluate background contributions from local charge conservation and transverse momentum conservation as modeled by Pratt (2010, 2011). The review enables future definitive tests through the ongoing RHIC isobaric collision program (Ru+Ru vs. Zr+Zr) and the Beam Energy Scan II, which together may establish whether any fraction of the observed charge-dependent correlations arises from the chiral magnetic effect.
