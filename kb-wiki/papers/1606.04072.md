# Factorization Breaking of $A^T_d$ for polarized deuteron targets in a relativistic framework

> We discuss the possible factorization of the tensor asymmetry $A^T_d$ measured for polarized deuteron targets within a relativistic framework. We define a reduced asymmetry and find that factorization holds only in plane wave impulse approximation and if p-waves are neglected. Our numerical results show a strong factorization breaking once final state interactions are included. We also compare the d-wave content of the wave functions with the size of the factored, reduced asymmetry and find that there is no systematic relationship of this quantity to the d-wave probability of the various wave functions.

## Key claim

The tensor asymmetry \(A^T_d\) for polarized deuteron targets factorizes into a single-nucleon part and a momentum distribution only in the plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA) when \(p\)-waves are neglected; once final state interactions (FSI) are included, factorization is strongly broken and the reduced asymmetry acquires dependence on kinematic variables beyond the missing momentum.

## Method

The authors computed the reduced tensor asymmetry \(a^T_d = A^T_d / \Xi(\theta_m,\phi,\theta_{kq})\) for the \(^2H(e,e'p)\) reaction using a relativistic Bethe-Salpeter framework with eight deuteron wave functions (including IIB, WJC 1/2, AV18, CD Bonn, and Nijmegen 1/2/3), three nucleon form factor parametrizations (GKex05, AMT, MMD), and two FSI parametrizations (Regge and SAID). They compared the factored PWIA result (neglecting \(p\)-waves) with full PWBA and DWBA (FSI) calculations at \(x = 1.00\) and \(x = 1.35\).

## Key numbers

- At \(x = 1.00\), \(Q^2 = 2.4\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\), \(\varepsilon = 8.3\ \mathrm{GeV}\), \(\phi = 165^\circ\): the FSI envelope shows a spread more than twice as large as the PWBA envelope for missing momenta above \(0.35\ \mathrm{GeV}\).
- At \(x = 1.35\), \(Q^2 = 4.25\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\), \(\varepsilon = 11\ \mathrm{GeV}\), \(\phi = 165^\circ\): the FSI envelope shows a much larger spread than the PWBA envelope, particularly for missing momenta above \(0.7\ \mathrm{GeV}\).
- The factored reduced asymmetry \((a^T_d)_{\text{factored}} = -\sqrt{2\pi/5}\ w(p)(2\sqrt{2}u(p)+w(p))/(u^2(p)+w^2(p))\) depends only on missing momentum \(p\).
- Deuteron \(d\)-wave probabilities range from \(4.85\%\) (CD Bonn) to \(7.34\%\) (WJC 1); Nijmegen 2 (\(5.68\%\)) and Nijmegen 3 (\(5.65\%\)) have nearly identical \(d\)-wave content but produce different reduced asymmetries for \(p_m > 0.5\ \mathrm{GeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic treatment of deuteron electrodisintegration from earlier studies by the same authors (Jeschonnek and Van Orden, cited as "targetpol" and "sabine_factor") and from Ford et al. (2014). It contradicts the naive expectation that the tensor asymmetry can be used to extract the deuteron \(d\)-wave probability, consistent with the argument by Friar (1977, 1979) and Amado (1974, 1978) that the \(d\)-state probability is not a physical observable. The results provide guidance for planned Jefferson Lab experiments (Long proposal) by showing that extracting \(d\)-wave information from \(A^T_d\) requires careful treatment of reaction dynamics and carries significant theoretical uncertainty from model inputs.
