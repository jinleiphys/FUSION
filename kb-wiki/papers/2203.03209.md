# CompOSE Reference Manual

> CompOSE (CompStar Online Supernovae Equations of State) is an online repository of equations of state (EoS) for use in nuclear physics and astrophysics, e.g., in the description of compact stars or the simulation of core-collapse supernovae and neutron-star mergers, see http://compose.obspm.fr. The main services, offered via the website, are: a collection of data tables in a flexible and easily extendable data format for different EoS types and related physical quantities with extensive documentation and referencing; software for download to extract and to interpolate these data and to calculate additional quantities; webtools to generate EoS tables that are customized to the needs of the users and to illustrate dependencies of various EoS quantities in graphical form. This manual is an update of previous versions that are available on the CompOSE website, at arXiv:1307.5715 [astro-ph.SR], and that was originally published in the journal "Physics of Particles and Nuclei" with doi:10.1134/S1063779615040061. It contains a detailed description of the service, containing a general introduction as well as instructions for potential contributors and for users. Short versions of the manual for EoS users and providers will also be available as separate publications.

## Key claim

CompOSE (CompStar Online Supernovae Equations of State) is an online repository and service that provides equations of state (EoS) data tables in a common format for astrophysical and nuclear physics applications, but it cannot run all codes online due to limitations in storage and computation times, and it assumes thermodynamic equilibrium with specific conditions on chemical potentials (e.g., vanishing strangeness chemical potential, no general assumption of beta-equilibrium) that may not apply in all physical scenarios.

## Method

The paper describes the design and operation of the CompOSE online repository, which collects EoS data tables in a flexible, extendable format with documentation and referencing, and provides downloadable software for data extraction, interpolation, and calculation of additional thermodynamic quantities. The service uses a modular structure with a common indexing scheme for particles (including baryons, leptons, quarks, and nuclei) and assumes natural units (hbar = c = k_B = 1) with energies in MeV and lengths in fm, following CODATA 2018 physical constants. The manual provides instructions for both contributors (who prepare EoS tables for inclusion) and users (who download and customize tables for simulations of core-collapse supernovae, neutron-star mergers, and compact star descriptions).

## Context

This reference manual updates previous versions (Typel et al., 2015, published in Physics of Particles and Nuclei, and the arXiv preprint from 2013) and serves as the documentation for the CompOSE online service at compose.obspm.fr. The work extends the review by Oertel et al. (2016) on dense matter equations of state and enables users to access, interpolate, and customize EoS tables for simulations of compact stars, core-collapse supernovae, and neutron-star mergers, while also providing a framework for contributors to make their EoS models available to the community.
