# Theoretical Study of Spin Observables in $pd$ Elastic Scattering at Energies $T_p = 800-1000$ MeV

> Various spin observables (analyzing powers and spin-correlation parameters) in $pd$ elastic scattering at $T_p = 800-1000$ MeV are analyzed within the framework of the refined Glauber model. The theoretical model uses as input spin-dependent $NN$ amplitudes obtained from the most recent partial-wave analysis and also takes into account the deuteron $D$ wave and charge-exchange effects. Predictions of the refined Glauber model are compared with the existing experimental data. Reasonable agreement between the theoretical calculations and experimental data at low momentum transfers $|t| \lesssim 0.2$ (GeV/$c)^2$ is found for all observables considered. Moderate discrepancies found in this region are shown to be likely due to uncertainties in the input $NN$ amplitudes. Qualitative agreement at higher momentum transfers is also found for most observables except the tensor ones with mixed $x$ and $z$ polarization components. Possible reasons for observed deviations of the model calculations from the data at $|t| > 0.2$ (GeV/$c)^2$ are discussed.

## Key claim

The refined Glauber model achieves reasonable agreement with experimental data for pd elastic scattering spin observables at T_p = 800–1000 MeV only for low momentum transfers |t| ≲ 0.2 (GeV/c)^2, with moderate discrepancies in this region likely due to uncertainties in the input NN amplitudes rather than failures of the model itself.

## Method

The authors extended the conventional Glauber diffraction model to incorporate spin degrees of freedom, using as input all ten helicity pp and np amplitudes from the SAID partial-wave analysis (solution SM16) and the CD-Bonn deuteron wave function including both S- and D-wave components. The model sums single- and double-scattering terms expressed through NN amplitudes and deuteron form factors, and also includes charge-exchange effects. The resulting pd elastic scattering amplitude is expanded into 12 invariant amplitudes, from which differential cross sections, analyzing powers, and spin-correlation parameters are computed and transformed to the Madison frame for comparison with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Energy range studied: T_p = 800–1000 MeV for proton-deuteron elastic scattering
- Good agreement found for |t| ≲ 0.2 (GeV/c)^2 for all observables considered
- At T_p = 800 MeV, the tensor analyzing power A_xz is poorly reproduced in the region 0.2 < |t| < 0.4 (GeV/c)^2, where the model gives a smooth curve near zero while data show a pronounced dip
- The vector analyzing powers show up to 25% difference between calculations at T_p = 800 and 1000 MeV at low momentum transfers
- The differential cross section is almost energy-independent at |t| ≤ 0.2 (GeV/c)^2 across T_p = 800–1000 MeV
- The tensor spin-correlation parameter C_z,xy shows strong energy dependence at low momentum transfers

## Context

This work extends the refined Glauber model previously applied by the same authors (Platonova and Kukulin, 2010) to pd elastic scattering at lower energies (T_p = 250 and 440 MeV) and to deuteron analyzing powers at 1000 MeV. It now provides a comprehensive analysis of all proton and deuteron analyzing powers and spin-correlation parameters at T_p = 800–1000 MeV, using the updated SAID PWA solution SM16 (Workman, 2016) rather than the older SP07 solution (Arndt, 2007). The study enables future experiments at facilities such as NICA-SPD at JINR (Dubna) and provides a baseline for identifying non-trivial effects from three-nucleon forces or non-nucleon degrees of freedom when deviations from the model are observed.
