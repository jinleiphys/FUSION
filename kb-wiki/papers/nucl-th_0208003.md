# Charmonium mass in nuclear matter

> The mass shift of charmonium states in nuclear matter is studied in the perturbative QCD approach. The leading-order effect due to the change of gluon condensate in nuclear matter is evaluated using the leading-order QCD formula, while the higher-twist effect due to the partial restoration of chiral symmetry is estimated using a hadronic model. We find that while the mass of $J/ψ$ in nuclear matter decreases only slightly, those of $ψ(3686)$ and $ψ(3770)$ states are reduced appreciably. Experimental study of the mass shift of charmonium states in nuclear matter can thus provide valuable information on the changes of the QCD vacuum in nuclear medium.

## Key claim

The mass shifts of excited charmonium states in nuclear matter are predicted to be large enough to be observable in experiments, but the result depends on combining two separate effects: a leading-order QCD contribution from the change in the gluon condensate and a higher-twist contribution from the change in the D meson mass due to partial restoration of chiral symmetry.

## Method

The leading-order effect from the change of the gluon condensate in nuclear matter was evaluated using the QCD second-order Stark effect formula, with Gaussian wave functions for the charmonium states whose oscillator constants were determined from their squared radii as obtained from potential models. The higher-twist effect from the change in the light quark condensate was estimated using a hadronic model where the charmonium self-energy from a D meson loop was calculated, using a Lagrangian for interacting charmonium and D mesons with a coupling constant determined from the 3P0 model.

## Key numbers

- Mass shift of J/ψ at normal nuclear matter density: -8 MeV (from LO QCD) + 3 MeV (from D meson loop) = -5 MeV total.
- Mass shift of ψ(3686) at normal nuclear matter density: -100 MeV (from LO QCD) - 30 MeV (from D meson loop) = -130 MeV total.
- Mass shift of ψ(3770) at normal nuclear matter density: -140 MeV (from LO QCD) + 15 MeV (from D meson loop) = -125 MeV total.
- The gluon condensate and quark condensate in nuclear matter decrease by 6% and 30%, respectively, at normal nuclear matter density.
- The D meson in-medium mass shift at normal nuclear matter density is taken as 50 MeV.
- The coupling constant g_{ψ(3770)DD} at the on-shell point is 15.4.
- The oscillator constants β used for J/ψ, ψ(3686), and ψ(3770) are 0.52, 0.39, and 0.37 GeV, respectively, corresponding to squared radii of 0.47², 0.96², and 1 fm².

## Context

This work extends previous studies of in-medium hadron masses, which had largely focused on light-quark hadrons (Lee, 1999) and on the J/ψ using QCD sum rules (Klingl, 1999) and leading-order perturbative QCD (Peskin, 1979; Luke, 1992). It also builds on calculations of D meson mass shifts in nuclear matter from QCD sum rules (Hayashigaki, 2000; Morath) and the quark-meson coupling model (Tsushima, 1999). The results enable experimental searches for charmonium mass shifts in antiproton-nucleus annihilation experiments proposed at GSI, which could provide information on changes of the QCD vacuum in nuclear medium.
