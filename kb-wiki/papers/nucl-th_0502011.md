# Breakup Threshold Anomaly: New Manifestation of the Dispersion Relation

> It is pointed out that the usual threshold anomaly, found operative in the energy behavior of the imaginary and real parts of the optical potential representing the elastic scattering of tightly bound nuclei at near- and below-barrier energies, suffers a drastic qualitative change in the case of the elastic scattering of weakly bound nuclei. Owing to the strong coupling to the breakup channel even at sub-barrier energies, the imaginary potential strength seems to increase as the energy is lowered down to below the natural, barrier, threshold, accompanied by a decrease in the real potential strength. This feature is consistent with the dispersion relation. The system $^{6}$Li + $^{208}$Pb is analyzed to illustrate this new phenomenon.

## Key claim

The usual threshold anomaly (TA) observed for tightly bound nuclei, where the imaginary part of the optical potential decreases near the Coulomb barrier, is reversed for weakly bound nuclei: the imaginary potential strength increases as energy decreases below the barrier, accompanied by a decrease in the real potential strength, a phenomenon the authors call the Breakup Threshold Anomaly (BTA). This behavior is consistent with the dispersion relation, but the authors note their findings are mainly based on results from the lowest energies of one system and earlier data analyses for similar systems, requiring further work to improve understanding.

## Method

The authors re-analyzed elastic scattering angular distribution data for the system \(^{6}\)Li + \(^{208}\)Pb using the Sao Paulo (SP) double-folding optical potential, with energy-dependent normalization factors \(N_R(E)\) and \(N_I(E)\) applied to the real and imaginary parts. They extracted best-fit \(N_R\) and \(N_I\) values from the data and checked consistency with the dispersion relation using a schematic piecewise-linear model for \(N_I\) that yields an analytical expression for the dispersive correction \(\Delta N_R\).

## Key numbers

- The standard (energy-independent) SP potential uses \(N_R = 1\) and \(N_I = 0.78\); the best-fit values at low energies deviate from these, with \(N_R\) decreasing and \(N_I\) increasing below the barrier.
- The folding potential uses a zero-range strength \(V_0 = -456\) MeV fm\(^3\).
- Nuclear densities are described by a two-parameter Fermi distribution with radius \(R_0 = 1.31 A^{1/3} - 0.84\) fm and average diffuseness \(a = 0.56\) fm.
- The best-fit values yield \(N_{R0} \approx 1\) and \(N_{I\infty} \approx 1\) (the high-energy limits), close to the standard values but with variations expected for particular systems.

## Context

This work extends the concept of the threshold anomaly (TA), previously established for tightly bound nuclei by several authors (e.g., the dispersion relation reviews), to weakly bound nuclei where breakup coupling remains strong even below the barrier. The BTA is contrasted with the normal TA and is consistent with earlier observations for systems such as \(^{9}\)Be + \(^{209}\)Bi, \(^{6}\)Li + \(^{28}\)Si, \(^{58}\)Ni, \(^{122}\)Sn, \(^{138}\)Ba, and \(^{9}\)Be + \(^{64}\)Zn, though those prior works did not explain the behavior as the BTA phenomenon. The findings enable further theoretical and experimental work to refine the understanding of breakup coupling effects on the optical potential at sub-barrier energies.
