# \textit{Ab initio} Gamow density matrix renormalization group for broad nuclear many-body resonances

> \textbf{Background} The reach of \textit{ab initio} theory has greatly increased in recent decades. However, predicting the location of the drip lines remains challenging due to uncertainties in nuclear forces and difficulties in describing nuclei that behave as open quantum systems. \textbf{Purpose} In this work, we extend the \textit{ab initio} Gamow Density Matrix Renormalization Group (G-DMRG) approach to the regime of broad many-body resonances to pave the way for systematic tests of nuclear forces in light exotic nuclei. \textbf{Methods} To stabilize calculations, we introduce a new truncation scheme in the reference space, and propose an orbital ordering based on entanglement considerations. We then show how continuum couplings increase entanglement in the many-body problem, and propose a new truncation scheme to stabilize the renormalization and accelerate calculations in extreme conditions. Finally, we demonstrate that natural orbitals can be used to efficiently describe broad resonances by introducing a new ordering scheme and by redefining the reference space based on occupations. \textbf{Results} Leveraging our findings, we propose a recipe to converge \textit{ab initio} G-DMRG calculations and apply it in low-lying states of \isotope[5,6]{He} and \isotope[4]{H}, demonstrating control of the renormalization and the emergence of convergence patterns. We also obtain the first direct \textit{ab initio} calculation of the $J^π= {1/2}^+$ ground state of \isotope[5]{H}. \textbf{Conclusions} We demonstrate that entanglement due to continuum couplings can be controlled in extreme conditions and successfully extend the G-DMRG approach in the regime of broad many-body resonances.

## Key claim

The authors extend the _ab initio_ Gamow Density Matrix Renormalization Group (G-DMRG) approach to describe broad many-body resonances, demonstrating that entanglement due to continuum couplings can be controlled through new truncation and orbital ordering schemes, but the method's success depends on the specific choice of reference space and the ability to stabilize calculations when continuum couplings become extremely strong.

## Method

The authors introduce a new truncation scheme for the reference space and propose an orbital ordering based on entanglement considerations to stabilize G-DMRG calculations for broad resonances. They analyze how continuum couplings increase entanglement in the many-body problem using complex-symmetric density matrices, then develop a stabilization technique to reduce entanglement from excessive continuum couplings. Finally, they demonstrate that natural orbitals with a new ordering scheme and redefined reference space based on occupations can efficiently describe broad resonances, applying their recipe to low-lying states of He-5, He-6, and H-4.

## Key numbers

* First direct _ab initio_ calculation of the J^pi = 1/2^+ ground state of H-5 was obtained.
* The narrow-to-broad resonance boundary is given by the condition E_r = Gamma/2, which corresponds to an angle of approximately 63.43 degrees in the (E_r, Gamma) plane.
* The boundary between broad and subthreshold resonances is given by the condition E_r = 0, corresponding to an angle of pi/2 in the (E_r, Gamma) plane.
* At the critical point of maximum entanglement, the eigenvalues of the density matrix are lambda_{1,2}^crit = 1/2 +/- rho_{1,2}, where rho_{1,2} is the off-diagonal matrix element.

## Context

This work extends the _ab initio_ Gamow Density Matrix Renormalization Group approach previously developed for narrow resonances to the regime of broad many-body resonances, building on the Gamow Shell Model framework of Michel and Ploszajczak (2021) and the open quantum system methods reviewed by Dobaczewski et al. (2007). The results enable systematic tests of nuclear forces in light exotic nuclei near the drip lines, addressing challenges in predicting drip line locations identified by Baumann et al. (2012) and Thoennessen (2013). The approach also connects to the broader effort in _ab initio_ nuclear theory reviewed by Hergert (2020) and Johnson et al. (2020).
