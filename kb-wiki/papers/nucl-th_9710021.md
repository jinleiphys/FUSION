# A self-consistent approach to the Wigner-Seitz treatment of soliton matter

> We propose a self-consistant approach to the treatment of nuclear matter as a crystal of solitons in the Wigner-Seitz approximation. Specifically, we use a Bloch-like boundary condition on the quarks at the edge of a spherical cell which allows the dispersion relation for a given radius to be calculated self-consistently along with the meson fields; in previous work some ansatz for the dispersion relation has always been an input. Results in all models are very sensitive to the form of the dispersion relation, so our approach represents a significant advance. We apply the method to both the Friedberg Lee model and the chiral quark-meson model of Birse and Banerjee. Only the latter shows short range repulsion; in the former the transition to a quark plasma occurs at unrealistically low densities.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a self-consistent method for calculating the dispersion relation of quarks in soliton matter within the Wigner-Seitz approximation, removing the need for an ad-hoc ansatz that previous work always required; the authors acknowledge that the results are not yet a realistic model of nuclear matter because spurious center-of-mass energy, Fermi motion of nucleons, and gluon-exchange effects have been omitted.

## Method

The authors treat nuclear matter as a crystal of solitons in the Wigner-Seitz approximation, imposing a Bloch-like boundary condition on quarks at the edge of a spherical cell that relates the wavefunction at antipodal points. For a given cell radius, the dispersion relation \(\epsilon(k)\) and the top of the band \(k_t\) are calculated self-consistently along with the meson fields by solving the Dirac equation for a superposition of partial waves and requiring the determinant of a boundary-condition matrix to vanish. The quark scalar density, integrated over the filled band, provides a spherically symmetric source for the mean-field equations, and the process is iterated until convergence. The method is applied to both the Friedberg-Lee model and the chiral quark-meson model of Birse and Banerjee.

## Key numbers

- In the Friedberg-Lee model, the isolated soliton has an isoscalar r.m.s. radius of 0.83 fm and energy of 1265 MeV; the soliton solution is lost at a cell radius of 1.427 fm, well below nuclear matter density.
- In the chiral quark-meson model, the isolated soliton energy is 1116 MeV; the solution is lost at a cell radius of 0.958 fm, corresponding to approximately 1.4 times nuclear density.
- For the chiral model, the transition to a deconfined state with a pion condensate occurs at 1.4 times nuclear density.
- The Friedberg-Lee model parameters used are \(a=0\), \(b=-700.43\) fm\(^{-1}\), \(c=10^4\), \(g=10.98\), giving \(B_{vac}=0.27\) fm\(^{-4}\) and \(\sigma_v=0.21\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- The chiral model parameters give \(\sigma_v=f_\pi=93\) MeV, \(m_\pi=139\) MeV, \(m_\sigma=1200\) MeV, and \(g=5.38\).

## Context

This work extends the Wigner-Seitz treatment of soliton matter previously applied by Achtzehnter et al. (1985), Birse et al. (1988), Banerjee et al. (1985), and others, all of which required an ad-hoc ansatz for the dispersion relation and the position of the top of the band. The new self-consistent boundary condition allows the band structure to be calculated without such assumptions, and the method is particularly suited to examining the deconfinement transition because the same boundary conditions are satisfied by the uniform plasma solution. The results enable future work to include physical effects such as gluon exchange, meson clouds, and center-of-mass corrections that were omitted here.
