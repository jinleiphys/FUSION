# The 3He spectral function in light-front dynamics

> A distorted spin-dependent spectral function for 3He is considered for the extraction of the transverse-momentum dependent parton distributions in the neutron from semi-inclusive deep inelastic electron scattering off polarized 3He at finite momentum transfers, where final state interactions are taken into account. The generalization of the analysis to a Poincaré covariant framework within the light-front dynamics is outlined.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the standard extraction formula for neutron single-spin asymmetries from polarized 3He semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering remains safe even when final-state interactions are included, because the effects of FSI on effective polarizations and dilution factors compensate each other to a large extent.

## Method

The authors compute a distorted spin-dependent spectral function for 3He using a generalized eikonal approximation (GEA) to treat final-state interactions between the observed pion and the spectator (A-1) system. They evaluate the effective polarizations for proton and neutron in both the plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) and with FSI included, using the AV18 interaction for the nuclear structure. They also develop a Poincaré-covariant light-front (LF) formulation of the spectral function using Melosh rotations to connect the non-relativistic spectral function to the LF framework.

## Key numbers

- Proton effective polarization in PWIA: p_p = -0.023; neutron effective polarization in PWIA: p_n = 0.878 (using AV18 interaction)
- Proton effective polarization with FSI: p_p^{FSI} = -0.026; neutron effective polarization with FSI: p_n^{FSI} = 0.76
- The difference between PWIA and FSI effective polarizations is 10-15%
- Longitudinal proton EP in LF approach: -0.02231 (compared to non-relativistic -0.02263); longitudinal neutron EP in LF: 0.87248 (compared to non-relativistic 0.87805)
- Transverse proton EP in LF: -0.02268 (NR: -0.02263); transverse neutron EP in LF: 0.87494 (NR: 0.87805)

## Context

This work extends the earlier PWIA treatment of 3He SIDIS by Ciofi degli Atti et al. (1993) and the non-relativistic spectral function approach of Kiev (2003), which used the AV18 interaction. The GEA treatment of FSI was previously applied to unpolarized SIDIS by Kaptari et al. (2014) and successfully described deuteron JLab data. The light-front relativistic formulation builds on the Bakamijan-Thomas construction of Poincaré generators and improves upon earlier LF descriptions of DIS off 3He by Sauer et al. (1998). The results enable reliable extraction of neutron TMDs from upcoming JLab experiments with polarized 3He targets.
