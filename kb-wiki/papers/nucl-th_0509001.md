# NA60 and BR Scaling in Terms of the Vector Manifestation: A Model Approach

> It is pointed out that the comparison between the recent NA60 dimuon data and the so-called "Brown-Rho (BR) scaling" as presented at QM2005 is $not$ founded on a correct interpretation of the prediction of BR scaling as formulated in 1991 and modernized recently and hence the conclusion drawn by both the experimental and theoretical speakers that "BR scaling is ruled out by NA60" is erroneous and should be disregarded. We use a simplified model description of how the vector manifestation of hidden local symmetry theory enters into the dilepton production, relegating more rigorous discussions to a follow-up paper.

# NA60 and BR Scaling in Terms of the Vector Manifestation: A Model Approach

## Key claim
The claim that Brown-Rho (BR) scaling is "ruled out" by the NA60 dimuon data is erroneous, because the comparison presented at Quark Matter 2005 used an incorrect interpretation of BR scaling that conflates the parametric mass with the physical pole mass, ignores the necessary fusion of BR scaling with the Rapp-Wambach many-body mechanism, and relies on vector dominance which is badly violated in the relevant temperature and density regime.

## Method
The authors use a simplified model within hidden local symmetry theory to show how the vector manifestation fixed point modifies dilepton production. They parameterize the temperature dependence of the hidden local symmetry parameter *a* (which equals 2 for vector dominance at T=0 and flows to 1 at the critical temperature T_c) and compute the reduction factor for dilepton production cross sections. They also incorporate the onset of soft glue melting at T=125 MeV from lattice calculations to constrain the temperature interval over which medium effects operate.

## Key numbers
- The ratio of dilepton production with *a* flowing to its fixed point value of 1 compared with vector dominance is 1/2, from integrating (1/(1+T/T_c))^2 over 0 to T_c.
- When the lower integration limit is set to T=125 MeV (where soft glue melting begins) rather than T=0, the reduction factor becomes approximately 0.3 rather than 1/2.
- In dense medium, the reduction factor is argued to be 1/4 compared with Rapp's calculation.
- Rapp's parametrization gives m_rho*/m_rho = (1-0.15n/n_0)(1-(T/T_c)^2)^0.3, which at T=125 MeV with T_c=175 MeV yields [1-(T/T_c)^2]^0.3 = 0.81, meaning a 19% mass decrease before medium effects should begin according to lattice results.
- The critical temperature T_c is taken as approximately 175 MeV, with soft glue melting beginning at approximately 125 MeV.

## Context
This paper responds to presentations at Quark Matter 2005 where five talks declared BR scaling "ruled out" by NA60 data, based on Rapp's interpretation (Rapp, 2005). The authors argue that the correct BR scaling framework, as formulated in Brown and Rho (1991) and modernized in Brown and Rho (2005), requires fusing BR scaling with the Rapp-Wambach many-body mechanism (Rapp and Wambach, 2000) rather than treating them as alternatives. The work extends the Harada-Yamawaki renormalization group approach to hidden local symmetry (Harada and Yamawaki, 2001) and its vector manifestation fixed point (Harada and Yamawaki, 2003), which established that vector dominance is inapplicable in generic hadronic systems. The results enable a correct calculation of dilepton production that incorporates both intrinsic vacuum properties and many-body effects, with a follow-up paper providing more rigorous treatment.
