# Nonequilibrium dynamics of quantum fields

> The nonequilibrium effective equation of motion for a scalar background field in a thermal bath is studied numerically. This equation emerges from a microscopic quantum field theory derivation and it is suitable to a Langevin simulation on the lattice. Results for both the symmetric and broken phases are presented.

## Key claim
The paper numerically solves a stochastic Ginzburg-Landau-Langevin (GLL) equation for a scalar background field derived from microscopic quantum field theory, but the derivation and simulations are restricted to the high-temperature approximation (T/m >> 1) and small coupling (λ = 0.25), with the additive noise term dropping out at O(λ²).

## Method
The authors derive an effective Langevin equation for the order parameter φ_c of a λφ⁴ scalar field theory using the real-time Schwinger closed time path formalism, obtaining a multiplicative noise term and field-dependent dissipation. They simulate this GLL equation in 3 spatial dimensions using a leapfrog algorithm for time discretization and Fast-Fourier transforms on a cubic lattice, introducing counterterms to remove lattice-spacing dependence in equilibrium solutions.

## Key numbers
- Coupling constant used in simulations: λ = 0.25
- Temperature in dimensionless units: T = 1
- Dissipation coefficient set to η = 1 in dimensionless units
- Time step: Δt = 0.001
- Critical temperature for symmetry restoration: T_c² = m²/(λ/4!)
- Dissipation coefficient approximation for T >> m_T: η ≈ (96/πT) ln(2T/m_T)

## Context
This work extends the derivation of stochastic GLL equations from quantum field theory, originally done for the symmetric phase by Greiner and collaborators (1996), to the broken phase of the λφ⁴ model. It enables future nonequilibrium studies of scalar fields in thermal baths using standard Langevin lattice methods, with the counterterm renormalization approach allowing lattice-spacing-independent equilibrium results.
