# Three-particle decays of light nuclei resonances

> We have studied the three-particle decay of 12C, 9Be and 6Be resonances. These nuclei have been described as three-body systems by means of the complex scaled hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method. The short-distance part of the wave-function is responsible for the energies whereas the information related to the observable decay properties is contained at large distances, which must be computed accurately. As an illustration we show the results for the angular distribution of 9Be and 6Be resonances.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the angular distributions of three-body decays of light nuclei resonances (9Be and 6Be) can be computed from a complex-scaled hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method, and that these distributions are sensitive to the total angular momentum and parity of the initial resonance, but the energy distributions of the decay fragments are conjectured to be insensitive to the initial many-body structure.

## Method

The authors describe each nucleus as a three-body system (12C as three alpha particles, 9Be as two alpha particles plus one neutron, 6Be as one alpha particle plus two protons) using the complex-scaled hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method. They solve the angular part of the Hamiltonian at fixed hyperradius to obtain effective potentials, then expand the total wavefunction in hyperradial functions. A structureless three-body potential of the form V₃b = S exp(-ρ²/b²) is fitted to reproduce resonance energies, and the large-distance part of the wavefunction is computed accurately to extract decay properties via Monte Carlo integration.

## Key numbers

- 12C: The previously assigned spin and parity of the 13.35 MeV state was changed from 2⁻ to 4⁻ based on the authors' theoretical work, later supported by experimental community.
- 9Be: Angular distributions were computed for five lowest resonances with Jπ = 5/2⁻, 1/2⁻, 5/2⁺, 3/2⁺, and 3/2⁻, after removing sequential decay via 8Be(0⁺).
- 6Be: Angular distributions were computed for the two low-lying resonances (0⁺ and 2⁺), showing that the alpha particle prefers to come out perpendicular to the direction formed by the two protons.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous successful application of the three-body cluster model to 12C resonances (Alvarez-Rodriguez 2008), where their suggestion to reassign the 13.35 MeV state from 2⁻ to 4⁻ was supported by experimental groups (Oli 2011, Freer 2011). The method enables direct comparison with experimental data for 9Be (Fulton 1994) and 6Be (Papka 2012), and can be used to determine the large-distance structure of resonances and to derive reaction rates for astrophysical nucleosynthesis processes.
