# Beyond the relativistic Hartree mean-field approximation: energy dependent effective mass

> The standard relativistic mean-field model is extended by including dynamical effects that arise in the coupling of single-nucleon motion to collective surface vibrations. A phenomenological scheme, based on a linear ansatz for the energy dependence of the scalar and vector components of the nucleon self-energy for states close to the Fermi surface, allows a simultaneous description of binding energies, radii, deformations and single-nucleon spectra in a self-consistent relativistic framework. The model is applied to the spherical, doubly closed-shell nuclei 132Sn and 208Pb.

## Key claim

By including a linear energy dependence of the scalar and vector components of the nucleon self-energy in the Dirac equation, the authors achieve a simultaneous self-consistent description of binding energies, radii, and single-nucleon spectra in doubly closed-shell nuclei, but the linear ansatz is a phenomenological approximation confined to a window of approximately ±10 MeV around the Fermi energy and the parameter α is adjusted rather than calculated microscopically.

## Method

The standard relativistic mean-field (RMF) Dirac equation is extended by adding a term 2α(E − E_F) to the effective single-nucleon potential for states within ±10 MeV of the Fermi energy, with the same linear energy dependence assumed for both the scalar σ and vector ω potentials. This defines a generalized eigenvalue problem that is solved self-consistently together with the Klein-Gordon equations for the meson fields, using an expansion in a spherical harmonic oscillator basis. The parameter α and the meson-nucleon coupling constants are refitted to nuclear matter and ground-state properties of spherical nuclei, starting from the NL3 parameter set.

## Key numbers

* The adjusted coefficient of the linear energy dependence is 2α = −0.288, compared with approximately −0.35 from the microscopic particle-vibration coupling model of Bernard and Nguyen Van Giai (1979, 1980) and Nguyen Van Giai and Pham Van Thieu (1983) for proton states in ²⁰⁸Pb.
* For ²⁰⁸Pb: experimental binding energy −1636.47 MeV; NL3 result −1639.54 MeV; full energy-dependent model result −1636.80 MeV.
* For ¹³²Sn: experimental binding energy −1102.90 MeV; NL3 result −1105.44 MeV; full energy-dependent model result −1101.31 MeV.
* The average neutron hole energy in ²⁰⁸Pb improves from −10.47 MeV (NL3) to −9.55 MeV (full model), compared with the experimental average of −9.25 MeV.
* The average neutron particle energy in ²⁰⁸Pb improves from −1.33 MeV (NL3) to −2.20 MeV (full model), compared with the experimental average of −2.63 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the standard relativistic mean-field model (RMF), which yields an energy-independent effective mass and therefore underestimates the density of single-nucleon states near the Fermi surface. It builds on non-relativistic studies of the energy-dependent effective mass via particle-vibration coupling (Bernard and Nguyen Van Giai, 1979, 1980; Nguyen Van Giai and Pham Van Thieu, 1983) and dispersion-relation analyses of the optical-model potential (Johnson, Horen and Mahaux, 1987; Mahaux and Sartor, 1989), as well as on the relativistic analysis of the energy-dependent effective mass in nuclear matter by Jaminon and Mahaux (1990). The phenomenological linear ansatz enables self-consistent calculations that can be extended to deformed nuclei and shape-coexistence studies far from stability, unlike first-order perturbation approaches.
