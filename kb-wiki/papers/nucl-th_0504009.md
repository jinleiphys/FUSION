# Proposal for a High Energy Nuclear Database

> We propose to develop a high-energy heavy-ion experimental database and make it accessible to the scientific community through an on-line interface. This database will be searchable and cross-indexed with relevant publications, including published detector descriptions. Since this database will be a community resource, it requires the high-energy nuclear physics community's financial and manpower support. This database should eventually contain all published data from Bevalac, AGS and SPS to RHIC and LHC energies, proton-proton to nucleus-nucleus collisions as well as other relevant systems, and all measured observables. Such a database would have tremendous scientific payoff as it makes systematic studies easier and allows simpler benchmarking of theoretical models to a broad range of old and new experiments. Furthermore, there is a growing need for compilations of high-energy nuclear data for applications including stockpile stewardship, technology development for inertial confinement fusion and target and source development for upcoming facilities such as the Next Linear Collider. To enhance the utility of this database, we propose periodically performing evaluations of the data and summarizing the results in topical reviews.

# Proposal for a High Energy Nuclear Database

## Key claim
The authors propose that a community-driven, web-accessible database of high-energy heavy-ion experimental data should be created and maintained, but they acknowledge that funding is unlikely before FY07 due to budget cuts and that the project requires both financial and manpower support from the high-energy nuclear physics community to succeed.

## Method
The authors outline a plan to build a MySQL relational database accessed through JSP or PHP dynamic web pages, using XML as the data storage format to ensure long-term readability and computational convenience. They propose to store cross sections, particle yields, single-particle spectra, and higher-energy observables such as multi-particle spectra, flow, and correlation data, cross-indexed with publications and detector descriptions. The database would be managed through a community-driven model similar to arXiv.org, with data submission linked to journal peer-review processes and annual workshops to guide development and propose topical reviews.

## Context
This proposal extends the approach of existing nuclear and high-energy databases such as ENDF/B, EXFOR, and HEPDATA (all cited as bib1 and bib2) into the high-energy heavy-ion regime, which the authors note is only partially served by these existing resources. The work is motivated by the risk of data loss as experiments end and their web servers cease maintenance, and it would enable systematic cross-experiment comparisons, theory benchmarking, and applications including proton radiography, heavy-ion driven inertial confinement fusion, neutrino and muon source development, and cosmic ray dose rate studies for NASA. The authors specifically reference the PHENIX collaboration's HBT results (bib10) and charm production calculations (bib5) as case studies demonstrating the need for centralized data archiving and evaluation.
