# Restoration of singlet axial symmetry at finite temperature

> To accomodate recent RHIC data on eta prime multiplicity, we propose a minimal modification of the Witten-Veneziano relation at high temperature. This renders a significant drop of eta prime mass at high temperature signaling a restoration of the U(1)A, and the Goldstone character of etaprime.

# Restoration of singlet axial symmetry at finite temperature

## Key claim

The authors propose a minimal modification of the Witten-Veneziano relation at finite temperature, using the Leutwyler-Smilga connection between QCD and Yang-Mills topological susceptibilities, which allows the eta-prime mass to drop by approximately 200 MeV near the chiral transition temperature, in quantitative agreement with RHIC data, whereas the unmodified Witten-Veneziano relation would instead predict an increase in the eta-prime mass.

## Method

The authors employ a Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter approach in the rainbow-ladder approximation using a rank-2 separable model for the gluon propagator to compute the non-anomalous pseudoscalar meson masses. The anomalous contribution to the eta and eta-prime masses is then added via a modified Witten-Veneziano relation, where the Yang-Mills topological susceptibility is replaced by a temperature-dependent quantity derived from the Leutwyler-Smilga relation, with the thermal dependence of the QCD topological susceptibility modeled by an ansatz controlled by a parameter delta between 0 and 1.

## Key numbers

- The eta-prime mass in vacuum is 958 MeV, and the RHIC fireball value is reported as 340 (+50+280, -60-140) ± 45 MeV, indicating a drop of at least 200 MeV.
- The Yang-Mills topological susceptibility at zero temperature is taken as chi_YM = (0.1757 GeV)^4, a weighted average of lattice results.
- The chiral transition temperature in the separable model used is T_Ch = 128 MeV.
- The characteristic melting temperature of the pure Yang-Mills topological susceptibility is T_YM = 260 MeV, approximately 100 MeV or more above the pseudocritical temperatures for chiral and deconfinement transitions in full QCD.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Horvatic et al. (2007) which found that applying the standard Witten-Veneziano relation with various lattice forms of the Yang-Mills topological susceptibility predicted an increase in the eta-prime mass near the chiral transition, contradicting the RHIC data from Csorgo et al. (2009). The proposed modification couples chiral restoration to U(1)_A restoration via the di Vecchia-Veneziano relation (1980) and the Leutwyler-Smilga relation (1992), enabling the topological susceptibility to melt earlier than the pion decay constant. This approach can be combined with Polyakov-loop extensions of the model, as shown by Horvatic et al. (2010), to raise the chiral transition temperature to the accepted range of 160-170 MeV.
