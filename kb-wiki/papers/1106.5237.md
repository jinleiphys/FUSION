# The Heine-Stieltjes correspondence and the polynomial approach to the standard pairing problem

> A new approach for solving the Bethe ansatz (Gaudin-Richardson) equations of the standard pairing problem is established based on the Heine-Stieltjes correspondence. For $k$ pairs of valence nucleons on $n$ different single-particle levels, it is found that solutions of the Bethe ansatz equations can be obtained from one (k+1)x(k+1) and one (n-1)x(k+1) matrices, which are associated with the extended Heine-Stieltjes and Van Vleck polynomials, respectively. Since the coefficients in these polynomials are free from divergence with variations in contrast to the original Bethe ansatz equations, the approach thus provides with a new efficient and systematic way to solve the problem, which, by extension, can also be used to solve a large class of Gaudin-type quantum many-body problems and to establish a new efficient angular momentum projection method for multi-particle systems.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Bethe ansatz (Gaudin-Richardson) equations for the standard pairing problem can be solved by constructing two matrices whose coefficients are free from divergence, unlike the original equations; however, the approach yields some solutions with complex eigen-energies and near-degenerate cases that must be discarded, indicating that further study is needed to resolve these ambiguities.

## Method

The authors reformulate the Bethe ansatz equations of the standard pairing Hamiltonian into a second-order Fuchsian differential equation via the Heine-Stieltjes correspondence. They then expand the extended Heine-Stieltjes polynomial y(x) and the Van Vleck polynomial V(x) in power series, which yields a (k+1)x(k+1) matrix F whose eigenvector gives the polynomial coefficients, and an (n-1)x(k+1) matrix P that determines the Van Vleck coefficients. The approach is demonstrated for k=5 pairs on n=5 single-particle levels using a MATHEMATICA code on a PC.

## Key numbers

- For k=5 pairs in the sixth major shell with n=5 levels (1h7/2, 2d5/2, 2d3/2, 3s1/2, 1h11/2) at equal spacing single-particle energies epsilon_i = i and pairing strength G = 0.5, the number of polynomial solutions is eta(5,5) = 71.
- The ground state eigen-energy (sum of zeros) is -3.6158 (arbitrary units), with the first excited state at 3.0299, second at 3.5444, third at 4.8379, and fourth at 5.7702.
- The approach is stated to be computationally feasible for k ~ 10^1 to 10^3 and n ~ 10^1 to 10^2, with the only CPU-intensive operation being solving the (k+1)x(k+1) matrix eigen-equation.

## Context

This work extends the Richardson-Gaudin exact solution method for the nuclear pairing problem, which was originally developed by Richardson (1963) and later extended by Gaudin and others. The approach builds on the electrostatic analogue of the Bethe ansatz equations established by Heine and Stieltjes, and on prior work by Pan and collaborators on angular momentum projection. The method enables practical numerical solution of the pairing problem for realistic nuclear systems with up to thousands of pairs and dozens of single-particle levels, and also provides a new angular momentum projection technique for multi-particle systems.
