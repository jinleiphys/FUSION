# Leading order corrections to the Bethe-Heitler process in the $γp\rightarrow l^+l^-p$ reaction

> The ratio of di-lepton production cross sections on a proton, using the $γp\rightarrow l^+ l^- p$ process, above and below di-muon production threshold allows to extract the effective lepton-proton interaction, which is required to be identical for electrons and muons if lepton universality is exact. To test for a scenario of broken universality at the percent level, of the size which could explain the different proton charge radii extracted from electron scattering and from muonic hydrogen spectroscopy, we evaluate all one-loop QED corrections to this process, including the full lepton mass dependencies. We furthermore show that two-photon exchange processes with both photons attached to the proton line vanish after averaging over di-lepton angles, and estimate the relatively small radiation off the proton. We compare the full one-loop calculation with a soft-photon approximation of the same order, and present estimates for a planned experiment.

## Key claim

The paper evaluates all one-loop QED corrections to the $\gamma p\rightarrow l^+l^-p$ process, including full lepton mass dependencies, to test for a scenario of broken lepton universality at the percent level that could explain the proton radius puzzle; the authors find that radiative corrections shift the cross-section ratio by about 1%, which is significantly larger than the 0.2% effect from the radius difference, making the full one-loop calculation imperative for interpreting upcoming experiments.

## Method

The authors performed a complete one-loop QED calculation for the Bethe-Heitler process $\gamma p\rightarrow l^+l^-p$, computing all leptonic vertex and self-energy corrections with full lepton mass dependence using dimensional regularization for UV and IR divergences, and employing on-shell renormalization. They used three independent setups: an analytic calculation using Integration-By-Parts identities, a numerical calculation with the Mathematica packages FeynArts and FormCalc using LoopTools, and a calculation of self-energy and vertex diagrams with projection techniques. Real corrections from soft photon emission were included and verified to cancel infrared divergences with the virtual corrections, and two-photon exchange processes with both photons attached to the proton line were shown to vanish after averaging over di-lepton angles.

## Key numbers

- The difference between the proton charge radius extracted from electron scattering (R_E = 0.879(8) fm from A1@MAMI) and from muonic hydrogen spectroscopy (R_E = 0.84087(39) fm) amounts to a 0.2% effect on the cross-section ratio of muon- and electron-pair production
- A measurement of the cross-section ratio with a precision of $7\times 10^{-4}$ would allow testing lepton universality at the $3\sigma$ significance level
- Radiative corrections to the cross-section ratio are of order 1%, which is significantly larger than the 0.2% effect from the radius difference
- The fine-structure constant is defined as $\alpha \equiv e^2/4\pi \approx 1/137$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous soft-photon approximation calculation (Heller, 2018) by providing the full one-loop QED calculation with complete lepton mass dependence, which is necessary because the soft-photon approximation was found to be insufficient for the required precision. The paper builds on the proposal by Pauk (2015) to test lepton universality by comparing electron- and muon-pair production cross sections in the $\gamma p\rightarrow l^+l^-p$ reaction, and provides the theoretical framework needed for the upcoming MAMI experiment. The results enable a conclusive test of lepton universality at the percent level, which could address the proton radius puzzle arising from the discrepancy between electron scattering results (Bernauer, 2010; Bernauer, 2013) and muonic hydrogen spectroscopy (Pohl, 2010; Antognini, 2013).
