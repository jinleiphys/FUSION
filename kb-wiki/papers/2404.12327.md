# Gapless non-hydrodynamic modes in relativistic kinetic theory

> We provide rigorous criteria to determine whether the non-hydrodynamic sector of a relativistic kinetic theory is gapless. These general criteria apply to both Boltzmann's equation and approximate models thereof, provided that the latter are consistent with thermodynamic principles. As an application of these criteria, we prove that the non-hydrodynamic sector is gapless whenever the scattering cross-section decays to zero at large energies. Physically, this happens because, if particles with very high energy (compared to the temperature) are free streaming, we can use them to build hot non-hydrodynamic waves, which live longer than any hydrodynamic wave. Since many standard cross-sections in quantum field theory vanish at high energies, the existence of these non-thermal long-lived waves is a rather general feature of relativistic systems.

## Key claim
The paper proves that the non-hydrodynamic sector of a relativistic kinetic theory is gapless (i.e., possesses infinitely long-lived excitations) whenever the scattering cross-section decays to zero at high energies, which is a common feature of quantum field theory interactions. The abstract's claim that "many standard cross-sections in quantum field theory vanish at high energies" is hedged by the paper's own admission that some theories with mass scales may allow mild growth of the cross-section with energy, making gapped non-hydrodynamic sectors possible as exceptions rather than the rule.

## Method
The author formulates the linearized relativistic Boltzmann equation in the homogeneous limit, defining the operator (p^0)^{-1}L on the Hilbert space L^2(R^3, f^{eq}_p) with inner product weighted by the equilibrium distribution. The Hilbert space is decomposed into a hydrodynamic subspace (spanned by collisional invariants 1 and p^nu) and its orthogonal complement of non-hydrodynamic excitations. A theorem is proven establishing four equivalent criteria for gaplessness: existence of arbitrarily long-lived non-hydrodynamic excitations, arbitrarily small norm of (p^0)^{-1}L acting on non-hydrodynamic states, arbitrarily small entropy production per unit free energy, and accumulation of the spectrum at zero frequency. The author then constructs explicit sequences of perturbations (Laguerre polynomials for lambda phi^4 theory, exponential functions for general interactions) to test these criteria, and derives a high-energy criterion based on the asymptotic behavior of the differential cross-section.

## Key numbers
- For massless scalar particles with lambda phi^4 interaction, the eigenvalues of the collision operator for the sequence phi_p^{(n)} = L_n^{(5)}(beta p^0) p^2 p^3 scale as chi_{n2} proportional to -(n+1)/(n+3), and the ratio ||(p^0)^{-1}L phi_p^{(n)}|| / ||phi_p^{(n)}|| is bounded by const/sqrt(n+3), which approaches 0 as n approaches infinity.
- For general interactions with cross-section bounded by A/s^a + B/s (0 < a < 1), the sequence phi_p^{(n)} = exp[(beta/2)(1-1/n)p^0] * 2p^2 p^3 / [(p^2)^2 + (p^3)^2] yields entropy production ratio varsigma/E^0 bounded by const/n^a, approaching 0 as n approaches infinity.
- In the intuitive argument for lambda phi^4 theory, the non-hydrodynamic energy at late times decays as E_non-hy(t) approximately equal to 4 N E_th (p^0)^2 / (J^0 g t), where J^0 is particle density, g is the coupling constant, E_th is thermal energy scale, and p^0 is the high-energy scale.
- For QCD energy loss with p^0(t) = [sqrt(p^0(0)) - C t]^2, a population with number density proportional to (p^0)^{-3} yields non-thermal energy decaying as 1/(6 C^2 t^2).

## Context
This work extends the mathematical analysis of "soft potentials" in non-relativistic kinetic theory (Grad 1963, McLennan 1965, Caflisch 1980) to the relativistic domain, providing rigorous criteria for the "poles or cut?" dilemma raised by Moore (2018). The paper directly addresses the question of whether the non-hydrodynamic sector of relativistic kinetic theory has a gap (poles, finite relaxation time) or is continuous (cut, infinite relaxation time), which was previously studied through qualitative models (Kurkela 2017, Gavassino 2024) and numerical experiments (Moore 2018, Ochsenfeld 2023, Rocha 2024). The results enable a systematic classification of relativistic kinetic theories based on their high-energy cross-section behavior, and suggest that most QFT-based plasmas (including QCD) likely have gapless non-hydrodynamic sectors, implying that hydrodynamics may not emerge spontaneously from generic initial conditions.
