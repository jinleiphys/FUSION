# Resumming Quark's Longitudinal Momentum Logarithms in LaMET Expansion of Lattice PDFs

> In the large-momentum expansion for parton distribution functions (PDFs), the natural physics scale is the longitudinal momentum ($p_z$) of the quarks (or gluons) in a large-momentum hadron. We show how to expose this scale dependence through resumming logarithms of the type $\ln^n p_z/μ$ in the matching coefficient, where $μ$ is a fixed renormalization scale. The result enhances the accuracy of the expansion at moderate $p_z>1$ GeV, and at the same time, clearly shows that the partons cannot be approximated from quarks with $p_z\sim Λ_{\rm QCD}$ which are not predominantly collinear with the parent hadron momentum, consistent with power counting of the large-momentum effective theory. The same physics mechanism constrains the coordinate space expansion at large distances $z$, the conjugate of $p_z$, as illustrated in the example of fitting the moments of the PDFs.

## Key claim
The paper shows that resumming logarithms of the type \(\ln^n(p_z/\mu)\) in the LaMET matching coefficient exposes the intrinsic physical scale of the quasi-PDF as \(2xP_z\) (the quark's longitudinal momentum), which improves accuracy at moderate \(p_z>1\) GeV but also reveals that perturbative matching breaks down when \(2xP_z\) becomes too small (below about 0.7 GeV), consistent with power-counting expectations from large-momentum effective theory.

## Method
The authors apply renormalization group resummation (RGR) to the LaMET matching kernel that converts quasi-PDFs to light-cone PDFs, using the DGLAP evolution equations to evolve the PDF from its intrinsic scale \(Q_{\rm eff}=2xP_z c'\) (with \(c'\sim 1\)) to a fixed renormalization scale \(\mu\). They implement this using lattice QCD matrix elements for the pion valence PDF from the BNL/ANL collaboration at lattice spacings \(a=0.04\) fm and \(a=0.06\) fm with momenta \(P_z=0\) to 2.4 GeV, renormalized in the hybrid scheme. They also apply the same RGR approach to the Wilson coefficients in the coordinate-space operator product expansion (OPE) for fitting PDF moments.

## Key numbers
- The intrinsic physical scale for quasi-PDF matching is \(Q_{\rm eff}=2xP_z\), with \(c'\sim 1\) varied between 0.8 and 1.2 to test perturbative stability.
- RGR matching improves accuracy in the region \(0.7~{\rm GeV} < 2xP_z < 1.5~{\rm GeV}\).
- Perturbative matching breaks down for \(2xP_z < 0.7~{\rm GeV}\), where the effective coupling \(\alpha_s(2xP_z)\) becomes too large.
- Lattice momenta used: \(P_z=0.43, 0.86, 1.29, 1.72, 2.15\) GeV at \(a=0.06\) fm and \(P_z=0.48, 0.97, 1.45, 1.93, 2.42\) GeV at \(a=0.04\) fm.
- The linear divergence coefficient extracted from static quark-antiquark potential: \(a\delta m(a=0.06~{\rm fm})=0.1586(8)\) and \(a\delta m(a=0.04~{\rm fm})=0.1508(12)\).
- Fitted effective renormalon ambiguity \(m_0^{\rm eff}=0.080(17)\) GeV from fit range \(z\in[0.08,0.16]\) fm.
- OPE with RGR is expected to work only at perturbatively-small distances \(z\ll 1/\Lambda_{\rm QCD}\) up to \(0.2\sim 0.3\) fm.

## Context
This work extends the LaMET framework developed by Ji (2013, 2014) and applied by numerous lattice collaborations including BNL/ANL (Gao et al., 2020, 2021, 2022) for pion PDFs. The resummation approach is analogous to standard renormalization group resummation in deep inelastic scattering, where the natural scale is the photon virtuality \(Q\). The paper shows that previous fixed-order matching analyses (Radyushkin, 2018; Gao, 2020) that used data up to \(z=0.6\) or 0.8 fm for moment fitting may have overestimated the reliability of perturbation theory at large distances. The RGR treatment clarifies that only data at \(z\ll 1/\Lambda_{\rm QCD}\) can be used to extract PDF moments, and that the first moment \(\langle x^2\rangle\) can be determined reliably with \(P_z\sim 2-3\) GeV, while \(\langle x^4\rangle\) is only marginally accessible.
