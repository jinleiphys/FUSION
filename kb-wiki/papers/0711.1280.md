# The classical pion field in a nucleus

> A self-consistent symmetry arises when the nucleon angular momentum j and the isospin t are coupled to a grand spin G. Closed G shells become sources of a classical pion field with a hedgehog shape. Although the amplitude of the pion field, as measured by the chiral angle, is small, it is found to perturb significantly the energies of the nucleon orbits.

## Key claim

Closed grand-spin (G) shells, where the nucleon angular momentum j and isospin t are coupled, act as sources of a classical pion field with a hedgehog shape; although the amplitude of this field (measured by the chiral angle) is small (not exceeding 0.05), it significantly perturbs the energies of the nucleon orbits, but the calculation is exploratory and does not allow quantitative predictions of nuclear spectra.

## Method

The author constructs a non-relativistic model Hamiltonian in which nucleons occupy spherical harmonic-oscillator orbits and interact with a classical pion field via a pseudoscalar coupling, plus a contact spin-orbit interaction. Single-particle states are expanded in a basis where angular momentum and isospin are coupled to a grand spin G, and closed G shells are filled. The chiral angle (pion field) is determined self-consistently by minimizing the total energy functional, iterating between solving the single-particle eigenvalue problem and updating the pion field via its source term derived from the occupied orbits.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: f_pi = 93.2 MeV; axial coupling constant: g_A = 1.257; pion mass: m_pi = 139.6 MeV; nucleon mass: M = 938.9 MeV.
- For the A=13 configuration (closed 1s_{1/2} and 1p_{3/2} j-shells, filled (1p_{1/2}) G=0 shell): energy gap = 4.14 MeV, maximum chiral angle theta_max = 0.038.
- For the A=31 configuration (closed 1s,1p,1d_{5/2} j-shells, filled (2s_{1/2}) G=1 shell): energy gap = 2.92 MeV, theta_max = 0.03.
- For the A=62 configuration (closed 1s,1p,2s,1d,1f_{7/2} j-shells, filled (2p_{3/2}) G=2 and (2p_{1/2}) G=0 shells): energy gap = 1.71 MeV, theta_max = 0.043.
- The chiral angle theta(x) = a x e^{-x} (with x = m_pi r) was used as an initial guess; self-consistent calculations yield maximum chiral angles in the range 0.03 < theta_max < 0.04 for configurations with appreciable energy gaps.
- Harmonic oscillator frequency for O-16: hbar omega = 12.71 MeV (fitting charge radius 2.71 fm); for Ca-40: hbar omega = 10.27 MeV (fitting charge radius 3.48 fm).
- Spin-orbit strength: W_ls = 15 fm^{-5}, adjusted to yield a 5 MeV splitting between 1d_{5/2} and 1d_{3/2} states in O-16.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Sugimoto, Ikeda, Toki et al. (2002-2007) who constructed independent-particle states with mixed parity and isospin orbits to explore the role of the tensor force from pion exchange. It contrasts with standard mean-field calculations using Skyrme or Gogny forces (reviewed by Heenen, 2003) or relativistic mean-field models (reviewed by Vretenar, 2005), which simulate pion-exchange effects via effective interactions and do not generate a classical pion field. The results point to possible deformations that could be incorporated into generator-coordinate calculations of odd-even and odd-odd nuclei, though the author notes that the energy gaps are not large enough to likely produce stable J=T rotational bands as seen in Skyrmions (Witten, 1983) or chiral solitons (Ripka, 1997).
