# Shape coexistence in N=14 isotones: 19B, 24Ne and 28Si

> Shape coexistence problems in N=14 isotones are studied with a microscopic method of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics. The present calculations reproduce features of deformation in 28Si, and predict possible shape coexistence of neutron density in neutron-rich nuclei(19B and 24Ne). We also systematically study the structures of the ground and excited states, and the molecular resonances of 28Si. Besides the shape coexistence in the low-energy region, the results indicate the high-lying levels with $α$-cluster and 12C+16O molecular structures in 28Si, which are consistent with the observed spin-assigned resonances. The resonance states above the threshold energies are connected with the low-lying deformed states from a view point of molecular excitation by discussing inter-cluster wave functions.

# Shape coexistence in N=14 isotones: 19B, 24Ne and 28Si

## Key claim

The AMD calculations reproduce the known oblate-prolate shape coexistence in 28Si and predict similar shape coexistence of neutron density in the neutron-rich N=14 isotones 19B and 24Ne, though in 24Ne both coexisting minima are prolate rather than oblate-prolate. The high-lying molecular resonances in 28Si are connected to the low-lying deformed states through inter-cluster excitation of the relative motion.

## Method

The authors applied antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) with parity projection before variation and angular momentum projection after variation, using the modified Volkov No.1 central force (case 3, m=0.62), G3RS spin-orbit force (u1=-u2=2800 MeV), and Coulomb force. For highly excited states in 28Si, they imposed a constraint on the total principal quanta of the spherical harmonic oscillator (N̂=W) and performed generator coordinate method (GCM) calculations by superposing 23 spin-parity projected AMD wave functions.

## Key numbers

- The 0+3 state in 28Si, considered the head of the prolate band, lies at 6.691 MeV excitation energy (experimental value)
- Proton deformation parameters in 19B: βp=0.3 in the oblate state, βp=0.4 in the prolate state
- The Q-moment of the prolate 3/2- state in 19B is 20% larger than that of 17B
- In 24Ne ground band: βp=0.3 and βn=0.2 for proton and neutron deformation respectively
- B(E2;2+1→0+1) in 24Ne: Bp(E2)=37 e2fm4 for protons, Bn(E2)=38 e2fm4 for neutrons
- Rotational parameter for the Mg-α band in 28Si: ℏ2/2I ∼ 130 keV
- Rotational parameters for the second and third C-O molecular bands in 28Si: ℏ2/2I ∼ 99 keV and 74 keV respectively
- The lowest C-O molecular band has n0=8 nodes in the inter-cluster relative motion; the second and third bands have 9 and 10 nodes respectively

## Context

This work extends earlier mean-field and 7α cluster model studies of shape coexistence in 28Si (Bauhoff and others), which failed to reproduce the large energy interval between oblate and prolate states, by showing that AMD captures the spin-orbit energy gain. It also connects to C-O cluster model studies of molecular resonances (Baye, Kato) and to experimental α-transfer reaction data (Artemov) and observed spin-assigned resonances (James, Charles, Frohlich, Baye). The results enable future searches for molecular states in unstable sd-shell nuclei and suggest that Q-moment measurements along B isotopes could probe deformation in 19B.
