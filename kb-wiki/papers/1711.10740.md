# Accelerating hydrodynamic description of pseudorapidity density and the initial energy density in p+p, Cu+Cu, Au+Au, and Pb+Pb collisions at RHIC and LHC

> A known class of analytic, exact, accelerating solutions of prefect relativistic hydrodynamics with longitudinal acceleration is utilized to describe results on the pseudorapidity distributions for different collision systems. These results include $dN/dη$ measured in p+p, Cu+Cu, Au+Au, and Pb+Pb collisions at RHIC and LHC, in a broad centrality range. Going beyond the traditional Bjorken model, from the accelerating hydrodynamic description we determine the initial energy density and other thermodynamic quantities in those collisions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a class of exact, accelerating solutions of perfect relativistic hydrodynamics can describe pseudorapidity density measurements across multiple collision systems and energies, yielding improved estimates of initial energy density that go beyond the traditional boost-invariant Bjorken model. The acceleration parameter lambda decreases with increasing collision energy but increases with centrality at fixed energy, with the correction to the Bjorken estimate being most significant at RHIC energies (factors near 3) and modest at LHC energies (20-30%).

## Method

The authors fit an analytic accelerating hydrodynamic solution (from the Buda-Lund model family) to charged particle pseudorapidity density dN/deta data from PHOBOS (130 GeV Au+Au, 200 GeV Au+Au, 200 GeV Cu+Cu), ALICE (2.76 TeV Pb+Pb), and CMS/TOTEM (7 and 8 TeV p+p) across multiple centrality classes. From the fits they extract the longitudinal acceleration parameter lambda, then compute a corrected initial energy density using Eq. (12) that accounts for the mapping between space-time rapidity and momentum rapidity, the volume element change, and the equation of state parameter kappa.

## Key numbers

- For 7 TeV p+p collisions: lambda = 1.073 +/- 0.001, corrected initial energy density epsilon_corr = 0.64 +/- 0.01 GeV/fm^3 (with tau_f/tau_0 = 2)
- For 8 TeV p+p collisions: lambda = 1.067 +/- 0.001, corrected initial energy density epsilon_corr = 0.64 +/- 0.01 GeV/fm^3 (with tau_f/tau_0 = 2)
- For 130 GeV Au+Au 0-6% central: lambda = 1.304 +/- 0.06, epsilon_corr = 13.02 +/- 2.51 GeV/fm^3 (with tau_f/tau_0 = 6 +/- 2, kappa = 10)
- For 200 GeV Au+Au 0-6% central: lambda = 1.239 +/- 0.045, epsilon_corr = 12.33 +/- 2.12 GeV/fm^3
- For 200 GeV Cu+Cu 0-6% central: lambda = 1.176 +/- 0.025, epsilon_corr = 5.67 +/- 0.93 GeV/fm^3
- For 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb 0-5% central: lambda = 1.075 +/- 0.005, epsilon_corr = 4.97 +/- 0.72 GeV/fm^3
- Bjorken estimate for 130 GeV Au+Au 0-6%: epsilon_Bj = 4.74 +/- 0.49 GeV/fm^3
- Bjorken estimate for 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb 0-5%: epsilon_Bj = 3.83 +/- 0.38 GeV/fm^3
- Initial temperature for 130 GeV Au+Au 0-6%: T_corr = 0.58 +/- 0.03 GeV
- Initial temperature for 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb 0-5%: T_corr = 0.62 +/- 0.02 GeV

## Context

This work extends the accelerating hydrodynamic solutions of Csorgo et al. (2006) and Nagy et al. (2007) from rapidity density fits to a systematic survey of pseudorapidity distributions across p+p, Cu+Cu, Au+Au, and Pb+Pb collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. It goes beyond the traditional Bjorken estimate (Bjorken, 1982) which assumes boost-invariant flow and no longitudinal acceleration, and also extends earlier p+p analyses at 7 and 8 TeV by Csanad et al. (2013, 2016). The results enable improved estimates of initial energy density, temperature, and pressure for the quark-gluon plasma across different system sizes and collision energies, and provide a benchmark for future viscous hydrodynamic studies of longitudinal acceleration.
