# Coulomb Effects in Few-Body Reactions

> The method of screening and renormalization is used to include the Coulomb interaction between the charged particles in the momentum-space description of three- and four-body nuclear reactions. The necessity for the renormalization of the scattering amplitudes and the reliability of the method is demonstrated. The Coulomb effect on observables is discussed.

# Coulomb Effects in Few-Body Reactions

## Key claim

The screening and renormalization method can reliably include the Coulomb interaction in momentum-space descriptions of three- and four-body nuclear reactions, provided the screening function is chosen with a sharpness parameter n between 3 and 8; the method converges to the full Coulomb results in the infinite screening radius limit, but fails at zero relative energy between charged particles where extrapolation is required.

## Method

The author solves momentum-space integral equations (generalized Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas equations) for three- and four-particle scattering at finite Coulomb screening radius R, using a screened Coulomb potential w_R(r) = w_C(r) exp[-(r/R)^n] with n=4 as the standard choice. The long-range part of the transition operator is separated analytically and renormalized by diverging phase factors, while the short-range remainder is calculated numerically and converges with increasing R. For breakup reactions involving two charged particles and one neutral particle, the final-state renormalization uses the two-particle screened Coulomb transition matrix between the charged pair.

## Key numbers

- Screening radius R = 10 to 30 fm is sufficient for convergence in most cases, with R = 20 fm needed for breakup amplitudes
- Yukawa screening (n=1) requires R = 1280 fm to approximate true Coulomb at r = 5 fm as well as the n=4 choice does with R = 20 fm
- The optimal screening sharpness parameter range is 3 ≤ n ≤ 8
- Proton-proton ¹S₀ phase shift at 3 MeV lab energy converges faster with n=4 than with Yukawa screening or sharp cutoff
- Proton-deuteron elastic scattering at E_p = 10 MeV shows R-independence for R ≥ 10 fm after renormalization
- Proton-deuteron breakup at E_p = 13 MeV in space star configuration converges for R ≥ 20 fm
- The renormalization phase Φ_αR(q) = K_α(q)[ln(2qR) - C/n] where C ≈ 0.5772156649 is the Euler number

## Context

This work extends the screening and renormalization method previously applied to two-particle scattering by Taylor (1974) and to three-particle systems by Alt (1994, 2002) and Deltuva (2005), now generalized to include three-body forces and four-body reactions. The approach contrasts with configuration-space methods by Kievsky (2001) that impose proper Coulomb boundary conditions explicitly and are favored at very low energies, while the momentum-space screening method is more efficient at higher energies and for three-body breakup reactions. The results contradict an alternative renormalization prescription proposed by Witala (2009) that claimed no renormalization is needed for elastic scattering; Deltuva shows that under exact calculations the standard renormalization is necessary and the alternative approach involves uncontrolled approximations.
