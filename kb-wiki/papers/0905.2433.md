# Viscous Hydrodynamics and the Quark Gluon Plasma

> One of the most striking results from the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider is the strong elliptic flow. This review summarizes what is observed and how these results are combined with reasonable theoretical assumptions to estimate the shear viscosity of QCD near the phase transition. A data comparison with viscous hydrodynamics and kinetic theory calculations indicates that the shear viscosity to entropy ratio is surprisingly small, $η/s < 0.4$. The preferred range is $η/s \simeq (1\leftrightarrow 3) \times 1/4π$.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the shear viscosity to entropy ratio of QCD near the phase transition is surprisingly small, with a preferred range of eta/s approximately (1 to 3) times 1/(4 pi), but the upper bound of eta/s < 0.4 is conditional on combining viscous hydrodynamic calculations with kinetic theory and specific assumptions about the initial eccentricity of the collision.

## Method

The review analyzes experimental elliptic flow data from the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and compares them to viscous hydrodynamic calculations and kinetic theory models. It categorizes the collision geometry using the standard Glauber participant eccentricity and the event plane method to extract the elliptic flow coefficient v2, then examines the scaled elliptic flow v2/epsilon as a function of centrality to infer the shear viscosity.

## Key numbers

* The shear viscosity to entropy ratio is bounded as eta/s < 0.4 from data comparison with viscous hydrodynamics and kinetic theory.
* The preferred range for eta/s is (1 to 3) x 1/(4 pi), where 1/(4 pi) is the conjectured lower bound from Kovtun, Son, and Starinets (2005).
* For a central collision of two gold nuclei, the number of participants Np is approximately 340, nearly equal to the total nucleon number N=394, leaving about 50 spectators.
* The inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section used is sigma_NN approximately 40 mb.
* For v2 approximately 7% and N approximately 500, the product N v2^2 is approximately 2.5, indicating statistical fluctuations are not negligible.
* The reaction plane resolution parameter R is approximately 0.7 in the STAR experiment and could be as large as 0.95 at the LHC.
* The root mean square radius of the gold nucleus is R_A approximately 6.3 fm.

## Context

This review extends the ideal hydrodynamic calculations of Huovinen and Ruuskanen (2006) and Kolb, Huovinen, Heinz, and Heiselberg (2001) by incorporating viscous corrections to describe the centrality dependence of elliptic flow. It builds on the discovery of strong elliptic flow at RHIC and the AdS/CFT conjecture of a lower bound on eta/s from Policastro, Son, and Starinets (2001) and Kovtun, Son, and Starinets (2005). The work enables systematic extraction of QCD transport coefficients from heavy ion data and motivates further viscous hydrodynamic modeling and lattice QCD calculations of the shear viscosity.
