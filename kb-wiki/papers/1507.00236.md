# Configuration Mixing of Quark States in Baryons

> Mixing angles are used to describe the $SU(6)\otimes O(3)$ symmetry breaking in $\left[70,~1^-\right]$ multiplet in the sector of the lowest mass nucleon resonances, which are investigated extensively in constituent quark models for baryon spectroscopy. The transition amplitudes for the meson photoproduction off nucleon can also be expressed in terms of the mixing angles to take into account the configuration mixing. Those amplitudes are derived as a function of the mixing angles between $|N^2P_M{J}^-\rangle$ and $|N^4P_M{J}^-\rangle$ states, with $J$=1/2 and 3/2, for the processes $γp \to ηp,~K^+ Λ,~K^+ Σ^\circ,~K^\circ Σ^+$. The present status of our knowledge on the mixing angles between $S_{11}(1535)$ and $S_{11}(1650)$ ($θ_{S}$), as well as between $D_{13}(1520)$ and $D_{13} (1700)$ ($θ_{D}$) is reported. Since these resonances play very important role in the threshold region for both $η$ and kaon production mechanisms, they are expected to provide crucial tests of different quark models for the baryon spectroscopy.

# Configuration Mixing of Quark States in Baryons

## Key claim

The paper derives explicit relations between SU(6)⊗O(3) symmetry breaking coefficients and mixing angles for S11 and D13 nucleon resonances in pseudoscalar meson photoproduction, but the extracted mixing angles depend on the chosen convention (Koniuk-Isgur versus Hey et al.) and on which photoproduction channel is analyzed, with values ranging from -35° to +38° for θS and +4° to +15° for θD across different models.

## Method

The authors use a chiral constituent quark model based on an effective chiral Lagrangian to derive transition amplitudes for γp → ηp, K+Λ, K+Σ°, and K°Σ+ processes. They express the SU(6)⊗O(3) symmetry breaking in terms of mixing angles between |N²PM J⁻⟩ and |N⁴PM J⁻⟩ states for J=1/2 and 3/2, and calculate the constant ℛ relating symmetry breaking coefficients to mixing angles using the Moorhouse selection rule and explicit matrix elements of the meson transition operator.

## Key numbers

- ℛS = -1 for γp → ηp, ℛS = 0 for γp → K+Λ, ℛS = -4 for γp → K+Σ° and K°Σ+
- ℛD = 1/√10 for γp → ηp, ℛD = 0 for γp → K+Λ, ℛD = 4/√10 for γp → K+Σ° and K°Σ+
- Extracted mixing angle θS = -27° from γp → ηp data up to W ≤ 2 GeV (Saghai-Li 2001)
- Extracted mixing angle θS = -35° and θD = +15° from γp → ηp data up to W ≈ 2.6 GeV (He et al. 2007)
- OGE (harmonic oscillator) model gives θS = -32° and θD = +6° (Isgur-Karl)
- OPE (harmonic oscillator) model gives θS = ±13° and θD = ±8° (Glozman-Riska)
- RCQM gives θS = +38°±4° and θD = +10° to +15° (Capstick-Roberts)
- 1/Nc expansion gives θS = +22° and θD = +28° (Pirjol-Schat)
- Decay rates analysis gives θS = -31.9° and θD = +10.4° (Hey et al.)

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model studies of baryon spectroscopy by Isgur and Karl (1977-1979) and Koniuk and Isgur (1979), who used OGE interactions with harmonic oscillator potentials, and the OPE/GBE approach of Glozman and Riska (1995). The derived relations enable systematic extraction of mixing angles from photoproduction data, complementing static analyses of baryon mass spectra and decay rates. The results show that η and kaon photoproduction in the threshold region, dominated by S11 and D13 resonances, provide crucial tests for distinguishing between OGE-based and GBE-based quark models, with future kaon photoproduction studies needed to resolve remaining ambiguities in the mixing angles.
