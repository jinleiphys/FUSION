# Functional renormalization group approach to neutron matter

> The chiral nucleon-meson model, previously applied to systems with equal number of neutrons and protons, is extended to asymmetric nuclear matter. Fluctuations are included in the framework of the functional renormalization group. The equation of state for pure neutron matter is studied and compared to recent advanced many-body calculations. The chiral condensate in neutron matter is computed as a function of baryon density. It is found that, once fluctuations are incorporated, the chiral restoration transition for pure neutron matter is shifted to high densities, much beyond three times the density of normal nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The chiral restoration transition for pure neutron matter is shifted to densities much beyond three times nuclear saturation density once fluctuations are incorporated via the functional renormalization group, in contrast to mean-field predictions; the paper hedges that this result applies specifically to cold neutron matter and that the crossover to chiral restoration sets in at about six to eight times nuclear saturation density.

## Method

The authors extend the chiral nucleon-meson model, previously applied to symmetric nuclear matter, to asymmetric nuclear matter by adding an isovector-vector rho field as a mean field. Fluctuations of the nucleon, pion, and sigma fields are included non-perturbatively using the functional renormalization group (FRG) with Wetterich's equation, while the omega and rho vector bosons are treated as non-fluctuating mean fields. The single additional parameter G_rho (the strength of the isovector short-distance interaction) is fixed by reproducing the symmetry energy E_sym = 32 MeV at saturation density. The flow equations are solved numerically using a grid method to obtain the grand-canonical potential, from which the equation of state and chiral condensate are derived for pure neutron matter at zero temperature.

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy at saturation density: E_sym = 32 MeV, used to fix G_rho = 1.12 fm^2
- Slope of symmetry energy: L = 66.3 MeV (compared to empirical range 40.5–61.6 MeV)
- Pion-nucleon sigma term extracted from low-density FRG fit: sigma_piN = 44 MeV
- Crossover to chiral restoration in FRG occurs at about six to eight times nuclear saturation density (n_0 = 0.16 fm^-3)
- Critical chemical potential at liquid-gas transition: mu_c = 923 MeV
- Pion mass: m_pi = 135 MeV; pion decay constant: f_pi = 93 MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral nucleon-meson model with FRG fluctuations previously applied to symmetric nuclear matter by Drews and Weise (2013, 2014) to the asymmetric case of pure neutron matter. The results are compared to chiral Fermi liquid theory by Holt et al. (2013) at low densities and to quantum Monte Carlo calculations by Gandolfi et al. (2012, 2014) up to about three times saturation density, showing good agreement. The finding that fluctuations stabilize the hadronic phase against chiral restoration to very high densities enables future applications to neutron star equations of state, which the authors state will meet mass-radius constraints from two-solar-mass neutron star observations in a forthcoming publication.
