# Neutron electric dipole moment on the lattice

> We carry out a feasibility study toward a lattice QCD calculation of the neutron electric dipole moment (NEDM) in the presence of the $θ$ term using two different approaches. In the first method, we calculate the CP-odd electromagnetic form factor $F_3$, which becomes the NEDM in the zero momentum transfer limit. At the first order in $θ$, we derive a formula connecting the lattice three-point function to the CP-odd electromagnetic form factor. In the second method we directly extract the NEDM from the energy difference between spin-up and spin-down neutron states in the presence of a constant electric field, without expanding a small but non-zero $θ$. We test both approaches numerically, employing the domain-wall quark action with the RG improved gauge action in quenched QCD at $a^{-1}\simeq 2$ GeV on a $16^3\times 32\times 16$ lattice, and further applying the second method to the clover quark action at a similar lattice spacing and nucleon mass. We obtain good signals from both approaches. In particular the second method works well with both fermion formulations.

# Neutron electric dipole moment on the lattice

## Key claim
A lattice QCD calculation of the neutron electric dipole moment (NEDM) from the CP-odd electromagnetic form factor and from the energy shift in an electric field is feasible in quenched QCD, but the numerical results are obtained at a single, relatively high momentum transfer (q^2 ~ 0.58 GeV^2) for the form factor method and at a heavy quark mass (m_N ~ 2.2 GeV) for the electric field method, so the results are not yet at the physical point.

## Method
Two methods were implemented in quenched lattice QCD. In the first method, the CP-odd electromagnetic form factor F_3 was extracted from three-point correlation functions with a topological charge insertion at first order in the theta parameter, using 730 gauge configurations with domain-wall fermions at a^{-1} = 1.81(4) GeV on a 16^3 x 32 x 16 lattice. In the second method, a uniform electric field was introduced via a phase factor on gauge links, and the NEDM was extracted from the exponential slope of the ratio of spin-up to spin-down neutron propagators as a function of time, using 1000 configurations with domain-wall fermions at a heavier quark mass (m_f = 0.12, m_N ~ 2.2 GeV) and also tested with clover fermions.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacing: a^{-1} = 1.81(4) GeV, determined from the rho meson mass at beta = 2.6 on a 16^3 x 32 lattice.
- CP-odd form factor at q^2 ~ 0.58 GeV^2: F_3(0.58 GeV^2)/2m_N = -0.024(5) e*fm for neutron and +0.021(6) e*fm for proton, from the domain-wall fermion calculation at m_f = 0.03 (m_PS/m_V = 0.63).
- CP-odd phase parameter from two-point functions: f_N^1 = -0.247(17) from 730 configurations.
- NEDM from the electric field method with domain-wall fermions at m_f = 0.12 (m_N ~ 2.2 GeV): d_n^theta/theta = -0.0183(60) e*fm, d_p^theta/theta = 0.0176(72) e*fm.
- Electric field strength used: E = +/- 0.004 (lattice units).
- Theta values tested: theta = 0.1 for the electric field method.

## Context
This work extends earlier model-dependent estimates of the NEDM (Crewther 1979, QCD sum rules and chiral perturbation theory) which gave order-of-magnitude differences and even sign ambiguities, by attempting a first-principles lattice QCD calculation. The form factor method follows the approach of Shintani (2005) and Faccioli (2004), while the electric field method was proposed by Aoki (2005). The results enable future calculations at lighter quark masses and in full QCD, with the authors noting they are working toward calculations on full QCD configurations generated by the CP-PACS collaboration.
