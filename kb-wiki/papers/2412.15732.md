# Quark-Quark and Quark-nucleon Potential model Extended-soft-core meson-exchange Interactions

> The Quark-quark (QQ) and Quark-nucleon (QN) interactions in this paper are derived from the Extended-soft-core (ESC) interactions. The meson-quark-quark (MQQ) vertices are determined in the framework of the constituent quark model (CQM). These vertices are such that upon folding with the ground-state baryon quark wave functions the one-boson-exchange (OBE) amplitudes for baryon-baryon (BB), and in particularly for nucleon-nucleon (NN), are reproduced. This opens the attractive possibility to define meson-quark interactions at the quark level which are directly related related to the interactions at the baryon level. the latter have been determined by the baryon-baryon data. Application of these "realistic" quark-quark interactions in the quark-matter phase is presumably of relevance for the description of highly condensed matter, as e.g. neutron-star matter.

## Key claim

The authors derive quark-quark (QQ) and quark-nucleon (QN) interactions from the Extended-soft-core (ESC) meson-exchange model by defining meson-quark-quark vertices within the constituent quark model (CQM), such that folding these vertices with ground-state baryon quark wave functions reproduces the one-boson-exchange amplitudes for baryon-baryon (BB) interactions, particularly nucleon-nucleon (NN). The claim is conditional on the assumption that the infrared behavior of QCD can be ignored in dense quark matter, justifying the use of the same formalism for quarks as for nucleons in nuclear matter.

## Method

The authors construct QQ and QN potentials from local and non-local contributions due to (i) one-boson-exchange (OBE) from nonets of pseudoscalar, vector, scalar, and axial-vector mesons, (ii) diffractive exchanges, (iii) two-pseudoscalar exchange (PS-PS), and (iv) meson-pair-exchange (MPE), with all vertices regulated by gaussian form factors. The connection between meson-baryon-baryon (BBM) couplings and meson-quark-quark (QQM) couplings is established via a 1/M expansion in Pauli-spinor space, where the CQM assumption m_Q = M_B/3 allows the QQM vertices to be chosen such that the ratios c_QQ^(l)/c_BB^(l) are constant for each meson type, achieved by scaling couplings, introducing extra quark-level couplings, and adding a QQM gaussian form factor. The scattering formalism uses the Kadyshevsky quasi-potential equation to derive a three-dimensional relativistic Lippmann-Schwinger type equation for the T-matrix.

## Key numbers

* The constituent quark mass is taken as m_Q = M_N/3, with the effective quark mass calculated in the instanton vacuum as m_Q(p=0) = 345 MeV, close to M_N/3.
* The chiral symmetry breaking scale is Lambda_chiSB approximately 1 GeV, and the confinement scale Lambda_QCD is approximately 100-330 MeV.
* The instanton density is n_c = 8 x 10^{-4} GeV^{-4} and the instanton radius is rho_c = (600 MeV)^{-1} approximately 0.3 fm, with the non-perturbative vacuum expectation value for quark fields <vac|bar(psi)psi|vac> approximately -10^{-2} GeV^3 and effective (u,d) quark masses approximately 200 MeV.
* The ESC16 model includes axial-vector meson potentials and a zero in the scalar- and axial-vector meson form factors as novel ingredients.

## Context

This work extends the ESC16 model (Rijken, Nagels, Yamamoto 2019), which describes nucleon-nucleon (NN), hyperon-nucleon (YN), and hyperon-hyperon (YY) interactions using broken SU(3)-symmetry, by deriving the corresponding quark-level interactions. The approach builds on the constituent quark model (Greenberg 1964, Micu 1969, Le Yaouanc 1973, Manohar and Georgi 1984) and the instanton-liquid model of the QCD vacuum (BPST 1975, Diakonov and Petrov 1986), and it enables future applications to mixed nuclear and quark matter in neutron stars.
