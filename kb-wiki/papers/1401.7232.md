# Strangeness on the nucleon

> Observables from parity violation in elastic electron-nucleon scattering and neutral current quasi-elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering are employed as tools to improve the current knowledge on the strangeness content in the nucleon.

## Key claim

Parity-violating elastic electron-proton scattering data and neutral-current quasielastic neutrino-nucleus scattering data can together constrain the strange quark content of the nucleon, but the analysis shows that the case of no strangeness is excluded by most fits only at the 1σ and 2σ confidence level, and further studies are needed before definite conclusions can be drawn.

## Method

The authors performed a χ²-analysis of world parity-violation elastic electron-proton scattering data (from SAMPLE, HAPPEX, PVA4, and G0 experiments) to extract confidence contours for the strange electric parameter ρₛ and strange magnetic parameter μₛ. Separately, they used MiniBooNE neutral-current quasielastic neutrino-nucleus cross section data within the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) and Superscaling Approach (SuSA) models to fit the axial mass parameter Mₐ and the strange axial parameter gₐ⁽ˢ⁾, then studied the ratio of proton to neutron cross section as a function of these parameters.

## Context

This work extends the global analysis of parity-violation asymmetry data from experiments such as SAMPLE, HAPPEX, PVA4, and G0, which the authors previously reviewed in González-Jiménez and Caballero (2014) to establish confidence contours for strange electric and magnetic parameters. It also builds on the authors' earlier work using MiniBooNE neutral-current data to test the RMF and SuSA models, and enables future studies to combine both electromagnetic and axial strangeness constraints for a more complete determination of the nucleon's strange quark content.
