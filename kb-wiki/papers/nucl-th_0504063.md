# Consistency of nuclear mass formulae

> The general scepticism and loss of faith on the predictive ability of different mass formulae, arising out of the divergence of their predictions in unknown regions taken with respect to a reference mass formula, is successfully dispelled. When the result of relativistic mean field (RMF) theory with a Lagrangian common for all nuclei is taken as reference, the divergence disappears, and clear trend with strong correlation appears restoring our faith in general on the predictions of mass formulae, qualifying them as useful guideline for theoretical and experimental studies of nuclear phenomena.

# Consistency of nuclear mass formulae

## Key claim
When the predictions of different nuclear mass formulae are compared relative to a reference that itself has uneven accuracy across nuclear regions, they appear to diverge in unknown regions; however, if a common substratum with strong theoretical validity, such as relativistic mean field (RMF) theory with a fixed Lagrangian, is used as reference, the divergence disappears and a clear common trend emerges, restoring faith in the predictive ability of mass formulae.

## Method
The authors compared the binding energy differences of ten mass formulae (including Duflo-Zuker, Möller et al., Comay-Kelson, and others) for chains of O, Ca, Sn, and Pb isotopes, first using the Duflo-Zuker mass formula as reference and then using RMF calculations with the NL3 parameter set as reference. They also repeated the comparison using the infinite nuclear matter (INM) mass formula as reference. The binding energies from RMF theory were computed with a single Lagrangian and parameter set applied uniformly to all nuclei.

## Key numbers
- Binding energy differences were plotted for Sn isotopes with neutron number varying between 45 and 110 (Fig. 1).
- The study examined isotopes of Z = 8 (O), 20 (Ca), 50 (Sn), and 82 (Pb) for all comparisons.
- Ten mass formulae were compared in Figs. 3 and 4.
- RMF calculations used the NL3 parameter set, with similar results also obtained using NL-SH, G1, and G2 parameters.

## Context
This work directly addresses the long-standing scepticism raised by comparisons such as those in Mittig (1997) and the NuPECC report, where predictions of different mass formulae diverged in unknown regions when referenced to Duflo-Zuker or Möller et al. The authors show that this divergence is an artifact of the reference choice, not a fundamental failure of the mass models. The result enables continued use of mass formulae as guidelines for experimental studies of exotic nuclei and nucleosynthesis, and validates the INM mass formula of Satpathy (1997) as a theoretically grounded alternative reference.
