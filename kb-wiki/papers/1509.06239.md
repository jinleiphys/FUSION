# Importance-Truncated Large-Scale Shell Model

> We propose an importance-truncation scheme for the large-scale nuclear shell model that extends its range of applicability to larger valence spaces and mid-shell nuclei. It is based on a perturbative measure for the importance of individual basis states that acts as an additional truncation for the many-body model space in which the eigenvalue problem of the Hamiltonian is solved numerically. Through a posteriori extrapolations of all observables to vanishing importance threshold, the full shell-model results can be recovered. In addition to simple threshold extrapolations, we explore extrapolations based on the energy variance. We apply the importance-truncated shell model for the study of 56-Ni in the pf valence space and of 60-Zn and 64-Ge in the pfg9/2 space. We demonstrate the efficiency and accuracy of the approach, which pave the way for future shell-model calculations in larger valence spaces with valence-space interactions derived in ab initio approaches.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an importance-truncation scheme for the large-scale nuclear shell model that extends its range of applicability to larger valence spaces and mid-shell nuclei, with the hedge that the full shell-model results can be recovered through a posteriori extrapolations of all observables to vanishing importance threshold, rather than being directly computed in the full space.

## Method

The method uses a perturbative measure based on first-order many-body perturbation theory to estimate the importance of individual basis states for describing specific eigenstates, defined through the amplitude κν(m) = -⟨Φν|H|Ψref(m)⟩/Δεν. Basis states with importance measure above a threshold κmin are retained, while the rest are discarded. The construction is embedded in an iterative scheme that sequentially increases a truncation parameter Tmax (the number of valence particles promoted to higher-lying orbits) combined with the importance-selection procedure, using reference states obtained from previous diagonalizations in smaller spaces. Extrapolations to vanishing importance threshold are performed using either simple polynomial fits or energy-variance-based extrapolations.

## Key numbers

- Full m-scheme dimension for 56Ni in the pf valence space: 1.09 × 10^9; importance-truncated spaces reduced by about two orders of magnitude
- Absolute energies in the largest importance-truncated spaces for 56Ni differ by only about 10 keV from the full shell model
- Full model-space dimensions for 60Zn and 64Ge in the pfg9/2 space: 2.2 × 10^13 and 1.7 × 10^14, respectively
- For 64Ge, the variance extrapolation gives the ground-state energy about 0.5 MeV lower than the threshold-extrapolated energy at Tmax=10

## Context

This work extends the importance-truncation scheme previously applied successfully in the no-core shell model (Roth 2009) to the valence-space shell model context. It enables shell-model calculations in valence spaces covering more than one major shell, which are currently at the limits of conventional approaches. The method is particularly relevant for use with new valence-space interactions derived from ab initio approaches such as the in-medium similarity renormalization group (Tsukiyama 2012, Bogner 2014) or the Lee-Suzuki approach (Jansen 2014, Dikmen 2015), and provides an alternative to the Monte Carlo shell model (Honma 1995, Otsuka 2001) and density matrix renormalization group treatments (Legeza 2015) for large model spaces.
