# Two-current transition amplitudes with two-body final states

> We derive the on-shell form of amplitudes containing two external currents with a single hadron in the initial state and two hadrons in the final state, denoted as $1+\mathcal{J}\to 2+\mathcal{J}$. This class of amplitude is relevant in precision tests of the Standard Model as well as for exploring the structure of excited states in the QCD spectrum. We present a model-independent description of the amplitudes where we sum to all orders in the strong interaction. From this analytic form we are able to extract transition and elastic resonance form factors consistent with previous work as well as a novel Compton-like amplitude coupling a single particle state to a resonance. The results also hold for reactions where the one-particle state is replaced with the vacuum, namely $\mathcal{J}\to 2+\mathcal{J}$ amplitudes. We also investigate constraints placed upon the formalism for the case of a conserved vector current in the form of the Ward-Takahashi identity. The formalism presented here is valid for currents of arbitrary Lorentz structure and quantum numbers with spinless hadrons where any number of two-particle intermediate channels may be open. When combined with the appropriate finite-volume framework, this work facilitates the extraction of physical observables from this class of amplitudes via lattice QCD calculations.

## Key claim

The paper derives an exact, model-independent on-shell representation of two-current transition amplitudes with a single hadron in the initial state and two hadrons in the final state (1+J→2+J), valid for kinematics where only one- and two-particle intermediate states can go on-shell and for spinless hadrons, with the hedge that the results hold only when three-particle intermediate states are kinematically forbidden.

## Method

The authors use all-orders perturbation theory within a generic effective field theory, assuming stable hadronic states against strong decay, to derive integral equations for the amplitudes. They express the Compton-like amplitudes in terms of known building blocks: the purely hadronic 2→2 scattering amplitude, single-current transition amplitudes (1+J→1, 1+J→2, 2+J→2), and smooth real-valued functions that contain no singularities in the kinematic region of interest. The derivation isolates the non-analytic pieces arising from on-shell intermediate states, including simple poles from single-particle propagators, two-particle threshold branch cuts, and logarithmic singularities from triangle diagrams.

## Key numbers

* The two-particle phase space factor ρ is given by ρ = ξ q* / (8π √s), where q* is the relative momentum in the center-of-momentum frame and ξ = 1/2 for identical particles or 1 otherwise.
* The single-particle propagator pole contribution is iD(k²) = i/(k² - m² + iε), where m is the mass of the stable particle.
* The transition form factor for a resonance is given by f_{1→R}(Q²) = c A_{21}(s_R, Q²), where c is the coupling between the resonance and the external two-particle state, and s_R is the resonance pole location.

## Context

This work extends the formalism developed by Briceno et al. (2019, 2020) for simpler amplitudes (1+J→1+J and 2+J→2) to the more complex case of 1+J→2+J, which is relevant for precision Standard Model tests such as the muon anomalous magnetic moment and for studying the internal structure of resonances like the Δ(1232) and scalar glueball candidates. The results enable future lattice QCD calculations of these amplitudes by providing the necessary on-shell parameterizations, and they constrain experimental analyses of reactions such as γ*γ* → ππ and γ*p → γ*pπ.
