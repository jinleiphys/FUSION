# Structures of $p$-shell double-$Λ$ hypernuclei studied with microscopic cluster models

> $0s$-orbit $Λ$ states in $p$-shell double-$Λ$ hypernuclei ($^{\ \,A}_{ΛΛ}Z$), $^{\ \,8}_{ΛΛ}\textrm{Li}$, $^{\ \,9}_{ΛΛ}\textrm{Li}$, $^{10,11,12}_{\ \ \ \ \ ΛΛ}\textrm{Be}$, $^{12,13}_{\ \ ΛΛ}\textrm{B}$, and $^{\,14}_{ΛΛ}\textrm{C}$ are investigated. Microscopic cluster models are applied to core nuclear part and a potential model is adopted for $Λ$ particles. The $Λ$-core potential is a folding potential obtained with effective $G$-matrix $Λ$-$N$ interactions, which reasonably reproduce energy spectra of $^{A-1}_{\,Λ}Z$. System dependence of the $Λ$-$Λ$ binding energies is understood by the core polarization energy from nuclear size reduction. Reductions of nuclear sizes and $E2$ transition strengths by $Λ$ particles are also discussed.

## Key claim

The system dependence of the Lambda-Lambda binding energies in p-shell double-Lambda hypernuclei can be understood by the core polarization energy from nuclear size reduction, but the calculated values depend significantly on the choice of density dependence in the effective Lambda-N interactions (ESC08a with no, mild, or strong density dependence), and the Lambda-Lambda bond energy is not a direct measure of the Lambda-Lambda interaction because it is also contributed by core polarization effects.

## Method

Microscopic cluster models (generator coordinate method with Brink-Bloch cluster wave functions) were applied to the core nuclei (6Li, 7Li, 8Be, 9Be, 10Be, 10B, 11B, 12C), and a folding potential model was adopted for the two S-wave Lambda particles. The Lambda-core potential was calculated by folding effective G-matrix Lambda-N interactions (ESC08a parametrization) with the nuclear density, using three choices for the density dependence (density-dependent, density-independent, and hybrid). Core polarization was simulated by artificially enhancing the central nuclear interactions to induce nuclear size reduction, with the optimum enhancement factor determined by minimizing the total energy for each state.

## Key numbers

- Lambda binding energy B_Lambda in 7_Lambda Li(1+_1): 5.35 MeV (ESC08a(Hyb)), compared to experimental spin-averaged value 5.12(3) MeV
- Lambda binding energy B_Lambda in 9_Lambda Be(0+_1): 6.53 MeV (ESC08a(Hyb)), compared to experimental value 6.71(4) MeV
- Nuclear size reduction delta_Lambda(R_N) in 9_Lambda Be(0+_1): -0.80 fm (ESC08a(Hyb)), -0.94 fm (ESC08a(DI)), -0.68 fm (ESC08a(DD))
- Nuclear size reduction delta_Lambda(R_N) in 7_Lambda Li(1+_1): -0.24 fm (ESC08a(Hyb)), -0.33 fm (ESC08a(DI)), -0.15 fm (ESC08a(DD))
- Excitation energy shift delta_Lambda(E_x) in 7_Lambda Li(3+_1): -0.58 MeV (ESC08a(Hyb)), -1.19 MeV (ESC08a(DI)), -0.21 MeV (ESC08a(DD)), compared to experimental -0.33 MeV
- Lambda-Lambda binding energy Delta_B_LLa(LLHe) obtained with ESC08a(Hyb): 0.58 MeV, using modified Lambda-Lambda interaction parameter a=1.244
- For ESC08a(DD) and ESC08a(DI), the Lambda-Lambda interaction parameter was readjusted to a=1.51 and a=1.07 respectively to give almost the same Delta_B_LLa(LLHe) values

## Context

This work extends the author's previous microscopic cluster model study of single-Lambda hypernuclei (Kanada-En'yo 2017) to double-Lambda hypernuclei, and builds on systematic studies of p-shell double-Lambda hypernuclei by Hiyama et al. (2002, 2010) using the orthogonal condition model and by Gal et al. (2011) using shell model calculations. The paper enables more precise extraction of the Lambda-Lambda interaction from binding energy data by clarifying how core polarization effects (nuclear size reduction) contribute to the system dependence of Lambda-Lambda binding energies, and by testing different density dependencies of the Lambda-N interactions against experimental excitation energy shifts.
