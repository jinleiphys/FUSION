# The medium-modified $g\to c\bar{c}$ splitting function in the BDMPS-Z formalism

> The formalism of Baier-Dokshitzer-Mueller-Peigné-Schiff and Zakharov determines the modifications of parton splittings in the QCD plasma that arise from medium-induced gluon radiation. Here, we study medium-modifications of the gluon splitting into a quark--anti-quark pair in this BDMPS-Z formalism. We derive a compact path-integral formulation that resums effects from an arbitrary number of interactions with the medium to leading order in the $1/N_c^2$ expansion. Analyses in the $N=1$ opacity and the saddle point approximations reveal two phenomena: a medium-induced momentum broadening of the relative quark--anti-quark pair momentum that increases the invariant mass of quark--anti-quark pairs, and a medium-enhanced production of such pairs. We note that both effects are numerically sizeable if the average momentum transfer from the medium is comparable to the quark mass. In ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions, this condition is satisfied for charm quarks. We therefore focus our numerical analysis on the medium modification of $g\to c\bar{c}$, although our derivation applies equally well to $g\to b\bar{b}$ and to gluons splitting into light-flavoured quark--anti-quark pairs.

## Key claim

The medium modification of the $g\to c\bar{c}$ splitting function is parametrically of order $\mathcal{O}(\langle \mathbf{q}^2\rangle_\text{med}/Q^2)$, which is comparable to the mass correction term $2m_c^2/Q^2$ in the vacuum splitting function, and is numerically sizeable only when the average momentum transfer from the medium is comparable to the quark mass, a condition satisfied for charm quarks in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The authors derived a compact path-integral formulation within the BDMPS-Z formalism that resums effects from an arbitrary number of interactions with the QCD medium to leading order in the $1/N_c^2$ expansion. They analyzed the medium-modified $g\to c\bar{c}$ splitting function using both the $N=1$ opacity expansion and the saddle point approximation, and evaluated the resulting expressions for static and time-dependent expanding media. The derivation uses time-ordered perturbation theory in the close-to-eikonal limit, where transverse momenta exchanged with the medium are small compared to longitudinal momenta.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass: $m_c = 1.27$ GeV (used throughout the numerical analysis)
- The medium correction scales as $P^{\text{med}}_{g\to q\bar{q}} \sim \mathcal{O}(\langle \mathbf{q}^2\rangle_\text{med}/Q^2)$, where $\langle \mathbf{q}^2\rangle_\text{med} = \int_{\tau_i}^{\tau_f} d\tau\, \hat{q}(\tau) \sim \mathcal{O}(m_c^2)$ for charm quarks
- The vacuum splitting function is $P^\text{vac}_{g \to c\, \bar{c}}(z, Q^2) = \frac{1}{2} \left(z^2 + (1-z)^2 + 2\frac{m_c^2}{Q^2}\right)$
- The longitudinal phase factor in the vacuum limit is $\Gamma_0 = \frac{m_c^2+\bm{\upkappa}^2}{2 \mu} = \frac{Q^2}{2 E_g}$, with $\mu \equiv E_g\, z\, (1-z)$

## Context

This work extends the BDMPS-Z formalism (Baier, Dokshitzer, Mueller, Peigné, Schiff 1996; Zakharov 1996), which previously focused on medium-induced gluon radiation for $q\to qg$ and $g\to gg$ splitting functions, to the $g\to q\bar{q}$ channel. It builds on the transverse momentum integrated result of Caron-Huot (2010) and the first-order opacity calculations of Kang (2016) and Sievert (2019) for heavy-flavored meson suppression. The derivation applies equally to $g\to b\bar{b}$ and light-flavored quark–anti-quark pairs, and the authors note that the medium modification is comparable to the mass term in vacuum parton showers, motivating its inclusion on the same footing in phenomenological studies of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC.
