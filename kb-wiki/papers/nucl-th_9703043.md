# Covariant equations for the three-body bound state

> The covariant spectator (or Gross) equations for the bound state of three identical spin 1/2 particles, in which two of the three interacting particles are always on shell, are developed and reduced to a form suitable for numerical solution. The equations are first written in operator form and compared to the Bethe-Salpeter equation, then expanded into plane wave momentum states, and finally expanded into partial waves using the three-body helicity formalism first introduced by Wick. In order to solve the equations, the two-body scattering amplitudes must be boosted from the overall three-body rest frame to their individual two-body rest frames, and all effects which arise from these boosts, including the Wigner rotations and rho-spin decomposition of the off-shell particle, are treated exactly. In their final form, the equations reduce to a coupled set of Faddeev-like double integral equations with additional channels arising from the negative rho-spin states of the off-shell particle.

## Key claim

The covariant spectator (Gross) equations for three identical spin-1/2 particles, in which two of the three interacting particles are always on shell, are developed and reduced to a form suitable for numerical solution, but the development is carried out only for the case where the three-body scattering amplitude can be obtained by iterating successive two-body interactions, neglecting the three-body forces of relativistic origin discussed in the original 1982 paper.

## Method

The authors start from the Bethe-Salpeter equations for three-body bound states and replace the two-body propagator with a new propagator that places one particle on the positive energy mass-shell via a projection operator, yielding the spectator Faddeev equations. They expand the operator equations into plane wave momentum states and then into partial waves using the three-body helicity formalism introduced by Wick, treating the two-body scattering amplitudes by boosting them from their individual two-body rest frames to the overall three-body rest frame with exact treatment of Wigner rotations and rho-spin decomposition of the off-shell particle. The final equations reduce to a coupled set of Faddeev-like double integral equations with additional channels arising from the negative rho-spin states of the off-shell particle.

## Key numbers

- The critical spectator momentum is approximately 4m/3, or about 1200 MeV, where the two-body subsystem recoils at the speed of light and the relativistic effects become enormous.
- The integration over spectator momentum q' is limited to the finite interval [0, q_crit], where q_crit is the root of the equation W_q = 0.
- The propagator for the off-shell particle in the positive rho-spin state is g^+(q, p_tilde) = 1/(2E_p_tilde - W_q), and in the negative rho-spin state is g^-(q, p_tilde) = -1/W_q.
- The total mass of the three-body bound state is denoted by M_t, and the three particles have mass m.
- Using a mean momentum of about 200 MeV, relativistic corrections are expected to be of order (v/c)^2 approximately equal to (p/m)^2, which is about 4 percent; if this is 4 percent of the binding energy, it amounts to about 300 keV.

## Context

This work extends the three-body spectator equations first introduced by Stadler, Gross, and Frank in 1982 for scalar particles, now developing the formalism for three identical spin-1/2 particles with all details needed for numerical solution. The authors compare their approach to the Bethe-Salpeter formalism, noting that the spectator equations reduce the number of independent variables to six (versus eight for Bethe-Salpeter), yielding coupled two-dimensional equations after partial wave decomposition rather than four-dimensional ones. The paper enables practical numerical calculations of relativistic three-body bound states, and the authors note that these equations have already been solved numerically for a variety of cases with some results already published.
