# Three-dimensional rotation of even-even triaxial nuclei

> With the self-consistent three-dimensional cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (3d-cranked HFB) method, various types of rotational motion near the yrast line are investigated in an even-even nucleus in the $A\simeq 130$ mass region ($^{134}_{58}$Ce$_{76}$). The possibilities of chiral rotations, tilted-rotations, and dynamical aspects of these rotations are discussed through the analysis of the 3d-cranked HFB solutions. Although a stable planar solution of the chiral rotation is obtained, an aplanar chiral configuration is found to be unstable when triaxial deformation is treated self-consistently.

## Key claim

Using a fully self-consistent three-dimensional cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (3d-cranked HFB) method, the authors find that while a stable planar chiral configuration (the "bandhead") exists in the even-even nucleus ¹³⁴Ce, the aplanar chiral partner configuration that would constitute genuine three-dimensional chiral rotation is unstable when triaxial deformation is treated self-consistently, contrary to expectations from simpler particle-rotor models.

## Method

The 3d-cranked HFB equation was solved self-consistently for ¹³⁴Ce using a pairing-plus-Q·Q force under constraints on particle numbers and angular momentum (both magnitude and orientation). The steepest descent method was used to solve the HFB equation consecutively from J=0 up to high spin, first through principal-axis cranking, then through tilted-axis cranking with polar angle θ from 0° to 90° (step 0.5°) and azimuthal angle φ from 0° to 90° (step 1°), producing energy surfaces E^J(θ,φ) and associated deformation and pairing parameters.

## Key numbers

- At J=26ħ, the energy surface minimum occurs at (θ,φ) ≈ (90°,45°) with deformation (β,γ) = (0.16, 17°); proton h₁₁/₂ single-particle spin components are (0.0, 9.3, 2.0) ħ and neutron h₁₁/₂ components are (0.2, 2.5, 9.6) ħ, with the 1-, 2-, 3-axes corresponding to i-, s-, l-axes respectively.
- At J=28ħ, the aplanar chiral candidate at (θ,φ) ≈ (70°,45°) has deformation (β,γ) = (0.13, 32°) and h₁₁/₂ alignments of (1.9, 9.3, 2.4) ħ for protons and (3.7, 4.3, 11.5) ħ for neutrons, but lies near a saddle point.
- At J=30ħ, the self-consistent calculation yields deformation (β,γ) = (0.132, 25°) and pairing gaps (Δ_p, Δ_n) = (0.00, 0.18) MeV; six shallow minima appear including group C (principal-axis rotation) with γ ≈ 25°, 30°, and -31° for C₁, C₂, C₃ respectively.
- The minimum A at J=30ħ has (β,γ) = (0.12, 60°) (oblate shape) with excitation energy E_ex ≈ 300 keV relative to the B minima; the B minima are the lowest (yrast candidates) and C minima are second lowest at E_ex ≈ 10 keV.

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear chirality, previously predicted for odd-odd nuclei by Frauendorf and Meng (1997) and Dimitrov, Frauendorf, and Dönau (2000), into even-even systems where four quasiparticles must be excited. It uses a fully microscopic self-consistent approach, unlike the particle-rotor model or the Tilted-Axis Cranking (TAC) model with fixed shape parameters used by Dimitrov et al. (2000). The results contradict the expectation from simpler models that aplanar chiral minima would be stable in even-even nuclei, and suggest that dynamical effects such as nutation and zero-mode restoration of symmetries (previously discussed by Bonche et al., 1991) are important for understanding high-spin rotation in this mass region.
