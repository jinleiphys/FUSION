# Pairing in Nuclear Matter and Finite Nuclei

> Effects of pairing with isospin $T=0$ and $T=1$ are systematically studied in a model, which is based on a realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction and allows to describe the transition from infinite nuclear matter to finite nuclei. Special attention is paid to the development of the spin-orbit term in the mean field of nucleons in finite nuclei. The spin-orbit term yields a drastic suppression of $T=0$ proton-neutron pairing but does not lead to a complete disappearance in finite nuclei. Arguments are presented, why no clear evidence of $T=0$ pairing can be observed in the binding energies of finite nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper systematically studies isospin T=0 (proton-neutron) and T=1 (like-nucleon) pairing in a unified model that transitions from infinite nuclear matter to finite nuclei, finding that the spin-orbit term in finite nuclei drastically suppresses T=0 pairing but does not completely eliminate it, and arguing that this suppression explains why no clear evidence of T=0 pairing appears in binding energies of finite nuclei.

## Method

The authors use a spherical box basis of plane waves with boundary conditions to describe both infinite nuclear matter and finite nuclei within the same computational framework. They employ a low-momentum interaction Vlowk derived from the CDBonn potential via the unitary-model-operator approach with a cutoff of 2 fm^-1, supplemented by a contact interaction to reproduce empirical saturation. Pairing gaps are extracted from the eigenvalues of the particle-particle hole-hole RPA Hamiltonian, using the imaginary part of complex eigenvalues or the minimal gap parameter needed to shift the lowest eigenvalue to zero energy.

## Key numbers

- Pairing gap for T=0 (proton-neutron) pairing in infinite nuclear matter is of order 10 MeV, while T=1 (like-nucleon) pairing gaps are considerably smaller (approximately 1-2 MeV)
- For quasi-40Ca nuclei with a Woods-Saxon radius of r0=3.5 fm, spin-orbit splittings are 6.20 MeV for l=1, 9.85 MeV for l=2, and 10.56 MeV for l=3 when all partial waves are included
- When interaction in 3P0, 3P1, and 3P2-3F2 channels is ignored, spin-orbit energies drop to 0.61 MeV (l=1), 0.87 MeV (l=2), and 0.70 MeV (l=3) for the same system
- The cutoff momentum for stable T=0 pairing results requires values larger than 2 fm^-1, while a lower cutoff suffices for T=1 pairing

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of pairing in nuclear matter by Baldo (1990), Elgaroy (1990), and Kuckei, which predicted large T=0 pairing gaps of about 10 MeV in infinite matter, and addresses the puzzle raised by Langanke (1996) and the VAMPIR group that no clear T=0 pairing evidence appears in finite nuclei. The study builds on the argument by Poves (1998) and Bertsch (2010, 2011) that the spin-orbit field spoils isoscalar pairing, but uses a realistic NN interaction rather than a phenomenological contact interaction as in Bertsch and Zuo (2010). The results enable future self-consistent Hartree-Fock calculations of pairing in finite nuclei using the same box basis and realistic interactions.
