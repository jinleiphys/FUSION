# Explanation of the excesses in associated di-photon production at 152 GeV in 2HDM

> Statistically significant excesses exist at around 152 GeV in associated di-photon production ($γγ+X$) in the sidebands of SM Higgs analyses of ATLAS (using the full run-2 dataset). They are most pronounced in the single-$τ$, missing-transverse-energy, four-jet and $\geqslant1\ell+\!\geqslant1b$-jet channels ($\approx3σ$) and can be explained by the Drell-Yan production of new Higgs bosons, i.e. $pp\to W^*\to H^\pm H^0$. We first examine the excesses in a simplified model approach, considering that $H^\pm$ decays to $τν$, $WZ$ or $tb$. Both the $τν$ and $tb$ decay modes individually lead to a significance of $\lessapprox4σ$ while for $WZ$ one can obtain at most $3.5σ$. This is because the decays of $WZ$ lead to multiple leptons contributing to the two-lepton channel which does not show an excess at 152 GeV. Next, we consider two-Higgs-doublet models where the charged Higgs does not decay to $WZ$ at tree-level, finding a significance of $\gtrapprox4σ$ for a branching ratio of the new neutral Higgs to photons of $\approx$2%. Even though this branching fraction is quite sizable, it can be obtained in composite models or via the Lagrangian term $λ_6 H_1^\dagger H_1 H_2^\dagger H_1+{\rm h.c.}$ breaking the commonly imposed $Z_2$ symmetry.

# Explanation of the excesses in associated di-photon production at 152 GeV in 2HDM

## Key claim

The statistically significant excesses at around 152 GeV in associated di-photon production observed by ATLAS can be explained by Drell-Yan production of new Higgs bosons via $pp\to W^*\to H^\pm H^0$, but only if the charged Higgs does not decay to $WZ$ at tree-level; in two-Higgs-doublet models where $H^\pm$ decays only to $\tau\nu$ and $tb$, a combined significance of $\gtrapprox 4\sigma$ is achieved, provided the branching ratio of the new neutral Higgs to photons is approximately 2%.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo simulations of $pp\to W^*\to H^\pm H$ using MadGraph5aMC@NLO with parton showering in Pythia8.3 and detector simulation in Delphes, normalizing the production cross section to 92 fb for a doublet Higgs with $m_{H^\pm}=150$ GeV. They fitted the ATLAS di-photon invariant mass distributions across eight signal regions (single-lepton, single-$\tau$, two-lepton, $\ell b$, leptonic top, four-jet, and two missing-transverse-energy categories) using a simultaneous log-likelihood ratio test with Poisson statistics, subtracting the SM Higgs signal at 125 GeV and modeling the continuum background with a three-parameter function. For the 2HDM analysis, they considered the effect of $pp\to Z^*\to HA$ with $A\to WH^\pm$ and included the Lagrangian term $\lambda_6 H_1^\dagger H_1 H_2^\dagger H_1$ to enhance the $H\to\gamma\gamma$ branching ratio.

## Key numbers

- The excesses in the single-$\tau$, missing-transverse-energy, four-jet, and $\geq 1\ell + \geq 1b$-jet channels each have a significance of approximately $3\sigma$.
- For $H^\pm\to\tau\nu$ alone, the combined significance is approximately $3.8\sigma$ at BR($H\to\gamma\gamma$) $\approx 1\%$.
- For $H^\pm\to tb$ alone, the combined significance is approximately $3.8\sigma$ at BR($H\to\gamma\gamma$) $\approx 3\%$.
- For $H^\pm\to WZ$, the maximum combined significance is $3.5\sigma$ at BR($H\to\gamma\gamma$) $\approx 2.5\%$.
- In the 2HDM with type-I-like branching ratios and $m_{H^\pm}=150$ GeV, a combined significance of $4.3\sigma$ is obtained.
- Including $pp\to Z^*\to HA$ with $m_A=200$ GeV and the $\lambda_6$ term yields a $\Delta\chi^2$ minimum of approximately $-23$, corresponding to $4.4\sigma$ for the two-dimensional hypothesis.
- The Drell-Yan production cross section for an $SU(2)_L$ doublet with $m_{H^\pm}=150$ GeV is normalized to 92 fb.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Crivellin (2024) which studied the same excesses using an $SU(2)_L$ triplet with $Y=0$, finding a combined significance of approximately $4\sigma$ but with a $\chi^2$ of 3 per degree of freedom, indicating imperfect agreement across signal regions. The present paper improves upon that by considering two-Higgs-doublet models where the charged Higgs does not decay to $WZ$, achieving better consistency across channels. The results are compatible with the multi-lepton anomalies studied by von Buddenbrock (2016, 2017) and Fischer (2021), and could be related to the 95 GeV excess discussed by Coloretti (2023). The required large branching ratio to photons motivates composite Higgs models as explored by Cacciapaglia (2021) and Benbrik (2019), or the introduction of the $\lambda_6$ term breaking the $Z_2$ symmetry.
