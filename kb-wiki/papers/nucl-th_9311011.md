# Deformed Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble Analysis of the Interacting Boson Model

> A Deformed Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (DGOE) which interpolates between the Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble and a Poissonian Ensemble is constructed. This new ensemble is then applied to the analysis of the chaotic properties of the low lying collective states of nuclei described by the Interacting Boson Model (IBM). This model undergoes a transition order-chaos-order from the $SU(3)$ limit to the $O(6)$ limit. Our analysis shows that the quantum fluctuations of the IBM Hamiltonian, both of the spectrum and the eigenvectors, follow the expected behaviour predicted by the DGOE when one goes from one limit to the other.

## Key claim
The paper constructs a Deformed Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (DGOE) that interpolates between the Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE) and a Poissonian ensemble, and shows that the quantum fluctuations of the Interacting Boson Model (IBM) Hamiltonian follow the DGOE predictions when going from the SU(3) limit to the O(6) limit, but only after removing secular variations from the eigenvector components.

## Method
The authors constructed a DGOE by maximizing entropy subject to constraints on the trace of the full Hamiltonian and the trace of its off-diagonal part, introducing a parameter β that controls the transition from GOE (β→0) to Poisson (β→∞). They then diagonalized IBM Hamiltonians for varying values of the parameter χ (from -1.25 to -0.1) and compared the level spacing distributions, spectral rigidity Δ₃, and eigenvector component distributions to those of the DGOE. To make a sensible comparison of eigenvector statistics, they removed secular variations by applying a Gaussian moving window to compute local averages of the squared components.

## Key numbers
- The DGOE parameter ε = 1/√(1+β/α) ranges from 0 (Poisson limit, β→∞) to 1 (GOE limit, β=0).
- The scaled parameter ε̃ = ε√(N-1) was used; for N=200 matrices, the GOE limit is approached when ε̃=1.
- The IBM Hamiltonian was studied for χ values in the range -√7/2 ≤ χ ≤ 0, specifically from -1.25 to -0.1, showing a Poisson-GOE-Poisson transition.
- The DGOE calculations used matrices of size N=200 for the statistical comparisons shown in Fig.4.

## Context
This work extends the DGOE construction of Pato (1993) from the case of SU(2) symmetry breaking to the general GOE-Poisson transition, and applies it to the Interacting Boson Model previously studied by Alhassid (1990) and Levine (1990). The analysis reproduces the level spacing and spectral rigidity results of Alhassid (1990) for the IBM, but provides a more rigorous comparison using eigenvector component statistics after removing secular variations. The approach enables a systematic test of whether realistic nuclear models follow random matrix theory predictions in intermediate regimes between chaos and regularity.
