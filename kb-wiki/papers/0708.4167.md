# Understanding the newly observed Y(4008) by Belle

> Very recently a new enhancement around 4.05 GeV was observed by Belle experiment. In this short note, we discuss some possible assignments for this enhancement, i.e. $Ōą(3S)$ and $D^*\bar{D}^*$ molecular state. In these two assignments, Y(4008) can decay into $J/ŌąŌÄ^0ŌÄ^0$ with comparable branching ratio with that of $Y(4008)\to J/ŌąŌÄ^+ŌÄ^-$. # Understanding the newly observed Y(4008) by Belle

## Key claim
The newly observed enhancement Y(4008) can be interpreted either as the Ōą(3S) charmonium state or as a D*DŐÖ* molecular state, but the available data do not yet distinguish between these two assignments; the paper proposes specific decay channels that would discriminate between them.

## Method
The author compares two possible assignments for Y(4008), the Ōą(3S) charmonium state and a D*DŐÖ* molecular state, by examining their predicted decay patterns. For the Ōą(3S) assignment, the author uses QCD multipole expansion calculations from Ke et al. to estimate transition rates to lower charmonia. For the molecular state assignment, the author considers both isoscalar (I^G=0^-) and isovector (I^G=1^+) possibilities and analyzes decay mechanisms via D*DŐÖ* recattering effects, including meson exchange diagrams.
