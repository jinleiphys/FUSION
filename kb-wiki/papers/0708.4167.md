# Understanding the newly observed Y(4008) by Belle

> Very recently a new enhancement around 4.05 GeV was observed by Belle experiment. In this short note, we discuss some possible assignments for this enhancement, i.e. $ψ(3S)$ and $D^*\bar{D}^*$ molecular state. In these two assignments, Y(4008) can decay into $J/ψπ^0π^0$ with comparable branching ratio with that of $Y(4008)\to J/ψπ^+π^-$. Thus one suggests high energy experimentalists to look for Y(4008) in $J/ψπ^0π^0$ channel. Furthermore one proposes further experiments to search missing channel $D\bar{D}$, $D\bar{D}^*+h.c.$ and especially $χ_{cJ}π^+π^-π^0$ and $η_cπ^+π^-π^0$, which will be helpful to distinguish $ψ(3S)$ and $D^*\bar{D}^*$ molecular state assignments for this new enhancement.

# Understanding the newly observed Y(4008) by Belle

## Key claim
The newly observed enhancement Y(4008) can be interpreted either as the ψ(3S) charmonium state or as a D*D̅* molecular state, but the available data do not yet distinguish between these two assignments; the paper proposes specific decay channels that would discriminate between them.

## Method
The author compares two possible assignments for Y(4008), the ψ(3S) charmonium state and a D*D̅* molecular state, by examining their predicted decay patterns. For the ψ(3S) assignment, the author uses QCD multipole expansion calculations from Ke et al. to estimate transition rates to lower charmonia. For the molecular state assignment, the author considers both isoscalar (I^G=0^-) and isovector (I^G=1^+) possibilities and analyzes decay mechanisms via D*D̅* recattering effects, including meson exchange diagrams.

## Key numbers
- Mass of Y(4008): m = 4008 ± 40 +114/−28 MeV; width: Γ = 226 ± 44 ± 87 MeV (Belle)
- Product branching ratio times leptonic width: B(J/ψπ⁺π⁻)·Γ_{e⁺e⁻} = 5.0 ± 1.4 +6.1/−0.9 eV and 12.4 ± 2.4 +14.8/−1.1 eV (two solutions from Belle fit)
- ψ(4040) leptonic width: Γ_{e⁺e⁻} = 0.86 ± 0.07 keV (PDG)
- Estimated B[Y(4008)→J/ψπ⁺π⁻] if Y(4008) is ψ(3S): 5.8×10⁻³ and 1.4×10⁻² for the two Belle solutions
- Upper limit B[ψ(4040)→J/ψπ⁺π⁻] < 4×10⁻³ (PDG)
- Calculated Γ[ψ(3S)→J/ψππ] = 589.91 keV (Cornell potential) and 12.38 keV (modified Cornell potential) from Ke et al.
- Calculated Γ[ψ(3S)→ψ(2S)ππ] = 14.96 keV (Cornell potential) and 8.84 keV (modified Cornell potential) from Ke et al.
- Predicted ratio B[Y(4008)→J/ψπ⁰π⁰] / B[Y(4008)→J/ψπ⁺π⁻] ∼ 1/2 for the isoscalar D*D̅* molecular state assignment

## Context
This work responds to the Belle Collaboration's observation of a new enhancement near 4.05 GeV in the ISR process e⁺e⁻ → γ_{ISR} J/ψπ⁺π⁻, reported alongside confirmation of the Y(4260) state. The paper extends earlier discussions of charmonium-like states including X(3872), Y(4260), and other XYZ states discovered by Belle, BaBar, CDF, and CLEO. It builds on the QCD multipole expansion method of Gottfried, Yan, and Kuang for charmonium transitions, and references the molecular state proposals of Okun and Voloshin, as well as Rujula, Georgi, and Glashow who suggested ψ(4040) as a D*D̅* molecule. The work also cites Dubynskiy and Voloshin's prediction of a resonance at the D*D̅* threshold. The paper enables experimental searches in J/ψπ⁰π⁰, ψ(2S)ππ, D D̅, D D̅*+h.c., and especially χ_{cJ}π⁺π⁻π⁰ and η_cπ⁺π⁻π⁰ channels to distinguish between the two assignments.
