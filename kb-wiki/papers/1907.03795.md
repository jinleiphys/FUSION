# Damping of density oscillations in neutrino-transparent nuclear matter

> We calculate the bulk-viscous dissipation time for adiabatic density oscillations in nuclear matter at densities of 1-7 times nuclear saturation density and at temperatures ranging from 1 MeV, where corrections to previous low-temperature calculations become important, up to 10 MeV, where the assumption of neutrino transparency is no longer valid. Under these conditions, which are expected to occur in neutron star mergers, damping of density oscillations arises from beta equilibration via weak interactions. We find that for 1 kHz oscillations the shortest dissipation times are in the 5 to 20 ms range, depending on the equation of state, which means that bulk viscous damping could affect the dynamics of a neutron star merger. For higher frequencies the dissipation time can be even shorter.

## Key claim

The paper calculates that bulk viscous damping of 1 kHz density oscillations in neutrino-transparent nuclear matter can occur on timescales of 5 to 20 ms, which could affect neutron star merger dynamics, but only under the specific conditions of densities 1–7 times nuclear saturation density and temperatures between 1 and 10 MeV where the neutrino-transparency assumption holds.

## Method

The authors computed the bulk viscosity of nuclear matter by integrating the full phase space for direct Urca processes (neutron decay and electron capture) without the Fermi Surface approximation, using two relativistic mean-field equations of state (DD2 and IUF). They then derived the dissipation time for adiabatic density oscillations from the bulk viscosity and the adiabatic compressibility, evaluating the temperature and density dependence across the neutrino-transparent regime.

## Key numbers

- Minimum dissipation time for 1 kHz oscillations: 5 ms at low density (0.5 times nuclear saturation density) for the IUF equation of state
- Minimum dissipation time for 1 kHz oscillations: 6 ms at low density (0.5 times nuclear saturation density) for the DD2 equation of state
- At densities above nuclear saturation density, dissipation times can be as low as 20 ms for 1 kHz oscillations
- For the IUF equation of state, a local minimum dissipation time of 19 ms occurs around 2 times nuclear saturation density
- The bulk viscosity peak occurs at temperatures of about 3 MeV for the DD2 equation of state across all densities studied
- The direct Urca threshold for the IUF equation of state is near 4 times nuclear saturation density
- The maximum mass neutron star for DD2 is 2.42 solar masses; for IUF it is 1.96 solar masses

## Context

This work extends previous low-temperature calculations by Alford et al. (2017) that suggested bulk viscous dissipation times could be a few milliseconds, by including corrections at temperatures above 1 MeV where the Fermi Surface approximation breaks down. The results enable more accurate modeling of neutron star merger dynamics by providing dissipation timescales that can be compared with the 10–20 ms timescale of the merger process itself, and the authors note that the dissipation time depends strongly on the equation of state, with DD2 showing dissipation only below twice saturation density while IUF shows dissipation up to four times saturation density.
