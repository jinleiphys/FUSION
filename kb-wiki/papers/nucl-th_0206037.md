# Pionic Atom Formation in Halo Nuclei by (d,3He) Reactions

> We investigate the formation cross sections of the deeply bound pionic atoms on halo nuclei theoretically. We consider one-neutron pick-up (d,3He) reaction for the pionic atom formation and find that the formation rate is significantly increased by the existence of the halo neutron with long tail of the wave function. This enhancement is expected to increase the experimental feasibility for the formation of the deeply bound pionic states on beta-unstable nuclei.

# Pionic Atom Formation in Halo Nuclei by (d,3He) Reactions

## Key claim

The formation cross section of deeply bound pionic atoms via the (d,3He) reaction is significantly enhanced when the target nucleus has a neutron halo structure, but this enhancement depends on the specific single-particle state of the valence neutron and is demonstrated only for a model parameter F that simulates the halo by reducing the neutron potential depth.

## Method

The authors solve the one-body Schrodinger equation for all occupied single-particle states of protons and neutrons using a Woods-Saxon potential with parameters fitted to the Pb region (Speth 1977). They introduce a single free parameter F that reduces the neutron potential depth to simulate halo-like neutron wave functions with long tails. Pionic atom wave functions and eigenenergies are obtained by solving the Klein-Gordon equation with the Ericson-Ericson pion-nucleus optical potential (Seki and Masutani 1983). The (d,3He) reaction cross sections are calculated using the effective number approach in the Eikonal approximation, with all nuclear densities and pion wave functions depending consistently on the parameter F.

## Key numbers

- For Pb at F=0.765 (halo case), the formation cross section of the [(2p3/2)n-1 ⊗ (2p)π] configuration is larger by a factor of approximately 3.5 compared to F=1.0 (no-halo case).
- For Xe at F=0.775 (halo case), the formation cross section of the [(3s1/2)n-1 ⊗ (1s)π] configuration is larger by a factor of approximately 2.9 compared to F=1.0 (no-halo case).
- The binding energy of the 1s pionic state in Pb is 6.93 MeV for F=1.0 and 6.67 MeV for F=0.765.
- The root mean square radius of the 3p1/2 neutron state in Pb increases from 5.9145 fm at F=1.0 to 7.9384 fm at F=0.765.
- The root mean square radius of the 3s1/2 neutron state in Xe increases from 5.1079 fm at F=1.0 to 6.2414 fm at F=0.775.
- The 1i13/2 neutron state in Pb becomes unbound for F values smaller than 0.765; the 1h11/2 neutron state in Xe becomes unbound for F values smaller than 0.775.
- All calculations are performed at an incident deuteron energy Td = 500 MeV with an assumed instrumental energy resolution of 50 keV.

## Context

This work extends the recoilless (d,3He) method for producing deeply bound pionic atoms, originally proposed by Hirenzaki (1991) and successfully applied to stable nuclei by Yamazaki (1996) and Gillitzer (2000), to the case of beta-unstable halo nuclei. The authors build on their previous work (Umemoto 2001) which did not consider halo-like neutron distributions, and they are motivated by theoretical predictions of neutron halo structures in medium-heavy nuclei such as Zr isotopes (Meng 1998). The results suggest that the enhanced formation cross sections could make experiments feasible at next-generation radioactive ion beam facilities such as the RIKEN RIB factory, enabling spectroscopic studies of deeply bound pionic states across a wide region of the nuclear chart including neutron-rich unstable nuclei.
