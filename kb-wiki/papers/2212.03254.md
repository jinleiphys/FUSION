# Life at the Landau pole

> If a quantum field theory has a Landau pole, the theory is usually called 'sick' and dismissed as a candidate for an interacting UV-complete theory. In a recent study on the interacting 4d O(N) model at large N, it was shown that at the Landau pole, observables remain well-defined and finite. In this work, I study both relevant and irrelevant deformations of the said model at the Landau pole, finding that physical observables remain unaffected. Apparently, the Landau pole in this theory is benign. As a phenomenological application, I compare the O(N) model to QCD, by identifying $Λ_{\overline{\rm MS}}$ with the Landau pole in the O(N) model.

## Key claim

Physical observables in the 4d O(N) model at large N remain finite and well-defined at the Landau pole, and this property persists when adding both relevant and irrelevant deformations, suggesting the Landau pole is "benign" rather than indicating the theory is sick.

## Method

The author studies the O(N) model in 3+1 dimensions at large N using auxiliary field methods and dimensional regularization, analytically continuing the theory beyond the Landau pole via PT-symmetric field theory techniques. The pressure and scattering amplitudes are computed non-perturbatively in the large N limit, and the analysis is extended to include relevant deformations (mass terms) and irrelevant deformations (sextic interactions).

## Key numbers

- The Landau pole scale in QED is estimated as Λ_LP ≈ 10^280 MeV (from the running coupling formula α(μ̄) = 1/((2/(3π)) ln(Λ_LP/μ̄)))
- The zero-temperature pressure per component at the thermodynamically preferred vacuum is p(m = Λ_LP√e, T = 0) = Λ_LP^4 e^2 / (128π^2)
- The critical temperature where the saddle point solution for m becomes complex is T_c ≈ Λ_LP/√e ≈ 0.616 Λ_LP
- The effective coupling in the infrared limit approaches lim_{μ̄→0} g_R,eff(μ̄) = 4π^2
- The PT-symmetric O(N) model running coupling is g_R(μ̄) = 4π^2 / ln(μ̄^2/Λ_LP^2)

## Context

This work extends the author's previous study (Romatschke 2022) showing observables remain finite at the Landau pole in the O(N) model, and builds on PT-symmetric field theory results from Ai (2022). The paper contrasts with the conventional view that Landau poles indicate UV-incompleteness, as seen in QED studies by Abrikosov (1954) and Landau, and suggests the O(N) model may be analogous to QCD by identifying Λ_MS̄ with the Landau pole. The results enable a reinterpretation of theories with Landau poles as potentially UV-complete rather than requiring a cutoff.
