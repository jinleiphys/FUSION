# Chiral effective field theory and nuclear forces

> We review how nuclear forces emerge from low-energy QCD via chiral effective field theory. The presentation is accessible to the non-specialist. At the same time, we also provide considerable detailed information (mostly in appendices) for the benefit of researchers who wish to start working in this field.

## Key claim

This review paper argues that nuclear forces can be systematically derived from low-energy quantum chromodynamics (QCD) using chiral effective field theory, where the expansion in powers of small momenta over the chiral symmetry breaking scale (approximately 1 GeV) provides a controlled perturbative framework, with the crucial caveat that nuclear interactions require a non-perturbative treatment because they do not vanish in the chiral limit and produce bound states.

## Method

The authors construct the most general Lagrangian consistent with the (broken) chiral symmetry of QCD, using pions (Goldstone bosons) and nucleons as the relevant degrees of freedom while integrating out heavier mesons and resonances. They organize the Lagrangian in a power expansion in derivatives and pion masses (chiral perturbation theory), then calculate the nucleon-nucleon potential perturbatively in this chiral expansion and iterate it to all orders in a Schroedinger or Lippmann-Schwinger equation to obtain the nuclear amplitude, with four-nucleon contact terms introduced for renormalization.

## Key numbers

* Pion decay constant: \(f_\pi\) (value not specified in text) used in constructing the chiral Lagrangian
* Chiral symmetry breaking scale: \(\Lambda_\chi \sim 1\) GeV, which sets the hard scale for the expansion
* Up quark mass: \(m_u = 2.5 \pm 0.8\) MeV
* Down quark mass: \(m_d = 5 \pm 0.9\) MeV
* Strange quark mass: \(m_s = 101 \pm 25\) MeV
* Rho meson mass: \(m_\rho = 0.78\) GeV (approximately 1 GeV), used as the hard scale \(\Lambda_\chi\)
* Pion mass: \(m_\pi\) (value not specified in text) sets the soft scale \(Q \sim m_\pi\)
* Smallest pion mass used in lattice QCD study cited: 354 MeV (Bea06), still too large for reliable extrapolations to the physical pion mass

## Context

This review extends the foundational work by Weinberg (1979, 1990-1992) who proposed applying effective field theory to nuclear forces, and builds upon the chiral perturbation theory developments for \(\pi\pi\) scattering by Gasser and Leutwyler (1984) and for \(\pi N\) scattering by Gasser, Sainio, and Svarc (1988). The paper summarizes the progress from early implementations by Ordonez, Ray, and van Kolck (1994, 1996) at next-to-next-to-leading order through the Idaho group's construction of chiral potentials at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (Entem and Machleidt, 2002-2003), which achieved the precision necessary for reliable few-nucleon and nuclear structure calculations. The work enables systematic calculations of nuclear reactions and structure using chiral two- and three-nucleon forces, which have been applied to light- and medium-mass nuclei, nuclear and neutron matter, and few-nucleon reactions.
