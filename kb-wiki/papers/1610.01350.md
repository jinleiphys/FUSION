# Chiral potential renormalized in harmonic-oscillator space

> We renormalize the chiral effective field theory (EFT) potential in harmonic-oscillator (HO) model space. The low energy constants (LECs) are utilized to absorb not just the ultra-violet part of the physics due to the cutoff, but also the infrared part due to the truncation of model space. We use the inverse J-matrix method to reproduce the nucleon-nucleon (NN) scattering phase shifts in the given model space. We demonstrate that by including the NLO correction, the nucleon-nucleon scattering in the continuum could be well reproduced in the truncated HO trap space up to laboratory energy $T_{lab}=100$ MeV with number of HO basis $n_{max}$ as small as 10. A perturbative power counting starts at subleading order is adopted in this work, and how to extract the perturbative contribution is demonstrated. Our work serves as the input to perform ab-initio calculations.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a chiral effective field theory (EFT) nucleon-nucleon potential can be renormalized directly in a truncated harmonic-oscillator (HO) model space, using the low-energy constants (LECs) to absorb both ultraviolet and infrared cutoff effects. The approach works up to laboratory energy T_lab = 100 MeV with as few as n_max = 10 HO basis states, but only when the subleading (NLO) correction is included and the ultraviolet cutoff of the model space exceeds the intrinsic cutoff of the potential by a factor R ~ 2 or more.

## Method
The author uses the inverse J-matrix method to connect the eigenvalues of the Hamiltonian in a truncated HO basis to nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts. The chiral EFT potential is constructed in momentum space with a regulator R(k,p,Λ) = exp[-(p⁴+k⁴)/Λ⁴], and its matrix elements in the HO basis are computed numerically. The LECs in the contact terms are adjusted to fit the Nijmegen phase shifts (or scattering lengths for S-waves) at low energies, and the subleading chiral potentials are treated perturbatively using a power counting that starts at subleading order, with the perturbative contributions extracted by varying a small parameter σ and diagonalizing the Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers
- Laboratory energy T_lab up to 100 MeV is reproduced with n_max as small as 10, using ω = 120 MeV and intrinsic cutoffs Λ_c = 800 MeV, Λ = 600 MeV (or Λ_c = 1000 MeV, Λ = 800 MeV).
- For the ¹S₀ channel at LO, n_max = 10 with ω = 20 MeV and Λ = 600 MeV, Λ_c = 800 MeV shows oscillatory phase shifts; increasing ω to 120 MeV eliminates the oscillations.
- The ultraviolet cutoff of the model space is Λ ~ √[M(N_max + 3/2)ħω]; for n_max = 8 and ω = 120 MeV, this gives Λ ~ 1385 MeV, exceeding the intrinsic cutoff.
- The ³S₁-³D₁ channel is renormalized to the scattering length a₀ = 5.4 fm and the deuteron binding energy E_b = -2.225 MeV.
- For the LO potential with Λ_c = 1000 MeV, Λ = 800 MeV and ω = 120 MeV, n_max = 8 is sufficient for most channels, but the mixing angle ε₁ and singular attractive P-waves (³P₀, ³P₂-³F₂) require n_max = 16 to converge.
- At NNLO with ω = 60 MeV, the minimum n_max required for convergence ranges from 15 to 20 depending on the channel.

## Context
This work extends the approach of the Arizona group (Ia05, Ia07a, Ia07b, Ia10, Ji10, Ia10a, RJ12), who renormalized pionless potentials directly in HO space, and of Shirokov et al. (oak), who used the J-matrix formalism. It contrasts with unitary transformation methods (such as Lee-Suzuki or similarity renormalization group) that introduce additional scales and potentially destroy EFT power counting. The method enables ab-initio calculations within the no-core-shell-model framework (ncsm) by providing a model-independent NN interaction that depends only on two scales (infrared cutoff λ and ultraviolet cutoff Λ), and it adopts the perturbative power counting of Birse (2011) to maintain renormalization group invariance.
