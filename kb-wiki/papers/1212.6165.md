# Quark-Hadron Phase Transition in the PNJL model for interacting quarks

> We study quark-hadron phase transition at finite temperature with zero net baryon density by the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model for interacting quarks in uniform background temporal color gauge fields. At low temperatures, unphysical thermal quark-antiquark excitations which would appear in the mean field approximation, are eliminated by en- forcing vanishing expectation value of the Polyakov-loop of the background gauge field, while at high temperatures this expectation value is taken as unity allowing thermal excitations of free quarks and antiquarks. Mesonic excitations in the low temperature phase appear in the correlation energy as contributions of collective excitations. We describe them in terms of thermal fluctuations of auxiliary fields in one-loop (Gaus- sian) approximation, where pions appear as Nambu-Goldstone modes associated with dynamical symmetry breaking of the chiral symmetry in the limit of vanishing bare quark masses. We show that at low temperatures the equations of state reduces to that of free meson gas with small corrections arising from the composite nature of mesons. At high temperatures, all these collective mesonic excitations melt into continuum of quark anti-quark excitations and mesonic correlations gives only small contributions the pressure of the system.

## Key claim
The paper shows that in the PNJL (Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio) model at zero net baryon density, the low-temperature equation of state reduces to that of a free meson gas with small corrections from the composite nature of mesons, but only when collective mesonic modes (not non-collective modes) are included; at high temperatures, all mesonic excitations melt into the quark-antiquark continuum and contribute little to the pressure.

## Method
The authors study the quark-hadron phase transition using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model for interacting quarks coupled to a uniform background temporal color gauge field (the Polyakov loop). At low temperatures, the Polyakov loop expectation value is forced to vanish, suppressing unphysical thermal quark-antiquark excitations, while at high temperatures it is set to unity, allowing free quark and antiquark excitations. Mesonic excitations are described as thermal fluctuations of auxiliary bosonic fields in a one-loop (Gaussian) approximation, with pions appearing as Nambu-Goldstone modes from chiral symmetry breaking in the limit of vanishing bare quark masses.

## Context
This work extends the original PNJL model proposed by Fukushima (2004), which introduced the Polyakov loop as a fugacity factor to suppress quark single-particle excitations at low temperatures, by incorporating mesonic excitations as thermal fluctuations beyond the mean-field approximation. The approach enables a unified description of the transition from a confined phase dominated by mesonic degrees of freedom to a deconfined quark-gluon plasma phase, and it connects to lattice QCD studies of the phase diagram at zero baryon density. The next step would be to extend this framework to finite baryon densities, where lattice QCD faces the sign problem.
