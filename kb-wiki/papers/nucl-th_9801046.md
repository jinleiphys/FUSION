# The proton and neutron distribution in Na isotopes: the development of halo and shell structure

> The interaction cross sections for $^A Na + ^{12}C$ reaction are calculated using Glauber model. The continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov theory has been generalized to treat the odd particle system and take the continuum into account. The theory reproduces the experimental result quite well. The matter distributions from the proton drip line to the neutron drip line in Na isotopes have been systematically studied and presented. The relation between the shell effects and the halos has been examined. The tail of the matter distribution shows a strong dependence on the shell structure. The neutron N=28 closure shell fails to appear due to the coming down of the $2p_{3/2}$ and $2p_{1/2}$. The development of the halo was understood as changes in the occupation in the next shell or the sub-shell close to the continuum limit. The central proton density is found to be decreasing near the neutron drip line, which is due to the proton-neutron interaction. However the diffuseness of the proton density does not change for the whole Na isotopes.

## Key claim

The development of a neutron halo in Na isotopes is directly linked to the filling of neutrons in the next major shell or sub-shell near the continuum limit, with the tail of the matter distribution being sensitive to whether a shell has occupation rather than to how many particles occupy it. The N=28 shell closure fails to appear because the 2p3/2 and 2p1/2 orbitals come down close to the 1f7/2 level.

## Method

The authors solved the Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) equations in coordinate space using a spherical box of radius 25 fm with a step size of 0.1 fm, employing the NLSH parameter set and a zero-range pairing force with density dependence. For odd-mass nuclei, the blocking effect was implemented by replacing the appropriate column in the U and V matrices. The resulting density distributions were used as input for Glauber model calculations of interaction cross sections for A Na + 12C reactions at 950A MeV, using free nucleon-nucleon cross sections.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies were calculated for Na isotopes with mass number A ranging from 17 to 45
- The neutron drip-line nucleus was predicted to be 45Na
- The tail density at r = 10 fm for 27Na is two orders of magnitude larger than for 25Na
- The tail density shows another two orders of magnitude increase from 31Na to 33Na
- The radius r0 (where density equals 10^-4 fm^-3) shows a slight decrease from the proton drip-line to 25Na, then remains almost constant from 26Na to 31Na, followed by a jump from 31Na to 32Na
- For each spin-parity channel, 20 radial wave functions were taken into account, corresponding to a cut-off energy of approximately 120 MeV
- The nuclear matter density rho0 used was 0.152 fm^-3

## Context

This work extends the fully self-consistent Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov approach previously applied to Lithium isotopes by Meng and Ring (1996) to the systematic study of Na isotopes from the proton drip-line to the neutron drip-line. The calculations reproduce experimental interaction cross sections measured by Suzuki (1995) and binding energies from Audi and Wapstra (1993). The finding that the proton density distribution remains nearly unchanged from the proton drip-line to the neutron drip-line is consistent with charge-changing cross section experiments by Bochkarev (1997). The results contradict the expectation of a N=28 shell closure for neutron-rich Na isotopes and demonstrate that the halo phenomenon is a spatial manifestation of shell effects, with the continuum treatment being essential for describing drip-line nuclei.
