# JM-ECS: A hybrid method combining the $J$-matrix and ECS methods for scattering calculations

> The paper proposes a hybrid method for calculating scattering processes that combines the $J$-matrix method with exterior complex scaling as an absorbing boundary condition. It represents the wave function as a finite sum of oscillator eigenstates in the inner region and on grid in the outer region. The method is validated for one and two dimensional model partial wave equation equations. Finally, the method calculates nuclear $p$-shell scattering.

Here is the wiki page body based on the provided paper.

## Key claim
The paper proposes a hybrid method (JM-ECS) that combines the $J$-matrix method in an inner region with an exterior complex scaling (ECS) absorbing boundary condition on a finite-difference grid in the outer region, validated for model problems and nuclear $p$-shell scattering, but the extraction of observables in two dimensions via surface integrals "still needs to be elaborated in the hybrid representation."

## Method
The wave function is represented as a finite sum of oscillator eigenstates in the inner (interaction) region and as a discretized grid using finite differences in the outer (asymptotic) region. The coupling between the two representations at the matching point uses an asymptotic relation linking oscillator expansion coefficients to the wave function values at classical turning points. The ECS transformation is applied to the finite-difference grid to enforce outgoing wave boundary conditions via a homogeneous Dirichlet condition at a complex endpoint, avoiding the need for explicit asymptotic wave forms.

## Key numbers
* The kinetic energy matrix for the oscillator basis has non-zero elements: diagonal $(2i+l+3/2) \hbar \omega / 2$, off-diagonal $-\sqrt{i(i+l+1/2)} \hbar \omega / 2$ and $-\sqrt{(i+1)(i+l+3/2)} \hbar \omega / 2$.
* The grid distance in the asymptotic region is set to $h = R_{n} - R_{n-1}$, where $R_{n}$ is the $n$-th classical turning point.
* The classical turning point for an oscillator basis function is given by $R_{i,l} = b \sqrt{4i+2l+3}$.
* The suppression of the incoming wave amplitude at the complex endpoint $L^\prime$ is given by $|B|/|A| = \exp(-2 k \,\text{Im}(L^\prime))$.

## Context
This work extends the $J$-matrix method (Heller, 1970s) by replacing its asymptotic analytic solutions with an ECS absorbing boundary condition (Simon, 1979; McCurdy, 2004), which is already used in atomic and molecular physics for breakup problems. The hybrid approach aims to combine the advantages of an $L^2$ oscillator basis for the compound nucleus with the simplicity of grid-based absorbing boundaries, enabling future calculations of nuclear breakup reactions without requiring explicit asymptotic wave functions.
