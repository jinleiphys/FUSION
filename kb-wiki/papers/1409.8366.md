# Multifragmentation model for the production of astrophysical strangelets

> Determination of baryon number (or mass) distribution of the strangelets, that may fragment out of the warm and excited strange quark matter ejected in the merger of strange stars in compact binary stellar systems in the Galaxy, is attempted here by using a statistical disassembly model. Finite mass of strange quarks is taken into account in the analysis. Resulting charges of the strangelets and the corresponding Coulomb corrections are included to get a plausible size distribution of those strangelets as they are produced in binary stellar mergers thus getting injected in the Galaxy. From this mass distribution of strangelets at their source, an approximate order of magnitude estimate for their possible flux in solar neighborhood is attempted by using a simple diffusion model for their propagation in the Galaxy. Such theoretical estimate is important in view of the ongoing efforts to detect galactic strangelets by recent satellite-borne experiments.

## Key claim

The paper uses a statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) to estimate the baryon number distribution of strangelets produced in strange star mergers, now including finite strange quark mass (taken as 95 ± 5 MeV) and its effects on electric charge and Coulomb corrections, which were ignored in the authors' earlier exploratory analysis (Biswas 2012). The resulting flux estimate in the solar neighborhood is only an approximate order-of-magnitude calculation, subject to the uncertainties of the simple diffusion model and the assumed freeze-out temperature range (0.001–1.0 MeV).

## Method

The authors apply a statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) to a globally charge-neutral "strangelet-complex", a blob of warm, excited strange quark matter at freeze-out, assuming thermodynamic and chemical equilibrium (including beta-equilibrium) at a constant temperature T. The thermodynamic potential of each spherical strangelet fragment is calculated using the multiple reflection expansion method within the MIT bag model, including volume, surface, and curvature contributions with finite strange quark mass, plus Coulomb energy from Debye screening. The multiplicity of each fragment species is then obtained from the equilibrium partition function, and the resulting mass distribution at the source is propagated to the solar neighborhood using a simple diffusion model.

## Key numbers

* Strange quark mass adopted: \( m_s = 95 \pm 5 \) MeV, based on recent consensus (Blossier 2013, Particle Data Group 2012).
* Quark chemical potential assumed: \( \mu_q \sim 300 \) MeV.
* Debye screening length: \( \lambda_D \approx 5 \) fm (from \( \lambda_D = 7.33/\mu_q \) with \( \mu_q \) in MeV).
* Quark mode surface tension at zero temperature: \( \sim 9 \) MeV fm\(^{-2}\) (for \( \mu_s \sim 300 \) MeV), within the range \( \sigma_s \sim (5-20) \) MeV fm\(^{-2}\) from LSMq and NJL models (Palhares 2010).
* Quark mode curvature coefficient at zero temperature and massless quarks: \( \sigma_c \sim 17 \) MeV fm\(^{-1}\) (for \( \mu_q \sim 300 \) MeV).
* Freeze-out temperature range considered: \( T = 0.001-1.0 \) MeV.
* Condition for no localized electrons inside strangelets: satisfied for baryon number \( A^i \lesssim 10^5 \) (radius \( R^i \lesssim 46 \) fm).
* For a strangelet with \( A^i \sim 10 \), curvature energy per baryon \( \sim 100 \) MeV; for \( A^i \sim 100 \), \( \sim 20 \) MeV; for \( A^i \sim 10^3 \), \( \sim 4 \) MeV (MIT bag model, \( m_s = 0 \), \( r_o^i \sim 1 \) fm, \( T = 0 \), \( \mu_q \approx 300 \) MeV).
* Surface energy per baryon from LSMq/NJL models (Palhares 2010): \( \sim (30-120) \) MeV for \( A^i \sim 10 \), \( \sim (10-50) \) MeV for \( A^i \sim 100 \), \( \sim (6-25) \) MeV for \( A^i \sim 10^3 \).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier exploratory analysis (Biswas 2012) by incorporating finite strange quark mass and its consequences (electric charge, Coulomb energy, dynamical surface tension) into the multifragmentation model, addressing a key simplification of that prior study. The results are intended to aid ongoing experimental searches for galactic strangelets by satellite-borne experiments such as PAMELA (Casolino 2008) and AMS-02 (Kounine 2012). The paper notes that fragmentation of color-flavor-locked (CFL) strange matter will be examined in future work.
