# A local and renormalizable framework for the gauge-invariant operator $A^2_{\min}$ in Euclidean Yang-Mills theories in linear covariant gauges

> We address the issue of the renormalizability of the gauge-invariant non-local dimension-two operator $A^2_{\rm min}$, whose minimization is defined along the gauge orbit. Despite its non-local character, we show that the operator $A^2_{\rm min}$ can be cast in local form through the introduction of an auxiliary Stueckelberg field. The localization procedure gives rise to an unconventional kind of Stueckelberg-type action which turns out to be renormalizable to all orders of perturbation theory. In particular, as a consequence of its gauge invariance, the anomalous dimension of the operator $A^2_{\rm min}$ turns out to be independent from the gauge parameter $α$ entering the gauge-fixing condition, being thus given by the anomalous dimension of the operator $A^2$ in the Landau gauge.

## Key claim

The paper proves that the gauge-invariant non-local dimension-two operator \(A^2_{\min}\) can be cast into a local and renormalizable form through the introduction of an auxiliary Stueckelberg field, provided the transversality condition \(\partial_\mu A^h_\mu = 0\) is enforced by a Lagrange multiplier. This localization yields an unconventional Stueckelberg-type action that is renormalizable to all orders of perturbation theory, unlike the standard Stueckelberg mass term which is non-renormalizable.

## Method

The authors start with the Faddeev-Popov action for Euclidean Yang-Mills theory in linear covariant gauges and add the non-local operator \(A^2_{\min}\) via a mass parameter \(m^2\), along with a Lagrange multiplier \(\tau^a\) enforcing the transversality condition \(\partial_\mu A^h_\mu = 0\). They localize the non-local expression for \(A^h_\mu\) by introducing a Stueckelberg field \(\xi^a\) such that \(A^h_\mu = h^\dagger A_\mu h + (i/g) h^\dagger \partial_\mu h\) with \(h = e^{ig\xi}\). Using the algebraic renormalization method, they derive the Ward identities and prove the multiplicative renormalizability of the resulting action to all orders, showing that the anomalous dimension of \(A^2_{\min}\) equals that of the operator \(A^2\) in the Landau gauge.

## Key numbers

* The anomalous dimension of the gauge-invariant operator \((A^h_\mu A^h_\mu)\) is given by \(\gamma_{(A^h)^2} = \gamma_{A^2}\big|_{\rm Landau} = \left( \frac{\beta(a)}{a} + \gamma^{\rm Landau}_{A}(a) \right)\), where \(a = g^2/(16\pi^2)\), valid to all orders of perturbation theory.
* In the standard Stueckelberg formulation, the Stueckelberg field propagator is \(\langle \xi^a(p) \xi^b(-p) \rangle_{\rm Stueck} = \delta^{ab} \left( 1 + \frac{\alpha m^2}{p^2} \right) \frac{1}{m^2 p^2}\), which contains the mass parameter \(m^2\) in the denominator.
* In the present formulation, the Stueckelberg field propagator becomes \(\langle \xi^a(p) \xi^b(-p) \rangle_{S} = \alpha \frac{\delta^{ab}}{p^4}\), which is free of the mass parameter \(m^2\) in the denominator.
* In the Landau gauge (\(\alpha = 0\)), the Stueckelberg field decouples: \(\langle \xi^a(p) \xi^b(-p) \rangle_{S}^{\alpha=0} = \langle A^a_\mu(p) \xi^b(-p) \rangle_{S}^{\alpha=0} = \langle A_\mu^a(p) \tau^b(-p) \rangle_{S}^{\alpha} = 0\).

## Context

This work extends the study of dimension-two condensates in Euclidean Yang-Mills theories, which have been investigated in Landau gauge by Verschelde (2001), Dudal (2003), and others, and shown to generate a dynamical gluon mass and account for \(1/Q^2\) corrections in the operator product expansion. The paper provides a gauge-invariant framework for the operator \(A^2_{\min}\) in linear covariant gauges, generalizing previous one-loop results by Gracey (2007) and enabling the study of the dimension-two condensate \(\langle A^h_\mu A^h_\mu \rangle\) in a BRST-invariant and gauge-parameter-independent way. This framework also arises within the BRST-invariant formulation of the Gribov-Zwanziger theory recently achieved by Capri (2015, 2016) and can be seen as the BRST-invariant extension in linear covariant gauges of the effective model considered by Tissier and Wschebor (2010, 2011) in the Landau gauge.
