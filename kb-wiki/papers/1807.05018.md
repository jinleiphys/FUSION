# Non-Equilibrium Quantum Transport of Chiral Fluids from Kinetic Theory

> We introduce the quantum-field-theory (QFT) derivation of chiral kinetic theory (CKT) from the Wigner-function approach, which manifests side jumps and non-scalar distribution functions associated with Lorentz covariance and incorporates both background fields and collisions. The formalism is utilized to investigate second-order responses of chiral fluids near local equilibrium. Such non-equilibrium anomalous transport is dissipative and affected by interactions. Contributions from both quantum corrections in anomalous hydrodynamic equations (EOM) of motion and those from the CKT and Wigner functions (WF) are considered in a relaxation-time approximation (RTA). Anomalous charged Hall currents engendered by background electric fields and temperature/chemical-potential gradients are obtained. Furthermore, chiral magnetic/vortical effects (CME/CVE) receive viscous corrections as non-equilibrium modifications stemming from the interplay between side jumps, magnetic-moment coupling, and chiral anomaly.

## Key claim

The paper derives a quantum-field-theory-based chiral kinetic theory (CKT) from the Wigner-function approach that includes both background fields and collisions, and uses it to compute second-order (dissipative) anomalous transport in chiral fluids near local equilibrium, finding that chiral magnetic/vortical effects receive viscous corrections that depend on interactions through the relaxation time.

## Method

The authors start from the Kadanoff-Baym-like equations for the Wigner function of right-handed fermions, solve perturbatively to first order in \(\hbar\), and obtain a covariant CKT that incorporates side-jump terms and non-scalar distribution functions. They then apply a gradient expansion and a relaxation-time approximation (RTA) for the collision term, solve for the non-equilibrium distribution function \(\delta f_q\) near local equilibrium, and compute the charge current from the Wigner function. The hydrodynamic equations of motion (charge and energy-momentum conservation with the chiral anomaly) are used to constrain the temporal derivatives of thermodynamic parameters via matching conditions.

## Key numbers

* The local equilibrium distribution function in the co-moving frame is \(f^\text{eq}_q = (e^{g}+1)^{-1}\) with \(g = (q\cdot u - \mu + \hbar(\omega\cdot q)(2q\cdot u)^{-1})/T\), where \(T\) is local temperature, \(\mu\) is chemical potential, and \(\omega^\mu\) is vorticity.
* The collision term in RTA is approximated as \(\mathcal{C}_\text{full} \simeq -(q\cdot u\,\delta f_q)/\tau_R\), where \(\tau_R\) is a constant relaxation time.
* The non-equilibrium second-order corrections to the charged current are linear in \(\tau_R\) and include terms such as \(\delta\hat{\sigma}_{BL}\theta B^\mu\) (bulk correction to CME) and \(\delta\hat{\sigma}_{BH}\pi^{\mu\nu} B_\nu\) (shear correction to CME), where \(\theta = \partial\cdot u\) is the bulk strength and \(\pi^{\mu\nu}\) is the shear strength.
* The chiral anomaly equation is \(\partial_\mu J^\mu = -\hbar E_\mu B^\mu/(4\pi^2)\).

## Context

This work extends the semi-classical CKT developed by Son and Stephanov (2012) and the side-jump formalism of Chen et al. (2014, 2015) by providing a full QFT derivation that includes background fields and collisions, building on the authors' own prior work (Hidaka et al., 2016, 2017, 2018). The results agree with previous findings from anomalous hydrodynamics with thermodynamic constraints (Son and Surowka, 2009; Neiman and Oz, 2010) for the equilibrium CME/CVE, and go beyond by computing dissipative second-order corrections. The formalism enables future phenomenological studies of anomalous transport in heavy-ion collisions and can be extended to study relativistic angular momenta and polarization, as well as to incorporate dynamical gluons and realistic collisions in the quark-gluon plasma.
