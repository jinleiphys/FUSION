# Fluctuation dynamics and formation of exotic shape

> To study the role of fluctuation in the collisional dynamics, Boltzmann- Langevin formalism is applied in a two dimensional scenario. The importance of collective flow towards the formation of hollow structure is exhibited in our simulation result. The observation of large clusters along the transverse direction indicates the non-equilibrium aspects of the fragmentation process in the late stage of the reaction.

## Key claim
The Boltzmann-Langevin formalism, applied in a two-dimensional simulation, shows that collective flow drives the formation of a hollow (ring-like) structure in the di-nuclear system, but this exotic shape is only stable at sufficiently high incident energy (68 MeV/nucleon) and collapses at lower energy (32 MeV/nucleon).

## Method
The Boltzmann-Langevin equation was solved in two-dimensional physical space using a phase-space lattice with (27×27) momentum cells and a coordinate cell width of 1/3 fm. Twenty identical samples were evolved separately to incorporate fluctuating collision terms, with a simplified Skyrme interaction (compressibility modulus K=288 MeV) and Coulomb interaction included for protons. Head-on collisions between two identical spherical blobs (rms radius 5 fm, representing a nucleus of mass number ≈100) were simulated at incident energies of 32, 68, and 117 MeV/nucleon.

## Key numbers
- Incident energies simulated: 32, 68, and 117 MeV/nucleon (corresponding to boost factors B_fac = 2, 3, and 4)
- Maximum central density reached at t ≈ 30 fm/c: ≈ 1.6 ρ_0 (where ρ_0 = 0.55 fm^{-2})
- At t = 200 fm/c for 68 MeV/nucleon: maximum density near y axis ≈ 0.60 ρ_0; average density in other parts of the ring ≈ 0.30 ρ_0
- At 32 MeV/nucleon: central rarefaction smeared out by t ≈ 250 fm/c, leaving an elongated residue with uniform density 0.8 ρ_0
- At 117 MeV/nucleon: final average density ≈ 0.20 ρ_0, with density near periphery along y direction ≈ 0.4 ρ_0
- Ratio δρ/ρ (density fluctuation relative to density) reaches ≈ 0.6 at t = 200 fm/c
- Time step of simulation: Δt ≈ 5 fm/c
- Number of successful collisions per time step per sample: up to >40,000; number of phase-space overlap events: <10

## Context
This work extends earlier Boltzmann-Langevin studies of clusterization in spinodal nuclear matter (Burgio et al., 1992; Colonna et al., 1994; Chattopadhyay, 1995) to finite nuclear systems, where fragment formation proceeds through exotic shapes rather than volume breakup. It builds on mean-trajectory calculations by Moretto (1993) and Xu et al. (1993) that predicted exotic di-nuclear shapes in the 50-100 MeV/nucleon energy window, and Bordignon et al. (1994) at lower energies. The results suggest that entrance-channel effects persist into the fragmentation stage, producing large clusters along the transverse direction, and call for full three-dimensional calculations to test the predictive power of semi-classical mean-field models for nuclear fragmentation.
