# Hadrons at high temperature: an update from the FASTSUM collaboration

> We present the most recent results from the FASTSUM collaboration for hadron properties at high temperature. This includes the temperature dependence of the light and charmed meson and baryon spectrum, as well as properties of heavy quarkonia. The results are obtained using anisotropic lattices with a fixed scale approach. We also present the status of our next generation gauge ensembles.

## Key claim

The FASTSUM collaboration reports that chiral partners (e.g., rho and a1 mesons) become degenerate at the chiral crossover temperature, with the a1 mass dropping substantially, while the pion mass increases with temperature; these results are obtained using anisotropic lattice QCD with a pion mass of approximately 240 MeV, and the analysis assumes narrow bound states, so results above T_c cannot be taken as evidence that states survive in the quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The collaboration used anisotropic lattice QCD with 2+1 flavours of clover-improved Wilson fermions and an O(a^2) improved gauge action, employing a fixed-scale approach where temperature is varied by changing the number of temporal lattice sites N_tau. Hadron masses were extracted from exponential fits to correlators of extended (smeared) operators with optimised ground-state overlap, and spectral functions were obtained using the BR method and the maximum entropy method (MEM). The parity-doubling parameter R, defined from the correlator asymmetry, was used as a qualitative order parameter for the chiral transition.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass m_pi ≈ 240 MeV on the Gen2L ensemble (the primary ensemble used for results)
- Spatial lattice spacing a_s = 0.112 fm and inverse temporal lattice spacing a_tau^{-1} = 6.08 GeV for Gen2L
- Open-charm meson masses (D, D_s, D*, D_s*) show a decrease of about 40 MeV at T ≈ 150 MeV
- Upsilon width increases by up to 60 MeV at T = 300 MeV
- Gen2L spatial extent L_s = 3.58 fm with N_s = 32 lattice sites in spatial directions
- Gen2P target: physical pion mass m_pi = 140 MeV, a_s ≈ 0.100 fm, a_tau^{-1} ≈ 6.80 GeV, L_s = 4.80 fm
- Gen3 target: anisotropy ξ = 7.0, a_s ≈ 0.120 fm, a_tau^{-1} ≈ 11.66 GeV, m_pi ≈ 390 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous FASTSUM studies of light and strange baryons (Aarts 2015, 2017, 2018) and charmonium (Kelly 2018) to include light mesons, charmed mesons and baryons, and beauty hadrons using the newer Gen2L ensemble with a lighter pion mass (240 MeV vs 390 MeV in Gen2). The results enable comparison with experimental observations of D_s/D ratios in heavy-ion collisions and provide a foundation for next-generation ensembles (Gen2P at physical pion mass and Gen3 with halved temporal lattice spacing) that will control finite lattice spacing and quark mass systematic effects.
