# Investigation of $πN$ contributions to nucleon matrix elements

> We investigate an improved method to extract nucleon matrix elements from lattice 3-point functions using a generalized eigenvalue problem (GEVP) with nucleon and pion-nucleon interpolating fields. Our method avoids the computation of the costly three-point functions that have pion-nucleon interpolators at both source and sink. We demonstrate that excited state contamination from $Nπ$ is minimized in nucleon matrix elements of the scalar, vector, pseudoscalar, axial, and tensor currents and discuss our results based on a physical-point ensemble with a pion mass value of 131 MeV. We find that the GEVP is most significant for the isovector pseudoscalar and axial currents.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that using a generalized eigenvalue problem (GEVP) with nucleon and pion-nucleon interpolating fields can minimize excited state contamination from \(N\pi\) states in nucleon matrix elements, but only for specific currents; the method shows no significant improvement for the scalar current (and thus the nucleon sigma term \(\sigma_{\pi N}\)), indicating that contamination in that case is unlikely to come from the lowest \(N\pi\) state.

## Method

The authors compute two-point and three-point correlation functions on a single lattice ensemble with physical pion mass (131 MeV) using nucleon (\(\mathcal{O}_N\)) and pion-nucleon (\(\mathcal{O}_{N\pi}\)) interpolating fields. They solve a GEVP to obtain optimized interpolators that suppress \(N\pi\) contamination, but avoid computing the computationally expensive three-point function with pion-nucleon interpolators at both source and sink by introducing weight factors derived from the GEVP eigenvectors. The method is applied to the scalar, vector, pseudoscalar, axial, and tensor currents for both isoscalar and isovector channels, including disconnected quark loop contributions for isovector operators.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: \(m_\pi = 131\) MeV; nucleon mass: \(m_N = 931(3)\) MeV; lattice spacing: \(a = 0.0938\) fm; lattice size: \(48^3 \times 96\) (physical extent \(L = 4.50\) fm); 1228 gauge configurations.
- For the isovector pseudoscalar current, the ratio that should yield zero due to parity symmetry becomes consistent with zero (within two standard deviations) after GEVP improvement, whereas without GEVP it is non-zero for all time separations except the midpoint.
- For the isovector axial charge \(g_A^{u-d}\), results using the temporal component of the axial current agree with the continuum limit of Alexandrou (2023) only when the GEVP-improved interpolator is used.
- For the isovector pseudoscalar form factor \(G_5^{u-d}\) and induced pseudoscalar form factor \(G_P^{u-d}\) at one unit of momentum transfer, GEVP improvement suppresses time dependence and brings results closer to the continuum limit of Alexandrou (2023).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Barca (2022), who found significant improvements using \(N\pi\) operators for the isovector pseudoscalar and axial channels at a heavier pion mass of 429 MeV, by applying it at the physical pion mass (131 MeV) and including disconnected quark loop contributions. The method addresses the discrepancy in the nucleon sigma term \(\sigma_{\pi N}\) between lattice QCD and phenomenology identified by Gupta (2021, 2023), but finds that \(N\pi\) states are not the dominant contamination for the scalar current. The results enable more reliable extraction of nucleon matrix elements for pseudoscalar and axial currents, and suggest that cutoff effects for the induced pseudoscalar form factor may be milder when disconnected diagrams are included.
