# Probing the in-medium $P_T$-broadening by $γ$+HF angular de-correlations

> Angular correlations between vector boson and heavy flavors~(HF) are potentially new effective tools to gain insight into the partonic interactions in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP). In this paper, we present the theoretical study of the azimuthal angular de-correlations of $γ+$HF in nucleus-nucleus collisions as a new probe of the in-medium $P_T$-broadening effect. The initial production of $γ+$HF in p+p is generated by SHERPA which matches the next-to-leading hard processes with parton shower. The in-medium heavy quark evolution is implemented by a Monte Carlo Langevin simulation, which takes into account the collisional and radiative energy loss. We observe considerable suppression at $Δϕ_{γD}\simπ$ and enhancement at $Δϕ_{γD}<2.8$ in $γ+$D azimuthal angular distribution in $0-10\%$ Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$5.02 TeV compared to the p+p baseline, which indicates evident in-medium $P_T$-broadening of charm quarks. We also find that the overall modification patterns of $γ+$D angular distribution are sensitive to the selection cut of D meson $p_T$. Furthermore, by constructing the 2D ($x_{J}^{γD}, Δϕ_{γD}$) correlation diagram, it's possible to display the respective impacts of the two aspects of jet quenching, energy loss and $P_T$-broadening, on the final-state $γ+$D observable simultaneously. Additionally, we find weaker angular de-correlations of $γ+$B compared to $γ+$D which may be helpful to understand the mass hierarchy in heavy-ion collisions. Finally, the nuclear modification of $Δϕ_{γD}$ distributions in central $0-10\%$ Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy is provided for completeness.

## Key claim

The paper claims that azimuthal angular de-correlations between a direct photon and a heavy flavor hadron (specifically D mesons) in nucleus-nucleus collisions can serve as a clean probe of the in-medium transverse momentum broadening of heavy quarks, provided that the same photon transverse momentum cut is applied in both p+p and A+A events to control the initial kinematics.

## Method

The initial production of photon plus heavy flavor in p+p collisions was generated using the SHERPA event generator at next-to-leading order matched with parton shower (NLO+PS). The in-medium evolution of heavy quarks was simulated with a Monte Carlo Langevin approach that includes both collisional (drag and thermal stochastic terms) and radiative (gluon emission via higher-twist formalism) energy loss mechanisms. The space-time evolution of the quark-gluon plasma was described by a (2+1)D viscous hydrodynamic model, and the initial production vertex was sampled using the Glauber model.

## Key numbers

* In central 0-10% Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV, the ratio of normalized azimuthal angular distributions (PbPb/pp) shows a suppression of approximately 0.82 at Delta_phi_gammaD ~ pi and an enhancement with a maximum value of about 1.15 at Delta_phi_gammaD ~ 2.1.
* The jet transport parameter q_0 was set to 1.2 GeV^2/fm at LHC energy and 0.6 GeV^2/fm at RHIC energy, extracted from identified hadron production in previous studies.
* The spatial diffusion coefficient D_s(2pi T) was set to 4 at LHC and 5 at RHIC, obtained by chi^2 fitting to D meson R_AA data and consistent with Lattice QCD calculations of D_s(2pi T)=3.7 to 7.
* The in-medium evolution stopped when the local temperature fell below T_c=165 MeV.
* D mesons were selected with p_T^D > 1 GeV and photons with p_T^gamma > 10 GeV for the main analysis at LHC.

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of jet quenching by proposing photon+heavy flavor correlations as a cleaner observable than hadron-jet or Z/gamma+jet correlations, which suffer from vacuum Sudakov effects or selection bias as noted by Mueller (2016) and Luo (2018). The approach builds on the authors' previous Langevin simulations of b-bbar dijets (Dai 2018) and Z+b-jet (Wang 2020), and on their successful description of the radial profile of heavy flavor mesons in jets compared to CMS data (Sirunyan 2019). The results enable future experimental measurements at both LHC and RHIC to disentangle energy loss from transverse momentum broadening effects in the quark-gluon plasma.
