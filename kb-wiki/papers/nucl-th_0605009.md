# Critical Survey of Isoscalar and Isovector Contributions to the Spin Orbit Potential in Relativistic Mean Field Theory

> The spin-orbit (SO) interaction, emerging naturally from the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) theory is examined critically in the light of the recently measured excitation energy differences between the terminating states built on two different configurations for nuclei belonging to the lower pf shell. The calculations are carried out using the cranked RMF framework. To further probe the iso-vector dependence of the spin-orbit potential, the energy spacing between the g_{7/2} and h_{11/2} states in the Sb-chain is compared to experiment. It is found that the calculation at the quantitative level deviates strongly from the experiment. In particular the balance of the iso-scalar and iso-vector strengths of the effective one body SO potential indicates that additional terms like tensor couplings may be needed to account for the experimental data.

# Critical Survey of Isoscalar and Isovector Contributions to the Spin Orbit Potential in Relativistic Mean Field Theory

## Key claim

Standard Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) Lagrangians cannot simultaneously reproduce the correct strength of the spin-orbit potential and the experimentally observed isovector dependence; the one-body spin-orbit potential is too weak and its isovector component is too small, suggesting that additional terms such as tensor couplings or Fock terms from π-meson exchange are needed to account for the experimental data.

## Method

The authors performed cranked Relativistic Mean Field (c-RMF) calculations using the basis expansion technique with 8 fermionic and 10 bosonic harmonic oscillator shells, employing the NL1 and NL3 Lagrangian parameter sets. They computed the excitation energy difference ΔE between terminating states built on the d₃/₂⁻¹f₇/₂ⁿ⁺¹ and f₇/₂ⁿ configurations for nuclei in the lower pf shell (⁴²Ca through ⁴⁷V), and also calculated the energy spacing E^gh between the g₇/₂ and h₁₁/₂ states in the Sb isotope chain using NL1, NL3, and the density-dependent DD-ME2 parameter set.

## Key numbers

- For NL1, the mean squared deviation between calculated and experimental ΔE values is 0.59 MeV; for NL3, it is 1.31 MeV.
- The ratio of isoscalar to isovector strength of the spin-orbit potential is 16.54 for NL1 and 19.62 for NL3; for density-dependent parameter sets DD-ME1 and DD-ME2, this ratio is typically around 20 or larger.
- Experimental E^gh values in Sb isotopes vary between approximately 0.5 MeV and 3 MeV, while NL1 and NL3 calculations vary within only 1 MeV; DD-ME2 yields an almost constant spacing.
- The experimental ΔE values for the terminating states range from 5.108 MeV (⁴²Ca) to 5.884 MeV (⁴⁵Ti).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of terminating states previously applied to Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations by Zduńczuk et al. (2005) and Satula and Wyss (2005), which showed that such states provide a unique tool for determining spin-orbit strength. The study contradicts the ability of standard RMF parameter sets (NL1, NL3, DD-ME2) to reproduce experimental single-particle data, in contrast to some Skyrme parameterizations that can be adjusted to agree. The results motivate extending the RMF model by including tensor couplings as proposed by Jiang et al. (2005) or by moving to a Hartree-Fock scheme including π-meson exchange as discussed by Bouyssy et al. (1987).
