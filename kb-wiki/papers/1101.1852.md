# Hadron-Hadron and Cosmic-Ray Interactions at multi-TeV Energies

> The workshop on "Hadron-Hadron and Cosmic-Ray Interactions at multi-TeV Energies" held at the ECT* centre (Trento) in Nov.-Dec. 2010 gathered together both theorists and experimentalists to discuss issues of the physics of high-energy hadronic interactions of common interest for the particle, nuclear and cosmic-ray communities. QCD results from collider experiments -- mostly from the LHC but also from the Tevatron, RHIC and HERA -- were discussed and compared to various hadronic Monte Carlo generators, aiming at an improvement of our theoretical understanding of soft, semi-hard and hard parton dynamics. The latest cosmic-ray results from various ground-based observatories were also presented with an emphasis on the phenomenological modeling of the first hadronic interactions of the extended air-showers generated in the Earth atmosphere. These mini-proceedings consist of an introduction and short summaries of the talks presented at the meeting.

## Key claim

The workshop aimed to improve theoretical understanding of soft, semi-hard, and hard parton dynamics by comparing QCD results from collider experiments (LHC, Tevatron, RHIC, HERA) to various hadronic Monte Carlo generators, with the ultimate goal of enabling more reliable determinations of cosmic-ray energy and composition around the GZK cutoff; however, the bulk of particle production in high-energy hadronic collisions still cannot be calculated from first-principles QCD and relies on phenomenological models constrained by existing collider data up to about 10^15 eV.

## Method

The workshop gathered theorists and experimentalists to discuss QCD results from collider experiments and cosmic-ray measurements from ground-based observatories, comparing them to hadronic Monte Carlo generators including Pythia, Herwig, Sherpa, Phojet, Dpmjet, Qgsjet, Sibyll, Epos, and Fluka. Talks and discussions were organized around four main blocks: experimental results at hadron colliders, theory of hadronic collisions, experimental cosmic-ray results at ultra-high energies, and theory of cosmic-rays at ultra-high energies. The mini-proceedings consist of an introduction and short summaries of the talks presented at the meeting.

## Key numbers

- ATLAS collected 45 pb^-1 of integrated luminosity in proton-proton collisions at 7 TeV center-of-mass energy
- CMS recorded about 40 pb^-1 of integrated luminosity at 7 TeV, and measured jet spectra at various rapidities in pp collisions at 7 TeV compared to NLO pQCD
- CMS excluded excited quarks within a contact interaction model up to a mass of 1.58 TeV at 95% confidence level from dijet cross section measurements
- CMS measured top cross section at 7 TeV, confirming a rise by a factor of about 25 with respect to proton-antiproton collisions at 2 TeV center-of-mass energy
- LHCb measured K_S production cross section at sqrt(s)=900 GeV in the kinematic region (p_T < 1.6 GeV/c) x (2.5 < y < 4.0)
- TOTEM extracted 80k elastic scattering events with t-values above 0.4 GeV^2, showing a diffractive dip at around 0.6 GeV^2
- The average charged-particle multiplicity in pp collisions can be described by f_pp(sqrt(s)) = f_ee(K sqrt(s)) + n_0 with K = 0.35 and n_0 = 2.2

## Context

This workshop extended the ongoing dialogue between the particle physics and cosmic-ray communities, building on earlier work from HiRes (Abbasi 2007) and Auger (Abraham 2008) experiments measuring the GZK cutoff, and from collider experiments at Tevatron, RHIC, and HERA. The results enable improved tuning of hadronic Monte Carlo generators for cosmic-ray air shower simulations, particularly around the GZK cutoff energy of about 10^20 eV, and allow for more reliable determinations of cosmic-ray energy and composition. The workshop was motivated by the availability of first LHC data and high-quality cosmic-ray results, aiming to expand the mutually beneficial interface between the two communities exploring strong interactions at the highest accessible energies.
