# Realistic shell-model calculations for p-shell nuclei including contributions of a chiral three-body force

> In this paper we present an evolution of our derivation of the shell-model effective Hamiltonian, namely introducing effects of three-body contributions. More precisely, we consider a three-body potential at next-to-next-to-leading order in chiral perturbation theory, and the induced three-body forces that arise from many-body correlations among valence nucleons. The first one is included, in the derivation of the effective Hamiltonian for one- and two-valence nucleon-systems, at first order in the many-body perturbation theory. Namely, we include only the three-body interaction between one or two valence nucleons and those belonging to the core. For nuclei with more than two valence particles, both induced - turned on by the two-body potential - and genuine three-body forces come into play. Since it is difficult to perform shell-model calculations with three-body forces, these contributions are estimated for the ground-state energy only. In order to establish the reliability of our approximations, we focus attention on nuclei belonging to the p shell, aiming to benchmark our calculations against those performed with the ab initio no-core shell-model. The obtained results are satisfactory, and pave the way to the application of our approach to nuclear systems with heavier masses.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including both induced three-body forces (arising from two-body correlations) and a genuine chiral three-body potential at next-to-next-to-leading order improves shell-model calculations for p-shell nuclei, but the ground-state energies from the realistic shell-model still underestimate those from ab initio no-core shell-model calculations because only first-order contributions of the three-body potential are included.

## Method

The authors derive a shell-model effective Hamiltonian using the Kuo-Lee-Ratcliff folded-diagram expansion, starting from a chiral N3LO two-body potential and a chiral N2LO three-body potential. The three-body potential is included at first order in many-body perturbation theory for one- and two-valence nucleon systems, while for nuclei with more valence particles, both induced three-body diagrams (from the two-body force) and genuine three-body force contributions are estimated for the ground-state energy only, using the monopole component. The calculations are performed in a model space consisting of the 0p3/2 and 0p1/2 orbitals outside a 4He core, and results are benchmarked against no-core shell-model calculations.

## Key numbers

- The largest discrepancy between realistic shell-model and no-core shell-model ground-state energies, after including three-body correlation diagrams from the two-body force, is about 4% for 8Be.
- The low-energy constants for the three-body potential are set to cD = -1 and cE = -0.34, as inferred from Navratil (2007).
- The proton and neutron 0p1/2 effective single-particle energies relative to 0p3/2 are approximately 4 to 5 MeV when the three-body potential is included, remaining almost constant across A = 6 to 12 for N=Z isotopes.
- The yrast 2+ state in 12C is underestimated by about 1 MeV when using only the two-body potential.

## Context

This work extends the realistic shell-model approach of Coraggio (2012) by adding three-body contributions that were previously neglected, addressing the growing underbinding of ground-state energies with increasing mass number. The results are benchmarked against ab initio no-core shell-model calculations by Navratil (2007) and Maris (2013) for p-shell nuclei. The approach is similar to that of Otsuka (2010), Holt (2013, 2014), and Simonis (2016), who included first-order normal-ordered two-body parts of three-body forces. The next step is to include higher-order contributions with three-body vertices in the perturbative expansion to improve ground-state energies for heavier nuclei.
