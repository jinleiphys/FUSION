# Nucleon Electric Dipole Moments in Paramagnetic Molecules through Effective Field Theory

> Electric dipole moment (EDM) measurements using paramagnetic molecules have significantly advanced over the last decade. Traditionally, these experiments have been analyzed in terms of the electron EDM. However, paramagnetic molecules are also sensitive to hadronic sources of charge-parity (CP) violation, highlighting the need for a new framework to interpret the experimental results. In this Letter, we introduce an effective field theory framework to relate molecular EDMs to the EDMs of neutrons and protons. We identify the dominant contributions through power counting and pinpoint the necessary nuclear matrix elements. As a practical application, we employ the nuclear shell model to calculate these nuclear matrix elements for the polar molecule BaF. Finally, we estimate the limits on the nucleon EDMs set by current molecular EDM experiments.

## Key claim

The paper introduces an effective field theory framework that connects electric dipole moment (EDM) measurements in paramagnetic molecules to the EDMs of neutrons and protons, showing that paramagnetic molecules are sensitive to hadronic CP-violation sources beyond the traditionally assumed electron EDM, with the caveat that the dominant contributions arise from potential-region two-nucleon effects and ultrasoft photon exchange, and that for the specific case of BaF the neutron EDM contribution vanishes due to the closed neutron shell in 138Ba.

## Method

The authors use heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory within an effective field theory framework to systematically separate contributions from soft, ultrasoft, and potential photon momentum regions. They compute nuclear matrix elements using the nuclear shell model with three different Hamiltonians (GCN5082, QX, Sn100pn) for 138Ba and several other nuclei, evaluating both the ultrasoft contribution (involving sums over intermediate 1+ nuclear states) and the potential contribution (involving two-nucleon operators with spin and isospin structure). The calculations are performed using modified versions of the NATHAN and imsrg++ codes, originally developed for double-beta decay matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- For 138Ba, the ultrasoft contribution to the CP-odd electron-nucleus interaction parameter C_SP(usoft) is 61.0 (e fm)^{-1} (GCN5082), 97.0 (e fm)^{-1} (QX), and 41.7 (e fm)^{-1} (Sn100pn) for the proton contribution, with zero for the neutron contribution
- For 138Ba, the potential-region nuclear matrix element m_SP(pot,p) is -1708 fm/e (GCN5082), -1694 fm/e (QX), and -1736 fm/e (Sn100pn), while m_SP(pot,n) is 1532 fm/e, 1532 fm/e, and 1535 fm/e respectively
- The potential NMEs scale linearly as m_SP(pot,X) = -(9.74 ± 0.19) × X × μ_X (fm/e), where X is either Z (protons) or N (neutrons)
- The molecular matrix element r_mol for BaF is 4.46[18] × 10^{-21} e cm, for ThO is 1.51[9] × 10^{-20} e cm, and for HfF+ is 9.17[52] × 10^{-21} e cm
- The theoretical uncertainty from nuclear structure effects is estimated at about 30%, combining nucleus-dependent corrections (up to 20%) and higher-order chiral corrections

## Context

This work extends the analysis of paramagnetic molecular EDM experiments beyond the traditional interpretation solely in terms of the electron EDM, building on earlier work by Flambaum (2019) and Flambaum (2020) who first noted the sensitivity of paramagnetic systems to hadronic CP violation, and on the calculation by Mulder (2025) using heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory. The nuclear matrix elements computed here are distinct from the Schiff moments used in diamagnetic systems (Schiff, 1963; Flambaum, 1984) and are related to matrix elements appearing in neutrinoless double-beta decay (Agostini, 2022) and double magnetic-dipole transitions (Simkovic, 2018). The framework enables future paramagnetic EDM experiments targeting molecules such as BaF (NL-eEDM collaboration), ThO, and HfF+ to set competitive constraints on nucleon EDMs, potentially surpassing limits from neutron EDM experiments.
