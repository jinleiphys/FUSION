# Nuclear-powered X-ray millisecond pulsars

> Nuclear-powered X-ray millisecond pulsars are the third type of millisecond pulsars, which are powered by thermonuclear fusion processes. The corresponding brightness oscillations, known as burst oscillations, are observed during some thermonuclear X-ray bursts, when the burning and cooling accreted matter gives rise to an azimuthally asymmetric brightness pattern on the surface of the spinning neutron star. Apart from providing neutron star spin rates, this X-ray timing feature can be a useful tool to probe the fundamental physics of neutron star interior and surface. This chapter presents an overview of the relatively new field of nuclear-powered X-ray millisecond pulsars.

## Key claim

Nuclear-powered X-ray millisecond pulsars (NMXPs) are the third type of millisecond pulsars, powered by thermonuclear fusion processes rather than rotational kinetic energy or gravitational potential energy; the brightness oscillations (burst oscillations) observed during some thermonuclear X-ray bursts are identified with the neutron star spin frequency, but this identification relies on the asymptotic frequency measured during burst decay and has been confirmed only for sources that are also accretion-powered millisecond X-ray pulsars (AMXPs).

## Method

The author reviews the observational and theoretical development of the field of nuclear-powered X-ray millisecond pulsars over approximately 25 years, focusing on burst oscillations detected primarily with the Rossi X-ray Timing Explorer (RXTE) Proportional Counter Array (PCA). The review covers the discovery of burst oscillations, their frequency evolution and coherence properties, amplitude measurements, harmonic content, and the connection to thermonuclear X-ray burst physics including ignition conditions, nuclear reaction regimes, and flame spreading on neutron star surfaces.

## Key numbers

- Burst oscillation frequencies range from 245 Hz (IGR J17511-3057) to 620 Hz (4U 1608-522) for the 19 confirmed NMXPs listed
- Typical fractional rms amplitude of burst oscillations is 0.02-0.2, with a median of approximately 0.05
- Burst oscillation frequency typically drifts upward by 1-3 Hz during a burst, with fractional dispersion in asymptotic frequencies over several years less than 10^{-3} for multiple sources
- Coherence Q (= nu/Delta-nu) can be as high as approximately 4000
- For two intermittent AMXPs, the asymptotic frequency is 0.5 Hz (Aql X-1) and approximately 1 Hz (HETE J1900.1-2455) below the known stellar spin frequencies
- Approximately 20% of burst sources are NMXPs
- Frequency drift of approximately 3.6 Hz observed for 4U 1916-053 and approximately 5 Hz for MXB 1659-298
- Very large burst decay oscillation amplitudes (fractional rms approximately 0.4-0.5) measured for two bursts from 4U 1728-34 with NICER above 6 keV

## Context

This chapter extends the work on millisecond pulsars from spin-powered MSPs (Bhattacharya 1991) and accretion-powered MSPs (Patruno and Watts 2012) to the third class of nuclear-powered MSPs, building on the discovery of burst oscillations by Strohmayer et al. (1996) in 4U 1728-34. The field enables probing supra-nuclear density neutron star core matter (Bhattacharyya 2010) and stellar surface physics under extreme conditions, and the review identifies open questions regarding the mechanism causing burst oscillations during burst decay and the need for continued observations with AstroSat and NICER following the decommissioning of RXTE in 2012.
