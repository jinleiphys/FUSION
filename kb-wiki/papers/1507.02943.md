# Muon g-2 estimates : Can one trust Effective Lagrangians and global fits ?

> Previous studies have shown that the Hidden Local Symmetry (HLS) Model, supplied with appropriate symmetry breaking mechanisms, provides an Effective Lagrangian (BHLS) which encompasses a large number of processes within a unified framework; a global fit procedure allows for a simultaneous description of the e+ e- annihilation into the 6 final states $π^+ π^-$, $π^0 γ$, $ηγ$, $π^+ π^- π^0$, $K^+ K^-$, $K_l K_s$ and includes the dipion spectrum in the τ decay and some more light meson decay partial widths. The contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment $a_{th}$ of these annihilation channels over the range of validity of the HLS model (up to 1.05 GeV) is found much improved compared to its partner derived from integrating the measured spectra directly. However, most spectra for the process $e^+ e^- \to π^+ π^-$ undergo overall scale uncertainties which dominate the other sources, and one may suspect some bias in the dipion contribution to $a_{th}$. However, an iterated fit algorithm, shown to lead to unbiased results by a Monte Carlo study, is defined and applied succesfully to the $e^+ e^- \to π^+ π^-$ data samples from CMD2, SND, KLOE, BaBar and BESSIII. The iterated fit solution is shown to be further improved and leads to a value for $a_μ$ different from $a_{exp}$ above the 4 $σ$ level. The contribution of the $π^+ π^-$ intermediate state up to 1.05 GeV to $a_{th}$ derived from the iterated fit benefits from an uncertainty about 3 times smaller than the corresponding usual estimate. Therefore, global fit techniques are shown to work and lead to improved unbiased results.

## Key claim

The authors show that an iterated global fit procedure, applied within the Broken Hidden Local Symmetry (BHLS) Effective Lagrangian framework, removes the bias that can arise from large overall scale uncertainties in the \(e^+e^- \to \pi^+\pi^-\) data samples, and yields a value for the muon anomalous magnetic moment that deviates from the experimental value above the \(4\sigma\) level, with an uncertainty on the dipion contribution about three times smaller than the usual estimate.

## Method

The authors use the BHLS Effective Lagrangian, which incorporates symmetry breaking mechanisms, to perform a global fit of \(e^+e^-\) annihilation data into six final states (\(\pi^+\pi^-\), \(\pi^0\gamma\), \(\eta\gamma\), \(\pi^+\pi^-\pi^0\), \(K^+K^-\), \(K_LK_S\)) together with the \(\tau\) dipion spectrum and light meson decay widths. To handle the dominant overall scale uncertainties in the \(e^+e^- \to \pi^+\pi^-\) data from CMD2, SND, KLOE, BaBar, and BESSIII, they define an iterative fit algorithm where the modified covariance matrix is updated using the model prediction from the previous iteration, starting from the experimental spectrum as the initial guess.

## Key numbers

* The contribution of the \(\pi^+\pi^-\) intermediate state up to 1.05 GeV to the theoretical muon anomalous magnetic moment \(a_\mu^{\rm th}\) has an uncertainty about 3 times smaller than the corresponding usual estimate.
* The iterated fit solution leads to a value for \(a_\mu\) that deviates from the experimental value \(a_{\rm exp}\) above the \(4\sigma\) level.
* The BHLS model's range of validity is up to 1.05 GeV, which covers more than 80% of the total leading-order hadronic vacuum polarization contribution to \(a_\mu\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous BHLS-based global fit studies (Benayoun et al., 2013, 2014) by addressing the bias issue raised by D'Agostini (1994) and known in nuclear physics as the "Peelle's Pertinent Puzzle" (Peelle, 1987; Chiba, 2000). The iterative method is similar to that used by Ball et al. (2009) for parton density functions. The results confirm the tension between the Standard Model prediction and the experimental muon \(g-2\) measurement from the BNL experiment (2004, 2006), and are closer to the central value preferred by lattice QCD studies (Colangelo et al., 2011).
