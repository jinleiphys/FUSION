# Characterizing quark gluon plasma by dilepton interferometry

> The Hanbury-Brown-Twiss (HBT) radii have been calculated from the two particle correlation functions with virtual photons produced in the collisions of two nuclei at ultra-relativistic energies. We show that the variation of the HBT radii with the invariant mass of the virtual photon can be used to characterize and distinguish the hadronic as well as the partonic phase that might have produced initially in the collisions. It has been illustrated that the non-monotonic variation of the HBT radii with invariant mass provides an access to the development of collective flow in the system.

## Key claim

The variation of Hanbury-Brown-Twiss (HBT) radii extracted from dilepton correlation functions with the invariant mass of the virtual photon can characterize and distinguish the hadronic and partonic phases produced in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions, but only if the non-monotonic behavior of the radii is interpreted as reflecting the development of collective flow in the system.

## Method

The authors computed two-particle correlation functions for virtual photons (dileptons) produced in Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy using a relativistic hydrodynamic model with cylindrical symmetry and boost invariance along the longitudinal direction. The source function was related to the thermal emission rate of virtual photons per unit four-volume, and the correlation functions were evaluated for different invariant mass windows (0.3, 0.5, 0.7, 1.2, 1.6, and 2.5 GeV) as a function of the transverse momentum difference components \(q_{side}\) and \(q_{out}\). The HBT radii \(R_{side}\) and \(R_{out}\) were extracted by parameterizing the correlation function with a Gaussian form \(C_2 = 1 + \frac{\lambda}{3} \exp(-R_i^2 q_i^2)\).

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature \(T_i = 290\) MeV and proper thermalization time \(\tau_i = 0.6\) fm/c were used, constrained by hadronic multiplicity \(dN/dy \sim T_i^3 \tau_i\).
- Chemical freeze-out temperature \(T_{ch} = 170\) MeV and kinetic freeze-out temperature \(T_{fo} = 120\) MeV were fixed by particle ratios and the slope of hadron \(k_T\) spectra.
- Invariant mass windows evaluated: \(\langle M \rangle = 0.3, 0.5, 0.7, 1.2, 1.6,\) and \(2.5\) GeV.
- A dip in \(R_{side}\) was observed around the \(\rho\)-mass region (\(\langle M \rangle \sim m_\rho\)), and this dip vanished when contributions from \(\rho\) and \(\omega\) mesons were switched off or when radial velocity \(v_r = 0\) was set.

## Context

This work extends the application of HBT interferometry from real photons to virtual photons (dileptons), building on prior studies of Bose-Einstein correlations for electromagnetic probes (HBT, HBTlep). The use of the additional kinematic variable (invariant mass \(M\)) for dileptons offers an advantage over real photon interferometry, as the \(k_T\)-integrated \(M\) spectra remain unaffected by radial flow, unlike the \(k_T\) spectra. The results enable future experimental studies at RHIC and LHC to use dilepton interferometry as a signal of the phase transition between QGP and hadronic matter, complementing earlier work on collective flow by Hirano (2002) and lattice QCD equations of state by the MILC Collaboration.
