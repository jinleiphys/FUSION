# Consistent Construction of Realistic One-Body Density Matrix in Nuclei

> A phenomenological method based on the natural orbital representation is applied to construct the ground state one-body density matrix which describes correctly both density and momentum distributions in $^{4}He$, $^{16}O$ and $^{40}Ca$ nuclei. The parameters of the matrix are fixed by a best fit to the experimental density distribution and to the correlated nucleon momentum distribution. The method allows the natural orbitals, the occupation probabilities and the depletion of the Fermi sea to be obtained. Ground-state characteristics of $^{4}He$, $^{16}O$ and $^{40}Ca$ nuclei, such as rms radii and mean kinetic energies are calculated, as well.

# Consistent Construction of Realistic One-Body Density Matrix in Nuclei

## Key claim
A phenomenological method based on natural orbital representation can construct a ground-state one-body density matrix that simultaneously reproduces both the experimental density distribution and the correlated nucleon momentum distribution for 4He, 16O, and 40Ca, but only when the natural orbitals are expanded in square-well single-particle wave functions rather than harmonic-oscillator functions; the harmonic-oscillator basis, used in prior work by Antonov et al. (1993, 1994), fails to describe the high-momentum components of the momentum distribution.

## Method
The authors expand the radial part of natural orbitals in a truncated basis of three orthonormal single-particle wave functions from one of three potentials: harmonic oscillator, infinite square well, or modified harmonic oscillator (which includes a repulsive 1/r² term). The occupation numbers, expansion coefficients, and potential parameters are fitted by minimizing a combined chi-squared function that simultaneously compares the calculated local density to experimental data from Sick et al. (1974, 1975) and the calculated nucleon momentum distribution to experimental y-scaling data for 4He (from Ciofi degli Atti et al.) or to Jastrow correlation method calculations for 16O and 40Ca (from Stoitsov et al., 1993). The minimization is performed using the MINUIT program with 12-20 fitting parameters depending on the nucleus.

## Key numbers
- Fermi sea depletion in 4He: 11.3% (square-well basis), 13.2% (modified harmonic-oscillator basis), 18.6% (harmonic-oscillator basis)
- Fermi sea depletion in 16O: 12.4% (square-well), 16.6% (modified harmonic-oscillator), 18% (harmonic-oscillator)
- Fermi sea depletion in 40Ca: 15% (square-well), 20% (harmonic-oscillator)
- Reference depletion values from Jastrow natural orbital representation: 6% for 4He, 4% for 16O, 6.3% for 40Ca; experimental depletion for 40Ca is 9.4% (from Muther et al., 1994)
- The minimal value of the combined fit function F is achieved using square-well single-particle wave functions for all three nuclei

## Context
This work extends the phenomenological method of Antonov et al. (1993, 1994) for constructing the one-body density matrix in 16O and 40Ca, which fitted only the density distribution and did not constrain the momentum distribution. The authors show that the earlier method, using harmonic-oscillator basis functions, cannot reproduce the high-momentum tail arising from short-range and tensor nucleon-nucleon correlations. The new method enables consistent calculation of ground-state properties including rms radii and mean kinetic energies while accounting for correlation effects, and provides natural orbitals and occupation probabilities that can be compared with Jastrow correlation method results from Stoitsov et al. (1993) and with experimental depletion data.
