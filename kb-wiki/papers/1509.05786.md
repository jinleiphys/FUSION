# The curvature of the freeze-out line in heavy ion collisions

> We calculate the mean and variance of net-baryon number and net-electric charge distributions from Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) using a next-to-leading order Taylor expansion in terms of temperature and chemical potentials. We compare these expansions with experimental data from STAR and PHENIX, determine the freeze-out temperature in the limit of vanishing baryon chemical potential, and, for the first time, constrain the curvature of the freeze-out line through a direct comparison between experimental data on net-charge fluctuations and a QCD calculation. We obtain a bound on the curvature coefficient, kappa_2^f < 0.011, that is compatible with lattice QCD results on the curvature of the QCD transition line.

## Key claim
The curvature of the freeze-out line in heavy ion collisions is small and consistent with the curvature of the QCD crossover line, but the bound obtained, κ₂^f < 0.011, is an upper limit that depends on the transverse momentum cuts used in the experimental data; the preliminary STAR data with a larger p_t range give a much smaller slope that is consistent with a vanishing curvature.

## Method
The authors performed a next-to-leading order Taylor expansion of the QCD pressure in terms of temperature and chemical potentials to calculate the mean and variance of net-baryon number and net-electric charge distributions. They compared these lattice QCD results with experimental data from STAR and PHENIX on net-proton and net-charge fluctuations, fitting the ratio Σ_r^{QB} as a function of (R_{12}^B)^2 to extract the freeze-out temperature at zero baryon chemical potential and the curvature coefficient κ₂^f. The analysis used (2+1)-flavor HISQ action lattice calculations with physical strange quark mass and two light quark masses corresponding to pion masses of about 160 MeV and 140 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Bound on the curvature coefficient: κ₂^f < 0.011 from the preliminary STAR2.0 data set.
- Freeze-out temperature at vanishing baryon chemical potential: T_{f,0} = 147(2) MeV from the STAR2.0 data set.
- Freeze-out temperature at vanishing baryon chemical potential: T_{f,0} = 145(2) MeV from the published STAR0.8 data set.
- Freeze-out temperature at vanishing baryon chemical potential: T_{f,0} = 155(4) MeV from the PHENIX/STAR2.0 combination.
- Curvature coefficient from the published STAR0.8 data: κ₂^f = -0.073(16).
- Curvature coefficient from the preliminary STAR2.0 data: κ₂^f = -0.001(12).
- Curvature coefficient from the PHENIX/STAR2.0 combination: κ₂^f = -0.056(67).
- Using r = 0.45 instead of r = 0.4 for the STAR2.0 data set shifts the curvature coefficient to κ₂^f = 0.004 and decreases T_{f,0} by 3 MeV.
- The QCD transition line curvature from prior lattice calculations: κ₂^B ≈ 0.007 (Kaczmarek 2011, Endrodi 2011) and κ₂^B ≈ 0.015-0.02 (Bonati 2015, Bellwied 2015, Cea 2015).

## Context
This work extends prior determinations of the freeze-out line from statistical hadronization models, such as Cleymans (2005) who found κ₂^f = 0.023(3), and Andronic (2005) who found a parametrization favoring κ₂^f ≈ 0. It provides the first direct comparison between experimental data on net-charge fluctuations and a QCD calculation to constrain the freeze-out line curvature, putting this determination on a par with lattice QCD results for the QCD transition line curvature. The results enable future studies of the proximity of the freeze-out line to the QCD crossover line, which is a prerequisite for exploring critical behavior near a possible critical point in the QCD phase diagram.
