# Light Front Treatment of Nuclei and Deep Inelastic Scattering

> A light front treatment of the nuclear wave function is developed and applied, using the mean field approximation, to infinite nuclear matter. The nuclear mesons are shown to carry about a third of the nuclear plus momentum; but their momentum distribution has support only at for vanishingly small values of the plus momentum, so the mesons do not contribute to nuclear deep inelastic scattering. This zero mode effect occurs because the meson fields are independent of space-time position.

# Light Front Treatment of Nuclei and Deep Inelastic Scattering

## Key claim

Nuclear mesons in the mean field approximation carry about one third of the nuclear plus momentum, but because the meson fields are constant in space-time (zero modes), their momentum distribution has support only at vanishingly small plus momentum, so they do not contribute to nuclear deep inelastic scattering. This result vitiates the predictive power of the momentum sum rule for models in which the mean field plays an important role.

## Method

The author uses a Lagrangian with nucleons, scalar mesons, and vector mesons, evaluated in the mean field approximation for infinite nuclear matter, and quantizes the system on the light front. The field equations are solved with the meson fields replaced by their expectation values, yielding plane-wave nucleon states with a shifted effective mass, and the plus momentum distribution is computed from the expectation values of the energy-momentum tensor components T++ and T+-.

## Key numbers

- Nucleons carry only 65% of the nuclear plus momentum; mesons carry the remaining 35%.
- The parameters used are g_v^2 M^2 / m_v^2 = 195.9 and g_s^2 M^2 / m_s^2 = 267.1, chosen to give a binding energy per particle of 15.75 MeV at Fermi momentum k_F = 1.42 fm^{-1}.
- The effective nucleon mass in the medium is M + g_s phi = 0.56 M.
- The average value of the dimensionless variable y = k^+ / (M - 15.75 MeV) is 0.65, compared to 0.91 computed for nuclear matter using equal-time quantization including correlations (Miller, 1997, citing a prior calculation).

## Context

This work extends the light front quantization approach of Soper (1971) and Yan and collaborators (1973) to nuclear matter including both scalar and vector mesons, building on earlier work by Glazek and Shakin (1988) who treated only scalar mesons. The result contradicts the conventional nuclear binding explanation of the EMC effect, in which pions (and other mesons) are expected to carry significant plus momentum and enhance the nuclear antiquark distribution; a nuclear Drell-Yan experiment (Alde et al., 1990) found no such enhancement, leading to questions about the pion as the dominant carrier of the nuclear force. The present work shows that in mean-field-type models, mesons can contribute to the momentum sum rule without being accessible in deep inelastic scattering, which complicates the interpretation of the EMC effect and motivates future work including fluctuations or finite-nucleus effects.
