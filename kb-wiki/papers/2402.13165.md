# Assessing Correlated Truncation Errors in Modern Nucleon-Nucleon Potentials

> We test the BUQEYE model of correlated effective field theory (EFT) truncation errors on Reinert, Krebs, and Epelbaum's semi-local momentum-space implementation of the chiral EFT ($χ$EFT) expansion of the nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential. This Bayesian model hypothesizes that dimensionless coefficient functions extracted from the order-by-order corrections to NN observables can be treated as draws from a Gaussian process (GP). We combine a variety of graphical and statistical diagnostics to assess when predicted observables have a $χ$EFT convergence pattern consistent with the hypothesized GP statistical model. Our conclusions are: First, the BUQEYE model is generally applicable to the potential investigated here, which enables statistically principled estimates of the impact of higher EFT orders on observables. Second, parameters defining the extracted coefficients such as the expansion parameter $Q$ must be well chosen for the coefficients to exhibit a regular convergence pattern -- a property we exploit to obtain posterior distributions for such quantities. Third, the assumption of GP stationarity across lab energy and scattering angle is not generally met; this necessitates adjustments in future work. We provide a workflow and interpretive guide for our analysis framework, and show what can be inferred about probability distributions for $Q$, the EFT breakdown scale $Λ_b$, the scale associated with soft physics in the $χ$EFT potential $m_{\rm eff}$, and the GP hyperparameters. All our results can be reproduced using a publicly available Jupyter notebook, which can be straightforwardly modified to analyze other $χ$EFT NN potentials.

## Key claim

The BUQEYE Gaussian process model of correlated EFT truncation errors is generally applicable to the semi-local momentum-space chiral potential of Reinert, Krebs, and Epelbaum, but only when the expansion parameter \(Q\), the effective soft scale \(m_{\rm eff}\), and the input space are carefully chosen; the assumption of GP stationarity across lab energy and scattering angle is not generally met, necessitating future adjustments.

## Method

The authors extracted dimensionless coefficient functions \(c_n(x)\) from order-by-order predictions of neutron-proton scattering observables (total cross section, differential cross section, and spin observables) computed with the 500 MeV cutoff SMS potential of Reinert, Krebs, and Epelbaum (2017). They tested whether these coefficient functions behave as independent and identically distributed draws from a Gaussian process with zero mean, a squared exponential correlation function, and unknown variance and length-scale hyperparameters, using a combination of visual inspection, Mahalanobis distance tests, pivoted Cholesky decompositions, and credible interval diagnostics. The analysis explored different parametrizations of the expansion parameter \(Q\) (smooth-maximum form \(Q_{\rm smax}\) and sum form \(Q_{\rm sum}\)), different choices of the characteristic momentum \(p\) (relative momentum \(p_{\rm rel}\), momentum transfer \(q_{\rm CM}\), and a smooth maximum of the two), and different input-space variables (lab energy \(E_{\rm lab}\) versus \(p_{\rm rel}\), and scattering angle \(\theta\) versus \(-\cos\theta\) versus \(q_{\rm CM}\)).

## Key numbers

* The conventional pion mass value \(m_\pi = 138\) MeV was tested as the soft scale \(m_{\rm eff}\), but an effective value of 200-225 MeV (extracted from credible-interval plots in Epelbaum (2019)) produced coefficient functions with stronger evidence of naturalness and stationarity.
* The breakdown scale \(\Lambda_b\) was set to 600 MeV for the coefficient extraction shown in the figures, with the expansion parameter defined so that \(Q(p = \Lambda_b, m_{\rm eff}) = 1\).
* The smooth-maximum expansion parameter \(Q_{\rm smax}\) used an exponent \(i = 8\) in the formula \(Q_{\rm smax}(p, m_{\rm eff}) = (p^i + m_{\rm eff}^i) / [\Lambda_b (p^{i-1} + m_{\rm eff}^{i-1})]\).
* The sum-form expansion parameter was defined as \(Q_{\rm sum}(p, m_{\rm eff}) = (p + m_{\rm eff}) / (\Lambda_b + m_{\rm eff})\).
* The GP variance prior was an inverse chi-squared distribution with \(\nu_0 = 1\) and \(\tau_0^2 = 1^2\); the length-scale prior was uniform over positive values (improper).

## Context

This work extends the BUQEYE correlated truncation error model, previously applied to infinite nuclear matter (Drischler, 2020), nucleon-nucleus elastic scattering (Baker, 2021), and other reactions, by testing it on the semi-local momentum-space chiral NN potential of Reinert, Krebs, and Epelbaum (2017). The paper provides a workflow and diagnostic framework for assessing whether a given chiral EFT potential produces coefficient functions consistent with the GP model, and demonstrates that the values of the expansion parameter \(Q\), the breakdown scale \(\Lambda_b\), and the effective soft scale \(m_{\rm eff}\) can be inferred from the convergence pattern. The results enable statistically principled estimates of higher-order truncation errors for NN observables, but also reveal that the stationarity assumption fails across the full energy and angle domain, motivating future development of nonstationary GP models.
