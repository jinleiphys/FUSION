# Confronting new NICER mass-radius measurements with phase transition in dense matter and twin compact stars

> The (re)analysis of data on the X-ray emitting pulsars PSR J0030+0451 and J0740+6620, as well as new results on PSR J0437-4715 and J1231-1411, are confronted with the predictions of the equation of state (EoS) models allowing for strong first-order phase transition for the mass-radius ($M$-$R$) diagram. We use models that are based on a covariant density functional (CDF) EoS for nucleonic matter at low densities and a quark matter EoS, parameterized by the speed of sound, at higher densities. To account for the variations in the ellipses for PSR J0030+0451 obtained from different analyses, we examined three scenarios to assess their consistency with our models, focusing particularly on the potential formation of twin stars. We found that in two scenarios, where the ellipses for PSR J0030+0451 and J0437-4715 with masses close to the canonical mass $\sim 1.4\,M_{\odot}$ are significantly separated, our models allow for the presence of twin stars as a natural explanation for potential differences in the radii of these stars.

## Key claim

The paper finds that in two of three scenarios (A and C) where the mass-radius ellipses for the canonical-mass pulsars PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0437-4715 are significantly separated, hybrid star models with a strong first-order phase transition can naturally explain the potential radius difference via twin star configurations, but this conclusion depends on which analysis model for PSR J0030+0451 is adopted.

## Method

The authors construct hybrid star equations of state by combining a covariant density functional nucleonic EoS at low densities with a constant-speed-of-sound quark matter EoS at high densities, using four representative nucleonic models (DDLS-40, DDLS-60, DDLS-80, DDLS-100) and two values of the squared speed of sound in quark matter (1 and 2/3). They then compute mass-radius relations and compare them with NICER observational constraints for four pulsars (PSR J0030+0451, J0437-4715, J0740+6620, J1231-1411) under three scenarios that differ in which analysis model is used for PSR J0030+0451. The models are further validated against tidal deformability constraints from GW170817 and moment of inertia constraints from PSR J0737-3039 A.

## Key numbers

- Transition mass of the hadronic branch (M^H_max) ranges from 1.2 to 1.5 solar masses across models
- Maximum mass of the hybrid branch (M^Q_max) ranges from 2.0 to 2.3 solar masses
- For the DDLS100 model with c_s^2 = 1 and transition energy density 300.570 MeV/fm^3, the twin mass range ΔM_twin = 0.0748 M_sun and radius difference ΔR_twin = 1.84 km
- For the DDLS40 model with c_s^2 = 1 and transition energy density 310.074 MeV/fm^3, ΔM_twin = 0.0187 M_sun and ΔR_twin = 0.61 km
- For c_s^2 = 2/3, the DDLS100 model at transition energy density 300.570 MeV/fm^3 gives ΔM_twin = 0.0225 M_sun and ΔR_twin = 0.92 km
- The moment of inertia constraint for a 1.338 M_sun star is 0.91 ≤ I_1.338 ≤ 2.16 in units of 10^45 g cm^2 (from PSR J0737-3039 A)
- The tidal deformability constraint for a 1.362 M_sun star comes from GW170817 analysis

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous hybrid star models (Li et al., 2024; Li et al., 2023a; Li et al., 2023b) by confronting them with the newest NICER analyses from Vinciguerra et al. (2024), Salmi et al. (2024a, 2024b), and Choudhury et al. (2024). It builds on the twin star concept developed by Glendenning (2000), Zdunik (2013), Benic (2015), and Alford (2017), and tests whether the observed radius differences between canonical-mass pulsars can be explained by a strong first-order hadron-quark phase transition. The results enable future combined analyses of X-ray pulsar data and gravitational wave observations to distinguish between purely hadronic and hybrid star scenarios.
