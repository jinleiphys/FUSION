# Nonlinear dynamics of giant resonances in atomic nuclei

> The dynamics of monopole giant resonances in nuclei is analyzed in the time-dependent relativistic mean-field model. The phase spaces of isoscalar and isovector collective oscillations are reconstructed from the time-series of dynamical variables that characterize the proton and neutron density distributions. The analysis of the resulting recurrence plots and correlation dimensions indicate regular motion for the isoscalar mode, and chaotic dynamics for the isovector oscillations. Information-theoretic functionals identify and quantify the nonlinear dynamics of giant resonances in quantum systems that have spatial as well as temporal structure.

# Nonlinear dynamics of giant resonances in atomic nuclei

## Key claim

The authors claim that isoscalar monopole giant resonances in 208Pb exhibit regular, periodic motion while isovector monopole oscillations display chaotic dynamics, based on analysis of time-series from time-dependent relativistic mean-field calculations. This conclusion is conditional on the specific choice of the NL1 effective interaction and the assumption that the collective dynamics of monopole resonances is representative of higher multipole oscillations.

## Method

The authors performed fully time-dependent relativistic mean-field calculations for 208Pb, starting from self-consistent ground-state solutions and applying initial monopole deformations to excite isoscalar (proton and neutron densities in phase) and isovector (proton and neutron densities out of phase) oscillations. From the time-series of the monopole moments, they reconstructed phase spaces using time-delay embedding, determined embedding dimensions via false nearest neighbors analysis, and computed recurrence plots and correlation dimensions to characterize the dynamics. They also evaluated information-theoretic functionals including information entropy from one-body densities and conditional entropy from two-body densities.

## Key numbers

- Experimental isoscalar giant monopole resonance energy in 208Pb: 13.7 ± 0.3 MeV
- Experimental isovector giant monopole resonance energy in 208Pb: 26 ± 3 MeV
- Calculated isoscalar mode frequency: approximately 11 MeV (single dominant mode)
- Calculated isovector mode main peaks: 25–30 MeV region
- Time delay from first zero of autocorrelation function for isoscalar mode: 27 fm/c
- Time delay from first zero of autocorrelation function for isovector mode: 13 fm/c
- Embedding dimension for isoscalar mode: d_E = 3
- Embedding dimension for isovector mode: d_E = 4
- Correlation dimension for isoscalar mode: D_2 = 2 (integer, indicating regular dynamics)
- Correlation dimension for isovector mode: fractional value between 2 and 3 (indicating chaotic dynamics)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Vretenar et al., 1997) on nonlinear dynamics of giant monopole resonances using time-dependent relativistic mean-field theory, applying more sophisticated nonlinear analysis tools including recurrence plots, correlation dimensions, and information-theoretic functionals. The results contradict the expectation that collective nuclear motion is always regular due to mean-field averaging, showing that isovector modes can become chaotic. This enables future studies of nonlinear dynamics in more complex excitations involving spin and isospin degrees of freedom, such as Gamow-Teller resonances, and provides a framework for analyzing spatial correlations in finite quantum systems exhibiting temporal chaos.
