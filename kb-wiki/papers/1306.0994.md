# Phase Evolution and Freeze-out within Alternative Scenarios of Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Global evolution of the matter in relativistic collisions of heavy nuclei and the resulting global freeze-out parameters are analyzed in a wide range of incident energies 2.7 GeV $\le \sqrt{s_{NN}}\le$ 39 GeV. The analysis is performed within the three-fluid model employing three different equations of state (EoS): a purely hadronic EoS, an EoS with the first-order phase transition and that with a smooth crossover transition. Global freeze-out parameters deduced from experimental data within the statistical model are well reproduced within the crossover scenario. The 1st-order-transition scenario is slightly less successful. The worst reproduction is found within the purely hadronic scenario. These findings make a link between the EoS and results of the statistical model, and indicate that deconfinement onset occurs at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}\gsim$ 5 GeV.

## Key claim

The crossover equation of state (EoS) reproduces the global freeze-out parameters deduced from experimental data within the statistical model better than the first-order-transition EoS, and both deconfinement-transition scenarios are significantly more successful than a purely hadronic EoS; however, the crossover EoS used is very smooth and contradicts lattice QCD results, so a more realistic EoS is needed for better reproduction.

## Method

The analysis was performed within the three-fluid (3FD) model, which simulates the collision from the stage of cold nuclei up to freeze-out using three different equations of state: a purely hadronic EoS, an EoS with a first-order phase transition, and an EoS with a smooth crossover transition. The freeze-out criterion was a fixed energy density of 0.4 GeV/fm³, and the model parameters (friction, freeze-out energy density, and fireball formation time) were fitted to reproduce net-proton rapidity distributions and mid-rapidity pion densities at three incident energies (√sNN = 4.9, 17.3, and 62.4 GeV). Phase-space trajectories of the matter in a central box (|x| ≤ 2 fm, |y| ≤ 2 fm, |z| ≤ γcm 2 fm) were studied for central collisions of Au+Au (b = 2 fm) and Pb+Pb (b = 2.4 fm) over the energy range 2.7 GeV ≤ √sNN ≤ 39 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out energy density: εfrz = 0.4 GeV/fm³, chosen for best reproduction of secondary particle yields (mid-rapidity pion densities) for all scenarios.
- Energy range analyzed: 2.7 GeV ≤ √sNN ≤ 39 GeV, covering AGS, SPS, and RHIC beam-energy scan programs.
- Deconfinement onset indicated at √sNN ≳ 5 GeV (top AGS energies).
- At √sNN ≤ 3.9 GeV, the baryon-free fluid contributes less than 10% to midrapidity pion values; at √sNN ≥ 6.4 GeV, this contribution exceeds 25%.
- For the hadronic scenario at √sNN = 17.3 GeV, the delay time for baryon-free fluid production is 2 fm/c; for the 2-phase and crossover EoS, the delay is 0.17 fm/c.
- At √sNN > 12 GeV, the freeze-out time span in the central box is of order 1 fm/c in the c.m. frame.
- The central-box matter spends approximately 25% of the expansion time in the mixed phase for the first-order-transition scenario.

## Context

This work extends the three-fluid dynamics simulations of Ivanov (2013) on baryon stopping and the analysis of hadron yields by Ivanov (2013), which showed that the hadronic scenario fails to reproduce experimental yields of antibaryons starting at √sNN ≥ 6.4 GeV and all species above √sNN > 17.3 GeV. The results link the equation of state to the freeze-out parameters of the statistical model as parametrized by Andronic (2008), and indicate that deconfinement-transition scenarios are preferred over the purely hadronic EoS of the earlier 3FD simulations. The findings enable future work with more realistic equations of state that better match lattice QCD results, such as those from Aoki (2006).
