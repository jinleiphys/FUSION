# Converging sequences in the ab initio no-core shell model

> We demonstrate the existence of multiple converging sequences in the ab initio no-core shell model. By examining the underlying theory of effective operators, we expose the physical foundations for the alternative pathways to convergence. This leads us to propose a revised strategy for evaluating effective interactions for $A$-body calculations in restricted model spaces. We suggest that this strategy is particularly useful for applications to nuclear processes in which states of both parities are used simultaneously, such as for transition rates. We demonstrate the utility of our strategy with large-scale calculations in light nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that multiple converging sequences exist in the ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM), and proposes a revised strategy for evaluating effective interactions where the same effective Hamiltonian is used for adjacent model spaces (e.g., \(N_\mathrm{max,eff} = N_\mathrm{max} + 1\) for natural-parity states) rather than the conventional choice of \(N_\mathrm{max,eff} = N_\mathrm{max}\). This strategy converges to the same exact results as the traditional approach but provides faster convergence of the relative energies of opposite-parity states.

## Method

The authors perform NCSM calculations at the two-body cluster level for \(^6\)Li and \(^9\)Be using realistic nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions (CD-Bonn 2000 and JISP16). They compare two strategies for constructing the effective Hamiltonian: the conventional choice where the effective interaction cutoff equals the model space cutoff (\(N_\mathrm{max,eff} = N_\mathrm{max}\)), and a revised choice where \(N_\mathrm{max,eff} = N_\mathrm{max} + 1\) for natural-parity states while using \(N_\mathrm{max,eff} = N_\mathrm{max}\) for unnatural-parity states. The many-body calculations are performed using two independent shell-model codes (Antoine and MFD) with m-scheme Lanczos diagonalization, including a Lawson projection term to separate center-of-mass motion.

## Key numbers

* Ground-state binding energy of \(^6\)Li with CD-Bonn 2000 at \(\hbar\Omega = 10\) MeV: difference between the two strategies in the \(N_\mathrm{max} = 16\) model space is less than 70 keV (approximately 0.2% of total binding energy)
* Ground-state binding energy of \(^9\)Be with CD-Bonn 2000 at \(\hbar\Omega = 12\) MeV: difference between the two strategies in the \(N_\mathrm{max} = 10\) model space is 350 keV (less than 0.7% of total binding energy)
* Constrained exponential fit to \(^9\)Be ground state (\(3/2_\mathrm{gs}^-\)) binding energies from both strategies yields a common asymptote of -51.40 MeV with uncertainty of about 200 keV
* Excitation energy of the first unnatural-parity state (\(1/2_1^+\)) in \(^9\)Be: common asymptote from constrained fit is \(E_x(1/2_1^+) = 2.10\) MeV
* For \(^6\)Li with JISP16 interaction: constrained exponential fit to eight sequences at different HO frequencies (12.5-30 MeV) yields a common asymptote of -31.33 MeV with uncertainty of about 100 keV
* Maximum model space dimensions: \(d_P = 7.9 \times 10^8\) for \(^6\)Li (up to \(N_\mathrm{max} = 16\)) and \(d_P = 5.4 \times 10^8\) for \(^9\)Be (up to \(N_\mathrm{max} = 10\))

## Context

This work extends the NCSM formalism developed by Navratil et al. (2000-2001) and the effective interaction theory of Da Providencia and Shakin (1964) and Lee, Suzuki, and Okamoto (1980-1994). The revised strategy was already utilized in two recent papers applying the NCSM to heavier systems with A = 47-49 (Vary et al., 2006). The approach enables improved calculations of electromagnetic transitions between states of opposite parity, particularly for low-energy reactions such as electric dipole radiative capture processes, and provides a method for estimating uncertainties through constrained exponential fits to multiple converging sequences.
