# Bose-Einstein Condensation of Bound Pairs of Relativistic Fermions in a Magnetic Field

> The Bose-Einstein condensation of bound pairs made of equally and oppositely charged fermions in a magnetic field is investigated using a relativistic model. The Gaussian fluctuations have been taken into account in order to study the spectrum of bound pairs in the strong coupling region. We found, in weak coupling reagion, the condensation temperature increases with an increasing magnetic field displaying the magnetic catalysis effect. In strong coupling region, the inverse magnetic catalysis appears when the magnetic field is low and is replaced by the usual magnetic catalysis effect when magnetic field is sufficiently high, in contrast to the nonrelativistic case where the inverse magnetic catalysis prevails in strong coupling region regardless of the strength of the magnetic field. The resulting response to the magnetic field is the consequence of the competition between the dimensional reduction by Landau orbitals in pairing dynamics and the anisotropy of the kinetic spectrum of the bound pairs. We thus conclude that dimensional reduction dominates in weak domain and strong coupling one except the small magnetic field region, where the enhanced fluctuations dominates.

## Key claim

The paper finds that for relativistic fermions in a magnetic field, the Bose-Einstein condensation temperature of bound pairs shows a non-monotonic dependence on magnetic field strength in the strong coupling region: inverse magnetic catalysis appears at low magnetic fields but is replaced by magnetic catalysis at sufficiently high fields, in contrast to the nonrelativistic case where inverse magnetic catalysis prevails regardless of field strength.

## Method

The authors use a relativistic four-fermion model with a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to introduce a bosonic pairing field, then compute the thermodynamic potential at mean-field level and include Gaussian fluctuations around the trivial saddle point. The bound pair spectrum is extracted from the pole of the inverse propagator \(\Gamma^{-1}(\omega,\mathbf{p})\) using the proper-time representation of Dirac propagators in a constant magnetic field, with Pauli-Villars regularization to handle divergences. The BEC critical temperature is determined by solving the number density equation including the bosonic contribution from the bound pair pole.

## Key numbers

* The bound pair low-energy spectrum is \(\omega = (p_3^2 + \kappa \mathbf{p}_\perp^2)/(4\bar{\mu})\), where \(\kappa = b/a \le 1\) is the anisotropicity factor and \(2\bar{\mu}\) is the rest energy of a bound pair.
* The condensation temperature at sufficiently low density is \(T_C = [\kappa n / (2\zeta(3/2))]^{2/3} (\pi/\bar{\mu})\), with \(\zeta(3/2)\) the Riemann zeta function.
* The dimensionless critical temperature is \(t_C \equiv T_C/T_C^0 = \kappa^{2/3} (\bar{\mu}_0/\bar{\mu})\), where \(\bar{\mu}_0\) is the solution of the gap equation at zero magnetic field.
* The critical coupling constant for bound state formation at zero magnetic field is \(1/(G_R^C m^2) = (1/(2\pi^2))\ln\Lambda^2 + 1/\pi^2\), where \(\Lambda\) is the UV cutoff.

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic analysis of Feng (2015) to relativistic fermions, where the bound pair mass \(\bar{\mu}\) becomes magnetic field dependent in addition to the anisotropicity factor \(\kappa\). The study is motivated by the inverse magnetic catalysis puzzle in QCD, where lattice simulations show the chiral critical temperature decreases with increasing magnetic field (latticetemp, 2010s), contrary to the magnetic catalysis effect predicted by Klimenko and Miransky (1980s). The results provide an analytically tractable model for understanding the competition between dimensional reduction from Landau orbitals and fluctuation enhancement from bosonic anisotropy, which is relevant for heavy ion collisions and neutron star physics.
