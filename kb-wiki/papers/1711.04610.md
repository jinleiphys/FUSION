# Matching polynomial tails to the cut-off Woods-Saxon potential

> Cutting off the tail of the Woods-Saxon and generalized Woods-Saxon potentials changes the distribution of the poles of the $S$-matrix considerably. Here we modify the tail of the cut-off Woods-Saxon (CWS) and cut-off generalized Woods-Saxon (CGWS) potentials by attaching Hermite polynomial tails to them beyond the cut. The tails reach the zero value more or less smoothly at the finite ranges of the potential. Reflections of the resonant wave functions can take place at different distances. The starting points of the pole trajectories have been reproduced not only for the real values and the moduli of the starting points but also for the imaginary parts.

# Matching polynomial tails to the cut-off Woods-Saxon potential

## Key claim
By attaching Hermite polynomial tails to cut-off Woods-Saxon (CWS) and cut-off generalized Woods-Saxon (CGWS) potentials, the starting points of the pole trajectories can be reproduced not only for the real values and moduli but also for the imaginary parts, provided the smoothness parameters at the cut and at the range are properly chosen. The method works for the l=0 case where analytical solutions exist.

## Method
The authors modified the tails of CWS and CGWS potentials by attaching polynomial tails (Hermite-type polynomials) beyond the cut-off radius R_f, extending to a finite range R_max. They characterized the smoothness at the joining point R_f by an integer nu and at the outer range R_max by an integer mu, constructing nine potential variants (f^{nu H mu}) with polynomial tails of orders 2 through 5. Using the new JOZSO program, they calculated S-matrix pole positions for the l=0 case of a 56Fe system and fitted linear polynomials to the moduli |k_n| and to the imaginary parts k^I_n as functions of n and ln(n), respectively, to extract the effective range R and the smoothness parameter sigma.

## Key numbers
- Potential parameters: V_0 = 47.78 MeV, a = 0.6 fm, R = 4.92 fm (for 56Fe system)
- Cut-off radius R_f = 8.5 fm and range R_max = 10 fm used in primary calculations
- For the CGWS potential with V_1 = -200 MeV and nu=3, the extracted ranges R_A (from group A poles) ranged from 4.45 to 4.87 fm across different tail variants, close to the potential radius R = 4.92 fm
- The sum R_A + R_C ranged from 13.31 to 17.90 fm, deviating from R_max by less than 1 fm in all cases
- For the f^{3H3} potential with R_f = 17 fm and R_max = 18 fm, R_A + R_C = 17.90 fm, within 0.1 fm of R_max
- The extracted sigma values from fitting imaginary parts approached the expected sigma = mu values (1, 2, or 3) as the lower cut m_s increased

## Context
This work extends the analysis of S-matrix pole distributions in finite-range potentials previously studied by the same group (Sa16) for CWS and CGWS potentials with sharp cut-offs. In that prior work, three groups of poles (A, B, C) were identified corresponding to reflections at the potential radius R, the cut-off radius R_max, and the difference R_max - R, respectively. The present paper shows that attaching polynomial tails introduces a fourth group (D) when the joining at R_f is not smooth (nu=1 or 2), and that smooth joining (nu=3) eliminates this unphysical group. The improved JOZSO program (No17) enables accurate extraction of the imaginary parts of pole trajectories, confirming the theoretical relation of Newton (1982) for the sigma parameter, which earlier programs (ANTI, GAMOW) could not achieve. This enables more reliable construction of Berggren ensembles for Gamow shell model calculations of drip-line nuclei.
