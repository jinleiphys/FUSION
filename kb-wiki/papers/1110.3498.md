# The phase structure of a chiral model with dilatons in hot and dense matter

> We explore the phase structure of a chiral model of constituent quarks and gluons implementing scale symmetry breaking at finite temperature and chemical potential. In this model the chiral dynamics is intimately linked to the trace anomaly saturated by a dilaton field. The thermodynamics is governed by two condensates, thermal expectation values of sigma and dilaton fields, which are the order parameters responsible for the phase transitions associated with the chiral and scale symmetries. Within the mean field approximation, we find that increasing temperature a system experiences a chiral phase transition and then a first-order phase transition of partial scale symmetry restoration characterized by a melting gluon-condensate takes place at a higher temperature. There exists a region at finite chemical potential where the scale symmetry remains dynamically broken while the chiral symmetry is restored. We also give a brief discussion on the sigma-meson mass constrained from Lattice QCD.

## Key claim

Within a mean-field chiral model of constituent quarks and gluons that implements scale symmetry breaking via a dilaton field, the chiral phase transition occurs at a lower temperature than a first-order phase transition of partial scale symmetry restoration (characterized by a melting gluon condensate), and there exists a region at finite chemical potential where scale symmetry remains dynamically broken while chiral symmetry is restored, but this phase structure depends crucially on the vacuum sigma-meson mass, with a heavy sigma meson (∼1 GeV) being favored by lattice QCD constraints on the thermal gluon condensate.

## Method

The authors construct a two-flavor linear sigma model coupled to a dilaton field χ representing the gluon condensate, with constituent quarks and massive constituent gluons. They apply the mean-field approximation to compute the thermodynamic potential at finite temperature T and quark chemical potential μ, solving coupled gap equations for the thermal expectation values ⟨σ⟩ and ⟨χ⟩. The model parameters are fixed using vacuum pion mass (138 MeV), pion decay constant (93 MeV), vacuum energy density (0.76 GeV fm⁻³), and vacuum glueball mass (1.7 GeV), while the sigma mass mσ is varied between 0.6 and 1.2 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum sigma mass mσ varied in the range 0.6–1.2 GeV; pion mass mπ = 138 MeV; pion decay constant fπ = 93 MeV.
- Vacuum energy density ℰ = 0.76 GeV fm⁻³; vacuum glueball mass MG = 1.7 GeV.
- Constituent quark mass at T=μ=0: Mq = 300 MeV (one-third of nucleon mass); constituent gluon mass at T=μ=0: MA = 850 MeV (one-half of glueball mass).
- At μ=0 and mσ=600 MeV: chiral crossover occurs at T≈153 MeV; first-order ⟨χ⟩→0 transition occurs at T≈251 MeV.
- At high temperature, the trace anomaly (interaction measure) Δ(T) = (ℰ − 3P)/T⁴ = B/T⁴ + γq Mq²/(24T²), where γq = 12 and B = 4×0.76 GeV fm⁻³.
- Matching the model's trace anomaly to QCD yields a discrepancy of ∼15% (model exceeds lattice QCD value).

## Context

This work extends the linear sigma model approach to include scale symmetry breaking via a dilaton field, building on earlier implementations of the trace anomaly in chiral Lagrangians (Schechter, 1980) and studies of dilaton thermodynamics (Campbell et al., 1990). The model aims to reproduce the Stefan-Boltzmann limit for quarks and gluons, which the standard linear sigma model fails to achieve. The results are compared to lattice QCD calculations of the thermal gluon condensate (Miller, 2007) and the QCD trace anomaly (Boyd et al., 1996). The paper suggests a phase diagram with three distinct regions (chirally broken/scale broken, chirally restored/scale broken, and chirally restored/scale restored) and proposes that the deconfinement temperature for gluons may differ from that for quarks, consistent with anomaly matching arguments ('t Hooft, 1980) and renormalization group analyses (Braun, 2010).
