# Precise strength of th piNN coupling constant

> We report here a preliminary value for the piNN coupling constant deduced from the GMO sumrule for forward piN scattering. As in our previous determination from np backward differential scattering cross sections we give a critical discussion of the analysis with careful attention not only to the statistical, but also to the systematic uncertainties. Our preliminary evaluation gives $g^2_c$(GMO) = 13.99(24).

# Precise strength of the piNN coupling constant

## Key claim

The preliminary value of the charged pion-nucleon coupling constant from the GMO sumrule is \(g_c^2 = 13.99(24)\), but this result depends on whether an s-p interference term in the deuteron correction is included or excluded; excluding it gives 14.20(24). The two model-independent methods presented provide no support for the low value near 13.5 advocated by the Nijmegen group, but that lower value cannot be completely excluded at present.

## Method

The authors evaluate the Goldberger-Miyazawa-Oehme (GMO) sumrule for forward pion-nucleon scattering, expressed in terms of the dispersion integral \(J^-\) of the total cross section difference and the pion-nucleon scattering lengths. The scattering lengths are obtained from recent pionic atom experiments at PSI, and the deuteron correction terms (double s-wave scattering, p-wave Fermi motion, dispersive absorption, and s-p interference) are applied to extract the \(\pi\)NN coupling constant from the \(\pi^- d\) scattering length.

## Key numbers

- \(g_c^2\)(GMO) = 13.99(24) including the s-p interference term; 14.20(24) excluding it
- \(J^- = -1.077(47)\) mb from the dispersion integral
- The Difference Method applied to np backward scattering gives \(g_c^2 = 14.52(26)\) from Uppsala data at 162 MeV
- The GMO sumrule expression used: \(g_c^2 = -4.50 J^- + 103.3 (a_{\pi^- p} - a_{\pi^+ p})/2\)
- The cross section integral \(J^-\) is the largest source of error, currently at \(\pm 4.6\%\)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous determination of the coupling constant from np backward differential scattering cross sections (Ericson et al. 1995, Rahm et al. 1998) using a Difference Method. It directly contradicts the lower values near 13.5 from the Nijmegen group (1993, 1997) based on NN interaction analyses, which the authors argue are not transparently linked to underlying data. The GMO analysis follows the approach of Arndt et al. (1994) but uses new precise scattering lengths from PSI pionic atom experiments (Chatellard et al. 1997). Future improvements in the dispersion integral evaluation to \(\pm 2\)-3% should yield a coupling constant precision of \(\pm 1\)-1.5%.
