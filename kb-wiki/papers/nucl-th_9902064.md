# Proton-Proton Fusion in Leading Order of Effective Field Theory

> Using a recently developed effective field theory for the interactions of nucleons at non-relativistic energies, we calculate the rate for the fusion process $p + p \ra d + e^+ + ν_e$ to leading order in the momentum expansion. Coulomb effects are included non-perturbatively in a systematic way. The resulting rate is independent of specific models for the strong interactions at short distances and is in agreement with the standard result in the zero-range approximation.

## Key claim
The authors calculate the proton-proton fusion rate to leading order in an effective field theory for non-relativistic nucleons, including Coulomb effects non-perturbatively. The resulting rate agrees with the standard zero-range approximation result from potential models, but the authors note that the leading-order accuracy is expected to be around 20% or better, and they explicitly ignore effective-range corrections, D-wave admixture, vacuum polarization, two-body current interactions, and unknown counterterms.

## Method
The authors use the effective Lagrangian of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998) with only the lowest-order contact interaction in the S-channel, where the coupling constant \(C_0\) is determined by the scattering length. The transition matrix element for \(p + p \to d + e^+ + \nu_e\) is evaluated as an overlap integral between the deuteron wavefunction and the proton-proton scattering state, which includes Coulomb effects via the full Coulomb propagator summed to all orders. The calculation is performed in the zero-momentum limit for the weak current, and the ultraviolet divergence in the bubble diagram is regularized using the PDS scheme, with the renormalized coupling constant fixed by matching to the measured proton-proton scattering length.

## Key numbers
- Deuteron binding energy \(B = 2.225\) MeV, corresponding to a bound nucleon momentum \(\gamma = \sqrt{BM} = 45.71\) MeV.
- The parameter \(\chi = \alpha M / \gamma = 0.15\).
- The integral \(I(0.15) = 4.96\).
- The measured proton-proton scattering length \(a_p = -7.82\) fm.
- The reduced matrix element at zero momentum \(\Lambda(0) = 2.51\) from the leading-order effective field theory calculation.
- A recent potential-model calculation (Stoks, year not given) gives \(\Lambda^2(0) = 7.05 \pm 0.02\), corresponding to \(\Lambda(0) = 2.66\); the leading-order result is accurate to within 6% of this full result.

## Context
This work extends the effective field theory approach of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998) to the proton-proton fusion process, following their successful application to deuteron form factors and Compton scattering. It directly parallels the calculation of proton-neutron fusion \(p + n \to d + \gamma\) by Savage, Scaldeferri, and Wise (year not given), with the key addition of strong Coulomb effects in the proton-proton channel. The result agrees with the zero-range approximation of potential models used by Bahcall and May (year not given) and provides a systematic framework for including higher-order corrections, which the authors note will be important to verify that they are as small as found in potential models.
