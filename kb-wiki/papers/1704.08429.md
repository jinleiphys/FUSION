# From chiral NN(N) interactions to giant and pygmy resonances via extended RPA

> The properties of giant and pygmy resonances are calculated starting from chiral two-and three-nucleon interactions. The aim is to assess the predictive power of modern Hamiltonians and especially the role of the three-nucleon force. Methods based on the random-phase approximation (RPA) provide an optimal description of the modes of interest with minimal computational requirements. Here we discuss the giant resonances (GRs) of 40,48Ca isotopes and their low-energy dipole response. A comparison with previous results obtained with a transformed Argonne V18 two-nucleon potential points to certain improvements.

# From chiral NN(N) interactions to giant and pygmy resonances via extended RPA

## Key claim

Chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions (χEFT+SRG) produce giant resonance energies that are overestimated (rather than underestimated as with AV18+UCOM), representing an improvement, though charge radii remain too small; the low-energy isoscalar dipole response in calcium isotopes is reduced by an order of magnitude compared to AV18+UCOM results, removing the previous qualitative contradiction with experiment.

## Method

The authors start from a chiral NN interaction at N³LO and a chiral NNN interaction at N²LO with a 400 MeV cutoff, evolved via the Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG). The three-nucleon interaction is normal-ordered and reduced to one- and two-body operators, neglecting the residual three-nucleon part. They then perform Random Phase Approximation (RPA) and Second RPA (SRPA) calculations in a 13-shell harmonic-oscillator basis for ⁴⁰Ca and ⁴⁸Ca, using the diagonal approximation (SRPA0) for the 2p2h couplings.

## Key numbers

- Charge radii from χEFT+SRG: 2.41 fm for ¹⁶O, 2.98 fm for ⁴⁰Ca, and 2.6 fm for ⁴⁸Ca; experimental values are 2.70, 3.48, and 3.48 fm respectively.
- With the previous AV18+UCOM potential, energetic discrepancies for giant resonances ranged from approximately -10 MeV (giant monopole resonance) to 0 MeV (giant quadrupole resonance).
- The low-energy isoscalar dipole strength in calcium isotopes is reduced by an order of magnitude when using χEFT+SRG compared to AV18+UCOM.

## Context

This work extends earlier SRPA calculations using the Argonne V18 potential transformed via the unitary correlation operator method (AV18+UCOM) by Papakonstantinou et al. (2009), which underestimated giant monopole resonance energies and produced extremely strong low-energy dipole transitions in calcium that contradicted observations by Derya et al. (2014). The new results with chiral NN+NNN interactions show improved giant resonance energies and qualitatively correct low-energy dipole behavior. The approach enables systematic testing of modern chiral interactions (such as the SAT family and the Daejeon16 interaction) for collective nuclear response, aiming toward predictive ab initio descriptions of giant and pygmy resonances across the nuclear chart.
