# Extraction of Meson Resonances from Three-pions Photo-production Reactions

> We have investigated the model dependence of meson resonance properties extracted from the Dalitz-plot analysis of the three-pions photoproduction reactions on the nucleon. Within a unitary model developed in Phys. Rev. D 84, 114019 (2011), we generate Dalitz-plot distributions as data to perform an isobar model fit that is similar to most of the previous analyses of three-pion production reactions. It is found that the resonance positions from the two models agree well when both fit the data accurately, except for the resonance poles near branch points. The residues of the resonant amplitudes extracted from the two models and by the usual Breit-Wigner procedure agree well only for the isolated resonances with narrow widths. For overlapping resonances, most of the extracted residues could be drastically different. Our results suggest that even with high precision data, the resonance extraction should be based on models within which the amplitude parametrization is constrained by three-particle unitarity condition.

## Key claim
The study finds that resonance positions extracted from a unitary model (UM) and an isobar model (IM) agree well when both fit the data accurately, except for resonance poles near branch points; however, the residues of resonant amplitudes agree well only for isolated resonances with narrow widths, while for overlapping resonances most extracted residues can be drastically different.

## Method
The authors used a unitary coupled-channels model developed in their earlier work (Phys. Rev. D 84, 114019, 2011) to generate Dalitz-plot distributions for the γp → M* n → π⁺π⁺π⁻ n reaction at photon energy Eγ = 5 GeV and momentum transfer t = -0.4 GeV². They then performed an isobar model fit to these generated distributions, similar to previous analyses of three-pion production reactions, and compared the resonance parameters extracted from both models. The isobar model neglected final state interactions from the Z-diagram mechanism and replaced the M* propagator with a Breit-Wigner form.

## Key numbers
- Photon energy: Eγ = 5 GeV; momentum transfer: t = -0.4 GeV²
- For a₂(1320): UM pole position (1263, -21) MeV; IM pole position (1262, -22) MeV; Breit-Wigner position (1267, -24) MeV
- For π₁(1600): UM pole position (1599, -4) MeV; IM pole position (1599, -4) MeV; Breit-Wigner position (1599, -4) MeV
- For a₁ second pole: UM pole position (1443, -342) MeV; IM pole position (1201, -212) MeV; Breit-Wigner position (1391, -389) MeV
- Largest background contribution relative to full Dalitz-plot distribution: 37% at W = 1140 MeV; below a₂(1320) peak: 22%; below a₂(1700) peak: 29%

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous unitary model (Nakamura et al., Phys. Rev. D 84, 114019, 2011) to investigate the model dependence of meson resonance extraction from three-pion photoproduction, relevant to experiments at JLab (CLAS), BNL (E852), and CERN (COMPASS). The results demonstrate the limitations of the conventional isobar model approach used in prior analyses, particularly for overlapping resonances and poles near branch points, and suggest that future analyses of high-precision data from the JLab 12 GeV upgrade should employ models constrained by three-particle unitarity.
