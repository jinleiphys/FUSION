# Links Between Heavy Ion and Astrophysics

> Heavy ion experiments provide important data to test astrophysical models. The high density equation of state can be probed in HI collisions and applied to the hot protoneutron star formed in core collapse supernovae. The Parity Radius Experiment (PREX) aims to accurately measure the neutron radius of $^{208}$Pb with parity violating electron scattering. This determines the pressure of neutron rich matter and the density dependence of the symmetry energy. Competition between nuclear attraction and coulomb repulsion can form exotic shapes called nuclear pasta in neutron star crusts and supernovae. This competition can be probed with multifragmentation HI reactions. We use large scale semiclassical simulations to study nonuniform neutron rich matter in supernovae. We find that the coulomb interactions in astrophysical systems suppress density fluctuations. As a result, there is no first order liquid vapor phase transition. Finally, the virial expansion for low density matter shows that the nuclear vapor phase is complex with significant concentrations of alpha particles and other light nuclei in addition to free nucleons.

## Key claim

Heavy ion experiments provide important data to test astrophysical models, but the data must be extrapolated to longer times, larger systems, and more neutron-rich conditions; in particular, Coulomb interactions in astrophysical systems suppress density fluctuations such that there is no first-order liquid-vapor phase transition in the thermodynamic limit, contrary to what simpler models might suggest.

## Method

The author uses large-scale semiclassical molecular dynamics simulations with 40,000 to 200,000 nucleons, employing a simple two-body potential fitted to nuclear saturation density, binding energy, and symmetry energy, with screened Coulomb interactions representing the background electron gas. The virial expansion is also applied to low-density matter, calculating second virial coefficients from elastic scattering phase shifts for nucleons and alpha particles. The static structure factor S_q is computed from the simulations to characterize density fluctuations and neutrino scattering properties.

## Key numbers

- The Parity Radius Experiment (PREX) aims to measure the parity-violating asymmetry A ≈ 0.6 ppm for 850 MeV electrons scattering from 208Pb at six degrees, with a goal accuracy of 3%, allowing the neutron rms radius to be deduced to 1%.
- A typical neutron matter equation of state gives neutron star radius R(M) ≈ 11-12 km for M = 1.4 solar masses, while a stiff equation of state could give R ≈ 13-14 km, and exotic phases could lead to radii of 10 km or less.
- The relation between neutron skin thickness in 208Pb and the symmetry energy density dependence is given by: <r_n^2>^{1/2} - <r_p^2>^{1/2} ≈ 0.22 γ + 0.06 fm, where S(ρ) ≈ S_0 ρ^γ.
- If the neutron-minus-proton rms radius in 208Pb is larger than 0.25 fm, all mean-field models considered allow direct URCA cooling for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star; if less than 0.2 fm, none do.
- At density ρ = 0.01 fm^{-3}, temperature T = 1 MeV, and proton fraction 0.2, the average heavy nucleus mass is <A> ≈ 100 with Z/A ≈ 0.3; at ρ = 0.025 fm^{-3}, <A> ≈ 200.
- The static structure factor S_q shows a large peak at q ≈ 0.3 fm^{-1} for densities from 0.01 to 0.05 fm^{-3}, corresponding to coherent neutrino scattering from clusters or pasta.
- The wavelength of a 10 MeV supernova neutrino is 120 fm; at 1/3 nuclear saturation density there are 100,000 nucleons in a cube 120 fm on a side.

## Context

This paper reviews and extends connections between heavy-ion physics and astrophysics, building on prior work on the nuclear equation of state from flow observables (ScienceEOS, 1999) and kaon yields (KaonsEOS). It discusses the PREX experiment at Jefferson Laboratory (Donnelly, 1989; PREX collaboration) and its relation to the symmetry energy density dependence (Brown, 2000; Steiner, 2005). The molecular dynamics simulations of nuclear pasta extend earlier work on pasta phases (NucPasta) and multifragmentation (Multifragmentation), using special-purpose MDGRAPE hardware. The virial expansion treatment of low-density nuclear matter (VirialNeut, VirialNuc) provides model-independent predictions for alpha particle fractions. The finding that Coulomb interactions suppress a first-order liquid-vapor phase transition in astrophysical systems contrasts with simpler phase-transition models (PhaseTransition) and has implications for neutrino transport in supernovae (Margueron).
