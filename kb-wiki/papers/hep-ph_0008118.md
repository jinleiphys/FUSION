# Chiral Symmetry and the Nucleon Nucleon Interaction

> Various aspects of the application of Effective Field Theory (EFT) to the Nucleon-Nucleon (NN) interaction are considered. We look for contributions beyond One Pion Exchange which are predicted by Chiral Symmetry. Using the formalism of the Wilson Renormalisation Group (RG) we review power counting in a simplified EFT containing only nucleons. For weak scattering at low energy, we find a natural expansion of the scattering matrix around the unique trivial fixed point of the RG. For strong scattering at low energy, the calculation can be organised in a useful and systematic way by expanding the potential around a non-trivial fixed point corresponding to a bound state of two nucleons at threshold. The resulting expansion of the inverse of the scattering matrix reproduces the effective range expansion order by order. The extension of this EFT to include pions in a manner consistent with chiral symmetry is discussed. By considering a modified effective range expansion, we find that the small momentum expansion in S-wave scattering converges slowly, if at all. The NN potential is written down to third order in small momenta. With a cut-off in coordinate space, we calculate phase shifts in peripheral partial waves for which the EFT predictions are parameter-free, and for which we may use the expansion around the trivial fixed point. We find several partial waves in which the effects of two-pion exchange can be isolated, but no strong evidence is found for the convergence of the small momentum expansion at this order.

## Key claim

The paper argues that effective field theory (EFT) with chiral symmetry can describe nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions systematically, but the small momentum expansion in S-wave scattering converges slowly, if at all, and no strong evidence is found for convergence of the expansion at third order in peripheral partial waves.

## Method

The author uses the Wilson Renormalisation Group (RG) formalism to review power counting in a simplified EFT containing only nucleons, expanding the potential around either the trivial fixed point (for weak scattering) or a non-trivial fixed point corresponding to a bound state at threshold (for strong scattering). The NN potential is written down to third order in small momenta, including leading and next-to-leading order two-pion exchange contributions, and phase shifts are calculated in peripheral partial waves (l>1) using a coordinate-space cutoff, where the EFT predictions are parameter-free.

## Context

This work extends the application of chiral perturbation theory (CHPT) from the pionic and single-nucleon sectors to the two-nucleon system, building on the foundational work of Weinberg (1979) on effective Lagrangians and power counting. The paper addresses complications arising from the large attraction in S-wave NN scattering that produces the deuteron and the unnaturally large spin singlet scattering length, which require modifying the effective theory structure compared to approaches like the non-linear sigma model. The results enable systematic calculation of NN phase shifts with parameter-free predictions in peripheral partial waves, though the slow convergence suggests that higher-order calculations or alternative expansion schemes may be needed for S-waves.
