# Hadronic scattering of charmed mesons

> The scattering cross sections of charm mesons with hadrons such as the pion, rho meson, and nucleon are studied in an effective Lagrangian. In heavy ion collisions, rescattering of produced charm mesons by hadrons affects the invariant mass spectra of both charm meson pairs and dileptons resulting from their decays. These effects are estimated for heavy ion collisions at SPS energies and are found to be significant.

## Key claim

Hadronic rescattering of charm mesons in heavy ion collisions at SPS energies can significantly harden the charm meson transverse mass spectra and enhance the intermediate-mass dilepton yield from charm meson decays, but the estimates are based on a schematic model with simplified assumptions about the time evolution of the hadronic system and neglect of transverse expansion, chemical equilibration, and contributions from heavier exchanged particles.

## Method

The authors constructed an effective Lagrangian based on SU(4) symmetry to describe interactions of charm mesons (D, D*) with pions, rho mesons, and nucleons. They evaluated spin- and isospin-averaged differential cross sections for t-channel, u-channel, and s-channel (Breit-Wigner resonance) processes using empirical coupling constants and monopole form factors with cutoff parameters. Thermal-averaged cross sections and momentum-transfer-weighted cross sections were computed, and a schematic model with a 1/tau density evolution was used to estimate the total squared momentum transfer to charm mesons in central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies.

## Key numbers

- Total squared momentum transfer to charm mesons from hadronic scatterings: ⟨p_S^2⟩ ≈ 0.61 GeV^2 for central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies
- Thermal-averaged momentum-transfer-weighted cross sections at T=150 MeV: ⟨σ v p_0^2⟩ ≈ 1.1 mb GeV^2 for pion scatterings, 1.5 mb GeV^2 for rho meson scatterings, and 2.7 mb GeV^2 for nucleon scatterings with charm mesons
- Initial densities (ρ_0 τ_0) at central rapidity: 0.79 fm^{-2} for pions, 0.35 fm^{-2} for rho mesons, and 0.28 fm^{-2} for nucleons
- Resulting effective temperature parameter from rescatterings: T_S ≈ 96 MeV
- Final effective inverse slope parameter for charm meson transverse mass spectrum: T_eff = 235 MeV, starting from an initial value of 160 MeV
- Dimuon enhancement factor from charm meson decays into the NA50 acceptance: about 2.1
- Resonance widths used: Γ_{D_2^{*0}} = 23 MeV, Γ_{D_2^{*+}} = 25 MeV, Γ_{D_1^0} = 18.9 MeV, Γ_{D_1^+} = 28 MeV; Γ_{D^{*0}} ≈ 42 keV and Γ_{D^{*+}} ≈ 46 keV from relativistic potential model

## Context

This work extends the schematic model of Lin and Ko (1998) by providing explicit cross section calculations for charm meson hadronic scatterings, giving quantitative support to the earlier proposal that final-state rescattering could explain the intermediate-mass dilepton enhancement observed by the HELIOS-3 and NA50 Collaborations at CERN SPS. The results enable future hadronic cascade simulations with full isospin treatment and time evolution, and highlight the need to include partonic rescattering and radiative energy loss for predictions at RHIC energies, as discussed by Baier et al. (1997) and others.
