# Interpretation of near-threshold peaks using the method of independent S-matrix poles

> We propose a model-independent analysis of near-threshold enhancements using independent S-matrix poles. In this formulation, we constructed a Jost function with controllable zeros to ensure that no poles are generated on the physical Riemann sheet. We show that there is a possibility of misinterpreting the observed near-threshold signals if one utilized a limited parametrization and restrict the analysis to only one element of the S-matrix. Specifically, there is a possibility of the emergence of ambiguous pair of poles which are singularities of the full S-matrix but may not manifest in one of its elements. For a more concrete discussion, we focused on an effective two-channel scattering where the full S-matrix is a $2\times2$ matrix. We apply our method to the coupled two-channel analysis of the $P_ψ^N(4312)^+$ and found that the compact pentaquark interpretation cannot be ruled out yet.

## Key claim

The paper argues that near-threshold enhancements can be misinterpreted when analysis is restricted to a single diagonal element of the S-matrix using limited parametrizations such as the effective range expansion; specifically, the compact pentaquark interpretation of the \(P_\psi^N(4312)^+\) cannot be ruled out yet because ambiguous pairs of poles that are singularities of the full S-matrix may not manifest in the elastic channel alone.

## Method

The authors construct a Jost function with controllable, independent zeros to build a model-independent S-matrix for an effective two-channel scattering system, ensuring no poles appear on the physical Riemann sheet. They use the uniformization scheme to map the four Riemann sheets of the two-channel complex energy plane onto a single uniformized variable, allowing independent placement of poles. They then compare the line shapes of the diagonal element \(T_{11}\) for different pole configurations and demonstrate that identical elastic scattering line shapes can arise from either a 1-pole or a 3-pole configuration, with the ambiguity resolved only by including the off-diagonal \(T_{21}\) channel.

## Key numbers

* The analysis focuses on the \(P_\psi^N(4312)^+\) state observed in \(\Lambda^0_b \to J/\psi pK^-\) decays by LHCb (2019), with the state lying below the \(\Sigma_c\bar{D}\) or \(\Sigma_c\bar{D}^*\) thresholds.
* The two-channel system has a first energy threshold \(\epsilon_1\) and a second energy threshold \(\epsilon_2\), with the scattering region lying on the physical sheet \([tt]\) between these thresholds.
* The Riemann sheets are labeled \([tt]\), \([bt]\), \([bb]\), and \([tb]\) following the notation of Pearce and Gibson, with poles on \([bt]\) and \([bb]\) sheets having negative imaginary parts and poles on \([tb]\) having positive imaginary parts.

## Context

This work extends the pole-counting method of Morgan (1992) and Morgan and Pennington (1991) by showing that coupled-channel effects can produce ambiguous pole configurations that are not captured by standard parametrizations like the effective range expansion or Flatté forms. The authors note that previous analyses of the \(P_\psi^N(4312)^+\) by LHCb (2019), by Matuschek et al. (2021), and by deep learning approaches concluded it is a virtual state or molecular state, while QCD sum rule studies and the compact diquark model of Ali et al. (2019) favor a compact pentaquark interpretation. The method enables future studies to distinguish between molecular and compact interpretations by including off-diagonal S-matrix elements in experimental fits.
