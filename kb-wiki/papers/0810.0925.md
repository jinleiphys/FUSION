# Charge distribution and radii in clusters from nuclear pasta models

> We study the consistency of the description of charge distributions and radii of nuclear clusters obtained with semiclassical nuclear pasta models. These nuclei are expected to exist in the low density outer crust of neutron stars. Properties of the arising clusterized nucleon matter can be compared to realistic nuclear properties as experimentally extracted on earth. We focus on non iso-symmetric light clusters with nucleon number $8 \le A \le 30$ and use Monte Carlo many-body techniques. We simulate isotopic chains for a set of selected nuclei using a model Hamiltonian consisting of the usual kinetic term, hadronic nucleon nucleon (NN), Coulomb and an effective density dependent Pauli potential. It is shown that for neutron rich (deficient) clusters neutron (proton) skins develop. Different (matter, neutron, proton, electric charge) radii are computed for this set of non iso-symmetric nuclei. Nuclear binding energies are also analyzed in the isotopic chains.

## Key claim
Semiclassical nuclear pasta models, using a simplified Gaussian NN interaction with a density-dependent Pauli potential, can reproduce experimental binding energies and radii for light non-isosymmetric clusters (8 ≤ A ≤ 30) to within about 16% for the most asymmetric cases, but the agreement degrades as isospin asymmetry increases and cannot reproduce deformation-driven anomalies such as the Neon isotope anomaly.

## Method
The author performed Monte Carlo many-body simulations using the Metropolis algorithm at a fixed temperature of T = 1 MeV to find ground-state configurations of finite nuclear clusters. The model Hamiltonian included kinetic energy, a two-Gaussian hadronic NN interaction (the "GM model"), Coulomb repulsion between protons, and an effective density-dependent Pauli potential that prevents identical nucleons from occupying the same phase-space volume. For each thermalized cluster, the author statistically sampled binding energies and root-mean-square (RMS) radii for matter, neutrons, protons, and electric charge, folding the proton charge radius with a finite proton size of s_p ≈ 0.8 fm to obtain the charge RMS radius.

## Key numbers
- The Fermi momentum for nuclei with A > 50 saturates to a value ≈ 260 MeV/c (e.g., 265 MeV/c for ²⁰⁸Pb).
- For symmetric nuclear matter at low temperatures, the binding energy per particle stabilizes at ≈ -16 MeV.
- The GM model parameters used are: a = 50 MeV, b = -4 MeV, c = 3 MeV, λ₁ = 0.8 fm, λ₂ = 4 fm.
- For the Neon isotope chain at A = 30 (highest isospin asymmetry considered), the discrepancy between the GM model and the relativistic mean-field (DEF) model for Δr_np is about 16%.
- The linear fit to experimental neutron skin data from antiprotonic atoms gives Δr_np = (0.9 ± 0.15)I + (-0.03 ± 0.02) fm, where I = (N-Z)/A.

## Context
This work extends the author's previous semiclassical Monte Carlo studies of isosymmetric nuclei (Aka and Aka2, 2006-2007) to non-isosymmetric light clusters relevant to the "pasta phase" of neutron star crusts at densities below ≈ 0.1ρ₀ (ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³). The results are compared to experimental data from Ozawa (2001) for matter radii and to relativistic mean-field calculations by Sharma (1993) and deformed-basis calculations by Lalazissis (1999). The approach enables consistent testing of nuclear pasta models across the full density range where the nucleon picture holds, which is important for calculating neutrino opacities in astrophysical contexts.
