# Spin-dependent interaction in the deconfined phase of QCD

> Spin-dependent deconfined interaction in the Q\barQ system is derived from the field correlators known from lattice and analytic calculations. As a result hyperfine splitting is found numerically for charmonium, bottomonium and strangeonium in the range T_c<T<2T_c. Spin-orbit interaction due to magnetic correlators (the Thomas term) is able to produce numerous Q\barQ bound states with accumulation point at M=m_Q+m_\barQ. Possible influence of these effects on the thermodynamics of quark-gluon plasma is discussed.

## Key claim

Spin-dependent interactions in the deconfined phase of QCD (T > T_c) are derived from field correlators, with the key condition that the colorelectric correlator D^E(x) vanishes at T = T_c while all other correlators remain intact, leading to spin-orbit and hyperfine splittings that change only slightly above T_c.

## Method

The author uses the Field-Correlator Method (FCM) to express spin-dependent potentials V_i(r) through Gaussian correlators D(x) and D_1(x), which are known from lattice calculations. For T > T_c, the four independent correlators D^E, D^H, D_1^E, and D_1^H are used to derive the spin-orbit and spin-spin potentials via the Fock-Feynman-Schwinger Representation (FSR). The hyperfine splitting is computed numerically for charmonium, bottomonium, and strangeonium in the temperature range T_c ≤ T ≤ 2T_c.

## Key numbers

- Hyperfine splitting is computed numerically for charmonium, bottomonium, and strangeonium in the range T_c ≤ T ≤ 2T_c.
- The dilaton mass M_d is of order 1 to 1.5 GeV, the scale below which correlators do not change significantly for T ≪ M_d.
- The dissociation temperature T_d is approximately 2T_c for D_1^E calculated analytically, and approximately 1.12 T_c for D_1^E from lattice calculations.
- The mass gap between J/ψ and η_c for T_c ≤ T ≤ 1.3 T_c is estimated to be of the same order as at T = 0, i.e., δE ∼ 0.1 GeV.
- The spin-orbit Thomas term at large R yields a mass spectrum M_n = 2m - (σ_H^2 / (4m^3)) * (L^2 / (L + n + 1)^2) for n = 0, 1, 2, ...

## Context

This work extends the Field-Correlator Method (FCM) developed by Simonov and collaborators (2000-2005) from the confined phase to the deconfined phase of QCD. It builds on lattice calculations of field correlators by the Pisa group (2002) and earlier spin-dependent potential analyses by Eichten and Feinberg (1981) and Gromes (1984). The results enable further study of bound states in the quark-gluon plasma and their influence on QCD thermodynamics, supporting the view that strong interactions persist in the deconfined phase as advocated by earlier work (2000-2004).
