# Bottom-strange mesons in hyperonic matter

> The in-medium behavior of bottom-strange pseudoscalar mesons in hot, isospin asymmetric and dense hadronic environment is studied using a chiral effective model. The same was recently generalized to the heavy quark sector and employed to study the behavior of open-charm and open-bottom mesons. The heavy quark (anti-quark) is treated as frozen and all medium modifications of these bottom-strange mesons are due to their strange anti-quark (quark) content. We observe a pronounced dependence of their medium mass on baryonic density and strangeness content of the medium. Certain aspects of these in-medium interactions are similar to those observed for the strange-charmed mesons in a preceding investigation, such as the lifting of mass-degeneracy of $B_S^0$ and ${\bar B}_S^0$ mesons in hyperonic matter, while the same is respected in vacuum as well as in nuclear matter. In general, however, there is a remarkable distinction between the two species, even though the formalism predicts a completely analogous in-medium interaction Lagrangian density. We discuss in detail the reason for different in-medium behavior of these bottom-strange mesons as compared to charmed-strange mesons, despite the dynamics of the heavy quark being treated as frozen in both cases.

# Bottom-strange mesons in hyperonic matter

## Key claim

The in-medium masses of bottom-strange mesons ($B_S^0$ and $\bar{B}_S^0$) show a pronounced dependence on baryonic density and strangeness content of the medium, with the mass-degeneracy between the two mesons being lifted in hyperonic matter (but respected in vacuum and nuclear matter); however, despite the formalism predicting a completely analogous interaction Lagrangian density for bottom-strange and charmed-strange mesons, the two species exhibit remarkably different in-medium behavior because the scalar meson exchange contribution scales by a factor of approximately 2.86 when going from the charm to the bottom sector, while the Weinberg-Tomozawa and range terms change by factors close to unity.

## Method

The authors use a chiral effective model originally formulated for SU(3) and generalized to SU(4) and then to the bottom sector, treating the heavy quark (antiquark) as frozen in the medium. They solve the coupled equations of motion for scalar meson fields in the mean-field approximation to obtain baryonic scalar and number densities, then use these to solve the in-medium dispersion relations for $B_S^0$ and $\bar{B}_S^0$ mesons, which yield quadratic equations in the center-of-mass frame. The self-energy includes contributions from the Weinberg-Tomozawa term, scalar meson exchange, and three range terms, with the Weinberg-Tomozawa term being the only contribution that distinguishes between $B_S^0$ and $\bar{B}_S^0$.

## Key numbers

- In asymmetric ($\eta = 0.5$) hyperonic matter with $f_s = 0.5$ at $T=0$, the $B_S^0$ meson experiences mass drops of 67 MeV at $\rho_B = \rho_0$, 161 MeV at $2\rho_0$, and 326 MeV at $4\rho_0$; the corresponding drops for $\bar{B}_S^0$ are 73 MeV, 173 MeV, and 349 MeV.
- At $\rho_B = 6\rho_0$ and $T=0$ in asymmetric ($\eta = 0.5$) hyperonic matter ($f_s = 0.5$), the mass drops are 448 MeV for $B_S^0$ and 481 MeV for $\bar{B}_S^0$.
- At the same density ($6\rho_0$) but $T=150$ MeV, these mass drops reduce to 379 MeV for $B_S^0$ and 413 MeV for $\bar{B}_S^0$.
- In cold ($T=0$) asymmetric ($\eta = 0.5$) hyperonic matter with $f_s = 0.5$ at $\rho_B = 6\rho_0$, the medium masses are 4918 MeV for $B_S^0$ and 4886 MeV for $\bar{B}_S^0$, compared to the nuclear matter value of 5040 MeV.
- The $B_S$ decay constant is taken as $f_{B_S} = 224$ MeV.
- For comparison, at $\rho_B = 6\rho_0$, $T=100$ MeV in asymmetric ($\eta = 0.5$) hyperonic matter ($f_s = 0.5$), the mass drops for $B_S^0$ and $\bar{B}_S^0$ are 383 MeV and 416 MeV respectively, while the corresponding values for $D_S^+$ and $D_S^-$ from prior work were 161 MeV and 131 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective model approach previously applied to open-charm mesons (Mishra et al., 2004; Arvind et al., various years), charmed-strange mesons (Pathak and Mishra, 2014), and open-bottom mesons (Pathak and Mishra, 2014) to the final class of pseudoscalar mesons treatable within this generalized formalism: bottom-strange mesons. The study reveals that despite the analogous interaction Lagrangian density, bottom-strange mesons exhibit much larger mass drops than charmed-strange mesons due to the significantly larger scalar meson exchange contribution (scaling by a factor of about 2.86 versus near-unity scaling for other terms), and unlike the charmed-strange case, both $B_S^0$ and $\bar{B}_S^0$ show appreciable strangeness-fraction dependence with no near-cancellation effect. This work enables further investigation of heavy-light meson properties in dense matter relevant to relativistic heavy-ion collision experiments and neutron star physics.
