# Nucleon resonances in omega photoproduction

> The role of the nucleon resonances ($N^*$) in $ω$ photoproduction is investigated by using the resonance parameters predicted by Capstick and Roberts [Phys. Rev. D {\bf 46}, 2864 (1992); {\bf 49}, 4570 (1994)]. In contrast with the previous investigations based on the ${SU}(6) \times {O}(3)$ limit of the constituent quark model, the employed $N^* \to γN$ and $N^* \to ωN$ amplitudes include the configuration mixing effects due to the residual quark-quark interactions. The contributions from the nucleon resonances are found to be significant relative to the non-resonant amplitudes in changing the differential cross sections at large scattering angles and various spin observables. In particular, we suggest that a crucial test of our predictions can be made by measuring the parity asymmetry and beam-target double asymmetry at forward scattering angles.

## Key claim

The nucleon resonances predicted by Capstick and Roberts (1992, 1994) using a constituent quark model with configuration mixing produce significant contributions to omega photoproduction, particularly at large scattering angles and in spin observables, but the most experimentally testable predictions are the parity asymmetry and beam-target double asymmetry at forward scattering angles, where the well-understood non-resonant background allows clean extraction of resonance effects.

## Method

The authors constructed a model for omega photoproduction combining non-resonant amplitudes (Pomeron exchange, pseudoscalar-meson exchange via pi and eta, and direct/crossed nucleon terms) with resonant amplitudes from 22 nucleon resonances (12 positive parity, 10 negative parity, up to spin-9/2) using resonance parameters from Capstick and Roberts (1992, 1994). The non-resonant parameters were fixed by fitting existing differential cross section data at low energies and total cross section data from threshold to high energies, while the resonant amplitudes used an averaged total width of 300 MeV for all resonances and a harmonic oscillator cutoff parameter of 1 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The averaged total width for all nucleon resonances was set to Gamma_0 = 300 MeV, based on the range of 200-400 MeV observed for known resonances in the Particle Data Group listings.
- The pion exchange cutoff parameters were determined as Lambda_pi = 0.6 GeV and Lambda_omega-gamma-pi = 0.7 GeV from fitting forward cross section data.
- The nucleon term cutoff parameter was set to Lambda_N = 0.5 GeV with g_omega-NN = 10.35 and kappa_omega = 0.
- The Pomeron exchange parameters were taken from a global fit: mu_0^2 = 1.1 GeV^2, beta_0 = 2.05 GeV^{-1}, s_0 = 4 GeV^2, with Pomeron trajectory alpha_P(t) = 1.08 + 0.25 t.
- The dominant resonances were N3/2+(1910) (a missing resonance) and N3/2-(1960) (identified as D13(2080) of PDG), with N7/2-(2090) (identified as G17(2190)) becoming comparable at W = 2.11 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the investigation of nucleon resonances in vector meson photoproduction beyond the SU(6) x O(3) constituent quark model calculations of Zhao et al. (1998, 1999) by incorporating configuration mixing effects from residual quark-quark interactions as predicted by Capstick and Roberts (1992, 1994). The non-resonant background follows the established framework of Pomeron exchange (Donnachie and Landshoff, 1984-1992) and one-pion exchange (Friman and Soyeur, 1996). The results enable experimental tests of the "missing resonance problem" through measurements of parity asymmetry and beam-target double asymmetry at forward angles, which could distinguish between different quark model predictions and help resolve the identification of resonances such as D13(2080).
