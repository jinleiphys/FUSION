# Azimuthal decorrelation for photon induced dijet production in ultra-peripheral collisions of heavy ions

> We study the azimuthal angular decorrelation of the dijet production via photon fusion in ultra-peripheral heavy ion collisions. The impact parameter dependent cross section of quark-antiquark pairs production is derived using the equivalent photon approximation, and the contribution from final-state QCD radiations to the azimuthal angular distribution are calculated within Soft-Collinear Effective Theory. We carry out the QCD resummation of large logarithms of the azimuthal angle as well as the jet radius at next-to-leading logarithmic accuracy. In the end we present the normalized differential cross section for azimuthal decorrelation of the dijet pair and find that our results are consistent with the measurements reported by the ATLAS collaboration.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the azimuthal decorrelation of dijets produced via photon fusion in ultra-peripheral heavy ion collisions can be described by combining the impact-parameter-dependent equivalent photon approximation for the initial-state photons with next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) resummation of final-state QCD radiation, and that this combined result is consistent with ATLAS measurements, but only after including both the intrinsic photon transverse momentum and the all-order resummation of large logarithms.

## Method

The authors derived the impact-parameter-dependent cross section for quark-antiquark pair production via photon-photon fusion using the equivalent photon approximation (EPA) with Woods-Saxon nuclear charge form factors. They then applied Soft-Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) to develop a factorization formula for the azimuthal angle distribution, separating contributions from hard scattering, jet functions, soft functions, and collinear-soft functions. The large logarithms of the azimuthal angle and jet radius were resummed at NLL accuracy by solving renormalization group equations, and non-global logarithms were included via a fitting function.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of azimuthal decorrelation in dijet production, previously studied for hadronic collisions by Banfi (2008), Hautmann (2008), Sun (2014, 2015), Chen (2018), Liu (2018, 2020), Chien (2019, 2020, 2022), and others, to the case of photon-induced dijets in ultra-peripheral heavy ion collisions. It addresses the discrepancy found by the ATLAS collaboration (2022) where the Pythia8 event generator underestimated the total cross section by about an order of magnitude and produced a narrower azimuthal distribution than observed. The results enable direct comparison with ATLAS data and provide a framework for future studies of QCD and QED radiation effects in UPC dijet production.
