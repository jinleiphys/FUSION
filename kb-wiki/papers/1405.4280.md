# Relativistic effects in two-particle emission for electron and neutrino reactions

> Two-particle two-hole contributions to electroweak response functions are computed in a fully relativistic Fermi gas, assuming that the electroweak current matrix elements are independent of the kinematics. We analyze the genuine kinematical and relativistic effects before including a realistic meson-exchange current (MEC) operator. This allows one to study the mathematical properties of the non-trivial seven-dimensional integrals appearing in the calculation and to design an optimal numerical procedure to reduce the computation time. This is required for practical applications to CC neutrino scattering experiments, where an additional integral over the neutrino flux is performed. Finally we examine the viability of this model to compute the electroweak 2p-2h response functions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the fully relativistic 2p-2h phase-space integral, computed without any two-body current operator, contains a divergence in the angular integrand for certain kinematics that must be handled analytically; this divergence arises from the two solutions of the energy conservation equation for a fixed emission angle and is not present in the non-relativistic limit.

## Method

The authors compute the 2p-2h phase-space function F(q,omega) for a relativistic Fermi gas by evaluating the seven-dimensional integral over the momenta of two final nucleons and two hole states, using the energy delta function to reduce one dimension analytically. They first set the elementary hadronic tensor to a constant (r^mu^nu = 1) to isolate pure kinematical and relativistic effects, then compare the relativistic calculation with a semi-analytical non-relativistic method based on the Van Orden (1980) formalism. For high momentum transfer they introduce a "frozen nucleon" approximation where hole momenta are neglected, and they derive an analytical formula for the integral around the divergent angles.

## Key numbers

* Fermi momentum used throughout: k_F = 225 MeV/c
* Non-relativistic phase-space function F(q,omega) computed for q = 300, 400, 500 MeV/c at omega = 300, 400, 500 MeV (shown in Fig. 4)
* Relativistic phase-space function computed for q = 300, 400, 500, 700, 1000, 1500 MeV/c (shown in Figs. 5 and 6)
* Relativistic reduction relative to non-relativistic: about 15% for q = 500 MeV/c
* Asymptotic limit for omega -> infinity: non-relativistic F_a proportional to sqrt(omega); relativistic F_a goes to a constant value 4 pi (4/3 pi k_F^3)^2 (m_N^2/2)
* Number of integration points in 7D straightforward integration: 10^7 for low q, 0.42 x 10^9 for high q (25 points for theta'_1, 16 for each remaining dimension)

## Context

This work extends the relativistic 2p-2h model of De Pace et al. (2003) for electron scattering to the weak sector, aiming to include meson-exchange currents in the SuSA approach of Amaro et al. (2011, 2012). It contrasts with the non-relativistic models of Martini et al. (2010-2014) and Nieves et al. (2011-2013), which use approximations to reduce the 7D integral to lower dimensions, and provides a benchmark for evaluating the accuracy of those approximations. The results enable future inclusion of realistic weak meson-exchange currents for charged-current neutrino scattering calculations, where an additional integral over the neutrino flux is required.
