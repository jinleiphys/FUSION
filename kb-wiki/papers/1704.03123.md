# Correlated-Gaussian approach to linear-chain states -Case of four $α$-particles-

> We show that correlated Gaussians with good angular momentum and parity provide flexible basis functions for specific elongated shape. As its application we study linear-chain states of four-alpha particles in variation-after-projection calculations in which all the matrix elements are evaluated analytically. We find possible chain states for $J=0^+$, $2^+$, $4^+$ and perhaps $6^+$ with the bandhead energy being about 33 MeV from the ground state of $^{16}$O. No chain states with $J\geq 8$ are found. The nature of the rotational sequence of the chain states is clarified in contrast to a rigid-body rotation. The quadrupole deformation parameters estimated from the chain states increase from 0.59 to 1.07 for $2^+$ to $6^+$. This work suggests undeveloped fields for the correlated Gaussians beyond those problems which have hitherto been solved successfully.

## Key claim

Correlated Gaussians with good angular momentum and parity can accurately represent linear-chain configurations of four alpha particles, with possible chain states found for J=0+, 2+, 4+, and perhaps 6+, but no chain states with J≥8 are found; the bandhead energy is about 33 MeV from the ground state of 16O, though the authors note this is a "possible" chain state and the 6+ assignment is tentative.

## Method

The authors performed variation-after-projection calculations using correlated Gaussian basis functions to describe linear-chain configurations of four alpha particles in 16O. They determined the correlated Gaussian parameters (matrix A, vector u, and integer K) by matching expectation values of R² and kinetic energy to those of the linear-chain state derived from Gaussian wave packets, then superposed different chain configurations with boson symmetry imposed. The alpha-alpha interaction included a two-body nuclear potential, Coulomb potential, and a three-body nuclear potential, with parameters taken from Suno (2016).

## Key numbers

- Bandhead energy of the linear-chain states is about 33 MeV from the ground state of 16O
- Quadrupole deformation parameters increase from 0.59 for 2+ to 1.07 for 6+
- The point-alpha rms radius at the energy minimum for L=0,2,4 is approximately 3.52 fm (corresponding to H≈50)
- For L=0, the four alpha particles are positioned at S₁=-4.295 fm, S₂=-1.921 fm, S₃=1.302 fm, S₄=4.914 fm
- Relative distances in the L=0 configuration: D₁₂=2.68 fm, D₁₃=5.70 fm, D₁₄=9.22 fm, D₂₃=3.44 fm, D₂₄=6.90 fm, D₃₄=3.81 fm
- The correlated Gaussian for the L=0 minimum has K=43 and a=0.874
- No energy minimum appears for L>12 in the region H<130

## Context

This work extends the correlated Gaussian method, previously successful in few-body problems with small K values (0, 1, 2) as developed by Varga and Suzuki (1995), to describe strongly deformed states rotating with high angular momentum. The linear-chain structure in 16O was proposed by Morinaga (1956) and experimentally searched by Chevallier (1967) without firm confirmation. This study provides a theoretical framework for analyzing such states, complementing recent renewed interest in nuclear linear-chain states by Suhara (2010, 2011, 2014), Ichikawa (2011), Yao (2014), Zhao (2015), and others, and enables future calculations of decay widths and comparison with experimental data from Freer (1995, 2004, 2014) and Curtis (2016).
