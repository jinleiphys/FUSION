# Radioactivity of Quark Nuggets

> Quark nuggets $^A_ZQ$, as Fermionic non-topological solitons, could have their mass per baryon smaller than ordinary nuclei and behave as exotic nuclei with different relations of atomic number and atomic mass number. Using both the degenerate Fermi gas model and the Friedberg-Lee shell model, we calculate the properties of quark nuggets made of up and down quarks. Similar to ordinary nuclei, quark nuggets could exhibit their own radioactivity, including gamma decay, beta decay, and (explosive) spontaneous fission, with the qualitative properties presented here. These quark nugget properties may provide guidance for searching for quark nuggets in situ from binary neutron star mergers.

## Key claim

Quark nuggets composed of up and down quarks (without strange quarks) can exhibit radioactivity analogous to ordinary nuclei, including gamma decay, beta decay, and spontaneous fission, but only for a specific benchmark model point in the linear sigma model where the energy per baryon falls below that of iron, and the qualitative properties depend sensitively on the phenomenological model parameters that remain uncertain.

## Method

The authors used two complementary theoretical approaches: a degenerate Fermi gas model (Thomas-Fermi approximation) and the Friedberg-Lee shell model, both based on the linear sigma model with constituent quarks and a fixed Yukawa coupling g=3.55. For the degenerate Fermi gas model, they solved for the total energy density including vacuum, Fermi, and Coulomb contributions in both the bulk limit and with surface effects; for the Friedberg-Lee shell model, they solved the coupled Dirac and scalar field equations with a step-function approximation for the scalar profile to obtain quantized quark energy levels, then filled levels according to Pauli exclusion to compute total energies for given baryon number A and charge Z.

## Key numbers

- Minimum energy per baryon in the bulk limit: E/A|min = 905.6 MeV at pF = 368.7 MeV and σn = 8.55 MeV, using the degenerate Fermi gas model with the benchmark linear sigma model parameters
- The bag parameter extracted from the linear sigma model benchmark: B = (141.2 MeV)^4
- Surface thickness parameter minimizing E/A: amin = 0.0019 MeV^{-1}, found to be constant across different quark nugget radii
- For the Friedberg-Lee shell model with A=1000, the optimal radius is Rmin = 17 m0^{-1} where m0 = g vn = 327 MeV, yielding a naive Fermi momentum pF^naive = 368.7 MeV
- The minimum charge Zmin scales as A^{1/3} in the large-A limit, with an analytic formula Zmin = [5(2^{1/3} - 1) π^{1/3} A^{1/3}] / [2 × 6^{2/3} α] where α = 1/137

## Context

This work extends the earlier study of strange quark matter radioactivity by Berger (1986) using the degenerate Fermi gas model with a bag parameter, and also extends the two-flavor quark nugget modeling of Holdom (2017) by adding not only the degenerate Fermi gas approach but also the Friedberg-Lee shell model (Friedberg and Lee, 1976, 1977) which provides more precise modeling including excited states. The results enable future searches for quark nugget radioactivity signatures in binary neutron star merger events, analogous to how heavy element r-process nucleosynthesis was confirmed from GW170817 (LIGO/Virgo Collaboration, 2017; Pian, 2017).
