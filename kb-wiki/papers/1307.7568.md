# Heavy flavour in nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC and LHC: a Langevin approach

> A snapshot of the results for heavy-flavour observables in heavy-ion (AA) collisions at RHIC and LHC obtained with our transport calculations is displayed. The initial charm and beauty production is simulated through pQCD tools (POWHEG+PYTHIA) and is validated through the comparison with data from pp collisions. The propagation of c and b quarks in the medium formed in heavy-ion collisions is studied through a transport setup based on the relativistic Langevin equation. With respect to past works we perform a more systematic study, providing results with different choices of transport coefficients, either from weak-coupling calculations or from lattice-QCD simulations. Our findings are compared to a rich set of experimental data (D-mesons, non-photonic electrons, non-prompt J/ψ's) which have meanwhile become accessible.

# Heavy flavour in nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC and LHC: a Langevin approach

## Key claim
The POWLANG transport setup, based on the relativistic Langevin equation, can reproduce the quenching (R_AA) of heavy-flavour observables in AA collisions at both RHIC and LHC energies reasonably well up to momenta of about 10 GeV/c, but systematically underestimates the observed elliptic flow (v_2) of D mesons at the LHC, even with the most extreme choice for the thermalization time of the medium (tau_0 = 0.1 fm/c).

## Method
The authors simulate initial charm and beauty production using the POWHEG-BOX package at NLO accuracy interfaced with PYTHIA for parton showering, including nuclear PDF effects and Cronin broadening for AA collisions. Heavy-quark propagation through the quark-gluon plasma is modeled via the relativistic Langevin equation, with transport coefficients (kappa_T and kappa_L) taken from either weak-coupling hard-thermal-loop (HTL) calculations or from lattice-QCD simulations. The medium background is provided by hydrodynamic codes, and heavy quarks hadronize via standard in-vacuum fragmentation, with subsequent decay chains simulated for final-state observables (D mesons, non-photonic electrons, non-prompt J/psi).

## Key numbers
- D^0 meson R_AA in central (0-10%) Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV: results compared to preliminary STAR data, showing overall agreement with a peak structure possibly from coalescence.
- D-meson nuclear modification factor versus p_T in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV: results with HTL and lattice-QCD transport coefficients compared to ALICE data.
- Elliptic flow v_2 of D mesons in semi-peripheral (30-50%) Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC: results underestimate ALICE data even with tau_0 = 0.1 fm/c.
- Non-prompt J/psi R_AA from B decays in minimum-bias Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC: results show good agreement with preliminary CMS data.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous transport calculations (Beraudo et al., 2012; Beraudo et al., 2013) by including a more realistic simulation of initial heavy-quark production, a wider systematic study of transport coefficients from both weak-coupling and lattice-QCD approaches, and comparison to newly available experimental data from STAR, PHENIX, ALICE, and CMS. The results enable future work on in-medium hadronization via coalescence, heavy-flavour correlations, and forward-rapidity predictions using (3+1)D hydrodynamic codes. Independent approaches include those by Rapp, Aichelin, and the BAMPS group.
