# Dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and confinement with an infrared-vanishing gluon propagator?

> We study a model Dyson-Schwinger equation for the quark propagator closed using an {\it Ansatz} for the gluon propagator of the form \mbox{$D(q) \sim q^2/[(q^2)^2 + b^4]$} and two {\it Ansätze} for the quark-gluon vertex: the minimal Ball-Chiu and the modified form suggested by Curtis and Pennington. Using the quark condensate as an order parameter, we find that there is a critical value of $b=b_c$ such that the model does not support dynamical chiral symmetry breaking for $b>b_c$. We discuss and apply a confinement test which suggests that, for all values of $b$, the quark propagator in the model {\bf is not} confining. Together these results suggest that this Ansatz for the gluon propagator is inadequate as a model since it does not yield the expected behaviour of QCD.

## Key claim

Using a Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) model closed with an infrared-vanishing gluon propagator of the form \(D(q) \sim q^2/[(q^2)^2 + b^4]\) and two different quark-gluon vertex Ansätze (minimal Ball-Chiu and Curtis-Pennington), the authors find that the model does not support dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB) for \(b > b_c\) and, crucially, that the quark propagator is **not** confining for any value of \(b\). The paper concludes that this gluon propagator Ansatz is inadequate as a model of QCD because it fails to produce the expected behavior of both DCSB and confinement.

## Method

The authors solved the renormalized Dyson-Schwinger equation for the quark propagator in Euclidean space, closed by an Ansatz for the gluon propagator that vanishes at \(q^2=0\) (Eq. 1) and two Ansätze for the quark-gluon vertex: the minimal Ball-Chiu vertex and the Curtis-Pennington vertex. The gluon propagator was ultraviolet-improved by combining the infrared form with a running coupling \(\alpha(\tau; Q^2)\) containing an infrared regularization parameter \(\tau\). The coupled integral equations for the scalar functions \(A(p^2)\) and \(B(p^2)\) were solved numerically by iteration on a logarithmic grid, and the quark condensate was used as an order parameter for DCSB. A confinement test based on the large-time behavior of the scalar part of the Schwinger function was applied to determine whether the quark propagator represents a confined particle.

## Key numbers

- Critical exponent for the chiral phase transition with the minimal Ball-Chiu vertex: \(\beta_{\rm BC} = 0.575 \pm 0.024\) (or \(0.572 \pm 0.020\) excluding the \(\ln\tau=0\) point).
- Critical exponent with the Curtis-Pennington vertex at \(\ln\tau=0.6\): \(\beta_{\rm CP} = 0.579 \pm 0.015\).
- The ultraviolet cutoff used in the numerical solutions was \(\Lambda^2 = 5 \times 10^8 \;\Lambda_{\rm QCD}^2\).
- The quark condensate was evaluated at a renormalization point of \(\mu^2 = 1\;\text{GeV}^2\).
- For the family of propagators with \(\ln\tau = 0.1\) and \(b^2\) in the range \([0.1, 1.0]\), the extracted dressed-quark mass decreases with increasing \(b^2\), and an unambiguous finite mass was obtained, indicating a non-confining propagator.

## Context

This work extends DSE studies of QCD that aim to develop nonperturbative approaches competitive with lattice QCD, following earlier DSE studies of the gluon propagator by Stingl (1986, 1990) and the Gribov-copy analysis by Zwanziger (1991). The infrared-vanishing gluon propagator form had been suggested by these studies and by lattice simulations (Bernard et al., 1993), but the present paper tests it in a quark DSE framework and finds it inadequate. The results contradict the expectation that such a gluon propagator would lead to a confining quark propagator, and they complement the study of Ref. [RM90] which found a critical value of \(\ln\tau = 1.69\) for \(b^2=0\). The confinement test method is adapted from lattice QCD and follows the approach of Ref. [HRM92]. The paper enables future work to explore other gluon propagator Ansätze that can simultaneously yield DCSB and confinement.
