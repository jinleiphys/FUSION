# Refraction of Gluon jet in QGP

> We introduce photon-like refraction for gluon jet in quark gluon plasma created in high energy nucleus-nucleus collisions. A quark jet goes straightly while a gluon jet is assumed to bend in the non-homogeneous medium. Combining refraction and energy loss, we calculate the away-side dihadron $Δϕ$ distribution in central $Au+Au$ collisions at RHIC energy. The dihadrons from quark-quark pair center around $Δϕ=π$, while the dihadrons from quark-gluon and gluon-gluon pair are deflected from $Δϕ=π$. The resulting distribution shows three peaks at intermediate $p_T$ since both quark-quark and quark-gluon are important. However we only observe one center peak at high $p_T$, where the quark-quark contribution dominates. These observation qualitatively agrees with the experiment data.

# Refraction of Gluon jet in QGP

## Key claim
The authors propose that gluon jets, unlike quark jets, undergo photon-like refraction in the non-homogeneous quark-gluon plasma created in high-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions. This refraction, combined with energy loss, can qualitatively reproduce the observed away-side dihadron azimuthal angle distribution, though the calculated side-peak location of 0.72 radians under-predicts the experimental value of 1.1 radians, suggesting additional deflection mechanisms may be needed.

## Method
The authors implement gluon jet refraction using an optical analogy, where the refractive index for a gluon jet is proportional to the gluon density of the medium, with a fixed parameter κ fitted to data. They combine this with a NLO pQCD parton model including jet quenching via induced gluon radiation, calculating the away-side dihadron Δφ distribution separately for quark-quark, quark-gluon, and gluon-gluon dijet processes in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy.

## Key numbers
- Energy loss parameter ε₀ = 1.7 GeV/fm used in the calculation, constrained by simultaneous fit to single and dihadron data within range ε₀ = 1.6-2.1 GeV/fm
- Refractive index parameter κ = 0.25 chosen for most central Au+Au collisions
- Side-peak location at Δφ = π ± 0.72 radians, compared to experimental value of 1.1 radians
- Gluon jet deflection angle θ_c = 0.72 radians for dihadrons from quark-gluon dijets
- Medium parameters: μ₀ = 1.5 GeV, ε₀λ₀ = 0.5 GeV, τ₀ = 0.2 fm/c
- For LHC energy, gluon-gluon and quark-gluon dominate over quark-quark up to p_T ~ 100 GeV/c

## Context
This work extends the NLO pQCD jet quenching framework of Zhang, Owens, and Wang (2007) by introducing gluon refraction as a new mechanism for the away-side jet modification observed by PHENIX at RHIC. It contrasts with other proposed mechanisms such as Mach cones (Casalderrey-Solana, 2004), large angle gluon radiation (Vitev, 2005; Polosa, 2006), Cherenkov gluon radiation (Koch, 2005), or deflected jets (Armesto, 2004; Chiu, 2006). The calculation qualitatively describes the evolution of the away-side distribution from broad to concave to convex shapes with increasing p_T, but the under-prediction of the split angle opens the door for three-dimensional expansion effects or additional mechanisms to be included in future work.
