# Effective Continuum Thresholds for Quark-Hadron Duality in Dispersive Sum Rules

> Modifying the standard approaches to nonperturbative QCD based on Borel-transformed dispersive sum rules by allowing the effective continuum thresholds required for the implementation of quark-hadron duality to depend on the Borel parameters and on any relevant momentum promises to provide higher accuracy and reliable error estimates for the extracted predictions of hadron characteristics. A careful analysis reveals that the exact effective continuum thresholds do indeed exhibit dependence on the Borel parameter (and on external momenta) and that they are not universal but vary with the correlators under consideration. The striking similarity of our hadron-parameter extraction procedures in QCD, on the one hand, and quantum-theoretical potential models, on the other hand, calls for application of the proposed techniques in QCD.

# Effective Continuum Thresholds for Quark-Hadron Duality in Dispersive Sum Rules

## Key claim
Allowing effective continuum thresholds to depend on the Borel parameter and on any relevant momentum, rather than treating them as constants, improves the accuracy of QCD sum rule predictions and enables reliable error estimates; however, the exact thresholds are not universal but vary with the specific correlator under study, and their extraction requires a polynomial ansatz fitted within a Borel window.

## Method
The authors test their approach using an exactly solvable nonrelativistic quantum-mechanical harmonic oscillator model, computing Borelized 2-point and 3-point correlators and vacuum-to-hadron amplitudes. They define dual correlators that exactly reproduce the ground-state contributions, extract the exact effective continuum thresholds as functions of the Borel parameter T and momentum q, and then fit polynomial ansatze for these thresholds by minimizing the squared difference between dual energies and the known ground-state energy within the Borel window. The same procedure is then applied to QCD for the B meson system using the Borelized correlator of two pseudoscalar quark currents.

## Key numbers
- Ground-state energy of the harmonic oscillator model: \(E_\mathrm{g} = \frac{3}{2}\,\omega\)
- Ground-state decay constant (squared wavefunction at origin): \(R_\mathrm{g} \equiv |\psi_\mathrm{g}(\mathbf{x}=\mathbf{0})|^2 = \left(\frac{m\,\omega}{\pi}\right)^{3/2}\)
- Ground-state elastic form factor: \(F_\mathrm{g}(q) = \exp\left(\frac{-q^2}{4\,m\,\omega}\right)\)
- For the B meson in QCD, the dual mass \(M_\mathrm{dual}^2(\tau)\) and decay constant \(f_\mathrm{dual}^2(\tau)\) are extracted by fitting a polynomial threshold \(s^{(n)}_\mathrm{eff}(\tau)=\sum_{j=0}^n s_j^{(n)}\,\tau^j\) minimizing \(\chi^2 = \frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^N \left[M^2_\mathrm{dual}(\tau_i)-M_\mathrm{B}^2\right]^2\) for \(\tau_i\) in the Borel window.

## Context
This work extends the authors' prior program (Lucha, Melikhov, Simula, 2007-2010) of quantifying uncertainties in Shifman-Vainshtein-Zakharov QCD sum rules by allowing Borel-parameter-dependent continuum thresholds. It demonstrates the method first in a quantum-mechanical harmonic oscillator model where exact results are known, then applies it to QCD for the B meson decay constant. The striking similarity between the extraction procedures in potential models and in QCD calls for further application of these techniques to extract hadron properties such as decay constants and form factors with controlled uncertainties.
