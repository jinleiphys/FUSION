# Quarkyonic matter state of neutron stars

> This work extends our previous study of isospin symmetric quarkyonic matter to quarkyonic neutron matter which might be relevant to the inner cores of neutron stars. The vector-isovector $ρ$ mesons are introduced to the model mainly to account for isospin density interactions, just like $ω$ meson for baryon density interactions. The modified Lagrangian still preserves approximate chiral symmetry which could be significantly broken at lower density. And new free parameters are fixed by adopting the experimental constraints on the symmetry energy and its slope at saturation density. Eventually, the pressure, mass-radius relation, and tidal deformability are explored in advance for the quarkyonic neutron stars. While the pressure and tidal deformability are well consistent with experimental and observational restrictions, the mass-radius relation is unable to reproduce the observed two solar mass of PSR J0740+6620.

## Key claim
The quarkyonic neutron matter model, extended from isospin-symmetric quarkyonic matter by introducing vector-isovector rho mesons, produces pressure and tidal deformability consistent with experimental and observational constraints, but its mass-radius relation is unable to reproduce the observed two solar mass of PSR J0740+6620 because the equation of state is too soft.

## Method
The author extended a previous field-theoretical model for isospin-symmetric quarkyonic matter to isospin-asymmetric neutron matter by adding rho meson interactions to account for isospin density effects. New free parameters were fixed by fitting to the rho meson vacuum mass and to experimental constraints on the symmetry energy (32 MeV) and its slope (60 MeV) at saturation density. The resulting equation of state was then used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equation and compute the tidal deformability for neutron star structure.

## Key numbers
- Symmetry energy at saturation density: fixed to \(E_{\rm sym}(n_0)=32\) MeV, consistent with the experimental constraint \(31.6\pm2.7\) MeV from Oertel (2016).
- Slope of symmetry energy at saturation density: fixed to \(L_{\rm sym}(n_0)=60\) MeV, consistent with the experimental constraint \(58.7\pm28.1\) MeV from Oertel (2016).
- New fitted parameters: \(g_{\rm N\rho}=3.491\), \(g_{\rm s\rho}=92.49\), \(h_\rho=446.5\) MeV.
- Radius of a \(1.4 M_\odot\) neutron star: \(12.94\) km, within the NICER constraint \(R_{1.4}\in (11.39,13.10)\) km from Riley (2021) and Miller (2021).
- Tidal deformability for a \(1.4 M_\odot\) neutron star: within the GW170817 constraint \(70\leq\Lambda(1.4M_\odot)\leq580\) from LIGO Scientific (2018).

## Context
This work extends the author's previous study of isospin-symmetric quarkyonic matter (Cao 2020) to the isospin-asymmetric case relevant for neutron star cores, introducing rho mesons to handle isospin density interactions. The model fails to reach the two-solar-mass maximum observed for PSR J0740+6620 (Fonseca 2021), unlike stiffer equations of state from other approaches such as McLerran (2018) or Kojo (2021). The author suggests future improvements by incorporating gluon degrees of freedom via a Polyakov loop potential to suppress early quark dominance and produce a peak in the sound velocity.
