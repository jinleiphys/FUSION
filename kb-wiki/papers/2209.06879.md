# Polarization in heavy ion collisions: a theoretical review

> In these proceedings I discuss the recent progress in the theory of spin polarization in relativistic fluids. To date, a number of studies have begun to examine the impact of the shear tensor on the local spin polarization and whether this contribution can restore agreement between the measurements and the predictions obtained from a polarization induced by the gradients of the plasma. I present the derivation of the spin polarization vector of a fermion at local thermal equilibrium and I discuss the role of pseudo-gauge transformations and of dissipative effects. I list what we can learn from the polarization measured at lower energies. Finally, I discuss possible applications of spin polarization measurements in relativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the long-standing "sign puzzle" in local spin polarization of Λ hyperons, where models based solely on thermal vorticity predicted the wrong sign for the longitudinal component, can be resolved by including the contribution of the thermal shear tensor, and that using an isothermal local equilibrium approximation (rather than a Taylor expansion of the temperature) yields quantitative agreement with experimental data at 200 GeV and 5 TeV.

## Method

The author derives the spin polarization vector of a fermion at local thermal equilibrium from the Wigner function formalism, expanding the statistical operator to first order in gradients of the four-temperature and chemical potential. This yields a Cooper-Frye-like formula containing three contributions: the thermal vorticity term, a recently identified thermal shear term, and a gradient-of-chemical-potential (spin Hall) term. For high-energy collisions where the freeze-out temperature is constant, the author advocates using an isothermal local equilibrium operator that involves only gradients of the fluid velocity (kinematic vorticity and shear), avoiding the unnecessary Taylor expansion of temperature.

## Key numbers

- Global polarization predictions from the vorticity-only formula were confirmed in 2017 with Λ hyperons in Au-Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV.
- The thermal shear tensor at the freeze-out hypersurface can be larger than the thermal vorticity.
- Local spin polarization data were taken at 200 GeV and at 5 TeV.
- A maximum of spin polarization is expected to occur around √s_NN = 7 GeV.
- The leading-order correction to the spin-rotation coupling from strong interactions is expected to be g_Ω = 1 - (N_c^2 - 1)/2 × (1/6) × (g^2 T^2 / m^2) for T ≪ m, resulting in a reduction of about 40% in the spin polarization of the strange quark in a typical event.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of spin polarization in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, which began with the prediction of global polarization proportional to fluid vorticity (Liang 2004, Becattini 2007) and its confirmation by STAR (2017). It directly addresses the "local polarization sign puzzle" identified in later measurements (Adam 2019, Niida 2018, ALICE 2021), which could not be explained by feed-down corrections (Xia 2019, Becattini 2019). The inclusion of thermal shear (Becattini 2021, Liu 2021, Yi 2021) and the isothermal formulation (Becattini 2021) now provide quantitative agreement with data. The paper enables future use of spin polarization as a probe of quark-gluon plasma properties, including vorticity structure, local parity violation, jet-plasma interactions, and tests of the Einstein Equivalence Principle at finite temperature.
