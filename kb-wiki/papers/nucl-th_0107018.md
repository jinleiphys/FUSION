# Effects of Short-Range Correlations in (e,e'p) reactions and nuclear overlap functions

> A study of the effects of short-range correlations over the (e,e'p) reaction for low missing energy in closed shell nuclei is presented. We use correlated, quasi-hole overlap functions extracted from the asymptotic behavior of the one-body density matrix, containing central correlations of Jastrow type, up to first-order in a cluster expansion, and computed in the very high asymptotic region, up to 100 fm. The method to extract the overlap functions is checked in a simple shell model, where the exact results are known. We find that the single-particle wave functions of the valence shells are shifted to the right due to the short-range repulsion by the nuclear core. The corresponding spectroscopic factors are reduced only a few percent with respect to the shell model. However, the (e,e'p) response functions and cross sections are enhanced in the region of the maximum of the missing momentum distribution due to short-range correlations.

## Key claim

Short-range correlations of Jastrow type, treated to first order in a cluster expansion, shift the single-particle wave functions of valence shells outward and enhance the (e,e'p) response functions and cross sections near the maximum of the missing momentum distribution, even though spectroscopic factors are reduced by only a few percent relative to the shell model.

## Method

The authors extract correlated quasi-hole overlap functions from the asymptotic behavior (up to 100 fm) of the one-body density matrix (OBDM) for closed-shell nuclei, using a Jastrow correlation function with Gaussian form and parameters A=0.7, B=2.2 fm⁻². They apply the "√ρ-method" (taking the ratio ρ_l(r,a)/√ρ_l(a,a) in the limit a→∞) to obtain radial overlap functions from the OBDM multipoles, and then use these overlap functions in a distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) model of the (e,e'p) reaction with a relativistic current expansion to first order in missing momentum.

## Key numbers

- Spectroscopic factors for the p₁/₂ and p₃/₂ shells of ¹⁶O are reported as 0.981 in a prior work (Gai00), but the authors note this equality likely arises from using an OBDM computed only up to 11 fm, which is insufficient to separate the two spin-orbit partners.
- The Jastrow correlation parameters used are A=0.7 and B=2.2 fm⁻², fixed by minimizing binding energies for the Afnan and Tang S3 interaction (Ari96).
- The OBDM is computed up to distances of approximately 100 fm to ensure convergence of the asymptotic extraction procedure.

## Context

This work extends the asymptotic method for extracting overlap functions from the OBDM, originally developed by Van Neck (1993) and applied by Stoitsov (1996) and Gai (2000), but addresses problems with prior numerical restoration procedures that used harmonic-oscillator wave functions or insufficient asymptotic range. The authors use Woods-Saxon single-particle wave functions with spin-orbit splitting, enabling separation of spin-orbit partners (e.g., p₁/₂ and p₃/₂ in ¹⁶O) that were not distinguished in Gai (2000). The results enable more reliable calculations of (e,e'p) cross sections and response functions for low missing energy, and the method can be applied to other reactions such as (γ,p).
