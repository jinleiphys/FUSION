# Sensitivity of EDM experiments in paramagnetic atoms and molecules to hadronic CP violation

> Experiments searching for the electric dipole moment (EDM) of the electron $d_e$ utilise atomic/molecular states with one or more uncompensated electron spins, and these paramagnetic systems have recently achieved remarkable sensitivity to $d_e$. If the source of $CP$ violation resides entirely in the hadronic sector, the two-photon exchange processes between electrons and the nucleus induce $CP$-odd semileptonic interactions, parametrised by the Wilson coefficient $C_{SP}$, and provide the dominant source of EDMs in paramagnetic systems instead of $d_e$. We evaluate the $C_{SP}$ coefficients induced by the leading hadronic sources of $CP$ violation, namely nucleon EDMs and $CP$-odd pion-nucleon couplings, by calculating the nucleon-number-enhanced $CP$-odd nuclear scalar polarisability, employing chiral perturbation theory at the nucleon level and the Fermi-gas model for the nucleus. This allows us to translate the ACME EDM limits from paramagnetic ThO into novel independent constraints on the QCD theta term $|\bar θ| < 3 \times 10^{-8}$, proton EDM $|d_p| < 2 \times 10^{-23}\,e\,{\rm cm}$, isoscalar $CP$-odd pion-nucleon coupling $|\bar g^{(1)}_{πNN}| < 4 \times 10^{-10}$, and colour EDMs of quarks $|\tilde d_u - \tilde d_d| < 2 \times 10^{-24}\,{\rm cm}$. We note that further experimental progress with EDM experiments in paramagnetic systems may allow them to rival the sensitivity of EDM experiments with neutrons and diamagnetic atoms to these quantities.

## Key claim

If the source of CP violation resides entirely in the hadronic sector, the dominant contribution to electric dipole moments (EDMs) in paramagnetic atoms and molecules comes from CP-odd semileptonic interactions mediated by two-photon exchange (parametrised by the Wilson coefficient C_SP) rather than from the electron EDM d_e itself, and the authors evaluate these C_SP coefficients to translate existing experimental limits into novel independent constraints on hadronic CP-violating parameters.

## Method

The authors calculated the CP-odd nuclear scalar polarisability induced by nucleon EDMs and CP-odd pion-nucleon couplings, employing chiral perturbation theory at the nucleon level for the leading-order (pi0 and eta meson exchange) and next-to-leading-order (charged-pion loop) contributions, and a Fermi-gas model for the nucleus to compute the two-photon exchange processes involving nucleon magnetic and electric dipole moments. They then integrated over the diphoton loop to obtain the semileptonic operator C_SP and averaged the result over the nucleon content of a heavy nucleus, using the ACME ThO experimental limit on C_SP to derive constraints on hadronic CP-violating parameters.

## Key numbers

* QCD theta term: |theta_bar| < 3 x 10^-8 from ThO EDM limits
* Proton EDM: |d_p| < 2 x 10^-23 e cm from ThO EDM limits
* Isoscalar CP-odd pion-nucleon coupling: |g_bar^(1)_(piNN)| < 4 x 10^-10 from ThO EDM limits
* Colour EDMs of quarks: |tilde_d_u - tilde_d_d| < 2 x 10^-24 cm from ThO EDM limits
* ACME ThO constraint on effective C_SP coupling: |C_SP^s - 0.22 C_SP^t| < 7.3 x 10^-10 at 90% confidence level
* Fermi momentum used in nuclear model: p_F approximately 250 MeV
* NLO polarisability contribution to C_SP(theta_bar) for heavy nucleus: approximately 1.0 x 10^-2 theta_bar
* mu-d contribution to C_SP(theta_bar) for heavy nucleus: approximately 1.7 x 10^-2 theta_bar

## Context

This work extends the analysis of hadronic CP violation in paramagnetic systems beyond earlier treatments that focused on the electron EDM d_e, such as those by Choi (1990) and Ghosh (2017), by showing that the C_SP semileptonic operator provides the dominant sensitivity when CP violation originates in the hadronic sector. The derived constraints on the QCD theta term and isoscalar pion-nucleon coupling from ThO are about a factor of 100 less stringent than those from neutron EDM experiments and Hg EDM experiments respectively, but the authors note that the Hg limits on g_bar^(1)_(piNN) are subject to significant nuclear structure uncertainties as shown by Ban (2010), making the ThO results a valuable independent constraint. Further experimental progress with paramagnetic systems may allow them to rival the sensitivity of neutron and diamagnetic atom EDM experiments to these hadronic CP-violating quantities.
