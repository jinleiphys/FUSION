# Microscopic Investigation of Ground State Properties and Shape Evolution in Osmium Isotopes

> The present study focuses on investigating the shape evolution of neutron-rich even-even Osmium (Os) transitional nuclei within the range of neutron number N = 82 to N = 190. The investigation is conducted using density-dependent meson-nucleon and point-coupling models within the framework of the covariant density functional theory (CDFT). Additionally, the results obtained from the CDFT calculations are compared with those obtained using the relativistic mean-field model with a non-linear meson-nucleon interaction. The potential energy curve for Os isotopes (ranging from $^{158}$Os to $^{260}$Os) is analyzed in order to identify phase shape transitions, such as oblate-spherical-prolate. Furthermore, ground state bulk properties are calculated to gain insights into the structure of Os isotopes. The self-consistent calculations reveal a clear shape transition in the even-even Os isotopes, and overall, good agreement is observed among the different models employed as well as with the available experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that covariant density functional theory (CDFT) calculations reveal a clear shape transition in even-even osmium isotopes from spherical to prolate to oblate and back to spherical as neutron number increases, but this conclusion is qualified by the fact that the study only considers axially deformed cases, explicitly excluding triaxial symmetry which the authors acknowledge plays a crucial role in these nuclei.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent calculations using two classes of covariant density functional models: density-dependent meson-exchange (DD-ME1 and DD-ME2) and density-dependent point-coupling (DD-PC1 and DD-PCX) interactions within CDFT, alongside relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations with the nonlinear NL3* parameter set and BCS pairing. The axially deformed harmonic oscillator basis was employed with basis size N_F = N_B = 20 for both fermions and bosons. Binding energies, quadrupole deformation parameters, radii, and potential energy curves were computed for even-even osmium isotopes from ^158Os to ^260Os and compared with non-relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations using Skyrme interactions (SkP, SLy4, SkM*) and CHFB+5DCH calculations with the Gogny D1S interaction.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per nucleon reaches a maximum at A = 182 for nearly all interactions, identifying ^182Os as the most stable isotope in the chain
- Total binding energies range from approximately 1190.90 MeV (DD-ME1 for ^156Os) to 1688.43 MeV (DD-PC1 for ^260Os)
- Experimental binding energies are available for isotopes from ^162Os (1262.47 MeV) to ^202Os (1584.08 MeV), with which the calculations show good agreement
- The charge radius is calculated using the relation r_c = sqrt(r_p^2 + 0.64), where 0.64 accounts for the finite size of the proton (units: fm^2)
- The quadrupole deformation parameter beta_2 is extracted from quadrupole moments using R = 1.2 A^(1/3) fm

## Context

This work extends previous studies of shape evolution in transitional nuclei with Z = 72-80, building on investigations by Nomura and collaborators, Ramos (2014), and earlier relativistic functional studies by Afana and collaborators (2014, 2016). The results are compared with non-relativistic calculations using Skyrme functionals (SkP, SLy4, SkM*) from prior work (referenced as [19] in the text) and CHFB+5DCH calculations with the Gogny D1S interaction (referenced as [39]). The study enables systematic comparison of relativistic and non-relativistic density functionals for predicting shape transitions in neutron-rich osmium isotopes up to the neutron drip line, though the authors note that future work should incorporate triaxial degrees of freedom which were excluded from this investigation.
