# Higher Order Hadronic Energy Level Shifts in Protonium from Effective Field Theory

## Key claim
Using effective field theory for a proton and antiproton bound in a Coulomb potential, the ground state energy shift can be calculated to arbitrary order in the scattering length, and including the next order contact interaction yields the correction due to the effective range parameter. However, the result for the effective range correction contains an explicit dependence on the renormalization point μ, which the authors remove by choosing μ = γ (the ground state momentum), making the first term vanish.

## Method
The authors construct an effective Lagrangian for non-relativistic proton and antiproton fields, including local contact interactions up to order C₂ (the effective range term), with the Coulomb interaction treated exactly. They sum the infinite series of bubble diagrams for the scattering amplitude, regularize power divergences using minimal subtraction at a non-zero momentum (equivalent to power-divergence subtraction or PDS), and then apply the same resummation technique to the bound state problem in the Coulomb potential to obtain the energy level shifts.

## Key numbers
- Ground state unperturbed energy: E₀ = −γ²/2m, where γ = mα is the momentum in the ground state.
- Leading energy shift from scattering length: ΔE₀(C₀) = mα² × 2a/(b − a) = 2a m²α³ [1 + a/b + …], where b = 1/γ is the Bohr radius and a is the scattering length.
- Full energy shift including effective range correction: ΔE₀ = a m²α³ [2b/(b − a) − a r₀/(b − a)²], where r₀ is the effective range.
- The renormalized coupling C₂ is related to the effective range by C₂^R(μ) = (m r₀ / 8π) C₀^R(μ)².

## Context
This work extends the effective field theory approach of Weinberg (1990) and the subsequent developments by Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1996) and Gegelia (1997) for nucleon-nucleon systems to the hadronic atom protonium. The leading term in the energy shift was first derived by Deser et al. (1954), with electromagnetic corrections later obtained by Trueman (1961) and Kudryavtsev and Popov (1979). The results enable calculation of hyperfine splitting in protonium in terms of spin-dependent scattering lengths and effective ranges, and can incorporate hadronic annihilation effects by allowing complex scattering lengths as reviewed by Batty (1987).
