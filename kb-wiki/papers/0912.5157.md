# $K/π$, $K/p$ and $p/π$ Ratio Fluctuations within the HSD Transport Approach

> Particle number fluctuations and correlations in nucleus-nucleus collisions at SPS and RHIC energies have been studied within the Hadron-String-Dynamics (HSD) transport approach. Event-by-event fluctuations of pion-to-kaon, proton-to-pion and kaon-to-proton number ratios are calculated for the samples of most central collision events and compared with the available experimental data. It has been found that the HSD model can qualitatively reproduce the measured excitation function for the $K/π$ ratio fluctuations in central Au+Au (or Pb+Pb) collisions from low SPS up to top RHIC energies. These predictions impose a challenge for future experiments.

# K/π, K/p and p/π Ratio Fluctuations within the HSD Transport Approach

## Key claim
The Hadron-String-Dynamics (HSD) transport model can qualitatively reproduce the measured excitation function for K/π ratio fluctuations in central Au+Au (or Pb+Pb) collisions from low SPS up to top RHIC energies, but the comparison with preliminary STAR data is not fully conclusive: HSD results approximately agree with data for the kaon-to-proton ratio but overestimate experimental results for the proton-to-pion ratio.

## Method
Event-by-event fluctuations of the K/π, K/p, and p/π multiplicity ratios (with K = K⁺ + K⁻, π = π⁺ + π⁻, and p = p + p̅) were calculated within the HSD transport approach for central Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions. The dynamical fluctuation measure σ_dyn was computed by subtracting the mixed-event baseline from the full event-by-event variance, and experimental acceptance cuts (pseudorapidity and transverse momentum) as well as centrality selections (3.5% most central for NA49, 5% most central for STAR) were applied to the simulated events to enable direct comparison with data.

## Key numbers
- At the lowest SPS energy, the UrQMD model gives σ_dyn^{Kπ} values about 40% smaller than the HSD results.
- Switching from NA49 acceptance to STAR acceptance at 160 A GeV leads to a jump in σ_dyn^{Kp} of 3%.
- For RHIC energies, the acceptance cuts used were |η| < 1, with 0.2 < p_T < 0.6 GeV/c for kaons and pions and 0.4 < p_T < 1 GeV/c for protons.
- The NA49 data correspond to 3.5% most central collisions; STAR data correspond to 5% most central events.

## Context
This work extends earlier event-by-event fluctuation studies by the NA49 Collaboration (Af01, Al08) and the STAR Collaboration (Da06, Ab09) by providing HSD transport model calculations for the excitation function of K/π, K/p, and p/π ratio fluctuations from SPS to RHIC energies. The results are compared with UrQMD calculations (Al08, Ro04, KrFr06) and with statistical model calculations (GoHaKoBr09, KoHaGoBr09). The HSD model's ability to reproduce the K/π fluctuation data while overestimating p/π fluctuations at RHIC poses a challenge for future experiments and calls for new data to clarify the situation.
