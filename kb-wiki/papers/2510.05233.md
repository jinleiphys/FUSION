# Ab initio study of the neutron and Fermi polarons on the lattice

> We have used the auxiliary-field quantum Monte Carlo (AFQMC) many-body approach on the lattice to study the equation of state for a fermionic impurity interacting with a background sea of spin-polarized fermions. The impurity, or polaron, is an interesting system in both cold atomic and nuclear physics. Our approach is general, and we are able to straightforwardly study the polaron across these regimes. We first study the Fermi polaron at unitarity and for a wide range of scattering lengths, comparing against previous theoretical and experimental studies. We then explore the neutron polaron which has been shown to be an important constraint for nuclear physics. We have also employed the recently developed parametric matrix model to emulate AFQMC solutions to the two-body problem on the lattice, to accelerate the tuning of our lattice Hamiltonian parameters directly to two-body energies in a periodic box, following Luscher's formula. Our lattice quantum Monte Carlo results for the polaron in both a cold atomic and nuclear physics context can serve as stringent benchmarks for future theoretical and experimental research.

## Key claim

The paper claims that auxiliary-field quantum Monte Carlo (AFQMC) on the lattice can be used to study both the cold-atomic Fermi polaron and the neutron polaron within a single many-body Hamiltonian framework, but the results are subject to the constrained path approximation to control the fermion sign problem and require extrapolation to the continuum limit to provide meaningful predictions.

## Method

The authors employed the auxiliary-field quantum Monte Carlo (AFQMC) method on a discretized spatial lattice with a Hamiltonian containing a kinetic energy term with a modified dispersion relation and an attractive on-site Hubbard interaction. They tuned the lattice Hamiltonian parameters (the dispersion modifier gamma and the interaction strength U) to reproduce two-body scattering properties by using a parametric matrix model (PMM) emulator trained on AFQMC calculations for small lattices, then matching the emulated energies to Luscher's formula for a periodic box. The polaron energy was computed as the difference between the total energy of the system with one impurity plus N background particles and the energy of the N background particles alone, with results extrapolated to the continuum limit by varying lattice size and spacing at fixed filling factor.

## Key numbers

* Fermi polaron energy at unitarity (infinite scattering length, zero effective range) was computed and extrapolated to the continuum limit of zero filling factor, showing agreement with previous diagrammatic Monte Carlo calculations by Prokofev (2008) and experiments by Schirotzek (2009) and Yan (2019).
* Neutron-neutron s-wave scattering length of approximately -18.5 fm and effective range of roughly 2.7 fm were used as target parameters for tuning the lattice interaction.
* The PMM emulator achieved less than 1 percent error when compared to the test set withheld from the model, as shown for a 4x4 PMM on an 11^3 lattice.
* Full AFQMC calculations of the two-neutron system at larger lattice sizes (beyond the 7^3, 9^3, and 11^3 lattices used for tuning) showed less than 1 percent disagreement with the exact Luscher's formula result.

## Context

This work extends previous AFQMC studies of cold-atomic systems by Carlson (2011) and low-density neutrons by Curry (2023) to the polaron problem, and provides the first lattice QMC calculations of the neutron polaron. The results update constraints from earlier diffusion Monte Carlo and chiral effective field theory calculations by Forbes (2013) and configuration interaction Monte Carlo by Roggero (2014), and enable direct comparison between cold-atomic and nuclear physics regimes of the polaron, which can serve as benchmarks for future energy-density functional development and experimental studies of neutron-rich matter.
