# Probing the density dependence of the symmetry potential at low and high densities

> We investigate the sensitivity of several observables to the density dependence of the symmetry potential within the microscopic transport model UrQMD (ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics model). The same systems are used to probe the symmetry potential at both low and high densities. The influence of the symmetry potentials on the yields of $π^{-}$, $π^{+}$, the $π^{-}/π^{+}$ ratio, the $n/p$ ratio of free nucleons and the $t/^3$He ratio are studied for neutron-rich heavy ion collisions ($^{208}Pb+^{208}Pb$, $^{132}Sn+^{124}Sn$, $^{96}Zr+^{96}Zr$) at $E_b=0.4A {\rm GeV}$. We find that these multiple probes provides comprehensive information on the density dependence of the symmetry potential.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that multiple observables from the same heavy-ion reaction system can simultaneously probe the density dependence of the symmetry potential at both subnormal and high densities, but the sensitivity of each observable is restricted to a specific density region: charged pion yields and the π⁻/π⁺ ratio at low momenta are sensitive to high-density behavior, while the n/p ratio of free nucleons and the t/³He ratio are sensitive to subnormal-density behavior.

## Method

The authors performed UrQMD (version 1.3) transport model simulations of central collisions for three neutron-rich systems (²⁰⁸Pb+²⁰⁸Pb, ¹³²Sn+¹²⁴Sn, ⁹⁶Zr+⁹⁶Zr) at a beam energy of 0.4A GeV, using a hard Skyrme-type equation of state (K=300 MeV) without momentum dependence. Four different forms of the symmetry potential were employed: two from Skyrme-type interactions (F15 with γ=1.5 and Fa3 with a=3) and two from relativistic mean-field theory (DDHρ* and DDH3ρδ*), all normalized to a symmetry energy coefficient S₀=34 MeV at normal nuclear density. A conventional phase-space coalescence model (P₀=0.3 GeV/c, R₀=3.5 fm) was used to construct clusters, and the freeze-out time was taken as 150 fm/c.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: E_b = 0.4A GeV for all three reaction systems
- Isospin asymmetry values: ²⁰⁸Pb+²⁰⁸Pb has δ ≃ 0.212 (N/Z ≃ 1.54), ¹³²Sn+¹²⁴Sn has δ ≃ 0.218, ⁹⁶Zr+⁹⁶Zr has δ ≃ 0.167
- Symmetry energy coefficient at normal density: S₀ = 34 MeV
- For the π⁻/π⁺ ratio at p_t^cm ≃ 0.1 GeV/c in ²⁰⁸Pb+²⁰⁸Pb, the maximum relative difference D_max between symmetry potential forms is 18%
- For the π⁻/π⁺ ratio at p_t^cm ≃ 0.1 GeV/c in ¹³²Sn+¹²⁴Sn, D_max is 18%
- For the t/³He ratio in full phase space (4π), D_max is about 22%; for the mid-rapidity cut |y_c^(0)|<0.5, D_max ≃ 27%
- Coalescence parameters: P₀ = 0.3 GeV/c, R₀ = 3.5 fm; freeze-out time = 150 fm/c
- The n/p ratio shows sensitivity for nucleons with transverse momenta larger than ∼700 MeV/c

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Li et al. (2005) that used only Skyrme-type symmetry potentials (F15 and Fa3) by adding two relativistic mean-field based forms (DDHρ* and DDH3ρδ*) from the work of Gaitanos et al. (2004). It addresses the need for simultaneous probing of both low-density and high-density regions of the symmetry potential within a single reaction system, which had been treated separately in most prior studies. The results enable future experimental comparisons using stable neutron-rich beams (such as ²⁰⁸Pb) rather than requiring radioactive beams, and suggest that combined measurements of π⁻/π⁺ ratios at low momenta and t/³He ratios can provide comprehensive constraints on the density dependence of the symmetry potential across the full density range.
