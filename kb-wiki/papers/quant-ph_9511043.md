# On Quantum Field Brownian Motion, Decoherence and Semiquantum Chaos

> Entropy production in quantum (field) systems requiring environment-induced decoherence is described in a Gaussian variational approximation. The new phenomenon of Semiquantum Chaos is reported. (Presented at the International Conference on Nonlinear Dynamics, Chaotic and Complex Systems, Zakopane (Poland), 7-12.11.95.)

## Key claim

Entropy production in quantum field systems, which requires environment-induced decoherence, is described using a Gaussian variational approximation (time-dependent Hartree-Fock, TDHF); the paper reports the new phenomenon of "Semiquantum Chaos" in a zero-dimensional infrared limit of a prototype \(\phi^4\) field theory (a quantum mechanical double-well oscillator) that is classically regular, but where including TDHF quantum corrections introduces deterministic chaos at intermediate energies.

## Method

The author generalizes the Feynman-Vernon influence functional for single-particle quantum Brownian motion to quantum field theory, employing a nonperturbative Gaussian variational principle (time-dependent Hartree-Fock approximation in terms of Wightman functions). For the study of semiquantum chaos, the field theory is reduced to a quantum mechanical double-well oscillator, and TDHF corrections are included into the classical equations of motion, yielding an autonomous first-order nonlinear flow system of four equations (instead of the classical two). The resulting trajectories are analyzed by Poincaré sections and a new frequency correlation function related to the system's density matrix.

## Key numbers

* The entropy production in a quantum system that is classically chaotic is found to be exponentially fast, requiring only a small decohering effect (e.g., from vacuum fluctuations coupled in from a higher energy scale); a zero temperature environment inducing partial decoherence is sufficient.
* The non-equilibrium von Neumann entropy for scalar fields is given by \(S(t)=-\text{Tr}\{\ln(1-Y)+Y(1-Y)^{-1}\ln Y\}\), where \(Y\) explicitly involves two-point functions of both fields.
* The rapid thermalization context references high energy density matter at \(\gg 1\;\text{GeV/fm}^3\) in strong interactions.

## Context

This work extends the Feynman-Vernon influence functional approach (originally for single-particle quantum Brownian motion) to quantum field theory, building on prior work by the author and collaborators (Elze, 1995) and on the decoherence program of Zurek (1991). It addresses the long-standing "entropy puzzle" dating back to Fermi and Landau regarding rapid thermalization in strong interactions. The new phenomenon of semiquantum chaos suggests that tuned radiation driving a double-well oscillator could make the effect measurable, potentially in experiments with irradiated solid-state quantum dots.
