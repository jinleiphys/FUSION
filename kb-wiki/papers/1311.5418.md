# Rotational response of superconductors: magneto-rotational isomorphism and rotation-induced vortex lattice

> The analysis of nonclassical rotational response of superfluids and superconductors was performed by Onsager (in 1949) \cite{Onsager} and London (in 1950) \cite{London} and crucially advanced by Feynman (in 1955) \cite{Feynman}. It was established that, in thermodynamic limit, neutral superfluids rotate by forming---without any threshold---a vortex lattice. In contrast, the rotation of superconductors at angular frequency ${\bf Ω}$---supported by uniform magnetic field ${\bf B}_L\propto {\bf Ω}$ due to surface currents---is of the rigid-body type (London Law). Here we show that, neglecting the centrifugal effects, the behavior of a rotating superconductor is identical to that of a superconductor placed in a uniform fictitious external magnetic filed $\tilde{\bf H}=- {\bf B}_L$. In particular, the isomorphism immediately implies the existence of two critical rotational frequencies in type-2 superconductors.

## Key claim
Neglecting centrifugal effects, the behavior of a rotating superconductor is exactly isomorphic to that of a non-rotating superconductor placed in a uniform fictitious external magnetic field, which immediately implies the existence of two critical rotational frequencies in type-2 superconductors.

## Method
The authors derive a free-energy functional for a rotating superconductor in the rotating frame, assuming electroneutrality and constant density. By introducing a shifted vector potential, they show that the functional becomes isomorphic to that of a non-rotating superconductor in a uniform external magnetic field. This mapping allows them to translate known results for magnetic critical fields into critical rotational frequencies for type-1 and type-2 superconductors.

## Key numbers
- London magnetic field: \( {\bf B}_L = -\frac{2mc}{e}{\bf \Omega} \), where \( m \) and \( e \) are the electron mass and charge.
- First critical rotation frequency for a type-2 superconductor: \( \Omega_{c1} = \frac{e}{8\pi m c} \left(\frac{\Phi_0}{\lambda^2}\right) \ln \left( \frac{\lambda}{\xi}\right) \), where \( \Phi_0 \) is the magnetic flux quantum, \( \lambda \) is the penetration depth, and \( \xi \) is the coherence length.
- Second critical rotation frequency for a type-2 superconductor: \( \Omega_{c2} = \frac{e\Phi_0}{8mc\pi\xi^2} \).
- Critical rotation frequency for a type-1 superconductor: \( \Omega_c = \frac{\gamma q\Phi_0}{8\pi\xi\lambda} \), where \( \gamma \) and \( q \) are the bare inverse mass and charge of two electrons.
- In thin superconducting films, the effective penetration length \( \lambda_f = 2 \lambda_b^2/d \) can be as large as ~1 cm, making \( \Omega_{c1} \) of order 10 Hz experimentally feasible.

## Context
This work extends the foundational analysis of nonclassical rotational response by Onsager (1949), London (1950), and Feynman (1955). It resolves the apparent contradiction between the London Law (rigid-body rotation with uniform magnetic field) and the vortex-lattice formation in neutral superfluids by establishing a direct isomorphism between rotation and an external magnetic field. The result enables predictions for vortex lattice formation in rotating type-2 superconductors, including protonic superconductors in neutron stars, and opens the possibility of observing rotation-induced vortex lattices in thin superconducting films at accessible rotation frequencies.
