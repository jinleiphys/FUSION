# Exotic phases in finite-density $\mathbb{Z}_3$ theories

> Lattice $\mathbb{Z}_3$ theories with complex actions share many key features with finite-density QCD including a sign problem and $CK$ symmetry. Complex $\mathbb{Z}_3$ spin and gauge models exhibit a generalized Kramers-Wannier duality mapping them onto chiral $\mathbb{Z}_3$ spin and gauge models, which are simulatable with standard lattice methods in large regions of parameter space. The Migdal-Kadanoff real-space renormalization group (RG) preserves this duality, and we use it to compute the approximate phase diagram of both spin and gauge $\mathbb{Z}_3$ models in dimensions one through four. Chiral $\mathbb{Z}_3$ spin models are known to exhibit a Devil's Flower phase structure, with inhomogeneous phases which can be thought of as $\mathbb{Z}_3$ analogues of chiral spirals. Out of the large class of models we study, we find that only chiral spin models and their duals have a Devil's Flower structure with an infinite set of inhomogeneous phases, a result we attribute to Elitzur's theorem. We also find that different forms of the Migdal-Kadanoff RG produce different numbers of phases, a violation of the expectation for universal behavior from a real-space RG. We discuss extensions of our work to $\mathbb{Z}_N$ models, SU($N$) models and nonzero temperature.

## Key claim

The paper claims that only chiral $\mathbb{Z}_3$ spin models and their duals exhibit a Devil's Flower phase structure with an infinite set of inhomogeneous phases, while other $\mathbb{Z}_3$ models studied do not; this result is attributed to Elitzur's theorem, and the authors note that different forms of the Migdal-Kadanoff renormalization group produce different numbers of phases, violating the expectation for universal behavior from a real-space RG.

## Method

The authors applied the Migdal-Kadanoff real-space renormalization group (RG) to compute approximate phase diagrams of complex and chiral $\mathbb{Z}_3$ spin and gauge models in dimensions one through four, using a generalized Kramers-Wannier duality that maps complex $\mathbb{Z}_3$ models (which have a sign problem) onto chiral $\mathbb{Z}_3$ models (which are simulatable with standard lattice methods in large regions of parameter space). The RG transformations were built from decimation and bond-moving operations, with blocking factor $\lambda = 2$, and the phase structure was determined by mapping out basins of attraction of fixed points under these RG transforms.

## Key numbers

- The complex $\mathbb{Z}_3$ spin model in 1D has Boltzmann weights $w(1) = 1$, $w(e^{2\pi i/3}) = e^{-J/2 + i\theta}$, and $w(e^{4\pi i/3}) = e^{-J/2 - i\theta}$, where $J$ and $\theta$ are real parameters.
- The character coefficients for the complex model are $a = 1 + 2e^{-3J/2}\cos\theta$, $b = 1 - 2e^{-3J/2}\cos(\theta - 2\pi/3)$, and $c = 1 - 2e^{-3J/2}\cos(\theta - 4\pi/3)$.
- The sign problem-free region for chiral $\mathbb{Z}_3$ models is defined by the triangle inequalities $1 + 2x > 0$, $1 - x - \sqrt{3}y > 0$, and $1 - x + \sqrt{3}y > 0$, where $z = x + iy$ parameterizes the chiral model.
- For $\mathbb{Z}_3$ models in dimensions $d \geq 2$, there is a nontrivial fixed point $z_0 = x_0 + i y_0$ with $x_0 \in [0,1]$ and $y_0 = 0$; the Roberge-Weiss symmetry leads to additional fixed points at $\omega_0 z_0$ and $\omega_0^2 z_0$ for $\omega_0 = \exp(2\pi i/3)$, depending on the RG scheme parameter $p$.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of $\mathcal{PT}$-symmetric $\mathbb{Z}_N$ models by Meisinger (2012) and Schindler (2019, 2021), and builds on the known Devil's Flower phase structure of chiral $\mathbb{Z}_3$ spin models studied by Yeomans (1981) and Bak (1982). The results enable future exploration of $\mathbb{Z}_N$ models with $N > 3$ and SU($N$) models at nonzero temperature, and the finding that different Migdal-Kadanoff RG schemes yield different numbers of phases raises questions about the universality of real-space RG methods in finite-density systems.
