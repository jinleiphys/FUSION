# Canonical interpretation of $Y(10750)$ and $Υ(10860)$ in the $Υ$ family

> Inspired by the new resonance $Y(10750)$, we calculate the masses and two-body OZI-allowed strong decays of the higher vector bottomonium sates within both screened and linear potential models. We discuss the possibilities of $Υ(10860)$ and $Y(10750)$ as mixed states via the $S-D$ mixing. Our results suggest that $Y(10750)$ and $Υ(10860)$ might be explained as mixed states between $5S$- and $4D$-wave vector $b\bar{b}$ states. The $Y(10750)$ and $Υ(10860)$ resonances may correspond to the mixed states dominated by the $4D$- and $5S$-wave components, respectively. The mass and the strong decay behaviors of the $Υ(11020)$ resonance are consistent with the assignment of the $Υ(6S)$ state in the potential models.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that the newly observed $Y(10750)$ and the established $\Upsilon(10860)$ cannot be explained as pure $S$- or $D$-wave vector bottomonium states, but might be interpreted as mixed states between the $5S$- and $4D$-wave $b\bar{b}$ states via $S-D$ mixing, with a sizeable mixing angle around $20-30^\circ$.

## Method

The authors calculated the masses of higher vector bottomonium states above the $B\bar{B}$ threshold using both linear and screened potential models, solving the radial Schrodinger equation with a three-point difference central method. They then computed the two-body OZI-allowed strong decay widths of these states using the $^3P_0$ model, with wave functions taken from their quark model predictions and the quark pair creation strength $\gamma = 0.232$ calibrated to the measured width of $\Upsilon(10580) \to B\bar{B}$. Finally, they analyzed the $S-D$ mixing scheme between the $\Upsilon(5S)$ and $\Upsilon_1(4D)$ states by varying the mixing angle $\theta$ and comparing predicted masses, total widths, and partial decay branching ratios to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The Breit-Wigner mass of $Y(10750)$ is $M = (10752.7 \pm 5.9^{+0.7}_{-1.1})$ MeV and width $\Gamma = (35.5^{+17.6}_{-11.3}^{+3.9}_{-3.3})$ MeV from Belle.
- The mass of $\Upsilon(10860)$ is $10885$ MeV and $\Upsilon(11020)$ is $11000$ MeV from experimental observations.
- The predicted mass of pure $\Upsilon(5S)$ is about $70-90$ MeV lower than the observed $\Upsilon(10860)$ mass; the mass splittings $m[\Upsilon(5S)-\Upsilon(4S)]_{\mathrm{th}} \simeq 210$ MeV and $m[\Upsilon(6S)-\Upsilon(5S)]_{\mathrm{th}} \simeq 180$ MeV are inconsistent with experimental values of $306$ MeV and $115$ MeV, respectively.
- With a mixing angle $\theta \sim 27^\circ$, the dielectron width of $\Upsilon(10860)$ is predicted to be $\Gamma_{ee} = 0.28$ keV, consistent with the measured $0.31 \pm 0.07$ keV; the dielectron width of $Y(10750)$ is estimated at $\sim 0.07$ keV.
- The ratio of production rates $R \sim \Gamma_{ee}[Y(10750)] / \Gamma_{ee}[\Upsilon(10860)] \simeq 1/4$ explains comparable cross sections in $e^+e^- \to \Upsilon(nS)\pi^+\pi^-$ processes.
- The total decay width of pure $\Upsilon(5S)$ is predicted as $\sim 44$ MeV (linear potential) or $\sim 31$ MeV (screened potential), while pure $\Upsilon_1(4D)$ gives $\sim 81$ MeV (linear) or $\sim 54$ MeV (screened), compared to the measured $\Upsilon(10860)$ width $\sim 37$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of $S-D$ mixing in heavy quarkonia, previously applied to the charmonium $\psi(3770)$ as a $1^3D_1$ state with $2^3S_1$ admixture (Eichten 2007) and to $D$ and $D_s$ meson families (Close 2006, Chen 2011, Zhong 2009). It addresses the mass and decay puzzles of $\Upsilon(10860)$ and $Y(10750)$ that pure $S$- or $D$-wave assignments cannot resolve, as noted in earlier potential model calculations (Godfrey 2015, Segovia 2016, Wang 2018). The results enable future experimental searches for the predicted decay patterns of the mixed states and motivate further theoretical studies of the intermediate meson loop mechanism for $S-D$ mixing, as investigated for axial-vector $D_{s1}$ states (Wu 2011) and $\psi(4S)$-$ \psi_1(2D)$ mixing (Cao 2017).
