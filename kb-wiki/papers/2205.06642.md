# Coherent pion production in neutrino (anti-neutrino)-nucleus interaction

> We present a study of coherent pion production in neutrino-nucleus interactions using the formalism based on partially conserved axial current theorem which connects the neutrino-nucleus cross section to the pion-nucleus elastic scattering cross section. Pion-nucleus elastic scattering cross section is calculated using Glauber model which takes three inputs, nuclear densities, pion-nucleon cross section and $α_{πN}$ which is the ratio of real to imaginary part of $πN$ forward scattering amplitude, for which the parametrizations are obtained from measured data. We calculate the differential and integrated cross sections for charge and neutral current coherent pion production in neutrino (anti-neutrino)-nucleus scattering for a range of nuclear targets from light to heavy materials such as lithium, carbon, hydrocarbon, oxygen, silicon, argon, iron and lead. The results of these cross section calculations are compared with the measured data and with the calculations from the Berger-Sehgal model and GENIE package. There is an excellent agreement between the calculated and measured cross sections with Glauber model. While GENIE and Berger-Sehgal model give a good description of the data in the lower energy range the present calculations describe the data in all energy ranges. Predictions are also made for upcoming experiments like INO and DUNE in the coherent region of neutrino cross section.

## Key claim

The authors claim that their Glauber-model-based approach for calculating coherent pion production in neutrino-nucleus interactions describes measured cross section data across all energy ranges, whereas the Berger-Sehgal model and GENIE package only give good descriptions at lower energies. This claim is hedged by the use of a band of results arising from varying both the ratio of real to imaginary part of the pion-nucleon forward scattering amplitude (απN between 0.9 and 1.4) and the kinematic parameter ξ (between 1 and 2).

## Method

The authors use the partially conserved axial current (PCAC) theorem to relate the neutrino-nucleus coherent pion production cross section to the pion-nucleus elastic scattering cross section. The pion-nucleus elastic scattering cross section is calculated using the Glauber model, which takes as inputs nuclear density functions (harmonic oscillator for light nuclei, two-parameter Fermi for heavier nuclei), measured total pion-nucleon cross sections fitted with Breit-Wigner and Regge functions, and the parameter απN (the ratio of real to imaginary part of the πN forward scattering amplitude) determined by fitting to pion-nucleus elastic scattering data. The differential and integrated cross sections for charge current and neutral current coherent pion production are then computed for neutrino and anti-neutrino interactions on targets including lithium, carbon, hydrocarbon, oxygen, silicon, argon, iron, and lead, and compared with data from MINERνA, CHARM-II, and other experiments, as well as with the Berger-Sehgal model and GENIE package.

- απN is varied in the range 0.9 to 1.4 to produce a band of cross section predictions.
- For MINERνA data comparisons: average neutrino energy ⟨Eν⟩ = 4.5 GeV; average anti-neutrino energy ⟨Eν̄⟩ = 4 GeV.
- Nuclear density parameters: for ¹²C, harmonic oscillator a = 1.687 fm, α = 1.029; for ⁵⁶Fe, two-parameter Fermi c = 4.106 fm, d = 0.519 fm; for ²⁰⁷Pb, c = 6.62 fm, d = 0.546 fm.

## Context

This work extends the PCAC-based formalism for coherent pion production previously applied by Rein and Sehgal (1982) and Berger and Sehgal (2008), and builds on earlier Glauber model calculations by Saraswat et al. (2016). The authors improve upon prior work by using updated parametrizations of total pion-nucleon cross sections from the Particle Data Group (2020) and by determining απN from fits to pion-nucleus elastic scattering data on carbon, calcium, and lead at kinetic energies of 400, 500, and 675.5 MeV. The results enable predictions for upcoming experiments such as INO (using iron targets) and DUNE (using argon targets), providing cross section estimates for both charge current and neutral current coherent pion production channels that are relevant for neutrino oscillation analyses.
