# Linear response of homogeneous nuclear matter with energy density functionals

> Response functions of infinite nuclear matter with arbitrary isospin asymmetry are studied in the framework of the random phase approximation. The residual interaction is derived from a general nuclear Skyrme energy density functional. Besides the usual central, spin-orbit and tensor terms it could also include other components as new density-dependent terms or three-body terms. Algebraic expressions for the response functions are obtained from the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the particle-hole propagator. Applications to symmetric nuclear matter, pure neutron matter and asymmetric nuclear matter are presented and discussed. Spin-isospin strength functions are analyzed for varying conditions of density, momentum transfer, isospin asymmetry, and temperature for some representative Skyrme functionals. Particular attention is paid to the discussion of instabilities, either real or unphysical, which could manifest in finite nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper presents a general formalism for deriving the full linear response of homogeneous nuclear matter using a Skyrme energy density functional that can include central, spin-orbit, tensor, and additional density-dependent or three-body terms, but the algebraic solutions rely on the assumption that the effective mass is independent of momentum, which limits direct applicability when this condition is not met.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the particle-hole propagator in the random phase approximation, using a residual interaction derived as the second functional derivative of a general Skyrme energy density functional. They obtain algebraic expressions for the response functions by exploiting the polynomial dependence of the particle-hole interaction on hole momenta, which allows them to reduce the integral equation to a set of algebraic equations for momentum averages. The formalism is applied to symmetric nuclear matter, pure neutron matter, and asymmetric nuclear matter at varying densities, temperatures, and transferred momenta for several representative Skyrme functionals.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter is denoted as \(\rho_0\), and calculations are performed at densities up to several times \(\rho_0\) (e.g., \(1.1\rho_0\) is cited as a critical density for magnetic instability in some Skyrme interactions)
- The Fermi momentum used in illustrative calculations is \(k_F = 1.33\ \text{fm}^{-1}\)
- The critical density for ferromagnetic instability in pure neutron matter can be as low as \(\simeq 1.1\rho_0\) for some Skyrme parametrizations
- Brueckner Hartree-Fock and Monte Carlo calculations exclude ferromagnetic instability up to densities of \((5-6)\rho_0\)
- The Landau parameter stability analysis suggests it is possible to construct a Skyrme interaction with a critical density around \((3.5-4)\rho_0\)

## Context

This work extends the linear response formalism of Garcia-Recio et al. (1992) for symmetric nuclear matter with central interactions to include spin-orbit and tensor terms, building on earlier studies by Davesne et al. (2009) and Pastore et al. (2012). The formalism enables detection of finite-size instabilities in all scalar/vector and isoscalar/isovector channels, as previously demonstrated by Lesinski et al. (2006) for the central-only case, and provides a tool to test new terms being introduced in Skyrme functionals such as those by Carlsson et al. (2008) or Sadoudi et al. (2011-2013).
