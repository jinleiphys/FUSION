# Merger and post-merger of binary neutron stars with a quark-hadron crossover equation of state

> Fully general-relativistic binary-neutron-star (BNS) merger simulations with quark-hadron crossover (QHC) equations of state (EOSs) are studied for the first time. In contrast to EOSs with purely hadronic matter or with a first-order quark-hadron phase transition (1PT), in the transition region QHC EOSs show a peak in sound speed, and thus a stiffening. We study the effects of such stiffening in the merger and post-merger gravitational (GW) signals. Through simulations in the binary-mass range $2.5 < M/M_{\odot} < 2.75$, characteristic differences due to different EOSs appear in the frequency of the main peak of the post-merger GW spectrum ($f_2$), extracted through Bayesian inference. In particular, we found that (i) for lower-mass binaries, since the maximum baryon number density ($n_{\rm max}$) after the merger stays below $3\text{--}4$ times the nuclear-matter density ($n_0$), the characteristic stiffening of the QHC models in that density range results in a lower $f_2$ than that computed for the underlying hadronic EOS and thus also than that for EOSs with a 1PT, (ii) for higher-mass binaries, where $n_{\rm max}$ may exceed $4\text{--}5 n_0$ depending on the EOS model, whether $f_2$ in QHC models is higher or lower than that in the underlying hadronic model depends on the height of the sound-speed peak. Comparing the values of $f_2$ for different EOSs and BNS masses gives important clues on how to discriminate different types of quark dynamics in the high-density end of EOSs and is relevant to future kilohertz GW observations with third-generation GW detectors.

## Key claim

For binary neutron star mergers with quark-hadron crossover equations of state, the sign of the shift in the main post-merger gravitational-wave frequency (f2) relative to the baseline hadronic model depends on the binary mass and the height of the sound-speed peak; for low-mass binaries the shift is always negative, which is opposite to the positive shift predicted for first-order phase transitions.

## Method

The authors performed fully general-relativistic simulations of equal-mass binary neutron star mergers using two QHC19 models (QHC19-soft and QHC19-stiff) and the purely hadronic Togashi equation of state. They simulated four binary configurations with total gravitational masses between 2.50 and 2.75 solar masses, covering the last 5-7 orbits before merger. The post-merger gravitational-wave spectra were analyzed using Bayesian inference with Markov Chain Monte Carlo fitting to extract the dominant peak frequency f2.

## Key numbers

- Binary mass range simulated: 2.5 < M/M_sun < 2.75 (four models: M1.25, M1.30, M1.35, M1.375)
- Nuclear saturation density: n0 = 0.16 fm^-3
- For QHC19-stiff, the maximum number density in the most massive case reaches approximately 3.8 n0
- The difference Delta f2 between QHC and baseline EOS is in the range +/- (50-100) Hz
- f2 can be measured to within about 36 Hz (stiff EOS), 27 Hz (moderate EOS), or 45 Hz (soft EOS) at 90% credible level for a post-merger signal-to-noise ratio of 5
- Thermal effects were modeled with an ideal-gas index Gamma_th = 2.0

## Context

This work extends previous studies of binary neutron star mergers with first-order quark-hadron phase transitions (Bauswein 2019, Most 2018, Most 2020, Weih 2020, Blacker 2020, Liebling 2021, Prakash 2021) by introducing the first simulations using quark-hadron crossover equations of state. The results enable future discrimination between different types of quark dynamics in high-density matter using kilohertz gravitational-wave observations with third-generation detectors such as the Einstein Telescope and Cosmic Explorer, which are expected to achieve sensitivity sufficient to measure f2 to within approximately 50 Hz.
