# Cooperative internal conversion process by proton exchange

> A generalization of the recently discovered cooperative internal conversion process is investigated theoretically. In the cooperative internal conversion process by proton exchange investigated the coupling of bound-free electron and proton transitions due to the dipole term of their Coulomb interaction permits cooperation of two nuclei leading to proton exchange and an electron emission. General expression of the cross section of the process obtained in the one particle spherical nuclear shell model is presented. As a numerical example the cooperative internal conversion process by proton exchange in $Al$ is dealt with. As a further generalization, cooperative internal conversion process by heavy charged particle exchange and as an example of it the cooperative internal conversion process by triton exchange is discussed. The process is also connected to the field of nuclear waste disposal.

## Key claim

The cooperative internal conversion process by proton exchange (CICP-PE) is theoretically possible when the reaction energy \(\Delta > 0\), but its cross section is significantly smaller than that of the analogous neutron-exchange process, with the ratio for the specific case of aluminum versus neon being only 0.030, due to the appearance of two Coulomb factors that suppress the rate.

## Method

The authors performed a second-order perturbation calculation within the one-particle spherical nuclear shell model, coupling bound-free electron transitions and proton transitions via the dipole term of the Coulomb interaction. The initial bound electron is in a hydrogen-like 1s state, the final free electron is described by a Coulomb-corrected plane wave, and the nuclear states are taken as \(0\Lambda\) shell-model states. The intermediate virtual proton is captured by a second nucleus through a rectangular nuclear potential well, and the cross section is derived including Coulomb factors \(F_{2'3}\) and \(F_{34}\) for the charged particles in the final state.

## Key numbers

- For the reaction \(e + ^{27}_{13}\mathrm{Al} + ^{27}_{13}\mathrm{Al} \rightarrow e' + ^{26}_{12}\mathrm{Mg} + ^{28}_{14}\mathrm{Si} + \Delta\) from the K shell: \(\sigma_{0bf,sh}(K) = 2.41 \times 10^{-46}\ \mathrm{cm}^2\).
- The reaction energy for this aluminum case is \(\Delta = 3.31362\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The electron binding energy in the K shell of aluminum is \(E_{Bi} = 1.5596\ \mathrm{keV}\).
- The ratio of the CICP-PE cross section in Al to the CICP by neutron exchange in Ne is \(0.030\).
- For a gas of atomic Al at normal state (\(n = 2.652 \times 10^{19}\ \mathrm{cm}^{-3}\), \(T = 273.15\ \mathrm{K}\), \(p = 100\ \mathrm{kPa}\)), the estimated transition probability per unit time is \(\lambda_1 > 1.92 \times 10^{-16}\ \mathrm{s}^{-1}\) and the total rate per unit volume is \(r_{tot} > 5.09 \times 10^{3}\ \mathrm{cm}^{-3}\mathrm{s}^{-1}\).
- The proton energy excess used is \(\Delta_p = 7.288969\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The nuclear potential well depth used is \(V_0 = 50\ \mathrm{MeV}\).

## Context

This work generalizes the cooperative internal conversion process (CICP) previously introduced by Kalman and Keszthelyi (2015) for neutron exchange, extending the mechanism to proton exchange and, more broadly, to heavy charged particle exchange such as triton exchange. The paper provides numerical examples for stable isotopes (e.g., \({}^{19}\mathrm{F}\), \({}^{23}\mathrm{Na}\), \({}^{27}\mathrm{Al}\), \({}^{31}\mathrm{P}\)) and lists long-lived fission products (e.g., \({}^{99}\mathrm{Tc}\), \({}^{129}\mathrm{I}\), \({}^{135}\mathrm{Cs}\), \({}^{137}\mathrm{Cs}\), \({}^{155}\mathrm{Eu}\)) that could potentially undergo CICP-PE, suggesting a possible application in nuclear waste disposal by accelerating the decay of such isotopes if collected in sufficiently high concentration and density in atomic state.
