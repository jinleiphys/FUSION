# Halo breakup and the Coulomb-nuclear interference problem

> The Coulomb-nuclear interference is studied as a function of the projectile ground-state binding energy in the 8Li + 12C and 8Li + 208Pb breakup reactions, by considering an arbitrary range for the 8Li ground-state binding energies εb, varying from the experimental one 2.03 MeV down to 0.01 MeV. Regardless the target mass, we first show that the Coulomb breakup cross section is stronger dependent on εb than the nuclear breakup cross section, due to the long-range nature of the Coulomb forces and to the electromagnetic transition matrix elements. For example, in case of 8Li + 208Pb reaction at Elab = 60MeV, it is found that |σ_int|\simeq 4\timesσ_nucl$, while $σ_Coul\simeq 35\times{σ_nucl}$. This shows clearly that small nuclear contribution in a Coulomb-dominated reaction does not imply insignificant Coulomb-nuclear interference. Such result can be mainly attributed to peripheral interference phenomenon, represented by a function of the binding energy, which determines the peripheral range of nuclear forces, where Coulomb and nuclear forces strongly interfere destructively.

## Key claim

The paper shows that even when the nuclear breakup cross section is very small compared to the Coulomb breakup cross section in a Coulomb-dominated reaction, the Coulomb-nuclear interference can still be large and destructive, contradicting the intuitive expectation that a small nuclear contribution implies insignificant interference. This result is demonstrated by varying the projectile ground-state binding energy of 8Li from the experimental value of 2.03 MeV down to 0.01 MeV, rather than comparing different projectiles.

## Method

The authors performed Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) calculations using the Fresco codes for 8Li breakup on 12C and 208Pb targets, varying the 8Li ground-state neutron binding energy from 0.01 MeV to 2.03 MeV by adjusting the depth of the Woods-Saxon potential for the 7Li+n system. The total, Coulomb, and nuclear breakup cross sections were obtained by solving the coupled differential equations, with Coulomb breakup isolated by switching off nuclear forces and vice versa, and the Coulomb-nuclear interference was defined as the difference between the coherent sum (total breakup) and the incoherent sum of Coulomb and nuclear breakup cross sections.

## Key numbers

- For 8Li + 208Pb at Elab = 60 MeV, |σ_int| ≃ 4 × σ_nucl, while σ_Coul ≃ 35 × σ_nucl.
- For 8Li + 12C at Elab = 14 MeV, the ratio σ_Coul/σ_tot is about 0.06 for ε_b = 2.03 MeV and about 0.98 for ε_b = 0.01 MeV.
- For 8Li + 12C at Elab = 24 MeV, the ratio σ_Coul/σ_tot is about 0.12 for ε_b = 2.03 MeV and about 0.65 for ε_b = 0.01 MeV.
- For 8Li + 208Pb at Elab = 36 MeV, the ratio σ_Coul/σ_tot is about 2.61 for ε_b = 2.03 MeV and about 1.06 for ε_b = 0.01 MeV.
- For 8Li + 208Pb at Elab = 60 MeV, the ratio σ_Coul/σ_tot is about 4.55 for ε_b = 2.03 MeV and about 1.1 for ε_b = 0.01 MeV.
- For 8Li + 12C at Elab = 14 MeV, the ratio σ_Coul/σ_nucl is about 0.08 for ε_b = 2.03 MeV and about 2 for ε_b = 0.01 MeV.
- For 8Li + 208Pb at Elab = 36 MeV, the ratio σ_Coul/σ_nucl is about 14 for ε_b = 2.03 MeV and about 41 for ε_b = 0.01 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of Coulomb-nuclear interference in halo breakup reactions (Hor10, Thomp20, Hussein20, Chat10, Margueron 2002, Lubian 2009, Canto 2009, Canto 2006, Kucuk10, Mukeru10, Mukeru20, Tarutina10, Pierre100) by systematically varying the binding energy of a single projectile (8Li) rather than comparing different projectiles with different structures, as was done in earlier work (Wang10, Rath100, Lei100). The results enable a better understanding of why pure Coulomb breakup is difficult to achieve in experiments with halo nuclei (Nakamura10, Noc10, Aum10, Fukuda10, Abu10), and show that the Coulomb-nuclear interference is a peripheral phenomenon strongly dependent on the ground-state wave function tail through the function δ_R(ε_b).
