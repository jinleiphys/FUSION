# Nuclear-matter saturation and symmetry energy within $Δ$--full chiral effective field theory

> Nuclear saturation and the symmetry energy are key properties of low-energy nuclear physics that depend on fine details of the nuclear interaction. The equation-of-state around saturation is also an important anchor for extrapolations to higher densities and studies of neutron stars. Here we develop a unified statistical framework that uses realistic nuclear forces to link the theoretical modeling of finite nuclei and infinite nuclear matter. We construct fast and accurate emulators for nuclear-matter observables and employ an iterative history-matching approach to explore and reduce the enormous parameter domain of $Δ$-full chiral interactions. We perform rigorous uncertainty quantification and find that model calibration including \nuc{16}{O} observables gives saturation predictions that are more precise than those that only use few-body data.

## Key claim

Model calibration that includes $^{16}$O observables yields saturation predictions that are more precise and accurate than those using only few-body data, but the emergence of nuclear saturation depends on the choice of calibration observables.

## Method

The authors constructed fast and accurate emulators for coupled-cluster computations of symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) and pure neutron matter (PNM) using the subspace-projected coupled-cluster (SPCC) method with a new small-batch voting algorithm to remove spurious states. They employed history matching to explore the 17-dimensional parameter space of $\Delta$-full chiral effective field theory interactions at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) with a cutoff of 394 MeV/$c$, collecting $1.7 \times 10^6$ non-implausible interaction samples. They then used importance resampling to obtain posterior predictive distributions for nuclear matter properties conditional on either few-body data alone or few-body data plus $^{16}$O observables.

## Key numbers

- $1.7 \times 10^6$ non-implausible interaction samples were collected from the 17-dimensional parameter space of $\Delta$NNLO chiral interactions
- Emulators predict energy per particle for SNM with $< 10^{-2}$ relative precision and for PNM with $< 10^{-4}$ relative precision, at a computational cost six to eight orders of magnitude smaller than full solution
- Gaussian-process interpolation of emulated equation of state yields $\approx 1\%$ precision for saturation properties, increasing to $3\%$ for symmetry energy slope $L$ and $10\%$ for incompressibility $K$
- 73% of non-implausible samples predict saturation within the studied density region ($\rho \in \{0.12, 0.14, 0.16, 0.18, 0.20\}$ fm$^{-3}$)
- Strong anti-correlation between saturation energy $E_0/A$ and saturation density $\rho_0$ with Pearson correlation coefficient $r = -0.92$
- Symmetry energy $S$ and its slope $L$ show positive correlation with $r = 0.60$
- 90% credible intervals for $^6$Li threshold energy $S_d$ are $[-0.14, 1.54]$ MeV with few-body calibration and $[1.23, 1.84]$ MeV with $^{16}$O added, compared to experimental value $1.474$ MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of correlations between symmetry energy and neutron skin properties in $^{208}$Pb by Hu et al. (2021) using chiral interactions at NNLO with explicit delta isobars. It also builds on the Coester line correlation observed by Coester (1970) for nucleon-nucleon interactions and on density functional theory calculations by Ducoin et al. (2011) and Santos et al. (2014) that showed similar correlations. The results enable rigorous uncertainty quantification for extrapolations of the nuclear equation of state to higher densities relevant for neutron star physics, and provide a framework for future work using history matching and Bayesian posterior predictive distribution sampling to study the information content of low-energy observables and the predictive power of ab initio modeling across the nuclear landscape.
