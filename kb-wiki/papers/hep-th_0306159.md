# Linked-cluster Tamm-Dancoff Field Theory

> To solve the relativistic bound-state problem one needs to systematically and simultaneously decouple the high-energy from the low-energy modes and the many-body from the few-particle states using a consistent renormalization scheme. In a recent paper we have shown that one such approach can be a combination of the coupled cluster method as used in many-body theory and the Wilsonian exact renormalization group. Even though the method is intrinsically non-perturbative, one can easily implement a loop expansion within it. In this letter we provide further support for this aspect of our formalism by obtaining results for the two-loop renormalized $ϕ^{4}$ theory. We show that the non-unitary representation inherent in our method leads to an economic computation and does not produce any non-hermiticity in the relevant terms.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the linked-cluster Tamm-Dancoff field theory, which uses a non-unitary double similarity transformation, correctly reproduces the two-loop renormalization of φ⁴ theory without introducing non-hermiticity in the relevant terms, despite the non-unitary representation.

## Method
The authors combine the coupled cluster method (CCM) with the Wilsonian exact renormalization group to construct a renormalized low-energy Hamiltonian. They apply a double similarity transformation using linked-cluster amplitudes S and S' to decouple high-energy modes from low-energy modes, imposing decoupling conditions that partially diagonalize the Hamiltonian in particle and momentum space. The method is applied to φ⁴ theory in equal-time quantization, truncating the expansion at the SUB(4,2) level (up to 4-body operators and second order in the ratio of cutoffs μ/Λ), and the renormalization factors Z_g, Z_φ, and Z_π are computed up to two-loop order.

## Key numbers
- One-loop mass renormalization: Z_m = 1 − (3g/2π²)(Λ² − μ²)
- One-loop coupling renormalization: Z_g = 1 + (9g²/2π²) ln(Λ/μ)
- One-loop wave-function renormalization: Z_φ = 1 − (3g²/8π⁴) ln(Λ/μ)
- Two-loop coupling renormalization: Z_g = 1 + (9g²/2π²) ln(Λ/μ) + (g³/4π⁴)[81(ln(Λ/μ))² − 51 ln(Λ/μ)]
- Two-loop wave-function renormalization: Z_π = Z_φ⁻¹, verified by the result δH = −(3g²/16π⁴) ln(Λ/μ) ∫ d³x π²(x)
- Tadpole contribution: δH^(1-loop) = (3g/4π²)(Λ² − μ²) ∫ d³x φ²(x)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous formulation (Rezaeian and Walet, 2003) that combined the coupled cluster method with the Wilsonian exact renormalization group, providing a two-loop calculation as further evidence of the method's efficiency. It addresses long-standing problems with the Tamm-Dancoff approximation (Tamm, 1950s) which produced non-perturbative divergences, and offers an alternative to the similarity renormalization group approach of Glazek and Wilson and independently Wegner, which suffers from computational complexity and lack of systematic diagonalization. The method enables a consistent renormalization scheme for relativistic bound-state problems that can maintain Poincaré invariance and cluster decomposition, with the non-perturbative aspect remaining to be exploited.
