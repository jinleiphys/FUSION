# The hard scattering amplitude for the higher helicity components of the pion form factor

> For obtaining the spin space wave function of the pion meson in the light-cone formalism from the naive quark model, it is necessary to take into account Wigner rotation. Consequently there are higher helicity ($λ_1+λ_2=\pm 1$) components in the light-cone spin space wave function of the pion besides the usual helicity ($λ_1+λ_2=0$) components. For the pion electromagnetic form factor, we calculate the hard-scattering amplitude for the higher helicity components in the light-cone perturbation theory. It is found that the hard-scattering amplitude for the higher helicity components is of order $1/Q^4$, which is vanishingly small compared to that of the ordinary helicity component at very high $Q^2$ but should be considered in the $Q^2$ region where experimental data are available.

## Key claim

The hard-scattering amplitude for the higher helicity components of the pion form factor is of order \(1/Q^4\), vanishingly small compared to the ordinary helicity component at very high \(Q^2\), but should be considered in the \(Q^2\) region where experimental data are available; this resolves a conflict between prior works by Ma and Huang (1996) and Wang and Kisslinger (1996), which had assumed opposite signs for this amplitude.

## Method

The authors calculated the hard-scattering amplitude for the higher helicity (\(\lambda_1+\lambda_2=\pm 1\)) components of the pion form factor using light-cone perturbation theory, summing over six time-ordered diagrams. They first showed that the amplitude vanishes at leading order \(O(1/Q^2)\) when parton intrinsic transverse momenta are neglected, then included the intrinsic transverse momenta \({\bf k}_\perp\) and \({\bf l}_\perp\) to obtain the next-to-leading order contribution, applying two simplifying prescriptions: neglecting bound-energy terms proportional to \({\bf k}_\perp^2/(x_1x_2)\) and \({\bf l}_\perp^2/(y_1y_2)\), and neglecting terms of order \({\bf k}_\perp^2/{\bf q}_\perp^2\) and \(({\bf k}_\perp\cdot{\bf l}_\perp)/{\bf q}_\perp^2\).

## Key numbers

- The hard-scattering amplitude for the higher helicity components is of order \(1/Q^4\), which is next-to-leading compared to the ordinary helicity component's leading order \(1/Q^2\).
- The ordinary helicity hard-scattering amplitude (when intrinsic transverse momenta are neglected) is \(T_H^{(\lambda_1+\lambda_2=0)} = \frac{4g^2C_F}{x_2y_2 Q^2}\).
- The asymptotic (\(Q^2\rightarrow\infty\)) hard-scattering amplitude used in Ma and Huang (1996) was \(T_H^{(\lambda_1+\lambda_2=\pm 1)} = \frac{4g^2C_F}{x_2y_2 Q^2}\), while that used in Wang and Kisslinger (1996) was \(T_H^{(\lambda_1+\lambda_2=\pm 1)} = -\frac{4g^2C_F}{x_2y_2 Q^2}\); both are shown to be incorrect.
- The Wigner rotation factors are \({\cal W}_1 = m/[(m^2+{\bf k}_\perp^2)(m^2+{\bf l}_\perp^2)]^{1/2}\) and \({\cal W}_2 = {\bf k}_\perp\cdot{\bf l}_\perp/[(m^2+{\bf k}_\perp^2)(m^2+{\bf l}_\perp^2)]^{1/2}\).

## Context

This work extends the light-cone perturbative QCD framework for exclusive processes developed by Brodsky and Lepage (1980) and the analysis of Wigner rotation effects in pion wave functions by Huang, Ma, and Shen (1994) and Ma and Huang (1996). It directly addresses a conflict between Ma and Huang (1996), who found that higher helicity components provide a large enhancement to the pion form factor, and Wang and Kisslinger (1996), who found a large suppression; the authors show both prior calculations used incorrect hard-scattering amplitudes. The derived amplitude enables a proper quantitative calculation of higher helicity contributions to the pion form factor in the intermediate \(Q^2\) region (a few GeV\(^2\)), which may help resolve the discrepancy between perturbative QCD predictions and experimental data.
