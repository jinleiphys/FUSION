# Pion-Production in Heavy-Ion Collisions at SIS energies

> We investigate the production of pions in heavy-ion collisions in the energy range of $1$ - $2$ GeV/A. The dynamics of the nucleus-nucleus collisions is described by a set of coupled transport equations of the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck type for baryons and mesons. Besides the $N(938)$ and the $Δ(1232)$ we also take into account nucleon resonances up to masses of $1.9 GeV/c^2$ as well as $π$-, $η$- and $ρ$-mesons. We study in detail the influence of the higher baryonic resonances and the $2π$-production channels ($NN\to NN ππ$) on the pion spectra in comparison to $π^-$ data from $Ar + KCl$ collisions at $1.8$ GeV/A and $π^0$-data for $Au+Au$ at 1.0 GeV/A. We, furthermore, present a detailed comparison of differential pion angular distributions with the BEVALAC data for Ar + KCl at 1.8 GeV/A. The general agreement obtained indicates that the overall reactions dynamics is well described by our novel transport approach.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Coupled-Channel BUU (CBUU) transport model, which includes all baryonic resonances up to 1950 MeV/c² and explicitly propagates π, η, ρ, and σ mesons, reproduces the overall reaction dynamics of heavy-ion collisions at SIS energies (1-2 GeV/A) when compared to pion data, but the agreement is described as "general" rather than exact, and the authors note that pion selfenergies (not included) might alter the results.

## Method

The authors solve a set of coupled Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport equations for baryons (nucleons and resonances up to 1950 MeV/c²) and mesons (π, η, ρ, σ) using the test-particle method with a momentum-dependent mean-field potential derived from the Welke parametrization. Inelastic baryon-baryon collisions include resonance excitation (NN ↔ NR and NR ↔ NR'), while resonance decays are modeled via 1π, 2π, and η channels using Breit-Wigner formulations and energy-dependent widths from the Particle Data Group. The Coulomb potential is included for charged particles, and the equations are solved with a predictor-corrector method using the Kodama algorithm for collision sequencing.

## Key numbers

- The energy range investigated is 1 to 2 GeV/A for heavy-ion collisions.
- Nuclear matter is compressed to 2-3 times normal nuclear matter density ρ₀ during the collision.
- The mean-field potential uses an incompressibility of K = 250 MeV with parameters A = -29.253 MeV, B = 57.248 MeV, C = -63.516 MeV, τ = 1.760, and Λ = 2.13 1/fm.
- The Δ(1232) resonance has a decay width Γ_R = 120 MeV; the ρ-meson has mean mass M_R = 770 MeV/c² and Γ_R = 118 MeV; the σ-meson has M_R = 800 MeV/c² and Γ_R = 800 MeV.
- The matrix element for N(1535) production in proton-proton collisions is fitted as 16π × 8 mb GeV², while for proton-neutron collisions it is 16π × 40 mb GeV².
- The paper compares with π⁻ data from Ar + KCl collisions at 1.8 GeV/A and π⁰ data for Au + Au at 1.0 GeV/A.

## Context

This work extends earlier BUU models by Wolf (1990, 1993) and the IQMD transport model by Bass (1993) by including all baryonic resonances up to 1950 MeV/c² and explicitly propagating η and ρ mesons as additional degrees of freedom. The CBUU model enables more detailed studies of pion spectra as probes of compressed nuclear matter, and is particularly relevant for understanding dilepton production (via π⁺π⁻ → e⁺e⁻) and kaon/η production channels that depend on pion-induced reactions.
