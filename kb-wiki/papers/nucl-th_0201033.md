# Nuclear shell effects near the r-process path

> We have studied the evolution of the shell structure of nuclei near the neutron drip line in the Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) theory with the vector self-coupling of omega meson. The experimental data on the shell effects about the waiting-point nucleus $^{80}$Zn are reproduced successfully. It is shown that the shell effects at N=82 near the r-process path remain strong. A quenching exhibited by the HFB+SkP approach is shown not to be compatible with the available data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that shell effects at N=82 near the r-process path remain strong, contrary to the quenching predicted by the HFB+SkP approach, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) framework with the vector self-coupling of the omega meson (force NL-SV1) and is demonstrated only for nuclei with Z ≥ 40 that are relevant to the r-process path.

## Method

The authors performed Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations with spherical symmetry, expanding fermionic and bosonic wavefunctions in 20 oscillator shells. They used three forces: NL-SH (scalar self-coupling only), NL-SV2 (scalar and vector self-couplings), and NL-SV1 (the benchmark force with both couplings). They computed two-neutron separation energies (S₂ₙ) for isotopic chains of Zn (Z=30), Kr (Z=36), Sr (Z=38), Zr (Z=40), and Mo (Z=42) to evaluate shell gaps at N=50 and N=82, comparing results with the HFB+SkP approach and experimental data for ⁸⁰Zn.

## Key numbers

- For ⁸⁰Zn (N=50), the RHB force NL-SV1 reproduces the empirical shell effects, while NL-SH shows a shell gap "much stronger than the experimental data" and HFB+SkP shows "strongly quenched" shell effects.
- For N=82 nuclei, the shell gap with NL-SV1 is reduced compared to NL-SV2 but remains stronger than HFB+SkP results, especially for Mo (Z=42) and Zr (Z=40) isotopes.
- For ¹¹⁸Kr (N=82), all forces except NL-SH show a "complete washing out" of shell effects because the Fermi energy is very close to the continuum, making the binding energy of an extra neutron nearly zero.
- The r-process path passes through nuclei with Sₙ ~ 2-4 MeV, and for Mo and Zr at N=82, the shell effects with NL-SV1 are "much stronger than with SkP."

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous RHB studies (Sharma et al., 2000) that introduced the vector self-coupling of the omega meson to soften shell effects near stability. It directly contradicts the HFB+SkP results of Dobaczewski et al. (1994) and Sharma et al. (1994), which predicted quenched shell effects at N=82 near the drip line. The paper argues that the quenching seen in SkP is inconsistent with experimental data on ⁸⁰Zn, and that alternative nucleosynthesis mechanisms should be explored rather than assuming shell quenching for r-process abundance fits (Kratz, 1993).
