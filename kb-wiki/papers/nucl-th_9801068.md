# Excitation function of entropy and pion production from AGS to SPS energies

> Entropy production in the initial compression stage of relativistic heavy-ion collisions from AGS to SPS energies is calculated within a three-fluid hydrodynamical model. The entropy per participating net baryon is found to increase smoothly and does not exhibit a jump or a plateau as in the 1-dimensional one-fluid shock model. Therefore, the excess of pions per participating net baryon in nucleus-nucleus collisions as compared to proton-proton reactions also increases smoothly with beam energy.

## Key claim

Entropy production in the compression stage of relativistic heavy-ion collisions increases smoothly with beam energy from AGS to SPS, without the jump or plateau predicted by the one-dimensional one-fluid shock model, because the three-fluid model's finite mean free path broadens shock fronts and smears out the threshold behavior.

## Method

The authors used a three-fluid hydrodynamical model in which projectile nucleons, target nucleons, and produced particles are each treated as separate fluids in local thermodynamic equilibrium, interacting via binary nucleon collisions. Entropy production was calculated during the initial compression stage only, neglecting possible entropy increase during expansion from finite viscosity, and an equation of state with a first-order phase transition to quark-gluon plasma was employed for all three fluids.

## Key numbers

- Entropy per participating net baryon at SPS energies: \(S/A = 35\) in the three-fluid model versus 25 in the one-fluid shock model.
- The plateau in the one-fluid shock model is expected to shift to \(\sqrt{s} \approx 5\text{--}10\) AGeV in the three-fluid model, but broadened shock fronts produce a smooth increase instead of a sharp threshold.
- Freeze-out density assumed for all energies: \(\rho_{\text{fo}} = 0.5 \rho_0\).

## Context

This work extends the one-dimensional one-fluid shock model calculations of entropy production (Rigo et al., Hofmann et al.) by introducing a three-fluid treatment that accounts for non-equilibrium effects and finite mean free path. The results explain the observed sign reversal of \(\Delta(n_\pi/n_B)\) between AGS and SPS energies (noted in earlier pion production studies), showing that excess "non-baryonic" entropy at SPS drives the smooth increase in pion excess per baryon relative to proton-proton collisions. The calculation enables future comparisons with experimental data on pion yields across the AGS-to-SPS energy range.
