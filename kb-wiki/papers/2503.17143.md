# Dipole Polarizability of Finite Nuclei as a Probe of Neutron Stars

> Nuclear ground state and collective excitation properties provide a means to probe the nuclear matter equation of state and establish connections between observables in finite nuclei and neutron stars. Specifically, the electric dipole polarizability, measured with high precision in various neutron-rich nuclei, serves as a robust constraint on the density dependence of the symmetry energy. In this Letter, we employ a class of relativistic energy density functionals in a twofold process: first, to link the electric dipole polarizability from recent experiments to the slope of the symmetry energy, and second, to translate this information into constraints on the tidal deformability and radii of neutron stars, in connection with multimessenger astrophysical observations from pulsars and binary neutron stars. We provide compelling evidence that the electric dipole polarizability represents a key nuclear observable to probe the neutron star properties. By significantly reducing the uncertainties in the mass-radius plane, our findings also align with recent multimessenger observations.

# Dipole Polarizability of Finite Nuclei as a Probe of Neutron Stars

## Key claim

The electric dipole polarizability measured in neutron-rich nuclei provides a robust constraint on the slope of the symmetry energy, which can be translated into refined predictions for neutron star tidal deformability and radius; however, the reliability of these constraints depends on whether the dipole polarizability data for Sn isotopes are taken from measurements that include model-dependent corrections for high-energy excitations above 20 MeV.

## Method

The authors employed a family of relativistic density-dependent point-coupling (DD-PC) energy density functionals, systematically varying the symmetry energy at saturation density from J=29 to 36 MeV with corresponding slope L from 29 to 94.1 MeV, to compute the electric dipole response via the relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation (RQRPA). The calculated dipole polarizabilities for multiple neutron-rich nuclei (48Ca, 68Ni, 112-124Sn, 208Pb) were compared with experimental values to extract constraints on the symmetry energy slope L, which were then used to predict the dimensionless tidal deformability and radius of 1.4 solar mass neutron stars through the same DD-PC equation of state framework.

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy slope L = 46.08 ± 6.06 MeV from the CNSP-4 set (48Ca, 68Ni, 120Sn, 208Pb)
- Symmetry energy slope L = 37.16 ± 3.96 MeV from the CNSP-10 set (CNSP-4 plus 112-124Sn isotopes)
- Symmetry energy at saturation density J = 31.34 ± 0.79 MeV (CNSP-4) and J = 30.37 ± 0.64 MeV (CNSP-10)
- For CNSP-4: dimensionless tidal deformability Λ₁.₄ = 313.80 to 343.18 and radius R₁.₄ = 11.92 to 12.12 km
- For CNSP-10: dimensionless tidal deformability Λ₁.₄ = 298.39 to 316.35 and radius R₁.₄ = 11.81 to 11.94 km
- From 208Pb alone: Λ₁.₄ = 313.36 to 368.47, R₁.₄ = 11.96 to 12.15 km, and L = 39.12 to 55.93 MeV
- GW170817 upper limit on neutron star radius: R₁.₄ = 13.366 km

## Context

This work extends previous efforts linking nuclear structure observables to neutron star properties, particularly following the connection between neutron-skin thickness and the symmetry energy slope established by PhysRevLett.120.172702 and PhysRevLett.126.172503. The authors address the tension between CREX (48Ca) and PREX-2 (208Pb) parity-violating electron scattering results, which yield inconsistent symmetry energy parameters as noted by Yuksel (2023) and Reed (2024b). By using dipole polarizability as an alternative to neutron-skin thickness, the study provides constraints consistent with multimessenger observations from GW170817 and NICER, and enables refined predictions for the Einstein Telescope era. The CNSP-4 results are considered more reliable than CNSP-10 because the Sn isotope data from Bassauer (2020) include model-dependent quasiparticle phonon model calculations for excitations above 20 MeV.
