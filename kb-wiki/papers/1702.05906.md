# Virtual photon polarization in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The polarization of direct photons produced in an ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collision reflects the momentum anisotropy of the quark-gluon plasma created in the collision. This paper presents a general framework, based on the photon spectral functions in the plasma, for analyzing the angular distribution and thus the polarization of dileptons in terms of the plasma momentum anisotropies. The rates of dilepton production depend, in general, on four independent spectral functions, corresponding to two transverse polarizations, one longitudinal polarization, and -- in plasmas in which the momentum anisotropy is not invariant under parity in the local rest frame of the matter -- a new spectral function, $ŌĀ_n$, related to the anisotropy direction in the collision. The momentum anisotropy appears in the difference of the two transverse spectral functions, as well as in $ŌĀ_n$. As an illustration, we delineate the spectral functions for dilepton pairs produced in the lowest order Drell-Yan process of quark-antiquark annihilation to a virtual photon.

## Key claim

The polarization of virtual photons (measured via dilepton angular distributions) can serve as a probe of momentum-space anisotropy in the quark-gluon plasma, but the new spectral function ŌĀ_n that mixes longitudinal and transverse polarizations appears only when the momentum anisotropy is not invariant under parity in the local rest frame; for symmetric heavy-ion collisions with parity invariance, ŌĀ_n must vanish and only the difference of the two transverse spectral functions remains.

## Context

This work extends the framework of Baym and Hatsuda (2015) for using direct photon polarization to measure gluon anisotropy, generalizing it to virtual photons (dileptons) which are experimentally more accessible. The formalism provides the theoretical basis for extracting quark and gluon momentum anisotropies from dilepton angular distributions, complementing the approach of Friman and collaborators (2017) which did not include the anisotropic terms ŌĀ^T_1 - ŌĀ^T_2 and ŌĀ_n. The next step is to generalize prior real-photon calculations by Schenke et al. (2006) and Bhattacharya et al. (2015) to include higher-order processes (Compton scattering and quark-antiquark annihilation with gluon emission) using 3+1 dimensional anisotropic hydrodynamics.
