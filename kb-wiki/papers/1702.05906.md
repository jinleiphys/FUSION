# Virtual photon polarization in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The polarization of direct photons produced in an ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collision reflects the momentum anisotropy of the quark-gluon plasma created in the collision. This paper presents a general framework, based on the photon spectral functions in the plasma, for analyzing the angular distribution and thus the polarization of dileptons in terms of the plasma momentum anisotropies. The rates of dilepton production depend, in general, on four independent spectral functions, corresponding to two transverse polarizations, one longitudinal polarization, and -- in plasmas in which the momentum anisotropy is not invariant under parity in the local rest frame of the matter -- a new spectral function, $ρ_n$, related to the anisotropy direction in the collision. The momentum anisotropy appears in the difference of the two transverse spectral functions, as well as in $ρ_n$. As an illustration, we delineate the spectral functions for dilepton pairs produced in the lowest order Drell-Yan process of quark-antiquark annihilation to a virtual photon.

## Key claim

The polarization of virtual photons (measured via dilepton angular distributions) can serve as a probe of momentum-space anisotropy in the quark-gluon plasma, but the new spectral function ρ_n that mixes longitudinal and transverse polarizations appears only when the momentum anisotropy is not invariant under parity in the local rest frame; for symmetric heavy-ion collisions with parity invariance, ρ_n must vanish and only the difference of the two transverse spectral functions remains.

## Method

The authors construct a general decomposition of the in-medium photon spectral function ρ_μν in terms of two transverse polarization vectors (ε_1, ε_2), a longitudinal polarization vector (ε_L), and a four-vector N^μ derived from the anisotropy axis n^μ. They then contract this spectral function with the leptonic tensor L_μν to obtain the dilepton production rate, expressing the result in terms of four independent spectral functions: ρ^L, ρ^T_1, ρ^T_2, and ρ_n. As an illustration, they apply the formalism to the leading-order Drell-Yan process (quark-antiquark annihilation to a virtual photon) in a plasma with anisotropic distributions of the Romatschke-Strickland form.

## Key numbers

- The dilepton angular distribution is parametrized as ∝ 1 + λ cos²θ + μ sin 2θ cos φ + (ν/2) sin²θ cos 2φ, with leading-order predictions λ ≃ 1, μ = ν = 0 for high-energy pp and p̅p collisions at q_T ≲ 5 GeV and M_{l⁺l⁻} ≃ M_Z.
- The NA60 experiment at CERN-SPS measured dilepton angular distributions in In-In collisions for 0.4 < M_{l⁺l⁻} < 0.9 GeV, finding λ = μ = ν = 0 consistent with hadronic source dominance.
- The momentum anisotropy is expected to be detectable in dilepton production for 1 GeV < q_T < 3 GeV and M_{l⁺l⁻} ≲ 0.3 GeV, where the direct photon excess is observed experimentally.
- For weak anisotropy, δρ^T ∼ -β₂ where β₂ = β₀ξ/15 is the second spherical harmonic component of the angular-dependent temperature.

## Context

This work extends the framework of Baym and Hatsuda (2015) for using direct photon polarization to measure gluon anisotropy, generalizing it to virtual photons (dileptons) which are experimentally more accessible. The formalism provides the theoretical basis for extracting quark and gluon momentum anisotropies from dilepton angular distributions, complementing the approach of Friman and collaborators (2017) which did not include the anisotropic terms ρ^T_1 - ρ^T_2 and ρ_n. The next step is to generalize prior real-photon calculations by Schenke et al. (2006) and Bhattacharya et al. (2015) to include higher-order processes (Compton scattering and quark-antiquark annihilation with gluon emission) using 3+1 dimensional anisotropic hydrodynamics.
