# Modeling $^{19}$B as a $^{17}$B-n-n three-body system in the unitary limit

> We present a model description of the bound $^{17}$B isotope in terms of a $^{17}$B-n-n three-body system where the two-body subsystems $^{17}$B-n and n-n are unbound (virtual) states close to the unitary limit. The $^{17}$B ground state is well described in terms of two-body potentials only, and two low-lying resonances are predicted. Their eventual link with the Efimov physics is discussed. This model can be naturally used to describe the recently discovered resonant states in $^{20,21}$B.

## Key claim

The paper models the bound isotope $^{19}$B as a $^{17}$B-n-n three-body system where both two-body subsystems ($^{17}$B-n and n-n) are unbound virtual states close to the unitary limit, and finds that the ground state can be described using only two-body potentials without any three-body force, though the existence of Efimov excited states in $^{19}$B is deemed highly unlikely.

## Method

The authors constructed a local, finite-range $n$-$^{17}$B potential with a hard-core radius and a single strength parameter adjusted to reproduce the large negative scattering length $a_S$, supplemented with realistic $n$-$n$ interactions (Bonn A and a charge-dependent version of MT13). The three-body $^{17}$B-n-n system was solved independently using two different formalisms: Faddeev equations in configuration space and the Gaussian Expansion Method. The model was then used to compute the $^{19}$B ground-state energy as a function of the $n$-$^{17}$B scattering length, and to search for resonant excited states.

## Key numbers

- The $n$-$n$ singlet scattering length is $a^{nn}_0 = -18.5$ fm (experimental value used for tuning the CD MT13 potential).
- The $n$-$^{17}$B scattering length has an experimental upper bound $a_S < -50$ fm (from MSU 2012).
- The $^{19}$B two-neutron separation energy is $S_{2n} = 0.14 \pm 0.39$ MeV (experimental value from B19_mass).
- In the spin-symmetric model, the $^{19}$B ground-state energy at the unitary limit ($a_S \to -\infty$) is $E_u = -0.081$ MeV for version (i) (S-wave only) and $E_u = -0.185$ MeV for version (ii) (all partial waves).
- When both $a_S$ and $a_{nn}$ are taken to the unitary limit, the $^{19}$B energy is $E_{uu} = -0.160$ MeV.
- Two broad resonances are predicted for $a_S = -150$ fm: $E_{1^-} \approx 0.24(2) - 0.31(4)i$ MeV and $E_{2^+} \approx 1.02(5) - 1.22(6)i$ MeV.
- The critical $n$-$^{17}$B scattering length below which $^{19}$B becomes unbound is $a_S^c \approx -30$ fm for version (i) and $a_S^c \approx -15$ fm for version (ii).
- For the spin-dependent case with $a_2 = -150$ fm fixed, the critical value for the $S=1$ channel lies within $-7 < a_1^c < -3$ fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier three-body models of $^{19}$B, such as that of Mazumdar (2000), which overbound the ground state and predicted bound excited states due to a purely attractive non-local $n$-$^{17}$B interaction. It also relates to effective field theory treatments of other Borromean nuclei like $^{22}$C (Yamashita 2011, Phillips 2013), but here the authors rely solely on finite-range two-body interactions. The model is motivated by recent experimental observations of heavy boron isotopes (MSU 2012, Gibelin FB22, 20B_PRL121_2018) and is intended to be extended to describe resonances in $^{20}$B and $^{21}$B as $^{17}$B+$3n$ and $^{17}$B+$4n$ systems.
