# Hadron Freeze-Out and Unruh Radiation

> We consider hadron production in high energy collisions as an Unruh radiation phenomenon. This mechanism describes the production pattern of newly formed hadrons and is directly applicable at vanishing baryochemical potential, mu = 0. It had already been found to correctly yield the hadronisation temperature, T_h = sqrt(sigma / 2 pi) = 165 MeV in terms of the string tension sigma. Here we show that the Unruh mechanism also predicts hadronic freeze-out conditions, giving s/T_h^3 = 3 pi^2 / 4 = 7.4 in terms of the entropy density s and E/N = \sqrt(2 pi sigma) = 1.09 for the average energy per hadron. These predictions provide a theoretical basis for previous phenomenological results and are also in accord with recent lattice studies.

# Hadron Freeze-Out and Unruh Radiation

## Key claim
The Unruh radiation mechanism, previously shown to predict the hadronisation temperature, also predicts the freeze-out conditions for hadron production at vanishing baryochemical potential (μ ≈ 0), specifically the entropy density ratio s/T_h³ = 3π²/4 ≈ 7.4 and the average energy per hadron ⟨E⟩/⟨N⟩ = √(2πσ) ≈ 1.09 GeV, but the authors explicitly note this formalism cannot address freeze-out at larger baryochemical potential where initial-state nucleons are present.

## Method
The authors model hadron production in high-energy collisions as sequential Unruh radiation from accelerating quarks at the confinement horizon. They compute the string breaking distance R = √(2π/σ) from the transverse string thickness r_T = √(2/πσ) and the transverse momentum k_T = √(πσ/2), then derive the hadron energy from σR and the entanglement entropy from a Bekenstein-Hawking-type relation S_h = (1/4)(A_h/r_T²) using the horizon area A_h = 4πR².

## Key numbers
- String tension: σ ≈ 0.19 ± 0.03 GeV²
- Hadronisation temperature at μ=0: T_h = √(σ/2π) ≈ 175 ± 15 MeV
- Average energy per hadron: ⟨E⟩/⟨N⟩ = √(2πσ) ≈ 1.09 ± 0.08 GeV
- Entropy density ratio: s/T_h³ = 3π²/4 ≈ 7.4
- String breaking distance: R = √(2π/σ)
- Transverse string thickness: r_T = √(2/πσ)
- Transverse momentum: k_T = √(πσ/2)

## Context
This work extends the Unruh radiation approach to hadron production previously proposed by Castorina, Kharzeev, and Satz (2007), which had only addressed the hadronisation temperature. The predicted freeze-out conditions provide a theoretical basis for phenomenological fits from Cleymans and Tawfik (various years) and agree with lattice QCD results from the HotQCD Collaboration (2014). The authors note that the formalism cannot explain why these μ≈0 conditions appear to hold at larger baryochemical potential, leaving that as an open question for future work.
