# Deuteron life-time in hot and dense nuclear matter near equilibrium

> We consider deuteron formation in hot and dense nuclear matter close to equilibrium and evaluate the life-time of the deuteron fluctuations within the linear response theory. To this end we derive a generalized linear Boltzmann equation where the collision integral is related to equilibrium correlation functions. In this framework we then utilize finite temperature Green functions to evaluate the collision integrals. The elementary reaction cross section is evaluated within the Faddeev approach that is suitably modified to reflect the properties of the surrounding hot and dense matter.

## Key claim

The deuteron lifetime in hot and dense nuclear matter near equilibrium is evaluated using linear response theory and finite-temperature Green functions, with the key hedge that the calculation assumes small fluctuations from equilibrium and that the collision rate is sufficiently high compared to the reaction rate so that each component is close to thermal equilibrium.

## Method

The authors derived a generalized linear Boltzmann equation where the collision integral is related to equilibrium correlation functions via linear response theory. They utilized finite-temperature Green functions to evaluate the collision integrals, and solved the effective three-body problem using AGS-type (Faddeev) equations modified to include medium effects such as Pauli blocking and self-energy shifts in mean-field approximation. The elementary reaction cross section for deuteron break-up (Nd → NNN) was evaluated using a separable representation of the NN-potential parameterized by Phillips (1968).

## Key numbers

- The Mott transition for deuterons occurs at density n ≈ 8 × 10⁻³ fm⁻³.
- At Elab = 100 MeV, the deviation of the in-medium cross section from the free cross section is on the order of 10%.
- Near the maximum and close to the Mott density, the in-medium break-up cross section increases almost one order of magnitude compared to the isolated cross section.
- The difference between using the isolated cross section versus the medium-dependent cross section in the lifetime calculation amounts to almost a factor of three near the Mott density.
- The break-up cross section was calculated for densities n = 0.1, 1, 3, 5, 7 × 10⁻³ fm⁻³.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of deuteron production kinetics that used the impulse approximation at energies above 200 MeV (Danielewicz and collaborators, 1991) and Born approximation treatments (Roepke, 1988). It provides a full three-body treatment of the scattering problem necessary for energies below E/A ≤ 200 MeV, where the impulse approximation fails badly, as shown by Beyer and Roepke (1996). The results are intended as input for transport equations used to describe heavy-ion collision dynamics, and the formalism can be extended to treat formation of higher clusters such as helium, tritons, and alpha particles.
