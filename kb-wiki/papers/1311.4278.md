# Transport coefficients of nuclear matter in neutron star cores

> We calculate thermal conductivity and shear viscosity of nucleons in dense nuclear matter of neutron star cores in the non-relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock framework. Nucleon-nucleon interaction is described by the Argonne v18 potential with addition of the Urbana IX effective three-body forces. We find that this three body force leads to decrease of the kinetic coefficients with respect to the two-body case. The results of calculations are compared with electron and muon transport coefficients as well as with the results of other authors.

## Key claim

The authors calculate thermal conductivity and shear viscosity of nucleons in beta-stable nuclear matter using the non-relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock framework, finding that inclusion of the Urbana IX three-body forces decreases the kinetic coefficients relative to calculations using only two-body forces, contrary to the general result from prior studies that medium effects strongly increase these coefficients.

## Method

The authors solved the Brueckner-Bethe-Salpeter equation with the Argonne v18 two-body potential and Urbana IX three-body forces to obtain the in-medium G-matrix and quasiparticle effective masses at the Fermi surface. They then computed the spin-averaged squared matrix elements of the transition amplitude in the partial wave representation and used these to evaluate effective cross-sections and solve the multicomponent system of linearized kinetic equations for thermal conductivity and shear viscosity in the variational approximation, with exact solutions also calculated numerically.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: $\rho_0 \approx 2.8 \times 10^{14}$ g/cm³
- The difference between exact and variational solutions is of the order of 20% for thermal conductivity and less than 5% for shear viscosity
- Effective masses were interpolated with smooth functions of density within 2% accuracy
- In-medium cross-sections were calculated at density $\rho = 0.35$ fm⁻³, approximately twice the nuclear saturation density, with c.m. energy $E_{\rm c.m.} = 75$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations of transport coefficients previously applied to pure neutron matter by Wambach et al. (1993) and Benhar et al. (2007), and to beta-stable matter by Zhang et al. (2010) and Carbone and Benhar (2011). The authors note that their approach is similar to Zhang et al. (2010) but differs in the treatment of three-body forces and effective masses, and they compare their results with the earlier Flowers and Itoh (1979) calculations that used free-space nucleon scattering amplitudes. The results enable improved modeling of thermal relaxation in young neutron stars (age less than 100 years) and damping of neutron star oscillations.
