# Generalized parton distributions through universal moment parameterization: non-zero skewness case

> We present the first global analysis of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) combing lattice quantum chromodynamics (QCD) calculations and experiment measurements including global parton distribution functions (PDFs), form factors (FFs) and deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) measurements. Following the previous work where we parameterize GPDs in terms of their moments, we extend the framework to allow for the global analysis at non-zero skewness. Together with the constraints at zero skewness, we fit GPDs to global DVCS measurements from both the recent JLab and the earlier Hadron-Electron Ring Accelerator (HERA) experiments with two active quark flavors and leading order QCD evolution. With certain choices of empirical constraints, both sea and valence quark distributions are extracted with the combined inputs, and we present the quark distributions in the proton correspondingly. We also discuss how to extend the framework to accommodate more off-forward constraints beyond the small $ξ$ expansion, especially the lattice calculated GPDs.

## Key claim

The authors present the first global analysis of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) that combines lattice QCD calculations with experimental measurements including parton distribution functions (PDFs), form factors (FFs), and deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) data, but only for two active quark flavors at leading order QCD evolution and with the caveat that extra empirical constraints are needed to handle the deconvolution problem, meaning the extracted GPD shapes are model-dependent.

## Method

The authors use the GPDs through universal moment parameterization (GUMP) framework, expressing GPDs in terms of their conformal moments parameterized by Euler beta functions, Regge trajectories, and residual exponential t-dependence. They split the global analysis into two steps: first performing semi-forward fits at zero skewness for each of the four t-dependent PDFs (H, E, H̃, Ẽ) using PDFs from the JAM collaboration, globally extracted form factors, and lattice QCD results; then performing off-forward fits at non-zero skewness using DVCS measurements from JLab and HERA with the semi-forward parameters fixed, while introducing a single parameter R₂ for the ξ²-dependence of each GPD.

## Key numbers

- Total χ² per degree of freedom: 1.40 across 1481 data points
- Semi-forward fit for tPDF H: χ² = 281.7 with 217 data points (χ²/ν = 1.41)
- Semi-forward fit for tPDF E: χ² = 59.7 with 50 data points (χ²/ν = 1.36)
- Semi-forward fit for tPDF H̃: χ² = 159.3 with 206 data points (χ²/ν = 0.84)
- Semi-forward fit for tPDF Ẽ: χ² = 63.8 with 58 data points (χ²/ν = 1.23)
- Off-forward fit to JLab DVCS: χ² = 1413.7 with 926 data points
- Off-forward fit to H1 DVCS: χ² = 19.7 with 24 data points
- Regge slope α' for sea quarks and gluons fixed to 0.15 from the pomeron trajectory
- JLab DVCS data selected with Q > 1.8 GeV and x_B < 0.5 to ensure ξ² ≲ 0.1

## Context

This work extends the GUMP framework previously developed by the same authors (Guo et al., 2022) for zero-skewness GPDs to the non-zero skewness case, enabling inclusion of exclusive DVCS measurements. It follows the general conformal moment parameterization approach of Mueller (2005) and Kumericki and Mueller (2009), but differs by focusing on extraction of all four leading-twist GPDs with both valence and sea quarks using combined lattice and experimental inputs. The framework is designed to accommodate future constraints from lattice QCD calculations of GPDs at non-zero skewness and from the planned Electron-Ion Collider.
