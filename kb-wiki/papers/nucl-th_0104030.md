# Towards a Perturbative Theory of Nuclear Forces

> We show that an expansion of nuclear forces about the chiral limit is formally consistent and is equivalent to KSW power counting in the 1S0 channel and Weinberg power counting in the 3S1-3D1 coupled channels. Numerical evidence suggests that this expansion converges. The feasibility of making contact between nuclear physics and lattice-QCD simulations is discussed.

## Key claim

The authors show that an expansion of nuclear forces about the chiral limit (treating deviations from the massless-pion limit perturbatively) is formally consistent, and that this expansion is equivalent to KSW power counting in the 1S0 channel and Weinberg power counting in the 3S1-3D1 coupled channels, thereby selecting only the desirable features of both previous power-counting schemes.

## Method

The authors analyze nucleon-nucleon scattering in the 1S0 channel and the 3S1-3D1 coupled channels using a square-well regulator to renormalize the short-distance interactions, matching analytic solutions of the Schrodinger equation to determine the renormalization-group flow of the counterterms. They compute phase shifts at leading order in Weinberg power counting for the 3S1-3D1 channels and demonstrate analytic renormalization, then perform a perturbative expansion of the scattering amplitudes in the difference between the one-pion-exchange potential at the physical pion mass and at zero pion mass, carried out to N6LO.

## Key numbers

* 1S0 channel scattering length: a = -23.714 fm (used to tune the square-well depth V0)
* 1S0 channel effective range: r = 2.73 fm (comparable to twice the pion Compton wavelength)
* 3S1 channel scattering length: a = 5.425 fm (used to determine the phase phi0)
* 3S1 channel effective range: r = 1.749 fm (used to determine the phase phi1)
* Pion decay constant: F_pi = 93 MeV; axial coupling: g_A = 1.26; nucleon mass: M = 940 MeV (used to define alpha_pi = g_A^2 / (16 pi F_pi^2))
* Square-well regulator radii used: R = 0.10 fm (Lambda = 1970 MeV), R = 0.21 fm (Lambda = 938 MeV), R = 0.45 fm (Lambda = 438 MeV) for the 3S1-3D1 phase-shift calculations

## Context

This work addresses the formal inconsistency in Weinberg power counting identified by Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998) for the 1S0 channel, where logarithmic divergences at leading order require counterterms that appear only at higher orders. It also addresses the failure of KSW power counting in the 3S1-3D1 coupled channels found by Fleming, Mehen, and Stewart (2000), where the tensor component of one-pion-exchange is nonperturbative. The expansion about the chiral limit enables systematic contact between nuclear physics and lattice-QCD simulations by providing a controlled framework for the light-quark mass dependence of deuteron binding energy and nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitudes.
