# Speed of Sound in Hybrid Stars and the Role of Bag Pressure in the Emergence of Special Points on M-R Variation of Hybrid Stars

> We compute the hybrid star (HS) properties with the help of Maxwell construction. For the purpose we choose a fixed hadronic model and four different forms of MIT bag model for the quark phase. We investigate thoroughly the effects of the different parameters of the bag model on the speed of sound in HS matter and the structural properties of HSs in the light of the various recent constraints on them from astrophysical observations. We also examine the importance of each parameter involved in these four forms of bag model in the context appearance of special points (SPs) in the mass-radius (M-R) variation of HSs. We find that among all these parameters the bag pressure play the most significant role in the emergence of the SPs in the M-R dependence of HSs.

## Key claim
The bag pressure is the most significant parameter among those in the four MIT bag model variants for the emergence of special points (SPs) in the mass-radius diagram of hybrid stars, but this conclusion is drawn specifically within the Maxwell construction framework and using a single fixed hadronic model (BSR2).

## Method
The authors used the Maxwell construction for the hadron-quark phase transition, combining a fixed relativistic mean-field hadronic model (BSR2) with four different forms of the MIT bag model: the modified bag model with strong repulsive interaction, the vector bag (vBag) model, a density-dependent bag model with Gaussian form, and a density-dependent bag model with hyperbolic form. They varied one parameter at a time in each quark model while keeping others fixed, computed the hybrid equation of state, and solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass, radius, and tidal deformability of hybrid stars.

## Key numbers
- The BSR2 hadronic model has saturation density 0.149 fm^{-3}, binding energy per particle -16.03 MeV, nuclear incompressibility 240.0 MeV, symmetry energy coefficient 31.4 MeV, and slope parameter 62.2 MeV.
- For the modified bag model with strong repulsive interaction, the maximum value of the peak of the squared speed of sound (C_S^2) in hybrid star matter is 0.57, obtained for the minimum value of the repulsive interaction parameter alpha in the hadronic phase.
- For the vBag model, the maximum value of the peak of C_S^2 is 0.62, obtained for the maximum value of the vector coupling G_V in the hadronic phase.
- For the density-dependent bag model with Gaussian form, the peak values of C_S^2 average around 0.95 across parameter variations, with the peak located in the quark phase.
- The strange quark mass m_s is varied close to the known value of 93.4^{+8.6}_{-3.4} MeV.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Sen, 2022) which found a nearly linear relationship between the special point mass and maximum mass of hybrid stars using different hadronic models and the Gaussian density-dependent bag model. It also builds on studies by Cierniak (2019), Yudin (2019), and Kaltenborn (2017) that identified special points as a universal feature of hybrid star models. The paper systematically compares four bag model forms to isolate which parameters control special point emergence, enabling future work to use special points as a diagnostic tool for interpreting multi-messenger data from neutron star mergers and NICER observations.
