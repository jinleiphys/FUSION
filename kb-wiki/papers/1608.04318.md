# Hydrodynamic modeling of a pure-glue initial scenario in high-energy hadron and heavy-ion collisions

> Partonic matter produced in the early stage of ultrarelativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions is assumed to be composed mainly of gluons, and quarks and antiquarks are produced at later times. The comparable hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions for (2+1)-flavor and Yang-Mills equations of state performed by using three different hydrodynamic codes are presented. Assuming slow chemical equilibration of quarks, the spectra and elliptic flows of thermal dileptons and photons are calculated for central Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC energy of $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}} = 2.76$ TeV. It is shown that a suppression of quarks at early times leads to a significant reduction of the yield of the thermal dileptons, but only to a rather modest suppression of the $p_T$-distribution of direct photons. It is demonstrated that an enhancement of photon and dilepton elliptic flows might serve as a promising signature of the pure-glue initial state. Calculations based on Bjorken hydrodynamics suggest that collisions of small systems at intermediate energies available at RHIC or future FAIR facilities may show stronger effects associated with initial pure gluodynamic evolution.

# Hydrodynamic modeling of a pure-glue initial scenario in high-energy hadron and heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

Assuming slow chemical equilibration of quarks (with a characteristic timescale τ* of 1–5 fm/c) in a pure-glue initial state for Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies, the yield of thermal dileptons is significantly reduced while the elliptic flow of both photons and dileptons is enhanced compared to the chemically equilibrated scenario; however, the direct photon pT-distribution shows only a modest suppression, and the effect on hadron observables is small enough that existing agreement with data is not spoiled.

## Method

The authors performed (2+1)-dimensional boost-invariant ideal hydrodynamic simulations of central and semi-central Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV using three different hydrodynamic codes (CLVisc, SHASTA-based solver, and vHLLE). They constructed an equation of state for chemically nonequilibrated quark-gluon plasma by linearly interpolating between lattice QCD results for (2+1)-flavor full QCD and pure SU(3) Yang-Mills theory, with a time-dependent quark fugacity λ(τP) that evolves from 0 at initial proper time τ0 = 0.1 fm/c toward 1 on a timescale τ*. Thermal photon and dilepton emission rates were computed including the λ-dependent suppression of quark densities.

## Key numbers

- LHC collision energy: √sNN = 2.76 TeV for Pb+Pb collisions
- Initial proper time τ0 = 0.1 fm/c for the LHC calculations; τ0 = 0.5 fm/c for lower-energy Bjorken estimates
- Chemical equilibration timescale τ* varied between 0, 1, and 5 fm/c
- Critical temperature for pure Yang-Mills (SU(3)) deconfinement phase transition: Tc ≃ 270 MeV
- Crossover transition temperature for full QCD: T ∼ 155 MeV
- Maximum initial energy density in central cell for CLVisc simulation: 166 GeV/fm³
- Impact parameter b = 7.74 fm for 20–30% centrality class in SHASTA-based simulation
- Lifetime of the deconfined phase in LHC calculations: does not exceed 10 fm/c
- Lifetime of the mixed phase in the chemically nonequilibrium scenario: does not exceed 0.5 fm/c
- About 25% of total final entropy is generated during ideal hydro evolution of chemically undersaturated QCD matter
- Cutoff temperature for photon and dilepton calculations: Tf = 125 MeV (photons) and Tf = 155 MeV (dileptons)
- For lower-energy Bjorken estimates: transverse radius R varied between 6–9 fm for A+A and 0.6–0.9 fm for p+p collisions
- Proposed PANDA energy for p̅-p collisions: √sNN ≃ 30 GeV

## Context

This work extends the pure-glue initial scenario previously discussed by Stoecker et al. (2016) and Vovchenko et al. (2016) by implementing it in three independent hydrodynamic codes and computing electromagnetic probes. It builds on the two-step equilibration picture proposed by Raha, Shuryak, and Sinha (1980s), where gluons thermalize early while quark chemical equilibration proceeds until later times (τth = 5–10 fm/c according to Xu et al., 2005). The results enable experimental searches for signatures of initial gluon dominance at LHC via dilepton suppression and enhanced elliptic flow, and suggest that smaller systems at intermediate energies (e.g., p̅-p at FAIR/PANDA) may show stronger effects from the pure gluodynamic evolution and its first-order phase transition.
