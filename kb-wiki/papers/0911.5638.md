# Diffractive $J/Ψ$ photoproduction at large momentum transfer in coherent hadron - hadron interactions at CERN LHC

> The vector meson production in coherent hadron-hadron interactions at LHC energies is studied assuming that the color singlet $t$-channel exchange carries large momentum transfer. We consider the non-forward solution of the BFKL equation at high energy and large momentum transfer and estimate the rapidity distribution and total cross section for the process $h_1 h_2 \to h_1 J/ΨX$, where $h_i$ can be a proton or a nucleus. We predict large rates, which implies that the experimental identification can be feasible at the LHC.

## Key claim

The authors predict that diffractive $J/\Psi$ photoproduction at large momentum transfer in coherent hadron-hadron collisions at LHC energies will produce "large rates" that make experimental identification feasible, but this conclusion depends on the specific choice of the BFKL parameters $\alpha_s = 0.21$ and the scale parameters $\beta = 1.0$ and $\gamma = 0.25$ fitted to HERA data, and the actual event rates are strongly sensitive to the assumed luminosity and detector acceptance cuts.

## Method

The authors compute the cross section for $h_1 h_2 \to h_1 J/\Psi X$ by factorizing the process into an equivalent photon flux from one hadron and the photoproduction cross section $\gamma h \to J/\Psi X$ at large momentum transfer. The photoproduction cross section is obtained using the impact factor representation of the non-forward BFKL equation in leading logarithmic approximation, with the amplitude expressed in terms of the BFKL Green's function and impact factors for the $\gamma \to J/\Psi$ transition and the quark target, integrated over parton distribution functions (CTEQ6L for protons, EKS for nuclei) in the range $0.01 < x_j < 1$.

## Key numbers

* For $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 7$ TeV, the integrated cross section for $2.0 < |t| < 5.0$ GeV$^2$ is 32.0 nb, corresponding to 320.0 events per second at design luminosity ${\cal L}_{\mathrm{pp}} = 10^{34}$ cm$^{-2}$s$^{-1}$.
* For $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 14$ TeV, the integrated cross section for $2.0 < |t| < 5.0$ GeV$^2$ is 97.0 nb, corresponding to 970.0 events per second at design luminosity.
* For $PbPb$ collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 5.5$ TeV, the integrated cross section for $2.0 < |t| < 5.0$ GeV$^2$ is 3.0 mb, corresponding to 13.0 events per second at design luminosity ${\cal L}_{\mathrm{PbPb}} = 4.2 \times 10^{26}$ cm$^{-2}$s$^{-1}$.
* For $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 14$ TeV, the integrated cross section for $5.0 < |t| < 10.0$ GeV$^2$ is 21.0 nb (210.0 events/s), and for $10.0 < |t| < 30.0$ GeV$^2$ it is 6.0 nb (60.0 events/s).
* The BFKL dynamics predicts $\sigma_{\gamma h} \propto W^{\lambda}$ with $\lambda \approx 1.4$, leading to a factor of about 3 increase in cross section when the $pp$ energy increases from 7 to 14 TeV.
* The nuclear cross sections are amplified by a factor $Z^4$ relative to proton-proton due to the coherent photon flux.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of exclusive vector meson production in coherent hadron-hadron collisions (Goncalves et al., 2007-2009) to the case of large momentum transfer with hadron dissociation, where the $t$-channel color singlet carries large momentum transfer and one hadron dissociates. The formalism follows the non-forward BFKL approach previously applied to $ep$ collisions at HERA by Forshaw and Poludniowski (2001) and by the authors in earlier work, and the predictions for $pp$ and $PbPb$ collisions at LHC are compared with the exclusive $J/\Psi$ photoproduction at $t=0$ calculated in the Color Glass Condensate formalism (Goncalves et al., 2007). The results enable experimental searches for this process at LHC using forward detectors, though the authors note that detector acceptance effects for CMS and ATLAS may limit detection to $\Upsilon$ states rather than $J/\Psi$, while ALICE may be better suited for reconstruction.
