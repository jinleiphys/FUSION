# Quarkyonic Matter and Chiral Symmetry Breaking

> The appearance of a new phase of QCD, Quarkyonic Matter in the limit of large number of colors is studied within Nambu-Jona-Lassinio effective chiral model coupled to the Polyakov loop. The interplay of this novel QCD phase with chiral symmetry restoration and color deconfinement is discussed. We find that at vanishing temperature and at large $N_c$, the quarkyonic transition occurs at densities only slightly lower than that expected for the chiral transition. This property is also shown to be valid at finite temperature if the temperature is less than that of deconfinement. The position and $N_c$-dependence of chiral critical end point is also discussed.

# Quarkyonic Matter and Chiral Symmetry Breaking

## Key claim

In the large $N_c$ limit of QCD, a quarkyonic phase transition (onset of finite baryon number density in the confined phase) occurs at a chemical potential only slightly lower than that of the chiral phase transition; the difference is so small that it may be an artifact of the model, and the two transitions may be numerically identical.

## Method

The authors study the PNJL (Polyakov-loop extended Nambu–Jona-Lasinio) model in the mean-field approximation for two quark flavors, with the Polyakov-loop effective potential fitted to lattice gauge theory results for $SU(N_c=3)$. They solve the coupled gap equations for the chiral condensate and the Polyakov-loop expectation value, first in the large $N_c$ limit (where quark loops do not affect the gluon potential) and then for finite $N_c$, to map the phase boundaries for chiral symmetry restoration, deconfinement, and the quarkyonic transition.

## Key numbers

- At $T=0$ and $N_c=3$, the chiral transition occurs at $\mu_{\rm chiral} = 1.04\, M_Q$, where $M_Q = 320$ MeV is the vacuum constituent quark mass; the quarkyonic transition occurs at $\mu = M_Q$.
- For $N_c=3$ with current quark mass $m=5$ MeV, the chiral critical end point (CEP) appears at finite temperature and chemical potential; the chiral transition at $\mu=0$ occurs at $T_{\rm chiral} \sim 177$ MeV.
- The deconfinement transition temperature in the pure gauge sector is $T_0 = 270$ MeV, independent of $N_c$ at leading order.
- The vacuum parameters are: cutoff $\Lambda = 646$ MeV, four-fermion coupling $G = 10.2\,{\rm GeV}^{-2}\times(3/N_c)$, and pion decay constant $f_\pi = 92\,\mbox{MeV}\times\sqrt{3/N_c}$.

## Context

This work extends the concept of the quarkyonic phase, rigorously shown to exist in large $N_c$ QCD by McLerran and Pisarski (2007), by incorporating chiral symmetry breaking within a PNJL model. It builds on prior PNJL studies by Fukushima (2004) and Weise (2006), and on lattice results by de Forcrand and Philipsen (2007) showing that the confinement transition weakens with increasing baryon density. The results suggest that for realistic $N_c=3$, the critical end point of the QCD phase diagram is driven by the chiral transition rather than deconfinement, and that the quarkyonic and chiral transitions are nearly coincident. This enables future work to test whether the chiral condensate at the Fermi surface of quarkyonic matter requires much higher energy density for full restoration.
