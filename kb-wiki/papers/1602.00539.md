# Kinetic theory of a longitudinally expanding system

> We use kinetic theory in order to study the role of quantum fluctuations in the isotropization of the pressure tensor in a system subject to fast longitudinal expansion, such as the matter produced in the early stages of a heavy ion collision.

## Key claim

Quantum fluctuations are essential for the isotropization of the pressure tensor in a longitudinally expanding system; purely classical approximations (which omit certain terms in the Boltzmann equation) do not correctly capture the relevant physics and overestimate their own range of validity, even at very small couplings where classical behavior might be expected to dominate.

## Method

The author uses kinetic theory (the Boltzmann equation with 2-to-2 scatterings) to study the role of quantum fluctuations in the isotropization of the pressure tensor. Two implementations of the classical statistical approximation (CSA) are compared: one with a flat spectrum of fluctuations on top of a non-fluctuating classical field (quantum coherent state), and one with a compact-support spectrum describing an incoherent classical state. The full Boltzmann equation, including terms of quantum origin (the second line in Eq. (4) of the paper), is solved numerically for both scalar fields and gluons in Yang-Mills theory, with CGC-like highly occupied initial distributions of the form \(f_0(\mathbf{k}) = (n_0/g^2)\,\theta(Q_s - k_\perp)\,\theta(\xi Q_s - |k_z|)\).

## Key numbers

* For Yang-Mills theory at \(\lambda = 0.5\) (i.e., \(\alpha_s \approx 0.02\) for \(N_c = 2\) colors), the classical and quantum evolutions depart from each other around \(Q_s\tau \approx 2\), well before the conjectured limit of validity of the classical approximation at \(Q_s\tau \approx \alpha_s^{-3/2} \approx 350\).
* The classical approximation of the Boltzmann equation leads to a forever decreasing ratio \(P_L/P_T\), while the full Boltzmann equation (including quantum terms) shows isotropization for couplings as small as \(\lambda = 0.5\).

## Context

This work extends the study of isotropization in heavy ion collisions beyond the leading-order classical Yang-Mills description, which yields a negative longitudinal pressure just after the collision and a ratio \(P_L/P_T\) that decreases forever. It builds on the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective theory and the JIMWLK evolution equation (Gelis 2010), and on prior implementations of the classical statistical approximation by Epelbaum (2013), Gelis (2013), and Berges (2013, 2014). The kinetic theory approach clarifies that quantum fluctuations are essential for isotropization, contradicting earlier claims that quantum corrections may be ignored up to times of order \(\alpha_s^{-3/2}Q_s^{-1}\). This enables a more reliable matching between CGC initial conditions and hydrodynamic models of the fireball expansion.
