# Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model description of weakly interacting Bose condensate and BEC-BCS crossover in dense QCD-like theories

> QCD-like theories possess a positively definite fermion determinant at finite baryon chemical potential $μ_{\text B}$ and the lattice simulation can be successfully performed. While the chiral perturbation theories are sufficient to describe the Bose condensate at low density, to describe the crossover from Bose-Einstein condensation (BEC) to BCS superfluidity at moderate density we should use some fermionic effective model of QCD, such as the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. In this paper, using two-color two-flavor QCD as an example, we examine how the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model describes the weakly interacting Bose condensate at low density and the BEC-BCS crossover at moderate density. Near the quantum phase transition point $μ_{\text B}=m_π$ ($m_π$ is the mass of pion/diquark multiplet), the Ginzburg-Landau free energy at the mean-field level can be reduced to the Gross-Pitaevskii free energy describing a weakly repulsive Bose condensate with a diquark-diquark scattering length identical to that predicted by the chiral perturbation theories. The Goldstone mode recovers the Bogoliubov excitation in weakly interacting Bose condensates. The results of in-medium chiral and diquark condensates predicted by chiral perturbation theories are analytically recovered. The BEC-BCS crossover and meson Mott transition at moderate baryon chemical potential as well as the beyond-mean-field corrections are studied. Part of our results can also be applied to real QCD at finite baryon or isospin chemical potential.

## Key claim

The Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, at the mean-field level, can reproduce the Gross-Pitaevskii free energy of a weakly repulsive Bose condensate near the quantum phase transition point μ_B = m_π, with a diquark-diquark scattering length identical to that predicted by chiral perturbation theories, but only when the mixing between the sigma meson and diquarks is properly accounted for.

## Method

Using two-color two-flavor QCD as an example, the author bosonizes the NJL model via auxiliary fields for mesons and diquarks, then performs a Ginzburg-Landau expansion of the effective potential near the quantum phase transition point μ_B = m_π. The mean-field thermodynamic potential is evaluated with a hard three-momentum cutoff Λ, and the quadratic fluctuations around the mean field are computed via derivative expansion to study Gaussian corrections.

## Key numbers

- Diquark-diquark scattering length: a_dd = m_π / (16π f_π²), recovering the tree-level result from chiral Lagrangian.
- The BEC-BCS crossover occurs at μ_B ≈ (m_σ / m_π)^(1/3) m_π ≈ (1.6–2) m_π.
- Chiral symmetry is approximately restored at μ_B ≈ 3 m_π.
- Four parameter sets are given: Λ ranges from 565.4 to 657.9 MeV, GΛ² from 3.105 to 4.776, m_0 from 4.90 to 5.53 MeV, M_* from 300 to 600 MeV, m_π ≈ 133.6–134.4 MeV, with f_π fixed at 75 MeV.
- Quark condensate ⟨ūu⟩_0^(1/3) ranges from -209.1 to -217.4 MeV across the four parameter sets.
- The beyond-mean-field correction to the diquark-diquark scattering length is proportional to m_π² / f_π².

## Context

This work extends the NJL model description of two-color QCD (previously studied by the same author and others, e.g., 2CNJL01–2CNJL04) to analytically recover the low-density Bose condensate results of chiral perturbation theories (QL01–QL06). It enables a unified fermionic description of both the dilute Bose condensate and the BEC-BCS crossover in QCD-like theories, analogous to the Leggett (1980) approach for cold Fermi gases. The results can also be applied to real QCD at finite baryon or isospin chemical potential.
