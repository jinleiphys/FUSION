# Novel microscopic approaches for Spin-Isospin excitations and Beta-decay

> We explore unsolved nuclear structure problems related with the spin and isospin degree of freedom by using microscopic models which accommodate realistic isoscalar and isovector pairing interactions, and also tensor correlations. For the attempt of universal theoretical framework for both nuclear and astrophysical phenomena, we adopt a self-consistent Hartree-Fock (HF)+random phase approximation (RPA) models, and a state-of-the-art beyond mean field model, Subtracted Second RPA (SSRPA), including the couplings to two-particle two-hole states. The quenching problems of magnetic dipole and Gamow-Teller transitions are discussed in terms of the coupling to 2p-2h configurations and also the tensor correlations. The $β$ decay life time of semi-magic and magic nuclei are discussed in RPA and SSRPA models.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the long-standing quenching problem of Gamow-Teller (GT) sum rule strengths can be addressed by including 2p-2h configuration mixing via the Subtracted Second RPA (SSRPA) model, but the results depend on whether tensor terms are included in the Skyrme energy density functional; the SSRPA model shifts about 20% of GT strength to higher energies, bringing calculated strengths closer to experimental data, though the abstract's claim of "no free parameters" is qualified by the dependence on the chosen effective interaction.

## Method

The authors employ a self-consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) + random phase approximation (RPA) framework and extend it to the Subtracted Second RPA (SSRPA) model, which includes couplings to two-particle two-hole (2p-2h) configurations. The subtraction method is applied to cure the convergence problem of standard SRPA, using the static limit (ω→0) to cancel energy-dependent terms that would otherwise make the solution dependent on the 2p-2h model space size. The Okubo-Lee-Suzuki similarity transformation is discussed as a formal justification for the subtraction procedure, showing that the renormalization term in the effective interaction has the same functional form as the subtraction term in SSRPA.

## Key numbers

* The Ikeda sum rule S_- - S_+ = 3(N-Z) is exhausted by 100% in RPA calculations for all nuclei studied, while experimental data exhaust only about 60% of the Ikeda sum rule.
* SSRPA calculations show about 20% of the GT strength is shifted to higher energy region than 20 MeV excitation energy.
* The effect of tensor interaction on beta decay half-life in SSRPA model changes the half-lives by about one to two orders of magnitude with respect to results obtained without tensor force.
* The SSRPA model is applied to beta decay half-lives of four semi-magic and magic nuclei: ³⁴Si, ⁶⁸Ni, ⁷⁸Ni, and ¹³²Sn.

## Context

This work extends the long line of research on the quenching of spin-dependent operators, following the seminal work by Arima and Horie who identified configuration mixing as the most important effect on magnetic moment quenching. The paper discusses GT excitations in ⁴⁸Ca, ⁹⁰Zr, ¹³²Sn, and ²⁰⁸Pb, comparing RPA and SSRPA results against experimental data from references cited as GT-48Ca, GT-90Zr, GT-132Sn, and GT-208Pb. The approach enables future studies of double-beta decay processes (both two-neutrino and neutrino-less) and astrophysical r-process nucleosynthesis, where quantitative understanding of GT and spin-dipole excitations is essential.
