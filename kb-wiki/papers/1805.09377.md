# Formation of topological vortices during superfluid transition in a rotating vessel

> Formation of topological defects during symmetry breaking phase transitions via the {\it Kibble mechanism} is extensively used in systems ranging from condensed matter physics to the early stages of the universe. Kibble mechanism uses topological arguments and predicts equal probabilities for the formation of defects and anti-defects. Certain situations, however, require a net bias in the production of defects (or antidefects) during the transition, for example, superfluid transition in a rotating vessel, or flux tubes formation in a superconducting transition in the presence of external magnetic field. In this paper we present a modified Kibble mechanism for a specific system, $^4$He superfluid transition in a rotating vessel, which can produce the required bias of vortices over antivortices. Our results make distinctive predictions which can be tested in superfluid $^4$He experiments. These results also have important implications for superfluid phase transitions in rotating neutron stars and also for any superfluid phases of QCD arising in the non-central low energy heavy-ion collision experiment due to an overall rotation.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a modified Kibble mechanism for superfluid transitions in rotating vessels that produces a net bias of vortices over antivortices, contrary to the standard Kibble mechanism which predicts equal probabilities for defects and anti-defects on average. The bias arises from systematic variations of the order parameter phase within each correlation domain and a modified geodesic rule, both induced by the external rotation.

## Method

The authors perform two-dimensional lattice simulations of vortex formation during the superfluid transition of 4He in a rotating cylindrical vessel. They model correlation domains as circular regions of radius equal to the correlation length ξ, with random phase values at domain centers but systematic phase gradients inside each domain determined by the rotation. The modified geodesic rule for phase interpolation between domains is derived by minimizing free energy density in the inter-domain region, accounting for the rotation bias. For each angular velocity Ω, 5000 simulation events are generated to obtain statistics of vortex and antivortex production.

## Key numbers

- For Ω = 0, the distribution of net defect number Δn (vortices minus antivortices) is Gaussian with mean 0 and standard deviation σ = 30.46, with total defects N = 1,857,948 and exponent ν = 0.2604 (close to the theoretical value 0.25)
- For Ω = 10^3 rad s^-1, the mean net defect number shifts to 25.29 ± 0.44 (for a quadrant; about 100 for the full vessel), with σ = 30.41 ± 0.44
- For Ω = 10^7 rad s^-1, the mean net defect number reaches 240,603 ± 4.96 with σ = 43.47 ± 0.35 and N = 2,745,682
- The critical angular velocity for vortex production from energetics is Ω_cr ≈ 78 rad s^-1 for a vessel of radius R = 40 μm and correlation length ξ = 140 Å
- The best fit line for mean net defect number versus Ω gives slope 0.024 for the quadrant (about 0.1 for the full cylinder), matching the vortex model prediction n ≈ 2πR^2 mΩ/h ≈ 0.1 Ω

## Context

This work extends the Kibble mechanism (Kibble, 1976) and Kibble-Zurek mechanism (Zurek, 1985) to systems with external bias, specifically superfluid transitions in rotating vessels. It addresses deviations from the vortex model prediction observed in the experiment of Hess and Fairbank (1967), where the number of vortices formed during the superfluid transition differed from energetics-based predictions. The results have implications for superfluid phase transitions in rotating neutron stars and for superfluid phases of QCD arising in non-central heavy-ion collisions. The modified mechanism can also be applied to biased formation of flux tubes in superconductors in the presence of external magnetic fields.
