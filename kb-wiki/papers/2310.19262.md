# Hartree-Fock Formulation of the QMC Model at Finite Temperature

> We present, for the first time, a detailed theory of high density matter including the entire baryon octet at finite temperature, based on a fully relativistic mean field model with a consistent treatment of exchange (Fock) terms, using the quark-meson-coupling model (QMC). It has been already demonstrated that the QMC equation of state is applicable in thermodynamic scenarios in stationary and rotating isentropic proto-neutron stars, producing results in agreement with recent observation. It is also suitable for the simulation of the behavior following a binary neutron star merger [1]; https://compose.obspm.fr/eos/205. We develop a comprehensive demonstration of the impact of the Fock terms in the QMC energy density functional on properties of neutrinoless proto-neutron stars with cores containing the full hyperon octet with constant entropy, S/A=2kB. Given the interest in the properties of the proto-neutron star remaining after either a supernova explosion or the merger of two neutron stars, it is vital to develop modern equations of state at finite temperature. While much attention has been paid to relativistic mean-field calculations at finite temperature, it is crucial to explore the consequences of a consistent treatment of the Fock terms.

## Key claim

The paper presents, for the first time, a fully consistent relativistic Hartree-Fock formulation of the quark-meson-coupling (QMC) model equation of state for hyperonic matter at finite temperature, including the entire baryon octet; the abstract's claim of a "detailed theory" is qualified by the fact that the authors neglect the effect of the scalar polarizability on the effective sigma meson mass and treat the meson fluctuation terms perturbatively at leading order.

## Method

The authors start from the QMC Hamiltonian with sigma, omega, rho, and pi mesons and compute the grand partition function using a saddle-point approximation for the mean meson fields, while treating the quadratic fluctuations around the mean fields perturbatively to generate the Fock (exchange) terms. The single-particle energies are determined self-consistently via the finite-temperature Hartree-Fock variational principle, with the zero-temperature solution used as a starting point for iterative solution at higher temperatures. The mean field equations for the meson fields are solved including the rearrangement terms arising from the field dependence of the Fock interaction kernel, and the resulting grand potential yields the thermodynamic quantities and beta-equilibrium conditions for proto-neutron star matter.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density range explored: rho_0 = 0.14 to 0.18 fm^{-3} (in steps of 0.01 fm^{-3})
- Energy per particle at saturation (E_0/A) range: -14 to -18 MeV (in steps of 1 MeV)
- Symmetry energy coefficient J range: 28 to 32 MeV (in steps of 2 MeV), with a total of 110 points in the 3D parameter mesh
- Sigma meson mass fixed to M_sigma = 700 MeV; lambda_4 set to zero
- Entropy per particle for the proto-neutron star calculations: S/A = 2 k_B
- Temperature range for the full EoS tables: T = 0 to 100 MeV; entropy per particle range: 0 to 6 k_B; lepton fraction range: Y_L = 0.0 to 0.6; baryon number density range: n_B = 0.05 to 1.2 fm^{-3}

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous QMC-A model calculations for cold neutron stars and hot isentropic proto-neutron stars (Stone 2021) by adding a consistent treatment of Fock exchange terms, which were omitted in almost all prior relativistic mean-field simulations of proto-neutron star properties (e.g., Pons 1999, Dexheimer 2008, Oertel 2012, Marques 2017). The only previous finite-temperature calculation including Fock terms was limited to nucleon-only matter (Zhang 2016). The new formulation enables more realistic equations of state for simulations of core-collapse supernovae and binary neutron star mergers, with full EoS tables already posted at the CompOSE database.
