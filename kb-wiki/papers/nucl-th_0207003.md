# Symmetry constraints for the emission angle dependence of Hanbury Brown--Twiss radii

> We discuss symmetry constraints on the azimuthal oscillations of two-particle correlation (Hanbury Brown--Twiss interferometry) radii for non-central collisions between equal spherical nuclei. We also propose a new method for correcting in a model-independent way the emission angle dependent correlation function for finite event plane resolution and angular binning effects.

## Key claim

The paper derives symmetry constraints on the azimuthal (emission angle) dependence of Hanbury Brown-Twiss (HBT) radius parameters for non-central collisions between equal spherical nuclei, showing that the Fourier expansions of the HBT radii contain only specific harmonic terms (even cosine, even sine, or odd cosine, odd sine) depending on the symmetries applied. The authors also propose a model-independent correction method for finite event plane resolution and angular binning effects, but the symmetry results are conditional on the specific symmetries assumed (reflection at reaction plane, point reflection from projectile-target interchange, and optional longitudinal boost invariance).

## Method

The authors analytically decompose the spatial correlation tensor S_mu_nu (which encodes the space-time widths of the emission function) into azimuthal Fourier series, then impose three symmetry constraints: reflection at the reaction plane (Symmetry I), point reflection from projectile-target interchange for equal-mass nuclei (Symmetry II), and longitudinal boost invariance (Symmetry III). They then fold these constrained Fourier expansions into the explicit azimuthal dependence of the six HBT radius parameters R^2_ij (Eq. 5) to derive the allowed harmonic structure of each radius parameter at midrapidity and for boost-invariant sources. The analysis is extended to finite symmetric rapidity windows and to the case where the impact parameter sign cannot be determined (e.g., when using elliptic flow for event plane reconstruction).

- The Fourier expansion of the spatial correlation tensor component <x y> (S_12) at midrapidity contains only sin(n*Phi) terms with n >= 2 even, and vanishes at Phi = 0 degrees and 90 degrees (Table I).
- The Fourier expansion of <t x> (S_01) at midrapidity contains only cos(n*Phi) terms with n >= 1 odd, and vanishes at Phi = 90 degrees (Table I).
- The Fourier expansion of <t y> (S_02) at midrapidity contains only sin(n*Phi) terms with n >= 1 odd, and vanishes at Phi = 0 degrees (Table I).
- For boost-invariant sources, <t z> (S_03) has Fourier expansion G_0 + 2*sum_{n>=2 even} G_n*cos(n*Phi) and vanishes at Y=0 (Table II).
- For boost-invariant sources, <x z> (S_13) has Fourier expansion 2*sum_{n>=1 odd} H_n*cos(n*Phi) and vanishes at Y=0 and Phi=90 degrees (Table II).
- The "sum rule" R_{o,2}^2 - R_{s,2}^2 + 2*R_{os,2}^2 is approximately zero if higher-order harmonics (n>=3) of the spatial correlation tensor are small and the emission duration is independent of emission angle (Eq. 28).
- The leading first-order harmonic coefficients R_{ol,1}^2 and R_{sl,1}^2 vanish for boost-invariant sources (Eq. 26 and text).
- When averaging over a symmetric rapidity interval Delta Y around Y=0, the Fourier structure of Eq. 24 is preserved, but the coefficients in Eqs. 25-27 acquire additional terms that vanish quadratically as Delta Y -> 0 (Section V).

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Wiedemann (1997) and Heinz and Wiedemann (1999) by systematically including all Fourier harmonics of the spatial correlation tensor (including time-space correlations and emission duration effects) rather than truncating at n=2 or neglecting time-space correlations. It also generalizes the earlier study by Lisa, Heinz, and Wiedemann (2000) which considered only the limit of vanishing implicit Phi dependence. The symmetry constraints enable model-independent extraction of the spatial correlation tensor components from measured HBT radii, and the correction method for event plane resolution addresses a key experimental challenge in azimuthally-sensitive HBT analyses at RHIC and LHC. The results are directly applicable to experiments using either directed flow (v_1) or elliptic flow (v_2) for event plane determination, with the latter case automatically leading to vanishing R_ol and R_sl cross terms.
