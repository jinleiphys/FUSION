# On the missing momentum dependence of the color transparency effects in (e,e'p) scattering

> We explore the missing momentum dependence of the CT effects in quasielastic (e,e'p) scattering. We develop the coupled-channel multiple-scattering theory (CCMST) description of final-state interaction including both the coherent and incoherent rescatterings of the ejectile state. We demonstrate that the contribution of the off-diagonal incoherent rescattering does not vanish at low Q^{2} which is a novel correction to the conventional Glauber theory evaluation of nuclear transparency. We comment on the nontrivial impact of this correction on the onset of CT. The sensitivity of the onset of CT to the 3q-nucleon reggeon amplitudes is discussed for the first time. We present numerical results for nuclear transparency as a function of the missing momentum for exclusive (e,e'p) reaction in the kinematical region of Q^{2} \le 40 GeV^{2} and p_{m} \le 250$ MeV/c. Our evaluations show that at Q^{2}\sim 10 CT effects are substantial only in antiparallel kinematics at p_{m,z}\sim -250 MeV/c. The effect is enhanced on light nuclei and could be observed in a high precision experiment.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the contribution of off-diagonal incoherent rescatterings to nuclear transparency does not vanish at low \(Q^{2}\), which is a novel correction to conventional Glauber theory, and that color transparency effects in \((e,e'p)\) scattering depend significantly on missing momentum, being substantial only in antiparallel kinematics at \(p_{m,z}\sim -250\) MeV/c for \(Q^{2}\sim 10\) GeV\(^{2}\), with the effect enhanced on light nuclei.

## Method

The authors develop a coupled-channel multiple-scattering theory (CCMST) description of final-state interactions in quasielastic \((e,e'p)\) scattering, including both coherent and incoherent rescatterings of the ejectile state. They perform an exact evaluation of the CCMST series using the independent particle shell model for the target nucleus, employing a realistic Pomeron part of the \(3q\)-nucleon diffraction scattering matrix, and study the convergence of the CCMST expansion in the number of excited proton states. Numerical calculations are performed for exclusive \(^{16}O(e,e'p)\) and \(^{40}Ca(e,e'p)\) reactions in the kinematical region of \(Q^{2} \le 40\) GeV\(^{2}\) and missing momentum \(p_{m} \le 250\) MeV/c.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear transparency is evaluated for \(Q^{2} \sim 10\) GeV\(^{2}\), where CT effects are found to be substantial only in antiparallel kinematics at \(p_{m,z} \sim -250\) MeV/c.
- The analysis covers the missing momentum region \(p_{m} \le 250\) MeV/c, with the Fermi momentum \(k_{F} \approx 250\) MeV/c.
- The incoherent rescatterings increase the missing momentum distribution by 3-7% at \(|\vec{p}_{m}| \le 250\) MeV/c in parallel kinematics, and for transverse kinematics the same estimate holds for \(p_{m\perp} \le 200\) MeV/c.
- The ratio between the Fermi correlation length and the interaction length for the struck proton with spectator nucleons is approximately 0.25, justifying the neglect of Fermi correlations.

## Context

This work extends prior coupled-channel analyses of color transparency in \((e,e'p)\) scattering by Benhar et al. (OnsetCT), Jennings and Kopeliovich (JK), and Jasym (Jasym), which studied the forward-backward asymmetry of the missing momentum distribution but omitted the nonzero Re/Im ratio of the forward \(pN\) scattering amplitude and used various approximations. The paper improves upon these by performing an exact evaluation of the CCMST series and including the sensitivity of CT onset to \(3q\)-nucleon reggeon amplitudes for the first time. The results enable future high-precision experiments at CEBAF to use the missing momentum dependence of nuclear transparency as a signature of color transparency, particularly in antiparallel kinematics on light nuclei.
