# On the energy-shell contributions of the three-particle~-~ three-hole excitations

> The response functions for the extended second and third random phase approximation are compared. A second order perturbation calculation shows that the first-order amplitude for the direct $3p3h$ excitation from the ground state cancels with those that are engendered by the $1p1h$-$3p3h$ coupling. As a consequence nonvanishing $3p3h$ effects to the $1p1h$ response involve off energy shell renormalization only. On shell $3p3h$ processes are absent.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that, up to second order in the residual interaction, the first-order amplitude for direct 3p3h excitation from the ground state cancels exactly with amplitudes generated by 1p1h-3p3h coupling, so that nonvanishing 3p3h effects on the 1p1h response involve only off-energy-shell renormalization and on-shell 3p3h processes are absent.

## Method

The authors compare the response functions of the extended second random phase approximation (ESRPA) and the extended third random phase approximation (ETRPA) using a second-order perturbative expansion of the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the 1p1h Green's function. They evaluate the matrix elements for the norm, energy, and excitation operators within each approximation, keeping terms up to second order in the residual interaction V for the forward sector, linear terms for the backward sector and P-Q coupling, and zeroth-order terms within the Q space. The equivalence of the ESRPA and ETRPA response functions is then shown by explicitly performing the sums over 3p3h states and using Rayleigh-Schrodinger perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

- The ground state correlation coefficients are given by c_{2_0} approximately equal to -V_{2_0 0}/E_{2_0}, where E_{2_0} is the unperturbed 2p2h energy and V_{2_0 0} is the matrix element of the residual interaction between the 2p2h state and the HF ground state.
- The norm matrix elements include Delta N_{13} = sum_{2_0} c_{2_0}^* times the overlap between 1p1h x (2p2h)_0 and 3p3h configurations.
- The forward energy matrix elements include Delta A_{13} = Delta N_{13} E_3, where E_3 is the unperturbed 3p3h energy.
- The squared amplitude |<tilde{3}|F|0>|^2 for 3p3h final states is of order O(V^4), meaning it vanishes up to second order in the residual interaction.

## Context

This work extends the generalized random phase approximation frameworks developed by Arima and collaborators (Takayanagi et al., 1988-1992) and the Julich group (Drozdz et al., 1986-1990), who previously included 2p2h ground state correlations in the extended second RPA. The paper also builds on a Tamm-Dancoff approach by Mariano et al. (1993) that included 3p3h degrees of freedom for Gamow-Teller sum-rule studies, and on the ETRPA formalism used by Mariano et al. (1993) for quasielastic electron scattering. The result enables future work to focus on off-energy-shell 3p3h renormalization effects in nuclear response functions, while clarifying that three-nucleon ejection does not occur in leading-order processes.
