# Scalar and vector decomposition of the nucleon self-energy in the relativistic Brueckner approach

> We investigate the momentum dependence of the nucleon self-energy in nuclear matter. We apply the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach and adopt the Bonn A potential. A strong momentum dependence of the scalar and vector self-energy components can be observed when a commonly used pseudo-vector choice for the covariant representation of the T-matrix is applied. This momentum dependence is dominated by the pion exchange. We discuss the problems of this choice and its relations to on-shell ambiguities of the T-matrix representation. Starting from a complete pseudo-vector representation of the T-matrix, which reproduces correctly the pseudo-vector pion-exchange contributions at the Hartree-Fock level, we observe a much weaker momentum dependence of the self-energy. This fixes the range of the inherent uncertainty in the determination of the scalar and vector self-energy components. Comparing to other work, we find that extracting the self-energy components by a fit to the single particle potential leads to even more ambiguous results.

## Key claim

The momentum dependence of the scalar and vector components of the nucleon self-energy in nuclear matter is strongly influenced by the choice of pseudo-scalar versus pseudo-vector representation of the T-matrix, and a complete pseudo-vector representation yields a much weaker momentum dependence than the commonly used "pseudo-vector choice" which still contains pseudo-scalar admixtures from pion exchange.

## Method

The authors applied the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach using the Bonn A potential as the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction. They decomposed the T-matrix into Lorentz-invariant amplitudes using different covariant representations: a direct amplitude representation, a symmetrized representation following Tjon and Wallace (1985), the Horowitz-Serot (1987) representation, and a complete pseudo-vector representation that removes pseudo-scalar admixtures via Fierz transformation identities. The self-energy components were then extracted by taking traces over the Dirac propagator and the T-matrix, integrating over occupied states in the Fermi sea.

## Key numbers

- At nuclear matter density of ρ = 0.166 fm⁻³, the pseudo-scalar treatment of pion exchange yields self-energy components that are approximately two orders of magnitude larger than the complete pseudo-vector treatment at low momenta
- The pseudo-scalar and pseudo-vector Hartree-Fock contributions to the self-energy show opposite signs for the scalar and vector components, with the pseudo-vector representation giving much weaker momentum dependence
- The effective mass at saturation density is reduced to about 0.6 M, consistent with effective hadron field theories

## Context

This work extends the analysis of ambiguities in the relativistic Brueckner approach first noted by Nuppenau et al. (1989), who observed that the determination of the self-energy depends on the projection of the T-matrix onto positive energy states. The authors build on the covariant amplitude decomposition methods of Horowitz and Serot (1987) and Tjon and Wallace (1985), showing that the commonly used "pseudo-vector choice" in Refs. [thm87a, nupp89, boersma94, sefu97] still contains pseudo-scalar admixtures. The complete pseudo-vector representation fixes the range of uncertainty in the scalar and vector self-energy components, which is important for applications to heavy ion collisions using relativistic transport models where momentum dependence plays a crucial role.
