# Comparison of two Minkowski-space approaches to heavy quarkonia

> In this work we compare mass spectra and decay constants obtained from two recent, independent, and fully relativistic approaches to the quarkonium bound-state problem: the Hamiltonian basis light-front quantization (BLFQ) approach, where light-front wave functions are naturally formulated; and, the covariant spectator theory (CST), based on a reorganization of the Bethe-Salpeter equation. Even though conceptually different, both solutions are obtained in Minkowski space. Comparisons of decay constants for more than ten states of charmonium and bottomonium show favorable agreement between the two approaches as well as with experiment where available. We also apply the Brodsky-Huang-Lepage prescription to convert the CST amplitudes into functions of light-front variables. This provides an ideal opportunity to investigate the similarities and differences at the level of the wave functions. Several qualitative features are observed in remarkable agreement between the two approaches even for the rarely addressed excited states. Leading twist distribution amplitudes as well as parton distribution functions of heavy quarkonia are also analyzed.

## Key claim

Both the Hamiltonian basis light-front quantization (BLFQ) and covariant spectator theory (CST) approaches produce mass spectra and decay constants for heavy quarkonia that agree favorably with each other and with experiment, despite their conceptually different formulations, but the agreement is qualified by the fact that both models include a one-gluon exchange interaction and a confining interaction, and the comparison is limited to states below open flavor thresholds.

## Method

The BLFQ approach diagonalizes an effective light-front Hamiltonian that includes a "soft-wall" transverse confining potential from light-front holography, a longitudinal confining potential, and a one-gluon exchange term with a running coupling. The CST approach solves a one-channel spectator equation derived from the Bethe-Salpeter equation, using a kernel with a covariant generalization of a linear confining potential, one-gluon exchange, and a constant interaction, with Pauli-Villars regularization. The Brodsky-Huang-Lepage prescription is applied to convert CST amplitudes into functions of light-front variables for direct comparison with BLFQ light-front wave functions.

## Key numbers

- Root-mean-square difference to measured bottomonium states: 39 MeV for BLFQ and 11 MeV for CST (11 states)
- Root-mean-square difference to measured charmonium states: 33 MeV for BLFQ and 42 MeV for CST (7 states)
- Decay constants calculated from CST mapped amplitudes differ from direct CST calculations by less than 2% for bottomonium and less than 10% for charmonium
- BLFQ confinement parameter kappa: 0.966 GeV for charmonium and 1.389 GeV for bottomonium (with Nmax=32)
- CST linear potential strength sigma: 0.217 GeV^2 for both charmonium and bottomonium
- CST constituent quark masses: 1.431 GeV for charm and 4.786 GeV for bottom
- BLFQ constituent quark masses: 1.603 GeV for charm and 4.902 GeV for bottom (with Nmax=32)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of heavy quarkonia using improved versions of the CST model from Leitao (2016) and the BLFQ model from Li (2015), providing new predictions for mass spectra, decay constants, and light-front distributions. The comparison is motivated by the shared Minkowski-space nature of both approaches, making them complementary to Euclidean methods such as Dyson-Schwinger equations and lattice QCD. The results enable further applications including calculation of parton distribution amplitudes and parton distribution functions for heavy quarkonia, which are relevant for processes such as quarkonium production at high energies and Higgs boson decays into quarkonia.
