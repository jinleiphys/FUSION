# Phase transitions and gluodynamics in 2-colour matter at high density

> We investigate 2-colour QCD with 2 flavours of Wilson fermion at nonzero temperature T and quark chemical potential mu, with a pion mass of 700 MeV (m_pi/m_rho=0.8). From temperature scans at fixed mu we find that the critical temperature for the superfluid to normal transition depends only very weakly on mu above the onset chemical potential, while the deconfinement crossover temperature is clearly decreasing with mu. We find indications of a region of superfluid but deconfined matter at high mu and intermediate T. The static quark potential determined from the Wilson loop is found to exhibit a 'string tension' that increases at large mu in the 'deconfined' region. The electric (longitudinal) gluon propagator in Landau gauge becomes strongly screened with increasing temperature and chemical potential. The magnetic (transverse) gluon shows little sensitivity to temperature, and exhibits a mild enhancement at intermediate mu before becoming suppressed at large mu.

## Key claim

The paper finds that in 2-colour QCD with heavy quarks (pion mass 717 MeV), the critical temperature for the superfluid-to-normal transition is remarkably constant across a range of chemical potentials above the onset threshold, while the deconfinement crossover temperature decreases clearly with increasing chemical potential; the authors report only indications of a region of superfluid but deconfined matter at high mu and intermediate T, rather than a definitive observation.

## Method

The authors performed lattice simulations of 2-colour QCD with two flavours of unimproved Wilson fermion on \(16^3 \times N_\tau\) lattices, using a standard Wilson gauge action with a diquark source term. They carried out temperature scans at fixed chemical potentials \(a\mu = 0.35, 0.4, 0.5, 0.6\) by varying \(N_\tau\) from 4 to 20, and measured the diquark condensate (order parameter for superfluidity), the renormalised Polyakov loop (deconfinement order parameter), the static quark potential from Wilson loops, and the Landau-gauge gluon propagator decomposed into chromoelectric and chromomagnetic components.

## Key numbers

*   Pion mass \(m_\pi = 717(25)\) MeV, with \(m_\pi/m_\rho = 0.8\), at lattice spacing \(a = 0.178(6)\) fm.
*   Critical temperature for the superfluid-to-normal transition \(T_s\) at \(a\mu = 0.50\) is \(T_s = 93(6)\) MeV; at \(a\mu = 0.60\) it is \(T_s = 93(7)\) MeV.
*   Deconfinement crossover temperature at \(\mu = 0\) is \(T_d = 217(23)\) MeV; at \(a\mu = 0.60\) the crossover region is \(T_d = 67\)–\(152\) MeV.
*   The static quark potential fit yields a string tension \(\sigma\) that increases with \(\mu\) for \(\mu a > 0.6\), with no significant dependence on the diquark source \(j\).
*   The gluon dressing function fit parameter \(\Lambda a = 0.999(3)\) on the \(16^3 \times 24\) lattice at \(\mu = j = 0\).

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies of 2-colour QCD by Hands et al. (2006, 2010) and Cotter et al. (2012), which provided rough estimates of phase boundaries and evidence for a quarkyonic region. The results enable more precise comparisons with continuum approaches such as Dyson–Schwinger equations, functional renormalisation group, and Nambu–Jona-Lasinio models, and provide benchmarks for methods that circumvent the sign problem in dense QCD. The observed increase of the string tension at large chemical potential in the deconfined region raises open questions about the nature of confinement and screening in dense matter.
