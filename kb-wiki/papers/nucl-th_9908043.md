# Dibaryons with Strangeness: their Weak Nonleptonic Decay using SU(3) Symmetry and how to find them in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Weak SU(3) symmetry is successfully applied to the weak hadronic decay amplitudes of octet hyperons. Weak nonmesonic and mesonic decays of various dibaryons with strangeness, their dominant decay modes, and lifetimes are calculated. Production estimates for BNL's Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider are presented employing wave function coalescence. Signals for detecting strange dibaryon states in heavy-ion collisions and revealing information about the unknown hyperon-hyperon interactions are outlined.

# Dibaryons with Strangeness: Weak Nonleptonic Decay and Production in Heavy-Ion Collisions

## Key claim

Weak SU(3) symmetry with contact interactions can successfully describe both parity-violating and parity-conserving amplitudes for hyperon decays, and when extended to dibaryon systems predicts that several strange dibaryons have decay lengths of 1–5 cm, making them detectable in relativistic heavy-ion collisions via invariant mass spectroscopy or vertex tracking, provided the binding energy exceeds a few MeV.

## Method

The authors fit an effective SU(3)-symmetric Lagrangian with five independent coupling constants (D, F for parity-violating amplitudes; G, H, J for parity-conserving amplitudes) to the seven measured hyperon weak decay amplitudes. They then apply this interaction to calculate weak mesonic and nonmesonic decay branching ratios of strange dibaryons using a Hulthen wave function, including pion and kaon exchange. Production rates for Au+Au collisions at RHIC are estimated by combining baryon source distributions from RQMD2.4 transport simulations with a phase-space coalescence model using the same Hulthen wave function.

## Key numbers

- Hyperon decay amplitudes fitted with D = 4.72, F = −1.62 (parity-violating) and G = 40.0, H = 47.8, J = −7.1, all in units of 10⁻⁷
- Decay lengths for all strange dibaryons studied: cτ ≈ 1–5 cm
- Invariant mass of (Σp)ₐ dibaryon: M = 2.128 GeV minus binding energy ε
- Production estimates shown for binding energies Eₐ = 1 MeV and Eₐ = 5 MeV, with wave function parameter α = 2
- For 10⁶ central events at RHIC, the (Ξ⁰Ξ⁻)ₐ dibaryon can be detected with reconstruction efficiency of 0.2% or better for lifetimes around 10⁻¹⁰ s

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of weak dibaryon decay by Donoghue et al. (1986) for the H dibaryon and Krivoruchenko (1982) for the (ΛΛ)ₐ bound state, and complements predictions of bound strange dibaryons from the Nijmegen soft-core potential by Stoks et al. (1999). The production estimates using RQMD with coalescence follow the same approach previously applied to deuteron yields and momentum distributions. The results enable experimental searches at RHIC (particularly with STAR) for short-lived strange dibaryons via invariant mass peaks in pp, pΛ, ΛΛ, pΞ⁻, and ΛΞ⁻ channels, and provide a pathway to extract the unknown hyperon-hyperon strong interaction from two-particle correlation functions.
