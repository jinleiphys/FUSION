# QCD hard scattering results from PHENIX at RHIC

> Data on hadron production at high transverse momentum ($p_T>$ 2 GeV/$c$) in p+p, d+Au, and Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}$ = 200 GeV from the PHENIX experiment at RHIC are reviewed. The single inclusive spectrum of light hadrons produced in central Au+Au reactions shows significant differences compared to p+p and d+Au collisions, and provides interesting information on the properties of the underlying QCD medium present in heavy-ion reactions at collider energies.

## Key claim

The paper reviews PHENIX data showing that high-\(p_T\) hadron production in central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV is suppressed by a factor of 4–5 compared to expectations from binary nucleon-nucleon collision scaling, while d+Au collisions show no suppression (a slight enhancement of \(R_{dAu} \approx 1.1\)), indicating that the suppression in Au+Au is a final-state effect of the dense medium rather than an initial-state nuclear effect.

## Method

The PHENIX experiment measured single inclusive \(\pi^0\) spectra at mid-rapidity (\(|\eta| < 0.38\)) in p+p, d+Au, and Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV, using electromagnetic calorimeters to reconstruct neutral pions via their decay photon pairs. The nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}(p_T)\) was computed as the ratio of the Au+Au invariant yield to the p+p cross-section scaled by the nuclear overlap function \(\langle T_{AB}(b)\rangle\), quantifying deviations from incoherent superposition of nucleon-nucleon collisions. The same analysis was applied to d+Au collisions to isolate initial-state effects.

## Key numbers

- Suppression factor of 4–5 for \(\pi^0\) production in central (0–10% centrality) Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV relative to binary collision scaling.
- Minimum bias trigger acceptances: (52±4)% for p+p, (88±4)% for d+Au, and (92±3)% for Au+Au of the total inelastic cross-sections (\(\sigma_{pp}^{inel} \approx 42\) mb, \(\sigma_{dAu}^{inel} \approx 2150\) mb, \(\sigma_{AuAu}^{inel} \approx 6850\) mb).
- Total events sampled: 0.2×10⁹ (Au+Au), 5.5×10⁹ (d+Au), and 4.0×10⁹ (p+p) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV.
- \(\pi^0\) measured up to \(p_T \approx 10\) GeV/\(c\) in all collision systems.
- \(R_{dAu} \approx 1.1\) for minimum bias d+Au collisions, indicating slight enhancement (Cronin effect) rather than suppression.

## Context

This work extends the study of high-\(p_T\) hadron suppression observed at RHIC, contrasting with earlier results from CERN-ISR (Angelis et al., 1987) and CERN-SPS (WA98 Collaboration, 2002) where \(R_{AA} > 1\) was observed (Cronin enhancement). The d+Au results rule out initial-state gluon saturation models (Color-Glass-Condensate approach) as the primary cause of suppression in Au+Au, favoring final-state parton energy loss (jet quenching) in a Quark-Gluon Plasma. The data enable quantitative constraints on medium properties such as gluon densities (\(dN^g/dy \approx 1000\)), transport coefficients (\(\hat{q}_0 \approx 3.5\) GeV/fm²), and effective parton energy loss (\(dE/dx \approx 14\) GeV/fm).
