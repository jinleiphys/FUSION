# Event-plane dependent dihadron correlations with harmonic $v_n$ subtraction in Au+Au Collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}=200$ GeV

> STAR measurements of dihadron azimuthal correlations ($Δϕ$) are reported in mid-central (20-60\%) Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}=200$ GeV as a function of the trigger particle's azimuthal angle relative to the event plane, $ϕ_{s}=|ϕ_{t}-ψ_{\rm EP}|$. The elliptic ($v_2$), triangular ($v_3$), and quadratic ($v_4$) flow harmonic backgrounds are subtracted using the Zero Yield At Minimum (ZYAM) method. The results are compared to minimum-bias d+Au collisions. It is found that a finite near-side ($|Δϕ|<π/2$) long-range pseudorapidity correlation (ridge) is present in the in-plane direction ($ϕ_{s}\sim 0$). The away-side ($|Δϕ|>π/2$) correlation shows a modification from d+Au data, varying with $ϕ_{s}$. The modification may be a consequence of pathlength-dependent jet-quenching and may lead to a better understanding of high-density QCD.

## Key claim
A finite near-side long-range pseudorapidity correlation (ridge) is observed in the in-plane direction (trigger particle azimuthal angle relative to the event plane, phi_s ~ 0) in mid-central Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV, but only after subtracting elliptic (v2), triangular (v3), and quadratic (v4) flow harmonic backgrounds using the Zero Yield At Minimum (ZYAM) method; the away-side correlation modification varies with phi_s and may be a consequence of pathlength-dependent jet-quenching.

## Method
The STAR Collaboration measured dihadron azimuthal correlations (Delta phi) in 20-60% centrality Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, binning the trigger particle's azimuthal angle relative to the event plane (phi_s = |phi_t - psi_EP|). The elliptic (v2), triangular (v3), and quadratic (v4) flow harmonic backgrounds were subtracted using the ZYAM method. The resulting correlations were compared to minimum-bias d+Au collision data.

## Key numbers
- Collision system: Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, centrality 20-60%
- Trigger particle azimuthal angle relative to event plane: phi_s = |phi_t - psi_EP|
- Near-side ridge defined as |Delta phi| < pi/2; away-side defined as |Delta phi| > pi/2
- Flow harmonics subtracted: v2 (elliptic), v3 (triangular), v4 (quadratic)
- Comparison data: minimum-bias d+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV

## Context
This work extends prior STAR measurements of dihadron correlations in heavy-ion collisions by introducing event-plane dependence and subtracting multiple flow harmonics (v2, v3, v4) via the ZYAM method, building on earlier ridge observations in Au+Au collisions. The results are compared to d+Au data from the same collaboration, enabling separation of initial-state effects from final-state jet-quenching phenomena. The findings enable future studies of pathlength-dependent energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma and may constrain theoretical models of high-density QCD matter.
