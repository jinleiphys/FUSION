# Baryons in AdS/QCD

> We construct a holographic model for baryons in the context of AdS/QCD and study the spin-1/2 nucleon spectra and its couplings to mesons, taking fully account of the effects from the chiral symmetry breaking. A pair of 5D spinors is introduced to represent both left and right chiralities. Our model contains two adjustable parameters, the infrared cutoff and the Yukawa coupling of bulk spinors to bulk scalars, corresponding to the order parameter of chiral symmetry. Taking the lowest-lying nucleon mass as an input, we calculate the mass spectrum of excited nucleons and the nucleon couplings to pions. The excited nucleons show a parity-doubling pattern with smaller pion-nucleon couplings.

# Baryons in AdS/QCD

## Key claim
A holographic model for spin-1/2 baryons in AdS/QCD, using a pair of 5D Dirac spinors to represent left and right chiralities, can reproduce the nucleon spectrum and pion-nucleon couplings with only two adjustable parameters, but the model's predictions for highly excited states deviate from data, and the pion-nucleon coupling for the lowest-lying nucleons is about 50% less than the observed value.

## Method
The authors introduce a pair of 5D bulk Dirac spinors N1 and N2, corresponding to left-handed and right-handed chiral baryon operators, in a slice of AdS5 spacetime with an IR cutoff at z_m = 1/Lambda_QCD. The bulk spinors interact via a Yukawa coupling to a bulk scalar field X whose classical solution encodes the chiral condensate sigma. The Kaluza-Klein reduction yields coupled mode equations for the spinor wavefunctions, which are solved numerically using the shooting method with boundary conditions f_{1R}(z_m) = f_{2L}(z_m) = 0 and normalizability at the UV boundary.

## Key numbers
- Lowest-lying nucleon mass taken as input: 0.94 GeV
- Best-fit IR cutoff: z_m = (0.205 GeV)^{-1}
- Best-fit Yukawa coupling: g = 14.4
- Predicted mass for N(1440): 1.44 GeV (input for best fit)
- Predicted mass for N(1535): 1.50 GeV
- Next parity-doublet predicted at (2.08, 2.12) GeV
- For z_m = (0.33 GeV)^{-1} from meson sector, predicted N(1440) mass: 2.14 GeV
- Pion-nucleon coupling g_piNN ranges from 5.84 to 7.34 for best-fit z_m depending on chiral condensate parameter xi (3.4 to 4.0)
- Pion-nucleon coupling for z_m = (0.33 GeV)^{-1} ranges from 3.30 to 4.35

## Context
This work extends the AdS/QCD approach previously applied to mesons by Erlich (2005) and DaRold (2005) to include baryons, taking full account of chiral symmetry breaking effects that were not exploited in earlier baryon studies by de Teramond (2005). The model predicts a parity-doubling pattern in excited nucleons, consistent with the effective chiral restoration observed in excited baryons. The approach enables calculation of excited nucleon spectra and pion-nucleon couplings within the holographic framework, and the authors note their results may improve by including anomalous dimensions of baryon operators or using a soft IR wall geometry.
