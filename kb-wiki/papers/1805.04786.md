# DREENA-B framework: first predictions of $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$ within dynamical energy loss formalism in evolving QCD medium

> Dynamical energy loss formalism allows generating state-of-the-art suppression predictions in finite size QCD medium, employing a sophisticated model of high-$p_\perp$ parton interactions with QGP. We here report a major step of introducing medium evolution in the formalism though $1+1D$ Bjorken (``B'') expansion, while preserving all complex features of the original dynamical energy loss framework. We use this framework to provide joint $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$ predictions, for the first time within the dynamical energy loss formalism in evolving QCD medium. The predictions are generated for a wide range of observables, i.e. for all types of probes (both light and heavy) and for all centrality regions in both $Pb+Pb$ and $Xe+Xe$ collisions at the LHC. Where experimental data are available, DREENA-B framework leads to a good joint agreement with $v_2$ and $R_{AA}$ data. Such agreement is encouraging, i.e. may lead us closer to resolving $v_2$ puzzle (difficulty of previous models to jointly explain $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$ data), though this still remains to be thoroughly tested by including state-of-the-art medium evolution within DREENA framework. While introducing medium evolution significantly changes $v_2$ predictions, $R_{AA}$ predictions remain robust and moreover in a good agreement with the experimental data; $R_{AA}$ observable is therefore suitable for calibrating parton-medium interaction model, independently from the medium evolution. Finally, for heavy flavor, we observe a strikingly similar signature of the dead-cone effect on both $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$ - we also provide a simple analytical understanding behind this result. Overall, the results presented here indicate that DREENA framework is a reliable tool for QGP tomography.

# DREENA-B framework: first predictions of $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$ within dynamical energy loss formalism in evolving QCD medium

## Key claim

The DREENA-B framework, which introduces $1+1D$ Bjorken expansion into the dynamical energy loss formalism while preserving all complex features of parton-medium interactions, achieves good joint agreement with both $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$ data for light and heavy flavor probes in Pb+Pb and Xe+Xe collisions at the LHC. However, the authors caution that this agreement may lead closer to resolving the "$v_2$ puzzle" only if further confirmed by including state-of-the-art medium evolution within the DREENA framework, which remains a non-trivial ongoing task.

## Method

The authors extended the dynamical energy loss formalism (which includes radiative and collisional energy loss, finite parton mass, dynamical scattering centers, finite temperature QCD medium modeled by generalized HTL approach, running coupling, and finite magnetic mass) to incorporate a $1+1D$ Bjorken expanding QCD medium with temperature changing along the jet path. They derived analytical expressions for collisional and radiative energy loss with explicit temperature dependence, then implemented these in a numerically optimized C-based framework (DREENA-B) that uses nonuniform adaptive grids, tabulation, and interpolation to achieve nearly three orders of magnitude speedup over the previous DREENA-C constant-temperature framework. Predictions were generated for charged hadrons, D mesons, and B mesons across multiple centrality regions in both Pb+Pb at 5.02 TeV and Xe+Xe at 5.44 TeV, using standard literature parameters with no fitting parameters.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature $T_0 = 500$ MeV for most central (0-5%) 5.02 TeV Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC, estimated from an average medium temperature of 348 MeV and QCD transition temperature $T_c \approx 150$ MeV
- Proper time $\tau_0 = 0.6$ fm used for the Bjorken expansion initialization
- Charm quark mass $M = 1.2$ GeV and bottom quark mass $M = 4.75$ GeV used in calculations
- Magnetic to electric mass ratio range $0.4 < \mu_M/\mu_E < 0.6$ extracted from non-perturbative calculations, defining the uncertainty bands in predictions
- $\Lambda_{QCD} = 0.2$ GeV and $n_f = 3$ used for the QCD medium parameters
- DREENA-B predictions for charged hadrons show good agreement with ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS $R_{AA}$ data at 5.02 TeV Pb+Pb across centralities from 0-5% to 50-60%
- DREENA-B predictions for D mesons show good joint agreement with available $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$ data from ALICE and CMS at 5.02 TeV Pb+Pb for 0-10%, 10-30%, and 30-50% centrality regions

## Context

This work extends the dynamical energy loss formalism previously implemented in the DREENA-C framework (Zigic et al., 2018), which assumed constant mean temperature and visibly overestimated $v_2$ data while agreeing well with $R_{AA}$ data. The introduction of Bjorken expansion significantly reduces $v_2$ predictions while leaving $R_{AA}$ nearly unchanged, supporting the use of $R_{AA}$ for calibrating parton-medium interaction models independently of medium evolution. The framework addresses the "$v_2$ puzzle" where other models (e.g., those discussed by Noronha-Hostler et al., 2016; Betz and Gyulassy, 2014; Molnar and Sun, 2013) systematically underestimated $v_2$ data, while DREENA-C overestimated it. The authors note that more complex QGP evolution models (such as those in CUJET3 by Xu, Liao, and Gyulassy, 2015) introduced new phenomena to achieve agreement, whereas DREENA-B achieves it within the existing dynamical energy loss framework. The work enables future development of a full QGP tomography tool by providing a step-by-step transition from constant-temperature to evolving-medium predictions.
