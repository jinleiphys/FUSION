# Statistical Model for the Nucleon Structure Functions

> A phenomenological model for the nucleon structure functions is presented. Visualising the nucleon as a cavity filled with parton gas in equilibrium and parametrizing the effects due to the finiteness of the nucleon volume, we obtain a good fit to the data on the structure function $F_2^p$. The model then successfully predicts other unpolarized structure function data.

# Statistical Model for the Nucleon Structure Functions

## Key claim

A statistical model treating the nucleon as a cavity filled with a noninteracting parton gas in equilibrium, augmented by finite-size corrections parametrized by two free coefficients (a and b), can fit the proton structure function F2^p data and then predict other unpolarized structure function data without additional free parameters, provided the finite-size corrections and QCD evolution are both included.

## Method

The nucleon is modeled as a spherical volume of radius 1.11 fm containing a gas of massless quarks, antiquarks, and gluons in thermal equilibrium at temperature T with chemical potentials for u and d flavors. The density of states is modified from the infinite-volume form by adding surface and curvature terms with two free parameters a and b. The temperature and chemical potentials are determined by solving three constraints: net u quark number = 2, net d quark number = 1, and total longitudinal momentum fraction = 1. The resulting parton distributions at input scale Q0^2 = M^2 are evolved to experimental Q^2 using leading-order Dokshitzer-Gribov-Lipatov-Altarelli-Parisi equations with Λ_QCD = 0.3 GeV.

## Key numbers

- At Q^2 = 15 GeV^2, the best-fit parameters for the power-law F2^p(x)/x = c/x^m in the range 0.0004 ≤ x ≤ 0.2 are c = 0.229 ± 0.005 and m = 1.22 ± 0.01.
- At Q^2 = 35 GeV^2, the power-law fit gives c = 0.229 ± 0.006 and m = 1.26 ± 0.01; at Q^2 = 120 GeV^2, c = 0.163 ± 0.037 and m = 1.35 ± 0.04.
- The fitted finite-size correction parameters are a = -0.400 and b = 0.475.
- The resulting temperature is T = 72 MeV, with chemical potentials μ_u = 162 MeV and μ_d = 81 MeV.
- The proton charge rms radius is ρ = 0.862 fm, giving R = 1.11 fm.
- The model predicts the Gottfried sum S_G = 0.22 at Q^2 = 4 GeV^2, consistent with the experimental value 0.235 ± 0.026.
- The NA51 collaboration ratio ū(x)/d̄(x) at ⟨x⟩ = 0.18 is about 0.51.

## Context

This work extends the statistical or parton gas model approach to nucleon structure functions, which had previously struggled to simultaneously satisfy number constraints, the momentum constraint, the Gottfried sum, and the observed low-x rise of F2^p. Prior attempts by Mac and Ugaz (1989) obtained only crude agreement for x ≥ 0.2 without imposing the momentum constraint; Cleymans et al. (1994) fit data for x ≥ 0.25 but overpredicted σ_L/σ_T by a factor of 6; Bourrely et al. (1994) used eight parameters without QCD evolution and could not reproduce the fast rise of antiquark distributions below x = 0.1. The present model introduces finite-size corrections to the density of states, a feature not previously reported in structure function calculations, and demonstrates that with only two free parameters it can describe data from x ≈ 1 down to x ≈ 10^{-4}. This success motivates further theoretical study of finite-size effects in QCD bound states.
