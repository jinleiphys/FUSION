# Semiclassical trace formulae for systems with spin-orbit interactions: successes and limitations of present approaches

> We discuss the semiclassical approaches for describing systems with spin-orbit interactions by Littlejohn and Flynn (1991, 1992), Frisk and Guhr (1993), and by Bolte and Keppeler (1998, 1999). We use these methods to derive trace formulae for several two- and three-dimensional model systems, and exhibit their successes and limitations. We discuss, in particular, also the mode conversion problem that arises in the strong-coupling limit.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the weak-coupling limit (WCL) of semiclassical trace formulae for spin-orbit systems works well for weak spin-orbit coupling and yields correct leading-order terms in Planck's constant, while the strong-coupling limit (SCL) correctly includes higher-order terms but fails in the limit of vanishing spin-orbit coupling; both approaches break down for self-retracing orbits due to the mode conversion problem at turning points where the spin-orbit interaction locally vanishes.

## Method

The authors applied the semiclassical approaches of Littlejohn and Flynn (1991, 1992), Frisk and Guhr (1993), and Bolte and Keppeler (1998, 1999) to derive trace formulae for several model systems: a two-dimensional electron gas (2DEG) with Rashba spin-orbit interaction in a magnetic field, a 2DEG with Rashba term and anisotropic harmonic confinement (quantum dot), and a three-dimensional harmonic oscillator with Thomas-type spin-orbit interaction. For each system, they compared the semiclassical results to exact quantum-mechanical level densities obtained analytically or by numerical diagonalization, using coarse-grained trace formulae with Gaussian smoothing to ensure convergence of the periodic orbit sums.

## Key numbers

- For the free 2DEG with Rashba term in a magnetic field, the exact quantum eigenvalues are \(E_0 = \hbar\omega_c/2\) and \(E^\pm_n = \hbar\omega_c\left(n \pm \sqrt{1/4 + \hbar\,2n\tilde{\kappa}^2}\right)\) for \(n=1,2,3,\dots\), where \(\tilde{\kappa} = \kappa\langle v'_z\rangle\sqrt{m^*/\omega_c}\) is the renormalized coupling constant.
- The smooth part of the level density for the free 2DEG with Rashba term is \(\tilde{g}(E) = 2/\hbar\omega_c\).
- For the quantum dot with harmonic confinement, the unperturbed eigenenergies are \(E_{n_+,n_-} = \hbar\omega_+(n_+ + 1/2) + \hbar\omega_-(n_- + 1/2)\) with normal-mode frequencies \(\omega_\pm = \left[\frac{1}{2}\left(\omega_c^2+\omega_x^2+\omega_y^2 \pm \sqrt{(\omega_c^2+\omega_x^2+\omega_y^2)^2 - 4\omega_x^2\omega_y^2}\right)\right]^{1/2}\).
- In the numerical quantum dot example, the deformation was \(\omega_x = \omega_0\), \(\omega_y = 1.23\,\omega_0\), the cyclotron frequency \(\omega_c = 0.2\,\omega_0\), the spin-orbit coupling parameter \(\kappa = 0.1\), and the coarse-graining width \(\gamma = 0.3\,\hbar\omega_0\).

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical periodic orbit theory (POT) initiated by Gutzwiller to systems with spin-orbit interactions, building on the theoretical frameworks of Littlejohn and Flynn (1991, 1992), Frisk and Guhr (1993), and Bolte and Keppeler (1998, 1999). The paper tests these approaches on model systems relevant to nuclear physics (three-dimensional harmonic oscillator with Thomas spin-orbit term, as in the nuclear shell model of Nilsson), atomic physics, and semiconductor nanostructures (Rashba spin-orbit interaction in 2DEGs and quantum dots). The results enable future applications of semiclassical trace formulae to physical systems with spin-orbit coupling, but also identify the mode conversion problem at points where the spin-orbit field vanishes as an unresolved limitation requiring further theoretical development.
