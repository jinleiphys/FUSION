# Effect of Vector Meson Mass Decrease on Superfluidity in Nuclear Matter

> $^1S_0$ pairing in nuclear matter is investigated by taking the hadron mass decrease into account via the ``In-Medium Bonn potential'' which was recently proposed by Rapp et al. The resulting gap is significantly reduced in comparison with the one obtained with the original Bonn-B potential and we ascertain that the meson mass decrease is mainly responsible for this reduction.

## Key claim

The reduction of the $^1S_0$ pairing gap in nuclear matter when using the In-Medium Bonn potential is primarily caused by the decrease of vector meson masses (and the consequent lengthening of the repulsive force range), rather than by the decrease of the nucleon mass, although the nucleon mass decrease contributes to some extent.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) equations for symmetric nuclear matter, using the In-Medium Bonn potential, a one-boson-exchange (OBE) potential constructed by Rapp et al. that incorporates Brown-Rho (BR) scaling of hadron masses, as the particle-particle (p-p) channel interaction. They computed the $^1S_0$ pairing gap at the Fermi surface as a function of Fermi momentum, and separately applied BR scaling to only the nucleon mass, only the meson masses, or both, to isolate the origin of the gap reduction.

## Key numbers

- The scaling factor $C$ in the Brown-Rho scaling relation $M^*/M = m_{\rho,\omega}^*/m_{\rho,\omega} = \Lambda_{\rho,\omega}^*/\Lambda_{\rho,\omega} = 1 - C\rho/\rho_0$ is taken as $C = 0.15$.
- The maximum pairing gap from non-relativistic studies, cited as a consistent reference range, is 2–4 MeV.
- The particle-particle channel interactions are compared at a Fermi momentum $k_{\rm F} = 0.9$ fm$^{-1}$.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic treatment of $^1S_0$ pairing in nuclear matter initiated by Kucharek and Ring (1991), who derived the RHB equation, and addresses the excessive gap problem found when using standard RMFT parameter sets like NL1. It builds on the Brown-Rho scaling hypothesis (Brown and Rho) and the In-Medium Bonn potential constructed by Rapp et al., which showed that hadron mass decrease is compatible with nuclear matter saturation. The results enable further investigation into whether polarization of the Dirac sea and Fermi sea contributes to pairing, and they make QHD-based descriptions of pairing in finite nuclei and neutron-star physics more reliable.
