# Description of the nuclear octupole and quadrupole deformation

> A parametrization of octupole plus quadrupole deformation, in terms of intrinsic variables defined in the rest frame of the overall tensor of inertia, is presented and discussed. The model is valid for situations close to the axial symmetry, but non axial deformation parameters are not frozen to zero. The properties of the octupole excitations in the deformed Thorium isotopes Th-226, Th-228 are interpreted in the frame of this model. A tentative interpretation of octupole oscillations in nuclei close to the X(5) symmetry, in terms of an exactly separable potential, is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a parametrization of simultaneous octupole and quadrupole deformation that is valid for situations close to axial symmetry but does not freeze non-axial deformation parameters to zero, and applies this model to interpret octupole excitations in Thorium isotopes and nuclei near the X(5) critical point.

## Method

The authors construct a geometrical model in the Bohr hydrodynamical framework, defining intrinsic variables in the rest frame of the overall tensor of inertia that diagonalizes the kinetic energy. They linearize the conditions for vanishing off-diagonal moments of inertia under the assumption that non-axial amplitudes are small compared to axial ones, and derive a matrix of inertia whose determinant has the correct limit when the octupole amplitude goes to zero. The resulting Schrödinger equation is solved for two cases: a quadratic potential and a critical (square-well) potential, and the model is extended to treat simultaneous quadrupole-octupole oscillations with an exactly separable potential.

## Key numbers

- For $^{226}$Th and $^{228}$Th, excitation energies of positive and negative parity states in the ground-state band are compared with model predictions, with $^{226}$Th close to a critical-point potential and $^{228}$Th closer to a harmonic potential (Figure 3.1).
- The determinant of the inertia matrix $G$ is proportional to $(\beta_2^2+2\beta_3^2)^2 u_0^2 v^2 u^2 w^2$ (Equation 3.10).
- For the separable potential model applied to $^{152}$Sm and $^{150}$Nd, the negative-parity band is fitted with $\Delta_- = 20$ for $^{152}$Sm and $\Delta_- = 15$ for $^{150}$Nd (Figure 3.2).
- The parameter $\nu$ for the Bessel function zeros is given by $\nu = \sqrt{J(J+1)/3 + 9/4 + \Delta_\pi}$ (Equation 6.15).

## Context

This work extends the geometrical approach to nuclear deformation beyond the pure quadrupole Bohr model, building on the earlier treatment by Donner and Greiner (1966) and the intrinsic-frame parametrization for octupole modes by Wexler and Dussel (1999). It is motivated by experimental observations of octupole excitations in Thorium and Radium isotopes, where a phase transition from octupole vibration to stable octupole deformation appears near the quadrupole critical point. The model enables interpretation of negative-parity bands in X(5) candidate nuclei like $^{152}$Sm and $^{150}$Nd, and provides a framework for future work on simultaneous quadrupole-octupole oscillations in transitional nuclei.
