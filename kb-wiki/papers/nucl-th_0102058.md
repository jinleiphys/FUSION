# Nuclear shadowing at low photon energies

> We calculate the shadowing effect in nuclear photoabsorption at low photon energies (1-3 GeV) within a multiple scattering approach. We avoid some of the high energy approximations that are usually made in simple Glauber theory like the narrow width and the eikonal approximation. We find that the main contribution to nuclear shadowing at low energies stems from $ρ^0$ mesons with masses well below their pole mass. We also show that the possibility of scattering in non forward directions allows for a new contribution to shadowing at low energies: the production of neutral pions as intermediate hadronic states enhances the shadowing effect in the onset region. For light nuclei and small photon energies they give rise to about 30% of the total shadowing effect.

## Key claim

The paper shows that nuclear shadowing in photoabsorption at low photon energies (1–3 GeV) can be explained by a multiple scattering approach that avoids the eikonal and narrow-width approximations of simple Glauber theory, with the main contribution coming from very light ρ⁰ mesons (well below their pole mass) and a new contribution from neutral pions that is absent in eikonal-based calculations.

## Method

The authors calculate the nuclear forward Compton scattering amplitude using a multiple scattering expansion (up to triple scattering) in terms of free nucleonic scattering amplitudes, then obtain the total nuclear photoabsorption cross section via the optical theorem. They include intermediate hadronic states (ρ⁰, ω, φ, and π⁰) with momentum-dependent vacuum self-energies for the ρ⁰, use a Woods-Saxon nuclear density distribution, and allow nonforward scattering by integrating over momentum transfers weighted by the nuclear formfactor. The π⁰ contribution is treated via an effective propagator that accounts for multiple π⁰N scattering in an optical potential.

## Key numbers

- The ρ⁰ meson contributes more than 90% of the total shadowing effect (dominant intermediate state).
- For light nuclei and photon energies around 1 GeV, the π⁰ contribution accounts for about 30% of the total shadowing effect.
- For heavy nuclei (Pb) at low energies, the π⁰ contribution is reduced to about 10% of the total shadowing.
- The slope parameter for elastic VN scattering is B ≈ 6 GeV⁻², estimated from ρ⁰ photoproduction data.
- The cutoff parameter Λ in the off-shell formfactor for vector meson photoproduction is 1.2 GeV.
- The total φN cross section is taken as σ_φ = 12 mb.
- The ρ⁰ pole mass is m_ρ = 770 MeV; the threshold for ρ photoproduction is ν_th ≈ 1.1 GeV.
- The characteristic momentum at which NN correlations become important is q_c = 780 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the simple Glauber model calculations of Falter (2000) by avoiding the narrow-width and eikonal approximations, which become questionable at low energies. It addresses the early onset of shadowing observed in photoabsorption data on C, Al, Cu, Sn, and Pb from Bia (1996) and Muc (1998) in the 1–2.6 GeV range, which some newer models (Pil (1995), Bof (1996), Eng (1997)) could not explain. The results enable a more realistic treatment of nuclear shadowing in the onset region and show that intermediate π⁰ states, previously neglected in eikonal-based calculations, provide a significant contribution at low energies.
