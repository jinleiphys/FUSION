# Phase transition and hybrid star in a SU(2) chiral sigma model

> We use a modified SU(2) chiral sigma model to study nuclear matter at high density using mean field approach. We also study the phase transition of nuclear matter to quark matter in the interior of highly dense neutron stars. Stable solutions of Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations representing hybrid stars are obtained with a maximum mass of 1.69 $M_{\odot}$, radii around 9.3 kms and a quark matter core constituting nearly 55-85 % of the star radii.

## Key claim

Using a modified SU(2) chiral sigma model with mean-field approximation, a first-order phase transition from nuclear matter to quark matter is found at densities about 1.8 to 2.5 times nuclear saturation density, and stable hybrid star solutions exist only up to a central energy density of approximately 1700 MeV/fm³, beyond which instability sets in.

## Method

The authors employed a modified SU(2) chiral sigma model (MCH) including two extra higher-order scalar field interaction terms, plus isospin triplet ρ-meson interactions, to derive the nuclear matter equation of state in mean-field approximation under beta-equilibrium conditions. For the quark phase, they used a perturbative QCD equation of state including u, d, and s quarks (with strange quark mass 180 MeV) plus electrons, with bag constants B = (155 MeV)⁴ and (150 MeV)⁴ and strong coupling α_s = 0.5 and 0.6. The Gibbs criteria for a single conserved baryon chemical potential determined the phase transition points, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations were solved with the mixed equation of state to obtain hybrid star structures.

## Key numbers

- Maximum hybrid star mass: 1.69 M⊙
- Maximum hybrid star radius: approximately 9.3 km
- Quark matter core constitutes 55-85% of the star radius
- For B = (150 MeV)⁴ and α_s = 0.5: critical pressure P_crit = 7 MeV/fm³, critical chemical potential μ_crit = 993 MeV
- For B = (150 MeV)⁴ and α_s = 0.5: critical energy density in quark matter ε_crit^qm = 387 MeV/fm³, in nuclear matter ε_crit^nm = 252 MeV/fm³
- For B = (150 MeV)⁴ and α_s = 0.5: critical baryon number density in quark matter n_B^qm = 0.37 fm⁻³, in nuclear matter n_B^nm = 0.27 fm⁻³
- For central density ε_c = 450 MeV/fm³: quark core radius 5.5 km, nuclear crust 2.2 km
- For central density ε_c = 1700 MeV/fm³: quark core radius 8.2 km, nuclear crust 0.6 km
- Maximum mass uncertainty: M = 1.69^{+0.005}_{-0.001} M⊙
- Maximum radius uncertainty: R = 9.3^{+0.21}_{-0.05} km
- Surface gravitational redshift Z_s ranges from 0.2 to 0.5
- Maximum Keplerian angular velocity approximately 10⁴ sec⁻¹, implying rotation period less than 0.5 millisecond

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of hadron-quark phase transitions in neutron stars, notably those by Kapusta and others using a nonlinear Walecka model for the nuclear phase and a bag model for the quark phase, and by Glendenning and Burgio who considered both baryon and electron chemical potentials as conserved charges. The modified chiral sigma model used here produces a softer equation of state than the original chiral sigma model of earlier work, and the resulting hybrid stars (maximum mass 1.69 M⊙, radius 9.3 km) are more compact than pure neutron stars from the same model (2.1 M⊙, 12.1 km), consistent with the expectation that quark matter has a softer equation of state due to QCD asymptotic freedom.
