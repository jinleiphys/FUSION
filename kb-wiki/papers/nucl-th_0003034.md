# Double giant dipole resonances in time-dependent density-matrix theory

> The strength functions of the DGDRs in 16O and 40Ca are calculated using an extended version of TDHF known as the time-dependent density-matrix theory (TDDM). The calculations are done in a self-consistent manner, in which the same Skyrme force as that used for the mean-field potential is used as the residual interaction to calculate two-body correlations. It is found that the DGDR in 16O has a large width due to the Landau damping, although the centroid energy of the DGDR is close to twice the energy of the GDR calculated in RPA. The DGDR in 40Ca is found more harmonic than that in 16O.

# Double giant dipole resonances in time-dependent density-matrix theory

## Key claim
The double giant dipole resonance (DGDR) in 16O has a centroid energy close to twice the GDR energy calculated in RPA, but exhibits a large width due to Landau damping, indicating anharmonicity; the DGDR in 40Ca is more harmonic, with a strength function similar to that predicted from the GDR in RPA.

## Method
The author applied the time-dependent density-matrix theory (TDDM), an extension of time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF), to calculate the strength functions of DGDRs in 16O and 40Ca. The same Skyrme III force (SKIII) was used self-consistently for both the mean-field potential and the residual interaction for two-body correlations, with the spin-dependent parameter x0 reduced from 0.45 to 0.3 to match GDR excitation energies from TDHF continuum calculations. The single-particle space was truncated to 1s, 1p, 2s, 1d orbits for 16O and 1s, 1p, 2s, 1d, 2p, 1f orbits for 40Ca, and the strength function was obtained via Fourier transform of the time-dependent two-body dipole moment with a smoothing width of 1 MeV.

## Key numbers
- 16O DGDR centroid energy: 44.6 MeV (energy range 35–55 MeV), compared to twice the GDR energy of 45.8 MeV, giving an energy difference of 1.2 MeV.
- 16O: fraction of the energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) for the DGDR depleted in the energy interval 10–60 MeV is 82%.
- 40Ca DGDR centroid energy: 39.6 MeV (energy range 30–50 MeV), compared to the GDR prediction centroid of 39.0 MeV, giving an energy difference of 0.6 MeV.
- 40Ca: fraction of the EWSR for the DGDR depleted in the energy interval 10–60 MeV is 88%.
- The second term in the EWSR for the DGDR (due to momentum dependence of the Skyrme force) contributes about 30% to the total EWSR value.
- RPA calculations in the truncated space depleted 90% of the EWSR for 16O and 93% for 40Ca in the energy interval 0–40 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the author's previous self-consistent TDDM approach applied to double giant quadrupole resonances (Tohyama, 1999) and provides microscopic calculations of DGDR strength functions that go beyond earlier shell-model studies (Nishi, 1990s) and quasiparticle-phonon models (Bertulani, 1990s; Dang, 1990s). The results for 16O show larger anharmonicity than found by de Souza Cruz and Weiss using the generator coordinate method (energy difference 0.8 MeV) and by Bertsch and Feldmeire (energy difference less than 0.7 MeV). For 40Ca, the energy difference of 0.6 MeV is smaller than the 1.0 MeV from shell-model calculations (Nishi, 1990s) but larger than the 0.2 MeV from de Souza Cruz and Weiss and Bertsch and Feldmeire. The work enables further studies of anharmonicities in heavier nuclei, as pursued by Catara et al. using boson expansion approaches and other groups (Chomaz, 1990s; Bertulani, 1990s; Lanza, 1990s).
