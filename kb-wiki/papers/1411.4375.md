# The neutron anomaly in the gamma N --> eta N cross section through the looking glass of the flavour SU(3) symmetry

> We study the implications of the flavour SU(3) symmetry for various interpretations of the neutron anomaly in the $γN\toηN$ cross section. We show that the explanation of the neutron anomaly due to interference of known N(1535) and N(1650) resonances implies that N(1650) resonance should have a huge coupling to $ϕ$-meson -- at least 5 times larger than the corresponding $ρ^0$ coupling. In terms of quark degrees of freedom this means that the well-known N(1650) resonance must be a "cryptoexotic pentaquark"-- its wave function should contain predominantly an $s\bar s$ component. It turns out that the "conventional" interpretation of the neutron anomaly by the interference of known resonances metamorphoses into unconventional physics picture of N(1650).

## Key claim

The conventional explanation of the neutron anomaly, interference of the known N(1535) and N(1650) resonances, implies, under flavour SU(3) symmetry, that the N(1650) resonance must have a huge coupling to the φ-meson (at least 5 times larger than its ρ⁰ coupling), meaning its wave function is predominantly an s s̄ component, i.e., a “cryptoexotic pentaquark.”

## Method

The authors used the flavour SU(3) decomposition of the γN → ηN amplitude into irreducible representations (octet symmetric, octet antisymmetric, 27-plet, and anti-decuplet) to classify possible explanations of the neutron anomaly. They then applied SU(3) relations to express the vector-meson couplings (ω, ρ⁰, φ) of the N(1650) resonance in terms of the ratio of its proton to neutron photocouplings, using the value extracted by the Bonn-Gatchina (BnGa) group from fits to A2 collaboration data.

## Key numbers

- The BnGa value for the ratio of proton to neutron photocouplings of N(1650) is Rₚₙ ≡ Aᵖ₁/₂(1650) / Aⁿ₁/₂(1650) = 1.74 ± 0.66.
- From this ratio, the SU(3) analysis yields F_V/D_V = –1.50 ± 0.44 for the octet vector couplings of N(1650).
- The inequality R_ω² + R_φ² ≥ 27 follows from the BnGa value, where R_ω and R_φ are the ratios of ω and φ couplings to the ρ⁰ coupling of N(1650).
- Assuming R_ω² ≤ 4 (ω yield not exceeding four times ρ yield), the φ-meson coupling satisfies |R_φ| ≥ 6.
- The extracted properties of the putative anti-decuplet nucleon resonance include mass 1680 ± 15 MeV, total width ≤ 40 MeV, πN width ≤ 0.5 MeV, √(Br_ηN) Aⁿ₁/₂ = 12–18 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/², and √(Br_ηN) Aᵖ₁/₂ = 1–3 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/².

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the neutron anomaly, a narrow peak in the γn → ηn cross section near W ≈ 1680 MeV, confirmed by the GRAAL, LNS, CBELSA/TAPS, and A2 collaborations, by applying flavour SU(3) constraints. It contrasts two scenarios: (I) dominance of a narrow anti-decuplet baryon resonance (predicted by Diakonov et al. (1997) and others), which also explains the neutron anomaly in Compton scattering; and (II) fine-tuned interference of N(1535) and N(1650) as advocated by the Bonn-Gatchina group (Anisovich et al., various years), which the authors show requires N(1650) to be a cryptoexotic pentaquark. The analysis enables future tests: scenario (II) fails for Compton scattering, while scenario (I) predicts a universal neutron anomaly across channels.
