# Complexified boost invariance and holographic heavy ion collisions

> At strong coupling holographic studies have shown that heavy ion collisions do not obey normal boost invariance. Here we study a modified boost invariance through a complex shift in time, and show that this leads to surprisingly good agreement with numerical holographic computations. When including perturbations the agreement becomes even better, both in the hydrodynamic and the far-from-equilibrium regime. One of the main advantages is an analytic formulation of the stress-energy tensor of the longitudinal dynamics of holographic heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a modified boost invariance, implemented by shifting the time coordinate by a complex parameter, yields an analytic stress-energy tensor that agrees surprisingly well with full numerical holographic computations of strongly coupled heavy ion collisions, even in far-from-equilibrium regimes. The hedge is that this agreement is demonstrated only for planar shock waves in strongly coupled N=4 SYM theory, not for real-world QCD.

## Method

The authors construct a complexified version of Bjorken flow by shifting the time coordinate t → t + t₃ (with t₃ complex) in the proper time τ = √((t+t₃)² - z²), then taking the real part of the resulting complex stress tensor. They compare this analytic construction to numerical solutions of colliding gravitational shock waves in AdS₅, which represent strongly coupled heavy ion collisions. The comparison uses a quantitative measure Δ that integrates differences in energy density, longitudinal pressure, and flux over the forward light-cone at two snapshots (μt = 1.5 and μt = 3.0), with a cutoff at proper time τ_min = 0.75/μ.

## Key numbers

- The dimensionless quantifier Δ for standard boost-invariant hydrodynamics (second order) is Δ = 1.08, using Λ/μ = 0.20 fitted at mid-rapidity.
- For complexified boost invariance with second-order viscous hydrodynamics, using parameters μ t₃ = 0.080 + 0.318i and phase θ = -0.425, the quantifier improves to Δ = 0.106.
- First-order viscous complexified hydrodynamics gives similar results: μ t₃ = 0.084 + 0.326i, θ = -0.390, Δ = 0.102.
- Adding a perturbation with n = 1 - 2i improves the fit further to Δ = 0.079, a roughly 25% improvement over the unperturbed complexified case.
- At μt = 3.0 with μτ_min = 0.75, the individual δ(t) values are: δ_BI = 0.87, δ_2nd = 0.057, δ_pert = 0.032.

## Context

This work extends the complexified boost invariance idea of Gubser (2012) to holographic heavy ion collisions, which were previously shown by Casalderrey-Solana et al. (2013) to violate ordinary boost invariance. The paper provides an analytic alternative to the numerically intensive gravitational shock wave calculations of Chesler et al. (2010, 2013) and Casalderrey-Solana et al. (2013). The results enable analytic modeling of longitudinal dynamics in strongly coupled heavy ion collisions, which could be useful for studying rapidity distributions and thermal fluctuations, complementing weakly coupled approaches by Gelis et al. (2010) and Schenke et al. (2010).
