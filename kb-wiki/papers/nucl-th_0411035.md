# Missing Resonances in Kaon Photoproduction on the Nucleon

> New kaon photoproduction data on a proton, gamma + p --> K+ + Lambda, are analyzed using a multipole approach. The background terms are given in terms of gauge invariant, crossing symmetric, Born diagrams with hadronic form factors, while the resonances are parameterized using Breit-Wigner forms. Preliminary results suggest a number of new resonances, as predicted by many quark model studies. A comparison between the extracted multipoles and those obtained from KAON-MAID is presented.

# Missing Resonances in Kaon Photoproduction on the Nucleon

## Key claim

Preliminary multipole analysis of new SAPHIR and JLAB data for γ + p → K⁺ + Λ suggests the presence of multiple new nucleon resonances in several partial waves, but the authors caution that the extracted resonance parameters are not unique and that χ² minimization alone cannot establish new resonances without multichannel consistency.

## Method

The authors performed a multipole analysis of kaon photoproduction data using gauge-invariant, crossing-symmetric Born diagrams with hadronic form factors for the background, and parameterized resonant electric and magnetic multipoles with Breit-Wigner forms including Blatt-Weisskopf barrier penetration factors. They allowed resonances up to spin 7/2 and constrained all resonance masses to vary between 1600 and 2200 MeV, fitting two separate models (Model I with two states in certain partial waves, Model II with one state) to the new data from SAPHIR and JLAB.

## Key numbers

- First S₁₁ state mass: 1610 MeV in both Model I and Model II, with widths 365 MeV and 354 MeV respectively
- Lower P₁₁ state mass: 1728 MeV (Model I) and 1707 MeV (Model II), both with a narrow width of 100 MeV
- Lower P₁₃ state mass: 1654 MeV (Model I) and 1688 MeV (Model II), with widths 184 MeV and 121 MeV
- D₁₃ lower state mass: 1741 MeV (Model I) and 1750 MeV (Model II), with widths 315 MeV and 252 MeV
- G₁₇ lower state mass: 1723 MeV (Model I) and 1730 MeV (Model II), both with width 100 MeV
- χ²/d.o.f.: 0.85 for Model I, 0.91 for Model II
- Interaction radius R fixed such that 1/R = 200 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier multipole analyses by Thom (1966) and Tanabe et al., which used a more limited data base, and is motivated by quark model predictions from Capstick and others that suggest many missing resonances. The new data from SAPHIR (Glander, 2003) and JLAB (McNabb, 2003) enable a more detailed search for these predicted states. The results are compared with the KAON-MAID solution, which only includes resonances with l ≤ 2, and the authors note that a previous analysis by Mart (2000) suggested a D₁₃(1900) resonance to explain structure in older SAPHIR data, but the new data present a more confusing picture. The work enables future multichannel analyses that must achieve consistency with other reactions to firmly establish new resonances.
