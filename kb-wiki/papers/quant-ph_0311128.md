# About evolution of particle transitions from one well to another in double-well potential

> The Poincare's period of particle oscillations between wells is obtained in double-well potential. The dependencies of oscillation period on transmission coefficient on distance between levels are obtained. The cases of squared potentials and some potentials having rounded off forms are considered.

## Key claim

The Poincare oscillation period for a particle tunneling between wells in a double-well potential can be derived from the energy-level spacing, but this derivation assumes the discrete energy levels have an exactly determined largest common divisor; for systems where such a divisor does not exist, only "quasi-cycles" with a given degree of accuracy can be defined.

## Method

The authors solve the stationary Schrodinger equation for two classes of double-well potentials: squared potentials (with exact analytic solutions in elementary functions) and rounded-off potentials (with solutions expressed through confluent hypergeometric functions). For the squared potential, they obtain eigenvalue equations from continuity conditions at boundaries and derive the transmission coefficient from the continuous-spectrum scattering problem. For the rounded-off case, they use the double-well Morse potential and obtain eigenvalue equations requiring numerical solution. They then connect the level splitting to the transmission coefficient via the WKB approximation and compute the oscillation period from the level spacing.

## Key numbers

* For the symmetric infinite squared potential, the oscillation period is \( T = \frac{\pi\hbar}{2E_0} \), where \( E_0 \) is the ground-state energy (Eq. 4.15).
* In the limiting cases \( D \to 0 \) and \( D \to \infty \), the oscillation period approaches \( T = \frac{4m (b-a)^2}{\pi\hbar} \) (Eq. 4.19).
* The level splitting in a quasi-classic symmetric potential is related to the transmission coefficient by \( \Delta E = const \cdot \frac{w\hbar}{\pi} \sqrt{D} \), where \( w \) is the classical oscillation frequency (Eq. 3.4).

## Context

This work extends the standard treatment of tunneling in double-well potentials found in Landau and Lifshitz (1989) and Razavy (1988) by providing exact analytic solutions for squared potentials and a detailed treatment of the Morse potential as an example of rounded-off forms. The authors classify double-well potentials into squared and rounded-off types based on the boundary-condition approach required. The results enable calculation of the Poincare oscillation period from energy-level data for any double-well system where the level spacing has a common divisor, and provide a framework for analyzing cases where only quasi-cycles are definable.
