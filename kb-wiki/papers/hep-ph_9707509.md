# New results in rho^0 meson physics

> We compare the predictions of a range of existing models based on the Vector Meson Dominance hypothesis with data on e^+ e^- -> pi^+ pi^$ and e^+ e^- -> mu^+ mu^- cross-sections and the phase and near-threshold behavior of the timelike pion form factor, with the aim of determining which (if any) of these models is capable of providing an accurate representation of the full range of experimental data. We find that, of the models considered, only that proposed by Bando et al. is able to consistently account for all information, provided one allows its parameter "a" to vary from the usual value of 2 to 2.4. Our fit with this model gives a point-like coupling (gamma pi^+ π^-) of magnitude ~ -e/6, while the common formulation of VMD excludes such a term. The resulting values for the rho mass and pi^+ pi^- and e^+e^- partial widths as well as the branching ratio for the decay omega -> pi^+ pi^- obtained within the context of this model are consistent with previous results.

## Key claim

Of the Vector Meson Dominance models tested, only the Hidden Local Symmetry (HLS) model of Bando et al. can consistently account for all data on e+e- → π+π- and e+e- → μ+μ- cross-sections and the timelike pion form factor, but only if its parameter "a" is allowed to vary from the usual value of 2 to 2.4, and a point-like coupling γπ+π- of magnitude approximately -e/6 is included.

## Method

The authors performed simultaneous fits to e+e- → π+π- data from the DM1, OLYA, and CMD collaborations (tabulated in Barkov) and e+e- → μ+μ- data from the OLYA collaboration (Shwartz), covering invariant masses from threshold to about 1 GeV. They compared four classes of models: standard VMD (in two formulations, VMD1 and VMD2), the Hidden Local Symmetry (HLS) model, and a phenomenological WCCWZ model, all with momentum-dependent ρ widths and unitarisation via the N/D formalism. The fits extracted ρ mass, width, the shape parameter λ, the universality-violating parameter ε, and for HLS the parameter a, while also including ω and φ contributions with an Orsay phase.

## Key numbers

- The HLS model parameter a was fitted to 2.4, deviating from the usual value of 2.
- The point-like coupling γπ+π- was found to have magnitude approximately -e/6.
- The ρ mass and π+π- and e+e- partial widths obtained from the HLS model fit were consistent with previous results.
- The branching ratio for ω → π+π- obtained from the HLS model fit was consistent with previous results.
- The ρ mass and width values extracted from Fπ(q2) were found to shift by about 10 MeV when a non-resonant photon-hadron coupling was accommodated.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Benayoun et al. (1995) and Benayoun et al. (1996) which showed that data on Fπ(q2) alone were insufficient to determine whether a non-resonant γπ+π- coupling exists, and which introduced phenomenological models M1, M2, and M3. The simultaneous fit to both e+e- → π+π- and e+e- → μ+μ- data allows decoupling of the ρ parameters from any non-resonant coupling, and the results enable improved tests of QCD via processes such as η'/η → π+π-γ as proposed by Chanowitz (1995), as well as more precise determinations of the hadronic contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment.
