# Asymmetric nuclear matter in a Hartree-Fock approach to non-linear QHD

> The Equation of State (EOS) for asymmetric nuclear matter is discussed starting from a phenomenological hadronic field theory of Serot-Walecka type including exchange terms. In a model with self interactions of the scalar sigma-meson we show that the Fock terms naturally lead to isospin effects in the nuclear EOS. These effects are quite large and dominate over the contribution due to isovector mesons. We obtain a potential symmetry term of "stiff" type, i.e. increasing with baryon density and an interesting behaviour of neutron/proton effective masses of relevance for transport properties of asymmetric dense matter.

## Key claim

The authors show that including Fock (exchange) terms in a non-linear Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) model with self-interacting sigma mesons naturally produces isospin effects in the nuclear equation of state, and these effects dominate over the contribution from isovector mesons. The key hedge is that this result depends on the approximation of neglecting derivative terms in the meson field equations, which is justified only for mesons with small Compton wavelengths (sigma, omega, rho) but not for light mesons such as pions.

## Method

The authors start from a QHD-II Lagrangian with nucleons coupled to neutral scalar sigma, vector omega, and isovector rho mesons, including nonlinear sigma self-interaction terms. They perform a Hartree-Fock calculation using the Wigner transform of the one-body density matrix, neglecting derivative terms in the meson field equations to connect meson field operators directly to nucleon scalar and current densities. The energy density for asymmetric nuclear matter is then derived from the statistical average of the canonical energy-momentum tensor, with Fock contributions appearing as traces of powers of the Wigner function that naturally produce scalar and vector isovector contributions.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Binding energy at saturation: E/A = -16 MeV
- Compressibility modulus: K_0 = 245 MeV
- Nucleon effective (Dirac) mass at saturation: M_0^* = 0.73 M
- Symmetry coefficient (Weizsaecker mass formula): a_4 = 31.5 MeV
- Fitted parameters: f_V = 3.998 fm^2, f_S = 9.731 fm^2, A = 0.088 fm^{-1}, B = -0.015, f_rho = 0.6 fm^2
- For comparison, Hartree calculations require f_rho = 2.3 fm^2 (about four times the free space value) with delta meson coupling f_delta = 1.4 fm^2, or f_rho = 1.2 fm^2 (almost two times free space) without delta

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts to include exchange terms in QHD without nonlinear sigma self-interactions (Bouyssy et al., 1987), which gave unsatisfactory results due to model inadequacy. It also builds on the relativistic mean field (RMF) approximation of QHD developed by Serot and Walecka (1986) and the nonlinear extensions by Boguta and Bodmer (1977) and Boguta and Rapp (1989). The results enable improved transport equations for asymmetric dense nuclear matter relevant to intermediate-energy heavy ion collisions with radioactive beams and neutron star properties, and the density-dependent isovector couplings from Fock terms produce a softening of the symmetry term at sub-nuclear densities that could be observed in fragmentation events and collective flows.
