# Subleading contributions to the decay width of the $T_{cc}^+$ tetraquark

> Recently the LHCb collaboration has announced the discovery of the $T_{cc}^+$ tetraquark. Being merely a few hundred ${\rm keV}$ below the $D^{*+} D^0$ threshold, the $T_{cc}^+$ is expected to have a molecular component, for which there is a good separation of scales that can be exploited to make reasonably accurate theoretical predictions about this tetraquark. Independently of its nature, the most important decay channels will be $D^+ D^0 π^0$, $D^0 D^0 π^+$ and $D^+ D^0 γ$. Its closeness to threshold suggests that the mass and particularly the width of the $T_{cc}^+$ tetraquark depend on the resonance profile. While the standard Breit-Wigner parametrization generates a $T_{cc}^+$ that is too broad for current theoretical calculations to reproduce, a three-body unitarized Breit-Wigner shape reveals instead a decay width ($Γ_{\rm pole} = 48\pm 2\,{}^{+0}_{-12}\,{\rm keV}$) consistent with theoretical expectations. Here we consider subleading order contributions to the decay amplitude, which though having at most a moderate impact in the width still indicate potentially significant differences with the experimental width that can be exploited to disentangle the nature of the $T_{cc}^+$. Concrete calculations yield $Γ^{\rm LO} = 49 \pm 16\,{\rm keV}$ and $Γ^{\rm NLO} = 58^{+7}_{-6}\,{\rm keV}$, though we expect further corrections to the ${\rm NLO}$ decay widths from asymptotic normalization effects. We find that a detailed comparison of the ${\rm NLO}$ total and partial decay widths with experiment suggests the existence of a small (but distinguishable from zero) non-molecular component of the $T_{cc}^+$.

## Key claim

The paper calculates next-to-leading order (NLO) contributions to the decay width of the molecular $T_{cc}^+$ tetraquark, finding that while these subleading effects have only a moderate impact on the width, a detailed comparison with experiment suggests the existence of a small but distinguishable non-molecular component of the $T_{cc}^+$.

## Method

The authors compute the $T_{cc}^+$ decay width into $DD\pi$ and $DD\gamma$ channels using an effective field theory (EFT) with a power counting where the pion mass is treated as a hard scale. They evaluate one-body decay operators (leading order), seagull diagrams (next-to-leading order), and include rescattering effects in the final $DD$ pair, using a contact-range molecular wave function for the $T_{cc}^+$ with low-mass ($D^{*+}D^0$) and high-mass ($D^{*0}D^+$) channel components.

## Key numbers

- The $T_{cc}^+$ pole mass difference relative to the $D^{*+}D^0$ threshold is $\delta m_{\rm pole} = -360 \pm 40 {}^{+4}_{-0} \,{\rm keV}$, with pole width $\Gamma_{\rm pole} = 48 \pm 2 {}^{+0}_{-12} \,{\rm keV}$ from the three-body unitarized Breit-Wigner analysis of LHCb data.
- The leading order (LO) decay width calculation yields $\Gamma^{\rm LO} = 49 \pm 16\,{\rm keV}$.
- The next-to-leading order (NLO) decay width calculation yields $\Gamma^{\rm NLO} = 58^{+7}_{-6}\,{\rm keV}$.
- The wave numbers for the $T_{cc}^+$ molecular components are $\gamma_L = 26.4\,{\rm MeV}$ for the low-mass channel and $\gamma_H = 58.5\,{\rm MeV}$ for the high-mass channel, using the central value of $\delta m_{\rm pole}$.
- The naive EFT convergence parameter is estimated to lie between $0.18$ and $0.42$, giving relative uncertainties $\Delta \Gamma^{\rm LO}/\Gamma^{\rm LO} \sim 0.18-0.42$ and $\Delta \Gamma^{\rm NLO}/\Gamma^{\rm NLO} \sim 0.03-0.18$.

## Context

This work extends the molecular description of the $T_{cc}^+$ tetraquark discovered by LHCb (2021), following earlier decay width calculations by Meng (2021), Ling (2021), and Feijoo (2021) that used one-body decay operators. The authors build on the EFT framework for shallow bound states developed by van Kolck (1998) and Chen (1999), and their power counting has similarities with X-EFT by Fleming (2007). The results enable future disentanglement of molecular and compact tetraquark components of the $T_{cc}^+$ through comparison of NLO predictions with experimental data.
