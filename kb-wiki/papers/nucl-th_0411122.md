# Nuclear density functional constrained by low-energy QCD

> We have developed a relativistic point-coupling model of nuclear many-body dynamics constrained by the low-energy sector of QCD. The effective Lagrangian is characterized by density-dependent coupling strengths determined by chiral one- and two-pion exchange (with single and double delta isobar excitations) and by large isoscalar background fields that arise through changes of the quark condensate and the quark density at finite baryon density. The model has been tested in the analysis of nuclear ground-state properties along different isotope chains of medium and heavy nuclei. The agreement with experimental data is comparable with purely phenomenological predictions. The built-in QCD constraints and the explicit treatment of pion exchange restrict the freedom in adjusting parameters and functional forms of density-dependent couplings. It is shown that chiral pionic fluctuations play an important role for nuclear binding and saturation mechanism, whereas background fields of about equal magnitude and opposite sign generate the effective spin-orbit potential in nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a relativistic point-coupling model of nuclear many-body dynamics, constrained by low-energy QCD through in-medium chiral perturbation theory and QCD sum rules, can achieve quantitative agreement with experimental nuclear ground-state properties comparable to purely phenomenological models, while restricting the freedom in parameter adjustment.

## Method

The authors constructed a relativistic point-coupling Lagrangian with density-dependent coupling strengths, where the density dependence is determined by two components: (1) large isoscalar background fields derived from finite-density QCD sum rules describing changes in the quark condensate and quark density, and (2) pionic contributions from one- and two-pion exchange (including single and double delta isobar excitations) calculated using in-medium chiral perturbation theory. The model was implemented in a mean-field approximation and fitted to ground-state observables (binding energies and charge radii) of nuclei along the valley of beta-stability from oxygen-16 to lead-214 using a least-squares minimization procedure.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon sigma mass term is taken as approximately 50 MeV, and the sum of light quark masses (m_u + m_d) is approximately 12 MeV at a renormalization scale of 1 GeV
- The QCD sum rules predict scalar and vector self-energies of about 300-400 MeV, approximately equal in magnitude and opposite in sign, with an uncertainty of about 20% in their ratio
- The empirical saturation point of symmetric nuclear matter is given as binding energy per nucleon E_bar_0 = -16 MeV at density rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-1}
- The Borel mass scale Lambda_B is approximately 1 GeV, separating perturbative and non-perturbative energy domains

## Context

This work extends the program of connecting QCD to nuclear structure by combining two previously separate approaches: finite-density QCD sum rules (which predict large scalar and vector self-energies) and in-medium chiral perturbation theory calculations of nuclear matter (Kaiser et al., recent years). The model is compared against two standard relativistic phenomenological parametrizations: NL3 (which uses explicit sigma-meson self-interactions) and DD-ME1 (which uses phenomenological density-dependent couplings). The approach enables a more fundamental understanding of nuclear saturation and the spin-orbit potential, showing that chiral pionic fluctuations contribute to binding while background fields of opposite sign generate the effective spin-orbit splitting.
