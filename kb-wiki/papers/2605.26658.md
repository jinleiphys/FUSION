# Isospin-breaking effects on the threshold cusp structures in $ΛN$-$ΣN$ scattering

> We discuss the isospin-breaking effects on threshold cusp structures in multichannel scattering near two-body thresholds. In hadronic systems with isospin symmetry, two or more nearly degenerate thresholds can appear, and their small splitting due to isospin breaking can generate multiple cusp structures in a narrow energy region. In this paper, using the $K$-matrix representation, we derive a general expression for the scattering amplitude near the thresholds and show that the cusp structures can be classified by the signs of the slopes of the cross section above and below threshold. We also show that additional restrictions appear in two- or three-channel systems and in the Flatté amplitude. For three-channel scattering with two nearby thresholds, we clarify how the two cusp structures are related when the threshold splitting is small and how they merge into a single cusp in the degenerate limit. Finally, we discuss the cusp structures in the $Λp$ elastic cross section in the coupled $ΛN$-$ΣN$ system with charge $Q=+1$. We show that, when isospin breaking is small, the two cusp structures are constrained by isospin symmetry. We also perform quantitative calculations using both simplified examples and realistic input based on N$^2$LO chiral effective field theory, and find that isospin breaking can significantly modify the relative sharpness of the cusps and may even change the cusp type itself.

## Key claim

The paper shows that isospin-breaking effects in the coupled $\Lambda N$-$\Sigma N$ system can significantly modify the relative sharpness of threshold cusp structures and may even change the cusp type itself, contrary to the simplified picture where isospin symmetry constrains the two cusps to behave similarly.

## Method

The authors derive a general expression for the $N$-channel $s$-wave scattering amplitude near threshold using the $K$-matrix formalism, expressing the normalized cross section in terms of the scattering length $a_N$ and complex constants $b_{ij}$. They classify cusp structures by the signs of the slopes above and below threshold, then apply this formalism to the coupled $\Lambda p$-$\Sigma^+ n$-$\Sigma^0 p$ system with charge $Q=+1$, performing quantitative calculations using both simplified examples and realistic input based on N$^2$LO chiral effective field theory.

## Key numbers

- The threshold energy difference between $\Lambda p$ and $\Sigma^+ n$ is about 75 MeV, whereas the difference between the isospin partners $\Sigma^+ n$ and $\Sigma^0 p$ is only about 2 MeV.
- The scattering length of channel $N$, $a_N$, is defined via $d(E) = \det[\hat{R}](1 + ia_N p_N(E))$, where $\Im[a_N] < 0$ due to unitarity.
- For two-channel scattering, the slope above threshold is determined only by $\Im[a_2]$, restricting cusp types to (a) and (c) in the four-type classification.
- For the Flatté amplitude, the scattering length is $a_N^{\rm F} = -1/(\alpha - i\sum_{k=1}^{N-1} r_k p_k)$, and the slope above threshold is always negative.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of threshold cusp analysis developed by Wigner (1948), Badalian (1981), and Guo (2019) to include isospin-breaking effects in multichannel scattering. It builds on prior studies of cusp structures in hadron physics, including the determination of $\pi\pi$ scattering lengths from $K$ meson decays (Cabibbo, 2004; Batley, 2005) and the analysis of $\Sigma N$ cusps in chiral EFT (Haidenbauer, 2021). The results enable future experimental analyses of the $\Sigma N$ cusp in the $K^- d \to p \Lambda \pi^-$ reaction proposed as J-PARC E90 (Ichikawa, 2022), and provide tools for distinguishing genuine near-threshold exotic hadrons from threshold-induced enhancements.
