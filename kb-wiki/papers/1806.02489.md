# Threshold corrections of $χ_{\rm c}(2P)$ and $χ_{\rm b}(3P)$ states and $J/ψρ$ and $J/ψω$ transitions of the $X(3872)$ in a coupled-channel model

> We calculate the masses of $χ_{\rm c}(2P)$ and $χ_{\rm b}(3P)$ states with threshold corrections in a coupled-channel model. Here, the meson quarkonium core is augmented by higher Fock components due to pair-creation effects. According to our results, we interpret the resonances characterized by very small threshold corrections, like $χ_{\rm b}(3P)$'s, as almost pure quarkonia, and those states characterized by non-negligible threshold corrections, like the $X(3872)$, as quarkonium cores plus meson-meson components. We also study the $J/ψρ$ and $J/ψω$ hidden-flavor strong decays of the $X(3872)$. The decays are calculated as the dissociation of one of these components ($D^0 \bar D^{0*}$) into a $c \bar c$ state ($J/ψ$) plus a light meson ($ρ$ or $ω$) in a potential model. In particular, our result for the ratio between the $X(3872) \rightarrow J/ψω$ and $J/ψρ$ widths (0.6) is compatible with the present experimental data ($0.8\pm0.3$) within the experimental error.

## Key claim

The paper argues that resonances with very small net threshold corrections (like the χb(3P) states) can be interpreted as almost pure quarkonia, while states with non-negligible corrections (like the X(3872)) should be viewed as quarkonium cores mixed with meson-meson molecular components; this interpretation depends on subtracting a "zero-mode" self-energy from each multiplet member.

## Method

The authors used a coupled-channel model where the quarkonium core wave function is augmented by higher Fock components from quark-antiquark pair creation, computing self-energy corrections from a complete set of ground-state (1S1S) open-flavor meson-meson intermediate states. For the X(3872) decays, they combined this coupled-channel formalism with a nonrelativistic potential model to calculate the dissociation of the D0 D̄0* molecular component into J/ψ plus ρ or ω mesons via quark recombination diagrams.

## Key numbers

- Self-energy corrections for χc(2P) multiplet: Σ(Mhc(2P)) = -119 MeV, Σ(Mχc0(2P)) = -103 MeV, Σ(Mχc1(2P)) = -168 MeV, Σ(Mχc2(2P)) = -133 MeV
- Calculated physical mass of χc1(2P) (X(3872)): 3888 MeV, compared to experimental 3872 MeV
- Self-energy corrections for χb(3P) multiplet: Σ(Mhb(3P)) = -116 MeV, Σ(Mχb0(3P)) = -112 MeV, Σ(Mχb1(3P)) = -114 MeV, Σ(Mχb2(3P)) = -119 MeV
- Calculated physical mass of χb1(3P): 10537 MeV, compared to experimental 10512 MeV (CMS: 10513.42 ± 0.41 ± 0.18 MeV)
- Probability of continuum component in X(3872) wave function: 0.853 (85.3%), valence component: 0.147 (14.7%)
- D0 D̄0* molecular component probability in X(3872): 0.816 (81.6%)
- Calculated width ΓX(3872)→J/ψρ = 10 keV, ΓX(3872)→J/ψω = 6 keV
- Ratio ΓX(3872)→J/ψω / ΓX(3872)→J/ψρ = 0.6, compared to experimental 0.8 ± 0.3

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel (unquenched quark model) approach previously applied to charmonium and bottomonium spectra by Ferretti and collaborators (2013-2014), and contrasts with predictions by Karliner (2014) and Lu (2016) who suggested non-negligible mixing in χb(3P) states. The results agree with recent CMS data (Sirunyan 2018) for χb1(3P) and χb2(3P) masses, supporting the interpretation of these as nearly pure bottomonia. The calculated decay ratio for X(3872) falls within the range of other molecular-model calculations by Li (2012) and Zhou (2017), and is compatible with experimental data from the PDG and BaBar (del Amo Sanchez 2010).
