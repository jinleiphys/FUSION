# Low-energy magnetic radiation: deviations from GOE

> A pronounced spike at low energy in the strength function for magnetic radiation (LEMAR) is found by means of Shell Model calculations, which explains the experimentally observed enhancement of the dipole strength. LEMAR originates from statistical low-energy M1-transitions between many excited complex states. Re-coupling of the proton and neutron high-j orbitals generates the strong magnetic radiation. LEMAR is closely related to Magnetic Rotation. LEMAR is predicted for nuclides participating in the r-process of element synthesis and is expected to change the reaction rates. An exponential decrease of the strength function and a power law for the size distribution of the $B(M1)$ values are found, which strongly deviate from the ones of the GOE of random matrices, which is commonly used to represent complex compound states.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the low-energy enhancement of the dipole strength function observed in experiments is caused by Low-Energy Magnetic Radiation (LEMAR), which consists of statistical M1 transitions between many excited complex states. The authors hedge that the statistical properties of these transitions, an exponential decrease of the strength function and a power-law distribution of B(M1) values, deviate strongly from the Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE) of random matrices, which is commonly used to represent complex compound states.

## Method

Shell Model calculations were performed for the nuclides ⁹⁰Zr, ⁹⁴Mo, ⁹⁵Mo, and ⁹⁶Mo within two model spaces (SM1 and SM2) relative to ⁶⁶Ni and ⁶⁸Ni cores, using empirical matrix elements and effective g-factors of g_s^eff = 0.7 g_s^free. The calculations included states with spins from J=0 to 6 for even-even nuclei and J=1/2 to 13/2 for ⁹⁵Mo, with the lowest 40 states calculated for each spin, yielding more than 14000 M1 transitions per parity. The average B(M1) values were sorted into 100 keV bins of transition energy, and the M1 strength functions were computed by multiplying the average B(M1) by the combinatorial level density obtained from counting calculated levels within 1 MeV energy intervals.

## Key numbers

- For ⁹⁴Mo, the experimental strength function around 1 MeV is about a factor of 10 larger than expected for a damped Giant Dipole Resonance calculated by the standard GLO expression.
- For ⁹⁰Zr, the exponential fit parameters for positive and negative parity are: B₀ = (0.36, 0.58) μ_N² and T_B = (0.33, 0.29) MeV.
- For ⁹⁴Mo, the exponential fit parameters for positive and negative parity are: B₀ = (0.32, 0.16) μ_N² and T_B = (0.35, 0.51) MeV.
- For ⁹⁵Mo, the exponential fit parameters for positive and negative parity are: B₀ = (0.23, 0.12) μ_N² and T_B = (0.39, 0.58) MeV.
- For ⁹⁶Mo, the exponential fit parameters for positive and negative parity are: B₀ = (0.20, 0.13) μ_N² and T_B = (0.41, 0.50) MeV.
- The M1 strength function parameters f₀ for ⁹⁰Zr, ⁹⁴Mo, ⁹⁵Mo, and ⁹⁶Mo are (34, 37, 39, 55) × 10⁻⁹ MeV⁻³, respectively, with T_f values of (0.50, 0.50, 0.51, 0.48) MeV.
- The level density fit parameters (ρ₀, T_ρ) for ⁹⁴Mo, ⁹⁵Mo, and ⁹⁶Mo are (1.37, 0.67), (1.90, 0.54), and (1.25, 0.58) in units of MeV⁻¹ and MeV, respectively.
- The power-law exponent ν for the size distribution of B(M1) values scatters around 1.2 for all studied nuclides.
- When the residual interaction matrix elements were multiplied by 0.5, the characteristic parameter T_B became 0.24 MeV, half of the full calculation value of 0.48 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of low-energy enhancement of the dipole strength function previously observed in experiments using (³He,³He') reactions on Mo isotopes by Guttormsen et al. (2005) and builds on the authors' own earlier Shell Model studies of nuclei with N=46-54 (Schwengner et al., 2009). The paper establishes a connection between LEMAR and Magnetic Rotation, which was observed in the mass 90 region by Schwengner et al. (1999) and reviewed by Frauendorf (2001). The finding that LEMAR deviates from GOE statistics challenges the standard use of random matrix theory for compound nuclear states and enables future calculations of r-process element abundances using gamma-strength functions that include the LEMAR spike, with particular relevance for the waiting-point nucleus ¹³¹Cd.
