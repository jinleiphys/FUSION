# The influence of the nuclear medium on inclusive electron and neutrino scattering off nuclei

> We present a model for inclusive electron and neutrino scattering off nuclei paying special attention to the influence of in-medium effects on the quasi-elastic scattering and pion-production mechanisms. Our results for electron scattering off Oxygen are compared to experimental data at beam energies ranging from 0.7-1.5 GeV. The good description of electron scattering serves as a benchmark for neutrino scattering.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a model incorporating in-medium effects, specifically a momentum-dependent mean-field potential and in-medium spectral functions for nucleons and the Delta resonance, can achieve good agreement with inclusive electron scattering data on Oxygen at beam energies from 0.7 to 1.5 GeV, and that this success serves as a benchmark for neutrino scattering predictions. The hedge is that the model underestimates data at high energy transfer where 2-pion production channels are missing, and that the neutrino predictions cannot be directly validated due to lack of experimental data.

## Method

The target nucleus is treated within a local Thomas-Fermi approximation as a Fermi gas of nucleons bound by a mean-field potential parametrized with a Skyrme term and a momentum-dependent contribution. Quasi-elastic scattering and Delta resonance excitation are evaluated with full in-medium kinematics, including effective masses from the mean-field potential and spectral functions for outgoing nucleons and Deltas that account for collisional broadening via a low-density approximation. For electron scattering, the single-pion background is extracted from MAID parametrizations by subtracting the Delta contribution from the total single-pion cross section; for neutrino scattering, only Delta dominance is assumed with no background terms.

## Key numbers

- The Delta resonance potential depth is about -30 MeV at nuclear saturation density rho_0, compared to a momentum-independent nucleon potential of approximately -50 MeV.
- Electron scattering data on Oxygen-16 are compared at beam energies of 700, 1080, and 1500 MeV at a fixed scattering angle of 32 degrees.
- For neutrino charged-current scattering on Oxygen-16, results are presented at a neutrino energy of 1 GeV and lepton scattering angle of 30 degrees.
- The axial form factor uses a dipole ansatz with M_A = 1 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the GiBUU transport model framework (Buss et al., 2006; Leitner et al., 2006) to a combined study of inclusive electron and neutrino scattering, building on earlier calculations that included only quasi-elastic and Delta contributions. It contrasts with the approach of Benhar et al. (2005), who use realistic spectral functions from nuclear many-body theory and achieve better results in the quasi-elastic region at 700 MeV but comparable results at 1080 MeV. The model enables future improvements to exclusive channel calculations (pion production, nucleon knockout) within the GiBUU framework and provides a benchmark for neutrino oscillation experiments that use nuclear targets.
