# Quark-hadron phase transition with surface fluctuation

> The effect of surface fluctuation on the observables of quark-hadron phase transition is studied. The Ginzburg-Landau formalism is extended by the inclusion of an extra term in the free energy that depends on the vertical displacements from a flat surface. The probability that a bin has a particular net displacement is determined by lattice simulation, where the physics input is color confinement. The surface fluctuation from bin to bin is related to multiplicity fluctuation, which in turn is measured by the factorial moments. It is found that both the F-scaling behavior and the scaling exponent are essentially unaffected by the inclusion of surface fluctuation.

## Key claim

The authors find that the $F$-scaling behavior and the scaling exponent $\nu$ of the Ginzburg-Landau description of the quark-hadron phase transition are essentially unaffected by the inclusion of surface fluctuations, with the caveat that this stability holds for the parameter ranges studied ($0<C<2$ and $0<\alpha<2$) and that the method would break down for large enough $\alpha$ where scaling would fail.

## Method

The authors extend the Ginzburg-Landau (GL) free energy by adding a term $F_s = C \sum_{\langle ij \rangle} |z_i - z_j|$ that depends on vertical displacements $z_i$ from a flat surface on a square lattice, representing color confinement. They use lattice simulation with the Metropolis algorithm to determine the probability distribution $S(\zeta)$ for the net displacement $\zeta$ of a bin, then convolve this with the GL multiplicity distribution $G(m)$ to obtain the dynamical multiplicity distribution $D(\mu)$. From $D(\mu)$ they compute normalized factorial moments $F_q$ and test $F$-scaling ($F_q \propto F_2^{\beta_q}$) and the scaling exponent $\nu$ defined by $\beta_q = (q-1)^\nu$.

## Key numbers

- The scaling exponent for pure GL (without surface fluctuation) is found to be $\nu = 1.308$ from lattice simulation, in essential agreement with prior analytic work.
- With surface fluctuations included, the net scaling exponent is $\nu = 1.306 \pm 0.035$, showing no significant change from the pure GL value.
- The range of bin sizes used corresponds to $2 \leq \delta \leq 40$ on a lattice of size $L=120$, covering the scaling range $0.018 < x < 7.5$ where $x = A a^2 / 2b$.
- The confinement parameter $C$ is varied over $0 < C < 2$ and the coupling parameter $\alpha$ over $0 < \alpha < 2$; the width $\Gamma_\zeta = \langle \zeta^2 \rangle^{1/2}$ shows a maximum at $C=2$ for $\delta=40$.

## Context

This work extends the Ginzburg-Landau description of the quark-hadron phase transition previously developed by Hwa and collaborators (1990-1995), which had found $F$-scaling with $\nu \approx 1.3$ in mean-field theory. It also builds on Ising model simulations of spatial fluctuations by Hwa and Nazirov (1993). The result that surface fluctuations do not alter the scaling exponent strengthens the GL formalism as a realistic description for heavy-ion collisions, enabling experimental tests of $F$-scaling and the universal exponent $\nu \approx 1.3$ in hadronic multiplicity data.
