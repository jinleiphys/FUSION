# Role of baryonic resonances in the dilepton emission in nucleon-nucleon collisions

> Within an effective Lagrangian model, we present calculations for cross sections of the dilepton production in proton-proton and proton-neutron collisions at laboratory kinetic energies in 1-5 GeV range. Production amplitudes include contributions from the nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung as well as from the mechanism of excitation, propagation, and radiative decay of Delta(1232) and N*(1520) intermediate baryonic resonances. It is found that the delta isobar terms dominate the cross sections in the entire considered beam energy range. Our calculations are able to explain the data of the DLS collaboration on the dilepton production in proton-proton collisions for beam energies below 1.3 GeV. However, for incident energies higher than this the inclusion of contributions from other dilepton sources like Dalitz decay of pi0 and eta mesons, and direct decay of rho and omega mesons is necessary to describe the data.

# Role of baryonic resonances in the dilepton emission in nucleon-nucleon collisions

## Key claim

The Delta(1232) isobar resonance dominates dilepton production cross sections in both proton-proton and proton-neutron collisions across the entire 1-5 GeV beam energy range, but the effective Lagrangian model can only explain DLS collaboration data for proton-proton collisions at beam energies below 1.3 GeV; at higher incident energies, additional contributions from Dalitz decay of pi0 and eta mesons and direct decay of rho and omega mesons are necessary to describe the data.

## Method

The authors employed an effective Lagrangian model where the initial nucleon-nucleon interaction is described by pi, rho, omega, and sigma meson exchange with coupling constants fitted to elastic NN scattering T-matrices. Dilepton production amplitudes were calculated including nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung, excitation and radiative decay of Delta(1232) and N*(1520) intermediate baryonic resonances, with all interference terms between amplitudes taken into account and gauge invariance preserved at electromagnetic vertices.

## Key numbers

- Delta(1232) resonance coupling constants: g(Npi) = 2.13, g(Nrho) = 7.14, with cut-off parameters 1.421 GeV and 2.273 GeV respectively
- N*(1520) resonance coupling constants: g(Npi) = 1.55, g(Nrho) = 6.44, g(Nomega) = 3.42, g(Nsigma) = 1.24, all with cut-off parameter 0.800 GeV
- Proton-neutron collision cross sections are about a factor of 2-3 larger than proton-proton cross sections at beam energies of 1.04 GeV, 2.09 GeV, and 4.88 GeV
- At beam energies above 1.27 GeV, the model calculations fall below DLS data by factors ranging from 2 to 20
- The total cross section at 1.04 GeV is slightly larger than at 1.27 GeV in both pp and pn cases, then rises monotonically with beam energy

## Context

This work extends earlier effective Lagrangian model calculations of pion and kaon production in proton-proton collisions (Engel 1996, Shyam 1998, 1999) to the dilepton channel, and improves upon prior approaches that either omitted resonance contributions entirely (Haglin 1991) or limited them to the Delta isobar only (Schaefer 1994, Faldt 1996). The study provides the first field-theoretic calculation of dilepton production in NN collisions that fully accounts for the N*(1520) resonance, and its results serve as necessary input for transport model calculations of dilepton spectra in heavy-ion collisions, enabling more reliable extraction of in-medium vector meson modifications related to chiral symmetry restoration.
