# Radiative corrections to neutron beta decay and (anti)neutrino-nucleon scattering from low-energy effective field theory

> We study radiative corrections to neutron beta decay and low-energy (anti)neutrino-nucleon scattering within a top-down effective field theory approach. As it was recently shown, a few electromagnetic and electroweak low-energy coupling constants in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory are yet to be determined. Performing matching to the four-fermion effective field theory, we relate these low-energy constants to correlation functions of vector and axial-vector currents. Such relations allow us to explicitly clarify scheme dependence for radiative corrections to neutron decay and low-energy charged-current (anti)neutrino scattering, provide a robust prediction of leading in the electromagnetic coupling constant contributions, and achieve a clear separation between short-distance and long-distance contributions.

## Key claim

The paper provides a consistent top-down effective field theory (EFT) framework for radiative corrections to neutron beta decay and low-energy (anti)neutrino-nucleon scattering, relating previously undetermined low-energy constants in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory to correlation functions of vector and axial-vector currents, thereby clarifying scheme dependence and achieving a clear separation between short-distance and long-distance contributions.

## Method

The author performs a two-step matching from the Standard Model to the four-fermion EFT and then to heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBχPT), working in the MS-bar renormalization and dimensional regularization schemes. By equating functional derivatives of the generating functional with respect to spurion fields between the χPT and four-fermion calculations at tree level and O(α), the low-energy coupling constants are expressed in terms of hadronic two-current correlation functions. The hadron tensor is decomposed into symmetric and antisymmetric parts, and its imaginary parts are related to proton structure functions via the optical theorem.

## Key numbers

- The neutron lifetime discrepancy between beam and bottle methods is 8 seconds, corresponding to 3-to-5 standard deviations.
- The latest bottle method measurement at LANL reaches a relative accuracy between 3 and 4 permilles (0.3-0.4%).
- The updated hadronic contribution to the vector coupling is (α/2π) C_V = 3.83(11) × 10^{-3}, a few standard deviations above the previous evaluation of (α/2π) C_V = 3.26(19) × 10^{-3} (Czarnecki 2004).
- The relative theory uncertainty for the beta decay rate is at the level 4-8 × 10^{-4}, which is 2 times above the experimental precision for the neutron lifetime (UCNt 2021).
- The electromagnetic coupling constant α ≈ 1/137 is used throughout.

## Context

This work extends the bottom-up EFT approach of Ando (2004) and the pion-inclusion study of Cirigliano (2022) by providing a top-down matching procedure that determines the previously unknown counterterms Ĉ_V and Ĉ_A from first principles. The framework enables a robust prediction of leading electromagnetic contributions to neutron decay and charged-current (anti)neutrino-nucleon scattering, and it clarifies the scheme dependence that has complicated comparisons between dispersive calculations (Seng 2018, Czarnecki 2019, Hayen 2020, Shiells 2020) and lattice QCD evaluations (Seng 2020, Feng 2020, Ma 2021, Yoo 2022). The results provide a path toward reducing the theoretical uncertainty below the current experimental precision, which is needed to resolve the neutron lifetime puzzle and to enable precise extraction of the CKM matrix element V_ud.
