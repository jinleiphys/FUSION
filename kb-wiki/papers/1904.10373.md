# Full distribution of clusters with universal couplings and in-medium effects

> Light and heavy clusters are calculated for asymmetric warm nuclear matter in a relativistic mean-field approach. In-medium effects, introduced via a universal cluster-meson coupling, and a binding energy shift contribution, calculated in a Thomas-Fermi approximation, were taken into account. This work considers, besides the standard lightest bound clusters $^4$He, $^3$He, $^3$H, and $^2$H, also stable and unstable clusters with higher number of nucleons, in the range $5\leq A \leq 12$, as it is natural that heavier clusters also form in core-collapse supernova matter, before the pasta phases set in. We show that these extra degrees of freedom contribute with non-negligible mass fractions to the composition of nuclear matter, and may prevail over deuterons and $α$ particles at high density in strongly asymmetric matter, and not too high temperatures. The presence of the light clusters reduces the contribution of heavy clusters to a much smaller density range, and to a smaller mass fraction.

## Key claim

The authors show that including "exotic" light clusters with mass numbers between 5 and 12 in relativistic mean-field calculations of warm asymmetric nuclear matter yields non-negligible mass fractions, and these heavier clusters may become more abundant than deuterons and alpha particles at high density in strongly asymmetric matter at not too high temperatures, but only if the universal scalar cluster-meson coupling is fixed to reproduce the Virial equation of state in the low-density limit.

## Method

The authors employ a relativistic mean-field model (FSU parametrization) with point-like light clusters (A ≤ 12) coupled to meson fields via a universal scalar coupling x_s = 0.85 ± 0.05, determined by reproducing the Virial equation of state at low densities. In-medium effects are included through a binding energy shift calculated in the Thomas-Fermi approximation, which acts as an excluded-volume effect, and through the scalar cluster-meson coupling. For heavy clusters (pasta phases), a compressible liquid drop model is used with Gibbs equilibrium conditions, temperature- and isospin-dependent surface tension, and Coulomb contributions.

## Key numbers

- Universal scalar cluster-meson coupling: x_s = 0.85 ± 0.05, determined to reproduce the Virial equation of state at low densities
- Maximum baryonic number considered for light clusters: A ≤ 12, with convergence shown between A ≤ 10 and A ≤ 12
- At T = 5 MeV and y_p = 0.2, the most abundant exotic cluster at ρ = 1×10⁻² fm⁻³ is ⁵He with mass fraction 9.90%, and ⁷He appears at 4.86%
- At T = 5 MeV and y_p = 0.41, ⁴He reaches 19.19% and ⁵He reaches 14.17% at ρ = 5×10⁻³ fm⁻³
- At T = 10 MeV and y_p = 0.2, ²H reaches 12.02% at ρ = 5×10⁻³ fm⁻³, while ⁴H reaches 10.32% at ρ = 2×10⁻² fm⁻³
- Average mass number of light clusters reaches approximately 5 at T = 5 MeV and approximately 4 at T = 10 MeV

## Context

This work extends the approach of Pais (2018) for light clusters in relativistic mean-field models by including clusters with A up to 12, going beyond the standard four light clusters (²H, ³H, ³He, ⁴He) considered in earlier studies by Typel (2010), Roepke (2011), and Providência (2012). The results are relevant for core-collapse supernova matter before pasta phases set in, and the model reproduces both the Virial equation of state at low densities (Virial (2009), Typel (2012)) and equilibrium constants from heavy-ion collision data (Qin (2012)). The work enables improved nuclear statistical equilibrium models that correctly include in-medium effects for describing supernova matter.
