# Rapidity dependence of particle densities in pp and AA collisions

> We use multiple scattering and energy conservation arguments to describe $dn/dη_{NANA}$ as a function of $dn/dη_{pp}$ in the framework of string percolation. We discuss the pseudo-rapidity $η$? and beam rapidity Y dependence of particle densities. We present our results for pp, Au- Au, and Pb-Pb collisions at RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim

The paper extends a previously established string percolation model for mid-rapidity particle densities to describe the full pseudo-rapidity dependence of charged particle densities in pp and AA collisions, with the condition that the model reproduces TOTEM data at large pseudo-rapidity where Monte Carlo generators fail.

## Method

The authors use the Dual Parton Model with string percolation, where color strings stretched between projectile and target partons fuse and percolate in the impact parameter plane, introducing a color reduction factor F(η^t) that slows the increase of particle density with energy and number of participating nucleons. They generalize their earlier mid-rapidity formula (Bautista 2012) to include pseudo-rapidity dependence by adding a Fermi-like function in rapidity and a Jacobian factor, obtaining a unified expression for both pp and AA collisions. The model parameters κ = 0.63 ± 0.01, λ = 0.201 ± 0.003, and √s₀ = 245 ± 29 GeV are taken from the earlier mid-rapidity work, while new parameters α ≈ 0.34, δ ≈ 0.84, and k₁ = 1.2 are set to fit pp data.

## Key numbers

- κ = 0.63 ± 0.01, λ = 0.201 ± 0.003, and √s₀ = 245 ± 29 GeV, taken from Bautista (2012) for mid-rapidity particle density description
- α ≈ 0.34, δ ≈ 0.84, k₁ = 1.2, set to adjust equation (13) to pp data
- String radius r₀ ~ 0.2–0.3 fm, used in string density calculations
- Predictions made for Pb-Pb collisions at 3.2, 3.9, and 5.5 TeV at 0–5% centrality

## Context

This work extends the string percolation model previously applied to mid-rapidity particle densities in Bautista (2012) to the full pseudo-rapidity range, following the approach of Bautista (2011), Brogueira (2009), and Dias de Deus (2007). The model successfully describes data from RHIC (Cu-Cu at 22.4, 62.4, 200 GeV; Au-Au at 19.6, 62.4, 130, 200 GeV) and LHC (Pb-Pb at 2.76 TeV, pp at various energies including 7 TeV TOTEM data), and provides predictions for future LHC Pb-Pb runs at higher energies. The authors note that recent TOTEM measurements at √s = 7 TeV for 5.3 < |η| < 6.4 are not fully described by any Monte Carlo generator but are reproduced by their model.
