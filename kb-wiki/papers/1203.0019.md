# Monte Carlo Eikonal Scattering

> Monte Carlo evaluation is used to calculate heavy-ion elastic scattering including the center-of-mass correction and the Coulomb interaction.Angular distributions are presented for a number of nuclear pairs over a wide energy range using nucleon-nucleon scattering parameters taken from phase-shift analyses and densities from independent sources. A technique for the efficient expansion of the Glauber amplitude in partial waves is developed.

## Key claim

The Monte Carlo eikonal method can accurately predict heavy-ion elastic scattering angular distributions, but only up to a momentum transfer near 1 fm\(^{-1}\); beyond this limit the predictions fail, with the exact cutoff angle varying by nuclear pair.

## Method

The Glauber multiple-scattering amplitude was evaluated directly as a product of factors without expanding into separate terms, using Monte Carlo integration (direct sampling or Metropolis algorithm) over the many-body nuclear density. The center-of-mass correction was implemented by sampling auxiliary single-particle densities and subtracting the center-of-mass vector from each nucleon coordinate, while the Coulomb interaction was added as a correction to the strong phase. A technique for efficient partial-wave projection of the Glauber amplitude was developed.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{12}\)C\(-^{12}\)C scattering at 1.016 GeV (85 MeV/A), the maximum center-of-mass angle was 18\(^\circ\) and \(-t_{Max}=0.560\) (GeV/c)\(^2\)
- For \(\alpha-\alpha\) scattering at 2.554 GeV (638 MeV/A), the maximum center-of-mass angle was 23\(^\circ\) and \(-t_{Max}=0.765\) (GeV/c)\(^2\)
- For \(\alpha-^{208}\)Pb scattering at 0.288 GeV (72 MeV/A), the maximum center-of-mass angle was 33\(^\circ\) and \(-t_{Max}=0.700\) (GeV/c)\(^2\)
- For \(^{16}\)O\(-^{16}\)O scattering at 1.120 GeV (70 MeV/A), the maximum center-of-mass angle was 22\(^\circ\) and \(-t_{Max}=1.224\) (GeV/c)\(^2\)
- The center-of-mass correction factor for \(\alpha-\alpha\) scattering at \(-t=3\) (GeV/c)\(^2\) was \(3.6 \times 10^7\)
- The nucleon-nucleon total cross section \(\sigma\) ranged from 29.7 mb (at 343 MeV/A) to 163.5 mb (at 38.3 MeV/A) across the cases studied

## Context

This work extends the full Glauber multiple-scattering evaluation beyond the \(\alpha-\alpha\) case previously treated by Franco and Yin (1986) and Yin et al., using Monte Carlo techniques to handle heavier nuclei. It provides a systematic comparison with data for 20 nuclear pairs/energies, demonstrating that center-of-mass corrections are essential even for heavy nuclei, contrary to common practice in optical limit approximations such as those of Charagi and Gupta (1990) or Lenzi et al. The method enables future calculations of elastic scattering for arbitrary nuclear pairs without the Gaussian density approximations required by earlier analytic approaches.
