# Coexistence of planar and aplanar rotations in $^{195}$Tl

> The chirality suggested for the doublet bands B2 and B2a in $^{195}$Tl in $A\sim190$ region is reexamined. The potential-energy curves and the configurations together with the deformation parameters are obtained by the constrained covariant density functional theory. The corresponding experimental energy spectra, energy differences between doublet bands, and the available $B(M1)/B(E2)$ values are investigated by the fully quantal particle rotor model. Analysis on the basis of the angular momentum components, the $K$-plots, and the azimuthal plots suggest a planar rotation interpretation for the bands B2 and B2a. Hence, it coexists with the aplanar rotation in the other doublet bands B4 and B4a in $^{195}$Tl.

## Key claim

The doublet bands B2 and B2a in \(^{195}\)Tl, previously interpreted as chiral (aplanar rotation) bands, are instead shown to arise from planar rotation within the \(i\)-\(l\) plane, coexisting with aplanar rotation in the other doublet bands B4 and B4a.

## Method

The authors used constrained covariant density functional theory (CDFT) with the PC-PK1 functional to obtain potential-energy curves, deformation parameters, and valence-nucleon configurations for the bands of interest. They then performed fully quantal particle rotor model (PRM) calculations for the \(\pi h_{9/2}\otimes \nu i^{-2}_{13/2}\) configuration of bands B2 and B2a, using deformation parameters \(\beta=0.16\), \(\gamma=45.6^\circ\) from CDFT and an irrotational-flow moment of inertia with \(\mathcal{J}_0 = 20.0~\hbar^2/\text{MeV}\). The angular momentum geometry was analyzed via angular-momentum components, \(K\)-plots, and azimuthal plots.

## Key numbers

- Deformation parameters for state C (configuration \(\pi h_{9/2}\otimes \nu i^{-2}_{13/2}\)): \(\beta=0.16\), \(\gamma=45.6^\circ\).
- Energy difference \(\Delta E\) between bands B2 and B2a: minimum 588 keV, maximum 747 keV, much larger than the typical 200–300 keV for chiral doublet bands.
- Moment of inertia parameter: \(\mathcal{J}_0 = 20.0~\hbar^2/\text{MeV}\).
- Coriolis attenuation factor: \(\xi=0.95\).
- For bands B2 and B2a, the valence proton contributes about \(4.5\hbar\) to angular momentum along the \(s\)-axis; the valence neutron holes contribute \(\sim 10\hbar\) mainly along the \(l\)-axis.
- Ratios of moments of inertia for the three principal axes at \(\gamma=45.6^\circ\): \(\mathcal{J}_i:\mathcal{J}_s:\mathcal{J}_l = 1.00 : 0.07 : 0.54\).

## Context

This work reexamines the chirality assignment for bands B2 and B2a in \(^{195}\)Tl originally proposed by Roy (2018) based on total Routhian surface calculations and systematics of Tl isotopes. It extends the study of multiple chiral doublets (M\(\chi\)D) in the \(A\sim190\) mass region by showing that not all doublet bands in a candidate nucleus necessarily exhibit aplanar rotation. The results caution that the asymmetric degree of the valence-nucleon configuration must be carefully considered in future searches for nuclear chirality, and suggest that lifetime measurements and further transition-probability comparisons are needed to fully characterize the rotational nature.
