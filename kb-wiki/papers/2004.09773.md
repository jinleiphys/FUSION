# Light $p$-shell nuclei with cluster structures ($4 \le A \le 16$) in nuclear matter

> The composition of hot and dense nuclear matter is calculated including the $1p$-shell nuclei $4 \le A \le 16$. In-medium shifts, in particular Pauli blocking, are determined by the intrinsic wave function of the nuclei. Results are given within a shell-model approach for the nucleon wave function. Light nuclei are not always well described by the shell model. The 'clustered' nucleus $^8$Be exhibits strong correlation effects because of $α$-like clustering. Intrinsic cluster structures are also significant for the nuclei $^6$Li, $^7$Li, $^7$Be, and $^9$Be. The contribution of the relatively rare elements Li, Be, and B, to the equation of state (EoS) of matter near the saturation density is overestimated in simple approaches such as the nuclear statistical equilibrium (NSE) model. Both, the treatment of continuum correlations and the account of in-medium modifications are considered for the contribution of $^5$He and $^4$H clusters. Compared to the extended NSE including unstable nuclei, the contributions of the corresponding P$_{3/2}$ channel with $A=5, Z=2$ and P$_{2}$ channel with $A=4, Z=1$, respectively, to the EoS are strongly suppressed at high densities owing to Pauli blocking effects. For the shifts of the binding energies of the light $p$-shell nuclei, simple fit formula are given to calculate the composition of hot and dense matter in a wide parameter range.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the composition of hot and dense nuclear matter including light $p$-shell nuclei ($4 \le A \le 16$), finding that the contribution of rare elements like Li, Be, and B to the equation of state near saturation density is overestimated in simple nuclear statistical equilibrium (NSE) models because in-medium effects, particularly Pauli blocking, significantly suppress their mass fractions at high densities.

## Method

The author employs a quantum statistical approach using Green's functions to derive an in-medium Schrodinger equation for $A$-nucleon clusters, where Pauli blocking is calculated from the overlap of the cluster's intrinsic wave function (approximated by Gaussian shell-model orbitals or Woods-Saxon potentials) with the Fermi distribution of the surrounding nucleon medium. The in-medium binding energy shifts are computed for each nucleus, and the composition is obtained by summing partial densities over all channels, including continuum correlations via a generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck approach for unstable nuclei like $^5$He and $^4$H.

## Key numbers

*   The saturation density of nuclear matter is $n_{\rm sat}=0.15$ fm$^{-3}$.
*   For $^6$Li, the point rms radius is 2.435 fm, and the Gaussian width parameters are $B_s = 0.982$ fm$^{-1}$ and $B_p = 0.617$ fm$^{-1}$ (with $\beta = B_s^2/B_p^2 = 2.533$).
*   The binding energy per nucleon for $^8$Be is 7.062 MeV, but it decays with a half-life of $8.19 \times 10^{-17}$ s into two alpha particles.
*   For $^{12}$C, the binding energy per nucleon is 7.680 MeV, and the point rms radius is 2.309 fm.
*   The Mott density for each nucleus is given by $n^{\rm Mott}_{A,Z}(T)=B_{A,Z}/ \delta E^{\rm Pauli}_{A,Z}(P=0,T)$, with specific values tabulated for each nucleus at different temperatures (e.g., for $^6$Li at $T=5$ MeV, $\delta E^{\rm Pauli}_{A,Z}(5)/A = 640.6$ MeV fm$^3$ times the density).

## Context

This work extends the quantum statistical treatment of in-medium clusters previously applied to $A \le 4$ nuclei (Ropke et al., various years) to the $1p$-shell nuclei up to $A=16$, going beyond simpler approaches like the NSE model (NSE, various authors) and excluded-volume methods (Hempel, 2010). It provides fit formulas for the energy shifts and virial coefficients needed to calculate the equation of state for applications in supernovae explosions (Fischer, 2014) and heavy-ion collisions (Wolter, 2012), and it enables a more systematic foundation for generalized relativistic mean-field approaches (Pais, 2018; Typel, 2010).
