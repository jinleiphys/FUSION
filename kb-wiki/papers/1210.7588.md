# Forward-backward correlations between multiplicities in windows separated in azimuth and rapidity

> The forward-backward (FB) charged particle multiplicity correlations between windows separated in rapidity and azimuth are analyzed using a model that treats strings as independent identical emitters. Both the short-range (SR) contribution, originating from the correlation between multiplicities produced from a single source, and the long-range (LR) contribution, originating from the fluctuation in the number of sources, are taken into account. The dependencies of the FB correlation coefficient, $b$, on the windows' rapidity and azimuthal acceptance and the gaps between these windows are studied and compared with the preliminary data of ALICE. The analysis of these dependencies effectively separates the contributions of two above mechanisms. It is also demonstrated that traditional definitions of FB correlation coefficient $b$ have a strong nonlinear dependence on the acceptance of windows. Suitable alternative observables for the future FB correlation studies are proposed. The connection between $b$ and the two-particle correlation function, $C_2$, is traced, as well as its connection to the untriggered di-hadron correlation analysis. Using a model independent analysis, it is shown that measurement of the FB multiplicity correlations between two small windows separated in rapidity and azimuth fully determine the two-particle correlation function $C_2$, even if the particle distribution in rapidity is not uniform.

## Key claim

The paper argues that by studying forward-backward multiplicity correlations between windows separated in both rapidity and azimuth, the long-range contribution from event-by-event fluctuations in the number of emitting sources (strings) can be isolated from the short-range contribution originating from correlations between particles produced by a single source, and that this measurement fully determines the two-particle correlation function \(C_2\) even when the particle distribution in rapidity is not uniform.

## Method

The author uses a two-stage model where strings are treated as independent identical emitters of charged particles, with the number of strings fluctuating event-by-event. The forward-backward correlation coefficient \(b\) is calculated for windows of varying rapidity and azimuthal acceptance and separation, incorporating both the short-range correlation from a single string (parameterized as a sum of a near-side peak and an away-side ridge) and the long-range correlation from source number fluctuations. The model parameters are fitted to preliminary ALICE data on the FB correlation coefficient for small symmetric windows in pp collisions at 0.9 TeV and 7 TeV.

## Key numbers

- At \(\sqrt{s}=0.9\) TeV, the fitted product \(\mu_0\omega_N\) (average rapidity density per string times scaled variance of string number) is 0.7.
- At \(\sqrt{s}=7.0\) TeV, the fitted product \(\mu_0\omega_N\) is 2.1.
- The near-side peak parameters: \(\mu_0\Lambda_1 = 1.5\) (0.9 TeV) and 2.3 (7.0 TeV); rapidity width \(\eta_1 = 0.75\) at both energies; azimuthal width \(\phi_1 = 1.2\) (0.9 TeV) and 1.1 (7.0 TeV).
- The away-side ridge parameters: \(\mu_0\Lambda_2 = 0.4\) at both energies; rapidity width \(\eta_2 = 2.0\); azimuthal width \(\phi_2 = 1.7\); rapidity shift \(\eta_0 = 0.9\).
- The FB correlation coefficient is calculated for small symmetric windows with rapidity acceptance \(\delta\eta = 0.2\) and azimuthal acceptance \(\delta\phi = \pi/4\).

## Context

This work extends the string-based model of Vechernin (2010) by including short-range correlations from a single string, which were previously omitted. It addresses the problem of isolating the "volume" contribution from source number fluctuations, a challenge noted by Capella and Krzywicki (1978). The paper proposes alternative observables to avoid the strong nonlinear dependence of the traditional FB correlation coefficient on window acceptance, and shows that the measurement of FB correlations between two small windows determines the two-particle correlation function \(C_2\) without requiring an event mixing procedure, unlike the standard di-hadron correlation analysis.
