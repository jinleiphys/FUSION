# A model-agnostic analysis of hybrid stars with reactive interfaces

> We study hybrid stars considering the effects on stellar stability of the hadron-quark conversion speed at the sharp interface. The equation of state is constructed by combining a model-agnostic hadronic description with a constant speed of sound model for quark matter. We show that current LIGO/Virgo, NICER, low-density nuclear and high-density perturbative QCD constraints can be satisfied in two scenarios, with low and high transition pressures. If the conversion speed at the interface is slow, a new class of dynamically stable hybrid objects is possible and very stiff hadronic equations of state cannot be discarded. Densities tens of times larger than the nuclear saturation density are possible at the center of these objects. We discuss possible formation mechanisms for the new class of hybrid stars and smoking guns for their observational identification.

## Key claim

If the hadron-quark conversion speed at the sharp interface is slow (timescale much larger than ~1 ms), a new class of dynamically stable hybrid stars is possible even when the derivative of mass with respect to central energy density is negative, and very stiff hadronic equations of state that would otherwise be ruled out by GW170817 constraints cannot be discarded.

## Method

The authors construct hybrid star equations of state by combining a model-agnostic generalized piecewise polytropic hadronic description with a constant speed of sound model for quark matter, using the Maxwell construction for a sharp interface. They compute mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities for over 3000 hybrid EOSs, applying two different sets of junction conditions at the hadron-quark interface to represent slow and rapid conversion scenarios, and check consistency with constraints from LIGO/Virgo, NICER, low-density nuclear theory, and perturbative QCD.

## Key numbers

- The hadron-quark conversion is considered slow if the conversion timescale is much larger than ~1 ms, which is the typical oscillation period of perturbed fluid elements in neutron stars.
- The hadronic EOS is matched at 1.1 times nuclear saturation density (n0 = 0.16 fm^-3) to the upper limit of chiral effective field theory calculations.
- The quark EOS parameters are explored in the ranges: transition pressure pt from 10 to 300 MeV/fm^3, energy density jump Δε from 100 to 3000 MeV/fm^3, and squared speed of sound cs^2 from 0.2 to 1.
- Model 2 reaches a central density of approximately 66 times nuclear saturation density (66 n0) at the last stable object in the slow-stable hybrid branch.
- The dimensionless tidal deformability Λ1.4 of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star from GW170817 is in the range 70-580 at the 90% confidence level.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of hybrid star stability with reactive interfaces previously studied by Vasquez Flores (2012) and Pereira (2017), who first established the connection between slow interface conversions and stable configurations with negative ∂M/∂εc. It generalizes earlier studies that used specific equations of state (Mariani 2019, Tonetto 2020, Parisi 2020) to a model-agnostic framework. The results show that stiff hadronic equations of state, which appeared ruled out by GW170817 constraints from Annala (2017) and Capano (2019), remain viable if slow interface conversions produce a new branch of hybrid stars. This enables future observational searches for "twin stars" with identical masses but different radii, and motivates further study of nucleation timescales at hadron-quark interfaces.
