# Investigation of giant dipole resonance in heavy deformed nuclei with the EQMD model

> The deformation evolution of giant dipole resonance (GDR), in the chains of Sm and Nd isotopes, are investigated in the framework of an extended quantum molecular dynamics (EQMD) model. The mass number dependence of resonance peak position $(E_{m})$ in the major and minor axis directions of deformed nuclei as well as the difference $ΔE_{m}$ between them are described in detail. The correlation between the splitting ($ΔE_{m} /\bar{E}_m $) of the GDR spectra and the deformation($ β_{2}$) is further studied. The results confirm that $ΔE_{m} /\bar{E}_m $ is proportional to $ β_{2}$. By comparing the calculation with the experimental data on photon absorption cross section $σ_γ$, it shows that the EQMD model can quite well reproduce the shape of GDR spectra from spherical to prolate shape. The GDR shapes in $^{134}$Sm, $^{136}$Sm, $^{138}$Sm, $^{130}$Nd, $^{132}$Nd and $^{134}$Nd are also predicted. In addition, the symmetry energy coefficient $(E_{sym})$ dependence of GDR spectra of $^{150}$Nd is also discussed. It is found that the calculated GDR spectrum in the EQMD model is perfectly consistent with the experimental results when $E_{sym}$ equals to 32 MeV.

# Investigation of giant dipole resonance in heavy deformed nuclei with the EQMD model

## Key claim
The extended quantum molecular dynamics (EQMD) model can reproduce the shape of giant dipole resonance (GDR) spectra from spherical to prolate deformed nuclei in the Sm and Nd isotopic chains, but only when the initial ground-state nuclei are selected by matching their calculated deformation parameter β₂ to experimental data. The proportionality between the GDR splitting ΔEₘ/Ēₘ and β₂ is confirmed, with the specific linear coefficients differing between the Sm and Nd isotope chains.

## Method
The EQMD model, which treats nucleon Gaussian wave-packet widths as dynamical variables and includes a Pauli potential, was used to generate ground-state deformed nuclei via damped equations of motion. Dipole excitation was imposed at t = 0 fm/c by displacing protons relative to neutrons, and the time evolution of dipole moments in coordinate and momentum space was followed for 300 fm/c. GDR spectra were obtained by Fourier transforming the second time derivative of the dipole moment, and total spectra for deformed nuclei were constructed by superposing two Lorentzian lines corresponding to oscillations along the major (x) and minor (z) axes.

## Key numbers
- For the Sm isotope chain, the linear fit gives ΔEₘ/Ēₘ = 0.69402 β₂ − 0.00869.
- For the Nd isotope chain, the linear fit gives ΔEₘ/Ēₘ = 0.53845 β₂ + 0.02872.
- The calculated GDR spectrum of ¹⁵⁰Nd is perfectly consistent with experimental data when the symmetry energy coefficient Eₛᵧₘ equals 32 MeV.
- The deformation parameter β₂ values calculated in the EQMD model range from 0.0869 ± 0.0087 (¹⁴⁴Sm) to 0.3576 ± 0.0053 (¹³⁴Sm) for Sm isotopes, and from 0.0941 ± 0.0136 (¹⁴²Nd) to 0.3586 ± 0.0109 (¹³⁰Nd) for Nd isotopes, all consistent with experimental values from Raman (2001).
- The evolution time for the Fourier transform was taken as tₘₐₓ = 300 fm/c.

## Context
This work extends the application of the EQMD model, previously used for GDR in alpha-clustering nuclei by He (2014, 2016), to systematically study deformation effects in heavy deformed nuclei. It confirms the well-established proportionality between GDR splitting and deformation parameter β₂, originally described by Berman and Fultz (1975) and later by Aberg, Bhatt, and Ray (1998). The results enable predictions of GDR shapes for six neutron-rich isotopes (¹³⁴Sm, ¹³⁶Sm, ¹³⁸Sm, ¹³⁰Nd, ¹³²Nd, ¹³⁴Nd) where experimental photoabsorption data are currently unavailable, and suggest the EQMD model could be extended to study pygmy dipole resonance in neutron-rich nuclei, following the approach of traditional QMD applied to Ni isotopes by Cao, Tian, and Ye (2013).
