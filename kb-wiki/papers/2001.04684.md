# Nuclear structure uncertainties in coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering

> The effects of the nuclear structure uncertainties on the description of processes induced by coherent scattering of neutrinos on nuclei are investigated. A reference calculation based on a specific nuclear model is defined and the cross sections and also the expected number of events produced by neutrinos generated by the explosion of a supernova in our galaxy, and by a spallation neutron source are evaluated. By changing the input parameters of the reference calculation their relevance on cross sections and on the number of the detected events is estimated. Seven spherical nuclei with different proton to neutron ratios are considered as possible targets of the neutrinos in the detector, the lightest being $^{12}$C and the heaviest $^{208}$Pb. The effects generated by the uncertainties of the nuclear model are much smaller than those due to the supernova neutrino flux models. This makes the coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering a reliable tool to investigate the details of the neutrino sources, the neutrino-nucleus interaction, and, eventually, also to extract information about neutron distributions in nuclei.

## Key claim
The uncertainties in nuclear structure models produce effects on coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (CEνNS) cross sections and event rates that are much smaller than those due to uncertainties in supernova neutrino flux models, making CEνNS a reliable probe of neutrino sources and neutron distributions, but only for the spherical nuclei considered and within the specific nuclear model used as the reference calculation.

## Method
The authors performed Hartree-Fock (HF) plus Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) calculations using the Gogny D1M effective interaction to obtain proton and neutron density distributions for seven spherical nuclei (¹²C, ¹⁶O, ²⁰Ne, ⁴⁰Ar, ⁷⁶Ge, ¹³²Xe, ²⁰⁸Pb). They defined a reference calculation (RC) and then systematically varied input parameters, including setting form factors to unity, setting the proton form factor to zero, and equating neutron and proton form factors, to quantify the resulting changes in total cross sections and expected event numbers for supernova and spallation neutron source (SNS) neutrino spectra.

## Key numbers
- For ²⁰⁸Pb at the lowest detection threshold of 10⁻¹ keV, the expected number of events is 20 times larger than for ¹²C.
- The prompt SNS muon neutrino energy is 29.79 MeV, from the decay π⁺ → μ⁺ + ν_μ.
- The Fermi constant used is G_F = 1.1663787 × 10⁻¹¹ MeV⁻², and sin²θ_W = 0.23129.
- The supernova model assumes total energy release ℰ_B = 3.0 × 10⁵³ erg at distance D = 10 kpc, with a 1 t detector.
- The SNS calculation assumes a flux of 10⁷ neutrinos per cm² per second, a 1 t detector, and one year exposure.
- Relative differences between calculated and experimental binding energies per nucleon and rms radii are smaller than 6.5% for all nuclei studied.

## Context
This work extends previous studies by Co' and collaborators (2012, 2018, 2019) on nuclear ground-state descriptions using Gogny interactions, and directly addresses the nuclear structure uncertainties highlighted in the context of the first CEνNS observation by the COHERENT collaboration (Akimov et al., 2017). The results enable more reliable interpretation of planned experiments such as CONUS, MINER, RICOCHET, and NU-CLEUS by demonstrating that nuclear model uncertainties are subdominant to astrophysical flux uncertainties, thereby strengthening the case for using CEνNS to study supernova neutrino properties and neutron distributions.
