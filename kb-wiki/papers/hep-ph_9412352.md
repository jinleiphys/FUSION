# Open Charm Production in an Equilibrating Parton Plasma

> Open charm production during the equilibration of a gluon dominated parton plasma is calculated, with both the time-dependent temperature and parton densities given by a set of rate equations. Including pre-thermal production, the total enhancement of open charm production over the initial gluon fusion depends sensitively on the initial parton density and the effective temperature. The dependence of the pre-thermal charm production on the space-momentum correlation in the initial parton phase-space distribution is also discussed.

## Key claim

Open charm production during the equilibration of a gluon-dominated parton plasma is calculated, but the total enhancement over initial gluon fusion depends sensitively on the initial parton density and effective temperature, and pre-thermal charm production is significantly suppressed when space-momentum correlation in the initial parton phase-space distribution is included.

## Method

The authors model the evolution of a parton plasma produced in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions using rate equations for gluon and quark densities coupled to a hydrodynamic equation for longitudinal expansion, with initial conditions from the HIJING Monte Carlo model. Charm production is calculated in three stages: initial fusion during the overlapping period, pre-thermal production from secondary parton scatterings during thermalization (using a correlated phase-space distribution with free-streaming), and thermal production during the equilibrating phase (using time-dependent temperature and fugacities from the rate equations). The LPM effect is incorporated via a theta-function suppression factor in the gluon equilibration rate.

## Key numbers

- At RHIC energy, the initial conditions at thermalization time tau_iso = 0.7 fm/c give initial gluon fugacity lambda_g^0 = 0.09, initial temperature T_0 = 0.57 GeV, and initial parton density n_0 = 2.2/fm^3.
- At LHC energy, the initial conditions give lambda_g^0 = 0.28, T_0 = 0.82 GeV, and n_0 = 14.5/fm^3.
- The total integrated cross sections for initial charm production are sigma_c\bar{c} = 0.16 mb at RHIC and 5.75 mb at LHC, using charm quark mass M = 1.5 GeV.
- The parton plasma at LHC may exist in a deconfined phase for as long as 4-5 fm/c, while at RHIC the deconfined phase lasts only 1-2 fm/c.
- Increasing the initial parton density at RHIC by a factor of 4 (with same temperature) increases pre-thermal and thermal charm production by about a factor of 12.
- A fully equilibrated parton plasma (lambda_g = lambda_q = 1) at the same initial temperature would give charm enhancement about 4 times higher than the initial production.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Levai, Muller, Wang 1992) by incorporating space-momentum correlation in the initial parton phase-space distribution, which suppresses pre-thermal charm production compared to earlier estimates. The results contradict the much higher charm enhancement predictions of Geiger (1993), which the authors attribute to overestimate of intrinsic charm production. The paper enables experimental probes of initial parton density and equilibration time through measurement of charm enhancement at RHIC and LHC, and the results are consistent with the independent study by Lin and Gyulassy (1994) on formation time effects.
