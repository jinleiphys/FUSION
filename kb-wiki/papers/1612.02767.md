# Medium effects in $ΛK^+$ pair production by 2.83 GeV protons on nuclei

> We study $ΛK^+$ pair production in the interaction of protons of 2.83 GeV kinetic energy with C, Cu, Ag, and Au target nuclei in the framework of the nuclear spectral function approach for incoherent primary proton--nucleon and secondary pion--nucleon production processes, and processes associated with the creation of intermediate ${Σ^0}K^+$ pairs. The approach accounts for the initial proton and final $Λ$ hyperon absorption, final $K^+$ meson distortion in nuclei, target nucleon binding, and Fermi motion, as well as nuclear mean-field potential effects on these processes. We calculate the $Λ$ momentum dependence of the absolute $ΛK^+$ yield from the target nuclei considered, in the kinematical conditions of the ANKE experiment, performed at COSY, within the different scenarios for the $Λ$-nucleus effective scalar potential. We show that the above observable is appreciably sensitive to this potential in the low-momentum region. Therefore, direct comparison of the results of our calculations with the data from the ANKE-at-COSY experiment can help to determine the above potential at finite momenta. We also demonstrate that the two-step pion--nucleon production channels dominate in the low-momentum $ΛK^+$ production in the chosen kinematics and, therefore, they have to be taken into account in the analysis of these data.

## Key claim

The absolute yield of ΛK⁺ pairs produced by 2.83 GeV protons on nuclei is appreciably sensitive to the Λ-nucleus effective scalar potential in the low-momentum region, meaning that direct comparison of the calculations with ANKE-at-COSY data can help determine this potential at finite momenta, but only if the two-step pion-nucleon production channels (which dominate low-momentum production) are properly accounted for.

## Method

The authors employed the nuclear spectral function approach to calculate incoherent primary proton-nucleon and secondary pion-nucleon production processes, including processes with intermediate Σ⁰K⁺ pairs, for C, Cu, Ag, and Au targets. The model accounts for initial proton and final Λ hyperon absorption, final K⁺ meson distortion, target nucleon binding and Fermi motion, and nuclear mean-field potential effects, using parametrizations of elementary cross sections for pp and pn channels with zero, one, and two pions in the final state.

## Key numbers

- Incident proton kinetic energy: 2.83 GeV
- Target nuclei: C, Cu, Ag, Au
- Average nucleon density ratio ⟨ρ_N⟩/ρ_0 for targets: C (0.55), Cu (0.65), Ag (0.72), Au (0.77)
- K⁺ mass shift U_K⁺ = 22 MeV used in calculations
- Λ-nucleus Schrödinger equivalent potential at zero momentum: -(32±2) MeV, extracted from hypernuclear binding energies
- Λ potential becomes repulsive above approximately 0.7 GeV/c, reaching about 70 MeV at Λ momentum of 3 GeV/c
- Effective scalar hyperon potential U_Λ ≈ 30 MeV at Λ momentum around 1 GeV/c
- Ratio of Λ production cross sections in pp and pn processes with one or two pions to those with zero pions: about 1/9 at 2.83 GeV

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of hyperon-nucleus interactions at finite momenta, where experimental information is currently lacking. Prior theoretical work on the Λ-nucleus potential includes calculations from chiral unitary approaches (Refs. 18-20 in the paper), relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory (Ref. 51), and the constituent quark model (Ref. 54), which give somewhat different momentum dependences. The paper provides predictions for the ANKE experiment at COSY, enabling determination of the Λ-nuclear potential at finite momenta from coincident ΛK⁺ production data, which offers clearer interpretation than inclusive measurements.
