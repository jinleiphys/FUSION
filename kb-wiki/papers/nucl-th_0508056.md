# Proton-neutron pairing energies in N=Z nuclei at finite temperature

> Thermal behavior of isoscalar and isovector proton-neutron (pn) pairing energies at finite temperature are investigated by the shell model calculations. These pn pairing energies can be estimated by double differences of "thermal" energies which are extended from the double differences of binding energies as the indicators of pn pairing energies at zero temperature. We found that the delicate balance between isoscalar and isovector pn pairing energies at zero temperature disappears at finite temperature. When temperature rises, while the isovector pn pairing energy decreases, the isoscalar pn pairing energy rather increases. We discuss also the symmetry energy at finite temperature.

# Proton-neutron pairing energies in N=Z nuclei at finite temperature

## Key claim

The delicate balance between isoscalar (τ=0) and isovector (τ=1) proton-neutron pairing energies that exists at zero temperature disappears at finite temperature; as temperature rises, the isovector pairing energy decreases while the isoscalar pairing energy increases, making the isoscalar pairing dominant at high temperature.

## Method

The authors performed exact shell model calculations in the sd shell using the isospin-invariant USD interaction for odd-odd N=Z nuclei (²²Na, ²⁶Al, ³⁰P, ³⁴Cl), then extended the model space to a larger space (sd + fp + s₁/₂d₅/₂) using an independent-particle approximation with Woods-Saxon single-particle energies. They defined "thermal" pn pairing gaps as double differences of thermal energies (canonical ensemble averages of the Hamiltonian) analogous to the double differences of binding energies used at zero temperature, and computed these as functions of temperature.

## Key numbers

- At zero temperature, the isoscalar pn pairing gap is approximately described by 122.25(1 − 1.67A⁻¹/³)/A and the isovector pn pairing gap by 5.18A⁻¹/³ (these curves are shown in Fig. 1 for A=18-58)
- At T = 2.0 MeV, the τ=1 and τ=0 pn pairing gaps are "largely separated" for ²²Na, ²⁶Al, ³⁰P, and ³⁴Cl (Fig. 2)
- The calculated symmetry energy coefficient at zero temperature for A=24 is a_sym(T=0) ≈ 16 MeV
- The relative increase of the symmetry energy coefficient between T=0.0 and T=1.0 MeV, averaged over various pairs of nuclei, is approximately 4%

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of like-nucleon pairing energies at finite temperature using "thermal" odd-even mass differences (Kaneko, 2005) to the case of proton-neutron pairing in N=Z nuclei. It builds on the finding that the degeneracy between τ=0 and τ=1 ground states in odd-odd N=Z nuclei reflects competition between isoscalar and isovector pairing energies (Satula, 1997; Kaneko, 2004; Hasegawa, 2004), rather than the earlier interpretation involving symmetry energy and like-nucleon pairing (Vogel, 1984; Janecke, 1987; Zeldes, 1996; Frauendorf, 1999). The temperature dependence of the symmetry energy coefficient (increase of about 4% between T=0 and T=1 MeV) agrees with SMMC results (Donati, 2000) and is smaller than the QRPA result of about 8% (Dean, 1995). The results suggest that pn pairing energies could be estimated from measured level densities and may be relevant for astrophysical rp-process modeling.
