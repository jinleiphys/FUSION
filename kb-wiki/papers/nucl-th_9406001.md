# Nucleon Properties from Unconventional Interpolating Fields

> Interpolating fields, used to excite hadrons from the QCD vacuum in nonperturbative field-theoretic investigations of hadron properties, are explored with an emphasis on unconventional nucleon interpolators. The QCD continuum model for excited state contributions to QCD correlation functions is a central element in extracting the physics contained in these alternate correlation functions. The analysis confirms the independence of nucleon properties obtained from different interpolating fields. However, this independence comes about in a trivial manner. These results provide a resolution to the long standing debate over the optimal nucleon interpolating field to be used in QCD Sum Rule analyses.

## Key claim

The analysis confirms that nucleon properties obtained from different interpolating fields are independent, but this independence arises trivially because the unconventional interpolating field \(\chi_2\) has negligible overlap with the nucleon ground state, contrary to earlier expectations in QCD Sum Rule debates.

## Method

The author computed two-point correlation functions for nucleon interpolating fields \(\chi_1\) and \(\chi_2\) on a \(24 \times 12 \times 12 \times 24\) periodic lattice at \(\beta=5.9\) using Wilson fermions with three values of the hopping parameter (\(\kappa_1 = 0.152\), \(\kappa_2 = 0.154\), \(\kappa_3 = 0.156\)). A pole plus QCD continuum model, inspired by QCD Sum Rule analyses, was used to extract ground state nucleon properties from the correlation functions, with the continuum model parameters optimized to account for excited state contributions and lattice artifacts.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon coupling strength for \(\chi_1\) at the chiral limit: \(\lambda_1 = 0.013(2)\ \text{GeV}^3\)
- Nucleon coupling strength for \(\chi_2\) at the chiral limit: \(\lambda_2 = 0.00016(22)\ \text{GeV}^3\), approximately 100 times smaller than \(\lambda_1\)
- Combined coupling for the sum rule interpolator \(\chi_{\text{SR}}\) and alternate interpolator \(\chi_{\text{A}}\): \(\lambda_{\text{SR}} \simeq \lambda_{\text{A}} \simeq 2\lambda_1 = 0.026(4)\ \text{GeV}^3\)
- Mixed coupling at the chiral limit: \((\lambda_1 \lambda_2)^{1/2} = 0.0014(10)\ \text{GeV}^3\)
- Lattice spacing: \(a = 0.132(4)\ \text{fm}\) and \(a^{-1} = 1.49(5)\ \text{GeV}\)
- Critical hopping parameter: \(\kappa_{\text{cr}} = 0.1598(2)\)

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD analyses of nucleon interpolating fields previously attempted by Leinweber (1991) and Bowler (1984), where correlation functions deteriorated too quickly for ground state properties to be determined. The results resolve a long-standing debate in QCD Sum Rule analyses over the optimal nucleon interpolating field, which had been argued by Chung (1982, 1984), Dosch (1989), Ioffe (1981, 1983), Leinweber (1990), Furnstahl (1992), and Jin (1994). The finding that \(\chi_2\) has negligible overlap with the nucleon ground state enables lattice practitioners to use \(\chi_1\) exclusively, while for QCD Sum Rules the optimal interpolator must still involve \(\chi_1\) with careful mixing of \(\chi_2\) to balance OPE convergence and continuum contributions.
