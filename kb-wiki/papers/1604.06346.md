# Electromagnetic probes of a pure-glue initial state in nucleus-nucleus collisions at energies available at the CERN Large Hadron Collider

> Partonic matter produced in the early stage of ultrarelativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions is assumed to be composed mainly of gluons, and quarks and antiquarks are produced at later times. To study the implications of such a scenario, the dynamical evolution of a chemically nonequilibrated system is described by the ideal (2+1)-dimensional hydrodynamics with a time dependent (anti)quark fugacity. The equation of state interpolates linearly between the lattice data for the pure gluonic matter and the lattice data for the chemically equilibrated quark-gluon plasma. The spectra and elliptic flows of thermal dileptons and photons are calculated for central Pb+Pb collisions at the CERN Large Hadron Collider energy of $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}} = 2.76$ TeV. We test the sensitivity of the results to the choice of equilibration times, including also the case where the complete chemical equilibrium of partons is reached already at the initial stage. It is shown that a suppression of quarks at early times leads to a significant reduction of the yield of the thermal dileptons, but only to a rather modest suppression of the $p_T$-distribution of direct photons. It is demonstrated that an enhancement of photon and dilepton elliptic flows might serve as a promising signature of the pure-glue initial state.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether a pure-glue initial state (where the early-stage partonic matter consists almost entirely of gluons, with quarks and antiquarks appearing later) can be identified through electromagnetic probes in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies. The authors show that such a scenario leads to a significant reduction in thermal dilepton yields but only a modest suppression of direct photon spectra, while an enhancement of photon and dilepton elliptic flows may serve as a promising signature, though the effect on photon spectra is largely masked by prompt photon contributions at high transverse momentum.

## Method

The authors use (2+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamics with longitudinal boost invariance to model the space-time evolution of chemically nonequilibrated quark-gluon plasma in central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV. The equation of state is constructed by linearly interpolating between lattice QCD results for pure gluonic matter (SU(3) gluodynamics) and for chemically equilibrated (2+1)-flavor QCD matter, with a time-dependent quark fugacity lambda that evolves from zero at initial time to unity at late times according to a simple exponential parametrization with a characteristic equilibration time tau_star. Thermal photon and dilepton emission rates are computed using the leading-order QCD processes, with suppression factors of lambda or lambda^2 applied to account for the undersaturation of quarks, and the results are compared for different values of tau_star including the equilibrium limit tau_star = 0.

## Key numbers

- The initial time of hydrodynamic evolution is set to tau_0 = 0.1 fm/c for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV.
- The quark chemical equilibration time parameter tau_star is varied between 0, 1, and 5 fm/c, with tau_star = 0 corresponding to instantaneous chemical equilibrium.
- The pure-glue equation of state (SU(3) gluodynamics) has a first-order phase transition at T_c = 270 MeV, while the full QCD equation of state has a crossover at T = 155 MeV.
- For tau_star = 5 fm/c, the total entropy per unit rapidity increases by about 30% during the evolution, as shown in Fig. 5.
- The typical lifetime of the deconfined phase does not exceed 10 fm/c, and the mixed-phase region of the first-order transition lasts less than 0.5 fm/c.
- Prompt photons become dominant at transverse momenta p_T > 5 GeV/c, reducing the sensitivity of the combined photon spectrum to chemical nonequilibrium.
- The photon elliptic flow v_2^gamma is enhanced in the pure-glue scenario compared to the equilibrium case, with the enhancement being more pronounced for larger impact parameters (20-40% centrality) than for more central collisions (0-20% centrality).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of chemically nonequilibrated QGP evolution, such as those by Shuryak (1993), Biró et al. (1993), and others, by incorporating a lattice-based equation of state that interpolates between pure-glue and full QCD results rather than using an ideal gas approximation. The authors build on their previous work (Vovchenko et al., 2016) on entropy generation in chemically nonequilibrated QGP and on the pure-glue initial scenario discussed by Stoecker et al. (2015, 2016). The results enable future experimental searches for signatures of the pure-glue initial state using photon and dilepton elliptic flow measurements at the LHC, particularly addressing the discrepancy between the ALICE data on direct photon elliptic flow and predictions from chemically equilibrated hydrodynamic models (e.g., Paquet et al., 2015).
