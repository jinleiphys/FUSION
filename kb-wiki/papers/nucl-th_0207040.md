# New parameterization of the trinucleon wavefunction and its application to the $π^3$He scattering length

> We present a new parameterization of the trinucleon wavefunction. As a novel feature a separable parameterization for the complete wavefunction is given. In this way any calculation that considers two body forces only is largely simplified. To demonstrate this we calculate the $π^3$He scattering length.

# New parameterization of the trinucleon wavefunction and its application to the π³He scattering length

## Key claim
The paper presents a separable parameterization of the full antisymmetrized trinucleon wavefunction (for ³He and ³H) that includes a second product term to account for correlations between pair and spectator momenta, which the authors claim is necessary for accurate reproduction of the wavefunction in partial waves where the NN pair is in a ³d₁ state. The calculation of the π³He scattering length using this parameterization yields a value of Re(a_He) = (67 ± 15) × 10⁻³ m_π⁻¹, which agrees with experimental values inferred from pionic atom level shifts within the given uncertainties, but the authors caution that a reliable theoretical error estimate requires consistent inclusion of isospin breaking effects and wavefunctions consistent with the chiral counting scheme.

## Method
The authors obtained numerical Faddeev wavefunctions for the trinucleon system using the CD Bonn and Paris nucleon-nucleon potentials, then performed antisymmetrization to project onto five partial wave channels (¹s₀S, ³s₁S, ³d₁S, ³s₁D, ³d₁D). Each channel's wavefunction Ψ^ν(p,q) was fitted as a sum of two separable products v^ν_λ(p) w^ν_λ(q), where each function is a five-term expansion of Lorentz functions with parameters constrained to ensure convergence in momentum space and regularity at the origin in coordinate space. The resulting parameterization was then used to compute the π³He scattering length in next-to-leading order chiral perturbation theory using a hybrid approach, where chiral transition operators were convoluted with the phenomenological wavefunctions.

## Key numbers
- One-body contribution to π³He scattering length: a^(1b)_He = (92 ± 15) × 10⁻³ m_π⁻¹, using πN scattering lengths a^(-) = (90.5 ± 4.2) × 10⁻³ m_π⁻¹ and a^(+) = (-2.2 ± 4.3) × 10⁻³ m_π⁻¹
- Two-body contribution: a^(2b)_He = -24 × 10⁻³ m_π⁻¹ (Paris potential) and -26 × 10⁻³ m_π⁻¹ (CD Bonn potential)
- Final theoretical result: Re(a_He^(theo)) = (67 ± 15) × 10⁻³ m_π⁻¹
- Experimental value (arithmetic mean from pionic atom data): Re(a_He^(exp)) = (47 ± 13) × 10⁻³ m_π⁻¹
- D-state probability for CD Bonn potential: exact 7.127%, one-term fit 6.995%, two-term fit 7.082%
- D-state probability for Paris potential: exact 8.428%, one-term fit 8.319%, two-term fit 8.386%
- Relative weight of the five considered channels in fully converged antisymmetrization: 0.954 (CD Bonn) and 0.945 (Paris)
- Omission of the second term in the wavefunction expansion changes the two-body contribution by only 2% for threshold π scattering

## Context
This work extends the earlier separable parameterization of trinucleon Faddeev amplitudes by Hajduk et al. (1983) by providing fits for modern NN potentials (CD Bonn and Paris), parameterizing the full antisymmetrized wavefunction rather than individual Faddeev components, and including a second separable term to account for correlations. The calculation of the π³He scattering length follows the same hybrid chiral perturbation theory approach used by Beane et al. (1995) and Baru et al. (2002) for πd scattering, and the authors argue that combined analysis of πd, π³He, and πt scattering could provide direct access to the πN scattering lengths while testing chiral perturbation theory in few-body systems. The parameterization enables simplified calculations of two-body operators on three-nucleon systems, with applications already reported for pion absorption on ³He.
