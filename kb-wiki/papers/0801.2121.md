# On the time-modulation of the K-shell electron capture decay of H-like 140Pr58+ ions produced by neutrino-flavour mixing

> According to recent experimental data at GSI, the rate of the number of daughter ions 140Ce58+, produced by the nuclear K-shell electron capture (EC) decay of the H-like ion 140Pr58+, is modulated in time with a period T_d = 7.06(8) sec and an amplitude a_(EC) = 0.20(2). We show that this phenomenon can be explained by neutrino-flavour mixing and derive a value for the difference of squared masses Delta m^2_(21) = m^2_2 - m^2_1 = 2.22(3)x10^(-4) eV^2.

## Key claim
The observed time-modulation of the K-shell electron capture decay rate of H-like 140Pr58+ ions at GSI can be explained by neutrino-flavour mixing and quantum beats due to neutrino mass differences, yielding a derived squared-mass difference Δm²₂₁ = 2.22(3)×10⁻⁴ eV² that is 2.75 times larger than the KamLAND value of 0.80⁺⁰·⁰⁶₋₀·₀₅×10⁻⁴ eV².

## Method
The authors model the electron capture decay amplitude as a coherent sum over neutrino mass-eigenstates, using the standard weak interaction Hamiltonian with neutrino-flavour mixing. They treat massive neutrinos as Gaussian wave packets with a spatial spread δ, allow non-conservation of neutrino 3-momenta, and compute the time-dependent decay rate from the interference term of the amplitude squared. The resulting rate contains two periodic terms with frequencies ω₂₁ = Δm²₂₁/2M_m and Ω₂₁ = Δm²₂₁/2Q_H, where M_m is the mother ion mass and Q_H is the Q-value; the faster Ω₂₁ term is averaged out by the experimental time resolution.

## Key numbers
- Period of time-modulation for 140Pr58+ EC decay: T_EC = 7.06(8) s (experimental).
- Amplitude of time-modulation: a_EC = 0.18(3) (experimental; abstract states 0.20(2), full text states 0.18(3)).
- Derived squared neutrino mass difference: Δm²₂₁ = 2.22(3)×10⁻⁴ eV².
- Q-value for 140Pr58+ EC decay: Q_H = 3.348(6) MeV.
- Lorentz factor of mother ions in laboratory frame: γ = 1.43.
- Period for 142Pm60+ EC decay: T_EC = 7.10(22) s (experimental).
- Experimental time resolution: ΔT = 0.32 s.
- Fast oscillation period T_g ≈ 1.8×10⁻⁴ s (not observable).
- KamLAND best-fit Δm²₂₁ = 0.80⁺⁰·⁰⁶₋₀·₀₅×10⁻⁴ eV².

## Context
This work extends the GSI experimental results (GSI, 2007) on time-modulated electron capture decays of H-like 140Pr58+ and 142Pm60+ ions, previously described within standard weak interaction theory by Ivanov et al. (2007). The authors contrast their quantum-beats interpretation with neutrino oscillation analyses (PDG, 2006) and address a critique by Gal (2007) regarding the modulation frequency. The derived Δm²₂₁ discrepancy with KamLAND data motivates further work on neutrino mass corrections from strong Coulomb fields (Ivanov et al., 2008).
