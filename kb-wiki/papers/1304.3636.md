# Triply heavy baryon mass estimated within pNRQCD

> Potential-NRQCD offers an effective-theory based approach to heavy-quark physics. While meson Q-anti_Q computations are tractable in pure alpha_s-perturbation theory, more complex many-body quark systems transcend it. A possibility inherited from nuclear physics is to employ the perturbative static potentials in a numerical diagonalization, eventually obtaining the exact lowest eigenvalue in each channel for a given-order perturbative potential. The power counting is manifest in the potential instead of the spectrum. The NNLO-potential for the 3-body problem is already available, so we have addressed triply-heavy baryons in this initial work with a computer-aided 2-parameter variational treatment.

## Key claim

The paper provides variational estimates for triply-heavy baryon masses using pNRQCD potentials up to NNLO, but the authors acknowledge that the predictions are not particularly precise (with uncertainties of 200-300 MeV) and that the largest source of error (approximately 200 MeV) comes from the treatment of the potential in the infrared regime.

## Method

The authors performed a two-parameter variational treatment of the three-body problem using Jacobi coordinates, with a Gaussian wavefunction ansatz in momentum space. They employed the Rayleigh-Ritz variational principle to obtain upper bounds on the binding energy, using the pNRQCD Hamiltonian that includes LO, NLO, and NNLO static potentials (including an intrinsic three-body force at NNLO) plus a 1/m correction. The variational parameters were optimized to find the minimum energy for each baryon channel, and the method was validated by applying it to atomic-physics systems (orthohelium, parahelium, and the dihydrogen-cation).

## Key numbers

- The intrinsic three-body force at NNLO contributes approximately 30-40 MeV to the mass of Omega_{QQQ} J^P=3/2^+ baryons for QQQ=ccc, ccb, bbc, bbb
- M_{Omega_{ccc}} = 4.9(0.25) GeV
- M_{Omega_{bcc}} = 8.15(0.3) GeV
- M_{Omega_{bbc}} = 11.4(0.3) GeV
- M_{Omega_{bbb}} = 14.7(0.3) GeV
- The variational approximation overestimates binding energies by about 25 percent, corresponding to approximately 40 MeV in the ccc system
- Changing from NLO to NNLO potentials contributes approximately 100 MeV to the error budget
- Input parameters alpha_s, m_c, and m_b contribute approximately 50 MeV to the error budget

## Context

This work extends the pNRQCD approach from meson systems (Q-anti-Q) to triply-heavy baryons, building on prior variational treatments by Jia (2011) who used only the LO Coulomb potential. The authors note that their results are similar to other approaches including Flynn (2011) and Bjorken (1985). The work enables future studies of many-body heavy-quark systems such as tetraquarks (QQ-anti-Q-anti-Q) or molecules, and suggests that coupling pNRQCD to chiral perturbation theory could address light-quark degrees of freedom, following similar work by Bicudo (1998) combining light and heavy quark systems.
