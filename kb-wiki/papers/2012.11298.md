# A formalism for the study of $K^{+}π\,Σ$ photoproduction in the $Λ^{\ast}(1405)$ region

> We present a formalism for the low-energy analysis of the $γp\rightarrow K^{+}πΣ$ photoproduction reaction in the $Λ^{\ast}(1405)$ resonance region. In particular, the constraints arising from unitarity, gauge invariance and chiral perturbation theory are discussed in some detail.

## Key claim

The paper presents a formalism for analyzing the $\gamma p\rightarrow K^{+}\pi\Sigma$ photoproduction reaction in the $\Lambda^{\ast}(1405)$ region that implements rigorous low-energy constraints from chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) while allowing flexibility in the elementary photoproduction amplitude, aiming to find a compromise between purely phenomenological parameterizations and fully microscopic models.

## Method

The author constructs a manifestly covariant decomposition of the photoproduction amplitude into 16 invariant Lorentz structures $\mathcal{M}_{1,\ldots,16}$, imposes gauge-invariance constraints, and derives leading-order BChPT tree-level results for the threshold amplitudes from Weinberg-Tomozawa, Born, and anomalous Wess-Zumino-Witten vertices. A coupled-channel unitarization scheme is then developed, where partial-wave projections $\mathcal{A}^{i}_{0+}$ and $\mathcal{A}^{i}_{1-}$ of the photoproduction amplitude are constructed to satisfy unitarity relations with the meson-baryon scattering amplitudes $f_{\ell\pm}$, enabling the description of final-state interactions in the $S=-1$ sector.

## Key numbers

- The threshold amplitude estimate: $\left|\mathcal{A}_{0+,thr}^{1}(\gamma p\rightarrow K^{+}\pi\Sigma)\right| \sim \frac{e(M_{\pi}+M_{K})}{2F_{\pi}F_{K}} \sim 10\,\mathrm{GeV}^{-1}$
- A tentative Breit-Wigner fit to $\gamma p\rightarrow K^{+}\pi^{0}\Sigma^{0}$ data at $\sqrt{s}=2\,\mathrm{GeV}$ yields: $m_{\Lambda^{\ast}}\approx 1400\,\mathrm{MeV}$, $\Gamma_{\Lambda^{\ast}}\approx 49\,\mathrm{MeV}$, and $\left|\overline{\mathcal{B}}_{\Lambda^{\ast}}(s=(2\,\mathrm{GeV})^2)\right|\approx 0.0155\,\mathrm{GeV}^{-1}$
- The threshold amplitude for $\gamma p\rightarrow K^{+}\pi^{0}\Sigma^{0}$: $\mathcal{A}_{0+,thr}^{1} = \frac{e}{4F_{\pi}F_{K}}\left(M_{\pi} + 2(D^2-F^2)(M_{\pi}+M_{K})\right) + \mathcal{O}(M^2)$
- The threshold amplitude for $\gamma p\rightarrow K^{+}\pi^{+}\Sigma^{-}$: $\mathcal{A}_{0+,thr}^{1} = \frac{2e(M_{\pi}+M_{K})}{3F_{\pi}F_{K}}\left(D^2-3F^2\right) + \mathcal{O}(M^2)$
- The threshold amplitude for $\gamma p\rightarrow K^{+}\pi^{-}\Sigma^{+}$: $\mathcal{A}_{0+,thr}^{1} = \frac{e(M_{\pi}+M_{K})}{2F_{\pi}F_{K}}\left(1 + 2F(D-F)+\frac{2}{3}D(D+3F)\right) + \mathcal{O}(M^2)$

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel approach to meson-baryon scattering developed by Oller (2000), Jido (2003), and others, which revealed two poles corresponding to the $\Lambda^{\ast}(1405)$ resonance, by incorporating photoproduction constraints. It provides a framework to analyze CLAS data from Moriya (2013) on $\pi\Sigma$ line shapes and $K^{+}$ angular distributions, enabling tests of different scattering models that currently show considerable model dependence for the broader, singlet-related pole position. The formalism enables future fits that can rule out classes of models inconsistent with experimental data, improving upon the approach of Roca (2013) and Mai (2014) where the elementary amplitude was parameterized by bin-dependent constants without microscopic constraints.
