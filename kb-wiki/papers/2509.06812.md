# Ab initio calculations of beta-decay half-lives for $N=50$ neutron-rich nuclei

> Beta-decay rates of extreme neutron-rich nuclei remain largely unknown experimentally, while they are critical inputs for $r$-process nucleosynthesis. We present first ab initio calculations of total beta-decay half-lives, with a focus on $N=50$ nuclei. Starting from nuclear forces and currents based on chiral effective field theory, we use the in-medium similarity renormalization group to consistently derive valence-space Hamiltonians and weak operators, from which we calculate the nuclear states involved and the Gamow-Teller transition strengths, without phenomenological adjustments. In addition, we explore effects of first-forbidden contributions. Our results show that the inclusion of two-body currents increases the total half-lives, which then show good agreement with the existing experimental data, thereby validating the predictive capability of our approach.

## Key claim
The paper presents the first *ab initio* calculations of total beta-decay half-lives for neutron-rich N=50 nuclei, showing that including two-body currents from chiral effective field theory increases the half-lives and yields good agreement with experiment without phenomenological adjustments. The authors note that the similarity of results at Z=24 and 32 between two different interactions "may be accidental" and that assessing Hamiltonian uncertainty will require significantly more work.

## Method
The authors used the valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) starting from chiral nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions (1.8/2.0 EM and DeltaN2LO_GO 394). They consistently evolved both the Hamiltonian and the weak operators (including one-body and two-body axial-vector currents) into a valence space consisting of proton orbitals {0f7/2, 0f5/2, 1p3/2, 1p1/2} and neutron orbitals {0f5/2, 1p3/2, 1p1/2, 0g9/2, 1d5/2} on top of a Ca-48 core, then diagonalized the valence-space Hamiltonian and computed Gamow-Teller transition strengths using the Lanczos strength-function method.

## Key numbers
- Total beta-decay half-life of Ni-78: the VS-IMSRG result with two-body currents shows "very nice agreement" with the experimental value from Xu (2014) and NNDC; the inclusion of two-body currents increases the half-life in all cases
- Ground-state energy of Ni-78: the 1.8/2.0 (EM) interaction overestimates the experimental value by about 1% for the worst case, with an estimated uncertainty of 2.3 MeV from model-space convergence
- For the 1.8/2.0 (EM) interaction, the ground-state energy uncertainty for Cu-78 is estimated at 2.2 MeV
- The VS-IMSRG(2) truncation is expected to contribute 1%-2% to the correlation energy, corresponding to 2-4 MeV for the Ni-78 ground-state energy with the 1.8/2.0 (EM) interaction and 3-6 MeV with the DeltaN2LO_GO (394) interaction
- The integral constant kappa used in the half-life formula is 6144.48 plus or minus 3.7 seconds, from Hardy (2020)

## Context
This work extends the *ab initio* explanation of the g_A quenching puzzle in Gamow-Teller transitions by Gysbers (2019) to total beta-decay half-lives involving many states, which had not been done previously. It contrasts with phenomenological shell-model calculations that require *ad hoc* quenching of g_A (e.g., Zhi 2013) and with quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) approaches that underestimate many-body correlations (e.g., Marketin 2016). The results provide validated predictions for half-lives at Z=24-26 that can serve as input for r-process nucleosynthesis simulations and guide future beta-decay experiments at neutron-rich extremes.
