# Fusion of light nuclei in a multicluster realization of the three-body problem

> This work describes a few-body dynamics method based on the Faddeev integral equations in momentum space for determining the total cross sections of fusion and breakup reactions with two- and three-body final channels in the continuum, employing a cluster representation of the colliding nuclei. Total cross sections were obtained for the reactions $^3\text{He}(T,D)^4\text{He}$, $^3\text{He}(T,np)^4\text{He}$, $^3\text{He}(T,nD)^3\text{He}$, $^3\text{He}(^3\text{He},2p)^4\text{He}$, $^3\text{He}(^3\text{He},pD)^3\text{He}$, $^7\text{Li}(^3\text{He},\phantom{0}^4\text{He})^6\text{Li}$, $^7\text{Li}(^3\text{He},D^4\text{He})^4\text{He}$, and $^7\text{Li}(^3\text{He},T^3\text{He})^4\text{He}$, in which both the projectile and the target nucleus were treated in a cluster representation. The work also implements a two-potential method to determine the Coulomb $t-$matrix and to account for Coulomb effects in short-range dynamics in momentum space. Calculations of the initial-state Coulomb interaction were performed; furthermore, an estimate was obtained for the magnitude of the off-shell effect of the Coulomb $t-$matrix, as well as the magnitude of the atomic electron anti-screening effect on the Coulomb interaction of the colliding nuclei. The calculated contribution of the cluster mechanism to the total cross section of the considered fusion reactions in the kinetic energy range $T\in[1~\text{keV},20~\text{MeV}]$ is in good agreement with known experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a multicluster Faddeev integral equation approach in momentum space, which treats both projectile and target nuclei in a cluster representation, can yield total cross sections for fusion and breakup reactions of light nuclei that agree with known experimental data across the kinetic energy range 1 keV to 20 MeV, provided that Coulomb effects (including off-shell Coulomb t-matrix contributions and atomic electron anti-screening) are properly incorporated.

## Method

The author solves a system of coupled Faddeev integral equations in three-dimensional momentum space for three-body systems, where the colliding nuclei (e.g., ³He, T, ⁷Li) are represented as bound clusters (e.g., n-D, p-D, α-t, α-d). Two-body scattering t-matrices are constructed using local potentials for fusion channels and separable potentials fitted to known partial-wave phase shifts for elastic channels, with the Bonn NN potential used for n-D scattering. The Coulomb interaction is treated via a two-potential method to compute the Coulomb t-matrix and its off-shell effects, and the atomic electron anti-screening effect is estimated. The system of equations is solved iteratively up to second order, and total cross sections for two-body rearrangement and three-body breakup channels are obtained from the resulting amplitudes.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous Faddeev equation studies of neutron- and deuteron-induced reactions on lithium nuclei (Egorov, prior work) to fusion reactions involving ³He projectiles on triton and ³He targets, as well as ⁷Li(³He,X) reactions. It contrasts with the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) method, which faces challenges in describing rearrangement and three-body breakup channels, and with the Resonating Group Method (RGM) and Gaussian basis expansion methods (Hiyama, Ogawa) that require careful treatment of asymptotic boundary conditions. The approach enables a unified description of elastic scattering, rearrangement, and three-body breakup within a single framework, and provides theoretical cross sections for reactions such as ⁷Li(³He,⁴He)⁶Li where experimental data below 20 MeV are very scarce (Forsyth).
