# Pseudo-rapidity distribution from a perturbative solution of viscous hydrodynamics for heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC

> The charged-particle's final state spectrum is derived from an analytic perturbative solution for the relativistic viscous hydrodynamics. By taking into account the longitudinal acceleration effect in relativistic viscous hydrodynamics, the pseudorapidity spectrum describes well the nucleus-nucleus colliding systems at RHIC and LHC. Based on both the extracted longitudinal acceleration parameters $λ^{*}$ and a phenomenological description of the $λ^{*}$, the charged-particle's pseudorapidity distributions for $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.44 TeV Xe+Xe collisions are computed from the final state expression in a limited space-time rapidity $η_{s}$ region.

# Pseudo-rapidity distribution from a perturbative solution of viscous hydrodynamics for heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC

## Key claim
The pseudorapidity distribution derived from a first-order perturbative solution of relativistic viscous hydrodynamics, including longitudinal acceleration, describes experimental data well for central nucleus-nucleus collisions from RHIC to LHC energies, but only under the condition that the acceleration parameter λ* is small (0 < λ* << 1) and the space-time rapidity integration is limited to |η_s| ≤ 5.0 to maintain perturbative validity.

## Method
The authors solve the relativistic viscous hydrodynamic equations in Rindler coordinates using a perturbative expansion to leading order O(λ*) in the longitudinal acceleration parameter, where the flow rapidity is parameterized as Ω = (1+λ*)η_s. The temperature profile is obtained analytically, and the final-state charged-particle pseudorapidity distribution is computed via the Cooper-Frye freeze-out prescription including first-order viscous corrections (Navier-Stokes limit) with constant shear viscosity η/s = 0.16 and bulk viscosity ζ/s = 0.015. The freeze-out temperature is set to T_f = 140 MeV, and the freeze-out proper time is τ_f = 8 fm.

## Key numbers
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: η/s = 0.16 (fixed from Song (2010))
- Bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio: ζ/s = 0.015 (fixed from Meyer (2007))
- Freeze-out temperature: T_f = 140 MeV
- Freeze-out proper time: τ_f = 8 fm
- Average charged-particle mass: m = 220 ± 20 MeV (weighted average of π±, K±, p±)
- Equation of state parameter: κ ≈ 7 at T ≈ 140 MeV (from Wuppertal-Budapest lattice QCD)
- Extracted longitudinal acceleration parameters λ* for central collisions: 130 GeV Au+Au: 0.076 ± 0.003; 200 GeV Au+Au: 0.062 ± 0.002; 200 GeV Cu+Cu: 0.060 ± 0.003; 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb: 0.035 ± 0.003; 5.02 TeV Pb+Pb: 0.032 ± 0.002
- Predicted λ* for 5.44 TeV Xe+Xe: 0.030 ± 0.003
- Central multiplicity for 5.44 TeV Xe+Xe (0-5% centrality): dN/dη|_(η=η0) = 1167 ± 26.0 (from ALICE data)
- Phenomenological fit parameters for λ*(√s_NN): A = 0.045, B = 0.23 (with √s_0 = 1 GeV)

## Context
This work extends the exact accelerating perfect-fluid solutions of Csorgo (2006) and Jiang (2017) by including first-order viscous corrections in the Navier-Stokes limit, building on the perturbative solution framework of Jiang (2018). The model successfully describes pseudorapidity distributions from PHOBOS (2010) and ALICE (2016) data for systems ranging from 130 GeV Au+Au to 5.02 TeV Pb+Pb, and provides predictions for the 5.44 TeV Xe+Xe collisions measured by ALICE (2018). The extracted λ* values show a systematic decrease with increasing collision energy, following a power-law scaling that enables predictions for other systems. The analytical spectra can serve as test tools for numerical hydrodynamic codes such as those of Pang (2018).
