# Pileup Correction on Higher-order Cumulants with Unfolding Approach

> Higher-order cumulants of conserved charge distributions are sensitive observables to probe the critical fluctuations near QCD critical point in heavy-ion collisions. Due to high interaction rate, pileup event can be one of the major sources of background in the measurements of higher-order cumulants. In this paper, we studied the effects of pileup events on higher-order cumulants of proton multiplicity distributions using UrQMD model. It is found that the proposed pileup correction fails if the correction parameters are determined by the Glauber fitting of charged particle multiplicities, which is usually done in the real heavy-ion experiment. To address this, we propose a model independent unfolding approach to determine the parameters in the pileup correction. This approach can be applied in the pileup correction for the future measurement of higher-order cumulants in heavy-ion collision experiment.

## Key claim

The proposed pileup correction for higher-order cumulants works well in a closure test where the true multiplicity distribution is known, but fails when the correction parameters are determined by a Glauber fit to the measured charged-particle multiplicity distribution, as would be done in real experiments.

## Method

The authors generated approximately 80 million UrQMD model events for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 3 GeV and created pileup events by superposing two single-collision events with a probability alpha ranging from 0.1% to 10%. They applied the pileup correction formula from Nonaka (2020) using two methods to determine the correction parameters: a closure test using the true UrQMD multiplicity distribution, and a Glauber model fit to the pileup-UrQMD multiplicity distribution. They then developed a model-independent unfolding approach that iteratively adjusts a toy Monte Carlo multiplicity distribution to match the measured distribution, using response matrices computed during the pileup filter process.

## Key numbers

- 80 million events generated with impact parameter from 0 fm to 15 fm for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 3 GeV
- Pileup probability alpha studied in range 0.1% to 10%
- Glauber fit parameters: mu = 0.304, k = 3.6, x = 0.11
- The best-fit pileup probability from Glauber fitting was alpha = 0.1%, which underestimated the true value of 0.5%
- The pileup correction using Glauber fit failed for alpha > 1.0%
- After 100 iterations of the unfolding approach, the correction functions became flat, indicating successful reconstruction of the true multiplicity distribution
- The fourth-order cumulant ratio C4/C2 changed sign from negative to positive and went above unity with increasing pileup probability

## Context

This work extends the pileup correction method for higher-order cumulants developed by Nonaka (2020), which was previously tested only in a simple toy model with pre-defined correction parameters. The study demonstrates that the Glauber model fitting approach commonly used for centrality determination in heavy-ion experiments (as done by the STAR collaboration) cannot perfectly describe the multiplicity distribution from UrQMD, leading to insufficient pileup corrections. The proposed unfolding approach, inspired by the efficiency correction method of Esumi (2020), provides a model-independent way to determine correction parameters that can be applied to future measurements of higher-order cumulants in heavy-ion collision experiments, particularly for the STAR Beam Energy Scan program.
