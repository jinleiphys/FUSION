# Ground state correlations and mean field using the exp(S) method

> This document gives a detailed account of the terms used in the computation of the ground state mean field and the ground state correlations. While the general approach to this description is given in a separate paper (nucl-th/9802029) we give here the explicite expressions used.

## Key claim

This paper provides the explicit algebraic expressions for all terms used in computing the ground state mean field and two-body ground state correlations within the exp(S) (coupled-cluster) method, but the authors note that while terms (I) through (IV) are essentially complete, from the higher-order terms they have "selectively included only those terms that we expected to be significant."

## Method

The authors use the exp(S) method (coupled-cluster formalism) to compute ground state correlations and the mean field in nuclear many-body systems. They evaluate expectation values of operator products in the bare ground state, generating terms via a 1/epsilon expansion to correct for 3p3h and 4p4h correlations. The calculations are performed in angular momentum coupling, requiring recoupling between particle-hole (ph) coupling and particle-particle (pp) coupling, with explicit expressions given for each term contributing to the effective two-body matrix element and the effective single-particle energy.

## Key numbers

- The omega-dependent single-particle energies are computed for a set of 9 values of omega in the range from 0 to 1 GeV, with interpolation for intermediate values.
- The density matrix d(p1,p3,omega) is defined with a factor of -1/2 and denominators of the form (epsilon_{p2h2} + epsilon_{p1h4} + omega).
- The Z amplitude (2p2h amplitude) is computed as Z = -V^{tot} / [epsilon_{p1h1} + epsilon_{p2h2} + Delta epsilon_{p1h1}(omega=epsilon_{p2h2}) + Delta epsilon_{p2h2}(omega=epsilon_{p1h1})].
- The effective ph-hp matrix element has eight labeled contributions (V1 through V8), with V5 being the sum of all contributions from 3p3h correlations discussed in a separate subsection.

## Context

This paper serves as a detailed appendix to the companion paper (Heisenberg and Mihaila, nucl-th/9802029), which describes the general approach to computing ground state correlations using the exp(S) method. The work extends the coupled-cluster many-body techniques (PACS codes 21.60.Gx and 2160.Jz) by providing explicit working expressions for practitioners. The document enables other researchers to implement the full set of correlation terms in nuclear structure calculations, including the treatment of omega-dependent single-particle energies and the recoupling between different angular momentum coupling schemes.
