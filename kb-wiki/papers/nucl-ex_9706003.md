# Direct neutron capture of 48Ca at kT = 52 keV

> The neutron capture cross section of 48Ca was measured relative to the known gold cross section at kT = 52 keV using the fast cyclic activation technique. The experiment was performed at the Van-de-Graaff accelerator, Universitaet Tuebingen. The new experimental result is in good agreement with a calculation using the direct capture model. The 1/v behaviour of the capture cross section at thermonuclear energies is confirmed, and the adopted reaction rate which is based on several previous experimental investigations remains unchanged.

# Direct neutron capture of 48Ca at kT = 52 keV

## Key claim

The neutron capture cross section of 48Ca measured at kT = 52 keV agrees well with a direct capture model calculation, confirming the 1/v behaviour at thermonuclear energies; however, the abstract glosses over that a previous measurement at kT = 25 keV showed a roughly 25% deviation from the calculated cross section (2.8 times the experimental uncertainty), which could be explained either by experimental uncertainty or by destructive interference with a possible s-wave resonance near 1.5 keV.

## Method

The neutron capture cross section of 48Ca was measured relative to the known gold cross section using the fast cyclic activation technique at the Van-de-Graaff accelerator at Universitaet Tuebingen. A quasi-Maxwellian neutron energy spectrum with kT = 52 keV was produced by bombarding a thick tritium target with protons at Ep = 1091 keV (72 keV above the T(p,n) threshold). Two CaCO3 samples enriched to 77.87% in 48Ca were sandwiched between thin gold foils, irradiated for 149.15 s cycles, then moved to a high-purity germanium detector for counting of gamma rays from the beta decay of 49Ca and 198Au.

## Key numbers

- Experimental cross section: sigma_exp(48Ca) = 623 +/- 50 microbarns at kT = 52 keV
- Calculated direct capture cross section: sigma_calc(48Ca) = 655 microbarns
- Gold reference cross section: sigma(197Au) = 431 +/- 15 mb at kT = 52 keV
- Total experimental uncertainty: approximately 8%, from quadratic sum of statistical (1.5%), neutron energy variations (<5%), enrichment (2.4%), gamma branching ratios (1% for 3084.4 keV line, 10% for 4071.9 keV line), detector efficiency (3%), neutron flux monitoring (1%), neutron beam divergence corrections (2%), gold cross section (3.4%), and unknown uncertainties (2%)
- Reaction rate factor: NA <sigma v> = 1.19 x 10^5 cm^3 mole^-1 s^-1, unchanged from previous work
- Sample masses: m1 = 48.83 mg and m2 = 49.06 mg, diameter 6 mm
- Half-life of 49Ca: 8.716 +/- 0.011 minutes
- Half-life of 198Au: 2.69 days

## Context

This work extends previous measurements of the 48Ca(n,gamma)49Ca cross section by Beer et al. (1996) and Kaeppeler et al. (1985) using activation techniques, and addresses a discrepancy at kT = 25 keV where the experimental value was roughly 25% smaller than the calculated direct capture cross section. The new measurement at kT = 52 keV confirms the 1/v behaviour and leaves the adopted reaction rate unchanged, while the possible existence of a 1/2+ s-wave resonance near 1.5 keV suggested by Beer et al. (1996) remains an open question for future time-of-flight experiments such as those by Carlton et al. (1987).
