# Triton binding energy with realistic precision

> We compute the binding energy of triton with realistic statistical errors stemming from NN scattering data uncertainties and the deuteron and obtain $E_t=-7.638(15) \, {\rm MeV}$. Setting the numerical precision as $ΔE_t^{\rm num} \lesssim 1 \, {\rm keV}$ we obtain the statistical error $ΔE_t^{\rm stat}= 15(1) \, {\rm keV}$ which is mainly determined by the channels involving relative S-waves. This figure reflects the uncertainty of the input NN data, more than two orders of magnitude larger than the experimental precision $ΔE_t^{\rm exp}= 0.1 \, {\rm keV}$ and provides a bottleneck in the realistic precision that can be reached. This suggests an important reduction in the numerical precision and hence in the computational effort.

## Key claim

The triton binding energy computed from NN scattering data carries a statistical error of 15(1) keV, which is more than two orders of magnitude larger than the experimental precision of 0.1 keV, meaning that the input NN data uncertainty, not numerical precision, sets the fundamental limit on realistic theoretical accuracy.

## Method

The authors performed a least-squares fit of a smooth local NN potential (with charge-dependent one-pion exchange and a short-range Gaussian expansion in the extended AV18 operator basis) to 6713 np and pp scattering data, verifying normality of residuals. They then generated 205 Monte Carlo samples from the multivariate error matrix of the fitted parameters, solved the three-body problem for each sample using the Hyperspherical Adiabatic Expansion Method with up to 2000 hyperspherical harmonics, and extracted the statistical distribution of the triton binding energy.

## Key numbers

- Triton binding energy: \(E_t = -7.638(15) \, \mathrm{MeV}\) (statistical error 15 keV).
- Statistical error of the binding energy: \(\Delta E_t^{\mathrm{stat}} = 15(1) \, \mathrm{keV}\).
- Experimental triton binding energy: \(E_t^{\mathrm{exp}} = -8.4820(1) \, \mathrm{MeV}\) (experimental precision 0.1 keV).
- Numerical precision of the calculation: \(\Delta E_t^{\mathrm{num}} \lesssim 1 \, \mathrm{keV}\).
- For the reduced Hilbert space of Ss+Sd+Ds channels: \(\Delta B_t^{\mathrm{stat}}(Ss+Sd+Ds) = 20 \, \mathrm{keV}\).
- For Ss+Sd+Ds+Pp channels: \(\Delta B_t^{\mathrm{stat}}(Ss+Sd+Ds+Pp) = 19 \, \mathrm{keV}\).
- Number of NN scattering data used: \(N = 6713\).
- Number of Monte Carlo samples: \(M = 205\).
- \(\chi^2/\nu = 1.06\) for the fitted potential.

## Context

This work extends the pioneering error analysis of Adam (1993), who estimated \(\Delta E_t^{\mathrm{th}} > 40 \, \mathrm{keV}\) from the \(^1S_0\) channel alone using the Paris potential, by providing a complete statistical propagation from all NN channels with verified normality of residuals. The result quantifies the irreducible statistical floor from NN data, showing that further numerical refinement beyond 1 keV is unnecessary; it also enables benchmarking of systematic errors from off-shell ambiguities, which previous work (e.g., Friar 1993, Kievsky 1997) had estimated at 340 keV across different potential forms.
