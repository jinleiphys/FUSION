# UrQMD Study of the Effects of Centrality Definitions on Higher Moments of Net Protons at RHIC

> A study using UrQMD is presented concerning the higher moments of net protons from Au+Au collisions at 7.7, 11.5, 14.6, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV, concentrating on $C_{4}/C_{2} = κσ^{2}$. Higher moments of net protons are predicted to be a sensitive probe of the critical point of QCD. At the QCD critical point, particular ratios of the moments of net protons are predicted to differ from the Poisson baseline. Recently STAR has published the higher moments of net protons for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV. UrQMD quantitatively reproduces STAR's measured $C_{4}/C_{2} = κσ^{2}$ for net protons for all Au+Au collisions more central than 30\% and at all centralities for $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 7.7 and 11.5 GeV. The effects are investigated of three different centrality definitions on the values of $C_{4}/C_{2} = κσ^{2}$ from UrQMD calculations including the impact parameter given by UrQMD. It is shown that using a centrality definition based on multiplicity to calculate the higher moments of net protons gives a biased answer for $C_{4}/C_{2} = κσ^{2}$, except in the most central bin (0-5\%).

## Key claim

UrQMD quantitatively reproduces STAR's measured \(C_{4}/C_{2} = \kappa\sigma^{2}\) for net protons only for Au+Au collisions more central than 30% at all incident energies, and at all centralities only for the two lowest energies (7.7 and 11.5 GeV); the agreement fails for peripheral collisions at higher energies.

## Method

The study uses UrQMD version 3.3 with default parameters to simulate Au+Au collisions at eight beam energies (7.7, 11.5, 14.6, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV). The higher moments of net protons (protons minus antiprotons) are calculated within the STAR acceptance (\(|y| < 0.5\), \(0.4 < p_T < 0.8\) GeV/\(c\)) using the same techniques as the STAR collaboration. Three centrality definitions are compared: the impact parameter \(b\) from UrQMD, the STAR refMult3 definition (charged particles with \(|\eta| < 1\) excluding protons and antiprotons), and the refMult2 definition (charged particles with \(0.5 < |\eta| < 1.0\)).

## Key numbers

- At \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 7.7\) GeV, \(\chi^2 = 13.4\) for 9 degrees of freedom when comparing UrQMD to STAR data across all centralities.
- At \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 11.5\) GeV, \(\chi^2 = 11.9\) for 9 degrees of freedom when comparing UrQMD to STAR data across all centralities.
- For centralities 0-5%, 5-10%, 10-20%, and 20-30% across all energies, \(\chi^2 = 13.5, 3.1, 7.9, 13.1\) respectively for 7 degrees of freedom.
- For higher energies (above 11.5 GeV), \(\chi^2 > 250\) for 9 degrees of freedom when comparing UrQMD to STAR data across all centralities.
- For peripheral centralities (beyond 30%), \(\chi^2 > 80\) for 7 degrees of freedom.

## Context

This work extends the STAR measurement of net proton higher moments (STAR, 2014) by using the UrQMD transport model to test whether deviations from the Poisson baseline could arise from known hadronic physics rather than the QCD critical point. The study directly addresses the concern raised by STAR's sampled-singles model that correlations and fluctuations from centrality selection could bias the observable. The finding that multiplicity-based centrality definitions (refMult3) systematically lower \(C_{4}/C_{2}\) except in the most central bin (0-5%) enables future experimental analyses to correct for this bias, and suggests that only the most central collisions should be compared to critical-point predictions without additional centrality-related corrections.
