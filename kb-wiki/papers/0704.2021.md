# Mass Shift and Width Broadening of J/psi in hot gluonic plasma from QCD Sum Rules

> We investigate possible mass shift and width broadening of J/psi in hot gluonic matter using QCD sum rule. Input values of gluon condensates at finite temperature are extracted from lattice QCD data for the energy density and pressure. Although stability of the moment ratio is achieved only up to T/Tc ~ 1.05, the gluon condensates cause a decrease of the moment ratio, which results in change of spectral properties. Using the Breit-Wigner form for the phenomenological side, we find that mass shift of J/psi just above Tc can reach maximally 200 MeV and width can broaden to dozens of MeV.

## Key claim

The QCD sum rule analysis, using gluon condensates extracted from quenched lattice QCD data, shows that the mass of J/psi can decrease by up to 200 MeV and its width can broaden to dozens of MeV just above the critical temperature, but only up to T/Tc ~ 1.05 where the moment ratio remains stable; beyond that temperature the OPE truncation at dimension four breaks down.

## Method

The authors used QCD sum rules with the operator product expansion (OPE) up to dimension four, including both scalar and twist-2 gluon condensates. The temperature-dependent gluon condensates were extracted from quenched lattice QCD data for energy density and pressure, using the trace anomaly relation for the scalar condensate and the energy-momentum tensor for the twist-2 condensate. The phenomenological side was modeled with a Breit-Wigner spectral function, and the sum rule was solved by matching the moment ratio r_n = M_{n-1}/M_n from the OPE side to that from the phenomenological side, using Vegas Monte-Carlo integration for the dispersion integral.

## Key numbers

* Maximum mass shift of J/psi at T = 1.05 Tc: delta m ~ 200 MeV (in the Gamma -> 0 limit)
* Maximum width broadening at T = 1.05 Tc: Gamma ~ 140 MeV (in the delta m -> 0 limit)
* Vacuum gluon condensate value used: G_0^vac = (0.35 GeV)^4
* Running coupling at Tc from short-distance force: alpha_qq(Tc) = 0.626
* Running coupling at Tc from screening part: tilde_alpha(Tc) = 0.47
* Charm quark mass: m_c = 1.24 GeV
* Strong coupling at scale 8 m_c^2: alpha_s(8 m_c^2) = 0.21

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach for J/psi in vacuum by Reinders (1981) and in nuclear matter by Klingl (1999) and Hayashigaki (1999) to finite temperature in the deconfined phase, using for the first time gluon condensates systematically extracted from quenched lattice QCD data by Boyd (1996) and Kaczmarek (2004). The results are consistent with potential model calculations by Mocsy (2007) and full lattice QCD by Aarts (2007) that show a shift of the J/psi peak, and the predicted mass shift could increase J/psi yield in statistical hadronization by a factor of 2 at T = 170 MeV. The analysis is limited to T/Tc ~ 1.05 due to OPE convergence issues, and future work should include higher-dimensional operators and a temperature-dependent continuum contribution.
