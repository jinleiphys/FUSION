# Hidden and open heavy-flavor hadronic states

> We discuss the stability of hidden and open heavy-flavor hadronic states made of either two or three mesons. References are made in passing to studies regarding two and three-body systems containing baryons. We perform a comparative study analyzing the results in terms of quark and hadron degrees of freedom. Compact and molecular states are found to exist in very specific situations. We estimate the decay width for the different scenarios: weak decays for bound states by the strong interaction, and strong decays for hadronic resonances above a decay threshold. The experimental observation of narrow hadrons lying well above their lowest decay threshold is theoretically justified.

## Key claim

The paper argues that hidden and open heavy-flavor hadronic states (made of two or three mesons) can exist as either compact multiquark states or molecular states, but only under very specific conditions related to the mass ratio of heavy to light quarks and the dynamics of coupled-channel thresholds. The authors hedge that while narrow hadrons lying well above their lowest decay threshold are theoretically justified, the stability of such states depends critically on the interplay between chromomagnetic interactions, threshold mass differences, and the non-Abelian nature of color algebra.

## Method

The authors performed a comparative study using the AL1 constituent quark model (Semay and Silvestre-Brac, 1994), which includes a Coulomb-plus-linear central potential with a smeared chromomagnetic interaction, to compute ground-state properties of four-quark (tetraquark) systems. For two-meson states, they solved the Schrödinger equation for $QQ\bar q\bar q$ and $Qq\bar Q\bar q$ configurations, analyzing binding energies, color wave function probabilities, and meson-meson component probabilities. For three-meson states, they solved Faddeev equations exactly for the $BB^*B^*-B^*B^*B^*$ system using two-body $t$-matrices derived from the same constituent model. Decay widths for bound tetraquarks were calculated within the factorization approximation for semileptonic and nonleptonic modes.

## Key numbers

- For $QQ\bar q\bar q$ ground state (isoscalar $J^P=1^+$): binding energy increases from 13 MeV for $M_Q=1836$ MeV (charm) to 151 MeV for $M_Q=5227$ MeV (bottom), with the $bb\bar u\bar d$ state bound by 151 MeV below its lowest strong-decay threshold of 10644 MeV
- The $bc\bar u\bar d$ $J^P=1^+$ state has a binding energy of 23 MeV, larger than the 13 MeV binding of the $cc\bar u\bar d$ state despite the heavier bottom quark suppressing chromomagnetic interactions
- For the $T_{bbb}$ three-body system $(I)J^P=(1/2)2^-$: binding energy is 90 MeV below the lowest strong decay threshold when the $T_{bb}$ binding is 180 MeV; the $T_{bbb}$ remains stable for $T_{bb}$ binding energies ranging from 87 to 180 MeV
- The largest partial decay widths for the $T_{bb}$ tetraquark are of order $10^{-15}$ to $10^{-14}$ GeV (approximately $10^9$ to $10^{10}$ s$^{-1}$) for semileptonic decays with $b\to c$ vertices
- The mass difference between pseudoscalar-vector two-meson components is 57 MeV in the bottom sector and 154 MeV in the charm sector

## Context

This work extends the pioneering study of Adecker et al. (1982) on $QQ\bar q\bar q$ stability and the Weinstein and Isgur (1990) molecular picture for scalar mesons, while providing a unified framework connecting quark-level and hadronic-level descriptions via the formalism of Harvey (1981) and Vijande et al. (2009). The results contradict the widespread belief that increasing heavy quark mass always favors multiquark stability, as demonstrated by the $Qq\bar Q\bar q$ case where the lower $(Q\bar Q)-(q\bar q)$ threshold destroys binding for bottom quarks. The paper enables future searches for the $T_{bbb}$ three-meson bound state and provides theoretical justification for narrow hadrons above decay thresholds, relevant to understanding the $X(3872)$ and LHCb pentaquarks.
