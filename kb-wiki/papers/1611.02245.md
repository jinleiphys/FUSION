# Applications of KP Nuclear Parton Distributions

> We review the nuclear parton distribution functions computed on the basis of our microscopic model taking into account a number of nuclear effects including Fermi motion and nuclear binding, nuclear meson-exchange currents, off-shell corrections to bound nucleon distributions and nuclear shadowing. We discuss applications to a number of different processes including lepton-nucleus deep inelastic scattering, proton-nucleus Drell-Yan lepton pair production at Fermilab, as well as $W^\pm$ and $Z^0$ boson production in proton-lead collisions at the LHC.

# Applications of KP Nuclear Parton Distributions

## Key claim
The KP nuclear parton distribution model, based on a microscopic treatment of nuclear effects including Fermi motion, binding, meson-exchange currents, off-shell corrections, and shadowing, can simultaneously describe deep-inelastic scattering, Drell-Yan, and W/Z boson production data; however, the model's success depends on treating the off-shell correction function δf as universal and on including phenomenological energy-loss corrections for the projectile quark in Drell-Yan processes.

## Method
The nuclear PDFs are constructed by convolving bound nucleon spectral functions with nucleon PDFs that include an off-shell correction linear in the nucleon virtuality (p²-M²)/M², then adding separate contributions from meson-exchange currents (MEC) and from coherent Glauber-Gribov multiple scattering of the virtual boson's hadronic component. The model is applied to compute cross-section ratios for lepton-nucleus DIS, proton-nucleus Drell-Yan pair production at Fermilab, and W⁺, W⁻, Z⁰ boson production in p+Pb collisions at the LHC, with comparisons to experimental data from JLab, HERMES, E772, E866, CMS, and ATLAS.

## Key numbers
- DIS cross-section ratios for ¹²C, ⁹Be, ⁴He, and ³He relative to deuterium were compared with E03-103 data at JLab (points with x>0.85 have W²<2 GeV²) and HERMES data; a common normalization factor of 0.98 (1.03 for ³He) was applied to E03-103 data for consistency with SLAC and NMC data.
- Drell-Yan cross-section ratios for ¹²C, ⁴⁰Ca, ⁵⁶Fe, and ¹⁸⁴W relative to deuterium were compared with E772 data as a function of the target Bjorken variable x_T; the data show no significant antishadowing enhancement at x_T ~ 0.1.
- Energy loss effects in Drell-Yan were found consistent with a rate of the order of 1 GeV/fm.
- W⁺, W⁻, and Z⁰ production cross-sections in p+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV were compared with CMS measurements; the KP model predictions (ABMP15+KP) show excellent agreement with data across the entire kinematic range.

## Context
This work extends the microscopic nuclear PDF approach of Kulagin and Petti (2004, 2014) by applying it to new processes beyond DIS, including Drell-Yan data from E772 and E866 and LHC W/Z boson production. The model contradicts the expectation from Bickerstaff (1985) that nuclear binding should produce a marked enhancement in nuclear antiquark distributions at x_T ~ 0.1; instead, the KP model predicts a cancellation of different nuclear effects that matches the observed flat DY ratios. The results enable future LHC precision measurements to disentangle the underlying mechanisms of nuclear PDF modifications and study their flavor dependence.
