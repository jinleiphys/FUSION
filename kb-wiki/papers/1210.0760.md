# Meson dominance of hadron form factors and large-Nc phenomenology

> We discuss the pion and nucleon form factors and generalized form factors within the large-Nc approach in the space-like region. We estimate their theoretical uncertainties through the use of the half-width rule, amounting to taking the half-width of the resonances as the deviation of their mass parameters. The approach embodies the meson dominance of form factors and the high-energy constraints from perturbative QCD. We compare the results with the available experimental data and lattice simulations. The meson-dominance form factors are generally comparable to the available experimental data within the half-width-rule uncertainties. Our errors are comparable to the experimental uncertainties, but are smaller than lattice errors.

## Key claim
The paper argues that meson dominance of hadron form factors, combined with the large-\(N_c\) limit, can reproduce experimental data within uncertainties estimated by the half-width rule (treating resonance masses as uncertain by \(\pm \Gamma_R/2\)). The authors claim their theoretical errors are comparable to experimental uncertainties but smaller than current lattice errors, though they acknowledge that the mass shift from \(1/N_c\) corrections cannot be computed model-independently.

## Method
The authors construct form factors as sums of monopole terms (meson poles) in the large-\(N_c\) limit, imposing high-energy constraints from perturbative QCD. They estimate \(1/N_c\) corrections by applying the half-width rule: resonance masses are treated as random variables with a Gaussian distribution of width \(\Gamma_R/2\), and the resulting uncertainty bands are propagated to the form factors. The approach is applied to the pion electromagnetic, axial, and transition form factors, as well as nucleon form factors, using a minimal number of meson states to satisfy asymptotic constraints.

## Key numbers
- Average width-to-mass ratio for mesons and baryons from PDG: \(\langle \Gamma_R/m_R \rangle = 0.12(8)\) (Arriola 2011, Masjuan 2012).
- For the \(\rho\) meson: \(m_\rho = 0.77\) GeV, \(\Gamma_\rho = 0.15\) GeV; for the \(\rho'\) meson: \(m_{\rho'} = 1.465(25)\) GeV, \(\Gamma_{\rho'} = 0.400\) GeV.
- Pion charge radius: \(\langle r^2 \rangle = (0.672(8)\ \text{fm})^2\).
- Pion weak decay constant: \(f_\pi = 92.3\) MeV; chiral-limit value used for \(\rho\) mass relation gives \(m_\rho = 823\) MeV for \(f_\pi = 92.6\) MeV or \(m_\rho = 770\) MeV for \(f_\pi = 86.6\) MeV.
- Axial form factor normalization: \(F_A(0) = 0.0119(1)\) (PDG 2012).
- For the \(\rho''\) meson: \(m_{\rho''} = 1.720(20)\) GeV; slope parameter \(a_\pi = 0.032(4)\) yields \(b = 5.82(18)\) in the three-resonance transition form factor.

## Context
This work extends the vector meson dominance (VMD) approach, historically formulated by Sakurai (1969) and O'Connell (1995), by placing it on a large-\(N_c\) footing and adding a systematic uncertainty estimate via the half-width rule. The method builds on earlier studies of resonance mass uncertainties by Masjuan (2008, 2012) and Ruiz Arriola (2010, 2011). It enables a simple, parameter-free way to estimate \(1/N_c\) corrections to meson-dominated form factors, providing a benchmark for lattice QCD calculations (which currently have larger errors) and for future experimental measurements of pion and nucleon form factors at higher momentum transfers.
