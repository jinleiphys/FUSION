# Three Quark Clusters in Hot and Dense Nuclear Matter

> We present a relativistic in-medium three-body equation to study correlations in hot and dense quark matter. The equation is solved for a zero-range force for parameters close to the phase transition of QCD.

## Key claim

The paper presents a relativistic in-medium three-body equation for studying three-quark (qqq) correlations in hot and dense quark matter, solved for a zero-range force; the authors hedge that this is a first step toward systematic inclusion of medium effects and relativity, with many details still needing improvement before drawing firm conclusions about the QCD phase diagram.

## Method

The authors derive relativistic in-medium Alt-Grassberger-Sandberger (AGS) or Faddeev equations using a light-front formalism, incorporating finite temperature and chemical potential via Dyson equations. The gap equation in Hartree-Fock approximation gives effective quark masses m(μ,T) that depend on temperature and chemical potential. The three-body equation is solved for a zero-range interaction, with Pauli blocking factors and self-energy corrections included, and the two-body propagator τ(M₂) serves as input for the three-body vertex function Γ.

- At T=10 MeV, three-body binding energies B₃(μ,T) are calculated for various chemical potentials; the Mott transition occurs when B₃(μ_Mott,T_Mott)=0.

## Context

This work extends the study of three-quark correlations in hot and dense matter, which had received little attention compared to qq̄ bound states and condensates. It builds on prior in-medium three-body equations by Beyer et al. (2001) and the light-front approach at finite density suggested by Miller for T=0. The results enable systematic investigation of how three-quark correlations affect the critical temperature for color superconductivity, complementing work on two-particle Thouless instabilities in the Hubbard model by Letz et al. and NJL model calculations by Pepin et al.
