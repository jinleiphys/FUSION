# Inclusive and exclusive neutrino-nucleus cross sections and the reconstruction of the interaction kinematics

> We present a full kinematic analysis of neutrino-nucleus charged current quasielastic interactions based on the Local Fermi Gas model and the Random PhaseApproximation. The model was implemented in the NEUT Monte Carlo framework, which allows us to investigate potentially measurable observables, including hadron distributions. We compare the predictions simultaneously to the most recent T2K and MINERvA charged current (CC) inclusive, CC0$π$ and transverse kinematic-imbalance variable results. We pursuit a microscopic interpretation of the relevant reaction mechanisms, with the aim of achieving in neutrino oscillation experiments a correct reconstruction of the incoming neutrino kinematics, free of conceptual biasses. Such study is of the utmost importance for the ambitious experimental program which is underway to precisely determine neutrino properties, test the three-generation paradigm, establish the order of mass eigenstates and investigate leptonic CP violation.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a Local Fermi Gas model with Random Phase Approximation correlations, implemented in the NEUT Monte Carlo framework, can simultaneously describe T2K and MINERvA inclusive and exclusive charged-current neutrino-nucleus cross section data, but only when proper removal energy corrections are applied to avoid non-physical negative missing energies in the impulse approximation energy balance.

## Method

The authors implemented a fully exclusive charged-current quasielastic (CCQE) 1p1h event generator based on the Local Fermi Gas model and Random Phase Approximation within the NEUT Monte Carlo framework, providing predictions for hadron kinematics including the radial interaction point, target nucleon momentum, and final state nucleon momentum. They also implemented a consistent 2p2h (two particle-two hole) contribution using the same LFG scheme, with removal energy corrections accounting for the excitation energy of the residual nucleus. The model predictions were compared to T2K and MINERvA data for CC inclusive, CC0π, and transverse kinematic imbalance (TKI) variables, with three different configurations of nucleon re-scattering probability (nominal, 50%, and 150% strengths).

## Key numbers

- Average missing energy E_m^IA for 1p1h in carbon: 28 MeV, with average excitation energy ⟨ε'_A-1⟩ ~ 11 MeV using S_n + (m_p - m_n) = 17.4 MeV
- Average missing energy E_m^IA for 2p2h in carbon: around 45 MeV, larger than the 1p1h average of 28 MeV
- T2K CC0π χ² value: around 17 for 29 degrees of freedom, among the best reported in the T2K paper
- T2K CC inclusive χ² value close to the degrees of freedom, almost a factor of two smaller than those presented in the experimental paper using NEUT 5.3.2 and GENIE 2.8.0
- MINERvA CC inclusive χ² values: 54.2 (nominal), 53.3 (50% NrSP), 54.5 (150% NrSP) for 40 degrees of freedom
- MINERvA CC0π χ² values: 47.4 (nominal), 47.2 (50% NrSP), 47.5 (150% NrSP) for 40 degrees of freedom
- MINERvA CC0π1p δα_T χ² values: 11.2 (nominal), 10.3 (50% NrSP), 12.6 (150% NrSP) for 9 degrees of freedom
- T2K CC0π1p δα_T χ² values: 11.2 (nominal), 11.0 (50% NrSP), 11.4 (150% NrSP) for 9 degrees of freedom
- MINERvA CC0π1p δφ_T χ² values: 5.6 (nominal), 5.7 (50% NrSP), 5.8 (150% NrSP) for 9 degrees of freedom

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Nieves (2004) and Nieves (2011) for neutrino-nucleus interactions by implementing a fully exclusive Monte Carlo event generator within NEUT, enabling direct comparison with T2K and MINERvA data. The model addresses the problem of neutrino energy reconstruction bias in oscillation experiments caused by misidentification of 2p2h events as quasielastic, a concern previously raised by Nieves (2012) and Alvarez-Ruso (2014). The LFG approach is compared to the Spectral Function scheme of Benhar (1989, 1994, 2005), showing similar patterns in the (p_N, E_m^IA) distribution despite differences at the edges. The implementation enables future improvements for Hyper-Kamiokande and other long-baseline experiments by providing a framework for bias-free neutrino energy reconstruction.
