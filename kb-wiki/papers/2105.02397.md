# Hidden charm tetraquark states in a diquark model

> The purpose of the present study is to explore the mass spectrum of the hidden charm tetraquark states within a diquark model. Proposing that a tetraquark state is composed of a diquark and an antidiquark, the masses of all possible $[qc][\bar{q}\bar{c}]$, $[sc][\bar{s}\bar{c}]$, and $[qc][\bar{s}\bar{c}]$ $\left([sc][\bar{q}\bar{c}]\right)$ hidden charm tetraquark states are systematically calculated by use of an effective Hamiltonian, which contains color, spin, and flavor dependent interactions. Apart from the $X(3872)$, $Z(3900)$, $χ_{c2}(3930)$, and $X(4350)$ which are taken as input to fix the model parameters, the calculated results support that the $χ_{c0}(3860)$, $X(4020)$, $X(4050)$ are $[qc][\bar{q}\bar{c}]$ states with $I^GJ^{PC}=0^+0^{++}$, $1^+1^{+-}$, and $1^-2^{++}$, respectively, the $χ_{c1}(4274)$ is an $[sc][\bar{s}\bar{c}]$ state with $I^GJ^{PC}=0^+1^{++}$, the $X(3940)$ is a $[qc][\bar{q}\bar{c}]$ state with $I^GJ^{PC}=1^-0^{++}$ or $1^-1^{++}$, the $Z_{cs}(3985)^-$ is an $[sc][\bar{q}\bar{c}]$ state with $J^{P}=0^{+}$ or $1^+$, and the $Z_{cs}(4000)^+$ and $Z_{cs}(4220)^+$ are $[qc][\bar{s}\bar{c}]$ states with $J^{P}=1^{+}$. Predictions for other possible tetraquark states are also given.

## Key claim
The paper claims that several observed XYZ states can be accommodated as hidden charm tetraquark states composed of a diquark and an antidiquark, but only under the condition that the model parameters are fixed using the masses of X(3872), Z(3900), chi_c2(3930), and X(4350) as input, and that the flavor-dependent interactions are included to distinguish isospin multiplets.

## Method
The authors constructed an effective Hamiltonian containing color-spin, spin, flavor-spin, and flavor-dependent interactions for tetraquark states composed of a diquark and an antidiquark. They systematically calculated the masses of all possible [qc][q̅c̅], [sc][s̅c̅], and [qc][s̅c̅] ([sc][q̅c̅]) hidden charm tetraquark states using wave functions in color, flavor, and spin spaces, with no orbital excitation considered. The model parameters were fixed by fitting to the masses of X(3872), Z_c(3900), chi_c2(3930), X(4350), and heavy baryons (Lambda_c, Xi_c, Omega_c^0, Sigma_c, Xi_c', Sigma_c^*, Xi*_c).

## Context
This work extends the diquark model approach previously applied to XYZ states by Maiani (2005), Hogaasen (2006), and others, but adds explicit flavor-dependent interactions that were missing in most earlier diquark models. The inclusion of flavor-spin and flavor interactions allows the model to distinguish isospin multiplets, which earlier models by Giron (2019) attempted via one-pion exchange but without color-spin interactions between diquarks. The results enable future experimental searches for the predicted unobserved tetraquark states and provide assignments for states like chi_c0(3860), X(4020), X(4050), and chi_c1(4274) within the diquark framework.
