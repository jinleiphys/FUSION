# Universal relations for rapidly rotating cold and hot hybrid star

> Several global parameters of compact stars are related via empirical relations, which are (nearly) independent of the underlying equation of state (EoS) of dense matter and, therefore, are said to be universal. We investigate the universality of relations that express the maximum mass and the radius of non-rotating and maximally rapidly rotating configurations, as well as their moment of inertia, in terms of the compactness of the star. For this, we first utilize a collection of cold (zero-temperature) and hot (isentropic) nucleonic EoS and confirm that the universal relations are holding for our collection of EoS. We then go on, to add to our collection and test for the same universality models of EoS that admit a strong first-order phase transition from nucleonic to deconfined quark matter. Also in this case we find that the universal relations hold, in particular for hot, isentropic hybrid stars. By fitting the universal relations to our computed data, we determine the coefficients entering these relations and the accuracy to which they hold.

## Key claim
The paper confirms that universal relations connecting maximum mass, radius, and moment of inertia to compactness hold not only for cold and hot nucleonic equations of state but also for hybrid star equations of state that include a strong first-order phase transition from nucleonic to deconfined quark matter, with the qualification that the relations hold "in particular for hot, isentropic hybrid stars."

## Method
The authors used the open-source RNS code based on the Komatsu-Eriguchi-Hachisu formalism to construct equilibrium configurations of rotating compact stars in general relativity. They computed static and maximally rapidly rotating (Keplerian limit) configurations for a collection of 17 hadronic equations of state and 7 families of hybrid equations of state (including MIT bag, NJL, CSS, RDF, and VBAG models) at both zero temperature (beta-equilibrium) and finite temperature (constant entropy per particle s=3 kB and lepton fraction YL=0.3). Universal relations were fitted to the computed data to determine coefficients and accuracy.

## Key numbers
- Maximum masses for cold hadronic EoS range from 1.92 M_sun (DD2Fev-m10) to 2.79 M_sun (NL3)
- Maximum masses for cold hybrid EoS range from 1.90 M_sun (DD2F-VBAG(a)) to 2.69 M_sun (APR-nlNJL eta_V=0.20)
- Ratio of Keplerian to static maximum mass M*_max/M_max ranges from 1.1489 to 1.2539 across all cold hybrid EoS
- Ratio of Keplerian to static maximum mass M*_max/M_max for hot hadronic EoS (s=3 kB) ranges from 1.0979 to 1.1270
- Moment of inertia at maximum mass for cold hybrid EoS ranges from 2.54 x 10^45 g cm^2 to 7.94 x 10^45 g cm^2
- Ratio of equatorial radii for Keplerian to static configurations R*_max/R_max ranges from 1.2870 to 1.3729 for cold hybrid EoS

## Context
This work extends the universal relation studies of Breu and Rezzolla (2016) and Khadkikar et al. (2021) by including hybrid stars with quark cores, complementing earlier work by Paschalidis et al. (2017) and Bozzola et al. (2019) that focused on cold hybrid configurations. The results enable the use of universal relations to infer properties of non-rotating hybrid stars from gravitational wave observations of binary neutron star mergers, particularly for the hypermassive remnant scenario following GW170817, and extend the applicability to finite-temperature astrophysical environments such as core-collapse supernovae and post-merger phases.
