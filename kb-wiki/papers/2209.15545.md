# Effect of dipole size fluctuations on diffractive photo-production of vector mesons

> We consider the diffractive photo-production of vector mesons on a proton, in the dipole model. We take into account the effect of the fluctuations of the the dipole size, whose magnitude is controlled by the overlap between the photon and the vector meson wave functions. Our predictions for the incoherent diffractive cross section, obtained within the Impact Parameter Saturation Model (IPSat), are shown to be in excellent agreement with the HERA data on $J/Ψ$ photo-production, down to very low momentum transfer, where the dipole size fluctuations are shown to play an essential role. This study complements, without introducing any additional parameter, previous treatments of incoherent diffractive processes in terms of fluctuations of the proton shape, by adding another source of ``geometrical'' fluctuations, namely those coming from the splitting of the photon into color dipoles of various sizes.

## Key claim

The authors show that fluctuations in the size of the color dipole, arising from the quantum mechanical splitting of the photon into a quark-antiquark pair, provide an essential contribution to the incoherent diffractive cross section for J/Ψ photo-production at very low momentum transfer, where fluctuations of the proton shape alone are suppressed. This is achieved without introducing any additional free parameters beyond those already fixed in the Impact Parameter Saturation Model (IPSat).

## Method

The authors calculate the diffractive photo-production of J/Ψ on a proton within the color dipole model, using the impact parameter dependent saturation model (IPSat) with parameters fixed by fits to HERA deep inelastic scattering data. They treat the proton as a composite object of three valence quarks, each carrying a Gaussian gluon cloud, and sample random valence quark positions event-by-event to account for proton shape fluctuations. The novel element is the treatment of dipole size fluctuations: the overlap of the photon and vector meson wave functions is interpreted as a probability distribution for the dipole size, and the incoherent cross section is computed from the variance of the scattering amplitude over both the dipole size distribution and the proton configurations.

## Key numbers

- The Gaussian width parameter for the proton's average gluon density profile is \( B_G = 4 \; \text{GeV}^{-2} \), corresponding to a root mean square radius of \( 0.56 \; \text{fm} \).
- The Gaussian width for the valence quark distribution is \( B_{qc} = 3.3 \; \text{GeV}^{-2} \) (radius \( 0.51 \; \text{fm} \)), and for the gluon cloud around each valence quark \( B_q = 0.7 \; \text{GeV}^{-2} \) (radius \( 0.24 \; \text{fm} \)).
- The numerical results were obtained using \( N_C = 10\,000 \) configurations of random valence quark positions.
- The predictions are compared with HERA data on J/Ψ photo-production down to very low momentum transfer, where dipole size fluctuations are shown to play an essential role.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological approach of Mäntysaari and Schenke (2016), which treated incoherent diffraction via fluctuations of the proton shape from random valence quark locations, by adding a complementary source of geometrical fluctuations: those from the splitting of the photon into color dipoles of various sizes. The dipole size fluctuations bear analogy with the fluctuating cross sections introduced by Blaettel et al. (1993) and could also be interpreted as fluctuations of the saturation momentum. The study enables a more complete description of incoherent diffractive processes without introducing new parameters, and it explains the behavior of the cross section at small momentum transfer where proton shape fluctuations vanish due to the properties of the density-density correlation function.
