# A Framework for Quantum Simulations of Energy-Loss and Hadronization in Non-Abelian Gauge Theories: SU(2) Lattice Gauge Theory in 1+1D

> Simulations of energy loss and hadronization are essential for understanding a range of phenomena in non-equilibrium strongly-interacting matter. We establish a framework for performing such simulations on a quantum computer and apply it to a heavy quark moving across a modest-sized 1+1D SU(2) lattice of light quarks. Conceptual advances with regard to simulations of non-Abelian versus Abelian theories are developed, allowing for the evolution of the energy in light quarks, of their local non-Abelian charge densities, and of their multi-partite entanglement to be computed. The non-trivial action of non-Abelian charge operators on arbitrary states suggests mapping the heavy quarks to qubits alongside the light quarks, and limits the heavy-quark motion to discrete steps among spatial lattice sites. Further, the color entanglement among the heavy quarks and light quarks is implemented using hadronic operators, and Domain Decomposition is shown to be effective in quantum state preparation. Scalable quantum circuits that account for the heterogeneity of non-Abelian charge sectors across the lattice are used to prepare the interacting ground-state wavefunction in the presence of heavy quarks. The discrete motion of heavy quarks between adjacent spatial sites is implemented using fermionic SWAP operations. Quantum simulations of the dynamics of a system on $L=3$ spatial sites are performed using IBM's ${\tt ibm\_pittsburgh}$ quantum computer using 18 qubits, for which the circuits for state preparation, motion, and one second-order Trotter step of time evolution have a two-qubit depth of 398. A suite of error mitigation techniques are used to extract the observables from the simulations, providing results that are in good agreement with classical simulations. The framework presented here generalizes straightforwardly to other non-Abelian groups, including SU(3) for quantum chromodynamics.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a framework for quantum simulations of energy loss and hadronization in non-Abelian gauge theories, applied to a heavy quark moving across a 1+1D SU(2) lattice, but the framework is limited to discrete heavy-quark motion between lattice sites (rather than continuous translation) and requires mapping heavy quarks to qubits alongside light quarks due to the non-trivial action of non-Abelian charge operators on arbitrary states.

## Method

The authors map a 1+1D SU(2) lattice gauge theory with one flavor of light quark and static heavy quarks to qubits using the Jordan-Wigner transformation, with 18 qubits for a lattice of L=3 spatial sites. Heavy-quark motion is implemented via fermionic SWAP (FSWAP) gates acting on adjacent spatial sites, while ground-state wavefunctions in the presence of heavy quarks are prepared using SC-ADAPT-VQE combined with Domain Decomposition (DDec) to handle the heterogeneity of non-Abelian charge sectors. Time evolution is performed using one second-order Trotter step, and the quantum circuits are executed on IBM's ibm_pittsburgh quantum computer with error mitigation techniques applied to extract observables.

## Key numbers

- The quantum simulation uses L=3 spatial sites, requiring 18 qubits total
- The transpiled circuits for state preparation, motion, and one second-order Trotter step of time evolution have a two-qubit depth of 398
- For the L=1 system without heavy quarks at g=1 and m_q=0.1, the interacting ground-state energy is E_0 = -0.6578
- For the L=1 system without heavy quarks, the expectation values of Hamiltonian components are: ⟨H_k⟩ = -0.9474, ⟨H_m⟩ = +0.14553, ⟨H_g⟩ = +0.1440, ⟨H_λ⟩ = 0
- The variational wavefunction for the L=1 system without heavy quarks achieves an infidelity of I ∼ 10^{-6} with operator coefficients θ_1 = 0.267215 and θ_2 = 0.05484

## Context

This work extends the framework previously established for Abelian lattice gauge theory (Farrell 2024) to non-Abelian SU(2) gauge theories, addressing the additional complexities of color entanglement and non-Abelian charge operators that do not arise in U(1) theories like the Schwinger model. The framework builds on prior quantum simulations of SU(2) and SU(3) theories in 1+1D (Klco 2019, Ciavarella 2021, Atas 2021, Farrell 2022) and enables future simulations of energy loss, hadronization, and jet physics in quantum chromodynamics, with the authors noting that the framework generalizes straightforwardly to SU(3) for QCD.
