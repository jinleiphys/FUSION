# Photon Production in High-Energy Heavy-Ion Collisions: Thermal Photons and Radiative Recombination

> We present a comprehensive analysis of photon production at RHIC and the LHC, proposing radiative hadronization as an additional photon source in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. For thermal photon, we perform relativistic viscous hydrodynamic calculation with event-by-event fluctuations.

## Key claim
The paper proposes "radiative hadronization" as an overlooked photon source in high-energy heavy-ion collisions, but notes that the large photon yield measured by PHENIX at RHIC has not been confirmed by STAR and that the deviation between thermal photon calculations and experimental data is not so large at the LHC.

## Method
The authors performed relativistic viscous hydrodynamic calculations using the TRENTo initial condition model with shear and bulk viscosities, coupled with UrQMD for final-state interactions, to compute thermal photon emission from QGP and hadronic phases. They introduced a radiative ReCo model where meson formation via quark-antiquark recombination proceeds through a preformed state that decays into a meson and a photon, with an overall normalization factor kappa tuned to experimental data.

## Key numbers
- At RHIC (sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV Au+Au), the normalization factor for the radiative ReCo model was set to kappa=0.2 to reproduce observed photon yield for p_T < 3 GeV
- At the LHC (sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV Pb+Pb), the normalization factor was reduced to kappa=0.05 to fit data in the region p_T less than about 2 GeV
- The hydrodynamic evolution was continued until T_f=116 MeV, below the switching temperature T_SW=150 MeV
- The interpolation parameter T_c was set to 170 MeV with Delta T = 0.1 T_c
- For prompt photons at the LHC, the infrared cutoff a_2=4 GeV^2, a_1=1.2x10^{-2} GeV^{-2}, and a_3=-2.7 were used to fit pp-collision data at sqrt(s)=8 TeV

## Context
This work addresses the "direct photon puzzle" where hydrodynamic models that explain hadronic spectra and anisotropic flow underpredict the amplitude of photon elliptic flow, as noted in prior studies by Paquet (2015) and others. The radiative hadronization mechanism extends the recombination model of Fries (2003) by adding photon emission during meson formation. The results enable future investigation of why the normalization factor kappa decreases from RHIC to LHC energies, which the authors identify as an open question.
