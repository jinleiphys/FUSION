# Strangeness At Extremes

> We study the properties of strange mesons in vacuum and in the hot nuclear medium within unitarized coupled-channel effective theories. We determine transition probabilities, cross sections and scattering lengths for strange mesons. These scattering observables are of fundamental importance for understanding the dynamics of strangeness production and propagation in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper studies strange meson properties in vacuum and hot nuclear matter using unitarized coupled-channel effective theories, but the results are explicitly conditional on the specific model choices (subtraction constants, form-factor cutoffs) and the authors note that a simultaneous description of all observables involving $\bar K$ production is still missing.

## Method

The authors apply a self-consistent coupled-channel approach based on the SU(3) meson-baryon chiral Lagrangian to study $\bar K$ mesons in hot nuclear matter, implementing unitarization of $\bar K N$ scattering amplitudes in both $s$- and $p$-waves at finite density and temperature. For $KN$ and $K^*N$ interactions in free space, they use a formalism based on the chiral Lagrangian and hidden local symmetry, constructing $s$-wave amplitudes and solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation in its on-shell factorization form. The subtraction constants for the Bethe-Salpeter equation are obtained by fitting the $KN$ amplitudes to available data for isospin 0 and 1 $s$-wave phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear matter density used: $\rho_0 = 0.17\ {\rm fm}^{-3}$
- $\Lambda(1115)$ hyperon attractive potential at normal matter density and zero temperature: roughly $-50\ {\rm MeV}$
- $\Sigma(1195)$ hyperon attractive potential at normal matter density and zero temperature: roughly $-40\ {\rm MeV}$
- $\Sigma^*(1385)$ hyperon attractive potential at $\rho = \rho_0$ and zero temperature: about $-10\ {\rm MeV}$
- $KN$ scattering length for isospin 0, spin 1/2: $a^{0,1/2}_{KN} = -0.16\ {\rm fm}$
- $KN$ scattering length for isospin 1, spin 1/2: $a^{1,1/2}_{KN} = -0.29\ {\rm fm}$
- $K^*N$ scattering lengths: $a^{0,1/2}_{K^*N} = (0.2, 0.03)\ {\rm fm}$, $a^{0,3/2}_{K^*N} = (-0.08, 0.04)\ {\rm fm}$, $a^{1,1/2}_{K^*N} = (0.1, 0.0)\ {\rm fm}$, $a^{1,3/2}_{K^*N} = (-0.31, 0.03)\ {\rm fm}$
- Subtraction constants for Bethe-Salpeter equation with regularization scale $\mu = 630\ {\rm MeV}$: $b^{I=0}_{KN} = -6.82$, $b^{I=0}_{K^*N} = 1.84$, $b^{I=1}_{KN} = -1.59$, $b^{I=1}_{K^*N} = -1$

## Context

This work extends previous self-consistent coupled-channel studies of $\bar K$ mesons in nuclear matter by Tolos (2008) to include both $s$- and $p$-waves at finite density and temperature, and provides new results for $KN$ and $K^*N$ interactions in free space using hidden local symmetry. The computed transition probabilities, cross sections, and scattering lengths are intended for use in transport approaches such as PHSD and IQMD to analyze strangeness production in heavy-ion collisions, particularly for comparison with experimental data from HADES, STAR, and NA49 Collaborations.
