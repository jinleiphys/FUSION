# Studying the Interplay of Strong and Electromagnetic Forces in Heavy Ion Collisions with NICA

> In the following we stress the advantages of the NICA research programme in the context of studying the spectator-induced electromagnetic phenomena present in proton-nucleus and heavy ion collisions. We point at the specific interest of using these phenomena as a new, independent source of information on the space-time evolution of the reaction and of the non-perturbative process of particle production. We propose an extended series of measurements of well-defined observables to be performed in different types of nuclear reactions and in the whole range of collision energies available to NICA. We expect these measurements to bring very valuable new insight into the mechanism of non-perturbative strong interactions, complementary to the studies made at the SPS at CERN, RHIC at BNL, and the LHC.

## Key claim

The spectator-induced electromagnetic effect on charged particle spectra and flow can serve as a new, independent source of information on the space-time evolution of non-perturbative particle production in heavy ion collisions, but the available experimental data at SPS energies and above remain extremely limited, making NICA uniquely positioned to perform such measurements with little competition from RHIC or LHC experiments.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo simulations of the electromagnetic interaction between charged pions/kaons and the spectator nuclear remnant in peripheral Pb+Pb collisions at 158 GeV/nucleon beam energy (√sNN = 17.3 GeV), comparing the results to experimental data from the NA49 experiment at CERN SPS. They modeled the distortion of charged particle ratios (π+/π-, K+/K-) and directed flow as a function of Feynman xF and transverse momentum pT, varying the assumed particle emission time tE to probe sensitivity to the space-time evolution of the reaction.

## Key numbers

- The π+/π- ratio in peripheral Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 17.3 GeV goes close to zero near xF = 0.15 = mπ/mN, violating isospin symmetry and confirming electromagnetic origin
- The electromagnetic distortion pattern in π+/π- ratios shows clear sensitivity to the assumed pion emission time tE, with typically lower ratios obtained for larger tE at higher xF values
- For kaons, the position of the deep valley in K+/K- ratios is displaced towards higher xF values (xF > 0.75) compared to pions, with the region of highest sensitivity to emission time moved to very high xF
- The electromagnetically-induced directed flow of positive pions shows a large peak in the vicinity of projectile and target rapidities, integrated over pT from 0 to 1 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier studies (Rybicki et al., 2011; Sputowska et al., 2012) of spectator-induced electromagnetic effects at CERN SPS energies, which themselves followed from only one earlier experimental measurement at these energies (NA52, 1999) that was restricted to very narrow acceptance. The proposed NICA measurements would provide complementary information to studies at SPS, RHIC, and LHC, particularly addressing the lack of position-space information that characterizes most heavy ion experiments (with the exception of HBT measurements by Baym, 1998). The work enables systematic studies of the space-time evolution of particle production, spectator fragmentation, and their interplay across different collision systems and energies.
