# Neutrinos and Symmetries

> Three facets of symmetries in neutrino physics are briefly reviewed: i) The SO(5) symmetry of the neutrino mass and and its connection to the see-saw mechanism; ii) Flavor SU(N) symmetries of dense, self-interacting neutrino gases in astrophysical settings; iii) The neutrino mixing angle theta13 and possible CP-violation in the neutrino sector.

# Neutrinos and Symmetries

## Key claim
The paper reviews three symmetry-based approaches in neutrino physics, arguing that the SO(5) symmetry provides the largest symmetry framework for neutrino mass terms, that dense neutrino gases exhibit non-linear SU(N_flavor) symmetries as the only many-body system controlled entirely by weak interactions, and that under specific assumptions (equal muon and tau neutrino luminosities at the neutrinosphere and negligible V_τμ potential), the electron neutrino survival probability and luminosities become independent of the CP-violating phase δ, meaning observable CP-violation effects require relaxing these assumptions.

## Method
The author reviews the algebraic structure of the SO(5) symmetry generated by charge operators acting on neutrino fields, showing how the see-saw mechanism emerges from an SU(2)_PG rotation within this coset. For dense neutrino gases, the paper constructs SU(2) and SU(3) flavor algebras from creation and annihilation operators for neutrinos and antineutrinos, writing the Hamiltonian for neutrino propagation through matter and neutrino-neutrino interactions. The CP-violation analysis introduces rotated flavor fields and derives sum rules showing that the electron neutrino survival probability and the combination P(ν_μ→ν_e) + P(ν_τ→ν_e) are independent of δ when V_τμ is negligible and muon/tau luminosities are equal.

## Key numbers
- The number of neutrinos in a core-collapse supernova is approximately 10^58, though their distribution is very unlikely to be isotropic.
- The neutrino-neutrino interaction Hamiltonian includes a factor (1−cosϑ_pq) from the V-A nature of weak interactions, where ϑ_pq is the angle between neutrino momenta p and q.
- The mixing matrix is parameterized by three angles θ_12, θ_13, θ_23 and one CP-violating phase δ, with C_ij = cosθ_ij and S_ij = sinθ_ij.

## Context
This review extends the author's prior work on SO(5) symmetry (Balantekin 2000) and SU(2) flavor algebras for dense neutrino gases (Balantekin 2006), building on the observation that neutrino mass is the only dimension-five operator in the Standard Model. The paper connects to the see-saw mechanism originally considered by Pauli and Gürsey (1957), and references the saddle-point approximation approach (Balantekin 2006) and numerical investigations (Qian 1994) for neutrino many-body systems. The CP-violation sum rules extend earlier work (Balantekin 2007, Kneller 2009) and show that observing δ requires either different muon and tau luminosities at the neutrinosphere or non-zero V_τμ from Standard Model loop corrections (Akhmedov 2002) or beyond-Standard-Model physics, enabling future reactor experiments (Guo 2007, Ardellier 2006, Oh 2009) to probe non-zero θ_13.
