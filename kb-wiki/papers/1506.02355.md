# Electron and Muon production cross sections in quasielastic $ν(\barν)$-Nucleus scattering for $E_ν< 1~GeV$

> In this work, we have studied (anti)neutrino induced charged current quasielastic scattering from some nuclear targets in the energy region of $E_ν< 1~GeV$. Our aim is to confront electron and muon production cross sections relevant for $ν_μ\leftrightarrow ν_e$ or $\barν_μ\leftrightarrow \barν_e$ oscillation experiments. The effects due to lepton mass and its kinematic implications, radiative corrections, second class currents and uncertainties in the axial and pseudoscalar form factors are calculated for (anti)neutrino induced reaction cross sections on free nucleon as well as the nucleons bound in a nucleus where nuclear medium effects influence the cross section. For the nuclear medium effects we have taken some versions of Fermi gas model(FGM) available in literature. The results for (anti)neutrino-nucleus scattering cross section per interacting nucleons are compared with the corresponding results in free nucleon case.

## Key claim

The paper systematically quantifies how nuclear medium effects (Fermi motion, Pauli blocking, binding energy, and RPA correlations) modify the difference between electron and muon production cross sections in quasielastic (anti)neutrino-nucleus scattering, finding that the fractional difference between electron and muon cross sections is reduced in nuclei compared to the free nucleon case, particularly at energies below 0.4 GeV.

## Method

The authors calculated charged-current quasielastic (CCQE) cross sections for νₑ, ν_μ, and their antiparticles on ¹²C, ⁴⁰Ar, ⁵⁶Fe, and ²⁰⁸Pb targets using a Local Fermi Gas Model (LFG) with and without Random Phase Approximation (RPA) correlations. They incorporated lepton mass effects, radiative corrections, second class currents, and variations in axial dipole mass M_A and pseudoscalar form factor parameterizations, comparing results to the free nucleon case and to other Fermi gas models (Smith-Moniz, Llewellyn Smith, Gaisser-O'Connell).

## Key numbers

- In ¹²C using LFG, nuclear medium effects reduce the cross section from the free nucleon case by ~30% (ν_e) and ~42% (ν̄_e) at E_ν = 0.3 GeV; at E_ν = 0.6 GeV the reduction is ~20% (ν_e) and ~30% (ν̄_e)
- Including RPA correlations in LFG for ¹²C reduces the cross section from free nucleon by ~55% (ν_e) and ~56% (ν̄_e) at E_ν = 0.3 GeV; at E_ν = 0.6 GeV the reduction is ~35% (ν_e) and ~45% (ν̄_e)
- For ⁴⁰Ar using the Llewellyn Smith Fermi gas model, cross sections are reduced from free nucleon by ~16% (ν_e) and ~45% (ν̄_e) at E_ν = 0.3 GeV; at E_ν = 0.6 GeV the reduction is ~8% (ν_e) and ~30% (ν̄_e)
- For ⁴⁰Ar using the Smith-Moniz Fermi gas model, reduction from free nucleon is ~42% (ν_e) and ~45% (ν̄_e) at E_ν = 0.3 GeV; at E_ν = 0.6 GeV the reduction is ~24% (ν_e) and ~22% (ν̄_e)
- For ⁴⁰Ar using the Gaisser-O'Connell Fermi gas model, reduction from free nucleon is ~38% (ν_e) and ~52% (ν̄_e) at E_ν = 0.3 GeV; at E_ν = 0.6 GeV the reduction is ~20% (ν_e) and ~35% (ν̄_e)
- For ⁴⁰Ar using LFG, reduction from free nucleon is ~25% (ν_e) and ~44% (ν̄_e) at E_ν = 0.3 GeV; at E_ν = 0.6 GeV the reduction is ~15% (ν_e) and ~30% (ν̄_e)
- For ⁴⁰Ar using LFG+RPA, reduction from free nucleon is ~54% (ν_e) and ~58% (ν̄_e) at E_ν = 0.3 GeV; at E_ν = 0.6 GeV the reduction is ~32% (ν_e) and ~45% (ν̄_e)
- A 10% change in axial dipole mass M_A (from 1.0 GeV to 1.1 GeV) results in approximately a 10% increase in the CCQE scattering cross section

## Context

This work extends the free-nucleon study of lepton mass and radiative correction effects by Day and McFarland (2012) to nuclear targets, incorporating nuclear medium effects that are relevant for oscillation experiments like T2K, NOνA, and future experiments such as DUNE and T2HK. The results show that nuclear model dependence is larger for ν_μ scattering than for ν_e scattering below 0.8 GeV, and that different Fermi gas models (Smith-Moniz, Llewellyn Smith, Gaisser-O'Connell, and the authors' LFG with RPA) produce appreciably different cross section reductions, highlighting the need for improved nuclear modeling in neutrino event generators like GENIE, NEUT, and NuWro.
