# Effect of the Coulomb interaction on nuclear deformation and drip lines

> Nuclei are self-bound systems in which the strong interaction (nuclear force) plays a dominant role and the isospin is approximately a good quantum number. The isospin symmetry is primarily violated by the electromagnetic interactions, namely the Coulomb interaction among protons, effects of which need be studied to understand importance of the isospin symmetry. We investigate the effect of the Coulomb interaction on nuclear properties, especially the quadrupole deformation and neutron drip line, utilizing the density functional method which provides a universal description of nuclear systems in the entire nuclear chart. We carry out calculations of even-even nuclei with the proton number 2 to 60. The results show that the Coulomb interaction plays a significant role in enhancing the quadrupole deformation across a wide range of nuclei. We also find that nuclei near the neutron drip line gain an additional binding energy by the Coulomb interaction, which may lead to a shift of the neutron drip line toward a larger neutron number.

## Key claim
The Coulomb interaction significantly enhances quadrupole deformation across a wide range of nuclei and provides additional binding energy near the neutron drip line, potentially shifting the neutron drip line toward larger neutron numbers. This result is based on density functional calculations for even-even nuclei with proton numbers 2 to 60, and the abstract's claim of a "significant role" is hedged by the fact that the study is limited to this specific mass region and a particular density functional framework.

## Method
The authors employed the nuclear density functional method (energy density functional approach) to perform self-consistent calculations of ground-state properties for even-even nuclei across the nuclear chart. They systematically compared results obtained with and without the Coulomb interaction to isolate its effect on quadrupole deformation and binding energies, focusing on nuclei with proton numbers Z=2 to 60.

## Key numbers
- The study covers even-even nuclei with proton numbers from 2 to 60 (Z=2-60), spanning the light to medium-mass region of the nuclear chart.
- The Coulomb interaction was found to enhance quadrupole deformation across a wide range of nuclei within this region, though no specific deformation values or energy shifts are provided in the available text.
- The additional binding energy from the Coulomb interaction near the neutron drip line may shift the drip line toward larger neutron numbers, but no quantitative shift in neutron number is given in the truncated text.

## Context
This work extends systematic density functional studies of nuclear structure, such as those by Bender et al. (2003) and Erler et al. (2012), by isolating the specific role of the Coulomb interaction on deformation and drip-line positions. The results enable more precise predictions of neutron-rich nuclei and the location of the neutron drip line, which is important for understanding the limits of nuclear existence and for planning experiments at rare-isotope facilities. The study also provides a benchmark for future calculations that include isospin-breaking effects beyond the Coulomb interaction.
