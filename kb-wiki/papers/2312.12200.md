# Heavy quark momentum diffusion coefficient during hydrodynamization via effective kinetic theory

> In these proceedings, we compute the heavy quark momentum diffusion coefficient using QCD effective kinetic theory for a plasma going through the bottom-up thermalization scenario until approximate hydrodynamization. This transport coefficient describes heavy quark momentum diffusion in the quark-gluon plasma and is used in many phenomenological frameworks, e.g. in the open quantum systems approach. Our extracted nonthermal diffusion coefficient matches the thermal one for the same energy density within 30\%. At large occupation numbers in the earliest stage, the transverse diffusion coefficient dominates, while the longitudinal diffusion coefficient is larger for the underoccupied system in the later stage of hydrodynamization.

## Key claim

The heavy quark momentum diffusion coefficient computed via QCD effective kinetic theory for a non-equilibrium plasma undergoing bottom-up thermalization matches the thermal equilibrium value for the same energy density within 30%, but the agreement is considerably worse when matching for the same Debye screening mass or effective temperature.

## Method

The authors numerically solve the Boltzmann equation in QCD effective kinetic theory, including 1-to-2 splittings, 2-to-2 processes, and an effective expansion term to model the longitudinal expansion of the plasma. They extract the heavy quark momentum diffusion coefficient from the gluon phase space distribution as it evolves through the bottom-up thermalization scenario, and compare the non-equilibrium results to thermal systems matched by energy density, Debye mass, and effective temperature.

## Key numbers

- The nonthermal diffusion coefficient matches the thermal one for the same energy density within 30%.
- The heavy quark diffusion coefficient in the glasma stage is estimated to be roughly κ ≈ O(10) GeV²/fm, while in equilibrium it is κ ≈ O(0.1) GeV²/fm.
- The ratio of transverse to longitudinal diffusion coefficients κ_T/κ_z is at most 2 throughout the entire evolution.
- For λ = 2, the result is in rough agreement with the lattice estimate at T = 10⁴ T_c, while at T = 1.5 T_c the lattice result is considerably larger.

## Context

This work extends the study of heavy quark diffusion from equilibrium calculations (Brambilla 2022) and glasma-stage estimates (Avramescu 2023) into the hydrodynamization process, using the bottom-up thermalization framework of Baier (2000). The results enable phenomenological applications in heavy quark diffusion and quarkonium dynamics, and provide a benchmark for comparing non-equilibrium transport coefficients to lattice QCD results at different temperatures.
