# Spectroscopy of light $N^{*}$ baryons

> We present the masses of N baryon upto 3300 MeV. The radial and orbital excited states are determined using hypercentral constituent quark model with first order correction. The obtained masses are compared with experimental results and other theoretical prediction. The Regge Trajectories are also determined in (n, $M^2$) and (J, $M^2$) planes. Moreover, the magnetic moments for $J^{P}= \frac{1}{2}^{+}, \frac{1}{2}^{-}$ are calculated. We also calculate the $Nπ$ decay width of excited nucleons.

## Key claim

The hypercentral constituent quark model (hCQM) with a first-order correction to the potential can reproduce the masses of N* baryon resonances up to 3300 MeV, with many predicted states falling near experimentally observed resonances; however, some states such as N(1720), N(1680), N(2000), N(1990), and N(2190) are found to be "far from our determination."

## Method

The authors solved the non-relativistic Schrödinger equation in six-dimensional hyperspherical coordinates using a Coulomb-plus-linear confinement potential with a first-order correction term. The spin-dependent part of the potential included spin-spin, spin-orbit, and tensor terms derived from the one-gluon exchange potential. Quark masses were set to m_u = m_d = 0.290 GeV, and the model was used to compute masses for radial states 1S-5S and orbital states 1P-5P, 1D-4D, and 1F-2F, from which Regge trajectories, magnetic moments, and Nπ decay widths were derived.

## Key numbers

* Ground state N(939) mass: predicted 939 MeV, compared to experimental 938 MeV.
* 2S state (N(1440)): predicted 1425 MeV, within experimental range 1420-1470 MeV.
* 1P state with J^P = 1/2^- (N(1535)): predicted 1565 MeV, within experimental range 1525-1545 MeV.
* 1P state with J^P = 3/2^- (N(1520)): predicted 1535 MeV, within experimental range 1515-1525 MeV.
* 2P state with J^P = 1/2^- (N(1895)): predicted 1898 MeV, within experimental range 1880-1910 MeV.
* 2P state with J^P = 3/2^- (N(1875)): predicted 1865 MeV, within experimental range 1820-1920 MeV.
* 1D state with J^P = 5/2^+ (N(1680)): predicted 1769 MeV, approximately 80 MeV above experimental mass 1680-1690 MeV.
* 2D state with J^P = 7/2^+ (N(1990)): predicted 2083 MeV, within experimental range 1950-2100 MeV.
* 1F state with J^P = 7/2^- (N(2190)): predicted 2045 MeV, 55 MeV below experimental range 2100-2200 MeV.
* 2F state with J^P = 9/2^- (N(2250)): predicted 2379 MeV, 59 MeV above experimental range 2250-2320 MeV.
* Magnetic moment of N(939): predicted -1.997 nuclear magnetons, compared to experimental -1.913.
* Nπ decay width for N(1440): predicted 62%, within PDG range 55-75%.
* Nπ decay width for N(1535): predicted 86%, above PDG range 55-65%.
* Nπ decay width for N(1520): predicted 16%, below PDG range 32-52%.
* Nπ decay width for N(1720): predicted 11%, within PDG range 8-14%.
* Nπ decay width for N(1680): predicted 118%, above PDG range 60-70%.

## Context

This work extends the hypercentral constituent quark model, previously applied to heavy baryons by Shah, Gandhi, and Rai (2016-2018), to the light N* baryon sector, building on earlier light-baryon studies by Giannini et al. (2005) that covered states below 2 GeV. The predicted masses are compared with experimental resonances from the Particle Data Group (2018) and with theoretical results from models such as those of Isgur and Capstick (1980s), Santopinto (2015), and Anisovich (2012). The results enable identification of quantum numbers for experimentally observed resonances and provide predictions for higher excited states (4P, 5P, 2D, 3D, 4D, 2F) where no prior theoretical masses exist, guiding future experimental searches at facilities such as JLab, MAMI, and ELSA.
