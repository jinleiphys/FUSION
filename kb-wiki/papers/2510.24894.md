# Isotensor $πππ$ scattering with a $ρ$ resonant subsystem from QCD

> This work presents a lattice quantum chromodynamics (QCD) determination of $πππ$ scattering amplitudes for the isospin-2 channel with angular momentum and parity $J^{P} = 1^+$. The calculation is performed using unphysically heavy light-quark masses, corresponding to a pion mass of $m_π \approx 400$~MeV, for which the $ρ$ meson manifests as a narrow resonance. The analysis employs a previously developed formalism that non-perturbatively relates the finite-volume spectra of two- and three-pion systems to their infinite-volume scattering amplitudes. We combine earlier lattice QCD results for $ππ$ systems with isospins 1 and 2 with new determinations of the isospin-2 three-pion finite-volume spectrum, obtained for three cubic, periodic lattice volumes with box length ranging from $\approx 4/m_π$ to $6/m_π$. These combined data constrain the low-lying partial waves of the infinite-volume three-pion K matrix, from which we solve a set of coupled integral equations to extract the corresponding scattering amplitudes.

## Key claim
This work presents the first lattice QCD determination of a three-pion scattering amplitude with coupled resonant and non-resonant two-pion subchannels, specifically for the isotensor (isospin-2) channel with J^P = 1^+. The calculation is performed at unphysically heavy light-quark masses (m_pi ~ 400 MeV) where the rho meson is a narrow resonance, and the analysis relies solely on QCD, unitarity, and analyticity without imposing chiral effective theory constraints.

## Method
The authors computed finite-volume spectra for isospin-2 three-pion systems on three cubic, periodic lattice volumes with box lengths from approximately 4/m_pi to 6/m_pi, using distillation methods and generalized eigenvalue problems. They combined these new spectra with previously determined two-pion K matrices for isospin-1 and isospin-2 channels from Dudek (2012), then constrained the three-body K matrix by fitting to the finite-volume quantization condition. Finally, they solved coupled integral equations to extract infinite-volume scattering amplitudes and intensities.

## Key numbers
- Pion mass: m_pi ~ 400 MeV (unphysically heavy light-quark masses)
- Rho meson width: Gamma ~ 10 MeV at these quark masses
- Lattice volumes: m_pi L ~ 3.8, 4.8, 5.7 (three spatial extents)
- Temporal lattice spacing: a_t = 0.0348(3) fm
- Anisotropy: xi = a_s/a_t = 3.444(6)
- Rho Breit-Wigner mass: 2.184 m_pi (central value from Dudek (2012))
- Relevant thresholds: E^thr(K Kbar pi)/m_pi = 3.81, E^thr(omega pi pi)/m_pi = 4.25
- Energy values for Dalitz plots: E/m_pi = 3.3, 3.4, 3.5

## Context
This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of three-pion systems by Mai (2021), Yan (2024), and Yan (2025) by not imposing chiral perturbation theory constraints, and advances beyond the earlier calculation by Woss (2018) which studied heavier quark masses where the rho is stable. The formalism builds on the finite-volume framework developed by Hansen (2014, 2015, 2020) and the integral equation methods of Jackura (2023) and Briceno (2024). This enables future systematic studies of pion-mass dependence from m_pi ~ 700 MeV down to the physical point, and provides a template for analyzing other three-pion channels including those with resonances.
