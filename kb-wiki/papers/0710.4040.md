# Elastic Nd scattering at intermediate energies as a tool for probing the short-range deuteron structure

> A calculation of the deuteron polarization observables $A^d_y$, $A_{yy}$, $A_{xx}$, $A_{xz}$ and the differential cross-section for elastic nucleon-deuteron scattering at incident deuteron energies 270 and 880 MeV in lab is presented. A comparison of the calculations with two different deuteron wave-functions derived from the Bonn-CD $NN$-potential model and the dressed bag quark model is carried out. A model-independent approach, based on an optical potential framework, is used in which a nucleon-nucleon $T$-matrix is assumed to be local and taken on the energy shell, but still depends on the internal nucleon momentum in a deuteron.

## Key claim

Elastic nucleon-deuteron scattering at intermediate energies can discriminate between deuteron wave functions with a short-range node (from the dressed bag quark model) and those with uniform short-range depletion (from the Bonn-CD potential), but the differences become remarkable only at 880 MeV in the cross-section minimum region, while at 270 MeV the two calculations do not differ significantly and the disagreement with data is of the same order as the difference between the models.

## Method

The author used an optical potential formalism with a multiple scattering expansion including one-nucleon exchange, single-scattering, and double-scattering terms. The nucleon-nucleon T-matrix was taken on-shell but with an effective energy that depends on the internal nucleon momentum in the deuteron, so the T-matrix could not be factorized out of the integral. The scattering equation was solved in helicity basis using a K-matrix approximation, with NN T-matrices from the SP07 partial wave analysis up to total angular momentum J_NN=7.

## Key numbers

- Incident deuteron kinetic energies in lab: 270 MeV and 880 MeV
- Convergence of the partial wave sum achieved at total angular momentum J=25/2 for 270 MeV and J=39/2 for 880 MeV
- The dressed bag model deuteron wave function has a node in the ^3S_1 wave at a distance of approximately 0.6 fm
- The SP07 partial wave analysis extends to 3 GeV for pp scattering and 1.3 GeV for np scattering
- At 880 MeV, the two calculations give almost the same results only for scattering angles theta <= 50 degrees
- The dressed bag model contributes 3.6% from the 6q-bag to the deuteron wave function

## Context

This work extends the multiple scattering formalism of Alberi (1975) and Ladygina (2004) to test two different deuteron wave functions: the Bonn-CD potential model of Machleidt (2001) and the dressed bag model of Kukulin (2001). The approach is contrasted with Faddeev calculations that include three-nucleon forces of Fujita-Miyazawa or Tucson-Melbourne type, which are successful below 200 MeV but become computationally difficult at higher energies. The results at 270 MeV are compared with precise data from RIKEN (Sekiguchi, 2002), and the predictions at 880 MeV are made in view of a recent experiment at JINR (Ladygin, 2007). The work enables future experimental tests of short-range deuteron structure, though the author notes that three-nucleon force contributions from Delta-isobar excitation must be included for quantitative calculations at backward angles.
