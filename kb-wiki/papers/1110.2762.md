# How Self-Dual is QCD?

> Vacuum characteristics quantifying dynamical tendency toward self-duality in gauge theories could be used to judge the relevance of classical solutions or the viability of classically motivated vacuum models. Here we decompose the field strength of equilibrium gauge configurations into self-dual and anti-self-dual parts, and apply absolute X-distribution method to the resulting polarization dynamics in order to construct such characteristics. Using lattice regularization and focusing on pure-glue SU(3) gauge theory at zero temperature, we find evidence for positive but very small dynamical tendency for self-duality of vacuum in the continuum limit.

## Key claim

Using lattice regularization and the absolute X-distribution method, the authors find evidence for a positive but very small dynamical tendency toward self-duality in the vacuum of pure-glue SU(3) gauge theory at zero temperature, with the effect persisting in the continuum limit.

## Method

The field strength tensor of equilibrium gauge configurations was decomposed into self-dual and anti-self-dual parts. The absolute X-distribution method was applied to the resulting polarization dynamics, using overlap-based definitions of the field strength tensor on Wilson lattices with Iwasaki action. The correlation coefficient of polarization was computed for ensembles at fixed physical volume (1.32 fm)^4 across five lattice spacings, with an additional larger-volume ensemble to check finite-size effects.

## Key numbers

- Correlation coefficient of polarization in the continuum limit: C_A approximately 0.018
- Probability that a QCD-generated sample is more self-dual than a sample from statistically independent components exceeds equal chance by C_A/2 approximately 0.009
- Lattice spacings used: 0.110 fm (ensemble E2, 12^4), 0.0825 fm (E3, 16^4), 0.066 fm (E8, 20^4), 0.055 fm (E4, 24^4), 0.041 fm (E7, 32^4)
- Larger volume ensemble E6: 32^4 lattice at 0.055 fm spacing, physical volume (1.76 fm)^4
- Effective field strength at Lambda = 1000 MeV included 47 overlap near-zero modes on average

## Context

This work extends the absolute X-distribution method developed by Alexandru and Horvath (2010) to directly study self-duality in the gauge field, moving beyond earlier kinematical approaches by Horvath (2001) and Gattringer (2002) that could not determine the sign of dynamical tendency. The results are consistent with arguments by Witten (1979, 1998) and lattice studies by Horvath (2002, 2003, 2005) and Ilgenfritz (2007) indicating a non-classical paradigm of QCD topological charge fluctuations. The method provides a computationally accessible characteristic of classicality in 4-dimensional gauge theories, enabling future tests of semiclassical vacuum models.
