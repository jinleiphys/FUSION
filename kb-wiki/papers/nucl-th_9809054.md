# Production and decay of eta-mesic nuclei

> Using the Green function method, binding effects on produced eta-mesons in the two-stage reaction γ+ A \to N + η+ (A-1) \to N + (πN) + (A-2) are studied. The energy spectrum of the correlated pi-N pairs which arise from decays of etas inside the nucleus is strongly affected by an attractive eta-nucleus optical potential. Its resonant behavior gives a clear signal of formating intermediate eta-mesic nuclei.

# Production and decay of eta-mesic nuclei

## Key claim
Using the Green function method, the energy spectrum of correlated πN pairs from decays of η mesons inside the nucleus is strongly affected by an attractive η-nucleus optical potential, and its resonant behavior gives a clear signal of forming intermediate η-mesic nuclei, but the inclusive distribution of the total pair energy is smeared by the spread in nucleon separation energies and exhibits a single giant peak of width 40–50 MeV, rather than isolated resonances.

## Method
The two-stage reaction γ + A → N₁ + η + (A-1) → N₁ + (πN₂) + (A-2) was modeled using the Green function method, where the η propagates from its production point to its decay point in the mean field of the intermediate (A-1) nucleus. The ηN scattering amplitude from Greev (1997) was used with a first-order energy-dependent optical potential and a square-well nuclear density ρ(r) = 0.75 ρ₀ inside radius R_A = 1.2 A^{1/3} fm. The spectral function S(E_η), which characterizes the nuclear dependence of the total cross section for the two-step γ→η→π transition, was calculated to study how the η-nucleus attraction affects the pair production.

## Key numbers
- The average nucleon separation energy ⟨ε⟩ ≈ −23 MeV was used for the Fermi-gas average of hole states.
- The total cross section for η-mesic nucleus formation by photons is 5–10 μb for A = 12 to 16 (Trya 1995).
- The widths of η-nucleus bound states are Γ ∼ 20 MeV, far less than those found in an older work (Oset 1988) which overestimated broadening due to two-nucleon absorption ηNN → NN.
- The inclusive distribution of the total pair energy E_π + E_N₂ exhibits a single giant peak of width Γ ∼ 40–50 MeV.
- The nuclear density square-well radius is R_A = 1.2 A^{1/3} fm with ρ(r) = 0.75 ρ₀ inside.

## Context
This work extends the original suggestion by Sokol (1991) to observe stopped η mesons in nuclei via correlated πN pairs with opposite momenta, kinetic energies of about 300 MeV (pion) and 100 MeV (nucleon), and total energy close to m_η + m_N. It builds on earlier predictions that η-mesic nuclei exist for all A ≥ 3 (Raki 1996, Trya 1997) based on the S₁₁(1535) resonance coupling to the ηN channel, and on experimental evidence from reactions like p+d → ³He+η (Wilk 1993, Will 1997). The calculation uses a potential that gives ground-state energies and widths close to those found by Kulp (1998), and the predicted pairs have been recently observed in an experiment at the Lebedev Institute (LPI 1998). Further analysis of the energy distribution may reveal whether η-mesic nuclei were really found.
