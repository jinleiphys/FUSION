# Systematic study for particle transverse momentum asymmetry in minimum bias pp collisions at LHC energies

> In PYTHIA6 (PYTHIA8) once the transverse momentum $p_T$ of a generated particle is randomly sampled, $p_x$ and $p_y$ are set on the circle with radius of $p_T$ randomly. This may largely suppress the development of the final hadronic state transverse momentum anisotropy from the initial state spatial asymmetry. We modify PYTHIA6.4 by randomly setting $p_x$ and $p_y$ on the circumference of an ellipse with the half major and minor axes being $p_T(1+δ_p)$ and $p_T(1-δ_p)$, respectively. The modified PYTHIA6.4 is then employed to systematically study the charged particle transverse momentum asymmetry in the minimum bias pp collisions at $\sqrt s$=0.9, 7, and 14 TeV. The ALICE data on the transverse sphericity as a function of charged multiplicity, $<S_T>(N_{ch})$, are well reproduced with the modified PYTHIA6.4. It is found that the predicted charged particle $v_2$ upper limit is a measurable value, $\sim 0.12$, in the minimum bias pp collisions at $\sqrt s$=7 TeV. We suggest a systematic measurement for the particle transverse momentum sphericity, eccentricity (ellipticity), and elliptic flow parameter.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the standard PYTHIA6 (and PYTHIA8) treatment of randomly setting the transverse momentum components \(p_x\) and \(p_y\) on a circle of radius \(p_T\) suppresses the development of final-state transverse momentum anisotropy from initial-state spatial asymmetry. By instead randomly placing \(p_x\) and \(p_y\) on an ellipse with deformation parameter \(\delta_p\), they reproduce ALICE sphericity data and predict a measurable charged particle elliptic flow \(v_2\) upper limit of \(\sim 0.2\)–\(0.3\) in minimum bias pp collisions at LHC energies, though the exact value depends on the phase space and collision energy.

## Method

The authors modified PYTHIA6.4 by replacing the standard random assignment of \(p_x\) and \(p_y\) on a circle of radius \(p_T\) with a random assignment on an ellipse having half-major axis \(a = p_T(1+\delta_p)\) and half-minor axis \(b = p_T(1-\delta_p)\), where \(\delta_p\) is a fitted deformation parameter. They then simulated minimum bias pp collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 0.9\), 7, and 14 TeV, fitting \(\delta_p\) to reproduce the ALICE data on the event-averaged transverse sphericity \(\langle S_T \rangle\) as a function of charged multiplicity \(N_{ch}\). The resulting modified PYTHIA6.4 (denoted PYTHIA6_1) was used to compute the elliptic flow parameter \(v_2\) as a function of pseudorapidity \(\eta\) and transverse momentum \(p_T\), as well as the \(p_T\)-averaged \(v_2\) upper limit.

## Key numbers

- The deformation parameter \(\delta_p\) was fitted to be 0.092 for \(\sqrt{s} = 0.9\) TeV and 0.091 for \(\sqrt{s} = 7\) TeV, reproducing the ALICE \(\langle S_T \rangle(N_{ch})\) data.
- The charged particle \(v_2\) upper limit (partial phase space: \(p_T \geq 0.5\) GeV/c, \(|\eta| \leq 0.8\)) from PYTHIA6_1 is 0.297 at \(\sqrt{s} = 0.9\) TeV, 0.275 at \(\sqrt{s} = 7\) TeV, and 0.267 at \(\sqrt{s} = 14\) TeV.
- The charged particle \(v_2\) upper limit (full \(p_T\) and \(\eta\) phase space) from PYTHIA6_1 is 0.291 at \(\sqrt{s} = 0.9\) TeV, 0.243 at \(\sqrt{s} = 7\) TeV, and 0.237 at \(\sqrt{s} = 14\) TeV.
- The ALICE data show that the event-averaged transverse sphericity increases from 0.613 at \(\sqrt{s} = 0.9\) TeV to 0.700 at \(\sqrt{s} = 7\) TeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of hadronic event shapes in pp collisions at LHC energies, following measurements by CMS, ALICE, and ATLAS. It directly addresses the failure of standard PYTHIA6 and PYTHIA8 to reproduce the ALICE sphericity data, which had previously been best described by the PERUGIA-2011 tune (Skands 2011). The modified PYTHIA6_1 provides a simpler alternative that reproduces the sphericity data and yields predictions for the elliptic flow parameter \(v_2\), which is a key observable in heavy-ion collisions for characterizing the quark-gluon plasma. The authors suggest simultaneous measurement of sphericity and elliptic flow to cross-check results, noting that different \(v_2\) extraction methods (event plane, cumulant, Lee-Yang zeros) can yield discrepancies of 10–100%.
