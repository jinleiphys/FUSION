# Covariant relativistic separable kernel approach for electrodisintegration of the deuteron at high momentum transfer

> The paper considers the electrodisintegration of the deuteron for kinematic conditions of the JLab experiment E-94-019. The calculations have been performed within the covariant Bethe-Salpeter approach with a separable kernel of nucleon-nucleon interactions. The results have been obtained using the relativistic plane wave impulse approximation and compared with experimental data and other models. The influence of nucleon electromagnetic form factors has been investigated.

## Key claim

The covariant Bethe-Salpeter approach with a rank-six separable kernel (MY6) describes the exclusive electrodisintegration of the deuteron at high momentum transfer in reasonable agreement with JLab experiment E-94-019 data, but only within the plane wave impulse approximation and with the caveat that final state interactions, negative-energy partial-wave states, and two-body currents have not yet been included.

## Method

The authors solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the deuteron bound state using a rank-six separable kernel (MY6) for the nucleon-nucleon interaction. The exclusive cross section for the d(e,e'n)p reaction was calculated in the relativistic plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA) using the Mandelstam technique for the hadron current, with the outgoing nucleons treated as non-interacting. Calculations were performed for four values of momentum transfer (Q^2 = 2, 3, 4, 5 GeV^2) and compared with experimental data from JLab experiment E-94-019, as well as with results from the nonrelativistic Graz II and relativistic Graz II separable kernels.

## Key numbers

- Cross sections were calculated at Q^2 = 2 ± 0.25 GeV^2, 3 ± 0.5 GeV^2, 4 ± 0.5 GeV^2, and 5 ± 0.5 GeV^2 for the JLab experiment E-94-019 kinematics.
- Good agreement with experimental data was found for neutron momenta |p_n| < 0.25 GeV/c across all Q^2 values.
- The relativistic models (MY6 and Graz II) showed improved agreement with data at high neutron momenta, with theoretical curves running practically along experimental points at Q^2 = 5 GeV^2.
- The influence of the ^3D_1^+ partial-wave state on the cross section was shown to increase at high neutron momenta, while the ^3S_1^+ state dominates at low and medium neutron momenta.

## Context

This work extends the covariant Bethe-Salpeter approach with a separable kernel (Bondarenko 2008, 2009) to the electrodisintegration of the deuteron at high momentum transfer, where relativistic effects are expected to be important. It compares results with the nonrelativistic Graz II model (Mathelitsch 1981) and the relativistic Graz II model (Rupp 1989), and references the Paris potential calculations (Lacombe 1980) used in the original JLab experiment analysis (Egiyan 2007). The MY6 model enables future inclusion of final state interactions without constraining the zeroth component of relative momentum p_0, which is not possible with the Graz II kernel.
