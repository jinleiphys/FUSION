# Heavy Quarkonia in a Potential Model: Binding Energy, Decay Width, and Survival Probability

> Recently a lot of progress has been made in deriving the heavy quark potential within a QCD medium. In this article we have considered heavy quarkonium in a hot quark gluon plasma phase. The heavy-quark potential has been modeled properly for short as well as long distances. The potential at long distances is modeled as a QCD string which is screened at the same scale as the Coloumb field. We have numerically solved the 1+1-dimensional Schrodinger equation for this potential and obtained the eigen wavefunction and binding energy for the $1S$ and $2S$ states of charmonium and bottomonium. Further, we have calculated the decay width and dissociation temperature of quarkonium states in the QCD plasma. Finally, we have used our recently proposed unified model with these new values of decay widths to calculate the survival probability of the various quarkonium states with respect to centrality at relativistic heavy ion collider (RHIC) and large hadron collider (LHC) energies. This study provides a unified, consistent and comprehensive description of spectroscopic properties of various quarkonium states at finite temperatures along with their nuclear modification factor at different collision energies.

## Key claim

The authors claim that a unified model incorporating a complex heavy-quark potential (with both screened Coulomb and string terms) can consistently describe the spectroscopic properties and nuclear modification factors of charmonium and bottomonium states at finite temperature, but this agreement depends on using numerically obtained temperature-dependent wavefunctions rather than analytic hydrogenic wavefunctions for calculating decay widths.

## Method

The authors numerically solved the 1+1-dimensional Schrodinger equation using the real part of a medium-modified heavy-quark potential that includes both a Coulomb term and a string term screened at the same Debye mass scale. They obtained eigenfunctions and binding energies for the 1S and 2S states of charmonium and bottomonium, then calculated thermal decay widths by folding the imaginary part of the potential with these numerically obtained wavefunctions. Finally, they used a unified kinetic model based on the Boltzmann equation, incorporating gluo-dissociation, collisional damping, and recombination, together with 1+1-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics, to compute survival probabilities as a function of centrality at RHIC and LHC energies.

## Key numbers

- Charmonium masses: m_J/psi = 3.1 GeV for J/psi, m_Upsilon = 9.2 GeV for Upsilon
- String tension: sigma = 0.184 GeV^2
- Lambda_MSbar = 0.1 GeV
- Critical temperature: T_C = 160 MeV
- QGP lifetime: tau_0 = 0.5 fm to tau_f = 6.0 fm
- Cross sections for LHC at sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV: sigma_cc^NN = 3.546 mb, sigma_bb^NN = 0.1105 mb
- Cross sections for RHIC at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV: sigma_cc^NN = 0.346 mb, sigma_bb^NN = 0.01035 mb
- Real part of potential saturates at 0.4 GeV at T=160 MeV and 0.05 GeV at T=480 MeV

## Context

This work extends prior potential model studies by Agotiya et al. (2008) and Thakur et al. (2013, 2016) by incorporating both the real and imaginary parts of a complex heavy-quark potential with a non-vanishing string tension above T_C, consistent with lattice QCD results showing a crossover transition. The authors build on their own unified model from Singh et al. (2015) and compare with experimental data from the STAR collaboration at RHIC (Adamczyk 2012, 2014) and the ALICE collaboration at LHC (Murray 2012). The approach enables calculation of the double ratio [psi'/J/psi]_PbPb/[psi'/J/psi]_pp, which previous suppression models had failed to reproduce.
