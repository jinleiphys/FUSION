# Nature of X(3872) from recent BESIII data: Considering the universal feature of an S-wave threshold resonance

> We analyze the recent data from the BESIII collaboration on the $X(3872)$ state in the $J/ψπ^+π^-$ and $D^0\bar{D}^0π^0$ decay channels. The quantum number and mass of the $X(3872)$ state allow us to exploit the universal feature of the very near-threshold $D\bar D^*$ scattering in the $S$ wave. The analysis of $J/ψπ^+π^-$ data and $D^0\bar{D}^0π^0$ data separately as well as the combined analysis of these data together, all support the conclusion that $X(3872)$ is an extremely weakly bound charm meson molecule.

# Wiki Page: Nature of X(3872) from BESIII Data

## Key claim
The X(3872) state is an extremely weakly bound charm meson molecule (a bound state of D0 and D*0), with the simultaneous fit of BESIII data yielding a binding energy of only 0.03 MeV. This conclusion holds under the assumption that the universal feature of S-wave near-threshold D D* scattering applies, where the scattering length dominates the effective range expansion.

## Method
The authors analyzed BESIII data on the J/ψπ+π- and D0D0π0 invariant mass distributions near the X(3872) resonance. They employed a nonrelativistic scattering amplitude for S-wave D0D*0 scattering, parameterized by a complex inverse scattering length γ = γre + iγim, and included the finite D*0 width (Γ*0 = 0.066 MeV). The line shapes for both decay channels were derived from this amplitude, and separate fits as well as a simultaneous fit to both datasets were performed using maximum likelihood, with experimental energy resolution and linear backgrounds taken into account.

## Key numbers
- Simultaneous fit: γre = 8.2 ± 5.6 MeV, giving a binding energy EX = 0.03 MeV (with asymmetric uncertainty +0.03/-0.03 MeV)
- Corresponding scattering length: 29.5 ± 14.8 fm (compared to 5.4 fm for nucleon-nucleon scattering in the 3S1 wave)
- D*0 width used: Γ*0 = 0.066 ± 0.015 MeV
- Reduced mass of D*0 and D0: μ = 966.6 MeV
- D0D*0 mass threshold: mth = 3871.69 MeV
- X(3872) mass from PDG: 3871.64 ± 0.06 MeV
- Separate fit to J/ψπ+π- data (γim = 0): γre = 35.0 ± 10.3 MeV, binding energy EX = 0.63 MeV (+0.32/-0.43 MeV)
- Separate fit to D0D0π0 data: γre = 11.3 ± 9.4 MeV, binding energy EX = 0.07 MeV (+0.06/-0.16 MeV)

## Context
This work extends the universal scattering-length approach of Braaten (2007, 2009) to new BESIII data from 2023, which includes a true D0D0π0 invariant mass distribution rather than the D0D*0 mass-constrained distribution used in earlier analyses by Braaten (2009) and Kang (2016). The results support the molecular interpretation of X(3872) and contradict scenarios where it would be a virtual state (γre < 0). The analysis enables future inclusion of coupled-channel effects from D+D*- (8 MeV away), which could describe the line shape over a larger energy region and potentially reveal a c-cbar component as discussed in Kang (2016).
