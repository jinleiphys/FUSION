# Mixing of the low-lying three- and five-quark $Ω$ states with negative parity

> Mixing of the low-1/2^{-}$ and $3/2^{-}$ is investigated, employing an instanton-induced quark-antiquark pair creation model, which precludes transitions between $s^{3}$ and $s^{4}\bar{s}$ configurations. Models with hyperfine interactions between quarks of three different kinds, namely, one-gluon-exchange, Goldstone-boson-exchange (GBE) and an instanton-induced interaction (INS) are called OGE, GBE and INS models, respectively. Numerical results show that the instanton-induced pair creation causes strong mixing between the three- and five-quark configurations with spin 3/2, and that this mixing decreases the energy of the lowest spin 3/2 states in all three different hyperfine interaction models to $\sim 1750\pm50$ MeV. On the other hand, transition couplings between $s^3$ and $s^{3}q\bar{q}$ states with spin 1/2 caused by instanton-induced $q\bar{q}$ creation is very small and the resulting mixing of three- and five-quark configurations in the OGE and INS models is negligible, while the mixing of the spin 1/2 states in GBE model is not, but effects of this mixing on energies of mixed states are also very small. Accordingly, the lowest $Ω$ states with negative parity in all three hyperfine interactions models have spin 3/2.

## Key claim

The instanton-induced pair creation mechanism causes strong mixing between three- and five-quark configurations for spin 3/2 negative-parity Ω states, lowering the energy of the lowest spin 3/2 states to approximately 1750±50 MeV across all three hyperfine interaction models; however, for spin 1/2 states the transition couplings are very small (less than 20 MeV), leading to negligible mixing in the OGE and INS models and only small energy shifts in the GBE model despite non-negligible mixing there.

## Method

The authors constructed a Hamiltonian matrix mixing three-quark (s³) and five-quark (s³q\bar{q}) configurations for Ω states with negative parity, using three different hyperfine interaction models: one-gluon-exchange (OGE), Goldstone-boson-exchange (GBE), and instanton-induced (INS). The transition coupling between three- and five-quark configurations was computed using a non-relativistic reduction of the instanton-induced quark-antiquark pair creation interaction, which can create q\bar{q} pairs with quantum numbers ¹S₀ and ³S₁ (unlike the ³P₀ model which does not contribute here). The diagonal matrix elements for the three-quark sector were taken from prior work by Chao (1980) and Glozman (1995) for OGE and GBE models, and computed for the INS model using empirical parameters that reproduce the Ω(1672) ground state mass.

## Key numbers

- The lowest spin 3/2 negative-parity Ω states in all three hyperfine models lie at approximately 1750±50 MeV (with the fixed-parameter results giving 1727 MeV for OGE, 1767 MeV for INS, and 1773 MeV for GBE)
- The three-quark diagonal matrix elements for spin 1/2 and 3/2 states are: 2020 MeV (OGE model), 1991 MeV (GBE model), and 1887 MeV (INS model)
- Absolute values of transition matrix elements V₃₅ for spin 3/2 configurations are in the range 100±20 MeV, while for spin 1/2 configurations they are less than 20 MeV
- In the GBE model, the diagonal matrix element of the third five-quark configuration differs from the three-quark configuration by only 19 MeV
- The instanton-induced interaction strength used is g′ ≈ 33.3 MeV, and the oscillator parameter for five-quark configurations is ω₅ ≈ 196 MeV (from the relation ω₅ = √(5/6) ω₃)
- Varying the ratio R₃₅ = ω₅/ω₃ from 0.5 to 2 changes energies by up to 160 MeV, with the lowest states remaining within ~1750±50 MeV

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the s³Q\bar{Q} five-quark spectrum by An, Metsch, and Zou (2013) by now including the mixing between three- and five-quark Fock components, which was previously neglected. The instanton-induced pair creation mechanism used here differs from the traditional ³P₀ model employed by Le Yaouanc (1972) and others for meson and baryon decays, and from the string-breaking models of Dosch (1986) and Alcock (1983). The results show that five-quark components are essential for understanding negative-parity Ω resonances, predicting that the lowest such states have spin 3/2 rather than 1/2, which can guide experimental searches for Ω resonances beyond the four known states reported by Barnes (1982), Biagi (1985), Aston (1987, 1988).
