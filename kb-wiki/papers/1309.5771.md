# High-resolution probes of low-resolution nuclei

> Renormalization group (RG) methods used to soften Hamiltonians allow large-scale computational resources to be used to greater advantage in calculations of nuclear structure and reactions. These RG transformations lower the effective resolution of the nuclei, which raises questions about how to calculate and interpret high-momentum transfer probes of nuclear structure. Such experiments are conventionally explained in terms of short-range correlations, but these disappear with the evolution to low-momentum scales. We highlight the important issues and prospects in the context of recent developments in RG technology, with guidance from the analogous extraction of parton distributions.

## Key claim

Renormalization group (RG) transformations that soften nuclear Hamiltonians and lower the effective resolution of nuclei raise questions about how to calculate and interpret high-momentum transfer probes of nuclear structure; the conventional explanation in terms of short-range correlations (SRCs) disappears with evolution to low-momentum scales, but the cross section must be unchanged because the transformation is unitary, so the physics shifts between structure and reaction mechanism.

## Method

The paper uses the similarity renormalization group (SRG) to continuously evolve nuclear Hamiltonians and operators via unitary transformations toward more diagonal form in momentum representation, decoupling low- and high-momentum modes. It examines how the SRG evolution of the Hamiltonian, current operators, and wave functions must be treated consistently to preserve cross sections, and applies the operator product expansion (OPE) to demonstrate that the unitary transformation factorizes into a product of functions depending separately on low and high momenta when the momenta are well separated. The analysis uses the extraction of parton distribution functions from deep inelastic scattering as a paradigm for understanding scale and scheme dependence in nuclear observables.

## Key numbers

- SRG evolution from \(\lambda = \infty\) (initial high-resolution potential) down to \(\lambda = 1.5\ \text{fm}^{-1}\) (lowest resolution) eliminates the high-momentum tail of the deuteron momentum distribution identified with SRC physics.
- Off-diagonal matrix elements of chiral EFT NN potentials evolved by SRG to \(\lambda = 5\ \text{fm}^{-1}\) (left) and \(\lambda = 1.5\ \text{fm}^{-1}\) (right) show suppression of off-diagonal strength for \(k > \lambda\) and flow to universal values.
- The deuteron momentum distribution at \(q \approx 3.0\ \text{fm}^{-1}\) shows negligible amplitude at small \(\lambda\) from the original one-body operator, with a smooth contribution at low momenta from the induced two-body operator.
- Ratios of momentum distributions in nuclei to the deuteron are almost flat in the high momentum region above \(k = 2\ \text{fm}^{-1}\).
- \(U_\lambda\)-factorization tests in the \(^3\)S\(_1\) channel show plateaus close to one (at the 10–15% level) for curves with \(k_i < \lambda\) in the \(q > \lambda\) region.

## Context

This work extends the application of renormalization group methods to nuclear structure, building on prior developments in SRG evolution of Hamiltonians reviewed by Bogner (2009), Roth (2012), and Furnstahl (2013). It addresses the apparent contradiction between the success of low-momentum Hamiltonians in nuclear structure calculations and the interpretation of high-momentum transfer electron scattering experiments in terms of short-range correlations, as discussed by Arrington (2011) and Alvioli (2012). The paper enables future quantitative calculations of operator evolution using recently developed technology for three-body force evolution by Roth (2012), Hebeler (2012), and Wendt (2013), and suggests that the linear correlation between the A-dependence of scaling ratios and the slope of the EMC effect observed by Arrington (2011) could be understood through \(U_\lambda\)-factorization and subsequent cancellations in cross section ratios.
