# A discussion of deuteron transverse charge densities

> The deuteron transverse charge density $ρ_C(b)$ is the two-dimensional Fourier transform of its charge form factor in the impact space. We show that different parameterizations of the charge form factors provide different $ρ_C(b)$, in particular at the central value of impact parameter ($b=0$), although all the parameterizations can well reproduce the form factors in the region of small $Q^2$. In addition, we also check the explicit contributions from the different coordinate intervals of the deuteron wave function to its root-mean-square radius.

# Deuteron transverse charge densities

## Key claim

Different parameterizations of the deuteron charge form factor, although all reproducing the measured form factors well at low momentum transfer $Q^2$, yield very different transverse charge densities $\rho_C(b)$ at small impact parameter $b$, particularly at $b=0$, where one parameterization gives a divergent result while others give finite values between 0.72 and 1.42 fm$^{-2}$.

## Method

The authors compute the deuteron transverse charge density $\rho_C(b)$ as the two-dimensional Fourier transform of the charge form factor $G_C(Q^2)$ in the Drell-Yan frame, using four different phenomenological parameterizations of $G_C(Q^2)$ from Tomasi-Gustafsson (2004) that incorporate isoscalar vector meson ($\omega$, $\phi$) contributions. They decompose the Fourier integral into separate $Q$-intervals (0-1, 1-2, 2-10, 10-$\infty$ GeV) to identify which momentum regions dominate the density at each $b$. Separately, they use the Paris nucleon-nucleon potential wave function to compute the deuteron root-mean-square radius and decompose its contributions by radial intervals.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron binding energy is $\epsilon_D \sim 2.22$ MeV.
- At $b=0$, the four parameterizations give $\rho_C(0)$ values of: divergent (Scheme I), 0.72 fm$^{-2}$ (Scheme II), 1.42 fm$^{-2}$ (Scheme III), and 0.90 fm$^{-2}$ (Scheme IV).
- The deuteron root-mean-square radius from the Paris wave function is $r_d = 1.966$ fm.
- The squared radius $r_d^2$ receives contributions of $1.720\times 10^0$ fm$^2$ from the radial interval $r=5-10$ fm, $6.107\times 10^{-1}$ fm$^2$ from $r=10-20$ fm, and $1.994\times 10^{-2}$ fm$^2$ from $r=20-\infty$ fm.
- The normalization values at $Q^2=0$ are $G_C(0)=1$, $G_Q(0)=25.83$, and $G_M(0)=1.714$.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Miller (2007, 2009) on nucleon and pion transverse charge densities to the deuteron, a spin-1 system. It parallels the finding for the pion that different form factor parameterizations that agree at low $Q^2$ give different $\rho(b=0)$ values. The analysis uses the parameterization scheme of Tomasi-Gustafsson (2004) and the Paris N-N potential wave function of Lacombe (1981). The results motivate future experimental measurements of deuteron form factors at large $Q^2$ to constrain the transverse charge density at small $b$, and highlight that the deuteron root-mean-square radius receives sizeable contributions from the wave function at large distances ($r > 5$ fm), analogous to the proton radius puzzle discussed by Sick (2003) and Pohl (2010).
