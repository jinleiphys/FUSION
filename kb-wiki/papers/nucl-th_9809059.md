# On the treatment of $NN$ interaction effects in meson production in $NN$ collisions

> We clarify under what circumstances the nucleon--nucleon final state interaction fixes the energy dependence of the total cross--section for the reaction $NN \to NNx$ close to production threshold, where $x$ can be any meson whose interaction with the nucleon is not too strong. We strongly criticize the procedure used recently by several authors to include the final state interaction in the reactions under discussion. In addition, we give a formula that allows one to estimate the effect of the initial state interaction for the production of heavy mesons.

## Key claim

The energy dependence of the total cross-section for \(NN \to NNx\) near threshold is indeed fixed by the on-shell nucleon-nucleon final state interaction (FSI) as Watson argued, but the strength (normalization) of the cross-section also depends on off-shell effects contained in a function \(\mathcal{P}(E,p')\) that cannot be neglected; therefore, simply multiplying a tree-level production amplitude by the on-shell \(NN\) \(T\)-matrix is unjustified for quantitative predictions.

## Method

The authors decompose the total transition amplitude \(A\) into a production amplitude \(M\) and the \(NN\) FSI, then derive an expression for \(A\) in terms of the on-shell \(NN\) \(T\)-matrix, the phase shifts \(\delta(p')\), the inelasticity \(\eta(p')\), and a function \(\mathcal{P}(E,p')\) that encodes all off-shell effects of both the \(NN\) interaction and the production amplitude. They analyze the conditions under which the energy dependence of the cross-section follows the on-shell FSI formula, and use a separable-potential model in an appendix to demonstrate the regularization dependence of \(\mathcal{P}\) and the necessity of consistent treatment of production and scattering amplitudes. For the initial state interaction (ISI), they derive a compact formula for the reduction factor \(\lambda\) in terms of the on-shell \(NN\) phase shifts and inelasticities.

## Key numbers

- For the reaction \(pp \to pp\eta\), inclusion of the ISI in a meson-exchange model leads to a reduction of the total cross-section by roughly a factor of **0.3** (reference to Lee, 1996).
- At threshold for \(\eta\) production, the \(^3P_0\) initial state has phase shift \(\delta_L(p) = -60.7^\circ\) and inelasticity \(\eta_L(p) = 0.57\) at \(T_{Lab} = 1250\) MeV (from the model used by Lee, 1996).
- Using these values, the reduction factor \(\lambda\) defined in eq. (isieff) is **0.2**.
- For the separable potential model in the PDS scheme with \(\mu \simeq m_\pi\), the function \(\mathcal{P} \simeq 6\) (using the \(pp\) scattering length \(a = -7.8\) fm).

## Context

This work extends and criticizes the approach of several authors (Moalem, 1996; Mosel, 1996; Sibirtsev, 1996; etc.) who used the on-shell \(NN\) \(T\)-matrix to include FSI in meson production, arguing that this procedure is insufficient for quantitative predictions. It clarifies the conditions of Watson's (1952) original theorem, showing that attractive FSI is not required for the energy dependence to follow the on-shell formula. The derived formula for ISI effects enables future theoretical studies of heavy meson production (e.g., \(\eta\), \(\eta'\), \(K\)) near threshold, where the initial state interaction becomes important.
