# In-medium meson effects on the equation of state of hot and dense nuclear matter

> The influence of the in-medium mesons on the effective nucleon mass and in turn on the equation of state of hot/dense nuclear matter is discussed in the Walecka model. Due to the self-consistent treatment of couplings between nucleons and $σ$ and $ω$ mesons, the temperature and density dependence of the effective hadron masses approaches more towards the Brown-Rho scaling law, and the compression modulus $K$ is reduced from $550 MeV$ in mean field theory to an accepted value $318.2 MeV$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including the self-consistent back-reaction of in-medium sigma and omega mesons on the nucleon propagator (rather than using bare meson masses) softens the equation of state of hot and dense nuclear matter within the original renormalizable Walecka model, reducing the compression modulus from 550 MeV in mean field theory to 318.2 MeV, which falls within the accepted range of 300 +/- 50 MeV.

## Method

The authors use the Walecka model (QHD-I) with nucleons interacting through sigma and omega meson exchange. They solve the coupled Dyson-Schwinger equations for the nucleon and meson propagators self-consistently: the nucleon self-energy is computed in the relativistic Hartree approximation (RHA) using in-medium meson propagators, while the meson self-energies are computed in the random phase approximation (RPA) using the full nucleon propagator. The off-shell meson masses (defined at zero four-momentum transfer) are used in the nucleon self-energy equations and the equation of state, forming a closed set of equations solved simultaneously at finite temperature and density.

## Key numbers

- Compression modulus K = 318.2 MeV at normal nuclear density rho_0 = 0.1484 fm^-3 at T=0, reduced from 547.2 MeV in MFT and 452.6 MeV in RHA.
- Effective nucleon mass ratio M_N*/M_N = 0.803 at normal density, compared to 0.540 in MFT and 0.731 in RHA.
- Coupling constants in the self-consistent approach: g_sigma^2 = 42.229, g_omega^2 = 69.729, with m_sigma = 458 MeV and m_omega = 783 MeV in vacuum.
- Critical temperature for liquid-gas phase transition T_c is about 21 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the standard mean field theory (MFT) and relativistic Hartree approximation (RHA) treatments of the Walecka model, which gave compression moduli of 550 MeV and 450 MeV respectively, both above the accepted range. The approach is motivated by Bhattacharyya et al. (1999) who pointed out that in-medium meson effects were not reflected in the effective nucleon mass in previous calculations. The results show that the temperature and density dependence of effective hadron masses approaches the Brown-Rho scaling law, consistent with the hidden local symmetry theory of Harada and Yamawaki (2001) and with recent works by Brown and Song (2002). The paper enables more realistic equation of state calculations for hot and dense nuclear matter relevant to heavy-ion collisions and neutron stars, using the original renormalizable QHD-I without introducing nonlinear sigma terms or additional parameters.
