# Hot Nuclear Matter Equation of State with a Three-body Force

> The finite temperature Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach is extended by introducing a microscopic three-body force. In the framework of the extended model, the equation of state of hot asymmetric nuclear matter and its isospin dependence have been investigated. The critical temperature of liquid-gas phase transition for symmetric nuclear matter has been calculated and compared with other predictions. It turns out that the three-body force gives a repulsive contribution to the equation of state which is stronger at higher density and as a consequence reduces the critical temperature of liquid-gas phase transition. The calculated energy per nucleon of hot asymmetric nuclear matter is shown to satisfy a simple quadratic dependence on asymmetric parameter $β$ as in the zero-temperature case. The symmetry energy and its density dependence have been obtained and discussed. Our results show that the three-body force affects strongly the high-density behavior of the symmetry energy and makes the symmetry energy more sensitive to the variation of temperature. The temperature dependence and the isospin dependence of other physical quantities, such as the proton and neutron single particle potentials and effective masses are also studied. Due to the additional repulsion produced by the three-body force contribution, the proton and neutron single particle potentials are correspondingly enhanced as similar to the zero-temperature case.

## Key claim

The finite-temperature Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (FTBHF) approach is extended by incorporating a microscopic three-body force (TBF) based on meson-exchange currents; the TBF provides a repulsive contribution that grows with density, stiffens the high-density equation of state, and reduces the critical temperature of the liquid-gas phase transition in symmetric nuclear matter from about 16 MeV to about 13 MeV.

## Method

The authors solve the finite-temperature Bethe-Goldstone equation self-consistently using the Argonne V18 two-body potential plus an effective two-body interaction derived from a microscopic three-body force. The TBF, constructed from meson-exchange currents (pi, rho, sigma, omega) with the sigma mass fixed at 540 MeV, is transformed into a temperature-dependent effective two-body force via the Fermi distribution and the defect function. The coupled equations for the G-matrix, single-particle potentials, chemical potentials, and densities are iterated to convergence for given density, temperature, and isospin asymmetry.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature of liquid-gas phase transition for symmetric nuclear matter: approximately 16 MeV without TBF, reduced to about 13 MeV with TBF.
- The TBF reduction of the critical temperature is 3 MeV (from 16 MeV to 13 MeV).
- The sigma meson mass in the TBF is fixed at 540 MeV.
- Symmetry energy is calculated at densities up to 0.32 fm^-3 and temperatures of 0, 10, and 20 MeV.
- Single-particle potentials and effective masses are computed at density 0.16 fm^-3 for temperatures 0, 10, and 20 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the finite-temperature Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach of Bombaci, Kuo, and Lombardo (1994) by adding a microscopic three-body force, following the zero-temperature TBF implementation of Grange et al. (1989) and Zuo et al. (2002). The calculated critical temperature of 13 MeV lies between the non-relativistic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock predictions of 15-20 MeV (Jaqaman, Mekjian, and Zamick, 1983) and the Dirac-Brueckner results of about 8-10 MeV (ter Haar and Malfliet, 1986; Huber, Weber, and Weigel, 1998). The results enable improved modeling of supernova dynamics and protoneutron star structure by providing a TBF-inclusive equation of state for hot asymmetric nuclear matter.
