# Transverse Takahashi Identities and Their Implications for Gauge Independent Dynamical Chiral Symmetry Breaking

> In this article, we employ transverse Takahashi identities to impose valuable non-perturbative constraints on the transverse part of the fermion-photon vertex in terms of new form factors, the so called $Y_i$ functions. We show that the implementation of these identities is crucial in ensuring the correct local gauge transformation of the fermion propagator and its multiplicative renormalizability. Our construction incorporates the correct symmetry properties of the $Y_i$ under charge conjugation operation as well as their well-known one-loop expansion in the asymptotic configuration of incoming and outgoing momenta. Furthermore, we make an explicit analysis of various existing constructions of this vertex against the demands of transverse Takahashi identities and the previously established key features of quantum electrodynamics, such as gauge invariance of the critical coupling above which chiral symmetry is dynamically broken. We construct a simple example in its quenched version and compute the mass function as we vary the coupling strength and also calculate the corresponding anomalous dimensions $γ_m$. There is an excellent fit to the Miransky scalling law and we find $γ_m=1$ rather naturally in accordance with some earlier results in literature, using arguments based on Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis effective potential technique. Moreover, we numerically confirm the gauge invariance of this critical coupling.

## Key claim

The transverse Takahashi identities (TTIs) impose non-perturbative constraints on the transverse part of the fermion-photon vertex in terms of new form factors called \(Y_i\) functions, and the implementation of these identities is crucial for ensuring the correct local gauge transformation of the fermion propagator and its multiplicative renormalizability; the authors construct a quenched example and find an excellent fit to the Miransky scaling law with \(\gamma_m = 1\), numerically confirming the gauge invariance of the critical coupling for dynamical chiral symmetry breaking.

## Method

The authors decompose the fermion-photon vertex into longitudinal and transverse parts using the Ball-Chiu basis, then apply the transverse Takahashi identities to express the eight transverse form factors \(\tau_i\) in terms of the \(Y_i\) functions and the fermion propagator. They impose multiplicative renormalizability constraints on the \(Y_i\) in the leading logarithm approximation, enforce charge conjugation symmetry, and require the correct one-loop asymptotic expansion. Using these constraints, they construct a simple quenched vertex ansatz, solve the fermion gap equation (Schwinger-Dyson equation for the propagator) numerically, and compute the mass function and anomalous dimension \(\gamma_m\) as a function of coupling strength.

## Key numbers

- The anomalous dimension \(\gamma_m = 1\) is found naturally, in accordance with earlier results using the Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis effective potential technique.
- The critical coupling for dynamical chiral symmetry breaking is numerically confirmed to be gauge invariant (no specific numerical value for the critical coupling is given in the truncated text).
- The one-loop expansion of the wave function renormalization yields \(F(k^2) = 1 + \beta \log(k^2/\Lambda^2)\) with \(\beta = \alpha \xi/(4\pi)\), where \(\xi\) is the gauge-fixing parameter.

## Context

This work extends the program of constraining the fermion-photon vertex using transverse Takahashi identities, building on the foundational vertex decomposition of Ball and Chiu (1980) and the TTI formalism developed by Takahashi (1985), Kondo (1996), and He (2000, 2006, 2007). It also connects to earlier studies of multiplicative renormalizability constraints on the transverse vertex by Parker (1984), Delbourgo (1984), and Cornwall (1981), as well as the Curtis-Pennington vertex constructions (1990-1993). The gauge invariance of the critical coupling for dynamical chiral symmetry breaking has been used previously as a constraint by Bashir (2011) and others; this work provides a systematic TTI-based framework that naturally incorporates that requirement. The results enable improved truncations of Schwinger-Dyson equations for QED and provide a benchmark for analogous studies of the quark-gluon vertex in QCD.
