# Final State Charge Exchange Interactions in the $^{12}C(e,e'p)$ Reaction

> The $^{12}C(e,e'p)$ reaction is analyzed in a model which explicitly includes final state interactions due to the coupling of the proton and neutron emission channels. We find that the effects of the final state interactions due to charge exchange reactions are important to get a good description of the symmetry properties of the recently measured Mainz spectral functions. We discuss the possible role the off-shell effects may play for the correct interpretation of spectral functions at large positive missing momenta.

# Final State Charge Exchange Interactions in the $^{12}C(e,e'p)$ Reaction

## Key claim
Final state interactions due to charge exchange reactions, specifically the coupling between proton and neutron emission channels, are important for describing the symmetry properties of the measured Mainz spectral functions for $^{12}C(e,e'p)$, but the model cannot resolve the approximately 30% difference in absolute normalization between the Mainz, NIKHEF, and Saclay data sets.

## Method
The authors analyzed the $^{12}C(e,e'p)$ reaction using a continuum random phase approximation that explicitly includes the coupling of the $^{12}C(e,e'p)$ and $^{12}C(e,e'n)$ decay channels, with both direct and Pauli exchange diagrams iterated to infinite order. The residual interaction was derived from a one-boson exchange potential, and calculations were performed under the precise kinematic conditions of the Mainz experiment, where both the kinetic energy of the outgoing proton and the momentum transfer were varied.

## Key numbers
- The spectral function averaged over missing momenta from 77.5 MeV/c to 92.5 MeV/c is 34.0 (GeV/c)$^{-3}$, while the average for missing momenta from -92.5 to -107.5 MeV/c is 30.09 (GeV/c)$^{-3}$, giving a ratio of 1.13 for positive to negative missing momenta in the Mainz data.
- The ratio of the maxima of the spectral function for positive and negative missing momenta is 1.14 for the NIKHEF data, 1.10 in the authors' approach, and 1.30 in the mean field calculation.
- An additional factor of 0.76 is needed to describe the Mainz data when using the spectroscopic factor from Cohen and Kurath that described the NIKHEF data well.
- The incident electron energy was 855.1 MeV, with outgoing proton kinetic energies varied from 82 MeV to 118 MeV in the Mainz experiment.
- The effect of proton-neutron coupling is particularly large near the maximum of the spectral function at missing momentum $p_m$ = 100 MeV/c, corresponding to a momentum transfer of approximately 280 MeV/c.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of final state interactions in $(e,e'p)$ reactions previously suggested by Jeschonnek, Krewald, and Szczurek (1995) and addresses discrepancies found in standard distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) analyses of NIKHEF data by van der Steenhoven and collaborators (1990). The model differs from the simple mean field Lane potential approach of SBTM (1987) by using a microscopic approach with realistic nucleon-nucleon residual interactions. The results are relevant for interpreting forthcoming quasi-elastic neutrino scattering data on carbon, as discussed by Garvey and Musolf, and for disentangling short range correlations from final state interactions at higher missing momenta.
