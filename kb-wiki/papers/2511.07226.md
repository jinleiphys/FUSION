# Renormalization-Group Invariant Parity-Doublet Model for Nuclear and Neutron-Star Matter

> The Parity-Doublet Model (PDM) is a chirally invariant effective theory for strong-interaction matter involving nucleons and their opposite-parity partners in a parity-doubling framework. We introduce a multiplicatively renormalizable mean-field approach to include the baryonic vacuum contributions to the resulting grand-canonical potential in an explicitly renormalization-group invariant form. As an application, we evaluate the pertinent thermodynamics of two-flavor symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter, focusing on the restoration of spontaneously broken chiral symmetry at baryon densities and temperatures relevant for the astrophysics of neutron stars. Special attention is paid to the effect of the baryonic vacuum fluctuations on the evolution of chiral condensate with baryon density and temperature for specific choices of the chirally invariant baryon mass $m_0$ to demonstrate the importance of consistently including these vacuum fluctuations in the PDM.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including baryonic vacuum fluctuations in a multiplicatively renormalizable mean-field treatment of the Parity-Doublet Model (PDM) is crucial for the evolution of the chiral condensate with baryon density and temperature, with the results depending on the specific choice of the chirally invariant baryon mass \(m_0\) (ranging between 500 and 800 MeV).

## Method

The authors construct a two-flavor Parity-Doublet Model with nucleons \(N(939)\) and their negative-parity partner \(N^*(1535)\) in a mirror-assignment framework, including Yukawa couplings to sigma and pion fields, a chirally invariant mass term \(m_0\), and vector repulsion via omega and rho meson fields. They regularize and renormalize the divergent fermionic vacuum contribution to the grand-canonical potential using an RG-invariant scheme analogous to that applied to the quark-meson model by Brandt (2025), expressing the mesonic potential as a polynomial in the parity-partner masses \(m_\pm(\phi)\) up to eighth order. Model parameters are fixed by fitting to vacuum phenomenology (pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV, nucleon mass 939 MeV, \(N^*\) mass 1510 MeV) and nuclear saturation properties (density, binding energy, compressibility, symmetry energy) using a hybrid global-local minimization.

## Key numbers

*   Nuclear saturation density: \(n_0 = 0.157 \pm 0.010\) fm\(^{-3}\).
*   Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: \(E_B = 15.97 \pm 0.40\) MeV.
*   Pion decay constant: \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV.
*   Nucleon mass: \(m_N = 939\) MeV; \(N^*(1535)\) mass: \(m_{N^*} = 1510\) MeV.
*   Pion mass: \(m_\pi = 135\) MeV.
*   Chirally invariant baryon mass range considered: \(500\;\text{MeV} \leq m_0 \leq 860\;\text{MeV}\).
*   Vector meson masses used: \(m_\omega = 783\) MeV, \(m_\rho = 776\) MeV.
*   Sigma-meson curvature mass parameter allowed to vary between 400 and 550 MeV.
*   Yukawa coupling difference fixed by vacuum masses: \(g_2 - g_1 = (m_{N^*} - m_N)/f_\pi \approx 6.2\).

## Context

This work extends previous two-flavor studies by Eser (2023, 2024) in a one-loop renormalized mean-field approximation by introducing a novel multiplicatively renormalizable treatment of fermionic vacuum fluctuations that ensures explicit renormalization-group invariance of the grand-canonical potential. The RG-invariant formulation is analogous to that used by Brandt (2025) for the quark-meson model, which successfully described lattice QCD results at finite isospin density. The results enable improved modeling of chiral symmetry restoration in dense matter relevant for neutron-star astrophysics, including the equation of state, mass-radius relations, and tidal deformabilities.
