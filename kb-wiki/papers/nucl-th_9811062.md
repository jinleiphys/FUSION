# The pion photoproduction in the Δ(1232) region

> We investigate the pion photoproduction in the Δ(1232) region in the framework of an effective Lagrangian including pions, nulceon and Δ(1232). We work to third order in a small scale expansion with both $m_π$ and $M_Δ-M_N$ treated as light scales. We note that in the $Δ$ region, straightward power counting breaks as the amplitudes become very large, to deal with this problem, we suggest that the appropriate way to compare theoretical calculations with experimental data is via weighted integrals of the amplitudes through the $Δ$ region.

## Key claim

The paper argues that standard power counting in chiral perturbation theory with explicit Δ(1232) degrees of freedom breaks down for pion photoproduction amplitudes in the Δ resonance region because the Δ propagator diverges near the pole; the authors propose that meaningful comparison between theory and experiment can only be made via weighted integrals of the amplitudes through the Δ region rather than direct pointwise comparison.

## Method

The authors construct an effective chiral Lagrangian including pions, nucleons, and Δ(1232) fields, working to third order in the "small scale expansion" where both the pion mass mπ and the mass difference Δ ≡ MΔ − MN are treated as light scales of order ε. They use heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) with the Rarita-Schwinger formalism for the spin-3/2 Δ field, compute one-loop and Born graphs up to O(ε³), and renormalize the theory using dimensional regularization. The calculation is performed in the center-of-mass frame with Coulomb gauge (ε·v=0).

## Key numbers

- The mass difference Δ ≡ MΔ − MN is approximately 300 MeV, comparable to mπ (∼140 MeV) within a factor of about 2
- The reported experimental value for the ratio REM = ImE1+^{3/2}/ImM1+^{3/2} at the Δ(1232) peak is REM = −(2.5 ± 0.2 ± 0.2)% from Mainz measurements
- The "speed plot" technique applied to the amplitude of Tiator et al. gives RΔ ≡ rE e^{iφE} / (rM e^{iφM}) = −0.035 − 0.046i
- Th. Wilbois et al. found RΔ = −0.040 − 0.047i using a more sophisticated implementation
- Nonrelativistic constituent quark models with gluon exchange predict REM ranging between −0.008% and −2%
- Cloudy bag models give −2% < REM < 0
- Skyrme and hedgehog models give −5.9% < REM < −2.5%
- Exchange current models yield REM = −3.5%

## Context

This work extends the small scale expansion formalism developed by Hemmert, Holstein, and Kambor (1998) for including Δ(1232) as an explicit degree of freedom in chiral perturbation theory, and applies it to pion photoproduction in the Δ region. The paper addresses the long-standing problem of separating resonant and non-resonant contributions in the γN → Δ transition, which has been treated by various model-dependent approaches including dynamical models solving Lippmann-Schwinger equations (Sato and Lee, 1996; Teshima and Ono, 1997) and speed plot techniques (Höhler et al., 1997). The proposed method of comparing weighted integrals rather than pointwise amplitudes enables future tests of chiral dynamics once spin-dependent measurements allow extraction of individual amplitudes from experimental data.
