# Dissipative effects on the propagation of spin modes

> In relativistic hydrodynamics with spin, following de Groot--van Leeuwen--van Weert's energy-momentum and spin tensor definitions, we analyze the propagation of spin degrees of freedom. We deduce an analytical formula for spin wave velocity, finding that it approaches half the speed of light in the ultra-relativistic limit. Only transverse degrees of freedom propagate, similar to electromagnetic waves. Additionally, we explore dissipative effects and determine the damping coefficients for Maxwell-Jüttner statistics.

## Key claim
The paper derives an analytical formula for the spin wave velocity in relativistic spin hydrodynamics, finding that it approaches exactly half the speed of light in the ultra-relativistic limit, but only for transverse spin degrees of freedom; longitudinal components do not propagate at all. The analysis is restricted to the de Groot--van Leeuwen--van Weert (GLW) tensor definitions and assumes an unpolarized background fluid where spin perturbations are small.

## Method
The authors linearize the conservation equations for the spin tensor around a stationary, unpolarized fluid background, using the GLW decomposition of the spin tensor into phenomenological and auxiliary parts. They derive the dispersion relation for spin modes by requiring the divergence of the total spin tensor to vanish, obtaining a wave equation for the transverse components of the spin polarization vectors. Dissipative effects are incorporated via the relaxation time approximation, leading to damping coefficients for both longitudinal and transverse modes.

## Key numbers
- Spin wave velocity in the ultra-relativistic limit (z = m/T → 0): c_spin = 1/2 (exactly half the speed of light), independent of statistics.
- Spin wave velocity in the non-relativistic limit (z >> 1): c_spin ≈ 1/√(2z), which approaches zero for cold massive gases.
- For Maxwell-Jüttner statistics, c_spin² = (1/4) × K₃(z) / [K₃(z) + (z/2) K₂(z)], independent of chemical potential ξ.
- Small-z expansion: c_spin = 1/2 × [1 - z²/16 + O(z⁴)].
- The spin wave speed satisfies 0 < c_spin ≤ 1/2, monotonically decreasing with increasing mass-to-temperature ratio z.

## Context
This work extends the perfect-fluid spin hydrodynamics framework developed by Florkowski et al. (2018) and Florkowski et al. (2019) by including dissipative effects from Bhadury et al. (2020). The spin wave analysis builds on earlier linear perturbation studies by Ambrus et al. (2017) and provides a foundation for understanding spin transport in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, where Λ hyperon polarization measurements by STAR (2017, 2019) and ALICE (2019) have motivated the development of spin hydrodynamics. The results enable future studies of spin wave propagation in dissipative media and tests of stability in spin hydrodynamic theories.
