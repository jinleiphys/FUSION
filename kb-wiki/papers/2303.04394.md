# Analytic continuation of the relativistic three-particle scattering amplitudes

> We investigate the relativistic scattering of three identical scalar bosons interacting via pair-wise interactions. Extending techniques from the non-relativistic three-body scattering theory, we provide a detailed and general prescription for solving and analytically continuing integral equations describing the three-body reactions. We use these techniques to study a system with zero angular momenta described by a single scattering length leading to a bound state in a two-body sub-channel. We obtain bound-state--particle and three-particle amplitudes in the previously unexplored kinematical regime; in particular, for real energies below elastic thresholds and complex energies in the physical and unphysical Riemann sheets. We extract positions of three-particle bound-states that agree with previous finite-volume studies, providing further evidence for the consistency of the relativistic finite-volume three-body quantization conditions. We also determine previously unobserved virtual bound states in this theory. Finally, we find numerical evidence of the breakdown of the two-body finite-volume formalism in the vicinity of the left-hand cuts and argue for the generalization of the existing formalism.

## Key claim

The authors extend the relativistic three-body scattering formalism to real energies below elastic thresholds and into the complex energy plane, demonstrating that the integral equations can be analytically continued to extract bound-state, virtual-state, and resonance poles, but caution that the two-body finite-volume (Lüscher) formalism breaks down in the presence of left-hand cuts from one-particle exchange, requiring generalization.

## Method

The authors solve the partial-wave projected (S-wave, total angular momentum J=0) integral equation for the amputated ladder amplitude d_S(p,k) of three identical scalar bosons, using a separable two-body amplitude with a single scattering length that produces a two-body bound state (dimer). They perform analytic continuation of the integral equation into the complex energy plane by deforming the integration contour in momentum space to avoid singularities of the one-particle exchange propagator and the two-body amplitude, following techniques from Brayshaw (1968) and Glöckle (1978). Bound-state positions are found by solving the homogeneous version of the integral equation via a determinant condition, and virtual states are located by continuing the φ+b amplitude to the unphysical Riemann sheet using the known unitarity relation.

## Key numbers

* Two-body bound-state mass: m_b² = 4(m² − κ²), where κ = 1/a and a is the scattering length (positive for bound state).
* Two-body coupling: g = 8√(2π √σ_b κ), where σ_b = m_b².
* The φ+b phase space: ρ_{φb}(s) = q_b/(8π√s), with q_b = λ^{1/2}(s, σ_b, m²)/(2√s).
* The integration cutoff: q_max = (s − m²)/(2√s).

## Context

This work extends the numerical solution of the relativistic three-body integral equations from Jackura (2020), which only treated energies above the φ+b threshold, into the previously unexplored regime below that threshold and into the complex plane. It directly validates the three-body bound-state positions found in the finite-volume study of Romero-Lopez (2019), but reveals a discrepancy in the φ+b amplitude below the φ+b threshold attributed to left-hand cuts from the one-particle exchange, indicating a breakdown of the Lüscher formalism in that region. The analytic continuation techniques follow the non-relativistic methods of Brayshaw (1968) and Glöckle (1978), and the results enable future studies of three-body resonances and virtual states in relativistic systems, including applications to lattice QCD.
