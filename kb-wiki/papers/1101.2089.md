# Quantum simulations of thermodynamic properties of strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma

> A strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma (QGP) of heavy constituent quasi-particles is studied by a path-integral Monte-Carlo method. This approach is a quantum generalization of the model developed by Gelman, Shuryak and Zahed. It is shown that this method is able to reproduce the QCD lattice equation of state and also yields valuable insight into the internal structure of the QGP. The results indicate that the QGP reveals liquid-like rather than gas-like properties. At temperatures just above the critical one it was found that bound quark-antiquark states still survive. These states are bound by effective string-like forces and turns out to be colorless. At the temperature as large as twice the critical one no bound states are observed. Quantum effects turned out to be of prime importance in these simulations.

## Key claim
The paper claims that a path-integral Monte-Carlo method, which is a quantum generalization of the classical molecular dynamics model by Gelman, Shuryak and Zahed, can reproduce the QCD lattice equation of state for a strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma (QGP) of heavy constituent quasi-particles. The key hedge is that this agreement is achieved only after fitting the quasi-particle density as a free parameter to match the lattice pressure, and the chosen parameter set remains debatable.

## Method
The authors performed path-integral Monte-Carlo (PIMC) simulations of a three-component QGP consisting of dressed quarks, antiquarks, and gluons interacting via a color Coulomb potential, with the SU(3) color group replaced by SU(2) for technical simplicity. The quasi-particle mass was taken from a parametrization fitting lattice data from Lattice02 and LiaoShuryak, and quantum effects were included via the color Kelbg potential, which removes classical divergences at short distances. The simulations were carried out in the canonical ensemble at zero baryon density with equal numbers of quarks, antiquarks, and gluons, and the quasi-particle density was chosen to optimize agreement with the lattice equation of state.

## Key numbers
- The critical temperature used is \(T_c = 175\) MeV.
- The quasi-particle mass parametrization is \(m(T)/T_c = 0.9/(T/T_c - 1) + 3.45 + 0.4 T/T_c\), giving masses such that \(m_q/T \simeq 4\) and \(m_g/T \simeq 3.5\) at lattice points from Lattice02.
- The fitted quasi-particle density is \(n(T) = 0.24 T^3\), which is much lower than the ideal gas density of massless particles (\(n(T)/T^3 = 6.3\)) used in the classical simulations of Gelman, Shuryak and Zahed.
- The degeneracy parameter \(\chi_a = n_a \lambda_a^3\) varies from 0.1 to 1.7 over the studied temperature range \(1 < T/T_c < 3\).
- The plasma coupling parameter \(\Gamma\) is of order unity, indicating a strongly coupled Coulomb liquid.

## Context
This work extends the classical non-relativistic molecular dynamics model of the QGP developed by Gelman, Shuryak and Zahed by incorporating quantum effects through a path-integral Monte-Carlo method, which self-consistently accounts for Fermi and Bose statistics. The approach was previously successfully applied to strongly coupled electrodynamic plasmas by Filinov (2001) and Bonitz (2003), including partially-ionized dense hydrogen plasmas and electron-hole plasmas in semiconductors. The results enable future studies of the internal structure and non-equilibrium dynamics of the QGP, and the authors plan to apply a grand-canonical approach with more realistic parameter sets to remove the need for fitting the density.
