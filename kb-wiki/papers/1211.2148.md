# Understanding the property of $η(1405/1475)$ in the $J/ψ$ radiative decay

> In this work we make a systematic analysis of the correlated processes $J/ψ\to γη(1440)/f_1(1420)$ with $η(1440)/f_1(1420)\to K\bar{K}π$, $ηππ$ and $3π$, where the role played by the so-called "triangle singularity mechanism" (TSM) is clarified. Our results agree well with the experimental data and suggest a small fraction of $f_1(1420)$ contributions in these processes. This study confirms our conclusion in [Phys. Rev. Lett. 108, 081803 (2012)] that the dynamic feature of the TSM can be recognized by the strong narrow peak observed in the $ππ$ invariant mass spectrum of $η(1440)\to 3π$ with anomalously large isospin violations. Nevertheless, we explicitly demonstrate that the TSM can produce obvious peak position shifts for the same $η(1440)$ or $f_1(1420)$ state in different decay channels. This is a strong evidence that the $η(1405)$ and $η(1475)$ are actually the same state, i.e. $η(1440)$. We also make an analysis of the radiative decays of $η(1440)\to γV$ ($V=ϕ$, $ρ^0$ or $ω$) which shows that such a one-state prescription seems not to have a conflict with the so-far existing experimental data. Our analysis may shed a light on the long-standing puzzling question on the nature of $η(1405)$ and $η(1475)$.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the two previously thought distinct resonances, η(1405) and η(1475), are actually the same state (η(1440)), with the apparent mass difference arising from peak position shifts caused by the triangle singularity mechanism (TSM) in different decay channels, rather than from two separate physical states.

## Method

The authors performed a systematic analysis of J/ψ → γη(1440)/f₁(1420) with subsequent decays to K\bar{K}π, ηππ, and 3π, using effective Lagrangians and helicity amplitude formalisms to model the production and decay processes. They calculated triangle loop integrals involving intermediate K*K rescatterings using both LoopTools without form factors and an exponential form factor method, then fitted the resulting invariant mass spectra and angular distributions to experimental data from BESIII.

## Key numbers

- The branching ratio ratio BR(η(1405)→f₀(980)π⁰→3π)/BR(η(1405)→a⁰₀(980)π⁰→ηππ) = (17.9 ± 4.2)% from BESIII data, indicating anomalously large isospin violation
- The f₀(980) signal width observed in BESIII data is about 10 MeV, compared to the Breit-Wigner width of 40-100 MeV
- A constant width Γ(f₁(1420)) = 0.0549 GeV is adopted for f₁(1420)
- Coupling constants used: g_{aK\bar{K}} = 3.33 GeV, g_{aηπ} = 2.45 GeV, g_{K*Kπ}^n = 3.208, g_{K*Kπ}^c = 3.268
- The hadron scale parameter Q₀ = 0.197321/R GeV with R = 0.35 fm (radius of J/ψ)

## Context

This work extends the triangle singularity mechanism (TSM) proposed by Wu et al. (2011) in Phys. Rev. Lett. 108, 081803 (2012) to explain the anomalously large isospin violation in η(1405/1475)→3π observed by BESIII (2012). The study contradicts the interpretation of η(1405) and η(1475) as two separate states, which was the conventional assignment in the Particle Data Group review (Nakamura 2010). The analysis also addresses the role of f₁(1420) contributions, which had been a confounding factor in previous experimental analyses. This work enables a unified description of the η(1405/1475) puzzle and provides a framework for understanding similar triangle singularity effects in other meson decays.
