# Beam Fragmentation in Heavy Ion Collisions with Realistically Correlated Nuclear Configurations

> We develop a new approach to production of the spectator nucleons in the heavy ion collisions. The energy transfer to the spectator system is calculated using the Monte Carlo based on the updated version of our generator of configurations in colliding nuclei which includes a realistic account of short-range correlations in nuclei. The transferred energy distributions are calculated within the framework of the Glauber multiple scattering theory, taking into account all the individual inelastic and elastic collisions using an independent realistic calculation of the potential energy contribution of each of the nucleon-nucleon pairs to the total potential. We show that the dominant mechanism of the energy transfer is tearing apart pairs of nucleons with the major contribution coming from the short-range correlations. We calculate the momentum distribution of the emitted nucleons which is strongly affected by short range correlations including its dependence on the azimuthal angle. In particular, we predict a strong angular asymmetry along the direction of the impact parameter b, providing a unique opportunity to determine the direction of b. Also, we predict a strong dependence of the shape of the nucleon momentum distribution on the centrality of the nucleus-nucleus collision.

## Key claim

The paper claims that short-range correlations (SRC) between nucleons are the dominant mechanism for energy transfer to the spectator system in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions, and that this produces a strong azimuthal asymmetry in nucleon emission that can be used to determine both the magnitude and sign of the impact parameter vector **b**, going beyond current methods that only determine |**b**|.

## Method

The authors used a Monte Carlo event generator based on the Glauber multiple scattering theory, incorporating realistic nucleon configurations with state-dependent short-range correlations (spin, isospin, and central) generated via a Metropolis method. They calculated the potential energy freed by instantaneous removal of inelastically interacting nucleons using the Koltun sum rule analogy, and modeled nucleon emission from the spectator system including contributions from correlated pairs, surface emission, elastic scattering, and evaporation.

## Key numbers

- In Pb-Pb collisions at P_Lab = 160 GeV, the fraction of total potential energy carried by proton-neutron (pn) pairs is 83%, compared to 8% for pp pairs, when full correlations are included.
- The ratio of isospin 0 to isospin 1 pair contributions to the potential energy is 74% for I=0 and 26% for I=1.
- The Glauber parameters used for NA49 energy were sigma^{tot}_{NN} = 39 mb and B = 13.59 GeV^2.
- Transferred energy calculations were performed for impact parameters from b=0 to b=12 fm, with results shown for Pb-Pb collisions at 160 GeV per nucleon.
- The energy dependence of transferred energy was compared for NA49, RHIC, and LHC energies, showing weak dependence for average impact parameters but a drop at small impact parameters with increasing energy due to increased sigma^{tot}_{NN}.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous program of including short-range correlations in nuclear configurations (Alvioli 2009) by adding spin and isospin dependence, and applies it to heavy ion collisions at NA49 energies. It contrasts with the abrasion-ablation model of Scheidenberger (2004) which treated excitation energy as an input parameter, and with mean-field approaches where pp and pn contributions are proportional to pair numbers. The results enable event-by-event determination of impact parameter direction and centrality from nucleon emission patterns, which is feasible at RHIC where directed flow v1 for neutrons has been observed by PHENIX (Afanasiev 2009) and STAR (Adams 2005, Abelev 2008).
