# pT distribution of hyperons in 200A GeV Au-Au by smoothed particle hydrodynamics

> The transverse momentum distributions for hadrons in 200GeV Au-Au collision at RHIC is calculated using a smoothed particle hydrodynamics code SPheRIO, and are compared with the data from STAR and PHOBOS Collaborations. By employing the equation of state which explicitly incorporate the strangeness conservation and introducing strangeness chemical potential into the code, the transverse spectrums give a reasonable description for the experimental data.

## Key claim

The smoothed particle hydrodynamics code SPheRIO, using an equation of state that incorporates strangeness conservation and a strangeness chemical potential, gives a reasonable description of the transverse momentum spectra for hyperons in 200A GeV Au-Au collisions, except that the calculated multiplicities of hyperons show visible discrepancies compared to experimental data.

## Method

The authors used the SPheRIO smoothed particle hydrodynamics code to solve relativistic hydrodynamic equations for 200A GeV Au-Au collisions at RHIC. Initial conditions were generated with the NEXUS event generator, with a rescaling factor to fix the pseudorapidity distribution and an additional transverse velocity term. The equation of state employed a hadronic resonance model with finite volume correction for the hadronic phase and an ideal gas model for the quark-gluon plasma phase, with strangeness conservation enforced and a strangeness chemical potential introduced for hyperons and strange mesons.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out temperature set to \(T_f = 110\) MeV, with a radial velocity parameter \(\alpha = 0.04\) fm\(^{-1}\), used to reproduce the pseudorapidity distribution of all charged particles for most central Au-Au collisions at 200A GeV.
- Transverse momentum distributions of \(\Lambda\), \(\Xi\), and \(\Omega\) hyperons were calculated for most central Au-Au collisions at 200A GeV with rapidity interval \(-1.0 < \eta < 1.0\) and compared to STAR Collaboration data.

## Context

This work extends previous hydrodynamic studies by the Sao Paulo - Rio de Janeiro Collaboration using the SPheRIO code, which had investigated initial-condition fluctuations and continuous emission decoupling (Qian et al., 2006; Grassi et al., 2006). The results indicate that the system may undergo chemical freeze-out at a higher temperature than thermal freeze-out, a mechanism previously discussed by Hirano and Tsuda (2002) and others, which could improve the hyperon multiplicity predictions. The authors note that for Pb-Pb collisions at 158A GeV, the difference between equations of state with and without strangeness becomes more significant at high rapidity (Kodama et al., 2005), and they hope similar data will become available for Au-Au at RHIC.
