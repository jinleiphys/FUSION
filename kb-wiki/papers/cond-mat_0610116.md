# Resonant Dimer Relaxation in Cold Atoms with a Large Scattering Length

> Efimov physics refers to universal phenomena associated with a discrete scaling symmetry in the 3-body problem with a large scattering length. The first experimental evidence for Efimov physics was the recent observation of a resonant peak in the 3-body recombination rate for 133Cs atoms with large negative scattering length. There can also be resonant peaks in the atom-dimer relaxation rate for large positive scattering length. We calculate the atom-dimer relaxation rate as a function of temperature and show how measurements of the relaxation rate can be used to determine accurately the parameters that govern Efimov physics.

## Key claim
The paper calculates the atom-dimer relaxation rate as a function of temperature and shows that measurements of this rate can determine the Efimov parameters κ* and η* accurately, but the universal results are strictly valid only in the scaling limit where the range of the interaction is negligible compared to the scattering length |a|.

## Method
The authors derive the inelastic atom-dimer cross section using an effective range expansion truncated after the effective range term, valid for collision energies E ≪ E_D where E_D = ħ²/(ma²). They then compute the thermal average of the inelastic cross section using Boltzmann statistics to obtain the dimer relaxation event rate β as a function of temperature. The effects of deep dimers are incorporated by making the complex substitution ln a* → ln a* - iη*/s0 in the universal expressions for the atom-dimer scattering length and effective range.

## Key numbers
- The discrete scaling factor for identical bosons is e^(π/s0) ≈ 22.7, where s0 ≈ 1.00624.
- The binding energies of Efimov states at a = ±∞ are given by E_T^(n) = (e^(2π/s0))^(n*-n) ħ²κ*²/m, with successive bound states shallower by a factor e^(2π/s0) ≈ 515.
- The universal atom-dimer scattering length is a_AD = (1.46 + 2.15 cot[s0 ln(a/a*)]) a.
- The universal atom-dimer effective range is r_s,AD = (1.13 + 0.73 cot[s0 ln(a/a*) + 0.98])² a.
- In the low-temperature limit, the dimer relaxation rate reduces to β = [20.3 sinh(2η*)] / [sin²(s0 ln(a/a*)) + sinh²η*] × (ħ a / m).
- For the ¹³³Cs experiment, the observed resonance occurs at a ≈ 400 a₀, giving a temperature scale T* = 8145 nK, with measurements carried out near 250 nK.
- The value η* = 0.06(1) was measured for large negative a in the Innsbruck experiment.

## Context
This work extends the universal theory of Efimov physics for identical bosons, previously developed by Efimov (1970, 1971, 1979) and Braaten and Hammer (2003, 2004), to include finite-temperature effects on the atom-dimer relaxation rate. It directly addresses the experimental observations by the Innsbruck group (Grimm 2006) of resonant inelastic losses in ¹³³Cs atoms, where a resonance in atom-dimer collisions was observed near a ≈ 400 a₀ (FB18Santos). The results enable accurate determination of the Efimov parameters κ* and η* from measurements at nonzero temperature, which can then be used to make universal predictions for other Efimov phenomena such as local minima and maxima in the 3-body recombination rate.
