# Empirical Proton-Neutron Interactions and Nuclear Density Functional Theory: Global, Regional and Local Comparisons

> Calculations of nuclear masses, using nuclear density functional theory, are presented for even-even nuclei spanning the nuclear chart. The resulting binding energy differences can be interpreted in terms of valence proton-neutron interactions. These are compared globally, regionally, and locally with empirical values. Overall, excellent agreement is obtained. Discrepancies highlight neglected degrees of freedom and can point to improved density functionals.

## Key claim

Nuclear density functional theory (DFT) calculations of the proton-neutron interaction indicator δVpn, derived from binding energy differences, can reproduce empirical values to well under 100 keV in many mass regions, despite the average error of calculated binding energies being several MeV; the agreement is best in deformed regions where mean-field theory captures essential physics.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using a transformed harmonic oscillator basis spanning 20 major shells, with an approximate Lipkin-Nogami treatment of pairing followed by exact particle number projection. They employed the SkP and SLy4 Skyrme functionals in the particle-hole channel with a density-dependent delta interaction for pairing, studying over 1000 even-even nuclei across the nuclear chart. The δVpn values were computed from the double difference of binding energies of four neighboring even-even nuclei, requiring very high numerical accuracy.

## Key numbers

- The average δVpn values follow Eq. (3) with SkP values of asym = 30 MeV and assym = -45 MeV for the symmetry and surface-symmetry energy coefficients
- DFT calculations reproduce empirical δVpn values to significantly better than 100 keV in many mass regions
- Standard functionals reproduce total binding energies with an rms error of 1.5 to 4 MeV
- For Cd and U isotopes, agreement with experiment is within a few to 10's of keV for both SkP and SkPV functionals
- The predicted mass excess for 236Th using the DFT approach is -46325 keV, compared to the extrapolation from systematics of -46454 keV
- Differences in δVpn between SkP and SkPV pairing treatments are essentially zero, while mass differences between the two calculations range up to nearly an MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier empirical studies of δVpn patterns by Cakirli, Casten, and collaborators (2005) that revealed bifurcations near closed shells and systematic patterns across major shells, as well as correlations with collectivity growth rates. The DFT calculations provide the first large-scale microscopic test of these empirical systematics, confirming that the surface symmetry term is important and that δVpn values are small in the currently data-free lower right quadrant of the major shell Z=50-82, N=82-126. The approach enables predictions for unknown nuclei when three of the four masses needed for a δVpn value are known, and discrepancies at magic numbers, N=Z nuclei, and in the octupole-correlated Ra region point to needed improvements in density functionals.
