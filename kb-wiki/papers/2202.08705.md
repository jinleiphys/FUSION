# Effects of isoscalar- and isovector-scalar meson mixing on neutron star structure

> Based on the accurately calibrated interaction FSUGold, we show that including isovector scalar $δ$ meson and its coupling to isoscalar scalar $σ$ meson in the relativistic mean field (RMF) model can soften the symmetry energy $E_{\rm{sym}}(n)$ at intermediate densities while stiffen the $E_{\rm{sym}}(n)$ at high densities. We find this new RMF model can be simultaneously compatible with (1) the constraints on the equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter at suprasaturation densities from flow data in heavy-ion collisions, (2) the neutron skin thickness of $^{208}$Pb from the PREX-II experiment, (3) the largest mass of neutron star (NS) reported so far from PSR J0740+6620, (4) the limit of $Λ_{1.4}\leq580$ for the dimensionless tidal deformability of the canonical 1.4$M_{\odot}$ NS from the gravitational wave signal GW170817, (5) the mass-radius relation of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620 measured by NICER, and thus remove the tension between PREX-II and GW170817 observed in the conventional RMF model.

## Key claim

By adding the isovector scalar δ meson and its coupling to the isoscalar scalar σ meson to the FSUGold relativistic mean field (RMF) model, the authors show that the symmetry energy can be softened at intermediate densities (around 2-3 times saturation density) while stiffened at high densities, which allows the model to simultaneously satisfy constraints from PREX-II, GW170817, NICER, and heavy-ion collision flow data, removing the tension between PREX-II and GW170817 that exists in conventional RMF models.

## Method

The authors start from the accurately calibrated FSUGold RMF interaction and extend its Lagrangian density to include the δ meson and a δ-σ mixing term (C_{δσ}). They fix the seven bulk nuclear matter parameters (n_0, e_0, K_0, J_0, m*_Dirac, E_sym,c, and L_c) to values consistent with PREX-II and heavy-ion collision flow constraints, then vary g_δ and C_{δσ} to explore the effects on the symmetry energy and neutron star properties. The equations of motion for the meson fields are solved self-consistently in the mean-field approximation, and the resulting equation of state is used to compute neutron star structure, including the core-crust transition density obtained via the thermodynamical method.

## Key numbers

- Neutron skin thickness of 208Pb from PREX-II: Δr_np = 0.283 ± 0.071 fm
- FSU-J0 model (without δ meson) gives M_max = 1.93 M_⊙ and Λ_1.4 = 576, but cannot reach the observed 2.08 M_⊙ for PSR J0740+6620
- FSU-δ6.7 model (g_δ = 6.7, C_{δσ} = 0.0385) gives Λ_1.4 = 578.5 and M_max = 2.05 M_⊙
- FSU-δ6.2 model (g_δ = 6.2, C_{δσ} = 0.054) gives Λ_1.4 = 420 and M_max = 2.10 M_⊙
- The constraint from GW170817 requires Λ_1.4 ≤ 580
- The observed mass of PSR J0740+6620 is 2.08^{+0.07}_{-0.07} M_⊙
- The symmetry energy slope at subsaturation cross density n_c = 0.11/0.16 n_0 is L_c = 55 MeV for the new models, consistent with the PREX-II constraint L_c ≳ 52 MeV

## Context

This work extends the study of δ-σ mixing by Zabari et al. (2019), who first showed this mixing could soften the symmetry energy at intermediate densities but whose parameter sets violated constraints from heavy-ion collision flow data and predicted Λ_1.4 larger than 580. The present work builds on the FSUGold interaction (Todd-Rutel and Piekarewicz, 2005) and also incorporates ω meson self-interactions and ρ-ω coupling that were absent in the earlier study. The new RMF model enables simultaneous compatibility with multimessenger constraints (PREX-II, GW170817, NICER, and heavy-ion flow data) that were previously in tension within conventional RMF models, and provides a framework for further studies of neutron star structure and dense matter properties.
