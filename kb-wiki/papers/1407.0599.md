# Medium-induced gluon radiation and colour decoherence beyond the soft approximation

> We derive the in-medium gluon radiation spectrum off a quark within the path integral formalism at finite energies, including all next-to-eikonal corrections in the propagators of quarks and gluons. Results are computed for finite formation times, including interference with vacuum amplitudes. By rewriting the medium averages in a convenient manner we present the spectrum in terms of dipole cross sections and a colour decoherence parameter with the same physical origin as that found in previous studies of the antenna radiation. This factorisation allows us to present a simple physical picture of the medium-induced radiation for any value of the formation time, that is of interest for a probabilistic implementation of the modified parton shower. Known results are recovered for the particular cases of soft radiation and eikonal quark and for the case of a very long medium, with length much larger than the average formation times for medium-induced radiation. Technical details of the computation of the relevant $n$-point functions in colour space and of the required path integrals in transverse space are provided. The final result completes the calculation of all finite energy corrections for the radiation off a quark in a QCD medium that exist in the small angle approximation and for a recoilless medium.

## Key claim

The authors derive the complete medium-induced gluon radiation spectrum off a quark including all next-to-eikonal corrections, showing that colour coherence between the outgoing quark and gluon survives longer than in previous eikonal calculations, which suppresses the spectrum of radiated gluons; these corrections vanish only in the limit of negligible formation time of the produced gluon.

## Method

The authors use the path-integral formalism to compute the in-medium gluon radiation spectrum off a quark, allowing transverse motion of all particles in the emission process rather than assuming only the softest particle moves. They rewrite the medium averages in terms of dipole cross sections and a colour decoherence parameter, then employ the Gaussian approximation for medium averages, the large number of colours limit, and the multiple soft scattering (harmonic oscillator) approximation to obtain analytic solutions for the path integrals describing non-eikonal parton propagation.

## Key numbers

- The Altarelli-Parisi splitting function recovered in all terms is $P_{g \leftarrow q}(\zeta) = C_F [1 + (1-\zeta)^2 ]/\zeta$, where $\zeta$ is the fraction of longitudinal momenta carried by the gluon
- The dipole cross section is approximated as $n(\xi) \sigma (\mbf{r}) \simeq \frac{1}{2} \hat{q} \mbf{r}^2 + \mathcal{O}(\mbf{r}^2 \ln \mbf{r}^2)$, where $\hat{q}$ is the transport coefficient characterising typical squared transverse momentum per mean free path
- The colour decoherence parameter is given by $\Delta_{coh}=1+ \int_{x_{2+}}^{L_+} d \tau \, N m_{12}(\tau) e^{N (m_{11}-m_{22})}|_{(\tau,x_{2+})}$, with $m_{11}-m_{22}\simeq -\frac{\hat q}{2} \int_{\tau}^{x_{2+}} d\xi \, (\mbf{r}_3-\mbf{\bar{w}}_3)\cdot (\mbf{\bar{r}}_3-\mbf{w}_3)$ and $m_{12}(\tau)\simeq \frac{\hat q}{2} \,(\mbf{r}_3-\mbf{\bar{r}}_3) \cdot (\mbf{w}_3-\mbf{\bar{w}}_3)$

## Context

This work extends the results of Apolinario (2012), where an interpolation function between soft and hard limits for gluon radiation off a quark was deduced, to account for an arbitrary momentum fraction carried by the radiated gluon. It goes beyond Blaizot (2012), which computed gluon radiation off a gluon assuming small formation times, by presenting the full result at finite formation time and finite energy. The colour decoherence parameter has the same physical origin as the $\Delta_{\rm med}$ found in antenna radiation studies by Mehtar-Tani (2010, 2011, 2012), Casalderrey-Solana (2011), and Armesto (2011), enabling a probabilistic implementation suitable for Monte Carlo parton shower generators.
