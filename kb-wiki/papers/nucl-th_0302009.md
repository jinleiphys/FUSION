# Pseudoscalar Mesons in Nuclear Medium

> The behavior of pseudoscalar mesons in nuclear medium is reviewed with an emphasis on the possibility of their Bose-Einstein condensation in dense matter. In particular pion condensation is reexamined in detail, stimulated by recent theoretical and observational developments.

# Pseudoscalar Mesons in Nuclear Medium

## Key claim
The paper reviews the possibility that pion condensation may occur at densities as low as 1–2.5 times nuclear saturation density, contrary to earlier conclusions based on the universality ansatz for Landau-Migdal parameters. The key qualifier is that this result depends critically on new experimental determinations of the Landau-Migdal parameters, particularly the finding that g'_{NΔ} is much smaller than previously assumed.

## Method
The author analyzes pion propagation in nuclear matter using Landau Fermi-liquid theory, incorporating p-wave πN and πNΔ interactions with particle-hole and Δ-hole excitations. Short-range correlations are parametrized by Landau-Migdal parameters (g'_{NN}, g'_{NΔ}, g'_{ΔΔ}), and the critical density for pion condensation is determined from the softening condition of the longitudinal spin-isospin mode (D^{-1}(0, k_c; ρ_c) → 0). New experimental constraints from the Gamow-Teller resonance in ⁹⁰Zr(p,n)⁹⁰Nb at 295 MeV are used to fix g'_{NΔ} and g'_{NN}, leaving g'_{ΔΔ} as a free parameter.

## Key numbers
- Quenching factor from Gamow-Teller resonance analysis: Q = 90 ± 5% (compared to previous Q = 0.5–0.7)
- Extracted Landau-Migdal parameters: g'_{NΔ} = 0.18 + 0.05 g'_{ΔΔ}, g'_{NN} ≈ 0.59
- Critical density for neutral pion condensation in symmetric matter (N=Z): ρ_c ∼ 1.5 ρ₀ (with m*_N/m_N = 0.8, g'_{ΔΔ} = 1)
- Critical density for neutral pion condensation in pure neutron matter (Z=0): ρ_c ∼ 2.4 ρ₀ (with m*_N/m_N = 0.8, g'_{ΔΔ} = 1)
- Critical density for charged pion condensation in pure neutron matter: ρ_c ∼ 2.0 ρ₀ (with m*_N/m_N = 0.8, g'_{ΔΔ} = 1)
- Range of critical densities for neutral pion condensation: 1 < ρ_c/ρ₀ < 2.5 for g'_{ΔΔ} < 1
- Variational calculations with modern potentials give ρ_c ∼ 2 ρ₀ (symmetric) and ρ_c ∼ 1.3 ρ₀ (neutron matter) for neutral pion condensation

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of pion condensation by Migdal and others, which used the universality ansatz (g'_{NN} = g'_{NΔ} = g'_{ΔΔ} ≈ 0.6–0.8) and concluded that pion condensation does not occur at several times nuclear density. The new analysis uses experimental constraints from the Gamow-Teller resonance study by Wakasa et al. and Suzuki et al., which show the universality ansatz does not hold. The results are compared with variational calculations by Akmal et al. and Heiselberg et al. using modern potentials, which also find low-density phase transitions. The paper enables improved calculations of neutron star cooling, as demonstrated by Tsuruta et al., who used ρ_c = 2.5 ρ₀ for charged pion condensation and found that pion cooling can explain the low surface temperature of the pulsar in 3C58.
