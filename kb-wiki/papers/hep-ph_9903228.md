# Diffraction driven steep rise of spin structure function $g_{LT}=g_{1}+g_{2}$ at small x and DIS sum rules

> We derive a unitarity relationship between the spin structure function $g_{LT}(x,Q^{2})=g_{1}(x,Q^{2})+g_{2}(x,Q^{2})$, the LT interference diffractive structure function and the spin-flip coupling of the pomeron to nucleons. Our diffractive mechanism gives rise to a dramatic small-$x$ rise $g_{LT}(x,Q^{2}) \sim g^{2}(x,\bar{Q}^{2}) \sim (1/x)^{2(1+δ_{g})}$, where $δ_{g}$ is an exponent of small-$x$ rise of the unpolarized gluon density in the proton $g(x,\bar{Q}^{2})$ at a moderate hard scale $\bar{Q}^{2}$ for light flavour contribution and large hard scale $\bar{Q}^{2} \sim m_{f}^{2}$ for heavy flavour contribution. It invalidates the Burkhardt-Cottingham sum rule. The found small-$x$ rise of diffraction driven $g_{LT}(x,Q^{2})$ is steeper than given by the Wandzura-Wilczek relation under conventional assumptions on small-$x$ behaviour of $g_{1}(x,Q^{2})$.

## Key claim

The authors derive a unitarity relationship showing that the spin structure function $g_{LT}(x,Q^{2}) = g_1 + g_2$ rises steeply at small $x$ as $g_{LT}(x,Q^{2}) \sim (1/x)^{2(1+\delta_g)}$, where $\delta_g$ is the exponent of the unpolarized gluon density's small-$x$ rise, but this result depends on the existence of a nonvanishing pomeron spin-flip coupling $r_5$ whose numerical value remains uncertain and is not calculable from first principles of perturbative QCD.

## Method

The authors apply the optical theorem and eikonal unitarity to relate the forward Compton scattering amplitude for $g_{LT}$ to the product of two diffractive DIS amplitudes, one involving an LT interference transition and the other involving a pomeron-nucleon spin-flip transition. They evaluate the resulting unitarity-driven contribution using a QCD model for diffractive DIS based on excitation of $q\bar{q}$ Fock states of the photon, with the gluon structure function input taken from standard parameterizations.

## Key numbers

* The conservative estimate for $x \lesssim 10^{-3}$ and $Q^2 = 5$ GeV$^2$ gives $x^2 g_{LT}^{(U)}(x,Q^2) \approx r_5(0) \cdot 10^{-4} (0.001/x)^{0.4}$, assuming $r_5 = -0.1$
* The resulting spin asymmetry $A_2 \approx 6 \cdot 10^{-4} \cdot r_5(0) m_p / \sqrt{Q^2}$ is approximately flat for $x = 10^{-3}$ to $10^{-4}$
* For light flavour contribution at $x_g = 10^{-3}$, the effective exponent $2\delta_g \approx 0.4$; for charm contribution $2\delta_g \approx 0.6$
* The diffractive mechanism takes over the Wandzura-Wilczek relation at $x \lesssim 10^{-3}$
* Experimental data on pion-nucleon scattering give $|r_5| = 0.2 \pm 0.3$; proton-proton scattering allows $r_5 = -0.6$ with about 100 per cent uncertainty

## Context

This work extends the earlier derivation of a rising tensor structure function $b_2(x,Q^2)$ for DIS off spin-1 deuterons by Nikolaev and Schäfer (1999), which invalidated the Close-Kumano sum rule, and applies similar unitarity arguments to the spin-1/2 nucleon case. The result contradicts the common wisdom that spin asymmetries vanish at small $x$ and invalidates the Burkhardt-Cottingham sum rule, which assumed superconvergence of the relevant Compton amplitude. The authors suggest that the diffractive component may be identified with the long-sought deviation from the Wandzura-Wilczek relation, and propose that vector meson production with polarized real photons could provide experimental constraints on the pomeron spin-flip parameter $r_5$.
