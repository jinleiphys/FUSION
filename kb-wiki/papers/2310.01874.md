# Relativistic magnetohydrodynamics of a spinful and vortical fluid: Entropy current analysis

> We generalize a recently introduced formulation of relativistic spinful and vortical fluid to relativistic magnetohydrodynamics (MHD). We refer to it as the "Spinful-Vortical MHD" (SVMHD). The aim is to scrutinize the interplay between the vorticity, magnetic field, and spin, which is treated as a quantum object, in contrast to other formulations of spin hydrodynamics. To this purpose, we first perform a standard entropy current analysis up to first-order gradient expansion, $\mathcal{O}\left(\partial\right)$ as well as $\mathcal{O}\left(\hbar\partial\right)$, where $\hbar$ is the Planck constant. In contrast to alternative formulations of spin MHD, in the absence of vorticity, the zeroth-order energy-momentum tensor includes an additional magneto-vorticity mixed term and reduces, as expected, to the energy-momentum tensor of MHD. We show that in the first-order of gradient expansion, $36$ dissipative transport coefficients appear. They satisfy certain constraints that guarantee the positive definiteness of the entropy production rate. We then modify the formulation of SVMHD by replacing the magnetic part of the thermal vorticity tensor with its electric part. Carrying out the same analysis as in the standard formulation, we show that in this case, the first-order constitutive relations consist of $11$ nondissipative Hall-like coefficients, apart from $25$ dissipative coefficients. This difference arises from different behavior of the electric and magnetic part of the thermal vorticity under time-reversal transformation.

## Key claim
The paper extends a recently introduced formulation of relativistic spinful and vortical fluid (Huang, 2022) to relativistic magnetohydrodynamics (MHD), showing that in the zeroth-order gradient expansion the energy-momentum tensor contains a novel magneto-vorticity mixed term proportional to \(b^{(\mu}\omega^{\nu)}\) that couples spin, magnetic field, and vorticity, a feature absent in alternative formulations of spin MHD where such a term appears only at first order in gradients.

## Method
The authors perform a standard entropy current analysis up to first-order gradient expansion at both \(\mathcal{O}(\partial)\) and \(\mathcal{O}(\hbar\partial)\) for a charged, rotating, spinful fluid in the presence of electromagnetic fields. They decompose the energy-momentum tensor, spin tensor, electromagnetic current, and dual field strength tensor into ideal and dissipative parts, then enforce the second law of thermodynamics to determine the ideal coefficients and the dissipative transport coefficients. Two formulations are compared: one using the magnetic part of the thermal vorticity tensor \(\omega^{\mu}\) and another replacing it with the electric part \(\kappa^{\mu}\).

## Key numbers
* 36 dissipative transport coefficients appear in the first-order gradient expansion when using the magnetic part of the thermal vorticity tensor, consisting of four thermal conductivities, five rotational viscosities, ten bulk viscosities, four shear viscosities, eight cross viscosities, and five electric resistivities.
* 11 nondissipative Hall-like coefficients appear in addition to 25 dissipative coefficients when the electric part of the thermal vorticity tensor replaces the magnetic part.
* The vorticity of the quark-gluon plasma created in heavy-ion collisions is estimated to be \(0.02\ \text{fm}^{-1} \approx 10^{22}\ \text{Hz}\) (Becattini, 2016).
* Strong magnetic fields up to \(10^{18}\)–\(10^{20}\ \text{G}\) are produced in noncentral heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC (Warringa, 2007; Skokov, 2009).

## Context
This work generalizes the spinful and vortical fluid formulation of Huang (2022) to include electromagnetic fields, extending prior MHD studies that incorporated magnetization and electric polarization effects (Rischke, 2009; Rischke, 2011; Sadooghi, 2016) and earlier spin MHD formulations based on kinetic theory (Florkowski, 2022). The results enable systematic study of the interplay between vorticity, magnetic field, and quantum spin in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, particularly for understanding spin polarization of \(\Lambda\) hyperons and vector meson spin alignment observed at RHIC and LHC.
