# Collective flows of light particles in the Au+Au collision at intermediate energies

> The Skyrme potential energy density functional is introduced into the Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) model and the updated version is applied to studying the directed and elliptic flows of light particles (protons, neutrons, deuterons, tritons, $^3$He and $^4$He) in $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au collisions at beam energies 150, 250 and 400 MeV/nucleon. The results are compared with the recent FOPI experimental data. It is found that the yields and collective flows of light particles can be described quite well. The influence of the equation of state (EoS), medium-modified nucleon-nucleon elastic cross sections (NNECS) and cluster recognition criteria on the directed and elliptic flows is studied in detail. It is found that the flows of light particles are sensitive to the medium-modified NNECS, but not sensitive to the isospin dependent cluster recognition criteria. It seems difficult, however, even with the new data and calculations, to obtain a more accurate constraint on the nuclear incompressibility $K_0$ than the interval 200-260 MeV.

## Key claim

The updated UrQMD model with the Skyrme potential energy density functional can describe the yields and collective flows of light particles (protons, neutrons, deuterons, tritons, 3He, and 4He) in Au+Au collisions at 150-400 MeV/nucleon quite well, but it remains difficult to obtain a more accurate constraint on the nuclear incompressibility K0 than the interval 200-260 MeV even with the new FOPI data and calculations.

## Method

The Skyrme potential energy density functional was introduced into the Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) model, and the updated version was used to simulate 197Au+197Au collisions at beam energies of 150, 250, and 400 MeV/nucleon. Several hundred thousand events were simulated within the impact parameter region 0-7.5 fm, and the directed and elliptic flows of light particles (protons, neutrons, deuterons, tritons, 3He, and 4He) were calculated and compared with recent FOPI experimental data. The influence of the equation of state (EoS), medium-modified nucleon-nucleon elastic cross sections (NNECS), and cluster recognition criteria (MST vs. iso-MST) on the flows was studied in detail.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear incompressibility K0 values for the three Skyrme forces used: SkP (201 MeV), SV-mas08 (233 MeV), and SkA (263 MeV), with saturation densities of 0.163, 0.160, and 0.155 fm-3 respectively
- Beam energies studied: 150, 250, and 400 MeV/nucleon for 197Au+197Au collisions
- Impact parameter range for calculations: 0-7.5 fm, divided into four centrality groups: b0<0.15, 0.15<b0<0.25, 0.25<b0<0.45, and 0.45<b0<0.55 (with bmax=13.4 fm)
- Cluster recognition parameters: R0=3.2 fm for MST; R0nn=R0np=4.5 fm and R0pp=3.2 fm for iso-MST; P0=0.25 GeV/c for both
- The medium correction factor FU3 uses parameters zeta=1/3 and lambda=1/6
- The momentum region where flows are most sensitive to in-medium NNECS: p=0.2-0.4 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends previous studies of collective flow in heavy-ion collisions, particularly the FOPI collaboration measurements (FOPI, 2010; FOPI, 2011) of directed and elliptic flows for light charged particles at GSI. The updated UrQMD model builds on earlier QMD and ImQMD approaches (Aichelin, 1991; Zhang, 2006; Zhang, 2007) and prior efforts to constrain the nuclear EoS through sub-threshold kaon production (Hartnack, 2005) and collective flow observables (Danielewicz, 2002). The results enable further investigation of the momentum dependence of in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections and suggest that new experiments such as S394 by the ASY-EOS collaboration (Russotto, 2012) may help reduce uncertainties in both K0 and the symmetry energy.
