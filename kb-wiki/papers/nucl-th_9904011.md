# Meson and Isobar Degrees of Freedom in A($\vec{e},e'\vec{p}$) reactions at $0.2 \leq Q^2 \leq 0.8 (GeV/c)^2$

> The effect of meson and isobar degrees of freedom in A($\vec{e},e'\vec{p}$) and A(e,e'n) is studied for four-momentum transfers Q^2 in the range between 0.2 and 0.8 (GeV/c)^2. The calculations are performed in a non-relativistic framework with explicit (N,Δ,π) degrees-of-freedom. For the whole range of momentum transfers under investigation the relative effect of the meson-exchange and isobar degrees of freedom is significant. At low missing momenta and quasi-elastic conditions, a tendency to reduce the (e,e'p) and (e,e'n) differential cross sections is noticed. The greatest sensitivity is found in the interference structure functions $W_{LT}$ and $W_{TT}$. The recoil polarization observables, on the other hand, are moderately affected by the meson-exchange and $Δ$-isobar currents.

## Key claim

Meson-exchange and Delta-isobar two-body currents produce significant effects on A($\vec{e},e'\vec{p}$) and A($e,e'n$) observables across the entire momentum transfer range $0.2 \leq Q^2 \leq 0.8$ (GeV/c)$^2$, but the magnitude of these effects depends on the specific observable: the interference structure functions $W_{LT}$ and $W_{TT}$ show the greatest sensitivity, while recoil polarization observables are only moderately affected, suggesting that double polarization measurements may be relatively clean probes of medium-modified nucleon form factors even when two-body currents are present.

## Method

The authors performed non-relativistic calculations for exclusive single-nucleon knockout reactions using a mean-field potential from Hartree-Fock with Skyrme-type effective nucleon-nucleon force, including central, spin-orbit, and Coulomb terms. They implemented explicit two-body current operators derived from one-pion exchange (meson-exchange currents) and from Delta-isobar excitation with $\pi N \Delta$ and $\gamma N \Delta$ couplings, including a medium-dependent Delta propagator with complex potential from Oset and Salcedo (1987). The transition matrix elements were computed via multipole expansion up to $J_{max}=30$ for the highest momentum transfers, with bound and scattering states generated from the same mean-field potential to preserve orthogonality and gauge invariance at the one-body level.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfer range: $0.2 \leq Q^2 \leq 0.8$ (GeV/c)$^2$, corresponding to $|\vec{q}| \leq 1$ GeV/c
- For $^{16}$O calculations at highest $|\vec{q}| \approx 1$ GeV, convergence required multipolarities up to $J_{max}=30$, consistent with estimate $J_{max}=2qR_A$ where $R_A = 1.2 A^{1/3}$ fm
- Delta resonance mass: $M_{\Delta}=1232$ MeV; coupling constants: $f^2_{\pi N \Delta}/4\pi = 0.37$ and $f_{\gamma N\Delta} = 0.12$
- Delta electromagnetic form factor parameters: $\Lambda_1=0.84$ GeV/c, $\Lambda_2=1.2$ GeV/c
- Pion-nucleon cutoff parameter: $\Lambda_{\pi NN} = 1250$ MeV/c
- Medium correction to Delta propagator: $V_{\Delta} [\mathrm{MeV}] = -30-40i$ (from Chen and Lee, comparable to Oset and Salcedo calculations)
- The paper reports that at low missing momenta and quasi-elastic conditions, two-body currents tend to reduce the $(e,e'p)$ and $(e,e'n)$ differential cross sections

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of two-body currents in exclusive A($e,e'p$) reactions from finite nuclei, including the pioneering work of Suzuki (1989), systematic investigations by the Pavia group (Boffi et al., 1990-1992), and $^4$He($e,e'p$) studies by Laget (1994) and Epstein et al. (1993). Unlike the Pavia group's approach which reduced two-body currents to effective one-body operators using a Fermi-gas approximation, this work treats the two-body currents in full non-relativistic complexity. The calculations are motivated by the advent of higher-$Q^2$ experimental capabilities at TJNAF and upgraded Mainz, and aim to establish whether recoil polarization observables from A($\vec{e},e'\vec{p}$) measurements can serve as relatively ambiguity-free probes of medium-dependent nucleon form factors, as suggested by Laget (1994) and Kelly (1997-1998). The results enable future comparisons with experimental data at higher momentum transfers and provide a baseline for assessing when relativistic effects and quark-gluon degrees of freedom become necessary.
