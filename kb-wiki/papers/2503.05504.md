# Systematic optimization of resonance parameters in a transport approach

> This study optimizes resonance parameters responsible for strangeness production in the SMASH (Simulating Many Accelerated Strongly-interacting Hadrons) transport model using a genetic algorithm. By fitting resonance parameters to experimental data on exclusive strangeness cross-sections at low energies, we significantly improve the model's accuracy, especially in pion-proton interactions. Our approach explores how machine learning tools can be used for precise resonance tuning in transport approaches.

## Key claim

The genetic algorithm optimization of resonance parameters in the SMASH transport model significantly improves agreement with experimental exclusive strangeness cross-sections, particularly for pion-proton interactions, but the fit is worse for one proton-proton cross-section (K0 + p + Sigma+), indicating tension between observables that may stem from missing resonances or overly restrictive mass bounds.

## Method

The authors used a genetic algorithm to optimize resonance parameters (branching ratios, masses, widths) for strangeness production in the SMASH hadron transport model. They employed an emulation approach that multiplies resonance cross-sections by mass-dependent branching ratios integrated over the spectral function, then added a separately simulated string contribution. The fitness function minimized the weighted relative difference between simulated and experimental exclusive strangeness cross-sections for proton-proton and pion-proton collisions at low energies, with a mutation rate of 10% and elimination of the bottom 50% of solutions each generation.

## Key numbers

- The N(1900) resonance has PDG bounds on its Lambda + K decay mode ranging from 2% to 20%, illustrating the uncertainty in resonance properties
- The genetic algorithm used a mutation rate of 10% (each parameter has a 10% chance of being randomized)
- The selection process eliminates the bottom 50% of solutions in each generation
- SMASH incorporates resonances up to a mass of approximately 2 GeV as degrees of freedom
- A bump observed around sqrt(s) approximately 2 GeV in the K0 + Lambda cross-section is attributed to the N(1990) and N(2060) resonances

## Context

This work extends the SMASH transport model (SMASH-3.1) by systematically optimizing resonance parameters that were previously tuned to experimental data by Vinzent (no year given) but without a systematic approach. It builds on an UrQMD study by Steinberg et al. (no year given) that demonstrated transport model sensitivity to resonance parameters at lower energies. The optimized parameter set was incorporated into SMASH-3.2, with the N(2080) and N(1720) resonances kept at their SMASH-3.1 default values to maintain agreement with dilepton production constraints on phi meson yields from Steinberg (2018). The approach enables more reliable simulations of strangeness production in heavy-ion collisions and demonstrates how machine learning tools can systematically tune resonance parameters in transport models.
