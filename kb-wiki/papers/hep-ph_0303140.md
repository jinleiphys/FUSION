# Higher twists in the pion structure function

> We calculate the QCD moments of the pion structure function using Drell-Yan data on the quark distributions in the pion and a phenomenological model for the resonance region. The extracted higher twist corrections are found to be larger than those for the nucleon, contributing around 50% of the lowest moment at Q^2=1 GeV^2.

## Key claim

Higher twist corrections to the pion structure function moments are larger than those for the nucleon, contributing around 50% of the lowest moment at Q^2 = 1 GeV^2, but this estimate depends on a phenomenological model for the low-W resonance spectrum dominated by elastic and rho-pole contributions, with significant uncertainty from the poorly known pi-to-rho transition form factor.

## Method

The QCD moments of the pion structure function were computed using the operator product expansion, with the leading twist contribution obtained from parameterizations of leading twist quark distributions in the pion (specifically the Gluck-Reya-Vogt fit at low Q^2) derived from global analyses of pion Drell-Yan data. The resonance region (W < 1 GeV) was modeled using the elastic pion form factor from world data and the pi-to-rho transition form factor from three different theoretical models (Bethe-Salpeter, Dyson-Schwinger, and light-cone QCD sum rules) to estimate uncertainty. The total moments were constructed by combining the leading twist, elastic, and rho contributions, and higher twist contributions were extracted as the difference between the total moment and the leading twist plus target mass corrections.

## Key numbers

- The resonance region (W < 1 GeV) contributes approximately 50% of the n=2 moment at Q^2 = 2 GeV^2, dropping to below 10% for Q^2 > 5 GeV^2
- The n=10 moment is virtually saturated by the resonance region at Q^2 = 2 GeV^2, and still receives about 40% of its strength from W < 1 GeV at Q^2 = 10 GeV^2
- Higher twist corrections to the n=2 moment amount to approximately 50% at Q^2 = 1 GeV^2, approximately 30% at Q^2 = 2 GeV^2, and become negligible beyond Q^2 approximately 6 GeV^2
- The elastic contribution is as large as the leading twist at Q^2 approximately 1 GeV^2, but becomes negligible beyond Q^2 approximately 3 GeV^2
- The E615 Collaboration found the intrinsic transverse momentum of quarks in the pion to be 0.8 +/- 0.3 GeV^2, larger than the typical quark transverse momentum in the nucleon (approximately 500 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Bloom-Gilman duality and higher twist effects from the nucleon (previously studied by Ji and Unrau (1995) for the proton F2 structure function and by Ji and Melnitchouk (1997) for the spin-dependent g1 structure function) to the pion, the simplest q-qbar system in QCD. The finding that higher twist corrections are larger for the pion than for the nucleon implies that quark-hadron duality would set in at higher Q^2 for the pion, and the paper identifies the need for experimental data on the inclusive pion spectrum from semi-inclusive charge-exchange reactions (e p to e n X) at low t, which could be obtained with CLAS at Jefferson Lab, to enable more quantitative tests of duality in the meson sector.
