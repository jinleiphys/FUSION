# Many-body correlations and Isospin equilibration in multi-fragmentation processes

> Isospin equilibration in multi-fragmentation processes is studied for the system $^{40}Cl+^{28}Si$ at 40 MeV/nucleon. The investigation is performed through semiclassical microscopic many-body calculations based on the CoMD-II model. The study has been developed to describe isospin equilibration processes involving the gas and liquid "phases" of the total system formed in the collision processes. The investigation of the behavior of this observable in terms of the repulsive/attractive action of the symmetry term, highlights many-body correlations which are absent in semiclassical mean-field approaches.

## Key claim

The isospin equilibration process in multi-fragmentation reactions cannot be explained solely by isospin diffusion and drift between the main reaction partners; the gas "phase" contribution (light charged particles) acts as a "dissipation" mechanism and must be included, and many-body correlations produce a negative sign for the leading factor \(\beta_{M}\) in the symmetry potential, which is absent in mean-field approaches.

## Method

The authors performed semiclassical microscopic many-body calculations using the Constrained Molecular Dynamics-II (CoMD-II) model for the system \(^{40}\text{Cl}+^{28}\text{Si}\) at 40 MeV/nucleon. They studied the ensemble average of the time derivative of the total dipole \(\overline{\overrightarrow{V}}\) as a function of time, decomposing it into contributions from the gas "phase" (light particles), the liquid "phase" (fragments), and their relative motion. Three different options for the symmetry potential term \(U^{\tau}\) (Stiff1, Stiff2, and Soft) were compared, and the impact parameter was varied to identify the optimal range for sensitivity to the symmetry energy.

## Key numbers

- The difference in the average total dipolar signal along the \(\hat{z}\) direction between the Stiff1 and Soft cases is about 85%, and between Stiff1 and Stiff2 about 53%.
- The total number of neutrons and protons emitted at 160 fm/c is on average about 15 for the Soft case and 11 for the Stiff1 case.
- The neutron excess fraction of the gas "phase" is \(Y_{G}\cong 0.23\) for the Soft case and \(Y_{G}\cong 0.18\) for the Stiff1 case.
- The maximum value of the average overlap integral \(s\) is reached at about 30 fm/c; the Stiff1 case reaches about 1.25 times the ground-state value \(s_{g.s.}\).
- For the total system (A=68, Y=0.088) at normal density, \(\beta_{M}\simeq -0.43\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) in the correlated case and \(\beta_{M}=0.065\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) in the mean-field case.
- The \(\beta_{M}\) factor becomes positive for relevant asymmetry \(|Y_{c}|>0.25\) for systems with mass around 64 units.
- The optimal impact parameter range for enhanced sensitivity to the symmetry energy is \(b\cong 3.5-5.5\) fm.

## Context

This work extends studies of isospin diffusion in binary reactions, such as those using Sn isotopes by Betty (2005) and dynamical BUU calculations, by examining the full multi-fragmentation regime where a gas and liquid "phase" coexist. The CoMD-II results contradict the behavior predicted by mean-field approaches (e.g., Bao0 (2000)) because many-body correlations change the sign and strength of the symmetry potential. The findings enable future investigations of isospin-dependent observables and suggest a critical charge/mass asymmetry where the symmetry potential changes from density-attractive to density-repulsive for asy-stiff cases.
