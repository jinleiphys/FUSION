# Three-flavor chiral phase transition and axial symmetry breaking with the functional renormalization group

> The interplay of mesonic fluctuations with an axial $U(1)_A$-symmetry breaking and resulting effects on the location of a possibly existing critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram are investigated in a framework of the functional renormalization group within a $N_f = 2 + 1$ flavor quark-meson model truncation. The axial $U(1)_A$-symmetry breaking is imposed by a mesonic Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft determinant. The quark mass sensitivity of the chiral phase transition with and without the $U(1)_A$-symmetry breaking is studied.

# Three-flavor chiral phase transition and axial symmetry breaking with the functional renormalization group

## Key claim

The location of the critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram depends strongly on whether axial U(1)_A symmetry is broken, but only when mesonic fluctuations are included via the functional renormalization group; mean-field approximations show only a weak dependence on U(1)_A symmetry breaking. In the light chiral limit with a physical strange quark mass, the transition is first-order without U(1)_A breaking but second-order with a constant Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft determinant.

## Method

The authors solve functional renormalization group (FRG) flow equations for a three-flavor (2+1) quark-meson model truncation at finite temperature and quark chemical potential, using a leading-order derivative expansion with an optimized three-dimensional regulator. The axial U(1)_A symmetry breaking is implemented by adding a Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft determinant term with a constant (temperature- and scale-independent) coupling to the mesonic potential, and results are compared to standard mean-field and extended mean-field approximations that neglect mesonic fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- The η'-meson mass drops by about 200 MeV near the chiral crossover when U(1)_A symmetry breaking is included, in agreement with experimental observations.
- The σ-meson mass is fixed to m_σ = 480 MeV in all calculations of critical endpoint locations.
- With U(1)_A symmetry breaking and mesonic fluctuations included, the critical endpoint is pushed to larger chemical potentials and smaller temperatures compared to the case without the breaking term.
- Without the U(1)_A determinant, the critical endpoint moves in the opposite direction (towards smaller chemical potentials and larger temperatures) when mesonic fluctuations are taken into account.

## Context

This work extends previous two-flavor O(4)-symmetric FRG investigations by Schaefer (2006) to three flavors, and confronts analytical predictions by Pisarski (1984) about the order of the chiral transition in the chiral limit. The results are relevant for interpreting recent lattice QCD simulations by Bazavov (2012), Cossu (2012, 2013), and Buchoff (2013) that show conflicting evidence about U(1)_A restoration at the chiral crossover. The findings enable improved truncations with temperature- and scale-dependent U(1)_A breaking couplings, which the authors note should produce results intermediate between their two scenarios.
