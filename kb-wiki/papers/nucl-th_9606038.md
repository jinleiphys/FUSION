# Strong Pionic Decays From a Spectroscopic Quark Model

> From a refined non-relativistic quark model that fits the baryonic low-energy spectrum the study of strong pion decay processes within an elementary emission model scheme points out the need of incorporating size-contributing components into the baryon wave functions. In particular the effect of a (qqq qantiq) component is investigated in the framework of a quark pair creation model.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a refined non-relativistic quark model which successfully fits the baryonic low-energy spectrum still fails to reproduce strong pion decay widths and the \(NN\pi\) form factor unless size-contributing components, specifically a \((qqq\;q\bar{q})\) component, are incorporated into the baryon wave functions via a quark pair creation model.

## Method

The authors start from a non-relativistic quark model with a two-body potential (confinement, Coulomb, and spin-spin terms) and add a phenomenological three-body force to correctly reproduce the baryon spectrum up to 0.7 GeV excitation energy. They then compute strong pion decay widths for nucleon and delta resonances using two approaches: the Elementary Emission Model (EEM), where a point-like pion is emitted by a single quark, and the Quark Pair Creation Model (QPCM), where a \(q\bar{q}\) pair with vacuum quantum numbers (\(^3P_0\)) is created and recombines to form the outgoing pion. The \(NN\pi\) form factor is calculated in both models and compared to a phenomenological parameterization.

## Key numbers

- The quark mass \(m_u, m_d\) is taken between 300 and 360 MeV, chosen to fit the nucleon magnetic moments within 10% error.
- The fitted \(f_{qq\pi}\) values are 0.602, 0.604, and 0.600 for potentials \(V_I\), \(V_{II}\), and \(V_{III}\) respectively, using \(f_{NN\pi}(0)=0.998\).
- The range of the spin-spin interaction in the corrected potential \(V_{II}^{(2)}\) is 0.4 fm, compared to 1.28 fm in the uncorrected version.
- The three-body force range in \(V_{III}^{(3)}\) is 0.25 fm (gaussian form), down from 0.8 fm in \(V_{II}^{(3)}\).
- The pion wave function uses a gaussian with \(R_A^2 = 8\) GeV\(^{-2}\) to reproduce the pion root mean square radius.
- The QPCM parameter \(\gamma\) is extracted from \(f_{NN\pi}(0)\) and differs between models: values are given in table 4 (not fully shown in the truncated text), with the authors noting that for \(V_I\) the value is quite different from \(V_{II}\) and \(V_{III}\).
- The \(NN\pi\) form factor from \(V_{II}\) and \(V_{III}\) deviates by at most 10% from the phenomenological parameterization of Oset (1983) for small \(k\) values.

## Context

This work extends the spectroscopic quark model of Desplanques et al. (1992), which introduced a three-quark force to correctly predict baryon masses for negative and positive parity states up to 0.7 GeV excitation energy. The present study tests those wave functions against strong pion decay data and finds that the predicted nucleon charge radius is too small, consistent with the absence of a mesonic cloud. The QPCM approach, following the formalism of Le Yaouanc et al. (1988) and earlier work by Mitra and Ross, partially corrects the \(NN\pi\) form factor by incorporating the finite size of the pion, but the authors note that a \((qqq\;q\bar{q})\) component is still needed to fully resolve the discrepancies. This enables future refinements of the three-body potential and the inclusion of explicit mesonic degrees of freedom in non-relativistic quark models.
