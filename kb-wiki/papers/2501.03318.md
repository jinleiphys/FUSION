# Neutrino energy and momentum emission from magnetized dense quark matter

> Using first-principles field-theoretic methods, we investigate neutrino emission from strongly magnetized dense quark matter under conditions relevant to compact stars. We develop a customized approximation that fully accounts for the Landau-level quantization of electron states while neglecting such quantization for quarks. This approach is well-justified in dense quark matter, where the chemical potentials of up and down quarks significantly exceed those of electrons. Our analysis provides a detailed exploration of the influence of strong magnetic fields on neutrino emission, including both the modification of the total emission rate and the emergence of emission asymmetry relative to the magnetic field direction. We further examine the role of temperature in smoothing the oscillatory behavior of neutrino emission as a function of magnetic field strength. Additionally, we study the interplay between the Landau-level quantization of electrons and the Fermi-liquid effects of quarks in modifying the phase space of relevant weak processes. Finally, we briefly discuss the broader implications of magnetic fields on stellar cooling processes and the potential contribution of asymmetric neutrino emission to pulsar kicks.

## Key claim

Using first-principles field-theoretic methods, the authors calculate neutrino energy and net longitudinal momentum emission rates from strongly magnetized dense quark matter, but only under the condition that the chemical potentials of up and down quarks significantly exceed those of electrons, which justifies neglecting Landau-level quantization for quarks while fully accounting for it for electrons.

## Method

The authors employ the Kadanoff-Baym transport equation for neutrinos within the low-energy Fermi theory of weak interactions, incorporating the Landau-level quantization of electron states via their spectral function while treating quarks as Fermi liquids with Fermi-liquid corrections to their dispersion relations. They derive expressions for the neutrino-number production rate, then integrate over neutrino momenta to obtain the energy and net longitudinal momentum emission rates, which are evaluated numerically for various magnetic field strengths and temperatures. The analysis accounts for the direct Urca processes (electron capture by an up quark and down quark decay) in unpaired dense quark matter composed of up and down quarks.

## Key numbers

- The Fermi coupling constant is \(G_F \approx 1.166 \times 10^{-11}~\text{MeV}^{-2}\) and \(\cos^2\theta_C \approx 0.948\) for the Cabibbo angle.
- Quark chemical potentials are on the order of \(300~\text{MeV}\), while the electron chemical potential is on the order of \(50~\text{MeV}\).
- The magnetic field strength considered is up to \(10^{17}~\text{G}\), corresponding to \(\sqrt{|eB|} \lesssim 25~\text{MeV}\).
- The Fermi velocity correction is \(\kappa = 2\alpha_s/(3\pi)\), giving \(v_F = 1-\kappa\).
- The energy emission rate in the zero-temperature limit is proportional to \(T^6\) with a prefactor involving \(\frac{457 \pi N_c G_F^2 \cos^2\theta_C}{5040} v_F \mu_u \mu_d \mu_e\).
- The net longitudinal momentum emission rate in the zero-temperature limit is proportional to \(T^6\) with a prefactor involving \(\frac{457 \pi N_c G_F^2 \cos^2\theta_C}{60480 \ell^2} v_F \mu_e\).

## Context

This work extends the pioneering studies of neutrino emission from dense quark matter by Iwamoto (1980, 1982) and the Fermi-liquid corrections by Baym (1975) to include the effects of strong magnetic fields, which previous studies such as Baiko (1998), Riquelme (2005), and Potekhin (2015) had investigated primarily for nuclear matter. The results enable more accurate modeling of magnetar cooling and provide a quantitative foundation for understanding the potential contribution of asymmetric neutrino emission to pulsar kicks, addressing a gap left by earlier oversimplified models such as those by Sagert (2007) and Ayala (2018, 2024) that used electron spin polarization to estimate emission asymmetry.
