# Strange magnetic moment of the nucleon and SU(3) breaking: group theoretical approach

> An extended group-theoretical approach to magnetic moments of the octet baryons is proposed with the aim of extracting the strange magnetic moment of the nucleon. Special attention is given to flavor SU(3) breaking. In this approach, isoscalar and isovector magnetic moments are treated separately in view of their different behavior under SU(3) breaking. We conclude that the anomalous magnetic moment associated with the flavor singlet current is small. Together with the small isoscalar anomalous magnetic moment of the nucleon, this implies suppression of the strange magnetic moment of the proton which is found to be small and positive, mu^(s) = (0.16 \pm 0.03) mu_N in units of the nuclear magneton.

# Strange magnetic moment of the nucleon and SU(3) breaking: group theoretical approach

## Key claim

The strange magnetic moment of the proton is small and positive, μ^(s) = (0.16 ± 0.03) μ_N in units of the nuclear magneton, provided that the isovector and isoscalar magnetic moments are treated separately under SU(3) breaking and that the anomalous flavor singlet magnetic moment is small.

## Method

The authors propose an extended group-theoretical approach to octet baryon magnetic moments, treating isovector and isoscalar components separately under SU(3) breaking. They perform separate fits of the isovector and isoscalar magnetic moments using three scenarios: (Fit 1) only the octet operator; (Fit 2) octet plus singlet operators; (Fit 3) a complete set including decuplet, anti-decuplet, and 27-plet operators. The reduced matrix elements F, D, and S are extracted from the observed octet baryon magnetic moments, and the strange magnetic moment is evaluated using the relation ⟨p|μ̂^(s)|p⟩ = [S − 2(F − D/3)] μ_0, where μ_0 is the SU(3) symmetric magneton.

## Key numbers

* Strange magnetic moment of the proton: μ^(s) = +0.155(22) μ_N (Fit 2) and +0.161(28) μ_N (Fit 3), in units of the nuclear magneton.
* SU(3) breaking parameter a ≈ −0.17, corresponding to a light-to-strange constituent quark mass ratio m_{u,d}/m_s = 0.62.
* SU(3) symmetric baryon mass M_0 = 1152 MeV, obtained from fitting octet baryon masses.
* Flavor singlet anomalous magnetic moment S^(a) = S − 1 ≈ 0.27 μ_0, less than one third of the normal singlet moment.
* Ratio of isoscalar to isovector octet coefficients s_8/v_8 ≈ 1.4, showing sizable deviation from unity under SU(3) breaking.

## Context

This work extends the group-theoretical approach to baryon magnetic moments originally developed by Coleman and Glashow (1961) for the SU(3) symmetric limit, and later extended by Caldi and Pagels (1974) with chiral perturbation theory corrections. The separate treatment of isovector and isoscalar sectors builds on relations derived by Franklin (1969) and Caldi (1974). The result μ^(s) ≈ +0.16 μ_N is compatible with the combined experimental analysis of HAPPEX, SAMPLE, and A4 (Mainz) measurements at Q² ∼ 0.1 GeV², which suggest positive G_M^s values. The positive sign contrasts with predictions from Skyrme soliton models (Park 1990, 1991) and vector meson dominance (Jaffe 1989), but is consistent with chiral perturbation theory (Hemmert 1998) and the chiral quark soliton model (Kim 1998). The smallness of the anomalous singlet moment is consistent with the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule applied to the flavor singlet current, which suggests suppression because the singlet current does not couple the baryon octet to the decuplet.
