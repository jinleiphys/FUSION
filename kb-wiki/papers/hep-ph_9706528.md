# Squeezed condensate of gluons and the mass of the eta'

> The relation between the large mass of the $η'$ and the structure of the gluon vacuum via the $U_A(1)$ anomaly is discussed. A squeezed gluon vacuum is considered as an alternative to existing models. Considering Witten's formula for the $η_0$ mass we show that the contact term can give a sizable contribution and relate it to the physical gluon condensate. The values of the gluon condensate obtained through this relation are compared with the value by Shifman, Vainshtein and Zakharov and the recent update values by Narison.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the contact term in Witten's formula for the eta-prime mass, which is often dropped, can give a sizable contribution to the gluonic part of the eta_0 mass when evaluated in a squeezed gluon vacuum model, and that this contribution can be related to the physical gluon condensate.

## Method

The authors use the squeezed gluon vacuum, a homogeneous condensate of colour singlet gluon pairs in Minkowski space, as an alternative to the instanton gas model. They evaluate the contact term in Witten's formula for the topological susceptibility in the Schrodinger picture, approximating the exact QCD eigenstates by squeezed states. This yields a relation between the eta_0 mass shift and the gluon condensate, which is then compared with empirical values from Shifman, Vainshtein and Zakharov (1979) and Narison (1996).

## Key numbers

*   The experimental mixing angle for eta-eta' is phi = -(18.4 +/- 2) degrees.
*   The experimental masses are m_eta = 547.45 +/- 0.19 MeV and m_eta' = 957.77 +/- 0.14 MeV.
*   The extracted mass of the eta_0 is M_00 = 931.21 (+5.40, -5.94) MeV.
*   The squared mass difference due to the U_A(1) anomaly is Delta m_eta0^2 = 0.696 GeV^2.
*   The gluon condensate estimate by Shifman, Vainshtein and Zakharov is <alpha_s G^2> ~ 0.04 GeV^4.
*   The update average gluon condensate by Narison is <alpha_s G^2> = (0.071 +/- 0.009) GeV^4.
*   The strong coupling constant used by Narison in the low energy region is alpha_s(1.3 GeV) ~ 0.64 (+0.36, -0.18) +/- 0.02.

## Context

This work extends the squeezed gluon vacuum model, previously considered by Celenza et al. and Blaschke et al., to the U_A(1) problem. It contrasts with the instanton gas model of Novikov et al. (1978) and Hutter (1995), which only considers the propagator term in Witten's formula, and with the magnetic monopole condensate model of Ezawa and Iwazaki. The result enables a direct test of the squeezed vacuum model by comparing its predicted gluon condensate with the empirical values from Shifman, Vainshtein and Zakharov (1979) and Narison (1996).
