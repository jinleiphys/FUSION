# Stability of Classical Chromodynamic Fields -- Addendum

> A system of chromodynamic fields, which can be treated as classical, is generated at the earliest stage of relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Numerical simulations show that the system is unstable but the nature of the instability is not well understood. We study the problem systematically. In the first paper, we have performed a linear stability analysis of space-time uniform chromoelectric and chromomagnetic fields. There they have been considered the Abelian configurations of single-color potentials linearly depending on coordinates and nonAbelian ones where the fields are generated by the multi-color non-commuting uniform potentials. Here we extend and supplement the analysis. We discuss the parallel chromoelectric and chromomagnetic fields which occur simultaneously. We also consider a general nonAbelian configurations of the uniform fields. Finally, we discuss the gauge dependence of our results.

## Key claim

The paper extends the linear stability analysis of classical chromodynamic fields to the case of parallel chromoelectric and chromomagnetic fields occurring simultaneously, and to general nonAbelian configurations of uniform fields; the authors find that the parallel-field configuration is genuinely unstable because its dynamics is dominated by the chromoelectric field, and that the nonAbelian configurations are also classically unstable despite recent conjectures about stability against the Schwinger process.

## Method

The authors solve the linearized Yang-Mills equations in the background gauge for small fluctuations around constant uniform background fields. For parallel electric and magnetic fields, they separate variables and reduce the equations to harmonic-oscillator and inverted-harmonic-oscillator forms. For nonAbelian configurations of uniform magnetic or electric fields parameterized by an arbitrary real number λ, they derive algebraic dispersion relations from the linearized equations and solve them analytically at zero wave vector (k=0) to identify unstable modes.

## Key numbers

- For the parallel-field configuration (Abelian potential), the equations for fluctuations reduce to an inverted harmonic oscillator with no normalizable solutions, indicating genuine instability.
- For the nonAbelian magnetic field configuration at k=0, the unstable mode frequency squared is ω₃² = (1 + λ⁴ - √(1 + 14λ⁴ + λ⁸))/(2λ²) gB, which is negative for all λ² > 0.
- For the nonAbelian electric field configuration at k=0, the unstable mode from the y/z equations has ω₃² = ½(2λ² + 1/λ² - √(8 + 1/λ⁴)) gE, which is negative for λ² < 1; the tx equations yield ω₁² < 0 for λ² > 1 and complex ω² for all λ.
- For the unstable mode U⁻ of the Abelian magnetic field (lowest energy, k_x = k_z = 0, n=0), the growth rate is γ² = gB, and the energy density grows as ε = ½B² + gB δ² e^{-gB y²} e^{2√(gB) t}.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous linear stability analysis (Bazak and Mrowczynski, 2021) which studied uniform chromoelectric and chromomagnetic fields separately, both in Abelian and nonAbelian configurations. It addresses the instability observed in numerical simulations of the Color Glass Condensate approach to relativistic heavy-ion collisions (Romatschke and Strickland, 2005; Romatschke and Venugopalan, 2006). The analysis of gauge-inequivalent nonAbelian configurations (parameterized by λ) responds to a conjecture by Vachaspati (2022) about stability against the Schwinger process; the present results show classical instability persists. The gauge invariance of the energy-momentum tensor in the linearized theory is demonstrated, enabling physical interpretation of the unstable modes.
