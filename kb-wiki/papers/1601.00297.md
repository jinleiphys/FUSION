# Zero energy scattering calculation in Euclidean space

> We show that the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the scattering amplitude in the limit of zero incident energy can be transformed into a purely Euclidean form, as it is the case for the bound states. The decoupling between Euclidean and Minkowski amplitudes is only possible for zero energy scattering observables and allows determining the scattering length from the Euclidean Bethe-Salpeter amplitude. Such a possibility strongly simplifies the numerical solution of the Bethe-Salpeter equation and suggests an alternative way to compute the scattering length in Lattice Euclidean calculations without using the Luscher formalism. The derivations contained in this work were performed for scalar particles and one-boson exchange kernel. They can be generalized to the fermion case and more involved interactions.

## Key claim

The Bethe-Salpeter equation for scattering at zero incident energy can be transformed into a purely Euclidean form, decoupling from the Minkowski amplitude, but this decoupling is only possible for zero energy scattering observables and allows determining the scattering length from the Euclidean Bethe-Salpeter amplitude alone.

## Method

The authors start from the Bethe-Salpeter equation for two spinless particles exchanging a scalar boson (one-boson exchange kernel) in Minkowski space. They analyze the pole singularities of the propagators, perform a Wick rotation of the integration contour, and show that the residual terms from crossed poles vanish in the limit of zero incident momentum \(k_s \to 0\). The resulting self-consistent integral equation for the Euclidean amplitude \(F_E(k_4,k)\) is solved numerically using spline expansion, and the scattering length is extracted from the on-shell value \(F_E(0,0)\).

## Key numbers

* Scattering length \(a_0 = -3.66\) for parameters \(m=1\), \(\mu=0.50\), \(\alpha=0.50\) (no bound state).
* Scattering length \(a_0 = +12.3\) for parameters \(\mu=0.15\), \(\alpha=2.50\) (two bound states present).
* The scattering length is obtained from the Euclidean amplitude via \(a_0 = -F_E(0,0)/m\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous coupled Euclidean-Minkowski system for arbitrary incident momentum (Carbonell and Karmanov, 2013; "bs_long") to the special case of zero energy, where the coupling disappears. It provides an alternative to the Luscher formalism (Luscher, 1986; Luscher and Lellouch, 2003) for extracting scattering lengths from lattice QCD calculations, and contrasts with the HAL-QCD method (Ishii, 2007; Kurth, 2013) which requires constructing a potential and solving the Schrodinger equation. The result is consistent with the Maiani-Testa no-go theorem (Maiani and Testa, 1990) which forbids extracting scattering amplitudes from Euclidean correlators except at zero energy.
