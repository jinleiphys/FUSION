# A kinetic regime of hydrodynamic fluctuations and long time tails for a Bjorken expansion

> We develop a set of kinetic equations for hydrodynamic fluctuations which are equivalent to nonlinear hydrodynamics with noise. The hydro-kinetic equations can be coupled to existing second order hydrodynamic codes to incorporate the physics of these fluctuations, which become dominant near the critical point. We use the hydro-kinetic equations to calculate the modifications of energy momentum tensor by thermal fluctuations from the earliest moments and at late times in Bjorken expansion. The solution to the kinetic equations precisely determines the coefficient of the first fractional power ($\propto τ^{-3/2}$) in the energy momentum tensor gradient expansion. Numerically, we find that the contribution to the longitudinal pressure from hydrodynamic fluctuations is comparable to the second order hydrodynamic terms for typical medium parameters used to simulate heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper develops a kinetic theory framework for hydrodynamic fluctuations that is equivalent to nonlinear hydrodynamics with noise, and uses it to show that the contribution of these fluctuations to the longitudinal pressure in Bjorken expansion is comparable to second-order hydrodynamic terms for typical heavy-ion collision parameters, but only when the shear viscosity to entropy ratio is small (eta/s = 1/(4pi)).

## Method

The authors derive kinetic equations for the two-point correlation functions of four linearized hydrodynamic fluctuation modes (two sound modes and two transverse diffusive modes) in a Bjorken-expanding background, using a comoving frame with metric g_munu = diag(-1,1,1,tau^2). They solve these hydro-kinetic equations to compute the nonlinear contributions of fluctuations to the energy-momentum tensor, regularizing ultraviolet divergences by renormalizing the background pressure and shear viscosity, and then numerically evaluate the remaining finite long-time tail contribution.

## Key numbers

- The emergent scale defining the kinetic regime is k_* = 1/sqrt(gamma_eta * tau), where gamma_eta = eta/(e+p) is the momentum diffusion coefficient
- The long-time tail contribution to the longitudinal pressure scales as proportional to tau^{-3/2}, with a numerical coefficient of 1.08318/(s * (4pi * gamma_eta * tau)^{3/2})
- For eta/s = 1/(4pi) and tau*T = 4.5: the normalized longitudinal pressure is (1/4)[1 - 0.092(4.5/(tau*T)) + 0.034(4.5/(tau*T))^{3/2} - 0.0008(4.5/(tau*T))^2]
- For eta/s = 2/(4pi) and tau*T = 4.5: the normalized longitudinal pressure is (1/4)[1 - 0.185(4.5/(tau*T)) + 0.013(4.5/(tau*T))^{3/2} - 0.003(4.5/(tau*T))^2]
- The entropy density used is s/T^3 = 13.5, from Borsanyi (2013) and Bazavov (2014)

## Context

This work extends the study of hydrodynamic fluctuations and long-time tails previously discussed by Kovtun, Moore, and Romatschke for static media, now applied to the expanding Bjorken flow relevant for heavy-ion collisions. The hydro-kinetic equations can be coupled to existing second-order hydrodynamic codes to incorporate fluctuation effects that become dominant near the QCD critical point, and the formalism enables future study of non-equilibrium Kibble-Zurek scaling behavior in expanding systems and renormalization of bulk viscosity for non-conformal fluids.
