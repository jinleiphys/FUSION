# On stability of the neutron rich Oxygen isotopes

> Stability with respect to neutron emission is studied for highly neutron-excessive Oxygen isotopes in the framework of Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approach with Skyrme forces Sly4 and Ska. Our calculations show increase of stability around 40O.

## Key claim

Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations with the Skyrme force Ska predict that the neutron-rich isotope ⁴⁰O is stable against neutron emission, whereas with the Sly4 force it appears unstable or only "quasistable" because the last filled level is close to zero and a centrifugal barrier may prevent neutron emission.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations for Oxygen isotopes ¹⁴–⁴⁴O using two Skyrme effective interactions, Sly4 and Ska, with pairing included via a standard pairing constant G = 19/A for both protons and neutrons, restricted to bounded one-particle states. They computed one-neutron and two-neutron separation energies, binding energies, root-mean-square radii, quadrupole moments, and deformation parameters, and compared results to experimental data from Audi (2003). The same approach was also applied to He isotopes ⁴–¹²He and Ca isotopes ³⁸–⁸⁰Ca.

## Key numbers

- ⁴⁰O is predicted stable with Ska forces: binding energy E = 168.274 MeV, one-neutron separation energy Sₙ = 0.593 MeV, one-proton separation energy Sₚ = 36.822 MeV.
- For ⁴⁰O with Ska: neutron rms radius rₙ = 4.202 fm, proton rms radius rₚ = 2.943 fm; neutron and proton deformation parameters β₂ⁿ = 0.004, β₂ᵖ = 0.004.
- Nearby stable even-even isotopes predicted with Ska: ⁴⁰Ne (Sₙ = 0.13 MeV), ⁴²Ne (Sₙ = 0.43 MeV), ⁴⁴Ne (Sₙ = 0.1 MeV), ⁴⁴Mg (Sₙ = 0.8 MeV), ⁴⁶Mg (Sₙ = 0.67 MeV).
- The neutron halo around ⁴⁰O is about 2 fm thick.
- Stability with respect to neutron emission is defined within a narrow range of binding energy of about 0.5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the search for neutron-rich stable nuclei beyond the known drip-line, following experimental progress on the proton-rich side (Mittig 2004, and others) and theoretical studies of new magic numbers near the neutron drip-line (Jonson 2004). The authors previously found that the Ska force gave satisfactory descriptions of deformed nuclei ²⁵Mg and ²⁹–³¹Si (Gontchar, no year given). The results suggest a possible island of stability around ⁴⁰O and neighboring Ne and Mg isotopes, but the authors note that the effect should be checked with other theoretical models because the binding energies involved are very small.
