# Further understanding the nature of $Ω(2012)$ within a chiral quark model

> In our previous works, we have analyzed the two-body strong decays of the low-lying $Ω$ baryon states within a chiral quark model. The results show that the $Ω(2012)$ resonance favors the three-quark state with $J^P=3/2^-$ classified in the quark model. With this assignment, in the present work we further study the three-body strong decay $Ω(2012)\to Ξ^*(1530)\bar{K} \to Ξπ\bar{K}$ and coupled-channel effects on $Ω(2012)$ from nearby channels $Ξ\bar{K}$, $Ωη$ and $Ξ^*(1530)\bar{K}$ within the chiral quark model as well. It is found that the $Ω(2012)$ resonance has a sizeable decay rate into the three-body final state $Ξπ\bar{K}$. The predicted ratio $R_{Ξ\bar{K}}^{Ξπ\bar{K}}=\mathcal{B}[Ω(2012)\to Ξ^*(1530)\bar{K}\to Ξπ\bar{K}]/\mathcal{B}[Ω(2012)\to Ξ\bar{K}]\simeq 12\%$ is close to the up limit $11\%$ measured by the Belle Collaboration in 2019, however, our predicted ratio is too small to be comparable with the recent data $0.97\pm 0.31$. Furthermore, our results show that the coupled-channel effects on the $Ω(2012)$ is not large, its components should be dominated by the bare three-quark state, while the proportion of the molecular components is only $\sim 16\%$. To clarify the nature of $Ω(2012)$, the ratio $R_{Ξ\bar{K}}^{Ξπ\bar{K}}$ is expected to be tested by other experiments.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the $\Omega(2012)$ resonance is predominantly a conventional three-quark $1P$-wave state with $J^P=3/2^-$, but acknowledges that the recently measured three-body decay ratio $R_{\Xi\bar{K}}^{\Xi\pi\bar{K}}=0.97\pm 0.31$ from Belle (2022) is too large to be explained within this picture, while the earlier upper limit of $11\%$ (Belle 2019) was consistent with the quark-model prediction of $\sim 12\%$.

## Method

The authors compute the two-body strong decay $\Omega(2012)\to \Xi\bar{K}$ and the cascade three-body decay $\Omega(2012)\to \Xi^*(1530)\bar{K}\to \Xi\pi\bar{K}$ within the chiral quark model, using nonrelativistic quark-meson interactions and harmonic-oscillator wave functions. They also evaluate coupled-channel effects from the nearby channels $\Xi\bar{K}$, $\Omega\eta$, and $\Xi^*(1530)\bar{K}$ by solving a coupled-channel Schrodinger equation where the bare three-quark state couples to two-hadron continua via the same chiral interaction.

## Key numbers

- $\Gamma_{\Xi \bar{K}} = 3.0 + 2.7 = 5.7$ MeV for $\Omega(2012)\to \Xi^0 K^- + \Xi^- \bar{K}^0$, consistent with the experimental total width $\Gamma_{exp}=6.4^{+2.5}_{-2.0}\pm 1.6$ MeV
- $\Gamma_{\Xi\pi\bar{K}} \simeq 0.67$ MeV for the three-body decay $\Omega(2012)\to \Xi^*(1530)\bar{K}\to \Xi\pi\bar{K}$
- Predicted ratio $R_{\Xi\bar{K}}^{\Xi\pi\bar{K}} = \mathcal{B}[\Omega(2012)\to \Xi^*(1530)\bar{K}\to \Xi\pi\bar{K}] / \mathcal{B}[\Omega(2012)\to \Xi\bar{K}] \simeq 12\%$, compared to the Belle 2019 upper limit $<11\%$ and the Belle 2022 value $0.97\pm 0.31$
- Total width including three-body decay: $\Gamma_{total}\simeq 6.37$ MeV
- Mass shift from coupled-channel effects: $\Delta M \simeq -14.6$ MeV
- Bare three-quark state proportion: $83.6\%$; molecular (continuum) components: $\sim 16\%$ (with $\Xi^*(1530)\bar{K}$ contributing $13.7\%$, $\Xi\bar{K}$ contributing $1.9\%$, $\Omega\eta$ contributing $0.8\%$)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous chiral quark model analyses of $\Omega$ baryon decays (Liu 2019, Xiao 2018) which identified $\Omega(2012)$ as a $1P$-wave $J^P=3/2^-$ three-quark state. It directly addresses the tension between the three-quark picture and the hadronic molecular interpretation proposed by Valderrama (2018), Pavao (2018), Huang (2018), and Gutsche (2019), where similar branching fractions for $\Xi\pi\bar{K}$ and $\Xi\bar{K}$ are expected. The predicted ratio $R_{\Xi\bar{K}}^{\Xi\pi\bar{K}}\simeq 12\%$ is close to the constituent quark model result of $4.5\%$ from Arifi (2022) but disagrees with the latest Belle measurement. The authors call for experimental tests of the ratio and of the radiative decay $\Omega(2012)\to \Omega\gamma$ (predicted branching fraction $\sim 1.7\times 10^{-3}$) to clarify the nature of $\Omega(2012)$.
