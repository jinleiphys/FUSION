# Higher-rank discrete symmetries in the IBM. III Tetrahedral shapes

> In the context of the sf-IBM, the interacting boson model with s and f bosons, the conditions are derived for a rotationally invariant and parity-conserving Hamiltonian with up to two-body interactions to have a minimum with tetrahedral shape in its classical limit. A degenerate minimum that includes a shape with tetrahedral symmetry can be obtained in the classical limit of a Hamiltonian that is transitional between the two limits of the model, U_f(7) and SO_{sf}(8). The conditions for the existence of such a minimum are derived. The system can be driven towards an isolated minimum with tetrahedral shape through a modification of two-body interactions between the f bosons. General comments are made on the observational consequences of the occurrence of shapes with a higher-rank discrete symmetry in the context of algebraic models.

## Key claim

A Hamiltonian of the sf-IBM (interacting boson model with s and f bosons) can have a minimum with tetrahedral shape in its classical limit only if the mixing matrix element \(v^0_{ssff}\) is non-zero and if the specific combination of f-boson two-body matrix elements \(11v_{ffff}^2-18v_{ffff}^4+7v_{ffff}^6\) is positive; the symmetry Hamiltonian transitional between the U\(_f\)(7) and SO\(_{sf}\)(8) limits cannot produce an isolated tetrahedral minimum because this combination vanishes identically.

## Method

The classical limit of the sf-IBM Hamiltonian was obtained as the expectation value in a coherent state parameterized by the octupole shape variables \(\{\beta_3,\delta_3,\vartheta_3,\varphi_3\}\), following the method of Ginocchio and Dieperink (1980). A catastrophe analysis of the resulting energy surface \(E(\beta_3,\delta_3,\vartheta_3,\varphi_3)\) was performed by solving the extremum conditions and requiring positive eigenvalues of the stability matrix at a point with tetrahedral symmetry (\(\beta_3>0\), \(\delta_3=\pi/2\)). The conditions were translated into inequalities on the single-boson energies \(\epsilon_s,\epsilon_f\) and the two-body interaction matrix elements \(v^L_{\ell_1\ell_2\ell'_1\ell'_2}\).

## Key numbers

* The tetrahedral minimum requires \(\beta_3^* = \sqrt{(2c'_0-c'_2)/(2c'_4-c'_2)} > 0\), where \(c'_0,c'_2,c'_4\) are modified coefficients involving the boson energies and interaction matrix elements.
* The condition for a minimum includes \(c_{200}>0\), which translates to \(11v_{ffff}^2-18v_{ffff}^4+7v_{ffff}^6>0\).
* For the symmetry Hamiltonian, the combination \(22(a_f+b_f-9c_f)-36(a_f+b_f-2c_f)+14(a_f+b_f+9c_f)=0\) vanishes identically, preventing an isolated tetrahedral minimum.
* In the example with \(N=6\) bosons, the symmetry Hamiltonian gave an octupole-deformed minimum at \(\beta_3^*\approx0.32\) with no \(\delta_3\) dependence; modifying \(v_{ffff}^2\) to 500 keV introduced a tetrahedral minimum at \(\delta_3^*=\pi/2\).

## Context

This work extends the series by Van Isacker, Bouldjedri, and Zerguine (2015, 2018) on higher-rank discrete symmetries in the IBM, which previously treated octahedral shapes in the sdg-IBM. It addresses the search for tetrahedral nuclear shapes motivated by the octupole degree of freedom, following the geometric parameterization of Hamamoto et al. (1991) and the algebraic framework of the spdf-IBM developed by Engel (1987), Kusnezov (1989, 1990). The results enable future studies of tetrahedral minima in more realistic spdf-IBM calculations and provide conditions for experimental signatures of such shapes.
