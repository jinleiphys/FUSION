# Nucleon Spin Polarisabilities from Polarised Deuteron Compton Scattering

> We present recent results on elastic deuteron Compton scattering calculations for polarised beans and targets up to next-to-leading order within Chiral Effective Field Theory in the Small Scale Expansion variant to implement a dynamical Δ(1232) degree of freedom. A simple power-counting argument discloses that np-intermediate rescattering states must be explicitly included at leading order already. This automatically results in the correct Thomson limit and guarantees current conservation. In view of ongoing effort at MAXlab, proposals at HIGS and plans at MAMI, we address in detail single- and double-polarised observables with linearly or circularly polarised photons on both unpolarised and vector-polarised deuterons. Our results indicate that several of the polarisation observables can be instrumental to extract not only spin-independent nucleon polarisabilities, but also the so-far practically un-determined spin-dependent polarisabilities which parameterise the stiffness of the nucleon spin in external electro-magnetic fields. Amongst the questions addressed are: convergence of the expansion for including the Δ, the role of the np-rescattering contributions, and sensitivity to the deuteron wave function. An interactive Mathematica 7.0 notebook of these findings is available from the authors.

## Key claim

The paper argues that several single- and double-polarised observables in elastic deuteron Compton scattering can be instrumental to extract not only the spin-independent nucleon polarisabilities, but also the so-far practically un-determined spin-dependent polarisabilities, provided that the scalar polarisabilities are first determined to high accuracy from lower-energy experiments.

## Method

The authors performed calculations of elastic deuteron Compton scattering up to next-to-leading order within Chiral Effective Field Theory using the Small Scale Expansion variant to include a dynamical Δ(1232) degree of freedom. They explicitly included np-intermediate rescattering states at leading order to satisfy the Thomson limit and current conservation, and implemented rescattering via the Green's function method. The calculations produced cross-sections and asymmetries for six observables involving linearly or circularly polarised photons on unpolarised and vector-polarised deuterons.

## Key numbers

- The static scalar dipole polarisabilities for the proton are approximately \(\bar{\alpha}^p \approx 11.0\) and \(\bar{\beta}^p \approx 2.8\) (in units of \(10^{-4}\;\mathrm{fm}^3\)), with a theoretical uncertainty of \(\approx 1\).
- A global analysis of 28 deuteron Compton scattering points gave iso-scalar nucleon polarisabilities \(\bar{\alpha}^s = 11.3 \pm 0.7_\mathrm{stat} \pm 0.6_\mathrm{Baldin} \pm 1_\mathrm{th}\) and \(\bar{\beta}^s = 3.2 \mp 0.7_\mathrm{stat} \pm 0.6_\mathrm{Baldin} \pm 1_\mathrm{th}\), with the Baldin sum rule constraint \(\bar{\alpha}^{(s)}+\bar{\beta}^{(s)} = 14.5 \pm 0.6\).
- The Δ(1232) contributes a paramagnetic component to the static magnetic dipole-polarisability of \(\bar{\beta}_{\Delta} = +[7\dots13]\) (in \(10^{-4}\;\mathrm{fm}^3\)).
- The np-rescattering contributions are mandatory when the photon energy \(\omega \lesssim 50\;\mathrm{MeV}\); at higher energies \(\omega \gtrsim 60\;\mathrm{MeV}\) the amplitude becomes perturbative.
- The existing unpolarised deuteron data cover only 28 points in the energy range \(\omega \in [49;94]\) MeV with error bars on the order of 15%.

## Context

This work extends previous χEFT calculations of unpolarised deuteron Compton scattering (Griesshammer 2005a, 2005b) that resolved the "SAL puzzle" where widely varying iso-scalar nucleon polarisabilities had been extracted from data at 94 MeV. The paper builds on the dynamical polarisability framework (Hildebrandt 2004) and the consistent NN power-counting scheme (Griesshammer 2005). The results enable planning of upcoming polarised experiments at MAXlab, HIγS, and MAMI, and provide a roadmap for extracting the four spin-dependent polarisabilities (\(\gamma_{E1E1}\), \(\gamma_{M1M1}\), \(\gamma_{E1M2}\), \(\gamma_{M1E2}\)) that remain practically un-determined.
