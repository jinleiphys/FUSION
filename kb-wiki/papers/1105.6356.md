# Fusion11 Conference Summary

> A summary account of the conference "Fusion11", held in Saint Malo, France, May 2-6, 2011.

## Key claim

This is a conference summary of Fusion11 (Saint Malo, France, May 2-6, 2011) and therefore contains no single original claim; instead it surveys the field's open questions, including that no practical ab-initio many-body theory for fusion exists and that coupled-channel calculations remain "one of the least controllable calculations in physics."

## Method

The author attended 77 talks by experts and provides a narrative summary organized by topic: new facilities (SPIRAL2, ELI-NP, ITER), fusion cross-section models (Barrier Penetration Model, optical potentials, coupled channels), barrier distributions, fusion hindrance, transfer channels, incomplete fusion/fission/evaporation, rare-isotope fusion, cluster states, nuclear astrophysics, and superheavy elements. The summary reports experimental results and theoretical approaches presented by individual speakers, with no new experimental or theoretical work performed by the author.

## Key numbers

- SPIRAL2 at GANIL will produce radioactive beams planned for 2015, enabling study of fusion-evaporation with cross sections possibly at the nb level.
- ELI-NP will use lasers up to 20 petawatts, up to 5 orders of magnitude higher than today's laser intensity.
- ITER is estimated at 15 billion euros.
- New S-factor for the $^3$He+$^4$He reaction from LUNA: $S_{34}=0.567\pm0.018\pm0.004$ keV b; the reduced uncertainty on neutrino flux implies a reduction $\Phi_{^8B} =7.5\% \rightarrow 4.3\%$ and $\Phi_{^7Be} =8\% \rightarrow 4.5\%$.
- Fusion of $^{15}$C+$^{232}$Th shows a fusion enhancement by a factor of 5-6 at $E/V_B\sim 0.85$.
- For $^{40}$Ca+$^{48}$Ca, a first evidence of an S factor maximum in a positive Q-value system was reported, but no firm conclusion could be made due to background levels.
- For $^{96}$Zr+$^{40}$Ca, two-nucleon transfer probabilities require an enhancement of a factor 3 relative to the square of one-nucleon transfer probabilities.

## Context

This summary extends the series of Fusion conference proceedings and covers the state of the field in 2011. It reports that coupled-channel calculations (e.g., by Canto, Hagino, Moro) remain the standard method but struggle with continuum-continuum couplings and fusion hindrance at deep sub-barrier energies, where approaches such as the M3Y-based shallow potential (EB07) and neck-formation models (IHI07) are competing explanations. The summary enables future work by identifying open questions: the origin of fusion hindrance for both Q<0 and Q>0 systems, the role of neutron transfer in sub-barrier enhancement, and the need for a "holy grail" measurement of $^{11}$Li+$^{208}$Pb fusion (Loveland).
