# Collectivity and isomers in the Pb isotopes

> In the present work, we aim to study collectivity in the Pb isotopes in the framework of nuclear shell model. We have performed shell-model calculations using KHH7B effective interaction. The model space of KHH7B interaction consists of 14 orbitals. We have reported results for even-even $^{196-206}$Pb isotopes for spectra and electromagnetic properties. The shell model results for isomeric states are also reported. Our results will be useful to compare upcoming experimental data.

## Key claim

Shell-model calculations using the KHH7B effective interaction reproduce the low-lying energy spectra and isomeric states of even-even \(^{196-206}\)Pb isotopes with reasonable agreement to experiment, but the calculated spectra are systematically compressed for \(^{196-204}\)Pb, and the predictive power improves only as the neutron shell closure at N=126 is approached.

## Method

The authors performed nuclear shell-model diagonalizations using the KSHELL code with the KHH7B effective interaction, which includes 14 orbitals spanning proton orbitals between Z=58-114 and neutron orbitals between N=100-164. For the Pb isotopes, neutrons were restricted to the N=100-126 shell (occupying the \(1f_{5/2}\), \(2p_{3/2}\), \(2p_{1/2}\), and \(0i_{13/2}\) orbitals) while protons were fully filled up to Z=82. Energy levels, B(E2) transition strengths, magnetic and quadrupole moments, and half-lives of isomeric states were computed and compared with experimental data.

## Key numbers

* For \(^{196}\)Pb, the experimental B(E2; \(2^+_1 \rightarrow 0^+_1\)) is \(18.2^{+4.8}_{-4.1}\) W.u., while the shell-model value is 1.4 W.u.
* For \(^{198}\)Pb, the calculated half-life of the \(7^-\) isomeric state is 1.2 \(\mu\)s, compared to the experimental value of 4.19(10) \(\mu\)s.
* For \(^{198}\)Pb, the calculated half-life of the \(9^-\) isomeric state is 4199 ns, while the experimental value is 137(10) ns.
* For \(^{204}\)Pb, the calculated half-life of the \(7^-\) isomeric state is 0.40 \(\mu\)s, compared to the experimental value of \(0.45^{+10}_{-3}\) \(\mu\)s.
* For \(^{204}\)Pb, the calculated half-life of the \(4^+\) isomeric state is 36.2 ns, while the experimental value is 265(6) ns.
* For \(^{206}\)Pb, the calculated B(E2; \(2^+_1 \rightarrow 0^+_1\)) is 0.9 W.u., compared to the experimental value of 2.80(9) W.u.
* The experimental \((5)^-\), \((7)^-\), and \((9^-)\) states in \(^{200}\)Pb lie at 1.908, 2.153, and 2.183 MeV excitation energy, while the calculated values are 1.748, 1.868, and 1.897 MeV, respectively.

## Context

This work extends systematic shell-model studies of Pb isotopes around the Z=82 shell closure, following earlier experimental and theoretical investigations by Brown (2004), Bhoy (2023), and Blomqvist (1980). The calculations use the KHH7B interaction originally developed by Kuo (1974) and McGrory (1975). The results provide a benchmark for interpreting upcoming experimental data from Coulomb excitation and decay studies, and highlight the need for extended model spaces crossing the Z=82 and N=126 shells to improve agreement for neutron-deficient isotopes.
