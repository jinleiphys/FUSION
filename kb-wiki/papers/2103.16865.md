# Investigation of multi-step effects for proton inelastic scattering to the $2^{+}_{1}$ state in $^6$He

> Multi-step effects between bound, resonant, and non-resonant states have been investigated by the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC). In the CDCC, a resonant state is treated as multiple states fragmented in a resonance energy region, although it is described as a single state in usual coupled-channel calculations. For such the fragmented resonant states, one-step and multi-step contributions to the cross sections should be carefully discussed because the cross sections obtained by the one-step calculation depend on the number of those states, which corresponds to the size of the model space. To clarify the role of the multi-step effects, we propose the one-step calculation without model-space dependence for the fragmented resonant states. Furthermore, we also discuss the multi-step effects between the ground, $2^{+}_{1}$ resonant, and non-resonant states in $^6$He for proton inelastic scattering.

## Key claim

The multi-step effects between the ground state and the $2^{+}_{1}$ resonant state in $^6$He are negligible for proton inelastic scattering, contrary to what conventional one-step calculations (which omit coupling between fragmented resonant states) would suggest; the apparent multi-step effects seen in standard CDCC one-step calculations are an artifact of model-space dependence.

## Method

The authors used the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC) with pseudo-state discretization via the Gaussian expansion method to describe $^6$He as a $^4$He + $n$ + $n$ three-body system. They introduced a "resonant one-step calculation" that includes coupling effects between fragmented resonant states but neglects coupling to other states, in contrast to the "conventional one-step calculation" that neglects all coupling between discretized states. The method was first validated on the $^6$Li + $^{40}$Ca system at 26 MeV/nucleon, where $^6$Li is described as a $d$ + $\alpha$ two-body system, before being applied to $^6$He + $p$ at 41 and 25 MeV/nucleon.

## Key numbers

- For $^6$Li + $^{40}$Ca at $E$ = 26 MeV/nucleon: the resonant energy of the $2^+$ state in $^6$Li is 2.75 MeV with a decay width of 0.2 MeV; fragmented resonant states were selected within the range 1.1 MeV $\leq \varepsilon \leq$ 3.9 MeV.
- For $^6$He + $p$: the resonant energy of the $2^{+}_{1}$ state is 0.848 MeV with a decay width of 0.136 MeV; fragmented resonant states were selected within the range 0.5 MeV $\leq \varepsilon \leq$ 1.0 MeV, yielding ten fragmented states.
- Coupling effects from non-resonant states for $I^\pi=0^+$, $1^-$, and $2^+$ reduce the inelastic cross section by approximately 20-30% at 41 MeV/nucleon, with stronger reduction at 25 MeV/nucleon.
- For $^6$Li + $^{40}$Ca, coupling effects from $I^\pi=0^+$ states reduce the cross section by approximately 20-30%.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of proton inelastic scattering to the $2^{+}_{1}$ state of $^6$He at 41 MeV/nucleon previously studied by Lag01 using two methods: AMD-MCC (Kan20-2) and CDCC (Oga20). The AMD-MCC found negligible multi-step effects between ground and $2^{+}_{1}$ states, while the CDCC appeared to show significant multi-step effects. The present paper resolves this discrepancy by showing that the apparent multi-step effects in CDCC arise from model-space dependence in the conventional one-step treatment of fragmented resonant states. The work enables reliable extraction of resonant properties from inelastic scattering data on unstable nuclei by properly accounting for coupling to non-resonant continuum states, which the authors show are important for $^6$He because it is an unstable nucleus.
