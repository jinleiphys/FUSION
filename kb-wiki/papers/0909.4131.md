# Identified particle production, azimuthal anisotropy, and interferometry measurements in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 9.2 GeV

> We present the first measurements of identified hadron production, azimuthal anisotropy, and pion interferometry from Au+Au collisions below the nominal injection energy at the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC) facility. The data were collected using the large acceptance STAR detector at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 9.2 GeV from a test run of the collider in the year 2008. Midrapidity results on multiplicity density (dN/dy) in rapidity (y), average transverse momentum (<pT>), particle ratios, elliptic flow, and HBT radii are consistent with the corresponding results at similar $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ from fixed target experiments. Directed flow measurements are presented for both midrapidity and forward rapidity regions. Furthermore the collision centrality dependence of identified particle dN/dy, <pT>, and particle ratios are discussed. These results also demonstrate the readiness of the STAR detector to undertake the proposed QCD critical point search and the exploration of the QCD phase diagram at RHIC.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first measurements of identified hadron production, azimuthal anisotropy, and pion interferometry from Au+Au collisions at a center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair of 9.2 GeV, which is below the nominal injection energy of the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC). The results are consistent with corresponding data from fixed-target experiments at similar energies, and the measurements demonstrate the readiness of the STAR detector for the proposed QCD critical point search.

## Method

The STAR Collaboration collected data from Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 9.2 GeV during a 2008 test run of the RHIC collider, using the large-acceptance STAR detector. Midrapidity results were obtained for multiplicity density (dN/dy), average transverse momentum (<pT>), particle ratios, elliptic flow (v2), and Hanbury Brown-Twiss (HBT) radii for identified hadrons. Directed flow (v1) was measured at both midrapidity and forward rapidity, and the collision centrality dependence of dN/dy, <pT>, and particle ratios was studied.

## Key numbers

- Midrapidity multiplicity density (dN/dy) and average transverse momentum (<pT>) for identified particles were measured at sqrt(s_NN) = 9.2 GeV in Au+Au collisions.
- Particle ratios (e.g., antiparticle-to-particle ratios) were reported at this energy.
- Elliptic flow (v2) values were extracted for identified hadrons at sqrt(s_NN) = 9.2 GeV.
- HBT radii (pion interferometry) were measured at this energy.
- Directed flow (v1) was measured at both midrapidity and forward rapidity regions.

## Context

This work extends measurements from fixed-target experiments at similar center-of-mass energies (e.g., AGS experiments at Brookhaven) to the RHIC collider environment, providing a bridge between lower-energy fixed-target data and higher-energy RHIC results. The measurements establish a baseline for the QCD critical point search and phase diagram exploration at RHIC, enabling future systematic studies of the beam energy scan program. The paper also demonstrates that the STAR detector can operate effectively at energies below its nominal design injection energy, validating its use for the proposed critical point search program.
