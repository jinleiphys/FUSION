# An example of resonance saturation at one loop

> We argue that the large-Nc expansion of QCD can be used to treat a Lagrangian of resonances in a perturbative way. As an illustration of this we compute the L_10 coupling of the Chiral Lagrangian by integrating out resonance fields at one loop. Given a Lagrangian and a renormalization scheme, this is how in principle one can answer in a concrete and unambiguous manner questions such as at what scale resonance saturation takes place.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the large-Nc expansion of QCD provides a perturbative framework for treating resonance Lagrangians, and demonstrates that at one-loop order the scale at which resonance saturation occurs for the L10 coupling is much lower than previously assumed (around 380 MeV rather than the vector meson mass scale of 770 MeV), with the caveat that this result is renormalization scheme dependent.

## Method

The authors compute the L10 coupling of the Chiral Lagrangian by integrating out vector, axial-vector, scalar, and singlet scalar resonance fields at one-loop order using the antisymmetric tensor representation for spin-one fields. They match the Pi_LR two-point Green's function computed from the resonance Lagrangian to the same function computed from the low-energy Chiral Lagrangian, imposing the condition L10^R(mu*)=0 to define the scale of resonance saturation.

## Key numbers

- The scale at which resonance saturation occurs (L10^R(mu*)=0) is mu* ~ 380 MeV, much lower than M_V = 770 MeV
- At mu ~ 490 MeV the one-loop contribution to L10 vanishes altogether
- At mu ~ 800 MeV (approximately M_V), the one-loop corrections produce a ~60% reduction relative to the tree-level contribution
- The tree-level prediction for the combination F_A^2/M_A^2 - F_V^2/M_V^2 is -15/(32 pi^2 sqrt(6))
- The experimental value used for L10 at M_rho is L10(M_rho) = (-5.13 +/- 0.19) x 10^(-3)
- The chiral limit value of f_pi is taken as 87 +/- 3.5 MeV
- Scalar masses used: M_S = M_{S1} = 983 MeV; scalar couplings: c_d^2 = tilde(c_d)^2/3 = 1024 MeV^2

## Context

This work extends the tree-level resonance saturation program of Ecker et al. (1989) and Ecker et al. (1989b) by incorporating one-loop quantum corrections within the large-Nc expansion framework proposed by 't Hooft (1974). The analysis is complementary to the short-distance matching studies of Knecht and Nyffeler (2000) and Moussallam (1997), and demonstrates that the simple resonance Lagrangian of Eq. (1) lacks the proper short-distance properties to produce a fully consistent description at next-to-leading order in 1/Nc, motivating the search for a more complete resonance Lagrangian of QCD.
