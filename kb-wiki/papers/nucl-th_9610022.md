# Photoproduction of pions and etas in nuclei

> We calculate the cross sections for inclusive one-pion, two-pion and eta photoproduction in nuclei in the photon energy range from 300 MeV to 900 MeV within the framework of a semi-classical BUU transport model. Our results are compared with existing experimental data and discussed with respect to a calculation of the total photoabsorption cross section.

## Key claim

The semi-classical BUU transport model, using the same microscopic ingredients as a prior calculation of total photoabsorption, reproduces the size of observed pion production cross sections in nuclei reasonably well but shows too much structure in the Δ-resonance region, suggesting that multi-body absorption mechanisms not fully captured in the model may be important.

## Method

The authors solve coupled Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport equations for nucleons, baryonic resonances up to 2 GeV mass, pions, eta mesons, and rho mesons, initialized with a Woods-Saxon density distribution and local Thomas-Fermi momentum distribution. Elementary photon-nucleon cross sections for one-pion, two-pion, and eta production are parameterized using Breit-Wigner resonance amplitudes with helicity amplitudes from the Particle Data Group (1994) and from Kru95, plus background terms; the coherent sum of resonance and background amplitudes is replaced by a properly scaled incoherent sum for implementation in the transport model. Medium modifications include Fermi motion, Pauli blocking, collision broadening of resonance widths, and a real Δ-potential of −30 MeV at normal nuclear density.

## Key numbers

- The calculated total π⁰ cross section on ¹²C overestimates experimental data from Arends (1984) in the Δ-resonance region by about 30%.
- For charged pion photoproduction on nuclei (measured up to 400 MeV), the calculation overestimates cross sections by about 25%.
- On ²⁰⁸Pb, the calculated total π⁰ cross section shows somewhat better agreement with experiment than on ¹²C, but still fails to explain the broad structure in the Δ-region.
- The calculated total eta production cross section on ⁴⁰Ca and ²⁰⁸Pb agrees very well with experimental data from Robig-Landau; on ¹²C, the calculation overestimates the cross section by about 20% at higher photon energies.
- Using a constant inelastic ηN cross section of 30 mb and a constant elastic ηN cross section of 20 mb reproduces both angular and energy differential eta cross sections simultaneously.
- The duration of the photon-nucleus reaction is about 20 fm/c, independent of photon energy.
- For ⁴⁰Ca, about 1/3 of produced etas survive propagation through the nucleus; for ²⁰⁸Pb, about 1/6 survive.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior BUU calculation of total photoabsorption (Effenberger et al., 1996) to exclusive channels, aiming to understand medium modifications of the elementary photon-nucleon interaction. The results are compared with experimental data from Arends (1984) for pion production and from Robig-Landau for eta production, and with Δ-hole model calculations by Gomez Tejedor et al. (1995) for two-pion production, where the two approaches differ by about a factor of 2. The paper identifies the need for experimental measurements of exclusive cross sections (one-pion, two-pion, nucleon knock-out) in the 400 MeV to 1 GeV range to separate in-medium modifications from final-state interactions and to explain the disappearance of the D₁₃ resonance in total photoabsorption.
