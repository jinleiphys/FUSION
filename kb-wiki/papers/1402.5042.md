# Beyond Rainbow-Ladder in bound state equations

> In this work we devise a new method to study quark anti-quark interactions beyond simple ladder-exchange that yield massless pions in the chiral limit. The method is based on the requirement to have a representation of the quark-gluon vertex that is explicitly given in terms of quark dressings functions. We outline a general procedure to generate the Bethe-Salpeter kernel for a given vertex representation. Our method allows not only the identification of the mesons' masses but also the extraction of their Bethe-Salpeter wave functions exposing their internal structure. We exemplify our method with vertex models that are of phenomenological interest.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new method to construct Bethe-Salpeter kernels for quark-antiquark interactions that go beyond the simple rainbow-ladder truncation, with the condition that the quark-gluon vertex must be explicitly expressed in terms of quark dressing functions. The method preserves chiral symmetry and yields massless pions in the chiral limit only when the vertex representation is fully specified in all Dirac-space directions, not just on the physical point.

## Method

The authors derive the Bethe-Salpeter kernel as the functional derivative of the quark self-energy with respect to the dressed quark propagator, generating two types of contributions: type I from cutting the quark propagator (modified ladder exchange) and type II from varying the quark-gluon vertex. They apply this cutting procedure to three vertex models: a two-term Ball-Chiu vertex (2BC), the full Ball-Chiu vertex (BC/ABC), and the Munczek vertex formulated in coordinate space. The chiral properties are tested by verifying the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity (AXWTI) and the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation (GMOR).

## Key numbers

- The renormalization point for quark mass evaluation is set to μ = 19 GeV
- The squared pion mass scales linearly with the quark bare mass and passes through the origin for the 1BC and 2BC vertex models, confirming GMOR relation
- The paper reports no specific meson mass values; the numerical results focus on demonstrating the GMOR relation rather than providing a spectrum of masses

## Context

This work extends the functional approach using Dyson-Schwinger equations and Bethe-Salpeter equations beyond the rainbow-ladder truncation, which has been widely applied for QCD phenomenology by Maris (2003), Maris (2005), Goecke (2011), and Eichmann (2009). The rainbow-ladder approach fails for excited states, states with finite width, and mesons with axial-vector or scalar quantum numbers as shown by Eichmann (2008) and Qin (2011). The method is similar in spirit but technically different from the approach outlined by Chang (2009, 2010, 2011), and has the advantage of allowing extraction of Bethe-Salpeter wave functions for studies of form factors and distribution amplitudes. The Munczek vertex model follows the work of Munczek (1994) on chiral symmetry in bound state equations.
