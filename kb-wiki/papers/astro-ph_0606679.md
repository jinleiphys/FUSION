# Influence of protons on the capture of electrons by 7Be in the Sun

> The capture of electrons by the nucleus $^{7}Be$ from the three-body initial state $p+e^{-}+^{7}Be$ in the continuum is studied. On the basis of the expansion of the three-body continuum wave function in the small parameter $ε\approx ({m_e}/{m_p})^{1/2}$ [$m_e$ ($m_p$) is the electron (proton) mass], the role of the protons on the electron capture is considered. The results are compared with the traditional treatment of the electron capture by the nucleus $^{7}Be$. For stars with the density and temperature like in the centre of the Sun the studied mechanism can make non-negligible contribution to the capture rate.

## Key claim
The presence of a proton near \(^{7}\mathrm{Be}\) in the Sun can increase the electron capture rate by about 10% relative to the standard binary reaction, because the electron feels an effective charge \(Z=5\) instead of \(Z=4\); however, this contribution is only non-negligible at densities and temperatures similar to the solar centre, and the effect is suppressed at lower densities.

## Method
The authors expand the three-body continuum wave function for the system \(p + e^- + ^{7}\mathrm{Be}\) in the small parameter \(\epsilon \approx (m_e/m_p)^{1/2} \approx 0.0233\), separating the Jacobi coordinates in the leading order. They then compute the ratio \(\varsigma(R,T)\) of the electron capture rate from the three-body initial state to the standard binary capture rate, using Coulomb continuum wave functions for the electron with effective charge \(Z=5\) (for the ternary process) and with the Fermi function for \(Z=4\) (for the binary process). The ratio is averaged over a Gaussian distribution of the proton–\(^{7}\mathrm{Be}\) distance \(R\) using solar density and temperature profiles from the SSMBP2004 model.

## Key numbers
- The expansion parameter \(\epsilon \approx 0.0233\), implying the leading-order correction is at the 2% level.
- For solar conditions at \(R_s/R_\odot = 0.06\) (where the electron capture intensity is maximal), the mean ratio \(\langle \varsigma(R_0,T) \rangle = 0.0965\) (about 9.7% of the binary rate).
- At the same solar radius, the alternative ratio \(\langle \varsigma_C(R_0,T) \rangle = 0.0696\).
- For the analogous process with \(^{4}\mathrm{He}\) instead of a proton, \(\langle \varsigma(R_0,T) \rangle = 0.0036\) at \(R_s/R_\odot = 0.06\), about 27 times smaller than the proton case.
- The proton density at \(R_s/R_\odot = 0.06\) is \(\rho_p = 48.9\ \mathrm{g/cm^3}\), and the mean distance \(R_0 = 3.250 \times 10^4\ \mathrm{fm}\).
- At the very centre of the Sun (\(R_s/R_\odot = 0.007\)), \(\langle \varsigma(R_0,T) \rangle = 0.0991\) and \(\langle \varsigma_C(R_0,T) \rangle = 0.0718\).

## Context
This work extends the standard treatment of the \(pp\) cycle, which only considers the binary reactions \(p + ^{7}\mathrm{Be} \rightarrow ^{8}\mathrm{B} + \gamma\) and \(e^- + ^{7}\mathrm{Be} \rightarrow ^{7}\mathrm{Li} + \nu\). It introduces a three-body mechanism previously omitted, using an expansion method from Belyaev et al. (2003) as an alternative to the Born–Oppenheimer approach. The results imply that the concentration of \(^{8}\mathrm{B}\) (the source of high-energy solar neutrinos) may be reduced, which could affect comparisons with solar neutrino observations. The effect cannot be mimicked by Debye screening corrections, which were shown to be small in earlier work (e.g., Bahcall 1994).
