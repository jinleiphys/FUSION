# Generalized parton distributions in constituent quark models

> An approach is proposed to calculate Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs) in a Constituent Quark Model (CQM) scenario. These off-diagonal distributions are obtained from momentum space wave functions to be evaluated in a given non relativistic or relativized CQM. The general relations linking the twist-two GPDs to the form factors and to the leading twist quark densities are consistently recovered from our expressions. Results for the leading twist, unpolarized GPD, H, in a simple harmonic oscillator model, as well as in the Isgur and Karl model, are shown to have the general behavior found in previous estimates. NLO evolution of the obtained distributions, from the low momentum scale of the model up to the experimental one, is also shown. Further natural applications of the proposed formalism are addressed.

# Generalized parton distributions in constituent quark models

## Key claim

The authors propose a formalism to calculate Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs) from any non-relativistic or relativized Constituent Quark Model (CQM), but the resulting expressions are valid only under the condition that the squared momentum transfer is much smaller than the nucleon mass squared (Δ² ≪ M²), and only the valence quark contribution in the positive DGLAP region (x ≥ ξ/2) can be evaluated, not the sea region.

## Method

The authors start from the field-theoretical definition of the light-cone correlation function for the twist-two unpolarized GPD H(x,ξ,Δ²), perform an Impulse Approximation analysis, and take the non-relativistic limit to express H in terms of the nucleon momentum-space wave functions. The resulting expression is evaluated using the Isgur and Karl (IK) constituent quark model and a simple harmonic oscillator model, with the proton wave function including contributions up to the 2ℏω shell. The low-scale model results are then evolved to experimental scales using NLO perturbative QCD evolution via the Golec-Biernat–Martin off-diagonal parton distributions.

## Key numbers

- The harmonic oscillator parameter is fixed to α² = 1.35 fm⁻² to reproduce the low-t behavior of the charge form factor (the proton rms radius).
- The Isgur and Karl model wave function coefficients are: a_S = 0.931, a_S' = -0.274, a_M = -0.233, a_D = -0.067.
- The initial scale for QCD evolution is chosen as μ₀² = 0.34 GeV², corresponding to the valence scenario of Glück, Reya, and Vogt (1995).
- Evolved GPDs are presented at Q² = 10 GeV² for two values of ξ and Δ².
- The quark mass used throughout is m_q ≈ M/3, where M is the nucleon mass.

## Context

This work extends the successful CQM approach, previously applied to standard parton distributions (e.g., by Traini et al. and by Jaroń and Rożynek), to the calculation of GPDs, which had not been done in a CQM framework before. It builds on the non-relativistic limit discussion by Burkardt (2000) and on the general GPD formalism of Ji (1997). The results are compared qualitatively to earlier bag model estimates by Melnitchouk et al. and to soliton model calculations by Pobylitsa et al. The formalism enables future calculations of other GPD functions, Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering observables, and extensions to relativistic models and constituent quark substructure.
