# Prediction of the analyzing power for $\vec{p}$+$^{6}$He elastic scattering at 200 MeV from $\vec{p}$+$^{4}$He elastic scattering at 200 MeV

> We apply the cluster-folding (CF) model for $\vec{p}+^{6}$He scattering at 200 MeV, where the potential between $\vec{p}$ and $^{4}$He is fitted to data on $\vec{p}+^{4}$He scattering at 200 MeV. For $\vec{p}+^{6}$He scattering at 200 MeV, the CF model reproduces measured differential cross section with no free parameter, We then predict the analyzing power $A_y(q)$ with the CF model, where $q$ is the transfer momentum. Johnson, Al-Khalili and Tostevin construct a theory for one-neutron halo scattering, taking (1) the adiabatic approximation and (2) neglecting the interaction between a valence neutron and a target, and yield a simple relationship between the elastic scattering of a halo nucleus and of its core under certain conditions. We improve their theory with (3) the eikonal approximation in order to determine $A_y(q)$ for $^{6}$He from the data on $A_y(q)$ for $^{4}$He. The improved theory is accurate, when approximation (1)--(3) are good. Among the three approximations, approximation (2) is most essential. The CF model shows that approximation (2) is good in $0.9 < q < 2.4$ fm$^{-1}$. In the improved theory, the $A_y(q)$ for $^{6}$He is the same as that for $^{4}$He. In $0.9 < q < 2.4$ fm$^{-1}$, we then predict $A_y(q)$ for $\vec{p}+^{6}$He scattering at 200 MeV from measured $A_y(q)$ for $\vec{p}+^{4}$He scattering at 200 MeV. We thus predict $A_y(q)$ with the model-dependent and the model-independent prescription. The ratio of differential cross sections measured for $^{6}$He to that for $^{4}$He is related to the wave function of $^{6}$He. We then determine the radius between $^{4}$He and the center-of-mass of valence two neutrons in $^{6}$He. The radius is 5.77 fm.

## Key claim

The authors predict the analyzing power \(A_y(q)\) for \(\vec{p}+^{6}\)He elastic scattering at 200 MeV from measured \(A_y(q)\) for \(\vec{p}+^{4}\)He scattering at the same energy, but only in the momentum-transfer range \(0.9 \lesssim q \lesssim 2.4\) fm\(^{-1}\) where the approximation of neglecting the interaction between the valence neutrons and the target proton is valid.

## Method

The authors improve the valence-core cutting (VCC) theory of Johnson, Al-Khalili, and Tostevin (1997) by adding the eikonal approximation to the existing adiabatic approximation and the neglect of valence-neutron–target interactions. They use a cluster-folding (CF) model for \(\vec{p}+^{6}\)He scattering, where the \(\vec{p}-^{4}\)He optical potential is fitted to data at 200 MeV, to test the validity of neglecting the valence-neutron interactions. The improved VCC theory then yields that \(A_y(q)\) for \({}^{6}\)He equals that for \({}^{4}\)He, allowing a model-independent prediction from the measured \({}^{4}\)He data.

## Key numbers

* The radius between \({}^{4}\)He and the center-of-mass of the two valence neutrons in \({}^{6}\)He is determined to be 5.77 fm.
* The approximation of neglecting valence-neutron–target interactions is good in the momentum-transfer range \(0.9 \lesssim q \lesssim 2.4\) fm\(^{-1}\) for 200 MeV.
* The binding energy of the valence two-neutron system relative to the \({}^{4}\)He core, obtained from a one-range Gaussian potential fit, is 0.172 MeV.
* The Jensen-Shannon divergence between the \(A_y\) distributions for \({}^{4}\)He at 72 MeV and \({}^{6}\)He at 71 MeV is approximately 0.0028, indicating close similarity.

## Context

This work extends the VCC theory of Johnson, Al-Khalili, and Tostevin (1997) for one-neutron halo scattering to the two-neutron halo nucleus \({}^{6}\)He, using the eikonal approximation to relate the scattering of the halo nucleus directly to that of its \({}^{4}\)He core at the same laboratory energy. It enables model-independent predictions of analyzing powers for halo-nucleus scattering from core-scattering data, and determines the core–valence-neutron separation radius from the ratio of differential cross sections. The CF model results are compared with prior folding-model calculations by Toyokawa (2013) and Burrows (2018), which did not fully reproduce the \({}^{4}\)He data at larger momentum transfers.
