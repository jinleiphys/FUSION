# Comments and extensions of a suggestion for a relativistic charge density definition

> A recent suggested definition of a relativistically correct three dimensional charge density of an extended hadron is shown to be physically and intuitively connected to an earlier relativistically correct two dimensional charge density studied in the context of light-front physics. Looking at spin-1/2 hadrons, such a connection is shown to exist for both the polarized and unpolarized cases.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a recently suggested relativistically correct three-dimensional charge density for spin-1/2 hadrons is physically and intuitively connected to an earlier relativistically correct two-dimensional charge density from light-front physics, with the 3D density being the 2D areal density averaged over all orientations of the hadron's momentum direction.

## Method

The author evaluates the expectation value of the charge density operator for a spin-1/2 hadron in a state that is a superposition of momentum eigenstates with unlimited momentum magnitude traveling in all directions, effectively a position eigenstate centered at the origin. The matrix element of the electromagnetic current is expressed in terms of the Dirac and Pauli form factors, and the integration over momentum is performed by splitting vectors into components parallel and perpendicular to the hadron's momentum direction. The resulting expressions are compared to the known light-front two-dimensional charge densities, and the angular averaging is carried out explicitly for both unpolarized and polarized cases.

## Key numbers

* The unpolarized 3D charge density is related to the 2D light-front density by \(\rho_1(r) = \sigma_1(r) / (2r)\), where \(\sigma_1(r)\) is the unpolarized surface charge density on the disc.
* The polarized 3D charge density has the form \(\rho(\vec r) = \rho_1(r) + \rho_2(r) \sin(\phi_r - \phi_s) \, \sin\theta_r / (1 + |\cos\theta_r|)\), with \(\rho_{1,2}(r) = \sigma_{1,2}(r) / (2r)\).
* The 2D light-front densities are given by \(\sigma_1(r) = \int (Q dQ / 2\pi) J_0(Qr) F_1(-Q^2)\) and \(\sigma_2(r) = \int (dQ / 2\pi) (Q^2 / 2m) J_1(Qr) F_2(-Q^2)\), where \(F_1\) and \(F_2\) are the Dirac and Pauli form factors.

## Context

This work extends the suggestion of Epelbaum (2022) for a relativistic three-dimensional charge density definition and its follow-ups by Panteleeva (2022, 2023). It connects that definition to the earlier light-front two-dimensional charge density formalism developed by Miller (2007) and Burkardt (2002), and to the spin-dependent results of Carlson (2007) and Miller (2010). The paper enables a physical interpretation of the new 3D density as an orientation average of the well-established 2D light-front density, providing intuitive support for the new definition and clarifying its relationship to existing relativistic charge density approaches.
