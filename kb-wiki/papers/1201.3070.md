# Pre-neutron emission mass distributions for low-energy neutron-induced actinide fission

> According to the driving potential of a fissile system, we propose a phenomenological fission potential for a description of the pre-neutron emission mass distributions of neutron-induced actinide fission. Based on the nucleus-nucleus potential with the Skyrme energy-density functional, the driving potential of the fissile system is studied considering the deformations of nuclei. The energy dependence of the potential parameters is investigated based on the experimental data for the heights of the peak and valley of the mass distributions. The pre-neutron emission mass distributions for reactions 238U(n, f), 237Np(n, f), 235U(n, f), 232Th(n, f) and 239Pu(n, f) can be reasonably well reproduced. Some predictions for these reactions at unmeasured incident energies are also presented.

## Key claim

The authors propose a phenomenological fission potential, based on the driving potential of the fissile system calculated with the Skyrme energy-density functional, that can "reasonably well" reproduce pre-neutron emission mass distributions for five actinide reactions at low incident neutron energies, but the model's accuracy is limited by the assumption that peak and valley heights change linearly with energy and by the omission of temperature-dependent nuclear structure effects.

## Method

The driving potential of the fissile system was calculated using the dinuclear system (DNS) concept, combining the Q-value of the fragment pair with the Coulomb barrier height obtained from the Skyrme energy-density functional and the extended Thomas-Fermi approximation, including ground-state deformations from the finite-range droplet model and a dynamical octupole deformation of |β₃| = 0.08. A phenomenological fission potential U(A) was constructed from three harmonic-oscillator functions, with the heavy-fragment peak fixed at A₂ = 140 and the potential parameters (peak height P(A₁), valley height P(A₀)) assumed to vary linearly with incident neutron energy Eₙ. The parameters α₀, β₀, α₁, and β₁ in the linear relations P(A₀) = α₀ + β₀ Eₙ and P(A₁) = α₁ + β₁ Eₙ were determined by fitting experimental data for each reaction system.

## Key numbers

- For ²³⁸U(n,f) at Eₙ = 1.6 MeV: calculated peak height P(A₁) = 6.3765, valley height P(A₀) = 0.0106; at Eₙ = 5.5 MeV: P(A₁) = 5.9515, P(A₀) = 0.0870.
- Potential parameters for ²³⁸U(n,f): α₁ = 6.5508, β₁ = -0.1090, α₀ = -0.0208, β₀ = 0.0196.
- Potential parameters for ²³⁵U(n,f): α₁ = 6.6550, β₁ = -0.0952, α₀ = -0.0042, β₀ = 0.0237.
- Potential parameters for ²³⁹Pu(n,f): α₁ = 6.1293, β₁ = -0.1517, α₀ = 0.0067, β₀ = 0.0343.
- Potential parameters for ²³²Th(n,f): α₁ = 7.0900, β₁ = -0.0631, α₀ = -0.0656, β₀ = 0.0350.
- Potential parameters for ²³⁷Np(n,f): α₁ = 6.4132, β₁ = -0.1008, α₀ = -0.0113, β₀ = 0.0338.
- The heavy-fragment peak position was fixed at A₂ ≈ 140 for all reactions studied.

## Context

This work extends phenomenological approaches to fission mass distributions, such as the multi-Gaussian fits of Katakura (2003, 2008) and Wahl (2008), and the systematics of Liu et al. (2008) and Gorodisskiy et al. (2008), by introducing a fission potential derived from the driving potential of the dinuclear system. The model enables predictions of pre-neutron emission mass distributions at unmeasured incident neutron energies for actinides from thorium to plutonium, and the authors suggest that combining this approach with the radial basis function method of Wang and Liu (2011) could further improve accuracy.
