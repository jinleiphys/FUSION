# Emerging of massive gauge particles in inhomogeneous local gauge transformations: replacement of Higgs mechanism

> A generalized theory of gauge transformations is presented on the basis of the covariant Hamiltonian formalism of field theory, for which the covariant canonical field equations are equivalent to the Euler-Lagrange field equations. Similar to the canonical transformation theory of point dynamics, the canonical transformation rules for fields are derived from generating functions. Thus---in contrast to the usual Lagrangian description---the covariant canonical transformation formalism automatically ensures the mappings to preserve the action principle, and hence to be {\em physical}. On that basis, we work out the theory of inhomogeneous local gauge transformations that generalizes the conventional local SU(N) gauge transformation theory. It is shown that massive gauge bosons naturally emerge in this description, which thus could supersede the Higgs mechanism.

## Key claim

The paper claims that massive gauge bosons can emerge naturally from a generalized theory of inhomogeneous local gauge transformations formulated in the covariant Hamiltonian (DeDonder-Weyl) formalism, potentially superseding the Higgs mechanism without requiring a symmetry-breaking potential or a "Mexican hat" potential.

## Method

The author constructs a generating function of type F₂ that defines an inhomogeneous linear transformation of an N-tuple of complex fields, combining a local unitary transformation U(x) with a local shifting vector φ(x). The covariant canonical transformation formalism is then used to derive the transformation rules for the fields and their conjugates, and the requirement of local form-invariance forces the introduction of two independent sets of gauge fields: a Hermitian matrix of 4-vector gauge fields a_{KJμ} and a vector of 4-vector gauge fields b_{Iμ} coupled via a constant mass-dimension matrix M. The final gauge-invariant Hamiltonian is constructed by adding terms that compensate the explicit x-dependence of the generating function, and the corresponding Lagrangian is obtained via Legendre transformation.

## Key numbers

* The constant matrix M has dimension [M] = L⁻¹, which the paper states "has the natural dimension of mass."
* The gauge coupling constant g is introduced as a real coupling constant in the gauge Hamiltonian H_a.
* The transformation involves an N-tuple of complex fields φ_I with I = 1, …, N, where N is the number of fields in the tuple.

## Context

This work extends the conventional local SU(N) gauge transformation theory by including inhomogeneous linear mappings of the fields, generalizing the homogeneous unitary transformations treated in Struckmeier and Reichau (2012). The paper directly addresses the long-standing inconsistency that the conventional gauge principle requires gauge bosons to be massless, which previously led to the Higgs-Kibble mechanism (Higgs 1964, Kibble 1967) as a supplement. The approach is formulated within the DeDonder-Weyl covariant Hamiltonian formalism (DeDonder 1930, Weyl 1935), which the author argues is more natural than the Lagrangian formulation for constructing physical gauge transformations.
