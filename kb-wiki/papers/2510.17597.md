# Photon radiation induced by rescattering in strong-interacting medium with a magnetic field

> The photon radiation induced by rescattering in a magnetized medium is investigated in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Within the high-energy limit, the photon emission rate and the associated electromagnetic energy loss are derived using the Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev formalism at first order in opacity, for a quark jet propagating a quark-gluon plasma under a background magnetic field. Quantitative analysis shows a slight suppression of the overall photon radiation over a broad range of jet energies in this process. This reduction in photon yield consequently leads to a moderate decrease in the electromagnetic energy loss of the jet. Our results contribute to a better understanding of the electromagnetic properties of strongly interacting matter in high-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions and motivate experimental comparison of photon yields from quark-gluon plasma with similar properties but distinct magnetic field strengths.

## Key claim

The authors derive that a background magnetic field causes a slight suppression of medium-induced photon radiation from a quark jet traversing a quark-gluon plasma, leading to a moderate decrease in the jet's electromagnetic energy loss, but only within the high-energy limit and using a weak-field expansion of the propagator that neglects higher-order magnetic field terms.

## Method

The authors employ the Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev (GLV) formalism at first order in opacity, using the Gyulassy-Wang (GW) model with static color-screened Yukawa potentials to describe jet-medium interactions. They replace the vacuum scalar propagator with a charged scalar propagator expanded to quadratic order in the magnetic field via the weak-field approximation, and compute the photon emission rate and electromagnetic energy loss for a quark jet propagating perpendicular to the reaction plane in a magnetized quark-gluon plasma.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic field strength at RHIC is estimated as approximately \(eB\sim 5m_{\pi}^2 \sim 10^{9}\mathrm{G}\) and at LHC as \(eB\sim 50m_{\pi}^2 \sim 10^{10}\mathrm{G}\).
- The quark thermal mass is given by \(m_{\mathrm{T}}^2 = g_s^2 C_F T^2/8\) with \(C_F=4/3\).
- The kinematic limits for transverse momentum integration are \(\mu^2 \le \bm{k}_\perp^2 \le 4E^2x(1-x)\).
- The longitudinal location distribution integral yields \(I(\omega_0,L) = 4/(4+\omega_0^2 L^2)\).

## Context

This work extends the GLV formalism for medium-induced photon radiation and electromagnetic energy loss (Zhang 2010, Zhang 2016) by incorporating a background magnetic field, building on prior studies of magnetic field effects in heavy-ion collisions (Kharzeev 2007, Skokov 2009, Tuchin 2013). The results enable future experimental comparisons of photon yields from quark-gluon plasmas with different magnetic field strengths, and contribute to understanding electromagnetic properties of strongly interacting matter in high-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions.
