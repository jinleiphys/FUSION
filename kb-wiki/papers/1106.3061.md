# Jet Flavor Tomography of Quark Gluon Plasmas at RHIC and LHC

> A new Monte Carlo model of jet quenching in nuclear collisions, CUJET1.0, is applied to predict the jet flavor dependence of the nuclear modification factor for fragments {\it $f=π,D,B,e^-$} from quenched jet flavors {\it $g,u,c,b$} in central collisions at RHIC and LHC. The nuclear modification factors for different flavors are predicted to exhibit a novel level crossing pattern over a transverse momentum range $5< p_T< 100$ GeV which can test jet-medium dynamics in quark gluon plasmas.

## Key claim
The CUJET1.0 Monte Carlo model predicts that the nuclear modification factors for different jet flavors (g, u, c, b) will exhibit a novel level crossing pattern over a transverse momentum range of 5 < p_T < 100 GeV, which can test jet-medium dynamics in quark gluon plasmas. The pattern is surprisingly insensitive to systematic uncertainties such as the initial functional form of opacity or the value of the formation time τ₀, making it a robust prediction.

## Method
The CUJET1.0 code uses Monte Carlo techniques to compute finite order in opacity contributions to the jet-medium induced gluon radiative spectrum, replacing the static Debye screened effective potential with a dynamical (magnetic enhanced) transverse momentum exchange distribution. The model integrates over longitudinally expanding and transverse diffuse QGP geometries, includes elastic and radiative fluctuating energy loss distributions, and convolves over flavor-dependent pQCD invariant jet spectral densities and final fragmentation functions. The opacity is constrained at RHIC by fitting a reference point R_AA^π(p_T=10 GeV)=0.2 with α_s=0.3, and the extrapolation to LHC assumes dN_ch/dη scaling of the opacity as measured by ALICE.

## Key numbers
- RHIC reference point: R_AA^π(p_T=10 GeV)=0.2, used to constrain the model with α_s=0.3 and dN/dy=1000
- LHC charged particle pseudorapidity density: dN_ch^LHC/dη=1600 for central 0-5% Pb+Pb at 2.76 ATeV, giving a 2.2 increase factor of ρ_QGP at LHC relative to RHIC
- RHIC energy: √s=0.2 ATeV; LHC energy: √s=2.76 ATeV
- Coupling parameter varied as α_s=0.3±0.03 to test robustness
- Formation time τ₀ tested at 0 and 1 fm/c; freeze-out temperature T_f with μ_f=g T_f=200 MeV
- Transverse momentum range for predictions: 5 < p_T < 100 GeV at LHC, out to p_T < 50 GeV at RHIC

## Context
This work extends the GLV (Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev), DGLV (Djordjevic-Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev), and WHDG (Wicks-Horowitz-Djordjevic-Gyulassy) opacity series approaches by including dynamical features requiring Monte Carlo techniques, developed as part of the DOE JET Topical Collaboration. The model addresses the heavy quark jet quenching puzzle discovered at RHIC and the surprising increase of jet transparency at LHC compared to expected linear scaling of jet opacity. The predicted flavor-dependent level crossing pattern could discriminate between pQCD-based tomographic models and holographic gravity dual models, which predict qualitatively different heavy quark quenching patterns as noted by Horowitz (2007).
