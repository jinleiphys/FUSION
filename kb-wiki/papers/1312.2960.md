# BPS Skyrme model and nuclear binding energies

> We use the classical BPS soliton solutions of the BPS Skyrme model together with corrections from the collective coordinate quantization of spin and isospin, the electrostatic Coulomb energies, and a small explicit breaking of the isospin symmetry - accounting for the proton-neutron mass difference - to calculate nuclear binding energies. We find that the resulting binding energies are already in excellent agreement with their physical values for heavier nuclei, demonstrating thereby that the BPS Skyrme model is a distinguished starting point for a detailed quantitative investigation of nuclear and low-energy strong interaction physics.

# BPS Skyrme model and nuclear binding energies

## Key claim

The BPS Skyrme model, when combined with collective coordinate quantization of spin and isospin, Coulomb energies, and a small explicit isospin breaking term, yields nuclear binding energies in excellent agreement with experimental values for heavier nuclei, but the model overestimates binding energies for small nuclei, as the authors explicitly acknowledge.

## Method

The authors take the classical BPS soliton solutions of the BPS Skyrme model (the limit of the generalized Skyrme model where only the sextic term and the potential term remain) as the leading contribution to nuclear masses. They then add corrections from rigid rotor quantization of spin and isospin, electrostatic Coulomb energies calculated from the electric charge density operator via collective coordinate quantization, and a linear isospin-breaking term parametrized by a constant fit to the proton-neutron mass difference. The three model parameters (lambda, mu, and a_I) are fit to the masses of the proton, neutron, and Ba-138, and binding energies are computed for nuclei with even baryon number using an axially symmetric ansatz.

## Key numbers

* The classical soliton energy for baryon number n is E_sol = (64 sqrt(2) pi / 15) mu lambda |n|.
* The fitted parameter values are: lambda mu = 48.9902 MeV, (mu/lambda)^(1/3) = 0.604327 fm^(-1), and a_I = -1.68593 MeV.
* The rotational energy for B=1 (proton/neutron, j=1/2) is E_rot = (105/(512 sqrt(2) pi)) * (3/4) * (hbar^2) / (lambda^2 (mu/lambda)^(1/3)).
* The proton mass used for fitting is M_p = 938.272 MeV; the neutron mass is M_n = 939.565 MeV; the Ba-138 mass is 137.894 u = 137.894 * 931.494 MeV.
* The Coulomb energy for B=1 proton includes the term (1/(sqrt(2) pi epsilon_0)) (mu/lambda)^(1/3) * (128/(315 pi^2) + 156625/1317888); for the neutron the second term is -53585/1317888.

## Context

This work extends the BPS Skyrme model proposed by Adam et al. (2013) and Speight, which provides a field-theoretic realization of the liquid drop model of nuclei. It contrasts with the standard Skyrme model where binding energies are too large (about 10% per baryon) compared to physical nuclei, and where calculations require demanding numerics. The authors differ from the prior approach of Marleau (2013) in their treatment of the isospin current density operator and in not including perturbative contributions from the L_2 and L_4 terms, which they argue would grow faster than linearly with baryon number. The work enables a unified field-theoretic description of nuclear binding energies that is almost entirely analytical, and suggests that extensions including pion degrees of freedom could improve results for light nuclei.
