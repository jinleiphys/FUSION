# Azimuthal asymmetry of J/Psi suppression in non-central heavy-ion collisions

> The azimuthal asymmetry of $J/ψ$ suppression in non-central heavy-ion collisions is studied within a dynamic model of $J/ψ$ suppression in a deconfined partonic medium. Within this model, $J/ψ$ suppression in heavy-ion collisions is caused mainly by the initial state nuclear absorption and dissociation via gluon-$J/ψ$ scattering in deconfined partonic medium. Only the second mechanism gives arise to azimuthal asymmetry of the final $J/ψ$ production. We demonstrate that if there is an onset of suppression by quark-gluon plasma (QGP) in the NA50 data, it must be accompanied by the non-vanishing azimuthal asymmetry. Using the same critical density above which the QGP effect enters, we predict the azimuthal asymmetric coefficient $v_2$ as well as the survival probability for $J/ψ$ at the RHIC energy.

# Azimuthal asymmetry of J/Psi suppression in non-central heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
If the anomalous J/ψ suppression observed by NA50 at SPS energies is caused by quark-gluon plasma formation above a critical parton density, then this suppression must be accompanied by a non-vanishing azimuthal asymmetry (v₂) that rises sharply with transverse energy, distinguishing it from hadronic absorption scenarios which would produce a continuous centrality dependence.

## Method
The authors model J/ψ suppression using a micro-dynamic approach where dissociation occurs via gluon-J/ψ scattering in a deconfined partonic medium, with the gluonic dissociation cross section calculated perturbatively for both J/ψ and χc states. The survival probability is computed by integrating the velocity-averaged dissociation cross section over the plasma evolution with 1+1D Bjorken longitudinal expansion, including transverse expansion effects, and the azimuthal asymmetry coefficient v₂ is extracted from the azimuthal angle dependence of the final J/ψ distribution relative to the impact parameter.

## Key numbers
- Critical parton density determined from NA50 data fit: ρc = 3.3 fm⁻³
- Nuclear absorption cross section for pre-resonance c-cbar pairs: σabs = 5.8 mb
- χc binding energy: εχ = 0.250 GeV
- Plasma formation time: τ₀ = 1 fm
- Transverse expansion velocity used: 0.4c for SPS, 0.6c for RHIC
- χc contributes approximately 40% of initial J/ψ production
- At RHIC, v₂ for J/ψ is predicted to be sizable at pT = 3 GeV in non-central collisions

## Context
This work extends the dynamic model of J/ψ suppression by Wang (2000) to include azimuthal asymmetry as a new observable, and contrasts with the screened potential model of Blaizot et al. (1996) where all J/ψ are dissociated above a critical density. The study enables experimental discrimination between QGP-based and hadronic absorption explanations of the anomalous suppression seen by NA50 (1999), and provides predictions for RHIC experiments such as PHENIX. The approach differs from hadronic dissociation models by Matinian (1999), Haglin (2000), and others which give larger hadron-J/ψ cross sections.
