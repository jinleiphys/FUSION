# Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations for isospin asymmetric nuclear matter based on improved approximation schemes

> We present Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations for isospin asymmetric nuclear matter which are based on improved approximations schemes. The potential matrix elements have been adapted for isospin asymmetric nuclear matter in order to account for the proton-neutron mass splitting in a more consistent way. The proton properties are particularly sensitive to this adaption and its consequences, whereas the neutron properties remains almost unaffected in neutron rich matter. Although at present full Brueckner calculations are still too complex to apply to finite nuclei, these relativistic Brueckner results can be used as a guidance to construct a density dependent relativistic mean field theory, which can be applied to finite nuclei. It is found that an accurate reproduction of the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock equation of state requires a renormalization of these coupling functions.

## Key claim

The authors present improved Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) calculations for isospin asymmetric nuclear matter that go beyond previous work by adapting the Bonn potential matrix elements to explicitly account for the proton-neutron mass splitting in the neutron-proton channel, rather than using an averaged mass approximation. The improved treatment primarily affects proton properties in neutron-rich matter, while neutron properties remain almost unchanged.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Bethe-Salpeter equation in the ladder approximation using the Thompson propagator, with the Bonn A potential as the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction. They apply the subtracted T-matrix representation scheme and introduce a sixth independent covariant amplitude in the neutron-proton channel to properly handle distinct neutron and proton effective masses, along with a modified angle-averaged Pauli operator that accounts for Fermi ellipsoids of different sizes for neutrons and protons. The self-consistency scheme uses the quasi-particle approximation and reference spectrum approximation, iterating until convergence is reached.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy curves are presented for asymmetry parameters β = (n_n - n_p)/n_B ranging from 0.0 (symmetric nuclear matter) to 1.0 (pure neutron matter)
- At a fixed nuclear density of n_B = 0.166 fm^{-3}, the isovector optical potential at k=0 is in good agreement with the empirical value of 22-34 MeV
- All three microscopic EoSs (DBHF, BHF, variational) yield maximum neutron star masses between 2.2-2.3 solar masses, consistent with the observed binary pulsar mass of 2.1 ± 0.2 solar masses (1σ level)
- The DBHF calculations predict a Dirac mass splitting of m*_{D,n} < m*_{D,p} in neutron-rich matter across the entire density range, while the nonrelativistic mass shows the opposite behavior m*_{NR,n} > m*_{NR,p}

## Context

This work extends previous DBHF calculations by van Dalen et al. (2004-2005) that used only five covariants in the np channel, improving the treatment of the proton-neutron mass splitting. The results are compared to nonrelativistic BHF calculations by Lejeune (2000) and variational calculations by Akmal et al. (1998), showing reasonable agreement up to 1.5 times saturation density. The DBHF results can serve as guidance for constructing density dependent relativistic mean field (DDRMF) theory applicable to finite nuclei, following the approach of Fuchs et al. (1995) and Hofmann et al. (2001).
