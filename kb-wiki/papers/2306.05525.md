# Exploration of hadronization through heavy flavor production at the future Electron-Ion Collider

> The future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC), which is expected to start construction at Brookhaven National Laboratory in 2025, will utilize high-luminosity high-energy electron+proton and electron+nucleus collisions to explore several fundamental questions in the high energy and nuclear physics fields. Exploring how matter is formed from quarks and gluons, which is referred to as the hadronization process, is one of the EIC science objectives. The EIC project detector design led by the ePIC collaboration can realize a series of high precision heavy flavor hadron and jet measurements. Heavy flavor jet substructure and heavy flavor hadrons inside jets, which can provide direct information about the heavy quark hadronization process, have been studied in simulation for electron+proton and electron+nucleus collisions at EIC. The associated physics projections and comparison with latest theoretical calculations will be presented.

## Key claim

Simulation studies of heavy flavor jet substructure and heavy flavor hadrons inside jets at the future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) show that these measurements can extract heavy quark energy loss and fragmentation processes in the less constrained kinematic regions with significantly better precision than existing measurements, but the results are based on the current conceptual detector design and the e+A event generator is still under development.

## Method

The analysis framework used PYTHIA8 for event generation, GEANT4 for detector performance evaluation, and included beam remnant and QCD backgrounds for electron+proton and electron+nucleus collisions at the EIC. Heavy flavor hadrons (D-mesons and B-mesons) and jets were reconstructed using the ePIC project detector design with a pseudorapidity coverage of |η| ≤ 3.5, and the reconstructed yields were evaluated with an integrated luminosity of 10 fb⁻¹. For e+A collisions, yields were scaled from e+p results by the corresponding mass number A.

## Key numbers

- Instantaneous luminosity of the EIC: 10³³⁻³⁴ cm⁻²s⁻¹ with bunch crossing rate around 10 ns
- Electron beam energy: 5 GeV to 18 GeV; proton/nucleus beam energies: 41 GeV, 100-275 GeV
- Reconstructed D-mesons (D±, D⁰, Dₛ±) and B-mesons (B±, B⁰, Bₛ⁰) mass spectra obtained in simulation for 10 GeV electron and 100 GeV proton collisions
- Reconstructed charm and bottom jets in 63.2 GeV e+p collisions can probe kinematics in the low transverse momentum region pT < 15 GeV/c with around one year EIC operation (10 fb⁻¹)
- Projected accuracy of the nuclear modification factor RₑAᵤ for D⁰ mesons inside charm jets studied in 63.2 GeV e+Au collisions across pseudorapidity regions -2<η<0, 0<η<2, and 2<η<3.5
- Normalized hadron momentum fraction zproj distributions of inclusive hadrons and D⁰ mesons inside charm jets evaluated in three jet pT bins: 3 GeV/c < pT,jet < 5 GeV/c, 5 GeV/c < pT,jet < 8 GeV/c, and pT,jet > 8 GeV/c in 63.2 GeV e+p collisions

## Context

This work extends the heavy flavor physics program outlined in the EIC Yellow Report (2021) and prior studies from the Los Alamos National Laboratory heavy flavor group (Lanl_hf, 2022). The simulation results demonstrate that the EIC will provide significantly better precision in extrapolating the charm quark fragmentation function in medium, especially in the high zproj region (zproj > 0.6), compared to existing results. The study enables future extraction of fragmentation functions for different heavy flavor hadron species and will be expanded to other observables such as heavy flavor jet angularity (Lanl_hf1, 2022) and measurements in different e+A collisions to extract medium-induced parton showering and energy loss effects.
