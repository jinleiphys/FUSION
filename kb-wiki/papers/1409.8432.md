# Dynamical holographic QCD model

> We develop a dynamical holographic QCD model, which resembles the renormalization group from ultraviolet (UV) to infrared (IR). The dynamical holographic model is constructed in the graviton-dilaton-scalar framework with the dilaton background field $Φ$ and scalar field $X$ responsible for the gluodynamics and chiral dynamics, respectively. We summarize our results on hadron spectra, QCD phase transition and transport properties including the jet quenching parameter and the shear/bulk viscosity in the framework of the dynamical holographic QCD model.

## Key claim

The authors claim that their dynamical holographic QCD model, constructed in a graviton-dilaton-scalar framework, can simultaneously describe hadron spectra, QCD phase transition thermodynamics, and transport properties (jet quenching parameter, shear/bulk viscosity) in a unified way, with the hedge that the quenched (pure-gluon) version is used for thermodynamics and transport, while light flavors are added separately for meson spectra.

## Method

The model is built in 5D anti-de Sitter space with a graviton-dilaton-scalar action, where the dilaton field Φ(z) is dual to the dimension-4 gluon operator Tr G² and the scalar field X(z) is dual to the chiral condensate. The dilaton profile is chosen as Φ(z) = μ_G² z² tanh(μ_{G²}⁴ z² / μ_G²), which behaves as z⁴ at UV (matching gauge invariance) and as z² at IR (producing linear confinement). The metric is solved self-consistently from Einstein equations, and finite temperature is introduced via a black hole horizon, with thermodynamics derived from the Bekenstein-Hawking entropy and Hawking temperature. Transport coefficients are computed using holographic Kubo formulas: the jet quenching parameter from the Nambu-Goto action for a Wilson loop, bulk viscosity from metric perturbations h_xx, and shear viscosity from h_xy with higher-derivative R² corrections.

## Key numbers

- Scalar glueball spectra computed with μ_G = 1 GeV and μ_{G²} = 1 GeV; the model reproduces the correct ground state and Regge slope (figure shows lattice data comparison).
- Trace anomaly (ε-3p)/T⁴ computed for pure SU(3) gluon system with G_5 = 1.25 and μ_G = 0.75 GeV; the near-T_c sharp peak matches lattice data from LAT-EOS-G.
- Jet quenching parameter ĝ/T³ shows a peak around T = 1.1 T_c with height around 40, for G_5 = 1.25 and λ = 6π.
- Bulk viscosity over entropy density ζ/s shows a sharp peak near the transition temperature (μ_G = 0.75 GeV, G_5 = 1.25), in agreement with lattice results from LAT-xis-KT.
- Shear viscosity over entropy density η/s shows a valley at around T = 1.1 T_c for β = 0.01, γ = -√(8/3), with the universal AdS value 1/(4π) modified by higher-derivative corrections.

## Context

This work extends the soft-wall holographic QCD model of Karch (2006) by making the dilaton background dynamical and self-consistently solving the metric, rather than imposing a fixed AdS metric. It also builds on the gauge/gravity duality framework of Maldacena (1997), Gubser (1998), and Witten (1998). The model enables simultaneous study of hadron spectra, equation of state, and transport properties within a single holographic setup, and its predictions for bulk viscosity and jet quenching can be compared with lattice QCD and heavy-ion collision data. Future work could extend the model to include full chiral dynamics and finite baryon density.
