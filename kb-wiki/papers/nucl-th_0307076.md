# Mass Formulae for Supernarrow Dibaryons and Exotic Baryons

> The mass formulae for the supernarrow dibaryons and the exotic baryons with small masses are consructed. With this aim their self energies are calculated in one loop approximation using the dispersion relations with two subtractions. The values of the masses obtained in this approach are in a good agreement with the experimental data. The mass formula for the baryons is also used to calculate the mass of the $Δ(1232)$ resonance.

# Mass Formulae for Supernarrow Dibaryons and Exotic Baryons

## Key claim

The mass formulae constructed using dispersion relations with two subtractions in the one-loop approximation reproduce the experimentally observed masses of supernarrow dibaryons and exotic baryons, provided that the final baryon and the baryon in the intermediate state have opposite parities for solutions below the πN production threshold; the coupling constant is assumed equal to the πNN coupling constant, which may be an effective value rather than a physical one.

## Method

The self energies of supernarrow dibaryons and exotic baryons were calculated in the one-loop approximation using dispersion relations with two subtractions at the mass shell of the intermediate-state baryon or dibaryon. For exotic baryons, the nonlinear integral equation was solved iteratively by taking the nucleon plus pion in the intermediate state to obtain the first state, then using each successive state as the intermediate baryon for the next. For supernarrow dibaryons, the self energy was calculated through pion interaction with the 6-quark state of the deuteron or with lighter supernarrow dibaryons, using vertices with either opposite or same parities for the final and intermediate dibaryons.

## Key numbers

- Three supernarrow dibaryon peaks observed at M = 1904 ± 2, 1926 ± 2, and 1942 ± 2 MeV with widths equal to the experimental resolution (~5 MeV) and statistical significances of 6.0, 7.0, and 6.3 standard deviations respectively
- Model calculation yields supernarrow dibaryon masses: 1904 MeV (J^P = 1^-), 1924 MeV (1^+), 1943 MeV (1^-), 1962 MeV (1^+), 1982 MeV (1^-), and 2001 MeV (1^+)
- Exotic baryon masses calculated: 963.4 MeV (J^P = 1/2^-), 987 MeV (1/2^+), 1010 MeV (1/2^-), 1033 MeV (1/2^+), 1056 MeV (1/2^-), and 1079 MeV (1/2^+)
- The Δ(1232) resonance mass calculated from the mass formula gives 1247 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 1232 MeV
- Missing mass peaks in the reaction pd → p + pX₁ observed at M_X₁ = 966 ± 2, 986 ± 2, and 1003 ± 2 MeV
- Coupling constant g²₁/4π = 26.585 fixed by requiring reproduction of the 1904 MeV supernarrow dibaryon mass
- Probability of the 6-quark state existence in the deuteron estimated as η ∼ 1 × 10⁻⁴ from the production cross section

## Context

This work extends the experimental observations of supernarrow dibaryons by Konobeevski et al. (2001-2003) and exotic baryons by Tatischeff et al. (2000), providing a theoretical framework that reproduces the observed mass values where previous models failed. The approach contrasts with the MIT bag model calculations by Mulders et al. (1980), which predicted dibaryon masses exceeding the pion production threshold, and with the chiral soliton model by Kopeliovich (1995), which gave masses below threshold but could not reproduce the experimental data. The mass formula enables predictions of yet-unobserved states at 1033 and 1079 MeV for exotic baryons and at 2001 MeV for supernarrow dibaryons, and the author notes that if the exotic baryons do not exist as real resonances, the equation may instead describe effective masses corresponding to narrow peaks in the phase space of supernarrow dibaryon decay products.
