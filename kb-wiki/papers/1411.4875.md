# Many-body forces in the equation of state of hyperonic matter

> In this work we introduce an extended version of the formalism proposed originally by Taurines et al. that considers the effects of many-body forces simulated by non-linear self-couplings and meson-meson interaction contributions. In this extended version of the model, we assume that matter is at zero temperature, charge neutral and in beta-equilibrium, considering that the baryon octet interacts by the exchange of scalar-isoscalar ($σ$,$\,σ^*$), vector-isoscalar ($ω$,$\,ϕ$), vector-isovector ($\varrho$) and scalar-isovector ($δ$) meson fields. Using nuclear matter properties, we constrain the parameters of the model that describe the intensity of the indirectly density dependent baryon-meson couplings to a small range of possible values. We then investigate asymmetric hyperonic matter properties. We report that the formalism developed in this work is in agreement with experimental data and also allows for the existence of massive hyperon stars (with more than $2M_{\odot}$) with small radii, compatible with astrophysical observations.

## Key claim

The paper claims that an extended relativistic mean-field model incorporating many-body forces through a parametric derivative coupling can simultaneously satisfy experimental nuclear matter constraints and produce hyperon stars with masses exceeding 2 solar masses and small radii, but only for a restricted range of the parameter zeta that controls the many-body force strength.

## Method

The authors extended the Taurines et al. (2001) formalism by including the full baryon octet (n, p, Lambda, Sigma+, Sigma0, Sigma-, Xi0, Xi-) and the meson fields sigma, sigma*, omega, phi, rho, and delta in a relativistic mean-field model at zero temperature with beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality. They constrained the nucleon-meson coupling constants by fitting symmetric nuclear matter properties (saturation density 0.15 fm^-3, binding energy -15.75 MeV, effective nucleon mass 0.66-0.78 m_N, compressibility modulus 200-300 MeV) and asymmetric matter properties (symmetry energy 25-35 MeV, symmetry energy slope 60-115 MeV), while hyperon-meson couplings were determined from SU(6) symmetry relations and fits to hyperon potential depths.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: rho_0 = 0.15 fm^-3
- Binding energy per baryon: B/A = -15.75 MeV
- Effective nucleon mass at saturation ranges from 0.66 m_N (zeta=0.040) to 0.78 m_N (zeta=0.129)
- Compressibility modulus K_0 ranges from 297 MeV (zeta=0.040) to 211 MeV (zeta=0.129)
- Lambda hyperon potential depth: U_Lambda^N = -28 MeV
- Sigma hyperon potential depth: U_Sigma^N = +30 MeV
- Xi hyperon potential depth: U_Xi^N = -18 MeV
- Symmetry energy a_sym^0 range: 25-35 MeV
- Symmetry energy slope L_0 range: 60-115 MeV

## Context

This work extends the parametric coupling formalism of Taurines et al. (2001) by adding the delta, sigma*, and phi meson fields to better describe asymmetric matter and hyperon interactions. It builds on the Walecka model (1974) and its extensions by Boguta and Bodmer (1977), and addresses the "hyperon puzzle" raised by observations of 2-solar-mass neutron stars by Demorest (2010) and Antoniadis (2013). The model enables systematic studies of how many-body forces, hyperon potentials, and strange meson couplings affect neutron star mass-radius relations and composition.
