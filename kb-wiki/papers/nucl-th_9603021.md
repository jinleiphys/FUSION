# Neutron Structure Functions From Nuclear Data

> The spin-averaged structure function of the neutron, F_2^n, is extracted from recent deuteron data, taking into account the small but significant corrections due to nuclear effects in the deuteron. At small x, the F_2D/F_2p ratio measured by the New Muon and Fermilab E665 Collaborations is interpreted to suggest a small amount of shadowing in deuterium, which acts to enhance F_2^n for x < 0.1. A careful treatment of Fermi motion, binding and nucleon off-shell effects in the deuteron also indicates that the neutron/proton structure function ratio as x -> 1 is consistent with the perturbative QCD expectation of 3/7, but larger than the traditional value of 1/4.

## Key claim

The neutron/proton structure function ratio \(F_2^n/F_2^p\) as \(x \rightarrow 1\) is consistent with the perturbative QCD expectation of 3/7, but larger than the traditional value of 1/4, provided one includes a careful treatment of Fermi motion, binding, and nucleon off-shell effects in the deuteron rather than using on-mass-shell kinematics.

## Method

The authors extracted the spin-averaged neutron structure function \(F_2^n\) from deuteron data using a deconvolution method that subtracts off-shell corrections and smears the proton data with a nucleon momentum distribution function. At large \(x\) (\(x \gtrsim 0.3\)), they applied the impulse approximation with binding, Fermi motion, and off-shell effects modeled via relativistic quark-nucleon vertex functions. At small \(x\) (\(x \lesssim 0.1\)), they used a hybrid model combining vector meson dominance (VMD) for low \(Q^2\) and Pomeron exchange for high \(Q^2\) to account for nuclear shadowing, along with meson-exchange contributions.

## Key numbers

- The off-shell correction \(\delta^{\rm (off)} F_2^D\) is of order \(1-2\%\) for \(x \lesssim 0.9\).
- The shadowing correction to \(F_2^D\) is less than \(1\%\) at \(x \sim 0.004\), leading to a less than \(2\%\) increase in the neutron structure function over the uncorrected value.
- The E665 Collaboration data extend to \(x \sim 10^{-5}\), where the \(F_2^D/F_2^p\) ratio deviates from unity by about \(4\%\), suggesting shadowing in deuterium.
- The effect of shadowing on the Gottfried sum \(S_G\) is a reduction between \(-0.010\) and \(-0.026\), or about \(4\) to \(10\%\) of the measured value, yielding \(S_G \approx 0.2\).
- The reanalyzed SLAC data at an average \(Q^2 \approx 12\) GeV\(^2\) show \(F_2^n/F_2^p\) approaching 3/7 as \(x \rightarrow 1\), with the \(d/u\) ratio approaching a non-zero value of 1/5.

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses of deuteron data by Bodek et al. (1979) and the European Muon Collaboration (EMC), which used on-mass-shell kinematics and omitted binding effects, leading to a lower \(F_2^n/F_2^p\) ratio of 1/4. The off-shell calculation is qualitatively similar to results from Uchiyama and Saito (1988), Kaptari and Umnikov (1991), and Braun and Tokarev (1993), and also to the nuclear density method of Frankfurt and Strikman (1988). The findings enable more reliable extraction of neutron structure functions for testing perturbative QCD predictions and for evaluating the Gottfried sum rule, with implications for future semi-inclusive experiments at CEBAF and HERMES.
