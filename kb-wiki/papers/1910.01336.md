# Single- and two-nucleon antikaon absorption in nuclear matter with chiral meson-baryon interactions

> We developed a microscopic model for antikaon absorption on two nucleons in nuclear matter. The absorption is described within a meson-exchange picture and the primary $K^-N$ interaction strength is derived from state-of-the-art chiral coupled channel meson-baryon interaction models. We took into account the medium modification of the $K^-N$ scattering amplitudes due to the Pauli correlations. We derived the $K^-NN$ as well as $K^-N$ optical potentials as functions of nuclear matter density including the real part of the $K^-NN$ potential. We calculated the $K^-$ single- and two-nucleon absorption fractions and branching ratios for various mesonic and non-mesonic channels. We confirmed the crucial role of in-medium effects in our calculations. Our results are in very good agreement with available experimental data from old bubble chamber experiments as well as with the latest results from the AMADEUS collaboration.

## Key claim

The authors developed a microscopic model for antikaon absorption on two nucleons in nuclear matter, finding that the Pauli blocking effect reduces the absorptive potentials by approximately one half at saturation density and that the results reproduce experimental data remarkably well, especially when these in-medium effects are taken into account.

## Method

The absorption was described within a meson-exchange picture using Feynman diagrams, where the primary K-N interaction strength was derived from two chiral coupled channel meson-baryon interaction models (the Barcelona model and the Prague model). The K-NN optical potential was obtained from the corresponding K- self-energy by summing contributions from two-fermion-loop and one-fermion-loop diagrams for all considered mesonic and non-mesonic channels, incorporating Pauli blocking effects in the K-N scattering amplitudes. The authors employed form factors with cut-off values in the range 800-1200 MeV to account for uncertainties tied to short-range baryon-baryon correlation effects.

## Key numbers

- The multi-nucleon absorption fraction measured for K- capture on a mixture of C, F, and Br was found to be 0.26 ± 0.03
- The multi-nucleon absorption fraction for capture on Ne was 0.28 ± 0.03, and for capture on C was 0.19 ± 0.03
- The branching ratio for the reaction K- + 4He → Λ + d + n was 0.035 ± 0.002
- The ratio of branching ratios R = BR(K-pp → Λp) / BR(K-pp → Σ0p) was found to be around 0.7 by the AMADEUS collaboration
- The depth of the imaginary K-NN potential calculated with free-space amplitudes reached approximately -80 MeV at saturation density ρ0
- The K-NN absorption started to be dominant over the K-N absorption for ρ > 0.9ρ0
- The difference between potentials calculated for nucleon momentum k = 0 and k = kF was up to 10 MeV at ρ0
- At ρ0, the BCN model was less absorptive by about 20 MeV than the P model
- The depth of the total K- absorptive potential obtained for BK- = 50 MeV was reduced by more than one half at ρ0 with respect to the BK- = 0 MeV case

## Context

This work extends the approach of Nagahiro et al. (2012) for obtaining meson-nucleon-nucleon self-energies within a meson-exchange picture, and builds upon the exploratory study by Sekihara et al. (2009, 2012) who connected two-nucleon antikaon absorption to chiral K-N interactions. The model provides a unified description of the K- single- and multi-nucleon potential that can be used to evaluate K- multi-nucleon potential around normal nuclear density ρ0, which is relevant for K- nuclear quasibound states and goes beyond what kaonic atom data can probe (reliably only up to about 50% of normal nuclear density).
