# Model-Independent Determination of Isotopic Cross Sections per Fission for Reactor Antineutrinos

> Model-independent reactor isotopic cross sections per fission are determined by global fits of the reactor antineutrino data from High-Enriched Uranium (HEU) reactor rates, Low-Enriched Uranium (LEU) reactor rates, and reactor fuel evolution data. Taking account of the implicit quasi-linear relationship between the fission fractions of $^{239}\rm{Pu}$ and $^{241}\rm{Pu}$ in the LEU reactor data, the Inverse-Beta-Decay (IBD) yields and their correlations of the fissionable isotopes $^{235}\rm{U}$, $^{238}\rm{U}$, and Pu's are obtained. The data-driven isotopic IBD yields provide an anomaly-free model for the reactor isotopic cross sections per fission, where better than 1\% accuracy of the expected reactor IBD yields can be achieved for future experiments.

## Key claim

The authors claim that by exploiting a quasi-linear relationship between the fission fractions of 239Pu and 241Pu in low-enriched uranium reactors, they can determine isotopic inverse-beta-decay yields from reactor data in a way that is practically model-independent, achieving better than 1% accuracy for future reactor antineutrino experiments, but only if the residual 241Pu contribution is treated with a model correction that is small enough to be negligible.

## Method

The authors performed global chi-squared fits of reactor antineutrino data from high-enriched uranium (HEU) reactor rates, low-enriched uranium (LEU) reactor rates, and fuel evolution data from Daya Bay and RENO. They used a quasi-linear relationship f_241 = k * f_239 between the fission fractions of 239Pu and 241Pu in LEU reactors, with k = 0.177 from combined data, to define a combined plutonium isotopic IBD yield sigma_Pu = sigma_239 + k * sigma_241, and treated the residual 241Pu contribution with a model correction using the Huber-Mueller model.

## Key numbers

* The best-fit isotopic IBD yield for 235U from HEU reactor rates alone is (6.36 ± 0.08) × 10^(-43) cm^2/fission, which is about 6% lower than the Huber-Mueller model prediction.
* The combined fit of evolution data and HEU reactor rates yields sigma_235 = (6.36 ± 0.08) × 10^(-43) cm^2/fission, sigma_Pu = (5.60 ± 0.21) × 10^(-43) cm^2/fission, and sigma_238 = (7.25 ± 1.88) × 10^(-43) cm^2/fission.
* The fit of all data (evolution data plus all reactor rates) gives sigma_235 = (6.37 ± 0.08) × 10^(-43) cm^2/fission, sigma_238 = (6.63 ± 1.30) × 10^(-43) cm^2/fission, and sigma_Pu = (5.64 ± 0.20) × 10^(-43) cm^2/fission.
* The chi-squared minimum for the full fit is 25.7 with 37 degrees of freedom, giving a goodness-of-fit of 92%.

## Context

This work extends the model-independent approach to reactor antineutrino fluxes by using the quasi-linear relationship between 239Pu and 241Pu fission fractions, building on the fuel evolution analyses of Daya Bay (2017) and RENO (2018). It provides an anomaly-free model that can be used to predict reactor antineutrino yields for future experiments such as JUNO, and it contrasts with the Huber-Mueller, Estienne-Fallot, HKSS, and KI theoretical flux models, which show discrepancies with experimental data (the 6% rate anomaly and the shape anomaly).
