# Multiplicity Scaling of Light Nuclei Production in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Using the nucleon coalescence model based on kinetic freeze-out nucleons from the 3D MUSIC+UrQMD and the 2D VISHNU hybrid model with a crossover equation of state, we study the multiplicity dependence of deuteron ($d$) and triton ($t$) production from central to peripheral Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=$ 7.7, 14.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4 and 200 GeV and Pb+Pb at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=2.76$ TeV, respectively. It is found that the ratio $N_t N_p/N_d^2$ of the proton yield $N_p$, deuteron yield $N_d$ and triton yield $N_t$ exhibits a scaling behavior in its multiplicity dependence, i.e., decreasing monotonically with increasing charged-particle multiplicity. A similar multiplicity scaling of this ratio is also found in the nucleon coalescence calculation based on kinetic freeze-out nucleons from a multiphase transport (AMPT) model. The scaling behavior of $N_t N_p/N_d^2$ can be naturally explained by the interplay between the sizes of light nuclei and the nucleon emission source. We further argue that the multiplicity scaling of $N_t N_p/N_d^2$ can be used to validate the production mechanism of light nuclei, and the collision energy dependence of this yield ratio can further serve as a baseline in the search for the QCD critical point in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The yield ratio \(N_t N_p/N_d^2\) of triton, deuteron, and proton yields exhibits a universal scaling behavior with charged-particle multiplicity that is independent of collision energy from \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 7.7\) GeV to 2.76 TeV, decreasing monotonically as multiplicity increases. This scaling arises from the interplay between the finite sizes of light nuclei and the nucleon emission source, and the authors argue it can serve as a baseline for the QCD critical point search, but only under the condition that the underlying hydrodynamic and transport models used (MUSIC+UrQMD, VISHNU, AMPT) contain no dynamical density fluctuations from a critical point or first-order phase transition.

## Method

The authors used the nucleon coalescence model with Gaussian Wigner functions for deuteron (width parameter \(\sigma_d = \frac{2}{\sqrt{3}} r_d\) with \(r_d = 1.96\) fm) and triton (\(\sigma_t = r_t\) with \(r_t = 1.59\) fm) to compute light nuclei yields from kinetic freeze-out nucleons. The nucleon phase-space distributions were obtained from three different models: the 3D MUSIC+UrQMD hybrid model with a crossover equation of state (NEOS-BQS) for Au+Au collisions at seven beam energies from 7.7 to 200 GeV, the 2D VISHNU hybrid model for Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV, and the AMPT model for comparison.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron root-mean-square matter radius \(r_d = 1.96\) fm; triton root-mean-square radius \(r_t = 1.59\) fm (from experimental measurements, used as input to the coalescence model).
- The yield ratio \(N_t N_p/N_d^2\) decreases monotonically with increasing charged-particle multiplicity across all collision energies from \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 7.7\) GeV to 2.76 TeV.
- The asymptotic value of \(N_t N_p/N_d^2\) for a large source (\(R \gg r_d, r_t\)) is \(4/9 \approx 0.444\) from the analytic Gaussian-source formula; the MUSIC+UrQMD results are well described by multiplying this formula by \(p_0 = 0.683\) with the relation \(R = 0.547 (dN_{\mathrm{ch}}/d\eta)^{0.331}\) fm.
- The coalescence parameters \(B_2(d)\) and \(\sqrt{B_3(t)}\) at \(p_T/A = 0.65\) GeV/c and 1.05 GeV/c decrease monotonically with increasing multiplicity; \(\sqrt{B_3(t)}\) decreases more rapidly than \(B_2(d)\) when \(dN_{\mathrm{ch}}/d\eta > 200\).
- For central Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV, the VISHNU+coalescence model gives \(N_t N_p/N_d^2\) close to the value at central Au+Au at 200 GeV, but the ALICE measurement of the analogous \(N_{^3\mathrm{He}} N_p/N_d^2\) ratio is about 0.9, roughly a factor of three larger than the theoretical value of about 0.33 from prior VISHNU+coalescence calculations.

## Context

This work extends the coalescence model studies of light nuclei production by Sun (2017, 2018) and Zhao (2018, 2020) to a systematic multiplicity-dependent analysis across a wide range of collision energies, using state-of-the-art hybrid hydrodynamic models (MUSIC+UrQMD, VISHNU) that reproduce bulk hadron observables. The results contradict the thermal model predictions of Vovchenko (2018, 2020), which show an opposite (increasing) multiplicity dependence of \(N_t N_p/N_d^2\). The multiplicity scaling provides a baseline for the experimental search for the QCD critical point in the RHIC Beam Energy Scan, as the models used contain no critical fluctuations, and the upcoming multiplicity-dependent measurements of this ratio can discriminate between coalescence and thermal production mechanisms.
