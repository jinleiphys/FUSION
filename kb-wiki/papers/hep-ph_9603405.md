# Determination of light-flavor asymmetry in the $Σ^{\pm}$ sea by the Drell-Yan process

> We propose a flavor asymmetry in the light quark sea of $Σ^{\pm}$, which can be measured in Drell-Yan experiments using charged hyperon beams on proton and deuteron targets. Such a measurement would help to reinforce the presence of pseudoscalar mesons in a quark model of baryons.

# Determination of light-flavor asymmetry in the Σ± sea by the Drell-Yan process

## Key claim
The authors propose that the Σ+ hyperon has an excess of anti-down quarks (d̄) and the Σ- hyperon has an excess of anti-up quarks (ū) in their sea quark distributions, based on a pseudoscalar meson cloud model. This flavor asymmetry can be tested via Drell-Yan experiments with charged hyperon beams, and the ratio r̄Σ(x) = ūΣ(x)/d̄Σ(x) for the Σ+ is expected to be even smaller than the measured proton value of r̄p ≈ 0.51 at x ≈ 0.18.

## Method
The authors derive expressions for Drell-Yan cross sections for Σ+p, Σ+n, Σ-p, and Σ-n collisions at y ≈ 0, using isospin reflection symmetry to relate distribution functions between Σ+ and Σ- and between proton and neutron. They construct a ratio R(x) from combinations of these cross sections that isolates the unknown ratio r̄Σ(x) in terms of the known proton ratios rp(x) and r̄p(x), and also provide formulas to extract d̄Σ(x) and sΣ(x) directly from measured cross sections if the QCD K-factor is known.

## Key numbers
- The measured proton sea ratio r̄p(x) = ūp(x)/d̄p(x) is 0.51 ± 0.04 ± 0.05 (approximately 20 percent accuracy) at x ≈ 0.18.
- For rp(x) ≈ 2 and r̄p(x) ≈ 0.5, the derived relation gives R(x) ≈ 0.3 r̄Σ(x).
- An error e in the measurement of R leads to an error of approximately 3e in r̄Σ.
- The difference D ≡ x[d̄p(x) − ūp(x)] is predicted by meson cloud models to peak at x ≈ 0.1.
- The region 0 ≤ x ≤ 0.2 is identified as suitable for obtaining r̄Σ.

## Context
This work extends the meson cloud model explanation for the flavor asymmetry observed in the proton sea (excess of d̄ over ū) to the charged sigma hyperons Σ+ and Σ-. The proton sea asymmetry was established by deep inelastic muon scattering experiments (NMC, 1991; Arneodo et al., 1994) and Drell-Yan measurements (NA51, 1994), and has been explained by Thomas and colleagues (1983-1991), Henley and Miller (1990), and others (Eichten et al., 1992; Kumano, 1991; Szczurek et al., 1993-1996) as arising from a pion cloud. A measurement of the Σ± sea asymmetry would test whether the same pseudoscalar meson mechanism applies to strange baryons and could distinguish between the meson cloud and Pauli exclusion principle explanations.
