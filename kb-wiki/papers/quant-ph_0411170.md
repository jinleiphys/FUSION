# Nonlinear Bogolyubov-Valatin transformations and quaternions

> In introducing second quantization for fermions, Jordan and Wigner (1927/1928) observed that the algebra of a single pair of fermion creation and annihilation operators in quantum mechanics is closely related to the algebra of quaternions H. For the first time, here we exploit this fact to study nonlinear Bogolyubov-Valatin transformations (canonical transformations for fermions) for a single fermionic mode. By means of these transformations, a class of fermionic Hamiltonians in an external field is related to the standard Fermi oscillator.

## Key claim
For the first time, the paper exploits the known isomorphism between the algebra of a single fermionic mode and the quaternion algebra H to systematically study nonlinear Bogolyubov-Valatin transformations, showing that the most general canonical transformation for one fermionic mode is equivalent to the group SO(3) and can be implemented by SU(2) transformations.

## Method
The authors write the most general Ansatz for a Bogolyubov-Valatin transformation of a single fermionic mode as a linear combination of the identity, annihilation, creation, and number operators with complex coefficients, then impose the canonical anticommutation relations to derive constraints on these coefficients. They re-express the transformation in terms of quaternionic units derived from the fermion operators, showing that the transformation matrix in the operator basis reduces to a real 3x3 orthogonal matrix with unit determinant, establishing equivalence to SO(3). The unitary implementation is then constructed explicitly as a unit quaternion (SU(2) element) parametrized by an angle and a rotation axis.

## Context
This work extends the linear Bogolyubov-Valatin transformations introduced by Bogolyubov and Valatin for superconductivity and later generalized to n fermionic modes by Bogolyubov and collaborators, to the nonlinear case for a single mode. The paper builds on the observation by Jordan and Wigner (1927/1928) that fermion creation/annihilation operators are related to quaternions, and provides a methodological framework that can be generalized to multiple fermionic modes. The results enable the study of equivalences between non-quadratic fermionic Hamiltonians and standard Fermi oscillators, with potential applications to models involving boson-fermion interactions and supersymmetric transformations.
