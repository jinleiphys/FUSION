# Ab-initio QCD calculations impact the inference of the neutron-star-matter equation of state

> We demonstrate that ab-initio calculations in QCD at high densities offer significant and nontrivial information about the equation of state of matter in the cores of neutron stars, going beyond that which is obtainable from current astrophysical observations. We do so by extrapolating the equation of state to neutron-star densities using a Gaussian process and conditioning it sequentially with astrophysical observations and QCD input. Using our recent work, imposing the latter does not require an extrapolation to asymptotically high density. We find the QCD input to be complementary to the astrophysical observations, offering strong additional constraints at the highest densities reached in the cores of neutron stars; with the QCD input, the equation of state is no longer prior dominated at any density. The QCD input reduces the pressure and speed of sound at high densities, and it predicts that binary collisions of equal-mass neutron stars will produce a black hole with greater than $95\%$ ($68\%$) credence for masses $M \geq 1.38 M_\odot$ ($M \geq 1.25 M_\odot$). We provide a Python implementation of the QCD likelihood function so that it can be conveniently used within other inference setups.

## Key claim
Ab-initio QCD calculations at high densities provide significant and nontrivial constraints on the neutron-star-matter equation of state that are complementary to astrophysical observations, and when included, the equation of state is no longer prior-dominated at any density. The QCD input reduces the pressure and speed of sound at high densities, and predicts that binary collisions of equal-mass neutron stars will produce a black hole with greater than 95% credence for masses M >= 1.38 solar masses (68% credence for M >= 1.25 solar masses).

## Method
The authors extrapolate the equation of state from chiral effective theory at nuclear saturation density (1.1 n_s, where n_s ≈ 0.16 fm^-3) to neutron-star densities (10 n_s) using a Gaussian process regression on an auxiliary variable related to the speed of sound. They condition this prior sequentially with astrophysical observations (pulsar mass measurements, NICER mass-radius data, tidal deformability from GW170817, and the black-hole hypothesis for the GW170817 merger product) and with a QCD likelihood function derived from perturbative QCD calculations at N3LO, using a scale-averaging procedure to estimate missing-higher-order uncertainties.

## Key numbers
- The QCD input impacts the equation of state down to n ~ 2.2 n_s before including neutron-star observations, based on the causality and stability constraints from Komoltsev (2021).
- Binary collisions of equal-mass neutron stars are predicted to produce a black hole with greater than 95% credence for masses M >= 1.38 solar masses, and greater than 68% credence for M >= 1.25 solar masses.
- The low-density chiral effective theory constrains the equation of state up to n ≲ 1.1 n_s, where n_s ≈ 0.16 fm^-3.
- Perturbative QCD calculations become reliable at densities n ≳ 40 n_s, well above neutron-star densities.
- The Gaussian process hyperparameters are drawn from distributions: correlation length l ~ N(1.0 n_s, (0.25 n_s)^2), variance η ~ N(1.25, 0.2^2), mean speed of sound squared ~ N(0.5, 0.25^2).

## Context
This work extends the Bayesian inference framework for the neutron-star equation of state by incorporating QCD constraints without requiring interpolation through two orders of magnitude in density, building on the model-independent approach of Komoltsev (2021) that showed causality and stability feed high-density QCD information down to lower densities. The results confirm the strong softening of the equation of state and lower speed of sound at energy densities around 750 MeV/fm^3 reported by Annala (2019), which was interpreted as the onset of a quark-matter phase, and demonstrate that these features are genuine predictions of QCD rather than artifacts of restrictive interpolation functions. The authors provide a Python implementation of the QCD likelihood function for use in other inference setups.
