# Regularity and chaos at critical points of first-order quantum phase transitions

> We study the interplay between regular and chaotic dynamics at the critical point of a generic first-order quantum phase transition in an interacting boson model of nuclei. A classical analysis reveals a distinct behavior of the coexisting phases in a broad energy range. The dynamics is completely regular in the deformed phase and, simultaneously, strongly chaotic in the spherical phase. A quantum analysis of the spectra separates the regular states from the irregular ones, assigns them to particular phases, and discloses persisting regular rotational bands in the deformed region.

## Key claim

At the critical point of a first-order quantum phase transition in the interacting boson model of nuclei, the two coexisting phases exhibit a sharp dichotomy in dynamics: the deformed phase remains completely regular while the spherical phase becomes strongly chaotic, a separation that persists over a broad energy range up to and above the potential barrier height.

## Method

The authors studied the classical limit of the interacting boson model (IBM) Hamiltonian at the critical point of a first-order quantum phase transition, using coherent states to obtain a classical Hamiltonian in the shape variables β and γ. They constructed Poincaré surfaces of section for L=0 vibrations to visualize regular and chaotic trajectories at various energies relative to the barrier height, and performed quantum spectral analysis using Peres lattices (plotting ⟨n̂_d⟩ versus energy eigenvalues) to separate regular from irregular eigenstates for N=50 and N=80 bosons.

## Key numbers

- Potential barrier heights studied: V_b/h_2 = 0.04, 0.10, 0.16 for β_0 = 1.0, 1.3, 1.5 respectively (compared to V_b/h_2 = 0.0009 in prior work by Alhassid and Whelan (1993))
- Poincaré sections analyzed at five energies relative to barrier top: E_1 = 0.25 V_b, E_2 = 0.9 V_b, E_3 = V_b, E_4 = 1.1 V_b, E_5 = 4 V_b
- For (N=80, β_0=1.5): 32 regular levels identified in the deformed phase from a set of 364 states with E < 0.8; Brody parameter ω = 0.78 ± 0.10 for the mixed spectrum and ω = 1.01 ± 0.10 after removing the regular levels
- Quantum Peres lattices computed for N=80 (β_0=1.0, 1.3, 1.5) and N=50 (β_0=1.5) with L=0,2,3,4

## Context

This work extends studies of quantum chaos in systems undergoing quantum phase transitions, particularly the analysis by Emary and Brandes (2003) in quantum optics models where chaos is triggered by continuous QPTs. It contrasts with earlier IBM chaos studies by Alhassid and Whelan (1993) that used a simplified Hamiltonian with an extremely low barrier (V_b/h_2 = 0.0009), whereas the present Hamiltonian allows adjustable barrier heights. The results validate the recently proposed regularity-induced adiabatic separation of intrinsic and collective dynamics by Macek and Leviatan (2010), and enable future investigation of how the mixed regular-chaotic dynamics manifests in observables such as electromagnetic transition rates at the critical point.
