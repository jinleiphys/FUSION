# Symmetry breaking and the random-phase approximation in small quantum dots

> The random-phase approximation has been used to compute the properties of parabolic two-dimensional quantum dots beyond the mean-field approximation. Special emphasis is put on the ground state correlation energy, the symmetry restoration and the role of the spurious modes within the random-phase approximation. A systematics with the Coulombic interaction strength is presented for the 2-electron dot, while for the 6- and 12-electron dots selected cases are discussed. The validity of the random-phase approximation is assessed by comparison with available exact results.

## Key claim

The random-phase approximation (RPA) systematically improves the mean-field Hartree-Fock description of small parabolic quantum dots by restoring broken rotational symmetry and providing ground-state correlation energies, but it systematically overestimates the correlation energy (by approximately 12% for 6-electron and 21% for 12-electron dots) compared to exact results, and for the 2-electron dot at interaction strength \(R_W=1\) the exact energy lies almost halfway between the HF and RPA values.

## Method

The authors solved the Hartree-Fock (HF) equations for electrons confined in a two-dimensional parabolic potential using an oscillator basis truncated by an energy cutoff, allowing spontaneous breaking of rotational symmetry. They then constructed the RPA equations within the quasi-boson approximation to obtain vibrational excitations (vibrons) and identified the spurious zero-energy mode corresponding to the broken rotational symmetry, computing the Thouless-Valatin moment of inertia. The RPA ground state was constructed as an exponential of two-boson operators acting on the HF vacuum, with the condition that it is the vacuum for all vibrons and for the angular momentum operator, yielding occupation numbers and correlation energies that were compared to exact solutions (obtained by solving the relative-coordinate problem for \(N=2\) and from diffusion Monte Carlo results of Pederiva et al. for \(N=6\) and 12).

## Key numbers

- For the 2-electron dot, the RPA ground state energy lies below the exact value for all interaction strengths \(R_W = 0.5\) to 2.5; at \(R_W=1\) the exact energy is approximately halfway between HF and RPA.
- For the 6-electron dot at \(R_W=1.89\), the extrapolated RPA correlation energy is \(\Delta_{\rm RPA}(6)=1.27\,E_0\) compared to the diffusion Monte Carlo value \(\Delta_{\rm MC}(6)=1.13\,E_0\) (overestimation of about 12%).
- For the 12-electron dot at \(R_W=1.89\), the extrapolated RPA correlation energy is \(\Delta_{\rm RPA}(12)=3.21\,E_0\) compared to \(\Delta_{\rm MC}(12)=2.64\,E_0\) (overestimation of about 21%).
- The Thouless-Valatin moments of inertia for the spurious rotational mode are: for \(N=2\) at \(R_W=1.5\), \({\cal J}_0 = 0.68\,m\ell_0^2\); at \(R_W=2.0\), \({\cal J}_0 = 1.26\,m\ell_0^2\); at \(R_W=2.5\), \({\cal J}_0 = 1.63\,m\ell_0^2\); for \(N=6\) at \(R_W=1.89\), \({\cal J}_0 = 16.4\,m\ell_0^2\); for \(N=12\) at \(R_W=1.89\), \({\cal J}_0 = 57.6\,m\ell_0^2\).

## Context

This work extends the application of the RPA, originally developed for nuclear physics by Rowe and for the electron gas by Bohm and Pines, to finite semiconductor quantum dots, building on mean-field studies of Wigner molecule formation by Yannouleas and Landman (2002) and on diffusion Monte Carlo calculations by Pederiva et al. (2000). The results demonstrate that RPA can restore rotational symmetry broken at the HF level and provide a consistent scheme for ground-state correlations, but the systematic overestimation of correlation energy highlights the need for improved many-body treatments beyond RPA for small quantum dots.
