# Chiral Vortical Effect in Extended Rarita-Schwinger Field Theory and Chiral Anomaly

> We consider the theory of Rarita-Schwinger field interacting with a field with spin 1/2, in the case of finite temperature, chemical potential and vorticity, and calculate the chiral vortical effect for spin 3/2. We have clearly demonstrated the role of interaction with the spin 1/2 field, the contribution of the terms with which to CVE is 6. Since the contribution from the Rarita-Schwinger field is -1, the overall coefficient in CVE is 6-1=5, which corresponds to the recent prediction of a gauge chiral anomaly for spin 3/2. The obtained values for the coefficients $μ^2$ and $T^2$ are proportional to each other, but not proportional to the spin, which indicates a possible new universality between the temperature-related and the chemical potential-related vortical effects. The results obtained allow us to speculate about the relationship between the gauge and gravitational chiral anomalies.

## Key claim

The chiral vortical effect (CVE) coefficient for spin 3/2 is exactly 5, matching the gauge chiral anomaly coefficient, but this result arises from a qualitatively different microscopic mechanism than the chiral anomaly or chiral separation effect: the interaction terms with the spin 1/2 field contribute +6 while the pure Rarita-Schwinger field contributes -1, and these contributions cancel ultraviolet divergences at intermediate stages.

## Method

The authors considered the extended Rarita-Schwinger field theory of Adler (2017) at finite temperature, chemical potential, and vorticity, using the Zubarev density operator for global thermodynamic equilibrium. They computed the axial current expectation value in the first order of thermal vorticity by evaluating three-point correlators of the stress-energy tensor and axial current, using finite-temperature propagators in Euclidean space, and taking the limit m to infinity. The calculation involved summing Matsubara frequencies and integrating over momentum, with the finite parts expressed in terms of polylogarithms.

## Key numbers

- The total CVE coefficient for spin 3/2 is 5, obtained as 6 (interaction contribution) minus 1 (pure Rarita-Schwinger contribution)
- The axial current in a medium with vorticity is: ⟨j_A^ν⟩ = (5 T^2/6 + 5 μ^2/(2π^2)) ω^ν
- For spin 1/2, the corresponding CVE coefficient is: ⟨j_A^ν⟩ = (T^2/6 + μ^2/(2π^2)) ω^ν
- The chiral anomaly coefficient for spin 3/2 is 5/(16π^2), compared to 1/(16π^2) for spin 1/2
- The chiral separation effect for spin 3/2 gives: ⟨j_A^ν⟩ = (5 μ)/(2π^2) B^ν

## Context

This work extends the consistent Rarita-Schwinger field theory developed by Adler (2017), which introduced an interacting spin 1/2 field to resolve pathologies of the pure spin 3/2 theory and calculate the chiral anomaly. The authors verify the correspondence between the chiral anomaly and the chiral vortical effect predicted by Son and Surowka (2009) and Sadofyev et al. (2010), now for higher spin. The result differs from the naive spin-proportionality prediction of Huang et al. (2018), which would give a factor of 4 rather than 5, suggesting a new universality between temperature-related and chemical potential-related vortical effects. The work enables speculation about the relationship between gauge and gravitational chiral anomalies for higher-spin fields.
