# Exploring the hadron resonance gas phase on the QCD phase diagram

> Lattice computations of strongly interacting matter at finite temperature $T$ and baryon chemical potential $μ_B$ suggest that the QCD thermodynamics deep in the hadronic phase can be adequately modeled by an ideal hadron resonance gas (I-HRG). However, it is not clear where on the $(μ_B, T)$ plane this description breaks down, making it essential to account for hadronic interactions and change in the nature of the degrees of freedom. We have studied several thermodynamic functions within the I-HRG model and try to identify the region of the QCD phase diagram where it becomes essential to include non-ideal effects into the I-HRG model. We work with only those thermodynamic quantities that show a monotonic rise with $T$ and $μ_B$ in I-HRG. Their high temperature limiting values where QCD becomes simply a Stefan-Boltzmann (SB) gas of massless quarks and gluons is known. The rise of these quantities in I-HRG beyond the corresponding SB limit values indicate the need to include interactions into I-HRG to study QCD thermodynamics. This works as a guiding principle on the QCD phase diagram where interacting HRG can take over from I-HRG. For $μ_B/T\leq2$, $χ^Q_2$ shoots the SB limit at the smallest $T$, while for higher values of $μ_B/T$,$C_{BS}=-3χ^{BS}_{11}/χ^S_2$ takes over. We further comment on the relative positions between the freezeout curve obtained by thermal fits to the measured hadron yields and the obtained line where I-HRG overshoots SB limit.

## Key claim

The paper identifies the region on the QCD phase diagram where the ideal hadron resonance gas (I-HRG) model breaks down, defined as the set of points where any of several monotonic thermodynamic quantities computed in I-HRG exceed their corresponding Stefan-Boltzmann (SB) limit values for a massless quark-gluon gas. The authors explicitly hedge that this is an estimate of the upper limit of I-HRG applicability, not a precise phase boundary, and note that for some thermodynamic quantities the limit is "loose" and could be improved by lattice QCD computations.

## Method

The authors computed pressure, energy density, entropy density, second-order fluctuations of baryon number, strangeness, and electric charge susceptibilities (χ²_B, χ²_S, χ²_Q), and correlations (χ¹¹_BS, χ¹¹_QS, C_BS = -3χ¹¹_BS/χ²_S) within the I-HRG model using the hadron spectrum from PDG 2016 and an extended set PDG 2016+ that includes unconfirmed resonances. For each thermodynamic quantity at a given baryon chemical potential μ_B, they found the temperature T where the I-HRG value equals the corresponding SB limit; the envelope of the lowest such T across all quantities defines the estimated boundary of I-HRG validity. The analysis was performed both with μ_S = μ_Q = 0 and under heavy-ion collision conditions where strangeness neutrality and a fixed net-charge-to-baryon ratio are enforced.

## Key numbers

- At μ_B = 0 with PDG 2016 spectrum: pressure crosses SB limit at T = 268 MeV, energy density at T = 216 MeV, entropy density at T = 224 MeV; χ²_B at T = 186 MeV, χ²_S at T = 212 MeV, χ²_Q at T = 176 MeV; C_BS at T = 198 MeV.
- At μ_B = 0 with PDG 2016+ spectrum: pressure crosses SB limit at T = 252 MeV, energy density at T = 204 MeV, entropy density at T = 212 MeV; χ²_B at T = 180 MeV, χ²_S at T = 200 MeV, χ²_Q at T = 174 MeV; C_BS at T = 180 MeV.
- For μ_B/T ≤ 2, χ²_Q reaches the SB limit at the smallest T among all quantities studied; for higher μ_B/T, C_BS provides the tightest bound.
- The lattice QCD transition line used for comparison has T(0) = 154(9) MeV and curvature κ = 0.020(4) from Cea et al. (2014).

## Context

This work extends the known result that I-HRG describes lattice QCD thermodynamics at zero chemical potential up to T ~ 150 MeV (Borsanyi et al., 2011; Bazavov et al., 2012, 2014; Bellwied et al., 2013, 2015) by mapping the breakdown boundary onto the full (μ_B, T) plane. The authors compare their boundaries to the lattice QCD pseudocritical line from Cea et al. (2014) and to chemical freeze-out curves from Cleymans et al. (2005) and Andronic et al. (2008). The finding that the I-HRG breakdown boundary lies at lower T than the freeze-out curve at high μ_B suggests that freeze-out parameter extraction should be revisited using interacting HRG models, such as the Van der Waals HRG studied by Vovchenko et al. (2017). The work enables experimental searches at RHIC Beam Energy Scan, FAIR, and NICA to test where non-ideal effects become essential.
