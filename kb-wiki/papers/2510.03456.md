# Semiclassical treatment of bottomonium suppression and regeneration in $p+{\rm Pb}$ collisions

> We study bottomonium suppression in $p+ {\rm Pb}$ relative to $p+p$ collisions at center-of-mass energies of $\sqrt{s_{NN}}= 5.02$ and 8.16~TeV. Specifically, we combine cold nuclear matter effects (nuclear modifications of the parton densities, energy loss and momentum broadening) with those from hot nuclear matter (suppression and regeneration) by implementing the formation of a quark-gluon plasma in hydrodynamic simulations. Bottomonium transport in the quark-gluon plasma is evaluated semiclassically, employing two different reaction rates. The first includes quasi-free inelastic scattering and gluo-dissociation employing a perturbative coupling to the medium. The second is based on in-medium $T$-matrix calculations where the input potential is constrained by lattice quantum chromodynamics to extract the bottomonium masses and dissociation rates. These semiclassical results are compared to previous calculations in an open quantum systems approach and to the experimental data. Predictions for $χ_b$ suppression at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 8.16$~TeV are also presented.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a semiclassical transport approach, combining cold nuclear matter effects (nPDF modifications, energy loss, and momentum broadening) with hot nuclear matter effects (suppression and regeneration in a quark-gluon plasma), can describe bottomonium suppression in p+Pb collisions at LHC energies, but the authors hedge that the role of regeneration from a single b-bbar pair may still be significant even though there is rarely more than one such pair in the fireball.

## Method

The authors combine cold nuclear matter effects (EPPS21 nPDFs, coherent energy loss and transverse momentum broadening following Arleo and Peigne) with hot nuclear matter effects from a 3+1D viscous hydrodynamic QGP evolution. Bottomonium transport in the QGP is evaluated using a semiclassical kinetic rate equation with two different reaction rates: one using perturbative quasi-free inelastic scattering and gluo-dissociation, and another based on in-medium T-matrix calculations constrained by lattice QCD. The results are compared to previous open quantum systems calculations and experimental data from ALICE, ATLAS, CMS, and LHCb.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energies studied: sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 and 8.16 TeV
- Bottom quark mass: m = 4.65 +/- 0.09 GeV (from fit to total b-bbar cross section at NLO)
- Average Upsilon mass used for energy loss: M_Upsilon = 9.95 GeV (average of Upsilon(1S), Upsilon(2S), and Upsilon(3S) states)
- Intrinsic k_T broadening: <k_T^2>_p = 2.84 GeV^2 at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV and 3.0 GeV^2 at sqrt(s_NN) = 8.16 TeV
- Transport coefficient: hat_q_0 = 0.075 GeV^2/fm (at x = 10^-2)
- Effective path lengths: L_Pb = 10.41 fm, L_p = 1.5 fm
- QCD scale parameter: Lambda_QCD = 0.308 GeV, giving alpha_s(1.5 GeV) = 0.326
- Parameters for pp Upsilon cross section parametrization: p_0 = 6.6 GeV, upsilon = 2.8, beta = 13.8

## Context

This work extends previous studies of bottomonium suppression in p+Pb collisions by combining cold nuclear matter effects with hot QGP effects using a semiclassical transport approach, implementing bottomonium regeneration for the first time in p+A collisions. It builds on prior work by Du (2018) on J/psi transport, by Strickland (2024) using an open quantum systems approach with pNRQCD, and by the TAMU group (Du 2017, Wu 2024) on non-perturbative bottomonium reaction rates. The results enable systematic comparisons with experimental data from ALICE, ATLAS, CMS, and LHCb collaborations across different rapidity intervals and provide predictions for chi_b suppression at 8.16 TeV.
