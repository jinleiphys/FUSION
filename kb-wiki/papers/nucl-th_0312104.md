# The Adler-Weisberger and Goldberger-Miyazawa-Oehme sum rules as probes of constraints from analyticity and chiral symmetry in dynamical models for pion-nucleon scattering

> The Adler-Weisberger and Goldberger-Miyazawa-Oehme sum rules are calculated within a relativistic, unitary and crossing symmetric dynamical model for pion-nucleon scattering using two different methods: 1) by evaluating the scattering amplitude at the corresponding low-energy kinematics and 2) by evaluating the sum-rule integrals with the calculated total cross section. The discrepancy between the results of the two methods provides a measure of the breaking of analyticity and chiral symmetry in the model. The contribution of the $Δ$ resonance, including its dressing with meson loops, is discussed in some detail and found to be small.

## Key claim

The Adler-Weisberger (AW) and Goldberger-Miyazawa-Oehme (GMO) sum rules are used as quantitative probes of the violation of analyticity and chiral symmetry in a dynamical model for pion-nucleon scattering, where the discrepancy between the low-energy evaluation of the amplitude and the sum-rule integral provides a measure of these violations, with the caveat that the intrinsic accuracy of the AW sum rule is of order O(mu^2/m^2) ~ 0.02 and of the GMO sum rule is of order O(mu/m) ~ 0.15.

## Method

The authors calculated the AW and GMO sum rules within the "Dressed K-matrix Model," a relativistic, unitary, and crossing-symmetric dynamical model for pion-nucleon scattering. They evaluated the sum rules using two methods: (1) by computing the scattering amplitude at low-energy kinematics (the Cheng-Dashen point and threshold) and (2) by evaluating the sum-rule integrals using the calculated total cross sections for pi- p and pi+ p scattering. The calculations were performed for three cases: a "Bare" calculation with free propagators and bare vertices, a "Dressed" calculation with fully dressed vertices and propagators, and a "Dressed, but bare Delta" calculation to isolate the effects of Delta resonance dressing.

## Key numbers

- The pion-nucleon coupling constant used is g_{pi N} = 13.02, with nucleon mass m = 938 MeV and pion mass mu = 138 MeV.
- The pion decay constant used is F_pi = 92.4 MeV.
- In the "Dressed" calculation, C_AW at the Cheng-Dashen point (s=u=m^2, t=2 mu^2) is 1.16, while I_AW integrated up to omega_up = 1000 MeV is 1.08, giving a difference of 0.08.
- In the "Bare" calculation, C_AW at the Cheng-Dashen point is 1.31, while I_AW integrated up to omega_up = 1000 MeV is 0.93, giving a difference of 0.38.
- In the "Dressed" calculation, C_GMO is 1.10, while I_GMO integrated up to omega_up = 1000 MeV is 1.01, giving a difference of 0.09.
- In the "Bare" calculation, C_GMO is 1.22, while I_GMO integrated up to omega_up = 1000 MeV is 0.77, giving a difference of 0.45.
- The Delta resonance mass used is m_Delta = 1232 MeV.
- The difference C_AW(s=u=m^2,t=2 mu^2) - C_AW(s=u=m^2,t=0) in the "Dressed" calculation is 0.06.

## Context

This work extends the methodology of Kondratyuk et al. (2002), who performed a similar comparison of low-energy and sum-rule evaluations for the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn and Baldin-Lapidus sum rules in nucleon Compton scattering. The present study applies this approach to the AW and GMO sum rules for pion-nucleon scattering, using the "Dressed K-matrix Model" which provides a good description of both intermediate- and low-energy regions. The results enable future comparisons with other approaches to pion-nucleon scattering, such as the Bethe-Salpeter equation of Lahiff et al. (1999), traditional K-matrix models of Feuster et al. (1998) or Korkin et al. (1998), and chiral Lagrangian approaches of Lutz et al. (2002) or Becher et al. (2001).
