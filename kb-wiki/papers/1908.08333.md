# Intrinsic three-body nuclear interaction from a constituent quark model

> We study the short distance part of the intrinsic three-nucleon interaction in a constituent quark model with color-spin interaction. For that purpose we first calculate the transformation coefficient between the tribaryon configuration and their corresponding three baryon basis. Using a formula for the intrinsic three-body interaction in terms of a tribaryon configuration, we find that after subtracting the corresponding two-baryon contributions, the intrinsic three-body interaction vanishes in flavor SU(3) symmetric limit for all quantum numbers for the three nucleon states. We further find that the intrinsic three-body interaction also vanishes for flavor-spin type of quark interaction.

## Key claim

After subtracting the corresponding two-baryon contributions, the intrinsic three-body interaction vanishes in the flavor SU(3) symmetric limit for all quantum numbers of the three-nucleon states, and also vanishes for a flavor-spin type of quark interaction. The authors note that in a realistic flavor SU(3) breaking case the cancellation will not be exact, but argue the dominant contribution likely cancels such that the intrinsic three-nucleon interaction will be small.

## Method

The authors use a constituent quark model with a non-relativistic Hamiltonian containing color-color and color-spin two-quark interactions. They first calculate transformation coefficients between compact tribaryon configurations (nine-quark states) and their corresponding three-baryon basis, extending Harvey's earlier work on dibaryon coefficients. Using a formula that expresses the intrinsic three-body interaction as the tribaryon mass minus the sum of three dibaryon masses plus the sum of three baryon masses (with only color-spin contributions), they evaluate this quantity for all possible flavor and spin quantum numbers in the flavor SU(3) symmetric limit.

## Key numbers

* The transformation coefficient for the dibaryon (8,1) state into 8⊗8 and 8⊗10 baryon channels is 4/9 and 5/9, respectively (Table TC-dibaryon).
* The probability of the 8⊗8⊗8 three-baryon channel in the tribaryon (8,1/2) configuration is 13/54 (Table Prob-tribaryon).
* The color-spin matrix element for the dibaryon (1,0) state is -24, and for the tribaryon (8,1/2) state is 4 (Tables color-spin-dibaryon and color-spin-tribaryon).
* The intrinsic three-body interaction strength for the 8⊗8⊗8 channel with (F=8, S=1/2) evaluates to exactly 0 after the full calculation using the transformation coefficients and matrix elements.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous constituent quark model study of static three-baryon configurations (Park, 2018), which found repulsion at short distance but included all two-body interaction effects. It builds on the quark-cluster model approach of Oka (1981) and Shimizu (1989), and the transformation coefficient method of Harvey (1988). The result contrasts with lattice QCD calculations by Doi (2011) and Aoki (2011), which found repulsive three-nucleon potentials at short distance in isospin 1 and 0 channels. The vanishing of the intrinsic three-body interaction in the SU(3) symmetric limit suggests that short-distance three-nucleon forces in dense matter (relevant to the hyperon puzzle in neutron stars) may be small, but the authors note that a full calculation with realistic quark masses and spatial dependence remains future work.
