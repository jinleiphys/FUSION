# QCD sum rule analysis for light vector and axial-vector mesons in vacuum and nuclear matter

> Extending previous work we study the constraints of QCD sum rules on mass and width of light vector and axial-vector mesons in vacuum and in a medium with finite nuclear density. For the latter case especially the effect of nuclear pions leading to vector-axial-vector mixing is included in the analysis.

## Key claim

Extending previous work, this paper studies the constraints of QCD sum rules on the mass and width of light vector (ρ) and axial-vector (a₁) mesons in vacuum and in nuclear matter, including for the first time the effect of vector–axial-vector mixing induced by nuclear pions at finite nuclear density. The analysis assumes the operator product expansion (OPE) works for these channels, but the author explicitly cautions that the QCD sum rule approach, especially at finite nuclear density, is not as fundamental as QCD and should be regarded as a QCD-based model rather than QCD itself.

## Method

The author uses Borel-transformed QCD sum rules to relate the hadronic spectral functions of the ρ and a₁ mesons (parametrized with Breit-Wigner forms) to the operator product expansion (OPE) side, which is expressed in terms of quark and gluon condensates. For the in-medium case, the linear density approximation is employed, with the medium approximated as a Fermi gas of nucleons; twist-2 condensates from parton distributions and a Landau damping term are included. The analysis allows for mass shifts, peak broadening, and mixing between the vector and axial-vector channels, and the Borel window is determined by requiring the last OPE term to be 10% of the total and the continuum contribution not to exceed the resonance contribution.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum gluon condensate: ⟨(αₛ/π) G²⟩_vac = (330 MeV)⁴.
- Pion decay constant: f_π = 93 MeV.
- Four-quark condensate values used: ⟨𝒪^V_4⟩_vac = (−292 MeV)⁶, ⟨𝒪^A_4⟩_vac = (−281 MeV)⁶.
- Twist-2 parameters: A₂ = 0.9, A₄ = 0.12.
- Strong coupling at 1 GeV: αₛ(1 GeV) ≈ 0.36.
- Landau damping term in linear density approximation: c̃₁ = ρ_N / (4 m_N).

## Context

This work extends previous QCD sum rule analyses for the ρ meson in nuclear matter by Hatsuda and Lee (1992, 1995) and by Leupold (1997), which considered only mass shifts or mass–width correlations but excluded mixing. It also builds on finite-temperature studies by Dey (1990) that found only mixing at O(T²). The analysis treats ρ and a₁ on equal footing, enabling the study of chiral symmetry restoration scenarios (mass shifts, peak broadening, and mixing) in a cold nuclear medium, and provides constraints for hadronic models used to interpret dilepton spectra from heavy-ion collisions (e.g., those from the CERES and HELIOS collaborations).
