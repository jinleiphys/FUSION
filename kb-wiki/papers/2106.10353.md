# Feeble DM-SM Interaction via New Scalar and Vector Mediators in Rotating Neutron Stars

> We investigate the possible presence of dark matter (DM) in massive and rotating neutron stars (NSs). For the purpose we extend our previous work [1] to introduce a light new physics vector mediator besides a scalar one in order to ensure feeble interaction between fermionic DM and $β$ stable hadronic matter in NSs. The masses of DM fermion, the mediators and the couplings are chosen consistent with the self-interaction constraint from Bullet cluster and from present day relic abundance. Assuming that both the scalar and vector mediators contribute equally to the relic abundance, we compute the equation of state (EoS) of the DM admixed NSs to find that the present consideration of the vector new physics mediator do not bring any significant change to the EoS and static NS properties of DM admixed NSs compared to the case where only the scalar mediator was considered [1]. However, the obtained structural properties in static conditions are in good agreement with the various constraints on them from massive pulsars like PSR J0348+0432 and PSR J0740+6620, the gravitational wave (GW170817) data and the recently obtained results of NICER experiments for PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620. We also extended our work to compute the rotational properties of DM admixed NSs rotating at different angular velocities. The present results in this regard suggest that the secondary component of GW190814 may be a rapidly rotating massive DM admixed NS. The constraints on rotational frequency from pulsars like PSR B1937+21 and PSR J1748-2446ad are also satisfied by our present results. Also, the constraints on moment of inertia are satisfied considering slow rotation. The universality relation in terms of normalized moment of inertia also holds good with our DM admixed EoS.

## Key claim

The inclusion of a vector mediator alongside a scalar mediator for feeble dark matter (DM) interaction with hadronic matter in neutron stars does not produce significant changes to the equation of state or static neutron star properties compared to the case with only a scalar mediator, but the authors suggest that the secondary component of GW190814 may be a rapidly rotating massive DM-admixed neutron star.

## Method

The authors extended a previous model (Sen 2021) that introduced a scalar mediator mixing with the Higgs boson by adding a vector mediator that couples to nucleons through kinetic portal mixing with Standard Model mesons. They computed the equation of state for DM-admixed neutron stars using an effective chiral model for hadronic matter with fermionic DM interacting via both scalar and vector mediators, assuming constant DM number density throughout the star. The structural properties in both static and rotating conditions were obtained by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations and using the rotating neutron star code based on the Komatsu-Eriguchi-Hachisu method.

## Key numbers

* DM fermion mass values considered: 5 GeV and 15 GeV, with corresponding mediator masses m_phi = 9 MeV, m_xi = 11 MeV (for 5 GeV DM) and m_phi = 20 MeV, m_xi = 34 MeV (for 15 GeV DM)
* DM-nucleon couplings: y_phi = 0.13 and y_xi = 0.13 for 5 GeV DM; y_phi = 0.21 and y_xi = 0.21 for 15 GeV DM
* DM number density assumed constant at approximately 0.15 x 10^(-2) fm^(-3), corresponding to a Fermi momentum of about 0.06 GeV
* Hadronic model parameters yield saturation density rho_0 = 0.153 fm^(-3), binding energy B/A = -16.3 MeV, nuclear incompressibility K = 231 MeV, symmetry energy J = 32 MeV, and slope parameter L_0 = 88 MeV
* The self-interaction constraint from the Bullet cluster requires sigma_T/m_chi <= 1.25 cm^2/gm

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Sen 2021) that considered only a scalar mediator for feeble DM-SM interaction, motivated by the fact that vector mesons generate repulsive effects in the hadronic sector. The results satisfy constraints from massive pulsars PSR J0348+0432 and PSR J0740+6620, gravitational wave data from GW170817, and NICER observations of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620. The rotational properties also satisfy constraints from pulsars PSR B1937+21 and PSR J1748-2446ad, and the universality relation for normalized moment of inertia holds for the DM-admixed equation of state.
