# Multidimensionally-constrained relativistic mean field models and potential energy surfaces of actinide nuclei

> By breaking both the axial and the spatial reflection symmetries, we develop multidimensionally constrained relativistic mean field (MDC-RMF) models. The nuclear shape is assumed to be invariant under the reversion of $x$ and $y$ axes, i.e., the intrinsic symmetry group is $V_{4}$ and all shape degrees of freedom $β_{λμ}$ with even $μ$, such as $β_{20}$, $β_{22}$, $β_{30}$, $β_{32}$, $β_{40}$, $\dots$, are included self-consistently. The Dirac wave functions are expanded in an axially deformed harmonic oscillator basis. The RMF functional can be one of the following four forms: the meson exchange or point-coupling nucleon interactions combined with the nonlinear or density-dependent couplings. The pairing effects are taken into account with the BCS approach. The 1-, 2-, and 3-D potential energy surfaces (PES's) of $^{240}$Pu are illustrated for numerical checks and for the study of the effect of the triaxiality on the fission barriers. Potential energy curves of even-even actinide nuclei around the first and second fission barriers are studied systematically. Besides the first ones, the second fission barriers in these nuclei are also lowered considerably by the triaxial deformation. This lowering effect is independent of the effective interactions used in the RMF functionals. Further discussions are made about different predictions on the effect of the triaxiality between the macroscopic-microscopic and MDC-RMF models, possible discontinuities on PES's from self-consistent approaches, and the restoration of broken symmetries. MDC-RMF models give reasonably good description of fission barriers of even-even actinide nuclei. It is important to include both the nonaxial and the reflection asymmetric shapes simultaneously for the study of PES's and fission barriers of actinide nuclei and of those in unknown mass regions such as, e.g., superheavy nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper claims that triaxial deformation lowers not only the first fission barrier but also the second fission barrier in even-even actinide nuclei, and that this lowering effect is independent of the specific effective interaction used in the relativistic mean field functional. The hedge is that this conclusion is drawn from systematic calculations of potential energy curves around the first and second fission barriers, but the authors note that the macroscopic-microscopic models give different predictions on the effect of triaxiality.

## Method

The authors developed multidimensionally-constrained relativistic mean field (MDC-RMF) models that break both axial and spatial reflection symmetries simultaneously, assuming the nuclear shape is invariant under the reversion of x and y axes (intrinsic symmetry group V4). The Dirac wave functions were expanded in an axially deformed harmonic oscillator basis, and the RMF functional could take one of four forms: meson exchange or point-coupling nucleon interactions combined with nonlinear or density-dependent couplings. Pairing effects were treated with the BCS approach, and the potential energy surfaces were obtained using a modified linear constraint method.

## Key numbers

- The potential energy surface of $^{240}$Pu was calculated in 1-, 2-, and 3-dimensional deformation spaces for numerical checks and study of triaxiality effects on fission barriers.
- Pairing strength parameters used were $V_{\rm n} = -349.5$ MeV fm$^3$ and $V_{\rm p} = -330.0$ MeV fm$^3$, obtained by fitting average pairing gaps.
- For the azimuthal angle $\phi$, about 20 mesh points were used for heavy nuclei in the spatial lattice calculations.
- The center-of-mass correction was calculated either in the oscillator approximation as $E_{\rm c.m.} = -\frac{3}{4} \times 41 A^{1/3}$ MeV or from the quasi-particle vacuum.

## Context

This work extends previous covariant density functional theory calculations by including both nonaxial and reflection asymmetric shapes simultaneously, building on earlier studies that considered triaxial but reflection symmetric shapes for the inner barrier and axially symmetric but reflection asymmetric shapes for the outer barrier (e.g., Egido (2000), Bonneau (2004), Moeller (2009)). The MDC-RMF models enable systematic study of potential energy surfaces and fission barriers in actinide nuclei and can be applied to unknown mass regions such as superheavy nuclei, where accurate predictions of fission barriers are needed to explore the island of stability.
