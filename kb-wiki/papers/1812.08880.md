# First observation of unbound $^{11}$O, the mirror of the halo nucleus $^{11}$Li

> The structure of the extremely proton-rich nucleus $^{11}_{~8}$O$_3$, the mirror of the two-neutron halo nucleus $^{11}_{~3}$Li$_8$, has been studied experimentally for the first time. Following two-neutron knockout reactions with a $^{13}$O beam, the $^{11}$O decay products were detected after two-proton emission and used to construct an invariant-mass spectrum. A broad peak of width $\sim$3\,MeV was observed. Within the Gamow coupled-channel approach, it was concluded that this peak is a multiplet with contributions from the four-lowest $^{11}$O resonant states: $J^π$=3/2$^-_1$, 3/2$^-_2$, 5/2$^+_1$, and 5/2$^+_2$. The widths and configurations of these states show strong, non-monotonic dependencies on the depth of the $p$-$^9$C potential. This unusual behavior is due to the presence of a broad threshold resonant state in $^{10}$N, which is an analog of the virtual state in $^{10}$Li in the presence of the Coulomb potential. After optimizing the model to the data, only a moderate isospin asymmetry between ground states of $^{11}$O and $^{11}$Li was found.

## Key claim

The broad peak observed in the invariant-mass spectrum of $^{11}$O is not a single state but a multiplet containing contributions from the four lowest resonant states ($J^{\pi}=3/2^-_1$, $3/2^-_2$, $5/2^+_1$, and $5/2^+_2$), and after optimizing the model to the data, only a moderate isospin asymmetry between the ground states of $^{11}$O and $^{11}$Li was found, contrary to expectations of large mirror symmetry breaking.

## Method

A $^{13}$O beam at $E/A=69.5$ MeV was produced at the National Superconducting Cyclotron Laboratory and impinged on a 1-mm-thick $^{9}$Be target to induce two-neutron knockout reactions. The charged decay products ($2p$+$^{9}$C) were detected 85 cm downstream in the High Resolution Array (HiRA) consisting of 14 $\Delta E$-$E$ [Si-CsI(Tl)] telescopes covering polar angles from 2.1$^\circ$ to 12.4$^\circ$, and an invariant-mass spectrum was reconstructed. Theoretical analysis employed the Gamow coupled-channel (GCC) approach with a deformed $^{9}$C core and a Woods-Saxon potential, using a Berggren basis to treat resonant and continuum states.

## Key numbers

- The observed broad peak in the $^{11}$O invariant-mass spectrum has an intrinsic width of 3.4 MeV (FWHM), compared to the experimental resolution of 0.45 MeV at $Q_{2p}=4.5$ MeV.
- The fitted $Q_{2p}$ values (and widths) for the four lowest resonant states are: 4.16 (1.30) MeV for $3/2^-_1$; 4.65 (1.06) MeV for $5/2^+_1$; 4.85 (1.33) MeV for $3/2^-_2$; and 6.28 (1.96) MeV for $5/2^+_2$, with statistical uncertainties of about 5 keV on centroids and 2 keV on widths, plus an additional $\pm$10 keV systematic uncertainty.
- The $^{12}$O ground state was measured with $Q_{2p}=1.688(29)$ MeV and intrinsic width $\Gamma=51(19)$ keV, consistent with but slightly larger than the previous value of 1.638(24) MeV from Jager (2012).
- The IMME extrapolation for the $^{11}$O ground state gave 3.21(84) MeV, and the fitted $3/2^-_1$ centroid at 4.16 MeV is only 1.1$\sigma$ from this prediction.
- The $(s_{1/2})^2$ configuration weight in the $^{11}$O ground state is 29%, compared to 25% for the mirror nucleus $^{11}$Li.

## Context

This work presents the first experimental observation of $^{11}$O, the mirror of the well-known two-neutron halo nucleus $^{11}$Li, extending studies of proton-rich nuclei beyond the drip line. The results contradict simpler expectations of large mirror symmetry breaking based on the Thomas-Ehrman effect and extrapolations from the IMME fit to the $A=11$ sextet by Charity (2012). The finding that the broad threshold resonant state in $^{10}$N (the analog of the virtual state in $^{10}$Li) strongly influences the structure of $^{11}$O enables future studies of continuum coupling effects in unbound mirror pairs and tests of three-body models for Borromean systems.
