# Light quark masses from scalar sum rules

> In this work, the mass of the strange quark is calculated from QCD sum rules for the divergence of the strangeness-changing vector current. The phenomenological scalar spectral function which enters the sum rule is determined from our previous work on strangeness-changing scalar form factors [1]. For the running strange mass in the MS_bar scheme, we find m_s(2 GeV) = 99 +- 16 MeV. Making use of this result and the light-quark mass ratios obtained from chiral perturbation theory, we are also able to extract the masses of the lighter quarks m_u and m_d. We then obtain m_u(2 GeV) = 2.9 +- 0.6 MeV and m_d(2 GeV) = 5.2 +- 0.9 MeV. In addition, we present an updated value for the light quark condensate.

## Key claim

The strange quark mass is determined from QCD sum rules for the scalar correlator, yielding m_s(2 GeV) = 99 ± 16 MeV, where the dominant uncertainty comes from the hadronic spectral function rather than from perturbative QCD; the result is conditional on the specific parametrisation of the scalar form factors from the authors' previous coupled-channel analysis.

## Method

The authors apply Borel-transformed QCD sum rules to the two-point function of the divergence of the strangeness-changing vector current, using the operator product expansion up to dimension-six operators and perturbative QCD up to O(α_s^3). The phenomenological spectral function is constructed from a dispersive coupled-channel analysis of Kπ, Kη, and Kη' scalar form factors, based on unitarised chiral perturbation theory fits to S-wave Kπ scattering data. The strange quark mass is extracted by matching the theoretical and phenomenological sides of the sum rule in the Borel window where the continuum contribution is about 25% of the total.

## Key numbers

- m_s(2 GeV) = 99 ± 16 MeV, extracted from the scalar sum rule at the stability point near the K_0^*(1430) resonance
- m_u(2 GeV) = 2.9 ± 0.6 MeV, obtained from m_s and chiral perturbation theory mass ratios
- m_d(2 GeV) = 5.2 ± 0.9 MeV, obtained from m_s and chiral perturbation theory mass ratios
- Light quark condensate ⟨q̅q⟩(2 GeV) = −(267 ± 17 MeV)^3, updated from the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation
- Light quark condensate ⟨q̅q⟩(1 GeV) = −(242 ± 16 MeV)^3, provided for comparison with other determinations

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous dispersive analysis of strangeness-changing scalar form factors (Jamin, Oller, Pich 2001) to a QCD sum rule determination of quark masses, improving upon earlier scalar sum rule analyses that used Breit-Wigner parametrisations (Jamin 1995, Chetyrkin et al. 1997) which overestimated the spectral function. The result is consistent with the pseudoscalar finite-energy sum rule determination of m_s(2 GeV) = 100 ± 12 MeV by Kuehn and Mirkes (2001) and with lattice QCD reviews (Lubicz 2000, Gupta and McNeile 2001), while the extracted up and down quark masses enable updated determinations of the light quark condensate and provide input for precision tests of the Standard Model, including the CP-violating parameter ε'/ε.
