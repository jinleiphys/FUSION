# Chiral Dynamics of Baryons from String Theory

> We study baryons in an AdS/CFT model of QCD by Sakai and Sugimoto, realized as small instantons with fundamental string hairs. We introduce an effective field theory of the baryons in the five-dimensional setting, and show that the instanton interpretation implies a particular magnetic coupling. Dimensional reduction to four dimensions reproduces the usual chiral effective action, and in particular we estimate the axial coupling $g_A$ between baryons and pions and the magnetic dipole moments, both of which are proportional to $N_c$. We extrapolate to finite $N_c$ and discuss subleading corrections.

## Key claim
The authors derive a five-dimensional effective action for baryons in the Sakai-Sugimoto holographic QCD model, showing that the instanton interpretation implies a specific magnetic coupling. They estimate the axial coupling \(g_A\) and magnetic dipole moments, both proportional to \(N_c\), but note that direct comparison with nature is difficult to justify rigorously because all computations were carried out in the large \(\lambda\) and large \(N_c\) limit.

## Method
The authors model baryons as small instantons with fundamental string hairs in the five-dimensional Sakai-Sugimoto AdS/CFT setup. They construct an effective field theory for the baryons in five dimensions, including a magnetic coupling term derived from the instanton interpretation, then perform a Kaluza-Klein reduction along the fifth coordinate \(w\) to obtain a four-dimensional chiral effective action. The baryon couplings to pions and vector mesons are computed via overlap integrals of the mode-expanded wavefunctions.

## Key numbers
- Baryon size: \(\rho_{baryon} \simeq 9.6 / (M_{KK} \sqrt{\lambda})\), where \(M_{KK}\) is the Kaluza-Klein scale and \(\lambda\) is the 't Hooft coupling.
- Leading axial coupling: \(g_A \simeq 0.7 (N_c/3)\), independent of \(\lambda\) and \(M_{KK}\).
- Anomalous isovector magnetic moment: \(\Delta\mu_{an} / e_{EM} \simeq 0.36 N_c / M_{KK}\).
- With parameters \(M_{KK} \approx 0.94\) GeV and \(\lambda N_c \approx 26\), including subleading corrections: \(g_A \approx 1.32\) and \(\Delta\mu_{an} / \mu_B \approx 3.6\) (experimental values: \(g_A^{exp}=1.26\), \(\Delta\mu_{an}^{exp}/\mu_B=3.7\)).

## Context
This work extends the Sakai-Sugimoto holographic model of QCD (Sakai and Sugimoto, 2004) by providing a five-dimensional description of baryons as instantons, building on earlier connections between instantons and Skyrmions (Son and Stephanov, 2003). The authors show that the infinite tower of vector mesons participates in baryon dynamics, simplifying and relating four-dimensional interactions. The results enable future calculations of baryon electromagnetic form factors and vector dominance structure, and suggest a possible \(N_c \to N_c+2\) shift for comparing with constituent quark model predictions (Dashen, Jenkins, and Manohar, 1994).
