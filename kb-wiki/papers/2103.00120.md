# Symmetry projection to coupled-cluster singles and doubles wave function through the Monte Carlo method

> A new method for calculating the symmetry-projected energy of coupled-cluster singles and doubles (CCSD) wave function through the Monte Carlo method is proposed. We present benchmark calculations in considering the three-level Lipkin model which is a simple and minimal model with two phases: spherical and deformed ones. It is demonstrated that this new method gives good ground state energy and low-lying spectra.

# Symmetry projection to coupled-cluster singles and doubles wave function through the Monte Carlo method

## Key claim

The proposed method combines symmetry projection with coupled-cluster singles and doubles (CCSD) wave functions via Monte Carlo sampling, yielding good ground state energies and low-lying spectra in the three-level Lipkin model; however, the method is demonstrated only on this minimal model and the Monte Carlo procedure requires a truncation scheme for the deformed basis whose scaling to larger systems remains to be investigated.

## Method

The authors start with a CCSD wave function optimized by the standard coupled-cluster method, then eliminate the one-body operator iteratively to obtain a CCD wave function (with only two-body correlations) expressed in a deformed mean-field basis. They apply a symmetry projection operator to extract states with good quantum number L0, and evaluate the projected energy expectation value using a Markov chain Monte Carlo sampling of spherical basis states, combined with a truncation of the deformed basis based on the amplitude of the CCD wave function components.

## Key numbers

- For N=20 particles, the projected ground state energy with the CCD wave function recovers about 95% of the correlation energy relative to the Hartree-Fock energy difference ΔE_HF = E_HF - E_exact.
- The VAP (variation-after-projection) energy achieves approximately 10^-4 to 10^-5 percentage error relative to exact energies.
- The Monte Carlo sampling converges with sampling numbers N0 ranging from 30 to 30000, with well-controlled statistical errors demonstrated over 100 independent sets.
- At a basis truncation ratio of 0.02 (2% of the full Hilbert space dimension), the Monte Carlo energies for L0=0 through 3 at χ=5 and N=20 agree with exact projected energies within statistical errors.

## Context

This work extends the use of symmetry projection from mean-field approaches (Hartree-Fock, Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov) to coupled-cluster wave functions, which previously were considered poorly suited for such projections. It builds on prior studies of symmetry projection in the two-level Lipkin model by Wibowo and Stevenson (2017) and implementations of symmetry projection in coupled-cluster methods by Qiu et al. (2018), Qiu et al. (2019), and Bally and Duguet (2021). The method enables future applications to realistic nuclear structure calculations such as shell model studies, and the authors indicate that extension to variation-after-projection Monte Carlo is under investigation.
