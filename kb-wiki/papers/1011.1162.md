# Isoscalar dipole coherence at low energies and forbidden E1 strength

> In 16O and 40Ca an isoscalar, low-energy dipole transition (IS-LED) exhausting approximately 4% of the isoscalar dipole (ISD) energy-weighted sum rule is experimentally known, but conspicuously absent from recent theoretical investigations of ISD strength. The IS-LED mode coincides with the so-called isospin-forbidden E1 transition. We report that for N=Z nuclei up to 100Sn the fully self-consistent Random-Phase-Approximation with finite-range forces, phenomenological and realistic, yields a collective IS-LED mode, typically overestimating its excitation energy, but correctly describing its IS strength and electroexcitation form factor. The presence of E1 strength is solely due to the Coulomb interaction between the protons and the resulting isospin-symmetry breaking. The smallness of its value is related to the form of the transition density, due to translational invariance. The calculated values of E1 and ISD strength carried by the IS-LED depend on the effective interaction used. Attention is drawn to the possibility that in N-not-equal-Z nuclei this distinct mode of IS surface vibration can develop as such or mix strongly with skin modes and thus influence the pygmy dipole strength as well as the ISD strength function. In general, theoretical models currently in use may be unfit to predict its precise position and strength, if at all its existence.

# Isoscalar dipole coherence at low energies and forbidden E1 strength

## Key claim

Self-consistent Random-Phase-Approximation (RPA) calculations with finite-range forces predict a collective isoscalar low-energy dipole (IS-LED) mode in N=Z nuclei up to 100Sn, typically overestimating its excitation energy but correctly describing its isoscalar strength and electroexcitation form factor; however, theoretical models currently in broad use may be unfit to predict its precise position and strength, if at all its existence.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent Hartree-Fock–RPA calculations for closed-shell N=Z nuclei (16O, 40Ca, 56Ni, 100Sn) using finite-range interactions including phenomenological Gogny D1S and Brink-Boeker B1 forces, as well as realistic unitarily transformed AV18 potentials (UCOM and SRG variants) with and without three-body contact terms. The HF problem was solved within a single-particle basis spanning 13–15 harmonic-oscillator shells, and the same effective interaction was used to construct the RPA equations. The ISD response was calculated using the translationally-invariant operator O_ISD = sum_i (r_i^3 - 5/3 ⟨r²⟩ r_i)Y_{1m}(Ω_i) and the E1 response using the operator O_E1 = (Z/A) sum_n r_n Y_{1m} - (N/A) sum_p r_p Y_{1m}.

## Key numbers

- The experimentally known IS-LED states in 16O at 7.12 MeV and in 40Ca at 6.95 MeV exhaust approximately 4% of the isoscalar dipole energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR)
- For 40Ca using Gogny D1S, the IS-LED at 9.82 MeV shows N*/N_ph = 11/180, compared with 15/180 for the first GDR peak at 19.34 MeV
- The IS-LED carries 3–13% of the ISD energy-weighted strength depending on the interaction, in agreement with experimental data for 16O and 40Ca
- The percentage of energy-weighted E1 strength (TRK sum) carried by the IS-LED increases by one order of magnitude from 16O to 100Sn, while the absolute E1 strength increases by two orders of magnitude
- For 16O, the node of the proton transition density appears at r = 3.1–3.2 fm with all interactions except UCOM_var, in agreement with measured transition densities
- The Gogny D1S interaction predicts for 100Sn a proton skin thickness of 0.82 fm or 18% of the predicted charge radius, versus 20% for AV18-derived Hamiltonians
- The IS-LED carries approximately 15–30% of the total vortical strength corresponding to the IS convection current and 4–12% of its energy-weighted sum

## Context

This work extends early theoretical efforts from the 1970s (AMS 1975, Bar 1976, HeS 1976, COS 1990) that attempted to account for the E1 strength of the isospin-forbidden transitions in 16O and 40Ca, but which were very sensitive to input assumptions about isospin mixing. The authors note that recent theoretical investigations of ISD strength have conspicuously omitted these states, and that only scant explicit reports exist within self-consistent RPA (BlG 1977, Hey 1990). The results enable future investigation of whether the IS-LED mode has counterparts in N≠Z nuclei where it could mix with neutron-skin modes and influence pygmy dipole strength, and raise the question of whether theoretical models currently used to describe exotic dipole modes in neutron-rich species can be relied upon when they have not been tested against these well-studied stable nuclei.
