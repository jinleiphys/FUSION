# The electromagnetic form factors of the Omega in lattice QCD

> We present results on the Omega baryon electromagnetic form factors using $N_f=2+1$ domain-wall fermion configurations for three pion masses in the range of about 350 to 300 MeV. We compare results obtained using domain wall fermions with those of a mixed-action (hybrid) approach, which combine domain wall valence quarks on staggered sea quarks, for a pion mass of about 350 MeV. We pay particular attention in the evaluation of the subdominant electric quadrupole form factor to sufficient accuracy to exclude a zero value, by constructing a sequential source that isolates it from the dominant form factors. The $Ω^-$ magnetic moment, $μ_{Ω^{-}}$, the electric charge and magnetic radius, $\langle r^{2}_{E0/M1} \rangle$, are extracted for these pion masses. The electric quadrupole moment is determined for the first time using dynamical quarks.

## Key claim

The paper claims to determine the electric quadrupole form factor of the Omega baryon for the first time using dynamical (unquenched) lattice QCD, with sufficient accuracy to exclude a zero value; however, this result is obtained at unphysical pion masses (297-355 MeV) and requires extrapolation to the physical point.

## Method

The electromagnetic form factors of the Omega baryon were computed using \(N_f=2+1\) domain-wall fermion configurations on two lattice volumes (\(24^3\times64\) and \(32^3\times64\)) at three pion masses (297, 330, and 355 MeV), plus a mixed-action approach combining domain-wall valence quarks on staggered sea quarks at 353 MeV. Three-point correlation functions were evaluated using sequential inversions through the sink with a fixed source-sink separation of \(t_f=0.91\) fm for the coarse lattice, and the form factors were extracted from optimized combinations of the ratio \(R_{\sigma\mu\tau}(\Gamma,\vec q,t)\) that isolate the dominant electric monopole, magnetic dipole, and subdominant electric quadrupole contributions.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic moment \(\mu_{\Omega^{-}} = -1.875(399)\ \mu_N\) after chiral extrapolation to the physical pion mass (0.140 GeV), compared to the experimental value of \(-2.02(5)\ \mu_N\) from the PDG (2008)
- Electric charge radius \(\langle r^{2}_{E0}\rangle = 0.353(8)\ \mathrm{fm}^2\) on the coarse DWF lattice at \(m_\pi=330\) MeV, and \(0.355(14)\ \mathrm{fm}^2\) on the fine DWF lattice at \(m_\pi=297\) MeV
- Magnetic radius \(\langle r^{2}_{M1}\rangle = 0.332(23)\ \mathrm{fm}^2\) on the coarse DWF lattice at \(m_\pi=330\) MeV
- Electric quadrupole moment \(Q^{\Omega}_{3/2} = -1.892(204)\ e/m_{\Omega}^2\) on the fine DWF lattice at \(m_\pi=297\) MeV, with extrapolated value \(-1.651(262)\ e/m_{\Omega}^2\)
- The \(\Omega^{-}\) mass computed on the fine DWF lattice at \(m_\pi=297\) MeV is \(1.76(2)\) GeV, about 50 MeV above the experimental value of 1.672 GeV
- Lattice spacings: \(a^{-1}=1.729(28)\) GeV for the coarse lattice and \(a^{-1}=2.34(3)\) GeV for the fine lattice

## Context

This work extends previous quenched lattice QCD calculations of Omega baryon form factors by Boinepalli et al. (2009) to include dynamical quark effects, and provides the first determination of the electric quadrupole form factor using unquenched configurations. The results are compared with a mixed-action approach using MILC staggered sea configurations and with a background field method calculation by Aubin et al. (2008) using clover fermions. The extraction of the subdominant electric quadrupole form factor with non-zero value enables future studies of hadron deformation in the baryon decuplet, and the computed magnetic moment provides a lattice QCD prediction that can be compared with the precisely measured experimental value.
