# Deconfinement and Quarkonium Suppression

> Modifications in the production pattern of heavy quark bound states have long been considered to provide sensitive signatures for the thermal properties of dense matter created in heavy ion collisions. The original concept of Matsui and Satz for quarkonium suppression as signature for deconfinement in heavy ion collisions has been challenged recently through lattice studies of spectral functions, which indicate the persistence of heavy quark bound states at temperatures well above the transition, as well as through the refined analysis of hadronization and recombination models, which take into account the thermal evolution of the medium generated in a heavy ion collision. We will review here recent developments on these topics.

## Key claim

The original concept of Matsui and Satz that quarkonium suppression serves as a direct signature for deconfinement in heavy ion collisions has been challenged by lattice studies of spectral functions, which indicate the persistence of heavy quark bound states at temperatures well above the transition, and by refined hadronization and recombination models that account for the thermal evolution of the medium.

## Method

The paper reviews lattice QCD calculations of heavy quark free energies and running couplings in pure SU(3) gauge theory and in 2- and 3-flavor QCD, extracting screening masses and the scale \(r_{med}\) beyond which the force between a static quark-antiquark pair is strongly modified by temperature. It also reviews the reconstruction of charmonium spectral functions from thermal hadron correlation functions using the Maximum Entropy Method (MEM), comparing results from different lattice discretizations and analyzing the temperature dependence of correlation functions normalized to a low-temperature reference.

## Key numbers

* The scale \(r_{med}\) extracted from singlet free energies in pure SU(3) gauge theory and 2-flavor QCD provides an estimate for the distance beyond which temperature effects strongly modify the heavy quark force; this scale is compared to mean squared charge radii of charmonium and bottomonium states.
* Dissociation temperatures from singlet free energies (\(F_1\)) in pure gauge theory: \(J/\psi\) at \(T_d/T_c = 1.1\), \(\chi_c\) at \(0.74\), \(\psi'\) at \(0.1-0.2\); from singlet energies (\(U_1\)): \(J/\psi\) at \(\sim 2.0\), \(\chi_c\) at \(\sim 1.1\), \(\psi'\) at \(\sim 1.1\), \(\Upsilon\) at \(\sim 4.5\), \(\chi_b\) at \(\sim 2.0\), \(\Upsilon'\) at \(\sim 2.0\).
* The running coupling \(\alpha_{\rm qq}(r,T)\) at short distances coincides with the zero-temperature coupling and runs as \(\alpha_{\rm qq}(r,T) \sim 6\pi/((33-2N_f)\ln(1/r\Lambda))\) for \(r\Lambda \ll 1\); at large distances it drops exponentially as \(\alpha_{\rm qq}(r,T) \sim \alpha(T) \exp\{-m_D(T) r\}\) for \(r\gg 1/T\).
* MEM analysis of charmonium spectral functions shows no significant modification of \(S\)-state spectral functions up to \(1.5\,T_c\); the \(J/\psi\) resonance disappears abruptly at \(T\simeq 1.9\,T_c\) in one study (Asakawa 2003) or gradually, with a reduced peak still visible at \(2.25\,T_c\) and disappearing at \(3\,T_c\) in another (Datta 2004).
* Correlation functions for \(P\)-state charmonium (\(\chi_c\)) show strong thermal modifications already slightly above \(T_c\); no evidence for bound states is found in these channels above the transition temperature.
* The number of \(c\bar{c}\) pairs created per central collision is estimated as at most one at SPS, a few at RHIC, and well above 100 at LHC.

## Context

This review extends the original screening-based quarkonium suppression scenario of Matsui and Satz (1986) by incorporating lattice QCD results on spectral functions and free energies, which suggest that \(J/\psi\) may survive to higher temperatures than originally expected. It also contrasts the sequential suppression pattern (Mehr 1988, Digal 2001) with statistical hadronization and recombination models (Thews 2001, Rapp 2003, Braun-Munzinger 2000, Rapp 2004) that become relevant at higher collision energies. The work enables a more quantitative understanding of how measured charmonium yields in heavy ion collisions at SPS, RHIC, and LHC can be interpreted in terms of the thermal evolution of the medium, and it identifies the need for refined lattice studies with dynamical quarks and improved spectral analyses.
