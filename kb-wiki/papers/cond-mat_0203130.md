# Cooper pairing and single particle properties of trapped Fermi gases

> We calculate the elementary excitations and pairing of a trapped atomic Fermi gas in the superfluid phase. The level spectra and pairing gaps undergo several transitions as the strength of the interactions between and the number of atoms are varied. For weak interactions, the Cooper pairs are formed between particles residing in the same harmonic oscillator shell. In this regime, the nature of the paired state is shown to depend critically on the position of the chemical potential relative to the harmonic oscillator shells and on the size of the mean field. For stronger interactions, we find a region where pairing occur between time-reversed harmonic oscillator states in different shells also.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the nature of Cooper pairing in a trapped atomic Fermi gas depends critically on the relative magnitude of the pairing field, the harmonic oscillator level spacing, and the Hartree mean field, with distinct regimes where pairing occurs only within the same harmonic oscillator shell (intra-shell) or between different shells (inter-shell), and where the chemical potential's position relative to the oscillator shells determines whether a finite critical coupling is required for pairing to appear.

## Method

The authors solved the Bogoliubov-de Gennes (BdG) equations for a dilute gas of fermionic atoms in a spherical harmonic trap at zero temperature, using both numerical methods and analytical approximations. For weak interactions they employed a degenerate shell pairing approximation that ignored inter-shell matrix elements, while for strong interactions they used semiclassical methods treating the gas as quasi-homogeneous. The Hartree mean field was included perturbatively to examine its effect on level splitting within harmonic oscillator shells.

## Key numbers

- For a partly filled shell (chemical potential at a harmonic oscillator shell), the effective coupling strength is \( G = \frac{32\sqrt{2n_F+3}}{15\pi^2} \frac{|a|}{a_{osc}}\hbar\omega \), and the gap is \( \Delta = G/(1 - 2\nu \ln(e^\gamma n_F) G/\hbar\omega) \) when \( \Delta \ll \hbar\omega \), with \( \nu = 2^{3/2}/3 \) and \( \gamma \simeq 0.577 \)
- For a filled shell (chemical potential between shells), the critical coupling below which pairing vanishes is \( G_c = 3\hbar\omega / [2^{5/2} \ln(4e^\gamma n_F)] \), and for \( G \ge G_c \) the gap scales as \( \Delta/\hbar\omega = [3\hbar\omega/(2^{3/2}7\xi(3))]^{1/2} \sqrt{1/G_c - 1/G} \) with \( \xi(3) \simeq 1.202 \)
- For single-level pairing in the presence of Hartree splitting, the gap for \( l=n_F \) is \( \Delta_{n_F l=n_F}/\hbar\omega = |a|/a_{osc} / \sqrt{\pi} \)
- The condition for neglecting the Hartree field in the very weak coupling regime (\( \Delta = G \)) requires \( n_F \lesssim 6 \); for \( \Delta \sim 0.5\hbar\omega \) the intra-shell pairing assumption holds for \( n_F \lesssim 30 \)
- The condition for single-level pairing (only the level at the chemical potential contributes) requires \( n_F \gg 200 \) (corresponding to \( N \gg 10^6 \) particles)

## Context

This work extends the study of pairing in trapped Fermi gases beyond the simple BCS treatment by accounting for the discrete harmonic oscillator shell structure and the Hartree mean field, building on earlier work by Heiselberg (2001) using the seniority scheme and by Baranov (2000) on semiclassical methods. The results bridge condensed matter and nuclear physics: in nuclei pairing gaps are always smaller than \( \hbar\omega \) by an order of magnitude (Bohr and Mottelson, 1969), whereas trapped atoms can access regimes where \( \Delta(r=0) \gg \hbar\omega \), enabling study of superfluid properties intermediate between bulk systems and finite nuclei. The findings enable predictions for single-particle response experiments and provide building blocks for calculating collective properties of the superfluid gas.
