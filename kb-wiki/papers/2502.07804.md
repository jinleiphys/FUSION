# Nuclear Fusion Enhancement by Heavy Nuclear Catalysts

> We seek to understand the effect of high electron density in the proximity of a heavy nucleus on the fusion reaction rates in a hot plasma phase. We investigate quantitatively the catalytic effect of gold ($Z=79$) ions embedded in an electron plasma created due to plasmonic focusing of high-intensity short laser pulses. Using self-consistent strong plasma screening, we find highly significant changes in the internuclear potential of light elements present nearby. For gold, we see a $14\,$keV change in the internuclear potential near the nuclear surface, independent of the long-distance thermal Debye-Hückel screening. The dense polarization cloud of electrons around the gold catalyst leads to a $\sim 1.5$ enhancement of proton-boron ($^{11}$B) fusion above $T=100\,$keV.

## Key claim

Self-consistent strong screening of a gold nucleus (Z=79) in a hot electron plasma produces a ~14 keV reduction of the internuclear potential near the nuclear surface that is independent of the long-distance thermal Debye-Hückel screening, leading to a ~1.5 enhancement of proton-boron fusion rates above T=100 keV, but this enhancement is likely underestimated because contributions to the self-energy above linear order in the polarization charge were neglected.

## Method

The authors solve the Poisson-Boltzmann equation with a finite-sized Gaussian source charge density for gold, using relativistic Fermi-Dirac statistics for the electron distribution rather than the Boltzmann approximation. They integrate the equation numerically from large distances (where the weak-field Debye solution applies) inward to the nuclear surface, varying a shooting parameter until convergence at r=0. The induced polarization potential is extracted by subtracting the vacuum potential from the total plasma potential, and the reaction rate enhancement is computed using the Salpeter enhancement factor evaluated at the Gamow energy turning point.

## Key numbers

- For gold (Z=79), the self-consistent strong screening contribution to the potential near the origin is 14.2 keV, independent of temperature.
- The Hartree-Fock estimate for the zero-temperature potential shift of gold is 14.7 keV, and with electron exchange corrections it is 14.5 keV.
- The enhancement factor for proton-boron (p-¹¹B) fusion is approximately 1.5 above T=100 keV.
- The gold nuclear charge radius used is R_Au = 5.3 fm.
- The laser intensities considered for creating the electron plasma are 10¹⁷–10²⁰ W/cm² over timescales of roughly 20 fs.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent strong screening formalism developed by Grayson and Rafelski (2024) for Big Bang nucleosynthesis to the case of heavy nuclear catalysts in laser-generated plasmas. It contrasts with earlier strong screening models by Salpeter (1954), Dewitt (1973), Itoh (1977, 1979), Ichimaru (1982), Chugunov (2007, 2009), and Kravchuk (2014) that were developed for white dwarf plasmas with Coulomb coupling parameters Γ_e between 1 and 200, whereas this work addresses "intermediate screening" in globally weakly-coupled plasmas. The results suggest that heavy nuclear catalysts could explain the long-standing riddle of anomalous screening observed in low-energy nuclear reaction experiments by Schröder (1989), Shoppa (1993), Aliotta (2001), Czerski (2001), and Zhang (2020). The work enables future experimental tests using inverse kinematics with heavy metal proton-hydride targets or colliding beam p-¹¹B interactions within thin gold foils.
