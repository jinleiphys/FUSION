# Relativistic hydrodynamics for spin-polarized media

> We summarize the key ingredients of the recently proposed formalism of relativistic perfect-fluid hydrodynamics with spin. Based on the underlying kinetic theory definitions for the equilibrium distribution functions we obtain the evolution equations governing the system's expansion. Employing Bjorken symmetry we study the spin polarization dynamics of the system.

## Key claim
The paper summarizes a recently proposed formalism of relativistic perfect-fluid hydrodynamics with spin, but explicitly states that under the simplifying assumption of Bjorken symmetry the model cannot reproduce the characteristic quadrupole structure of longitudinal spin polarization observed in experimental data from the STAR Collaboration.

## Method
The authors derive hydrodynamic equations for spin-polarized media from kinetic theory, starting from equilibrium distribution functions (spin density matrices) for spin-1/2 particles. They obtain conservation laws for baryon current, energy-momentum tensor, and spin tensor using de Groot-van Leeuwen-van Weert (GLW) expressions. The equations are then specialized to the case of boost-invariant Bjorken flow, yielding six independent evolution equations for the coefficients describing the electric-like and magnetic-like components of the spin polarization tensor.

## Key numbers
- Initial temperature: T0 = 150 MeV at proper time τ0 = 1 fm
- Initial baryon chemical potential: μ0 = 800 MeV
- Mass of Λ particles: m = 1116 MeV
- Initial spin polarization coefficients: Cκ,0 = (0,0,0) and Cω,0 = (0,0.1,0)

## Context
This work extends the framework of relativistic spin hydrodynamics developed in a series of papers by Florkowski et al. (2017-2019) and builds on the equilibrium distribution function approach of Becattini et al. (2013). It directly addresses the discrepancy between thermal-vorticity-based models that correctly describe global Λ polarization (Karpenko 2016, Li 2017) but fail to explain differential observables measured by the STAR Collaboration (2019). The results show that the Bjorken symmetry assumption is too restrictive to reproduce experimental features, motivating the need for more realistic (e.g., 3+1 dimensional) hydrodynamic simulations with spin.
