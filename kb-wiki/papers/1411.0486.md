# Analysis of the Crystal Ball data on $K^-p\to\pi^0\Sigma^0$ reaction with center-of-mass energies of $1536\sim 1676$ MeV

## Key claim

The PDG 4-star resonance $\Lambda(1690){3\over 2}^-$ is not needed by the Crystal Ball differential cross section data; instead, there is strong evidence for a new $\Lambda({3\over 2}^+)$ resonance around 1680 MeV, which improves $\chi^2$ by more than 100 regardless of which set of polarization data is used. The 3-star $\Lambda(1600){1\over 2}^+$ resonance is found to be definitely needed, contrary to its lower PDG rating.

## Method

The authors performed an effective Lagrangian analysis of the $K^-p\to\pi^0\Sigma^0$ reaction, fitting differential cross sections and $\Sigma^0$ polarization data from the Crystal Ball experiment at center-of-mass energies from 1536 to 1676 MeV. They included t-channel $K^*$ exchange, u-channel proton exchange, and s-channel $\Lambda$ resonance exchanges, using Breit-Wigner propagators for unstable resonances with energy-dependent widths and Blatt-Weisskopf barrier factors. The fitting procedure started with five well-established 4-star $\Lambda$ resonances (18 tunable parameters) and then systematically added additional $\Lambda$ resonances with various $J^P$ assignments to improve the description.

## Key numbers

- The fit with only five 4-star $\Lambda$ resonances gave $\chi^2 = 1565$ for 236 differential cross section data points
- Adding one additional $\Lambda({1\over 2}^+)$ resonance (mass $1581.7 \pm 32$ MeV, width $142.5 \pm 4.5$ MeV) reduced $\chi^2$ to 654
- The most favored fit (Table IIIB) achieved $\chi^2 = 419$ with: $\Lambda(1670){1\over 2}^-$ at mass $1660.9 \pm 0.4$ MeV and width $48.3 \pm 0.8$ MeV; $\Lambda(1600){1\over 2}^+$ at mass $1576.3 \pm 0.5$ MeV and width $80.7 \pm 1.1$ MeV; an additional ${3\over 2}^-$ at mass $1511.2 \pm 1$ MeV and width $256 \pm 2.9$ MeV; and an additional ${3\over 2}^+$ at mass $1679.8 \pm 0.7$ MeV and width $115.3 \pm 0.8$ MeV
- Dropping the $\Lambda(1690){3\over 2}^-$ from the fit increased $\chi^2$ by only 1 (to 419), while dropping any other resonance increased $\chi^2$ by more than 120
- Replacing the new ${3\over 2}^+$ resonance by the well-established $\Lambda(1890){3\over 2}^+$ gave $\chi^2 = 512$, significantly worse
- Including VA group polarization data gave $\chi^2 = 550$ for 308 data points; including UCLA group polarization data gave $\chi^2 = 881$ for 360 data points

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of $\overline{K}N$ reactions by Manley et al. and Kamano et al., who performed multichannel partial-wave analyses but failed to reproduce either set of Crystal Ball polarization data. It also builds on the chiral unitary approach of Oset et al., who found two ${1\over 2}^-$ resonances near 1390 and 1420 MeV, and the chiral-quark model analysis of Zhong et al. The new $\Lambda(1680){3\over 2}^+$ resonance claimed here has implications for hyperon spectroscopy: while the classical qqq constituent quark model (Capstick, 1986) predicts the lowest $\Lambda({3\over 2}^+)$ around 1900 MeV, penta-quark dynamics predicts one below 1700 MeV. The result enables re-examination of other reactions to see if this new resonance can replace the PDG $\Lambda(1690){3\over 2}^-$ and calls for further precise polarization data to resolve spin-parity ambiguities.
