# Volution of nuclear spin-orbit splittings with Skyrme functional SAMi-T

> A new Skyrme functional has been developed with tensor term guided by ab initio relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) studies on neutron-proton drops. Instead of extracting information on the tensor force from experimental single-particle energy splittings, the RBHF calculations do not contain beyond mean-field effects such as particle-vibration coupling and therefore the information on the tensor force can be obtained without ambiguities. The new functional gives a good description of nuclear ground-state properties as well as various giant resonances. The description for the evolution of single-particle energy splittings is also improved by the new functional.

## Key claim

The new Skyrme functional SAMi-T, whose tensor term is constrained by *ab initio* relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) calculations on neutron-proton drops rather than by experimental single-particle energy splittings, improves the description of the evolution of spin-orbit splittings along isotopic and isotonic chains, although the minima of the calculated energy splittings do not exactly match the experimental positions.

## Method

The tensor parameters of the Skyrme functional were determined by fitting to the spin-orbit splittings of neutron-proton drops calculated within the RBHF framework using the Bonn A interaction, which avoids ambiguities from beyond-mean-field effects such as particle-vibration coupling. The resulting functional, SAMi-T, was then used in Hartree-Fock plus BCS calculations for finite nuclei, with a zero-range density-dependent pairing force (V0 = 680 MeV fm³, σ = 1, ρ0 = 0.16 fm⁻¹). The evolution of single-particle energy splittings was computed for the Z = 50 isotopic chain (proton h11/2 and g7/2) and the N = 82 isotonic chain (neutron i13/2 and h9/2), and compared with experimental data from Schiffer (2004).

## Key numbers

- The shift applied to SAMi-T results for the Z = 50 chain was δ = 6.8 - 2.8 = 4 MeV; for SAMi the shift was δ = 6.5 - 2.8 = 3.7 MeV.
- The β parameter (neutron-proton tensor contribution) for SAMi-T is β = 102 MeV fm⁵, compared to β = 32 MeV fm⁵ for SAMi.
- The shift applied to SAMi-T results for the N = 82 chain was δ = 4.7 - 1.1 = 3.6 MeV; for SAMi the shift was δ = 5.3 - 1.1 = 4.2 MeV.
- The pairing strength used was V0 = 680 MeV fm³, with σ = 1 and ρ0 = 0.16 fm⁻¹.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Shen (2018, 2018a) who showed a clear tensor-force signal in neutron drops using RBHF, and of Shen (2019) who extended the study to neutron-proton drops. The new SAMi-T functional improves upon the earlier SAMi functional of Roca-Maza (2012) by including tensor terms constrained by *ab initio* calculations rather than by experimental single-particle splittings, which are mixed with particle-vibration coupling as noted by Afanasjev (2015). The results enable further refinement of nuclear energy density functionals, though the authors note that the minima of the calculated energy splittings (at N = 70 and Z = 58) do not yet match the experimental minima (at N = 62 and Z = 64), indicating that other features of the functional besides the tensor force require further investigation.
