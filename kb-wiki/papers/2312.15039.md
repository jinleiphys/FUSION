# Signatures of baryon junctions in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering

> Local gauge invariance of the baryon wave function leads to the emergence of a baryon junction, where three (or $N$, in $SU(N)$ gauge theory) string operators merge. The existence of baryon junction dramatically affects the dynamics of baryon stopping at high energies, and the corresponding predictions are supported by the recent data from STAR Collaboration at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider. Here we outline the ways in which the baryon junctions can be tested in semi-inclusive deep inelasttic scattering at Jefferson Laboratory and the future Electron Ion Collider.

## Key claim

The paper argues that baryon junctions (gluonic structures carrying baryon number) can be tested in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering, not just in hadron collisions, and predicts that at sufficiently high energy the forward baryon production becomes flavor-blind, with the cross section scaling as \(s^{-1/4} e^{-y^*/2}\) when the purely gluonic junction exchange dominates.

## Method

The authors apply the Mueller-Kancheli theorem to relate the semi-inclusive cross section for \(\gamma^* + p \rightarrow B + X\) to the imaginary part of a forward scattering amplitude, then use Regge theory to describe the exchange of baryonium states (\(M_0^J\), \(M_2^J\), \(M_4^J\)) with different quark-gluon content. They compute the intercepts of these exotic meson trajectories using topological expansion and Regge factorization, and derive the energy and rapidity dependence of forward baryon production for each exchange channel.

## Key numbers

- The intercept of the purely gluonic baryonium state \(M_0^J\) is \(\alpha_0^J(0) \approx 1/2\), for \(M_2^J\) it is \(\alpha_2^J(0) \approx 0\), and for \(M_4^J\) it is \(\alpha_4^J(0) \approx -1/2\).
- The predicted cross section for forward baryon production via gluonic junction exchange scales as \(s^{-1/4} e^{-y^*/2}\) in the high-energy limit.
- The average meson multiplicity for \(M_0^J\) exchange is \(n(M_0^J) = \frac{3}{2} \bar{n}\), where \(\bar{n}\) is the multiplicity of an inclusive event; for \(M_2^J\) exchange it is \(n(M_2^J) = \bar{n}\); for \(M_4^J\) exchange it is \(n(M_4^J) = \frac{1}{2} \bar{n}\).

## Context

This work extends the baryon junction picture proposed by Kharzeev (1996) for hadron collisions, which was supported by recent STAR Collaboration data on baryon stopping in RuRu and ZrZr isobar collisions (Brandenburg, 2022). It contrasts with the perturbative baryon number transfer mechanism with intercept 1 proposed by Kopeliovich (1988, 1998) to explain H1 data at HERA (H1 Collaboration, 1998), which is inconsistent with the observed energy dependence of baryon stopping at RHIC and LHC. The predictions can be tested at Jefferson Lab and the future Electron Ion Collider, enabling discrimination between different mechanisms of baryon number transfer in QCD.
