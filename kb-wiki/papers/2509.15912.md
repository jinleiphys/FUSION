# Hiding in Plain Sight, the electroweak $η_W$

> The presence of a topological susceptibility in the electroweak sector of the Standard Model implies the existence of a pseudoscalar state in the spectrum, $η_W$. We show that, within the Standard Model, no new particle is required to form this state. We identify the $η_W$ state with the CP-odd linear combination of the ground states of hydrogen and antihydrogen atoms.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the electroweak topological susceptibility of the Standard Model does not require a new fundamental particle; instead, the required pseudoscalar state \(\eta_W\) is identified with the CP-odd linear combination of the ground states of hydrogen and antihydrogen atoms, which are already part of the Standard Model spectrum.

## Method
The authors analyze the topological susceptibility of the \(\mathrm{SU}(2)_L\) electroweak sector using the three-form/Higgsing formalism, which shows that a non-decoupling susceptibility necessitates a pseudoscalar degree of freedom. They construct the 't Hooft operator \(\mathcal{O}_W = (qqql)\) for one light generation of fermions, which carries \(B+L=2\) charge and saturates the \(B+L\) anomaly in the deep infrared. After QCD confinement and electroweak symmetry breaking, they match this operator onto a QED bound state of a proton and an electron, identifying the \(\eta_W\) as the CP-odd superposition of the \(1s\) spin-singlet ground states of hydrogen and antihydrogen.

## Key numbers
- The electroweak scale from the Higgs is \(v_{EW} = 246\) GeV, which is the only scale in the electroweak sector.
- For the EW axion scenario (discussed in the text but not the main claim), the bound on the decay constant \(f_W\) from typical axion searches is in the range \(10^8 \div 10^9\) GeV.
- For the EW axion scenario, the strongest bound from proton decay \(p \to e^+ + \pi^0\) from Super-Kamiokande gives \(\tau_p \leq 1.4 \times 10^{34}\) years, implying \(\sqrt{\Lambda^3 / \langle \Phi_W \rangle} \geq 2.4 \times 10^{16}\) GeV.
- For the EW axion scenario, the proton decay channel \(p \to e^+ + a_W\) gives a lower bound on the proton lifetime of \(\tau_p \geq 790 \times 10^{30}\) years, leading to \(\Lambda \geq 3.8 \times 10^{10}\) GeV for \(\langle \Phi_W \rangle = f_W\).

## Context
This work extends the argument by Dvali (2005) that topological susceptibility always requires a pseudoscalar state, and directly addresses the proposal by Dvali, Kobakhidze, and Sakhelashvili (2024) for an \(\eta_W\) state. The paper contrasts with the QCD case where the \(\eta'\) arises from the axial anomaly, noting that the electroweak \(\mathrm{SU}(2)_L\) is Higgsed while still weakly coupled, so the state emerges as a QED bound state rather than an electroweak meson. This identification enables future work on computing the matching \(\langle 0|O_W|\eta_H\rangle\) in effective field theory, estimating hydrogen-antihydrogen mixing amplitudes in \(B+L\)-breaking models, and revisiting constraints on oscillations, connecting the electroweak topological sector to atomic physics observables.
