# Bethe-Salpeter equation: 3D reductions, heavy mass limits and abnormal solutions

> We show that the 3D reductions of the Bethe-Salpeter equation have the same bound state spectrum as the original equation, with the possible exception of some solutions for which the corresponding 3D wave function vanishes. The abnormal solutions of the Bethe-Salpeter equation (corresponding to excitations in the relative time-energy degree of freedom), when they exist, are recovered in the 3D reductions via a complicated dependence of the final potential on the total energy. We know however that the one-body (or one high mass) limit of some 3D reductions of the exact Bethe-Salpeter equation leads to a compact 3D equation (by a mutual cancellation of the ladder and crossed graph contributions), which does not exhibit this kind of dependence on the total energy anymore. We conclude that the exact Bethe-Salpeter equation has no abnormal solution at this limit, or has only solutions for which our 3D wave function vanishes. This is in contrast with the results of the ladder approximation, where no such cancellation occurs. We draw the same conclusions for the static model, which we obtain by letting the mass of the lighter particle go also to infinity. These results support Wick's conjecture that the abnormal solutions are a spurious consequence of the ladder approximation.

## Key claim

The exact Bethe-Salpeter equation has no abnormal solutions (excitations in the relative time-energy degree of freedom) at the one-body or static limits, or has only solutions for which the 3D-reduced wave function vanishes, in contrast to the ladder approximation where such solutions persist; this supports Wick's conjecture that abnormal solutions are a spurious consequence of the ladder approximation.

## Method

The author performs a 3D reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation for two fermions using Breit's propagator, which imposes a constraint fixing the relative energy. The one-body limit (one fermion mass going to infinity) and the static model (both masses infinite) are then taken for both the exact Bethe-Salpeter equation and its 3D reduction, comparing the spectra. The analysis is carried out in QED, examining the cancellation of higher-order terms (crossed graphs) in the exact kernel versus their persistence in the ladder approximation.

## Key numbers

- The one-body limit is taken as \(m_2 \to \infty\), with \(P_0 = m_2 + E\) and \(\mu \to -m_2^2/(2P_0) \approx (E - m_2)/2\).
- In the exact QED case at the one-body limit, the 3D potential reduces to a single Coulomb term \(W = -\alpha\) (with \(\alpha\) the fine-structure constant), yielding only one root (the normal solution).
- At the ladder approximation in the static model, the zero-D reduction yields an equation with multiple roots; the first 20 eigenvalues computed in prior work (Bijtebier, 1995) were checked numerically and none corresponded to a solution vanishing at the required relative energy value.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of abnormal solutions in the Bethe-Salpeter equation, following Wick's conjecture (Wick, 1954) that they are artifacts of the ladder approximation. It directly addresses the counterexample of Mugibayashi (who found abnormal solutions in the exact static model) and the more recent questioning by Jallouli and Sazdjian (1994). The results support the possibility that higher-order irreducible graphs conspire to suppress abnormal solutions, analogous to the cancellation seen in the one-body limit of 3D reductions studied by Gross and others (Gross, 1982; Bijtebier, 1990-1995). This enables further investigation into whether abnormal solutions exist in the exact finite-mass Bethe-Salpeter equation or are entirely spurious.
