# Azimuthal Anisotropy: Ridges, Recombination and Breaking of Quark Number Scaling

> Azimuthal anisotropy is studied by taking into account the ridges created by semi-hard scattering, which is sensitive to the initial spatial configuration in non-central heavy-ion collisions. No rapid thermalization is required. Although hydrodynamics is not used in this study, the validity of hydrodynamical expansion is not excluded at later time after equilibration is achieved. Phenomenological properties of the bulk and ridge behaviors are used as inputs to determine the elliptic flow of pion and proton at low p_T. At intermediate p_T the recombination of shower partons with thermal partons becomes more important. The phi dependence arises from the variation of the in-medium path length of the hard parton that generates the shower. The p_T dependence of v_2 is therefore very different at intermediate p_T compared to that at low p_T. Quark number scaling of v_2 is shown to be only approximately valid at low p_T, but is broken at intermediate p_T, even though recombination is the mechanism of hadronization in all p_T regions considered.

## Key claim

The paper argues that quark number scaling (QNS) of elliptic flow is only approximately valid at low transverse momentum and is broken at intermediate transverse momentum, even though recombination is the hadronization mechanism across all considered momentum regions. The authors claim this breaking arises because the nature of azimuthal anisotropy changes when thermal-shower recombination replaces thermal-thermal recombination as the dominant mechanism.

## Method

The authors use a recombination model with thermal and shower partons, incorporating ridges from semi-hard scattering near the surface of the overlap region in non-central heavy-ion collisions. They calculate the second harmonic coefficient v₂ for pions and protons by treating the bulk medium as having a thermal distribution with temperature T and the ridge as having a higher inverse slope T', with the ridge distribution represented by a box approximation in azimuthal angle φ. For intermediate p_T, they include shower partons from hard partons whose energy loss depends on path length through the medium, parameterized by an energy-loss coefficient ε(b).

## Key numbers

- T' = 0.3 GeV for pions from fitting π⁺ distribution at 0-5% centrality for 1 < E_T < 3 GeV
- T = 0.255 GeV for pions (derived from ΔT = 45 MeV)
- T''_π = 1.7 GeV for pions
- T' = 0.35 GeV for protons from proton distribution at 0-5% centrality
- T = 0.305 GeV for protons (derived from ΔT = 45 MeV)
- T''_p = 2.37 GeV for protons at 0-10% centrality
- ΔT = 45 MeV (difference between T' and T) determined from fitting ridge data
- Initial slope of v₂ for pions: ∂v₂^π/∂E_T at E_T=0 equals (1/π T''_π) sin 2Φ(b)
- Initial slope of v₂ for protons: ∂v₂^p/∂E_T at E_T=0 equals (1/π T''_p(ĉ)) sin 2Φ(b)
- T'_p = 0.35 (1 - 0.5 ĉ) GeV, where ĉ = c/100 for centrality dependence
- T''_p = 2.37 (1 - 1.05 ĉ + 0.26 ĉ²) GeV

## Context

This work extends the recombination model of Hwa and Yang (2004) by incorporating ridges from semi-hard scattering as an alternative to rapid thermalization for generating azimuthal anisotropy. It contradicts the assumption that quark number scaling holds universally across all p_T, as suggested by earlier recombination-coalescence models (Dong and Molnar, 2004; Voloshin and Gary, 2004). The paper enables future studies of the transition region where thermal-shower recombination becomes dominant, and predicts a dip in the proton ridge distribution at low p_T that could be tested experimentally.
