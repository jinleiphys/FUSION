# $η/s$ of the Normal Phase of Unitary Fermi Gas from $\varepsilon$ Expansion

> Using $\varepsilon$-expansion technique we compute $η/s$, where $η$ is the shear viscosity, $s$ is the entropy density, of the normal phase of unitary Fermi gas in $d=4-\varepsilon$ dimensions to LO in $\varepsilon$. We use kinetic theory approach and solve transport equations for medium perturbed by a shear hydrodynamic flow. The collision integrals are calculated to $\varepsilon^2$ which is LO. The LO result is temperature independent with $η/\rm s\simeq (0.11/\varepsilon^2)(\hbar/k_B).$ The $d=3$ prediction for $η/\rm s$ exceeds the $\hbar/4 πk_B$ bound by a factor of about $1.4.$

## Key claim
Using the ε-expansion technique around d=4 spatial dimensions, the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s of the normal phase of unitary Fermi gas is computed to leading order (LO) in ε, yielding a temperature-independent result; the d=3 prediction exceeds the conjectured η/s ≥ ħ/(4πk_B) bound by a factor of about 1.4, but the hedge is that this is the simplest extrapolation (setting ε=1) and the author notes the agreement with a lattice result is believed to be a fluke.

## Method
The calculation uses kinetic theory with the Boltzmann equation approach, solving linearized transport equations for a system of spin-up and spin-down fermions interacting via exchange of spin-0 bosons (dimer fields) in d=4-ε spatial dimensions. The collision integrals are computed to O(ε²) from two-loop self-energy diagrams in the finite-temperature real-time formalism, and the linearized integral equations for the perturbation functions χ_F and χ_B are solved numerically on a discretized domain using adaptive Monte Carlo integration.

## Key numbers
- η/s ≃ (0.11/ε²)(ħ/k_B) at LO in ε, temperature independent.
- The d=3 prediction (ε=1) gives η/s ≃ 0.11 ħ/k_B, exceeding the conjectured bound ħ/(4πk_B) by a factor of about 1.4.
- The ratio of left-hand side to right-hand side of the linearized Boltzmann equation was 1.07±0.07 for fermions and 0.97±0.03 for bosons over the range 0 ≤ ε_k/T ≤ 13.

## Context
This work extends the ε-expansion technique pioneered by Nishida and Son (2006) for the unitary Fermi gas, which has previously been applied to the zero-temperature equation of state, fermion quasi-particle spectrum, and critical temperature of the superfluid-normal phase transition. The result for η/s is compared to the AdS/CFT bound from Kovtun, Son, and Starinets (2004), to experimental bounds from quark-gluon plasma and unitary Fermi gas measurements (η/s ≤ 0.5 ħ/k_B), and to other theoretical approaches such as the lattice calculation by Wlazlowski et al. (2012) which gave (η/s)_min ≃ 0.11, and variational calculations by Enss et al. (2011) and LeClair (2010) that gave ratios exceeding the bound by factors of ~7 and 4.7 respectively. The work enables future higher-order calculations in ε and combined expansions around d=2 and d=4 with convergence improvement techniques.
