# Investigation of weak-rates for odd-A nuclei for presupernova simulations

> Calculating weak decay rates under stellar conditions for studying presupernova evolution of massive stars is a challenging task. Here we show the importance of odd A nuclei for presupernova simulations. In order to calculate the required nuclear matrix elements we apply the pn QRPA model in a deformed basis. Nuclear deformation, thought to play an integral role in calculation of associated weak decay rates, is taken into account in our model. We calculate Gamow Teller (GT) strength distributions, emission and positron capture rates for selected odd A nuclei. Our model does not employ the Brink Axel hypothesis as used in previous calculations of weak decay rates and we perform a state by state microscopic calculation of GT strength distributions from all parent excited states. Our calculated decay rates are in good agreement with large scale shell model calculations.

## Key claim

The paper shows that deformed pn-QRPA calculations of beta-decay and positron capture rates for odd-A nuclei (45Sc and 55Mn) are in good agreement with large-scale shell model calculations, but the authors note that small differences exist which "might fine-tune the modeling and simulation results" for presupernova evolution, urging simulators to test-run their rates.

## Method

The authors applied the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) in a deformed Nilsson basis to compute Gamow-Teller (GT) strength distributions, beta-minus decay rates, and positron capture rates for the odd-A nuclei 45Sc and 55Mn. Unlike previous calculations that employed the Brink-Axel hypothesis, the model performed a state-by-state microscopic calculation of GT strength distributions from all parent excited states (200 excited states considered) using a model space of 7 hbar-omega. The pairing gaps were set to 12/sqrt(A) MeV, nuclear deformation parameters were derived from electric quadrupole moments, and Q-values were taken from the 2012 Atomic Mass Evaluation.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous pn-QRPA calculations (Majid et al., 2018) on electron capture rates for the same nuclei to now include beta-minus decay and positron capture rates. It provides an alternative to the earlier Fuller, Fowler, and Newman (FFN) calculations (1980) that used the independent particle model with the Brink-Axel hypothesis, and to large-scale shell model calculations that become computationally prohibitive beyond the fp-shell. The calculated rates are intended for use in presupernova evolution simulations and core-collapse supernova modeling, where the lepton-to-baryon ratio Ye is governed by charge-changing reactions.
