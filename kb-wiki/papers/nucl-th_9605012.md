# Quest for deconfined quark phase: A new avenue

> Stability of droplets of $ud$ matter, {\it viz} udilets, is studied using non-relativistic and relativistic bag models. It is found that there is a strong possibility for forming hypercharged metastable udilets with charge fraction $Z/A \ge 1$ in semicentral relativistic heavy ion collisions. It is then speculated that protons hadronised from these hypercharged udilets may be contributing towards the directed sideward flow observed recently in experiment. This exotic state with large charge to mass ratio presents an unique possibility for the spontaneous emission of positrons due to the `sparking' of the vacuum.

# Quest for deconfined quark phase: A new avenue

## Key claim
Stability calculations using both non-relativistic and relativistic bag models suggest a strong possibility for forming hypercharged metastable udilets (droplets of up and down quark matter) with charge fraction Z/A ≥ 1 in semicentral relativistic heavy ion collisions, but the actual stability and decay properties depend sensitively on the initial baryon number, charge fraction, and equilibrium density acquired at formation.

## Method
The authors calculated the mass per baryon of udilets using a non-relativistic model with constituent quark mass 300 MeV and a relativistic bag model with massless quarks, both incorporating Coulomb energy and finite-size corrections. They then evaluated the energetics of fission, neutron emission, proton emission, and Δ⁺⁺ decay for udilets of various baryon numbers and charge fractions, using Q-value calculations and Coulomb barrier comparisons. For small baryon numbers in the relativistic bag model, shell model calculations were employed to refine the stability analysis.

## Key numbers
- Equilibrium baryon number density taken as ρ_ob = 0.25 fm⁻³ for the non-relativistic model and ρ_ob = 0.40 fm⁻³ for the relativistic bag model
- Mass per baryon shows a minimum at charge fraction f_Z^min ≈ 0.4 for A = 300 in both models
- In the relativistic bag model, after neutron and proton emission, the udilet reaches f_Z ≈ 1.9 with A ≈ 20
- In the non-relativistic model, after neutron and proton emission, the udilet reaches f_Z ≈ 2 with A ≈ 58 (entirely u quarks)
- In the relativistic bag model, if initial A = 200 instead of 300, the limiting values after neutron and proton emissions are A ≈ 14 and f_Z ≈ 2
- For the relativistic bag model, E_f/E_b ≈ 1 at f_Z ≈ 0.7 for A = 300, and at f_Z ≈ 1.0 for A = 200

## Context
This work extends the study of deconfined quark matter droplets beyond the well-studied strangelets (characterized by small charge fraction Z/A << 1) to udilets consisting only of up and down quarks, which had been less explored. The authors build on their own non-relativistic mass formula (Maheswari and Satpathy, prior work) and the relativistic bag model of Jaffe (prior work), as well as Berger and Jaffe's treatment of quark-quark interactions absorbed into the bag parameter. The speculation that protons from udilet decay may contribute to directed sideward flow connects to experimental observations of collective flow in heavy ion collisions. The possibility of reaching Z ≈ 137 through hypercharged udilets opens a new avenue for studying spontaneous positron emission via vacuum sparking, a phenomenon previously considered only in the context of superheavy nuclei.
