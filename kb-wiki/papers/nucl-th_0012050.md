# Degeneracies when T=0 Two Body Matrix Elements are Set Equal to Zero and Regge's 6j Symmetry Relations

> The effects of setting all T=0 two body interaction matrix elements equal to a constant (or zero) in shell model calculations (designated as $<T=0>=0$) are investigated. Despite the apparent severity of such a procedure, one gets fairly reasonable spectra. We find that using $<T=0>=0$ in single j shell calculations degeneracies appear e.g. the $I={1/2} ^{-}$ and ${13/2}^{-}$ states in $^{43}$Sc are at the same excitation energies; likewise the I=$3_{2}^{+}$,$7_{2}^{+}$,9$^{+}_{1}$ and 10$^{+}_{1}$ states in $^{44}$Ti. The above degeneracies involve the vanishing of certain 6j and 9j symbols. The symmetry relations of Regge are used to explain why these vanishings are not accidental. Thus for these states the actual deviation from degeneracy are good indicators of the effects of the T=0 matrix elements. A further indicator of the effects of the T=0 interaction in an even - even nucleus is to compare the energies of states with odd angular momentum with those that are even.

# Degeneracies when T=0 Two Body Matrix Elements are Set Equal to Zero and Regge's 6j Symmetry Relations

## Key claim

Setting all T=0 two-body interaction matrix elements to a constant (or zero) in single j shell calculations produces fairly reasonable spectra despite the severity of the approximation, with specific degeneracies appearing that are not accidental but rather explained by the vanishing of certain 6j and 9j symbols via Regge symmetry relations. The actual deviation from these degeneracies in the physical spectrum serves as a good indicator of the effects of the T=0 matrix elements.

## Method

The authors performed single j shell calculations in the f7/2 region using two sets of matrix elements: one where all T=0 two-body matrix elements were set to a constant (effectively zero for states of a given isospin), and one using the full experimental spectrum of 42Sc. They then extended the analysis to full fp-space calculations using the FPD6 interaction. The degeneracies that appear in the T=0=0 limit were analyzed using Racah coefficients, Wigner 6j and 9j symbols, and Regge symmetry relations to explain why certain angular momentum recoupling coefficients vanish.

## Key numbers

- In 42Sc, the two-body matrix elements for J=0 through J=7 were: 0.000 MeV, 0.6110 MeV, 1.5863 MeV, 1.4904 MeV, 2.8153 MeV, 1.5101 MeV, 3.242 MeV, and 0.6163 MeV respectively
- In the single j shell calculation with T=0=0 for 43Ti, the I=1/2- and I=13/2- states are degenerate at 3.279 MeV
- In the single j shell calculation with T=0=0 for 44Ti, the I=9+ and I=10+ states are degenerate at 7.200 MeV
- In the full fp calculation with FPD6 and T=0=0 for 44Ti, the I=9+ and I=10+ states are nearly degenerate at 6.574 MeV and 6.476 MeV respectively
- In the full fp calculation with full FPD6 for 44Ti, the I=9+ state rises to 9.030 MeV while I=10+ is at 7.790 MeV
- The 6j symbol {7/2 7/2 4; 7/2 13/2 6} vanishes, and more generally all 6j symbols of the form {j j (2j-3); j (3j-4) (2j-1)} vanish for all j

## Context

This work extends the single j shell calculations in the f7/2 region originally performed by McCullen, Bayman, and Zamick (1963-1964) and by Ginocchio and French (1963), where two-body matrix elements were taken from experiment. The authors make a more severe approximation than Poves and Martinez-Pinedo (1998), who studied removing T=1 pairing from T=0 S=1 pairing in the fp shell using the KB3 interaction; here all T=0 matrix elements are set to zero, which produces specific degeneracies whose deviation in the physical spectrum can be attributed to T=0 two-body matrix elements. The work enables identification of spectral features in odd-I states of even-even nuclei and in odd-A nuclei that serve as clean indicators of T=0 interaction effects, and demonstrates that Regge symmetry relations (Regge, 1959) provide a systematic explanation for the vanishing of the relevant 6j and 9j symbols.
