# Structure of unstable light nuclei

> The structure of light nuclei out to the drip lines and beyond up to Z = 8 is interpreted in terms of the shell model. Special emphasis is given to the underlying supermultiplet symmetry of the p-shell nuclei which form cores for neutrons and protons added in sd-shell orbits. Detailed results are given on the wave functions, widths, and Coulomb energy shifts for a wide range of non-normal parity states in the p-shell.

## Key claim

The structure of light nuclei up to Z ∼ 8, including those near the drip lines, can be interpreted using the shell model with an emphasis on the underlying supermultiplet (SU(4)) symmetry of p-shell nuclei, which serve as cores for neutrons and protons added in sd-shell orbits; however, the paper notes that for some mass numbers the tabulations are up to 12 years out of date and that the non-normal-parity states in ab initio no-core shell-model calculations are predicted too high in energy.

## Method

The author performed shell-model calculations using an SU(3) shell-model code based on the formalism of French, adapted for SU(3), with a p-shell Hamiltonian fitted to 34 level energies for A=10-12 nuclei. The central interaction was decomposed into SU(4) invariants, and Woods-Saxon radial wave functions evaluated at the physical separation energy were used to calculate transition matrix elements and nucleon decay widths via the relation Γ_N = C² S Γ^{sp}(E_N).

## Key numbers

- The spin-orbit splitting from the A=10-12 fit is 4.78 MeV (compared to 3.49 MeV for a similar A=6-9 fit), much larger than that of the Cohen-Kurath interactions for the light p-shell nuclei.
- The ground state of ¹²C is typically ∼80% [44] spatial symmetry (LS = 00) with a [431] admixture (LS = 11) of ∼16% induced by the spin-orbit interaction.
- In ⁸He β decay (Q_β = 10.653 MeV), the 1⁺_4 state near 9 MeV has 74.0% [22] L=0 S=0 T=2 configuration in the initial state and 71.5% [22] L=0 S=1 T=1 in the final state.
- In ¹¹Li β decay (Q_β = 20.7 MeV), a 5/2⁻ state is predicted at 17.8 MeV with 87% [322] symmetry with S=3/2.
- For ¹⁰Li, a p-wave resonance is observed at 500 keV above threshold with a width of 400(60) keV; shell-model calculations predict a 1⁺, 2⁺ doublet separated by only 23 keV for the A=10-12 interaction (169 keV for the A=6-9 interaction).
- The Warburton and Brown interactions predict essential degeneracy of the lowest 0ℏω and 2ℏω configurations in ¹¹Li and ¹²Be.
- For the ¹¹Be 5/2⁻ level at 3.96 MeV, the predicted width is 17 keV for S(2⁺) = 0.66 compared with the experimental value of 15(5) keV.
- The 2.65 MeV shift in the relative 1/2⁺, 5/2⁺ separation between ¹⁷O and ¹¹Be is roughly accounted for by half the difference between the interactions with the full p shell.
- The rms deviation of the Warburton and Brown cross-shell fit to energies from A=10-22 is ∼330 keV.

## Context

This work extends the shell-model interpretation of light nuclei pioneered by Elliott and Flowers (1955) and Kurath (1956), and builds on the supermultiplet symmetry framework developed by Wigner. It provides a unified description of p-shell nuclei and their non-normal-parity states, enabling predictions for neutron-rich nuclei near the drip lines such as ⁹He, ¹⁰He, ¹¹Li, and ¹²Be. The results connect to recent cluster model calculations by Ogawa (2000), Kanada-En'yo (1999), and Itagaki (2000), and to the Warburton and Brown (1992) cross-shell interactions, while also informing future radioactive beam experiments at facilities like those discussed at ENAM98.
