# Lattice QCD calculation of the Compton amplitude subtraction function

> The Compton amplitude subtraction function is an essential component in work concerning both the proton radius puzzle and the proton-neutron mass difference. However, owing to the difficulty in determining the subtraction function, it remains a key source of uncertainty in these two contexts. Here, we use the Feynman-Hellmann method to determine this subtraction function directly from lattice QCD. Furthermore, we demonstrate how to control dominant discretisation artefacts for this calculation, eliminating a major source of systematic error. This calculation is performed for a range of hard momentum scales, and three different sets of gauge configurations for pion masses about 400 MeV. Our results show good agreement with continuum OPE expectations. As such, this work paves the way for model-independent and precise determinations of the subtraction function over a wide range of kinematics.

## Key claim

The paper uses the Feynman-Hellmann method to compute the Compton amplitude subtraction function directly from lattice QCD, demonstrating that leading discretisation artefacts can be controlled and removed, yielding results that agree with continuum operator product expansion (OPE) expectations over a momentum range of approximately 1 to 16 GeV².

## Method

The authors perturb the lattice QCD action by introducing external fields that couple to valence quarks, then extract the ground-state energy shift of the nucleon from ratios of two-point correlators at second order in the coupling. The subtraction function is obtained from the flavour-diagonal (uu, dd) and flavour-mixed (ud) components of the Compton amplitude at zero energy transfer (ω=0), using three ensembles of 2+1 flavour clover fermion gauge configurations at the SU(3) symmetric point with pion masses around 400 MeV. To correct for discretisation artefacts, a lattice operator product expansion (LOPE) is performed for Wilson fermions with the local vector current, yielding an analytic correction term proportional to the scalar condensate.

## Key numbers

- Pion masses: approximately 400 MeV (470, 410, and 430 MeV on the three ensembles)
- Lattice spacings: 0.074 fm, 0.068 fm, and 0.058 fm
- Momentum range: 1.10 GeV² to 15.9 GeV² (coarsest ensemble), 1.44 GeV² to 9.38 GeV² (β=5.65 ensemble), 4.96 GeV² to 9.92 GeV² (finest ensemble)
- External coupling values: λ ∈ [0.0125, 0.0250]
- Proton mass on the coarsest ensemble: 0.4646(43) in lattice units
- Wilson coefficient for discretisation correction: C_W(q,m₀) = 2r Σ_ρ [1 - cos(aq_ρ)] / [Σ_ρ sin²(aq_ρ) + a²M²(q,m₀)]

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice calculations of the subtraction function using four-point correlation functions by Xu et al. (2024) and prior Feynman-Hellmann studies of the forward Compton amplitude by the same collaboration. It directly addresses the dominant source of uncertainty in both the proton radius puzzle (through two-photon exchange corrections to muonic hydrogen) and the proton-neutron mass difference via the Cottingham sum rule, where previous evaluations by Gasser and Leutwyler (1974) and Walker-Loud et al. (2012) gave discrepant results due to different treatments of the subtraction function. The method enables model-independent, first-principles determinations of S₁(Q²) over a wide kinematic range, complementing low-Q² effective field theory calculations by Paz (2011) and high-Q² OPE predictions by Collins (1979) and Hill and Paz (2017).
