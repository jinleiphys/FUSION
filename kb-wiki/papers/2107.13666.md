# Nucleon-nucleon scattering with perturbative pions: The uncoupled $P$-wave channels

> The uncoupled $P$-wave channels of nucleon-nucleon scattering are studied in an effective field theory (EFT) including a perturbative dibaryon field and perturbative pions. Good agreement between EFT results and the Nijmegen partial wave analysis is observed up to a center-of-mass momentum $k \approx 400$ MeV. Using a method that combines renormalization and fitting together, the long-standing convergence problem of EFTs in these channels with perturbative pions, for momenta above the pion mass is addressed from a new perspective.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that introducing a perturbative dibaryon field into an effective field theory (EFT) with perturbative pions resolves the long-standing convergence problem in uncoupled $P$-wave nucleon-nucleon scattering channels for momenta above the pion mass, achieving good agreement with the Nijmegen partial wave analysis up to center-of-mass momentum $k \approx 400$ MeV. The claim is hedged by the condition that this works at least up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) for the specific uncoupled $P$-wave channels studied.

## Method
The author constructs an EFT Lagrangian including nucleon, pion, and a perturbative dibaryon field for each uncoupled $P$-wave channel (${}^{1}\!P_{1}$, ${}^{3}\!P_{0}$, ${}^{3}\!P_{1}$), with pions treated perturbatively. The $T$ matrix is calculated order-by-order using the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a sharp-cutoff regulator, and a renormalization method is employed that combines renormalization and fitting together by using data points at specific momenta to fix low-energy constants (LECs) at each order, keeping the central values of chiral NLO plus chiral-symmetry-breaking NNLO parameters unchanged.

## Key numbers
- Good agreement between EFT results and the Nijmegen partial wave analysis is observed up to center-of-mass momentum $k \approx 400$ MeV.
- The low-energy scale is taken as $M_{\text{lo}} \approx 300$ MeV and the high-energy scale as $M_{\text{hi}} \approx 1$ GeV.
- For the ${}^{1}\!P_{1}$ channel, fitted LEC values for Fit 1: $g_{1}^{(1)} = 0.00112$ MeV$^{-1}$, $\Delta^{(0)} = -97.8$ MeV, $C_{2}^{(2)} = -3.8 \times 10^{-9}$ MeV$^{-3}$, with $\sqrt{m_{N} |\Delta^{(0)}|} = 303.0$ MeV.
- For the ${}^{1}\!P_{1}$ channel, fitted LEC values for Fit 2: $g_{1}^{(1)} = 0.00123$ MeV$^{-1}$, $\Delta^{(0)} = -149.2$ MeV, $C_{2}^{(2)} = -7.7 \times 10^{-9}$ MeV$^{-3}$, with $\sqrt{m_{N} |\Delta^{(0)}|} = 374.3$ MeV.

## Context
This work extends the approach of Kaplan (1996) and subsequent studies (Bedaque 1999, Soto 2007, Long 2013, Sanchez Sanchez 2017, Habashi 2020) that introduced dibaryon fields in $S$-wave channels of nucleon-nucleon scattering, showing that pions can be treated perturbatively in the presence of a dibaryon field. It addresses the convergence problem identified by Fleming (1999) and Kaplan and Steele (1999) in the KSW power counting for spin-triplet channels above the pion mass. The results enable future applications of this EFT framework to other partial waves and to processes requiring a consistent treatment of pion interactions with perturbative dibaryon fields.
