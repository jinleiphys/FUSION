# Magnetic moments of light nuclei from lattice quantum chromodynamics

> We present the results of lattice QCD calculations of the magnetic moments of the lightest nuclei, the deuteron, the triton and ${}^3$He, along with those of the neutron and proton. These calculations, performed at quark masses corresponding to $m_π\sim 800$ MeV, reveal that the structure of these nuclei at unphysically heavy quark masses closely resembles that at the physical quark masses. In particular, we find that the magnetic moment of ${}^3$He differs only slightly from that of a free neutron, as is the case in nature, indicating that the shell-model configuration of two spin-paired protons and a valence neutron captures its dominant structure. Similarly a shell-model-like moment is found for the triton, $μ_{{}^3{\rm H}} \sim μ_p$. The deuteron magnetic moment is found to be equal to the nucleon isoscalar moment within the uncertainties of the calculations.

## Key claim
Lattice QCD calculations at an unphysical pion mass of ~800 MeV show that the magnetic moments of the deuteron, triton, and ³He, when rescaled by the nucleon mass, are remarkably close to their experimental values at physical quark masses, suggesting that the nuclear shell-model description holds even at these heavier quark masses.

## Method
The NPLQCD Collaboration performed lattice QCD calculations on a single ensemble of gauge-field configurations with N_f=3 clover-improved fermions and a Lüscher-Weisz gauge action, at a lattice spacing of a~0.12 fm and with all three light-quark masses set equal to the physical strange quark mass (m_π~806 MeV). A background U_Q(1) gauge field producing a uniform magnetic field along the z-axis was multiplied onto each QCD gauge field, and two-point correlation functions for the proton, neutron, deuteron, ³He, and triton in j_z=±j magnetic substates were computed from ~750 configurations using 48 Gaussian-smeared sources per configuration for four magnetic field strengths (ñ=0,1,-2,+4). The magnetic moments were extracted from the energy splittings between spin-aligned and anti-aligned states by fitting the correlator ratios to a function linear in the magnetic field with a cubic correction term.

## Key numbers
- Proton magnetic moment: μ_p = 1.792(19)(37) nuclear magnetons (NM) at m_π~806 MeV (first uncertainty statistical, second systematic)
- Neutron magnetic moment: μ_n = -1.138(03)(10) NM at m_π~806 MeV
- Deuteron magnetic moment: μ_d = 1.218(38)(87) natural nuclear magnetons (nNM) at m_π~806 MeV
- ³He magnetic moment: μ_{³He} = -2.29(03)(12) nNM at m_π~806 MeV
- Triton magnetic moment: μ_{³H} = 3.56(05)(18) nNM at m_π~806 MeV
- Deviation of ³He from Schmidt limit: δμ_{³He} = μ_{³He} - μ_n = -0.340(24)(93) nNM (experimental difference is -0.215 NM)
- Deviation of triton from Schmidt limit: δμ_{³H} = μ_{³H} - μ_p = +0.45(04)(16) nNM (experimental difference is +0.186 NM)
- Lattice spacing: a~0.12 fm; pion mass: m_π~806 MeV; magnetic field strengths: e|B|~0.046|ñ| GeV²

## Context
This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations of nucleon magnetic moments (Primer et al., 2013) to the first-ever QCD calculation of nuclear magnetic moments, building on the NPLQCD Collaboration's earlier studies of light nuclei at unphysical quark masses (Beane et al., 2012; Beane et al., 2013). The results demonstrate that the shell-model description of these s-shell nuclei, where the deuteron moment equals the sum of proton and neutron moments and ³He (triton) is close to the neutron (proton) moment, holds at these heavy quark masses, and the deviations from the Schmidt limits (Schmidt, 1937) are similar in magnitude to those in nature. This work enables future lattice QCD calculations of nuclear structure at lighter quark masses and for larger nuclei, and suggests that the phenomenological nuclear shell-model may be applicable over a broad range of quark masses.
