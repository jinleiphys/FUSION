# Effect of a realistic three-body force on the spectra of medium-mass hypernuclei

> We adopt the Hartree-Fock (HF) method in the proton-neutron-$Λ$ (p-n-$Λ$) formalism and the nucleon-$Λ$ Tamm-Dancoff Approximation (N$Λ$ TDA) to study the energy spectra of medium-mass hypernuclei. The formalism is developed for a potential derived from effective field theories which includes explicitly the 3-body $NNN$ forces plus the $YN$ LO potential. The energy spectra of selected medium-mass hypernuclei are presented and their properties discussed. The present calculation is the first step of a project devoted to {\it ab initio} studies of hypernuclei in medium and heavy mass regions. This may provide a guide for a better understanding of the $YN$ interactions at momentum scales not accessible in few-body hypernuclei.

## Key claim

The inclusion of the chiral three-nucleon (NNN) force from the NNLO$_{\rm{sat}}$ potential significantly improves the agreement of calculated charge radii and nucleon single-particle energies with experimental data for $^{16}$O and $^{40}$Ca, and yields realistic energy gaps in $\Lambda$ single-particle levels for hypernuclei, but the absolute binding energies remain severely underestimated (by a factor of about 3 for $^{16}$O and 3.7 for $^{40}$Ca), indicating that ground-state correlations beyond the Hartree-Fock mean field are necessary.

## Method

The authors adopt the Hartree-Fock (HF) method in the proton-neutron-$\Lambda$ (p-n-$\Lambda$) formalism to generate the mean field for hypernuclei with even-even nuclear cores, using the chiral NNLO$_{\rm{sat}}$ potential (which includes both $NN$ and $NNN$ forces) for nucleons and the chiral leading-order (LO) $YN$ potential for the nucleon-$\Lambda$ interaction. For hypernuclei with odd-even or even-odd cores, they develop the nucleon-$\Lambda$ Tamm-Dancoff Approximation (N$\Lambda$ TDA), where the $NNN$ force is included only at the two-body level of its normal-ordered form. The calculations are performed in a harmonic oscillator basis with $N_{\rm{max}} = 12$ and $\hbar \omega_{\rm{HO}} = 16$ MeV.

## Key numbers

- Charge radius of $^{16}$O: 2.77 fm with $NN+NNN$ (experiment: 2.70 fm); with $NN$ only: 2.19 fm.
- Charge radius of $^{40}$Ca: 3.54 fm with $NN+NNN$ (experiment: 3.48 fm); with $NN$ only: 2.58 fm.
- Binding energy per nucleon of $^{16}$O: 2.66 MeV with $NN+NNN$ (experiment: 7.98 MeV); with $NN$ only: 7.36 MeV.
- Binding energy per nucleon of $^{40}$Ca: 2.31 MeV with $NN+NNN$ (experiment: 8.55 MeV); with $NN$ only: 11.65 MeV.
- Regulator cutoff $\lambda = 550$ MeV used for the LO $YN$ potential, chosen because it yields more bound $\Lambda$ energies closer to empirical values.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous mean-field studies of hypernuclei (Vesely et al., 2015-2016) by replacing the phenomenological density-dependent (DD) term simulating the $NNN$ interaction with the explicit chiral three-body force from the NNLO$_{\rm{sat}}$ potential (Ekstrom et al., 2015). The approach contrasts with phenomenological models such as Skyrme mean-field (Rayet, Schulze) and relativistic mean-field (Mares) theories, and with ab initio methods limited to light hypernuclei (no-core shell model by Wirth et al., fermionic molecular dynamics by Neff). The results enable future application of the Equation of Motion Phonon Method (EMPM) to include ground-state correlations and multiphonon excitations, with planned calculations for $^{40}_{\Lambda}$K and $^{48}_{\Lambda}$K relevant to experiments at JLab.
