# Dynamics of heavy flavour in a weakly magnetized hot QCD medium

> We obtain the spatial and momentum diffusion coefficients ($D_s$ and $κ$), and the collisional energy loss ($dE/dx$) of a heavy quark (HQ) traversing through a thermal medium of quarks and gluons in a weak magnetic field ($B$), for the two cases of the HQ moving either parallel or perpendicular to $\bm{B}$. For that purpose, we consider Coulomb scatterings ($t$-channel) of the HQ with the light quarks, obtained from the imaginary part of the HQ self-energy via the cutting rules. Both the normalised (by $T^3$) %\textit{momentum} diffusion coefficients, $κ$, as well as $dE/dx$, for charm quarks are larger than that for bottom quarks due to the larger mass of the latter. Also, the effect of $B$ is more feeble on the bottom quark, compared to the charm quark. Comparatively, the magnitudes of both $κ$ and $dE/dx$ are significantly smaller for the case of $\bm{v}\perp\bm{B}$. For both the cases, our results show that the momentum transfer between the HQ and the medium takes place preferentially along the direction of HQ velocity, thus leading to a significant increase in the momentum diffusion anisotropy, compared to $B=0$. We also calculate the (scaled) spatial diffusion coefficient, which we find to be independent of the heavy flavor mass and is almost unaffected by changes in $B$.

## Key claim

The paper calculates heavy quark transport coefficients (momentum diffusion, spatial diffusion, and collisional energy loss) in a weakly magnetized quark-gluon plasma under the specific condition that the magnetic field strength satisfies the hierarchy \(M_Q \gg T \gg eB/T\), meaning the magnetic field is treated as a perturbation to the thermal medium rather than a dominant scale.

## Method

The authors compute the scattering rate \(\Gamma\) of a heavy quark with light thermal quarks via \(t\)-channel Coulomb scattering from the imaginary part of the heavy quark self-energy using cutting rules, with an effective gluon propagator resummed under hard thermal loop (HTL) perturbation theory in a weak background magnetic field. The gluon propagator is expanded to second order in \(qB\) using projection tensors, and the spectral functions for the propagator components are evaluated in the spacelike frequency region (\(|\omega| < q\)) where Landau damping contributes. The momentum diffusion coefficients \(\kappa_L\) and \(\kappa_T\) and the collisional energy loss \(dE/dx\) are then obtained from the differential scattering rate for two geometric cases: heavy quark velocity parallel to the magnetic field (\(\bm{v} \parallel \bm{B}\)) and perpendicular to it (\(\bm{v} \perp \bm{B}\)).

## Key numbers

- The scale hierarchy assumed is \(M_Q \gg T \gg eB/T\); for charm quark mass \(M = 1.28\) GeV and bottom quark mass \(M = 4.18\) GeV, with typical QGP temperature \(T \sim 0.2\text{--}0.4\) GeV.
- Heavy quark formation time is \(\sim 1/(2M_Q)\): approximately 0.08 fm/c for charm and 0.03 fm/c for bottom.
- Heavy quark thermal relaxation time is parametrically larger than light quarks by a factor \(M_Q/T \sim 5\text{--}15\), giving a HQ thermalisation time of 3–9 fm/c compared to the QGP lifetime of \(\sim 5\) fm/c.
- The spatial diffusion coefficient \(D_s\) is found to be independent of heavy flavor mass and almost unaffected by changes in \(B\).
- Both the normalized momentum diffusion coefficient \(\kappa/T^3\) and the energy loss \(dE/dx\) are larger for charm quarks than for bottom quarks, and the effect of the magnetic field is more feeble on bottom quarks than on charm quarks.
- The magnitudes of both \(\kappa\) and \(dE/dx\) are significantly smaller for the case \(\bm{v} \perp \bm{B}\) compared to \(\bm{v} \parallel \bm{B}\).

## Context

This work extends perturbative studies of heavy quark diffusion in a thermal QCD medium (Braaten and Thoma 1991; Moore and Teaney 2005) to include the effect of a weak background magnetic field, motivated by the large magnetic fields generated in non-central heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC. It builds on recent studies of QGP transport in weak magnetic fields (Gupta 2004; Aarts 2015; Ding 2016; Rath 2019; Khan 2021) and complements strong-field studies of HQ dynamics (Fukushima 2016; Singh 2020; Bandyopadhyay 2022). The results enable phenomenological modeling of heavy quark observables (such as elliptic flow and energy loss) in heavy-ion collisions where the magnetic field has decayed to weak values by the time heavy quarks interact with the medium.
