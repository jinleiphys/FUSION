# Extracting the QGP viscosity from RHIC data -- a status report from viscous hydrodynamics

> We report recent progress on causal viscous hydrodynamics for relativistic heavy ion collisions. For fixed specific shear viscosity eta/s, uncertainties in the elliptic flow arising from initial conditions, equation of state, bulk viscosity and numerical viscosity, and the treatment of the highly viscous hadronic stage and freeze-out procedure are analysed. A comparison of current viscous hydrodynamic results with experimental data yields a robust upper limit eta/s < 5/(4pi).

## Key claim
The paper establishes a robust upper limit for the specific shear viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) of η/s < 5/(4π), but this limit carries significant systematic uncertainties from initial conditions, equation of state, bulk viscosity, and hadronic stage treatment that prevent a precise lower bound from being determined.

## Method
The authors used the VISH2+1 code (Viscous Israel-Stewart Hydrodynamics in 2+1 space-time dimensions) to simulate QGP and hadronic matter expansion with longitudinal boost invariance and transverse dynamics. They systematically varied the specific shear viscosity η/s, the equation of state (comparing SM-EOS Q and EOS L), the version of Israel-Stewart equations (simplified vs. full), the freeze-out procedure (isothermal vs. dynamical), the bulk viscosity profile, and the initial conditions (Glauber model vs. Color Glass Condensate/KLN parametrization) to quantify how each affects the elliptic flow v₂ suppression.

## Key numbers
- For minimal viscosity η/s = 1/(4π) ≈ 0.08, the smaller Cu+Cu system at b=7 fm shows almost 70% viscous v₂ suppression below the ideal fluid value at p_T = 2 GeV/c, while the larger Au+Au system at the same impact parameter shows roughly half that suppression.
- For Au+Au at b=7 fm with SM-EOS Q, the viscous v₂ suppression is ~40% below ideal at p_T = 2 GeV/c; switching to EOS L reduces suppression to ~30%; using full Israel-Stewart equations further reduces it to ~25% below ideal at p_T = 2 GeV/c.
- Bulk viscosity with minimal shear viscosity η/s = 1/(4π) increases the viscous suppression of pion elliptic flow at p_T = 0.5 GeV by an additional 25% (for C=1 normalization) to 50% (for C=2 normalization) relative to the case ζ = 0.
- The Color Glass Condensate model leads to ~30% larger initial source eccentricities than the Glauber model, which translates into an ~100% uncertainty in the extracted η/s value.
- For Glauber initial conditions, the allowed range from charged hadron elliptic flow data is 0 < η/s < 0.1; for CGC initial conditions, the range is 0.08 < η/s < 0.2.
- Numerical viscosity in the VISH2+1 code with standard grid spacing (Δx = Δy = 0.1 fm, Δτ = 0.04 fm/c) suppresses differential elliptic flow v₂(p_T) by less than 2%.

## Context
This work extends earlier viscous hydrodynamic calculations by Romatschke (2007), Dusling and Teaney (2007), and the authors' own previous studies (Song and Heinz, 2008) by systematically resolving apparent discrepancies between groups through analysis of system size, equation of state, and Israel-Stewart equation versions. The paper identifies the initial source eccentricity uncertainty (Glauber vs. CGC models) as the single largest obstacle to precision extraction of η/s, following the analysis of Luzum and Romatschke (2008). The results enable future work to focus on reducing systematic uncertainties through improved treatment of the hadronic stage, bulk viscosity, and experimental determination of initial eccentricity.
