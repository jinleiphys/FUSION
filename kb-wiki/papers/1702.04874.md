# Three-body decay of linear-chain states in $^{14}$C

> The decay properties of the linear-chain states in $^{14}$C are investigated by using the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics. The calculation predicts two rotational bands with linear-chain configurations having the $π$-bond and $σ$-bond valence neutrons. For the $π$-bond linear-chain, the calculated excitation energies and the widths of $α$-decay to the ground state of $^{10}{\rm Be}$ reasonably agree with the experimental candidates observed by the $α+{}^{10}{\rm Be}$ resonant scattering. On the other hand, the $σ$-bond linear-chain is the candidate of the higher-lying resonant states reported by the break-up reaction. As the evidence of the $σ$-bond linear-chain, we discuss its decay pattern. It is found that the $σ$-bond linear-chain not only decays to the excited band of $^{10}{\rm Be}$ but also decays to the three-body channel of $^{6}{\rm He}+α+α$, and the branching ratio of these decays are comparable. Hence, we suggest that this characteristic decay pattern is a strong signature of the linear-chain formation and a key observable to distinguish two different linear-chains.

## Key claim

The paper argues that two distinct linear-chain rotational bands exist in $^{14}$C, distinguished by whether the valence neutrons occupy $\pi$-bond or $\sigma$-bond molecular orbits, and that the characteristic decay pattern of the $\sigma$-bond band (comparable branching ratios to $^{10}$Be excited states and to the $^{6}$He+$\alpha$+$\alpha$ three-body channel) provides a strong signature to differentiate the two bands, though the experimental spin-parity assignments for the $\sigma$-bond candidates remain ambiguous.

## Method

The authors used antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) with the Gogny D1S effective nucleon-nucleon interaction to calculate the wave functions of linear-chain states in $^{14}$C, performing variational calculations with quadrupole deformation parameters as generator coordinates in a generator coordinate method (GCM) framework. They then estimated reduced width amplitudes for $\alpha$+$^{10}$Be and $^{6}$He+$^{8}$Be decays by computing overlaps between the GCM wave functions and Brink-Bloch cluster wave functions at channel radii of 5.2 fm (for $\pi$-bond states) and 6.0 fm (for $\sigma$-bond states), from which partial decay widths were obtained using the penetration factor formalism.

## Key numbers

- $\pi$-bond linear-chain bandhead: $0^+$ state at 14.64 MeV excitation energy, with calculated $\alpha$-decay width to $^{10}$Be($0^+_1$) of 250 keV (at 5.2 fm channel radius) and 179 keV (at 6.0 fm)
- $\sigma$-bond linear-chain bandhead: $0^+$ state at 22.16 MeV, with calculated $\alpha$-decay width to $^{10}$Be($0^+_1$) of only 0.2 keV (at 6.0 fm)
- Moment of inertia parameter $\hbar/2\Im$ = 179 keV for $\pi$-bond band and 98 keV for $\sigma$-bond band
- $\sigma$-bond linear-chain $0^+$ state at 22.16 MeV: decay width to $^{10}$Be($0^+_2$; $\sigma^2$) = 136 keV, decay width to $^{6}$He+$^{8}$Be = 38 keV (both at 6.0 fm channel radius)
- $\pi$-bond linear-chain $0^+$ state at 14.64 MeV: overlap with intrinsic linear-chain configuration = 87%
- $\sigma$-bond linear-chain $0^+$ state at 22.16 MeV: overlap with intrinsic linear-chain configuration = 99%
- Experimental candidates from Yamaguchi et al. (2017): $0^+$ at 15.07 MeV with $\Gamma_\alpha$ = 760 keV, $2^+$ at 16.22 MeV with $\Gamma_\alpha$ = 190 keV, $4^+$ at 18.87 MeV with $\Gamma_\alpha$ = 45 keV

## Context

This work extends previous AMD calculations by Baba and Kimura (2016) that predicted two positive-parity rotational bands with linear-chain configurations in $^{14}$C, and responds to new experimental data from Yamaguchi et al. (2017) on $\alpha$+$^{10}$Be elastic scattering and from Tian et al. (2016) and Li et al. (2017) on breakup reactions. The study builds on earlier theoretical predictions of linear-chain states in neutron-rich carbon isotopes by Itagaki (2001), Oertzen (2003, 2004), and others, and on experimental candidates reported by Freer et al. (2014) and Fritsch et al. (2016). The results enable future experimental searches for the predicted three-body decay $^{14}$C$^* \rightarrow {}^6$He + $^8$Be $\rightarrow {}^6$He + $\alpha$ + $\alpha$ as a definitive signature of $\sigma$-bond linear-chain formation.
