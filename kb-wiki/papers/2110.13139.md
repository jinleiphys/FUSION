# Hadron Production in terms of Green's Functions in Non-Equilibrium Matter

> Following the quark-hadron duality concept, we show that the number of hadrons generated in the deconfinement matter is entirely determined by the exact non-equilibrium Green's functions of partons in the medium and the vertex function governing the probability of the confinement-deconfinement phase transition. In such an approach, compactifying the standard (3+1) chromodynamics into $ QCD_{xy} + QCD_{zt}$, the rate of the hadrons produced in particle collisions is derived in the explicit form provided that the hadronization is the first order phase transition. The pion production is found to be in good agreement to the experimental results on the pion yield in pp collisions.

## Key claim

The number of hadrons produced in deconfined matter can be expressed entirely through exact non-equilibrium Green's functions of partons and a vertex function for the confinement-deconfinement phase transition, but only under the condition that hadronization proceeds as a first-order equilibrium phase transition.

## Method

The author compactifies standard (3+1) QCD into a QCD\(_{xy}\) + QCD\(_{zt}\) factorization, separating transverse and longitudinal dynamics. Using the quark-hadron duality concept, the hadron production rate is derived from the squared matrix element of the transition between deconfined and confined quark states, expressed in terms of non-equilibrium single-particle Green's functions. The resulting distribution is obtained by convolving Wigner functions for parton motion with a probability factor governed by the first-order phase transition, and the final formula is compared to experimental pion yields in pp collisions.

## Key numbers

- The phase transition temperature used in the model is \(T_c = 160\) MeV.
- The pion rate from the model is normalized to the experimental value at \((m_T - m_\pi) = 0.2\) GeV/\(c\), where \(m_T = \sqrt{p_T^2 + m_\pi^2}\).
- Rapidity distributions are compared at center-of-mass energies \(\sqrt{s} = 17.3\) GeV, \(\sqrt{s} = 12.3\) GeV, and \(\sqrt{s} = 8.8\) GeV.
- The parameter \(\sigma_a\) is related to the beam rapidity \(y_b\) by \(y_b/2 = \sigma/\sqrt{2}\).

## Context

This work extends the color flux tube approach (LUND model, Andersson 1983) and the QCD\(_{xy}\)+QCD\(_{zt}\) compactification (Koshelkin 2012) by embedding them within the quark-hadron duality concept (Melnitchouk 2005). The derived analytic expressions for \(p_T\) and rapidity distributions show good agreement with experimental data from the NA61 collaboration for pp collisions at intermediate energies. The approach enables future extension to p-A and A-A collisions, where multiple parton collisions and collective nuclear excitations must be incorporated.
