# Sensitivity of the Jet Quenching Observables to the Temperature Dependence of the Energy Loss

> The quenching of minijet (particles with $p_T>> T, Λ_{QCD}$) in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions has been one of the main prediction and discovery at RHIC. We analyze the correlation between different observables like the nuclear modification factor $\Rapt$, the elliptic flow and the ratio of quark to gluon suppressions. We show that the temperature (or entropy density) dependence of the in-medium energy loss strongly affects the relation among these observables. In particular the large elliptic flow and the nearly equal $\Rapt$ of quarks and gluons can be accounted for only if the energy loss occurs mainly around $T_c$ and the $q\leftrightarrow g$ conversion is significant. The use of an equation of state fitted to lattice QCD calculations, slowing down the cooling as $T \to T_c$, seems to contribute to both the enhancement of $v_2$ and the efficiency of the conversion mechanism.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the temperature (or entropy density) dependence of the in-medium energy loss strongly affects the correlation between the nuclear modification factor, elliptic flow, and the ratio of quark to gluon suppression; the large elliptic flow and nearly equal suppression of quarks and gluons can be accounted for only if the energy loss occurs mainly around the critical temperature \(T_c\) and the \(q\leftrightarrow g\) conversion is significant.

## Method

The authors model jet quenching in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV using the GLV (Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev) energy loss formula at first order in opacity, with parton distributions from NLO pQCD and initial conditions from the Glauber model. They compare two bulk density profiles (Wood-Saxon Glauber and sharp elliptic shape) and four different temperature dependences of the energy loss, including constant coupling, running coupling \(\alpha_s(T)\), and two extreme cases where energy loss is concentrated either near \(T_c\) or at high temperature. Hadronization is performed via independent fragmentation using AKK fragmentation functions.

## Key numbers

- Initial bulk temperature \(T_0 = 340\) MeV at \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm for central collisions (\(b=3\) fm), with \(dN/dy \sim 1000\) and transverse area \(A_T \sim 90\) fm\(^2\).
- The nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) is approximately 0.2 for the most central Au+Au collisions at \(p_T > 6\) GeV.
- The GLV formula uses coupling \(\alpha_s = 0.27\) rescaled by a factor \(Z = 0.45\) to reproduce the \(R_{AA}\) data at \(p_T = 6\) GeV for the 0-5% centrality bin.
- For the standard GLV with constant coupling, the ratio \(R_{AA}(q)/R_{AA}(g)\) is about 1.8, deviating from the naive expectation of 9/4 = 2.25.
- With energy loss dominated near \(T_c\), \(R_{AA}(q)/R_{AA}(g)\) increases to about 2.3 for the Wood-Saxon profile; with energy loss dominated at high temperature, it decreases to about 1.5.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of jet quenching beyond the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\), following earlier studies by Shuryak and Liao (2006) and Pantuev (2005) that suggested energy loss occurs mainly near \(T_c\). It contradicts the assumption that the ratio of quark to gluon suppression is simply 9/4, and shows that the temperature dependence of energy loss simultaneously affects elliptic flow and flavor-dependent suppression. The results enable future studies to constrain the temperature dependence of energy loss by requiring simultaneous agreement with \(R_{AA}\), \(v_2\), and the ratio of quark to gluon suppression, using realistic fireball geometries and lattice QCD-based equations of state.
