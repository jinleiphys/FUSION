# Effective Field Theory with Dibaryon Fields: Nucleon-Nucleon amplitudes at NNLO

> We calculate the nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitudes in the 1S0 and 3S1-3D1 channels at next-to-next to leading order starting from a recently proposed non-relativistic chiral effective theory, which includes dibaryon fields as fundamental degrees of freedom. We restrict ourselves to center of mass energies (E) smaller than the pion mass (m_pi), and further divide the calculation into two relative momentum (p) regions, a high energy one p ~ m_pi >> delta m_i, δm_i being the dibaryon residual masses, and low energy one p ~ delta m_i. We first match to a lower energy effective theory in which we calculate the amplitudes in the high energy region. We further match this effective theory to the so called pionless effective theory in the low energy region, and carry out the calculations in the latter. Dimensional regularization and minimal subtraction scheme are used throughout. For 1S0 channel a good description of the phase shift data is obtained for E < 50 MeV. For the 3S1-3D1 channel, the 3S1 phase shift data is only well described up to E < 20 MeV.

## Key claim

The authors calculate nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitudes at next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO) using a chiral effective theory with dibaryon fields, but the description of data is energy-dependent: for the 1S0 channel a good description is obtained only for center-of-mass energies below 50 MeV, while for the 3S1-3D1 channel the 3S1 phase shift data is only well described up to 20 MeV, indicating a more limited range of validity than the abstract's phrasing might suggest.

## Method

The calculation proceeds by matching the full chiral effective theory with dibaryon fields (NNEFT) to a lower-energy "potential" effective theory (pNNEFT) without explicit pions, then further matching pNNEFT to a pionless effective theory (slashed-pi NNEFT) for the low-momentum region where p is less than or comparable to the dibaryon residual masses. The authors use dimensional regularization and minimal subtraction throughout, and organize the calculation into two relative momentum regions: a high-energy region where p is of order the pion mass m_pi and much larger than the dibaryon residual masses delta_m_i, and a low-energy region where p is of order delta_m_i. The dibaryon propagator is resummed to include the bubble self-energy from nucleon loops, which becomes the leading-order propagator.

## Key numbers

- For the 1S0 channel, a good description of phase shift data is obtained for E less than 50 MeV
- For the 3S1-3D1 channel, the 3S1 phase shift data is only well described up to E less than 20 MeV
- The leading-order S-wave amplitudes are given by A_LO = i * 4*pi / (m_N * p) for both singlet and triplet channels, where m_N is the nucleon mass and p = sqrt(m_N * E)
- The dibaryon residual masses delta_m_i are of order 1/(pi * a_i), where a_i are the scattering lengths, and are estimated to be less than m_pi^2 / Lambda_chi
- The pion decay constant f_pi is approximately 132 MeV and the axial vector coupling g_A is approximately 1.25

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous NLO calculation (Soto 2007) to N2LO to test convergence of the effective field theory, motivated by the fact that the KSW approach (Kaplan, Savage, Wise) also produced good NLO results but showed poor convergence in the 3S1 channel at N2LO (Fleming 1999). The approach uses dibaryon fields as fundamental degrees of freedom, which differs from standard chiral EFT treatments that use only nucleon and pion fields. The calculation enables systematic improvement of nucleon-nucleon scattering predictions within a renormalizable framework with simple counting rules, and the matching procedure between effective theories at different momentum scales provides a template for similar multi-scale problems in nuclear physics.
