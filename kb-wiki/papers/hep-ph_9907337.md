# Initial Time Singularities in Non-Equilibrium Evolution of Condensates and Their Resolution in the Linearized Approximation

> The real time non-equilibrium evolution of condensates in field theory requires an initial value problem specifying an initial quantum state or density matrix. Arbitrary specifications of the initial quantum state (pure or mixed) results in initial time singularities which are not removed by the usual renormalization counterterms. We study the initial time singularities in the linearized equation of motion for the scalar condensate in a renormalizable Yukawa theory in 3+1 dimensions. In this renormalizable theory the initial time singularities are enhanced. We present a consistent method for removing these initial time singularities by specifying initial states where the distribution of high energy quanta is determined by the initial conditions and the interaction effects. This is done through a Bogoliubov transformation which is consistently obtained in a perturbative expansion.The usual renormalization counterterms and the proper choice of the Bogoliubov coefficients lead to a singularity free evolution equation. We establish the relationship between the evolution equations in the linearized approximation and linear response theory. It is found that only a very specific form of the external source for linear response leads to a real time evolution equation which is singularity free. We focus on the evolution of spatially inhomogeneous scalar condensates by implementing the initial state preparation via a Bogoliubov transformation up to one-loop. As a concrete application, the evolution equation for an inhomogenous condensate is solved analytically and the results are carefully analyzed. Symmetry breaking by initial quantum states is discussed.

## Key claim

Initial time singularities in the real-time evolution of scalar condensates in renormalizable Yukawa theory can be removed by preparing the initial quantum state via a Bogoliubov transformation, but only if the Bogoliubov coefficients for high-momentum modes are constrained to vanish in a particular manner, specifically, the \(1/p\) and \(1/p^3\) contributions must be uniquely fixed by the initial data, the coupling, and the mass.

## Method

The authors study the linearized equation of motion for a scalar condensate in a renormalizable Yukawa theory in 3+1 dimensions, using the closed-time-path (CTP) formalism. They compute the one-loop self-energy and tadpole contributions for both homogeneous and inhomogeneous condensates, identifying ultraviolet divergences and initial time singularities. To resolve the initial time singularities, they introduce a Bogoliubov transformation on the fermionic fields that dresses the initial free-field Fock vacuum state, and determine the Bogoliubov coefficients perturbatively to one-loop order by requiring cancellation of the singular terms at the initial time. The resulting singularity-free evolution equation is then solved analytically for an inhomogeneous spherical-wave condensate.

## Key numbers

- The coefficient of \(\phi_{\bf 0}(0)\) in the initial-time singular terms diverges as \(1/t^2\), the coefficient of \(\dot\phi_{\bf 0}(0)\) diverges as \(1/t\), and the coefficient of \(\ddot\phi_{\bf 0}(0)\) diverges as \(\log t\) as \(t \to 0\).
- The mass counterterm in dimensional regularization is \(\delta M^2 = -\frac{3 g^2 m^2}{4 \pi^2}\left[\frac{2}{\epsilon} - \gamma + \frac{1}{3} + \ln\frac{4\pi \mu^2}{m^2}\right]\).
- The wave-function counterterm in dimensional regularization is \(\delta Z = -\frac{g^2}{8 \pi^2}\left[\frac{2}{\epsilon} - \gamma - \frac{2}{3} + \ln\frac{4\pi \mu^2}{m^2}\right]\).
- The Bogoliubov coefficients to one-loop order are given by \(\beta_p \cos\delta_p = g \frac{p}{E_p}\left[-\frac{1}{2E_p}\phi_{\bf 0}(0) + \frac{1}{(2E_p)^3}\ddot\phi_{\bf 0}(0)\right]\) and \(\beta_p \sin\delta_p = g \frac{p}{E_p}\left[-\frac{1}{(2E_p)^2}\dot\phi_{\bf 0}(0)\right]\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier studies of initial time singularities by Baacke (1998a, 1998c) from self-consistent homogeneous evolution to the linearized approximation for inhomogeneous condensates in a renormalizable Yukawa theory. The method of Bogoliubov-transformed initial states provides a systematic, perturbative resolution of these singularities, which were originally noted by Stueckelberg and later studied by Symanzik in the context of sharp boundary conditions. The results enable consistent real-time evolution calculations for non-equilibrium field theory, particularly for inhomogeneous condensates relevant to cosmology and heavy-ion collisions, and establish a direct correspondence with linear response theory where only a very specific form of the external source yields a singularity-free initial value problem.
