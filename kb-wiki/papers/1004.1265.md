# Scalar response of the nucleon chiral symmetry and nuclear matter properties

> In this talk we present a description of nuclear binding in a chiral approach based on the existence of a chiral invariant scalar field associated with the generation of the masses through spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking. We discuss the emergence of such a field on the example of the NJL model. We also incorporate the effect of confinement at the level of the nucleon substructure to stabilize nuclear matter. In a particular quark-diquark model we illustrate the simutaneous influences of spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking and confinement on the nucleon mass and on the nuclear matter description.

## Key claim

The paper argues that nuclear binding can be described by a chiral invariant scalar field associated with spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking, but that the nucleon's scalar response, dependent on confinement, is essential to stabilize nuclear matter; without this response, the chiral effective potential generates an attractive three-body force that destroys saturation.

## Method

The authors formulate an effective theory where the nuclear scalar field is identified with radial fluctuations of the chiral condensate, and incorporate the nucleon's scalar response via a quadratic term in the nucleon mass expansion. They then perform Hartree and Hartree-Fock calculations of nuclear matter, including omega and rho meson exchange, pion loops with short-range correlations, and Delta-hole excitations, using parameters constrained by lattice data and vector meson dominance. A quark-diquark model with a confining harmonic potential is used to compute the nucleon mass and its scalar response as a function of the in-medium scalar field.

## Key numbers

- Lattice value of the nucleon mass expansion coefficient: \(a_4 \simeq -0.5\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-3}\) from Thomas et al. (2004), compared to the chiral model value \((a_4)_{\text{Chiral}} \simeq -3.5\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-3}\).
- Dimensionless scalar response parameter \(C \equiv (f_\pi^2 / 2 M_N) \kappa_{NS} \simeq 1.25\) reproduces the lattice \(a_4\) value; in the Hartree-Fock calculation, \(C = 1.33\) is used.
- In the Hartree-Fock calculation: scalar coupling \(g_S = 10\), sigma mass \(m_\sigma = 800\ \mathrm{MeV}\), rho coupling \(g_\rho = 2.6\) (VDM), omega coupling \(g_\omega = 6.4\) (close to VDM value \(3 g_\rho\)).
- In the NJL-based model: cutoff \(\Lambda = 1\ \mathrm{GeV}\), bare quark mass \(m = 3.5\ \mathrm{MeV}\), coupling \(G_1 = 7.8\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\); vacuum quark mass \(M_0 = 371\ \mathrm{MeV}\), condensate \(\langle \bar{q} q \rangle = -(286\ \mathrm{MeV})^3\); low-momentum mass parameters \((M_\sigma)_{\text{eff}} = 659\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(M_V = 653\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(M_A = 797\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- In the quark-diquark model: diquark mass \(M_D = 398.5\ \mathrm{MeV}\), string tension \(K = (290\ \mathrm{MeV})^3\), vacuum nucleon mass \(M_N = 1304\ \mathrm{MeV}\); effective scalar coupling \((g_S)_{\text{eff}} = 7.14\); pion-nucleon sigma term (non-pionic part) \(\sigma_{\sigma}^{(\text{no pion})} = 30\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Correlation energy from pion loops is modest: \(\simeq -10\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Rho Hartree contribution to the asymmetry energy is \(7\ \mathrm{MeV}\), insufficient to reach the accepted value around \(30\ \mathrm{MeV}\) without the Fock tensor term.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean-field approach of Walecka by grounding the scalar field in chiral symmetry breaking, and addresses the instability problem identified by Bentz and Thomas (2001) where the chiral effective potential generates an attractive three-body force. It uses lattice data from Thomas et al. (2004) to constrain the nucleon scalar response, and builds on the authors' previous Hartree and Hartree-Fock calculations (Chanfray and Ericson 2005, 2007; Margueron and Chanfray 2008, 2009). The quark-diquark model with confinement follows the approach of Bentz et al. (2001) but uses a harmonic confining potential instead of an infrared cutoff. The results enable a consistent description of nuclear matter saturation, compressibility, and asymmetry energy within a chiral framework, and predict a neutron-proton mass difference in neutron-rich matter consistent with ab-initio BHF calculations (Samaddar et al. 2005).
