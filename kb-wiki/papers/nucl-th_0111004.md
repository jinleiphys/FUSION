# Spectral Function of Quarks in Quark Matter

> We investigate the spectral function of light quarks in infinite quark matter using a simple, albeit self-consistent model. The interactions between the quarks are described by the SU(2) Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. Currently mean field effects are neglected and all calculations are performed in the chirally restored phase at zero temperature. Relations between correlation functions and collision rates are used to calculate the spectral function in an iterative process.

## Key claim

The spectral function of light quarks in infinite quark matter can be calculated self-consistently using a simple model based on the SU(2) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio interaction, but only in the chirally restored phase at zero temperature with mean-field effects neglected, and the resulting short-range correlations are weak at nuclear-matter-equivalent densities unless the coupling strength is artificially increased.

## Method

The authors used the SU(2) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a pointlike interaction and a three-momentum cutoff to describe quark interactions, then employed relations between correlation functions and collision rates to set up a self-consistency problem linking the collisional self-energies (gain and loss rates) to the spectral function. They solved this problem iteratively on a finite energy-momentum grid, restricting calculations to the chirally restored phase (massless quarks) at zero temperature with the real part of the retarded self-energy set to zero, and considered only two-particle correlations via the direct and exchange Born diagrams.

## Key numbers

* Quark density of 0.51 fm\(^{-3}\) (three times nuclear matter density) gives a Fermi energy and Fermi momentum of \(\omega_F = p_F = 0.268\) GeV for massless quarks.
* Higher density of 1.53 fm\(^{-3}\) gives a Fermi energy of \(\omega_F = 0.387\) GeV.
* NJL model parameters: three-momentum cutoff \(\Lambda = 653\) MeV and coupling constant \(G = 2.14 \cdot \Lambda^{-2}\), chosen to reproduce the vacuum quark condensate \(\langle\bar u u\rangle = \langle\bar d d\rangle = -(250\ \mathrm{MeV})^{3}\) and pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV.
* At the lower density and original coupling, the width \(\Gamma_0\) in the populated quark region (\(0 < p_0 < \omega_F\)) takes values of 0.1–1 MeV.
* At the lower density, the depletion of the quark momentum distribution (high-momentum tail) is only 0.1% for the original coupling, rising to a few percent for a coupling four times larger (\(G = 4 \cdot 2.14 \cdot \Lambda^{-2}\)).
* At the higher density (\(\omega_F = 0.387\) GeV) with original coupling, the depletion grows to almost 1%, comparable to the result for twice the coupling at the lower density.

## Context

This work extends the transport-theory-based approach of Lehr et al. (2000), who successfully calculated the nucleon spectral function in nuclear matter using an averaged constant scattering amplitude, to the quark sector using the NJL model. The results show that short-range correlations in quark matter are much weaker than in nuclear matter at comparable densities, unless the coupling is increased or the density is raised well beyond the chiral phase transition. The authors note that future work could incorporate an attractive quark-quark channel to study color superconductivity, as done in extended NJL models by Berges and Rajagopal (1999) and Langfeld et al. (2000).
