# Advances in the ab initio description of nuclear three-cluster systems

> We introduce the extension of the ab initio no-core shell model with continuum to describe three-body cluster systems. We present results for the ground state of 6He and show improvements with respect to the description obtained within the no-core shell model and the no-core shell model/resonating group methods.

## Key claim

The extension of the ab initio no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC) to three-cluster systems yields improved ground-state energies for 6He compared to the no-core shell model (NCSM) and the no-core shell model/resonating group method (NCSM/RGM), but only when the NCSM eigenstate is included to compensate for missing cluster excitations and short-range correlations.

## Method

The authors solve the Schrödinger equation using a wave function ansatz that superposes discrete NCSM eigenstates of the compound 6He system with continuum three-cluster NCSM/RGM basis states describing an 4He core plus two neutrons. The resulting coupled equations are orthogonalized and solved via the microscopic R-matrix method on a Lagrange mesh, using the similarity-renormalization-group (SRG) evolved chiral N3LO nucleon-nucleon interaction with ΛSRG = 1.5 fm⁻¹.

## Key numbers

- NCSMC ground-state energy of 6He at Nmax=12: -29.86 MeV
- NCSM ground-state energy of 6He at Nmax=12: -29.66 MeV
- NCSM/RGM ground-state energy of 6He at Nmax=12: -28.70 MeV
- Extrapolated NCSM ground-state energy of 6He to Nmax→∞: -29.84(4) MeV
- NCSMC ground-state energy at Nmax=10: -29.86 MeV, showing convergence already at Nmax=10

## Context

This work extends the NCSMC formalism, previously introduced for binary systems by the same group (PhysRevLett.110.022505, PhysRevC.87.034326), to three-cluster systems. It improves upon earlier three-cluster NCSM/RGM calculations (Quaglioni 2013, Romero-Redondo 2014) by including NCSM eigenstates that capture short-range correlations and core polarization missing in the cluster-only basis. The method enables future studies of two-neutron halo nuclei like 6He and 11Li, resonant systems such as 5H, and three-fragment transfer reactions.
