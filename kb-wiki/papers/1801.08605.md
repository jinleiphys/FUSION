# Probing the tricritical endpoint of QCD phase diagram at NICA-FAIR energies

> In this contributions we discuss the novel version of hadron resonance gas model which is based on the induced surface tension concept. Also we present new arguments in favor of a hypothesis that the chiral symmetry restoration transition in central nuclear collisions may occur at the center of mass energies 4.3-4.9 GeV and that the deconfinement phase transition may occur at the center of mass energies 8.8-9.2 GeV. These arguments are based on the unique thermostatic properties of the mixed phase and the ones of an exponential mass spectrum of hadrons.

## Key claim

The authors present new arguments that the chiral symmetry restoration transition in central nuclear collisions may occur at center-of-mass energies 4.3–4.9 GeV and the deconfinement phase transition may occur at center-of-mass energies 8.8–9.2 GeV, based on the thermostatic properties of the mixed phase and an exponential hadron mass spectrum; these claims are hedged as a hypothesis requiring future experimental verification.

## Method

The authors used a novel version of the hadron resonance gas model (HRGM) based on the induced surface tension (IST) concept, which reduces the system of equations for particle number densities to three coupled equations regardless of the number of hadronic species. They fitted the model to all independent hadronic multiplicity ratios measured in central heavy-ion collisions at center-of-mass energies from 2.7 to 2760 GeV, then analyzed the collision energy dependence of thermodynamic quantities such as the trace anomaly, baryonic charge density, modified Wroblewski factor, and strangeness suppression factor at chemical freeze-out.

## Key numbers

- The chiral symmetry restoration transition is argued to occur at center-of-mass energies 4.3–4.9 GeV.
- The deconfinement phase transition is argued to occur at center-of-mass energies 8.8–9.2 GeV.
- Two sharp peaks in baryonic charge density at chemical freeze-out are located at center-of-mass energies 4.9 GeV and 9.2 GeV.
- Two peaks of the trace anomaly delta = (epsilon - 3p)/T^4 are observed at the maximal energy of each quasi-plateau set, i.e., at 4.9 GeV and 9.2 GeV.
- The number of effective degrees of freedom of almost massless particles estimated for the low-energy transition is about 1770.
- The hard-core radii used in the model are: pions R_pi = 0.15 fm, K-mesons R_K = 0.395 fm, nucleons R_p = 0.365 fm, and Lambda-hyperons R_Lambda = 0.085 fm.
- The IST equation of state is valid up to packing fractions eta = 0.2–0.22.
- The strangeness suppression factor gamma_s equals 1 (full chemical equilibrium) for center-of-mass energies above 8.8 GeV and also at 4.9 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Bugaev 2015, 2016a, 2016b, 2017a, 2017b) that found correlated quasi-plateaus in thermodynamic quantities at chemical freeze-out at two energy ranges. It contradicts the earlier interpretation by Gazdzicki (2010) that the Kink, Strangeness Horn, and Step signals in heavy-ion collisions are unambiguous deconfinement signals, because similar structures were later found in proton-proton collisions (StepPP). The results enable experimental searches for the tricritical endpoint of the QCD phase diagram at NICA (JINR Dubna) and FAIR (GSI Darmstadt), and are supported by the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics transport model from Giessen (Cassing 2016a, 2016b) which predicts a first-order chiral symmetry restoration transition near 4 GeV and a second-order deconfinement transition near 10 GeV.
