# Impressions of the Continuum Bound State Problem in QCD

> Modern and anticipated facilities will deliver data that promises to reveal the innermost workings of quantum chromodynamics (QCD). In order to fulfill that promise, phenomenology and theory must reach a new level, limiting and overcoming model-dependence, so that clean lines can be drawn that connect the data with QCD itself. Progress in that direction, made using continuum methods for the hadron bound-state problem, is sketched herein.

## Key claim

Continuum methods using Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) can now produce predictions for hadron observables that connect directly to QCD, but the rainbow-ladder (RL) truncation remains a controlled approximation whose infrared overmagnification of the effective interaction compensates for omitted vertex dressing, and its domain of validity excludes systems with significant orbital angular momentum or large quark-mass imbalance.

## Method

The authors employ the rainbow-ladder truncation of the DSEs, solving the quark gap equation and the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for mesons, and the Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation for baryons. The interaction kernel is a one-loop renormalisation-group-improved form with an infrared Gaussian term, Eq. (defcalG), whose parameters are fixed by reproducing the pion's leptonic decay constant. For heavy+light systems where direct solution is obstructed by complex conjugate poles in the dressed-quark propagator, the Schlessinger point method (sSPM) is used for analytic continuation.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: RL truncation gives \(f_\pi = 0.094\) GeV, experiment \(0.092\) GeV (Table obsuds)
- Kaon decay constant: RL gives \(f_K = 0.11\) GeV, experiment \(0.11\) GeV (Table obsuds)
- Rho meson mass: RL gives \(m_\rho = 0.75\) GeV, experiment \(0.78\) GeV (Table obsuds)
- Kaon-star mass: RL gives \(m_{K^\ast} = 0.95\) GeV, experiment \(0.89\) GeV (Table obsuds)
- Phi meson mass: RL gives \(m_\phi = 1.09\) GeV, experiment \(1.02\) GeV (Table obsuds)
- One-loop evolved current-quark masses used: \(m_{u,d}^{2\,\text{GeV}} = 4.8\) MeV, \(m_s^{2\,\text{GeV}} = 110\) MeV (caption Table obsuds)
- Interaction parameter \(\varsigma = 0.80\) GeV fixed to reproduce \(f_\pi\) empirically
- Predicted branching fraction for \(D_s^+ \to K^0 e^+ \nu_e\): \(10^3 \mathcal{B} = 3.31(33)\), compared with experiment \(3.25(38)\) (caption Fig. FigDsK)
- Extracted CKM element from that decay: \(|V_{cd}| = 0.216(17)\), world average \(0.218(4)\) (caption Fig. FigDsK)

## Context

This review sketches progress made since the introduction of symmetry-preserving truncations by Munczek (1994) and Bender (1996), extending earlier rudimentary DSE studies. The RL truncation successfully describes light pseudoscalar and vector mesons but fails for axial-vector, scalar, and tensor mesons because DCSB-induced corrections interfere constructively rather than destructively (Chang 2009, 2010, 2011). The work enables predictions for form factors out to arbitrarily large spacelike momenta via PTIRs (Chang 2013), motivating JLab experiments up to \(Q^2 \approx 9\) GeV\(^2\), and for heavy+light meson decays via sSPM (Binosi 2018, Yao 2020). Future challenges include generalised and transverse-momentum-dependent parton distributions and parton fragmentation functions, which connect confinement, DCSB, and emergent hadronic mass.
