# The Quark-Mass Dependence of T_C in QCD: Working up from m=0 or down from m=infinity ?

> We analyze the dependence of the QCD transition temperature on the quark (or pion) mass. We find that a linear sigma model, which links the transition to chiral symmetry restoration, predicts a much stronger dependence of T_c on m_pi than seen in present lattice data for m_pi >~ 0.4 GeV. On the other hand, working down from m_pi=infinity, an effective Lagrangian for the Polyakov loop requires only small explicit symmetry breaking, b_1 ~ exp(-m_pi), to describe T_c(m_pi) in the above mass range. Physically, this is a consequence of the flat potential (large correlation length) for the Polyakov loop in the three-color pure gauge theory at T_c. We quantitatively estimate the end point of the line of first order deconfining phase transitions: m_pi = 1.8 GeV and Tc = 240 MeV for three flavors and three colors.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the QCD transition temperature's dependence on the pion mass is better described by a small explicit breaking of the Z(3) center symmetry in a Polyakov-loop effective Lagrangian (working down from infinite quark mass) than by a linear sigma model based on chiral symmetry restoration (working up from zero quark mass), at least for pion masses above approximately 0.4 GeV.

## Method

The authors compare two effective field theory approaches to lattice data on the QCD transition temperature as a function of pion mass. First, they use a renormalized O(4) linear sigma model in the Hartree approximation, with vacuum parameters fixed by lattice data for the pion mass dependence of the pion decay constant and sigma mass, to compute the chiral crossover temperature. Second, they employ a mean-field effective Lagrangian for the Polyakov loop with a Z(3) symmetry-breaking term linear in the loop, extracting the explicit symmetry-breaking coupling b1 by matching the predicted shift in transition temperature to three-flavor lattice data from Karsch (2000).

## Key numbers

- For the linear sigma model, the chiral crossover temperature rises much more steeply with pion mass than lattice data; the data is "nearly flat" on the scale of the figure showing Tc versus m_pi.
- The endpoint of the line of first-order deconfining phase transitions is estimated at m_pi = 4.2 sqrt(sigma) ≈ 1.8 GeV and Tc ≈ 240 MeV for three flavors and three colors, where sqrt(sigma) ≈ 0.425 GeV.
- The explicit symmetry-breaking coupling b1 required to match lattice data is less than 0.15 for all pion masses down to m_pi/sqrt(sigma) ≈ 1; the critical b1 at which the first-order transition ends is b1^c = 0.026(1).
- The shift in transition temperature at the endpoint is ΔTc/Tc^* ≈ 12%, where Tc^* is the pure gauge theory transition temperature.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the quark-mass dependence of the QCD transition temperature by comparing two effective approaches: a linear sigma model (following Pisarski and Wilczek, and Gavin, Gocksch, and Pisarski) and a Polyakov-loop effective Lagrangian (following Pisarski, Dumitru, and collaborators). The paper provides a quantitative estimate of the deconfining critical point "D" in the schematic phase diagram of Gavin, Gocksch, and Pisarski (1994), and notes that the weak first-order transition in the pure gauge theory (with a flat Polyakov-loop potential near Tc) explains why small explicit symmetry breaking suffices to describe the lattice data. The results enable future lattice studies to test whether the QCD crossover for pion masses above the chiral critical point is primarily driven by Polyakov-loop dynamics rather than chiral order-parameter fluctuations.
