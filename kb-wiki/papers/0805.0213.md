# The polarizability of the pion: no conflict between dispersion theory and chiral perturbation theory

> Recent attempts to determine the pion polarizability by dispersion relations yield values that disagree with the predictions of chiral perturbation theory. These dispersion relations are based on specific forms for the absorptive part of the Compton amplitudes. The analytic properties of these forms are examined, and the strong enhancement of intermediate-meson contributions is shown to be connected with spurious singularities. If the basic requirements of dispersion relations are taken into account, the results of dispersion theory and effective field theory are not inconsistent.

## Key claim
The apparent conflict between dispersion theory and chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) predictions for the pion polarizability arises because the dispersion-relation analyses of Fil'kov and Kashevarov use specific resonance forms with spurious singularities (unphysical poles and cuts on the first Riemann sheet); when the basic analytic requirements of dispersion relations are respected, the results of the two approaches are not inconsistent.

## Method
The authors examined the analytic properties of three generic resonance models (constant-width pole, energy-dependent width, and small-width approximation) for the Compton amplitudes on the pion, using propagators with and without energy-dependent widths and coupling constants. They then constructed a proper fixed-\(t\) dispersion relation in the \(t\) channel for \(\gamma\gamma \to \pi\pi\), unitarizing the S-wave via the Omnès function with \(\pi\pi\) phase shifts and including vector-meson (\(\rho,\omega\)) exchanges and the \(f_2(1270)\) resonance, to compute the electric (\(\alpha\)) and magnetic (\(\beta\)) dipole polarizabilities at the Compton threshold.

## Key numbers
- ChPT at \({\cal O}(p^6)\) predicts \(\alpha_{\pi^+} + \beta_{\pi^+} = 0.16\) (error \(\sim 0.1\)) and \(\alpha_{\pi^+} - \beta_{\pi^+} = 5.7 \pm 1.0\) (in units of \(10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^3\)).
- The dispersion-relation analysis of Fil'kov and Kashevarov gives \(11.1 \leq \alpha_{\pi^+} - \beta_{\pi^+} \leq 15.6\) (same units).
- The MAMI experiment (radiative pion photoproduction) yields \(\alpha_{\pi^+} - \beta_{\pi^+} = 11.6 \pm 1.5_{\text{stat}} \pm 3.0_{\text{syst}} \pm 0.5_{\text{mod}}\).
- The Serpukhov Primakov experiment gives \(\alpha_{\pi^+} - \beta_{\pi^+} = 13.6 \pm 2.8_{\text{stat}} \pm 2.4_{\text{syst}}\).
- The \(f_2(1270)\) resonance contributes \((\alpha+\beta)^{\pi^+}_{f_2} = 0.17\) (same units).
- In the model study, the spurious left cut from the energy-dependent coupling in model \(C\) cancels 85% of the physical right-cut contribution to the backward polarizability.

## Context
This work addresses the long-standing discrepancy between ChPT predictions (Gasser et al., 2006) and dispersion-relation results (Fil'kov and Kashevarov, 2005) for the charged-pion polarizability. By demonstrating that the earlier dispersion analyses introduced unphysical singularities (e.g., a spurious pole at \(s \approx -11\,\text{GeV}^2\) and a \(1/\sqrt{s}\) cut) that artificially enhanced the meson contributions, the authors reconcile the two theoretical frameworks. The properly unitarized \(t\)-channel dispersion relation, including \(\rho\), \(\omega\), and \(f_2\) mesons, provides a consistent method to compute polarizabilities and enables future experimental analyses (e.g., COMPASS, Jefferson Lab 12 GeV upgrade) to be interpreted without assuming a conflict between theory and data.
