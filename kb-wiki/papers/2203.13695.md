# NLO JIMWLK evolution with massive quarks

> NLO evolution of the Jalilian-Marian-Iancu-McLerran-Weigert-Leonidov-Kovner (JIMWLK) equation with massless quarks was derived a few years ago. We make a step further to compute the evolution kernels focusing on the effects due to finite quark masses. To this goal, the light-cone wave function of a fast moving dilute hadronic projectile is computed up to ${\cal O}(g^3)$ in QCD coupling constant. Compared with the massless case, a new IR divergence emerges, which is eventually canceled by a mass dependent counter term. Our results extend the theoretical tools used in physics of gluon saturation and aim at improving precision in future phenomenological applications.

## Key claim
The paper computes the next-to-leading order (NLO) JIMWLK evolution kernels including finite quark mass effects, finding that a new infrared divergence appears compared to the massless case which is canceled by a mass-dependent counterterm, thereby extending the theoretical framework for gluon saturation physics to include phenomenologically relevant quark mass corrections.

## Method
The authors use light-cone quantization to compute the light-cone wave function of a fast-moving dilute hadronic projectile up to O(g^3) in the QCD coupling constant, following the Born-Oppenheimer approximation where valence modes are treated as a classical background and soft modes are computed perturbatively. The quark mass-dependent terms in the light-cone Hamiltonian are isolated, and the matrix elements for soft gluon emission into quark-antiquark pairs are derived with finite quark masses. The NLO JIMWLK Hamiltonian is then extracted from the diagonal matrix element of the S-matrix operator in the light-cone wave function, with dimensional regularization and the MS-bar scheme used for loop corrections.

## Key numbers
- The charm quark contribution to the structure function F2 at HERA at Bjorken x ~ 10^-2 to 10^-3 is about 10% (Gotsman 2002), motivating the inclusion of mass effects.
- The light-cone wave function is computed up to O(g^3) in the QCD coupling constant.
- The leading order QCD beta-function coefficient is b = (11N_c - 2N_f)/3.
- The NLO JIMWLK Hamiltonian with massless quarks contains kernels K_JSJ, K_JSSJ, K_q\bar{q}, K_JJSSJ, and K_JJSJ, with the quark mass corrections affecting only K_q\bar{q} and K_JSJ.

## Context
This work extends the NLO JIMWLK Hamiltonian previously derived for massless quarks by Kovner (2013) and Lublinsky (2016) to include finite quark masses, which are significant for phenomenological applications at realistic energies such as those at HERA and the future Electron-Ion Collider. The calculation is complementary to the work of Beuf (2021) which computed mass effects in photon impact factors, and together these developments aim to improve precision in NLO phenomenology of gluon saturation. The results enable more accurate theoretical predictions for high-energy QCD processes where quark masses cannot be neglected, particularly for charm and bottom quarks at moderate Bjorken x.
