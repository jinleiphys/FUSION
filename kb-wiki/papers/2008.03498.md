# Nuclear fission in intense laser fields

> Rapid-advancing intense laser technologies enable the possibility of a direct laser-nucleus coupling. In this paper the effect of intense laser fields on a series of nuclear fission processes, including proton decay, alpha decay, and cluster decay, is theoretically studied with the help of nuclear double folding potentials. The results show that the half-lives of these decay processes can be modified by non-negligible amounts, for example on the order of 0.01 or 0.1 percents in intense laser fields available in the forthcoming years. In addition to numerical results, an approximate analytical formula is derived to connect the laser-induced modification to the decay half-life and the decay energy.

# Nuclear fission in intense laser fields

## Key claim
Intense laser fields with intensities up to 10^24 W/cm^2 can modify the half-lives of proton decay, alpha decay, and cluster decay by small but non-negligible amounts on the order of 0.01 to 0.1 percent, under the condition that the laser field can be treated as quasistatic during the tunneling time (approximately 10^-20 seconds), which is much shorter than an optical cycle of currently available lasers.

## Method
The authors construct effective nucleus-nucleus potentials using double folding integrals with mass and charge density distributions in a Woods-Saxon form, combined with a density-dependent M3Y nucleon-nucleon interaction. The laser-nucleus interaction is included via a dipole-approximation coupling term -q_eff r epsilon(t) cos(theta) in the relative-motion Hamiltonian after separating center-of-mass coordinates. Decay half-lives are calculated using the WKB penetrability formula under the quasistatic approximation, where the laser field is treated as constant during the tunneling event.

## Key numbers
- Laser intensity assumed: 10^24 W/cm^2, expected to be achievable in forthcoming years
- For proton decay 109I -> 108Te + p (Q=0.83 MeV, l=2): half-life changes from 1.03039e-4 s to 1.02696e-4 s, relative change Delta_T = 3.329e-3 (0.33%)
- For alpha decay 243Cm -> 239Pu + 4He (Q=6.18 MeV, l=2): half-life changes from 9.12011e+8 s to 9.11587e+8 s, Delta_T = 4.646e-4 (0.046%)
- For 16O-cluster decay 154Gd -> 138Ba + 16O (Q=19.29 MeV, l=0): half-life changes from 3.16228e+48 s to 3.15936e+48 s, Delta_T = 9.236e-4 (0.092%)
- For 28Mg-cluster decay 236U -> 208Hg + 28Mg (Q=71.83 MeV, l=0): Delta_T = 1.553e-4 (0.016%)
- Proton decay modifications range from Delta_T = 1.066e-3 to 3.329e-3 (0.1% to 0.33%)
- Alpha decay modifications range from Delta_T = 1.127e-4 to 2.693e-3 (0.01% to 0.27%)
- Cluster decay modifications range from Delta_T = 9.666e-5 to 1.352e-3 (0.01% to 0.14%)
- The derived approximate analytical formula gives Delta_T proportional to Q^{-5/2} plus a constant term

## Context
This work extends previous theoretical studies of laser-nucleus coupling, including earlier work on alpha decay by Keitel (2013), Misicu (2013), Delion (2017), Palffy (2019), and Qi (2019), as well as studies of deuteron-triton fusion by Queisser (2019), Lv (2019), and Wang (2020). The paper provides a unified theoretical framework using double folding potentials (following Basu 2003 and Kobos 1984) that can treat proton decay, alpha decay, and cluster decay on equal footing. The derived analytical formula connecting Delta_T to Q^{-5/2} generalizes the Geiger-Nuttall law (Geiger 1911) to laser-modified decays and predicts that for isotopes of a given element, Delta_T versus Q^{-5/2} should fall approximately on a straight line, as demonstrated for 218-230Th alpha decays. This framework enables future calculations of laser effects on fission processes not included in the current study.
