# Roles of $^7$Be$(n,p)^7$Li resonances in big bang nucleosynthesis with time-dependent quark mass and Li reduction by a heavy quark mass

> Big bang nucleosynthesis (BBN) has been used as a probe of beyond-standard physics in the early Universe, which includes a time-dependent quark mass m_q. We investigate effects of a quark mass variation delta m_q on the cross sections of the 7Be(n, p)7Li reaction and primordial light element abundances taking into account roles of 8Be resonances in the reaction during BBN. This resonant reaction has not been investigated although behaviors of low-lying resonances are not trivial. It is found that a resonance at the resonance energy E_r=0.33 MeV enhances the reaction rate and lowers the 7Li abundance significantly when the quark mass variation is negative. Based upon up-to-date observational limits on primordial abundances of D, 4He and Li, the quark mass variation in the BBN epoch are derived. In a model in which the resonance energies of the reactions 3He(d, p)4He and 3H(d, n)4He are insensitive to the quark mass, we find that the Li abundance can be consistent with observations for delta m_q/m_q = (4--8) times 10^{-3}.

## Key claim

The paper finds that a resonance at 0.33 MeV in the 7Be(n,p)7Li reaction can significantly lower the primordial 7Li abundance when the quark mass variation is negative, but only under specific assumptions about how resonance energies shift. The lithium abundance can be made consistent with observations for a quark mass variation of δm_q/m_q = (4–8)×10^{-3} only in a model where the resonance energies of the 3He(d,p)4He and 3H(d,n)4He reactions are insensitive to the quark mass (Case C).

## Method

The authors calculated the effect of a time-dependent quark mass on the cross sections of the 7Be(n,p)7Li reaction by modeling the energy shifts of three 8Be resonances (at E_r = 2.67 keV, 0.33 MeV, and 2.66 MeV) using sensitivity coefficients K_A for nuclear binding energies from Flambaum (2008). They performed BBN calculations with updated reaction rates and the Planck 2015 baryon-to-photon ratio, considering six combinations of three cases (A, B, C) for how resonance energies shift in the 7Be(n,p)7Li reaction and the 3He(d,p)4He/3H(d,n)4He reactions separately. Likelihood functions were constructed from observational constraints on primordial D, 4He, and Li abundances.

## Key numbers

- The resonance at E_r = 0.33 MeV (excitation energy 19.07 MeV in 8Be) has partial widths Γ_i = 0.0767 MeV and Γ_f = 0.088 MeV
- The near-threshold resonance at E_r = 2.67 keV (excitation energy 18.91 MeV) has partial widths Γ_i = 0.225 MeV and Γ_f = 1.41 MeV
- In Case A, the resonance energy shift is δE_r^(n,p) = 17.2 × (δm_q/m_q) MeV
- In Case B, the resonance energy shift is δE_r^(n,p) = -0.172 × (δm_q/m_q) MeV
- The observed 7Li abundance from metal-poor stars is log(Li/H)+12 = 2.199 ± 0.086 (Sbordone 2010)
- The primordial D abundance is D/H = (2.527 ± 0.030)×10^{-5} (Cooke 2018)
- The 4He abundance is Y_p = 0.2449 ± 0.0040 (Aver 2015)
- The baryon-to-photon ratio η = (6.108 ± 0.060)×10^{-10} from Planck 2015
- The 95% C.L. for δm_q/m_q in Case C (including Li constraint) is (3.7–8.0)×10^{-3}
- The 95% C.L. for δm_q/m_q in Case A (including Li constraint) is (0.43–3.27)×10^{-3}
- The 95% C.L. for δm_q/m_q in Case B (including Li constraint) is (0.68–3.70)×10^{-3}

## Context

This work extends previous studies of BBN with time-dependent quark mass by Berengut (2010), Cheoun (2011), and Bedaque (2011), which treated resonances in 3He(d,p)4He and 3H(d,n)4He but ignored the 7Be(n,p)7Li resonances. The authors show that the 0.33 MeV resonance in 7Be(n,p)7Li, previously neglected because it lies above the BBN energy range in the standard case, can shift downward and enhance 7Be destruction when the quark mass is heavier. This provides a possible solution to the "lithium problem", the discrepancy between the standard BBN prediction and the observed Spite plateau abundance, under specific assumptions about how resonance energies respond to quark mass variation. The results enable tighter constraints on time-varying fundamental constants from combined D, 4He, and Li observations.
