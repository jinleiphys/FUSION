# Nucleon Compton scattering from the Dyson-Schwinger perspective

> We summarize results from our recent paper* on nucleon Compton scattering in the Dyson-Schwinger approach. We study the Compton scattering phase space and its various kinematic limits, and we introduce a covariant, transverse tensor basis for the scattering amplitude that is free of kinematic singularities. We illustrate the decomposition of the Compton amplitude at the quark-gluon level and calculate its nonperturbative handbag part, which also includes all t-channel meson resonances. The calculation requires numerical solutions for the dressed quark propagator, the nucleon's Faddeev amplitude and the quark Compton vertex as its input; they are consistently obtained from Dyson-Schwinger, Bethe-Salpeter and Faddeev equations. As a first application, we verify that the result for the Compton scattering amplitude reproduces the pi-gamma-gamma transition form factor when the pion pole in the t channel is approached. *G. Eichmann and C. S. Fischer, arXiv:1212.1761 [hep-ph].

## Key claim

The authors construct a covariant, transverse tensor basis for the nucleon Compton scattering amplitude that is free of kinematic singularities, and they calculate its nonperturbative handbag part using Dyson-Schwinger, Bethe-Salpeter, and Faddeev equations; however, a complete dynamical calculation of the full Compton amplitude (including the structure part beyond the handbag) is not yet achieved, so the extraction of gauge-invariant polarizabilities requires further assumptions.

## Method

The authors decompose the nucleon Compton amplitude into a Born contribution (containing nucleon poles in the s- and u-channels) and a one-particle irreducible (1PI) residual structure part. They introduce a 18-dimensional tensor basis built from transverse projectors \(t_{ab}^{\mu\nu}\) and \(\varepsilon_{ab}^{\mu\nu}\) that is regular in all kinematic limits (real, virtual, doubly-virtual Compton scattering). At the quark-gluon level, they calculate the handbag contributions by solving an inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for the quark Compton vertex, which includes Born, Ball-Chiu (Ward-Takahashi identity preserving), and transverse parts, using dressed quark propagators, nucleon Faddeev amplitudes, and quark-photon vertices obtained consistently from Dyson-Schwinger, Bethe-Salpeter, and Faddeev equations in a rainbow-ladder truncation.

## Key numbers

* The tensor basis consists of 18 independent structures for the general Compton amplitude, reducing to 12 for virtual Compton scattering (VCS), 6 for real Compton scattering (RCS), and 4 for forward doubly-virtual Compton scattering (VVCS).
* The Mandelstam variable \(t\) is defined as \(\Delta^2/(4M^2)\), with the nucleon mass \(M\); the photon virtualities are \(\tau = Q^2/(4M^2)\) and \(\tau' = {Q'}^2/(4M^2)\), and the crossing variable is \(\nu = -\Sigma\cdot P/M^2\).
* The spacelike domain for two-photon exchange integrals is \(t, X > 0\) and \(Y, Z \in (-1,1)\), where \(X = \Sigma^2/(M^2 t)\), \(Y = \widehat{P}\cdot\widehat{\Sigma_T}\), and \(Z = \widehat{\Sigma}\cdot\widehat{\Delta}\).
* Real Compton scattering corresponds to \(X = -1\) and \(Z = 0\), with the physical s-channel region defined by \(t \geq 0\) and \(Y \geq 1\).
* The generalized polarizabilities are defined in the limit \(X = Z = 1\) and \(Y = 0\); forward VVCS corresponds to \(t = 0\) and \(Z = 0\).
* The \(\pi^0\gamma\gamma\) transition form factor is reproduced at the pion pole \(t \rightarrow -m_\pi^2/(4M^2)\) from both the quark Compton vertex and the nucleon Compton amplitude.

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger approach to nucleon Compton scattering, building on prior calculations of nucleon and \(\Delta\) elastic and transition form factors (Chang 2011, Eichmann 2011). The tensor basis construction follows earlier work on kinematic singularity-free bases for Compton scattering (Tarrach 1975, Drechsel 1997, Gorchtein 2009). The calculation of the handbag part via the quark Compton vertex, including the Ball-Chiu vertex generalization to two photons (Ball 1980), enables future extraction of nucleon polarizabilities under handbag dominance assumptions and investigation of two-photon exchange contributions to electromagnetic form factors at spacelike momenta.
