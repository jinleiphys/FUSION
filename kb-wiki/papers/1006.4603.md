# The quark-gluon medium

> The properties of the quark-gluon medium observed in high energy nucleus-nucleus collisions are discussed. The main experimental facts about these collisions are briefly described and compared with data about proton-proton collisions. Both microscopic and macroscopic approaches to their description are reviewed. The chromodynamics of the quark-gluon medium at high energies is mainly considered. The energy loss of partons moving in this medium is treated. The principal conclusion is that the medium possesses some collective properties which are crucial for understanding the experimental observations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the quark-gluon medium created in high-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions possesses collective properties that are crucial for understanding experimental observations, but the authors hedge that the medium's properties are still in an actively developing stage and cannot be fully covered within a single review paper.

## Method

The authors review both microscopic and macroscopic QCD approaches to describing the quark-gluon medium produced in high-energy heavy ion collisions. Microscopically, they discuss the Color Glass Condensate, Glasma, and Quark-Gluon Plasma paradigms, along with parton energy loss mechanisms including bremsstrahlung and elastic scattering. Macroscopically, they treat the medium using in-medium QCD equations, chromopermittivity, classical polarization effects, instabilities, nonlinear effects, and hydrodynamics.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) for central Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV shows strong \(p_T\)-independent suppression of about 0.2 for hadrons with \(p_T > 4\) GeV, measured up to \(p_T = 20\) GeV
- The SPS value of \(R_{AA}\) for Pb-Pb at \(\sqrt{s} = 17.3\) GeV at \(p_T = 4\) GeV is much larger than at RHIC, exceeding 1 for \(p_T > 2\) GeV
- The away-side associated yield suppression \(I_{AA}\) is approximately 0.35 to 0.5, while \(R_{AA}\) is about 0.2
- The elliptic flow coefficient \(v_2\) reaches a maximum of about 0.2 at \(p_T = 3\) GeV, measured up to \(p_T = 12\) GeV
- The upper limit for bottom (\(\Upsilon\)) suppression is 0.64
- Direct photons show no suppression in the \(p_T\) region from 4 GeV to 15 GeV, while at \(p_T \sim 20\) GeV preliminary data show suppression with \(R_{AA} \approx 0.6\)
- The QCD scale \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD}\) is approximately 100 MeV

## Context

This review extends the line of work on the Color Glass Condensate paradigm developed by McLerran and Venugopalan (1994) and subsequent authors, and connects it to experimental findings from SPS and RHIC. The paper enables further theoretical and experimental studies of the quark-gluon medium at the LHC, particularly regarding jet quenching, collective flows, and the ridge phenomenon, while noting open questions such as the "RHIC HBT puzzle" where measured HBT radii contradict hydrodynamical predictions.
