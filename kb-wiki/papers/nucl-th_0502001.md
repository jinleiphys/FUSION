# Chiral symmetry and parametrization of scalar resonances

> The linear $\s$-model is used to study the effects of chiral symmetry in unitarized amplitudes incorporating scalar resonances. When just a single resonance is present, we show that the iteration of a chiral tree amplitude by means of regularized two-pion loops preserves the smallness of $\p\p$ interaction at low energies and estimate the importance of pion off-shell contributions. The inclusion of a second resonance is performed by means of a chiral extension of the linear $\s$-model lagrangian. The new $\p\p$ ampitude at tree level complies with low-energy theorems, depends on a mixing angle and has a zero for a given energy between the resonance masses. The unitarization of this amplitude by means of two-pion loops preserves both its chiral low energy behavior and the position of this zero confirming, in a lagrangian framework, conclusions drawn previously by Törnqvist. Finally, we approximate and generalize our results and give a friendly expression that can be used in the parametrization of $N$ coupled scalar resonances.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in the linear sigma model, chiral symmetry requires that the resonance and the non-resonating background must always be treated on the same footing to preserve the low-energy theorems for pion-pion scattering; the iteration of a chiral tree amplitude by means of regularized two-pion loops preserves the smallness of the interaction at low energies only when the full chiral amplitude (including both the four-pion contact term and the resonance exchange) is used as the building block.

## Method

The authors used the linear sigma model to construct tree-level amplitudes for pion-pion scattering including one or two scalar-isoscalar resonances. They unitarized these amplitudes by summing the geometric series of two-pion loop iterations (s-channel loops), regularizing divergences with counterterms, and imposing that the pole of the unitarized amplitude occurs at the physical resonance mass. For the two-resonance case, they extended the linear sigma model Lagrangian by adding a second chiral scalar field and a mixing term, then diagonalized the mass matrix via a mixing angle.

## Key numbers

* The tree-level amplitude for a single resonance is given by \(A_t(x) = (x - m^2)/f_\pi^2 \cdot [1 - (x - m^2)/(x - M_s^2)]\), where \(m\) is the pion mass, \(f_\pi\) is the pion decay constant, and \(M_s\) is the sigma mass.
* The unitarized width for a single resonance is \(\Gamma_A(s) = 3 (s - m^2) (M_s^2 - m^2) / (32\pi f_\pi^2) \cdot \sqrt{s-4m^2} / (M_s \sqrt{s}) \cdot \Theta(s-4m^2)\).
* For the two-resonance case with masses \(M_a = 4m\) and \(M_b = 8m\), the zero in the amplitude between the resonance masses occurs at \(s_1\) given by \(s_1 = (M_a^2 M_b^2 - m^2 (M_b^2 \cos^2\theta + M_a^2 \sin^2\theta)) / (M_a^2 \cos^2\theta + M_b^2 \sin^2\theta - m^2)\).
* The mixing angle \(\theta\) is related to the masses by \(\tan 2\theta = 4 g f_\pi / (M_\xi^2 - M_s^2)\), where \(g\) is the coupling constant between the new field and the sigma-pion system.

## Context

This work extends the unitarized quark model approach of Tornqvist (1995) by providing a Lagrangian framework that preserves chiral symmetry explicitly. The authors confirm Tornqvist's theorem that a zero in the partial wave amplitude in the physical region remains a zero after unitarization. The results enable parametrizations of multiple coupled scalar resonances in data analyses, particularly relevant for interpreting recent experimental evidence for low-mass, broad scalar resonances from Fermilab E791, CLEO, Belle, BaBar, KLOE, and BES collaborations.
