# Virtual versus Real Nuclear Compton Scattering in the Delta(1232) Region

> In this paper we calculate the cross section for Virtual Compton Scattering off nuclei in the delta resonance region. We also calculate the background for the process from Coherent Bremsstrahlung in nuclei and explore the regions where the Virtual Compton Scattering cross section dominates. The study also shows that it is possible to extract the cross section for Real Compton Scattering from the Virtual Compton one in a wide range of scattering angles.

# Virtual versus Real Nuclear Compton Scattering in the Delta(1232) Region

## Key claim
The cross section for Virtual Compton Scattering (VCS) off nuclei in the delta resonance region can be used to extract the Real Compton Scattering (RCS) cross section, provided the measurement is performed at small electron energies and small electron angles, where the intermediate virtual photon is almost real. However, the forward-angle region (photon angles below 30 degrees) is inaccessible due to Coherent Bremsstrahlung background, though this limitation is not severe because forward RCS information already exists from total photonuclear cross section data via the optical theorem.

## Method
The authors calculated the differential cross section for coherent VCS on spin-saturated nuclei in the delta resonance region, using a hadronic current dominated by the Delta(1232) contribution with a two-parameter Fermi nuclear density, Pauli-blocking corrected width, and Delta self-energy including quasielastic, two-body, and three-body absorption contributions. They also computed the Coherent Bremsstrahlung background cross section in the one-photon exchange approximation and the RCS cross section, then compared all three for carbon-12 and calcium-40 at electron energies of 500, 800, and 2000 MeV and electron scattering angles of 2, 5, 10, and 15 degrees, with the intermediate photon energy fixed at 340 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Intermediate photon energy fixed at 340 MeV (delta resonance peak) throughout the study
- For E_e = 500 MeV and small electron angles (2-5 deg), VCS dominates over Coherent Bremsstrahlung for photon angles larger than 25-30 degrees
- For E_e = 500 MeV and larger electron angles (10-15 deg), VCS signal appears above background only for photon angles above 40-60 degrees
- For E_e = 2000 MeV and electron angle 2 deg, VCS is visible above background for photon angles above 25 degrees; at 15 deg electron angle, photon angles above 120 degrees are needed
- At E_e = 500 MeV, VCS and RCS cross sections are essentially equal for electron angles below 15 degrees
- At E_e = 800 MeV, agreement between VCS and RCS remains good for electron angles of 2-5 degrees but not for 10-15 degrees
- At E_e = 2000 MeV, agreement between VCS and RCS only holds at electron angle 2 degrees

## Context
This work extends the only prior theoretical study of nuclear VCS by PAV (no year given in text) and builds on the Delta-dominated hadronic current formalism from PAR, CAR, and RAF (no years given). The study enables a practical alternative to traditional two-target RCS experiments, which are of order Z^2 alpha^5 A^2, compared to the one-step VCS process of order alpha (A alpha)^2. The method is particularly valuable because existing RCS data show persistent discrepancies between theory and experiment at non-forward angles, as noted by PAR, KOC, and CAR (no years given), while forward-angle RCS can already be obtained via the optical theorem from total photonuclear cross section data (AHREN, CARLOS, no years given) and dispersion relations (WEISE, no year given).
