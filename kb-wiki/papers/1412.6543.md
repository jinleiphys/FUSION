# Observation of the chiral magnetic effect in ZrTe5

> The chiral magnetic effect is the generation of electric current induced by chirality imbalance in the presence of magnetic field. It is a macroscopic manifestation of the quantum anomaly in relativistic field theory of chiral fermions (massless spin $1/2$ particles with a definite projection of spin on momentum) -- a dramatic phenomenon arising from a collective motion of particles and antiparticles in the Dirac sea. The recent discovery of Dirac semimetals with chiral quasi-particles opens a fascinating possibility to study this phenomenon in condensed matter experiments. Here we report on the first observation of chiral magnetic effect through the measurement of magneto-transport in zirconium pentatelluride, ZrTe_5. Our angle-resolved photoemission spectroscopy experiments show that this material's electronic structure is consistent with a 3D Dirac semimetal. We observe a large negative magnetoresistance when magnetic field is parallel with the current. The measured quadratic field dependence of the magnetoconductance is a clear indication of the chiral magnetic effect. The observed phenomenon stems from the effective transmutation of Dirac semimetal into a Weyl semimetal induced by the parallel electric and magnetic fields that represent a topologically nontrivial gauge field background.

## Key claim

The authors report the first observation of the chiral magnetic effect (CME) in a condensed matter system, specifically in the Dirac semimetal ZrTe5, through the measurement of a large negative longitudinal magnetoresistance that exhibits a quadratic dependence on magnetic field. The observation is conditional on the material having a 3D Dirac semimetal electronic structure and on the magnetic field being aligned parallel to the electric current (the Lorentz force free configuration).

## Method

The authors performed magneto-transport measurements on single crystals of ZrTe5, measuring resistivity as a function of magnetic field at various temperatures and angles between the field and current. They also conducted angle-resolved photoemission spectroscopy (ARPES) experiments to characterize the electronic structure and confirm the 3D Dirac semimetal nature of the material. The longitudinal magnetoconductance was fitted to the CME prediction σ_CME = σ_0 + a(T)B², where the quadratic term in magnetic field is the signature of the chiral magnetic effect.

## Key numbers

- The CME conductivity at 4 Tesla is approximately equal to the zero-field conductivity at T = 20 K.
- At 9 Tesla, the CME contribution increases by approximately 400% relative to zero-field conductivity.
- The zero-field resistivity of ZrTe5 is ρ_Ohm ≈ 1.2 mΩ cm at T = 20 K.
- The CME and Ohmic resistivities become equal at B ≈ 3 T.
- The in-plane Fermi velocity along the chain direction is v_a ≈ 6.4 eV·Å (≈ c/300), and perpendicular to the chain direction is v_c ≈ 4.5 eV·Å.
- The quantum scattering rate determined from ARPES quasiparticle broadening is Γ_Q = ħ/τ_Q ≈ 100 meV.
- The bi-layer splitting between the two Dirac cones is approximately 300 meV.
- The effective mass in the chain direction is m_a* ≈ 0.03 m_e, comparable to that in Cd3As2.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical predictions of the chiral magnetic effect in Dirac semimetals by Son and Spivak (2013) and Burkov (2014) to experimental realization in a condensed matter system. The observation in ZrTe5 follows the recent discovery of 3D Dirac semimetals Cd3As2 by Borisenko (2014) and Neupane (2014) and Na3Bi by Liu (2014), and enables future studies of the chiral anomaly and quantum field theory phenomena in tabletop experiments. The results also have implications for understanding the chiral magnetic effect in relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where similar signatures have been observed by the STAR Collaboration (2009) and ALICE Collaboration (2013).
