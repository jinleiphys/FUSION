# Axial anomaly and the three-flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model with confinement: Constructing the QCD phase diagram

> We investigate the phase structure of massless three-flavor QCD by extending the Nambu-Jona- Lasinio model to include the effects of confinement and the axial anomaly. We study the interplay between the chiral and diquark condensates induced by the axial anomaly, as well as their relationship with the Polyakov loop, which parameterizes confinement. By minimizing the thermodynamic potential we construct the QCD phase diagram and investigate the possibility of realizing a recently discovered low temperature critical point and an associated BEC-BCS crossover. We also perform a Ginzburg-Landau expansion of the thermodynamic potential, comparing our results to a prior analysis based purely on symmetry considerations, in order to assess the lowest-order effects of the condensate-confinement couplings.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the axial anomaly, mediated by the instanton-induced Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft (KMT) six-quark interaction, can produce a low-temperature critical point and an associated BEC-BCS crossover in the QCD phase diagram, but only when the ratio of anomaly couplings κ = K′/K lies in the range 4.2 ≤ κ ≤ 4.7; the inclusion of confinement via the Polyakov loop does not qualitatively alter this condition.

## Method

The authors extended the three-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model to include the Polyakov loop (forming the PNJL model) and the instanton-induced six-quark interactions that mediate the axial anomaly. They derived the thermodynamic potential in mean-field theory, including chiral and diquark condensates, and minimized it with respect to σ, d, and the Polyakov loop variable φ₃ at each point in the (μ, T) plane to construct the phase diagram. They also performed a Ginzburg-Landau expansion of the thermodynamic potential to compute the lowest-order coupling coefficients between the condensates and the Polyakov loop.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature at μ = 0 in the NJL model: T_c^(NJL) = 153 MeV, in agreement with Abuki et al. and within margin of error of lattice results.
- Low-temperature critical point in the NJL model (no confinement) at (μ, T) = (291 MeV, 35 MeV) for κ = 4.2.
- Low-temperature critical point in the PNJL model (with confinement) at (μ, T) = (288 MeV, 36 MeV) for κ = 4.2.
- The critical point exists only for κ between 4.2 and 4.7; for κ ≥ 4.8 it vanishes into the NG-NOR phase boundary.
- The parameter T₀ in the Polyakov loop potential was set to 50 MeV to match the deconfinement transition at T_c = 154 MeV for massless three-flavor QCD.
- Effective quark mass at μ = T = 0: M = 355.1 MeV, identical with or without confinement.
- Dimensionless chiral condensate maximum: σ₀ = 0.0636; diquark condensate maximum: d₀ = 0.0548.
- Coupling constants: GΛ² = 1.926, HΛ² = 1.74, KΛ⁵ = 12.36.

## Context

This work extends prior studies by Abuki et al. (2010) on the three-flavor NJL model with the axial anomaly, and by Hatsuda et al. (2006) and Yamamoto et al. (2007) who demonstrated the importance of the anomaly-induced attraction between chiral and diquark condensates. The authors also compare their Ginzburg-Landau expansion to the symmetry-based analysis of Hatsuda et al. (2006). The results enable future investigations of how confinement modifies the low-temperature critical point and BEC-BCS crossover in dense QCD matter, and provide explicit GL coefficients that can be used to assess condensate-confinement couplings beyond the lowest order.
