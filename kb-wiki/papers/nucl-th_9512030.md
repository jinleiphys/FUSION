# Variational calculation of antiprotonic helium atoms

> A few per cent fraction of antiprotons stopped in helium survives for an enormous time (up to tens of microseconds) in comparison with the usual lifetime ($10^{-12}s$) of these particles in matter. The explanation is that antiprotons are captured in the metastable antiprotonic helium atoms $^{3,4}\! H\! e\bar pe$ carrying an extremely large total angular momentum $L\sim 30-40$. Initial populations, level lifetimes and very precise values of the transition energies were obtained in experiments on the resonant laser--induced annihilation. Analogous long-lived systems were observed in experiments with negative kaons and pions. The purpose of this report is to present the results of calculation of eigenenergies, radiative transition rates, energy-level splitting due to relativistic interactions and Auger decay rates of antiprotonic helium atoms within the variational approach.

## Key claim

The variational method can accurately calculate eigenenergies, radiative transition rates, relativistic energy-level splittings, and Auger decay rates for antiprotonic helium atoms, but the calculated wavelengths agree with experimental measurements only to within about 5 × 10⁻⁴, and more elaborate trial functions are needed for higher accuracy.

## Method

The authors constructed an approximate Hamiltonian by projecting the full three-body Hamiltonian onto a closed-channel subspace defined by restricting the relative angular momenta of the heavy-particle pair and the electron. They used up to 1000 variational trial functions composed of bispherical harmonics with exponential radial factors, with nonlinear parameters optimized to minimize energies. Radiative transition rates were computed from the variational wave functions using dipole matrix elements, relativistic level splittings were obtained via first-order perturbation theory using the Breit interaction, and Auger decay rates were calculated using the Feshbach orthogonal projection method with continuum wave functions constructed in Jacobi coordinates.

## Key numbers

- Calculated energies for the lowest five levels of ⁴He p̄e and ³He p̄e for total angular momenta L = 31–37 are tabulated in atomic units; for example, the (L,N) = (35,1) state of ⁴He p̄e has energy −2.9836255 a.u.
- Radiative transition rates for dipole-allowed transitions range from about 0.1 × 10⁵ s⁻¹ to 8.5 × 10⁵ s⁻¹; for example, the (37,1)→(36,1) transition in ⁴He p̄e has rate 6.733 × 10⁵ s⁻¹.
- Experimental decay rates for the (35,4) and (34,3) states of ⁴He p̄e are 0.72 ± 0.02 × 10⁶ s⁻¹ and 1.18 ± 0.04 × 10⁶ s⁻¹, respectively; the present calculation gives 0.597 × 10⁶ s⁻¹ and 0.713 × 10⁶ s⁻¹.
- Relativistic energy-level splittings ΔE_LN range from about −0.81 × 10⁻⁶ a.u. to −1.20 × 10⁻⁶ a.u. for the states studied.
- Spectral line splittings Δν for unfavoured transitions are of order 1 GHz; for example, the (34,1)→(33,3) transition in ⁴He p̄e has Δν = −0.86 GHz.

## Context

This work extends earlier variational calculations by Russell and Born-Oppenheimer approximations by Ahlrichs to provide a unified variational treatment of antiprotonic helium atoms. The calculations support experimental measurements of transition wavelengths by Morita (1994), Maas (1995), and Widmann (1995) using laser-induced resonant annihilation, and the computed radiative rates are compared with those of Shimamura (1992) and Morita (1994). The results enable precise determination of antiproton properties and testing of fundamental symmetries, as noted by Eades (1995), and the predicted level splittings are within the reach of planned experiments with 1 GHz resolution.
