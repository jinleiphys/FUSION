# $α$-clustering effect on flows of direct photons in heavy-ion collisions

> In this work, we reconstruct the $γ$-photon energy spectrum which is in good agreement with the experimental data of $^{86}$Kr + $^{12}$C at $E/A$ = 44 MeV in the framework of our modified EQMD model. The directed flow and elliptic flow of free protons and direct photons have been investigated by taking $α$-clustering structure of $^{12}$C into account. Comparing with the free proton, the direct photon flows give a clearer information about early stage of nuclear reaction. Difference of collective flows between different configurations of $^{12}$C is observed in this work. This indicates that collective flows of direct photons are sensitive to the initial configuration, therefore the $γ$ bremsstrahlung process might be taken as an alternative probe to investigate $α$-clustering structure in light nucleus from heavy ion collisions at Fermi-energy region.

## Key claim

The directed and elliptic flows of direct photons are sensitive to the initial α-clustering configuration (triangle vs. sphere) of ¹²C in heavy-ion collisions at Fermi energies, but this sensitivity is only clearly observed for photons produced during the first compression stage, before the system reaches thermal equilibrium.

## Method

The authors used the extended quantum molecular dynamics (EQMD) model with dynamical wave packets to simulate ⁸⁶Kr + ¹²C collisions at E/A = 44 MeV. They initialized ¹²C in either a triangle (3α-cluster) or sphere configuration and implemented a bremsstrahlung photon production mechanism from first-chance proton-neutron collisions, with a modified sampling procedure that accounts for the dynamical wave-packet width and zero-point kinetic energy. Anisotropic flow coefficients v₁ and v₂ were computed for direct photons and free protons as functions of rapidity and transverse momentum.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per nucleon for triangle ¹²C: −7.721 MeV; for sphere ¹²C: −6.982 MeV; experimental value: −7.68 MeV
- Root-mean-square radius for triangle ¹²C: 2.313 fm; for sphere ¹²C: 2.333 fm; experimental value: 2.46 fm
- Impact parameter used for flow analysis: b = 5.0 fm
- Photon energy cuts for angular distributions: Eγ = 20 ± 3, 50 ± 3, and 80 ± 3 MeV
- Wave-packet width parameter: about 2 fm² for triangle configuration, about 3.6 fm² for sphere configuration
- The directed flow slope parameter F ≡ dv₁/dy|y=0 is positive for direct photons; the triangle configuration yields a slightly larger amplitude than the sphere configuration
- For free protons, elliptic flow v₂ increases rapidly with transverse momentum below pt ≈ 150 MeV/c; for direct photons, v₂ shows much weaker transverse momentum dependence and saturates quickly

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of α-clustering effects using collective flows of hadrons at relativistic energies (PRL1, ZhangSong 2017) to the Fermi-energy region, where the flow formation mechanism differs due to competing mean-field attraction and two-body collisions. It also builds on the authors' previous work (Shi 2020) showing that the standard QMD treatment underestimates photon yields in the EQMD model. The anti-correlation between direct photon and free proton flows was previously observed in symmetric systems (Liu 2008, Ma 2012, Wang 2014). The results suggest that direct photon bremsstrahlung can serve as a probe of α-clustering structure in light nuclei, complementing studies using giant dipole resonance (He 2014), photon disintegration (Huang 2010), HBT correlations (He 2015), and hadronic flows.
