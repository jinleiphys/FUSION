# Moments of parton distribution functions of any order from lattice QCD

> We describe a procedure to determine moments of parton distribution functions of any order in lattice quantum chromodynamics (QCD). The procedure is based on the gradient flow for fermion and gauge fields. The flowed matrix elements of twist-2 operators renormalize multiplicatively, and the matching with the physical matrix elements can be obtained using continuum symmetries and the irreducible representations of Euclidean 4-dimensional rotations. We calculate the matching coefficients at one-loop in perturbation theory for moments of any order in the flavor nonsinglet case. We also give specific examples of operators that could be used in lattice QCD computations. It turns out that it is possible to choose operators with identical Lorentz indices and still have a multiplicative matching. One can thus use twist-2 operators exclusively with temporal indices, thus substantially improving the signal-to-noise ratio in the computation of the hadronic matrix elements.

## Key claim
The paper describes a procedure to determine moments of parton distribution functions (PDFs) of any order from lattice QCD using the gradient flow, where the matching coefficients between flowed and physical matrix elements are calculated at one-loop in perturbation theory for the flavor nonsinglet case. The key condition is that the matching is multiplicative only when operators are symmetrized on Lorentz indices and all traces have been subtracted, and the method avoids the need for renormalization of flowed fields by using ratios of moments for n>2.

## Method
The procedure uses gradient flow for fermion and gauge fields to define flowed matrix elements of twist-2 operators, which renormalize multiplicatively. The matching with physical matrix elements in the MS scheme is obtained using continuum symmetries and irreducible representations of Euclidean 4-dimensional rotations, with matching coefficients calculated at one-loop in perturbation theory. For moments beyond the second (n>2), the method uses ratios of hadronic matrix elements R_n^h(t) that have a finite continuum limit and exhibit O(a^2) scaling violations after nonperturbative improvement, eliminating the need to renormalize the flowed fields.

## Key numbers
- The matching coefficients c_n(t,mu) are calculated at one-loop in perturbation theory for moments of any order in the flavor nonsinglet case
- The ratios R_n^h(t) for n>2 exhibit O(a^2) scaling violations once the lattice theory has been nonperturbatively improved
- The second moment A_2^{h,MS}(mu) = <x>_MS^h(mu) can be used as an observable input to compute all other moments for n>2 via the ratio formula <x^{n-1}>_MS^h(mu) = <x^{n-2}>_MS^h(mu) * [c_{n-1}(t,mu)/c_n(t,mu)] * R_n^h(t)

## Context
This work extends the use of gradient flow from quasi-PDF calculations (Monahan 2016, Monahan 2017) and the locally smeared operator product expansion in scalar field theory (Monahan 2015) to the direct determination of PDF moments of any order. The method enables precise reconstruction of PDFs from lattice QCD and can potentially be combined with quasi-PDF calculations (HadStruc Collaboration 2022). The approach can also be generalized to study distribution amplitudes, though care is needed due to operators with vanishing contributions for forward matrix elements (Bali 2017, RQCD 2019).
