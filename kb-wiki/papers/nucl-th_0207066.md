# Vector meson production and nucleon resonance analysis in a coupled-channel approach for energies m_N < sqrt(s) < 2 GeV I: pion-induced results and hadronic parameters

> We present a nucleon resonance analysis by simultaneously considering all pion- and photon-induced experimental data on the final states gamma N, pi N, 2 pi N, eta N, K Lambda, K Sigma, and omega N for energies from the nucleon mass up to sqrt(s) = 2 GeV. In this analysis we find strong evidence for the resonances P_{31}(1750), P_{13}(1900), P_{33}(1920), and D_{13}(1950). The omega N production mechanism is dominated by large P_{11}(1710) and P_{13}(1900) contributions. In this first part, we present the results of the pion-induced reactions and the extracted resonance and background properties with emphasis on the difference between global and purely hadronic fits.

## Key claim

The authors present strong evidence for four nucleon resonances (P31(1750), P13(1900), P33(1920), and D13(1950)) by simultaneously fitting pion- and photon-induced data across multiple final states, but the claim is hedged by the fact that the K-matrix Born approximation used cannot distinguish between genuine three-quark excitations and dynamically generated scattering resonances.

## Method

The authors employed a unitary coupled-channel effective Lagrangian model (the Giessen model) solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the K-matrix Born approximation, incorporating the final states gamma N, pi N, 2pi N, eta N, K Lambda, K Sigma, and omega N for center-of-mass energies from the nucleon mass up to 2 GeV. The interaction potential included s-channel, u-channel, and t-channel contributions generated from effective Lagrangians, with background contributions dynamically generated from Born terms and t-channel exchanges. The model parameters were determined by chi-squared minimization against over 6800 data points for global fits and about 2400 for purely hadronic fits, binned into 96 energy intervals.

## Key numbers

- Energy range: from nucleon mass (m_N = 0.939 GeV) up to sqrt(s) = 2 GeV
- Over 6800 data points included in global fits; about 2400 in purely hadronic fits
- Data binned into 96 energy intervals with up to 10-15 data points per energy bin for each angle differential observable
- Sigma meson mass fixed at 650 MeV (chosen from range 450-750 MeV)
- Pion decay constant f_pi = 93 MeV used in KSRF relation
- Rho meson mass: 0.769 GeV; omega meson mass: 0.783 GeV
- Zeta meson (effective 2pi N channel) mass: m_zeta = 2 m_pi = 0.276 GeV
- Four resonances found with strong evidence: P31(1750), P13(1900), P33(1920), D13(1950)
- Omega N production dominated by P11(1710) and P13(1900) contributions

## Context

This work extends the earlier Giessen model of Feuster and Mosel (1998, 1999) by incorporating the omega N and K Sigma final states, and by extending the energy range up to 2 GeV to investigate higher and missing nucleon resonances. The only other coupled-channel calculation considering the omega N channel was by Lutz et al., which used pointlike interactions and was restricted to S-wave dominance near threshold. This analysis differs from prior work by Vrana et al. and Manley et al. by including a larger channel space and simultaneously fitting both pion- and photon-induced data, enabling more reliable extraction of resonance properties and enabling future comparisons with lattice QCD predictions and quark model calculations.
