# Spatial distributions in static heavy-light mesons: a comparison of quark models with lattice QCD

> Lattice measurements of spatial distributions of the light quark bilinear densities in static mesons allow to test directly and in detail the wave functions of quark models. These distributions are gauge invariant quantities directly related to the spatial distribution of wave functions. We make a detailed comparison of the recent lattice QCD results with our own quark models, formulated previously for quite different purposes. We find a striking agreement not only between our two quark models, but also with the lattice QCD data for the ground state in an important range of distances up to about 4/GeV. Moreover the agreement extends to the L=1 states [j^P=(1/2)^+]. An explanation of several particular features completely at odds with the non-relativistic approximation is provided. A rather direct, somewhat unexpected and of course approximate relation between wave functions of certain quark models and QCD has been established.

## Key claim

The authors find a striking agreement between their two relativistic quark models (a Dirac-type model and a Bakamjian-Thomas approach) and lattice QCD data for spatial distributions of light quark bilinear densities in static heavy-light mesons, but only for distances up to about 4 GeV\(^{-1}\) and for the ground state \([j^P=(1/2)^-]\) and the first orbital excitation \([j^P=(1/2)^+]\). The agreement is approximate and establishes a direct but unexpected relation between quark model wave functions and QCD, though the scalar density relation between models and QCD is complicated by renormalization scheme dependence.

## Method

The authors computed vector, axial, and scalar density distributions for static heavy-light mesons using two relativistic quark models: a Dirac equation model with a scalar linear confining potential plus a softened Coulomb-like term, and a Bakamjian-Thomas approach based on the Godfrey-Isgur Hamiltonian. They compared these distributions to lattice QCD results from a companion paper, using parameters fixed solely from hadron mass spectroscopy (not from the distributions themselves), and evaluated the agreement for both the ground state doublet \([j^P=(1/2)^-]\) and the excited doublet \([j^P=(1/2)^+]\).

## Key numbers

- The agreement between quark models and lattice QCD holds for distances \(r \lesssim 4\) GeV\(^{-1}\) for the ground state.
- The excitation energy between \(L=1\) and \(L=0\) states in the static limit is \(\Delta E_1 \simeq 0.4\) GeV, used to constrain model parameters.
- In the Dirac model, the string tension is set to \(a = 0.25\) GeV\(^2\) and the Coulomb strength to \(\kappa = 0.75\), with a Gaussian cutoff radius \(R \simeq 0.25\) GeV\(^{-1}\).
- The constant term in the Dirac model is \(c_0 = -0.1\) GeV for strange and \(c_0 = -0.35\) GeV for nonstrange light quarks.
- The radial excitation energy in the Dirac model is \(\Delta E' = 0.576\) GeV for strange and \(0.556\) GeV for nonstrange states.
- The spin-orbit splitting in the Dirac model is \(-0.01\) GeV for strange and \(0.01\) GeV for nonstrange states.
- The mass difference between strange and nonstrange ground states is \(0.1\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier comparisons between quark models and lattice QCD by Duncan et al. (1995) and Michael et al. (1999), which studied Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes in Coulomb gauge or with Wilson lines, but suffered from gauge dependence or indirect relation to wave functions. The authors build on the approach of the UKQCD-Helsinki group (Koponen et al., 2009), who computed charge and matter densities for static mesons but used nonstandard confining potentials and did not reproduce the ground state charge distribution. The present results enable future studies of transition matrix elements between different doublets and radially excited states, and provide a benchmark for testing relativistic effects in quark models against lattice QCD.
