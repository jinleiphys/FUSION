# Explicit treatment of tensor force with a method of Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics

> In order to treat tensor force explicitly, we propose a microscopic model for nuclear structure based on antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD). As a result of the present study, it is found that some extentions of the AMD method are effective to incorporate the tensor correlation into wave functions. Calculating the deuteron, triton and $^4$He with the extended version of AMD, we obtained solutions that have large contribution of the tensor force. By analyzing the wave funtion of $^4$He, it is found that its single-particle orbits are composed of normal-size $0s$ orbits and shrunk $p$ orbits so as to gain the tensor force.

## Key claim

The authors propose an extended version of Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (AMD) that can explicitly incorporate tensor correlations into nuclear wave functions, but the quantitative reproduction of binding energies and radii for the deuteron and ⁴He requires a phenomenological doubling of the tensor force strength when using the Akaishi(T) potential, because the matrix element of the tensor force is reduced approximately by a factor of √(1/2) in simple mean-field approaches.

## Method

The authors extended the AMD framework by representing each single-nucleon wave function as a superposition of multiple Gaussian wave packets with independent spin and isospin variational parameters, allowing for parity-violating mean fields and charge mixing. They employed two effective interactions containing explicit tensor terms (the Furutani-Tamagaki potential and the Akaishi(T) potential derived from the Tamagaki potential by smoothing the repulsive core) and performed parity, angular-momentum, and charge-number projections on the total wave function. Energy variation was carried out using a frictional cooling method with constraints, and the resulting wave functions were analyzed for the deuteron, triton, and ⁴He.

## Key numbers

- For the deuteron calculated with the Furutani-Tamagaki potential and parity projection (N=4 wave packets with different spins): binding energy = 3.9 MeV, tensor force expectation value = −10.1 MeV, squared orbital angular momentum ⟨L²⟩ = 0.52.
- For the deuteron without parity projection: binding energy = 1.2 MeV, tensor force expectation value = −1.5 MeV, ⟨L²⟩ = 0.05.
- For ⁴He with the Furutani-Tamagaki potential and isospin-free wave functions: binding energy = 37.9 MeV, tensor force expectation value = −22.4 MeV, root-mean-square radius = 1.22 fm (experimental: B.E. = 28.3 MeV, R_rms = 1.47 fm).
- For ⁴He with the Furutani-Tamagaki potential and isospin-fixed wave functions: binding energy = 31.4 MeV, tensor force expectation value = −4.2 MeV, ⟨L²⟩ = 0.06.
- For ⁴He with the Akaishi(T) potential (twice-enhanced tensor force), the best solution (condition V') used 5 wave packets with different width parameters (0.30, 0.45, 0.67, 1.00, 1.50 fm⁻²) and angular-momentum projection and constraint.

## Context

This work extends the AMD method, previously applied successfully to describe various structures of light and middle nuclei (Kanada-En'yo 1995, Dote 1997, Kimura 2000, Taniguchi 2004), to explicitly treat the tensor force, which is usually renormalized into effective central and LS forces in standard model calculations. The approach is complementary to other contemporary efforts: Sugimoto et al. (2004) investigated tensor force contributions using relativistic and non-relativistic mean-field approaches, while Myo et al. (2004) studied tensor correlations in light unstable nuclei with an extended shell model. The results enable future systematic studies of tensor force effects on nuclear structure, including clustering phenomena and the properties of unstable nuclei such as ¹¹Be and ¹¹Li, where standard effective interactions have failed to reproduce experimental data without system-dependent adjustments.
