# Investigating the impact of spin effects at the high-energy neutrino-nucleon interactions while it crosses the Earth's core

> In this work, we investigate the impact of assuming the polarization of the Earth's outer core on the propagation of neutrinos that cross the all the Earth. We taking into account the spin-dependent structure functions to describe the polarized neutrino-nucleon cross-section, and also on the neutrino absorption while it crosses all of the Earth. We found that adding spin information and simultaneously assuming polarization of Earth's outer core impacts the probability of neutrino absorption in the energy range of 10 - 100 TeV and for upward neutrino direction. However, the magnitude of the effect is small and should be comparable with the magnitude of the errors associated with the IceCube neutrino data.

## Key claim

Adding spin information and assuming polarization of the Earth's outer core impacts the probability of neutrino absorption in the energy range of 10 - 100 TeV for upward neutrino direction, but the magnitude of the effect is small and should be comparable with the magnitude of the errors associated with the IceCube neutrino data.

## Method

The authors calculated the charged-current deep inelastic scattering cross section for muon and tau neutrinos and antineutrinos on isoscalar targets, including spin-dependent structure functions (g1, g4, g5) from the DSSV and KATAO parametrizations alongside unpolarized CTEQ18 parton distribution functions. They then computed the survival probability of neutrinos traversing the Earth using the PREM density profile, assuming partial (30% and 100%) polarization of the Earth's outer core located between 1221.5 km and 3480.0 km depth.

## Key numbers

- The effect of target polarization on the cross section is significant in the energy range 10^3 - 10^4 GeV, where unpolarized and polarized cross sections differ by multiplicative factors of 0.7 - 1.3 depending on the parametrization and whether the beam is neutrino or antineutrino.
- For 30% polarization of the outer core, the absorption changes between 0% and ±5% in the IceCube observation region where absorption is significant.
- For 100% polarization of the outer core, the percentage change in absorption rises to about ±18%.
- The effect is restricted to neutrinos with cos(θ_z) < -0.84, the condition for crossing the outer core, and is maximized at cos(θ_z) → -0.98.
- The normalized difference between unpolarized and polarized differential cross sections for neutrinos is maximized at approximately 80 GeV^2, while for antineutrinos the maximum goes beyond 200 GeV^2.

## Context

This work extends the formalism for spin-dependent neutrino-nucleon interactions developed in Pol (1999) and applies it to neutrino absorption by the Earth, motivated by the IceCube measurement of the muon neutrino cross section (IceCube Collaboration, 2017) which found the cross section about 1.3 times the standard model prediction in the 6.3 - 980 TeV range. The study uses the PREM model (Dziewonski and Anderson, 1981) for Earth's density profile and the spin-dependent structure function parametrizations from DSSV (de Florian et al., 2009) and KATAO (Kataev et al., 2001). The results suggest that distinguishing neutrino from antineutrino events would be necessary to observe the polarization effect, which could enable future estimates of Earth's outer core polarization.
