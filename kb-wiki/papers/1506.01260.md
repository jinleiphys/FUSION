# Cancellation of the sigma meson in thermal models

> The by now well-established scalar-isoscalar resonance $f_{0}(500)$ (the $σ$ meson) seems potentially relevant in the evaluation of thermodynamic quantities of a hadronic gas, since its mass is low. However, we recall that its contribution to isospin-averaged observables is, to a surprising accuracy, canceled by the repulsion from the pion-pion scalar-isotensor channel. As a result, in practice one should not incorporate $f_0(500)$ in standard hadronic resonance-gas models for studies of isospin averaged quantities. In our analysis we use the formalism of the virial expansion, which allows one to calculate the thermal properties of an interacting hadron gas in terms of derivatives of the scattering phase shifts, hence in a model-independent way directly from experimentally accessible quantities. A similar cancellation mechanism occurs for the scalar kaonic interactions between the $I=1/2$ channel (containing the alleged $K_{0}^{\ast}(800)$ or the $κ$ meson) and the $I=3/2$ channel.

## Key claim

The contribution of the $f_0(500)$ (sigma) meson to isospin-averaged thermodynamic observables in a hadron gas is nearly perfectly canceled by repulsion from the pion-pion scalar-isotensor channel, meaning that in practice one should not include $f_0(500)$ in standard hadronic resonance-gas models for studies of isospin-averaged quantities.

## Method

The authors used the virial expansion formalism, which incorporates two-body interactions into thermodynamics via derivatives of experimentally measured scattering phase shifts, rather than treating broad resonances as simple Breit-Wigner poles. They computed the second virial coefficient contributions from the isoscalar-scalar $(I=J=0)$ channel (containing $f_0(500)$) and the isotensor-scalar $(I=2,J=0)$ channel using parametrized pion-pion phase shifts from GarciaMartin (2011) and Nebreda (2011), then evaluated the combined effect on the trace of the energy-momentum tensor and on pion abundances in thermal models.

## Key numbers

- The pole position of $f_0(500)$ from the Particle Data Group lies in the range $M_\sigma = 400$ to $550$ MeV with half-width $\Gamma_\sigma/2 = 200$ to $350$ MeV.
- The lowest-order chiral perturbation theory scattering lengths are $a_{00} = (7/32\pi)(m_\pi/f_\pi^2)$ and $a_{20} = -(1/16\pi)(m_\pi/f_\pi^2)$, giving a relative strength of $-3.5$ between the isoscalar and isotensor channels.
- Including chiral corrections, the ratio of isoscalar to isotensor scattering lengths becomes $-4.95 \pm 0.16$ (Colangelo, 2000), compatible with $-5$ within uncertainties.
- With naive Breit-Wigner implementation of $f_0(500)$ using $M_\sigma = 484$ MeV and $\Gamma_\sigma/2 = 255$ MeV, the relative feeding to pion abundances would be up to 5%.
- The proper implementation of both scalar-isoscalar and scalar-isotensor channels yields a combined contribution to pion yields of only $-0.3\%$ at LHC conditions ($T = 156$ MeV).
- The cancellation holds for temperatures $T < 300$ MeV, bounded by the two-pion threshold.

## Context

This work extends the earlier observation by Venugopalan (1992) of cancellation between the isoscalar and isotensor channels, and the similar conclusions by Gomez Nicola (2012) regarding four-quark condensates and scalar susceptibilities. The results contradict the naive inclusion of $f_0(500)$ as a Breit-Wigner resonance in thermal models such as those by Torrieri (2004) and Andronic (2008), which found a 3.5% increase in pion yield from the sigma. The cancellation mechanism enables more accurate thermal model calculations of hadron yields and ratios, particularly affecting the kaon-to-pion ratio (potentially helping describe the horn structure at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.6$ GeV) and the proton-to-pion ratio at LHC (where the cancellation makes the existing proton-to-pion puzzle even stronger).
