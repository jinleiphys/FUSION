# Confinement, chiral symmetry breaking and the mass generation of hadrons

> A key question to QCD is what mechanism generates the hadron mass in the light quark sector, where both confinement and chiral symmetry breaking are in the game. Are confinement and chiral symmetry breaking in the vacuum uniquely interconnected? Can hadrons survive chiral symmetry restoration? If yes, what happens with their mass and what symmetries beyond the chiral symmetry are there? We review our recent insights. In particular, in a dynamical lattice simulation we artificially restore chiral symmetry by removing the low-lying Dirac modes of the valence quark propagators, which is a well defined procedure and keep gluodynamics intact. Hadrons survive this artificial chiral restoration and their mass is surprisingly large. All hadrons fall into chiral multiplets and some of them are degenerate, i.e. the spectrum reveals some higher symmetry, that includes the chiral symmetry as a subgroup. The U(1)_A symmetry does not get restored after removal of the chiral modes from the valence quarks.

## Key claim

Hadrons survive an artificial restoration of chiral symmetry (achieved by removing low-lying Dirac modes from valence quark propagators while keeping gluodynamics intact), and their masses remain surprisingly large; this contradicts the expectation that hadron mass is primarily generated by the quark condensate of the vacuum.

## Method

The authors performed unquenched lattice QCD simulations with two flavors of chirally improved fermions on a 2.4 fm lattice at a pion mass of 322 MeV. They artificially restored chiral symmetry by truncating an increasing number of the lowest eigenmodes of the Hermitian Dirac operator from the valence quark propagators, a procedure that removes the chiral condensate while leaving the gluonic dynamics unchanged. They then computed the masses of mesons (including pi, rho, a0, a1, b1, h1, omega, f1) and baryons (nucleon and Delta) as a function of the truncation level.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass before truncation: 322 MeV (lattice scale).
- At a truncation energy sigma ~ 40 MeV, the rho-a1 splitting vanishes, indicating chiral SU(2)L x SU(2)R restoration in the physical states.
- The nucleon and rho-meson masses do not decrease upon chiral restoration (qualitative result from Figs. 1 and 2; no explicit numerical masses given in the text for the truncated case).
- The b1-rho and b1-rho' splittings persist after truncation, showing that U(1)A symmetry does not get restored.

## Context

This work extends the earlier lattice study by Lang and Schroed (2011) on pi, rho, a0, and a1 mesons to include both baryons and mesons and their symmetries. It directly tests the long-held view from the instanton liquid model (Shuryak, 1982) and from phenomenological approaches such as Shifman-Vainshtein-Zakharov (1979) and Ioffe (1981) that hadron masses come mostly from the quark condensate. It also addresses the Casher argument (Casher, 1979) that confinement requires chiral symmetry breaking, showing that hadrons can exist in a world with restored chiral symmetry. The results enable further study of the phase diagram of QCD and the nature of higher symmetries in the hadron spectrum beyond chiral symmetry.
