# Properties of hot and dense matter from relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We review the progress achieved in extracting the properties of hot and dense matter from relativistic heavy ion collisions at the relativistic heavy ion collider (RHIC) at Brookhaven National Laboratory and the large hadron collider (LHC) at CERN. We focus on bulk properties of the medium, in particular the evidence for thermalization, aspects of the equation of state, transport properties, as well as fluctuations and correlations. We also discuss the in-medium properties of hadrons with light and heavy quarks, and measurements of dileptons and quarkonia. This review is dedicated to the memory of Gerald E. Brown.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the progress in extracting properties of hot and dense matter from relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC, but the abstract glosses over the fact that many key results, such as the nature of the QCD phase transition at finite baryon density and the location of a critical endpoint, remain uncertain, with lattice QCD calculations hampered by the fermion sign problem and model calculations providing only qualitative guidance.

## Method

The authors synthesize experimental data from RHIC and LHC heavy ion collisions, including measurements of hadron spectra, elliptic flow, jet quenching, dileptons, and quarkonia, and compare these to theoretical frameworks such as lattice QCD simulations, relativistic viscous hydrodynamics, hadron resonance gas models, and holographic duality. They also discuss the beam energy scan at RHIC, which systematically varies collision energy to probe the QCD phase diagram at finite baryon chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- Pseudo-critical transition temperature at vanishing baryon density: \(T_c \simeq 151 \pm 3 \pm 3\) MeV (Aoki et al., 2006, 2009), consistent with \(154 \pm 9\) MeV (Bazavov et al., 2011, 2014).
- QCD fine structure constant at the \(Z\) boson pole: \(\alpha_s(m_Z) = 0.1184 \pm 0.0007\) (Nakamura et al., 2010).
- QCD scale parameter in the \(\overline{MS}\) scheme: \(\Lambda_{QCD} \simeq 200\) MeV.
- Nuclear matter saturation density: \(\rho_0 \simeq 0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- Critical point temperature for the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition: \(T \simeq 18\) MeV (Pochodzalla et al., 1995; Elliott et al., 2013).
- Charged pion mass: \(m_\pi = 139\) MeV; rho meson mass: \(m_\rho = 770\) MeV; proton mass: \(m_p = 935\) MeV.

## Context

This review extends earlier work on the quark-gluon plasma, including the statistical bootstrap model of Hagedorn (1965) and early lattice QCD calculations for pure \(SU(2)\) gauge theory (Creutz, 1980; McLerran, 1980). It builds on discoveries at RHIC, such as elliptic flow and jet quenching (Ackermann et al., 2000; Adcox et al., 2001), and at the LHC, including higher flow harmonics and reduced \(J/\psi\) suppression (Abelev et al., 2013). The paper enables future searches for the QCD critical point via fluctuation measurements in the RHIC beam energy scan and further refinement of transport properties of the strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma.
