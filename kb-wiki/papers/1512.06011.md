# Constrained-Path Quantum Monte-Carlo Approach for Non-Yrast States Within the Shell Model

> The present paper intends to present an extension of the constrained-path quantum Monte-Carlo approach allowing to reconstruct non-yrast states in order to reach the complete spectroscopy of nuclei within the interacting shell model. As in the yrast case studied in a previous work, the formalism involves a variational symmetry-restored wave function assuming two central roles. First, it guides the underlying Brownian motion to improve the efficiency of the sampling. Second, it constrains the stochastic paths according to the phaseless approximation to control sign or phase problems that usually plague fermionic QMC simulations. Proof-of-principle results in the $sd$ valence space are reported. They prove the ability of the scheme to offer remarkably accurate binding energies for both even- and odd-mass nuclei irrespective of the considered interaction.

## Key claim

The paper extends the constrained-path quantum Monte-Carlo approach to reconstruct non-yrast (excited) states within the interacting shell model, enabling complete low-lying spectroscopy; the method provides remarkably accurate binding energies for both even- and odd-mass nuclei in the sd valence space, but the results are proof-of-principle and the accuracy depends on the quality of the variational symmetry-restored trial wave function used to guide and constrain the stochastic paths.

## Method

The authors extend the phaseless quantum Monte-Carlo scheme, previously applied to yrast states, by incorporating a symmetry-restored trial wave function that both guides the Brownian motion of Slater determinants via importance sampling and constrains the stochastic paths through the phaseless approximation to control the phase problem. The Hamiltonian is decomposed into a quadratic form of one-body operators using an isospin density decomposition, and the imaginary-time propagator is stochastically reformulated via the Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation with a second-order Trotter-Suzuki breakup. The method generates a weighted average of Slater determinants whose weights are updated based on the phase change of the overlap with the trial wave function, discarding paths where the phase change exceeds π/2.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies for sd-shell nuclei are reported as proof-of-principle results, with the method proving "remarkably accurate" for both even- and odd-mass nuclei irrespective of the interaction; no specific numerical binding energy values are given in the provided text.
- The imaginary-time step is denoted Δτ, and the constraint discards stochastic paths when |Δθ_τ| > π/2, where Δθ_τ is the phase change of the overlap with the trial wave function over one time step.
- The phaseless approximation uses a weight update factor of max{0; cos(Δθ_τ)} to gradually reduce weights of walkers whose phase deviates, with weights set to zero when |Δθ_τ| exceeds π/2.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous constrained-path quantum Monte-Carlo approach for yrast states (Bonnard and Juillet, 2013) to non-yrast states, aiming to achieve complete spectroscopy within the shell model. It builds on the phaseless quantum Monte-Carlo scheme developed by Zhang and Krakauer (2003) for quantum chemistry and the shell model Monte-Carlo method of Koonin, Dean, and Langanke (1997), which suffers from phase problems with realistic interactions and cannot achieve detailed spectroscopy. The method enables future calculations of excited-state properties for nuclei beyond the reach of conventional diagonalization techniques, particularly in large valence spaces where the Monte-Carlo shell model technique (Honma, Mizusaki, and Otsuka, 1996) provides variational estimates but not exact eigenstates.
