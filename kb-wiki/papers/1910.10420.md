# The evolution of Information entropy components in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Shannon information entropy provides an effective tool to study the evolution process in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The time evolution process of thermodynamic entropy $S_{\rm thermal}$, multiple entropy $S_{\rm mul}$, and configuration entropy $S_{\rm conf}$ at RHIC is studied using the AMPT model to generate central Au-Au collisions. By superimposing the three kinds of information entropy, we can get a more complete information entropy of the system to describe the physical information of the relativistic heavy ion collision. The results show that the four stages of the time evolution process of the system entropy $S$ seem to correspond to the four physical processes in the relativistic heavy ion collision, indicating that the total entropy of the system can reflect the physical information in the relativistic heavy ion collision more accurately.

## Key claim

The total Shannon information entropy of the system, obtained by summing thermodynamic, multiplicity, and configuration entropy, evolves through four stages that correspond to the four physical processes in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, but the authors note that the temporary decrease in total entropy violates the expectation for a closed system because string degrees of freedom are not included in the AMPT model evaluation.

## Method

Central Au-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 64.2\), 200, and 800 GeV were simulated using the string-melting version of the AMPT model (v2.26t9b; isoft=5) with impact parameter \(b \leq 3\) fm and parton cross section 10 mb. The time evolution of three Shannon entropy components was computed: thermodynamic entropy from the 6-dimensional phase-space distribution of particles, multiplicity entropy from the distribution of particle multiplicities, and configuration entropy from the internal quark-diquark configurations of baryons. The total entropy was obtained as the sum \(S = S_{\rm thermal} + S_{\rm mul} + S_{\rm conf}\).

## Key numbers

- Thermodynamic entropy of the system at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV shows a linear increase from \(t = 5\) to 10 fm/c, corresponding to the QGP-to-hadron-gas phase transition.
- The maximum of hadronic multiplicity entropy occurs at about 7 fm/c for \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV, with an extracted temperature of 147 MeV from the hadron resonance gas model, close to the chemical freeze-out temperature of 141 MeV from Xu and Ko (2017).
- Configuration entropy is lower at higher center-of-mass energies because higher-energy collisions produce a larger proportion of mesons without internal structure.
- The total entropy evolution at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV shows four stages: decrease for \(0 < t < 3\) fm, minimum for \(3 < t < 5\) fm (QGP state), rapid increase from \(t = 5\) to 9 fm (phase transition), and saturation thereafter (hadron scattering).

## Context

This work extends the application of Shannon information entropy to relativistic heavy-ion collisions, building on prior studies by Ma (1999) who used multiplicity entropy to diagnose nuclear liquid-gas phase transitions, and by Csernai et al. (2017) who considered internal hadron configurations. The authors use the AMPT model to compute three entropy components simultaneously, enabling a more complete description of the system's chaoticity than previous single-entropy approaches. The results suggest that total entropy can serve as a diagnostic for the four stages of heavy-ion collisions (compression, QGP formation, phase transition, hadron scattering), but the authors caution that the observed temporary entropy decrease indicates missing degrees of freedom (strings) in the model, pointing to the need for more complete treatments following the criteria of Csernai et al. (2017).
