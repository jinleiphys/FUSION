# Collective flow and two-pion correlations from a relativistic hydrodynamic model with early chemical freeze out

> We investigate the effect of early chemical freeze-out on radial flow, elliptic flow and HBT radii by using a fully three dimensional hydrodynamic model. When we take account of the early chemical freeze-out, the space-time evolution of temperature in the hadron phase is considerably different from the conventional model in which chemical equilibrium is always assumed. As a result, we find that radial and elliptic flows are suppressed and that the lifetime and the spatial size of the fluid are reduced. We analyze the p_t spectrum, the differential elliptic flow, and the HBT radii at the RHIC energy by using hydrodynamics with chemically non-equilibrium equation of state.

## Key claim
Incorporating early chemical freeze-out (at Tch = 170 MeV) into a 3D hydrodynamic model for Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 130 GeV suppresses radial and elliptic flows and reduces the fluid's lifetime and spatial size compared to the conventional assumption of full chemical equilibrium, but the effect on the transverse momentum spectrum is compensated by the interplay between temperature reduction and flow generation.

## Method
The authors performed fully three-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamic simulations using the Bjorken coordinate system, with initial conditions tuned to reproduce PHOBOS pseudorapidity distributions. They constructed three equations of state for the hadron phase: a chemically equilibrated resonance gas (model CE), a model with complete chemical freeze-out of all hadrons (model CFO), and a partial chemical equilibrium model (model PCE) where only stable hadrons (π, K, η, N, Λ, Σ) have fixed abundances while resonances remain in equilibrium with them. All models assume a first-order phase transition at Tc = 170 MeV and zero baryon chemical potential.

## Key numbers
- Chemical freeze-out temperature Tch = 170 MeV, consistent with thermal model analysis at RHIC (Braun-Munzinger, 2001)
- At Tth = 140 MeV, average radial flow for model PCE is reduced by 17.7% compared to model CE; at Tth = 120 MeV, reduction is 22.5%
- Initial energy density Emax = 35 GeV/fm³ at proper time τ₀ = 0.6 fm/c for central Au+Au collisions
- Bag constant B^(1/4) = 247.2 MeV, latent heat ΔE ≈ 1.7 GeV/fm³ at Tc = 170 MeV
- Impact parameters: b = 2.4 fm for 0-6% central collisions (342 participants), b = 8.9 fm for 35-45% non-central collisions (94 participants)

## Context
This work extends the fully three-dimensional hydrodynamic model of Hirano (2001) by incorporating the two-step freeze-out picture (chemical then thermal) suggested by analyses of particle ratios and transverse momentum slopes at AGS, SPS, and RHIC energies (Cleymans, 1993; Braun-Munzinger, 2001). The partial chemical equilibrium approach follows Bebie (1992). The results show that including early chemical freeze-out reduces elliptic flow compared to the chemically equilibrated calculations of Kolb (2000), and the model reproduces STAR and PHOBOS data for v₂(pₜ) and v₂(η) at RHIC when Tth = 140 MeV is chosen. This enables more realistic hydrodynamic descriptions that can simultaneously account for particle ratios, single-particle spectra, and azimuthal asymmetries.
