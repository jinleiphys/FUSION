# Physics Opportunities at ELFE

> I review some central physics opportunities at the 15 ... 30 GeV continuous beam electron accelerator ELFE, proposed to be built in conjunction with the DESY linear collider. Our present detailed knowledge of single parton distributions in hadrons and nuclei needs to be supplemented by measurements of compact valence quark configurations, accessible through hard exclusive scattering, and of compact multiparton subsystems which contribute to semi-inclusive processes. Cumulative (x>1, x_F>1) processes in nuclei measure short-range correlations between partons belonging to different nucleons in the same nucleus. The same configurations may give rise to subthreshold production of light hadrons and charm.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a 15–30 GeV continuous-beam electron accelerator like ELFE is uniquely suited to measure compact valence quark configurations via hard exclusive scattering and compact multiparton subsystems via semi-inclusive processes, which go beyond the single-parton distributions accessible in inclusive deep inelastic scattering. The hedge is that these measurements are extremely demanding and require the combination of high luminosity, moderate energy, and high duty factor that only ELFE can provide, and that the theoretical framework for semi-inclusive processes is still incomplete.

## Method

The author reviews the theoretical basis for three classes of measurements at ELFE: (1) hard exclusive scattering (e.g., elastic ep scattering, the pion transition form factor) which factorizes into a hard subprocess and hadron distribution amplitudes; (2) semi-inclusive processes (e.g., ep → eπ + X at large z) which may select compact qqbar subsystems; and (3) cumulative nuclear processes (x > 1, x_F > 1, subthreshold production) which probe short-range correlations between partons from different nucleons. The discussion uses standard QCD factorization, dimensional scaling rules, and light-cone Fock state expansions.

## Key numbers

- ELFE energy range: 15 to 30 GeV continuous electron beam.
- Luminosity range: 10^35 to 10^38 cm^−2 s^−1.
- Energy resolution: ΔE/E ≈ 5 × 10^−4.
- Duty factor: ~80%.
- Resolution of perturbative QCD: order 0.1 fm (corresponding to Q^2 ~ 10 GeV^2).
- For elastic ep scattering, the proton form factor scales as F_p(Q^2) ∝ 1/Q^4, consistent with data for Q^2 ≳ 5 GeV^2 up to Q^2 ≲ 30 GeV^2.
- The pion transition form factor data in the range 1 < Q^2 < 8 GeV^2 are well fit using a pion distribution amplitude close to the asymptotic form φ_π^AS ∝ x_1 x_2.
- For cumulative hadron production in nuclei, the average transverse momentum of produced protons reaches ⟨p_⊥^2⟩ = 2 GeV^2 at x_F = 4.
- Subthreshold antiproton production on ^63Cu has been observed down to E_lab ≈ 3 GeV, close to the kinematic limit of 2.8 GeV.

## Context

This paper extends the theoretical and experimental case for ELFE as presented in earlier ELFE physics proposals (e.g., Frois, 1996) and the review by Brodsky (1996). It builds on the established success of inclusive DIS measurements of single parton distributions (e.g., from CERN, Fermilab, SLAC) and argues that the next frontier is measuring compact parton configurations via exclusive and semi-inclusive reactions. The work directly references the factorization formalism of Brodsky and Lepage (1980) for exclusive processes, the dimensional scaling rules of Brodsky and Farrar (1973), and the cumulative nuclear effects observed by the BCDMS, SLAC, and NuTeV collaborations, as well as subthreshold production experiments. It enables future experimental programs at ELFE to map hadron distribution amplitudes, study color transparency, and probe short-range nuclear correlations.
