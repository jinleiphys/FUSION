# Calibrating the In-Medium Behavior of Quarkonia

> Quarkonium production has been considered as a tool to study the medium formed in high energy nuclear collisions, assuming that the formation of a hot and dense environment modifies the production pattern observed in elementary collisions. The basic features measured there are the relative fractions of hidden to open heavy flavor and the relative fractions of the different hidden heavy flavor states. Hence the essential question is if and how these quantities are modified in nuclear collisions. We show how the relevant data must be calibrated, i.e., what reference has to be used, in order to determine this in a model-independent way.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the correct, model-independent observable for quarkonium suppression or enhancement in nuclear collisions is the ratio of quarkonium production to open heavy flavor production, normalized to the same ratio in proton-proton collisions; simply comparing the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) of quarkonia to scaled \(pp\) results is insufficient because it conflates changes in total heavy quark pair production with changes in the binding probability.

## Method

The author defines the survival probability \(S_{\psi}\) for \(J/\psi\) as the ratio of the \(J/\psi\)-to-open-charm production rate in nucleus-nucleus (\(AA\)) collisions divided by the same ratio in \(pp\) collisions, which cancels initial-state effects such as shadowing and parton energy loss. Using existing experimental data from ALICE and CMS at the LHC and from PHENIX and STAR at RHIC, the author compares the centrality dependence of \(R_{AA}\) for \(J/\psi\) and for open charm (via \(D\) mesons) at different transverse momenta to determine whether the fraction of charm quark pairs that bind into charmonia changes in the medium.

## Key numbers

* In \(pp\) collisions, about 2% of the total \(c\bar{c}\) production goes into \(J/\psi\) production (the constant \(g_{c\bar{c} \to J/\psi}\)).
* Of the observed \(J/\psi\) rates, only about 60% is directly produced \(J/\psi(1S)\); about 30% comes from \(\chi_c(1P)\) decay and 10% from \(\psi'(2S)\) decay.
* At RHIC, for low transverse momentum (\(P_T\)), the \(R_{AA}\) of open charm is within errors unity over the whole centrality range, while the \(R_{AA}\) of \(J/\psi\) decreases strongly, with a suppression of up to 75% for the most central collisions.
* At the LHC, for intermediate and high transverse momenta, the \(R_{AA}\) of \(J/\psi\) and the \(R_{AA}\) of open charm show the same behavior within errors, so the ratio \(N_{AA}(J/\psi)/N_{AA}(c\bar{c})\) equals the \(pp\) value \(g_{c\bar{c} \to J/\psi}\) at all centralities.

## Context

This work extends the conceptual framework of sequential suppression (Matsui and Satz, 1986) and statistical enhancement (Braun-Munzinger and Stachel, 2000) by providing a calibration method that avoids model-dependent parametrizations of initial-state effects. It directly addresses the difficulties in interpreting \(J/\psi\) data from RHIC and the LHC (Kluberg and others, 2010) by showing that the apparent suppression at low \(P_T\) at RHIC is genuine, while the decrease at high \(P_T\) at the LHC is simply due to fewer \(c\bar{c}\) pairs. The analysis enables future studies to determine whether the hot medium modifies the binding probability, particularly by measuring low-\(P_T\) open charm rates at the LHC.
