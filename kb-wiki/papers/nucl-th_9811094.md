# The reaction NN \to NN ϕnear threshold

> The sensitivity of polarization observables in the reaction $NN \to NN ϕ$ slightly above the threshold is studied with respect to the details of the one-boson exchange model and a possible admixture of hidden strangeness in the nucleon. It is shown that the finite-energy predictions differ strongly from the threshold predictions. A measurement of the beam - target asymmetry and the $ϕ$ decay anisotropy can help to disentangle the role of various reaction mechanisms.

## Key claim

The paper shows that polarization observables in the reaction \(NN \to NN \phi\) slightly above threshold differ strongly from the universal threshold predictions, and that measurements of beam-target asymmetry and \(\phi\) decay anisotropy can help disentangle the role of conventional one-boson exchange mechanisms from exotic channels involving hidden strangeness in the nucleon, though the finite-energy predictions are described as "rather qualitative estimates than exact predictions" due to parameter uncertainties.

## Method

The authors employ a one-boson exchange (OBE) model with tree-level diagrams for three reaction channels: (1a) \(\phi\) production via internal conversion in a \(\phi\rho\pi\) vertex, (1b) \(\phi\) bremsstrahlung from nucleon legs with a \(\phi NN\) coupling, and (1c) a \(\phi\) shake-off mechanism from an \(s\bar s\) admixture in the nucleon wave function. The model uses coupling constants from the Bonn potential and the \(\phi\to\rho\pi\) decay width, with cut-off parameters \(\Lambda^\rho_{\phi\rho\pi} = 2.9\) GeV and \(\Lambda^\pi_{\phi\rho\pi} = 0.77\) GeV, and considers both real and imaginary phases for the two-body \(T\) matrix.

## Key numbers

- The \(\phi\to\rho\pi\) decay width of 0.69 MeV yields \(g_{\phi\rho\pi} = 1.10\) GeV\(^{-1}\).
- The \(\phi NN\) coupling constants used are \(g_{\phi NN} = -0.24\) and \(\kappa_\phi = 0.2\).
- The threshold prediction for the beam-target asymmetry in \(pp\) collisions is \(C^{BT}_{zz} = 1\).
- The threshold prediction for the ratio of unpolarized cross sections is \(\sigma_{np}/\sigma_{pp} = 5\).
- The threshold diagonal spin density matrix elements are \(\rho_{00}^{\rm thr} = \frac12 \sin^2\theta\) and \(\rho_{\pm1\pm1}^{\rm thr} = \frac12(1+\cos^2\theta)\).
- For the \(pn\) reaction at threshold, the beam-target asymmetry is \(C^{BT}_{zz} = 0.8\).
- The OBE model is considered reliable only for \(\Delta_s \equiv \sqrt{s} - 2M_N - M_\phi \le 0.15\) GeV.
- At \(\Delta_s = 0.1\) GeV, the separate contributions of the conventional OBE channel and the exotic channels differ by more than an order of magnitude, but interference may amount up to 50% of the total cross section.
- The cut-off parameter \(\Lambda^\pi_{\phi\rho\pi} = 2.9\) GeV (instead of 0.77 GeV) increases the OBE contribution by a factor of 4 to 6.

## Context

This work extends earlier threshold analyses by Rekalo and Ellis, who used spin and parity conservation to predict universal polarization observables at threshold for \(pp \to pp\phi\), and addresses the question of whether those predictions hold at finite energies above threshold. The paper also builds on the OBE model used by Ko and collaborators for \(\phi\) production in heavy-ion collisions, and on the photoproduction studies of Titov and Henley that explored hidden strangeness in the nucleon. The results enable experimental tests with the ANKE spectrometer at COSY and the HADES detector at GSI, where measurements of beam-target asymmetry and \(\phi\) decay anisotropy could distinguish between conventional meson-exchange mechanisms and contributions from intrinsic strangeness in the nucleon.
