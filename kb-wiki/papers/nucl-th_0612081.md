# Parameterized total cross sections for pion production in nuclear collisions

> Total inclusive cross sections for neutral and charged pion production in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus reactions have been calculated and compared to experiment. Nucleon-nucleon theoretical cross sections have been scaled up to nuclear collisions using a scaling factor similar to $(A_PA_T)^{2/3}$, where $A_P$ and $A_T$ are the nucleon numbers of the projectile and target nuclei. Variations in the power of this scaling factor have been studied and a good fit to experiment is obtained with a small modification of the power. Theoretical cross sections are written in a form that is very suitable for immediate input into transport codes.

## Key claim

The paper provides simple parameterizations of total inclusive pion production cross sections for proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions, scaled from nucleon-nucleon cross sections using a factor of $(A_P A_T)^N$, where the exponent $N$ is slightly modified from the naive geometric value of $2/3$ to achieve better agreement with experimental data.

## Method

The authors start from previously parameterized total inclusive cross sections for pion production in proton-proton collisions (given as functions of laboratory kinetic energy $T_{\rm lab}$). They then scale these elementary cross sections to nuclear collisions using a factor $(A_P A_T)^N$, where $A_P$ and $A_T$ are the mass numbers of projectile and target. For charged pions, the ratio of $\pi^+$ to $\pi^-$ production is derived from the probabilities of proton-proton, neutron-neutron, and proton-neutron collisions within the nuclei, weighted by the low-energy isospin ratios of the elementary cross sections. The best-fit exponents $N_\pm$ and $N_0$ are determined by comparing the resulting formulas to experimental data from Nagamiya and Schwalb.

## Key numbers

- The best-fit exponent for charged pion production is $N_\pm = 2.2/3$.
- The best-fit exponent for neutral pion production is $N_0 = 2.4/3$.
- For charged pion production in nucleus-nucleus reactions, the theory overlaps all error bars from 0.4 to 2.1 AGeV.
- For neutral pion production in nucleus-nucleus reactions at 1 AGeV, only 3 data points are available and agreement is satisfactory.
- The low-energy ratio of $\pi^+$ to $\pi^-$ cross sections in pp reactions is 4:1.
- The low-energy ratio of $\pi^+$ to $\pi^-$ cross sections in nn reactions is 1:4.
- The low-energy ratio of $\pi^+$ to $\pi^-$ cross sections in pn reactions is 1:1.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous parameterizations of pion production cross sections in proton-proton reactions (Blattnig et al.) to proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus reactions, motivated by the need for fast arithmetic parameterizations in heavy-ion transport codes for space radiation protection. The parameterized cross sections are written in a form suitable for immediate input into transport codes, and the formalism is expected to be valid for total cross sections in the GeV region. The approach enables rapid calculations of pion production in nuclear collisions without requiring full microscopic simulations.
