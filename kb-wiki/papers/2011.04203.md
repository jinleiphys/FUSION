# Impact of complex many-body correlations on electron capture in thermally excited nuclei around $^{78}$Ni

> We link complex many-body correlations, which play a decisive role in the structural properties of atomic nuclei, to the electron capture occurring during star evolution. The recently developed finite-temperature response theory, taking into account the coupling between single-nucleon and collective degrees of freedom, is applied to spin-isospin transitions, which dominate the electron capture rates. Calculations are performed for $^{78}$Ni and for the surrounding even-even nuclei associated with a high-sensitivity region of the nuclear chart in the context of core-collapse supernova simulations. The obtained electron capture rates are compared to those of a simpler thermal quasiparticle random phase approximation (TQRPA), which is standardly used in such computations. The comparison indicates that correlations beyond TQRPA lead to significantly higher electron capture rates under the typical thermodynamical conditions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including complex many-body correlations beyond the thermal quasiparticle random phase approximation (TQRPA) leads to significantly higher electron capture rates in neutron-rich nuclei around $^{78}$Ni under typical supernova thermodynamical conditions, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific temperature range (up to 2 MeV) and the particular set of nuclei studied (even-even isotopes around $^{78}$Ni).

## Method

The authors apply the finite-temperature proton-neutron relativistic time blocking approximation (FT-pnRTBA), which incorporates particle-vibration coupling (PVC) effects into the response function for spin-isospin transitions. They first solve the relativistic mean-field equations with the NL3 parametrization at finite temperature, then compute phonon modes via finite-temperature relativistic random phase approximation (FT-RRPA), and finally solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the proton-neutron response function including both static (pion and rho-meson exchange) and dynamic (PVC) kernels. The Gamow-Teller (GT$_+$) strength distributions are extracted from the response function and used to compute electron capture rates for $^{78}$Ni and the neighboring even-even nuclei $^{76,80}$Ni, $^{76}$Fe, and $^{80}$Zn.

## Key numbers

- The phonon model space included phonons with quantum numbers $J^{\pi}=2^{+},\;3^{-},\;4^{+},\;5^{-},\;6^{+}$ below an energy cutoff of 20 MeV, with reduced transition probabilities at least 5% of the maximal one for each $J^{\pi}$.
- The smearing parameter $\Delta = 0.02$ MeV was adopted for resolving spectral features and extracting electron capture rates.
- Calculations were performed on a temperature grid with 0.5 MeV step up to $T = 2$ MeV.
- The critical temperature for superfluid pairing vanishing in open-shell nuclei was found to be within $0.5 \leq T \leq 1$ MeV, with a specific value of 0.7 for $^{68}$Ni as found in prior work.
- The neutron-proton mass splitting used was $M_{np} = 1.293$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the finite-temperature relativistic time blocking approximation previously applied to beta decay (Litvinova, Robin, Wibowo 2020) to the GT$_+$ channel and electron capture in neutron-rich nuclei. It directly compares with the simpler thermal quasiparticle random phase approximation (TQRPA) used in prior calculations by Niu (2011), Dzhioev (2019, 2020), and Ravlic (2020), showing that PVC correlations produce significantly higher capture rates. The nuclei around N=50 were identified as a high-sensitivity region for core-collapse supernova simulations by Sullivan (2016), Titus (2018), Raduta (2017), and Pascal (2020). The approach enables more reliable predictions of electron capture rates for astrophysical modeling, particularly for nuclei inaccessible to experimental study under stellar thermodynamical conditions.
