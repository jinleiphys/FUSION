# Critical behaviour in QCD at finite isovector chemical potential

> We report an investigation of criticality in QCD at finite isovector chemical potential, mu_3, and at zero temperature. At the critical point, mu_3^c = m_pi, we find that an uncharged scalar and pseudoscalar and a charged pseudoscalar meson become massless within the resolution of our measurement. The effective long distance theory therefore breaks O(4) symmetry by charged pion condensation. This results in a rising quark number susceptibility. The baryon remains massive, as indicated by a vanishing baryon number susceptibility.

## Key claim
The paper reports that at zero temperature and finite isovector chemical potential, QCD exhibits a second-order phase transition at \(\mu_3^c \approx m_\pi\), where an uncharged scalar, an uncharged pseudoscalar, and a charged pseudoscalar meson become massless within the resolution of the lattice measurement, supporting an effective long-distance theory with broken O(4) symmetry due to charged pion condensation.

## Method
Quenched lattice QCD simulations were performed on \(8^4\) and \(12^4\) lattices at \(\beta = 5.8941\) with a valence quark mass \(ma = 1/6\). The order parameters (chiral condensate, isovector number density \(n_3\), and flavour off-diagonal susceptibility \(\chi_{ud}\)) and meson masses (from temporal correlators projected to zero momentum) were measured as functions of \(\mu_3\) using staggered quarks with two independent fields to represent up and down flavours.

## Key numbers
- Critical isovector chemical potential: \(a\mu_3^c \approx 1 \approx a m_\pi\) (lattice spacing \(a\) fixed at \(\beta = 5.8941\)).
- At \(T = \mu_3 = 0\): \(m_\pi a = 1.065 \pm 0.003\), \(m_\rho a = 1.323 \pm 0.007\), baryon mass \(m_p a = 1.59 \pm 0.05\).
- Lattice cutoff: \(a^{-1} = (6.9 \pm 0.3) \Lambda_{\overline{MS}}\).
- Saturation sets in at \(a\mu_3 \approx 2\), beyond which continuum physics cannot be extracted.
- Mass resolution on the \(12^4\) lattice: \(1/aN_t\) (effectively massless when mass reaches this value).

## Context
This work extends earlier predictions from chiral perturbation theory and perturbative QCD (Son, 2000) that a second-order O(4) phase transition occurs at \(\mu_3 = m_\pi\) due to charged pion condensation. It also follows a first quenched lattice investigation (Sinclair, 2001) that found pion condensation. The results enable future finite-size scaling studies to extract critical exponents and open the way to lattice exploration of the region \(\mu_3 \approx m_\rho\), which is inaccessible to effective theories or perturbation theory.
