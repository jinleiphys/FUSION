# A New Approach to the 3D Faddeev Equation for Three-Body Scattering

> A novel approach to solve the Faddeev equation for three-body scattering at arbitrary energies is proposed. This approach disentangles the complicated singularity structure of the free three-nucleon propagator leading to the moving and logarithmic singularities in standard treatments. The Faddeev equation is formulated in momentum space and directly solved in terms of momentum vectors without employing a partial wave decomposition. In its simplest form the Faddeev equation for identical bosons, which we are using, is an integral equation in five variables, magnitudes of relative momenta and angles. The singularities of the free propagator and the deuteron propagator are now both simple poles in two different momentum variables, and thus can both be integrated with standard techniques.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a reformulation of the Faddeev kernel for three-body scattering that eliminates logarithmic singularities in the free three-body propagator, replacing them with simple poles in a single momentum variable that can be integrated using standard subtraction techniques, but only for the case of three identical bosons without spin or isospin.

## Method

The authors start from the standard momentum-space Faddeev equation for three identical bosons and rewrite the permutation operator's delta functions to separate angular and magnitude variables. They then use a propagator separation technique, following Witala (2008), to split the product of the free three-body propagator and the deuteron propagator into two terms, each containing only a simple pole in either the p' or q'' integration variable. The resulting kernel is an integral equation in five variables (magnitudes of relative momenta and angles) that is solved directly without partial wave decomposition.

## Key numbers

- The free three-body propagator energy is given by E'' = (1/m)(p''^2 + 3/4 q''^2)
- The two-body t-matrix is evaluated at energy ε = E - (3/(4m))q^2
- The deuteron pole is separated by defining t_s(p,p';z) = t̂_s(p,p';z)/(z - E_d)
- The integration domain for q'' is bounded by |q/2 - p'| to q/2 + p' in one term and |q/2 - q''| to q/2 + q'' in the other
- An example integration area is shown for q = 40 MeV/c
- The new denominator function Ḡ(q,q'',p') = 1/(|E_d| + (1/m)p''^2) is always positive and cannot become singular

## Context

This work extends the three-dimensional (vector-variable) Faddeev approach of Liu (2004) and Elster (2008), which solved the nonrelativistic Faddeev equations for scalar interactions up to projectile energies of 2 GeV but still required special treatment of logarithmic singularities. The new formulation follows the suggestion of Witala (2008) for partial-wave decomposed equations and applies it to the vector-variable formulation, enabling simpler numerical integration that treats three-body continuum singularities similarly to two-body continuum singularities. This enables future applications of Faddeev integral equations in different areas of physics without the technical obstacle of logarithmic singularities.
