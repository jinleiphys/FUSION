# Thermal momentum distribution from path integrals with shifted boundary conditions

> For a thermal field theory formulated in the grand canonical ensemble, the distribution of the total momentum is an observable characterizing the thermal state. We show that its cumulants are related to thermodynamic potentials. In a relativistic system for instance, the thermal variance of the total momentum is a direct measure of the enthalpy. We relate the generating function of the cumulants to the ratio of (a) a partition function expressed as a Matsubara path integral with shifted boundary conditions in the compact direction, and (b) the ordinary partition function. In this form the generating function is well suited for Monte-Carlo evaluation, and the cumulants can be extracted straightforwardly. We test the method in the SU(3) Yang-Mills theory and obtain the entropy density at three different temperatures.

## Key claim

The generating function of the cumulants of the total-momentum distribution can be expressed as a ratio of two partition functions with different boundary conditions, making it well suited for Monte-Carlo evaluation; the method is tested in SU(3) Yang-Mills theory where the entropy density is obtained at three temperatures, but the approach extends to a wide class of theories including QCD at zero baryon chemical potential.

## Method

The generating function of the cumulants of the total-momentum distribution is expressed as the ratio of a partition function with shifted boundary conditions in the Euclidean time direction to the ordinary partition function. For the SU(3) Yang-Mills test, the ratio is computed via a product of reweighting factors over a set of interpolating systems, using the standard Wilson plaquette action on lattices with varying temporal extents. The entropy density is extracted from the second cumulant by taking the continuum limit of the quantity \(2\Fgen(\beta,\bz,a)/(|\bz|^2 T^5 L^3)\) with \(\bz=(2a,0,0)\).

## Key numbers

- Entropy density at \(1.5 T_c\): continuum extrapolated value \(4.77(8)\), compatible with published results from Boyd (1996) and Namekawa (2001) who obtained about \(4.92(20)\) and \(5.07(20)\) respectively.
- Entropy density at \(4.1 T_c\): results from lattices A1 through A5 give values ranging from \(5.10(3)\) to \(4.9(3)\) across different lattice spacings.
- Entropy density at \(9.2 T_c\): results from lattices C1 through C3 give values ranging from \(7.18(3)\) to \(6.94(12)\); these results are new.
- Lattice parameters: bare couplings \(g_0^2\) range from \(5.9\) to \(7.584\), with temporal extents \(\beta/a\) from \(4\) to \(10\) and spatial extents \(L/a\) from \(12\) to \(30\).

## Context

This work extends the techniques of Della Morte (2008, 2010) for computing relative contributions to the partition function from states with given quantum numbers, applying them to the total-momentum distribution in thermal field theory. It provides a new method for computing thermodynamic quantities that avoids the vacuum subtraction or renormalization constant required by established methods such as those of Engels (1981, 1990), Endrodi (2007), and Meyer (2009). The approach enables future applications including determination of partial pressures in different symmetry sectors of QCD, testing the hadron resonance gas model used in heavy-ion collision phenomenology, and studying the spontaneous breaking of translation invariance at finite baryon density.
