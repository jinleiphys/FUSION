# The Massive Schwinger Model - a Hamiltonian Lattice Study in a Fast Moving Frame

> We present a non-perturbative study of the massive Schwinger model. We use a Hamiltonian approach, based on a momentum lattice corresponding to a fast moving reference frame, and equal time quantization. We present numerical results for the mass spectrum of the vector and scalar particle. We find good agreement with chiral perturbation theory in the strong coupling regime and also with other non-perturbative studies (Hamer et al., Mo and Perry) in the non-relativistic regime. The most important new result is the study of the $θ$-action, and computation of vector and scalar masses as a function of the $θ$-angle. We find excellent agreement with chiral perturbation theory. Finally, we give results for the distribution functions. We compare our results with Bergknoff's variational study from the infinite momentum frame in the chiral region.

# The Massive Schwinger Model - a Hamiltonian Lattice Study in a Fast Moving Frame

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that equal-time quantization in a fast moving frame (Breit frame) works well for the massive Schwinger model, contrary to claims that low energy states are not well approximated in equal-time field theory. The authors show the problem is not equal-time quantization but the use of the rest frame, which they avoid.

## Method
The authors start from the Lagrangian in axial gauge, introduce a space-time lattice with spacing a and N lattice nodes, then perform a discrete Fourier transformation to a momentum lattice with cutoff Λ = π/a and resolution Δp = π/L. They assume left-moving particles are not dynamically important when the reference frame velocity is close to the speed of light, and diagonalize the Hamiltonian in sectors with momentum P=0 (for vacuum energy) and P≠0 (for physical energies). The mass spectrum is obtained from M_n = sqrt(E_n² - P²) after subtracting the vacuum energy.

## Key numbers
- Vector mass expansion coefficients from chiral perturbation theory: A = 2e^γ ≈ 3.5621, B ≈ 5.4807, C ≈ 2.0933, where (M_v/M^(0))² = 1 + A cos(θ)(m/M^(0)) + [B - C cos(2θ)](m/M^(0))²
- Fitted coefficients from lattice data: A = 0.56, B = 1.74, C = 0.22
- Chiral condensate coefficient a = e^γ/(2π) ≈ 0.2835, b ≈ 0.7825, c ≈ 0.7163
- Extracted condensate from slope: <ψ̅ψ>/g = 0.16 cos(θ), compared to exact massless result <ψ̅ψ>/g = e^γ/(2π√π) cos(θ) ≈ 0.1599 cos(θ)
- Lattice sizes used range from N=6 to N=24 for θ-angle studies, and up to N≈200 for non-relativistic distribution functions

## Context
This work extends Hamiltonian lattice methods previously applied to scalar φ⁴ theory in 3+1 dimensions (Kröger, 1997) to the massive Schwinger model in 1+1 dimensions. The results agree with chiral perturbation theory calculations by Adam (1996) and Vary et al. (1996) in the ultra-relativistic regime, with finite lattice results by Hamer et al. (1997), and with light-cone quantization results by Mo and Perry (1993). The θ-angle dependence is compared with Manton's (1985) analytic work on the circle and with standard lattice gauge theory results by Hamer et al. (1982). The distribution functions are compared with Bergknoff's (1977) variational study in the infinite momentum frame. The method enables further study of other θ-angles and higher-lying states with increased computational effort.
