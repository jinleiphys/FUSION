# Hadron Formation From Quark-Gluon Plasma Using Lattice QCD At Finite Temperature

> Recently we have reported the correct formulation of the lattice QCD method at the zero temperature to study the hadron formation from the quarks and gluons by incorporating the non-zero boundary surface term in QCD which arises due to the confinement of quarks and gluons inside the finite size hadron. In this paper we extend this to the finite temperature QCD and present the correct formulation of the lattice QCD method at the finite temperature to study the hadron formation from the quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The paper claims to present the correct formulation of lattice QCD at finite temperature for studying hadron formation from the quark-gluon plasma, but only if one incorporates the non-zero boundary surface term (non-zero energy flux) in QCD that arises from confinement of quarks and gluons inside a finite-size hadron. The abstract glosses over the fact that the hadron is actually formed in vacuum, not inside the quark-gluon plasma medium, and that the finite-temperature correlation function must include an extra factor of \(e^{-t'H}\) compared to the zero-temperature case.

## Method

The author extends the previously reported zero-temperature lattice QCD formulation to finite temperature by constructing the non-perturbative correlation function \(C_2^{QGP}(t',r') = \langle in| e^{-t'H} \hat{\mathcal{O}}^H(t',r') \hat{\mathcal{O}}^H(0) |in \rangle\) for a partonic operator \(\hat{\mathcal{O}}^H\) carrying the hadron's quantum numbers. The generating functional at finite temperature is written with periodic boundary conditions for quark and gluon fields over the Euclidean time interval \(0\) to \(1/T\). The hadron mass \(M^H\) and the hadronic decay matrix element are extracted by taking the Euclidean time \(t' \to \infty\) limit, using the non-zero boundary surface term from the zero-temperature QCD energy-momentum tensor to relate the parton energy inside the hadron to the physical hadron mass.

## Key numbers

- The temperature of the quark-gluon plasma is \(\ge 200\) MeV (stated in the introduction).
- The RHIC experiment collides Au-Au at total center-of-mass energy \(\sqrt{s} = 200 \times 197\) GeV.
- The LHC experiment collides Pb-Pb at total center-of-mass energy \(\sqrt{s}_{NN} = 5.2 \times 208\) TeV.
- The Euclidean time interval for the finite-temperature path integral is \(0\) to \(1/T\), where \(T\) is the temperature.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier zero-temperature lattice QCD formulation (Nayak, 2018) that incorporated the non-zero boundary surface term in QCD due to confinement inside a finite-size hadron. It contrasts with standard lattice QCD approaches that operate the unphysical QCD Hamiltonian of all partons inside the hadron on the physical energy eigenstate of the hadron, which the author argues is incorrect. The paper enables future lattice QCD calculations of the probability \(|\langle in|\hat{\mathcal{O}}^H(0)|H\rangle|^2\) for partons in the quark-gluon plasma to form a specific hadron \(H\), using a combination of finite-temperature and zero-temperature lattice QCD correlation functions.
