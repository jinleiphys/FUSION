# Simultaneous Microscopic Description of 16O(γ,n) and 16O(γ,p) Including Delta Degrees of Freedom

## Key claim
The inclusion of Δ(1232) isobar degrees of freedom slightly improves the description of photonuclear data on 16O, but complete quantitative agreement is still lacking; the conventional many-body calculation involving only nucleonic degrees of freedom describes the available data remarkably well, with Δ effects being small except at very large momentum transfer.

## Method
The authors generalized the microscopic continuum shell model treatment of Buck and Hill (1984) to simultaneously describe 16O(γ,n)15O and 16O(γ,p)15N within a coupled channels framework. They diagonalized the many-body Hamiltonian for 16O in a model space spanned by products of continuum single-particle states and mass A=15 bound states, using a realistic two-body, finite-range nucleon-nucleon interaction with spin, isospin, and tensor components, and rigorously included all non-localities from antisymmetrization. Δ isobars were incorporated by expanding the model space to include Δ components in the scattering wavefunction, with N-Δ and Δ-Δ interaction potentials taken from Niephaus, Gari, and Sommer (1986) involving π and ρ meson exchange.

## Key numbers
- The Δ(1232) magnetic moment was taken as 4.0 μ_N, consistent with the experimentally determined value.
- The regularization parameter Λ was taken as 1.2 GeV, within the usually quoted range, and also tested at 3.0 GeV to demonstrate sensitivity.
- Electric multipoles through E17 and magnetic multipoles through M15 were included to ensure convergence of the multipole expansion.
- The f²_ΔNρ/(4π) coupling constant was taken as 9.13, though noted to be not well known and potentially considerably smaller.
- The calculation covered photon energies below 400 MeV.

## Context
This work extends earlier separate investigations of nucleon-nucleon correlations (Schoch, 1980; Gori, 1985), relativistic equations of motion (McDonald, 1985; Lotz, 1990; Boff, 1987), meson exchange currents (Gari, 1985; Cavinato, 1985; Ryckebusch, 1988), and Δ(1232) resonance excitation (Londergan, 1980; Cheung, 1985) that had only semi-quantitatively described (γ,p) data and failed to describe (γ,n) data qualitatively. The authors found that their conventional model parameters, determined at lower energies by fitting the 16O(γ,p) giant dipole resonance, produced predictions for medium-energy (γ,N) reactions that described both channels simultaneously, unlike prior work. The results enable future studies incorporating more sophisticated short-ranged N-N and N-Δ interactions and meson exchange currents, which the authors note are needed to resolve model-dependent conclusions about exchange current effects.
