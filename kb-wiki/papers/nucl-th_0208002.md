# Differential Flow of Protons in Au+Au Collisions at AGS Energies

> We study the proton sideward and elliptic differential flow for Au+Au collisions at AGS energies (2 -- 8 A cdot GeV) in a microscopic relativistic transport model that includes all baryon resonances up to a mass of 2 GeV as well as string degrees of freedom for the higher hadronic excitations. In order to explore the sensitivity of the various differential flows to the nuclear equation of state (EoS) we use three different parameterizations of the scalar- and vector mean-fields, i.e. NL2 (soft), NL23 (medium) and NL3 (hard), with their momentum dependence fitted to the experimental Schrodinger equivalent potential (at normal nuclear matter density rho_0) up to kinetic energies of 1 GeV. We calculate the excitation function of sideward and elliptic flow within these parameter sets for Au+Au collisions and compare with the recent data from the E895 Collaboration as a function of rapidity, impact parameter and transverse momentum, respectively. We find that the best description of the differential data is provided by a rather 'stiff' EoS at 2 A cdot GeV (NL3) while at higher bombarding energies (4--8 A cdot GeV) a 'medium' EoS leads to the lowest chi^2 with respect to the data. However, the differences in the transverse and elliptic flows (from the different parameter sets) become of minor significance at 4--8 A cdot GeV. We attribute this insensitivity to a similar reduction of the vector potential in all models and to the dominance of string degrees of freedom at these bombarding energies.

# Differential Flow of Protons in Au+Au Collisions at AGS Energies

## Key claim

The best description of differential flow data for Au+Au collisions is provided by a "stiff" equation of state (NL3) at 2 A·GeV, while at higher bombarding energies (4–8 A·GeV) a "medium" equation of state (NL23) yields the lowest chi-squared values; however, the differences between parameter sets become of minor significance at 4–8 A·GeV, and the authors do not find convincing indications for a softening of the equation of state at higher energies contrary to prior suggestions.

## Method

The authors employed a microscopic relativistic transport model (based on the Hadron-String-Dynamics approach) that includes all baryon resonances up to 2 GeV mass and string degrees of freedom for higher hadronic excitations. They used three parameterizations of scalar and vector mean fields, NL2 (soft, K=210 MeV), NL23 (medium, K=300 MeV), and NL3 (hard, K=380 MeV), with momentum-dependent form factors fitted to the experimental Schrodinger equivalent potential at normal nuclear matter density up to 1 GeV kinetic energy. The sideward flow and elliptic flow were calculated for Au+Au collisions at 2–8 A·GeV and compared to E895 Collaboration data as functions of rapidity, impact parameter, and transverse momentum.

## Key numbers

- Incompressibilities of the three equations of state: K = 210 MeV (NL2, soft), K = 300 MeV (NL23, medium), and K = 380 MeV (NL3, hard)
- At 2 A·GeV, only the NL3 (stiff) parameter set describes the large negative elliptic flow as a function of impact parameter correctly; other parameter sets yield values too low in v2
- At 4 A·GeV with a transverse momentum cut of pt >= 0.7 GeV/c, the soft EoS (NL2) gives the wrong sign of v2, while the best chi-squared is provided by NL23 (medium)
- At 6 A·GeV with a pt cut of 1 GeV/c, all parameter sets are compatible with data within experimental error bars; chi-squared analysis gives slight preference for NL23 (medium) followed by NL2 (soft), with higher chi-squared for NL3 (stiff)
- The form factor parameters for NL2: Lambda_s = 1.05 GeV, Lambda_v = 1.52 GeV, a = 1/3, b = 1/4; for NL23: Lambda_s = 1.0 GeV, Lambda_v = 1.35 GeV, a = 1/3, b = 1/4; for NL3: Lambda_s = 1.0 GeV, Lambda_v = 0.9 GeV, a = 1/2, b = 1/6
- Normal nuclear matter density used: rho_0 = 0.168 fm^-3

## Context

This work extends prior studies by Sahu and Cassing (2000) that examined sideward and elliptic flow at AGS energies using only a stiff equation of state, and by Danielewicz (1998) who suggested that elliptic flow is sensitive to the stiffness of the equation of state and possibly to quark-gluon plasma formation. The present study uses differential flow data from the E895 Collaboration to test three equations of state systematically, finding that the sensitivity to stiffness decreases at higher bombarding energies due to similar vector potential reduction across models and dominance of string degrees of freedom above 4 A·GeV. The results enable future comparisons with electromagnetic probes (hard photons, dileptons) at the planned GSI upgrade to resolve whether a new state of matter is present.
