# Nuclear Mean Fields through Selfconsistent Semiclassical Calculations

> Semiclassical expansions derived in the framework of the Extended Thomas-Fermi approach for the kinetic energy density tau(r) and the spin-orbit density J(r) as functions of the local density rho(r) are used to determine the central nuclear potentials V_n(r) and V_p(r) of the neutron and proton distribution for effective interactions of the Skyrme type. We demonstrate that the convergence of the resulting semiclassical expansions for these potentials is fast and that they reproduce quite accurately the corresponding Hartree-Fock average fields.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that semiclassical expansions of the kinetic energy density and spin-orbit density, derived within the Extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) approach up to order \(\hbar^4\), converge rapidly and reproduce the corresponding Hartree-Fock average central potentials accurately, but only when the ETF functionals are used with selfconsistent semiclassical densities of the liquid-drop type, not with arbitrary densities.

## Method

The authors used the ETF method to derive semiclassical functionals for the kinetic energy density \(\tau_q[\rho_q]\) and the spin-orbit density \(\vec{J}_q[\rho_q]\) up to order \(\hbar^4\), including contributions from effective mass and spin-orbit coupling, based on the work of Grammaticos and Voros (1979, 1980). They then performed density variational calculations restricted to modified Fermi functions for spherical nuclei, using Skyrme interactions (SkM*, SIII, SLy4), and compared the resulting semiclassical central potentials, effective masses, and spin-orbit potentials to the corresponding Hartree-Fock results for nuclei such as \({}^{208}\)Pb.

## Key numbers

* The Thomas-Fermi contribution to the kinetic energy density scales as \(\rho_q^{5/3}\), the second-order correction scales as \(\rho_q\), and the fourth-order correction scales as \(\rho_q^{1/3}\); a hypothetical sixth-order term would scale as \(\rho_q^{-1/3}\) and diverge for exponentially decaying densities at large distances.
* For \({}^{208}\)Pb calculated with the SkM* Skyrme force, the second-order correction \(\tau_q^{(2)}\) is approximately one order of magnitude larger than the fourth-order correction \(\tau_q^{(4)}\) in the nuclear surface, but after integration the second-order and fourth-order contributions to the total energy are of comparable magnitude.
* The ETF densities obtained with modified Fermi functions (with parameters \(\rho_{0_q}\), \(R_{0_q}\), \(a_q\), \(\gamma_q\)) reproduce the HF densities for \({}^{208}\)Pb with SkM* to a similarly good quality as for other nuclei and interactions (SIII, SLy4).

## Context

This work extends the ETF approach, previously shown by Bartel et al. (1985) to accurately describe average nuclear properties in the sense of the Bethe-Weizsacker mass formula, from integrated quantities (total energy) to local quantities such as nuclear mean-field potentials, effective mass, and spin-orbit form factors. It provides a systematic test of the convergence of the semiclassical expansions for these local quantities, which had not been extensively investigated before. The results enable future applications to excited and rotating nuclei, where time-reversal symmetry is broken, as well as the use of these semiclassical potentials in nuclear reaction calculations.
