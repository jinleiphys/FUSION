# Determination of nuclear parton distribution functions and their uncertainties at next-to-leading order

> Nuclear parton distribution functions (NPDFs) are determined by global analyses of experimental data on structure-function ratios F_2^A/F_2^{A'} and Drell-Yan cross-section ratios σ_{DY}^A/σ_{DY}^{A'}. The analyses are done in the leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO) of running coupling constant α_s. Uncertainties of the NPDFs are estimated in both LO and NLO for finding possible NLO improvement. Valence-quark distributions are well determined, and antiquark distributions are also determined at x<0.1. However, the antiquark distributions have large uncertainties at x>0.2. Gluon modifications cannot be fixed at this stage. Although the advantage of the NLO analysis, in comparison with the LO one, is generally the sensitivity to the gluon distributions, gluon uncertainties are almost the same in the LO and NLO. It is because current scaling-violation data are not accurate enough to determine precise nuclear gluon distributions. Modifications of the PDFs in the deuteron are also discussed by including data on the proton-deuteron ratio F_2^D/F_2^p in the analysis. A code is provided for calculating the NPDFs and their uncertainties at given x and Q^2 in the LO and NLO.

## Key claim

The paper determines nuclear parton distribution functions (NPDFs) and their uncertainties at both leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO) in \(\alpha_s\), but finds that gluon modifications cannot be fixed at this stage and that gluon uncertainties are almost the same in LO and NLO because current scaling-violation data are not accurate enough to determine precise nuclear gluon distributions.

## Method

The authors perform a global \(\chi^2\) analysis of experimental data on structure-function ratios \(F_2^A/F_2^{A'}\) and Drell-Yan cross-section ratios \(\sigma_{DY}^A/\sigma_{DY}^{A'}\) for nuclear targets, using the MRST 1998 nucleonic PDFs as a baseline and parametrizing nuclear modifications via weight functions \(w_i(x,A,Z)\) with twelve free parameters. The NPDFs are evolved from an initial scale \(Q_0^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\) to experimental \(Q^2\) points using DGLAP evolution equations, and uncertainties are estimated via the Hessian method with \(\Delta\chi^2 = 13.7\) for the twelve-parameter fit.

## Key numbers

- Total \(\chi^2\)/d.o.f. = 1.35 for LO (1653.3/1228) and 1.21 for NLO (1485.9/1228), based on 1241 data points.
- For valence quarks in LO: \(x_{0v}^+ = 0.675 \pm 0.005\), \(x_{0v}^- = 0.208 \pm 0.004\), \(d_v^{(1)} = 5.089 \pm 0.315\), \(d_v^{(2)} = 0.876 \pm 0.143\).
- For valence quarks in NLO: \(x_{0v}^+ = 0.629 \pm 0.004\), \(x_{0v}^- = 0.178 \pm 0.004\), \(d_v^{(1)} = 6.157 \pm 0.254\), \(d_v^{(2)} = 1.063 \pm 0.143\).
- For antiquarks in LO: \(x_{0\bar q}^+ = 0.120 \pm 0.007\), \(x_{0\bar q}^- = 0.387 \pm 0.027\), \(a_{\bar q}^{(1)} = -0.394 \pm 0.027\), \(a_{\bar q}^{(2)} = 0.318 \pm 0.038\).
- For antiquarks in NLO: \(x_{0\bar q}^+ = 0.239 \pm 0.009\), \(x_{0\bar q}^- = 0.119 \pm 0.004\), \(a_{\bar q}^{(1)} = -0.540 \pm 0.024\), \(a_{\bar q}^{(2)} = 0.295 \pm 0.024\).
- For gluons in LO: \(x_{0g}^+ = 8.35 \pm 9.09\), \(d_g^{(1)} = -799 \pm 1561\), \(d_g^{(2)} = (0.194 \pm 0.341) \times 10^{-5}\).
- For gluons in NLO: \(x_{0g}^+ = 7.24 \pm 3.97\), \(d_g^{(1)} = -26.5 \pm 27.8\), \(d_g^{(2)} = (0.109 \pm 0.114) \times 10^{-3}\).
- The deuteron-proton ratio \(F_2^D/F_2^p\) contributes 290 data points with \(\chi^2 = 375.5\) (LO) and 322.5 (NLO).

## Context

This work extends the previous LO analyses of Hirai, Kumano, and Nagai (2001, 2004) by performing the first NLO global fit with full uncertainty estimation, and by including deuteron-proton ratio data to constrain nuclear modifications in the deuteron. It improves upon the earlier parametrization of Eskola, Kolhinen, and Salgado (1998-1999) which assigned parameter values by hand, and upon the NLO analysis of de Florian and Sassot (2004) by providing uncertainty bands. The results enable more precise calculations of cross sections for heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, and for neutrino oscillation experiments, though the large gluon uncertainties indicate that future scaling-violation data from nuclear DIS at small \(x\) are needed to constrain gluon modifications.
