# Characteristic Feature of Self-Consistent Mean-Field in Level Crossing Region

> A shape change of the self-consistent mean-field induced by a configuration change is discussed within the conventional constrained Hartree-Fock (CHF) theory. It is stressed that a single-particle level crossing dynamics should be treated carefully, because the shape of the mean-field in such a finite many-body system as the nucleus strongly changes depending on its configuration. This situation is clearly shown by applying an adiabatic assumption, where the most energetically favorable single-particle states are assumed to be occupied. The excited HF states and the continuously-connected potential energy curves are given by applying the configuration dictated CHF method. The effect of pairing correlation is discussed in the level crossing region. Triaxial deformed results in our Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculation with Gogny force nicely reproduce the available experimental data of Ge isotopes. From our numerical calculation, it is concluded that the CHFB state is more fragile than the CHF state in the level crossing region.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the shape of the self-consistent mean-field in a nucleus changes drastically at a single-particle level crossing, and that this change is driven not only by the direct occupation swap but also by a large rearrangement of the mean-field itself. The authors conclude that the CHFB state is more fragile than the CHF state in the level crossing region, meaning that the pairing correlation makes it difficult to define a continuous configuration-dependent potential energy surface through the crossing.

## Method

The authors solve the constrained Hartree-Fock (CHF) and constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (CHFB) equations using the Gogny D1S interaction in a three-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis up to principal quantum number N0=8 (and N0=12 for comparison). They compare two approaches: the conventional adiabatic method, which occupies the lowest single-particle states at each deformation, and the configuration dictated method, which enforces a maximum overlap criterion (overlap squared > 0.9) between occupied orbitals at successive deformation steps to preserve the identity of a given configuration. The configuration dictated method is also extended via a reference state (RS) method to obtain excited HF states by starting from a specified np-nh excitation and iterating while maintaining the orbital ordering of the reference state.

## Key numbers

* For the gap A in 70Ge, the total quadrupole moment change over an interval Δμ = 10.0 fm² is Δq = 65.50 fm², with proton contribution Δq_p = 20.08 fm² and neutron contribution Δq_n = 45.42 fm².
* The smooth contribution to the quadrupole change at gap A (no level crossing) is only Δq^(1) = 5.1325 fm², which is 11.25% of Δq_p and 6.33% of Δq_n.
* The direct configuration-change contribution at gap A is Δq_n^(2) ≈ 36.32 fm² (79.96% of the neutron effect), while the mean-field rearrangement contributes Δq_p^(3) ≈ 17.82 fm² (88.75% of Δq_p) and Δq_n^(3) ≈ 6.23 fm² (13.71% of Δq_n).
* For gap B in 70Ge, the mean-field rearrangement contributes Δq_p^(3) = 17.54 fm² (63.00% of Δq_p) and Δq_n^(3) = 3.4 fm² (4.40% of Δq_n).
* For gap C in 70Ge, the direct configuration-change contribution to Δq_p is 28.1 fm² (62.23%), and the mean-field rearrangement contributes Δq_p^(3) = 15.9 fm² (35.27%) and Δq_n^(3) = 11.12 fm² (88.54%).
* The ground state of 70Ge has configuration [12 0, 16 2] with β2 = 0.24; the first excited state (configuration [12 0, 18 0]) has excitation energy ≈ 1.1 MeV and β2 = 0.17; the second excited state (configuration [12 0, 14 4]) has excitation energy ≈ 2.7 MeV and β2 = 0.35; the fifth excited state has β2 = 0.42.
* Including pairing correlation increases the binding energy by 0.59 to 1.34 MeV for Ge isotopes, but does not substantially change β2 or γ deformations.

## Context

This work extends the configuration dictated CHF method previously proposed by the same group (Guo, Sakata, and Zhao, 2004) to study how the mean-field changes continuously as a function of deformation, in contrast to the conventional adiabatic approach which produces discontinuous potential energy surfaces at level crossings. The results for Ge isotopes are compared with earlier deformed mean-field calculations by Nazarewicz and collaborators (1992) and shell model calculations by Kaneko and collaborators (2003). The paper shows that the pairing correlation, while not decisive for ground state deformations, makes the CHFB state more fragile than the CHF state in the level crossing region, preventing the construction of continuous configuration-dependent potential energy surfaces. This finding raises questions about the applicability of the configuration dictated method when pairing is present and suggests that a more sophisticated treatment of level crossing dynamics is needed for superfluid nuclei.
