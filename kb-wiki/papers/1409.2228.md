# Defining medium-specific condensates in QCD sum rules for $D$ and $B$ mesons

> QCD sum rules for $D$ and $B$ mesons in a strongly interacting environment require the decomposition of Gibbs averaged operators related to tensor structures. We present a procedure to decompose these operators into vacuum and medium-specific parts, thus defining plain medium-specific condensates with coefficients vanishing in vacuum. Our decomposition allows for an identification of potential elements of order parameters for chiral restoration, in particular for higher mass-dimension quark-condensates which would be masked otherwise if operators with uncontracted Lorentz indices would be linked to DIS amplitudes.

## Key claim

The paper presents a formal decomposition procedure for Gibbs-averaged operators in QCD sum rules for \(D\) and \(B\) mesons that cleanly separates vacuum and medium-specific condensates, with the medium-specific coefficients vanishing by construction when the medium is turned off. This decomposition is necessary because higher mass-dimension quark condensates that could serve as order parameters for chiral restoration would otherwise be masked if operators with uncontracted Lorentz indices were directly linked to DIS amplitudes.

## Method

The authors develop a four-step projection method to decompose Gibbs-averaged operators carrying multiple Lorentz indices into vacuum tensor structures (built from \(g_{\mu\nu}\) and \(\varepsilon_{\mu\nu\lambda\sigma}\)) and medium-specific tensor structures (incorporating the medium four-velocity \(v_\mu\)). They enforce orthogonality between the vacuum and medium-specific basis tensors, which yields linear constraints on the decomposition coefficients and allows unambiguous identification of medium-specific condensates. The method is worked out explicitly for operators with four Lorentz indices, and applied to a specific four-quark condensate contribution in the OPE for pseudo-scalar \(D\) and \(B\) mesons.

## Key numbers

* The vacuum projection matrix for four-index operators has inverse elements \(\frac{5}{72}\), \(-\frac{1}{72}\), and \(-\frac{1}{72}\) in the diagonal and off-diagonal positions respectively.
* The medium-specific projection matrix for four-index operators has inverse elements including \(\frac{7}{240}\), \(-\frac{1}{2}\) scaled by \(\frac{1}{240}\), and \(\frac{4}{3}\) scaled by \(\frac{1}{240}\) for the last diagonal entry.
* The Borel-transformed OPE contribution from the four-quark condensate \(\langle \bar{q} \gamma_\mu [D_\nu, G_{\lambda\sigma}] q \rangle\) is evaluated at Borel masses around 1 GeV, showing a significant difference between vacuum and saturation density.
* The factorized two-quark condensate expressions use the sigma term \(\sigma_N\), pion mass \(m_\pi\), and pion decay constant \(f_\pi\) with linear nuclear density \(\rho\) approximation.

## Context

This work extends the in-medium QCD sum rule formalism previously applied to \(\rho\) mesons by Hatsuda and Lee (1992) and Leupold (1998) to the case of heavy-light mesons (\(D\) and \(B\)), where higher mass-dimension condensates become important. The decomposition method enables systematic inclusion of four-quark condensates up to mass dimension 6 in the OPE, which Hilger (2009) had begun for pseudo-scalar \(D\) mesons. The approach prepares the ground for evaluating QCD sum rules for charm and bottom mesons at FAIR experiments, where four-quark condensates may serve as order parameters for chiral restoration, as discussed by Hilger (2012) and Hohler and Rapp (2014).
