# Microscopic investigation of wobbling motion in atomic nuclei using the triaxial projected shell model approach

> A systematic investigation of the wobbling band structures observed in odd-mass nuclei of $^{161,163,165,167}$Lu, $^{167}$Ta $^{131}$Cs, $^{135}$Pr, $^{151}$Eu, $^{183}$Au, $^{133}$Ba, $^{105}$Pd, $^{133}$La, $^{187}$Au and $^{127}$Xe is performed using the triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) approach. It is demonstrated that all the studied band structures have transverse wobbling mode, except for $^{133}$La, $^{187}$Au (negative parity), $^{183}$Au (positive parity) and $^{127}$Xe nuclei where the wobbling frequency increases with spin, indicating that the collective motion has a longitudinal character. To elucidate further the wobbling nature of the band structures, electromagnetic transition probabilities have been evaluated and it is observed that inter-band transitions are dominated by $E2$ rather than $M1$ as expected for a typical signature partner band. It is shown that TPSM approach provides a reasonable description of all the measured properties of the studied nuclei.

# Microscopic investigation of wobbling motion in atomic nuclei using the triaxial projected shell model approach

## Key claim

The triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) approach provides a reasonable description of all measured properties of wobbling band structures in 13 odd-mass nuclei across multiple mass regions, with the majority exhibiting transverse wobbling (wobbling frequency decreasing with spin) except for 133La, 187Au (negative parity), 183Au (positive parity), and 127Xe, where the wobbling frequency increases with spin indicating longitudinal character. The classification into transverse versus longitudinal wobbling depends on whether the odd particle occupies a low-K orbital of a high-j intruder orbital (transverse) or a high-K orbital (longitudinal).

## Method

The authors performed triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) calculations for 13 odd-mass nuclei, using deformed intrinsic states from the triaxial Nilsson potential with axial deformation parameters (epsilon) ranging from 0.140 to 0.430 and triaxial deformation parameters (epsilon-prime) of 0.100 or 0.110. The basis included multi-quasiparticle states from three major harmonic oscillator shells for neutrons and protons, with rotational symmetry restored through three-dimensional angular-momentum projection, followed by diagonalization of a Hamiltonian containing quadrupole-quadrupole, monopole pairing, and quadrupole pairing terms. Electromagnetic transition probabilities were evaluated from the resulting wave functions to distinguish wobbling bands from signature partner bands.

## Key numbers

- Axial deformation parameters used: epsilon = 0.400 for 161Lu, 163Lu; 0.380 for 165Lu; 0.430 for 167Lu; 0.370 for 167Ta; 0.257 for 105Pd; 0.150 for 127Xe, 133Ba, 133La; 0.140 for 131Cs; 0.160 for 135Pr; 0.200 for 151Eu; 0.280 for 183Au; 0.220 for 187Au
- Triaxial deformation parameters: epsilon-prime = 0.110 for 161Lu, 165Lu, 167Lu, 105Pd, 133La, 183Au, 187Au; 0.100 for 163Lu, 167Ta, 127Xe, 131Cs, 133Ba, 135Pr, 151Eu
- Gamma values derived from deformations range from 14 degrees (163Lu, 167Lu) to 36 degrees (131Cs, 133La)
- For 163Lu, the mixing ratio for inter-band transitions was measured as 90.6% E2 and 9.4% M1, with an uncertainty of plus or minus 1.3% on each component
- The wobbling frequency in 183Au initially decreases with spin and then after some spin values increases, contrary to the pure transverse wobbling pattern seen in most other nuclei
- For 167Ta, the TSD1 band shows a bandcrossing feature with J(2) peaking at about hbar-omega = 0.35 MeV

## Context

This work extends the TPSM approach, previously applied to chiral band structures and gamma-vibrational bands, to a systematic survey of wobbling bands across multiple mass regions (A~160, A~130, A~100, A~190). The study builds on the original wobbling identification in 163Lu by Odegard et al. (2001) and the particle-rotor model predictions by Hamamoto (2001) and Frauendorf and Donau (2004) distinguishing transverse from longitudinal wobbling. The results enable future microscopic investigations of wobbling in even-even nuclei such as 130Ba and 136Nd, where two-quasiparticle wobbling bands have recently been reported.
