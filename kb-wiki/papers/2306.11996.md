# Structure of Heavy Nuclei Based on Nucleon Quartets

> Several topics concerning nuclear structure and electromagnetic interactions of heavy nuclei are reviewed. These comprehend the deformed single-particle shell model, nuclear collective motion, symmetry breaking and approximate symmetry restoring in atomic nuclei, particularly the proxy-SU(3) scheme. The relations between these theoretical frameworks are stated and it is shown how they contribute to a better and broader understanding of atomic nuclei. The semi-microscopic algebraic quartet model of atomic nuclei in the proxy-SU(3) scheme is applied to obtain previously unknown irreducible representations, deformation parameters, nuclear excited states and electromagnetic transition probabilities for certain heavy isotopes. The experimental data available for such nuclear region is currently very poor, so in preparation for future experimental efforts the theoretical background is developed along with some perspectives to work on are proposed.

## Key claim

The paper applies the semi-microscopic algebraic quartet model in the proxy-SU(3) scheme to obtain previously unknown irreducible representations, deformation parameters, nuclear excited states, and electromagnetic transition probabilities for certain heavy isotopes, but the experimental data for this nuclear region is currently very poor, so the work is presented as theoretical preparation for future experimental efforts rather than as a definitive prediction.

## Method

The author reviews the deformed single-particle shell model, nuclear collective motion, symmetry breaking and approximate symmetry restoring in atomic nuclei, particularly the proxy-SU(3) scheme, and states the relations between these theoretical frameworks. The semi-microscopic algebraic quartet model is then applied using the Racah form of U(N) algebra and SU(3) tensor operators to compute irreducible representations, deformation parameters, and electromagnetic transition probabilities for heavy isotopes. The computational tools used include Schur, UNTOU3, UNtoU3, and SU3lib software for calculating Lie group properties, branching problems, and SU(3) reduced Wigner coefficients.

## Key numbers

* The Woods-Saxon potential well depth V0 is in the range 40-50 MeV, with nuclear surface thickness a ≈ 0.5 fm and nuclear radius approximation R = r0 A^(1/3) with r0 ≈ 1.25 fm.
* The isotropic harmonic oscillator energy is given by ℏω = 41 A^(-1/3) MeV or a more refined ℏω = 45 A^(-1/3) - 25 A^(-2/3) MeV.
* For the isotope 220Ra with core 208Pb and three quartets, the 0ℏω ground state leader irrep of U(21) ⊃ SU(3)_π contained in [2^3]_π is (24,0)_π, and for U(28) ⊃ SU(3)_ν the leader irrep contained in [2^3]_ν is (30,0)_ν.
* The coupling (24,0)_π ⊗ (30,0)_ν yields SU(3)_{π+ν} irreps including (54,0), (52,1), (50,2), (48,3), (46,4), (44,5), (42,6), and others.
* For 1ℏω excitations in 220Ra, the total model space after deleting spurious states includes (56,1)^2, (54,2)^2, (55,0), (52,3)^2, (50,4)^2, (48,5)^2, (46,6)^2, and others.
* For 2ℏω excitations in 220Ra, the model space after spurious state deletion includes (58,2)^3, (59,0), (56,3)^3, (57,1)^2, (54,4)^3, (52,5)^3, (50,6)^3, and others.

## Context

This work extends the algebraic quartet model framework for heavy nuclei by applying the proxy-SU(3) scheme to obtain previously unknown structure predictions for isotopes where experimental data is scarce. The author builds upon the deformed single-particle shell model, collective nuclear models, and prior work on SU(3) symmetry in nuclei by Iachello (2014), Kota (2020), and others, while also referencing the computational tools of Draayer (1989) and Dytrych (2021). The results enable future experimental measurements of electromagnetic transitions and excited states in heavy isotopes such as 220Ra and 224Th, for which the theoretical framework and model spaces are now established.
