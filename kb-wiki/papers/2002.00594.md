# Effects of dark matter on the nuclear and neutron star matter

> We study the dark matter effects on the nuclear matter parameters characterising the equation of states of super dense neutron-rich nucleonic-matter. The observables of the nuclear matter, i.e. incompressibility, symmetry energy and its higher-order derivatives in the presence dark matter for symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter are analysed with the help of an extended relativistic mean-field model. The calculations are also extended to beta-stable matter to explore the properties of the neutron star. We analyse the dark matter effects on symmetric nuclear matter, pure neutron matter and neutron star using NL3, G3 and IOPB-I forces. The binding energy per particle and pressure are calculated with and without considering the dark matter interaction with the nuclear matter systems. The influences of dark matter are also analysed on the symmetry energy and its different coefficients. The incompressibility and the skewness parameters are affected considerably due to the presence of dark matter in the nuclear matter medium. We extend the calculations to the neutron star and find its mass, radius and the moment of inertia for static and rotating neutron star with and without dark matter contribution. The mass of the rotating neutron star is considerably changing due to rapid rotation with the frequency in the mass-shedding limit. The effects of dark matter are found to be important for some of the nuclear matter parameters, which are crucial for the properties of astrophysical objects.

## Key claim

Dark matter (DM) effects on nuclear matter parameters such as incompressibility, symmetry energy, and its higher-order derivatives are studied using an extended relativistic mean-field (E-RMF) model, but the DM does not affect the incompressibility or pressure of symmetric or pure neutron matter; the influence is confined to certain nuclear matter parameters and depends on the DM Fermi momentum, which is varied from 0 to 0.06 GeV.

## Method

The authors use an extended relativistic mean-field (E-RMF) model with three parameter sets (NL3, G3, and IOPB-I) to compute the equation of state of nucleonic matter with and without fermionic dark matter (Neutralino, mass 200 GeV) interacting via the Higgs portal (Higgs mass 125 GeV, coupling y = 0.07, form factor f = 0.35). The total energy density and pressure are obtained by adding DM contributions to the nucleonic E-RMF results, and the DM Fermi momentum is varied from 0 to 0.06 GeV. For neutron star properties, the beta-equilibrium and charge-neutrality conditions are imposed, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations are solved for static stars, while the RNS code is used for rotating stars at the Keplerian frequency.

## Key numbers

- Saturation incompressibility K_infinity for symmetric nuclear matter: 271.38 MeV (NL3), 243.96 MeV (G3), and 222.65 MeV (IOPB-I); these values are unaffected by dark matter.
- DM Fermi momentum varied from 0 to 0.06 GeV; the DM density inside the neutron star is taken such that M_chi/M = 1/6, yielding k_f^DM approximately 0.03 GeV.
- Neutralino DM mass M_chi = 200 GeV; Higgs mass M_h = 125 GeV; Higgs vacuum expectation value v = 246 GeV; DM-nucleon coupling y = 0.07; proton-Higgs form factor f = 0.35.
- The Higgs field contribution to the effective nucleon mass is very small, of order 10^-6 to 10^-8, even at maximum k_f^DM.
- The Keplerian frequency of rotating neutron stars is calculated using the empirical formula with chi = 1.08 kHz.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dark matter effects on neutron stars by Panotopoulos et al. (2017), Das et al. (2019), and Quddus et al. (2019), who used simpler linear sigma-omega models or single parameter sets. The present paper uses three non-linear E-RMF parameter sets (NL3, G3, IOPB-I) to provide a wider platform for analyzing DM effects on both nuclear matter parameters and neutron star observables (mass, radius, moment of inertia) for static and rotating configurations. The results are relevant for interpreting constraints from the GW170817 neutron star merger and the NICER mission measurements of neutron star mass and radius.
