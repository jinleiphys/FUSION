# Charmed signatures for phase transitions in heavy-ion collisions

> The interplay of charmonium production and suppression in In+In and Pb+Pb reactions at 158 AGeV and in Au+Au reactions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV is investigated with the HSD transport approach within the `hadronic comover model' and the `QGP melting scenario'. The results for the J/Psi suppression and the Psi' to J/Psi ratio are compared to the recent data of the NA50, NA60, and PHENIX Collaborations. We find that, at 158 AGeV, the comover absorption model performs better than the scenario of abrupt threshold melting. However, neither interaction with hadrons alone nor simple color screening satisfactory describes the data at sqrt(s)=200 GeV. A deconfined phase is clearly reached at RHIC, but a theory having the relevant degrees of freedom in this regime (strongly interacting quarks/gluons) is needed to study its transport properties.

## Key claim

The hadronic comover absorption model performs better than the abrupt QGP threshold melting scenario at SPS energies (158 AGeV), but neither model satisfactorily describes the J/Psi suppression data at RHIC (sqrt(s)=200 GeV), indicating that a deconfined phase is reached at RHIC but its transport properties require a theory with strongly interacting quark/gluon degrees of freedom.

## Method

The authors used the HSD (Hadron-String Dynamics) transport approach to simulate charmonium production and suppression in In+In and Pb+Pb collisions at 158 AGeV and Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV. They implemented two competing mechanisms: the "hadronic comover model" with dissociation and reformation channels (including D+anti-D recombination) and the "QGP threshold melting scenario" where charmonia fully dissociate when local energy density exceeds threshold values (16 GeV/fm^3 for J/Psi, 2 GeV/fm^3 for chi_c and Psi'). Results were compared to NA50, NA60, and PHENIX collaboration data.

## Key numbers

- For SPS energies, the comover absorption model uses a matrix element squared |M0|^2 = 0.18 fm^2/GeV^2, fixed by data
- Default threshold energy densities in the QGP scenario: epsilon_1 = 16 GeV/fm^3 for J/Psi, epsilon_2 = 2 GeV/fm^3 for chi_c, epsilon_3 = 2 GeV/fm^3 for Psi'
- Normalization factor B_mumu*sigma_NN(J/Psi)/sigma_NN(DY) = 36, based on NA60 compilation
- Psi' to J/Psi ratio for nucleon-nucleon collisions adopted as 0.0165
- At RHIC, initial energy densities above 30 GeV/fm^3 are reached in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV
- PHENIX collaboration found at least 20% of J/Psi survive at RHIC

## Context

This work extends earlier HSD transport studies by Bratkovskaya et al. (2003, 2004) and Linnyk et al. (2007) comparing charmonium suppression mechanisms across SPS and RHIC energies. It directly tests the "hadronic comover" model of Capella et al. and the "threshold melting" model of Blaizot et al. and Satz against NA50, NA60, and PHENIX data. The failure of both models at RHIC motivates the need for partonic transport descriptions of the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma (sQGP), as also suggested by the underestimation of collective flow v2 from open charm decay in Bratkovskaya et al. (2005).
