# The Off Shell $ρ$-$ω$ Mixing in the QCD Sum Rules

> The $q^2$ dependence of the $ρ-ω$ mixing amplitude is analyzed with the use of the QCD sum rules and the dispersion relation. Going off shell the mixing decreases, changes sign at $q^2 \simeq 0.4 m_ρ^2 > 0$ and is negative in the space like region. Implications of this result to the isospin breaking part of the nuclear force are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the off-shell ρ-ω mixing amplitude has a significant q² dependence, changing sign at q² ≈ 0.4 m_ρ² and becoming negative in the spacelike region, which contradicts the common assumption that the on-shell mixing value can be used for nuclear force calculations at spacelike momenta.

## Method

The authors used QCD sum rules combined with dispersion relations to analyze the q² dependence of the ρ-ω mixing amplitude. They performed a Borel sum rule analysis and a finite energy sum rule analysis, determining the mixing parameter β (related to the q² variation) from the sum rules themselves rather than assuming it as in prior work by Shifman, Vainshtein and Zakharov (SVZ). The analysis used updated experimental inputs for the on-shell mixing and current quark mass differences, and considered four different sets of input parameters for the quark condensate ratio and four-quark condensate.

## Key numbers

- The on-shell ρ-ω mixing amplitude at q² = m_ω² is (-4520 ± 600) MeV², as measured in e⁺e⁻ → π⁺π⁻ data.
- The QED contribution from ρ → γ → ω is approximately 610 MeV².
- The quark mass ratio (m_d - m_u)/(m_d + m_u) = 0.28 ± 0.03.
- The mixing parameter λ = β + 1 lies in the interval [1.43, 1.85].
- The mixing amplitude changes sign at q² ≈ 0.4 m_ρ².
- In coordinate space, the charge-dependent potential has a node at r ≈ 0.9 fm.

## Context

This work extends the earlier QCD sum rule analysis of ρ-ω mixing by Shifman, Vainshtein and Zakharov (SVZ), who only considered the on-shell case and assumed β = 0.5 without determination from the sum rules. The results are qualitatively consistent with earlier model calculations by Goldman, Henderson and Thomas (GHT) using free quark-antiquark intermediate states and by Piekarewicz and Williams (PW) using N̄N intermediate states, but the q² variation found here is stronger. The findings challenge the use of on-shell ρ-ω mixing in meson-theoretic calculations of charge-symmetry-breaking nuclear forces by Miller (MILLER), by the group of Machleidt, Thaler and Wiringa (MTW), and by Blunden and Iqbal (BI) for the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly.
