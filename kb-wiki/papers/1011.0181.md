# Comment on "Five-Body Cluster Structure of the Double-Lambda Hypernucleus Lambda-Lambda-11Be"

> Hiyama et al. [PRL 104 (2010) 212502] have recently reported on a pioneering five-body alpha-alpha-n-Lambda-Lambda cluster-model calculation of Lambda-Lambda-11Be in order to confront a possible interpretation of the KEK-E373 HIDA event. Unfortunately, a six-body alpha-alpha-n-n-Lambda-Lambda calculation of Lambda-Lambda-12Be to confront another possible interpretation is beyond reach at present. Using experimental B-Lambda values with small corrections based on recently determined Lambda-N spin-dependent interaction parameters, we obtain binding-energy shell-model estimates for both Lambda-Lambda hypernuclei, concluding that neither Lambda-Lambda-11Be nor Lambda-Lambda-12Be provide satisfactory interpretation of the HIDA event. The shell model approach is tested by reproducing the measured double-Lambda binding energy of the double-Lambda hypernucleus Lambda-Lambda-13B.

# Comment on "Five-Body Cluster Structure of the Double-Lambda Hypernucleus ΛΛ-11Be"

## Key claim
Using shell-model estimates based on experimental BΛ values with small corrections from recently determined ΛN spin-dependent interaction parameters, neither ΛΛ-11Be nor ΛΛ-12Be provides a satisfactory interpretation of the KEK-E373 HIDA event, contrary to the cluster-model interpretation proposed by Hiyama et al. (2010).

## Method
The authors performed shell-model (SM) calculations using three Λ-spin-dependent ΛN interaction parameters (Δ, SΛ, T) fitted to six known Λ hypernuclear doublet splittings beyond Λ-9Be, plus an induced nuclear spin-orbit parameter SN extracted from the excitation energy of Λ-16O(1₂⁻). The ΛΛ binding energy was computed as BΛΛ^SM = 2B̄Λ^SM + ⟨VΛΛ⟩SM, where ⟨VΛΛ⟩SM = 0.67 ± 0.17 MeV was deduced from the NAGARA event (ΛΛ-6He). The SM approach was tested by reproducing the measured double-Lambda binding energy of ΛΛ-13B.

## Key numbers
- ⟨VΛΛ⟩SM = 0.67 ± 0.17 MeV, deduced from BΛΛ(ΛΛ-6He) minus 2BΛ(Λ-5He)
- BΛΛ^SM(ΛΛ-11Be) = 18.39 ± 0.20 MeV, compared to experimental BΛΛ^exp = 20.83 ± 1.27 MeV
- BΛΛ^SM(ΛΛ-12Be) = 20.71 ± 0.20 MeV, compared to experimental BΛΛ^exp = 22.48 ± 1.21 MeV
- BΛΛ^SM(ΛΛ-13B) = 23.21 ± 0.21 MeV, in excellent agreement with experimental BΛΛ^exp = 23.3 ± 0.7 MeV
- Spin-independent ΛN interaction parameter V̄ΛN = -1.04 MeV, reproducing ground-state binding energies of Λ hypernuclei with A=10,11,12 to within δBΛ^SM ≤ 0.2 MeV
- The induced nuclear spin-orbit interaction contributes close to 400 keV to BΛ^SM(Λ-10Be_g.s.) and twice as much to BΛΛ^SM(ΛΛ-11Be)

## Context
This Comment directly challenges the five-body ααnΛΛ cluster-model calculation of ΛΛ-11Be by Hiyama et al. (2010), which was proposed to interpret the KEK-E373 HIDA event. The authors note that a six-body ααnnΛΛ calculation of ΛΛ-12Be is beyond current computational reach. The shell-model approach, tested against the known ΛΛ-13B binding energy from the NAGARA event (Nakazawa et al., 2010), provides a consistency check showing that neither ΛΛ-11Be nor ΛΛ-12Be satisfactorily explains the HIDA event. The work extends the shell-model framework of Millener (2010) and highlights that the cluster-model calculations lack the induced nuclear spin-orbit interaction (parameter SN), which contributes significantly to p-shell Λ hypernuclei binding energies.
