# The Subtleties of the Wigner Function Formulation of the Chiral Magnetic Effect

> We assess the applicability of the Wigner function formulation in its present form to the chiral Magnetic Effect and noted some issues regarding the conservation and the consistency of the electric current in the presence of an inhomogeneous and time dependent axial chemical potential. The problems are rooted in the ultraviolet divergence of the underlying field theory associated with the axial anomaly and can be fixed with the Pauli-Villars regularization of the Wigner function.

## Key claim

The Wigner function formulation of the chiral magnetic effect, while successful for constant axial chemical potential, suffers from ultraviolet ambiguities that cause nonconservation and inconsistency of the electric current when the axial chemical potential is inhomogeneous or time dependent; these issues can be fixed by introducing Pauli-Villars regularization of the Wigner function.

## Method

The authors analyze the Wigner function for massless Dirac fermions in external electromagnetic and axial vector fields using the closed-time-path (CTP) Green function formalism. They expand the CTP propagator to linear order in the gauge and axial vector potentials, compute the electric current from the point-split definition of the Wigner function, and examine the limit as the point-splitting separation y goes to zero. They then introduce Pauli-Villars regulator fields with masses M_s and coefficients C_s to regularize the ultraviolet divergences, and recompute the current for the case of a constant axial chemical potential with two different orders of the limit q_2 to 0.

## Key numbers

* The unregularized current divergence yields (3i/32pi^2) epsilon_{mu rho beta lambda} F_{mu rho}(x) F_{5 beta lambda}(x) after direction averaging of the point-splitting vector y.
* The unregularized consistency condition violation yields (3i/16pi^2) epsilon_{mu rho beta lambda} F_{5 beta lambda}(x) delta^4(x - x').
* The regularized chiral magnetic current kernel for the order lim_{q20 to 0} lim_{vec q2 to 0} gives -(1/2pi^2) epsilon_{ikj} q_{1k}, recovering the standard CME current.
* The difference between the two orders of the limit q_2 to 0 is given by an integral over the derivative of the distribution function f(|p|) with respect to |p|.

## Context

This work extends the Wigner function approach to the chiral magnetic effect developed by Gao et al. (2012) for constant axial chemical potential, and addresses issues previously noted by Hou et al. regarding the order of limits when mu_5 approaches a constant. The paper shows that the unregularized Wigner function's point-splitting procedure violates Bose symmetry in the triangle diagram, unlike the standard axial anomaly calculation. The Pauli-Villars regularization scheme resolves these issues and enables the Wigner function formalism to describe non-equilibrium systems with spacetime-dependent axial chemical potential, which is relevant for phenomenological applications in heavy-ion collisions and Weyl semimetals.
