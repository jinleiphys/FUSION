# Probing halo nucleus structure through intermediate energy elastic scattering

> This work addresses the question of precisely what features of few body models of halo nuclei are probed by elastic scattering on protons at high centre-of-mass energies. Our treatment is based on a multiple scattering expansion of the proton-projectile transition amplitude in a form which is well adapted to the weakly bound cluster picture of halo nuclei. In the specific case of $^{11}$Li scattering from protons at 800 MeV/u we show that because core recoil effects are significant, scattering crosssections can not, in general, be deduced from knowledge of the total matter density alone. We advocate that the optical potential concept for the scattering of halo nuclei on protons should be avoided and that the multiple scattering series for the full transition amplitude should be used instead.

# Probing halo nucleus structure through intermediate energy elastic scattering

## Key claim

For proton elastic scattering from halo nuclei at intermediate energies, the cross section cannot be deduced from knowledge of the total matter density alone because core recoil effects are significant; the authors advocate that the optical potential concept should be avoided and the multiple scattering series for the full transition amplitude should be used instead, at least for systems like 11Li where the core mass is not effectively infinite.

## Method

The authors developed a multiple scattering expansion of the proton-projectile transition amplitude truncated at second order in the proton-subsystem t-matrices, applied to a three-body model of 11Li (two valence neutrons plus a 9Li core). They evaluated single scattering terms from the valence neutrons and the core, and double scattering terms from valence-valence and valence-core rescattering, using Fadeev wave functions for 11Li from Thompson and Zhukov (1994) and a nucleon-nucleon transition amplitude derived from the Paris potential. The propagators were evaluated using the eikonal approximation, and the calculations were performed for proton scattering at 800 MeV/u.

## Key numbers

- Incident energy: 800 MeV/u for proton scattering from 11Li
- The ratio of the principal value to delta function terms in the eikonal propagator is less than 1/(k_i R), where R is a measure of the halo size, which is "very small" for the cases considered
- In the limit of infinite core mass (m_4 → ∞), the single scattering core form factor ρ_2(0, M_23/M_234 Δ) → 1, but this limit is "a poor approximation" for the cases studied
- In the limit of infinite core mass, the valence-core double scattering density ρ_2((m_3/M_23)q + (m_3/M_234)Δ, q) → ρ_v(Δ), and this limit "was found to be a good approximation here"

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier multiple scattering expansion of the optical model operator (Crespo, Johnson, and Tostevin, 1991, 1992) by instead expanding the full transition amplitude, which treats ground and excited states of the projectile on the same footing. The paper contradicts approaches that describe proton-halo scattering using an optical potential expressed as a t_NN times total matter density, as in the work of Alkhazov and collaborators (1997). The formalism enables explicit delineation of how different halo density functions contribute to scattering, in contrast to the approach of Tostevin, Al-Khalili, and collaborators (1998) which treats core recoil consistently but is less transparent about structure effects. The authors note that two-body correlation effects from double scattering are small for 11Li at 800 MeV/u, but the core centre-of-mass density distribution has a large effect on the calculated cross section.
