# Hybrid protoneutron stars with the Dyson-Schwinger quark model

> We study the hadron-quark phase transition at finite temperature in the interior of protoneutron stars, combining the Dyson-Schwinger model for quark matter with the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach for hadronic matter. We discuss the dependence of the results on different nuclear three-body forces and on details of the quark model. %Compared to the bag model, the transition from the hadron phase %(without hyperons) to the quark phase appears at large density. A maximum mass exceeding two solar masses can be obtained with a strong three-body force and suitable parameter values in the Dyson-Schwinger model. With a hybrid configuration, the maximum mass of protoneutron stars is larger then that of cold neutron stars, such that a delayed collapse might be possible in principle.

## Key claim
The maximum mass of hybrid protoneutron stars (PNS) can exceed two solar masses when using a strong three-body force and suitable Dyson-Schwinger model parameters, and with a hybrid configuration the maximum mass of PNS is larger than that of cold neutron stars, making a delayed collapse possible in principle. However, this result depends on the specific choice of three-body force and quark model parameters, and no phase transition exists if the hadronic phase contains hyperons and the equation of state is very soft, or if the Dyson-Schwinger parameter α is too small.

## Method
The authors combined the Dyson-Schwinger quark model (DSM) for quark matter with the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach for hadronic matter at finite temperature, using the Argonne V18 nucleon-nucleon potential with either microscopic or phenomenological (Urbana) three-body forces. The hadron-quark phase transition was treated via the Gibbs construction, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were solved to obtain protoneutron star configurations for beta-stable matter with and without neutrino trapping.

## Key numbers
- Maximum mass exceeding 2 solar masses can be obtained with a strong three-body force and suitable DSM parameter values.
- For the Dyson-Schwinger model, parameters used were ω = 0.5 GeV, D = 1 GeV², with α = 2 and α = 4 as two typical values.
- The bag constant for the DSM was set to B_DS = 90 MeV.
- For the MIT bag model comparison, a constant bag parameter B = 90 MeV fm⁻³ was used, or a density-dependent bag with B_∞ = 50 MeV fm⁻³, B_0 = 400 MeV fm⁻³, and β = 0.17.
- The lepton fraction for neutrino-trapped matter was fixed to Y_e = 0.4 at T = 40 MeV.
- The strange quark mass was taken as m_s = 115 MeV in the DSM and m_s = 150 MeV in the MIT bag model.
- The transition density for the DSM occurs at higher baryon density than for the MIT bag model; for the MIT model the transition occurs at low baryon chemical potential, while for the DSM it occurs at higher values.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of protoneutron stars by Chen et al. (2011) and earlier work using the MIT bag model (protomit, pnsymit) by replacing the bag model with the more sophisticated Dyson-Schwinger quark model. It builds on finite-temperature hadronic equations of state including hyperons derived within Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone theory (pnsy, isen2). The results enable further studies of delayed collapse scenarios for protoneutron stars and demonstrate that the choice of quark model significantly affects the phase transition density and maximum mass, with implications for the interpretation of heavy neutron star observations.
