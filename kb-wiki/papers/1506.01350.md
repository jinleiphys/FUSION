# Thermodynamics and CP-odd transport in Holographic QCD with Finite Magnetic Field

> We consider a bottom-up holographic model of QCD at finite temperature T and magnetic field B, and study dependence of thermodynamics and CP-odd transport on these variables. As the magnetic field couples to the flavor sector only, one should take the Veneziano limit where the number of flavors and colors are large while their ratio is kept fixed. We investigate the corresponding holographic background in the approximation where the ratio of flavors to colors is finite but small. We demonstrate that B-dependence of the entropy of QCD is in qualitative agreement with the recent lattice studies. Finally we study the CP-odd transport properties of this system. In particular, we determine the Chern-Simons decay rate at finite B and T, that is an important ingredient in the Chiral Magnetic Effect.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that in a bottom-up holographic QCD model with a finite but small ratio of flavors to colors (x = N_f/N_c), the entropy density and the Chern-Simons (sphaleron) decay rate both increase with magnetic field B at fixed temperature above T_c, with the increase more substantial at lower temperatures, qualitatively matching lattice results for entropy but extending the analysis to CP-odd transport.

## Method
The authors employ the V-QCD holographic model, combining a glue sector (gravity-dilaton action) with a flavor sector (generalized Sen's action for N_f flavor branes) in the Veneziano limit where N_c and N_f are large but x = N_f/N_c is fixed. They solve the Einstein-dilaton equations numerically for a black hole background with an external magnetic field B introduced via the U(1) vector field on the flavor branes, working in the deconfined, chirally symmetric phase (τ = 0) and expanding the Dirac-Born-Infeld action to quadratic order in B for small x. The sphaleron decay rate Γ_CS is then computed by solving the axion fluctuation equation on this numerical background.

## Key numbers
- The entropy ratio S(B≠0)/S(B=0) increases with B at any fixed temperature above T_c, with the rate of increase more substantial at lower temperatures T ≳ T_c.
- For heavy ion collisions, estimates yield e|B|/m_π² ≈ 1–3 at 0.1–0.2 fm/c after a RHIC collision at √s = 200 AGeV, and e|B|/m_π² ≈ 10–15 at some earlier time after an LHC collision at √s = 2.76 ATeV.
- The Chern-Simons decay rate Γ_CS increases as a function of B, with the rate of increase more substantial at low temperatures (shown in figure 2 for IHQCD potentials with c_1 = 1 and c_4 = 1).

## Context
This work extends the Improved Holographic QCD (IHQCD) model of Gursoy et al. (2007–2009) for pure glue to include flavor in the Veneziano limit, following the V-QCD framework of Jarvinen et al. (2011–2015). The entropy results are in qualitative agreement with lattice studies by Bali et al. (2011). The sphaleron rate calculation extends prior holographic studies of the Chern-Simons decay rate in N=4 Super Yang-Mills by Basar et al. (2012) and in other holographic models by Son and Starinets (2002), Craps et al. (2012), and Gursoy et al. (2012). The findings enable quantitative assessment of the chiral magnetic effect in heavy ion collisions by showing that magnetic field enhances the effective chiral chemical potential μ_5 through increased sphaleron decay rate, in addition to the direct B-dependence of the chiral magnetic current.
