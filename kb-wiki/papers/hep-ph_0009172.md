# Non-Fermi Liquid Aspects of Cold and Dense QED and QCD: Equilibrium and Non-Equilibrium

> Infrared divergences from the exchange of dynamically screened magnetic gluons (photons) lead to the breakdown of the Fermi liquid description of the {\em normal} state of cold and dense QCD and QED. We implement a resummation of these divergences via the renormalization group to obtain the spectral density, dispersion relation, widths and wave function renormalization of single quasiparticles near the Fermi surface. We find that all feature scaling with anomalous dimensions: $ω_p({k}) \propto |k-k_F|^{\frac{1}{1-2λ}} ~ ; ~ Γ(k) \propto |k-k_F|^{\frac{1}{1-2λ}} ~;~ Z_p({k}) \propto |k-k_F|^{\frac{2λ}{1-2λ}}$ with $λ= \fracα{6π} ~ {for QED} \vspace{0.5 ex} ~,~ \frac{α_s}{6π} \frac{N^2_c-1}{2N_c} \~~{for QCD with}$. The discontinuity of the distribution function for quasiparticles near the Fermi surface vanishes. The dynamical renormalization group is implemented to study the relaxation of quasiparticles in real time. Quasiparticles with Fermi momentum have vanishing group velocity and relax with a power law with a coupling dependent anomalous dimension.

## Key claim

Infrared divergences from the exchange of dynamically screened magnetic gluons (photons) cause the breakdown of the Fermi liquid description of the normal state of cold and dense QCD and QED, with the quasiparticle residue vanishing at the Fermi surface and all quasiparticle features exhibiting scaling with coupling-dependent anomalous dimensions.

## Method

The authors implement a resummation of infrared divergences in the fermion propagator via the Euclidean renormalization group, treating the exchange of dynamically screened magnetic gauge bosons (screened via Landau damping) in the hard-dense-loop (HDL) approximation. They compute the spectral density, dispersion relation, widths, and wave function renormalization for single quasiparticles near the Fermi surface. For non-equilibrium aspects, they implement a dynamical renormalization group directly in real time to study the relaxation of quasiparticle states.

## Key numbers

- For QED, the effective coupling is lambda = alpha/(6 pi); for QCD with N_c colors and N_F flavors, lambda = (alpha_s/(6 pi)) * (N_c^2 - 1)/(2 N_c)
- The quasiparticle dispersion relation scales as omega_p(k) proportional to |k - k_F|^(1/(1-2*lambda))
- The quasiparticle width scales as Gamma(k) proportional to sin[pi*lambda] * |k - k_F|^(1/(1-2*lambda))
- The wave function renormalization scales as Z_p(k) proportional to |k - k_F|^(2*lambda/(1-2*lambda))
- The quasiparticle distribution function near the Fermi surface is n_{k approx k_F} = sin[pi*lambda]/(2*pi*lambda) - (k - k_F)/(pi*M*(1-4*lambda)) + O(k - k_F)^2, where M = g*mu/(2*pi) for QED and M = (g*mu/(2*pi))*(N_F/2) for QCD with N_F flavors
- The amplitude of single quasiparticle states near the Fermi surface falls off as |psi_{k approx k_F}(t)| proportional to |psi_{k approx k_F}(t_0)| * exp(-Gamma(k)*(t-t_0)) * [t_0/t]^(2*lambda)

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Ren (no year given) which revealed large corrections to the superconducting gap from non-Fermi liquid effects in cold dense QCD, and is analogous to the breakdown of Fermi liquid theory found in non-relativistic electron gases with magnetic interactions by Holstein, Reizer, and Chakraverty (no years given). The results enable further study of non-Fermi liquid corrections to color superconducting gaps, critical temperatures, neutrino emissivity and cooling rates of neutron stars with quark-matter cores, and transport phenomena in dense QCD. The spectral density and relaxation behavior show striking resemblance to Luttinger liquids as described by Schulz (no year given) and Shof (no year given).
