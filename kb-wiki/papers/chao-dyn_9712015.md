# Quantum Mechanics and Semiclassics of Hyperbolic n-Disk Scattering Systems

> The scattering problems of a scalar point particle from a finite assembly of n>1 non-overlapping and disconnected hard disks, fixed in the two-dimensional plane, belong to the simplest realizations of classically hyperbolic scattering systems. Here, we investigate the connection between the spectral properties of the quantum-mechanical scattering matrix and its semiclassical equivalent based on the semiclassical zeta-function of Gutzwiller and Voros. Our quantum-mechanical calculation is well-defined at every step, as the on-shell T-matrix and the multiscattering kernel M-1 are shown to be trace-class. The multiscattering determinant can be organized in terms of the cumulant expansion which is the defining prescription for the determinant over an infinite, but trace-class matrix. The quantum cumulants are then expanded by traces which, in turn, split into quantum itineraries or cycles. These can be organized by a simple symbolic dynamics. The semiclassical reduction of the coherent multiscattering part takes place on the level of the quantum cycles. We show that the semiclassical analog of the m-th quantum cumulant is the m-th curvature term of the semiclassical zeta function. In this way quantum mechanics naturally imposes the curvature regularization structured by the topological (not the geometrical) length of the pertinent periodic orbits onto the semiclassical zeta function. However, since the cumulant limit m->infinity and the semiclassical limit hbar->0 do not commute in general, the semiclassical analog of the quantum multiscattering determinant is a curvature expanded, truncated semiclassical zeta function. We relate the order of this truncation to the topological entropy of the corresponding classical system.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a direct "top-down" link between the exact quantum-mechanical scattering matrix determinant for finite n-disk systems and the semiclassical Gutzwiller-Voros zeta function, but shows that the cumulant limit (m to infinity) and the semiclassical limit (hbar to 0 or k to infinity) do not commute in general, so the semiclassical analog of the quantum multiscattering determinant is necessarily a curvature-expanded, truncated semiclassical zeta function whose truncation order relates to the topological entropy of the classical system.

## Method

The author constructs the exact quantum-mechanical S-matrix for any finite assembly of n non-overlapping hard disks using stationary scattering theory, showing that the on-shell T-matrix and the multiscattering kernel M-1 are trace-class operators. The multiscattering determinant is organized via the cumulant expansion, where quantum cumulants are expanded into traces that split into quantum itineraries or cycles organized by symbolic dynamics. The semiclassical reduction is performed on the level of these quantum cycles using Watson contour integration and complex angular-momentum plane methods, showing that the m-th quantum cumulant becomes the m-th curvature term of the semiclassical zeta function, with the curvature regularization structured by topological (not geometrical) length of periodic orbits.

## Context

This work extends the earlier studies of the three-disk scattering system by Gaspard and Rice (1990s) and by Cvitanovic and Eckhardt (1989), as well as the Korringa-Kohn-Rostoker (KKR) method applied to the Sinai billiard by Berry (1980s). It provides a rigorous derivation that resolves convergence problems in periodic orbit expansions for hyperbolic scattering systems, showing that the Gutzwiller-Voros zeta function has the correct stability structure and is superior to the dynamical zeta function of Ruelle and the quasiclassical zeta function of Vattay and Cvitanovic. The work enables a controlled semiclassical treatment of n-disk repellers with a well-defined truncation prescription tied to the topological entropy, and demonstrates that unitarity is preserved even at the semiclassical level under the appropriate truncation.
