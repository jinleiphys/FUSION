# Rotational properties of the superheavy nucleus 256Rf and its neighboring even-even nuclei in particle-number conserving cranked shell model

> The ground state band was recently observed in the superheavy nucleus 256Rf. We study the rotational properties of 256Rf and its neighboring even-even nuclei by using a cranked shell model (CSM) with the pairing correlations treated by a particle-number conserving (PNC) method in which the blocking effects are taken into account exactly. The kinematic and dynamic moments of inertia of the ground state bands in these nuclei are well reproduced by the theory. The spin of the lowest observed state in 256Rf is determined by comparing the experimental kinematic moments of inertia with the PNC-CSM calculations and agrees with previous spin assignment. The effects of the high order deformation varepsilon6 on the angular momentum alignments and dynamic moments of inertia in these nuclei are discussed.

## Key claim

The spin of the lowest observed state in the ground state band of 256Rf is determined to be 4ℏ by comparing experimental kinematic moments of inertia with PNC-CSM calculations, consistent with the spin assignment made using the Harris formula in Greenlees (2012). The high-order deformation ε6 has a noticeable effect on dynamical moments of inertia in the high rotational frequency region, lowering both neutron νj15/2 and proton πi13/2 intruder orbitals.

## Method

The authors used a cranked shell model (CSM) with pairing correlations treated by a particle-number conserving (PNC) method, where the cranked Nilsson Hamiltonian is solved directly in a truncated Fock-space (cranked many-particle configuration truncation) so that blocking effects are taken into account exactly. The Nilsson parameters were taken from a new set optimized for transfermium nuclei in Zhang (2012), and the deformation parameters for 256Rf were extrapolated as ε2=0.255 and ε4=0.025. The effective pairing strengths were fixed as Gp=0.40 MeV, G2p=0.035 MeV, Gn=0.30 MeV, and G2n=0.020 MeV for all even-even nuclei in this mass region.

## Key numbers

- The deformation parameters of 256Rf are ε2=0.255 and ε4=0.025.
- The effective pairing strengths used are Gp=0.40 MeV, G2p=0.035 MeV, Gn=0.30 MeV, and G2n=0.020 MeV.
- The Z=104 proton energy spacing in the calculation is about 0.5 MeV.
- The ε6 deformation values used are 0.044 for 250Fm, 0.040 for 252No, 0.042 for 254No, and 0.038 for 256Rf, taken from Moeller (1995).
- The spin of the lowest observed state in the ground state band of 256Rf is determined to be 4ℏ, with the 161 keV transition assigned as 6+→4+ and deduced energies of 4+→2+ (104 keV) and 2+→0+ (44 keV) from Greenlees (2012).

## Context

This work extends the systematic PNC-CSM investigation of rotational bands in Z≈100 nuclei from Zhang (2012) to the superheavy nucleus 256Rf, whose ground state band was recently observed by Greenlees (2012). The study confirms the existence of a proton gap at Z=100 and a neutron gap at N=152, while finding the Z=104 gap less pronounced than in self-consistent mean field models such as Bender (2003) and Afanasjev (2003). The results enable future investigations using a Woods-Saxon potential instead of the Nilsson potential to generate basis states, and the ε6 deformation effects on alignment mechanisms differ from those found by the Total Routhian Surface method in Liu (2012).
