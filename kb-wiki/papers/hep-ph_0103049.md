# Nonequilibrium time evolution of the spectral function in quantum field theory

> Transport or kinetic equations are often derived assuming a quasi-particle (on-shell) representation of the spectral function. We investigate this assumption using a three-loop approximation of the 2PI effective action in real time, without a gradient expansion or on-shell approximation. For a scalar field in 1+1 dimensions the nonlinear evolution, including the integration over memory kernels, can be solved numerically. We find that a spectral function approximately described by a nonzero width emerges dynamically. During the nonequilibrium time evolution the Wigner transformed spectral function is slowly varying, even in presence of strong qualitative changes in the effective particle distribution. These results may be used to make further analytical progress towards a quantum Boltzmann equation including off-shell effects and a nonzero width.

# Nonequilibrium time evolution of the spectral function in quantum field theory

## Key claim

The authors find that a spectral function approximately described by a nonzero width emerges dynamically during nonequilibrium time evolution, and that the Wigner transformed spectral function is slowly varying even in the presence of strong qualitative changes in the effective particle distribution. This result provides a necessary condition for a successful gradient expansion in the derivation of kinetic equations, but the authors caution that the positivity condition for the spectral function can only be shown to hold in thermal equilibrium, so the interpretation of the nonequilibrium spectral function as a density of states should be taken with care.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled evolution equations for the spectral function ρ and the statistical two-point function F, derived from the three-loop 2PI (two-particle irreducible) effective action in real time, without a gradient expansion or on-shell approximation. For a real scalar field in 1+1 dimensions with λφ³/6 interaction, they perform numerical integration including the memory kernels, using a lattice discretization with spatial spacing ma=0.3 and time spacing a₀/a=0.25 on a system size mL=24. The initial condition is a "tsunami" particle distribution peaked around momenta ±p_ts, far from thermal equilibrium.

## Key numbers

- Coupling used: λ/m² = 4
- Lattice parameters: spatial spacing ma = 0.3, time spacing a₀/a = 0.25, system size mL = 24
- For zero momentum mode (k=0) at mX⁰=35.1: Breit-Wigner fit gives E_p/m = 1.46 and Γ_p/m = 0.37
- For momentum mode k=4 at mX⁰=35.1: Breit-Wigner fit gives E_p/m = 1.82 and Γ_p/m = 0.30
- The sum rule I = ∫ dω/(2π) ω ρ(X⁰;ω,p) = 1 is numerically satisfied within 1.000 < I < 1.003
- Three-particle threshold for zero mode: 3E_p/m = 4.38

## Context

This work extends the numerical solution of nonequilibrium quantum field theory presented by Berges (2000) for scalar fields in 1+1 dimensions, which described both early-time behavior and thermalization. The authors directly investigate the validity of the on-shell (zero-width) approximation used in derivations of the classical Boltzmann equation by Kadanoff and Baym, Calzetta and Hu (1988), and Blaizot and Iancu (2001). The results enable further analytical progress toward a quantum Boltzmann equation that includes off-shell effects and a nonzero width, relevant for heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC where spectral properties of vector mesons are affected by nuclear matter, as discussed by Stachel (1999).
