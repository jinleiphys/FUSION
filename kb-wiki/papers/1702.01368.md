# Hydrodynamic fluctuations near a critical endpoint and Hanbury Brown-Twiss interferometry

> The field of high energy nuclear collisions has witnessed a surge of interest in the role played by hydrodynamic fluctuations. Hydrodynamic fluctuations may have significant effects on matter created in heavy-ion accelerators whose trajectories in the plane of temperature versus chemical potential pass near a possible critical endpoint. We extend previous studies to explore the impact of these fluctuations on Hanbury Brown-Twiss interferometry of identical hadrons. With an appropriately defined correlation function we find that the fluctuations increase substantially when the trajectory passes near a critical endpoint, and also displays a damped oscillatory behavior in the rapidity distance $Δy$ unlike that originating from initial-state fluctuations.

## Key claim

Hydrodynamic fluctuations near a QCD critical endpoint cause the two-point correlation function of the longitudinal HBT radius \(R_l^2\) to increase substantially and exhibit a damped oscillatory behavior in rapidity difference \(\Delta y\), unlike the pattern from initial-state fluctuations; the effect is strongest when the system trajectory passes close to the critical endpoint.

## Method

The authors model 1+1 dimensional Bjorken expansion with stochastic baryon-current fluctuations, linearizing the equations of motion around a smooth background and solving via Fourier transform in space-time rapidity. They compute the event-by-event longitudinal HBT radius \(R_l^2(y)\) from the emission function, expand it to first order in fluctuations \(\delta T\), \(\delta \mu\), and \(\omega\), and then evaluate the two-point correlator \(\langle \delta R_l^2(y_1) \delta R_l^2(y_2) \rangle\) using the fluctuation-dissipation relation for thermal conductivity \(\lambda\) near the critical point. The correlator is normalized by the mean multiplicity and mean HBT radii to define \(C_{\rm HBT}(\Delta y)\).

## Key numbers

* For trajectory III (passing closest to the critical endpoint at \(T_c = 160\) MeV, \(\mu_c = 411.74\) MeV), the relative width of \(R_l^2\) fluctuations for pions is \(\sigma_l / \langle R_l^2 \rangle \sim 0.5 A^{-1/3}\), giving \(\sim 9\%\) for Au+Au collisions.
* For trajectory III, the relative width for protons is \(\sigma_l / \langle R_l^2 \rangle \sim 1.3 A^{-1/3}\), giving \(\sim 22\%\) for Au+Au collisions.
* The average \(\pi^+\) multiplicity per unit rapidity per unit transverse area for trajectory III is \(\langle dN_{\pi^+}/dy \rangle / A_{\perp} \sim 0.255\) fm\(^{-2}\).
* The average proton multiplicity per unit rapidity per unit transverse area for trajectory III is \(\langle dN_{p}/dy \rangle / A_{\perp} \sim 0.07\) fm\(^{-2}\).
* The freeze-out proper times for trajectories I, II, and III are \(\tau_f = 3.0\), 3.3, and 3.7 fm/c, respectively.
* The entropy per baryon at the critical point is 19.96; for trajectories I, II, and III it is 37.98, 26.08, and 20.06, respectively.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of hydrodynamic fluctuations in Bjorken flow by Kapusta and collaborators (2011, 2012), which computed two-particle correlation functions near a critical endpoint, to the case of HBT interferometry. The results suggest that event-by-event fluctuations of the longitudinal HBT radius could become comparable to or larger than those from initial-state fluctuations (studied by Plumberg and collaborators in 2015 and 2016) when the system trajectory passes near a QCD critical point, making HBT radii a potential probe for the Beam Energy Scan II at RHIC and future FAIR experiments.
