# Comparison of Practical Expressions for E1 Photon Strength Functions

> The closed-form expressions for the photon strength functions (PSF) are tested using the gamma-decay data of OSLO group. The theoretical calculations are performed for the Lorentzian models of PSF for electric and magnetic dipole gamma-rays. The criteria of minimum of least-square value as well as the root-mean-square deviation factor are used. It is shown that a rather good agreement is obtained within the Simple Modified Lorentzian model for E1 PSF modelling.

## Key claim

The Simple Modified Lorentzian (SMLO) model for E1 photon strength functions provides a better description of experimental gamma-decay data from the OSLO group than the Standard Lorentzian (SLO), Generalized Lorentzian (GLO), or Triple Lorentzian (TLO) models, when judged by both least-square and root-mean-square deviation criteria, but the agreement is described as "rather good" rather than exact.

## Method

The authors calculated the sum of E1 and M1 photon strength functions using four Lorentzian-based models (SLO, SMLO, GLO, TLO) for E1 transitions, with M1 contributions including scissors, spin-flip modes, and an upbend term. They compared these theoretical calculations against experimental gamma-decay data from the OSLO group for 24 even-even nuclei, using two statistical criteria: the least-square value chi-squared and the root-mean-square deviation factor f. The GDR parameters for SLO and SMLO models were taken from a recent database (Plujko 2018), while TLO parameters came from Junghans (2008) with deformation parameters from Delaroche (2010).

## Key numbers

- The ratio of mean chi-squared values for SMLO relative to SLO is 0.10, for GLO is 0.04, and for TLO is 0.40
- The ratio of mean chi-squared/chi-squared_SLO values is 0.21 for SMLO, 0.29 for GLO, and 0.71 for TLO
- The ratio of mean f-values relative to SLO is 0.54 for SMLO, 0.58 for GLO, and 0.78 for TLO
- The ratio of mean f/f_SLO values is 0.63 for SMLO, 0.81 for GLO, and 0.84 for TLO
- The Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn sum rule is given as sigma_TRK = 15A(1-I^2) mb*MeV, where I = (N-Z)/A
- Experimental data cover gamma-ray energies up to approximately 10 MeV for even-even nuclei including 44,46Ti, 56Fe, 92,94,96,98Mo, 106,108Pd, 106,112Cd, 116,122Sn, 148Sm, 162,164Dy, 166Er, 170,172Yb, 206,208Pb, 232Th, and 238U

## Context

This work extends previous comparisons of Lorentzian models for photoabsorption data (Plujko 2011, 2018; Gorbachenko 2019) to gamma-decay data from the OSLO group. The SMLO model had previously shown good agreement with microscopic quasiparticle random-phase approximation and shell-model calculations (Gorbachenko 2019). The results enable the recommendation of the SMLO model for use in nuclear reaction codes for modeling E1 photon strength functions, particularly for applications in radiation shielding, reactor technology, and nuclear medicine.
