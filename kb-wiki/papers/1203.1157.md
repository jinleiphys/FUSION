# Deuteron Magnetic Quadrupole Moment From Chiral Effective Field Theory

> We calculate the magnetic quadrupole moment (MQM) of the deuteron at leading order in the systematic expansion provided by chiral effective field theory. We take into account parity and time-reversal violation which, at the quark-gluon level, results from the QCD vacuum angle and dimension-six operators that originate from physics beyond the Standard Model. We show that the deuteron MQM can be expressed in terms of five low-energy constants that appear in the parity- and time-reversal-violating nuclear potential and electromagnetic current, four of which also contribute to the electric dipole moments of light nuclei. We conclude that the deuteron MQM has an enhanced sensitivity to the QCD vacuum angle and that its measurement would be complementary to the proposed measurements of light-nuclear EDMs.

## Key claim

The deuteron magnetic quadrupole moment (MQM) is calculated at leading order in chiral effective field theory with pions treated nonperturbatively, and the results confirm the qualitative conclusions of Vri11b that the MQM has enhanced sensitivity to the QCD vacuum angle, but with important quantitative differences, particularly that the MQM/EDM ratio for the QCD theta term is about three times smaller than the perturbative-pion estimate.

## Method

The authors compute the deuteron MQM using chiral effective field theory with nonperturbative pion exchange, embedding the parity- and time-reversal-violating (PTV) interactions into P- and T-conserving wave functions from the Argonne v18 potential. The MQM is expressed as a sum of two reduced matrix elements: one from a PTV two-body electromagnetic current and one from the PT-conserving current acting on the parity-admixed deuteron wave function generated by the PTV potential. The calculation involves five low-energy constants (LECs) from the PTV Lagrangian: isoscalar and isovector pion-nucleon couplings (g0, g1), a pion-nucleon-photon coupling (c_pi), and two short-range nucleon-nucleon interactions (C1, C2).

## Key numbers

* The matrix element from the PTV two-body current (c_pi contribution) is 0.07 (F_pi c_pi / e) e fm^2, where F_pi = 185 MeV.
* For the isovector pion-exchange contribution (g1), the MQM matrix element is -[0.031(1+kappa_1) + 0.003 + 0.008] (g1/F_pi) e fm^2, where kappa_1 = 3.7 is the isovector magnetic moment.
* For the isoscalar pion-exchange contribution (g0), the MQM matrix element is -[0.044 (g0/F_pi) + 0.0013 F_pi^3 (C1 - 3C2)] (1+kappa_0) e fm^2, where kappa_0 = -0.12.
* For the QCD theta term, the ratio |m_d M_d / d_d| is less than or approximately 21(1+kappa_0), where m_d is the deuteron mass.
* For the quark chromo-EDM, the MQM/EDM ratio is approximately 1.6(1+kappa_1) + 2.2(1+kappa_0)(g0/g1) + 0.6.

## Context

This work extends the chiral EFT framework previously applied to light-nuclear EDMs in Vri12 and the perturbative-pion calculation of the deuteron MQM in Vri11b, treating pions nonperturbatively to address convergence issues found in the perturbative approach by Fleming (1999). The results are compared to earlier calculations by Khriplovich (2000) and Liu (2004) that used traditional meson-exchange models; the authors confirm the Liu (2004) results for g0 and g1 pion exchange but find the g0 coefficient is three times smaller than the Khriplovich (2000) result. The calculation enables future work to separate different PTV sources (QCD theta term, quark EDM, chromo-EDMs, four-quark operators) through combined measurements of the deuteron MQM and EDMs of light nuclei.
