# The electric dipole form factor of the nucleon

> The electric dipole form factor of the nucleon stemming from the QCD $\barθ$ term is calculated in chiral perturbation theory in leading order. To this order, the form factor originates from the pion cloud. Its momentum-dependence is proportional to a non-derivative time-reversal-violating pion-nucleon coupling, and the scale for momentum variation--appearing, in particular, in the radius of the form factor--is the pion mass.

## Key claim

The nucleon electric dipole form factor (EDFF) arising from the QCD \(\bar\theta\) term is, in leading-order chiral perturbation theory, an isovector function entirely determined by the pion cloud, with its momentum variation set by the pion mass; the result is a testable prediction only at momentum scales \(Q \sim m_\pi\), and higher-order corrections are expected to be of order \(Q/m_\rho\).

## Method

The authors constructed the most general Lagrangian for nucleons, pions, and delta isobars consistent with QCD symmetries, including the \(\bar\theta\) term as a \(T\)-violating source. They evaluated one-loop diagrams built from one \(T\)-violating pion-nucleon vertex (from \(\mathcal{L}^{(1)}_{\slashT}\)) and strong-interaction vertices from \(\mathcal{L}^{(0)}_{str/em}\), using heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory with dimensional regularization. ## Key numbers

- The neutron EDM experimental value is \(d_n = (-1.0 \pm 3.6) \cdot 10^{-26} \, e\,\text{cm}\) (current bound).
- The pion decay constant is \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV.
- The axial coupling constant is \(g_A = 1.267\).
- The isovector EDM square radius is \(\langle r_{ed}^{2}\rangle^{(1)} = \frac{e g_A \bar{g}_0}{8\pi^2 f_\pi} \frac{1}{m_\pi^2}\).
- The leading non-analytic contribution to the isovector EDM is \(d_1 = \tilde{d}_1 + \frac{e g_A \bar{g}_0}{8\pi^2 f_\pi} \left[\bar{\Delta} + 2\ln\frac{\mu}{m_\pi}\right]\), which using the Goldberger-Treiman relation becomes \(\frac{e g_{\pi NN} \bar{g}_0}{4\pi^2 m_N} \ln(m_N/m_\pi)\) at \(\mu = m_N\).
- The isovector EDFF is \(F_D^{(1)}(Q^2) = d_1 - \frac{e g_A \bar{g}_0}{12\pi^2 f_\pi} F\!\left(\frac{Q^2}{(2m_\pi)^2}\right)\), with \(F(x) = 3\left\{\frac{1}{2}\sqrt{1+\frac{1}{x}} \ln\!\left(\frac{\sqrt{1+1/x}+1}{\sqrt{1+1/x}-1}\right)-1\right\}\).
- For small \(x\), \(F(x) = x + \mathcal{O}(x^2)\).

## Context

This work extends the leading non-analytic contribution to the nucleon EDM first calculated in chiral perturbation theory by Crewther et al. (1979) and rederived by others (e.g., Bedaque, 2000), by computing the full momentum-dependent form factor rather than just the static EDM. The result enables future interpretation of neutron EDM measurements: once the static EDM is known, the momentum dependence of the EDFF could allow extraction of the \(T\)-violating pion-nucleon coupling \(\bar{g}_0\), and the EDFF radius contributes to the nuclear Schiff moment relevant for atomic EDM experiments. The authors note that unlike other nucleon form factors, a dipole approximation with a mass scale near the rho-meson mass should not be used for the EDFF.
