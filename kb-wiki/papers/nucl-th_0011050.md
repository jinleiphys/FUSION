# Kaon production at subthreshold and threshold energies

> We summarize what we have learnt about the kaon production in nucleus-nucleus collisions in the last decade. We will address three questions: a) Is the $K^+$ production sensitive to the nuclear equation of state? b) How can it happen that at the same excess energy the same number of $K^+$ and $K^-$ are produced in heavy ion collisions although the elementary cross section in pp collisions differs by orders of magnitudes? and c) Why kaons don't flow?

# Kaon production at subthreshold and threshold energies

## Key claim
The total $K^+$ production cross section at subthreshold or threshold energies in heavy-ion collisions depends on the nuclear equation of state (EOS), but this sensitivity is rendered useless for experimental determination of the EOS because the in-medium mass increase of the $K^+$ counterbalances the density-dependent collision frequency, and the remaining differences between soft and hard EOS are too small to overcome uncertainties in the elementary cross sections.

## Method
The authors employ the Isospin Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) simulation program to model nucleus-nucleus collisions, comparing calculations with different kaon production cross-section parametrizations (their own set and the Giessen group set) and different treatments of in-medium kaon mass modifications (no potential, chiral perturbation theory, Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, and coupled channel approach). They compare simulated rapidity distributions and yields to experimental data from the FOPI and KAOS collaborations for Ni+Ni, C+C, and Au+Au systems.

## Key numbers
- The $K^+$ mass increases by about 35 MeV per density unit in the nuclear medium, while the $K^-$ mass decreases by approximately the same amount.
- For standard soft and hard equations of state, the $K^+$ yield differed by a factor of two in earlier calculations.
- Using different cross-section parametrizations (the authors' set vs. the Giessen group set) in otherwise unchanged IQMD calculations changes the $dN/dy$ at midrapidity by about 30% for Ni(1.93 AGeV) + Ni central collisions.
- Without invoking in-medium mass changes, simulation programs miss the experimental $K^+$ cross sections by about a factor of 2.

## Context
This paper summarizes a decade of work on kaon production in nucleus-nucleus collisions, extending earlier theoretical calculations by Aichelin and collaborators (1980s) that first observed the EOS sensitivity of $K^+$ production. It contradicts the naive expectation that $K^-$ and $K^+$ yields should differ by orders of magnitude based on elementary pp cross sections, explaining the observed equality via secondary $K^-$ production from $\Lambda(\Sigma)\pi \rightarrow K^- N$ reactions at low density. The work builds on elementary cross-section measurements from the COSY program and in-medium mass calculations from chiral perturbation theory (Waas, 1996), the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Nebauer, 1999), and coupled channel approaches (Schafer, 1997). It enables future studies of kaon flow at AGS and SPS energies and raises puzzles about why thermal/statistical models describe kaon yields even at energies where rapidity distributions are incompatible with thermal equilibrium.
