# Dark Nucleosynthesis: Cross-sections and Astrophysical Signals

> We investigate dark matter bound-state formation and its implication for indirect-detection experiments. We focus on the case where dark matter is a baryon of a strongly-coupled dark sector and provide generic formulae for the formation of shallow nuclear bound states on emission of photons, and W and Z gauge bosons. These processes can occur via electric and magnetic transitions, and give rise to indirect signals that are testable in monochromatic and diffuse photon measurements by Fermi and HESS. We also study the validity of factorizing the bound-state formation cross section into a short-distance nuclear part multiplied by Sommerfeld-enhancement factors. We find that the short-distance nuclear potential often violates factorization, modifying in particular the location of the peaks associated with zero-energy bound states. Finally we revisit bound-state formation of a (weakly-coupled) Minimal DM quintuplet including isospin-breaking effects, and find it gives rise to indirect-detection signals that are compatible with current bounds.

# Dark Nucleosynthesis: Cross-sections and Astrophysical Signals

## Key claim

Bound-state formation cross sections for dark matter baryons in strongly-coupled dark sectors cannot be reliably factorized into a short-distance nuclear part multiplied by Sommerfeld-enhancement factors, because the short-distance nuclear potential modifies the location of zero-energy bound-state peaks; the authors provide general formulae for bound-state formation via electric and magnetic transitions and find that for a Minimal DM quintuplet including isospin-breaking effects, the indirect-detection signals are compatible with current bounds.

## Method

The authors compute bound-state formation cross sections for dark matter baryons (spin-1/2 SU(2)_L triplets from a confining dark sector) by solving the full Schroedinger equation numerically with a potential that includes both a long-range Yukawa component from electroweak interactions and a short-range nuclear potential parametrized as a spherical well. They provide quantum-mechanical formulae for magnetic and electric dipole transitions with emission of photons, W, and Z gauge bosons, and compare the full overlap-integral results to factorized approximations where the Sommerfeld enhancement is computed using only the long-range potential or the full potential evaluated at different radii.

## Key numbers

- The neutral component of the dark baryon triplet has a 165-MeV mass splitting from the charged component due to electroweak loop effects.
- For an SU(2) quintuplet, bound-state formation increases the mass that reproduces the critical relic abundance from 9.5 TeV (Cirelli 2007) to approximately 14 TeV.
- For the U(1) toy model, zero-energy s-wave bound states supported by a Yukawa potential alone occur at critical masses M_* = {1.65, 6.4, 14.5, ...} times M_V/alpha_D.
- The magnetic dipole cross section for shallow bound states at low velocity scales as (E_B/M)^(3/2) times (1 - M_a^2/E_B^2)^(3/2), with a prefactor containing (1 - a_r * gamma_r')^2 where a_r and 1/gamma_r' are scattering lengths of initial and final channels.
- The expansion parameter for the nuclear effective theory is sqrt(M E_B)/M_pi; in the Standard Model this is approximately 0.3, giving higher-order corrections of order 10 percent.

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of hydrogen-like dark matter bound states in weakly-coupled gauge theories (Mitridate 2017) to the strongly-coupled regime where dark matter is a baryon of a confining dark sector (Redi 2018). It builds on the observation by Bethe and Longmire (1950) and later nuclear effective theory (Kaplan 1998, Savage 1998) that shallow bound-state phenomenology depends only weakly on the underlying potential once the binding energy is fixed. The results enable predictions for monochromatic photon lines and diffuse photon signals from bound-state formation that are testable with Fermi and HESS observations of the Galactic center, and they clarify when the standard factorization of Sommerfeld enhancement and short-distance cross section breaks down due to finite-size effects and shifts in zero-energy bound states.
