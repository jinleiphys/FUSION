# Why does neutron transfer play different roles in sub-barrier fusion reactions $^{32}$S+$^{94,96}$Zr and $^{40}$Ca+$^{94,96}$Zr?

> The sub-barrier capture (fusion) reactions $^{32}$S+$^{90,94,96}$Zr, $^{36}$S+$^{90,96}$Zr, $^{40}$Ca+$^{90,94,96}$Zr, and $^{48}$Ca+$^{90,96}$Zr with positive and negative $Q$-values for neutron transfer are studied within the quantum diffusion approach and the universal fusion function representation. For these systems, the s-wave capture probabilities are extracted from the experimental excitation functions and are also analyzed. Different effects of the positive $Q_{xn}$-value neutron transfer in the fusion enhancement are revealed in the relatively close reactions $^{32}$S+$^{94,96}$Zr and $^{40}$Ca+$^{94,96}$Zr.

## Key claim

The influence of positive-Q-value neutron transfer on sub-barrier fusion enhancement depends critically on whether the acceptor nucleus is spherical or strongly deformed: in reactions like $^{40}$Ca+$^{94,96}$Zr the transfer strongly enhances fusion because the acceptor ($^{40}$Ca) is spherical and becomes deformed after transfer, whereas in $^{32}$S+$^{94,96}$Zr the acceptor ($^{32}$S) is already strongly deformed and the transfer only weakly suppresses fusion.

## Method

The authors applied the quantum diffusion approach, which treats the relative distance between colliding nuclei as a single collective variable with fluctuation and dissipation effects, to calculate capture cross sections. They also used the universal fusion function representation to compare reduced experimental cross sections and extracted s-wave capture probabilities from experimental excitation functions by differentiating $E_{\text{c.m.}}\sigma_{\text{cap}}(E_{\text{c.m.}})$. The two-neutron transfer was assumed to occur just before capture, populating the first excited $2^+$ state in the recipient nucleus, and the resulting changes in quadrupole deformation parameters $\beta_2$ were used to modify the nucleus-nucleus potential.

## Key numbers

- $Q_{2n}=4.9$ MeV for $^{40}$Ca+$^{94}$Zr $\rightarrow$ $^{42}$Ca+$^{92}$Zr, with $\beta_2$ changing from 0 to 0.25 for Ca and from 0.09 to 0.10 for Zr
- $Q_{2n}=5.5$ MeV for $^{40}$Ca+$^{96}$Zr $\rightarrow$ $^{42}$Ca+$^{94}$Zr, with $\beta_2$ changing from 0 to 0.25 for Ca and from 0.08 to 0.09 for Zr
- $Q_{2n}=5.1$ MeV for $^{32}$S+$^{94}$Zr $\rightarrow$ $^{34}$S+$^{92}$Zr, with $\beta_2$ changing from 0.31 to 0.25 for S and from 0.09 to 0.10 for Zr
- $Q_{2n}=5.7$ MeV for $^{32}$S+$^{96}$Zr $\rightarrow$ $^{34}$S+$^{94}$Zr, with $\beta_2$ changing from 0.31 to 0.25 for S and from 0.08 to 0.09 for Zr
- Ground-state deformation parameters: $^{32}$S $\beta_2=0.31$, $^{36}$S spherical, $^{40}$Ca $\beta_2=0$, $^{94}$Zr $\beta_2=0.09$, $^{96}$Zr $\beta_2=0.08$

## Context

This work extends the quantum diffusion approach previously applied by the same group (Sargsyan et al., 2012-2014) to explain why neutron transfer enhances fusion in some systems but not others. It contradicts the simple correlation between positive $Q_{xn}$-values and fusion enhancement suggested by Broglia (1983) for $^{58}$Ni+$^{64}$Ni, and instead shows that the effect is mediated by deformation changes. The results explain the contrasting behavior observed experimentally by Timmers (1998) and Stefanini (2007) for $^{40}$Ca+$^{94,96}$Zr versus the weak transfer effects seen by Scarlassara (2000) for $^{60}$Ni+$^{100}$Mo and by Liang (2007) for $^{18}$O+Sn isotopes. The findings enable predictions for future experiments on systems such as $^{60,62}$Ni+$^{150}$Nd and $^{28}$Si,$^{32}$S+Sn isotopes.
