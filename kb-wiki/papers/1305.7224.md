# Neutral-pion reactions induced by chiral anomaly in strong magnetic fields

> We investigate decay and production of neutral pions in strong magnetic fields. In the presence of strong magnetic fields, transition between a neutral pion and a virtual photon becomes possible through the triangle diagram relevant for the chiral anomaly. We find that the decay mode of a neutral pion into two photons cannot persist in the dominant mode in strong magnetic fields, and that decay into a dilepton instead dominates over the other modes. We also investigate the effects of magnetic fields on prompt virtual photons created in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. There is no anisotropy in the spectrum at the stage of creation of prompt virtual photons, but after traversing the strong magnetic field that is induced perpendicularly to the reaction plane, virtual photons turn into neutral pions, leading to an anisotropic spectrum of dileptons as a feasible signature in the measurement.

## Key claim

In strong magnetic fields, the dominant decay mode of a neutral pion shifts from the standard two-photon decay to decay into a dilepton (electron-positron pair), with the crossover occurring when the magnetic field strength approaches the pion mass squared scale |eB| ~ m_² or when the pion carries large energy.

## Method

The authors computed decay rates for neutral pions in a constant external magnetic field using the Wess-Zumino-Witten (WZW) effective vertex that couples a neutral pion to two photon fields. They separated the photon field into dynamical and external components, obtaining a three-point vertex that allows direct conversion of a neutral pion into a single virtual photon in the presence of the external field, which then decays into a dilepton pair. The decay rate for this magnetic-field-induced mode was calculated using the free photon propagator and the one-loop vacuum polarization tensor, and compared with the standard two-photon and Dalitz decay rates.

## Key numbers

- The two-photon decay width in vacuum is Γ₂γ = 7.62 eV (theoretical), compared to the experimental value Γ₂γ^exp = 7.74 ± 0.37 eV.
- The Dalitz decay width at rest is Γ_Dalitz^rest = 0.0909 eV.
- The critical magnetic field for neutral pions is defined as B^c_π = m_π²/e, where m_π ≈ 135 MeV.
- Magnetic fields in magnetars reach |B| ~ 10¹¹ T, and in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC reach |B| ~ 10¹³ T, providing a scale |eB| ~ m_π².
- The magnetic-field-induced decay rate Γ_Be⁺e⁻ scales as (λ·eB/m_π²)² and depends on the squared parallel momentum q_∥² = ω_π² - |q|² cos²θ, where θ is the angle relative to the magnetic field direction.

## Context

This work extends the understanding of neutral pion reactions beyond the standard Primakoff effect (Primakoff, 1951) and the classic chiral anomaly results of Bell and Jackiw (1969) and Adler (1969). It connects to studies of strong magnetic fields in magnetars (Duncan and Thompson, 1992; Thompson and Duncan, 1995, 1996) and in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions (Kharzeev, McLerran, and Warringa, 2008; Skokov, Illarionov, and Toneev, 2009; Bzdak and Skokov, 2012; Deng and Huang, 2012). The proposed anisotropic dilepton signal from prompt virtual photons converting to neutral pions in the magnetic field could provide a new observable in heavy-ion collision experiments, related to the elliptic flow coefficient v₂ measured by PHENIX (Adare et al., 2010).
