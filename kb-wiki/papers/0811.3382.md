# Chiral dynamics, structure of Lambda(1405), and Kbar N phenomenology

> We investigate the structure of the Lambda(1405) resonance and Kbar N phenomenology in the perspective of chiral SU(3) dynamics. Utilizing the chiral coupled-channel approach which well describes the Kbar N scattering observable, we perform three different analyses to clarify the structure of the Lambda(1405) resonance. The results consistently indicate the meson-baryon molecule picture of the Lambda(1405). We argue the consequence of the chiral dynamics in Kbar N phenomenology and the antikaon bound state in nucleus, emphasizing the important role of the strong pi Sigma interaction.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the Λ(1405) resonance is dominated by a meson-baryon molecule structure rather than a three-quark state, based on three independent analyses within the chiral coupled-channel framework. The key hedge is that the conclusion depends on the specific renormalization procedure and the choice of cutoff in the loop function, which can introduce or remove CDD pole contributions.

## Method

The author employs the chiral coupled-channel approach, using the leading-order Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction from chiral perturbation theory and solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation with unitarity constraints. Three distinct analyses are performed: (1) a renormalization analysis classifying resonance origin as dynamical (molecule) or CDD pole (elementary), (2) a large-Nc scaling analysis to probe quark content, and (3) an electromagnetic study computing the charge radius by coupling an external photon field to the meson-baryon loops. An effective single-channel Kbar N potential is then derived from the coupled-channel framework to study kaonic nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The electric mean squared radius of the Λ(1405) is about 2 fm² with negative sign, much larger than the ground state nucleon.
- The binding energy of the K⁻pp system calculated with the chiral effective potential is about 20 MeV.
- The resonance position in the Kbar N amplitude appears at around 1420 MeV, not at the nominal 1405 MeV, giving a binding energy of 15 MeV measured from the Kbar N threshold.
- The diagonal coupling strengths in the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction are C(Kbar N)=3 and C(πΣ)=4.

## Context

This work extends the chiral coupled-channel approach previously applied to S=0 nucleon resonances and mesonic sectors, and directly addresses the long-standing difficulty of constituent quark models in describing the Λ(1405) mass. The results contradict the phenomenological potential of Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002), which treats the Λ(1405) as a Feshbach resonance with zero diagonal πΣ interaction; here the strong πΣ attraction governed by SU(3) flavor symmetry reduces the effective Kbar N attraction. The findings enable more realistic calculations of kaonic nuclei, such as the K⁻pp system studied by Dote et al., and clarify the two-pole structure of the Λ(1405) identified by Jido et al. (2003).
