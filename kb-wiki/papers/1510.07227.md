# Finite-temperature phase transition of $N_{f}=3$ QCD with exact center symmetry

> For the $Z_{3}$-symmetric lattice QCD-like theory ($Z_3$-QCD), in which $SU(3)$ gauge theory is coupled with three fundamental Wilson quarks with flavor-dependent twisted boundary conditions, we calculate the expectation values of Polyakov loop and chiral condensate as functions of temperature on $16^3 \times4$ and $20^3 \times 4$ lattices with $m_{PS}/m_{V}=0.70$ fixed. We find the first-order phase transition with respect to the $Z_{3}$ center symmetry, where the Polyakov loop exhibits a hysteresis depending on the initial condition of thermalization process. We also show that the crossover behavior of chiral condensate around the critical temperature of the center transition and the manifestation of flavor symmetry breaking in the high-temperature phase.

# Finite-temperature phase transition of $N_{f}=3$ QCD with exact center symmetry

## Key claim
The $Z_{3}$-symmetric QCD-like theory ($Z_{3}$-QCD) exhibits a first-order phase transition for the $Z_{3}$ center symmetry, as indicated by a hysteresis in the Polyakov loop depending on the initial condition (cold start vs. hot start), but the precise critical temperature in the large volume and continuum limits remains undetermined.

## Method
The authors performed lattice QCD simulations using the Iwasaki gauge action with naive Wilson fermions, implementing flavor-dependent twisted boundary conditions on three fundamental quarks to preserve exact $Z_{3}$ center symmetry. They calculated the expectation values of the Polyakov loop and the subtracted chiral condensate on $16^3 \times 4$ and $20^3 \times 4$ lattices with a fixed pseudoscalar-to-vector meson mass ratio $m_{PS}/m_{V}=0.70$, using the Rational Hybrid Monte Carlo (RHMC) algorithm. Configurations were generated from both cold start (all link variables set to unity) and hot start (random link variables) initial conditions to probe hysteresis.

## Key numbers
- Simulations were performed on $16^3 \times 4$ and $20^3 \times 4$ lattices with $m_{PS}/m_{V}=0.70$ fixed.
- The hopping parameter $\kappa$ was tuned for each $\beta$ value across the range $\beta = 1.30$ to $2.20$; for example, $\kappa = 0.2019$ at $\beta = 1.30$ and $\kappa = 0.1539$ at $\beta = 2.20$.
- For the $Z_{3}$-QCD model with cold start, the magnitude of the Polyakov loop shows a clear jump from zero to non-zero values in the region $1.55 \le \beta \le 1.60$ on both lattice sizes.
- For the $Z_{3}$-QCD model with hot start, the jump occurs in the region $1.80 \le \beta \le 1.90$ on $16^3 \times 4$ and $1.90 \le \beta \le 1.95$ on $20^3 \times 4$.
- The hysteresis in the Polyakov loop spans the range $1.55 < \beta < 1.90$.
- Zero-temperature simulations on $16^4$ lattices generated $2,000$ to $3,000$ trajectories; finite-temperature simulations used $500$ to $5,000$ trajectories.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework of $Z_{3}$-QCD developed by Kouno (2012), Sakai (2012), and Kouno (2013, 2015), which proposed that flavor-dependent twisted boundary conditions preserve exact center symmetry in $SU(3)$ gauge theory with fundamental quarks. The numerical results confirm the first-order nature of the center phase transition predicted by these earlier studies and show that the chiral crossover occurs around the same temperature range, with flavor symmetry breaking manifesting only in the high-temperature phase. The study enables future precise determination of the critical temperature in the large-volume and continuum limits, and raises questions about whether the chiral transition temperature in $Z_{3}$-QCD coincides with that of standard three-flavor QCD, as discussed by Roberge (1986) and D'Elia (2002) in the context of imaginary chemical potential.
