# Coherent Description for Hitherto Unexplained Radioactivities by Super- and Hyperdeformed Isomeric States

> Recently long-lived high spin super- and hyperdeformed isomeric states with unusual radioactive decay properties have been discovered. Based on these newly observed modes of radioactive decay, consistent interpretations are suggested for previously unexplained phenomena seen in nature. These are the Po halos, the low-energy enhanced 4.5 MeV alpha-particle group proposed to be due to an isotope of a superheavy element with Z = 108, and the giant halos.

# Coherent Description for Hitherto Unexplained Radioactivities by Super- and Hyperdeformed Isomeric States

## Key claim
Long-lived super- and hyperdeformed isomeric states with unusual decay properties can provide a consistent explanation for three previously unexplained natural phenomena (Po halos, a 4.5 MeV alpha-particle group attributed to Z=108, and giant halos), but the interpretations rely on specific deformation parameters and the assumption that these isomeric states exist in the relevant mass regions.

## Method
The authors propose that superdeformed (SD) and hyperdeformed (HD) isomeric states, previously observed in heavy-ion reactions (16O + 197Au and 28Si + 181Ta), can decay via enhanced or retarded alpha-particle transitions between minima of the nuclear potential. They apply this framework to reinterpret existing observations of Po halos in mica, a low-energy 4.5 MeV alpha group detected in minerals, and giant halos, by calculating half-lives for HD-to-HD alpha transitions using deformation parameters from the literature and extrapolating from known data on Cm and Fm isotopes.

## Key numbers
- The 4.5 MeV alpha-particle group has an estimated half-life of (2.5 ± 0.5) × 10^8 y (Cherdyntsev 1963), which is too short by a factor of 10^8 compared to predictions for normal 4.5 MeV alpha decay from Hs (Z=108) that give about 5 × 10^16 y.
- For a HD-to-HD 4.5 MeV alpha transition in 271Hs, calculated half-lives range from 1.3 × 10^8 y (using deformation parameters from Cwiok 1994 for 232Th) to 4.6 × 10^9 y and 1.8 × 10^11 y for other parameter sets.
- For a 4.8 MeV HD-to-HD alpha transition from 282114, the predicted half-life is 10^8 to 10^11 y.
- For a 5.1 MeV HD-to-HD alpha transition from 316126, the predicted half-life using Cwiok (1994) parameters is 3 × 10^11 y.
- Normal 10 MeV alpha particles from Z=114 nuclei have predicted half-life of about 1 s; for 13 MeV alpha particles from Z=126 nuclei, about 10^-4 s.

## Context
This work extends the authors' prior experimental findings of unusual alpha and proton radioactivities from SD and HD isomeric states in heavy-ion reactions (Marinov 1996a, 1996b, 2001a, 2001b) to explain natural phenomena that have remained puzzling since the observations of Gentry (1968, 1970) on Po halos and giant halos, and Cherdyntsev (1963) on the 4.5 MeV alpha group. The interpretation contradicts standard predictions for superheavy element decay (Viola 1966, Royer 2000) by invoking deformation-enhanced lifetimes. The proposed scenarios enable a search for superheavy elements in nature using cold fusion reactions such as 208Pb + 60Fe → 267Hs + n, 208Pb + 74Ge → 282114, and 232Th + 84Kr → 316126.
