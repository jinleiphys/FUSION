# Multiple $SO(5)$ isovector pairing and seniority $Sp(2Ω)$ multi-$j$ algebras with isospin

> With nucleons occupying several shell model $j$ orbits, the isovector pair creation operator $A^1_μ$ (creates a two particle state with angular momentum $J=0$ and isospin $T=1$) is no longer unique. Choosing it to be a sum of single-$j$ isovector pair creation operators each with a phase, there will be multiple pair $SO(5)$ algebras with isospin; with $r$ number of $j$ orbits, there will be $2^{r-1}$ $SO(5)$ algebras each with a corresponding complementary $Sp(2Ω)$ algebra [$2Ω= \sum_j (2j+1)$] that gives seniority and reduced isospin quantum numbers. Three applications of multiple $SO(5)$ algebras are presented demonstrating the usefulness of considering $SO(5)$ pairing algebras with general sign factors.

# Multiple SO(5) isovector pairing and seniority Sp(2Ω) multi-j algebras with isospin

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that when nucleons occupy several shell-model j-orbits, there exist 2^(r-1) distinct SO(5) pairing algebras (where r is the number of j-orbits), each with a complementary Sp(2Ω) algebra, depending on the choice of sign factors β_j = ±1 in the generalized isovector pair creation operator; these multiple algebras are useful for understanding selection rules and transition strengths, but the eigenstate labels (seniority v and reduced isospin t) do not depend on the sign choice, only the explicit wavefunction structure does.

## Method

The authors construct generalized isovector pair creation operators as linear combinations of single-j isovector pair operators with phase factors β_j = ±1, and show that these operators together with the isospin generators and number operator form an SO(5) algebra for each sign set. They then derive the complementary Sp(2Ω) algebra generators with a specific phase condition X(j1,j2,k) = (-1)^(j1+j2+k) β_j1 β_j2, and construct many-particle matrix elements of the pairing Hamiltonian using SO(5) ⊃ SO(3) ⊗ U(1) reduced Wigner coefficients. Numerical diagonalization is performed for a two-orbit system with Ω1=6 and Ω2=5 (appropriate for A=56-80 nuclei) with six nucleons at T=0.

## Key numbers

- With r number of j-orbits, there are 2^(r-1) distinct SO(5) algebras and corresponding Sp(2Ω) algebras
- 2Ω = Σ_j (2j+1) is the total degeneracy; for the two-orbit example, Ω1=6 and Ω2=5
- For the m=6, T=0 system with two orbits, there are 24 basis states
- Eigenvalues of the pairing Hamiltonian H_p^(β) for (m=6,T=0) are 0, -44/m^2, and -84/m^2 for (v,t) = (6,0), (4,1), and (2,0) respectively, with degeneracies 13, 9, and 2
- For the m=4, T=1 system, eigenvalues are -44/m^2, -40/m^2, and 0 for (v,t) = (2,1), (2,0), and (4,1) respectively, with degeneracies 3, 2, and 9

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous results on multiple SU(2) pairing algebras for identical nucleons (Kota, 2020) to include isospin degrees of freedom via SO(5) algebras, and complements investigations of multiple SU(3) algebras (msu3-1, msu3-2). The mathematical foundation builds on the early work of Helmers (1961), Flowers (1952, 1964), and Kerman (1961) on single-j shell SO(5) and Sp(2j+1) algebras, as well as the general complementarity theory of Neergard based on Howe's duality theorem. The results enable systematic study of selection rules for electromagnetic transitions and two-particle transfer reactions in multi-j shell model calculations with isospin, and allow investigation of order-chaos-order transitions by varying the pairing Hamiltonian parameters ξ and α.
