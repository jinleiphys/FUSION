# Nuclear Physics on the Light Front - a new old way to do an old new problem

> A brief introduction to light front techniques is presented. This is followed by a review of recent attempts to perform realistic, relativistic nuclear physics with those techniques.

## Key claim

Light-front dynamics can be used to compute realistic nuclear wave functions and plus-momentum distributions, but the mean-field approximation yields nucleons carrying only 65% of the nuclear plus-momentum (far less than the ~90% needed to explain the EMC effect), and the vector meson contribution is concentrated at unobservable \(k^+ = 0\); going beyond mean-field via Brueckner theory raises the nucleon plus-momentum share to 81%, which is a vast improvement but still not sufficient for a satisfactory description of nuclear deep inelastic scattering data.

## Method

The author applies light-front quantization to a Lagrangian containing scalar and vector mesons coupled to nucleons (a Walecka-type model). For infinite nuclear matter, mean-field theory replaces meson fields by constant expectation values, yielding a self-consistent Fermi gas solution; for finite nuclei, a constrained variational procedure fixes total plus-momentum to restore rotational invariance. Beyond mean-field, a light-front one-boson-exchange potential is used in a Brueckner-type correlated calculation that includes nucleon-nucleon correlations and explicit mesonic Fock-space components.

## Key numbers

- In mean-field infinite nuclear matter with parameters from Chin and Walecka: \(k_F = 1.42\ \text{fm}^{-1}\), binding energy per nucleon = 15.75 MeV, effective nucleon mass \(M + g_s\phi = 0.56 M\).
- Nucleons carry 65% of the nuclear plus-momentum in mean-field infinite nuclear matter; vector mesons carry 35%.
- For finite nuclei \(^{16}\text{O}\), \(^{40}\text{Ca}\), and \(^{80}\text{Zr}\) in mean-field theory, vector mesons carry approximately 30% of the nuclear plus-momentum.
- In the correlated (Brueckner) calculation for infinite nuclear matter: binding energy per nucleon = 14.71 MeV at \(k_F = 1.35\ \text{fm}^{-1}\), compressibility = 180 MeV.
- In the correlated calculation, nucleons carry 81% of the nuclear plus-momentum (versus 65% in mean-field).
- The correlated calculation finds about 0.05 excess pions per nucleon.
- The minimum value of the ratio \(F_{2A}/F_{2N}\) is increased by a factor of twenty toward the data compared to the mean-field result.

## Context

This work extends the light-front formalism to realistic nuclear structure calculations, motivated by the EMC effect discovered by Aubert et al. (1982) and Arnold et al. (1984). It directly addresses the challenge raised by Geesaman et al. (1995) that little experience existed in calculating realistic nuclear wave functions in light-front dynamics. The mean-field results reproduce standard Walecka-model energies and densities (Chin and Walecka), but the large vector-meson plus-momentum fraction at \(k^+ = 0\) makes the momentum sum rule unusable for analyzing deep inelastic data. The correlated Brueckner calculation improves the description of nuclear deep inelastic scattering and yields a 5% pionic enhancement consistent within errors with the Drell-Yan data of Alde et al. (1990), which showed no enhancement. Future work should include two-particle-two-hole states and apply the correlated approach to finite nuclei.
