# Ab initio multi-shell valence-space Hamiltonians and the island of inversion

> In the shell-model framework, valence-space Hamiltonians connecting multiple major-oscillator shells are of key interest for investigating the physics of neutron-rich nuclei, which have been the subject of intense experimental activity for decades. Here we present an extension of the ab initio valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group which allows the derivation of such Hamiltonians nonperturbatively. Starting from initial two- and three-nucleon forces from chiral effective field theory, we then calculate properties of nuclei in the important island-of-inversion region above oxygen, so far unexplored with ab initio methods. Our results in the neon and magnesium isotopes indicate the importance of neutron excitation from the $sd$ to $pf$ shells and ground states dominated by intruder configurations around $N=20$, consistent with the conclusions from phenomenological studies. We also benchmark the excitation spectrum of $^{16}$O with coupled-cluster theory, finding generally good agreement, and discuss implications for ground state energies and charge radii in oxygen and calcium isotopes. Finally we outline the proper procedure for treating the long-standing issue of center-of-mass contamination, and show that with a particular choice of valence space, these spurious states can be removed successfully.

## Key claim

The paper extends the ab initio valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) to derive multi-shell valence-space Hamiltonians nonperturbatively, enabling the first ab initio description of the island of inversion region above oxygen; however, the IMSRG(2) approximation (keeping only up to two-body terms) introduces systematic errors, leading to excitation energies that are systematically higher than experiment and B(E2) values that are systematically lower.

## Method

Starting from chiral effective field theory two- and three-nucleon forces (the 1.8/2.0(EM) interaction), the authors perform an IMSRG evolution using a new generator that includes an energy denominator shift Δ (set to 10 MeV) to avoid divergent flow when decoupling across multiple major oscillator shells. The effective valence-space Hamiltonian is derived in a harmonic oscillator basis with e_max=12 and E_3max=16, keeping up to two-body terms (IMSRG(2) approximation), and the final valence-space problem is solved by exact diagonalization using the KSHELL code. Center-of-mass contamination is treated by adding a Gloeckner-Lawson term β H_cm at the beginning of the calculation, before the IMSRG transformation, and results are checked for β-independence.

## Key numbers

- The energy denominator shift Δ is set to 10 MeV; the dependence of computed energies on Δ between 10 and 20 MeV is "several tens keV".
- For the 1.8/2.0(EM) interaction, the rms deviation from experiment for ground-state energies across light and medium-mass regions is roughly 3.5 MeV (Holt 2019).
- The IMSRG evolution is performed in a space with e_max=12 and E_3max=16.
- For the N²LO_sat interaction benchmark of ¹⁶O, calculations use e_max=12 and E_3max=14 with β=3 for the center-of-mass term.
- The 0⁺₂ state in ¹⁶O has 1p1h, 3p3h, and 5p5h components of 77%, 22%, and 1%, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the VS-IMSRG method (Stroberg 2017, 2019) to multi-shell valence spaces, which was previously challenging due to intruder state problems (Schucan 1972, 1973). It provides the first ab initio description of the island of inversion region above oxygen, where previous studies relied on phenomenological or quasi-microscopic models such as the extended Kuo-Krenciglowa method (Tsunoda 2014, 2017) or large-scale shell model calculations (Caurier 2014). The results enable future ab initio investigations of neutron-rich nuclei near N=20 and N=28, though the authors note that for N≥26 a different valence space may be needed to discuss the N=28 gap.
