# Benchmarking neutrino-nucleus quasielastic scattering model predictions against a missing energy profile obtained using a monoenergetic neutrino beam

> We examine three exclusive nuclear ground state shell models implemented in the NEUT neutrino event generator and benchmark them against the recent JSNS$^2$ measurement of missing energy using a monoenergetic neutrino source. The nature of the measurement allows a detailed investigation of nuclear ground-state modeling using a neutrino source, and gives access to a direct measurement of the neutron spectral function in a $^{12}$C nucleus. The NEUT intranuclear cascade and nuclear deexcitation \textsc{NucDeEx} are used to simulate inelastic final-state interactions and nuclear deexcitations respectively. We find that the spectral function (SF) models perform better than relativistic mean field models in modeling both the ground state and the tail of the missing energy distribution when the NEUT cascade and nuclear excitation channels are turned on. We also find that taking into account the missing energy threshold for single nucleon knockout interactions results in all nuclear models being accepted based on the obtained $p$-values.

## Key claim

The spectral function (SF) models perform better than relativistic mean field (ED-RMF) models in modeling both the ground state and the tail of the missing energy distribution when the NEUT cascade and nuclear excitation channels are turned on; however, when the missing energy threshold for single nucleon knockout interactions is taken into account, all nuclear models are accepted based on the obtained p-values.

## Method

The authors generated neutrino-nucleus interactions using the NEUT event generator (version 6.0.3) with a monoenergetic neutrino energy of 235.5 MeV, implementing three exclusive shell-based models: two versions of the Benhar Spectral Function (SF and SF*) and the energy-dependent relativistic mean field (ED-RMF) model. They compared the simulated missing energy distributions against the JSNS^2 measurement, performing chi-squared analyses with and without applying a single nucleon knockout (1NKO) threshold at 18.72 MeV, and sequentially turning on the NEUT intranuclear cascade and the NucDeEx nuclear deexcitation routine.

## Key numbers

- The KDAR neutrino energy is 235.5 MeV, produced by kaon decay at rest (K+ -> mu+ nu_mu) with a branching ratio of 63.6%
- The 1NKO threshold (neutron separation energy) for 12C is 18.72 MeV
- The SF model with NEUT cascade and NucDeEx deexcitation, without 1NKO threshold, yields chi^2/ndof = 12.68/16 with p-value = 0.70 (accepted)
- The SF* model with NEUT cascade and NucDeEx deexcitation, without 1NKO threshold, yields chi^2/ndof = 38.96/16 with p-value = 0.00 (rejected)
- The ED-RMF model with NEUT cascade and NucDeEx deexcitation, without 1NKO threshold, yields chi^2/ndof = 28.48/16 with p-value = 0.03 (rejected)
- With the 1NKO threshold applied and both cascade and deexcitation active, all three models are accepted: SF (chi^2/ndof = 8.89/13, p = 0.78), SF* (chi^2/ndof = 19.05/13, p = 0.12), ED-RMF (chi^2/ndof = 17.54/13, p = 0.18)
- The SF model uses axial mass M_A^QE = 1.03 GeV/c^2; the ED-RMF model uses M_A^QE = 1.05 GeV/c^2
- The neutron spectral function correction for carbon applies a shift Delta_E = 2.76 MeV relative to the proton spectral function
- Pauli blocking uses a Fermi surface momentum p_F = 209 MeV
- Short-range correlations (SRCs) account for approximately 10% of the total integrated SF probability, contributing about 1% to the CCQE cross section at 235.5 MeV

## Context

This work benchmarks nuclear models against the first JSNS^2 measurement of missing energy using a monoenergetic neutrino source from kaon decay at rest, extending prior validation efforts that relied on electron scattering data (Benhar 1994, Benhar 2005) and earlier neutrino measurements (Gonzalez-Jimenez 2019, Franco-Patino 2022). The results enable improved modeling of neutrino-nucleus interactions for oscillation experiments such as T2K (Abe 2011), NOvA, Hyper-Kamiokande (2025), and DUNE, by demonstrating that the NEUT cascade and deexcitation routines are essential for achieving agreement with data, and that the 1NKO threshold must be considered when evaluating model performance.
