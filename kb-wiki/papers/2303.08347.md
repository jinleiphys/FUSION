# Colloquium: Gravitational Form Factors of the Proton

> The physics of the gravitational form factors of the proton, and their understanding within quantum chromodynamics, has advanced significantly in the past two decades through both theory and experiment. This Colloquium provides an overview of this progress, highlights the physical insights unveiled by studies of gravitational form factors, and reviews their interpretation in terms of the mechanical properties of the proton.

# Gravitational Form Factors of the Proton

## Key claim
The gravitational form factors of the proton, accessible through deeply virtual Compton scattering and related processes, provide a framework for extracting the mechanical properties (pressure, shear forces, and mass distribution) of the proton from experimental data, though the interpretation relies on model-dependent assumptions and the deconvolution of generalized parton distributions from cross-section measurements remains an open problem.

## Method
The paper reviews the theoretical framework connecting gravitational form factors to matrix elements of the energy-momentum tensor in quantum chromodynamics, and surveys experimental extractions from deeply virtual Compton scattering data measured at Jefferson Lab. The extraction procedure involves fitting the beam-spin asymmetry and cross-section data to obtain the Compton form factor H, from which the gravitational form factors A(t), B(t), and D(t) are derived through dispersion relations and sum rules. Lattice quantum chromodynamics calculations provide complementary determinations of these form factors from first principles.

## Key numbers
- Proton magnetic moment measured as approximately 2.5 nuclear magnetons, compared to the Dirac prediction of 1 nuclear magneton for a pointlike spin-1/2 particle
- Neutron magnetic moment measured as approximately -1.5 nuclear magnetons, confirming non-pointlike structure
- Proton charge radius first determined as (0.74 ± 0.24) fm from elastic electron-proton scattering
- Nucleon mass approximately 940 MeV/c^2
- Strong coupling constant α_s(91 GeV) ≈ 0.12 at the Z-boson mass scale
- Quarks and antiquarks carry only half of the nucleon's momentum between them, with gluons accounting for the remainder

## Context
This Colloquium synthesizes two decades of progress following the initial theoretical work by Ji (1997) on the relation between generalized parton distributions and gravitational form factors, and the experimental program at Jefferson Lab that began extracting these quantities from deeply virtual Compton scattering data. The review extends earlier work on electromagnetic form factors (Sachs, 1962) and parton distribution functions from deep inelastic scattering (Bloom, 1969; Feynman, 1969) by connecting these to the mechanical properties of the proton. The results enable future studies at the Electron-Ion Collider and with positron beams to resolve remaining ambiguities in the deconvolution of generalized parton distributions and to access the gluon contributions to gravitational form factors.
