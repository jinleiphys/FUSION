# Review of anisotropic flow correlations in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Anisotropic flow phenomena is a key probe of the existence of Quark-Gluon Plasma. Several new observable associated with correlations between anisotropic flow harmonics are developed, which are expected to be sensitive to the initial fluctuations and transport properties of the created matter in heavy ion collisions. I review recent developments of correlations of anisotropic flow harmonics. The experimental measurements, together with the comparisons to theoretical model calculations, open up new opportunities of exploring novel QCD dynamics in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

This review argues that correlations between different anisotropic flow harmonics, measured via new observables like Symmetric Cumulants and Event Shape Engineering, provide a more discriminating probe of both the initial-state fluctuations and the specific shear viscosity of the Quark-Gluon Plasma than individual flow harmonic measurements alone, but that no existing theoretical calculation can quantitatively describe all the experimental data simultaneously.

## Method

The paper reviews two experimental approaches to study correlations between flow harmonics of different orders (v2, v3, v4, etc.) in Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC. Event Shape Engineering selects events based on the magnitude of the reduced flow vector to study how v3 and v4 change with v2 within a fixed centrality class. Symmetric Cumulants SC(m,n) = ⟨v_m^2 v_n^2⟩ − ⟨v_m^2⟩⟨v_n^2⟩ are four-particle cumulants that isolate correlations between flow harmonic magnitudes independent of symmetry plane angles and suppress non-flow contributions. The review compares experimental results from ALICE and ATLAS to calculations from AMPT, EKRT, and VISH2+1 hydrodynamic models with various initial conditions and η/s values.

## Key numbers

- SC(4,2) is positive for all centralities in Pb-Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV, indicating a correlation between v2 and v4 fluctuations
- SC(3,2) is negative for all centralities in Pb-Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV, indicating an anti-correlation between v2 and v3 fluctuations
- In AMPT calculations, decreasing the partonic cross section from 10 mb to 3 mb (higher shear viscosity) decreases the strength of SC(4,2)
- The normalized NSC(3,2) is compatible with its corresponding initial-state observable SC^ε(3,2), suggesting it can directly constrain initial conditions without precise knowledge of transport properties
- For v2-v4 correlations measured via ESE, the non-linear component of v4 from v2 increases dramatically with collision centrality and becomes dominant in the most peripheral collisions

## Context

This review extends the study of anisotropic flow beyond single harmonic measurements (v2, v3, v4) that established the QGP as a strongly coupled liquid with small η/s near the quantum limit 1/4π (Kovtun 2004). It builds on earlier work on symmetry plane correlations (Qiu 2011, Teaney 2013) and the realization that the Elliptic-Power function best describes single harmonic distributions (Yan 2014). The new SC(m,n) observables, proposed by Bilandzic (2013), enable quantitative comparisons between experimental data and hydrodynamic calculations that were not possible with earlier approaches. The inability of current models (EKRT, VISH2+1) to simultaneously describe SC(4,2) and SC(3,2) opens up the need for refined initial conditions and temperature-dependent η/s parameterizations.
