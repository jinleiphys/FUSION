# Regularization of a Light-Front Qqq Model

> We study the mass of the ground state of Qqq system using differents regularization schemes of the relativistic integral equation obtnaide with a flavor independente contact interaction in a QCD-inspired model. We calculated the masses of the spin 1/2 low-lying states of the $ﾎ嫂0$, $ﾎ嫂{+}_c$ and $ﾎ媽{b}^{0}$ for differentes values of the regularization cut-off parameter with a fixed nucleon mass. Our results are remarkable agreement with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The qualitative features of the binding energy of spin-1/2 low-lying Qqq baryons as a function of baryon mass are largely independent of the regularization scheme, provided the model uses a flavor-independent effective short-range contact interaction and the nucleon mass is fixed to its experimental value.

## Method

The authors solved coupled light-front Bethe-Salpeter integral equations for the Qqq system using a flavor-independent contact interaction between constituent quarks, with spin averaged out. They introduced a sharp transverse momentum cut-off (Lambda_perp) as the regularization parameter, dropping the previous constraint that virtual two-body subsystem masses be real, and adjusted the renormalized coupling constant for each cut-off value to reproduce the experimental nucleon mass. The heavy quark mass was then varied while keeping the interaction strength fixed, and the resulting baryon masses and binding energies were computed for three different cut-off values.

## Key numbers

- Constituent light quark mass: m_q = m_{u,d} = 0.386 GeV (taken from Sui02)
- Transverse momentum cut-off values used: 3 m_q, 5 m_q, and 7 m_q
- The bound Qqq system goes to the diquark threshold for baryon masses above 2.3 GeV
- Binding energy defined as B_{3B} = 2 m_q + m_Q - M_{3B}, where M_{3B} is the baryon mass

## Context

This work extends the earlier QCD-inspired light-front constituent quark model of Sui02, which used a special regularization scheme constraining virtual two-body subsystem masses to be real and qualitatively reproduced the binding of Lambda^0, Lambda_c^+, and Lambda_b^0. It also builds on the three-boson system regularization study of Beyer (2003) and the infinite cut-off limit analysis of Karmanov (2002). The results show that the qualitative conclusions of Sui02 are robust against changes in regularization scheme, supporting the model's predictive power for heavy baryon spectroscopy.
