# Screening of light mesons and charmonia at high temperature

> We present lattice QCD results for the screening masses of light mesons and charmonia. The lattice computations were performed with 2+1 flavors of improved staggered quarks using quark masses which correspond to realistic pion and kaon masses at zero temperature. For the light quark sector we have found that the screening masses in the pseudo-scalar and the isovector scalar channels do not become degenerate at the chiral crossover temperature indicating an effective non-restoration of the axial symmetry. Also the splitting between the vector and the pseudo-scalar screening masses persists even in the limit of zero lattice spacing and at a moderately high temperature around 420 MeV. In the charmonium sector our investigation shows that the screening masses of the pseudo-scalar and the vector charmonia are almost (within a few percent) equal to their zero temperature masses for temperatures less than 300 MeV. We also present results for the charmonium screening masses using periodic boundary conditions along the temporal direction and discuss their implications.

## Key claim
The paper presents lattice QCD evidence that the screening masses of pseudo-scalar and isovector scalar light mesons do not become degenerate at the chiral crossover temperature, indicating that effective restoration of the U_A(1) axial symmetry does not coincide with chiral symmetry restoration and occurs at a higher temperature. Additionally, the splitting between vector and pseudo-scalar screening masses persists even in the continuum limit at a temperature around 420 MeV.

## Method
The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with 2+1 flavors of improved staggered (p4fat3) quarks, using quark masses tuned to realistic zero-temperature pion and kaon masses (m_pi ~ 220 MeV, m_K ~ 500 MeV). They used three different temporal extents (N_tau = 4, 6, and 8) with spatial extents fixed at N_s = 4 N_tau, and analyzed screening masses from spatial correlation functions using eight different local staggered meson operators in the isovector scalar, pseudo-scalar, vector, and axial-vector channels for four quark combinations (bar u d, bar u s, bar s s, and bar c c). Charm quarks were treated in a partially-quenched approximation.

## Key numbers
- The chiral crossover temperature is T_pc ~ 190 MeV.
- At T ~ 420 MeV, the vector screening mass M^s_V remains above the free continuum limit of 2 pi T, while the pseudo-scalar screening mass M^s_PS remains well below 2 pi T, with the splitting persisting in the continuum limit.
- For charmonia, the ratios M^s_PS / m_eta_c and M^s_V / m_psi stay within 5% of 1 for temperatures less than 300 MeV.
- At T ~ 275 MeV, the relative difference between screening mass and zero-temperature pole mass is nearly zero in the bar c c sector, while it is ~90% in the bar s s sector.
- The difference between screening masses with periodic and anti-periodic boundary conditions is only a few percent at T ~ 220 MeV, but grows to ~20% or more for T ~ 300 MeV.

## Context
This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of mesonic screening masses reviewed by Karsch (2002) and more recent results from Laermann (2007) and others. The findings on the non-degeneracy of pseudo-scalar and scalar screening masses address the question of whether U_A(1) axial symmetry restoration coincides with chiral symmetry restoration in QCD. The charmonium results suggest that eta_c and J/psi may survive in the quark-gluon plasma up to temperatures of about 1.5 T_pc, enabling further studies of heavy quarkonium dissociation in hot QCD matter.
