# Overview of ALICE results in pp, pA and AA collisions

> The ALICE experiment at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) at CERN is optimized for recording events in the very large particle multiplicity environment of heavy-ion collisions at LHC energies. The ALICE collaboration has taken data in Pb-Pb collisions in Run I and Run II at nucleon-nucleon center-of-mass energies $\sqrt{s_{\text{NN}}}$ = 2.76 and \mbox{5.02 TeV}, respectively, and in pp collisions at center-of-mass energies $\sqrt{s}$ = 0.9, 2.76, 5.02, 7, 8 and 13 TeV. The asymmetric system p-Pb was measured at a center-of-mass energy $\sqrt{s_{\text{NN}}}$ = 5.02 TeV. Selected physics results from the analysis of these data are presented, and an outline of the ALICE prospects for Run III is given.

## Key claim

The ALICE experiment's measurements in pp, pA, and AA collisions at the LHC provide a comprehensive set of observables to study QCD, but the interpretation of medium effects in heavy-ion collisions requires disentangling initial cold-matter and final-state effects by comparing heavy-ion observables to corresponding quantities in pp and p-Pb collisions, with the caveat that many model comparisons show only partial agreement with data.

## Method

The ALICE collaboration recorded data in Pb-Pb collisions at nucleon-nucleon center-of-mass energies of 2.76 and 5.02 TeV, in pp collisions at 0.9, 2.76, 5.02, 7, 8, and 13 TeV, and in p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV. The detector systems (ITS, TPC, TRD, TOF, V0, muon spectrometer, electromagnetic calorimeters) provide track reconstruction, momentum measurement, and particle identification via dE/dx in the TPC and time-of-flight in the TOF. Physics analyses include multi-parton interactions, strangeness and charm production, pion source sizes from HBT correlations, azimuthal anisotropy coefficients, jet quenching, and low-mass dilepton spectra.

## Key numbers

- Pb-Pb collisions recorded at nucleon-nucleon center-of-mass energies sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV (Run I) and 5.02 TeV (Run II)
- pp collisions recorded at center-of-mass energies sqrt(s) = 0.9, 2.76, 5.02, 7, 8, and 13 TeV
- p-Pb collisions recorded at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV with integrated luminosity 30 nb^-1
- Pb-Pb integrated luminosity: 0.1 nb^-1 at 2.76 TeV, 0.24 nb^-1 at 5.02 TeV
- pp integrated luminosity: 0.15 nb^-1 at 0.9 TeV, 1.1 nb^-1 at 2.76 TeV, 4.8 pb^-1 at 7 TeV, 9.7 pb^-1 at 8 TeV, 2.5 nb^-1 at 5.02 TeV, 4.35 pb^-1 at 13 TeV
- Lattice QCD critical temperature for QCD phase transition: T_cr ~ 160 MeV
- Jets with transverse momenta 40 < p_T,jet < 120 GeV/c in heavy-ion collisions are strongly suppressed in the 10% most central events
- ALICE low-mass dilepton measurements in pp at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV and p-Pb at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV at pseudorapidities |eta| < 0.8
- ALICE upgrade target interaction rate: 50 kHz in Run III, corresponding to instantaneous luminosity L = 6 x 10^27 cm^-2 s^-1

## Context

This overview extends the ALICE physics program from Run I and Run II (2009-2015) and sets the stage for Run III upgrades starting in 2021. The results build on prior measurements from RHIC (flow coefficient scaling interpreted as evidence for quark degrees of freedom) and earlier heavy-ion experiments CERES (Pb-Au at 158A GeV/c) and NA60 (In-In collisions), which studied in-medium rho-meson modifications. The comparison of pp, p-Pb, and Pb-Pb systems enables disentangling cold-matter effects from QGP signatures, with model comparisons to PYTHIA 8, EPOS, DPMJET, HIJING, AMPT, and Color Glass Condensate approaches. The upgrade program aims to measure rare probes (heavy flavor hadrons, quarkonia, low-mass lepton pairs, jets, light nuclei, hypernuclei) with much improved statistics.
