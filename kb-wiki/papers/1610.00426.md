# Vector meson condensation in a pion superfluid

> We revisit the suggestion that charged rho-mesons undergo Bose-Einstein condensation in isospin-rich nuclear matter. Using a simple version of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, we conclude that rho-meson condensation is either avoided or postponed to isospin chemical potentials much higher than the rho-meson mass as a consequence of the repulsive interaction with the preformed pion condensate. In order to support our numerical results, we work out a linear sigma model for pions and rho-mesons, showing that the two models lead to similar patterns of medium dependence of meson masses. As a byproduct, we analyze in detail the mapping between the NJL model and the linear sigma model, focusing on conditions that must be satisfied for a quantitative agreement between the models.

## Key claim

The authors conclude that charged rho-meson Bose-Einstein condensation in isospin-rich nuclear matter is either avoided entirely or postponed to isospin chemical potentials much higher than the rho-meson mass, due to repulsive interactions with the pre-existing pion condensate; this conclusion is conditional on the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model parameters being tuned to reproduce physical vacuum observables.

## Method

The authors use a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector four-point interactions, bosonized via Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation and treated in the mean-field approximation. They compute the thermodynamic potential and meson polarization functions at zero temperature to determine the condensates and the in-medium meson mass spectrum as functions of the isospin chemical potential. To support the numerical results, they also construct a linear sigma model for pions and rho-mesons with gauged isospin symmetry, solving the coupled gap equations for the pion and temporal vector condensates.

## Key numbers

* Vacuum constituent quark mass fixed to M = 300 MeV, pion mass m_pi = 140 MeV, pion decay constant f_pi = 92.4 MeV, and rho-meson mass tuned to m_rho = 500 MeV for the primary parameter set.
* The resulting NJL model parameters are G = 2.92 GeV^{-2}, Lambda = 817 MeV, G_V = 3.12 GeV^{-2}, and m = 3.30 MeV.
* For the parameter set with m_rho = 500 MeV, the fermionic quasiparticle gap is reduced by about 3% compared to the pion condensate at the BEC-to-Fermi-sea crossover near mu_I / m_pi ≈ 1.6.
* The critical isospin chemical potential for rho-meson condensation rises steeply with vacuum rho-meson mass; for m_rho values shown in Fig. 5, the critical mu_I lies well beyond the NJL model cutoff of 817 MeV.

## Context

This work revisits the suggestion by Voskresensky (1997), Lenaghan (2001), and Sannino (2002) that charged rho-mesons condense at high isospin density, and contrasts with a holographic QCD model by Aharony (2007) that found rho-meson condensation at mu_I ≈ 1.7 m_rho. The results also address a preliminary lattice study by Forcrand (2007) that found the charged rho-meson mass dropping to zero, which the authors argue is inconsistent with exact chiral perturbation theory predictions for the neutral pion mass. The analysis enables future work on the phase structure of isospin-rich matter and provides a systematic mapping between the NJL model and the linear sigma model for meson dynamics.
