# The non-relativistic limit of first-order relativistic viscous fluids

> Out-of-equilibrium effects may play an important role in the dynamics of neutron star mergers and in heavy-ion collisions. Bemfica, Disconzi, Noronha and Kovtun (BDNK) recently derived a causal, locally well-posed, and modally stable relativistic fluid model that incorporates the effects of viscosity and heat diffusion. We study the non-relativistic limit of this fluid model and show that causality for relativistic motion restricts the transport coefficients in the non-relativistic limit. This restriction provides an upper bound on the ratio of the shear viscosity to the entropy density for fluids that can be described as relativistic within the BDNK model and can be exactly modeled using the Navier-Stokes equation in the non-relativistic limit. Furthermore, we show that the Fourier law of heat conduction must be modified by higher gradient corrections for such fluids. We also show that the non-relativistic limit of the BDNK equations of motion are never hyperbolic, in contrast to the non-relativistic limit of extended variable models, whose non-relativistic equations of motion can be hyperbolic or not depending on the scaling of the transport coefficients present in the auxiliary equations for viscous degrees of freedom.

## Key claim

The non-relativistic limit of the BDNK first-order relativistic viscous fluid model is never hyperbolic, and causality constraints from the relativistic theory impose an upper bound on the ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density for fluids that can be described by the Navier-Stokes equations in the non-relativistic limit, provided the BDNK transport coefficients scale appropriately with the speed of light.

## Method

The authors perform a non-relativistic expansion (c → ∞) of the BDNK fluid equations of motion, assuming the fluid retains the same scaling in powers of c⁻¹ up to O(c⁻⁵) as a perfect fluid. They analyze the hyperbolicity of the resulting non-relativistic equations and examine how the relativistic causality constraints from Bemfica, Disconzi, Noronha and Kovtun (2020) translate into bounds on physical transport coefficients in the non-relativistic regime. They also compare the non-relativistic limit of the BDNK model with that of an extended variable (Mueller-Israel-Stewart-type) model.

## Key numbers

* The non-relativistic BDNK equations of motion are never hyperbolic, in contrast to extended variable models whose non-relativistic equations can be hyperbolic or not depending on the scaling of transport coefficients with c⁻¹.
* The causality constraints provide an upper bound on the ratio of shear viscosity η to entropy density s, expressed in terms of the thermal conductivity κ (see Eq. (6.1) in the paper).
* The Fourier law of heat conduction is always modified by higher-order gradient corrections in the non-relativistic limit of the BDNK model, due to relativistic causality constraints.
* For the toy model with transport coefficients τ₁ and τ₂, the relativistic causality condition requires τ₁^(2)/τ₂^(0) > 0 when τ₁^(0) = 0, where τ₁ = τ₁^(0) + τ₁^(2)/c² + O(c⁻⁴) and τ₂ = τ₂^(0) + τ₂^(2)/c² + O(c⁻⁴).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the BDNK fluid model (Bemfica, Disconzi, Noronha and Kovtun, 2020; Kovtun, 2019) by examining its non-relativistic limit, which had not been systematically studied before. The results complement the Kovtun-Son-Starinets (KSS) conjecture (Kovtun, Son and Starinets, 2004) from holography, which provides a lower bound on η/s, by providing an upper bound from causality considerations. The findings also show that the fluid frame used in numerical simulations by Pandya, Pretorius and others (2021, 2022) violates the physical conditions required for the non-relativistic limit to have nonzero viscosity and preserve the post-Newtonian metric structure, indicating that new fluid frames need to be investigated for astrophysical applications such as neutron star mergers and accretion disks.
