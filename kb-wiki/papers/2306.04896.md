# The 3+1D initialization and evolution of the Glasma

> The IP-Glasma initial condition has been highly successful in the phenomenology of ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions. The assumption of boost invariance, however, while good for collision energies probed at the LHC, limits the use of IP-Glasma to the transverse dynamics of heavy ion collision to near mid-rapidity. There is a wealth of physics to be explored and understood in the longitudinal dynamics of heavy ion collisions, and a full understanding of heavy ion collisions can only come from 3-dimensional studies. In particular, long range rapidity correlations are seeded in the initial collision and provide additional information on the high energy nuclear wave functions that has thus far been inaccessible to the IP-Glasma model. In this work, we introduce a way to extend the IP-Glasma model to 3+1-dimensions while preserving its key features.

## Key claim

The authors introduce a method to extend the IP-Glasma model to 3+1 dimensions by using JIMWLK renormalization group evolution to provide longitudinal structure, but note that their 3+1D initial conditions will not recover the 2+1D (boost-invariant) initial conditions in the infinite momentum limit, unlike some other 3+1D Glasma models.

## Method

The authors generalize the 2+1D IP-Glasma model by introducing rapidity dependence through JIMWLK evolution of the color charge densities, which breaks boost invariance by introducing a reference rapidity. They modify the initial longitudinal gluon fields to maintain pure-gauge conditions for individual nuclei, solve Gauss' law using a covariant Poisson equation with an ansatz relating transverse electric fields to a scalar field, and evolve the Glasma fields using classical Yang-Mills equations on a 3-dimensional lattice in Milne coordinates. The initial proper time is set to τ₀ = 0.01 fm, and the system is subsequently evolved hydrodynamically using MUSIC and hadronically using UrQMD.

## Key numbers

- Infrared regulator mass m = 0.4 GeV used in the Poisson equation for pre-collision gauge fields
- Initial proper time set to τ₀ = 0.01 fm for the 3+1D evolution
- Saturation scale at LHC energies Q_s ≈ 2–5 GeV, corresponding to 1/Q_s ≈ 0.04–0.2 fm
- T₀ = 0.2 fm and Δξ = 0.005 fm for the variable time step scheme
- Typical hydrodynamic initialization times of τ = 0.2–0.6 fm where 3+1D fields have similar contributions to energy density
- Hydro switching times: τ = 0.6 fm for 3+1D simulations and τ = 0.4 fm for 2+1D simulations
- Running coupling parameters: Λ_QCD = 0.2 GeV, c = 0.2, μ₀ = 0.4 GeV

## Context

This work extends the phenomenologically successful IP-Glasma model (Schenke 2012) from 2+1D to 3+1D, building on prior efforts to generalize the McLerran-Venugopalan model and IP-Glasma to include longitudinal dynamics (Schenke 2016, Gelfand 2016, Schlichting 2020, Ipp 2021). The approach differs from Schenke (2016) by keeping longitudinal interactions between transverse planes at different rapidities rather than evolving each plane independently. This enables study of long-range rapidity correlations and longitudinal dynamics that were previously inaccessible, and allows investigation of whether viscosities extracted from 3+1D studies are consistent with those from 2+1D studies.
