# Dynamical coupled-channels model of $K^- p$ reactions (II): Extraction of $Λ^*$ and $Σ^*$ hyperon resonances

> Resonance parameters (pole masses and residues) associated with the excited states of hyperons, Lambda^* and Sigma^*, are extracted within a dynamical coupled-channels model developed recently by us [Phys. Rev. C 90, 065204 (2014)] through a comprehensive partial-wave analysis of the K^- p --> barK N, pi Sigma, pi Lambda, eta Lambda, K Xi data up to invariant mass W = 2.1 GeV. We confirm the existence of resonances corresponding to most, if not all, of the four-star resonances rated by the Particle Data Group. We also find several new resonances, and in particular propose a possible existence of a new narrow J^P=3/2^+ Lambda resonance that couples strongly to the eta Lambda channel. The J^P=1/2^- Lambda resonances located below the barK N threshold are also discussed. Comparing our extracted pole masses with the ones from a recent analysis by the Kent State University group, some significant differences in the extracted resonance parameters are found, suggesting the need of more extensive and accurate data of K^- p reactions including polarization observables to eliminate such an analysis dependence of the resonance parameters. In addition, the determined large branching ratios of the decays of high-mass resonances to the pi Sigma^* and barK^* N channels also suggest the importance of the data of 2 --> 3 reactions such as K^- p --> pi pi Lambda and K^- p --> pi barK N. Experiments on measuring cross sections and polarization observables of these fundamental reactions are highly desirable at hadron beam facilities such as J-PARC for establishing the Lambda^* and Sigma^* spectrum.

## Key claim

The authors confirm most four-star hyperon resonances listed by the Particle Data Group and propose a possible new narrow \(J^P=3/2^+\) \(\Lambda\) resonance coupling strongly to the \(\eta\Lambda\) channel, but they note that significant differences between their extracted pole masses and those from the Kent State University group indicate the current \(K^- p\) data are insufficient to uniquely determine the resonance parameters.

## Method

The authors performed a dynamical coupled-channels (DCC) partial-wave analysis of \(K^- p \to \bar K N, \pi \Sigma, \pi \Lambda, \eta \Lambda, K\Xi\) reactions from threshold to invariant mass \(W = 2.1\) GeV, using two models (Model A and Model B) constructed by fitting both unpolarized and polarized observables. Resonance pole masses and residues were extracted by solving for poles of the dressed \(Y^*\) propagator in the complex \(W\) plane, with uncertainties estimated via a Monte Carlo sampling procedure that varied the bare resonance mass parameters while keeping other Hamiltonian parameters fixed.

## Key numbers

- Pole masses for \(\Lambda^*\) resonances above \(\bar K N\) threshold: e.g., \(S_{01}\) \(\Lambda(1/2^-)\) at \((1669^{+3}_{-8}, 9^{+9}_{-1})\) MeV (Model A) and \((1512^{+1}_{-1}, 185^{+1}_{-2})\) MeV plus \((1667^{+1}_{-2}, 12^{+3}_{-1})\) MeV (Model B); \(D_{03}\) \(\Lambda(3/2^-)\) at \((1517^{+4}_{-4}, 8^{+5}_{-4})\) MeV and \((1697^{+6}_{-6}, 33^{+7}_{-7})\) MeV (both models)
- Pole masses for \(\Sigma^*\) resonances: e.g., \(D_{15}\) \(\Sigma(5/2^-)\) at \((1767^{+2}_{-2}, 64^{+2}_{-1})\) MeV (Model A) and \((1765^{+2}_{-1}, 64^{+3}_{-1})\) MeV (Model B); \(F_{17}\) \(\Sigma(7/2^+)\) at \((2025^{+10}_{-5}, 65^{+3}_{-12})\) MeV (Model A) and \((2014^{+12}_{-1}, 103^{+3}_{-9})\) MeV (Model B)
- New narrow \(J^P=3/2^+\) \(\Lambda\) resonance in Model B: \(M_R = 1671^{+2}_{-8} - i(5^{+11}_{-2})\) MeV, contributing about 40% of the \(K^- p \to \eta\Lambda\) total cross section near threshold
- The analysis examined resonances with total width less than 400 MeV and real part of pole mass up to 2.1 GeV, extracting 18 resonances in Model A and 20 in Model B

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous DCC model development (Kamano et al., Phys. Rev. C 90, 065204, 2014) by extracting pole parameters from the same amplitudes, and it provides the first systematic comparison of pole masses from a dynamical model with those from the Kent State University group's energy-independent analysis (Zhang et al., 2013). The results highlight the need for more extensive \(K^- p\) data including polarization observables and \(2 \to 3\) reactions such as \(K^- p \to \pi\pi\Lambda\) and \(K^- p \to \pi\bar K N\) at facilities like J-PARC to resolve analysis-dependent discrepancies in the hyperon resonance spectrum.
