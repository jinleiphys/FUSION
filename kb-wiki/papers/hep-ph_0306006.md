# Exploring QCD: from LEAR to GSI

> In this invited contribution I briefly review some of the principal topics in hadron spectroscopy that were studied at the CERN low-energy antiproton facility LEAR, from its beginnings in the early 1980s to the present. These topics include the nature of multiquark systems, the short-ranged nuclear force, and gluonic hadrons, including glueballs and hybrids. Lessons we have learned from the LEAR program that are relevant to the future GSI project are given particular emphasis.

# Exploring QCD: from LEAR to GSI

## Key claim

The LEAR program has provided "proof of principle" for gluonic hadrons (glueballs and hybrids) and exotic mesons, but the existing candidates represent a thin and ambiguous beginning; the partner states that should exist if these are indeed glueball and hybrid states have not yet been found, and the nature of short-ranged nuclear forces remains under investigation with ambiguous conclusions.

## Method

The paper reviews hadron spectroscopy results from the CERN low-energy antiproton facility LEAR, covering studies of multiquark systems (baryonia), glueballs, hybrid mesons, and conventional quarkonia produced in nucleon-antiproton collisions. It compares experimental findings from LEAR and other facilities (meson beams, e+e- collisions) with theoretical predictions from the MIT bag model, flux-tube model, lattice gauge theory, and quark potential models, and discusses implications for the future GSI antiproton facility.

## Key numbers

- The scalar glueball is predicted by lattice gauge theory at a mass of about 1.7 GeV, with the next glueballs (0-+ and 2++) near 2.5 GeV and the first exotic (2+-) near 4 GeV
- The f0(1500) glueball candidate has a width of approximately 100 MeV and a branching fraction ratio B_{KK/ππ} = 0.19 ± 0.07
- The f0(1710) glueball candidate has a branching fraction ratio B_{ππ/KK} = 0.39 ± 0.14
- The lightest hybrid exotic (1-+) is predicted by flux-tube model and lattice gauge theory at about 1.9-2.1 GeV, with two experimental candidates at lower masses: π1(1400) and π1(1600)
- Of 44 predicted n̄n mesons below 2.1 GeV (spanning 1S, 2S, 3S, 1P, 2P, 1D and 1F multiplets), about 35 have been reported (approximately 80% complete)
- The excited kaon spectrum in this mass range has about 16 of 22 states known; the s̄s spectrum has only about 6 of 22 states well established
- The Godfrey-Isgur prediction for the 2P excitation of the h1(1170) was 1.78 GeV, whereas the candidate reported by E852 is at 1.59 GeV

## Context

This invited review extends the line of hadron spectroscopy work from the early LEAR program (beginning in the 1980s) through the present, summarizing lessons for the future GSI antiproton facility. It discusses the "multiquark fiasco" (Isgur, 1985) where predictions of a rich light q²q̄² spectrum proved incorrect because such states can "fall apart" into two mesons, and contrasts the meson-exchange model of short-range nuclear forces (Richard, 1982) with the one-gluon exchange description. The paper enables future searches for multiplet partner states of the glueball and hybrid candidates, and for conventional q̄q mesons in the 1.5-2.0 GeV mass range where non-q̄q states are expected to appear.
