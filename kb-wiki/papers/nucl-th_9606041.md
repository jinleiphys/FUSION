# Kaon Interferometry

> Two-kaon and two-pion correlation functions for an expanding thermalized source are compared. In the Yano-Koonin-Podgoretskii parametrization of the correlation function, the HBT radius parameters are shown to obey M_\bot- scaling in the absence of collective transverse flow. This scaling is broken by transverse flow. An accurate comparison of pion and kaon correlations can thus resolve issue whether the observed M_\bot-dependence of the transverse radius parameter is due to transverse collective flow or other transverse gradients. Effects from resonance decays are shortly discussed.

## Key claim
The paper shows that in the Yano-Koonin-Podgoretskii parametrization of two-particle correlation functions, the HBT radius parameters obey M⊥-scaling for an expanding thermalized source only in the absence of collective transverse flow; transverse flow breaks this scaling, so comparing pion and kaon correlations can determine whether the observed M⊥-dependence of the transverse radius parameter arises from transverse collective flow or from other transverse gradients.

## Method
The authors compared two-kaon and two-pion correlation functions calculated for an expanding thermalized source. They used the Yano-Koonin-Podgoretskii parametrization to extract HBT radius parameters and examined their dependence on the transverse mass M⊥, both with and without collective transverse flow. The effects of resonance decays were briefly discussed.

## Key numbers
- The HBT radius parameters obey M⊥-scaling in the absence of collective transverse flow; this scaling is broken when transverse flow is present.
- The paper discusses the M⊥-dependence of the transverse radius parameter as a diagnostic for transverse collective flow versus other transverse gradients.

## Context
This work extends the study of HBT interferometry for pions to include kaons, building on prior analyses of two-pion correlations in expanding sources. The authors propose that comparing pion and kaon correlation data can resolve whether the observed M⊥-dependence of the transverse radius parameter is due to transverse collective flow or other transverse gradients. This enables future experimental analyses to distinguish between these physical mechanisms in heavy-ion collisions.
