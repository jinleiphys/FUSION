# Infrared Behaviour of the Gauge Boson Propagator in a Confining Theory

> We study the gauge boson propagator in the dual Abelian Higgs theory which confines electric charges. The confinement is due to dual Abrikosov-Nielsen-Olesen strings. We show that the infrared double pole 1/(p^2)^2 in the propagator is absent due to the confinement phenomenon. Instead, specific angular singularities signal the eventual set in of the confinement. These angular singularities are a manifestation of the confining strings.

## Key claim

The gauge boson propagator in the dual Abelian Higgs model does not exhibit either the infrared double pole \(1/(p^2)^2\) or a vanishing propagator at \(p^2 \to 0\), which had been proposed as signatures of confinement; instead, confinement is signaled by angular singularities in the variable \((p \cdot n)\) that arise from the Dirac strings associated with dual Abrikosov-Nielsen-Olesen strings.

## Method

The authors study the gauge boson (analogue of the gluon) propagator in the dual Abelian Higgs theory, which is an effective infrared description of \(SU(2)\) gluodynamics with condensed magnetic monopoles. Using the Zwanziger local field formalism for electrically and magnetically charged particles, they derive an exact expression for the propagator in the axial gauge \(n_\mu A_\mu = 0\) in the London limit (\(\lambda \to \infty\)). The propagator separates into a term from the Higgs mechanism and a string contribution from closed ANO string world sheets; the infrared behavior of the unknown string–string correlation function \(D^\Sigma(p^2)\) is estimated by modeling it as a sum over glueball poles.

## Key numbers

* The mass of the dual gauge boson \(B_\mu\) is \(M_B = e \eta\), where \(e\) is the electric charge and \(\eta\) is the Higgs field vacuum expectation value.
* In the infrared limit \(p \to 0\), the coefficient function \(F(p^2)\) becomes \(F^{\mathrm{infr}}(p^2) = 1/M_B^2 + C/M_{\mathrm{gl}}^2 + O(p^2)\), where \(C\) is a dimensionless parameter and \(M_{\mathrm{gl}}\) is the mass of the lowest \(1^-\) glueball.
* In the same limit, the coefficient function \(G(p^2)\) becomes \(G^{\mathrm{infr}}(p^2) = -1 + O(p^2)\).
* The no-string (Higgs-only) coefficient functions are \(F^{\mathrm{no-string}}(p^2) = 1/(p^2 + M_B^2)\) and \(G^{\mathrm{no-string}}(p^2) = -M_B^2/(p^2 + M_B^2)\).

## Context

This work extends the dual superconductor model of quark confinement (t Hooft 1976, Mandelstam 1976) by analyzing the gauge boson propagator, a quantity central to the phenomenological confinement criterion of a double pole (West 1983, Baker, Ball, and Zachariasen 1988). It contradicts the Gribov–Zwanziger scenario (Zwanziger 1991) that the gluon propagator vanishes in the infrared, attributing that result to a specific gauge choice in QCD. The results enable a consistent treatment of confinement via string singularities rather than propagator poles, and the authors note that the correct confining potential must be obtained from the Wilson loop in the string representation, as detailed in their companion paper (Gubarev, Polikarpov, and Zakharov 1998).
