# Relativistic Constituent Quark Model for Baryons

> The electroweak properties of nucleons and hyperons are calculated in a relativistic constituent quark model. The baryons are treated as three quark bound states, and the diagrams of perturbation theory are considered on the light front. The electroweak properties of the baryons are of nonperturbative nature and can be represented by one-loop diagrams. We consider different extensions of the simplest model: quark form factors, configuration mixing of the wave function, asymmetric wave function, wave function different from the one of a harmonic oscillator valid up to energies of more than 30 GeV$^2$. A comprehensive study of various baryonic properties is given: elastic form factors of the nucleon, magnetic moments of the baryon octet, semileptonic weak form factors. This analysis also gives the Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix element $V_{us}$ and a sound symmetry breaking scheme for the Cabibbo theory. A consistent physical picture appears in this work. The nucleon consists of an unmixed, symmetric three quark state, the wave function of the hyperons is however asymmetric with a spin-isospin-0 diquark. Only for the strangeness-changing weak decay do we need nontrivial form factors.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a consistent description of baryon electroweak properties requires an asymmetric wave function for hyperons (with a spin-isospin-0 diquark) and nontrivial quark form factors only for strangeness-changing weak decays, while the nucleon can be described by an unmixed, symmetric three-quark state.

## Method

The author treats baryons as three-quark bound states using light-front dynamics, reducing the Bethe-Salpeter equation to a relativistic Schrödinger equation via a quasipotential method. The current matrix element is calculated from one-loop diagrams with the quarks on their mass shells, using either a Gaussian or a Lorentz-shaped momentum wave function. The model is extended by introducing quark form factors, configuration mixing of the wave function, and an asymmetric wave function with separate scale parameters for quark-diquark and quark-quark binding.

## Key numbers

- The Lorentz-shaped wave function fits nucleon form factors up to energies of more than 30 GeV².
- The r.m.s. radius of the nucleon is estimated from 0.8 fm (charge radius) to 0.4 fm (hyperon decays), implying quark momentum in the range 250–500 MeV.
- Light constituent quark mass is in the range 210–360 MeV.
- The minimal quark model uses five parameters: quark masses m_u = m_d ≡ m_{u/d} and m_s, and wave function parameters β for nucleons, Σs, and Ξs.
- The ratio g₁/f₁(Λ → p e⁻ ν̄_e) / g₁/f₁(Σ⁻ → n e⁻ ν̄_e) is approximately −3.5 ± 0.1 with configuration mixing, compared to the MQM value −3 and experimental data −2.11 ± 0.15.

## Context

This work extends the light-front constituent quark model originally formulated by Terent'ev and Berestetskii (1976, 1977) and applied by Aznauryan et al. (1982, 1984), and by Jaus (1990, 1991) in the meson sector. It also builds on studies by Chung and Coester (1991) on nucleon electromagnetic form factors. The paper addresses the controversy over SU(3) breaking in hyperon beta decay and the Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix element V_us, which is important for testing unitarity of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix. The asymmetric wave function approach provides a framework for future calculations of baryon properties beyond the minimal quark model.
