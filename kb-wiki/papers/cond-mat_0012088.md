# An SU(4) Model of High-Temperature Superconductivity and Antiferromagnetism

> We present an SU(4) model of high-temperature superconductivity having many similarities to dynamical symmetries known to play an important role in microscopic nuclear structure physics and in elementary particle physics. Analytical solutions in three dynamical symmetry limits of this model are found: an SO(4) limit associated with antiferromagnetic order; an SU(2) X SO(3) limit that may be interpreted as a d-wave pairing condensate; and an SO(5) limit that may be interpreted as a doorway state between the antiferromagnetic order and the superconducting order. The model suggests a phase diagram in qualitative agreement with that observed in the cuprate superconductors. The relationship between the present model and the SO(5) unification of superconductivity and antiferromagnetic order proposed by Zhang is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an SU(4) dynamical symmetry model for high-temperature superconductivity in cuprates, claiming that antiferromagnetic order and d-wave superconductivity emerge as different subgroup chains of the same parent symmetry, with the SO(5) limit acting as a critical dynamical symmetry that interpolates between them. The hedge is that the model only suggests a phase diagram in *qualitative* agreement with observations, and the quantitative tests are promised in separate publications.

## Method

The authors construct a closed Lie algebra of 16 fermion operators (including d-wave singlet and triplet pair creation/annihilation operators, spin operators, and staggered magnetization operators) defined on a periodic lattice with a d-wave form-factor approximated by sign(cos k_x – cos k_y). They identify three subgroup chains of SU(4) that conserve charge and spin: an SO(4) chain (antiferromagnetic order), an SU(2) × SO(3) chain (d-wave pairing condensate), and an SO(5) chain (transitional phase). For each chain, they construct dynamical symmetry Hamiltonians as polynomials in the Casimir invariants, which are diagonal in the corresponding basis and thus yield analytical solutions for eigenvalues and eigenstates.

## Key numbers

- The collective subspace for the simplest implementation has dimension scaling as approximately Ω^4: Dim(Ω/2,0,0) = (1/12)(Ω/2 + 1)(Ω/2 + 2)^2(Ω/2 + 3), where Ω is the number of lattice sites.
- In the SO(4) (antiferromagnetic) limit, the ground state has staggered magnetization Q = (1/2)Ω(1 – x) = n/2, where x = 1 – n/Ω is the hole doping parameter.
- In the SO(4) limit, the energy gap is ΔE = 2χ_eff(1 – x)Ω and the pairing gap is Δ = (1/2)G^0_eff Ω √[x(1 – x)].
- In the SU(2) (superconducting) limit, the ground state has pairing gap Δ = (1/2)G^0_eff Ω √(1 – x^2) and staggered magnetization Q = 0.
- In the SO(5) limit, the excitation energy for states with λ π-pairs is ΔE = λ G^0_eff Ω x, which vanishes at half filling (x = 0).
- The energy minimum in the SO(4) limit lies at β = √[(1/4)(1 – x)], where β is directly related to the antiferromagnetic order parameter.

## Context

This work extends the SO(5) unification of superconductivity and antiferromagnetism proposed by Zhang (1997) by embedding that symmetry as a subgroup of a larger SU(4) algebra defined microscopically in fermion degrees of freedom, and by identifying two additional dynamical symmetry limits (SO(4) and SU(2)) that correspond to pure antiferromagnetic and pure superconducting phases. The approach is inspired by fermion dynamical symmetries previously developed in nuclear structure physics by Wu (1994) and Sun (1998), and the paper suggests that the SU(4) model can provide a framework for studying phase transitions between antiferromagnetic and superconducting order as a function of hole doping in cuprate superconductors.
