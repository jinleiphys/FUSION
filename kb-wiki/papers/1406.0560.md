# Proton-Neutron Pairing Amplitude as a Generator Coordinate for Double-Beta Decay

> We treat proton-neutron pairing amplitudes, in addition to the nuclear deformation, as generator coordinates in a calculation of the neutrinoless double-beta decay of 76Ge. We work in two oscillator shells, with a Hamiltonian that includes separable terms in the quadrupole, spin-isospin, and pairing (isovector and isoscalar) channels. Our approach allows larger single-particle spaces than the shell model and includes the important physics of the proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) without instabilities near phase transitions. After comparing the results of a simplified calculation that neglects deformation with those of the QRPA, we present a more realistic calculation with both deformation and proton-neutron pairing amplitudes as generator coordinates. The future should see proton-neutron coordinates used together with energy-density functionals.

## Key claim

The generator-coordinate method (GCM) with proton-neutron pairing amplitudes as a collective coordinate can treat isoscalar pairing as a large-amplitude mode, avoiding the spurious phase-transition collapse that plagues the quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) near critical isoscalar pairing strengths, while still including the important physics of proton-neutron pairing that suppresses double-beta decay matrix elements.

## Method

The authors constructed a Hamiltonian with separable quadrupole, spin-isospin, and pairing (isovector and isoscalar) terms in a two-oscillator-shell (fp and sdg) space for A=76 nuclei. They generated Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) vacua constrained to have specific values of axial deformation β and isoscalar pairing amplitude φ, projected these states onto good particle numbers and angular momentum zero, then solved the Hill-Wheeler equation to obtain the ground states of 76Ge and 76Se and compute the neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix element.

## Key numbers

- The neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix element M^0ν for 76Ge in the full calculation including both deformation and proton-neutron pairing as generator coordinates is 4.7 for both the SkO'-based and SkM*-based interactions.
- In the simplified calculation neglecting deformation, the full GCM matrix element is 5.4 (SkO') and 4.1 (SkM* unquenched) or 2.8 (SkM* quenched), compared to QRPA values of 5.6 (SkO') and 3.5 (SkM* unquenched) or 2.5 (SkM* quenched).
- The isoscalar pairing strength g^T=0 extracted from fitting B(GT+) ≈ 1 in 76Se is 1.47 × ḡ^T=1 for SkO' and 1.56 × ḡ^T=1 for SkM* (unquenched) in the simplified calculation.
- In the full calculation with deformation, g^T=0 = 1.75 × ḡ^T=1 for SkO' and 1.51 × ḡ^T=1 for SkM*.
- The Gamow-Teller part of the full matrix element is 3.4 (SkO') and 3.7 (SkM*); the Fermi part is 1.2 (SkO') and 1.0 (SkM*).

## Context

This work extends the generator-coordinate method applied to double-beta decay by Rodriguez and collaborators (2010, 2011, 2013), who used only axial quadrupole deformation and like-particle pairing as coordinates and consequently obtained larger matrix elements that omitted the suppression from proton-neutron pairing. The approach also improves upon the proton-neutron QRPA, which was first used to show that proton-neutron pairing quenches double-beta matrix elements (Vogel and Zirnbauer, 1986; Engel, Vogel, and Zirnbauer, 1987), by avoiding the spurious collapse near phase transitions and treating isoscalar pairing as a genuinely large-amplitude mode. The method enables future calculations using energy-density functionals (Skyrme or Gogny) with proton-neutron generator coordinates, which should improve the accuracy of density-functional-based double-beta matrix elements.
