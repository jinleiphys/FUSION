# Importance Truncation for Large-Scale Configuration Interaction Approaches

> We introduce an iterative importance truncation scheme which aims at reducing the dimension of the model space of configuration interaction approaches by an a priori selection of the physically most relevant basis states. Using an importance measure derived from multiconfigurational perturbation theory in combination with an importance threshold, we construct a model space optimized for the description of individual eigenstates of a given Hamiltonian. We discuss in detail various technical aspects and refinements of the importance truncation, such as perturbative corrections for excluded basis states, threshold extrapolation techniques, and different iterative model-space update schemes. We apply the idea of the importance truncation in the context of the no-core shell model (NCSM) for the ab initio description of nuclear ground states. In a series of benchmark calculations for closed- and open-shell nuclei up to O-16 we compare the ground-state energies obtained in the importance truncated NCSM to the full NCSM. All calculations show an excellent agreement of importance truncated and full NCSM for all cases where the latter is feasible. The results demonstrate that the importance truncated NCSM, while preserving most of the advantages of the full NCSM, gives access to much larger N_max*hbar*Omega spaces and heavier nuclei. In this way we are able to perform importance truncated NCSM calculations for nuclei like C-12 and O-16 up to N_max=22.

## Key claim

The paper introduces an iterative importance truncation scheme that reduces the dimension of configuration interaction model spaces by selecting basis states based on a perturbative importance measure, enabling access to much larger model spaces than the full no-core shell model (NCSM) while preserving variational upper bounds on the ground-state energy.

## Method

The method uses multiconfigurational perturbation theory to define an importance measure kappa_nu for each basis state, computed from the Hamiltonian matrix element between that state and a reference state divided by the energy denominator. Starting from a single Slater determinant or a small reference space, the scheme iteratively constructs importance-truncated model spaces by including only states with |kappa_nu| above a threshold kappa_min, solves the eigenvalue problem in each reduced space, and uses the resulting eigenvector to define an improved reference state for the next iteration. Perturbative corrections for excluded states and multi-reference Davidson corrections are applied to account for discarded configurations and higher-order excitations.

## Key numbers

* Ground-state energies for closed- and open-shell nuclei up to O-16 were compared between importance-truncated NCSM and full NCSM, showing "excellent agreement" for all cases where full NCSM is feasible.
* Importance-truncated NCSM calculations for C-12 and O-16 were performed up to N_max = 22, which is far beyond the reach of full NCSM (for O-16, the full NCSM model space dimension reaches order 10^9 already at N_max = 8).
* The importance threshold kappa_min controls model space size; a reference threshold C_min can be chosen up to 10 times larger than kappa_min without affecting results.

## Context

This work extends the no-core shell model (NCSM) approach developed by Navratil, Vary, and collaborators (2000-2009) by adding an importance truncation scheme that overcomes the combinatorial growth of model space dimensions with particle number A and energy truncation N_max. The importance selection idea was pioneered in quantum chemistry in the 1970s, and the present work adapts it to nuclear physics using a perturbative importance measure derived from multiconfigurational perturbation theory. The method enables ab initio calculations for heavier nuclei and larger model spaces than previously possible with the NCSM, and the general framework can be applied to any configuration interaction-type many-body method.
