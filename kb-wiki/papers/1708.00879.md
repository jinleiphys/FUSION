# Hadron Resonance Gas with Repulsive Interactions and Fluctuations of Conserved Charges

> We discuss the role of repulsive baryon-baryon interactions in a hadron gas using relativistic virial expansion and repulsive mean field approaches. The fluctuations of the baryon number as well as strangeness-baryon correlations are calculated in the hadron resonance gas with repulsive interactions and compared with the recent lattice QCD results. In particular, we calculate the difference between the second and fourth order fluctuations and correlations of baryon number and strangeness, that have been proposed as probes of deconfinement. We show that for not too high temperatures these differences could be understood in terms of repulsive interactions.

## Key claim
The paper shows that deviations from the ideal hadron resonance gas (HRG) model in certain differences of fourth and second order fluctuations and correlations of baryon number and strangeness, previously interpreted as signals of deconfinement, can instead be explained by repulsive baryon-baryon interactions for temperatures not too high (i.e., near and below the QCD crossover temperature).

## Method
The authors used two approaches to model repulsive baryon-baryon interactions: a relativistic virial expansion based on the S-matrix for nucleon-nucleon scattering, and a repulsive mean field model where the interaction strength is parameterized by a constant K. They calculated the fluctuations and correlations of conserved charges (baryon number, strangeness, electric charge) in a hadron resonance gas with these repulsive interactions, and compared the results to lattice QCD data from HISQ and stout actions, focusing on the differences χ₂ᴮ−χ₄ᴮ, χ₂ᴮ−χ₆ᴮ, and χ₃₁ᴮˢ−χ₁₁ᴮˢ.

## Key numbers
- The repulsive mean field parameter used is K = 450 MeV fm³, taken from prior phenomenological work (Sollfrank 1996).
- For the nucleon-nucleon scattering, the scattering lengths used are a_{I=1} = -23.7 fm and a_{I=0} = 5.4194 fm, with effective ranges r_{I=1}⁰ = 2.76 fm and r_{I=0}⁰ = 1.7536 fm.
- The inelastic cross-section for pp scattering is about 10% of the total cross-section for 400 MeV < E < 500 MeV, rising to about 50% for E > 800 MeV, leading to estimated uncertainties in the virial coefficient b₂ of 20%, 50%, 80%, and 100% in those respective energy ranges.
- At T = 175 MeV, the mean field correction βU = 0.077 for nucleons, justifying the expansion in K.
- The first quantum correction to the nucleon gas pressure is about 20 times smaller than the repulsive interaction contribution.

## Context
This work extends the hadron resonance gas model by incorporating repulsive baryon-baryon interactions, building on earlier studies of excluded volume effects by Albright (2015), Vovchenko (2016, 2017) and mean field approaches by Olive (1980, 1982). It directly addresses the lattice QCD results of Bazavov (2013, 2017) and Bellwied (2015), who proposed that deviations from ideal HRG in χ₂ᴮ−χ₄ᴮ signal deconfinement. The paper shows that repulsive interactions can reproduce these deviations, and it enables future refinements using information from chiral effective theory (Polinder 2006, Haidenbauer 2013) and lattice QCD to treat strangeness-dependent baryon interactions.
