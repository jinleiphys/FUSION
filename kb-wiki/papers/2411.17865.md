# Neutron emission from the photon-induced reactions in ultraperipheral ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The ultraperipheral collisions are the source of various interesting phenomena based on photon-induced reactions. We calculate cross sections for single and any number of n, p, $α$, $γ$-rays in ultraperipheral heavy-ion collision for LHC energies. We analyze the production of a given number of neutrons relevant for a recent ALICE experiment, for $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.02~TeV. In our approach, we include both single and multiple photon exchanges as well as the fact that not all photon energies are used in the process of equilibration of the residual nucleus. We propose a simple two-component model in which only part of photon energy $E_γ$ is changed into the excitation energy of the nucleus ($E_{exc} \neq E_γ$) and compare its results with outcomes of HIPSE and EMPIRE codes. The role of high photon energies for small neutron multiplicities is discussed. Emission of a small number of neutrons at high photon energies seems to be crucial to understand the new ALICE data. All effects work in the desired direction, but the description of the cross section of four- and five-neutron emission cross sections from first principles is rather demanding. The estimated emission of charged particles such as protons, deuterons and $α$ is shortly discussed and confronted with very recent ALICE data, obtained with the proton Zero Degree Calorimeter.

## Key claim

The authors propose a two-component model (TCM) in which only part of the incident photon energy \(E_\gamma\) is converted into the excitation energy of the equilibrated compound nucleus (\(E_{exc} \neq E_\gamma\)), and they show that this mechanism is essential to reproduce the ALICE data for neutron multiplicities up to five, though a first-principles description of four- and five-neutron emission remains demanding.

## Method

The authors calculate cross sections for neutron emission in ultraperipheral Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02\) TeV by convolving the equivalent-photon-approximation (EPA) photon flux with a photoabsorption model. They introduce a two-component model (TCM) where the probability distribution for excitation energy \(E_{exc}\) given photon energy \(E_\gamma\) is a weighted sum of a delta function (full energy transfer) and a uniform distribution (partial energy transfer), with the weight parameter \(E_0\) fitted to data. The deexcitation of the hot residual nucleus is then simulated using the GEMINI++ statistical code, and results are compared with the HIPSE and EMPIRE codes as well as with ALICE data.

## Key numbers

*   Cross section for 1-neutron emission: \(108.4 \pm 3.90\) b (ALICE experiment), compared to TCM (step-like, \(E_0=50\) MeV) prediction of 98.75 b.
*   Cross section for 2-neutron emission: \(25.0 \pm 1.30\) b (ALICE), compared to TCM prediction of 25.55 b.
*   Cross section for 3-neutron emission: \(7.95 \pm 0.25\) b (ALICE), compared to TCM prediction of 6.07 b.
*   Cross section for 4-neutron emission: \(5.65 \pm 0.33\) b (ALICE), compared to TCM prediction of 6.32 b.
*   Cross section for 5-neutron emission: \(4.54 \pm 0.44\) b (ALICE), compared to TCM prediction of 4.91 b.
*   The TCM parameter \(E_0\) is set to 50 MeV as a representative value, based on a fit to the ALICE data and earlier photofission results from Guaraldo et al.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations (Klusek-Gawenda et al., 2013) which assumed full energy transfer (\(E_{exc} = E_\gamma\)) and could only describe up to 3-neutron emission at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV. The new ALICE data at 5.02 TeV, showing up to 5 neutrons, require the TCM to account for pre-equilibrium energy loss. The results are compared with the RELDIS code and with the HIPSE and EMPIRE nuclear-reaction models. The study enables improved modeling of electromagnetic dissociation in ultraperipheral collisions at the LHC and is relevant for future experiments at the EIC.
