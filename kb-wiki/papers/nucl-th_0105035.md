# Fluctuations of rare particles as a measure of chemical equilibration

> We calculate the time evolution of fluctuations for rare particles such as e.g. kaons in 1 AGeV or charmonium in 200 AGeV heavy ion collisions. We find that these fluctuations are a very sensitive probe of the degree of chemical equilibration reached in these collisions. Furthermore, measuring the second factorial moment the size of the initial population can be determined.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the second factorial moment \(F_2\) of rare particle multiplicities (e.g., kaons at 1 AGeV or charmonium at 200 AGeV) is a sensitive probe of the degree of chemical equilibration, but the sensitivity depends on whether the initial population is much smaller or much larger than the equilibrium population; the abstract glosses over the fact that the measurement can distinguish these two scenarios only when the equilibrium population is very small (\(\epsilon \ll 1\)).

## Method

The authors solve a master equation for the probability distribution \(P_n\) of rare particles subject to exact \(U(1)\) charge conservation (e.g., strangeness), derived in prior work (Jeon et al., 2000). They numerically evolve the time dependence of the second factorial moment \(F_2 = \langle N(N-1) \rangle / \langle N \rangle^2\) for two distinct initial conditions: a Poisson distribution (initial population much smaller than equilibrium) and a binomial distribution (initial population much larger than equilibrium). They also compare with the case without \(U(1)\) conservation, where the equilibrium distribution is Poissonian.

## Key numbers

- For rare particles with \(\langle N \rangle_{\rm eq.} \ll 1\) (i.e., \(\epsilon \ll 1\)), the equilibrium value of the second factorial moment is \(F_2 = 1/2 + \epsilon/6 + \ldots\), so \(F_2^{\rm eq.} \approx 0.5\).
- For binomial initial conditions with \(N_0 \ll N_{\rm eq.}\), \(F_2\) starts at 0, reaches a maximum of about 1 after a time \(\tau_{\rm max} \simeq N_0 / N_{\rm eq.}\), and then approaches equilibrium from above.
- For a Poissonian initial distribution with \(N_0 = 5\) and \(\epsilon = 0.1\), the numerical solution shows \(F_2\) drops to a minimum of order \(\epsilon\) (i.e., \(\sim 0.1\)) at \(\tau \sim 1\) and stays small until \(\tau \sim -\ln \epsilon\).
- For \(\epsilon = 0.001\) with the same initial condition, the interval where \(F_2\) remains of order \(\epsilon\) is visibly longer, consistent with \(\tau \sim -\ln \epsilon\).
- For kaon production at 1 AGeV, the chemical equilibration time from transport models is about 300 fm/c, while the typical reaction duration is 30–50 fm/c.

## Context

This work extends the authors’ previous study of rate equations with exact \(U(1)\) conservation (Jeon et al., 2000), which showed that equilibration times are shortened compared to standard rate equations but still exceed the lifetime of the system. It contradicts the assumption that the success of statistical models (e.g., Braun-Munzinger et al., 1999; Redlich et al., 2000) implies full chemical equilibrium, and it provides a new observable, the second factorial moment, to experimentally test equilibration. The paper also connects to Bose-Einstein correlation measurements, predicting that for rare particles with \(U(1)\) conservation, the correlation function should asymptote to 1/2 rather than 1, offering a direct experimental signature.
