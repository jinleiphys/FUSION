# Medium Modifications of Charm and Charmonium in High-Energy Heavy-Ion Collisions

> The production of charmonia in heavy-ion collisions is investigated within a kinetic theory framework simultaneously accounting for dissociation and regeneration processes in both quark-gluon plasma (QGP) and hadron-gas phases of the reaction. In-medium modifications of open-charm states (c-quarks, D-mesons) and the survival of J/psi mesons in the QGP are included as inferred from lattice QCD. Pertinent consequences on equilibrium charmonium abundances are evaluated and found to be especially relevant to explain the measured centrality dependence of the psi'/psi ratio at SPS. Predictions for recent In-In experiments, as well as comparisons to current Au-Au data from RHIC, are provided.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in-medium modifications of open-charm states (reduced D-meson masses in the hadron gas and increased effective charm-quark masses in the quark-gluon plasma), as inferred from lattice QCD, are essential to reproduce the measured centrality dependence of the \(\psi'/\psi\) ratio at SPS, a result that the abstract presents as a finding but which depends on the specific choice of nuclear absorption cross sections and the magnitude of the in-medium mass shifts.

## Method

The authors model charmonium production using a kinetic rate equation that simultaneously accounts for dissociation and regeneration in both the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) and hadron-gas phases, solved over a schematic thermal fireball expansion. In-medium open-charm properties are implemented by reducing the D-meson mass at the critical temperature by approximately 140 MeV (based on an NJL model of chiral symmetry restoration) and setting the effective charm-quark mass in the QGP to 1.6–1.7 GeV, with these values used to compute equilibrium charmonium abundances via a fugacity-adjusted statistical model. The thermal widths of charmonia are obtained from quasifree parton-induced breakup in the QGP and from pion/rho-induced breakup in the hadron gas using a flavor-SU(4) effective Lagrangian, with in-medium D-meson masses affecting both the phase space for these reactions and the opening of direct \(\Psi \rightarrow D\bar{D}\) decay channels.

## Key numbers

- In-medium D-meson mass reduction at \(T_c\): \(\Delta m_D(T_c) \simeq -140\) MeV, from an NJL model evaluation.
- Effective charm-quark mass in the QGP: \(m_c^* \simeq 1.6\)–\(1.7\) GeV.
- Charm pair production in central Au-Au at RHIC: \(N_{c\bar{c}} \sim 10\)–\(20\).
- Nuclear absorption cross section for \(\psi'\): \(\sigma_{\rm{nuc}}(\psi') = 7.9 \pm 0.6\) mb, compared to \(\sigma_{\rm{nuc}}(\psi) = 4.4\) mb.
- Uncertainty band for in-medium effects on the J/\(\psi\) yield at RHIC: \(-250 < \Delta m_D(T_c) < -80\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier "two-component" model (Grandchamp 2001, 2002) by replacing the ad-hoc combination of suppression and statistical hadronization with a unified kinetic rate equation that includes in-medium open-charm modifications inferred from lattice QCD (Karsch 2000, Datta 2002, Umeda 2002, Asakawa 2003). The approach enables simultaneous treatment of dissociation and regeneration at both SPS and RHIC energies, and provides predictions for In-In collisions at SPS that can be tested by the NA60 experiment, while also offering a framework for future studies of charmonium transverse momentum distributions and bottomonium production at LHC.
