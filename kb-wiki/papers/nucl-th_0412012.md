# Quantum tunneling of a complex systems: effects of finite size and intrinsic structure

> A simple model is considered to study the effects of finite size and internal structure in the tunneling of bound two-body systems through a potential barrier. It is demonstrated that these effects are able to increase the tunneling probability. Applications may include nuclear fusion,hydrogen atom and Cooper pair tunneling.

## Key claim
The finite size and internal structure of a composite tunneling object can increase the tunneling probability, contrary to the common assumption that internal degrees of freedom only hinder tunneling. The effect is most pronounced when the charged constituent is light relative to its partner, and when the barrier is non-parabolic, allowing energy transfer from internal motion to translational motion.

## Method
The authors model a two-body system (a "deuteron") bound by a harmonic oscillator potential, with an additional parabolic potential acting on one particle to simulate a Coulomb barrier. They diagonalize the Hamiltonian to find normal modes and their frequencies, then compute the tunneling probability using the semiclassical transmission coefficient for a parabolic barrier. They also treat the adiabatic polarization of the composite system in a realistic Coulomb field, using the WKB approximation to estimate the enhancement of barrier penetration.

## Key numbers
- For equal masses and strong binding (\(k \gg |q|\)), the imaginary frequency of the unstable mode is \(|\omega_{-}| \approx \sqrt{|q|/(2m)} \, (1 + |q|/(8k))\), giving an exponential enhancement factor \(\exp(\pi |E| m \omega_0 / (2\hbar k))\) relative to a point particle.
- For a very light charged particle (\(m_x/m_y \ll 1\)), the enhancement grows: \(|\omega_{-}| \approx \sqrt{|q|/(m_x+m_y)} \, (1 + |q|/(8k) \cdot m_y/m_x)\).
- The deuteron polarizability is \(\alpha = 0.70(5) \, \text{fm}^3\) (empirical value from Rodning 1982).
- For deuteron-deuteron fusion, the polarization correction to the barrier penetration factor is small (\(\sim 1\%\)).
- For a bound electron pair (Cooper pair), the imaginary frequency \(\omega_{-}^2 = q/m\) does not depend on the binding strength \(k\), so the parabolic barrier is not modified by finite size.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of subbarrier fusion that considered probabilistic barrier distributions (Balantekin 1998) and friction-like hindrance (Caldeira and Leggett 1981-1983), as well as the authors' own prior work on virtual electromagnetic field effects (Flambaum and Zelevinsky 1999). It also builds on the old result by Zakhariev and Sokolov (1964) that a finite-size system modifies the effective barrier to facilitate penetration. The model provides analytical estimates for applications including low-energy deuteron fusion (relevant to astrophysics and thermonuclear reactors, where experimental data below 10 keV are lacking), muon-catalyzed fusion, and Cooper pair tunneling in high-temperature superconductors and point contacts.
