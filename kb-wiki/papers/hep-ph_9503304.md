# DIMENSIONAL REDUCTION AT HIGH TEMPERATURE FOR FERMIONS

> The concept of dimensional reduction in the high temperature regime is generalized to static Green's functions of composite operators that contain fermionic fields. The recognition of a natural kinematic region where the lowest Matsubara modes are close to their mass-shell, and the ultraviolet behavior of the running coupling constant of the original theory are crucial for providing the necessary scale hierarchy. The general strategy is illustrated in the asymptotically-free Gross-Neveu model in 1+1 dimensions, where we verify that dimensional reduction occurs to the leading order in $g^2(T)$. We also find, in the same model, that the scale parameter characterizing the dependence on temperature of the coupling constant in the reduced theory, $Λ_T$, is considerably smaller than $Λ_{\bar{\text{MS}}}$. Implications of our results for QCD are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes dimensional reduction to static Green's functions of composite operators containing fermionic fields, but the reduction is only partial: corrections are suppressed by powers of the coupling constant \(\tilde{g}^2(T)\) rather than by powers of \(T\), because the lowest fermionic Matsubara modes are themselves of order \(T\) and no complete scale separation exists at tree level.

## Method

The authors study the Gross-Neveu model in 1+1 dimensions in the large-\(N\) limit, computing the one-loop bubble graph for static fermionic currents. They isolate the contribution from the lightest Matsubara modes (\(\omega_n = \pm \pi T\)), expand the remaining heavy-mode contributions in the residual momentum \(q_1^2 = p_1^2 + 4\pi^2 T^2\), and define a temperature-dependent coupling \(\tilde{g}^2(T)\) by requiring that all finite corrections to the divergent 1PI graph vanish at one loop. They then compare the screening mass obtained from the full 2-dimensional theory with that from the reduced 1-dimensional effective theory.

## Key numbers

- The screening mass in the original 1+1 dimensional theory: \(\tilde{m}_{1+1} = 2\pi T \left[1 - \frac{\tilde{g}^2(T)}{\pi} + 3(\ln 16 - 3)\left(\frac{\tilde{g}^2(T)}{\pi}\right)^3 + \mathcal{O}(\tilde{g}^8(T))\right]\).
- The screening mass in the reduced 1-dimensional theory: \(\tilde{m}_1 = 2\pi T \left[1 - \frac{\tilde{g}^2(T)}{\pi}\right]\).
- The scale parameter in the reduced theory: \(\Lambda_T^2 = \Lambda_{\overline{\text{MS}}}^2 e^{2\gamma_E - 1} / (4\pi)^2\), which is about \(0.15\, T_c\) where \(T_c = \Lambda_{\overline{\text{MS}}} e^{\gamma_E} / \pi\) is the chiral symmetry restoration temperature.
- The optimal renormalization scale choice: \(\mu^2 = 16\pi^2 T^2 / e^{2\gamma_E - 1}\).

## Context

This work extends the dimensional reduction framework of Appelquist (1981) and Landsman (1989), which previously applied only to static bosonic zero modes, to observables involving fermionic fields such as screening currents in QCD. The results provide a formal basis for the non-relativistic reduction assumed by Koch (1992), Hansson (1992), Schramm (1993), and others when interpreting lattice QCD screening data at high temperature. The authors note that the large reduction of the scale parameter (\(\Lambda_T \ll \Lambda_{\overline{\text{MS}}}\)) makes the perturbative regime of the reduced theory accessible at temperatures as low as about 0.5 GeV in QCD, compared to 5 GeV needed at zero temperature.
