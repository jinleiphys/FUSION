# Observing Supernova Neutrino Light Curves with Super-Kamiokande. II. Impact of the Nuclear Equation of State

> The late-time evolution of the neutrino event rate from supernovae is evaluated for Super-Kamiokande using simulated results of proto-neutron star (PNS) cooling. In the present work we extend the result of Suwa et al. (2019) [arXiv:1904.09996], which studied the dependence on the PNS mass, but focus on the impact of the nuclear equation of state (EOS). We find that the neutrino event rate depends on both the high-density and low-density EOS, where the former determines the radius of the PNS and the latter affects its surface temperature. Based on the present evaluation of the neutrino event rate we propose a new analysis method to extract the time variability of the neutrino average energy taking into account the statistical error in the observation.

## Key claim

The neutrino event rate from supernova PNS cooling depends on both the high-density EOS (which determines the PNS radius) and the low-density EOS (which affects the surface temperature via the mass number of heavy nuclei), and the authors propose a new backward-in-time analysis method that extracts the time variability of the neutrino average energy while accounting for statistical error.

## Method

The authors performed general relativistic quasi-static PNS cooling simulations using a Henyey-type method with multigroup flux-limited neutrino transfer, adopting four nuclear EOS models (Shen, LS220, Togashi, and T+S). Initial entropy and electron fraction profiles were taken from core-collapse simulations of 15 and 40 solar mass progenitors using a general relativistic neutrino radiation hydrodynamics code. The neutrino event rate at Super-Kamiokande was then evaluated from the simulated neutrino emission over 10 seconds of PNS cooling.

## Key numbers

- For the 162S15 model (Shen EOS, baryon mass 1.62 solar masses), the total expected neutrino events at Super-Kamiokande is 2,117, with 719 events after 10 seconds and 264 after 20 seconds; the last event time is 53.1 seconds.
- For the 162T15 model (Togashi EOS, same baryon mass), the total expected events is 3,605, with 1,802 events after 10 seconds and 1,153 after 20 seconds; the last event time is 146.0 seconds.
- The gravitational mass of the cold neutron star for the Shen EOS with baryon mass 1.62 solar masses is 1.473 solar masses with a radius of 14.24 km; for the Togashi EOS with the same baryon mass, the gravitational mass is 1.443 solar masses with a radius of 11.55 km.
- The binding energy difference (gravitational mass subtracted from baryon mass) for the 162S15 model is 0.088 solar masses, while for the 162T15 model it is 0.121 solar masses.

## Context

This work extends the results of Suwa et al. (2019) (Paper I), which studied the dependence of supernova neutrino signals on PNS mass but did not address the EOS dependence. The authors systematically compare four EOS models (Shen, LS220, Togashi, and T+S) and show that the neutrino light curve is sensitive to both high-density and low-density EOS properties, building on earlier work by Nakazato et al. (2019, 2020) who proposed methods to estimate neutron star mass and radius from neutrino observations. The results enable future extraction of both PNS mass and EOS from a Galactic supernova neutrino signal observed by Super-Kamiokande.
