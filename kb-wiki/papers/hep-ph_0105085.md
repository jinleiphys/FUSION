# In-medium pion weak decay constants

> In nuclear matter, the pion weak decay constant is separated into the two components $f_t, f_s$ corresponding to the time and space components of the axial-vector current. Using QCD sum rules, we compute the two decay constants from the pseudoscalar-axial vector correlation function in the matter $i \int d^4x~ e^{ip\cdot x} < ρ| T[{\bar d}(x) i γ_5 u (x)~ {\bar u}(0) γ_μγ_5 d (0)] | ρ>$. It is found that the sum rule for $f_t$ satisfies the in-medium Gell-Mann--Oakes--Renner (GOR) relation precisely while the $f_s$ sum rule does not. The $f_s$ sum rule contains the non-negligible contribution from the dimension 5 condensate $<{\bar q} i D_0 iD_0 q >_N + {1\over 8} < {\bar q} g_s σ\cdot {\cal G} q >_N$ in addition to the in-medium quark condensate. Using standard set of QCD parameters and ignoring the in-medium change of the pion mass, we obtain $f_t =105$ MeV at the nuclear saturation density. The prediction for $f_s$ depends on values of the dimension 5 condensate and on the Borel mass. However, the OPE constrains that $f_s/f_t \ge 1 $, which does not agree with the prediction from the in-medium chiral perturbation theory. Depending on the value of the dimension 5 condensate, $f_s$ at the saturation density is found to be in the range $ 112 \sim 134$ MeV at the Borel mass $M^2 \sim 1$ GeV$^2$.

# In-medium pion weak decay constants

## Key claim

Using QCD sum rules, the in-medium pion weak decay constant separates into time ($f_t$) and space ($f_s$) components, where $f_t$ satisfies the in-medium Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner (GOR) relation precisely while $f_s$ does not; the operator product expansion (OPE) constrains $f_s/f_t \ge 1$, which disagrees with predictions from in-medium chiral perturbation theory.

## Method

The author computed the pseudoscalar-axial vector correlation function in nuclear matter using finite-density QCD sum rules, employing the linear density approximation to express nuclear matrix elements in terms of nucleon matrix elements. The phenomenological side was constructed following in-medium chiral perturbation theory, with the low-lying intermediate state being an in-medium quasi-pion. The OPE was calculated up to dimension 5 condensates, and Borel sum rules were constructed separately for the time and space components of the correlation function in the nuclear rest frame.

## Key numbers

- $f_t = 105$ MeV at nuclear saturation density, using standard QCD parameters and ignoring in-medium change of the pion mass
- $f_s$ at saturation density is in the range $112 \sim 134$ MeV at Borel mass $M^2 \sim 1$ GeV$^2$, depending on the value of the dimension 5 condensate
- The dimension 5 condensate $\langle {\bar q} i D_0 iD_0 q \rangle_N + {1\over 8} \langle {\bar q} g_s \sigma \cdot {\cal G} q \rangle_N$ is estimated to be in the range $0.08$ to $0.3$ GeV$^2$ from bag model and other standard estimates
- The nucleon matrix element $\langle q^\dagger iD_0 q \rangle_N \sim 0.18$ GeV from twist-2 quark distribution functions
- The ratio $f_s/f_t$ from in-medium chiral perturbation theory is 0.28 at saturation density $\rho = 0.17$ fm$^{-3}$ using parameters $c_2=0.28$ fm and $c_3=-0.55$ fm

## Context

This work extends QCD sum rule techniques previously applied to nucleon self-energies in matter by Griegel and collaborators to the calculation of pion decay constants, and provides a model-independent alternative to the in-medium chiral perturbation theory calculation by Kirchbach and Wirzba (2000). The results are relevant for understanding partial chiral symmetry restoration in nuclear matter, the Brown-Rho scaling scenario, and muon capture processes in nuclei. The finding that $f_s/f_t \ge 1$ contradicts the in-medium chiral perturbation theory prediction of substantial quenching of the space component, motivating further study of the dimension 5 condensate and higher-order corrections.
