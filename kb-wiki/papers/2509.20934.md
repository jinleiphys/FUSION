# Nonlinear corrections to the nuclear heavy flavor structure functions

> We study numerically the small-$x$ behaviour of the nuclear gluon distribution function $ G^A(x,Q^2)$ at next-to-leading order (NLO) approximation of the Gribov-Levin-Ryskin-Mueller-Qiu, Zhu-Ruan-Shen (GLR-MQ-ZRS) nonlinear equation and quantify the impact of gluon recombination in the kinematic range of $x \le 10^{-2}$ and $Q^2 \ge 5 \text{GeV}^2$ respectively. The results are comparable to the Rausch-Guzey-Klasen (RGK) [J.Rausch, V.Guzey and M.Klasen, Phys. Rev. D 107, 054003 (2023)] nuclear gluon distributions and the nCTEQ15 parametrization at the corresponding $Q^2$ values. Using the solutions of $ G^A(x,Q^2)$ in the framework of the nonlinear GLR-MQ-ZRS evolution equation, the linear and nonlinear behavior of the charm structure functions of nuclei per nucleon $F^{c\bar{c}-A}_2(x,Q^2)$ and $F^{c\bar{c}-A}_L(x,Q^2)$ are considered. The results reveal that nonlinear corrections play an important role in charm nuclear reduced cross sections at small-$ x$ and low $Q^2$ values. The computed results are compared with experimental data from the H1 and ZEUS Collaborations.

# Nonlinear corrections to the nuclear heavy flavor structure functions

## Key claim

The authors claim that nonlinear corrections from gluon recombination, modeled via the GLR-MQ-ZRS evolution equation at next-to-leading order (NLO), significantly affect the charm structure functions and reduced cross sections of nuclei at small Bjorken-x (x ≤ 10⁻²) and low Q² (≥ 5 GeV²), with the magnitude of these effects depending on the nuclear mass number A and the correlation radius R of interacting gluons.

## Method

The authors numerically solved the GLR-MQ-ZRS nonlinear evolution equation for the nuclear gluon distribution function G^A(x,Q²) at NLO, using a linear gluon distribution from a saturation model as input. They then computed the charm structure functions F₂^{c̄c-A} and F_L^{c̄c-A} per nucleon by convolving the nonlinear gluon distributions with LO and NLO Wilson coefficient functions in the collinear generalized double asymptotic scaling (DAS) approach. The results were compared with the nCTEQ15 parametrization, the Rausch-Guzey-Klasen (RGK) model, and experimental data from the H1 and ZEUS Collaborations.

## Key numbers

- Kinematic range: x ≤ 10⁻² and Q² ≥ 5 GeV²
- Charm quark mass: m_c = 1.65 GeV
- Correlation radius values used: R = 2, 5, and 6.34 GeV⁻¹ (where 6.34 GeV⁻¹ corresponds to 1.25 fm for nuclear radius)
- Saturation scale parameters: σ₀ = 27.32 mb, λ = 0.248, x₀ = 0.42 × 10⁴ for the 4-flavor case
- QCD parameters: Λ_QCD = 0.12 GeV (LO) and 0.292 GeV (NLO) yielding α_s(M_Z) = 0.118 with N_f = 4
- Center-of-mass energies studied: √s = 1.3 TeV (LHeC) and √s = 89.4 GeV (EIC)
- Q² values for structure function plots: 6.5 and 12 GeV²
- Q² values for reduced cross section plots: 7 and 12 GeV²
- W values for structure function F₂^{c̄c}: 60, 95, and 140 GeV

## Context

This work extends the study of nonlinear QCD evolution effects from the GLR-MQ-ZRS equation, previously applied to proton targets, to nuclear targets across a range of light and heavy nuclei (C-12, Fe-56, Ag-108, Au-197, Pb-208). It builds upon the RGK model of Rausch, Guzey, and Klasen (2023) and the nCTEQ15 nuclear PDF parametrization, showing comparable results. The findings are relevant for the physics programs of the future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) and the Large Hadron Electron Collider (LHeC), where precise measurements of nuclear parton distributions and the transition to the saturation regime are key goals. The results also show consistency with the GBW, KST, BGBK, and IP-sat phenomenological models for gluon shadowing.
