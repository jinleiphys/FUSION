# On the non-uniqueness of the energy-momentum and spin currents

> The macroscopic energy-momentum and spin densities of relativistic spin hydrodynamics are obtained from the ensemble average of their respective microscopic definitions (quantum operators). These microscopic definitions suffer from ambiguities, meaning that one may obtain different forms of symmetric energy-momentum tensor and spin tensor through pseudogauge transformations (or, in other words, Belinfante improvement procedure). However, this ambiguity may be fixed if we obtain these currents using Noether's second theorem instead of widely used Noether's first theorem. The second theorem fixes the super-potential determined by local symmetry, thereby selecting a unique physically consistent pseudogauge. In this article, we use Noether's second theorem to derive energy-momentum and spin currents without the need of pseudogauge transformations for free Dirac massive particles with spin one-half.

## Key claim

Noether's second theorem, applied to local spacetime translations, yields a symmetric energy-momentum tensor and a vanishing spin tensor for free Dirac massive spin-half particles, thereby fixing the super-potential ambiguity that arises from pseudogauge transformations based on Noether's first theorem. The author claims this removes the need for arbitrary pseudogauge choices, but the derivation is restricted to free fields and the result (symmetric EMT, zero spin tensor) coincides with the Belinfante-Rosenfeld form.

## Method

The author applies Noether's second theorem to the free Dirac Lagrangian density for massive spin-half fermions, using local spacetime translations and the transformation properties of spinor fields under infinitesimal Lorentz transformations. The energy-momentum tensor is obtained from the functional derivative of the Lagrangian with respect to the local translation parameter, and the spin tensor is derived from the same variational procedure. The resulting expressions are compared to the canonical, Belinfante-Rosenfeld, de Groot–van Leeuwen–van Weert (GLW), and Hilgevoord–Wouthuysen (HW) forms obtained via pseudogauge transformations.

## Key numbers

* The canonical energy-momentum tensor for free Dirac fields is \( \T^{\mu\nu}_{\rm Can} = \frac{i}{2}\,\bar{\psi}\,\gamma^\mu\, \overleftrightarrow{\partial}^\nu\,\psi \), which is asymmetric.
* The Belinfante-Rosenfeld (BR) energy-momentum tensor is \( \T^{\mu\nu}_{\rm BR} = \frac{i}{4}\bar{\psi}\left(\gamma^{\mu}\overleftrightarrow{\partial}^\nu + \gamma^{\nu}\overleftrightarrow{\partial}^\mu \right)\psi \), with spin tensor \( \S^{\lambda,\mu\nu}_{\rm BR} = 0 \).
* The GLW energy-momentum tensor is \( \T^{\mu\nu}_{\rm GLW} = -\frac{1}{4m}\bar{\psi}\overleftrightarrow{\partial}^\mu\overleftrightarrow{\partial}^\nu\psi \), with a non-zero spin tensor given in Eq. (4.5) of the paper.
* Using Noether's second theorem, the derived energy-momentum tensor is \( \T^{\mu\nu}(x) = \frac{i}{4}\bar{\psi}\left(\gamma^{\mu}\overleftrightarrow{\partial}^\nu + \gamma^{\nu}\overleftrightarrow{\partial}^\mu \right)\psi \) and the spin tensor is \( \S^{\lambda,\mu\nu} = 0 \), matching the BR form.
* The canonical spin tensor has 4 independent components (completely antisymmetric); the total angular momentum tensor \( \J^{\lambda,\mu\nu} \) has 24 independent components; the antisymmetric part of the EMT has 6 independent components.

## Context

This work addresses the long-standing ambiguity in defining energy-momentum and spin currents for relativistic spin hydrodynamics, previously studied via pseudogauge transformations by Belinfante (1939, 1940), Rosenfeld (1940), Hehl (1976), and more recently by Florkowski (2018) and Speranza (2020). The author extends the use of Noether's second theorem, previously applied to the energy-momentum tensor by Freese (2021), to also derive the spin tensor for free Dirac fields. This enables a unique pseudogauge for relativistic spin hydrodynamics, relevant to interpreting spin polarization measurements in heavy-ion collisions (STAR Collaboration 2017, 2019, 2021, 2023; ALICE Collaboration 2019, 2021). The result is consistent with the Belinfante-Rosenfeld form and is favored by a recent study (Das 2021) showing smaller energy-density quantum fluctuations for small subsystems compared to GLW or HW forms.
