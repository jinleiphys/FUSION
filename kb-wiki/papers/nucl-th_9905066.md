# Structure effects on the Coulomb dissociation of 8B at relativistic energies

> We investigate the Coulomb dissociation of 8B on 208Pb target at the beam energy of 250 MeV/nucleon, employing the cross sections for the radiative capture reaction 7Be(p,gamma)8B calculated within the Shell Model Embedded in the Continuum (SMEC) approach. In contrast to the situation at lower beam energies, the Coulomb breakup cross sections are found to be sensitive to the M1 transitions. Comparisons of SMEC and single-particle potential model predictions show that the Coulomb breakup cross sections at these high energies are sensitive to the structure model of 8B. Analysis of the preliminary data taken recently at GSI reveal that E2 multipolarity contributes up to 25 % to the cross sections even for the relative energies of p - 7Be below 0.25 MeV.

# Structure effects on the Coulomb dissociation of 8B at relativistic energies

## Key claim

At a beam energy of 250 MeV/nucleon, the Coulomb dissociation cross sections for 8B on a 208Pb target are sensitive to M1 transitions and to the nuclear structure model of 8B, in contrast to the situation at lower beam energies; however, the E2 multipolarity contributes up to 25% to the cross sections even for p-7Be relative energies below 0.25 MeV if the angular integration range extends to 1.87 degrees, but these contributions are reduced by an order of magnitude if the maximum integration angle is approximately 1 degree.

## Method

The authors calculated Coulomb dissociation cross sections for 8B on a 208Pb target at 250 MeV/nucleon using radiative capture cross sections for the 7Be(p,gamma)8B reaction obtained from the Shell Model Embedded in the Continuum (SMEC) approach, which couples many-particle quasi-bound states to the one-particle scattering continuum. They also performed calculations using a single-particle potential model with parameters from Esbensen and Bertsch (1996) for comparison. The double differential Coulomb dissociation cross sections were computed using the equivalent photon method, integrating over the center-of-mass angle of the p-7Be system with respect to the beam direction.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 250 MeV/nucleon for the 8B projectile on a 208Pb target
- E2 multipolarity contributes up to 25% to the cross sections for p-7Be relative energies below 0.25 MeV when angular integration is performed over 0.01 degrees to 1.87 degrees
- The Q-value for the breakup 8B → 7Be + p is 0.137 MeV
- The experimental energy resolution is approximately 110 keV at ECM = 0.63 MeV
- In the SMEC approach with spin exchange parameter (1-alpha) = 0.05, the astrophysical S-factor at zero energy S17(0) = 19.594 eV·b, with fitting parameters alpha = -1.544 MeV^-1 and beta = 6.468 MeV^-2 for the low-energy behavior
- The ratio of E2 to E1 contributions at the 1_1+ resonance position is 8.15 × 10^-4 for (1-alpha) = 0.27 and 7.72 × 10^-4 for (1-alpha) = 0.05
- The quadrupole moment of 8B calculated in SMEC is 6.99 e·fm^2, compared to the experimental value of 6.83 ± 0.21 e·fm^2

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of Coulomb dissociation of 8B at lower beam energies, such as the RIKEN experiments at approximately 50 MeV/nucleon (Motobayashi 1994, Kikuchi 1997, 1998), where M1 contributions were found to be negligible and E2 contributions could be avoided by restricting measurements to ECM below 0.70 MeV and angles below 4 degrees. The paper also contrasts with sub-Coulomb energy measurements (Johannsen 1996) where nuclear and E2 contributions were dominant everywhere. The SMEC approach used here was previously applied to study the structure of mirror nuclei 8B and 8Li and their capture cross sections (Bennaceur 1998, 1999). The results enable future extraction of the astrophysical S-factor S17 for the 7Be(p,gamma)8B reaction from high-energy Coulomb dissociation data, provided the center-of-mass angle of the outgoing p-7Be pair is restricted to below 1 degree to minimize E2 contributions.
