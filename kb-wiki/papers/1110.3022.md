# Chiral Symmetry and the Nucleon-Nucleon Interaction

> We summarize the current status of our understanding of nuclear forces based upon chiral symmetry--an idea that was advocated by Gerry Brown already several decades ago.

## Key claim

Chiral effective field theory provides a systematic, order-by-order expansion for nuclear forces based on the symmetries of QCD, but the nonperturbative renormalization of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation remains an unresolved problem; the authors argue that Weinberg counting with soft cutoffs (scheme two) is likely the practical path forward, though a systematic investigation of cutoff independence using Lepage plots does not yet exist.

## Method

The authors review the construction of nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials from chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) using Weinberg power counting, where the potential is defined as a sum of irreducible graphs up to a given order (LO, NLO, NNLO, N3LO). The potential is then inserted into the Lippmann-Schwinger equation and regularized with exponential cutoff functions; low-energy constants (LECs) from the πN Lagrangian and contact terms are fitted to NN scattering data. The review covers the hierarchy of two-nucleon, three-nucleon, and four-nucleon forces that emerge at successive chiral orders.

## Key numbers

- The chiral expansion parameter is Q/Λχ, with Λχ ≈ 1 GeV (the chiral-symmetry breaking scale, set by the rho meson mass).
- At NLO, Juelich np potentials give χ²/datum of 67–105 for the 0–290 MeV np database (2402 data points); at NNLO, this improves to 12–27.
- At N3LO, the potential uses 24 contact terms (24 parameters) contributing to partial waves with L ≤ 2, compared to 35 parameters in the Nijmegen partial wave analysis and 38 in the CD-Bonn high-precision potential.
- The one-pion exchange potential uses gA²/(4fπ²) with axial-vector coupling gA, pion decay constant fπ, and pion mass mπ; the 1PE form is exact on-shell to all orders.
- The c_i LECs from the dimension-two πN Lagrangian are: c1 = –0.81 GeV⁻¹, c2 = 2.80 GeV⁻¹, c3 = –3.20 GeV⁻¹, c4 = 5.40 GeV⁻¹ (from NN fit); these differ from πN analysis values (e.g., c3 = –4.69 ± 1.34 GeV⁻¹, c4 = 3.40 ± 0.04 GeV⁻¹).
- Typical cutoff choices for the regulator are Λ ≈ 0.5 GeV, well below Λχ ≈ 1 GeV.

## Context

This review extends the program initiated by Weinberg (1979, 1990–1992) to apply chiral effective field theory to nuclear forces, following earlier work by Ordóñez, Ray, and van Kolck (1994, 1996) at NNLO and the Idaho group’s N3LO potential (Entem and Machleidt, 2002–2003). It contrasts with the Kaplan-Savage-Wise (1996) criticism of Weinberg counting and discusses two renormalization schemes: Valderrama’s (2009) approach with perturbative subleading orders and the standard Weinberg scheme with soft cutoffs. The work enables systematic calculations of nuclear structure and reactions using chiral two- and three-nucleon forces, as already applied in few-nucleon reactions, light- and medium-mass nuclei, and nuclear matter by many groups (e.g., Navrátil 2007, Otsuka 2009, Bogner 2005, Hebeler and Schwenk 2009).
