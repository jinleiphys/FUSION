# Constraining the equation of state of high-density cold matter using nuclear and astronomical measurements

> The increasing richness of data related to cold dense matter, from laboratory experiments to neutron-star observations, requires a framework for constraining the properties of such matter that makes use of all relevant information. Here, we present a rigorous but practical Bayesian approach that can include diverse evidence, such as nuclear data and the inferred masses, radii, tidal deformabilities, moments of inertia, and gravitational binding energies of neutron stars. We emphasize that the full posterior probability distributions of measurements should be used rather than, as is common, imposing a cut on the maximum mass or other quantities. Our method can be used with any parameterization of the equation of state (EOS). We use both a spectral parameterization and a piecewise polytropic parameterization with variable transition densities to illustrate the implications of current measurements and show how future measurements in many domains could improve our understanding of cold catalyzed matter. We find that different types of measurements will play distinct roles in constraining the EOS in different density ranges. For example, better symmetry energy measurements will have a major influence on our understanding of matter somewhat below nuclear saturation density but little influence above that density. In contrast, precise radius measurements or multiple tidal deformability measurements of the quality of those from GW170817 or better will improve our knowledge of the EOS over a broader density range.

## Key claim

The paper presents a rigorous Bayesian framework for combining diverse nuclear and astronomical data to constrain the equation of state (EOS) of high-density cold matter, emphasizing that full posterior probability distributions should be used rather than imposing hard cuts on maximum mass or other quantities, as is common in previous work.

## Method

The authors develop a Bayesian statistical procedure that marginalizes over nuisance parameters (central densities, stellar-specific parameters, and measurement-specific parameters) to obtain posterior probability densities for EOS parameters. They demonstrate the method using both a spectral parameterization (with expansion up to x^3 in the adiabatic index) and a piecewise polytropic parameterization with variable transition densities, incorporating constraints from nuclear symmetry energy measurements, neutron-star mass measurements, tidal deformabilities from gravitational-wave events, and hypothetical future measurements of radii, moments of inertia, and binding energies.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density is taken as n_s = 0.16 baryons per fm^3, with symmetric matter energy per nucleon E_sym = -16.0 MeV
- The chirp mass for GW170817 is M_ch = 1.186 ± 0.001 M_sun
- The spectral parameterization uses uniform priors: γ_0 ∈ [0.2, 2], γ_1 ∈ [-1.6, 1.7], γ_2 ∈ [-0.6, 0.6], γ_3 ∈ [-0.02, 0.02]
- The proton fraction at nuclear saturation density is approximately 1%, small enough to be neglected for symmetry energy calculations
- For GW170817, the binary tidal deformability for mass ratio M_2/M_1 = 0.6 is only ~5% larger than for M_2/M_1 = 1, using the scaling Λ ∝ M^{-6}

## Context

This work extends the fully Bayesian approaches of Steiner et al. (2015), Alvarez-Castillo et al. (2015), Alvarez-Castillo et al. (2016), and Raithel et al. (2018), while explicitly correcting the common practice of imposing hard lower bounds on maximum mass (as done in most previous analyses except Alvarez-Castillo et al. 2016). The method enables future combination of measurements from NICER, gravitational-wave events like GW170817, and improved nuclear symmetry energy measurements to constrain the EOS across different density ranges, with symmetry energy measurements primarily affecting densities below nuclear saturation and radius or tidal deformability measurements constraining a broader density range.
