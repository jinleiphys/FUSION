# A Solvable Model for Many Quark Systems in QCD Hamiltonians

> Motivated by a canonical, QCD Hamiltonian we propose an effective Hamiltonian to represent an arbitrary number of quarks in hadronic bags. The structure of the effective Hamiltonian is discussed and the BCS-type solutions that may represent constituent quarks are presented. The single particle orbitals are chosen as 3-dimensional harmonic oscillators and we discuss a class of exact solutions that can be obtained when a subset of single-particle basis states is restricted to include a certain number of orbital excitations. The general problem, which includes all possible orbital states, can also be solved by combining analytical and numerical methods.

## Key claim

The authors propose an effective QCD-inspired Hamiltonian for many-quark systems in hadronic bags and show that, when the gluon-mediated potential is replaced by a spatially averaged constant interaction, the Hamiltonian can be solved nearly analytically for an arbitrary number of quark orbital levels, provided the mass term is neglected for the two- and three-level cases and a BCS-type mean-field scheme is adopted for the general case.

## Method

The authors construct an effective Hamiltonian starting from a canonical QCD Hamiltonian, using three-dimensional harmonic oscillator orbitals for confinement and replacing the static gluon-quark potential by a constant \(V_0\) after spatial averaging over the hadronic bag. They expand the fermion fields in a complete particle basis, couple the creation and annihilation operators to total spin \(j\), and derive the kinetic and potential terms. For two- and three-level systems (restricted subsets of single-particle orbitals for a given \(j\)), they identify an \(SU(2)\) pseudo-spin algebra in the kinetic energy and obtain analytic eigenvalues. For an arbitrary number of levels, they combine analytical methods with a BCS-type mean-field treatment to handle the kinetic energy and mass term.

## Key numbers

* The potential term after spatial averaging reduces to \(V = \frac{V_0}{2} \mathcal{C}_2(SU(3))\), where \(\mathcal{C}_2(SU(3))\) is the second-order Casimir operator of color \(SU(3)\).
* For two-level systems, the energy eigenvalues are \(E^{jN} = 2A_{\kappa Nj} M_J + \frac{V_0}{2} (\lambda_C^2 + \lambda_C\mu_C + \mu_C^2 + 3\lambda_C + 3\mu_C)\), where \(A_{\kappa Nj}\) is defined in terms of \(\gamma\) and the oscillator quantum numbers \(N\) and \(j\).
* The kinetic energy coupling constants for two-level systems are \(A_{aNj} = \sqrt{\frac{\gamma (N-j+\frac{3}{2})}{2}}\) (case a) and \(A_{bNj} = \sqrt{\frac{\gamma (N+j+\frac{3}{2})}{2}}\) (case b).

## Context

This work extends the method of Adam-Peter (2008) and RMF (2009) by including all orbital levels in the quark sector, generalizing from \(SU(2)\) color to \(SU(3)\) color, and keeping approximately mass-degenerate \(u\) and \(d\) quarks in the flavor sector. It enables the construction of solvable many-quark models that can produce meson and baryon spectra from the lowest s- and p-quark orbitals, and provides a framework for treating residual interactions with standard many-body methods.
