# On eta photoproduction on the neutron and pi^0 eta photoproduction

> The recently discovered enhancement of eta photoproduction on the quasi-free neutron at energies around s^1/2 ~ 1.67 GeV is addressed within an SU(3) coupled channels model. The K Sigma threshold plays a significant role and the quasi-free cross sections on proton and neutron, sigma_n and sigma_p, can be quantitatively explained. For the reaction gamma p --> pi^0 eta p, we evaluate the polarization observables I^S and I^C from a chiral unitary amplitude developed earlier. The I^S and I^C observables have been recently measured for the first time by the CBELSA/TAPS collaboration. We show the significance of the Delta(1700)D33 resonance and its S-wave decay into eta Delta(1232) which further confirms the dynamical nature of this resonance.

## Key claim

The enhancement of eta photoproduction on the quasi-free neutron near sqrt(s) ~ 1.67 GeV can be explained quantitatively by an SU(3) coupled-channels model without invoking pentaquark states, provided the K Sigma threshold dynamics and the difference in photon coupling to charged kaons in the intermediate K Lambda states for proton versus neutron targets are properly accounted for.

## Method

The hadronic interaction was mediated by the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction in the coupled channels pi N, eta N, K Lambda, and K Sigma, unitarized via a Bethe-Salpeter equation, with explicit resonance states for the N*(1650) and a phenomenological background. A global fit of E0+ multipoles, S-wave cross sections, and partial waves was performed for multiple reactions including gamma N to pi N, pi N to pi N, gamma p to eta p, gamma n to eta n, pi N to eta N, gamma N to K Y, and pi N to K Y (Y = Lambda, Sigma). For the reaction gamma p to pi0 eta p, the polarization observables I^S and I^C were evaluated from a previously developed chiral unitary amplitude, with the photon coupling to the Delta(1700) obtained from experimental data through a manifestly gauge-invariant expression.

## Key numbers

- The enhancement in eta photoproduction on the quasi-free neutron occurs at energies around sqrt(s) ~ 1.67 GeV.
- The cross section ratio sigma(gamma n to eta n) / sigma(gamma p to eta p) shows a sharp peak structure attributed to intermediate K Lambda and K Sigma states.
- For the reaction gamma p to pi0 eta p, the polarization observables I^S and I^C were evaluated at three different energies W (sqrt(s)) and compared to CBELSA/TAPS data; the model reproduces the complex shapes of the angular distributions, with deviations becoming noticeable at the highest energies.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary framework of Doring (2009) and Doring (2009) to explain the neutron eta photoproduction enhancement previously interpreted as a potential pentaquark signal by Diakonov (1997) and Fix (2007). The results contradict the pentaquark interpretation and instead support a dynamical origin from K Sigma threshold effects. The successful prediction of I^S and I^C observables for gamma p to pi0 eta p provides further evidence for the dynamically generated nature of the Delta(1700) resonance, building on earlier theoretical work by Jido (2001) and Doring (2005) and enabling future tests within hadron exchange frameworks such as Doring (2010).
