# Extracting Nucleon Magnetic Moments and Electric Polarizabilities from Lattice QCD in Background Electric Fields

> Nucleon properties are investigated in background electric fields. As the magnetic moments of baryons affect their relativistic propagation in constant electric fields, electric polarizabilities cannot be determined without knowledge of magnetic moments. This is analogous to the experimental situation, for which determination of polarizabilities from the Compton amplitude requires subtraction of Born terms. With the background field method, we devise combinations of nucleon correlation functions in constant electric fields that isolate magnetic moments and electric polarizabilities. Using an ensemble of anisotropic gauge configurations with dynamical clover fermions, we demonstrate how both observables can be determined from lattice QCD simulations in background electric fields. We obtain results for the neutron and proton, however, our study is currently limited to electrically neutral sea quarks. The value we extract for the nucleon isovector magnetic moment is comparable to those obtained from measuring lattice three-point functions at similar pion masses.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that nucleon magnetic moments and electric polarizabilities can be extracted from lattice QCD two-point functions in background electric fields, but only by using boost-projected correlation functions to perform a Born-like subtraction; the electric polarizability cannot be determined without knowledge of the magnetic moment, and the study is currently limited to electrically neutral sea quarks.

## Method

The authors compute nucleon two-point correlation functions on an ensemble of 200 anisotropic gauge configurations with (2+1)-flavors of dynamical clover fermions, modifying the gauge links by multiplying with Abelian links to implement a constant background electric field. They derive analytic forms for boost-projected correlation functions from a single-hadron effective action, then perform simultaneous fits to both boost-projected correlators at each field strength to extract ground-state energies and magnetic couplings, followed by field-correlated fits to determine the mass, electric polarizability, and magnetic moment.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: \( m_\pi \approx 390 \, \text{MeV} \)
- Spatial lattice spacing: \( a_s = 0.123(3) \, \text{fm} \)
- Anisotropy parameter: \( \xi \equiv a_s / a_t = 3.5 \)
- Lattice size: \( 20^3 \times 128 \)
- Expansion parameter for field strength: \( (e \mathcal{E} / m_\pi^2)^2 = 0.18 \, n^2 \) for integer \( n \)
- Neutron ground state mass from zero-field fit: \( a_t M = 0.206(2) \) (lattice units)
- Neutron electric polarizability (fit I): \( \alpha_E^{\text{latt}} = 40(9)(2) \) (lattice units)
- Neutron magnetic coupling (fit I): \( \mu^{\text{latt}} = -52(2)(1) \) (lattice units)

## Context

This work extends the background field method for hadron properties, previously applied to pseudoscalar meson electric polarizabilities by Detmold (2009), to spin-half baryons by treating baryon spin relativistically. It addresses the same Born-subtraction problem encountered in experimental Compton scattering analyses, and the results for the isovector magnetic moment are comparable to those from lattice three-point function calculations at similar pion masses. The method enables future fully dynamical calculations with charged sea quarks and larger volumes to control systematic uncertainties from the electric field expansion.
