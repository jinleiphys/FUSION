# Strong-coupling Jet Energy Loss from AdS/CFT

> We propose a novel definition of a holographic light hadron jet and consider the phenomenological consequences, including the very first fully self-consistent, completely strong-coupling calculation of the jet nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$, which we find compares surprisingly well with recent preliminary data from LHC. We show that the thermalization distance for light parton jets is an extremely sensitive function of the \emph{a priori} unspecified string initial conditions and that worldsheets corresponding to non-asymptotic energy jets are not well approximated by a collection of null geodesics. Our new string jet prescription, which is defined by a separation of scales from plasma to jet, leads to the re-emergence of the late-time Bragg peak in the instantaneous jet energy loss rate; unlike heavy quarks, the energy loss rate is unusually sensitive to the very definition of the string theory object itself. A straightforward application of the new jet definition leads to significant jet quenching, even in the absence of plasma. By renormalizing the in-medium suppression by that in the vacuum we find qualitative agreement with preliminary CMS $R_{AA}^{jet}(p_T)$ data in our simple plasma brick model. We close with comments on our results and an outlook on future work.

## Key claim

The authors propose a new holographic definition of a light hadron jet based on a radial cutoff in the dual string theory, and find that their simple plasma brick model yields qualitative agreement with preliminary CMS jet nuclear modification factor data, suggesting that strong-coupling dynamics alone might describe jet suppression, but only after renormalizing the in-medium suppression by the vacuum suppression, which itself produces significant jet quenching even without plasma.

## Method

The authors numerically solve the classical Nambu-Goto/Polyakov string equations of motion in an AdS-Schwarzschild background, using a set of point-like initial conditions parameterized by the radial creation position \(u_c\) and amplitude \(A\). They define a new "\(\Delta u\)" jet prescription that separates the string into a jet part (above a radial cutoff \(u^*\)) and a thermalized part (below the cutoff), compute the instantaneous energy loss rate, and calculate the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}^{jet}(p_T)\) by renormalizing the in-medium suppression by the vacuum suppression in a static plasma brick model.

## Key numbers

- Thermalization distance \(x_{therm}\) for a 100 GeV jet varies by a factor of \(\sim 10\) depending on the initial condition parameters \(u_c\) and \(A\) (plasma temperature \(T = 350\) MeV).
- The null geodesic approximation to the string endpoint trajectory is only valid for \(Q^2 < 0\) at \(E = 100\) GeV; for \(Q^2 = 100\) GeV\(^2\) the endpoint is not well approximated.
- The authors use \(\lambda = 5.5\) throughout the paper.
- The plasma temperature used in the brick model is 350 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the holographic jet energy loss program of Chesler et al. (2008) and Ficnar et al. (2012) by introducing a new jet definition that addresses the problem of thermalized string segments counting toward the jet energy. It contrasts with the null geodesic approximation approach of Chesler (2014) by showing that at non-asymptotic energies (100 GeV) the approximation depends sensitively on string initial conditions. The qualitative agreement with preliminary CMS data suggests that strong-coupling dynamics might describe jet suppression, though the authors note that perturbative QCD models (e.g., Majumder 2010, Djordjevic 2014) also successfully describe jet observables.
