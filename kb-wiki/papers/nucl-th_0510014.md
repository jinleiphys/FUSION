# Dissipative hydrodynamics for viscous relativistic fluids

> Explicit equations are given for describing the space-time evolution of non-ideal (viscous) relativistic fluids undergoing boost-invariant longitudinal and arbitrary transverse expansion. The equations are derived from the second-order Israel-Stewart approach which ensures causal evolution. Both azimuthally symmetric (1+1)-dimensional and non-symmetric (2+1)-dimensional transverse expansion are discussed. The latter provides the formal basis for the hydrodynamic computation of elliptic flow in relativistic heavy-ion collisions including dissipative effects.

## Key claim

The paper provides explicit causal equations of motion for dissipative (viscous) relativistic hydrodynamics with boost-invariant longitudinal flow and arbitrary transverse expansion, derived from the second-order Israel-Stewart approach. The authors argue that certain higher-order terms in the relaxation equations for the dissipative flows should be dropped for consistency, contrary to some prior treatments, because keeping them introduces modifications to transport coefficients that depend on the macroscopic expansion rate rather than purely microscopic physics.

## Method

The authors start from the conservation laws for energy-momentum and baryon number, decompose the energy-momentum tensor and baryon current into equilibrium and dissipative parts (bulk pressure Π, shear pressure tensor π^{μν}, and heat flow q^μ), and derive the non-ideal fluid equations in the Landau frame. They then apply the second-order Israel-Stewart formalism to obtain relaxation-type transport equations for the dissipative flows, using an expansion of the entropy flow vector Q^μ truncated at second order in the irreversible flows. These equations are specialized to systems with longitudinal boost invariance using (τ,x,y,η) coordinates, and explicit forms are given for both azimuthally symmetric (1+1)-dimensional and non-symmetric (2+1)-dimensional transverse expansion.

## Key numbers

- The metric tensor in (τ,x,y,η) coordinates is given as g^{mn} = diag(1, -1, -1, -1/τ²) and g_{mn} = diag(1, -1, -1, -τ²)
- The local expansion rate θ for the azimuthally symmetric case is θ = (1/τ) ∂_τ(τ γ_r) + (1/r) ∂_r(r v_r γ_r)
- The relaxation equation for bulk pressure is ∂_τ Π + v_r ∂_r Π = -(1/(γ_r τ_Π))[Π + ζθ]
- The relaxation equation for the shear component π^{ηη} is ∂_τ π^{ηη} + v_r ∂_r π^{ηη} = -(1/(γ_r τ_π))[π^{ηη} - (2η/τ²)(θ/3 - γ_r/τ)]
- The relaxation equation for π^{φφ} is ∂_τ π^{φφ} + v_r ∂_r π^{φφ} = -(1/(γ_r τ_π))[π^{φφ} - (2η/r²)(θ/3 - γ_r v_r/r)]

## Context

This work extends the recent numerical implementations of viscous relativistic hydrodynamics by Muronga and Rischke (2004) and Chaudhuri (2005), which were limited to (1+1)-dimensional cylindrically symmetric expansion with boost-invariant longitudinal dynamics. The paper provides the formal basis for computing elliptic flow in relativistic heavy-ion collisions including dissipative effects, which was previously only possible with ideal fluid dynamics as reviewed by Heinz (2004). The (2+1)-dimensional equations presented here are original and enable future numerical codes to study non-central collisions with shear and bulk viscosity, allowing extraction of transport coefficients from experimental data at RHIC.
