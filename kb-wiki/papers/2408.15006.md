# Sources of longitudinal flow decorrelations in high-energy nuclear collisions

> The longitudinal structure of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) consists of several components spanning various scales. However, its short-range features are often obscured by final-state non-flow correlations. Here, we introduce a data-driven approach to separate initial state structures from non-flow effects. The longitudinal structure is found having two distinct components: one that reflects the global twisted geometry of the QGP, and another that captures localized fluctuations in rapidity. The characteristics of this second component, contributing to short- and medium-range flow decorrelations, can be quantified by comparing collisions of nuclei with different shapes. This study represents the first successful attempt to disentangle long- and short-range flow decorrelations from non-flow backgrounds, providing new insights into the initial conditions of heavy-ion collisions.

# Sources of longitudinal flow decorrelations in high-energy nuclear collisions

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that longitudinal flow decorrelations in the quark-gluon plasma arise from two distinct initial-state components, a global twisted geometry and localized rapidity fluctuations, and that the short-range component can be separated from final-state non-flow by comparing collisions of nuclei with different shapes, specifically using the large quadrupole deformation of uranium to expose these effects.

## Method
The study uses the AMPT transport model to simulate U+U collisions at 193 GeV and Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV, with nucleons sampled from Woods-Saxon distributions including quadrupole deformation parameters. Elliptic flow is calculated via the standard two-particle correlation method, and also by projecting the flow along the direction of eccentricity vectors defined from all participating nucleons (capturing long-range correlations) and from partons at mid-rapidity after string melting (capturing both long- and short-range initial-state correlations). The ratios of flow observables between U+U with and without deformation, and between U+U and Au+Au, are used to decompose the flow into long-range, short-range, and non-flow components.

## Key numbers
- U+U collisions simulated at sqrt(s_NN) = 193 GeV; Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV
- Quadrupole deformation beta = 0.28 for U-238, beta = -0.14 for Au-197, beta = 0.0 for spherical U-238
- Nuclear radius R_0 = 6.81 fm for U, 6.62 fm for Au; skin thickness a_0 = 0.55 fm for U, 0.52 fm for Au
- Partonic cross-section of 3 mb used in AMPT string-melting mode
- Final-state particles selected from transverse momentum range 0.2 < p_T < 3 GeV/c and pseudorapidity range |eta| < 5
- Analysis focuses on 0–5% most central collisions defined by charged particle multiplicity in |eta| < 0.5
- Estimated non-flow in 0–5% centrality within STAR acceptance: 6.9% for deformed U+U, 14.4% for spherical U+U, 11.8% for Au+Au (full event, |Delta eta| < 2); subevent method (0.8 < |Delta eta| < 2) gives 4.2%, 8.7%, and 6.8% respectively
- Non-flow from scaling method is about 30–40% lower than the subtraction method when integrated over |Delta eta| < 2

## Context
This work extends the study of longitudinal flow decorrelations in heavy-ion collisions, building on prior investigations of initial-state fluctuations in string models by Bozek (2015) and Pang (2015), and on the use of nuclear deformation to constrain initial conditions as demonstrated by Giacalone (2021) and Jia (2021). The method provides a data-driven approach to separate initial-state short-range correlations from non-flow, which has been a longstanding challenge in interpreting two-particle correlation measurements. The results enable more precise extraction of nuclear deformation parameters from experimental data, such as the STAR measurement of U+U versus Au+Au collisions (STAR 2024), and can be extended to other bulk observables like triangular flow and transverse momentum fluctuations.
