# Central and peripheral interactions of hadrons

> Surprisingly enough, the ratio of elastic to inelastic cross sections of proton interactions increases with energy in the interval correspond- ing to ISR - LHC (i.e. from 10 GeV to 10 TeV). That leads to special features of their spatial interaction region at these and higher ener- gies. Within the framework of some phenomenological models, we show how the particular ranges of the transferred momenta measured in elastic scattering experiments expose the spatial features of the in- elastic interaction region according to the unitarity condition. The difference between their predictions at higher energies is discussed. The notion of central and peripheral collisions of hadrons is treated in terms of the impact parameters description. It is shown that the shape of the differential cross section in the diffraction cone is mostly determined by collisions with intermediate impact parameters. Elastic scattering at very small transferred momenta is sensitive to peripheral processes with large impact parameters. The role of central collisions in formation of the diffraction cone is less significant.

# Central and peripheral interactions of hadrons

## Key claim

The ratio of elastic to inelastic cross sections of proton interactions increases with energy from 10 GeV to 10 TeV, and the spatial shape of the inelastic interaction region depends critically on whether the imaginary part of the elastic amplitude at large transferred momenta is negative (as in the kfk-model) or positive (as in alternative approaches), with the latter leading to a toroidal hollow region at asymptotically high energies rather than continued blackening.

## Method

The authors use the kfk-model, a QCD-motivated phenomenological model derived from the Stochastic Vacuum Model, which provides analytical expressions for the elastic scattering amplitude in both transferred-momentum (t) and impact-parameter (b) representations. They apply the unitarity condition in b-space to relate the elastic amplitude to the inelastic overlap function, then decompose the integrals over t and b into separate regions to study how different spatial zones (central, intermediate, peripheral) contribute to the measured differential cross section and how different t-ranges map onto the b-profile of the interaction region.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of elastic to total cross sections, expressed as 4σ_el/σ_tot, increases from 1.03 at 7 TeV to 1.148 at 100 TeV and 1.26 at 10^4 TeV in the kfk-model.
- The parameter ζ (the integral of the imaginary part of the amplitude over all t at b=0) is 0.95058 at 7 TeV, 0.99531 at 100 TeV, and 0.99793 at 10^4 TeV in the kfk-model.
- The zero of the imaginary part of the amplitude, |t_0|, moves from 0.47566 GeV^2 at 7 TeV to 0.28458 GeV^2 at 100 TeV and 0.13978 GeV^2 at 10^4 TeV.
- The inelastic profile at central collisions G(s,0) reaches 0.99756 at 7 TeV, 0.99998 at 100 TeV, and 1.00000 at 10^4 TeV in the kfk-model.
- The ratio σ_inel/σ_el decreases from 2.89 at 7 TeV to 2.48 at 100 TeV and 2.17 at 10^4 TeV.
- The product B|t_0| (slope times zero position) decreases from 9.4676 at 7 TeV to 7.6956 at 100 TeV and 6.2829 at 10^4 TeV.
- The parameter a_0 in the kfk-model is fixed at 1.39 GeV^{-2}.
- The real part ρ = f_R/f_I at t=0 is measured to be about 0.1 to 0.14 at high energies, contributing only about 1% to 2% to the forward differential cross section.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the spatial structure of hadron interactions from the ISR energy range (11-60 GeV) through LHC energies (2.76-13 TeV) using the kfk-model of KFK (2015), which successfully describes elastic scattering data from TOTEM and other collaborations. The paper contrasts the kfk-model predictions with alternative approaches that assume a positive imaginary part at large t, as used by Amaldi and Schubert (1980), Drenska and collaborators, and others, which lead to a toroidal hollow (TEH) regime rather than the black-edge-large (BEL) regime. The results enable future tests at higher energies, particularly through the predicted rapid motion of the dip position to smaller |t| and the non-exponential curvature of the diffraction cone, which may already be visible in TOTEM 8 TeV data and has been interpreted by Jenkovszky (2017) as an effect of one-pion exchange in the t-channel.
