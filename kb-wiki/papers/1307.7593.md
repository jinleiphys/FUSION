# A microscopic model beyond mean-field: from giant resonances properties to the fit of new effective interactions

> A completely microscopic beyond mean-field approach has been elaborated to overcome some intrinsic limitations of self-consistent mean-field schemes applied to nuclear systems, such as the incapability to produce some properties of single-particle states (e.g. spectroscopic factors), as well as of collective states (e.g. their damping width and their gamma decay to the ground state or to low lying states). Since commonly used effective interactions are fitted at the mean-field level, one should aim at refitting them including the desired beyond mean-field contributions in the refitting procedure. If zero-range interactions are used, divergences arise. We present some steps towards the refitting of Skyrme interactions, for its application in finite nuclei.

## Key claim

A fully microscopic particle-vibration coupling (PVC) model built on Skyrme functionals can reproduce the spreading width and gamma-decay width of giant resonances in finite nuclei, but the use of zero-range Skyrme interactions at beyond-mean-field level introduces divergences that require a renormalization procedure before the interactions can be refitted for use beyond the mean-field approximation.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) plus random-phase approximation (RPA) calculations using Skyrme interactions, then added particle-vibration coupling (PVC) on top to compute the strength function of the isovector giant quadrupole resonance (IVGQR) and the gamma-decay width of the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance (ISGQR) in 208Pb. For the divergence problem, they introduced a cutoff on relative momenta in the zero-range Skyrme interaction, modifying the interaction vertex with parameters lambda and lambda' to regularize the matrix elements for the total energy and single-particle energy at second order in many-body theory.

## Key numbers

- The IVGQR in 208Pb has an energy centroid at 21 MeV and a spreading width of about 3.8 MeV, in good agreement with experimental findings.
- For the ISGQR gamma-decay to the ground state in 208Pb, the SLy5 interaction gives 231.54 eV at E_ISGQR = 12.28 MeV; SGII gives 163.22 eV at 11.72 MeV; SkP gives 119.18 eV at 10.28 MeV; LNS gives 176.57 eV at 12.10 MeV.
- For the ISGQR gamma-decay to the first 3- state in 208Pb, SLy5 gives 3.39 eV (E_3- = 3.62 MeV); SGII gives 29.18 eV (E_3- = 3.14 MeV); SkP gives 8.34 eV (E_3- = 3.29 MeV); LNS gives 39.87 eV (E_3- = 3.19 MeV).
- The experimental gamma-decay width of the ISGQR to the ground state is 130 +/- 40 eV at E_ISGQR = 10.60 MeV (Beene 1989).
- The experimental gamma-decay width of the ISGQR to the 3- state is 5 +/- 5 eV (Beene 1989).

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological surface coupling model of Bortignon (1981) by using a consistent Skyrme force in both the HF+RPA diagonalization and the PVC vertex, applied to giant resonances in 208Pb. The results enable future refitting of Skyrme interactions at the beyond-mean-field level, addressing the divergence problem first studied in infinite nuclear matter by Moghrabi (2010, 2012) and now extended to finite nuclei. The approach aims to overcome limitations of self-consistent mean-field models, which cannot reproduce spectroscopic factors, damping widths, or gamma decay of collective states.
