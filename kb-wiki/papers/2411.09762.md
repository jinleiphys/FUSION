# Efficient Quantum Simulation of QCD Jets on the Light Front

> Quark and gluon jets provide one of the best ways to probe the matter produced in ultrarelativistic high-energy collisions, from cold nuclear matter to hot quark-gluon plasma. In this work, we propose a unified framework for efficient quantum simulation of many-body dynamics using the (3+1)-dimensional QCD Hamiltonian on the light front, particularly suited for studying the scattering of quark and gluon jets on nuclear matter in heavy-ion collisions. We describe scalable methods for mapping physical degrees of freedom onto qubits and for simulating in-medium jet evolution. We then validate our framework by implementing an algorithm that directly maps second-quantized Fock states onto qubits and uses Trotterized simulation for simulating time dynamics. Using a classical emulator, we investigate the evolution of quark and gluon jets with up to three particles in Fock states, extending prior studies. These calculations enable the study of key observables, including jet momentum broadening, particle production, and parton distribution functions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a unified framework for efficient quantum simulation of (3+1)-dimensional QCD jet evolution on the light front using a direct encoding scheme that maps second-quantized Fock states onto qubits, but the numerical validation is limited to classical emulation of jets with up to three particles in Fock states (quark plus up to two gluons, or gluon plus up to two gluons) using 36-128 qubits, rather than on actual quantum hardware.

## Method

The authors discretize the light-front momentum space with harmonic resolution K and transverse cutoff N⊥, then map the second-quantized fermionic and bosonic creation/annihilation operators directly onto qubit operators using the Jordan-Wigner transformation for fermions and a bosonic encoding scheme. The time evolution is performed via Trotterized simulation of the QCD Hamiltonian plus a background field representing the medium, with the background field modeled using the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model where the color charge density is a Gaussian stochastic variable. The algorithm is implemented on the Qiskit quantum simulator to evolve initial quark or gluon jet states through multiple timesteps, measuring observables such as transverse momentum broadening, gluon number, and parton distribution functions.

## Key numbers

- The quantum simulation uses 36-128 qubits for the numerical demonstrations
- The harmonic resolution K determines the maximum number of particles in a Fock state, with the maximum gluon occupancy per mode given by floor(K/k⁺_j)
- The transverse momentum discretization uses N⊥ modes in each transverse direction, with transverse momentum quanta kⁱ = -N⊥, -N⊥+1, ..., N⊥-1
- The background field is discretized into Nη uncorrelated layers, each with duration τ = Lη/Nη
- The correlation function for the color charge density includes factors of 1/(a⊥²) in transverse space and 1/τ in longitudinal time

## Context

This work extends the time-dependent Basis Light-Front Quantization (tBLFQ) approach previously applied to QED (Zhao 2013) and QCD jet evolution (Li 2020, Li 2021, Li 2023) by enabling simulation of multi-gluon Fock states beyond the leading sectors. It also builds on the basis state encoding approach of Barata (2021, 2022, 2023) which was limited to one dynamical gluon, by introducing a direct encoding scheme that scales polynomially with system size. The framework enables non-perturbative simulation of jet evolution without the eikonal approximation (Dumitru 2002) or multiple soft scattering approximation (Baier 1996, Zakharov 1997), and allows access to real-time observables including momentum broadening, gluon production, and parton distribution functions.
