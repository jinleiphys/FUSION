# Chiral unitary approach to hadron spectroscopy

> The s-wave meson-baryon interaction in the $S = -1$, $S= 0$ and $S= -2$ sectors is studied by means of coupled channels, using the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian and the N/D method or equivalently the Bethe-Salpeter equation to implement unitarity. This chiral approach leads to the dynamical generation of the $Λ(1405)$, $Λ(1670)$ and $Σ(1620)$ states for $S = -1$, the $N^*(1535)$ for $S= 0$ and the $Ξ(1620)$ for $S= -2$. We look for poles in the complex plane and extract the couplings of the resonances to the different final states. This allows identifying the $Λ(1405)$ and the $Λ(1670)$ resonances with $\bar{K}N$ and $KΞ$ quasibound states, respectively. Our results are found to be incompatible with the measured properties of the $Ξ(1690)$ resonance, thus ruling this state out as the remaining member of this octet of dynamically generated resonances. We therefore assign $1/2^-$ for the spin and parity of the $Ξ(1620)$ resonance as the $S=-2$ member of the lowest-lying $1/2^-$ octet.

## Key claim

The chiral unitary approach dynamically generates a nonet of \(J^P = 1/2^-\) baryon resonances from meson-baryon interactions, but the \(\Xi(1690)\) resonance is ruled out as the \(S=-2\) member of this octet because its observed decay properties are incompatible with the model's predictions; instead, the \(\Xi(1620)\) is assigned as that member, with the caveat that the model's pole width (up to 280 MeV) is much larger than the experimental width, but the apparent width in the \(\pi\Xi\) invariant mass distribution is reduced to about 50 MeV by the Flatte effect due to the nearby \(\bar{K}\Lambda\) threshold.

## Method

The s-wave meson-baryon interaction was studied using coupled channels with the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian, and unitarity was implemented via the N/D method (equivalent to the Bethe-Salpeter equation with on-shell factorized amplitudes). The loop function \(G_l\) was regularized using dimensional regularization with subtraction constants \(a_l\) matched to a previous cutoff calculation at 630 MeV for the \(S=-1\) sector, and fitted to \(\pi N\) scattering data for the \(S=0\) sector. Poles in the complex plane were searched for on the second Riemann sheet to identify dynamically generated resonances and extract their couplings to final states.

## Key numbers

- For \(S=-1\), two poles were found in the \(I=0\) amplitude: at \(1426 + i\,16\) MeV (the \(\Lambda(1405)\)) and \(1708 + i\,21\) MeV (the \(\Lambda(1670)\)); one pole in the \(I=1\) amplitude at \(1579 + i\,296\) MeV (the \(\Sigma(1620)\)).
- For \(S=0\), the \(N^*(1535)\) resonance was generated; the subtraction constants used were \(\mu = 1200\) MeV, \(a_{\pi N} = 2.0\), \(a_{\eta N} = 0.1\), \(a_{K\Lambda} = 1.5\), and \(a_{K\Sigma} = -2.8\).
- For \(S=-2\), with all subtraction constants set to \(-2.0\), a pole was found at \(1607 + i\,140\) MeV; by varying \(a_{\pi\Xi}\) and \(a_{\bar{K}\Lambda}\) within a natural range, the pole mass remained around 1596–1607 MeV, while the half-width \(\Gamma/2\) varied from 66 to 140 MeV.
- The \(\bar{K}\Lambda\) threshold lies at 1611 MeV, and the resonance couples strongly to \(\pi\Xi\) (coupling squared \(|g_{\pi\Xi}|^2 = 5.9\) to 8.7) and \(\bar{K}\Lambda\) (4.2 to 7.0), but weakly to \(\bar{K}\Sigma\) (0.54 to 0.93) and \(\eta\Xi\) (0.14 to 0.38).
- For the \(\Xi(1690)\), the experimental ratio of branching ratios for \(\bar{K}\Sigma\) to \(\bar{K}\Lambda\) is about 3, and for \(\pi\Xi\) to \(\bar{K}\Sigma\) is less than 0.09, which is opposite to the model's predictions.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach of Kaiser, Waas, and Weise (1995, 1997) and Oset and Ramos (1998) from low-energy \(K^-N\) scattering to higher energies, dynamically generating the full nonet of \(J^P = 1/2^-\) baryon resonances predicted by SU(3) symmetry. It contradicts the assignment of the \(\Xi(1690)\) as the \(S=-2\) member of this octet, which had been suggested by quark model expectations, and instead identifies the \(\Xi(1620)\) as that state, providing a prediction for its spin and parity (\(1/2^-\)) that was not assigned by the Particle Data Group at the time. The work enables future studies of the Flatte effect in meson-baryon systems and the role of chiral symmetry breaking terms encoded in the subtraction constants.
