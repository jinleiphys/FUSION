# Higher order effects in the $^{16}O(d,p)^{17}O$ and $^{16}O(d,n)^{17}F$ transfer reactions

> Full Coupled Channels Calculations were performed for the $^{16}O(d,n)^{17}F$ and $^{16}O(d,p)^{17}O$ transfer reactions at several deuteron incident energies from $E_{lab}=2.29$ MeV up to 3.27 MeV. A strong polarization effect between the entrance channel and the transfer channels $^{16}O(d,n)^{17}F(1/2^{+},0.495)$ and $^{16}O(d,p)^{17}O(1/2^{+},0.87)$ was observed. This polarization effect had to be taken into account in order to obtain realistic spectroscopic factors from these reactions.

# Higher order effects in the $^{16}O(d,p)^{17}O$ and $^{16}O(d,n)^{17}F$ transfer reactions

## Key claim

Standard DWBA calculations fail to reproduce the $(d,n_1)$ and $(d,p_1)$ angular distributions for the $^{16}O(d,n)^{17}F$ and $^{16}O(d,p)^{17}O$ reactions at deuteron energies of 2–3 MeV unless anomalously small spectroscopic factors are used; full Coupled Reaction Channels (CRC) calculations that include polarization between the entrance and transfer channels restore spectroscopic factors close to unity, particularly for the $(d,n_1)$ channel.

## Method

Full Coupled Reaction Channels (CRC) calculations were performed for the $^{16}O(d,n)^{17}F$ and $^{16}O(d,p)^{17}O$ transfer reactions at several deuteron incident energies from $E_{lab}=2.29$ MeV to 3.27 MeV, using the computer code FRESCO. The calculations included transfer couplings in both directions between the entrance channel and the transfer channels populating the ground and first excited states of $^{17}F$ and $^{17}O$, with non-orthogonality corrections included. The deuteron optical potential parameters and spectroscopic amplitudes were adjusted by $\chi^2$ minimization against experimental angular distributions from Dietzsch et al. (1968).

## Key numbers

- Deuteron incident energies studied: $E_{lab}=2.29$ MeV to 3.27 MeV (specifically $E_d=2.279$ MeV to 3.155 MeV for $(d,p)$ and $E_d=2.29$ MeV to 3.186 MeV for $(d,n)$)
- Spectroscopic factor for $\langle^{17}F^*|^{16}O\rangle$ from CRC (Set II): $0.96 \pm 0.12$ (average over four energies)
- Spectroscopic factor for $\langle^{17}O^*|^{16}O\rangle$ from CRC (Set II): $0.69 \pm 0.17$ (average over four energies)
- Spectroscopic factor for $\langle^{17}F_{gs}|^{16}O\rangle$ from CRC (Set II): $0.93 \pm 0.11$
- Spectroscopic factor for $\langle^{17}O_{gs}|^{16}O\rangle$ from CRC (Set II): $1.19 \pm 0.05$
- Compound nucleus contribution for $(d,n_0)$: 2.0 mb/sr at $E_d=2.85$ MeV
- Compound nucleus contribution for $(d,p_0)$: 2.95 mb/sr at $E_d=2.85$ MeV
- r.m.s. radii of $^{17}F$ states: 3.75 fm (ground state) and 5.40 fm (excited state at 0.495 MeV)
- Deuteron optical potential parameters from CRC (Set II): $V_0=104$ MeV, $W_d=24.9$ MeV, $a_i=0.233$ fm

## Context

This work extends the analysis of $^{16}O(d,p)^{17}O$ and $^{16}O(d,n)^{17}F$ reactions originally measured by Dietzsch et al. (1968), showing that the Born approximation is inadequate at these sub-Coulomb energies. The authors demonstrate that CRC calculations, which go beyond the DWBA and CCBA formalisms, are necessary to extract realistic spectroscopic factors. The results support earlier concerns by Satchler (1966) and Ascuitto (1978) about the choice of distorting potentials in DWBA, and are consistent with the findings of Ichimura and Kawai (1983) who also found that both conventional and alternative DWBA prescriptions fail for the $^{16}O(d,p_1)^{17}O$ reaction. The work enables more reliable extraction of spectroscopic factors for astrophysical transfer reactions, particularly for the $(d,n_1)$ channel where a dynamic polarization potential can be constructed to mimic the CRC effects within DWBA.
