# Crossover-model approach to QCD phase diagram, equation of state and susceptibilities in the 2+1 and 2+1+1 flavor systems

> We construct a simple model for describing the hadron-quark crossover transition by using lattice QCD (LQCD) data in the 2+1 flavor system, and draw the phase diagram in the 2+1 and 2+1+1 flavor systems through analyses of the equation of state (EoS) and the susceptibilities. In the present hadron-quark crossover (HQC) model is successful in reproducing LQCD data on the EoS and the flavor susceptibilities.We define the hadron-quark transition temperature. For the 2+1 flavor system, the transition line thus obtained is almost identical in planes that are created by temperature and the chemical potential for the baryon-number(B), the isospin(I), the hypercharge(Y), when the chemical potentials are smaller than 250 MeV. This BIY approximate equivalence persists also in the 2+1+1 flavor system. We plot the phase diagram also in planes that are created by temperature and the chemical potential for u,d,s quark number in order to investigate flavor dependence of transition lines. In the 2+1+1 flavor system, c quark does not affect the 2+1 flavor subsystem composed of u, d, s. The flavor off-diagonal susceptibilities are good indicators to see how hadrons survive as T increases, since the independent quark model hardly contributes to them.

## Key claim

The hadron-quark crossover (HQC) model, which defines the entropy density as a weighted sum of hadron-resonance-gas and independent-quark contributions, reproduces lattice QCD data on the equation of state and flavor susceptibilities for both the 2+1 and 2+1+1 flavor systems, but the model's success depends on adjusting the Polyakov-loop potential parameter \(a_0\) to 2.457 (rather than the pure-gauge value 3.51) to match lattice data at \(T = 400\) MeV, and the transition lines in the \(\mu_B\)-\(T\), \(\mu_I\)-\(T\), and \(\mu_Y\)-\(T\) planes are approximately identical only when the chemical potentials are smaller than 250 MeV.

## Method

The authors constructed the HQC model by defining the total entropy density as \(s = f_{\rm H} s_{\rm H} + (1 - f_{\rm H}) s_{\rm Q}\), where \(s_{\rm H}\) is calculated from the hadron resonance gas model using all hadrons listed in the Particle Data Table and \(s_{\rm Q}\) is evaluated from an independent quark model that includes a Polyakov-loop potential with a retuned parameter \(a_0 = 2.457\). The hadron-production probability \(f_{\rm H}(T, \{\mu_\alpha\})\) was determined from lattice QCD data on the entropy density and the baryon-number, isospin, and hypercharge susceptibilities in the 2+1 flavor system, and then applied without change to the 2+1+1 flavor system.

## Key numbers

- The transition region at zero chemical potential is \(170 \lesssim T \lesssim 400\) MeV for both the 2+1 and 2+1+1 flavor systems, determined from the \(T\) dependence of \(f_{\rm H}\) and the off-diagonal susceptibilities.
- For the 2+1 flavor system, the transition lines in the \(\mu_B\)-\(T\), \(\mu_I\)-\(T\), and \(\mu_Y\)-\(T\) planes are almost identical when the chemical potentials are smaller than 250 MeV.
- The IQ model with \(a_0 = 2.457\) reproduces lattice QCD data on the entropy density in the range \(400 \lesssim T \leq 500\) MeV for the 2+1 flavor system and \(400 \lesssim T \leq 1000\) MeV for the 2+1+1 flavor system.
- The entropy density from lattice QCD is about 80% of the Stefan-Boltzmann limit value at \(T = 500\) MeV for the 2+1 flavor system and at \(T = 1000\) MeV for the 2+1+1 flavor system.

## Context

This work extends the hadron-quark hybrid model approach of Albright (2014) and Miyahara (2016) by using updated lattice QCD data from Borsanyi (2016) and Bellwied (2015) for both the 2+1 and 2+1+1 flavor systems, and by reformulating the independent quark model to remove the momentum cutoff \(\Lambda_{\rm T}\) used previously. The model enables the prediction of the QCD phase diagram in multiple chemical-potential planes and provides a method to identify the hadron-quark transition region using off-diagonal flavor susceptibilities, which are sensitive to hadronic degrees of freedom that the independent quark model cannot reproduce.
