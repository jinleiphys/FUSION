# Lambda-Lambda interaction from relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We investigate the two-particle intensity correlation function of $Λ$ in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. We find that the behavior of the $ΛΛ$ correlation function at small relative momenta is fairly sensitive to the interaction potential and collective flows. By comparing the results of different source functions and potentials, we explore the effect of intrinsic collective motions on the correlation function. We find that the recent STAR data gives a strong constraint on the scattering length and effective range of $ΛΛ$ interaction as, $-1.8 \mathrm{fm}^{-1} < 1/a_0 < -0.8 \mathrm{fm}^{-1}$ and $3.5 \mathrm{fm} < r_\mathrm{eff} < 7 \mathrm{fm}$, respectively,if $Λ$ samples do not include feed-down contribution from long-lived particles. We find that feed-down correction for $Σ^0$ decay reduces the sensitivity of the correlation function to the detail of the $ΛΛ$ interaction. As a result, we obtain a weaker constraint $1/a_0 <-0.8$ fm$^{-1}$. Implication for the signal of existence of $H$-dibaryon is discussed. Comparison with the scattering parameters obtained from the double $Λ$ hypernucleus may reveal in-medium effects in the $ΛΛ$ interaction.

## Key claim

The STAR data on ΛΛ correlation functions can constrain the scattering length and effective range of the ΛΛ interaction, but only if Λ samples do not include feed-down contributions from long-lived particles; when feed-down correction from Σ⁰ decay is included, the constraint weakens to only a lower bound on 1/a₀.

## Method

The authors calculated the two-particle intensity correlation function for ΛΛ pairs using the Koonin-Pratt formalism, solving the Schrödinger equation with various ΛΛ interaction potentials (Nijmegen models D, F, NSC89, NSC97, ESC08, Ehime, fss2, and potentials fitted to double Λ hypernucleus data from Filikhin-Gal and Hiyama-Kamimura-Motoba-Yamada-Yamamoto). They compared the model correlation functions to STAR data from Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV (0-80% centrality) using a static spherically symmetric Gaussian source model, computing χ² as a function of source size R to identify favored potentials.

## Key numbers

- Without feed-down correction: −1.8 fm⁻¹ < 1/a₀ < −0.8 fm⁻¹ and 3.5 fm < r_eff < 7 fm
- With Σ⁰ feed-down correction: 1/a₀ < −0.8 fm⁻¹ (weaker constraint, no upper bound)
- The ΛΛ bond energy in ⁶_ΛΛHe from the Nagara event is ΔB_ΛΛ = 1.01 MeV
- Scattering parameters from double Λ hypernucleus: (a₀, r_eff) = (−0.77 fm, 6.59 fm) from Filikhin-Gal or (−0.575 fm, 6.45 fm) from Hiyama et al.
- χ² analysis used data for 0.01 ≤ Q < 0.5 GeV with N_dof = 24
- Potentials with stable χ² minima (ND56, NF50, fss2, ESC08) have −1.2 fm < a₀ < −0.8 fm and 3.2 fm < r_eff < 6.5 fm

## Context

This work extends the proposal from the 1980s (Greiner and Muller, 1989) that correlation functions in heavy-ion collisions can fix resonance parameters, and the suggestion by Ohnishi et al. that the sign of the scattering length can be discriminated when the source size is large. The authors compare their results to the STAR collaboration's own analysis using the Lednický and Lyuboshitz analytical model, and to scattering parameters extracted from double Λ hypernucleus data (Nagara event). The results enable future J-PARC experiments (E42) to further constrain the ΛΛ interaction and search for the H-dibaryon, and may reveal in-medium effects when compared with hypernuclear data.
