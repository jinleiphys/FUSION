# Shape and pairing fluctuations effects on neutrinoless double beta decay nuclear matrix elements

> Nuclear matrix elements (NME) for the most promising candidates to detect neutrinoless double beta decay have been computed with energy density functional methods including deformation and pairing fluctuations explicitly on the same footing. The method preserves particle number and angular momentum symmetries and can be applied to any decay without additional fine tunings. The finite range density dependent Gogny force is used in the calculations. An increase of $10\%-40\%$ in the NME with respect to the ones found without the inclusion of pairing fluctuations is obtained, reducing the predicted half-lives of these isotopes.

## Key claim

Including pairing fluctuations explicitly alongside shape fluctuations in energy density functional calculations increases the nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double beta decay by 10%–40% compared to calculations that consider only shape fluctuations, thereby reducing the predicted half-lives. The exception is the double-magic nucleus \(^{48}\)Ca, where the NME decreases by 6% because the initial wave function shifts toward weaker pairing correlations.

## Method

The authors used the generator coordinate method (GCM) within the energy density functional (EDF) framework, mixing particle-number and angular-momentum projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) wave functions constrained in both axial quadrupole deformation \(\beta_2\) and pairing fluctuation \(\delta\). The finite-range density-dependent Gogny D1S force was used consistently for both the intrinsic states and the Hill-Wheeler-Griffin diagonalization, with up to 440 intrinsic states per nucleus spanning \(\beta_2 \in [-0.85, 0.95]\) and \(\delta \in [0.5, 6.5]\). The nuclear matrix elements were computed in the closure approximation, including Fermi and Gamow-Teller contributions with the tensor term neglected, using neutrino potentials with finite size, higher order currents, and short range correlations corrections.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{136}\)Xe \(\rightarrow\) \(^{136}\)Ba: NME increases by 14% when pairing fluctuations are included, from \(M^{0\nu}(\beta_2) = 4.199\) to \(M^{0\nu}(\beta_2,\delta) = 4.773\), reducing the half-life by a factor of 0.77.
- For \(^{128}\)Te \(\rightarrow\) \(^{128}\)Xe: NME increases by 38% (from 4.107 to 5.687), the largest relative increase among the candidates, reducing the half-life by a factor of 0.52.
- For \(^{100}\)Mo \(\rightarrow\) \(^{100}\)Ru: NME increases by 30% (from 5.084 to 6.588), reducing the half-life by a factor of 0.60.
- For \(^{150}\)Nd \(\rightarrow\) \(^{150}\)Sm: NME increases by 29% (from 1.707 to 2.190), reducing the half-life by a factor of 0.61.
- For \(^{76}\)Ge \(\rightarrow\) \(^{76}\)Se: NME increases by 21% (from 4.601 to 5.551), reducing the half-life by a factor of 0.69.
- For \(^{48}\)Ca \(\rightarrow\) \(^{48}\)Ti: NME decreases by 6% (from 2.370 to 2.229), increasing the half-life by a factor of 1.13.
- The NMEs are zero for weakly correlated states with \(\delta < 2\) and \(\delta' < 2\), and grow rapidly for larger pairing values within a band \(\delta' \approx \delta \pm 3\).

## Context

This work extends previous EDF calculations of \(0\nu\beta\beta\) nuclear matrix elements that considered only shape fluctuations (Rodriguez and Martinez-Pinedo, 2010; Rodriguez and Martinez-Pinedo, 2013) by adding pairing fluctuations on the same footing for the first time. The results bring the EDF NMEs closer to those from the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) and interacting boson model (IBM) for decays such as \(^{48}\)Ca, \(^{76}\)Ge, \(^{128}\)Te, and \(^{150}\)Nd, while moving away from large-scale shell model (LSSM) values. The work enables more precise predictions of half-lives for ongoing and future experiments (EXO-200, KamLAND-Zen, GERDA) and motivates further study of isospin symmetry restoration and calculations along isotopic chains to resolve remaining discrepancies between methods.
