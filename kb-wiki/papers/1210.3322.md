# Anisotropic pressure in strange quark matter under the presence of a strong magnetic field

> Thermodynamic properties of strange quark matter in strong magnetic fields $H$ up to $10^{20}$ G are considered within the MIT bag model at zero temperature implying the constraints of total baryon number conservation, charge neutrality and chemical equilibrium. The pressure anisotropy, exhibiting in the difference between the pressures along and perpendicular to the field direction, becomes essential at $H>H_{th}$, with the estimate $10^{17}<H_{th}\lesssim10^{18}$ G. The longitudinal pressure vanishes in the critical field $H_c$, which can be somewhat less or larger than $10^{18}$ G, depending on the total baryon number density and bag pressure. As a result, the longitudinal instability occurs in strange quark matter, which precludes: (1) a significant drop in the content of $s$ quarks, which, otherwise, could happen at $H\sim10^{20}$ G; (2) the appearance of positrons in weak processes in a narrow interval near $H\sim2\cdot10^{19}$ G (replacing electrons). The occurrence of the longitudinal instability leaves the possibility only for electrons to reach a fully polarized state, while for all quark flavors the polarization remains mild even for the fields near $H_c$. The anisotropic equation of state is determined under the conditions relevant to the interiors of magnetars.

## Key claim

The pressure anisotropy in strange quark matter becomes essential only above a threshold field \(10^{17} < H_{th} \lesssim 10^{18}\) G, and the longitudinal pressure vanishes at a critical field \(H_c\) that can be somewhat less or larger than \(10^{18}\) G depending on the total baryon number density and bag pressure, which precludes both a significant drop in strange quark content at \(H \sim 10^{20}\) G and the appearance of positrons near \(H \sim 2 \cdot 10^{19}\) G.

## Method

The authors used the MIT bag model at zero temperature to study strange quark matter composed of deconfined \(u\), \(d\), and \(s\) quarks plus electrons in a strong magnetic field directed along the \(z\)-axis. They imposed constraints of total baryon number conservation, charge neutrality, and chemical equilibrium from weak processes, solving for the chemical potentials and number densities of all fermion species. The longitudinal and transverse pressures were computed including the Maxwell term, which was missed in prior work by FMRO, and the critical field \(H_c\) for longitudinal instability was determined.

## Key numbers

- Threshold field for pressure anisotropy: \(H_{th} \approx 5.5 \times 10^{17}\) G at \(\varrho_B = 3\varrho_0\), \(B = 100\) MeV/fm\(^3\); \(H_{th} \approx 9.9 \times 10^{17}\) G at \(\varrho_B = 4\varrho_0\), \(B = 120\) MeV/fm\(^3\).
- Critical field for longitudinal instability: \(H_c \approx 7.4 \times 10^{17}\) G at \(\varrho_B = 3\varrho_0\), \(B = 100\) MeV/fm\(^3\); \(H_c \approx 1.4 \times 10^{18}\) G at \(\varrho_B = 4\varrho_0\), \(B = 120\) MeV/fm\(^3\).
- Electron saturation field for full spin polarization: \(H_s^e \approx 2.2 \times 10^{16}\) G at \(\varrho_B = 3\varrho_0\); \(H_s^e \approx 1.9 \times 10^{16}\) G at \(\varrho_B = 4\varrho_0\).
- Quark spin polarization at \(H = H_c\) for \(\varrho_B = 4\varrho_0\): \(\Pi_u \approx 0.06\), \(\Pi_d \approx -0.03\), \(\Pi_s \approx -0.04\).
- Positron appearance interval: \(1.56 \times 10^{19}\) G to \(1.80 \times 10^{19}\) G at \(\varrho_B = 3\varrho_0\); \(1.86 \times 10^{19}\) G to \(2.21 \times 10^{19}\) G at \(\varrho_B = 4\varrho_0\).

## Context

This work extends studies of magnetized strange quark matter by Chakrabarty (1996), Bandyopadhyay et al. (1997), and others, but corrects the omission of the Maxwell term in the pressure equations by Felipe et al. (2008), which led to the incorrect inequality \(p_l > p_t\) instead of the correct \(p_t > p_l\). The results are relevant for the interiors of magnetars and hybrid stars, and the anisotropic equation of state enables future studies of structural properties and neutrino transport in strongly magnetized compact stars, as well as comparisons with neutron matter calculations using the Skyrme BSk20 interaction by Isayev and Yang (2011).
