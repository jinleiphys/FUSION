# Unitary limit and linear scaling of neutrons in harmonic trap with tuned CD-Bonn and square-well interactions

> We study systems of finite-number neutrons in a harmonic trap at the unitary limit. Two very different types of neutron-neutron interactions are applied, namely, the meson-theoretic CD-Bonn potential and hard-core square-well interactions, all tuned to possess infinite scattering lengths, and with effective ranges comparable to or larger than the trap size. The potentials are renormalized to equivalent, scattering-length preserving low-momentum potentials, $V_{{\rm low}-k}$, with which the particle-particle hole-hole ring diagrams are summed to all orders to yield the ground-state energy $E_0$ of the finite neutron system. We find the ratio $E_0/E_0^{\rm free}$ (where $E_0^{\rm free}$ denotes the ground-state energy of the corresponding non-interacting system) to be remarkably independent from variations of the harmonic trap parameter, the number of neutrons, the decimation momentum of $V_{{\rm low}-k}$, and the type and effective range of the unitarity potential. Our results support a special virial linear scaling relation of $E_0$. Certain properties of Landau's quasi-particles for trapped neutrons at the unitary limit are also discussed.

# Unitary limit and linear scaling of neutrons in harmonic trap with tuned CD-Bonn and square-well interactions

## Key claim
The ratio E0/E0^free for trapped unitary neutrons with effective ranges comparable to or larger than the trap size is remarkably invariant across variations of trap parameter, neutron number, decimation momentum, and potential type/range, supporting a linear scaling relation E0 = αℏω that holds even when the effective range is finite rather than zero as assumed in the original virial theorem.

## Method
The authors employed two distinct neutron-neutron interactions, the meson-theoretic CD-Bonn potential (tuned by adjusting its σ meson mass by 2.4% to achieve a scattering length of −12070 fm) and hard-core square-well (HCSW) potentials with five parameter sets, all tuned to infinite scattering length. These potentials were renormalized to scattering-length-preserving low-momentum potentials Vlow−k, then particle-particle hole-hole (pphh) ring diagrams were summed to all orders via an RPA equation to obtain the ground-state energy E0 for closed-shell systems of 20, 40, and 70 neutrons in a harmonic trap. Open-shell systems (A=26, 36, 46) were treated using a Q-box folded-diagram method with shell-model diagonalization.

## Key numbers
- CD-Bonn potential tuned to ¹S0 scattering length a_s = −12070 fm (compared to the original −18.97 fm)
- Trap ratio R_t = E0/E0^free for 40 neutrons with CD-Bonn at ℏω=10.5 MeV and Λ=2.0 fm⁻¹: R_t^(all) = 0.754 (all-order pphh sum)
- Trap ratio R_t for 70 neutrons with CD-Bonn at ℏω=10.5 MeV and Λ=2.0 fm⁻¹: R_t^(all) = 0.751
- Intrinsic ratio R_i for 70 neutrons with CD-Bonn at ℏω=10.5 MeV: R_i^(all) = 0.500
- Effective ranges r_e of HCSW potentials: 2.63 fm (HCSW-1), 2.36 fm (HCSW-2), 2.22 fm (HCSW-3), 2.20 fm (HCSW-4), 1.80 fm (HCSW-5); CD-Bonn r_e = 2.54 fm
- Trap sizes a_h.o. ranged from 1.72 fm (ℏω=14.0 MeV) to 2.35 fm (ℏω=7.5 MeV)
- All R_t values across all potentials, ℏω values (7.5–14.0 MeV), Λ values (1.8–2.3 fm⁻¹), and neutron numbers (40, 70) fell within 0.748–0.762

## Context
This work extends studies of unitary Fermi gases in harmonic traps, previously focused on zero-range interactions where trap size greatly exceeds effective range (Werner and Castin 2006; Chang and Blume 2007; Blume and Daily 2007; Yin and Blume 2015), to the regime where effective ranges are comparable to or larger than the trap size. The authors' earlier work on infinite unitary neutron matter (Siu and Kuo 2008; Dong and Kuo 2010) found a universal ξ ≈ 0.44 using the same pphh ring-diagram method. The present results show that the linear scaling relation E0 = αℏω, derived from the virial theorem for zero-range interactions, also holds for finite-range interactions, and the universal ratio R_t ≈ 0.75 suggests a sum rule for Landau quasi-particles. This enables future studies of trapped unitary neutrons using Fermi liquid theory and may inform neutron star calculations (Kievsky and Gattobigio 2018).
