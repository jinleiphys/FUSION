# Efimov three-body states on top of a Fermi sea

> The stabilization of Cooper pairs of bound electrons in the background of a Fermi sea is the origin of superconductivity and the paradigmatic example of the striking influence of many-body physics on few-body properties. In the quantum-mechanical three-body problem the famous Efimov effect yields unexpected scaling relations among a tower of universal states. These seemingly unrelated problems can now be studied in the same setup thanks to the success of ultracold atomic gas experiments. In light of the tremendous effect of a background Fermi sea on two-body properties, a natural question is whether a background can modify or even destroy the Efimov effect. Here we demonstrate how the generic problem of three interacting particles changes when one particle is embedded in a background Fermi sea, and show that Efimov scaling persists. It is found in a scaling that relates the three-body physics to the background density of fermionic particles.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the Efimov effect, the existence of an infinite geometric scaling tower of three-body bound states, persists when one of the three identical particles is embedded in a background Fermi sea, but the scaling is modified by a new relation that connects the three-body physics to the background fermion density. The claim is conditional on the assumption of an inert Fermi sea with no particle-hole excitations, and the authors caution that corrections from particle-hole fluctuations may shift absolute threshold positions while preserving the scaling law.

## Method

The authors solve the momentum-space Skorniakov–ter-Martirosian (STM) integral equations for three equal-mass particles with zero-range interactions, modified to include Pauli blocking by a Fermi sea in one component. The dimer propagator is dressed by the Fermi sea via a pair bubble integral with a lower momentum cutoff at the Fermi wave number \(k_{\rm{F}}\), and the bound-state equations are solved using a pole expansion to extract trimer energies. The three-body parameter \(k^*\) sets the scale of the vacuum Efimov spectrum, and the Fermi wave number \(k_{\rm{F}}\) is varied to map the flow of trimer thresholds.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum Efimov scaling factor is \(e^{\pi/s_0} \sim 22.7\), where \(s_0 = 1.00624\) for equal mass particles, corresponding to an energy ratio of \(22.7^2 \sim 515\) between successive trimer states.
- For a Fermi sea in one component, the critical scattering length for dimer formation at zero energy shifts to \(1/a_{ij}^c = 0.24 k_{\rm{F}}\) in the equal-mass case.
- The universal relation for the Fermi wave numbers at which trimer thresholds cross resonance is \(k_{\rm{F}}^{(n)} = \alpha e^{n\pi/s_0} k^*\), where \(\alpha\) is a non-universal constant.
- For the \(^6\)Li three-component system, the three-body parameter is \(k^* = 6.9(2) \times 10^{-3} a_0^{-1}\) (with \(a_0 = 5.29 \times 10^{-11}\) m), and the conversion to density is \(k_{\rm{F}}/k^* = 3 \times 10^{-6} (n/\text{cm}^{-3})^{1/3}\).
- To observe a 40 Gauss shift of the second trimer loss peak in \(^6\)Li, densities of approximately \(5 \times 10^{12}\) atoms/cm\(^3\) are required, with possible increase by an order of magnitude due to uncertainties in \(k^*\).

## Context

This work extends the classic Efimov three-body problem (Efimov 1970) into the many-body regime, motivated by the Cooper pair phenomenon (Cooper 1956) where a Fermi sea stabilizes bound pairs. It builds on prior studies of Pauli blocking in three-body systems using the Born-Oppenheimer limit (Nishida 2009, MacNeill and Zhou 2011) and variational wave functions (Mathy et al. 2011), but provides a general STM framework valid for any masses and interaction strengths. The results enable experimental searches for modified Efimov signatures in ultracold Fermi gases, particularly in heteronuclear mixtures where the scaling factor is smaller and in the three-component \(^6\)Li system at higher densities than previously explored.
