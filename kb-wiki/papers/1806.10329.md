# Enhancement of strange baryons in high-multiplicity proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions

> We investigate the enhancement of yields of strange and multi-strange baryons in proton-proton (p+p), proton-lead (p+Pb) and lead-lead (Pb+Pb) collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) energies from a dynamical core-corona initialization model. We first generate partons just after the collisions by using event generators. These partons dynamically generate the quark gluon plasma (QGP) fluids through the source terms in the hydrodynamic equations. According to the core-corona picture, this process tends to happen where the density of generated partons is high and their transverse momentum is low. Some partons do not fully participate in this process when they are in dilute regions or their transverse momentum is high and subsequently fragment into hadrons through string fragmentation. In this framework, the final hadrons come from either chemically equilibrated fluids as in the conventional hydrodynamic models or string fragmentation. We calculate the ratio of strange baryons to charged pions as a function of multiplicity and find that it monotonically increases up to $dN_{\mathrm{ch}}/dη\sim 100$ and then saturates above. This suggests that the QGP fluids are \textit{partly} created and that their fraction increases with multiplicity in p+p and p+Pb collisions at LHC energies.

## Key claim

The ratio of strange baryons to charged pions increases monotonically with charged-particle multiplicity up to dNch/dη ~ 100 and then saturates above that value, suggesting that quark-gluon plasma fluids are *partly* created in high-multiplicity p+p and p+Pb collisions and that their fraction increases with multiplicity, rather than the size of the colliding systems being the controlling factor.

## Method

The authors developed a dynamical core-corona initialization model that couples partons generated by PYTHIA 8.230 to (3+1)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamic equations with source terms. Partons in high-density regions deposit their energy and momentum into chemically equilibrated QGP fluids via a parametrized energy-momentum deposition rate proportional to the local parton density and inversely proportional to the square of the parton transverse momentum, while partons in dilute regions or with high transverse momentum undergo string fragmentation in PYTHIA. The hydrodynamic fields are initialized dynamically from formation time τ00 = 0.1 fm to hydrodynamic initial time τ0 = 0.6 fm, after which the fluid expands and hadronizes via the Cooper-Frye formula at decoupling temperature Tdec = 160 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The ratio NΞ/Nπ increases from the fragmentation limit of ~0.002 at dNch/dη ~ 1 to the chemically equilibrated fluid limit of ~0.005 at dNch/dη ~ 100 and above.
- The ratio NΛ/Nπ increases from the fragmentation limit of ~0.025 at low multiplicity to the chemically equilibrated fluid limit of ~0.04 at dNch/dη ~ 100 and above.
- The fraction of fluidized energy saturates above dNch/dη ~ 50-100 regardless of collision system or energy.
- The model parameters used: a0 = 100, σ⊥ = 0.5 fm, σηs = 0.5, τ00 = 0.1 fm, τ0 = 0.6 fm, Tdec = 160 MeV.
- Resonance correction factors: cπ = 3.2 for pions, cΛ = 4.7 for lambdas, cΞ = 1.7 for cascades.
- Collision energies: p+p at √s = 7 TeV, p+Pb at √sNN = 5.02 TeV, Pb+Pb at √sNN = 2.76 TeV.

## Context

This work extends the core-corona picture previously applied by Werner (2007), Aichelin (2008), Becattini (2008), and others to a dynamical initialization framework that treats soft and hard physics in a unified manner. The results reproduce the ALICE Collaboration data (2017) showing that the ratio of multi-strange hadrons to charged pions increases with multiplicity and saturates above dNch/dη ~ 100, supporting the interpretation that QGP fluids are partially created in high-multiplicity small collision systems. This enables future studies of flow observables such as two-particle correlation functions and anisotropic flow parameters, as well as investigation of the ratio of φ mesons to pions to discriminate between core-corona effects and canonical strangeness suppression.
