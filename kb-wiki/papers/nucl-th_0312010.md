# Taylor expansions in chemical potential

> Properties of QCD at finite chemical potential are extracted using Taylor series expansions. The continuum limits of lattice results are presented. The result of expanding the free energy density, ie, the pressure, to 6th order in the expansion is shown. The Taylor coefficients of the chiral condensate are also shown. Relations between various Taylor coefficients are demonstrated. All this information is utilised to remove various lattice artifacts from the determination of the Wroblewski parameter in strangeness production.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that high-order Taylor expansions of the QCD free energy (pressure) in baryon chemical potential, combined with continuum extrapolations of lattice data, can reliably extract finite-density physics; however, the method is limited up to the nearest phase boundary from μ=0, and the continuum limit is crucial because lattice artifacts from the ambiguity in defining chemical potential on the lattice can shift estimates of the critical end point by amounts much larger than present statistical errors.

## Method

The authors perform lattice QCD simulations with two flavors of dynamical staggered quarks and compute the diagonal generalized susceptibilities χ_{uu}, χ_{uuuu}, and χ_{uuuuuu} (the Taylor coefficients of the pressure) at zero chemical potential, then extrapolate these coefficients to the continuum limit. They also compute the Taylor coefficients of the chiral condensate and use Maxwell relations and chiral Ward identities to connect these coefficients to the quark mass dependence of the Wroblewski parameter λ_s for strangeness production.

## Key numbers

- The continuum limits of diagonal susceptibilities are presented at T=2T_c, with χ_{uuuuuu} vanishing at the 99% confidence level.
- The SPS heavy-ion collision corresponds to μ/T_c=0.45 and RHIC to μ/T_c=0.15.
- The renormalized second derivative of the quark condensate, c_2^r, is consistent with zero at the 99% confidence level at T=1.5T_c, 2T_c, and 3T_c.
- The ratio χ_{uu}(cont)/χ_{uu}(N_t=4) is used to rescale lattice results to the continuum.

## Context

This work extends the Taylor expansion method for the QCD pressure first presented by Gottlieb et al. (1997) and for meson correlators by Miyamura et al. (2003), and provides a systematic framework for computing continuum limits of finite-density observables. The results enable reliable extraction of the Wroblewski parameter for strangeness production in heavy-ion collisions and establish that at high temperature the linear susceptibility χ_{uu} alone suffices to determine the continuum pressure at chemical potentials relevant to experiments, while also showing that the quark condensate and pseudo-scalar susceptibility are independent of μ to quadratic order, consistent with the argument by Cohen (2003).
