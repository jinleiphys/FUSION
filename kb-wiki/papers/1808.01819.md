# Cooling of neutron stars in "nuclear medium cooling scenario" with stiff equation of state including hyperons

> We demonstrate that the existing neutron-star cooling data can be appropriately described within "the nuclear medium cooling scenario" including hyperons under the assumption that different sources have different masses. We use a stiff equation of state of the relativistic mean-field model MKVORH$ϕ$ with hadron effective couplings and masses dependent on the scalar field. It fulfills a large number of experimental constraints on the equation of state of the nuclear matter including the $2\,M_{\odot}$ lower bound for the maximum predicted neutron-star mass and the constraint for the pressure from the heavy-ion particle flow. We select appropriate $^1S_0$ proton and $Λ$ hyperon pairing gap profiles from those exploited in the literature and allow for a variation of the effective pion gap controlling the efficiency of the medium modified Urca process. The $^3P_2$ neutron pairing gap is assumed to be negligibly small in our scenario. The possibility of the pion, kaon and charged $ρ$-meson condensations is for simplicity suppressed. The resulting cooling curves prove to be sensitive to the value and the density dependence of the pp pairing gap and rather insensitive to the values of the $^1S_0$ neutron pairing gaps.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the existing neutron-star cooling data can be appropriately described within the "nuclear medium cooling scenario" including hyperons, under the assumption that different sources have different masses, using a stiff equation of state that satisfies the 2 solar mass lower bound for the maximum predicted neutron-star mass.

## Method

The authors employ the relativistic mean-field model MKVORHφ with hadron effective couplings and masses dependent on the scalar field, including hyperons, and select appropriate ¹S₀ proton and Λ hyperon pairing gap profiles from the literature while assuming the ³P₂ neutron pairing gap is negligibly small. They allow for variation of the effective pion gap controlling the efficiency of the medium modified Urca process, and suppress the possibility of pion, kaon and charged ρ-meson condensations for simplicity. The resulting cooling curves are computed for neutron stars of different masses and compared to observational surface temperature-age data.

## Key numbers

- The MKVORHφ model yields a maximum neutron star mass compatible with the measured mass M = 2.01 ± 0.04 M⊙ of pulsar PSR J0348+0432 (Antoniadis 2013)
- Λ hyperons appear first at n = 2.621 n₀, where n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ is the nucleon saturation density
- Ξ⁻ hyperons arise at n = 2.929 n₀
- The central density exceeds 2.621 n₀ for M > 1.426 M⊙
- The MKVOR model without hyperons yields maximum NS mass of 2.01 M⊙
- The DU processes on nucleons exist only in NSs with masses M > M_c^DU, where the proton concentration in npeμ matter must be above 11.1-14.8%
- The MMU emissivity at n = n_c^PU may exceed the MU emissivity calculated with the FOPE model by 3-4 orders of magnitude

## Context

This work extends the nuclear medium cooling scenario previously developed by Voskresensky (1985-2001), Blaschke (2004), and Grigorian (2005, 2016) to include hyperons with a stiff equation of state, addressing the "hyperon puzzle" where hyperon appearance typically softens the EoS and reduces maximum NS mass below 2 M⊙. The MKVORHφ model solves this puzzle through σ-scaled hadron effective masses and coupling constants (Maslov 2015), and the cooling calculations demonstrate that the existing pulsar cooling data can be explained without requiring direct Urca processes in the majority of neutron stars, in contrast to the minimal cooling paradigm of Page (2004, 2009) which struggles to explain objects like XMMU J173203.3-344518.
