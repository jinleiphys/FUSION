# Tricritical phenomena in holographic chiral phase transitions

> We study critical phenomena at a tricritical point associated with a chiral phase transition which emerges in the D3/D7 model in the presence of a finite baryon number density and an external magnetic field. We numerically determine critical exponents related to the thermodynamic quantities and correlation functions. We find that the values of the critical exponents agree with the mean-field values. The scaling relations between the critical exponents are satisfied, implying that the scaling hypothesis for the free energy and the correlation functions hold. Our results indicate that the critical phenomena at the tricritical point in the D3/D7 model are well described by the conventional Landau theory.

## Key claim

The critical exponents at the tricritical point in the D3/D7 model agree with mean-field values, and the scaling relations between them are satisfied, indicating that the critical phenomena are well described by conventional Landau theory, but this result holds specifically for the equilibrium regime, in contrast to the non-equilibrium steady state studied previously by the same author (Matsumoto 2022), where some exponents deviated from mean-field values.

## Method

The author numerically determines critical exponents associated with thermodynamic quantities and correlation functions at the tricritical point that emerges in the D3/D7 holographic model with finite baryon number density and an external magnetic field. The analysis follows the scaling theory of tricritical points as presented in Domb (1984), distinguishing between paths approaching the tricritical point that are asymptotically tangential to the phase boundary and those that are not. The author computes the order parameter, chiral susceptibility, specific heat, density, and correlation functions, then extracts the critical exponents and checks the scaling relations (Rushbrooke, Widom, Griffiths, Fisher) for consistency.

## Key numbers

- Order parameter exponent along the triple line (tangential path): β = 1/2
- Order parameter exponent along t = 0 (non-tangential path): β_t = 1/4
- Chiral susceptibility exponent along the triple line: γ = 2
- Chiral susceptibility exponent along t = 0: γ_t = 1
- Specific heat exponent along the triple line: α = -1
- Specific heat exponent along t = 0: α_t = 1/2
- Order parameter exponent at the TCP (with respect to source h): δ = 5
- Density exponent along the triple line: β_2 = 1
- Density exponent along t = 0: δ_2 = 2
- Correlation length exponent along the triple line: ν = 1
- Correlation length exponent along t = 0: ν_t = 1/2
- Anomalous dimension for order parameter correlation function: η = 0
- Anomalous dimension for density correlation function: η_2 = 1

## Context

This work extends the study of chiral phase transitions in the D3/D7 model, where a tricritical point was previously identified by Evans (2010), by providing the first numerical determination of all critical exponents and verifying the scaling relations. The results confirm that the equilibrium tricritical phenomena in this holographic system belong to the mean-field universality class, consistent with conventional Landau theory. This contrasts with the author's earlier work (Matsumoto 2022) on the non-equilibrium steady state in the same model, where the critical exponents γ and ν in the chiral symmetry broken phase deviated from mean-field values, enabling future comparisons between equilibrium and non-equilibrium tricritical behavior in holographic systems.
