# Anomalous Transport Processes in Turbulent non-Abelian Plasmas

> Turbulent color fields, which can arise in the early and late stages of relativistic heavy ion collisions, may contribute significantly to the transport processes in the matter created in these collisions. We review the theory of these anomalous transport processes and discuss their possible phenomenology in the glasma and quasistationary expanding quark-gluon plasma.

# Anomalous Transport Processes in Turbulent non-Abelian Plasmas

## Key claim
Turbulent color fields arising from plasma instabilities may dominate the transport properties of the quark-gluon plasma at weak and moderately weak coupling, potentially explaining the extreme opaqueness observed in RHIC experiments without invoking super-strong coupling. The authors argue that anomalous transport coefficients from these fields can dominate over collisional transport for a fixed velocity gradient in the weak coupling limit, but the transition between regimes depends on numerical constants missing from the scaling relations.

## Method
The authors derive anomalous transport coefficients from a Vlasov-Boltzmann equation for the phase space distribution of particles, assuming random chromo-electric and chromo-magnetic fields. They transform this equation into a Fokker-Planck equation describing momentum diffusion, where the diffusion coefficient is given by the time integral over the force correlation function along a plasma particle's trajectory. They then apply this formalism to estimate the anomalous shear viscosity and jet quenching parameter in two regimes: the quasistationary expanding quark-gluon plasma and the glasma phase.

## Key numbers
- The nuclear saturation scale: Q_s ~ 1-2 GeV
- For the quasithermal medium, the anomalous shear viscosity to entropy density ratio scales as: η_A/s ~ (T / (g^3 |∇u|))^ν, where ν ≈ 1/2
- The collisional shear viscosity to entropy density ratio scales as: η_C/s ~ 1/(g^4 ln g^{-1})
- An estimate for the anomalous jet quenching parameter in the glasma: hat{q}(τ) ≈ Q_s^3 / (Q_s τ) ≈ 10 GeV^2/fm / (Q_s τ)
- The relationship between anomalous shear viscosity and jet quenching parameter: η_A/s ≈ c T^3 / hat{q}_A, where c is a constant of order unity

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous theoretical framework (Asakawa 2006) on anomalous transport in non-Abelian plasmas, building on the color glass condensate picture of McLerran (2008) and lattice QCD results of Cheng (2009). The paper reviews how Weibel-type instabilities (Weibel 1959, Mrowczynski 1996) and Nielsen-Olesen instabilities (Nielsen 1978, Fujii 2008, Iwazaki 2008) generate turbulent color fields in expanding quark-gluon plasmas and glasma flux tubes. The results enable future numerical simulations of momentum broadening (Dumitru 2007, Schenke 2010) and nonlinear gauge field dynamics (Krasnitz 2001, Lappi 2003) to provide more accurate determinations of the momentum diffusion constant in the glasma phase.
