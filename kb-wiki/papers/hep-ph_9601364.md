# Solving the Bethe-Salpeter Equation in Minkowski Space: Scalar Theories and Beyond

> The Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) for bound states in scalar theories is reformulated and solved in terms of a generalized spectral representation directly in Minkowski space. This differs from the conventional approach, where the BSE is solved in Euclidean space after a Wick rotation. For all but the lowest-order (i.e., ladder) approximation to the scattering kernel, the {\it naive} Wick rotation is invalid. Our approach generates the vertex function and Bethe-Salpeter amplitude for the entire allowed range of momenta, whereas these cannot be directly obtained from the Euclidean space solution. Our method is quite general and can be applied even in cases where the Wick rotation is not possible.

## Key claim

The authors reformulate and solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) for bound states in scalar theories directly in Minkowski space using a generalized spectral representation, bypassing the Wick rotation which is invalid for all but the lowest-order (ladder) approximation to the scattering kernel.

## Method

The method uses the Perturbation Theoretic Integral Representation (PTIR) of Nakanishi to convert the BSE, which is an integral equation involving complex distributions, into a real integral equation for the weight functions of the bound-state vertex and Bethe-Salpeter amplitude. The resulting equations are solved numerically by iteration as eigenvalue equations for the coupling parameter lambda, defined as lambda = g^2/(4pi)^2. The approach works for arbitrary scattering kernels and can include nonperturbative propagators via their spectral representation.

## Key numbers

- For the pure ladder kernel with m_sigma/m = 0.5, the eigenvalue lambda_M (Minkowski space solution) at eta = 0 (zero binding, M = 0) is 2.56615, compared to the Wick-rotated value lambda_E = 2.5658 from Linden and Mitter
- At eta = 0.9 (bound state mass M = 1.8m), lambda_M = 1.03497 versus lambda_E = 1.0349
- At eta = 0.99, lambda_M = 0.51684 versus lambda_E = 0.5167
- At eta = 0.999, lambda_M = 0.38530 versus lambda_E = 0.3852
- At eta = 1 (threshold, M = 2m), lambda_E = 0.3296 (no Minkowski value given)
- Vertex solutions obtained for n=2 have accuracy of approximately 1 part in 10^4

## Context

This work extends the Euclidean space treatment of the scalar-scalar BSE in the ladder approximation by Linden and Mitter (1990s), with which the Minkowski space results agree exactly. The method is based on Nakanishi's PTIR formalism and enables direct calculation of Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes and vertex functions for the entire allowed range of momenta, including timelike momenta that cannot be unambiguously obtained from Euclidean space solutions. The approach enables future systematic studies of non-ladder kernels, orbital excitations, and the inclusion of dressed propagators, and may be extended to spinor-spinor and scalar-spinor BSEs for more realistic scattering kernels in QCD.
