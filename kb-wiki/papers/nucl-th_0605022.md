# Scaling of Anisotropic Flow and Momentum-Space Densities for Light Particles in Intermediate Energy Heavy Ion Collisions

> Anisotropic flows ($v_2$ and $v_4$) of light nuclear clusters are studied by Isospin-Dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics model for the system of $^{86}$Kr + $^{124}$Sn at intermediate energy and large impact parameters. Number-of-nucleon scaling of the elliptic flow ($v_2$) are demonstrated for the light fragments up to $A$ = 4, and the ratio of $v_4/v_2^2$ shows a constant value of 1/2. In addition, the momentum-space densities of different clusters are also surveyed as functions of transverse momentum, in-plane transverse momentum and azimuth angle relative to the reaction plane. The results can be essentially described by momentum-space power law. All the above phenomena indicate that there exists a number-of-nucleon scaling for both anisotropic flow and momentum-space densities for light clusters, which can be understood by the coalescence mechanism in nucleonic degree of freedom for the cluster formation.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that number-of-nucleon scaling of elliptic flow ($v_2$) holds for light fragments up to mass number $A=4$ in intermediate-energy heavy ion collisions, and that the ratio $v_4/v_2^2$ is approximately constant at 1/2, but this scaling is a nucleonic-level phenomenon (driven by coalescence) rather than the partonic-level scaling seen at RHIC energies.

## Method

The authors used the Isospin-Dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IDQMD) model to simulate $^{86}$Kr + $^{124}$Sn collisions at 25 MeV/nucleon with impact parameters of 7-10 fm, accumulating 50,000 events. Nuclear clusters were identified using a coalescence criterion: nucleons within 3.5 fm in coordinate space and 300 MeV/c in momentum space. Anisotropic flow coefficients $v_2$ and $v_4$ were extracted from the azimuthal Fourier expansion of particle distributions, and momentum-space densities were examined as functions of transverse momentum, in-plane transverse momentum, and azimuthal angle relative to the reaction plane.

## Key numbers

- System: $^{86}$Kr + $^{124}$Sn at 25 MeV/nucleon, impact parameter range 7-10 fm
- Number-of-nucleon scaling of $v_2/A$ vs $p_t/A$ holds for light fragments up to $A=4$ when $p_t/A < 0.25$ GeV/$c$
- The ratio $v_4/v_2^2$ shows a constant value of 1/2 for all studied particles (neutrons, protons, $A=2$, $A=3$, $A=4$ fragments)
- Coalescence parameters: $r_{min} \leq 3.5$ fm in coordinate space, $p_{min} \leq 300$ MeV/c in momentum space
- The nuclear mean field uses a hard equation of state with incompressibility $K = 380$ MeV, with parameters $\alpha = -124$ MeV, $\beta = 70.5$ MeV, $\gamma = 2.0$, and symmetry energy $C_{sym} = 32$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the concept of number-of-nucleon scaling from directed flow studies (Huang, Kunde) to elliptic flow and fourth-order anisotropy in the intermediate energy domain, where such systematic theoretical studies were previously rare. The results parallel the number-of-constituent-quark (NCQ) scaling observed at RHIC (J. Adams, 2004) but operate at the nucleonic rather than partonic level. The momentum-space power law behavior, previously demonstrated experimentally for beam energies between 0.1 A and 15 A GeV (H.H. Gutbrod), is here tested and confirmed at 25 MeV/nucleon. The findings enable future experimental measurements of $v_4/v_2^2$ in intermediate-energy heavy ion collisions to test whether the predicted value of 1/2 holds experimentally.
