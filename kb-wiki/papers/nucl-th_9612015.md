# Microscopic origins of effective charges in the shell model

> We use a large-scale 6 hbar Omega calculation for Li6 with microscopically derived two-body interaction to construct the 0 hbar Omega 0p-shell effective hamiltonian, electric quadrupole, and magnetic dipole operators. While the E2 and M1 6 hbar Omega operators are one-body operators with free nucleon charges, the effective operators are two-body operators with substantially different renormalization for the isoscalar and isovector matrix elements, especially for the E2 operator. We show that these operators can be very well approximated by one-body operators provided that effective proton and neutron charges are used. The obtained effective charges are compatible with those used in phenomenological shell-model studies. The two-body part of the effective operators is estimated.

# Microscopic origins of effective charges in the shell model

## Key claim

Model-space truncation alone is sufficient to generate the renormalization of electromagnetic operators that yields effective proton and neutron charges compatible with phenomenological shell-model values, without invoking non-nucleonic degrees of freedom. The isoscalar and isovector parts of the E2 operator are renormalized very differently, which is the source of a non-zero neutron effective charge.

## Method

The authors performed a no-core shell-model calculation for ⁶Li in a 6ħΩ model space using a microscopically derived two-body interaction from the Reid 93 nucleon-nucleon potential. They then constructed a 0ħΩ 0p-shell effective Hamiltonian and effective E2 and M1 operators via the Lee-Suzuki similarity transformation method, using the known full-space eigenvectors to determine the transformation operator ω. The effective operators were extracted in the m-scheme and transformed to the J,T basis, and effective charges were obtained by least-square fits to reproduce the reduced matrix elements of the effective operators.

## Key numbers

- Effective proton charge for E2: e^p_eff = 1.527 e (j-independent, from 6ħΩ calculation)
- Effective neutron charge for E2: e^n_eff = 0.364 e (j-independent, from 6ħΩ calculation)
- Effective proton orbital g-factor: g^p_l_eff = 0.907 (6% quenching from free value of 1)
- Effective neutron orbital g-factor: g^n_l_eff = 0.085 (generated from zero)
- Effective proton spin g-factor: g^s_p_eff = 0.937 g^p_s + 0.001 g^n_s (about 6% quenching)
- Two-body content of effective operators (j-independent charges): 12.3% for E2, 5.3% for Ml, 4.4% for Ms
- Two-body content of effective operators (j-dependent charges): 10.1% for E2, 3.8% for Ml, 3.2% for Ms
- Effective E2 charges from 4ħΩ calculation: e^p_eff-4 = 1.302 e, e^n_eff-4 = 0.244 e

## Context

This work extends the no-core shell-model approach of Zheng, Vary, and Barrett (1994) and Zheng et al. (1995) by using the full-space eigenvectors to construct effective operators in a severely truncated model space. It addresses the long-standing problem of deriving effective charges microscopically, which earlier perturbation approaches (Ellis and Osnes, 1977) and expanded shell-model methods (Federman and Pittel, 1978) had only partially succeeded in doing. The results enable systematic studies of how space truncation alone renormalizes electromagnetic operators, and provide a benchmark for comparing the convergence rates of effective Hamiltonians versus effective operators as the full-space size increases.
