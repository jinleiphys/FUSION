# Pion correlations in Nuclear Matter

> The saturation properties of the nuclear matter taking pion correlations into account is studied. We construct a Bogoliubov transformations for the pion pair operators and calculate the energy associated with the pion pairs. The pion dispersion relation is investigated. We next study the correlation energy due to one pion exchange in nuclear matter and neutron matter at random phase approximation using the generator coordinate method. The techniques of the charged pion correlations are discussed in the neutron matter calculations. We observe that there is no sign of the pion condensation in this model.

## Key claim

The paper claims that, within a model that replaces classical meson fields with quantum coherent states for pion pairs and treats one-pion-exchange correlations at the random phase approximation (RPA) level using the generator coordinate method, there is no sign of pion condensation in nuclear matter or neutron matter, even at higher densities.

## Method

The authors derive a non-relativistic pion-nucleon Hamiltonian and construct a Bogoliubov transformation for pion pair operators to define squeezed coherent states that simulate the scalar-isoscalar attraction usually attributed to the sigma meson. They then apply the generator coordinate method to bosonize the particle-hole excitations and diagonalize the Hamiltonian via a second Bogoliubov transformation, obtaining RPA correlation energies for both symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter. The model parameters (a repulsion strength A, a length scale R_pi, and an omega-meson repulsion parameter lambda_omega) are fixed self-consistently to reproduce the saturation density rho_0 = 0.15 fm^{-3} and binding energy per nucleon of -16 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: rho_0 = 0.15 fm^{-3}
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: -16 MeV
- Nucleon mass M = 940 MeV, pion mass m = 140 MeV, omega meson mass m_omega = 783 MeV
- Pion-nucleon coupling constant: G^2/4pi = 14.6
- Fitted parameters: A = 14.58 MeV, R_pi = 1.45 fm, lambda_omega = 3.07 fm^2
- Spin degeneracy factor gamma = 4 for nuclear matter, gamma = 2 for neutron matter

## Context

This work extends the mean-field approach of Walecka (1974) and Serot (1986), where classical meson fields are replaced by quantum coherent states for pion pairs, following earlier developments by Mishra (1990, 1992), Panda (1992, 1996), and Sarangi (2008). The generator coordinate method treatment of one-pion-exchange correlations at RPA level goes beyond the simpler coherent-state description used by Providencia (1977) for the pion condensation problem. The absence of pion condensation in the RPA modes suggests that alternative mechanisms beyond one-pion exchange may be needed to produce such a phase transition, and the formalism enables future studies of nuclear matter and neutron matter equations of state with explicit pion dynamics.
