# Selecting specific initial configuration using spectator neutrons in U+U collisions

> We present a method to select events with specific initial configuration, namely body-tip, in heavy-ion collisions using deformed Uranium nuclei. We propose to use asymmetry in spectator neutron numbers to filter out these body-tip events from the unbiased configurations in U+U collisions. We have used a variable S_eta to differentiate between the body-tip and unbiased configurations. We have calculated the second order azimuthal anisotropy, namely elliptic flow (v2), for this body-tip configuration in the framework of a transport model and found it to be consistently lower compared to that in unbiased configurations as we expected. The purity of selecting such events in a real experiment is also discussed.

# Selecting specific initial configuration using spectator neutrons in U+U collisions

## Key claim

The authors propose that asymmetry in spectator neutron numbers, measurable via Zero Degree Calorimeters (ZDC), can select body-tip collision events from unbiased U+U collisions at 0-5% centrality, but the purity of such selection depends strongly on the chosen slope parameter and angular relaxation, ranging from 24% to 74% depending on cuts.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT model (string melting mode, version 2.25t7) with a parton-parton cross section of 10 mb to generate 0.6 million 0-5% central U+U events at √sNN = 193 GeV. Uranium nuclei were implemented with a deformed Woods-Saxon profile using deformation parameters β2 and β4 from prior work. Spectator neutron energy deposited in ZDC was simulated by smearing each neutron's energy (mean 96.5 GeV) with a Gaussian of 18% width, following STAR ZDC response. Events were selected as body-tip candidates by applying a linear cut (slope = 0.25, intercept = -180) on the correlation of ZDC energies in left and right directions.

## Key numbers

- 0.6 million 0-5% central U+U events were generated at √sNN = 193 GeV
- Single neutron ZDC response resolution: 18% (Gaussian width relative to mean)
- Mean energy deposited by a single spectator neutron: 96.5 GeV
- Charged particle v2(pT) in selected body-tip events is systematically 25% lower than in all configurations
- Purity ranges from 24% (slope = 0.35, ±10° angular relaxation) to 74% (slope = 0.10, ±30° angular relaxation)
- For slope = 0.25, purity is 26% (±10°), 48% (±20°), and 67% (±30°)

## Context

This work extends studies of deformed Uranium collisions (UU_paper, 2014) which showed that elliptic flow correlates strongly with initial overlap configuration. The method addresses the experimental challenge of selecting specific nuclear orientations, which is relevant for searches of the chiral magnetic effect (CME) and chiral separation effect (CSE) where low v2 and high magnetic fields are desired. The approach enables future experimental selection of body-tip events at RHIC, potentially providing cleaner conditions for CME studies compared to Au+Au or Pb+Pb collisions where either magnetic field or v2 background is unfavorable.
