# Entanglement between Valence and Sea Quarks in Hadrons of 1+1 Dimensional QCD

> The conceptual interpretation of valence- and sea-quark separation, which is a key aspect of the parton model and of an intuitive picture of hadron structure, becomes obscured by quantum effects in QCD. This suggests that there may be measures of entanglement between quark degrees of freedom that are present in QCD, but absent in the intuitive picture with a clear valence-sea (VS) separation. In this paper, we define the first rigorous measure of VS entanglement in QCD in an attempt to bring conceptual clarity to this issue, and, potentially, to find a measure of the applicability of the parton model to QCD bound states. This VS entanglement vanishes in the large-$N_c$ limit, and it remains low when finite-$N_c$ states resemble their large-$N_c$ counterparts. We perform a numerical study of VS entanglement in 1+1 dimensional discrete light-cone quantized QCD, and in the process develop a method for building the color-singlet basis of 1+1d QCD that is manifestly complete and orthogonal by construction. We calculate this VS entanglement entropy for the first time and find that it is relatively low for the first few excited states of both mesons and baryons compared to all other states in the spectrum, with the VS entropy of ground state hadrons providing a minimum. We also see that for ground state mesons the entropy is well described in the $1/N_c$ approximation. These results suggest that low energy hadrons may be the only QCD bound states for which the large-$N_c$ expansion, and perhaps the parton model, provide an accurate description. This work also provides the first evidence that the VS entanglement entropy of QCD in 3+1d, which would likely serve as an order parameter for the transition between quark and hadron degrees of freedom, may be perturbatively accessible through a large-$N_c$ expansion.

## Key claim

The paper defines the first rigorous measure of valence-sea (VS) entanglement entropy in QCD, finding that it vanishes in the large-\(N_c\) limit and remains low for low-energy hadrons, suggesting that only these states may be accurately described by the large-\(N_c\) expansion and the parton model.

## Method

The author performs a numerical study of 1+1 dimensional QCD using discrete light-cone quantization (DLCQ), developing a method to construct the color-singlet basis that is manifestly complete and orthogonal by construction using Young's orthogonal form for permutations of quarks in a Fock state. The VS entanglement entropy is calculated by duplicating the Hilbert space, applying an operator that moves valence quarks of appropriate flavors from the hadron state into the duplicate vacuum, and then tracing out the original Hilbert space to obtain a reduced density matrix whose von Neumann entropy quantifies the entanglement.

## Key numbers

* The VS entanglement entropy of ground state mesons is well described by a \(1/N_c\) approximation, indicating rapid convergence of the large-\(N_c\) expansion for these states.
* The VS entropy is relatively low for the first few excited states of both mesons and baryons compared to all other states in the spectrum, with the ground state hadrons providing a minimum.
* For mesons in the large-\(N_c\) limit, which have no sea quarks, the VS entanglement entropy is zero.

## Context

This work extends the author's prior study of helicity entanglement in nucleons (Beane 2019) by introducing a more general measure of entanglement between valence and sea quark degrees of freedom, making fewer assumptions about the nucleon state. It builds on the 't Hooft model (t Hooft 1974) of 1+1d QCD in the large-\(N_c\) limit, where mesons have no sea quarks and thus zero VS entanglement, and uses DLCQ methods developed by Pauli (1985), Brodsky (1997), and Hornbostel (1988). The results suggest that the VS entanglement entropy of QCD in 3+1 dimensions may serve as an order parameter for the transition between quark and hadron degrees of freedom and may be perturbatively accessible through a large-\(N_c\) expansion.
