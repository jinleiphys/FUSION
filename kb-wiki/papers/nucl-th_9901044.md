# Backbending in Dy isotopes within the Projected Shell Model

> A systematic study of the yrast band in 154-164 Dy isotopes using the Projected Shell Model is presented. It is shown that, in the context of the present model, enlarging the mean field deformation by about 20 % allows a very good description of the spectrum of yrast band in these isotopes. The dependence of the B(E2) values on angular momentum is also better described when larger deformations are used. The observed oscillation of g-factors at low spin states remains an open question for this model.

## Key claim

Enlarging the mean field deformation by about 20% over the standard values allows the Projected Shell Model to quantitatively describe the yrast band spectra, backbending behavior, and B(E2) values across the 154-164 Dy isotope chain, but the observed oscillation of g-factors at low spin states (around I = 6ℏ) cannot be reproduced within the current model space.

## Method

The authors used the Projected Shell Model (PSM) with a multi-quasiparticle basis built on a Nilsson + BCS mean field, assuming axial symmetry. They diagonalized a quadrupole-plus-pairing Hamiltonian (including quadrupole-quadrupole, monopole pairing, and quadrupole pairing interactions) in an angular-momentum-projected basis, with the quadrupole-quadrupole strength obtained self-consistently from the mean field deformation ε₂. The deformation was increased by approximately 20% from standard tabulated values for each isotope, and the monopole and quadrupole pairing strengths were taken from systematics as G_M = (20.12 ∓ 13.13(N-Z)/A)A⁻¹ and G_Q = 0.18 G_M.

## Key numbers

- Deformation increase of approximately 20% was found necessary to best reproduce experimental data across the 154-164 Dy chain (Table 1).
- For 158Dy, effective charges were changed from e_p=1.5, e_n=0.5 (standard deformation) to e_p=1.41, e_n=0.41 (enlarged deformation) using the prescription e_p=e(1+Z/A), e_n=e(Z/A).
- In 154Dy, the experimental yrast band exhibits a very sharp second backbending at I = 30 ℏ that is not predicted by either standard or enlarged deformation calculations.
- For 158Dy, the crossing angle between the ground band and the 2-quasiparticle band occurs at I = 12 ℏ for standard deformation and at I = 16 ℏ for enlarged deformation.
- The g-factor calculations used free g_l values and g_s values damped by a factor of 0.75: g_s^p = 5.586 × 0.75, g_s^n = -3.826 × 0.75.

## Context

This work extends earlier PSM studies of Dy isotopes by Sun (1994), which found that the PSM seemed to exaggerate backbending, and addresses limitations of cranked mean-field theories studied by Cescato et al. (1991) that could not explain band crossing details. The improved description using enlarged deformation is also found to be operative in other rare earth isotope chains (Velazquez et al., 1999). The paper notes that a recent extension of the PSM by Sheihk and Hara (1998) including γ-deformation and 3-dimensional angular momentum projection could remove discrepancies found for 154Dy and 156Dy, and that the observed g-factor oscillation at low spins remains an open question with no microscopic calculations able to reproduce it.
