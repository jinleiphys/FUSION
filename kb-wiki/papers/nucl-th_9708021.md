# Computations of Three-Body Continuum Spectra

> We formulate a method to solve the coordinate space Faddeev equations for positive energies. The method employs hyperspherical coordinates and analytical expressions for the effective potentials at large distances. Realistic computations of the parameters of the resonances and the strength functions are carried out for the Borromean halo nucleus 6He (n+n+alpha) for J = 0+, 0-, 1+, 1-, 2+,2-. PACS numbers: 21.45.+v, 11.80.Jy, 31.15.Ja, 21.60.Gx

## Key claim

The paper formulates a method to solve the coordinate-space Faddeev equations for positive energies that combines numerical treatment at short distances with analytical expressions for the effective potentials at large distances, enabling accurate computation of three-body continuum spectra for Borromean halo nuclei. The method's practical success depends on the assumption of short-range two-body potentials and the use of square-well approximations at large hyperradii, with the caveat that the asymptotic behavior must be reached (typically around 40 fm) before the analytical solutions become valid.

## Method

The authors expand the total three-body wave function in hyperspherical coordinates and solve the coupled Faddeev equations for each hyperradius ρ. At short distances, the angular eigenvalue equations are solved numerically, while at large distances (where the effective potentials decay as ρ⁻³ or faster), analytical solutions are obtained by matching square-well approximations inside the interaction region to hyperspherical Bessel functions outside. The S-matrix is constructed from the asymptotic boundary conditions, and resonances are extracted either from rapid phase-shift variations or directly via the complex-energy method by solving for S-matrix poles at E = E_r − iΓ/2.

## Key numbers

- For ⁶He, the radial equations must be integrated up to ρ_max = 180 fm to reach the region where the 1/ρ³ tail of the effective potential becomes negligible.
- The two lowest S-matrix poles for J^π = 2⁺ are (E_r = 0.845 MeV, Γ = 0.093 MeV) and (E_r = 1.05 MeV, Γ = 0.40 MeV) with one three-body force parametrization; with another parametrization they are (E_r = 1.02 MeV, Γ = 0.37 MeV) and (E_r = 1.23 MeV, Γ = 0.45 MeV).
- For J^π = 1⁻, the two lowest S-matrix poles are (E_r = 0.95 MeV, Γ = 0.38 MeV) and (E_r = 1.43 MeV, Γ = 0.56 MeV) with one three-body force; with another they are (E_r = 1.11 MeV, Γ = 0.42 MeV) and (E_r = 1.67 MeV, Γ = 0.58 MeV).
- The strength functions show 91% of monopole, 60% of dipole, and 70% of quadrupole strength below 5 MeV for ⁶He.
- The monopole sum rule value is 749 fm⁴; the dipole and quadrupole sum rules are 1.31 e²fm² and 8.19 e²fm⁴ respectively.
- Maximum K_n-values up to 142 are used in the basis expansion.

## Context

This work extends the bound-state Faddeev method of Fedorov, Jensen, and collaborators (1993, 1996) to positive energies, enabling computation of continuum spectra for Borromean halo nuclei such as ⁶He and ¹¹Li. It addresses the long-standing difficulty of treating the precise large-distance behavior in three-body continuum problems, which had been noted as a limitation by Friar (1995) and others. The method enables systematic computation of resonance parameters, phase shifts, and strength functions for halo nuclei, providing a framework to interpret experimental data from systems like those studied by Danilin and collaborators (1993).
