# Advanced parametrisations for hadronic form factors

> The rich analytic structure of hadronic form factors makes a theoretically consistent yet easily applicable parametrisation cumbersome. Consequently, most parametrisations are limited to reproducing the simplest analytic features sufficient to describe form factors on their first Riemann sheet. Here, we introduce two novel form factor parametrisations that allow resonance poles and left-hand cuts on the second Riemann sheet to be studied, while also making the connection to partial-wave amplitudes manifest.

## Key claim

The paper introduces two novel conformal parametrisations for hadronic form factors that, unlike the standard z-expansion, allow resonance poles and left-hand cuts on the second Riemann sheet to be studied explicitly, while also making the connection to partial-wave amplitudes manifest, though the ζ-expansion's region of convergence is limited by higher-energy branch cuts to |ζ(s)| < ζ(s_in), where s_in is the first inelastic threshold.

## Method

The authors construct an improved conformal map (ζ-map) by applying a second ω-map to the standard z-map, unfolding the left-hand cut of the second Riemann sheet so that four Riemann sheets become accessible within the unit disc. They test this parametrisation by fitting truncated ζ-series expansions to pseudo-data for the scalar isoscalar and vector isovector pion form factors, generated using the inverse amplitude method (IAM) and Omnès representation, and compare the extracted resonance pole parameters with those obtained from the standard z-expansion. A second parametrisation (φ-map) is introduced that incorporates two thresholds simultaneously, mapping four Riemann sheets to distinct regions in the complex plane.

## Key numbers

- For the f0(500) resonance, the exact pole position from the modified IAM is √s = 0.4515 − 0.2266i GeV; the ζ-expansion with n=4 yields √s = 0.4514 − 0.2266i GeV, while the z-expansion with n=4 gives √s = 0.4493 − 0.2267i GeV.
- For the ρ(770) resonance, the exact pole position from the IAM is √s = 0.76143 − 0.07548i GeV; the ζ-expansion with n=4 yields √s = 0.76142 − 0.07551i GeV, while the z-expansion with n=4 gives √s = 0.76142 − 0.07558i GeV.
- The ζ-expansion with n=3 achieves χ² = 1.43 × 10⁻⁶ for the scalar form factor fit (50 data points, s ∈ [0,1] GeV²), compared to χ² = 67.8 × 10⁻⁶ for the z-expansion at the same truncation order.
- For the vector form factor fit (50 data points, s ∈ [0,1.2] GeV²), the ζ-expansion with n=3 gives χ² = 215 × 10⁻⁶ versus χ² = 698 × 10⁻⁶ for the z-expansion.
- The ζ-map maps the f0(500) pole to ζ = −0.110 − 0.041i and the ρ(770) pole to ζ = 0.077 − 0.335i, both inside the unit disc, whereas the z-map places them at z = 0.956 − 1.459i and z = 0.796 − 0.730i respectively.

## Context

This work extends the standard conformal mapping approach for hadronic form factors, originally developed by Meiman (1963) and Okubo (1971) and later refined into the Boyd–Grinstein–Lebed (BGL) parametrisation (Boyd 1997) and the Bourrely–Caprini–Lellouch (BCL) parametrisation (Bourrely 2008). It directly addresses convergence issues observed by Buck and Lebed (1998) when fitting form factors above the elastic threshold, and builds on the approach of Kirk (2024) that introduced explicit poles outside the unit disc. The new parametrisations enable simultaneous description of form factors and partial-wave amplitudes, allowing extraction of resonance pole parameters from data in the timelike region and prediction of scattering amplitudes from form factor fits, which was not possible with previous z-expansion methods.
