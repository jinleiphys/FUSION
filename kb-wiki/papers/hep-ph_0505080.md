# Hadronic Modes and Quark Properties in the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Based on interaction potentials between a heavy quark and antiquark as extracted from recent QCD lattice calculations, we set up a Brueckner-type many-body scheme to study the properties of light (anti-) quarks in a Quark-Gluon Plasma at moderate temperatures, $T\simeq$ 1-2 $T_c$. The quark-antiquark $T$-matrix, including both color-singlet and -octet channels, and corresponding quark self-energies and spectral functions are calculated self-consistently. The repulsive octet potential induces quasiparticle masses of up to 150 MeV, whereas the attractive color-singlet part generates resonance structures in the $q$-$\bar q$ $T$-matrix, which in turn lead to quasiparticle widths of $\sim$200 MeV. This corresponds to scattering rates of $\sim$1 fm$^{-1}$ and may reflect liquid-like properties of the system.

## Key claim

Based on quark-antiquark potentials extracted from lattice QCD, the authors find that the repulsive color-octet potential generates quasiparticle masses of up to 150 MeV while the attractive color-singlet part produces resonance structures in the q-qbar T-matrix, leading to quasiparticle widths of approximately 200 MeV; these results may reflect liquid-like properties of the system at temperatures around 1-2 times the critical temperature.

## Method

The authors extracted a color-singlet heavy-quark potential from unquenched lattice QCD free energy data by subtracting the entropy contribution, then applied a 3-dimensionally reduced Bethe-Salpeter (Lippmann-Schwinger) equation to compute the q-qbar T-matrix in both color-singlet and -octet channels. Quark self-energies (both real and imaginary parts) were calculated from the T-matrices and reinserted into the propagator of the scattering equation, iterating until self-consistency was achieved. The calculations assumed zero current quark mass, vanishing quark chemical potential, and used a gluon-induced mass term of 0.1 GeV as default.

## Key numbers

- Quasiparticle masses of up to 150 MeV induced by the repulsive octet potential at temperatures 1.2-1.5 Tc
- Quasiparticle widths of approximately 200 MeV generated by resonant scattering in the color-singlet channel at temperatures around 1.5 Tc
- Scattering rates of approximately 1 fm^{-1} corresponding to the quasiparticle widths
- At T=1.2 Tc, the color-singlet T-matrix exhibits a bound state significantly below the q-qbar threshold energy of approximately 0.52 GeV
- At T=1.5 Tc with a gluon-induced mass term of 250 MeV, the combined real part at low momenta amounts to a quark mass of approximately 350 MeV
- The charmonium (c-cbar) system with charm-quark mass of 1.8 GeV shows the resonance crossing threshold at approximately 2 Tc

## Context

This work extends earlier studies that extracted heavy-quark potentials from lattice QCD to study quarkonium properties (Digal 2001, Wong 2004, Mocsy 2004) and applies a similar approach to light quarks, building on suggestions of hadronic states surviving above the phase transition from the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Hatsuda 1984, 1985) and the instanton-liquid model (Schafer 1995). The self-consistent many-body treatment including finite widths for both quarks and bound states goes beyond previous work by Shuryak and Zahed (2004) and Brown et al. (2003) which did not include such effects. The results enable improved calculations of thermal relaxation times for heavy quarks and provide a framework for understanding the strongly interacting nature of the quark-gluon plasma observed at RHIC.
