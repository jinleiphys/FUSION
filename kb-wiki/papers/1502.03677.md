# Fitting Single Particle Energies in $sdgh$ Major Shell

> We have performed two kinds of non-linear fitting procedures to the single-particle energies in the $sdgh$ major shell to obtain better shell model results. The low-lying energy eigenvalues of the light Sn isotopes with $A=103-110$ in the $sdgh$-shell are calculated in the framework of the nuclear shell model by using CD-Bonn two-body effective nucleon-nucleon interaction. The obtained energy eigenvalues are fitted to the corresponding experimental values by using two different non-linear fitting procedures, i.e., downhill simplex method and clonal selection method. The unknown single-particle energies of the states $2s_{1/2}$, $1d_{3/2}$, and $0h_{11/2}$ are used in the fitting methods to obtain better spectra of the $^{104,106,108,110}$Sn isotopes. We compare the energy spectra of the $^{104,106,108,110}$Sn and $^{103,105,107,109}$Sn isotopes with/without a nonlinear fit to the experimental results.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that non-linear fitting of three unknown single-particle energies in the $sdgh$ major shell (for the $2s_{1/2}$, $1d_{3/2}$, and $0h_{11/2}$ states) can improve shell-model energy spectra for light Sn isotopes, but the fitted values depend strongly on the optimization method used, and the improvement is not uniform across all states or isotopes.

## Method
Shell-model calculations using the CD-Bonn two-body effective interaction were performed for the low-lying yrast states ($0^+$ through $10^+$) of $^{104,106,108,110}$Sn. The single-particle energies of the $2s_{1/2}$, $1d_{3/2}$, and $0h_{11/2}$ neutron orbits were treated as free parameters and optimized by minimizing the sum of squared deviations between calculated and experimental excitation energies using two non-linear fitting procedures: the downhill simplex method and the clonal selection algorithm. The $1d_{5/2}$ and $0g_{7/2}$ single-particle energies were fixed at 0.00 MeV and 0.172 MeV, respectively, based on experimental data from $^{101}$Sn.

## Key numbers
- Initial single-particle energies before fitting: $\varepsilon_{2s_{1/2}} = 2.45$ MeV, $\varepsilon_{1d_{3/2}} = 2.55$ MeV, $\varepsilon_{0h_{11/2}} = 3.20$ MeV.
- Fitted single-particle energies from simplex method: $\varepsilon_{2s_{1/2}} = 1.53$ MeV, $\varepsilon_{1d_{3/2}} = 2.47$ MeV, $\varepsilon_{0h_{11/2}} = 4.80$ MeV.
- Fitted single-particle energies from clonal selection method: $\varepsilon_{2s_{1/2}} = 1.56$ MeV, $\varepsilon_{1d_{3/2}} = 3.20$ MeV, $\varepsilon_{0h_{11/2}} = 4.15$ MeV.
- Total $\sigma^2$ before fitting: 1.127; after simplex fitting: 0.208 (76 iterations); after clonal selection fitting: 0.256 (130 iterations).
- Largest Hamiltonian dimension in the fitting process: 156,674 for the $8^+$ state of $^{110}$Sn.
- Calculation time for the $4^+$ state: approximately 10 minutes for $^{108}$Sn and 156 minutes for $^{110}$Sn.
- For $^{106}$Sn, the simplex method reproduced the $4^+$ and $10^+$ states exactly (rms deviation 0.00 MeV).
- For $^{108}$Sn, the simplex method reproduced the $10^+$ state exactly (rms deviation 0.00 MeV).

## Context
This work extends the approach of Wildenthal (1984) for the $sd$-shell and Honma et al. (2002) for the $pf$-shell, where single-particle energies and effective interaction matrix elements were fitted to experimental data. Unlike those global fits, the present study restricts itself to fitting only the three unknown single-particle energies in the $sdgh$ major shell, leaving the 542 two-body matrix elements of the CD-Bonn interaction unchanged. The fitted single-particle energies are intended for use in future shell-model calculations of other nuclei near $^{100}$Sn, particularly mid-heavy Sn isotopes ($A=110-120$) and light Sb isotopes. The ambiguity in the $0g_{7/2}$ single-particle energy was resolved by the experimental identification of the $0g_{7/2}$ state at 171.7 keV in $^{101}$Sn by Seweryniak et al. (2007).
