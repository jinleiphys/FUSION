# Lattice QCD calculation of the Collins-Soper kernel from quasi TMDPDFs

> This work presents a lattice quantum chromodynamics (QCD) calculation of the nonperturbative Collins-Soper kernel, which describes the rapidity evolution of quark transverse-momentum-dependent parton distribution functions. The kernel is extracted at transverse momentum scales in the range 400 MeV $< q_T < 1.7$ GeV in a calculation with dynamical fermions and quark masses corresponding to a larger-than-physical pion mass, $m_π=538(1)$ MeV. It is found that different approaches to extract the Collins-Soper kernel from the same underlying lattice QCD matrix elements yield significantly different results and uncertainty estimates, revealing that power corrections, such as those associated with higher-twist effects, and perturbative matching between quasi and light-cone beam functions, cannot be neglected.

## Key claim

The Collins-Soper kernel is extracted from lattice QCD at transverse momentum scales 400 MeV < q_T < 1.7 GeV, but different analysis approaches applied to the same underlying lattice matrix elements yield significantly different results and uncertainty estimates, revealing that power corrections (higher-twist effects) and perturbative matching between quasi and light-cone beam functions cannot be neglected.

## Method

The Collins-Soper kernel was computed from ratios of nonsinglet quasi TMDPDFs at different hadron momenta using Eq. (4) of the paper, which relates the kernel to renormalized quasi beam functions. The calculation used a single lattice ensemble with 48^3 x 64 volume, a = 0.12 fm lattice spacing, dynamical fermions with m_pi = 538(1) MeV, and three pion boosts P^z = 0.65, 1.1, and 1.5 GeV. Nonperturbative renormalization was performed using the RI-prime/MOM scheme with matching to the MSbar scheme at one-loop order, and asymmetry corrections from incomplete cancellation of Wilson-line self-energy were applied to the renormalized quasi beam functions.

## Key numbers

* Pion mass: m_pi = 538(1) MeV (larger than physical)
* Lattice spacing: a = 0.12 fm
* Lattice volume: 48^3 x 64
* Pion boosts studied: P^z = 0.65 GeV (n_z=3), 1.1 GeV (n_z=5), and 1.5 GeV (n_z=7)
* Transverse momentum range: 400 MeV < q_T < 1.7 GeV
* Renormalization scale: mu = 2 GeV
* RI-prime/MOM scale used for best estimates: p_R = 4.9 GeV (n^mu = (12,12,12,12) in lattice units)
* Wilson line lengths studied: eta/a = 12, 14, and 23
* Number of configurations: 96-596 depending on boost momentum and operator geometry
* Average chi^2/d.o.f. for asymmetry fits: 0.56

## Context

This work extends the approach of Ebert (2018, 2019) for computing the Collins-Soper kernel from ratios of quasi TMDPDFs, building on previous lattice QCD studies by Shanahan (2019, 2020), Zhang (2020), Schlemmer (2021), and Li (2021). The calculation includes several advances over prior work: one-loop perturbative matching between quasi and light-cone TMDPDFs, full accounting for operator mixing under renormalization (consistent with Ji (2021) but contrasting with Constantinou (2019) and Green (2020) who predicted no mixing for gamma^3 at O(a^0)), and improved treatments of power corrections and systematic effects. The results enable future lattice QCD calculations with physical pion masses and multiple lattice spacings to provide complementary constraints to global analyses of experimental data from Drell-Yan production and semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering.
