# Partial dynamical symmetry from energy density functionals

> We show that the notion of partial dynamical symmetry is robust and founded on a microscopic many-body theory of nuclei. Based on the universal energy density functional framework, a general quantal boson Hamiltonian is derived and shown to have essentially the same spectroscopic character as that predicted by the partial SU(3) symmetry. The principal conclusion holds in two representative classes of energy density functionals: nonrelativistic and relativistic. The analysis is illustrated in application to the axially-deformed nucleus $^{168}$Er.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that partial dynamical symmetry (PDS) is not merely a phenomenological concept but has a microscopic foundation in nuclear many-body theory, specifically showing that boson Hamiltonians derived from universal energy density functionals (EDFs) produce eigenstates with essentially the same spectroscopic character as those of the partial SU(3) symmetry, with the principal conclusion holding for both nonrelativistic and relativistic EDFs.

## Method

The authors performed constrained self-consistent mean-field (SCMF) calculations using four different EDF parameterizations (Skyrme SLy4 and SkP for nonrelativistic; DD-PC1 and DD-ME2 for relativistic) with varying pairing strengths to generate energy surfaces for ¹⁶⁸Er. They then mapped these microscopic energy surfaces onto the energy surface of an interacting boson model (IBM) Hamiltonian of the form that exhibits SU(3)-PDS, determining the Hamiltonian parameters by requiring topological similarity near the global minimum. The resulting IBM Hamiltonians were diagonalized and their wave functions analyzed for SU(3) content to compare with the pure SU(3)-PDS predictions.

## Key numbers

- For ¹⁶⁸Er, the experimental bandhead energies are E(2₂)=821 keV and E(0₂)=1217 keV, with ratio R=1.48.
- The SU(3)-PDS parameters from phenomenological fit are h₀=8.0 keV, h₂=4.0 keV, ρ=13.0 keV, and β₀=√2≈1.412.
- For the Skyrme SLy4 EDF with V₀=1250 MeVfm³, the derived parameters are h₀=10.4 keV, h₂=4.0 keV, ρ=12.3 keV, β₀=1.39, yielding E(2₂)=809 keV and E(0₂)=1334 keV (R=1.65).
- For the relativistic DD-ME2 EDF with V₀=837 MeVfm³, the derived parameters are h₀=9.9 keV, h₂=4.2 keV, ρ=11.73 keV, β₀=1.50, yielding E(2₂)=883 keV and E(0₂)=1499 keV (R=1.70).
- For SU(3)-PDS, the β band contains a mixture of irreps: (2N-4,2) at 87.5%, (2N-6,0) at 9.6%, and (2N-8,4) at 2.9% with N=16.
- For the SLy4 and SkP EDFs with V₀=1250 and 800 MeVfm³ respectively, the ground and γ bands show SU(3) purity with probability 99.8%.

## Context

This work provides the first microscopic justification of partial dynamical symmetry in heavy nuclei, extending prior phenomenological studies of SU(3)-PDS in ¹⁶⁸Er by Leviatan (1996) and Casten (2014). It connects the algebraic PDS concept to the universal energy density functional framework, complementing recent demonstrations of emergent Sp(3,R) dynamical symmetry in light nuclei by Dytrych (2020) and SU(3) quasi dynamical symmetry in ³⁶Ar by Johnson (2020). The results enable using empirically manifested PDS to constrain and optimize EDFs in specific mass regions, and predict where partial symmetries may appear in exotic nuclei.
