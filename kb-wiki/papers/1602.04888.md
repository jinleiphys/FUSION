# Long Range Azimuthal Correlations of Multiple Gluons in Gluon Saturation Limit

> We calculate the inclusive gluon correlation function for arbitrary number of gluons with full rapidity and transverse momentum dependence for the initial glasma state of the p-p, p-A and A-A collisions. The formula we derive via superdiagrams generates cumulants for any number of gluons. Higher order cumulants contain information on correlations between multiple gluons, and they are necessary for calculations of higher dimensional ridges as well as flow coefficients from multi-particle correlations.

## Key claim

The paper derives a general formula for the inclusive $n$-gluon correlation function $C_n$ in the glasma initial state of p-p, p-A, and A-A collisions, valid in the gluon saturation limit. The derivation uses a superdiagram technique that reduces the number of diagrams needed from hundreds or thousands to only $2(n-2)$ diagrams for $C_n$, but the result is limited to the Gaussian white noise approximation and the saturation regime.

## Method

The author develops a superdiagram technique that replaces the need to calculate large numbers of connected glasma rainbow diagrams for multi-gluon cumulants. For $C_3$, 16 connected diagrams are replaced by 2 superdiagrams; for $C_4$, 96 diagrams become 4 superdiagrams; for $C_5$, 448 diagrams become 6 superdiagrams. The superdiagrams encode the rapidity index structure of the unintegrated gluon distribution functions (UGDs) from both nuclei, with rules specifying that the first rapidity site visited is always squared, only nearest-neighbor hoppings are allowed, and each site can be visited at most twice. The resulting formula for $C_n$ is given in Eq. (Cn) with prefactor $\alpha_s^n N_c^n S_\perp / [\pi^{4n}(N_c^2-1)^{2n-1}]$ times an integral over transverse momentum $\bm{k}_\perp$ of terms involving UGDs.

## Key numbers

- For $C_3$, 16 connected glasma diagrams are replaced by 2 superdiagrams.
- For $C_4$, 96 connected glasma diagrams are replaced by 4 superdiagrams.
- For $C_5$, 448 connected glasma diagrams are replaced by 6 superdiagrams.
- The total number of connected and disconnected rainbow diagrams for five gluons ($\mu_5$) is 1889.
- The prefactor for $C_n$ is $\alpha_s^n N_c^n S_\perp / [\pi^{4n}(N_c^2-1)^{2n-1}]$, where $S_\perp$ is the transverse area and $n$ is the number of gluons.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier calculation of triple and quadruple gluon correlations (Ozonder, 2014) to arbitrary $n$, and builds on the glasma approach to initial-state correlations from gluon saturation (Dumitru 2010, Dusling 2012-2013). The derived $C_n$ cumulants can be used to compute higher-dimensional ridges and flow coefficients $v_m(n\text{PC})$ from multi-particle correlations, providing a testing ground to distinguish between hydrodynamical final-state explanations (Bozek 2011-2015) and initial-state glasma explanations for the collective behavior observed in high-multiplicity p-p, p-A, and A-A collisions.
