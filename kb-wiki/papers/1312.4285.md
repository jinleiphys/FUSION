# Coulomb and spin-orbit interactions in random phase approximation calculations

> We present a fully self-consistent computational framework composed by Hartree-Fock plus ran- dom phase approximation where the spin-orbit and Coulomb terms of the interaction are included in both steps of the calculations. We study the effects of these terms of the interaction on the random phase approximation calculations, where they are usually neglected. We carry out our investigation of excited states in spherical nuclei of oxygen, calcium, nickel, zirconium, tin and lead isotope chains. We use finite-range effective nucleon-nucleon interactions of Gogny type. The size of the effects we find is, usually, of few hundreds of keV. There are not simple approximations which can be used to simulate these effects since they strongly depend on all the variables related to the excited states, angular momentum, parity, excitation energy, isoscalar and isovector characters. Even the Slater approximation developed to account for the Coulomb exchange terms in Hartree-Fock is not valid in random phase approximation calculations.

## Key claim

The spin-orbit and Coulomb terms of the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, usually neglected in random phase approximation (RPA) calculations, produce effects of a few hundreds of keV on excitation energies, but these effects strongly depend on angular momentum, parity, excitation energy, and isoscalar/isovector character, with no simple approximation (including the Slater approximation for Coulomb exchange) capable of reproducing them.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) plus RPA calculations using the finite-range Gogny D1M interaction, including both the spin-orbit and Coulomb terms in both the HF and RPA steps. They solved the RPA equations in matrix form for spherical nuclei across oxygen, calcium, nickel, zirconium, tin, and lead isotope chains, evaluating the Coulomb and spin-orbit matrix elements exactly. The study focused on low-lying 2+ and 3- electric excitations, as well as isoscalar and isovector excitations in N=Z nuclei, and excitations dominated by single particle-hole pairs.

## Key numbers

- Maximum energy differences from Coulomb interaction alone: below 100 keV for 2+ states, below 200 keV for 3- states, with sign reversal when exchange terms are included
- Largest combined Coulomb+spin-orbit effects: about 1.2 MeV for the 3- state in 52Ca and for the 2+ state in 56Ni
- For 208Pb, the low-lying 2+ state energy shifted from 5.61 MeV (without Coulomb and spin-orbit) to 4.93 MeV (with both terms), compared to the experimental value of 4.08 MeV, giving a shift of about 0.85 MeV
- For isovector excitations, the combined Coulomb+spin-orbit effect produced a reduction of about 150 keV, almost independent of multipolarity and nucleus
- For isoscalar excitations, the combined effect reached at most about 300 keV
- For neutron-dominated excitations, spin-orbit effects exceeded 0.8 MeV for the 3- of 60Ca and the 2+ of 78Ni

## Context

This work extends the fully self-consistent HF+RPA framework previously developed by the same group (De Donno et al., 2011) by including spin-orbit and Coulomb terms that were neglected in those earlier RPA calculations. It complements earlier studies by Peru et al. (2005) using the Gogny D1S' interaction on doubly magic nuclei, by using the D1M parameterization and a wider set of spherical nuclei, focusing on low-lying states rather than giant resonance centroids. The results confirm that the Slater approximation (Slater, 1951) for Coulomb exchange, valid in HF calculations, is not valid in RPA calculations, and that no simple approximation can reproduce the spin-orbit and Coulomb effects due to their strong dependence on the specific excitation.
