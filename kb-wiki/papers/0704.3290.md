# Residual correlations between decay products of $π^0π^0$ and $pΣ^0$ systems

> Residual correlations between decay products due to a combination of both correlations between parents at small relative velocities and small decay momenta are discussed. Residual correlations between photons from pion decays are considered as a new possible source of information on direct photon fraction. Residual correlations in $pγ$ and $pΛ$ systems due to $pΣ^0$ interaction in final state are predicted based on the $pΣ^0$ low energy scattering parameters deduced from the spin-flavour SU$_6$ model by Fujiwara et al. including effective meson exchange potentials and explicit flavour symmetry breaking to reproduce the properties of the two-nucleon system and the low-energy hyperon-nucleon cross section data. The $pγ_{Σ^0}$ residual correlation is concentrated at $k^* \approx 70$ Mev/$c$ and its shape and intensity appears to be sensitive to the scattering parameters and space-time dimensions of the source. The $pΛ_{Σ^0}$ residual correlation recovers the negative parent $pΣ^0$ correlation for $k^* > 70$ Mev/$c$. The neglect of this negative residual correlation would lead to the underestimation of the parent $pΛ$ correlation effect and to an overestimation of the source size.

## Key claim

Residual correlations between decay products (photons from neutral pions, or protons and photons/lambdas from Sigma-0 decays) can serve as a new source of femtoscopic information, not merely as a distorting effect; however, the practical utility of the two-photon residual correlation for measuring the direct photon fraction depends on the ability to predict the residual correlation function with sufficient accuracy, and the predicted residual correlations in the p-gamma and p-Lambda systems are contingent on the specific low-energy p-Sigma-0 scattering parameters deduced from the spin-flavour SU6 model by Fujiwara et al.

## Method

The authors simulated two-photon correlations by generating neutral pions and direct photons with a thermal-like momentum distribution (T0 = 168 MeV) and applying quantum statistics weights to pairs, assuming a Gaussian source radius r0 = 5 fm. For the baryon systems, they calculated the p-Sigma-0, p-Lambda, and residual p-Lambda-from-Sigma-0 and p-gamma-from-Sigma-0 correlation functions using a single-channel and a two-channel approach for final-state interactions, employing the s-wave scattering amplitudes and low-energy scattering parameters (scattering lengths and effective radii) from the spin-flavour SU6 quark model of Fujiwara et al. (2004) and, for comparison, from the NSC89 model.

## Key numbers

*   The decay momentum in the decay Sigma-0 -> Lambda gamma is p_D = 74 MeV/c.
*   For the two-photon correlation study, the thermal-like momentum distribution used T0 = 168 MeV, and photons with energy below 80 MeV were rejected.
*   The residual p-gamma-from-Sigma-0 correlation is concentrated at k* ≈ 70 MeV/c.
*   The p-Lambda-from-Sigma-0 residual correlation recovers the negative parent p-Sigma-0 correlation for k* > 70 MeV/c.
*   For the p-Lambda system, the scattering lengths from Fujiwara et al. are a^0 = 2.59 fm and a^1 = 1.60 fm; for the p-Sigma-0 system with isospin 3/2, a_{3/2}^0 = 2.51 fm and a_{3/2}^1 = -0.73 fm; for the p-Sigma-0 system with isospin 1/2 and spin S=0, a_{1/2}^0 = -1.1 fm; for the p-Sigma-0 system with isospin 1/2 and spin S=1, a_{1/2}^1 = (-1.1 + i4.3) fm.
*   The fraction of Lambdas from Sigma-0 decay in high-energy heavy ion collisions is stated to be approximately 40 percent.

## Context

This work extends the concept of residual correlations, previously observed for two-photon systems (WA98 experiment, 2004), to baryon systems involving strange baryons, specifically the p-Sigma-0 system. It builds upon the low-energy hyperon-nucleon scattering parameters calculated by Fujiwara et al. (2004) using a spin-flavour SU6 quark model. The paper predicts that the residual p-Lambda-from-Sigma-0 correlation, if neglected, would lead to an underestimation of the parent p-Lambda correlation effect and an overestimation of the source size in femtoscopic analyses, and it suggests that the residual p-gamma-from-Sigma-0 correlation could provide a new way to study p-Sigma-0 final-state interactions and source size parameters.
