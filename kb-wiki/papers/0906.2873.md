# Charged-Current and Neutral-Current Neutrino-Nucleus Scattering in a Relativistic Approach

> Relativistic models developed for the exclusive and inclusive QuasiElastic (QE) electron scattering have been extended to Charged-Current (CC) and Neutral-Current (NC) neutrino-nucleus scattering. The results of different descriptions of Final-State Interactions (FSI) are compared.

## Key claim
Relativistic models originally developed for quasielastic electron scattering can be extended to charged-current and neutral-current neutrino-nucleus scattering, but the treatment of final-state interactions (FSI) must be chosen carefully depending on whether the reaction is exclusive, semi-inclusive, or inclusive, because the imaginary part of the optical potential that correctly absorbs flux in exclusive channels would incorrectly remove flux in inclusive channels.

## Method
The authors apply the Relativistic Distorted-Wave Impulse Approximation (RDWIA) for exclusive one-nucleon knockout reactions, where bound and scattering states are derived from a Feshbach-type optical potential. For semi-inclusive neutrino-nucleus scattering (only the outgoing nucleon detected), the transition amplitudes are summed over shell-model states using the same RDWIA amplitudes with a complex optical potential. For inclusive lepton-nucleus scattering (only the outgoing lepton detected), final-state interactions are treated via the Green's Function Approach (GFA), which uses a biorthogonal expansion in eigenfunctions of the optical potential and its Hermitian conjugate to conserve total flux.

## Key numbers
- For the \(^{16}\text{O}(\nu_\mu,\mu^-)\) reaction at \(E_\nu = 500\) and 1000 MeV with \(\theta_\mu = 30^\circ\), the GFA, RPWIA, and rROP cross sections are compared as a function of muon kinetic energy \(T_\mu\).
- For \(^{12}\text{C}(e,e')\) at incident electron energy 1 GeV and momentum transfer \(q = 500\) MeV/\(c\) and 1000 MeV/\(c\), GFA results with two optical potential parametrizations (EDAD1 and EDA2) are compared with RPWIA, rROP, and RMF calculations.
- At \(q = 1000\) MeV/\(c\), the RMF cross section shows a long tail extending towards higher \(\omega\), while GFA cross sections are higher than RMF in the maximum region, with the enhancement differing between the two optical potentials.
- The imaginary part of the optical potential has its maximum strength around 500 MeV and is sensitive to the parametrization of the relativistic optical potential.

## Context
This work extends relativistic models that were successfully tested against exclusive \((e,e'p)\) data (as reviewed in the textbook by Boffi et al.) and inclusive \((e,e')\) data to neutrino-nucleus scattering. The authors compare their GFA and RDWIA approaches with the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) model of the Madrid-Sevilla group (Caballero et al.) and with simpler approximations (RPWIA, rROP). The results enable more reliable predictions for neutrino oscillation experiments where nuclei are used as detectors, by quantifying the theoretical uncertainties from different FSI treatments.
