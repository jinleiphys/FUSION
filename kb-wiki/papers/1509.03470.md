# Chiral Three-Nucleon Interactions in Light Nuclei, Neutron-$α$ Scattering, and Neutron Matter

> We present quantum Monte Carlo calculations of light nuclei, neutron-$α$ scattering, and neutron matter using local two- and three-nucleon ($3N$) interactions derived from chiral effective field theory up to next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^2$LO). The two undetermined $3N$ low-energy couplings are fit to the $^4$He binding energy and, for the first time, to the spin-orbit splitting in the neutron-$α$ $P$-wave phase shifts. Furthermore, we investigate different choices of local $3N$-operator structures and find that chiral interactions at N$^2$LO are able to simultaneously reproduce the properties of $A=3,4,5$ systems and of neutron matter, in contrast to commonly used phenomenological $3N$ interactions.

## Key claim
Chiral three-nucleon interactions at next-to-next-to-leading order can simultaneously reproduce the properties of A=3,4,5 systems and neutron matter, but only for specific choices of local operator structures and cutoffs; the fit fails for the harder cutoff (R0=1.2 fm) with the V_D1 operator form.

## Method
The authors performed Green's function Monte Carlo calculations for light nuclei (A=3,4) and neutron-alpha scattering, and auxiliary-field diffusion Monte Carlo calculations for neutron matter, using local two- and three-nucleon interactions from chiral effective field theory at N2LO. The two undetermined three-nucleon low-energy couplings c_D and c_E were fit to the 4He binding energy and, for the first time, to the spin-orbit splitting in neutron-alpha P-wave phase shifts. Three different local operator structures for the three-nucleon contact interaction (V_Eτ, V_E1, V_E𝒫) and two forms for the one-pion-exchange-contact interaction (V_D1, V_D2) were investigated.

## Key numbers
- 4He binding energy fit to within approximately 0.03 MeV of the experimental value
- For R0=1.0 fm and V_Eτ, c_D ≈ 0.0 and c_E = -0.63 for both V_D1 and V_D2
- For R0=1.2 fm and V_D2 with V_Eτ, c_D = 3.5 and c_E = 0.09
- For R0=1.0 fm and V_D2 with V_E1, c_D = 0.5 and c_E = 0.62
- For R0=1.0 fm and V_D2 with V_E𝒫, c_D = 0.0 and c_E = 0.59
- At saturation density n0 ≈ 0.16 fm^-3, the difference in central energy per neutron between V_E1 and V_Eτ is approximately 2 MeV, with a total error band range of approximately 6.5 MeV

## Context
This work extends previous quantum Monte Carlo studies that were limited to two-nucleon interactions only (Gezerlis 2013, Lynn 2014, Tews 2015) or exploratory neutron matter with only long-range three-nucleon parts. It addresses the known failure of phenomenological three-nucleon models such as Urbana IX (Pudliner 1997) and Illinois (Pieper 2001), which tend to underbind nuclei and overbind neutron matter (Sarsa 2003, Maris 2013). The results enable future tests in light p-shell nuclei, medium-mass nuclei, and isospin-symmetric nuclear matter, while also highlighting the need for further investigation of regulator choices and contact-operator ambiguities.
