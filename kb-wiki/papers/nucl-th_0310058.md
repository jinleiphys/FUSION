# Electric and magnetic form factors of strange baryons

> Predictions for the electromagnetic form factors of the Lambda$, Sigma and Xi hyperons are presented. The numerical calculations are performed within the framework of the fully relativistic constituent-quark model developed by the Bonn group. The computed magnetic moments compare favorably with the experimentally known values. Most magnetic form factors G_M(Q^2) can be parametrized in terms of a dipole with cutoff masses ranging from 0.79 to 1.14 GeV.

## Key claim

The paper presents predictions for the electromagnetic form factors of strange baryons (Lambda, Sigma, Xi) computed within a fully relativistic constituent-quark model, claiming that most magnetic form factors can be parametrized as dipoles with cutoff masses between 0.79 and 1.14 GeV, but the electric form factors for neutral hyperons show a sign opposite to that predicted by the chiral quark/soliton model of Kim (1996).

## Method

The authors solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation for three-quark bound states using the instantaneous approximation, with a linear confinement potential and the 't Hooft instanton-induced interaction as the two-particle residual interaction. They computed current matrix elements from the resulting vertex functions and extracted Sachs electric and magnetic form factors for the octet baryons with strangeness, as well as transition form factors for the Sigma0 to Lambda transition.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic moment of Lambda: -0.61 mu_N (experimental: -0.613 +/- 0.004 mu_N)
- Magnetic moment of Sigma+: 2.47 mu_N (experimental: 2.458 +/- 0.010 mu_N)
- Magnetic moment of Sigma-: -0.99 mu_N (experimental: -1.160 +/- 0.025 mu_N)
- Magnetic moment of Sigma0: 0.73 mu_N (derived from isospin invariance)
- Magnetic moment of Xi0: -1.33 mu_N (experimental: -1.250 +/- 0.014 mu_N)
- Magnetic moment of Xi-: -0.57 mu_N (experimental: -0.6507 +/- 0.0025 mu_N)
- Dipole cutoff mass for Sigma0 magnetic form factor: 0.88 GeV
- Dipole cutoff mass for Lambda magnetic form factor: 1.14 GeV
- Dipole cutoff mass for Sigma+ magnetic form factor: 0.79 GeV
- Dipole cutoff mass for Xi0 magnetic form factor: 0.94 GeV
- Dipole cutoff mass for Xi- magnetic form factor: 1.03 GeV
- Electric mean square radius of Sigma-: 0.49 fm^2 (experimental values: 0.60 +/- 0.08 +/- 0.08 fm^2 from Fermilab and 0.91 +/- 0.32 +/- 0.40 fm^2 from CERN)
- Sigma- magnetic form factor changes sign at Q^2 approximately 1.6 GeV^2
- Sigma+ electric form factor changes sign at Q^2 approximately 1.1 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the Bonn group's relativistic constituent-quark model, previously applied to nonstrange baryons by Merten (2002), to the strange baryon sector. The predictions are compared with the chiral quark/soliton model of Kim (1996) and with heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory calculations by Kubis (2001). The computed form factors are intended to serve as input for isobar models of kaon photoproduction and electroproduction reactions being studied by the CLAS, LEPS, SAPHIR, and GRAAL collaborations.
