# Massive quark effects in two flavor color superconductors

> The high density effective theory formalism (HDET) is employed to describe high density QCD with two massive flavors (2SC). The gap equation is derived and explicitly solved for the gap parameter. The parameters associated to the pseudo Nambu-Goldstone boson of $U(1)_A$ are evaluated in the limit $μ\to\infty$ and $m/μ$ fixed. In particular we find for the velocity of the NG boson the relation $v^2=\sqrt{μ_1^2-m_1^2}\sqrt{μ_2^2-m_2^2}/3μ_1μ_2$.

## Key claim
The paper derives the gap equation and properties of the pseudo Nambu-Goldstone boson for two-flavor color superconductivity with massive quarks in the limit where the ratio m/mu is held fixed as mu goes to infinity, rather than the more common perturbative expansion in m/Delta. The key new result is a mass-dependent velocity for the pseudo NGB, v^2 = sqrt(mu_1^2 - m_1^2) sqrt(mu_2^2 - m_2^2) / (3 mu_1 mu_2), which reduces to 1/3 for massless quarks.

## Method
The authors employ the High Density Effective Theory (HDET) formalism, introducing velocity-dependent fields and projectors modified by a Cini-Touschek transformation to handle massive quarks. They derive the gap equation from a Schwinger-Dyson equation with a four-fermion interaction, solve it analytically, and compute the effective Lagrangian for the U(1)_A pseudo Nambu-Goldstone boson from one-loop diagrams including contributions from antiquark gap terms.

## Key numbers
- Gap parameter: Delta = sqrt((4 alpha + beta^2)/4) * delta / sinh( (3 Lambda^2 pi^2) / ((mu^2 - delta mu^2) alpha g^2) * sqrt(4 alpha + beta^2) / (2(1 + alpha)) ), where alpha = sqrt(1 - x_1^2) sqrt(1 - x_2^2) and beta = sqrt(1 - x_2^2) - sqrt(1 - x_1^2)
- For the approximate case with x_1 approx 0 and x = x_2: Delta approx (1 - delta mu/mu) * delta / sinh( (3 Lambda^2 pi^2) / (2 mu^2 g^2 (1 - 2 delta mu/mu)) )
- Pseudo NGB mass: m_sigma^2 = 4 Delta^2 (m_1 m_2 / mu^2) log(mu/Delta)
- Pseudo NGB velocity: v^2 = sqrt(1 - x_1^2) sqrt(1 - x_2^2) / 3 = |v_1| |v_2| / 3
- Decay constant: f_sigma^2 = (8 mu_1 mu_2 / pi^2) * (|v_1| |v_2|) / (|v_1| + |v_2|)
- The ratio delta mu / mu = (m_2^2 - m_1^2) / (4 mu^2) from the zero-momentum pairing condition

## Context
This work extends the study of massive quark effects in two-flavor color superconductors beyond the perturbative m/Delta expansion used by Son (1999), Rho (1999), Hong (1999), Beane (2000), and Schafer (2000). It generalizes the results of Ruster and Rischke (2002) by providing an analytic expression for the gap and allowing both quark masses to be nonzero. The new velocity result for the pseudo NGB goes beyond the m/Delta expansion and should hold at all orders in the m/mu expansion. The findings enable more realistic phenomenological analyses of compact star interiors where the strange quark mass may be comparable to the gap, as discussed by Bedaque (2001) and Kaplan (2001).
