# Study of multinucleon transfer mechanism in ${}^{160}\mathrm{Gd}+{}^{186}\mathrm{W}$ collisions in stochastic mean-field theory}

> Multinucleon transfer mechanism in collision of ${}^{160}\mathrm{Gd}+{}^{186}\mathrm{W}$ system is investigated in the framework of quantal transport description, based on the stochastic mean-field (SMF) theory. The SMF theory provides a microscopic approach for nuclear dynamics beyond the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) approach by including mean-field fluctuations. Cross-sections for the primary fragment production are determined in the quantal transport description and compared with the available data.

## Key claim

The stochastic mean-field (SMF) theory, combined with a quantal transport description, can reproduce primary fragment production cross-sections for the multinucleon transfer reaction ${}^{160}\mathrm{Gd}+{}^{186}\mathrm{W}$ without adjustable parameters, but only when the potential energy surface curvature parameters are extracted from TDHF drift paths and averaged over suitable time intervals during which the overlap between colliding nuclei is sufficiently large.

## Method

The authors performed TDHF calculations for the ${}^{160}\mathrm{Gd}+{}^{186}\mathrm{W}$ system at two center-of-mass energies (502.6 MeV and 461.9 MeV) across four collision geometries (tip-tip, tip-side, side-tip, side-side) using the SLy4d Skyrme energy density functional. From these TDHF results, they extracted mean drift coefficients and diffusion coefficients, then used Einstein relations in the overdamped limit to determine reduced curvature parameters of the di-nuclear potential energy surface along the iso-scalar and iso-vector directions. These curvature parameters were employed in coupled linear Langevin equations to compute neutron, proton, and mixed dispersions, from which correlated Gaussian fragment probability distributions were constructed for each initial orbital angular momentum.

## Key numbers

- At $E_{\text{c.m.}} = 502.6$ MeV, the iso-scalar reduced curvature parameter $\overline{\beta}$ ranges from 0.008 (tip-side geometry) to 0.022 (tip-tip geometry)
- At $E_{\text{c.m.}} = 502.6$ MeV, the iso-vector reduced curvature parameter $\alpha$ ranges from 0.113 (tip-tip geometry) to 0.143 (side-side geometry)
- The iso-scalar path makes an angle of approximately $\phi = 33^\circ$ with the horizontal neutron axis
- At $E_{\text{c.m.}} = 502.6$ MeV, the total kinetic energy loss (TKEL) for tip-tip geometry ranges from 85.3 MeV (at $\ell_i = 240\hbar$) to 245.7 MeV (at $\ell_i = 120\hbar$)
- At $E_{\text{c.m.}} = 461.9$ MeV, only tip-tip and tip-side geometries produced fragments within the detector acceptance range of $25^\circ-65^\circ$ in the laboratory frame

## Context

This work extends the quantal transport description based on SMF theory, previously applied to symmetric systems by Ayik et al. (2017, 2018, 2019) and Yilmaz et al. (2018, 2020), to an asymmetric deformed system exhibiting inverse quasi-fission behavior. The approach provides a microscopic alternative to phenomenological models such as the multidimensional Langevin model (Zagrebaev 2006, 2008, 2011; Karpov 2017; Saiko 2019, 2022), the di-nuclear system model (Feng 2009, 2017), and quantum molecular dynamics approaches (Zhao 2009, 2016; Wang 2016). The results enable future parameter-free predictions of primary fragment distributions for multinucleon transfer reactions involving deformed actinide targets, which may provide a more efficient mechanism for producing heavy neutron-rich isotopes than fusion, fission, or fragmentation reactions.
