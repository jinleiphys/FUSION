# Axial-vector form factors for the low lying octet baryons in the chiral quark constituent model

> We have calculated the axial-vector form factors of the low lying octet baryons ($N$, $Σ$, $Ξ$ and $Λ$) in the chiral constituent quark model ($χ$CQM). In particular, we have studied the implications of chiral symmetry breaking and SU(3) symmetry breaking for the singlet ($g^0_{A}$) and non-singlet ($g^3_{A}$ and $g^8_{A}$) axial-vector coupling constants expressed as combinations of the spin polarizations at zero momentum transfer. The conventional dipole form of parametrization has been used to analyse the $Q^2$ dependence of the axial-vector form factors ($G^0_{A}(Q^2)$, $G^3_{A}(Q^2)$ and $G^8_{A}(Q^2)$). The total strange singlet and non-singlet contents ($G_s^0(Q^2)$, $G_s^3(Q^2)$ and $G_s^8(Q^2)$) of the nucleon determining the strange quark contribution to the nucleon spin ($Δs$) have also been discussed.

## Key claim

The chiral constituent quark model (χCQM) provides a fairly good description of the axial-vector form factors of the low-lying octet baryons, but the singlet axial coupling constant g^0_A for the nucleon (0.519) still shows a large deviation from the experimental value (0.30 ± 0.06), indicating that the model captures only part of the spin structure.

## Method

The authors calculated the quark spin polarizations Δu, Δd, and Δs for the N, Σ, Ξ, and Λ baryons using the χCQM, which incorporates chiral symmetry breaking through Goldstone boson fluctuations and SU(3) symmetry breaking via parameters a, α, β, and ζ. Configuration mixing between the |56,0⁺⟩ and |70,0⁺⟩ states was included with a mixing angle φ = 20°. The axial-vector coupling constants g^0_A, g^3_A, and g^8_A at zero momentum transfer were expressed as combinations of the spin polarizations, and their Q² dependence was analyzed using a conventional dipole parametrization with axial mass M_A = 1.10 GeV from the MiniBooNE Collaboration.

## Key numbers

- For the nucleon: Δu_N = 0.904, Δd_N = -0.362, Δs_N = -0.023; g^0_{A,N} = 0.519, g^3_{A,N} = 1.266, g^8_{A,N} = 0.588
- For Σ: Δu_Σ = 0.881, Δd_Σ = -0.137, Δs_Σ = -0.252; g^0_{A,Σ} = 0.492, g^3_{A,Σ} = 1.018, g^8_{A,Σ} = 1.248
- For Ξ: Δu_Ξ = -0.329, Δd_Ξ = 0.00, Δs_Ξ = 1.109; g^0_{A,Ξ} = 0.780, g^3_{A,Ξ} = -0.329, g^8_{A,Ξ} = -2.547
- For Λ: Δu_Λ = 0.002, Δd_Λ = 0.002, Δs_Λ = 0.805; g^0_{A,Λ} = 0.809, g^3_{A,Λ} = 0.00, g^8_{A,Λ} = -1.606
- Experimental values for nucleon: Δu_N = 0.85 ± 0.05, Δd_N = -0.41 ± 0.05, Δs_N = -0.07 ± 0.05; g^3_{A,N} = 1.267 ± 0.0025, g^8_{A,N} = 0.588 ± 0.033
- Explicit strangeness contribution to nucleon spin polarizations at Q²=0: Δu_s = -0.092, Δd_s = 0.013; g^0_s = -0.102, g^3_s = -0.105, g^8_s = -0.033
- Axial mass used: M_A = 1.10^{+0.13}_{-0.15} GeV (MiniBooNE value)

## Context

This work extends the chiral constituent quark model (χCQM), previously applied to proton spin, magnetic moments, and charge radii by Cheng (1995), Song (1996), and Dahiya (2006), to the axial-vector form factors of the entire low-lying octet. The results improve upon the Naive Quark Model (NQM) predictions (which gave Δu_N = 1.33, Δd_N = -0.33, Δs_N = 0) and partially resolve the "proton spin crisis" from the EMC experiment (1988), though the singlet coupling g^0_A remains overestimated. The calculations enable comparison with ongoing experiments such as MiniBooNE, SAMPLE, G0, PVA4, and HAPPEX that probe strangeness content in the nucleon, and provide predictions for hyperon axial form factors that could be tested by future neutrino scattering experiments.
