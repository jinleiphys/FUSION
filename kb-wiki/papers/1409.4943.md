# Axial-vector form factors for the low lying octet baryons in the chiral quark constituent model

> We have calculated the axial-vector form factors of the low lying octet baryons ($N$, $Œ£$, $Œû$ and $Œõ$) in the chiral constituent quark model ($œá$CQM). In particular, we have studied the implications of chiral symmetry breaking and SU(3) symmetry breaking for the singlet ($g^0_{A}$) and non-singlet ($g^3_{A}$ and $g^8_{A}$) axial-vector coupling constants expressed as combinations of the spin polarizations at zero momentum transfer. The conventional dipole form of parametrization has been used to analyse the $Q^2$ dependence of the axial-vector form factors ($G^0_{A}(Q^2)$, $G^3_{A}(Q^2)$ and $G^8_{A}(Q^2)$). The total strange singlet and non-singlet contents ($G_s^0(Q^2)$, $G_s^3(Q^2)$ and $G_s^8(Q^2)$) of the nucleon determining the strange quark contribution to the nucleon spin ($Œîs$) have also been discussed.

## Key claim

The chiral constituent quark model (œáCQM) provides a fairly good description of the axial-vector form factors of the low-lying octet baryons, but the singlet axial coupling constant g^0_A for the nucleon (0.519) still shows a large deviation from the experimental value (0.30 ¬± 0.06), indicating that the model captures only part of the spin structure.

## Method

The authors calculated the quark spin polarizations Œîu, Œîd, and Œîs for the N, Œ£, Œû, and Œõ baryons using the œáCQM, which incorporates chiral symmetry breaking through Goldstone boson fluctuations and SU(3) symmetry breaking via parameters a, Œ±, Œ≤, and Œ∂. Configuration mixing between the |56,0‚Å∫‚ü© and |70,0‚Å∫‚ü© states was included with a mixing angle œÜ = 20¬∞. The axial-vector coupling constants g^0_A, g^3_A, and g^8_A at zero momentum transfer were expressed as combinations of the spin polarizations, and their Q¬≤ dependence was analyzed using a conventional dipole parametrization with axial mass M_A = 1.10 GeV from the MiniBooNE Collaboration.

## Context

This work extends the chiral constituent quark model (œáCQM), previously applied to proton spin, magnetic moments, and charge radii by Cheng (1995), Song (1996), and Dahiya (2006), to the axial-vector form factors of the entire low-lying octet. The results improve upon the Naive Quark Model (NQM) predictions (which gave Œîu_N = 1.33, Œîd_N = -0.33, Œîs_N = 0) and partially resolve the "proton spin crisis" from the EMC experiment (1988), though the singlet coupling g^0_A remains overestimated. The calculations enable comparison with ongoing experiments such as MiniBooNE, SAMPLE, G0, PVA4, and HAPPEX that probe strangeness content in the nucleon, and provide predictions for hyperon axial form factors that could be tested by future neutrino scattering experiments.
