# Effect of strong magnetic fields on the pasta phase structure

> The effect of strong magnetic fields on the properties of the pasta structures is calculated within a Thomas Fermi approach using relativistic mean field models to modulate stellar matter. It is shown how quantities such as the size of the clusters and Wigner-Seitz cells, the surface tension and the transition between configurations are affected. It is expected that these effects may give rise to large stresses in the pasta phase if the local magnetic field suffers fluctuations.

## Key claim

Strong magnetic fields in the range 10^16–10^18 G modify the pasta phase structure in the inner crust of neutron stars, but the effects are generally small (∼2% for free energy at 2×10^17 G) and depend on the filling of Landau levels; the authors caution that fields above ∼10^18 G are likely not allowed in the inner crust based on the scalar virial theorem and other constraints.

## Method

The authors used a Thomas-Fermi approach within a relativistic mean-field (RMF) framework, employing the NL3 and TM1 parametrizations, to describe inhomogeneous nuclear matter in a strong external magnetic field. They considered Wigner-Seitz cells with droplet, rod, slab, and bubble geometries at zero temperature, with fixed proton fractions Y_p = 0.3 and 0.1, and included Landau quantization for protons and electrons while neglecting the anomalous magnetic moment. The magnetic field was taken as homogeneous and oriented along the symmetry axis of each geometry.

## Key numbers

- Free energy per particle decreases by about 2% for B = 2×10^17 G compared to the field-free case (NL3, Y_p = 0.3)
- Surface energy increases by about 20% for B = 10^18 G and by about 2% for B = 2×10^17 G (slab configuration, ρ = 0.06 fm^−3, Y_p = 0.3)
- For bubble geometry at ρ = 0.095 fm^−3, surface energy can increase by up to 50% for B = 3×10^18 G (NL3, Y_p = 0.3)
- Transition density between droplet and rod geometries changes by up to 5% for fields between 10^17 G and 10^18 G, but by no more than 1.5% for fields up to 2×10^17 G
- Neutron skin thickness decreases by about 3–4% when the field increases from 5×10^16 G to 2×10^17 G (NL3, Y_p = 0.3)
- Crust-core transition density increases by about 1% for B = 2×10^17 G and up to 5% for B = 10^18 G (NL3, Y_p = 0.3)
- For Y_p = 0.1, increasing the magnetic field eliminates the neutron dripped gas completely and drives a transition to rod geometry

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the pasta phase within the Thomas-Fermi approximation using RMF models (Avancini et al. 2009, 2010; Grill et al. 2012) by incorporating the effect of strong magnetic fields, motivated by observations of magnetars with surface fields of 10^14–10^15 G (Duncan 1992; Usov 1992; Paczynski 1992). The authors build on earlier work by Chakrabarty (1997) on density-dependent magnetic field profiles and by Broderick et al. (2000) on Landau quantization effects in nuclear matter. The results suggest that magnetic field fluctuations in the pasta phase could generate internal stresses that may contribute to starquakes and bursting activity in magnetars, though the authors note that a more careful study with realistic symmetry energy and β-equilibrium conditions is needed.
