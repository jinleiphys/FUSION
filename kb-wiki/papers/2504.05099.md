# Finite density signatures of confining and chiral dynamics in QCD thermodynamics and fluctuations of conserved charges

> We evaluate thermodynamic observables such as pressure, baryon number, entropy and energy density, as well as the second and fourth order baryon number cumulants in the phase structure of QCD. The intertwined confinement and chiral dynamics is resolved within functional QCD, aiming for quantitative accuracy at larger densities. Specifically it is shown that the self-consistent resolution of the confining gluonic background is crucial in particular for even the qualitative properties of the cumulants. Our results are in quantitative agreement with lattice and functional QCD benchmarks at vanishing and small chemical potentials. Moreover, they offer novel insights in the dynamics at larger chemical potentials including the regime of the critical end point. A welcome by-product of this analysis is the computation of the Polyakov loop potential in finite density QCD, which, alongside the aforementioned observables, can be used as input and benchmark for effective theory computations at finite density.

## Key claim

The self-consistent resolution of the confining gluonic background is crucial for even the qualitative properties of the baryon number cumulants, not just quantitative accuracy; without it, the kurtosis does not approach unity at low temperatures, failing to describe the hadronic phase.

## Method

The authors used a functional QCD approach based on Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) with a background field method that decomposes the gauge field into a background and a dynamical fluctuation field. They computed quark and gluon propagators from coupled DSEs in the miniDSE scheme, using vacuum 2+1 flavour QCD input from precision DSE computations and fRG-compatible renormalisation, then evaluated thermodynamic observables and baryon number cumulants on the quantum equations of motion with a non-trivial temporal gauge field background.

## Key numbers

- Chiral crossover temperature at vanishing chemical potential: T_c(0) = 157 MeV
- Curvature of the chiral crossover line at mu_B = 0: kappa_2 = 0.0153
- Critical end point location: (T_CEP, mu_B_CEP) = (103, 660) MeV
- Confinement-deconfinement crossover temperature at vanishing chemical potential: T_L(0) = T_barL(0) = 152 MeV
- Quantitative reliability of the approximation is limited to mu_B/T less than or approximately equal to 4

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent functional QCD approach developed by Gao et al. (2020, 2021) and Lu et al. (2023) to compute thermodynamic observables and fluctuations of conserved charges at finite density. It provides the first quantitative DSE computation of baryon number cumulants, which are crucial for interpreting experimental data from CBM at FAIR and CEE+ at HIAF at collision energies between 3 and 5 GeV. The results agree with lattice QCD benchmarks at vanishing chemical potential from Borsanyi et al. (2010) and with functional QCD benchmarks from Fu et al. (2019), Gao et al. (2020), and Gunkel et al. (2021), while offering novel insights into the dynamics around the critical end point regime.
