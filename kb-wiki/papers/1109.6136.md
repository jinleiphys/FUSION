# Effect of isospin dependence of cross section on symmetric and neutron rich systems

> We aim to explore the effect of isospin dependence of cross section on symmetric and neutron rich system. We also aim to explore whether the analysis is affacted if one discusses in terms of "$E_{bal}$ as a function of N/Z or N/A" of the system.

## Key claim

The N/Z dependence of balance energy \(E_{bal}\) is highly sensitive to symmetry energy, but the effect of isospin dependence of the nucleon-nucleon cross section (setting \(\sigma_{np} = 3\sigma_{nn} = 3\sigma_{pp}\)) on \(E_{bal}\) decreases as the N/Z of the system increases, because neutron-neutron collision pairs dominate over neutron-proton pairs in neutron-rich systems.

## Method

The author used the Isospin-dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) model to simulate thousands of events for Ca+Ca isotopic systems with N/Z ranging from 1.0 to 2.0 (N/A from 0.5 to 0.67) at incident energies around the balance energy in steps of 10 MeV/nucleon for semicentral collisions (impact parameter range 0.2–0.4). The directed transverse momentum \(\langle p_x^{dir}\rangle\) was computed as a function of incident energy to extract the balance energy \(E_{bal}\) for each system. To isolate the effect of isospin-dependent cross sections, the symmetry energy was switched off and the cross section was made isospin independent (\(\sigma_{np} = \sigma_{nn}\)) for the two extreme N/Z values.

## Key numbers

- For the linear fit of \(E_{bal}\) as a function of N/A, the slope \(m = -191\) when symmetry energy proportional to \(\rho/\rho_0\) (F\(_1\)(u)) is included, and \(m = -68\) when symmetry energy is omitted (F\(_4\)).
- The percentage difference \(\Delta E_{bal}\%\) between calculations with and without symmetry energy is about 65% for both the N/Z and N/A representations.
- When symmetry energy is included, the magnitude of the slope \(|m|\) for \(E_{bal}\) versus N/Z increases by a factor of 3 compared to the case without symmetry energy.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Sood (2011) on the disappearance of flow (balance energy) for isotopic Ca chains, which showed that the N/Z dependence of \(E_{bal}\) is sensitive to both the symmetry energy and its density dependence. It also builds on findings by Gautam (2010) that collective transverse flow and its disappearance are affected by Coulomb repulsion, symmetry energy, and isospin dependence of the cross section, with Coulomb repulsion dominating over symmetry energy. The results enable future studies to disentangle the contributions of symmetry energy and isospin-dependent cross sections in heavy-ion collisions using neutron-rich beams.
