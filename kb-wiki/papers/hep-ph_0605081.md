# BCS/BEC crossover in Quark Matter and Evolution of its Static and Dynamic properties

> We study the evolution of dynamic properties of the BCS/BEC (Bose-Einstein Condensate) crossover in a relativistic superfluid as well as its thermodynamics. We put particular focus on the change in the soft mode dynamics throughout the crossover, and find that three different effective theories describe it; these are, the time-dependent Ginzburg-Landau (TDGL) theory in the BCS regime, the Gross-Pitaevskii (GP) theory in the BEC regime, and the relativistic Gross-Pitaevskii (RGP) equation in the relativistic BEC (RBEC) regime. Based on these effective theories, we discuss how the physical nature of soft mode changes in the crossover. We also discuss some fluid-dynamic aspects of the crossover using these effective theories with particular focus on the shear viscosity. In addition to the study of soft modes, we show that the ``quantum fluctuation'' is present in the relativistic fermion system, which is in contrast to the usual Nozi`eres--Schmit-Rink (NSR) theory. We clarify the physical meaning of the quantum fluctuation, and find that it drastically increases the critical temperature in the weak coupling BCS regime.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the BCS/BEC crossover in relativistic quark matter is described by three distinct effective theories depending on the coupling regime, time-dependent Ginzburg-Landau (TDGL) in the BCS regime, Gross-Pitaevskii (GP) in the BEC regime, and relativistic Gross-Pitaevskii (RGP) in the relativistic BEC (RBEC) regime, and that a "quantum fluctuation" term, absent in the standard nonrelativistic Nozieres-Schmitt-Rink (NSR) theory, drastically increases the critical temperature in the weak coupling BCS regime.

## Method

The author extends the relativistic Nozieres-Schmitt-Rink (NSR) framework to SU(2) flavor and SU(3) color fermions interacting via a four-Fermi contact interaction inspired by one-gluon exchange, formulating the thermodynamic potential up to Gaussian fluctuations about the mean-field approximation. The dynamic pair susceptibility is computed at one-loop level, and effective theories for the soft modes are derived by analyzing the analytic structure of the pair correlation function across the crossover. The shear viscosity is then discussed based on these effective theories.

## Key numbers

* The shear viscosity to entropy ratio in the strongly coupled QGP is close to the minimum bound of 1/(4π) speculated by Kovtun, Son, and Starinets (2004), with the lattice value for purely gluonic plasma being a factor 2-3 smaller than the perturbative estimate of Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2000).
* The partonic cross section inferred from RHIC data is almost 50 times larger than its perturbative estimate.
* The critical coupling for zero binding (unitary limit) is given by 1/G₀ = 0, corresponding to 1/a_s = 0.
* The bound state binding energy in the relativistic case is given by 1/(m a_s²), with the pole energy approximated as |ω| ≈ 2m - 1/(m a_s²) = 2m - 16π²/(m³ G_R²) for loosely bound states.

## Context

This work extends the BCS/BEC crossover scenario previously conjectured for color superconductivity by Matsuzaki (1999) and Abuki (2001), and builds on the first explicit relativistic investigation by Nishida (2005) which showed a two-step crossover including a relativistic BEC (RBEC) regime. The paper aims to connect two apparently independent aspects of the quark-gluon plasma, the survival of bound states above T_c and the near-perfect fluidity observed at RHIC, by studying how transport properties evolve across the crossover, complementing earlier work on the unitary Fermi gas by Thomas, Grimm, and others.
