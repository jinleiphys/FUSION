# A glimpse into an effective world

> Our contribution aims to celebrate the immeasurable contribution that Tom Kuo has provided to the understanding of the structure of atomic nuclei, and also of the infinite nuclear matter, in terms of the fundamental principles governing the realistic nuclear potential. The authors want to testify Tom Kuo's heritage and impact on their approach to the study of nuclear systems by reviewing some recent findings on the role of the two-body component of shell-model effective $β$-decay operators. The focus is spotted on the so-called Pauli-blocking effect, that plays a non-negligible role in nuclei characterized by a large number of valence nucleons.

## Key claim

The two-body component of shell-model effective Gamow-Teller decay operators provides a non-negligible correction to the one-body quenching factor in nuclei with many valence nucleons, softening the quenching by up to 50% for the total GT strength and up to 35% for double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements, contrary to the assumption of a universal quenching factor.

## Method

The authors start from the high-precision CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential, renormalized via the \(V_{low-k}\) procedure to remove repulsive high-momentum components. They derive a shell-model effective Hamiltonian and effective Gamow-Teller decay operators using the Kuo-Lee-Ratcliff folded-diagram expansion and the Suzuki-Okamoto method, including one-body and two-body diagrams up to second order in perturbation theory. The two-body contributions to the effective decay operator are calculated in an angular-momentum coupled representation following Kuo's formalism, and the nuclear matrix element for the two-neutrino double-beta decay of \(^{100}\)Mo is computed using the Lanczos strength-function method.

## Key numbers

- Total GT\(^-\) strength for \(^{100}\)Mo: 1.082 (bare operator), 0.274 (effective one-body operator), 0.611 (effective one- plus two-body operator)
- Quenching factor for the one-body effective GT\(^+\) operator: \(q = 0.454\) (for \(\pi 0g_{9/2} \rightarrow \nu 0g_{7/2}\))
- Quenching factor for the one-body effective GT\(^-\) operator: \(q = 0.503\) (for \(\nu 0g_{9/2} \rightarrow \pi 0g_{7/2}\))
- Experimental \(^{100}\)Mo \(\rightarrow\) \(^{100}\)Ru \(0^+_1 \rightarrow 0^+_1\) nuclear matrix element: \(0.224 \pm 0.002\) MeV\(^{-1}\); calculated values: 0.896 (bare), 0.205 (one-body effective), 0.374 (one- plus two-body effective)
- Experimental \(^{100}\)Mo \(\rightarrow\) \(^{100}\)Ru \(0^+_1 \rightarrow 0^+_2\) nuclear matrix element: \(0.182 \pm 0.006\) MeV\(^{-1}\); calculated values: 0.479 (bare), 0.109 (one-body effective), 0.135 (one- plus two-body effective)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous shell-model study of \(^{100}\)Mo double-beta decay (Coraggio 2022) by including two-body components of the effective Gamow-Teller operator, following the formalism for effective decay operators developed by Suzuki and Okamoto (1995) and the diagrammatic methods of Kuo (1981). The results contradict the notion of a universal quenching factor for Gamow-Teller transitions, showing that the quenching depends on the model space and number of valence nucleons, consistent with earlier findings by Ellis (1977) on the reduction of effective charges in many-valence-nucleon systems. This enables more accurate predictions for double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements in heavy nuclei with many valence particles.
