# Quantum computation of SU(2) lattice gauge theory with continuous variables

> We present a quantum computational framework for SU(2) lattice gauge theory, leveraging continuous variables instead of discrete qubits to represent the infinite-dimensional Hilbert space of the gauge fields. We consider a ladder as well as a two-dimensional grid of plaquettes, detailing the use of gauge fixing to reduce the degrees of freedom and simplify the Hamiltonian. We demonstrate how the system dynamics, ground states, and energy gaps can be computed using the continuous-variable approach to quantum computing. Our results indicate that it is feasible to study non-Abelian gauge theories with continuous variables, providing new avenues for understanding the real-time dynamics of quantum field theories.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that continuous-variable quantum computing (using qumodes) can feasibly simulate non-Abelian SU(2) lattice gauge theories, but only after gauge fixing to the maximal tree gauge reduces the degrees of freedom and simplifies the Hamiltonian; the approach is validated for small systems (one plaquette and two plaquettes) and for a two-dimensional grid, with the caveat that the coupled cluster ansatz used for ground and excited states is variational and approximate for intermediate coupling strengths.

## Method

The authors map the SU(2) lattice gauge theory Hamiltonian, expressed in terms of unit quaternions and angular momentum operators, onto continuous variables by working in the maximal tree gauge, which fixes all local gauge symmetries except a global one. They then apply a coupled cluster variational ansatz (e.g., \(\braket{\bm{x} | \psi_0 (\alpha)} \propto e^{\alpha W[C]}\) for the ground state) to compute ground state energies and energy gaps for a single plaquette, a ladder of two plaquettes, and a two-dimensional grid of plaquettes. The results are compared with exact diagonalization in the electric basis (truncated at various \(j_{\text{max}}\)) and with analytic solutions in the weak-coupling (large \(\lambda\)) limit where the Hamiltonian reduces to coupled harmonic oscillators.

## Key numbers

* Single plaquette ground state energy in the weak-coupling limit: \(E_0 = \frac{3}{2} \sqrt{\lambda} + \mathcal{O}(\lambda^0)\).
* Single plaquette energy gap in the weak-coupling limit: \(\Delta E = 2\sqrt{\lambda} + \mathcal{O}(\lambda^0)\).
* Two-plaquette ladder ground state energy in the weak-coupling limit: \(E_0 = 2.976\sqrt{\lambda} + \mathcal{O}(\lambda^0)\).
* Two-plaquette ladder energy gap in the weak-coupling limit: \(\Delta E = \sqrt{3\lambda} + \mathcal{O}(\lambda^0)\).
* For the single plaquette at \(\lambda = 200\), exact diagonalization requires \(j_{\text{max}} = 7\) to converge to the exact result, while \(j_{\text{max}} = 1\) suffices at \(\lambda = 1\).
* Two-plaquette ladder normal mode frequencies: \(\omega_- = \frac{\sqrt{3\lambda}}{2}\) and \(\omega_+ = \frac{\sqrt{5\lambda}}{2}\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous continuous-variable formulation of the O(3) model (Jha 2023) to a non-Abelian gauge theory, specifically SU(2) lattice gauge theory in the Hamiltonian formulation of Kogut and Susskind (1975). It contrasts with qubit-based approaches that require truncating the infinite-dimensional Hilbert space of gauge fields (e.g., Assi 2024) and with the electric basis truncation used in exact diagonalization studies (e.g., Klco 2019, Rahman 2021). The gauge-fixing procedure follows Creutz (1976) and recent applications by Bauer (2023) and others. The results enable future quantum simulations of real-time dynamics and larger lattices using continuous-variable quantum computers, with the ultimate goal of extending to QCD.
