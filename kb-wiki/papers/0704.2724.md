# Neutrino beams as a probe of the nuclear isospin and spin-isospin excitations

> We explore the possibility of performing nuclear structure studies using low energy neutrino beams. In particular, low energy beta-beams and conventional sources (muon decay-at-rest) are considered. We present results on the total charged-current as well as flux-averaged cross sections associated to electron (anti)-neutrino scattering on oxygen, iron, molybdenum and lead, as typical examples. It is shown that by using neutrinos from low energy beta-beams, information on forbidden states, in particular the spin-dipole, could be extracted.

## Key claim

By varying the Lorentz boost factor (γ) of low-energy beta-beams, the contribution of forbidden states (particularly the spin-dipole, 1⁻ and 2⁻) to the charged-current neutrino-nucleus cross section can become as important as the Isobaric Analogue State (IAS) and Gamow-Teller (GT) transitions, enabling nuclear structure studies of these higher multipoles.

## Method

The authors performed microscopic calculations of charged-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections using a self-consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) plus random-phase-approximation (RPA) or quasiparticle RPA (QRPA) approach, with Skyrme-type effective interactions and pairing correlations included via the BCS approximation. Total cross sections as a function of neutrino energy and flux-averaged cross sections were computed for oxygen-16, iron-56, molybdenum-100, and lead-208, considering both low-energy beta-beam fluxes (from boosted ¹⁸Ne and ⁶He ions at γ = 6, 10, 14) and the conventional muon decay-at-rest (DAR) Michel spectrum.

## Key numbers

- For neutrino-iron at 60 MeV neutrino energy, the total cross section is 1.67 × 10⁻⁴² cm² (for ν-⁵⁶Fe) and 3.53 × 10⁻⁴³ cm² (for ν̄-⁵⁶Fe).
- For neutrino-lead at 100 MeV, the total cross section is 4.16 × 10⁻⁴¹ cm² (ν-²⁰⁸Pb) and 3.67 × 10⁻⁴³ cm² (ν̄-²⁰⁸Pb).
- The measured muon decay-at-rest flux-averaged cross section for iron is 256 ± 108 ± 43 (10⁻⁴² cm²); the authors' calculated value is 352 × 10⁻⁴² cm².
- For neutrino-iron at γ = 6, the flux-averaged cross section is dominated by 1⁺ (73.1%) and 0⁺ (22.6%) states; at γ = 14, the 1⁺ contribution drops to 39.0% while 1⁻ reaches 23.7% and 2⁻ reaches 15.8%.
- For neutrino-oxygen at γ = 6, the 1⁻ and 2⁻ states contribute 34.1% and 63.8% respectively to the flux-averaged cross section; at γ = 14, these become 42.4% and 44.8%.

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Volpe (2003) to use low-energy beta-beams for nuclear structure studies, and provides detailed microscopic calculations for four nuclei of interest to neutrino detectors and supernova observatories (oxygen for water Cherenkov detectors like Super-Kamiokande, iron for proposed detectors, molybdenum and lead for detectors under study). The results address the open question of whether quenching of forbidden states is necessary, which has been debated from muon capture studies by Zinner (2006) and charge-exchange experiments by Yako (2006), and could enable improved parametrization of effective interactions in microscopic nuclear models.
