# CE$ν$NS Search with Cryogenic Sapphire Detectors at MINER: Results from the TRIGA reactor data and Future Sensitivity at HFIR

> We report on a search for coherent elastic neutrino--nucleus scattering (CE$ν$NS) using cryogenic sapphire (Al$_2$O$_3$) detectors deployed at the Mitchell Institute Neutrino Experiment at Reactor (MINER), located near the 1~MW$_\text{th}$ TRIGA research reactor at Texas A\&M University. The experiment operated with a primary detector mass of 72~g and achieved a baseline energy resolution of $\sim 40$~eV. Using exposures of 158~g-days (reactor-on) and 381~g-days (reactor-off), we performed a statistical background subtraction in the energy region of 0.25--3~keV. A GEANT4 simulation has been performed to understand the reactor-correlated background present in the data and it agrees with our observations. The resulting best-fit ratio of the observed CE$ν$NS rate to the Standard Model prediction after rejecting the reactor induced background from the data with the help of simulation, is $ρ= 0.26\pm 1534.74~\mathrm{(stat)} \pm 0.05~\mathrm{(sys)}$ with a significance of $0.007 \pm 0.022~\mathrm{(stat)} \pm 0.001~\mathrm{(sys)}$. This low significance indicates a high background rate at low energies. To have enhanced sensitivity, the MINER collaboration plans to relocate the experiment to the 85~MW$_\text{th}$ High Flux Isotope Reactor (HFIR) at Oak Ridge National Laboratory (ORNL). With improved shielding, increased detector mass, and higher antineutrino flux, the upgraded setup is projected to achieve a 3$σ$ CE$ν$NS detection within 30~kg$\cdot$days of exposure.

## Key claim

The MINER collaboration reports a search for coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (CEvNS) using cryogenic sapphire detectors at a 1 MWth TRIGA reactor, but the resulting best-fit ratio of observed to Standard Model predicted rate is \(\rho = 0.26 \pm 1534.74~\mathrm{(stat)} \pm 0.05~\mathrm{(sys)}\) with a significance of only \(0.007 \pm 0.022~\mathrm{(stat)} \pm 0.001~\mathrm{(sys)}\), meaning no statistically significant detection was achieved due to high background rates at low energies.

## Method

The experiment deployed a 72 g cryogenic sapphire (Al2O3) primary detector with two active veto detectors in a dilution refrigerator at approximately 10 mK, located about 4 meters from the TRIGA reactor core. Data were collected during reactor-on (158 g-days) and reactor-off (381 g-days) periods, and a statistical background subtraction was performed in the 0.25-3 keV energy region after applying single-scatter selection, fiducial volume cuts, and efficiency corrections. A GEANT4 simulation of the reactor-induced gamma and neutron backgrounds was used to model the reactor-correlated background and subtract it from the residual on-minus-off spectrum before performing a chi-squared minimization to extract the signal scaling factor.

## Key numbers

- Baseline energy resolution of the primary detector: 40-45 eV (1-sigma) across sub-datasets
- Exposures: 158 g-days (reactor-on) and 381 g-days (reactor-off)
- Best-fit ratio of observed CEvNS rate to Standard Model prediction: \(\rho = 0.26 \pm 1534.74~\mathrm{(stat)} \pm 0.05~\mathrm{(sys)}\)
- Significance of the result: \(0.007 \pm 0.022~\mathrm{(stat)} \pm 0.001~\mathrm{(sys)}\)
- Measured integrated signal-plus-background rate: \(R_\mathrm{S+B} \approx 341 \pm 229~\mathrm{(stat)} \pm 23~\mathrm{(sys)}~\mathrm{kg}^{-1}\mathrm{day}^{-1}\)
- Projected sensitivity at HFIR: 3-sigma CEvNS detection within 30 kg-days of exposure at the 85 MWth High Flux Isotope Reactor

## Context

This work extends the search for CEvNS from reactor antineutrinos, following the first observation by the COHERENT Collaboration (2017) using spallation neutrons, and joins other reactor-based efforts including CONUS, TEXONO, CONNIE, NUCLEUS, Ricochet, and nuGEN. The MINER experiment demonstrates the feasibility of cryogenic sapphire detectors for low-threshold neutrino detection but highlights the challenge of low-energy backgrounds that currently prevent a statistically significant observation. The planned relocation to the High Flux Isotope Reactor (HFIR) at Oak Ridge National Laboratory, with improved shielding, larger detector mass, and higher antineutrino flux, is projected to achieve a definitive 3-sigma detection of CEvNS.
