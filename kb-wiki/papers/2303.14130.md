# A tight universal relation between the shape eccentricity and the moment of inertia for rotating neutron stars

> Universal relations that are insensitive to the equation of state (EoS) are useful in reducing the parameter space when measuring global quantities of neutron stars (NSs). In this paper, we reveal a new universal relation that connects the eccentricity to the radius and moment of inertia of rotating NSs. We demonstrate that the universality of this relation holds for both conventional NSs and bare quark stars (QSs) in the slow rotation approximation, albeit with different relations. The maximum relative deviation is approximately $1\%$ for conventional NSs and $0.1\%$ for QSs. Additionally, we show that the universality still exists for fast-rotating NSs if we use the dimensionless spin to characterize their rotation. The new universal relation will be a valuable tool to reduce the number of parameters used to describe the shape and multipoles of rotating NSs, and it may also be used to infer the eccentricity or moment of inertia of NSs in future X-ray observations.

## Key claim

The paper claims a new universal relation connecting the surface eccentricity of rotating neutron stars to their radius and moment of inertia, which holds to within approximately 1% for conventional neutron stars and 0.1% for bare quark stars in the slow-rotation approximation, and remains valid for fast rotation when using the dimensionless spin parameter rather than the spin frequency.

## Method

The authors constructed slowly rotating neutron star models using the Hartle-Thorne perturbative expansion to second order in spin, solving the Einstein equations for eight conventional neutron star equations of state and six MIT bag model quark star equations of state. For fast rotation, they used the RNS code to compute stationary axisymmetric spacetimes in quasi-isotropic coordinates, generating models with dimensionless spin parameters up to 0.6 for neutron stars and 0.8 for quark stars. They then fitted polynomial relations between the ratio of eccentricity to the product of radius and angular frequency and the dimensionless moment of inertia.

## Key numbers

- Maximum relative deviation of the universal relation for slowly rotating conventional neutron stars: approximately 1%
- Maximum relative deviation for slowly rotating bare quark stars: approximately 0.1%
- For neutron stars with mass greater than approximately 1 solar mass, the dimensionless moment of inertia is less than approximately 30, and the simpler fitting formula gives relative deviation less than approximately 1%
- For quark stars with mass greater than approximately 1 solar mass, the dimensionless moment of inertia is less than approximately 20, and the simpler fitting formula gives relative deviation less than approximately 0.3%
- For fast rotating neutron stars with dimensionless spin chi = 0.6, the relative deviation from the universal relation is on the order of 0.3%
- The maximum dimensionless spin parameter for neutron stars rotating at the Keplerian frequency is approximately 0.7 for various equations of state

## Context

This work extends the family of equation-of-state-insensitive universal relations for neutron stars, following the discovery of the I-Love-Q relation by Yagi (2013) and the multipole moment relations by Pappas (2013) and Yagi (2014). It improves upon the eccentricity-compactness relation of Baubock (2013) by incorporating the moment of inertia as an additional parameter, yielding a much tighter relation. The new relation can be combined with the I-Love relation to test gravity theories and may be used to infer the eccentricity or moment of inertia of neutron stars from future X-ray observations of pulsar pulse profiles.
