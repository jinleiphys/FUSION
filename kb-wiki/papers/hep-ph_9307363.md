# Lepton pairs from thermal mesons

> We study the net dielectron production rates from an ensemble of thermal mesons, using an effective Lagrangian to model their interaction. The coupling between the electromagnetic and the hadronic sectors is done through the vector meson dominance approach. For the first time, a complete set of light mesons is considered. We include contributions from decays of the type V~(PS)~$\rightarrow$~PS~(V)~+~$e^+~e^-$, where V is a vector meson and PS is a pseudoscalar, as well as those from binary reactions PS~+~PS, V~+~V, and V~+~PS~$\rightarrow~e^+e^-$. Direct decays of the type V~$\rightarrow~e^+ e^-$ are included and shown to be important. We find that the dielectron invariant mass spectrum naturally divides in distinct regions: in the low mass domain the decays from vector and pseudoscalar mesons form the dominant contribution. The pion--pion annihilation and direct decays then pick up and form the leading signal in an invariant mass region that includes the $ρ- ω$ complex and extends up to the $ϕ$. Above invariant mass $M\ \approx$~1~GeV other two-body reactions take over as the prominent mechanisms for lepton pair generation. These facts will have quantitative bearing on the eventual identification of the quark--gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The dielectron invariant mass spectrum from a thermal meson gas naturally divides into distinct regions, with decays from vector and pseudoscalar mesons dominating at low masses, pion-pion annihilation and direct decays dominating through the rho-omega complex up to the phi, and other two-body reactions taking over above approximately 1 GeV; the authors state that the "usual" pion results for lepton pair production hold "rather poorly" over all invariant mass regions considered, with the total rate at M ≈ 1 GeV exceeding the pion gas approximation by a factor of approximately 3.

## Method

The authors calculated thermal dielectron production rates from an ensemble of mesons (π, η, ρ, ω, η′, φ, K, K*) using relativistic kinetic theory with an effective Lagrangian to model meson interactions. The coupling between electromagnetic and hadronic sectors was implemented through the vector meson dominance (VMD) approach, with coupling constants calibrated to reproduce experimental radiative decay widths for processes such as ω → π⁰γ, ρ → πγ, and φ → ηγ. They included contributions from decays V(PS) → PS(V) + e⁺e⁻, binary reactions PS+PS, V+V, and V+PS → e⁺e⁻, and direct decays V → e⁺e⁻, evaluating the rates at temperatures of 100, 150, and 200 MeV using Monte Carlo integration methods.

## Key numbers

- At T = 150 MeV, the total dielectron rate at M ≈ 1 GeV exceeds the pion gas approximation result by a factor of approximately 3.
- At M = 1.5 GeV and T = 150 MeV, the total rate differs from the pion-only result by a little more than an order of magnitude.
- The dominant decay contribution up to invariant mass ≈ 0.65 GeV is from ω → π⁰ e⁺e⁻, with ρ → π e⁺e⁻ as the next-to-leading contribution.
- Above threshold, the sum of V+V processes outshines the PS+PS contributions by roughly an order of magnitude at T = 150 MeV.
- The Landau-Pomeranchuk correction to pion-pion bremsstrahlung is approximately a factor of 2 for low invariant masses at T = 150 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous thermal dilepton calculations that were typically restricted to a pure pion gas approximation, such as the annihilation channel studied by Kajantie et al. (1986), and thermal meson decay contributions considered by Gudima, Titov, and Toneev (1992) and Koch (1993). The authors explicitly compare their results to the pion gas approximation and note that their calculated rates for M ≳ 1 GeV are approximately one order of magnitude larger than conventional meson background calculations, which has important implications for identifying a quark-gluon plasma signal in the invariant mass window between the φ and J/ψ mesons. The work enables more realistic background estimates for heavy-ion collision experiments and motivates further study of three-body processes and the A₁ meson contribution to thermal dilepton spectra.
