# Quantum vortices in fermionic superfluids: from ultracold atoms to neutron stars

> Superfluid dilute neutron matter and ultracold gas, close to the unitary regime, exhibit several similarities. Therefore, to a certain extent, fermionic ultracold gases may serve as emulators of dilute neutron matter, which forms the inner crust of neutron stars and is not directly accessed experimentally. Quantum vortices are one of the most significant properties of neutron superfluid, essential for comprehending neutron stars' dynamics. The structure and dynamics of quantum vortices as a function of pairing correlations' strength are being investigated experimentally and theoretically in ultracold gases. Certain aspects of these studies are relevant to neutron stars. We provide an overview of the characteristics of quantum vortices in s-wave-type fermionic and electrically neutral superfluids. The main focus is on the dynamics of fermionic vortices and their intrinsic structure.

## Key claim

Fermionic ultracold gases can serve as emulators of dilute neutron matter in the inner crust of neutron stars because both systems are electrically neutral and close to the unitary regime, but the analogy is limited to s-wave-type superfluids and the correspondence holds only to a certain extent, as the structure and dynamics of quantum vortices depend on the strength of pairing correlations.

## Method

The authors review the Bogoliubov-de Gennes (BdG) formalism for spin-imbalanced fermionic superfluids, presenting the equations for quasiparticle wavefunctions and the pairing field self-consistency condition. They analyze the Andreev approximation to derive quantization conditions for in-gap states in vortex cores, and present numerical solutions of Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations on a 3D spatial lattice using the BSk energy density functional for pure neutron matter. The review also examines the specific heat modification due to vortex flow using local density approximation, and the effective mass of quasiparticles along the vortex line from scattering kinematics.

## Key numbers

- In the inner crust of a neutron star, the pairing gap is within the interval (1.2, 1.6) MeV where s-wave pairing is strongest.
- Vortex core radii in the inner crust do not exceed 12 fm, while typical lattice constants range between 40 and 70 fm for nuclear densities (0.006 - 0.034) fm^{-3}.
- The surface vortex density in the neutron star crust is approximately 10^{-21} fm^{-2}, but local increases to about 10^{-2} fm^{-2} lead to approximately twice larger specific heat at T ≈ 0.1 MeV.
- The minimal magnetic field required to produce spin excitation in the vortex core is estimated to range between about 10^{15} - 10^{16} G, more than an order of magnitude smaller than the field needed to destroy superfluidity (B ≥ 10^{17} G).
- At unitarity, the ratio Δ/εF ≈ 0.5, compared to the deep BCS limit where Δ/εF ∝ exp(-π/2|a|kF).

## Context

This review extends the line of work connecting ultracold atomic gas experiments (Zwierlein 2005) to neutron star physics, building on earlier theoretical studies of vortex structure in neutron matter using Density Functional Theory (Pęcak 2021) and the Caroli-De Gennes-Matricon states (Caroli 1964) for fermionic vortex cores. The work enables better constraints for models of neutron star glitches and vortex dynamics, particularly by showing how ultracold gas experiments can test predictions for vortex core structure and the effects of spin polarization, which are relevant to magnetar conditions (Kaspi 2017).
