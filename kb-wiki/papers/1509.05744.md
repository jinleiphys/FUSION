# Extended Skyrme Equation of State in asymmetric nuclear matter

> We present a new equation of state for infinite systems (symmetric, asymmetric and neutron matter) based on an extended Skyrme functional constrained by microscopic Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone results. The resulting equation of state reproduces with very good accuracy the main features of microscopic calculations and it is compatible with recent measurements of two times Solar-mass neutron stars. We provide all necessary analytical expressions to facilitate a quick numerical implementation of quantities of astrophysical interest.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new equation of state (EoS) for infinite nuclear matter based on an *extended* Skyrme functional that includes up to 6th-order derivative terms, constrained by Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone (BBG) microscopic calculations. The authors claim the resulting EoS reproduces BBG results with very good accuracy and is compatible with two-solar-mass neutron star observations, but the fit prioritizes pure neutron matter (PNM) data at high density, causing deviations in the S=0 spin-isospin channels above n ≈ 0.6 fm⁻³.

## Method

The authors fitted the parameters of an extended Skyrme interaction (including terms up to 6th order in gradient expansion) to BBG results from Baldo et al. (1997) for symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) and pure neutron matter (PNM) EoS, plus the spin-isospin (S,T) decomposition of the potential energy in SNM. A density-dependent term (t₃, x₃, α=1/6) was included to avoid an unrealistically low Landau effective mass, and the 6th-order parameters were adjusted specifically to correct the PNM EoS at high density while keeping the SNM fit quality.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density n₀ = 0.169 fm⁻³ with binding energy E/A = -17.02 MeV
- Incompressibility K = 231 MeV at saturation density
- Symmetry energy at saturation J = 33.8 MeV
- Slope parameter of symmetry energy L = 64.5 MeV
- Landau effective mass in SNM at saturation m*/m = 0.707
- Skewness Q = -463 MeV
- K_sym = -75.6 MeV, Q_sym = 464 MeV
- Maximum sound speed in PNM v_s/c = 0.97 at n ≈ 1.1 fm⁻³

## Context

This work extends the earlier LNS functional of Cao et al. (2006), which used a standard (2nd-order) Skyrme interaction fitted to BBG results but failed to reproduce PNM at high density and supported only neutron stars below 1.6 solar masses. The extended functional (LYVA1) corrects this high-density behavior by including higher-order derivative terms, following the hierarchy established by Davesne et al. (2015). The resulting EoS is compared to the BCPM functional (Baldo et al., 2013) and the BSk family (Goriely et al., 2009, 2013), and enables unified descriptions of neutron star crust and core properties using the same functional.
