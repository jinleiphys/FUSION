# Inclusive Neutrino Scattering in C-12: Implications for nu_mu to nu_e Oscillations

> We study inclusive nu_e and nu_mu cross sections in C-12 in a theory that takes into account significant nuclear renormalization of strengths. Our calculation is in excellent agreement with the measured inclusive muon capture rate and the flux-averaged nu_e cross section, but overestimates the flux-averaged nu_mu inclusive cross section. These reactions are of crucial relevance to the issue of possible nu_mu to nu_e oscillations.We also calculate the flux-averaged cross sections in C-13 and Al-27, which are found to be consistent with the available experimental result.

# Inclusive Neutrino Scattering in C-12: Implications for nu_mu to nu_e Oscillations

## Key claim

The authors' RPA-based calculation reproduces the measured flux-averaged nu_e cross section on C-12 and the inclusive muon capture rate, but overestimates the flux-averaged nu_mu inclusive cross section by at least 15% compared to the LSND experimental value, even after accounting for theoretical uncertainties. This discrepancy persists despite the model achieving a theoretical accuracy of about +/- 8% for the nu_mu cross section.

## Method

The authors use a local density approximation with a Random Phase Approximation (RPA) response that includes particle-hole and Delta-hole excitations interacting through a Landau-Migdal spin-isospin effective interaction. The nuclear response is built from single-particle states of an uncorrelated local Fermi sea, and the Lindhard function is computed with improvements to avoid pathologies at low momentum transfer. The calculation incorporates Pauli blocking, Coulomb corrections for the outgoing lepton, and Q-value effects, with the flux-averaged cross sections obtained by folding the computed energy-dependent cross sections with the appropriate neutrino spectra (Michel spectrum for nu_e, LSND beam spectrum for nu_mu).

## Key numbers

* Flux-averaged nu_e cross section on C-12: (15.48 +/- 1.13) x 10^{-42} cm^2, in excellent agreement with the LSND experimental value of (14.8 +/- 0.7 +/- 1.1) x 10^{-42} cm^2 and the KARMEN value of (15.2 +/- 1.0 +/- 1.3) x 10^{-42} cm^2.
* Flux-averaged nu_mu cross section on C-12: (16.65 +/- 1.37) x 10^{-40} cm^2, which is at least 15% higher than the LSND experimental value of (11.2 +/- 0.3 +/- 1.8) x 10^{-40} cm^2.
* Inclusive muon capture rate on C-12: (3.60 +/- 0.22) x 10^4 s^{-1}, in excellent agreement with the world average experimental value of (3.80 +/- 0.10) x 10^4 s^{-1}.
* Flux-averaged nu_e cross section on C-13: 7.25 x 10^{-41} cm^2 for the Michel spectrum.
* Flux-averaged nu_e cross section on Al-27: 11.48 x 10^{-41} cm^2 for g' = 0.7.
* Theoretical uncertainty from radial density variations: about 2% for both nu_e and nu_mu cross sections.
* Theoretical uncertainty from the Landau-Migdal parameter g' variation: +/- 7% for nu_e and +/- 8% for nu_mu around the central value g' = 0.7.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier RPA-based approach (Singh, Mukhopadhyay, Oset, 1995) to inclusive neutrino reactions, addressing discrepancies between theory and experiment that had been noted by Kolbe et al. (who overestimated the nu_mu cross section by about 50%) and Auerbach et al. (who overestimated both nu_e and nu_mu cross sections by 15-20%). The calculation provides a benchmark for interpreting LSND neutrino oscillation results, since the discrepancy in the nu_mu cross section directly affects the determination of excess events in the nu_e channel. The results also predict cross sections for C-13 and Al-27 that are consistent with the only available experimental limit from Krakauer et al. (1992), and the authors call for high-quality measurements of the inclusive cross section on C-13 to help resolve the wide range of theoretical predictions for Gamow-Teller strength quenching in that nucleus.
