# Interplay of dynamical and explicit chiral symmetry breaking effects on a quark

> The relative contributions of explicit and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking in QCD models of the quark-gap equation are studied in dependence of frequently employed ansätze for the dressed interaction and quark-gluon vertex. The explicit symmetry breaking contributions are defined by a constituent-quark sigma term whereas the combined effects of explicit and dynamical symmetry breaking are described by a Euclidean constituent-mass solution. We extend this study of the gap equation to a quark-gluon vertex beyond the Abelian approximation complemented with numerical gluon- and ghost-dressing functions from lattice QCD. We find that the ratio of the sigma term over the Euclidean mass is largely independent of nonperturbative interaction and vertex models for current-quark masses, $m_{u,d}(μ) \leq m(μ) \leq m_b(μ)$, and equal contributions of explicit and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking occur at $m(μ) \approx 400$~MeV. For massive solutions of the gap equation with lattice propagators this value decreases to about 200~MeV.

## Key claim

The ratio of explicit chiral symmetry breaking (CSB) to the sum of CSB and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB) in the quark constituent mass is largely independent of the nonperturbative interaction model and quark-gluon vertex ansatz for current-quark masses from the light up to the bottom quark, with equal contributions of CSB and DCSB occurring at a current-quark mass of approximately 400 MeV for model interactions and at about 220 MeV when using lattice QCD propagators.

## Method

The quark Dyson-Schwinger equation (gap equation) was solved numerically for different combinations of gluon interaction models (Maris-Tandy and Qin-Chang) and quark-gluon vertex ansatze (rainbow-ladder, Ball-Chiu, Ball-Chiu plus transverse terms, and a Slavnov-Taylor identity inspired vertex). The renormalization-point invariant ratio ζ = σ_f / M_f^E was computed, where σ_f is the constituent-quark sigma term derived via the Hellmann-Feynman theorem and M_f^E is the Euclidean constituent mass defined by the condition p^2 = M^2(p^2). For the lattice-based kernel, unquenched (N_f = 2+1+1) gluon and ghost dressing functions from lattice QCD were used with the STI vertex.

## Key numbers

- For light u/d quarks, the ratio ζ (CSB contribution relative to total constituent mass) is approximately 0.018–0.025 across all model interactions and vertices, meaning about 98% of the constituent mass comes from DCSB.
- For the charm quark, ζ ranges from 0.638 to 0.691 across model interactions, indicating DCSB still contributes roughly 30–36% of the constituent mass.
- For the bottom quark, ζ ranges from 0.850 to 0.864 across model interactions, meaning about 15% of the constituent mass comes from DCSB.
- Equal contributions of CSB and DCSB (ζ = 0.5) occur at a current-quark mass m(μ) ≈ 400 MeV for model interactions, and at m(μ) ≈ 220 MeV when using lattice QCD propagators with the STI vertex.
- Euclidean constituent masses for light quarks range from 0.381 to 0.408 GeV across model interactions; for the lattice-based kernel with the STI vertex, M_{u,d}^E = 0.272 GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Holl (2005) and El-Bennich (2012) on the relative roles of explicit and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking in the quark mass function, using more sophisticated vertex ansatze beyond the rainbow-ladder truncation. The finding that the ratio ζ is nearly independent of the interaction kernel for a given flavor confirms and generalizes previous results obtained with simpler truncations. The results enable more reliable calculations of hadronic observables, particularly for heavy-light mesons where a constant-mass charm-quark propagator is inadequate, and motivate future studies of hadronic sigma terms using consistent Bethe-Salpeter kernels.
