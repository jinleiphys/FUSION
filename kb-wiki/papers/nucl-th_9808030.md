# Unified Description of Freeze-Out Parameters in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> It is shown that the chemical freeze-out parameters obtained at CERN/SPS, BNL/AGS and GSI/SIS energies all correspond to a unique value of 1 GeV per hadron in the local rest frame of the system, independent of the beam energy and of the target and beam particles.

# Unified Description of Freeze-Out Parameters in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim

The chemical freeze-out parameters extracted from heavy ion collisions at CERN/SPS, BNL/AGS, and GSI/SIS energies all lie on a single curve corresponding to a fixed energy per hadron of approximately 1 GeV in the local rest frame, independent of beam energy and colliding species. This generalizes Hagedorn’s earlier observation for proton-proton collisions, but now includes the finite baryon density described by the baryon chemical potential.

## Method

The authors analyzed fully integrated particle multiplicities and their ratios from heavy ion collisions across three accelerator facilities (CERN/SPS, BNL/AGS, GSI/SIS), using thermal model fits to extract freeze-out temperature and baryon chemical potential. They also incorporated results from LEP electron-positron annihilation and from proton-proton and antiproton-proton collisions, with a slightly different treatment of the strangeness sector to account for strangeness undersaturation. The freeze-out parameters were then plotted in the temperature–baryon chemical potential plane and compared to curves of constant energy per hadron calculated from the hadronic gas model.

## Key numbers

- The freeze-out curve corresponds to approximately 1 GeV per hadron in the rest frame of the produced system; the solid line in the figure represents 1 GeV per hadron, and the dashed line represents 0.94 GeV per hadron.
- The analysis covers beam energies from 200 A·GeV down to below 1 A·GeV, spanning CERN/SPS, BNL/AGS, and GSI/SIS accelerators.
- For GSI/SIS, data using Ni and Au beams at energies between 0.8 and 1.9 A·GeV were included, with Ni–Ni at 1.9 A·GeV shown as one point and Au–Au at 0.8 and 1.0 A·GeV as the lowest-temperature points.
- At LEP, the fit reproduced no less than 29 different hadronic abundances at zero baryon chemical potential.

## Context

This work extends Hagedorn’s (1965) concept of a limiting temperature in proton-proton collisions to heavy ion collisions by introducing a “limiting” freeze-out energy per particle of 1 GeV, while also incorporating the finite baryon density via the baryon chemical potential as first indicated by Braun-Munzinger and Stachel (1996). The unified description covers a wide range of beam energies and collision systems, and the same approach was also applied to LEP data by Becattini (1996) and to proton-proton and antiproton-proton collisions at CERN. This enables a systematic thermal-model interpretation of hadronic freeze-out across essentially all high-energy collision systems, with the energy-per-hadron criterion serving as a simple predictive tool for future experiments.
