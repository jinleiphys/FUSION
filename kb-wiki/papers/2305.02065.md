# Impacts of dark matter interaction on nuclear and neutron star matter within the relativistic mean-field model

> This thesis explores the effects of dark matter (DM) on neutron stars (NSs) using the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model. The effects of DM on NS properties, including the mass-radius relation, the moment of inertia, and tidal deformability, are calculated by varying its fraction. The study found that the EOS becomes softer with increasing DM momentum, and the DM has marginal effects on nuclear matter properties, except for the EOSs and binding energy per particle. The study also calculated the properties of isolated, static, and rotating DM admixed NS and found that the DM has significant effects on both static and rotating NS. We have also observed that a tiny amount of DM can accumulate inside the NS, and more amount of it makes the NS unstable. The study also suggests that the secondary component might be a NS with DM content if the underlying nuclear EOS is sufficiently stiff. The $f$-mode oscillations of the DM admixed hyperon stars are calculated and found that there exist a correlation between canonical $f$-mode frequency and the dimensionless tidal deformability parameter ($Λ_{1.4}$) and we have put a constraint on $f$-mode frequency using GW170817 data. Finally, we have calculated the DM admixed binary NS properties and found that the binary system becomes less deformed and sustains more time in its inspiral phases with the addition of DM. Therefore, we suggest that one can take DM inside the compact objects while modeling the inspiral waveforms for the BNS systems.

## Key claim

The thesis claims that dark matter (DM) accretion inside neutron stars (NSs) significantly alters their macroscopic properties, and that the secondary component of the GW190814 event might be a NS with DM content if the underlying nuclear equation of state (EOS) is sufficiently stiff, though the DM fraction must be small to maintain stability.

## Method

The author uses the relativistic mean-field (RMF) and effective-field-theory motivated relativistic mean-field (E-RMF) models to describe nucleon interactions via exchange of σ, ω, ρ, and δ mesons, with self and cross-couplings up to fourth order. DM particles are assumed to interact with nucleons via Standard Model Higgs exchange, with DM density taken as constant throughout the NS at one-third of the total baryon density. The TOV equations are solved for static and rotating configurations, and post-Newtonian methods are used for binary NS inspiral properties.

## Key numbers

- The mass of PSR J0740+6620 is measured as M = 2.14^{+0.10}_{-0.09} M⊙ (68.3% CI), later refined to M = 2.08 ± 0.07 M⊙ (68.3% CI)
- The heaviest pulsar PSR J0952–0607 has a reported mass of 2.35 ± 0.17 M⊙
- NICER measurements give canonical NS radius R_{1.4} = 12.45 ± 0.65 km and maximum NS radius R_{2.08} = 12.35 ± 0.75 km
- GW170817 gives chirp mass M = 1.188^{+0.004}_{-0.002} M⊙ and canonical tidal deformability Λ_{1.4} = 190^{+390}_{-120}
- The secondary component of GW190814 has mass 2.50–2.67 M⊙
- The time for thermal relaxation between core and surface is almost 300 years less for DM-admixed NS compared to without DM

## Context

This work extends the RMF and E-RMF modeling of NSs (following Furnstahl et al. 1996, Kumar et al. 2017, 2018) by incorporating DM as an additional component, building on earlier DM capture hypotheses by Goldman (1989) and Kouvaris (2008, 2010, 2011, 2012). The study enables constraints on DM fractions in NSs using observational data from GW170817 and NICER, and suggests that DM should be considered when modeling inspiral waveforms for binary NS systems.
