# Interference of resonances and observation of the Θ^{+}-pentaquark

> After brief discussion of how the quantum interference may influence manifestations of resonances, description is given of the recent experimental evidence for possible manifestation of the Θ^{+}-photoproduction in interference with the ϕ-photoproduction.

# Interference of resonances and observation of the Θ⁺-pentaquark

## Key claim

The paper argues that a narrow peak near 1.54 GeV in the M(pK_L) distribution from the reaction γp → K_S K_L p, observed in interference with φ-photoproduction, may be evidence for the Θ⁺ pentaquark, but the authors hedge that the strangeness of the pK_L system is not fixed, so the peak could alternatively be a new Σ*⁺, and final confirmation requires a direct photoproduction signal with definite strangeness.

## Method

The authors reanalyzed the CLAS data set for the reaction γp → K_S K_L p, applying additional cuts to improve K_S identification and selecting events under the φ-peak (M_X(p) = 1.02 ± 0.01 GeV) where rearrangement interference is most efficient. They further applied cuts to eliminate known Σ* resonances in the 1.5–1.7 GeV region and to restrict momentum transfers (|t_{γK_S}| < 0.45 GeV²) to isolate a diffractive φ-production mechanism, then compared the resulting M(pK_L) distribution to a Monte Carlo simulation based on the Titov–Lee model for forward φ-photoproduction.

## Key numbers

- The peak in M(pK_L) is observed near M ~ 1.54 GeV, with a width consistent with experimental resolution.
- Statistical significance of the peak is about 5.3 σ.
- The φ meson has cτ < 3 × 10⁻¹⁰ cm, compared to cτ = 2.7 cm for K_S mesons.
- The decay φ → ωπ⁰ has branching ratio ≈ 5 × 10⁻⁵.
- The cut M_X(p) = 1.02 ± 0.01 GeV was used to select events under the φ-peak.
- The cut |t_{γK_S}| < 0.45 GeV² was applied to separate a diffractive mechanism.
- The original CLAS analysis applied a cut M_X(p) > 1.04 GeV to exclude the φ-peak.

## Context

This work extends the earlier CLAS analysis of the reaction γp → K K p (2009), which found no Θ⁺ signal and set a strict bound on its production cross section. The paper builds on the suggestion by Azimov, Danilin, and Polyakov (2012) to use rearrangement interference to search for new resonances with small production cross sections. The analysis uses the Titov–Lee model for forward φ-photoproduction (2004) as the background description. The result challenges the Particle Data Group’s position since 2008 and the common opinion that the pentaquark baryon is dead, and it aligns with continued positive observations from the LEPS and DIANA collaborations. The paper enables future searches for a direct photoproduction signal of the Θ⁺ and for a signal with definite strangeness (e.g., in nK⁺) to confirm the pentaquark interpretation.
