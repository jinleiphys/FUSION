# The $ΞN$ interaction constrained by recent $Ξ^-$ hypernuclei experiments

> The $ΞN$ interaction is investigated in the quark mean-field (QMF) model based on recent observables of the $Ξ^-+^{14}\rm{N}$ ($_{Ξ^-}^{15}\rm{C}$) system. The experimental data about the binding energy of $1p$-state $Ξ^-$ hyperon in $_{Ξ^-}^{15}\rm{C}$ hypernuclei at KISO, IBUKI, E07-T011, E176-14-03-35 events are conflated as $B_{Ξ^-}(1p)=1.14\pm0.11$ MeV. With this constraint, the coupling strengths between the vector meson and $Ξ$ hyperon are fixed in three QMF parameter sets. Meanwhile, the $Ξ^-$ binding energy of $1s$ state in $_{Ξ^-}^{15}\rm{C}$ is predicted as $B_{Ξ^-}(1s)=5.66\pm0.38$ MeV with the same interactions, which are completely consistent with the data from the KINKA and IRRAWADDY events. Finally, the single $ΞN$ potential is calculated in the symmetric nuclear matter in the framework of QMF models. It is $U_{ΞN}=-11.96\pm 0.85$ MeV at nuclear saturation density, which will contribute to the study on the strangeness degree of freedom in compact star.

# The ΞN interaction constrained by recent Ξ− hypernuclei experiments

## Key claim
The ΞN interaction is attractive, but its depth at nuclear saturation density is significantly shallower (U_ΞN = -11.96 ± 0.85 MeV) than the value extracted from Wood-Saxon potential analyses (≈ -24 MeV), and this shallower potential is consistent with the combined experimental binding energies of the 1p and 1s states in Ξ−+14N (15Ξ−C) hypernuclei.

## Method
The authors used the quark mean-field (QMF) model, where baryons consist of three constituent quarks confined by harmonic potentials and interact through meson exchange. They conflated four independent experimental measurements of the 1p-state Ξ− binding energy in 15Ξ−C (from KEK E176, KEK E373, and J-PARC E07 experiments) using a statistical conflation method to obtain a single constraint. This constraint was used to fix the ω-Ξ coupling strength g_ωΞ for three QMF parameter sets with different constituent quark masses, and the resulting interactions were then used to predict the 1s-state binding energy and the single ΞN potential in symmetric nuclear matter.

## Key numbers
- Conflated 1p-state Ξ− binding energy in 15Ξ−C: B_Ξ−(1p) = 1.14 ± 0.11 MeV, combining data from KISO (1.03 ± 0.18 MeV), IBUKI (1.27 ± 0.21 MeV), E07-T011 (0.90 ± 0.62 MeV), and E176-14-03-35 (1.18 ± 0.22 MeV) events.
- Predicted 1s-state Ξ− binding energy in 15Ξ−C: B_Ξ−(1s) = 5.66 ± 0.38 MeV, consistent with IRRAWADDY event (6.27 ± 0.27 MeV) and KINKA event (4.96 ± 0.77 MeV interpreted as 1s state).
- Single ΞN potential at nuclear saturation density: U_ΞN(ρ0) = -11.96 ± 0.85 MeV, with individual QMF parameter sets giving values from -10.72 to -13.15 MeV.
- ω-Ξ coupling ratios: g_ωΞ/g_ωN ranges from 0.4638 to 0.5024 across the six parameter sets (QMF-NK1X1 through QMF-NK3X2).

## Context
This work extends previous QMF model calculations by Hu (2017) on 15Ξ−C and provides a systematic constraint on the ΞN interaction using multiple recent experimental events from KEK E373 and J-PARC E07. The resulting ΞN potential depth of about -12 MeV is consistent with HAL-QCD lattice potential calculations (Sasaki 2020) within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock model (which gave -10.6 to -16.2 MeV) and with chiral NLO potential G-matrix results (Haidenbauer 2019, Kohno 2019) of about -5 to -8 MeV, but is significantly shallower than the Wood-Saxon potential analysis by Friedman (2021) which found about -24 MeV. The results enable more reliable predictions for the onset density of Ξ hyperons in neutron star cores and constrain the strangeness degree of freedom in compact star matter.
