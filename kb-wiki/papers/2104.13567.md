# The $őěN$ interaction constrained by recent $őě^-$ hypernuclei experiments

> The $őěN$ interaction is investigated in the quark mean-field (QMF) model based on recent observables of the $őě^-+^{14}\rm{N}$ ($_{őě^-}^{15}\rm{C}$) system. The experimental data about the binding energy of $1p$-state $őě^-$ hyperon in $_{őě^-}^{15}\rm{C}$ hypernuclei at KISO, IBUKI, E07-T011, E176-14-03-35 events are conflated as $B_{őě^-}(1p)=1.14\pm0.11$ MeV. With this constraint, the coupling strengths between the vector meson and $őě$ hyperon are fixed in three QMF parameter sets. Meanwhile, the $őě^-$ binding energy of $1s$ state in $_{őě^-}^{15}\rm{C}$ is predicted as $B_{őě^-}(1s)=5.66\pm0.38$ MeV with the same interactions, which are completely consistent with the data from the KINKA and IRRAWADDY events. Finally, the single $őěN$ potential is calculated in the symmetric nuclear matter in the framework of QMF models. It is $U_{őěN}=-11.96\pm 0.85$ MeV at nuclear saturation density, which will contribute to the study on the strangeness degree of freedom in compact star.

# The őěN interaction constrained by recent őě‚ąí hypernuclei experiments

## Key claim
The őěN interaction is attractive, but its depth at nuclear saturation density is significantly shallower (U_őěN = -11.96 ¬Ī 0.85 MeV) than the value extracted from Wood-Saxon potential analyses (‚Čą -24 MeV), and this shallower potential is consistent with the combined experimental binding energies of the 1p and 1s states in őě‚ąí+14N (15őě‚ąíC) hypernuclei.

## Method
The authors used the quark mean-field (QMF) model, where baryons consist of three constituent quarks confined by harmonic potentials and interact through meson exchange. They conflated four independent experimental measurements of the 1p-state őě‚ąí binding energy in 15őě‚ąíC (from KEK E176, KEK E373, and J-PARC E07 experiments) using a statistical conflation method to obtain a single constraint. This constraint was used to fix the ŌČ-őě coupling strength g_ŌČőě for three QMF parameter sets with different constituent quark masses, and the resulting interactions were then used to predict the 1s-state binding energy and the single őěN potential in symmetric nuclear matter.

## Key numbers
- Conflated 1p-state őě‚ąí binding energy in 15őě‚ąíC: B_őě‚ąí(1p) = 1.14 ¬Ī 0.11 MeV, combining data from KISO (1.03 ¬Ī 0.18 MeV), IBUKI (1.27 ¬Ī 0.21 MeV), E07-T011 (0.90 ¬Ī 0.62 MeV), and E176-14-03-35 (1.18 ¬Ī 0.22 MeV) events.
- Predicted 1s-state őě‚ąí binding energy in 15őě‚ąíC: B_őě‚ąí(1s) = 5.66 ¬Ī 0.38 MeV, consistent with IRRAWADDY event (6.27 ¬Ī 0.27 MeV) and KINKA event (4.96 ¬Ī 0.77 MeV interpreted as 1s state).
- Single őěN potential at nuclear saturation density: U_őěN(ŌĀ0) = -11.96 ¬Ī 0.85 MeV, with individual QMF parameter sets giving values from -10.72 to -13.15 MeV.
- ŌČ-őě coupling ratios: g_ŌČőě/g_ŌČN ranges from 0.4638 to 0.5024 across the six parameter sets (QMF-NK1X1 through QMF-NK3X2).

## Context
This work extends previous QMF model calculations by Hu (2017) on 15őě‚ąíC and provides a systematic constraint on the őěN interaction using multiple recent experimental events from KEK E373 and J-PARC E07. The resulting őěN potential depth of about -12 MeV is consistent with HAL-QCD lattice potential calculations (Sasaki 2020) within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock model (which gave -10.6 to -16.2 MeV) and with chiral NLO potential G-matrix results (Haidenbauer 2019, Kohno 2019) of about -5 to -8 MeV, but is significantly shallower than the Wood-Saxon potential analysis by Friedman (2021) which found about -24 MeV. The results enable more reliable predictions for the onset density of őě hyperons in neutron star cores and constrain the strangeness degree of freedom in compact star matter.
