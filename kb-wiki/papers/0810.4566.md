# Forward and midrapidity charmonium production at RHIC

> $J/ψ$ production at forward and midrapidity at the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC) is calculated within a previously constructed rate-equation approach accounting for both direct production and regeneration from $c$ and $\bar c$. The results are compared to experimental data. The observed stronger suppression at forward rapidity can be qualitatively explained by a smaller statistical regeneration component together with stronger cold nuclear matter induced suppression compared to midrapidity. The $χ_c$ over $J/ψ$ ratio and $ψ'$ over $J/ψ$ ratio are also calculated.

## Key claim

The observed stronger suppression of J/psi at forward rapidity compared to midrapidity at RHIC can be partially explained by a smaller regeneration component from charm quark coalescence combined with stronger cold nuclear matter suppression, but the approach does not fully account for the experimental findings even with shadowing effects included.

## Method

The authors applied a previously constructed thermal rate-equation approach to 200 AGeV Au-Au collisions at RHIC, solving a Boltzmann transport equation for charmonium phase space distributions in a thermalized medium. The approach accounts for both direct (primordial) production and continuous regeneration via coalescence of c and anti-c quarks throughout the QGP and hadronic gas phases, using quasifree dissociation cross sections with in-medium binding energy reduction. Cold nuclear matter effects were implemented in two scenarios: one with nuclear absorption plus Cronin effect, and another adding shadowing effects separately.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark cross section at midrapidity: d sigma_ccbar/dy (y=0) = 95 microbarns, based on PHENIX measurements
- Charm quark cross section at forward rapidity: d sigma_ccbar/dy (y=1.7) = 60 microbarns, guided by perturbative QCD
- J/psi production cross section in p-p collisions: d sigma_pp^J/psi/dy = 750 nb at midrapidity and 500 nb at forward rapidity (with about 30% uncertainty)
- Nuclear absorption cross section: sigma_nuc = 1.5 mb for J/psi and 2.7 mb for psi'
- Strong coupling constant alpha_s = 0.24, adjusted to reproduce J/psi yield in central Pb-Pb at SPS
- Charm quark thermal relaxation time tau_eq^c = 4-7 fm/c, adjusted to inclusive J/psi yield in central Au-Au at RHIC midrapidity
- Charged-particle rapidity density: dN_ch/dy = 660 at midrapidity and 615 at forward rapidity (for 0-5% centrality)
- Thermalization time tau_0 = 1/3 fm/c; critical temperature T_c = 180 MeV; freeze-out temperature T_fo = 120 MeV
- Cronin effect coefficient a_gN = 0.1-0.3 GeV^2/fm, estimated from d-Au data with Delta <p_t^2> = 0.8 +/- 0.4 GeV^2 and path length <l> = 4 fm

## Context

This work extends the rate-equation approach previously developed by Grandchamp and Rapp (2003) and Zhao and Rapp (2007) for midrapidity charmonium production to forward rapidity at RHIC. It addresses the puzzle that J/psi suppression at forward rapidity is stronger than at midrapidity despite lower energy density, which contradicts expectations from the original color screening signature proposed by Matsui and Satz (1986). The results are compared to the kinetic recombination model of Thews (2005), the statistical hadronization model of Andronic et al. (2006), and the comovers interaction model of Capella et al. (2007). The approach enables future microscopic calculations of charm quark coalescence with time-dependent charm quark distributions based on the work of van Hees and Rapp (2007).
