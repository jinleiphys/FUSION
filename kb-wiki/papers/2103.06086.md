# Triaxial deformation and the loss of the N = 28 shell gap

> Background: Recent accumulation of experimental data is revealing the nuclear deformation in vicinity of 42Si. This requests systematic theoretical studies to clarify more specific aspects of nuclear deformation and its causes. Purpose: The purpose of this study is to investigate the nature and cause of the nuclear deformations and its relation to the loss of the neutron magic number N = 28 in vicinity of 42Si. Method: The framework of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics with Gogny D1S density functional has been applied. The model assumes no spatial symmetry and can describe triaxial deformation. It also incorporates with the configuration mixing by the generator coordinate method. Results: We show that the shell effects and the loss of the magicity induce various nuclear deformations. In particular, the N = 26 and N = 30 isotones have triaxially deformed ground states. We also note that the erosion of the N = 28 magicity gradually occurs and has no definite boundaries. Conclusion: The present calculation predicts various nuclear deformations in vicinity of 42Si and suggests that the inter-band electric transitions are good measure for it. We also remark that the magicity is lost without the single-particle level inversion in the oblate deformed nuclei such as 42Si.

## Key claim

The erosion of the N=28 magicity occurs gradually and has no definite boundaries in the nuclear chart, with two distinct patterns: single-particle level inversion in prolate deformed nuclei (e.g., 42Mg) and loss without inversion in oblate deformed nuclei (e.g., 42Si).

## Method

The authors applied antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) with the Gogny D1S density functional to neutron-rich N=26, 28, and 30 isotones of Mg, Si, S, and Ar. They performed variational calculations with constraints on the quadrupole deformation parameters β and γ, followed by angular momentum projection and generator coordinate method (GCM) configuration mixing to obtain the low-lying spectra and electric properties.

## Key numbers

- Spherical N=28 shell gap values: 4.0 MeV for 48Ar, 3.5 MeV for 46S, 3.4 MeV for 44Si, and 2.8 MeV for 42Mg
- Energy minimum of 44Ar at (β, γ) = (0.26, 30°), with prolate and oblate states only 1.5 and 1.6 MeV above the triaxial state
- Level inversion in N=30 isotones occurs at β = 0.22 in 46S, 0.24 in 44Si, and 0.15 in 42Mg
- All calculated B(E2; 0₁⁺ → 2₁⁺) strengths exceed 200 e²fm⁴, with values ranging from 209 e²fm⁴ (40Si) to 486 e²fm⁴ (42Mg)
- Energy ratios E(4₁⁺)/E(2₁⁺) close to 3.3 across all calculated nuclei, indicating rotational nature

## Context

This work extends systematic theoretical studies of nuclear deformation in the vicinity of 42Si, following experimental data accumulation by Mijatovic (2018), Momiyama (2020), and Longfellow (2020). The results contradict the shell model study by Tsunoda (2020) regarding the binding of 42Mg, and show that the N=28 magicity loss differs from the N=20 island of inversion studied by Warburton (1990), which has a distinct boundary at N=19 and Z=13. The calculations predict triaxial deformation in N=26 and 30 isotones and suggest inter-band electric transitions as experimental probes for future measurements.
