# Hadronic Parity Violation at Next-to-Leading Order

> The flavor-conserving non-leptonic weak interaction can be studied experimentally through the observation of parity violation in nuclear and few-body systems. At hadronic scales, matrix elements of parity-violating four-quark operators ultimately give rise to the parity violating couplings between hadrons, and such matrix elements can be calculated non-perturbatively using lattice QCD. In this work, we investigate the running of isovector parity-violating operators from the weak scale down to hadronic scales using the renormalization group. We work at next-to-leading order in the QCD coupling, and include both neutral-current and charged-current interactions. At this order, results are renormalization scheme dependent, and we utilize 't Hooft-Veltman dimensional regularization. The evolution of Wilson coefficients at leading and next-to-leading order is compared. Next-to-leading order effects are shown to be non-negligible at hadronic scales.

## Key claim
The next-to-leading order QCD evolution of isovector parity-violating operators from the weak scale to hadronic scales produces non-negligible effects, but the results are renormalization scheme dependent, specifically using 't Hooft-Veltman dimensional regularization with an augmented scheme to preserve chirality.

## Method
The author constructs a minimal basis of isovector parity-violating four-quark operators in three effective field theories (five-flavor, four-flavor, and three-flavor) and computes the Wilson coefficients at the weak scale including one-loop QCD corrections. The operators are then evolved from the weak scale down to hadronic scales using the renormalization group at next-to-leading order, matching across quark thresholds at the bottom and charm masses.

## Key numbers
- The tree-level Wilson coefficient for operator 5 from Z0 exchange is (c0)5 = -0.5376, while for operators 1 and 3 it is (c0)1 = (c0)3 = -0.0771.
- The charged-current contribution to isovector hadronic parity violation is subdominant at approximately 10% of the neutral-current contribution.
- At hadronic scales, coefficients of strangeness-containing |ΔI| = 1 operators are modified by the parity-violating charged-current interaction at the 10-20% level.
- The one-loop matching yields Wilson coefficients at MZ: C1(5)(MZ) = [1 - (13/2)(αs(MZ)/(4π))] (c0)1 and C2(5)(MZ) = (7/2)(αs(MZ)/(4π)) (c0)1.

## Context
This work extends the leading-order QCD analysis of isovector parity-violating operators by Dai et al. (1991) to next-to-leading order, including both neutral-current and charged-current interactions that were omitted in that earlier study. The results provide essential input for lattice QCD computations of parity-violating hadronic matrix elements, such as the isovector pion-nucleon coupling hπNN(1) recently computed by Wasem (2011), and enable conversion between perturbative renormalization schemes and the lattice regularization used in such calculations.
