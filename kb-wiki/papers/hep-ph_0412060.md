# Multiple Parton Scattering in Nuclei: Heavy Quark Energy Loss and Modified Fragmentation Functions

> Multiple scattering, induced radiative energy loss and modified fragmentation functions of a heavy quark in nuclear matter are studied within the framework of generalized factorization in perturbative QCD. Modified heavy quark fragmentation functions and energy loss are derived in detail with illustration of the mass dependencies of the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal interference effects and heavy quark energy loss. Due to the quark mass dependence of the gluon formation time, the nuclear size dependencies of nuclear modification of the heavy quark fragmentation function and heavy quark energy loss are found to change from a linear to a quadratic form when the initial energy and momentum scale are increased relative to the quark mass. The radiative energy loss of the heavy quark is also significantly suppressed due to limited cone of gluon radiation imposed by the mass. Medium modification of the heavy quark fragmentation functions is found to be limited to the large $z$ region due to the form of heavy quark fragmentation functions in vacuum.

## Key claim

Multiple scattering and induced gluon radiation for heavy quarks in nuclear matter are studied within generalized factorization in perturbative QCD, showing that the nuclear size dependence of the modified fragmentation function and energy loss changes from linear to quadratic when the initial energy and momentum scale increase relative to the quark mass, and that the radiative energy loss is significantly suppressed due to the limited cone of gluon radiation imposed by the mass.

## Method

The authors apply the twist-expansion framework of generalized factorization to compute the hard partonic cross sections for double parton scattering and induced gluon radiation off a heavy quark produced via charged-current deep inelastic scattering on a nuclear target. They evaluate 23 cut diagrams in an axial gauge, perform a collinear expansion in the transverse momentum of the initial partons, and extract the leading twist-four contribution to the semi-inclusive hadronic tensor. The modified heavy quark fragmentation function is then expressed in terms of modified splitting functions that incorporate mass-dependent formation times and the "dead cone" suppression factor.

## Key numbers

- The suppression factor for the gluon spectrum from a heavy quark relative to a light quark is \( f_{Q/q} = \left[ \ell_T^2 / (\ell_T^2 + (1-z)^2 M^2) \right]^4 = \left[ 1 + \theta_0^2 / \theta^2 \right]^{-4} \), where \( \theta_0 = M / q^- \) and \( \theta = \ell_T / \ell^- \).
- The formation time for gluon radiation from a heavy quark is \( \tau_f^Q = 1 / [ (x_L + (1-z) x_M / z) p^+ ] \), which is shorter than the light-quark formation time \( \tau_f^q = 1 / (x_L p^+) \).
- The initial gluon density in central Au+Au collisions at \( \sqrt{s} = 200 \) GeV is about 30 times higher than that in cold Au nuclei, as extracted from jet quenching data.

## Context

This work extends the twist-expansion formalism previously applied to light (massless) quarks by Guo and Wang (2000) and Zhang and Wang (2003) to the case of heavy quarks, incorporating the mass-dependent "dead cone" effect first discussed by Dokshitzer and Kharzeev (2001). The results enable quantitative predictions for heavy quark energy loss and modified fragmentation functions in both cold nuclear matter and hot QCD matter, providing a theoretical basis for interpreting heavy meson suppression data from RHIC experiments such as PHENIX and STAR.
