# HIJING 1.0: A Monte Carlo Program for Parton and Particle Production in High Energy Hadronic and Nuclear Collisions

> Based on QCD-inspired models for multiple jets production, we developed a Monte Carlo program to study jet and the associated particle production in high energy $pp$, $pA$ and $AA$ collisions. The physics behind the program which includes multiple minijet production, soft excitation, nuclear shadowing of parton distribution functions and jet interaction in dense matter is briefly discussed. A detailed description of the program and instructions on how to use it are given.

## Key claim

HIJING 1.0 provides a Monte Carlo framework that combines perturbative QCD minijet production with a Lund-type soft interaction model, but the program's validity is restricted to collisions with center-of-mass energy above 4 GeV per nucleon, and the space-time development of final state interactions among produced partons and hadrons is not considered in this version.

## Method

Multiple minijet production is combined with a Lund-type model for soft interactions using a semiclassical probabilistic eikonal formalism, where the number of hard scatterings per nucleon-nucleon collision is determined from the inclusive jet cross section with a low transverse momentum cutoff P0 = 2 GeV/c. For nuclear collisions, a binary approximation with Glauber geometry is used, incorporating a parametrized nuclear parton distribution function for shadowing and a schematic energy loss model dE/dz for jet quenching in dense matter. The program uses PYTHIA 5.3 to generate kinetic variables for each hard scattering and JETSET 7.2 for string fragmentation and hadronization.

## Key numbers

- Low transverse momentum cutoff for minijets: P0 = 2 GeV/c, chosen to fit experimental cross section data in pp and pbar-p collisions
- Soft interaction inclusive cross section: sigma_soft = 57 mb, taken as constant
- Minimum excited string mass cutoff: M_cut = 1.5 GeV/c^2 for nucleon collisions
- Invariant mass cutoff for string radiation: M_cut_rad = 2 GeV/c^2
- Minimum colliding energy for the model: sqrt(s) >= 4 GeV per nucleon
- Typical running time for pp at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV: approximately 700 events per minute
- Typical running time for central Au+Au at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV per nucleon: approximately 1 event per minute
- Typical running time for central Pb+Pb at sqrt(s) = 6.4 TeV per nucleon: approximately 1 event per 10 minutes
- Program storage requirement: 90k words (32-bit words)
- Program length: 6397 lines of FORTRAN 77 code

## Context

This work extends the Lund FRITIOF model and Dual Parton model for soft reactions at intermediate energies (sqrt(s) below 20 GeV per nucleon) by incorporating perturbative QCD processes similar to those in PYTHIA for hadronic collisions. HIJING was designed to explore possible initial conditions in relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, and has been tested against data on pp and pbar-p collisions from sqrt(s) = 50 to 1800 GeV, as well as pA and AA collisions at moderate energies up to sqrt(s) = 20 GeV per nucleon. The program enables systematic study of minijet backgrounds to quark-gluon plasma signatures such as jet quenching, and provides initial conditions for investigating thermalization and equilibration of the quark-gluon plasma.
