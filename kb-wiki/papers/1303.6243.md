# Quantum Monte Carlo Calculations with Chiral Effective Field Theory Interactions

> We present the first quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) calculations with chiral effective field theory (EFT) interactions. To achieve this, we remove all sources of nonlocality, which hamper the inclusion in QMC calculations, in nuclear forces to next-to-next-to-leading order. We perform auxiliary-field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) calculations for the neutron matter energy up to saturation density based on local leading-order, next-to-leading order, and next-to-next-to-leading order nucleon-nucleon interactions. Our results exhibit a systematic order-by-order convergence in chiral EFT and provide nonperturbative benchmarks with theoretical uncertainties. For the softer interactions, perturbative calculations are in excellent agreement with the AFDMC results. This work paves the way for QMC calculations with systematic chiral EFT interactions for nuclei and nuclear matter, for testing the perturbativeness of different orders, and allows for matching to lattice QCD results by varying the pion mass.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) calculations using chiral effective field theory (EFT) interactions, achieved by removing all sources of nonlocality in nuclear forces up to next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^2$LO). The results exhibit systematic order-by-order convergence, but the authors note that for the harder interactions (cutoff $R_0 = 0.8$ fm) the perturbative convergence is clearly slow, while for the softer interactions ($R_0 = 1.2$ fm) perturbative calculations are in excellent agreement with the nonperturbative QMC results.

## Method

The authors constructed local chiral EFT nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions at leading order (LO), next-to-leading order (NLO), and N$^2$LO by removing momentum-dependent $k^2$ contact interactions and regulator-generated nonlocalities, using a coordinate-space regulator $f_{\rm long}(r) = 1 - e^{-(r/R_0)^4}$ with cutoff scale $R_0 = 0.8-1.2$ fm. They then performed auxiliary-field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) calculations for neutron matter with 66 particles, including contributions from 26 neighboring cells, using these local chiral interactions in both the propagator and observable evaluation.

## Key numbers

- Neutron matter energy per particle calculated up to saturation density ($n \approx 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$) using AFDMC with chiral EFT NN interactions at LO, NLO, and N$^2$LO
- At the highest density studied, the size of the N$^2$LO energy band is approximately 10% of the potential energy
- For the softer $R_0 = 1.2$ fm interaction (corresponding to momentum cutoff $\Lambda \sim 400$ MeV), third-order perturbative corrections are small and agree with AFDMC results
- For the harder $R_0 = 0.8$ fm interaction ($\Lambda \sim 600$ MeV), perturbative convergence is clearly slow
- Low-energy constants for the N$^2$LO potential at $R_0 = 1.2$ fm: $c_1 = -0.81$ GeV$^{-1}$, $c_3 = -3.4$ GeV$^{-1}$, $c_4 = 3.4$ GeV$^{-1}$
- Neutron-neutron scattering length fit to $-18.8$ fm via isospin-symmetry-breaking contact interaction $C_{nn}$
- Short-range couplings at LO: $C_S = -1.83406$ fm$^2$, $C_T = 0.15766$ fm$^2$ (for $R_0 = 1.2$ fm)
- Short-range couplings at N$^2$LO: $C_S = 1.09225$ fm$^2$, $C_T = 0.24388$ fm$^2$ (for $R_0 = 1.2$ fm)
- NN phase shifts reproduced well for laboratory energies $E_{\rm lab} \lesssim 150$ MeV
- Spectral-function regularization cutoff $\widetilde{\Lambda} = 800$ MeV used for two-pion-exchange loop integrals

## Context

This work extends the program of chiral EFT interactions (Epelbaum 2009, Entem 2011) to QMC methods, which had previously been limited to phenomenological potentials such as the Argonne $v_8'$ interaction. The results provide nonperturbative benchmarks for neutron matter that validate perturbative many-body calculations (Hebeler 2010, Tews 2013) for soft interactions, and enable future QMC calculations with chiral 3N forces, studies of neutron drops and nuclei, and matching to lattice QCD results by varying the pion mass.
