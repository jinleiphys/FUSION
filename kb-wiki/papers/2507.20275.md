# Vibrational Modes in Strongly Deformed Nuclei

> Low-energy vibrational excitations associated with the fluctuation of quadrupole deformed shapes are discussed within the frame of state-of-the-art Configuration Interaction calculations, actually performed via the Quasi-particle Vacua Shell Model version of the Monte Carlo Shell Model. Recently, low-lying $γ$ bands in heavy strongly deformed nuclei were shown to be rotational $K^P$ = 2$^+$ excitations of triaxially deformed states (see T. Otsuka \etal, Eur. Phys. J. A 61, 126 (2025)) rather than vibrational excitations as traditionally interpreted. In this context, it is important to identify possible low-lying vibrational excitations and to characterize the excitation energy at which they emerge. Focusing on two typical examples, $^{166}$Er and $^{162}$Dy, vibrational states are indeed identified above the $γ$ band using an extended version of the so-called T-plot. The phenomenon of shape coexistence is also shown to produce low-lying states below such vibrational band heads. These results suggest novel and rich structures in heavy deformed nuclei. While experimental counterparts are seen for some of such states, others are predictions opening doors to future dedicated experiments.

# Vibrational Modes in Strongly Deformed Nuclei

## Key claim

Low-lying vibrational excitations in heavy deformed nuclei exist above the γ band, but their band heads are not the traditional γ-vibration; instead, the γ band itself is a rotational K^P = 2^+ excitation of a triaxially deformed ground state. The authors identify genuine vibrational states using an extended T-plot method, finding that the lowest vibrational band head can be K^P = 0^+ rather than 2^+, and that shape coexistence produces additional low-lying states below these vibrational band heads.

## Method

The authors performed ultra-large-scale Configuration Interaction calculations using the Quasi-particle Vacua Shell Model (QVSM) version of the Monte Carlo Shell Model (MCSM), with a valence space of one-and-half harmonic oscillator shells above a ¹¹⁰Zr inert core, producing a many-body Hilbert space dimension of about 10³³. They introduced a "Phase-specified T-plot" (PT-plot) that encodes the sign of basis vector amplitudes relative to a reference state, allowing identification of vibrational modes by the nodal structure of the amplitude distribution across deformation parameters β₂ and γ. The method was applied to ¹⁶⁶Er and ¹⁶²Dy, with additional MCSM basis vectors compared to earlier rotational-state studies to target vibrational states below ~3 MeV.

## Key numbers

- ¹⁶⁶Er: 0⁺₃ state (γ-vibrational band head) decays to 2⁺₂ state with B(E2) = 9.8 W.u. in calculation
- ¹⁶⁶Er: Calculated B(E2; 4⁺_γγ → 2⁺_₂) = 10.9 W.u. for the γγ band (K^P = 4⁺) state
- ¹⁶²Dy: Recent experiment gives B(E2; 4⁺_γγ → 2⁺_₂) ~ 8.4 W.u., in good agreement with calculation
- ¹⁶⁶Er: Experimental 4⁺ state at excitation energy E_x = 2.028 MeV corresponds to calculated J^P = K^P = 4⁺ state
- The many-body Hilbert space dimension is about 10³³, compared to the conventional CI limit of ~10¹¹

## Context

This work extends the recent finding by Otsuka et al. (2025, Eur. Phys. J. A) that γ bands in heavy deformed nuclei are rotational excitations of triaxial states rather than vibrations, contradicting the traditional Bohr-Mottelson interpretation. The present paper addresses the resulting question of where genuine vibrational excitations appear. The analysis builds on prior MCSM/QVSM studies of heavy deformed nuclei by Otsuka et al. (2019) and uses the V_MU interaction previously applied to Zr, Sn, Nd, Sm, and Hg isotopes. The results enable future dedicated experiments to search for predicted vibrational states and shape-coexistence bands, and clarify that a 0⁺ state decaying strongly to the 2⁺_γ state can be a single γ-vibrational phonon rather than a double γ-phonon state as previously interpreted by Fahlander (1996) and Garrett (1997).
