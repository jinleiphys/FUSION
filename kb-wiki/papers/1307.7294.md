# Toward a microscopic description of reactions involving exotic nuclei

> We propose an extension of the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) method, where the projectile is described by a microscopic cluster model. This microscopic generalization (MCDCC) only relies on nucleon-target interactions, and therefore presents an important predictive power. Core excitations can be included without any further parameter. As an example we investigate the $\lipb$ elastic scattering at $E_{lab}=27$ and 35 MeV. The $^7$Li nucleus is known to present an $α+t$ cluster structure, and is well described by the Resonating Group Method. An excellent agreement is obtained for the $\lipb$ elastic cross sections, provided that breakup channels are properly included. We also present an application to inelastic scattering, and discuss future applications of the MCDCC.

## Key claim

The authors propose a microscopic generalization of the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (MCDCC) method that, unlike standard CDCC, relies only on nucleon-target interactions and can include core excitations without additional parameters; the method achieves excellent agreement with elastic scattering data for the $^{7}$Li+$^{208}$Pb system only when breakup channels are properly included.

## Method

The projectile ($^{7}$Li) is described by a microscopic cluster model using the Resonating Group Method (RGM) with an $\alpha + t$ cluster structure, where the 7-body wave functions are fully antisymmetric and generated via the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM). The projectile-target system is then solved through coupled-channel equations using the $R$-matrix method on a Lagrange mesh, with coupling potentials computed from one-body matrix elements between Slater determinants and nucleon-target optical potentials. The $^{7}$Li wave functions include bound states ($3/2^-$ and $1/2^-$), known resonances ($7/2^-$ at $E_{cm}=2.18$ MeV and $5/2^-$ at $E_{cm}=4.13$ MeV), and continuum pseudostates up to 20 MeV, with partial waves up to $j_{\rm max}=7/2$.

## Key numbers

- Elastic scattering of $^{7}$Li+$^{208}$Pb calculated at $E_{\rm lab}=27$ and 35 MeV, compared to experimental data from MGR96
- The $^{7}$Li ground-state quadrupole moment from the GCM is $-37.0\,e\cdot$mb, compared to the experimental value $-40.6 \pm 0.8\,e\cdot$mb
- The $B(E1, 3/2^{-} \rightarrow 1/2^{-})$ value from the GCM is $7.5 \,e^2\cdot$fm$^4$, compared to the experimental value $8.3 \pm 0.5 \,e^2\cdot$fm$^4$
- The $^{7}$Li wave functions used 20 values of the generator coordinate $S$ ranging from 0.8 fm to 16 fm in steps of 0.8 fm
- The Minnesota force admixture parameter $u = 1.011$ and spin-orbit amplitude $S_0 = 20.0$ MeV$\cdot$fm$^5$ reproduce the experimental energies of the $3/2^{-}$ ground state and $1/2^{-}$ first excited state
- Continuum states up to 20 MeV are included in the basis; typical channel radius is 30 fm with 120 basis functions

## Context

This work extends the standard CDCC method (originally developed for deuteron-induced reactions by Ra74b) by incorporating a microscopic cluster description of the projectile, addressing limitations where optical potentials for projectile constituents are unknown and where core excitations are neglected. The approach contrasts with non-microscopic CDCC calculations (e.g., PJR08) that require renormalization of $\alpha$-target and $t$-target optical potentials by an energy-dependent factor close to 0.6. The method enables future applications to exotic nuclei such as $^{11}$Be, $^{17}$C, and Borromean two-neutron halo nuclei ($^{6}$He and $^{11}$Li), as well as to breakup and fusion reactions.
