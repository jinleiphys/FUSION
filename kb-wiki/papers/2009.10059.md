# Near-critical supernova outflows and their neutrino signatures

> We demonstrate that the neutrino-driven outflows inside exploding core-collapse supernovae possess a special property of near-criticality, that is, they are on the edge of forming termination shocks. We derive a novel criterion for the formation of the shock, in terms of the fundamental parameters of the problem: the neutrino luminosity and energy as well as the properties of the protoneutron star. The criterion provides a unified description of the available numerical results and motivates future simulations. The property of near-criticality makes the neutrino signatures of the termination shocks a sensitive diagnostic of the physical conditions around the PNS several seconds into the explosion. The expected signal at DUNE is found to be statistically significant.

## Key claim

The neutrino-driven outflows in core-collapse supernovae are shown to be "near-critical," meaning they are on the edge of forming termination shocks, rather than being either always subsonic or always supersonic. This near-criticality makes the neutrino signatures of termination shocks a sensitive diagnostic of the physical conditions around the protoneutron star (PNS) several seconds into the explosion, with the expected signal at DUNE found to be statistically significant.

## Method

The authors develop a semi-analytical, spherically symmetric, steady-state model of neutrino-driven outflows, solving a system of ordinary differential equations for velocity, temperature, and entropy. They treat the problem as a boundary value problem, where the character of the outflow is determined by the interplay of conditions near the PNS and the pressure at the edge of the hot bubble, and they develop a shooting algorithm that handles both subsonic and transonic regimes. The critical flow separating these regimes is identified, and power-law scaling relations for the critical temperature and density are derived numerically and verified analytically.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature scaling: \( T_{f,crit} \simeq (112 \text{ keV}) L_{52}^{0.702} E_{\nu20}^{1.404} M_{1.4}^{-0.96} R_{20}^{0.08} \), where \( L_{52}=L/10^{52} \) erg, \( E_{\nu20}=E_{\nu}/20 \) MeV, \( M_{1.4}=M/1.4 M_\odot \), and \( R_{20}=R/20 \) km.
- Critical density scaling: \( \rho_{f,crit} \simeq (8.1 \times 10^3 \text{ g/cm}^3) L_{52}^{2.61} E_{\nu20}^{5.2} M_{1.4}^{-4.0} R_{20}^{1.03} \).
- For a typical galactic distance of 10 kpc, the modulation signal can have a \( 5\sigma \) significance.
- Neutrino luminosities are fixed at \( L_{\nu_e}=L_{\bar\nu_{e}}=8 \times 10^{51} \) erg/s, with average neutrino energy \( \epsilon \equiv \sqrt{\left<E_\nu^3\right>/\left<E_\nu\right>}=20 \) MeV, PNS mass \( M=1.4 M_\odot \), and gain radius \( R=20 \) km.
- Average neutrino energies used: \( \left<E_{\nu_e}\right> = 10.5 \) MeV, \( \left<E_{\bar{\nu_e}}\right> = 15.5 \) MeV, \( \left<E_{\nu_{\chi}}\right> = 17.7 \) MeV.
- Luminosity evolution assumed: \( L_{\nu} = L_0 \exp{(-{t}/{t_e})} \) with \( t_e = 2.5 \) s.

## Context

This work extends semi-analytical studies of neutrino-driven winds (Duncan 1986, Qian 1996, Otsuki 1999, Wanajo 2001, Thompson 2001) by including the variation of effective relativistic degrees of freedom and correctly identifying the outer boundary condition. It reconciles seemingly disparate simulation results where some models show termination shocks (Tomas 2004, Arcones 2006, Stockinger 2020) while others do not (Woosley 1992, Witti 1994, Fischer 2009, Bollig 2020), by showing that the system is near-critical and thus sensitive to detailed conditions. The findings enable the use of neutrino signals at DUNE as a diagnostic of explosion conditions and provide essential input for analyses of \( \nu p \)-process nucleosynthesis yields.
