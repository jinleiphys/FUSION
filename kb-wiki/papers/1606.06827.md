# Delta excitation in deuteron-proton elastic scattering

> Deuteron-proton elastic scattering is studied in the multiple scattering expansion formalism. The four contributions are taken into account: one-nucleon-exchange, single- and double scattering, and $Δ$-isobar excitation. The presented approach was applied to describe the differential cross sections at deuteron energies between 500 and 1300 MeV in a whole angular range. The obtained results are compared with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The inclusion of the Δ-isobar excitation term in the multiple scattering expansion formalism allows the model to describe the sharp rise of the differential cross section at scattering angles above 140°, which was not captured by the previous approach that included only one-nucleon-exchange, single scattering, and double scattering terms.

## Method

The deuteron-proton elastic scattering amplitude was constructed using the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas formalism, iterated to second order in the two-particle t-matrices to yield four contributions: one-nucleon-exchange, single scattering, double scattering with a nucleon in the intermediate state, and double scattering with a Δ-isobar in the intermediate state. The nucleon-nucleon t-matrix was parameterized using the Love-Franey model with parameters fitted to the SP07 phase-shift analysis, while the NN→NΔ transition potential was based on π- and ρ-meson exchanges with a monopole form factor (Λ=1 GeV) and an energy-dependent Δ-isobar width. Calculations were performed in the deuteron Breit frame using a non-relativistic deuteron wave function with S- and D-wave components.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron kinetic energies studied: 500, 880, 1200, and 1300 MeV
- Δ-isobar parameters: Γ₀ = 0.120 GeV, γ = 0.200 GeV, m_Δ = 1.232 GeV
- Coupling constants: f_π = 1.008, f_π* = 2.156, f_ρ = 7.8, f_ρ* = 1.85 × f_ρ
- At T_d = 880 MeV, the difference between ONE+SS and ONE+SS+DS results reaches about 15 times in the 60°–140° angular range
- At T_d = 500 MeV, the double scattering contribution changes the cross section by 2–3 times at θ* = 120°
- The Δ-isobar contribution is negligible at T_d = 500 MeV and becomes significant at T_d = 1300 MeV

## Context

This work extends the author's previous multiple scattering expansion calculations (Ladygina, 2014, 2015) that included only one-nucleon-exchange, single scattering, and double scattering terms, which failed to describe the backward-angle rise. The approach contrasts with the Kerman-Kisslinger model (1969) that proposed N* resonance admixture in the deuteron, and with later works by Konig and collaborators (1990s) that considered Δ-isobar in backward scattering but omitted double scattering with an intermediate nucleon. The results enable future calculations of polarization observables, where the ρ-meson exchange term (neglected in the present spin calculation) may become important.
