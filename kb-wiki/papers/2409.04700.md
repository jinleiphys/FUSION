# Spin-charge separation for paired Dirac fermions in $(1+1)$ dimensions

> We study Dirac fermions at finite density coupled to a complex pairing field assumed to obey scalar field theory with quartic self-repulsion. The bulk of our work develops the mathematics that elucidates the propagation of fermionic excitations in such systems as independent spin (boosts) and charge (fermion number) degrees of freedom. A necessary ingredient is the presence of broken $U(1)$ symmetry in the pairing field and decoupling of its density and phase. In the fermion sector, these elements give rise to an emergent spin-dependent gauge coupling which binds in-vacuum spin and charge into elementary fermions, while driving proliferation of unbound spin and charge for finite condensation in the pairing field. Notably, the onset of spin-charge separation is signaled by $\mathcal{P} \mathcal{T}$-symmetry breaking and decoupling of spin components under Lorentz transformations. Our investigation concludes with two theorems that identify generic features of spin-charge separation in such systems.

## Key claim

Spin-charge separation in this (1+1)-dimensional Dirac fermion system occurs only when the difermion pairing field has broken U(1) symmetry and its density and phase decouple, with the onset signaled by PT-symmetry breaking and decoupling of spin components under Lorentz transformations; the separation is momentum-dependent, appearing at small single-particle momenta where background fluctuations dominate, while at large momenta the system reverts to elementary spinors with bound spin and charge.

## Method

The authors study Dirac fermions at finite density coupled to a complex pairing field described by scalar field theory with quartic self-repulsion, using a mean-field approximation at low temperatures. They factor the spinor wavefunction into real and complex parts, where the complex part splits into overall and internal phase factors encoding fermion number charge and an emergent gauge field respectively. The decomposition maps naturally to subgroups of the Lorentz group via Iwasawa decomposition, with the charge field belonging to U(2) and the spin field to SL(2,R), and they analyze the momentum-scale dependence of the separation by examining limits of large versus small single-particle momenta relative to difermion fluctuation momenta.

## Key numbers

- The modified boost parameter for dressed spinors is given by e^{eta'} = sqrt[(E + mu + (p + bar{Delta}))/(E + mu - (p + bar{Delta}))], where E is energy, mu is chemical potential, p is momentum, and bar{Delta} is the difermion field
- In the large momentum limit, the dressed spinor components approach psi_pm' -> (bar{Delta}/E_-)^{pm 1/2} psi_pm = sqrt{rho} e^{i theta_N} (bar{Delta}/E_+)^{pm 1/2}, where rho is density and theta_N is the charge phase
- In the small momentum limit, the dressed spinor components become psi_pm' -> sqrt{rho} [(1 + Re bar{Delta}/rho)/(1 - Re bar{Delta}/rho)]^{mp 1/2} e^{mp i beta_Delta/2} e^{i theta_N}, where beta_Delta is the difermion phase
- The condensate parameters satisfy the relation rho^2 - bar{Delta}^2 = sigma^2, where sigma is the chiral condensate

## Context

This work extends the concept of spin-charge separation, originally developed for strongly correlated electrons in one-dimensional systems by Tomonaga (1950), Luttinger (1963), and Haldane (1981), to relativistic Dirac fermions in (1+1) dimensions with pairing interactions. The model incorporates both chiral (Gross-Neveu) and difermion pairing channels, connecting to studies of superconductivity in dense nuclear matter and quantum chromodynamics at high densities as investigated by Alford (2008) and others. The results enable future investigation of spin-charge separation in dense nuclear matter relevant to neutron star physics, and establish two general theorems identifying generic features of such separation in paired Dirac fermion systems.
