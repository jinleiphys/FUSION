# Quantum Phase Transition in the Shape of Zr isotopes

> The rapid shape change in Zr isotopes near neutron number $N$=60 is identified to be caused by type II shell evolution associated with massive proton excitations to its $0g_{9/2}$ orbit, and is shown to be a quantum phase transition. Monte Carlo shell-model calculations are carried out for Zr isotopes of $N$=50-70 with many configurations spanned by eight proton orbits and eight neutron orbits. Energy levels and B(E2) values are obtained within a single framework in a good agreement with experiments, depicting various shapes in going from $N$=50 to 70. Novel coexistence of prolate and triaxial shapes is suggested.

## Key claim

The rapid shape change in Zr isotopes near neutron number N=60 is identified as a quantum phase transition (QPT) caused by type II shell evolution associated with massive proton excitations to the 0g_{9/2} orbit, occurring with almost no mixing between the spherical and deformed configurations even at their crossing point.

## Method

Monte Carlo shell-model (MCSM) calculations were performed for even-even Zr isotopes from N=50 to N=70 using a fixed Hamiltonian with a model space spanned by eight proton orbits and eight neutron orbits, reaching a dimension of 3.7×10^23. The Hamiltonian combined the JUN45, SNBG3, and V_MU interactions with minor fine-tuning of T=1 two-body matrix elements using observed levels of 2^+_1 and 4^+_1 states in 90-96Zr and the 0^+_2 state in 94-100Zr. Shape assignments were made using T-plot analysis, where each MCSM basis vector is plotted on the potential energy surface according to its quadrupole moments Q_0 and Q_2.

## Key numbers

- The 2^+_1 excitation energy drops by a factor of ~6 at N=60, from ~1-2 MeV for N=50-58 to ~0.2 MeV for N≥60
- The ratio R_{4/2} (4^+_1 to 2^+_1 level ratio) approaches the rotational limit of 10/3 at N=60, while R_{4/2} < 2 for N≤58 indicates seniority-type structure
- B(E2; 2^+_1→0^+_1) values show a sharp increase at N=60, with effective charges (e_p, e_n) = (1.3e, 0.6e) used in calculations
- For 98Zr, the proton 0g_{9/2} occupation number increases from 0.4 in the spherical 0^+_1 state to 3.5 in the prolate 0^+_2 state
- Neutron effective single-particle energies (ESPEs) from 2s_{1/2} to 0g_{7/2} are distributed over 4 MeV for the spherical 0^+_1 state of 98Zr but within 2 MeV for prolate states
- The triaxial band in 110Zr shows B(E2) values nearly identical to the rigid-triaxial rotor model with γ=28°

## Context

This work extends prior shell-model calculations that reproduced experimental data up to N=58 but not beyond (Sieja 2009), and improves upon Interacting Boson Model calculations that showed more gradual shape changes (IBM-1, IBM-2). The results contradict earlier approaches that indicated gradual shape-changes (HFB, FRDM, RMF) by demonstrating an abrupt first-order quantum phase transition. The paper enables future investigation of a possible second-order phase transition at larger N values and suggests that similar type II shell evolution effects may occur in Sr isotopes and other nuclei, while also motivating further studies of octupole shapes in this region.
