# Superheavy element production, nucleus-nucleus potential and mu-catalysis

> The semi-microscopic potential between heavy nuclei is evaluated for various colliding ions in the approach of frozen densities in the framework of the extended Thomas-Fermi approximation with hbar^2 correction terms in the kinetic energy density functional. The proton and neutron densities of each nucleus are obtained in the Hartree-Fock-BCS approximation with SkM* parameter set of the Skyrme force. A simple expression for the nuclear interaction potential between spherical nuclei is presented. It is shown that muon bound with light projectile induces the superheavy elements production in nucleus-nucleus collisions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a muon bound to a light projectile nucleus can enhance superheavy element production in nucleus-nucleus collisions by modifying the capture pocket, the potential-energy landscape, and the fission barrier height, but this enhancement depends on the muon's ability to reduce the Coulomb repulsion between protons and on the specific conditions of the fusion reaction.

## Method

The nuclear part of the interaction potential between heavy nuclei was evaluated using a semi-microscopic frozen density approximation within the extended Thomas-Fermi approximation with hbar^2 correction terms in the kinetic energy density functional. Proton and neutron densities for each nucleus were obtained from the Hartree-Fock-BCS approximation with the SkM* parameter set of the Skyrme force. A simple analytical expression for the nuclear interaction potential between spherical nuclei was then derived by fitting to a database of 7140 nucleus-nucleus potentials evaluated at 15 distances each, covering 119 spherical or near-spherical nuclei from 16O to 212Po. The muon-catalysis effect was analyzed by comparing the potential-energy landscape of the muon-nuclear system to the pure nuclear system.

## Key numbers

- The analytical expression for the nuclear potential includes a coefficient of -1.989843 and a term with 0.003525139 (A1/A2 + A2/A1)^(3/2) and -0.4113263 (I1 + I2), where Ii = (Ni - Zi)/Ai.
- The effective nuclear radius is given by Ri = Rip (1 - 3.413817 / R1p^2) + 1.284589 (Ii - 0.4 Ai/(Ai + 200)), with the proton radius Rip = 1.24 Ai^(1/3) (1 + 1.646/Ai - 0.191 Ii).
- The muon lifetime is approximately 2.2 × 10^(-6) s, sufficient for forming a 1s bound state with a light projectile before collision.
- A variation of the fission barrier height by 0.3 MeV leads to a change in superheavy element production cross section of approximately 30%, and the muon increases the fission barrier of a heavy nucleus by near 1 MeV, potentially raising the production cross section by about one order of magnitude.

## Context

This work extends the semi-microscopic approach for nucleus-nucleus potentials previously developed by Denisov and collaborators (2002, 2003) and applies it to superheavy element synthesis, particularly for reactions with 48Ca and 64Ni projectiles. The analytical potential expression is compared to empirical fusion barriers extracted from subbarrier fusion data by an earlier analysis (2000), and it shows better agreement than older models such as Bass (1974, 1980), proximity (1977, 2000), Krappe-Nix-Sierk, and Winther. The muon-catalysis proposal builds on known muon catalysis of thermonuclear reactions (Kim, 1990s) and suggests a new pathway for synthesizing elements with Z ≥ 120, which are currently inaccessible with 48Ca beams due to the limited availability of heavy transuranic targets.
