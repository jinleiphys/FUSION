# Correction to Relativistic Mean Field binding energy and $N_pN_n$ scheme

> The differences between the experimental and Relativistic Mean Field binding energies have been calculated for a large number of even-even nuclei from A=50 to 220. Excluding certain mass regions, the differences, after suitable corrections for particular isotope chains, are found to be proportional to the Casten factor $P$, chosen as a measure of n-p interaction strength in a nucleus. Results for even-$Z$ odd-$N$ nuclei are also seen to follow the same relation, if the odd-even mass difference is taken into account following the semiempirical formula. This indicates that the n-p interaction is the major contributor to the difference between the calculated and the experimental binding energies.

## Key claim

The difference between experimental and Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) binding energies, after correcting for the isotope with magic neutron number, is proportional to the Casten factor \(P = N_p N_n / (N_p + N_n)\) for most even-even nuclei from A=50 to 220, except in certain mass regions (rare earth nuclei with Z=64-74, N=78-104 and actinide nuclei with Z=88-92, N=114-148) where the trend deviates.

## Method

The authors calculated the difference \(\Delta_{\nu\pi}(Z,N) = A(B_{th}(Z,N) - B_{ex}(Z,N) + B_{corr}(Z))\) for even-even nuclei using the FSU Gold RMF Lagrangian density with zero-range pairing force (300 MeV-fm), where \(B_{corr}(Z)\) is the experimental minus theoretical binding energy for the isotope with magic neutron number. They then fitted \(\Delta_{\nu\pi}\) as a linear function of the Casten factor \(P\) and extended the analysis to even-Z odd-N nuclei by including an odd-even mass difference term \(d/A\).

## Key numbers

- For even-even nuclei, the fitted slope is \(a = -2.148 \pm 0.029\) with an rms deviation of 1.15 MeV for the relation \(\Delta_{\nu\pi} = aP\).
- For even-Z odd-N nuclei, the fitted parameters are \(a = -2.129 \pm 0.042\) and \(d = 145.7 \pm 14.3\) MeV for the relation \(\Delta_{\nu\pi} = aP + d/A\), with a standard deviation of 1.09 MeV for 209 nuclei.
- Combining all isotopes (443 nuclei), the least-square fit gives \(a = -2.139 \pm 0.017\) with an rms deviation of 1.09 MeV.
- Using the NL3 Lagrangian density instead of FSU Gold gives a slope \(a = -2.609 \pm 0.044\) with an rms deviation of 1.68 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the systematics of the \(N_p N_n\) scheme, previously applied to deformation, B(E2) values (Casten 1985), rotational moments of inertia (Saha 2004), and ground band energies (Saha 2005), to binding energy corrections in RMF calculations. The authors had earlier used similar \(\Delta_{\nu\pi}\) systematics for spectroscopic factors in actinides (Bhattacharya and Gangopadhyay 2007) but with subshell closures; the present work aims for a more robust systematics valid across a large mass region using only known major shells. The corrected RMF binding energies achieve agreement comparable to or better than deformed RMF calculations (Lalazissis 1998) for Nd and Sm isotopes, and the approach enables improved binding energy predictions for nuclei where experimental data for magic-neutron isotopes are unavailable.
