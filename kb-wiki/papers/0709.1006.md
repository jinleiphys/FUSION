# A New Efficient Method of Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov Calculation for Weakly Bound Nuclei

> We propose a new method to solve the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations for weakly bound nuclei, which works for both spherical and axially deformed cases. In this approach, the quasiparticle wave functions are expanded in a complete set of analytical Poschl-Teller-Ginocchio and Bessel/Coulomb wave functions. Correct asymptotic properties of the quasiparticle wave functions are endowed in the proposed algorithm. Good agreement is obtained with the results of the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculation using the box boundary condition for a set of benchmark spherical and deformed nuclei.

## Key claim

The authors propose that expanding quasiparticle wave functions in a basis of analytical Pöschl-Teller-Ginocchio (PTG) and Bessel/Coulomb wave functions can solve the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations for weakly bound nuclei, correctly reproducing asymptotic properties that the harmonic oscillator (HO) and transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) bases fail to capture, while maintaining numerical stability and efficiency for both spherical and axially deformed cases.

## Method

The method (HFB/PTG) starts with an initial HFB calculation in the harmonic oscillator basis to obtain an approximate HF potential and effective mass. For each partial wave, a PTG potential is fitted to the HF potential when bound or narrow resonant states exist, otherwise Bessel/Coulomb wave functions are used; the PTG potential converts narrow resonances into weakly bound states, eliminating rapid phase shifts in the continuum. The HFB equations are then diagonalized in the combined PTG and Bessel/Coulomb basis, and the procedure is iterated to self-consistency, with quasiparticle wave functions matched to their exact asymptotic forms at a matching radius of about 10 fm.

## Key numbers

- For spherical Ni isotopes near the drip line (⁸⁴Ni to ⁹⁰Ni), the difference in total energy between HFB/PTG and HFB/Box calculations is less than 85 keV.
- Neutron rms radii from HFB/PTG differ from HFB/Box by less than 0.003 fm for the Ni isotopes.
- For ⁸⁴Ni, a maximum linear momentum cutoff of k_max = 3 fm⁻¹ is sufficient for convergence; calculations use k_max = 5 fm⁻¹ with 60 continuum states per partial wave.
- For the deformed nucleus ¹¹⁰Zr, the HFB/PTG calculation yields a total quadrupole moment of 12.53 barn, compared to 12.088 barn (HFB/Box) and 12.303 barn (HFB/THO).
- The neutron pairing gap Δ_n for ⁸⁴Ni is 1.532 MeV (HFB/PTG) versus 1.481 MeV (HFB/Box); for ⁹⁰Ni it is 1.892 MeV (HFB/PTG) versus 1.899 MeV (HFB/Box).
- For ⁸⁶Ni, the pairing energy from HFB/PTG is -35.92 MeV, while the THO calculation differs by about 4 MeV from the HFB/Box value.

## Context

This work extends the configuration-space HFB approach beyond the limitations of the harmonic oscillator basis (used in codes such as HFB/HO) and the transformed harmonic oscillator basis (THO) developed by Stoitsov et al. (2004), which cannot simultaneously reproduce the correct asymptotic behavior of both normal and pairing densities for different channels. The method enables efficient HFB calculations for weakly bound and deformed nuclei near the drip line, and the authors note that the resulting quasiparticle basis can serve as a starting point for Gamow quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) calculations of resonant excited states.
