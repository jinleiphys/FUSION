# Full Configuration Study of Light No-Core Nuclei with Novel Realistic NN Interaction JISP16

> We introduce the no-core full configuration (NCFC) approach and present results for $^4$He, $^{12}$C and $^{14}$F with the realistic NN interaction, JISP16. We obtain ground state energies and their uncertainties through exponential extrapolations that we demonstrate are reliable in $^4$He where fully converged results are obtained. We find $^{12}$C is overbound by 1.7 MeV and we predict the yet-to-be-measured binding energy of $^{14}$F to be $70.2\pm 3.5$ MeV. The extrapolated spectrum of $^{14}$F is in reasonable agreement with known features of the $^{14}$B spectrum.

## Key claim

The paper introduces the no-core full configuration (NCFC) approach, which uses a "bare" realistic NN interaction (JISP16) without renormalization, and claims that ground state energies can be obtained through exponential extrapolations to the infinite basis limit; however, the method contains several arbitrary aspects (choice of HO energy window, number of points used in fits, weighting scheme) that the authors acknowledge and attempt to validate through cross-checks with solvable cases.

## Method

The authors diagonalize the many-body Hamiltonian in a harmonic oscillator basis truncated by Nmax (the maximum number of oscillator quanta above the minimum configuration), using the code MFDn with the Lanczos algorithm. They perform calculations at multiple Nmax values and multiple HO energies hbar-Omega, then fit the ground state energies at fixed hbar-Omega to an exponential plus constant function E(Nmax) = a exp(-c Nmax) + E(infinity). Two extrapolation variants are used: method A fits four successive Nmax points with a common asymptote constraint and weighted chi-square minimization; method B fits three successive Nmax points and assigns uncertainty from the difference between the two highest Nmax results.

## Key numbers

- 4He ground state: overbound by 3 ± 1 keV relative to experimental 28.296 MeV, with Nmax=18 results within 3 keV of exact answer
- 12C ground state: overbound by 1.7 MeV (extrapolated 93.9 ± 1.1 MeV vs experimental 92.162 MeV), using Nmax=2-8
- 14F ground state: predicted binding energy 70.2 ± 3.5 MeV (extrapolation A) and 71.8 ± 2.4 MeV (extrapolation B), with experimental value unknown
- 14B ground state: extrapolated 83.7 ± 3.1 MeV (method A) vs experimental 85.42 MeV
- 16O ground state: extrapolated 143.5 ± 1.0 MeV (method A) vs experimental 127.619 MeV, using Nmax up to 8

## Context

This work extends the no-core shell model (NCSM) approach of Navratil (2007) by using a "bare" interaction without Lee-Suzuki-Okamoto renormalization, which the authors argue produces more uniform convergence trends suitable for extrapolation. The JISP16 NN interaction of Shirokov (2007) is specifically designed to describe light nuclei without three-nucleon forces. The NCFC method enables predictions for exotic nuclei like 14F (planned experiment at Texas A&M Cyclotron Institute) and provides a framework for testing new renormalization schemes such as those of Bogner (2008) that may yield good convergence with other interactions.
