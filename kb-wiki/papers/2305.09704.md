# The deuteron charge radıus $r_c$ in the framework of the hard-wall AdS/QCD model

> We study the deuteron charge radius in the framework of a hard-wall AdS/QCD model. We present basic elements of the hard-wall model and write metrics for the AdS space. We introduce a vector field with twist $τ=6$ describing deuteron in the bulk of AdS space and other vector fields to describe photon respectively, write an effective action for the bulk fields interactions, find a $G_1(Q^2)$, $G_2(Q^2)$ and $G_3(Q^2)$ form factors, then quadrupole $G_Q(Q^2)$ and charge $G_C(Q^2)$ form-factors of a deuteron. Thus, from the charge $G_C(Q^2)$ form factor we find the deuteron charge radius $R_C$ in the framework of a hard-wall AdS/QCD model. Then we compare our result with the results soft-wall model and experimental data.

## Key claim

The hard-wall AdS/QCD model yields a deuteron charge radius of 1.07956 fm, which is significantly smaller than both the soft-wall model result (1.92 fm) and the experimental value (2.13 ± 0.01 fm), indicating that the hard-wall framework underestimates this observable compared to the soft-wall approach.

## Method

The authors construct a 5D effective action in a hard-wall AdS space (cut off at an infrared boundary z_m) containing a vector field with twist τ=6 dual to the deuteron and a vector field dual to the photon. They perform Kaluza-Klein decomposition and Fourier transformation to compute the three electromagnetic form factors G_1(Q^2), G_2(Q^2), and G_3(Q^2), from which they derive the charge form factor G_C(Q^2) and then extract the charge radius R_C via the derivative at Q^2=0 using MATHEMATICA.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron charge radius from hard-wall model: R_C^(h.w.) = 1.07956 fm
- Deuteron charge radius from soft-wall model (for comparison): R_C^(s.w.) = 1.92 fm
- Experimental deuteron charge radius: R_C^(exp) = 2.13 ± 0.01 fm
- Fixed coupling constants used: c_2 = 1.67066, c_3 = 22.507

## Context

This work extends the study of deuteron electromagnetic properties within AdS/QCD models, following the soft-wall model calculations of Gutsche (2015, 2016) which obtained R_C = 1.92 fm at zero temperature. The hard-wall approach, previously applied by Huseynova (2022) to deuteron form factors, yields a charge radius substantially below both the soft-wall result and experimental data, suggesting the hard-wall model's charge form factor is systematically smaller. This discrepancy motivates further refinement of AdS/QCD treatments of nuclear structure.
