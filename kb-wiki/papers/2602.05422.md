# Decay of three-body resonances in a discrete basis

> We present a theoretical framework for calculating the asymptotic properties and decay dynamics of three-body resonances described in a discrete basis. The method involves solving an inhomogeneous Schrödinger equation to determine the non-normalizable resonant state by identifying a normalizable source state, which captures the short-range internal structure. The long-range behavior is then calculated using the free three-body propagator, providing accurate asymptotic coefficients necessary for describing decay correlations. We apply this formalism to the two-neutron decay of the 0$^{+}$ ground-state and the 2$^{+}$ excited-state resonances of $^{16}\text{Be}$ ($^{14}\text{Be}+n+n$), working within the hyperspherical expansion method with an analytical transformed harmonic oscillator basis. Our results show that the decay is strongly dominated by the lowest hypermomentum components at large separations, reflecting effective three-body barrier penetration dynamics that shape the final state. The calculated relative-energy distributions exhibit clear neutron-neutron correlations for both states, arising from mixing between different asymptotic channels, and are consistent with a direct two-neutron emission mechanism, in agreement with recent experimental observations. This work provides a reliable tool for linking the internal structure of three-body resonances to their decay properties.

## Key claim

The paper presents a theoretical framework for calculating the asymptotic decay properties of three-body resonances by solving an inhomogeneous Schrödinger equation, where the non-normalizable resonant state is determined from a normalizable source state that captures the short-range internal structure. The formalism is applied to the two-neutron decay of \(^{16}\text{Be}\) resonances, and the authors claim the calculated relative-energy distributions are consistent with a direct two-neutron emission mechanism, but this agreement depends on the specific choice of source state that maximizes the source term norm.

## Method

The method solves an inhomogeneous Schrödinger equation \((H - E_r)|\psi_r\rangle = \lambda|\phi_r\rangle\) where \(|\phi_r\rangle\) is a normalizable source state obtained as an eigenstate of a resonance operator within a discrete basis of pseudostates. The long-range behavior of the resonant state is then computed using the free three-body propagator \(G_0(E_r) = (H_0 - E_r)^{-1}\) in the hyperspherical expansion method with an analytical transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) basis. The asymptotic coefficients for each hyperangular channel \(\beta\) are obtained from integrals of the source term with Bessel functions, and these coefficients determine the decay correlations between fragments.

## Key numbers

- The formalism is applied to the \(0^{+}\) ground-state resonance and the \(2^{+}\) excited-state resonance of \(^{16}\text{Be}\) (the \(^{14}\text{Be}+n+n\) system).
- The complex resonance energy is defined as \(E_r = \varepsilon_r - i\Gamma/2\), where \(\varepsilon_r\) is the real part and \(\Gamma\) is the width.
- The free three-body propagator for systems without Coulomb interaction uses Bessel functions \(x^{1/2} J_{K+2}(x)\) and Hankel functions \(x^{1/2} H^+_{K+2}(x)\), where \(K\) is the hypermomentum.
- The asymptotic amplitude for each channel \(\beta\) is given by \(A_\beta = \int d\rho' \sqrt{k\rho'} J_{K+2}(k\rho') \langle \beta, \rho' |\Phi_s\rangle\), where \(k\) is the complex momentum satisfying \(\hbar^2 k^2 / (2m) = E_r\).

## Context

This work extends the resonance-operator formalism previously developed by Casal and Gómez-Camacho (2019) for characterizing three-body resonances in a discrete basis, and puts on firmer ground the theoretical interpretation of experimental data on \(^{16}\text{Be}\) reported by Monteagudo et al. (2024). The approach contrasts with time-dependent formalisms such as that of Wang et al. (2021) and with final-state interaction models like those of Lednicky and Lyuboshitz (1982), by providing a stationary framework that directly connects the short-range internal structure to asymptotic decay correlations. The method enables the calculation of relative-energy distributions and neutron-neutron correlations for three-body resonances, which can be compared with invariant-mass spectroscopy experiments on neutron-rich systems beyond the dripline.
