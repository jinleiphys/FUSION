# Matching the Hagedorn mass spectrum with Lattice QCD results

> Based on recent Lattice QCD (LQCD) results obtained at finite temperature, we discuss modeling of the hadronic phase of QCD in the framework of Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) with discrete and continuous mass spectra. We focus on fluctuations of conserved charges, and show how a common limiting temperature can be used to constrain the Hagedorn exponential mass spectrum in different sectors of quantum number, through a matching of HRG and LQCD. For strange baryons, the extracted spectra are found to be consistent with all known and expected states listed by the Particle Data Group (PDG). The strange-mesonic sector, however, requires additional states in the intermediate mass range beyond that embodied in the database.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a common Hagedorn limiting temperature of approximately 180 MeV can describe the hadron mass spectrum across different quantum-number sectors, but that the strange-mesonic sector requires additional resonances beyond those listed in the Particle Data Group (PDG) database to match lattice QCD results on fluctuations of conserved charges.

## Method

The authors reanalyzed the PDG hadron mass spectrum using a continuous Hagedorn exponential mass spectrum parametrized as ρ(m) ∝ (m² + m₀²)⁻⁵/⁴ exp(m/T_H), with a common Hagedorn temperature T_H = 180 MeV applied across all hadronic sectors. They fitted the spectrum parameters to PDG data and then performed a matching procedure using lattice QCD results on second-order fluctuations of conserved charges (baryon number, strangeness, and baryon-strangeness correlations) to extract the continuous mass spectrum in different strange-hadron sectors.

## Key numbers

- The Hagedorn limiting temperature is set to T_H = 180 MeV, which exceeds the lattice QCD chiral crossover temperature T_c = 155(1)(8) MeV
- For all hadrons (ρ_H), the PDG fit yields m₀ = 0.529(22) GeV and a₀(m₀) = 0.744(40) GeV³/²
- For all baryons (ρ_B), the PDG fit yields m₀ = 0.145(23) GeV and a₀(m₀) = 0.135(7) GeV³/²
- For strange baryons with |S|=1 (ρ_B^{S=-1}), the lattice QCD fit yields m₀ = 0.193(96)(122) GeV and a₀(m₀) = 0.067(27) GeV³/²
- For strange mesons with |S|=1 (ρ_M^{S=-1}), the lattice QCD fit yields m₀ = 0.378(32)(95) GeV and a₀(m₀) = 0.099(24) GeV³/²
- At temperature T = 150 MeV, contributions from particles with mass M > 1.5 GeV account for 7% of the pressure, 26% of net-baryon number fluctuations, and 33% of baryon-strangeness correlations

## Context

This work extends the Hagedorn statistical bootstrap model approach to hadron mass spectra, previously studied by Broniowski (2000, 2004) who found different limiting temperatures for mesons (T_H^M ≈ 195 MeV) and baryons (T_H^B ≈ 140 MeV). The authors show that those lower baryon temperatures are inconsistent with lattice QCD results from the HotQCD and Budapest-Wuppertal collaborations, which indicate a common crossover temperature. The matching procedure enables identification of missing resonance states in the PDG database, particularly for strange mesons in the intermediate mass range, and provides a framework for using lattice QCD fluctuation data to constrain hadron spectra.
