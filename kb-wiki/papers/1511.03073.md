# The effect of the chiral chemical potential on the chiral phase transition in the NJL model with different regularization schemes

> We study the chiral phase transition in the presence of the chiral chemical potential $μ_5$ using the two-flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. In particular, we analyze the reason why one can obtain two opposite behaviors of the chiral critical temperature as a function of $μ_5$ in the framework of different regularization schemes. We compare the modifications of the chiral condensate and the critical temperature due to $μ_5$ in different regularization schemes, analytically and numerically. Finally, we find that, for the conventional hard-cutoff regularization scheme, the increasing dependence of the critical temperature on the chiral chemical potential is an artifact, which is caused by the fact that it does not include complete contribution from the thermal fluctuations. When the thermal contribution is fully taken into account, the chiral critical temperature should decrease with $μ_5$.

## Key claim

The chiral critical temperature as a function of the chiral chemical potential $\mu_5$ can show opposite behaviors depending on the regularization scheme used in the NJL model; the increasing dependence found with the conventional hard-cutoff scheme is an artifact because it does not include the complete contribution from thermal fluctuations, and when the thermal contribution is fully taken into account the chiral critical temperature should decrease with $\mu_5$.

## Method

The authors study the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the chiral limit at mean-field level, introducing a chiral chemical potential $\mu_5$ to account for chirality imbalance. They compare three regularization schemes: a soft cutoff using a form factor $f_\Lambda(p) = \sqrt{\Lambda^{2N}/(\Lambda^{2N}+|\mathbf{p}|^{2N})}$ with $N=5$, a conventional hard cutoff at momentum $\Lambda$, and a revised hard cutoff where the thermal part of the thermodynamic potential is integrated to infinity instead of being cut off at $\Lambda$. The thermodynamic potential is computed analytically in the small-$\mu_5$ and high-temperature limits, and numerically for arbitrary $\mu_5$, to determine the chiral condensate and critical temperature.

## Key numbers

* At $\mu_5=0$, the chiral critical temperature is $T_c^S = 174.3$ MeV for the soft cutoff scheme, $T_c^H = 194.9$ MeV for the conventional hard cutoff, and $T_c^{H'} = 174.6$ MeV for the revised hard cutoff.
* The model parameters are $\Lambda = 626.76$ MeV and $G_S\Lambda^2 = 2.02$ for the soft cutoff, and $\Lambda = 633.27$ MeV and $G_S\Lambda^2 = 2.19$ for the hard cutoff schemes, corresponding to $f_\pi = 92.3$ MeV and constituent quark mass $M = 325$ MeV.
* For the conventional hard cutoff scheme, the critical temperature at $\mu_5 = 0.2$ GeV is $T_c^H = 198.7$ MeV, and at $\mu_5 = 0.4$ GeV it is $T_c^H = 198.2$ MeV, showing a non-monotonic behavior with a maximum between these values.
* For the soft cutoff and revised hard cutoff schemes, the chiral critical temperature decreases monotonically with $\mu_5$, and a critical $\mu_5^c$ exists between 300 and 400 MeV (soft cutoff) or between 400 and 500 MeV (revised hard cutoff) above which the phase transition becomes first-order.

## Context

This work addresses a contradiction in the literature: Fukushima (2010) and Gatto (2011) found that the chiral critical temperature decreases with $\mu_5$ using the NJL model, while Cao (2015) found it increases with $\mu_5$ using the same model. The authors show this discrepancy arises solely from the choice of regularization scheme, specifically whether the thermal part of the thermodynamic potential is integrated to infinity or cut off at $\Lambda$. The analysis enables future studies to avoid the hard-cutoff artifact when investigating the QCD phase diagram with chirality imbalance, and clarifies that the decreasing $T_c$ with $\mu_5$ is the physically consistent result when thermal fluctuations are fully included.
