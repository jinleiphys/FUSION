# Structure of the mirror nuclei $^9$Be and $^9$B in a microscopic cluster model

> The structure of the mirror nuclei $^9$Be and $^9$B is studied in a microscopic $α+ α+ n$ and $α+ α+ p$ three-cluster model using a fully antisymmetrized 9-nucleon wave function. The two-nucleon interaction includes central and spin-orbit components and the Coulomb potential. The ground state of $^9$Be is obtained accurately with the stochastic variational method, while several particle-unbound states of both $^9$Be and $^9$B are investigated with the complex scaling method.The calculation for $^9$Be supports the recent identification for the existence of two broad states around 6.5 MeV, and predicts the $\frac{3}{2}^{-}_2$ and $\frac{5}{2}^{-}_2$ states at about 4.5 MeV and 8 MeV, respectively. The similarity of the calculated spectra of $^9$Be and $^9$B enables one to identify unknown spins and parities of the $^9$B states. Available data on electromagnetic moments and elastic electron scatterings are reproduced very well. The enhancement of the $E$1 transition of the first excited state in $^9$Be is well accounted for. The calculated density of $^9$Be is found to reproduce the reaction cross section on a Carbon target. The analysis of the beta decay of $^9$Li to $^9$Be clearly shows that the wave function of $^9$Be must contain a small component that cannot be described by the simple $α+ α+ n$ model. This small component can be well accounted for by extending a configuration space to include the distortion of the $α$-particle to $t+p$ and $h+n$ partitions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a microscopic α+α+n and α+α+p three-cluster model, using a fully antisymmetrized 9-nucleon wave function, can accurately describe the structure of the mirror nuclei ⁹Be and ⁹B, but the wave function of ⁹Be must contain a small component beyond the simple α+α+n model to account for the beta decay of ⁹Li, which can be included by extending the configuration space to allow α-particle distortion into t+p and h+n partitions.

## Method

The authors constructed a fully antisymmetrized 9-nucleon wave function using a microscopic three-cluster model with α+α+n and α+α+p arrangements, including both (αα)N and (αN)α cluster configurations. The ground state of ⁹Be was obtained using the stochastic variational method (SVM) with nodeless harmonic-oscillator basis functions, while particle-unbound states of both nuclei were investigated using the complex scaling method and the stabilization method. The Minnesota nucleon-nucleon interaction with central and spin-orbit components plus Coulomb potential was employed, with the exchange-mixture parameter u=0.94 chosen to reproduce the ground state energy of ⁹Be.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy of ⁹Be from the α+α+n threshold: −1.431 MeV (full calculation)
- Root mean square (rms) radius of point nucleon in ⁹Be ground state: 2.50 fm
- Charge radius of ⁹Be (including proton finite size): 2.54 fm, fitting the experimental value of 2.519±0.012 fm
- Magnetic moment of ⁹Be ground state: calculated value matches experimental μ_exp = −1.1778 μ_N
- The 1/2⁺ state of ⁹Be lies at 1.68 MeV excitation energy, just 111 keV above the three-body threshold, with a width of 217±10 keV
- The 3/2⁻₂ state predicted at about 4.3 MeV excitation energy
- Two broad overlapping resonances with 7/2⁻ and 9/2⁺ obtained at about 6.5 MeV
- The 5/2⁻₂ state predicted at about 7.9 MeV (8 MeV in abstract)
- The 3/2⁻₂ state predicted at about 4.5 MeV (from abstract)
- The 5/2⁻₂ state predicted at about 8 MeV (from abstract)
- B(E1) value for the 1/2⁺ to ground state transition: 0.24 W.u. with u=1.0, 0.18 W.u. with u=0.98
- The 1/2⁻ state in ⁹B predicted at 2.43 MeV, compared to experimental location at 2.83 MeV from a recent ⁹Be(p,n) reaction
- Dimension of the basis for the ⁹Be ground state: around 90

## Context

This work extends previous microscopic cluster-model calculations on ⁹Be using the resonating group method (Zahn, 1970s) and generator coordinate method (Okabe, 1970s; Descouvemont, 1980s), which were limited either in treating the Pauli principle or in handling three-body dynamics. The calculation supports the recent experimental identification by Glickman (1990s) and Dixit (1990s) of two broad states around 6.5 MeV in ⁹Be, and predicts unknown spins and parities for ⁹B states based on mirror symmetry. The results enable future studies of heavier Be isotopes within the same α+α+neutrons cluster framework, and demonstrate that the beta decay of ⁹Li to ⁹Be reveals components requiring cluster distortion beyond the simple three-cluster picture.
