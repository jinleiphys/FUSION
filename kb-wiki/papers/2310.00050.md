# Scalar boson emission from a magnetized relativistic plasma

> We investigate the differential emission rate of neutral scalar bosons from a highly magnetized relativistic plasma. We show that three processes contribute at the leading order: particle splitting ($ψ\rightarrow ψ+ϕ$), antiparticle splitting ($\barψ \rightarrow \barψ+ϕ$), and particle-antiparticle annihilation ($ψ+ \barψ\rightarrow ϕ$). This is in contrast to the scenario with zero magnetic field, where only the annihilation processes contribute to boson production. We examine the impact of Landau-level quantization on the energy dependence of the rate and investigate the angular distribution of emitted scalar bosons. The differential rate resulting from both (anti)particle splitting and annihilation processes are typically suppressed in the direction of the magnetic field and enhanced in perpendicular directions. Overall, the background magnetic field significantly amplifies the total emission rate. We speculate that our model calculations provide valuable theoretical insights with potentially important applications.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in a highly magnetized relativistic plasma, neutral scalar boson emission at leading order receives contributions from three processes, particle splitting, antiparticle splitting, and particle-antiparticle annihilation, whereas in the zero-magnetic-field case only annihilation contributes; however, this conclusion is conditional on the magnetic field being nonzero and on the scalar boson having arbitrary momentum direction, because for momenta exactly parallel to the field the splitting processes are kinematically forbidden.

## Method

The authors compute the one-loop retarded self-energy of a neutral scalar field coupled to a single fermion flavor via a Yukawa interaction, using the Landau-level representation of the fermion propagator in a constant background magnetic field. They extract the imaginary part of the self-energy via the Sokhotski formula, perform the Matsubara sum to include finite-temperature effects, and integrate over the longitudinal momentum analytically using the energy-conservation delta function. The resulting expression is a convergent double series over Landau levels, which is then evaluated numerically to obtain the differential emission rate and its angular distribution.

## Key numbers

- For the subthreshold case \(M = m/3\) and a moderate magnetic field \(|qB| = (2m)^2\) at temperature \(T = 5m\), the total emission rate is dominated by particle-splitting processes at low boson energies, with the annihilation contribution becoming comparable only near the threshold \(\Omega \approx 2m\).
- For the suprathreshold case \(M = 3m\) and a strong magnetic field \(|qB| = (5m)^2\) at \(T = 15m\), the differential rate is suppressed in the direction parallel to the magnetic field (\(\theta = 0\)) and enhanced in the perpendicular direction (\(\theta = \pi/2\)), with the ellipticity parameter \(v_2\) positive for most energies.
- The zero-field limit of the self-energy (Eq. (A18)) yields \(\text{Im}[\Sigma^R] = -\frac{g^2}{8\pi} (M^2 - 4m^2) \left[ \frac{\sqrt{M^2 - 4m^2}}{\sqrt{M^2}} + \frac{2T}{|\mathbf{k}|} \ln \frac{1+e^{-E_+/T}}{1+e^{-E_-/T}} \right] \theta(M^2 - 4m^2)\), which is nonvanishing only when \(M \geq 2m\).

## Context

This work extends the study of scalar boson emission from a magnetized plasma beyond the simplified kinematics of Bandyopadhyay (2018), which only considered momenta parallel to the magnetic field, and beyond the zero-temperature weak-field decay analysis of Jaber-Urquiza (2018). It provides a full treatment of arbitrary momentum direction, arbitrary magnetic field strength, and finite temperature, enabling comparison with photon emission (Yee 2013, Tuchin 2014, Wang 2020) and dilepton emission (Sadooghi 2016, Bandyopadhyay 2016, Wang 2022) to isolate the role of particle spin. The results may inform models of dark matter or inflationary cosmology where neutral scalar bosons interact with magnetized plasmas.
