# Beyond-mean-field effects on the symmetry energy and its slope from the low-lying dipole response of $^{68}$Ni

> We study low-energy dipole excitations in the unstable nucleus $^{68}$Ni with the beyond-mean-field (BMF) subtracted second random-phase-approximation (SSRPA) model based on Skyrme interactions. First, strength distributions are compared with available experimental data and transition densities of some selected peaks are analyzed. The so-called isospin splitting is also discussed by studying the isoscalar/isovector character of such excitations. We estimate then in an indirect way BMF effects on the symmetry energy of infinite matter and on its slope starting from the BMF SSRPA low-lying strength distribution. For this, several linear correlations are used, the first one being a correlation existing between the contribution (associated with the low-energy strength) to the total energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) and the slope of the symmetry energy. BMF estimates for the slope of the symmetry energy can be extracted in this way. Correlations between such a slope and the neutron-skin thickness of $^{68}$Ni and correlations between the neutron-skin thickness of $^{68}$Ni and the electric dipole polarizability times the symmetry energy are then used to deduce BMF effects on the symmetry energy.

## Key claim

Beyond-mean-field (BMF) effects, as modeled by the subtracted second random-phase-approximation (SSRPA), increase the slope of the symmetry energy and the neutron-skin thickness of $^{68}$Ni compared to mean-field predictions, but the authors emphasize that their estimation is qualitative and not a precise quantitative prediction.

## Method

The authors computed the low-energy dipole strength distribution of $^{68}$Ni using the SSRPA model with the Skyrme interaction SGII, and compared it to RPA results and experimental data. They then used linear correlations between the percentage of the energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) associated with the low-lying strength and the slope of the symmetry energy, between the neutron-skin thickness and the slope, and between the electric dipole polarizability times the symmetry energy and the neutron-skin thickness, to extract BMF estimates for the symmetry energy $J$ and its slope $L$.

## Key numbers

* The SSRPA low-energy EWSR percentage for $^{68}$Ni computed up to 12 MeV with SGII is 3.75%, compared to 2.35% in RPA with the same interaction.
* The mean-field value of the slope $L$ for SGII is 37.7 MeV; the extracted BMF value is 60.815 $\pm$ 16.982 MeV.
* The mean-field neutron-skin thickness $\Delta r_{np}$ for $^{68}$Ni with SGII is 0.154 fm; the BMF value is 0.173 $\pm$ 0.018 fm.
* The mean-field symmetry energy $J$ for SGII is 26.83 MeV; the BMF value is 27.617 $\pm$ 5.004 MeV.
* The electric dipole polarizability $\alpha$ for $^{68}$Ni computed in RPA with SGII is 4.48 fm$^3$.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of correlations between low-lying dipole strength, neutron-skin thickness, and nuclear matter symmetry energy parameters (Piekarewicz 2006, Klimkiewicz 2007, Carbone 2010) by incorporating beyond-mean-field effects via the SSRPA model. The analysis uses linear correlations similar to those explored by Roca-Maza (2013) and Lattimer and Steiner (2014) for $^{208}$Pb, but applied to $^{68}$Ni. The results suggest that BMF correlations tend to stiffen the neutron matter equation of state and increase the neutron skin, and the authors note that their BMF estimate for $L$ and $J$ is compatible with experimental constraints from the electric dipole polarizability of $^{208}$Pb and from heavy-ion collisions, within uncertainties.
