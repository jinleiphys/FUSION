# Non-relativistic Model for the Semileptonic Λ_b \to Λ_c Decay

> We calculate the decay width for $Λ_b \to Λ_c e {\bar ν}$ in the frame work of a nonrelativistic quark (NRQ) model of heavy baryons where the light quarks play the role of spectators. Our calculation does not make an explicit use of the heavy quark symmetry. The branching ratio for the above process as calculated here agrees reasonably well with the experimental value.

# Non-relativistic Model for the Semileptonic Λ_b → Λ_c Decay

## Key claim

The paper calculates the decay width and branching ratio for Λ_b → Λ_c e ν̅ using a nonrelativistic quark model without explicit use of heavy quark symmetry, and finds that the branching ratio agrees reasonably well with the experimental value, though the experimental measurement is not a pure measurement because it involves the product branching fraction with the fraction of b-flavored baryons produced in Z decays.

## Method

The authors employ a nonrelativistic quark model where the Λ_b baryon contains a heavy b quark and two light spectator u and d quarks, and the Λ_c contains a c quark with the same spectator light quarks. They use a variational approach with Gaussian trial wavefunctions in Jacobi coordinates, minimizing the ground state energies for both baryons using a Hamiltonian with linear confinement and Coulomb-like terms. The form factors are extracted by comparing the hadronic matrix elements computed in the model with the general Lorentz-covariant decomposition, and the decay width is then integrated over the kinematic variable y = q²/m_Λ_b².

## Key numbers

- Decay width: 5.7 × 10¹⁰ sec⁻¹
- Branching ratio: 7.6%
- Form factors at zero recoil: g = 8.02 GeV, g₊ = −0.15, g₋ = 0.075, a = 7.07 GeV, a± = 0.008
- Variational parameters for Λ_b: α₁ᵇ = 0.264, α₂ᵇ = 0.653
- Variational parameters for Λ_c: α₁ᶜ = 0.261, α₂ᶜ = 0.568
- Quark masses used: m_u = 0.35 GeV, m_c = 1.80 GeV, m_b = 5.00 GeV
- Strong coupling: α_s = 0.5
- Confinement parameter: a₁ = 0.18 GeV²
- Numerical values of kinematic parameters: α = 0.43, β = 0.83, γ = 0.69
- Experimental branching ratio (from PDG): (10.0 ± 3.0)% for B(Λ_b → Λ_c l⁻ ν_l X)

## Context

This work extends nonrelativistic quark model calculations, previously applied to heavy meson semileptonic decays by Isgur, Scora, Grinstein, and Wise (ISGW), to the baryonic case of Λ_b → Λ_c decay. The results are compared with the pole-dominated form factor approach of Single (1993), which assumed a common resonance mass of 6.0 GeV for all form factors and obtained a decay width of 5.7 × 10¹⁰ sec⁻¹ and branching ratio of 7.7%, nearly identical to the present calculation. The paper enables further tests of nonrelativistic quark models in heavy baryon decays and provides a framework for extracting the CKM matrix element V_cb from this channel, though the experimental measurement remains complicated by the need to know the fraction f(b → Λ_b) of b-flavored baryons produced in Z decays.
