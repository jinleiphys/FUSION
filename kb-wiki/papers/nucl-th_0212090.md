# Momentum dependence of symmetry potential in asymmetric nuclear matter for transport model calculations

> For transport model simulations of collisions between two nuclei which have $N/Z$ significantly different from unity one needs a one-body potential which is both isospin and momentum dependent. This work provides sets of such potentials.

# Momentum dependence of symmetry potential in asymmetric nuclear matter for transport model calculations

## Key claim

This work provides parametrized, momentum-dependent one-body potentials for neutrons and protons in asymmetric nuclear matter that are suitable for use in Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model simulations of heavy-ion collisions, but the authors explicitly state they do not aim to add anything fundamental to the many-body theory, their goal is purely to produce a usable parametrization that captures the main characteristics of the momentum dependence already known from more sophisticated calculations.

## Method

The authors used the Gogny effective interaction (Dechargé and Gogny, 1980) as a reference to derive the momentum dependence of the single-particle potential in cold asymmetric nuclear matter. They then constructed two families of parametrized potentials: a more accurate "MDI" form using two Gaussian exchange terms, and a simpler "GBD" form (extending the Gale-Bertsch-Das Gupta parametrization) using a single Lorentzian momentum dependence. Parameters were fixed by reproducing saturation properties (density 0.16 fm⁻³, binding energy 16 MeV, compressibility ≈210 MeV, symmetry energy ≈30 MeV) and by fitting the momentum dependence of the Gogny potential. For each family, two variants were produced: one where the symmetry energy continues to rise with density (x=0) and one where it bends over at high density (x=1).

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: 0.16 fm⁻³ for all parametrizations (except BGBD at 0.163 fm⁻³)
- Binding energy at saturation: -16 MeV per nucleon for all parametrizations
- Symmetry energy at saturation: 31.623 MeV (including kinetic contribution) for all parametrizations
- Compressibility K: 210.68 MeV for MDI(0) and MDI(1); 214.68 MeV for GBD(0) and GBD(1); 215.0 MeV for BGBD(1/15)
- The momentum-dependent symmetry potential is strongly momentum dependent for momenta k ≤ 5 fm⁻¹, with particularly strong dependence at high densities (ρ/ρ₀ = 2)
- The symmetry potential is approximately momentum independent only at high momenta above about 1 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends earlier momentum-dependent mean-field parametrizations for symmetric nuclear matter (Gale, Bertsch and Das Gupta, 1987; Welke et al., 1988; Gale et al., 1990) to the asymmetric case where neutrons and protons experience different potentials. It corrects the deficiency in earlier BUU calculations (Li, 1997-2002) that used momentum-independent isospin-dependent potentials. The parametrizations are guided by the Gogny interaction and by Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations (Bombaci and Lombardo, 1991). The resulting potentials enable transport model studies of observables sensitive to the density dependence of the symmetry energy, such as neutron/proton ratios, differential flow, and correlation functions, which are essential for understanding neutron star properties and the equation of state of neutron-rich matter.
