# Evolution of E2 transition strength in deformed hafnium isotopes from new measurements on $^{172}$Hf, $^{174}$Hf, and $^{176}$Hf

> The available data for E2 transition strengths in the region between neutron-deficient Hf and Pt isotopes are far from complete. More and precise data are needed to enhance the picture of structure evolution in this region and to test state-of-the-art nuclear models. In a simple model, the maximum collectivity is expected at the middle of the major shell. However, for actual nuclei, this picture may no longer be the case, and one should use a more realistic nuclear-structure model. We address this point by studying the spectroscopy of Hf. We remeasure the 2^+_1 half-lives of 172,174,176Hf, for which there is some disagreement in the literature. The main goal is to measure, for the first time, the half-lives of higher-lying states of the rotational band. The new results are compared to a theoretical calculation for absolute transition strengths. The half-lives were measured using γ-γand conversion-electron-γdelayed coincidences with the fast timing method. For the determination of half-lives in the picosecond region, the generalized centroid difference method was applied. For the theoretical calculation of the spectroscopic properties, the interacting boson model is employed, whose Hamiltonian is determined based on microscopic energy-density functional calculations. The measured 2^+_1 half-lives disagree with results from earlier γ-γfast timing measurements, but are in agreement with data from Coulomb excitation experiments and other methods. Half-lives of the 4^+_1 and 6^+_1 states were measured, as well as a lower limit for the 8^+_1 states. We show the importance of the mass-dependence of effective boson charge in the description of E2 transition rates in chains of nuclei. It encourages further studies of the microscopic origin of this mass dependence. New data on transition rates in nuclei from neighboring isotopic chains could support these studies.

## Key claim

The measured 2⁺₁ half-lives for ¹⁷²Hf, ¹⁷⁴Hf, and ¹⁷⁶Hf disagree with earlier γ-γ fast timing measurements but agree with data from Coulomb excitation experiments and other methods, and the interacting boson model description of E2 transition rates across the isotopic chain requires a mass-dependent effective boson charge.

## Method

Half-lives were measured using γ-γ and conversion-electron-γ delayed coincidences with the fast timing method, applying the generalized centroid difference method for picosecond-regime determinations. The 2⁺₁ half-lives were obtained from ce-γ coincidences using the Cologne Orange spectrometer and LaBr₃(Ce) detectors, while higher-lying yrast state half-lives (4⁺₁, 6⁺₁, 8⁺₁) were measured via γ-γ coincidences with the GCD method. Theoretical calculations employed the interacting boson model (IBM-2) whose Hamiltonian was determined by mapping deformation energy surfaces from microscopic energy-density functional calculations using the Gogny D1M parametrization.

- ¹⁷⁴Hf 8⁺₁ half-life: lower limit less than 10 ps
- For ¹⁷⁶Hf, the K-conversion coefficient α_K = 1.18 and L-conversion coefficient α_L = 3.49 for the 88.35 keV E2 transition

## Context

This work extends measurements of E2 transition strengths in the neutron-deficient hafnium-to-platinum region, where data were previously incomplete and sometimes contradictory. The results improve the data situation for hafnium isotopes and enable testing of the IBM Hamiltonian determined from microscopic mean-field calculations (Gogny D1M and D1S parametrizations), following similar studies on tungsten and osmium isotopes by Williams (2012) and Rudigier (2010). The findings encourage further studies of the microscopic origin of the mass-dependent effective boson charge and motivate new experimental values on transition rates from neighboring isotopic chains.
