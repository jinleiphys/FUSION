# Transient state of matter in hadron and nucleus collisions

> We discuss properties of the specific strongly interacting transient collective state of matter in hadron and nuclei reactions and emphasize similarity in their dynamics. We consider elliptic flow introduced for description of nucleus collisions and discuss its possible behavior in hadronic reactions.

# Transient state of matter in hadron and nucleus collisions

## Key claim
The authors argue that the same strongly interacting transient collective state of matter, a coherent system of massive constituent quarks undergoing collective rotation, is produced in both hadron and nucleus collisions, and that elliptic flow in hadronic reactions can arise from this rotation rather than requiring a deconfined quark-gluon plasma. The claim is conditional on the assumption that the transient state originates from the mechanism of spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking in QCD, and that the geometrical picture of hadron collisions is in complete analogy with nucleus collisions.

## Method
The authors develop a theoretical model based on the chiral quark model, where hadrons consist of valence constituent quarks embedded in a quark condensate. In non-central collisions, the overlap region generates a rotating cloud of massive virtual quarks via the effective Lagrangian of non-perturbative QCD; the valence quarks then excite parts of this rotating cloud, and the resulting hadronization (via coalescence or fragmentation) produces the observed elliptic flow. The model uses a U-matrix formalism with s-channel unitarity to relate the bare anisotropic flow coefficients to the measured ones, and estimates the orbital angular momentum of the transient matter as proportional to the impact parameter and the square root of the collision energy.

## Key numbers
- The constituent quark interaction radius is given by \(R_Q = \xi / m_Q\) with \(\xi \simeq 2\), yielding \(R_Q \simeq 1\) fm, while the geometrical radius of a constituent quark is \(r_Q \simeq 0.2\) fm.
- The saturation of the quark elliptic flow \(v_2^Q\) occurs at transverse momentum \(p_\perp^Q \geq 1\) GeV/c, corresponding to \(1/r_Q\).
- The total inelastic cross-section in the preasymptotic region up to \(\sqrt{s} \sim 0.5\) TeV follows \(\sigma_{tot}(s) = a + b\sqrt{s}\).
- The parameter \(k\) in the negative binomial multiplicity distribution varies from 4.6 to 3.2 at CERN SPS energies, interpreted as the average number of active valence quarks.
- The integrated elliptic flow \(v_2\) is predicted to be proportional to \(\sqrt{s}\), consistent with experimental data for nucleus collisions.

## Context
This work extends the chiral quark model approach of Troshin and Tyurin (2005) for elastic scattering to particle production, and builds on the observation by Weiner (2005) that deconfined matter may already have been observed in hadronic reactions. The authors draw on the constituent quark scaling of elliptic flow established by Voloshin (2003) and the experimental results from RHIC experiments, particularly the sQGP (strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma) interpretation. The paper proposes that the same rotating transient state appears in both hadron and nucleus collisions, enabling future studies of collective rotation effects in pp collisions at the LHC, and suggests that such rotation effects would be absent if a genuine gas of free quarks and gluons were formed.
