# Quark jet evolution: from classical to quantum simulation

> Quark jet provides one of the best ways to probe the matter produced in ultrarelativistic high-energy collisions, from cold nuclear matter to the hot quark-gluon plasma. In this proceeding paper, we review a series of works on the development of nonperturbative computational framework of in-medium quark jet evolution, from classical to quantum simulation. The application of the time-dependent Basis Light-front Quantization (tBLFQ), a nonperturbative computational approach based on light-front Hamiltonian formalism, to in-medium jet evolution enables a fully quantum treatment to the jet state on the amplitude level. Based on the tBLFQ framework, with applying novel quantum technologies, we have constructed a digital quantum circuit that tracks the evolution of a multi-particle jet probe within a stochastic color background field. With the obtained simulation results, we extracted the medium induced modification in terms of jet momentum broadening and gluon production. These studies provide a baseline for future works of in-medium jet evolution using quantum computers.

# Quark jet evolution: from classical to quantum simulation

## Key claim
The paper reviews a nonperturbative computational framework for in-medium quark jet evolution that has been implemented both on classical computers and as a digital quantum circuit, but the quantum simulation results shown are limited to a highly simplified basis with only one transverse lattice site per side (N_perp = 1) and a reduced color group (N_c = 2), making them a proof-of-principle rather than a full physical simulation.

## Method
The authors apply time-dependent Basis Light-front Quantization (tBLFQ), a nonperturbative approach based on light-front Hamiltonian formalism, to evolve a quark jet state in a truncated Fock space of |q> and |qg> components through a stochastic color background field modeled by the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model. The classical simulation uses a product formula with Fourier transforms between transverse position and momentum space to implement the time-evolution operator, while the quantum simulation encodes the basis states into qubit registers and uses trotterized evolution with Pauli evolution gates and quantum Fourier transforms.

## Key numbers
- Transverse lattice parameters for classical simulation: N_perp = 16, L_perp = 50 GeV^{-1}, with total longitudinal momentum quanta K = 8.5, and medium length L_eta = 50 GeV^{-1}
- Transverse lattice parameters for quantum simulation: N_perp = 1, L_perp = 32 GeV^{-1}, with K = 3.5 and L_eta = 10 fm
- The total number of basis states for the |q> + |qg> space is given by N_tot = 2 N_c (2 N_perp)^2 + 4 N_c(N_c^2 - 1) floor(K) (2 N_perp)^4
- The saturation scale is defined as Q_s^2 = C_F (g^2 mu_tilde)^2 L_eta / (2 pi), with C_F = (N_c^2 - 1) / (2 N_c)
- The quantum simulation requires n_Q = log_2(2^3 * ceil(K) * (2 N_perp)^4) qubits

## Context
This work extends the tBLFQ framework developed by Li et al. (2020, 2021, 2023) for classical simulation of quark jet evolution, and builds on the quantum strategy proposed by Barata et al. (2021) for computing the jet quenching parameter. The quantum simulation algorithm was first developed for the single quark Fock sector by Barata et al. (2022) and then extended to the |q> + |qg> Fock space by Barata et al. (2023). These studies provide a baseline for future works of in-medium jet evolution using quantum computers, with extensions to higher Fock sectors and more efficient strategies underway.
