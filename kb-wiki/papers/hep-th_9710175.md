# Light-Front QCD: A Constituent Picture of Hadrons

> Light-front coordinates offer a scenario in which a constituent approximation of hadron structure can emerge from QCD. This requires cutoffs that violate Lorentz covariance and gauge invariance, and a new renormalization group formalism based on a similarity transformation is used with coupling coherence to fix counterterms that restore these symmetries. The counterterms contain functions of longitudinal momentum fractions which severely complicate renormalization, but they also offer possible resolutions of apparent contradictions between the constituent picture and QCD. The similarity transformation and coupling coherence are applied to QED; and it is shown that the resultant Hamiltonian leads to the correct bound state results. The same techniques are applied to QCD and it is shown that a simple confinement mechanism and a reasonable description of heavy quark bound states emerge naturally.

# Light-Front QCD: A Constituent Picture of Hadrons

## Key claim

A constituent approximation for hadron structure can be derived from QCD using light-front coordinates, but only if cutoffs that violate Lorentz covariance and gauge invariance are employed, and a similarity renormalization group formalism with coupling coherence is used to fix counterterms that restore these symmetries. The author acknowledges that this approach requires the assumption that all effective interactions are local in the transverse direction, which Ken Wilson does not believe will hold true.

## Method

The approach uses a perturbative similarity renormalization group and coupling coherence to compute the renormalized Hamiltonian as an expansion in powers of the canonical coupling, without adding any operators beyond those generated by the underlying theory. Bound state perturbation theory is then applied by separating the Hamiltonian into a nonperturbative part treated exactly and a perturbative part, with the cutoff lowered to a critical value where a constituent approximation becomes valid. The method is first applied to QED through the Lamb shift, then to QCD for heavy quark bound states, using light-cone gauge with zero modes removed and a trivial vacuum.

## Key numbers

- Schwinger model (massless QED in 1+1 dimensions): the physical boson invariant mass is M² = e²/π, with a flat momentum-space wave function φ(k) = √(4π) for the electron-positron pair
- Schwinger model: the linear Coulomb potential in position space is v(x⁻) = (q₁q₂/π)[2/ε − (π/2)|x⁻| + O(ε)], containing both a divergent constant and the linear confining term
- Schwinger model: the divergent 1/ε term in the one-body operator H'₀ is (e²/π)(1/ε), which cancels exactly in charge-neutral states against the divergent constant from the potential

## Context

This work extends the light-front Hamiltonian approach initiated by Dirac (1949) and builds on the similarity renormalization group developed by Glazek and Wilson (1993-1994) and coupling coherence introduced by Perry and Wilson (1993). The Schwinger model analysis follows Bergknoff (1977), who showed the physical boson is a pure electron-positron state in light-front coordinates. The paper enables a program for computing hadron bound states from QCD using a truncated Fock space, with confinement emerging from effective two-body interactions rather than requiring explicit gluon degrees of freedom. Open questions include whether transverse locality holds, how to handle chiral symmetry breaking operators that do not appear at any finite order in the coupling, and whether the assumption of a trivial vacuum with zero modes removed is justified in 3+1 dimensions.
