# Finite formation time effects in quasi-elastic $(e,e')$ scattering on nuclear targets

> The problem of the final state interaction in quasi-elastic $(e,e')$ scattering at large $Q^2$, is investigated by exploiting the idea that the ejected nucleon needs a finite amount of time to assume its asymptotic form. It is shown that when the dependence of the scattering amplitude of the ejected nucleon on its virtuality is taken into account, the final state interaction is decreased. The developed approach is simpler to implement than the one based on the color transparency description of the damping of the final state interaction, and is essentially equivalent to the latter in the case of the single rescattering term. The $(e,e')$ process on the deuteron is numerically investigated and it is shown that, at $x=1$, appreciable finite formation time effects at $Q^2$ of the order of 10 (GeV/c)$^2$ are expected.

## Key claim

The paper argues that final state interactions in quasi-elastic $(e,e')$ scattering at large $Q^2$ are reduced when one accounts for the finite formation time of the ejected nucleon, and that this reduction is essentially equivalent to color transparency effects at the single rescattering level, but the approach is simpler to implement than multi-channel Glauber models.

## Method

The authors introduce a dependence of the nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitude on the virtuality of the ejected nucleon, using a factorized form-factor $F(v)$ that suppresses the amplitude for off-shell nucleons. They derive modified Glauber-type expressions where the standard step function $\theta(z)$ in the nucleon propagator is replaced by a function $J(z)$ that vanishes at large $Q^2$, representing the finite formation length $l(Q^2) = Q^2/(xm M^2)$. The formalism is applied to the deuteron target, where only single rescattering contributes, and the structure function is computed by taking discontinuities of the forward virtual photon-deuteron amplitude.

## Key numbers

- At $x=1$, appreciable finite formation time effects are expected at $Q^2$ of the order of 10 (GeV/c)$^2$.
- The formation length is given by $l(Q^2) = Q^2/(xm M^2)$, where $M^2$ is the average excitation mass squared of the ejectile.
- The leading rescattering correction to the structure function vanishes as $1/Q^4$ at high $Q^2$, while the relative correction to the proton production channel is of order $1/Q^2$.
- For the deuteron, the average excitation mass $m^*$ is taken between 1.44 GeV and 2.4 GeV, with a reasonable value of 1.8 GeV used for numerical predictions.
- The single rescattering contribution to the forward amplitude behaves as $m M^2 R_A / Q^2$ at large $Q^2$, where $R_A$ is the nuclear radius and $m$ the nucleon mass.

## Context

This work extends the study of final state interactions in quasi-elastic electron scattering by introducing finite formation time effects as an alternative to the color transparency description developed by earlier authors (e.g., the multi-channel Glauber approach of Frankfurt, Strikman, and collaborators in the 1990s). The approach is simpler than the coupled-channel formalism because it requires only specifying the dependence of the amplitude on virtuality rather than modeling the propagation of excited $3q$ states. The numerical results for the deuteron agree with the conclusions of the conventional color transparency approach (e.g., as discussed by Farrar et al. and by Jennings and Miller), and the formalism can be generalized to heavier nuclei where multiple rescatterings become important.
