# Shocks in Quark-Gluon Plasmas

> Hydrodynamics is known to describe matter created in high energy heavy ion collisions well. Large deposition of energy by passing jets should create not only the sound waves, already discussed in literature, but also the shocks waves of finite amplitude. This paper is an introduction to relativistic shocks, which go through elementary energy and momentum continuity argument, to weak shocks treated in Navier-Stokes approximation, to out-of-equilibrium setting of AdS/CFT. While we have not yet found numerical solution to corresponding Einstein equations, we have found a variational approximation to the sum of their squares. Our general conclusion is that deviations from LS and NS hydrodynmical shock profiles are surprisingly small, even for strong shocks. We end with a list of open questions which the exact solution should be able to answer.

## Key claim

The paper argues that strong shocks (with rapidity jump Y=O(1)) in quark-gluon plasma can be treated with Navier-Stokes hydrodynamics, and that deviations from NS shock profiles due to higher-gradient corrections are surprisingly small, even for strong shocks where no small parameter exists.

## Method

The author derives relativistic shock continuity equations from energy-momentum conservation for a conformal equation of state, then solves the Navier-Stokes equations numerically for strong shocks. To test the validity of the NS approximation, the paper applies the Lublinsky-Shuryak (LS) resumed hydrodynamics, which incorporates higher-gradient terms from AdS/CFT, and compares the gradient expansion to the NS solution. For the full AdS/CFT setting, the author proposes a variational approach using the scalar square of the modified Einstein tensor, since the Einstein equations themselves are too complex to solve directly.

## Key numbers

* The holographic viscosity-to-entropy ratio is eta/s = 1/(4 pi) for any strongly coupled theory with a holographic dual.
* For a strong shock example, the width of the shock is about 4 units of the holographic distance to the horizon 1/(pi T), corresponding to about 0.6 fm for T = 0.4 GeV.
* The energy deposited by a jet into QGP can be as large as Delta E approximately 100 GeV, deposited into a volume of about 10 fm^3, giving an energy density delta epsilon approximately 10 GeV/fm^3.
* The LS resumed correction to the NS solution for a strong shock is about 0.003, more than an order of magnitude below the level suggested by the expansion coefficient 1/8.
* For a Fermi-step profile with width w = 0.75, the gradient expansion shows oscillating corrections of increasing amplitude at 12 derivatives, indicating asymptotic series behavior.

## Context

This work extends the study of shocks in heavy ion collisions initiated by Landau and Belenky, who applied shock theory to matter compression at initial time, and later work on deflagration and detonation shocks. It builds on the discovery that quark-gluon plasma is a very good liquid with small viscosity, as shown by Teaney (2001), Huovinen (2001), and Hirano (2002), and on the AdS/CFT prediction of eta/s = 1/(4 pi) by Policastro (2001). The paper introduces the LS resumed hydrodynamics from Lublinsky and Shuryak (2009) as a tool to test the convergence of the gradient expansion. It enables future study of modified Mach cones from strong shocks in jet quenching events at LHC, where the energy deposition is large enough to create strong shocks at early times.
