# Detailed analysis of eta production in proton-proton collisions

## Key claim
The paper tests whether existing multiresonance coupled-channel partial wave analyses (PWA) of two-body processes can reliably describe the three-body process pp → ppη, specifically questioning whether the inverted shape of the proton-eta differential cross section in the three-body final state can be explained without invoking strong initial-state interactions (ISI), final-state interactions (FSI), or off-mass-shell effects.

## Method
The authors developed a simple meson-exchange model for the pp → ppη reaction, using the two-body T-matrices from existing coupled-channel multiresonance PWA (Carnegie-Mellon-Berkeley type) as input. They compared the predicted differential cross sections for the proton-eta subsystem against experimental data from Uppsala near threshold, and also compared their model's predictions to those of other existing models (Lag91, Vet91, Fal96b, Ged97, San98) under equivalent assumptions.

## Key numbers
- The experimental pp → ppη total and differential cross sections were measured near threshold at Uppsala (Upp).
- The comparison shows that the differential pη cross section in the three-body process has a different curvature from the two-body π⁻p → ηn process at comparable energy.
- Only higher partial waves such as D₁₃ can account for the opposite curvature observed in the data.

## Context
This work extends the ongoing re-evaluation of baryon resonance parameters by the Baryon Resonance Analysis Group (BRAG), which had chosen three independent analyses to repeat the earlier CMU-LBL (Cutkosky 1979) and Höhler (1983) formalisms with improved data. The paper specifically tests whether the two-body T-matrices from these analyses can explain the three-body process pp → ppη, which had been measured carefully by Uppsala (Upp). The results raise the possibility that the D₁₃ partial wave may not be confidently extracted from two-body processes alone, and that future work should investigate whether ρ-meson exchange dominance in the hadron η production channel can resolve the discrepancy.
