# Phase Structure and Transport Properties of Dense Quark Matter

> We provide a summary of our current knowledge of the phase structure of very dense quark matter. We concentrate on the question how the ground state at asymptotically high density -- color-flavor-locked (CFL) matter -- is modified as the density is lowered. We discuss the nature of the quasi-particle excitations, and present work on the transport properties of dense QCD matter.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase is the rigorously computable ground state of dense quark matter at asymptotically high densities, its modification at the non-asymptotic densities relevant to neutron stars (chemical potential μ < 500 MeV) is uncertain because the coupling is not small, and the phase structure depends on the poorly known ratio of the strange quark mass correction to the gap parameter, m_s²/(μΔ).

## Method

The author uses weak-coupling perturbative QCD to compute the gap parameter, screening masses, and effective field theory coefficients for the CFL phase, then applies chiral effective theory to determine the phase structure as a function of the strange quark mass and lepton chemical potentials. Transport coefficients (shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, thermal conductivity, neutrino emissivity) are calculated from the effective lagrangian for Goldstone bosons and baryon fields, using kinetic theory to evaluate scattering rates and particle number changing processes.

## Key numbers

- Gap parameter Δ₀ ≃ 2^{-1/3} 512π⁴ μ (2/(3g²))^{5/2} exp(-3π²/(√2 g) - (π²+4)/8) at asymptotically high density
- Gap estimates from extrapolations and model calculations give Δ₀ ≃ (50-100) MeV at density ρ ≃ 5ρ₀, where ρ₀ is nuclear matter saturation density
- Gluon screening mass m² = (21-8 log(2))/(54) × g²μ²/(2π²)
- Pion decay constant f_π² = (21-8 log(2))/18 × (p_F²/(2π²)), with pion velocity v_π² = 1/3
- Critical strange quark mass for kaon condensation: m_s(crit) = 3.03 × m_d^{1/3} Δ^{2/3}, giving m_s(crit) ≃ 70 MeV for Δ ≃ 50 MeV
- Shear viscosity η ≃ 1.3 × 10^{-4} μ⁸/T⁵
- Bulk viscosity ζ₂ ≃ 0.011 m_s⁴/T
- Thermal conductivity κ ≃ 4.01 × 10^{-2} μ⁸/Δ⁶ MeV²
- Neutrino mean free path l_ν > 10 km for T < 1 MeV, and l_ν ~ 1 m for T ~ 30 MeV

## Context

This work extends the perturbative QCD calculations of the CFL phase by Son (1999), Brown (1999), and Wang (2001) to non-asymptotic densities, and builds on the effective field theory framework for CFL matter developed by Casalbuoni (1999) and Son (2002). The phase structure analysis follows the work of Kaplan and Reddy (2001) on kaon condensation, and the transport calculations complete a program that includes shear viscosity from Manuel (2004), bulk viscosity from Manuel (2007) and Alford (2007), and thermal conductivity from Braby (2009). The results enable quantitative predictions for neutron star observables such as r-mode instabilities, cooling curves, and gravitational wave signatures, and provide a baseline for comparing with less dense phases like the 2SC phase and crystalline color superconductivity.
