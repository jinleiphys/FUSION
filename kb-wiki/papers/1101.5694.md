# Critical Zeeman Splitting of a Unitary Fermi Superfluid

> We determine the critical Zeeman energy splitting of a homogeneous Fermi superfluid at unitary in terms of the Fermi energy $ε_{\text F}$ according to recent experimental results in LKB-Lhomond. Based on the universal equations of state for the superfluid and normal phases, we show that there exist two critical fields $H_{c1}$ and $H_{c2}$, between which a superfluid-normal mixed phase is energetically favored. Universal formulae for the critical fields and the critical population imbalance $P_c$ are derived. We have found a universal relation between the critical fields and the critical imbalances: $H_{c1}=γξε_{\text F}$ and $H_{c2}=(1+γP_c)^{2/3}H_{c1}$ where $ξ$ is the universal constant and $γ$ is the critical value of the chemical potential imbalance in the grand canonical ensemble. Since $ξ$, $γ$ and $P_c$ have been measured in the experiments, we can determine the critical Zeeman fields without the detail information of the equation of state for the polarized normal phase. Using the experimental data from LKB-Lhomond, we have found $H_{c1}\simeq0.37ε_{\text F}$ and $H_{c2}\simeq0.44ε_{\text F}$. Our result of the polarization $P$ as a function of the Zeeman field $H/ε_{\text F}$ is in good agreement with the data extracted from the experiments. We also give an estimation of the critical magnetic field for dilute neutron matter at which the matter gets spin polarized, assuming the properties of the dilute neutron matter are close to those of the unitary Fermi gas.

## Key claim

The paper determines that for a homogeneous unitary Fermi superfluid, there exist two critical Zeeman fields, \(H_{c1}\) and \(H_{c2}\), rather than a single critical field, and that a superfluid-normal mixed phase is energetically favored between them. The authors derive universal formulae for these fields using experimentally measured parameters, without requiring detailed knowledge of the equation of state for the polarized normal phase.

## Method

The authors used universal equations of state for the superfluid and normal phases at unitarity, constructed from the universality hypothesis. They solved the phase equilibrium condition in the grand canonical ensemble to find the critical chemical potential imbalance \(\gamma\), then converted to the canonical ensemble with fixed total density to derive expressions for the lower and upper critical Zeeman fields \(H_{c1}\) and \(H_{c2}\) in terms of the universal constant \(\xi\), the critical imbalance \(\gamma\), and the critical population imbalance \(P_c\). The final results were obtained by inserting experimental data from the LKB-Lhomond, MIT, and QMC studies.

## Key numbers

*   Using LKB-Lhomond experimental data (exp2), the lower critical field is \(H_{c1} \simeq 0.369 \epsilon_{\text F}\) and the upper critical field is \(H_{c2} \simeq 0.436 \epsilon_{\text F}\).
*   Using LKB-Lhomond experimental data (exp3), the lower critical field is \(H_{c1} \simeq 0.368 \epsilon_{\text F}\) and the upper critical field is \(H_{c2} \simeq 0.443 \epsilon_{\text F}\).
*   Using QMC data, the lower critical field is \(H_{c1} \simeq 0.406 \epsilon_{\text F}\) and the upper critical field is \(H_{c2} \simeq 0.503 \epsilon_{\text F}\).
*   Using MIT experimental data, the lower critical field is \(H_{c1} \simeq 0.399 \epsilon_{\text F}\) and the upper critical field is \(H_{c2} \simeq 0.485 \epsilon_{\text F}\).
*   The universal relation between the critical fields and the critical population imbalance is \(H_{c2} = (1 + \gamma P_c)^{2/3} H_{c1}\).
*   For dilute neutron matter at density \(n = 10^{-3} n_0\), the estimated lower critical magnetic field is \(B_{c1} \simeq 3.6 \times 10^{16}\) Gauss and the upper critical field is \(B_{c2} \simeq 4.3 \times 10^{16}\) Gauss.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of the Chandrasekhar-Clogston limit in unitary Fermi superfluids, which had predicted a single critical field, by demonstrating the existence of two critical fields and a mixed phase, similar to type-II superconductors but with a different mechanism. It builds upon QMC calculations (Carlson, 2003) and cold atom experiments (MIT, LKB-Lhomond) that measured the critical chemical potential imbalance \(\gamma\) and critical population imbalance \(P_c\), but had not determined the critical fields in terms of the Fermi energy. The results enable a direct connection between cold atom experiments and the estimation of critical magnetic fields for spin polarization in dilute neutron matter, relevant to neutron star crusts and magnetars.
