# Nucleon-Nucleon Interactions and Observables

> A class of nucleon-nucleon interactions which are exactly phase equivalent to a given realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction are exhibited. These interaction have the property that the RMS radius of the deuteron can be made arbitrarily large without changing any of the scattering or bound-state observables. With this construction it is possible to find realistic interactions that do not obey the linear relation between the RMS radius and the triplet scattering length observed by Klarsfeld et. al. The interpretation of these examples is discussed.

# Nucleon-Nucleon Interactions and Observables

## Key claim
The paper constructs a sequence of realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions that are exactly phase-equivalent to a given realistic interaction, yet have arbitrarily large deuteron RMS radius, demonstrating that the empirical linear relation between triplet scattering length and deuteron RMS radius observed by Klarsfeld et al. (1986) is not a necessary consequence of fitting scattering and bound-state data.

## Method
The author uses unitary transformations that are compact perturbations of the identity, specifically of the form \(W = I + (2i/(1-i))|\chi\rangle\langle\chi|\), which preserve all S-matrix elements and bound-state observables when the Riemann-Lebesgue lemma ensures the required asymptotic condition. By choosing vectors \(|\chi_m\rangle\) constructed from an orthonormal basis in the domain of \(r^2\), the transformed Hamiltonian \(H_m = W_m^\dagger H_0 W_m\) differs from the original only on a two-dimensional subspace, and the deuteron RMS radius \(\langle r^2\rangle_m\) can be made to satisfy \(\langle r^2\rangle_m \geq m r_0^2\) for any integer \(m\).

## Key numbers
- The deuteron RMS radius for the transformed Hamiltonian satisfies \(\langle r^2\rangle_m \geq m r_0^2\), where \(r_0\) is the original deuteron RMS radius, for any integer \(m\).
- The construction yields \(\langle r^2\rangle' = \langle \psi | r^2 | \psi \rangle + 2 |\langle \psi | \chi \rangle|^2 \langle \chi | r^2 | \chi \rangle\) for the transformed deuteron RMS radius.
- The triplet scattering length is identical for all interactions in the sequence, as they share the same scattering operator.

## Context
This work extends the observation by Klarsfeld et al. (1986) of an empirical linear relation between triplet scattering length and deuteron RMS radius in realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions, showing by explicit counterexample that this relation is not theoretically required. The construction enables the study of representation dependence in nuclear interactions and shows that the RMS radius is not a true quantum mechanical observable, as it can be changed arbitrarily without affecting any scattering or bound-state observables. The paper connects to earlier work by Coester and Polyzou (1982) on unitary transformations and to Friar, Martorell, and Sprung (1997) on practical considerations for interpreting radii as observables.
