# Effect of deformation on two-neutrino double beta decay matrix elements

> We study the effect of deformation on the two-neutrino double beta decay for ground state to ground state transitions in all the nuclei whose half-lives have been measured. Our theoretical framework is a deformed QRPA based in Woods-Saxon or Hartree-Fock mean fields. We are able to reproduce at the same time the main characteristics of the two single beta branches, as well as the double beta matrix elements. We find a suppression of the double beta matrix element with respect to the spherical case when the parent and daughter nuclei have different deformations.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the two-neutrino double beta decay matrix element is suppressed relative to the spherical case when the parent and daughter nuclei have different deformations, but this suppression is conditional on the specific deformation difference and is studied within a deformed QRPA framework using either Woods-Saxon or Hartree-Fock mean fields.

## Method

The authors used a deformed quasiparticle random phase approximation (pnQRPA) with a deformed quasiparticle basis and residual spin-isospin separable interactions in both particle-hole and particle-particle channels. They applied this framework to all ten nuclei whose two-neutrino double beta decay half-lives have been measured (48Ca, 76Ge, 82Se, 96Zr, 100Mo, 116Cd, 128Te, 130Te, 136Xe, and 150Nd), using either a phenomenological Woods-Saxon potential with deformation as an input parameter or a self-consistent Hartree-Fock calculation with Skyrme interactions. The double beta decay process was described as two successive Gamow-Teller transitions via intermediate 1+ states, including an overlap term to account for non-orthogonality of the intermediate states reached from parent and daughter nuclei.

## Key numbers

- For the Hartree-Fock calculation, the coupling strengths used were chi_GT^ph = 0.1 MeV and kappa_GT^pp = 6/A MeV.
- For the Woods-Saxon calculation, the parametrization of Homma was used: chi_GT^ph = 5.2/A^0.7 MeV and kappa_GT^pp = 0.58/A^0.7 MeV.
- The study covered all ten measured double beta emitters: 48Ca, 76Ge, 82Se, 96Zr, 100Mo, 116Cd, 128Te, 130Te, 136Xe, and 150Nd.
- The matrix element M_2nu^GT was computed as a function of the particle-particle interaction strength, with experimental values extracted using g_A = 1.0 and g_A = 1.25.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the suppression of two-neutrino double beta decay matrix elements relative to pure quasiparticle transitions, which had been observed experimentally but not fully explained. The authors build on the deformed QRPA approach previously developed by Simkovic and collaborators (Simkovic, year not specified) and use the parametrization of Homma (Homma, year not specified) for the Woods-Saxon mean field. The results provide a starting point for testing nuclear structure calculations and for tackling the neutrinoless double beta decay problem, which is the next step in understanding the role of deformation in double beta decay processes.
