# Neutrino Energy Reconstruction and the Shape of the CCQE-like Total Cross Section

> We show that because of the multinucleon mechanism effects, the algorithm used to reconstruct the neutrino energy is not adequate when dealing with quasielastic-like events, and a distortion of the total flux unfolded cross section shape is produced. This amounts to a redistribution of strength from high to low energies, which gives rise to a sizable excess (deficit) of low (high) energy neutrinos. This distortion of the shape leads to a good description of the MiniBooNE unfolded CCQE-like cross sections published in Phys.Rev. D81 (2010) 092005. However, these changes in the shape are artifacts of the unfolding process that ignores multinucleon mechanisms.

## Key claim

The algorithm used to reconstruct neutrino energy from muon kinematics (assuming a stationary target nucleon) is inadequate for quasielastic-like events because multinucleon (2p2h) mechanisms produce broad, flat distributions of true neutrino energies that do not peak around the reconstructed energy, leading to a distortion of the unfolded cross section shape that is an artifact of the unfolding process, not a genuine physical effect.

## Method

The authors compute the conditional probability density P(E_reco|E) for both genuine quasielastic (QE) and 2p2h contributions using a fully microscopic model of neutrino-nucleus interactions that includes relativistic corrections, RPA correlations, and a value of the nucleon axial mass M_A = 1.049 GeV consistent with the world average. They then simulate the experimental unfolding procedure by using a simple Fermi gas model with M_A = 1.32 GeV (which ignores 2p2h contributions and RPA corrections) as the assumed model in the Bayesian unfolding, while using their full QE+2p2h model to generate the "measured" reconstructed-energy distribution.

## Key numbers

- The full model (QE+2p2h with M_A = 1.049 GeV) describes the MiniBooNE flux-folded double differential cross section dσ/dT_μ dcosθ_μ with a global scale λ = 0.89 ± 0.01 (χ²/dof = 53/137)
- The simple QE-only Fermi gas model with M_A = 1.32 GeV (no RPA, no 2p2h) also describes the same flux-folded data with χ²/dof = 35/137
- The MiniBooNE total normalization uncertainty is 10.7%
- The world average nucleon axial mass is 1.03 ± 0.02 GeV
- The MiniBooNE neutrino flux mean energy ⟨E⟩ is approximately 800 MeV

## Context

This work extends the findings of Martini et al. (2009, 2010, 2011) and the authors' own previous work (Nieves et al. 2011) showing that 2p2h mechanisms are essential to describe MiniBooNE CCQE-like data with standard values of M_A, rather than the anomalously large M_A ≈ 1.3–1.4 GeV required by QE-only analyses. The paper demonstrates that the unfolded cross section published by MiniBooNE (Aguilar-Arevalo et al. 2010) is biased because the unfolding procedure assumes pure QE interactions, and that the apparent excess of low-energy neutrinos in those data is an artifact. This has implications for neutrino oscillation experiments that use CCQE samples to reconstruct neutrino energy for determining Δm² and mixing angles.
