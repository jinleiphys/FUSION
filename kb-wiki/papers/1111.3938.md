# Global investigation of odd-even mass differences and radii with isospin dependent pairing interactions

> The neutron and proton odd-even mass differences are systematically studied with Hartree-Fock+BCS (HFBCS) calculations with Skyrme interactions and an isospin dependent contact pairing interaction. The strength of pairing interactions is determined to reproduce empirical odd-even mass differences in a wide region of mass table. By using the optimal parameter, we perform global HF+BCS calculations of nuclei and compare with experimental data. The importance of isospin dependence of the pairing interaction is singled out for odd-even mass differences in medium and heavy isotopes. The proton and neutron radii are studied systematically by using the same model.

## Key claim

The isospin-dependent (IS+IV) pairing interaction systematically improves the agreement with experimental odd-even staggering (OES) data compared to the isoscalar (IS) pairing interaction, but the improvement is clearest for specific isotopic and isotonic chains (e.g., neutron OES for Z=52, 78, 92 isotopes and proton OES for N=102 isotones) rather than globally across the entire mass table.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock+BCS (HFBCS) calculations using the SLy4 Skyrme interaction with two types of contact pairing interactions: an isoscalar (IS) surface-peaked interaction and an isospin-dependent (IS+IV) interaction whose density dependence was fitted to pairing gaps in symmetric and neutron matter from realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions. Odd-A nuclei were treated using the filling approximation with blocking of a single orbital near the Fermi energy, and the pairing strength V0 was adjusted to reproduce empirical odd-even mass differences extracted via the three-point formula Δ(3) over a wide region of the mass table.

## Key numbers

- Optimal pairing strength V0 for IS+IV interaction: 1040 MeV·fm³ for isotopes and 1120 MeV·fm³ for isotones
- Optimal pairing strength V0 for IS interaction: 1300 MeV·fm³ for isotopes and 1500 MeV·fm³ for isotones
- For Z=52 isotopes, average neutron Δ(3) values: experimental low-isospin 1.36 MeV, high-isospin 1.08 MeV; IS+IV gives 1.40 MeV and 1.19 MeV; IS gives 1.52 MeV and 1.41 MeV
- For N=102 isotones, average proton Δ(3) values: experimental low-isospin 0.96 MeV, high-isospin 0.63 MeV; IS+IV gives 0.92 MeV and 0.59 MeV; IS gives 0.79 MeV and 0.39 MeV
- Calculated neutron skin of 208Pb: rn−rp = 0.157 fm, compared to empirical values of 0.17±0.02 fm and 0.15±0.02 fm
- Neutron skin reaches up to 0.4 fm near the neutron drip line for Z<28, and at most 0.25 fm for neutron-rich nuclei with Z>50

## Context

This work extends the study of isospin-dependent pairing by Bertulani, Liu, and Sagawa (2009) from a few selected isotopic and isotonic chains to the entire nuclear chart, using the EV8odd code modified to include isospin-dependent pairing. The results confirm the importance of isospin dependence in pairing interactions for reproducing odd-even mass differences, particularly for neutron-rich nuclei, and provide systematic calculations of neutron and proton radii that agree with empirical data from antiprotonic atom experiments and spin-dipole resonance studies. The findings enable more reliable predictions of pairing gaps and neutron skins for exotic nuclei far from stability, which are relevant for understanding neutron matter properties and for future parity-violation experiments at Jefferson Laboratory.
