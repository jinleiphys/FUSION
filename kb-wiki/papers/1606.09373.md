# The effect of minijet on hadron spectra and azimuthal anisotropy in heavy-ion collisions

> Here I review the transverse momentum distributions of identified hadrons produced in Au-Au collisions at RHIC and Pb-Pb collisions at LHC in the framework of recombination model. Minijets play an important role in generating shower partons in the intermediate $p_T$ region. At LHC, the resultant soft shower partons are even found to dominate over the thermal partons in the non-strange sector. The azimuthal anisotropy of the produced hadrons could also be explained as the consequence of the effects of minijets. Harmonic analysis of the $ϕ$ dependence leads to $v_n(p_T, b)$ that can be well produced without reference to flow.

## Key claim

Minijets, through their shower partons, dominate hadron production even at low transverse momentum in Pb-Pb collisions at LHC, and the azimuthal anisotropy coefficients v_n can be reproduced without invoking hydrodynamic flow, using only the azimuthal dependence of minijet production and recombination.

## Method

The author applies the Hwa-Yang recombination model to compute hadron spectra from thermal and shower parton recombination in Au-Au collisions at RHIC (sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV) and Pb-Pb collisions at LHC (sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV). The momentum degradation of hard and semihard partons traversing the medium is parametrized by a dynamical path length formalism, with a momentum-dependent degradation factor gamma_g(q)=gamma_0/(1+q/q_0) for gluons and gamma_q=gamma_g/2 for quarks. The azimuthal anisotropy is computed by decomposing the single-particle distribution into a base (isotropic thermal), ridge (thermal with spatial-to-momentum anisotropy from elliptical geometry), and minijet component, with harmonic coefficients v_n obtained from the phi-dependent parton distributions.

## Key numbers

- At LHC, the shower parton distribution S(p_1) dominates over the thermal parton distribution T(p_1) for all p_1 > 0.5 GeV/c; at RHIC the crossover occurs at p_1 > 2 GeV/c.
- For Au-Au at 200 GeV, gamma_g=0.14 and gamma_q=0.07 yield fits for pion and proton spectra up to p_T < 10 GeV/c across six centralities.
- For Pb-Pb at 2.76 TeV, gamma_0=0.8 and q_0=10 GeV/c in gamma_g(q) fit pion and proton spectra up to p_T=20 GeV/c for 0-5% centrality.
- For the recalculated RHIC pion distribution at 0-10% centrality, gamma_0=0.6 and q_0=10 GeV/c are used.
- The thermal parton distribution uses T=0.245 GeV for the base component in v_2 calculations.
- The normalization factor for thermal partons is C(N_part)=3.43 N_part^0.32.
- For v_n calculations, the parameters a_2=0.6, a_3=1.6, and a_4=1.2 are used.

## Context

This review extends the recombination model of Hwa and Yang (2004) from RHIC to LHC energies, building on prior work by Zhu and Hwa (2013, 2014) on momentum degradation and by Chiu, Hwa, and Yang (2008) on ridge effects. The approach contrasts with hydrodynamic descriptions of azimuthal anisotropy (e.g., by Snellings, 2005; Hirano and Kolb, 2002) by attributing v_n to minijet production rather than pressure gradients. The results challenge the meaningfulness of hydrodynamical studies that ignore minijet contributions at LHC, and enable a unified description of hadron spectra and anisotropy across the full p_T range without invoking flow.
