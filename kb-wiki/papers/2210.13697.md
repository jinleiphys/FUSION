# Exotic Compact Objects with Two Dark Matter Fluids

> The generic properties of compact objects made of two different fluids of dark matter are studied in a scale invariant approach. We investigate compact objects with a core-shell structure, where the two fluids are separated, and with mixed dark matter components, where both dark matter fluids are immersed within each other. The constellations considered are combinations of incompressible fluids, free and interacting Fermi gases, and equations of state with a vacuum term, i.e. self-bound dark matter. We find novel features in the mass-radius relations for combined dark matter compact objects which distinguishes them from compact objects with a single dark matter fluid and compact stars made of ordinary baryonic matter, as white dwarfs, neutron stars and quark stars. The maximum compactness of certain combined dark matter stars can reach values up to the causality limit for compact stars but not beyond that limit if causality of the dark matter fluids is ensured.

## Key claim

The paper claims that compact objects composed of two different dark matter fluids can exhibit mass-radius relations and compactness values that distinguish them from single-fluid dark matter stars and ordinary baryonic compact stars, with maximum compactness reaching the causality limit (C = 3/8) but not exceeding it, provided causality of the dark matter fluids is ensured.

## Method

The authors solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for spherically symmetric, static gravitating spheres using a scale-invariant approach with dimensionless variables. They construct five model configurations: (1) two incompressible fluids in a core-shell structure, (2) an incompressible fluid core with an interacting Fermi gas shell, (3) an interacting Fermi gas core with a free Fermi gas shell, (4) a self-bound matter core (conformal equation of state with vacuum term) with an interacting Fermi gas shell, and (5) two homogeneously mixed interacting Fermi fluids. The equations of state include constant density (incompressible), free and interacting Fermi gases at zero temperature, and the conformal equation of state P = (1/3)(rho - rho_vac) for self-bound matter.

## Key numbers

- The Buchdahl limit for compactness is C_max = 4/9, and the causality limit is C_s approximately 3/8.
- For the incompressible fluid core with an interacting Fermi gas shell (model 2), the maximum allowed core radius from the causality limit is r'_c less than or equal to sqrt(9/(32*pi)) approximately 0.3 in dimensionless units.
- For the incompressible fluid core with an interacting Fermi gas shell (model 2), the maximum allowed core radius from the Buchdahl limit is r'_c less than or equal to sqrt(1/(3*pi)) approximately 0.33 in dimensionless units.
- The MIT bag model vacuum energy density is rho_vac = 4B with B^(1/4) approximately 145 MeV.
- The particle mass ratio used in models 3, 4, and 5 is m_2/m_1 = 10^(-6), where m_1 is the scaling unit (nucleon mass).
- The interaction strength used for the interacting Fermi gas in models 2, 3, 4, and 5 is y = 10, with additional values y = 0, 100, 1000 explored for the mixed star (model 5).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dark matter compact stars, including investigations of fermionic dark matter stars by Narain (2006) and studies of dark white dwarfs by Ryan (2022) and exotic cores by Zollner (2022). The paper also builds on the concept of self-bound stars originally developed for absolutely stable strange quark matter by Witten (1984), Alcock (1986), and Haensel (1986). The results enable future gravitational wave observations to potentially distinguish exotic compact objects made of two dark matter fluids from neutron stars and black holes, particularly for objects in the mass gap between approximately 2 solar masses and 5 solar masses, such as the GW190814 event with a 2.6 solar mass compact object.
