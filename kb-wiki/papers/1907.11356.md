# Lepton angular distributions of Drell-Yan process in pQCD and a geometric approach

> The lepton angular distributions of the Drell-Yan process in the fixed-target experiments are investigated by NLO and NNLO perturbative QCD. We present the calculated angular parameters $λ$, $μ$, $ν$ and the degree of violation of the Lam-Tung relation, $1-λ-2ν$, for the E615 experiment as well as predictions for the COMPASS experiment. Many salient features of transverse momentum and rapidity dependence could be qualitatively understood by a geometric approach.

## Key claim

Fixed-order NLO and NNLO perturbative QCD calculations can describe the transverse momentum dependence of the lepton angular parameters in fixed-target Drell-Yan data, but the Lam-Tung relation is violated only at NNLO (not at NLO), and many features of the rapidity and transverse momentum dependence can be qualitatively understood via a geometric model of the collision axes.

## Method

The authors used the DYNNLO (version 1.5) package with CT14nlo and CT14nnlo parton distribution functions for protons and neutrons, and GRVPI1 for pion PDFs, to compute the angular coefficients A0, A1, A2 from moments of spherical harmonic polynomials of the lepton angular distributions. These coefficients were then converted to the angular parameters λ, μ, ν and the Lam-Tung violation quantity 1−λ−2ν for the E615 experiment (252 GeV π− + W) and as predictions for the COMPASS experiment (190 GeV π− beam on tungsten targets).

## Key numbers

* For E615 at 252 GeV π− + W: at qT → 0, λ approaches 1 and μ, ν approach 0 (collinear parton model limit); at large qT, λ approaches −1/3 and ν approaches 2/3 for both qq̅ and qG processes.
* The Lam-Tung violation quantity 1−λ−2ν is zero in NLO pQCD and becomes nonzero and positive in NNLO pQCD calculations.
* For COMPASS predictions: three bins for dimuon mass Q in the range 4.0–7.0 GeV and three bins for Feynman-x (xF) in the range 0–0.6, with 190 GeV π− beam.
* The μ parameter shows strong xF dependence: magnitude is small when xF is close to 0 and can become negative at some qT region; as xF increases, the magnitude of μ increases pronouncedly.

## Context

This work extends the comparison of pQCD calculations to the E615 data (Conway et al.) and provides NLO predictions for the ongoing COMPASS experiment (COMPASS collaboration). The geometric approach developed by Peng (2016), Chang (2017), and Peng (2018) is used to interpret the pQCD results, particularly explaining the acoplanarity effect (φ1 ≠ 0) that leads to violation of the Lam-Tung relation (Lam and Tung, 1980) at NNLO. The results show that pQCD effects must be included when extracting nonperturbative Boer-Mulders functions (Boer, 1999) from ν data.
