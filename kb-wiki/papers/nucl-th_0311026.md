# Correlation energies by the generator coordinate method: computational aspects for quadrupolar deformations

> We investigate truncation schemes to reduce the computational cost of calculating correlations by the generator coordinate method based on mean-field wave functions. As our test nuclei, we take examples for which accurate calculations are available. These include a strongly deformed nucleus, 156Sm, a nucleus with strong pairing, 120Sn, the krypton isotope chain which contains examples of soft deformations, and the lead isotope chain which includes the doubly magic 208Pb. We find that the Gaussian overlap approximation for angular momentum projection is effective and reduces the computational cost by an order of magnitude. Cost savings in the deformation degrees of freedom are harder to realize. A straightforward Gaussian overlap approximation can be applied rather reliably to angular-momentum projected states based on configuration sets having the same sign deformation (prolate or oblate), but matrix elements between prolate and oblate deformations must be treated with more care. We propose a two-dimensional GOA using a triangulation procedure to treat the general case with both kinds of deformation. With the computational gains from these approximations, it should be feasible to carry out a systematic calculation of correlation energies for the nuclear mass table.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the Gaussian overlap approximation (GOA) for angular momentum projection reduces computational cost by an order of magnitude, but the authors hedge that cost savings in the deformation degrees of freedom are harder to realize and that matrix elements between prolate and oblate deformations require special treatment via a proposed two-dimensional GOA using a triangulation procedure.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock+BCS calculations using the SLy6 Skyrme interaction for the particle-hole channel and a density-dependent delta-function pairing interaction, with Lipkin-Nogami prescription to avoid BCS phase transitions. They then applied angular momentum projection onto J=0 states using the generator coordinate method (GCM) with quadrupolar deformation as the generator coordinate, testing various GOA formulations (improved GOA, topological GOA, and a triangular GOA) against full 12-point Gauss-Legendre integration. The triangular GOA was used to estimate off-diagonal matrix elements between prolate and oblate configurations by defining a two-dimensional metric with a fixed angle of 45 degrees.

## Key numbers

- The topological GOA for angular momentum projection in 208Pb at deformation -5 b (beta2=-0.063) gave an error of 1.3% in the projected integral, compared to 18% for the improved GOA
- For 208Pb at q=500 fm^2 and q'=-500 fm^2, the topGOA estimated the off-diagonal correlation energy as 2.49 MeV versus 2.50 MeV from the fine angular mesh
- The total correlation energies (projection plus configuration mixing) ranged from 1.7 MeV (208Pb) to 3.9 MeV (194Pb) across the nuclei studied
- The r.m.s. error of the triangular GOA approximation compared to full treatment was 0.15 MeV
- For 120Sn, the configuration set could be thinned from 13 states to 4 states (overlap threshold of 55%) while keeping the configurational correlation energy within the desired error bound of 0.2 MeV

## Context

This work extends the generator coordinate method studies by the Paris-Brussels collaboration (Berger et al. 1991, Heenen et al. 1993, Meyer et al. 1995, Valor et al. 2000, Bender et al. 2003, Duguet et al. 2003) to develop computationally tractable approximations for systematic nuclear mass calculations. The topological GOA approach was previously advocated by Hagino et al. (2002) and tested on the Interacting Boson Model (Hagino et al. 2003). The study enables systematic calculation of correlation energies for the nuclear mass table, which previously relied on phenomenological methods whose range of validity was not apparent. The optimal spacing of configurations was previously studied by Bonche et al. (1990) for unprojected matrix elements in 194Hg.
