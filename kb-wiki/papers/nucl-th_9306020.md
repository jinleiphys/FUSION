# Antiproton Production in p-Nucleus and Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions within a Relativistic Transport Approach

> The production of antiprotons in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus reactions is calculated within the relativistic BUU approach employing proper selfenergies for the baryons and antiprotons and treating the p-bar annihilation nonperturbatively. The differential cross section for the antiprotons is found to be very sensitive to the p-bar selfenergy adopted. A detailed comparison with the available experimental data for p-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus reactions shows that the antiproton feels a moderately attractive mean-field at normal nuclear matter density which is in line with a dispersive potential extracted from the free annihilation cross section.

## Key claim

The antiproton feels a moderately attractive mean-field at normal nuclear matter density, which is consistent with a dispersive potential extracted from the free annihilation cross section, but this attraction is far weaker than the strong attractive vector selfenergy predicted by simple charge-conjugation of the nucleon selfenergy in relativistic mean-field models.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (RBUU) transport calculations for antiproton production in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions, treating antiproton annihilation nonperturbatively via individual rate equations. Antiproton production was included perturbatively from baryon-baryon channels (NN, ΔN, ΔΔ) using a phase-space parametrization of the elementary cross section, with nucleon selfenergies taken from Dirac-Brueckner calculations and antiproton selfenergies treated as free parameters in a σ-ω model. The antiproton selfenergies were then constrained by comparing calculated invariant differential cross sections to experimental data from KEK (p + C, p + Cu at 3.5-5 GeV) and GSI/BEVALAC (Si+Si at 2.1 GeV/u, Ni+Ni at 1.85 GeV/u).

## Key numbers

- For p + A reactions, an attractive scalar antiproton selfenergy of -50 to -100 MeV at normal nuclear matter density ρ₀ (≈ 0.17 fm⁻³) significantly improves reproduction of the data at all bombarding energies (3.5, 4.0, and 5.0 GeV).
- For A + A reactions (Si+Si at 2.1 GeV/u, Ni+Ni at 1.85 GeV/u), attractive scalar (or vector) selfenergies at ρ₀ of about -100 to -150 MeV are needed to reproduce the data.
- The contribution from collisions involving Δ resonances is a factor of 10 larger than those involving only nucleons.
- Using antiproton selfenergies in line with charge conjugation of the nucleon vector potential overestimates the antiproton yield by more than an order of magnitude at all energies for both p + A systems.
- The elementary production cross section parametrization used is σ_pp→p̅+X(√s) = 0.01 (√s - √s₀)^1.846 [mb], with √s₀ = 3.7532 GeV for free particles.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic transport calculations of Cassing (1992) by including explicit momentum-dependent nucleon selfenergies, which had been omitted previously, and provides a systematic comparison with data from KEK (p + A) and GSI (A + A). It contradicts the predictions of Schaffner et al. (thermal relativistic model) and simple relativistic mean-field models (Serot) that assume antiproton selfenergies given by charge conjugation of the nucleon selfenergy, which would produce far stronger attraction. The extracted antiproton potential is instead consistent with a dispersive potential derived from the annihilation cross section and with the potential analysis for antiproton-nucleus reactions by Dover. The results enable future studies at AGS energies (around 15 GeV/u) to probe antiproton selfenergies at even higher densities.
