# On the relation between E(5)-models and the interacting boson model

> The connections between the $E(5)-$models (the original E(5) using an infinite square well, $E(5)-β^4$, $E(5)-β^6$ and $E(5)-β^8$), based on particular solutions of the geometrical Bohr Hamiltonian with $γ$-unstable potentials, and the interacting boson model (IBM) are explored. For that purpose, the general IBM Hamiltonian for the $U(5)-O(6)$ transition line is used and a numerical fit to the different $E(5)-$models energies is performed. It is shown that within the IBM one can reproduce very well all these $E(5)-$models. The agreement is the best for $E(5)-β^4$ and reduces when passing through $E(5)-β^6$, $E(5)-β^8$ and E(5), where the worst agreement is obtained (although still very good for a restricted set of lowest lying states). The fitted IBM Hamiltonians correspond to energy surfaces close to those expected for the critical point.

## Key claim

The interacting boson model (IBM) can reproduce the energies of all E(5)-type models (E(5), E(5)-β⁴, E(5)-β⁶, E(5)-β⁸) with remarkable accuracy, but the agreement is best for E(5)-β⁴ and worst for the original E(5) model, where discrepancies appear in higher-lying bands while the lowest band remains perfectly matched.

## Method

The authors performed numerical fits of a general IBM Hamiltonian along the U(5)-O(6) transition line (with κ₂=0 to enforce γ-instability) to the energy levels of four E(5)-type models derived from the Bohr Hamiltonian with different β-potentials. They minimized a standard χ² function using five free parameters (ε_d, κ₀, κ₁, κ₃, κ₄) as a function of boson number N, then checked the resulting wave functions by calculating B(E2) values. The fitted Hamiltonians were analyzed using the IBM intrinsic state formalism to determine their proximity to the critical point via the essential parameter r₁.

## Key numbers

- χ²(E(5)-β⁴) ≈ χ²(E(5))/50 for any boson number N, indicating the best fit is roughly 50 times better for the β⁴ model than for the original E(5) model.
- For N=60, the fitted IBM parameters for E(5)-β⁴ are ε_d=2543.00, κ₀=39.92, κ₁=0.7143, κ₃=6.2221, κ₄=1.29 (all in arbitrary energy units).
- For N=60, the E(5)-β⁴ model gives the 2₁⁺ state at 1.000 (normalized), the 4₁⁺ at 2.093, and the 0₂⁺ at 2.390; the IBM fit reproduces these as 1.000, 2.092, and 2.389 respectively.
- For N=60, the original E(5) model gives the 0₂⁺ state at 3.031 and the 2₃⁺ at 4.800; the IBM fit gives 3.035 and 4.761 respectively.
- All fitted IBM Hamiltonians satisfy |r₁|<0.05, placing them very close to the critical point of the U(5)-O(6) transition.

## Context

This work extends the numerical mapping between the Bohr-Mottelson collective model and the interacting boson model for transitional nuclei, building on the E(5) critical point symmetry introduced by Iachello (2000) and the β⁴, β⁶, β⁸ variants by Bonatsos et al. (2004). The results demonstrate that all E(5)-type models correspond to IBM Hamiltonians near the critical point of the U(5)-O(6) transition, and the excellent agreement suggests the existence of a quasi-critical point symmetry as proposed by Garcia-Ramos and Arias (2008). This enables future experimental studies to use either the geometrical or algebraic framework interchangeably for describing γ-unstable transitional nuclei near the critical point.
