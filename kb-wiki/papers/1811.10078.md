# Spin-isospin correlated configurations in complex nuclei and neutron skin effect in W$^\pm$ production in high-energy proton-lead collisions

> We extend our Monte Carlo generator of global configurations in nuclei to include different spatial distributions of protons and neutrons in heavy nuclei taking into account the difference of spatial correlations between two protons, two neutrons and proton-neutron pairs. These configurations are used for building an event generator for proton-heavy nucleus collisions at the LHC for final states with a hard interaction in the channels where cross section for p-p and p-n scattering differ. Soft interactions are taken into account in the color fluctuation extension of the Glauber algorithm taking into account the inherently different transverse geometry of soft and hard p-N collisions. We use the new event generator to test an interesting observation of Physics Letters B745 (2015) 73-78, that the ratio of W$^+$/W$^-$ production rates in p-Pb collisions should significantly deviate from the inclusive value for peripheral collisions due to the presence of a neutron skin. We qualitatively confirm expectation of Physics Letters B745 (2015) 73-78, though for a realistic centrality trigger, we find the effect to be a factor of two smaller than the original estimate.

## Key claim

The authors qualitatively confirm the expectation of Paukkunen (2015) that the ratio of W$^+$ to W$^-$ production rates in p-Pb collisions should deviate from the inclusive value for peripheral collisions due to the neutron skin, but they find that for a realistic centrality trigger the effect is a factor of two smaller than the original estimate.

## Method

The authors extended their Monte Carlo generator of global nuclear configurations to include different spatial distributions of protons and neutrons in heavy nuclei, accounting for the difference in spatial correlations between proton-proton, neutron-neutron, and proton-neutron pairs. They generated configurations for $^{48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb and built an event generator for proton-lead collisions at the LHC that includes a hard interaction trigger, soft interactions treated with the color fluctuation extension of the Glauber algorithm, and a realistic centrality classification based on the ATLAS experimental method using total transverse energy ($\Sigma E_T$) rather than simple impact parameter cuts.

## Key numbers

* The neutron radius Woods-Saxon parameter for $^{208}$Pb is $R^n_0 = 6.7$ fm with skin depth $a_n = 0.55$ fm, obtained from coherent pion photoproduction data (Tarbert 2013).
* The proton Woods-Saxon parameters for $^{208}$Pb are $R^p_0 = 6.68$ fm and $a_p = 0.447$ fm, taken from high-energy elastic electron scattering measurements.
* For $^{48}$Ca, the three-parameter Fermi model uses $c_p = 3.81$ fm, $c_n = 4.12$ fm, $z_p = 0.53$ fm, $z_n = 0.51$ fm, and $w = -0.08$.
* The deviation of the W$^+$/W$^-$ asymmetry from the inclusive value is reduced by approximately 50% (a factor of two) when using the most advanced approximation (Glauber plus color fluctuations with $\Sigma E_T$ centrality classification) compared to the simplest approximation (Glauber with $T(b)$ centrality cuts).
* Color fluctuation effects produce about 10% difference in the proton-to-neutron ratio in the most peripheral events.

## Context

This work extends the Monte Carlo Glauber approach of Alvioli (2009) by including fully spin-isospin dependent nucleon-nucleon correlations and neutron skin effects in nuclear configurations for $^{48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb. It directly tests the prediction of Paukkunen (2015) that W$^\pm$ production asymmetry in p-Pb collisions is sensitive to the neutron skin, but finds a reduced effect when accounting for color fluctuations (Baym 1994, 1995) and realistic centrality determination following the ATLAS experimental method (Aad 2015). The generated configurations are made available for use in other event generators such as HIJING, SMASH, Glissando, and the Angantyr model, enabling improved modeling of peripheral heavy-ion collisions at collider energies.
