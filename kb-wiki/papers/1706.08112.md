# Deformed shell model study of event rates for WIMP- $^{73}$Ge scattering

> The event detection rates for the WIMP (a dark matter candidate) are calculated with $^{73}$Ge as the detector. The calculations are performed within the deformed shell model (DSM) based on Hartree-Fock states. First the energy levels and magnetic moment for the ground state and two low lying positive parity states for this nucleus are calculated and compared with experiment. The agreement is quite satisfactory. Then the nuclear wave functions are used to investigate the elastic and inelastic scattering of WIMP from $^{73}$Ge. The nuclear structure factors which are independent of supersymmetric model are also calculated as a function of WIMP mass. The event rates are calculated for a given set of SUSY parameters. The calculation shows that $^{73}$Ge is a good detector for detecting dark matter.

## Key claim

The deformed shell model (DSM) calculation shows that $^{73}$Ge is a good detector for dark matter, with the inelastic scattering channel to the $7/2^+$ state at 68.7 keV being a potential candidate for detection, contingent on the specific set of SUSY parameters chosen for the event rate calculation.

## Method

The authors performed deformed shell model (DSM) calculations based on Hartree-Fock (HF) intrinsic states with angular momentum projection and band mixing, using $^{56}$Ni as an inert core with the $1p_{3/2}$, $0f_{5/2}$, $1p_{1/2}$, and $0g_{9/2}$ orbitals as the basis space and a modified Kuo interaction. They first computed the energy levels and magnetic moments for the ground state and low-lying positive parity states of $^{73}$Ge to validate the nuclear wave functions, then used those wave functions to calculate nuclear structure factors and event rates for both elastic and inelastic WIMP-nucleus scattering, separating the nuclear part from the supersymmetric part in the formulation.

## Key numbers

- The ground state $9/2^+$ magnetic moment calculated with DSM is $-0.811$ $\mu_N$, compared to the experimental value of $-0.879$ $\mu_N$.
- The first excited positive parity state $7/2^+$ is at an excitation energy of 68.7 keV; its calculated magnetic moment is $-0.232$ $\mu_N$.
- The static spin matrix elements for elastic scattering are $\Omega_0 = 0.798$ and $\Omega_1 = -0.803$.
- The static spin matrix elements for inelastic scattering to the $7/2^+$ state are $\Omega_0 = -0.167$ and $\Omega_1 = 0.142$.
- The annual modulation magnitude at WIMP mass $\sim$35 GeV is about 3.3% for spin-dependent channels and about 4% for the spin-independent case.
- The nuclear structure coefficients $D_1$, $D_2$, $D_3$, and $D_4$ for elastic scattering peak at WIMP mass $\sim$35 GeV for a detector threshold $Q_{thr} = 0$ keV.
- The inelastic nuclear structure coefficients $E_1$, $E_2$, and $E_3$ peak at around $m_\chi = 45$ GeV, with peak values about 13 times larger than those reported for $^{83}$Kr.

## Context

This work extends previous DSM applications to dark matter detection, following similar truncated shell model studies for detectors such as $^{83}$Kr (Vergados, 2015), $^{125}$Te (Suhonen, 2016), and earlier elastic-only calculations for $^{73}$Ge (Suhonen, 2006). The inelastic scattering channel for $^{73}$Ge, which had not been studied previously, is considered here for the first time, and the results enable comparison with experimental efforts such as CDMS and EDELWEISS, as well as with the DAMA annual modulation claims.
