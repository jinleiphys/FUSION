# Gluon PDF of the proton using twisted mass fermions

> In this paper, we present lattice QCD results for the $x$-dependence of the unpolarized gluon PDF for the proton. We use one ensemble of $N_f=2+1+1$ maximally twisted mass fermions with a clover improvement, and the Iwasaki improved gluon action. The quark masses are tuned to produce a pion with a mass of 260 MeV. The ensemble has a lattice spacing of $a=0.093$ fm and a spatial extent of 3 fm. We employ the pseudo-distribution approach, which relies on matrix elements of non-local operators that couple to momentum-boosted hadrons. In this work, we use five values of the momentum boost between 0 and 1.67 GeV. The gluon field strength tensors of the non-local operator are connected with straight Wilson lines of varying length $z$. The light-cone Ioffe time distribution (ITD) is extracted utilizing data with $z$ up to 0.56 fm and a quadratic parametrization in terms of the Ioffe time at fixed values of $z$. We explore systematic effects, such as the effect of the stout smearing for the gluon operator, excited states effects, and the dependence on the maximum value of $z$ entering the fits to obtain the gluon PDF. Also, for the first time, the mixing with the quark singlet PDFs is eliminated using matrix elements with non-local quark operators that were previously analyzed within the quasi-PDF framework on the same ensemble. Here, we expand the data set for the quark singlet and reanalyze within the pseudo-PDFs method eliminating the corresponding mixing in the gluon PDF.

## Key claim
The authors present the first lattice QCD calculation of the unpolarized gluon PDF that explicitly eliminates the mixing with the quark singlet PDF using non-local quark operator matrix elements, but the results are obtained on a single ensemble with a pion mass of 260 MeV rather than at the physical point, and the PDF reconstruction relies on a simple two-parameter fitting ansatz.

## Method
The calculation uses one ensemble of \(N_f=2+1+1\) maximally twisted mass fermions with clover improvement and Iwasaki improved gluon action, with lattice spacing \(a=0.093\) fm and spatial extent 3 fm. The pseudo-distribution approach is employed, computing matrix elements of non-local gluon operators with straight Wilson lines for five proton momentum boosts between 0 and 1.67 GeV, using over 1.3 million measurements from 1,134 configurations with 200 source positions each. The gluon PDF is extracted by constructing reduced Ioffe-time distributions, applying one-loop matching that includes evolution, conversion to the \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) scheme, and for the first time the mixing with the quark singlet PDF, then fitting a functional form \(x g(x) = N x^a (1-x)^b\) to the light-cone ITD data.

## Key numbers
- Pion mass: 260 MeV (approximately twice the physical value)
- Lattice spacing: \(a = 0.0938(2)(3)\) fm
- Spatial extent: \(L = 3.0\) fm
- Momentum boosts used: 0, 0.42, 0.83, 1.25, 1.67 GeV
- Maximum Wilson line length used in ITD extraction: \(z = 0.56\) fm (6 lattice units)
- Total measurements: 1,360,800 (1,134 configurations \(\times\) 200 source positions \(\times\) 6 spatial directions)
- Source-sink time separations investigated: \(t_s = 8a, 9a, 10a, 11a\)
- Stout smearing combinations tested: 25 combinations with \(N^{\rm F}_{\rm stout}\) and \(N^{\rm W}_{\rm stout}\) each taking values 0, 5, 10, 15, 20
- Preferred stout smearing: \(N^{\rm F}_{\rm stout} = 20\), \(N^{\rm W}_{\rm stout} = 10\)
- Relative error on matrix elements at \(z=0\): about 6% for \(P=0\) GeV, about 9% for \(P=1.67\) GeV

## Context
This work extends previous lattice calculations of gluon PDFs by the HadStruc collaboration (HadStruc 2021) and others (Fan 2018, Fan 2020) that neglected the mixing between the gluon and quark singlet PDFs. The authors also build on their own prior work on quark PDFs using the quasi-distribution approach (Alexandrou 2021) by reanalyzing the quark singlet data within the pseudo-PDF framework. The results enable comparisons with global phenomenological analyses such as those from the JAM collaboration (JAM 2021) and provide lattice input that could help constrain future global fits of parton distributions for experiments at the LHC, Jefferson Lab, and the future Electron-Ion Collider.
