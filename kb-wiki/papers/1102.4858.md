# 11B and Constraints on Neutrino Oscillations and Spectra from Neutrino Nucleosynthesis

> We have studied the sensitivity to variations in the triple alpha and 12C(alpha, gamma)16O reaction rates, of the yield of the neutrino process isotopes 7Li,11B, 19F, 138La, and 180Ta in core collapse supernovae. Compared to solar abundances, less than 15% of 7Li, about 25-80% of 19F, and about half of 138La is produced in these stars. Over a range of two sigma for each helium-burning rate, 11B is overproduced and the yield varies by an amount larger than the variation caused by the effects of neutrino oscillations. The total 11B yield, however, may eventually provide constraints on supernova neutrino spectra.

## Key claim

The yield of \(^{11}\)B from the neutrino process in core-collapse supernovae varies by a factor of two or more due to uncertainties in the triple-alpha and \(^{12}\)C(\(\alpha,\gamma\))\(^{16}\)O reaction rates, which is larger than the variation caused by neutrino oscillations; therefore, \(^{11}\)B cannot currently constrain the oscillation parameter \(\sin^2 2\theta_{13}\) or the neutrino mass hierarchy, though the total \(^{11}\)B yield may eventually constrain supernova neutrino spectra if nuclear rate uncertainties are reduced.

## Method

The authors used the KEPLER stellar evolution code to model 15, 20, and 25 solar mass stars from hydrogen burning through core collapse, with a piston-driven explosion. They varied the triple-alpha and \(^{12}\)C(\(\alpha,\gamma\))\(^{16}\)O reaction rates by \(\pm 2\sigma\) around their central values (1.0 times the Caughlan and Fowler rate for triple-alpha, with \(\sigma = 12\%\); 1.2 times the Buchmann rate for \(^{12}\)C(\(\alpha,\gamma\))\(^{16}\)O, with \(\sigma = 25\%\)), and computed the yields of the neutrino-process isotopes \(^{7}\)Li, \(^{11}\)B, \(^{19}\)F, \(^{138}\)La, and \(^{180}\)Ta using neutrino cross sections from Heger (2005) and Fermi-Dirac neutrino spectra with temperatures of 4 MeV for \(\nu_e\) and \(\bar{\nu}_e\) and 6 MeV for other flavors.

## Key numbers

- For a 15 \(\mathrm{M}_\odot\) star, variations in the triple-alpha and \(^{12}\)C(\(\alpha,\gamma\))\(^{16}\)O rates within \(\pm 2\sigma\) cause factor-of-two changes in the \(^{11}\)B yield.
- The neutrino process produces less than 15% of solar \(^{7}\)Li, about 25-80% of solar \(^{19}\)F, and about half of solar \(^{138}\)La.
- The meteoritic \(^{11}\)B/\(^{10}\)B ratio is 4.045 (Lodders 2003); the cosmic-ray production ratio is 2.2-2.5, implying that about \(42 \pm 4\%\) of solar \(^{11}\)B must come from the neutrino process.
- Increasing the \(\nu_e\) and \(\bar{\nu}_e\) temperature from 4 to 6 MeV increases neutrino-process yields by factors of 1.5 to 2 (Heger 2005).
- The mean energies of the new neutrino spectra (Janowski 2007, Langanke 2008) differ by less than 12% from the Fermi-Dirac distributions used here.

## Context

This work extends studies by Tur (2007, 2009) on the sensitivity of nucleosynthesis to helium-burning reaction rates, and directly tests the proposals by Yoshida (2006a, 2006b) and Kajino (2008) that \(^{7}\)Li and \(^{11}\)B yields could constrain the neutrino oscillation parameter \(\sin^2 2\theta_{13}\) and the mass hierarchy. The authors find that current rate uncertainties preclude such constraints, but they identify \(^{11}\)B as the most promising isotope for future work because its neutrino cross sections on \(^{12}\)C are experimentally well-determined. They call for improved measurements of the triple-alpha and \(^{12}\)C(\(\alpha,\gamma\))\(^{16}\)O rates, better grain observations (noting that Hoppe (2001) found only upper limits on neutrino-process boron in supernova SiC grains), and more realistic neutrino spectra from supernova simulations.
