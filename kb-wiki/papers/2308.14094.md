# Production of various elements in ultraperipheral $^{208}$Pb-$^{208}$Pb collisions at the LHC

> As predicted by theory and confirmed by measurements, one, two or three neutrons are emitted frequently in ultraperipheral collisions (UPCs) of heavy relativistic nuclei, in particular, $^{208}$Pb. The exchange of low-energy Weizsäcker--Williams photons dominates in such interactions. This leads to the excitation and decay of Giant Dipole Resonances (GDR) in colliding nuclei below the proton emission threshold. Less is known about the electromagnetic dissociation of $^{208}$Pb induced by energetic photons leading to violent fragmentation of $^{208}$Pb. The UPCs of lead nuclei at the LHC were modelled with Relativistic ELectromagnetic Dissociation (RELDIS) model to evaluate the contribution of photonuclear reactions in the domain of quasideuteron absorption and at higher photon energies. It was demonstrated that due to the presence of a single heavy residue in the final state mostly accompanied by free protons and neutrons, the cross sections of the production of specific elements can be well approximated by the proton emission cross sections, which can be measured in the ALICE experiment at the LHC.

## Key claim

The cross sections for producing specific elements (such as Tl, Hg, Au) in ultraperipheral Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC can be well approximated by the cross sections for emitting corresponding numbers of protons, because the nuclear residue after the intranuclear cascade evaporates nucleons but does not undergo fission or multifragmentation; this approximation holds best for elements produced with up to three proton emissions.

## Method

The authors used the Relativistic ELectromagnetic Dissociation (RELDIS) model to simulate ultraperipheral collisions of 208Pb nuclei at the LHC at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV. The model treats the Lorentz-contracted Coulomb fields as fluxes of equivalent Weizsacker-Williams photons and calculates cross sections for photon absorption in three energy regimes: Giant Dipole Resonance (GDR) excitation at 7-40 MeV, quasideuteron absorption at 40-140 MeV, and hadron photoproduction above 140 MeV. The calculations separately evaluated contributions from each photon energy range to the production of specific elements and to the emission of given numbers of protons and neutrons.

## Key numbers

- Total electromagnetic dissociation cross section for 208Pb at the LHC is close to 210 b (barns)
- Cross sections for production of thallium isotopes 207Tl through 197Tl range between 1 and 4 barns
- Approximately 85% of 207Tl production events are from photons above 140 MeV (knocking out a single proton)
- About 50% of 204Tl and 205Tl nuclei are produced by photons in the 40-140 MeV range (quasideuteron absorption)
- Less than 1 out of 15 Tl nuclei are produced by low-energy GDR photons (7-40 MeV)
- Beam energy per nucleon is 2.51 TeV

## Context

This work extends previous measurements by the ALICE collaboration (Dmitrieva et al., 2022) of forward neutron emission in UPCs of 208Pb at the LHC, which were described by the RELDIS model (Pshenichnov, 2011) based on the Weizsacker-Williams method (Bertulani, 1988). The present study focuses on the less-explored proton emission channels and production of secondary elements, showing that element production cross sections can be inferred from proton emission measurements. The calculated distributions of total proton energy in Zero Degree Calorimeters provide guidelines for future ALICE measurements, though the much wider proton peaks compared to neutron peaks make it difficult to measure cross sections beyond 1p, 2p, and 3p emission.
