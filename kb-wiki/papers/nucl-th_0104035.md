# Discovery of Jet Quenching at RHIC and the Opacity of the Produced Gluon Plasma

> The predicted quenching of jets in A+A at RHIC energies has been discovered by STAR and PHENIX in preliminary data reported at this conference. We apply the GLV theory of QCD radiative energy loss to estimate the opacity, L/λ_g, of the gluon plasma produced in Au+Au collisions at 130 AGeV. We show that (in contrast to the factor of two Cronin enhancement of π^0 found at the SPS by WA98) the factor of 5-8 suppression of p_T \sim 2-4 GeV π^0 reported by PHENIX can be accounted for with an effective static plasma opacity L/λ_g \approx 3-4.

## Key claim
The factor of 5-8 suppression of π⁰ production at p_T ∼ 2-4 GeV observed by PHENIX in central Au+Au collisions at 130 AGeV can be accounted for by the GLV theory of QCD radiative energy loss using an effective static plasma opacity L/λ_g ≈ 3-4, in contrast to the factor-of-two Cronin enhancement seen at SPS energies by WA98.

## Method
The authors applied the GLV (Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev) formalism for QCD radiative energy loss, solved to first order in opacity, to compute the energy-dependent energy loss of gluon mini-jets in an idealized static plasma with screening scale μ = 0.5 GeV, α_s = 0.3, and mean free path λ_g = 1 fm. They incorporated this energy loss into a perturbative QCD calculation of pion production in Au+Au collisions at 130 AGeV, including nuclear shadowing, intrinsic parton transverse momentum, and impact-parameter dependence, and compared the resulting spectra to preliminary PHENIX data.

## Key numbers
- The observed suppression factor of π⁰ at p_T ∼ 2-4 GeV in central Au+Au collisions is 5-8 fold compared to pQCD expectations.
- The effective static plasma opacity extracted is L/λ_g ≈ 3-4.
- The gluon mean free path is taken as λ_g = 1 fm, with screening scale μ = 0.5 GeV and α_s = 0.3.
- For 10% central Au+Au collisions, the impact parameter integration upper limit is b_max = 4.7 fm.
- The ratio of central to peripheral pion yields is normalized by the number of binary collisions: 857 for central and 5.5 for peripheral.

## Context
This work extends the GLV theory of radiative energy loss (Gyulassy, Levai, Vitev, 2000) to interpret the first RHIC jet quenching observations by STAR and PHENIX, contrasting with the Cronin enhancement seen at SPS by WA98. The results contradict earlier asymptotic BDMS approximations (Baier, Dokshitzer, Mueller, Peigne, Schiff, 1997) that predicted a different energy dependence, and they enable future studies of expanding plasmas (Gyulassy, Levai, Vitev, 2001) that could extract initial parton densities up to 100 times nuclear matter density.
