# Shape of atomic nuclei in heavy ion collisions

> In the hydrodynamic model description of heavy ion collisions, the final-state anisotropic flow $v_n$ are linearly related to the strength of the multi-pole shape of the distribution of nucleons in the transverse plane $\varepsilon_n$, $v_n\propto \varepsilon_n$. The $\varepsilon_n$, for $n=1,2,3,4$, are sensitive to the shape of the colliding ions, characterized by the quadrupole $β_2$, octupole $β_3$ and hexadecapole $β_4$ deformations. This sensitivity is investigated analytically and also in a Monte Carlo Glauber model. One observes a robust linear relation, $\langle\varepsilon_n^2\rangle = a_n'+b_n'β_n^2$, for events in a fixed centrality. The $\langle\varepsilon_1^2\rangle$ has a contribution from $β_3$ and $β_4$, and $\langle\varepsilon_3^2\rangle$ from $β_4$. In the ultra-central collisions, there are little cross contributions between $β_2$ and $\varepsilon_3$ and between $β_3$ and $\varepsilon_2$, but clear cross contributions are present in non-central collisions. Additionally, $\langle\varepsilon_n^2\rangle$ are insensitive to non-axial shape parameters such as the triaxiality. This is good news because the measurements of $v_2$, $v_3$ and $v_4$ can be used to constrain simultaneously the $β_2$, $β_3$, and $β_4$ values. This is best done by comparing two colliding ions with similar mass numbers and therefore nearly identical $a_n'$, to obtain simple equation that relates the $β_n$ of the two species. This opens up the possibility to map the shape of the atomic nuclei at a timescale ($<10^{-24}$s) much shorter than probed by low-energy nuclear structure physics ($<10^{-21}$s), which ultimately may provide information complementary to those obtained in the nuclear structure experiments.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a robust linear relation between the mean square eccentricity \(\langle\varepsilon_n^2\rangle\) and the square of the nuclear deformation parameter \(\beta_n^2\) for fixed centrality, \(\langle\varepsilon_n^2\rangle = a_n' + b_n'\beta_n^2\), with the hedge that cross-contributions from different-order deformations (e.g., \(\beta_3\) to \(\varepsilon_1\), \(\beta_4\) to \(\varepsilon_3\)) are significant in non-central collisions but negligible in ultra-central collisions, and that \(\langle\varepsilon_n^2\rangle\) is insensitive to non-axial shape parameters such as triaxiality.

## Method

The relation between initial-state eccentricities \(\varepsilon_n\) (for \(n=1,2,3,4\)) and nuclear deformation parameters \(\beta_2\), \(\beta_3\), \(\beta_4\) was investigated analytically using a liquid-drop model with a sharp nuclear surface and numerically via a Monte Carlo Glauber model with a Woods-Saxon density profile. The Monte Carlo Glauber model simulated collisions of \(^{238}\)U+\(^{238}\)U and \(^{96}\)Zr+\(^{96}\)Zr systems at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=200\) GeV, calculating \(\varepsilon_n\) from participant nucleons and from participant quarks (using a quark Glauber model with three quark constituents per nucleon) as a function of the number of participants \(N_{\mathrm{part}}\) or quark participants \(N_{\mathrm{qp}}\).

## Key numbers

* For \(^{238}\)U+\(^{238}\)U collisions, the slope parameter \(b_n'\) in the relation \(\langle\varepsilon_n^2\rangle = a_n' + b_n'\beta_n^2\) is approximately 0.2–0.3 for \(n=2,3,4\) in ultra-central collisions (0–0.2% centrality), decreasing toward mid-central and peripheral collisions.
* The octupole deformation \(\beta_3\) contributes to \(\langle\varepsilon_1^2\rangle\) with a slope \(b_{1,3}' \approx 0.015\) in ultra-central collisions; a modest \(\beta_3 = 0.1\) increases \(\langle\varepsilon_1^2\rangle\) by about 15% relative to the deformation-free baseline \(a_1'\).
* The hexadecapole deformation \(\beta_4\) contributes to \(\langle\varepsilon_3^2\rangle\) with a slope \(b_{3,4}'\) that is significant in mid-central collisions but negligible in ultra-central collisions; values in Zr+Zr are about 20% smaller than in U+U in mid-central collisions.
* For the quadrupole deformation \(\beta_2 = 0.28\) in U+U collisions, the triaxiality parameter \(\gamma\) has very little effect on \(\langle\varepsilon_2^2\rangle\) over most of the centrality range; the deviation scales as \(\cos(3\gamma)\) with a coefficient \(c_2'\) that is small.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of quadrupole deformation effects on elliptic flow \(v_2\) in heavy ion collisions (e.g., Giacalone (2021), Adamczyk (2015), Acharya (2018), Sirunyan (2019), Aad (2019)) by systematically including octupole (\(\beta_3\)) and hexadecapole (\(\beta_4\)) deformations and their cross-contributions to \(\varepsilon_1\), \(\varepsilon_2\), \(\varepsilon_3\), and \(\varepsilon_4\). It also builds on the observation that the Pearson correlation coefficient \(\rho(v_2^2,[p_T])\) is sensitive to \(\beta_2\) and triaxiality (Giacalone (2019, 2020), Jia (2021)). The results enable simultaneous constraints on \(\beta_2\), \(\beta_3\), and \(\beta_4\) from measurements of \(v_2\), \(v_3\), and \(v_4\) in ultra-central collisions, particularly by comparing two collision systems with similar mass numbers to cancel the centrality-dependent baseline \(a_n'\). This provides a method to map nuclear shapes at timescales \(<10^{-24}\) s, complementary to low-energy nuclear structure experiments probing timescales \(<10^{-21}\) s.
