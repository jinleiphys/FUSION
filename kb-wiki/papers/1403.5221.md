# Next-to-leading order forward hadron production in the small-$x$ regime: rapidity factorization

> Single inclusive hadron production at forward rapidity in high energy p+A collisions is an important probe of the high gluon density regime of QCD and the associated small-$x$ formalism. We revisit an earlier one-loop calculation to illustrate the significance of the "rapidity factorization" approach in this regime. Such factorization separates the very small-$x$ unintegrated gluon density evolution and leads to a new correction term to the physical cross section at one-loop level. Importantly, this rapidity factorization formalism remedies the previous unphysical negative next-to-leading order contribution to the cross section. It is much more stable with respect to "rapidity" variation when compared to the leading-order calculation and provides improved agreement between theory and experiment in the forward rapidity region.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a "rapidity factorization" procedure, which separates the very small-$x$ unintegrated gluon density evolution from finite one-loop contributions, introduces a new NLO correction term that remedies the previously unphysical negative cross sections obtained in the one-loop calculation of Stasto (2013).

## Method
The authors revisit the NLO calculation for single inclusive hadron production at forward rapidity in p+A collisions within the small-$x$ formalism. They separate the rapidity divergence in the one-loop correction by introducing a rapidity cut-off $Y_0$, absorbing the divergent part into a redefinition of the dipole scattering amplitude via the Balitsky-Kovchegov evolution equation, and identifying a finite correction term $\Delta H_Y$ from the remaining rapidity interval. The calculation is performed in light-front perturbation theory with dimensional regularization, and the collinear divergences are absorbed into the DGLAP evolution of parton distribution functions and fragmentation functions.

## Key numbers
- The previous NLO result from Stasto (2013) becomes negative for $p_\perp \gtrsim 2.5$ GeV at $y=3.2$ in d+Au collisions at RHIC, while the new formalism yields positive-definite cross sections.
- For the rapidity factorization scale variation $\kappa = x_g/x_A$ in the range (0.25, 2), the LO result shows $\pm 50\%$ variation, while the new NLO result shows only $\pm 10\%$ variation; the previous NLO result from Stasto (2013) shows more than a factor of 2 variation.
- The GBW model parameters used are: $Q_{s0}=1$ GeV, $x_0=3.04\times 10^{-4}$, $\lambda=0.288$, and $c=0.56$ for minimum bias p+A collisions.

## Context
This work extends the first NLO calculation of forward hadron production by Chirilli (2011) and addresses the unphysical negative cross sections identified by Stasto (2013). It introduces a rapidity factorization scheme that separates the small-$x$ dynamics of "fast" and "slow" gluon fields, analogous to how collinear factorization separates short-distance from long-distance physics. The formalism enables more reliable NLO phenomenology for gluon saturation searches at RHIC and LHC, and is expected to have important applications for the planned Electron Ion Collider.
