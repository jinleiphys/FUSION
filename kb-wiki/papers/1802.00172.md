# Phenomenology of anomalous chiral transports in heavy-ion collisions

> High-energy Heavy-ion collisions can generate extremely hot quark-gluon matter and also extremely strong magnetic fields and fluid vorticity. Once coupled to chiral anomaly, the magnetic fields and fluid vorticity can induce a variety of novel transport phenomena, including the chiral magnetic effect, chiral vortical effect, etc. Some of them require the environmental violation of parity and thus provide a means to test the possible parity violation in hot strongly interacting matter. We will discuss the underlying mechanism and implications of these anomalous chiral transports in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that strong magnetic fields and fluid vorticity generated in heavy-ion collisions, when coupled to the chiral anomaly, induce anomalous chiral transport phenomena such as the chiral magnetic effect (CME) and chiral vortical effect (CVE); however, experimental signals for these effects are ambiguous because elliptic-flow-driven background effects strongly mask the signals and complicate interpretation.

## Method

The author reviews the generation of magnetic fields and vorticity in non-central heavy-ion collisions using numerical simulations, then derives the anomalous chiral transport currents from the chiral anomaly using the lowest Landau level (LLL) approximation in a strong magnetic field and an analogous rotating-frame treatment for vorticity. The paper further discusses how these transport effects can be probed experimentally via charge-dependent two-particle azimuthal correlations and three-particle correlations, and examines background subtraction strategies such as comparing isobaric collisions (Ru+Ru vs. Zr+Zr) and using central U+U collisions.

## Key numbers

* Magnetic field in RHIC Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV: ~10^19 Gauss.
* Magnetic field in LHC Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV: ~10^20 Gauss.
* Event-averaged vorticity from STAR Collaboration across beam energies 7.7 GeV < sqrt(s) < 200 GeV: omega ~ (9 +/- 1) x 10^21 /s.
* For isobaric collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV, the CME signal differs by more than 10% between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr, while elliptic-flow-driven backgrounds differ by less than 2% for centrality 20-60%, assuming the CME contributes 1/3 of the gamma correlation.
* The chiral magnetic wave (CMW) equations yield two gapless wave modes with propagation speeds determined by the susceptibilities chi_R and chi_L.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of anomalous chiral transport developed by Kharzeev (2007), Fukushima (2008), Son (2004), and others, and reviews experimental searches by the STAR, PHENIX, ALICE, and CMS collaborations. The paper enables future experimental tests of parity violation in hot QCD matter, particularly through the planned isobaric run at RHIC in 2018, and motivates further studies to disentangle CME signals from background effects such as local charge conservation and transverse momentum conservation.
