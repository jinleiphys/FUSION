# Shear and bulk viscosities of the gluon plasma across the transition temperature from lattice QCD

> We investigate the temperature dependence of the shear viscosity ($η$) and bulk viscosity ($ζ$) of the gluon plasma using lattice QCD over the range 0.76--2.25$\,T_c$, extending from below the transition temperature $T_c$ across the transition region and into the deconfined phase. At each temperature, we employ three large, fine lattices, which enables controlled continuum extrapolations of the energy-momentum tensor correlators. Using gradient flow together with a recently developed blocking technique, we achieve percent-level precision for these correlators, providing strong constraints for a model-based spectral analysis. Since the inversion to real-time information is intrinsically ill posed, we extract viscosities by fitting spectral functions whose ultraviolet behavior is matched to the best available perturbative result, while the infrared region is described by a Lorentzian transport peak. The dominant modeling uncertainty associated with the transport peak width is bracketed by varying it over a physically motivated range set by thermal scales. We find that the shear-viscosity-to-entropy-density ratio, $η/s$, exhibits a minimum near the transition temperature $T_c$ and increases for $T>T_c$, whereas the bulk-viscosity-to-entropy-density ratio, $ζ/s$, decreases monotonically over the entire temperature range studied.

## Key claim

The shear-viscosity-to-entropy-density ratio (η/s) of the gluon plasma exhibits a minimum near the transition temperature Tc and increases for T > Tc, while the bulk-viscosity-to-entropy-density ratio (ζ/s) decreases monotonically over the entire range 0.76–2.25 Tc; these results depend on a model-based spectral reconstruction where the dominant modeling uncertainty from the transport peak width is bracketed by varying it over a physically motivated range set by thermal scales.

## Method

The authors computed Euclidean energy-momentum tensor (EMT) correlators in SU(3) Yang-Mills theory on the lattice at seven temperatures from 0.76 Tc to 2.25 Tc, using three large, fine lattices at each temperature to enable controlled continuum extrapolations. Gradient flow combined with a blocking technique achieved percent-level precision for the correlators, which were then extrapolated to zero flow time and to the continuum limit. Shear and bulk viscosities were extracted by fitting spectral functions whose ultraviolet behavior was matched to the next-to-leading-order perturbative result, while the infrared region was described by a Lorentzian transport peak whose width was varied over a range set by thermal scales to bracket the modeling uncertainty.

## Key numbers

- Temperature range studied: 0.76–2.25 Tc, covering below Tc, across the transition, and into the deconfined phase.
- Finest lattice spacing used: a = 0.01164 fm; largest spatial extent: L = 3.31 fm.
- Aspect ratio not smaller than 4 across all ensembles.
- Approximately 5000 configurations generated for each ensemble.
- The blocking method improved signal quality by a factor of 3–7 without increasing computational cost.
- For the shear channel, the flow-time window used for extrapolation was sqrt(8 τF)/τ ∈ [0.45, 0.52]; an additional constraint from discretization effects required τF/r0² ≥ 0.0002 at 0.76 Tc.
- For the bulk channel, discretization effects on the renormalization constant c2 were less than 0.8% across all τF/r0² values.
- The shear channel flow-time extrapolation fits yielded χ²/d.o.f. in the range 0.003–0.304; the bulk channel fits yielded χ²/d.o.f. in the range 0.002–1.127.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Altenkort (2022), which extracted viscosities at a single temperature (1.5 Tc), to a wide temperature range 0.76–2.25 Tc, exceeding the coverage of earlier lattice studies by Astrakhantsev (2017, 2018) that were restricted to 0.9–1.5 Tc and used small, relatively coarse lattices without continuum extrapolations. The results provide nonperturbative first-principles constraints on transport coefficients of the gluon plasma, enabling improved comparisons with phenomenological studies of heavy-ion collisions and with perturbative calculations such as the next-to-leading-order weak-coupling QCD estimate η/s ∼ 0.25 near Tc by Ghiglieri (2018).
