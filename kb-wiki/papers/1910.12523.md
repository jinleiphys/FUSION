# Gluon transversity in polarized proton-deuteron Drell-Yan process

> Nucleon spin structure functions have been investigated mainly by longitudinally-polarized ones for finding the origin of the nucleon spin. Other types of spin structure functions are transversely-polarized ones. In particular, quark transversity distributions in the nucleons have very different properties from the longitudinally-polarized quark distribution functions, especially in scaling violation, because they are decoupled from the gluon transversity, due to the fact that they are helicity-flip (chiral-odd) distributions. Such studies are valuable for finding not only the origin of the nucleon spin but also a signature on physics beyond the standard model, because the electric dipole moment of the neutron is proportional to the transversity distributions. Now, there is experimental progress on the quark transversity distributions; however, there is no experimental information on gluon transversity. In fact, the gluon transversity does not exist for the spin-1/2 nucleon due to the helicity-conservation constraint. One needs a hadron with spin more than or equal to one, so that the helicity flip of two units is allowed. A stable spin-1 target is, for example, the deuteron for studying the gluon transversity. In this work, we propose a possibility for finding the gluon transversity at hadron-accelerator facilities, especially in the proton-deuteron Drell-Yan process with the linearly-polarized deuteron, by showing theoretical formalism and numerical results. We show the dependencies of the Drell-Yan cross section on the dimuon-mass squared $M_{μμ}^{\,2}$, the dimuon transverse-momentum $q_T$, the dimuon rapidity $y$ in the center-of-momentum frame, and the magnitude of the gluon transversity $Δ_T g$.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the gluon transversity distribution \(\Delta_T g\) can be measured for the first time at hadron accelerator facilities using the proton-deuteron Drell-Yan process with a linearly-polarized deuteron target, but they note that the proton beam does not need to be polarized and that the gluon transversity exists only in hadrons with spin \(\ge 1\) such as the deuteron.

## Method

The authors develop the theoretical formalism for the Drell-Yan process \(p + d \to \mu^+\mu^- + X\) using collinear factorization, expressing the cross section in terms of parton distribution functions including the gluon transversity of the deuteron. They derive the spin-dependent cross section and spin asymmetries by incorporating the spin-1 deuteron polarization density matrix and the gluon correlation function, then present numerical results showing dependencies on the dimuon-mass squared \(M_{\mu\mu}^{\,2}\), the dimuon transverse momentum \(q_T\), the dimuon rapidity \(y\), and the magnitude of the gluon transversity \(\Delta_T g\).

## Key numbers

* The dimuon-mass squared range considered is \(M_{J/\psi}^{\, 2} < Q^2 < M_{\Upsilon}^{\, 2}\), corresponding to the large \(Q^2\) region accessible by the Drell-Yan process, in comparison with the JLab region typically from a few GeV\(^2\) to several GeV\(^2\).
* The Fermilab SpinQuest experiment (E1039) is identified as a specific experimental project where the proposed proton-deuteron Drell-Yan measurement could be performed.

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of transversity distributions, which have been investigated for quarks (e.g., by Ralston and Soper (1979), Artru and Mekhfi (1990), Jaffe and Ji (1991)) but have no experimental information for gluons. It proposes a hadron-accelerator alternative to the JLab electron-deuteron DIS measurement of gluon transversity proposed by JLab (JLab gluon transversity experiment), and it is related to ongoing Fermilab SpinQuest (E1039) and future EIC measurements. The paper enables experimental searches for non-nucleonic components in the deuteron beyond the simple proton-neutron bound state, as the internal spin-1/2 nucleons cannot contribute directly to the gluon transversity.
