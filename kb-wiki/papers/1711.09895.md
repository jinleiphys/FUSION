# $ϕ$ meson in nuclear matter and nuclei

> The mass and decay width of the $ϕ$ meson in cold nuclear matter are computed in an effective Lagrangian approach, with the medium dependence of these properties obtained by evaluating kaon-antikaon loop contributions to the $ϕ$ self-energy. At normal nuclear matter density, we find a downward shift of the $ϕ$ mass by a few percent, while the decay width is enhanced by an order of magnitude. We also present $ϕ$--nucleus bound state energies and absorption widths for some selected nuclei, using complex optical potentials obtained in the local density approximation. Our results suggest that the $ϕ$ should form bound states with all the nuclei considered. However, the identification of the signal for these predicted bound states will need careful investigation because of their sizable absorption widths.

# φ meson in nuclear matter and nuclei

## Key claim
The φ meson is expected to form bound states with all nuclei considered (⁴He, ¹²C, ¹⁶O, ²⁰⁸Pb) when produced in nearly recoilless kinematics, but the identification of these bound states will require careful investigation because their absorption widths are sizable and may make experimental observation difficult.

## Method
The in-medium φ meson mass and decay width were computed by evaluating kaon-antikaon (K K̅) loop contributions to the φ self-energy using an effective Lagrangian with a φ K K̅ vertex, where the in-medium kaon and antikaon masses were calculated within the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model. The φ-nucleus bound state energies and absorption widths were then obtained by solving the Klein-Gordon equation with complex optical potentials constructed via the local density approximation, using nuclear density distributions from the QMC model for all nuclei except ⁴He.

## Key numbers
- At normal nuclear matter density ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³, the effective kaon mass decreases by about 13%
- The φ mass shift at normal nuclear matter density is a downward shift of a few percent (less than about 5%)
- The φ decay width in nuclear matter is enhanced by an order of magnitude at normal nuclear matter density, and up to a factor of approximately 20 at the largest density considered
- For ²⁰⁸Pb with Λ_K = 4000 MeV, the 1s bound state energy is -25.8 MeV with half-width Γ/2 = 16.0 MeV (including the absorptive potential); with only the real potential the energy is -26.0 MeV
- For ⁴He with Λ_K = 4000 MeV, the 1s bound state energy is -1.0 MeV with half-width Γ/2 = 8.3 MeV; no bound state is found for Λ_K = 2000 or 3000 MeV when the absorptive potential is included
- For ¹²C with Λ_K = 4000 MeV, the 1s bound state energy is -9.8 MeV with half-width Γ/2 = 11.2 MeV

## Context
This work extends earlier theoretical studies of vector mesons in nuclear matter (Klingl 1996) by using the QMC model (Tsushima 1997) to compute in-medium kaon masses, and then applying these results to predict φ-nucleus bound states for the first time. The results are directly relevant to planned experiments at J-PARC (JPARC E29 Proposal) and Jefferson Lab (JLab φ Proposal) that aim to produce slowly moving φ mesons in nuclei. The finding that bound states may exist but with large absorption widths motivates future work on explicit reaction cross section estimates to determine experimental feasibility.
