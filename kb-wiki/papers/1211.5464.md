# In-medium effects on hypernuclear formation

> We study strangeness dynamics in reactions relevant for the formation of multi-strange hypernuclei. In particular, we discuss the in-medium effects on elementary hyperon-nucleon ($YN$) channels, which are relevant for the production of hypernuclei at PANDA. The results indicate strong in-medium effects on $YN$-scattering, which might be important for hypernuclear studies at PANDA.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in-medium effects on hyperon-nucleon (YN) scattering, primarily from Pauli blocking of intermediate nucleon states, are strong enough to significantly suppress YN cross sections inside nuclear matter, and that these effects must be incorporated into transport simulations to accurately model hypernucleus formation at PANDA.

## Method

The authors used the Giessen-Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) transport model to simulate the non-equilibrium stage of reactions leading to hypernucleus formation. In-medium YN scattering was calculated by solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the R-matrix, using a free-space YN potential obtained by rescaling the Bonn NN potential coupling constants, and including a Pauli projector for intermediate states. The density and energy dependence of the resulting in-medium cross sections were then analyzed.

## Key numbers

- The rescaling parameters for the NN coupling constants to obtain the free-space YN potential are: \(b_{\Lambda N, \Sigma N} \simeq 0.8-0.86\) for \(\Lambda N \rightarrow \Lambda N\) and \(\Sigma N \rightarrow \Sigma N\), and \(b_{\Lambda\Sigma}=0.02\) for \(\Lambda N \rightarrow \Sigma N\).
- At saturation density (\(\rho = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\)), the in-medium YN cross sections are suppressed by about 50% compared to free-space values.
- In-medium cross sections were calculated at densities of \(\rho = 0, 0.08, 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The scattering length \(a_s\) for the \(n\Lambda\) and \(p\Sigma^{-}\) channels is shown as a function of Fermi momentum \(k_F\), with a strong reduction observed for the \(p\Sigma^{-}\) channel near saturation.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous GiBUU studies on the formation of \(S=-1\) and \(S=-2\) hypernuclei (Gaitanos et al., 2012) by focusing on the in-medium dependence of elementary YN channels. It builds on Dirac-Brueckner studies of NN and YN in-medium interactions (DBHF) and on YN potential results from the Juelich group (ref4, julich). The findings are directly relevant for the PANDA experiment and the HypHI experiment, and the authors state that parametrizing the density and energy dependence of the in-medium cross sections for implementation in transport codes is in progress.
