# Charged-current quasielastic neutrino scattering from $^{12}$C in an extended superscaling model with two-nucleon emission

> The quasielastic cross-section of charged-current neutrino and antineutrino scattering on $^{12}$C is calculated using an improved superscaling model with relativistic effective mass. Our model encompasses two-particle emission induced by neutrinos, which we distinguish into two contributions. The first contribution arises from meson-exchange currents, and its calculation is performed at a microscopic level. The second contribution is phenomenological and extracted from the high-energy tail of the scaling function, assumed to be produced by 2p2h mechanisms where the one-body current plays a role, such as short-range correlations and interferences with MEC, final-state interaction, etc. The model explicitly includes the modification of the relativistic effective mass of the nucleon within the relativistic mean field model of nuclear matter. The meson exchange currents are also consistently calculated within the same model. With this model, we present predictions for the neutrino and antineutrino cross sections of $^{12}$C that have been measured in accelerator experiments.

## Key claim

The paper presents predictions for charged-current quasielastic neutrino and antineutrino scattering on $^{12}$C using an extended superscaling model that explicitly separates two-nucleon emission into a microscopically calculated meson-exchange current contribution and a phenomenological tail contribution attributed to short-range correlations and other processes, with the caveat that the model overestimates data at very small scattering angles where the factorization approximation breaks down.

## Method

The authors extended the SuSAM* (superscaling analysis with relativistic effective mass) model by adding two 2p2h contributions: a microscopically calculated meson-exchange current (MEC) contribution computed within the relativistic mean field (RMF) model of nuclear matter, and a phenomenological 2p2h tail contribution extracted from the high-energy tail of the scaling function using a pure phase-space model. The 1p1h contribution was obtained from a symmetric Gaussian scaling function fitted to $(e,e')$ data after subtracting the MEC contribution, while the SRC tail was parameterized using a phase-space function with $q$-dependent 2p2h coefficients. The model used a relativistic effective mass $M^*=0.8$, Fermi momentum $k_F=225$ MeV/c, and nucleon vector energy $E_v=141$ MeV for $^{12}$C.

## Key numbers

- Relativistic effective mass parameter $M^* = 0.8$ for $^{12}$C
- Nucleon vector energy $E_v = 141$ MeV for $^{12}$C
- Fermi momentum $k_F = 225$ MeV/c for $^{12}$C
- Gaussian scaling function parameters: $a = 0.744$ and $b = 0.682$ for $f_{1p1h}^*(\psi^*) = b e^{-(\psi^*)^2 / a^2}$
- 2p2h parameter coefficient $a_0 = 345$ in $c^{pn}(q)=a_0 \sqrt{(m_N^*+q)/q} \, (m_N^*/q)$
- Axial form factor value $C_5^A(0) = 0.89$ used for the $\Delta$ current (revised downward from 1.2)
- Estimated $\Delta$ effective mass $M_\Delta^* = 1042$ MeV under universal coupling assumption
- Total 2p2h contribution approximately 30% of the total cross section
- Estimated uncertainty band for 2p2h response on the order of $\pm 20\%$ due to $\Delta$ current uncertainties

## Context

This work extends the SuSAM* model of Amaro et al. (2015, 2017) by incorporating two-nucleon emission channels, building on the earlier SuSA model of Donnelly et al. (1999) and the SuSAv2+MEC model of Megias et al. (2016, 2018). The approach differs from SuSAv2+MEC by using a unified analytical scaling function derived from $(e,e')$ data, incorporating the relativistic effective mass in the scaling variable, and including the full $\Delta$ propagator rather than only its real part. The model enables neutrino event generators to distinguish between 1p1h and 2p2h final states, which is important for reducing uncertainties in neutrino oscillation experiments such as MiniBooNE, T2K, and MINERvA.
