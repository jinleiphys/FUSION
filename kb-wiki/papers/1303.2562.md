# Relativistic Mean-Field Models and Nuclear Matter Constraints

> This work presents a preliminary study of 147 relativistic mean-field (RMF) hadronic models used in the literature, regarding their behavior in the nuclear matter regime. We analyze here different kinds of such models, namely: (i) linear models, (ii) nonlinear σ^3+σ^4 models, (iii) σ^3+σ^4+ω^4 models, (iv) models containing mixing terms in the fields σand ω, (v) density dependent models, and (vi) point-coupling ones. In the finite range models, the attractive (repulsive) interaction is described in the Lagrangian density by the σ(ω) field. The isospin dependence of the interaction is modeled by the ρmeson field. We submit these sets of RMF models to eleven macroscopic (experimental and empirical) constraints, used in a recent study in which 240 Skyrme parametrizations were analyzed. Such constraints cover a wide range of properties related to symmetric nuclear matter (SNM), pure neutron matter (PNM), and both SNM and PNM.

## Key claim

Out of 147 relativistic mean-field (RMF) hadronic models tested against eleven macroscopic constraints, only 9 models satisfy all constraints except the one related to the isospin dependence of the incompressibility (Kτ,v); if that constraint is included, none of the models are consistent.

## Method

The authors submitted 147 RMF parametrizations from the literature to 11 experimental and empirical constraints covering symmetric nuclear matter (4 constraints), pure neutron matter (2 constraints), and symmetry energy properties involving both (5 constraints). The models were grouped into six types: linear models, nonlinear σ³+σ⁴ models, σ³+σ⁴+ω⁴ models, models with σ-ω mixing terms, density-dependent models, and point-coupling models. For each model, the equations of state (energy density and pressure) were calculated from the energy-momentum tensor in the mean-field approximation, and nuclear matter properties at saturation density were extracted.

## Key numbers

- Out of 147 RMF models tested, only 9 satisfy all constraints except the Kτ,v constraint
- Saturation densities (ρ₀) for the 9 approved models range from 0.146 fm⁻³ (BSR15, BSR16, BSR17, BSR18, FSUGZ06) to 0.153 fm⁻³ (TW99)
- Binding energies at saturation (E₀) range from -16.40 MeV (FSUGold4) to -16.03 MeV (BSR15)
- Incompressibility (K₀) values range from 221.13 MeV (BSR18) to 240.27 MeV (TW99)
- Symmetry energy at saturation (J) ranges from 30.97 MeV (BSR15) to 32.77 MeV (TW99)
- Slope parameter L ranges from 51.74 MeV (FSUGold4) to 72.65 MeV (BSR18)
- Effective nucleon masses (m*) are 0.61 for all nonlinear models and 0.55-0.56 for the density-dependent models (DD-F and TW99)

## Context

This work extends a previous study (Dutra et al., 2012) that analyzed 240 Skyrme parametrizations against the same set of 11 macroscopic constraints, applying the same methodology to relativistic mean-field models. The study identifies that among the 147 RMF models, only 9 (7 nonlinear with σ-ω mixing terms and 2 density-dependent) pass the constraints, and none pass when the Kτ,v constraint is included, indicating that the isospin dependence of incompressibility requires further investigation. This enables the selection of RMF parametrizations that best describe both symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter for use in astrophysical applications such as neutron star modeling.
