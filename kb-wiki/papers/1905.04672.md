# Mean-Field Theory for Fermion Pairs and the ab initio Particle-Vibration-Coupling Approach

> A Dyson Bethe-Salpeter equation (Dyson-BSE) for fermion pairs is presented whose kernel has a static and a one frequency dependent contribution, analogous to the self energy of the single particle Dyson equation with the (static) mean field term and the energy dependent correlation term. The static part of the Dyson-BSE is the self-consistent mean field for the vibrations. At the same time, for the correlated single particle self-energy a full particle-vibration coupling (PVC) scattering equation is established where the vibration is the same as obtained from the Dyson-BSE. Both equations, single particle Dyson equation and Dyson-BSE, are coupled through self-consistency. Numerical results for Lipkin and 1D Hubbard chain are very promising.

## Key claim

The paper presents a formally exact Dyson-Bethe-Salpeter equation (Dyson-BSE) for fermion pairs whose kernel splits into a static "pair mean field" part and a frequency-dependent dynamic part, analogous to the single-particle Dyson equation. The approach is intended to be ab initio (starting from a bare force) rather than phenomenological, but the numerical demonstrations are limited to the Lipkin model and the 1D Hubbard chain, not to realistic nuclear systems.

## Method

The author derives a Dyson-BSE for the two-time fermion-pair propagator in both the particle-particle (pp) and particle-hole (ph) channels, with the kernel separated into a static commutator term (the "fermion pair mean field") and a dynamic term involving a 3p-1h propagator. The static part is shown to lead to self-consistent RPA (SCRPA) equations, while the dynamic part is approximated to yield a particle-vibration coupling (PVC) scattering equation for the single-particle self-energy. The single-particle Dyson equation and the Dyson-BSE are then coupled through self-consistency, with occupation numbers determined from the single-particle Green's function.

## Key numbers

* Lipkin model: numerical results from the eo-SCRPA (extended self-consistent RPA) approach are described as "very promising" when compared with exact solutions, but no specific numerical values are given in the provided text.
* 1D Hubbard chain: numerical results are similarly described as "very promising" when compared with exact solutions, but no specific numerical values are given in the provided text.
* The paper references a screening factor of "almost a factor of two" for the pairing force from Gorkov-Melik-Barkhudarov (GMB) screening, but this is a literature result, not a new numerical result from this work.

## Context

This work extends the particle-vibration coupling (PVC) approach of Bortignon and collaborators (Colo), which was based on phenomenological effective forces (Skyrme or Gogny type), toward an ab initio framework starting from a bare force such as the Bonn force (CD-Bonn). The formalism builds on prior work by the author and collaborators on the self-consistent RPA (SCRPA) and the coupled-cluster doubles (CCD) wave function (Jemai 2013), and on a longer article discussing the one-frequency BSE in the ph channel for electronic systems (Julien). The approach enables a fully self-consistent treatment of single-particle and two-particle propagators, with potential applications to electron systems (plasmon coupling) and nuclear systems, though the numerical demonstrations are currently limited to model systems.
