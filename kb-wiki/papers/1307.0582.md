# Renormalization and power counting of chiral nuclear forces

> I discuss the progress we have made on modifying Weinberg's prescription for chiral nuclear forces, using renormalization group invariance as the guideline. Some of the published results are presented.

# Renormalization and power counting of chiral nuclear forces

## Key claim
Weinberg's power counting (WPC) for chiral nuclear forces must be modified in certain partial waves to satisfy renormalization group (RG) invariance; specifically, the singlet-1S0 channel requires a non-vanishing O(Q) correction (a two-derivative contact term) that WPC had assumed to be zero, while the triplet channels 3P0 and 3P2-F2 require enhancement of all counterterms by O(M_hi^2/M_lo^2) relative to WPC.

## Method
The author examines the residual cutoff dependence of NN scattering amplitudes when a momentum cutoff Lambda is used to regulate NN-reducible diagrams, requiring that cutoff errors vanish at least as fast as (Q/Lambda)^2 for LO amplitudes to maintain the O(Q^2) theoretical uncertainty claimed by WPC. For triplet channels, the superficial degree of divergence of matrix elements of two-pion exchanges (TPEs) between LO scattering wave functions is analyzed to determine which subleading counterterms are needed for renormalization. For the singlet 1S0 channel, the LO amplitude is expressed in a form that isolates the cutoff dependence, revealing that the residual cutoff error scales as O(Q^2/M_lo*Lambda) rather than O(Q^2/Lambda^2), necessitating an O(Q) counterterm.

## Key numbers
- The low-energy scale M_lo ~ 4*pi*f_pi^2/m_N ~ 0.1 GeV emerges from the ratio of the box diagram to one-pion exchange: V_box/V_1pi ~ (m_N/(4*pi*f_pi))*(k/(A*f_pi)), where A ~ O(1) in lower partial waves.
- The high-energy scale M_hi ~ 4*pi*f_pi ~ 1 GeV sets the breakdown scale of the chiral EFT.
- The LO 1S0 amplitude has residual cutoff dependence of O(Q^2/M_lo*Lambda), which is parametrically larger than the O(Q^2/Lambda^2) required for WPC's claimed O(Q^2) theoretical uncertainty.
- In triplet channels 3P0 and 3P2-F2, all counterterms require enhancement by O(M_hi^2/M_lo^2) relative to WPC.

## Context
This work extends the approach of Long (2007) and follows the renormalization-group invariance criterion to modify Weinberg's original power counting (Weinberg, 1990-1991) for chiral nuclear forces. It builds on earlier findings by Nogga, Timmermans, and van Kolck (2005) that singular attractions in 3P0 and 3P2 demand more counterterms than WPC at LO, and by Beane, Bedaque, Phillips, and Rupak (2000) and Pavon Valderrama and Ruiz Arriola (2007) regarding triplet channel cutoff dependence. The results enable a more consistent chiral nuclear EFT with controlled theoretical uncertainties, particularly for S and P waves where one-pion exchange must be resummed nonperturbatively.
