# Spectator and participant decay in heavy ion collisions

> We analyze the thermodynamical state of nuclear matter in transport calculations of heavy--ion reactions. In particular we determine temperatures and radial flow parameters from an analysis of fragment energy spectra and compare to local microscopic temperatures obtained from an analysis of local momentum space distributions. The analysis shows that the spectator reaches an equilibrated freeze-out configuration which undergoes simultaneous fragmentation. The fragments from the participant region, on the other hand, do not seem to come from a common fragmenting source, but rather heavier fragments seem to be formed earlier.

## Key claim

The spectator region in heavy ion collisions reaches an equilibrated freeze-out configuration that undergoes simultaneous fragmentation, but fragments from the participant region do not come from a common fragmenting source in thermodynamical equilibrium; instead, heavier fragments appear to be formed earlier than the nucleon freeze-out time.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic Landau-Vlasov transport calculations for Au+Au collisions at beam energies of 0.25-0.8 AGeV, using the NL2 parametrization for self energies. They determined local thermodynamic temperatures by fitting phase space distributions with covariant hot Fermi-Dirac distributions, and generated fragments using a phase space coalescence algorithm. Fragment kinetic energy spectra were then analyzed with the Siemens-Rasmussen blast model to extract slope temperatures and radial flow parameters, which were compared to the local microscopic temperatures.

## Key numbers

- Spectator local temperature: T_loc ~ 5-6 MeV at densities ρ ~ 0.4-0.5 ρ₀, occurring at about 45 fm/c after the collision
- Spectator fragment slope temperature (A_f ≥ 2): T_slope ~ 17 ± 2 MeV from blast model fits
- Spectator nucleon low-energy component slope temperature: T_slope,low = 7.3 ± 3.5 MeV
- Spectator fragment kinetic energies range from 23.7 MeV (A_f=2) to 28.1 MeV (A_f=8)
- Participant region local temperature at nucleon freeze-out (about 35-40 fm/c): T_loc ~ 15 MeV at approximately normal nuclear density

## Context

This work extends the method developed by Fuchs (1997) for determining local thermodynamic variables from transport calculations, applying it to Au+Au collisions studied experimentally by the ALADIN and EOS collaborations. The results for spectator fragments agree well with ALADIN data (1999), showing that slope temperatures are not thermodynamic temperatures but include contributions from Fermi motion as described by the Goldhaber model (Bauer). For the participant region, the mass-dependent slope temperatures contradict the assumption of a common freeze-out source used in experimental analyses by EOS (1995) and FOPI (Reisdorf), indicating that fragment formation times vary with fragment mass.
