# Strange Baryons in Nuclei and Neutron Stars

> In this contribution we review the dynamics of hyperons with nucleons and nuclear matter, paying a special attention to hypernuclei. We also discuss the presence of hyperons in the inner core of neutron stars and the consequences for the structure of these compact stars.

# Strange Baryons in Nuclei and Neutron Stars

## Key claim
Hyperons likely appear in the inner core of neutron stars, softening the equation of state and producing maximum masses below the observed 2 solar mass threshold, but this "hyperon puzzle" may be resolved by stiffening mechanisms such as hyperonic three-body forces, stiffer YN and YY interactions, or phase transitions to quark matter.

## Method
The author reviews theoretical approaches to hyperon-nucleon (YN) and hyperon-hyperon (YY) interactions, including meson-exchange models, chiral effective field theory (χEFT), lattice QCD, low-momentum schemes, and quark-model potentials. Properties of hypernuclei are examined, with particular focus on the hypertriton lifetime puzzle. The equation of state for neutron star matter is computed within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock framework using LO and NLO chiral YN interactions, including three-body forces for the Λ-nuclear interaction.

## Key numbers
- The separation energy of the Λ in hypertriton (³ΛH) is about 130 keV, resulting in an RMS radius of 10.6 fm for the hypertriton.
- The Λ single-particle potential becomes repulsive about two to three times nuclear saturation density.
- Observations of 2 solar mass neutron stars have been determined (Demorest 2010, Antoniadis 2013, Cromartie 2020).
- The STAR collaboration measurement suggests a binding energy for hypertriton about three times larger than the original benchmark from Juric (1973).

## Context
This review extends the author's earlier comprehensive review (Tolos 2020) on strange baryons in nuclear systems and neutron stars. It addresses the hypertriton lifetime puzzle, where recent ALICE measurements (2019) indicate consistency with the free Λ lifetime, while STAR (2019) suggests a larger binding energy. The hyperon puzzle, the tension between predicted hyperon softening and observed 2 solar mass neutron stars, is discussed with proposed solutions including three-body forces (Gerstung 2020), Δ baryons, meson condensates, and quark matter phase transitions (Vidana 2018, Tolos 2020).
