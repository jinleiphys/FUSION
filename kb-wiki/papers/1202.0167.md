# Properties of universal bosonic tetramers

> The system of four identical bosons is studied using momentum-space equations for the four-particle transition operators. Positions, widths and existence limits of universal unstable tetramers are determined with high accuracy. Their effect on the atom-trimer and dimer-dimer scattering observables is discussed. We show that a universal shallow tetramer intersects the atom-trimer threshold twice leading to resonant effects in ultracold atom-trimer collisions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a universal shallow tetramer (the second tetramer associated with a given Efimov trimer) intersects the atom-trimer threshold twice as the two-boson scattering length is varied, becoming an inelastic virtual state in a specific regime; this behavior was not predicted in prior coordinate-space calculations that neglected the finite width of higher tetramers.

## Method

The author solved the exact Alt, Grassberger, and Sandhas (AGS) four-particle equations for transition operators in momentum-space partial-wave framework, using a rank-1 separable two-boson potential with two different form factors to demonstrate universality. The tetramer positions, widths, and existence limits were extracted from the pole structure of the AGS transition operators in the complex energy plane, as inferred from the behavior of atom-trimer and dimer-dimer scattering observables at real energies.

## Key numbers

- At unitarity (a = infinity), the deeper tetramer position converges to B_{n,1}/b_n = 4.610(1) and width to Gamma_{n,1}/2b_n = 0.01483(1)
- At unitarity, the shallow tetramer position converges to B_{n,2}/b_n = 1.00227(1) and width to Gamma_{n,2}/2b_n = 2.38(1) x 10^{-4}
- The deeper tetramer intersects the dimer-dimer threshold at a_{n,1}^{dd}/a_n^{dd} = 0.3235(1)
- The shallow tetramer intersects the dimer-dimer threshold at a_{n,2}^{dd}/a_n^{dd} = 0.99947(2)
- The deeper tetramer intersects the four-free-atom threshold at a_{n,1}^{0}/a_n^{0} = 0.4254(2)
- The shallow tetramer intersects the four-free-atom threshold at a_{n,2}^{0}/a_n^{0} = 0.9125(2)
- The shallow tetramer intersects the atom-trimer threshold at a_n^{v,1}/a_n^{dd} = 13.75(5) and a_n^{v,2}/a_n^{dd} = 1.0016(1)
- The trimer binding energy ratio b_{n-1}/b_n at unitarity is approximately 515.035
- The atom-dimer threshold intersection occurs at a_n^{d} sqrt(m b_n^u) = 0.07076(1)
- The three-free-atom threshold intersection occurs at -a_n^{0} sqrt(m b_n^u) = 1.5077(1)

## Context

This work extends prior predictions by Hammer (2007) and Stecher (2009) that two 0+ tetramers exist for each Efimov trimer, but achieves much higher accuracy by considering highly excited trimers (n >= 4) where finite-range effects become negligible. The results contradict the coordinate-space calculations of Stecher (2009) and Dincao (2009), which overestimated the shallow tetramer's distance from the atom-trimer threshold by factors up to 35 and did not predict the double intersection with the atom-trimer threshold. The findings enable predictions of resonant enhancement in ultracold atom-trimer collisions and dimer-dimer relaxation processes at specific scattering lengths, and provide universal ratios that can guide experimental searches for tetramer resonances.
