# Threshold $J/ψ-$ production in nucleon-nucleon collisions

> We analyze $J/ψ-$ production in nucleon-nucleon collisions near threshold in the framework of a general model independent formalism, which can be applied to any reaction $N+N\to N+N+V^0$, where $V^0=ω$, $ϕ$, or $J/ψ$. Such reactions show large isotopic effects: a large difference for $pp$- and $pn$-collisions, which is due to the different spin structure of the corresponding matrix elements. The analysis of the spin structure and of the polarization observables is based on symmetry properties of the strong interaction. Using existing experimental data on the different decays of $J/ψ-$meson, we suggest a model for $N+N\to N+N+J/ψ$, based on $t-$channel $η+π$-exchanges. We predict polarization phenomena for the $n+p\to n+p+J/ψ$-reaction and the ratio of cross sections for $np$ and $pp$-collisions. For the processes $η(π)+N\to N+J/ψ$ we apply two different approaches: vector meson exchange and local four-particle interaction. In both cases we find larger $J/ψ$-production in $np$-collisions, with respect to $pp$-collisions.

## Key claim

The paper presents a model-independent formalism for threshold \(J/\psi\) production in nucleon-nucleon collisions, but the actual numerical predictions depend on a single complex parameter \(r\) whose value can only be estimated from existing \(J/\psi\) decay data, with the sign of \(r\) remaining undetermined.

## Method

The authors analyze the spin structure of threshold \(N+N\to N+N+V^0\) reactions using symmetry properties (Pauli principle, P-invariance, isotopic invariance) to derive partial \(S\)-wave amplitudes for \(pp\) and \(np\) collisions. They then construct a dynamical model based on \(t\)-channel \(\pi+\eta\) meson exchange, using experimental branching ratios of \(J/\psi\) hadronic decays to estimate the relative contribution of \(\eta\) and \(\pi\) exchanges via vector meson exchange and local four-particle interaction approaches.

## Key numbers

- Threshold energy for \(J/\psi\) production in nucleon-nucleon collisions: \(E_{th}=12.24\) GeV in the laboratory system, corresponding to \(\sqrt{s}=2m+m_{J/\psi}\simeq 5\) GeV
- At threshold, the momentum transfer squared is \(t=-mm_V\simeq -3\) GeV\(^2\) for \(J/\psi\) production
- For pure \(\pi\)-exchange: ratio of cross sections \(\mathcal{R}^{(\pi)}=5\) for \(np\) versus \(pp\) collisions
- For pure \(\sigma\)-exchange: \(\mathcal{R}^{(\sigma)}=1\) and \(\mathcal{A}_1^{(\sigma)}(np)=-1\)
- For pure \(\eta\)-exchange: \(\mathcal{R}^{(\eta)}=1\) and \(\mathcal{A}_1^{(\eta)}(np)=1/2\)
- Branching ratios used: \(BR(J/\psi\to \rho^0\pi^0)=(4.2\pm 0.5)\times 10^{-3}\), \(BR(J/\psi\to \omega\eta)=(1.58\pm 0.16)\times 10^{-3}\), \(BR(J/\psi\to p\overline{p}\eta)=(2.09\pm 0.18)\times 10^{-3}\), \(BR(J/\psi\to p\overline{n}\pi^-)=(2.00\pm 0.10)\times 10^{-3}\)

## Context

This work extends the formalism previously applied to light vector meson production (\(\omega\), \(\phi\)) in nucleon-nucleon collisions (Rekalo 1997, Titov 2001) to the heavy \(J/\psi\) meson, where perturbative QCD approaches are less straightforward. The analysis provides a framework for interpreting future threshold measurements at the GSI facility, and the predicted strong isotopic effects (larger \(np\) than \(pp\) cross sections) are essential for understanding \(J/\psi\) production in nucleus-nucleus collisions as a probe of quark-gluon plasma formation (Matsui 1986).
