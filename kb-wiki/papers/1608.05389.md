# Rapidity Correlation Structures from Causal Hydrodynamics

> Viscous diffusion can broaden the rapidity dependence of two-particle transverse momentum fluctuations. Surprisingly, measurements at RHIC by the STAR collaboration demonstrate that this broadening is accompanied by the appearance of unanticipated structure in the rapidity distribution of these fluctuations in the most central collisions. Although a first order classical Navier-Stokes theory can roughly explain the rapidity broadening, it cannot explain the additional structure. We propose that the rapidity structure can be explained using the second order causal Israel-Stewart hydrodynamics with stochastic noise.

## Key claim

First-order Navier-Stokes diffusion cannot simultaneously explain both the broadening and the non-Gaussian shape of the rapidity correlation structure of transverse momentum fluctuations observed by STAR; second-order causal Israel-Stewart hydrodynamics with stochastic noise is required, and the relaxation time \(\tau_\pi\) is the primary influence on the shape.

## Method

The authors derive evolution equations for two-particle transverse momentum correlations from linearized first-order Navier-Stokes and second-order Israel-Stewart hydrodynamics under Bjorken flow. They solve the resulting diffusion equations for the correlation function \(\Delta r_G\) in relative rapidity \(\eta_r\) and average rapidity \(\eta_a\), using moment methods to obtain the rapidity width \(\sigma^2\). The calculated rapidity correlation profiles and widths are compared to STAR data for Au+Au collisions at top RHIC energy across centrality classes, with parameters including kinematic viscosity \(\nu = \eta/(Ts)\), relaxation time \(\tau_\pi = \beta\nu\), formation time \(\tau_0\), and freeze-out time \(\tau_F\).

## Key numbers

*   STAR measured the RMS width in \(\eta_r\) of \(\mathcal{C}(\eta_r)\) for Au+Au collisions at top RHIC energy; the first-order fit gave \(\eta/s = 0.13 \pm 0.03\) (Agakishiev 2011).
*   For second-order fits, the authors use \(\eta/s = 1/(4\pi)\) with \(T = 150\) MeV, \(\tau_0 = 1.0\) fm, \(\tau_{Fc} = 10\) fm, and \(\beta = 10\) (where \(\tau_\pi = \beta\nu\)) to achieve agreement with data.
*   For first-order fits to the width, the authors use \(\eta/s = 1/(4\pi)\) with \(T = 140\) MeV, \(\tau_0 = 0.65\) fm, and \(\tau_{Fc} = 12\) fm.
*   The initial width in relative rapidity is set to \(\sigma_0 = 0.50\) to fit the most peripheral distribution.
*   Kinetic theory of massless Boltzmann particles predicts \(\beta = 5\); the authors find \(\beta = 10\) yields the best agreement with data.

## Context

This work extends earlier work by Gavin and Abdel-Aziz (2006) that suggested viscous diffusion broadens the rapidity dependence of two-particle transverse momentum correlations, which stimulated the STAR measurement (Agakishiev 2011). The STAR data revealed a non-Gaussian structure that first-order Navier-Stokes cannot reproduce, motivating the use of second-order causal Israel-Stewart hydrodynamics. The results enable experiments to constrain the relaxation time \(\tau_\pi\), a transport coefficient sensitive to the thermalization process, and build on linearized second-order equations discussed by Romatschke (2009) and Young (2014).
