# Hyperon single-particle potentials in nuclear matter based on baryon-baryon interactions derived within chiral effective field theory

> An analysis of the Lambda and Sigma single-particle potentials is presented, based on YN interactions derived within chiral effective field theory up to next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^2$LO). The self-consistent Brueckner-Hartree-Fock framework is employed within the continuous choice for the single-particle potential. The result for the Lambda single-particle potential is comparable to the ones obtained with previous chiral YN interactions up to next-to-leading order (NLO). The Sigma single-particle potential is found weakly attractive, in contrast to earlier weakly repulsive results, reflecting new constraints from the recent J-PARC E40 data on $Σ^+p$ scattering. An estimate of the theoretical uncertainty of the single-particle potentials is provided.

## Key claim

The Lambda single-particle potential predicted by the new chiral N²LO interactions is slightly more attractive (around -41 to -46 MeV at saturation density) than the quasi-empirical value of approximately -30 MeV, while the Sigma single-particle potential is found weakly attractive (around -10 to -11 MeV at saturation density), in contrast to earlier weakly repulsive results from NLO13 and NLO19 interactions, reflecting new constraints from J-PARC E40 data on Σ⁺p scattering.

## Method

The authors employed the self-consistent Brueckner-Hartree-Fock framework within the continuous choice for the single-particle potential, using hyperon-nucleon (YN) interactions derived from chiral effective field theory up to next-to-next-to-leading order (N²LO) with semi-local momentum space (SMS) regularization. The nucleon single-particle potential was evaluated from the SMS N⁴LO⁺ NN force with cutoff 450 MeV, and the YN interactions were used with various cutoffs (500, 550, and 600 MeV) and two variants (a and b) for the N²LO(550) potential with different P-wave interactions. Theoretical uncertainties were estimated using the Epelbaum-Krebs-Meissner (EKM) method.

## Key numbers

- Lambda single-particle potential at saturation density (k_F = 1.35 fm⁻¹) for SMS N²LO interactions: -41.2 MeV (500 cutoff), -45.8 MeV (550a), -43.4 MeV (550b), -44.1 MeV (600 cutoff)
- Lambda single-particle potential for NLO13(500): -33.1 MeV; for NLO19(500): -40.9 MeV at saturation density
- Sigma single-particle potential at saturation density for SMS N²LO(550)a: -10.3 MeV; for SMS N²LO(550)b: -11.0 MeV
- Sigma single-particle potential for NLO13(500): +3.7 MeV (repulsive); for NLO19(500): +11.2 MeV (repulsive) at saturation density
- Uncertainty estimate for Lambda at NLO: U_NLO_Lambda(rho_0) = -39.3 ± 7.0 MeV
- Uncertainty estimate for Lambda at N²LO: U_N²LO_Lambda(rho_0) = -43.5 ± 3.8 MeV
- Uncertainty estimate for Sigma in SNM at NLO: U_NLO_Sigma(rho_0) = -11.3 ± 6.7 MeV
- Uncertainty estimate for Sigma in SNM at N²LO: U_N²LO_Sigma(rho_0) = -10.6 ± 3.7 MeV
- Uncertainty estimate for Sigma⁻ in pure neutron matter at NLO: U_NLO_Sigma⁻(rho_0) = 9.4 ± 7.2 MeV
- Uncertainty estimate for Sigma⁻ in pure neutron matter at N²LO: U_N²LO_Sigma⁻(rho_0) = 4.1 ± 4.0 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous chiral YN interactions up to next-to-leading order (NLO) by Haidenbauer et al. (2013, 2019) to next-to-next-to-leading order (N²LO) using the SMS regularization scheme that had been successful in the NN sector by Reinert et al. (2017). The new SMS YN potentials incorporate constraints from recent J-PARC E40 experiments (2022) on Σ⁺p and Σ⁻p differential cross sections, which resolve the discrepancy between earlier NLO13 and NLO19 predictions for the Sigma single-particle potential. The results enable future inclusion of YNN three-body forces to complete the N²LO calculation, which is needed to resolve the hyperon puzzle of neutron stars, and provide uncertainty estimates that can guide phenomenological analyses such as the Lambda-nucleus optical potential work by Friedman (2023).
