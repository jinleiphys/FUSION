# Does quark number scaling breakdown in Pb+Pb collisions at Root_s = 2.76 TeV?

> The anisotropy coefficient $v_2$, for unidentified and identified charged hadrons [pions ($π$), kaons ($K$) and protons ($p$)] measured in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}= 0.20$ TeV (RHIC) and Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}= 2.76$ TeV (LHC), are compared for several collision centralities ($\text{cent}$) and particle transverse momenta $p_T$. In contrast to the measurements for charged hadrons, the comparisons indicate a sizable increase of $v_2(p_T)$ for $π,K$ and $p$, as well as a blueshift of proton $v_2(p_T)$, from RHIC to LHC. When this blueshift is accounted for, the LHC data [for $π$, $K$, $p$] show excellent scaling of $v_2({KE}_T)$ with the number of valence quarks ($n_q$), for a broad range of transverse kinetic energies (${KE}_T$) and collision centralities. These observations suggest a larger mean sound speed $<c_s(T)>$ for the plasma created in LHC collisions, and significant radial flow generation after its hadronization.

## Key claim

The apparent breakdown of quark number scaling (QNS) for identified hadrons in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies is not a fundamental failure of partonic flow, but rather an artifact of a blueshift in the proton \(v_2(p_T)\) data; after correcting for this blueshift by redshifting the proton data by approximately 0.2 GeV/c, excellent QNS is restored for pions, kaons, and protons across a broad range of centralities and transverse kinetic energies.

## Method

The authors compared the elliptic flow coefficient \(v_2(p_T)\) for unidentified charged hadrons and identified pions, kaons, and protons from Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 0.20\) TeV (RHIC, PHENIX data) and Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV (LHC, ALICE and ATLAS data) for several collision centralities. They quantified the increase in \(v_2(p_T)\) from RHIC to LHC by applying a scale factor of approximately 1.2 to the RHIC data for pions and kaons, and for protons they additionally applied a blueshift correction of approximately 0.2 GeV/c to the LHC data before testing QNS by plotting \(v_2(KE_T)/n_q\) versus \(KE_T/n_q\).

## Key numbers

- LHC \(v_2(p_T)\) values for pions and kaons are approximately 20% larger than the corresponding RHIC values (scale factor \(\sim 1.2\)).
- The proton \(v_2(p_T)\) data from LHC exhibit a blueshift of approximately 0.2 GeV/c relative to RHIC proton data.
- After redshifting the LHC proton data by \(\sim 0.2\) GeV/c, excellent quark number scaling of \(v_2(KE_T)/n_q\) vs. \(KE_T/n_q\) is observed for pions, kaons, and protons for a broad range of centralities and transverse kinetic energies.
- The comparison of \(v_2(p_T)\) for charged hadrons shows good agreement between RHIC and LHC for a broad range of \(p_T\) and centralities, in contrast to the identified hadron results.

## Context

This work extends the comparison of anisotropic flow measurements from RHIC (PHENIX, 2006-2012) to LHC (ALICE, ATLAS, 2010-2012), addressing the conundrum that while charged hadron \(v_2(p_T)\) appeared similar between the two energies, identified hadron measurements suggested a breakdown of quark number scaling at LHC (Raimond, 2011). The authors show that the apparent scaling breakdown is due to increased radial flow in the hadronic phase at LHC, and that after correcting for the proton blueshift, partonic flow still dominates. This enables future studies of the temperature dependence of the equation of state and transport properties using identified particle measurements at both RHIC and LHC.
