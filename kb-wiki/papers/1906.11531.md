# A look at multiplicity distributions via modified combinants

> The experimentally measured multiplicity distributions exhibit, after closer inspection, peculiarly enhanced void probability and oscillatory behavior of the modified combinants. We show that both these features can be used as additional sources of information, not yet fully explored, on the mechanism of multiparticle production. We provide their theoretical understanding within the class of compound distributions.

# A look at multiplicity distributions via modified combinants

## Key claim

The experimentally observed enhanced void probability (P(0) > P(1)) and oscillatory behavior of modified combinants C_j in multiplicity distributions can be understood within the class of compound distributions, specifically those based on the Binomial Distribution (BD) compounded with other distributions, but only when the BD component includes the M=0 term, a condition that single or multi-component Negative Binomial Distributions (NBDs) cannot satisfy.

## Method

The authors derive a recurrence relation (Eq. rCj) that allows modified combinants C_j to be extracted directly from experimentally measured multiplicity distributions P(N) using only probabilities P(N<j). They then analyze C_j behavior for various compound distributions: Poisson compounded with any distribution yields non-oscillating C_j, while BD-based compound distributions produce oscillatory C_j whose period and amplitude depend on the decay distribution. The authors fit experimental data from ALEPH (e+e- at 91 GeV) and ALICE (pp collisions) using a two-component compound distribution of BD with NBD, adjusting parameters to simultaneously reproduce both P(N) and C_j.

## Key numbers

- ALEPH e+e- data at 91 GeV fitted with BD parameters k'=1 and p'=0.8725, NBD parameters k=4.2 and p=0.75
- ALICE pp data fitted with two-component compound distribution (BD+NBD) using parameters: K1=K2=3, p1=0.9, p2=0.645, k1=2.8, k2=1.34, m1=5.75, m2=23.5, w1=0.24, w2=0.76
- For BD compounded with delta-function decay P(n)=δ_{n,m}, C_j = (-1)^{j/m+1} (K/⟨N⟩) (p/(1-p))^{j/m+1} for j=mk, and C_j=0 for j≠mk
- The condition P(0) > P(1) requires ⟨N⟩C_0 < 1; additionally P(2) > P(1) requires C_1 > C_0(2 - ⟨N⟩C_0)
- In e+e- annihilations, C_j oscillations have period 2; in pp collisions, the oscillation period is approximately 10 times longer

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous development of modified combinants (Rybczynski et al., 2014; 2015) and addresses the long-standing observation of enhanced void probability (P(0) > P(1)) that cannot be reproduced by standard NBD fits (Kittel, 2004; Giovannini and Ugoccioni, 1999). The analysis reveals a qualitative difference between e+e- and pp/pbar-p collision mechanisms: while both show oscillatory C_j, the period and amplitude differ dramatically, challenging claims of universality in hadron production (Biswas, 2018; G-OR, 2018). The compound distribution approach enables future discrimination between competing production models by using C_j as an additional constraint beyond the multiplicity distribution itself.
