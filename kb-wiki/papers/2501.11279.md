# Center vortices and the $\mathrm{SU}(3)$ conformal window

> A novel approach for estimating the lower end of the $\mathrm{SU}(3)$ conformal window is presented through the study of center vortex geometry and its dependence on the number of fermion flavors $N_f$. Values ranging from $N_f = 2$--$8$ are utilized to infer an upper limit for vortex behavior in the low $N_f$ phase, which may inform the transition to the conformal window. The simulations are performed at a single lattice spacing and pion mass, both fixed for all $N_f$. Visualizations of the center vortex structure in three-dimensional slices of the lattice reveal a growing roughness in the vortex matter as a function of $N_f$, embodied by an increase in the density of vortex matter in the percolating cluster and a simultaneous reduction in secondary clusters disconnected from the percolating cluster in 3D slices. This is quantified by various bulk properties, including the vortex and branching point densities. A correlation of the vortex structure reveals a turning point near $N_f \simeq 5$ past which a randomness in the vortex field becomes the dominant aspect of its evolution with $N_f$. As a byproduct, extrapolations to the vortex content of a uniform-random gauge field provide a critical point at which there must be a drastic shift in vacuum field structure. A precise estimate for the critical value is extracted as $N_f^* = 11.43(16)(17)$, close to various other estimates.

## Key claim

The paper estimates the lower end of the SU(3) conformal window by studying how center vortex geometry changes with the number of fermion flavors N_f, using simulations at a single lattice spacing and pion mass for N_f = 2–8. The authors infer that a drastic shift in vacuum field structure must occur near N_f = 11–12, but note that their extrapolation relies on proposing bounds on vortex observables rather than direct simulation at the critical point.

## Method

The authors generate lattice QCD ensembles with N_f = 2–8 dynamical staggered fermions on a 24^3 × 48 lattice, using a tree-level Symanzik-improved gauge action and two steps of stout smearing. They fix the line of constant physics by tuning the bare parameters so that a m_π = 0.248 and a f_π = 0.0568 for all N_f, then extract center vortices via maximal center gauge fixing and center projection. Vortex density, branching point density, and a correlation measure between consecutive three-dimensional slices are computed for each N_f ensemble, and extrapolations to the vortex content of a uniform-random (Haar) gauge field provide an estimate for the critical flavor number.

## Key numbers

- Vortex density ρ_vortex rises from 0.0324(3) at N_f = 2 to 0.3779(38) at N_f = 8
- Branching point density ρ_branch rises from 0.0073(1) at N_f = 2 to 0.2297(23) at N_f = 8
- Correlation on consecutive slices C(Δ=1) decreases from 0.1833(19) at N_f = 2 to a minimum near N_f = 5 (0.1545(15)), then increases to 0.2057(21) at N_f = 8
- The critical flavor number is extracted as N_f^* = 11.43(16)(17), where the first error is statistical and the second systematic
- The Haar-random ensemble (uniform random gauge fields) yields ρ_vortex = 0.66669(3), ρ_branch = 0.50208(4), and C(Δ=1) = 0.33337(4)

## Context

This work extends the study of center vortices from QCD with 2+1 dynamical flavors (Biddle 2023) to a range of N_f values, using the same lattice setup as Nogradi (2019) and Kotov (2021). The estimate N_f^* = 11.43(16)(17) falls within the range 8 ≤ N_f^* ≤ 13 from prior approaches (Appelquist 1988, Sannino 2004, Dietrich 2006, Braun 2009, Ryttov 2016) and is close to the value suggested by studies of N_f = 12 (Fodor 2011, Hasenfratz 2011, DeGrand 2011). The method enables a new nonperturbative constraint on the conformal window lower edge using vortex geometry, and the authors note that the vortex degrees of freedom may also be sensitive to the bulk phase transition in the bare parameter space.
