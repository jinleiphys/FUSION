# High-Energy Approach for Heavy-Ion Scattering with Excitations of Nuclear Collective States

> A phenomenological optical potential is generalized to include the Coulomb and nuclear interactions caused by the dynamical deformation of its surface. In the high-energy approach analytical expressions for elastic and inelastic scattering amplitudes are obtained where all the orders in the deformation parameters are included. The multistep effect of the 2$^+$ rotational state excitation on elastic scattering is analyzed. Calculations of inelastic cross sections for the $^{17}$O ions scattered on different nuclei at about hundred Mev/nucleon are compared with experimental data, and important role of the Coulomb excitation is established.

## Key claim

The high-energy approximation (HEA) combined with an adiabatic treatment of collective excitations can describe elastic and inelastic heavy-ion scattering at energies of tens of MeV/nucleon and above, but the method requires including both nuclear and Coulomb deformation effects to comparable order, and the deformation parameters used for the nucleus-nucleus potential may differ from those of the target nucleus alone.

## Method

The authors generalize a phenomenological Woods-Saxon optical potential to include quadrupole deformation of the nuclear surface, adding both nuclear and Coulomb transition potentials derived from first-order expansion in the deformation parameter β₂. Using the high-energy (eikonal) approximation, they obtain analytical expressions for the elastic and inelastic scattering amplitudes that include all orders in β₂ for the μ=0 component and up to second order for μ=±2 components, then compute differential cross sections for ¹⁷O projectiles at 1435 MeV on ⁶⁰Ni, ⁹⁰Zr, ¹²⁰Sn, and ²⁰⁸Pb targets.

## Context

This work extends the diffraction theory of collective state excitations originally developed for nucleon scattering by Drozdov (1955), Inopin (1956), and Blair (1959), and later applied in the Glauber-Sitenko microscopic diffraction theory by Bertsch (1974) and Starodubsky (1980) including second-order terms. The authors compare their results with experimental data from Neto (1995) and with coupled-channel calculations using the ECIS code by Raynal (1981). The key advance is demonstrating that for heavy-ion scattering at high energies, Coulomb excitation cannot be neglected as it was for proton and alpha-particle projectiles, and that the high-energy approximation provides a simpler analytical framework than full coupled-channel calculations for understanding the scattering mechanism.
