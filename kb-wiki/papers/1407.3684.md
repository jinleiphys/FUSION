# Resolving the puzzling decay patterns of charged $Z_c$ and $Z_b$ states

> We investigate the ratio of the branching fractions of the molecular candidates decaying into the ground and radially excited states within the quark interchange model. Our numerical results suggest that these molecular candidates are more likely to decay into the radially excited states than into ground states. Especially, the ratio $Γ[Z_c(4430)\to πψ(2S)]$/$Γ[Z_c(4430)\to πJ/ψ]\sim 9.8$ is close to the experimental measurement, which supports the interpretation of $Z_c(4430)$ as the $\bar{D}D^*(2S)$ molecular state. The ratios of the branching fractions of $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$ to $πΥ(2S, 3S)$ and $πΥ(1S)$ agrees very well with Belle's measurement. We also predict the similar ratios for $Z_c(3900)$, $Z_c(4020)$, $R_{Z_c(3900)}$$\approx$1.3 and $R_{Z_c(4020)}$$\approx$$4.7$. Hopefully the $πψ(2S)$ mode, and ratios $R_{Z_c(3900)}$ and $R_{Z_c(4020)}$ will be measured by the BESIII and Belle collaborations in the near future, which shall be very helpful to understand the underlying dynamics of these exotic states.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the puzzling decay pattern of $Z_c(4430)$, which preferentially decays into $\pi\psi(2S)$ over $\pi J/\psi$ by a factor of about 10, can be explained if $Z_c(4430)$ is a $\bar{D}D^*(2S)$ molecular state rather than a conventional tetraquark or a $D_1^{(\prime)}\bar{D}^*$ molecule; the key hedge is that this conclusion depends on the quark-interchange model and the specific choice of wave functions and potential parameters.

## Method

The authors employed the non-relativistic Barnes-Swanson quark-interchange model to compute Born-order T-matrix elements for anticharmed meson-charmed meson scattering into a pion and a charmonium state. They used a Coulomb-plus-linear confining potential with a short-range spin-spin hyperfine term, expanded hadron wave functions as linear combinations of six Gaussian basis functions, and accounted for the finite widths of the molecular candidates by integrating over a Breit-Wigner mass distribution. The "prior" diagrams (interaction before quark interchange) were favored based on the OZI rule and lattice QCD results.

## Key numbers

*   $R_{Z_c(4430)} \equiv \Gamma[Z_c(4430)\to \pi\psi(2S)] / \Gamma[Z_c(4430)\to \pi J/\psi] \approx 9.8$, compared to the experimental value of $\sim 10$.
*   $R_{Z_c(3900)} \approx 1.3$ for $Z_c(3900)$ as a $\bar{D}D^*$ molecular state.
*   $R_{Z_c(4020)} \approx 4.7$ for $Z_c(4020)$ as a $\bar{D}^*D^*$ molecular state.
*   For $Z_b(10610)$ as a $B\bar{B}^*$ molecule: $R_{Z_b(10610)} \approx 11.4$ and $R^\prime_{Z_b(10610)} \approx 2.2$, compared to experimental ranges of $4.8-8.8$ and $2.7-5.3$, respectively.
*   For $Z_b(10650)$ as a $B^*\bar{B}^*$ molecule: $R_{Z_b(10650)} \approx 15.8$ and $R^\prime_{Z_b(10650)} \approx 4.0$, compared to experimental ranges of $4.5-12.1$ and $5.4-14.0$, respectively.
*   Quark model parameters used: $m_u = m_d = 0.334$ GeV, $m_c = 1.776$ GeV, $m_b = 5.102$ GeV, $\sigma = 0.897$ GeV, $b = 0.18$ GeV$^2$, $V_{con} = 0.62$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the molecular interpretation of charged exotic states, previously applied to $Z_c(3900)$ and $Z_c(4020)$ as $\bar{D}D^*$ and $\bar{D}^*D^*$ molecules (Liu et al., earlier work), to the radially excited $Z_c(4430)$. It directly addresses the puzzle created by the LHCb measurement of $J^P = 1^+$ for $Z_c(4430)$, which ruled out the earlier $D_1^{(\prime)}\bar{D}^*$ molecular hypothesis (Rosner, 2007; Meng, 2007; Lee, 2007). The results enable experimental searches for $Z_c(3900)$ and $Z_c(4020)$ in the $\pi\psi(2S)$ final state at BESIII and Belle, and support the molecular picture for the $Z_b$ states observed by Belle (2011).
