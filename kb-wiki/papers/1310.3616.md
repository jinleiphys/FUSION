# Charmonium suppression in a baryon rich quark-gluon plasma

> We have investigated the charmonium survival probability, in a high baryon density parton plasma, expected to be produced in nuclear collisions at FAIR. Charmonia are assumed to undergo complete dissociation by color screening, if the in-medium Debye radius becomes comparable to the spatial size of the corresponding bound state. Results indicate a non-trivial dependence of the suppression pattern on the plasma evolution dynamics. A much larger magnitude of suppression is foreseen induced by cold nuclear matter compared to that due to plasma screening.

# Charmonium suppression in a baryon rich quark-gluon plasma

## Key claim

The authors predict that in low-energy nuclear collisions at FAIR, cold nuclear matter effects will dominate charmonium suppression, with Debye screening in the quark-gluon plasma contributing only an additional 15–25% suppression of the surviving J/ψ yield, contrary to the stronger plasma screening effects seen at higher collision energies.

## Method

The authors use a geometrical screening model where charmonium states (J/ψ, χc, ψ′) undergo complete dissociation when the in-medium Debye screening radius becomes smaller than the state's rms separation radius. The Debye screening mass is computed using leading-order perturbative QCD as a function of temperature T and baryon chemical potential μB, with space-time dependent T and μB extracted from UrQMD model simulations of Au+Au collisions at 30 A GeV, combined with a phenomenological QGP equation of state (Kapusta, 2010). Cold nuclear matter effects (shadowing and final-state dissociation of c c̅ pairs) are calculated using the QVZ model with power-law transition probability, MSTW2008 parton distributions, and EPS09 nuclear modifications.

## Key numbers

- At FAIR energies (Eb = 10–40 A GeV), the Lorentz factor for charmonium states γR,i ≈ 1, so formation times are τR,i ≈ 1–2 fm for J/ψ, ψ′, χc.
- The passing time of colliding nuclei at FAIR is td ≈ 3–4 fm/c, compared to ≈ 1 fm/c at SPS (√sNN ≈ 17.3 GeV).
- Average transverse momentum of J/ψ at FAIR energies is ⟨pT⟩ ≈ 0.17 GeV (exponential distribution) or ⟨pT⟩ ≈ 0.45 GeV (Gaussian distribution), based on p+A data from AGS and CERN-PS.
- In 30 A GeV Au+Au collisions, cold nuclear matter effects cause approximately 90% reduction of the primordial J/ψ yield in central collisions (≈ 15% from initial-state shadowing, ≈ 75% from final-state nuclear dissociation).
- Of the remaining ≈ 10% surviving cold matter suppression, Debye screening dissolves an additional 15–20% depending on the plasma EOS parameter T0 (tested values: 140, 154, and 175 MeV).
- Non-perturbative corrections to the Debye mass (up to a factor A = 2) change the J/ψ suppression by at most 2% in near-central collisions.

## Context

This work extends the geometrical screening model approach used at SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies (Blaizot, 1996; Chu, 1998; Karsch, 1998; Satz, 2000) to the low-energy, high-baryon-density regime of FAIR. It contrasts with prior studies at SPS where NA50 and NA60 collaborations observed anomalous J/ψ suppression of 25–30% in central Pb+Pb collisions beyond cold nuclear matter effects. The authors note that at FAIR, unlike at higher energies, charmonia form before the plasma thermalizes, so the plasma encounters fully formed bound states rather than c c̅ precursors. The work enables experimental planning for the CBM experiment at FAIR, showing that precise measurements with low systematic and statistical errors will be needed to isolate plasma screening effects from the dominant cold nuclear matter suppression. The authors also identify the need for future lattice QCD calculations of screening masses at finite baryon density and for quantitative assessments of partonic dissociation mechanisms (Rapp, 2008) in this energy regime.
