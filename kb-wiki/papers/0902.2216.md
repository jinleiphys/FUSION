# The Double Life of Thermal QCD

> We study the gravity dual of a thermal gauge theory whose behavior parallels that of thermal QCD in the far IR. The UV of our theory has infinite degrees of freedom. We holographically renormalize the supergravity action to compute the stress tensor of the dual gauge theory with fundamental flavors incorporating the logarithmic running of the gauge coupling. From the stress tensor we obtain the shear viscosity and the entropy of the medium at a temperature T, and investigate the violation of the bound for the viscosity to the entropy ratio. This is a shortened and simplified companion paper to arXiv:0902.1540, and is based on the talks given by M. Mia at the "Strong and Electroweak Matter 2008" workshop and the McGill workshop on "AdS/CFT, Condensed Matter and QCD" in the fall of 2008.

## Key claim

The KSS bound for the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio may be violated in certain thermal gauge theories with logarithmic running couplings, but only for a specific parametric range where the UV degrees of freedom are infinite (the parent cascading theory limit); the violation is not present for small values of the UV cutoff parameter.

## Method

The authors construct a gravity dual for a thermal gauge theory by stacking N D3 branes at the tip of a conifold, adding M D5 branes wrapping the S^2 cycle, and N_f D7 branes via Ouyang embedding to introduce fundamental matter, then inserting a black hole to introduce temperature. They holographically renormalize the supergravity action to compute the stress tensor of the dual gauge theory, incorporating the logarithmic running of the gauge coupling, and extract the shear viscosity from the Kubo formula and the entropy density using Wald's formula.

## Context

This work extends the AdS/CFT approach to thermal QCD by constructing a gravity dual with fundamental flavors and logarithmic running couplings, building on the zero-temperature Ouyang embedding (Ouyang, 2004) and the cascading gauge theory framework of Strassler (2005). The authors compare their results to the KSS bound (Kovtun, 2005) and to prior work on viscosity bound violation by Kats (2007). The paper enables further study of transport coefficients in strongly coupled plasmas with running couplings, though the authors note that the physical origin of the parameters needs better definition and that empirical input from RHIC and LHC heavy ion experiments will be crucial.
