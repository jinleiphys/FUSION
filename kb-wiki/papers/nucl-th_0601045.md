# Nuclear Effects in Neutrino Induced Coherent Pion Production at K2K and MiniBooNE Neutrino Energies

> The coherent pion production induced by neutrinos in nuclei is studied using a delta hole model in local density approximation taking into account the renormalization of $Δ$ properties in a nuclear medium. The pion absorption effects have been included in an eikonal approximation. These effects give a large reduction in the total cross section. The numerical results for the total cross section are found to be consistent with recent experimental results from K2K and MiniBooNE collaborations and other older experiments in the intermediate energy region.

# Nuclear Effects in Neutrino Induced Coherent Pion Production at K2K and MiniBooNE Neutrino Energies

## Key claim
The authors show that including nuclear medium effects (via a delta-hole model in local density approximation) and pion absorption effects (via eikonal approximation) reduces the total cross section for coherent pion production enough to bring theoretical predictions into agreement with K2K and MiniBooNE data, whereas earlier PCAC-based calculations overestimated the cross section at low energies.

## Method
The authors compute coherent charged and neutral pion production from nuclei using a delta-resonance dominance model, with the weak transition currents for W and Z exchange evaluated via Feynman diagrams in the s- and u-channels. Nuclear medium effects are incorporated by modifying the delta mass and width through a density-dependent self-energy calculated in the local density approximation of the delta-hole model (Oset and Salcedo, 1987), and final-state pion interactions are treated in the eikonal approximation using a pion optical potential derived from the pion self-energy in the nuclear medium.

## Key numbers
- Total cross section for coherent charged-current π⁺ production on ¹²C with a 450 MeV muon momentum cut: 6.93 × 10⁻⁴⁰ cm², corresponding to 0.578 × 10⁻⁴⁰ cm²/nucleon, consistent with the K2K experimental upper limit of <0.642 × 10⁻⁴⁰ cm²/nucleon.
- Nuclear medium effects reduce the charged-current cross section by 30–35% around E_ν = 1–1.5 GeV.
- Off-shell effects at the πNΔ vertex give a 4–6% reduction in the cross section in the 1–2 GeV energy region.
- Vector current contribution to the cross section is less than 2%; the dominant contribution comes from the axial current.
- Uncertainty in the delta self-energy (15–20%) leads to an 8–12% uncertainty in the total cross section; additional form factor uncertainties contribute 3–5%, giving a total estimated uncertainty of about 15%.

## Context
This work extends earlier theoretical approaches that used Adler's PCAC theorem (Adler, 1964) to relate forward neutrino cross sections to pion-nucleus elastic scattering, which overestimated experimental cross sections at low energies. The authors' microscopic delta-hole model with local density approximation provides a more complete treatment of nuclear medium effects than the approach of Paschos and Kartavtsev (2005), who used PCAC with pion-¹²C elastic scattering data and an approximate A-dependence scaling. The results enable improved background predictions for neutrino oscillation experiments at K2K, MiniBooNE, and future J-PARC measurements.
