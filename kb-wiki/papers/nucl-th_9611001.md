# Inversion Potentials for Meson-Nucleon and Meson-Meson Interactions

> Two-body interactions of elementary particles are useful in particle and nuclear physics to describe qualitatively and quantitatively few- and many-body systems. We are extending for this purpose the quantum inversion approach for systems consisting of nucleons and mesons. From the wide range of experimentally studied two-body systems we concentrate here on $πN$, $ππ$, $K^+N$, $K π$ and $K\bar{K}$. As input we require results of phase shift analyses. Quantum inversion Gelfand-Levitan and Marchenko single and coupled channel algorithms are used for Schrödinger type wave equations in partial wave decomposition. The motivation of this study comes from our two approaches: to generate and investigate potentials directly from data by means of inversion and alternatively use linear and nonlinear boson exchange models. The interesting results of inversion are coordinate space informations about radial ranges, strengths, long distance behaviors, resonance characteristics, threshold effects, scattering lengths and bound state properties.

## Key claim

The authors extend quantum inversion methods (Gelfand-Levitan and Marchenko algorithms) to meson-nucleon and meson-meson systems, using phase shift analyses as input, to generate local, energy-independent potentials that reproduce the on-shell two-body t-matrix; they caution that these local potentials may differ from non-local potentials in their off-shell continuation, which could affect few- and many-body calculations.

## Method

The authors apply fixed-angular-momentum quantum inversion (Gelfand-Levitan and Marchenko single and coupled channel algorithms) to a Schrödinger-type wave equation in partial wave decomposition, using phase shift analyses from Arndt et al. for πN and K⁺N, Froggatt et al. for ππ, Estabrooks et al. for Kπ, and an effective range parameterization for K̄K. They limit input to subinelastic and subreaction threshold data, and the inversion algorithm reproduces input phase shifts to better than 0.02°. For π⁻p and π⁺π⁻ systems, they rotate good-isospin potentials into particle eigenstates and solve coupled-channel Schrödinger equations including Coulomb effects.

## Context

This work extends the quantum inversion approach previously applied to nucleon-nucleon interactions (von Geramb and Kohlhoff, 1994) to meson-nucleon and meson-meson systems, using phase shift analyses from Arndt et al. (1995), Froggatt and Petersen (1977), Estabrooks et al. (1978), and Kaminski and Lesniak (1995). The resulting local potentials enable comparison with boson exchange models (Lohse et al., 1990; Bütten et al., 1990) and chiral perturbation theory (Gasser and Leutwyler, 1983), and allow calculation of hadronic shifts in pionic hydrogen and pionium for comparison with PSI experiments (Sigg et al., 1995). The authors intend to use these potentials to study the hypothetical σ meson and its role in medium-range NN attraction and microscopic optical potentials for nucleon-nucleus scattering.
