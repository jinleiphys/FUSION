# Saturation corrections to dilute-dense particle production and azimuthal correlations in the Color Glass Condensate

> We perform a numerical study of higher order saturation corrections to the dilute-dense approximation for multi-particle production in high-energy hadronic collisions in the framework of the Color Glass Condensate. We compare semi-analytical results obtained by performing a leading order expansion in the dilute field of the projectile with numerical simulations of the full Classical Yang-Mills dynamics for a number of phenomenologically relevant observables. By varying the saturation momentum of the target and the projectile, we establish the regime of validity of the dilute-dense approximation and assess the magnitude and basic features of higher order saturation corrections. In particular, we find that dilute-dense approximation faithfully reproduces dense-dense results if restricted to the range of its validity.

# Saturation corrections to dilute-dense particle production and azimuthal correlations in the Color Glass Condensate

## Key claim
The dilute-dense approximation faithfully reproduces dense-dense results only when restricted to its range of validity, i.e., when the projectile saturation momentum is much smaller than the target saturation momentum; deviations increase with the projectile saturation momentum and are significantly larger for double inclusive observables (such as azimuthal correlations) than for single inclusive observables.

## Method
The authors performed a systematic numerical comparison between semi-analytical dilute-dense calculations (based on a leading-order expansion in the dilute projectile field, including the first saturation correction for odd harmonics) and full Classical Yang-Mills (CYM) simulations of dense-dense collisions. Both approaches used a minimal saturation model with Gaussian color charge distributions for a dilute projectile (proton-like) and a homogeneous dense target (nucleus-like), varying the saturation momenta of projectile and target to map the regime of validity of the dilute-dense approximation.

## Key numbers
- For projectile saturation scale QsP = 1 GeV and target saturation scale QsA = 2.5 GeV, the dilute-dense approximation over-predicts single inclusive multiplicity by approximately 10% and double inclusive variance by approximately 20% compared to dense-dense CYM results.
- For QsP ≳ 1 GeV, higher-order saturation corrections to single inclusive multiplicity remain on the order of ~50% even up to QsP ∼ 4 GeV.
- For QsP ∼ 2 GeV, discrepancies in the variance of the multiplicity reach ~50% between dilute-dense and dense-dense calculations.
- For QsP = 1 GeV, the odd harmonic v3 increases from v3 = 0 at τ = 0+ to v3 ≈ 2% over the course of the classical Yang-Mills evolution (up to τ = 1 fm/c).
- For QsP ≲ 2 GeV, the increase of v3 is well described by the first saturation correction to the dilute-dense limit.

## Context
This work extends previous analytical efforts on saturation corrections in the Color Glass Condensate framework, where the leading-order dilute-dense approximation for single inclusive production was established by Kovchegov (1998), Dumitru and McLerran (2001), and Blaizot et al. (2004), and the first saturation correction for odd harmonics in double inclusive production was derived by McLerran and Kovchegov (2016) and Kovchegov and Skokov (2018). The study enables quantitative assessment of when the dilute-dense approximation is reliable for phenomenological applications such as p+A collisions at RHIC and the LHC, and highlights the need for full dense-dense calculations when projectile saturation scales become comparable to target saturation scales.
