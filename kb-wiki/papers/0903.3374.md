# Study of Quantum Decoherence in a Finite System -- Three Schroedinger Cats and Crossing of Classical Orbits

> Nuclei are rather classical systems in a sense. In the old days, their phenomena were roughly explained in classical rules such as the liquid drop model. This fact may be understood that when we see an finite quantum many body system like nucleus, though which is a group of quantum mechanical particles, its any collective degree of freedom has any classicality. Getting a classicality does not depend on spatial scales of objects. It is made by a phenomenon called Quantum Decoherence. We have studied about Quantum Decoherence in a finite system as nucleus. In this paper, at the harmonic three body problem (3 Schroedinger cats), it is shown that one degree of freedom as a sub-system would get classicality because of the other two degrees of freedom. Therefore we can assume that a nuclear collective degree of freedom would get classicality when it couples with any other internal degrees of freedom. In this paper, we also note that there is some relationship between the Quantum Decoherence and Crossing of classical orbits in 3 Schroedinger cats model.

## Key claim

Using a harmonic three-body model (three Schroedinger cats), the paper shows that one degree of freedom can acquire classicality through quantum decoherence caused by coupling to the other two degrees of freedom, even in a finite isolated quantum system without external environments. The author suggests this mechanism may explain why nuclear collective degrees of freedom behave classically when coupled to internal degrees of freedom.

## Method

The author constructs a model of three harmonically coupled particles with different spring frequencies (asymmetric triangular harmonic oscillators), transforms the system into normal coordinates yielding two uncoupled harmonic oscillators and one free particle, and derives the Feynman propagator for the total wave function. The initial state for each particle is prepared as a pair of Gaussian wave packets (Schroedinger cat states), and the reduced density function for particle-1 is obtained by integrating out the degrees of freedom of the other two particles, corresponding to a coarse-graining procedure.

## Key numbers

- The eigenvalues of the coupling matrix W are given as λ₁ = -ω₁₂² - ω₁₃² - ω₂₃² + Δω² < 0 and λ₂ = -ω₁₂² - ω₁₃² - ω₂₃² - Δω² < 0, with λ₃ = 0, where Δω² = √(ω₁₂⁴ - ω₁₂²ω₁₃² + ω₁₃⁴ - ω₁₃²ω₂₃² + ω₂₃⁴ - ω₂₃²ω₁₂²)
- The normal mode frequencies are Ω₁ = √(-λ₁) and Ω₂ = √(-λ₂)
- The effective masses in normal coordinates are m₁ = 2m(ξ₁² + ξ₁η₁ + η₁²), m₂ = 2m(ξ₂² + ξ₂η₂ + η₂²), and m₃ = 3m

## Context

This work extends the Caldeira and Leggett harmonic oscillator plus reservoir model (Caldeira and Leggett, 1981) to a finite three-body system, investigating whether quantum decoherence can occur without an external infinite reservoir. The author applies this to nuclear physics, motivated by the question of whether nuclear fission states ("fission has done" and "it has not") maintain quantum superposition until observation, or whether decoherence from coupling to internal degrees of freedom collapses the wave function. The paper also discusses the relationship between irreversibility (time reversal symmetry breaking) and non-unitarity (quantum decoherence), suggesting that coarse graining through projection procedures makes systems irreversible and therefore non-unitary.
