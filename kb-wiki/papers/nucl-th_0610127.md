# Propagation of strangelets in the Earth's atmosphere

> A new model for the description of the behaviour of strangelets in the Earth's atmosphere is presented. Strangelet fission induced by collision with air nuclei is included. It is shown that strangelets with certain parameters of initial mass and energy may reach depths near sea level, which can be examined by ground-based experiments.

# Propagation of strangelets in the Earth's atmosphere

## Key claim

Strangelets with initial baryon number A0 ≥ 3000 or initial Lorentz factor γ0 ≥ 140 can penetrate the Earth's atmosphere to reach sea level (atmospheric depth ~1000 g cm⁻²), but this conclusion depends on the rotating liquid drop model and neglects possible color-superconductivity effects; the relevant flux remains uncertain, with Madsen (2005) predicting 1–10 per square meter year steradian for ordinary strangelets.

## Method

The authors model strangelets as a gas of up, down, and strange quarks confined in an MIT bag, using the rotating liquid drop model to compute ground-state properties (mass per baryon, charge-to-mass ratio, surface energy, radius) and fission barriers. They then simulate propagation through the atmosphere by treating each collision with an air nucleus (mass 14 proton masses) as either fusion followed by possible fission into two equal-mass fragments, or as a stripping interaction that removes the air nucleus mass, depending on the center-of-mass energy relative to the Coulomb barrier and the critical rotation parameter y_crit = 0.79. Energy loss from ionization is included via the Bethe-Bloch stopping power formula with electron capture corrections at low velocity.

## Key numbers

- Minimum baryon number for strangelet stability: A_crit = 27 (mass per baryon below 930 MeV)
- Ground-state mass fit at zero temperature: M_str/A = (314.6 A^{-0.532} + 875.9) MeV
- Surface energy fit: E_S/A^{2/3} = (69.0 A^{-0.466} + 77.9) MeV
- Rescaled radius fit: r_0 = R/A^{1/3} = (0.124 A^{-0.445} + 0.941) fm
- Fissionability parameter x of strangelets: 0.001 to 0.030 (much smaller than normal nuclei)
- Critical rotation parameter for vanishing fission barrier: y_crit = 0.79
- Baryon evaporation energy loss rate: dE/dt|_baryon = -6.23 × 10¹⁹ A^{2/3} T² exp(-999.9/T) MeV s⁻¹
- Meson evaporation energy loss rate: dE/dt|_meson = -1.12 × 10²⁰ A^{2/3} T² exp(-381.1/T) MeV s⁻¹
- Lower limits for ground-based detection: initial baryon number A0 ≥ 3000 or initial Lorentz factor γ0 ≥ 140
- Atmospheric depth at sea level: ~1000 g cm⁻² (specifically 1036 g cm⁻² in final mass distribution)
- Predicted flux from Madsen (2005): 1–10 per square meter year steradian

## Context

This work extends earlier phenomenological models of strangelet atmospheric propagation by Wilk et al. (1996), who assumed rapid mass decrease until disintegration, and Banerjee et al. (2000), who proposed mass pickup from atmospheric atoms. It introduces a more detailed treatment of fission using the rotating liquid drop model, building on the ground-state calculations of He et al. (1996) and the fission barrier calculations of Cohen et al. (1974). The results enable ground-based experiments (such as those at mountain altitudes or sea level) to set constraints on strangelet flux and initial parameters, complementing the flux estimates of Madsen (2005) and the AMS space-based detection of a candidate doubly-charged event reported by Co (2003).
