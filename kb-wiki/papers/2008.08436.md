# Hard-core deconfinement and soft-surface delocalization from nuclear to quark matter

> We propose a conceptual distinction between hard and soft realizations of deconfinement from nuclear to quark matter. In the high density region of Hard Deconfinement the repulsive hard cores of baryons overlap each other and bulk thermodynamics is dominated by the core properties that can be experimentally accessed in high-energy scattering experiments. We find that the equation of state estimated from a single baryon core is fairly consistent with those empirically known from neutron star phenomenology. We next discuss a novel concept of Soft Deconfinement, characterized by quantum percolation of quark wave-functions, at densities lower than the threshold for Hard Deconfinement. We make a brief review of quantum percolation in the context of nuclear and quark matter and illustrate a possible scenario of quark deconfinement at high baryon densities.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a conceptual distinction between two types of deconfinement in dense baryonic matter: "Hard Deconfinement," where overlapping repulsive hard cores of baryons dominate thermodynamics, and "Soft Deconfinement," a novel mechanism at lower densities characterized by quantum percolation of quark wave-functions rather than classical percolation of meson clouds.

## Method

The authors use a chiral soliton model with pion, rho, and omega meson fields to quantify the nucleon's hard core (radius ~0.5 fm) and soft meson cloud (radius ~1 fm). They compute the pressure and energy density distributions inside a single nucleon from this model, then rescale the results by the ratio of physical nucleon mass to model mass (0.64) to estimate the equation of state of dense quark matter under Hard Deconfinement. For Soft Deconfinement, they develop a conceptual analogy to quantum percolation on a lattice, where nucleon sites are connected by color-singlet bonds representing meson exchanges.

## Key numbers

*   The r.m.s. radius of the baryon density distribution in the nucleon core is approximately 0.49 fm, extracted from the chiral soliton model.
*   The r.m.s. radius of the isoscalar charge density (meson cloud) is approximately 1.03 fm from the same model.
*   The closest packed density for hard cores of radius ~0.5 fm is estimated as ~1.4 fm^{-3}, corresponding to ~8.3 times normal nuclear density (rho_0 ~ 0.17 fm^{-3}).
*   The rescaling factor between the model baryon mass (1460 MeV) and the physical nucleon mass (940 MeV) is chi = 0.64.
*   The empirical proton charge radius from electron-proton scattering is 0.831 +/- 0.014 fm (Xiong 2019).
*   The neutron electric form factor slope gives <r_n^2> = -0.106 +/- 0.006 fm^2 (Filin 2019).
*   The isoscalar and isovector charge radii from these data are sqrt(<r_S^2>) ≈ 0.77 fm and sqrt(<r_V^2>) ≈ 0.89 fm.
*   The radius of the baryon density distribution inferred from vector meson dominance is sqrt(<r_B^2>) ≈ 0.45 fm.
*   The scalar-isoscalar two-pion cloud radius is approximately 1.0 - 1.2 fm.

## Context

This work extends the concept of Quarkyonic Matter (McLerran 2007) from the large-Nc limit to the real world with Nc=3, proposing that Quarkyonic Matter can be identified with matter having comparable strengths of all n-body interactions. The hard-core/soft-surface delineation builds on established nucleon structure phenomenology including vector meson dominance and chiral soliton models (Meissner 1986). The Soft Deconfinement scenario introduces a novel quantum percolation mechanism for deconfinement, contrasting with classical percolation of meson clouds and suggesting a momentum-shell model of confinement (McLerran 2018, Jeong 2019). The equation of state from Hard Deconfinement shows remarkable agreement with neutron star equations of state from chiral effective theory (Hebeler 2013), the QHC18 model (Baym 2017), and other approaches, enabling a new connection between single-nucleon structure and dense matter properties.
