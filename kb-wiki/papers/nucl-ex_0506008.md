# Comment on "Quantum Opacity, the RHIC HBT Puzzle, and the Chiral Phase Transition"

> We are submitting a comment on the paper "Quantum Opacity, the RHIC HBT Puzzle, and the Chiral Phase Transition" by J.G. Cramer, G.A. Miller, J.M.S. Wu and J. Yoon, published in Phys. Rev. Lett. 94, 102302 (2005).

## Key claim

The PHOBOS Collaboration argues that the model of Cramer, Miller, Wu, and Yoon (CMWY) claiming consistency of RHIC HBT data with chiral symmetry restoration is not uniquely supported, because the model predictions for low-transverse-momentum pion yields disagree with PHOBOS data after correcting an error in the original fit; the predicted yield at low pT is highly sensitive to parameter choices and model details.

## Method

The PHOBOS Collaboration compared the CMWY model predictions for charged pion transverse momentum (pT) spectra with their own published data for central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, which reach pT as low as 30 MeV/c. They note that the original CMWY paper contained an error in representing the STAR pion momentum spectra, leading to incorrect model parameters; after this was pointed out, CMWY provided corrected predictions and an alternative fit that removed pions not participating in Bose-Einstein symmetrization. The PHOBOS data for the 15% most central collisions were divided by 2 to represent (pi+ + pi-)/2 yields, and a scaling factor of 15-20% was estimated to account for the difference between 15% and 5% centrality selection.

## Key numbers

- PHOBOS charged pion yields measured down to pT as low as 30 MeV/c for central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV.
- The most recent CMWY model predictions (solid curve) exceed the PHOBOS data by more than 30% even after accounting for differences in centrality (15% vs. 5% most central).
- Scaling factors of 15% to 20% are estimated to convert the 15% most central PHOBOS yields to the 5% most central selection used in the model predictions, based on PHENIX and PHOBOS centrality dependence measurements.

## Context

This Comment directly challenges the conclusions of Cramer, Miller, Wu, and Yoon (2005), who claimed that their model fit to HBT radii and pion spectra supported chiral symmetry restoration in the dense medium created at RHIC. The PHOBOS Collaboration demonstrates that the CMWY model predictions disagree with their unique low-pT pion data, which cover the region where the model predicted notable spectral structure. This work highlights the need for model testing across a range of centralities and shows that low-pT measurements provide critical constraints for evaluating models of the dense medium in heavy ion collisions.
