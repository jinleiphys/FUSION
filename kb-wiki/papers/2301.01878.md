# Cluster-shell competition and effect of adding hyperons

> The fundamental question is how the hyperon plays a role in the nuclear structure. It is of particular importance, especially in the light mass region, to verify the structure change when $Λ$ particle(s) is added to normal nuclei. The ground state of $^{8}$Be has been know to have a well-developed $α$--$α$ cluster structure, whereasn$^{12}$C has a mixed structure of three $α$ clusters and $jj$-coupling shell model, where $α$ clusters are partially broken. Adding $Λ$ particle(s) could induce the structure change. We compare the Be and C cases. Using the antisymmetrized quasi-cluster model (AQCM), the $α$-cluster states and $jj$-coupling shell-model states of $^8$Be and $^{12}$C are prepared on the same footing, and we add $Λ$ particles. The cluster-shell competition in the ground state can be well described with this model. Using AQCM, we calculate $^8$Be, $^{9}_Λ$Be, $^{10}_{ΛΛ}$Be, $^{12}$C, $^{13}_Λ$C, and $^{14}_{ΛΛ}$C. By adding one or two $Λ$ particle(s), the ground state of $^{12}$C approaches the $jj$-coupling shell model side. On the other hand, in the Be case, although the $Λ$ particle(s) shrinks the $α$--$α$ distance, the breaking effect of the cluster structure is rather limited. The spin-orbit interaction is the driving force of breaking the $α$ clusters, and whether the glue-like effect of $Λ$ particle(s) attracts the cluster inside the range of this interaction is crucial. In $^{14}_{ΛΛ}$C, the breaking of $α$ clusters in $^{12}$C is much enhanced by the addition of the $Λ$ particles than the case of free $^{12}$C. We also found that breaking $α$ clusters in the ground state of $^{14}_{ΛΛ}$C affects the excited state with the pure cluster structure.

## Key claim

Adding one or two Lambda hyperons to carbon-12 enhances the breaking of alpha clusters in the ground state, pushing it further toward the jj-coupling shell model side, whereas in beryllium-8 the cluster-breaking effect remains limited even with added hyperons because the alpha-alpha distance stays largely outside the range of the spin-orbit interaction.

## Method

The authors used the antisymmetrized quasi-cluster model (AQCM), which allows a smooth transformation between alpha-cluster wave functions and jj-coupling shell-model wave functions by introducing a complex Gaussian center parameter (lambda) that controls cluster breaking. They calculated the ground states of Be-8, Lambda-9-Be, LambdaLambda-10-Be, C-12, Lambda-13-C, and LambdaLambda-14-C by superposing Slater determinants with different cluster-cluster distances, lambda values, and Lambda particle positions, then diagonalizing the Hamiltonian after angular momentum projection using the generator coordinate method.

## Key numbers

- The optimal alpha-alpha distance in Be-8 is about 3.5 fm, with lambda = 0 (pure clusters) giving the minimum energy
- In Lambda-9-Be, the optimal alpha-alpha distance shrinks to about 3 fm, where lambda = 0 and lambda = 0.1 give nearly degenerate energies
- In LambdaLambda-10-Be, the optimal distance is less than 3 fm, with lambda = 0.1 slightly lower than lambda = 0
- The three-alpha threshold energy in the calculation is -82.7 MeV (experimental: -84.9 MeV)
- In C-12, the energy difference between the Brink model (no breaking) and AQCM is about 3.3 MeV
- In LambdaLambda-14-C, the energy difference between Brink and AQCM increases to about 5.2 MeV
- The expectation value of the one-body spin-orbit operator is 1.55 for C-12, 1.86 for Lambda-13-C, and 2.05 for LambdaLambda-14-C
- The binding energy of two Lambdas in LambdaLambda-10-Be is 15.23 MeV (compared to the experimental value of 14.7 +/- 0.4 MeV from Dalitz (1989))
- The binding energy of one Lambda in Lambda-13-C is 11.88 MeV (compared to 11.69 MeV from PhysRevC.83.044323)
- In LambdaLambda-14-C, the pure three-alpha cluster excited state appears at about 15 MeV excitation energy with an alpha-alpha distance of about 2.5 fm

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Motoba (1983) on the shrinkage of the alpha-alpha distance in Lambda-9-Be and by Hiyama (1997) on the state-dependent dynamical change in Lambda-13-C, but goes beyond those by explicitly including alpha-cluster breaking via the spin-orbit interaction using AQCM. The results enable future studies of how hyperon addition affects excited cluster states, particularly the Hoyle-state analogue in hypernuclei, and demonstrate that the "shrink effect" known in C-12 is much enhanced in LambdaLambda-14-C.
