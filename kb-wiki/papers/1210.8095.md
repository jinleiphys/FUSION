# Double handbag description of proton-antiproton annihilation into a heavy meson pair

> We propose to describe the process $ p \bar{p} \,\to\, \bar{D^0} D^0$ in a perturbative QCD motivated framework where a double-handbag hard process $ u d \bar u \bar d \to \bar{c} c$ factorizes from transition distribution amplitudes, which are quasi forward hadronic matrix elements of $Ψ_q Ψ_q Ψ_c $ operators, where q denotes light quarks and c denotes the heavy quark. We advocate that the charm-quark mass acts as the large scale allowing this factorization. We calculate this process in the simplified framework of the scalar diquark model and present the expected cross sections for the PANDA experiment at GSI-FAIR.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the process $p\bar{p} \to \overline{D^0} D^0$ can be described by a double-handbag mechanism where a hard partonic subprocess $S[ud] \overline{S[ud]} \to \bar{c}c$ factorizes from nucleon-to-charmed-meson transition distribution amplitudes (TDAs), using the charm-quark mass as the large scale; however, they explicitly state they have no proof of the validity of this approximation and take it as an assumption to be confronted with experimental data.

## Method

The authors model the proton as a composite of a scalar $S[ud]$ diquark and a u-quark, and describe the annihilation via a double-handbag mechanism where the diquark and antidiquark from the proton and antiproton annihilate into a gluon that produces a $\bar{c}c$ pair, which then recombine with spectator quarks to form the $\overline{D^0}$ and $D^0$ mesons. They derive an overlap representation for the nucleon-to-charmed-meson transition matrix elements using light-cone wave functions for the valence Fock components, and calculate the hard scattering kernel perturbatively using the scalar diquark model. The hadronic amplitude is expressed as a convolution of the hard subprocess amplitude with transition distribution amplitudes integrated over longitudinal momentum fractions $\bar{x}_1$ and $\bar{x}_2$ from the skewness parameter $\xi$ to 1.

## Key numbers

- The charm-quark mass is taken as $m_c = 1.275 \pm 0.025$ GeV (from the Particle Data Group), giving the partonic threshold $\hat{s} \geq 4 m_c^2 \approx 6.5$ GeV$^2$.
- The peak position of the $p \to \overline{D^0}$ transition matrix element occurs at momentum fraction $x_0 = m_c / M = 0.68$, where $M$ is the $D^0$ mass.
- The Mandelstam variable $s$ must satisfy $s > 4 M^2$ to produce a $\overline{D^0} D^0$ pair.

## Context

This work extends the collinear factorization framework previously applied to $p\bar{p} \to \Lambda_c \bar{\Lambda}_c$ by Goritschnig, Pire, and Schweiger (2009) to the case of heavy meson pair production, and builds on the definition of transition distribution amplitudes (TDAs) developed for backward deeply virtual Compton scattering by Pire et al. (2004) and Lansberg et al. (2012). Unlike the $N \to \pi$ TDAs discussed by Pobylitsa, Polyakov, and Strikman, which have a soft meson limit for normalization, the $N \to \overline{D^0}$ TDAs require an overlap representation in the spirit of Diehl (2000). The predicted cross sections are intended for comparison with future measurements at the PANDA experiment at GSI-FAIR.
