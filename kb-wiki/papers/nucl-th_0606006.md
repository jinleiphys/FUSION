# The Three-Nucleon System at Next-To-Next-To-Leading Order

> We calculate higher order corrections for the three-nucleon system up to next-to-next-to-leading within an effective field theory with contact interactions alone. We employ a subtraction formalism previously developed and for which it has been shown that no new three-body force counterterm is needed for complete renormalization up to this order. We give results for the neutron-deuteron phaseshifts and the triton binding energy. Our results are in very good agreement with experimental results and calculations using realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials.

# The Three-Nucleon System at Next-To-Next-To-Leading Order

## Key claim
Higher-order corrections for the three-nucleon system up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) can be calculated within an effective field theory with contact interactions alone using a subtraction formalism, and no new three-body force counterterm is needed for complete renormalization at this order, contrary to findings by Bedaque et al. (2002) and Barford and Birse (2004) who required an additional energy-dependent three-body counterterm when using an explicit three-body force with cutoff kept at approximately 1/R.

## Method
The author employed an effective field theory with contact interactions only, using an auxiliary field formalism to describe the neutron-deuteron system in the doublet channel. The two-body propagator was expanded to a given order in R/a and used within a set of coupled integral equations for nucleon-deuteron scattering, which were renormalized by subtracting the known threshold amplitude at the deuteron binding energy. The neutron-deuteron scattering length from Huffman et al. (2005) was used as the three-body input parameter.

## Key numbers
- Triton binding energy at NNLO: 8.48 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 8.54 MeV (using neutron-deuteron scattering length a3 = 0.645 fm as input)
- Neutron-deuteron scattering length used as three-body input: a3 = 0.645 ± 0.005 fm (from Huffman et al., 2005)
- Two-body parameters: γt^(-1) = 4.317 fm, rt = 1.764 fm, γs^(-1) = -25.04 fm, rs = 2.73 fm
- Expected error at NNLO: approximately (rt γt)^3 ~ 3%
- Triton binding energy at LO: 8.08 MeV; at NLO: 8.19 MeV

## Context
This work extends the effective field theory treatment of the three-nucleon system beyond leading order (Bedaque et al., 1999) and next-to-leading order (Hammer and Mehen, 2001; Afnan and Phillips, 2003) to NNLO. It contradicts the findings of Bedaque et al. (2002) and Barford and Birse (2004) who concluded an additional energy-dependent three-body counterterm was needed at NNLO. The results enable predictions for the Phillips line (the correlation between nucleon-deuteron scattering length and triton binding energy) up to NNLO, and the author notes that at N3LO an additional three-body input will be needed for renormalization.
