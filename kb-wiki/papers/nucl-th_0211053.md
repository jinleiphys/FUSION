# Estimate of Contribution from P-D Mixing in Atomic PNC

> We investigate possible contributions to future atomic parity nonconservation (PNC) experiments from parity admixtures between single-electron atomic states with total angular momentum $j = 3/2$. We develop new formalism for studying these admixtures between atomic $p$ and $d$ states, which enter only when finite nuclear size effects are considered and have been neglected in the literature to date. We use analytic approximations to provide an order-of-magnitude estimate of the contribution from these admixtures, and identify a dimensionless ratio which sets the scale of the correction. Using realistic numerical wavefunctions in \baplus we confirm the results of our analytic expressions, and conclude quantitatively that these novel admixtures are likely to be negligible in essentially all cases.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether parity admixtures between atomic \(p\) and \(d\) states with total angular momentum \(j = 3/2\) could contribute to future atomic parity nonconservation (PNC) experiments, and concludes that these contributions are negligible in essentially all cases, with the correction factor \(\mathcal{R}_{\mathrm{p-d}}\) estimated to be on the order of \(10^{-6}\) for the specific system studied.

## Method

The authors developed a new formalism to study parity admixtures between \(P_{3/2}\) and \(D_{3/2}\) states, which arise only when finite nuclear size effects are considered. They used analytic approximations based on power series expansions of Dirac radial wavefunctions near the nucleus to derive a dimensionless ratio \(\mathcal{R}_{\mathrm{p-d}}\) that sets the scale of the correction. They then confirmed their analytic estimates using realistic numerical Dirac-Hartree-Fock wavefunctions for the \(\mathrm{Ba}^{+}\) ion, performing the relevant transition radial integrals numerically.

## Key numbers

- For \(\mathrm{Ba}^{+}\) (Z=56), the dimensionless correction factor \(\mathcal{R}_{\mathrm{p-d}} \approx 4 \times 10^{-6}\) from the full numerical calculation.
- The analytic hydrogenic estimate gave \(\mathcal{R}_{\mathrm{p-d}} \sim 1 \times 10^{-6}\) for the same system.
- The nonrelativistic hydrogenic estimate of the ratio of PNC radial integrals gave \(1 \cdot 10^{-6}\), while the estimate from fitting numerical wavefunctions gave \(0.6 \cdot 10^{-6}\).
- The nuclear root-mean-square radius used for \(\mathrm{Ba}^{+}\) was \(\langle r^{2} \rangle_{\mathrm{N}}^{1/2} = 4.84~\mathrm{fm}\).

## Context

This work extends the formalism of finite nuclear size corrections to atomic PNC, previously developed for \(s-p\) mixing by Fortson, Pollock, and others (1990s), to the case of \(p-d\) mixing which had been neglected in the literature. The paper specifically addresses proposed experiments involving \(j = 3/2\) final states, such as the \(\mathrm{Ba}^{+}\) measurement by Fortson (2000s) and Ytterbium experiments by DeMille (2000s). The result enables future atomic PNC experiments to safely neglect \(p-d\) mixing contributions, as they are well below other sub-\(1\%\) effects such as three-vector nucleon currents and radiative corrections.
