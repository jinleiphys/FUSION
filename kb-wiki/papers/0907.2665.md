# Chiral effective field theory on the light front

> We propose a new approach to describe baryonic structure in terms of an effective chiral Lagrangian. The state vector of a baryon is defined on the light front of general position ω. x=0, where ωis an arbitrary light-like four vector. It is then decomposed in Fock components including an increasing number of pions. The maximal number of particles in the state vector is mapped out to the order of decomposition of the chiral effective Lagrangian to have a consistent calculation of both the state vector and the effective Lagrangian. An adequate Fock sector dependent renormalization scheme is used in order to restrict all contributions within the truncated Fock space. To illustrate our formalism, we calculate the anomalous magnetic moment of a fermion in the Yukawa model in the three-body truncation. We present perspectives opened by the use of a new regularization scheme based on the properties of fields as distributions acting on specific test functions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new framework, Light-Front Chiral Effective Field Theory (LFχEFT), to describe baryon structure by combining light-front dynamics with chiral perturbation theory, but the approach is explicitly limited to an expansion in the number of pions in flight at a given light-front time rather than a momentum expansion, and the illustrative calculation is performed only for the Yukawa model (not full QCD) in the three-body truncation.

## Method

The state vector of a baryon is defined on a light-front plane of general orientation ω·x=0 and decomposed into Fock components with an increasing number of pions. The maximal number of particles in the state vector is mapped to the order of the chiral effective Lagrangian, so that for an N-body truncation (one fermion plus N-1 pions) the Lagrangian includes up to 2(N-1) pion fields. A Fock-sector-dependent renormalization scheme is employed, where mass counterterms and bare coupling constants depend on the number of particles in flight, and the system of equations for vertex functions is solved successively for N=1,2,3 approximations.

## Key numbers

* Anomalous magnetic moment of a fermion in the Yukawa model calculated for the N=3 Fock space truncation, with fermion and boson masses set to 1 GeV, shown as a function of the Pauli-Villars boson mass.
* For coupling constant α=0.2, the calculation shows convergence as the Pauli-Villars mass increases; for α=0.5, the results indicate higher-order Fock components may become sizeable.
* The mapping rule: the effective Lagrangian for N-body truncation is given by L_eff^N = L_CPT^{p=2(N-1)}, where p is the chiral order.

## Context

This work extends the heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory approach of Manohar (1990s) and the infrared regularization scheme of Becher and Leutwyler (1999) by providing a framework that does not require an infinite nucleon mass or a momentum expansion. It also addresses the need to incorporate pion-nucleon resonances like the Δ and Roper resonances without adding them by hand, as was done in prior work by Hemmert et al. (1998). The formalism enables systematic non-perturbative calculations of baryon properties and opens the possibility of using a new regularization scheme based on field distributions acting on test functions, as proposed by Oberg and Pervin (2008) and Gasser and Wanders (2009).
