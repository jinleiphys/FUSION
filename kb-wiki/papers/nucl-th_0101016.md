# Collisional Damping of Giant Monopole and Quadrupole Resonances

> Collisional damping widths of giant monopole and quadrupole excitations for $^{120}$Sn and $^{208}$Pb at zero and finite temperatures are calculated within Thomas-Fermi approximation by employing the microscopic in-medium cross-sections of Li and Machleidt and the phenomenological Skyrme and Gogny forces, and are compared with each other. The results for the collisional widths of giant monopole and quadrupole vibrations at zero temperature as a function of the mass number show that the collisional damping of giant monopole vibrations accounts for about 30-40% of the observed widths at zero temperature, while for giant quadrupole vibrations it accounts for only 20-30% of the observed widths of zero temperature.

# Collisional Damping of Giant Monopole and Quadrupole Resonances

## Key claim

The collisional damping of giant monopole vibrations accounts for about 30-40% of the observed widths at zero temperature, while for giant quadrupole vibrations it accounts for only 20-30% of the observed widths at zero temperature, based on calculations using microscopic in-medium cross-sections of Li and Machleidt. These results are conditional on the use of effective nucleon mass; calculations with bare nucleon mass produce considerably larger damping widths that deviate strongly from the microscopic results.

## Method

The authors calculated collisional damping widths of isoscalar giant monopole and quadrupole resonances in 120Sn and 208Pb at zero and finite temperatures within the Thomas-Fermi approximation, using a semi-classical non-Markovian transport approach. The collisional widths were evaluated by integrating over phase-space the two-body transition rates expressed in terms of in-medium scattering cross-sections, comparing three different inputs: the microscopic in-medium cross-sections of Li and Machleidt, and the phenomenological Skyrme (SkM*) and Gogny forces. The distortion factors for the momentum distribution were obtained from a fluidynamical model using Bessel function parameterizations for the velocity field, and additionally from a scaling approximation for quadrupole vibrations.

## Key numbers

- Collisional damping of giant monopole vibrations accounts for 30-40% of observed widths at zero temperature
- Collisional damping of giant quadrupole vibrations accounts for 20-30% of observed widths at zero temperature
- The giant monopole resonance energy formula used: ℏω = 31.2 A^(-1/3) + 20.6 A^(-1/6) MeV for A ≥ 70
- The giant quadrupole resonance energy formula used: ℏω = 64 A^(-1/3) MeV
- The microscopic in-medium proton-proton cross-section parameterization: σ_pp^LM = [23.5 + 0.00256 (18.2 - E_lab^0.5)^4.0] × (1.0 + 0.1667 E_lab^1.05 ρ^3) / (1.0 + 9.704 ρ^1.2)
- The microscopic in-medium neutron-proton cross-section parameterization: σ_pn^LM = [31.5 + 0.092 |20.2 - E_lab^0.53|^2.9] × (1.0 + 0.0034 E_lab^1.51 ρ^2) / (1.0 + 21.55 ρ^1.34)
- Nuclear density parameters used: Wood-Saxon potential depth V_0 = -44 MeV, thickness a = 0.67 fm, sharp radius R_0 = 1.27 A^(1/3) fm
- Normal nuclear matter density: ρ_0 = 0.18 fm^(-3)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of collisional damping of giant dipole excitations (Yilmaz, Gokalp, Yildirim, Ayik, 2000) to giant monopole and quadrupole resonances. It contrasts with earlier estimates that used either free nucleon-nucleon cross-sections or effective Skyrme forces (Belkacem, Ayik, Bonasera, 1995; Ayik, Yilmaz, Gokalp, Schuck, 1998), which the authors argue provide poor approximations because Skyrme forces do not match free cross-sections near the nuclear surface. The results enable improved understanding of the relative importance of collisional damping versus coherent coupling to low-lying surface modes (Bortignon, Broglia, Bertsch, Pacheco, 1986) in the total spreading width of giant resonances at zero and finite temperatures.
