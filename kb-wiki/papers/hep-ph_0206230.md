# Dependence of hadron spectra on decoupling temperature and resonance contributions

> Using equilibrium hydrodynamics with initial conditions for the energy and net baryon number densities from the perturbative QCD + saturation model, a good simultaneous description of the measured pion, kaon and (anti)proton spectra in central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt s=130 A$GeV is found with a single decoupling temperature $\Tdec=150...160$ MeV. The interplay between the resonance content of the EoS and the development of the transverse flow leads to inverse slopes and $<p_T>$ of hadrons which increase with decreasing $\Tdec$. The origin of this result is discussed.

## Key claim

Using equilibrium hydrodynamics with initial conditions from the perturbative QCD + saturation model, a single decoupling temperature of 150–160 MeV simultaneously describes the measured pion, kaon, and (anti)proton spectra in central Au+Au collisions at √s = 130 A GeV, but the agreement is limited to the shape and normalization of the spectra without a χ² fit, and the average transverse momenta for pions and kaons at this temperature are about 10% larger than the measured values, slightly outside experimental error bars.

## Method

The authors used boost-invariant hydrodynamics with initial energy and net baryon number densities from the pQCD + saturation model (EKRT framework), with a K-factor of 2.3 fixed from next-to-leading order minijet calculations. The equation of state (EoS) was a hadron resonance gas (HRG) with hadrons up to mass 2 GeV and a repulsive mean field, transitioning to a quark-gluon plasma (QGP) with a bag constant at Tc = 165 MeV. Thermal spectra of final stable hadrons were computed via the Cooper-Frye prescription, including all strong and electromagnetic two- and three-body resonance decays with known branching ratios from the Particle Data Group.

## Key numbers

- Initial transverse energy ET = 1750 GeV and net baryon number NB = 16.7 in |y| ≤ 0.5 for central Au+Au at √s = 130 A GeV with a 5% centrality cut (effective Aeff = 181, number of participants = 346).
- Saturation scale psat = 1.06 GeV, corresponding to thermalization time τ0 = 0.19 fm/c.
- For Tdec = 160 MeV: π⁺ multiplicity dN/dy = 258, average transverse momentum ⟨pT⟩ = 0.44 GeV; K⁺ multiplicity = 52.1, ⟨pT⟩ = 0.61 GeV; proton multiplicity = 24.5, ⟨pT⟩ = 0.80 GeV; antiproton-to-proton ratio = 0.69.
- For Tdec = 150 MeV: π⁺ multiplicity = 259, ⟨pT⟩ = 0.46 GeV; K⁺ multiplicity = 51.2, ⟨pT⟩ = 0.65 GeV; proton multiplicity = 20.2, ⟨pT⟩ = 0.86 GeV; antiproton-to-proton ratio = 0.65.
- For Tdec = 120 MeV: π⁺ multiplicity = 265, ⟨pT⟩ = 0.53 GeV; K⁺ multiplicity = 40.7, ⟨pT⟩ = 0.78 GeV; proton multiplicity = 9.79, ⟨pT⟩ = 1.08 GeV; antiproton-to-proton ratio = 0.34.
- Thermal fraction F of pions at Tdec = 160 MeV is 0.34 (66% from resonance decays); at Tdec = 120 MeV, F = 0.71.
- dET/dη at Tdec = 150–160 MeV agrees within 8% with the PHENIX result.
- At Tdec = 150 MeV, the calculated antiproton-to-proton ratio (0.65) and Λ-bar/Λ ratio (0.70) are consistent with PHENIX results (0.75 ± 0.09 for Λ-bar/Λ).

## Context

This work extends the pQCD + saturation model of Eskola et al. (2000, 2001) by adding a hydrodynamic description of transverse expansion to compute hadron spectra, and it confirms the higher kinetic freeze-out temperature (150–160 MeV) previously suggested by Florkowski (2002) using a fireball parametrization. The results contradict the common assumption that kinetic freeze-out occurs at about 120 MeV (as used at AGS and SPS energies by Sollfrank et al., 1997) and show that the resonance content of the EoS, rather than the phase transition, is the dominant cause of the increase in spectral slopes with decreasing decoupling temperature. This enables future studies to use a single decoupling temperature for both chemical and kinetic freeze-out in hydrodynamic models of RHIC collisions.
