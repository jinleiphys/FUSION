# Forward trijet production in proton-nucleus collisions

> Using the formalism of the light-cone wave function in perturbative QCD together with the hybrid factorization, we compute the cross-section for three particle production at forward rapidities in proton-nucleus collisions. We focus on the quark channel, in which the three produced partons -- a quark accompanied by a gluon pair, or two quarks plus one antiquark -- are all generated via two successive splittings starting with a quark that was originally collinear with the proton. The three partons are put on-shell by their scattering off the nuclear target, described as a Lorentz-contracted "shockwave". The three-parton component of the quark light-cone wave function that we compute on this occasion is also an ingredient for other interesting calculations, like the next-to-leading order correction to the cross-section for the production of a pair of jets.

## Key claim

The paper computes the leading-order (tree-level) cross-section for three-parton production (trijet) in the quark channel for forward proton-nucleus collisions, but the authors explicitly state that they "shall not attempt to explore the physical consequences" of their results, noting that the final expressions remain "cumbersome" and require further simplifications (e.g., large-\(N_c\) limit) for phenomenological applications.

## Method

Using light-cone perturbation theory and the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective theory with hybrid factorization, the authors construct the three-parton Fock state components of the quark light-cone wave function via two successive parton splittings (either \(q \to qgg\) or \(q \to qq\bar q\)). The scattering off the nuclear target is treated eikonally as a shockwave, with partons acquiring Wilson lines in the appropriate color representation, and the cross-section is obtained by averaging over target color field configurations.

## Key numbers

- The calculation is performed at leading order (tree-level) in the strong coupling \(g\), with the quark channel initiated by a valence quark carrying a large longitudinal momentum fraction \(x_p \sim \mathcal{O}(1)\).
- The target saturation momentum \(Q_s(x_g)\) sets the scale for transverse momentum broadening, where \(x_g\) is the longitudinal momentum fraction of the target gluon.
- The cross-section for single inclusive quark production (transverse momentum broadening) is given by \(\frac{d\sigma_{\text{LO}}^{qA \to q+X}}{dp^+ d^2\mathbf{p}} = \delta(p^+ - q^+) \frac{1}{(2\pi)^2} \int d^2\mathbf{x} d^2\mathbf{y} e^{i(\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{y})\cdot\mathbf{p}} \frac{1}{N_c} \langle \text{tr}(V^\dagger(\mathbf{y}) V(\mathbf{x})) \rangle\), where \(V\) is a fundamental-representation Wilson line.

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of dijet production at forward rapidities in proton-nucleus collisions (Marquet 2007, Dominguez 2011, Iancu 2013) and three-particle final states in deep inelastic scattering (Ayala 2016, Boussarie 2016) or with a photon (Altinoluk 2018). The three-parton component of the quark light-cone wave function computed here is a necessary ingredient for the next-to-leading order (NLO) correction to dijet production, which the authors identify as the primary motivation for the calculation.
