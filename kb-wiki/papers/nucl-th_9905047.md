# (j,0)+(0,j) Covariant spinors and causal propagators based on Weinberg formalism

> A pragmatic approach to constructing a covariant phenomenology of the interactions of composite, high-spin hadrons is proposed. Because there are no known wave equations without significant problems, we propose to construct the phenomenology without explicit reference to a wave equation. This is done by constructing the individual pieces of a perturbation theory and then utilizing the perturbation theory as the definition of the phenomenology. The covariant spinors for a particle of spin $j$ are constructed directly from Lorentz invariance and the basic precepts of quantum mechanics following the logic put forth originally by Wigner and developed by Weinberg. Explicit expressions for the spinors are derived for j=1, 3/2 and 2. Field operators are constructed from the spinors and the free-particle propagator is derived from the vacuum expectation value of the time-order product of the field operators. A few simple examples of model interactions are given. This provides all the necessary ingredients to treat at a phenomenological level and in a covariant manner particles of arbitrary spin.

## Key claim
The paper proposes constructing a covariant phenomenology for composite, high-spin hadrons without relying on a wave equation, instead building perturbation theory directly from Lorentz invariance and quantum mechanics following the Wigner-Weinberg approach. The authors acknowledge that "there are no known wave equations without significant problems" for such particles, making this a pragmatic alternative rather than a fundamental solution.

## Method
The authors construct covariant spinors for spin-j particles in the (j,0)+(0,j) representation directly from Lorentz invariance and quantum mechanical precepts, following the logic of Wigner as developed by Weinberg. Explicit spinor expressions are derived for j=1, 3/2, and 2, from which field operators are constructed. The free-particle propagator is then obtained from the vacuum expectation value of the time-ordered product of these field operators, and several simple model interactions are presented to illustrate the formalism.

## Context
This work extends the Wigner (1939) classification of particles by irreducible representations of the Poincare group and Weinberg's (1964) field operator construction to provide a practical tool for high-spin hadron phenomenology. It addresses the long-standing problem that wave equations for higher-spin particles (e.g., the Rarita-Schwinger equation for spin-3/2) suffer from well-known difficulties such as acausal propagation and auxiliary field constraints, as discussed by Johnson and Sudarshan (1961) and others. The formalism enables covariant calculations of interactions involving composite hadrons with spins up to at least j=2, with potential applications to meson-nucleon scattering and heavy-ion collision phenomenology.
