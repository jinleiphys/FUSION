# Incoherent solar-neutrino scattering off the stable Tl-isotopes

> Nuclear-structure calculations for the description of low-energy neutral-current neutrino scattering off the stable $^{203,205}$Tl isotopes are performed in the context of the nuclear shell model using the model space jj56pn. Cross-section and event-rate calculations focusing on inelastic solar-neutrino scattering off $^{203,205}$Tl are performed. The individual contributions of the various nuclear responses are presented and discussed, and the results are also illustrated in terms of the nuclear recoil energy. Analytical expressions entering the cross sections are given in order to achieve a direct connection with experimental observables.

## Key claim

The paper presents nuclear shell-model calculations for inelastic neutral-current solar-neutrino scattering off stable thallium isotopes, finding that coherent elastic scattering dominates over inelastic channels by up to four orders of magnitude, and that the axial-vector contribution dominates the inelastic cross section, with the vector contribution suppressed by three to four orders of magnitude at low neutrino energies.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale nuclear shell-model calculations using the NuShellX@MSU code with the khhe interaction within the jj56pn model space, computing transition matrix elements between the ground state and excited final states of 203Tl and 205Tl. They calculated cross sections and event rates using the Donnelly-Walecka formalism, including both Coulomb-longitudinal and transverse multipole contributions, and derived analytical expressions for the differential cross section in terms of nuclear recoil energy to connect with experimental observables.

## Key numbers

- For 203Tl at E_nu = 1.0 MeV, total inelastic cross section = 3.109 x 10^(-50) cm^2; for 205Tl at same energy, total = 6.941 x 10^(-50) cm^2
- For 203Tl at E_nu = 10.0 MeV, total inelastic cross section = 5.525 x 10^(-43) cm^2; for 205Tl at same energy, total = 4.396 x 10^(-43) cm^2
- The J=1+ transitions dominate the inelastic cross section for neutrino energies up to approximately 43 MeV, after which J=2+ transitions become dominant
- CEνNS cross section exceeds the inelastic cross section by up to four orders of magnitude across the energy range studied
- For 205Tl, 1393 final states were included in the model space; for 203Tl, the first 1000 most contributing states were selected from nearly 100000 total states
- The computed excitation spectra cover final states up to about 11 MeV for 205Tl and about 19 MeV for 203Tl

## Context

This work extends previous studies of inelastic neutral-current neutrino-nucleus scattering, which have been limited compared to coherent elastic scattering studies following the COHERENT Collaboration's first observation of CEνNS in 2017. The authors build on prior shell-model and quasiparticle random-phase approximation calculations by Chasioti (2009), Tsakstara (2011), and Divari (2012), as well as microscopic quasiparticle-phonon model work by Ydrefors (2011, 2012) and Hellgren (2022). The results are directly relevant to experiments using thallium-doped detectors such as COHERENT, COSINE, DAMA/LIBRA, PICO-LON, ANAIS, and SABRE, and the new recoil-energy formalism enables direct comparison with experimental detector thresholds.
