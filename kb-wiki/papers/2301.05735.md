# Classical Entanglement and Entropy

> Motivated by recent discussions of entanglement in the context of high energy scattering, we consider the relation between the entanglement entropy of a highly excited state of a quantum system and the classical entanglement entropy of the corresponding classical system. We show on the example of two weakly coupled harmonic oscillators, that the two entropies are equal. Quantum mechanically, the reduced density matrix which yields this entropy is close to the maximally entangled state. We thus observe that the nature of entanglement in this type of state is purely classical.

## Key claim

The entanglement entropy of a highly excited quantum state of two weakly coupled harmonic oscillators equals the classical entanglement entropy of the corresponding classical system, showing that the entanglement in this regime is purely classical rather than a genuine quantum effect.

## Method

The authors consider two classical coupled harmonic oscillators with very different frequencies (Ω ≫ ω) and weak coupling (C ≪ ω²). They calculate the classical entanglement entropy by integrating out the fast oscillator (y) to obtain the reduced probability distribution for the slow oscillator (x, p_x), then computing the Boltzmann entropy with a minimal phase space volume Δ = h/2. For the quantum system, they use WKB wave functions for highly excited states (E₁ ≫ ℏΩ, E₂ ≫ ℏΩ) and compute the von Neumann entropy of the reduced density matrix obtained by tracing over the fast oscillator.

## Key numbers

- The classical entanglement entropy is S_CE = ln[(πC/Ω) × √(E₁E₂)/(ℏωΩ)], where E₁ and E₂ are the energies of the two oscillators, C is the coupling, ω and Ω are the frequencies, and ℏ is the reduced Planck constant.
- The quantum entanglement entropy for highly excited states is S_E = ln[(πC/Ω) × √(E₁E₂)/(ℏωΩ)], identical to the classical result.
- The condition for positivity of the classical entropy requires that the interaction energy is much larger than ℏΩ, specifically C√(E₁E₂)/(Ωω) ≫ ℏΩ.
- The reduced density matrix describes a mixed ensemble where occupation numbers are spread over a range Δn = √(4E₁E₂C²)/(ℏ²ω²Ω⁴), with probability nearly independent of occupation number within this range.

## Context

This work is motivated by discussions of entanglement in high energy scattering, particularly the eigenstate thermalization hypothesis (Deutsch, 2018) and the proposal by Kharzeev (2017) that entanglement entropy in deep inelastic scattering reflects itself as Boltzmann entropy of produced particles. The authors previously questioned the validity of this approach (Duan, 2020; Duan, 2021). The result shows that in the regime relevant for high energy scattering (close to saturation, where the Color Glass Condensate description applies), entanglement is of classical origin rather than genuine quantum EPR-type entanglement. This enables a clearer understanding of when classical descriptions like hydrodynamics can capture quantum entanglement effects.
