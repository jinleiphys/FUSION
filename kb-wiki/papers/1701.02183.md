# Observables for longitudinal flow correlations in heavy-ion collisions

> We propose several new observables/correlators, based on correlations between two or more subevents separated in pseudorapidity $η$, to study the longitudinal flow fluctuations. We show that these observables are sensitive to the event-by-event fluctuations, as a function of $η$, of the initial condition as well as the non-linear mode-mixing effects. Experimental measurement of these observables shall provide important new constraints on the boost-variant event-by-event initial conditions required by all 3+1-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics models.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a set of new observables based on correlations between subevents separated in pseudorapidity to study longitudinal flow fluctuations, but the abstract glosses over the fact that the proposed observables are designed to separate the contributions from flow magnitude asymmetry and event-plane twist, which the previous CMS observable (Khachatryan 2015) could not do.

## Method

The authors define correlators using the scalar product of the k-th moment of flow vectors in two or more pseudorapidity intervals, extending the CMS observable r_{n|n} to higher moments (k>1) and to correlations between different harmonic orders. They introduce a four-subevent correlator R_{n,n|n,n} that isolates the event-plane twist contribution by canceling the flow magnitude dependence. The expected signal strength is estimated using a simple Glauber model that includes forward-backward asymmetry and eta-dependent participant-plane twist.

## Key numbers

- The slope parameter F_{n;k}^r in the linear approximation r_{n|n;k}(eta) = 1 - 2k F_{n;k}^r eta is defined, with contributions from asymmetry (F_{n;k}^asy) and twist (F_{n;k}^twi).
- The four-subevent correlator R_{n,n|n,n} is approximated as R = 1 - 4 F_{n;2}^twi eta, giving F_n^R = 2 F_{n;2}^twi.
- The asymmetry and twist components can be separated via F_{n;2}^twi = F_n^R/2 and F_{n;2}^asy = F_{n;2}^r - F_n^R/2.
- Glauber model estimates for E_{n;k} = <k epsilon_{n-}^2 epsilon_{n+}^{2k-2}> / <epsilon_{n+}^{2k}> are shown for n=2 and n=3 for k=1, 2, and 3 in Pb+Pb collisions.
- The ratio F_{n;2}^s,twi / F_{n;2}^s,asy from the Glauber model is shown for n=2 and n=3 as a function of centrality.

## Context

This work extends the CMS measurement of longitudinal flow decorrelations (Khachatryan 2015) by proposing observables that separate flow magnitude fluctuations from event-plane twist effects, which the CMS observable could not distinguish. It also extends the study to correlations between different harmonic orders (e.g., v2 with v4, v2 with v3 with v5) to investigate how non-linear mode-mixing effects evolve with pseudorapidity, building on prior work on mode-mixing in the transverse plane (Gardim 2011, Teaney 2012, Aad 2014, Aad 2015). The proposed observables are intended to provide constraints for 3+1-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic models (Bozek 2015, Pang 2015, Ma 2016, Schenke 2016, Ke 2016).
