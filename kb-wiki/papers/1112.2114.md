# Holographic Mean-Field Theory for Baryon Many-Body Systems

> We propose a mean-field approach to analyze many-body systems of fermions in the gauge/gravity duality. We introduce a non-vanishing classical fermionic field in the gravity dual, which we call the holographic mean field for fermions. The holographic mean field takes account of the many-body dynamics of the fermions in the bulk. The regularity condition of the holographic mean field fixes the relationship between the chemical potential and the density unambiguously. Our approach provides a new framework of gauge/gravity duality for finite-density systems of baryons in the confinement phase.

## Key claim
The authors propose a holographic mean-field theory for fermions that resolves two problems in the AdS/CFT correspondence for finite-density baryon systems in the confinement phase: the difficulty of incorporating dynamical baryons and the ambiguity in relating baryon chemical potential to baryon density. The approach provides an eigenvalue equation that fixes the relationship between chemical potential and density unambiguously, with the regularity condition of the fermionic mean field serving as the new bulk condition.

## Method
The authors introduce a non-vanishing classical Dirac field (the holographic mean field) in a five-dimensional dual geometry without an event horizon, coupled to a bulk U(1) gauge field. They replace the fermion field and gauge field with w-dependent mean fields, impose boundary conditions (chemical potential at the boundary, zero fermionic source), and solve the coupled equations of motion: the Gauss-law constraint for the gauge field and the Dirac equation for the fermion field. The chemical potential appears as an eigenvalue in the Dirac equation, and solving the coupled nonlinear equations yields the equation of state relating baryon density to chemical potential.

## Key numbers
- The model uses the Sakai-Sugimoto model parameters: M_KK = 949 MeV and λ N_c = 50, with N_f = 2.
- Normal nuclear matter density is taken as n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3} to scale the baryon number density.
- The boundary is located at w = w_max ≈ 3.64/M_KK.
- The chemical potential at the zero-density limit automatically agrees with the fermion mass m_f, with no nontrivial solutions for |q μ| < m_f.

## Context
This work extends the holographic approach to finite-density baryon systems in the confinement phase, where previous methods (e.g., Bergman (2007)) simply superposed baryon vertices at the same radial location without fully incorporating their dynamics. The approach builds on the approximation of baryon vertices as point-particle fermions described by a Dirac field on the dual geometry, as developed in earlier work (Hata (2007), Kim (2007)). The method enables the computation of the equation of state for baryon many-body systems and allows calculation of excitation spectra by introducing fluctuations on top of the mean fields.
