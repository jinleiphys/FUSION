# Two-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon potential:O(q^4) relativistic chiral expansion

> We present a relativistic procedure for the chiral expansion of the two-pion exchange component of the $NN$ potential, which emphasizes the role of intermediate $πN$ subamplitudes. The relationship between power counting in $πN$ and $NN$ processes is discussed and results are expressed directly in terms of observable subthreshold coefficients. Interactions are determined by one- and two-loop diagrams, involving pions, nucleons, and other degrees of freedom, frozen into empirical subthreshold coefficients. The full evaluation of these diagrams produces amplitudes containing many different loop integrals. Their simplification by means of relations among these integrals leads to a set of intermediate results. Subsequent truncation to $O(q^4)$ yields the relativistic potential, which depends on six loop integrals, representing bubble, triangle, crossed box, and box diagrams. The bubble and triangle integrals are the same as in $πN$ scattering and we have shown that they also determine the chiral structures of box and crossed box integrals. Relativistic threshold effects make our results to be not equivalent with those of the heavy baryon approach. Performing a formal expansion of our results in inverse powers of the nucleon mass, even in regions where this expansion is not valid, we recover most of the standard heavy baryon results. The main differences are due to the Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy and terms of $O(q^3)$, possibly associated with the iteration of the one-pion exchange potential.

## Key claim

The paper presents a relativistic chiral expansion of the two-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon potential to order O(q^4), showing that the results are not equivalent to those of the heavy baryon approach due to relativistic threshold effects, even though a formal expansion in inverse powers of the nucleon mass recovers most standard heavy baryon results except for the Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy and terms of O(q^3).

## Method

The authors derived the two-pion exchange potential (TPEP) using a covariant relativistic chiral perturbation theory framework, expressing the intermediate pion-nucleon amplitude in terms of empirical subthreshold coefficients. They evaluated one- and two-loop diagrams involving pions, nucleons, and other degrees of freedom frozen into these coefficients, then truncated the results to O(q^4) to obtain a relativistic potential depending on six loop integrals representing bubble, triangle, crossed box, and box diagrams.

## Key numbers

- The subthreshold coefficient d_{00}^+ has experimental value -1.46 ± 0.10 in units of m^{-n}, with medium range contribution 0.12
- The subthreshold coefficient d_{10}^+ has experimental value 1.12 ± 0.02 in units of m^{-n}
- The subthreshold coefficient d_{01}^+ has experimental value 1.14 ± 0.02 in units of m^{-n}, with medium range contribution -0.25
- The subthreshold coefficient b_{00}^- has experimental value 10.36 ± 0.10 in units of m^{-n}, with WT+mr contribution -0.99
- The Weinberg-Tomozawa contribution to d_{00}^- is 1.18 in units of m^{-n}
- The pion decay constant f_pi = 93 MeV, pion mass mu = 139.57 MeV, and nucleon mass m = 938.28 MeV are used throughout

## Context

This work extends the relativistic chiral treatment of the two-pion exchange potential previously developed by the same authors (RR94, RR97) and contrasts with the heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory calculations by Ordonez and van Kolck (OvK), Ordonez, Ray, and van Kolck (ORK), and others (KBW, Nij, EGM, K3). The paper addresses the uniqueness problem in chiral predictions for the B=2 sector, following the relativistic chiral pion-nucleon analysis by Becher and Leutwyler (BL1, BL2), and enables future comparisons between relativistic and heavy baryon approaches to nuclear forces.
