# Comprehensive description of $J/ψ$ production in proton-proton collisions at collider energies

> We employ a small $x$ Color Glass Condensate (CGC)+ Non-Relativistic QCD (NRQCD) formalism to compute $J/ψ$ production at low $p_\perp$ in proton-proton collisions at collider energies. Very good agreement is obtained for total cross-sections, rapidity distributions and low momentum $p_\perp$ distributions. Similar agreement is obtained for $ψ^\prime$ production. We observe an overlap region in $p_\perp$ where our results match smoothly to those obtained in a next-to-leading order (NLO) collinearly factorized NRQCD formalism. The relative contribution of color singlet and color octet contributions can be quantified in the CGC+NRQCD framework, with the former contributing approximately $10\%$ of the total cross-section.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a combined Color Glass Condensate (CGC) plus Non-Relativistic QCD (NRQCD) formalism can describe J/psi production at low transverse momentum in proton-proton collisions, with the caveat that the dilute-dense approximation employed breaks down at central rapidities at the LHC where both protons may have large phase space densities.

## Method

The authors compute short-distance coefficients for heavy quark pair production using the CGC effective theory, combined with universal NRQCD long-distance matrix elements (LDMEs) extracted from high-pT Tevatron data. They solve the running-coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (rcBK) equation for the dipole scattering amplitude, using McLerran-Venugopalan initial conditions fitted to HERA deep inelastic scattering data, and match the dipole amplitude to collinear parton distribution functions at x=0.01 to extend the formalism to larger x values.

## Key numbers

- Color singlet contribution is approximately 10% of the total J/psi cross-section
- The matching between CGC+NRQCD and NLO collinear factorized NRQCD occurs in an overlap region around pT ~ 5-6 GeV
- The proton transverse radius Rp is determined to be 0.48 fm from matching conditions
- The matching scale Q0 is determined to be 5.1 GeV from the same matching procedure
- Charm quark mass is set to m=1.5 GeV for J/psi (approximately half the J/psi mass)
- For psi-prime production, charm quark mass is set to m=1.84 GeV (half the psi-prime mass)
- The color octet LDMEs used are: <O^{J/psi}(1S0[8])> = 0.089 +/- 0.0098 GeV^3, <O^{J/psi}(3S1[8])> = 0.0030 +/- 0.0012 GeV^3, <O^{J/psi}(3P0[8])> = 0.0056 +/- 0.0021 GeV^3

## Context

This work extends the CGC+NRQCD framework previously developed by Kang (2013) for heavy quarkonium production, providing the first quantitative results for J/psi production in proton-proton collisions. It contrasts with the Color Singlet Model (Chang 1979, Baier 1981) which gives different predictions, and with the Color Evaporation Model in collinear factorization (Nelson 2012) which has larger uncertainties. The framework enables a unified description of quarkonium production across all phase space when combined with NLO collinear factorized NRQCD, and can be extended to proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions.
