# Electron scattering in isotonic chains as a probe of the proton shell structure of unstable nuclei

> Electron scattering on unstable nuclei is planned in future facilities of the GSI and RIKEN upgrades. Motivated by this fact, we study theoretical predictions for elastic electron scattering in the N=82, N=50, and N=14 isotonic chains from very proton-deficient to very proton-rich isotones. We compute the scattering observables by performing Dirac partial-wave calculations. The charge density of the nucleus is obtained with a covariant nuclear mean-field model that accounts for the low-energy electromagnetic structure of the nucleon. For the discussion of the dependence of scattering observables at low-momentum transfer on the gross properties of the charge density, we fit Helm model distributions to the self-consistent mean-field densities. We find that the changes shown by the electric charge form factor along each isotonic chain are strongly correlated with the underlying proton shell structure of the isotones. We conclude that elastic electron scattering experiments in isotones can provide valuable information about the filling order and occupation of the single-particle levels of protons.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the changes in the electric charge form factor along isotonic chains are strongly correlated with the underlying proton shell structure, specifically the filling order and occupation of single-particle proton levels, but this correlation is most clearly observed in the low-momentum transfer regime and is sensitive to the surface diffuseness parameter of the equivalent Helm charge density rather than to the nuclear size.

## Method

The authors performed Dirac distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) calculations of elastic electron scattering at 500 MeV beam energy for nuclei in the N=82, N=50, and N=14 isotonic chains, using charge densities from the relativistic mean-field model G2 that incorporates nucleon electromagnetic structure via vector-meson dominance. They fitted two-parameter Helm model distributions to the self-consistent mean-field charge densities by matching the first zero and second maximum of the PWBA form factor, then analyzed the behavior of the DWBA form factor at the first inflection point as a function of the Helm parameters.

## Key numbers

- Electron beam energy fixed at 500 MeV for the main calculations, with additional verification at 250 MeV showing the form factor is largely energy-independent in the low-momentum transfer domain
- For the N=82 chain, the Helm radius R0 ranges from 5.44 fm (122Zr) to 6.26 fm (154Hf), while the surface diffuseness parameter sigma^2 ranges from 0.472 fm^2 (140Ce) to 0.926 fm^2 (122Zr)
- For the N=50 chain, R0 ranges from 4.34 fm (70Ca) to 5.39 fm (100Sn), and sigma^2 ranges from 0.524 fm^2 (82Ge) to 0.847 fm^2 (70Ca)
- For the N=14 chain, R0 ranges from 2.89 fm (22O) to 3.69 fm (34Ca), and sigma^2 ranges from 0.657 fm^2 (22O) to 0.988 fm^2 (34Ca)
- The first zero of the spherical Bessel function j1(x) used in the Helm model is x=4.49341

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of elastic electron scattering along Ca and Sn isotopic chains (Roca-Maza 2008) to isotonic chains, providing complementary information about proton shell structure rather than neutron effects on the charge density. The study is motivated by planned electron scattering experiments on unstable nuclei at facilities such as SCRIT at RIKEN (Suda 2005, Wakasa 2008) and ELISe at FAIR (Simon 2007, Antonov 2011), and the N=82 and N=50 chains are relevant for understanding r-process nucleosynthesis waiting points (Grawe 2007). The results suggest that elastic electron scattering experiments on isotones can probe the filling order of proton single-particle levels, enabling future experimental tests of shell structure in exotic nuclei.
