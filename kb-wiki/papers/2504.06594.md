# Machine Learning for Extrapolating No-Core Shell Model Results to Infinite Basis

> We utilize the machine learning to extrapolate to the infinite model space the no-core shell model (NCSM) results for the energies and rms radii of the 6He ground state and 6Li lowest states. The extrapolated energies and rms radii converge as the NCSM results from larger model spaces are included in the training dataset for ensemble of artificial neural networks thus enabling an accurate predictions for these observables.

## Key claim

The paper claims that an ensemble of artificial neural networks can reliably extrapolate no-core shell model (NCSM) results for energies and rms radii of 6He and 6Li to the infinite model space, but only when the training dataset includes NCSM results from progressively larger model spaces (up to Nmax = 18) and when strict selection criteria are applied to filter trained networks, with the extrapolated values converging as larger model spaces are included.

## Method

The authors train an ensemble of 1024 fully connected feedforward neural networks (multilayer perceptrons with three hidden layers) on NCSM calculations for 6He and 6Li using the Daejeon16 nucleon-nucleon interaction, with inputs being the NCSM parameters Nmax and oscillator energy hbar-Omega and outputs being the observable of interest (energy or rms radius). They apply data preprocessing that selects only results to the right of the variational minimum in hbar-Omega, train each network for 10^6 epochs using Adam optimization with a cyclical learning rate schedule, then filter the ensemble using criteria including a soft variational principle, hbar-Omega independence at large Nmax, a convergence principle for energies, and loss-based filtering, with the final estimate taken as the median of the filtered predictions at Nmax = 300 and uncertainty given by the interquartile range.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy of 6He: -29.429 +0.007/-0.005 MeV (extrapolated), compared to experimental -29.269 MeV
- Ground state energy of 6Li: -32.036(3) MeV (extrapolated), compared to experimental -31.995 MeV
- 6Li (3+,0) state energy: -30.129 +0.004/-0.003 MeV (extrapolated), compared to experimental -29.809 MeV
- 6Li (0+,1) state energy: -28.552 +0.008/-0.005 MeV (extrapolated), compared to experimental -28.434 MeV
- The variational minimum (raw NCSM result at largest model space) for 6He ground state is -29.377 MeV, for 6Li ground state is -31.977 MeV
- Training uses NCSM results in model spaces up to Nmax = 18, with predictions made at Nmax = 300
- Selection criteria allow energy deviations of 2 keV for hbar-Omega independence and 0.002 fm for radii

## Context

This work extends the pioneering machine learning extrapolation approach of Negoita (2019) (referred to as ISU) for NCSM calculations, introducing modifications to neural network topology and stricter selection criteria for trained networks. The results are compared with the ISU approach and the alternative TUDa approach of Knoll and Wolfgruber (2023-2024), which trains universal networks on light nuclei, with benchmarking for Li isotopes reported by Knol (2025). The method enables more reliable extrapolations for p-shell nuclei where full NCSM convergence is computationally prohibitive, and was tested on a benchmark problem of the deuteron ground state using the Nijmegen II potential.
