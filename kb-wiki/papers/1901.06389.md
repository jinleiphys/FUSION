# The Jet Shape at NLL$'$

> The jet shape is the fraction of the jet energy within a cone $r$ centered on the jet axis. We calculate the jet shape distribution at next-to-leading logarithmic accuracy plus next-to-leading order (NLL$'$), accounting for logarithms of both the jet radius $R$ and the ratio $r/R$. This is the first phenomenological study that takes the recoil of the jet axis due to soft radiation into account, which is needed to reach this accuracy, but complicates the calculation of collinear radiation and requires the treatment of rapidity logarithms and non-global logarithms. We present numerical results, finding good agreement with ATLAS and CMS measurements of the jet shape in an inclusive jet sample, $pp \to {\rm jet}+X$, for different kinematic bins. The effect of the underlying event and hadronization are included using a simple one-parameter model, since they are not part of our perturbative calculation.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the jet shape distribution at next-to-leading logarithmic accuracy plus next-to-leading order (NLL$'$), which is the first phenomenological study to account for the recoil of the jet axis due to soft radiation. This accuracy is achieved only under the condition that the simple multiplicative treatment of non-global logarithms is valid, which limits the refactorized expression to NLL$'$ accuracy rather than full NLL.

## Method

The authors employ Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) to factorize the cross section for inclusive jet production, separating the physics at scales of the hard scattering ($p_T$), the jet scale ($p_T R$), and the subjet scale ($p_T r$). They refactorize the jet function in the limit $r \ll R$ into hard, collinear, and soft functions, solving renormalization group equations in both the invariant mass scale $\mu$ and the rapidity scale $\nu$ to resum logarithms of $r/R$. The collinear functions $C_d$ are calculated at one-loop order for the first time accounting for the dependence on the recoil $k_\perp$, and non-global logarithms are included at leading-logarithmic order.

## Key numbers

* The integrated jet shape for quark jets at one-loop order for $r \lesssim R$: $\psi_{q}(r) = 1 + \frac{\alpha_s C_F}{2\pi} \left[-\frac{1}{2}L_{r/R}^2 + \frac{3}{2}L_{r/R} - \frac{9}{2} + \frac{6r}{R} - \frac{3r^2}{2R^2}\right]$, where $L_{r/R} = \ln(r^2/R^2)$.
* The integrated jet shape for gluon jets at one-loop order for $r \lesssim R$: $\psi_{g}(r) = 1 + \frac{\alpha_s}{2\pi} \left[ -\frac{C_A}{2}L_{r/R}^2 + \frac{\beta_0}{2} L_{r/R} + C_A\left( -\frac{203}{36} + \frac{8r}{R} - \frac{3r^2}{R^2} + \frac{8r^3}{9R^3} - \frac{r^4}{4R^4}\right) + T_F n_f\left(\frac{41}{18} - \frac{4r}{R} + \frac{3r^2}{R^2} - \frac{16r^3}{9R^3} + \frac{r^4}{2R^4}\right)\right]$.
* The leading non-global logarithm contribution at order $\alpha_s^2$: $-\frac{\alpha_s^2 C_A C_i}{12} \ln^2(R/r)$, where $C_i = C_F$ for quarks and $C_A$ for gluons.
* The natural scales for the refactorized ingredients: $\mu_H \sim p_T R$, $\mu_C \sim p_T r$, $\mu_{S^{\rm G}} \sim p_T r$, $\nu_C \sim p_T$, $\nu_{S^{\rm G}} \sim p_T r/R$.

## Context

This work extends the framework for jet shapes developed by Kang, Ringer, and Waalewijn (2017) for subjet distributions, achieving full NLL$'$ accuracy for the first time. Earlier jet shape calculations by Seymour (1997), Li and collaborators (2011), and Chien and collaborators (2014) were formally only accurate to leading-logarithmic (LL) order in their treatment of logarithms of $r/R$, missing the rapidity evolution and non-global logarithms included here. The results enable precise comparisons with ATLAS and CMS measurements at 7 TeV, and the framework can be extended to other collider environments including heavy-ion collisions.
