# Proton number cumulants and correlation functions in Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 7.7-200$ GeV from hydrodynamics

> We present a dynamical description of (anti)proton number fluctuations cumulants and correlation functions in central Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 7.7-200$ GeV by utilizing viscous hydrodynamics simulations. The cumulants of proton and baryon number are calculated in a given momentum acceptance analytically, via an appropriately extended Cooper-Frye procedure describing particlization of an interacting hadron resonance gas. The effects of global baryon number conservation are taken into account using a generalized subensemble acceptance method. The experimental data of the STAR collaboration are consistent at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} \gtrsim 20$ GeV with simultaneous effects of global baryon number conservation and repulsive interactions in baryon sector, the latter being in line with the behavior of baryon number susceptibilities observed in lattice QCD. The data at lower collision energies show possible indications for sizable attractive interactions among baryons. The data also indicate sizable negative two-particle correlations between antiprotons that are not satisfactorily described by baryon conservation and excluded volume effects. We also discuss differences between cumulants and correlation functions (factorial cumulants) of (anti)proton number distribution, proton versus baryon number fluctuations, and effects of hadronic afterburner.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that experimental data from the STAR Collaboration for net-proton cumulant ratios at collision energies above 20 GeV are consistent with a combination of global baryon number conservation and repulsive baryon-baryon interactions modeled by excluded volume effects, while data at lower energies show possible indications for attractive interactions among baryons that are not captured by the current model.

## Method

The authors performed (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic simulations using the MUSIC code with event-averaged initial conditions for 0-5% central Au-Au collisions at seven beam energies from 7.7 to 200 GeV. Cumulants of (anti)proton number distributions were calculated analytically from the Cooper-Frye particlization hypersurface using an extended procedure that incorporates an interacting hadron resonance gas with excluded volume effects, and global baryon number conservation was imposed via the subensemble acceptance method 2.0 (SAM-2.0).

## Key numbers

- Collision energies studied: 7.7, 14.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV
- Switching energy density for particlization: epsilon_sw = 0.26 GeV/fm^3, corresponding to T_sw = 150.6 MeV at mu_B = 0
- Momentum acceptance for cumulant calculations: |y| < 0.5 and 0.4 < p_T < 2.0 GeV/c
- Net-proton hyperkurtosis kappa_6/kappa_2 is predicted to become negative at collision energies below approximately 40-60 GeV
- The proton-to-baryon ratio q(x_i) is approximately 1/2 in most hypersurface elements

## Context

This work extends the subensemble sampler method previously applied at LHC energies (Vovchenko, 2020) to RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies by relaxing the assumption of boost invariance and using realistic (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic hypersurfaces. The results are compared with STAR Collaboration measurements (Adam, 2020; Abdallah, 2021) and with a non-critical baseline from an ideal HRG model (Braun-Munzinger, 2020). The approach enables quantitative predictions for higher-order cumulants such as kappa_6/kappa_2 that can be tested by future high-statistics RHIC BES-II measurements.
