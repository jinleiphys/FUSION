# Evidence for Nonlinear Gluon Effects in QCD and their $A$ Dependence at STAR

> The STAR Collaboration reports measurements of back-to-back azimuthal correlations of di-$π^0$s produced at forward pseudorapidities ($2.6<η<4.0$) in $p$+$p$, $p+$Al, and $p+$Au collisions at a center-of-mass energy of 200 GeV. We observe a clear suppression of the correlated yields of back-to-back $π^0$ pairs in $p+$Al and $p+$Au collisions compared to the $p$+$p$ data. The observed suppression of back-to-back pairs as a function of transverse momentum suggests nonlinear gluon dynamics arising at high parton densities. The larger suppression found in $p+$Au relative to $p+$Al collisions exhibits a dependence of the saturation scale, $Q_s^2$, on the mass number, $A$. A linear scaling of the suppression with $A^{1/3}$ is observed with a slope of $-0.09$ $\pm$ $0.01$.

## Key claim

The STAR Collaboration reports a clear suppression of back-to-back di-π⁰ yields in p+Al and p+Au collisions relative to p+p at √s_NN = 200 GeV, which is interpreted as evidence for nonlinear gluon dynamics at high parton densities; however, the observed suppression is strongest at low transverse momentum and weakens at higher p_T, indicating that the nonlinear regime is only accessed at sufficiently small x and Q².

## Method

The STAR Collaboration measured azimuthal correlations of di-π⁰ pairs at forward pseudorapidities (2.6 < η < 4.0) using the Forward Meson Spectrometer (FMS) electromagnetic calorimeter. π⁰ mesons were reconstructed from photon pairs with invariant mass between 0.07 and 0.2 GeV/c², with trigger π⁰ p_T from 1.5 to 5 GeV/c and associated π⁰ p_T from 1 to 2.5 GeV/c. The correlation function C(Δφ) was defined as the yield of correlated trigger and associated π⁰ pairs per trigger particle per Δφ bin, corrected for detector acceptance using mixed events, and fitted with two Gaussians plus a constant pedestal to extract the away-side peak area, width, and pedestal.

## Key numbers

- Back-to-back π⁰ yields per trigger in p+Au (p+Al) are suppressed by about a factor of 1.7 (1.4) relative to p+p collisions for p_T^trig = 2-2.5 GeV/c and p_T^asso = 1-1.5 GeV/c
- For p_T^trig = 2-2.5 GeV/c and p_T^asso = 1-1.5 GeV/c, the correlation area×10³ (width) in p+p is 5.67 ± 0.12 (0.68 ± 0.01), in p+Al is 4.15 ± 0.24 (0.68 ± 0.03), and in p+Au is 3.30 ± 0.07 (0.64 ± 0.01)
- For p_T^trig = 2.5-3 GeV/c and p_T^asso = 2-2.5 GeV/c, the correlation area×10³ (width) in p+p is 0.18 ± 0.01 (0.47 ± 0.03), in p+Al is 0.13 ± 0.03 (0.51 ± 0.07), and in p+Au is 0.15 ± 0.01 (0.45 ± 0.03)
- A linear scaling of the suppression with A^{1/3} is observed with a slope of -0.09 ± 0.01
- For the lowest p_T bin (p_T^trig = 1.5-2 GeV/c, p_T^asso = 1-1.5 GeV/c), the mean values of x₂ and Q² are 0.05 and 2.2 GeV², respectively
- For the highest p_T bin (p_T^trig = 3-5 GeV/c, p_T^asso = 2-2.5 GeV/c), the mean values of x₂ and Q² are 0.1 and 4.6 GeV², respectively
- Systematic uncertainties of the relative area at p_T^asso = 1-1.5 GeV/c, 1.5-2 GeV/c, and 2-2.5 GeV/c are around 5%, 15%, and 22%, respectively, for p_T^trig = 2.5-3 GeV/c

## Context

This measurement extends previous RHIC results on forward hadron suppression in d+Au collisions (BRAHMS 2003, PHENIX 2004, STAR 2006) and di-hadron correlations (STAR 2006, PHENIX 2011) by using the theoretically cleaner p+A collision system. The results test predictions from the Color Glass Condensate framework (McLerran and Venugopalan 1993, Kharzeev and Levin 2004) and the Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff (1998) saturation model, which predicts Q_s² ∝ A^{1/3}. The observed A^{1/3} scaling of suppression and the absence of azimuthal broadening (consistent with PHENIX 2011 and ATLAS 2019) provide constraints on models of gluon saturation, multiple parton interactions (Strikman 2010), and energy loss mechanisms. The stable pedestal between p+A and p+p collisions enables future disentanglement of multiple parton scattering contributions in d+A collisions.
