# Hadrons on the worldline, holography, and Wilson flow

> Holographic principles have impacted the way we look at strong coupling phenomena in quantum chromodynamics, strongly interacting extensions of the standard model, and {condensed-matter} physics. In real world settings, however, we still lack understanding of why and when such an approach is justified. Therefore, here, without invoking any such principle a priori, we demonstrate how such a picture arises in the worldline formulation of quantum field theory. Among other connections to holographic models, a warped AdS5 geometry, a quantum mechanical picture, and hidden local symmetry emerge, as well as a Wilson flow (gradient flow), which extends the four-dimensional sources to five-dimensional fields and a link to the Gutzwiller trace formula. The worldline formulation also reproduces the non-relativistic case, which is important for condensed-matter physics.

## Key claim
Without invoking holographic principles a priori, the worldline formulation of quantum field theory naturally produces a warped AdS5 geometry, hidden local symmetry, and a Wilson flow that extends four-dimensional sources into five-dimensional fields, but only when supplemented with a harmonic oscillator potential does it yield a hadronic spectrum with equally spaced squared masses.

## Method
The author starts from the worldline representation of the scalar one-loop effective action, expressing it as a path integral over closed trajectories with Schwinger proper time T as the extra dimension. The two-point correlation function is computed using worldline propagators, and the resulting expressions are compared term-by-term with soft-wall AdS/QCD actions. A change of variables (exponential mapping) is introduced to transform the worldline path integral, and a harmonic oscillator potential is added to the worldline action, modifying the worldline propagator and producing a tower of states with linear mass-squared spacing.

## Key numbers
- The worldline two-point function contains the factor \(\E^{-G_{12}q^2}\) where the worldline propagator is \(G_{12}=T\hat\tau(1-\hat\tau)\) with \(\hat\tau=\tau/T\).
- The soft-wall AdS/QCD two-point function yields a digamma function expression \(\gamma+\psi(1-p^2/4\m^2)\) which can be expanded as a geometric series reflecting a tower of states with squared masses at \(q^2=4n\m^2\), \(n\in\mathbb{N}\).
- The change of variables \(cT = e^{c\Theta}-1\) transforms the proper-time integration measure and introduces a repulsive harmonic oscillator with frequency \(c^2/4\) in the transformed kinetic term.
- The harmonic oscillator potential modifies the worldline propagator to \(\C H(\tau)=\sin(\C|\tau|)-\sin(\C T)[1-\cos(\C\tau)]/[1-\cos(\C T)]\), with classical periodic solutions only for discrete values \(T=2\pi n/\C\), \(n\in\mathbb{N}\).

## Context
This work extends the bottom-up AdS/QCD approach of Karch (2006) by providing a first-principles derivation from the worldline formalism of Strassler (1992), rather than relying on the Maldacena conjecture. It connects to light-front holography of de Teramond (2008) and the conformal symmetry breaking transformations of de Alfaro (1976). The approach enables systematic inclusion of dynamical gluons via higher-loop effective actions and generalisation to non-relativistic systems with Schrodinger symmetry, relevant for condensed-matter applications.
