# Optimized Perturbation Theory Applied to a Model with Flavour Symmetry $SU_f(3)$

> The \textit{optimized perturbation theory} (OPT) is implemented in the $SU_f(3)$ flavor symmetric Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model to generate non-pertubative corrections to the quark pressure beyond the large-$N$ approximation. The correctness of this implementation is verified by the recovery of the already known non-perturbative results in the Hartree-Fock approximation, and by having the large-$N$ approximation as a limiting case. This formalism is then used to revisit a discussion on the discordance between the lattice data and the two flavor model prediction of the dynamical vector repulsive interactions, beyond the pseudocritical temperature. It is shown that these contradictory predictions can be corrected by considering a three quark flavor system.

## Key claim

The authors show that the dynamically generated repulsive vector interactions present in the chiral symmetric phase of the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model are a direct consequence of considering only two light quarks; when the optimized perturbation theory (OPT) is applied to the three-flavor SU_f(3) symmetric NJL model, these repulsive vector contributions cancel exactly, resolving the discrepancy with lattice data for the second-order coefficient c_2 above the pseudocritical temperature.

## Method

The authors implement the OPT in the SU_f(3) flavor symmetric NJL model by interpolating the quark masses via the addition and subtraction of a mass parameter eta_i for each flavor, and multiplying all vertices (the four-fermion coupling G and the six-fermion coupling K') by the expansion parameter delta. They compute the free energy to order delta, then apply the Principle of Minimal Sensibility (PMS) to determine the optimal eta_i, recovering the Hartree-Fock gap equations and the large-N_c limit as a check. The formalism is then used to compute the second-order coefficient c_2 of the Taylor expansion of the pressure around zero chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- The second-order coefficient c_2 in the SU_f(3) NJL model with OPT recovers convergence to the Stefan-Boltzmann limit, unlike the two-flavor case where c_2 develops a maximum at T ~ 1.2 T_c.
- The dynamically generated vector repulsive terms in the OPT have an intensity proportional to G_S/N_c, where G_S is the scalar coupling constant already present in the model.
- The large-N_c approximation is recovered as the limiting case N_c -> infinity, where K' -> K.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous application of OPT to the SU_f(2) flavor symmetric Polyakov-NJL model (Restrepo 2014), where finite-N_c corrections produced repulsive vector terms that spoiled agreement with lattice data above T_c. The present study shows that including a third light quark flavor in the SU_f(3) NJL model naturally cancels these repulsive vector contributions, supporting the conclusion of Stefan and collaborators that strong vector interactions must be absent in the deconfined phase. This enables a consistent description of lattice data for c_2 without requiring the vector coupling G_V to be set to zero by hand.
