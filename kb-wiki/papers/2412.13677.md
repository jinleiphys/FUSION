# The Baryon-Baryon Interaction in the Large-$N_c$ Limit

> We analyze the large-$N_c$ structure of the baryon-baryon potential derived in the framework of SU(3) chiral perturbation theory up to next-to-leading order including contact interactions as well as one-meson and two-meson exchange diagrams. Moreover, we assess the impact of SU(3) symmetry breaking from a large-$N_c$ perspective and show that the leading order results can successfully be applied to the hyperon-nucleon potential. Our results include a reduction of the number of relevant low-energy constants of the leading order contact interaction from fifteen to three, and we show that consistency is preserved if the $F/D$ ratio is given by $2/3$ and the $C/D$ ratio for the baryon decuplet-to-octet coupling is given by 2.

# The Baryon-Baryon Interaction in the Large-$N_c$ Limit

## Key claim
The large-$N_c$ analysis of the SU(3) chiral perturbation theory baryon-baryon potential up to next-to-leading order reduces the number of independent leading-order contact interaction low-energy constants from fifteen to three, provided that the $F/D$ ratio equals $2/3$ and the $C/D$ ratio for the baryon decuplet-to-octet coupling equals 2. This reduction holds only up to corrections of order $1/N_c$, and the leading-order results can be successfully applied to the hyperon-nucleon potential.

## Method
The authors analyze the large-$N_c$ structure of the baryon-baryon potential derived in SU(3) chiral perturbation theory, examining contact interactions as well as one-meson and two-meson exchange diagrams up to next-to-leading order in chiral power counting. They match the chiral perturbation theory potential to the general large-$N_c$ baryon-baryon potential derived from the contracted SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry Hartree Hamiltonian, using the large-$N_c$ scaling rules for spin-flavor generators and momentum-dependent suppression factors. The matching is performed by comparing the operator structures and their large-$N_c$ scalings between the two formalisms, and consistency is checked against existing hyperon-nucleon potentials from chiral perturbation theory.

## Key numbers
- The number of relevant low-energy constants for the leading order contact interaction is reduced from fifteen to three.
- The $F/D$ ratio is required to be $2/3$ for consistency.
- The $C/D$ ratio for the baryon decuplet-to-octet coupling is required to be 2.
- The central spin-independent potential $V_0^0$ and the flavor-dependent central potential $V_0^1$ scale as $\sim N_c$, while the spin-spin potential $V_\sigma^0$ and the tensor potential $V_\text{T}^0$ scale as $\sim 1/N_c$.
- The spin-spin potential $V_\sigma^1$ and the tensor potential $V_\text{T}^1$ scale as $\sim N_c$, while the spin-orbit potentials $V_\text{LS}^0$ and $V_\text{LS}^1$ scale as $\sim 1/N_c$.
- The ratios of the leading-order contact coefficients are found to be $C_T^{(2)}/C_T^{(1)} = 5/13$ and $C_T^{(3)}/C_T^{(1)} = -6/13$, each with corrections of order $1/N_c^2$.
- The large-$N_c$ sum rules for hyperon-nucleon contact terms are given by $C_{1S0}^{\Sigma\Sigma} \approx \frac{1}{9}(20 C_{1S0}^{\Lambda\Lambda} - 11 C_{3S1}^{\Lambda\Lambda} - 7 C_{3S1}^{\Lambda\Sigma})$ and $C_{3S1}^{\Sigma\Sigma} \approx -12 C_{1S0}^{\Lambda\Lambda} + 13 C_{3S1}^{\Lambda\Lambda} + 9 C_{3S1}^{\Lambda\Sigma}$, which are fulfilled to good accuracy especially for small cutoff masses.

## Context
This work extends the previous large-$N_c$ analysis of leading-order chiral contact interactions by Liu (2017) to include next-to-leading order contributions including one-meson and two-meson exchange diagrams. It builds on the foundational large-$N_c$ framework established by 't Hooft (1973), Witten (1979), and the contracted SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry developed by Dashen, Jenkins, and Manohar (1993-1994), as well as the large-$N_c$ nucleon-nucleon potential work by Kaplan and Savage (1996). The results enable a systematic reduction of free parameters in chiral perturbation theory descriptions of hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions, which is crucial for understanding hypernuclei and the role of hyperons in neutron star matter, particularly the "hyperon puzzle" where repulsive three-baryon forces may be required to sustain neutron stars of 2 solar masses as observed by Demorest (2010) and Antoniadis (2013).
