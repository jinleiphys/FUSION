# Path integral contour deformations for observables in $SU(N)$ gauge theory

> Path integral contour deformations have been shown to mitigate sign and signal-to-noise problems associated with phase fluctuations in lattice field theories. We define a family of contour deformations applicable to $SU(N)$ lattice gauge theory that can reduce sign and signal-to-noise problems associated with complex actions and complex observables. For observables, these contours can be used to define deformed observables with identical expectation value but different variance. As a proof-of-principle, we apply machine learning techniques to optimize the deformed observables associated with Wilson loops in two dimensional $SU(2)$ and $SU(3)$ gauge theory. We study loops consisting of up to 64 plaquettes and achieve variance reduction of up to 4 orders of magnitude.

## Key claim

Path integral contour deformations can reduce sign and signal-to-noise problems for observables in $SU(N)$ lattice gauge theory by defining deformed observables with identical expectation value but different variance, though the practical utility is demonstrated only for pure gauge theory in (1+1) dimensions as a proof-of-principle.

## Method

The authors define a family of complexified manifolds for $SU(N)$ gauge theory using angular parameterizations of the group manifold, where azimuthal angles $\phi_i \in [0,2\pi]$ and zenith angles $\theta_k \in [0,\pi/2]$ are deformed into the complex plane via vertical shifts $f(\Omega; \lambda, \chi)$ expanded in Fourier modes. The Jacobian determinants for these deformations are triangular matrices whose computation scales linearly with spacetime volume. Machine learning techniques optimize the deformation parameters to minimize the variance of deformed observables associated with Wilson loops, using the deformed observable method that relates path integrals over deformed contours to modified observables on undeformed contours.

## Key numbers

- Variance reduction of up to 4 orders of magnitude (factors of $10^3$--$10^4$) for Wilson loops with largest areas
- Wilson loops studied consist of up to 64 plaquettes in area
- Fourier cutoffs $\Lambda \in \{0,1,2\}$ explored for the deformation basis functions
- Calculations performed in $(1+1)$D $SU(2)$ and $SU(3)$ gauge theory

## Context

This work extends the deformed observable method introduced by Detmold et al. (2020) for improving signal-to-noise problems of complex observables in theories with real actions, applying it to non-Abelian gauge theories for the first time. Prior contour deformation approaches for $SU(N)$ gauge theory were restricted to simple contexts such as $(0+1)$D QCD using Lefschetz thimble methods (Di Renzo et al., 2018) and the generalized thimble method (Schmidt et al., 2017), while Lefschetz thimbles for finite-density observables in the heavy-dense limit predicted exponential growth of relevant thimbles with lattice size (Zambello et al., 2018). The linear scaling of computational cost with spacetime volume suggests feasibility for extending these methods to $(3+1)$D lattice gauge theory in future work.
