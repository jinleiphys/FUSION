# Probing cold nuclear matter effects with weak gauge boson production in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Within the framework of pQCD, we systematically study the $W^{\pm}/Z^0$ boson production as a probe of cold nuclear matter effects and nuclear parton distributions in p+Pb and Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC and future colliders. A detailed analysis at partonic level is performed. Moreover, with a semi-microscopic KP model of NPDFs, in which several nuclear effects (e.g. Fermi motion and nuclear binding, the off-shell correction, the nuclear coherent correction, and the nuclear meson correction) are included, we study the vector boson rapidity distribution in p+Pb collisions at the LHC, and a very good agreement with the latest data is found, including the $W$-boson charge asymmetry.

## Key claim

Within pQCD, weak gauge boson production in p+Pb and Pb+Pb collisions can probe cold nuclear matter effects and nuclear parton distributions, but the precision of current LHC measurements is not sufficient to exclude either the EPS09 or DSSZ nuclear PDF sets.

## Method

The authors used the DYNNLO program to calculate vector boson production at NLO and NNLO by incorporating nuclear parton distribution functions (EPS09, DSSZ, and the semi-microscopic KP model). They performed a partonic-level analysis of nuclear modification factors and studied the individual contributions of different nuclear effects (Fermi motion and nuclear binding, off-shell correction, nuclear coherent correction, and nuclear meson correction) in the KP model.

## Key numbers

- Z-boson rapidity distribution in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV shows NNLO correction is "rather small" in the studied rapidity region
- Weak boson invariant mass is approximately 80-90 GeV, with production timescale ~10^{-3} fm/c and decay timescale ~0.08-0.09 fm
- At LHC mid-rapidity, the kinematic region corresponds to x ~ 0.033, the transition between valence-dominated and sea-quark-dominated regions
- Z-boson production probes NPDFs at high Q^2 ~ (100 GeV)^2

## Context

This work extends previous global fits of nuclear PDFs by groups such as Eskola (2009), de Florian (2011), and Schienbein (2009), which still show considerable differences among extracted CNM effects. The study enables future constraints on NPDFs by combining LHC measurements with future higher-energy collider data, particularly for probing shadowing effects on sea quarks and gluons in new kinematic regions. The KP model approach (Kulagin and Petti, 2004, 2014) offers insights into underlying nuclear physics mechanisms that conventional fitting approaches do not provide.
