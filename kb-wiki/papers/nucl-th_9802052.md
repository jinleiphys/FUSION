# Damping rates of hot Giant Dipole Resonances

> The damping rate of hot giant dipole resonances (GDR) is investigated. Besides Landau damping we consider collisions and density fluctuations as contributions to the damping of GDR. Within the nonequilibrium Green's function method we derive a non-Markovian kinetic equation. The linearization of the latter one leads to complex dispersion relations. The complex solution provides the centroid energy and the damping width of giant resonances. The experimental damping widths are the full width half maximum (FWHM) and can be reproduced by the full width of the structure function. Within simple finite size scaling we give a relation between the minimal interaction strength which is required for a collective oscillation and the clustersize. We investigate the damping of giant dipole resonances within a Skyrme type of interaction. Different collision integrals are compared with each other in order to incorporate correlations. The inclusion of a conserving relaxation time approximation allows to find the $T^2$-dependence of the damping rate with a temperature known from the Fermi-liquid theory. However, memory effects turn out to be essential for a proper treatment of the damping of collective modes. We derive a Landau like formula for the one--particle relaxation time similar to the damping of zero sound.

# Damping rates of hot Giant Dipole Resonances

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that memory effects in the collision integral are essential for a proper treatment of the damping of collective modes in hot nuclear matter, and that the experimentally observed T²-dependence of the damping rate can be reproduced within a conserving relaxation time approximation, but only when non-Markovian (memory) effects are included.

## Method

The authors derive a non-Markovian kinetic equation using the nonequilibrium Green's function method with the generalized Kadanoff-Baym ansatz, then linearize this equation to obtain complex dispersion relations for infinite hot nuclear matter. They compare four approximations: the collisionless Vlasov equation (Lindhard/RPA), a Markovian Boltzmann (BUU) collision integral with a conserving relaxation time approximation (Mermin), a dynamical relaxation time approximation incorporating memory effects, and the inclusion of density fluctuations on the potential. The complex zeros of the dielectric function provide the centroid energy and damping width of giant dipole resonances, using a Skyrme-type interaction with parameters fitted to reproduce binding energy E/A = -16 MeV at saturation density n₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³.

## Key numbers

- For ²⁰⁸Pb: wave vector q ≈ 0.23 fm⁻¹ (with nuclear radius R = 6.7 fm)
- For ¹²⁰Sn: wave vector q ≈ 0.277 fm⁻¹ (with nuclear radius R = 5.6 fm)
- Saturation density n₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³ and binding energy E/A = -16 MeV used for Skyrme parameter fitting
- Nuclear cross section σ = 40 mb used as spin-isospin averaged cross section for relaxation time calculations
- Critical potential scaling relation: V₀_crit(A_crit) = (a + b/A_crit) MeV fm³ with a = 151.9 and b = 2997.4
- Fermi integral result: I^f_B = (2π²/3)T³
- Relaxation time in Boltzmann approximation: 1/τ_B = (8/3)T² m σ

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of giant resonance damping beyond the standard RPA and Fermi liquid theory approaches used by KPS95, KPS96, TKL97, KTO96, and KLT97. The authors compare their results with the collisionless Lindhard approach (LIN54) and the conserving relaxation time approximation following Mermin (MER70). The paper enables a systematic comparison of different collision integral approximations (Vlasov, BUU, memory, and density fluctuation) for understanding the temperature dependence of GDR damping, and provides a finite-size scaling relation linking minimal interaction strength for collective oscillations to cluster size.
