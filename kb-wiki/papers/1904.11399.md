# Reexamining Ginzburg-Landau theory for neutron $^3P_2$ superfluidity in neutron stars

> The Ginzburg-Landau (GL) effective theory is a useful tool to study a superconductivity or superfluidity near the critical temperature, and usually the expansion up to the 4th order in terms of order parameters is sufficient for the description of the second-order phase transition. In this paper, we discuss the GL equation for the neutron $^{3}P_{2}$ superfluidity relevant for interior of neutron stars. We derive the GL expansion up to the 8th order in the condensates and find that this order is necessary for the system to have the unique ground state, unlike the ordinary cases. Starting from the $LS$ potential, which provides the dominant attraction between two neutrons at the high density, we derive the GL equation in the path-integral formalism, where the auxiliary field method and the Nambu-Gor'kov representation are used. We present the detailed description for the trace calculation necessary in the derivation of the GL equation. As numerical results, we show the phase diagram of the neutron $^{3}P_{2}$ superfluidity on the plane spanned by the temperature and magnetic field, and find that the 8th order terms lead to a first-order phase transition, whose existence was predicted in the Bogoliubov-de Gennes equation but has not been found thus far within the framework of the GL expansion up to the 6th order.The first-order phase transition will affect the interior structures inside the neutron stars.

## Key claim

The Ginzburg-Landau expansion for neutron \(^{3}P_{2}\) superfluidity must be carried to 8th order in the condensates to obtain a unique, stable ground state; at 6th order the energy is not bounded from below, and the 8th-order terms produce a first-order phase transition between the D\(_{2}\)-BN and D\(_{4}\)-BN phases that had been predicted by the Bogoliubov-de Gennes equation but was absent in earlier GL expansions up to 6th order.

## Method

Starting from the \(LS\) (spin-orbit) potential that provides the dominant attraction between two neutrons at high density, the authors derived the Ginzburg-Landau free energy in the path-integral formalism using the auxiliary-field method and the Nambu-Gor'kov representation. They performed the trace calculation to expand the effective potential in powers of the condensate field \(A^{ab}\) up to 8th order and in powers of the magnetic field up to 4th order, then numerically minimized the resulting free energy to construct the phase diagram on the temperature-magnetic field plane.

## Key numbers

- The 8th-order GL expansion is the minimum order needed to uniquely determine the stable ground states (including at zero magnetic field); the 6th-order expansion makes the system unstable because the energy is not bounded from below.
- The neutron magnetic moment is \(\gamma_n = 1.2 \times 10^{-13}\) MeV/T (1 T = \(10^4\) G).
- Magnetar surface magnetic fields reach about \(10^{15}\) G and may reach \(10^{18}\) G in the interior.
- The phase diagram on the temperature-magnetic field plane shows a first-order phase transition line between the D\(_{2}\)-BN and D\(_{4}\)-BN phases, with a tricritical point separating first-order and second-order transition lines.

## Context

This work extends prior GL analyses of neutron \(^{3}P_{2}\) superfluidity that had been carried only to 4th or 6th order (Sauls 1978, Masuda 2015, Mizushima 2016). The 8th-order terms resolve the instability present at 6th order and reproduce the first-order transition previously seen only in the Bogoliubov-de Gennes equation under the quasiclassical approximation (Mizushima 2016). The results enable more reliable studies of vortex structures, spontaneous magnetization, and interior structures of neutron stars, particularly magnetars where strong magnetic fields favor the D\(_{4}\)-BN phase.
