# Description of Drip-Line Nuclei within Relativistic Mean-Field Plus BCS Approach

> Recently it has been demonstrated, considering Ni and Ca isotopes as prototypes, that the relativistic mean-field plus BCS (RMF+BCS) approach wherein the single particle continuum corresponding to the RMF is replaced by a set of discrete positive energy states for the calculation of pairing energy provides a good approximation to the full relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) description of the ground state properties of the drip-line neutron rich nuclei. The applicability of RMF+BCS is essentially due to the fact that the main contribution to the pairing correlations is provided by the low-lying resonant states. General validity of this approach is demonstrated by the detailed calculations for the ground state properties of the chains of isotopes of O, Ca, Ni, Zr, Sn and Pb nuclei. The TMA and NL-SH force parameter sets have been used for the effective mean-field Lagrangian. Comprehensive results for the two neutron separation energy, rms radii, single particle pairing gaps and pairing energies etc. are presented. The Ca isotopes are found to exhibit distinct features near the neutron drip line whereby it is found that further addition of neutrons causes a rapid increase in the neutron rms radius with almost no increase in the binding energy, indicating the occurrence of halos. A comparison of these results with the available experimental data and with the recent continuum relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) calculations amply demonstrates the validity and usefulness of this fast RMF+BCS approach.

# Description of Drip-Line Nuclei within Relativistic Mean-Field Plus BCS Approach

## Key claim

The RMF+BCS approach, where the single-particle continuum is replaced by discrete positive energy states in a spherical box, provides a good approximation to the full relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) description for drip-line nuclei, but only because the main contribution to pairing correlations comes from low-lying resonant states rather than the entire continuum. The authors demonstrate this general validity across six isotopic chains, though the approximation relies on the fact that non-resonant continuum states contribute negligibly to pairing.

## Method

The authors performed spherical relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations using the TMA and NL-SH force parameter sets, then applied a state-dependent BCS treatment with a delta-function pairing interaction (strength V0 = 350 MeV fm^3) to compute ground-state properties. The continuum was discretized by enclosing the nucleus in a spherical box of radius approximately 30 fm, generating a set of positive-energy states. Self-consistent solutions were obtained for the coupled RMF equations and the BCS gap equations for chains of O, Ca, Ni, Zr, Sn, and Pb isotopes.

## Key numbers

- Pairing interaction strength: V0 = 350 MeV fm^3, determined by fitting to binding energies of Ni isotopes and used unchanged for all other isotopic chains
- For the nucleus 62Ca, the resonant 1g9/2 state lies at 0.45 MeV and has a pairing gap energy of approximately 1 MeV, comparable to bound states like 1f5/2 and 1f7/2
- The neutron Fermi energy in 62Ca is lambda_n = -0.204 MeV
- Shell closures in Ca isotopes appear at neutron numbers N = 14, 20, 28, and 40; the conventional N = 50 shell closure is absent for neutron-rich Ca isotopes
- The neutron drip line for Ca isotopes extends to 72Ca, where the 3s1/2 and 1g9/2 states become bound

## Context

This work extends the earlier demonstration by Yadav et al. (2004) for Ni and Ca isotopes to a broader set of nuclei, confirming that the RMF+BCS approach reproduces results from the more computationally expensive continuum relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) calculations by Meng and others. The approach builds on non-relativistic studies by Sandulescu et al. and Grasso et al., who showed that only a few low-lying resonant states influence pairing properties in HF+BCS. The method enables systematic surveys of drip-line nuclei across the periodic table, with the finding that Ca and Zr isotopes exhibit halo-like features near the drip line due to low-angular-momentum states (3s1/2 in Ca, 3p1/2 and 3p3/2 in Zr), while Ni, Sn, and Pb isotopes do not favor halo formation.
