# Short-Range Correlations and the Nuclear EMC Effect in Deuterium and Helium-3

> The EMC effect in deuterium and helium-3 is studied using a convolution formalism that allows isolating the impact of high-momentum nucleons in short-ranged correlated (SRC) pairs. We assume that the modification of the structure function of bound nucleons is given by a universal (i.e. nucleus independent) function of their virtuality, and find that the effect of such modifications is dominated by nucleons in SRC pairs. This SRC-dominance of nucleon modifications is observed despite the fact that the bulk of the nuclear inelastic scattering cross-section comes from interacting with low-momentum nucleons. These findings are found to be robust to model details including nucleon modification function parametrization, free nucleon structure function and treatment of nucleon motion effects. While existing data cannot discriminate between such model details, we present predictions for measured, but not yet published, tritium EMC effect and tagged nucleon structure functions in deuterium that are sensitive to the neutron structure functions and bound nucleon modification functions.

## Key claim

The authors find that nucleon modification effects required to reproduce the measured EMC effect in deuterium and helium-3 are dominated by nucleons in short-range correlated (SRC) pairs, even though the bulk of the nuclear inelastic scattering cross-section comes from interactions with low-momentum (mean-field) nucleons. This SRC-dominance of nucleon modifications is robust to model details including the parametrization of the nucleon modification function, the free neutron structure function, and the treatment of nucleon motion effects.

## Method

The authors used a convolution formalism to calculate the nuclear structure function \(F_2^A(x_B)\) for deuterium and helium-3, incorporating a universal offshell nucleon modification function \(f^{off}(\tilde{x})\) that is linear in the nucleon fractional virtuality \(v\). They employed two approaches for the lightcone momentum and virtuality distributions: a spectral-function (SF) approximation using AV18-based wave functions, and a generalized-contact formalism lightcone (GCF-LC) approximation that separates mean-field and SRC contributions and manifestly satisfies the momentum sum rule. The parameters of the modification functions were determined by a \(\chi^2\)-minimization fit to experimental \(F_2^{^3He}/F_2^d\) and \(F_2^d/(F_2^p+F_2^n)\) data for \(0.17 \leq x_B \leq 0.825\), using two different \(F_2^n/F_2^p\) parametrizations (Segarra et al. and Arrington et al.) and several offshell function forms.

## Key numbers

* The best fit using the spectral-function approximation with the Segarra et al. \(F_2^n/F_2^p\) and a linear-in-\(\tilde{x}\) offshell function yielded a total reduced \(\chi^2\) of 15.4 / 30 = 0.51 for the combined deuterium and helium-3 data.
* The momentum sum rule violation in the spectral-function approximation was \(\leq 1\%\) for helium-3 and negligible (\(<0.1\%\)) for deuterium.
* The cutoff momentum separating mean-field and SRC nucleons in the GCF-LC approach was set to \(p_{cutoff}=240\) MeV\(/c\) for helium-3, chosen such that the SRC pair fraction matched ab-initio calculations: \(10.1\%\) for neutrons and \(5.9\%\) for protons.
* The SRC contribution to the offshell modification effect dominates over the mean-field contribution at high \(x_B\) in all models, despite SRC nucleons constituting only \(O(\sim 4\%)\) of the nuclear momentum distribution in deuterium.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Schmookler et al. (2019) and Segarra et al. (2019), who demonstrated that the EMC effect across nuclei from helium-3 to lead can be explained by a single effective universal modification function of nucleons in SRC pairs. The present study uses exact wave functions for light nuclei to separate mean-field and SRC contributions, contradicting claims by Wang et al. (2020) regarding the analysis of the SRC universal modification function. The authors provide predictions for the tritium EMC effect (to be tested by the MARATHON collaboration) and for tagged nucleon structure functions in deuterium (to be tested by the BAND and LAD collaborations), which will help disentangle the free neutron structure function from bound nucleon modification effects.
