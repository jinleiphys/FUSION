# Equivalence of Classical Skyrmions and Coherent States of Baryons II. Baryonic Coherent State Construction on Compact Manifolds

> In connection with the possibility of skyrmion production from small domain disoriented chiral condensates formation from heavy ion collisions, the direct relation of a classical skyrmion to baryon states is examined. It is argued that a skyrmion is a coherent state of baryons. The collective coordinate approach of quantization means that the physical baryon states exist not in flat space but on a compact manifold. This requires the construction of coherent states in such a curve space. Using the techniques associated with the Segal-Bargmann transform also known as the coherent state transform used for example in the study of the classical limit of quantum gravity, such states can be constructed in the context of the Skyrme model. They are made up directly of baryon states on S^3 but with quantum operators on the SO(3) manifold. In terms of wavefunctions, they are a superposition of the analytic baryon wavefunctions of Adkins, Nappi and Witten. The distribution of the baryon states in terms of the relative probabilities of the baryons inside a skyrmion can therefore be determined.

## Key claim

A classical skyrmion can be expressed as a coherent superposition of baryon states, but the construction requires using quantum operators on the SO(3) manifold while the baryon states themselves live on S^3, mixing ingredients from different spaces because straightforward application of the coherent state transform on S^3 fails.

## Method

The paper constructs baryonic coherent states on the compact manifold S^3 using the generalized Segal-Bargmann transform (coherent state transform) originally developed for the classical limit of quantum gravity. Annihilation operators are built via a phase space Wick rotation generated by the complexifier operator, which is the kinetic energy divided by a fundamental energy scale. The coherent states are then obtained as simultaneous eigenstates of these annihilation operators after analytic continuation of the position eigenvalues from real to complex values, yielding one coherent state per phase space point.

## Key numbers

- The energy of baryon states from SU(2) collective coordinate quantization depends on the polynomial degree l as E_l = 1/(8λ) l(l+2) + M, where only half-integral spin and isospin states are physical baryons.
- The energy of states from SO(3) collective coordinate quantization depends on l as E_l = 1/(2λ) l(l+1) + M, producing only integral spin and isospin states (none of which are physical particles).
- The Lagrangian for the Skyrme model contains the pion decay constant f_π and the ρ-π-π coupling g as parameters.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous paper (Wong, 2002) and builds directly on the baryon wavefunctions of Adkins, Nappi, and Witten (1983), which are analytic functions on S^3. It contrasts with the chiral soliton approach of Birse (1990) and others, which cannot be applied to the Skyrme model because it is a nonlinear sigma model, and with the group-theoretic coherent state method of Amado et al (1989) and Perelomov (1986), which requires selecting an arbitrary fixed state vector. The results enable determination of the relative probabilities of different baryons inside a skyrmion, with application to disoriented chiral condensate formation in heavy ion collisions to be addressed in future work (Wong, 2003).
