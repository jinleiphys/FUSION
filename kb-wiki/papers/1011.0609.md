# On the nature of the Roper resonance

> The lightest N* state, N(1440) P11, also known as Roper resonance, has puzzled physicists for decades. A large variety of theoretical models aimed to understand its properties have been proposed. Some of them are briefly reviewed here, together with the hadronic processes where the Roper resonance is revealed or plays an important role.

## Key claim

The Roper resonance (N(1440) P11) remains poorly understood despite decades of study; its dynamical origin is unclear, as different theoretical models either require a genuine three-quark state or can generate the resonance purely from meson-baryon interactions in coupled channels without any bare state.

## Method

The paper reviews a broad range of theoretical models for the Roper resonance, including quark models with harmonic oscillator potentials, Goldstone boson exchange models, lattice QCD calculations, hybrid (q³G) models, Skyrme soliton models, and coupled-channel meson-baryon dynamical models. It also surveys experimental hadronic reactions where the Roper is observed or plays a role, such as pion-nucleon scattering, electroproduction, alpha-particle scattering, J/ψ decay, and double-pion production in πN, NN, and np collisions.

## Key numbers

- Breit-Wigner mass of N(1440) = 1420 to 1470 MeV (PDG estimate, ≈1440 MeV)
- Breit-Wigner full width = 200 to 450 MeV (PDG estimate, ≈300 MeV)
- Real part of pole position = 1350 to 1380 MeV (PDG estimate, ≈1365 MeV)
- −2Im(pole position) = 160 to 220 MeV (PDG estimate, ≈190 MeV)
- Decay fraction Γ(Nπ)/Γ(total) = 0.55 to 0.75 (PDG estimate)
- Decay fraction Γ(Nππ)/Γ(total) = 30–40% (PDG estimate)
- Decay fraction Γ(Δπ)/Γ(total) = 20–30% (PDG estimate)
- Decay fraction Γ(Nρ)/Γ(total) < 8% (PDG estimate)
- Decay fraction Γ(N(ππ)^{I=0}_{S-wave})/Γ(total) = 5–10% (PDG estimate)
- Decay fraction Γ(pγ)/Γ(total) = 0.035–0.048% (PDG estimate)
- Decay fraction Γ(nγ)/Γ(total) = 0.009–0.032% (PDG estimate)
- K-matrix analysis (Ref. arXiv:0707.3591): Γ(πN)/Γ(total) ≈ 61%, Γ(πΔ)/Γ(total) ≈ 18%, Γ(σN)/Γ(total) ≈ 21%
- Two almost degenerate poles found near πΔ threshold: averaged values (Re M*, −Im M*) = (1363^{+9}_{-6}, 79^{+3}_{-5}) MeV and (1373^{+12}_{-10}, 114^{+14}_{-9}) MeV (Kamano 2010)
- Mass and width from J/ψ → N̄ Nπ at BES: 1358 ± 6 ± 16 MeV and 179 ± 26 ± 50 MeV
- Hybrid (q³G) state mass lower bound: M(hyb) > 1870 ± 100 MeV (Capstick 1999)
- Skyrme model breathing mode mass: M* = 1420 MeV (Kaulfuss 1985)
- qqqq̄q admixture in Roper: 3 to 25% depending on constituent quark mass (Julià-Díaz and Riska 2006)
- Lattice QCD (quenched): physical level ordering (1/2⁺ below 1/2⁻) recovered for pion masses below 380 MeV (Mahbub 2010)

## Context

This review extends the long-standing puzzle of the Roper resonance first discovered by Roper (1963) in πN partial-wave analysis, and contrasts the parity reversal problem (N(1440) P11 lighter than N(1535) S11) that challenges SU(6) quark models with color-spin interactions (nucl-th/0008028). The paper surveys alternative explanations including Goldstone boson exchange models (Glozman 1995), lattice QCD results (Mahbub 2009, 2010), and dynamical generation in coupled-channel models (Jülich model, Krehl 1999; arXiv:0903.4337). It enables future work by identifying that electroproduction data at low Q² (<1 (GeV/c)²) disagree with all existing quark models, while at higher Q² relativistic quark models assuming the Roper is the first radial excitation agree with data (Ramalho 2010), suggesting missing meson cloud effects are crucial.
