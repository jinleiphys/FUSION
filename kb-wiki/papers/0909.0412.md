# Viscosity and dilepton production of a chemically equilibrating quark-gluon plasma at finite baryon density

> By considering the effect of shear viscosity we have investigated the evolution of a chemically equilibrating quark-gluon plasma at finite baryon density. Based on the evolution of the system we have performed a complete calculation for the dilepton production from the following processes: $q\bar{q}{\to}l\bar{l}$, $q\bar{q}{\to}gl\bar{l}$, Compton-like scattering ($qg{\to}ql\bar{l}$, $\bar{q}g{\to}{\bar{q}}l\bar{l}$), gluon fusion $g\bar{g}{\to}c\bar{c}$, annihilation $q\bar{q}{\to}c\bar{c}$ as well as the multiple scattering of quarks. We have found that quark-antiquark annihilation, Compton-like scatterring, gluon fusion, and multiple scattering of quarks give important contributions. Moreover, we have also found that the dilepton yield is an increasing function of the initial quark chemical potential, and the increase of the quark phase lifetime because of the viscosity also obviously raises the dilepton yield.

# Viscosity and dilepton production of a chemically equilibrating quark-gluon plasma at finite baryon density

## Key claim

The dilepton yield from a chemically equilibrating quark-gluon plasma at finite baryon density is an increasing function of the initial quark chemical potential and is enhanced by shear viscosity, contrary to the suppression found in earlier thermodynamic equilibrium calculations. This result depends on the specific choice of shear viscosity coefficient; the authors compute it directly from thermodynamic quantities rather than treating it as an adjustable parameter.

## Method

The authors derived a set of coupled relaxation equations describing the space-time evolution of temperature, quark chemical potential, and parton fugacities for a longitudinally expanding quark-gluon plasma, incorporating shear viscosity via relativistic kinetic theory under the relaxation time approximation. They solved these equations for Au+Au central collisions at RHIC energies using initial conditions from the HIJING model, then calculated dilepton production rates from eight processes: qqbar to l lbar, qqbar to g l lbar, Compton-like scattering (qg to q l lbar and qbar g to qbar l lbar), gluon fusion (gg to c cbar), qqbar to c cbar, multiple scattering of quarks, and soft (infrared) contributions.

## Key numbers

- Initial conditions: proper time tau0 = 0.70 fm, temperature T0 = 0.570 GeV, gluon fugacity lambda_g0 = 0.08, quark fugacity lambda_q0 = 0.02
- Initial quark chemical potentials studied: mu_q0 = 0.000, 0.284, 0.568, and 0.852 GeV
- Quark flavor number N_f = 2.5
- Charged D-meson branching ratio to leptons: 17%
- Time interval between evolution points in phase diagram: 0.3 fm

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of chemically equilibrating QGP by Shuryak, Biro and co-workers (1990s) and the viscous hydrodynamic treatment of QGP at finite baryon density by He and collaborators (2006). It contrasts with thermodynamic equilibrium calculations by Kapusta and others (1980s-1990s) that found dilepton suppression with increasing chemical potential. The authors compute the shear viscosity coefficient directly from thermodynamic quantities using the approach of Danielewicz and Gyulassy (1988) and Hou and Li (2005), rather than treating it as an adjustable parameter as in earlier work. The results enable more realistic predictions for dilepton observables at RHIC and LHC energies.
