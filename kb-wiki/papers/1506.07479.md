# Bohr Hamiltonian with a deformation-dependent mass term: physical meaning of the free parameter

> Embedding of the 5-dimensional (5D) space of the Bohr Hamiltonian with a deformation-dependent mass (DDM) into a 6-dimensional (6D) space shows that the free parameter in the dependence of the mass on the deformation is connected to the curvature of the 5D space, with the special case of constant mass corresponding to a flat 5D space. Comparison of the DDM Bohr Hamiltonian to the 5D classical limit of Hamiltonians of the 6D interacting boson model (IBM), shows that the DDM parameter is proportional to the strength of the pairing interaction in the U(5) (vibrational) symmetry limit, while it is proportional to the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction in the SU(3) (rotational) symmetry limit, and to the difference of the pairing interactions among s, d bosons and d bosons alone in the O(6) (gamma-soft) limit. The presence of these interactions leads to a curved 5D space in the classical limit of IBM, in contrast to the flat 5D space of the original Bohr Hamiltonian, which is made curved by the introduction of the deformation-dependent mass.

# Bohr Hamiltonian with a deformation-dependent mass term: physical meaning of the free parameter

## Key claim
The free parameter *a* in the deformation-dependent mass (DDM) Bohr Hamiltonian is physically connected to the curvature of the 5D collective space and, through comparison with the classical limit of the interacting boson model (IBM), is proportional to specific interaction strengths in each IBM symmetry limit, but only after neglecting triaxiality terms proportional to *v*₂ that appear in the most general IBM Hamiltonian but are absent from the DDM Bohr Hamiltonian.

## Method
The authors embed the 5D space of the Bohr Hamiltonian into a 6D space via stereographic projection, showing that the DDM parameter *a* = 1/(4*R*²) where *R* is the radius of curvature of a hypersphere. They then compare the DDM Bohr Hamiltonian (with Davidson potential and mass function *f*(*β*) = 1 + *aβ*²) term-by-term to the classical limit of the most general IBM Hamiltonian, matching coefficients to relate *a* to IBM parameters in the U(5), SU(3), and O(6) symmetry limits.

## Key numbers
- The DDM parameter *a* is related to the curvature radius *R* of the 5D hypersphere by *a* = 1/(4*R*²), with *a* ≪ 1.
- In the U(5) (vibrational) limit: *ã* = −*κ*₅, where *κ*₅ is the strength of the *d*-boson pairing interaction (with *κ*₅ negative, ensuring *a* > 0).
- In the SU(3) (rotational) limit: *ã* = (1/2)*κ*, where *κ* is the strength of the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction (with *κ* positive, ensuring *a* > 0).
- In the O(6) (γ-soft) limit: *ã* = −(1/4)*A* + (1/6)*B*, where *A* and *B* are strengths of pairing interactions among *s* and *d* bosons and among *d* bosons alone.
- The constant mass case (*a* = 0) corresponds to a flat 5D space (infinite radius of curvature).

## Context
This work extends the DDM Bohr Hamiltonian introduced by Bonatsos et al. (2011) by giving physical meaning to its free parameter, which was previously phenomenological. It connects the Bohr collective model to the interacting boson model (IBM) of Arima and Iachello (1975), showing that the classical limit of IBM (Ginocchio and Kirson, 1980) naturally produces a curved 5D space, unlike the original flat Bohr Hamiltonian. The results enable future studies of how curvature affects critical point symmetries such as E(5) and X(5) (Iachello, 2000, 2001) and shape phase transitions in nuclei.
