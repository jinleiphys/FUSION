# Warm alpha-nucleon matter

> The properties of warm dilute alpha-nucleon matter are studied in a variational approach in the Thomas-Fermi approximation starting from an effective two-body nucleon-nucleon interaction. The equation of state, symmetry energy, incompressibility of the said matter as well as the alpha fraction are in consonance with those evaluated from the virial approach that sets a bench-mark for such calculations at low densities.

# Warm alpha-nucleon matter

## Key claim

The Thomas-Fermi variational approach with an effective two-body nucleon-nucleon interaction reproduces the equation of state, symmetry energy, incompressibility, and alpha fraction of warm dilute alpha-nucleon matter in close agreement with the virial (S-matrix) benchmark calculations, but only in the low-density regime (up to ρ_b = 0.01 fm⁻³) where the virial expansion is considered valid.

## Method

The authors employ a Thomas-Fermi mean-field model with the finite-range, momentum- and density-dependent modified Seyler-Blanchard (SBM) effective interaction to compute occupation probabilities for nucleons and alpha particles by minimizing the thermodynamic potential of the system. The alpha particles are treated as uniform nuclear drops with sharp surfaces (radius 2.16 fm) and are assumed not to interpenetrate. Results are compared with those from the S-matrix (virial) approach truncated at second-order virial coefficients, which includes only NN, αN, and αα scattering channels.

## Key numbers

- Alpha particle binding energy: 28.3 MeV (experimental value used in calculations)
- Alpha particle sharp-surface radius: 2.16 fm (derived from experimental rms charge radius)
- Nucleon Fermi momentum inside alpha particle: 220.5 MeV/c (consistent with the sharp-surface radius)
- Incompressibility of symmetric nucleon matter (K_∞): 238 MeV (governed by the parameter κ = 1/6)
- Calculations performed up to baryon density ρ_b = 0.01 fm⁻³ at temperatures T = 3, 5, and 10 MeV
- At low temperatures and higher densities, alphas are the major constituents of the matter; at T = 10 MeV and ρ_b = 0.001 fm⁻³, the alpha fraction Y_α is close to zero

## Context

This work extends mean-field studies of cold alpha matter (Clark and Carr, 1970s) and warm clusterized nuclear matter (Lamb, 1970s) to a variational Thomas-Fermi framework. It directly compares with the virial/S-matrix approach of Horowitz and Schwenk (2006) and the S-matrix framework of Mallik (2010), which are considered benchmark calculations for low-density nuclear matter. The results enable future extension to higher densities where the virial expansion breaks down, and provide a foundation for including heavier clusters in mean-field calculations of supernova matter and neutron star crust properties.
