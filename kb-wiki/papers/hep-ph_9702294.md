# Suppression of PiNN(1535) Coupling in the QCD Sum Rule

> The πNN^* and ηNN^* coupling constants are studied in the QCD sum rule ($N^* \equiv N(1535)). We investigate the two point functions between the vacuum and a one meson state in the soft meson limit. The operator product expansion is explicitly performed up to terms of dimension eight. We find that \gpNR vanishes to this order, while \geNR has a finite contribution. The vanishing of \gpNR is a rigorous consequence in the chiral limit. The results of the QCD sum rule are compared with those of low energy effective models.

# Suppression of πNN(1535) Coupling in the QCD Sum Rule

## Key claim

The πNN* coupling constant (gπNN*) vanishes identically in the chiral limit when computed via QCD sum rules up to dimension eight in the operator product expansion, while the ηNN* coupling constant (gηNN*) remains finite; this vanishing is a rigorous consequence of chiral symmetry, not merely a truncation artifact.

## Method

The authors compute two-point correlation functions between the vacuum and a one-meson state (π or η) in the soft meson limit (qμ → 0), using nucleon interpolating fields with mixing parameters t = -1 (Ioffe current for the nucleon N) and t = 0.8 (for the N* resonance). The operator product expansion is performed up to dimension-eight terms, and the soft meson theorem is applied to relate meson-to-vacuum matrix elements to vacuum condensates. The resulting correlation functions are analyzed for the term proportional to p̸γ5, which is expected to dominate the πNN* and ηNN* couplings.

## Key numbers

- gπNN* vanishes to order O(mq, αS) in the chiral limit; the correlation function for πNN* is identically zero up to dimension eight.
- gηNN* extracted from the sum rule analysis varies within the range 5 ≤ gηNN* ≤ 20, which is too large compared to the experimental value gηNN* ∼ 2.
- For comparison, the experimental πNN coupling constant gπNN ∼ 13 and ηNN coupling constant gηNN ≤ 5.
- The experimental πNN* coupling constant is gπNN* ∼ 0.7 and ηNN* ∼ 2 from decay widths.

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach of Shiomi and Hatsuda (1994) for πNN couplings to the negative-parity N(1535) resonance, and builds on the mass study of negative-parity octet baryons by Jido, Kodama, and Oka (1996). The results are consistent with the nonrelativistic quark model of Isgur and Karl (1978), where destructive interference between the two independent 1/2- states suppresses the πNN* coupling while enhancing ηNN*, and with the large-Nc method where the I=J rule suppresses the pion coupling. The vanishing of gπNN* also supports the parity-doublet model of DeTar and Kunihiro (1989), where the coupling is proportional to the chiral-invariant mass term m0, which appears to be small or zero. The work enables further QCD sum rule studies of baryon resonance couplings and tests of chiral symmetry in the baryon sector.
