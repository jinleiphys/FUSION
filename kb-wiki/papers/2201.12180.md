# Euclidean thermal correlation functions in local QFT

> In this work we outline the general analytic characteristics satisfied by scalar correlation functions at finite temperature in local quantum field theory. We demonstrate that the locality of the fields in particular imposes significant constraints on the spectral structure of the theory, and that this enables the non-perturbative effects experienced by thermal particle states to be directly calculated from Euclidean correlation functions, avoiding the inverse problem.

# Euclidean thermal correlation functions in local QFT

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that for scalar fields in local quantum field theory at finite temperature, the non-perturbative effects experienced by thermal particle states can be directly calculated from Euclidean correlation functions without performing an ill-posed inversion, provided the zero-temperature theory contains a single massive particle state with a sufficiently large gap to the continuum.

## Method
The author derives the general spectral representation of the thermal two-point function using the KMS condition and locality, showing that all thermal medium effects are encoded in a thermal spectral density \(\widetilde{D}_\beta(\vec{u},s)\). By analytically continuing the retarded and advanced propagators to imaginary time and exploiting the Fourier series expansion of the Euclidean two-point function, the author obtains an explicit relation between the \(N=0\) Fourier coefficient \(w_0(\vec{x})\) and the damping factor \(D_{m,\beta}(\vec{x})\) of a massive particle state. This relation allows direct extraction of the damping factor from either position-space or momentum-space Euclidean data.

## Key numbers
- The Euclidean two-point function is \(\beta\)-periodic, where \(\beta = 1/T\) is the inverse temperature, and is expanded in a Fourier series with Matsubara frequencies \(\omega_N = 2\pi N / \beta\).
- The \(N=0\) Fourier coefficient \(w_0(\vec{x})\) is related to the damping factor by \(D_{m,\beta}(\vec{x}) \sim 4\pi |\vec{x}|\, e^{|\vec{x}|m} w_0(\vec{x})\), where \(m\) is the zero-temperature particle mass.
- In the zero-temperature limit \(\beta \rightarrow \infty\), the thermal spectral density reduces to \(\widetilde{D}_\beta(\vec{u},s) \rightarrow (2\pi)^3 \delta^3(\vec{u})\, \rho(s)\), recovering the standard Källén-Lehmann representation.

## Context
This work extends the axiomatic thermal QFT framework developed by Bros and collaborators (Bros 1992, Bros 1995, Bros 1998, Bros 1996, Bros 2001), who first derived the spectral representation of the thermal commutator using locality. The paper directly addresses the inverse problem that has plagued lattice QCD calculations since Karsch (1986), where spectral information must be extracted from Euclidean data via non-unique inversion methods such as the Maximum Entropy Method (Jarrell 1996, Asakawa 2000, Nakahara 1999). The new approach avoids this inversion entirely for theories with a sufficiently large mass gap, enabling direct calculation of in-medium damping factors and observables such as shear viscosity from Euclidean data generated by functional methods like the functional renormalisation group or Dyson-Schwinger equations.
