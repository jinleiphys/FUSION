# Effect of longitudinal fluctuation in event-by-event (3+1)D hydrodynamics

> Hadron spectra and elliptic flow in relativistic heavy-ion collisions are studied in event-by-event (3+1)D ideal hydrodynamic simulations with fluctuating initial conditions given by the AMPT Monte Carlo model. Both the coherent soft gluon production from wounded nucleons and incoherent mini-jet production in semi-hard parton scatterings are considered. These partons take part in parton cascade and are assumed to be locally thermalized. They provide the fluctuating initial conditions for hydrodynamic simulations with a Gaussian smearing. Effects of both transverse and longitudinal fluctuations are studied. The initial fluctuations along rapidity direction lead to expanding hot spots in longitudinal direction, which will reduce elliptic flow and the yield of particles at high transverse momentum. The intrinsic correlation introduced in the fluctuating initial conditions is also found to influence the di-hadron correlation of the final hadrons.

## Key claim
Longitudinal fluctuations in the initial energy density and flow velocity reduce elliptic flow at high transverse momentum and suppress high-pT particle yields, compared to event-by-event tube-like initial conditions that lack such longitudinal structure. The intrinsic correlations in rapidity introduced by the AMPT initial conditions are necessary to produce a near-side peak in di-hadron correlations, which smooth tube-like initial conditions fail to generate.

## Method
The authors performed event-by-event (3+1)D ideal hydrodynamic simulations using initial conditions from the AMPT Monte Carlo model, which includes both soft gluon production from wounded nucleons and incoherent mini-jet production from semi-hard parton scatterings. The local energy-momentum tensor was constructed by Gaussian smearing of parton 4-momenta in transverse and longitudinal (spatial rapidity) directions with σ_r = 0.6 fm and σ_ηs = 0.6. To isolate longitudinal fluctuation effects, they compared full AMPT initial conditions against two smoothed versions: event-by-event tube-like initial conditions (same transverse fluctuations but smooth longitudinal profile) and one-shot-tube initial conditions (averaged over many events with rotation to a common participant plane).

## Key numbers
- Initial time τ₀ = 0.4 fm and normalization factor K = 1.45 used for Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV
- Gaussian smearing parameters: σ_r = 0.6 fm (transverse), σ_ηs = 0.6 (longitudinal spatial rapidity)
- Centrality studied: 30–40% semi-central Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV
- Transverse momentum range for di-hadron correlation trigger and associate particles: 2 < p_T < 3 GeV/c

## Context
This work extends event-by-event hydrodynamic studies beyond the (2+1)D approach of Qiu (2011) and the (3+1)D simulations of Schenke (2010) by incorporating longitudinal fluctuations from the AMPT model. The finding that longitudinal fluctuations reduce elliptic flow at high p_T provides a more stringent constraint on extracting the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (η/s) from comparisons with experimental data. The demonstration that intrinsic longitudinal correlations are required to produce near-side di-hadron correlation peaks opens the door to using such correlations as a probe of initial-state rapidity structure in heavy-ion collisions.
