# Exclusive vector-toponium photoproduction in hadronic collisions

> An exploratory study of the exclusive production of a vector - toponium $ψ_t$ state by photon - hadron interactions is performed considering proton - proton and proton - nucleus collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) and Future Circular Collider (FCC) energies. The scattering amplitude is calculated using the $k_T$ - factorization formalism assuming that the vector - toponium state can be described by a Gaussian light-cone wave function and considering different models for the unintegrated gluon distribution. Predictions for the rapidity distributions and total cross - sections are presented. Our results indicate that the experimental measurement of this final state will be very difficult for the expected integrated luminosities at the LHC and FCC.

## Key claim
The exclusive photoproduction of a vector toponium state in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions at the LHC and FCC is theoretically possible, but the predicted cross-sections are of order few ab at the LHC and tens of ab at the FCC, which combined with the small branching fraction into dileptons (order 10^{-5}) makes experimental measurement "very difficult" and "a very hard task", the authors explicitly state their predictions are optimistic upper bounds due to assuming a slope parameter B = 1 GeV^{-2}.

## Method
The scattering amplitude for exclusive vector-toponium photoproduction was calculated using the k_T-factorization formalism, with the toponium state described by a Gaussian light-cone wave function whose parameters were fixed using the radial wave function at the origin from Bai (2025) and the resulting electronic decay width of 9.962 keV. The cross-section was computed for proton-proton and proton-lead collisions at LHC (14 TeV pp, 8.16 TeV pPb) and FCC (100 TeV pp, 63 TeV pPb) energies, using two different parameterizations for the unintegrated gluon distribution (HERA I+II and CCFM) and the equivalent photon approximation for the photon flux.

## Key numbers
- Top quark mass assumed: m_t = 172.4 GeV; vector toponium mass: M_ψt = 342.1 GeV
- Predicted electronic decay width of ψt: Γ(ψt → e+e-) = 9.962 keV
- For pp collisions at √s = 14 TeV (LHC), total cross-section: 6.789 ab (CCFM) and 0.8712 ab (HERA I+II) for central rapidity range (-2.5 ≤ Y ≤ 2.5)
- For pp collisions at √s = 100 TeV (FCC), total cross-section: 83.40 ab (CCFM) and 16.55 ab (HERA I+II) for central rapidity range
- For pPb collisions at √s = 8.16 TeV (LHC), total cross-section: 349.8 ab (CCFM) and 29.63 ab (HERA I+II) for central rapidity range
- For pPb collisions at √s = 63 TeV (FCC), total cross-section: 35111.4 ab (CCFM) and 5314.9 ab (HERA I+II) for central rapidity range
- Branching fraction of vector toponium into dileptons: order 10^{-5} (from Bai 2025)

## Context
This exploratory study extends the recent observation of pseudoscalar toponium (ηt) events at the LHC by ATLAS (2019) and CMS (2025) to the vector toponium (ψt) case, which cannot be produced via gluon-gluon fusion due to the Landau-Yang theorem. It builds on the k_T-factorization formalism previously applied to J/ψ and Υ photoproduction by Ivanov (2004) and Cisek (2014), and uses the radial wave function from Bai (2025) who studied inclusive ψt production. The results suggest that while exclusive photoproduction offers a cleaner environment than inclusive channels, the extremely small cross-sections and branching fractions make detection at the LHC and FCC very challenging, motivating further studies with alternative event separation strategies.
