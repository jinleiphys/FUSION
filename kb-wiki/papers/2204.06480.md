# Benchmarking CASPT3 Vertical Excitation Energies

> Based on 280 reference vertical transition energies of various natures (singlet, triplet, valence, Rydberg, $n\toπ^*$, $π\toπ^*$, and double excitations) extracted from the QUEST database, we assess the accuracy of third-order multireference perturbation theory, CASPT3, in the context of molecular excited states. When one applies the disputable ionization-potential-electron-affinity (IPEA) shift, we show that CASPT3 provides a similar accuracy as its second-order counterpart, CASPT2, with the same mean absolute error of $0.11$ eV. However, as already reported, we also observe that the accuracy of CASPT3 is almost insensitive to the IPEA shift, irrespective of the transition type and system size, with a small reduction of the mean absolute error to $0.09$ eV when the IPEA shift is switched off.

## Key claim

CASPT3 provides essentially the same accuracy as CASPT2 when the IPEA shift is applied (both yield a mean absolute error of 0.11 eV), but CASPT3 is almost insensitive to the IPEA shift, unlike CASPT2; switching off the IPEA shift reduces the CASPT3 mean absolute error to 0.09 eV, whereas for CASPT2 the error increases to 0.27 eV without the shift.

## Method

The authors computed single-state CASPT2 and CASPT3 vertical excitation energies using the aug-cc-pVTZ basis set for 280 transitions (singlet, triplet, valence, Rydberg, n→π*, π→π*, and double excitations) in 35 small- to medium-sized organic molecules from the QUEST database. All calculations were performed in MOLPRO using the RS2 and RS3 contraction schemes, with a level shift of 0.3 hartree applied systematically to mitigate intruder state problems, and both methods were tested with and without the IPEA shift (default value 0.25 hartree). The reference theoretical best estimates (TBEs) were taken from the QUEST database, and statistical analysis was performed on the 265 "safe" TBEs (absolute error below 0.043 eV).

## Key numbers

- CASPT3 with IPEA shift: mean absolute error (MAE) of 0.11 eV over 265 safe transitions, identical to CASPT2 with IPEA shift
- CASPT3 without IPEA shift: MAE of 0.09 eV, a reduction compared to the IPEA-shifted value
- CASPT2 without IPEA shift: MAE of 0.27 eV, showing strong sensitivity to the IPEA shift
- All calculations used the aug-cc-pVTZ basis set and a level shift of 0.3 hartree
- The IPEA shift default value was 0.25 hartree

## Context

This work extends the benchmark of Sarkar (2022) on CASPT2 and NEVPT2 for the same set of 280 vertical excitation energies from the QUEST database, providing the first comprehensive assessment of CASPT3 for molecular excited states. The finding that CASPT3 accuracy is nearly independent of the IPEA shift contrasts with CASPT2, where the shift is crucial (Zobel 2017, Sarkar 2022). The results enable future work to consider CASPT3 without the IPEA shift as a reliable alternative, and suggest that third-order multireference perturbation theory may offer more robust performance than its second-order counterpart for excited-state calculations.
