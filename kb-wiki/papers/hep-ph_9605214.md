# pQCD-based Approach to Parton Production and Equilibration in High-Energy Nuclear Collisions

> A pQCD-based model for parton production and equilibration in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions is reviewed. The model combines pQCD processes including initial and final state radiations together with string phenomenology for nonperturbative soft processes. Nuclear effects on the initial parton production, such as multiple parton scattering and nuclear shadowing of parton distribution functions are considered. Comparisons with existing data are made and further tests of the model to constrain model parameters are proposed. With the obtained space-time history of the parton production, evolution of the minijet gas toward a fully equilibrated parton plasma is studied. Direct probes of the early parton dynamics, such as pre-equilibrium photon and dilepton production, open charm production, $J/ψ$ suppressionand jet quenching are also reviewed.

# pQCD-based Approach to Parton Production and Equilibration in High-Energy Nuclear Collisions

## Key claim

The paper argues that at collider energies above √s ≈ 100 AGeV, hard and semihard parton scatterings calculable via perturbative QCD become dominant over soft interactions, and that the initially produced dense partonic system can evolve toward a fully equilibrated quark-gluon plasma through further parton interactions, with the caveat that the separation between hard and soft physics depends on a phenomenological cutoff parameter p₀ whose value is subject to considerable controversy and must be bounded from below by experimental total cross section data.

## Method

The author reviews a pQCD-based model that combines perturbative QCD processes (including initial and final state radiations) with string phenomenology for nonperturbative soft interactions, implemented in the HIJING Monte Carlo generator. The model uses an eikonal formalism with Pomeron exchange to unitarize cross sections, decomposes the total inclusive quark-quark cross section into a soft component σ_soft and an inclusive jet cross section σ_jet with a transverse momentum cutoff p₀ ≈ 2 GeV/c, and treats multiple parton scatterings as independent cut-Pomerons. Nuclear effects including multiple parton scattering and nuclear shadowing of parton distribution functions are incorporated, and the space-time evolution of the minijet gas toward equilibration is studied using pQCD with the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect on induced gluon radiation.

## Key numbers

- The transverse momentum cutoff separating hard and soft processes is taken as p₀ ≈ 2 GeV/c, with a lower bound of p₀ = 1.2 GeV determined by fitting total cross section data
- Multiple minijet production is estimated to produce up to 50% of the transverse energy per unit rapidity at RHIC (√s = 200 AGeV) and up to 80% at LHC (√s = 5.5 ATeV) for heavy nuclei collisions
- The soft cross section is taken as σ_soft = 57 mb, assumed constant at high energies
- The nucleon radius is taken as R_N ≈ 0.85 fm, with the overlap function parameter μ₀ = 3.9
- The independence condition for multiple hard scatterings in nuclear collisions gives σ_jet ≲ 180 mb for A = 197 (using the relation σ_jet ≲ 2 σ_max/A^{2/3})
- The inclusive jet cross section σ_jet is calculated with a K factor ≈ 2 to correct lowest order pQCD for next-to-leading order effects

## Context

This review extends the author's earlier work developing the HIJING model (Wang and Gyulassy, 1991) and builds on the framework of multiple parton production estimates by Kajantie, Landshoff, and Lindfors (1987). It contrasts with purely hadronic models like RQMD and ARC (which describe AGS energies) and string models like FRITIOF, DPM, ATTILA, and VENUS (which describe CERN SPS energies), by incorporating pQCD hard processes alongside soft interactions. The work enables quantitative studies of pre-equilibrium photon and dilepton production, open charm production, J/ψ suppression, and jet quenching as direct probes of early parton dynamics and quark-gluon plasma formation at RHIC and LHC energies.
