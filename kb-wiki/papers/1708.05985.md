# Non-radial Oscillation Modes of Compact Stars with a Crust

> Oscillation modes of isolated compact stars can, in principle, be a fingerprint of the equation of state (EoS) of dense matter. We study the non-radial high-frequency l=2 spheroidal modes of neutron stars and strange quark stars, adopting a two-component model (core and crust) for these two types of stars. Using perturbed fluid equations in the relativistic Cowling approximation, we explore the effect of a strangelet or hadronic crust on the oscillation modes of strange stars. The results differ from the case of neutron stars with a crust. In comparison to fluid-only configurations, we find that a solid crust on top of a neutron star increases the p-mode frequency slightly with little effect on the f-mode frequency, whereas for strange stars, a strangelet crust on top of a quark core significantly increases the f-mode frequency with little effect on the p-mode frequency.

# Non-radial Oscillation Modes of Compact Stars with a Crust

## Key claim
The effect of a solid crust on oscillation modes differs fundamentally between neutron stars and strange quark stars: for neutron stars, a solid BPS crust increases the p-mode frequency slightly with little effect on the f-mode frequency, whereas for strange stars, a strangelet crust significantly increases the f-mode frequency (by about 150-200 Hz) with little effect on the p-mode frequency. This result is conditional on the use of the relativistic Cowling approximation and a two-component (core and crust) stellar model.

## Method
The authors solved the perturbed fluid equations in the relativistic Cowling approximation (neglecting gravitational back-reaction) for l=2 spheroidal modes of neutron stars and strange quark stars. They constructed two-component stellar models using a parameterized SLy equation of state for neutron stars with a BPS crust, and an interacting Bag model equation of state for strange stars with a strangelet crust (a mixed phase of positively charged strangelets and electrons). The shear modulus for the neutron star crust was computed using the Strohmayer formula, while for the strangelet crust it was derived from the strangelet properties. The eigenfrequencies were obtained by solving the coupled fluid and crust perturbation equations with continuity conditions at the core-crust interface.

## Key numbers
- For hadronic stars with SLy EoS, f-mode frequencies lie in the range 2.45-2.90 kHz as mass changes from 1.4 to 2.0 solar masses
- For strange stars with a strangelet crust, the f-mode frequency shows a large upward shift of 150-200 Hz compared to the homogeneous (bare) strange star case across the entire mass range up to maximum mass
- Strangelet crust parameters for one model (mu_crit=280 MeV, m_s=100 MeV): crust thickness 23.01 m, strangelet size 8.32 fm, shear modulus 1.04 keV/fm^3, stellar mass 2.236 solar masses, radius 12.327 km
- Strangelet crust parameters for another model (mu_crit=300 MeV, m_s=200 MeV): crust thickness 236.66 m, strangelet size 7.87 fm, shear modulus 1699 keV/fm^3, stellar mass 1.140 solar masses, radius 7.895 km
- For hadronic stars with SLy EoS, p-mode frequencies are slightly higher with a solid BPS crust than without, across the mass range 1.0-2.0 solar masses

## Context
This work extends previous studies of non-radial oscillation modes in compact stars, particularly the comparison of f, p, and g modes for hadronic, strange, and hybrid stars by Flores and Lugones (2013). It differs from earlier works such as McDermott (1988) and Strohmayer, which used Newtonian approximations and older equation of state parameterizations, by including relativistic effects and a modern treatment of the crust. The results enable future calculations of damping times and gravitational wave templates for pulsating compact stars, and suggest that gravitational wave observations could potentially distinguish between neutron stars and strange quark stars even when the latter are enveloped by a crust.
