# Low-energy interaction of composite spin-half systems with scalar and vector fields

> We consider a composite spin-half particle moving in spatially-varying scalar and vector fields. The vector field is assumed to couple to a conserved charge, but no assumption is made about either the structure of the composite or its coupling to the scalar field. A general form for the piece of the spin-orbit interaction of the composite with the scalar and vector fields which is first-order in momentum transfer ${\bf Q}$ and second-order in the fields is derived.

## Key claim

The paper derives a model-independent expression for the spin-orbit interaction of a composite spin-half particle with external scalar and vector fields, correct to first order in momentum transfer and second order in the fields, under the sole assumption that the vector field couples to a conserved charge, without any assumption about the composite's internal structure or its coupling to the scalar field.

## Method

The authors use a quantum-field-theoretic analysis of a generic composite spin-half system, constructing the composite's Green's function and expanding it in powers of external scalar and vector fields. They show that in the low-energy limit of slowly-varying fields, the second-order scattering amplitude can be evaluated with one field insertion carrying the total momentum transfer and the other at zero momentum, and then relate this amplitude to derivatives of the first-order vertex with respect to a constant scalar field using identities for the Green's function. The irreducible second-order potential is obtained by subtracting the iterated on-shell first-order interaction.

## Key numbers

- The forward scattering amplitude at zero momentum transfer reproduces the result of Birse (1995): \(\overline{{\cal V}}^{(2)}({\bf p}+{\bf Q}/2,{\bf p}-{\bf Q}/2) = \delta^{(3)}(Q) \frac{S^2}{2} \left. \frac{d^2 \epsilon_{\sigma}({\bf p})}{d \sigma^2} \right|_{\sigma=0}\), which expands to \(\frac{S^2}{2} \frac{M}{\epsilon({\bf p})} \frac{d^2M}{d \sigma ^2} + \frac{S^2 {\bf p}^2}{2 \epsilon ^3 ({\bf p})} \left( \frac{dM}{d \sigma} \right)^2\).
- The first-order spin-orbit interaction for the simple mass-shift case is given by \(V^{(1)}_{SO} = \left(S - V - V \kappa \frac{\epsilon({\bf p}) + M}{2 M}\right) \frac{i}{2 \epsilon({\bf p})}\frac{1}{\epsilon({\bf p}) + M} {\bf \sigma} \cdot ({\bf p} \times {\bf Q})\), where \(\kappa\) is the anomalous magnetic moment.
- In nucleon-nucleus scattering at nuclear matter density, the linear terms in \(S\) and \(V\) largely cancel, leaving a net interaction of about 50 MeV of attraction, while the scalar attraction is roughly 300 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the model-independent analysis of Birse (1995) for spatially-uniform fields to the case of spatially-varying fields, and generalizes the specific composite model calculations of Wallace (1994, 1995) which assumed a simple mass shift and a scalar field coupling to the dilatation current. The result shows that a composite system interacts at low momentum transfer like a Dirac particle whose mass and anomalous magnetic moment are functions of the external scalar field, confirming that the quadratic-in-\(S\) term arising from z-graphs in Dirac phenomenology is not suppressed by compositeness. This enables model-independent predictions for spin-orbit forces in relativistic descriptions of nucleon-nucleus scattering, where the cancellation in the central force and reinforcement in the spin-orbit force is a key ingredient of Dirac phenomenology (Wallace 1987).
