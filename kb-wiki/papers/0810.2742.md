# Mass and chemical asymmetry in QCD matter

> We consider two-flavor asymmetric QCD combined with a low-energy effective model inspired by chiral perturbation theory and lattice data to investigate the effects of masses, isospin and baryon number on the pressure and the deconfinement phase transition. Remarkable agreement with lattice results is found for the critical temperature behavior. Further analyses of the cold, dense case and the influence of quark mass asymmetry are also presented.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a simple effective model combining chiral perturbation theory and a fuzzy bag model reproduces lattice QCD results for the critical temperature of the deconfining phase transition as a function of isospin chemical potential and pion mass, but only within the model's domain of validity where the low-energy sector is based on leading-order chiral perturbation theory.

## Method

The authors constructed an effective model with two sectors: a low-energy regime described by a chiral perturbation theory-inspired model of dressed pions and nucleons with effective dispersion relations, and a high-energy regime described by 2-flavor perturbative QCD with non-perturbative corrections from the fuzzy bag model. They computed the free gas pressure in both sectors at finite temperature, isospin chemical potential, and baryon chemical potential, then extracted the critical temperature and chemical potential for the deconfining phase transition by maximizing the total pressure.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature as a function of isospin chemical potential shows agreement with lattice data for vacuum pion masses down to 25 MeV, with the fuzzy bag model outperforming the usual bag model (bag model curve for m_pi=25 MeV lies between fuzzy model curves for m_pi=400 MeV and m_pi=600 MeV)
- The critical temperature shows approximate mass independence for pion masses up to at least 600 MeV, consistent with lattice data from Karsch (2000) where the string tension sqrt(sigma)=425 MeV
- For baryon chemical potential mu_B <= 100 MeV, the effect on critical temperature is completely imperceptible
- The critical baryon chemical potential increases with vacuum pion mass, and finite small isospin density decreases the critical baryon chemical potential
- Finite quark mass difference (delta m) increases the critical temperature by a quantitatively small amount

## Context

This work extends earlier effective model approaches by Fraga (2008) and provides a systematic investigation of mass and isospin effects on the QCD phase diagram. The model successfully reproduces lattice results from de Forcrand (2007) for the critical temperature as a function of isospin chemical potential, and from Karsch (2000) for the pion mass dependence, where previous chiral field approaches failed to capture the approximate mass invariance of the critical temperature. The results for cold, dense matter can inform studies of compact stars and color superconductivity, as discussed by Glendenning (1997).
