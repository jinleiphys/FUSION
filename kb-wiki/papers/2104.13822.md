# The sound speed and core of massive compact stars: A manifestation of hadron-quark duality

> When baryon-quark continuity is formulated in terms of a topology change without invoking "explicit " QCD degrees of freedom at a density higher than twice the nuclear matter density $n_0$ the core of massive compact stars can be described in terms of fractionally charged particles, behaving neither like pure baryons nor deconfined quarks. Hidden symmetries, both local gauge and pseudo-conformal (or broken scale), lead to the pseudo-conformal (PC) sound velocity $v_{pcs}^2/c^2\approx 1/3$ at $\gsim 3n_0$ in compact stars. We argue these symmetries are "emergent" from strong nuclear correlations and conjecture that they reflect hidden symmetries in QCD proper exposed by nuclear correlations. We establish a possible link between the quenching of $g_A$ in superallowed Gamow-Teller transitions in nuclei and the precocious onset at $n\gsim 3n_0$ of the PC sound velocity predicted at the dilaton limit fixed point. We propose that bringing in explicit quark degrees of freedom as is done in terms of the "quarkyonic" and other hybrid hadron-quark structure and our topology-change strategy represent the "hadron-quark duality" formulated in terms of the Cheshire-Cat mechanism~\cite{CC} for the smooth cross-over between hadrons and quarks. Confrontation with currently available experimental observations is discussed to support this notion.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the core of massive compact stars can be described by fractionally charged particles behaving neither like pure baryons nor deconfined quarks, with the pseudo-conformal sound velocity \(v_{pcs}^2/c^2\approx 1/3\) emerging at densities \(\gtrsim 3n_0\) due to hidden symmetries that are "emergent" from strong nuclear correlations rather than explicitly present in QCD at low densities.

## Method

The authors formulate a generalized nuclear effective field theory (GenEFT) based on a scale-symmetric hidden local symmetry Lagrangian (\(\chi\)bHLS) that incorporates baryons, pions, vector mesons (\(\rho,\omega\)), and a dilaton field \(\chi\) identified with the \(f_0(500)\) scalar. They map the topology change from skyrmions to half-skyrmions at a density \(n_{1/2}\) onto the parameters of this Lagrangian, then apply a Wilsonian renormalization-group approach to reach Landau Fermi-liquid fixed-point theory, using \(V_{lowk}\) RG to go beyond the fixed point. The equation of state is then solved via the TOV equations to compute compact star properties.

## Key numbers

- The topology change density is taken as \(n_{1/2}=2.5n_0\) for illustration, with the allowed range \(2\lesssim n_{1/2}/n_0 \lesssim 4\).
- For \(n_{1/2}=2.5n_0\), the maximum star mass is \(M_{max}\sim 2.0 M_\odot\) with central density \(\sim 5n_0\).
- For a \(1.4 M_\odot\) neutron star: dimensionless tidal deformability \(\Lambda_{1.4} \approx 656\) and radius \(R_{1.4} \approx 12.8\) km.
- For \(n_{1/2}=4.0n_0\), the maximum star mass reaches \(M_{max}\simeq 2.25 M_\odot\).
- The sound velocity converges to \(v_{pcs}^2/c^2\approx 1/3\) at \(n\gtrsim 3n_0\), with the trace of the energy-momentum tensor becoming density-independent for \(n\gtrsim n_{1/2}\).
- The quenching factor from nuclear correlations in Gamow-Teller transitions is \(q_{snc}=0.78(1\pm 20\%)\), giving \(g_A^{\rm Landau}=1.0\pm 0.2\).
- Nuclear matter predictions for \(n_{1/2}=2.5n_0\): \(n_0=0.161\) fm\(^{-3}\), binding energy \(16.7\) MeV, symmetry energy \(E_{sym}(n_0)=30.2\) MeV, \(E_{sym}(2n_0)=56.4\) MeV, \(L(n_0)=67.8\) MeV, \(K_0=250.0\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous development of the \(\chi\)bHLS approach (Ma and Rho, 2020) and the pseudo-conformal sound velocity prediction (Paeng et al., 2017). It contrasts with standard chiral effective field theory (\(s\chi\)EFT) which breaks down at high densities, and with hybrid hadron-quark models such as "quarkyonic" matter. The paper establishes a link between the quenching of \(g_A\) in superallowed Gamow-Teller transitions in nuclei (observed in \(^{100}\)Sn experiments at GSI and RHIC) and the precocious onset of the pseudo-conformal sound velocity, suggesting that both phenomena reflect emergent scale symmetry. The approach enables predictions for neutron star structure that can be tested against gravitational wave observations and future measurements of the maximum neutron star mass and tidal deformability.
