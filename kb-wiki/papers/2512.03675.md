# Initial state and evolution of hot and dense medium produced in isobaric collisions at 200A GeV at RHIC

> Isobaric collisions provide a unique opportunity to investigate how variations in the charge to mass ratio affect the final state observables produced in relativistic heavy ion collisions. Most importantly, isobaric systems that differ in their nuclear structure offer valuable insights into the underlying nuclear geometries, making them powerful tools to probe the role of nuclear structure using heavy ion collisions. We study the initial state and evolution of the hot and dense medium formed in Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions at 200A GeV at RHIC using a relativistic hydrodynamical model. The initial geometry of the two isobaric collisions is found to influence the evolution of the hot and dense medium produced. The sensitivity of photon production, charged particle spectra and anisotropic flow coefficients ($v_n$) to the initial geometry, including different orientations of the isobaric set have been studied in detail. Significant variations in anisotropic flow of photons and hadrons are observed, highlighting the role of nuclear deformation in shaping final state observables. Moreover, photon anisotropic flow is found to be considerably more sensitive to the initial state than charged particle anisotropic flow, indicating that photon measurements in isobaric collisions have strong potential to constrain initial state modeling and improve our understanding of QGP properties in such systems.

# Initial state and evolution of hot and dense medium produced in isobaric collisions at 200A GeV at RHIC

## Key claim

The initial nuclear deformation differences between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions at 200A GeV at RHIC produce measurable variations in anisotropic flow of both hadrons and thermal photons, with photon anisotropic flow showing considerably stronger sensitivity to the initial state than charged particle anisotropic flow, suggesting that photon measurements in isobaric collisions can constrain initial state modeling of the quark-gluon plasma. However, the study uses ideal hydrodynamics with a single set of deformation parameters and smooth initial conditions, so the results are qualitative demonstrations rather than quantitative predictions.

## Method

The authors used the MUSIC relativistic hydrodynamical model with Glauber model initial conditions to simulate the evolution of the hot and dense medium produced in Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions at 200A GeV at RHIC. They considered two extreme collision orientations (tip-tip and body-body) for the most central collisions, using a two-parameter Woods-Saxon nuclear density profile with quadrupole deformation (beta2 = 0.162 for Ru, beta2 = 0.06 for Zr) and octupole deformation (beta3 = 0.20 for Zr only). Thermal photon production was calculated by integrating leading-order emission rates from the QGP phase and parameterized rates from the hadronic phase over the entire space-time evolution, with an initial formation time tau0 of 0.4 fm/c and a freeze-out temperature of 137 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Central energy density for Ru+Ru tip-tip: 43.28 GeV/fm^3; for Ru+Ru body-body: 34.79 GeV/fm^3
- Central energy density for Zr+Zr tip-tip: 38.02 GeV/fm^3; for Zr+Zr body-body: 37.21 GeV/fm^3
- Initial elliptic eccentricity epsilon2 for Ru+Ru body-body: 0.146; for Zr+Zr body-body: 0.062
- Initial triangular eccentricity epsilon3 for Zr+Zr body-body: 0.158; for Ru+Ru body-body: 0
- Initial average temperature range: 330 to 350 MeV across all configurations
- Initial average temperature for Ru+Ru tip-tip: 350 MeV; for Ru+Ru body-body: 333 MeV
- Initial average temperature for Zr+Zr tip-tip: 337 MeV; for Zr+Zr body-body: 336 MeV
- Total charged particle multiplicity range for most central collisions: 320 to 338
- Freeze-out temperature: 137 MeV (also tested at 150 MeV)
- Initial formation time: 0.4 fm/c (also tested at 1.0 fm/c)
- Nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross section: 42 mb
- Nuclear radius for Ru: 5.09 fm; for Zr: 5.02 fm
- Surface diffuseness for Ru: 0.46 fm; for Zr: 0.52 fm

## Context

This work extends the study of isobaric collisions at RHIC, which were originally motivated by searches for the chiral magnetic effect (CME) as proposed by M. Adallah (2022), to investigate how nuclear deformation affects QGP evolution and final state observables. The authors build on prior studies of nuclear structure in isobaric systems by G. Giacalone (2021), C. Zhang (2022), S. Zhao (2023), and J. Wang (2024), who reported deformation parameters for Ru and Zr. The finding that photon anisotropic flow is more sensitive to initial conditions than hadronic flow addresses the longstanding "direct photon puzzle" discussed in the literature, and the authors suggest that the ratio of photon anisotropic flow parameters (v2/v3) as a function of pT could serve as a clean probe for initial state properties, following work on this ratio by previous authors. A more realistic calculation incorporating event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions and viscosity effects is deferred to a future study.
