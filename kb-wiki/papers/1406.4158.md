# Resonances in coupled πK,ηK scattering from quantum chromodynamics

> Using first-principles calculation within Quantum Chromodynamics, we are able to determine a pattern of strangeness=1 resonances which appear as complex singularities within coupled $πK$, $ηK$ scattering amplitudes. We make use of numerical computation in the lattice discretized approach to the quantum field theory with light quark masses corresponding to $m_π\sim 400\, \textrm{MeV}$ and at a single lattice spacing. The energy dependence of scattering amplitudes is extracted through their relationship to the discrete spectrum in a finite-volume, which we map out in unprecedented detail.

## Key claim

Using lattice QCD with pion mass ~400 MeV and a single lattice spacing, the authors extract coupled-channel πK, ηK scattering amplitudes and find resonance poles whose qualitative pattern resembles experiment, but with the vector K* becoming a bound state rather than a resonance due to the heavier-than-physical light quark masses.

## Method

The authors compute discrete finite-volume spectra from lattice QCD correlation matrices built from single-meson and meson-meson operators at three spatial volumes (16³, 20³, 24³). They then parameterize the energy dependence of the coupled-channel t-matrix using a K-matrix with poles and polynomial terms, and fit the parameters to 80 energy levels for J^P=0⁺, 19 levels for 1⁻, and 24 levels for 2⁺. The resulting amplitudes are analytically continued to complex energies to locate resonance poles.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: 391 MeV; kaon mass: 549 MeV; eta mass: 589 MeV (heavier than physical values)
- Spatial lattice spacing: a_s ~ 0.12 fm; volumes: 16³ (~2 fm), 20³ (~2.5 fm), 24³ (~3 fm)
- Vector (1⁻) bound-state mass: m_R = 933(1) MeV; Breit-Wigner coupling: g_R = 5.93(26)
- 80 energy levels from 20 irreps used to constrain 0⁺ amplitudes from below πK threshold up to 1650 MeV; 19 levels for 1⁻ around πK threshold; 24 levels for 2⁺ between 1250 and 1700 MeV
- Experimental vector coupling for comparison: g_R^phys = 5.52(16)

## Context

This work extends the finite-volume formalism of Lüscher (1990) and its coupled-channel generalization by Guo (2012), He (2005), Hansen (2012), and Briceno (2012) to a first-principles QCD calculation of coupled πK, ηK scattering. Previous lattice studies by Fu (2011, 2012), Sasaki (2013), Lang (2012), and Prelovsek (2013) were limited to elastic πK scattering. The results enable future studies of more strongly coupled systems such as πη, KK̅ (the a₀(980) channel) and ππ, KK̅, ηη, and will require extension to three-hadron thresholds for quantitative comparison with experiment at physical quark masses.
