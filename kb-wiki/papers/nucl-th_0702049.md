# The Role of 5-quark Components on the Nucleon Form Factors

> The covariant quark model is shown to allow a phenomenological description of the neutron electric form factor, G_E^n(Q^2), in the impulse approximation, provided that the wave function contains minor (~ 3 %) admixtures of the lowest sea-quark configurations. While that form factor is not very sensitive to whether the \bar q in the qqqq\bar q component is in the P-state or in the S-state, the calculated nucleon magnetic form factors are much closer to the empirical values in the case of the former configuration. In the case of the electric form factor of the proton, G_E^p(Q^2), a zero appears in the impulse approximation close to 9 GeV^2, when the \bar q is in the P-state. That configuration, which may be interpreted as a pion loop ("cloud") fluctuation, also leads to a clearly better description of the nucleon magnetic moments. When the amplitude of the sea-quark admixtures are set so as to describe the electric form factor of the neutron, the qqqq\bar q admixtures have the phenomenologically desirable feature, that the electric form factor of the proton falls at a more rapid rate with momentum transfer than the magnetic form factor.

## Key claim

The covariant quark model can phenomenologically describe the neutron electric form factor in the impulse approximation only when the nucleon wave function contains minor (~3%) admixtures of the lowest sea-quark (qqqq\bar q) configurations, with the specific configuration where the antiquark is in the P-state (interpretable as a pion cloud fluctuation) being clearly preferred for describing the nucleon magnetic moments and magnetic form factors.

## Method

The authors extended the SU(2) constituent quark model by adding qqqq\bar q components to the nucleon wave function, considering two configurations: one with the antiquark in the S-state (requiring the qqqq subsystem to be in a P-state) and one with the antiquark in the P-state (interpretable as a pion loop fluctuation). They calculated the electromagnetic form factors in instant form kinematics using the impulse approximation, with the spatial wave functions having a simple algebraic form parameterized by two parameters (a and b) determined by fits to the electric form factors of the nucleons, and the amplitude of the qqqq\bar q component set by fitting the neutron electric form factor.

## Key numbers

- The qqqq\bar q component probability was set to ~3% based on the fit to the neutron electric form factor.
- The constituent quark mass used was 260 MeV, with wave function parameters b_3 = 310 MeV, b_5 = 315, a_3 = 3.9, and a_5 = 3.0.
- For the proton electric form factor, a zero appears in the impulse approximation between 10 and 11 GeV^2 when the antiquark is in the P-state.
- The calculated proton electric mean square radius was 0.736 fm^2 (S-state) and 0.730 fm^2 (P-state), compared to the experimental value of 0.774 fm^2.
- The calculated neutron electric mean square radius was -0.108 fm^2 (S-state) and -0.110 fm^2 (P-state), compared to the experimental value of -0.113 fm^2.
- The calculated proton magnetic moment was 2.97 μ_N (S-state) and 2.77 μ_N (P-state), compared to the experimental value of 2.79 μ_N.
- The calculated neutron magnetic moment was -2.56 μ_N (S-state) and -2.18 μ_N (P-state), compared to the experimental value of -1.91 μ_N.

## Context

This work extends the covariant quark model approach of Bruno (2002) by explicitly including sea-quark (qqqq\bar q) components that can be interpreted as meson cloud contributions, motivated by measurements of the \bar d/\bar u asymmetry in the nucleon sea from Towell (2001), the NA51 Collaboration, the NMC Collaboration, and the HERMES Collaboration. The results provide a non-exclusive alternative to the inclusion of mixed symmetry S-state and D-state components previously suggested by Bruno (2002) as partial explanations for the neutron electric form factor, and suggest that the best description may come from a combination of such effects with sea-quark components.
