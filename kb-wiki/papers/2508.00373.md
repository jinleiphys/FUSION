# Harmonic chiral vibration in triaxial nuclei

> The low spin states of chiral partner bands are described by means of harmonic oscillations of the total angular momentum vector. The validity of the adopted approximation is established in terms of triaxiality, quasiparticle alignments and the maximal total angular momentum. The spectral properties of the model are described in detail with experimental realizations presented for nuclei with large resultant quasiparticle alignments. The model provides a simple phenomenological way for the determination of the triaxial deformation from the experimental data and easily discernable observable signatures.

# Harmonic chiral vibration in triaxial nuclei

## Key claim
The low-spin states of chiral partner bands can be described as harmonic oscillations of the total angular momentum vector, but this harmonic approximation is valid only for nuclei with large resultant quasiparticle alignments and moderate triaxiality, and only for angular momenta below a critical spin where the approximation collapses.

## Method
The authors start from a particle rotor model Hamiltonian with two quasiparticle spins rigidly aligned along the 1 and 2 body-fixed axes. They apply a semiclassical time-dependent variational principle to obtain a classical energy function in terms of canonical variables (azimuth angle and angular momentum projection), then perform a harmonic expansion around the planar stationary point to derive an effective harmonic oscillator frequency. The resulting wave functions are used to compute electromagnetic transition probabilities, and the model is fitted to experimental energy splittings to extract the triaxial deformation parameter.

## Key numbers
- Critical spin for the planar-to-aplanar transition: \( I_c = \frac{\sqrt{A_1^2 j^2 (A_2 - A_3)^2 + A_2^2 j'^2 (A_1 - A_3)^2}}{(A_1 - A_3)(A_2 - A_3)} + \frac{1}{2} \), where \(A_k\) are inertial parameters and \(j, j'\) are quasiparticle spins.
- For \(\gamma = 90^\circ\), the chiral frequency has a closed form: \(\omega_I = \sqrt{2A_\perp \tilde{j} [2A_\perp \tilde{j} - (2I-1)(A_\perp - A_3)]}\), where \(\tilde{j} = \sqrt{j^2 + j'^2}\).
- The ratio \(R_I = \frac{1}{12} \left( \frac{\partial^4 \mathcal{H}_I}{\partial x^4} \right)_{0,\varphi_0} / \left( \frac{\partial^2 \mathcal{H}_I}{\partial x^2} \right)_{0,\varphi_0}\) quantifies the quality of the harmonic approximation; it shows slow linear increase at low spins and exponential increase near \(I_c\).
- Fitted triaxiality values from experimental data: \(^{103}\text{Rh}\), \(^{105}\text{Rh}\), \(^{107}\text{Ag}\), \(^{133}\text{Ce}\), \(^{135}\text{Nd}\), \(^{137}\text{Nd}\) (specific numerical values given in Fig. 5 of the paper).
- Empirical quadrupole moments used: \(Q_0 = 7.7\,e\,b\) for \(^{103}\text{Rh}\) and \(7.6\,e\,b\) for \(^{105}\text{Rh}\).

## Context
This work extends the concept of chiral vibration in triaxial nuclei, originally proposed by Frauendorf and Meng (1997) for odd-odd nuclei, by providing a harmonic oscillator description of the total angular momentum dynamics. It builds on previous semiclassical approaches (Budaca et al., various years) and the tilted axis cranking studies of harmonic chirality (Zhao et al., 2015; Chen et al., 2016). The model enables a simple phenomenological determination of triaxial deformation from experimental energy splittings and provides testable predictions for electromagnetic transition ratios, identifying \(^{105}\text{Rh}\) as the best candidate for a harmonic chiral vibrator. The connection to transverse wobbling is noted, with references to Frauendorf and Døssing (2014) and other wobbling studies.
