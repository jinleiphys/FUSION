# An algebraic model of baryon spectroscopy

> We discuss recent calculations of the mass spectrum, electromagnetic and strong couplings of baryon resonances. The calculations are done in a collective constituent model for the nucleon, in which the resonances are interpreted as rotations and vibrations of a symmetric top with a prescribed distribution of the charge and magnetization. We analyze recent data on eta-photo- and eta-electroproduction, and the tensor analyzing power in deuteron scattering.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a collective algebraic model, in which baryon resonances are interpreted as rotations and vibrations of a symmetric top, provides a good overall description of the mass spectrum, electromagnetic couplings, and strong couplings of nonstrange baryons, but the large observed η decay width of the N(1535)S11 resonance cannot be explained by a conventional three-quark (q³) state and may indicate the presence of other configurations.

## Method

The authors use a U(7) ⊗ SU_sf(6) ⊗ SU_c(3) algebraic framework where the spatial part of the baryon wave function is treated as a collective oblate symmetric top. The mass operator combines a Gürsey-Radicati spin-flavor term with vibrational and rotational terms linear in L, and the coefficients are determined by a simultaneous fit to all well-established three- and four-star nucleon and Δ resonances. Electromagnetic and strong decay widths are calculated using collective form factors obtained by folding U(7) dipole operators with a probability distribution g(β) = β² e^{-β/a} / 2a³, with the scale parameter a = 0.232 fm determined from a fit to nucleon charge radii and form factors.

## Key numbers

- The r.m.s. deviation of the mass fit for 24 resonances is δ_rms = 39 MeV.
- The scale parameter a for the collective form factors is 0.232 fm, determined from a simultaneous fit to proton and neutron charge radii and electric and magnetic form factors.
- The ratio of photocouplings for the N(1535)S11 resonance is A^n_{1/2}/A^p_{1/2} = -0.84 ± 0.15 (experimental, from Mukhopadhyay et al.) and -0.69 (collective model), or -0.81 with a mixing angle of θ = -38°.
- The ratio of proton photocouplings for the N(1520)D13 resonance is A^p_{3/2}/A^p_{1/2} = -2.5 ± 0.2 ± 0.4 (experimental, from Mukhopadhyay et al.) and -2.53 (collective model); a separate analysis by Tiator et al. gives -2.1 ± 0.2.
- The strong decay parameters are g = 1.164 GeV⁻¹ and h = -0.094 GeV⁻¹, determined from a least-square fit to Nπ partial widths excluding the N(1535)S11 and N(1650)S11 resonances.
- The calculated Nπ decay width for N(1535)S11 is 85 MeV (or 20 MeV with mixing angle θ = -38°), compared to the experimental value of 79 ± 38 MeV; the calculated Nη decay width is 0 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 74 ± 39 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the collective algebraic model of Bijker, Iachello, and Leviatan (1994) for baryon spectroscopy, applying it to new data on η-photo- and η-electroproduction and the tensor analyzing power in deuteron scattering. The results are compared with quark potential models such as those of Feynman, Kislinger, and Ravndal (1971), Koniuk and Isgur (1980), and Capstick (1992), as well as with the relativized quark model. The analysis enables further investigation of the nature of the N(1535) resonance, which the authors suggest may involve configurations beyond q³, such as quasi-bound meson-baryon states (Kaiser, 1995) or exotic q⁴q̄ states.
