# Correlation corrections to the thermodynamic properties of spin asymmetric QGP matter

> We calculate the free energy, entropy and pressure of the Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP) at finite temperature and density with a given fraction of spin-up and spin-down quarks using a MIT bag model with corrections up to ${\cal O} (g^4 \ln g^2)$. The expressions for the specific heat and the spin susceptibility are derived in terms of Fermi momentum and temperature. The effects of interaction between the quarks on the properties of the QGP phase are also investigated. Within our phenomenological model, we estimate the transition temperature $T_c$ by constructing the phase boundary between the hadronic phase and the QGP phase. Finally, we compute the equation of state of the QGP and its dependence on the temperature and the density.

## Key claim

The paper calculates thermodynamic properties of spin-asymmetric QGP using a MIT bag model with perturbative corrections up to O(g⁴ ln g²), finding that the unpolarized state is energetically favorable and that the critical temperature for the deconfinement transition lies between 130 and 170 MeV, consistent with lattice QCD results when the bag pressure is set to 442 MeV fm⁻³.

## Method

The author computes the free energy, entropy, pressure, specific heat, and spin susceptibility of QGP composed of massless up and down quarks with a given spin polarization parameter ξ, using a MIT bag model with one-gluon exchange interaction. The calculation includes kinetic, exchange, and correlation energy densities, where the correlation energy is obtained by summing bubble diagrams to yield the leading O(g⁴ ln g²) correction. The phase boundary between hadronic and QGP phases is constructed by equating pressures, and the equation of state is derived as a function of baryon density and temperature.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constant α_c = g²/(4π) = 0.2 used for numerical estimates
- Bag pressure B = 208 MeV fm⁻³ used for zero hadronic pressure
- Critical temperature T_c lies between 130 and 170 MeV for the deconfinement transition
- With bag pressure 442 MeV fm⁻³, the critical temperature T_c = 170 MeV matches lattice QCD results
- Free energy per baryon plotted at temperatures 30 MeV and 70 MeV
- Entropy per baryon plotted at baryon densities 0.2 fm⁻³ and 0.7 fm⁻³
- Specific heat per baryon shown at densities 0.2 fm⁻³ and 0.7 fm⁻³

## Context

This work extends previous studies of thermodynamic properties of unpolarized QGP matter using the MIT bag model with one-gluon exchange (Modarres 2008, 2013) and spin-polarized matter (Bordbar 2013) by including correlation corrections up to O(g⁴ ln g²). The calculation of correlation energy follows the method used for degenerate electron gas (Gell-Mann 1957, Pines 1958) and earlier work on spin-polarized quark matter (Pal 2013). The results enable comparison with lattice QCD predictions for the equation of state (Borsanyi 2010, 2011) and provide estimates for experimental signals of QGP formation in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
