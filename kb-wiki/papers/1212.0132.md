# Adiabatic Chemical Freeze-out and Wide Resonance Modification in a Thermal Medium

> Here we develop a model equation of state which successfully parameterizes the thermodynamic functions of hadron resonance gas model at chemical freeze-out and which allows us to naturally explain the adiabatic chemical freeze-out criterion. The present model enables us to clearly demonstrate that at chemical freeze-out the resulting hadronic mass spectrum used in the hadron resonance gas model is not an exponential-like, but a power-like. We argue that such a property of hadronic mass spectrum at chemical freeze-out can be explained by the two new effects found here for wide resonances existing in a thermal environment: the near threshold thermal resonance enhancement and the near threshold resonance sharpening. The effect of resonance sharpening is studied for a sigma meson and our analysis shows that for the temperatures well below 92 MeV the effective width of sigma meson is about 50 to 70 MeV. Thus, the effect of resonance sharpening justifies the usage of the sigma-like field-theoretical models for the strongly interacting matter equation of state at such temperatures. Also we argue that the most optimistic hope to find the quark gluon bags experimentally may be related to their sharpening and enhancement in a thermal medium. In this case the wide quark gluon bags may appear directly or in decays as narrow resonances that are absent in the tables of elementary particles and that have the width about 50-150 MeV and the mass about or above 2.5 GeV.

# Adiabatic Chemical Freeze-out and Wide Resonance Modification in a Thermal Medium

## Key claim

The authors develop a model equation of state that parameterizes the thermodynamic functions of the hadron resonance gas model at chemical freeze-out, demonstrating that the resulting hadronic mass spectrum is power-like rather than exponential-like, and that this behavior arises from two new effects for wide resonances in a thermal medium: near-threshold thermal resonance enhancement and near-threshold resonance sharpening. The adiabatic chemical freeze-out criterion (constant entropy per particle) is shown to be a natural consequence of the model, but the authors hedge that the simplified model omits excluded volume corrections, which could repair the description of baryon-antibaryon densities at the highest temperatures.

## Method

The authors parameterize the mesonic and baryonic pressure as power-law functions of temperature multiplied by exponentials of chemical potential and mass parameters, then fit these expressions to the particle densities obtained from a hadron resonance gas model analysis of experimental hadronic multiplicities at chemical freeze-out (Oliinychenko 2012). They extract the effective mass spectrum of baryons by inverting the Laplace transform of the baryon density, obtaining a power-law mass spectrum. They then analyze the Gaussian mass attenuation of resonances in a thermal medium, deriving analytic expressions for the effective resonance mass shift and effective width near threshold, and apply these to the sigma meson and several baryonic resonances.

## Key numbers

- The entropy per particle at chemical freeze-out deviates from 7.18 by about 8% over a center-of-mass energy range from sqrt(s_NN) = 2.2 GeV to sqrt(s_NN) = 7 TeV.
- The fitted baryon mass parameter M_B = 800.5 +/- 30 MeV; the meson mass parameter m_M - mu_M = -5 +/- 5 MeV.
- The fitted power indices are A_B = 6.097 +/- 0.38 for baryons and A_M = 5.31 +/- 0.14 for mesons.
- The effective width of the sigma meson at T = 50 MeV is 62.5 MeV, at T = 55 MeV is 71.5 MeV, and at T = 60 MeV is 82.5 MeV, for temperatures well below 92 MeV.
- The baryonic mass spectrum at chemical freeze-out behaves as rho_B(m) ~ m^{2.1} for large masses, while the experimental hadronic mass spectrum parameterization is rho_B^{exp}(m) ~ m^{5.72}.
- The threshold temperature for sigma meson sharpening is T_sigma^+ = 91.9 MeV.
- For quark-gluon bags with mass above 2.5 GeV, the estimated eigenlifetime at T = 85 +/- 5 MeV is tau_B <= 3.3 +/- 0.3 fm/c.
- The relative statistical probability of a quark-gluon bag of mass 2.5 GeV compared to a nucleon at T = 140 MeV is W_B ~ 3.85 x 10^{-4} to 10^{-3}, which is larger than the J/psi meson probability W_{J/psi} ~ 1.19 x 10^{-6} at the same temperature.

## Context

This work extends the hadron resonance gas model analysis of chemical freeze-out criteria by Oliinychenko (2012) and Andronic (2005, 2012), who found that none of the formerly suggested chemical freeze-out criteria are robust when realistic particle tables up to 2.5 GeV are used, and who proposed the adiabatic chemical freeze-out criterion of constant entropy per particle. The authors contrast their power-law mass spectrum with the traditional Hagedorn exponential mass spectrum (Hagedorn 1965-1970) and with the experimental mass spectrum parameterization of Hagedorn (2004). The finite width model for quark-gluon bags (FWM 2008, 2009) is used to predict that wide quark-gluon bags may appear as narrow resonances with width 50-150 MeV and mass above 2.5 GeV at NICA energy range, sharpened and enhanced in a thermal medium.
