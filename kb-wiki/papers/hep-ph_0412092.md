# Baryon masses and nucleon sigma terms in manifestly Lorentz-invariant baryon chiral perturbation theory

> We discuss the masses of the ground state baryon octet and the nucleon sigma terms in the framework of manifestly Lorentz-invariant baryon chiral perturbation theory. In order to obtain a consistent power counting for renormalized diagrams the extended on-mass-shell renormalization scheme is applied.

## Key claim

The extended on-mass-shell (EOMS) renormalization scheme, applied to manifestly Lorentz-invariant baryon chiral perturbation theory, yields a consistent power counting for baryon octet masses and nucleon sigma terms at one-loop order (O(q^3)), but the numerical results for the strangeness content and the eta-nucleon sigma term are extremely sensitive to the input value of the pion-nucleon sigma term, and a complete O(q^4) analysis is beyond the scope of the work.

## Method

The authors calculate the baryon octet self-energies up to O(q^3) using the EOMS renormalization scheme, which adds finite subtractions beyond the modified minimal subtraction (MS) scheme to restore power counting. The physical masses are obtained from the pole condition M_B - M_0 - Sigma_B(M_B) = 0, and the nucleon sigma terms are derived from the nucleon mass using the Hellmann-Feynman theorem. A least-squares fit to the mass equations and the pion-nucleon sigma term determines the low-energy constants M_0, b_0, b_D, and b_F, with partial estimates of O(q^4) loop contributions also provided.

## Key numbers

- For input (sigma_piN = 45 MeV, F_0 = 92.4 MeV): M_0 = 1039 MeV, b_0 = -0.833 GeV^{-1}, b_D = 0.0314 GeV^{-1}, b_F = -0.638 GeV^{-1}
- For input (sigma_piN = 40 MeV, F_0 = 92.4 MeV): M_0 = 1107 MeV, b_0 = -0.767 GeV^{-1}, b_D = 0.0314 GeV^{-1}, b_F = -0.638 GeV^{-1}
- For input (sigma_piN = 64 MeV, F_0 = 92.4 MeV): M_0 = 781 MeV, b_0 = -1.09 GeV^{-1}, b_D = 0.0314 GeV^{-1}, b_F = -0.638 GeV^{-1}
- Strangeness content y ranges from -0.751 (for sigma_piN = 40 MeV) to -0.0872 (for sigma_piN = 64 MeV)
- The isovector kaon-nucleon sigma term is estimated as sigma_KN^{I=1} approx 45 MeV
- The O(q^2) contribution to sigma_piN is 85.3 MeV, with O(q^3) corrections from pion, kaon, and eta loops of -22.8 MeV, -16.5 MeV, and -1.0 MeV respectively (for the second parameter set)

## Context

This work extends the EOMS renormalization scheme, previously applied to the two-flavor sector for the nucleon mass (Fuchs 2003) and electromagnetic form factors (Fuchs 2003), to the three-flavor sector for the full baryon octet. It contrasts with the heavy-baryon formulation of chiral perturbation theory (Jenkins 1990, Jenkins 1991, Bernard 1992), which gives up manifest Lorentz invariance, and with the infrared renormalization approach (Becher 1999). The results enable future unquenched three-flavor lattice QCD simulations by providing the chiral expansion of baryon masses to second order in quark masses, as noted in the recent work by Frink and Meissner (2004).
