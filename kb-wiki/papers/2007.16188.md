# Alternative derivation of the relativistic three-particle quantization condition

> We present a simplified derivation of the relativistic three-particle quantization condition for identical, spinless particles described by a generic relativistic field theory satisfying a $\mathbb Z_2$ symmetry. The simplification is afforded by using a three-particle quasilocal K matrix that is not fully symmetrized, $\widetilde{\mathcal{K}}_{\rm df,3}^{(u,u)}$, and makes extensive use of time-ordered perturbation theory (TOPT). We obtain a new form of the quantization condition. This new form can then be related algebraically to the standard quantization condition, which depends on a fully symmetric three-particle K matrix, $\mathcal{K}_{\rm df,3}$. The new derivation is fully explicit, allowing, for example, a closed-form expression for $\mathcal{K}_{\rm df,3}$ to be given in terms of TOPT amplitudes. The new form of the quantization condition is similar in structure to that obtained in the "finite-volume unitarity" approach, and in a companion paper we make this connection concrete. Our simplified approach should also allow a more straightforward generalization of the quantization condition to nondegenerate particles, and perhaps also to more than three particles.

## Key claim

The paper presents a simplified derivation of the relativistic three-particle quantization condition for identical, spinless particles with a Z2 symmetry, using a not-fully-symmetrized three-particle quasilocal K matrix (K̃df,3(u,u)) and time-ordered perturbation theory throughout, which yields a new form that can be algebraically related to the standard symmetric form.

## Method

The authors use time-ordered perturbation theory (TOPT) to write the three-particle correlation function as a geometric series in terms of two- and three-particle irreducible TOPT amplitudes, then rewrite the expression using on-shell quantities and finite-volume quantities W̃G and W̃F. They reorganize the correlator into a geometric series involving on-shell, infinite-volume kernels, which directly yields a new quantization condition involving an asymmetric three-particle K matrix, and then show through algebraic steps how this can be rewritten in terms of the fully symmetric three-particle K matrix Kdf,3 from the original Hansen-Sharpe formalism.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic field theory (RFT) approach of Hansen and Sharpe (HS1, HS2) for three-particle quantization conditions, providing a simpler derivation that uses TOPT throughout rather than the Feynman-diagram-based skeleton expansion of the original work. The new form is similar in structure to the "finite-volume unitarity" (FVU) approach of Mai and Döring, and the companion paper makes this connection concrete; the simplified approach should allow generalization to nondegenerate particles and possibly to more than three particles.
