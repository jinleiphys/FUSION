# Why the OZI rule is so strongly violated in J/Psi decays?

> The new $f_0(1790)$ meson recently observed by BES collaboration in $J/Ψ$-decay, is seen only in the OZI-forbidden channel. It is shown that chiral symmetry restoration in excited hadrons implies a new selection rule of dynamical origin that forbids some of the OZI-favoured mechanisms of decays. Hence decays into channels that are suppressed by OZI can become dominant.

## Key claim

The strong violation of the OZI rule in the decay of J/Psi into phi f0(1790) is explained by a new dynamical selection rule arising from chiral symmetry restoration in excited hadrons; this rule forbids the OZI-allowed decay mechanism for J/Psi into omega f0(1790), making the OZI-forbidden channel dominant, though the paper does not attempt to explain why the specific OZI-forbidden doubly disconnected diagram is relatively enhanced.

## Method

The author analyzes the chiral symmetry properties of the valence quark wave functions for the f0(1790) and pi(1800) mesons, which form an approximate chiral pair under SU(2)L x SU(2)R restoration. Using the fact that the quark-gluon vertex conserves chirality for massless quarks, the author shows that the (R L + L R)/sqrt(2) structure of the f0(1790) wave function cannot serve as an interpolating field for the omega meson (a vector particle), thereby forbidding the OZI-allowed decay J/Psi -> omega f0(1790) via perturbative gluons. The argument is extended to other excited mesons in the chirally restored regime.

## Key numbers

- The f0(1790) meson has mass M = 1790^{+40}_{-30} MeV and width Gamma = 270^{+60}_{-30} MeV, observed as a 15 sigma peak in pi+ pi-.
- The f0(1790) is the radial excitation of the predominantly n n-bar state f0(1370); the approximate chiral partner pi(1800) has mass 1800 MeV, with the f0(1790) at 1790 MeV showing near degeneracy.
- The lowest-lying approximate chiral pair is pi(1300 +/- 100) and f0(1370^{+130}_{-170}) in the pi-f0 spectra.
- Additional radial excitations of n n-bar f0 mesons are f0(2040) and f0(2337) seen in p-bar p reactions.

## Context

This work extends the concept of chiral symmetry restoration in excited hadrons, previously developed by Glozman (2001-2005), Cohen and Glozman (2002), and Shifman (2002), to explain the anomalous decay pattern of the f0(1790) observed by the BES collaboration. The result contradicts the naive expectation from the OZI rule and rules out many existing scenarios for light mesons, including the constituent quark model picture for excited mesons. The selection rule enables predictions for searches of missing a0-mesons (chiral partners of eta-mesons) at BES, CLEO, and similar facilities, and suggests that charmonium decays into two mesons where one is in the chirally restored regime should generically show strong OZI violations.
