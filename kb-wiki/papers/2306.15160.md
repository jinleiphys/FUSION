# Study of Baryon Number Transport Dynamics and Strangeness Conservation Effects Using $Ω$-hadron Correlations

> In nuclear collisions at RHIC energies, an excess of $Ω$ hyperons over $\barΩ$ is observed, indicating that $Ω$ carries a net baryon number despite $s$ and $\bar{s}$ quarks being produced in pairs. The baryon number in $Ω$ could have been transported from the incident nuclei and/or produced in baryon-pair production of $Ω$ with other types of anti-hyperons, such as $\barΞ$. To investigate these two scenarios, we propose to measure correlations between $Ω$ and $K$, as well as between $Ω$ and anti-hyperons. We will use two versions, the default and string-melting, of a multiphase transport (AMPT) model to illustrate the method to measure the correlation and to demonstrate the general shape of the correlation. We will present the $Ω$-hadron correlations from simulated $\mathrm{Au}$+$\mathrm{Au}$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7$ and $14.6 \ \mathrm{GeV}$, and discuss the dependence on collision energy and on the hadronization scheme in these two AMPT versions. These correlations can be used to explore the mechanism of baryon number transport and the effects of baryon number and strangeness conservation in nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that measuring correlations between $\Omega$ hyperons and kaons or anti-hyperons in Au+Au collisions can distinguish whether the net baryon number carried by $\Omega$ hyperons is transported from incident nuclei (Scenario 1) or produced via baryon-pair production with other anti-hyperons (Scenario 2), but the AMPT model simulations only provide a baseline and do not include exotic gluon junction dynamics.

## Method

The authors used both the default and string-melting versions of the AMPT (A Multiphase Transport) model to simulate minimum bias Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7$ and $14.6$ GeV, generating over 50 million events per energy. They computed correlation functions between $\Omega^-$ and strange hadrons ($K^+$, $\bar{\Lambda}$, $\bar{\Xi}^+$) using two techniques: event mixing normalization and combinatorial background subtraction (CBS), where the latter takes the difference between $\Omega^-$-hadron and $\bar{\Omega}^+$-hadron correlations normalized by the respective hyperon counts.

## Key numbers

- At $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7$ GeV, the difference in $s\bar{s}$ pairs between events with one $\Omega^-$ and events without any $\Omega^-$ or $\bar{\Omega}^+$ is 3.22 (AMPT string-melting) and 3.25 (AMPT default) for 0-5% centrality.
- At $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 14.6$ GeV, the same difference is 3.07 (AMPT string-melting) and 3.16 (AMPT default) for 0-5% centrality.
- At 7.7 GeV, $\Delta N_K$ (excess kaons in $\Omega^-$ events) is 2.46 (AMPT string-melting) and 1.74 (AMPT default); $\Delta N_{\bar{B}}$ (excess anti-baryons) is 0.017 (string-melting) and 0.078 (default).
- At 14.6 GeV, $\Delta N_K$ is 2.44 (AMPT string-melting) and 1.76 (AMPT default); $\Delta N_{\bar{B}}$ is 0.119 (string-melting) and 0.28 (default).
- The event mixing normalization for correlation functions was determined using the $k^*$ region of 0.6-1.5 GeV/$c$.

## Context

This work extends studies of baryon number transport dynamics at RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies, where measured anti-baryon-to-baryon ratios for $\Lambda$, $\Xi$, and $\Omega$ are significantly below unity at midrapidity (STAR Collaboration, 2019). The paper builds on theoretical proposals that gluon junctions (Kharzeev, 1996) could transport baryon number over large rapidity gaps, and uses the AMPT model (Lin, 2005) to provide baselines for future experimental measurements. The correlations proposed could enable experimental searches for exotic baryon transport mechanisms such as gluon junction interactions, which are not included in the current AMPT simulations.
