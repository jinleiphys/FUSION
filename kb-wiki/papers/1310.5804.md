# Compact Stars - How Exotic Can They Be?

> Strong interaction physics under extreme conditions of high temperature and/or density is of central interest in modern nuclear physics for experimentalists and theorists alike. In order to investigate such systems, model approaches that include hadrons and quarks in a unified approach, will be discussed. Special attention will be given to high-density matter as it occurs in neutron stars. Given the current observational limits for neutron star masses, the properties of hyperonic and hybrid stars will be determined. In this context especially the question of the extent, to which exotic particles like hyperons and quarks affect star masses, will be discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a 2-solar-mass compact star can be obtained within a flavor-SU(3) hadronic model even when hyperons and Delta resonances are included, but only if the model incorporates repulsive vector meson exchange (specifically the phi meson) between hyperons to suppress their densities. The authors also find that a consistent description of a heavy hybrid star with a quark core and agreement with lattice QCD data at small chemical potential remains elusive.

## Method

The authors use an effective chiral SU(3) hadronic model where baryon masses are generated by coupling to scalar fields (sigma, delta, zeta) whose expectation values relate to quark condensates, and effective chemical potentials are shifted by vector fields (omega, rho, phi). They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for static spherical stars, considering purely nucleonic matter, matter with hyperons, matter with Delta baryons, and matter with kaon condensates. For hybrid stars, they adopt a MIT bag model for quarks with varying bag constant and perturbative corrections, and also a unified quark-hadron model with a Polyakov loop field that couples quarks to the same mean fields as hadrons.

## Key numbers

- Maximum star masses for purely nucleonic stars, hyperonic stars, and stars with Delta baryons all remain above 2.0 solar masses, consistent with the observed mass of PSR J1614-2230 at M = 1.97 ± 0.04 M_sun and PSR J0348-0432 at M = 2.01 ± 0.04 M_sun.
- Kaon condensation only sets in at densities of about 6 times nuclear matter saturation density (6 rho_0) and beyond, which are not reached in the center of the star within the model.
- In the unified quark-hadron model, hybrid star masses of up to approximately 2.2 M_sun are obtained with repulsive vector interactions in the quark sector.
- For specific fine-tuned parameters with a strong vector coupling of the strange quark, a second family of stable compact stars appears with a strangeness fraction f_s larger than 1 and a distinctly smaller radius than the other stable branch.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous chiral SU(3) hadronic model studies (Papazoglou 1997, Papazoglou 1998) and their earlier work on Delta resonances in stars (Dexheimer 2008). It directly addresses the tension between the high masses measured for PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348-0432 and the softening of the equation of state expected from exotic particles. The paper enables future work on reconciling hybrid star models with lattice QCD constraints at small chemical potential, specifically the Taylor expansion coefficients of the pressure (Bazavov 2012, Cheng 2008, Borsanyi 2011), which currently show clear discrepancies when quark vector couplings are introduced.
