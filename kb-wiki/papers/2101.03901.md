# Nuclear reactions in artificial traps

> Coupled-channel two-particle systems bound by a harmonic trap are discussed in the present paper. We derive the formula that relates the energy levels of such trapped systems to phase shifts and inelasticity of coupled-channel reactions. The formula makes it possible to extract amplitudes of inelastic nuclear reactions from ab initio calculations of discrete levels of many-nucleon systems in a harmonic trap.

# Nuclear reactions in artificial traps

## Key claim
The authors derive a quantization condition (QC) that relates discrete energy levels of two-particle systems in a harmonic trap to coupled-channel reaction amplitudes (phase shifts and inelasticity), enabling extraction of inelastic nuclear reaction amplitudes from ab initio calculations of many-nucleon systems. The derivation assumes short-range interactions and that the trap width is much larger than the interaction range, and it neglects Coulomb repulsion near threshold.

## Method
The authors use a variational approach with contact (separable) interactions to describe the two-body dynamics both in infinite volume and inside a harmonic oscillator trap. They derive the coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equations in momentum space, eliminate the contact coupling constants by matching the trapped and free Green's functions at short distances, and obtain a determinant condition linking the trapped energy eigenvalues to the phase shifts and inelasticity. The resulting quantization condition for coupled channels is expressed in terms of functions \(g_L^{(\alpha)}\) that play the role of Luscher zeta functions for the harmonic trap.

## Key numbers
- The oscillator length characterizing the trap width is defined as \(b \equiv \left(\frac{1}{2} \mu \omega\right)^{-\frac{1}{2}}\), where \(\mu\) is the reduced mass and \(\omega\) the harmonic oscillator frequency.
- The single-channel BERW formula gives \(\cot \delta_L(q) = g_L(q)\) with \(g_L(q) = (-1)^{L+1} \left( \frac{4 \mu \omega}{q^2} \right)^{L+\frac{1}{2}} \frac{\Gamma(\frac{3}{4}+\frac{L}{2}-\frac{\epsilon^{(\omega)}}{2\omega})}{\Gamma(\frac{1}{4}-\frac{L}{2}-\frac{\epsilon^{(\omega)}}{2\omega})}\).
- The coupled-channel quantization condition (Eq. 55) involves two phase shifts \(\delta_L^{(1)}, \delta_L^{(2)}\) and one inelasticity \(\eta_L\), with functions \(g_L^{(\alpha)}\) defined analogously for each channel \(\alpha = 1,2\).
- For a spherical hard-wall trap of radius \(b\), the single-channel QC becomes \(\cot \delta_L(q) = n_L(q b)/j_L(q b)\).

## Context
This work extends the BERW formula (Busch et al., 1998) for elastic scattering in a harmonic trap to coupled-channel reactions relevant to nuclear fusion and rearrangement processes. It parallels similar developments in lattice QCD for periodic cubic boxes (Luscher, 1990; Guo et al., 2012) and provides a framework for extracting inelastic nuclear reaction amplitudes from ab initio many-body calculations that use harmonic-oscillator basis expansions (Navratil et al., 2007; Epelbaum et al., 2008; Barrett et al., 2013). The method enables future extraction of phase shifts and inelasticity from trapped energy levels, either by using multiple trap frequencies or by fitting parametrized scattering amplitudes to several eigenvalues.
