# Temperature Dependence of the Effective Bag Constant and the Radius of a Nucleon in the Global Color Symmetry Model of QCD

> We study the temperature dependence of the effective bag constant, the mass, and the radius of a nucleon in the formalism of the simple global color symmetry model in the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach of QCD with a Gaussian-type effective gluon propagator. We obtain that, as the temperature is lower than a critical value, the effective bag constant and the mass decrease and the radius increases with the temperature increasing. As the critical temperature is reached, the effective bag constant and the mass vanish and the radius tends to infinity. At the same time, the chiral quark condensate disappears. These phenomena indicate that the deconfinement and the chiral symmetry restoration phase transitions can take place at high temperature. The dependence of the critical temperature on the interaction strength parameter in the effective gluon propagator of the approach is given.

# Temperature Dependence of the Effective Bag Constant and the Radius of a Nucleon in the Global Color Symmetry Model of QCD

## Key claim

The deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration phase transitions occur simultaneously at a critical temperature of approximately 133 MeV, but the process is likely a crossover rather than a first-order phase transition, as the bag constant and mass decrease gradually and the radius increases continuously with temperature before vanishing or diverging at the critical point.

## Method

The authors solve the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) at finite temperature using a Gaussian-type effective gluon propagator with parameters ω = 0.5 GeV and D = 1.0 GeV², obtaining both Nambu-Goldstone and Wigner solutions. They then apply the global color symmetry model (GCM) soliton formalism, treating the nucleon as a nontopological soliton bag, and compute the temperature-dependent bag constant, mass, and radius by solving the coupled equations of motion for quarks and chiral meson fields with the equilibrium condition dE_B/dR = 0.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration: T_c = 133 MeV (with ω = 0.5 GeV, D = 1.0 GeV²)
- Bag constant at zero temperature: B(0) = (162 MeV)⁴
- Nucleon mass at zero temperature: M(0) = 939 MeV (with Z₀ fixed at 3.08)
- Nucleon radius at zero temperature: R(0) = 0.85 fm
- The critical temperature increases with increasing interaction strength parameter D in the effective gluon propagator; below a critical scaled coupling strength D' = D/ω², the critical temperature remains zero

## Context

This work extends previous GCM studies of nucleon properties at finite density by Liu et al. (2003) to the finite-temperature case, and goes beyond the earlier finite-temperature analysis by Blaschke et al. that used only the Munczek-Nemirovsky model for the effective gluon propagator. The results enable further investigation of the nature of the QCD phase transition (crossover versus first-order) and provide a framework for studying hadron properties in hot dense matter using more sophisticated DSE truncations, such as the BC vertex employed by Chang et al. (2009) for meson properties.
