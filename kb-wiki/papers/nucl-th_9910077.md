# Sum rules regarding the sign problem in Monte Carlo shell model calculations

> The Monte Carlo shell model is a powerful technique for computational nuclear structure. Only a certain class of nuclear interactions, however, such as pairing and quadrupole, are free of the numerical noise known as the sign problem.This paper presents sum rules that relate the sign problem to the J=0 pairing matrix elements, thus illuminating the extrapolation procedure routinely used for realistic shell-model interactions.

# Sum rules regarding the sign problem in Monte Carlo shell model calculations

## Key claim

The paper derives two sum rules that relate the sign problem in auxiliary-field Monte Carlo (AFMC) shell model calculations to the J=0 pairing matrix elements, proving that the conventional extrapolation procedure used for realistic interactions effectively increases the strength of attractive pairing. The sum rules confirm that setting the isovector particle-hole matrix elements to zero (conventional wisdom #1) is not merely convenient but necessary to avoid the sign problem, and that the extrapolation procedure (conventional wisdom #2) works by enhancing pairing correlations.

## Method

The authors decompose the nuclear shell-model Hamiltonian into a quadratic form in one-body density operators via a Pandya transformation to the particle-hole representation, then diagonalize the resulting particle-hole matrix elements E_{KI}(ac,bd) to obtain eigenvalues λ_{KI}(α). They derive sum rules for the weighted traces of these eigenvalues over angular momentum K, separately for isoscalar (I=0) and isovector (I=1) channels, and relate them to the J=0, T=1 pairing matrix elements V^A_{J=0,T=1}(aa,cc). The sum rules are proven using properties of six-j symbols and the symmetry properties of the two-body matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- The isoscalar sum rule: Σ_K (2K+1) Σ_α (-1)^K π(α) λ_{K,I=0}(α) = (1/2) Σ_{ac} π(ac) √((2j_a+1)(2j_c+1)) V^A_{J=0,T=1}(aa,cc)
- The isovector sum rule: Σ_K (2K+1) Σ_α (-1)^K π(α) λ_{K,I=1}(α) = 0
- For an attractive pairing interaction with V^A_{J=0,T=1}(aa,cc) = -G √((2j_a+1)(2j_c+1)), the eigenvalues become λ_{K,I=0} = -(-1)^K G/2 for all K
- The extrapolation function used is f(g) = [1 - (1-g)/4] for the good part of the Hamiltonian, with g=1 giving the original interaction and g ≤ 0 giving a sign-problem-free interaction

## Context

This work extends the foundational theorem of Lang (1993) on sign-problem-free interactions in nuclear AFMC, which showed that interactions with strictly time-even effective one-body Hamiltonians avoid the sign problem for even-N, even-Z systems. The sum rules provide the first analytic proof of the conventional wisdom used in the extrapolation procedure introduced by Alhassid et al. (1994) for realistic interactions such as the Wildenthal (1983) interaction. The results enable systematic analysis of the extrapolation method and potentially the development of alternative approaches for handling the sign problem in Monte Carlo shell model calculations, particularly for cross-shell interactions as demonstrated with the example of sd-pf shell interactions.
