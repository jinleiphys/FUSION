# Filling fractions for the formation of nuclear pasta in neutron stars: semiclassical vs liquid-drop predictions

> Historically, a sequence of nuclear pasta shapes was predicted to appear in the deepest region of the inner crust of a neutron star within the compressible liquid-drop picture, when the filling fraction $u$ exceeds some threshold values. However, later calculations showed that these values depend on the details of the liquid-drop model. Here we investigate the existence of pasta in neutron stars within the semiclassical extended Thomas-Fermi approach using various generalized Skyrme functionals. The filling fractions for the different transitions are found to be quasi-universal, unlike the pasta density ranges governed by the symmetry energy at relevant densities. In particular, pasta emerge at $u_\mathrm{sp}\approx0.13-0.15$. By applying a simplified stability criterion within the liquid-drop framework, we show that these values of $u_\mathrm{sp}$ can be explained by the nuclear curvature correction. In this way, the abundance of pasta can be easily estimated. This criterion can also be used to optimize the search of pasta within the more realistic extended Thomas-Fermi approach.

## Key claim

The filling fractions for nuclear pasta shape transitions in neutron star crusts are quasi-universal across different generalized Skyrme functionals, unlike the density ranges which depend on the symmetry energy; the onset of pasta occurs at filling fraction u_sp ≈ 0.13-0.15, and this value can be explained by including the nuclear curvature correction in the liquid-drop model.

## Method

The authors performed extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) calculations using generalized Skyrme functionals BSk22, BSk24, and BSk25, minimizing the total energy per particle for spherical, cylindrical, and platelike Wigner-Seitz cells to determine equilibrium pasta configurations. They then applied a simplified stability criterion within the compressible liquid-drop model (CLDM) including curvature corrections, using surface tensions fitted either to ETF nuclear mass tables or to experimental atomic masses, to compare the predicted threshold filling fractions with the ETF results.

## Key numbers

- Pasta onset (spheres to spaghetti) occurs at filling fraction u_sp ≈ 0.13-0.14 from ETF calculations
- Spaghetti to lasagna transition at u_t ≈ 0.26-0.27
- Lasagna to bucatini transition at u_t ≈ 0.48-0.51
- Bucatini to Swiss cheese transition at u_t ≈ 0.66-0.69
- CLDM instability criterion including curvature gives u_sp ≈ 0.14-0.15 for the onset of pasta
- Without curvature, the CLDM criterion reproduces the traditional sequence at u_t = [0.19, 0.35, 0.65, 0.81] for spheres/spaghetti/lasagna/bucatini (Oyamatsu et al. 1984)
- For BSk24 CLDM with curvature fitted to experimental masses, threshold filling fractions are u_t ≈ [0.16, 0.29, 0.56, 0.7]

## Context

This work extends the earlier liquid-drop predictions of Hashimoto et al. (1984) and Oyamatsu et al. (1984) by including curvature corrections, and builds on the ETF calculations of Shchechilin et al. (2023) which studied symmetry energy effects on pasta abundance. The quasi-universal filling fractions found here can be used to optimize the search for pasta phases in more realistic ETF calculations, and the simplified CLDM stability criterion provides a computationally efficient way to estimate pasta abundance in neutron star crusts.
