# Unified Chew-Mandelstam SAID analysis of pion photoproduction data

> A unified description of single-pion photoproduction data, together with pion- and eta-hadroproduction data, has been achieved in a Chew-Mandelstam parametrization which is consistent with unitarity at the two-body level. Energy-dependent and single-energy partial wave analyses of pion photoproduction data have been performed and compared to previous SAID fits and multipoles from the Mainz and Bonn-Gatchina groups.

## Key claim

A unified Chew-Mandelstam parametrization, consistent with two-body unitarity, provides a slightly better fit to single-pion photoproduction data than the previous SAID SN11 parametrization, using fewer free parameters, but the improvement is modest and the new fit changes the phase behavior of the \(E_{0+}^{1/2}\) multipole significantly, now resembling the Bonn-Gatchina result rather than the previous SAID or MAID07 solutions.

## Method

The authors performed energy-dependent (ED) and single-energy (SES) partial-wave analyses of single-pion photoproduction data over the center-of-mass energy range from near threshold to about 2.5 GeV. The hadronic rescattering matrix \([1-\bar{K}C]^{-1}\) was fixed by fitting \(\pi N \to \pi N\) and \(\pi N \to \eta N\) data from a prior SAID analysis (Arndt 2006), and only the electromagnetic Chew-Mandelstam K-matrix elements \(\bar{K}_{\sigma\gamma}\) (for channels \(\pi N, \pi\Delta, \rho N, \eta N\)) were varied to fit the photoproduction data. The fit quality and number of parameters were compared to the previous SN11 solution (Workman 2011) over three energy ranges up to 2.7 GeV.

## Key numbers

*   The CM12 solution achieved \(\chi^2/N_{\rm Data} = 2.01\) with 200 parameters for the full energy range up to 2700 MeV, compared to \(\chi^2/N_{\rm Data} = 2.08\) with 209 parameters for the SN11 solution.
*   For the energy range up to 2100 MeV, CM12 gave \(\chi^2/N_{\rm Data} = 1.88\) with 194 parameters, versus SN11 at 1.96 with 206 parameters.
*   For the energy range up to 1200 MeV, CM12 gave \(\chi^2/N_{\rm Data} = 1.64\) with 166 parameters, versus SN11 at 1.69 with 175 parameters.
*   The extracted photo-decay helicity amplitude \(A_{1/2}\) for the \(N(1535)S_{11}\) resonance was \(128 \pm 4\) (in units of GeV\(^{-1/2} \times 10^{-3}\)), compared to the PDG average of \(90 \pm 30\).
*   The extracted \(A_{3/2}\) for the \(N(1520)D_{13}\) resonance was \(141 \pm 2\) (same units), compared to the PDG average of \(166 \pm 5\).
*   The extracted \(A_{1/2}\) for the \(\Delta(1232)P_{33}\) resonance was \(-139 \pm 2\) and \(A_{3/2}\) was \(-262 \pm 3\) (same units), compared to PDG averages of \(-135 \pm 6\) and \(-250 \pm 8\), respectively.

## Context

This work extends the SAID program of partial-wave analyses (Arndt 1989, Workman 2011) by replacing the phenomenological parametrization of photoproduction amplitudes with a Chew-Mandelstam form that explicitly couples the electromagnetic channel to the same hadronic channels (\(\pi N, \pi\Delta, \rho N, \eta N\)) used in the hadronic fits of Arndt (2006). The results are compared to the MAID07 solution from the Mainz group and the Bonn-Gatchina solution (BG2010-02). The new parametrization enables a more theoretically consistent extraction of resonance photo-decay couplings and provides a framework for incorporating future high-precision double-polarization data from electromagnetic facilities.
