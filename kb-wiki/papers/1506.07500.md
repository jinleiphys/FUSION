# Investigating the domain of validity of the Gubser solution to the Boltzmann equation

> We study the evolution of the one particle distribution function that solves exactly the relativistic Boltzmann equation within the relaxation time approximation for a conformal system undergoing simultaneously azimuthally symmetric transverse and boost-invariant longitudinal expansion. We show, for arbitrary values of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio, that the distribution function can become negative in certain kinematic regions of the available phase space depending on the boundary conditions. For thermal equilibrium initial conditions, we determine numerically the physical boundary in phase space where the distribution function is always positive definite. The requirement of positivity of this particular exact solution restricts its domain of validity, and it imposes physical constraints on its applicability.

## Key claim

The exact solution of the relativistic Boltzmann equation with relaxation time approximation for Gubser flow, assuming thermal equilibrium initial conditions at a finite de Sitter time, produces a distribution function that becomes negative in certain phase-space regions when evolved backward in de Sitter time, thereby losing its probabilistic meaning and restricting the solution's domain of validity for heavy-ion phenomenology.

## Method

The authors solved the conformal RTA Boltzmann equation in de Sitter space, where the Gubser flow velocity becomes static, reducing the equation to a one-dimensional ordinary differential equation in the de Sitter time variable ρ. They first iteratively solved an integral equation for the Weyl-rescaled temperature T̂(ρ) and then evaluated the distribution function f(ρ; p̂_Ω², p̂_ς) point-by-point in momentum space using the formal solution of the Boltzmann equation, assuming Boltzmann equilibrium initial conditions at ρ₀.

## Key numbers

- For 4πη/S = 3 and T̂₀ = 1, the distribution function becomes negative at ρ = -4 and ρ = -6 when starting from ρ₀ = 0, with the unphysical region appearing at large p̂_Ω and small p̂_ς.
- For 4πη/S = 10 (compared to 3) at fixed ρ₀ = 0, ρ = -6, and T̂₀ = 1, the f = 0 boundary moves closer to the p̂_ς = 0 axis and toward larger p̂_Ω values, indicating slower evolution toward negativity for larger specific shear viscosity.
- For T̂₀ = 2 and T̂₀ = 3 (compared to T̂₀ = 1) at fixed ρ₀ = 0, ρ = -6, and 4πη/S = 3, the f = 0 boundary moves to smaller p̂_Ω and larger p̂_ς values, meaning the unphysical region grows with increasing initial temperature.
- In Minkowski space at τ = 1.5 fm/c with q = 0.04 GeV, ρ₀ = 0, T̂₀ = 1, and 4πη/S = 3, the critical surface separating f > 0 from f < 0 is approximately linear, with |p_z^crit| = α(r)(p_T - p_T₀(r)), where α(2 fm) ≈ 1.47 and α(10 fm) ≈ 2.08.

## Context

This work extends the exact solutions of the RTA Boltzmann equation for Bjorken flow (Baym, 1984) and Gubser flow (Denicol, 2014) by examining the distribution function directly rather than only its moments. It confirms the conjecture in Denicol (2014) that complex-valued temperatures found when evolving backward in de Sitter time arise from positivity violations of the distribution function. The results show that this exact solution, while useful for testing hydrodynamic approximations in the physically allowed region, is unsuitable for heavy-ion phenomenology when thermal equilibrium initial conditions are imposed at a finite de Sitter time; an alternative approach starting from ρ₀ → -∞ with non-equilibrium initial conditions might restore applicability.
