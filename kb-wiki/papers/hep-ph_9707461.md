# Baryon chiral perturbation theory

> I review recent progress made in the description of pion-nucleon scattering, the $σ$--term, the scalar form factor and the strangeness content of the nucleon in the framework of heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. Other topics are single pion production by real and virtual photons as well as in proton-proton collisions.

## Key claim

Heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBCHPT) provides a systematic, quantitative framework for low-energy pion-nucleon interactions, but the convergence of the chiral expansion in the three-flavor sector (including strangeness) is not yet definitively established, and tree-level calculations at order \(p^2\) for the isoscalar pion-nucleon amplitude are insufficiently accurate for describing threshold pion production in proton-proton collisions.

## Method

The author reviews calculations within heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, which expands observables in powers of small momenta, quark masses, and the inverse nucleon mass (collectively denoted \(p\)), with the chiral symmetry scale \(\Lambda_\chi \simeq 1\) GeV. Low-energy constants (LECs) appearing at each order are fixed by fitting to selected observables (e.g., nine observables for the \(p^3\) \(\pi N\) scattering LECs) or estimated via resonance exchange, including a consistent scheme to avoid double-counting and respect analyticity. For pion production in \(pp\) collisions, the transition operator is constructed from the chiral Lagrangian and compared to meson-exchange model results.

## Key numbers

- \(\sigma_{\pi N}(0) = 45\) MeV from the \(p^4\) calculation, with the chiral expansion showing terms \(58.3\,(1 - 0.56 + 0.33)\) MeV.
- Strangeness fraction \(y = 0.21 \pm 0.20\) and \(\hat\sigma = 36 \pm 7\) MeV from the \(p^4\) baryon mass calculation.
- Octet baryon mass in the chiral limit: \(\krig{m} = 770 \pm 110\) MeV.
- Remainder at the Cheng-Dashen point: \(\Delta_R \le 2\) MeV, with contributions \(\Delta_R^{\rm loop} = 0.5\) MeV, \(\Delta_R^{\Delta} = 0.6\) MeV, \(\Delta_R^{S} \le 1.1\) MeV, and \(\Delta_R^{\rm strange} \le 0.4\) MeV.
- Threshold electric dipole amplitudes for pion photoproduction (in units of \(10^{-3}/M_{\pi^+}\)): \(E_{0+}^{\rm thr}(\pi^+ n) = 28.2 \pm 0.6\), \(E_{0+}^{\rm thr}(\pi^- p) = -32.7 \pm 0.6\), \(E_{0+}^{\rm thr}(\pi^0 p) = -1.16\), \(E_{0+}^{\rm thr}(\pi^0 n) = 2.13\).
- Deuteron electric dipole amplitude for \(\pi^0\) photoproduction: \(E_d = (-1.8 \pm 0.2) \times 10^{-3}/M_{\pi^+}\).
- P-wave low-energy theorem for \(\pi^0\) photoproduction: \(P_1^{\rm LET} = 10.33\) compared to \(P_1^{\rm exp} = 10.02 \pm 0.15\) (conventional units).
- LECs for the isoscalar \(\pi N\) amplitude at order \(p^2\): \(c_1 = -0.64 \pm 0.14\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(c_2 = 1.78 \pm 0.10\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(c_3 = -3.90 \pm 0.09\) GeV\(^{-1}\); at order \(p^3\): \(c_1 = -0.93 \pm 0.10\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(c_2 = 3.34 \pm 0.20\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(c_3 = -5.29 \pm 0.25\) GeV\(^{-1}\).

## Context

This review extends the work of Gasser (1991) on the \(\sigma\)-term and strangeness content, and the \(p^3\) \(\pi N\) scattering calculation of Mojzis (1996). It presents the first \(p^4\) calculation of octet baryon masses and the \(\sigma\)-term by Borasoy and Meissner (1996), which includes all terms of second order in the light quark masses. The discussion of pion production in \(pp\) collisions highlights a discrepancy between tree-level CHPT calculations (which find destructive interference between direct and rescattering contributions) and the meson-exchange model of the Julich group (which finds constructive interference), arguing that one-loop corrections are needed before drawing conclusions. The work enables future tests of chiral dynamics through more precise low-energy data, particularly for neutral pion photoproduction on the neutron via deuteron targets, and for the zero-crossing of the electric dipole amplitude in pion electroproduction.
