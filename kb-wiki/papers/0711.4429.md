# How do diquark fluctuations and chiral soft modes affect di-lepton production in the deconfined phase?

> We examine diquark fluctuations and chiral soft modes existing in the precritical region of color superconductivity and chiral transition, respectively, at finite temperature and density. We evaluate how they contribute to anomalous di-lepton production; although there appear peaks in the spectral function owing to the existence of the soft modes, the enhancement of the production rate may not be so prominent to be a clear signal of the phsase transitions.

## Key claim

The paper examines whether diquark fluctuations (precursors to color superconductivity) and chiral soft modes (precursors to chiral symmetry restoration) can produce observable enhancements in di-lepton production rates; although these soft modes create peaks in the spectral function, the enhancement of the production rate may not be prominent enough to serve as a clear experimental signal of the phase transitions.

## Method

The authors calculate the photon self-energy in hot, dense quark matter using a T-matrix approximation that includes the coupling of quarks to fluctuating diquark pair fields (the Aslamazov-Larkin and Maki-Thompson diagrams) and to chiral soft modes (sigma and pion fluctuations). They then compute the di-lepton production rate from the imaginary part of the photon self-energy using the standard thermal field theory formula, integrating over four-momenta to obtain the invariant mass spectrum. The calculations are performed within Nambu-Jona-Lasinio-type models for two-flavor quark matter at finite temperature and chemical potential, focusing on temperatures above the critical temperature for color superconductivity and chiral transition.

## Key numbers

- The diquark coherence length to inter-quark distance ratio is proportional to \(E_F/\Delta\) and can be as small as \(\sim 10\) at moderate densities.
- The pseudogap in the density of states survives up to reduced temperature \(\varepsilon \equiv (T-T_c)/T_c \approx 0.1\) irrespective of chemical potential \(\mu\).
- The di-lepton production rate from the Aslamazov-Larkin term shows a sharp peak structure at energies less than the pion mass, with the peak becoming larger and sharper as temperature approaches \(T_c\).
- The di-electron production rate is shown for temperatures \(1.3T_c\), \(1.2T_c\), \(1.1T_c\), \(1.05T_c\), and \(1.01T_c\) at \(\mu = 400\) MeV.
- The di-muon production rate is calculated using the same formalism with the muon mass \(m_\mu\) included in the kinematic factors.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of precritical fluctuations in color superconductivity by Kitazawa (2005) and of chiral soft modes by Hatsuda and Kunihiro (1984). It connects to the broader effort to understand the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma (QGP) near the critical temperature, as suggested by perfect hydrodynamics at RHIC. The results indicate that while soft-mode-induced enhancements of di-lepton production exist at low invariant masses (below the pion mass), disentangling these signals from other hadronic and electromagnetic processes in heavy-ion collisions will be difficult; the authors call for more definitive calculations including the Maki-Thompson term and for experimental strategies to separate these precursor signals from background.
