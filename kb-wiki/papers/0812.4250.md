# Hard bremsstrahlung in the pp -> pp gamma reaction

> The pp -> pp gamma reaction has been measured at a beam energy of 310 MeV by detecting both final protons in the PROMICE-WASA facility and identifying a missing-mass peak. For those events where the pp excitation is less than 3 MeV, the final diproton is almost purely in the 1S0 state and, under these conditions, there is complete coverage in the photon c.m. angle theta_gamma. The linear behaviour observed in cos^2(theta_gamma) shows that there is almost no influence of an E2 multipole at this energy, though the E1 and M2 must be rather similar in size.

## Key claim

The differential cross section for $pp\to\{pp\}_s\gamma$ at 310 MeV shows a linear dependence on $\cos^2\theta_\gamma$, indicating almost no influence of an $E2$ multipole at this energy, contrary to theoretical expectations that the $E2$ term should be quite large above 200 MeV.

## Method

The $pp\to pp\gamma$ reaction was measured at a beam energy of 310 MeV using the PROMICE-WASA facility at the CELSIUS storage ring, detecting both final protons and identifying a missing-mass peak. Events were selected with diproton excitation energy $E_{pp}<3$ MeV to ensure the final diproton is almost purely in the $^{1}S_0$ state, and the detector acceptance was determined via Monte Carlo simulations incorporating the $S$-wave final state interaction. The differential cross section was extracted over the full photon center-of-mass angular range for $\cos\theta_\gamma < 0.8$, exploiting forward-backward symmetry.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: $T_p = 309.7 \pm 0.3$ MeV
- Integrated luminosity: $340 \pm 35$ nb$^{-1}$
- Missing-mass peak contains about 1450 events with width $\sigma(M_X^2)_\gamma \approx 0.06\,M_{\pi^0}^2$
- Fitted multipole contributions: $|E1+M2|^2 = 2.3 \pm 0.5 \pm 0.3$ nb; $|E1-M2|^2 = 18.9 \pm 0.9 \pm 0.8$ nb; $|E2|^2 < 0.2$ nb (one standard deviation upper limit)
- Overall systematic uncertainty of about 15% from luminosity, acceptance, and background subtraction
- Detector acceptance between 20% and 30% for most kinematic regions
- Proton polar angle coverage: $3^\circ < \theta_p < 22^\circ$; minimum proton energy 39 MeV

## Context

This work extends measurements of $pp\to\{pp\}_s\gamma$ beyond the limited angular coverage of earlier COSY-ANKE data at 353, 500, and 550 MeV (Komarov, 2008), which showed a forward dip at higher energies. The results contradict theoretical predictions by Jouni (2008) and Kanzo (2008) that the $E2$ multipole should be large above 200 MeV, and also conflict with claims from $^3$He$(\gamma,2p)n$ experiments (Emura, 2005; Niccolai, 2006; Audit, 2007) that the reaction is dominated by $E2$ transitions. The data enable future measurements of proton analyzing powers and spin correlations at COSY (Kulikov, 2008) to further clarify the role of higher multipoles and the $\Delta(1232)$ isobar at higher energies.
