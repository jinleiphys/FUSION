# Thermal Color-superconducting Fluctuations in Dense Quark Matter

> Thermal fluctuations of the color superconducting order parameter in dense quark matter are investigated in terms of the phenomenological Ginzburg - Landau approach. Our estimates show that fluctuations of the di-quark gap may strongly affect some of thermodynamic quantities even far below and above the critical temperature. If the critical temperature $T_c$ of the di-quark phase transition were rather high ($\gsim (50÷70)$ MeV) one could expect a manifestation of fluctuations of the di-quark gap in the course of heavy ion collisions (above $T_c$). For $T_c \sim 50 $MeV color superconducting fluctuations may also affect an initial stage of the hybrid star evolution.

# Thermal Color-superconducting Fluctuations in Dense Quark Matter

## Key claim

Thermal fluctuations of the di-quark gap may strongly affect thermodynamic quantities even far below and above the critical temperature, provided the critical temperature is rather high (greater than about 50 to 70 MeV). The author estimates that fluctuations dominate over mean-field contributions for temperatures as low as approximately 0.6 to 0.8 times the critical temperature below T_c, and up to about twice T_c above it, depending on the ratio T_c/mu_q.

## Method

The author employs a phenomenological Ginzburg-Landau approach, expanding the thermodynamic potential up to fourth order in the di-quark field operators and assuming a second-order phase transition. Fluctuations are treated within a self-consistent Hartree approximation (Phi-functional up to one vertex), and the contribution of fluctuations to the specific heat is calculated analytically in the low-temperature and high-temperature limits using Matsubara frequency summation. The Ginzburg-Levanyuk criterion is applied to compare fluctuation and mean-field contributions to the specific heat, and the Ginzburg number is estimated to determine the width of the fluctuation region.

## Key numbers

- The Ginzburg number Gi = Delta T/T_c is estimated as Gi ~ A (T_c/mu_q)^4 with A ~ 500, giving Gi ~ 1 when T_c is about 1/3 to 1/5 of mu_q.
- For typical quark chemical potentials mu_q ~ 350 to 500 MeV and T_c greater than about 50 to 70 MeV, the temperature below T_c where fluctuations become as important as mean field is estimated as T_{fl,<}^C ~ (0.6 to 0.8) T_c.
- The zero-temperature pairing gap Delta is predicted to be ~ 20 to 200 MeV for mu_q ~ 300 to 500 MeV.
- The critical temperature is related to the gap as T_c ~ 0.57 Delta for the 2SC phase and T_c ~ 0.7 Delta for the CFL phase.
- For the specific heat of the quark-gluon plasma in the weak coupling limit: C_V^{qg} ~ 6 mu_q^2 T + (42 pi^2 T^3)/15 + (32 pi^2 T^3)/15.
- The fluctuation contribution to specific heat above T_c is estimated to become comparable to the normal-state contribution at T_{fl,>}^C ~ 2 T_c for T_c/mu_q ~ 0.1 to 0.3.

## Context

This work extends the study of color-superconducting fluctuations beyond the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model treatment of Kitazawa et al. (2002), which demonstrated that fluctuating pair fields produce a prominent peak in the spectral function only within a narrow temperature interval |T-T_c| less than about (0.1 to 0.2) T_c. The present Ginzburg-Landau analysis suggests a much broader fluctuation region, potentially affecting heavy-ion collision observables at the future GSI Compressed Baryon Matter facility and the initial cooling stage of hybrid stars with quark cores. The approach parallels fluctuation theory in condensed matter physics as reviewed by Larkin and Varlamov, but with the important difference that the time (frequency) dependence of fluctuations cannot be neglected in the color-superconducting case because the low-temperature limit applies over much of the fluctuation region.
