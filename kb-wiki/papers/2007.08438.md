# Nucleon polarizabilities in covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory with explicit $Δ$ degrees of freedom

> We compute various nucleon polarizabilities in chiral perturbation theory implementing the $Δ$-full ($Δ$-less) approach up to order $ε^3 + q^4$ ($q^4$) in the small-scale (chiral) expansion. The calculation is carried out using the covariant formulation of $χ$PT by utilizing the extended on-mass shell renormalization scheme. Except for the spin-independent dipole polarizabilities used to fix the values of certain low-energy constants, our results for the nucleon polarizabilities are pure predictions. We compare our calculations with available experimental data and other theoretical results. The importance of the explicit treatment of the $Δ$ degree of freedom in the effective field theory description of the nucleon polarizabilities is analyzed. We also study the convergence of the $1/m$ expansion and analyze the efficiency of the heavy-baryon approach for the nucleon polarizabilities.

## Key claim

The authors compute nucleon polarizabilities in covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory up to order \(\epsilon^3 + q^4\) (with explicit \(\Delta\) degrees of freedom) and \(q^4\) (without \(\Delta\)), finding that most results are pure predictions except for the spin-independent dipole polarizabilities, which are used to fix certain low-energy constants; the explicit inclusion of the \(\Delta\) is shown to improve convergence compared to the \(\Delta\)-less scheme.

## Method

The Compton scattering amplitude for nucleons is constructed using the covariant formulation of chiral perturbation theory with the extended on-mass-shell (EOMS) renormalization scheme, including explicit \(\Delta(1232)\) degrees of freedom via the small-scale expansion (SSE). Tree-level and loop diagrams up to order \(\epsilon^3 + q^4\) are evaluated, with the \(\Delta\)-full and \(\Delta\)-less approaches compared; the spin-independent dipole polarizabilities \(\alpha_{E1}\) and \(\beta_{M1}\) are fitted to experimental data, while all other polarizabilities are parameter-free predictions.

## Key numbers

- Proton dipole polarizabilities: \(\alpha_{E1}^p = (10.65 \pm 0.35) \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^3\) and \(\beta_{M1}^p = (3.15 \pm 0.35) \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^3\) (fitted to experiment, order \(\epsilon^3 + q^4\)).
- Neutron dipole polarizabilities: \(\alpha_{E1}^n = (11.8 \pm 1.1) \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^3\) and \(\beta_{M1}^n = (3.7 \pm 1.2) \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^3\) (predictions at order \(\epsilon^3 + q^4\)).
- Proton backward spin polarizability: \(\gamma_\pi^p = (8.0 \pm 1.8) \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^4\) (prediction at order \(\epsilon^3 + q^4\)).
- Proton forward spin polarizability: \(\gamma_0^p = (-0.9 \pm 0.2) \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^4\) (prediction at order \(\epsilon^3 + q^4\)).
- Neutron backward spin polarizability: \(\gamma_\pi^n = (7.8 \pm 1.9) \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^4\) (prediction at order \(\epsilon^3 + q^4\)).
- Neutron forward spin polarizability: \(\gamma_0^n = (1.1 \pm 0.3) \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^4\) (prediction at order \(\epsilon^3 + q^4\)).
- The \(\Delta\)-full results at order \(\epsilon^3 + q^4\) reduce the \(\Delta\)-less \(q^4\) values for \(\alpha_{E1}^p\) by about \(1.5 \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^3\) and for \(\beta_{M1}^p\) by about \(0.5 \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^3\).

## Context

This work extends prior chiral perturbation theory calculations of nucleon polarizabilities, such as those by Bernard et al. (1991) and Hemmert et al. (1997), by using a covariant formulation with explicit \(\Delta\) degrees of freedom, improving upon the heavy-baryon approach which violates certain analytic properties (Becher, 1999). The results are compared with fixed-\(t\) dispersion-relation analyses by Babusci et al. (1998) and Pasquini et al. (2007), and with \(\delta\)-counting schemes by Pascalutsa (2002); the inclusion of the \(\Delta\) is shown to enhance convergence, enabling more reliable predictions for higher-order polarizabilities and generalized (momentum-transfer-dependent) polarizabilities.
