# Meson elastic and transition form factors

> The Dyson-Schwinger equations of QCD, truncated to ladder-rainbow level, are used to calculate meson form factors in impulse approximation. The infrared strength of the ladder-rainbow kernel is described by two parameters fitted to the chiral condensate and f_pi; the ultraviolet behavior is fixed by the QCD running coupling. This obtained elastic form factors F_pi(Q^2) and F_K(Q^2) agree well with the available data. We also calculate the rho to pi gamma and K* to K gamma transition form factors, which are useful for meson-exchange models.

## Key claim

The Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) of QCD, truncated to ladder-rainbow level and with two infrared parameters fitted to the chiral condensate and \(f_\pi\), produce elastic form factors \(F_\pi(Q^2)\) and \(F_K(Q^2)\) that agree well with available data, and transition form factors for \(\rho \to \pi \gamma\) and \(K^\star \to K \gamma\) that are useful for meson-exchange models; however, the agreement for the charged \(K^\star \to K \gamma\) decay width is notably worse than for the other channels, and the isospin-symmetric impulse approximation cannot account for the difference between neutral and charged \(\rho\) decay widths.

## Method

The dressed quark propagator, meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes (BSAs), and dressed quark-photon vertex are all obtained as solutions of their respective DSEs in rainbow-ladder truncation, using an effective quark-quark interaction that reduces to the one-loop QCD running coupling in the ultraviolet and is fitted to the chiral condensate, \(m_{\pi/K}\), and \(f_\pi\) in the infrared.  Meson elastic form factors are then computed via a triangle-loop integral in impulse approximation, with the photon coupled to the quark or antiquark; transition form factors for vector-pseudoscalar-photon vertices are computed analogously, using one vector meson BSA, one pseudoscalar BSA, and the quark-photon vertex.  No model parameters are adjusted beyond the initial fit.

## Key numbers

*   The calculated \(\rho\)-meson mass is \(m_\rho = 742\ \mathrm{MeV}\), slightly below the experimental value.
*   The calculated vector meson masses are within 5% of their experimental values; the vector meson electroweak decay constants are within 9% of the data.
*   For the \(\rho^\pm \to \pi^\pm \gamma\) decay, the calculated \(g/m = 0.69\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\) and partial width \(\Gamma = 53\ \mathrm{keV}\) (experiment: \(0.74\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\) and \(68\ \mathrm{keV}\)).
*   For the \(\omega \to \pi \gamma\) decay, the calculated \(g/m = 2.07\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\) and \(\Gamma = 479\ \mathrm{keV}\) (experiment: \(2.31\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\) and \(717\ \mathrm{keV}\)).
*   For the charged \(K^{\star\pm} \to K^\pm \gamma\) decay, the calculated \(g/m = 0.99\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\) and \(\Gamma = 90\ \mathrm{keV}\) (experiment: \(0.83\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\) and \(50.3\ \mathrm{keV}\)).
*   For the neutral \(K^{\star 0} \to K^0 \gamma\) decay, the calculated \(g/m = 1.19\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\) and \(\Gamma = 130\ \mathrm{keV}\) (experiment: \(1.28\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\) and \(116\ \mathrm{keV}\)).
*   The \(F_{\rho\pi\gamma}(Q^2)\) transition form factor is found to have an asymptotic behavior of \(1/Q^4\).

## Context

This work extends the DSE-based approach of Maris and Tandy (2000) and Maris and Tandy (2000) for elastic form factors to include vector-pseudoscalar-photon transition form factors, using the same rainbow-ladder truncation and model parameters fitted to the chiral condensate and pion properties (Maris and Tandy, 1999).  The results for the elastic form factors agree with experimental data from JLab (Volmer, 2001) and earlier experiments (A86, DESY), and the transition form factors are intended for use in meson-exchange models of hadronic processes (Tandy, 1997; VODG95).  The paper enables future comparisons with JLab data at higher \(Q^2\) (3 to 5 GeV\(^2\)) and motivates a microscopic description of meson electroproduction, building on recent progress in describing four-point processes within the DSE framework (Bicudo, 2001).
