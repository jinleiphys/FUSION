# J/Psi production off nuclei: a detour from SPS to LHC

> We present a collection of selected phenomena observed in J/Psi production from proton-nucleus and heavy ion collisions at energies, ranging between the SPS and LHC. The emphasis is placed on the related theoretical ideas or techniques, which are either not widely known, or offer an alternative explanation to the observed nuclear effects.

# J/Psi production off nuclei: a detour from SPS to LHC

## Key claim

The paper argues that the commonly assumed factorization between initial-state interactions (ISI) and final-state interactions (FSI) in J/Psi production off nuclei is not straightforward, because the "cold nuclear matter" in AA collisions is actually not cold, it consists of excited nucleon debris with enhanced interaction cross sections, and because at high energies the saturation scales in colliding nuclei become mutually boosted, making the medium more opaque than simple extrapolations from pA data would suggest.

## Method

The authors employ the dipole model of QCD, treating the produced c-cbar pair as a color dipole whose transverse size evolves from an initial small separation (~0.1 fm) up to the J/Psi mean size (~0.5 fm) over a formation time. They use the path-integral technique to describe the quantum-mechanical evolution of the dipole in nuclear matter, with a light-cone potential containing both a real binding part (oscillatory form) and an imaginary part proportional to the dipole cross section. For the transition from pA to AA collisions, they introduce bootstrap equations for the mutual boosting of saturation scales in the two colliding nuclei and a double-color-filtering effect that modifies the survival probability of dipoles traversing both nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Initial c-cbar dipole separation: r_cc ~ 1/m_c ~ 0.1 fm; J/Psi mean size: r_J/Psi ~ 0.5 fm
- Formation time: t_f = 0.1 fm * (E_psi / 1 GeV)
- Production time: t_p = 1/(x_2 m_N), about 5 times shorter than t_f
- Mean number of radiated gluons per nucleon: 0.69 at sqrt(s)=20 GeV, 0.0069 at sqrt(s)=200 GeV, 0.0012 at sqrt(s)=1200 GeV
- Transport coefficient for gold-gold at RHIC: q_0 = 0.6 GeV^2/fm (adjusted to data)
- Transport coefficient for lead-lead at LHC: q_0 = 0.8 GeV^2/fm (extracted from ALICE high-pT hadron data)
- Broadening formula: Delta_pA(b) = (9/8) * (d^2 sigma_dip/dr_T^2)|_{r_T=0} * T_A(b)
- Mean pT^2 parametrization for pp: <pT^2> = [-2.4 + 0.6 ln(s)] GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the dipole model approach of Kopeliovich et al. (1991) and the path-integral technique of Kopeliovich and Zakharov (1991) to describe J/Psi production across a wide energy range from SPS to LHC. It contradicts the oversimplified constant-absorption-cross-section model used by the NA60 collaboration, showing that the effective absorption cross section decreases with energy due to color transparency. The paper also challenges the interpretation of "anomalous" J/Psi suppression at SPS as a signal of hot matter, arguing instead that enhanced ISI from excited nucleon debris (as predicted by Hufner, Kopeliovich, and collaborators) can explain the data. The results enable predictions for J/Psi suppression at LHC energies and provide an alternative probe for the transport coefficient of the medium created in heavy ion collisions, yielding values much smaller than those extracted from jet quenching analyses based on energy loss scenarios.
