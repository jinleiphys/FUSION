# Early evolution constrained by high-$p_T$ QGP tomography

> We show that high-$p_T$ $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$ are sensitive to the early expansion dynamics, and that the high-$p_T$ observables prefer delayed onset of energy loss and transverse expansion. To calculate high-$p_T$ $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$, we employ our newly developed DREENA-A framework, which combines state-of-the-art dynamical energy loss model with 3+1-dimensional hydrodynamical simulations. The model applies to both light and heavy flavor, and we predict a larger sensitivity of heavy flavor observables to the onset of transverse expansion. This presents the first time when bulk QGP behavior has been constrained by high-$p_\perp$ observables and related theory, i.e., by so-called QGP tomography.

## Key claim

High-pT RAA and v2 observables are sensitive to the early expansion dynamics of the quark-gluon plasma, and the data prefer a scenario where both the onset of energy loss and transverse expansion are delayed to approximately 1 fm/c, though the authors acknowledge this "no energy loss nor transverse expansion" scenario is unrealistic and serves only as a limiting test of their hypothesis.

## Method

The authors employed the DREENA-A framework, which combines a dynamical energy loss formalism (including both radiative and collisional energy loss) with 3+1-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic simulations. They tested four scenarios for early evolution: (1) early fluid-dynamical evolution starting at tau0 = 0.2 fm with energy loss starting at tau_q = 0.2 fm; (2) early fluid-dynamical evolution starting at tau0 = 0.2 fm but with energy loss delayed to tau_q = 1.0 fm; (3) free-streaming initialization until tau0 = tau_q = 1.0 fm; and (4) "old-fashioned" scenario with no pre-hydro flow and tau0 = tau_q = 1.0 fm. The hydrodynamic parameters were tuned separately for each scenario to match observed charged particle multiplicities and v2{4} in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV.

## Key numbers

- Charged hadron RAA and v2 predictions were compared with ALICE, CMS, and ATLAS data for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV across four centrality classes: 10-20%, 20-30%, 30-40%, and 40-50%
- The dynamical energy loss formalism uses Lambda_QCD = 0.2 GeV, charm mass M = 1.2 GeV, bottom mass M = 4.75 GeV, and magnetic to electric mass ratio mu_M/mu_E = 0.5 (leading to uncertainty of up to 10% for both RAA and v2)
- Freeze-out temperatures were T_chem = 150 MeV and T_dec = 100 MeV for cases without pre-hydro flow, and T_chem = 175 MeV and T_dec = 140 MeV for free-streaming initialization
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio was eta/s = 0.12 for cases without pre-hydro flow and eta/s = 0.16 for free-streaming initialization
- The predictions are considered reliable only for pT > 10 GeV, as the dynamical energy loss assumptions break down below this momentum

## Context

This work extends the QGP tomography approach where bulk QGP parameters are jointly constrained by low- and high-pT physics, in contrast to traditional approaches that use high-pT probes almost exclusively to study parton-medium interactions. The authors note that previous work by Renk et al. (2010) suggested delayed cooling, but this study additionally finds that the early build-up of transverse expansion must be delayed as well. The results are compared with approaches like KOPOST based on effective kinetic theory (Kurkela et al., 2018), which the authors expect would not resolve the discrepancy. The findings enable future testing through upcoming high luminosity heavy flavor measurements, as heavy flavor observables show larger sensitivity to early evolution details.
