# Global study of beyond-mean-field correlation energies in covariant energy density functional theory using a collective Hamiltonian method

> We report the first global study of dynamic correlation energies (DCEs) associated with rotational motion and quadrupole shape vibrational motion in a covariant energy density functional (CEDF) for 575 even-even nuclei with proton numbers ranging from $Z=8$ to $Z=108$ by solving a five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian, the collective parameters of which are determined from triaxial relativistic mean-field plus BCS calculation using the PC-PK1 force. After taking into account these beyond mean-field DCEs, the root-mean-square (rms) deviation with respect to nuclear masses is reduced significantly down to 1.14 MeV, which is smaller than those of other successful CEDFs: NL3* (2.96 MeV), DD-ME2 (2.39 MeV), DD-ME$δ$ (2.29 MeV) and DD-PC1 (2.01 MeV). Moreover, the rms deviation for two-nucleon separation energies is reduced by $\sim34\%$ in comparison with cranking prescription.

## Key claim

After including beyond-mean-field dynamic correlation energies computed via the five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian (5DCH) method, the root-mean-square deviation of covariant energy density functional (CEDF) predictions for nuclear masses is reduced to 1.14 MeV, which is about 100 keV better than the cranking prescription used in prior work. The improvement is conditional on the use of the PC-PK1 force and on setting the correlation energy to zero for nuclei around double-closed shells where the 5DCH method is inappropriate.

## Method

The authors performed triaxial relativistic mean-field plus BCS (RMF+BCS) calculations with the PC-PK1 point-coupling force and a separable pairing force to generate mean-field wave functions across the entire (beta, gamma) deformation plane for 575 even-even nuclei (Z=8 to Z=108). From these mean-field results, they computed the mass parameters, moments of inertia, and collective potentials needed to solve the five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian (5DCH), which approximates angular momentum projection and generator coordinate method (GCM) under the Gaussian overlap approximation (GOA). The dynamic correlation energy (DCE) was defined as the energy difference between the mean-field ground state and the 5DCH ground state, with DCE set to zero for nuclei near double-closed shells.

## Key numbers

- Root-mean-square (rms) deviation of CEDF masses from experimental data after including DCEs: 1.14 MeV (for 575 even-even nuclei)
- Rms deviations for other CEDFs without DCEs: NL3* (2.96 MeV), DD-ME2 (2.39 MeV), DD-MEδ (2.29 MeV), DD-PC1 (2.01 MeV)
- Rms deviation for two-neutron separation energies after including DCEs: 0.62 MeV (for 521 nuclei)
- Rms deviation for two-proton separation energies after including DCEs: 0.69 MeV (for 497 nuclei)
- Reduction in rms deviation for two-nucleon separation energies compared to cranking prescription: approximately 34%
- DCE values range from 0 to approximately 4.4 MeV, mostly between 2.0 and 3.5 MeV
- Average static triaxial deformation energy correction: approximately 0.36 MeV (for 31 nuclei)

## Context

This work extends previous global studies of dynamic correlation energies in CEDF that used the cranking prescription (Zhang et al., 2014) by adopting the more rigorous 5DCH method, which properly accounts for shape coexistence and shape fluctuations in transitional nuclei. The results are compared with 5DCH calculations based on the Gogny D1S force (Delaunay et al., 2010) and with Skyrme-based mass tables that used phenomenological cranking prescriptions (Goriely et al., 2009, 2013). The improved mass predictions enable more reliable calculations of nuclear properties for astrophysical nucleosynthesis simulations and for further refinement of energy density functionals.
