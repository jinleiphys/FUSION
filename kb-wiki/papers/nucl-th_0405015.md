# Study on chemical equilibrium in nucleus-nucleus collisions at relativistic energies

> We present a detailed study of chemical freeze-out in nucleus-nucleus collisions at beam energies of 11.6, 30, 40, 80 and 158A GeV. By analyzing hadronic multiplicities within the statistical hadronization approach, we have studied the chemical equilibration of the system as a function of center of mass energy and of the parameters of the source. Additionally, we have tested and compared different versions of the statistical model, with special emphasis on possible explanations of the observed strangeness hadronic phase space under-saturation.

## Key claim

The statistical hadronization model with a strangeness under-saturation parameter (γ_S) fits all analyzed nucleus-nucleus collision data, but the observed peak in relative strangeness production at 30A GeV beam energy may be an indication of deconfinement phase transition, though the data do not definitively require additional non-equilibrium parameters beyond γ_S.

## Method

The authors analyzed hadronic multiplicities from central Pb-Pb collisions at 30, 40, 80, and 158A GeV (NA49 experiment) and Au-Au collisions at 11.6A GeV (AGS experiments) using the statistical hadronization model (SHM). They performed χ² minimization fits with temperature T, baryon chemical potential μ_B, strangeness suppression factor γ_S, and scaling volume V as free parameters, then tested three alternative models: strangeness canonical ensemble with local strangeness correlation, superposition of nucleon-nucleon collisions with an equilibrated fireball (SHM(TC)), and a model with separate non-equilibrium parameters for light quarks (γ_q) and strange quarks (γ_s).

## Key numbers

- At 158A GeV Pb-Pb collisions: T = 157.8 ± 1.4 MeV, μ_B = 247.3 ± 5.2 MeV, γ_S = 0.843 ± 0.024 (main analysis A)
- At 11.6A GeV Au-Au collisions: T = 118.1 ± 3.5 MeV, μ_B = 555 ± 12 MeV, γ_S = 0.652 ± 0.069 (main analysis A)
- At 30A GeV Pb-Pb collisions: γ_S = 0.938 ± 0.078 (main analysis A), the highest value across all beam energies
- At 40A GeV Pb-Pb collisions: γ_S = 0.757 ± 0.024 (main analysis A)
- At 80A GeV Pb-Pb collisions: γ_S = 0.730 ± 0.021 (main analysis A)
- Chemical freeze-out curve fit: T = 0.167 - 0.153 μ_B² (T and μ_B in GeV)
- Strangeness canonical ensemble fit at 158A GeV with γ_S=1: χ²/dof = 37.2/9, with cluster volume about 2.5% of overall volume
- SHM(TC) model at 158A GeV: predicted number of single NN collisions ≈ 50 with 16% uncertainty
- γ_q model absolute minimum at γ_q ≈ 1.62 with T ≈ 140 MeV and χ² ≈ 13; local minimum at γ_q = 0.6 with T = 187 MeV

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadronization approach of Becattini (2001) and others to a systematic study across five beam energies in the AGS-SPS range, confirming earlier findings of strangeness under-saturation (γ_S < 1) reported by Becattini (2001), Bialas (1999), and Cleymans (1999). The observed peak in γ_S and the K⁺/π⁺ ratio at 30A GeV connects to the deconfinement phase transition anomalies reported by Gazdzicki (1998), and the results enable future comparisons with RHIC and LHC energies to map the full energy dependence of chemical freeze-out parameters.
