# Non-Abelian Alice strings in two-flavor dense QCD

> Quark-hadron continuity with two-flavor quarks that was proposed recently connects hadronic matter with neutron $^3P_2$ superfluidity and two-flavor dense quark matter. This two-flavor dense quark phase consists of the coexistence of the 2SC condensates and the $P$-wave diquark condensates of $d$-quarks, which gives rise to color superconductivity as well as superfluidity. We classify vortices in this phase. The most stable vortices are what we call the non-Abelian Alice strings, which are superfluid vortices with non-Abelian color magnetic fluxes therein, exhibiting so-called topological obstruction, or a non-Abelian generalization of the Alice property. We show that a single Abelian superfluid vortex is unstable against decay into three non-Abelian Alice strings. We discover that a non-Abelian Alice string carries orientational moduli of the real projective space $\mathbb{R}P^2$ corresponding to the color flux therein in the presence of the $P$-wave condensates alone. We calculate Aharanov-Bohm (AB) phases around the non-Abelian Alice string, and find that the 2SC condensates and string's orientational moduli must be aligned with each other because of single-valuedness of the AB phases of the 2SC condensates.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in the two-flavor dense quark matter phase (2SC+dd phase), the most stable vortices are non-Abelian Alice strings carrying 1/3 fractional winding in U(1)B and non-Abelian color magnetic fluxes, but this stability depends on the specific symmetry breaking pattern where the dd condensate (P-wave diquark condensate of d-quarks) is turned on first, followed by the 2SC condensate.

## Method

The authors classify vortices in the 2SC+dd phase by analyzing the homotopy groups of the order parameter manifolds resulting from sequential spontaneous symmetry breaking of G_QCD = SU(3)_C × U(1)_B. They first turn on the dd condensate (phidd) to break symmetry to H_dd = SO(3)_C ⋉ (Z_6)_C+B, then turn on the 2SC condensate (phiud) to further break to K_2SC+dd = SO(2)_C × (Z_6)_C+B. They construct explicit vortex ansätze for the condensate and gauge fields, compute the holonomy at spatial infinity, and analyze the Aharanov-Bohm phases of quasi-quarks around the strings.

## Key numbers

- The non-Abelian Alice string carries 1/3 quantized circulation in U(1)_B and 1/6 quantized color-magnetic flux, where the unit color-magnetic flux is defined as F_0 = 2π/g with g being the SU(3) gauge coupling constant
- A single Abelian superfluid vortex (carrying unit quantized circulation in U(1)_B) is unstable against decay into three non-Abelian Alice strings
- The orientational moduli space of the non-Abelian Alice string is RP^2 (real projective space), corresponding to S^2/Z_2, in contrast to the CP^2 moduli space of non-Abelian vortices in the CFL phase
- The unbroken symmetry around the vortex core is O(2)_C × (Z_6)_C+B, where the Z_2 factor is generated by diag(1,-1,-1), diag(-1,1,-1), or their linear combinations

## Context

This work extends the concept of quark-hadron continuity proposed by Fujimoto (2019, 2020) to vortex classification in the two-flavor dense quark matter phase, which connects to neutron 3P2 superfluidity in hadronic matter. The non-Abelian Alice strings exhibit topological obstruction similar to Alice strings studied by Schwarz (1982), Alford (1990, 1992), and Preskill (1990), but with a non-Abelian generalization. The vortices are analogous to non-Abelian semi-superfluid vortices in the CFL phase studied by Balachandran (2005), Nakano (2007, 2008), and Eto (2009, 2013), but with different orientational moduli (RP^2 versus CP^2). This work enables future studies of vortex dynamics in neutron star cores and the connection between quark matter vortices and hadronic vortices in the context of quark-hadron continuity.
