# Coulomb corrections to low energy antiproton annihilation cross sections on protons and nuclei

> We calculate, in a systematic way, the enhancement effect on antiproton-proton and antiproton-nucleus annihilation cross sections at low energy due to the initial state electrostatic interaction between the projectile and the target nucleus. This calculation is aimed at future comparisons between antineutron and antiproton annihilation rates on different targets, for the extraction of pure isospin channels.

## Key claim

The paper systematically calculates the Coulomb enhancement factor for antiproton annihilation cross sections on protons and nuclei at low momenta (30-400 MeV/c), showing that the commonly used pointlike approximation significantly overestimates the Coulomb correction for heavy nuclei, while for light nuclei the model dependence of the correction is small.

## Method

The authors use an optical potential model with a Woods-Saxon form for the annihilation interaction (standard nuclear potential: imaginary strength 25 MeV, radius 1.3 A^(1/3) fm, diffuseness 0.5 fm) and a uniform charge distribution with radius 1.25 A^(1/3) fm for the electrostatic potential. They calculate the ratio R_A,Z = sigma_charged/sigma_neutral for various target nuclei by solving the scattering problem with and without the Coulomb interaction, and compare results using different optical potential parameters to assess model dependence.

## Key numbers

* For heavy nuclei (A > 50) in the range 50-400 MeV/c, R_A,Z can be fit by R = 1 + 10^(-5) (45 - 0.0075 A) Z beta^(-2.07) with a few percent precision.
* For hydrogen target with the standard nuclear potential, R_1,1 = 1 + 0.00030 beta^(-2) fits within 5% over 35-400 MeV/c, while a more precise fit for 70-400 MeV/c uses C_2 = 0.0040 with errors within 1%.
* For hydrogen target with the BP potential, the same beta^(-2) law with C_2 = 0.0040 works for 70-400 MeV/c, and for 30-70 MeV/c a beta^(-1.4) law with C_1.4 = 0.0110 fits within 5%.
* For helium-4 target with the standard nuclear potential, the range 80-400 MeV/c fits with C_2 = 0.00130 using the Z beta^(-2) law, and the range 40-400 MeV/c fits with C_1.4 = 0.0040 using the Z beta^(-1.4) law (within 3%).
* For deuteron target with the standard nuclear potential, R_2,1 fits perfectly in the range 30-200 MeV/c with the beta^(-1.4) law and C_1.4 = 0.0060.
* For heavy nuclei (A = 200, Z = 100), the ratio sigma_ann(k)/sigma_ann(2k) equals 3.85 at 20-40 MeV/c, compared to the k^(-2) law prediction of 4.
* For heavy nuclei, model dependence of R_A,Z is below 10% at momenta around 30 MeV/c, half of that at 50 MeV/c, and satisfactory above 100 MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the understanding of Coulomb corrections beyond the pointlike approximation used in earlier treatments (e.g., the Landau-Lifshitz formula) by accounting for finite nuclear size, the extended nature of the annihilation region, and the interplay between strong and Coulomb forces. The calculations are aimed at enabling future comparisons between antiproton and antineutron annihilation rates on different targets for extracting pure isospin channels, as discussed in the context of data from experiments such as OBELIX (Obe1-Obe4) and the potential AD facility measurements. The paper provides analytical fits that allow interpolation for most nuclear targets, addressing the need identified in earlier work by Bruckner et al. (1990s) on antiproton-proton elastic scattering and by the authors' own previous studies on inversion effects in low-energy annihilation.
