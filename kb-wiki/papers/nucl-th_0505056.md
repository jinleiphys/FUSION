# Low energy 0+ excitations in 158Gd

> High precision (p,t) studies of the deformed nucleus 158Gd allowed the observation of 13 excited 0+ states below an excitation energy of 3.1 MeV. This high density of low energy states, and particularly their measured B(E2) transition strengths to the first excited 2+ state challenge nuclear models. The pseudo SU(3) model, which successfully describes many excited bands in Dy isotopes, is used to analyze this nuclei. We have fairly good reproduction of most of the states but the absence of actively including nucleons occupying intruders orbits may be the reason for the observed limitations of the model.

## Key claim

The pseudo SU(3) model can reproduce the excitation energies of many of the 13 observed excited 0+ states in 158Gd below 3.1 MeV, but the calculated B(E2) transition strengths to the first excited 2+ state are two or more orders of magnitude smaller than the experimental values, suggesting that the model's omission of explicit nucleons in intruder orbits is the likely cause of this limitation.

## Method

The authors constructed a pseudo SU(3) many-body basis for 158Gd by filling deformed Nilsson levels (deformation epsilon_2 = 0.25) to determine normal and unique parity orbital occupancies for the 14 valence protons (8 in normal parity, 6 in intruder orbits) and 12 valence neutrons (8 in normal parity, 4 in intruder orbits). They performed two sets of calculations: one using only pseudo-spin zero proton and neutron states, and an enlarged calculation including pseudo-spin zero and one states, both truncated by a C_2 cutoff. The Hamiltonian included spherical Nilsson single-particle terms, quadrupole-quadrupole and pairing interactions, plus five rotor-like terms (J^2, K_J^2, C_2, C_3, S^2) used to fine-tune the calculated spectra.

## Key numbers

- 13 excited 0+ states were observed below an excitation energy of 3.1 MeV in 158Gd via high-precision (p,t) studies
- Experimental B(E2) values for transitions from excited 0+ states to the first excited 2+ state range from 0.9 +/- 0.9 W.u. (0+_12 at 2911.2 keV) to 6.4 +/- 3.7 W.u. (0+_10 at 2643.4 keV)
- In the restricted basis (case 1), only 4 calculated 0+ states appear below 3 MeV, compared to 13 observed experimentally
- In the enlarged basis (case 2), 7 calculated 0+ states appear below 3 MeV
- The calculated B(E2; 0_i -> 2_1) values in both cases are 0.08 W.u. or smaller, while experimental values are all above 0.9 W.u.
- The 1+ state is calculated at 620 keV when all fitting parameters are zero, while it is observed at 1.84 MeV

## Context

This work extends the pseudo SU(3) model, previously successful in describing excited bands in 157Gd, 163Dy, 169Tm (Vargas 2002) and 158,160,162,164Dy (Vargas 2004), to the newly observed high density of 0+ states in 158Gd from high-precision (p,t) studies (Lesher 2002). The results challenge earlier calculations using the geometrical collective model (Troltenier 1991) and sd-IBM (Iachello 1987) which predicted only 5 excited 0+ states below 3 MeV, while the pseudo SU(3) model with enlarged basis produces 7 states. The inability to reproduce the experimental B(E2) strengths to the ground-state band points to the need for explicit inclusion of intruder orbit configurations, which the authors note is under investigation.
