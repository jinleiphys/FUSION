# Relativistic Energy Density Functional Description of Shape Transition in Superheavy Nuclei

> Relativistic energy density functionals (REDF) provide a complete and accurate, global description of nuclear structure phenomena. A modern semi-empirical functional, adjusted to the nuclear matter equation of state and to empirical masses of deformed nuclei, is applied to studies of shapes of superheavy nuclei. The theoretical framework is tested in a comparison of calculated masses, quadrupole deformations, and potential energy barriers to available data on actinide isotopes. Self-consistent mean-field calculations predict a variety of spherical, axial and triaxial shapes of long-lived superheavy nuclei, and their alpha-decay energies and half-lives are compared to data. A microscopic, REDF-based, quadrupole collective Hamiltonian model is used to study the effect of explicit treatment of collective correlations in the calculation of Qα values and half-lives.

## Key claim

The relativistic energy density functional DD-PC1, adjusted only to masses of deformed nuclei, reproduces ground-state properties, fission barriers, and alpha-decay energies of actinides and superheavy nuclei with good accuracy, but the agreement with experimental alpha-decay data for superheavy nuclei is not quite as good as that obtained with the macro-micro model specially adapted to heaviest nuclei.

## Method

Self-consistent relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations were performed using the DD-PC1 functional for the particle-hole channel and a separable pairing interaction in the particle-particle channel, with nucleon spinors expanded in a 3D harmonic oscillator basis in Cartesian coordinates. Constrained energy surfaces were computed for axial, triaxial, and octupole deformations to map potential energy surfaces, and a quadrupole collective Hamiltonian based on the same functional was used to calculate ground-state-to-ground-state transition energies including collective correlations.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy deviations from experiment for actinide isotopes (Th, U, Pu, Cm, Cf, Fm, No): absolute difference less than 1 MeV in all cases
- Nuclear matter saturation properties of DD-PC1: density 0.152 fm^-3, volume energy -16.06 MeV, surface energy 17.498 MeV, symmetry energy 33 MeV, compression modulus 230 MeV
- Separable pairing force parameters: G = -728 MeV fm^3, a = 0.644 fm, adjusted to Gogny D1S force
- Inclusion of triaxial shapes lowers inner fission barriers of actinides by approximately 2 MeV
- Triaxiality lowers outer fission barriers of superheavy nuclei by 1.5 to 3 MeV
- Largest difference between theoretical and experimental Q_alpha values for superheavy decay chains is less than 1 MeV

## Context

This work extends self-consistent mean-field studies of superheavy nuclei previously performed with Skyrme functionals (Cwiok et al., 1996; Cwiok et al., 1999; Cwiok and Heenen, 2005) and the macro-micro model (Sobiczewski and Pomorski, 2007) to the relativistic energy density functional framework using the recently introduced DD-PC1 functional. The results enable systematic predictions of shape transitions and alpha-decay properties for superheavy elements with Z=110-120, and the collective Hamiltonian approach provides a path toward more accurate calculations that include correlations beyond the mean-field level.
