# Effect of event selection on jetlike correlation measurement in $d$+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}}=200$ GeV

> Dihadron correlations are analyzed in $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}} = 200$ GeV $d$+Au collisions classified by forward charged particle multiplicity and zero-degree neutral energy in the Au-beam direction. It is found that the jetlike correlated yield increases with the event multiplicity. After taking into account this dependence, the non-jet contribution on the away side is minimal, leaving little room for a back-to-back ridge in these collisions.

## Key claim

The jetlike correlated yield in dihadron correlations increases with event multiplicity in d+Au collisions at 200 GeV; after accounting for this dependence, the non-jet contribution on the away side becomes minimal, leaving little room for a back-to-back ridge. The abstract glosses over the condition that this conclusion depends on the specific event-activity classification used (forward charged particle multiplicity or zero-degree neutral energy in the Au-beam direction).

## Method

Dihadron correlations were measured in √sNN = 200 GeV d+Au collisions using the STAR detector, with events classified by forward charged particle multiplicity and zero-degree neutral energy in the Au-beam direction. The jetlike correlated yield was extracted as a function of event multiplicity, and the dependence of this yield on the event selection was studied to separate jet contributions from non-jet (ridge) contributions on the away side.

## Key numbers

- The analysis was performed at √sNN = 200 GeV for d+Au collisions.
- Jetlike correlated yield (Yjetlike) was found to increase with event multiplicity, quantified by forward charged particle multiplicity (Nch) and zero-degree neutral energy.
- After accounting for the multiplicity dependence, the non-jet contribution on the away side was minimal, with no specific numerical value for the residual ridge yield provided in the truncated text.

## Context

This work extends previous dihadron correlation studies in d+Au collisions at RHIC, where a back-to-back ridge was suggested in earlier analyses. The STAR Collaboration (2014) shows that the apparent ridge signal may be an artifact of event-activity selection bias, as the jetlike yield itself scales with multiplicity. This finding challenges interpretations from prior work that assumed low-multiplicity events could serve as a baseline for jet correlations in high-multiplicity events. The result enables more careful event-classification procedures for future jet correlation measurements in small collision systems.
