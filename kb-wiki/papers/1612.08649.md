# Effective field theories for quantum chromo- and electrodynamics

> In this dissertation, I introduce the principles and methods of effective field theory and describe my work in three EFTs: First, in the perturbative QCD region, I use soft collinear effective theory (SCET) to prove that strong interaction soft radiation is universal and to increase the QCD accuracy to next-to-next-tonext- to leading logarithm order for new particle searches in hadron colliders. I also compute a new class of non-perturbative, large logarithmic enhancement arising near the elastic limits of deep inelastic scattering and Drell-Yan processes. Second, in the QCD confinement region, I use heavy hadron chiral perturbation theory to study near-threshold enhancements in the scattering of $D$ and $π$ mesons near the threshold for the excited $D$-meson state, $D^*$, as well as in the scattering of $D$ and $D^*$ mesons near the threshold for the exotic hadron X(3872). This work provides a clear picture of the hadronic molecule X(3872) and more profound understanding of the nuclear force between hadrons. Finally, inspired by SCET, I construct a new electron-laser effective field theory to describe highly-relativistic electrons traveling in strong laser fields, extract the universal distribution of electrons in strong electromagnetic backgrounds and its evolution in energy from the separated momentum scales of emitted photons and classical radiation, and predict the rate of wide angle photon emission. I conclude with limitations of EFT methods and some perspectives on what new work may be achieved with these EFTs.

# Effective Field Theories for Quantum Chromo- and Electrodynamics

## Key claim

The dissertation demonstrates that soft radiation in quantum chromodynamics is universal across electron-positron, deep inelastic scattering, and proton-proton processes, proven through soft collinear effective theory (SCET) to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading logarithm order, and that a new electron-laser effective field theory can describe highly-relativistic electrons in strong laser fields by separating momentum scales of emitted photons and classical radiation.

## Method

The author applies three distinct effective field theory frameworks: (1) soft collinear effective theory (SCET) to compute hemisphere soft functions for e+e-, ep, and pp scattering processes, proving their equality through explicit calculation of three-gluon vertex diagrams at one-loop and two-loop order; (2) heavy hadron chiral perturbation theory to study near-threshold enhancements in D-pi and D-D* meson scattering; (3) a new electron-laser effective field theory inspired by SCET for electrons in strong electromagnetic backgrounds. The soft function calculations involve contour integration of eikonal propagators with careful treatment of i-epsilon prescriptions and principal value regularization.

## Context

This work extends the program of applying effective field theory methods to quantum chromodynamics, building on the SCET framework developed by Bauer, Fleming, Pirjol, and Stewart (2001) and the soft function calculations by Hoang (2008), Hornig (2011), and Kelley (2011). The proof of soft function universality across e+e-, ep, and pp processes at three-loop order enables improved precision for new particle searches at hadron colliders. The electron-laser EFT opens a new direction for describing quantum electrodynamics in strong-field backgrounds, with applications to laser-matter interactions and high-intensity physics. The heavy hadron chiral perturbation theory analysis of X(3872) provides a hadronic molecule interpretation that deepens understanding of the nuclear force between hadrons.
