# Alpha clustering and weak coupling in the A=90 region

> From the viewpoint of a unified description of cluster structure and scattering in the A=90 region, $α$ scattering from $^{89}$Y is investigated. $α$ clustering and weak coupling in $^{93}$Nb is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the weak coupling picture due to alpha clustering, previously established in lighter nuclei, persists in the A=90 region, specifically in 93Nb, based on a unified description of alpha+89Y scattering and bound states.

## Method

The author analyzed alpha+89Y scattering data at energies from 16.2 to 25 MeV using a Woods-Saxon squared phenomenological potential, with real parameters fixed for all energies and imaginary parameters adjusted to fit the data. The same real potential was then used to calculate the alpha cluster structure in 93Nb, with a small angular-momentum-dependent correction to the potential, and the resulting energy levels and B(E2) values were compared to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The first excited state in 93Nb, 1/2-, appears at an extremely low excitation energy of 0.031 MeV above the 9/2+ ground state.
- The experimental B(E2) value for the transition from the 9/2- state at 1.500 MeV to the 5/2- state at 0.810 MeV is 26.4 (+9.7/-6.2) W.u.
- The calculated B(E2) value for this transition is reproduced with a small effective charge delta e = 0.2.
- The alpha-hole energy estimated from experimental binding energies is 0.17 MeV for a proton hole in the 2p1/2 shell, compared to 0.86 MeV in 19F.
- The real potential parameters used are V0=35 MeV, RV=7.49 fm, aV=0.43 fm, rho=4.86 fm, and alpha=4.748.

## Context

This work extends the weak coupling model due to alpha clustering, previously established in the sd-shell region (16O-20Ne) by Arima (1967) and Nemoto and Bando (1972), and in the fp-shell region (40Ca-44Ti) by Sakuda (1998), to the heavier A=90 region. The author's earlier work on 94Mo (Ohkubo 1995) using the alpha+90Zr cluster model is directly extended to 93Nb via the alpha+89Y system, and the results enable systematic experimental study of B(E2) values in the 90Zr-94Mo region to further test the universality of weak coupling due to alpha clustering.
