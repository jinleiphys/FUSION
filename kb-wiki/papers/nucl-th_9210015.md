# Skyrmions and the Nuclear Force

> The derivation of the nucleon-nucleon force from the Skyrme model is reexamined. Starting from previous results for the potential energy of quasistatic solutions, we show that a calculation using the Born-Oppenheimer approximation properly taking into account the mixing of nucleon resonances, leads to substantial central attraction. We obtain a potential that is in qualitative agreement with phenomenological potentials. We also study the non-adiabatic corrections, such as the velocity dependent transition potentials, and discuss their importance.

## Key claim

The authors show that a Born-Oppenheimer treatment of the Skyrmion-Skyrmion interaction, which properly includes mixing of nucleon resonances (particularly the Delta) and finite-\(N_{\rm C}\) corrections, yields a nucleon-nucleon potential with substantial mid-range central attraction, in qualitative agreement with phenomenological potentials, a result that earlier product-ansatz calculations had failed to achieve.

## Method

The authors start from previously computed numerical solutions for the static energy of two interacting Skyrmions on the adiabatic (unstable) manifold, parametrized by separation \(R\) and relative orientation. They expand this Skyrmion–Skyrmion interaction in terms of the algebraic operators \(W\) and \(Z\), which encode spin–spin and tensor structure, and then quantize the system using a bosonic \(SU(4)\) algebraic model that incorporates finite-\(N_{\rm C}\) effects. The nucleon-nucleon potential is obtained by diagonalizing the intrinsic Hamiltonian (potential plus rotational kinetic energy) at fixed \(R\) in a basis of two-baryon states (NN, N\(\Delta\), \(\Delta\Delta\)), following a Born-Oppenheimer separation of the slow radial motion from the faster rotational degrees of freedom.

## Key numbers

* The N–\(\Delta\) energy difference used in the perturbative correction is 300 MeV.
* The finite-\(N_{\rm C}\) correction factor for nucleon matrix elements is \(P_N = 1 + 2/N_{\rm C}\) (with \(N_{\rm C}=3\)).
* The reduced matrix elements of the operator \(T^{(11)}\) are: \(\langle N ||| T^{(11)} ||| N \rangle = -10\), \(\langle \Delta ||| T^{(11)} ||| \Delta \rangle = -20\), and \(\langle \Delta ||| T^{(11)} ||| N \rangle = -8\sqrt{2}\).
* The toroidal lowest \(B=2\) state has about 70 MeV of binding.

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts to derive the nucleon-nucleon force from the Skyrme model, which used the product ansatz and found no mid-range central attraction (Jackson, Vinh Mau, and others). It builds directly on the numerical solutions of the constrained \(B=2\) field equations by Walhout and Wambach (1992), which gave weak central attraction when projected only onto nucleon states. By combining those solutions with a Born-Oppenheimer treatment of state mixing and finite-\(N_{\rm C}\) corrections, the authors obtain a potential that reproduces the phenomenological features of the NN force, including one-pion exchange at long range, short-range repulsion, tensor force, and mid-range central attraction. This enables a more systematic path from QCD-inspired effective theories to nuclear phenomenology.
