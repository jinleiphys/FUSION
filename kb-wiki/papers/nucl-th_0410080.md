# Two Quark Potentials

> In this Chapter QCD interactions between a quark and an anti-quark are discussed. In the heavy quark limit these potentials can be related to quarkonia and $1/m$ corrections can be systematically determined. Excitations of the ground state potential provide an entry point into the phenomenology of quark-gluon hybrids. The short-distance behaviour of non-perturbative potentials can serve as a test of resummation and convergence of perturbative expansions. Torelons and potentials between non-fundamental colour charges offer a window into the origin of the confinement mechanism and relate to effective string descriptions of low energy aspects of QCD.

# Two Quark Potentials

## Key claim
The static QCD potential between a quark and an antiquark can be systematically extracted from Wilson loop expectation values on the lattice, and this potential provides a rigorous bridge between QCD and non-relativistic potential models for quarkonia, but only in the heavy quark limit where the typical binding energy (~400 MeV) is small compared to the quark mass.

## Method
The authors derive the static potential from the large-time behavior of rectangular Wegner-Wilson loops in Euclidean lattice gauge theory, using transfer matrix arguments to relate the Wilson loop expectation value to a sum over energy eigenstates of the static quark-antiquark system. They then compare strong coupling expansions (up to O(g^{-24})), weak coupling perturbation theory, bosonic string model predictions, and non-perturbative lattice Monte Carlo results for the string tension and potential shape.

## Key numbers
- The typical non-perturbative scale Λ is of order 400 MeV, which sets the binding energy scale for QCD bound states.
- For the bosonic string picture, the critical distance below which the string description breaks down is r_c ≈ 0.33 fm, derived from √σ ≈ 430 MeV (the string tension from the ρ, a₂ Regge trajectory).
- The strong coupling expansion for SU(3) gauge theory gives a string tension σ_d a² = -d_r ln(β/18), with convergence apparently limited to β < 5.
- For torelon masses in d=4 SU(2) gauge theory, the bosonic string prediction E_n(l) = σ l - (d-2)π/(6l) + ... agrees at the 3% level for distances 1 fm ≤ l ≤ 2.4 fm.

## Context
This chapter reviews and synthesizes lattice QCD results on static interquark potentials, extending the foundational work of Wilson (1974) on the Wilson loop as a confinement order parameter and the strong coupling expansion of Creutz (1978). The authors connect these lattice results to effective string descriptions (Lüscher 1980, Arvis 1983) and to heavy quark effective theory (HQET) and non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) frameworks for systematic 1/m corrections. The work enables quantitative tests of confinement mechanisms and provides input for quarkonium phenomenology and hybrid meson spectroscopy.
