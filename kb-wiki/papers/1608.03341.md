# Improved treatment of blocking effect at finite temperature

> The blocking effect caused by the odd particle on the pairing properties of systems with odd number of fermions at finite temperature interacting via the monopole pairing force is studied within several approximations. The results are compared with the predictions obtained by using the exact solutions of the pairing Hamiltonian. The comparison favors the approximation with the odd particle occupying the top level, which is the closest to the Fermi surface and whose occupation number decreases with increasing temperature.

## Key claim

The conventional assumption that the odd particle's blocked level has unity occupation number at finite temperature is invalid; the authors propose a temperature-dependent occupation number \(q_k\) for the blocked level, derived from the standard finite-temperature BCS particle-number equation, and show that the odd particle always remains on the top level \(k_0\) (the level just above the Fermi surface at \(T=0\)), not averaged over all levels as in prior approaches.

## Method

The authors solve the finite-temperature BCS equations for an odd system with monopole pairing force, modifying the particle-number equation to include a temperature-dependent occupation number \(q_k\) for the blocked level (Eq. Nodd q), while keeping the standard blocked gap equation (Eq. oddBCSgap). They compare results from this "\(q_k\)-blocked BCS" (with \(k=k_0\)), an "average-blocked BCS" that averages over all blocked levels, and the prior "Maino's approach" (which assumes \(q_k=1\)), against exact solutions of the pairing Hamiltonian obtained by diagonalization and extended to finite temperature via the canonical ensemble. They also incorporate quasiparticle number fluctuations (QNF) via the FTBCS1 method to smooth the superfluid-normal phase transition.

## Key numbers

* For the schematic model with \(N=9\) particles, \(\Omega=10, 14, 20\) equidistant levels (spacing 1 MeV), and pairing strength \(G=0.6\) MeV, the exact occupation number of the odd-particle level \(k_0=5\) is 1 at \(T=0\) and decreases with increasing temperature, e.g., dropping significantly as \(T\) rises from 0 to 1 MeV in the equidistant case.
* The critical temperature for the superfluid-normal phase transition in the standard FTBCS is \(T_c \simeq 0.57 \Delta(0)\), where \(\Delta(0)\) is the pairing gap at \(T=0\).
* In the \(q_k\)-blocked BCS1 (with QNF), the heat capacity becomes negative at a very low temperature \(T \simeq 0.15\) MeV for the equidistant model with \(\Omega=20\), attributed to the level spectrum.
* The chemical potential within the \(q_k\)-blocked BCS can exceed the energy of the blocked level \(k_0\) (5 MeV), whereas in the blocked BCS (with \(q_k=1\)) it remains always below that level.

## Context

This work extends the finite-temperature treatment of blocking in odd-fermion systems beyond the "blocked BCS" approach of Wahlborn and Ring (1960s) and the finite-temperature extension by Maino et al. (1990s), which assumed unity occupation of the blocked level. The authors show that the exact solutions (Richardson, 1963; exact diagonalization method, 2000s) contradict that assumption, and their \(q_k\)-blocked BCS with \(k=k_0\) recovers the correct zero-pairing limit (Fermi-Dirac distribution) and avoids the jump between zero-temperature and finite-temperature results present in Maino's approach. The work enables improved description of pairing in odd-mass nuclei at finite temperature, with the QNF extension (FTBCS1, Dang 2007) smoothing the phase transition.
