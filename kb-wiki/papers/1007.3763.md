# Title: Quadrupole collective inertia in nuclear fission: cranking approximation

> Collective mass tensor derived from the cranking approximation to the adiabatic time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (ATDHFB) approach is compared with that obtained in the Gaussian Overlap Approximation (GOA) to the generator coordinate method. Illustrative calculations are carried out for one-dimensional quadrupole fission pathways in 256Fm. It is shown that the collective mass exhibits strong variations with the quadrupole collective coordinate. These variations are related to the changes in the intrinsic shell structure. The differences between collective inertia obtained in cranking and perturbative cranking approximations to ATDHFB, and within GOA, are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the collective mass tensor for quadrupole fission pathways in 256Fm exhibits strong variations with the quadrupole coordinate, with the non-perturbative cranking approximation (ATDHFB-C) producing less adiabatic behavior and sharper peaks compared to the perturbative cranking and Gaussian Overlap Approximation methods, though the abstract does not emphasize that these peaks coincide with configuration changes and that the adiabatic theory cannot provide meaningful results at diabatic jumps between energy sheets.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) and HF+BCS calculations using the SkM* energy density functional with a density-dependent pairing interaction, computing one-dimensional fission pathways constrained by the axial quadrupole moment Q20 for 256Fm. They evaluated the collective mass tensor using four approaches: the full cranking approximation to ATDHFB (ATDHFB-C), the canonical-basis cranking approximation (ATDHFB-Cc), the perturbative cranking approximation (ATDHFB-Cp), and the Gaussian Overlap Approximation (ATDHFB-GOA), with derivatives of densities and mean fields computed via a three-point Lagrange formula.

## Key numbers

- The pairing interaction strengths used were Vn0 = -372.0 MeV fm3 and Vp0 = -438.0 MeV fm3, adjusted to reproduce ground-state pairing gaps in 252Fm.
- Density matrices were evaluated for quadrupole deformations ranging from Q20 = 0 to 320 b in steps of 1 b.
- Results obtained with 3-point and 5-point Lagrange formulas for derivatives differed only in the third decimal place.
- The ATDHFB-C mass parameter showed peaks at deformation points where local variations in pairing energy (Epp) and HF energy (Eph) were largest, with the anticorrelation between these two energy components clearly visible in the total energy curve.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent investigations of Yul (1999), who used the Gogny energy density functional and found that ATDHFB cranking masses could be an order of magnitude greater than perturbative cranking masses, with significant implications for fission lifetime calculations. The present study applies the same methodology using Skyrme energy functionals (SkM*) and compares results across multiple approximation schemes (cranking, perturbative cranking, and GOA) for 256Fm, building on the fission pathway analysis of Sta (2009). The findings enable more accurate calculations of spontaneous fission half-lives by demonstrating that the full cranking treatment captures shell-structure-induced variations in collective inertia that are suppressed in perturbative approaches.
