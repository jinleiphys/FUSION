# Thermodynamics of large N gauge theory from top down holography

> By considering fluxes on D7 branes and explicitly computing their back-reaction on the geometry with and without a black hole, we show how the UV divergence of Klebanov-Strassler model can be regulated. Using the form of the metric and fluxes in the extremal and non-extremal limit, we compute the on-shell gravity action including the localized sources up to linear order in perturbation parameter g_sM^2/N, g_sN_f where N,M and N_f are units of D3,D5 and D7 charges in the dual gauge theory. Using the gravitational description, we show how the gauge theory undergoes a first-order Hawking-Page like phase transition and compute the critical temperature T_c. Finally, we obtain the equation of state for the gauge theory by computing thermodynamic state functions of the black hole and exhibit how black holes in deformed cone geometry can lead to results that are qualitatively similar to lattice QCD simulations.

## Key claim

By introducing worldvolume fluxes on D7 branes embedded in the Klebanov-Strassler geometry and computing their back-reaction to linear order in the perturbation parameters \(g_s M^2/N\) and \(g_s N_f\), the authors show that the UV divergence of the Klebanov-Strassler model can be regulated, producing a dual geometry that is a warped deformed cone at small radial distances and \(AdS_5 \times T^{1,1}\) in the far UV, enabling a first-order Hawking-Page like phase transition with a computable critical temperature.

## Method

The authors work directly with the ten-dimensional type IIB supergravity action including localized D7 brane sources, avoiding Kaluza-Klein reduction. They solve the coupled system of flux equations, Bianchi identities, and Einstein equations perturbatively to linear order in the parameters \(\frac{g_s M^2}{N}\) and \(g_s N_f\), where \(N\), \(M\), and \(N_f\) are the numbers of D3, D5, and D7 branes in the dual gauge theory. The metric and warp factors are evaluated up to linear order, while the fluxes are obtained at zeroth order; the on-shell gravity action including localized sources is then computed to linear order to analyze the phase transition and thermodynamic state functions.

## Key numbers

- The perturbation parameters are \(\frac{g_s M^2}{N}\) and \(g_s N_f\), where \(N\), \(M\), and \(N_f\) are units of D3, D5, and D7 charges in the dual gauge theory.
- The warp factor scales as \(e^{-4A} = \frac{27\pi N \alpha'^2}{4r^4} \left[1 + \sum_{j=0}^{\infty} \mathcal{O}\left(\frac{\tilde{r}_h}{r}\right)^{4j} \mathcal{O}\left(\frac{g_s \widetilde{M}^2}{N}\right)\right]\), where \(\tilde{r}_h\) is the horizon radius at zeroth order.
- The blackening factor is \(e^{2B} = 1 - \frac{\tilde{r}_h^4}{r^4} + \sum_{l=1} \mathcal{O}\left(\frac{\tilde{r}_h}{r}\right)^{4l} \mathcal{O}\left(\frac{g_s \widetilde{M}^2}{N}\right)\).
- The true horizon radius \(r_h\) is related to the zeroth-order horizon by \(r_h = \tilde{r}_h \left(1 + \mathcal{O}\left(\frac{g_s \widetilde{M}^2}{N}\right)\right)\).
- The localized source terms in the equations are of order \(\mathcal{O}\left(\frac{g_s^2 N_f \mathcal{M}^2}{N^2}\right)\), which is second order in the perturbation parameters.

## Context

This work extends the Klebanov-Strassler (KS) model (1998) and the Klebanov-Tseytlin (KT) model (2000), which provided a holographic description of a confining gauge theory but suffered from UV divergences. The authors' previous work (FEP, Mia:2012ue) proposed a general procedure for UV modifications; here they provide an explicit calculation. The approach contrasts with bottom-up holographic models (Klebanov 2004, kirit) that tune an effective dilaton potential to fit lattice QCD results but lack a direct derivation from brane configurations. This top-down construction enables quantitative comparisons with large N QCD and lattice QCD simulations (Petreczky 2012, Borsanyi 2012, Panero 2009, Panero 2012), particularly for the equation of state near the critical temperature.
