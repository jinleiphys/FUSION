# On the generalized Friedrichs-Lee model with multiple discrete and continuous states

> In this study, we present several improvements of the non-relativistic Friedrichs-Lee model with multiple discrete and continuous states and still retain its solvability. Our findings establish a solid theoretical basis for the exploration of resonance phenomena in scenarios involving multiple interfering states across various channels. The scattering amplitudes associated with the continuum states naturally adhere to coupled-channel unitarity, rendering this framework particularly valuable for investigating hadronic resonant states appearing in multiple coupled channels. Moreover, this generalized framework exhibits a wide-range applicability, enabling investigations into resonance phenomena across diverse physical domains, including hadron physics, nuclear physics, optics, and cold atom physics, among others.

## Key claim

The authors extend the non-relativistic Friedrichs-Lee model to include both a more general discrete-continuum interaction (where the coupling function depends on both the discrete state and the continuum channel) and separable continuum-continuum interactions that are independent of the discrete-continuum coupling, while retaining exact solvability. The key condition is that the continuum-continuum interaction must be of separable form for the model to remain exactly solvable.

## Method

The authors construct a Hamiltonian with D discrete states and C continuum states, introducing a general coupling function f_{jn}(ω) between the j-th discrete state and the n-th continuum, and a separable continuum-continuum interaction with coupling constants v_{mn} and form factors g_n(ω). They solve the eigenvalue problem by expanding eigenstates in terms of bare discrete and continuum states, deriving coupled equations for the coefficient functions, and expressing the solutions in matrix form using the resolvent matrix η(E). The S-matrix is obtained from the inner product of "in" and "out" states, and discrete state solutions are found from the condition det η(E)=0.

## Key numbers

* The Hamiltonian includes D kinds of discrete states and C kinds of continuum states, with thresholds a_n for each continuum channel n.
* The S-matrix is given by S = 1 - 2πi f^T(E) · η^{-1}(E) · f^*(E), where f is a D×C matrix of coupling functions.
* There exist 2^C different Riemann sheets for the analytically continued S-matrix, with the m-th sheet defined as the continuation from the physical region (a_m, a_{m+1}).
* For bound states below the lowest threshold, the normalization condition involves the derivative η'_{kj}(E) and yields the elementariness Z^{(i)} = Σ_k |α_k^{(i)}(E_i)|^2 and compositeness X^{(i)} = Σ_m X^{(i)}_m.

## Context

This work extends the Friedrichs-Lee model framework previously developed by Xiao (2016) and Zhou (2017, 2020) for hadronic scattering, where earlier versions required the continuum-continuum interaction to share the same form factors as the discrete-continuum interaction. The generalized model provides a theoretical foundation for incorporating background contributions from t- and u-channel exchanges and contact interactions while preserving coupled-channel unitarity, which is not guaranteed by K-matrix parameterizations (as noted by the authors). This enables more realistic phenomenological applications to hadronic resonances in multiple coupled channels, such as those studied by Guo (2016) using separable potentials in momentum space.
