# Threshold meson production and cosmic ray transport

> An interesting accident of nature is that the peak of the cosmic ray spectrum, for both protons and heavier nuclei, occurs near the pion production threshold. The Boltzmann transport equation contains a term which is the cosmic ray flux multiplied by the cross section. Therefore when considering pion and kaon production from proton-proton reactions, small cross sections at low energy can be as important as larger cross sections at higher energy. This is also true for subthreshold kaon production in nuclear collisions, but not for subthreshold pion production.

## Key claim

The paper argues that small meson production cross sections near threshold can be as important as larger cross sections at higher energy for cosmic ray transport, because the cosmic ray spectrum peaks near the pion production threshold and the Boltzmann transport equation multiplies flux by cross section; however, the authors explicitly state that the paper does **not prove** that these reactions must be included in all transport codes, only that they might need to be under certain circumstances depending on the incident spectrum, medium, and particles of interest.

## Method

The authors computed an "importance" factor (flux multiplied by microscopic cross section) for pion and kaon production in proton-proton collisions, and for subthreshold pion and kaon production in nucleus-nucleus collisions, using cosmic ray flux data from Simpson (1983) and experimental cross sections from the literature. They then performed a sample transport calculation using the MESTRN code (an extension of HZETRN) through a Martian atmosphere model, artificially multiplying the pion production cross section by a factor of two from threshold to 2 GeV kinetic energy to quantify the effect on charged pion and muon fluxes.

## Key numbers

- The peak of the cosmic ray spectrum for protons occurs at about 300 MeV, near the pion production threshold of 290 MeV.
- For neutral pion production in proton-proton collisions: cross section at 375 MeV is 40 μb, while at 7 GeV it is 1.7 mb, yet both give approximately equal importance (72 vs. 102 μb/(m² sr s MeV)).
- For kaon production in proton-proton collisions: cross section at 1.60 GeV (just above threshold of 1.58 GeV) is 0.16 μb with importance 0.1, comparable to 50 GeV where cross section is 16.7 μb with importance 0.01.
- For subthreshold pion production in Carbon-Carbon collisions at 84 A MeV (well below 290 A MeV threshold), the cross section is 18.9 × 10⁻³ mb with importance 1.3 × 10⁻⁴ mb/(m² sr s AMeV), compared to 1000 A MeV where cross section is 576 mb with importance 1.7.
- For subthreshold kaon production in Ni-Ni collisions at 1.0 A GeV (below 1.58 A GeV threshold), cross section is 2.7 mb with importance 2.7 × 10⁻⁵, comparable to 7.29 A GeV where cross section is 168 mb with importance 6.7 × 10⁻⁵.
- The sample transport calculation showed that enhancing pion production cross sections near threshold by a factor of two led to an enhanced flux of charged pions and muons by approximately 20-30% in the kinetic energy region up to 1 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of meson production near threshold, which had been studied in detail by Hanhart, dos Santos, Machner, and Moskal (various years), where early calculations of total cross sections disagreed with experiment by a factor of five. The paper specifically addresses the implications for cosmic ray transport codes such as GEANT and FLUKA, which typically ignore near-threshold cross sections because they are about a thousand times smaller (microbarn region) than typical cross sections away from threshold (tens of millibarn). The authors show that subthreshold pion production in nucleus-nucleus collisions is not important for transport because the nuclear cosmic ray spectrum peak remains near the pion threshold, but subthreshold kaon production may need inclusion, and they speculate that subthreshold production of heavier hadrons may also need to be included.
