# Nuclear effects and neutron structure in deeply virtual Compton scattering off 3He

> The study of nuclear generalized parton distributions (GPDs) could be a crucial achievement of hadronic physics since they open new ways to obtain new information on the structure of bound nucleons, in particular, to access the neutron GPDs. Here, the results of calculations of 3He GPDs in Impulse Approximation are presented. The calculation of the sum of GPDs H + E, and "tilde H", with the correct limits, will be shown. These quantities, at low momentum transfer, are largely dominated by the neutron contribution so that 3He is an ideal target for these kind of studies. Nevertheless the extraction of neutron information from future 3He data could be non trivial. A procedure, which takes into account nuclear effects encoded in IA, is presented. The calculation of H,E and "tilde H" allows also to evaluate the cross section asymmetries for deeply virtual compton scattering at Jefferson Lab kinematics. Thanks to these observations, DVCS off 3He could be an ideal process to access the neutron information in the next future.

## Key claim

The paper argues that coherent deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) off 3He can provide access to neutron generalized parton distributions (GPDs), but only if nuclear effects encoded in the Impulse Approximation (IA) are properly taken into account via a dedicated extraction procedure; the neutron dominance holds at low momentum transfer but becomes non-trivial to extract at higher momentum transfer.

## Method

The author calculates 3He GPDs (H, E, and the sum H+E, as well as tilde H) within the Impulse Approximation, using a non-relativistic reduction of the light-cone correlator and realistic 3He wave functions from the AV18 potential. The nuclear GPDs are expressed as convolutions of off-diagonal spectral functions with free nucleonic GPDs, using three different nucleonic models (a simple factorized model, a constituent quark model, and a MIT bag model) to test model dependence. An extraction procedure is then developed by approximating the convolution integrals with point-like axial and magnetic form factors, allowing the neutron GPDs to be solved from the 3He and proton GPDs.

## Key numbers

- The neutron contributes approximately 90% of the 3He spin, motivating the use of 3He as an effective polarized neutron target.
- The dipole magnetic moments are: mu_3 = -2.13 mu_N and mu_n = -1.91 mu_N, showing close similarity.
- The anomalous magnetic moments are k_p = 1.79 mu_N and k_n = -1.91 mu_N.
- The effective polarizations from the AV18 potential are p_n = 0.878 and p_p = -0.023 in the forward limit.
- The extraction procedure works perfectly for x <= 0.7, the region accessible at Jefferson Lab kinematics.
- The IA calculation is accurate to order O(p^2/M^2, Delta^2/M^2).
- The magnetic form factor of 3He from the IA calculation agrees with data for -Delta^2 <= 0.15 GeV^2.
- The axial nucleonic form factor uses M_A = 5.076 fm^-1.

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear GPDs for few-body systems, following earlier calculations of 3He GPDs in IA by the same group (Rinaldi, 2014) and building on the formalism for polarized DIS off 3He developed by Ciofi degli Atti et al. (1993). The paper provides a practical extraction procedure that enables future DVCS experiments at Jefferson Lab to isolate neutron GPDs, which is essential for accessing the quark orbital angular momentum via Ji's sum rule. The approach contrasts with isoscalar targets like 2H and 4He, where the proton and neutron contributions to the E GPD cancel due to opposite-sign anomalous magnetic moments.
