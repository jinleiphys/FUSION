# Searching for the Next Yukawa Phase of QCD

> QCD predicts that the interactions between quarks and gluons change from a confining to a screened Yukawa form above a critical temperature $T_c\sim 150$ MeV. In this talk, I review some of the key observables in heavy ion reactions which are being used to search for this new partonic Yukawa phase at SPS and RHIC. These include collective observables such as $dE_\perp/dyd^2p_\perp$, meson interferometry, jet quenching, and $J/ψ$ suppression.

## Key claim

QCD predicts a transition from a confining to a screened Yukawa form of partonic interactions above a critical temperature T_c ~ 150 MeV, but the author argues that SPS data have not yet provided unambiguous evidence for this deconfined phase, and that higher-energy RHIC collisions with shorter-wavelength probes are needed to actually resolve partonic degrees of freedom.

## Method

The paper reviews lattice QCD calculations of the heavy quark potential in both confined and deconfined phases for SU(2) quenched QCD, showing the temperature-dependent modification of the string tension and the emergence of a screened Yukawa form above T_c. It then surveys experimental observables from heavy ion collisions at SPS and projected for RHIC, including transverse energy distributions, azimuthal flow coefficients, hadron interferometry, and J/ψ suppression, comparing hydrodynamic and transport model predictions to existing data.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature T_c ~ 150 MeV for the QCD deconfinement transition
- String tension κ ~ 1 GeV/fm in the confining phase
- For T > 2 T_c, the lattice QCD screening mass μ_L ≈ 2.5 T, close to the perturbative QCD estimate of μ_L = 1.6 g(T) T for N_c=2, N_f=0
- Near T_c, the effective exponent d < 1 and μ ~ T/2, indicating a longer-range interaction than perturbative QCD predicts
- The effective coupling above T_c is α ~ 0.15 from lattice results
- Energy density threshold for the deconfined phase is ε > 2 GeV/fm^3
- HIJING predicts dE_⊥/dy ≈ 1 GeV × A^1.3 × (1 + log(√s/200)) at RHIC, yielding about 1 TeV per unit rapidity
- HIJING predicts initial energy density ε_0 ≈ 0.6 GeV/fm^3 × A^0.63 × (1 + log(√s/200))
- At SPS, dE_⊥/dy and dN/dy scale nearly linearly with A as ~ A^1.07
- At RHIC, estimates yield 500-1500 gluons per unit rapidity
- Bjorken formula gives ε(τ_0 ~ 0.5 fm/c) > 10 GeV/fm^3 at RHIC for dE_T/dy ~ 400-2000 GeV

## Context

This review extends the line of work on the QCD phase transition and deconfinement, referencing lattice QCD calculations by Karsch et al (1999) and the MILC collaboration (Bernard et al, 1997) for the equation of state and heavy quark potential. The author critically evaluates the CERN press release claiming evidence for a deconfined state at SPS, arguing that the data can be explained without invoking partonic degrees of freedom, and that RHIC energies are needed to test the QCD predictions shown in the lattice results. The paper enables future searches for collective signatures of the quark-gluon plasma including longitudinal work, elliptic flow, and time delay from hadron interferometry.
