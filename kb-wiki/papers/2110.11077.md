# Probing dense matter physics with transiently-accreting neutron stars: the case of source MXB 1659-29

> Recent observational data on transiently-accreting neutron stars has unequivocally shown fast-cooling sources, such as in the case of neutron star MXB 1659-29. Previous calculations have estimated its total neutrino luminosity and heat capacity, as well as suggested that direct Urca reactions take place in $1 \%$ of the volume of the core. In this paper, we reproduce the inferred luminosity of this source with detailed models of equations of state (EOS) and nuclear pairing gaps. We show that three superfluidity gap models are inconsistent with data for all EOS and another three are disfavoured because of fine tuning arguments. We also calculate the total heat capacity for all constructed stars and show that independent observations of mass and luminosity could set constraints on the core superfluidity of a source as well as the density slope of the symmetry energy, L. This is an important step towards defining a universal equation of state for neutron stars and therefore, towards a better understanding of the phase diagram of asymmetric matter at high densities.

## Key claim

The paper reproduces the inferred neutrino luminosity of MXB 1659-29 using detailed models of equations of state and nuclear pairing gaps, but the agreement depends on the specific gap model and requires that only about 1% of the core volume undergoes unsuppressed direct Urca reactions, implying fine-tuning for some models.

## Method

The authors constructed neutron star models using a family of relativistic mean-field equations of state based on FSUGold2 with varying values of the symmetry energy slope L, combined with polynomial parametrizations of neutron triplet superfluidity and proton singlet superconductivity gap models from Ho et al. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for non-rotating spherically-symmetric stars and calculated the direct Urca neutrino luminosity with and without superfluid suppression, then compared these predictions to the inferred luminosity of MXB 1659-29.

## Key numbers

- Inferred neutrino luminosity of MXB 1659-29: L_nu = 3 x 10^34 erg/s (core temperature T_tilde = 2.5 x 10^7 K)
- Fraction of core volume undergoing unsuppressed direct Urca: 0.10% to 1.07% depending on the equation of state
- Direct Urca threshold proton fraction for the studied equations of state: 0.131 to 0.138 (not 1/9 due to muon presence)
- For low L equations of state (L = 47 MeV), only stars with masses M >= 1.8 solar masses allow direct Urca; for high L (L = 105 MeV), direct Urca occurs even for M <= 1.0 solar masses
- Core temperature used for MXB 1659-29: T_tilde = 2.5 x 10^7 K; a heavy element envelope would give T_tilde = 5 x 10^7 K but is inconsistent with accretion rates
- Difference between heat capacities for different gap models is at most a factor of 2

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Brown et al. (2006) who estimated the neutrino luminosity and heat capacity of MXB 1659-29 and suggested direct Urca occurs in 1% of the core volume. The paper tests specific gap model parametrizations from Ho et al. against this data, finding that three neutron triplet models (NT_AO, NT_BEEHS, NT_TToa) are inconsistent for all equations of state, while three others (NT_EEHOr, PS_BS, PS_CCYps) are disfavored due to fine-tuning. The results enable future constraints on the symmetry energy slope L and core superfluidity through independent measurements of neutron star mass and luminosity, and suggest that even colder sources such as SAX J1808.4-3658 and 1H 1905+000 should exist if direct Urca is operating.
