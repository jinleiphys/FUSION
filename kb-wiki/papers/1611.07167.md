# Medium effects in the deep-inelastic charged lepton/neutrino-A scattering

> In this work, we have discussed the recent developments that have taken place to understand the differences in the weak $F_{2A}^{Weak} (x,Q^2)$ and electromagnetic $F_{2A}^{EM} (x,Q^2)$ nuclear structure functions. Also we present the results of our work on nuclear medium effects on $F_{2A}^{Weak} (x,Q^2)$ and $F_{2A}^{EM} (x,Q^2)$ for a wide range of $x$ and $Q^2$. These results have been obtained using a microscopic nuclear model, where to incorporate nuclear medium effects, Fermi motion, binding energy, nucleon correlations, mesonic contributions from pion and rho mesons and shadowing effects are considered. The calculations are performed in local density approximation using relativistic nucleon spectral function. We have also compared the theoretical results with the recent experimental data on electromagnetic and weak structure functions. Furthermore, we have studied the nuclear medium effects in Drell-Yan(DY) process and present the results for differential cross section, and the results are compared with the data of E772 experiment.

## Key claim

The paper claims that nuclear medium effects (Fermi motion, binding energy, nucleon correlations, mesonic contributions, and shadowing) cause a measurable difference between electromagnetic and weak nuclear structure functions \(F_{2A}^{EM}(x,Q^2)\) and \(F_{2A}^{Weak}(x,Q^2)\) at low \(x\), but this difference becomes almost negligible for \(x > 0.3\); the authors hedge that the magnitude of this difference depends on the nuclear target, being larger for heavier nuclei like tin and lead.

## Method

The authors performed calculations in the local density approximation using a relativistic nucleon spectral function to incorporate Fermi motion, binding energy, and nucleon correlations. They added mesonic contributions from pion and rho meson clouds using meson propagators and included shadowing effects following the approach of Kulagin and Petti (2004). The nuclear structure functions were obtained by convolving nucleon-level parton distribution functions (PDFs) with the nuclear spectral function and meson contributions, and results were compared with JLab data for electromagnetic structure functions and with E772 data for Drell-Yan cross sections.

## Key numbers

- At \(Q^2 = 2\ \text{GeV}^2\) and \(x = 0.2\), the LO result for \(F_{2A}^{EM}\) in carbon using only the spectral function is about 18% smaller than the full model (spectral function plus mesonic contribution plus shadowing); this difference decreases to 12% at \(x = 0.4\) and becomes almost negligible at \(x = 0.6\).
- In iron at \(x = 0.1\), the electromagnetic structure function is about 4% lower than the weak structure function; for \(x > 0.3\) the difference becomes almost negligible.
- For the Drell-Yan process in carbon at \(\sqrt{s_N} = 38.8\ \text{GeV}\) and \(M = 4.5\ \text{GeV}\), the nuclear structure effects due to bound nucleons lead to a suppression of about 5-6% in the yield for \(0.2 < x_b < 0.6\); mesonic contributions increase the yield by about 6-8% in the same region.
- The net effect of shadowing and energy loss in carbon reduces the Drell-Yan yield by about 7% at \(x_b = 0.1\) and 4% at \(x_b = 0.2\) for \(M = 4.5\ \text{GeV}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of nuclear medium effects in deep-inelastic scattering by groups such as NMC (Arneodo, 1997), JLab (Mamyan, 2012), SLAC (Gomez, 1993), and BCDMS (Benvenuti, 1994), and addresses the recent finding by the nCTEQ collaboration (Kovarik, 2010) that nuclear correction factors differ between electromagnetic and weak interactions. The results enable more precise modeling of neutrino-nucleus cross sections for oscillation experiments like MINERvA and DUNE, where nuclear medium effects are a major source of systematic uncertainty in the few-GeV energy region.
