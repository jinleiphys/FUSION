# Searching for the BCS phase at nonzero isospin asymmetry

> According to perturbation theory predictions, QCD matter in the zero-temperature, high-density limits of QCD at nonzero isospin chemical potential is expected to be in a superfluid Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) phase of $u$ and $\bar{d}$ Cooper pairs. It is also expected, on symmetry grounds, that such phase connects via an analytical crossover to the phase with Bose-Einstein condensation (BEC) of charged pions at $μ_\text{I}\geq m_π/2$. With lattice results, showing some indications that the deconfinement crossover also smoothly penetrates the BEC phase, the conjecture was made that the former connects continuously to the BEC-BCS crossover. We compute the spectrum of the Dirac operator, and use generalized Banks-Casher relations, to test this conjecture and identify signatures of the superfluid BCS phase.

# Searching for the BCS phase at nonzero isospin asymmetry

## Key claim

The interpolated spectral density of the Dirac operator is sensitive to the BEC phase boundary, but no definite conclusions can yet be drawn about its sensitivity to the BEC-BCS crossover, because disentangling genuine nonperturbative behavior from growing cutoff effects at large isospin chemical potential requires further systematic study.

## Method

The authors compute the complex eigenvalue spectrum of the massless staggered Dirac operator for 2+1-flavor QCD at nonzero isospin chemical potential, using a Krylov-Schur solver from the SLEPc library to obtain approximately 150 eigenvalues closest to the origin. They evaluate the spectral density at the physical quark mass using kernel density estimation, and apply a generalized Banks-Casher relation to connect the spectral density at the origin to the BCS gap. Simulations are performed on N_s^3 x N_t lattices with N_t = 6 and 12 at physical quark masses and multiple values of the pionic source.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass m_pi approximately 135 MeV (physical value used for tuning quark masses along the line of constant physics)
- Lattices with N_t = 6 and N_t = 12 at various temperatures T = 1/(N_t a)
- Smallest pionic source value lambda = 0.0013 used for the main results
- For the finest ensemble (N_t = 12), a*mu_I < 0.25 up to mu_I/m_pi approximately 2.5
- Spectral density rho(ml) becomes nonzero exactly at the BEC phase boundary mu_I^BEC(T) for each temperature studied
- At mu_I/m_pi > 1.5, statistically significant nonzero values of rho(ml) are extracted at the largest simulated mu_I

## Context

This work extends lattice studies of the QCD phase diagram at pure isospin chemical potential previously mapped by Brandt et al. (2017, 2018), which identified at least three phases including a BEC phase of charged pions. The conjecture that the deconfinement crossover connects continuously to the BEC-BCS crossover was motivated by those lattice results showing large Polyakov loop values in the BEC phase. The present study tests this conjecture using a Banks-Casher type relation derived by Kanazawa (2013) for the zero-temperature, high-isospin-density limit, and aims to identify signatures of the superfluid BCS phase predicted by perturbation theory (Son, 2000) and model calculations (Adhikari, 2018). The results enable future systematic studies at larger mu_I values to check whether the spectral density eventually grows with mu_I as predicted by perturbative calculations of the BCS gap.
