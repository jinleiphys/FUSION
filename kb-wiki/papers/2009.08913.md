# Baryon stopping as a relativistic Markov process in phase space

> We reconsider baryon stopping in relativistic heavy-ion collisions in a nonequilibrium-statistical framework. The approach combines earlier formulations based on quantum chromodynamics with a relativistic diffusion model through a suitably derived fluctuation-dissipation relation, thus allowing for a fully time-dependent theory that is consistent with QCD. We use an existing framework for relativistic stochastic processes in spacetime that are Markovian in phase space, and adapt it to derive a Fokker-Planck equation in rapidity space, which is solved numerically. The time evolution of the net-proton distribution function in rapidity space agrees with stopping data from the CERN Super Proton Synchrotron and the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider.

## Key claim

The paper derives a fully time-dependent, nonequilibrium-statistical theory of baryon stopping in relativistic heavy-ion collisions by combining a relativistic Markov process in phase space with a fluctuation-dissipation relation derived from a color-glass condensate (CGC) stationary state, but the model's agreement with data depends on treating the evolution scale as a free parameter and assuming constant diffusion in rapidity.

## Method

The authors model baryon stopping as a relativistic Ornstein-Uhlenbeck process in phase space, decoupling the longitudinal rapidity coordinate from transverse degrees of freedom. They derive a Fokker-Planck equation for the marginal rapidity probability density, fixing the drift coefficient through a fluctuation-dissipation relation that identifies the stationary state with a CGC-based distribution from earlier QCD formulations. The dimensionless Fokker-Planck equation is solved numerically using an additive Runge-Kutta method, with the evolution scale treated as a free parameter fitted to experimental net-proton rapidity distributions.

## Key numbers

- Fermi rapidity for lead (Pb) nuclei: \( y_F = 0.3136 \); for gold (Au) nuclei: \( y_F = 0.3134 \), differing only in the fourth decimal place
- For large positive rapidities, the stationary distribution decays exponentially with decay constant \( \alpha_+ = -2(1 + \lambda) \), where \( \lambda = 0.2 \) is the gluon-saturation-scale exponent
- For large negative rapidities, the stationary distribution decays with \( \alpha_- = +2b(1+\lambda)/(2+\lambda) \), where \( b \) is the low-\( x \) exponent of the valence-quark distribution
- The gluon saturation scale is parameterized as \( Q_s^2 = Q_0^2 A^{1/3} x^{-\lambda} \) with \( Q_0^2 = 0.09\ \text{GeV}^2 \) and \( \lambda = 0.2 \)

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological relativistic diffusion model (RDM) of Wolschin (1999) by grounding it in a relativistic stochastic process framework and incorporating QCD-based stationary states from the color-glass condensate approach of Mehtar-Tani and Wolschin (2009). It enables time-dependent calculations of net-proton distributions that can be compared with stopping data from SPS and RHIC experiments, and provides a foundation for future extensions to nonconstant diffusion coefficients and produced-particle distributions.
