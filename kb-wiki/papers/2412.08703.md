# Off-shell vertices in heavy particle effective theories and $B\rightarrow Dπ\ell ν$

> We study the modifications to decay amplitudes in heavy to heavy semileptonic decays with multiple hadrons in the final state due to intermediate heavy hadrons being off-shell or having a finite width. Combining Heavy Hadron Chiral Perturbation Theory (HH$χ$PT) with a BCFW on-shell factorization formula, we show that these effects induce $O(1/M)$ corrections to the standard results computed in the narrow-width approximation and therefore are important in extracting form factors from data. A combination of perturbative unitarity, analyticity, and reparameterization invariance fully determine these corrections in terms of known Isgur-Wise functions without the need to introduce new form factors. In doing so, we develop a novel technique to compute the boundary term at complex infinity in the BCFW formula for theories with derivatively coupled scalars. While we have used the $\bar B\rightarrow Dπ\ellν$ decay as an example, these techniques can generally be applied to effective field theories with (multiple) distinct reference vectors.

## Key claim

The paper shows that off-shell effects from intermediate heavy hadrons (either $B$ or $D^{(*)}$ mesons) in $B \to D\pi\ell\nu$ decays induce $\mathcal{O}(1/M)$ corrections to the narrow-width approximation, and these corrections are fully determined by known Isgur-Wise functions using a combination of perturbative unitarity, analyticity, and reparameterization invariance, without introducing new form factors.

## Method

The authors combine Heavy Hadron Chiral Perturbation Theory (HH$\chi$PT) with a BCFW on-shell factorization formula to decompose multi-particle amplitudes into on-shell (or "on-pole" for unstable resonances) amplitudes with fewer external legs. They deform the momenta of the lepton-neutrino pair and the pion via a complex parameter $z$, apply Cauchy's theorem to extract residues at poles corresponding to intermediate heavy hadrons going on-shell, and use reparameterization invariance (RPI) of HH$\chi$PT to relate one-particle weak matrix elements at finite residual momenta to those computed at zero residual momenta. The boundary term from the "pole at infinity" is computed explicitly in terms of higher-dimensional contact operators.

## Key numbers

- The off-shell corrections are $\mathcal{O}(1/M)$ corrections to the standard narrow-width approximation results, where $M$ is the heavy hadron mass (either $M_B$ or $M_D$).
- The soft pion regime is defined by $p_\pi \lesssim 400$ MeV, with the HH$\chi$PT cutoff $\Lambda_\chi \sim 1$ GeV.
- The residual mass splittings $\Delta M^{(\prime)}$ between ground and excited hadrons are assumed to satisfy $\Delta M^{(\prime)} \lesssim \Lambda_\chi$.
- The double-pole residue terms contain the factor $\kpst^{(\prime)2} = m_\pi^2 - (\Delta M^{(\prime)})^2$, which appears in the $\mathcal{O}(1/M^{(\prime)})$ corrections.

## Context

This work extends the HH$\chi$PT framework of Boyd (1994, 1995) for $B\to D\ell\nu\pi$ decays beyond leading order in the heavy mass expansion, incorporating $\mathcal{O}(p_\pi/M)$ and $\mathcal{O}(\Gamma/M)$ corrections needed for percent-level precision required by Belle II and LHCb data. It provides a systematic method to compute off-shell corrections without introducing new form factors, enabling improved extraction of $|V_{cb}|$ and tests of lepton flavor universality. The techniques can be applied to other processes involving heavy particles and additional pions ($B \to D^{(*)}\ell\nu\pi\pi$) or soft final-state radiation ($B\to D^{(*)}\ell\nu\gamma$).
