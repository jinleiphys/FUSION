# Correlations in the Monte Carlo Glauber model

> Event-by-event fluctuations of observables are often modeled using the Monte Carlo Glauber model, in which the energy is initially deposited in sources associated with wounded nucleons. In this paper, we analyze in detail the correlations between these sources in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions. There are correlations arising from nucleon-nucleon correlations within each nucleus, and correlations due to the collision mechanism, which we dub twin correlations. We investigate this new phenomenon in detail. At the RHIC and LHC energies, correlations are found to have modest effects on size and eccentricity fluctuations, such that the Glauber model produces to a good approximation a collection of independent sources.

## Key claim

At RHIC and LHC energies, correlations between sources in the Monte Carlo Glauber model, arising from both nucleon-nucleon correlations within each nucleus and from the collision mechanism itself (twin correlations), have only modest effects on size and eccentricity fluctuations, so that the Glauber model produces to a good approximation a collection of independent sources.

## Method

The authors analyze the density-density correlation function \(S(\x,\y)\) for sources in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions using the wounded-nucleon Glauber model, with point-like sources placed at the centers of wounded nucleons (for A-A) or at mid-distance between the projectile proton and each hit target nucleon (for p-A). They develop semi-analytic expressions for the one-body and two-body source distributions, incorporating short-range nucleon-nucleon repulsion via a Gaussian parametrization and a new "twin" correlation arising from the pairing of projectile and target participants. Numerical simulations are carried out with the GLISSANDO code for central collisions (impact parameter \(b=0\)) at fixed source number \(n\), and the ratios \(R({\cal O})\) of variances with and without correlations are computed for the mean squared radius and eccentricities.

## Key numbers

- For p+Pb collisions at LHC energy (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02\) TeV, \(\sigma_{NN}^{\rm inel}\simeq 68\) mb), the Gaussian wounding profile parameters are \(A=0.92\) and \(\sigma_w=1.08\) fm (Eq. 3).
- The short-range nucleon-nucleon repulsion in the transverse plane is parametrized as \(d(s)=B\exp(-s^2/(2\sigma_d^2))\) with \(B=0.11\) and \(\sigma_d=0.56\) fm (Eq. 2).
- For p+Pb collisions with \(n=15\) participants, the increase in \(\langle r_{\rm rms}^2\rangle\) due to repulsive correlations is a modest \(0.6\%\) (Sec. IV C).
- For p+Pb collisions, correlations decrease \(\langle|\varepsilon_2|^2\rangle\) by a modest \(4\%\) and \(\langle|\varepsilon_3|^2\rangle\) by about \(1\%\) (Sec. IV C).
- For Pb-Pb collisions at \(b=0\) with \(\sigma_{NN}^{\rm inel}=20\) mb and \(n=371\), the twin correlation produces a sharp peak in the half-integrated pair correlation function \(g(\Delta)\) (Fig. 5).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of initial-state fluctuations in the Glauber model, such as those by Broniowski (2010) and Alvioli (2011) on nuclear correlations, and by Alver (2008) on twin correlations. It provides a systematic analytic and numerical framework for evaluating how correlations affect fluctuation observables (mean squared radius, eccentricities) that seed anisotropic flow and \(\langle p_T\rangle\) fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions. The results enable a clearer separation of initial-state correlation effects from other sources of fluctuations, and support the common approximation of independent sources in Monte Carlo Glauber simulations at RHIC and LHC energies.
