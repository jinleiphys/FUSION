# Isospin Diffusion in Heavy-Ion Collisions and the Neutron Skin Thickness of Lead

> The correlation between the thickness of the neutron skin in Pb-208, and the degree of isospin diffusion in heavy-ion collisions is examined. The same equation of state is used to compute the degree of isospin diffusion in an isospin-depedent transport model and the neutron skin thickness in the Hartree-Fock approximation. We find that skin thicknesses less than 0.15 fm are excluded by the isospin diffusion data.

## Key claim

Neutron skin thicknesses in lead-208 less than 0.15 fm are excluded by isospin diffusion data from heavy-ion collisions, but this conclusion may be modified if improved transport models with reduced in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections are used.

## Method

The authors used the IBUU04 isospin- and momentum-dependent transport model to compute the isospin diffusion parameter R_i for 124Sn on 112Sn at 50 MeV/nucleon and an impact parameter of about 6 fm. They then fitted the same set of momentum-dependent interaction (MDI) equations of state (with varying symmetry energy stiffness parameter x) to Skyrme models and used Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations to determine the neutron skin thickness in 208Pb. The correlation between the two observables was examined for four values of x (-2, -1, 0, 1).

## Key numbers

- Neutron skin thicknesses in 208Pb for the four equations of state: 0.38 fm (x=-2), 0.28 fm (x=-1), 0.22 fm (x=0), 0.16 fm (x=1)
- Experimental range for 208Pb neutron skin thickness: 0.07 to 0.24 fm
- Slope L of the symmetry energy at saturation density: 153.0 MeV (x=-2), 107.4 MeV (x=-1), 62.1 MeV (x=0), 16.4 MeV (x=1)
- Curvature K_sym of the symmetry energy at saturation density: 276.3 MeV (x=-2), 94.1 MeV (x=-1), -88.6 MeV (x=0), -270.4 MeV (x=1)
- Binding energy of 208Pb from calculations: 7.8598 to 7.8719 MeV (experimental: 7.8675 MeV)
- Charge radius of 208Pb from calculations: 5.517 to 5.547 fm (experimental: 5.503 fm)
- Maximum neutron star masses for MDI EOS: 1.92 solar masses (x=0), 2.08 solar masses (x=-1), 2.13 solar masses (x=-2)
- Radii of maximum mass neutron stars: 10.1 km (x=0), 11.2 km (x=-1), 11.8 km (x=-2)

## Context

This work makes a direct connection between nuclear structure and heavy-ion collision observables using the same equation of state, extending prior studies that treated these separately. The isospin diffusion data from NSCL/MSU (Tsang 2004, Chen 2005) had previously been analyzed with the IBUU04 model to constrain the symmetry energy. The correlation found here suggests neutron skin thicknesses above about 0.2 fm are consistent with the transport calculations, placing the skin on the upper range of experimental uncertainty. The results are consistent with the relativistic mean-field study by Todd-Rutel and Piekarewicz (2005) which found a skin thickness of 0.21 fm, and with neutron star observations including the redshift measurement of EXO0748-676 (Cottam 2002) and radius measurements (Gendre 2003).
