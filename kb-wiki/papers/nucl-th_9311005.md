# Medium Modification to the $Ω$-Meson Mass in the Walecka Model

> We calculate the effective mass of the $ω$ meson in nuclear matter in a relativistic random-phase approximation to the Walecka model. The dressing of the meson propagator is driven by its coupling to particle-hole pairs and nucleon-antinucleon ($N\bar{N}$) excitations. We report a reduction in the $ω$-meson mass of about 170~MeV at nuclear-matter saturation density. This reduction arises from a competition between the density-dependent (particle-hole) dressing of the propagator and vacuum polarization ($N\bar{N}$ pairs). While density-dependent effects lead to an increase in the mass proportional to the classical plasma frequency, vacuum polarization leads to an even larger reduction caused by the reduced effective nucleon mass in the medium.

## Key claim

The effective mass of the omega meson in nuclear matter is reduced by about 170 MeV at saturation density, but this result arises from a sensitive competition between density-dependent dressing (which increases the mass) and vacuum polarization (which decreases it), with the final outcome depending on the specific coupling constants adopted in the model.

## Method

The authors calculated the omega-meson self-energy in nuclear matter using a relativistic random-phase approximation (RPA) to the Walecka model (QHD-I), solving Dyson's equation for the dressed meson propagator. The ground state of nuclear matter was obtained in both the mean-field theory (MFT) approximation, which neglects vacuum loops, and the relativistic Hartree approximation (RHA), which includes vacuum contributions to the nucleon self-energy. The dressing of the omega-meson propagator was driven by its coupling to particle-hole pairs (density-dependent effects) and nucleon-antinucleon (N anti-N) excitations (vacuum polarization), with the effective mass found by locating the zero of the inverse transverse propagator.

## Key numbers

- Reduction in the omega-meson mass of about 170 MeV at nuclear-matter saturation density (Fermi momentum k_F = 1.30 fm^-1)
- Free-space omega-meson mass fixed at m_v = 783 MeV; free nucleon mass fixed at M = 939 MeV
- Nuclear matter saturation properties reproduced with binding energy 15.75 MeV and Fermi momentum k_F = 1.3 fm^-1
- In the low-density limit, the density-dependent dressing gives m_v*^2 = m_v^2 + Omega^2, where Omega^2 = g_v^2 rho_B / M is the classical plasma frequency
- The combination (g_v^2 / 3 pi^2)(C_s^2 / C_v^2) is approximately 5.36, exceeding unity and causing vacuum polarization to dominate
- Scalar-vector mixing contributes at most 1.5 MeV to the shift for all densities below saturation
- At large three-momentum transfer (|q| ~ 1 GeV), the longitudinal mass is reduced by an additional 50 MeV relative to the transverse value near saturation density

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculation by Chin (1977), who reported an increase in the omega-meson mass using an approximation that ignored coupling to N anti-N excitations. The present calculation includes vacuum polarization, which was neglected in the earlier MFT-based studies of collective modes in nuclear matter by Lim and Horowitz (1988). The results contrast with predictions from other models such as the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Hosaka, 1990) and QCD sum rules (Hatsuda and Lee, 1992; Koike, 1993), which give shifts ranging from -100 to +100 MeV, highlighting the need for experimental determination via photoproduction of lepton pairs from nuclei, as proposed for CEBAF (1990).
