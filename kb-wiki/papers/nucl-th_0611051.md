# Large two-pion-exchange contributions to the $pp\to ppπ^0$ reaction

> The large two-pion exchange amplitudes are calculated in HB$χ$PT and their net contribution to the reaction cross section is large.

# Large two-pion-exchange contributions to the $pp\to pp\pi^0$ reaction

## Key claim
The two-pion-exchange (TPE) amplitudes in HB$\chi$PT, while formally of higher chiral order than the one-pion-exchange (Resc) amplitude, are numerically as large or larger than the Resc contribution; however, the leading momentum terms of the three dominant TPE diagrams cancel, and the net large contribution arises from their sub-leading parts.

## Method
The TPE transition operators for the $pp\to pp\pi^0$ reaction were evaluated analytically in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HB$\chi$PT) following the approach of Dmitrašinović et al. (1999). These operators were then sandwiched between phenomenologically determined distorted $NN$ wave functions, and the momentum integrals were evaluated. The threshold fixed kinematics approximation (FKA) was used to perform an asymptotic expansion in the two-nucleon momentum transfer $|\vec{k}|$, separating the leading ($t_1$) and sub-leading ($t_2$, $t_3$) momentum contributions.

## Key numbers
- The ratio $R_K$ of each TPE transition operator to the Resc amplitude (in the plane wave approximation) for diagrams I through VII: I = -0.70, II = -6.70, III = -6.70, IV = 9.50, V = 0.18, VI = 0.14, VII = 2.65.
- The leading momentum terms $t_1$ for diagrams II, III, and IV are proportional to -2, -1, and +3 respectively, and sum to zero, confirming the result of Hanhart and Kaiser (2002).
- The ratios of the full amplitudes II:III:IV are approximately -2:-2:3, not -2:-1:3, indicating non-cancellation of the dominant amplitudes.
- After removing the $t_1$ leading term, the sum of the TPE amplitudes (II, III, and IV) is larger than the Resc amplitude.

## Context
This work extends the HB$\chi$PT calculations of Dmitrašinović et al. (1999) for the $pp\to pp\pi^0$ reaction at threshold, where earlier impulse approximation and one-pion-exchange diagrams were found to interfere destructively, giving a very small cross section. Lee and Riska (1993) had shown that heavy meson ($\sigma$ and $\omega$) exchange was needed to explain the measured cross section; the $\sigma$-meson exchange is more properly described by correlated two-pion-exchange. The results confirm the momentum counting rule proposed by Cohen et al. (1996) and reviewed by Hanhart (2004), and show that sub-leading TPE contributions are essential for the reaction amplitude.
