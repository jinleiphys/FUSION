# The small $K π$ component in the $K^*$ wave functions

> We use a recently developed formalism which generalizes the Weinberg's compositeness condition to partial waves higher than s-wave in order to determine the probability of having a $K π$ component in the $K^*$ wave function. A fit is made to the $K π$ phase shifts in p-wave, from where the coupling of $K^*$ to $K π$ and the $K π$ loop function are determined. These ingredients allow us to determine that the $K^*$ is a genuine state, different to a $K π$ component, in a proportion of about 80%.

## Key claim

The $K^*$ resonance is largely a "genuine" state (not a $K\pi$ molecule), with the $K\pi$ component in its wave function amounting to only about 20% – a result that holds under both a chiral unitary model and a purely phenomenological analysis, with the exact value depending on the regularization cutoff.

## Method

The authors fit the $K\pi$ $p$-wave phase shift data using a chiral unitary model with a tree-level potential, extracting the coupling $g_{K\pi}$ and the loop function $G$. They then locate the $K^*$ pole in the second Riemann sheet and apply a generalized Weinberg compositeness sum rule (Eq. 4) to compute $1-Z$, the probability that the $K^*$ is a $K\pi$ composite. A complementary phenomenological analysis uses the experimental mass and width to construct a Breit-Wigner amplitude, extrapolates to the complex plane, and evaluates $1-Z$ for a range of cutoff values.

## Key numbers

- Pole position from chiral unitary fit: $\sqrt{s_0} = (891.0 + i\,31.3)\ \text{MeV}$.
- Coupling from chiral unitary fit: $g_{K\pi} = 7.19 + i\,0.67$.
- $|1-Z|$ from chiral unitary fit: $0.229$ (i.e., about 23% $K\pi$ component).
- Pole position from phenomenological analysis: $\sqrt{s_0} = (892.0 + i\,22.4)\ \text{MeV}$.
- Coupling from phenomenological analysis: $\tilde{g}_{K\pi} = 6.08 + i\,0.50$.
- $|1-Z|$ from phenomenological analysis ranges from $0.158$ to $0.192$ for cutoffs $q_{\text{max}}$ between $700$ and $1200\ \text{MeV}$.
- Best-fit parameters: $f = 86.22\ \text{MeV}$, $G_V = 53.81\ \text{MeV}$, $M_{K^*} = 995.76\ \text{MeV}$, $q_{\text{max}} = 724.698\ \text{MeV}$.

## Context

This work extends the formalism developed by Aceti et al. (2012) for the $\rho$ meson to the $K^*$ resonance, generalizing Weinberg's compositeness condition to $p$-wave resonances. It uses the chiral unitary approach of Oller et al. (1998) for the $K\pi$ scattering amplitude and the N/D method. The result that the $K^*$ is predominantly a genuine state (about 80% non-$K\pi$ component) contrasts with the interpretation of some dynamically generated resonances and enables future studies of other vector mesons' internal structure using the same sum-rule technique.
