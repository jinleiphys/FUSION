# Photon production from non-equilibrium QGP in heavy ion collisions

> We present a calculation of thermal photon production i.e. photons from secondary interactions among particles produced in heavy ion collisions at collider energies. This is done within the framework of hydrodynamics. We take into account the lack of chemical equilibrium in QGP. It turns out that main effects from chemical non-equilibrium composition of QGP, reduction of particle number and increase in temperature, nearly cancel in photon spectrum.

# Photon production from non-equilibrium QGP in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim
The main effects of chemical non-equilibrium in QGP, a reduction in particle number and an increase in temperature, nearly cancel in the thermal photon spectrum, making the spectrum practically independent of the chemical composition of QGP in the experimentally accessible region around transverse momentum \(k_T \sim 5\) GeV. However, this near-cancellation is conditional: at high transverse momenta \(k_T \gtrsim 10\) GeV, the temperature increase becomes more important and the non-equilibrium spectrum begins to exceed the equilibrium one.

## Method
The authors model the space-time evolution of a heavy ion collision using hydrodynamics with longitudinal boost invariance and ideal fluid behavior, supplemented by rate equations for the time evolution of quark and gluon fugacities (following Biro et al.). The initial energy and particle densities are obtained from a pQCD + saturation model (EKRT). Photon emission rates in QGP, complete to leading order in \(\alpha_{em}\) and \(\alpha_s\), are extended to the non-equilibrium case using the method of Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe as adapted by the authors, and then integrated over the hydrodynamic space-time history to obtain the thermal photon spectrum.

## Key numbers
- Critical temperature \(T_c = 167\) MeV, used both in and out of chemical equilibrium.
- The experimentally accessible region for detecting thermal photons is around transverse momentum \(k_T \sim 5\) GeV, where the spectrum is practically independent of chemical composition.
- At high transverse momenta \(k_T \gtrsim 10\) GeV, the non-equilibrium spectrum starts to deviate from the equilibrium spectrum as the temperature increase becomes more important.
- The hadron gas equation of state includes all hadronic states up to a mass of 1.3 GeV.

## Context
This work extends hydrodynamic calculations of thermal photon production (e.g., the framework of Kolb et al. for RHIC) by incorporating chemical non-equilibrium in the QGP phase. It builds on prior work by Biro et al. on rate equations for fugacities and by Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe on photon emission rates. The results show that thermal photons are not a clean probe of initial temperature alone, but rather measure a combination of temperature and particle densities, which has implications for interpreting photon spectra from heavy ion collisions at LHC and RHIC energies. The paper also notes that a new contribution from jet-induced photon emission (proposed by Fries and Gale) will mix with the thermal spectrum, increasing the overall signal from QGP.
