# Bulk viscosity in a plasma of Gribov-Zwanziger gluons

> We investigate dynamic properties of a plasma whose constituents are confining gluons resulting from the Gribov quantization. In a static formulation, this system reproduces qualitatively the pure-glue equation of state and thereby encodes crucial features of the phase transition. The dynamic description proposed in this work allows us to study non-equilibrium transport phenomena with the inclusion of confinement effects. In particular, we determine the non-equilibrium behaviour of the interaction measure (trace anomaly) and find the form of the bulk viscosity coefficient. The latter may be used in phenomenological applications to heavy-ion collisions.

# Bulk viscosity in a plasma of Gribov-Zwanziger gluons

## Key claim
The bulk viscosity of a gluon plasma can be directly linked to the confining properties of Yang-Mills theory through the Gribov-Zwanziger dispersion relation, with the bulk viscosity coefficient derived from a linearized kinetic equation in the relaxation time approximation. The result is valid for close-to-equilibrium situations and shows a significant enhancement of the bulk viscosity near the phase transition temperature.

## Method
The authors construct a covariant kinetic theory for gluons obeying the Gribov-Zwanziger dispersion relation E(k) = sqrt(k^2 + gamma_G^4/k^2), where gamma_G is the Gribov parameter fixed to 700 MeV. They study a (0+1)-dimensional boost-invariant system (Bjorken flow) using the relaxation time approximation (RTA) kinetic equation, solving for the distribution function analytically and numerically. The bulk viscosity coefficient is extracted from the linearized kinetic equation by expanding the distribution function around local equilibrium and identifying the bulk viscous pressure Pi = -zeta * partial_mu u^mu.

## Key numbers
- Gribov parameter gamma_G = 700 MeV, chosen to best describe lattice data at T > T_c
- Initial proper time tau_0 = 0.5 fm/c, initial temperature T(tau_0) = 600 MeV
- Two fixed relaxation times studied: tau_rel = 1 fm/c and tau_rel = 2 fm/c
- Critical temperature T_c = 260 MeV used for comparison with lattice data
- The equilibrium GZ plasma reproduces the interaction measure peak at approximately half the lattice value in the temperature range T ~ 1.5-4 T_c
- The bulk viscosity vanishes in the gamma_G -> 0 limit (conformal massless gas)

## Context
This work extends the static Gribov-Zwanziger framework, previously applied to finite-temperature thermodynamics by Zwanziger (2004) and Fukushima (2013), into a dynamical non-equilibrium description. The Gribov dispersion relation qualitatively reproduces the pure-glue equation of state from lattice data (Borsanyi 2012). The bulk viscosity result is qualitatively similar to quasi-particle models (Huang 2010) but with significantly smaller magnitude, and the scaling zeta/eta proportional to (1/3 - c_s^2) at high temperature resembles results from holographic strongly-coupled theories (Benincasa 2005). The framework enables phenomenological applications to heavy-ion collision hydrodynamics and can be extended to shear viscosity and temperature-dependent Gribov parameter.
