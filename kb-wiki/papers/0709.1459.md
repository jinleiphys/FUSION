# The NICA/MPD Project at JINR (Dubna)

> A new project of Nuclotron-based Ion Collider fAcility (NICA) and Multi-Purpose Detector (MPD) is proposed at JINR (Dubna). Status of this NICA/MPD project is outlined. Main system parameters and some construction details are given.

# The NICA/MPD Project at JINR (Dubna)

## Key claim
The NICA/MPD project proposes a new heavy-ion collider facility at JINR (Dubna) designed to study hot and dense strongly interacting matter, with the specific aim of searching for deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration phase transitions and the QCD critical endpoint in the region of √s_NN = 3–9 GeV. The project claims this energy region will reach the highest baryonic density under laboratory conditions, but this is conditional on the completion of construction stages spanning 2007–2013 and a total estimated cost of approximately 70 million USD.

## Method
The paper outlines the design and construction plan for the NICA accelerator chain, which includes a KRION heavy-ion source, a Linac injector, a Booster ring, the existing Nuclotron synchrotron (6 AGeV), and two superconducting collider rings of 251.2 m circumference. The Multi-Purpose Detector (MPD) is designed with a silicon vertex tracker, a central tracker (either TPC or Straw Tracker), a Time-of-Flight system based on RPC, electromagnetic calorimeters, and zero-degree calorimeters, all operating in a 0.5 T magnetic field with |y| < 2 acceptance and 2π azimuthal coverage.

## Key numbers
- Maximum collision energy of √s = 9 AGeV for the NICA collider
- Average luminosity of 1 × 10^27 cm^−2 s^−1 for the collider
- Peak design kinetic energy of 238U ions in the collider is 3.5 AGeV
- Ring circumference of 251.2 m
- Particle number per bunch: 2.0 × 10^9 ions/bunch
- Number of bunches: 20
- Momentum spread Δp/p = 0.001
- IBS lifetime ≥ 100 seconds
- Beta function at interaction points β* = 0.5 m
- Laslett tune shift ΔQ = 0.0044
- Beam-beam parameter = 0.009
- Interaction rate of U+U events at luminosity 10^27 cm^−2 s^−1 is 10 kHz
- Interaction rate of central events is 500 s^−1
- Accuracy of vertex positioning better than 0.2 mm
- TPC momentum measurement accuracy of ~1% in the range p = 0.2–2 GeV/c
- RPC time resolution of 100 ps providing π–K separation with 5% probability for p < 2 GeV/c
- Total project cost estimate: ~70 million USD (NICA ~40 million USD, MPD ~25 million USD)

## Context
This project extends the existing Nuclotron facility (commissioned in 1993) at JINR (Dubna) and is designed to complement the FAIR facility in Darmstadt (expected SIS300 operation in 2015) in exploring the high-baryon-density region of the QCD phase diagram. The energy region of NICA covers the domain where the highest baryonic density at freeze-out is expected, in contrast to the lower net baryon density at RHIC and LHC energies. The paper references prior work on dynamical trajectories from relativistic 3-fluid hydrodynamics (IRT05), phase boundary calculations in a two-phase bag model (KSTR06), and lattice QCD critical endpoint calculations (Fodor01). The project enables systematic scanning of excitation functions for signatures of deconfinement, chiral symmetry restoration, and the QCD critical endpoint in the √s_NN = 3–9 GeV range.
