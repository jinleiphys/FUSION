# On the isoscalar-isovector splitting of pygmy dipole structures

> The electric dipole response of $^{140}$Ce is investigated using the fully consistent relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation. By analyzing the isospin structure of the E1 response, it is shown that the low-energy (pygmy) strength separates into two segments with different isospin character. The more pronounced pygmy structure at lower energy is composed of predominantly isoscalar states with surface-peaked transition densities. At somewhat higher energy the calculated E1 strength is primarily of isovector character, as expected for the low-energy tail of the giant dipole resonance. The results are in qualitative agreement with those obtained in recent $(γ,γ')$ and $(α,α'γ)$ experiments, and provide a simple explanation for the splitting of low-energy E1 strength into two groups of states with different isospin structure and radial dependence of the corresponding transition densities.

## Key claim

The low-energy (pygmy) dipole strength in \(^{140}\)Ce separates into two segments with different isospin character: a lower-energy, predominantly isoscalar structure with surface-peaked transition densities, and a higher-energy, primarily isovector component that forms the low-energy tail of the giant dipole resonance. This splitting provides a simple explanation for why the lower part of the pygmy dipole resonance is excited by both \((\alpha,\alpha'\gamma)\) and \((\gamma,\gamma')\) experiments, while the higher-energy component is only excited by photons.

## Method

The fully self-consistent relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation (RQRPA), based on the Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model with the DD-ME2 effective interaction, was used to calculate the electric dipole response of \(^{140}\)Ce. The isospin structure of the E1 strength was analyzed by examining the radial dependence of proton and neutron transition densities, identifying states as predominantly isoscalar when the proton and neutron transition densities were in phase over more than 70%, 80%, or 90% of the radial interval [0,8] fm.

## Key numbers

- The total RHB+RQRPA E1 transition strength for \(^{140}\)Ce shows the giant dipole resonance peaked at approximately 15 MeV.
- The low-energy pygmy dipole structure is peaked at 8.4 MeV excitation energy.
- The one-neutron separation energy for \(^{140}\)Ce is \(S_n = 9.2\) MeV.
- For the state at 8.4 MeV, 91% of the total QRPA amplitude corresponds to neutron transitions, while only 9% comes from proton \(2qp\) excitations.
- The predicted PDR structure is calculated more than 2 MeV above the pygmy structure seen in experiments.
- When experimental neutron single-particle energies replace the DD-ME2 values, the E1 strength below 10 MeV fragments into two structures centered at approximately 5 MeV and approximately 8 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the pygmy dipole resonance in \(^{140}\)Ce, following experimental observations from Savran (2006) using \((\alpha,\alpha'\gamma)\) and Zilges (2002) using \((\gamma,\gamma')\) scattering, which showed that the PDR separates into two parts with different excitation mechanisms. The theoretical results provide a microscopic explanation for this splitting based on isospin structure and radial dependence of transition densities, and suggest that future experimental studies across the neutron threshold may be necessary to account for missing low-lying E1 strength. The analysis is limited to simple QRPA and does not include coupling to low-energy surface phonons, which could be addressed using the quasiparticle phonon model of Tsoneva (2008) or the relativistic quasiparticle time blocking approximation of Litvinova (2007).
