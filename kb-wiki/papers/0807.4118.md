# The Dynamical Dipole Mode in Fusion Reactions with Exotic Nuclear Beams

> We report the properties of the prompt dipole radiation, produced via a collective bremsstrahlung mechanism, in fusion reactions with exotic beams. We show that the gamma yield is sensitive to the density dependence of the symmetry energy below/around saturation. Moreover we find that the angular distribution of the emitted photons from such fast collective mode can represent a sensitive probe of its excitation mechanism and of fusion dynamics in the entrance channel.

# The Dynamical Dipole Mode in Fusion Reactions with Exotic Nuclear Beams

## Key claim

The gamma yield from prompt dipole radiation in fusion reactions with exotic neutron-rich beams is sensitive to the density dependence of the symmetry energy below/around saturation, but the sensitivity is conditional on the specific Iso-EoS choice and is amplified by the larger initial dipole moment of the exotic system; the angular distribution of emitted photons can probe the excitation mechanism and fusion dynamics, though the observed anisotropy is reduced from the ideal sin²θ pattern due to rotation of the oscillation axis during emission.

## Method

The authors performed semiclassical Boltzmann-Nordheim-Vlasov (BNV) transport simulations of the reactions ¹³²Sn+⁵⁸Ni and ¹²⁴Sn+⁵⁸Ni at 10 MeV/nucleon for impact parameters b = 0, 2, and 4 fm, using 200 test particles per nucleon and in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections. Two parametrizations of the symmetry energy were tested: an "Asysoft" EoS with weak density dependence below saturation and an "Asystiff" EoS with linear density dependence. The gamma yield was computed via a bremsstrahlung formula from the Fourier transform of the dipole acceleration, and angular distributions were obtained by weighting the emission over time intervals accounting for the rotation of the dipole axis.

## Key numbers

- Initial dipole moment at touching configuration: ~45 fm for the ¹³²Sn+⁵⁸Ni system, compared to ~33 fm for the ¹²⁴Sn+⁵⁸Ni system.
- Total gamma yield (energy and impact parameter integrated): 3.0×10⁻³ (Asysoft) and 2.5×10⁻³ (Asystiff) for ¹²⁴Sn; 5.7×10⁻³ (Asysoft) and 4.4×10⁻³ (Asystiff) for ¹³²Sn.
- Ratio of total yields (Asysoft relative to Asystiff): 1.2 for the ¹²⁴Sn reaction and 1.3 for the ¹³²Sn reaction.
- Dipole oscillation extends over the first 250–300 fm/c; the dinuclear system rotates about 20 degrees during the dominant emission period (50–150 fm/c) at b = 4 fm.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of the dynamical dipole mode using BNV (Chomaz 1993, Baran 1996), TDHF (Bonche 1981, Umar 1985, Simenel 2001), and CoMD (Papa 2003) approaches, and builds on experimental evidence from comparisons of reactions with different entrance-channel charge asymmetry (Fliessbach 1985, Pierroutsakou 2005). The results enable planning of new experiments with radioactive beams to constrain the density dependence of the symmetry energy below saturation, complementing studies of isospin dynamics in heavy-ion collisions at higher energies (Xu 2000, Tsang 2004, Baran 2004, Fili 2005). The angular distribution analysis is motivated by recent data from Martin (2008) and provides a framework to extract the lifetime of the transient collective mode.
