# E2/M1 ratio from the Mainz $p(\vecγ, p)π^0$

> We reply to the Comment of R.L. Workman on the E2/M1 ratio from the Mainz photoproduction data, nucl-th/9702052. We support our published result for the E2/M1 ratio of REM = -(2.5 +- 0.2 +- 0.2)%.

## Key claim

The authors defend their published value of the E2/M1 ratio for the N→Δ transition, REM = -(2.5 ± 0.2 ± 0.2)%, against a Comment that questioned the systematic error, arguing that at the resonance energy (Eγ = 340 MeV) the neglected d-wave contributions and isospin-1/2 corrections are small enough to keep the result within the quoted uncertainty.

## Method

The authors used differential cross sections and polarized photon asymmetries from the reaction γp → pπ0 measured with the DAPHNE detector at the Mainz Microtron MAMI. They extracted the E2/M1 ratio from the coefficients C∥ and A∥ of the angular distribution, using exact s- and p-wave formulas, and demonstrated that at the resonance energy (Eγ = 340 MeV) contributions from d-waves and isospin-1/2 components are negligible or cancel. They also showed that a simplified approximation suggested by Workman yields REM = -2.65%, which lies within their systematic error of ±0.2%.

## Key numbers

- REM = -(2.5 ± 0.2 ± 0.2)% for the N→Δ transition from the Mainz p(γ,p)π0 data.
- At Eγ = 340 MeV, the resonance energy where δ33 = 90°, the simplified approximation gives REM = -2.65%, within the quoted systematic error.
- A fixed-t dispersion relation analysis by Hanstein et al. gives REM = -2.4%, in good agreement with the Mainz result.
- Below and above the resonance, contributions from partial waves with L ≥ 2 are of order 10–20% of the differential cross section at 0° and 180°.

## Context

This reply defends the Mainz result against a Comment by Workman (1997) that questioned the systematic error and suggested a different value from the VPI/SAID multipole analysis (REM = -1.5 ± 0.5%). The authors note that a recent fixed-t dispersion relation analysis by Hanstein et al., based on new data from ELSA, MAMI, and TRIUMF, gives REM = -2.4%, supporting their value. The work enables more precise tests of chiral perturbation theory predictions for the N→Δ quadrupole transition.
