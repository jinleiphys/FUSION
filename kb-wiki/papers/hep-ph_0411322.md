# Spectator-model operators in point-form relativistic quantum mechanics

> We address the construction of transition operators for electromagnetic, weak, and hadronic reactions of relativistic few-quark systems along the spectator model. While the problem is of relevance for all forms of relativistic quantum mechanics, we specifically adhere to the point form, since it preserves the spectator character of the corresponding transition operators in any reference frame. The conditions imposed on the construction of point-form spectator-model operators are discussed and their implications are exemplified for mesonic decays of baryon resonances within a relativistic constituent quark model.

## Key claim

The paper argues that point-form spectator-model (PFSM) operators, while preserving their spectator character in all reference frames, are inherently effective many-body operators rather than genuine one-body operators, and that the normalization factor in their construction is not uniquely determined by Poincaré invariance and charge normalization, leading to an ambiguity that significantly affects numerical predictions for inelastic processes such as mesonic decays.

## Method

The authors constructed transition operators for electromagnetic, weak, and hadronic reactions within the point form of relativistic quantum mechanics, imposing spectator conditions where the external particle couples to only one quark while the other two are spectators. They derived the general form of the normalization factor as a function of an asymmetry parameter \(y\) (or exponent \(x\)) and computed pionic decay widths of \(N\) and \(\Delta\) baryon resonances using wave functions from the Goldstone-boson-exchange constituent quark model (GBE CQM), comparing results for different normalization choices.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of point-form spectator-model calculations for electromagnetic baryon form factors (Wagenbrunn 2000, Boffi 2001) and mesonic decays (Melde 2002, Melde 2004) by systematically analyzing the ambiguity in the normalization factor and its impact on decay widths. The paper contrasts the point-form approach with other forms of relativistic quantum mechanics, noting that in the instant form the spectator-model character is not maintained under Lorentz transformations (Polyzou 1986, Lev 1995). The findings enable future work to incorporate explicit many-body operators beyond the spectator approximation, and the authors recommend the symmetric normalization choice for consistency with electroweak results.
