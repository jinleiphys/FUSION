# Partial Wave Analysis of Chiral NN Interactions

> We analyze chiral interactions to N2LO on the light of proton-proton and neutron-proton scattering data published from 1950 till 2013 and discuss conditions under which the chiral coefficients can be extracted

## Key claim

The chiral low-energy constants \(c_1, c_3, c_4\) can be extracted from proton-proton and neutron-proton scattering data with statistical errors, but only when the fit is carried out up to laboratory energies of 350 MeV and using a consistent database selected by an improved \(3\sigma\) criterion; the extraction depends crucially on making the fit to that energy range.

## Method

The authors performed a simultaneous partial-wave analysis (PWA) of proton-proton and neutron-proton scattering data from 1950 to 2013 below pion production threshold (laboratory energies up to 350 MeV), using delta-shell potentials for the unknown short-range part of the interaction and a charge-dependent one-pion-exchange (OPE) potential above a cutoff radius \(r_c\), together with electromagnetic effects, vacuum polarization, and magnetic moment contributions. They compared fits using OPE alone, OPE plus chiral two-pion-exchange (TPE) at NLO, and TPE at N2LO, for three different cutoff radii (1.8, 2.4, and 3.0 fm), and applied an improved \(3\sigma\) criterion to select a consistent database of 6713 data points.

## Key numbers

- Total database of 8124 fitting data (7709 pp and np data plus 415 normalization data) in the LAB energy-angle plane.
- With 46 fitting parameters and a cutoff radius \(r_c = 3.0\) fm, the OPE fit to the consistent database yields \(\chi^2/\nu = 1.06\).
- The N2LO TPE fit with \(r_c = 1.8\) fm and 30+3 parameters yields \(\chi^2/\nu = 1.10\) on the consistent database.
- Extracted chiral constants (in GeV\(^{-1}\) units): \(c_1 = -0.41 \pm 1.08\), \(c_3 = -4.66 \pm 0.60\), \(c_4 = 4.31 \pm 0.17\), with correlation \(r(c_1, c_3) = -1\).
- The maximal center-of-mass momentum for inelastic processes is \(p_{\rm CM} = \sqrt{m_\pi M_N}\), corresponding to a resolution scale \(\Delta r \sim 0.6\) fm.
- The number of parameters for an unknown interaction below \(r_c = 3\) fm and momentum \(p \le 2\) fm\(^{-1}\) is estimated as \(N_{\rm par} \sim 60\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous partial-wave analyses (Navarro Perez, 2011; Perez, 2013) by systematically comparing OPE and chiral TPE interactions at NLO and N2LO, and by applying an improved \(3\sigma\) criterion proposed by Gross and Stadler (2008) to rescue data that would otherwise be discarded. The results enable error propagation for phase shifts and nuclear matrix elements, and show that chiral TPE contributions become statistically invisible when fits are limited to laboratory energies below 125 MeV (Amaro, 2013), motivating the use of higher-energy data to determine the chiral constants with meaningful precision.
