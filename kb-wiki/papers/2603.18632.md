# Single-particle strength toward N = 32: Spectroscopy of 51 Ca via the 50 Ca(d, p) reaction

> States in the neutron-rich isotope 51 Ca were populated via the 50 Ca(d, p) transfer reaction in inverse kinematics at a beam energy of about 14 AMeV. The experiment was performed using a decelerated radioactive 50 Ca beam from the OEDO facility and the TiNA2 silicon array in combination with the SHARAQ magnetic spectrometer at RIBF/RIKEN. The energies of excited states in 51 Ca were reconstructed via missing mass spectroscopy, and angular distributions of protons were measured to extract differential cross sections. From a comparison with adiabatic distorted wave approximation (ADWA) calculations, spectroscopic factors were deduced for several states, including the ground state and excited states up to 4.2 MeV. These results are compared with shell-model calculations, as well as ab initio valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) predictions. The data support the assignment of the 1/2- and 5/2- single-particle states and provide evidence for a candidate 9/2+ state with a structure consistent with neutron excitation into the 0g9/2 orbital. These findings contribute new constraints on the single-particle structure and shell evolution in neutron-rich calcium isotopes.

## Key claim

The data support the assignment of the 1/2- and 5/2- single-particle states in 51Ca and provide evidence for a candidate 9/2+ state with a structure consistent with neutron excitation into the 0g9/2 orbital, but the authors note that the fit to the excitation-energy spectrum is improved when a fifth state at 2.378 MeV (previously assigned a (1p3/2)2J=2(1p1/2)1 configuration) is included, indicating that the population of this state cannot be excluded.

## Method

States in 51Ca were populated via the 50Ca(d,p) transfer reaction in inverse kinematics using a decelerated radioactive 50Ca beam at about 14 AMeV from the OEDO facility at RIBF/RIKEN. The TiNA2 silicon array detected protons in the laboratory angular range from 103 degrees to 170 degrees, while the SHARAQ magnetic spectrometer identified reaction products by mass-to-charge ratio. Excitation energies were reconstructed via missing mass spectroscopy, and angular distributions of protons were measured to extract differential cross sections, which were compared with adiabatic distorted wave approximation (ADWA) calculations to deduce spectroscopic factors.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: about 14 AMeV (14.2 AMeV in 2022, 13.1 AMeV in 2024)
- Excited states in 51Ca were reconstructed up to 4.2 MeV
- Neutron separation energy of 51Ca: Sn = 4.815 MeV (Wang 2021)
- Known excited states at 1.718 MeV (candidate 1/2-), 3.478 MeV (candidate 5/2-), and 4.155 MeV (candidate 9/2+) were populated
- Additional state at 2.378 MeV (first 5/2- state) was included in the improved fit (chi-squared/NDF = 1.005 vs 1.197 without it)
- Energy resolution of the excitation-energy spectrum: sigma = 0.543 +/- 0.03 MeV
- 315 events were assigned to known states populated in the 50Ca(d,p)51Ca reaction

## Context

This work extends prior spectroscopy of 51Ca from three-neutron transfer reactions (Catford 1988), beta decay studies (Perrot 2006), and multinucleon transfer reactions (Rejmund 2007; Fornal 2008), which had identified candidates for the 1/2- and 5/2- single-particle states and a 9/2+ state. The direct one-neutron transfer reaction provides sensitivity to orbital angular momentum and spectroscopic strength, enabling the authors to test spin-parity assignments and compare with shell-model calculations and ab initio valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) predictions. These results constrain the single-particle structure and shell evolution in neutron-rich calcium isotopes, particularly the emergence of new magic numbers at N=32 and N=34.
