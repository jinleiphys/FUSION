# Coupling Schemes for an n su(2) Spin System

> In the framework of the Schwinger boson representation for the su(2)-algebra, the closed form is derived for the total spin eigenstates which result from the coupling of n su(2)-spins. In order to demonstrate its usefulness, the orthogonal set for the so(5)-algebra, which is reduced to four su(2)-spin systems, is obtained.

## Key claim

The paper derives a closed-form expression for total spin eigenstates resulting from the coupling of \(n\) \(su(2)\)-spins within the Schwinger boson representation, but the construction depends on specifying the order of coupling (sequential versus grouped), and the eigenstates are given as products of raising operators acting on a boson vacuum, with the quantum numbers constrained by non-negativity conditions that reproduce the conventional angular-momentum coupling rules.

## Method

The authors generalize the Schwinger boson representation for two and three \(su(2)\)-spins (previously formulated by the same group) to \(n\) spins by introducing \(2n\) boson operators \(({\hat a}_i, {\hat b}_i)\) and building-block operators \({\hat X}_+(l) = {\hat a}_l^*{\hat b}_{l+1}^* - {\hat b}_l^*{\hat a}_{l+1}^*\) and \({\hat Y}_+(l) = {\hat a}_l^*{\hat a}_{l+1} + {\hat b}_l^*{\hat b}_{l+1}\). For the sequential coupling order \([[{\hat S}(1)+{\hat S}(2)]+{\hat S}(3)]+\cdots+{\hat S}(n)\), the total spin eigenstate is constructed as a product of powers of \({\hat S}_+\), \({\hat X}_+(l)\), and \({\hat Y}_+(l)\) acting on \(({\hat b}_n^*)^{2s}\ket{0}\). For a grouped coupling order (e.g., splitting into two blocks), additional connecting operators \({\hat X}_+(m,n)\) and \({\hat Y}_+(m,n)\) are introduced. The authors prove that these states are simultaneous eigenstates of the individual spin magnitudes \({\hat S}(l)\), the intermediate total spin operators \({\hat {\mib S}}[l]^2\), the total spin \({\hat {\mib S}}^2\), and \({\hat S}_0\), and they derive the coupling rules from non-negativity conditions on the boson occupation numbers.

## Key numbers

* The total spin eigenstate for \(n\) \(su(2)\)-spins under sequential coupling is given by Eq. (35): \(\ket{\lambda_1 \mu_1 \cdots \lambda_{n-1} \mu_{n-1};ss_0} = ({\hat S}_+)^{s+s_0} \prod_{l=1}^{n-1} ({\hat X}_+(l))^{2\lambda_l} ({\hat Y}_+(l))^{2\mu_l} ({\hat b}_n^*)^{2s} \ket{0}\), where \(s\) is the total spin quantum number and \(s_0\) its projection.
* The eigenvalues of the individual spin operators \({\hat S}(l)\) are given by Eq. (37): \(s_1 = \lambda_1 + \mu_1\), \(s_l = (\lambda_{l-1} - \mu_{l-1}) + (\lambda_l + \mu_l)\) for \(l=2,\dots,n-1\), and \(s_n = (\lambda_{n-1} - \mu_{n-1}) + s\).
* The eigenvalues of the intermediate total spin operators \({\hat {\mib S}}[l]^2\) are \(\sigma_l(\sigma_l+1)\) with \(\sigma_l = \lambda_l + \mu_l\) for \(l=2,\dots,n-1\) (Eq. (40)).
* The coupling rule for sequential order (Eq. (57)): \(|s_1 - s_2| \leq \sigma_2 \leq s_1 + s_2\), \(|\sigma_l - s_{l+1}| \leq \sigma_{l+1} \leq \sigma_l + s_{l+1}\) for \(l=2,\dots,n-2\), and \(|\sigma_{n-1} - s_n| \leq \sigma_n \leq \sigma_{n-1} + s_n\), with \(s \geq 0\) and \(|s_0| \leq s\).
* For the grouped coupling order (Eq. (58)), the final coupling rule includes \(|\sigma_m - \sigma_n| \leq s \leq \sigma_m + \sigma_n\), where \(\sigma_m\) and \(\sigma_n\) are the total spins of the two groups.

## Context

This work extends the Schwinger boson representation for \(su(2)\)-spin coupling previously developed by the same authors for two spins (Yamamura et al., Ref. [22]) and three spins (Yamamura et al., Ref. [3]) to the general \(n\)-spin case. The formalism is applied to construct an orthogonal set for the \(so(5)\)-algebra, which reduces to four \(su(2)\)-spins and is relevant to charge-independent pairing correlations in nuclei (Ref. [a5]) and high-temperature superconductivity (Ref. [a6]). The results enable systematic construction of total spin eigenstates for arbitrary numbers of coupled spins, which may be used in theoretical studies of Heisenberg antiferromagnets and other many-body systems reducible to multiple \(su(2)\)-spins.
