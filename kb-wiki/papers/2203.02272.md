# Direct URCA process in light of PREX-2

> We study the implications of the recent development in nuclear symmetry energy constraints from PREX-2 data on dense matter equation of state and its impact on dURCA threshold density. In this work, we construct the equation of state within the framework of covariant density functional theory implementing coupling schemes of non-linear and density-dependent models and exploring the coupling parameter space of isovector-vector meson to baryons constrained by the isospin asymmetry parameter values deduced from recent PREX-2 data. The modified parameter sets are applied to evaluate the dense matter properties. We find that the updated data suggests the occurrence of dURCA process within neutron star even with mass as low as one solar mass.

## Key claim
The updated PREX-2 constraints on nuclear symmetry energy (E_sym ~ 38.1 +/- 4.7 MeV, L_sym ~ 106 +/- 37 MeV) imply that the direct URCA (dURCA) process can occur in neutron stars with masses as low as ~1 solar mass, contradicting earlier expectations that dURCA is forbidden for typical 1.0-1.5 solar mass stars.

## Method
The authors constructed equations of state within covariant density functional theory using both non-linear (GM1, GM2, GM3) and density-dependent (DD1, DD2, DD-ME1, DD-ME2, DD-MEX) coupling schemes. They tuned the isovector-vector rho meson coupling parameters (g_rho at saturation density and the density-dependence parameter a_rho) to reproduce the PREX-2 values of E_sym = 38.1 MeV and L_sym = 75 or 106 MeV at nuclear saturation density, while keeping other saturation properties fixed. They then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for non-rotating stars and computed proton fractions to determine dURCA threshold densities and masses.

## Key numbers
- PREX-2 neutron skin thickness of 208Pb: Delta R_np = 0.283 +/- 0.071 fm
- PREX-2 deduced symmetry energy: E_sym(n_0) = 38.1 +/- 4.7 MeV
- PREX-2 deduced slope: L_sym(n_0) = 106 +/- 37 MeV
- For L_sym = 106 MeV, dURCA threshold density n_DU ~ 1.47-1.50 n_0 across all models
- For L_sym = 106 MeV, dURCA threshold mass M_DU ranges from 0.712 M_sun (GM3) to 0.809 M_sun (DD-MEX)
- For L_sym = 75 MeV, dURCA threshold density n_DU ranges from 2.61 n_0 (DD-ME2, DD-MEX) to 2.85 n_0 (DD2)
- For L_sym = 75 MeV, dURCA threshold mass M_DU ranges from 1.553 M_sun (GM1) to 1.878 M_sun (DD-MEX)
- Original DD models (DD1, DD2, DD-ME1, DD-ME2, DD-MEX) do not allow dURCA at any density
- Original NL models allow dURCA at n_DU ~ 1.82-1.95 n_0 with M_DU ~ 0.966-1.093 M_sun

## Context
This work extends the line of research connecting nuclear symmetry energy constraints from PREX-2 (Reed et al. 2021) to neutron star cooling physics. Prior studies (Pethick 1992, Yakovlev 2004, Page 2005) established that dURCA is the most efficient neutrino emission process in neutron stars, but earlier nuclear physics data suggested it was forbidden for stars below ~1.5 M_sun. The authors build on covariant density functional models (Glendenning 1991, Typel 2005, Lalazissis 2005) and show that the PREX-2 data enables dURCA even in low-mass stars, which will require revisiting neutron star cooling models. The results relate to findings by Horowitz (2001) and Muther (1996) on the limited effect of symmetry energy on maximum mass, and to constraints from NICER observations of PSR J0740+6620 and PSR J0030+0451.
