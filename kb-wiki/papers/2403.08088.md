# Nucleon charge and magnetisation distributions: flavour separation and zeroes

> A symmetry-preserving truncation of the quantum field equations describing hadron properties is used to deliver parameter-free predictions for all nucleon elastic electromagnetic form factors and their flavour separation to large values of momentum transfer, $Q^2$. The proton electric form factor, $G_E^p$, possesses a zero, whereas that of the neutron, $G_E^n$, does not. The difference owes to the behaviour of the Pauli form factor of the proton's singly-represented valence $d$-quark. Consequently, $G_E^n>G_E^p$ on a material large-$Q^2$ domain. These predictions can be tested in modern experiments.

## Key claim

The proton electric form factor \(G_E^p\) possesses a zero at \(Q^2 = 8.86^{+1.93}_{-0.86}\,\text{GeV}^2\), while the neutron electric form factor \(G_E^n\) does not; this difference is attributed to the behavior of the Pauli form factor of the proton's singly-represented valence \(d\)-quark, and the predictions are parameter-free within a symmetry-preserving truncation of quantum field equations.

## Method

The authors solved the rainbow-ladder (RL) truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) for the nucleon bound-state problem, using a quark+quark scattering kernel with a realistic gluon interaction (Eqs. 1-2) and a mass-independent renormalization scheme. Direct calculations of the nucleon elastic electromagnetic form factors were performed up to \(Q^2 \lesssim 4\,\text{GeV}^2\); to extend predictions to \(Q^2 \gtrsim 12\,\text{GeV}^2\), the statistical Schlessinger point method (SPM), a multi-point Padé approximant scheme, was applied to extrapolate the form factors with quantified uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- Proton electric form factor zero location: \(Q_{G_E^p\text{-zero}}^2 = 8.86^{+1.93}_{-0.86}\,\text{GeV}^2\) (average of two SPM methods, Eqs. 7-9).
- Neutron electric form factor is larger than the proton's for \(Q^2 > 4.66^{+0.18}_{-0.13}\,\text{GeV}^2\).
- Predicted static properties: proton magnetic moment \(\mu_p = 2.23\) (nuclear magnetons), neutron magnetic moment \(\mu_n = -1.33\); proton charge radius squared \(\langle r_E^2 \rangle^p = 0.788\,\text{fm}^2\), neutron charge radius squared \(\langle r_E^2 \rangle^n = -0.0621\,\text{fm}^2\) (Table 1).
- Relative \(L_1\) differences between predictions and the Kelly (2004) parametrization: \(G_E^p\): 4.9%, \(G_M^p\): 7.2%, \(G_E^n\): 21%, \(G_M^n\): 4.0% (Eq. 6).
- Interaction parameters: \(\alpha_{\mathpzc G}(0)/\pi = 1.45\), gluon mass scale \(m_{\mathpzc G} = 0.54\,\text{GeV}\) (Eq. 3).

## Context

This work extends the continuum Schwinger function method (CSM) approach to nucleon form factors beyond the previous direct-calculation limit of \(Q^2 \lesssim 4\,\text{GeV}^2\), using the SPM to reach \(Q^2 \gtrsim 12\,\text{GeV}^2\). It builds on the first symmetry-preserving DSE study of the nucleon by Eichmann (2009) and provides parameter-free predictions that can be tested at modern facilities like Jefferson Lab. The results are largely in semiquantitative agreement with the quark+diquark approach of Cui (2020), though that study found a possible zero in \(G_E^n\) at \(Q^2 = 20.1^{+10.6}_{-3.5}\,\text{GeV}^2\), beyond foreseeable measurements; the present work enables refinement of such simplified models and clarifies the role of gauge-sector dynamics in emergent hadron mass.
