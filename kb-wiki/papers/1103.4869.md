# Elliptic flow of the dilute Fermi gas: From kinetics to hydrodynamics

> We use the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation to study the expansion of a dilute Fermi gas at unitarity. We focus, in particular, on the approach to the hydrodynamic limit. Our main finding are: i) In the regime that has been studied experimentally hydrodynamic effects beyond the Navier-Stokes approximation are small, ii) mean field corrections to the Boltzmann equation are not important, iii) experimental data imply that freezeout occurs very late, that means that the relaxation time remains smaller than the expansion time during the entire evolution of the system, iv) the experimental results also imply that the bulk viscosity is significantly smaller than the shear viscosity of the system.

# Elliptic flow of the dilute Fermi gas: From kinetics to hydrodynamics

## Key claim

The Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation shows that for the unitary Fermi gas under experimentally studied conditions, hydrodynamic effects beyond the Navier-Stokes approximation are small, but the matching between kinetic theory and hydrodynamics begins to deviate from linearity when the dimensionless relaxation time exceeds unity.

## Method

The authors used the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation with a scaling ansatz for the distribution function to model the expansion of a harmonically trapped dilute Fermi gas at unitarity after trap release. They computed moments of the Boltzmann equation to obtain differential equations for the scale parameters and temperature parameters, then compared these solutions to Navier-Stokes hydrodynamics and to experimental data from Cao et al. (2010) on elliptic flow.

## Key numbers

- The shear viscosity coefficient extracted from fitting experimental data at initial energy E/E_F = 3.61 was α_n = 22.1 (where η = α_n n), corresponding to a dimensionless relaxation time τ̄_n = 0.78
- The dimensionless shear viscosity parameter β_η = 0.25 for the best fit to data at E/E_F = 3.61
- Mean field corrections to the extracted relaxation time were approximately 2%, changing τ̄_n from 1.14 to 1.12 when using ξ = -0.014 from the equation of state
- Freezeout was excluded for scale parameters b_⊥ less than approximately 5, meaning the density can drop by at least a factor of 25 before freezeout occurs
- The best fit to experimental data at E/E_F = 3.61 gave β_η = 0.25 and β_ζ = 0.0, favoring vanishing bulk viscosity

## Context

This work extends the analysis of elliptic flow in the unitary Fermi gas beyond the Navier-Stokes hydrodynamics used by Cao et al. (2010) and Schafer (2009). It builds on prior kinetic theory approaches for trapped Bose and Fermi gases by Pedri (2002), Guery (2002), and Bruun (2005, 2006). The results enable more reliable extraction of shear viscosity from expansion experiments and confirm that the unitary Fermi gas remains in the hydrodynamic regime throughout the expansion, supporting the conjecture of a universal lower bound on η/s proposed by Kovtun (2004).
