# Symmetry Energy, Temperature, Density and Isoscaling Parameter as a Function of Excitation energy in A $\sim$ 100 mass region

> The symmetry energy, temperature, density and isoscaling parameter, in $^{58}$Ni + $^{58}$Ni, $^{58}$Fe + $^{58}$Ni and $^{58}$Fe + $^{58}$Fe reactions at beam energies of 30, 40 and 47 MeV/nucleon, are studied as a function of excitation energy of the multifragmenting source. It is shown that the decrease in the isoscaling parameter is related to the near flattening of the temperature in the caloric curve, and the decrease in the density and the symmetry energy with increasing excitation energy. The decrease in the symmetry energy is mainly a consequence of decreasing density with increasing excitation rather than the increasing temperature. The symmetry energy as a function of density obtained from the correlation is in close agreement with the form, E$_{sym}(ρ)$ $=$ 31.6 ($ρ/ρ_{\circ})^{0.69}$.

# Symmetry Energy, Temperature, Density and Isoscaling Parameter as a Function of Excitation energy in A ∼ 100 mass region

## Key claim

The decrease in the symmetry energy with increasing excitation energy is mainly a consequence of decreasing density rather than increasing temperature, and the extracted symmetry energy as a function of density follows the form E_sym(ρ) = 31.6 (ρ/ρ_0)^0.69, but this result depends on assuming that the entropic contribution to the symmetry free energy is small and that the temperature in the isoscaling relation and the Fermi-gas relation are related.

## Method

Fragment yield distributions from 58Ni + 58Ni, 58Fe + 58Ni, and 58Fe + 58Fe reactions at 30, 40, and 47 MeV/nucleon were used to extract the isoscaling parameter α as a function of excitation energy. Excitation energies of the multifragmenting source were determined from Boltzmann-Nordheim-Vlasov (BNV) model simulations of the collision dynamics. The Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM) was then employed with a multiplicity-dependent break-up density (varying from approximately 1/2 to 1/3 saturation density) to reproduce the measured α values, and the symmetry energy in the calculation was varied until agreement was obtained. Density as a function of excitation energy was extracted using an expanding Fermi gas model relation T = sqrt(K_o (ρ/ρ_o)^(2/3) E*).

## Key numbers

- Beam energies used: 30, 40, and 47 MeV/nucleon for all three reaction pairs
- For the Fe + Fe and Ni + Ni pair at E* = 5.0 MeV/nucleon: α^(prim) = 0.44, temperature = 5.8 MeV, ρ/ρ_o = 0.56, symmetry energy = 20.0 MeV
- For the Fe + Fe and Ni + Ni pair at E* = 9.5 MeV/nucleon: α^(prim) = 0.30, temperature = 7.0 MeV, ρ/ρ_o = 0.38, symmetry energy = 16.5 MeV
- For the Fe + Ni and Ni + Ni pair at E* = 5.0 MeV/nucleon: α^(prim) = 0.22, temperature = 5.7 MeV, ρ/ρ_o = 0.52, symmetry energy = 20.0 MeV
- For the Fe + Ni and Ni + Ni pair at E* = 9.5 MeV/nucleon: α^(prim) = 0.15, temperature = 6.8 MeV, ρ/ρ_o = 0.34, symmetry energy = 16.0 MeV
- The extracted density dependence of the symmetry energy follows E_sym(ρ) = 31.6 (ρ/ρ_o)^0.69

## Context

This work extends prior studies of isoscaling by Shetty et al. (2004) and Iglio et al. (2006) by correlating the decrease in the isoscaling parameter with the caloric curve plateau observed by Natowitz et al. (2002) and with decreasing density and symmetry energy. The extracted density dependence of the symmetry energy agrees with results from Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamic (AMD) calculations by Ono et al. (2003) and with the symmetry energy value obtained by Khoa et al. (2005) from charge exchange reactions, suggesting that surface contributions to the symmetry energy may be small during multifragmentation. This correlation enables further constraints on the nuclear matter equation of state and the density dependence of the symmetry energy, a key unknown in asymmetric nuclear matter.
