# Gauge covariance of the fermion Schwinger-Dyson equation in QED

> Any practical application of the Schwinger-Dyson equations to the study of $n$-point Green's functions of a field theory requires truncations, the best known being finite order perturbation theory. Strong coupling studies require a different approach. In the case of QED, gauge covariance is a powerful constraint. By using a spectral representation for the massive fermion propagator in QED, we are able to show that the constraints imposed by the Landau-Khalatnikov-Fradkin transformations are linear operations on the spectral densities. Here we formally define these group operations and show with a couple of examples how in practice they provide a straightforward way to test the gauge covariance of any viable truncation of the Schwinger-Dyson equation for the fermion 2-point function.

## Key claim
The paper shows that the Landau-Khalatnikov-Fradkin transformations (LKFT) impose linear constraints on the spectral densities of the massive fermion propagator in QED, and that these constraints provide a necessary and sufficient condition for any truncation of the Schwinger-Dyson equation (SDE) to be gauge covariant, provided the SDE can be written as a linear operation on the spectral functions.

## Method
The authors introduce a spectral representation for the massive fermion propagator in QED and express the LKFT as linear operations on the spectral densities. They derive the differential form of the LKFT in momentum space and solve it using fractional calculus (Riemann-Liouville operators) to obtain explicit expressions for the gauge-transformed spectral functions. They then formulate the SDE for the fermion propagator as a linear integral equation for the spectral densities and derive the consistency condition that the kernel of the SDE must satisfy to be compatible with the LKFT. Two examples are examined: the free-propagator ansatz of Bashir and Raya (2002) and the Gauge Technique of Delbourgo, Salam and Strathdee.

## Context
This work extends the study of gauge covariance constraints on SDE truncations in QED, building on the LKFT formalism originally developed by Zumino (1959) and the spectral representation approach for fermion propagators. The authors explicitly test the Gauge Technique of Delbourgo, Salam and Strathdee (1963-1977) and find it inconsistent with LKFT, confirming earlier results by Delbourgo (1980). The formalism enables systematic testing of any vertex ansatz used in SDE studies of QED and provides a foundation for extending such gauge-covariance constraints to QCD, where similar truncations are used in hadronic physics.
