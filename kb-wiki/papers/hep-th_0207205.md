# Casimir Energies in Light of Quantum Field Theory

> We study the Casimir problem as the limit of a conventional quantum field theory coupled to a smooth background. The Casimir energy diverges in the limit that the background forces the field to vanish on a surface. We show that this divergence cannot be absorbed into a renormalization of the parameters of the theory. As a result, the Casimir energy of the surface and other quantities like the surface tension, which are obtained by deforming the surface, cannot be defined independently of the details of the coupling between the field and the matter on the surface. In contrast, the energy density away from the surface and the force between rigid surfaces are finite and independent of these complications.

## Key claim

The Casimir energy of a scalar field forced to vanish on a surface in any dimension is infinite and this divergence cannot be absorbed into a renormalization of the parameters of the theory, contrary to the common assumption that boundary conditions can be imposed ab initio without residual divergences.

## Method

The authors embed the Casimir calculation in a conventional quantum field theory by coupling a real scalar field φ to a static background field σ(x) via an interaction Lagrangian ℒ_int = ½ λ σ(x) φ²(x,t). They then take the "Dirichlet limit" where σ(x) becomes a delta-function on a surface S and λ → ∞, forcing all modes of φ to vanish on S. The renormalized Casimir energy is computed using a method that expresses it as a sum over bound states plus an integral over continuum modes with Born-subtracted scattering phase shifts, combined with explicit Feynman diagram contributions and counterterms fixed by renormalization conditions in perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

- In one dimension, the renormalized Casimir energy for two Dirichlet points at x = ±a diverges like λ ln λ as λ → ∞ in the strong coupling limit.
- For the standard boundary-condition calculation in one dimension, the Casimir energy in the massless limit is Ẽ₂(a) = -π/48a, and the force is F̃(a) = -π/96a².
- In two dimensions, the second-order Feynman diagram contribution to the Casimir energy for a Dirichlet circle of radius a diverges like ln Λ, where Λ is the ultraviolet cutoff.
- In three dimensions, the renormalized two-point function for a Dirichlet sphere diverges like Λ ln Λ.

## Context

This work extends the study of divergences in Casimir problems known from earlier work by Candelas (1982) and others, who recognized that the energy of a fluctuating field diverges when boundary conditions are imposed. The authors show that previous proposals to cancel these divergences with ad hoc surface-dependent counterterms (Symanzik, 1981) or quantum boundary functions (Actor, 1990) are not physically justified. The paper demonstrates that while the Casimir force between rigid surfaces and the energy density away from surfaces remain finite and match standard results, quantities requiring surface deformation (like surface tension) are inherently divergent and depend on the physical ultraviolet cutoff provided by the material properties. This enables a clear separation between well-defined observables and those that require detailed modeling of the material boundary.
