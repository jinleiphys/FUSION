# Bell Inequality Violation of Light Quarks in Back-to-Back Dihadron Pair Production at Lepton Colliders

> Spin correlations between particles produced at colliders provide valuable insights for quantum information studies. While traditional studies of quantum information at colliders are typically limited to massive particles with perturbative decay, we propose an innovative method to explore the Bell inequality in massless quark pair systems by analyzing the azimuthal correlations in back-to-back $π^+π^-$ dihadron pair production at lepton colliders. Revisiting the Belle data, we have shown the potential to detect Bell inequality violation of light quarks by introducing an additional angular cut, achieving a significance of 2.5 $σ$ even in the worst-case scenario of 100% correlated systematic uncertainties in each bins. The significance substantially exceeds $5σ$ when considering uncorrelated systematic uncertainties. Our approach opens avenues for exploring spin quantum information in the non-perturbative aspect and leverages existing data for quantum information research.

# Bell Inequality Violation of Light Quarks in Back-to-Back Dihadron Pair Production at Lepton Colliders

## Key claim
The authors propose that Bell inequality violation in massless quark pair systems can be detected by analyzing azimuthal correlations in back-to-back π⁺π⁻ dihadron pair production at lepton colliders, using interference dihadron fragmentation functions as spin analyzers. Revisiting existing Belle data with an additional angular cut on the hard scattering angle, they claim a significance of 2.5σ even in the worst-case scenario of 100% correlated systematic uncertainties, with the caveat that this significance depends on the assumption about systematic uncertainty correlations.

## Method
The authors reconstruct the Bell variable ℬ₋ = C_xx − C_yy from the azimuthal asymmetry A₁₂ = 2⟨cos(φ₁+φ₂)⟩ in back-to-back π⁺π⁻ dihadron pair production, using the JAMDiFF framework to compute the spin analyzing power α from interference dihadron fragmentation functions. They apply a selection cut |cosΘ| < c_max on the hard scattering angle to isolate a highly pure Bell state, then scale the existing Belle data (originally collected with |cosΘ| < 0.67) to estimate expected signals under tighter angular cuts, combining results across 64 bins in (z₁, z₂) phase space weighted by event counts.

## Key numbers
- The SM prediction for the Bell variable in the central scattering region (cosΘ = 0) gives ℬ₋ = 2, corresponding to the maximally entangled Bell state (1/√2)(|↑_y↓_y⟩ + |↓_y↑_y⟩).
- With an angular cut |cosΘ| < 0.1, the purity Tr(ρ̄²) > 0.99, and the expected significance for Bell inequality violation is 2.5σ assuming 100% correlated systematic uncertainties.
- With the same angular cut |cosΘ| < 0.1 and assuming uncorrelated systematic uncertainties, the expected significance reaches 6.2σ.
- The Belle dataset contains approximately 10⁸ events at center-of-mass energy 10.58 GeV.
- The analysis uses 64 data points from Belle, covering (z₁, z₂) bins from 0.275 to 1.0 after excluding bins with z_i < 0.275.

## Context
This work extends the study of quantum information at colliders beyond massive particles with perturbative decays (such as top quark pairs studied by ATLAS (2023) and CMS (2024), and τ lepton pairs, massive gauge boson pairs, and Λ_bΛ̄_b pairs) to massless quark pairs for the first time. The method uses interference dihadron fragmentation functions (diFFs) as spin analyzers, building on the Artru-Collins asymmetry framework (Artru 1995, Boer 2003) and the JAMDiFF global fitting framework (Cocuzza 2023). The approach enables quantum information studies using existing data from Belle (2011) and opens the possibility for the first measurement of Bell inequality violation in a collider scattering process, with potential generalization to transverse momentum-dependent (TMD) frameworks in future work.
