# On the Delta I = 1/2 rule in the Lambda N ----> N N reaction

> It is shown that the mass dependence of the $Λ$-lifetime in heavy hypernuclei is sensitive to the ratio of neutron-induced to proton-induced non-mesonic decay rates R_n/R_p. A comparison of the experimental mass dependence of the lifetimes with the calculated ones for different values of R_n/R_p leads to the conclusion that this ratio is larger than 2 on the confidence level of 0.75. This suggests that the phenomenological $Δ$I=1/2 rule might be violated for the nonmesonic decay of the $Λ$-hyperon.

# On the ΔI = 1/2 rule in the ΛN → NN reaction

## Key claim

The mass dependence of the Λ-lifetime in heavy hypernuclei is sensitive to the ratio of neutron-induced to proton-induced non-mesonic decay rates Rₙ/Rₚ. Comparing experimental lifetimes with calculations for different Rₙ/Rₚ values leads to the conclusion that this ratio is larger than 2 on a confidence level of 0.75, suggesting the phenomenological ΔI = 1/2 rule might be violated for the non-mesonic decay of the Λ-hyperon.

## Method

The authors used a phase-space model within a Coupled-Channel-Boltzmann-Uhling-Uhlenbeck approach to calculate the mesonic and non-mesonic decay widths of Λ-hyperons bound in hypernuclei. The mesonic decay width was computed including Pauli blocking via a Gaussian s-state phase-space density for the Λ and a local Fermi momentum distribution for nucleons. The non-mesonic decay width was calculated using an analogous formalism with separate effective weak decay constants Rₙ and Rₚ for neutron-induced and proton-induced channels, and the total lifetime was obtained as τ_Λ = ħ/(Γ_mesonic + Γ_n + Γ_p). The average weak decay constant R_av = (Rₙ + Rₚ)/2 was fixed by fitting to lifetime data for light hypernuclei (¹¹_ΛB and ¹²_ΛC), and then the mass dependence of the lifetime was computed for several Rₙ/Rₚ ratios.

## Key numbers

- The free Λ-hyperon lifetime is τ_free_Λ = 263 ps.
- The COSY-13 collaboration measured the lifetime for heavy hypernuclei produced in p+Bi interaction as τ_Λ = (161 ± 21) ps.
- For hypernuclei near mass number A = 200, the calculated lifetime is approximately 175 ps for the ratio Rₙ/Rₚ = 2, which is the limiting value compatible with the ΔI = 1/2 rule.
- The ratio Rₙ/Rₚ is found to be larger than 2 on a confidence level of 0.75.
- The pion momentum in mesonic decay is p_π = 102 MeV/c.
- The harmonic oscillator parameter is ħω = 41 A^{-1/3} MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the ΔI = 1/2 rule in weak hyperon-nucleon interactions, which had been tested primarily in light hypernuclei. Previous theoretical models relying on the ΔI = 1/2 rule (Cohen 1990) achieved reasonable agreement with total non-mesonic decay rates but failed to reproduce ratios of partial decay rates. Earlier analyses of light hypernuclei (Schumacher 1992) and proton spectra from ¹²_ΛC decay (Ramos 1997) suggested possible violation of the rule. The present method uses heavy hypernuclei to exploit the sensitivity of the mass dependence of the Λ-lifetime to the Rₙ/Rₚ ratio, enabling a new test. The authors note that planned experiments by the COSY-13 collaboration at Forschungszentrum Jülich and the E369 collaboration at KEK for ⁸⁹_ΛY aim to obtain more precise data to test the ΔI = 1/2 rule on a higher confidence level.
