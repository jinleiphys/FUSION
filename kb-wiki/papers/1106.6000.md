# The $Δ(1232)$ axial charge and form factors from lattice QCD

> We present the first calculation on the $Δ$ axial-vector and pseudoscalar form factors using lattice QCD. Two Goldberger-Treiman relations are derived and examined. A combined chiral fit is performed to the nucleon axial charge, N to $Δ$ axial transition coupling constant and $Δ$ axial charge.

# The Δ(1232) axial charge and form factors from lattice QCD

## Key claim

This paper presents the first lattice QCD calculation of the Δ axial-vector and pseudoscalar form factors, but the results are obtained at pion masses between approximately 300 MeV and 500 MeV, and the chiral extrapolation to the physical pion mass yields a nucleon axial charge that remains lower than the experimental value, indicating that the chiral behavior of these quantities is not yet fully captured.

## Method

The axial-vector and pseudoscalar form factors of the Δ baryon are extracted from three-point correlation functions computed on multiple lattice ensembles, including quenched Wilson fermions, mixed-action configurations (domain-wall valence quarks on asqtad staggered sea), and dynamical domain-wall fermions. The matrix elements are obtained via a ratio of three-point to two-point functions that eliminates unknown field renormalization constants, with sequential inversion through the sink at fixed sink time-slice and Fourier transform over all intermediate times. A global chi-squared minimization over all contributing momentum transfers simultaneously determines the form factors, and a combined chiral fit using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory in the small scale expansion is performed to the nucleon axial charge, the N to Δ axial transition coupling, and the Δ axial charge.

## Key numbers

- Δ axial charge defined as G_ΔΔ = -3 g_1(0), obtained at pion masses ranging from about 300 MeV to 500 MeV
- Quenched ensemble: m_π = 0.563(4) GeV, m_N = 1.267(11) GeV, m_Δ = 1.470(15) GeV on a 32^3 × 64 lattice with a^{-1} = 2.14(6) GeV
- Quenched ensemble: m_π = 0.411(4) GeV, m_N = 1.109(13) GeV, m_Δ = 1.382(19) GeV on a 32^3 × 64 lattice
- Mixed-action ensemble (m_π = 0.353(2) GeV): m_N = 1.191(19) GeV, m_Δ = 1.533(27) GeV on a 28^3 × 64 lattice with a^{-1} = 1.58(3) GeV
- Dynamical domain-wall fermion ensemble: m_π = 0.297(5) GeV, m_N = 1.27(9) GeV, m_Δ = 1.455(17) GeV on a 32^3 × 64 lattice with a^{-1} = 2.34(3) GeV
- Form factors extracted for Q^2 values up to approximately 2 GeV^2 using O(500) lattice measurements per ensemble
- Goldberger-Treiman ratios f_π G_πΔΔ / (m_Δ g_1) and f_π H_πΔΔ / (m_Δ h_1) show agreement with unity for Q^2 greater than about 0.8 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations of the π N Δ coupling (Alexandrou 2007, 2010) and the Δ electromagnetic form factors (Alexandrou 2009) to the axial sector, providing the first non-perturbative determination of the Δ axial charge and the effective π Δ Δ coupling constants G_πΔΔ and H_πΔΔ. The results enable direct comparison with QCD sum rule calculations (Choi 2010) and chiral perturbation theory (Jiang 2008), and the combined chiral fit to the nucleon axial charge, N to Δ transition coupling, and Δ axial charge provides a framework for testing the consistency of chiral expansions across multiple baryon observables. The paper calls for future lattice calculations at pion masses below 300 MeV to resolve the persistent discrepancy between lattice and experimental values of the nucleon axial charge.
