# Thermal Electromagnetic Radiation in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We review the potential of precise measurements of electromagnetic probes in relativistic heavy-ion collisions for the theoretical understanding of strongly interacting matter. The penetrating nature of photons and dileptons implies that they can carry undistorted information about the hot and dense regions of the fireballs formed in these reactions and thus provide a unique opportunity to measure the electromagnetic spectral function of QCD matter as a function of both invariant mass and momentum. In particular we report on recent progress on how the medium modifications of the (dominant) isovector part of the vector current correlator ($ρ$ channel) can shed light on the mechanism of chiral symmetry restoration in the hot and/or dense environment. In addition, thermal dilepton radiation enables novel access to (a) the fireball lifetime through the dilepton yield in the low invariant-mass window $0.3 \; \mathrm{GeV} \leq M \leq 0.7 \; \mathrm{GeV}$, and (b) the early temperatures of the fireball through the slope of the invariant-mass spectrum in the intermediate-mass region ($1.5 \; \mathrm{GeV} <M< 2.5 \; \mathrm{GeV}$). The investigation of the pertinent excitation function suggests that the beam energies provided by the NICA and FAIR projects are in a promising range for a potential discovery of the onset of a first order phase transition, as signaled by a non-monotonous behavior of both low-mass yields and temperature slopes.

## Key claim

The paper argues that thermal dilepton radiation can serve as a quantitative probe of fundamental fireball properties, specifically the total fireball lifetime (via low-mass yields) and early fireball temperatures (via intermediate-mass slopes), and that the beam energies of NICA and FAIR are promising for discovering the onset of a first-order phase transition through a non-monotonous behavior of these observables.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical framework for electromagnetic emission rates in heavy-ion collisions, expressed in terms of the in-medium spectral function of the electromagnetic current-current correlation function. They analyze dilepton invariant-mass spectra calculated using hadronic many-body theory for the in-medium rho meson spectral function, implemented into either a thermal fireball model with a lattice-QCD based equation of state or a coarse-grained UrQMD transport approach, and compare these to experimental data from STAR, NA60, and HADES.

## Key numbers

- The low invariant-mass window for fireball lifetime extraction is defined as 0.3 GeV ≤ M ≤ 0.7 GeV.
- The intermediate-mass region for temperature extraction is defined as 1.5 GeV < M < 2.5 GeV.
- The pseudo-critical chiral transition temperature is T_pc^chi ≈ 155 MeV.
- The electromagnetic coupling constant is α_em ≈ 1/137.
- The quark condensate in vacuum is approximately 2 fm^{-3} per quark flavor.
- The a_1 mass shift at finite temperature is up to about 200 MeV, with a 15-20% reduction of the pion decay constant and scalar condensate.
- The pseudo-critical temperature for the phase transition used in the fireball model is T_pc ≈ 170 MeV.
- The CERN-SPS collision energy is sqrt(s) = 17.3 GeV.
- The promising energy regime for excitation function studies is sqrt(s) ≈ 2-8 GeV.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on electromagnetic probes in heavy-ion collisions that began with Shuryak (1980) and Rapp (2011), and builds on the hadronic many-body calculations of Rapp (1999) that successfully described NA60 data from Specht (2010) and CERES data from Adamova (2002) and Agakichiev (2005). The paper enables future experimental programs at NICA and FAIR to search for a first-order phase transition by measuring the excitation function of dilepton yields and slopes, and it connects the observed rho meson melting to chiral symmetry restoration through sum rule analyses by Hohler (2013) and lattice-QCD calculations by Aarts (2015).
