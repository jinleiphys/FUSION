# Application of the Lagrange mesh method in continuum-discretized coupled-channels calculations

> We apply the Lagrange-mesh method to discretize continuum states of weakly bound nuclei for continuum-discretized coupled-channel (CDCC) calculations of three-body breakup reactions. This discretization method is compared with the bin method, which is regarded as the standard continuum discretization method, for the $d$ and $^6$Li induced reactions. An improved Numerov algorithm is used to solve the coupled channels equations, which permits a fast integration of equations and a convenient treatment of the closed channels. A new CDCC model code named \emph{CDCC-R} is developed. In all cases, the combination of the Lagrange-mesh method and the Numerov algorithm shows high efficiency and accuracy for the CDCC calculations of the elastic scattering and breakup reactions. Especially, various numerical and physical aspects are discussed for $^6$Li induced reactions. The coupling effects of the continuum states with high orbital angular momentum and closed channels are discussed. Moderate effects are found in the calculations for elastic scattering and breakup reaction when $F$- and $G$-wave continuum states are included in CDCC model space for $^6$Li induced reactions at the incident energies well above the Coulomb barrier. The closed channel effect is found to be indispensable for $^6$Li breakup reaction calculation when the incident energy is around the Coulomb barrier.

## Key claim

The Lagrange-mesh method combined with an improved Numerov algorithm provides a highly efficient and accurate alternative to the standard bin method for continuum-discretized coupled-channel (CDCC) calculations of elastic scattering and breakup reactions, with the caveat that the closed channel effect is indispensable for 6Li breakup calculations when the incident energy is around the Coulomb barrier, and moderate effects are found when F- and G-wave continuum states are included at energies well above the Coulomb barrier.

## Method

The authors applied the regularized Lagrange-Laguerre mesh method (RLLM) to discretize the continuum states of weakly bound nuclei (deuteron and 6Li) for CDCC calculations of three-body breakup reactions, using Gauss-quadrature approximation to compute coupling matrix elements efficiently. They solved the coupled channel equations with an improved Numerov algorithm that permits fast integration and convenient treatment of closed channels, and developed a new CDCC model code named CDCC-R. The RLLM results were compared with the standard bin method for d+58Ni at 80.0 MeV and for 6Li+12C and 6Li+59Co reactions at various incident energies.

## Key numbers

- For d+58Ni at EL=80.0 MeV elastic scattering: CDCC-RLLM used N=30 and h=0.4 fm, with 15 discretized states for (l=0), 13 for (l=2), and 12 for (l=4); CDCC-Bin used Δk=0.04 fm−1 and rbin=40 fm, with 31, 30, and 30 states respectively.
- For d+58Ni breakup at EL=80.0 MeV: CDCC-RLLM used N=70 and h=0.4 fm, with 165 discretized states; CDCC-Bin used Δk=0.03 fm−1 and rbin=100 fm, with 201 discretized states.
- For 6Li: the α-d interaction generated a ground state binding energy of -1.47 MeV; resonance energies were calculated as 0.710 MeV (3+ state), 3.00 MeV (2+ state), and 4.24 MeV (1+ state), compared with experimental values of 0.716, 2.84, and 4.18 MeV respectively.
- For 6Li+12C at EL=168.6 MeV: convergence of elastic S-matrix was achieved with k_max=2.2 fm−1, N=20, R_m=18 fm, and l_max=4; the S-matrix for Jπ=27− was 626.2+254.1i (multiplied by 1000) under these conditions.

## Context

This work extends the application of the Lagrange-mesh method in CDCC, previously introduced by Druet et al. (2010) for d+58Ni and by Druet and Descouvemont (2012) for 11Be+64Zn, by replacing the R-matrix approach with an improved Numerov algorithm that is much faster for large channel counts. The new CDCC-R code enables efficient treatment of heavy weakly bound nuclei where channel numbers typically exceed 100, and the authors demonstrate its validity for 6Li induced reactions including resonance states, discussing the coupling effects of high orbital angular momentum continuum states and closed channels that were not addressed in prior work.
