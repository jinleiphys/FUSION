# Neutrino Emissivity of Non-equilibrium beta processes With Nucleon Superfluidity

> We investigate the influence of nucleon superfluidity on the neutrino emissivity of non-equilibrium beta processes. Calculations are performed of the reduction factors for direct and modified Urca processes with three types of nucleon superfluidity in $npe$ matter. The numerical results are given since the analytical solution is impossible. We find that the behavior of the superfluid influence is closely related to the chemical departure from beta equilibrium. For small chemical departure, the superfluid reduction factor almost only depends on the gap but is hardly affected by the departure. While for the departure large enough, it rapidly enhances the neutrino emissivity. The onset of the "enchanced" emission has some corresponding thresholds which seem to be linked to the ratio of the energy gap to the chemical departure.

## Key claim

The influence of nucleon superfluidity on neutrino emissivity in non-equilibrium beta processes is closely tied to the chemical departure from beta equilibrium: for small chemical departure the reduction factor depends almost only on the gap and is hardly affected by the departure, while for large enough departure it rapidly enhances the neutrino emissivity, with thresholds linked to the ratio of the energy gap to the chemical departure.

## Method

The authors numerically computed reduction factors for the neutrino emissivity of direct and modified Urca processes in npe matter, considering three types of nucleon superfluidity (isotropic 1S0, anisotropic 3P2 with mJ=0, and anisotropic 3P2 with |mJ|=2). They assumed only one type of nucleon is superfluid at a time and used the methods of Villain and Haensel (2005) to evaluate the phase-space integrals, which cannot be solved analytically. The reduction factor R is defined as the ratio of the neutrino emissivity with superfluidity to that without superfluidity, as a function of the dimensionless chemical departure xi = delta-mu/T and the dimensionless gap amplitude v = Delta(T)/T.

## Key numbers

* For the direct Urca process with isotropic superfluidity, when log10(gap/T) is less than or equal to 1.0, the threshold chemical departure satisfies log10(delta-mu_th/T) = constant (approximately 0.78).
* For the direct Urca process with isotropic superfluidity, when log10(gap/T) is greater than 1.0, the threshold satisfies delta-mu_th approximately equals gap.
* The critical temperature to gap ratio for type A (1S0) superfluidity is T_c/Delta(0) = 0.5669; for type B (3P2, mJ=0) it is 0.8416; for type C (3P2, |mJ|=2) it is 0.4926.
* The direct Urca process is allowed in pure npe matter when the proton fraction n_p/n exceeds 1/9, which occurs at densities several times the nuclear saturation density rho_0 = 2.8 x 10^14 g cm^-3.
* The equilibrium neutrino emissivity of the direct Urca process without superfluidity contains the factor I_0^(D) = 457 pi^6 / 5040.
* The equilibrium neutrino emissivity of the modified Urca process without superfluidity contains the factor I_0^(MN) = 11513 pi^8 / 60480.

## Context

This work extends the calculations of Villain and Haensel (2005), who computed the net reaction rates for non-equilibrium beta processes with nucleon superfluidity, by instead computing the neutrino emissivities. It also improves upon the crude step-function model of Reisenegger (1997), who assumed complete suppression for delta-mu < Delta_p + Delta_n and no suppression otherwise; the present results show that the reduction factor is neither 0 nor 1 in the relevant parameter range, approaching the Reisenegger result only in the limit of infinite gap. The results are needed for numerical simulations of neutron star cooling, where neutrino emissivity plays a key role, and will be combined with the reaction rates from Villain and Haensel (2005) for such simulations.
