# Constituent quark model for baryons with strong quark-pair correlations and non-leptonic weak transitions of hyperon

> We study the roles of quark-pair correlations for baryon properties, in particular on non-leptonic weak decay of hyperons. We construct the quark wave function of baryons by solving the three body problem explicitly with confinement force and the short range attraction for a pair of quarks with their total spin being 0. We show that the existence of the strong quark-quark correlations enhances the non-leptonic transition amplitudes which is consistent with the data, while the baryon masses and radii are kept to the experiment.

# Constituent quark model for baryons with strong quark-pair correlations and non-leptonic weak transitions of hyperon

## Key claim

The authors show that introducing a strong attractive correlation for spin-0 quark pairs in a non-relativistic constituent quark model enhances the non-leptonic weak transition amplitudes of hyperons to values consistent with experimental data, while simultaneously reproducing baryon masses and the proton core radius. The hedge is that the agreement is good for Sigma decays but only about half the experimental value for the Lambda to n pi0 decay, which the authors state is still under improvement.

## Method

The authors solve the non-relativistic three-body problem for baryons using a Hamiltonian with a harmonic confinement force and a Gaussian short-range attraction acting only on quark pairs with total spin 0. They use the coupled-rearrangement-channel variational method with infinitesimally-shifted-Gaussian-Lobe basis functions to obtain fully antisymmetrized quark wave functions, breaking SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry through the spin-dependent correlation. The weak transition amplitudes are then computed within the baryon pole approximation using the effective weak Hamiltonian with dominant operators O1 and O2.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark masses: m_u = m_d = 330 MeV, m_s = 500 MeV
- Model parameters: K = 0.005 GeV^3, beta = 0.5 fm, C_SS / m_u^2 = 1.4 GeV
- Valence quark core radius: <r^2>_core = (0.6 fm)^2, obtained by subtracting vector meson dominance contribution 6/m_rho^2 from experimental proton electric radius (0.86 fm)^2
- Average Jacobi coordinates in nucleon for spin-0 quark pair: r = 0.92 fm, R = 0.97 fm; for spin-1 pair: r = 1.1 fm, R = 0.81 fm
- Weak matrix element <n | H_W | Lambda> = -0.546 with spin-dependent force, compared to -0.179 without
- Weak matrix element <p | H_W | Sigma+> = 1.65 with spin-dependent force, compared to 0.440 without
- P-wave decay amplitude for Sigma to p pi0: total 28.05 (pole 26.0, others 2.05), experiment 26.24 +/- 1.32
- P-wave decay amplitude for Sigma+ to n pi+: total 43.3 (pole 43.4, others 0.0), experiment 41.83 +/- 0.17
- P-wave decay amplitude for Lambda to n pi0: total -7.32 (pole -2.30, others -5.02), experiment -15.61 +/- 1.40

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model approach to baryons, specifically addressing the long-standing problem that standard quark models (such as the Isgur-Karl harmonic oscillator model and the MIT bag model) give weak transition amplitudes only about half the experimental data. The authors are motivated by non-perturbative QCD studies such as the instanton liquid model, which suggests strong attractive correlations for spin-0 quark pairs. The results enable a more realistic treatment of non-leptonic hyperon decays and the Delta I = 1/2 rule, though the Lambda decay discrepancy indicates further work is needed, possibly including relativistic effects.
