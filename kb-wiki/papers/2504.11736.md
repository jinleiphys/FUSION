# Low-energy neutrino responses for 71Ga by electron capture rates, charge exchange reactions and shell model calculations

> Weak Gamow-Teller (GT) responses for low-lying states in ${}^{71}\mathrm{Ga}$ are crucial for studying low-energy solar neutrinos and the Ga anomaly, i.e., the possible transition to the sterile state. The responses for the ground state, the first excited state, and the second excited state are evaluated for the first time using the experimental electron capture rates, the experimental charge exchange reaction (CER) rates corrected for the tensor-interaction effect and the theoretical interacting shell model (ISM) calculations. The contributions from the two excited states to the solar and ${}^{51}\mathrm{Cr}$ neutrinos are found to be $4.2 \pm 1.2\%$ of that for the ground state. This is slightly larger than the ISM values but little smaller than the CER values without corrections for the tensor interaction effect. The Ga anomaly is far beyond the uncertainty of the obtained nuclear responses.

# Low-energy neutrino responses for 71Ga by electron capture rates, charge exchange reactions and shell model calculations

## Key claim
The contributions from the first and second excited states in 71Ga to solar and 51Cr neutrino capture rates are 4.2 ± 1.2% of the ground-state contribution, which is slightly larger than interacting shell model values but slightly smaller than charge exchange reaction values without tensor-interaction corrections. The gallium anomaly is far beyond the uncertainty of the obtained nuclear responses, meaning nuclear cross-section uncertainties cannot explain the observed neutrino deficit.

## Method
The authors evaluated Gamow-Teller (GT) neutrino responses for the ground state (1/2⁻), first excited state at 175 keV (5/2⁻), and second excited state at 500 keV (3/2⁻) in 71Ge populated by neutrino capture on 71Ga. They combined experimental electron capture rates, high energy-resolution (³He,t) charge exchange reaction data at 0.42 GeV from RCNP Osaka University, and interacting shell model calculations using the JUN45 interaction in the fpg-shell model space, correcting the CER data for tensor-interaction contributions using a mixing coefficient ε = 0.26 ± 0.06 derived from the l-forbidden transition in 69Ga.

## Key numbers
- Ground-state GT nuclear matrix element M₀(GT) = 0.603 ± 0.02, consistent with the electron capture value of 0.585
- First excited state GT NME M₁(GT) = 0.01 ± 0.05, indicating a predominantly l-forbidden transition
- Second excited state GT NME M₂(GT) = 0.255 ± 0.03
- Axial-vector quenching coefficient k = g_A^eff/g_A = 0.74, derived from the ratio of experimental EC NME (0.585) to ISM NME (0.791)
- Tensor mixing coefficient ε = 0.26 ± 0.06, obtained from the 69Ga 3/2 → 5/2 transition
- Total solar neutrino rate enhancement factor K = 1.042 ± 0.012 after tensor correction (CER/ISM method)
- Total 51Cr neutrino rate enhancement factor K = 1.042 ± 0.012
- Combined contribution from the two excited states: 4.2 ± 1.2% of the ground-state contribution

## Context
This work extends previous analyses of 71Ga neutrino responses by Bahcall (1997) using (p,n) CER data and by Frekers (2011) using (³He,t) CER data, which did not correct for tensor-interaction contributions. The authors also compare with interacting shell model calculations by Haxton (1998) and Kostensalo (2019). The corrected CER/ISM result (K = 1.042) lies between the uncorrected CER value (K = 1.073) and the ISM value (K = 1.025), demonstrating that tensor corrections are essential for accurate neutrino response extraction. This enables more precise interpretation of the gallium anomaly observed in the BEST experiment, ruling out nuclear cross-section uncertainties as the cause.
