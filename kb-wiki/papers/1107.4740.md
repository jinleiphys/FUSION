# Baryon production and net-proton distributions in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> The higher order moments of the net-baryon distributions in relativistic heavy ion collisions are useful probes for the QCD critical point and fluctuations. We study the net-proton distributions and their moments in a simple model which considers the baryon stopping and pair production effects in the processes. It is shown that a single emission source model can explain the experimental data well. Centrality and energy dependence of the distributions and higher moments is discussed.

## Key claim

A single emission source model that only includes baryon stopping and independent baryon-antibaryon pair production can reproduce the experimentally observed net-proton distributions and their higher order moments in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV, without invoking thermal equilibrium or critical fluctuations. The authors explicitly state that their model "has nothing to do with thermal equilibrium and/or critical fluctuations" and is intended as a baseline for detecting novel physics.

## Method

The authors construct a model where the number of stopped baryons in the kinematic region follows a Poisson distribution with mean \(B\), and the number of produced baryon-antibaryon pairs follows an independent Poisson distribution with mean \(\mu\). Isospin conservation is enforced for the produced pairs, and the net-proton distribution is derived analytically as a function of two combined parameters: \(B\rho\) (the mean net-proton number, with \(\rho\) the proton fraction in the nucleus) and \(\mu\). The model is fitted to STAR net-proton distribution data at three centralities (0-5%, 30-40%, 70-80%) for Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV, and the parameters are then parameterized as polynomials of the number of participant nucleons \(N_{\rm part}\) to predict moments at other centralities and at LHC energies.

## Key numbers

* For 0-5% centrality Au+Au at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV: fitted parameters are \(\mu = 14.5\) and \(B\rho = 1.65\), with \(N_{\rm part} = 351.4\).
* For 30-40% centrality: fitted parameters are \(\mu = 5.5\) and \(B\rho = 0.632\), with \(N_{\rm part} = 114.2\).
* For 70-80% centrality: fitted parameters are \(\mu = 0.83\) and \(B\rho = 0.075\), with \(N_{\rm part} = 13.4\).
* The parameterization of \(\mu\) as a function of \(N_{\rm part}\) is: \(\mu = 0.171 + 0.0495 N_p - 2.5 \times 10^{-5} N_p^2\).
* The parameterization of \(B\rho\) as a function of \(N_{\rm part}\) is: \(B\rho = (-4.63 + 5.99 N_p - 3.7 \times 10^{-3}) / 1000\).
* The product \(\kappa\sigma^2\) is predicted to be almost exactly 1 across the centrality range, as expected from the hadron resonance gas model.
* For LHC Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV, the model predicts \(B\rho \simeq 0\) and a much smaller \(\mu\) value, leading to a symmetric net-proton distribution with zero skewness.

## Context

This work extends the study of net-proton fluctuations in heavy ion collisions by providing a simple analytical model that reproduces STAR data (2009) without the complexity of full event generators like AMPT, UrQMD (referred to as THEO3), or HIJING (Luo). The model contrasts with approaches that assume independent Poisson distributions for protons and antiprotons (Chen) or canonical ensemble treatments (Begun), and it provides a baseline prediction for LHC/ALICE measurements. The authors note that the product \(\kappa\sigma^2 \approx 1\) matches the hadron resonance gas model expectation (HRG), and that the model can be used to distinguish critical point signals from normal physics effects.
