# Antiproton-nucleus reactions at intermediate energies

> Antiproton-induced reactions on nuclei at the beam energies from hundreds MeV up to several GeV provide an excellent opportunity to study interactions of the antiproton and secondary particles (mesons, baryons and antibaryons) with nucleons. Antiproton projectile is unique in the sense that most of annihilation particles are relatively slow in the target nucleus frame. Hence, the prehadronic effects do not much influence their interactions with the nucleons of the nuclear residue. Moreover, the particles with momenta less than about 1 GeV/c are sensitive to the nuclear mean field potentials. This paper discusses the microscopic transport calculations of the antiproton-nucleus reactions and is focused on three related problems: (i) the antiproton potential determination, (ii) possible formation of strongly bound antiproton-nucleus systems, and (iii) strangeness production.

## Key claim

The antiproton-nucleus optical potential is not a simple superposition of vacuum antiproton-nucleon interactions; reproducing experimental data on absorption cross sections and particle production requires a strongly attractive potential with Re V_opt around -(150-200) MeV at normal nuclear density, though data at higher momenta (1.6-20 GeV/c) favor a much deeper potential of about -660 MeV, indicating a discrepancy that needs clarification.

## Method

The author performed microscopic transport calculations using the Giessen Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) model, which solves a coupled set of kinetic equations for baryons, antibaryons, and mesons including mean field potentials via a relativistic mean field (RMF) approach. The model incorporates elastic and inelastic binary collisions, resonance decays, and uses the NL3 parameterization for nucleon-meson coupling constants with a scaling factor xi applied to antinucleon-meson couplings. Calculations were compared with experimental data on antiproton absorption cross sections, pion and proton momentum spectra, and strangeness production yields across beam momenta from 0.1 to 15 GeV/c.

## Key numbers

- Best fit to KEK data at p_lab = 470-880 MeV/c gives scaling factor xi = 0.21 +/- 0.03, producing Re V_opt = -(150 +/- 30) MeV at rho = rho_0 = 0.16 fm^-3
- Im V_opt at rho = rho_0 is -(100-110) MeV, independent of the choice of xi
- The G-parity transformed potential (xi = 1) gives Re V_opt = -660 MeV at rho = rho_0
- Compressed nuclear configurations reach nucleon density rho ~ 2 rho_0 within t ~ 10 fm/c for antiproton implanted in 40Ca center
- Annihilation event spectrum on total invariant mass M_inv shifts downward by about 200 MeV from 2m_N for stopped antiproton, with further shift of about 100 MeV for compressed configurations
- For 16O target, the spectrum shift is nearly 500 MeV
- In 4 GeV/c antiproton on 181Ta, 72% of hyperon and hyperon resonance production comes from antikaon absorption processes, 23% from nonstrange meson-baryon collisions, 3% from antibaryon-baryon collisions, and 2% from baryon-baryon collisions
- Threshold beam momentum for the process anti-K N -> Xi K is 1.05 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends prior transport model calculations of antiproton-nucleus reactions by Ilinov (1982), Cugnon (1986, 1990), Strottman (1985), and Gibbs (1990), and is motivated by the upcoming PANDA experiment at FAIR. The paper addresses the long-standing discrepancy between the repulsive antiproton-nucleus potential predicted by the low-density theorem (Batty, 1997) and the strongly attractive potential favored by antiprotonic atom X-ray and radiochemical data analysis (Friedman, 2005). The results enable further study of compressed nuclear configurations and provide a test for distinguishing hadronic mechanisms from quark-gluon plasma formation in strangeness production, particularly through the forward rapidity shift of Xi hyperons relative to Lambda hyperons.
