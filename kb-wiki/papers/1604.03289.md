# Meson cloud effects in nucleon resonances at low and intermediate energies

> We review some results obtained by the Coimbra-Ljubljana collaboration that reveal the important role of the pion cloud in the low- and intermediate-energy nucleon and delta resonances.

# Meson cloud effects in nucleon resonances at low and intermediate energies

## Key claim
The paper reviews results from the Coimbra-Ljubljana collaboration showing that the pion cloud plays an important role in nucleon and delta resonances at low and intermediate energies, but the authors caution that studying meson scattering alone cannot reliably determine the nature of a resonance because coupling constants and interaction ranges can be adjusted to reproduce elastic and inelastic amplitudes in almost any model; instead, meson electro-production processes, particularly the Q²-dependence of helicity amplitudes, provide a much more severe test of resonance structure.

## Method
The authors combined quark core excitations with meson clouds using chiral quark models including the linear sigma-model with quarks, the chromodielectric model, and the cloudy bag model. For the Delta(1232) they used Peierls-Yoccoz projection to maintain correct spin and isospin of the composite state. For higher resonances they developed a multi-channel K-matrix approach including many-body quasi-bound quark states in the scattering formalism, computing the scattering T-matrix by solving the Heitler equation T = K + iTK, and calculating meson electro-production amplitudes by including the gamma-N channel.

## Key numbers
- For the Delta(1232) resonance, the helicity amplitude A_{1/2} is shown in units of 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2} (Figure 1, left panel)
- For the Roper resonance, the bag radius used was 0.83 fm as the standard choice, and using 1 fm instead reproduced the popular value of 0.5 (GeV/c)^2 for the zero crossing point of the helicity amplitude
- The multi-channel approach for the Roper resonance reproduced experimental scattering amplitudes up to W ~ 1600 MeV using three channels (pi-Delta, sigma-N, and the bare quark state)
- For the S11 partial wave, the mixing angle between the two 70-plet states was taken as theta ~ 30 degrees in a fixed-angle calculation
- The pion cloud contribution to helicity amplitudes for negative parity resonances is generally weak, of the order of ~10%, except for the Delta(1700) 3/2- resonance where quark core and pion cloud contribute almost equally

## Context
This work extends the original idea of Joao da Providencia and Joao Urbano (1978) that nucleon resonances arise as excitations of the pion cloud around the bare nucleon, and combines it with the quark model approach where resonances emerge as excitations of the three-quark core. The Coimbra-Ljubljana collaboration (including J. da Providencia, M. Fiolhais, P. Alberto, L. Amoreira, M. Rosina, S. Sirca, M. Cibej, and B. Golli) developed this combined framework over several decades. The results for the Delta(1232) quadrupole amplitudes (E2 and C2) were the first calculations of their Q² behavior and have since been rediscovered in different approaches and supported by new more precise measurements. The approach enables future studies of resonance structure through electro-production rather than relying solely on scattering data.
