# Quantum Münchhausen effect in tunneling

## Key claim

Radiative corrections from virtual photon exchange increase the tunneling probability of a charged particle, contrary to the intuitive expectation that radiation should cause energy losses and suppress tunneling. The effect arises from the real part of the self-energy operator, which produces a negative local correction to the barrier potential, while the imaginary part (real photon emission) is exponentially small and does not significantly influence the tunneling amplitude.

## Method

The authors solve the Schrödinger equation with a complex, nonlocal self-energy operator Σ that describes the coupling of the tunneling particle to virtual photons in the one-photon approximation. They separate the self-energy into a hermitian part M (responsible for the Lamb shift and the tunneling enhancement) and an antihermitian part Γ (describing real photon emission), then evaluate M in the logarithmic approximation using closure relations and a semiclassical WKB Green function for the radial motion under the barrier. The nonlocal kernel is shown to be sharply peaked and reducible to a local correction δU(r) proportional to the Laplacian of the barrier potential.

## Key numbers

- The local correction to the barrier potential is δU(r) = (Z²α / 3π m²) × ln(m / |U_p(r) − E|) × ∇²U(r), where α is the fine structure constant, Z is the charge number, m is the particle mass, and U_p(r) is the effective p-wave radial potential including the centrifugal term.
- For alpha-decay, the numerical value of the correction to the potential is small, approximately 1 keV.
- The logarithmic factor in the local correction is L(r) = ln(m / |U_p(r) − E|), with the lower cut-off set by the barrier height U₀ and the upper cut-off by the particle mass m (or by the inverse size 1/r₀ for an extended object).
- The width of the nonlocal kernel in the semiclassical approximation is |r − r′| ∼ 1/p_max, where p_max = (2β)^(1/2) m and β < 1 is a parameter separating low-frequency and high-frequency regions.

## Context

This work extends the study of environmental effects on tunneling initiated by Caldeira and Leggett, who concluded that friction-type interactions suppress tunneling, and by Esbensen, who showed that zero-point vibrations of the barrier source can enhance subbarrier nuclear reactions. The mechanism is related to phonon-assisted tunneling but does not require any special device, being always provided by the interaction of a charged particle with the radiation field. The results are relevant to alpha-decay, subbarrier nuclear fission and fusion (as reviewed by Balantekin and collaborators), and may influence electron tunneling through quantum dots or insulating surfaces; the authors also note that in theories like QCD the radiative corrections are not small.
