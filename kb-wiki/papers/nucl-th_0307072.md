# Pion-nucleon scattering in a meson-exchange model

> The pi-N interaction is studied within a meson-exchange model and in a coupled-channels approach which includes the channels pi-N, eta-N, as well as three effective pi-pi-N channels namely rho-N, pi-Delta, and sigma-N. Starting out from an earlier model of the Julich group systematic improvements in the dynamics and in some technical aspects are introduced. With the new model an excellent quantitative reproduction of the pi-N phase shifts and inelasticity parameters in the energy region up to 1.9 GeV and for total angular momenta J leq 3/2 is achieved. Simultaneously, good agreement with data for the total and differential pi-N -> eta-N transition cross sections is obtained. The connection of the pi_N dynamics in the S_{11} partial wave with the reaction pi-N -> eta-N is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a refined meson-exchange model for pion-nucleon scattering that achieves an excellent quantitative reproduction of phase shifts and inelasticity parameters up to 1.9 GeV for total angular momenta J ≤ 3/2, but the abstract's claim of "excellent quantitative reproduction" is hedged by the fact that the model still shows deficiencies in some partial waves (P13, S31, D33) and the inelasticity in P13 and D13 partial waves shows an incorrect trend at higher energies, potentially requiring the inclusion of the omega-N channel.

## Method

The authors solve a coupled-channel scattering equation in time-ordered perturbation theory, including the channels pi-N, eta-N, and three effective pi-pi-N channels (rho-N, pi-Delta, and sigma-N). The interaction potential is constructed from an effective Lagrangian based on Wess-Zumino theory, supplemented with terms for the Delta isobar, omega, eta, a0 meson, and sigma, as well as coupling terms for the N*(1535), N*(1520), and N*(1650) resonances. Key improvements over the earlier Julich model include derivative coupling for S11 N* resonances, coupling of the N*(1535) to the pi-Delta channel, and allowing a finite subtraction constant in the dispersion relations for correlated pi-pi exchange in the sigma channel.

## Key numbers

- The model reproduces pi-N phase shifts and inelasticity parameters up to 1.9 GeV for total angular momenta J ≤ 3/2
- The bare nucleon mass is set to M0 = 1239 MeV, with bare pi-N coupling constant (f^B_NNpi)^2/(4pi) = 0.0166 and cutoff Lambda = 1950 MeV
- The sigma meson mass in the sigma-N to sigma-N potential is taken as m_sigma = 650 MeV, extracted from a Breit-Wigner parameterization of correlated pi-pi exchange
- Cutoff masses for resonance diagrams are set to 2 GeV
- The total pi- p to eta n cross section is below 3 mb at its maximum, while the inelasticity from the S11 partial wave alone would give sigma_in ~ 3.5 mb at the maximum using the inelasticity from phase shift analysis
- The old Julich model overestimated the total pi- p to eta n cross section by about 20-30% around the maximum

## Context

This work extends the earlier Julich pi-N model by Krehl et al. (2000), which provided good qualitative and in many partial waves quantitative description of pi-N scattering from threshold up to 1.9 GeV, but suffered from several deficiencies including a long tail of the S11(1650) resonance affecting low-energy phase shifts and an unsatisfactory description of the S11 inelasticity and pi-N to eta-N transition cross section. The new model enables a consistent description of pi-N elastic scattering and pi-N to eta-N transition data, which is needed for investigating eta production in nucleon-nucleon collisions and for studies of associated strangeness production reactions like NN to N Lambda K and NN to N Sigma K.
