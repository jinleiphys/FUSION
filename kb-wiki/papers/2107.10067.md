## Context

This work extends previous studies of meson properties in the NJL model under magnetic fields (Mao 2011, Mao 2012, Wang 2019, Yu 2020) by systematically mapping the behavior of all light mesons (œÉ, œÄ‚Å∞, œÄ¬±) across the full T-ŒºB-ŒºI-eB parameter space, with particular attention to the critical end point region. The results enable future experimental searches for the QCD critical end point in relativistic heavy ion collisions, where the predicted mass jumps of pions may produce observable enhancements in pion production. The paper also addresses the open question of how to properly define the chiral restoration phase boundary when explicit chiral symmetry breaking is present, as the order-parameter-based and meson-based definitions yield different phase boundaries.
