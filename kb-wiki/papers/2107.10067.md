# Light mesons around critical end points in $T-μ_B-μ_I-eB$ space

> Light mesons $(σ, π^0, π^\pm)$ are investigated in $T-μ_B-μ_I-eB$ space by using a two-flavor NJL model, which are related to the chiral symmetry restoration and pion superfluid phase transition. In $T-μ_B-eB$ space, during the chiral restoration process, the mass of pseudo-Goldstone mode $π^0$ keeps increasing, together with the sudden mass jump. At the critical end point region, $π^0$ meson has a very sharp but continuous mass increase, together with a sudden mass jump at the Mott transition, and in the first order chiral phase transition region nearby, we observe twice $π^0$ mass jumps, induced by the Mott transition and quark mass jump, respectively. The mass of Higgs mode $σ$ first decreases and then increases associated with the chiral symmetry restoration, and shows a jump at the first order chiral phase transition. We plot the chiral phase diagram in terms of the change of quark mass, the Mott transition of $π^0$ and the minimum mass of $σ$. Due to the explicit breaking of chiral symmetry in physical case, the chiral restoration phase boundaries in $T-μ_B$ plane from the order parameter and meson side are different from each other. In $T-μ_I$ plane, the competition between pion superfluid phase transition and chiral symmetry restoration under magnetic fields is studied in terms of the Goldstone mode $π^+$ and the pseudo-Goldstone mode $π^0$. The separation of the two phase boundaries is enhanced by the external magnetic field. Different from the twice mass jumps of $π^0$ in the first order chiral phase transition region, the $π^+$ meson displays several mass jumps in the chiral crossover region. At the critical end point, $π^+$ also shows very sharp but continuous mass changes, together with a mass jump at the Mott transition.

## Key claim

The paper investigates light meson properties (σ, π⁰, π±) in the T-μB-μI-eB parameter space using a two-flavor NJL model, finding that the chiral restoration phase boundaries defined by the order parameter (quark mass) and by meson properties (π⁰ Mott transition, σ minimum mass) do not coincide in the physical case with explicit chiral symmetry breaking, unlike in the chiral limit.

## Method

The authors employ a two-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model at the mean-field level for quarks, with mesons constructed as quantum fluctuations via quark bubble summation in the random phase approximation (RPA). The covariant Pauli-Villars regularization scheme is used to handle ultraviolet divergences while preserving causality under external magnetic fields, with the Ritus scheme employed for charged meson propagators in magnetic fields. The model parameters (current quark mass m₀=5 MeV, coupling constant G=3.44 GeV⁻², cutoff Λ=1.127 GeV) are fixed by fitting vacuum properties: quark condensate ⟨ψ̄ψ⟩ = -2 × (250 MeV)³, pion mass mπ=134 MeV, and pion decay constant fπ=93 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of meson properties in the NJL model under magnetic fields (Mao 2011, Mao 2012, Wang 2019, Yu 2020) by systematically mapping the behavior of all light mesons (σ, π⁰, π±) across the full T-μB-μI-eB parameter space, with particular attention to the critical end point region. The results enable future experimental searches for the QCD critical end point in relativistic heavy ion collisions, where the predicted mass jumps of pions may produce observable enhancements in pion production. The paper also addresses the open question of how to properly define the chiral restoration phase boundary when explicit chiral symmetry breaking is present, as the order-parameter-based and meson-based definitions yield different phase boundaries.
