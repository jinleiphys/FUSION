# Predictions for production of $\rm{^3_ΛH}$ and $\rm{{^3_{\overline Λ}\overline H}}$ in isobaric $^{96}_{44}$Ru+$^{96}_{44}$Ru and $^{96}_{40}$Zr+$^{96}_{40}$Zr collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}}$ = 200 GeV

> The production of $\rm{^3_ΛH}$ and $\rm{{^3_{\overline Λ}\overline H}}$, as well as $\rm{^3H}$, $\rm{^3\overline H}$, $\rm{^3He}$, and $\rm{^3\overline {He}}$ are studied in central collisions of isobars $^{96}_{44}$Ru+$^{96}_{44}$Ru and $^{96}_{40}$Zr+$^{96}_{40}$Zr at $\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}}=200$ GeV, using the dynamically constrained phase-space coalescence model and the {\footnotesize PACIAE} model with chiral magnetic effect. The yield, yield ratio, coalescence parameters, and strangeness population factor of (anti-)hypertriton and (anti-)nuclei produced in isobaric $^{96}_{44}$Ru+$^{96}_{44}$Ru and $^{96}_{40}$Zr+$^{96}_{40}$Zr collisions are predicted. The (anti-)hypertriton and (anti-)nuclei production is found to be insensitive to the chiral magnetic effects. Experimental data of Cu+Cu, Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions from RHIC, LHC, and the results of {\footnotesize PACIAE+DCPC} model are presented in the results for comparison.

## Key claim

The production yields, yield ratios, coalescence parameters, and strangeness population factors of (anti-)hypertriton and light (anti-)nuclei in isobaric Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions at 200 GeV are predicted to be the same within uncertainties, and the production is found to be insensitive to the chiral magnetic effect (CME), despite the two systems having a 10% difference in proton number and different initial charge separation fractions.

## Method

The authors used the PACIAE (Parton And Hadron Cascade) model with an initial three-flavor (u,d,s) dipole charge separation to simulate the CME in central 0-10% and 0-15% isobaric collisions at midrapidity. Final-state hadrons (p, antiproton, Lambda, anti-Lambda) were then fed into the dynamically constrained phase-space coalescence (DCPC) model to reconstruct (anti-)hypertriton and (anti-)nuclei clusters, using radius parameters R = 5.0 fm for hypertriton and R = 1.74 fm (He-3) or 1.61 fm (H-3) for the normal nuclei.

## Key numbers

* Integrated yield dN/dy of hypertriton (³_ΛH) in 0-10% central Ru+Ru collisions: (6.50 ± 0.06) × 10⁻⁵, with |η| < 0.5 and pT < 3.0 GeV/c.
* Integrated yield dN/dy of anti-hypertriton (³_Λ̅H̅) in 0-10% central Ru+Ru collisions: (3.15 ± 0.03) × 10⁻⁵, with |η| < 0.5 and pT < 3.0 GeV/c.
* Yield ratio of anti-hypertriton to hypertriton (³_Λ̅H̅ / ³_ΛH) in Ru+Ru: 0.48 ± 0.02; in Zr+Zr: 0.48 ± 0.01.
* Coalescence parameter √B₃ for ³_ΛH in 0-10% Ru+Ru: (3.63 ± 0.46) × 10⁻⁴; for ³_Λ̅H̅: (3.46 ± 0.44) × 10⁻⁴.
* Strangeness population factor s₃ in 0-10% Ru+Ru: 0.87 ± 0.02; in Zr+Zr: 0.88 ± 0.01.
* Strangeness population factor s₃ᵗ (using tritium) in 0-10% Ru+Ru: 0.94 ± 0.02; in Zr+Zr: 0.94 ± 0.02.

## Context

This work extends coalescence-model studies of (anti-)hypertriton production from Cu+Cu, Au+Au, and Pb+Pb systems (e.g., STAR at RHIC and ALICE at LHC) to the isobaric Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr systems, filling a gap in collision system size between Cu+Cu (A=63) and Au+Au (A=197). The predictions enable comparison with upcoming experimental data from the RHIC isobar program, and the insensitivity to CME suggests that these observables can serve as a baseline for disentangling CME signals from other nuclear structure effects.
