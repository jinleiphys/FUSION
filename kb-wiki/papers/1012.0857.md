# Axial Nucleon form factors from lattice QCD

> We present results on the nucleon axial form factors within lattice QCD using two flavors of degenerate twisted mass fermions. Volume effects are examined using simulations at two volumes of spatial length $L=2.1$ fm and $L=2.8$ fm. Cut-off effects are investigated using three different values of the lattice spacings, namely $a=0.089$ fm, $a=0.070$ fm and $a=0.056$ fm. The nucleon axial charge is obtained in the continuum limit and chirally extrapolated to the physical pion mass enabling comparison with experiment.

## Key claim

The nucleon axial charge \(g_A\) is determined in the continuum limit and chirally extrapolated to the physical pion mass, yielding a value consistent with experiment, but only after applying volume corrections and assuming that cut-off effects are negligible for the range of lattice spacings studied (0.056–0.089 fm). The paper hedges that the chiral extrapolation uncertainty dominates over both finite-volume and finite-lattice-spacing errors.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with \(N_F=2\) degenerate twisted mass fermions at three lattice spacings (\(a = 0.089\), 0.070, 0.056 fm) and two spatial volumes (\(L = 2.1\) and 2.8 fm). They computed two-point and three-point correlation functions for the nucleon axial vector current, using Gaussian-smeared quark fields and sequential inversions through the sink, and extracted the axial form factors \(G_A(Q^2)\) and \(G_p(Q^2)\) from ratios that plateau at large Euclidean time separations. The axial renormalization constant \(Z_A\) was determined non-perturbatively in the RI'-MOM scheme, and the lattice spacing was set using the nucleon mass extrapolated to the physical point via heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HB\(\chi\)PT).

## Key numbers

*   Nucleon axial charge at the physical point after continuum and chiral extrapolation: \(g_A = 1.12(8)\) (from a three-parameter fit to volume-corrected, continuum-extrapolated data using SSE HB\(\chi\)PT).
*   Lattice spacings determined from the nucleon mass: \(a_{\beta=3.9} = 0.089(1)(5)\) fm, \(a_{\beta=4.05} = 0.070(1)(4)\) fm, \(a_{\beta=4.2} = 0.056(2)(3)\) fm.
*   Pion mass range covered: from 260 MeV to 470 MeV.
*   Spatial lengths used to study volume effects: \(L = 2.1\) fm and \(L = 2.8\) fm.
*   Axial renormalization constants: \(Z_A = 0.757(3)\) at \(\beta=3.9\), \(0.776(3)\) at \(\beta=4.05\), \(0.789(3)\) at \(\beta=4.2\).
*   Dipole fit to \(G_A(Q^2)\) yields an axial mass \(m_A\) larger than the experimental value of \(m_A^{\rm exp} = 1.1\) GeV extracted from electroproduction data.

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice QCD calculations of nucleon axial form factors by the same collaboration (Alexandrou 2008, 2009) using \(N_F=2\) twisted mass fermions, now including three lattice spacings to enable a continuum extrapolation and two volumes to assess finite-size effects. The results are compared with those from \(N_F=2+1\) domain wall fermions by the RBC-UKQCD collaboration (Yamazaki 2009) and a mixed-action approach by LHPC (Bratt 2010), showing agreement among different lattice actions before continuum and infinite-volume limits are taken. The chiral extrapolation using SSE HB\(\chi\)PT with explicit \(\Delta\) degrees of freedom (Hemmert 2003) provides a framework to reach the physical pion mass, and the extracted \(g_A\) is consistent with the experimental value from \(\beta\)-decay. The momentum dependence of \(G_A(Q^2)\) and \(G_p(Q^2)\) is found to be weaker than experiment, motivating future work with lighter pion masses and larger volumes to reduce the dominant chiral extrapolation uncertainty.
