# Neutron star properties with in-medium vector mesons

> We explore the impact of in-medium modification of the properties of vector mesons on the nuclear equation of state and neutron star properties. It is found that in-medium modifications stiffen the nuclear equation of state considerably. If this feature has its correspondence in the full treatment of dense hadronic matter, then very little room is left for the existence of exotic phases like quark matter or boson condensates in the centers of neutron stars of canonical mass.

## Key claim

In-medium modifications of vector meson properties stiffen the nuclear equation of state considerably; if this feature holds in a full treatment of dense hadronic matter, then very little room is left for exotic phases like quark matter or boson condensates in the centers of neutron stars of canonical mass.

## Method

The authors adopt the non-linear Walecka model in the mean-field approximation, with parameters fitted to nuclear saturation properties. They implement two schemes for in-medium vector meson modifications: the Brown-Rho scaling law, where vector meson masses scale with the effective nucleon mass, and a novel effective field theory approach that computes in-medium self-energies from meson-nucleon scattering amplitudes to leading order in density. The resulting equations of state are used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for non-rotating neutron star structure and the full Einstein field equations for rotating stars at the Kepler frequency.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: \(\rho_0 = 0.168\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\); binding energy: \(-16\ \mathrm{MeV}\); nuclear incompressibility: \(K = 240\ \mathrm{MeV}\); effective nucleon mass: \(m^*_N = 0.8\ m_N\)
- \(\rho N\) scattering length: \(a_{\rho N} = (-0.1 + 0.6i)\ \mathrm{fm}\); \(\omega N\) scattering length: \(a_{\omega N} = (-0.5 + 0.2i)\ \mathrm{fm}\)
- In-medium mass shifts at nuclear matter density: \(\Delta m_\rho \simeq 10\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(\Delta m_\omega \simeq 50\ \mathrm{MeV}\); width changes: \(\Delta\Gamma_\rho \simeq 120\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(\Delta\Gamma_\omega \simeq 40\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- Limiting gravitational neutron star masses: between about \(2\ M_\odot\) and \(3\ M_\odot\), with the high-mass end from equations of state including in-medium effects
- Central densities for canonical mass neutron stars (\(M \sim 1.4\ M_\odot\)): \(\sim 3\ \rho_0\) without in-medium effects; drops to \(\sim 1.5\) to \(2\ \rho_0\) with in-medium effects
- Direct Urca process threshold: \(\sim 2.3\ \rho_0\) without in-medium effects; \(\sim 1.5\ \rho_0\) with in-medium effects
- Kepler frequency for canonical mass neutron stars: up to \(\sim 1000\ \mathrm{Hz}\); limiting mass stars may rotate at 1500 to 1700 Hz (periods 0.7 to 0.4 ms)
- Rotation-induced mass increase: up to \(\sim 20\%\) higher than non-rotating mass
- Limiting mass of stable neutron stars in this theory: between 2.6 and \(2.8\ M_\odot\)

## Context

This work extends standard relativistic mean-field treatments of dense hadronic matter, such as the Walecka model as summarized in Weber (1999), by incorporating in-medium modifications of vector mesons that are typically ignored. The in-medium effects are motivated by experimental results from the CERES and HELIOS collaborations showing enhanced low-mass lepton pair production in relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions, which standard treatments fail to reproduce (Drees). The Brown-Rho scaling scheme follows Brown and Rho, while the effective field theory approach builds on the work of Lutz, Wolf, and collaborators. The results enable tighter constraints on the composition of neutron star interiors, suggesting that for canonical mass stars, exotic phases like quark matter or boson condensates may be excluded, and provide limiting masses that help distinguish neutron stars from black hole candidates.
