# Compatibility of recent $Ξ$-nuclear bound state signals

> J-PARC E05 reported recently a hint of a $Ξ^--{^{11}{\rm B}}$ nuclear state in the ${^{12}}{\rm C}(K^-,K^+){^{12}_Ξ}{\rm Be}$ spectrum, bound by $B_{Ξ^-}=8.9\pm 1.4 {^{+3.8}_{-3.1}}$ MeV. Using a density-dependent $Ξ$-nuclear optical potential $V_{\rm opt}^Ξ(ρ)$ we explore to what extent a $Ξ^-_{1s}$ assignment of this nuclear state is compatible with $Ξ^-_{1s}$ and $Ξ^-_{1p}$ nuclear-state interpretations of $Ξ^-$ capture events in light emulsion-nuclei experiments. We find that the only acceptable $Ξ^-_{1s}$ assignment at present, barring an abnormally strong repulsive $ρ^2$ component of $V_{\rm opt}^Ξ(ρ)$, is that for the $Ξ^--{^{11}{\rm B}}$ signal. This finding supports reassigning $Ξ^-$ capture events in $^{14}$N, originally assigned as $Ξ^-_{1s}-{^{14}{\rm N}}$ nuclear states, to $Ξ^0_{1p}-{^{14}{\rm C}}$ nuclear states. The depth of $V_{\rm opt}^Ξ(ρ)$ at nuclear-matter density $ρ_0=0.17$ fm$^{-3}$ is then $-V_{\rm opt}^Ξ(ρ_0)\sim 21$ MeV.

## Key claim
The J-PARC E05 hint of a Ξ⁻–¹¹B nuclear state bound by 8.9 ± 1.4 ⁺³·⁸₋₃·₁ MeV is compatible with a Ξ⁻₁s assignment only if one accepts an abnormally strong repulsive ρ² component of the Ξ-nuclear optical potential; otherwise, the only acceptable Ξ⁻₁s assignment at present is that for the Ξ⁻–¹¹B signal, which supports reassigning Ξ⁻ capture events in ¹⁴N from Ξ⁻₁s–¹⁴N to Ξ⁰₁p–¹⁴C nuclear states.

## Method
The authors used a density-dependent Ξ-nuclear optical potential V_opt^Ξ(ρ) consisting of a two-body ΞN term V_Ξ^(2)(ρ) and a three-body ΞNN term V_Ξ^(3)(ρ), with strength parameters b₀ and B₀ fitted to reproduce measured Ξ⁻ binding energies. They calculated Ξ⁻ binding energies in ¹²C, ¹⁴N, and ¹¹B by solving the Schrödinger equation with this potential plus a finite-size Coulomb potential, and performed χ² fits to test compatibility among the three binding-energy inputs.

## Key numbers
- Ξ⁻ binding energy of the J-PARC E05 hint in ¹¹B: B_Ξ⁻ = 8.9 ± 1.4 ⁺³·⁸₋₃·₁ MeV (statistical and systematic uncertainties added quadratically gives ±3.7 MeV).
- Ξ⁻₁s binding energy in ¹⁴N from J-PARC E07 event IRRAWADDY: B_Ξ⁻¹s(¹⁴N) = 6.27 ± 0.27 MeV.
- Ξ⁻₁p binding energy in ¹²C from KEK-E176: B_Ξ⁻¹p(¹²C) = 0.82 ± 0.15 MeV.
- Depth of V_opt^Ξ at nuclear-matter density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³: −V_opt^Ξ(ρ₀) ≈ 21 MeV.
- In the two-parameter fit including all three binding energies with δB = 3.7 MeV, χ² = 0.99; with δB = 1.4 MeV, χ² = 6.80.
- Calculated widths: Γ_Ξ⁻¹p(¹²C) = 0.23 MeV, Γ_Ξ⁻¹s(¹¹B) = 0.92 MeV, Γ_Ξ⁻¹s(¹⁴N) = 0.87 MeV (for Im b₀ = 0.01 fm).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous density-dependent optical-potential methodology applied to Λ hypernuclei (Friedman and Gal, 2023) and to Ξ⁻ hypernuclei (Friedman and Gal, 2021). It directly addresses the compatibility of the J-PARC E05 Ξ⁻–¹¹B signal with earlier KEK-E176, KEK-E373, and J-PARC E07 emulsion data on Ξ⁻ capture in ¹²C and ¹⁴N. The finding supports the authors' earlier proposal (Friedman and Gal, 2023) to reassign Ξ⁻ capture events in ¹⁴N from Ξ⁻₁s–¹⁴N to Ξ⁰₁p–¹⁴C states. Confirming the 9 MeV binding in ¹¹B with improved resolution (e.g., J-PARC E70) would rule out the Ξ⁻₁s–¹⁴N assignment and yield a Ξ-nuclear potential depth of about 21 MeV, larger than values from HAL-QCD (4 MeV), χEFT at NLO (9 MeV), and SU(6) (6 MeV), but consistent with some Nijmegen ESC16* model versions.
