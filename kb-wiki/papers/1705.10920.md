# Optimized perturbation theory applied to the study of the thermodynamics and BEC-BCS crossover in the three-color Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model

> The Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model with two flavors, three colors and diquark interactions is analyzed in the context of optimized perturbation theory (OPT). Corrections to the thermodynamical potential that go beyond the large-$N_c$ (LN) approximation are taken into account, and the region of the phase diagram corresponding to intermediate chemical potentials and very low temperatures is explored. The simultaneous presence of both the quark-antiquark and diquark condensates can cause the system to behave as a fluid composed of a Bose-Einstein condensate (BEC) or a color superconductor one, in the form of a Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) superfluid. The BEC-BCS crossover is then studied in the nonperturbative OPT scheme. The results obtained in the context of the OPT method are then contrasted with those obtained in the LN approximation. We show that there are values for the coupling constants related to quark-quark and quark-antiquark interactions where the corrections beyond LN brought by the OPT method can influence the behavior of the diquark condensate and the effective quark mass as a function of the baryon chemical potential. These changes in the behavior of the phase structure of the model modify the location of the critical point related to the phase structure as a whole of the model. Also, when we impose the color neutrality condition, our results show that the nature of the phase transition can change as well, shifting the ratio of the quark-antiquark and quark-quark interactions to higher values in the OPT case as compared to the LN approximation.

## Key claim

The OPT method, when applied to the NJL model with diquark interactions, reveals that corrections beyond the large-\(N_c\) (LN) approximation can modify the behavior of the diquark condensate and effective quark mass as functions of baryon chemical potential, and can shift the location of the critical point in the phase diagram; imposing color neutrality can also change the nature of the phase transition, shifting the required ratio of quark-antiquark to quark-quark couplings to higher values compared to the LN approximation.

## Method

The authors applied optimized perturbation theory (OPT) at first order in the bookkeeping parameter \(\delta\) to the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model with two flavors, three colors, and diquark interactions. They derived the thermodynamic effective potential at zero temperature and finite chemical potential, including both one-loop and two-loop diagrams, and used the principle of minimum sensitivity (PMS) to fix the OPT mass parameters \(\eta\) and \(\alpha\). The resulting gap equations for the chiral condensate \(\sigma\) and the diquark condensate \(\Delta\) were solved numerically, and the BEC-BCS crossover was studied by varying the ratio of coupling constants \(G_d/G_s\) and the baryon chemical potential, both with and without imposing color neutrality.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of coupling constants \(G_d/G_s\) is treated as a free parameter; for \(N_c=3\), the Fierz-transformed relation would give \(G_d = 3G_s/4\), but the authors do not fix this relation.
- The number of colors is \(N_c = 3\) and the number of flavors is \(N_f = 2\).
- The diquark condensate forms only for quarks with colors 1 and 2 (red and green); the blue quark (color 3) does not participate in diquark formation.
- The OPT mass parameters \(\eta\) and \(\alpha\) are determined by the PMS conditions \(\partial V_{\rm eff}/\partial\eta = 0\) and \(\partial V_{\rm eff}/\partial\alpha = 0\); at one-loop level these conditions yield \(\bar{\eta} = \sigma\) and \(\bar{\alpha} = \Delta\), recovering the LN approximation.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the BEC-BCS crossover in the NJL model, particularly those by Sun and He (Ref. [SUNHE] in the text), which used the large-\(N_c\) (LN) approximation. The OPT method includes mesonic fluctuations and diquark contributions that are absent in the LN approximation and would appear only at next-to-leading order in a \(1/N_c\) expansion. The results enable a more accurate determination of the phase structure of dense cold quark matter, relevant for compact star astrophysics, and provide a benchmark for comparing nonperturbative methods in effective QCD models.
