# Nucleon Polarizabilities from Low-Energy Compton Scattering

> An effective field theory is used to give a model-independent description of Compton scattering at energies comparable to the pion mass. The amplitudes for scattering on the proton and the deuteron, calculated to fourth order in small momenta in chiral perturbation theory, contain four undetermined parameters that are in one-to-one correspondence with the nucleon polarizabilities. These polarizabilities are extracted from fits to data on elastic photon scattering on hydrogen and deuterium. For the proton we find: alpha_p = 12.1 +/- 1.1 +/- 0.5, beta_p = 3.4 +/- 1.1 +/- 0.1, both in units of 10^{-4} fm^3. For the isoscalar polarizabilities we obtain: alpha_N = 13.0 +/- 1.9 +3.9/-1.5 (in the same units) while beta_N is consistent with zero, within sizeable error bars.

## Key claim

Using chiral perturbation theory (χPT) to fourth order in small momenta, the authors extract nucleon polarizabilities from Compton scattering data in a model-independent way, but the extraction for the isoscalar polarizabilities carries large systematic uncertainties from the choice of deuteron wave function and the treatment of very-low-energy data.

## Method

The authors computed Compton scattering amplitudes on the proton and deuteron to fourth order (O(Q⁴)) in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory, where four undetermined parameters correspond one-to-one to the nucleon polarizabilities. For the deuteron, the amplitude was constructed as a kernel of irreducible γNN→γNN graphs evaluated between deuteron wave functions from χPT and the Nijm93 potential, with two-nucleon contributions included up to O(Q⁴). The polarizabilities were extracted by fitting the calculated differential cross sections to existing elastic photon scattering data on hydrogen (from 160 to 200 MeV) and deuterium (49 to 94.5 MeV), with statistical and theoretical error estimates from varying fit cutoffs and wave function choices.

## Key numbers

- Proton electric polarizability: α_p = (12.1 ± 1.1 (stat.))_{-0.5}^{+0.5} (theory) × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Proton magnetic polarizability: β_p = (3.4 ± 1.1 (stat.))_{-0.1}^{+0.1} (theory) × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Isoscalar electric polarizability: α_N = (13.0 ± 1.9 (stat.))_{-1.5}^{+3.9} (theory) × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Isoscalar magnetic polarizability: β_N = (-1.8 ± 1.9 (stat.))_{-0.9}^{+2.1} (theory) × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Baldin Sum Rule for the proton: α_p + β_p = 13.8 ± 0.4 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (from Olmos de Leon et al.)
- With the Baldin Sum Rule constraint included: α_p = (11.0 ± 0.5 ± 0.2)_{-0.5}^{+0.5} × 10⁻⁴ fm³, β_p = (2.8 ± 0.5 ∓ 0.2)_{-0.1}^{+0.1} × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- The O(Q³) (parameter-free) prediction gave α_p = α_n = 12.2 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ and β_p = β_n = 1.22 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ from pion-loop effects
- Best fit to proton data had χ²/d.o.f. = 170/131

## Context

This work extends the O(Q³) χPT calculation of Compton scattering on the proton by McGovern (1999) and on the deuteron by Beane et al. (2001) to O(Q⁴), providing the first EFT extraction of all nucleon polarizabilities within the same framework. The results are consistent with dispersion-theoretic extractions (Particle Data Group, Drechsel et al.) and with the Baldin Sum Rule, but the deuteron results show sensitivity to the choice of wave function (Nijm93 vs. χPT) that warrants further study. The analysis enables future improvements including explicit inclusion of the Δ-isobar, a complete treatment of the very-low-energy region, and an EFT study of quasi-free γd→γpn scattering.
