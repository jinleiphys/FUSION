# On the background in the $γp \to ω(π^0γ) p$ reaction and mixed event simulation

> In this paper we evaluate sources of background for the $γp \to ωp$, with the $ω$ detected through its $π^0 γ$ decay channel, to compare with the experiment carried out at ELSA. We find background from $γp \to π^0 π^0 p$ followed by decay of a $π^0$ into two $γ$, recombining one $π^0$ and one $γ$, and from the $γp \to π^0 ηp$ reaction with subsequent decay of the $η$ into two photons. This background accounts for the data at $π^0 γ$ invariant masses beyond 700 MeV, but strength is missing at lower invariant masses which was attributed to photon misidentification events, which we simulate to get a good reproduction of the experimental background. Once this is done, we perform an event mixing simulation to reproduce the calculated background and we find that the method provides a good description of the background at low $π^0 γ$ invariant masses but fakes the background at high invariant masses, making background events at low invariant masses, which are due to $γ$ misidentification events, responsible for the background at high invariant masses which is due to the $γp \to π^0 π^0 p$ and $γp \to π^0 ηp$ reactions.

## Key claim

The mixed event method, which is often used to determine background in nuclear physics experiments, is shown to be unsuited for the specific case of the $\gamma p \to \omega p$ reaction with $\omega \to \pi^0 \gamma$ decay, because it produces a background at high $\pi^0 \gamma$ invariant masses that is largely determined by events at low invariant masses rather than the actual physical background sources in that region.

## Method

The authors evaluated two physical background sources for the $\gamma p \to \omega p$ reaction: the $\gamma p \to \pi^0 \pi^0 p$ reaction and the $\gamma p \to \pi^0 \eta p$ reaction, using Monte Carlo event generators with constant transition matrix elements fitted to reproduce experimental cross sections from ELSA. They also simulated a third background component from photon misidentification events, parameterized empirically to match the experimental data at low $\pi^0 \gamma$ invariant masses. They then applied the mixed event technique, combining $\pi^0$ and $\gamma$ from different events, to reproduce the total calculated background and tested its reliability by comparing with the known theoretical background and by artificially distorting the high-mass background.

## Key numbers

- The $\gamma p \to \pi^0 \pi^0 p$ and $\gamma p \to \pi^0 \eta p$ reactions account for the experimental background at $\pi^0 \gamma$ invariant masses beyond 700 MeV.
- The mixed event method (method I) reproduces the real background well across the whole invariant mass range when using events that individually may not satisfy the $|\vec p_{\pi^0} + \vec p_\gamma| < 500$ MeV/c cut.
- Method II, which only uses events that individually satisfy the $|\vec p_{\pi^0} + \vec p_\gamma| < 500$ MeV/c cut, shows clear disagreement with the real background except at low invariant masses.
- When the background was artificially cut off at 850 MeV or 750 MeV, the mixed event method output barely changed, demonstrating insensitivity to the actual high-mass background.
- The correlation analysis showed that for a final mixed event invariant mass of 800 MeV, the initial events used in the mixing accumulate in the region where $M_{\pi \gamma}(ini)$ is about 400-500 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the debate on the $\omega$ meson mass shift in nuclear matter, following earlier claims of a large mass shift from the CBELSA/TAPS experiment (david, prl) and the subsequent demonstration by Kaskulov (2006) that the shift could depend on background subtraction choices. The paper directly contradicts the conclusions of the mixed event analysis by metagerice, which had used the technique to support the claim of an $\omega$ mass shift. The results enable a critical reassessment of whether the mixed event method is appropriate for determining $\omega$ mass shifts in nuclear medium studies, and support the recent reanalysis by marianahad that concluded one cannot claim a shift of the $\omega$ mass from this experiment.
