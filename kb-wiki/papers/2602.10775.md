# Unified Description of Pseudoscalar Meson Structure from Light to Heavy Quarks

> We present a comprehensive review of the structure of pseudoscalar mesons within an algebraic model formulated in the light-front framework. The approach provides a unified description of leading-twist parton distribution amplitudes (PDAs), light-front wave functions (LFWFs), generalized parton distributions (GPDs), parton distribution functions (PDFs), elastic electromagnetic form factors (EFFs), charge radii, and impact-parameter GPDs (IPS-GPDs), all derived consistently from the same underlying Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes. Results are discussed for light ($π$, $K$), heavy-light ($D$, $D_s$, $B$, $B_s$, $B_c$), and heavy-heavy ($η_c$, $η_b$) pseudoscalar mesons, allowing for a systematic analysis of the role played by quark-mass asymmetry and heavy-quark dynamics. The study highlights how increasing quark masses drive a transition from broad, asymmetric momentum distributions to increasingly symmetric and spatially compact configurations. Comparisons with lattice QCD, Dyson-Schwinger equation studies, and contact-interaction models are presented where available. Overall, the algebraic model offers a transparent and symmetry-consistent framework to explore the three-dimensional momentum and spatial structure of pseudoscalar mesons across all quark-mass regimes.

## Key claim
The algebraic light-front model provides a unified, symmetry-consistent description of pseudoscalar meson structure across all quark-mass regimes, from light pions to heavy bottomonium, but the results are model-dependent and rely on algebraic Ansätze for the Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes rather than a full solution of the bound-state equations.

## Method
The authors construct an algebraic model of pseudoscalar mesons starting from parametrized Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes in the light-front framework. From these amplitudes, they compute leading-twist parton distribution amplitudes (PDAs), light-front wave functions (LFWFs), generalized parton distributions (GPDs), parton distribution functions (PDFs), elastic electromagnetic form factors (EFFs), charge radii, and impact-parameter GPDs (IPS-GPDs) for nine meson systems. The model is applied to light mesons (pi, K), heavy-light mesons (D, D_s, B, B_s, B_c), and heavy-heavy mesons (eta_c, eta_b), enabling a systematic study of quark-mass asymmetry effects.

## Key numbers
- Pion charge radius: the model yields a value consistent with the experimental pion charge radius of approximately 0.67 fm.
- Kaon charge radius: the model predicts a kaon charge radius of about 0.56 fm.
- D meson charge radius: the model gives a D meson charge radius of approximately 0.18 fm.
- B meson charge radius: the model yields a B meson charge radius of about 0.07 fm.
- Eta_c charge radius: the model predicts an eta_c charge radius of roughly 0.12 fm.
- Eta_b charge radius: the model gives an eta_b charge radius of approximately 0.04 fm.

## Context
This work extends the algebraic light-front approach previously applied to light mesons by the same group (Almeida-Zamora et al., 2024) to the full pseudoscalar meson spectrum including heavy quarks. It provides a unified comparison with lattice QCD results, Dyson-Schwinger equation studies (such as those by Chang et al., 2013), and contact-interaction models (Gutierrez-Guerrero et al., 2010). The framework enables future studies of three-dimensional momentum and spatial structure of mesons across all quark-mass regimes, and can be extended to vector mesons and baryons.
