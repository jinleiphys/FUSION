# ALP contributions to $╬╝\to e$ conversion

> We study the $╬╝\to e$ conversion process in nuclear targets arising in models of axion-like particles (ALPs) with hadronic and charged lepton flavor violating (CLFV) interactions. Contributions to this process generally fall into two categories: spin-independent (SI) and spin-dependent (SD). While the SI contribution can be generated by a dipole operator through purely leptonic ALP interactions, the SD contribution can also be present through ALP-quark interactions at tree-level. It is naively anticipated that the SI contribution would be dominant due to its coherent enhancement. In this $\textit{letter}$, we show that is not generically the case; in particular, for naturally-sized ALP couplings to quarks of order $\sim\!m_q/f_a$, the SD interaction induced by ALP-$ŽĆ^0$ mixing turns out to be the leading contribution to $╬╝\to e$ conversion. Intuitively, this stems from the suppressed dipole contribution by the QED one-loop factor which counters the effect of SI coherent enhancement. Our study highlights the importance of $╬╝\to e$ conversion searches in exploring the parameter space of generic ALP models, and demonstrates the competitiveness of these searches in probing the CLFV ALP parameter space in the heavy mass range of $m_a\gtrsim m_╬╝$.

## Method

The authors construct a simplified ALP model with pseudoscalar isovector couplings to light quarks, a flavor-diagonal coupling to muons, and flavor-violating couplings to ╬╝ and e. They compute the ╬╝ŌåÆe conversion rate in nuclear targets by evaluating both the one-loop dipole operator (from purely leptonic ALP interactions) and the tree-level t-channel a/ŽĆŌü░ exchange (from ALP-quark interactions via a-ŽĆŌü░ mixing), using chiral perturbation theory to map the ALP couplings to hadronic interactions below the QCD confinement scale.

## Context

This work extends previous EFT studies of ╬╝ŌåÆe conversion (Weinberg 1959, Shanker 1979, Kitano 2002, Cirigliano 2009, Rule 2021, Haxton 2022) that focused primarily on spin-independent contributions, and earlier SD studies (Cirigliano 2017, Davidson 2017, Crivellin 2017, Hoferichter 2022). It specifically builds on the leptonic ALP analysis of Cornella (2019) by adding hadronic ALP couplings. The results demonstrate that upcoming ╬╝ŌåÆe conversion experiments (Mu2e at Fermilab, COMET at J-PARC) can probe ALP parameter space inaccessible to ╬╝ŌåÆe╬│ and rare meson decay searches, particularly for ALP masses m_a Ōē│ m_╬╝ where the projected sensitivity on f_a improves by over an order of magnitude compared to current limits.
