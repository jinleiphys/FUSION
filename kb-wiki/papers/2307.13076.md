# ALP contributions to $μ\to e$ conversion

> We study the $μ\to e$ conversion process in nuclear targets arising in models of axion-like particles (ALPs) with hadronic and charged lepton flavor violating (CLFV) interactions. Contributions to this process generally fall into two categories: spin-independent (SI) and spin-dependent (SD). While the SI contribution can be generated by a dipole operator through purely leptonic ALP interactions, the SD contribution can also be present through ALP-quark interactions at tree-level. It is naively anticipated that the SI contribution would be dominant due to its coherent enhancement. In this $\textit{letter}$, we show that is not generically the case; in particular, for naturally-sized ALP couplings to quarks of order $\sim\!m_q/f_a$, the SD interaction induced by ALP-$π^0$ mixing turns out to be the leading contribution to $μ\to e$ conversion. Intuitively, this stems from the suppressed dipole contribution by the QED one-loop factor which counters the effect of SI coherent enhancement. Our study highlights the importance of $μ\to e$ conversion searches in exploring the parameter space of generic ALP models, and demonstrates the competitiveness of these searches in probing the CLFV ALP parameter space in the heavy mass range of $m_a\gtrsim m_μ$.

# ALP contributions to μ→e conversion

## Key claim

Contrary to the naive expectation that spin-independent (SI) contributions dominate μ→e conversion due to coherent enhancement by Z², the spin-dependent (SD) contribution from tree-level a/π⁰ exchange is actually the leading contribution for axion-like particles (ALPs) with naturally-sized couplings to quarks of order ~m_q/f_a. This is because the dipole-mediated SI contribution is suppressed by a QED one-loop factor (~α_em/4π) that counteracts the coherent enhancement.

## Method

The authors construct a simplified ALP model with pseudoscalar isovector couplings to light quarks, a flavor-diagonal coupling to muons, and flavor-violating couplings to μ and e. They compute the μ→e conversion rate in nuclear targets by evaluating both the one-loop dipole operator (from purely leptonic ALP interactions) and the tree-level t-channel a/π⁰ exchange (from ALP-quark interactions via a-π⁰ mixing), using chiral perturbation theory to map the ALP couplings to hadronic interactions below the QCD confinement scale.

## Key numbers

- Ratio of squared Wilson coefficients |C_S/P|²/|C_D|² ~ O(10⁷) for light ALPs (m_a ≪ m_π⁰), demonstrating the dominance of tree-level a/π⁰ exchange over the dipole contribution
- For ²⁷₁₃Al target: Z_eff = 11.8036, W_MM = 61.67, W¹¹_Σ''Σ'' = 9.2 × 10⁻²
- Ratio Γ_S/P/Γ_D ranges from O(10) to O(10³) for m_a from ultralight up to O(GeV) in ²⁷₁₃Al
- Current experimental bound: BR(μ→e; Ti) < 6.1 × 10⁻¹³ (Wintz 1998)
- Projected sensitivity: BR(μ→e; Al) = 8.0 × 10⁻¹⁷ for future Mu2e/COMET searches
- Current bound on μ→eγ: BR(μ⁺→e⁺γ) < 4.2 × 10⁻¹³ (MEG 2016)
- Current bound on π⁰→μe: BR < 3.6 × 10⁻¹⁰ (KTeV 2007)
- Current bound on K_L→μe: BR < 4.7 × 10⁻¹² (BNL 1998)
- Current bound on K⁺→π⁺μ⁺e⁻: BR < 1.3 × 10⁻¹¹ (Sher 2005)
- For f_a = 1 TeV fixed, upper limits on |v_eμ|, |a_eμ| ≲ O(10⁻²–10⁻³) for m_a = 200–1000 MeV
- Current limit from μ→e conversion indicates f_a ≳ O(1) TeV for m_a > m_μ
- Future searches projected to reach f_a ~ O(100) TeV in the heavy m_a region

## Context

This work extends previous EFT studies of μ→e conversion (Weinberg 1959, Shanker 1979, Kitano 2002, Cirigliano 2009, Rule 2021, Haxton 2022) that focused primarily on spin-independent contributions, and earlier SD studies (Cirigliano 2017, Davidson 2017, Crivellin 2017, Hoferichter 2022). It specifically builds on the leptonic ALP analysis of Cornella (2019) by adding hadronic ALP couplings. The results demonstrate that upcoming μ→e conversion experiments (Mu2e at Fermilab, COMET at J-PARC) can probe ALP parameter space inaccessible to μ→eγ and rare meson decay searches, particularly for ALP masses m_a ≳ m_μ where the projected sensitivity on f_a improves by over an order of magnitude compared to current limits.
