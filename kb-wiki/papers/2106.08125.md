# Probing early-time longitudinal dynamics with the $Λ$ hyperon's spin polarization in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We systematically study the hyperon global polarization's sensitivity to the collision systems' initial longitudinal flow velocity in hydrodynamic simulations. By explicitly imposing local energy-momentum conservation when mapping the initial collision geometry to macroscopic hydrodynamic fields, we study the evolution of systems' orbital angular momentum (OAM) and fluid vorticity. We find that a simultaneous description of the $Λ$ hyperons' global polarization and the slope of pion's directed flow can strongly constrain the size of longitudinal flow at the beginning of hydrodynamic evolution. We extract the size of the initial longitudinal flow and the fraction of orbital angular momentum in the produced QGP fluid as a function of collision energy with the STAR measurements in the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program. We find that there is about 100-200 $\hbar$ OAM that remains in the mid-rapidity fluid at the beginning of hydrodynamic evolution. We further exam the effects of different hydrodynamic gradients on the spin polarization of $Λ$ and $\barΛ$. The gradients of $μ_B/T$ can change the ordering between $Λ$'s and $\barΛ$'s polarization.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that simultaneously describing the Λ hyperon's global polarization and the slope of pion's directed flow can strongly constrain the size of longitudinal flow at the beginning of hydrodynamic evolution, but this constraint depends on the model parameter f that controls how much of the system's orbital angular momentum is attributed to initial fluid vorticity versus shifts in energy density flux tubes.

## Method

The authors developed a parametric 3D initial condition model based on a Monte Carlo Glauber approach, introducing a longitudinal rapidity fraction parameter f that controls the fraction of longitudinal momentum attributed to flow velocity while enforcing local energy-momentum conservation. They performed 3D viscous hydrodynamic simulations using the MUSIC code with a lattice QCD-based equation of state at finite baryon density (NEOS-BQS), coupled to UrQMD hadronic transport, and computed the Λ and anti-Λ spin polarization using the Pauli-Lubanski vector formalism including contributions from thermal vorticity, μB/T gradients, and velocity shear tensor.

## Key numbers

- The STAR Collaboration measured fluid vorticity of ω ≈ (9 ± 1) × 10^21 s^(-1) from Λ hyperon global polarization
- About 100-200 ħ of orbital angular momentum remains in the mid-rapidity fluid at the beginning of hydrodynamic evolution
- With f = 0.15, the model gives a good description of the centrality dependence of Λ global polarization in Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV, while f = 0.5 overestimates STAR measurements by a factor of two
- The averaged thermal vorticity ⟨ω_th^xz⟩ saturates around 10^(-4) (in natural units) after the first fm/c for f = 0 at 200 GeV
- The hyperon production is dominated by time-like surface elements at late times, with most contributions to total polarization coming from late hydrodynamic evolution

## Context

This work extends the 3D initial condition model of Shen (2020) by introducing a parameter to vary early-time longitudinal flow while conserving orbital angular momentum. It addresses the theoretical question of how collision geometry and orbital angular momentum induce local fluid vorticity, building on the STAR discovery of global hyperon polarization (STAR Collaboration, 2017). The results enable quantitative constraints on initial longitudinal dynamics at RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies and show that the μB/T gradient term can change the ordering between Λ and anti-Λ polarization, which previous works by Liu (2020) and Becattini (2021) had proposed as contributing to spin polarization.
