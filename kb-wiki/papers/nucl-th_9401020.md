# Power Moments and Scaling Properties of Nuclear Emulsion Data

> Various features of the charge yield in heavy ion collisions are studied. Considered here are the nuclear emulsion data of $^{84}$Kr, $^{131}$Xe, $^{197}$Au, $^{238}$U in the energy range of 1 GeV/A. Mean charge yields and multiplicity distribution of these data indicate that the nuclear fragmentation at this energy is a multiprocess phenomena. Power moments of the charge distribution exhibit a scale invariance. While small nuclei (Kr and Xe) show a nuclear size dependence, large nuclei (Au and U) exhibit scale independence in their charge distribution.

# Power Moments and Scaling Properties of Nuclear Emulsion Data

## Key claim
The charge distributions from nuclear emulsion data for heavy ion collisions at around 1 GeV/A exhibit scale invariance (bin-size independence of the generalized dimension) for large nuclei (Au and U), but show a nuclear size dependence for smaller nuclei (Kr and Xe), indicating that the fragmentation at this energy is a multiprocess phenomenon rather than a single-temperature process.

## Method
The authors analyzed nuclear emulsion data for 84Kr at 1.52 GeV/A, 131Xe at 1.22 GeV/A, 197Au at 0.99 GeV/A, and 238U at 0.96 GeV/A. They computed power moments P_q(L) of the charge distribution as a function of bin size L, and extracted the generalized (anomalous) dimension D_q(r) where r = L/Z is the dimensionless bin size. The data were compared to a simple exactly solvable fragmentation model (the "x-model") based on the symmetric group cycle indicator, using mixtures of two or three different x values to represent different temperature components.

## Key numbers
- 84Kr at 1.52 GeV/A, 131Xe at 1.22 GeV/A, 197Au at 0.99 GeV/A, 238U at 0.96 GeV/A: the four projectile-target systems analyzed
- For Au, the intermittency exponent alpha_q = (q-1) D_q'(1) is approximately 0.2, showing approximate q-independence
- For Kr, Xe, and U, the ratio alpha_q/q = (q-1) D_q'(1)/q is approximately 0.14, 0.22, and 0.16 respectively
- Generalized dimension D_q(r) for Au at L=1: D_2 = 0.136, D_3 = 0.103, D_4 = 0.0840, D_5 = 0.0714
- Generalized dimension D_q(r) for U at L=1: D_2 = 0.202, D_3 = 0.165, D_4 = 0.141, D_5 = 0.124
- For small nuclei (Kr and Xe), D_q(r) increases linearly with r until r approximately 0.5; for heavy nuclei (Au and U), D_q(r) saturates after r approximately 0.25

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous analysis of Au emulsion data at 0.99 GeV/A (Lee and Mekjian, 1993) to a systematic comparison of four nuclei spanning a range of sizes. It builds on the fragmentation model developed by Mekjian and Lee (1991, 1992) and Mekjian (1990), which uses the symmetric group cycle indicator as a partition function. The results show that the emulsion data require mixtures of multiple temperature components (different x values) to fit the power moments, unlike a single-temperature fragmentation process. The analysis enables future study of prompt fragmentation from central collisions at a single equilibriated temperature, which the authors predict would show q-independence in D_q(r).
