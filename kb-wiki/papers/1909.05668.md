# Finite Size Scaling in Time Evolution During the Colorless-QCD Confining Phase Transition

> The time evolution of the expanding Colorless Partonic Matter, created in Ultra-Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions and undergoing the confining phase transition towards a Hadronic Gas, is discussed in the context of a unified model combining our Colorless QCD-MIT Bag Model with the boost invariant Bjorken expansion. The Bjorken Equation in the case of a longitudinal expansion scenario of a non-ideal relativistic medium in finite volume is solved using certain initial conditions $(τ_i,T(τ_i))$ and their effect is studied in detail. The evolution of the temperature as a function of the proper time $T(τ,V)$ is then obtained at different volumes. Different times characterising different scales of the whole time evolution, like the time of the finite volume transition point $τ_0(V)$, the hadronic time $τ_H(V)$ at which the hadronization is completed, the lifetime of the Colorless Partonic Plasma $Δτ_{CPP}(V)$ and the lifetime of the confining phase transition $Δτ_{PT}(V)$ are calculated and their finite size scaling properties are studied in detail. New finite size scaling laws are derived. Also, the time evolution of some Thermal Response Functions as the order parameter $\mathcal{H}(τ,V)$, energy density $ε(τ,V)$, pressure $\mathscr{P}(τ,V)$ and the sound velocity $\mathscr{C}_{s}(τ,V)$ are investigated and studied in detail. We find that the time evolution of our system is really affected by the colorlessness requirement and the initial conditions of the partonic matter: the closer the volume is to the thermodynamic limit, the longer are the times and the lifetimes of the system. A detailed analysis of the temporal decreasing, in negative power, of the energy density $ε(τ,V) \propto τ^{-θ}$ in each of the three stages of the Bjorken expansion is carried out.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the time evolution of the expanding Colorless Partonic Plasma undergoing the confining phase transition is significantly affected by both the colorlessness requirement and the initial conditions, with the qualification that the closer the system volume is to the thermodynamic limit, the longer the characteristic times and lifetimes become.

## Method

The authors combine their Colorless QCD-MIT Bag Model with the boost-invariant Bjorken expansion to solve the Bjorken equation for a longitudinally expanding non-ideal relativistic medium in finite volume. They use the $\mathscr{L}_{m,n}$-method to compute thermal response functions (order parameter, energy density, pressure, sound velocity) as functions of proper time and volume, and extract finite-size scaling properties of characteristic times including the transition point time $\tau_0(V)$, hadronic time $\tau_H(V)$, and lifetimes of the Colorless Partonic Plasma and the phase transition.

## Key numbers

- Bag constant used: $\mathfrak{B}^{1/4} = 145\ \mathrm{MeV}$
- Number of quark flavors: $N_f = 2$ (u and d quarks only)
- Number of color charges: $N_c = 3$
- The energy density scales as $\epsilon(\tau,V) \propto \tau^{-\theta}$ in each of the three stages of Bjorken expansion, with $\theta$ being a negative power
- The degeneracy factors are $\mathbf{a}_{QG} = \frac{\pi^2}{180}(7N_c N_f + 4N_g)$ and $\mathbf{a}_{HG} = \frac{\pi^2}{90}N_\pi$ where $N_g$ is the number of gluons and $N_\pi$ the number of pions

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous development of the Colorless QCD-MIT Bag Model (Ladrem 2005, Ladrem 2015, Ladrem 2019) by incorporating finite-size scaling into the time evolution of the confining phase transition. It builds on the Bjorken boost-invariant hydrodynamic framework (Bjorken 1983) and the group-theoretical projection method for colorlessness (Redlich 1980, Turko 1981). The results enable quantitative study of how finite volume affects the temporal development of the partonic-to-hadronic transition in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions, with potential connections to lattice QCD calculations (Ejiri 2006, Cheng 2008) that show similar behavior of the trace anomaly.
