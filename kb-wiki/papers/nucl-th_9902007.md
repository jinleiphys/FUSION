# $g_{NKΛ}$ and $g_{NKΣ}$ from QCD Sum Rules in the $γ_5 σ_{μν}$ structure

> The NKY coupling constant for $Y = Λ$ and $Σ$ is evaluated in a QCD sum rule calculation. We study the Borel sum rule for the three point function of one pseudoscalar one nucleon and one hyperon current up to order six in the operator product expansion. The Borel transform is performed with respect to the nucleon and hyperon momenta, which are taken to be equal. We discuss and extend the result of a previous analysis in the $\rlap{/}{q}iγ_5$ structure and compare it with the result obtained with the use of the $γ_5 σ_{μν}$ structure. We find that the effect of pole-continuum transitions is very important in the $\rlap{/}{q}iγ_5$ structure and that it changes completely results obtained in previous analysis.

# g_{NKΛ} and g_{NKΣ} from QCD Sum Rules in the γ₅σ_{μν} structure

## Key claim

The NKY coupling constants extracted from QCD sum rules depend strongly on which Dirac structure is used in the analysis; the γ₅σ_{μν} structure yields smaller and more reliable values than the previously used /qiγ₅ structure because it is less contaminated by unknown single-pole contributions from N→N* transitions, but the results show a huge breaking of SU(3) symmetry compared to the SU(3) prediction.

## Method

The authors perform a QCD sum rule calculation of the three-point correlation function with one pseudoscalar kaon current, one nucleon current, and one hyperon (Λ or Σ) current, working up to dimension-six operators in the operator product expansion. They apply a Borel transform with respect to the nucleon and hyperon momenta (taken equal) and extract the coupling constant from the γ₅σ_{μν} Dirac structure by fitting the right-hand side of the sum rule to a straight line in the Borel mass squared and extrapolating to M²=0, which removes the unknown single-pole term. The continuum contribution is included through standard Eᵢ factors with continuum thresholds set at (M_Y + 0.5)² GeV² and (M_N + 0.5)² GeV², with a second set at (M_Y + 0.7)² GeV² used to estimate the uncertainty.

## Key numbers

- |g_{NKΛ}| = 2.37 ± 0.09, extracted from the γ₅σ_{μν} structure by fitting the QCD sum rule result to a straight line and extrapolating to M²=0, with the error estimated from using two different continuum thresholds.
- |g_{NKΣ}| = 0.025 ± 0.015, extracted from the γ₅σ_{μν} structure using the same fitting procedure.
- For comparison, the same sum rule in the /qiγ₅ structure (including continuum contributions) gives |g_{NKΛ}| = 1.5 ± 0.3 and |g_{NKΣ}| = 0.25 ± 0.05.
- The earlier calculation by Choe et al. (1995) without continuum contributions gave |g_{NKΛ}| = 6.96 and |g_{NKΣ}| = 1.05.
- The SU(3) symmetry prediction using α_D = 0.64 gives |g_{NKΛ}/g_{NKΣ}| = 3.55.
- Input parameters: quark condensate ⟨q̅q⟩ = -(0.23)³ GeV³, strange quark mass m_s = 150 MeV, kaon decay constant f_K = 113 MeV, nucleon mass M_N = 0.938 GeV, Λ mass M_Λ = 1.150 GeV, Σ mass M_Σ = 1.189 GeV, kaon mass m_K = 0.495 GeV.
- The Borel window used is 0.8 ≤ M² ≤ 1.6 GeV², where the continuum contribution is always smaller than 50% of the total OPE.

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach to NKY couplings by applying the γ₅σ_{μν} Dirac structure, following the method that Birse and Krippa (1996) and Kim et al. (1997) used for the πNN coupling to avoid scheme dependence and reduce single-pole contamination. It directly revisits and corrects the earlier three-point function calculations by Choe et al. (1995) and by Suh and Chun (1995), showing that those results were dominated by pole-continuum transitions that were not properly accounted for. The results enable improved determinations of hyperon-nucleon interactions for use in nuclear physics and hypernuclear studies, though the large SU(3) symmetry breaking found here raises open questions about the reliability of SU(3) predictions for these couplings.
