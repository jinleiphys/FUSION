# Coherent gamma-gamma and gamma-A interactions in very peripheral collisions at relativistic ion colliders

> Due to coherence, there are strong electromagnetic fields of short duration in very peripheral collisions. They give rise to photon-photon and photon-nucleus collisions with a high flux up to an invariant mass region hitherto unexplored experimentally. After a general survey of the field equivalent photon numbers and photon-photon luminosities, especially for relativistic heavy ion collisions, are discussed. Special care needs to be taken to include the effects of the strong interaction and nuclear size in this case. Photon-photon and photon-hadron physics at various invariant mass scales are then discussed. The maximum equivalent photon energy in the lab-system (collider frame) are typically of the order of 3 GeV for RHIC and 100 GeV for LHC. Diffractive processes are an important background process. Lepton-pair, especially electron-positron pair production is copious. Due to the strong fields there will be new phenomena, like multiple e+e- pair production. The experimental techniques to select gamma-gamma-processes are finally discussed together with important background processes.

## Key claim

The paper argues that very peripheral relativistic heavy-ion collisions provide strong, short-duration electromagnetic fields that enable photon-photon and photon-nucleus collisions at invariant masses not yet experimentally explored, but the practical feasibility depends on careful treatment of strong-interaction effects and nuclear size, and on experimental techniques to separate these coherent processes from diffractive backgrounds.

## Method

The authors survey the field by deriving equivalent photon numbers and photon-photon luminosities for relativistic heavy-ion collisions, incorporating the effects of strong interactions and finite nuclear size. They then discuss photon-photon and photon-hadron physics across various invariant mass scales, including lepton-pair production, vector meson production, and multiple e+e- pair production. Experimental techniques for selecting gamma-gamma processes and identifying background processes, particularly diffractive ones, are also reviewed.

## Key numbers

- Maximum equivalent photon energy in the lab (collider frame) is about 3 GeV for RHIC and about 100 GeV for LHC.
- Lepton-pair, especially electron-positron pair production, is described as "copious" in these collisions.
- Multiple e+e- pair production is identified as a new phenomenon arising from the strong electromagnetic fields.

## Context

This review extends earlier work on equivalent photon methods, such as the foundational studies by Fermi (1924) and the comprehensive review by Budnev, Ginzburg, Meledin, and Serbo (1975), as well as the topical review by Baur, Hencken, and Trautmann (1998). It also builds on the treatment of Coulomb excitation by Alder and Winther (1966, 1975) and the work on photonuclear interactions by Bertulani and Baur (1988). The paper enables future experimental programs at RHIC and LHC to study electromagnetic processes in peripheral collisions, complementing the central-collision program, and opens the door to searches for new physics in low-multiplicity, low-background events.
