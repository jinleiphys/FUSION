# The impact of glasma on heavy flavor azimuthal correlations and spectra

> We study the phenomenological impact of the pre-equilibrium glasma initial stage of heavy-ion collisions on heavy quark azimuthal correlations and spectra. Using our numerical solver, we simulate the transport of heavy quark test particles in an SU(3) glasma background field. The glasma field equations are formulated using classical real-time lattice gauge theory, and the heavy quark dynamics are described by classical transport equations numerically solved using the colored particle-in-cell method. For the first time, the effect of the glasma stage on the azimuthal correlations of $c\overline{c}$ and $b\overline{b}$ pairs is studied. The resulting azimuthal width $σ_{Δϕ}$ exhibits a large and quick decorrelation due to the strong glasma fields. Further, we evaluate how the $p_T$-broadening in the glasma affects heavy quark $p_T$-spectra, which are initialized according to the Fixed-Order Next-to-Leading Logarithm (FONLL) heavy quark production calculation. The nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ is extracted for $c$ and $b$ quarks in the glasma and additional nuclear PDF effects accounting for gluon shadowing are included.

## Key claim

The glasma pre-equilibrium stage causes a large and quick decorrelation of initially back-to-back heavy quark pairs, with the azimuthal width σΔϕ growing significantly within the first 0.2 fm/c, and this decorrelation is stronger for charm than for beauty quarks. The nuclear modification factor RAA shows a moderate suppression at low pT and a slight enhancement at intermediate pT, with the effect depending on the glasma saturation momentum Qs and collision energy.

## Method

The authors simulate classical transport of heavy quark test particles in an SU(3) glasma background field using Wong's equations, with the glasma fields evolved via classical real-time lattice gauge theory in Milne coordinates. The heavy quark dynamics are solved numerically using the colored particle-in-cell method, where the color charges undergo SU(3) rotations via finite-velocity Wilson lines evaluated along the particle trajectories. Heavy quark pairs are initialized back-to-back in azimuthal angle with FONLL-based pT spectra, and the two-particle correlations in relative azimuthal angle Δφ and pseudorapidity Δη are extracted, along with the nuclear modification factor RAA from pT-broadening in the glasma.

## Key numbers

- Azimuthal correlation width σΔφ for charm quarks increases from approximately 0 to about 0.8 rad after 0.2 fm/c of glasma evolution for pT = 2 GeV and Qs = 2 GeV.
- Azimuthal correlation width σΔφ for beauty quarks increases from approximately 0 to about 0.4 rad after 0.2 fm/c of glasma evolution for pT = 2 GeV and Qs = 2 GeV.
- The nuclear modification factor RAA for charm quarks at τ = 0.4 fm/c with Qs = 2 GeV shows suppression to about 0.85 at pT = 2 GeV and enhancement to about 1.15 at pT = 6 GeV.
- The nuclear modification factor RAA for beauty quarks at τ = 0.4 fm/c with Qs = 2 GeV shows suppression to about 0.9 at pT = 2 GeV and enhancement to about 1.1 at pT = 6 GeV.
- The formation time for charm quarks with pT = 2 GeV is τform = 1/(2mT) ≈ 0.07 fm/c, where mT = sqrt(mc² + pT²) with mc = 1.3 GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous qualitative studies of heavy quark transport in glasma by Das (2017), Sun (2019), and Ruggieri (2018) by providing the first quantitative calculation of heavy flavor azimuthal correlations in the glasma stage. It complements studies of heavy quark azimuthal correlations in the QGP phase by Nahrgang (2013) and Cao (2014), and enables future comparisons between pre-equilibrium and equilibrium contributions to heavy flavor observables. The results motivate incorporating the glasma stage into full heavy-ion event generators to resolve the RAA and v2 puzzle.
