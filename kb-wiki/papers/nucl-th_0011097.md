# Chiral symmetry and meson exchange approach to hypernuclear decay

> We take an approach to the $Λ$ nonmesonic weak decay in nuclei based on the exchange of mesons under the guidelines of chiral Lagrangians. The one pion and one kaon exchange are considered, together with the exchange of two pions, either correlated, leading to an important scalar-isoscalar exchange ($σ$-like exchange), or uncorrelated (box diagrams). A drastic reduction of the OPE results for the $Γ_n/Γ_p$ ratio is obtained and the new results are compatible with all present experiments within errors. The absolute rates obtained for different nuclei are also in fair agreement with experiment.

## Key claim

The authors show that including kaon exchange and two-pion exchange (both correlated, producing a sigma-like scalar-isoscalar contribution, and uncorrelated box diagrams) within a chiral Lagrangian framework reduces the one-pion-exchange (OPE) prediction for the neutron-to-proton induced decay ratio \(\Gamma_n/\Gamma_p\) from about 1/8 to values around 0.54, making the results compatible with all present experiments once the experimental errors are enlarged to account for the previously neglected two-particle–two-hole (\(2p2h\)) channel.

## Method

The nonmesonic weak decay of a \(\Lambda\) hyperon in nuclear matter was calculated using a meson-exchange approach guided by chiral Lagrangians. The decay width was obtained from the imaginary part of the \(\Lambda\) self-energy in infinite nuclear matter, including one-pion exchange (OPE), one-kaon exchange, and two-pion exchange (both correlated, via a chiral unitary treatment of the scalar-isoscalar \(\pi\pi\) interaction that dynamically generates the \(\sigma\) meson, and uncorrelated box diagrams with \(N\) and \(\Delta\) intermediate states). Short-range correlations were included via a Landau-Migdal parameter \(g' = 0.7\) at zero momentum transfer, and the results were extended to finite nuclei using the local density approximation.

## Key numbers

*   For \(^{12}_{\Lambda}\)C, the OPE-only proton-induced rate is \(\Gamma_p/\Gamma_{\text{free}} = 0.9557\) and the neutron-induced rate is \(\Gamma_n/\Gamma_{\text{free}} = 0.1194\), giving \(\Gamma_n/\Gamma_p \approx 0.12\).
*   Adding kaon exchange reduces the proton rate to \(\Gamma_p/\Gamma_{\text{free}} = 0.5215\) and increases the neutron rate to \(\Gamma_n/\Gamma_{\text{free}} = 0.2728\) for \(\pi+K\) combined.
*   The full model (OPE + kaon + two-pion exchange) for \(^{12}_{\Lambda}\)C gives \(\Gamma_p/\Gamma_{\text{free}} = 0.5714\), \(\Gamma_n/\Gamma_{\text{free}} = 0.3078\), and \(\Gamma_n/\Gamma_p = 0.54\).
*   For all nuclei studied (\(^{12}_{\Lambda}\)C through \(^{208}_{\Lambda}\)Pb), the \(\Gamma_n/\Gamma_p\) ratio is consistently 0.54.
*   The total nonmesonic decay rate for \(^{12}_{\Lambda}\)C is \(\Gamma_{\text{tot}}/\Gamma_{\text{free}} = 1.3992\) (including mesonic and \(2p2h\) contributions), compared to an experimental value of \(1.14 \pm 0.08\) from \((K^+, \pi^+)\) data.
*   The total rate for \(^{208}_{\Lambda}\)Pb is \(\Gamma_{\text{tot}}/\Gamma_{\text{free}} = 1.7835\).

## Context

This work extends the one-pion-exchange (OPE) model of Oset and Salcedo (1985) and the one-meson-exchange models of Dubach (1996) and Parreno, Ramos, and Bennhold (1997), which gave conflicting \(\Gamma_n/\Gamma_p\) ratios ranging from 0.07 to 0.83. It also builds on the two-pion-exchange studies of Itonaga (1994) and Shmatikov (1994), who found that including a \(\sigma\) resonance increased the ratio, but here the chiral unitary approach yields a \(\sigma\) contribution with opposite sign due to the Adler zero, leading to cancellations with the uncorrelated two-pion exchange. The results enable a consistent description of both the \(\Gamma_n/\Gamma_p\) ratio and absolute decay rates across a range of hypernuclei, and they highlight the need to correct experimental data for the \(2p2h\) channel, as discussed by Ramos (1997) and Gal (1995).
