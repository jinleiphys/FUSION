# Axial masses in quasielastic neutrino scattering and single-pion neutrinoproduction on nucleons and nuclei

> We analyse available experimental data on the total charged-current neutrino-nucleon and antineutrino-nucleon cross sections for quasielastic scattering and single-pion neutrinoproduction. Published results from the relevant experiments at ANL, BNL, FNAL, CERN, and IHEP are included dating from the end of sixties to the present day, covering muon neutrino and antineutrino beams on a variety of nuclear targets, with energies from the thresholds to about 350 GeV. The data are used to adjust the poorly known values of the axial masses.

## Key claim

The paper extracts best-fit values of the axial masses for quasielastic scattering (QES) and single-pion resonance production (RES) from a global fit to all available charged-current neutrino and antineutrino cross section data, but the authors explicitly state that these results are model-dependent and "can be recommended for use only within the same (or numerically equivalent) model assumptions as in the present analysis."

## Method

The authors performed a statistical fit of the total charged-current quasielastic and single-pion neutrinoproduction cross sections for muon neutrinos and antineutrinos on various nuclear targets, using data from experiments at ANL, BNL, FNAL, CERN, and IHEP spanning energies from threshold to about 350 GeV. For QES, they used the cross section formula from Kuzmin (2004) with the GKex(02S) and BBBA2006 parametrizations for vector form factors and a standard dipole form for the axial form factor, applying the relativistic Fermi gas model by Smith and Moniz (1972) for nuclear effects. For single-pion production, they used an extended version of the Rein-Sehgal (1981) model with 18 nucleon resonances below 2 GeV, updating all parameters according to current data and including Pauli blocking for nuclear targets.

## Key numbers

* World average axial mass for quasielastic scattering: M_A^QES = 0.95 ± 0.03 GeV (χ²/ndf = 0.92) using the GKex(02S) vector form factor model
* World average axial mass for quasielastic scattering: M_A^QES = 0.96 ± 0.03 GeV (χ²/ndf = 0.91) using the BBBA2006 vector form factor model
* World average axial mass for single-pion neutrinoproduction: M_A^RES = 1.12 ± 0.03 GeV (χ²/ndf = 1.14)
* K2K Collaboration result for quasielastic axial mass: M_A^QES = 1.20 ± 0.12 GeV, which the authors note is in "strong contradiction" with their world average
* Fit with naive dipole model for vector form factors yields M_A^QES = 0.93 ± 0.03 GeV (χ²/ndf = 0.95)

## Context

This work extends previous analyses by performing a self-consistent global fit rather than straightforward averaging over experimental values extracted under different assumptions, as done in Bernard (2001). The results contradict the K2K Collaboration result (Gran 2006) of M_A^QES = 1.20 ± 0.12 GeV, and the authors note their M_A^RES value is lower than the 1.2 GeV used in Super-Kamiokande atmospheric neutrino analyses (Ashie 2005). The analysis enables improved inputs for neutrino oscillation experiments, as the axial masses are critical parameters for modeling neutrino-nucleus interactions in detectors.
