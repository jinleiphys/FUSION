# Theory of surrogate nuclear and atomic reactions with three charged particles in the final state proceeding through a resonance in the intermediate subsystem

> Within a few-body formalism, we develop a general theory of surrogate nuclear and atomic reactions with the excitation of a resonance in the intermediate binary subsystem leading to three charged particles in the final state. The Coulomb interactions between the spectator and the resonance in the intermediate state and between the three particles in the final state are taken into account. Final-state three-body Coulomb multiple-scattering effects are accounted for using the formalism of the three-body Coulomb asymptotic states based on the work published by one of us (A.M.M.) under the guidance of L. D. Faddeev. An expression is derived for the triply differential cross section. It can be used for investigation of the Coulomb effects on the resonance line shape as well as the energy dependence of the cross section. We find that simultaneous inclusion of the Coulomb effects in the intermediate and final state decreases the effect of the final-state Coulomb interactions on the triply differential cross section.

## Key claim

The paper develops a general few-body formalism for surrogate nuclear and atomic reactions where a resonance in an intermediate binary subsystem decays into three charged particles in the final state, and finds that simultaneously including Coulomb interactions in both the intermediate and final states reduces the net effect of final-state Coulomb interactions on the triply differential cross section.

## Method

The authors start from the transition operator for the breakup reaction a + A → s + b + B and isolate the resonant term by projecting onto the ground states of the transferred particle x and the decay products b and B. They then perform a spectral decomposition of the Green's function for the s + F system, separate the continuum contribution, and extract the resonance pole in the S-matrix element S_{f i} for the binary subreaction x + A → b + B by taking the residue at the complex resonance momentum. The final-state three-body Coulomb interactions are treated using the formalism of three-body Coulomb asymptotic states developed by Mukhamedzhanov under Faddeev's guidance.

## Key numbers

- The resonance pole in the S-matrix element S_{f i} occurs at the b-B relative energy E_{R(bB)} = E_{0(bB)} - iΓ/2, where Γ is the total resonance width, and the paper assumes the resonance is narrow: Γ << E_{0(bB)}.
- The total resonance width is given by Γ = Γ_{xA} + Γ_{bB}, where Γ_{xA} and Γ_{bB} are the partial resonance widths for decay into channels i = x+A and f = b+B, respectively.
- The paper cites the specific THM reaction ¹⁴N + ¹²C → d + ²⁴Mg* → d + b + B, where b = p or α and B = ²³Na or ²⁰Ne, as an example system where Coulomb effects should have dramatic effect on the cross section.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical foundation of the Trojan Horse method (THM), an indirect technique developed by Baur (1986), Spitaleri (1990), and Mukhamedzhanov (2008) for obtaining astrophysical information about binary resonant subreactions at sub-Coulomb energies. The formalism generalizes earlier treatments by Kuchiev, Senashenko, and Godunov of post-collision Coulomb interactions in atomic surrogate reactions, and when applied to atomic cases the results coincide with those earlier works. The derived triply differential cross section enables future investigation of Coulomb effects on resonance line shapes and energy dependence in both nuclear and atomic surrogate reactions, with the transfer reaction cross section needed for the THM to be addressed in a subsequent publication.
