# Universal limiting pressure for a three-flavor color superconducting PNJL model phase diagram

> The phase diagram of a three-flavor Polyakov-loop Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model is analyzed for the case of isospin symmetric matter with color superconducting phases. The coexistence of chiral symmetry breaking and two-flavor color superconductivity (2SC phase) and a thermodynamic instability due to the implementation of a color neutrality constraint is observed. It is suggested to use a universal hadronization pressure to estimate the phase border between hadronic and quark-gluon plasma phases. Trajectories of constant entropy per baryon are analyzed for conditions appropriate for heavy-ion collisions in the NICA-FAIR energy range.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that a universal hadronization pressure of approximately 90 MeV/fm³ can be used to estimate the phase border between hadronic and quark-gluon plasma phases, thereby covering a thermodynamic instability region (negative pressure and baryon density) that arises from the color neutrality constraint in the 2SC phase.

## Method

The authors analyze the phase diagram of a three-flavor Polyakov-loop Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model with scalar, diquark, and vector interaction channels for isospin symmetric matter. They solve the mean-field gap equations self-consistently for the chiral condensates, diquark pairing gaps, and Polyakov-loop variables, while imposing color neutrality via additional equations for color chemical potentials. The model parameters are fixed by fitting vacuum meson properties (pion mass, pion decay constant, kaon mass) and the diquark and vector coupling strengths are chosen to satisfy the 2-solar-mass neutron star constraint.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass M(p=0) = 367.5 MeV used for the parameter set from Grigorian (2006)
- Model parameters: cutoff Lambda = 602.3 MeV, light quark mass m_u,d = 5.5 MeV, strange quark mass m_s = 138.757 MeV, scalar coupling G_S = 6.38427
- Diquark coupling eta_D = 1.0 and vector coupling eta_V = 0.30 chosen to satisfy the PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348-0432 mass constraints
- Universal hadronization pressure P_crit = 82 +/- 8 MeV/fm³, with the upper limit of 90 MeV/fm³ used to estimate the phase border
- Lines of constant entropy per baryon from s/n = 1 to s/n = 11 plotted for NICA-FAIR energy range conditions

## Context

This work extends previous two-flavor PNJL model studies with color superconductivity by GomezDumm (2008) to three-flavor quark matter, and builds on earlier three-flavor NJL model studies without Polyakov-loop coupling by Blaschke (2005) and Ruester (2005). The universal hadronization pressure concept follows the statistical model parametrization of Petran (2013). The results enable construction of hybrid star equations of state and provide trajectories for heavy-ion collision fireball evolution at NICA and FAIR energies, with a quadruple point where four phases meet identified by Ayriyan (2016).
