# Quadrupole deformation signatures in elastic electron scattering from oriented odd-A nuclei

> Elastic electron scattering from oriented odd-Aaxially deformed nuclei is studied in the plane-wave Born approximation. The nuclear structure is described within a microscopic selfconsistent Skyrme deformed Hartree-Fock approximation with pairing correlations. The interference form factors between monopole and quadrupole Coulomb terms that characterize the nuclear response with aligned nuclear targets are shown to increase or decrease the unpolarized cross-section, depending on the sign of the quadrupole deformation. This feature provides valuable information on the nuclear deformation that can be used as a signature of the oblate or prolate character of the nuclear shape. Some selected cases of nuclei with different spins are presented that exemplify the scope of the method.

## Key claim

Elastic electron scattering from oriented odd-A axially deformed nuclei, calculated in the plane-wave Born approximation (PWBA), shows that the interference form factor between monopole (C0) and quadrupole (C2) Coulomb terms can increase or decrease the unpolarized cross-section depending on the sign of the quadrupole deformation, providing a model-independent signature of the oblate or prolate character of the nuclear shape. The effect is demonstrated for selected cases but the paper notes that Coulomb distortion effects from the distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) will be needed for final comparison with experiment, though these are small at low momentum transfer (q ≤ 1.2 fm⁻¹) where the orientation effects are already significant.

## Method

The reaction mechanism uses the plane-wave Born approximation (PWBA) assuming one-photon exchange and plane waves for the electrons, with the longitudinal (Coulomb) response isolated from transverse contributions via Rosenbluth analysis. The nuclear structure is described by a selfconsistent Skyrme (SLy4) deformed Hartree-Fock (HF) approximation with pairing correlations in the BCS approximation, producing axially symmetric deformed charge densities whose multipole components (ρ₀ and ρ₂) are used to compute the Coulomb multipole form factors F^(Cλ)(q). The cross-section for aligned odd-A nuclei (I = k ≥ 3/2) is calculated for targets oriented at θ' = 0 relative to the momentum transfer direction, isolating the ℓ = 2 term containing the C0/C2 interference, which is proportional to the product F^(C0) × F^(C2) and carries the sign of the quadrupole deformation.

## Key numbers

- For ²³Na (I = 3/2), both prolate and oblate deformations are considered; the first peak of the interference term F_L^(ℓ=2) is about one order of magnitude smaller than (F^(C0))² and one order of magnitude larger than (F^(C2))².
- For ²⁵Mg (I = 5/2) and ⁵⁹Co (I = 7/2), only the prolate shape is studied because the oblate minima correspond to different spin-parity states.
- The statistical tensors α₂^(I_i) for aligned nuclei with P(M_i = +I_i) = P(M_i = -I_i) = 0.5 are: 1/√5 for I_i = 3/2, √(5/14) for I_i = 5/2, and √(7/15) for I_i = 7/2.
- The coefficient A(I_f) in the interference form factor F_L^(ℓ=2) = A(I_f) F^(C0) F^(C2) is -2 for I_f = 3/2, -5/7 for I_f = 5/2, and -2√(7/3) for I_f = 7/2.
- Coulomb distortion effects are stated to be small at momentum transfer q ≤ 1.2 fm⁻¹.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of charge form factors in deformed nuclei (Moya, 1999; Garrido, 2001) by focusing specifically on the C0/C2 interference term in elastic scattering from oriented odd-A nuclei, which provides a direct signature of the sign of quadrupole deformation. Previous work on ordinary (unpolarized) electron scattering (Liu, 1995; Liu, 1996; Liang, 1998; Wang, 2017) could only access |F^(C2)|², losing sign information, and found that C2 contributions manifest significantly only at diffraction minima where form factors are small. The method enables future experiments with polarized internal targets in storage rings (as described by experimental references) and upcoming facilities such as the Electron-Ion Collider at Brookhaven National Laboratory, the SCRIT project at RIKEN, and the ELISe project at GSI-FAIR, where the sign of deformation can be determined by comparing polarized and unpolarized cross-sections.
