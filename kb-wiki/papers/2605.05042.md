# Can a hybrid star with constant sound speed parametrization explain the new NICER mass-radius measurements ?

> We present a reanalysis of NICER observations of PSR J0740+6620 and PSR J0030+0451 to test the consistency of various nuclear equations of state (EoS) within the framework of hybrid star models. In particular, we examine how different surface temperature models for PSR J0030+0451, categorized as Scenarios A, B, and C, lead to significantly different mass-radius estimates. We perform a comprehensive study constraining the parameters of the constant speed of sound (CSS) model based on representative observational categories. Our findings indicate that for certain hadronic equations of state, including both density-independent and density-dependent cases, the results remain consistent for lower values of the energy density discontinuity, while discrepancies emerge as the discontinuity increases. Scenarios involving large jumps in energy density are generally disfavored by the requirement of supporting massive neutron stars, whereas higher values of the speed of sound in the quark matter phase tend to yield better agreement with observational trends. These results underscore the importance of phase transition characteristics in aligning hybrid star models with current astrophysical observations. We further constrain the CSS parameters using observational data from PSR J0740+6620 and PSR J0952-0607 by computing the maximum mass supported by these parameter sets.

# Can a hybrid star with constant sound speed parametrization explain the new NICER mass-radius measurements?

## Key claim
The constant speed of sound (CSS) model for hybrid stars can simultaneously satisfy NICER mass-radius constraints from PSR J0740+6620 and PSR J0030+0451 across multiple surface temperature scenarios, but only for lower values of the energy density discontinuity; scenarios involving large jumps in energy density are generally disfavored by the requirement of supporting massive neutron stars, while higher values of the speed of sound in the quark matter phase tend to yield better agreement with observational trends.

## Method
The authors model hybrid stars using a hadronic phase described by six relativistic mean field (RMF) parameterizations (GM1, TM1, NL3ρω4, FSU2R, BSR8, and DD2) and a quark matter phase described by the constant speed of sound (CSS) parametrization with three parameters: transition density ρ_tr, energy density discontinuity Δε, and squared speed of sound c_s^2. They compute mass-radius relations for hybrid star configurations and compare them against reanalyzed NICER data for PSR J0030+0451 under three surface temperature scenarios (A, B, C) and constraints from PSR J0740+6620, also constraining CSS parameters using maximum mass calculations for PSR J0740+6620 and PSR J0952-0607.

## Key numbers
- For PSR J0030+0451 Scenario A (ST+PST): M = 1.37^{+0.17}_{-0.17} M_⊙, R = 13.11^{+1.30}_{-1.30} km
- For PSR J0030+0451 Scenario B (ST+PDT): M = 1.20^{+0.14}_{-0.11} M_⊙, R = 11.16^{+0.90}_{-0.80} km
- For PSR J0030+0451 Scenario C (PDT-U): M = 1.41^{+0.20}_{-0.19} M_⊙, R = 13.12^{+1.35}_{-1.21} km
- For PSR J0740+6620: M = 2.07 ± 0.07 M_⊙, R = 12.49^{+1.28}_{-0.88} km
- Transition densities investigated: ρ_tr = 0.25 and 0.45 fm^{-3}
- Energy density discontinuities investigated: Δε = 50 and 150 MeV fm^{-3}
- Squared speed of sound values investigated: c_s^2 = 0.6, 0.75, and 0.9
- For the DD2 EoS with c_s^2 = 0.60, allowed Δε ranges for PSR J0740+6620 span 130–230 MeV fm^{-3} across ρ_tr = 0.25–0.45 fm^{-3}
- For the DD2 EoS with c_s^2 = 0.90, allowed Δε ranges for PSR J0740+6620 span 250–300 MeV fm^{-3} across ρ_tr = 0.25–0.45 fm^{-3}

## Context
This work extends the reanalysis of NICER observations by Vinciguerra (2023), who established a statistical hierarchy among three surface temperature models for PSR J0030+0451 (favoring Scenario C/PDT-U, ruling out Scenario A/ST+PST). It builds on the CSS parametrization approach used by Pal (2025) and others to study phase transitions in hybrid stars, and tests consistency with gravitational wave constraints from GW170817 via tidal deformability. The results enable future refinement of hybrid star models by identifying which combinations of transition density, energy density discontinuity, and quark matter stiffness remain viable under current multi-messenger observations.
