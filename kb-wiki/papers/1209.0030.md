# Baryon masses and axial charges in 1/Nc - ChPT

> An analysis is presented of the ground state baryon's (N and Δ) masses and axial couplings in the combined 1/Nc and chiral expansions. Renormalization and alternative power counting schemes are discussed to one loop (NNLO). An application is made to lattice QCD results for non-strange baryon masses and the nucleon axial coupling as functions of the pion mass. This work represents a brief summary of a more extensive analysis to be presented elsewhere.

## Key claim

The combined 1/Nc and chiral expansion (the "ξ-expansion") improves the low-energy description of baryons by incorporating the spin-flavor symmetry constraints of large Nc QCD, but the chiral and 1/Nc expansions do not commute and must be locked by fixing the hyperfine splitting to be of order p/Λχ, which is a specific power-counting choice that differs from the strict large Nc limit.

## Method

The authors construct a heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) Lagrangian for ground-state baryons (N and Δ) using the SU(4) spin-flavor multiplet B, with the hyperfine mass splitting δmHF = (CHF/Nc)Ŝ² treated as order 1/Nc. They calculate baryon self-energies to one loop (order ξ³) and axial-current corrections to one loop (order ξ²) using dimensional regularization, then perform a combined fit to lattice QCD results for N and Δ masses from PACS-CS and the nucleon axial charge from LHPC.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant is taken as Fπ = 92.4 MeV
- The physical nucleon axial coupling gA (standard normalization) is 1.267, but the gA used in the Lagrangian is a factor 6/5 larger due to generator normalization
- The 1-loop calculation gives baryon masses up to order ξ³ and axial charges up to order ξ²
- The fit to lattice results extrapolates gA^NN to a value 12% smaller than the physical value of 1.267
- The low-energy constant c1 changes by more than an estimated 30% when increasing the fit order by one unit in ξ

## Context

This work extends the combined 1/Nc and chiral expansion approach developed by Bedaque (1995) and Flores-Mendieta (2000), which itself built on the realization by Jenkins (1990) that including spin-3/2 baryons improves the baryon chiral expansion and the large Nc spin-flavor symmetry identified by 't Hooft (1973) and Dashen (1993). The analysis enables chiral extrapolations of lattice QCD results for baryon masses and axial couplings, and the authors note that future lattice calculations of gA^NΔ and gA^ΔΔ (pioneered by Dina) and calculations at larger Nc (initiated by DeGrand) will provide further tests of the approach.
