# Parton physics from a heavy-quark operator product expansion: Lattice QCD calculation of the second moment of the pion distribution amplitude

> The pion light-cone distribution amplitude (LCDA) is a central non-perturbative object of interest for high-energy exclusive processes in quantum chromodynamics. In this article, the second Mellin moment of the pion LCDA is determined as a proof-of-concept calculation for the first numerical implementation of the heavy-quark operator product expansion (HOPE) method. The resulting value for the second Mellin moment, determined in quenched QCD at a pion mass of $m_π=550$ MeV at a factorization scale of 2 GeV is $ \langle ξ^2 \rangle = 0.210 \pm 0.013\text{ (stat.)} \pm 0.034\text{ (sys.)}$. This result is compatible with those from previous determinations of this quantity.

## Key claim
The second Mellin moment of the pion light-cone distribution amplitude is determined as a proof-of-concept calculation for the first numerical implementation of the heavy-quark operator product expansion (HOPE) method, using quenched QCD at an unphysical pion mass of 550 MeV rather than at the physical pion mass.

## Method
The HOPE method extracts the second Mellin moment by computing hadronic amplitudes involving two axial-vector currents with a fictitious valence heavy quark, then performing an operator product expansion in the kinematic regime where the scaling variable is small. Lattice QCD calculations were performed at four lattice spacings ranging from 0.04 fm to 0.08 fm using quenched gauge configurations, with heavy quark masses between approximately 2 GeV and 4.5 GeV to control higher-twist effects. The antisymmetric combination of the hadronic tensor was used to reduce statistical noise, and target mass corrections were resummed using the Nachtmann prescription.

## Key numbers
- Second Mellin moment: ⟨ξ²⟩ = 0.210 ± 0.013 (stat.) ± 0.034 (sys.) at a factorization scale of 2 GeV
- Pion mass used: mπ = 550 MeV
- Lattice spacings: 0.0407 fm, 0.0502 fm, 0.0600 fm, and 0.0813 fm
- Physical volume: 1.92 fm (constant across ensembles)
- Heavy quark mass range: approximately 2 GeV to 4.5 GeV
- Lattice sizes: 24³ × 48, 32³ × 64, 40³ × 80, and 48³ × 96

## Context
This work extends the HOPE method proposed by Detmold (2005) from a theoretical framework to a numerical implementation, providing an alternative to conventional local operator approaches that suffer from power divergences in renormalization for higher moments. The result is compatible with previous determinations from Del Debbio (2003), Arthur (2011), Bali (2017), Zhang (2017), and Bali (2019). This proof-of-concept calculation enables future applications of HOPE to extract the full ξ-dependence of the pion distribution amplitude through perturbative matching, and can be extended to physical pion masses and unquenched QCD.
