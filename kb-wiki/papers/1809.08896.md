# $\bar{K}\bar{D} N$ molecular state as a "$u u d s \bar{c}$ pentaquark" in a three-body calculation

> We predict a new three-body hadronic molecule composed of antikaon $\bar{K}$, anticharm meson $\bar{D}$, and nucleon $N$ with spin/parity $J^{P} = 1/2^{+}$ and isospin $I = 1/2$. This state behaves like an explicit pentaquark state because its minimal quark configuration is $u u d s \bar{c}$ or $u d d s \bar{c}$. Owing to the attraction between every pair of two hadrons, in particular the $\bar{K}\bar{D}$ attraction which dynamically generates $D_{s 0} (2317)^{-}$ and $\bar{K} N$ attraction which dynamically generates $Λ(1405)$, the $\bar{K}\bar{D} N$ system is bound, and its eigenenergy is calculated as $3244 - 17 i$ MeV in a nonrelativistic three-body potential model. We discuss properties of this $\bar{K} \bar{D} N$ quasibound state which emerge uniquely in three-body dynamics.

## Key claim

The paper predicts a new three-body hadronic molecule composed of antikaon, anticharm meson, and nucleon with spin/parity \(J^{P} = 1/2^{+}\) and isospin \(I = 1/2\), which behaves as an explicit pentaquark state with minimal quark configuration \(u u d s \bar{c}\) or \(u d d s \bar{c}\); the claim is conditional on the assumption that the attraction between every pair of two hadrons, particularly the \(\bar{K}\bar{D}\) attraction generating \(D_{s0}(2317)^{-}\) and the \(\bar{K}N\) attraction generating \(\Lambda(1405)\), is sufficient to bind the three-body system.

## Method

The authors constructed effective local two-body potentials for the \(\bar{K}N\), \(\bar{K}\bar{D}\), and \(\bar{D}N\) subsystems by integrating out inelastic channels from full coupled-channels interactions, using the Kyoto \(\bar{K}N\) potential from chiral SU(3) dynamics, a phenomenological Lagrangian for \(\bar{K}\bar{D}\) that dynamically generates \(D_{s0}(2317)^{-}\), and a heavy-quark-symmetric model for \(\bar{D}N\) that generates a loosely bound \(\bar{D}N\) state. They then solved a nonrelativistic three-body Schrödinger equation for the \(\bar{K}\bar{D}N\) system in coordinate space using the Gaussian expansion method with three Jacobi coordinate rearrangements, assuming zero orbital angular momenta for both Jacobi modes and no three-body forces.

## Key numbers

- The \(\bar{K}\bar{D}N\) quasibound state eigenenergy is calculated as \(3244 - 17i\) MeV.
- The \(\bar{K}N\) (\(I=0\)) effective potential generates two \(\Lambda(1405)\) poles at \(\epsilon_{\text{pole}} = 1424 - 26i\) MeV and \(1381 - 81i\) MeV.
- The \(\bar{K}\bar{D}\) (\(I=0\)) interaction generates \(D_{s0}(2317)^{-}\) with mass \(\epsilon_{\text{pole}} = 2317\) MeV.
- The \(\bar{D}N\) (\(I=0\)) bound state has binding energy \(\sim 1\) MeV, with \(\epsilon_{\text{pole}} = 2805\) MeV.
- The average \(\bar{K}\bar{D}\) distance in \(D_{s0}(2317)^{-}\) is \(\sqrt{\langle r^{2} \rangle_{\bar{K}\bar{D}}} = 0.93\) fm.
- The average \(\bar{D}N\) distance in the \(\bar{D}N\) bound state is \(\sqrt{\langle r^{2} \rangle_{\bar{D}N}} = 3.66\) fm.
- The range parameter for the \(\bar{K}N\) potential is \(b = 0.38\) fm; for \(\bar{K}\bar{D}\), \(b = 0.36\) fm; for \(\bar{D}N\), \(b = 0.26\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the study of three-body hadronic molecules, following predictions and observations of the \(\bar{K}NN\) quasibound state by Akaishi (2002), Shevchenko (2006), and others, and the recent J-PARC E15 experiment by Sada (2016) and Ajimura (2018). It also builds on two-body molecular candidates \(\Lambda(1405)\) (Dalitz, 1960) and \(D_{s0}(2317)^{-}\) (Aubert, 2003; Abe, 2003), and on prior studies of similar three-body systems such as \(\bar{K}\bar{K}N\), \(K\bar{K}N\), and \(KDN\). The prediction enables experimental searches for a pentaquark with open charm and strangeness, and provides a testbed for three-body dynamics including energy-dependent two-body interactions.
