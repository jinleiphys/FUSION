# Properties of $K^π=0^+_1$, $K^π=2^-$, and $K^π=0^-_1$ bands of $^{20}$Ne probed via proton and alpha inelastic scattering

> The $K^π=0^+_1$, $K^π=2^-$, and $K^π=0^-_1$ bands of $^{20}$Ne are investigated with microscopic structure and reaction calculations via proton and $α$ inelastic scattering off $^{20}$Ne. Structures of $^{20}$Ne are calculated with variation after parity and total angular momentum projections in the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics(AMD). The $K^π=0^+_1$ and $K^π=0^-_1$ bands have $^{16}\textrm{O}+α$ cluster structures, whereas the $K^π=2^-$ band shows a $^{12}\textrm{C}+2α$-like structure. Microscopic coupled-channel calculations of proton and $α$ scattering off $^{20}$Ne are performed by using the proton-nucleus and $α$-nucleus potentials, which are derived by folding the Melbourne $g$-matrix $NN$ interaction with AMD densities of $^{20}$Ne. The calculation reasonably reproduces the observed cross sections of proton scattering at $E_p=25$--35 MeV and $α$ scattering at $E_α=104$--386 MeV. Transition properties from the ground to excited states are discussed by reaction analyses of proton and $α$ inelastic processes. Mixing of the $K^π=2^-$ and $K^π=0^-_1$ bands is discussed by detailed analysis of the $0^+_1\to 3^-_1$ and $0^+_1\to 3^-_2$ transitions. For the $3^-_1$ state, mixing of the $K^π=0^-_1$ cluster component in the $K^π=2^-$ band plays an important role in the transition properties.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the anomalously large observed $B(E3;3^-_1\to 0^+_1)$ transition strength in $^{20}$Ne, which is inconsistent with a simple particle-hole interpretation of the $K^\pi=2^-$ band, can be explained by mixing between the $K^\pi=2^-$ and $K^\pi=0^-_1$ bands, where the $3^-_1$ state acquires a significant $^{16}\mathrm{O}+\alpha$ cluster component from the $K^\pi=0^-_1$ band.

## Method

The authors performed antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) calculations with variation after parity and total angular momentum projections (VAP) to obtain structure wave functions for the $K^\pi=0^+_1$, $K^\pi=2^-$, and $K^\pi=0^-_1$ bands of $^{20}$Ne. They then used these AMD densities as inputs for microscopic coupled-channel (MCC) calculations of proton and $\alpha$ inelastic scattering, where the nucleon-nucleus and $\alpha$-nucleus potentials were constructed by folding the Melbourne $g$-matrix $NN$ interaction with the AMD densities. The theoretical transition densities were renormalized to fit experimental $B(E\lambda)$ values where known.

## Key numbers

- $B(E3;3^-_1\to 0^+_1)$ = 260(90) $e^2$fm$^6$ from experiment; AMD (default) gives 53 $e^2$fm$^6$, AMD-ls34 (with modified spin-orbit strength) gives 155 $e^2$fm$^6$, and CC-OCM gives 29.9 $e^2$fm$^6$
- $B(E3;3^-_2\to 0^+_1)$ = 543 $e^2$fm$^6$ from CM, 548 $e^2$fm$^6$ from AMD, and 335 $e^2$fm$^6$ from AMD-ls34
- $B(E2;2^+_1\to 0^+_1)$ = 65.4(3.2) $e^2$fm$^4$ from experiment; AMD gives 69 $e^2$fm$^4$, CM gives 53 $e^2$fm$^4$
- $B(E2;3^-_1\to 2^-_1)$ = 113(29) $e^2$fm$^4$ from experiment; AMD gives 95 $e^2$fm$^4$, AMD-ls34 gives 89 $e^2$fm$^4$
- Excitation energies: $3^-_1$ at 5.621 MeV (experiment), $3^-_2$ at 7.156 MeV (experiment); AMD gives 9.0 MeV and 7.5 MeV respectively
- Root-mean-square matter radii: $0^+_1$ = 3.01 fm (AMD), $1^-_1$ = 3.20 fm (AMD), $2^-_1$ = 2.94 fm (AMD)
- Proton scattering energies: $E_p$ = 25, 30, 35 MeV; $\alpha$ scattering energies: $E_\alpha$ = 104, 146, 386 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous microscopic coupled-channel studies by Kanada-En'yo and Ogata (2019-2020) that applied MCC calculations with AMD densities to proton and $\alpha$ scattering off $p$- and $sd$-shell nuclei. It addresses the long-standing discrepancy between the experimental $B(E3;3^-_1\to 0^+_1)$ value measured by Hausser (1971) and the much smaller theoretical values from the coupled-channel orthogonal condition model (CC-OCM) of Fujiwara (1979) and deformed-basis AMD of Kimura (2003). The results enable future combined structure-reaction analyses of cluster states in light nuclei, particularly for understanding band mixing between mean-field-type and cluster-type excitations.
