# $E1-E2$ interference in the Coulomb dissociation of $^8$B

> We investigate the effects arising out of the $E1 - E2$ interference in the Coulomb dissociation of $^8$B at beam energies below and around 50 MeV/nucleon. The theory has been formulated within a first order semiclassical scheme of Coulomb excitation, in which both the ground state and the continuum state wave functions of $^8$B enter as inputs. We find that the magnitude of the interference could be large in some cases. However, there are some specific observables which are free from the effects of the $E1 - E2$ interference, which is independent of the models used to describe the structure of $^8$B. This will be useful for the analysis of the breakup data in relation to the extraction of the astrophysical factor $S_{17}(0)$.

## Key claim

The magnitude of the E1-E2 interference term in the Coulomb dissociation of 8B can be large in some observables (such as triple differential cross sections), but any cross section obtained by integrating over the solid angle of the relative motion of the fragments is free from this interference, a result that is independent of the structure model used for 8B.

## Method

The authors developed a first-order semiclassical theory of Coulomb excitation that calculates triple differential cross sections for the breakup of a composite projectile into a core and valence particle in the Coulomb field of a target nucleus. The formalism uses a detailed coupling scheme of angular momenta and spins for the initial and final states, with ground-state and continuum wave functions of 8B obtained from a single-particle potential model (SPPM) with a Woods-Saxon plus spin-orbit potential. The calculations separate pure E1, pure E2, and E1-E2 interference contributions, and the theory does not assume straight-line trajectories or isotropic fragment emission.

## Key numbers

- One-proton separation energy of 8B: 0.137 MeV, used to adjust the ground-state well depth to -44.658 MeV.
- Observed 1+ and 3+ resonances in 8B at 0.637 MeV and 2.183 MeV, respectively, reproduced by adjusting well depths to -42.14 MeV and -36.80 MeV.
- RMS core-proton relative distance in 8B: 4.24 fm; RMS size of 8B: 2.64 fm.
- For the 8B + Pb system at 46.5 MeV/nucleon, the E1-E2 interference contribution in the triple differential cross section is larger than the pure E2 contribution at E_cv = 0.2 MeV and theta_cv = 1 degree.
- For the 8B + Ni system at 25.8 MeV beam energy, the E1-E2 interference term in the triple differential cross section d^3sigma/(dE_7Be dOmega_7Be dOmega_p) is even larger than the dipole component at E_7Be = 22.575 MeV, theta_p = 20 degrees, and phi_p = 0 degrees.

## Context

This work extends earlier semiclassical treatments of Coulomb excitation by Baur and Weber (1989) by using a more general coupling scheme of angular momenta and spins, and by not assuming straight-line trajectories as in the calculations of Esbensen and Bertsch (1995) and Bertulani (1996). The paper validates the isotropic emission approximation used in earlier analyses of RIKEN data by Shyam and Thompson (1999) and shows that those analyses are indeed free from E1-E2 interference effects. The results enable more reliable extraction of the astrophysical S-factor S_17(0) from Coulomb dissociation data of 8B, particularly for the RIKEN data at beam energies around 50 MeV/nucleon and forward scattering angles below 4 degrees.
