# The effect of Lorentz-like force on collective flows of K$^{+}$ in Au+Au collisions at 1.5 GeV/nucleon}{The effect of Lorentz-like force on collective flows of $K^{+}$ in Au+Au collisions at 1.5 GeV/nucleon

> Producing kaon mesons in heavy-ion collisions at beam energies below their threshold energy is an important way to investigate the properties of dense nuclear matter. In this study, based on the newly updated version of the ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics model, we introduce the kaon-nucleon (KN) potential, including both the scalar and vector (also dubbed Lorentz-like) aspects. We revisit the influence of the KN potential on the collective flow of K$^{+}$ mesons produced in Au+Au collisions at $E_{lab}$ = 1.5 GeV/nucleon and find that the contribution of the newly included Lorentz-like force is very important, particulary for describing the directed flow of K$^{+}$. Finally, the corresponding KaoS data of both directed and elliptic flows can be simultaneously reproduced well.

## Key claim
The Lorentz-like force (the spatial component of the kaon vector field) is essential for simultaneously reproducing the KaoS experimental data for both directed and elliptic flow of K$^+$ mesons in Au+Au collisions at 1.5 GeV/nucleon; without this force, the calculated directed flow becomes too negative and disagrees with the data.

## Method
The authors used the updated ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) model with a Skyrme potential energy density functional (SV-mas08 interaction, incompressibility K0 = 233 MeV). They introduced a kaon-nucleon (KN) potential including both scalar and vector (Lorentz-like) components, where the vector field depends on the baryon four-vector current and the kaon effective momentum. They simulated three million events for Au+Au collisions at 1.5 GeV/nucleon within an impact parameter range of 5.9 to 10.2 fm, and compared calculations without the KN potential, with the static KN potential only, and with the full KN potential including the Lorentz-like force.

## Key numbers
- Inverse slope parameter T from Maxwell-Boltzmann fit: 85 ± 2 MeV (without KN potential), 105 ± 3 MeV (with KN potential but without Lorentz-like force), and 101 ± 2 MeV (with both KN potential and Lorentz-like force). Experimental values are 111 ± 2 MeV (Forster 2007) and 116 ± 7 MeV (Forster 2003).
- The kaon vector potential parameter b_K = 3/(8f_pi^2) ≈ 0.333 GeV fm^3, assuming f_pi = 93 MeV.
- The scalar parameters a_K = 0.18 GeV^2 fm^3 and a_anti-K = 0.31 GeV^2 fm^3, giving kaon optical potential U_K ≈ 25 MeV and antikaon optical potential U_anti-K ≈ -100 MeV at nuclear saturation density.
- Impact parameter range for the analysis: 5.9 < b < 10.2 fm.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of subthreshold kaon production as a probe of dense nuclear matter, following the first measurement by Miskowiec (1994) and subsequent theoretical work using IQMD and LQMD models (Hartnack 2005, Feng 2011). The authors specifically address the finding from their prior work (LiMiao 2017) that the directed flow slope of K^+ becomes too negative when only the scalar KN potential is included. The results enable more reliable extraction of the nuclear equation of state from kaon flow data and provide a benchmark for future studies of antikaon potentials, which remain highly uncertain (varying from -40 MeV to -200 MeV at normal density according to Schaffner 1996, Li 1997, and others).
