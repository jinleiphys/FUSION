# Classical Fields Near Thermal Equilibrium

> We discuss the classical limit for the long-distance (``soft'') modes of a quantum field when the hard modes of the field are in thermal equilibrium. We address the question of the correct semiclassical dynamics when a momentum cut-off is introduced. Higher order contributions leads to a stochastic interpretation for the effective action in analogy to Quantum Brownian Motion, resulting in dissipation and decoherence for the evolution of the soft modes. Particular emphasis is put on the understanding of dissipation. Our discussion focuses mostly on scalar fields, but we make some remarks on the extension to gauge theories.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the correct semiclassical dynamics for long-wavelength ("soft") modes of a quantum field, when a momentum cutoff \(k_c \ll T\) is introduced, requires a stochastic interpretation of the effective action analogous to Quantum Brownian Motion, with dissipation and decoherence emerging from the coupling to hard thermal modes; this goes beyond the naive expectation that classical field equations suffice for highly occupied modes.

## Method

The authors divide a scalar quantum field with quartic self-interaction into soft modes (\(|\mathbf{p}| \le k_c\)) and hard modes (\(|\mathbf{p}| > k_c\)), assuming the hard modes remain in thermal equilibrium. Using the closed-time-path (CTP) method and the influence functional formalism of Feynman and Vernon, they integrate out the hard modes perturbatively up to order \(g^4\) to derive an effective stochastic equation of motion for the soft modes. They then analyze the dissipative terms by inserting a harmonic (quasi-particle) approximation for the soft mode time dependence to obtain a Markovian limit.

## Key numbers

* The average thermal occupation number of a low-momentum mode approaches \(n(\omega_{\mathbf{p}}) \to T/m^* \gg 1\) in the classical equipartition limit as \(|\mathbf{p}| \to 0\).
* The energy density of a classical field scales as \(\epsilon(T) \sim T k_c^3\) in the high-temperature limit, contrasting with the quantum result \(\epsilon(T) \sim T^4\).
* The dynamical mass gap scales as \(m^{*2} \sim (g^2/2) T \hbar k_c\) in the classical limit, versus \(m^{*2} \sim g^2 T^2\) in the full quantum theory.
* The cutoff scale must satisfy \(k_c \ll T\), and may be chosen comparable to the dynamical mass scale \(m^* \sim gT\).
* Quantal loop corrections from soft modes are suppressed by a factor \(\hbar k_c/T \ll 1\) relative to thermal fluctuations.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Bodeker, McLerran, and Smilga (1995) and Lombardo and Mazzitelli (1995) by deriving a complete stochastic, dissipative effective action for soft modes from first principles, rather than assuming a Langevin form. It contrasts with the Euclidean dimensional reduction method of Braaten and Nieto (1994, 1995), noting that Minkowski-space dynamics retains full (3+1) dimensionality because there is no gap in the excitation spectrum. The results enable systematic numerical treatment of nonperturbative long-distance dynamics in non-Abelian gauge theories at high temperature, where quantal corrections among soft modes are minor but classical thermal effects dominate.
