# Stopping and the K/pi horn

> We propose a non-equilibrium hadronic model to interpret the observed excitation function of kaon-to-pion ratios in nuclear collisions at AGS and SPS energies. The crucial assumption of our model is that due to stronger stopping at lower energies the lifetime of the fireball is prolonged because the system has to build up the longitudinal expansion from internal pressure.

## Key claim

The observed sharp peak ("horn") in the excitation function of the kaon-to-pion ratio at 30 AGeV can be reproduced by a purely hadronic non-equilibrium model without invoking a phase transition to deconfined matter, provided that the total lifetime of the fireball decreases with increasing collision energy due to weaker nuclear stopping.

## Method

The authors constructed a kinetic model that tracks the evolution of spatially averaged kaon densities using a parametrized expansion of the fireball, with the expansion rate tuned to quantify stopping and re-acceleration. They included hadronic reactions producing and destroying kaons (e.g., πN ↔ KY, ππ ↔ K̄K, NN → KNY) and assumed all non-strange species are chemically equilibrated, while strange species are balanced by strangeness neutrality. The initial energy density and total fireball lifetime were varied to fit measured particle ratios at AGS and SPS energies.

## Key numbers

- The "horn" peak in the ⟨K⁺⟩/⟨π⁺⟩ ratio occurs at projectile energy of 30 AGeV.
- At the lowest studied energy, 11.6 AGeV (AGS), the model misses the ⟨Λ⟩/⟨π⟩ ratio by about 3 standard deviations.
- At the top SPS energy √s_NN = 17 AGeV, the neglect of antibaryons introduces an estimated 10% error, considered acceptable for a schematic model.
- The initial energy density dependence is "completely irrelevant at the highest SPS energy" and gains more weight at lower energies.

## Context

This work extends the search for an explanation of the "horn" observed by the CERN SPS energy scan (Lung, 2005), which the statistical hadronisation model (Cleymans, 2004) cannot reproduce in sharpness and which hadronic transport codes (Bleicher, Cassing) also fail to match. The only previous successful interpretation used the Statistical Model of the Early Stage (SMES), which assumes early chemical equilibrium and interprets the peak as a deconfinement signature; the present work shows that a hadronic non-equilibrium scenario with energy-dependent fireball lifetime can also reproduce the data, challenging the deconfinement interpretation and motivating checks against dilepton spectra (CERES, NA60) and other observables.
