# Quark deconfinement transition in hyperonic matter

> We discuss the properties of the hadron-quark mixed phase in compact stars using a realistic equation of state of hyperonic matter and the MIT bag model. We find that the equation of state of the mixed phase is similar to that given by the Maxwell construction, but that the mixed phase becomes mechanically unstable if the surface tension of the interface between the two pure phases is strong enough. The composition of the mixed phase is very different from that of the Maxwell construction; in particular, hyperons are completely suppressed.

## Key claim

The hadron-quark mixed phase in hyperonic matter has an equation of state similar to that given by the Maxwell construction, but the mixed phase becomes mechanically unstable if the surface tension of the interface between the two pure phases exceeds approximately 65 MeV/fm²; in the mixed phase, hyperons are completely suppressed.

## Method

The authors combined a Brueckner-Hartree-Fock equation of state for hyperonic hadronic matter (using the Argonne V18 nucleon-nucleon potential, Urbana UIX three-body forces, and Nijmegen NSC89 hyperon-nucleon potentials) with the MIT bag model for quark matter (massless u and d quarks, massive s quark with m_s = 150 MeV). They employed a Wigner-Seitz approximation with Thomas-Fermi density profiles and solved the Poisson equation for the Coulomb potential, searching for the minimum energy density by varying the cell geometry, cell size, lump size, and density profiles at each baryon density.

## Key numbers

- The quark deconfinement transition in the mixed phase starts at baryon density ρ_B = 0.326 fm⁻³ with a quark droplet structure and ends at ρ_B = 0.666 fm⁻³ with a hadron bubble structure.
- The surface tension parameter used in the main calculation is τ_surf = 50 MeV/fm², with theoretical estimates suggesting a range of τ_surf ≈ 10–100 MeV/fm².
- The mixed phase becomes mechanically unstable above a limiting surface tension of τ_surf ≈ 65 MeV/fm².
- The bag constant used is B = 100 MeV/fm³ and the QCD fine structure constant α_s = 0.
- The threshold density for hyperons in pure nucleon matter is ρ_B = 0.34 fm⁻³.
- In a 3D Wigner-Seitz cell at ρ_B = 0.4 fm⁻³, the cell radius is R_W = 26.7 fm and the droplet radius is R = 17.3 fm.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the hadron-quark phase transition in compact stars, specifically comparing the Maxwell construction (sharp transition) with the Gibbs/Glendenning construction (mixed phase with geometrical structures). The authors build on the work of Voskresensky et al., who showed that charge screening effects can cause the Coulomb energy to change sign and behave like R⁻¹ at large radii, in contrast to the earlier work of Heiselberg et al. that neglected charge screening. The results are important for understanding neutrino propagation, baryonic superfluidity, and the maximum mass problem in neutron stars, and the authors plan to study these in an extended article.
