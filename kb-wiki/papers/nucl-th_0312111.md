# Spectra of anti-nucleons in the vacuum of finite nuclei

> The quantum vacuum in a many-body system of finite nuclei has been investigated within the relativistic Hartree approach which describes the bound states of nucleons and anti-nucleons consistently. The contributions of the Dirac sea to the source terms of the meson-field equations are taken into account up to the one-nucleon loop and one-meson loop. The tensor couplings for the $ω$- and $ρ$-meson are included in the model. After adjusting the parameters of the model to the properties of spherical nuclei, a large effective nucleon mass $m^{*}/M_{N} \approx 0.78$ is obtained. The overall nucleon spectra of shell-model states are in agreement with the data. The computed anti-nucleon spectra in the vacuum differ about 20 -- 30 MeV with and without the tensor-coupling effects.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including tensor couplings for the ω- and ρ-meson in a relativistic Hartree approach with Dirac-sea contributions yields anti-nucleon spectra that differ by 20–30 MeV compared to calculations without those couplings, while maintaining a large effective nucleon mass of m*/M_N ≈ 0.78.

## Method

The authors developed a relativistic Hartree approach that treats nucleon and anti-nucleon bound states consistently, including Dirac-sea contributions up to the one-nucleon loop and one-meson loop. The model incorporates tensor couplings for the ω- and ρ-meson, and the meson-field equations are solved self-consistently with the Dirac equation for finite spherical nuclei. Parameters were fixed by a least-square fit to the properties of eight spherical nuclei, producing two parameter sets: RHAT (with tensor couplings) and RHA1 (without tensor couplings).

## Key numbers

- Effective nucleon mass: m*/M_N ≈ 0.78, obtained after parameter adjustment to spherical nuclei properties.
- Anti-nucleon spectra computed with the RHAT parameter set are 20–30 MeV larger than those computed with the RHA1 set (without tensor couplings).
- Calculations were performed for three spherical nuclei: ¹⁶O, ⁴⁰Ca, and ²⁰⁸Pb.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory beyond the no-sea approximation used in earlier work by Serot and Walecka (1986), Gamow (1990), and others, by explicitly including Dirac-sea contributions. It also builds on the relativistic Hartree approach (RHA) developed by Horowitz and Serot (1984) and others. The consistent treatment of nucleon and anti-nucleon bound states enables determination of the individual scalar and vector potentials S and V, which is a prerequisite for studying anti-matter and anti-nuclei in relativistic heavy-ion collisions as discussed by Bea (2000) and Arm (2000).
