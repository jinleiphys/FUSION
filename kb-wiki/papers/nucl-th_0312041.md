# Meson Exchange Currents in (\vec{e},e'\vec{p}) observables

> Our main aim in this work is to study the effects of meson-exchange currents (MEC) over asymmetries and recoil polarization observables in A(e,e'p)B reactions. This DWIA+MEC model takes care of relativistic degrees of freedom by using semi-relativistic operators for the one-body current as well as for the two-body MEC. These SR currents are obtained by a direct Pauli reduction of the corresponding relativistic operators by an expansion in terms of missing momentum over the nucleon mass, while the current dependence on the transferred energy and momentum is treated exactly. Also some comparisons with experimental data are made.

## Key claim

Meson-exchange current (MEC) effects on asymmetries and recoil polarization observables in A(e,e'p)B reactions are generally small for low missing momentum but become appreciable for missing momentum above 300 MeV/c, though in this region dynamical relativistic effects are also essential and a fully relativistic model including MEC would be desirable.

## Method

The authors compute semi-relativistic (SR) one-body and two-body MEC operators via a direct Pauli reduction of relativistic operators, expanded in missing momentum over nucleon mass to order O(p/m_N), while treating the dependence on transferred energy and momentum exactly. Final-state interactions are incorporated through a phenomenological optical potential derived from the Schrödinger-equivalent form of a S-V Dirac optical potential, and the model is applied to proton knockout from the p_{1/2} and p_{3/2} shells of ¹⁶O.

## Key numbers

- For ¹⁶O proton knockout at q = 995 MeV/c and ω = 439 MeV, MEC effects on the A_TL asymmetry become important for missing momentum p ≥ 300 MeV/c.
- For the p_{1/2} shell at intermediate momentum transfer, the polarized response functions W^{TT}_t and W^{TT}_n are reduced by approximately 20% and 30% respectively due to MEC.
- For q = 1 GeV/c and ω = 450 MeV, with electron energy ε_e = 2450 MeV and φ = 180°, MEC effects lead to a global reduction of transferred polarization observables.
- MEC effects are negligible for the p_{3/2} shell at the same kinematics where p_{1/2} shows 20-30% reductions.

## Context

This work extends the DWIA+MEC model developed by Amaro et al. (2003) and Tabatabaei et al. (2003) to study polarization observables, building on earlier semi-relativistic approaches by Amaro et al. (2002). The results are compared with experimental data from Gao et al. (2000) and Malov et al. (2000) from TJlab, and the authors note that MEC effects bring their calculations closer to the fully relativistic results of Malov et al. (2000). The work enables future development of a completely relativistic model including MEC to properly describe the high missing momentum region.
