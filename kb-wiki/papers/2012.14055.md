# Shape of 13C studied by the real-time evolution method

> Background : Recently, Bijker et al. [Phys. Rev. Lett. 122, 162501 (2019)] explained the rotation-vibration spectrum of 13C by assuming triangular nuclear shape with D'3h symmetry. Purpose : The purpose of this work is to test the shape and symmetry of 13C based on a microscopic nuclear model without assumption of nuclear shape. Method : We have applied the real-time evolution method to 13C. By using the equation-of-motion of clusters, the model describes the 3alpha+n system without any assumption of symmetry. Results : REM described the low-lying states more accurately than the previous cluster model studies. The analysis of the wave functions showed that the ground band has approximate triangular symmetry, while the excited bands deviate from it. Conclusion : This work confirmed that the ground band has the intrinsic structure with the triangular arrangement of three alpha particles.

# Shape of 13C studied by the real-time evolution method

## Key claim
The ground band of 13C has an intrinsic structure with triangular arrangement of three alpha particles, confirming the D'3h symmetry proposed by Bijker et al. (2019), but the excited bands deviate from rigid triangular symmetry and exhibit non-small admixture of other configurations.

## Method
The real-time evolution method (REM) was applied to the 3alpha+n system by solving the equation-of-motion for Gaussian wave packet centroids from time-dependent variational principle, generating 300 basis wave functions without any symmetry assumption. These basis states were superposed using the generator coordinate method (GCM) after parity and angular momentum projection, with the Volkov No.2 force for the central nucleon-nucleon interaction and G3RS force for the spin-orbit part at two strengths (u_ls=1000 and 2000 MeV).

## Key numbers
- The minimum intrinsic energy of the 3alpha+n system was found to be -83.1 MeV
- The ground state (1/2_1^-) has maximum overlap of 0.83 with a basis wave function showing triangular configuration with side lengths 3.31, 3.30, and 3.02 fm
- REM yields approximately 1 MeV deeper binding energy for the 1/2_1^- state than the previous study by Furutachi et al. (2011) using the same Hamiltonian
- Intra-band E2 transition strength for 1/2_1^- to 3/2_1^- is 17.4 e^2 fm^4 (experimental: 12.7 e^2 fm^4)
- Intra-band E2 transition strength for 1/2_1^- to 5/2_1^- is 17.1 e^2 fm^4 (experimental: 16.9 e^2 fm^4)
- Inter-band E2 transition strength for 1/2_1^+ to 5/2_1^+ is 6.7 e^2 fm^4 (experimental: 9.0 e^2 fm^4)
- Total propagation time was 10,000 fm/c with wave functions recorded every 33 fm/c, generating 300 wave functions per ensemble
- The size parameter b was fixed to 1.46 fm

## Context
This work tests the triangular D'3h symmetry proposed by Bijker et al. (2019) using the algebraic cluster model (ACM) for 13C, but without assuming any symmetry a priori. The REM approach extends the method of Imai et al. (2019) from N-alpha systems to the non-N-alpha 3alpha+n system, and provides a benchmark calculation that yields deeper binding energies than the previous cluster model study by Furutachi et al. (2011). The results confirm the ground band triangular symmetry but show that excited bands deviate from rigid-body interpretation, suggesting that experimental E2 transitions between bands will provide crucial tests of the underlying symmetry.
