# Contributions from weak-interaction channels to inclusive hadron spectra at TeV energies

> At the FERMILAB TEVATRON and CERN LHC accelerators partonic processes reach such a high energy region, where the weak interactions could enter into consideration in the hadron yields through the opening of resonant W and Z exchange diagrams. We investigate these contributions to inclusive charged hadron production in p+p and p+{\bar p} reactions at a few TeV energies, and compare our results to existing CDF and CMS data. We perform a leading order perturbative QCD calculation and include electroweak channels to estimate the order of magnitude of charged hadron excess. The energy dependence of hadron production and the fine structure of transverse momentum distributions are investigated. As expected, in p+ {\bar p} collisions electroweak channels yield much larger contribution, than in p+p collisions. However, these types of contributions remains close to be negligible at the available accelerator energies.

# Contributions from weak-interaction channels to inclusive hadron spectra at TeV energies

## Key claim

The authors perform a leading-order perturbative QCD calculation including electroweak channels to estimate the charged hadron excess from W boson exchange in p+p and p+antip-bar collisions at TeV energies, and conclude that the weak-interaction contribution remains below the percent level compared to strong processes at both TEVATRON and LHC energies, contrary to earlier speculation that such channels might explain a scaling violation reported by CDF.

## Method

The authors use the perturbative QCD improved parton model at leading order, summing over partonic reactions with u, d, s, or c quarks, employing MSTW2008 parton distribution functions and KKP fragmentation functions. For the weak-interaction estimate, they include only the dominant resonant s-channel W boson exchange diagrams (neglecting Z boson exchange, t-channel diagrams, and photons), using the Born-level cross section with the weak coupling fixed at the vector boson mass scale.

## Key numbers

- The weak-interaction contribution is more than three orders of magnitude smaller than the strong-interaction contribution in both p+p and p+antip-bar collisions at the energies studied.
- The ratio of weak to strong contributions (R_W/S) in p+antip-bar collisions peaks at transverse momenta somewhat lower than m_W/2 (approximately 40 GeV/c), with the ratio higher at lower collision energies.
- The ratio R_pantip-bar/pp of weak-interaction contributions between p+antip-bar and p+p collisions increases with transverse momentum and is smaller at higher center-of-mass energies (e.g., 7 TeV versus 1.96 TeV).
- Varying the factorization scale parameter kappa between 1/2 and 3/2 changes the ratios by a few tens of percent, consistent with typical leading-order QCD scale sensitivity.

## Context

This work extends earlier efforts by Ioffe (who proposed electroweak channels to explain CDF data) and by Albino (who investigated fragmentation functions without finding an explanation for the CDF scaling violation). The authors note that the CDF collaboration later published reanalyzed data (CDFerr) that invalidated their earlier strong scaling violation claim, and CMS data at 7 TeV also showed no strong violation. The calculation provides quantitative predictions for future reference at higher energies or luminosities where weak contributions might become detectable.
