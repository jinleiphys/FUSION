# Strangeness production in hadronic models and recombination models

> We present recent results on the production, spectra and elliptic flow of strange particles in dynamic simulations employing hadronic degrees of freedom and from recombination models. The main focus will be on the Ultra-relativistic Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) Boltzmann approach to relativistic heavy ion collisions and a hybrid approach with intermediate hydrodynamic evolution based on UrQMD (available for download as UrQMD v3.3). Compared to the standard binary collision approach, an enhancement of the strange particle particle yields is found in the hybrid approach due to the assumption of local equilibration. The production origins of the Phi-meson in the hybrid approach are studied in further detail. We also present results on the transverse momentum spectra of baryon to meson ratios of strange particles. Due to the approximate energy independent scaling of this ratio as a function of p_T we argue, that a maximum in these spectra may not be a unique sign for quark coalescence but can be understood in terms of flow and fragmentation.

## Key claim

The maximum in baryon-to-meson ratios as a function of transverse momentum may not be a unique signature of quark coalescence or recombination, but can instead be understood as a natural consequence of flow and fragmentation in hadronic transport models.

## Method

The authors used the Ultra-relativistic Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) hadronic cascade model and a hybrid approach that couples an intermediate ideal hydrodynamic stage (using the SHASTA algorithm with a hadron gas equation of state) to the initial and final non-equilibrium Boltzmann transport stages. Two freeze-out procedures were tested: isochronous freeze-out, where all hydrodynamic cells are converted to particles simultaneously when energy density drops below five times ground state energy density, and gradual freeze-out, where conversion occurs in transverse slices of 0.2 fm thickness. Strange particle yields, transverse momentum spectra, and elliptic flow were computed for central Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions across a range of beam energies.

## Key numbers

- Enhancement of strange particle multiplicities (Lambda, Sigma, Omega, K) in the hybrid approach compared to pure UrQMD cascade, with gradual freeze-out providing improved description of experimental data over isochronous freeze-out
- Phi meson transverse mass distributions in In+In collisions at E_lab = 158 AGeV: hybrid model calculations favored over pure transport calculations for central collisions, with pure transport underestimating the yield
- The (Lambda+Sigma^0)/K^0_s ratio at midrapidity (|y| ≤ 0.5) shows a maximum at p_T ≈ 2 GeV/c across all center-of-mass energies from sqrt(s) = 3.8 GeV to sqrt(s) = 5.5 TeV
- When normalized to the integral yield, the (Lambda+Sigma^0)/K^0_s ratio collapses to a single energy-independent line for all energies from 3.8 GeV to 5.5 TeV

## Context

This work extends the UrQMD hybrid approach previously developed by Petersen et al. (2008, 2009) and applies it to strangeness production, addressing the long-standing difficulty of hadronic transport models to reproduce multi-strange hadron multiplicities without a QGP transition or additional effects like strong color fields (Bleicher, 2000) or multi-particle interactions (Greiner, 2000; Cassing, 2001). The analysis of baryon-to-meson ratios directly challenges the interpretation of Fries et al. (2003), who argued that the maximum in p/pi^0 and Lambda/K^0 ratios is a strong sign for a transition from recombination-dominated to fragmentation-dominated regimes. The results enable further investigation of whether thermalized phi meson production arises from hadronic mechanisms such as catalytic production (Kolomeitsev, 2009) or increased in-medium width (Vujanovic, 2009), or from multi-particle interactions (Braun-Munzinger, 2003).
