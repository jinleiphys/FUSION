# Phase diagram evolution at finite coupling in strong coupling lattice QCD

> We investigate the chiral phase transition in the strong coupling lattice QCD at finite temperature and density with finite coupling effects. We adopt one species of staggered fermion, and develop an analytic formulation based on strong coupling and cluster expansions. We derive the effective potential as a function of two order parameters, the chiral condensate sigma and the quark number density $ρ_q$, in a self-consistent treatment of the next-to-leading order (NLO) effective action terms. NLO contributions lead to modifications of quark mass, chemical potential and the quark wave function renormalization factor. While the ratio mu_c(T=0)/Tc(mu=0) is too small in the strong coupling limit, it is found to increase as beta=2Nc/g^2 increases. The critical point is found to move in the lower T direction as beta increases. Since the vector interaction induced by $ρ_q$ is shown to grow as beta, the present trend is consistent with the results in Nambu-Jona-Lasinio models. The interplay between two order parameters leads to the existence of partially chiral restored matter, where effective chemical potential is automatically adjusted to the quark excitation energy.

## Key claim

The ratio of the critical chemical potential at zero temperature to the critical temperature at zero chemical potential, which is too small in the strong coupling limit, increases as the finite coupling parameter beta = 2N_c/g^2 increases, and the critical point moves to lower temperature as beta increases, consistent with Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model results.

## Method

The authors adopt one species of unrooted staggered fermion in strong coupling lattice QCD and develop an analytic formulation based on strong coupling expansions in 1/g^2 and cluster expansions, combined with a 1/d (large dimensional) expansion at leading order. They derive the effective potential as a function of two order parameters, the chiral condensate sigma and the quark number density rho_q, by self-consistently treating next-to-leading order (NLO) effective action terms through an extended Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation, with gluon fields evaluated in the Polyakov gauge for finite temperature effects and quark chemical potential introduced via the lattice chemical potential method.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature at mu=0 for n_f=1 in the strong coupling limit is T_c(mu=0) = 5/3 (in lattice units)
- For n_f=2 and n_f=3, the critical temperatures at mu=0 are approximately 1.2 and 1.06 (in lattice units), respectively, differing from the n_f=1 result by 30-40%
- The critical chemical potentials at T=0 for n_f=2 and n_f=3 are found to be almost the same as for n_f=1
- The difference between phase boundaries in two and four flavor lattice Monte Carlo simulations is at most 7% within a region N_c*mu < 500 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous strong coupling lattice QCD studies of the chiral phase transition, which had established the phase diagram structure in the strong coupling limit with a tri-critical point (TCP) becoming a critical end point (CEP) for non-zero quark masses. The authors build on the analytic effective action formulations developed by Kawamoto (1981), Faldt (1986), and others, and the finite temperature treatments in the Polyakov gauge by Damgaard, Kawamoto, and Shigemoto (1984). The results are qualitatively consistent with recent lattice Monte Carlo simulations in the strong coupling limit by de Forcrand (2009) based on a Monomer-Dimer-Polymer formulation, and the finite coupling effects enable comparison with continuum Monte Carlo results around beta ~ 5-6. The work provides a framework for understanding how the phase diagram evolves from the strong coupling limit toward the continuum region, with implications for the location of the critical end point relevant to heavy-ion collision experiments at FAIR and low-energy RHIC programs.
