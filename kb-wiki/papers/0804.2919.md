# Low energy density correlation function of the degenerate unitary Fermi gas from epsilon expansion

> Using $ε$ expansion proposed in \cite{Nishida:2006br} we calculate density correlation function of the degenerate Fermi gas at infinite scattering length to next-to-leading order in $ε$ for excitation energies below quasi particle threshold. An expression for the low energy dynamic structure function is derived.

## Key claim

Using the epsilon expansion around d=4 spatial dimensions, the density correlation function of the degenerate unitary Fermi gas is calculated to next-to-leading order in epsilon for excitation energies below the quasiparticle threshold, with the caveat that the simplest extrapolation to d=3 is made by setting epsilon=1 in the final expressions.

## Method

The calculation uses the epsilon expansion technique proposed by Nishida and Son (2006), which treats the spatial dimension as d=4-epsilon and expands observables in powers of epsilon. The author computes the density-density correlation function from Feynman diagrams including one boson exchange (order 1/epsilon) and one fermion loop (order 1) contributions, using a dressed boson propagator that captures the superfluid mode pole. The theory is defined with a local four-Fermi interaction tuned to infinite scattering length, and the calculation is performed for energies below twice the quasiparticle gap.

## Key numbers

- The NLO uniform medium order parameter: phi_0 = 2 mu_0 [1 + epsilon(3C - 1 + Log 2)], where C ≈ 0.14424 and mu_0 = mu/epsilon
- The NLO sound velocity: c_s^2 = (mu/(2m)) [1 + epsilon/4]
- The NLO quasiparticle mass: Delta/mu_0 ≈ 2(1 - 0.345 epsilon)
- The NLO equilibrium density: n = (m mu_0/(2 pi))^(d/2) * (4/epsilon) * [1 + epsilon(6C - gamma_E/2 + 2 Log 2 - 7/4)], with gamma_E ≈ 0.57722
- The inelastic form factor per unit volume: S_inel(epsilon_p) = 2 sqrt(epsilon_p/(epsilon_p + 4 mu)) [1 - epsilon/4 - (5 epsilon epsilon_p)/(24 mu_0)]

## Context

This work extends the epsilon expansion technique of Nishida and Son (2006) to compute the density correlation function, a quantity measurable in cold atom experiments and relevant to dilute neutron matter. The calculation provides predictions for the dynamic structure function that could be tested experimentally, and the author notes that subsequent work by Arnold and others (2006) raised doubts about the convergence of the epsilon series. The results enable future calculations of the dynamic structure function above the quasiparticle threshold and of spin response functions relevant for neutrino coupling to dilute neutron matter.
