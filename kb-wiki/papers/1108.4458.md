# Charged bottomonium-like structures in the hidden-bottom dipion decays of $Υ(11020)$

> Under the Initial Single Pion Emission mechanism, we study the hidden-bottom dipion decays of $Υ(11020)$, i.e., $Υ(11020)\to Υ(nS)π^+π^-$ $(n=1,2,3)$ and $Υ(11020)\to h_b(mP)π^+π^-$ $(m=1,2)$. We predict explicit sharp peak structures close to the $B\bar{B}^*$ and $B^*\bar{B}^*$ thresholds and their reflections in the $Υ(1S)π^+$, $Υ(2S)π^+$ and $h_b(1P)π^+$ invariant mass spectrum distributions. We suggest future experiment, i.e., Belle, BaBar, and forthcoming BelleII or Super-B, carry out the search for these novel phenomena, which can provide important test to the Initial Single Emission mechanism existing in higher bottomonia.

## Key claim

Under the Initial Single Pion Emission (ISPE) mechanism, the hidden-bottom dipion decays of Υ(11020) are predicted to produce sharp peak structures in the Υ(1S)π⁺, Υ(2S)π⁺, and h_b(1P)π⁺ invariant mass spectra near the B\bar{B}* and B*\bar{B}* thresholds, but these peaks are not typical Breit-Wigner type distributions and their specification by mass and width is not appropriate; the calculation does not include background contributions, which could introduce uncertainty.

## Method

The authors applied the Initial Single Pion Emission (ISPE) mechanism, previously proposed for Υ(5S) decays (Chen et al., 2011), to the hidden-bottom dipion decays of Υ(11020) into Υ(nS)π⁺π⁻ (n=1,2,3) and h_b(mP)π⁺π⁻ (m=1,2). They used an effective Lagrangian approach with hadron-level diagrams involving intermediate B\bar{B}, B\bar{B}*+B*\bar{B}, and B*\bar{B}* states in triangle loops, computing differential decay widths as functions of the Υ(nS)π⁺ and h_b(mP)π⁺ invariant masses with a monopole form factor parameterized by Λ = ξΛ_QCD + m_{B*} (Λ_QCD = 220 MeV, ξ = 1).

## Key numbers

- Mass of Υ(11020): 11019 MeV; masses of Υ(3S), Υ(2S), Υ(1S): 10355, 10023, 9460 MeV respectively.
- Masses of h_b(1P) and h_b(2P): 9898 MeV and 10259 MeV respectively (Adachi et al., 2011).
- Masses of B, B*, π: 5279, 5325, 140 MeV respectively.
- The coupling constant g = 0.59, derived from D* → Dπ branching ratio measured by CLEO-c (Anastassov et al., 2001) with f_π = 132 MeV.
- The cutoff parameter Λ = ξΛ_QCD + m_{B*} with Λ_QCD = 220 MeV; numerical results are weakly dependent on ξ, taken as ξ = 1.

## Context

This work extends the ISPE mechanism previously applied to Υ(5S) decays (Chen et al., 2011), which successfully produced charged bottomonium-like structures matching the Z_b(10610) and Z_b(10650) observed by Belle (Belle Collaboration, 2011), and to hidden-charm dipion decays of ψ(4040), ψ(4160), ψ(4415), and Y(4260) (Chen et al., 2011). The predictions for Υ(11020) provide a test of the ISPE mechanism as a universal decay mechanism in higher bottomonia, and the authors suggest experimental searches at Belle, BaBar, Belle II, and SuperB to observe these charged bottomonium-like structures near the B\bar{B}* and B*\bar{B}* thresholds.
