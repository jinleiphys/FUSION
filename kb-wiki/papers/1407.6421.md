# Investigation of proton radioactivity with the effective liquid drop model

> Proton radioactivity has been investigated using the effective liquid drop model with varying mass asymmetry shape and effective inertial coefficient. An effective nuclear radius constant formula replaces the old empirical one in the calculations. The theoretical half-lives are in good agreement with the available experimental data. All the deviations between the calculated logarithmic half-lives and the experimental values are less than 0.8. The root-mean-square deviation is 0.523. Predictions for the half-lives of proton radioactivity are made for elements across the periodic table. From the theoretical results, there are 11 candidate nuclei for proton radioactivity in the region $Z <51$. In the region $Z >83$, no nuclei are suggested as probable candidate nuclei for proton radioactivity within the selected range of half-lives studied.

## Key claim

The effective liquid drop model with a new isospin-dependent nuclear radius constant formula reproduces experimental proton-radioactivity half-lives with all logarithmic deviations under 0.8 and a root-mean-square deviation of 0.523, but the agreement depends on selecting the correct orbital angular momentum \(l\) for each transition, which is not experimentally measured but inferred from decay-rate calculations.

## Method

The authors model proton emission as a one-dimensional barrier-penetration problem using the effective liquid drop model with varying mass asymmetry shape and an effective inertial coefficient. The parent nucleus radius is calculated from a new isospin-dependent formula \(R_m = \frac{1.38}{1.20} \times (1.2332 + \frac{2.8961}{A_m} - 0.18688 \times I) A_m^{1/3}\) instead of the old empirical constant \(R_m = 1.38 A_m^{1/3}\). The decay rate is \(\lambda = \lambda_0 \mathcal{P}\) with a fixed preexponential factor \(\lambda_0 = 0.5 \times 10^{21} \text{s}^{-1}\), and half-lives are computed for 19 known proton emitters and then predicted for candidate nuclei across the periodic table under three assumed angular momenta (\(l = 0, 2, 5\)).

## Key numbers

- Root-mean-square deviation between calculated and experimental logarithmic half-lives: \(\sigma = 0.523\) (over 19 nuclei).
- All deviations \(|\log_{10} T_{\text{Theo.}} - \log_{10} T_{\text{Exp.}}|\) are less than 0.8; the smallest deviation is 0.018 (for \(^{161}\text{Re}\)) and the largest is 0.793 (for \(^{113}\text{Cs}\)).
- For \(^{121}\text{Pr}\), the measured half-life is \(\log_{10} T = -2.000\) (10 ms); the model matches with \(l = 3\).
- For \(^{141}\text{Ho}\), the measured half-life is \(\log_{10} T = -2.376\) (4.2 ms); the model gives \(\log_{10} T = -1.948\) with \(l = 5\).
- For \(^{131}\text{Eu}\), the measured half-life is \(\log_{10} T = -1.585\) (26 ms); the model gives \(\log_{10} T = -1.186\) with \(l = 4\).
- 11 candidate nuclei for proton radioactivity are predicted in the region \(Z < 51\) (e.g., \(^{42}\text{V}\), \(^{54}\text{Cu}\), \(^{68}\text{Br}\), \(^{85}\text{Tc}\)).
- No candidate nuclei are found in the region \(Z > 83\) within the selected half-life range \(-10 < \log_{10} T_{\text{Theo.}} < 10\).

## Context

This work extends the effective liquid drop model, previously applied to cluster radioactivity by Goncalves and Duarte (2002) and to alpha decay, to the case of proton radioactivity. It improves upon earlier calculations by replacing the constant nuclear radius parameter with an isospin-dependent formula, achieving better agreement with experimental data than the old empirical constant. The predictions of 11 candidate proton emitters below \(Z = 51\) suggest new experimental targets for proton-radioactivity searches, while the null result above \(Z = 83\) indicates that such decays in that region are unlikely within the studied half-life window.
