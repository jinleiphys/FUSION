# Disoriented Chiral Condensate: Theory and Experiment

> It is thought that a region of pseudo-vacuum, where the chiral order parameter is misaligned from its vacuum orientation in isospin space, might occasionally form in high energy hadronic or nuclear collisions. The possible detection of such disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) would provide useful information about the chiral structure of the QCD vacuum and/or the chiral phase transition of strong interactions at high temperature. We review the theoretical developments concerning the possible DCC formation in high-energy collisions as well as the various experimental searches that have been performed so far. We discuss future prospects for upcoming DCC searches, e.g. in high-energy heavy-ion collision experiments at RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the theoretical and experimental status of disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) formation in high-energy collisions, concluding that while no clear positive signal has been reported so far, the present experimental upper bounds are actually consistent with theoretical expectations based on the quench scenario.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical developments concerning DCC formation, including the baked-Alaska scenario, classical solutions of the linear sigma model with boost-invariant boundary conditions, and the quench scenario where rapid expansion triggers exponential growth of long-wavelength pion modes. They also survey experimental searches in cosmic ray experiments, nucleon-nucleon collisions at CERN and Fermilab (including the dedicated MiniMAX experiment), and nucleus-nucleus collisions at the CERN SPS and RHIC.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: m_pi = 135 MeV, sigma mass: m_sigma = 600 MeV, pion decay constant: f_pi = 92.5 MeV (parameters used in the linear sigma model for the quench scenario)
- Energy density associated with explicit chiral symmetry breaking: epsilon ~ f_pi^2 * m_pi^2 ~ 20 MeV/fm^3 (rough order of magnitude for the baked-Alaska scenario)
- For the DCC neutral fraction distribution: probability that less than 10% of DCC pions be neutral is predicted to be as large as 30%
- Correlation functions for DCC emission: R_00 ~ 4/5, R_0+ ~ -2/5, R_++ ~ 1/5 (for large multiplicities, neglecting terms of relative order 1/<N>)

## Context

This review extends the theoretical framework developed by Bjorken (1991), Blaizot and Krzywicki (1992), and Rajagopal and Wilczek (1993) for DCC formation in high-energy collisions. It surveys experimental searches including cosmic ray Centauro events reported by the JACEE and Pamir collaborations, the MiniMAX experiment at Fermilab, and heavy-ion experiments at the CERN SPS (WA98, NA49) and RHIC (PHENIX, STAR). The paper enables future DCC searches at RHIC and LHC, particularly with the ALICE detector, and notes that the quench scenario has triggered theoretical developments in far-from-equilibrium quantum field theory relevant to condensed-matter physics and early-time cosmology.
