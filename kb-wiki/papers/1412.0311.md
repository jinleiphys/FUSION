# The chiral magnetic effect in heavy-ion collisions from event-by-event anomalous hydrodynamics

> The (3+1)D relativistic hydrodynamics with chiral anomaly is used to obtain a quantitative description of the chiral magnetic effect (CME) in heavy-ion collisions. We find that the charge-dependent hadron azimuthal correlations are sensitive to the CME, and that the experimental observations are consistent with the presence of the effect.

# The chiral magnetic effect in heavy-ion collisions from event-by-event anomalous hydrodynamics

## Key claim

The charge-dependent hadron azimuthal correlations measured at RHIC and LHC are consistent with the presence of the chiral magnetic effect (CME) when modeled using (3+1)D relativistic hydrodynamics with chiral anomaly, but the authors acknowledge that theoretical uncertainties remain substantial and that background effects from multi-particle correlations cannot be excluded.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event simulations using three-dimensional ideal chiral magnetohydrodynamics (CMHD) with fluctuating initial conditions from a Monte-Carlo Glauber model extended to include initial axial charge density generated by color flux tubes. The electromagnetic fields were treated as background fields with a parameterized magnetic field profile, and hadrons were sampled from an isothermal freezeout surface at 160 MeV using the Cooper-Frye approach. Simulations were run for three impact parameters corresponding to 20-30%, 30-40%, and 50-60% centrality, with 100k, 150k, and 250k events respectively for both anomalous and non-anomalous cases.

## Key numbers

- Freezeout temperature: T_fo = 160 MeV
- Magnetic field parameters: eB_0 = 0.5 GeV^2, tau_B = 3 fm, corresponding to eB_y(tau_in, 0, 0) ~ (3 m_pi)^2
- Gold nucleus radius: R = 6.38 fm
- Initial axial chemical potential coefficient: C_mu5 = 0.1 GeV
- Initialization time: tau_in ~ 0.6 fm (3 GeV^-1)
- Estimated initial axial charge density: n_5(tau_in) ~ (0.4 GeV)^3
- Impact parameters simulated: b = 7.2, 9.0, 11.2 fm (20-30%, 30-40%, 50-60% centrality)
- Event statistics: 100k events at b=7.2 fm, 150k at b=9.0 fm, 250k at b=11.2 fm for each case

## Context

This work extends the pilot numerical study of anomalous hydrodynamics by Hongo et al. (2013) by providing the first fully quantitative description of experimental charge-dependent azimuthal correlations using event-by-event CMHD. The theoretical framework builds on the formulation of anomalous hydrodynamics by Son and Surowka (2009) and the chiral magnetic wave concept by Kharzeev et al. (2010). The results enable future improvements including event-by-event fluctuations of magnetic fields as studied by Bzdak et al. (2011) and Bloczynski et al. (2012), dynamical electromagnetic fields, and dissipative effects, while the authors note that background effects from transverse momentum conservation (Bzdak et al., 2010; Pratt et al., 2010), charge conservation (Schlichting et al., 2010), and cluster correlations (Wang et al., 2009) are not included in their sampling procedure.
