# $Δ(1232)$-Resonance in the Hydrogen Spectrum

> The electromagnetic excitation of the $Δ(1232)$-resonance plays an appreciable role in the Lamb shift and hyperfine structure of muonic and electronic hydrogen. Its effect appears at the subleading order $\mathcal{O}(α^5)$, together with other proton-polarizability contributions from forward two-photon exchange. We use the large-$N_c$ relations for the nucleon-to-delta transition form factors to compute the effect of the $Δ(1232)$ in the hydrogen spectrum. We pay particular attention to a subtile difference between predictions based on a direct calculation of the two-photon exchange (or Compton scattering amplitudes) and predictions based on the $Δ(1232)$-production photoabsorption cross sections. The mismatch is explained by studying the dispersion relations for tree-level Compton scattering off the proton in more details.

## Key claim

The electromagnetic excitation of the Δ(1232) resonance contributes to the Lamb shift and hyperfine structure of muonic and electronic hydrogen at subleading order O(α⁵), but the effect is largely canceled between the Δ-pole and non-pole parts of the Compton scattering amplitudes, making the total Δ(1232) contribution much smaller than the individual pieces would suggest.

## Method

The author computes the forward doubly-virtual Compton scattering (VVCS) amplitudes for tree-level Compton scattering with intermediate Δ(1232) exchange using the γ*N→Δ transition vertex and spin-3/2 propagator, with the Jones-Scadron transition form factors related to nucleon electromagnetic form factors via large-Nc relations. The VVCS amplitudes are then integrated over photon energy and momentum transfer to obtain the two-photon exchange contribution to the Lamb shift and hyperfine splitting in muonic hydrogen. The calculation explicitly separates the Δ-pole terms (proportional to 1/(νΔ²-ν²)) from the non-pole terms that arise from the local Lagrangian but are not captured by dispersion relations using Δ-production photoabsorption cross sections alone.

## Key numbers

- Total Δ(1232) contribution to the 2P1/2-2S1/2 Lamb shift in muonic hydrogen: -0.95 ± 0.95 μeV
- Δ-exchange contribution to the subtraction function T1(0,Q²) in the Lamb shift: -7.58 ± 2.27 μeV
- Leading-order chiral dynamics polarizability contribution to the Lamb shift: 8⁺³₋₁ μeV
- Δ-pole contribution to the Lamb shift from T1: -2.22 μeV; from T2: -6.01 μeV; total Δ-pole: -8.23 μeV
- Non-pole contribution to the Lamb shift from T1: 0.40 μeV; from T2: 1.19 μeV; total non-pole: 1.59 μeV
- Nucleon mass MN = 938.27 MeV; Delta mass MΔ = 1232 MeV
- Magnetic coupling gM = 2.97; electric coupling gE = -1.0; Coulomb coupling gC = -2.6
- Isovector anomalous magnetic moment κV = κp - κn ≈ 3.7

## Context

This work extends baryon chiral perturbation theory (BχPT) studies of nucleon VVCS by Bernard et al. (2002-2012) and Lensky et al. (2014) to include the Δ(1232) resonance, using the large-Nc relations of Jenkins (1994) and Buchmann (2002) to connect transition form factors to nucleon electromagnetic form factors. The calculation resolves a subtle mismatch between direct two-photon exchange calculations (Faustov, 1998) and predictions based on Δ-production photoabsorption cross sections (Buchmann, 2009), showing that the non-pole terms missing from the cross-section approach are essential. The results agree with the model-independent calculation of Peset et al. (2014) within errors and enable improved theoretical predictions for the planned ground-state 1S hyperfine splitting measurements in muonic hydrogen by the CREMA, FAMU, and J-PARC/Riken-RAL collaborations.
