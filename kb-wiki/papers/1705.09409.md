# Chiral magnetohydrodynamics for heavy-ion collisions

> The chiral magnetic effect (CME) is a macroscopic transport effect resulting from the chiral anomaly. We review the recent progress in theoretical understanding the properties of chiral plasmas, in which the CME and other anomaly-induced transports take place. In particular, the nontrivial interplay of anomalous currents and dynamical electromagnetic fields is discussed. We also review the theoretical status of the modeling of anomalous transport effects in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper reviews theoretical progress in chiral magnetohrodynamics (MHD) for heavy-ion collisions, arguing that the interplay between anomalous chiral currents and dynamical electromagnetic fields leads to phenomena such as inverse cascade and instabilities, but notes that quantitative modeling of the chiral magnetic effect (CME) in heavy-ion collisions remains incomplete and requires further development of hydrodynamic and kinetic frameworks.

## Method

The paper surveys the theoretical formulation of chiral MHD, starting from the chiral anomaly equation and the conservation of total helicity (magnetic plus fermionic). It derives the equations of motion for a chiral plasma coupled to dynamical electromagnetic fields, including first-order corrections from finite conductivity that introduce anomalous chiral magnetic and chiral vortical conductivities. The review also covers real-time classical lattice simulations of chirality production in the glasma and event-by-event anomalous hydrodynamic calculations using 2+1 dimensional viscous fluid dynamics (VISHNew).

## Key numbers

- The critical wavenumber for the helicity-dependent instability in chiral MHD is given by \( k_{\rm c} = \sigma_B / (1 + \bar \eta \sigma) \), where \( \sigma_B \) is the chiral magnetic conductivity, \( \bar \eta \) is normalized shear viscosity, and \( \sigma \) is electrical conductivity.
- The MHD simulation of magnetic field evolution in heavy-ion collisions shows slower decay compared to vacuum evolution, with the calculation starting from \( \tau = 0.4 \) fm and using initial fields from Maxwell equations at finite conductivity.
- For isobaric collisions of \( ^{96}_{44}{\rm Ru} \) and \( ^{96}_{40}{\rm Zr} \), the quantity \( \langle B^2 \cos [2 (\Psi_{\rm B} - \Psi_{\rm RP})] \rangle \) shows a sizable difference of approximately 20% between the two collision types, as a function of centrality.

## Context

This review extends the theoretical understanding of chiral plasmas developed in earlier work on the chiral magnetic effect by Kharzeev (2007) and Fukushima (2010), and on chiral MHD by Joyce and Shaposhnikov (1997) and Akamatsu and Yamamoto (2013). It enables future quantitative modeling of anomalous transport in heavy-ion collisions, particularly through event-by-event anomalous hydrodynamic calculations (Jiang 2016) and real-time lattice simulations of CME in glasma (Mueller 2016, Mace 2016). The paper also discusses the planned isobaric collision program at RHIC (Deng 2016) as a way to isolate CME signals from background effects.
