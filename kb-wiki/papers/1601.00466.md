# Towards the QCD phase diagram from analytical continuation

> We calculate the QCD cross-over temperature, the equation of state and fluctuations of conserved charges at finite density by analytical continuation from imaginary to real chemical potentials. Our calculations are based on new continuum extrapolated lattice simulations using the 4stout staggered actions with a lattice resolution up to $N_t=16$. The simulation parameters are tuned such that the strangeness neutrality is maintained, as it is in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The QCD cross-over temperature, equation of state, and fluctuations of conserved charges at finite baryochemical potential can be reliably extracted by analytical continuation from imaginary chemical potentials, but the extrapolation error grows at larger real chemical potentials due to next-to-leading-order (μ_B^4) contributions that signal the end of the accessible μ_B range.

## Method

The authors performed continuum-extrapolated lattice QCD simulations using the 4stout staggered action with lattice resolutions N_t = 10, 12, and 16, at four imaginary chemical potentials μ_B^(j) = i j π T / 8 with j = 3, 4, 5, 6 plus a j = 0 reference. The simulation parameters were tuned to enforce strangeness neutrality (⟨S⟩ = 0) and a fixed electric charge-to-baryon ratio ⟨Q⟩ = 0.4 ⟨B⟩, matching heavy-ion collision conditions. Taylor coefficients c_2 through c_6 for the pressure expansion were fitted from the μ_B-derivatives of p/T^4 at fixed temperature, and the transition temperature curvature κ was extracted from three observables (chiral susceptibility, chiral condensate inflection point, and strangeness susceptibility inflection point).

## Key numbers

- Curvature of the QCD transition line: κ = 0.0149 ± 0.0021, obtained from combined analysis of three observables (chiral susceptibility, chiral condensate inflection point, strangeness susceptibility inflection point) at imaginary chemical potentials.
- Lattice simulations used resolutions up to N_t = 16 with the 4stout staggered action.
- Imaginary chemical potentials were selected at μ_B = i j π T / 8 for j = 3, 4, 5, 6, covering the range Im μ_B ∈ [0, π T] limited by Roberge-Weiss symmetry.
- The transition temperature at zero chemical potential is in the range around 155 MeV, consistent with prior work by Borsanyi (2010).

## Context

This work extends the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration's earlier lattice QCD results at zero chemical potential (Borsanyi 2010, 2013) to finite density using analytical continuation from imaginary chemical potentials, implementing strangeness neutrality unlike some prior studies (Bonati 2015, Cea 2015). The method enables extraction of higher-order Taylor coefficients more efficiently than direct μ_B = 0 derivative calculations, and the results are used to map constant entropy-per-baryon contours that match chemical freeze-out data from RHIC beam energy scan experiments. A recent related study by Bazavov (2015) extracted curvature from fluctuation data at μ_B = 0, finding a negative freeze-out curvature consistent with the present results, though the full systematics of lattice-to-experiment comparison remain to be understood.
