# Collective excitations of transactinide nuclei in a self-consistent mean field theory

> The ATDHFB approach has been applied for a study of properties of collective quadrupole states in several transactinide nuclei: 238U, 240Pu, 242Pu, 246Cm, 248Cm, 250Cf and 252Cf. Calculated energies and B(E2) transition probabilities are in a reasonable agreement with experimental data. Results concerning superdeformed collective states in the 240Pu nucleus are also presented.

# Collective excitations of transactinide nuclei in a self-consistent mean field theory

## Key claim
The ATDHFB approach with Skyrme interactions (SkM*, SIII, SLy4) can describe low-energy quadrupole collective states in transactinide nuclei (U, Pu, Cm, Cf) without fitting any parameters to the spectroscopic data, but the theoretical beta and gamma bandhead energies are systematically too large, and the agreement is only "reasonable" rather than precise.

## Method
The Adiabatic Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (ATDHFB) theory was used to construct a five-dimensional collective Bohr Hamiltonian from Skyrme effective interactions (primarily SkM* and SIII, with some tests using SLy4) and seniority-type pairing with constant G. The collective potential energy and six mass parameters (including moments of inertia) were calculated on a grid of 220 points in the (beta, gamma) deformation plane (beta from 0 to 1, gamma from 0 to 60 degrees), and the Bohr Hamiltonian was solved to obtain energies and B(E2) transition probabilities. An average factor of 1.3 was applied to multiply the ATDHFB mass parameters to account for pairing vibrations and Thouless-Valatin corrections.

## Key numbers
- Pairing strengths: G_n = 15.1/(11+N) MeV, G_p = 14.9/(11+Z) MeV, where N and Z are neutron and proton numbers respectively.
- For the lowest 2+ state in 240Pu: theoretical energy 0.046 MeV, experimental 0.043 MeV (normal deformed ground state band).
- For the beta bandhead (0+ state) in 240Pu: theoretical energy 1.420 MeV, experimental 0.860 MeV.
- For the gamma bandhead (2+ state) in 240Pu: theoretical energy 1.347 MeV, experimental 1.137 MeV.
- For the superdeformed 0+ state in 240Pu: theoretical energy 4.321 MeV, experimental 2.55 MeV; average beta = 0.829, average gamma = 2.94 degrees.
- For the superdeformed 2+ state in 240Pu: theoretical excitation energy above SD ground state 0.021 MeV.
- B(E2) for 2+_gs -> 0+_gs in 250Cf: experimental 2.3 +/- 0.3 W.u., SkM* result 6.26 W.u., SIII result 4.73 W.u. (gamma -> ground band transition).
- The Bohr Hamiltonian eigenvalue for the first 2+ state is typically 16-20 keV larger than the pure rotor estimate from the moment of inertia at the potential minimum.

## Context
This work extends the ATDHFB approach previously applied to lighter nuclei (Prochniak 2004, 2007) into the transactinide region, where most self-consistent mean-field studies had focused on fission barriers (Warda 2002, Bonneau 2004, Staszczak 2006) rather than collective spectroscopy. The results are compared with experimental data from the National Nuclear Data Center (2007) and with earlier calculations using the phenomenological Nilsson potential (Prochniak 2002) and Gogny forces (Libert 1999, Delaroche 2006). The study demonstrates that self-consistent methods can describe both normal and superdeformed collective states in heavy nuclei, but notes that rotational corrections (estimated at 0.8-0.9 MeV for superdeformed states) and Thouless-Valatin corrections remain open problems requiring further theoretical development.
