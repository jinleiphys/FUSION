# Rapidity-dependent eccentricity scaling in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> There is a well-established relation between the spatial asymmetry in the initial stage of a heavy-ion collision and the final momentum anisotropy, which allows for a separation of effects from initial conditions vs. later evolution and has proved exceptionally powerful. However, until recently it has only been studied in two dimensions -- either through boost-invariant simulations or studying only quantities at mid-rapidity. We explore an extension to 3 dimensions, in order to determine whether a similar understanding can be obtained for the rapidity dependence of the collision system. In particular, we introduce rapidity-dependent eccentricities and investigate a trivial extension of the 2D eccentricity scaling of elliptic and triangular flow, as well as a way to systematically improve these initial-state estimators. We then explore the dependence of the resulting response coefficients on shear viscosity and initial total energy.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the well-established linear relation between initial spatial eccentricity and final anisotropic flow can be extended to include rapidity dependence, but only as a leading-order approximation; the full mapping requires a gradient expansion in rapidity, with second-derivative corrections that become significant when the initial eccentricity has curvature in rapidity.

## Method

The authors performed 3+1D relativistic hydrodynamic simulations using the MUSIC code with the s95p-v1 equation of state and vanishing bulk viscosity, starting from a parameterized Gaussian energy density distribution at initial proper time τ = 1 fm. They constructed rapidity-dependent eccentricities ε_n(η) and tested both a simple local-in-rapidity estimator V_n(Y) = κ_n ε_n(η=Y) and an improved gradient expansion including second derivatives, extracting response coefficients from simulations with controlled rapidity dependence of the initial asymmetry and total energy.

## Key numbers

- κ₂ = 0.174 and κ₃ = 0.068, the linear response coefficients extracted from boost-invariant ideal hydrodynamic simulations with ρ = 3 fm and A = 50 fm⁻⁴ (roughly RHIC conditions)
- κ₂'' = 0.0135 and κ₃'' = 0.0092, the second-derivative response coefficients extracted from simulations with quadratic rapidity dependence of the initial eccentricity
- Freeze-out occurs at energy density 0.12 GeV/fm³ (equivalent to temperature 137 MeV)
- Initial proper time τ = 1 fm for all simulations

## Context

This work extends the 2D eccentricity-flow mapping established by Gardim et al. (2011) and the boost-invariant framework of Kolb et al. (2001) to full 3D rapidity-dependent systems, building on recent 3D work by Li et al. (2019). The results enable separation of initial-state and final-state effects in rapidity-dependent observables at RHIC and LHC, and provide a framework for interpreting how shear viscosity and energy-dependent response coefficients affect the longitudinal structure of anisotropic flow.
