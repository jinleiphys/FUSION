# Average phase factor in the PNJL model

> The average phase factor of the QCD determinant is evaluated at finite quark chemical potential (μ_q) with the two-flavor version of the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model with the scalar-type eight-quark interaction. For μ_q larger than half the pion mass at vacuum m_π, the average phase factor is finite only when the Polyakov loop is larger than 0.5, indicating that lattice QCD is feasible only in the deconfinement phase. A critical endpoint (CEP) lies in the region of the zero average phase factor. The scalar-type eight-quark interaction makes it shorter a relative distance of the CEP to the boundary of the region. For μ_q < m_π/2, the PNJL model with dynamical mesonic fluctuations can reproduce lattice QCD data below the critical temperature.

## Key claim

The average phase factor of the QCD determinant is finite only when the Polyakov loop is larger than approximately 0.5 for quark chemical potentials larger than half the pion mass, indicating that lattice QCD simulations are feasible only in the deconfinement phase. The critical endpoint lies in the region where the average phase factor is zero, and the scalar-type eight-quark interaction reduces the relative distance of the critical endpoint to the boundary of that region.

## Method

The authors evaluate the average phase factor using the two-flavor Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model with a scalar-type eight-quark interaction, applying a mean-field approximation plus static fluctuations (MF+SF) to the thermodynamic potential. The average phase factor is computed from the ratio of the partition functions of the ordinary two-flavor theory (1+1) and the phase-quenched theory (1+1*), where the Hessian matrix describing static fluctuations of the mean fields is included. For comparison with lattice QCD data, dynamical mesonic fluctuations are also incorporated via the random phase approximation (RPA) below the critical temperature.

## Key numbers

- The pion mass at vacuum is \(m_\pi = 139\) MeV for the parameter set with finite scalar-type eight-quark interaction.
- The critical temperature \(T_c\) is set to 173 MeV by rescaling \(T_0\) to 200 MeV.
- The average phase factor is zero for \(\Phi < \Phi_c\) and finite for \(\Phi > \Phi_c\), where \(\Phi_c\) is the critical Polyakov-loop value at which the pion condensate vanishes at \(\mu_q = 100\) MeV.
- For \(\mu_q < m_\pi/2\), the average phase factor is finite and calculable with lattice QCD.
- For \(\mu_q > m_\pi/2\), the average phase factor is zero when the Polyakov loop \(\Phi \lesssim 0.5\) and finite when \(\Phi \gtrsim 0.5\).
- The scalar-type eight-quark interaction enhances the average phase factor sizably above \(T_c\) (e.g., at \(T = 1.1 T_c\)).
- The lattice QCD data used for comparison have a pion mass \(m_\pi^{\rm LQCD} \approx 280\) MeV and a lattice size \(16^3 \times 4\).

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of the average phase factor using the NJL model (Anderson, 2009) and random matrix theory (Han, 2009) to the PNJL model, which incorporates both chiral and deconfinement transitions. The results show that the PNJL model yields a wider region of zero average phase factor than the NJL model, and that the scalar-type eight-quark interaction, essential for reproducing lattice QCD data at finite isospin chemical potential (Sasaki, 2010) and imaginary chemical potential (Sakai, 2009), reduces the relative distance of the critical endpoint to the boundary of the zero-phase region. This enables future lattice QCD data outside the zero-phase region to constrain the location of the critical endpoint via the PNJL model.
