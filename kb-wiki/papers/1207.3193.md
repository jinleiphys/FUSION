# Cold Nuclear Matter effects in Upsilon production in dAu collisions at RHIC

> We report on our recent study of Cold Nuclear Matter effects on the Upsilon production at RHIC in dAu collisions. The first experimental results available on the nuclear modification factor R_dAu have rather large uncertainties. They nevertheless allow to bring qualitative information on the nature of the nuclear effects at play on top of the usual nuclear absorption, since the latter is expected to lie in a quite small range around a value close to ten times smaller as for charmonia. At backward rapidities, the behavior of R_dAu hints at the presence of a gluon EMC effect, analogous to the quark EMC effect -- but possibly stronger. Mid rapidity measurements with a better precision are highly desirable to pin down the gluon anti-shadowing, still under debate. At forward rapidities, the data leave some room for an additional fractional energy loss mechanism, recently revived in the literature.

## Key claim

The first RHIC data on Upsilon production in dAu collisions, despite large uncertainties, provide qualitative hints that the usual cold nuclear matter effects (shadowing, anti-shadowing, and nuclear absorption) are insufficient to explain the observed rapidity dependence, and that a gluon EMC effect at backward rapidities and a fractional parton energy loss at forward rapidities may be at play.

## Method

The authors used a Monte-Carlo framework (JIN) based on the probabilistic Glauber model, employing a 2-to-2 partonic process (g+g to b bbar + g) for Upsilon production, with kinematics computed using the Colour-Singlet Model at leading order. They compared the nuclear modification factor R_dAu computed with three different nuclear parton distribution function parametrisations (EKS98, EPS08, nDSg) and with effective break-up cross sections ranging from 0 to 1 mb, against the rapidity-dependent data from STAR and PHENIX at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The formation time of the Upsilon in the gold rest frame ranges from 5.8 fm at y=-2.0 to 310 fm at y=+2.0 at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV.
- The effective break-up cross section for the bottomonium pair is expected to be about 0.1 times that of charmonia, following the early-time scaling m_c/m_b, with a conservative upper bound of 1 mb.
- At forward rapidity, a fractional energy loss mechanism may induce an extra suppression of about 10 to 20% of the Upsilon yield in dAu collisions at RHIC.
- The backward rapidity region corresponds to intermediate Bjorken-x values of 0.35 to 0.7, where a gluon EMC suppression is hinted at.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Ferreiro 2011) on Upsilon production in dAu collisions, and contrasts with earlier calculations by Vogt (2010) that assumed a 2-to-1 partonic process. The results suggest that the usual nuclear shadowing (as parametrised in EKS98, EPS08, nDSg, and EPS09) is too weak to explain the forward-rapidity suppression, and that the data favour a sizable gluon EMC effect analogous to the quark EMC effect first reported by the EMC collaboration (Aubert 1983). The study enables future precision measurements at RHIC and at a possible fixed-target experiment at the LHC (the AFTER project) to quantitatively constrain the gluon distribution at large Bjorken-x.
