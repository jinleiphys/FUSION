# QCD sum rule approach to Okamoto-Nolen-Schiffer anomaly

> A new framework is introduced to connect between a charge symmetry breaking (CSB) energy density functional (EDF) and the low-energy constants derived from quantum chromodynamics (QCD). By constructing a QCD-based CSB EDF, this method provides new insights into the Okamoto-Nolen-Schiffer anomaly, a long-standing puzzle in the energy differences of mirror nuclei that lacks a robust microscopic explanation. Using examples such as $ {}^{17} \mathrm{F} $-$ {}^{17} \mathrm{O} $, $ {}^{15} \mathrm{O} $-$ {}^{15} \mathrm{N} $, $ {}^{41} \mathrm{Sc} $-$ {}^{41} \mathrm{Ca} $, and $ {}^{39} \mathrm{Ca} $-$ {}^{39} \mathrm{K} $, we demonstrate that the proposed interaction effectively resolves the anomaly within the range of theoretical uncertainties.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the Okamoto-Nolen-Schiffer anomaly in mirror nuclei can be resolved by constructing a charge symmetry breaking energy density functional whose parameters are constrained by low-energy constants from QCD, specifically the u-d quark mass difference and the in-medium chiral condensate, rather than by fitting to nuclear data.

## Method
The authors derive a Skyrme-type charge symmetry breaking interaction by matching its density expansion (up to order ρ^(5/3)) to the density-dependent neutron-proton mass difference Δ_np(ρ) obtained from QCD sum rules. The QCD sum rule expression for Δ_np(ρ) depends on the in-medium chiral condensate, which is expanded using the π-N sigma term and Fermi-motion corrections. The resulting effective coupling strengths s̃_0 and s̃_1+3s̃_2 are then fixed by the QCD low-energy constants C_1 and σ_πN. The charge symmetry breaking interaction is applied in Hartree-Fock calculations with two energy density functionals (SGII and SAMi) for mirror nuclei with masses A=16±1 and A=40±1.

* The QCD-derived coefficient C_1 = 5.24^(+2.48)_(-1.21) MeV
* The contact term s̃_0 = -15.5^(+8.8)_(-12.5) MeV·fm^3
* The momentum-dependent combination s̃_1 + 3s̃_2 = 0.52^(+0.42)_(-0.29) MeV·fm^5
* The Coulomb energy differences from HF with SGII: 3.392 MeV (¹⁷F-¹⁷O), 3.298 MeV (¹⁵O-¹⁵N), 6.866 MeV (⁴¹Sc-⁴¹Ca), 6.977 MeV (³⁹Ca-³⁹K)

## Context
This work extends the phenomenological Skyrme-type charge symmetry breaking interactions developed by Roca-Maza (2018), Baczyk (2018, 2019), Sagawa (2019), and Naito (2022, 2023) by providing a QCD-based determination of their parameters. It connects to the QCD sum rule approach of Hatsuda (1991) that relates the neutron-proton mass difference in medium to the partial restoration of chiral symmetry. The approach enables a microscopic understanding of the Okamoto-Nolen-Schiffer anomaly, which was first identified by Okamoto (1964) and systematically studied by Nolen and Schiffer (1969), and which previous corrections (finite proton size, center-of-mass effect, Thomas-Ehrman effect, isospin impurity, electromagnetic spin-orbit, core polarization, vacuum polarization) could only explain about 1% of the discrepancy as reviewed by Shlomo (1978). The next step is to extend this framework to charge independence breaking interactions and apply it to isobaric analogue states in N>Z nuclei.
