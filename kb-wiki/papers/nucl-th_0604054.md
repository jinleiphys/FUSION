# Near-Fields and Initial Energy Density in High Energy Nuclear Collisions

> We calculate the classical gluon field created at early times in collisions of large nuclei at high energies. We find that the field is dominated by the longitudinal chromoelectric and chromomagnetic components. We estimate the initial energy density of this gluon field to be approximately 260 GeV/fm$^3$ at RHIC.

# Near-Fields and Initial Energy Density in High Energy Nuclear Collisions

## Key claim
The classical gluon field created at early times in high-energy nuclear collisions is dominated by longitudinal chromoelectric and chromomagnetic components, with transverse fields only appearing at higher order in proper time. The initial energy density estimate of approximately 260 GeV/fm³ at RHIC depends on an ultraviolet cutoff Q₀ and is only a partial contribution from soft gluon modes with transverse momentum below that cutoff.

## Method
The authors solve the classical Yang-Mills equations in the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model using a power-series expansion in proper time τ (a "near-field" approximation) to obtain the gluon field in the forward light cone after nuclear overlap. They introduce a mean-field approximation with a screening length Rc and an ultraviolet cutoff Q₀ via coarse-graining of color charges into Gaussian profiles, then compute the ensemble-averaged energy-momentum tensor at τ→0 using SU(3) color charge distributions weighted by parton densities from CTEQ.

## Key numbers
- Initial energy density ε₀ ≈ 260 GeV/fm³ at RHIC for central Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV, using a UV cutoff Q₀ = 2.5 GeV
- Saturation scale Qs varies only between 1.4 and 1.7 GeV when Q₀ is varied between 1 and 10 GeV
- The critical energy density for deconfinement is εc ≈ 2 GeV/fm³, which the initial value exceeds by over two orders of magnitude
- The result is compatible with 130 GeV/fm³ at τ = 0.1 fm/c found by Lappi (2006)

## Context
This work extends the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model and the color glass condensate (CGC) framework for initial conditions in heavy-ion collisions. The near-field expansion reveals that longitudinal chromoelectric and chromomagnetic fields dominate at τ=0, a feature not emphasized in prior work by Kovner, McLerran, and Weigert (1995) or in numerical solutions by Krasnitz and Venugopalan (1998) and Lappi (2003). The result enables matching between classical soft gluon fields and hard perturbative processes, and provides input for hydrodynamic simulations that typically assume thermalization times τ₀ between 0.15 and 1.0 fm/c.
