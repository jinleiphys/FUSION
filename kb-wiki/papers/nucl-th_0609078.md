# Parity-Violating Observables of Two-Nucleon Systems in Effective Field Theory

> A newly-proposed parity-violating nucleon-nucleon interaction based on effective field theory is studied in this work. It is found that at low energy, i.e., E_lab less than 90 MeV for 1S0-3P0 transitions and E_lab less than 40 MeV for 3S1-1P1 and 3S1-3P1 transitions, the parity-violating observables can be completely specified by a minimal set of six parameters. It contains five low-energy constants, which are equivalent to the Danilov parameters, and an additional parameter that characterizes the long-range one-pion exchange and is proportional to the parity-violating pion-nucleon coupling constant. Selected observables in two-nucleons systems are analyzed in this framework with their dependence on these parameters being expressed in a nearly model-independent way.

## Key claim

At low energy (E_lab less than 90 MeV for 1S0-3P0 transitions and E_lab less than 40 MeV for 3S1-1P1 and 3S1-3P1 transitions), parity-violating observables in two-nucleon systems can be completely specified by a minimal set of six parameters, but this reduction relies on the condition that the matrix elements of the spatial operators y_{m+} and y_{m-} have nearly the same energy dependence, which is not perfectly realized in realistic calculations.

## Method

The author used a hybrid effective field theory (EFT) framework, combining state-of-the-art phenomenological wave functions from the Argonne v18 potential with the general operator structure from EFT for the parity-violating interaction. Both a pionless EFT (with ten low-energy constants in the short-range interaction) and a pionful EFT (adding one-pion exchange and two-pion exchange terms) were studied. The parity-violating scattering amplitudes were computed via first-order distorted-wave Born approximation, and the energy dependence of the S-P amplitudes was analyzed to determine the conditions under which the 10-to-5 reduction of low-energy constants holds.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron binding energy is E_D = 2.22 MeV.
- The chiral symmetry breaking scale is Lambda_chi = 1.161 GeV, related to the pion decay constant F_pi by Lambda_chi = 4 pi F_pi.
- The pion mass used is m_pi = 139.57 MeV; the rho meson mass used is m_rho = 771.1 MeV.
- For the v-type amplitudes (1S0-3P0 transitions), the S-P dominance holds up to E_lab = 90 MeV within 10% tolerance.
- For the u-type (3S1-1P1) and w-type (3S1-3P1) amplitudes, the S-P dominance holds only up to E_lab = 40 MeV within 10% tolerance.
- For the v and w amplitudes, the scaling condition (ratio of y_{m+} to y_{m-} matrix elements being nearly constant) works within 10% up to E_lab = 100 MeV for the "bare" calculations.
- For the u amplitude, the 10% tolerance for scaling deviation only allows E_lab up to 40 MeV.
- The two-pion-exchange constants C2^{2pi} and C6^{2pi} are smaller than the one-pion-exchange constant C6^{pi} by roughly an order of magnitude; the short-range LECs C's are smaller than C6^{pi} by roughly a factor of m_rho^2/m_pi^2 ~ 30.

## Context

This work extends the EFT formulation of parity-violating nucleon-nucleon interactions proposed by Zhu et al. (2004) by quantitatively determining the energy ranges where the minimal six-parameter description (five Danilov parameters plus the parity-violating pion-nucleon coupling constant h_pi^1) is valid. It provides the foundation for a systematic re-analysis of parity-violating observables in few-body systems, moving beyond the earlier one-meson-exchange framework of Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein (1980) which has shown inconsistencies when constrained by experimental data. The results enable future searches for a consistent picture of nuclear parity violation using the hybrid EFT approach.
