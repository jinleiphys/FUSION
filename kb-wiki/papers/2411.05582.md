# Condensation and prescaling in spatial Polyakov loop correlations far from equilibrium

> The far-from-equilibrium dynamics of spatial Polyakov loop correlations, which provide gauge-invariant observables akin to effective particle numbers for gluon plasmas, are investigated within real-time $\mathrm{SU}(N_c)$ lattice gauge theory at weak couplings and large gluon occupations. The momentum zero mode of these correlations reveals the dynamic formation of a condensate, while at nonzero momenta, energy is transported toward the ultraviolet. We demonstrate that the non-zero momentum dynamics is well described by a direct cascade in terms of gauge-invariant Polyakov loop excitations, exhibiting self-similar prescaling indicative of a nonthermal attractor. This behavior can be analytically understood through perturbation theory for the Polyakov loop correlations and the established dynamics of gauge field correlations. We perform simulations for both $\mathrm{SU}(2)$ and $\mathrm{SU}(3)$ gauge groups, providing further consistency checks on the $N_c$-dependence of perturbative expectations. No evidence of an inverse cascade toward lower momenta is found for momenta above the electric screening scale.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that spatial Polyakov loop correlations in far-from-equilibrium SU(N_c) gauge theories exhibit a direct energy cascade toward the ultraviolet with self-similar prescaling, while simultaneously showing dynamic condensate formation in the zero mode, but only for momenta above the electric screening scale where no evidence of an inverse cascade is found.

## Method

Real-time classical-statistical lattice gauge theory simulations were performed for SU(2) and SU(3) gauge groups in 3+1 dimensions with weak couplings and large gluon occupations, using Gaussian initial conditions with momentum scale Q and spatial lattice sizes up to N_s=512. The time evolution of gauge fields was computed from the classical lattice Hamiltonian equations of motion, and connected two-point correlations of spatial Polyakov loop traces were extracted as gauge-invariant observables in momentum space.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of Polyakov loop correlations (Berges 2019, Berges 2023) to momenta above the electric screening scale, and builds on established results for nonthermal fixed points in gluonic plasmas (Berges 2013, Berges 2014, Schlichting 2012, Kurkela 2012). The prescaling analysis follows the framework developed for kinetic theory (Mazeliauskas 2018) and Bose gases (Schmied 2018), and the results enable future studies of gauge-invariant condensate dynamics and nonthermal attractors in gauge theories, particularly for understanding the early-time evolution of quark-gluon plasmas in heavy-ion collisions.
