# Enhanced Minijet Production in $A-A$ Collisions from Gluons with Large Transverse Momenta

> We find supersaturation for the intrinsic gluon distribution of nuclei, i.e. the low $x$ unintegrated nuclear gluon distribution peaks at intermediate transverse momenta $k_t=Q_s$ and vanishes at zero $k_t$. Taking into account the intrinsic transverse momenta of the gluons and the saturation scale $p_s$ of the produced gluons, we calculate the minijet cross section arising from gluon gluon scattering for RHIC energies. For central collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200GeV$ the saturation scale $p_s \approx 1.4$ GeV is larger due to intrinsic $k_t$ effects and increases with energy. Our theoretical results on charged particle multiplicity agree very well with the recent experimental data from RHIC.

## Key claim

The authors claim that including intrinsic transverse momenta of gluons in cold nuclei leads to "supersaturation" (the unintegrated gluon distribution peaks at an intermediate scale \(Q_s\) and vanishes at zero \(k_t\)), and that this effect enhances minijet production and raises the saturation scale of produced gluons \(p_s\) to about 1.4–1.5 GeV at RHIC energies, yielding charged particle multiplicities that agree well with PHENIX and PHOBOS data.

## Method

The authors derive the unintegrated gluon distribution of a nucleus by inverting the color-dipole–nucleus cross section obtained from Glauber theory, using a parameterized dipole–proton cross section. They then incorporate this intrinsic-\(k_t\) distribution into a \(k_t\)-factorization scheme for \(2\to2\) QCD processes to compute the minijet cross section for \(A+A\) collisions. A saturation condition is imposed on the produced gluons in the central rapidity region: the product of the produced gluon number and their individual transverse area \(\pi/p_s^2\) must not exceed the nuclear overlap area \(F(b)\), which determines the saturation scale \(p_s(b)\) self-consistently.

## Key numbers

* For central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV, the saturation scale of produced gluons is \(p_s \approx 1.4\) GeV (with intrinsic \(k_t\) effects) and \(p_s \approx 1.35\) GeV from the self-consistent calculation.
* For central collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=56\) GeV, \(p_s = 1.2\) GeV; at \(\sqrt{s}=130\) GeV, \(p_s = 1.3\) GeV.
* The intrinsic saturation scale for a single nucleus at central impact parameter (\(b=0\)) is \(Q_s \approx 0.96\) GeV at \(x=10^{-2}\) for gold (\(A=197\), \(R_A=6.37\) fm).
* The parameter \(\beta\) in the saturation condition is taken as \(\beta=1.8\), based on classical Yang-Mills simulations which give \(\beta=1.4-2.0\).

## Context

This work extends the saturation approach of Mueller and Qiu (1986) and Blaizot and Mueller (1987) by incorporating intrinsic transverse momentum effects from the unintegrated gluon distribution, which produces "supersaturation" rather than the usual saturation plateau. It contrasts with the local saturation model of Eskola, Kajantie, and Tuominen (2000), which gives a stronger centrality dependence of \(p_s\) and poorer agreement with peripheral data. The results enable predictions for charged particle multiplicity as a function of centrality at RHIC and suggest measuring "monojet" production to directly determine the intrinsic saturation scale \(Q_s\).
