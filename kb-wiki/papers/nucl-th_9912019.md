# What is the Physics behind the 3He-4He Anomaly ?

> We show that coalescence of nucleons emitted prior to thermalization in highly excited nuclei can explain the anomaly of kinetic energies of helium clusters. A new coalescence algorithm has been included into the statistical approach to nuclear reactions formerly used to describe intermediate mass fragment production.

# What is the Physics behind the 3He-4He Anomaly?

## Key claim
The anomalous observation that 3He clusters have higher mean kinetic energies than 4He clusters in intermediate-energy nuclear reactions can be explained by coalescence of nucleons emitted prior to thermalization, provided that the contributions from thermal production and coalescence are comparable; the anomaly disappears when both isotopes are predominantly produced by coalescence at sufficiently high beam energies.

## Method
The authors incorporated a new iterative coalescence algorithm into the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM) to simulate fragment production in central Au+Au collisions. Preequilibrium nucleons were assumed to populate the many-body momentum space uniformly, and clusters were formed when nucleons had relative momenta less than a coalescence radius p0 ≈ 94 MeV/c (corresponding to relative velocity vrel = 0.1c). The thermal source parameters (excitation energy, mass, radial flow) were parametrized by fitting charge distributions and multiplicities of intermediate mass fragments from previous SMM analyses.

## Key numbers
- Coalescence radius p0 ≈ 94 MeV/c, corresponding to relative velocities vrel = 0.1c
- For p+Ag at 1 GeV: ⟨Ekin(3He)⟩ = 17.7 ± 0.3 MeV, ⟨Ekin(4He)⟩ = 12.7 ± 0.2 MeV
- For 3He+Ag at 55 MeV: ⟨Ekin(3He)⟩ = 19.8 MeV, ⟨Ekin(4He)⟩ = 15.6 MeV
- For Au+Au at 100 A·MeV: ⟨Ekin(3He)⟩ = 85.0 ± 4.0 MeV, ⟨Ekin(4He)⟩ = 59.0 ± 2.0 MeV
- For Au+Au at 250 A·MeV: ⟨Ekin(3He)⟩ = 147.0 ± 4.0 MeV, ⟨Ekin(4He)⟩ = 136.0 ± 3.0 MeV
- For Au+Au at 1150 A·MeV: the anomaly vanishes (3He and 4He kinetic energies become similar)
- Transition in mass distribution occurs at ⟨p⟩/p0 ≈ 2, where ⟨p⟩ is the average momentum of thermalized nucleons
- Iterative coalescence procedure requires M = 5 steps for practical convergence

## Context
This work extends the statistical approach to nuclear reactions previously used to describe intermediate mass fragment production (Bondorf et al., SMM; Gross et al., MMMC) by adding a coalescence mechanism for light cluster formation. The paper contradicts the expectation from thermal models (Mekjian) that ⟨Ekin(3He)⟩ ≈ ⟨Ekin(4He)⟩ and from radial flow models (Siemens) that ⟨Ekin(3He)⟩ < ⟨Ekin(4He)⟩. The approach enables future inclusion of coalescence in dynamical models (Gutbrod, Ono, Hombach) and is qualitatively consistent with the EES model explanation (Friedman, 1990) for the puzzle around Fermi energy. The authors note that the coalescence mechanism may explain fragment production features across a wide energy range.
