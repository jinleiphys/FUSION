# Landau quantization and spin polarization of cold magnetized quark matter

> The magnetic field and density behaviors of various thermodynamic quantities of strange quark matter under compact star conditions are investigated in the framework of the thermodynamically self-consistent quasiparticle model. For individual species, a larger number density $n_i$ leads to a larger magnetic field strength threshold that align all particles parallel or antiparallel to the magnetic field. Accordingly, in contrast to the finite baryon density effect which reduces the spin polarization of magnetized strange quark matter, the magnetic field effect leads to an enhancement of it. We also compute the sound velocity as a function of the baryon density and find the sound velocity shows an obvious oscillation with increasing density. Except for the oscillation, similar to the zero-magnetic field case that the sound velocity grows with increasing density and approaches the conformal limit $V_s^2=1/3$ at high densities from below.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in cold magnetized strange quark matter under compact star conditions, the magnetic field enhances spin polarization while finite baryon density reduces it, and that the squared sound velocity approaches the conformal limit \(V_s^2 = 1/3\) from below at high densities, but with an oscillation pattern that becomes more pronounced at stronger magnetic fields.

## Method

The authors use a thermodynamically self-consistent quasiparticle model with a chemical-potential-dependent effective quark mass (derived from hard dense loop resummation) and a running strong coupling, extended to include Landau quantization in a uniform external magnetic field. They impose \(\beta\)-equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions to solve for the chemical potentials of up, down, strange quarks and electrons at zero temperature, then compute number densities, relative spin polarization \(\Delta_i\), and the squared sound velocity \(V_s^2\) as functions of baryon density and magnetic field strength.

## Key numbers

- The electron chemical potential \(\mu_e\) peaks at approximately \(n_b \approx 0.20\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) for both \(B_m = 10^{17}\ \text{G}\) and \(B_m = 10^{18}\ \text{G}\).
- At \(n_b = 2n_0\) (with \(n_0 = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\)), the electron maximum Landau level \(\nu_{e,\text{max}}\) drops to one at around \(1.8 \times 10^{16}\ \text{G}\); at \(n_b = 4n_0\), this occurs at around \(1.5 \times 10^{16}\ \text{G}\).
- Electrons become completely aligned antiparallel to the magnetic field (\(\Delta_e = -1\)) at approximately \(3.6 \times 10^{16}\ \text{G}\) for \(n_b = 2n_0\) and \(3.0 \times 10^{16}\ \text{G}\) for \(n_b = 4n_0\).
- Up, strange, and down quarks become completely saturated (\(\Delta_i = \pm 1\)) at magnetic field strengths of \(1.3 \times 10^{19}\ \text{G}\), \(1.7 \times 10^{19}\ \text{G}\), and \(2.5 \times 10^{19}\ \text{G}\) respectively at \(n_b = 2n_0\).
- The relative spin polarization of electrons begins to depart from zero at around \(10^{15}\ \text{G}\); for quarks, this onset is at around \(3.0 \times 10^{17}\ \text{G}\) and becomes notable after \(10^{18}\ \text{G}\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of strange quark matter in magnetic fields (e.g., Chakrabarty (1996), Isayev (2015), Menezes (2009)) by using a thermodynamically self-consistent quasiparticle model with a running coupling, rather than a fixed coupling or the MIT bag model. It provides the first analysis of spin polarization in dense strange quark matter within this framework, and complements prior work on sound velocity in zero magnetic field (Wen (2009)). The results enable more realistic modeling of the equation of state and transport properties of magnetized quark matter in compact stars, particularly for understanding the role of Landau quantization in spin alignment and the stiffness of the matter at high density.
