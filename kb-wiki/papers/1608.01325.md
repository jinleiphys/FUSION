# Canonical description of the new LHCb resonances

> The LHCb Collaboration has recently observed four $J/ψϕ$ structures called $X(4140)$, $X(4274)$, $X(4500)$ and $X(4700)$ in the $B^{+}\to J/ψϕK^{+}$ decays. We study them herein using a nonrelativistic constituent quark model in which the degrees of freedom are quark-antiquark and meson-meson components. The $X(4140)$ resonance appears as a cusp in the $J/ψϕ$ channel due to the near coincidence of the $D_{s}^{\pm}D_{s}^{\ast\pm}$ and $J/ψϕ$ mass thresholds. The remaining three $X(4274)$, $X(4500)$ and $X(4700)$ appear as conventional charmonium states with quantum numbers $3^{3}P_{1}$, $4^{3}P_{0}$ and $5^{3}P_{0}$, respectively; and whose masses and widths are slightly modified due to their coupling with the corresponding closest meson-meson thresholds. A particular feature of our quark model is a lattice-based screened linear confining interaction that has been constrained in the light quark sector and usually produces higher excited heavy-quark states with lower masses than standard quark model predictions.

## Key claim
The paper argues that three of the four LHCb resonances (X(4274), X(4500), X(4700)) can be described as conventional charmonium states with specific quantum numbers, while the X(4140) is not a resonance at all but a threshold cusp arising from the near coincidence of the D_s D_s* and J/psi phi mass thresholds.

## Method
The authors used a nonrelativistic constituent quark model with quark-antiquark and meson-meson components, solving the Schrodinger equation via the Gaussian expansion method. They performed coupled-channel calculations including the relevant open-flavor thresholds (such as D*D_1, D_s D_s*, D_s* D_s*, and J/psi phi) using a 3P0 decay model for the transition operator. The line shape of the J/psi phi channel was analyzed by evaluating production via direct point-like sources and via intermediate c c-bar states.

## Key numbers
- X(4274) as 3^3P_1 charmonium: predicted mass 4271.5 MeV (experiment 4273.3 ± 8.3 MeV), total decay width 29.8 MeV (experiment 56 ± 11+8-11 MeV)
- X(4500) as 4^3P_0 charmonium: predicted mass 4497.2 MeV (experiment 4506 ± 11+12-15 MeV), total decay width 115.11 MeV (experiment 92 ± 21+21-20 MeV)
- X(4700) as 5^3P_0 charmonium: predicted mass 4697.6 MeV (experiment 4704 ± 10+14-24 MeV), total decay width 122.02 MeV (experiment 120 ± 30+42-33 MeV)
- Coupled-channel calculation for X(4274): mass 4242.4 MeV, total decay width 25.9 MeV, with 48.7% c c-bar and 43.5% D_s D_s* components
- Coupled-channel calculation for X(4500): mass 4493.6 MeV, total decay width 79.2 MeV, with 57.2% c c-bar component
- Coupled-channel calculation for X(4700): mass 4674.1 MeV, total decay width 50.2 MeV, with 47.6% c c-bar component
- No pole found for X(4140); the structure is interpreted as a cusp from the D_s D_s* threshold

## Context
This work extends the constituent quark model approach of Vijande (2004) and Segovia (2008) to the newly observed LHCb resonances. It contradicts molecular interpretations by Liu (2009), Branz (2009), and others that predicted X(4140) as a 0++ or 2++ D_s* D_s* molecule, and tetraquark models by Drenska (2009) and Wang (2015) that predicted different quantum numbers. The results enable future experimental searches for the predicted decay patterns, particularly the dominant D*D_2* final state for X(4700) with ~50% branching fraction, and reinforce the validity of screened-linear confining potentials for describing excited heavy quark states.
