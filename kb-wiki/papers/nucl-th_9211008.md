# Test of Scalar Meson Structure in $ϕ$ Radiative Decays

> We show that $ϕ$ radiative decays into scalar mesons [$f_0$(975), $a_0$(980)$\equiv S$] can provide important clues on the internal structures of these mesons. Radiative decay widths vary widely: $B.R.=10^{-4}-10^{-6}$ depending on the substructures ($q\bar q$, $qq\bar q\bar q$, $K\bar K$, glueball). Hence, we could discriminate among various models by measuring these widths at future $ϕ$ factories. The understanding of these meson structures is valuable not only in hadron spectroscopy but also in nuclear physics in connection with the widely-used but little-understood $σ$ meson. We also find that the decay $ϕ\rightarrow Sγ\rightarrow K^0 \bar K^0γ$ is not strong enough to pose a significant background problem for studying CP violation via $ϕ\rightarrow K^0 \bar K^0$ at the $ϕ$ factories.

## Key claim

The branching ratios for $\phi$ radiative decays into scalar mesons $f_0(975)$ and $a_0(980)$ vary widely (from $10^{-4}$ to $10^{-6}$) depending on whether the mesons are $q\bar q$, $qq\bar q\bar q$, $K\bar K$, or glueball states, so measuring these widths at future $\phi$ factories could discriminate among the models; however, the calculations are naive order-of-magnitude estimates at an early stage.

## Method

The author estimated electric-dipole (E1) decay widths for $\phi \rightarrow S\gamma$ by scaling from observed charmonium E1 decays (e.g., $\chi_{c0}\rightarrow J/\psi\gamma$) using the relation $\Gamma(E1) \sim e^2 R^2 E_\gamma^3$, with charge factors and hadron sizes adjusted for each scalar-meson model ($n\bar n$, $s\bar s$, $qq\bar q\bar q$, $K\bar K$ molecule, glueball). For the $K\bar K$-molecule picture, a more detailed calculation introduced a momentum-space dipole form factor for the $SK\bar K$ vertex with a cutoff parameter related to the $K\bar K$ radius, and included an interaction current to maintain gauge invariance, evaluating the loop integrals numerically.

## Key numbers

- Branching ratio for $\phi\rightarrow f_0\gamma$ assuming $f_0=s\bar s$: $B.R. \approx 1 \times 10^{-5}$ (scaled from charmonium E1 decay).
- Branching ratio for $\phi\rightarrow f_0\gamma$ and $\phi\rightarrow a_0\gamma$ assuming both are $n\bar n$: $B.R. \lapproxeq 10^{-6}$ (OZI-violating).
- Branching ratio for $\phi\rightarrow f_0\gamma$ in the $K\bar K$-molecule picture with radius 1.2 fm: $B.R. = 4 \times 10^{-5}$ (width $1.8\times 10^{-4}$ MeV).
- Branching ratio for $\phi\rightarrow f_0\gamma$ if $f_0$ is a glueball: $B.R. \lapproxeq 10^{-6}$ (from mixing estimate $\lapproxeq 1/20$ with $q\bar q$).
- Ratio $\Gamma(\phi\rightarrow a_0\gamma)/\Gamma(\phi\rightarrow f_0\gamma) \approx 9$ for the $D\bar D$-like $qq\bar q\bar q$ bag model.
- Branching ratio for the background process $\phi\rightarrow K^0\bar K^0\gamma$: $B.R. \lapproxeq 10^{-7}$ (too small to be a serious background for CP-violation studies).
- Pointlike $K\bar K$ loop calculation gives width $6.3\times 10^{-4}$ MeV; with 1.2 fm radius it becomes $1.8\times 10^{-4}$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the investigation of scalar meson structure beyond the naive $q\bar q$ picture, which fails to explain the narrow width of $f_0(975)$ (33.6 MeV experimentally vs. 500-1000 MeV predicted for $q\bar q$) and is motivated by the $K\bar K$-molecule model of Weinstein and Isgur (1982) and the four-quark bag model of Jaffe (1977). The paper also addresses a background concern for CP-violation measurements at $\phi$ factories raised by Nussinov and Truong (1989) and others, finding that the radiative decay background is negligible. The results enable future $\phi$ factory experiments (proposed at Frascati, KEK, Novosibirsk, and UCLA) to distinguish between competing models of the scalar mesons, which is also relevant for nuclear physics through the poorly understood $\sigma$ meson.
