# Impact-parameter dependent nuclear parton distribution functions: EPS09s and EKS98s and their applications in nuclear hard processes

> We determine the spatial (impact parameter) dependence of nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDFs) using the $A$-dependence of the spatially independent (averaged) global fits EPS09 and EKS98. We work under the assumption that the spatial dependence can be formulated as a power series of the nuclear thickness functions $T_A$. To reproduce the $A$-dependence over the entire $x$ range we need terms up to $[T_A]^4$. As an outcome, we release two sets, EPS09s (LO, NLO, error sets) and EKS98s, of spatially dependent nPDFs for public use. We also discuss the implementation of these into the existing calculations. With our results, the centrality dependence of nuclear hard-process observables can be studied consistently with the globally fitted nPDFs for the first time. As an application, we first calculate the LO nuclear modification factor $R^{1jet}_{AA}$ for primary partonic-jet production in different centrality classes in Au+Au collisions at RHIC and Pb+Pb collisions at LHC. Also the corresponding central-to-peripheral ratios $R_{CP}^{1jet}$ are studied. We also calculate the LO and NLO nuclear modification factors for single inclusive neutral pion production, $R_{dAu}^{π^0}$, at mid- and forward rapidities in different centrality classes in d+Au collisions at RHIC. In particular, we show that our results are compatible with the PHENIX mid-rapidity data within the overall normalization uncertainties given by the experiment. Finally, we show our predictions for the corresponding modifications $R_{pPb}^{π^0}$ in the forthcoming p+Pb collisions at LHC.

## Key claim

The authors determine the spatial (impact-parameter) dependence of nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDFs) by assuming it can be expressed as a power series of nuclear thickness functions, with terms up to fourth order needed to reproduce the A-dependence of the spatially averaged global fits EPS09 and EKS98.

## Method

The spatial dependence of the nuclear modification for each parton flavor is parametrized as a power series of the nuclear thickness function T_A(s) up to fourth order, with coefficients that depend on x and Q² but not on A. These coefficients are determined by fitting to reproduce the A-dependence of the spatially averaged nuclear modifications from the EPS09 and EKS98 global fits, minimizing a χ² function over nuclei with A ≥ 16. The resulting spatially dependent nPDF sets are released as EPS09s (LO, NLO, and error sets) and EKS98s.

## Key numbers

- The fit reproduces the spatially averaged nuclear modifications within 2% at x < 0.75 for EPS09NLO, 1% at x < 0.85 for EPS09LO, and 0.2% at x < 0.95 for EKS98 for all parton flavors and nuclei included in the fits.
- For Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV, the central 0-5% centrality class corresponds to impact parameters b₁ = 0.0 fm and b₂ = 3.355 fm with ⟨N_bin⟩ = 1083, while the peripheral 60-80% class corresponds to b₁ = 11.62 fm and b₂ = 13.42 fm with ⟨N_bin⟩ = 15.10.
- For Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV, the central 0-5% class has b₁ = 0.0 fm and b₂ = 3.478 fm with ⟨N_bin⟩ = 1771, while the peripheral 60-80% class has b₁ = 12.05 fm and b₂ = 13.91 fm with ⟨N_bin⟩ = 19.08.
- The peripheral R_AA^(1jet) shows nearly 10% antishadowing effects at mid-p_T at RHIC and more than 20% shadowing effects at small p_T at the LHC, with up to 10% EMC effects at large p_T at both colliders.

## Context

This work extends the spatially averaged global nPDF fits EPS09 (Eskola 2009) and EKS98 (Eskola 1998) by adding impact-parameter dependence for the first time in a manner consistent with those global analyses. Earlier attempts at spatial dependence, such as those by Eskola (1991), Emel'yanov (1999), Klein (2003), and Vogt (2004), lacked consistency with global fits. The results enable consistent computation of centrality-dependent nuclear hard-process observables and allow comparison with the Gribov-Glauber modeling of nuclear shadowing (Frankfurt 2011), which previously was the only approach with self-consistent spatial dependence. The work enables future studies of centrality-dependent jet quenching and hadron production in d+Au and p+Pb collisions at RHIC and LHC.
