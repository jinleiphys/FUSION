# Nucleon structure from basis light-front quantization

> We produce the light-front wave functions (LFWFs) of the nucleon from a basis light-front ap- proach in the leading Fock sector representation. We solve for the mass eigenstates from a light-front effective Hamiltonian, which includes a confining potential adopted from light-front holography in the transverse direction, a longitudinal confinement, and a one-gluon exchange interaction with fixed coupling. We then employ the LFWFs to obtain the electromagnetic and axial form factors, the par- ton distribution functions (PDFs) and the generalized parton distribution functions for the nucleon. The electromagnetic and axial form factors of the proton agree with the experimental data, whereas the neutron form factors deviate somewhat from the experiments in the low momentum transfer region. The unpolarized, the helicity, and the transversity valence quark PDFs, after QCD scale evolution, are fairly consistent with the global fits to the data at the relevant experimental scales. The helicity asymmetry for the down quark also agrees well with the measurements, however, the asymmetry for the up quark shows a deviation from the data, especially in the small x region. We also find that the tensor charge agrees well with the extracted data and the lattice QCD predictions, while the axial charge is somewhat outside the experimental error bar. The electromagnetic radii of the proton, the magnetic radius of the neutron, and the axial radius are in excellent agreement with the measurements, while the neutron charge radius deviates from experiment.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a basis light-front quantization (BLFQ) approach restricted to the leading (three-valence-quark) Fock sector, with an effective Hamiltonian combining holographic confinement and one-gluon exchange, can reproduce many nucleon observables; however, the neutron form factors deviate from experiment at low momentum transfer, the up-quark helicity asymmetry deviates especially at small Bjorken x, and the axial charge falls outside the experimental error bar.

## Method

The authors solve for nucleon mass eigenstates from a light-front effective Hamiltonian using basis light-front quantization (BLFQ) in the leading Fock sector (three valence quarks). The Hamiltonian includes a transverse confining potential from light-front holography, a longitudinal confinement term, and a one-gluon exchange interaction with fixed coupling constant. The resulting light-front wave functions (LFWFs) are then used to compute electromagnetic and axial form factors, parton distribution functions (PDFs), and generalized parton distribution functions (GPDs) for the nucleon.

## Key numbers

- Quark mass in kinetic energy: 0.3 GeV; quark mass in OGE interaction: 0.2 GeV; confining strength κ = 0.34 GeV; coupling constant α_s = 1.1 ± 0.1 (for basis truncations N_max = 10, K = 16.5)
- Proton electromagnetic radii, neutron magnetic radius, and axial radius are in "excellent agreement" with measurements; neutron charge radius deviates from experiment
- Tensor charge agrees well with extracted data and lattice QCD predictions; axial charge is "somewhat outside the experimental error bar"
- Helicity asymmetry for down quark agrees well with measurements; up-quark helicity asymmetry shows deviation especially in the small x region

## Context

This work extends the BLFQ framework previously applied to QED systems (electron anomalous magnetic moment, positronium) and mesons (light mesons, heavy quarkonia, heavy-light mesons) to the nucleon. It builds on earlier proton studies by Xu et al. (2019) and incorporates the holographic QCD confinement potential from Brodsky et al. (2014) supplemented by longitudinal confinement from Li et al. (2017). The results enable future extensions to include higher Fock sectors (quark-antiquark pairs and gluons) and to compute additional observables such as transverse momentum distributions and Wigner distributions.
