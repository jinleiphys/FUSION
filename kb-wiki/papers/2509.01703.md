# The N$^3$LO Twist-2 Matching of Linearly Polarized Gluon TMDs

> We compute the twist-2 matching of the transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) linearly polarized gluon parton distribution and fragmentation functions at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^3$LO) in QCD, supplemented by next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic (NNLL) small-$x$ resummation for the gluon TMD fragmentation functions. These results provide high-precision fixed-order and resummed inputs to TMD phenomenology, and constitute essential theoretical ingredients for future studies of the spin structure and three-dimensional tomography of hadrons at the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC).

## Key claim

The paper presents the first analytic computation of the twist-2 matching coefficient for linearly polarized gluon transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) parton distribution functions (PDFs) at N$^3$LO in QCD, supplemented by next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic (NNLL) small-$x$ resummation for the gluon TMD fragmentation functions (FFs); the abstract's claim of "high-precision fixed-order and resummed inputs" is conditioned on the perturbative convergence being well-controlled only for momentum fractions $x > 10^{-3}$, with resummation effects becoming important at extremely small $x$.

## Method

The matching coefficients for linearly polarized gluon TMD PDFs and FFs are computed using operator product expansion (OPE) onto collinear PDFs and FFs, with the bare gluon beam function evaluated from splitting amplitudes integrated over collinear phase space using an exponential rapidity regulator. The computation proceeds by first evaluating the $k_\perp$-unintegrated beam function in momentum space, then applying a Fourier transformation to impact parameter space, followed by UV renormalization and zero-bin subtraction to obtain finite coefficient functions. The N$^3$LO results are presented as numerical fits to analytic expressions, with the scale-independent parts fitted in the region $10^{-6} < x < 1$ using elementary functions that subtract end-point divergences up to next-to-next-to-leading power.

## Key numbers

- The N$^3$LO matching coefficients for linearly polarized gluon TMD PDFs and FFs are computed at twist-2, with numerical fits provided for the $g \to g$ and $q \to g$ (PDF) and $g \to g$ and $g \to q$ (FF) channels
- In the $z \to 1$ limit, the N$^3$LO $g \to g$ coefficient function behaves as: $\lim_{z\to1} \mathcal{I'}^{(3)}_{gg}(z) = C_A^3(-\frac{68}{9}\ln(1-z)+\frac{4}{3}\zeta_2-\frac{836}{27}) + C_A^2N_fT_F(\frac{152}{9}\ln(1-z)-\frac{8}{3}\zeta_2+\frac{2000}{27}) + C_A N_f^2 T_F^2(-\frac{32}{9}\ln(1-z)-\frac{224}{27}) - 8 C_A C_F N_f T_F$
- The numerical fits in the full region $0 < x < 1$ have an accuracy better than $10^{-3}$
- The Higgs boson mass is taken as $m_H = 125$ GeV for the application to Higgs $p_T$ distributions

## Context

This work extends the NNLO matching coefficients for linearly polarized gluon TMDs computed by Gutierrez-Reyes et al. (2019), Luo et al. (2019), and Catani et al. (2022) to N$^3$LO, while the unpolarized gluon distribution matching was previously known at N$^3$LO from Ebert et al. (2020) and Luo et al. (2020). The results provide essential theoretical input for precision studies of azimuthal asymmetries in semi-inclusive DIS and dijet production at the upcoming Electron-Ion Collider (EIC), and enable improved predictions for Higgs boson transverse momentum distributions at hadron colliders.
