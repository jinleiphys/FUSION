# The influence of reconstruction criteria on the sensitive probes of the symmetry potential

> Different criteria of constructing clusters and tracing back $Δ$ resonances from the intermediate-energy neutron-rich HICs are discussed by employing the updated UrQMD transport model. It is found that both the phase-space and the coordinate-density criteria affect the single and the double neutron/proton ratios of free nucleons at small transverse momenta, but the influence becomes invisible at large transverse momenta. The effect of different methods of reconstructing freeze-out $Δ$s on the $Δ^0/Δ^{++}$ ratio is strong in a large kinetic energy region.

## Key claim

The sensitivity of the neutron/proton ratio of free nucleons and the Δ⁰/Δ⁺⁺ ratio to the density dependence of the symmetry potential depends strongly on the reconstruction criteria used to identify clusters and Δ resonances, with the influence of these criteria becoming negligible only at large transverse momenta for nucleons but remaining significant across all kinetic energies for Δ resonances.

## Method

The updated UrQMD transport model was used to simulate central collisions of ¹³²Sn+¹³²Sn and ¹¹²Sn+¹¹²Sn at 800A MeV and Pb+Pb at 400A MeV, employing a soft equation of state with momentum dependence (SM-EoS) and four forms of density-dependent symmetry potential (γ = 0.5, 1.0, 1.5, and the DDHρ* very soft potential). Free nucleons were identified using either a coalescence model with two sets of phase-space distance parameters (RP-I: R₀=2.8 fm, P₀=0.2 GeV/c; RP-II: R₀=3.5 fm, P₀=0.3 GeV/c) or a coordinate density cut (ρ_c = ρ₀/10), while Δ resonances at freeze-out were reconstructed using two methods: M-I where only the pion daughter does not rescatter, and M-II where neither daughter rescatters.

## Key numbers

- For Pb+Pb at 400A MeV, the initial n/p ratio of the system is 1.54; the RP-I mode drives the n/p ratio at small p_t toward this value.
- The influence of reconstruction criteria on the n/p ratio becomes invisible for transverse momenta p_t > 0.6 GeV/c across all three cluster-construction modes (RP-I, RP-II, Rc).
- For ¹³²Sn+¹³²Sn at 800A MeV, the Δ⁰/Δ⁺⁺ ratio shows a "crossing" behavior between soft and stiff symmetry potentials in the M-II mode but not in the M-I mode, and the difference between the two reconstruction methods persists even at kinetic energies above 0.5 GeV.
- The cut-time for stopping the transport program was set to t_c = 100 fm/c for all further investigations.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Li (2005) on the n/p ratio of free nucleons and by Li (2005) on Δ ratios as probes of the symmetry potential at supranormal densities, systematically testing how the reconstruction methods themselves affect these observables. The results show that the n/p ratio at large transverse momenta remains a clean probe despite reconstruction uncertainties, but the Δ⁰/Δ⁺⁺ ratio requires careful experimental reconstruction procedures before it can be reliably used, informing future experiments at facilities such as FAIR (Germany) and the cooling storage ring CSR (Lanzhou, China).
