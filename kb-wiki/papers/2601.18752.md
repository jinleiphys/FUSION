# Role of the symmetry energy on hybrid stars

> The impact of the symmetry energy on the properties of compact stars is analyzed considering constraints from nuclear physics and astrophysics. A compact star can be a neutron star composed only of nuclear matter or a hybrid star with a quark core. Two typical models (soft and stiff) are considered for the nuclear equation of state, and for the hybrid one, a parameterized first-order phase transition approach, completed with a linear quark matter equation of state, is implemented. We show that the phase transition reduces the tension between GW170817 and NICER observations, and we illustrate the impact of the symmetry energy for the understanding of the nature of the binary system in GW170817. We also confirm our previous findings that the GW170817 waveform is best described as a binary HS with a low-density onset of stiff quark matter. This could also be interpreted as a quarkyonic cross-over.

## Key claim

The paper shows that introducing a first-order phase transition to quark matter reduces the tension between the soft nuclear equation of state favored by GW170817 tidal deformability constraints and the stiff nuclear equation of state favored by NICER radius measurements, but the specific nature of the hybrid star solution depends on the stiffness of the nuclear symmetry energy.

## Method

The authors adopt two representative nuclear equations of state: a soft one (Skyrme SLy5) and a stiff one (relativistic PKDD), and combine each with a parameterized first-order phase transition to quark matter described by a linear equation of state controlled by three parameters (onset pressure, latent heat, and sound speed in quark matter). They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations and the pulsation equation to obtain masses, radii, and tidal deformabilities for hybrid stars, then apply Bayesian inference to quantify how nuclear physics constraints and astrophysical observations (GW170817, NICER, and radio pulsar masses) filter the allowed parameter space.

## Key numbers

- The soft nuclear model SLy5 has a slope of the symmetry energy L_sym,2 = 48.3 MeV; the stiff model PKDD has L_sym,2 = 79.5 MeV.
- The saturation density for SLy5 is n_sat = 0.1604 fm^-3; for PKDD it is n_sat = 0.1500 fm^-3.
- The symmetry energy at saturation for SLy5 is E_sym = 32.72 MeV; for PKDD it is E_sym = 37.94 MeV.
- The quadratic contribution to the symmetry energy at saturation for SLy5 is E_sym,2 = 32.03 MeV; for PKDD it is E_sym,2 = 36.79 MeV.
- The slope of the symmetry energy for SLy5 is L_sym = 49.8 MeV; for PKDD it is L_sym = 93.4 MeV.
- The kinetic energy per nucleon in symmetric matter at saturation is t^FG_sat approximately 22.1 MeV.
- The typical quark matter onset densities n_opt and transition densities n_tr are 2-3 n_sat.
- The maximum observed mass constraint is M_max^obs approximately 2 solar masses.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Bayesian analysis (Guven 2023) by explicitly examining how the nuclear symmetry energy influences the properties of hybrid stars with quark cores. It addresses the tension between the soft equation of state required by GW170817 tidal deformability measurements (Abbott 2017) and the stiff equation of state needed to support massive pulsars (Antoniadis 2013), a tension previously noted by Guven (2020), Somasundaram (2021), and Mondal (2023). The parameterized first-order phase transition model follows the approach of Zdunik (2013), Alford (2013), and Chamel (2013), and the results confirm the earlier finding that the GW170817 waveform is best described as a binary hybrid star system with a low-density onset of stiff quark matter, which could also be interpreted as a quarkyonic cross-over (McLerran 2019, Margueron 2021).
