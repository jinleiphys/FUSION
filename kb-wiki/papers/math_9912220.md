# Factorization theorem for the transfer function of a 2x2 operator matrix with unbounded couplings

> We consider the analytic continuation of the transfer function associated with a 2x2 operator matrix having unbounded couplings into unphysical sheets of its Riemann surface. We construct a family of non-selfadjoint operators which factorize the transfer function and reproduce certain parts of its spectrum including the nonreal (resonance) spectrum situated in the unphysical sheets neighboring the physical sheet.

## Key claim

The paper proves that the analytically continued transfer function of a 2x2 operator matrix with unbounded couplings can be factorized by a family of non-selfadjoint operators, whose spectrum reproduces the resonance spectrum on unphysical sheets, provided certain smallness conditions on the weighted variation of the coupling operators are satisfied.

## Method

The authors consider a 2x2 operator matrix acting in a direct sum of Hilbert spaces, where the diagonal entry A0 is an unbounded selfadjoint operator semibounded from below and the off-diagonal coupling operators Tij satisfy T01* = T10 with domain containing D(A0^(1/2)). They construct the analytic continuation of the transfer function M1(z) through the absolutely continuous spectrum of A0 into unphysical sheets using contour integration, then introduce a nonlinear operator equation Y = tilde_A1 + V1(Y,Gamma) whose solutions factorize the continued transfer function. Solvability of this equation is proved via Banach's fixed point theorem under conditions on the weighted variation Var_{tilde_A1}(B,Gamma).

## Key numbers

* The weighted variation condition for solvability is Var_{tilde_A1}(B,Gamma) < 1, with the additional inequality Var_{tilde_A1}(B,Gamma) * ||tilde_A1|| < (1/4) * d0(Gamma) * [1 - Var_{tilde_A1}(B,Gamma)]^2, where d0(Gamma) = dist{sigma(tilde_A1), Gamma}.
* The solution X satisfies ||X|| <= r_min(Gamma), where r_min(Gamma) = (1/2)*d0(Gamma)*[1 - Var_{tilde_A1}(B,Gamma)] - sqrt( (1/4)*d0(Gamma)^2*[1 - Var_{tilde_A1}(B,Gamma)]^2 - d0(Gamma)*Var_{tilde_A1}(B,Gamma)*||tilde_A1|| ).
* The factor W1(z,Gamma_l) in the factorization is boundedly invertible when dist{z, sigma(tilde_A1)} <= d0(Gamma_l)*[1 - Var_{tilde_A1}(B,Gamma_l)]/2, with the bound ||[W1(z,Gamma_l)]^{-1}|| <= (1 - 4*Var_{tilde_A1}(B,Gamma_l)*[d0(Gamma_l)+||tilde_A1||] / (d0(Gamma_l)*[1+Var_{tilde_A1}(B,Gamma_l)]^2))^{-1}.
* The operator Omega^{(l)} satisfies ||Omega^{(l)}|| < 1.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Mennicken and Motovilov, 1999) from bounded to unbounded coupling operators in 2x2 operator matrices, using a weighted variation with theta=1 instead of theta=0 to handle the unboundedness of A0. The factorization approach recalls the framework of Markus and Matsaev (1970s) and Virozub and Matsaev (1970s) for operator pencils. The results enable the study of resonance spectra in unphysical sheets for physical systems described by such operator matrices, with applications to problems in plasma physics (Goedbloed, Lifschitz) and nuclear physics (Motovilov and Rem).
