# System size and shape dependences of collective flow fluctuations in relativistic nuclear collisions

> Quantum fluctuations plays an essential role in forming the collective flow of hadrons observed in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Event-by-event fluctuations of the collective flow can arise from various sources, such as the fluctuations in the initial geometry, hydrodynamic expansion, hadronization, and hadronic evolution of the nuclear matter, while the exact contribution from each source is still an open question. Using a (3+1)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamic model coupled to a Monte-Carlo Glauber initial condition, Cooper-Frye particlization and a hadronic transport model, we explore the system size and shape dependences of the collective flow fluctuations in Au+Au, Cu+Au, and O+O collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=200$~GeV. The particle yields, mean transverse momenta, 2-particle and 4-particle cumulant elliptic flows ($v_2\{2\}$ and $v_2\{4\}$) from our calculation agree with the currently existing data from RHIC. Different centrality dependences of the flow fluctuations, quantified by the $v_2\{4\}/v_2\{2\}$ ratio, are found for different collision systems due to their different sizes and shapes. By comparing $v_2\{4\}/v_2\{2\}$ between different hadron species, and comparing $v_2\{4\}/v_2\{2\}$ to the initial state geometric fluctuations quantified by the cumulant eccentricity ratio $\varepsilon_2\{4\}/\varepsilon_2\{2\}$, we find that while the initial state fluctuations are the main source of the $v_2$ fluctuations in large collision systems, other sources like nonlinear hydrodynamic response, hadronization, and hadronic afterburner can significantly affect the $v_2$ fluctuations in small systems.

## Key claim

While initial state geometric fluctuations are the main source of elliptic flow fluctuations in large collision systems (Au+Au), other sources such as nonlinear hydrodynamic response, hadronization, and hadronic afterburner significantly affect the flow fluctuations in small systems (O+O), as demonstrated by comparing the ratio of 4-particle to 2-particle cumulant elliptic flow between different hadron species and to the initial state eccentricity ratio.

## Method

The authors used a (3+1)-dimensional relativistic viscous hydrodynamic model (CLVisc) with Monte-Carlo Glauber initial conditions, Cooper-Frye particlization, and the SMASH hadronic transport model to simulate Au+Au, Cu+Au, and O+O collisions at 200 GeV per nucleon pair. They computed particle yields, mean transverse momenta, and 2-particle and 4-particle cumulant elliptic flow coefficients ($v_2\{2\}$ and $v_2\{4\}$) across different centrality bins, then compared the ratio $v_2\{4\}/v_2\{2\}$ to the initial state eccentricity ratio $\varepsilon_2\{4\}/\varepsilon_2\{2\}$ to isolate the sources of flow fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- Inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section: $\sigma_\mathrm{NN}^\mathrm{inel} = 42$ mb at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 200$ GeV
- Specific shear viscosity: $C_{\eta_\mathrm{v}} = 0.08$ used throughout calculations
- Baryon diffusion coefficient parameter: $C_\mathrm{B} = 0.4$ used throughout calculations
- Freeze-out energy density: $e_\mathrm{frz} = 0.26$ GeV/fm$^3$ for the hadronization hypersurface
- 500 events simulated per centrality bin for Au+Au collisions; 2000 sampling repetitions per hydrodynamic event for Au+Au and Cu+Au; 20000 for O+O collisions
- Nuclear density parameters: $^{197}$Au radius $R = 6.38$ fm, surface thickness $a = 0.535$ fm; $^{64}$Cu $R = 4.21$ fm, $a = 0.598$ fm; $^{16}$O $R = 2.61$ fm, $a = 0.513$ fm, $w = -0.051$

## Context

This work extends previous studies of flow fluctuations in large systems (Pb+Pb and Au+Au) at LHC and RHIC energies, where fluctuations were found insensitive to collision energy and dominated by initial geometry, particularly in central collisions (Alba 2017, Schenke 2019, Magdy 2020). It provides systematic predictions for the ongoing O+O measurements at RHIC (Huang 2023) and comparisons to recent Cu+Au data from STAR (2022), using the same hydrodynamic framework previously applied to Au+Au collisions (Wu 2021). The results enable disentangling initial-state geometric effects from final-state hydrodynamic and hadronic contributions to flow fluctuations across different system sizes.
