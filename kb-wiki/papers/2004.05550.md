# Momentum-dependent potential and collective flows within the relativistic quantum molecular dynamics approach based on relativistic mean-field theory

> Relativistic quantum molecular dynamics based on the relativistic mean field theory (RQMD.RMF) is extended by including momentum-dependent potential. The equation of state (EoS) dependence of the directed and the elliptic flow of protons in the beam energy range of $2.3 < \sqrt{s_{NN}}< 20$ GeV is examined. It is found that the directed flow depends strongly on the optical potential at high energies,$\sqrt{s_{NN}} > 3 $ GeV, where no information is available experimentally. The correlation between effective mass at saturation density and the optical potential is found: smaller values of effective mass require smaller strengths of the optical potential to describe the directed flow data.This correlation can also be seen in the beam energy dependence of the elliptic flow at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}>3$ GeV, although its effect is rather weak. On the other hand, stiff EoS is required to describe the elliptic flow at lower energies.Experimental constraints on the optical potential from $pA$ collisions will provide important information on the EoS at high energies.The proton directed and the elliptic flow are well described in the RQMD.RMF model from $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.3$ to 8.8 GeV. In contrast,to reproduce the collapse of the directed flow above 10 GeV, pressure has to be reduced, which indicates a softening of the EoS around $\sqrt{s_{NN}} =10 $ GeV.

## Key claim

The directed flow of protons in heavy-ion collisions depends strongly on the optical potential at high energies (√sNN > 3 GeV), where no experimental information on the optical potential is available; the correlation between effective mass at saturation density and the optical potential means that smaller effective masses require smaller optical potential strengths to describe the directed flow data.

## Method

The authors extended the relativistic quantum molecular dynamics approach based on relativistic mean-field theory (RQMD.RMF) by incorporating a momentum-dependent potential of Lorentzian form for both scalar and vector interactions, following the formulation of Weber (1992). They implemented this into the transport code JAM and simulated midcentral Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions across beam energies from √sNN = 2.3 to 20 GeV, using several parameter sets with different incompressibilities, effective masses, and optical potential strengths. The directed and elliptic flow of protons were computed and compared to experimental data from E895, STAR, and NA49.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy range studied: 2.3 < √sNN < 20 GeV (1 < Elab < 160 A GeV)
- Directed flow depends strongly on optical potential at √sNN > 3 GeV
- Proton directed and elliptic flow are well described from √sNN = 2.3 to 8.8 GeV
- Collapse of directed flow observed above √sNN ≈ 10 GeV, indicating softening of the EoS
- Parameter sets used: incompressibilities K = 210 MeV and 380 MeV; effective mass ratios m*/m = 0.65, 0.7, and 0.83; asymptotic optical potentials Uopt(∞) ranging from -0.4 to 200 MeV
- Normal nuclear matter density: ρ0 = 0.168 fm⁻³
- Binding energy per nucleon: E/A = -16 MeV at saturation
- Gaussian width: L = 1.0 fm²
- Impact parameter range for midcentral collisions: 4.6 < b < 9.4 fm

## Context

This work extends the RQMD.RMF approach of Nara (2019) by adding momentum-dependent potentials, which were previously incorporated only in relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (RBUU) models by Weber (1992) and Maruyama (1992, 1993). The study addresses the long-standing problem of extracting the equation of state from collective flow measurements, following earlier work by Stoecker (1980, 1981, 1986) and Danielewicz (2002). The results enable future experimental constraints from pA collisions to determine the optical potential at high energies, which will provide important information on the EoS at high baryon densities relevant to the RHIC beam energy scan program and future FAIR and NICA experiments.
