# Dissipative superfluid hydrodynamics for the unitary Fermi gas

> In this work we establish constraints on the temperature dependence of the shear viscosity $η$ in the superfluid phase of a dilute Fermi gas in the unitary limit. Our results are based on analyzing experiments that measure the aspect ratio of a deformed cloud after release from an optical trap. We discuss how to apply the two-fluid formalism to the unitary gas, and provide a suitable parametrization of the equation of state. We show that in expansion experiments the difference between the normal and superfluid velocities remains small, and can be treated as a perturbation. We find that expansion experiments favor a shear viscosity that decreases significantly in the superfluid regime. Using an exponential parametrization we find $η(T_c/(2T_F))< 0.37η(T_c/T_F)$, where $T_c$ is the critical temperature, $T_F$ is the local Fermi temperature of the gas.

## Key claim

The paper establishes that expansion experiments of the unitary Fermi gas favor a shear viscosity that decreases significantly below the superfluid transition temperature, with the constraint that the viscosity at half the critical temperature is less than 0.37 times the viscosity at the critical temperature, using an exponential parametrization.

## Method

The authors analyze aspect ratio measurements from expansion experiments of a unitary Fermi gas released from a deformed optical trap (Joseph et al., 2014), applying two-fluid superfluid hydrodynamics with a perturbative treatment of the difference between normal and superfluid velocities. They solve the fluid dynamic equations using an anisotropic fluid dynamics method, parametrizing the low-temperature shear viscosity as an exponential function of the ratio of critical temperature to temperature, and compare the predicted aspect ratios to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature of the unitary Fermi gas: T_c = 0.167(3) T_F (from Ku et al., 2011)
- Shear viscosity at T_c: eta/n approximately 0.4 (from Bluhm et al., 2017)
- Constraint from exponential parametrization: eta(T_c/(2T_F)) less than 0.37 eta(T_c/T_F)
- Trap frequencies used in the experiment: (omega_x, omega_y, omega_z) = (2pi)(2210, 830, 64.3) Hz, with residual magnetic bowl omega_mag = 2pi x 21.5 Hz
- Fixed measurement time: t* = 1.2 milliseconds
- Initial aspect ratio: A_R(0) approximately 0.37
- Virial expansion coefficients for shear viscosity above T_c: eta_0 = 0.265 +/- 0.02, eta_2 = 0.060 +/- 0.02 (from Bluhm et al., 2017)
- Data constrain viscosity in the regime 0.5 less than or approximately equal to (T/T_c) less than or approximately equal to 1.0

## Context

This work extends previous hydrodynamic analyses of the unitary Fermi gas above T_c (Bluhm et al., 2017) into the superfluid regime, and addresses conflicting predictions for the low-temperature behavior of shear viscosity: kinetic theory with phonon quasiparticles predicts eta/n grows as T approaches 0 (Rupak and Schafer, 2007), while alternative quasiparticle models and Quantum Monte Carlo calculations (Wlazlowski et al., 2013) predict eta/n approaches zero. The results enable improved understanding of transport in strongly correlated quantum fluids, with implications for neutron matter and the quark-gluon plasma.
