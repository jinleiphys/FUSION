# Modern shell-model diagonalizations with realistic NN forces

> The spectral and statistical properties of nuclei $^{46}$V and $^{48}$Cr are studied in the framework of nuclear shell model. A microscopical effective Hamiltonian derived from the CD-Bonn \textit{NN} potential is employed. The calculations are preliminary results of a newly developed parallel shell-model code. Calculations with the realistic \textit{NN} interaction reproduce well the ground-state rotational band and backbending phenomenon in $^{48}$Cr. A sudden alignment of all particles is seen above the backbend. The nearest-neighbour spacing distribution in $^{46}$V is presented and compared with the predictions of random-matrix theory. Significant deviation from the single Gaussian-orthogonal-ensemble distribution is obtained, indicating that the isospin-symmetry breaking is not so strong in the nucleus.

## Key claim
Calculations with a realistic isospin-nonconserving effective Hamiltonian derived from the CD-Bonn NN potential reproduce the yrast band and backbending in ⁴⁸Cr, while the nearest-neighbour spacing distribution in ⁴⁶V shows significant deviation from the single Gaussian-orthogonal-ensemble distribution, indicating that isospin-symmetry breaking is not so strong in the nucleus.

## Method
The authors developed a new parallel shell-model code in Fortran 90 using a hybrid algorithm that generates M-scheme bases, then projects onto good angular momentum J via a partition-by-partition procedure, and diagonalizes the Hamiltonian matrix with the Lanczos method. The effective Hamiltonian was derived microscopically from the CD-Bonn NN potential, with single-particle energies taken from experimental observations relative to a ⁴⁰Ca core, including the Thomas-Ehrman shift of proton 1p orbits. Calculations were performed in the full fp shell for ⁴⁸Cr and ⁴⁶V, using up to 30 processors on a Beowulf cluster.

## Key numbers
- The largest j-j scheme dimension in the calculations is about 2.5 × 10⁵, for which scalability using up to 30 processors remains optimal.
- For ⁴⁸Cr, the backbend appears at I = 12⁺, and excellent agreement with experiment is seen up to the band-terminated state at I = 16⁺.
- For ⁴⁶V, the lowest 150 positive-parity states for each spin between I = 0 and 16 were calculated, yielding 2550 states generated on a single processor within one hour.
- The isospin symmetry breaking in the effective Hamiltonian includes Coulomb shifts of proton 1p single-particle orbits of about 200 keV.

## Context
This work extends previous shell-model studies of ⁴⁸Cr by Caurier et al. (1995) using a modified Kuo-Brown interaction, now employing a microscopically derived interaction from the CD-Bonn potential. The statistical analysis of ⁴⁶V addresses the controversy over isospin symmetry breaking effects on nearest-neighbour spacing distributions, previously studied in sd-shell nuclei like ²⁶Al by Mitchell et al. (1988) and in Ca isotopes by Caurier et al. (1996). The new parallel code enables efficient calculations in the fp shell and can be applied to larger systems and to further investigate the role of isospin symmetry in nuclear chaos.
