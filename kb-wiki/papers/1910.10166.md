# Power Counting the Small-$x$ Observables

> We emphasize the importance of applying power counting to the small-$x$ observables, which introduces novel soft contributions usually missing and allows for a unified treatment of the Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) evolution and various Sudakov logarithms. We use $pA \to h(p_{h\perp})X$ at forward rapidity to highlight how the power counting yields a partonic cross section with collinear and soft sectors. We show how the kinematic constraints can be obtained in the soft sector without violating the power counting. We further show how one can resum the threshold Sudakov logarithms systematically to all orders in a re-factorized framework with additional collinear-soft contributions. Direct applications to other small-$x$ processes involving heavy particles, jet (sub-)observables and EIC physics are straightforward.

# Power Counting the Small-$x$ Observables

## Key claim

The paper argues that applying observable-originated power counting to small-$x$ processes introduces novel soft contributions that are usually missing in previous treatments, and that this approach allows for a unified framework that simultaneously incorporates the Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) evolution and systematic resummation of threshold Sudakov logarithms, provided one carefully accounts for the kinematic constraints in the soft sector without violating the power counting.

## Method

The authors use single inclusive hadron production in proton-nucleus collisions, $pA \to h(p_{h\perp})X$ at forward rapidity, as a concrete example. They perform a systematic power expansion in the small parameter $\lambda = p'_{\perp}/\bar{n}\cdot p \ll 1$ to identify three distinct modes (collinear, soft, and Glauber) that contribute at leading power. They re-derive the NLO corrections using covariant Feynman rules with dimensional regularization and an $\eta$-regulator for rapidity divergences, then separate the cross section into collinear and soft sectors. For the threshold region where $1-z \sim \mathcal{O}(\lambda)$, they introduce an additional collinear-soft mode and re-factorize the cross section to enable resummation of threshold Sudakov logarithms via renormalization group evolution.

## Key numbers

- The small parameter defining forward scattering is $\lambda = p'_{\perp}/\bar{n}\cdot p \sim \sqrt{-t/s} \ll 1$, where $p'_{\perp}$ is the outgoing quark transverse momentum.
- The saturation scale $Q_s$ characterizes the typical transverse momentum of gluons at small-$x$ and grows as $x \to 0$, with $Q_s \gg \Lambda_{\rm QCD}$ in the perturbative regime.
- The NLO rapidity pole structure yields a logarithm $\ln(p_\perp'^2 / (\nu \bar{n}\cdot p))$ which, when minimized, sets the rapidity scale $\nu = p_\perp'^2 / (\bar{n}\cdot p) = x_A \sqrt{s}$, relating it to the gluon momentum fraction $x_A$ from the nucleus.
- The collinear-soft function's anomalous dimension at leading order has $\Gamma^{(0)} = \alpha_s/\pi$ and $\gamma^{(0)} = 0$, with the next-to-leading cusp anomalous dimension $\Gamma^{(1)} = (\alpha_s/\pi)^2 \frac{1}{4}[(67/9 - \pi^2/3)C_A - (20/9)T_F n_F]$.

## Context

This work extends the NLO calculations for small-$x$ processes in the color glass condensate (CGC) formalism, particularly the seminal work by Chirilli, Xiao, and Yuan (2011) which confirmed CGC factorization for inclusive hadron production at NLO. It addresses the difficulty noted in earlier attempts by Kang, Ma, and Yuan (2014) and Liu, Ma, and Yuan (2019) regarding the relation between the rapidity scale and the nucleus target. The framework enables systematic threshold resummation, which was previously out of reach for maintaining positivity of the cross section as noted by Xiao, Yuan, and Zhang (2018), and provides a unified treatment applicable to heavy particle production, jet observables, and precision predictions for the future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC).
