# Probing quark gluon plasma properties by heavy flavours

> The Fokker Planck (FP) equation has been solved to study the interaction of non-equilibrated heavy quarks with the Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP) expected to be formed in heavy ion collisions at RHIC energies. The solutions of the FP equation have been convoluted with the relevant fragmentation functions to obtain the $D$ and $B$ meson spectra. The results are compared with experimental data measured by STAR collaboration. It is found that the present experimental data can not distinguish between the $p_T$ spectra obtained from the equilibrium and non-equilibrium charm distributions. Data at lower $p_T$ may play a crucial role in making the distinction between the two. The nuclear suppression factor, $R_{\mathrm AA}$ for non-photonic single electron spectra resulting from the semileptonic decays of hadrons containing heavy flavours have been evaluated using the present formalism. It is observed that the experimental data on nuclear suppression factor of the non-photonic electrons can be reproduced within this formalism by enhancing the pQCD cross sections by a factor of 2 provided the expansion of the bulk matter is governed by the velocity of sound, $c_s\sim 1/\sqrt{4}$. Ideal gas equation of state fails to reproduce the data even with the enhancement of the pQCD cross sections by a factor of 2.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the nuclear suppression factor (R_AA) for non-photonic electrons from heavy-flavor decays can be reproduced by enhancing pQCD cross sections by a factor of 2, but only if the expanding QGP bulk matter follows an equation of state with sound velocity c_s ~ 1/sqrt(4); the ideal gas equation of state (c_s ~ 1/sqrt(3)) fails to reproduce the data even with the same cross-section enhancement.

## Method

The authors solved the Fokker-Planck equation to model the Brownian motion of non-equilibrated charm and bottom quarks in a thermalized QGP bath, using pQCD-based drag and diffusion coefficients. The resulting heavy-quark momentum distributions were convoluted with three different sets of fragmentation functions to obtain D and B meson p_T spectra, which were then compared to STAR data. For the nuclear suppression factor R_AA, the electron spectra from semileptonic decays of D and B mesons were computed, and the space-time evolution of the QGP was modeled with relativistic hydrodynamics using different equations of state (characterized by sound velocity).

## Key numbers

- Initial conditions for RHIC: T_i = 400 MeV, tau_i = 0.2 fm, giving dN/dy ~ 1100.
- Initial conditions for LHC: T_i = 700 MeV, tau_i = 0.08 fm, giving dN/dy = 2100.
- Drag and diffusion coefficients were evaluated at T = 200 MeV for charm and bottom quarks.
- Blast wave fit to D meson data with equilibrium distribution gave freeze-out temperature T_F = 0.170 GeV and surface flow velocity beta_s = 0.6.
- For non-equilibrium (FP) distributions, beta_s values were 0.45 (SET-I), 0.35 (SET-II), and 0.4 (SET-III) at the same T_F = 170 MeV.
- R_AA data could be reproduced with c_s = 1/sqrt(4) and pQCD cross sections enhanced by a factor of 2; c_s = 1/sqrt(3) (ideal gas EoS) failed even with the same enhancement.

## Context

This work extends the Fokker-Planck approach to heavy-quark energy loss in QGP (Svetitsky 1988, Moore 2005, Rapp 2005) by explicitly comparing equilibrium and non-equilibrium charm distributions against STAR D meson data. It finds that current data cannot distinguish the two scenarios, but lower p_T measurements could. The paper also addresses the puzzle of non-photonic electron suppression measured by STAR and PHENIX, showing that the equation of state (sound velocity) of the expanding medium is a critical parameter. The results enable future studies that couple the Fokker-Planck equation with full 3D hydrodynamics including a source term for energy deposition by heavy quarks.
