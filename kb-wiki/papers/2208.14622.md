# Impacts of the direct URCA and Superfluidity inside a Neutron Star on Type-I X-Ray Bursts and X-Ray Superbursts

> We investigate the impacts of neutrino cooling mechanism inside the neutron star (NS) core on the light curves of type-I X-ray bursts and X-ray superbursts. From several observations of NS thermal evolution, physical processes of fast neutrino cooling, such as the direct Urca (DU) process, are indicated. They significantly decrease the surface temperature of NSs, though the cooling effect could be suppressed by nucleon superfluidity. In the present study, focusing on the DU process and nucleon superfluidity, we investigate the effects of NS cooling on the X-ray bursts using a general-relativistic stellar-evolution code. We find that the DU process leads find the longer recurrence time and the higher peak luminosity, which could be obstructed by the neutrons superfluidity. We also apply our burst models to the comparison with {\it Clocked burster} GS 1826$-$24, and to the recurrence time of superburst triggered by carbon ignition. These effects are significant within a certain range of binary parameters and uncertainty of the NS equation of state.

## Key claim

The direct Urca (DU) process inside a neutron star core leads to longer recurrence times and higher peak luminosities in type-I X-ray bursts, but this effect can be partially or fully obstructed by neutron superfluidity, with the degree of suppression depending strongly on the specific neutron \(^3P_2\) superfluid model (critical temperature and density dependence).

## Method

The authors used a one-dimensional general-relativistic stellar-evolution code with an 88-nuclei reaction network for mixed H/He burning, adopting the LS220 equation of state. They calculated steady-state thermal profiles of accreting neutron stars including crustal heating, minimal cooling processes, the direct Urca process, and various models of neutron \(^3P_2\) superfluidity (TTav, TToa, AO, AO3) plus the CCDK model for \(^1S_0\) superfluidity, then simulated sequences of type-I X-ray bursts and carbon ignition conditions for superbursts.

## Key numbers

- For a 2.0 \(M_\odot\) neutron star (radius 11.3 km) with accretion rate \(\dot{M}_{-9}=2.0\), the recurrence time \(\Delta t\) ranges from 3.54 hr (No DU model) to 3.90 hr (Normal and CCDK models); models with strong neutron \(^3P_2\) superfluidity give intermediate values: 3.83 hr (CCDK+TToa), 3.81 hr (CCDK+AO), 3.76 hr (CCDK+AO3).
- Ignition pressure \(P_{\rm ign}\) at \(\dot{M}_{-9}=2.0\) ranges from \(4.18\times10^{22}\) dyn cm\(^{-2}\) (No DU) to \(4.60\times10^{22}\) dyn cm\(^{-2}\) (Normal, CCDK).
- The ratio \(P_{\rm ign}/\Delta t\) is constant at \(1.2\times10^{22}\) dyn cm\(^{-2}\) hr\(^{-1}\) across all models.
- The DU process threshold density for LS220 EOS is reached for neutron star masses \(M_{\rm NS}\gtrsim1.35~M_\odot\).
- The nuclear saturation density for LS220 EOS is \(\rho_0 = 2.57\times10^{14}\) g cm\(^{-3}\).
- The maximum critical temperature for the CCDK \(^1S_0\) superfluid model is \(T_{\rm cr}\simeq6\times10^9\) K at \(\rho=0.56\rho_0\) for neutrons and \(1.1\rho_0\) for protons.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Dohi et al. 2021) which considered only slow cooling scenarios, by now including the direct Urca process and nucleon superfluidity. It builds on observations of neutron star thermal evolution (e.g., Cassiopeia A, indicating minimal cooling scenarios) and the clocked burster GS 1826-24, which was previously modeled by Johnston et al. (2020) to constrain accretion rate and metallicity. The results enable more realistic modeling of X-ray burst light curves and superburst recurrence times by incorporating core neutrino cooling physics, and show that the DU process and superfluidity effects are significant within a certain range of binary parameters and equation of state uncertainty.
