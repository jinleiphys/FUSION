# Diproton correlation in a proton-rich Borromean nucleus 17Ne

> We carry out three-body-model calculations for a proton-rich Borromean nucleus $^{17}$Ne by assuming a $^{15}$O + $p$ + $p$ structure. To this end, we use a density-dependent contact interaction between the valence protons, explicitly treating also the Coulomb interaction. We find that the two-particle density distribution for $^{17}$Ne is similar to that for $^{16}$C, which has two valence neutrons outside the $N$ = 8 core. That is, the two protons take a spatially compact configuration, while the Coulomb repulsion plays a minor role. This indicates that there is a strong diproton correlation in the ground state of the $^{17}$Ne nucleus. We also show that the Coulomb interaction reduces the expectation value of the proton-proton interaction by about 14\%.

## Key claim

The ground state of the proton-rich Borromean nucleus 17Ne exhibits a strong diproton correlation, with the two valence protons taking a spatially compact configuration despite the Coulomb repulsion between them; the Coulomb interaction reduces the expectation value of the proton-proton interaction by about 14%, but plays a minor role in the density distribution.

## Method

The authors performed three-body-model calculations for 17Ne assuming a 15O + p + p structure, using a density-dependent contact interaction between the valence protons with explicit treatment of the Coulomb interaction. The three-body Hamiltonian was solved by expanding the ground state wave function with single-particle wave functions in a truncated space with a cutoff energy of 60 MeV, including single-particle angular momentum up to l=12. The strength of the pairing interaction was determined using the neutron-neutron scattering length (a_nn = -18.5 fm) assuming charge independence of nuclear force, while the density-dependent parameters were adjusted to reproduce the ground state energy of 17Ne at -0.944 MeV from the two-proton emission threshold.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy of 17Ne: -0.944 MeV measured from the two-proton emission threshold
- Expectation value of nuclear proton-proton interaction: -3.26 MeV
- Expectation value of Coulomb proton-proton interaction: 0.448 MeV
- Coulomb repulsion reduces pairing energy by about 14%
- Root-mean-square distance between valence protons: 4.688 fm
- Root-mean-square distance between center of mass of protons and core: 3.037 fm
- Opening angle between valence protons: 76.64 degrees
- Matter radius of 17Ne: 2.62 fm
- s-wave probability P([s_1/2]^2): 15.16%
- d_5/2 probability P([d_5/2]^2): 75.19%
- d_3/2 probability P([d_3/2]^2): 3.83%
- Sum rule value for E1 transition B(E1)_SR: 1.494 e^2 fm^2

## Context

This work extends the three-body model approach previously applied to dineutron correlations in neutron-rich Borromean nuclei like 11Li and 6He (Hagino and Sagawa, 2005) to the proton-rich side, specifically 17Ne. The results are compared with similar calculations for 16C (Hagino and Sagawa, 2007), showing that despite the Coulomb repulsion, the two-proton density distribution in 17Ne closely resembles the two-neutron distribution in 16C. The study confirms earlier conjectures of diproton correlation in 17Ne (Kobayashi et al., 2009) and enables future investigations of diproton correlation in medium-heavy and heavy proton-rich nuclei, as well as studies of the role of diproton correlation in two-proton radioactivity.
