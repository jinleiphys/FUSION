# Opportunities in (semi-)inclusive deep inelastic scattering

> Inclusive and semi-inclusive deep inelastic leptoproduction offers possibilities to study details of the quark and gluon structure of the hadrons involved. In many of these experiments polarization is an essential ingredient. We also emphasize the dependence on transverse momenta of the quarks, which leads to azimuthal asymmetries in the produced hadrons.

# Opportunities in (semi-)inclusive deep inelastic scattering

## Key claim
Inclusive and semi-inclusive deep inelastic leptoproduction can reveal details of quark and gluon structure of hadrons, but the full exploitation of these opportunities requires the inclusion of transverse momentum dependence of quarks and polarization, which together produce azimuthal asymmetries in the produced hadrons that are accessible at subleading twist.

## Method
The paper presents a tree-level QCD diagrammatic expansion of hard scattering amplitudes for lepton-hadron scattering, separating hard and soft parts at large momentum transfer Q. The soft parts are parametrized as quark-quark correlation functions (distribution and fragmentation functions) including transverse momentum dependence, with the analysis extended to subleading order in 1/Q by incorporating quark-quark-gluon correlation functions related via QCD equations of motion. The resulting cross sections are expressed in terms of twist-two and twist-three distribution and fragmentation functions for polarized spin-1/2 targets.

## Key numbers
- The typical hadronic scale Λ is of order 1 GeV, below which Q must be considerably larger for the hard scattering framework to apply.
- The inclusive cross section up to order 1/Q contains a twist-3 term proportional to M/Q, with coefficient 2y√(1-y) cos(φ_s) x_B^2 g_T(x_B), where M is the target mass.
- The semi-inclusive cross section for unpolarized scattering is proportional to (y²/2 + 1 - y) x_B f_1^a(x_B) D_1^a(z_h) G(Q_T;R)/z_h², with the Gaussian transverse momentum factor G(Q_T;R) = (R²/π) exp(-R² Q_T²).
- The semi-inclusive cross section contains a term proportional to sin(3φ_h - φ_s) with coefficient Q_T³ R⁶/(2M² M_h R_H⁴ R_h²) involving the transverse spin distribution h_{1T}^{⊥a}(x_B) and the Collins fragmentation function H_1^{⊥a}(z_h).

## Context
This work extends the framework of Jaffe and Ji (1992) for k_T-integrated distribution functions to include transverse momentum dependence, building on earlier work by Ralston and Soper (1979) and Tangerman and Mulders (1995). The analysis shows that all six twist-two transverse-momentum-dependent quark distribution functions for a spin-1/2 hadron can be accessed in leading-order asymmetries, enabling future experimental extraction of the transverse spin distribution h_1 (Collins, 1993) and the interaction-dependent fragmentation function H_1^⊥. The results are presented as opportunities for experiments at facilities such as ELFE.
