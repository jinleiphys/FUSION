# Running Coupling Corrections to High Energy Inclusive Gluon Production

> We calculate running coupling corrections for the lowest-order gluon production cross section in high energy hadronic and nuclear scattering using the BLM scale-setting prescription. In the final answer for the cross section the three powers of fixed coupling are replaced by seven factors of running coupling, five in the numerator and two in the denominator, forming a 'septumvirate' of running couplings, analogous to the 'triumvirate' of running couplings found earlier for the small-x BFKL/BK/JIMWLK evolution equations. It is interesting to note that the two running couplings in the denominator of the 'septumvirate' run with complex-valued momentum scales, which are complex conjugates of each other, such that the production cross section is indeed real. We use our lowest-order result to conjecture how running coupling corrections may enter the full fixed-coupling kT-factorization formula for gluon production which includes non-linear small-x evolution.

## Key claim

Running coupling corrections to the lowest-order gluon production cross section replace the three powers of fixed coupling with seven factors of running coupling (a "septumvirate"), with two of those running couplings evaluated at complex-conjugate momentum scales to ensure the cross section remains real; this result is obtained using the BLM scale-setting prescription and applies to the inclusive production of gluons and collinear gluon-gluon or quark-antiquark pairs with invariant mass below a collinear cutoff.

## Method

The authors calculated running coupling corrections to the O(α_s^3) gluon production cross section in high-energy quark-quark scattering using the BLM scale-setting prescription, first resumming all quark bubble corrections (powers of α_s N_f) to the bremsstrahlung and triple-gluon vertex diagrams in the A^+ = 0 light-cone gauge, then completing N_f to the full QCD beta function. To fix the scale of the remaining ambiguous coupling, they redefined the observable to include production of collinear gluon-gluon and quark-antiquark pairs with invariant mass below a collinear IR cutoff Λ_coll, using a spectral representation and the optical theorem to sum the cut diagrams.

## Key numbers

* The fixed-coupling cross section at lowest order is dσ/(d²k_T dy) = (2 α_s³ C_F)/(π²) (1/k²) ∫ d²q / [q² (k-q)²] (Eq. 2.14 in the paper).
* The three powers of fixed coupling α_s in the bremsstrahlung diagrams become α_s(Λ_coll² e^{-5/3}) α_s²(q² e^{-5/3}) after running coupling corrections (Eq. 3.14).
* The final running-coupling cross section contains seven factors of α_s: five in the numerator and two in the denominator, forming a "septumvirate" of running couplings.
* The two running couplings in the denominator run with complex-conjugate momentum scales, ensuring the cross section is real.

## Context

This work extends the running coupling corrections previously calculated for the BFKL, BK, and JIMWLK evolution equations (Kovchegov 2006, Balitsky 2006, Albacete 2007) to the gluon production cross section itself, which had three additional factors of the strong coupling whose scales were unknown. The result enables consistent inclusion of running coupling effects in Color Glass Condensate predictions for particle production in proton-proton, proton-nucleus, and nucleus-nucleus collisions, and provides a conjectured running-coupling generalization of the k_T-factorization formula (Eq. 1.1) that includes non-linear small-x evolution.
