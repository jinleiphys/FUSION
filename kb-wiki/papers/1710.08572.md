# Different production sources of light nuclei in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We propose a new method, i.e., an exclusive quark combination model + an inclusive hadron recombination model, to study different production sources of light nuclei in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. We take deuterons and $^3$He produced in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV as examples to present contributions of different production sources by studying their rapidity densities $dN/dy$, yield ratios and transverse momentum ($p_T$) spectra just after the hadronization as well as at the final kinetic freeze-out. We find that: only very small fractions of $d$ and $^3$He are created just after the hadronization; nucleons from $Δ$ resonance decays make a much larger contribution to the regeneration of light nuclei at the hadronic phase stage, and this contribution takes about 79\% and 92\% for $d$ and $^3$He, respectively, observed at the final kinetic freeze-out. We also find that yield ratios $d/p$, $^3$He$/p$ and $^3$He$/d$ are good observables to probe contributions for light nuclei from different production sources, and provide a natural explanation for constant values of $d/p$ and $^3$He$/p$ as a function of the averaged charged multiplicity measured by the ALICE Collaboration.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that only a very small fraction of deuterons and ³He are created directly at hadronization; instead, the majority (about 79% for deuterons and 92% for ³He) observed at final kinetic freeze-out are regenerated during the hadronic phase, primarily from nucleons originating from Δ resonance decays.

## Method

The authors combine an exclusive quark combination model (SDQCM) to compute momentum distributions of directly produced hadrons and nucleons with an inclusive hadron recombination model that uses Gaussian kernel functions for coordinate and momentum dependencies to describe light nucleus formation. They calculate rapidity densities, yield ratios, and transverse momentum spectra for deuterons and ³He both just after hadronization and at the final kinetic freeze-out in central Pb-Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV, distinguishing surviving nuclei (formed at hadronization) from regenerated ones (formed later from nucleons including Δ decay products).

## Key numbers

- For deuterons: dN/dy just after hadronization = 2.59 × 10⁻²; at final kinetic freeze-out = 10.83 × 10⁻²; surviving = 2.27 × 10⁻²; regenerated = 8.56 × 10⁻²
- For ³He: dN/dy just after hadronization = 0.25 × 10⁻⁴; at final kinetic freeze-out = 2.30 × 10⁻⁴; surviving = 0.19 × 10⁻⁴; regenerated = 2.11 × 10⁻⁴
- About 79% of deuterons and 92% of ³He observed at final kinetic freeze-out are regenerated from Δ resonance decay nucleons
- d/p ratio at kinetic freeze-out = 3.28 × 10⁻³; ³He/p = 0.70 × 10⁻⁵; ³He/d = 2.12 × 10⁻³
- Fireball radius Rf = 6.1 fm just after hadronization and 7.0 fm at final kinetic freeze-out
- Root-mean-square radii used: Rd = 1.96 fm, R³He = 1.76 fm

## Context

This work extends the recombination/coalescence model approach (Butler 1963, Sato 1981, Dover 1991) by quantitatively separating light nuclei produced just after hadronization from those regenerated during hadronic evolution, using the SDQCM (Shao 2005, Shao 2009) for exclusive hadron descriptions. The results provide a natural explanation for the constant d/p and ³He/p ratios as functions of charged multiplicity observed by the ALICE Collaboration (2016), and suggest that the factor of about 2.2 difference in d/p between Pb-Pb and pp collisions arises from the absence of hadronic regeneration in smaller systems. Future measurements of ³He/p and ³He/d in pp collisions could test this decrease effect.
