# Renormalization group analysis of electromagnetic properties of the deuteron

> The role of radiative corrections in low energy nuclear physics is beginning to receive more scrutiny. We examine the impact of these corrections for the deuteron charge form factor and the radiative capture process $np \to d γ$ through the velocity renormalization group. In both cases, we find percent level shifts in the relevant observables after evolving the subtraction velocity to the typical velocity of nucleons in the bound state. This suggests that electromagnetic corrections constitute a non-negligible source of uncertainty in existing few-body calculations.

## Key claim

Radiative corrections from quantum electrodynamics produce percent-level shifts in the deuteron charge form factor and the radiative neutron capture cross section after evolving the subtraction velocity to the typical velocity of nucleons in the bound state, indicating that electromagnetic corrections constitute a non-negligible source of uncertainty in existing few-body calculations.

## Method

The authors use the velocity renormalization group (vRG) within pionless effective field theory (EFT\(_{\not{\pi}}\)) to resum leading-logarithm-in-\(\alpha\) (LL\(_\alpha\)) contributions from ultrasoft photons into the low-energy coefficients of the two-nucleon potential. They calculate the running of the \(C_2\) and \(C_4\) contact interactions in the \(^3S_1\) and \(^1S_0\) channels and the running of the single-nucleon Darwin term \(c_D^{(s)}\), then evaluate the deuteron charge form factor at N\(^2\)LO+LL\(_\alpha\) and the \(np \to d\gamma\) cross section at NLO+LL\(_\alpha\).

## Key numbers

- The running couplings \(\hat C_2\) and \(\hat C_4\) in the \(^3S_1\) channel change by approximately 2–3% when evolved from the matching scale \(\nu = m_\pi/M_N\) to the bound-state scale \(\nu \sim 1/M_N a_s\), as shown in Fig. 3 of the paper.
- The fine-structure constant runs from \(\alpha_{\text{OS}} = 1/137\) at the electron mass \(m_e = 511\) keV to \(\alpha(M_N \nu^2)\) at the ultrasoft scale \(\mu_{US} = M_N \nu^2\).
- The matching conditions for the LECs use the scattering lengths and effective ranges from Wiringa (1995) with \(\alpha = 0\) and shape parameters from de Swart (1995).

## Context

This work extends the vRG analysis of radiative corrections in the two-nucleon system initiated by Richardson and Reis (2024), which found few-percent shifts in the deuteron binding energy at N\(^2\)LO+LL\(_\alpha\). It applies similar techniques to electromagnetic observables, complementing existing pionless EFT calculations of the deuteron charge form factor by Kaplan (1999), Chen (1999), Phillips (2000), and Lensky (2021), and of radiative capture by Rupak (2000). The results enable improved uncertainty quantification in precision cosmology (big bang nucleosynthesis) and muonic atom spectroscopy by separating QED effects from strong-interaction contributions.
