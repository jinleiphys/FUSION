# Current status of dynamical modeling of fluctuations at the QCD phase transition in heavy-ion collisions

> For a complete understanding of the QCD phase diagram it is important to connect first-principle thermodynamic calculations to experimental data from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan and the future experimental facilities FAIR, GSI, and NICA, Dubna. This can only be achieved by a realistic modeling of the dynamical evolution of critical fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions at the QCD phase transition. In this note I will summarize the current status of these dynamical models and highlight some of the important issues, which need to be addressed in the future.

# Current status of dynamical modeling of fluctuations at the QCD phase transition in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
Realistic dynamical modeling of critical fluctuations at the QCD phase transition is necessary to connect first-principle thermodynamic calculations to experimental data from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan and future facilities, but current models are not yet flexible or complete enough to be coupled consistently to bulk models of the underlying noncritical dynamics.

## Method
The paper reviews three dynamical modeling approaches: (1) a nonequilibrium equation of state using an explicit spline over the coexistence and spinodal region in fluid dynamical simulations; (2) nonequilibrium chiral fluid dynamics (NχFD), which combines explicit propagation of order parameters via a Langevin equation with fluid dynamical expansion of the bulk medium; and (3) stochastic viscous fluid dynamics, where noise terms are added to the stress-energy tensor and net-baryon current with correlation functions derived from the dissipation-fluctuation theorem.

## Key numbers
- RHIC Beam Energy Scan Phase I took data at center-of-mass energies per nucleon-nucleon pair of 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV, with an additional run at 14.5 GeV in 2014.
- In NχFD, the correlation length of fluctuations at the critical point only grows up to 1.5–2.5 fm due to critical slowing down.
- The LHC will soon achieve nuclear collisions at a center-of-mass energy per nucleon-nucleon pair of 5 TeV.
- Functional methods (DSE, FRG) currently cannot make reliable claims for baryochemical potential values above μ_B/T ∼ 2.

## Context
This conference proceedings from CPOD2014 reviews the status of dynamical models for fluctuations at the QCD phase transition, extending work on equilibrium thermodynamics from lattice QCD calculations (Aoki 2006, Borsanyi 2010) and functional methods (Fischer 2012, Pawlowski 2005). The paper highlights that while first-principle calculations predict a critical point and first-order phase transition line at finite baryochemical potential, the dynamical evolution in heavy-ion collisions must be modeled to connect these predictions to experimental observables. The work enables future development of consistent models that include both critical fluctuations and noncritical sources of fluctuations, which will be essential for interpreting data from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan Phase II in 2018/2019.
