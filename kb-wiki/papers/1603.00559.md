# Strong decay mode $J/ψp$ of hidden charm pentaquark states $P_c^+(4380)$ and $P_c^+(4450)$ in $Σ_c \bar{D}^*$ molecular scenario

> We study the strong decay channels $P_c^+(4380) \to J/ψp$ and $P_c^+(4450) \to J/ψp$ under $Σ_c \bar{D}^*$ molecular state ansatz. With various spin-parity assignments, the partial decay widths of $J/ψp$ final state are calculated. Our results show that all the $P$ wave $Σ_c {\bar D}^*$ assignments are excluded, while $S$ wave $Σ_c {\bar D}^*$ pictures for $P_c(4380)$ and $P_c(4450)$ are both allowed by present experimental data. The $J^P=3/2^-$ $Σ_c^* \bar{D}$ and $Σ_c^* \bar{D}^*$ molecules are also discussed in the heavy quark limit, and find that the $Σ_c^* \bar{D}$ system for $P_c(4380)$ is possible. More experimental information on spin-parities and partial decay widths and theoretical investigations on other decay modes are needed to clarify the nature of the two $P_c$ states.

# Strong decay mode $J/\psi p$ of hidden charm pentaquark states $P_c^+(4380)$ and $P_c^+(4450)$ in $\Sigma_c \bar{D}^*$ molecular scenario

## Key claim
The $S$-wave $\Sigma_c \bar{D}^*$ molecular assignments for both $P_c(4380)$ and $P_c(4450)$ are allowed by the present experimental data, but all $P$-wave $\Sigma_c \bar{D}^*$ assignments are excluded; the authors note that more experimental information on spin-parities and partial decay widths is needed to clarify the nature of the two states.

## Method
The authors employed an effective Lagrangian approach with a compositeness condition (Weinberg (1962), Salam (1962)) to compute the coupling constants of $P_c$ states to their $\Sigma_c \bar{D}^*$ molecular constituents. They then calculated the partial decay widths for $P_c^+ \to J/\psi p$ via triangle loop diagrams involving $D^*$, $D$, and $\Sigma_c$ exchanges, using coupling constants determined from heavy quark symmetry and SU(4) flavor symmetry. The calculation was performed for spin-parity assignments $J^P = 3/2^\pm$ and $5/2^\pm$, with a Gaussian correlation function characterized by a size parameter $\Lambda$ around 1 GeV.

## Key numbers
- Mass and width of $P_c^+(4380)$: $(M,\Gamma) = (4380\pm8\pm29, 205\pm18\pm86)$ MeV (LHCb, Aaij (2015)).
- Mass and width of $P_c^+(4450)$: $(M,\Gamma) = (4449.8\pm1.7\pm2.5, 39\pm5\pm19)$ MeV (LHCb, Aaij (2015)).
- Branching fraction product for $P_c(4380)$: ${\cal B}(\Lambda_b^0 \to P_c^+(4380) K^-){\cal B}(P_c^+(4380) \to J/\psi p) = 2.56\pm0.22\pm1.28^{+0.46}_{-0.36} \times 10^{-5}$.
- Branching fraction product for $P_c(4450)$: ${\cal B}(\Lambda_b^0 \to P_c^+(4450) K^-){\cal B}(P_c^+(4450) \to J/\psi p) = 1.25\pm0.15\pm0.33^{+0.22}_{-0.18} \times 10^{-5}$.
- Partial decay width for $P_c(4380) \to J/\psi p$ with $J^P = 3/2^-$ (S-wave $\Sigma_c \bar{D}^*$): 38.12 MeV at $\Lambda = 1$ GeV.
- Partial decay width for $P_c(4450) \to J/\psi p$ with $J^P = 3/2^-$ (S-wave $\Sigma_c \bar{D}^*$): 25.00 MeV at $\Lambda = 1$ GeV.
- Partial decay width for $P_c(4380) \to J/\psi p$ with $J^P = 3/2^+$: 173.12 MeV at $\Lambda = 1$ GeV.
- Partial decay width for $P_c(4450) \to J/\psi p$ with $J^P = 3/2^+$: 369.82 MeV at $\Lambda = 1$ GeV.
- Coupling constant $g_{P_c\Sigma_c \bar{D}^*}$ for $P_c(4380)$ with $J^P = 3/2^-$: 5.48 at $\Lambda = 1$ GeV.
- Coupling constant $g_{P_c\Sigma_c \bar{D}^*}$ for $P_c(4450)$ with $J^P = 3/2^-$: 2.38 at $\Lambda = 1$ GeV.
- Estimated partial decay width for $P_c(4380)$ as $J^P = 3/2^-$ $\Sigma_c^* \bar{D}$ molecule: 102.92 MeV.
- Estimated partial decay width for $P_c(4450)$ as $J^P = 3/2^-$ $\Sigma_c^* \bar{D}^*$ molecule: 135.00 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the molecular interpretation of the hidden-charm pentaquark states $P_c(4380)$ and $P_c(4450)$ discovered by LHCb (Aaij (2015)) in $\Lambda_b^0 \to J/\psi K^- p$ decays. It builds on prior studies of $\Sigma_c \bar{D}^*$ molecular candidates by Chen (2015), Karliner (2015), and He (2015), and uses the compositeness condition approach previously applied to molecular states by Dong (2008, 2009, 2013, 2014) and Gutsche (2014). The results enable future experimental tests of spin-parity assignments and distinguish between $\Sigma_c \bar{D}^*$, $\Sigma_c^* \bar{D}$, and $\Sigma_c^* \bar{D}^*$ molecular scenarios, complementing the model-independent ratio analysis of Wang (2015) and the decay ratio study of Shen (2016).
