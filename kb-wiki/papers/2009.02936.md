# Resolving the spacetime structure of jets with medium

> Away from the strictly soft and collinear limit of QCD radiation the choice of evolution scale in a parton shower algorithm is ambiguous and several options have been implemented in existing Monte Carlo event generators for proton-proton collisions. However, the resulting space-time evolution could result in subtle differences depending on the particular choice. In this work we quantify measurable consequences of the choice of the evolution variable and show how the implications of such a choice propagates into jet quenching observables. We develop a parton shower algorithm for a general evolution variable, that includes as special cases the virtuality, angle, transverse momentum and formation time. We study the interplay between the shower history for different evolution variables and the phase space affected by parton energy loss. In particular, we implement effects of jet quenching in the dense limit and highlight the role of color coherence effects. We compare the results of the different ordering variables to existing Monte Carlo shower implementations on the parton level by analyzing primary Lund planes. Finally, we study the sensitivity of quenched jets to the choice of evolution variable by confronting our results for a certain key observable, such as the jet mass.

## Key claim

The choice of evolution variable in parton shower algorithms (virtuality, angle, transverse momentum, or formation time) leads to measurable differences in jet quenching observables, even though all choices recover the proper soft and collinear limits; these differences can be used to constrain the spacetime structure of jets interacting with a medium.

## Method

The authors developed a general parton shower framework that implements ordering by mass, relative transverse momentum, formation time, and energy-angle, with three momentum schemes (PYTHIA6-like with off-shell partons, PYTHIA8-like with exchanged energy and on-shell partons, and Catani-Seymour dipole scheme with exchanged four-momentum and on-shell partons). They initiated all showers at the same hard scale and evolved to a minimum scale, using LO e+e- → qqbar matrix elements and running coupling αs(p⊥). Jet quenching was implemented via a quenching factor Q per parton, where a splitting is resolved by the medium only if its formation time is less than its decoherence time and both are less than the medium length L.

## Key numbers

- The parton showers were initiated at the same p⊥,hard scale and evolved down to p⊥,min (exact numerical values not specified in the text).
- The quenching factor Q modifies the n-particle spectrum as d^nσmed/dp1...dpn = Q^n(pjet) × d^nσvac/dp1...dpn.
- The condition for a splitting to be resolved by the medium is tf < td < L, where tf is formation time, td is decoherence time, and L is medium length.
- The jet mass distribution shows suppression of high masses and flattening at low masses when quenching is applied, with ratios of quenched to vacuum results falling on top of each other across different orderings.

## Context

This work extends studies of parton shower uncertainties in vacuum, such as those by Dasgupta (2020) on whether different ordering variables recover proper limits, and applies them to jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions. It builds on the quenching factor approach of Baier (2001) and the color coherence framework of Mehtar-Tani (2017) for resolved splittings. The results enable future experimental constraints on the spacetime structure of jets and the medium by comparing jet mass distributions and Lund plane patterns from different ordering choices.
