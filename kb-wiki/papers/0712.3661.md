# A nuclear matter description based on quark structure of the nucleon and pion exchange

> We investigate the possibility to describe nuclear matter in an approach constrained by the prominent features of quantum chromodynamics. We mapped the in-medium nucleon self-energies of a point coupling relativistic mean-field model on self-energies obtained in effective theories of QCD. More precisely, the contributions to the nucleon self-energy have been separated into the short range part, driven principally by the quark structure of the nucleon described in a quark-diquark picture, and the long range part, dictated by pion dynamics and determined using in-medium chiral perturbation theory. A saturation point, although unrealistic, is obtained without any free parameter. A realistic description of nuclear matter saturation properties has been obtained with the inclusion of a small phenomenological correction term to the short range part of the self-energy.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a realistic description of nuclear matter saturation properties can be obtained by combining a quark-diquark model for short-range nucleon self-energies with chiral perturbation theory for long-range pion exchange, but only after adding a small phenomenological correction term to the short-range part; without this correction, a saturation point is obtained but at an unrealistic density.

## Method

The in-medium nucleon self-energies were separated into short-range and long-range contributions. The short-range part was computed using a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a quark-diquark picture of the nucleon, solving the Faddeev equation in static approximation. The long-range part was determined from one-pion and iterated one-pion exchange diagrams using in-medium chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) up to order O(k_F^5). These self-energies were then mapped onto a density-dependent point-coupling relativistic mean-field model to describe symmetric infinite nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- At baryonic density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³, the quark-diquark model gives scalar self-energies Σ_S⁰ = -260, -347, and -490 MeV for vacuum constituent quark masses m = 370, 400, and 430 MeV respectively, with corresponding vector self-energies Σ_V⁰ = 130, 160, and 230 MeV.
- The ratio of scalar to vector self-energy from the quark-diquark model is approximately Σ_S⁰/Σ_V⁰ ∼ -2.
- The pion-exchange contribution to the self-energies at ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³ is approximately Σ_{S,V} ∼ 130 MeV.
- The one-pion exchange Fock diagram's m_π²/M_N² correction represents 5% of the first-order contribution at k_F ≈ 250 fm⁻³.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Finelli et al. (2004) by replacing phenomenological contact terms in the chiral perturbation theory description with a quark-diquark model based on the NJL model, following the quark matter treatment of Ripka (1997). The short-range self-energies are comparable in magnitude to those from QCD sum rules (Cohen, 1995) but alone do not yield realistic saturation (Aguirre, 2000). The approach enables a more direct connection between nuclear matter saturation properties and QCD-inspired quark dynamics, though the need for a phenomenological correction indicates that the quark-diquark picture alone is insufficient for quantitative nuclear matter description.
