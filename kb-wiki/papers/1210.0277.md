# Interplay between the 0+_2 resonance and the nonresonant continuum of the drip-line two-neutron halo nucleus 22C

> The breakup cross section (BUX) of 22C by 12C at 250 MeV/nucleon is evaluated by the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method incorporating the cluster-orbital shell model (COSM) wave functions. Contributions of the low-lying 0+_2 and 2+_1 resonances predicted by COSM to the BUX are investigated. The 2+_1 resonance gives a narrow peak in the BUX, as in usual resonant reactions, whereas the 0+_2 resonant cross section has a peculiar shape due to the coupling to the nonresonant continuum, i.e., the Fano effect. By changing the scattering angle of 22C after the breakup, a variety of shapes of the 0+_2 resonant cross sections is obtained. Mechanism of the appearance of the sizable Fano effect in the breakup of 22C is discussed.

## Key claim

The breakup cross section of the drip-line nucleus 22C shows a sizable Fano effect for the 0+_2 resonance, meaning its shape deviates from the standard Breit-Wigner form due to coupling with the nonresonant continuum, and this effect is predicted to be unique to an s-wave two-neutron halo nucleus.

## Method

The authors used a four-body continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC) incorporating cluster-orbital shell model (COSM) wave functions for the 20C+n+n system, which they call COSM-CDCC. They evaluated the double differential breakup cross section of 22C by 12C at 250 MeV/nucleon, and used the complex-scaling method (CSM) smoothing technique to decompose the cross section into resonant and nonresonant components.

## Key numbers

- The 0+_2 resonance of 22C is predicted at Er = 1.02 MeV with a width of 0.52 MeV, with main configuration (0d3/2)^2.
- The 2+_1 resonance of 22C is predicted at Er = 0.86 MeV with a width of 0.10 MeV, with main configuration (1s1/2)(0d3/2).
- The 0+ ground state of 22C is bound by 289 keV below the 20C+n+n threshold, with a matter radius of 3.49 fm.
- The ground state of 22C has approximately 80% (1s1/2)^2 configuration and about 13% (0d3/2)^2 configuration.

## Context

This work extends the application of COSM, previously used successfully for 6He, 7He, and 8He (Myo 2007, 2009, 2010), to the drip-line nucleus 22C. The study builds on experimental evidence that 22C is an s-wave two-neutron halo nucleus from reaction cross section measurements (Tanaka 2010) and neutron removal cross sections (Kobayashi 2011), and on theoretical predictions from a 20C+n+n three-body model (Horiuchi 2006). The predicted Fano effect in the 0+ channel enables future experimental verification using the SAMURAI spectrometer at RIBF, and motivates theoretical extensions to include core recoil and dynamical excitation.
