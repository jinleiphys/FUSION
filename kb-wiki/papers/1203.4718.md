# Particle motion in a deformed potential using a transformed oscillator basis

> The quantum description of a particle moving in a deformed potential is investigated. A pseudostate (PS) basis is used to represent the states of the composite system. This PS basis is obtained by diagonalizing the system Hamiltonian in a family of square integrable functions. In this work the Transformed Harmonic Oscillator (THO) functions, obtained from the solutions of the Harmonic Oscillator using a Local Scale Transformation (LST), are used. The proposed method is applied to the 11Be nucleus, treated in a two-body model (10Be+n). Both structure and reaction observables have been studied. Wavefunctions and energies obtained for the bound states and some low-lying resonances are compared with those obtained by direct integration of the Schroedinger equation. The dipole and quadrupole electric transition probabilities for the low-energy continuum have been calculated in the THO basis, and compared with the exact distributions obtained with the scattering states. Finally, the method is applied to describe the 11Be states in the Coulomb breakup of 11Be+208Pb at 69 MeV/nucleon. The energy and angular distributions of the exclusive breakup have been calculated using the Equivalent Photon Method, including both E1 and E2 contributions. The calculated distributions are found to be in good agreement with the available experimental data from RIKEN [Phys. Rev. C70, 054606]. At the very forward angles, the cross section is completely dominated by the dipole couplings.

## Key claim

The Transformed Harmonic Oscillator (THO) pseudostate basis, when extended to deformed potentials within a particle-rotor model, can accurately reproduce bound states, low-lying resonances, and continuum transition probabilities for a two-body system with a deformed core, as demonstrated for the 11Be nucleus treated as 10Be+n, with the caveat that the method's accuracy depends on the choice of Local Scale Transformation parameters and basis size.

## Method

The authors diagonalize the core+valence Hamiltonian in a basis of Transformed Harmonic Oscillator (THO) functions, obtained by applying a Local Scale Transformation (LST) of the form s(r) = (1/sqrt(2) b) * [1/((1/r)^m + (1/(gamma*sqrt(r)))^m)]^(1/m) to the harmonic oscillator basis. For the deformed case, the particle-rotor model is used with a permanently deformed core, expanding the valence-core interaction in multipoles and including core-excited states (0+ and 2+ for 10Be). The resulting pseudostates are used to compute bound states, identify resonances via the stabilization method, and calculate electric transition probabilities to the continuum using a smoothing procedure based on the closure relation in the truncated basis.

## Key numbers

- The 10Be core deformation parameter is beta_2 = 0.67
- The first excited state of 10Be is at E_x = 3.368 MeV
- The Woods-Saxon geometry uses R = 2.483 fm and a = 0.65 fm
- The spin-orbit strength is V_so = 8.5 MeV
- The LST parameters used are m = 4, b = 1.6 fm, and gamma = 1.84 fm^(1/2)
- The Coulomb breakup calculation is for 11Be + 208Pb at 69 MeV/nucleon
- The maximum excitation energy targeted is about 10 MeV
- The valence-core orbital angular momenta considered are ell <= 3

## Context

This work extends the THO pseudostate method, previously applied to spherical systems by Moro et al. (2009) and Lay et al. (2010), to deformed potentials using the particle-rotor model of Bohr and Mottelson. It builds on the approach of Summers et al. (2006) for describing core-excited admixtures in one-neutron halo nuclei within CDCC, but replaces the continuum binning method with a discrete basis diagonalization. The method enables future applications to three-body composite systems with core deformation and to scattering calculations including dynamic core excitation, as well as providing a framework for identifying resonances in multi-channel deformed systems via the stabilization method.
