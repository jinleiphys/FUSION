# Self Consistent and Renormalized particle-particle RPA in a Schematic Model

> The dynamical effects of ground state correlations for excitation energies and transition strengths near the superfluid phase transition are studied in the soluble two level pairing model, in the context of the particle-particle self consistent Random Phase Approximation (SCRPA). Exact results are well reproduced across the transition region, beyond the collapse of the standard particle-particle Random Phase Approximation. The effects of two-body correlation in the SCRPA are displayed explicitly.

# Self Consistent and Renormalized particle-particle RPA in a Schematic Model

## Key claim
The self-consistent particle-particle Random Phase Approximation (pp-SCRPA) and its renormalized approximation (pp-RRPA) both avoid the collapse of standard pp-RPA near the superfluid phase transition and reproduce exact results across the transition region, but the simpler pp-RRPA actually outperforms the full pp-SCRPA for some observables (occupancy and two-particle transfer) while the full pp-SCRPA gives better excitation energies.

## Method
The authors study the soluble two-level pairing model with equal pair degeneracy Ω = 10 and level spacing ε = 2, using the SU(2) ⊗ SU(2) algebra to construct the phonon vacuum state. They solve the non-linear pp-SCRPA equations self-consistently by computing one- and two-body densities from the explicit correlated ground state, and compare these results to the pp-RRPA which neglects two-body correlations in the equations of motion, as well as to exact solutions and standard pp-RPA.

## Key numbers
- The standard pp-RPA collapses at g_crit = ε/(2Ω - 1), where ω_+ = ω_- = -g_crit; for Ω=10 and ε=2, this gives g_crit = 2/19 ≈ 0.105
- For large coupling g, the exact strong coupling limits are ω_+ → 0 and ω_- → -2g
- The strong coupling limit for the squared two-particle transfer matrix element to the upper level is (Ω+1)/4
- The system studied has twenty particles distributed in two Ω=10 levels separated by ε=2

## Context
This work extends the particle-particle self-consistent RPA formalism introduced by Dukelsky, Röpke and Schuck (1998) for pairing vibrations, examining the behavior across the normal-to-superfluid phase transition. The authors compare the full pp-SCRPA with the simplified pp-RRPA, which neglects two-body correlations, and find that the pp-RRPA gives better agreement with exact results for occupancy and transfer matrix elements while the pp-SCRPA is superior for excitation energies. The results indicate that for strong coupling, neither approximation is adequate and the system should be treated as a pairing-deformed (superfluid) system, where the pair-coupling model is asymptotically exact.
