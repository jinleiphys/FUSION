# Quantum-number projected generator coordinate method for $^{21}$Ne with a chiral two-nucleon-plus-three-nucleon interaction

> We report a study of the low-lying states of deformed $^{21}$Ne within the framework of quantum-number projected generator coordinate method (PGCM), starting from a chiral two-nucleon-plus-three-nucleon (NN+3N) interaction. The wave functions of states are constructed as a linear combination of a set of axially-deformed Hartree-Fock-Bogliubov (HFB) wave functions with different quadrupole deformations. These HFB wave functions are projected onto different angular momenta and the correct neutron and proton numbers for $^{21}$Ne. The results of calculations based on the effective Hamiltonians derived by normal-ordering the 3N interaction with respect to three different reference states, including the quantum-number projected HFB wave functions for $^{20}$Ne, $^{22}$Ne, and an ensemble of them with equal weights, are compared. This study serves as a key step towards ab initio calculations of odd-mass deformed nuclei with the in-medium GCM.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the quantum-number projected generator coordinate method (PGCM) can be applied to the odd-mass deformed nucleus ²¹Ne starting from a chiral two-nucleon-plus-three-nucleon (NN+3N) interaction, but the results depend on the choice of reference state used for normal-ordering the 3N interaction, with three different schemes (based on ²⁰Ne, ²²Ne, and an equal-weight ensemble) compared.

## Method

The authors constructed wave functions for ²¹Ne as linear combinations of axially-deformed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) wave functions with different quadrupole deformations, projected onto good angular momentum, neutron number, and proton number. The chiral NN+3N interaction (EM1.8/2.0) was normal-ordered to two-body (NO2B) form with respect to three different reference states: particle-number projected HFB states for ²⁰Ne, ²²Ne, and an equal-weight ensemble of both. The resulting effective Hamiltonians were used in PGCM calculations, with the Hill-Wheeler-Griffin equation solved to obtain energies and mixing weights.

## Key numbers

- The 3N interaction contributes 80.338 MeV to the energy E₀ for the ²⁰Ne reference state, while the zero-point energy ℰ₀ of the unnormal-ordered Hamiltonian is 50.093 MeV for the same reference state.
- The term depending solely on one-body density (⅙ Σ w λ³) contributes 65.215 MeV to the energies E₀⁽³⁾ and ℰ₀.
- The difference between E₀ and ℰ₀ for the ²⁰Ne reference state gives ½ Σ w λ² λ = 30.245 MeV.
- The expectation values E₀ for the three effective Hamiltonians are -96.931 MeV (magic-Ne20), -101.781 MeV (magic-ENO/EW), and -109.034 MeV (magic-Ne22).
- The harmonic oscillator basis frequency is ℏω = 20 MeV, with e_max = 6 and a 3N interaction truncation at e₁+e₂+e₃ > 14.
- The energy of PNP-HFB states for ²¹Ne with K^π = 1/2⁺ at β₂ = 0.0 remains constant for N_φ ≥ 5 meshpoints in the gauge angle.

## Context

This work extends the PGCM framework, previously applied to even-even nuclei and to odd-mass nuclei using energy density functionals (EDFs) by Kimura (2007, 2011), Bally (2014, 2022, 2023), Borrajo (2016, 2017, 2018), and Zhou (2023), to ab initio calculations with chiral interactions. Unlike EDF-based PGCM approaches, which may suffer from spurious divergences and discontinuities as shown by Bender (2009) and Duguet (2009), the Hamiltonian-based framework used here is free of those problems because the same interaction is applied to both particle-hole and particle-particle channels. This study serves as a key step toward ab initio calculations of odd-mass deformed nuclei with the in-medium GCM, as previously developed by Yao (2018, 2020).
