# Nuclear Many-Body Effect on Particle Emissions Following Muon Capture on $^{28}$Si and $^{40}$Ca

> Muon captures on nuclei have provided us with plenty of knowledge of nuclear properties. Recently, this reaction attracts attention in electronics, because it is argued that charged particle emissions following muon capture on silicon trigger non-negligible soft errors in memory devices. To investigate the particle emissions from a nuclear physics point of view, we develop a new approach using a microscopic model of muon capture and up-to-date particle emission models. We paid attention to the muon capture rates, the particle emission spectra, and the multiplicities that have a close interrelation with each other, and found that the nuclear many-body correlation including two-particle two-hole excitations is a key to explaining them simultaneously.

# Nuclear Many-Body Effect on Particle Emissions Following Muon Capture on $^{28}$Si and $^{40}$Ca

## Key claim
The nuclear many-body correlation including two-particle two-hole (2p-2h) excitations is essential to simultaneously explain muon capture rates, particle emission spectra, and multiplicities for $^{28}$Si and $^{40}$Ca, but the effect of meson-exchange current (MEC) must also be included phenomenologically to reproduce proton emission spectra, and the calculated alpha particle multiplicity for $^{28}$Si and neutron multiplicity for $^{40}$Ca still deviate from experimental data.

## Method
The authors combined a microscopic model of muon capture using the Tamm-Dancoff approximation (TDA) and second TDA (STDA) with the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock method (using SGII and SkO' effective forces) to describe the initial 1p-1h and 2p-2h excited states, then coupled this to a two-component exciton model for preequilibrium particle emission and the Hauser-Feshbach statistical model for compound nucleus decay. The muon wave function was calculated by solving the Dirac equation including Coulomb potentials from the nucleus and electrons (with electron density from density functional theory) and vacuum polarization via the Uehling effective potential.

## Key numbers
- Muon capture rate for $^{28}$Si with STDA: 0.81–1.18 × 10⁶ s⁻¹ (SGII force, range from g_A = -1 to -1.26); experimental value: 0.8712(18) × 10⁶ s⁻¹
- Muon capture rate for $^{40}$Ca with STDA: 2.44–3.32 × 10⁶ s⁻¹ (SGII force); experimental value: 2.557(14) × 10⁶ s⁻¹
- Neutron multiplicity per 10³ muon captures for $^{28}$Si with STDA+MEC: 729 (SGII) and 983 (SkO'); experimental: 864(72)
- Proton multiplicity (4–20 MeV) per 10³ muon captures for $^{28}$Si with STDA+MEC: 54.4 (SGII) and 63.5 (SkO'); experimental: 52.46(192)
- Deuteron multiplicity (5–17 MeV) per 10³ muon captures for $^{28}$Si with STDA+MEC: 7.96 (SGII) and 9.15 (SkO'); experimental: 9.80(46)
- Triton multiplicity (6–17 MeV) per 10³ muon captures for $^{28}$Si with STDA+MEC: 1.63 (SGII) and 1.97 (SkO'); experimental: 1.70(13)
- Alpha particle multiplicity (15–20 MeV) per 10³ muon captures for $^{28}$Si with STDA+MEC: 1.28 (SGII) and 1.47 (SkO'); experimental: 0.57(10)
- Neutron multiplicity per 10³ muon captures for $^{40}$Ca with STDA+MEC: 613 (SGII) and 681 (SkO'); experimental: 764(32)
- MEC contribution approximated as Gaussian with mean excitation energy 56 MeV and width 15 MeV, set to 5% of total capture rate
- Single-particle state densities adjusted to g_π = Z/19 and g_ν = N/19 (from standard Fermi gas values Z/15 and N/15)
- Model space for 1p-1h states: ε_m − ε_i ≤ 100 MeV; for 2p-2h states: 2n_q + l_q ≤ 4 except 0g states

## Context
This work extends the theoretical treatment of muon capture beyond the phenomenological models used in the 1970s–1980s (reviewed by Measday (2001)), which explained particle emission spectra qualitatively but not accurately enough for practical applications. The authors build on recent studies by Lovato (2019), Jokiniemi (2019), and Ciccarelli (2020) that showed the importance of nucleon-nucleon correlations in muon capture. The approach addresses the need for a nuclear-structure-based framework to improve Monte-Carlo transport simulations of muon-induced soft errors in memory devices (Serre (2012), Abe (2017), Kossov (2007)). The remaining discrepancies in alpha and neutron multiplicities motivate new experiments at TRIUMF, RAL, J-PARC, and the planned facility at RCNP Osaka University.
