# Operator overlaps in harmonic oscillator bases with different oscillator lengths

> We apply a formalism recently developed to carry out Generator Coordinate Method calculations using a set of Hartree- Fock- Bogoliubov wave functions, where each of the members of the set can be expanded in an arbitrary basis. In this paper it is assumed that the HFB wave functions are expanded in Harmonic Oscillator (HO) bases with different oscillator lengths. General expressions to compute the required matrix elements of arbitrary operators are given. The application of the present formalism to the case of fission is illustrated with an example.

## Key claim

The paper provides a practical formalism for computing operator overlaps between Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) wave functions expanded in harmonic oscillator (HO) bases with different oscillator lengths, which is necessary because the finite bases are not connected by unitary transformations and therefore standard formulas fail. The key condition is that the formalism works by formally extending the finite bases to complete bases and then expressing all quantities in terms of intrinsic quantities computed solely within the given finite bases.

## Method

The author applies a recently developed generalization of Wick's theorem (Rob22) to the specific case where HFB wave functions are expanded in HO bases with different oscillator lengths. The overlap matrix between the two HO bases is constructed and its Lower-Upper (LU) decomposition is used to define transformed Bogoliubov amplitudes and contractions that have the same form as standard formulas. The method requires computing matrix elements of operators using a common intermediate HO basis with oscillator lengths given by the geometric mean of the two original bases, then using the transformed amplitudes to evaluate norm and operator overlaps.

## Context

This work extends the generalized Wick's theorem formalism developed in Rob22 and earlier work by PhysRevC.50.2874 to the practical case of HO bases with different oscillator lengths, which commonly arises in Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) calculations for fission. The formalism corrects the Onishi formula (Onishi 1966) and Balian-Brezin (1969) formulas that assume bases connected by unitary transformations, which give incorrect overlaps when oscillator lengths differ. The approach enables GCM calculations and symmetry restoration along fission paths without requiring impractically large common bases, and could also improve Time Dependent GCM formulations for fission dynamics.
