# Chaotic Behavior of Particle Production in Branching Processes

> The notion of chaotic behavior is examined for particle production in branching processes. Two types of branching are considered: non-Abelian gauge interaction and an Abelian cascade model. Properties of the production processes are investigated by Monte Carlo stimulation. The ``temporal'' behavior is studied by following the fluctuations in the multiplicities of each generation as the branching evolves. The ``spatial'' behavior is described in terms of the fluctuations of the normalized factorial moments from event to event. The information dimension and a new entropy index are determined. When all the measures are taken together, they collectively give a strong suggestion that the QCD branching process is chaotic, while the Abelian cascade model is not.

# Chaotic Behavior of Particle Production in Branching Processes

## Key claim

The authors suggest that perturbative QCD branching processes exhibit signatures indicative of chaotic behavior, while an Abelian cascade model (the chi model) does not; however, they acknowledge that no firm criterion exists to prove chaoticity from their measures, and the correspondences to classical Lyapunov exponents remain interpretive rather than proven.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo simulations of two branching processes: pure-gauge QCD (using Odorico's algorithm with Sudakov form factors and splitting function P(z) = 6[(1-z)/z + z/(1-z) + z(1-z)]) and an Abelian chi model using only the regular term P(z) = 6z(1-z) with no infrared or collinear divergences. They studied "temporal" behavior by tracking fluctuations in parton multiplicities across generations using the normalized variance V_i, and "spatial" behavior by analyzing fluctuations of normalized factorial moments F_q in a cumulative momentum-fraction variable X space, introducing a new entropy index mu_q derived from moments of moments C_{p,q}(M).

## Key numbers

- For QCD branching, the normalized variance V_i follows a power law V_i proportional to <n_i>^kappa with kappa approximately 0.4, independent of Q/Q_0 over a wide range.
- The QCD simulation used Q/Q_0 = 10^3 (with Q_0 = 1 GeV and Lambda = 250 MeV), while the chi model used Q/Q_0 = 15, chosen so average multiplicities were comparable.
- The distribution P(i_max) from 10^5 simulations was Gaussian-like for both models, with QCD showing a larger width than the chi model.
- The information dimension D_1 was found to be exactly 1 for both branching processes, indicating no nontrivial multifractal structure in the X-space fluctuations.

## Context

This work extends the study of chaos from classical non-Abelian gauge theory (where chaotic solutions and Lyapunov exponents have been found under simplifying conditions by Savvidy et al. (1980s) and on the lattice by Muller et al. (1990s)) into the quantum domain of perturbative QCD branching processes. The authors introduce new measures (normalized variance V_i and entropy index mu_q) because conventional trajectory-based methods are inapplicable when the number of particles increases during evolution. The work enables experimental tests of chaotic behavior in high-energy collisions through the entropy index, which is measurable from final-state particle fluctuations, and contrasts with the approach of Bialas and Peschanski (1986) using factorial moments for self-similarity analysis.
