# Theory Summary at Strangeness in Quark Matter 2019

> This is the theory summary of Strangeness in Quark Matter 2019 conference. Results include the state-of-the-art updates to the Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) phase diagram with contributions both from heavy-ion collisions and nuclear astrophysics, studies on the QCD freeze-out lines, and several aspects regarding small systems including collectivity, heavy flavor dynamics, strangeness, and hard probes.

# Theory Summary at Strangeness in Quark Matter 2019

## Key claim
The conference summary reports that the assumption of strangeness neutrality in the QCD equation of state with three conserved charges (baryon number, strangeness, and electric charge) pushes heavy-ion collision experiments to larger baryon chemical potentials than previously expected, potentially bringing them closer to nuclear astrophysics conditions than originally thought. However, the author notes that caveats exist regarding finite size effects, centrality binning, and detector efficiencies when searching for a critical point via net-proton fluctuations.

## Method
The paper is a conference theory summary that synthesizes presentations from the Strangeness in Quark Matter 2019 meeting, covering lattice QCD results for the equation of state with BSQ conserved charges, thermal fits to hadron yields and fluctuations, relativistic hydrodynamic calculations coupled to general relativity for neutron star mergers, and transport model comparisons for heavy flavor and small systems. The author reviews results from multiple working groups including QCD phase diagram studies, freeze-out analyses, hadron spectrum constraints, heavy flavor dynamics, and small system collectivity.

## Key numbers
- The minimum multiplicity to live long enough to reach thermalization in small systems is estimated as dN/dη ≳ 100 using effective kinetic theory (Kurkela 2018)
- In a core-corona model, significant core contribution persists down to dN/dη ≳ 10 (Kanakubo 2019)
- The ratio v2{4}/v2{2}(pT) of D mesons is identified as the best observable to distinguish between two different initial condition models (Katz 2019)
- At very low beam energies, chemical and kinetic freeze-out temperatures appear to converge to the same value (Adamczyk 2017)
- Virtual photon measurements from HADES at low beam energies found temperatures somewhat higher than originally expected (Adamczewski-Musch 2019)

## Context
This summary extends the ongoing effort to map the QCD phase diagram from both heavy-ion collisions and neutron star mergers, building on the lattice QCD cross-over result of Aoki (2006) and the critical point search framework of Stephanov (2011). The work connects heavy-ion physics to nuclear astrophysics through the BSQ equation of state, following the approach of Noronha-Hostler (2019) for conserved charges, and discusses mass twins as a possible signal for deconfinement in neutron stars as proposed by Alford (2013) and Benic (2014). The summary enables future cross-disciplinary collaborations between heavy-ion physicists and nuclear astrophysicists, particularly for full BSQ hydrodynamic calculations and viscous fluid calculations coupled to general relativity in neutron star mergers.
