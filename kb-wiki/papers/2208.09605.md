# Hadro-chemistry effects on charm decayed leptons in heavy-ion collisions

> Charm-hadrons possess versatile hadro-chemistry as characterized by various transverse-momentum-dependent ratios between their different species. In particular, the charm hadro-chemistry may be modified in relativistic heavy-ion collisions with respect to proton-proton collisions at the same energy, as caused by novel diffusion and hadronization mechanisms of charm quarks in the environment of the created quark-gluon plasma (QGP) in the former. Inspired by recent measurements of leptons from charm-hadron decays (separated from bottom decays) in Pb-Pb and Au-Au collisions, we investigate the effects of the charm hadro-chemistry on the leptonic observables. We find that full consideration of charm hadro-chemistry in both proton-proton and heavy-ion collisions causes only mild change of charm-leptons' suppression factor with respect to previous calculations hadronizing charm quarks into $D$ mesons only, whereas the resulting change (increase) in the charm-leptons' elliptic flow turns out to be more pronounced as a consequence of the larger collectivity of $Λ_c$ baryons than $D$ mesons.

## Key claim

Full consideration of charm hadro-chemistry (including $\Lambda_c$ baryons) in both proton-proton and heavy-ion collisions causes only a mild change in the charm-leptons' nuclear modification factor compared to calculations that hadronize charm quarks into $D$ mesons only, but the resulting increase in the charm-leptons' elliptic flow is more pronounced, though still significantly below the ATLAS data in nearly the whole measured $p_T$ range.

## Method

The authors employed a comprehensive charm transport approach with a lattice-constrained $T$-matrix transport coefficient for charm quark diffusion in hydrodynamically expanding QGP, combined with a three-body resonance recombination model (RRM) for charm-baryon formation that incorporates space-momentum correlations. Semileptonic decays of ground state charm-hadrons ($D^0$, $D^+$, $D_s^+$, $\Lambda_c^+$) were simulated as free quark decays using the low-energy $V$-$A$ theory, with branching ratios from the Particle Data Group. The calculations were performed for $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02$ TeV Pb-Pb collisions in the 30-50% centrality bin and $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200$ GeV Au-Au collisions in the 20-30% centrality bin, and compared to a scenario where all charm quarks were hadronized into $D$ mesons only.

## Key numbers

- In $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV Pb-Pb collisions in the 30-50% centrality bin, the total charm number is $dN_{c\bar{c}}/dy\sim 3.44$ after shadowing reduction.
- The $D^+$ semileptonic branching ratio ($\sim 16.1\%$) is almost 2.5 times that of $D^0$ ($\sim 6.5\%$).
- The $\Lambda_c^+$ semileptonic branching ratio is $\sim 4.5\%$.
- At $p_T\sim 1$ (6) GeV, muons decayed from $D^0$ and $D^+$ account for $\sim 70\%$ ($\sim 90\%$) of the total charm muons spectrum.
- The scalar diquark mass used for $\Lambda_c$ states is $\sim 710$ MeV and the axial-vector diquark mass for $\Sigma_c$ states is $\sim 909$ MeV.
- The total charm cross section per unit rapidity in $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV was fitted to be $d\sigma^{c\bar{c}}/dy\sim 1$ mb.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous transport approach for charm probes (He 2019) by applying it to charm leptonic observables, inspired by recent measurements of charm leptons separated from bottom decays by ATLAS in Pb-Pb collisions and STAR in Au-Au collisions. It contrasts with earlier studies of heavy flavor leptons (van Hees 2005, Gossiaux 2008, He 2011, He 2014, Song 2016, Katz 2019) that only accounted for $D$ mesons in charm quark hadronization, and with a schematic attempt (Sorensen 2005, Martinez-Garcia 2007) that assumed charm-baryon-to-meson enhancement without realistic energy loss. The results suggest that full bottom hadro-chemistry, including $\Lambda_b$ baryons, may be needed to explain the recently measured bottom electron $v_2$ by ALICE, which was underestimated by transport models hadronizing bottom quarks into $B$ mesons only.
