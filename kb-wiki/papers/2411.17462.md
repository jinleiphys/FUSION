# Lattice simulation of nucleon distribution and shell closure in the proton-rich nucleus $^{22}$Si

> The proton-rich nucleus $^{22}$Si is studied using Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory with high-fidelity chiral forces. Our results indicate that $^{22}$Si is more tightly bound than $^{20}$Mg, thereby excluding the possibility of two-proton emission. The $Z = 14$ shell closure in $^{22}$Si is supported by the evolution of the $2^+$ state in the neighboring nuclei. We then focus on the charge radius and spatial distribution information of $^{22}$Si, considering the novel phenomena that may emerge due to the small two-proton separation energy and the shell closure. We present the distribution of the $14$ protons and $8$ neutrons obtained from our lattice simulation, revealing insights into the spatial arrangement of the nucleons. Moreover, the spatial localization of the outermost proton and neutron suggests that $^{22}$Si is a doubly magic nucleus. Furthermore, we develop the pinhole method based on the harmonic oscillator basis, which gives insight into the nuclear structure in terms of the shell model picture from lattice simulations. Our calculated occupation numbers support that $Z = 14$ and $N = 8$ are the shell closures and show that the $π1s_{1/2}$ orbital component is minor in $^{22}$Si.

# Lattice simulation of nucleon distribution and shell closure in the proton-rich nucleus $^{22}$Si

## Key claim
The authors find that $^{22}$Si is more tightly bound than $^{20}$Mg within statistical uncertainty, thereby excluding two-proton emission, but note that the two-proton separation energy of $S_{2p} = 0.72 (48)$ MeV carries a large relative error that leaves the conclusion dependent on the chosen Euclidean time extrapolation and interaction set.

## Method
The authors perform auxiliary-field Monte Carlo simulations on a periodic cubic lattice (side length $L = 10$, lattice spacing $a \simeq 1.32$ fm) using Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory (NLEFT) with chiral nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon forces at N$^3$LO, employing the wavefunction matching method to control sign oscillations. They use two interaction sets: a global chiral interaction and one fine-tuned for silicon isotopes, and extract ground-state energies, $2^+$ excitation energies, charge radii, and nucleon distributions via the pinhole algorithm, including a newly developed harmonic-oscillator-basis pinhole method for occupation numbers.

## Key numbers
- Ground-state energy of $^{22}$Si: $-134.38 (39)$ MeV (global interaction); $-136.28 (41)$ MeV (silicon-tuned interaction)
- Ground-state energy of $^{20}$Mg: $-133.66 (28)$ MeV (global interaction); $-134.94 (18)$ MeV (silicon-tuned interaction); experiment gives $-134.61$ MeV
- Two-proton separation energy $S_{2p}$ of $^{22}$Si: $0.72 (48)$ MeV (global interaction, maximum $\tau = 0.4$ MeV$^{-1}$); $1.34 (45)$ MeV (silicon-tuned interaction)
- $2^+$ excitation energy in $^{22}$Si: $2.11 (57)$ MeV (global interaction); $1.98 (70)$ MeV (silicon-tuned interaction)
- $2^+$ excitation energies in $^{18}$Ne and $^{20}$Mg: $1.70 (44)$ MeV and $1.47 (32)$ MeV (global interaction), compared to experimental values $1.89$ MeV and $1.60$ MeV
- Charge radius of $^{22}$Si: $3.277 (19)$ fm (global interaction); $3.196 (17)$ fm (silicon-tuned interaction); combined $3\sigma$ range $3.145$ to $3.334$ fm
- Charge radius difference $\Delta R_{\text{ch}}(^{22}\text{Si}-^{20}\text{Mg})$: $0.070 (31)$ fm (global); $0.070 (28)$ fm (silicon-tuned)
- Mirror charge radius difference $\Delta R_{\text{ch}}(^{22}\text{Si}-^{22}\text{O})$: $0.361 (32)$ fm (global); $0.370 (18)$ fm (silicon-tuned)

## Context
This work extends NLEFT calculations into the proton-rich region, using the same chiral Hamiltonian from Elhatisari (2022) and the silicon-tuned interaction from Konig (2023). It addresses inconsistencies in prior theoretical predictions for $^{22}$Si's two-proton separation energy from coupled-cluster and other ab initio methods (PhysRevLett.110.022502, PhysRevLett.126.022501, ZHANG2022136958), and provides the first lattice-based occupation numbers and spatial nucleon distributions for this nucleus. The results enable future experimental tests of the $Z=14$ shell closure at the proton dripline and constrain the symmetry energy slope $L$ via the mirror charge radius difference with $^{22}$O, complementing density functional theory predictions (PhysRevLett.119.122502) and coupled-cluster linear regression results (PhysRevLett.130.032501).
