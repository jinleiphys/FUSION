# An SO(10) GUT Model With Lopsided Mass Matrix and Neutrino Mixing Angle theta_13

> An alternative supersymmetric SO(10) grand unification model with lopsided fermion mass matrices is introduced. It generates a large solar-neutrino-mixing angle through the neutrinos' Dirac mass matrix constrained by the SO(10) group structure, avoiding the fine-tuning required in the Majorana mass matrix of right-handed neutrinos. The model fits well the known data on masses and mixings of quarks and leptons, and predicts a sizable lepton mixing $\sin^22θ_{13}\simeq 0.074$, which is significantly larger than that of the original lopsided model.

# An SO(10) GUT Model With Lopsided Mass Matrix and Neutrino Mixing Angle theta_13

## Key claim

The model predicts a sizable reactor mixing angle sin^2 2θ_13 ≃ 0.074, which is significantly larger than the range 0.002 ≤ sin^2 θ_13 ≤ 0.003 predicted by the original lopsided model of Albright, Babu, and Barr, and lies within the reach of next-generation reactor experiments. This prediction depends on generating the large solar-neutrino mixing angle through the neutrino Dirac mass matrix rather than through fine-tuning of the right-handed neutrino Majorana mass matrix.

## Method

The authors construct a supersymmetric SO(10) grand unification model with lopsided fermion mass matrices, modifying the original lopsided model by introducing additional off-diagonal couplings (parameters ω, κ, ρ) in the up-type-quark and neutrino Dirac mass matrices while keeping the right-handed neutrino Majorana mass matrix M_R diagonal. They fit 12 parameters to experimental data on quark masses, charged lepton masses, CKM matrix elements, and neutrino oscillation parameters at the GUT scale, then compute the resulting PMNS mixing angles from the seesaw mechanism.

## Key numbers

- Predicted sin^2 2θ_13 = 0.074 (central value)
- Predicted sin^2 θ_atm = 0.49 (corresponding to 44.6 degrees)
- Best-fit parameters include: σ = 1.83, ε = 0.1446, ω = -0.0466, ρ = 0.0092, κ = 0.0191, η = 1.02 × 10^{-5}
- Right-handed neutrino masses: M_1 = 3.77 × 10^{10} GeV, M_2 = 3.77 × 10^{10} GeV, M_3 = 1.85 × 10^{13} GeV
- Down-type quark masses at GUT scale: m^0_d = 1.08 MeV, m^0_s = 25.97 MeV, m^0_b = 1.242 GeV
- Input neutrino mass squared differences: Δm^2_ν12 = 7.9 × 10^{-5} eV^2, Δm^2_ν23 = 2.4 × 10^{-3} eV^2
- With 3σ constraints, the predicted range is 0.055 ≤ sin^2 2θ_13 ≤ 0.110 and 0.44 ≤ sin^2 θ_atm ≤ 0.52

## Context

This work extends the lopsided fermion mass matrix approach of Albright, Babu, and Barr (1998, 2000, 2001) by providing an alternative mechanism for generating the large solar-neutrino mixing angle that avoids the fine-tuning required in the right-handed neutrino Majorana mass matrix of the original model. The model predicts a θ_13 value significantly larger than both the original lopsided model (which gave sin^2 θ_13 between 0.002 and 0.003 under constraints) and the charged-lepton-based lopsided model of Babu (2001) which found sin^2 2θ_13 ≤ 0.01, making it testable by next-generation reactor neutrino experiments.
