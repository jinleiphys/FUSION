# Crystalline character of high-magnetic-field cusp states in quantum dots

> Conditional probability distributions from exact diagonalization are used to investigate the crystalline or liquid character of the downward cusp states in parabolic quantum dots (QD's) at high magnetic fields. These states are crystalline in character for fractional fillings covering both low and high values, unlike the liquid Jastrow-Laughlin wave functions, but in remarkable agreement with the rotating-electron-molecule ones [Phys. Rev. B 66, 115315 (2002)]. The cusp states are precursors to the bulk fractional quantum Hall states (and not to the bulk Wigner crystal), since the collective rotation stabilizes the rotating Wigner molecule (formed in the QD) relative to the static one.

## Key claim
The downward cusp states in parabolic quantum dots at high magnetic fields are crystalline in character for fractional fillings covering both low and high values (0.467 > nu > 0.111), contrary to the long-held view that they are liquid-like; this crystalline character is in agreement with rotating-Wigner-molecule wave functions rather than Jastrow-Laughlin or composite-fermion wave functions.

## Method
The authors used exact diagonalization (EXD) to compute conditional probability distributions (CPDs) for N=6 to 9 electrons in a parabolic quantum dot under high magnetic fields in a disk geometry. They compared these EXD results with trial wave functions from the rotating-Wigner-molecule (RWM) approach and from Jastrow-Laughlin (JL) / composite-fermion (CF) approaches. The RWM wave functions were constructed via a two-step method: first, an unrestricted Hartree-Fock determinant describing a static Wigner molecule with broken circular symmetry, followed by angular-momentum projection to restore good angular momentum.

## Key numbers
- For N=6, L=135 (nu=1/9): EXD Coulomb interaction energy = 1.6305 e^2/kappa l_B; RWM energy error relative to EXD = 0.34%, overlap = 0.860; JL energy error = 0.50%, overlap = 0.665.
- For N=6, L=105 (nu=1/7): EXD Coulomb interaction energy = 1.8533 e^2/kappa l_B; RWM energy error = 0.48%, overlap = 0.850; JL energy error = 0.46%, overlap = 0.710.
- For N=6, L=75 (nu=1/5): EXD Coulomb interaction energy = 2.2018 e^2/kappa l_B; RWM energy error = 0.85%, overlap = 0.817; JL energy error = 0.32%, overlap = 0.837.
- For N=7, L=105 (nu=1/5): EXD Coulomb interaction energy = 2.9144 e^2/kappa l_B; RWM energy error = 0.59%, overlap = 0.842; JL energy error = 0.55%, overlap = 0.754.
- The fractional fillings examined ranged from nu = 0.467 down to nu = 0.111 (specifically 1/9, 1/7, 1/5, 4/13, 7/15, and 36/101).

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier proposal of rotating-Wigner-molecule wave functions (Yannouleas and Landman, 2002) and directly contradicts the long-held view in the fractional quantum Hall effect literature, dating back to Laughlin (1983), that cusp states are liquid-like and described by Jastrow-Laughlin or composite-fermion wave functions. The findings challenge the expectation (based on Laughlin 1983 and Lam and Girvin 1984) that a liquid-to-crystal phase transition would occur only at fillings nu <= 1/7, and instead suggest that fractional quantum Hall behavior should be observable at all fractional fillings in clean systems, with impurities and defects being the main practical limitation.
