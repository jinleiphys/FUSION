# Viscous Hydrodynamics and Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> The matter created in relativistic heavy ion collisions is fairly well described by ideal hydrodynamics, and somewhat better described by viscous hydrodynamics. To this point, most viscous calculations have been two-dimensional, based on an assumption of Bjorken boost invariance along the beam axis. Here, first results are presented for a fully three-dimensional viscous model. The model is described and tests of the numerical accuracy of the code are presented. Two- and three-dimensional runs are compared, and modest changes are observed for mid-rapidity observables at the highest RHIC (Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider) energies.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first results for a fully three-dimensional (3+1)D viscous hydrodynamic model of relativistic heavy ion collisions, finding that the effects of the non-trivial longitudinal expansion on mid-rapidity observables are modest (at the 5% level), suggesting that the commonly used two-dimensional boost-invariant approximation is adequate for predictions at that level of precision.

## Method

The authors solve the Israel-Stewart equations for viscous hydrodynamics on a fixed mesh in Bjorken coordinates using a second-order Runge-Kutta method with second-order central finite differences for spatial derivatives. The model uses averaged optical Glauber initial conditions with a Gaussian profile in spatial rapidity (radius 1.4 units), a thermalization time of 0.8 fm/c, and a shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of 0.16. Two-dimensional (boost-invariant) and three-dimensional runs are compared for Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies.

## Key numbers

- Central energy density at b=2.21 fm and τ₀=0.8 fm/c: ≈19.5 GeV/fm³
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: η/s = 0.16
- Initial thermalization time: τ₀ = 0.8 fm/c
- Fraction of initial condition scaled to wounded nucleons: α = 0.85
- Gaussian radius in spatial rapidity: 1.4 units
- Longitudinal velocity gradient at origin deviates from Bjorken value by approximately 5%
- Energy density at mid-rapidity differs by up to 5% between 2D and 3D calculations
- Collective transverse velocity at mid-rapidity differs by a maximum of 1% between 2D and 3D
- Freeze-out surface lifetime reduced by about 5% in 3D compared to 2D
- Cell densities used: dx = dy = dη_s = 0.15 fm, dτ = 0.05 fm/c
- Impact parameters: b=2.21 fm (0-5% centrality), b=5.70 fm (10-20%), b=7.23 fm (20-30%)
- Woods-Saxon parameters for Au: R=6.37 fm, χ=0.54 fm, ρ₀=0.17 fm⁻³
- Merging temperatures for equation of state: T_l = 190 MeV, T_f = 150 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous two-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic calculations (Romatschke 2009, Song 2007) that assumed Bjorken boost invariance, by implementing a fully three-dimensional model. The results show that the boost-invariant approximation is valid at the 5% level for mid-rapidity observables at top RHIC energies, but the effects are expected to be larger at lower energies and smaller at LHC energies. The model enables future comparisons with experimental data away from mid-rapidity, which could provide unique constraints on the properties of the Quark-Gluon Plasma.
