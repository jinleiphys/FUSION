# Measurement of the neutron charge radius and the role of its constituents

> The neutron is a cornerstone in our depiction of the visible universe. Despite the neutron zero-net electric charge, the asymmetric distribution of the positively- (up) and negatively-charged (down) quarks, a result of the complex quark-gluon dynamics, lead to a negative value for its squared charge radius, $\langle r_{\rm n}^2 \rangle$. The precise measurement of the neutron's charge radius thus emerges as an essential part of unraveling its structure. Here we report on a $\langle r_{\rm n}^2 \rangle$ measurement, based on the extraction of the neutron electric form factor, $G_{\rm E}^{\rm n}$, at low four-momentum transfer squared $(Q^2)$ by exploiting the long known connection between the $N \rightarrow Δ$ quadrupole transitions and the neutron electric form factor. Our result, $\langle r_{\rm n}^2 \rangle = -0.110 \pm0.008~({\rm fm}^2)$, addresses long standing unresolved discrepancies in the $\langle r_{\rm n}^2 \rangle$ determination. The dynamics of the strong nuclear force can be viewed through the precise picture of the neutron's constituent distributions that result into the non-zero $\langle r_{\rm n}^2 \rangle$ value.

## Key claim

The authors report a measurement of the neutron squared charge radius, ⟨r_n²⟩ = -0.110 ± 0.008 fm², extracted from the neutron electric form factor G_E^n at low four-momentum transfer squared (Q²) using the relation between N→Δ quadrupole transitions and the neutron electric form factor, which addresses long-standing discrepancies in previous determinations from neutron-electron scattering length measurements.

## Method

The authors extracted G_E^n at low Q² from high-precision measurements of the Coulomb quadrupole (C2/M1) and electric quadrupole (E2/M1) transition form factors of the proton N→Δ transition, using form factor relations from SU(6) and large-N_c frameworks. The symmetry breaking corrections in the SU(6) relation were determined experimentally from world data and refined using lattice QCD calculations. The final G_E^n values were obtained from the weighted average of the SU(6) and large-N_c analyses, with the variance treated as a theoretical uncertainty, and ⟨r_n²⟩ was determined from the slope of G_E^n at Q²=0 using a three-parameter functional fit.

## Key numbers

- ⟨r_n²⟩ = -0.110 ± 0.008 fm² (total uncertainty at 68% confidence level)
- When using conservative symmetry breaking treatment (n_b = 1.1 ± 0.1): ⟨r_n²⟩ = -0.109 ± 0.009 fm²
- Fit parameters for G_E^n(Q²) = (1 + Q²/A)^(-2) × (Bτ)/(1 + Cτ): A = 0.505 ± 0.079 (GeV/c)², B = 1.655 ± 0.126, C = 0.909 ± 0.583, with reduced χ² = 0.74
- When including CLAS data up to Q² = 0.52 (GeV/c)²: ⟨r_n²⟩ = -0.107 ± 0.007 fm²
- New weighted average excluding Aleksandrov (1986): ⟨r_n²⟩ = -0.1152 ± 0.0017 fm²
- Previous PDG value: ⟨r_n²⟩ = -0.1161 ± 0.0022 fm²

## Context

This work provides an alternative determination of the neutron charge radius that is independent of the traditional method based on neutron-electron scattering length measurements (b_ne), which had shown discrepancies between results from Kopecky (1997), Koester (1995), and Aleksandrov (1986). The result supports the lower-magnitude values from Kopecky (1997) and Koester (1995) while disagreeing with Aleksandrov (1986), and the authors argue for excluding the Aleksandrov value from the world average. The measurement enables improved precision in the flavor decomposition of nucleon charge densities, revealing the u- and d-quark distributions in the neutron, and lays the path for future higher-precision measurements using this method, such as Jefferson Lab LOI 12-20-002.
