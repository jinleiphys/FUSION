# Viscous Quark-Gluon Plasma Model Through Fluid QCD Approach

> A Lagrangian density for viscous quark-gluon plasma has been constructed within the fluid-like QCD framework. Gauge symmetry is preserved for all terms inside the Lagrangian, except for the viscous term. The transition mechanism from point particle field to fluid field, and vice versa, is discussed. The energy momentum tensor that is relevant for the gluonic plasma having the nature of fluid bulk of gluon sea is derived within the model. By imposing conservation law in the energy momentum tensor, shear viscosity appears as extractable from the equation.

# Viscous Quark-Gluon Plasma Model Through Fluid QCD Approach

## Key claim

The paper constructs a Lagrangian density for viscous quark-gluon plasma (QGP) within a fluid-like QCD framework, where gauge symmetry is preserved for all terms except the viscous term; the shear viscosity coefficient is presented as extractable from the conservation law of the energy-momentum tensor, but no explicit numerical value for the shear viscosity is actually derived or computed in this work.

## Method

The authors start from the conventional SU(3) QCD Lagrangian and reformulate the gluon field as a fluid field by expressing it as a product of a relativistic four-velocity and a scalar field distribution. A viscous term is added to the Lagrangian by performing a reverse calculation from the standard viscous energy-momentum tensor used in relativistic hydrodynamics, assuming negligible bulk viscosity. The total energy-momentum tensor is then constructed for a gluon-dominated plasma (neglecting quark contributions), and the conservation law is applied via covariant derivative to obtain an equation from which shear viscosity could in principle be extracted.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of electromagnetic to strong coupling is estimated as e/g_s = sqrt(alpha/alpha_s) ~ O(10^{-1}), justifying the neglect of U(1) electromagnetic effects.
- The gluon field strength tensor is defined with the SU(3) structure constants f^{abc} and the strong coupling constant g_F (denoted g_s in the text).
- The final viscous Lagrangian term is given as L_vis = -(1/2) eta T^a U^{a mu} U^{b l} partial_l U^b_mu, where eta is the shear viscosity coefficient.

## Context

This work extends the fluid-like QCD approach previously developed by Sulaiman et al. (2009) and Djun and Handoko (2011), who described QGP as a strongly interacting gluon sea. The model aims to provide a field-theoretic foundation for the small shear viscosity-to-entropy ratio observed in relativistic heavy-ion collision experiments at RHIC, as reported by the STAR Collaboration (2003) and PHENIX Collaboration (2008). The approach enables future extraction of shear viscosity through further elaboration of the conservation equation, potentially using linear response theory and the Kubo formula, and complements existing relativistic hydrodynamics treatments by Bouras et al. (2009) and Romatschke (2010).
