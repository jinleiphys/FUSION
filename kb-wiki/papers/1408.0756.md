# Viscous hydrodynamics for strongly anisotropic expansion

> A new formulation of second-order viscous hydrodynamics, based on an expansion around a locally anisotropic momentum distribution, is presented. It generalizes the previously developed formalism of anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) to include a complete set of dissipative currents for which equations of motion are derived by solving the Boltzmann equation in the 14-moment approximation. By solving the vaHydro equations for a transversally homogeneous, longitudinally boost-invariant system ((0+1)-dimensional expansion) and comparing with the exact solution of the Boltzmann equation in relaxation-time approximation we show that vaHydro performs much better than all other known second-order viscous hydrodynamic approximations.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new formulation of second-order viscous hydrodynamics (vaHydro) that combines a non-perturbative treatment of the largest component of the shear stress tensor (the longitudinal/transverse pressure anisotropy) via anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) with a perturbative Israel-Stewart treatment of the remaining four shear stress components, and claims that for a (0+1)-dimensional boost-invariant expansion this scheme reproduces the exact solution of the Boltzmann equation in relaxation-time approximation much better than all other known second-order viscous hydrodynamic approximations.

## Method

The authors derive equations of motion for the vaHydro scheme by solving the Boltzmann equation in the 14-moment approximation, expanding the distribution function around a spheroidally deformed local momentum distribution (the aHydro ansatz) rather than around an isotropic distribution as in standard Israel-Stewart theory. They then test the scheme by solving the vaHydro equations for a transversally homogeneous, longitudinally boost-invariant system ((0+1)-dimensional expansion) and comparing the results with the exact solution of the Boltzmann equation in relaxation-time approximation (RTA), as well as with standard second-order viscous hydrodynamics, third-order viscous hydrodynamics, and aHydro alone.

## Key numbers

* For an initial temperature of 600 MeV at proper time 0.25 fm/c evolving to a final temperature of 150 MeV, the particle production measure (τ_f n(τ_f))/(τ_0 n(τ_0)) - 1 as a function of 4πη/s shows that vaHydro reproduces the exact solution of the Boltzmann equation almost perfectly, dramatically outperforming all other hydrodynamic approximations.
* The relaxation time is related to the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio by τ_rel = 5η/(sT) = 5η/(R^{1/4}(ξ) s Λ), where Λ is the transverse temperature parameter and R(ξ) is a function of the momentum-anisotropy parameter ξ.

## Context

This work extends the previously developed formalism of anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) by Martinez and Florkowski (2010) and Florkowski (2010), which accounted only for the largest component of the shear stress tensor and could not compute the viscous suppression of elliptic flow. The new vaHydro scheme generalizes aHydro to include a complete set of dissipative currents, enabling treatment of all five independent components of the shear stress tensor. The approach enables more accurate hydrodynamic modeling of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, particularly during the early stage when large anisotropies between longitudinal and transverse expansion rates cause standard Israel-Stewart theory to break down.
