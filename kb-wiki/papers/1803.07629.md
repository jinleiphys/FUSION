# Towards observation of three-nucleon short-range correlations in high Q^2 A(e, e')X reactions

> We present a detailed study of kinematical and dynamical conditions necessary for probing highly elusive three-nucleon short range correlations~(3N-SRCs) in nuclei through inclusive electron scattering. The kinematic requirements that should be satisfied in order to isolate 3N-SRCs in inclusive processes are derived. We demonstrate that a sequence of two short-range NN interactions represents the main mechanism for 3N-SRCs in inclusive processes. Within this mechanism we predict a quadratic dependence of the inclusive cross section ratios of nuclei to $^3$He in the 3N-SRC region to the same ratio measured in 2N-SRC domain. The extended analysis of the available data satisfying the necessary 3N-SRC kinematical conditions is presented. This analysis provides tantalizing signatures of scaling associated with the onset of 3N-SRC dominance. The same data are also consistent with the prediction of the quadratic relation between the ratios measured in the 3N and 2N-SRC regions for nuclei ranging $4 \le A \le 197$. This agreement made it possible to extract $a_3(A)$, the probability of 3N-SRCs relative to the A=3 nucleus. We find $a_3(A)$ to be significantly larger in magnitude than the analogous parameter, $a_2(A)$, for 2N-SRCs.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a sequence of two short-range NN interactions is the main mechanism for three-nucleon short-range correlations (3N-SRCs) in inclusive electron scattering, and predicts a quadratic dependence of the inclusive cross section ratios of nuclei to ³He in the 3N-SRC region to the same ratio measured in the 2N-SRC domain, with the caveat that the available data provide "tantalizing signatures" rather than definitive proof of scaling associated with 3N-SRC dominance.

## Method

The authors derived kinematic requirements for isolating 3N-SRCs in inclusive A(e,e')X reactions using the light-cone momentum fraction variable α, identifying that 3N-SRC dominance begins at α ≥ 1.6 with characteristic struck nucleon momenta p_z ≥ 700 MeV/c. They analyzed existing inclusive electron scattering data from JLab experiments (Fomin 2011) that satisfy these kinematic conditions, testing for scaling behavior in per-nucleon cross section ratios of nuclei (4 ≤ A ≤ 197) to ³He and comparing these ratios to the quadratic relation predicted from the successive NN-interaction mechanism.

## Key numbers

- The threshold for 3N-SRC dominance is identified at α ≥ 1.6, corresponding to struck nucleon longitudinal momenta p_z ≥ 700 MeV/c
- For Q² ≈ 3 GeV², a finite kinematic domain exists with α₃N ≥ 1.6; for Q² ≥ 5 GeV², the onset of 3N-SRCs becomes practically insensitive to the recoil mass of the spectator system
- The analysis covers nuclei ranging from A = 4 to A = 197
- For Q² > 3 GeV², final state interactions may alter α_N by no more than 0.14, too small to shift mean field nucleons (α_N ≈ 1) into the 3N-SRC domain
- The extracted a₃(A) parameter (probability of 3N-SRCs relative to A=3) is found to be significantly larger in magnitude than the analogous a₂(A) parameter for 2N-SRCs

## Context

This work extends the study of short-range correlations beyond the well-established 2N-SRC scaling observed in inclusive electron scattering experiments at SLAC and JLab (Frankfurt 1993, Kim 1996, Fomin 2011), where a₂(A) parameters were extracted for nuclei relative to the deuteron. The paper builds on the earlier observation of strong pn-SRC dominance in 2N-SRCs (Subedi 2008, Hen 2014) and the suggestion by Frankfurt and Strikman (1981) that α > 2 kinematics requires at least three nucleons in close proximity. The analysis enables future experiments at Jefferson Lab's 12 GeV upgrade to unambiguously verify 3N-SRCs and advance understanding of super-dense nuclear matter relevant to the nuclear equation of state above saturation densities.
