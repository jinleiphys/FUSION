# Evolution of nuclear charge radii in copper and indium isotopes

> Systematic trends in nuclear charge radii are of great interest due to universal shell effects and odd-even staggering (OES). The modified root mean square (rms) charge radius formula, which phenomenologically accounts for the formation of neutron-proton ($np$) correlations, is here applied for the first time to the study of odd-$Z$ copper and indium isotopes. Theoretical results obtained by the relativistic mean field (RMF) model with NL3, PK1 and NL3$^{*}$ parameter sets are compared with experimental data. Our results show that both OES and the abrupt changes across $N=50$ and $82$ shell closures are clearly reproduced in nuclear charge radii. The inverted parabolic-like behaviors of rms charge radii can also be described remarkably well between two neutron magic numbers, namely $N=28$ to $50$ for copper isotopes and $N=50$ to $82$ for indium isotopes. This implies that the $np$-correlations play an indispensable role in quantitatively determining the fine structures of nuclear charge radii along odd-$Z$ isotopic chains. Also, our conclusions have almost no dependence on the effective forces.

# Evolution of nuclear charge radii in copper and indium isotopes

## Key claim
The modified root-mean-square charge radius formula that phenomenologically accounts for neutron-proton (np) correlations, when applied within the relativistic mean field (RMF) model, reproduces both odd-even staggering and abrupt changes across N=50 and N=82 shell closures in odd-Z copper and indium isotopes, with the conclusions showing almost no dependence on the effective force used (NL3, PK1, or NL3*). However, the odd-even oscillation amplitude is slightly overestimated in 65-73Cu isotopes, which the authors attribute to the omission of np-correlations from unpaired neutrons and protons.

## Method
The authors applied the RMF model with NL3, PK1, and NL3* parameter sets to calculate charge radii of copper and indium isotopes, using a modified formula that adds a term proportional to the difference in Cooper pair condensation between neutrons and protons (ΔD) divided by the square root of the mass number. The pairing strength was set to V0=350 MeV fm3 for NL3 and V0=380 MeV fm3 for PK1 and NL3*, with the normalization constant a0 set to 0.834 for copper and 0.22 for indium. Results were compared with experimental data and with non-relativistic Hartree-Fock plus BCS calculations using the SV-bas Skyrme force.

## Key numbers
- The normalization constant a0 in the modified charge radius formula is 0.834 for copper isotopes and 0.22 for indium isotopes.
- Pairing strength V0 = 350 MeV fm3 is used for the NL3 parameter set, while V0 = 380 MeV fm3 is used for PK1 and NL3* forces.
- The modified formula reproduces the inverted parabolic-like behavior of charge radii between N=28 and N=50 for copper isotopes and between N=50 and N=82 for indium isotopes.
- The RMF(BCS) approach (without the modified term) fails to reproduce the rising trend of charge radii in copper isotopes between N=28 and N=50.

## Context
This work extends the RMF(BCS)* approach previously applied to calcium and 10 other even-Z isotopic chains (An, 2020) to odd-Z copper and indium isotopes. It addresses the long-standing challenge of describing fine structures in nuclear charge radii, particularly odd-even staggering and shell effects, which earlier mean-field approaches (such as the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov model by Goriely (2009, 2010) and the Skyrme Hartree-Fock model with SV-bas force) failed to reproduce quantitatively. The results are consistent with recent studies on cadmium (Hammen, 2018), tin (Gorges, 2019), and mercury (Goodacre, 2021) isotopic chains, and support the conclusion that np-correlations play an indispensable role in determining charge radius fine structures, as argued by Miller (2018) and Ryckebusch (2021). The work enables further unified approaches for predicting nuclear charge radii across the nuclear chart, which are important input quantities for astrophysics.
