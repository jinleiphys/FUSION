# An experimentalist's view of the uncertainties in understanding heavy element synthesis

> The overall uncertainties in predicting heavy element synthesis cross sections are examined in terms of the uncertainties associated with the calculations of capture cross sections, fusion probabilities and survival probabilities. Attention is focussed on hot fusion reactions. The predicted heavy element formation cross sections are uncertain to at least one order of magnitude.

# An experimentalist's view of the uncertainties in understanding heavy element synthesis

## Key claim
The overall uncertainty in predicting heavy element formation cross sections for hot fusion reactions is at least one order of magnitude, but the uncertainty varies dramatically by reaction type: for reactions involving familiar targets and ⁴⁸Ca projectiles the uncertainty is a factor of 2, while for symmetric reactions or those using exotic targets like ²⁵⁴Es the uncertainties can be several orders of magnitude.

## Method
The paper decomposes the evaporation residue cross section into three sequential stages, capture, fusion probability (P_CN), and survival probability (W_sur), and examines the uncertainties in each component separately. For survival probabilities, the author compares postdictions of fission barrier heights and neutron-to-fission width ratios against experimental data from reactions such as ²⁶Mg + ²⁴⁸Cm forming ²⁷⁴Hs. For fusion probabilities, measured P_CN values from multiple systems are organized by fissility-related scaling variables (x_m) and compared to phenomenological formulas from Zagrebaev and Greiner (2007). Capture cross sections are evaluated by comparing coupled-channel calculations against measured cross sections for reactions that synthesize heavy nuclei.

## Key numbers
- The reported cross sections for superheavy element synthesis in hot fusion reactions decrease by a factor of ~3 in going from element 114 to element 118.
- For the ²⁶Mg + ²⁴⁸Cm reaction forming ²⁷⁴Hs at E* = 63 MeV, the measured Γ_n/Γ_total for first-chance fission was 0.89 ± 0.13.
- The average deviation between calculated and known inner fission barrier heights among various models is ~0.7 MeV.
- An uncertainty of 0.5–1.0 MeV in fission barrier heights can cause an order of magnitude error in EVR cross sections; for the ²³⁸U(²⁶Mg,3-4n) reaction, such uncertainty causes a factor of 6–7 uncertainty in EVR cross sections.
- TDHF calculations for ⁴⁸Ca + ²³⁸U predicted capture cross sections within ±20% of measured values; the predicted fusion-to-capture ratio was 0.09 ± 0.07 at 205.9 MeV and 0.16 ± 0.06 at 225.4 MeV, compared to measured ratios of 0.06 ± 0.03 and 0.14 ± 0.05 respectively.
- Capture cross section models describe magnitudes within 50% and interaction barriers within 20%.
- The phenomenological formula for asymptotic P_CN⁰ uses ζ ≈ 1760 and τ ≈ 45 in a Fermi function of Z₁Z₂.

## Context
This work extends the critical assessment of uncertainties in heavy element synthesis predictions, following comments by leading figures in the field (Oganessian and colleagues, 2013) that formation cross sections for Z > 112 can be calculated with at best two orders of magnitude accuracy. The paper specifically examines the component uncertainties that previous postdiction studies (Siwek-Wilczynska and collaborators, 2007; Adamian and colleagues, 2004; Feng and colleagues, 2007) have masked by achieving overall agreement with measured cross sections while disagreeing on individual factors like P_CN. The analysis enables experimentalists to better estimate which new element synthesis experiments (such as attempts at elements 119 and 120 using ⁵⁰Ti projectiles) are feasible given current apparatus sensitivity, and highlights the need for improved theoretical treatment of nuclear viscosity and shell correction damping in hot fusion reactions.
