# Implications of Recent Data on Non-mesonic Decay of Light Lambda-hypernuclei

> We analyze the recent data on the non-mesonic decays of light Lambda-hypernuclei up to ^12_Lambda C using the phenomenological model of Block and Dalitz. Fitting the spin-isospin dependent Lambda N -> NN reaction rates to six data points, we predict the remaining data in reasonable consistency. We find that despite the short-range nature of the Lambda N -> NN interaction, the non-mesonic decay of p-shell hypernuclei seems to be strongly induced by the p-shell neutrons. Also, the recent data indicate that the Delta I = 1/2 rule, well proved at the hadronic level, may not be sacred in the nuclear medium and the Delta I = 3/2 interactions seem to be needed to describe the non-mesonic decays of Lambda-hypernuclei.

# Implications of Recent Data on Non-mesonic Decay of Light Lambda-hypernuclei

## Key claim

The recent data on non-mesonic decays of light Lambda-hypernuclei favor including Delta I = 3/2 interactions in addition to Delta I = 1/2 ones in the effective Hamiltonian, suggesting the Delta I = 1/2 rule may not hold in the nuclear medium; however, with experimental error bars taken into account, the Delta I = 1/2 rule is not completely excluded.

## Method

The authors applied the phenomenological model of Block and Dalitz (1963), which expresses non-mesonic decay widths of hypernuclei in terms of spin- and isospin-dependent elementary Lambda N to NN reaction rates and mean nucleon densities at the Lambda position. They fitted six elementary reaction rates (R_p0, R_p1, R_n0, R_n1 for s-shell nucleons, and R_p', R_n' for p-shell nucleons) to six data points from recent measurements on ^4_Lambda H, ^4_Lambda He, ^5_Lambda He, and ^11_Lambda B, then used these rates to predict the remaining decay widths for ^4_Lambda He and ^12_Lambda C.

## Key numbers

- Free Lambda decay width: Gamma_Lambda = 2.50 micro-eV
- Energy release in non-mesonic decay: m_Lambda - m_N = 175 MeV (compared to 35 MeV for pi-mesonic decay)
- Mean nucleon densities at the Lambda position: rho(^4_Lambda H) = 0.017 fm^-3, rho(^4_Lambda He) = 0.019 fm^-3, rho(^5_Lambda He) = 0.038 fm^-3, rho_s(^11_Lambda B) = 0.048 fm^-3, rho_p(^11_Lambda B) = 0.038 fm^-3, rho_s(^12_Lambda C) = 0.047 fm^-3, rho_p(^12_Lambda C) = 0.044 fm^-3
- Input data used for fitting: Gamma_nm/Gamma_Lambda for ^4_Lambda H = 0.24 +/- 0.13, for ^5_Lambda He = 0.41 +/- 0.14, for ^11_Lambda B = 0.95 +/- 0.17; Gamma_p/Gamma_Lambda for ^5_Lambda He = 0.21 +/- 0.07, for ^11_Lambda B = 0.30 (+0.15/-0.11); Gamma_n/Gamma_p for ^4_Lambda He = 0.40 +/- 0.15
- Predicted values: Gamma_nm/Gamma_Lambda for ^4_Lambda He = 0.20 (data: 0.17 +/- 0.05), for ^12_Lambda C = 0.95 (data: 0.89 +/- 0.18); Gamma_p/Gamma_Lambda for ^12_Lambda C = 0.31 (data: 0.31 +0.18/-0.11)
- Elementary reaction rates (in units of Gamma_Lambda fm^3): R_p0 = 21.3, R_p1 = 7.6, R_n0 = 8.8, R_n1 = 11.1, R_p' = 1.8, R_n' = 20.9
- Ratios from fitted rates: R_n0/R_p0 = 0.4, R_n1/R_p1 = 1.5, R_n'/R_p' = 11.5

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological approach of Block and Dalitz (1963) and the subsequent analyses by Cohen (1992) and Schumacher (1992), who used older or preliminary data on s-shell hypernuclei only. By incorporating new data from Outa (1992), Szymanski (1991), and Noumi (1995) on ^4_Lambda H, ^4_Lambda He, ^5_Lambda He, ^11_Lambda B, and ^12_Lambda C, the authors extend the analysis to p-shell hypernuclei for the first time within this model. The finding that p-shell neutrons contribute strongly to non-mesonic decay contradicts the expectation from Fetisov (1995) that short-range Lambda N to NN interactions should favor s-shell nucleons, and the evidence for Delta I = 3/2 contributions challenges the assumption used in meson-exchange models by Bando (1990) and Oset (1996). The authors report that a microscopic approach using an effective Hamiltonian with both Delta I = 1/2 and Delta I = 3/2 four-fermion interactions is in progress.
