# Emergent Canonical Spin Tensor in the Chiral-Symmetric Hot QCD

> The spin tensor is fundamental to relativistic spin hydrodynamics, but its definition is ambiguous due to the pseudogauge symmetry. We show that this ambiguity can be solved in interacting field theories. We prove that the mean-field limit of a modified Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with spin-spin interactions is equivalent to nondissipative spin hydrodynamics with a canonical spin tensor.

# Emergent Canonical Spin Tensor in the Chiral-Symmetric Hot QCD

## Key claim
The authors prove that the mean-field limit of a modified Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with spin-spin interactions is equivalent to nondissipative spin hydrodynamics with a canonical spin tensor, suggesting that the canonical spin tensor is dynamically generated in the quark-gluon plasma through the chiral separation effect and the axial vortical effect. However, this equivalence holds specifically in the mean-field approximation, and the authors note that different microscopic models could result in different pseudogauge choices.

## Method
The authors start from the statistical operator of spin hydrodynamics with a canonical spin tensor, derived via the Zubarev formalism by maximizing entropy under constraints on energy-momentum and spin tensor densities. They rewrite this partition function as a Euclidean path integral in an emergent thermal spacetime. Separately, they consider a modified NJL model with an axial-axial current interaction term and perform a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to introduce a pseudovector field. By comparing the resulting actions, they show that in the mean-field approximation the NJL partition function coincides with the spin hydrodynamic partition function, with the mean axial field playing the role of the difference between thermal vorticity and spin potential.

## Key numbers
- The canonical spin tensor for a Dirac fermion is given by $\mathcal{S}_{\rm C}^{\lambda,\mu\nu} = \frac{1}{2}\bar\psi\{\gamma^\lambda,\Sigma^{\mu\nu}\}\psi = \epsilon^{\rho\lambda\mu\nu} \frac{\bar\psi \gamma_\rho \gamma^5 \psi}{2}$, where $\Sigma_{\mu\nu}=(\mathrm{i}/4)[\gamma_\mu,\gamma_\nu]$.
- The spin potential $\Theta$ is defined as $\Theta = (\varpi - \SP)$, where $\varpi$ is thermal vorticity and $\SP$ is the spin potential Lagrange multiplier.
- The pseudovector field $f^{\tilde\sigma}$ is related to the spin potential by $f^{\tilde\sigma} = \frac{1}{4}\epsilon^{\tilde\mu\tilde\nu\tilde\rho\tilde\sigma}(\varpi_{\tilde{\mu}\tilde{\nu}}-\SP_{\tilde{\mu}\tilde{\nu}})n_{\tilde\rho}$.
- The NJL coupling constant $G_A$ appears in the Lagrangian term $G_A (\bar{q}\gamma_{\mu} \gamma^5 q)(\bar{q}\gamma^{\mu}\gamma^5 q)$ and in the Hubbard-Stratonovich transformed action as a factor $1/(4G_A)$ multiplying $f_{\tilde\mu}f^{\tilde\mu}$.

## Context
This work addresses the long-standing ambiguity in defining the spin tensor in relativistic spin hydrodynamics, which has been debated in works by Becattini (2011, 2018, 2020), Buzzegoli (2021), Speranza (2020), Fukushima (2020), and others. Different versions of spin hydrodynamics have been proposed based on different spin tensors: the Belinfante (Fukushima 2020), canonical (Hattori 2019, Hongo 2021, Dey 2023), de Groot-van Leeuwen-van Weert (Florkowski 2018), and Hilgevoord-Wouthuysen (Weickgenannt 2022). The authors show that the NJL model, which is justified as an effective description of QCD by functional renormalization group methods (Braun 2011, 2017, 2018, 2019), dynamically selects the canonical spin tensor. This enables a microscopic foundation for choosing the canonical spin tensor in spin hydrodynamics for heavy-ion collisions, and the authors suggest that the chiral separation effect (observed in lattice QCD by Brandt 2023) and axial vortical effect provide the physical mechanisms for spin potential generation in the quark-gluon plasma.
