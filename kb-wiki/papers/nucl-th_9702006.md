# Combined analysis of the reactions $pp \to pp$, $πd\to πd$, and $πd\to pp$

> Results are presented for a combined analysis of the reactions $pp\to pp$, $πd\to πd$ and $πd\to pp$ over the $\sqrt{s}$ interval from pion threshold to approximately 2.4 GeV. These results for $πd\to pp$ and $πd$ elastic scattering are superior to our previous analyses of these reactions. In particular, the overall phase in $πd\to pp$ has now been determined. Comparisons are made with previous (separate and combined) analyses of this two-nucleon system.

## Key claim

The combined K-matrix analysis of \(pp\to pp\), \(\pi d\to \pi d\), and \(\pi d\to pp\) yields partial-wave amplitudes for the pion-induced reactions that are superior to previous separate analyses, and for the first time determines the overall phase in \(\pi d\to pp\); however, the most significant improvements over the separate analyses appear only above approximately 2.3 GeV in \(\sqrt{s}\) (or 300 MeV pion kinetic energy), where existing data are sparse and the combined fit is less stable.

## Method

A coupled-channel K-matrix formalism was constructed coupling \(pp\), \(\pi d\), and an \(N\Delta\) channel (accounting for all other inelastic channels). The \(pp\) elastic partial-wave amplitudes were held fixed to the authors' prior single-reaction analysis up to 1.6 GeV, and the remaining K-matrix parameters were determined by fitting the combined \(\pi d\) elastic and \(\pi d\to pp\) data bases over the \(\sqrt{s}\) interval from pion threshold to approximately 2.4 GeV. Partial waves with \(J\le 7\) were used for \(pp\) elastic scattering, and \(J\le 5\) for both \(\pi d\) elastic scattering and \(\pi d\to pp\).

## Key numbers

- The analysis covers the \(\sqrt{s}\) interval from pion threshold to approximately **2.4 GeV** (corresponding to pion laboratory kinetic energies \(T_\pi = 0\)–**500 MeV**).
- For \(pp\) elastic scattering, all partial waves with \(J\le 7\) were used; for \(\pi d\) elastic and \(\pi d\to pp\), partial waves with \(J\le 5\) were retained.
- The \(pp\) elastic amplitudes were fixed to the authors' prior analysis up to **1.6 GeV**.
- Significant differences between the combined and separate analyses for \(\pi d\) elastic partial waves begin above a pion laboratory kinetic energy of **300 MeV** (or **2.3 GeV** in \(\sqrt{s}\)).
- The combined analysis reproduces the energy dependence of \(\pi d\) total cross section data between **300 and 500 MeV** much more successfully than the separate analysis, showing a rise at **500 MeV** where the separate analysis showed a monotonic decrease from 400 to 500 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior separate analyses of \(pp\to pp\) (Arndt et al., 1990s), \(\pi d\to \pi d\) (Arndt et al., 1990s), and \(\pi d\to pp\) (Arndt et al., 1990s) by enforcing unitarity across all three channels simultaneously. It differs from a joint analysis by Nagata et al. (1990s) near the \(N\Delta\) threshold and an older multi-channel K-matrix study by Edwards (1980s) of \(J^P=2^+\) and \(3^-\) dibaryon candidates, in that it does not restrict the analysis to specific partial waves and uses no outside model constraints. The resulting amplitudes provide a unified description of resonancelike behavior previously noted in the separate analyses, which should help constrain models of dibaryon resonances versus \(N\Delta\) intermediate-state effects.
