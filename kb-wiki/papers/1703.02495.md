# Kinetic description of Bose-Einstein condensation with test particle simulations

> We present a kinetic description of Bose-Einstein condensation for particle systems being out of thermal equilibrium, which may happen for gluons produced in the early stage of ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The dynamics of bosons towards equilibrium is described by a Boltzmann equation including Bose factors. To solve the Boltzmann equation with the presence of a Bose-Einstein condensate we make further developments of the kinetic transport model BAMPS (Boltzmann Approach of MultiParton Scatterings). In this work we demonstrate the correct numerical implementations by comparing the final numerical results to the expected solutions at thermal equilibrium for systems with and without the presence of Bose-Einstein condensate. In addition, the onset of the condensation in an over-populated gluon system is studied in more details. We find that both expected power-law scalings denoted by the particle and energy cascade are observed in the calculated gluon distribution function at infrared and intermediate momentum regions, respectively. Also, the time evolution of the hard scale exhibits a power-law scaling in a time window, which indicates that the distribution function is approximately self-similar during that time.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a kinetic transport model (BAMPS) with a new stochastic collision method can correctly simulate Bose-Einstein condensation for over-populated gluon systems, showing that both the expected particle cascade (with exponent \(r=2\)) and energy cascade (with exponent \(r=7/3\)) appear in the distribution function, but the exact onset time of condensation must be chosen based on when the \(p^{-2}\) power law is reached at low momenta rather than when the effective chemical potential crosses zero.

## Method

The authors solve the Boltzmann equation including Bose enhancement factors for massless bosons using a test-particle Monte Carlo approach within the BAMPS framework. They introduce a new stochastic collision method that avoids computing the full effective cross section by sampling collision angles first and then accepting or rejecting based on the Bose factors \((1+f_1)(1+f_2)\) evaluated at the proposed outgoing momenta. For gluons, they use leading-order perturbative QCD matrix elements with HTL screening, and for the condensate they derive constraints on matrix elements involving zero-momentum particles to ensure energy-momentum conservation.

## Key numbers

- For an under-populated system with \(f_0=0.05\) and \(Q_s=1\ \mathrm{GeV}\), the equilibration timescale is about \(170\ \mathrm{fm/c}\), and the final equilibrium temperature is \(T=0.258\ \mathrm{GeV}\) with chemical potential \(\mu=-0.205\ \mathrm{GeV}\).
- For the critical case \(f_0=0.154\) (where condensation begins), the equilibration timescale is about \(65\ \mathrm{fm/c}\) with final temperature \(T=0.277\ \mathrm{GeV}\) and \(\mu=0\).
- For the over-populated gluon system with \(f_0=1\) and \(Q_s=1\ \mathrm{GeV}\), the onset time of condensation is chosen as \(t_c=0.826\ \mathrm{fm/c}\), when the \(p^{-2}\) power law is achieved at low momenta around \(5\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The particle cascade scaling \(f(p) \sim p^{-2}\) is observed from about \(0.812\ \mathrm{fm/c}\) to \(0.826\ \mathrm{fm/c}\) in the infrared region, while the energy cascade scaling \(f(p) \sim p^{-7/3}\) appears earlier at about \(0.73\ \mathrm{fm/c}\) and extends toward lower momentum with time.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous BAMPS study (Xu 2014) by providing full numerical details on implementing Bose statistics and condensate scatterings, and by demonstrating turbulent cascades and self-similarity in the distribution function. It builds on theoretical predictions by Semikoz (1994) for self-similar solutions of the Boltzmann equation, and on Fokker-Planck approaches by Blaizot (2011, 2012, 2013, 2014, 2015) for gluon condensation. The results enable future studies of number-changing processes and expanding systems, and provide a numerical framework for investigating Bose-Einstein condensation in heavy-ion collision contexts.
