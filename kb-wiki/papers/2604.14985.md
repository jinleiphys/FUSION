# Perturbative calculations of light nuclei up to N$^3$LO in chiral effective field theory

> We predict ground-state energies of $^3$H, $^4$He, and $^6$Li in chiral effective field theory up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading-order (N$^3$LO) using a power counting guided by renormalization-group invariance. Subleading two-nucleon interactions are treated perturbatively, and for $^4$He and $^6$Li, we calculate the perturbative corrections from numerical derivatives of ground-state energies obtained with Lanczos diagonalization. We find that including the $^3$H binding energy in the calibration is essential for robust predictions of $^4$He and $^6$Li. This work demonstrates that the employed power counting can be applied to construct nuclear interactions with predictive power for light nuclei, bringing nuclear structure predictions closer to a foundation in quantum chromodynamics.

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that a renormalization-group-invariant power counting in chiral effective field theory can be applied to construct nuclear interactions up to N$^3$LO that predict ground-state energies of $^3$H, $^4$He, and $^6$Li, but only if the $^3$H binding energy is included in the calibration of the low-energy constants; without this few-body input, the predictions are poor and show large cutoff dependence.

## Method
The authors construct nucleon-nucleon potentials up to N$^3$LO using the Long and Yang power counting, where only leading-order (LO) interactions are treated non-perturbatively and all subleading orders are included perturbatively. For $^3$H, they implement Rayleigh-Schrödinger perturbation theory in a Jacobi-coordinate no-core shell model (NCSM) code, explicitly summing over the full LO spectrum. For $^4$He and $^6$Li, they use an M-scheme NCSM with Lanczos diagonalization and compute perturbative corrections via numerical derivatives of the ground-state energy with respect to coupling parameters multiplying the subleading potentials.

## Key numbers
- Ground-state energy of $^3$H computed up to N$^3$LO for cutoffs $\Lambda = 400, 450, 500$ MeV, with harmonic oscillator frequency $\omega = 24$ MeV, showing convergence with basis size $\Nmax$ up to $\Nmax = 30$.
- For $^4$He and $^6$Li, ground-state energies computed up to N$^3$LO with $\Nmax = 16$ ($^4$He) and $\Nmax = 14$ ($^6$Li), using $\omega = 24$ MeV.
- The finite-difference method for perturbative corrections uses a stencil step length $h = 0.06$ with second-order stencils ($p=2$), requiring 13 exact diagonalizations for a complete N$^3$LO computation.
- Single precision ($\epsilon = 10^{-7}$) in the ground-state eigenvalue from Lanczos diagonalization is sufficient for sub-percent numerical error in $^4$He up to N$^3$LO.
- For $^6$Li, the precision achieved at N$^3$LO is approximately 1 MeV.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of renormalization-group-invariant chiral effective field theory that investigated NN scattering up to N$^3$LO (Long 2012, Thim 2024) and light nuclei up to NLO (Song 2016, Yang 2020). It also builds on a recent study of $^3$H at N$^2$LO (Thim 2025) that identified exceptional cutoff effects. The results demonstrate that the perturbative power counting can be applied to compute ground-state energies of light nuclei beyond the NN system, but the need to include $^3$H binding energy in the calibration highlights the importance of few-body data for constraining the tensor interaction in the $^3S_1$-$^3D_1$ channel. The work enables future inclusion of three-nucleon forces and extension to heavier nuclei, while also raising questions about the role of exceptional cutoffs at the relatively low cutoffs ($\Lambda \approx 500$ MeV) used here.
