# Auxiliary field method at finite temperature and/or finite density

> We discuss the relation between the effective vector meson mass and equation of state (EOS) for nuclear matter. In the mean field approximation, the EOS becomes softer due to the reduction of the effective omega-meson mass, if we assume that the omega-meson mean field is proportional to the baryon density. We examine the assumption by using the auxiliary field method at finite temperature and /or density.

## Key claim

The paper argues that if the omega-meson mean field is proportional to baryon density, then a reduction in the effective omega-meson mass makes the nuclear matter equation of state (EOS) softer, not stiffer as previously reported. This claim is conditional on the proportionality assumption, which the author examines using the auxiliary field method in a simple four-fermion interaction model.

## Method

The author uses the generalized mean field theory to derive relations between effective meson masses, effective meson-nucleon couplings, and the pressure derivative with respect to baryon density. The proportionality assumption is then tested by introducing an auxiliary field into a generating functional for a four-fermion interaction model at finite temperature and/or finite density, and differentiating with respect to the source current to obtain the relation between the omega-meson mean field and baryon density.

## Key numbers

- The effective omega-meson mass \(m_\omega^*\) and effective coupling \(\hat{g}_\omega\) are related by \(\hat{g}_\omega / g_\omega = {m_\omega^*}^2 / m_\omega^2\), where \(g_\omega\) and \(m_\omega\) are the zero-density values.
- The pressure derivative is given by \(dP/d\rho_{\rm B} = \left( k_{\rm F}^2 / (3\rho_{\rm B} E_{\rm F}^*) + \hat{g}_\omega g_\omega / m_\omega^2 + \hat{g}_\rho^2 / {m_\rho^*}^2 - \hat{g}_\sigma^2 / {m_\sigma^*}^2 \cdot {m^*}^2 / {E_{\rm F}^*}^2 \right) \rho_{\rm B}\).
- The omega-meson mean field is shown to be \(\omega = (g_\omega / m_\omega^2) \rho_{\rm B}\), using the relation \(\lambda = g_\omega^2 / m_\omega^2\).

## Context

This work extends the generalized mean field theory of nuclear matter (references [4,5] in the paper) by examining the assumption that vector meson mean fields are proportional to baryonic currents. It contradicts the earlier finding (reference [3]) that reducing the effective vector meson mass makes the EOS stiffer. The paper enables future work beyond the mean field approximation or with more complex interaction models, as the author notes that the simplest mean field approximation does not produce density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings.
