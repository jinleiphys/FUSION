# In search of chiral magnetic effect: separating flow-driven background effects and quantifying anomaly-induced charge separations

> We report our recent progress on the search of Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) by developing new measurements as well as by hydrodynamic simulations of CME and background effects, with both approaches addressing the pressing issue of separating flow-driven background contributions and possible CME signal in current heavy ion collision measurements.

## Key claim

The paper reports progress on separating flow-driven background contributions from possible Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) signals in heavy-ion collision measurements, but the results depend on key theoretical uncertainties such as the initial axial charge density and the lifetime of the magnetic field, which must be constrained by data.

## Method

The authors performed anomalous viscous hydrodynamic simulations using the data-validated VISH code to compute the evolution of axial and vector charge densities on top of a background hydrodynamic profile. They also developed a new measurement proposal using Cu + Au collisions to test the relative importance of electromagnetic-field versus flow-driven contributions. For the same-charge correlation analysis, they generalized the analytic formula for transverse momentum conservation (TMC) background effects to multiple hadron types, fitting the TMC parameters to STAR data and then subtracting them to extract the CME signal.

## Key numbers

* The peak magnetic field strength \(eB_0\) is taken from Ref. Bloczynski (2012), with magnitude on the order of \(eB \sim m_\pi^2\) in non-central collisions.
* The initial axial charge parameter \(\lambda_A \equiv n_A / (N_f s_I)\) is extracted from data for two representative magnetic field lifetimes: \(\tau_B = 1\) fm/c and \(\tau_B = 3\) fm/c, shown as a function of centrality in Fig. 3 (right).
* The extracted \(\langle Q_A^2 \rangle\) increases mildly from central toward peripheral collisions, with the order of magnitude consistent with theoretical estimates based on Chern-Simons diffusion rate for gluonic topological fluctuations.
* The CME signal \(a_1\) is found to be approximately proportional to \(\lambda_A\) and \(eB\), and grows linearly for small \(\tau_B\) while saturating when \(\tau_B\) becomes comparable to the fireball lifetime.

## Context

This work extends prior efforts to detect the Chiral Magnetic Effect in heavy-ion collisions, which have been complicated by elliptic-flow-driven background contributions that mimic the CME signal in standard observables like \(\gamma_{\alpha\beta}\). The authors build on earlier proposals using U + U collisions by Voloshin (2010) and Wang (2012) to disentangle magnetic field and flow effects, and they compare their anomalous hydrodynamic approach to the complementary event-by-event simulations of Hirono (2014) (HHK). The results enable future quantitative extraction of topological fluctuation information from CME measurements and provide predictions for identified hadron correlations (e.g., \(\gamma_{K^+K^+}\)) that can be tested by upcoming experimental data.
