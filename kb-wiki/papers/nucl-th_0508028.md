# Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov calculations with Gaussian expansion method

> We extensively develop an algorithm of implementing the Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov calculations, in which the Gaussian expansion method is employed. This algorithm is advantageous in describing the energy-dependent exponential and oscillatory asymptotics of the quasiparticle wave functions at large $r$, and in handling various effective interactions including those with finite ranges. We apply the present method to the oxygen isotopes with the Gogny interaction, keeping the spherical symmetry. In respect to the new magic numbers, effects of the pair correlation on the N=16 and 32 nuclei are investigated.

# Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov calculations with Gaussian expansion method

## Key claim
The Gaussian expansion method (GEM) can efficiently represent both the exponential and oscillatory asymptotics of quasiparticle wave functions in Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov calculations, even with finite-range interactions, but the asymptotic behavior of density and pair current near the neutron drip line may become sensitive to computational details when no discrete quasiparticle levels satisfy the bound-state condition.

## Method
The author implements the Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) equations using Gaussian basis functions with complex range parameters, allowing simultaneous description of exponential decay and oscillatory behavior at large radial distances. The method is applied to spherical oxygen isotopes using the Gogny D1S interaction, with three different basis sets (real-range, complex-range, and mixed) tested for convergence. For investigating shell structure at N=16 and N=32, the D1S interaction is used for the pairing channel while comparing D1S and M3Y-P2 interactions for the Hartree-Fock potential.

## Key numbers
- Basis set parameter K fixed to 12 for each (ℓ,j) channel, with ℓ ≤ 4 truncation (ℓ ≤ 5 for N=32 nuclei)
- Energy gain from adding ℓ=5 bases is ≤ 0.06 MeV
- For ²⁶O, the neutron chemical potential λ ≈ −0.4 MeV, with quasiparticle energies ranging from 1.4 MeV to 40 MeV
- Total energy differences between basis Sets A and B are less than 10 keV in most oxygen isotopes
- Set C gives energies lower than Sets A and B by several tens keV for all oxygen isotopes
- In ²⁵O, the neutron 0d₃/₂ single-particle energy is ≈ −0.05 MeV

## Context
This work extends the author's previous Gaussian expansion method for Hartree-Fock calculations (Nakada 2003, Nakada and Sato 2002) to the Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov framework, addressing the need for proper treatment of wave-function asymptotics near the drip line. The method enables systematic investigation of finite-range effective interactions including the Gogny interaction and M3Y-type interactions, and allows study of how the pair correlation affects the new magic numbers N=16 and N=32. The results show that while the D1S interaction predicts a constant N=16 shell gap across Z=8 to Z=14, the M3Y-P2 interaction gives a narrowing shell gap that is partially compensated by pairing, leading to similar quasiparticle energies but different occupation numbers that could be tested against shell-model calculations using the USD Hamiltonian.
