# A Note on the Consistent-$Q$ Scheme for Odd-Odd Nuclei

> Compared with even-even nuclear systems, the dynamical structure of low-lying excited states in odd-odd nuclei poses significantly greater theoretical challenges. The interacting boson fermion fermion model provides an effective framework for capturing the structural properties of odd-odd nuclei. Within this algebraic framework, the consistent-$Q$ scheme, which was widely used to map nuclear shape phase diagram, is extended to odd-odd systems to examine how unpaired nucleons influence level structures and their evolution across prototypical transitional regions. The results demonstrate that the presence of unpaired nucleons does not suppress the critical behavior characterizing level evolution across the U(5)-SU(3), U(5)-O(6) and SU(3)-O(6) shape transitions, suggesting that shape phase transitions remain a fundamental mechanism governing the low-lying structural evolution of odd-odd nuclei in the heavy and intermediately-heavy mass regions.

# A Note on the Consistent-$Q$ Scheme for Odd-Odd Nuclei

## Key claim
The consistent-$Q$ Hamiltonian, previously used to map shape phase transitions in even-even nuclei, is extended to odd-odd nuclei within the interacting boson fermion fermion model (IBFFM). The presence of two unpaired nucleons does not suppress the critical behavior characterizing level evolution across the U(5)-SU(3), U(5)-O(6), and SU(3)-O(6) shape transitions, but simple energy ratios like $R_{4/2}$ become less sensitive indicators of these transitions in odd-odd systems compared to even-even systems.

## Method
The authors diagonalized the consistent-$Q$ Hamiltonian for both the IBM (even-even core) and IBFFM (core plus two unpaired fermions) using a weak-coupling SU(3) basis, with total boson number $N=4$ and both unpaired nucleons occupying a $j=11/2$ single-particle orbit. They computed yrast-state energy levels and the energy ratio $R_{4/2}=E((J_g+4)_1^+)/E((J_g+2)_1^+)$ (with $J_g=0$ for IBM and $J_g=11$ for IBFFM) across the three transitional regions connecting the U(5), SU(3), and O(6) dynamical symmetry limits.

## Key numbers
- In the O(6) limit, the yrast levels exhibit $E(4_1^+)/E(2_1^+)=2.5$ for the IBM core.
- In the U(5)-SU(3) transition for the IBM, $R_{4/2}$ rises sharply from $R_{4/2}=2.0$ to $R_{4/2}=3.3$ near the critical point $\eta=0.5$.
- In the SU(3)-O(6) transition for the IBM, $R_{4/2}$ drops from $R_{4/2}=3.33$ to $R_{4/2}=2.5$ across the transitional region.
- The critical line in the large-$N$ limit is analytically described by $\eta_\mathrm{c}=14/(28+\chi^2)$ with $\chi\in[-\sqrt{7}/2,0)$.
- The second-order transition along the U(5)-O(6) leg ($\chi=0$) occurs at the critical point $\eta_\mathrm{c}=0.5$.

## Context
This work extends previous IBFM-based analyses of shape phase transitions in odd-A nuclei by Zhang (2018, 2021) to the more complex odd-odd case using the IBFFM framework. It contrasts with earlier approaches by Zhang (2013) that relied solely on ground-state property variations (e.g., odd-even binding energy differences) to identify shape phase transitions in odd-odd nuclei. The results demonstrate that high-spin excitation spectra can also probe these transitions under fixed single-particle configurations, while highlighting that standard energy ratios used for even-even systems (e.g., $E(4_1^+)/E(2_1^+)$) are less effective order parameters for odd-odd nuclei. The authors note that quantitative application to odd-odd nuclei may require additional Hamiltonian terms beyond the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction, such as boson-fermion exchange or monopole terms.
