# Nuclear effects in neutrino and antineutrino CCQE scattering at MINERvA kinematics

> We compare the charged-current quasielastic neutrino and antineutrino observables obtained in two different nuclear models, the phenomenological SuperScaling Approximation and the Relativistic Mean Field approach, with the recent data published by the MINERvA Collaboration. Both models provide a good description of the data without the need of an ad hoc increase in the mass parameter in the axial-vector dipole form factor. Comparisons are also made with the MiniBooNE results where different conclusions are reached.

## Key claim
Both the SuperScaling Approximation (SuSA) and Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) models, based on the impulse approximation, provide a good description of MINERvA charged-current quasielastic data without requiring an ad hoc increase of the axial-vector dipole mass parameter \(M_A\) from its standard value of 1.032 GeV, in contrast to conclusions drawn from MiniBooNE data.

## Method
The authors computed flux-folded differential cross sections \(d\sigma/dQ^2_{QE}\) for neutrino and antineutrino charged-current quasielastic scattering on a hydrocarbon target, using the SuSA and RMF nuclear models. The SuSA model incorporates a phenomenological superscaling function extracted from electron scattering data, with Pauli blocking implemented via a subtraction procedure and Coulomb corrections applied; the RMF model uses Dirac-Hartree solutions for bound and scattering nucleon states with scalar and vector potentials. Results were folded with the MINERvA neutrino and antineutrino fluxes and compared to experimental data, with additional comparisons to the Relativistic Fermi Gas (RFG) model and the effective momentum approximation (EMA).

## Key numbers
- Standard axial-vector dipole mass used: \(M_A = 1.032\) GeV (standard value from neutrino-deuterium scattering).
- Binding energy assumed for reconstruction: \(E_b = 34\) MeV for neutrinos, \(E_b = 30\) MeV for antineutrinos.
- Spread among the four models (RFG, SuSA, RMF, EMA) is less than 7% for neutrinos and less than 5% for antineutrinos in the differential cross section.
- Total cross section per nucleon (flux-averaged, 1.5 to 10 GeV): for neutrinos, SuSA gives \(0.834 \times 10^{-38}\) cm\(^2\), RMF gives \(0.901 \times 10^{-38}\) cm\(^2\), experimental value is \(0.93 \pm 0.12 \times 10^{-38}\) cm\(^2\); for antineutrinos, SuSA gives \(0.550 \times 10^{-38}\) cm\(^2\), RMF gives \(0.583 \times 10^{-38}\) cm\(^2\), experimental value is \(0.604 \pm 0.083 \times 10^{-38}\) cm\(^2\).
- Discrepancy between theory and data central values is at most 9-10% for SuSA/EMA, reduced to 2-3% for RMF/RFG.
- Preliminary estimates indicate 2p2h meson exchange current effects might add about 12-15% to the impulse approximation results.

## Context
This work extends comparisons of SuSA and RMF models, previously tested against electron scattering data (Amaro 2004, Amaro 2011), to the MINERvA experiment at beam energies peaked near 3 GeV. It contrasts with MiniBooNE results (Aguilar-Arevalo 2010, Aguilar-Arevalo 2013) where impulse approximation models underestimate data unless \(M_A\) is raised to 1.35 GeV, and with NOMAD data (Amaro 2013) where impulse approximation works well at higher energies. The findings suggest either inconsistencies between experiments in defining quasielastic events or that nuclear effects required at MiniBooNE kinematics diminish at MINERvA kinematics, enabling future work on relativistic 2p2h meson exchange current modeling at intermediate energies.
