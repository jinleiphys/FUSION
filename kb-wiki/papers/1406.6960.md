# Ratio B(E2, 4\rightarrow2)/B(E2, 2\rightarrow 0) in Even-Even Nuclei:Apparent Anomalous Behavior of the Chromium Isotopes

> We consider the ratio RE4 = B(E2,4\rightarrow2)/B(E2,2\rightarrow 0) for the lowest 2^{+} and 4^{+} states in even-even nuclei. In the rotatonal and vibrational models and the shell model calculations here considered, RE4 is greater than one, however empirically, using adopted values from NNDC for ^{48}Cr and ^{50} Cr this ratio is less than one.

## Key claim

The ratio RE4 = B(E2,4→2)/B(E2,2→0) is expected to be greater than one in rotational, vibrational, and shell model calculations, but for ⁴⁸Cr and ⁵⁰Cr the empirical values from NNDC adopted half-lives and energies yield ratios less than one, presenting an unresolved puzzle that may point to either theoretical or experimental issues.

## Method

The authors compiled adopted half-lives and excitation energies for the lowest 2⁺ and 4⁺ states of even-even nuclei from the National Nuclear Data Center (NNDC) and computed RE4 using the formula RE4 = (T₁/₂(2⁺) × E(2⁺)⁵) / (T₁/₂(4⁺) × (E(4⁺)−E(2⁺))⁵). They compared these empirical ratios to shell model calculations using the FPD6, GXPF1, JUN45, and JJ4B interactions in the fp shell, as well as to the rotational model value of 10/7 and the vibrational model value of 2. They also examined the effect of the T=0 two-body interaction by setting all T=0 matrix elements to zero (T0FPD6) while keeping T=1 elements unchanged.

## Key numbers

- Rotational model prediction: RE4 = 10/7 ≈ 1.429
- Vibrational model prediction: RE4 = 2
- ⁴⁸Cr empirical RE4: 0.862 (from NNDC half-lives: 2⁺ half-life 7.3 ps at 752.19 keV, 4⁺ half-life 1.23 ps at 1858.47 keV)
- ⁵⁰Cr empirical RE4: 0.756 (from NNDC half-lives: 2⁺ half-life 9.08 ps at 783.32 keV, 4⁺ half-life 2.22 ps at 1881.31 keV)
- ⁴⁴Ti empirical RE4: 2.285 (2⁺ half-life 3.1 ps at 1083.06 keV, 4⁺ half-life 0.42 ps at 2452.33 keV)
- ⁴⁶Ti empirical RE4: 1.034 (2⁺ half-life 5.32 ps at 889.28 keV, 4⁺ half-life 1.62 ps at 2009.846 keV)
- ⁴⁸Ti empirical RE4: 1.255 (2⁺ half-life 4.04 ps at 983.539 keV, 4⁺ half-life 0.762 ps at 2295.654 keV)
- ⁵²Fe empirical RE4: 1.839 (2⁺ half-life 7.8 ps at 849.45 keV, 4⁺ half-life 0.22 ps at 2384.55 keV)
- Using data from Brandolini et al. instead of NNDC, the RE4 values for ⁴⁸Cr and ⁵⁰Cr become 0.997 and 0.921 respectively, still less than one
- Shell model FPD6 calculations give RE4 = 1.396 for ⁴⁸Cr and 1.450 for ⁵⁰Cr, both greater than one

## Context

This work extends the shell model studies of Robinson et al. (2005, 2014) on B(E2) ratios and quadrupole moments in the fp shell, and contrasts the empirical RE4 values from NNDC with the rotational and vibrational model predictions of Bohr and Mottelson. The anomalous sub-unity ratios for ⁴⁸Cr and ⁵⁰Cr contradict the expectation from all standard models and from shell model calculations using FPD6, GXPF1, and other interactions; the authors suggest that new, more precise experiments on these chromium isotopes are needed before drawing definitive conclusions.
