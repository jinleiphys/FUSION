# BCS Pairing Gap in the Infrared Limit of the Similarity Renormalization Group

> Effective interactions have been used to compute the pairing gap for nuclear and neutron matter in several schemes. In this work we analyze the impact of phase-shift equivalent interactions within the BCS theory on the $^1S_0$-channel pairing gap for a translational invariant many-fermion system such as nuclear and neutron matter. We solve the BCS pairing gap equation on a finite momentum grid for a toy model separable Gaussian potential in the $^1S_0$-channel explicitly evolved through the Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) transformation and show that in the on-shell and continuum limits the pairing gap vanishes. For finite size systems the momentum is quantized and the on-shell limit is realized for SRG cutoffs comparable to the momentum resolution. In this case the pairing gap can be computed directly from the scattering phase-shifts by an energy-shift formula. While the momentum grid is usually used as an auxiliary way of solving the BCS pairing gap equation, we show that it actually encodes some relevant physical information, suggesting that in fact finite grids may represent the finite size of the system.

## Key claim

The BCS pairing gap in the $^1S_0$-channel for nuclear and neutron matter vanishes in the strict continuum limit ($N \to \infty$) when the interaction is evolved to the on-shell limit via the Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) transformation, but for finite-size systems the gap is non-zero and can be computed directly from scattering phase-shifts via an energy-shift formula.

## Method

The authors solve the BCS pairing gap equation on a finite momentum grid for a toy model separable Gaussian potential in the $^1S_0$-channel, with parameters fitted to reproduce the experimental scattering length and effective range. They evolve the potential through the SRG transformation using the Wegner generator, tracking the pairing gap as a function of the SRG cutoff $\lambda$ toward the infrared limit ($\lambda \to 0$). The calculations are performed for different numbers of grid points ($N = 10, 20, 50, 100$) to study the continuum limit.

## Key numbers

- The toy model potential parameters $C$ and $L$ are determined by fitting the Effective Range Expansion to second order: scattering length $a_0$ and effective range $r_e$.
- The phase-shifts calculated with the toy model are reasonable for CM on-shell momenta $k$ below $\sim 1~{\rm fm}^{-1}$ when compared to realistic $NN$ potentials.
- In the SRG infrared limit ($\lambda \to 0$), the BCS pairing gap on the grid is given by $\Delta^{ES}_\lambda(p_n) = w_n \frac{p_n \delta^{\rm ES}(p_n)}{\pi M}$, where $w_n$ are integration weights.
- In the continuum limit ($N \to \infty$), the pairing gap scales as $\Delta_F \sim \Delta p_F \frac{p_F \delta(p_F)}{\pi M}$, where $\Delta p_F = {\cal O}(1/R)$ for a system of size $R$, and vanishes as $N \to \infty$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of SRG-evolved interactions (Arriola 2013, 2014) to the BCS pairing problem, addressing the well-known scheme dependence of BCS gaps noted in comparisons between $V_{\rm low-k}$ calculations (which give a maximum gap of about $3~{\rm MeV}$ at $p_F \sim 0.8~{\rm fm}^{-1}$) and ab initio results (Gezerlis 2009, Gandolfi 2009, Baldo 2010). The key insight is that finite momentum grids, usually treated as a numerical tool, may physically represent finite system size, and that the on-shell limit of the SRG evolution eliminates off-shell ambiguities that plague standard BCS calculations. This enables a direct connection between phase-shifts and pairing gaps for finite systems, while showing that the infinite-system BCS gap vanishes in the on-shell limit.
