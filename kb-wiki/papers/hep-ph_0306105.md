# Chiral symmetry breaking, color superconductivity and color neutral quark matter: a variational approach

> We investigate the vacuum realignment for chiral symmetry breaking and color superconductivity at finite density in Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model in a variational method. The treatment allows us to investigate simultaneous formation of condensates in quark antiquark as well as in diquark channels. The methodology involves an explicit construction of a variational ground state and minimisation of the thermodynamic potential. Color and electric charge neutrality conditions are imposed through introduction of appropriate chemical potentials. Color and flavor dependent condensate functions are determined through minimisation of the thermodynamic potential. The equation of state is calculated. Simultaneous existence of a mass gap and superconducting gap is seen in a small window of quark chemical potential within the model when charge neutrality conditions are not imposed. Enforcing color and electric charge neutrality conditions gives rise to existence of gapless superconducting modes depending upon the magnitude of the gap and the difference of the chemical potentials of the condensing quarks.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that simultaneous chiral symmetry breaking and color superconductivity can coexist in a small window of quark chemical potential (about 23 MeV) within the NJL model when charge neutrality conditions are not imposed, but enforcing color and electric charge neutrality leads to gapless superconducting modes depending on the gap magnitude and the chemical potential difference of the condensing quarks.

## Method
The authors construct a variational ground state with both quark-antiquark and diquark condensates using successive Bogoliubov transformations in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. They minimize the thermodynamic potential with respect to condensate functions, mass gaps, and superconducting gaps, imposing color and electric charge neutrality through chemical potentials for baryon number, electric charge, and color charges. The gap equations and thermodynamic potential are solved self-consistently at zero temperature for two-flavor color superconductivity with three flavors (u, d, s).

## Key numbers
- The NJL model parameters used are: coupling \(g^2\Lambda^2 = 17.6\), cutoff \(\Lambda = 0.68\) GeV, and strange current quark mass \(m_s = 0.12\) GeV.
- At zero temperature and density, constituent quark masses are \(M_1 = M_2 = 0.35\) GeV for u and d quarks and \(M_3 = 0.575\) GeV for strange quarks.
- Without charge neutrality, simultaneous chiral symmetry breaking and color superconductivity exist in a window of about 23 MeV in quark chemical potential, with the superconducting gap reaching a maximum of 65 MeV.
- With charge neutrality imposed, u quarks remain massive for about 80 MeV in quark chemical potential after d quarks become massless.

## Context
This work extends the variational approach of Mishra and Mishra (2002) for chiral symmetry breaking and diquark condensation to include color and electric charge neutrality conditions relevant for neutron star interiors. It builds on studies of two-flavor color superconducting matter by Krisch (2000) and Reddy (2000), who found that such a phase would be absent in neutron star cores except for a small density window. The results enable further investigation of gapless superconducting phases and the equation of state for neutral quark matter in compact stars.
