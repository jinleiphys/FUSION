# Electric fields at the quark surface of strange stars in the color-flavor locked phase

> It is shown that extremely strong electric fields may be generated at the surface of strange quark matter in the color-flavor locked phase because of the surface effects. Some properties of strange stars made of this matter are briefly discussed.

## Key claim

Extremely strong electric fields (supercritical with respect to the pair-creation threshold) may be generated at the surface of strange quark matter in the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase, despite the bulk phase being electrically neutral with no electrons, because surface effects deplete massive strange quarks near the boundary.

## Method

The author calculates the surface charge density arising from the flavor-dependent modification of quark state densities near the boundary, using Madsen's (2001) result for the reduction in number of massive quarks per unit area. A Thomas-Fermi model is then applied to solve Poisson's equation for the electrostatic potential and electron distribution near the surface, with boundary conditions set by the surface charge.

## Key numbers

- Surface electric field at the CFL-phase quark surface: \(E_0 \simeq 2.7 \times 10^{18}\ \mathrm{V\ cm^{-1}} \simeq 200\,E_{\mathrm{cr}}\), where \(E_{\mathrm{cr}} \simeq 1.3 \times 10^{16}\ \mathrm{V\ cm^{-1}}\) is the critical field for vacuum pair creation; this is about 5 times larger than the field calculated for unpaired strange quark matter.
- Electrostatic potential at the CFL surface at zero temperature: \(V(0,0) \simeq 3.6 \times 10^7\ \mathrm{V}\) (i.e., \(eV \simeq 36\ \mathrm{MeV}\)), for \(m_s \simeq 150\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and \(p_{F,s} \simeq 300\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Characteristic temperature above which thermal effects become important: \(T_* \simeq 11.6\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Typical thickness of the surface electron layer: \(z_0 \simeq 2 \times 10^2\ \mathrm{fm}\).
- Maximum crust density limited by neutron drip: \(\sim 4.3 \times 10^{11}\ \mathrm{g\ cm^{-3}}\); maximum crust mass: \(\sim 10^{-5} M_\odot\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of strange star surfaces from the unpaired quark matter case (Alcock et al., 1986; Kettner et al., 1995; Heiselberg and Xu, 2002) to the color-flavor locked superconducting phase, which had been thought to eliminate the surface electric field because of bulk charge neutrality (Rajagopal and Wilczek, 2001). The result shows that CFL strange stars can still possess supercritical electric fields, enabling pair emission and normal-matter crust formation similar to unpaired strange stars, but with a higher field strength. The paper enables future work on the observational discrimination between neutron stars and strange stars with CFL surfaces, and on the detailed pair-production luminosity from such stars.
