# Hybrid Stars in a Strong Magnetic Field

> We study the effects of high magnetic fields on the particle population and equation of state of hybrid stars using an extended hadronic and quark SU(3) non-linear realization of the sigma model. In this model the degrees of freedom change naturally from hadrons to quarks as the density and/or temperature increases. The effects of high magnetic fields and anomalous magnetic moment are visible in the macroscopic properties of the star, such as mass, adiabatic index, moment of inertia, and cooling curves. Moreover, at the same time that the magnetic fields become high enough to modify those properties, they make the star anisotropic.

## Key claim

The study shows that high magnetic fields delay the deconfinement phase transition in hybrid stars and stiffen the hadronic equation of state, but the quantitative effect on star masses and cooling depends strongly on how the anisotropic magnetic pressure is approximated; only the lowest central magnetic field configuration of \(1.4\times 10^{18}\) G gives reliable results because its pressure anisotropy is only about 20%.

## Method

The authors used an extended SU(3) non-linear realization of the sigma model that includes both hadronic and quark degrees of freedom, with the deconfinement order parameter \(\Phi\) modeled to reproduce lattice QCD behavior. They introduced a density-dependent effective magnetic field \(B^*\) that increases smoothly with baryon chemical potential from a surface value of \(10^{15}\) G to a chosen central value \(B_c\), and included Landau level quantization and anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) for hadrons. The equation of state was computed under global charge neutrality, and macroscopic properties (mass-radius, adiabatic index, moment of inertia, and thermal evolution) were obtained by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations and the thermal balance equations, using two different isotropic approximations for the anisotropic magnetic pressure contribution.

## Key numbers

- Surface magnetic field set to \(B_{\text{surf}} = 10^{15}\) G, with central fields \(B_c\) ranging from \(1.4\times 10^{18}\) G to \(1.4\times 10^{19}\) G.
- The maximum central magnetic field considered, \(1.4\times 10^{19}\) G, is above the hydrostatic stability limit from Bocquet (1995) and Cardall (2001) of about \(4.2\times 10^{18}\) G.
- For \(B_c = 1.4\times 10^{19}\) G with AMM, the moment of inertia increases by about 10% from the unmagnetized case for the maximum mass star; for \(B_c = 7.2\times 10^{18}\) G the increase is about 2%.
- The pressure anisotropy \((p_z - p_{x,y})/(p_z + p_{x,y})\) for \(B_c = 1.4\times 10^{18}\) G is about 20%.
- Cooling calculations were performed for hybrid star masses of 1.1, 1.4, and 1.93 \(M_\odot\).
- The AMM coupling constants used include \(\kappa_p = 1.79\), \(\kappa_n = -1.91\), \(\kappa_\Lambda = -0.61\), \(\kappa_{\Sigma^+} = 1.67\), \(\kappa_{\Sigma^0} = 1.61\), \(\kappa_{\Sigma^-} = -0.38\), \(\kappa_{\Xi^0} = -1.25\), \(\kappa_{\Xi^-} = 0.06\).

## Context

This work extends the chiral SU(3) sigma model approach of Dexheimer (2009) and Schramm (2011) to include strong magnetic fields in hybrid stars, complementing earlier studies of magnetized hadronic stars by Bocquet (1995) and Cardall (2001) that used simpler equations of state. The results show that magnetic field effects on the equation of state are not strong enough to significantly alter cooling curves unless the macroscopic magnetic pressure is included, which then stiffens the equation of state and can suppress direct Urca cooling in higher-mass stars. The authors note that a full two-dimensional treatment of the anisotropic structure, as in Negreiros (2012), is needed for reliable quantitative predictions, and that the finite-temperature phase diagram at high magnetic fields remains an open question connecting heavy-ion collision physics to neutron star interiors.
