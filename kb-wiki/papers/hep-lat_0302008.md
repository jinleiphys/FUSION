# Delta Baryon Magnetic Moments From Lattice QCD

> Theoretical predictions for the magnetic moments of the physical Delta baryons are extracted from lattice QCD calculations. We utilize finite-range regulated effective field theory that is constructed to have the correct Dirac moment mass dependence in the region where the up and down quark masses are heavy. Of particular interest is the chiral nonanalytic behaviour encountered as the nucleon-pion decay channel opens. We find a Delta^++ magnetic moment (at the Delta pole) of 4.99 \pm 0.56 μ_N. This result is within the Particle Data Group range of 3.7-7.5 μ_N and compares well with the experimental result of Bosshard et al. of 4.52 \pm 0.51 \pm 0.45 μ_N. The interplay between the different pion-loop contributions to the Delta^+ magnetic moment leads to the surprising result that the proton moment may exceed that of the Delta^+, contrary to conventional expectations.

## Key claim
The paper extracts predictions for the magnetic moments of the physical Delta baryons from lattice QCD calculations, using finite-range regulated effective field theory that incorporates the correct Dirac moment mass dependence at heavy quark masses and the chiral nonanalytic behavior at the nucleon-pion decay threshold. The key hedge is that the lattice data used are from quenched QCD simulations over a decade old, with pion masses above 600 MeV, and the results depend on the regulator mass parameter (varied between 0.5 and 1.0 GeV) with a sensitivity of 3.6% or less for charged Delta baryons.

## Method
The authors calculate the pion-loop diagrams giving leading and next-to-leading nonanalytic behavior for Delta baryon magnetic moments using finite-range regularization with a dipole-vertex regulator (mass parameter Lambda = 0.8 GeV). They fit the extrapolation function, which combines an analytic term (a_0/(1 + c_0 m_pi^2)) with regulated loop contributions for decuplet-decuplet and decuplet-octet transitions, to quenched lattice QCD data at three pion masses (0.665, 0.820, and 0.964 GeV) from Leinweber (1992). The complex mass scheme is adopted to incorporate the finite lifetime of the Delta resonances, extracting moments at the pole position (delta_R - i delta_I = 270 - i 50 MeV).

## Key numbers
- Delta^{++} magnetic moment at the Delta pole: 4.99 +/- 0.56 mu_N (nuclear magnetons), compared to the Particle Data Group range of 3.7–7.5 mu_N and the experimental result of Bosshard et al. of 4.52 +/- 0.51 +/- 0.45 mu_N.
- Delta^{+} magnetic moment: 2.49 +/- 0.27 mu_N, compared to the initial experimental measurement of Kotulla et al. of 2.7^{+1.0}_{-1.3} (stat.) +/- 1.5 (syst.) +/- 3 (theor.) mu_N.
- Delta^{-} magnetic moment: -2.45 +/- 0.27 mu_N.
- Delta^{0} magnetic moment: 0.06 +/- 0.00 mu_N (with the caveat that quenched lattice data gives identically zero for this state).
- Regulator-mass dependence: 3.6% or less for charged Delta baryons when the dipole mass parameter Lambda is varied between 0.5 and 1.0 GeV.
- Lattice data points: at pion masses 0.964(12) GeV (Delta^{++} = 2.81(18) mu_N), 0.820(11) GeV (3.19(28) mu_N), and 0.665(13) GeV (3.67(43) mu_N).

## Context
This work extends the Padé approximant and finite-range regulated chiral perturbation theory methods previously applied to octet baryon magnetic moments by Leinweber (1998), Hackett-Jones (2000), and Cloet (2002) to the decuplet baryons. It incorporates next-to-leading nonanalytic terms that are vital for decuplet moments because they contain information about the branch point at the octet-decuplet mass splitting (m_pi = delta = 270 MeV) associated with the Delta to N pi decay channel. The prediction that the proton magnetic moment (2.66 +/- 0.17 mu_N) may exceed that of the Delta^+ (2.49 +/- 0.27 mu_N) contradicts conventional quark model expectations and previous linear extrapolations of the same lattice data by Leinweber (1992). The results enable future tests of QCD predictions when new unquenched lattice data at lower pion masses become available.
