# Using the $ϕ$-meson elliptic flow to map the strength of partonic interaction

> A compilation of recently measured STAR data for elliptic flow ($v_{2}$) of $ϕ$ mesons in RHIC Beam Energy Scan program and comparison with a multiphase transport model (AMPT) has been presented. The experimental data at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ $\geq$ 19.6 GeV agrees well with string melting version of the AMPT model. The model includes partonic interactions and quark coalescence as a mechanism of hadronization. This indicates that there is a substantial contribution to collectivity from partonic interactions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ $\geq$ 19.6 GeV. The measured $ϕ$-meson $v_{2}$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 7.7 and 11.5 GeV are found to be smaller than those obtained from AMPT model without partonic interactions. This indicates negligible contribution of partonic collectivity to the observed $ϕ$-meson $v_{2}$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ $\leq$ 11.5 GeV.

## Key claim

The paper uses the measured elliptic flow of phi mesons across RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies to argue that partonic collectivity contributes substantially to the final observed flow at collision energies of 19.6 GeV and above, but becomes negligible at 11.5 GeV and below. The claim is conditional on the agreement between data and the AMPT model with string melting, which includes partonic interactions and quark coalescence.

## Method

The author compiled STAR experimental data for phi-meson elliptic flow (v2) as a function of transverse momentum (pT) for Au+Au minimum-bias collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV. These data were compared to results from the AMPT model (version 1.11) in both its default version (without partonic interactions) and its string melting version (with partonic interactions and quark coalescence), using parton-parton cross sections of 3 mb and 10 mb. The pT-integrated elliptic flow was also computed by folding the v2(pT) distributions with the phi-meson pT spectra fitted with a Levy function.

## Key numbers

- At sqrt(s_NN) = 62.4 GeV, experimental phi-meson v2 data agree well with the AMPT String Melting model using a 10 mb parton-parton cross section.
- For the energy range 19.6 <= sqrt(s_NN) <= 39 GeV, measured phi v2 for pT < 1.5 GeV/c lies between the AMPT String Melting predictions for 3 mb and 10 mb cross sections; for pT > 1.5 GeV/c, a cross section of order 10 mb is required.
- At sqrt(s_NN) = 11.5 GeV, both AMPT-SM and AMPT Default models overpredict the measured phi-meson v2 data.
- The pT-integrated phi-meson v2 at sqrt(s_NN) = 62.4 and 200 GeV is well described by AMPT-SM with 10 mb cross section; at sqrt(s_NN) = 19.6, 27, and 39 GeV, a 3 mb cross section is sufficient.
- The AMPT model v2 values remain constant across all energies for a given parton-parton cross section, in contrast to the energy-dependent trend seen in data.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of phi-meson elliptic flow from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program, building on earlier STAR publications (v2_BES_prc, v2_BES_prl) and the prior observation of number-of-constituent-quark scaling in v2 at higher energies. The comparison with the AMPT model enables a qualitative mapping of the onset of partonic collectivity as a function of collision energy, and suggests that the specific shear viscosity (eta/s) of the system increases as beam energy decreases, consistent with theoretical expectations (eta_by_s). The results motivate further measurements in the proposed BES phase II program and at the FAIR experiment.
