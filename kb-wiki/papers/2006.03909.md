# Pairing, quasi-spin and seniority

> We present our concise notes for the lectures and tutorials on pairing, quasi-spin and seniority delivered at SERB school on Role of Symmetries in Nuclear Physics, AMITY University, $2019$. Starting with some generic features of residual nucleon-nucleon interactions, we provide detailed derivation of the matrix elements for the $δ$-interaction which is the basis for the standard pairing Hamiltonian. The eigen values for standard pairing Hamiltonian are then obtained within the quasi-spin formalism. The algebra involving quasi-spin operators is performed explicitly using the annihilation and creation operators for single nucleon together with the application of Wicks theorem. These techniques are expected to be helpful in deriving the mean-field equations for the $Hartree-Fock$, $Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer$ and $Hartree-Fock$ Bogoliubov theories.

## Key claim

This paper presents pedagogical lecture notes that provide a self-contained, step-by-step derivation of the matrix elements for the delta-interaction and the exact eigenvalues of the standard pairing Hamiltonian using quasi-spin formalism, with the explicit application of Wick's theorem. The authors emphasize that while the angular part of two-body matrix elements is "straightforward but lengthy," their derivation is not available in full detail at a single place elsewhere.

## Method

The authors first derive the direct and exchange terms of the two-body matrix elements for the delta-interaction between antisymmetrized coupled states of two nucleons, using multipole expansion and 3j- and 6j-symbol identities. They then introduce creation and annihilation operators for single nucleons and apply Wick's theorem to simplify products of these operators. Finally, they construct quasi-spin operators in terms of these creation and annihilation operators and use the quasi-spin algebra to obtain the eigenvalues of the standard pairing Hamiltonian.

## Context

This work extends the pedagogical tradition of nuclear structure textbooks such as Talmi (1963, 1993), Pal (1982), Heyde (1990), and Casten (2000) by providing a single, detailed reference for the derivation of delta-interaction matrix elements and quasi-spin algebra. The techniques presented are intended to be directly applicable to deriving mean-field equations for Hartree-Fock, Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer, and Hartree-Fock Bogoliubov theories. The paper enables students and researchers to follow the complete algebraic steps that are typically omitted or scattered across multiple sources.
