# Efficient calculation for the quasiparticle random-phase approximation matrix

> We present an efficient numerical technique to evaluate the matrix of the (quasiparticle)-random-phase approximation, using the finite amplitude method (FAM). The method is tested in calculation of monopole excitations in 120Sn, compared with result obtained with the former iterative FAM. The neutron-pair-transfer modes are calculated with the present method and their character change in neutron-rich Pb isotopes is discussed. Computational aspects of different FAM approaches are also discussed for future applications to a large-scale computation.

# Efficient calculation for the quasiparticle random-phase approximation matrix

## Key claim

The paper presents a "matrix FAM" (m-FAM) approach that explicitly constructs the (Q)RPA matrix by using the finite amplitude method to evaluate residual interactions numerically, avoiding complicated analytical derivations; however, this method is computationally advantageous only when the number of two-quasiparticle states is relatively small, as its computational cost scales as (N_2qp)^2 for matrix construction and (N_2qp)^3 for diagonalization, while the iterative FAM (i-FAM) scales more favorably for larger model spaces.

## Method

The authors extend the finite amplitude method (FAM) to explicitly construct the RPA and QRPA matrices by choosing unit vectors (X,Y) corresponding to individual two-quasiparticle configurations and computing the induced fields δh and δΔ via finite-difference calculations of the single-particle Hamiltonian and pair potential at slightly modified densities. For the QRPA case with pairing, the method uses modified quasiparticle wave functions to construct the FAM densities (ρ_η, κ_η^(±)) and evaluates the residual fields δH^(20)_μν without explicit calculation of the complicated residual interactions. The resulting A and B matrices are then diagonalized using standard Lapack routines to obtain normal-mode energies and eigenvectors.

## Key numbers

- For ¹²⁰Sn with quasiparticle energy cutoff E_qp^c = 60 MeV, the number of J^π = 0⁺ two-quasiparticle states is N_2qp = 1741
- The spurious Nambu-Goldstone mode in ¹²⁰Sn is calculated at ω_NG = 662 keV (should be zero in a fully self-consistent calculation)
- This single spurious state carries 99.9% of the total strength associated with the neutron number operator
- For ²⁰⁸Pb, the lowest pair-transfer mode around E = 2.6 MeV has B(add; ²⁰⁸Pb → ²¹⁰Pb) = 7.47 and B(rem; ²⁰⁸Pb → ²⁰⁶Pb) = 4.93
- The excited 0⁺ state in ²⁰⁶Pb is located at about 1.4 MeV higher than the ground state (experimental value: E_ex = 1.17 MeV from (p,t) reactions)
- For ²¹⁰Pb, the lowest pair-addition strength appears at E_ex = 3.0 MeV, decreasing to a minimum of E_ex = 2.3 MeV in ²¹⁸Pb
- With E_qp^c = 60 MeV, the m-FAM is six times faster than the i-FAM for ¹²⁰Sn
- The i-FAM calculation used precision ε = 10⁻⁵, FAM parameter η = 10⁻⁸, energy range 0 < E < 45 MeV with ΔE = 0.1 MeV and smoothing γ = 1 MeV
- The number of i-FAM iterations ranges from about 30 at low energy to about 160 near the peak strength (around 15-20 MeV)
- The m-FAM density construction is 1/5 of the original time compared to using the general formula

## Context

This work extends the finite amplitude method (FAM) originally proposed by Nakatsukasa, Inakura, and Yabana (2007) for strength function calculations, and the quasiparticle-basis FAM extension by Avogadro and Nakatsukasa (2011). The m-FAM approach complements the iterative FAM (i-FAM) used in prior calculations for spherical nuclei (Avogadro and Nakatsukasa 2011) and deformed systems (Stoitsov 2011), and provides explicit normal-mode eigenvectors that the i-FAM cannot deliver. The method enables studies of low-lying discrete states such as pair-transfer modes in neutron-rich Pb isotopes, and could be applied to massively parallel computations for deformed superfluid nuclei, where the number of two-quasiparticle states may reach order 10⁵ even with axial symmetry (Teran and Engel 2010, Yoshida and Nakatsukasa 2011).
