# Improving the convergence of the chiral expansion for nuclear forces I: Peripheral phases

> We study the two-pion exchange potential at next-to-next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory. We propose a new cut-off scheme for the pion loop integrals based on spectral function regularization. We show that this method allows for a consistent implementation of constraints from pion-nucleon scattering. It leads to a much improved description of the partial waves with angular momentum l \geq 2 as compared to the calculation utilizing dimensional regularization.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the poor convergence of the chiral expansion for nucleon-nucleon (NN) peripheral partial waves at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) is caused by high-momentum components of exchanged pions in the two-pion exchange (TPE) potential, and that using a finite momentum-space cutoff (spectral function regularization) instead of dimensional regularization removes these spurious short-distance contributions, allowing the use of large low-energy constants \(c_{1,3,4}\) consistent with pion-nucleon scattering.

## Method

The authors derived the two-pion exchange potential at NLO and NNLO in chiral effective field theory using spectral function regularization, where the spectral functions for the TPE are cut off at a scale \(\Lambda\) via a step function \(\theta(\Lambda - \mu)\) in the dispersion integrals. They obtained analytic expressions for the regularized loop functions \(L^\Lambda(q)\) and \(A^\Lambda(q)\) in momentum space and computed neutron-proton phase shifts for partial waves with angular momentum \(l \geq 2\) by solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation, comparing results to the Nijmegen phase shift analysis and to calculations using dimensional regularization.

## Key numbers

- The low-energy constants from a \(Q^3\) analysis of pion-nucleon scattering are in the ranges: \(c_1 = -0.81 \ldots -1.53\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(c_3 = -4.70 \ldots -6.19\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(c_4 = 3.25 \ldots 4.12\) GeV\(^{-1}\).
- The NNLO* potential used reduced values \(c_3 = -1.15\) GeV\(^{-1}\) and \(c_4 = 1.20\) GeV\(^{-1}\) to avoid spurious deeply bound states, but these are incompatible with pion-nucleon scattering.
- The cutoff scale \(\Lambda\) is taken of the order of the chiral symmetry breaking scale \(\Lambda_\chi \sim 1\) GeV, and the regularized spectral functions are cut off at \(\mu = \Lambda\).
- The coordinate-space TPE potential at intermediate distances \(r \sim 1/M_\pi \sim 1.4\) fm receives dominant contributions from spectral masses \(\mu \sim 0.6\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Kaiser et al. (1997), who found poor convergence of the chiral expansion for peripheral NN partial waves using dimensional regularization, and the authors' own NNLO* potential (Epelbaum et al., 2002), which used artificially small \(c_{3,4}\) values. The spectral function regularization method is analogous to approaches used in octet baryon mass calculations by Jenkins (1992) and in SU(3) baryon chiral perturbation theory by Donoghue and Holstein (1998, 1999) and Borasoy (2002). The results enable consistent inclusion of pion-nucleon scattering constraints in NN potentials at NNLO without spurious deeply bound states, and provide a framework for improving convergence in higher-order chiral calculations.
