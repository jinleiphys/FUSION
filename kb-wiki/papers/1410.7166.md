# Neutron stars with Hyperons in Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach

> Using the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach including the hyperon degrees of freedom, we investigate the properties of neutron-star matter. To handle the hyperons in matter, we first examine the importance of the space part of baryon self-energies at high densities, and secondly study the effect of negative-energy states of baryons, which can provide an unambiguous relationship between the in-medium reaction matrices for baryon-baryon scattering and the baryon self-energies. We solve the coupled, Bethe-Salpeter equations in the nuclear-matter rest frame by using the Bonn potentials. We assume that eight kinds of nonstrange and strange mesons ($σ,\,δ,\,ω,\,ρ,\,η,\,π,\,K,\,K^{\ast}$) take part in the interactions between two baryons. Then, we calculate the baryon self-energies, the energy density and pressure of matter. The present calculation provides a hard equation of state in neutron-star matter at high densities, which is generated by the effect of Pauli exclusion, the short-range correlations between two baryons, etc. We finally predict the maximum neutron-star mass of $2.02\,M_{\odot}$, which is consistent with both the recently observed masses, $1.97\pm0.04M_{\odot}$ (J1614-2230) and $2.01\pm0.04M_{\odot}$ (J0348+0432).

## Key claim

The Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) calculation including hyperon degrees of freedom, with full treatment of the space component of baryon self-energies and negative-energy states, predicts a maximum neutron-star mass of 2.02 solar masses, which is consistent with the observed masses of J1614-2230 (1.97 +/- 0.04 solar masses) and J0348+0432 (2.01 +/- 0.04 solar masses), but only under the condition that the resulting equation of state is hard at high densities due to Pauli exclusion and short-range correlations.

## Method

The authors solved coupled Bethe-Salpeter equations in the nuclear-matter rest frame using Bonn potentials (A, B, and C) and eight meson exchanges (sigma, delta, omega, rho, eta, pi, K, K*). They fully incorporated the space component of baryon self-energies and included negative-energy states of baryons in the Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes, using standard spinors rather than helicity spinors to avoid complications from momentum non-alignment. The baryon self-energies, energy density, and pressure were then calculated self-consistently to determine the equation of state and maximum neutron-star mass.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron-star mass: 2.02 solar masses (predicted by the DBHF calculation including hyperons)
- Observed mass of J1614-2230: 1.97 +/- 0.04 solar masses
- Observed mass of J0348+0432: 2.01 +/- 0.04 solar masses
- Saturation density for symmetric nuclear matter (Bonn A, DBHF(1)): 0.124 fm^-3 with binding energy -8.1 MeV per particle
- Saturation density for symmetric nuclear matter (Bonn A, DBHF(2)): 0.149 fm^-3 with binding energy -10.5 MeV per particle
- Incompressibility for Bonn A: 147 MeV (DBHF(1)) and 204 MeV (DBHF(2))
- Symmetry energy for Bonn A: 24.4 MeV (DBHF(1)) and 28.8 MeV (DBHF(2))
- Fermi momentum at normal nuclear density: 1.34 fm^-1 (empirical value used)

## Context

This work extends previous DBHF calculations by Sammarruca et al. (without partial-wave decomposition) and Poschenrieder, Huber, and collaborators (who partially included negative-energy states), but is the first DBHF calculation to include hyperon degrees of freedom for neutron-star matter. The approach contrasts with relativistic mean-field theory (RMFT), which requires extrapolation from normal density to high densities, whereas DBHF naturally incorporates short-range correlations and a class of three-body forces. The results enable future comparisons with observational constraints from massive neutron stars and provide a foundation for including additional strange baryons or exotic degrees of freedom in DBHF calculations.
