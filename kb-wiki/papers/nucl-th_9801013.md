# Electromagnetic transition form factors of negative parity nucleon resonances

> We have calculated the transition form factors for the electromagnetic excitation of the negative parity resonances of the nucleon using different models previously proposed and we discuss their results and limits by comparison with experimental data.

# Electromagnetic transition form factors of negative parity nucleon resonances

## Key claim

The hypercentral constituent quark models that include a hypercoulomb plus linear confinement term (models 1 and 2) give a reasonable description of the electromagnetic transition form factors for negative parity nucleon resonances at medium momentum transfer (1-2 (GeV/c)^2), but none of the non-relativistic constituent quark models studied can adequately explain the transition form factors at low momentum transfer, suggesting that missing effects such as polarization of the Dirac sea or explicit q-qbar pair degrees of freedom are needed.

## Method

The authors calculated the helicity amplitudes A_{1/2}(Q^2) and A_{3/2}(Q^2) for the electromagnetic excitation of negative parity nucleon resonances using five different hypercentral potential models within a non-relativistic constituent quark framework. The quark dynamics were described using hyperspherical coordinates with hypercentral potentials (depending only on the hyperradius x), and the transition matrix elements of the transverse electromagnetic interaction were computed numerically in the Breit frame, with the computer code tested against analytical results from the harmonic oscillator model and the hypercoulomb model.

## Key numbers

- Model 1 potential parameters: tau = 4.59 and beta = 1.61 fm^{-2} (hyperCoulomb plus linear confinement, fitted to the spectrum)
- Model 2 (analytical hypercoulomb model) parameters: tau = 6.39 and beta = 0.15 fm^{-2}, with hyperfine parameters A = 140.7 MeV, a = 1.53 fm^{-1}, and tensor parameter B = 14 MeV fm^3
- Model 3 (dipole-fit potential) parameters: tau = 1.6 and kappa = -0.875 fm
- Model 4 harmonic oscillator parameter: alpha = 0.229 GeV (reproduces proton charge radius)
- Model 5 harmonic oscillator parameter: alpha = 0.410 GeV (confinement radius ~0.5 fm, reproduces A^p_{3/2} at Q^2 = 0 for D_{13}(1520))
- Experimental data available up to Q^2 = 3 (GeV/c)^2 for some resonances
- The potentials 1 and 2 give confinement radius of order 0.5 fm
- Agreement between models 1 and 2 and data is found at Q^2 = 1-2 (GeV/c)^2

## Context

This work extends the hypercentral approach to quark dynamics previously developed by the same group (Pl, 1995) by applying it to the calculation of electromagnetic transition form factors for negative parity resonances, going beyond their earlier study of helicity amplitudes at the photon point (Aie, 1997). The results are compared with the harmonic oscillator models of CKO and KI (1990) and with the analytical hypercoulomb model of Sig (1995). The authors note that the transition form factors are more sensitive to quark wave functions than the spectrum alone, and that these observables are being accurately measured at TJNAF (CEBAF). The work highlights that reproducing the elastic proton form factor (as model 3 does) does not guarantee describing transition form factors, and that relativistic corrections (studied by CK, 1990 and Des, 1995) modify the low-Q^2 behavior but still cannot fully reproduce the data, suggesting the need for explicit q-qbar pair degrees of freedom.
