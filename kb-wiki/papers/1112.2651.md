# Universality of nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations: two-nucleon momentum distributions in few-body systems

> Using realistic wave functions, the proton-neutron and proton-proton momentum distributions in $^3He$ and $^4He$ are calculated as a function of the relative, $k_{rel}$, and center of mass, $K_{CM}$, momenta, and the angle between them. For large values of ${k}_{rel}\gtrsim 2\,\,fm^{-1}$ and small values of ${K}_{CM} \lesssim 1.0\,\,fm^{-1}$, both distributions are angle independent and decrease with increasing $K_{CM}$, with the $pn$ distribution factorizing into the deuteron momentum distribution times a rapidly decreasing function of $K_{CM}$, in agreement with the two-nucleon (2N) short range correlation (SRC) picture. When $K_{CM}$ and $k_{rel}$ are both large, the distributions exhibit a strong angle dependence, which is evidence of three-nucleon (3N) SRC. The predicted center-of-mass and angular dependence of 2N and 3N SRC should be observable in two-nucleon knock-out processes $A(e,e'pN)X$.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for relative momenta \(k_{rel} \gtrsim 2\,\text{fm}^{-1}\) and center-of-mass momenta \(K_{CM} \lesssim 1\,\text{fm}^{-1}\), the two-nucleon momentum distributions in \(^3\text{He}\) and \(^4\text{He}\) become independent of the angle between the relative and CM momenta, with the \(pn\) distribution factorizing into the deuteron momentum distribution times a CM-dependent function; this factorization breaks down when both \(k_{rel}\) and \(K_{CM}\) are large, signaling three-nucleon correlations.

## Method

The authors calculated the proton-neutron and proton-proton two-body momentum distributions \(n^{NN}(k_{rel}, K_{CM}, \Theta)\) in \(^3\text{He}\) and \(^4\text{He}\) using realistic wave functions from the Schrödinger equation with the AV18 and AV8' nucleon-nucleon interactions. The distributions were computed as functions of the relative momentum \(k_{rel}\), the center-of-mass momentum \(K_{CM}\), and the angle \(\Theta\) between them, using the two-body non-diagonal density matrix integrated over the remaining nucleons. The results were compared to the deuteron momentum distribution and to preliminary CLAS data on \(^3\text{He}(e,e'pp)n\).

## Key numbers

- For \(k_{rel} \gtrsim 2\,\text{fm}^{-1}\) and \(K_{CM} \lesssim 1.0\,\text{fm}^{-1}\), the \(pn\) and \(pp\) momentum distributions become angle-independent and decrease with increasing \(K_{CM}\).
- At \(K_{CM}=0\), the ratio \(R^{pn} = n^{pn}(k_{rel},0)/n_D(k_{rel})\) becomes constant for \(k_{rel} \gtrsim 1.5\,\text{fm}^{-1}\), showing the \(pn\) distribution follows the deuteron shape.
- The CM momentum distribution \(n_{CM}^{pN}(K_{CM})\) for \(^3\text{He}\) and \(^4\text{He}\) is shown for \(K_{CM} \lesssim 1.5\,\text{fm}^{-1}\), with \(^3\text{He}\) exhibiting a sharper peak at \(K_{CM}=0\) due to weaker binding.
- The ratio of correlated \(pp\) to \(pn\) pairs from \(^3\text{He}(e,e'pp)n\) is presented for \(1.5 < k_{rel} < 3.0\,\text{fm}^{-1}\), integrated over the angle \(\Theta\).
- For \(K_{CM}=3\,\text{fm}^{-1}\), a strong angle dependence appears, indicating three-nucleon correlations.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of short-range correlations by Schiavilla et al. (2006) and Alvioli et al. (2007), which focused on relative momentum distributions integrated over CM variables, and by Wiringa et al. (2008), which examined the CM dependence only for parallel configurations. The present calculations provide the full angular dependence of two-nucleon momentum distributions in few-body systems, confirming the factorization picture for 2N SRC and identifying the onset of 3N SRC. These results enable quantitative predictions for two-nucleon knockout experiments \(A(e,e'pN)X\) and have implications for high-energy hadron-nucleus scattering, deep inelastic scattering, and the equation of state of nuclear and neutron matter.
