# Isovector response and energy-weighted sums in hot nuclei

> We investigate the collective response function and the energy-weighted sums (EWS) $m_k$ for isovector mode in hot nuclei. The approach is based on the collisional kinetic theory and takes into consideration the temperature and the relaxation effects. We have evaluated the temperature dependence of the adiabatic, $E_1=\sqrt{m_1/m_{-1}}$, and scaling, $E_3=\sqrt{m_3/m_1}$, energy centroids of the isovector giant dipole resonances (IVGDR). The centroid energy $E_3$ is significantly influenced by the Fermi surface distortion effects and, in contrast to the isoscalar mode, shows much weaker variation with temperature. Taking into account a connection between the isovector sound mode and the corresponding surface vibrations we have established the $A$-dependence of the IVGDR centroid energy which is in a good agreement with experimental data. We have shown that the enhancement factor for the "model independent" sum $m_1$ is only slightly sensitive to the temperature change.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the temperature dependence of the isovector giant dipole resonance (IVGDR) centroid energy \(E_3 = \sqrt{m_3/m_1}\) is significantly influenced by Fermi surface distortion effects and shows much weaker variation with temperature than the isoscalar mode, while the enhancement factor for the "model independent" sum \(m_1\) is only slightly sensitive to temperature change.

## Method

The authors used collisional kinetic theory based on the linearized Landau-Vlasov equation with a Uehling-Uhlenbeck collision integral in the relaxation time approximation, incorporating temperature and relaxation effects. They evaluated the density-density response function for isovector modes in a two-component nuclear Fermi liquid, including velocity-dependent (nonlocal) interactions via Landau parameters \(F_0'\) and \(F_1'\), and applied a macroscopic boundary condition at the nuclear surface to derive the secular equation for the wave number. The energy-weighted sums \(m_{-1}\), \(m_1\), and \(m_3\) were computed from the strength function to obtain the adiabatic and scaling centroid energies of the IVGDR.

## Key numbers

- The isospin symmetry energy \(C_{\mathrm{sym}} = b_{\mathrm{sym,vol}} = \frac{2}{3}\epsilon_F (1 + F_0') \approx 60\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (Eq. c1).
- The Landau parameter \(F_0' = 1.41\) was used to keep \(C_{\mathrm{sym}}\) at about 60 MeV.
- The effective nucleon mass \(m^* = 0.9m\) corresponds to the Landau parameter \(F_1 = -0.3\).
- The interaction parameter \(F_1' = 1.1\) was adopted, yielding an enhancement factor of about 10% for light nuclei and 20% for heavy nuclei.
- The relaxation parameter \(\alpha_2 = 5.4\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and \(\eta = -0.1\) were used for in-medium reduced NN cross sections.
- The vacuum estimate of \(\alpha_{2,\mathrm{vac}} = 2.2 \div 2.3\ \mathrm{MeV}\) was given for comparison.
- The nuclear radius parameter \(r_0 = 1.2\ \mathrm{fm}\) was adopted.

## Context

This work extends previous semiclassical kinetic theory studies of isovector modes in hot nuclei, such as those by Diko et al. (1999) and Lacasse et al. (1999), which neglected the velocity-dependent part of Landau forces and used the simpler Steinwedel-Jensen boundary condition. The present approach incorporates the nonlocal interaction parameter \(F_1'\) and a modified boundary condition with an isovector surface tension coefficient, enabling a correct description of the \(A\)-dependence of the IVGDR energy for light nuclei. The results enable future combination with the thermal fluctuation model (TFM) of Orbo et al. (1996) to provide a satisfactory description of both temperature and mass number dependencies of the IVGDR width.
