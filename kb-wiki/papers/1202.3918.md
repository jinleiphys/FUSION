# Competition of different coupling schemes in atomic nuclei

> Shell model calculations reveal that the ground and low-lying yrast states of the $N=Z$ nuclei $^{92}_{46}$Pd and $^{96}$Cd are mainly built upon isoscalar spin-aligned neutron-proton pairs each carrying the maximum angular momentum J=9 allowed by the shell $0g_{9/2}$ which is dominant in this nuclear region. This mode of excitation is unique in nuclei and indicates that the spin-aligned pair has to be considered as an essential building block in nuclear structure calculations. In this contribution we will discuss this neutron-proton pair coupling scheme in detail. In particular, we will explore the competition between the normal monopole pair coupling and the spin-aligned coupling schemes. Such a coupling may be useful in elucidating the structure properties of $N=Z$ and neighboring nuclei.

## Key claim

Shell model calculations reveal that the ground and low-lying yrast states of the N=Z nuclei 92Pd and 96Cd are mainly built upon isoscalar spin-aligned neutron-proton pairs each carrying the maximum angular momentum J=9 allowed by the 0g9/2 shell, but the authors note that the microscopic origin of the equidistant pattern generated by these aligned np pairs remains unclear at present.

## Method

The authors performed nuclear shell model calculations within the 1p3/2 0f5/2 1p1/2 0g9/2 (fpg), 0g9/2 1p1/2 (pg), and 0g9/2 model spaces using the Hamiltonian from Hon09 and other interactions. They evaluated the pair content in many-fermion wave functions by projecting shell-model wave functions onto a pair-coupled basis using two-particle coefficients of fractional parentage, and calculated the average number of isoscalar and isovector pairs as a function of total angular momentum.

## Key numbers

- The ground state wave function of 92Pd in the 0g9/2 shell is dominated by the stretch configuration (((νπ)9⊗(νπ)9)I'=16⊗(νπ)9)I''=9⊗(νπ)9)I with amplitude X(0+1)=0.81, occupying around 66% of the wave function.
- For 92Pd with four np pairs, the total number of interacting pairs is N=28, with 10 isoscalar pairs and 18 isovector pairs for T=0 states.
- In 94Ag, the lowest T=0 state is a 7+ state at excitation energy E*~0.7 MeV, dominated by the aligned np pair coupling with |X|≈0.97 in restricted 0g9/2 shell calculations.
- The 2+1 state in 92Pd is dominated by the configuration 96Cd(gs)⊗96Cd(2+1) with x=0.973, and its projection on the stretch configuration 96Cd(16+)⊗96Cd(16+) is x=0.862.
- For the fpg model space with A=92, single-hole energies relative to 0g9/2 are: ε(0g9/2)=0 MeV, ε(1p1/2)=0.144 MeV, ε(1p3/2)=1.443 MeV, and ε(0f5/2)=4.407 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the findings of Cederkäll et al. (2010) and Qi et al. (2011) who first observed the transition from isovector pair coupling to spin-aligned np coupling in 92Pd. The paper contrasts this new coupling scheme with the seniority pairing coupling that dominates semi-magic nuclei (Bohr 1976, Bohr 1980, Rowe 1998) and with the rotational-like behavior seen in deformed N=Z nuclei such as 76Sr and 80Zr. The results enable future exploration of how deformation influences the aligned np pair coupling and generalization to systems with more than one shell, potentially through four-body quartets as proposed by Danos (1969).
