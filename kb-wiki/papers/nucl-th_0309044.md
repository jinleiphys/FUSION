# Physical Baryon Resonance Spectroscopy from Lattice QCD

> We complement recent advances in the calculation of the masses of excited baryons in quenched lattice QCD with finite-range regulated chiral effective field theory enabling contact with the physical quark mass region. We examine the P-wave contributions to the low-lying nucleon and delta resonances.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first quantitative analysis of the baryon resonance spectrum from lattice QCD that incorporates the chiral non-analytic behavior of QCD, enabling extrapolation of quenched lattice data to physical quark masses. The authors note that a small downward shift in the lattice results upon unquenching would provide remarkable agreement with experiment, and that incorporating chiral non-analytic behavior inverts the ordering of the N(1535) and N(1520) resonances compared to naive linear extrapolation.

## Method

The authors combine quenched lattice QCD data from the CSSM Lattice collaboration for ground-state and low-lying excited baryon masses with finite-range regulated chiral effective field theory. They incorporate P-wave pion-baryon self-energy contributions into the chiral expansion of baryon masses, using the most important baryon states as determined by the strength of meson-baryon coupling constants. The resulting functional form is then used to extrapolate lattice data from the simulated quark mass region (where m_pi^2 > 0.3 GeV^2) down to the physical pion mass.

## Key numbers

- Lattice data exist for pion masses in the range m_pi^2 > 0.3 GeV^2, corresponding to quark masses where established fitting techniques have successfully extracted resonance masses
- The chiral effective field theory expansion is considered applicable for m_pi < 400-500 MeV, where Goldstone boson loops lead to rapid non-analytic variation with quark mass
- The analysis examines P-wave contributions to the low-lying nucleon and delta resonances, including the N(1535), N(1520), and delta(1700) states

## Context

This work extends the well-understood chiral extrapolation techniques previously applied to octet and decuplet baryons and the rho-meson by Leinweber (1999) and Cloet (2003) to the baryon excited states accessible on the lattice. It builds upon recent advances in spin and parity projection methods by Allton (2002) and Zanotti (2002) that enabled calculation of excited baryon masses in quenched lattice QCD. The results enable future work incorporating S-wave self-energy contributions and the formulation of quenched chiral perturbation theory to quantitatively estimate predictions of full QCD.
