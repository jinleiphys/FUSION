# Chirality distributions inside baryons in ${\rm QCD_2}$

> The connection between the spin distribution and the topological structure of the baryon is an open and important problem. Here we address it using QCD in $(1+1)$ spacetime dimensions, which is exactly solvable at large number of colors $N$. It is found that the distribution of chirality inside a baryon is drastically different from a chirality distribution inside states with zero baryon number, ``mesons". This difference is shown to arise from the topological structure of the baryon -- at large $N$, all of the baryon's chirality is concentrated near $x=0$, whereas in a meson state it vanishes in the small $x$ limit. Our results illustrate how the constituent features of the baryon reemerge and are tied to the topological features of the bosonized solitonic solution. Possible implications for QCD in $(3+1)$ dimensions and for deep inelastic scattering experiments are discussed.

## Key claim

The distribution of chirality inside a baryon in QCD₂ at large N is drastically different from that inside a meson, with all of the baryon's chirality concentrated near x=0 (small Bjorken x) due to the topological structure of the baryon, whereas in a meson state the chirality distribution vanishes in the small x limit; this result is derived in the 't Hooft limit (N → ∞, g²N fixed) where QCD₂ reduces to an exactly solvable sine-Gordon model.

## Method

The authors used the bosonized representation of QCD₂ in the 't Hooft limit, which reduces to an interacting sine-Gordon model where baryons emerge as topological soliton (kink) solutions. They quantized these solitons as coherent states of "solitonic constituents" following the method of Dvali et al. (2015), then computed the matrix element of the axial current (which in 1+1 dimensions is related to chirality) on the light cone in the infinite momentum frame, expressing the result in terms of Bjorken x. The topological and nontopological contributions were separated by decomposing the soliton wave function into a convolution of a sign function (topology) and a sech function (energy).

## Key numbers

- The classical kink mass is M_kink = 4√(N/π) m', where m' is a low-energy parameter related to the original QCD₂ coupling and quark mass.
- The one-loop correction to the kink mass is M_kink^(1-loop) = (1 - 1/(2N)) M_kink.
- The matrix element of the axial current in a baryon state as a function of Bjorken x_B is ⟨kink|j_5+(x_B)|kink⟩ = N / cosh(N x_B), showing enhancement at x_B ∼ 1/N.
- For the nontopological "mesonic" soliton, the axial current matrix element is ⟨nontop.|j_5+(x_B)|nontop.⟩ = (2N² x_B i) / (π cosh(N x_B)), which vanishes as x_B → 0.
- The constituent occupation number N_k+ = |α_k+|² = (π N)/(√2 x_B p_+) · 1/cosh(N x_B)² has a logarithmic divergence at small x_B due to the kink's topological structure.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Dvali et al. (2015) on quantum solitons in scalar φ⁴ theory to the sine-Gordon model, which is the bosonic dual of QCD₂ in the 't Hooft limit (t'Hooft 1974, Witten 1983). It addresses the long-standing "proton spin puzzle" by providing an exactly solvable model where the connection between baryon topology and chirality distribution can be studied from first principles, in contrast to the Skyrme model (Skyrme 1962, Witten 1979) which is only an effective low-energy description. The results enable future tests of the predicted difference between baryon and meson spin distributions in diffractive deep inelastic scattering experiments and lattice QCD calculations.
