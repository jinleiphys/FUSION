# Towards the Minimal Spectrum of Excited Baryons

> In the light baryon sector resonances can be broad and overlapping and are in most cases not directly visible in the cross section data. Automatized model selection techniques that introduce penalties for resonances can be used to determine the minimally needed set of resonances to describe the data. Several possible penalization schemes are compared. As an application we perform a blindfold identification of hyperon resonances in the $\bar{K} N \to K Ξ$ reaction based on the Least Absolute Shrinkage and Selection Operator (LASSO) in combination with the Bayesian Information Criterion (BIC). We find ten resonances --- out of the 21 above-threshold hyperon resonances with spin $J\le 7/2$ listed by the Particle Data Group. In traditional analyses, it is practically impossible to test the relevance of all resonances and their combinations that may potentially contribute to the reaction. By contrast, the present method proves capable of determining the relevant resonances among a large pool of candidates.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that automated model selection using LASSO regularization combined with the Bayesian Information Criterion can identify the minimal set of hyperon resonances needed to describe the reaction data, finding only ten out of 21 above-threshold hyperon resonances listed by the Particle Data Group are required, but this result is conditional on the specific reaction channel and penalization scheme used.

## Method

The authors applied LASSO regularization with a penalty on resonance coupling strengths to partial-wave amplitudes for the reaction K^- p → K Ξ, using both forward and backward selection schemes. They compared several penalization approaches including direct coupling penalization, automatic shutoff of small couplings, and second-derivative penalization of the partial-wave amplitudes, first testing these methods on synthetic data with known resonance content before applying to real experimental data. The optimal model was selected using the Bayesian Information Criterion (BIC), with effective degrees of freedom determined through bootstrap aggregation.

## Key numbers

- Ten resonances were identified as relevant out of 21 above-threshold hyperon resonances with spin J ≤ 7/2 listed by the Particle Data Group
- The synthetic data analysis used a scale parameter Λ = 10^3 MeV for the partial-wave parametrization
- For the backward automatic shutoff LASSO on synthetic data, the optimal λ value was 6, at which the χ² passed Pearson's χ² test at 90% confidence level
- For the second-derivative penalty on synthetic data, the optimal λ was approximately 50, with Γ_max tested in the range [250, 400] MeV
- The synthetic data covered energies up to W_max = 3200 MeV, with the real data extending to W ~ 3.0 GeV

## Context

This work extends the LASSO-based model selection approach previously applied to multipole analysis in neutral pion photoproduction (Landay 2016) to the determination of the baryon resonance spectrum itself. It contrasts with traditional "by hand" resonance identification methods such as those used by Jackson (2015) for the same K^- p → K Ξ reaction, and with mass scan techniques (Arndt 2003, Anisovich 2015) and F-test approaches that cannot systematically test all resonance combinations. The method is expected to be applicable to meson spectroscopy and lattice QCD analyses where limited data points make parameter selection critical.
