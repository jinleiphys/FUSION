# Dilepton production at SIS energies

> In this short review the results of detailed studies for dilepton production from $p+A$ and $A+A$ reactions at SIS energies are presented. The calculations are based on a semi-classical BUU transport model that includes the off-shell propagation of vector mesons and evaluates the width of the vector mesons dynamically. Different scenarios of in-medium modifications of vector mesons, such as collisional broadening and dropping vector meson masses, are investigated and the possibilities for an experimental observation of in-medium effects in $p+A$ reactions at 1--4 GeV are discussed for a variety of nuclear targets.

## Key claim

The paper reviews how dilepton production in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions at SIS energies (1–4 GeV) can reveal in-medium modifications of vector mesons, but notes that no consistent explanation for the DLS data has been achieved so far, as even with in-medium effects the yield around M ≈ 0.3 GeV remains underestimated while the rho-meson peak region is overestimated.

## Method

The calculations use a semi-classical BUU (Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck) transport model that includes off-shell propagation of vector mesons, with the width of vector mesons evaluated dynamically. Different in-medium modification scenarios are implemented: collisional broadening (adding a collisional width to the vacuum width) and dropping vector meson masses according to Hatsuda-Lee or Brown/Rho scaling (M* = M0 (1 − α ρ/ρ0) with α ≈ 0.18). The model incorporates resonance production from baryon-baryon and meson-baryon collisions, with high-energy collisions described by the LUND string fragmentation model FRITIOF.

## Key numbers

- The dropping mass scenario uses α ≈ 0.18 for the ρ and ω mesons, with normal nuclear density ρ0 = 0.16 fm⁻³
- For p + Pb collisions at 3–4 GeV, the collisional broadening + dropping mass scenario reduces the ω-peak by up to a factor of 2
- The dominant contribution at low invariant mass M > mπ⁰ comes from η Dalitz decay, while for M > 0.4 GeV dileptons stem from direct vector meson decays (ρ and ω)
- The dropping mass scenario produces a pronounced peak around M ≈ 0.65 GeV attributed to in-medium ω decay, with a strong reduction of dilepton yield in the vector meson pole mass region around 0.77 GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier transport studies of dilepton production at SPS energies (CERES, HELIOS collaborations) and at Bevalac energies (DLS collaboration) to the SIS energy range. It builds on the off-shell propagation formalism of Cassing and collaborators (2000) and the resonance model of Manley (1992). The predictions are intended for upcoming experimental data from the HADES Collaboration at GSI, and the paper notes that comparing dilepton spectra from C and Pb targets should allow experimental distinction of an in-medium mass shift of the ω meson.
