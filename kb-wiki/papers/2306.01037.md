# Proton charge radius extraction from muon scattering at MUSE using dispersively improved chiral effective field theory

> The MUSE experiment at Paul Scherrer Institute will perform the first measurement of low-energy muon-proton elastic scattering (muon lab momenta 115-210 MeV) with the aim of determining the proton charge radius. We study the prospects for the proton radius extraction using the theoretical framework of Dispersively Improved Chiral Effective Field Theory (DI$χ$EFT). It connects the proton radii with the finite-$Q^2$ behavior of the form factors through complex analyticity and enables the use of data up to $Q^2 \sim$ 0.1 GeV$^2$ for radius extraction. We quantify the sensitivity of the $μp$ cross section to the proton charge radius, the theoretical uncertainty of the cross section predictions, and the size of two-photon exchange corrections. We find that the optimal kinematics for radius extraction at MUSE is at momenta 210 MeV and $Q^2 \sim$ 0.05-0.08 GeV$^2$. We compare the performance of electron and muon scattering in the same kinematics. As a byproduct, we obtain explicit predictions for the $μp$ and $ep$ cross sections at MUSE as functions of the assumed value of the proton radius.

# Proton charge radius extraction from muon scattering at MUSE using dispersively improved chiral effective field theory

## Key claim
The MUSE experiment can extract the proton charge radius from low-energy muon-proton elastic scattering using Dispersively Improved Chiral Effective Field Theory (DIχEFT), but the extraction is characterized by a trade-off between cross-section sensitivity to the radius, theoretical uncertainty in form factor predictions, and the size of two-photon exchange corrections. The optimal kinematics for radius extraction is at the highest beam momentum of 210 MeV and momentum transfers Q² ~ 0.05–0.08 GeV², where the required experimental precision for discriminating a radius difference of 0.01 fm is estimated at approximately 0.8%.

## Method
The authors used Dispersively Improved Chiral Effective Field Theory (DIχEFT) to generate proton electric and magnetic form factors that depend explicitly on the assumed proton charge radius as a parameter, incorporating dispersion relations and chiral EFT input for the spectral functions. They computed the muon-proton elastic scattering cross section for muon lab momenta of 115–210 MeV as a function of Q², including two-photon exchange corrections from Tomalak (2015), and quantified the sensitivity of the cross section to the proton radius, the theoretical uncertainty from undetermined parameters in the spectral function, and the size of TPE corrections. The analysis compared the performance of muon and electron scattering in the same kinematics and determined the optimal Q² range for radius extraction by evaluating the minimal cross section differences between predictions for different assumed radii.

## Key numbers
- Muon lab momenta studied: 115–210 MeV (the MUSE experiment range)
- Optimal kinematics for radius extraction: beam momentum 210 MeV and Q² ~ 0.05–0.08 GeV²
- At Q² ~ 0.05 GeV², a relative experimental accuracy of approximately 0.5% is needed to discriminate a radius difference Δr_E = 0.01 fm
- At the optimal kinematics (k = 210 MeV, Q² ~ 0.05–0.08 GeV²), the required experimental precision for Δr_E = 0.01 fm is estimated at approximately 0.8%
- The TPE correction for muon-proton scattering is ≤ 0.5% in the MUSE kinematic range
- The magnetic FF contribution to the cross section uncertainty is at most 0.04% at the highest Q², compared to ~1% variation from a 1% change in electric radius
- The nominal proton charge radius from previous DIχEFT analysis of ep scattering: r_E = 0.842(2) fm
- The ratio of magnetic to electric contributions to the cross section is ~0.1 at Q² = 0.04 GeV² and ~0.4 at Q² = 0.08 GeV²
- At k = 115 MeV and Q² = 0.02 GeV², the TPE correction in ep scattering corresponds to Δr_E ≈ 0.03 fm, compared to Δr_E ≈ 0.015 fm in μp scattering

## Context
This work extends the DIχEFT method previously applied to electron-proton scattering data by Alarcon (2018, 2020) to the upcoming MUSE experiment at Paul Scherrer Institute, which will perform the first measurement of low-energy muon-proton elastic scattering. The proton radius puzzle, originating from the discrepancy between the muonic hydrogen result of Pohl (2010) (r_E = 0.84184(67) fm) and the earlier CODATA value (r_E = 0.8768(69) fm), motivates this study. The DIχEFT approach offers advantages over empirical polynomial fits and traditional dispersion analyses by Hohler (1976), Belushkin (2006), Lorenz (2012), and Hoferichter (2016), as it allows the isovector spectral function strength in the rho resonance region to vary with the proton radius in a theoretically controlled manner. The results enable the MUSE collaboration to optimize their experimental design and analysis strategy for extracting the proton charge radius, and provide explicit cross section predictions for the nominal radius value.
