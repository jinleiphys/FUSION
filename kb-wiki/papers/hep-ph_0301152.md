# Spin-5/2 resonance effects in a coupled channel nucleon resonance analysis

> Spin-$\ffh$ resonance contributions in pion-nucleon scattering are investigated within the coupled channel $K$-matrix approach for c.m. energies up to $\sqrt s=$ 2 GeV. All previously studied $πN$, $2πN$, $ηN$, $K Λ$, $K Σ$, and $ωN$ final states are included. We find a significant improvement of $χ^2$ in almost all channels by inclusion of the spin-$\ffh$ states. The obtained coupling parameters are discussed.

## Key claim

Including spin-5/2 resonances in the coupled channel K-matrix approach for pion-nucleon scattering up to 2 GeV significantly improves the chi-squared fit in almost all channels, but the improvement is conditional on the choice of coupling prescription (conventional vs. Pascalutsa) and the D35 partial wave remains problematic.

## Method

The authors extended the Giessen coupled-channel K-matrix model by adding spin-5/2 resonance states to the existing framework that included πN, 2πN, ηN, KΛ, KΣ, and ωN final states. They performed partial-wave decomposition of the T-matrix and used effective Lagrangians for the spin-5/2 resonance couplings, implementing both conventional Rarita-Schwinger propagators and Pascalutsa gauge-invariant couplings, with form factors and off-shell parameters varied during chi-squared minimization against experimental data up to √s = 2 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The total chi-squared for the conventional coupling fit including spin-5/2 resonances (C-p-π+ (5/2)) was 2.60, compared to 2.66 without them
- The Pascalutsa coupling fit including spin-5/2 resonances (P-p-π+ (5/2)) gave a total chi-squared of 3.65, compared to 3.53 without them
- The D15(1675) resonance mass was extracted as 1665 MeV (conventional) and 1662 MeV (Pascalutsa), with total widths of 144 MeV and 138 MeV respectively
- The F15(1680) resonance mass was 1674 MeV (conventional) and 1669 MeV (Pascalutsa), with total widths of 120 MeV and 122 MeV
- The F15(2000) resonance mass was 1981 MeV (conventional) and 1986 MeV (Pascalutsa), with total widths of 361 MeV and 488 MeV
- The F35(1905) resonance mass was 1859 MeV (conventional) and 1830 MeV (Pascalutsa), with total widths of 400 MeV and 457 MeV
- The D35(1930) resonance mass was found to be about 2050 MeV in both approaches
- The πN decay ratio for D15(1675) was 40.2% (conventional) and 41.2% (Pascalutsa); the 2πN decay ratio was 59.1% and 58.4% respectively

## Context

This work extends the Giessen coupled-channel model previously developed by the same group (Penner 2002, Feustel 1998, 1999) which had included only spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 resonances. The inclusion of spin-5/2 states addresses a known shortcoming of those earlier analyses. The results enable future simultaneous analysis of pion-induced and photon-induced reactions including higher-spin resonances, which is necessary because spin-5/2 resonances have large electromagnetic couplings as noted in the Particle Data Group listings. The study also confronts the "missing resonance" problem predicted by quark models (Capstick 2002) by testing whether weakly-coupled states appear when additional final states are included.
