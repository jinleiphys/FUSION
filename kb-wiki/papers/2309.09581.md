# The LHC as a Neutrino-Ion Collider

> Proton-proton collisions at the LHC generate a high-intensity collimated beam of neutrinos in the forward (beam) direction, characterised by energies of up to several TeV. The recent observation of LHC neutrinos by FASER$ν$ and SND@LHC signals that this hitherto ignored particle beam is now available for scientific inquiry. Here we quantify the impact that neutrino deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) measurements at the LHC would have on the parton distributions (PDFs) of protons and heavy nuclei. We generate projections for DIS structure functions for FASER$ν$ and SND@LHC at Run III, as well as for the FASER$ν$2, AdvSND, and FLArE experiments to be hosted at the proposed Forward Physics Facility (FPF) operating concurrently with the High-Luminosity LHC (HL-LHC). We determine that up to one million electron- and muon-neutrino DIS interactions within detector acceptance can be expected by the end of the HL-LHC, covering a kinematic region in $x$ and $Q^2$ overlapping with that of the Electron-Ion Collider. Including these DIS projections into global (n)PDF analyses, specifically PDF4LHC21, NNPDF4.0, and EPPS21, reveals a significant reduction of PDF uncertainties, in particular for strangeness and the up and down valence PDFs. We show that LHC neutrino data enables improved theoretical predictions for core processes at the HL-LHC, such as Higgs and weak gauge boson production. Our analysis demonstrates that exploiting the LHC neutrino beam effectively provides CERN with a "Neutrino-Ion Collider" without requiring modifications in its accelerator infrastructure.

## Key claim
The LHC's forward neutrino beam, recently observed by FASERnu and SND@LHC, can provide up to one million electron- and muon-neutrino deep-inelastic scattering events by the end of the HL-LHC era, and including these projected measurements in global PDF analyses significantly reduces PDF uncertainties for strangeness and valence quarks, effectively giving CERN a "Neutrino-Ion Collider" without accelerator modifications.

## Method
The authors generated projections for neutrino-nucleus DIS structure functions by convolving expected electron and muon neutrino fluxes from the LHC with detector acceptance and scattering rates for FASERnu, SND@LHC, FASERnu2, AdvSND, and FLArE experiments. They evaluated binned event yields in x, Q2, and neutrino energy using the EKO and YADISM theory pipeline interfaced with PineAPPL for fast interpolation grids, then quantified the impact of these pseudo-data on global PDF analyses using Hessian profiling with PDF4LHC21, NNPDF4.0, and EPPS21 as prior sets.

## Key numbers
- Up to one million electron- and muon-neutrino DIS interactions within detector acceptance expected by the end of the HL-LHC (3 ab^-1 integrated luminosity)
- FASERnu detector mass: 1.1 tonnes with fiducial volume of 20 cm x 25 cm x 80 cm
- SND@LHC fiducial volume: 830 kg detector with 35 cm length
- FASERnu2 proposed mass: 20 tonnes with tungsten volume of 40 cm x 40 cm x 6.6 m
- AdvSND target mass: 5 tonnes with fiducial length of 50 cm
- FLArE fiducial mass: 10 tonnes (FLArE10) and 100 tonnes (FLArE100) with 7 m length
- FASERnu and SND@LHC acquire data for Run III at 150 fb^-1; FASERnu2, AdvSND, and FLArE take data for HL-LHC at 3 ab^-1
- FLArE targets: electron energy resolution delta E_mu ~5%, electron angular resolution delta theta_e ~15 mrad, hadronic energy resolution delta E_h ~30%

## Context
This work extends the recent observations of LHC neutrinos by FASERnu and SND@LHC (2023) by quantifying the potential PDF impact of future measurements, complementing charged-lepton DIS at the Electron-Ion Collider. The analysis uses neutrino fluxes from Kling (2021) and builds on the FPF simulations of Feng (2022), showing that LHC neutrino data provides orthogonal constraints to HL-LHC measurements from AbdulKhalek (2018) and enables improved theoretical predictions for Higgs and weak gauge boson production at the HL-LHC.
