# Kaon Zero-Point Fluctuations in Neutron Star Matter

> We investigate the contribution of zero-point motion, arising from fluctuations in kaon modes, to the ground state properties of neutron star matter containing a Bose condensate of kaons. The zero-point energy is derived via the thermodynamic partition function, by integrating out fluctuations for an arbitrary value of the condensate field. It is shown that the vacuum counterterms of the chiral Lagrangian ensure the cancellation of divergences dependent on $μ$, the charge chemical potential, which may be regarded as an external vector potential. The total grand potential, consisting of the tree-level potential, the zero-point contribution, and the counterterm potential, is extremized to yield a locally charge neutral, beta-equilibrated and minimum energy ground state. In some regions of parameter space we encounter the well-known problem of a complex effective potential. Where the potential is real and solutions can be obtained, the contributions from fluctuations are found to be small in comparison with tree-level contributions.

## Key claim

The zero-point energy contribution from kaon fluctuations in neutron star matter containing a kaon condensate is derived via the thermodynamic partition function, but the authors find that in regions of parameter space where the effective potential remains real and solutions can be obtained, the contributions from fluctuations are small compared to tree-level contributions.

## Method

The authors start from the chiral Lagrangian of Kaplan and Nelson, retaining only kaonic pseudoscalar degrees of freedom and treating nucleons non-relativistically. They compute the kaon partition function by integrating out fluctuations around an arbitrary condensate field value to one-loop order, then extremize the total grand potential (tree-level potential plus zero-point contribution plus counterterm potential) to enforce local charge neutrality, beta-equilibrium, and minimum energy. The zero-point energy is regularized with an ultraviolet cutoff, and vacuum counterterms are used to cancel chemical-potential-dependent divergences.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant is taken as \( f = 93 \) MeV.
- The nuclear saturation density is \( n_0 = 0.16 \) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The compression modulus for equilibrium nuclear matter is chosen as \( K = 240 \) MeV.
- The bulk symmetry energy parameter is \( S_0 \simeq 30 \) MeV.
- The kaon-nucleon sigma term range considered is 167–520 MeV, corresponding to \( a_3 m_s \) values from \(-134\) to \(-310\) MeV.
- The constant \( \zeta \) in the finite counterterm is 0.443 with three-momentum cutoff regularization and 0.461 with dimensional regularization.
- The zero-point energy at threshold ( \( \theta = 0 \) ) is given analytically as \( V_{zp} + V_{ct} = -\frac{1}{64\pi^2} \left[ (m_K^2 - 3\xi^2)(m_K^2 - \xi^2) + 2\xi^4 \ln \frac{m_K^2}{\xi^2} \right] \), which vanishes in vacuum where \( \xi = m_K \).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of kaon condensation in neutron stars (Kaplan and Nelson, 1986; Brown, Lee, Rho, and Thorsson, 1992) by including one-loop zero-point fluctuations around the condensate, going beyond the threshold-density approximation. The formalism provides a consistent treatment that could be extended to include thermal contributions for studies of newborn neutron stars, and the methods developed could incorporate the full octet of pseudoscalar fluctuations. The results are relevant for determining the equation of state and maximum mass of neutron stars with kaon condensates, which has implications for the observed pulsar mass of 1.44 solar masses from PSR 1913+16.
