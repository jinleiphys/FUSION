# Nuclear Level Density of ${}^{161}$Dy in the Shell Model Monte Carlo Method

> We extend the shell-model Monte Carlo applications to the rare-earth region to include the odd-even nucleus ${}^{161}$Dy. The projection on an odd number of particles leads to a sign problem at low temperatures making it impractical to extract the ground-state energy in direct calculations. We use level counting data at low energies and neutron resonance data to extract the shell model ground-state energy to good precision. We then calculate the level density of ${}^{161}$Dy and find it in very good agreement with the level density extracted from experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors extend shell-model Monte Carlo (SMMC) calculations to the odd-even nucleus \({}^{161}\)Dy, but the projection on an odd number of particles introduces a sign problem that makes direct extraction of the ground-state energy impractical at low temperatures; they circumvent this by using level counting data and neutron resonance data to determine the ground-state energy, then calculate the level density and find it in very good agreement with experimental data.

## Method

The authors used a model space of proton orbitals (0g7/2, 1d5/2, 1d3/2, 2s1/2, 0h11/2, 1f7/2) and neutron orbitals (0h11/2, 0h9/2, 1f7/2, 1f5/2, 2p3/2, 2p1/2, 0i13/2, 1g9/2) with single-particle energies from a spherical Woods-Saxon plus spin-orbit potential. The effective interaction included monopole pairing and multipole-multipole terms with strengths determined from odd-even mass differences and self-consistency conditions. Because the sign problem limited SMMC calculations to temperatures above about 0.18 MeV, the ground-state energy was determined by a one-parameter fit of the SMMC thermal excitation energy to an experimental thermal excitation energy derived from a partition function that combined known low-lying levels with a back-shifted Bethe formula (BBF) parametrized using level counting data and neutron resonance data.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of \({}^{161}\)Dy: \(E_0 = -363.920 \pm 0.020\) MeV, determined from a one-parameter fit of SMMC thermal energy to experimental thermal energy.
- BBF parameters for \({}^{161}\)Dy: single-particle level density parameter \(a = 18.564\) MeV\(^{-1}\) and backshift \(\Delta = -0.615\) MeV, extracted from level counting data and s-wave neutron resonance data.
- Neutron resonance data point at excitation energy \(E_x = 6.454\) MeV (shown as a triangle in the level density figure).
- SMMC calculations for \({}^{161}\)Dy were feasible only up to inverse temperature \(\beta \sim 5.5\) MeV\(^{-1}\) (corresponding to \(T \approx 0.18\) MeV), compared to \(T = 0.05\) MeV for the even-even nucleus \({}^{162}\)Dy (Alhassid 2008).

## Context

This work extends SMMC applications in the rare-earth region from the even-even nucleus \({}^{162}\)Dy (Alhassid 2008) to the odd-even nucleus \({}^{161}\)Dy, addressing the sign problem that arises from projecting on an odd number of particles. The method of combining SMMC calculations with experimental level counting and neutron resonance data to determine the ground-state energy enables microscopic level density calculations for odd-even nuclei in this mass region, where direct SMMC calculations at low temperatures are impractical. The approach requires sufficient low-energy level counting data and neutron resonance data, and the results agree well with the experimentally extracted BBF level density.
