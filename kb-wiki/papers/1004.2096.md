# Pauli blocking and medium effects in nucleon knockout reactions

> We study medium modifications of the nucleon-nucleon (NN) cross sections and their influence on the nucleon knockout reactions. Using the eikonal approximation, we compare the results obtained with free NN cross sections with those obtained with a purely geometrical treatment of Pauli-blocking and with NN obtained with more elaborated Dirac-Bruecker methods. The medium effects are parametrized in terms of the baryon density. We focus on symmetric nuclear matter, although the geometrical Pauli-blocking allows for the treatment of asymmetric nuclear matter. It is shown that medium effects can change the nucleon knockout cross sections and momentum distributions up to 10% in the energy range E=50-300 MeV/nucleon. The effect is more evident in reactions involving halo nuclei.

# Pauli blocking and medium effects in nucleon knockout reactions

## Key claim
Medium modifications of nucleon-nucleon cross sections can change nucleon knockout cross sections and momentum distributions by up to 10% in the energy range E=50-300 MeV/nucleon, with the effect being more evident in reactions involving halo nuclei; however, it is not clear which of the several parametrizations of medium effects is more adequate for the precision required by experiments.

## Method
The authors used the eikonal approximation to calculate one-nucleon stripping and diffraction dissociation cross sections and momentum distributions, comparing results obtained with free nucleon-nucleon cross sections against those using a geometrical Pauli-blocking treatment, Dirac-Brueckner theory results, and a phenomenological parametrization. The medium effects were parametrized in terms of baryon density, with an effective local density obtained by taking the point along the impact parameter direction where projectile and target densities cross each other. Calculations were performed using a modified version of the code MOMDIS, with spectroscopic factors set to unity to isolate the medium effects.

## Key numbers
- Medium effects change nucleon knockout cross sections and momentum distributions up to **10%** in the energy range **E=50-300 MeV/nucleon**
- For the reaction $^9$Be($^{11}$Be,$^{10}$Be) at **40 MeV/nucleon**, total knockout cross sections range from **198 mb** (free) to **181 mb** (geometrical Pauli), **185 mb** (Brueckner), and **194 mb** (phenomenological)
- For the same reaction at **250 MeV/nucleon**, total cross sections range from **85.8 mb** (free) to **83.1 mb** (geometrical Pauli), **75.0 mb** (Brueckner), and **81.7 mb** (phenomenological)
- For $^9$Be($^{15}$C,$^{14}$C) at **40 MeV/nucleon**, total cross sections range from **125 mb** (free) to **116 mb** (geometrical Pauli), **118 mb** (Brueckner), and **123 mb** (phenomenological)
- For $^9$Be($^{34}$Ar,$^{33}$Ar(1/2$^+$)) at **40 MeV/nucleon**, total cross sections are nearly identical across all parametrizations at **13.6 mb**
- The saturation density used is **rho_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}**
- The halo neutron in $^{15}$C is bound by **1.218 MeV**; the neutron in $^{34}$Ar is bound by **17.06 MeV**

## Context
This work extends the theory of one-nucleon knockout reactions, previously developed by Hansen (2003), Tostevin (1999), and Gade (2008), to systematically include medium modifications of nucleon-nucleon cross sections. The authors compare their results with the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach of Li and Machleidt (1993, 1994) and the phenomenological parametrization of Xiangzhou (1998). The work enables more precise spectroscopic factor extraction from knockout reactions involving halo nuclei, where medium effects are most significant, and highlights the need for experimental discrimination between different in-medium cross section models.
