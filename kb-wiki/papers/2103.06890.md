# Signatures of Moat Regimes in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Heavy-ion collisions at small beam energies have the potential to reveal the rich phase structure of QCD at nonzero temperature and density. Among the possible phases are regimes which feature periodic modulations of the spatial structure, where the energy spectrum is shaped like a moat, with the minimum of the energy over a sphere at nonzero momentum. We argue that if the matter created in heavy-ion collisions traverses such a regime, it can produce a characteristic momentum dependence of particle number and their correlations. As an explicit example, we consider a quantum pion liquid phase to compute these quantities on the freeze-out surface. These results can serve as a first guideline for a systematic search of spatially modulated phases in heavy-ion collisions.

# Signatures of Moat Regimes in Heavy-Ion Collisions

## Key claim

If matter created in heavy-ion collisions freezes out in a moat regime where the minimum of the energy lies at nonzero momentum, then particle numbers and their correlations will peak at nonzero transverse momentum rather than at zero; this effect is much more dramatic for multi-particle correlations than for single-particle spectra, and the results serve as a first guideline for a systematic search of spatially modulated phases.

## Method

The authors compute particle spectra on the freeze-out surface using a generalized Cooper-Frye formula adapted for arbitrary dispersion relations, with a quasi-particle spectral function derived from an O(N) effective Lagrangian containing a negative wave-function renormalization Z and a positive quartic spatial momentum term W. They model the hydrodynamic expansion using a boost-invariant blast wave parametrization with parameters fixed to beam energy sqrt(s) = 5 GeV, and compute thermodynamic fluctuations on the freeze-out surface by constructing a generating functional for Gaussian fluctuations of temperature, baryon chemical potential, and fluid velocity, using a hadron resonance gas model for the equation of state.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out temperature T_f = 115 MeV and baryon chemical potential mu_B,f = 536 MeV, taken from statistical model fits for sqrt(s) = 5 GeV collisions (Andronic 2017).
- Blast wave parameters: radial velocity bar u = 0.3, fireball radius bar R = 8 fm, freeze-out proper time tau_f = 5 fm/c, taken from fits to experimental data (Zhang 2016).
- Illustrative moat parameters: W = 2.5 GeV^{-2}, m_eff = 20 MeV, with Z negative (e.g., Z = -0.05 for correlation calculations).
- In the normal phase (Z > 0), the two-particle correlation function is smaller than the moat-regime peak by at least a factor of about 10^{-2}.
- The minimum of the moat energy is E_min = sqrt(m_eff^2 - Z^2/(4W)), which is less than the normal-phase minimum m_eff = 20 MeV.
- Experimental detection of momenta down to approximately 60 MeV is anticipated in the Beam Energy Scan II at RHIC.

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of spatially modulated phases in QCD at high baryon density, building on prior model calculations that found moat spectra in the T-mu_B plane (Fu 2019) and on large-N analyses showing that Goldstone bosons disorder chiral spirals into a quantum pion liquid (Pisarski 2020, Pisarski 2021, Tsvelik 2021). The results provide experimental signatures that could distinguish a moat regime from a critical endpoint, where long-range correlations would appear, and offer a first guideline for searches in the Beam Energy Scan II at RHIC.
