# The impact of bound states on similarity renormalization group transformations

> We study a simple class of unitary renormalization group transformations governed by a parameter f in the range [0,1]. For f=0, the transformation is one introduced by Wegner in condensed matter physics, and for f=1 it is a simpler transformation that is being used in nuclear theory. The transformation with f=0 diagonalizes the Hamiltonian but in the transformations with f near 1 divergent couplings arise as bound state thresholds emerge. To illustrate and diagnose this behavior, we numerically study Hamiltonian flows in two simple models with bound states: one with asymptotic freedom and a related one with a limit cycle. The f=0 transformation places bound-state eigenvalues on the diagonal at their natural scale, after which the bound states decouple from the dynamics at much smaller momentum scales. At the other extreme, the f=1 transformation tries to move bound-state eigenvalues to the part of the diagonal corresponding to the lowest momentum scales available and inevitably diverges when this scale is taken to zero. We analyze the shift mechanism analytically in a 3x3 matrix model, which displays the essense of this RG behavior.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the simple similarity renormalization group (SRG) transformation with generator parameter f=1, which is widely used in nuclear theory, develops divergent couplings when the SRG flow parameter approaches the momentum scale of a bound state, whereas Wegner's transformation (f=0) places bound-state eigenvalues on the diagonal at their natural scale and remains well-behaved. The divergence arises because the f=1 transformation attempts to shift bound-state eigenvalues to the lowest available momentum scale on the diagonal, forcing off-diagonal matrix elements to grow without bound.

## Method

The authors numerically solve SRG flow equations for Hamiltonian matrices in two discretized momentum-space models: an asymptotically free model derived from the two-dimensional delta-function potential, and a related model with a limit cycle obtained by adding an imaginary antisymmetric component. They study a family of SRG generators parameterized by f in [0,1] that interpolates between Wegner's generator (f=0, using the diagonal part of the Hamiltonian) and the simpler generator (f=1, using the fixed kinetic energy operator T). The evolution of the full Hamiltonian matrix is reduced to the evolution of a single running coupling constant g_f(s), and the mechanism is further analyzed analytically using a 3x3 matrix truncation in the limit of large momentum spacing b.

## Key numbers

- The coupling constant g in the asymptotically free model is set to g=0.0400022797581725654, with b=4, N=16, M=-25, and the bound-state eigenvalue is approximately E ~ -7.644479 x 10^-6
- For f=0 (Wegner), the coupling constant g_f reaches a maximum of approximately 1.234710 at ln(lambda)/ln(b) = -8.369638 before dropping to negative values
- For f=1, the coupling constant g_1 must reach approximately b^{|M|} times the bound-state energy scale, which is 4^{16} in the illustrated case, causing numerical calculations to fail
- When f increases sufficiently to shift the bound-state eigenvalue by one state down the diagonal, the maximal coupling constant increases by factor b=4; for a shift by two states, the increase is by factor b^2=16
- The critical value f_c, below which bound-state eigenvalues appear at their natural scale, is computed analytically in the 3x3 matrix model

## Context

This work extends the analysis of SRG transformations introduced by Glazek and Wilson (1993, 1994, 1998) and independently by Wegner (1994), comparing Wegner's original flow equations with the simpler transformation used in nuclear few-body problems by Bogner et al. (2006, 2007) and Perry and Szpigel. The paper shows that while the simpler f=1 transformation produces universal low-energy nucleon-nucleon interactions when the SRG parameter lambda stays above bound-state scales, it becomes problematic for systems exhibiting the Efimov effect or limit cycles, where Wegner's transformation remains capable of resolving the behavior. The analysis enables understanding of convergence limitations in SRG applications to nuclear physics, particularly for three-nucleon problems where the Efimov effect cannot be avoided.
