# Exploring sQGP and Small Systems

> A strongly interacting Quark-Gluon Plasma (sQGP) is created in the high energy heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC. Our present understanding of sQGP as a very good liquid with astonishingly low viscosity is reviewed. With the arrival of the interesting results from LHC in high-energy p+p and p+A, a new endeavour to characterize the transition from these small systems to heavy ions (A+A) is now in place, since, even the small systems showed prominent similarities to heavy ions in the rising multiplicity domains. An outlook of future possibilities for better measurements is also made at the end of this brief review.

## Key claim

This review paper argues that the strongly interacting Quark-Gluon Plasma (sQGP) created in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC behaves as a nearly perfect liquid with very low shear viscosity, and that unexpectedly, small collision systems (p+p and p+A) at LHC energies show collective flow features and other similarities to heavy ion collisions in high-multiplicity events, challenging the assumption that QGP effects are absent in such small systems.

## Method

The paper surveys experimental results from RHIC and LHC heavy ion programs, focusing on elliptic flow (v₂) measurements and hydrodynamic model comparisons that characterize the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (η/s) of the QGP. It then reviews nuclear modification factor (R_AA) measurements for light and heavy flavor hadrons in p+p, p+A, and A+A collisions, and examines long-range azimuthal correlation ("ridge") observations in small systems from CMS, ALICE, and ATLAS experiments.

## Key numbers

- Initial energy density at LHC at τ₀ = 1 fm/c is about 15 GeV fm⁻³, approximately a factor of three higher than Au+Au collisions at the highest RHIC energy
- The proposed lower bound for η/s from string theory is η/s ≥ ℏ/(4πk_B)
- CMS observed a long-range ridge-like structure in p+p collisions at √s = 7 TeV for high multiplicity N ≈ 90 or higher, with |Δη| > 2
- Nuclear modification factor for charged particles in p+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV shows no suppression in the 2-10 GeV/c p_T region
- D-meson nuclear modification factor in p+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV shows no suppression within uncertainties in the measured p_T range of 1-24 GeV/c
- LHC delivered nearly 30 fb⁻¹ by end of 2012 and proposes to reach 300 fb⁻¹ in its first 13-15 years of operation

## Context

This review extends the understanding of sQGP established from RHIC experiments (Adams 2005, Adcox 2004, Back 2004, Arsene 2004) and LHC heavy ion results, particularly the discovery that QGP is a strongly coupled liquid rather than a weakly coupled gas as previously expected from perturbative QCD. The paper addresses the emerging puzzle from LHC results showing collective behavior in small systems (p+p and p+A), which were traditionally used only as baseline measurements for heavy ion collisions, and points to future Run-3 and High-Luminosity LHC data as necessary to bridge the gap between small and large collision systems.
