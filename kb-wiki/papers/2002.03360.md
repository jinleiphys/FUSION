# Local Spin Polarization in 200 GeV Au+Au and 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb Collisions

> We calculated the azimuthal angle dependence of the local spin polarization of hyperons in 200 GeV Au+Au and 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions in the framework of the (3+1)D viscous hydrodynamic model CLVisc with AMPT initial conditions encoding initial orbital angular momenta. We find that the azimuthal angle dependence of the hyperon polarization strongly depends on the choice of the spin chemical potential $Ω_{μν}$. With $Ω_{μν}$ chosen to be proportional to the temperature vorticity, our simulation shows coincidental results with the recent measurements at RHIC.

## Key claim
The azimuthal angle dependence of hyperon polarization in heavy-ion collisions strongly depends on the choice of the spin chemical potential; only when the spin chemical potential is chosen proportional to the temperature vorticity (T-vorticity) do the simulations agree with recent measurements at RHIC, while other choices fail to reproduce the data.

## Method
The authors used the (3+1)D viscous hydrodynamic model CLVisc with AMPT initial conditions that encode initial orbital angular momenta. They tested four different types of vorticities (kinematic, non-relativistic, temperature, and thermal) as candidates for the spin chemical potential in the polarization formula, and computed the azimuthal angle dependence of the longitudinal and transverse polarization of Lambda hyperons at freeze-out.

## Key numbers
- For Au+Au collisions at sqrt(S_NN) = 200 GeV and 20%-50% centrality, the longitudinal polarization from T-vorticity reproduces experimental data; the calculation used rapidity range Y in [-1,1] and transverse momentum range p_T in [0, 2.0] GeV.
- For Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(S_NN) = 2.76 TeV and 10%-60% centrality, the longitudinal polarization from T-vorticity shows a periodic structure but is smaller than at 200 GeV; the calculation used Y in [-1,1] and p_T in [0, 3.0] GeV.
- The hyperon decay parameter used was alpha_Lambda = alpha_anti-Lambda = 0.642 +/- 0.013 for both Lambda and anti-Lambda.

## Context
This work extends previous hydrodynamic and transport model calculations (Karpenko 2016, Li 2017, Xie 2017, Wei 2018) that could reproduce global polarization but failed to describe the longitudinal polarization data from STAR (2017, 2019), showing a sign difference. The results suggest that the spin degree of freedom is not in global equilibrium, so the thermal vorticity is not the correct spin chemical potential; the T-vorticity choice resolves this discrepancy. This enables further studies of spin polarization mechanisms in heavy-ion collisions and may guide the development of spin hydrodynamics.
