# Pygmy dipole resonance: collective features and symmetry energy effects

> A very important open question related to the pygmy dipole resonance is about its quite elusive collective nature. In this paper, within a harmonic oscillator shell model, generalizing an approach introduced by Brink, we first identify the dipole normal modes in neutron rich nuclei and derive the energy weighted sum rule exhausted by the pygmy dipole resonance. Then solving numerically the self-consistent Landau-Vlasov kinetic equations for neutrons and protons with specific initial conditions, we explore the structure of the different dipole vibrations in the $^{132}Sn$ system and investigate their dependence on the symmetry energy. We evidence the existence of a distinctive collective isoscalar-like mode with an energy well below the Giant Dipole Resonance (GDR), very weakly dependent on the isovector part of the nuclear effective interaction. At variance the corresponding strength is rather sensitive to the behavior of the symmetry energy below saturation, which rules the number of excess neutrons in the nuclear surface.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Pygmy Dipole Resonance (PDR) in neutron-rich nuclei is a collective isoscalar-like mode, with an energy centroid well below the Giant Dipole Resonance (GDR) that is insensitive to the isovector part of the nuclear interaction, but its strength is sensitive to the density dependence of the symmetry energy below saturation.

## Method

The authors first generalize Brink's harmonic oscillator shell model (HOSM) to separate the dipole normal modes in neutron-rich nuclei into a core dipole mode and a pygmy mode of excess neutrons oscillating against the core. They then solve the self-consistent Landau-Vlasov kinetic equations for neutrons and protons numerically using a test-particle method for the nucleus \(^{132}\)Sn, employing three different parametrizations of the symmetry energy (asysoft, asystiff, asysuperstiff) with a Skyrme-like (\(SKM^*\)) mean field, and analyze the time evolution and Fourier power spectra of the dipole moments under pygmy-like and GDR-like initial excitations.

## Key numbers

* The total E1 absorption cross section from the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn sum rule is \(60 \frac{NZ}{A}\) mb·MeV.
* For \(^{132}\)Sn, the HOSM predicts a fraction \(f_y = 19.5\%\) of the energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) exhausted by the pygmy mode if all \(N_e = 32\) excess neutrons participate.
* The neutron skin thickness \(\Delta R_{np}\) for \(^{132}\)Sn is 0.25 fm for asysoft, 0.30 fm for asystiff, and 0.31 fm for asysuperstiff EoS.
* The PDR energy centroid for \(^{132}\)Sn is around 8.5 MeV, insensitive to the asy-EoS.
* The integrated cross section over the PDR region (from GDR-like initial excitation) represents 2.7% for asysoft, 4.4% for asystiff, and 4.5% for asysuperstiff EoS out of the total cross section.
* The fraction of photon emission probability from the PDR region is 4.7% for asysoft, 7.7% for asystiff, and 9% for asysuperstiff EoS.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the PDR using hydrodynamical models (Mohan 1971, Bastrukov 2008), non-relativistic RPA (Tsoneva 2008, Co 2009, Yoshida 2009), and relativistic quasi-particle RPA (Vretenar 2001, Litvinova 2008), which left open questions about the collective nature of the PDR. The results support the isoscalar-like character of the PDR, consistent with a relativistic mean-field approach (Liang 2007), and show that the PDR strength is proportional to the neutron skin thickness, in agreement with Inakura (2011). The findings enable future studies of the collective response in lighter neutron-rich nuclei such as Ni or Ca isotopes, and in other mesoscopic systems.
