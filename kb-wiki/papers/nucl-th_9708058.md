# Electromagnetic Spectrum from QGP Fluid

> We calculate thermal photon and electron pair distribution from hot QCD matter produced in high energy heavy-ion collisions, based on a hydrodynamical model which is so tuned as to reproduce the recent experimental data at CERN SPS, and compare these electromagnetic spectra with experimental data given by CERN WA80 and CERES. We investigate mainly the effects of the off-shell properties of the source particles on the electromagnetic spectra.

# Electromagnetic Spectrum from QGP Fluid

## Key claim

The authors calculate thermal photon and electron pair spectra from hot QCD matter using a hydrodynamical model tuned to reproduce CERN SPS hadron data, and find that while the photon spectra are consistent with WA80 data, the off-shell properties of source particles are insufficient to reproduce the low-mass enhancement in the CERES electron pair data.

## Method

The authors solve (3+1)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamics with a Bag model equation of state, using initial temperatures tuned to fit hadron spectra from S+Au 200A GeV and Pb+Pb 158A GeV collisions at CERN SPS. Thermal production rates for photons and dileptons are derived from a quantum Langevin equation that incorporates off-shell properties of source particles, and these rates are integrated over the space-time evolution from the hydrodynamic model to obtain final electromagnetic spectra.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature: T_c = 160 MeV; freeze-out temperature: T_f = 140 MeV
- Initial temperature for S+Au 200A GeV collisions: T_i = 209 MeV
- Initial temperature for Pb+Pb 158A GeV collisions: T_i = 190 MeV
- Thermal photon distribution from the model is consistent with CERN WA80 data for S+Au 200A GeV collisions
- The off-shell model shows deviation from the on-shell model near the threshold M ~ 2 m_pi, but this off-shell effect is not enough to reproduce the CERES electron pair enhancement in the low invariant mass region

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous hydrodynamic analysis (Akase 1991) by adopting a Bag model equation of state instead of a simple phase transition model, and by applying a quantum Langevin approach to compute electromagnetic emission (Hirano 1997). The results are compared with experimental data from CERN WA80 and CERES collaborations. The authors note that their model does not include partial restoration of chiral symmetry or finite-temperature mass shift of the rho meson, which could improve agreement with the CERES data, as suggested by Sollfrank and others.
