# Removing cutoff artifacts in the NJL model by a Renormalization Group consistent treatment

> We summarize how a renormalization-group (RG)-consistent treatment removes well-known artifacts in NJL-model descriptions of color-superconducting quark matter. We introduce two RG-consistent schemes, "minimal" and "massless", and present analytic solutions for the diquark gap at $T=0$ and for the phase boundary $T_c(μ)$ in symmetric massless matter, representing the high-density limit of the model. We compare the pairing gaps, phase diagram, and speed of sound with results obtained using conventional regularization.

# Removing cutoff artifacts in the NJL model by a Renormalization Group consistent treatment

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a renormalization-group (RG) consistent treatment removes four well-known cutoff artifacts in NJL-model descriptions of color-superconducting quark matter, but only for the "minimal" and "massless" schemes introduced; the conventional 3d-cutoff regularization still exhibits these artifacts, including acausal speed of sound and incorrect phase melting sequences.

## Method
The authors implement RG consistency by integrating the vacuum flow of the three-flavor NJL model up to a high scale Lambda much larger than the chemical potential, temperature, and original cutoff Lambda-prime, then integrating the medium flow to the infrared. They define two subtraction schemes ("minimal" and "massless") by demanding different correlators of the effective action vanish at the RG scale Lambda-prime, and solve the gap equations and neutrality conditions numerically for locally neutral quark matter at finite temperature and chemical potential.

## Key numbers
- The original NJL cutoff is Lambda-prime = 602.3 MeV.
- In the RG-consistent minimal scheme, the asymptotic diquark gap at infinite chemical potential is bar-Delta^(min)(mu -> infinity) = 213.3 MeV.
- In the RG-consistent massless scheme, the asymptotic diquark gap at infinite chemical potential is bar-Delta^(massless)(mu -> infinity) = 279.3 MeV.
- For the minimal scheme, the asymptotic gap value is bar-Delta^(min)(mu -> infinity) = (2^(2/3) * Lambda-prime) / e^(3/2).
- The ratio of critical temperature to zero-temperature diquark gap for the minimal scheme approaches lim_(mu->infinity) T_c(mu) / bar-Delta^(min)(mu) = 2^(1/3) * e^(gamma_E) / pi, approximately 0.714, where gamma_E is approximately 0.577.
- The BCS ratio e^(gamma_E)/pi is approximately 0.567.
- A first-order transition into the 2SC phase occurs around mu approximately 350 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the RG-consistent treatment previously developed for two-flavor Quark-Meson-Diquark models by Gholami (2024) and Gholami (2025) to the three-flavor NJL model with color superconductivity. It directly addresses the cutoff artifacts identified in earlier NJL studies by Ruester (2005), where diquark gaps vanish and the speed of sound diverges when chemical potential approaches the cutoff. The RG-consistent approach enables physically reliable thermodynamics for astrophysical applications, particularly for compact-star physics, and correctly reproduces the Ginzburg-Landau theory prediction by Iida (2003) that the high-density melting sequence is CFL to dSC to 2SC, which conventional regularization fails to produce.
