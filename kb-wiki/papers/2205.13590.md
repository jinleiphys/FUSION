# Light- and heavy-quark symmetries and the $Y(4230)$, $Y(4360)$, $Y(4500)$, $Y(4620)$ and $X(4630)$ resonances

> The heavy hadron spectrum is constrained by symmetries, of which two of the most important ones are heavy-quark spin and SU(3)-flavor symmetries. Here we argue that in the molecular picture the $Y(4230)$ (or $Y(4260)$), the $Y(4360)$ and the recently discovered $Y(4500)$ and $Y(4620)$ vector-like resonances are linked by these two symmetries. By formulating a contact-range effective field theory for the $D \bar{D}_1$ and $D_s \bar{D}_{s1}$ family of S- and P-wave charmed meson-antimeson systems, we find that if the $Y(4230)$ were to be a pure $D \bar{D}_1$ molecular state, there would be a $D^* \bar{D}_1$ partner with a mass similar to the $Y(4360)$, a $D_s \bar{D}_{s1}$ partner with a mass close to the $Y(4500)$ and three $J=1,2$ $D_s^* \bar{D}_{s1}$ and $J=3$ $D_s^* \bar{D}_{s2}^{*}$ bound states with a mass in the vicinity of $4630\,{\rm MeV}$, of which the first one ($J=1$) might correspond with the $Y(4620)$. The previous predictions can in turn be improved by modifying the assumptions we have used to build the effective field theory. In particular, if we consider the closeness of the $D^* \bar{D}_1$-$D^* \bar{D}_2^*$ and $D_s^* \bar{D}_{s1}$-$D_s^* \bar{D}_{s2}^*$ thresholds and include the related coupled channel dynamics, we predict a $J=2$ positive C-parity state with a mass around $4650\,{\rm MeV}$. This hidden-strange and hidden-charm state might in turn be identified with the $X(4630)$ that has been discovered past year by the LHCb in the $J/ψϕ$ invariant mass distribution.

## Key claim

If the Y(4230) is a pure D D̅₁ molecular state, then heavy-quark spin and SU(3)-flavor symmetries predict a D* D̅₁ partner near the Y(4360), a D_s D̅_{s1} partner near the Y(4500), and three D_s* D̅_{s1} and D_s* D̅_{s2}* bound states near 4630 MeV, with the J=1 state possibly corresponding to the Y(4620); including coupled-channel dynamics further predicts a J=2 positive C-parity state around 4650 MeV that might be identified with the X(4630).

## Method

The authors formulate a contact-range effective field theory for S- and P-wave charmed meson-antimeson systems (D D̅₁, D* D̅₁, D_s D̅_{s1}, D_s* D̅_{s1}, D_s* D̅_{s2}*), incorporating heavy-quark spin symmetry (HQSS) and SU(3)-flavor symmetry at lowest order. They derive the S-wave contact-range potential using both heavy superfield and light-quark subfield notations, expressing it in terms of four coupling constants (C_a, C_b, D_b, D_c) that are further split into isoscalar and isovector components. The potential is then applied to predict partner-state masses from the assumed Y(4230) binding energy, using a power counting where the D_b (electric dipole) coupling dominates.

## Key numbers

* Y(4230) mass: 4218.6 ± 4.5 MeV (from BESIII in η J/ψ, with statistical and systematic errors summed in quadrature), binding energy B = 70.8 ± 4.5 MeV relative to the D₁ D̅ threshold at 4289.4 MeV
* Y(4360) mass: 4382.0 ± 13.4 MeV (from BESIII), binding energy B = 48.7 ± 13.4 MeV relative to the D₁ D̅* threshold at 4430.7 MeV
* Y(4500) mass: 4484.7 ± 27.5 MeV (from BESIII in e⁺ e⁻ → K⁺ K⁻ J/ψ), binding energy B = 18.8 ± 27.5 MeV relative to the D_{s1} D̅_s threshold at 4503.5 MeV
* Y(4620) mass: 4625.9^{+6.2}_{-6.0} MeV (from Belle in e⁺ e⁻ → D_s⁺ D_{s1}(2526)⁻ + c.c.), binding energy B = 52.8^{+6.0}_{-6.2} MeV relative to the D_{s1} D̅_s* threshold at 4647.3 MeV
* X(4630) mass: 4626^{+24}_{-111} MeV (from LHCb in J/ψ φ), binding energy B = 21^{+111}_{-24} MeV relative to the D_{s1} D̅_s* threshold at 4647.3 MeV
* Predicted Y(4500) mass from symmetry: 4468.1 MeV (from the Y(4230) binding energy scaled by 1/2)
* Predicted Y(4620) mass from symmetry: 4623.0 MeV (from the Y(4360) binding energy scaled by 1/2)
* Predicted J=2 positive C-parity state mass: around 4650 MeV (from including D* D̅₁-D* D̅₂* and D_s* D̅_{s1}-D_s* D̅_{s2}* coupled-channel dynamics)

## Context

This work extends the molecular interpretation of the Y(4230) (originally proposed by Liu (2005) and others) to a family of five vector-like resonances using HQSS and SU(3)-flavor symmetry, building on previous EFT treatments of S-wave charmed meson-antimeson systems by AlFiky (2005), Fleming (2007), Mehen (2011), Valderrama (2012), Nieves (2012), Hidalgo Duque (2012), and Baru (2016). The predictions connect with recent experimental discoveries: the Y(4500) by BESIII (2022), the Y(4620) by Belle (2019), and the X(4630) by LHCb (2021) in J/ψ φ. The results enable future experimental searches for the predicted J=2 and J=3 partner states, and motivate refined coupled-channel calculations to distinguish between the Y(4620) and X(4630) interpretations.
