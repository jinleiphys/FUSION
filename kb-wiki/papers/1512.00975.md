# Interplay of fission modes in mass distribution of light actinide nuclei 225,227Pa

> Fission-fragment mass distributions were measured for 225,227Pa nuclei formed in fusion reactions of 19F + 206, 208Pb around fusion barrier energies. Mass-angle correlations do not indicate any quasi-fission like events in this bombarding energy range. Mass distributions were fitted by Gaussian distribution and mass variance extracted. At below-barrier energies, the mass variance was found to increase with decrease in energy for both nuclei. Results from present work were compared with existing data for induced fission of 224, 226Th and 228U around barrier energies. Enhancement in mass variance of 225, 227Pa nuclei at below-barrier energies shows evidence for presence of asymmetric fission events mixed with symmetric fission events. This is in agreement with the results of mass distributions of nearby nuclei 224, 226Th and 228U where two-mode fission process was observed. Two-mode feature of fission arises due to the shell effects changing the landscape of the potential energy surfaces at low excitation energies. The excitation-energy dependence of the mass variance gives strong evidence for survival of microscopic shell effects in fission of light actinide nuclei 225, 227Pa with initial excitation energy ~30 - 50 MeV.

# Interplay of fission modes in mass distribution of light actinide nuclei 225,227Pa

## Key claim
The mass variance of fission fragments for 225,227Pa nuclei increases at below-barrier energies, which the authors interpret as evidence for the presence of asymmetric fission events mixed with symmetric fission events, rather than quasi-fission. This two-mode fission behavior arises from shell effects that survive at initial excitation energies of approximately 30-50 MeV, and the effect is observed in both 225Pa (N=134) and 227Pa (N=136), suggesting similar fission properties despite straddling the transition mass limit A_CN=226.

## Method
Fission-fragment mass distributions were measured for 225,227Pa compound nuclei formed in fusion reactions of 19F + 206,208Pb at beam energies from 87 to 120 MeV, corresponding to excitation energies of 30-50 MeV. The experiment used pulsed 19F beams from the 15UD Pelletron accelerator at IUAC, New Delhi, with two large-area position-sensitive multiwire proportional counters (MWPCs) detecting complementary fission fragments in coincidence. Mass distributions were extracted via velocity reconstruction, fitted with single symmetric Gaussian functions, and the mass variance was obtained as a function of excitation energy.

## Key numbers
- Beam energies varied from 87 MeV to 120 MeV for the 19F + 206,208Pb reactions
- Excitation energy range of compound nuclei: approximately 30-50 MeV
- Projectile-target charge product Z_p Z_T = 738 for 19F + 206,208Pb systems (below 800)
- Energy range studied: 0.93 ≤ E_cm/V_B ≤ 1.29
- Fusion barrier corresponds to initial excitation energy of approximately 35 MeV
- For 227Pa, potential energy surface calculation shows a valley near mass-asymmetry η = 0.16, corresponding to mass division 132/95
- Relative yield of asymmetric component in nearby 225,227Pa from 16,18O + 209Bi reactions was about 10% of total fission yield at 29 MeV excitation energy

## Context
This work extends systematic studies of multi-mode fission in light actinide nuclei, comparing directly with existing data for 224,226Th (from 16,18O + 208Pb reactions by Itkis (1995) and Rusanov) and 228U (from 19F + 209Bi by Pant). The results agree with the two-mode fission process observed in those neighboring nuclei and with the work of Schmidt and colleagues on charge distribution in Coulomb fission, which suggested a transition from symmetric to asymmetric fission around mass A≈226. The paper also references the potential energy surface calculations by Moller (2001) predicting two deformation paths, and the experimental observation of asymmetric fission yields in 225,227Pa by Nishinaka and colleagues using 16,18O + 209Bi reactions. The findings enable further investigation into the dynamics of shell damping as a function of excitation energy, particularly in neutron-deficient transitional nuclei, and complement recent discoveries of asymmetric fission in 180,190Hg by Andreyev and Nishio.
