# The tidal deformability of an anisotropic compact star: Implications of GW170817

> We use gravitational wave (GW) and electromagnetic (EM) observations of GW170817 to constrain the extent of pressure anisotropy in it. While it is quite likely that the pressure inside a neutron star is mostly isotropic, certain physical processes or characteristics, such as phase transitions in nuclear matter or the presence of strong magnetic fields, can introduce pressure anisotropy. In this work, we show that anisotropic pressure in neutron stars can reduce their tidal deformability substantially. For the anisotropy-pressure model of Bowers and Liang and a couple of relativistic EOSs -- DDH$δ$ and GM1 -- we demonstrate that this reduction in spherical neutron stars with masses in the range of 1 to 2 $M_\odot$ can be 23% to 46%. This suggests that certain EOSs that are ruled out by GW170817 observations, under assumptions of pressure isotropy, can become viable if the stars had a significant enough anisotropic pressure component, but do not violate causality. We also show how the inference of the star radius can be used to rule out certain EOSs (such as GM1), even for high anisotropic pressure, because their radii are larger than what the observations find.

# The tidal deformability of an anisotropic compact star: Implications of GW170817

## Key claim

The paper shows that positive pressure anisotropy in neutron stars can reduce their tidal deformability by 23% to 46% for masses between 1 and 2 solar masses, which means that certain equations of state previously ruled out by GW170817 observations under the assumption of isotropic pressure can become viable if the stars possess a sufficiently large anisotropic pressure component, provided causality is not violated.

## Method

The authors solve the modified Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for static, spherically symmetric neutron stars with anisotropic pressure, using the Bowers and Liang anisotropy model where the transverse pressure differs from the radial pressure by a term proportional to the anisotropy parameter λ_BL. They compute the tidal Love number k₂ and dimensionless tidal deformability Λ by solving the linearized Einstein equations for even-parity l=2 perturbations, matching the interior solution to the exterior solution at the stellar surface. Two relativistic mean-field equations of state are used for the radial pressure: DDHδ and GM1, with a Douchin-Haensel crust added below 10⁻³ fm⁻³ density.

## Key numbers

- Tidal deformability reduction of 23% to 46% for neutron stars with masses in the range 1 to 2 M⊙, depending on the equation of state and anisotropy parameter
- The effective tidal deformability Λ̄ of the binary is constrained to 400 ≤ Λ̄ ≤ 720 by combining GW170817 gravitational wave data (Λ̄ ≤ 720 at 90% confidence for low spin prior) and the electromagnetic counterpart AT2017gfo (Λ̄ ≥ 400)
- The chirp mass of GW170817 was measured as 1.188⁺⁰·⁰⁰⁴₋₀·₀₀₂ M⊙, with mass ratio q constrained between 0.7 and 1 for low spin prior
- For DDHδ with λ_BL = 0.59, the radius inferred from the C-Λ universality relation is 11.6⁺²·²₋₁·₆ km (90% CL), with a minimum radius of 12.9 km for stars in the 1-2 M⊙ range
- For GM1 with λ_BL = 0.80, the radius inferred is 11.7⁺²·³₋₁·₆ km (90% CL), but the minimum radius for such stars is 14.2 km, ruling out this combination
- For GM1 with λ_BL = 2, the radius inferred is 12.0⁺²·³₋₁·₆ km (90% CL), with a minimum radius of 15.1 km, also ruled out
- The anisotropy parameter range studied is -2 ≤ λ_BL ≤ 2, with only positive values (λ_BL > 0) yielding physically acceptable solutions where transverse pressure remains non-negative

## Context

This work extends the study of anisotropic neutron stars by Yagi and Yunes (2013), who examined universal relations between moment of inertia, tidal Love number, and quadrupole moment for slowly rotating stars, by instead using the Bowers and Liang anisotropy model and focusing on the tidal deformability constraints from GW170817. The paper shows that pressure anisotropy can resolve the tension between stiff equations of state like GM1 and the GW170817 tidal deformability upper bound, which previously ruled out such EOSs in isotropic analyses (Abbott 2018). The authors also demonstrate that radius measurements from universality relations between compactness C and tidal deformability Λ, first noted by Maselli (2013), can break degeneracies between soft isotropic EOSs and stiff EOSs with positive anisotropy, enabling future gravitational wave observations to constrain the anisotropy parameter more tightly.
