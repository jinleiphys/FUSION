# Spurious finite-size instabilities in nuclear energy density functionals

> It is known that some well-established parametrizations of the EDF do not always provide converged results for nuclei and a qualitative link between this finding and the appearance of finite-size instabilities of SNM near saturation density when computed within the RPA has been pointed out. We seek for a quantitative and systematic connection between the impossibility to converge self-consistent calculations of nuclei and the occurrence of finite-size instabilities in SNM for the example of scalar-isovector (S=0, T=1) instabilities of the standard Skyrme EDF. We aim to establish a stability criterion based on computationally-friendly RPA calculations of SNM that is independent on the functional form of the EDF and that can be utilized during the adjustment of its coupling constants. Tuning the coupling constant $C^{ρΔρ}_{1}$ of the gradient term that triggers scalar-isovector instabilities of the standard Skyrme EDF, we find that the occurrence of instabilities in finite nuclei depends strongly on the numerical scheme used to solve the self-consistent mean-field equations. The link to instabilities of SNM is made by extracting the lowest density $ρ_{\text{crit}}$ at which a pole appears at zero energy in the RPA response function when employing the critical value of the coupling constant $C^{ρΔρ}_{1}$ extracted in nuclei. Our analysis suggests a two-fold stability criterion to avoid scalar-isovector instabilities: (i) The density $ρ_{\text{min}}$ corresponding to the lowest pole in the RPA response function should be larger than about 1.2 times the saturation density; (ii) one needs to verify that $ρ_{p}(q_{\text{pq}})$ exhibits a distinct global minimum and is not a decreasing function for large transferred momenta.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a quantitative link between the impossibility to converge self-consistent mean-field calculations of finite nuclei and the occurrence of finite-size instabilities in symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) computed within the random phase approximation (RPA), but the connection depends strongly on the numerical scheme used to solve the mean-field equations and on the specific Skyrme parametrization.

## Method

The authors modified nine Skyrme energy density functional (EDF) parametrizations by tuning the scalar-isovector gradient coupling constant \(C^{\rho\Delta\rho}_{1}\) from its nominal value upward until instabilities appeared in finite-nucleus calculations. They performed self-consistent mean-field calculations for a set of spherical doubly closed-shell nuclei and one deformed nucleus using three different numerical codes: Hosphe (harmonic oscillator basis), Lenteur (spherical coordinate space), and Ev8 (3D Cartesian mesh). For each parametrization at its critical coupling constant, they extracted the lowest density \(\rho_{\text{crit}}\) at which a pole appears at zero energy in the RPA response function of SNM, and compared this to the central density of \(^{40}\)Ca.

## Key numbers

* The critical coupling constant \(C^{\rho\Delta\rho}_{1,\text{crit}}\) for SLy5 was \(31.7(2)\) MeV fm\(^5\) at mesh size \(dx=0.4\) fm, compared to its nominal value of \(16.375\) MeV fm\(^5\).
* The ratio \(\rho_{\text{crit}}/\rho_{\text{sat}}\) for SLy5 was \(1.08\) at \(dx=0.4\) fm, while for UNEDF0 it was \(1.02\) and for NRAPRii it was \(1.67\).
* The central density of \(^{40}\)Ca, \(\rho_{\text{cent}}\), is typically about 20% larger than the saturation density \(\rho_{\text{sat}}\) (which is approximately \(0.160\) fm\(^{-3}\) for SLy5).
* The proposed stability criterion requires that \(\rho_{\text{min}}\) (the minimum of \(\rho_{p}(q_{\text{ph}})\)) should be larger than about \(1.2\) times the saturation density.

## Context

This work extends earlier qualitative observations by Lesinski et al. (2006) and Tondeur (1984) linking non-convergence in EDF calculations to finite-size instabilities in SNM. It provides a systematic, quantitative protocol for detecting scalar-isovector instabilities that complements the Landau-Migdal parameter approach, which only captures instabilities at zero momentum transfer. The proposed two-fold stability criterion enables future EDF parametrization efforts to avoid unphysical finite-size instabilities during the adjustment of coupling constants, particularly for more elaborate functional forms such as those explored by Carlsson et al. (2008) and Stoitsov et al. (2010).
