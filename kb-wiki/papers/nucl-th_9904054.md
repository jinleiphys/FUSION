# Local harmonic approaches with approximate cranking operators

> Methods of large amplitude collective motion in the adiabatic limit are examined with a special emphasis on conservation laws. We show that the restriction to point transformations, which is a usual assumption of the adiabatic time-dependent mean-field theory, needs to be lifted. In order to facilitate the application of large amplitude collective motion techniques, we examine the possibility of representing the RPA normal-mode coordinates by linear combinations of a limited number of one-body operators. We study the pairing-plus-quadrupole model of Baranger and Kumar as an example, and find that such representations exist in terms of operators that are state-dependent in a characteristic manner.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the usual restriction to point transformations in adiabatic time-dependent mean-field theory must be lifted to properly handle conservation laws, and that the symplectic version of the local harmonic approximation (LHA) combined with an extended adiabatic transformation automatically separates spurious modes from physical collective modes at any point in configuration space, not just at equilibrium.

## Method

The authors formulate the local harmonic approximation (LHA) for large amplitude collective motion within the time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (TDHFB) framework, comparing Riemannian and symplectic versions. They develop a projected LHA method that approximates the random-phase approximation (RPA) normal-mode coordinates by linear combinations of a limited set of preselected one-body operators, and test this approach on the pairing-plus-quadrupole (P+Q) model of Baranger and Kumar.

## Key numbers

- The P+Q model reduces the number of two-quasiparticle (2qp) degrees of freedom from "of the order of a thousand" to only two collective coordinates (β and γ) in the Baranger-Kumar approach.
- The TDHFB dynamics of the models in Refs. [NW98_1] and [NW98_2] is described by 4 to 12 degrees of freedom, while realistic problems in heavy nuclei require "at least tens of thousands, and possibly millions of degrees of freedom."
- The Hamiltonian contains five operators of Rσ-type and four of Sσ-type, with coupling constants G_n, G_p, and χ for the pairing and quadrupole interactions respectively.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of the local harmonic approximation on simple models including a semi-microscopic pairing model coupled to a harmonic variable (Nakatsukasa, Walet, and Dang, 1998) and a fully microscopic O(4) model (Nakatsukasa, Walet, and Dang, 1998). It directly addresses the problem of spurious Nambu-Goldstone modes that arise when mean-field states break Hamiltonian symmetries, which could be removed by hand in those simpler models but must be treated systematically for realistic nuclear calculations. The paper also builds on the extensive Baranger-Kumar analysis of the P+Q model (Baranger and Kumar, 1965-1968) and on the work of Kishimoto and Tamura (1972, 1976) who used boson expansion techniques to study the same model. The projected LHA method aims to enable practical large amplitude collective motion calculations in heavy nuclei where full RPA diagonalization is computationally prohibitive.
