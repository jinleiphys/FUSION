# Contributions of strange quarks to the magnetic moment of the proton

> Using the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule and experimental total cross sections for photoproduction of $η$, $K$ and $ϕ$ mesons on the proton, we obtain upper bounds for the contribution of strange quarks to the anomalous magnetic moment of the proton, $|κ_{s}| \le 0.20 κ_{p}$. The proposed experiments to measure the spin-dependence of the absorption cross section are expected to lower these bounds considerably. The existing data on $η$ production and phenomenological models for $K$ production agree with a negative sign for $κ_{s}$.

# Contributions of strange quarks to the magnetic moment of the proton

## Key claim
Using the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule and total cross sections for photoproduction of η, K, and φ mesons on the proton, the authors obtain an upper bound for the strange quark contribution to the anomalous magnetic moment of the proton, |κ_s| ≤ 0.20 κ_p. This bound should be considered an order-of-magnitude estimate only, because the total cross section overestimates the spin-dependent cross section that appears in the sum rule, and cancellations can occur.

## Method
The authors applied the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule, which relates the anomalous magnetic moment of the nucleon to the energy-weighted integral of the difference between polarized total photoabsorption cross sections σ_{1/2} and σ_{3/2}. Since experimental data for the spin-dependent cross sections in strange-quark channels are not available, they used the total unpolarized cross section σ_tot as an upper bound for the spin-difference integrand, exploiting the inequality −σ_tot ≤ σ_{1/2} − σ_{3/2} ≤ σ_tot. They integrated total cross section data for photoproduction of η, K, and φ mesons on the proton up to cutoff energies of 2.0, 5.0, and 50.0 GeV, with the η contribution weighted by a factor of 1/3 to account for its strange quark content.

## Key numbers
- Upper bound on the strange quark contribution to the anomalous magnetic moment of the proton: |κ_s| ≤ 0.20 κ_p, where κ_p = 1.79.
- For a cutoff ν_max = 5.0 GeV, the squared contributions to κ_s² are: κ_s²(φ) = 0.01, κ_s²(η) = 0.03, κ_s²(K) = 0.06, giving a total κ_s² = 0.10, which corresponds to κ_s²/κ_p² = 3% and |κ_s|/κ_p = 17%.
- For ν_max = 2.0 GeV, the φ contribution is negligible (κ_s²(φ) = 0.00), with total κ_s² = 0.07, corresponding to κ_s²/κ_p² = 2% and |κ_s|/κ_p = 14%.
- For ν_max = 50.0 GeV, total κ_s² = 0.16, corresponding to κ_s²/κ_p² = 5% and |κ_s|/κ_p = 22%.
- Model calculation for kaon photoproduction up to ν_max = 2.2 GeV gives κ_p²(K) = −0.07 and κ_n²(K) = −0.02, indicating contributions to both isoscalar and isovector-isoscalar interference terms.

## Context
This work extends the use of the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule, originally derived by Drell, Hearn, and Gerasimov (1966), to constrain strange quark contributions to nucleon structure, complementing model calculations from chiral perturbation theory (Musolf, 1996) and dispersion theory (Musolf, 1996). The results are consistent with most theoretical calculations cited in the paper. The approach is complementary to parity-violating electron scattering experiments planned at MAMI, MIT/Bates, and Jefferson Lab. Future experiments by the Bonn-Mainz GDH collaboration and at Jefferson Lab, measuring the spin-dependent absorption cross sections directly, are expected to lower these bounds considerably and enable evaluation of the Burkhardt-Cottingham sum rule (Burkhardt and Cottingham, 1970) as a function of momentum transfer, which could provide estimates of nucleon charge and magnetic radii.
