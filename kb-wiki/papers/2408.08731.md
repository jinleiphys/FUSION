# Higgs production at NLL accuracy in the BFKL approach

> Precision physics in the Higgs sector has been one of the main challenges of particle physics in the recent years. The pure fixed-order calculations entering the collinear factorization framework, which have been pushed up to next-cube-leading-order, are not able to describe the entire kinematic spectrum. In particular sectors, they have to be necessarily enhanced by all-order resummations. In the so-called semi-hard regime, large energy-type logarithms spoil the perturbative convergence of the series and must be resummed to all orders. This resummation is a core ingredient for a correct description of the inclusive hadroproduction of a forward Higgs boson in the limit of small Bjorken $x$, as well as for a precision study of inclusive forward emissions of a Higgs boson in association with a backward identified object. A complete resummation for these processes can be achieved at the at next-to-leading logarithmic accuracy thanks to the Balitsky-Fadin-Kuraev-Lipatov approach. In the present work we present and discuss a series of recent phenomenological results within a partial next-to-leading accuracy. They include the analysis of rapidity and azimuthal-angle differential rates for Higgs plus jet and Higgs plus charm reactions in forward and ultraforward directions of rapidity at the LHC.

## Key claim

The paper presents phenomenological results for Higgs boson production in association with a jet or a charmed hadron using a partial next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) accuracy within the Balitsky-Fadin-Kuraev-Lipatov (BFKL) approach, where the NLL BFKL Green's function is combined with leading-order coefficient functions rather than a complete NLL calculation.

## Method

The authors employ a hybrid factorization (HyF) formalism that convolutes the universal BFKL Green's function with process-dependent impact factors, which themselves involve a sub-convolution of singly off-shell coefficient functions and collinear parton distribution functions (PDFs). They compute differential cross sections for Higgs plus jet and Higgs plus charm hadron production at 14 TeV LHC, using the NLL BFKL Green's function while keeping the coefficient functions at leading order, which constitutes a partial NLL accuracy. Rapidity and azimuthal-angle distributions are analyzed for forward and ultraforward rapidity regions.

## Key numbers

- Higgs plus jet transverse-momentum spectrum at 14 TeV LHC with rapidity separation ΔY = 5, Higgs boson detected in barrel calorimeter (|y_H| < 2.5) and jet reconstructed by endcaps (|y_J| < 4.7)
- Higgs plus D*± meson azimuthal multiplicity at 14 TeV for different ΔY values, with D*± meson detected in ultraforward rapidity directions (6 < y_C < 7.5) reachable at the Forward Physics Facility
- Uncertainty bands from μ_R and μ_F variation in the range 1 < C_μ < 2

## Context

This work extends previous BFKL-based phenomenological studies of semi-hard processes including Mueller-Navelet jets (Ducloue 2013, Celiberto 2015-2022), Drell-Yan pair production (Motyka 2014-2016, Celiberto 2018), and heavy-light hadron emissions (Celiberto 2017-2024). The results demonstrate that Higgs boson emission acts as a natural stabilizer of high-energy resummation in the peak region, while the large transverse-momentum tail shows decoupling between NLL and LL BFKL due to unaccounted DGLAP-type and threshold logarithms. Future work requires matching the NLL hybrid formalism with the NLO fixed-order background and including NLO contributions to the Higgs impact factor.
