# Recent progress in the studies of neutron rich and high-$Z$ systems within the covariant density functional theory

> The analysis of statistical and systematic uncertainties and their propogation to nuclear extremes has been performed. Two extremes of nuclear landscape (neutron-rich nuclei and superheavy nuclei) have been investigated. For the first extreme, we focus on the ground state properties. For the second extreme, we pay a particular attention to theoretical uncertainties in the description of fission barriers of superheavy nuclei and their evolution on going to neutron-rich nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper argues that systematic and statistical uncertainties in covariant density functional theory (CDFT) predictions grow substantially when extrapolating to neutron-rich and superheavy nuclei, but that the statistical uncertainties are generally smaller than the systematic ones, with the caveat that the analysis is restricted to the mean-field level and to functionals fitted to ground-state properties.

## Method

The authors performed systematic and statistical uncertainty analyses for ground-state observables (binding energies, neutron skins) across even-even nuclei up to Z=106 using four covariant energy density functionals (NL3*, DD-ME2, DD-MEδ, DD-PC1) and for inner fission barriers in superheavy nuclei using three functionals (NL3*, DD-PC1, PC-PK1). Statistical uncertainties were quantified via standard deviations from a set of "reasonable" variations of the NL3* functional within the spherical relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) framework, while systematic uncertainties were assessed from the spread of predictions across different functionals.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy spreads among the four CEDFs stay within 5-6 MeV in the region of known nuclei, but increase to 15 MeV when approaching the neutron drip line.
- Statistical uncertainties in binding energies for nuclei near the two-neutron drip line reach values comparable to the systematic spreads shown in the binding energy figure.
- Systematic uncertainties in inner fission barrier heights are typically less than 2 MeV for nuclei with N ≤ 180; only in a few nuclei around Z=110, N~164 and Z~110, N~176 do these uncertainties reach 4 and 5.5 MeV respectively.
- For nuclei with N ≥ 182, the systematic uncertainties in inner fission barrier heights increase by roughly 1 MeV compared to the N ≤ 180 region.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous comprehensive studies of systematic uncertainties in CDFT (Afanasjev 2014, 2015, 2016) and compares their statistical uncertainty results with Skyrme DFT analyses using the UNEDF0 functional (Kortelainen 2013, Gao 2013). The paper identifies that the increase in uncertainties near the neutron drip line is dominated by the poorly defined isovector channel of the effective interaction, and that benchmarking functionals against experimental fission barriers in actinides reduces the number of suitable functionals to three, which decreases theoretical uncertainties in inner fission barrier heights.
