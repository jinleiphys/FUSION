# Enhancement of $ϕ$ mesons in Pb+Pb collisions at 158A GeV/c

> Using a hadron-string cascade model LUCIAE, the $ϕ$ meson production in heavy ion collisions (Pb+Pb) and elementary collisions (p+p) were studied systematically. Within the framework of the model, the experimentally measured $ϕ$ enhancement in Pb+Pb over p+p collisions can be mostly explained by the collective effects in the gluon string emission and the reduction of the s-quark suppression.

# Enhancement of φ mesons in Pb+Pb collisions at 158A GeV/c

## Key claim

Within the hadron-string cascade model LUCIAE, the experimentally measured φ meson enhancement in Pb+Pb over p+p collisions at 158A GeV/c can be mostly explained by collective effects in gluon string emission (firecracker model) and reduction of s-quark suppression in string fragmentation, without invoking quark-gluon plasma formation or significant final state interactions.

## Method

The authors used the LUCIAE hadron-string cascade model, which extends the FRITIOF model by including rescattering between participants and produced particles, a firecracker model for collective gluon emission from high-density strings, and a phenomenological reduction of the s-quark suppression factor (JETSET parameter parj(2)) based on effective string tension. The model parameters were previously fixed by fitting to p+p data for multi-strange baryon production, so no additional free parameters were introduced for the φ meson calculations. Final state interactions were neglected for φ mesons based on their small cross sections with non-strange hadrons.

## Key numbers

- φ meson yield in p+p collisions: LUCIAE gives 0.0141 per event, compared to NA49 data of 0.012 ± 0.0015 per event
- φ meson yield in central Pb+Pb collisions: LUCIAE gives 7.89 per event, compared to NA49 data of 7.6 ± 1.1 per event
- φ/π ratio in p+p: LUCIAE gives 0.00528, NA49 gives 0.00460 ± 0.00053 (after correction for pion trigger)
- φ/π ratio in central Pb+Pb: LUCIAE gives 0.0116, NA49 gives 0.0124 ± 0.0018
- φ enhancement factor (φ/π ratio in Pb+Pb divided by φ/π ratio in p+p): LUCIAE gives 2.2, NA49 gives 2.7 ± 0.7 (after correction)
- Rapidity distribution width σ from Gaussian fit: LUCIAE gives 0.967 (p+p) and 1.05 (Pb+Pb); NA49 gives 0.89 ± 0.06 (p+p) and 1.22 ± 0.16 (Pb+Pb)
- Inverse slope parameter T from transverse mass distributions (Pb+Pb, highest four data points): LUCIAE gives 212 MeV, NA49 gives 289 MeV
- Inverse slope parameter T (p+p, highest three data points): both LUCIAE and NA49 give approximately 189 MeV
- Without firecracker mechanism: φ yield drops to 4.29 per event (φ/π = 0.00632)
- Without reduction of s-quark suppression: φ yield drops to 6.28 per event (φ/π = 0.00914)
- Without both mechanisms: φ yield is 5.48 per event (φ/π = 0.00852)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous LUCIAE studies of multi-strange baryon enhancement (Sa et al., 2000; Tai et al., 2000) to φ meson production, providing the first theoretical description of the full set of NA49 data on φ production at SPS energies. The model differs from approaches such as UrQMD (Soff et al., 2000; Xu et al., 2000), which attribute strangeness enhancement to a threefold increase in string tension from chiral restoration, and from thermal models assuming equilibrated quark-gluon plasma (Rafelski, 1982; Petran et al., 2000; Redlich et al., 2000). The results enable consistent investigation of strangeness production across p+p, p+A, and A+A collisions within a hadron-string framework without explicitly invoking QGP formation, though the authors note that further improvement of rapidity and transverse mass distributions may require transverse excitation of strings and intrinsic transverse momentum broadening.
