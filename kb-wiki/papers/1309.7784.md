# Probing the QCD phase diagram with the measurements of $ϕ$-meson production and elliptic flow in the heavy-ion collision at STAR

> We present the measurements of the $ϕ$-meson production and elliptic flow ($v_{2}$) at mid-rapidity in Au + Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 7.7 - 200 GeV. The data are collected using the STAR detector in the years 2010 and 2011. The energy dependence of nuclear modification factor ($R_{\rm{CP}}$) of $ϕ$ meson is presented. The $ϕ$-meson $R_{\rm{CP}}$ has a value $\geq$ 1.0 for $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ $\leq$ 39 GeV. The $Ω/ϕ$ ratios are also presented and show a different trend at the intermediate transverse momentum ($p_{T}$) for $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 11.5 GeV compared to higher beam energies. The number-of-constituent quark (NCQ) scaling of $v_{2}$ has been studied at various beam energies. The NCQ scaling holds for particles and anti-particles separately including the $ϕ$ meson for $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ $\geq$ 19.6 GeV, which can be considered as an evidence of partonic collectivity. We observe at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 7.7 and 11.5 GeV, the $ϕ$-meson $v_{2}$ falls off the trend from the other hadrons at highest measured $p_{T}$ values by 1.8$σ$ and 2.3$σ$, respectively.

# Probing the QCD phase diagram with the measurements of φ-meson production and elliptic flow in the heavy-ion collision at STAR

## Key claim
The φ-meson nuclear modification factor (R_CP) is ≥ 1.0 for center-of-mass energies √s_NN ≤ 39 GeV, indicating that parton energy loss effects become less important at lower beam energies; however, the NCQ scaling of elliptic flow (v_2) holds only for √s_NN ≥ 19.6 GeV, while at 7.7 and 11.5 GeV the φ-meson v_2 deviates from the trend of other hadrons by 1.8σ and 2.3σ respectively, suggesting a transition in the dominant collectivity mechanism.

## Method
The STAR detector collected minimum-bias Au+Au collision data at √s_NN = 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV during 2010 and 2011. φ-mesons were reconstructed via the K⁺K⁻ invariant mass technique (branching ratio 49.04 ± 0.6%) using the Time Projection Chamber and Time-of-Flight detectors for particle identification at mid-rapidity (|y| < 0.5). Elliptic flow was measured using the η-sub event plane method with a gap of |Δη| < 0.05, and combinatorial background was estimated with the mixed-event technique.

## Key numbers
- φ-meson R_CP (0-10%/40-60%) ≥ 1.0 for √s_NN ≤ 39 GeV at intermediate p_T (p_T > 1.5 GeV/c)
- χ²/ndf for deviation of (Ω⁻ + Ω̅⁺)/2φ ratio between 11.5 and 19.6 GeV is ~8.3/2 for p_T > 2.4 GeV/c
- φ-meson v_2 deviates from other hadrons at highest measured p_T by 1.8σ at √s_NN = 7.7 GeV and 2.3σ at √s_NN = 11.5 GeV
- φ-meson mass is 1.01945 ± 0.00002 GeV/c²
- φ-meson lifetime is ~42 fm/c
- φ → K⁺K⁻ branching ratio is 49.04 ± 0.6%

## Context
This work extends the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC by systematically measuring φ-meson production and elliptic flow across √s_NN = 7.7–200 GeV, building on previous STAR measurements at 200 GeV (STAR Collaboration, 2009). The observation that NCQ scaling holds for √s_NN ≥ 19.6 GeV supports the interpretation of partonic collectivity at higher energies, consistent with recombination model calculations using thermal strange quarks (Greco et al., 2003). The deviations at lower energies enable future studies with higher statistics to determine whether the change in φ-meson v_2 and the (Ω⁻ + Ω̅⁺)/2φ ratio at 11.5 GeV signals a shift from parton-dominated to hadron-dominated dynamics in the QCD phase diagram.
