# Diffractive structure functions in nuclei

> We calculate proton and nuclear diffractive structure functions in the IPsat (Kowalski-Teaney) dipole model. This parametrization has previously been shown to provide good agreement with inclusive F_2 measurements and exclusive vector meson measurements at HERA. We discuss how the impact parameter dependence crucially affects our analysis, in particular for small beta.

## Key claim

The paper calculates nuclear diffractive structure functions using the IPsat dipole model, showing that a realistic impact-parameter-dependent dipole cross section (rather than a factorized one) leads to different spatial distributions for the qq-bar and qq-bar-g components, which in turn affects the nuclear enhancement or suppression of diffractive structure functions depending on the kinematic region.

## Method

The authors decompose the diffractive structure function into transverse and longitudinal qq-bar components and a qq-bar-g component, using the IPsat dipole cross section parametrization which incorporates an eikonalized DGLAP-evolved gluon distribution with an impact-parameter profile. For the proton, the impact parameter profile is taken as a Gaussian exp(-b^2/(2 B_G)), while for nuclei the profile is a sum over nucleon positions drawn from a Woods-Saxon distribution. The qq-bar-g component is evaluated by interpolating between the small-beta large-N_c formula and the finite-beta large-Q^2 formula, and the authors fit the strong coupling constant alpha_s to HERA diffractive data.

## Key numbers

- The fit to combined ZEUS and H1 data (both with and without identified protons) yields chi^2/N_dof = 1.3 with alpha_s = 0.14 for the qq-bar-g term.
- The fit to only ZEUS LPS data gives chi^2 = 0.5 for IPsat, and to only H1 FPS data gives chi^2 = 0.8; considering only LPS data accommodates alpha_s = 0.21 with chi^2 < 1.
- For gold nuclei at Q^2 = 5 GeV^2 and x_P = 10^{-3}, the diffractive structure function in the "non breakup" (coherent) case is about 15% lower than in the "breakup" (incoherent) case.
- The expected fraction of diffractive events at an eA collider is 20% to 25%, compared to a typical ep fraction of 15%, based on a nuclear shadowing factor of ~0.8 for inclusive structure functions and a nuclear enhancement factor of ~1.2 for diffraction at moderate beta >= 0.2.

## Context

This work extends the IPsat dipole model, previously shown to agree with inclusive F_2 and exclusive vector meson measurements at HERA (Kowalski and Teaney, 2003; Kowalski et al., 2006), to nuclear diffractive structure functions. It contrasts with earlier calculations by Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff (1999) and Marquet (2007) that used a factorizable impact-parameter dependence, which forces the qq-bar-g component to have the same b-dependence as the qq-bar component; the present work shows that a realistic b-dependence leads to a smaller fitted alpha_s. The results enable predictions for future electron-ion colliders such as the EIC and LHeC, and the authors note that measuring coherent diffraction from an intact recoil nucleus is experimentally challenging but would provide a test of high-energy QCD.
