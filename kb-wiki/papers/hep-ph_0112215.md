# Polarization Phenomena in Fragmentation of Deuterons to Pions and Non-Nucleon Degrees of Freedom in the Deuteron

> The fragmentation of deuterons into pions emitted forward in the kinematic region forbidden for free nucleon-nucleon collisions is analyzed. It is shown that the inclusion of the non-nucleon degrees of freedom in a deuteron allows to describe the experimental data about inclusive pion spectrum rather satisfactory and improves the description of data concerning the deuteron analyzing power T_{20}. The experimental data show the positive sign and very small values, less than 0.2, of T_{20} what can't be reproduced by the calculations ignoring these degrees of freedom.

## Key claim

The inclusion of non-nucleon (quark) degrees of freedom in the deuteron wave function allows a satisfactory description of the inclusive pion spectrum and improves the description of the tensor analyzing power T_{20} in deuteron fragmentation to pions, but the experimental data show a positive sign and very small values (less than 0.2) of T_{20} that cannot be reproduced by calculations ignoring these degrees of freedom.

## Method

The authors analyze the inclusive process D p → π X for polarized deuterons at initial energies of a few GeV using a fully covariant Bethe-Salpeter formalism. They construct new S- and D-wave components of the deuteron wave function by including a non-nucleon component parameterized as a state of two colorless (3q) systems, with a mixing parameter a introduced to redistribute the wave function between the S- and D-waves while preserving the total probability density.

## Key numbers

- The probability α_{2(3q)} for a non-nucleon component in the deuteron is taken as 0.02 to 0.04 for the pion spectrum calculations (dot-dashed and dashed curves in Figure 1).
- The tensor analyzing power T_{20} is calculated with α_{2(3q)} = 0.03 and a mixing parameter a = 2.3, which gives an optimal description of the experimental data.
- Experimental data show T_{20} values less than 0.2 with positive sign at cumulative variable x_C ≥ 1.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Lykasov (1993) and Efremov (1988) on non-nucleon degrees of freedom in the deuteron, which was previously applied to inclusive proton spectra in deuteron fragmentation D p → p X. The analysis of T_{20} from D p → π X provides independent information on deuteron structure at small nucleon-nucleon distances, complementing earlier calculations using relativistic deuteron wave functions by Illarionov (2000) that could not reproduce the near-zero T_{20} values observed by Afanasiev (1998).
