# Bayesian Survey of the Dense Matter Equation of State built upon Skyrme effective interactions

> The non-relativistic model of nuclear matter with zero-range Skyrme interactions is employed within a Bayesian approach in order to study the behavior of neutron stars (NSs) equation of state (EOS). A minimal number of constraints from nuclear physics and ab initio calculations of pure neutron matter (PNM) are imposed together with causality and a lower limit on the maximum mass of NS to all our models. Our key result is that accounting for correlations among the values that the energy per neutron in PNM takes at various densities, and that are typically disregarded, efficiently constrains the behavior of the EOS at high densities. A series of global NS properties, e.g., maximum mass, central density of the maximum mass configuration, minimum NS mass that allows for direct URCA, radii of intermediate and massive NS, appear to be correlated with the value of effective neutron mass in PNM at 0.16 fm$^{-3}$. Together with similar studies in the literature our work contributes to a better understanding of the NS EOS as well as its link with the properties of dense nuclear matter.

## Key claim
Accounting for correlations among the energy per neutron in pure neutron matter at different densities, which are typically disregarded, efficiently constrains the behavior of the neutron star equation of state at high densities; global neutron star properties appear correlated with the value of the effective neutron mass in pure neutron matter at 0.16 fm⁻³.

## Method
The authors employed a non-relativistic mean-field model with standard Skyrme effective interactions, using a Bayesian approach to explore the parameter space. They imposed constraints from nuclear empirical parameters (saturation density, energy, compression modulus, symmetry energy), chiral effective field theory calculations of pure neutron matter energy per particle and Landau effective masses, a lower limit of 2.0 solar masses on the maximum neutron star mass, and causality. Five runs with different constraint sets were compared, including one run (run 0) that incorporated correlations among the pure neutron matter energy values at different densities via a covariance matrix computed from six Hamiltonians.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density: 0.16 ± 0.004 fm⁻³ (from Margueron 2018)
- Saturation energy: −15.9 ± 0.2 MeV (from Margueron 2018)
- Compression modulus at saturation: 240 ± 30 MeV (from Margueron 2018)
- Symmetry energy at saturation: 30.8 ± 1.6 MeV (from Margueron 2018)
- Energy per particle in pure neutron matter at 0.04 fm⁻³: 6.165 ± 0.059 MeV (from Drischler 2021)
- Energy per particle in pure neutron matter at 0.08 fm⁻³: 9.212 ± 0.226 MeV (from Drischler 2021)
- Energy per particle in pure neutron matter at 0.12 fm⁻³: 12.356 ± 0.512 MeV (from Drischler 2021)
- Energy per particle in pure neutron matter at 0.16 fm⁻³: 15.877 ± 0.872 MeV (from Drischler 2021)
- Neutron Landau effective mass in pure neutron matter at 0.16 fm⁻³: 0.880 ± 0.026 m_n (from Drischler 2021)
- Nucleon Landau effective mass in symmetric nuclear matter at 0.16 fm⁻³: 0.638 ± 0.013 m_N (from Drischler 2021)
- Lower limit on maximum gravitational mass: > 2.0 solar masses (from Fonseca 2021)

## Context
This work extends the Bayesian inference approach of Beznogov (2023) by employing Skyrme effective interactions rather than covariant density functionals, and by incorporating correlations among pure neutron matter energy values from chiral effective field theory calculations by Drischler (2021). It complements studies using piece-wise polytropes (Fasano 2019, Raaijmakers 2020), spectral parameterizations (Dietrich 2020), and other phenomenological models (Malik 2022, Ghosh 2022, Huang 2023, Papakonstantinou 2023). The results enable comparison of model-dependent predictions for neutron star properties and can be checked a posteriori against NICER and GW170817 constraints.
