# Ingredients of nuclear matrix element for two-neutrino double-beta decay of 48Ca

> Large-scale shell model calculations including two major shells are carried out, and the ingredients of nuclear matrix element for two-neutrino double beta decay are investigated. Based on the comparison between the shell model calculations accounting only for one major shell ($pf$-shell) and those for two major shells ($sdpf$-shell), the effect due to the excitation across the two major shells is quantitatively evaluated.

# Ingredients of nuclear matrix element for two-neutrino double-beta decay of 48Ca

## Key claim

Large-scale shell model calculations including two major shells (sdpf-shell) provide a better description of Gamow-Teller transition strengths around 48Ca than one-major-shell calculations, but significant discrepancies with experimental data remain in high-energy regions, particularly for the GT+ transition above 7.5 MeV excitation energy, indicating that the effective nuclear interaction (SDPF-MU) is still insufficient within the truncated model space.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell model calculations using the KSHELL code, comparing results from the one-major-shell GXPF1A interaction (pf-shell only) with those from the two-major-shell SDPF-MU interaction (sdpf-shell including sd and pf shells). Due to computational limitations, the two-major-shell calculations employed the Lanczos strength function method with a truncated model space limited to 2-hbar-omega type excitations (SDPFMU-2-hbar-omega), reducing the m-scheme dimension from over 10^10 to approximately 10^9. The nuclear matrix element for two-neutrino double-beta decay was computed from the sum over intermediate 1+ states in 48Sc using the Gamow-Teller transition operator with an effective quenching factor q = 0.77.

## Key numbers

- Experimental half-life of two-neutrino double-beta decay of 48Ca: (4.3 ± 2.3) × 10^19 yr (evaluated 2006)
- Nuclear matrix element extracted from experiment: M^(2nu)(GT) = 0.0560 ± 0.0162 MeV^-1
- Nuclear matrix element from one-major-shell calculation (GXPF1A): 0.0539 MeV^-1
- Phase space factor used: G^(2nu) = 1.044 × 10^-17 yr^-1 MeV^2 (from Suhonen, 1998)
- Q-value of double-beta decay: Q_ββ = 4.26698 ± 0.00038 MeV (from AME2012)
- Effective GT operator quenching factor: q = 0.77
- Excitation energies of 0+_2 states from SDPFMU-2-hbar-omega calculations: 5.099 MeV for 48Ca, 4.097 MeV for 48Ti; experimental values: 4.283 MeV for 48Ca, 2.997 MeV for 48Ti
- Excitation energies of 0+_2 states from GXPF1A calculations: 5.275 MeV for 48Ca, 4.048 MeV for 48Ti
- Number of excited protons and neutrons across major shells for 48Ca (0+_1): 0.17 protons, 0.11 neutrons; for 48Ca (0+_2): 0.22 protons, 0.19 neutrons

## Context

This work extends previous one-major-shell shell model calculations by Horoi (2007) that reproduced the half-life of 48Ca two-neutrino double-beta decay but underestimated Gamow-Teller transition strengths measured by Yako et al. (2009). The authors aim to establish a shell model framework for ultimately calculating the neutrino-less double-beta decay matrix element with high accuracy. The study reveals that while including two major shells improves agreement with experiment for GT- transitions in the 12.5-15.0 MeV region, significant discrepancies persist for GT+ transitions above 7.5 MeV, motivating further refinement of the effective nuclear interaction.
