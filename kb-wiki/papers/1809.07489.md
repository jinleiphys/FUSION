# New narrow nucleon resonances $N^{\ast}(1685)$ and $N^{\ast}(1726)$ within the chiral quark-soliton model

> We investigate the strong and radiative decay widths of the narrow nucleon resonances $N^*(1685)$ and $N^{\ast}(1726)$ within the framework of the SU(3) chiral quark-soliton model. All the relevant parameters are taken from those used to describe the properties of the baryon octet and decuplet in previous works. The masses of the antidecuplet nucleon and the eikosiheptaplet (27-plet) nucleon with spin 3/2 are determined respectively to be $(1690.2\pm 10.5)\, \mathrm{MeV}$ and $(1719.6\pm7.4)\,\mathrm{MeV}$. The decay width for $N^{\ast}(1685)\to η+ N$ is found to be approximately three times larger than that for $N^{\ast}(1685)\to π+N$. The width of the decay $N^{\ast}\left(1726\right)3/2^+\to η+N$ is even about 31 times larger than that of $N^{\ast}\left(1726\right)3/2^+\to π+ N$. The ratio of the radiative decays for $N^*(1685)$ is obtained to be $Γ_{nn^*(1685)}/Γ_{pp^*(1685)}=8.62\pm3.45$ which explains very well the neutron anomaly. In contrast, we find $Γ_{pp^*(1726)}/Γ_{nn^*(1726)}=3.72\pm0.64$, which indicates that the production of $N^*(1726)$ is more likely to be observed in the proton channel. We also examined the decay modes of these narrow nucleon resonances with the strangeness hadrons involved.

## Key claim

The narrow nucleon resonances $N^*(1685)$ and $N^*(1726)$ can be identified as members of the baryon antidecuplet and eikosiheptaplet (27-plet), respectively, within the chiral quark-soliton model, with all parameters fixed from previous descriptions of the baryon octet and decuplet without any additional adjustable parameters.

## Method

The authors used the SU(3) chiral quark-soliton model ($\chi$QSM) with collective quantization to compute the masses, strong decay widths, and radiative decay widths of the antidecuplet nucleon $N_{\overline{10}}$ and the eikosiheptaplet nucleon $N_{27}$ with spin 3/2. The collective Hamiltonian included rotational, isospin breaking, and SU(3) symmetry breaking terms, with all dynamical parameters ($\alpha$, $\beta$, $\gamma$, $a_i$, $w_i$) taken from previous works that reproduced the baryon octet and decuplet masses. The wavefunctions included mixing between representations due to SU(3) symmetry breaking, and electromagnetic self-energies were incorporated using the operator formalism from Yang et al. (2010).

## Key numbers

- Mass of the antidecuplet nucleon $N_{\overline{10}}$: $1690.2 \pm 10.5$ MeV (average of proton and neutron)
- Mass of the eikosiheptaplet nucleon $N_{27}$ with spin 3/2: $1719.6 \pm 7.4$ MeV (average of proton and neutron)
- Mass of the eikosiheptaplet nucleon $N_{27}$ with spin 1/2: $2116.6 \pm 17.0$ MeV (average of proton and neutron)
- Ratio of strong decay widths for $N^*(1685)$: $\Gamma(N^*(1685) \to \eta N) / \Gamma(N^*(1685) \to \pi N) \approx 3$ (approximately three times larger for the $\eta$ channel)
- Ratio of strong decay widths for $N^*(1726) 3/2^+$: $\Gamma(N^*(1726) \to \eta N) / \Gamma(N^*(1726) \to \pi N) \approx 31$ (about 31 times larger for the $\eta$ channel)
- Ratio of radiative decay widths for $N^*(1685)$: $\Gamma_{nn^*(1685)} / \Gamma_{pp^*(1685)} = 8.62 \pm 3.45$ (neutron channel strongly favored)
- Ratio of radiative decay widths for $N^*(1726)$: $\Gamma_{pp^*(1726)} / \Gamma_{nn^*(1726)} = 3.72 \pm 0.64$ (proton channel favored)
- Predicted masses of the antidecuplet proton and neutron: $p_{\overline{10}} = 1688.2 \pm 10.5$ MeV, $n_{\overline{10}} = 1692.2 \pm 10.5$ MeV
- Predicted masses of the eikosiheptaplet (spin 3/2) proton and neutron: $p_{27} = 1718.6 \pm 7.4$ MeV, $n_{27} = 1720.6 \pm 7.4$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral quark-soliton model predictions for baryon antidecuplet properties from Polyakov and Rathke (2003) and Kim et al. (2005, 2007), and for eikosiheptaplet masses from Praszalowicz and Goeke (2007), by providing quantitative calculations of both strong and radiative decay widths without additional free parameters. The results explain the "neutron anomaly" observed by Kuznetsov et al. (2006, 2008) in $\eta$ photoproduction off the neutron, where $N^*(1685)$ appears strongly in the neutron channel but weakly in the proton channel, and predict a "proton anomaly" for $N^*(1726)$ where the opposite behavior occurs. The work enables future experimental searches at facilities such as J-PARC to identify these narrow resonances in $\eta$ photoproduction and strangeness channels, and provides testable predictions for the decay modes of the eikosiheptaplet nucleon with spin 3/2.
