# v4: A small, but sensitive observable for heavy ion collisions

> Higher order Fourier coefficients of the azimuthally dependent single particle spectra resulting from noncentral heavy ion collisions are investigated. For intermediate to large transverse momenta, these anisotropies are expected to become as large as 5 %, and should be clearly measurable. The physics content of these observables is discussed from two different extreme but complementary viewpoints, hydrodynamics and the geometric limit with extreme energy loss.

## Key claim

The fourth Fourier coefficient \(v_4\) of the azimuthal particle distribution in noncentral heavy ion collisions is expected to reach values as large as 5% at intermediate to large transverse momenta, making it clearly measurable; however, the paper qualifies that this estimate comes from two extreme and complementary models (hydrodynamics and a geometric opacity limit), and the actual value is highly sensitive to the initial conditions of the system.

## Method

The paper uses two complementary theoretical approaches: (1) a hydrodynamic calculation with a first-order phase transition at \(T_{\rm crit}=165\) MeV, initialized to fit single-particle spectra from Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200\) GeV, to compute Fourier coefficients \(v_n\) for pions and protons up to order 8; (2) a geometric model assuming extreme jet quenching (opacity) where particles are emitted only within \(\pm 90^\circ\) of the surface normal of the deformed overlap region of two hard spheres, yielding analytic expressions for \(v_n\) as functions of impact parameter. The time evolution of flow anisotropy coefficients \(\delta_n\) in the transverse velocity field is also computed from the hydrodynamic model.

## Key numbers

- For pions at \(p_T=2\) GeV in a hydrodynamic calculation at impact parameter \(b=7\) fm, \(v_4\) reaches about 2.8%, while \(v_2\) is about 29% and \(v_6\) is about -1.2%.
- For (anti-)protons at \(p_T=4\) GeV in the same hydrodynamic calculation, \(v_4\) is expected to be about 7%.
- In the geometric opacity model, the limiting value of \(v_4\) for \(b \rightarrow 2R\) (most peripheral collisions) is \(v_4 = -1/15 \approx -6.67\%\), with a local maximum at \(b \approx 0.865 R\) giving \(v_4 \approx 1.45\%\).
- The momentum-integrated value of \(v_4\) from an earlier hydrodynamic calculation at SPS energies was predicted not to exceed 0.5%.
- The hydrodynamic calculation shows that about 2/3 of the final \(\delta_4\) value is generated during the earliest stages of evolution (within the quark-gluon plasma phase).

## Context

This work extends earlier hydrodynamic calculations of elliptic flow (\(v_2\)) by Kolb, Sollfrank, and Heinz (1999) and Teaney and Shuryak (1999), which had predicted small momentum-integrated \(v_4\) values, by showing that \(v_4\) becomes sizable at intermediate and high transverse momenta. It also introduces a geometric opacity model following Voloshin (2003) for \(v_2\) and extends it to higher harmonics. The paper enables experimental searches for \(v_4\) at RHIC, proposing a new measurement method using azimuthal spectra at angles 0, \(\pi/2\), and \(\pi/4\) that enhances the \(v_4\) signal by a factor of 8, and it highlights \(v_4\) as a sensitive probe of initial conditions and the equation of state in heavy ion collisions.
