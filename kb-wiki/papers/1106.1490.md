# Rotational states in deformed nuclei: An analytic approach

> The consequences of the spontaneous breaking of rotational symmetry are investigated in a field theory model for deformed nuclei, based on simple separable interactions. The crucial role of the Ward-Takahashi identities to describe the rotational states is emphasized. We show explicitly how the rotor picture emerges from the isoscalar Goldstone modes, and how the two-rotor model emerges from the isovector scissors modes. As an application of the formalism, we discuss the M1 sum rules in deformed nuclei, and make connection to empirical information.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates analytically that the rotor picture for isoscalar rotational bands and the two-rotor model for isovector scissors modes emerge directly from the Ward-Takahashi identities of spontaneously broken rotational symmetry in a separable-interaction field theory, without requiring explicit single-particle wave functions or model-space truncations for the isoscalar case.

## Method

The authors construct a field-theory model with separable quadrupole-quadrupole (QQ) interactions between protons and neutrons, treat the mean field in a Hartree-type approximation that spontaneously breaks rotational symmetry, and formulate the Bethe-Salpeter (RPA) equation for particle-hole excitations in the K=1 channel. They derive Ward-Takahashi identities from angular-momentum conservation and use them to relate the Goldstone pole at zero energy to the self-consistency conditions for the deformed mean field, and to obtain low-energy theorems for the Q-L correlator. The vertex functions for collective states are extracted from the pole structure of the T-matrix, and transition matrix elements are computed via the spectral representation of the correlators.

## Key numbers

* The Inglis formula for the moment of inertia: \( I_{\tau} = 2 \sum_{(\alpha i) \in \tau} \frac{|\langle \alpha | L^1_{\tau} | i \rangle|^2}{\omega_{\alpha i}} \) is derived for protons and neutrons separately, with total moment of inertia \( I = I_p + I_n \).
* The Goldstone vertex function normalization gives \( N = \sqrt{3/I} \), leading to \( N_{\tau}(0) = \sqrt{3/I} \, \beta_{\tau} \).
* The transition matrix element for the Goldstone mode satisfies \( \langle \omega_0, K=1 | L^1_{\tau} | 0 \rangle = \sqrt{\omega_0/(2I)} \, I_{\tau} \) in the limit \( \omega_0 \rightarrow 0 \), and summing over nucleons gives \( \langle \omega_0, K=1 | L^1 | 0 \rangle = \sqrt{\omega_0 I/2} \).
* The pole equation for collective states is \( \text{Det}(1 + \chi \pi(\omega)) = 0 \), where \( \pi_{\tau}(\omega) = -2 \sum_{(\alpha i) \in \tau} |\langle \alpha | Q^1_{\tau} | i \rangle|^2 \frac{\omega_{\alpha i}}{\omega^2 - \omega_{\alpha i}^2 + i\delta} \).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical foundation of rotational states in deformed nuclei beyond the standard mean-field and RPA treatments (e.g., Rowe (1970), Ring and Schuck (1980)) by providing an analytic derivation of the rotor and two-rotor pictures using Ward-Takahashi identities, which had previously been applied mainly to infinite systems (Nambu and Jona-Lasinio). The formalism connects to the earlier work on self-consistency relations by Iudice and collaborators and to the two-rotor model of Lo Iudice and Palumbo. The paper enables a transparent analytic understanding of M1 sum rules in deformed nuclei and their relation to empirical scissors-mode data, without presenting numerical results, and suggests extension to more general interactions and inclusion of pairing.
