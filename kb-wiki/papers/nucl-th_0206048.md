# Heuristic Models of Two-Fermion Relativistic Systems with Field-Type Interaction

> We use the chain of simple heuristic expedients to obtain perturbative and exactly solvable relativistic spectra for a family of two-fermionic bound systems with Coulomb-like interaction. In the case of electromagnetic interaction the spectrum coincides up to the second order in a coupling constant with that following from the quantum electrodynamics. Discrepancy occurs only for S-states which is the well-known difficulty in the bound-state problem. The confinement interaction is considered too. PACS number(s): 03.65.Pm, 03.65.Ge, 12.39.Pn

## Key claim

The paper constructs relativistic wave equations for two-fermion bound systems with Coulomb-like interactions that reproduce quantum electrodynamics (QED) results up to second order in the coupling constant, except for S-states where the well-known difficulty in the bound-state problem persists. The heuristic models also extend to confinement interactions, yielding approximate Regge trajectories for light mesons.

## Method

The author starts from the Todorov quasipotential approach for spinless particles, which maps the two-body problem to an effective single-particle Klein-Gordon equation via substitutions for energy and mass. For two-fermion systems, spin-dependent corrections (spin-orbit, spin-spin, and tensor terms) are incorporated from the Breit-Fermi Hamiltonian for scalar, vector, and gravitational interactions. These spin terms are treated as perturbations to the effective Coulomb Hamiltonian, and the first-order perturbation theory yields mass spectra; alternatively, the spin corrections are transformed into \(1/r^2\) terms to construct an exactly solvable model. For confinement, the Todorov recipe is applied to a linear scalar potential combined with a Coulomb vector potential, and the resulting equation is solved in the oscillator approximation for large angular momentum \(\ell\).

## Key numbers

- For the vector (electromagnetic) interaction, the mass spectrum up to \(\alpha^4\) is given by \(M \approx m_+ - \frac{m_r\alpha^2}{2n^2} + \frac{m_r\alpha^4}{2n^4}\left[f'(1)-\frac14 -\frac{m_r}{4m_+}\right] + \frac{m_r\alpha^4}{n^3}\frac{\gamma}{2\ell+1}\), where \(m_+ = m_1+m_2\), \(m_r\) is the reduced mass, \(n\) is the principal quantum number, and \(\gamma_{\rm v} = -1\).
- For the scalar interaction, \(\lambda_{\rm s} = \sqrt{1 - \alpha^2/\nu^2}\) with \(\nu = n_r + \frac12 + \sqrt{(\ell+\frac12)^2 + \alpha^2\gamma_{\rm s}}\) and \(\gamma_{\rm s}=1\).
- For the gravitational interaction, \(\lambda_{\rm g} = \frac1{2\sqrt{2}} \sqrt{4 - \frac{\nu^2}{\alpha^2} + \frac{\nu}{\alpha}\sqrt{8 + \nu^2/\alpha^2}}\) with \(\gamma_{\rm g} = -6\).
- For the confinement model with linear potential \(V(r)=ar\) and zero rest masses, the exact mass spectrum is \(M^2 = 8a\left[\ell + 2n_r + \frac32\right]\).
- For the confinement model with non-zero masses and Coulomb term, the asymptotic spectrum for large \(\ell\) is \(M^2 = 8a\left[\ell + 2n_r + \frac32 - \sqrt2\alpha\right] + 2\left(m_1^2 + m_2^2 + \sqrt2 m_1 m_2\right) + O(\ell^{-1})\).

## Context

This work extends the Todorov quasipotential approach (Todorov 1971, Rizov et al. 1985) from spinless particles to two-fermion systems, incorporating spin effects from the Breit-Fermi Hamiltonian (Ohta and Maekawa 1983, Lucha and Schoberl 1989) and from gravitational interaction (Barker et al. 1966). The perturbative results reproduce the muonium spectrum (Herzog 1992) and positronium spectrum (Berestetskii et al. 1971) up to \(\alpha^4\). The confinement model connects to the covariant oscillator model (Takamura 1979) and provides a framework for describing light meson spectra, including Regge trajectories, with adjustable parameters for different meson families.
