# Perturbation Theory of Nuclear Matter with a Microscopic Effective Interaction

> An updated and improved version of the effective interaction based on the Argonne\textendash Urbana nuclear Hamiltonian\textemdash derived using the formalism of Correlated Basis Functions (CBF) and the cluster expansion technique\textemdash is employed to obtain a number of properties of cold nuclear matter at arbitrary neutron excess within the formalism of many-body perturbation theory. The numerical results\textemdash including the ground-state energy per nucleon, the symmetry energy, the pressure, the compressibility, and the single-particle spectrum\textemdash are discussed in the context of the available empirical information, obtained from measured nuclear properties and heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that an improved effective interaction derived from correlated basis functions (CBF) and the cluster expansion technique, which simultaneously describes both symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter, can be used in first-order perturbation theory to compute nuclear matter properties at arbitrary neutron excess, with results compatible with empirical data at most densities.

## Method

The authors construct an effective interaction from the Argonne AV6P nucleon-nucleon potential and Urbana IX three-nucleon force using correlated basis functions and the cluster expansion technique, with correlation functions adjusted so that the first-order Hartree-Fock energy reproduces the variational FHNC/SOC ground-state energy for both symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter simultaneously. They then compute the ground-state energy per nucleon, symmetry energy, pressure, compressibility, and single-particle spectra using first-order perturbation theory (Hartree-Fock approximation) with this effective interaction for nuclear matter at arbitrary proton fractions.

## Key numbers

- Equilibrium density of symmetric nuclear matter: \(\rho_0 \approx 0.16\ {\rm fm}^{-3}\), with binding energy \(\sim 11\) MeV (below the empirical value of 16 MeV)
- Symmetry energy at equilibrium density: \(E_{\rm sym}(\rho_0) = 30.9\) MeV, compatible with the survey value of \(31.6\pm2.66\) MeV
- Slope parameter of symmetry energy: \(L = 67.9\) MeV, compatible with the survey value of \(58.9\pm16\) MeV
- Compressibility modulus: \(K_0 \approx 200\) MeV, compared to the empirical value of \(240 \pm 20\) MeV
- The quadratic approximation for the energy as a function of neutron excess shows deviations less than 3% over the whole proton fraction range at \(\rho = \rho_0\)

## Context

This work extends the CBF perturbation theory approach of Cowell (2004) and Lovato (2012, 2013) by constructing an effective interaction that simultaneously describes both symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter, enabling calculations at arbitrary neutron excess for astrophysical applications such as neutron star modeling. The results are compared to empirical constraints from heavy-ion collision flow data of Danielewicz (2002), the ASY-EOS collaboration results of Russotto (2016), and nuclear structure analyses of Tsang (2012), Brown (2000), and Zhang (2013), showing general compatibility while noting that the non-relativistic formalism will predict causality violation at high density.
