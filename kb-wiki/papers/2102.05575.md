# Sequential single pion production explaining the dibaryon "$d^*(2380)$" peak

> We study the two step sequential one pion production mechanism, $np(I=0)\to π^-pp$, followed by the fusion reaction $pp\to π^+d$, in order to describe the $np\to π^+π^-d$ reaction with $π^+π^-$ in $I=0$, where a narrow peak, so far identified with a "$d(2380)$" dibaryon, has been observed. We find that the second step $pp\to π^+d$ is driven by a triangle singularity that determines the position of the peak of the reaction and the large strength of the cross section. The combined cross section of these two mechanisms produce a narrow peak with the position, width and strength compatible with the experimental observation within the approximations done. This novel interpretation of the peak without invoking a dibaryon explains why the peak is not observed in other reactions where it has been searched for.

## Key claim
The narrow peak in the $np\to\pi^+\pi^-d$ reaction previously attributed to the $d^*(2380)$ dibaryon can be explained by a conventional two-step sequential single-pion production mechanism ($np(I=0)\to \pi^-pp$ followed by $pp\to \pi^+d$), where the second step is driven by a triangle singularity that determines the peak position and large cross section strength, without invoking any dibaryon resonance.

## Method
The authors factorize the two-step process using experimental cross sections for $np(I=0)\to \pi^-pp$ (from WASA-at-COSY data) and $pp\to \pi^+d$ (from Serre data), employing an on-shell approximation for the intermediate propagator that is justified by chiral unitary approaches. They parameterize the measured cross sections with Breit-Wigner forms, then combine them via a formula that relates the $np\to\pi^+\pi^-d$ cross section to the product of the two sub-reaction cross sections, accounting for phase space factors and isospin relations. Off-shell effects are evaluated by replacing the on-shell delta function with the full loop integral $G$ function, regularized with a cutoff $p_{1,\mathrm{max}}$ in the range 700-800 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Peak strength of $\sigma_{np\to\pi^+\pi^-d}$: 0.72-0.96 mb (depending on parameter set and off-shell cutoff), compared to experimental value around 0.5 mb
- Peak position: 2332-2345 MeV, compared to experimental peak around 2365 MeV (within the experimental resolution of about 20 MeV in $\sqrt{s}$)
- Width of the peak: 75-88 MeV, compared to experimental width of 70-75 MeV
- Triangle singularity in $pp\to\pi^+d$ appears at $M_{\mathrm{inv}}(pp)\sim 2179$ MeV, where $\tilde{p}_1=552$ MeV/c
- Parameters for $np(I=0)\to NN\pi$ fit Set I: $\tilde{M}_1=2326$ MeV, $\tilde{\Gamma}_1=70$ MeV; Set II: $\tilde{M}_2=2335$ MeV, $\tilde{\Gamma}_2=80$ MeV
- Parameters for $pp\to\pi^+d$ fit: $\tilde{M}_3=2165$ MeV, $\tilde{\Gamma}_3=123.27$ MeV

## Context
This work extends the earlier sequential mechanism proposed by Bar-Nir (1973) for the $np\to\pi^+\pi^-d$ reaction, which had been abandoned in favor of the dibaryon interpretation after improved data from WASA-at-COSY (Bashkanov, Adlarson, Adlardos, 2009-2014). The authors show that the $pp\to\pi^+d$ subprocess is driven by a triangle singularity (Landau, 1959; Bayar and Guo, 2018), explaining its large cross section without requiring a dibaryon. This interpretation explains why the narrow peak is not observed in reactions like $\gamma d\to\pi^0\pi^0d$ (Guenther, 2020), where the deuteron is already present initially and the mechanism is fundamentally different. The work enables a conventional understanding of the $np\to\pi\pi d$ peak and suggests that the $d^*(2380)$ interpretation is unnecessary, though the peak may still affect $NN$ phase shifts as discussed by Colin (2014) and Miguel (2015).
