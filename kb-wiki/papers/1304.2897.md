# Nuclear in-medium and isospin effects on subthreshold kaon production in heavy-ion collisions

> Subthreshold kaon (K$^{0}$ and K$^{+}$) production in neutron-rich nuclear reactions to probe the kaon-nucleon interaction in nuclear medium and to extract the isospin dependent part of the nuclear equation of state at high-baryon densities, is investigated within an isospin and momentum dependent transport model. A repulsive kaon-nucleon potential is implemented in the model through fitting the flow data and inclusive spectra in heavy-ion collisions, which enhances the energetic kaon emission squeezed out in the reaction zone and leads to a variation of the high-momentum spectrum of the K$^{0}$/K$^{+}$ yields. It is found that the stiffness of nuclear symmetry energy plays a significant role on the isospin ratio with decreasing the incident energy and a hard symmetry energy has a larger value of the K$^{0}$/K$^{+}$ ratio in the domain of subthreshold energies.

# Nuclear in-medium and isospin effects on subthreshold kaon production in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
Subthreshold kaon production in neutron-rich heavy-ion collisions can probe both the repulsive kaon-nucleon potential in nuclear medium and the high-density behavior of the nuclear symmetry energy, but the sensitivity of the K⁰/K⁺ ratio to the symmetry energy stiffness is pronounced only in heavy neutron-rich systems at subthreshold energies, while the double ratio from different systems shows very weak sensitivity.

## Method
The Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics (LQMD) transport model, an isospin- and momentum-dependent approach, was used to simulate central ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au collisions at incident energies from 0.8 to 2.0 A GeV. Kaon production was computed from baryon-baryon (BB→BYK, BB→BBK¯K) and pion-baryon (Bπ→YK) channels, with a repulsive kaon-nucleon potential implemented via chiral Lagrangian parameters (b_K=0.32 GeV fm³, a_K=0.18 GeV² fm³) giving a repulsive KN potential of 25.5 MeV at saturation density. Two forms of symmetry energy were compared: a hard case (γ_s=2) and a supersoft case (Eq. 7), both crossing at 31.5 MeV at saturation density.

## Key numbers
- Repulsive kaon-nucleon potential strength: 25.5 MeV at saturation baryon density (ρ₀=0.16 fm⁻³)
- Attractive antikaon-nucleon potential strength: -96.8 MeV at saturation density
- Effective kaon mass ratio in medium: m*_K/m_K = 1.05 at normal baryon density
- Inclusion of KN potential leads to approximately 30% reduction of total kaon yields in the subthreshold domain
- Compression modulus of isospin symmetric nuclear matter: K=230 MeV
- Effective nucleon mass ratio: m*/m = 0.75 at saturation density for symmetric nuclear matter
- Subthreshold kaon production threshold energy: E_th(K) = 1.58 GeV

## Context
This work extends prior transport model studies of kaon production as a probe of the nuclear equation of state, which had already favored a soft symmetric EoS at high densities (Li 1997, Fuchs 2001, Hartnack 2006). The K⁰/K⁺ ratio was previously proposed as a sensitive probe of the high-density symmetry energy (Feng 2006, Prassa 2010). The results are compared with experimental data from the KaoS collaboration (Fo07) and with relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (RBUU) calculations (Lo07). The findings enable future experimental constraints on the high-density symmetry energy through precise measurements of subthreshold kaon isospin ratios in neutron-rich heavy systems.
