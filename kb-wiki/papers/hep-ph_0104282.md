# Phases of QCD with nonvanishing isopin density

> The propagation of low-momentum baryons through QCD matter at low isospin density is studied using chiral perturbation theory. When the isospin chemical potential exceeds a critical value, the dispersion relation for the lowest nucleon mode becomes anomalous near zero momentum---increasing momentum yields decreasing energy---so that the momentum for the lowest energy state, p_min, becomes nonzero. This can be interpreted as a new phase of QCD, with p_min serving as the order parameter and the kinetic mass (defined as the inverse of the second derivative of the energy with respect to the momentum at p_min) serving as the susceptibility. A lowest-order chiral-perturbation-theory calculation yields a critical isospin chemical potential of 285 MeV. Since this is small compared to the chiral-symmetry-breaking scale, corrections to it are likely to be modest.

# Phases of QCD with nonvanishing isospin density

## Key claim

Using lowest-order chiral perturbation theory, the authors predict a new phase of QCD at isospin chemical potentials above approximately 285 MeV, where the nucleon dispersion relation becomes anomalous (energy decreases with increasing momentum at low momenta) and the minimum-energy state occurs at nonzero momentum. The authors note that chiral corrections of order Q^2/Lambda^2 may alter this critical value by roughly 20 percent, though they argue corrections are likely modest since the critical value is small compared to the chiral-symmetry-breaking scale.

## Method

The authors study nucleon propagation through pion-condensed QCD matter using chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) at lowest order, incorporating the isospin chemical potential as a time-like gauge field coupled to the conserved vector-isovector current. They solve the Dirac equation for nucleons in the background of the pion condensate, using the standard nonlinear realization of chiral symmetry for the pion-nucleon Lagrangian, and compute the nucleon dispersion relation as a function of isospin chemical potential. The ground state of the pion field is determined by minimizing the effective potential from the purely pionic Lagrangian, yielding a chiral rotation angle alpha that depends on mu_I.

## Key numbers

- Critical isospin chemical potential mu_cr = 285 MeV for the transition to the anomalous dispersion phase, obtained from the equation 2 M_N m_pi^2 mu_cr / ((1 - g_A^2) m_pi^4 + g_A^2 mu_cr^4) = 1
- Pion mass m_pi = 139 MeV and pion decay constant f_pi = 93 MeV used as input parameters
- Pion condensation transition occurs at mu_I = m_pi = 139 MeV, below the critical value for the new phase
- The critical chemical potential mu_cr is approximately 2 m_pi, well below the nucleon mass M_N
- Leading corrections from higher-order terms in the pion-nucleon Lagrangian (the c_i low-energy constants) contribute approximately 58 +/- 1 MeV at mu = mu_cr but are momentum-independent and thus do not affect mu_cr

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Son and Stephanov (2001), who studied QCD at nonzero isospin density using chiral perturbation theory and identified the pion-condensed phase. While Son and Stephanov considered the nucleon mass defined at zero momentum, the present authors show that the full dispersion relation reveals a new phase where the minimum-energy nucleon state occurs at nonzero momentum. The kinetic mass diverges at the critical point like a susceptibility, and p_min serves as an order parameter, making the transition appear second-order. The authors also discuss implications for neutron matter, finding that in a mean-field Fermi gas model, s-wave pion condensation (and thus the new phase) does not occur in neutron stars at physical pion mass. This work enables future lattice QCD studies at finite isospin density, which are feasible because the Euclidean functional determinant is real and positive, unlike the case of finite baryon density.
