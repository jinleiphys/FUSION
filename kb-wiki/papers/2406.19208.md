# Study of the $Ω_{ccc}Ω_{ccc}$ and $Ω_{bbb}Ω_{bbb}$ dibaryons in constituent quark model

> Dibaryons are the simplest system in which the baryon-baryon interaction, and hence the underlying quark-quark interaction, can be studied in a clear way. Although the only dibaryon known today is the deuteron (and possibly the $d^*$), fully heavy dibaryons are good candidates for bound states because in such systems the kinetic energy is small and the high symmetry of the wave function favours binding. In this study, the possible existence of $Ω_{ccc}Ω_{ccc}$ and $Ω_{bbb}Ω_{bbb}$ dibaryons is investigated in the framework of a constituent quark model that satisfactorily describes the deuteron, the $d^*(2380)$ and the $NN$ interaction. $J^P=0^+$ candidates are found in both systems with binding energies of the order of MeV.

## Key claim

Using a constituent quark model that successfully describes the deuteron and the \(d^*(2380)\), the authors find that both \(\Omega_{ccc}\Omega_{ccc}\) and \(\Omega_{bbb}\Omega_{bbb}\) systems form \(J^P=0^+\) bound states with binding energies of the order of MeV, but only in the \(0^+\) channel; no other partial waves bind.

## Method

The authors employ a QCD-inspired constituent quark model with one-gluon exchange and a screened confinement potential, previously applied to light and heavy hadrons. They compute the \(\Omega_{QQQ}\) baryon wave functions using a single-Gaussian variational ansatz that minimizes the baryon mass, then derive the baryon-baryon interaction via the Resonating Group Method (RGM), where the direct kernel vanishes for color-singlet baryons and only exchange diagrams contribute. The coupled-channel Schrodinger equation is solved for the \(^1S_0\) and \(^5D_0\) partial waves, including the tensor interaction from one-gluon exchange.

## Key numbers

- \(\Omega_{bbb}\Omega_{bbb}\) binding energy: \(E = -1.9876\) MeV (coupled \(^1S_0\) and \(^5D_0\) channels, with minimization procedure parameters); \(D\)-wave probability \(6.6 \times 10^{-4}\%\).
- \(\Omega_{ccc}\Omega_{ccc}\) binding energy: \(E = -0.7104\) MeV (coupled channels, minimization \(b\) and GEM reduced mass); \(D\)-wave probability \(1.7 \times 10^{-3}\%\).
- \(\Omega_{bbb}\) baryon mass: 14413.8 MeV (variational) vs 14396.9 MeV (GEM); \(\sqrt{\langle r_{ij}^2 \rangle} = 0.2716\) fm (variational) vs 0.2762 fm (GEM).
- \(\Omega_{ccc}\) baryon mass: 4810.9 MeV (variational) vs 4798.6 MeV (GEM); \(\sqrt{\langle r_{ij}^2 \rangle} = 0.4360\) fm (variational) vs 0.4432 fm (GEM).
- For \(\Omega_{bbb}\Omega_{bbb}\), kinetic energy \(\langle T \rangle = 11.2\) MeV and interaction \(\langle V \rangle = -13.2\) MeV; for \(\Omega_{ccc}\Omega_{ccc}\), \(\langle T \rangle = 7.46\) MeV and \(\langle V \rangle = -8.17\) MeV.
- Without tensor interaction, the \(\Omega_{bbb}\Omega_{bbb}\) \(^1S_0\) binds at \(E = -1.9859\) MeV; excluding OGE entirely gives \(E = -7.3698\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model of Fernandez (1993) and Vijande (2004), which successfully describes the deuteron and predicts the \(d^*(2380)\) as a \(\Delta\Delta\) dibaryon, to the fully heavy dibaryon sector. It addresses conflicting results in the literature: lattice QCD calculations by Lyu (2021) found \(\Omega_{ccc}\Omega_{ccc}\) bound by 5.68 MeV, while Mathur (2023) found \(\Omega_{bbb}\Omega_{bbb}\) bound by 81 MeV; quark model studies by Huang (2020) found binding energies of 2.5 MeV and 0.9 MeV respectively, but Richard (2020) and Lu (2022) found no bound states. The present results support the existence of loosely bound molecular-type dibaryons, with binding energies smaller than lattice predictions, and enable future experimental searches at facilities such as LHCb.
