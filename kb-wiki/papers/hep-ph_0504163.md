# Structure Functions and Pair Correlations of the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Recent experiments at RHIC and theoretical considerations indicate that the quark-gluon plasma, present in the fireball of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, might be in a liquid phase. The liquid state can be identified by characteristic correlation and structure functions. Here definitions of the structure functions and pair correlations of the quark-gluon plasma are presented as well as perturbative results. These definitions might be useful for verifying the quark-gluon-plasma liquid in QCD lattice calculations.

## Key claim

The quark-gluon plasma (QGP) formed in relativistic heavy-ion collisions may be in a liquid phase rather than a gas, identifiable by characteristic peaks in its static and dynamic structure functions and pair correlation functions; the paper provides perturbative results as a baseline for future non-perturbative (lattice QCD) calculations, noting that the perturbative regime corresponds to the gas phase with no liquid-like structures.

## Method

The author defines the static density-density autocorrelation function, pair correlation function, static structure function, and dynamic structure function (van Hove function) for the quark component of the QGP, relating them to the longitudinal part of the QCD polarization tensor via the fluctuation-dissipation theorem. These definitions are then evaluated perturbatively using the hard thermal loop (HTL) limit of the one-loop polarization tensor with bare quark propagators, and subsequently with a resummed Dyson-Schwinger equation incorporating the HTL dielectric function, to compute the static structure function and pair correlation function in the weak-coupling limit.

## Key numbers

- The QGP coupling parameter is estimated as \(\Gamma = 1.5\)–\(5\), assuming a temperature \(T \simeq 200\) MeV, inter-particle distance \(d \simeq 0.5\) fm, and strong coupling constant \(g \simeq 2\).
- The transition temperature for the QGP liquid-gas phase transition is estimated to be of the order of a few times \(T_c = 170\) MeV.
- In the HTL limit, the static structure function is constant: \(S(p) = 2N_f T^3 / n\), indicating an uncorrelated ideal gas.
- After resummation, the static structure function becomes \(S(p) = (2N_f T^3 / n) \cdot p^2 / (p^2 + m_D^2)\), where the Debye screening mass is \(m_D^2 = N_f g^2 T^2 / 6\).
- The resummed pair correlation function is \(g(r) = -(N_f T^3 / 2\pi n) \cdot (m_D^2 / r) \cdot e^{-m_D r}\).

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Thoma (2005) that the QGP at RHIC temperatures is a strongly coupled liquid, supported by experimental observations of strong elliptic flow, jet quenching, and fast thermalization (Shuryak, Gyulassy, Heinz, Cassing). It provides formal definitions of correlation and structure functions, standard tools in liquid-state physics (Hansen, Ichimaru), adapted to the QGP, enabling future lattice QCD calculations (Karsch) to test for liquid-phase signatures such as a pronounced peak in the static structure function, which would distinguish a liquid from a gas or supercritical fluid.
