# Scale-Invariant Hidden Local Symmetry, Topology Change and Dense Baryonic Matter

> When scale symmetry is implemented into hidden local symmetry in low-energy strong interactions to arrive at a scale-invariant hidden local symmetric (HLS) theory, the scalar $f_0(500)$ may be interpreted as pseudo-Nambu-Goldstone (pNG) boson, i.e., dilaton, of spontaneously broken scale invariance, joining the pseudo-scalar pNG bosons $π$ and the matter fields $V=(ρ,ω)$ as relevant degrees of freedom. Implementing the skyrmion-half-skyrmion transition predicted at large $N_c$ in QCD at a density roughly twice the nuclear matter density found in the crystal simulation of dense skyrmion matter, we determine the intrinsically density-dependent (IDD) "bare parameters" of the scale-invariant HLS Lagrangian matched to QCD at a matching scale $Λ_M$. The resulting effective Lagrangian, with the parameters scaling with the density of the system, is applied to nuclear matter and dense baryonic matter relevant to massive compact stars by means of the double-decimation renormalization-group $V_{lowk}$ formalism. We satisfactorily post-dict the properties of normal nuclear matter and more significantly {\it predict} the EoS of dense compact-star matter that quantitatively accounts for the presently available data coming from both the terrestrial and space laboratories. We interpret the resulting structure of compact-star matter as revealing how the combination of hidden-scale symmetry and hidden local symmetry manifests itself in compressed baryonic matter.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the scalar $f_0(500)$ can be interpreted as a pseudo-Nambu-Goldstone boson (dilaton) of spontaneously broken scale invariance, and that combining this scale symmetry with hidden local symmetry in a Lagrangian matched to QCD can post-dict normal nuclear matter properties and predict the equation of state for dense compact-star matter, but only if a topology change from skyrmions to half-skyrmions at roughly twice nuclear matter density is implemented, and the resulting intrinsic density dependence is treated separately in two density regimes.

## Method

The authors construct a scale-invariant hidden local symmetric (sHLS) Lagrangian incorporating pions, the dilaton, and vector mesons ($\rho$, $\omega$), then match its bare parameters to QCD at a scale $\Lambda_M$ to obtain intrinsic density dependence (IDD) from QCD condensates. They translate the skyrmion-to-half-skyrmion topology change predicted at large $N_c$ into two distinct density regimes (Region I below $n_{1/2}$ and Region II above $n_{1/2}$), each with different IDD scaling laws for masses and couplings. Nuclear many-body correlations are then treated via the double-decimation renormalization-group $V_{lowk}$ formalism, decimating from the matching scale down to the Fermi surface.

## Key numbers

- The topology change density $n_{1/2}$ is estimated to lie in the range $(1.3 - 2.0)n_0$, where $n_0$ is nuclear matter density.
- The symmetry energy factor $S$ shows a cusp at $n_{1/2}$: it decreases monotonically toward $n_{1/2}$ and then increases monotonically above $n_{1/2}$.
- In Region I, the scaling function $\Phi_I(n) \approx 1/(1 + c_I n/n_0)$ is used, with $c_I$ a positive constant to be fine-tuned.
- In Region II, the $\rho$ meson mass and gauge coupling scale linearly with density as $\Phi^\rho_{II}(n) \approx (1 - c^\rho_{II} n/n_0)$, with the chiral restoration density estimated at $n_c \sim (6-7)n_0$.
- The pion mass in Region I scales as $m_\pi^*/m_\pi \approx \Phi_I^{1/2}$.
- The nucleon and vector meson masses in Region I scale as $m_N^*/m_N \approx m_V^*/m_V \approx \Phi_I$.

## Context

This work extends the scale-invariant hidden local symmetry framework introduced by the same authors in a preceding note (LPR15), which itself builds on Crewther and Tunstall's (CT) proposal that QCD with $N_f \sim 3$ has an infrared fixed point leading to scale-chiral symmetry. The paper contrasts its approach with chiral perturbation theory ($\chi$PT$_2$), which breaks down at high density, and with relativistic mean-field models that lack the topology-change mechanism. The results enable predictions for the equation of state of massive compact stars that can be tested against terrestrial and space laboratory data, and they suggest that hidden scale symmetry and hidden local symmetry emerge at high density in a manner analogous to their role in beyond-the-Standard-Model dilatonic Higgs models (Yamawaki, 2015).
