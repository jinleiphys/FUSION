# Meson-meson correlations in baryon-baryon and antibaryon-baryon interactions

> Recent work of the Jülich group about the role of meson-meson correlations in baryon-baryon and antibaryon-baryon interactions is reviewed.

## Key claim

Meson-meson correlations are important in both baryon-baryon and antibaryon-baryon interactions and must be included in any realistic model description, but the paper reviews a program of work rather than presenting a single new experimental result; the claim is that these correlations can account for much of the interaction without invoking explicit quark-gluon effects, provided the models are consistently applied.

## Method

The Jülich group constructed dynamical, dispersion-theoretic models for correlated ππ, K K̅, and πρ exchange in baryon-baryon (NN, YN) and nucleon-antinucleon (N N̅) systems. For the BB sector, the B B̅′ → ππ, K K̅ amplitudes were built from baryon-exchange and ρ-pole Born terms, and the ππ–K K̅ correlations were treated via a coupled-channel meson-exchange model fitted to empirical ππ phase shifts up to 1.3 GeV. For the N N̅ sector, a coupled-channel model (p p̅, ππ, K K̅, ρρ) was used for annihilation reactions, and a DWBA calculation with initial- and final-state interactions was applied to p p̅ → πρ.

## Key numbers

- QCD lattice calculation yields g_{NNπ} = 12.7 ± 2.4 and a monopole cutoff mass Λ_{NNπ} = 0.75 ± 0.14 GeV for the πNN vertex (Liu, Dong, Wilcox 1995).
- The Bonn NN potential achieves a χ² per observable of less than 2 (Haidenbauer and Holinde 1989).
- Strength function G^{(0⁺)}(t=0) for correlated ππ and K K̅ exchange in the scalar-isoscalar channel: 1.0 (NN), 0.49 (ΛN), 0.41 (ΣN), 0.26 (ΛΛ), 0.30 (ΣΣ), 0.19 (NΞ).
- For correlated πρ exchange in the ω channel, the renormalized ωNN coupling constant at the pole is g²_{NNω}/4π = 11.0, about a factor of 2 smaller than the effective value of 20 used in the Bonn potential; the non-pole piece can be parametrized by a sharp-mass ω′ exchange with g²_{NNω}/4π = 8.5, f_{NNω}/g_{NNω} = 0.4, and m_{ω′} = 1120 MeV.
- Partial cross sections for p̅p → πρ at p_{lab} = 100 MeV/c: Born 435 μb (³S₁, I=0) and 126 μb (¹S₀, I=1); after initial-state interaction (ISI) 3.40 μb and 0.73 μb; after final-state interaction (FSI) 4.42 μb and 0.14 μb; ratio R = 32 (full calculation) compared to experimental 35 ± 16.

## Context

This work extends the Bonn NN potential (Machleidt, Holinde, Elster 1987) and the Jülich hyperon-nucleon models (Holzenkamp, Holinde, Speth 1989; Reuber, Holinde, Speth 1994) by replacing sharp-mass σ′ and ρ exchange with explicit dynamical models of correlated ππ and K K̅ exchange. It also addresses the missing correlated πρ exchange in the Bonn potential, showing that it can soften the πNN form factor (toward Λ_{NNπ} ≈ 0.8 GeV as suggested by Coon and Scadron 1990) and provide much of the short-range NN repulsion previously attributed to ω exchange. In the N N̅ sector, the work enables a more reliable identification of baryon-exchange versus quark-gluon mechanisms in annihilation, resolving the “πρ puzzle” by showing that final-state meson-meson correlations can bring the ratio of partial cross sections into agreement with experiment.
