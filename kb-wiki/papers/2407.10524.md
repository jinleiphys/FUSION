# Polarized $ϕ$ meson rates with viscous corrections at RHIC

> We discuss the emission of $ϕ$ mesons with longitudinal and transverse polarization in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions. In the hadronic phase and in leading order in the kaon diluteness, $ϕ$ emission is isotropic and driven by the flavor singlet and octet vector spectral functions. At next to leading order, the emissivities receive a non-isotropic correction from the flavor octet spectral function, and the non-equilibrium contributions originating from the shear and bulk viscosities. Implications for the $ϕ$ meson alignment in heavy-ion collisions are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the spin alignment of $\phi$ mesons in heavy-ion collisions receives only very small corrections from hadronic interactions and viscous effects, with the dominant contribution remaining the isotropic equilibrium rate; the authors explicitly state that their predicted $\rho_{00}$ is "compatible with 1/3" and that the "chiral mixing through thermal kaons in the $\phi$-channel is very small compared to that observed in the $\rho$-channel through thermal pions."

## Method

The authors compute polarized $\phi$ meson emission rates using vector meson dominance to relate the $\phi$ field to the strange quark current, combined with a chiral reduction scheme that enforces Ward identities from broken chiral symmetry. They perform a virial expansion in the kaon diluteness parameter $\kappa_K$ (the density of thermal kaons normalized by the kaon decay constant squared), evaluating the Feynman propagator for the strange current at leading order (resonance gas) and next-to-leading order (kaon interactions). Viscous corrections from shear and bulk viscosity are incorporated using the framework developed by Liu et al. (2017) for electromagnetic emissivities, and the rates are evaluated numerically for both a static fluid and a Bjorken flow background using spectral functions parametrized from known resonances.

## Key numbers

- The kaon diluteness parameter $\kappa_K \simeq 0.03$ at $T=170$ MeV with $\mu/T=0.8$, and $\kappa_K \simeq 0.06$ with $\mu/T=1.3$
- The $\phi$ meson mass is $m_\phi = 1019.461$ MeV, with decay constant $f_\phi = -81.53$ MeV (from KFSR relations)
- The hadronic phase temperature is taken as $T^{(had.)}=170$ MeV, with freeze-out at $T^{f.o.}=145$ MeV
- The hydrodynamic stage starts at $\tau_0^{(hyd.)}=0.5$ fm with $T_0^{(hyd.)}=300$ MeV
- For the static fluid at $T_0=170$ MeV with $\eta/s=\zeta/s=0.1$, the predicted $\rho_{00}$ is consistent with $1/3$ and with STAR data (STAR 2022)
- The "chiral mixing" would become total when $4\kappa_K = 3/5$, requiring $\kappa_K \simeq 0.6$ which corresponds to an unrealistic hadronic temperature of $T=650$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral reduction approach previously applied to electromagnetic emissivities by Steele et al. (1996), Dusling et al. (2006, 2007), and Kim et al. (2016), and to viscous corrections by Liu et al. (2017), now applied to polarized $\phi$ meson emission. It contrasts with the $\rho$ meson case studied by Yin et al. (2024) in chiral effective theory, where thermal pions produce much larger chiral mixing effects. The results show that thermal model predictions for $\phi$ spin alignment remain near the isotropic value of $1/3$, consistent with STAR data (2022), unlike the $\Lambda$ hyperon polarization which is well described by local equilibrium models. The formalism enables future inclusion of baryonic states for lower-energy collisions and can be extended to study other vector mesons.
