# Dissipative hydrodynamics for relativistic multi-component systems

> Second-order dissipative hydrodynamic equations for each component of a multi-component system are derived using the entropy principle. The shear viscosity of the whole system, appearing in the equation summed-up over all components, is related to the partial shear pressures and cannot be considered as an external parameter. We demonstrate that it is essential to solve hydrodynamic equations for each component, instead of treating a mixture as an effective one-component system with a free parameter $η/s$. Thus, extractions of the $η/s$ value of the QGP at RHIC and LHC have to be reexamined.

# Dissipative hydrodynamics for relativistic multi-component systems

## Key claim

The standard one-component hydrodynamic description with a single shear viscosity coefficient cannot generally be applied to a multi-component system such as the quark-gluon plasma; the effective shear viscosity of the mixture depends on the ratios of partial shear pressures and therefore cannot be treated as an externally specified free parameter, meaning that extractions of the η/s value of the QGP at RHIC and LHC must be reexamined.

## Method

Second-order dissipative hydrodynamic equations for each component of a multi-component system were derived using the entropy principle, starting from a generalization of the Israel-Stewart entropy current to N species. The resulting evolution equations for each partial shear stress tensor π_i^{μν} were then validated by comparing their solutions to kinetic transport calculations using the partonic cascade model BAMPS, for a simplified one-dimensional boost-invariant expansion and for a spatially homogeneous two-component system with massless Boltzmann particles undergoing binary elastic collisions.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperatures: T₁ = T₂ = 400 MeV for the two-component box simulation
- Initial number density ratio: n₁/n₂ = 5
- Initial shear pressure ratios: π₁/e₁ = π₂/e₂ = 0.3
- Cross sections: σ₁₁ = 3.88 mb, σ₂₂ = σ₁₁/4, σ₁₂ = σ₁₁/2
- Relaxed ratio π₁/π₂ = 0.488, calculated analytically from Eq. (weight) for the two-component system in equilibrium
- Effective relaxation time for π₁/π₂: approximately 0.6 fm/c
- BAMPS simulation used a box of 16 × 16 × 16 fm³ with 2 × 10² test particles per run, total particle number 1.3 × 10⁷, averaged over 10⁴ runs

## Context

This work extends the Israel-Stewart second-order hydrodynamics (Israel and Stewart, 1979; Muronga, 2003) to multi-component systems, differing from the approach of Monnai and Hirano (2010) by introducing separate evolution equations and transport coefficients for each component. The results contradict the common assumption in hydrodynamic calculations for heavy-ion collisions (e.g., those by Romatschke, 2009; Denicol et al., 2010) that the quark-gluon mixture can be treated as an effective one-component system with a free η/s parameter. The findings enable future investigations of hydrodynamic behavior of the quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and LHC using multi-component hydrodynamics, where temperatures and mean free paths become the relevant scales rather than η/s.
