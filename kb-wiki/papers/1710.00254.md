# Partial restoration of spin-isospin SU(4) Symmetry and the One-QRPA method in Double Beta Decay

> The one-QRPA method is used to describe simultaneously both double decay beta modes, giving special attention to the partial restoration of spin-isospin SU(4) symmetry. To implement this restoration and to fix the model parameters, we resort to the energetics of Gamow-Teller resonances and to the minima of the single $β^+$-decay strengths. This makes the theory predictive regarding the $ββ_{2ν}$-decay, producing the $2ν$ moments in $^{48}$Ca, $^{76}$Ge, $^{82}$Se, $^{96}$Zr, $^{100}$Mo, $^{128,130}$Te, and $^{150}$Nd, that are of the same order of magnitude as the experimental ones; however, the agreement with $ββ_{2ν}$ data is only modest. To include contributions coming from induced nuclear weak currents, we extend the $ββ_{0ν}$-decay formalism employed previously in C. Barbero et. al, Nuc. Phys. A628, 170 (1998). The numerical results for the $ββ_{0ν}$ moments in the above mentioned nuclei are similar to those obtained in other theoretical studies although smaller on averag by $\sim 40\%$. We attribute this difference basically to the one-QRPA-method, employed here for the first time, instead of the currently used two-QRPA-method. The difference is partially due to the way of carrying out the restoration of the spin-isospin symmetry. It is hard to say which is the best way to make the restoration, since the $ββ_{0ν}$ moments are not experimentally measurable. The numerical uncertainties in the $ββ$ moments, related with i) their strong dependence on the residual interaction in the p-p channel when evaluated within the QRPA, and ii) lack of proper knowledge of single-particle energies, have been quantified. It is concluded that the partial restoration of the SU (4) symmetry is crucial in the description of the $ββ$-decays, regardless of the nuclear model used.

# Partial restoration of spin-isospin SU(4) Symmetry and the One-QRPA method in Double Beta Decay

## Key claim

The one-QRPA method, applied for the first time to neutrinoless double beta decay, yields nuclear moments that are on average about 40% smaller than those from the standard two-QRPA method, but the authors caution that the neutrinoless moments are not experimentally measurable, making it impossible to determine which restoration method is best. The partial restoration of SU(4) symmetry is deemed crucial regardless of the nuclear model used, but the agreement with two-neutrino double beta decay data is only modest.

## Method

The authors solve the QRPA equations only once for the intermediate odd-odd nucleus, rather than solving twice for the initial and final even-even nuclei and averaging. The residual interaction is a delta-force with spin-singlet and spin-triplet parameters in the particle-particle channel, fixed by requiring that the vector beta-plus strength becomes minimal (isospin restoration) and that the Gamow-Teller beta-plus strength becomes minimal (partial SU(4) restoration). The particle-hole parameters are fixed from the experimental energetics of the Isobaric Analog State and the Gamow-Teller Resonance. The formalism for neutrinoless decay is extended from Barbero et al. (1998) to include pseudoscalar and weak-magnetism contributions from induced nuclear weak currents.

## Key numbers

- The neutrinoless double beta decay moments from the one-QRPA method are smaller than those from the two-QRPA method by approximately 40% on average.
- The vector beta-plus strength is minimized when the ratio s = v^s_pp / v^s_pair equals s_sym = 1, indicating isospin restoration.
- The Gamow-Teller resonance energy relative to the Isobaric Analog State is given by E_GTR - E_IAS = 26 A^{-1/3} - 18.5 (N-Z)/A (in MeV), used to fix particle-hole parameters.
- The short-range correlation function uses k_c = 3.93 fm^{-1}, the Compton wavelength of the omega-meson.
- The vector and axial-vector cutoff parameters for finite nucleon size are Lambda_V = 0.85 GeV and Lambda_A = 1.086 GeV.
- The weak coupling constants are fixed as g_V = 1, g_M = 3.7, g_A = 1.27, and g_P from the partially conserved axial current hypothesis.
- The Ikeda sum rule S^- - S^+ = N - Z is fulfilled by the calculated strengths.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous QRPA studies on double beta decay (Krmpotic et al. 1990-1997, Barbero et al. 1998-1999, Ferreira et al. 2016) by incorporating pseudoscalar and weak-magnetism currents and applying the one-QRPA method for the first time to neutrinoless decay. It contrasts with the standard two-QRPA approach pioneered by Vogel and Zirnbauer (1986) and Civitarese, Faessler and Tomoda (1987), where the QRPA equations are solved separately for initial and final nuclei and then averaged. The work also differs from Simkovic et al. (2013) and other recent QRPA calculations that fit the isoscalar particle-particle parameter to reproduce experimental two-neutrino moments; here the parameters are fixed from resonance energetics and strength minima, making the model predictive for two-neutrino decay. The results enable comparison of one-QRPA versus two-QRPA methods and quantification of uncertainties from the residual interaction in the particle-particle channel and from single-particle energies.
