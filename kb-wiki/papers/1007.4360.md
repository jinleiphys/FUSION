# Pulsar glitch recovery and the superfluidity coefficients of bulk nuclear matter

> A two-component hydrodynamic model is constructed of the global superfluid flow induced by two-component Ekman pumping during the recovery stage of a glitch. The model successfully accounts for the quasi-exponential recovery observed in pulsars like Vela and the "overshoot" observed in pulsars like the Crab. By fitting the model to high-resolution timing data, three important constitutive coefficients in bulk nuclear matter can be extracted: the shear viscosity, the mutual friction parameter, and the charged fluid fraction. The fitted coefficients for the Crab and Vela are compared with theoretical predictions for several equations of state, including the color-flavor locked and two-flavor color superconductor phases of quark matter.

# Pulsar glitch recovery and the superfluidity coefficients of bulk nuclear matter

## Key claim
The two-component hydrodynamic model of Ekman pumping during glitch recovery can account for both quasi-exponential recovery (as in Vela) and overshoot recovery (as in the Crab), and by fitting the model to timing data, three constitutive coefficients of bulk nuclear matter can be extracted: shear viscosity, mutual friction parameter, and charged fluid fraction. However, the model neglects entrainment, vortex tension, pinning, and magnetic fields to remain analytically tractable, and the fitted coefficients represent body-averaged effective values rather than depth-dependent transport coefficients.

## Method
The authors constructed a two-fluid hydrodynamic model using the linearized Hall-Vinen-Bekharevich-Khalatnikov (HVBK) equations in spherical Couette geometry, with a rigid crust containing a viscous component (proton-electron plasma) and an inviscid component (neutron condensate) coupled by mutual friction. They derived an analytic solution for the interior azimuthal flow and a closed integro-differential equation for the crust's spin evolution, then solved numerically by iteration. The model was fitted to published multi-exponential fits of post-glitch timing data for Vela and the Crab pulsars, using conservation of angular momentum and initial slope conditions to constrain the six free parameters.

- Vela pulsar spin frequency: $\nu_{g0} = 11.2$ Hz
- Vela glitch recovery time-scales range from $t_4 = 0.0007$ d to $t_1 = 916$ d across 14 glitches (Table 1)
- Vela glitch amplitudes $\Delta\nu$ range from 2.2 $\mu$Hz to 77.3 $\mu$Hz
- For the approximate dual-exponential solution, the mutual friction parameter is given by $B = (2\pi\nu_{g0} t_1)^{-1}$ and the Ekman-related time-scale satisfies $(20/7)E^{1/2}\rho_n(1+K) = (2\pi\nu_{g0} t_2)^{-1}$
- The Ekman number in neutron stars is typically $E \lesssim 10^{-10}$
- The mutual friction parameter regime for glitching pulsars satisfies $B, B' \ll 1$

## Context
This work extends earlier hydrodynamic treatments of Ekman pumping in neutron stars (Easson 1979, Abney & Epstein 1996, Reisenegger 1993) and two-fluid spin-up theory (van Eysden & Melatos 2010) by solving self-consistently for the global two-fluid flow pattern with crustal back-reaction for the first time. It contrasts with previous studies that incorporate vortex pinning and creep but do not solve for the global flow (Alpar et al. 1984, 1993, 1996; Sedrakian & Cordes 2002). The model enables extraction of nuclear transport coefficients from glitch recovery data, offering a way to measure dissipative transport in bulk nuclear matter in the MeV regime inaccessible to terrestrial laboratories, and can be compared with theoretical predictions for standard $^1S_0$ neutron superfluidity (Cutler et al. 1987, Mendell 1991) and exotic quark matter phases such as color-flavor locked superconductivity (Madsen 2000, Mannarelli et al. 2008, Alford et al. 2009).
