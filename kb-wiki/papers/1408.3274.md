# Measurement of the transverse target and beam-target asymmetries in $η$ meson photoproduction at MAMI

> We present new data for the transverse target asymmetry T and the very first data for the beam-target asymmetry F in the $\vec γ\vec p\toηp$ reaction up to a center-of-mass energy of W=1.9 GeV. The data were obtained with the Crystal-Ball/TAPS detector setup at the Glasgow tagged photon facility of the Mainz Microtron MAMI. All existing model predictions fail to reproduce the new data indicating a significant impact on our understanding of the underlying dynamics of $η$ meson photoproduction. The peculiar nodal structure observed in existing T data close to threshold is not confirmed.

## Key claim

The new high-statistics data for the transverse target asymmetry \(T\) and the first-ever data for the beam-target asymmetry \(F\) in \(\vec\gamma\vec p\to\eta p\) up to \(W=1.9\) GeV do not confirm the nodal structure in the angular dependence of \(T\) near threshold that was reported in earlier data from Bock (1998), thereby resolving a long-standing puzzle about the relative phase between \(s\)- and \(d\)-wave amplitudes; however, all existing model predictions fail to reproduce the new data, indicating that the partial-wave structure of all current models must be revised.

## Method

The experiment was performed at the MAMI C accelerator using the Crystal Ball/TAPS detector setup with a tagged photon beam from 700 to 1450 MeV and a frozen-spin butanol target providing transversely polarized protons with an average polarization of 70%. The \(\eta\) mesons were identified via their \(\eta\to 2\gamma\) or \(\eta\to 3\pi^0\to 6\gamma\) decay modes, and the observables \(T\) and \(F\) were extracted from count-rate asymmetries after subtracting the unpolarized carbon and oxygen background using template fits from separate hydrogen and carbon target measurements.

## Key numbers

- The photon beam energy range covered was 700 to 1450 MeV, corresponding to a center-of-mass energy up to \(W=1.9\) GeV.
- The longitudinally polarized electron beam had an energy of 1557 MeV and a polarization degree of 80%; the circular polarization of the photons ranged from 65% at 700 MeV to 78% at 1450 MeV.
- The average proton polarization in the butanol target was 70% with relaxation times of about 1500 hours; the holding magnet operated at 0.45 Tesla.
- The total systematic uncertainty of the asymmetry measurements is less than 6%, dominated by the proton polarization determination (4%), photon beam polarization (2%), and background subtraction (3–4%).
- For the Legendre decomposition, a truncation to \(\ell_{\mathrm{max}}=2\) (\(d\) waves) was sufficient below \(W=1.6\) GeV and to \(\ell_{\mathrm{max}}=3\) (\(f\) waves) above \(W=1.6\) GeV for the differential cross section; for the spin-dependent cross sections, truncation to \(pd\) interferences below \(W=1.6\) GeV and \(df\) interferences above was sufficient.

## Context

This work extends the earlier measurement of the target asymmetry \(T\) by Bock (1998) with higher statistics and provides the first data for the beam-target asymmetry \(F\) in \(\eta\) photoproduction. The new results contradict the nodal structure in the old \(T\) data near threshold, which had forced a large and rapidly varying phase between \(E_{0+}\) and \(E_{2-},M_{2-}\) multipoles in the model-independent analysis of Tiator (1999). The failure of all existing models, including \(\eta\)-MAID, the Giessen model, the Bonn-Gatchina analysis (BG2011-02), and the SAID GE09 solution, to describe the new data will require a significant revision of the partial-wave amplitudes in future analyses, particularly in the region around \(W=1670\) MeV where a narrow structure in \(\eta\) production off neutrons has been debated.
