# Role of $Y(4630)$ in the $p\bar{p}\rightarrowΛ_c\barΛ_c$ reaction near threshold

> We investigate the charmed baryon production reaction $p\bar{p}\rightarrowΛ_c\barΛ_c$ in an effective Lagrangian approach. Besides the $t$-channel $D^0$ and $D^{*0}$ mesons exchanges, the $s$-channel $Y(4630)$ meson exchange is taken into account. For the total cross sections, the $D^0$ and $D^{*0}$ mesons provide minor background contributions, while the $Y(4630)$ state gives a clear peak structure with the magnitude of 10 $μ$b at center of mass energy 4.63 GeV. Basing on the results, we suggest that the reaction of $p\bar{p}\rightarrowΛ_c\barΛ_c$ can be used to search for the $1^{--}$ charmonium-like $Y(4630)$ state, and our predictions can be tested in future by the $\rm{\bar PANDA}$ facility.

## Key claim
The reaction \(p\bar{p} \rightarrow \Lambda_c \bar{\Lambda}_c\) can be used to search for the \(Y(4630)\) state, provided the \(Y(4630)\) has a sizeable coupling to \(p\bar{p}\) (predicted branching ratio of order a few percent). The \(Y(4630)\) contribution produces a clear peak in the total cross section, but the background from \(t\)-channel \(D\) and \(D^*\) exchanges is minor and the tensor coupling term in the \(D^*\) exchange is neglected.

## Method
The authors employ an effective Lagrangian approach with tree-level Feynman diagrams. They include \(t\)-channel \(D^0\) and \(D^{*0}\) meson exchanges and an \(s\)-channel \(Y(4630)\) resonance exchange. Coupling constants for the \(\Lambda_c p D\) and \(\Lambda_c p D^*\) vertices are taken from prior literature, while the \(Y(4630)\) couplings are derived from its assumed dominant decay to \(\Lambda_c \bar{\Lambda}_c\) and a 1% branching ratio to \(p\bar{p}\). Monopole form factors are used for the \(t\)-channel exchanges and a dipole form factor for the \(s\)-channel resonance.

## Key numbers
- The \(Y(4630)\) mass is taken as \(m_Y = 4634\) MeV and its total width as \(\Gamma_Y = 92\) MeV (from Belle collaboration).
- The coupling constants used are \(g_{\Lambda_c p D} = 10.7^{+5.3}_{-4.3}\) and \(g_{\Lambda_c p D^*} = -5.8^{+2.1}_{-2.5}\).
- The \(Y(4630)\) couplings derived are \(g_{Y \Lambda_c \bar{\Lambda}_c} = 1.78\) and \(g_{Y p \bar{p}} = 0.087\).
- The total cross section at the peak (c.m. energy 4.63 GeV) is of order 10 \(\mu\)b.
- The cut-off parameter for the \(Y(4630)\) form factor is \(\Lambda_Y = 500\) MeV; the \(t\)-channel cut-off uses \(\alpha = 1.5\) with \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD} = 220\) MeV.

## Context
This work extends the study of charmed baryon production in \(p\bar{p}\) collisions, previously investigated using models such as the quark-diquark picture, handbag approach, and meson-exchange models (Kroll 1988, Kaidalov 1994, Titov 2008, Haidenbauer 2009, Shyam 2014). It specifically builds on the prediction by Guo (2016) that the \(Y(4630)\) has a sizeable branching ratio to \(p\bar{p}\). The results suggest that the \(\bar{\rm PANDA}\) facility, with a maximum antiproton beam momentum of 15 GeV, can test the predicted peak structure and thereby help clarify the nature of the \(Y(4630)\) state.
