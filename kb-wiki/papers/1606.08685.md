# Heavy Hadrons in Nuclear Matter

> Current studies on heavy hadrons in nuclear medium are reviewed with a summary of the basic theoretical concepts of QCD, namely chiral symmetry, heavy quark spin symmetry, and the effective Lagrangian approach. The nuclear matter is an interesting place to study the properties of heavy hadrons from many different points of view. We emphasize the importance of the following topics: (i) charm/bottom hadron-nucleon interaction, (ii) structure of charm/bottom nuclei, and (iii) QCD vacuum properties and hadron modifications in nuclear medium. We pick up three different groups of heavy hadrons, quarkonia ($J/ψ$, $Υ$), heavy-light mesons ($D$/$\bar{D}$, $\bar{B}$/$B$) and heavy baryons ($Λ_{c}$, $Λ_{b}$). The modifications of those hadrons in nuclear matter provide us with important information to investigate the essential properties of heavy hadrons. We also give the discussions about the heavy hadrons, not only in nuclear matter with infinite volume, but also in atomic nuclei with finite baryon numbers, to serve future experiments.

## Key claim

This review argues that studying heavy hadrons (charm and bottom) in nuclear matter provides a unique probe of QCD vacuum properties and hadron dynamics, but the field remains in progress with systematic knowledge not yet obtained, and the authors emphasize that SU(4) and SU(5) flavor symmetries cannot be applied for hadron spectroscopy despite being useful for classification.

## Method

The authors survey theoretical approaches to heavy hadrons in nuclear matter organized by three hadron classes (quarkonia, heavy-light mesons, and heavy baryons) and three topics (hadron-nucleon interaction, structure of charm/bottom nuclei, and QCD vacuum properties). They review effective Lagrangian methods based on chiral symmetry and heavy quark spin symmetry, few-body calculations using the Gaussian expansion method, nuclear matter treatments via Fermi gas and G-matrix formalism, and QCD sum rules for condensate modifications.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass: m_c = 1.275 ± 0.025 GeV
- Bottom quark mass: m_b = 4.66 ± 0.03 GeV
- QCD scale: Λ_QCD = 214 ± 7 MeV (MS-bar scheme with N_f = 5)
- One-loop beta function coefficient: b_1 = (33 - 2N_f)/12π

## Context

This review extends the study of flavor degrees of freedom in nuclear matter from strangeness (hypernuclei, kaon-nucleon systems) to charm and bottom flavors, building on earlier work on vector meson modifications in nuclei reviewed by Hayano (2008) and Leupold (2009). It connects to the recent discoveries of exotic heavy hadrons (X, Y, Z states and P_c pentaquarks) and aims to motivate future experimental studies of heavy-flavor nuclei at accelerator facilities.
