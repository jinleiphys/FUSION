# Formation and dynamics of exotic hypernuclei in heavy-ion collisions

> The dynamics of exotic hypernuclei in heavy-ion collisions has been investigated thoroughly with a microscopic transport model. All possible channels on hyperon ($Λ$, $Σ$ and $Ξ$) production near threshold energies are implemented in the transport model. The light complex fragments (Z$\leq$2) are constructed with the Wigner-function method. The classical phase-space coalescence is used for recognizing heavy nuclear and hyperfragments and the statistical model is taken for describing the decay process. The nuclear fragmentation reactions of the available experimental data from the ALADIN collaboration are well reproduced by the combined approach. It is found that the in-medium potentials of strange particles influence the strangeness production and fragment formation. The hyperfragments are mainly created in the projectile or target-like rapidity region and the yields are reduced about the 3-order magnitude in comparison to the nuclear fragments. The hypernuclear dynamics of HypHI data is well described with the model. The possible experiments for producing the neutron-rich hyperfragments at the high-intensity heavy-ion accelerator facility (HIAF) are discussed.

## Key claim

The LQMD transport model, combined with a coalescence approach and the GEMINI statistical decay code, can describe hypernucleus formation in heavy-ion collisions, but the yields of hyperfragments are reduced by about three orders of magnitude compared to nuclear fragments, and the hyperfragments are mainly created in the projectile or target-like rapidity region rather than the mid-rapidity region.

## Method

The Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics (LQMD) transport model was extended to include all possible channels for hyperon (Λ, Σ, Ξ) production near threshold energies via baryon-baryon and meson-baryon collisions. Light complex fragments (Z ≤ 2) were constructed using the Wigner-function method, while heavy nuclear fragments and hyperfragments (Z ≥ 3) were recognized via classical phase-space coalescence with a relative momentum cutoff of 200 MeV/c and distance cutoffs of 3 fm (nuclear) and 5 fm (hyperfragments). The statistical decay of excited primary fragments was modeled using the GEMINI code, modified to include hyperon decay widths via the Hauser-Feshbach formalism.

## Key numbers

- Hyperon mean-field potentials: attractive values of -32 MeV for Λ and -16 MeV for Ξ at normal nuclear density, based on the light-quark counting rule (two-thirds of the nucleon self-energy).
- Coalescence parameters: relative momentum cutoff P₀ = 200 MeV/c; relative distance cutoff R₀ = 3 fm for nuclear fragments and R₀ = 5 fm for hyperfragments (due to larger rms radii, e.g., 5 fm for ³ΛH vs. 1.74 fm for ³He).
- In the reaction ¹²⁴Sn+¹²⁴Sn at 2 GeV/nucleon, the inclusion of the optical potential reduces K⁺ yields by about 30%.
- Hyperfragment yields are reduced by about 2–3 orders of magnitude compared to nuclear fragments in the large mass domain.
- For the reaction ⁶Li+¹²C at 2 GeV/nucleon, the model reproduces the rapidity and transverse momentum spectra of ³ΛH and ⁴ΛH measured by the HypHI collaboration.
- The planned HIAF experiment uses ²⁰Ne+¹²C at a beam energy of 4.25 GeV/nucleon.

## Context

This work extends the LQMD transport model (Feng, 2011, 2018) to hypernucleus formation, building on prior statistical multifragmentation models (Botvina, 2007, 2012) and statistical thermal-source approaches (Andronic, 2011). It reproduces nuclear fragmentation data from the ALADIN collaboration (Schüttauf, 1996) and hypernucleus data from the HypHI collaboration (Rappold, 2015). The results enable predictions for future experiments at the HIAF facility in China, particularly for producing neutron-rich and double-strangeness hypernuclei, and complement approaches using the PHQMD model with MST/SACA algorithms (Leifels, 2019; Aichelin, 2019).
