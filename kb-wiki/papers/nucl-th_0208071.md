# Facets of confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking

> The gap equation is a cornerstone in understanding dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and may also provide clues to confinement. A symmetry-preserving truncation of its kernel enables proofs of important results and the development of an efficacious phenomenology. We describe a model of the kernel that yields: a momentum-dependent dressed-quark propagator in fair agreement with quenched lattice-QCD results; and chiral limit values: f_pi= 68 MeV and <q-bar q> = -(190 MeV)^3. It is compared with models inferred from studies of the gauge sector.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a symmetry-preserving truncation of QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) can simultaneously describe dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB) and provide clues to confinement, but the model's infrared interaction strength must be an order of magnitude larger than the critical value needed for DCSB alone to achieve an acceptable description of hadrons.

## Method

The authors solve QCD's gap equation using a renormalisation-group-improved rainbow-ladder truncation with a model effective interaction that combines an infrared Gaussian term with an ultraviolet perturbative running coupling. They compare the resulting dressed-quark propagator to quenched lattice-QCD data and also test a vertex-consistent Bethe-Salpeter kernel to compute pion and rho meson masses at various truncation orders.

## Key numbers

- Critical interaction tension for DCSB: \(\sigma_c^\Delta \sim 2.5\) GeV/fm; acceptable hadron description requires \(\sigma^\Delta \sim 25\) GeV/fm
- Chiral limit results from the model fitted to lattice data (parameters \(D=(0.74\ \text{GeV})^2\), \(\omega=0.3\) GeV): \(f_\pi^0 = 68\) MeV and \(\langle \bar q q \rangle^0_{1\ \text{GeV}} = -(190\ \text{MeV})^3\)
- With parameters \(D=(0.96\ \text{GeV})^2\), \(\omega=0.4\) GeV and current-quark masses \(m_u^{1\ \text{GeV}}=5.5\) MeV, \(m_s^{1\ \text{GeV}}=125\) MeV, the model yields \(f_\pi = 0.092\) GeV and \((-\langle\bar q q\rangle^0_{1\ \text{GeV}})^{1/3} = 0.24\) GeV
- Pion massless in chiral limit at all truncation orders; rho meson mass in chiral limit changes from 0.678 GeV (rainbow-ladder, \(n=0\)) to 0.754 GeV (fully resummed, \(n=\infty\)), a 10% correction
- Effective interaction inferred from gauge-sector DSE studies (Alkofer et al.) gives \(f_\pi = 0.053\) GeV, too small by a factor of about 2

## Context

This work extends the systematic symmetry-preserving truncation scheme developed by the authors and collaborators (Roberts, 1998; Maris, 2000) that proved Goldstone's theorem in QCD and unified light- and heavy-quark mass formulas. It directly compares DSE predictions for the dressed-quark propagator to quenched lattice-QCD simulations (Bowman et al., 2002), confirming long-standing DSE predictions of infrared suppression of the wave function renormalisation and enhancement of the mass function. The paper also shows that effective interactions inferred from gauge-sector DSE studies (Alkofer, 2002) underestimate DCSB strength, but that including a \(1/Z^2(Q^2)\) factor from the quark wave function can bridge this gap. The results enable future work connecting gauge-sector studies to observable hadron properties.
