# Relativistic symmetry in deformed nuclei by similarity renormalization group

> The similarity renormalization group is used to transform a general Dirac Hamiltonian into diagonal form. The diagonal Dirac operator consists of the nonrelativistic term, the spin-orbit term, the dynamical term, and the relativistic modification of kinetic energy, which are very useful to explore the symmetries hidden in the Dirac Hamiltonian for any deformed system. As an example, the relativistic symmetries in an axially deformed nucleus are investigated by comparing the contributions of every term to the single particle energies and their correlations with the deformation. The result shows that the deformation considerably influences the spin-orbit interaction and dynamical effect, which play a critical role in the relativistic symmetries and its breaking.

## Key claim

The similarity renormalization group (SRG) can transform a general Dirac Hamiltonian into a diagonal form applicable to any deformed system, yielding Hermitian terms that separately represent the nonrelativistic, spin-orbit, dynamical, and relativistic kinetic-energy contributions. For axially deformed nuclei, the spin-orbit interaction always improves pseudospin symmetry (PSS), while the dynamical effect can either break or improve PSS depending on whether the state is deeply bound or near the continuum.

## Method

The authors applied Wegner's SRG formulation to a Dirac Hamiltonian with scalar and vector potentials, using the generator η(l) = [βM, H(l)] to evolve the Hamiltonian into diagonal form. For an axially quadrupole-deformed nucleus (¹⁵⁴Dy), they adopted Woods-Saxon potentials with a deformation parameter β₂ and expanded the single-particle states in a harmonic oscillator basis. The contribution of each Hermitian term Oᵢ (nonrelativistic, dynamical, spin-orbit, relativistic kinetic energy, and other) to the single-particle energies was computed as ⟨k|Oᵢ|k⟩.

## Key numbers

- The scalar and vector potential depths used were S_WS = -405 MeV and V_WS = 350 MeV, with diffuseness a = 0.67 fm and radius parameter r₀ = 1.27 fm.
- The deformation parameter β₂ was varied over the range from -0.3 to 0.5 for the axially deformed nucleus ¹⁵⁴Dy.
- For spin doublets with Ω = 1/2, the spin-orbit splittings at β₂ = 0 were described as "almost the most remarkable," with splittings reducing as deformation increased toward oblate or prolate directions.
- The energy splitting from the nonrelativistic term was described as "the most remarkable" among all terms over the range of β₂ considered.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous SRG-based diagonalization of the Dirac Hamiltonian for spherical systems (Guo 2012) to deformed nuclei, filling the gap between perturbation calculations and supersymmetry descriptions noted by Liang (2013). The results clarify the deformation-driven interplay between spin-orbit and dynamical contributions to PSS breaking, building on earlier studies of pseudospin symmetry in deformed nuclei by Bohr (1982) and Leviatan (2009). The formalism is applicable beyond nuclear physics to systems with cylindrical geometries, such as electron channeling in crystals and quark confinement in spheroidal flux tubes.
