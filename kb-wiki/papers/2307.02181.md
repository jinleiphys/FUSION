# X17 discovery potential from $γD \to e^+ e^- p n$ with neutron tagging

> We propose a novel direct search experiment for X17 using the reaction $γD \to e^+ e^- pn$. X17 is a hypothetical particle conjectured by the ATOMKI collaboration to explain anomalous signals around 17 MeV in excited ${}^8$Be, ${}^4$He and ${}^{12}$C nuclear decays via internal pair creation. It has been subject to a global experimental and theoretical research program. The proposed direct search in $γD \to e^+ e^- pn$ can verify the existence of X17 through the production on a quasi-free neutron, and determine its quantum numbers separate from ongoing and planned nuclear-decay experiments. This is especially timely in view of the theoretical tension between results from the ${}^{12}$C and ${}^8$Be measurements. Using the plane-wave impulse approximation, we quantify the expected signal and background for pseudoscalar, vector and axial-vector X17 scenarios. We optimize the kinematics for the quasi-free neutron region with the upcoming MAGIX experiment at MESA in mind and show that for all three scenarios the X17 signal is clearly visible above the QED background.

# X17 discovery potential from γD → e⁺e⁻ pn with neutron tagging

## Key claim

The reaction γD → e⁺e⁻ pn with neutron tagging can serve as a direct search for the hypothetical X17 particle, with the signal expected to be clearly visible above the QED background for pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector X17 scenarios, but only under the plane-wave impulse approximation and for kinematics optimized within the neutron quasi-free peak region (|p_p| ≲ 45.7 MeV/c). The proposed experiment can verify X17's existence and determine its quantum numbers separately from ongoing nuclear-decay experiments, which is especially timely given the theoretical tension between results from ¹²C and ⁸Be measurements.

## Method

The authors computed the differential cross section for γD → e⁺e⁻ pn using the plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA), separating the process into a quasi-free neutron amplitude and a spectator proton. They employed the CD-Bonn parametrization for the deuteron s- and d-wave functions and modeled the QED background as the sum of Bethe-Heitler and Compton processes (including Born, π⁰ t-channel exchange, and nucleon polarizability contributions). X17 couplings to nucleons were constrained using branching fractions from ATOMKI's ⁸Be(18.15) and ¹²C(17.23) decays, with separate models for pseudoscalar (Alves and Weiner), vector (Feng et al.), and axial-vector (Kozaczuk et al.) scenarios, and kinematics were optimized for the MAGIX experiment at MESA with E_γ = 105 MeV.

## Key numbers

- X17 averaged mass: 17.02(10) MeV (from ATOMKI collaboration)
- Deuteron binding energy Δ ≈ 2.2 MeV; neutron quasi-free peak condition: |p_p| ≲ √(m_N Δ) ≈ 45.7 MeV/c
- Optimized kinematics for MAGIX at E_γ = 105 MeV: |p₊| = 65.7 MeV/c, θ₊ = -165.0°; |p₋| = 20.1 MeV/c, θ₋ = 168.0°; θ_n = 5.0°
- Branching fractions used for coupling constraints: Γ_X/Γ_γ|_⁸Be(18.15) = 6(1) × 10⁻⁶; Γ_X/Γ_γ|_¹²C(17.23) = 3.6(3) × 10⁻⁶
- Vector X17 neutron coupling from ⁸Be: |ε_n| = (1.1 – 1.7) × 10⁻²; from ¹²C: |ε_n| = (2.6 – 5.3) × 10⁻³ (1σ ranges)
- Pseudoscalar isoscalar coupling: |g⁽⁰⁾_{XNN}| = (2.0 – 5.4) × 10⁻³; isovector bound: |g⁽¹⁾_{XNN}| ≤ 0.6 × 10⁻³
- Axial-vector couplings: |a_{p,n}| = (2.7 – 11.6) × 10⁻⁵ (from ⁸Be)
- Signal cross section averaged over bin width δm_ee = 0.1 MeV (MAGIX resolution)
- Meson exchange current corrections estimated at approximately 5%

## Context

This work proposes a direct production search for X17 via γD → e⁺e⁻ pn, complementing the indirect nuclear-decay experiments by ATOMKI (Krasznahorkay et al., 2015, 2021, 2022) that observed anomalous internal pair creation in excited ⁸Be, ⁴He, and ¹²C. The approach extends earlier work by Backens et al. (2021) on γn → e⁺e⁻ n by using a deuteron target with neutron tagging (following Levchuk, 1994) to access quasi-free neutron kinematics. The results address the tension between ⁸Be and ¹²C data for vector X17 (noted by Barducci et al., 2022) and enable a direct test at the upcoming MAGIX experiment at MESA, which offers an e⁺e⁻ invariant-mass resolution of at least 0.1 MeV.
