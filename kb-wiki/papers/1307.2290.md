# Superconducting phases of strange quark matter in the NJL model

> We analyze the color-flavor-locked phase of strange quark matter modelled by the three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) framework with and without magnetic field and discuss some additional constraints on the stability scenario when a high magnetic field is applied. We compare the results obtained by employing the MIT Bag Model and discuss the pairing gap behavior and its influence on the equation of state.

## Key claim

The color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase of strange quark matter, when modeled self-consistently within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) framework, yields an equation of state that *softens* with increasing pairing gap (i.e., with stronger diquark coupling), contrary to the intuitive expectation from the MIT bag model where a larger gap produces a stiffer equation of state.

## Method

The authors used the three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a smooth momentum cutoff at 1 GeV, neglecting all quark masses to enforce color and electrical neutrality. They solved the gap equation for the pairing gap Δ at a baryonic chemical potential μ = 500 MeV for three values of the diquark coupling constant G (4.32, 5.15, and 7.10 GeV⁻²), then computed the thermodynamic potential, energy density, and pressure for the CFL phase. For the magnetic-field case, they extended the analysis to the magnetized CFL (MCFL) phase, incorporating a bag constant B = 58 MeV/fm³ and an in-medium magnetic field, yielding anisotropic pressures parallel and perpendicular to the field.

## Key numbers

- At μ = 500 MeV, the pairing gaps obtained for G = 4.32, 5.15, and 7.10 GeV⁻² are Δ = 10, 25, and 100 MeV, respectively.
- The smooth cutoff in the NJL model is set to Λ = 1 GeV.
- The bag constant used in the magnetic-field calculations is B = 58 MeV/fm³.
- The magnetic field on the surface of a neutron star ranges from 1.7 × 10⁸ G to 2.1 × 10¹³ G, and may reach 10¹⁴–10¹⁵ G in magnetars; interior fields are estimated up to ~10¹⁹–10²⁰ G.
- Pressure anisotropy becomes significant for magnetic fields above ~10¹⁸ G.

## Context

This work extends the study of strange quark matter stability and equation of state from the MIT bag model (Farhi and Jaffe, 1984) to the self-consistent NJL framework, highlighting a qualitative difference in how the pairing gap affects stiffness. It also builds on prior analyses of the CFL phase (Buballa, 2005) and the magnetized CFL phase (Paulucci et al., 2011). The results indicate that for strong magnetic fields, the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations become inadequate, requiring an anisotropic formalism for stable star configurations. The authors plan to introduce a diquark repulsion term in the two-flavor superconducting (2SC) model to explore hardening the equation of state, and to compute hybrid star mass-radius sequences for comparison with recent pulsar mass measurements such as PSR J1614-2230 at 1.97 ± 0.04 M⊙ (Demorest et al., 2010).
