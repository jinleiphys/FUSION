# Early thermalization of quark-gluon matter with the elastic scattering of ggq and ggqbar

> Elastic gluon-gluon-quark (gluon-gluon-antiquark) scattering is studied in perturbative QCD with 123 Feynman diagrams at the tree level. Individually squared amplitudes and interference terms of the Feynman diagrams are derived. With the elastic gluon-gluon-quark scattering and the elastic gluon-gluon-antiquark scattering transport equations are established. In the thermalization process of initially created quark-gluon matter, this matter is governed by elastic 2-to-2 scattering and elastic 3-to-3 scattering. Solutions of the transport equations show that initially created quark-gluon matter takes early thermalization, i.e., thermal states are established rapidly. Different thermalization times of gluon matter and quark matter are obtained.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including elastic 3-to-3 scattering processes (specifically gluon-gluon-quark and gluon-gluon-antiquark scattering) in transport equations leads to rapid thermalization of initially produced quark-gluon matter, but the thermalization times differ between gluon matter and quark matter, and the early thermalization is understood as an effect of many-body scattering rather than 2-to-2 scattering alone.

## Method

The authors derived the full squared amplitude for elastic gluon-gluon-quark scattering at tree level in perturbative QCD, involving 123 Feynman diagrams divided into four classes based on the presence of triple-gluon and four-gluon vertices. They then established transport equations incorporating these elastic 3-to-3 scattering processes alongside elastic 2-to-2 scattering, and solved them numerically to study the thermalization of initially created quark-gluon matter.

## Key numbers

- Gluon number density of initially produced quark-gluon matter in central Au-Au collisions at RHIC energies: 19.4 fm^{-3}
- At an interaction range of 0.62 fm, gluon-gluon scattering has occurrence probability 0.3 and gluon-gluon-gluon scattering has occurrence probability 0.2
- Hydrodynamic calculations have revealed that the thermalization time is less than 1 fm/c

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Xu and collaborators (2012) on thermalization of quark-gluon matter with elastic 2-to-2 and 3-to-3 scattering, now specifically including the previously omitted elastic gluon-gluon-quark and gluon-gluon-antiquark scattering processes. The paper builds on hydrodynamic calculations by Heinz and Kolb (2002) and Teaney, Lauret, and Shuryak (2001) that indicated rapid thermalization, and on earlier work by Xu and collaborators (2009, 2010) showing that 2-gluon scattering alone cannot cause early thermalization. The results enable more complete transport simulations of the early stages of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies.
