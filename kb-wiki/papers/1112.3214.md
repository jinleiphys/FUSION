# Model comparison of Delta and Omega masses in a covariant Faddeev approach

> We compute the vector-meson, nucleon and delta/omega-baryon masses and their evolution with the current-quark mass using a covariant generalized Bethe-Salpeter equation approach. The interaction kernel is truncated to a dressed gluon exchange. We study the model dependence of our results with the quark-gluon dressing to assess the validity of the truncation.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that two different effective interaction models (Maris-Tandy and Alkofer-Fischer-Williams) within the rainbow-ladder truncation of the covariant Faddeev approach yield comparable hadron masses across a wide quark-mass range, with agreement to experimental and lattice data within approximately 10%, thereby demarcating the model sensitivity within this truncation scheme.

## Method

The authors solve the covariant generalized Bethe-Salpeter equation for mesons and the covariant Faddeev equation for baryons, truncating the interaction kernel to a dressed gluon exchange (rainbow-ladder truncation). They employ two distinct effective interactions for the quark-gluon vertex: the Maris-Tandy (MT) model, which uses a Gaussian infrared distribution, and the Alkofer-Fischer-Williams (AFW) model, which is constructed from separate gluon dressing and quark-gluon vertex dressings. The three-body kernel for baryons is decomposed into permuted two-body kernels, omitting the three-quark irreducible contribution.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: 0.140 GeV (MT) and 0.139 GeV (AFW), fitted to experimental value 0.138 GeV
- Rho meson mass: 0.743 GeV (MT) and 0.710 GeV (AFW), compared to experimental 0.775 GeV
- Nucleon mass: 0.94 GeV (MT) and 0.97 GeV (AFW), compared to experimental 0.94 GeV
- Delta baryon mass: 1.26 GeV (MT) and 1.22 GeV (AFW), compared to experimental 1.23 GeV
- Omega baryon mass: 1.72 GeV (MT) and 1.80 GeV (AFW), compared to experimental 1.67 GeV
- Triply-charmed Omega (Omega_ccc) mass: 4.4 GeV (MT) and 4.9 GeV (AFW), compared to lattice value 4.7 GeV
- Triply-bottom Omega (Omega_bbb) mass: 13.7 GeV (MT) and 13.8 GeV (AFW), compared to lattice value 14.4 GeV
- Quark masses at renormalization point mu=19 GeV for MT model: 3.7 MeV (u/d), 85.2 MeV (s), 869 MeV (c), 3750 MeV (b)
- Quark masses at renormalization point mu=19 GeV for AFW model: 2.76 MeV (u/d), 55.3 MeV (s), 688 MeV (c), 3410 MeV (b)

## Context

This work extends previous covariant three-body calculations (Sanchis-Alepuz 2011, Eichmann 2011) by comparing two effective interaction models within the rainbow-ladder truncation, going beyond the dominant Maris-Tandy model (Maris and Tandy 1999) that has been standard in such studies. The results enable future investigations of beyond-rainbow-ladder effects, particularly in form-factor studies of nucleon and delta electromagnetic properties, and help identify which features of hadron masses are model-independent within this truncation scheme.
