# Unified mechanism behind the even-parity ground state and neutron halo of $^{11}$Be

> Using the axially deformed relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (D-RHFB) model, we explore the mechanism behind the parity inversion and halo occurrence in $^{11}$Be, which are well reproduced by the RHF Lagrangian PKA1. It is illustrated that evidently enhanced deformation effects by the $π$-pseudo-vector and $ρ$-tensor couplings in PKA1 are crucial for correctly describing both even-parity ground state (GS) and neutron halo of $^{11}$Be. Coupling with the deformation, the intrude $1d_{5/2}$ component largely enhances the couplings between the even-parity orbit $1/2_2^+$ and the nuclear core to promise the even-parity GS, whereas the $2s_{1/2}$ component therein dominates the halo formation in $^{11}$Be. Moreover, the deformed halo in $^{11}$Be is found to be stabilized by the attractive inherent correlations between the $1d_{5/2}$ and $2s_{1/2}$ components of the halo orbit $1/2_2^+$, instead of pairing correlations, which paves a new way to understand the halo pictures in deformed unstable nuclei.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the even-parity ground state and neutron halo of $^{11}$Be can be explained by a unified mechanism, but only when using the relativistic Hartree-Fock Lagrangian PKA1, which includes both π-pseudo-vector and ρ-tensor couplings. These couplings are essential because they enhance deformation effects that other models (PKO2, PKO3, DD-LZ1) lack, and the halo is stabilized by attractive inherent correlations between the 1d5/2 and 2s1/2 components of the halo orbit rather than by pairing correlations.

## Method
The authors used the axially deformed relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (D-RHFB) model with the PKA1 Lagrangian, expanding quasi-particle wave functions in a spherical Dirac Woods-Saxon basis. They performed shape-constrained calculations of binding energy as a function of quadrupole deformation β for both even-parity (1/2+) and odd-parity (1/2−) blocked states, and decomposed the canonical single-particle energies into diagonal and off-diagonal (correlation) terms to analyze the contributions of different spherical components. For comparison, calculations were also performed with the RHF Lagrangians PKO2, PKO3, and the RMF Lagrangian DD-LZ1, using the finite-range Gogny force D1S for pairing.

## Key numbers
- The ground state of 11Be calculated with PKA1 has a large prolate deformation of β ≈ 1.2.
- The experimental binding energy of 11Be is −65.478 MeV; PKA1 overestimates it by roughly 3 MeV, giving −68.50 MeV.
- The one-neutron separation energy Sn for 11Be calculated with PKA1 is 2.10 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 0.50 MeV.
- The two-neutron separation energy S2n for 12Be calculated with PKA1 is 5.81 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 3.67 MeV.
- For spherical 11Be, the 2s1/2 orbit is loosely bound; in the deformed ground state, the 1d5/2 component in the 1/2_2+ orbit increases gradually while the 2s1/2 component decreases simultaneously.

## Context
This work extends previous attempts to explain the parity inversion in 11Be using particle-core coupling (Otsuka 1993, Sagawa 1993, Esbensen 1995) and deformation (Ragnarsson 1981, Li 1996, Pei 2006), which individually could not reproduce both the even-parity ground state and the halo in a unified framework. The authors show that the D-RHFB model with PKA1 succeeds where earlier energy density functional theories (e.g., Skyrme Hartree-Fock and relativistic mean field) failed. The finding that the halo is stabilized by inherent correlations between the 1d5/2 and 2s1/2 components, rather than by pairing correlations as in spherical halo nuclei (Meng 2006), opens a new avenue for understanding halo structures in deformed unstable nuclei.
