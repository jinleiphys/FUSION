# Systematics of Anti magnetic rotation in even-even Cd isotopes

> The lifetimes for the high spin levels of the yrast band of $^{110}$Cd has been measured. The estimated B(E2) values decrease with increase in angular momentum. This is the characteristic of Anti magnetic rotation as reported in $^{106,108}$Cd. However, alignment behavior of $^{110}$Cd is completely different from its even-even neighbors. A model based on classical particle plus rotor has been used to explore the underlying systematics and develop a self consistent picture for the observed behavior of these isotopes.

## Key claim

The high-spin states of the yrast band of \(^{110}\)Cd exhibit the characteristic falling B(E2) values of anti-magnetic rotation (AMR), but unlike the neighboring isotopes \(^{106,108}\)Cd where AMR is pure, the behavior in \(^{110}\)Cd arises from an interplay between collective core rotation and the shear mechanism, with the band head for the shear structure at \(12\hbar\) rather than the \(20\hbar\) that a simplified AMR picture would suggest.

## Method

Lifetimes of high-spin levels in the yrast band of \(^{110}\)Cd were measured using the Doppler-shift attenuation method via the \(^{96}\)Zr(\(^{18}\)O,4n)\(^{110}\)Cd reaction at 70 MeV, with gamma rays detected by the Indian National Gamma Array (INGA) of 18 Compton-suppressed Clover detectors. Lineshape analysis was performed using the code of Wells and Johnson with Monte Carlo velocity profiles, and the extracted B(E2) values were compared to a classical particle-plus-rotor model incorporating both particle-hole (\(V_{\pi\nu}\)) and hole-hole (\(V_{\pi\pi}\)) interactions of \(P_2(\cos\theta)\) form, applied to \(^{106,108,110}\)Cd.

## Key numbers

- In \(^{110}\)Cd, the effective lifetime for the top feed to the \(24^+\) level was measured as 0.35(02) ps.
- The B(E2) values in \(^{110}\)Cd at \(20\hbar\) are lower by approximately 25% compared to those in \(^{106,108}\)Cd at \(16\hbar\) and \(18\hbar\), implying an effective quadrupole moment roughly 50% lower in \(^{110}\)Cd.
- For \(^{106,108}\)Cd, the particle-hole interaction strength \(V_{\pi\nu}\) was found to be 600 keV, consistent with systematics for the A \(\sim\) 110 mass region.
- For \(^{110}\)Cd, the hole-hole interaction strength \(V_{\pi\pi}\) was estimated for the first time in a shears structure as approximately 150 keV.
- The core moment of inertia used for \(^{110}\)Cd was 19.2 MeV\(^{-1}\hbar^2\), corresponding to half the rigid rotor value.
- The band head spins for AMR in \(^{106,108,110}\)Cd are \(18\hbar\), \(16\hbar\), and \(20\hbar\) respectively, but in \(^{110}\)Cd the shear structure actually starts at \(12\hbar\) with \(\theta = 90^\circ\).
- The effective quadrupole moment \(eQ_{eff} = 1.1\ eb\) was used for all three isotopes in the B(E2) calculation.

## Context

This work extends the study of anti-magnetic rotation (AMR) previously reported in \(^{106,108}\)Cd by Simons et al. and by Datta et al., where the yrast bands show pure shear behavior with B(E2) falling as \(\sin^4\theta\) and no core rotation. The present measurement resolves the puzzling alignment behavior of \(^{110}\)Cd first noted by Juutinen et al., which could not be explained by Cranking Shell Model calculations assuming collective rotation. The classical particle-plus-rotor model of Clark and Macchiavelli, previously applied to shears bands in A \(\sim\) 200 and A \(\sim\) 110 regions, is here extended to include a hole-hole interaction term, enabling a unified description of all three Cd isotopes and providing the first estimate of the hole-hole interaction strength (150 keV) in a shears structure.
