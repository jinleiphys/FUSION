# Possible Dibaryons with Strangeness s=-5

> In the framework of $RGM$, the binding energy of the six quark system with strangeness s=-5 is systematically investigated under the SU(3) chiral constituent quark model. The single $Ξ^*Ω$ channel calculation with spins S=0 and 3 and the coupled $ΞΩ$ and $Ξ^*Ω$ channel calculation with spins S=1 and 2 are considered, respectively. The results show following observations: In the spin=0 case, $Ξ^* Ω$ is a bound dibaryon with the binding energy being $80.0 \sim 92.4 MeV$. In the S=1 case, $ΞΩ$ is also a bound dibaryon. Its binding energy is ranged from $26.2 MeV$ to $32.9 MeV$. In the S=2 and S=3 cases, no evidence of bound dibaryons are found. The phase shifts and scattering lengths in the S=0 and S=1 cases are also given.

## Key claim

Under the SU(3) chiral constituent quark model with the Resonating Group Method, the six-quark system with strangeness s=-5 may contain two bound dibaryons: the Ξ*Ω state with spin 0 and the ΞΩ state with spin 1, but only if the binding energy is evaluated within a specific range of model parameters (Set 1 and Set 2) that reproduce known baryon and scattering data.

## Method

The authors performed Resonating Group Method (RGM) calculations for the six-quark system with strangeness s=-5, isospin T=1/2, using the SU(3) chiral constituent quark model that includes confinement, one-gluon exchange, and chiral-field-induced potentials. Single-channel calculations were done for the Ξ*Ω system with spins S=0 and S=3, while coupled-channel calculations of ΞΩ and Ξ*Ω were performed for spins S=1 and S=2, using two parameter sets (Set 1 and Set 2) that fit empirical baryon masses and NN/NY scattering data.

## Key numbers

- Ξ*Ω dibaryon (S=0, T=1/2, L=0): binding energy 80.0–92.4 MeV, RMS radius 0.71–0.76 fm, scattering length -1.18 to -1.31 fm
- ΞΩ dibaryon (S=1, T=1/2, L=0) in coupled-channel calculation: binding energy 26.2–32.9 MeV, RMS radius 0.78–0.85 fm, scattering length -1.55 to -1.73 fm
- The Ξ*Ω dibaryon mass lies 4.0–16.4 MeV below the ΞΩπ threshold, making it stable against strong decay Ξ* → Ξ + π
- No bound states found for S=2 or S=3 cases with either parameter set

## Context

This work extends prior dibaryon predictions by Yu et al. (1995) for the ΩΩ and ΞΩ systems to the higher strangeness s=-5 sector, using the same SU(3) chiral constituent quark model previously applied to the H particle and NN/NY scattering by Shen et al. (1995-1998). The results suggest two new stable dibaryons that could be searched for in heavy ion collision experiments, and demonstrate that coupled-channel effects between ΞΩ and Ξ*Ω are substantial for the S=1 case, increasing the binding energy from 6.3–9.6 MeV (single channel) to 26.2–32.9 MeV (coupled channels).
