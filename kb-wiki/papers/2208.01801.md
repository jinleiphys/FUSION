# Modeling the electric dipole strength of neutron-rich $^8$He: Prediction of multineutron collective excitations

> We investigate the multineutron excitations of neutron-rich $^8$He in the electric dipole strength using a $^4$He+$n$+$n$+$n$+$n$ five-body model. Many-body unbound states of $^8$He are obtained in the complex scaling method. We found that the different excitation modes coexist in the dipole strength below the excitation energy of 20 MeV. The strength below 10 MeV comes from the $^7$He+$n$ channel, indicating the sequential breakup of $^8$He via the $^7$He resonance to $^6$He+$n$+$n$. Above 10 MeV, the strength is obtained from many-body continuum states with strong configuration mixings, suggesting a new collective motion of four neutrons with the dipole oscillation against $^4$He.

## Key claim

The electric dipole strength of neutron-rich \(^8\)He below 20 MeV excitation energy is shown to contain two distinct components: a sequential breakup via the \(^7\)He resonance below 10 MeV, and a new collective motion of four neutrons oscillating against the \(^4\)He core above 10 MeV, but the collective state is not a discrete resonance, it emerges from non-resonant many-body continuum states with strong configuration mixing.

## Method

The authors employed a \(^4\)He+\(n\)+\(n\)+\(n\)+\(n\) five-body cluster model using the cluster orbital shell model (COSM) to describe \(^8\)He. Many-body unbound states were obtained by solving the complex-scaled Schrödinger equation using the complex scaling method (CSM), which rotates coordinates into the complex plane to expose resonances and continuum states. The electric dipole strength function was then calculated using the complex-scaled Green's function, and decomposed into contributions from different breakup channels (e.g., \(^7\)He+\(n\), \(^6\)He+\(n\)+\(n\), \(^4\)He+\(n\)+\(n\)+\(n\)+\(n\)) via the extended completeness relation.

## Key numbers

* The ground state of \(^8\)He is calculated at \(-3.22\) MeV relative to the \(^4\)He+\(n\)+\(n\)+\(n\)+\(n\) five-body threshold.
* The lowest particle-emission threshold in \(^8\)He is the \(^6\)He+\(n\)+\(n\) three-body channel at 2.1 MeV excitation energy; the \(^7\)He+\(n\) channel opens at 2.6 MeV.
* The dipole strength shows a spike at 2.8 MeV (just above the \(^7\)He+\(n\) threshold) and a mild peak at around 5 MeV, then gradually decreases above 5 MeV.
* A broad peak from collective states appears at around 13 MeV excitation energy.
* The relative distance between \(^4\)He and the four-neutron system in the ground state is 2.05 fm; a harmonic-oscillator estimate gives \(\hbar\omega = 17\) MeV for the dipole excitation of the relative motion, about 4 MeV above the calculated collective peak.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous systematic studies of neutron-rich He isotopes using the same five-body cluster model and CSM (Myo 2010, Myo 2012, Myo 2021). It contrasts with the electric dipole response of two-neutron halo nuclei such as \(^6\)He and \(^{11}\)Li (Myo 2001, Myo 2007b, Kikuchi 2010, Kikuchi 2013), where no such collective four-neutron mode was seen. The predicted collective excitation at 13 MeV offers a target for experimental Coulomb breakup measurements (Lehr 2022) and relates to the ongoing search for multineutron systems such as the tetraneutron (Kisamori 2016, Duer 2022).
