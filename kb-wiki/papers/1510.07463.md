# Chiral symmetry breaking and monopoles

> To understand the relation between the chiral symmetry breaking and monopoles, the chiral condensate which is the order parameter of the chiral symmetry breaking is calculated in the $\overline{\mbox{MS}}$ scheme at 2 [GeV]. First, we add one pair of monopoles, varying the monopole charges $m_{c}$ from zero to four, to SU(3) quenched configurations by a monopole creation operator. The low-lying eigenvalues of the Overlap Dirac operator are computed from the gauge links of the normal configurations and the configurations with additional monopoles. Next, we compare the distributions of the nearest-neighbor spacing of the low-lying eigenvalues with the prediction of the random matrix theory. The low-lying eigenvalues not depending on the scale parameter $Σ$ are compared to the prediction of the random matrix theory. The results show the consistency with the random matrix theory. Thus, the additional monopoles do not affect the low-lying eigenvalues. Moreover, we discover that the additional monopoles increase the scale parameter $Σ$. We then evaluate the chiral condensate in the $\overline{\mbox{MS}}$ scheme at 2 [GeV] from the scale parameter $Σ$ and the renormalization constant $Z_{S}$. The final results clearly show that the chiral condensate linearly decreases by increasing the monopole charges.

# Chiral symmetry breaking and monopoles

## Key claim

The chiral condensate in quenched SU(3) lattice QCD linearly decreases as the magnetic charge of an added monopole-antimonopole pair is increased from zero to four, providing evidence that monopoles are related to chiral symmetry breaking; however, the additional monopoles do not affect the low-lying eigenvalues of the Overlap Dirac operator or their consistency with random matrix theory predictions.

## Method

The authors generated SU(3) quenched configurations on a $14^4$ lattice at $\beta = 6.00$ using the Wilson gauge action, then added one monopole-antimonopole pair with magnetic charges $m_c = 0, 1, 2, 3, 4$ via a monopole creation operator. They computed the low-lying eigenvalues of the Overlap Dirac operator from the gauge links, compared the nearest-neighbor spacing distributions and scale-independent eigenvalue ratios with random matrix theory predictions, and extracted the scale parameter $\Sigma$ from fits to the lowest eigenvalue distributions in each topological charge sector. The chiral condensate in the $\overline{\mathrm{MS}}$ scheme at 2 GeV was then evaluated from $\Sigma$ and the renormalization constant $Z_S$ computed from pseudo-scalar masses.

## Key numbers

- Chiral condensate at the continuum limit from normal configurations: $\langle \bar{\psi} \psi \rangle^{\overline{MS}} (2\ \mathrm{GeV}) = -\{285(4)\ \mathrm{MeV}\}^3$ (from $r_0 = 0.5\ \mathrm{fm}$)
- For $m_c = 0$: $\langle\bar{\psi}\psi\rangle^{\overline{MS}} = -0.0217(9)\ \mathrm{GeV}^3$, $\{\langle\bar{\psi}\psi\rangle^{\overline{MS}}\}^{1/3} = -279(4)\ \mathrm{MeV}$
- For $m_c = 4$: $\langle\bar{\psi}\psi\rangle^{\overline{MS}} = -0.0347(15)\ \mathrm{GeV}^3$, $\{\langle\bar{\psi}\psi\rangle^{\overline{MS}}\}^{1/3} = -326(5)\ \mathrm{MeV}$
- Linear fit parameters: slope $A = -0.0036(4)\ \mathrm{GeV}^3$ per unit $m_c$ for the condensate, slope $C = -12.9(1.4)\ \mathrm{MeV}$ per unit $m_c$ for the cube root
- Scale parameter for normal configurations: $r_0^3\Sigma^{(1)} = 0.263(6)$, $r_0^3\Sigma^{(2)} = 0.264(7)$
- Scale parameter for $m_c = 4$: $r_0^3\Sigma^{(1)} = 0.417(11)$, $r_0^3\Sigma^{(2)} = 0.418(11)$
- Lattice parameters: $V = 14^4$, $\beta = 6.00$ ($a/r_0 = 0.1863$), $N_{\mathrm{conf}} = O(2\times10^3)$ per charge value

## Context

This work extends previous findings by Di Giacomo et al. (2015) that monopole-antimonopole pairs create instantons and that the chiral condensate decreases with monopole charge when computed directly from eigenvalues. The present study confirms this result using the more rigorous method of extracting the condensate from the scale parameter $\Sigma$ via random matrix theory fits and renormalization in the $\overline{\mathrm{MS}}$ scheme. The continuum limit result for the chiral condensate from normal configurations is consistent with prior determinations by Wennekers et al. (2008) and Giusti et al. (2003, 2004). The results enable further investigation of the mechanism by which monopole condensation induces chiral symmetry breaking in QCD.
