# Comment on "Determination of the chiral coupling constants c_3 and c_4 in new pp and np partial-wave analyses"

> In a recent study [M.C.M. Rentmeester et al., Phys. Rev. C 67, 044001 (2003)], the Nijmegen group reports on the determination of the chiral low-energy constants (LEC), c_3 and c_4, involved in the 2-pi-exchange part of the NN amplitude at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) of chiral perturbation theory. This analysis does not apply the uniquely-determined and model-independent NN amplitudes at NNLO and uses, instead, amplitudes that are up 90% smaller. We point out that this flaw produces a large systematic error, rendering the Nijmegen method unsuitable for a reliable determination of the LEC.

# Comment on "Determination of the chiral coupling constants c_3 and c_4 in new pp and np partial-wave analyses"

## Key claim

The Nijmegen group's determination of the chiral low-energy constants c_3 and c_4 from NN data is unreliable because their analysis uses NN amplitudes at next-to-next-to-leading order that are up to 90% smaller than the uniquely-determined and model-independent amplitudes of chiral perturbation theory, introducing a large systematic error.

## Method

Entem and Machleidt computed the correct on-shell NN amplitudes for the 1D2 and 3F4 partial waves using one-pion exchange plus two-pion exchange at next-to-next-to-leading order of chiral perturbation theory, with fixed constants g_A = 1.29, f_pi = 92.4 MeV, c_1 = -0.76 GeV^-1, c_3 = -4.78 GeV^-1, and c_4 = 3.96 GeV^-1. They compared these amplitudes to those used by the Nijmegen group (Rentmeester et al., 2003), which were obtained by representing the chiral OPE and TPE as an r-space potential cut off at r = 1.6 fm. They also note that the Nijmegen group applies chiral perturbation theory at NNLO up to 500 MeV, whereas the theory is appropriate only up to about 50 MeV in D waves and 150 MeV in F waves.

## Key numbers

- The Nijmegen group's NN amplitudes at NNLO are up to 90% smaller than the correct chiral perturbation theory amplitudes for the same constants.
- The Nijmegen group claimed to determine c_3 and c_4 with an accuracy of 2-5%.
- The cutoff radius used by the Nijmegen group to represent the chiral potential in r-space is r = 1.6 fm.
- Chiral perturbation theory at NNLO is appropriate only up to about 50 MeV in D waves and about 150 MeV in F waves, but the Nijmegen analysis applies it up to 500 MeV laboratory energy.

## Context

This comment directly criticizes the analysis of Rentmeester et al. (2003) and their earlier work (Rentmeester et al., 1999), which attempted to extract the chiral low-energy constants c_3 and c_4 from partial-wave analyses of pp and np data. The authors argue that the Nijmegen approach confuses the range-expansion philosophy of meson-exchange models with the momentum-expansion foundation of chiral perturbation theory, as developed by Weinberg (1990) and pioneered by Ordonez, Ray, and van Kolck (1992, 1994, 1996, 1999). The correct NNLO amplitudes for partial waves with L >= 2 are uniquely determined by OPE and TPE as derived by Kaiser, Brockmann, and Weise (1997) and Epelbaum, Gloeckle, and Meissner (2000), and the constants c_3 and c_4 should instead be determined from pion-nucleon data as done by Fettes, Meissner, and Steiniger (1998) and Buettiker and Meissner (2000). This work enables a proper model-independent extraction of the chiral low-energy constants from NN scattering.
