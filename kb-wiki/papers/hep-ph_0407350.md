# CFL Phase of High Density QCD at Non Zero Strange Quark Mass

> We compute free energy of quark matter at asymptotically high baryon number density in the presence of non zero strange quark mass including dynamics of pseudo Nambu-Goldstone bosons due to chiral symmetry breaking, extending previously existing analysis based on perturbative expansion in $m_s^2/4μΔ.$ We demonstrate that the CFL$K^0$ state has lower free energy than the symmetric CFL state for $0<m_s^2/4μΔ<2/3$. We also calculate the spectrum of the fermionic quasiparticle excitations about the kaon condensed ground state in the regime $m_s^2/4μΔ\sim 1$ and find that $(m_s^2/4μΔ)_{crit}=2/3$ for the CFL-gCFL phase transition, the leading order result reported in [1], is not modified.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the CFL$K^0$ (kaon-condensed) state remains the energetically favored ground state over the symmetric CFL state even when the expansion parameter $m_s^2/4\mu\Delta$ approaches unity, extending previous leading-order analyses that relied on the smallness of this parameter. The critical value $(m_s^2/4\mu\Delta)_{crit}=2/3$ for the CFL-gCFL phase transition, previously reported at leading order, is not modified by the higher-order corrections computed here.

## Method

The authors compute the free energy of quark matter in the CFL phase at asymptotically high baryon density, including the dynamics of pseudo-Nambu-Goldstone bosons from chiral symmetry breaking, using the High Density Effective Theory (HDET) in a mean-field approximation. They integrate out fermionic degrees of freedom and expand the effective action in powers of $m_s^2/4\mu\Delta$ to all orders (not truncating the series), then evaluate the free energy difference between the CFL$K^0$ and symmetric CFL ground states for spatially homogeneous, time-independent, gauge-charge-neutral configurations. The quasiparticle excitation spectrum about the kaon-condensed ground state is calculated from the resulting effective Lagrangian for the nonet of quasiparticle fields.

## Key numbers

- The CFL$K^0$ state has lower free energy than the symmetric CFL state for $0 < m_s^2/4\mu\Delta < 2/3$.
- The critical value for the CFL-gCFL phase transition is $(m_s^2/4\mu\Delta)_{crit} = 2/3$, which is $4/3$ times higher than the symmetric CFL value of $1/2$.
- The massless CFL BCS condensation free energy is $\Omega^0_{CFL} = -3\mu^2\Delta_0^2/\pi^2$.
- The critical strange quark mass at the phase transition is $m_s^{crit} = \sqrt{8\mu\Delta/3}$.
- The leading-order result for the pion decay constant is $f_\pi^2 = (21 - 8\ln 2)\mu^2/36\pi^2$.
- The hypercharge density in the CFL$K^0$ ground state is $n_Y = f_\pi^2 (m_s^2/2\mu)$ at leading order, and the numerical results remain close to this leading-order EFT prediction even for $m_s^2/4\mu\Delta \sim 1$.

## Context

This work extends the leading-order analysis of kaon condensation in the CFL phase by Schafer (2000), Bedaque and Schafer (2001), and Kaplan and Reddy (2001) into the regime where $m_s^2/4\mu\Delta \sim 1$, where the chiral expansion is expected to break down. It also addresses the CFL-gCFL phase transition observed by Alford, Kouvaris, and Rajagopal (2003) and by Ruster, Shovkovy, and Rischke (2004) at $(m_s^2/4\mu\Delta)_{crit}=1/2$ in the symmetric CFL case, showing that kaon condensation raises this critical value to $2/3$. The results enable future investigation of whether the kaon condensate persists in the gCFL phase and motivate construction of a low-energy EFT describing light bosonic and fermionic modes in the regime $m_s^2/4\mu\Delta \sim 1$.
