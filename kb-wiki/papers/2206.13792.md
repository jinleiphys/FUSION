# Neutrinos and their interactions with matter

> We have presented a review of the properties of neutrinos and their interactions with matter. The different (anti)neutrino processes like the quasielastic scattering, inelastic production of mesons and hyperons, and the deep inelastic scattering from the free nucleons are discussed and the results for the scattering cross sections are presented. The polarization observables for the leptons and hadrons produced in the final state, in the case of quasielastic scattering, are also studied. The importance of nuclear medium effects in the low, intermediate and high energy regions, in the above processes along with the processes of the coherent neutrino-nucleus scattering, coherent meson production, and trident production, have been highlighted. In some cases the results of the cross sections are also given and compared with the available experimental data as well as with the predictions in the different theoretical models. This study would be helpful in understanding the (anti)neutrino interaction cross section with matter in the few GeV energy region relevant to the next generation experiments like DUNE, Hyper-Kamiokande, and other experiments with accelerator and atmospheric neutrinos. We have emphasized the need of better theoretical models for some of these processes for studying the nuclear medium effects in nuclei.

## Key claim

This review paper systematically summarizes Standard Model neutrino interactions with matter across energy scales, but explicitly limits its scope to SM processes only, noting that phenomena like neutrino oscillations, CP violation, and flavor-changing neutral currents require physics beyond the Standard Model and are not covered in detail.

## Method

The authors present a comprehensive review of (anti)neutrino scattering processes from free nucleons and nuclei, covering quasielastic scattering, inelastic meson and hyperon production, and deep inelastic scattering. They discuss nuclear medium effects such as Fermi motion, Pauli blocking, and multinucleon correlations for nuclei including ¹²C, ¹⁶O, ⁴⁰Ar, and ²⁰⁸Pb, and compare cross section results with available experimental data and predictions from different theoretical models. The review also covers polarization observables for leptons and hadrons in quasielastic scattering, coherent neutrino-nucleus scattering, coherent meson production, and trident production.

## Context

This review extends and updates earlier review articles on neutrino interactions by Katori (2016), Alvarez-Ruso (2014), Morfin (2012), Gallagher (2011), and Sajjad Athar (2021), with a particular focus on nuclear medium effects across low, intermediate, and high energy regions. The work is intended to support next-generation experiments such as DUNE, Hyper-Kamiokande, and other accelerator and atmospheric neutrino experiments operating in the few GeV energy region, and emphasizes the need for better theoretical models of nuclear medium effects in nuclei.
