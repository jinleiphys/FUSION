# High-energy scissors mode

> All the orbital M1 excitations, at both low and high energies, obtained from a rotationally invariant QRPA, represent the fragmented scissors mode. The high-energy M1 strength is almost purely orbital and resides in the region of the isovector giant quadrupole resonance. In heavy deformed nuclei the high-energy scissors mode is strongly fragmented between 17 and 25 MeV (with uncertainties arising from the poor knowledge of the isovector potential). The coherent scissors motion is hindered by the fragmentation and $B(M1) < 0.25 \; μ^2_N$ for single transitions in this region. The $(e,e^{\prime})$ cross sections for excitations above 17 MeV are one order of magnitude larger for E2 than for M1 excitations even at backward angles.

## Key claim

All orbital M1 excitations, both low- and high-energy, obtained from a rotationally invariant QRPA represent the fragmented scissors mode, but the high-energy scissors mode is strongly fragmented between 17 and 25 MeV with uncertainties arising from the poor knowledge of the isovector potential, and the coherent scissors motion is hindered by this fragmentation.

## Method

The authors performed QRPA calculations for \(K^{\pi}=1^+\) excitations in the intrinsic frame using a rotationally invariant Hamiltonian with a Woods-Saxon deformed mean field, a symmetry-restoring isoscalar interaction, a decoupled relative isovector interaction with a free coupling ratio \(r = k_R/k_S\), and a spin-spin residual interaction. The calculations were applied to \(^{160}\)Gd with deformations \(\beta_2 = 0.26\), \(\beta_4 = 0.025\) and pairing gaps \(\Delta_n = 0.8\) MeV, \(\Delta_p = 1.1\) MeV, using bare orbital and effective spin gyromagnetic factors \(g_s = 0.7 g_s^{\rm free}\) and no effective charges for E2 transitions.

## Key numbers

- The summed M1 strength between 17 and 25 MeV in \(^{160}\)Gd is 3.6 \(\mu^2_N\).
- The strongest single \(1^+\) excitation in the 17-25 MeV region lies at 22.5 MeV with \(B(M1) = 0.25 \; \mu^2_N\).
- The theoretical E2 strength between 17 and 25 MeV (IVGQR with \(K^{\pi}=1^+\)) amounts to 1475 \(e^2 \; fm^4\).
- The isoscalar GQR with \(K^{\pi}=1^+\) at 11 MeV has \(B(E2) = 1193 \; e^2fm^4\).
- The total RPA E2 energy-weighted strength (0-30 MeV) exhausts 99.5% of the classical sum \(S(E21)_C = 57573 \; e^2fm^4\)MeV.
- The low-lying \(1^+\) excitations below 9 MeV overlap 77% with the scissors state, while those between 17 and 25 MeV overlap 55%.
- The \((e,e^{\prime})\) cross sections for excitations above 17 MeV are one order of magnitude larger for E2 than for M1 excitations even at backward angles (\(\theta = 165^{\circ}\)).

## Context

This work extends earlier QRPA studies by Nojarov and Faessler (1988, 1994) that showed the energy and amount of high-lying M1 strength depend strongly on the residual quadrupole-quadrupole interaction, and it addresses the controversy over whether the scissors mode appears at low energy (as in the Two-Rotor Model by Lo Iudice and Palumbo, 1978) or high energy (as found by Speth and Zawischa, 1988-1993 using Landau-Migdal interactions). The results enable experimental guidance for identifying the isovector giant quadrupole resonance in heavy deformed nuclei and show that high-energy M1 excitations will be difficult to resolve in \((e,e^{\prime})\) experiments due to E2 dominance and strong fragmentation.
