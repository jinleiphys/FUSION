# Local energy-momentum conservation and initial conditions for quark-gluon plasma evolution in nucleus-nucleus collisions at SPS and RHIC BES energies

> We investigate consequences of local energy-momentum conservation for initial eccentricity coefficients in heavy ion collisions at not too high energies relevant for CERN SPS and RHIC BES. Different models of energy density available for pion production are considered. We study dependence of eccentricity coefficients on space-time rapidity for different impact parameters. The naive formula how to define eccentricities breaks with our initial conditions and must be corrected. We predict considerable eccentricities for $ε_{1,2,3,4}$ and specific dependence on space-time rapidity as well as on impact parameter for lower energies. The effect becomes smaller at larger energies when restricting to narrow rapidity interval arround zero. Our predictions are in principle input for further hydrodynamical evolution but it is not clear whether they can be easily used. Our initial condition suggest a strong preequilibrium phase which is difficult for modelling.

## Key claim

Local energy-momentum conservation in peripheral heavy-ion collisions forces a strong, position-dependent longitudinal flow of the created plasma, which breaks the standard midrapidity definition of eccentricity coefficients and requires a rapidity-dependent shift of the reference point; the resulting eccentricities are large and strongly rapidity-dependent, but the model predicts a strong preequilibrium phase that is difficult to model and it is not clear whether the initial conditions can be easily used for further hydrodynamical evolution.

## Method

The author models the initial energy density in the transverse plane using thickness functions from Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov nuclear densities, then distributes that energy in space-time rapidity around a local stopping rapidity \(y_{stop}(x,y;b)\) using either a Gaussian or a step-like transparency function. The available energy for particle production is obtained by subtracting the cold mass energy and the kinetic energy of the collective longitudinal flow from the total energy density. Eccentricity coefficients \(\epsilon_n\) are computed as functions of space-time rapidity, first with the naive formula centered at \((x,y)=(0,0)\) and then with a corrected formula that shifts the origin to the energy-density-weighted center \(x_{shift}(\eta_s;b)\).

## Key numbers

* For Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 17.3\) GeV, the energy available for particle production is about 10% smaller than the total energy in the collision, with the relative global effect only weakly dependent on impact parameter.
* The Gaussian transparency function uses \(\sigma_\eta = 1.0\), adjusted roughly to experimental pion rapidity distributions; the value \(\sigma_\eta = 0.3\) from Szczurek and Andrzej (2020) is considered too narrow.
* The shift \(x_{shift}\) for large \(|\eta_s|\) is "really large" at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 17.3\) GeV, shown for impact parameters \(b = 2, 4, 6, 8\) fm with Gaussian transparency \(\sigma_\eta = 1\).
* The corrected eccentricity coefficients \(\epsilon_2\) and \(\epsilon_4\) are "relatively large" and "rather weakly dependent on space-time rapidity", while \(\epsilon_1\) and \(\epsilon_3\) are "rather small" at midrapidity but become "nonnegligible" at large space-time rapidities.
* A simplified hydrodynamical relation gives \(v_2 \approx 0.25 \epsilon_2\) at midrapidity, cited from Nijs, van der Schee, and Gürsoy (2016).

## Context

This work extends the fire-streak model of Szczurek, Kłusek-Gawenda, and Rybicki (2017), which introduced local energy-momentum conservation in longitudinal streaks, and contrasts with the approach of Szczurek and Andrzej (2020), which did not subtract the collective longitudinal energy from the total energy density. The results provide initial eccentricity coefficients as input for hydrodynamical evolution codes, particularly for the NA49 experiment at SPS and the RHIC Beam Energy Scan, and naturally explain the torqued initial conditions of Bożek, Broniowski, and Moreira (2011) that lead to forward-backward azimuthal correlations.
