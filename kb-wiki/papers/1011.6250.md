# Unified BCS-like model of pairing and alpha-correlations

> We construct a BCS-like model that combines nucleonic pairing correlations and possible quartic correlations of alpha-type in a single variational wave function and derive corresponding gap equations. In the approximation of large logarithms typical for the BCS approach, we show that the system reveals two possible types of a condensate which cannot coexist. If the alpha-type condensate prevails at N=Z, the growth of the neutron excess will naturally lead to the first order phase transition to the nucleon condensates.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a BCS-like model that combines nucleonic pairing correlations and quartic alpha-type correlations in a single variational wave function, and shows that in the approximation of large logarithms typical for the BCS approach, the two types of condensate (nucleon pairs and alpha clusters) cannot coexist; if the alpha-type condensate prevails at N=Z, the growth of neutron excess will naturally lead to a first-order phase transition to the nucleon condensates.

## Method

The authors introduce a trial wave function that includes, for each time-reversal-conjugate pair of single-particle states, the possibility of an empty cell, a pair with isospin projection t=0,±1, or a full four-nucleon alpha-like cluster. They derive variational equations for the amplitudes and corresponding gap equations for the isovector pairing gaps Δ_n, Δ_p, Δ_0 and the alpha gap Δ^(α). In the logarithmic approximation, they replace the interaction matrix elements by constants G and C within a layer of thickness Θ around the Fermi surface and convert the gap equations into integral equations that are analyzed for their leading logarithmic behavior.

## Key numbers

- The interaction layer thickness Θ is taken as the typical oscillator shell frequency, with the condition that all gaps are much smaller than Θ and ln(Θ/Δ) ≫ 1.
- For the pure pairing solution at N=Z with δϵ=0, the gap equation for solution type B gives 1 = G ∫ (ρ dϵ)/(2e) with e = √(ϵ² + Δ²/2), and the excitation energy is E₊ = 2e.
- For solution type A at N=Z, the gap equation gives 1 = G ∫ (ρ dϵ)/(2√(ϵ² + Δ²)), yielding a smaller gap than solution B.
- The relation between gaps in the symmetric case δϵ=0 gives two classes: 2Δ_nΔ_p = Δ₀² (type A) or Δ_n + Δ_p = 0 (type B).

## Context

This work extends the BCS theory of nuclear pairing, originally developed by Bohr (1958), Belyaev (1959), Migdal (1959), Soloviev (1961), and Thouless (1962), to include quartic alpha-type correlations alongside isovector pairing. It generalizes earlier discussions of coexistence and competition between pairing and alpha-correlations by Apostol (1987), Dumitrescu (1990), Koh (1996), and Baran (2006). The model enables future numerical studies of the phase transition between nucleon-pair and alpha-cluster condensates as a function of neutron excess, and provides an analytic framework analogous to the BCS-BEC crossover studied in cold fermionic atoms by Giorgini (2008).
