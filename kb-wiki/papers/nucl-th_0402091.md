# Hypernuclear structure from gamma-ray spectroscopy

> The energies of p-shell hypernuclear gamma rays obtained from recent experiments using the Hyperball at BNL and KEK are used to constrain the YN interaction which enters into shell-model calculations that include both Lambda and Sigma configurations.

## Key claim

The energies of p-shell hypernuclear gamma rays from Hyperball experiments at BNL and KEK can be understood within a single shell-model parametrization of the YN interaction that includes both Lambda and Sigma configurations, although the ground-state doublet of $^{10}_{\ \Lambda}$B remains unexplained.

## Method

Shell-model calculations were performed for p-shell hypernuclei using a basis of $p^ns_\Lambda$ and $p^ns_\Sigma$ configurations, with two-body matrix elements computed from the YNG interaction using harmonic oscillator wave functions with $b = 1.7$ fm. The $\Lambda$N effective interaction was parametrized by four radial integrals ($\Delta$, S$_\Lambda$, S$_N$, T) associated with spin-dependent operators, and the $\Lambda -\Sigma$ coupling matrix elements were calculated from the SC97f(S) effective interaction and scaled to match the observed $^4_\Lambda$He doublet splitting.

## Key numbers

- $^7_\Lambda$Li ground-state doublet separation: 692 keV (experiment), fitted with $\Delta = 0.432$, S$_\Lambda = -0.010$, S$_N = -0.390$, T = 0.028
- $^7_\Lambda$Li $5/2^+ \to 1/2^+$ transition: 2050 keV (experiment), calculated 2052 keV
- $^7_\Lambda$Li $7/2^+ \to 5/2^+$ transition: 470 keV (new from BNL E930), calculated 511 keV
- $^9_\Lambda$Be doublet $\gamma$-ray energies: 3024(3) keV and 3067(3) keV, separation 43(5) keV
- $^{16}_{\ \Lambda}$O ground-state doublet splitting: $26.5 \pm 2.3$ keV from $\gamma$-ray energies of $6558.6 \pm 1.4$ keV and $6532.1 \pm 1.8$ keV
- $^{11}_{\ \Lambda}$B strongest $\gamma$-ray: 1482 keV, with additional observed lines at 262, 454, 500, 564, and 2479 keV
- $\Lambda -\Sigma$ coupling energy shifts for $^7_\Lambda$Li $T=0$ states: 77, 6, 74, and 0 keV for $1/2^+$, $3/2^+$, $5/2^+$, and $7/2^+$ states respectively

## Context

This work extends the shell-model analysis of Millener (2001) by including explicit $\Lambda -\Sigma$ coupling effects, following the demonstration by Akaishi et al. (2000) of its importance for $A=4$ hypernuclei. The new $\gamma$-ray data from Hyperball experiments at BNL E930 (on $^{16}$O and $^{10}$B targets) and KEK E509/E518 provide constraints that enable determination of the spin-dependent components of the YN interaction. The results enable future few-body calculations for $A=4$ and $A=7$ hypernuclei to constrain the overall strength of $\Lambda -\Sigma$ coupling in the free YN interaction, while the unexplained $^{10}_{\ \Lambda}$B ground-state doublet remains an open problem.
