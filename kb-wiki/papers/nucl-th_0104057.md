# Benchmark Test Calculation of a Four-Nucleon Bound State

> In the past, several efficient methods have been developed to solve the Schroedinger equation for four-nucleon bound states accurately. These are the Faddeev-Yakubovsky, the coupled-rearrangement-channel Gaussian-basis variational, the stochastic variational, the hyperspherical variational, the Green's function Monte Carlo, the no-core shell model and the effective interaction hyperspherical harmonic methods. In this article we compare the energy eigenvalue results and some wave function properties using the realistic AV8' NN interaction. The results of all schemes agree very well showing the high accuracy of our present ability to calculate the four-nucleon bound state.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that seven different computational methods for solving the four-nucleon bound state problem produce binding energies agreeing within 0.5% when using the realistic AV8' NN interaction, but this agreement is conditional on the AV8' potential being a simplified version of the full AV18 interaction that omits charge dependence, \(L^2\) terms, and \((L\cdot S)^2\) terms.

## Method

Seven methods were applied to the \(^4\)He ground state using the AV8' NN potential: Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations in momentum space with partial-wave truncation; coupled-rearrangement-channel Gaussian-basis variational method; stochastic variational method with correlated Gaussians; hyperspherical harmonic variational method; Green's function Monte Carlo method; no-core shell model with three-body effective interaction; and effective interaction hyperspherical harmonic method. Each method solved the Schroedinger equation independently, and results for binding energy, kinetic and potential energy expectation values, radii, operator expectation values, and correlation functions were compared.

## Context

This work extends prior benchmark calculations for three-nucleon bound states (Nogga 1997, Kievsky 1998) and four-boson systems (Krivec) to the four-fermion case with a realistic nuclear force. The AV8' potential is a simplified version of the Argonne AV18 interaction (Wiringa 1995) that retains most of its complexity including tensor and spin-orbit components. The agreement among methods establishes a reliable foundation for extending calculations to larger nuclei (A > 4) and to more realistic potentials such as AV18, CD-Bonn, and Nijmegen, which pose additional difficulties for some methods.
