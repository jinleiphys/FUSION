# A Model Study of the Strength Distribution for a Collective State Coupled with Chaotic Background System

> We consider a model in which a collective state couples to a large number of background states. The background states can be chosen to have properties which are classically characterized as regular or chaotic. We found that the dynamical nature of the background system considerably affects some fluctuation properties of the strength function. }

## Key claim

The dynamical nature of the background system (regular vs. chaotic) considerably affects some fluctuation properties of the strength function of a collective state, although the gross structure (shape and low-order cumulants) of the strength function remains similar regardless of whether the background is integrable or chaotic.

## Method

The authors constructed a model Hamiltonian where a single boson collective state (energy ε = 220) couples via a simple interaction V_coupl = χ Σ (B^†|0⟩⟨N| + h.c.) to eigenstates of a two-dimensional anharmonic oscillator H_bg = ½(p_x² + p_y² + x⁴ + y⁴) − k x² y². The parameter k controls the transition from integrable (k=0.0) to chaotic (k=0.6) dynamics in the background system. They diagonalized H_bg in a basis of 5776 harmonic oscillator states, included the lowest 800 background eigenstates in the full diagonalization, and computed the strength function S(E) for the operator Ô = B^† + B at coupling strength χ = 1.0.

## Key numbers

- The collective state energy ε was fixed to 220, placing it in the middle of the 800 background states.
- For k = 0.0 (integrable), the second cumulant ⟨E²⟩_c = 842.5 and the third cumulant ⟨E³⟩_c = 28516.5.
- For k = 0.2 (partially irregular), ⟨E²⟩_c = 779.5 and ⟨E³⟩_c = 15784.9.
- For k = 0.6 (chaotic), ⟨E²⟩_c = 965.9 and ⟨E³⟩_c = −1942.3.
- The fractal dimension D₂ = 0.73 at k = 0.0, D₂ = 0.64 at k = 0.6, and D₂ = 0.98 for an ideal equidistant case.
- The fractal dimension D₅ = 0.69 at k = 0.0, D₅ = 0.47 at k = 0.6, and D₅ = 0.97 for the ideal case.
- The mean level distance of the background states was approximately 0.5, and the mean square coupling matrix element was approximately χ² = 1.0.

## Context

This work extends the random matrix theory approach to spreading width used by Guhr and Weidenmüller (1989) by explicitly modeling the background system's dynamics (integrable vs. chaotic) rather than assuming purely random coupling. It also connects to studies of level statistics in realistic shell model calculations by Brody et al. (1981) and Bae et al. (1992). The results suggest that scaled moment (multifractal) analysis of the strength function can distinguish regular from chaotic background dynamics, potentially offering a new tool for analyzing nuclear giant resonance data, though the authors note that finite experimental resolution and continuum effects limit direct application.
