# Coherent Vector Meson Photoproduction with Nuclear Breakup in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> Relativistic heavy ions are copious sources of virtual photons. The large photon flux gives rise to a substantial photonuclear interaction probability at impact parameters where no hadronic interactions can occur. Multiple photonuclear interactions in a single collision are possible. In this letter, we use mutual Coulomb excitation of both nuclei as a tag for moderate impact parameter collisions. We calculate the cross section for coherent vector meson production accompanied by mutual excitation, and show that the median impact parameter is much smaller than for untagged production. The vector meson rapidity and transverse momentum distribution are very different from untagged exclusive vector meson production.

# Coherent Vector Meson Photoproduction with Nuclear Breakup in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim

Mutual Coulomb excitation of both nuclei can serve as an effective experimental tag for selecting moderate impact parameter collisions in relativistic heavy ion interactions, producing vector meson events with median impact parameters much smaller than for untagged exclusive production; however, the authors note that the theoretical arguments for the dominance of the three-photon exchange diagram rely on the assumption that the elastic scattering of vector mesons does not collectively excite the nucleus, supported by STAR data showing similar rho meson transverse momentum spectra with and without excitation.

## Method

The authors calculated the cross section for coherent vector meson production (rho, omega, phi, and J/psi) accompanied by mutual Coulomb dissociation using the Weizsacker-Williams equivalent photon approximation, with the photon flux depending on impact parameter b through the nuclear charge form factor. The probability of mutual excitation was computed from photoexcitation cross sections for giant dipole resonances and higher-energy excitations, using a Poisson distribution to unitarize the probabilities at small impact parameters where the mean number of excitations exceeds unity. The total cross section was obtained by integrating over impact parameter the product of the vector meson production probability, the mutual dissociation probability, and a Glauber-model factor to exclude hadronic interactions.

## Key numbers

- Untagged rho production cross section in gold-gold collisions at RHIC (gamma_cm = 108): 590 mb, with median impact parameter b_m = 46 fm
- Rho production with mutual XnXn (any neutron emission) breakup at RHIC: 39 mb, b_m = 18 fm
- Rho production with mutual 1n1n (single neutron emission) breakup at RHIC: 3.5 mb, b_m = 19 fm
- Untagged J/psi production at RHIC: 0.29 mb, b_m = 23 fm
- J/psi production with XnXn breakup at RHIC: 0.044 mb, b_m = 17 fm
- Untagged rho production in lead-lead collisions at LHC (gamma_cm = 2940): 5200 mb, b_m = 280 fm
- Rho production with XnXn breakup at LHC: 210 mb, b_m = 19 fm
- Rho production with 1n1n breakup at LHC: 12 mb, b_m = 22 fm
- Untagged J/psi production at LHC: 32 mb, b_m = 68 fm
- J/psi production with XnXn breakup at LHC: 2.5 mb, b_m = 19 fm
- Nucleon-nucleon cross section used: 52 mb at 200 GeV per nucleon pair (RHIC), 88 mb at 5.5 TeV per nucleon pair (LHC)

## Context

This work extends the model for exclusive vector meson production developed by Klein and Nystrand (1999) by adding mutual Coulomb dissociation as an impact parameter tag, building on earlier calculations of mutual dissociation by Hencken, Trautmann, and Baur (1996) and Baltz, Chasman, and White (1998). The technique enables experimental selection of smaller impact parameter events, producing vector mesons with different rapidity and transverse momentum distributions than untagged production, and could also be applied to study two-photon interactions such as electron-positron pair production at impact parameters smaller than the electron Compton wavelength (386 fm).
