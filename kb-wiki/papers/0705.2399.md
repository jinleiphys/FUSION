# Gluonic phases and phase diagram in neutral two flavor dense QCD

> A numerical analysis of several phases in the cold neutral two flavor dense quark matter is realized. In the analysis, besides the normal, single plane wave LOFF, and color superconducting 2SC/g2SC phases, we also include two gluonic phases (the gluonic cylindrical phase II and the gluonic color-spin locked one). It is shown that these two gluonic phases are dynamically realized and, on the basis of this analysis, the phase diagram with respect to the coupling strength in this medium is suggested. In particular, it is shown that the gluonic phases are energetically favorable in a wide region of the parameter space. On the other hand, there also exists a window where the LOFF phase is stable. The 2SC state and the normal one are realized in the strong and weak coupling regimes, respectively, as was expected.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that two specific gluonic phases (the gluonic cylindrical phase II and the gluonic color-spin locked phase) are dynamically realized in cold neutral two-flavor dense quark matter and are energetically favorable over the 2SC/g2SC and single plane wave LOFF phases in a wide region of the parameter space, potentially resolving the chromomagnetic instability problem that plagues the 2SC/g2SC phases.

## Method

The authors performed a self-consistent numerical analysis using a gauged Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with two massless quark flavors, solving the coupled gap equations and neutrality conditions for the diquark gap, gluon condensates, and color chemical potentials. They computed the effective potential at the one-loop fermion level (hard dense loop approximation) for five candidate phases: normal, single plane wave LOFF, 2SC/g2SC, gluonic cylindrical phase II (with condensates mu_8, mu_3, and B = g<A_z^6>), and gluonic color-spin locked (GCSL) phase (with condensates mu_8 and K = g<A_y^4> = g<A_z^6>). The free energies of these phases were compared as a function of the zero-mismatch gap parameter Delta_0, which controls the four-diquark coupling strength.

## Key numbers

- The gluonic cylindrical phase II exists in the region 65 MeV < Delta_0 < 160 MeV for mu = 400 MeV and Lambda = 653.3 MeV.
- The GCSL phase exists in the region 76 MeV < Delta_0 < 160 MeV for alpha_s = 1.
- The single plane wave LOFF phase is stable only in the narrow window 65 MeV < Delta_0 < 67 MeV.
- The g2SC phase exists only in the range 92.2 MeV < Delta_0 < 134.6 MeV, appearing at larger Delta_0 than the gluonic phases.
- The normal phase is the ground state for Delta_0 < 65 MeV, and the 2SC phase is realized for Delta_0 > 160 MeV.
- At the endpoint of the gluonic cylindrical phase II, the ratio delta_mu / Delta = 0.84-0.86, exceeding the critical value 1/sqrt(2) = 0.707.
- The color chemical potentials reach at most |mu_3| ~ 50 MeV and |mu_8| ~ 40 MeV for mu between 300 and 500 MeV.
- The starting point of the GCSL phase shifts with coupling: Delta_0_start = 82, 76, 69 MeV for alpha_s = 0.75, 1.00, 1.25 respectively.

## Context

This work extends the Ginzburg-Landau analysis of gluonic phases by Gorbar, Miransky, and Shovkovy (2005, 2007) beyond the scaling region near the critical point, providing the first fully self-consistent numerical treatment. It contradicts the earlier approximate analysis by Fukushima (2006) and Kiriyama (2006), who neglected color chemical potentials and assumed the mismatch and gap were unchanged from the 2SC/g2SC values; the present results show those approximations are not justified. The findings enable future studies of the Meissner masses in the gluonic phases to confirm the absence of chromomagnetic instability, and open the door to investigating exotic phenomena such as anisotropic superconductivity, abnormal numbers of Nambu-Goldstone bosons, and vortex-like excitations in dense QCD matter.
