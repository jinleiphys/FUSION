# Accessing transversity GPDs in neutrino-production of a charmed meson

> We demonstrate that the transversity chiral odd generalized parton distributions can be accessed through the azimuthal dependence of the nu N -> l D N' or nubar N -> l D N' differential cross sections, in the framework of the colinear QCD approach, where GPDs factorize from perturbatively calculable coefficient functions calculated up to order mc/Q.

# Accessing transversity GPDs in neutrino-production of a charmed meson

## Key claim

The transversity chiral-odd generalized parton distributions (GPDs) can be accessed through the azimuthal dependence of neutrino-nucleon exclusive charmed meson production cross sections, but only because the heavy quark mass \(m_c\) in the hard scattering amplitude generates a non-zero transverse amplitude that couples to these otherwise inaccessible GPDs; the effect appears at order \(m_c/Q\) and relies on the collinear QCD factorization framework.

## Method

The authors compute the leading-twist factorized amplitudes for the exclusive reactions \(\nu_l N \to l^- D^+ N'\) and \(\bar\nu_l N \to l^+ D^- N'\) in the generalized Bjorken regime, using handbag Feynman diagrams where the hard subprocess involves a \(W\) boson scattering off a quark to produce a charmed meson. They include the heavy quark mass \(m_c\) in the off-shell quark propagator, which generates a non-zero transverse amplitude involving chiral-odd transversity GPDs, while the longitudinal amplitude involves only chiral-even GPDs. The differential cross section is expressed in terms of azimuthal angle \(\varphi\) and the observables \(\langle\cos\varphi\rangle\) and \(\langle\sin\varphi\rangle\) are derived from the interference between longitudinal and transverse amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- The transverse amplitude \(T_T\) is proportional to \(\frac{m_c}{Q^2}\), with the explicit prefactor \(\frac{iC \xi m_c}{\sqrt 2 Q^2}\) in the amplitude expression.
- The transverse cross section \(\sigma_{--}\) scales as \(\frac{4\xi^2 C^2 m_c^2}{Q^4}\) times combinations of transversity GPD form factors.
- The interference cross section \(\sigma_{-0}\) is linear in \(\Delta_T/m_N\) and proportional to \(\frac{m_c}{Q^3}\), with explicit prefactor \(\frac{- \xi \sqrt 2 C^2}{m_N} \frac{ m_c}{Q^3}\).
- The approximate expressions for \(\langle\cos\varphi\rangle\) and \(\langle\sin\varphi\rangle\) contain a factor \(K = -\frac{\sqrt{1+\varepsilon}+\sqrt{1-\varepsilon} }{2 \sqrt{\epsilon} } ~\frac{ 2\sqrt 2 \xi m_c}{Q } \, \frac{\Delta_T}{m_N}\), showing the suppression by \(m_c/Q\) and \(\Delta_T/m_N\).

## Context

This work extends the program of accessing transversity GPDs, which have proven difficult to measure due to their chiral-odd nature that decouples them from most hard amplitudes (pioneering works by various authors, followed by efforts reviewed in Barone). It builds on the factorization framework for meson leptoproduction established by Collins (1998) and on previous calculations of longitudinal amplitudes for neutrino production of charmed mesons by Kopeliovich (2012). The proposed method enables a new experimental avenue using planned high-energy neutrino facilities such as NOvA, which are primarily designed for neutrino oscillation studies but could also contribute to hadronic physics through exclusive charmed meson production.
