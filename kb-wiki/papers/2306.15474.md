# Mission Target: Tetraquark Mesons of Flavour-Cryptoexotic Type

> Currently, flavour-cryptoexotic tetraquarks form the most common sort of all experimentally established exotic multiquark hadrons. This note points out a few promising concepts that should help improve theoretical (but, for several reasons, not quite straightforward) analyses of such kind of states: among others, their scope of application encompasses the strong interactions in the limit of (arbitrarily) large numbers of colours, and equally analytical and nonperturbative approaches to multiquark states.

## Key claim

Flavour-cryptoexotic tetraquarks, which are the most common experimentally established exotic multiquark hadrons, require theoretical analysis that accounts for their unavoidable mixing with conventional mesons of matching spin and parity; this mixing is a complication not encountered for tetraquarks with totally disparate quark flavours.

## Method

The paper examines four-point correlation functions of quark-antiquark bilinear currents that correspond to scattering of two conventional mesons into two conventional mesons, identifying "tetraquark-phile" contributions that exhibit a nonpolynomial dependence on the Mandelstam variable s and develop a four-quark branch cut starting at the threshold (m_a+m_b+m_c+m_d)^2. The analysis applies the Landau equations to determine which perturbative QCD contributions can support intermediate-state tetraquark poles, and then studies the large-N_c scaling behaviour of these contributions for both flavour-preserving and flavour-rearranging correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- Tetraquark-phile contributions to flavour-cryptoexotic four-point correlation functions first appear at order O(α_s^2), corresponding to two internal gluon exchanges
- The large-N_c upper bound for tetraquark-phile contributions to both flavour-preserving and flavour-rearranging correlation functions is O(N_c^2 α_s^2) = O(N_c^0)
- The tetraquark-to-two-conventional-meson transition amplitudes A(T ↔ M_ab M_bc) and A(T ↔ M_ac M_bb) are both of order O(N_c^{-1})
- The total decay width Γ(T) of flavour-cryptoexotic tetraquarks is of order O(N_c^{-2})
- The mixing parameter g_TM_ac between a flavour-cryptoexotic tetraquark and its conventional-meson counterpart is of order O(N_c^{-1/2})
- Conventional-meson decay constants f_M scale as O(N_c^{1/2})
- Conventional-meson three-point amplitudes A(M_ac ↔ M_ab M_bc) scale as O(N_c^{-1/2})
- Conventional-meson decay widths are of order O(N_c^{-1}), meaning flavour-cryptoexotic tetraquarks are parametrically narrower than conventional mesons

## Context

This work extends the large-N_c analysis of tetraquarks by Lucha, Melikhov, and Sazdjian (2021-2023), contrasting with earlier predictions by Knecht and Peris (who found O(1/N_c) decay widths) and by Maiani, Polosa, and Riquer (who found O(1/N_c^3) decay widths). The paper argues these discrepancies arise from either trusting contributions that do not qualify as tetraquark-phile or neglecting N_c-leading contributions. The results enable systematic QCD sum-rule analyses of flavour-cryptoexotic tetraquarks by clarifying which correlation function contributions must be retained and how tetraquark-conventional meson mixing affects large-N_c predictions.
