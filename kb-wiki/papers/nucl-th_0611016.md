# Hypercentral Constituent Quark Model with a Meson Cloud

> The results for the elastic nucleon form factors and the electromagnetic transition amplitudes to the Delta(1232) resonance, obtained with the Hypercentral Constituent Quark Model with the inclusion of a meson cloud correction are briefly presented. The pion cloud effects are explicitly discussed.

## Key claim

The Hypercentral Constituent Quark Model (HCQM) with a hyper-Coulomb (Hyc) potential, when augmented with explicit pion meson cloud corrections, can reproduce the elastic nucleon form factors and the electromagnetic transition amplitudes to the Δ(1232) resonance in the low momentum transfer region, but the model without hyperfine mixing still underestimates the neutron electric form factor.

## Method

The authors model a baryon as a three-quark core described by the hypercentral potential V(x) = -τ/x + αx (with τ=4.59, α=1.61 fm⁻²) plus a pion cloud, using a πqq coupling Lagrangian with coupling constant g=0.585. They compute the electromagnetic current from photon-quark, photon-quark-with-pion-cloud, and photon-charged-pion couplings, restricting intermediate baryons to N and Δ states, and compare results from the Hyc potential with those from a conventional harmonic oscillator (HO) potential.

## Key numbers

- Three-quark core probability for the nucleon: Z₂^N = 0.567 in the Hyc potential, Z₂^N = 0.442 in the HO potential.
- Proton charge radius ⟨r⟩_P = 0.68 fm in the Hyc potential, 0.54 fm in the HO potential.
- At Q²=0, the Hyc prediction for the Δ(1232) helicity amplitude A₃/₂ is -243 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/² (with pion cloud) versus -187 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/² (without cloud), compared to experimental (-250 ± 8) × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/².
- At Q²=0, the Hyc prediction for the Δ(1232) helicity amplitude A₁/₂ is -135 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/² (with pion cloud) versus -108 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/² (without cloud), compared to experimental (-135 ± 6) × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/².
- The E2/M1 ratio for the N-Δ transition is -0.012, compared to the experimental value -0.015 ± 0.004 from the Particle Data Group.
- The γππ contribution to proton form factors exceeds 15% in the small Q² region and nearly vanishes beyond Q² = 1.0 GeV².

## Context

This work extends the Hypercentral Constituent Quark Model (HCQM) previously applied to baryon spectroscopy and form factors by Giannini, Santopinto, and collaborators (PLB364, JPG24, PRC62) by adding explicit pion cloud corrections. It contrasts with the Isgur-Karl model (Isgur) and the harmonic oscillator model, showing that the Hyc potential with meson cloud gives better agreement with experimental nucleon form factors and Δ(1232) helicity amplitudes. The results enable future studies of pion cloud effects on the Roper resonance and the S₁₁(1535) and D₁₃(1520) resonances, and suggest that including both hyperfine mixing and relativistic corrections will be needed for a complete description at higher Q².
