# The K+K+ Scattering Length from Lattice QCD

> The K+K+ scattering length is calculated in fully-dynamical lattice QCD with domain-wall valence quarks on the MILC asqtad-improved gauge configurations with rooted staggered sea quarks. Three-flavor mixed-action chiral perturbation theory at next-to-leading order, which includes the leading effects of the finite lattice spacing, is used to extrapolate the results of the lattice calculation to the physical value of m_{K+}/f_{K+}. We find m_{K+} a_{K+K+} = -0.352 +- 0.016, where the statistical and systematic errors have been combined in quadrature.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first lattice QCD calculation of the K+K+ scattering length, finding m_K+ a_K+K+ = -0.352 ± 0.016 at the physical point, where the statistical and systematic errors have been combined in quadrature; this result depends on the use of three-flavor mixed-action chiral perturbation theory at next-to-leading order to extrapolate from unphysical quark masses and finite lattice spacing to the physical continuum limit.

## Method

The calculation used fully-dynamical lattice QCD with domain-wall valence quarks on MILC asqtad-improved gauge configurations with rooted staggered sea quarks, predominantly on coarse lattices with b ~ 0.125 fm and L ~ 2.5 fm, with a preliminary calculation on one fine ensemble. The K+K+ interaction energy was extracted from the ratio of two-kaon to single-kaon correlation functions, and the scattering length was determined using Luscher's finite-volume method relating the two-particle energy shift to the infinite-volume scattering amplitude. Three-flavor mixed-action chiral perturbation theory at next-to-leading order, which includes the leading finite-lattice-spacing effects, was used to extrapolate the lattice results to the physical value of m_K+/f_K+.

## Key numbers

- m_K+ a_K+K+ = -0.352 ± 0.016 (statistical and systematic errors combined in quadrature) at the physical point after continuum and chiral extrapolation
- On the coarse MILC ensembles (b ~ 0.125 fm), the lattice values of m_K+ a_K+K+ (not extrapolated to continuum) ranged from -0.448(19)(20) at m_l = 0.007 to -0.590(15)(21) at m_l = 0.030
- On the fine MILC ensemble (b ~ 0.09 fm), the lattice value was m_K+ a_K+K+ = -0.391(28)(82) at m_l = 0.0062
- The mass splitting between domain-wall and staggered taste-identity pions was b^2 Delta_I = 0.0769(22) (lattice units) on the coarse lattices and 0.0295(27) on the fine lattices

## Context

This work extends the mixed-action lattice QCD methodology previously applied to I=2 pi-pi scattering (Beane 2005, Beane 2007) and I=3/2 K-pi scattering (Beane 2006) to the K+K+ system, providing the first lattice determination of this scattering length. The result enables improved analysis of kaon interferometry in heavy-ion collisions, where the non-resonant contributions to final-state interactions between kaons were previously estimated using three-flavor chiral perturbation theory alone, and provides input for understanding strange hadron interactions in neutron star interiors and kaon condensate formation (Kaplan 1986).
