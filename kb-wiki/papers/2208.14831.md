# Equation of state and strangeness in neutron stars

> A brief survey is presented of our present understanding of the equation-of-state of cold, dense matter and the speed of sound in the interior of neutron stars, based on the constraints inferred from observational data. The second part focuses on strangeness in baryonic matter and the role of hyperon-nuclear two- and three-body forces, with reference to the "hyperon puzzle" in neutron stars and possible scenarios for its solution.

# Equation of state and strangeness in neutron stars

## Key claim
The paper surveys current understanding that the speed of sound in neutron stars systematically exceeds the conformal limit (c_s² = 1/3) at energy densities above ~0.5 GeV/fm³, and that repulsive hyperon-nuclear three-body forces may prevent the appearance of Λ hyperons in neutron star cores up to ~2 M⊙, potentially resolving the "hyperon puzzle", though the author notes that uncertainties remain and the puzzle is not yet solved.

## Method
The author reviews Bayesian inference analyses of neutron star observational data (masses from Shapiro delay measurements, radii from NICER X-ray profiles, tidal deformabilities from gravitational wave events GW170817 and GW190425) to constrain the equation-of-state and sound velocity. For the strangeness component, Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations using chiral SU(3) effective field theory at next-to-leading order (NLO19) for hyperon-nucleon two-body forces are combined with ΛNN three-body forces derived from decuplet-dominance approximations, and the resulting chemical potentials are compared to determine hyperon onset conditions in neutron star matter.

- PSR J0348+0432 mass: M = 2.01 ± 0.04 M⊙
- PSR J0740+6620 mass: M = 2.08 ± 0.07 M⊙
- PSR J0030+0451 radius: 13.02^{+1.24}_{-1.06} km (Miller 2019) and 12.71^{+1.14}_{-1.19} km (Riley 2019) at 68% credible level
- PSR J0740+6620 radius: 13.7^{+2.6}_{-1.5} km (Miller 2021) and 12.39^{+1.30}_{-0.98} km (Riley 2021) at 68% credible level
- GW170817 tidal deformability: Λ̄ = 320^{+420}_{-230} at 90% level; GW190425: Λ̄ ≤ 600
- Equilibrium density of normal nuclear matter: ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Central pressures in most massive stars: P ≳ 300 MeV/fm³
- Sound speed exceeds conformal limit at energy densities ε ≳ 0.5 GeV/fm³ (ρ ≳ 3 ρ₀)
- Empirical Λ-nuclear potential depth: UΛ(ρ₀) = -(26.5 ± 1.6) MeV, decomposed into two-body contribution U₀^(2) = -(40.4 ± 0.6) MeV and repulsive three-body contribution U₀^(3) = (13.9 ± 1.4) MeV

## Context
This survey extends the observational constraints established by Demorest (2010), Antoniadis (2013), and Cromartie (2020) for neutron star masses, and the NICER analyses by Miller (2019, 2021) and Riley (2019, 2021) for radii. The discussion of the hyperon puzzle builds on earlier work by Djapo (2010) and Lonardoni (2015) on hyperonic matter, and the chiral EFT framework follows Haidenbauer (2013, 2020) and Petschauer (2016, 2020). The Bayesian inference results for sound speed from Brandes (2022) and the deep learning approach of Fujimoto (2020) provide the most recent constraints. The paper enables future work on quantifying the balance between two- and three-body hyperon forces, and motivates next-generation hypernuclear spectroscopy at J-PARC and JLab with mass resolution better than 0.5 MeV.
