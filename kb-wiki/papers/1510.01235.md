# Entropy production in chemically non-equilibrium quark-gluon plasma created in central Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies

> We study the possibility that partonic matter produced at early stage of ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions is out of chemical equilibrium. It is assumed that initially this matter is mostly composed of gluons, but quarks and antiquarks are produced at later times. The dynamical evolution of partonic system is described by the Bjorken-like ideal hydrodynamics with a time dependent quark fugacity. The results of this model are compared with those obtained by assuming the complete chemical equilibrium of partons already at the initial stage. It is shown that in a chemically non-equilibrium scenario the entropy gradually increases, and about 25% of the total final entropy is generated during the hydrodynamic evolution of deconfined matter. We argue that the (anti)quark suppression included in this approach may be responsible for reduced (anti)baryon to meson ratios observed in heavy-ion collisions at LHC energies.

# Entropy production in chemically non-equilibrium quark-gluon plasma created in central Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies

## Key claim

The paper argues that about 25% of the total final entropy in central Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies is generated during the hydrodynamic evolution of deconfined matter, but only if the initial partonic matter is assumed to be chemically undersaturated (mostly gluons with quarks and antiquarks produced later). This entropy production occurs even in ideal hydrodynamics, unlike the usual dissipative entropy production in viscous hydrodynamics, and the authors propose that the resulting (anti)quark suppression may explain the reduced (anti)baryon-to-meson ratios observed at LHC.

## Method

The authors model the space-time evolution of quark-gluon plasma produced in central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV using (1+1)-dimensional boost-invariant Bjorken ideal hydrodynamics with a time-dependent quark fugacity lambda(tau). The equation of state is that of an ideal gas of massless gluons (always in chemical equilibrium) and massless quarks and antiquarks (with a time-dependent fugacity parameterized as lambda(tau) = 1 - exp[(tau_0 - tau)/tau_*], where tau_* is the chemical equilibration time). The freeze-out condition is set at T = 156 MeV, and the final entropy per unit rapidity is fixed to match the observed pion multiplicity of dN_pi/dy ≈ 2700 at y=0, using an entropy-per-pion factor nu = 6.3 from a hadron resonance gas model.

## Key numbers

- About 25% of the total final entropy is generated during the hydrodynamic evolution of deconfined matter in the chemically non-equilibrium scenario.
- The freeze-out temperature is set to T(tau_f) = 156 MeV, extracted from thermal fits of hadron ratios at LHC.
- The final entropy per unit space-time rapidity is dS(tau_f)/d_eta ≈ 1.7 × 10^4, derived from the observed pion rapidity density dN_pi/dy ≈ 2700 and entropy-per-pion factor nu = 6.3.
- For tau_0 = 0.1 fm/c and tau_* = 5 fm/c, the initial temperature T_0 = 1023 MeV and freeze-out time tau_f = 13.2 fm/c, with quark fugacity at freeze-out lambda_f = 0.927 and chemical potential mu_f = -12 MeV.
- For tau_0 = 0.1 fm/c and tau_* = 10 fm/c, the initial temperature T_0 = 1024 MeV and freeze-out time tau_f = 14.5 fm/c, with lambda_f = 0.763 and mu_f = -42 MeV.
- For tau_0 = 0.5 fm/c and tau_* = 5 fm/c, the initial temperature T_0 = 598 MeV and freeze-out time tau_f = 13.2 fm/c, with lambda_f = 0.921 and mu_f = -13 MeV.
- The chemical equilibration time parameter tau_* ranges from about 1 fm/c (Ruggieri 2015) to about 5 fm/c (Xu and Greiner).
- The saturation scale Q_s is approximately 1-2 GeV, and gluon thermalization occurs at tau_g < 1 fm/c.

## Context

This work extends the "pure glue" initial scenario proposed by Stoecker et al. (2016, 2015) for Pb+Pb collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, building on earlier two-step equilibration scenarios by Raha, Shuryak, and Sinha, and by Bir93, Stri94, Tra96, Ell00, Dut02, Gel04, Scardina (2013), Liu (2014), Mon14, and Ruggieri (2015). The authors contrast their mechanism of entropy production via chemical non-equilibrium in ideal hydrodynamics with the usual entropy production via dissipative processes in viscous hydrodynamics. The paper proposes that (anti)quark suppression at freeze-out (lambda_f < 1) may explain the reduced (anti)proton-to-pion ratios observed by ALICE at LHC, which cannot be explained by the equilibrium hadron resonance gas model of Cleymans (2006), Andronic (2006, 2009). This is an alternative to the baryon-antibaryon annihilation mechanism proposed by Satarov (2013) and Prakash (2014), which predicts stronger centrality dependence than observed. The work enables future studies of thermal photon and dilepton emission from chemically non-equilibrium QGP, and motivates extension to (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamics with rate equations for quark and gluon densities.
