# Effective Field Theory Lagrangians for Baryons with Two and Three Heavy Quarks

> By analogy with pNRQCD, we construct Effective Field Theories suitable to describe the heavy-quark sector of baryons made of two and three heavy quarks. A long-standing discrepancy between the hyperfine splitting of doubly heavy baryons obtained in the HQET and potential models is solved. The one-loop matching of the 4-quark operators of dimension 6 is provided.

## Key claim

By constructing a potential non-relativistic QCD (pNRQCD) framework for doubly and triply heavy baryons, the authors resolve a long-standing discrepancy between the hyperfine splitting of doubly heavy baryons obtained in Heavy Quark Effective Theory (HQET) and potential models, provided the typical momentum transfer between the two heavy quarks is much larger than Λ_QCD (the "case (A)" hierarchy mv ≫ Λ_QCD).

## Method

The authors integrate out gluons of energy and momentum of order mv (the typical momentum transfer between heavy quarks) from NRQCD to obtain a pNRQCD Lagrangian for QQq and QQQ baryons, analogous to the established pNRQCD framework for quarkonium. For QQq baryons, the heavy-quark pair is decomposed into colour antitriplet and sextet fields, the Lagrangian is constructed with multipole expansion in the heavy-quark separation r, and the matching coefficients are computed at tree level and argued to receive no one-loop corrections. For QQQ baryons, the leading-order and spin-dependent potentials are expressed in terms of Wilson loop amplitudes.

## Key numbers

* The static potential for the antitriplet channel at leading order in α_s is V_T^(0)(r) = - (2/3) α_s / |r| (attractive).
* The static potential for the sextet channel at leading order in α_s is V_Σ^(0)(r) = (1/3) α_s / |r| (repulsive).
* The SELEX collaboration reported the ccd^+ state at a mass of 3518.7 ± 1.7 MeV with an average lifetime less than 33 fs.
* The one-loop matching of the 4-quark operators of dimension 6 is provided (matching coefficients d_{Q_h Q_{h'}} start at order α_s^2).

## Context

This work extends the pNRQCD formalism, previously developed for heavy quarkonium (Brambilla, Pineda, Soto, Vairo, 1999-2004), to baryons containing two or three heavy quarks. It resolves a discrepancy noted by Savage (1990) between HQET and potential model predictions for doubly heavy baryon hyperfine splitting, and provides a systematic EFT framework that can be used for precision calculations of doubly heavy baryon spectra (e.g., Ξ_cc, Ω_cc) and for predicting properties of triply heavy baryons (QQQ) that have not yet been observed experimentally.
