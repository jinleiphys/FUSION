# Two-phonon structures for beta-decay theory

> The $β$-decay rates of $^{60}$Ca have been studied within a microscopic model, which is based on the Skyrme interaction T45 to construct single-particle and phonon spaces. We observe a redistribution of the Gamow-Teller strength due to the phonon-phonon coupling, considered in the model. For $^{60}$Sc, the spin-parity of the ground state is found to be $1^+$. We predict that the half-life of $^{60}$Ca is 0.3 ms, while the total probability of the $βx n$ emission is 6.1%. Additionally, the random matrix theory has been applied to analyse the statistical properties of the $1^+$ spectrum populated in the $β$-decay to elucidate the obtained results.

# Two-phonon structures for beta-decay theory

## Key claim
The half-life of ⁶⁰Ca is predicted to be 0.3 ms with a total βxn emission probability of 6.1% when phonon-phonon coupling is included, but the authors note that neglecting γ-deexcitation of the daughter nucleus may lead to some overestimation of the resulting Pxn values.

## Method
The authors constructed single-particle and phonon spaces using the Skyrme interaction T45 within a Hartree-Fock-BCS plus quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) framework, then diagonalized the Hamiltonian in a space spanned by one- and two-phonon configurations following the quasiparticle-phonon model (QPM). The two-phonon configurations were constructed from 1⁺ and 3⁺ charge-exchange phonons and 2⁺ and 4⁺ phonons. Random matrix theory was applied to analyze the statistical properties of the 1⁺ spectrum by replacing the microscopic coupling matrix elements with Gaussian-distributed random numbers having the same root-mean-square value.

## Key numbers
- Half-life of ⁶⁰Ca: 0.3 ms (predicted with phonon-phonon coupling)
- Total βxn emission probability: 6.1% (with phonon-phonon coupling), compared to 4.8% within QRPA alone
- The 1₁⁺ state contributes 93% of the total half-life
- The dominant configuration {π1f₇/₂, ν1f₅/₂} contributes 94% of the wave function of the first 1⁺ state
- For comparison: pn-RQRPA (Marketin 2016) predicted half-life 5.3 ms with P_tot = 7.7%; DF3+cQRPA (B18) predicted half-life 1.8 ms with P_tot = 11.3%
- Random matrix theory with Gaussian-distributed coupling gave P_tot = 5.3%

## Context
This work extends previous QRPA studies of β-decay in neutron-rich calcium isotopes by incorporating phonon-phonon coupling, which redistributes the Gamow-Teller strength. The authors compare their results to pn-RQRPA calculations by Marketin (2016) and DF3+cQRPA calculations by B18, noting that their predicted half-life is significantly shorter while maintaining a similarly low total neutron emission probability. The approach enables analysis of β-delayed multi-neutron emission for exotic nuclei relevant to radioactive beam experiments and astrophysical r-process modeling, with the random matrix theory analysis suggesting that the restriction to two-phonon configurations is justified for half-life and neutron-emission probability calculations.
