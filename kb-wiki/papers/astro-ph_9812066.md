# A Model for Most Luminous and Long Duration Cosmic Gamma Ray Bursts

> We exploit the fact that General Theory of Relativity (GTR) predicts the existence of compact objects having surface gravitational redshift z_s < =z_c=0.615 when the Equation of State (EOS) has the stringest general constraint that the speed of sound c_s < = 0.57 c. This z_c can be occur if a Neutron Star (NS) has a mass of ~2 M (solar) and radius ~8 Km, as is allowed by most of the existing EOSs. Thus massive spherical stars can indeed collapse to such (more compact) NSs. We have shown that the efficiency for production of electron-positron pairs out of the annihilation of resulting neutrino-antineutrino burst rises dramatically with the value of z_c and can saturate to ~40%. This may explain a gamma ray burst of energy as high as ~5. 10**53 erg. By using the already existing detailed (Newtonian) calculations, it follows that the neutrino heating driven mass loss should be negligible, and the bulk Lorentz factor of the initial fireball could be ~1000. Most of the existing supernova calculations also show that it is extremely difficult to simulate the direct neutrino driven mass loss, and, the shock is not launched if the gravitational field becomes stronger. Since the gravitation potential well of the more compact NS is indded very deep, we do not expect additional baryonic mass ejection. So, without invoking any exotic physics (like strange stars) or overstretching any theory, we may explain most of the luminous GRBs in this simple model.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the most luminous and long-duration gamma-ray bursts (GRBs) can be explained by the formation of ultra-compact neutron stars (UCOs) with surface gravitational redshift up to z_s = 0.615, without invoking exotic physics like strange stars, provided the equation of state obeys the stringent causality constraint that the speed of sound c_s ≤ c/√3.

## Method

The author calculates the neutrino-antineutrino burst energy released during the formation of a UCO from the binding energy, then computes the efficiency of electron-positron pair production from neutrino-antineutrino annihilation using the standard weak interaction cross-section and a rectilinear neutrino propagation model, with a tentative (1+z_s)^2 correction for gravitational bending. The baryonic mass above the neutrinosphere is estimated from the neutrino-matter interaction cross-section and compared to existing Newtonian calculations by Duncan, Shapiro and Wasserman (1986) to determine the fireball Lorentz factor.

## Key numbers

- Maximum surface gravitational redshift for a static star with causal equation of state: z_c = 0.615 (from Bondi 1964)
- Neutrino burst energy: Q_ν ≈ 1.2 × 10^54 M_2 erg, where M_2 = M / (2 M_⊙)
- Neutrino temperature: T' ≈ 13.3 MeV × z_s^0.25 (1+z_s)^0.5 M_2^0.25 R_6^-0.5 t_10^-0.25, where R_6 = R / 10^6 cm and t_10 = t_ν / 10 s
- Mean neutrino energy: E_ν' ≈ 48 MeV for z_s = 0.6
- Efficiency of pair production: ε_± ≈ 15.5% for z_s = 0.615, M_2 = 1, R_6 = 1, t_10 = 1, saturating at ~40%
- Maximum fireball energy: Q_FB ≈ 4.8 × 10^53 erg for M = 2.5 M_⊙ and R = 7 km
- Baryonic mass above neutrinosphere: ΔM ≈ 10^-4 M_⊙ R_6^2, yielding Lorentz factor η between 10^2 and 10^3

## Context

This work extends the earlier suggestion by Mitra (1998) that accretion-induced collapse of a white dwarf to a neutron star could produce GRBs, but improves the model by using the tightest general relativistic bound on surface redshift (z_c = 0.615) rather than the canonical neutron star value (z_s ≈ 0.16). The model directly contradicts the common numerical simulation result that collapse becomes irreversible at z_s ≈ 0.16, attributing this to numerical inaccuracies in strong-gravity collapse codes. The paper enables a spherical, baryon-clean model for luminous GRBs without requiring non-spherical geometries, collapsar models (Woosley 1993), or neutron star mergers (Ruffert et al. 1997, 1998), and explains the energy of GRB971214 (~3 × 10^53 erg) without exotic physics.
