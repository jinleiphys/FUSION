# Testing the formation scenarios of binary neutron star systems with measurements of the neutron star moment of inertia

> Two low mass neutron stars, J0737-3039B and the companion to J1756-2251, show strong evidence of being formed from the collapse of an ONeMg core in an electron capture supernova (ECSN) or in an ultra-stripped iron core collapse supernova (FeCCSN). Using three different systematically generated sets of equations of state we explore the relationship between the moment of inertia of J0737-3039A and the binding energy of the two low mass neutron stars. We find this relationship, a less strict variant of the recently discovered I-Love-Q relations, is nevertheless more robust than a previously explored correlation between the binding energy and the slope of the nuclear symmetry energy L. We find that, if either J0737-3039B or the J1756-2251 companion were formed in an ECSN, no more than 0.06 solar masses could have been lost from the progenitor core, more than four times the mass loss predicted by current supernova modeling. A measurement of the moment of inertia of J0737-3039A to within 10% accuracy from pulsar timing, possible within a decade, can discriminate between formation scenarios such as ECSN or ultra-stripped FeCCSN and, given current constraints on the predicted core mass loss, potentially rule them out. Using the I-Love-Q relations we find that an Advanced LIGO can potentially measure the neutron star tidal polarizability to equivalent accuracy in a neutron star-neutron star merger at a distance of 200 Mpc, thus obtaining similar constraints on the formation scenarios. Such information on the occurrence of ECSNe is important for population synthesis calculations, especially for estimating the rate of binary neutron star mergers and resulting electromagnetic and gravitational wave signals. Further progress needs to be made modeling the core collapse process that leads to low-mass neutron stars, particularly in making robust predictions for the mass loss from the progenitor core.

## Key claim

A measurement of the moment of inertia of PSR J0737-3039A to within 10% accuracy, possible within a decade, can discriminate between electron capture supernova (ECSN) and ultra-stripped iron core collapse supernova (FeCCSN) formation scenarios for the low-mass neutron stars J0737-3039B and the companion to J1756-2251, and potentially rule them out given current constraints on predicted core mass loss.

## Method

The authors used three systematically generated sets of equations of state (EOS): two based on quantum Monte Carlo calculations of pure neutron matter (GCR+Model A and GCR+Model C) and one based on Skyrme energy-density functionals (Skyrme+Poly). They computed the relationship between the dimensionless moment of inertia I/M_G^3 of a 1.338 solar mass neutron star (mass of J0737-3039A) and the dimensionless binding energy BE/M_G of neutron stars with masses corresponding to J0737-3039B (1.25 solar masses) and the companion to J1756-2251 (1.223-1.237 solar masses). The parameter space was explored using Markov chain Monte Carlo simulations for the GCR models and systematic variation of the Skyrme+Poly parameters, with constraints from the maximum neutron star mass (M_max >= 2.0 solar masses) and causality.

## Key numbers

- The mass of PSR J0737-3039B is 1.2489 +/- 0.0007 solar masses; the companion to PSR J1756-2251 has mass 1.230 +/- 0.007 solar masses
- The ONeMg progenitor core mass range for ECSN is 1.366 - 1.377 solar masses
- Current supernova modeling predicts core mass loss of up to approximately 1.5 x 10^{-2} solar masses (0.015 solar masses) for ECSN
- If either low-mass neutron star formed in an ECSN, no more than 0.06 solar masses could have been lost from the progenitor core, more than four times the mass loss predicted by current supernova modeling
- For the lowest mass ultra-stripped FeCCSN, the baryon mass is approximately 1.35 solar masses with core mass loss of approximately 0.1 solar masses
- A measurement of I/M_G^3 = 16 +/- 0.8 (stiff EOS) constrains binding energy to approximately 0.085-0.1 solar masses, requiring core mass loss greater than 0.015 solar masses for J0737-3039B
- A measurement of I/M_G^3 = 14 +/- 0.7 (intermediate EOS) constrains binding energy to approximately 0.09-0.11 solar masses, requiring core mass loss greater than 0.005 solar masses for J0737-3039B
- Advanced LIGO can measure the dimensionless tidal polarizability to equivalent accuracy in a neutron star-neutron star merger at a distance of 200 Mpc

## Context

This work extends the I-Love-Q universal relations (Yagi 2013) to a correlation between moment of inertia and binding energy, which is less universal but more robust than the previously explored correlation between binding energy and the slope of the nuclear symmetry energy L (Newton 2009). The paper builds on population synthesis calculations (Andrews 2015) suggesting J0737-3039B most likely formed in an ECSN, and on binary evolution simulations (Tauris 2013, 2015) identifying ultra-stripped FeCCSN as an alternative formation channel. A measurement of the moment of inertia of J0737-3039A, combined with the I-Love-Q relations, can constrain formation scenarios and inform population synthesis calculations for binary neutron star merger rates and gravitational wave signals.
