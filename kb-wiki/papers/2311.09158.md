# Differential Rotation in Compact Objects with Hyperons and Delta Isobars

> Neutron stars may experience differential rotation on short, dynamical timescales following extreme astrophysical events like binary neutron star mergers. In this work, the masses and radii of differentially rotating neutron star models are computed. We employ a set of equations of states for dense hypernuclear and $Δ$-admixed-hypernuclear matter obtained within the framework of CDF theory in the relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) approximation. Results are shown for varying meson-$Δ$ couplings, or equivalently the $Δ$-potential in nuclear matter. A comparison of our results with those obtained for non-rotating stars shows that the maximum mass difference between differentially rotating and static stars is independent of the underlying particle composition of the star. We further find that the decrease in the radii and increase in the maximum masses of stellar models when $Δ$-isobars are added to hyperonuclear matter (as initially observed for static and uniformly rotating stars) persist also in the case of differentially rotating neutron stars.

# Differential Rotation in Compact Objects with Hyperons and Delta Isobars

## Key claim
The maximum mass increase from differential rotation relative to static stars is independent of whether the equation of state includes hyperons alone or hyperons plus delta isobars, with all models showing a consistent 16-17% mass increase at maximal differential rotation. The previously observed radius decrease and mass increase when adding delta isobars to hypernuclear matter persists for differentially rotating stars, but the magnitude of the rotational mass boost itself is unaffected by the particle composition.

## Method
The authors solved the general relativistic stellar structure equations for axisymmetric, differentially rotating neutron stars using the Komatsu-Eriguchi-Hachisu formalism with a linear rotation law parameterized by the dimensionless rotation parameter $\hat{A}^{-1}$. They employed four equations of state derived from covariant density functional theory in the relativistic Hartree-Fock approximation: one purely hypernuclear model (npY) and three hyperon-delta admixed models with varying delta isoscalar potential depths ($V_\Delta = 2/3 V_N$, $V_N$, and $4/3 V_N$). Stellar sequences were computed for rotation parameters $\hat{A}^{-1} = 0.3, 0.5, 0.7, 1.0$ at a fixed polar-to-equatorial radius ratio of 0.7, and density distribution maps were generated for individual stellar models at $r_{\text{ratio}} = 0.6$.

## Key numbers
- Maximum mass of static (TOV) hypernuclear star: 2.011 $M_\odot$; with maximal differential rotation ($\hat{A}^{-1}=1.0$): 2.356 $M_\odot$ (17.2% increase)
- Maximum mass of static star with $V_\Delta = 4/3 V_N$: 2.103 $M_\odot$; with maximal differential rotation: 2.461 $M_\odot$ (17.0% increase)
- Canonical 1.4 $M_\odot$ star radius at $\hat{A}^{-1}=1.0$: 17.65 km for hypernuclear EOS, 15.46 km for $V_\Delta = 4/3 V_N$ EOS
- Onset density for $\Lambda$ hyperons in differentially rotating hypernuclear star: 329.4 MeV/fm$^3$ at equatorial radius ~5 km
- Onset density for $\Delta^-$ isobars in $V_\Delta = 4/3 V_N$ star: 168.66 MeV/fm$^3$ at equatorial radius ~8 km
- Onset density for $\Delta^{++}$ isobars: 763.6 MeV/fm$^3$ at radius < 3 km

## Context
This work extends previous studies of static and uniformly rotating hypernuclear and delta-admixed stars by Li et al. (2018) and Li et al. (2023) to differentially rotating configurations, which are relevant for binary neutron star merger remnants following events like GW170817 observed by LIGO-Virgo (2017). The finding that the fractional mass increase from differential rotation is independent of exotic particle content enables simpler modeling of post-merger remnants, while the density maps showing competition between $\Sigma^-$ hyperons and $\Delta^-$ isobars clarify which exotic degrees of freedom dominate at different depths. Future work can use these results to constrain the delta isoscalar potential from observations of merger remnants and to study the evolution of differentially rotating stars as they relax to uniform rotation.
