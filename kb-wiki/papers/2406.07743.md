# Off-shell pion properties: electromagnetic form factors and light-front wave functions

> The off-shell pion electromagnetic form factors are explored with corresponding off-shell light-front wave functions modeled by constituent quark and anti-quark. We apply the Mandelstam approach for the microscopic computation of the form factors relating the model parameters with the pion decay constant and charge radius. Analyzing the existing data on the cross-sections for the Sullivan process, H(e,e',pi)n, we extract the off-shell pion form factor using the relation derived from the generalized Ward-Takahashi identity for the pion electromagnetic current. They are compared with our previous results from exactly solvable manifestly covariant model of a (3+1)-dimensional fermion field theory. We find that the adopted constituent quark model reproduces the extracted off-shell form factor $F_1(Q^2,t)$ from the experimental data within a few percent difference and matches well with our previous theoretical simulation which exhibits a variation of about 10\% for the extracted off-shell pion form factor $g(Q^2,t)$. We also identify the pion valence parton distribution function (PDF) and transverse momentum distribution (TMD) in terms of the light-front wave function and discuss their off-shell properties.

## Key claim
The constituent quark model with a symmetric (SYM) vertex reproduces the extracted off-shell pion form factor \(F_1(Q^2,t)\) from experimental Sullivan process data within a few percent difference, and matches well with the authors' previous theoretical simulation which exhibited a variation of about 10% for the extracted off-shell pion form factor \(g(Q^2,t)\).

## Method
The authors applied the Mandelstam approach to compute the microscopic pion electromagnetic current using a constituent quark-antiquark model with a symmetric (SYM) vertex function and Pauli-Villars regularization. They analyzed existing cross-section data from the Sullivan process \(^1\mathrm{H}(e,e',\pi^+)n\) to extract the off-shell pion form factor using the relation derived from the generalized Ward-Takahashi identity. The model parameters were fixed by fitting the pion decay constant and charge radius, and the results were compared with a previous manifestly covariant constant-vertex model using dimensional regularization.

## Key numbers
- Pion decay constant from the SYM model: \(f_\pi = 92.4\) MeV (with \(m_R = 0.6\) GeV, \(m_q = 0.22\) GeV), compared to the experimental value of 92.28(7) MeV.
- Pion charge radius from the SYM model: \(\sqrt{\langle r_\pi^2 \rangle} = 0.736\) fm (with \(m_R = 0.6\) GeV), compared to the experimental value of 0.672(8) fm.
- The quantity \(g(0,m_\pi^2)\) from the SYM model: 2.32 GeV\(^{-2}\), compared to the experimental value of 1.93(5) GeV\(^{-2}\).
- The monopole cutoff parameter for the pion-nucleon form factor: \(\Lambda_\pi = 0.80\) GeV, with \(G_{\pi NN}(m_\pi^2) = 13.4\), taken from the Jefferson Lab experiment.
- Constituent quark mass in the SYM model: \(m = 0.22\) GeV; regulator mass: \(m_R = 0.6\) GeV.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Choi 2019) which used a manifestly covariant constant-vertex model with dimensional regularization, by employing a more phenomenological relativistic constituent quark model with a symmetric vertex and Pauli-Villars regularization. The analysis enables a consistent description of both on-shell and off-shell pion properties, including the extraction of the off-shell form factor from Sullivan process data (Blok 2008, Huber 2008). The results also allow identification of the pion valence parton distribution function and transverse momentum distribution in terms of the light-front wave function, enabling further studies of off-shell effects in hadron structure.
