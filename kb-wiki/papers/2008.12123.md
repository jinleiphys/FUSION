# Inverse catalysis effect of quark anomalous magnetic moment to chiral restoration and deconfinement phase transitions

> The effect of quark anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) to chiral restoration and deconfinement phase transitions under magnetic fields is investigated in a Pauli-Villars regularized PNJL model. A linear-in-$B$ term for quark anomalous magnetic moment is introduced to the Lagrangian density of our model, and it plays the role of inverse catalysis to the phase transitions. With fixed magnetic field, the critical temperature decreases with quark AMM. When fixing quark AMM, the critical temperature increases with magnetic field for a small quark AMM, but decreases with magnetic field for a large quark AMM. The critical temperature of chiral restoration and deconfinement phase transitions is determined by the two competing factors, the catalysis effect of magnetic field and inverse catalysis of quark anomalous magnetic moment.

# Inverse catalysis effect of quark anomalous magnetic moment to chiral restoration and deconfinement phase transitions

## Key claim

The quark anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) acts as an inverse catalyst for both chiral restoration and deconfinement phase transitions under magnetic fields, competing with the usual magnetic catalysis effect; the critical temperature decreases with increasing quark AMM at fixed magnetic field, and for sufficiently large quark AMM the critical temperature decreases with magnetic field rather than increasing.

## Method

The authors employ a two-flavor Polyakov-extended Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model in the chiral limit, incorporating a linear-in-B term for the quark anomalous magnetic moment into the Lagrangian density. A gauge-covariant Pauli-Villars regularization scheme is used to avoid nonphysical oscillations that arise from hard/soft cutoff schemes under magnetic fields, with the quark coupling constant G = 7.04 GeV^{-2} and Pauli-Villars mass parameter Λ = 1127 MeV fixed by fitting f_π = 93 MeV and the chiral condensate. The thermodynamic potential is minimized via coupled gap equations for the dynamical quark mass m and Polyakov loop Φ, considering all Landau levels and longitudinal momenta.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant f_π = 93 MeV and chiral condensate ⟨ψ̄ψ⟩ = (−250 MeV)^3 used to fix model parameters in vacuum
- Quark coupling constant G = 7.04 GeV^{-2} and Pauli-Villars mass parameter Λ = 1127 MeV (with N = 3 regulated masses)
- Two sets of quark AMM parameters: κ_u^(1) = 0.00995 GeV^{-1}, κ_d^(1) = 0.07975 GeV^{-1} (small AMM); κ_u^(2) = 0.29016 GeV^{-1}, κ_d^(2) = 0.35986 GeV^{-1} (large AMM)
- Critical quark AMM values where magnetic catalysis turns into inverse magnetic catalysis: κ_u^(c) = 0.05072 GeV^{-1}, κ_d^(c) = 0.12422 GeV^{-1}
- Magnetic field values studied: eB = 10 m_π^2, 15 m_π^2, and 20 m_π^2 with m_π = 134 MeV
- Polyakov potential parameters: a_0 = 6.75, a_1 = −1.95, a_2 = 2.625, a_3 = −7.44, b_3 = 0.75, b_4 = 7.5, T_0 = 270 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of magnetic catalysis in PNJL models (reviewed in multiple references) and addresses the inverse magnetic catalysis puzzle observed in lattice QCD simulations (Lattice collaborations, 2010s). It builds upon earlier cutoff-regularized studies of quark AMM effects (Mei and Mao, 2019) by using a Pauli-Villars regularization to eliminate nonphysical oscillations. The results show that quark AMM can convert second-order phase transitions into first-order ones and that the competition between magnetic catalysis and AMM-induced inverse catalysis determines the phase structure, enabling future comparisons with magnetic field independent regularization schemes (MFIR approach).
