# Nature of Light Scalar Mesons in Bright Light of Photon-Photon Collisions

> The surprising thing is that the light scalar meson problem, arising 50 years ago from the linear sigma model (LSM) with spontaneously broken chiral symmetry, has become central in the nonperturbative quantum chromodynamics (QCD), because it has been made clear that LSM could be the low energy realization of QCD. First, we review briefly signs of four-quark nature of light scalars. Then we show that the light scalars are produced in the two-photon collisions via four-quark transitions in contrast to the classic P wave tensor q\bar q mesons that are produced via two-quark transitions γγ->q\bar q. Thus we get new evidence of the four-quark nature of the lower scalar states.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the light scalar mesons sigma(600), f0(980), and a0(980) are produced in two-photon collisions via four-quark (q^2 bar q^2) rescattering transitions, not via direct two-quark (q bar q) transitions, providing new evidence for their four-quark nature; this claim is hedged by the fact that the analysis depends on detailed modeling of rescattering mechanisms and final-state interactions rather than on direct observation of the four-quark states.

## Method

The authors performed a simultaneous analysis of high-statistics Belle data on the reactions gamma gamma -> pi+ pi-, gamma gamma -> pi0 pi0, and gamma gamma -> pi0 eta for energies up to 1.5 GeV. They constructed a dynamical model for the helicity amplitudes that includes electromagnetic Born terms (one-pion and one-kaon exchanges modified by form factors), strong elastic and inelastic final-state interactions in the pi+ pi- and K+ K- channels, and direct resonance-photon couplings, with the amplitudes satisfying the Watson theorem in the elastic region.

## Key numbers

- The predicted gamma gamma widths for a0(980) and f0(980) as four-quark MIT bag states are approximately 0.27 keV each, an order of magnitude smaller than q bar q model predictions.
- Experimental gamma gamma widths: Gamma(a0 -> gamma gamma) = (0.19 +/- 0.07 +0.1/-0.07) keV (Crystal Ball) and (0.28 +/- 0.04 +/- 0.1) keV (JADE); Gamma(f0 -> gamma gamma) = (0.31 +/- 0.14 +/- 0.09) keV (Crystal Ball) and (0.29 +/- 0.07 +/- 0.12) keV (MARK II).
- The q bar q model anticipated Gamma(a0 -> gamma gamma) approximately (1.5-5.9) times Gamma(a2 -> gamma gamma) approximately (1.5-6) keV, and Gamma(f0 -> gamma gamma) approximately (1.7-5.5) times Gamma(f2 -> gamma gamma) approximately (4.5-14) keV.
- The two-photon light scalar widths averaged over resonance mass distributions: <Gamma(f0 -> gamma gamma)>_pi pi approximately 0.19 keV, <Gamma(a0 -> gamma gamma)>_pi eta approximately 0.4 keV, <Gamma(sigma -> gamma gamma)>_pi pi approximately 0.45 keV.
- The q bar q model prediction for the 0++ -> gamma gamma coupling constants ratio g^2_f0 gamma gamma : g^2_a0 gamma gamma = 25:9 is excluded by experiment.
- The estimated cross section for gamma gamma -> K+ K- -> a0(980) -> pi0 eta at maximum is approximately 24 nb times R_a0, where R_a0 = g^2_a0 K+ K- / g^2_a0 pi eta.
- The pi eta scattering length a^1_0 = 0.0098 m^-1_pi, in agreement with chiral theory expectations of (0.005-0.01) m^-1_pi.

## Context

This work extends the authors' long-standing program (Achasov and Shestakov, various years from 1982 to 2010) arguing for the four-quark nature of light scalar mesons, building on earlier evidence from the inverted mass spectrum of the scalar nonet compared to the P-wave tensor q bar q nonet, and from the kaon-loop mechanism in phi(1020) radiative decays observed by SND, CMD-2, and KLOE detectors. The analysis enables future investigations of the sigma(600), f0(980), and a0(980) in gamma gamma -> K bar K reactions and in gamma gamma* collisions, where the four-quark contributions should decrease more rapidly with Q^2 than those from classical tensor mesons, as recently observed by the L3 Collaboration for the exotic q^2 bar q^2 resonance in gamma gamma* -> rho rho.
