# Quons Restricted to the Antisymmetric Subspace: Formalism and Applications

> In this work we develop a formalism to treat quons restricted to the antisymmetric part of their many-body space. A model in which a system of identical quons interact through a pairing force is then solved within this restriction and the differences between our solution and the usual fermionic model solution are then presented and discussed in detail. Possible connections to physical systems are also considered.

## Key claim

The paper develops a formalism for restricting quon (deformed-statistics) particles to the antisymmetric subspace of their many-body space, and shows that for a pairing interaction the quon deformation weakens Cooper-pair formation relative to the fermionic case, with the effect becoming more pronounced as the number of particles approaches the level degeneracy.

## Method

The authors construct normalized antisymmetric many-quon states by applying the usual anti-symmetrizer operator to products of quon creation operators, then derive the action of the annihilation operator on these states using the quon algebra \(a_i a_j^\dagger - q a_j^\dagger a_i = \delta_{ij}\). They apply this formalism to a one-level and a two-level pairing Hamiltonian, originally formulated for fermions, by rewriting the Hamiltonian with quon commutation rules and computing matrix elements in the antisymmetric basis. The ground state energy and the energy gap (the energy to break one Cooper pair) are then obtained as functions of the deformation parameter \(q\) and the number of quon pairs.

## Key numbers

- The energy gap in the one-level pairing model is \(\Delta = \frac{G\Omega}{2}(1+q^4) \frac{\{2N\}\{2N-1\}}{2N(2N-1)}\), where \(G\) is the pairing strength, \(\Omega = j+1/2\) is the level degeneracy, \(N\) is the number of quon pairs, and \(\{n\} = (1-(-q)^n)/(1+q)\) is the anti-deformed quantum number.
- For the two-level model with \(\epsilon=1\) and \(G=0.3\), the ground state energy is plotted as a function of angular momentum \(j\) for selected \(q\) values, showing the system becomes less bound when the deformation is turned on.
- The factor \(F(N) = \{2N\}\{2N-1\} / [2N(2N-1)]\) is always smaller than 1 for \(q \neq -1\) and equals 1 when \(q=-1\) (the fermionic limit).
- In the fermionic limit \(q=-1\), the term \(\langle H' \rangle\) goes to zero when \(N=\Omega\), but this is no longer true for other \(q\) values.
- For \(\Omega=40\), the ratio \(\Delta / (G\Omega)\) is plotted as a function of a redefined deformation parameter \(x = |q|^\Omega\) (with \(0 \leq x \leq 1\)), showing departure from the fermionic value of 1, especially when the number of pairs approaches \(\Omega\).

## Context

This work extends the formalism previously applied to the symmetric subspace (Avancini et al., 2002) to the antisymmetric subspace, enabling the study of fermion-like quons. The pairing model used is a standard one from nuclear physics (Cambiaggio et al., 1997) and has been previously investigated with quantum and quon algebras (Avancini et al., 2000). The results suggest that quon deformation could model deviations from fermionic behavior in composite particles with half-integer spin, such as complex atoms in high-density fermion traps (Amusia, 2001; Mottelson, 1999), where pairing interactions may occur.
