# The simplest ``strange'' three-body halo

> The recently developed method to solve the Faddeev equations in coordinate space is used to study the weakly bound halo nucleus, the hypertriton (Lambda + n + p). The long distances are treated carefully to achieve convergence and high accuracy. We use several sets of two-body interactions which reproduce the deuteron properties and provide the low-energy Lambda-nucleon scattering data close to that of two of Nijmegen potentials. We show that the details of the potentials are unimportant unless the accuracy of the hypertriton binding energy is required to be better than 50 keV. We find that the most significant parameter of the Lambda-nucleon interaction, the singlet s-wave scattering length, must be within 10% of 1.85 fm, when the Lambda-separation energy from the deuteron is about 130 keV. Other details of the Lambda-nucleon interaction are less important for the hypertriton structure. The scattering length and effective range are computed for scattering of a Lambda-particle on a deuteron. The folding model reducing the three-body problem to a two-body problem is investigated in this context and found to be inadequate.

## Key claim

The authors show that the hypertriton binding energy can be reproduced with about 50 keV accuracy without needing detailed knowledge of the Lambda-nucleon potential, provided the singlet s-wave scattering length is constrained to within 10% of 1.85 fm when the Lambda separation energy from the deuteron is about 130 keV.

## Method

The authors solve the Faddeev equations in coordinate space using a hyperspherical expansion, treating the long-range wavefunction out to about 50 fm. They parametrize the nucleon-nucleon and Lambda-nucleon interactions with gaussian, exponential, and Yukawa radial shapes, adjusting strengths and ranges to reproduce deuteron properties and low-energy Lambda-nucleon scattering data from the Nijmegen F and Nijmegen SC potentials. The three-body bound state and Lambda-deuteron scattering are computed, and the folding model reduction to a two-body problem is tested.

## Key numbers

- The Lambda separation energy from the deuteron is B_Lambda = 0.13 ± 0.05 MeV (experimental value).
- The deuteron binding energy is B_d = 2.224575(9) MeV and its root mean square radius is R_d = 1.971(6) fm.
- The estimated Lambda-deuteron root mean square radius is 10.2 fm, about 5 times the deuteron radius.
- The singlet s-wave scattering length of the Lambda-nucleon interaction must be within 10% of 1.85 fm to reproduce the measured hypertriton binding energy.
- Different radial shapes (gaussian vs. exponential) produce variations in B_Lambda of about 50 keV; including Yukawa shapes increases the spread to about 66 keV.
- A 10% increase in the singlet s-wave scattering length increases B_Lambda by 50-100 keV; a 10% decrease in the triplet s-wave scattering length decreases B_Lambda by 15-30 keV.
- The p-state contributions to the binding energy amount to approximately 10 keV for most interactions, up to 40 keV for Yukawa potentials.
- The d-state contributions in the Lambda-nucleon channel amount to 10-25 keV for most interactions and 55-70 keV for coupled-channel nucleon-nucleon interactions.

## Context

This work extends the authors' series on three-body halo systems (Fedorov et al. 1994a, 1994b, 1995) from Borromean and ordinary nuclear halos to the strange sector, using the hypertriton as the simplest example. It contradicts earlier Faddeev calculations (Migdal 1993) that found the Lambda-deuteron system unbound, and provides an independent coordinate-space verification of momentum-space Faddeev results (Afnan and Gibson 1989). The finding that the folding model is inadequate for this system, and that only low-energy Lambda-nucleon scattering parameters matter, enables future studies to constrain the Lambda-nucleon interaction from hypertriton binding without requiring detailed potential models.
