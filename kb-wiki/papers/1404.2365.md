# Relativistic Formulation of Reaction Theory

> A relativistic formulation of reaction theory for nuclei with a dynamics given by a unitary representations of the Poincaré group is developed. Relativistic dynamics is introduced by starting from a relativistic theory of free particles to which rotationally invariant interactions are added to the invariant mass operator. Poincaré invariance is realized by requiring that simultaneous eigenstates of the mass and spin transform as irreducible representations of the Poincaré group. A relativistic formulation of scattering theory is presented and approximations emphasizing dominant degrees of freedom that preserve unitarity, exact Poincaré invariance and exchange symmetry are discussed. A Poincaré invariant formulation of a (d,p) reaction as a three-body problem is given as an explicit example.

## Key claim

The paper develops a Poincaré invariant formulation of nuclear reaction theory by constructing dynamical unitary representations of the Poincaré group, where interactions are added to the invariant mass operator rather than to a non-relativistic Hamiltonian, and the authors note that the Bakamjian-Thomas construction for more than two particles fails to satisfy cluster properties, though these corrections are expected to be small in nuclear physics applications.

## Method

The authors start with a relativistic theory of free particles and add rotationally invariant interactions to the invariant mass operator, then solve for simultaneous eigenstates of the interacting mass and spin. They construct a basis of N-particle rest states using rotationless Lorentz boosts and constituent spins that can be added like non-relativistic spins, then project the exact mass operator onto relativistically invariant coupled-channel subspaces. The formulation is illustrated by constructing a Poincaré invariant three-body problem for a (d,p) reaction.

## Key numbers

- Model calculations show that exclusive breakup reaction cross sections using Poincaré invariant treatment can differ up to an order of magnitude from Galilei invariant calculations at projectile energies around 500 MeV
- At the same energy of 500 MeV, elastic scattering cross sections differ by about 15% between relativistic and non-relativistic treatments
- Realistic calculations with two and three-nucleon forces were performed for projectile energies up to 250 MeV, showing differences between non-relativistic and relativistic treatment at large momentum transfers
- Model calculations with spin-independent forces were performed up to 1 GeV

## Context

This work extends the Poincaré invariant formulation of Faddeev equations pioneered by Lin et al. (2007, 2008) for model calculations and by Witala et al. (2011) for realistic two and three-nucleon forces, where those calculations showed that breakup reactions are more sensitive to relativistic treatment than elastic scattering. The paper aims to provide a framework for interpreting experimental data from few-GeV scale nuclear reactions at facilities such as RIKEN, FAIR, GANIL, and FRIB, where non-relativistic approaches like coupled discretized continuum channel (CDCC) methods and standard Faddeev approaches are insufficient.
