# Universality of second order transport coefficients from the gauge-string duality

> We consider the strongly coupled limit of conformal gauge theory plasmas with conserved U(1) charges which have a gravity dual. We show that, under mild restrictions, the second order transport coefficients of such theories satisfy a universal relation among themselves, similar to the shear viscosity to entropy ratio.

## Key claim

The authors show that, for a broad class of strongly coupled conformal gauge theory plasmas with conserved U(1) charges that admit a gravity dual, the second order transport coefficients satisfy the universal relation \(4\lambda_1 + \lambda_2 = 2\eta\tau_\pi\), provided the bulk dual of the stationary fluid can be described by a stationary and static black hole that can be extended in the sense of Bhattacharyya (2008) and the boundary theory spacetime dimension is larger than three.

## Method

The authors consider a general action of gravity coupled to matter, including scalars and Abelian vectors, in asymptotically AdS spacetime. They assume the existence of a stationary, static black brane solution and use the fluid-gravity correspondence to perturbatively solve the Einstein equations to second order in derivatives of the hydrodynamic fields, following the method of Bhattacharyya (2008). By imposing conformal invariance of the boundary theory, which forces the coefficients of non-Weyl-invariant terms to vanish, they derive the universal relation among the second order transport coefficients and an explicit integral expression for \(\lambda_2\).

## Key numbers

* The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio is \(\eta / s = 1/(4\pi)\), universal for the class of theories considered.
* The second order transport coefficients satisfy \(4\lambda_1 + \lambda_2 = 2\eta\tau_\pi\) for boundary theory spacetime dimension \(d > 3\).
* \(\lambda_2\) is given by \(\lambda_2 = \kappa^{-2} \int_{r_+}^{\infty} \frac{\left(r_+^{2 d-2}+(f(x)-1)x^{2 d-2}\right)s(x)}{x^{d+1} f(x)}dx\), where \(r_+\) is the largest zero of \(f(r)\), and \(s(r)\) and \(f(r)\) are metric components of the bulk dual.
* The shear viscosity is \(\eta = r_+^{D-2}/(2\kappa^2)\) and the entropy density is \(s = 4\pi r_+^{D-2}/(2\kappa^2)\), where \(D = d+1\) is the bulk spacetime dimension.

## Context

This work extends the universal relation for second order transport coefficients, previously observed in specific examples such as Einstein gravity (Baier 2007, Bhattacharyya 2008) and five-dimensional Einstein-Maxwell gravity (Erdmenger 2008, Banerjee 2008), to a much broader class of theories with general matter content including scalars and multiple U(1) gauge fields. The result parallels the universality of the shear viscosity to entropy ratio established by Kovtun (2004) and Buchel (2003, 2004), but differs in that it relates transport coefficients to each other rather than to a thermodynamic quantity. This enables future work to test whether the relation holds for theories with more general bulk actions, including those with Chern-Simons terms or non-conformal boundary theories.
