# Non-Perturbative Lepton Sea Fermions in the Nucleon and the Proton Radius Puzzle

> A potential explanation [U.~D.~Jentschura, Phys.\ Rev.\ A {\bf 88}, 062514 (2013)] of the proton radius puzzle originating from the non-perturbative lepton-pair content of the proton is studied. Well-defined quantities that depend on this lepton-pair content are evaluated. Each is found to be of the order of $10({α\over π})^2$, so that we find such a lepton-pair content exists in the proton. However, we argue that this relatively large result and general features of loop diagrams rule out the possibility of lepton-pair content as an explanation of the proton radius puzzle. In addition, the contributions of a class of explanations of the proton radius puzzle (for which the dependence on the $μp$ relative distance is as a contact interaction) is determined.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while a non-perturbative lepton-pair content of the proton does exist and is larger than previously assumed, it cannot explain the proton radius puzzle because a correct, gauge-invariant loop calculation yields an effective interaction suppressed by the constituent quark mass rather than the lepton mass, making the effect negligible.

## Method

The author evaluates two gauge-invariant, ultraviolet-finite quantities that depend on the lepton-pair content of the proton: the contribution to the quark anomalous magnetic moment and the induced pseudoscalar axial coupling, both computed at zero momentum transfer using the dressed photon propagator with the lepton vacuum polarization. The calculations are performed for a single constituent quark with mass one-third of the proton mass, using Feynman diagram techniques with combined denominators and numerical integration over Feynman parameters. The results are then compared to the value of the lepton-pair probability needed in the Jentschura (2013) model to resolve the proton radius puzzle.

## Key numbers

* The electron-positron pair contribution to the quark magnetic moment is 3.4 (α/π)², or 1.8 × 10⁻⁵, which is about 100 times larger than the value ε_p = 2.1 × 10⁻⁷ used in the Jentschura (2013) model.
* The induced pseudoscalar coupling from electron-positron pairs gives J(electron) = 18.3, leading to an estimated ε_p ∼ 5 × 10⁻⁵, about 250 times larger than the Jentschura (2013) value.
* The ratio of muon to electron contributions is about 1/10 for the magnetic moment (I(muon)=0.17 vs I(electron)=1.7) and about 1/30 for the axial coupling (J(muon)=0.593 vs J(electron)=18.3), rather than the 1/200 ratio expected from a 1/m_l² dependence.
* For ⁴He, the contact-interaction model of Jentschura (2013) predicts a Lamb shift of about 10 meV, while the model of Miller (2012) predicts 6 meV and the model of Wang (2013) predicts 12 meV.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of the proton radius puzzle initiated by the muonic hydrogen measurements of Pohl et al. (2010) and Antognini et al. (2013), and specifically addresses the non-perturbative lepton-pair explanation proposed by Jentschura (2013). The author argues that the Jentschura (2013) model is ruled out because a proper gauge-invariant calculation yields a 1/m² dependence (with m the constituent quark mass) rather than the 1/m_e² dependence assumed, making the effect about 400,000 times too small. The paper also shows that all contact-interaction models for the proton radius puzzle predict rapidly increasing Lamb shifts with atomic number, which will be tested by upcoming experiments on muonic helium (Nebel, 2012) and the MUSE scattering experiment (Gilman, 2013).
