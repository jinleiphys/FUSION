# Light baryon resonances from a coupled-channel study including $\mathbf{KΣ}$ photoproduction

> The Jülich-Bonn dynamical coupled-channel approach is extended to include $KΣ$ photoproduction off the proton. Differential cross section and (double) polarization data for $K^+Σ^0$ and $K^0Σ^+$ are analysed simultaneously with the pion- and photon-induced production of $πN$, $ηN$, $KΛ$, and $KΣ$ final states, totaling more than 67,000 data points for center-of-mass energies $W<2.4$ GeV. Based on the fit results the spectrum of $N^*$ and $Δ$ resonances is extracted in terms of pole positions and residues. We discuss the impact of the $γp\to KΣ$ channels in detail and investigate the influence of recent polarization data for $ηp$ photoproduction.

## Key claim

The Jülich-Bonn dynamical coupled-channel approach is extended to include $K\Sigma$ photoproduction off the proton, and the extracted resonance spectrum depends on the simultaneous fit of over 71,000 data points across multiple channels, with the caveat that the absolute size of uncertainties on resonance parameters is not well determined because systematic uncertainties and amplitude ambiguities dominate the error bars.

## Method

The Jülich-Bonn dynamical coupled-channel (DCC) approach solves a Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the hadronic T-matrix in partial-wave basis up to total spin J=9/2, using a potential constructed from t- and u-channel exchanges of known mesons and baryons, s-channel pole terms for genuine resonances, and phenomenological contact diagrams. The photon is coupled to the hadronic final-state interaction through a semi-phenomenological approach where the photoproduction multipole amplitude is given by a driving term plus a convolution of the hadronic T-matrix with the photoproduction kernel, with the driving potential parameterized as energy-dependent polynomials. The free parameters are adjusted to experimental data in a chi-squared minimization using MINUIT, with weighting factors applied to different data sets to balance the varying number of data points per channel.

## Key numbers

- Total data set: 71,756 data points for center-of-mass energies W < 2.4 GeV
- For $\gamma p\to K^+\Sigma^0$: chi-squared per data point = 1.66 (total), with dsigma/dOmega (4381 points) chi-squared = 1.52, recoil polarization P (402 points) chi-squared = 2.60, beam asymmetry Sigma (280 points) chi-squared = 1.88, target asymmetry T (127 points) chi-squared = 1.50, beam-recoil Cx' (94 points) chi-squared = 3.11, beam-recoil Cz' (94 points) chi-squared = 2.65, Ox (127 points) chi-squared = 1.87, Oz (127 points) chi-squared = 1.39
- For $\gamma p\to K^0\Sigma^+$: chi-squared per data point = 3.16 (total), with dsigma/dOmega (281 points) chi-squared = 3.53, recoil polarization P (167 points) chi-squared = 2.54
- The threshold energy of the $K\Sigma$ channel is W = 1686 MeV
- The weak decay parameter alpha_- of the Lambda baryon was updated from the previous PDG value of 0.642(13) to 0.721(6)(5), requiring all affected polarization data to be scaled by a factor of 0.642/0.721 (about 17 percent smaller)
- The fit includes 12 genuine isospin I=1/2 poles and 10 I=3/2 poles, totaling 134 pole parameters, plus 61 contact term parameters, 68 background parameters, and 764 photo parameters

## Context

This work extends the Jülich-Bonn DCC approach, previously applied to $K\Lambda$ photoproduction (Rönchen et al., 2018), by adding $K\Sigma$ photoproduction off the proton, while the corresponding $K\Sigma$ channel and its pion-induced production were introduced earlier (Rönchen et al., 2012). The analysis is currently the only one taking the changed Lambda decay parameter alpha_- (BES-III, 2018; Ireland, 2019) into account, which affects polarization data with KY final states. This extension represents a prerequisite for an analysis of $K\Sigma$ electroproduction, since the JüBo photoproduction amplitude enters the electroproduction potential as a boundary condition at Q^2=0, and a wealth of electroproduction data for different hadronic final states is already available including the $K\Sigma$ final state (CLAS, 2022).
