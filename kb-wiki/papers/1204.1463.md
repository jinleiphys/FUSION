# Mass of highly magnetized white dwarfs exceeding the Chandrasekhar limit: An analytical view

> In recent years a number of white dwarfs has been observed with very high surface magnetic fields. We can expect that the magnetic field in the core of these stars would be much higher (~ 10^{14} G). In this paper, we analytically study the effect of high magnetic field on relativistic cold electron, and hence its effect on the stability and the mass-radius relation of a magnetic white dwarf. In strong magnetic fields, the equation of state of the Fermi gas is modified and Landau quantization comes into play. For relatively very high magnetic fields (with respect to the energy density of matter) the number of Landau levels is restricted to one or two. We analyse the equation of states for magnetized electron degenerate gas analytically and attempt to understand the conditions in which transitions from the zero-th Landau level to first Landau level occur. We also find the effect of the strong magnetic field on the star collapsing to a white dwarf, and the mass-radius relation of the resulting star. We obtain an interesting theoretical result that it is possible to have white dwarfs with mass more than the mass set by Chandrasekhar limit.

## Key claim
It is theoretically possible for white dwarfs with very high central magnetic fields (≳ 10¹⁵ G) to have masses exceeding the Chandrasekhar limit of 1.44 M☉, but only when the magnetic field is strong enough to restrict electrons to the lowest one or two Landau levels, and the central density is sufficiently high (∼10¹⁰ g/cc).

## Method
The authors analytically derived the equation of state for a degenerate relativistic electron gas at zero temperature in a strong magnetic field, using Landau quantization. They considered the extreme cases of one-level (only the ground Landau level occupied) and two-level systems, fitting the resulting pressure-density relations to polytropic forms. They then solved the Lane-Emden equation piecewise to obtain the mass-radius relation for magnetic white dwarfs with a constant magnetic field.

## Key numbers
- For a one-level system with Fermi energy ε_F = 20 m_e c², the minimum magnetic field required is 8.7 × 10¹⁵ G.
- For that system, the maximum white dwarf mass is about 2.3 M☉ at a radius of 8 × 10⁷ cm.
- The critical density for transition from the ground Landau level (ν=0) to the first excited level (ν=1) is ρ_t = √(2γ) K, where γ = B/B_c and B_c = 4.14 × 10¹³ G.
- For ε_F = 2, the maximum central density is 7.4 × 10⁶ g/cc; for ε_F = 20, the maximum central density is 1.2 × 10¹⁰ g/cc.
- The one-level equation of state was fitted as P(ρ) = 6.98 × 10⁻¹ ρ²·⁹² for ρ ≤ 1.4 × 10⁹ g/cc, and P(ρ) = 1.98 × 10⁸ ρ² for ρ ≥ 1.4 × 10⁹ g/cc.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of magnetic white dwarfs by Suh et al. (2000), who considered weak fields (B ≤ 10¹³ G) and found both mass and radius increase, and by Ostriker and Hartwick (1968), who constructed models with central fields of 10¹² G. The present analytical treatment focuses on the regime B > 10¹³ G where Landau quantization becomes dominant, enabling masses above the Chandrasekhar limit. The results enable future numerical studies of multi-level systems and provide a framework for understanding how surface magnetic fields (observed up to 10⁹ G) may be much smaller than the core fields that govern the star's structure.
