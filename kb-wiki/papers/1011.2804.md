# Lattice study of trapped fermions at unitarity

> We present a lattice study of up to N=20 unitary fermions confined to a harmonic trap. Our preliminary results show better than 1% agreement with high precision solutions to the many-body Schrodinger equation for up to N=6. We are able to make predictions for larger N which were inaccessible by the Hamiltonian approach due to computational limitations. Harmonic traps are used experimentally to study cold atoms tuned to a Feshbach resonance. We show that they also provide certain benefits to numerical studies of many-body correlators on the lattice. In particular, we anticipate that the methods described here could be used for studying nuclear physics.

## Key claim

The paper presents a lattice study of up to N=20 unitary fermions in a harmonic trap, achieving better than 1% agreement with exact solutions for N ≤ 6, but the results for larger N are preliminary and the extracted Bertsch parameter (ξ = 0.450(1)) is noted to be higher than values from untrapped calculations and experiment, suggesting slower convergence to the thermodynamic limit when the local density approximation must be invoked.

## Method

The authors use a lattice field theory approach with an improved four-fermion interaction tuned so that the s-wave scattering phase shift satisfies p cot δ₀ ≪ 1 for two fermions in a finite box up to arbitrarily high momenta. A harmonic trap is introduced via a field-independent term added to the interaction operator, and sources are constructed from single-particle harmonic oscillator wavefunctions combined with two-body correlated pair states to form Slater determinants. Ground state energies are extracted from effective mass plateaus using correlated χ² fits with bootstrapping, and systematic errors are controlled by varying the fitting region endpoints by Δτ = 2.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy for N=3: E/ω = 4.232(2)(3) (statistical and systematic errors), compared to exact result 4.273 from Blume (2010)
- Ground state energy for N=6: E/ω = 8.362(3)(12), compared to exact result 8.358 from Blume (2010)
- Ground state energy for N=20: E/ω = 40.12(7)(4), approximately 7% lower than Green's function Monte Carlo results from Chang (2007) and 3% lower than fixed-node diffusion Monte Carlo results from Forbes (2007)
- Bertsch parameter from trapped system at largest volume: ξ = 0.450(1) (preliminary), compared to ξ = 0.40(1) from a Green's function Monte Carlo calculation by Gandolfi (2008) and experimental values ξ = 0.39(2) and ξ = 0.41(2) from Ku (2009)
- Temporal discretization parameter: ω bτ = 0.0136
- Spatial lattice parameters: L₀/b_s = 4.0, L/b_s = 64 for final results, with finite volume errors estimated at less than ~0.5%
- Approximately 10 million configurations generated for each of four volumes, using 350 thousand CPU hours

## Context

This work extends lattice methods for unitary fermions developed by Endres (2010) and Chen (2003) to include a harmonic trap, enabling studies of up to N=20 fermions that were computationally inaccessible to Hamiltonian approaches. The results are benchmarked against high-precision solutions to the many-body Schrödinger equation from Blume (2010) for N ≤ 6, and compared to variational fixed-node calculations from Chang (2007) and Forbes (2007). The method enables future applications to nuclear physics, particularly given recent interest in using harmonic traps for extracting nuclear properties as discussed by Luu (2010).
