# Neutron skin thickness for $^{nat}$Pb, $^{159}$Tb and effects of deformation on matter radii for Mg, Na, Ar isotopes

> Data on reaction cross section $σ_{\rm R}$ of proton scattering are available for $^{nat}$Pb (natural lead) and $^{159}$Tb. In addition, data on $σ_{\rm R}$ and/or interaction ones $σ_{\rm I}$ are available for Mg, Na, Ar isotopes. Our aim is to determine neutron skin thickness $r_{\rm skin}$ for $^{nat}$Pb, $^{159}$Tb and to investigate effects of deformation for Mg, Na, Ar isotopes. We use the chiral (Kyushu) $g$-matrix folding model for lower energies and the folding model based on the Love-Franey (LF) $t$-matrix for higher energies. For $^{159}$Tb, our skin value is $r_{\rm skin}=0.273 \pm 0.073$~fm. As for $^{24}$Mg, our matter radius $r_{\rm m}(σ_{\rm R})=3.03 \pm 0.08$~fm determined from $σ_{\rm R}$ includes effects of deformation, whereas the corresponding $r_{\rm m}(σ_{\rm I})=2.79 \pm 0.15$~fm does not. Effects of deformation are seen for Mg isotopes, $^{21, 23}$Na, $^{40}$Ar. Our result $r_{\rm skin}=0.271 \pm 0.008$~fm for $^{nat}$Pb agrees with $r_{\rm skin}^{208}({\rm PREX\text{-}II}) = 0.283\pm 0.071$~fm for $^{208}$Pb, where we assume $A=208$ for $^{nat}$Pb.

## Key claim

The neutron skin thickness for natural lead ($^{nat}$Pb) is determined to be $r_{\rm skin}=0.271 \pm 0.008$ fm under the assumption that $A=208$ for $^{nat}$Pb, which agrees with the PREX-II value for $^{208}$Pb. For $^{159}$Tb, the skin value is $r_{\rm skin}=0.273 \pm 0.073$ fm, and effects of deformation on matter radii are observed for Mg, Na, and Ar isotopes when comparing radii extracted from reaction cross sections versus interaction cross sections.

## Method

The authors used the chiral (Kyushu) $g$-matrix folding model for lower incident energies (up to approximately 450 MeV) and the Love-Franey (LF) $t$-matrix folding model for higher energies (above approximately 550 MeV) to analyze reaction cross sections $\sigma_{\rm R}$ and interaction cross sections $\sigma_{\rm I}$ from proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus scattering data. For $^{nat}$Pb and $^{159}$Tb, proton and neutron densities from D1S-GHFB+AMP (with angular momentum projection) were scaled to reproduce experimental $\sigma_{\rm R}$ data while fixing the proton radius to known values from electron scattering. For Mg, Na, and Ar isotopes, densities from SLy7 or D1S-GHFB were used, and matter radii were extracted from both $\sigma_{\rm R}$ and $\sigma_{\rm I}$ data to investigate deformation effects.

## Key numbers

- $r_{\rm skin} = 0.271 \pm 0.008$ fm for $^{nat}$Pb (assuming $A=208$), extracted from p+$^{nat}$Pb scattering data at $E_{\rm lab}=21.1$ to $341$ MeV
- $r_{\rm m}(\sigma_{\rm R}) = 5.610 \pm 0.005$ fm for $^{nat}$Pb from the same analysis
- $r_{\rm skin} = 0.273 \pm 0.073$ fm for $^{159}$Tb, extracted from p+$^{159}$Tb scattering data at $20 \leq E_{\rm lab} \leq 47.5$ MeV
- $r_{\rm m}(\sigma_{\rm R}) = 3.03 \pm 0.08$ fm for $^{24}$Mg (includes deformation effects), while $r_{\rm m}(\sigma_{\rm I}) = 2.79 \pm 0.15$ fm for $^{24}$Mg (does not include deformation effects)
- $r_{\rm skin}^{208}({\rm PREX\text{-}II}) = 0.283 \pm 0.071$ fm for $^{208}$Pb from the PREX-II collaboration

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analyses of neutron skin thickness for $^{208}$Pb (Tagami 2020) and Sn isotopes (Tagami 2023) to natural lead and $^{159}$Tb, using the same chiral (Kyushu) $g$-matrix folding model. The study also builds on measurements of matter radii for Mg isotopes by Watanabe (2014) and Na isotopes by Suzuki (1995, 1998), and for Ar isotopes by Ozawa (2002). The finding that $r_{\rm m}(\sigma_{\rm R})$ includes deformation effects while $r_{\rm m}(\sigma_{\rm I})$ does not resolves discrepancies between previous extractions and enables more reliable determination of nuclear structure properties from scattering data.
