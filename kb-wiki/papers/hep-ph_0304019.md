# Ladder-QCD at finite isospin chemical potential

> We use an effective QCD model (ladder-QCD) to explore the phase diagram for chiral symmetry breaking and restoration at finite temperature with different $u,d$ quark chemical potentials. In agreement with a recent investigation based on the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, we find that a finite pion condensate shows up for high enough isospin chemical potential $μ_{I}=(μ_{u}-μ_{d})/2$. For small $μ_{I}$ the phase diagram in the $(μ_B,T)$ plane shows two first order transition lines and two critical ending points.

## Key claim

Using the ladder-QCD model (a two-loop approximation to QCD with a parametrized self-energy), the authors find that for small isospin chemical potential (μ_I = 30 MeV) the first-order chiral transition line in the (μ_B, T) plane splits into two separate first-order lines, each associated with one light quark flavor, provided the pion condensate remains zero and the flavor sectors factorize.

## Method

The authors evaluate the Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis effective action at two-loop order with one-gluon exchange (ladder approximation) in the Landau gauge, using a smooth parametrization of the quark self-energy (N=2 in their family of functions) and a fixed coupling at scale M. They introduce finite temperature via Matsubara frequencies and finite chemical potentials via the substitution p_0 → p_0 + iμ, then minimize the effective potential to determine the scalar and pseudoscalar condensates for u, d, and s quarks. The model parameters (M = 529 MeV, c = 1.0, m_u = 4.4 MeV, m_d = 6.2 MeV, m_s = 110 MeV) are fitted to reproduce m_π±, m_K±, m_K0, f_π, and f_K.

## Key numbers

- At T = μ = 0, the chiral-limit quark condensate is ⟨ψ̄ψ⟩_0 = −(248 MeV)^3.
- At T = μ = 0 with physical masses, ⟨ūu⟩ = −(251 MeV)^3, ⟨d̄d⟩ = −(253 MeV)^3, ⟨s̄s⟩ = −(305 MeV)^3.
- Constituent quark masses: M_{u,d} = 256 MeV, M_s = 385 MeV.
- For μ_I = 0, the crossover transition line starts at P_T = (0, 135) MeV and ends at E = (240, 97) MeV; the first-order line runs from E to P^μ = (325, 0) MeV.
- For μ_I = 30 MeV, the two crossover lines start at P_T = (0, 135) MeV and end at E_u = (210, 97) MeV and E_d = (270, 97) MeV; the first-order lines run from E_u to P^μ_u = (295, 0) MeV and from E_d to P^μ_d = (355, 0) MeV.
- At T = μ_B = 0, a finite pion condensate appears at μ_I = 70 MeV (with m_π ≈ 140 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier ladder-QCD study of the tricritical point (Barducci et al., 1989) to include a finite isospin chemical potential. It confirms the splitting of the first-order transition line found in a Random Matrix model (Klein, Toublan, Verbaarschot, 2003) and in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Toublan and Kogut, 2003). The result suggests that the splitting may be a universal feature of QCD at small μ_I, but the authors note that flavor-mixing instanton effects (as studied by Frank, Buballa, and Oertel, 2003) could wash out the splitting in two-flavor models; they plan to investigate this in the three-flavor case.
