# Precision studies of v_n fluctuations

> The power spectrum of heavy ion collisions is investigated by studying initial state fluctuations on top of a smooth hydrodynamic flow. In particular, the stability of the location of the first minimum of the power spectrum and the dependence of the hydrodynamic response v_n / epsilon_n on n and on eta / s are discussed. In our study we develop a new Green's function method for the analytic hydrodynamic flow by S. Gubser and make use of a fully non-linear hydrodynamics code. We find that there will be no well-defined first minimum of the response for n < 10, due to the fact that all minima in that region are found to be sensitive to the location of the initial perturbations. Also, we find that the often proposed form of the hydrodynamical response, that ln ( v_n / epsilon_n ) depend quadratically on n and linearly on eta / s, should not hold once many events have been averaged over.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the first minimum of the fixed-\(p_T\) differential power spectrum is not a stable observable even in linear hydrodynamics, and that the commonly proposed quadratic dependence of \(\ln(v_n/\epsilon_n)\) on \(n\) and linear dependence on \(\eta/s\) should not hold once many events have been averaged over.

## Method

The authors studied linear perturbations of the analytic Gubser flow using both a standard initial value problem approach and a newly developed Green's function method, solving the first-order hydrodynamic equations for perturbations expanded in spherical harmonics up to \(L \leq 25\). They also performed fully nonlinear hydrodynamic simulations using the Causal Viscous Hydro Code, adding a Gaussian hotspot perturbation to a central collision background, and computed particle spectra via the Cooper-Frye freezeout prescription with a Boltzmann distribution.

## Key numbers

* The first minimum of the power spectrum was found to vary by two or three harmonics when the initial radial position \(r_0\) of the perturbation was changed, with increasing \(r_0\) shifting the minimum to larger \(m\) values.
* For the Gubser flow analysis, the parameter \(q = (4.3\ \text{fm})^{-1}\) was used, with initial proper time \(\tau_0 = 1\ \text{fm}\) and initial temperature \(T_0 = 500\ \text{MeV}\) (corresponding to \(\hat{T}_0 = 7.9\)).
* In the nonlinear hydrodynamic simulations with \(\eta/s = 0.08\), no well-defined minimum was observed for \(n < 10\) when varying the initial perturbation position between \(2-6\ \text{fm}\).
* The numerical error in the Gubser flow power spectra was estimated at \(2-3\%\) in the plotted region, with larger errors at the minima.

## Context

This work extends the linear perturbation analysis of Staig and Shuryak (2011) on the Gubser flow, finding that the first minimum of the power spectrum is less stable than previously reported. The authors identify differences in methodology, including their use of a full Cooper-Frye integral rather than a saddle-point approximation and a different initial temperature (\(\hat{T}_0 = 7.9\) vs. \(\hat{T}_0 = 10.1\) used by Staig and Shuryak). The paper also challenges the response form proposed by Lacey et al. (2011), showing that the simple quadratic dependence of \(\ln(v_n/\epsilon_n)\) on \(n\) does not survive event averaging. The new Green's function method developed here enables direct calculation of two-point correlation functions for sourced hydrodynamic systems.
