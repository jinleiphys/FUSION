# Partial dynamical symmetries and shape coexistence in nuclei

> We present a symmetry-based approach for shape coexistence in nuclei, founded on the concept of partial dynamical symmetry (PDS). The latter corresponds to a situation when only selected states (or bands of states) of the coexisting configurations preserve the symmetry while other states are mixed. We construct explicitly critical-point Hamiltonians with two or three PDSs of the type U(5), SU(3), ${\overline{\rm SU(3)}}$ and SO(6), appropriate to double or triple coexistence of spherical, prolate, oblate and $γ$-soft deformed shapes, respectively. In each case, we analyze the topology of the energy surface with multiple minima and corresponding normal modes. Characteristic features and symmetry attributes of the quantum spectra and wave functions are discussed. Analytic expressions for quadrupole moments and $E2$ rates involving the remaining solvable states are derived and isomeric states are identified by means of selection rules.

## Key claim

The paper presents a symmetry-based approach for shape coexistence in nuclei using partial dynamical symmetries (PDS), where only selected states or bands of states preserve the symmetry while others are mixed, and constructs critical-point Hamiltonians that can accommodate two or three coexisting shapes simultaneously.

## Method

The authors construct number-conserving interacting boson model (IBM) Hamiltonians with multiple partial dynamical symmetries by requiring that the intrinsic part of the Hamiltonian annihilates the equilibrium intrinsic states corresponding to each coexisting shape. The construction relies on an intrinsic-collective resolution of the Hamiltonian, where the intrinsic part determines the energy surface with multiple minima and the collective part contains rotational kinetic terms. For each case of double or triple shape coexistence, the authors analyze the topology of the energy surface, normal modes, and derive analytic expressions for quadrupole moments and E2 transition rates involving the solvable states.

## Key numbers

- For the spherical-prolate coexistence Hamiltonian, the barrier height separating the two minima is \(\tilde{E}_{\rm bar} = \frac{1}{2}h_2(\sqrt{3}-1)^2 = 0.268h_2\), which is significantly higher than typical barriers of order \(0.001\tilde{E}_{\rm lim}\) obtained in standard Hamiltonians mixing U(5) and SU(3) Casimir operators
- For large N, the deformed normal modes about the prolate minimum have frequencies \(\epsilon_{\beta} = 4h_{2}N\) for \(\beta\) vibrations and \(\epsilon_{\gamma} = 12h_{2}N\) for \(\gamma\) vibrations, while the spherical normal modes have frequency \(\epsilon = 4h_{2}N\)
- The equilibrium deformations for the DS limits are \(\beta_{\rm eq}=0\) for U(5), \((\beta_{\rm eq}=\sqrt{2},\gamma_{\rm eq}=0)\) for SU(3), \((\beta_{\rm eq}=\sqrt{2},\gamma_{\rm eq}=\pi/3)\) for \(\overline{\rm SU(3)}\), and \((\beta_{\rm eq}=1,\gamma_{\rm eq}\ {\rm arbitrary})\) for SO(6)

## Context

This work extends the concept of partial dynamical symmetries, previously applied to single shapes and quantum phase transitions by Leviatan (2007) and Macek (2014), to the description of multiple coexisting nuclear shapes. The approach provides an alternative to configuration mixing IBM treatments of shape coexistence near shell closure by Duval and Barrett (1981, 1982) and to mean-field calculations of nuclear shape isomers by Moller (2009, 2012). The constructed Hamiltonians can serve as analytic benchmarks for describing double or triple shape coexistence in medium-heavy nuclei, including the triple coexistence of spherical, prolate and oblate shapes observed in \(^{186}\)Pb by Andreyev (2000).
