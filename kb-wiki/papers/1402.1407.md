# Nonperturbative shell-model interactions from the in-medium similarity renormalization group

> We present the first ab initio construction of valence-space Hamiltonians for medium-mass nuclei based on chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions using the in-medium similarity renormalization group. When applied to the oxygen isotopes, we find experimental ground-state energies are well reproduced, including the flat trend beyond the drip line at 24O. Similarly, natural-parity spectra in 21,22,23,24O are in agreement with experiment, and we present predictions for excited states in 25,26O. The results exhibit a weak dependence on the harmonic-oscillator basis parameter and reproduce spectroscopy within the standard sd valence space.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first *ab initio* construction of valence-space Hamiltonians for medium-mass nuclei using the in-medium similarity renormalization group (IM-SRG) starting from chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions, but the results exhibit a weak dependence on the harmonic-oscillator basis parameter and the calculations are truncated to the normal-ordered two-body (NO2B) approximation, meaning residual three-body terms are neglected.

## Method

The authors start from chiral N3LO nucleon-nucleon (NN) and N2LO three-nucleon (3N) interactions, apply a free-space SRG evolution to soften the Hamiltonians, then solve Hartree-Fock equations for the 16O core. The Hamiltonian is normal-ordered with respect to the core's HF reference state, and the IM-SRG flow equations are solved in the IM-SRG(2) approximation (truncated to normal-ordered two-body operators) to decouple valence-space states from non-valence states, producing single-particle energies and two-body matrix elements for the sd valence space above an inert 16O core.

## Key numbers

- Single-particle energies for the sd-shell orbits with NN+3N-full (Lambda_3N = 400 MeV) at hw = 24 MeV: d5/2 = -4.62 MeV, s1/2 = -2.96 MeV, d3/2 = 3.17 MeV
- The d3/2 orbit in 24O is bound by only 160 keV due to repulsive 3N forces
- Ground-state energies for 22O, 24O, 28O with NN+3N-full (Lambda_3N = 400 MeV) at lambda_SRG = 1.88 fm^-1: -166.51 MeV, -175.701 MeV, -179.68 MeV respectively
- For Lambda_3N = 500 MeV at lambda_SRG = 1.88 fm^-1: 22O = -202.18 MeV, 24O = -215.42 MeV, 28O = -219.94 MeV
- The 2+ state in 24O is predicted 1.2 MeV higher than experiment
- The 2+ state in 26O is predicted near 2 MeV

## Context

This work extends the IM-SRG method, originally developed for closed-shell ground-state calculations by Tsukiyama (2011), to construct valence-space Hamiltonians nonperturbatively, overcoming limitations of many-body perturbation theory (MBPT) approaches used by Otsuka (2009) and Holt (2010) where order-by-order convergence was unclear. The results enable future calculations for F, Ne, and Mg isotopic chains and, with extended valence spaces, access to the island-of-inversion region and the full sd-shell neutron dripline.
