# Some exact analytical results and a semi-empirical formula for single electron ionization induced by ultrarelativistic heavy ions

> The delta function gauge of the electromagnetic potential allows semiclassical formulas to be obtained for the probability of exciting a single electron out of the ground state in an ultrarelativistic heavy ion reaction. Exact formulas have been obtained in the limits of zero impact parameter and large, perturbative, impact parameter. The perturbative impact parameter result can be exploited to obtain a semi-empirical cross section formula of the form, sigma = A ln(gamma) + B, for single electron ionization. A and B can be evaluated for any combination of target and projectile, and the resulting simple formula is good at all ultrarelativistic energies. The analytical form of A and B elucidates a result previously found in numerical calculations: scaled ionization cross sections decrease with increasing charge of the nucleus being ionized. The cross section values obtained from the present formula are in good agreement with recent CERN SPS data from a Pb beam on various nuclear targets.

# Some exact analytical results and a semi-empirical formula for single electron ionization induced by ultrarelativistic heavy ions

## Key claim

The paper derives exact semiclassical formulas for single-electron ionization probability in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions at zero impact parameter and at large perturbative impact parameters, then uses the perturbative result to construct a semi-empirical cross section formula of the form sigma = A ln(gamma) + B that is claimed to be good at all ultrarelativistic energies, but the "empirical" aspect refers to fitting the formula to previous numerical calculations rather than to experimental data.

## Method

The author works in the delta function gauge of the electromagnetic potential, which allows exact solution of the Dirac equation at the interaction point z = t. Exact survival probabilities for the initial K-shell bound state are derived analytically for the limits b = 0 and b >> rho using exact Dirac wave functions. The perturbative large-impact-parameter result is matched onto a Weizsacker-Williams expression at a cutoff, and the resulting impact parameter integral yields the semi-empirical formula for A and B, with the constant factor in A adjusted to fit previous numerical calculations from Baltz (2000).

## Key numbers

- The square of the electron Compton wavelength lambda_c^2 is 1491 barns
- The coefficient A in barns is given by A = 1792 (Z_1^2 / Z_2^2) (1 + 3 gamma_2 + 2 gamma_2^2), where gamma_2 = sqrt(1 - alpha^2 Z_2^2)
- For Pb + Pb, the formula gives A = 8400 barns and B = 14,133 barns
- For H + H, the formula gives A = 10,752 barns and B = 69,629 barns
- At b = 0, the ionization probability in the low Z_1 limit is P_ion(b=0) = (pi^2/3 - 1) alpha^2 Z_1^2 = 2.29 alpha^2 Z_1^2
- The formula predicts a single electron ionization cross section of 101 kilobarns for Au + Au at RHIC (gamma = 23,000)
- For a 160 GeV/A one-electron Pb projectile on a Be target, the formula gives 0.14 kilobarns, in agreement with CERN SPS data of 0.14 kilobarns
- For a Pb projectile on an Au target, the formula gives 53 kilobarns versus SPS data of 42 kilobarns

## Context

This work extends the author's previous numerical calculations (Baltz, 2000) by providing analytical expressions that reproduce those numerical results and illuminate the systematic behavior of the ionization cross section with changing target and projectile species. The semi-empirical formula clarifies a result previously found numerically: scaled ionization cross sections decrease with increasing charge of the nucleus being ionized, contrary to the calculations of Anholt and Becker (1987) which used approximate relativistic bound state wave functions and showed increasing A with Z. The formula enables easy calculation of ionization cross sections for any ion-ion pair at ultrarelativistic energies, and is compared with CERN SPS data from Krause et al. (1998) for a Pb beam on various targets, showing considerably better agreement than the Anholt and Becker numbers.
