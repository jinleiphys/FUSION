# Flow coefficients in O-O Al-Al and Cu-Cu collisions at 200 GeV in the fusing color string model

> In view of the planning experiments for collisions of light nuclei at RHIC the flow coefficients for O-O, Al-AL and Cu-Cu collisions are studied in the color string percolation model. Our results for $v_2$ are somewhat smaller than predicted by other groups although with the same dependence on centrality. Our obtained $v_3$ lie between predictions of other groups.

## Key claim

The fusing color string model predicts elliptic flow coefficients \(v_2\) for O-O, Al-Al, and Cu-Cu collisions at 200 GeV that are somewhat smaller than those from hydrodynamical or AMPT approaches, while its triangular flow \(v_3\) values lie between the predictions of other groups, with the same centrality dependence for \(v_2\).

## Method

The authors performed Monte-Carlo simulations of the fusing color string model, distributing strings in the transverse overlap area of colliding nuclei and fusing strings that fall into the same cell of size \(\sim 0.3\) fm. Particle production and azimuthal anisotropy arise simultaneously from the gluon field of the fused strings, with the anisotropy generated by quenching of emitted partons as they pass through the string field, using a quenching formula with a single adjustable parameter \(\kappa = 0.45\) fixed from Pb-Pb data at LHC. The simulations used 400 runs divided into 11 centrality intervals from 0% to 100%, with transverse momenta from 0.1 to 4 GeV/c and azimuthal angles divided into 360 points.

## Key numbers

- Quenching coefficient \(\kappa = 0.45\), adjusted to reproduce the experimental \(v_2\) in mid-central Pb-Pb collisions at 5-13 TeV.
- For O-O collisions at 200 GeV, the model's \(v_2\) is nearly 40% smaller than predictions from Schenke et al. and about 30% smaller than predictions from the Chinese group.
- The Monte-Carlo simulation precision is estimated as \(\sim 1/\sqrt{N_{\text{run}}} \sim 5\%\) based on \(N_{\text{run}} = 400\).
- Transverse momentum range for calculations: \(0.1 < p_T < 4\) GeV/c, divided into 80 points.

## Context

This work extends the fusing color string model, previously applied to pp, p-A, d-A, and heavy A-A collisions (Braun, Pajares, and collaborators, various years), to light-ion collisions planned at RHIC. It provides a simpler alternative to hydrodynamical approaches with Color Glass Condensate initial conditions (Schenke et al., 2020) and the AMPT model (Chinese group, 2020), suggesting that the complicated mechanisms in those models may not be essential for the final flow results. The predictions serve as reference points for future experimental data on O-O, Al-Al, and Cu-Cu collisions at 200 GeV.
