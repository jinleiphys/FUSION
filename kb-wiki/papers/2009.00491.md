# Isospin dependent hybrid model for studying isoscaling in heavy ion collisions around the Fermi energy domain

> Investigation of observables from nuclear multifragmentation reactions depending on isospin led to the development of a hybrid model. The mass and charge distribution as well as isotopic distribution was studied using this model for $^{112}$Sn+$^{112}$Sn reaction as well as $^{124}$Sn+$^{124}$Sn reactions at different energies. The agreement of the results obtained from the model with those from experimental data confirms the accuracy of the model. Isoscaling coefficients were extracted from these observables which can throw light on the symmetry energy coefficient. Another important facet of this model is that temperature of the studied reaction can be directly extracted using this model.

## Key claim

The hybrid model, which combines an isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport calculation with the Canonical Thermodynamical Model (CTM) for fragmentation, reproduces experimental charge, mass, and isotopic distributions for both neutron-poor and neutron-rich tin reactions at 50 MeV/nucleon, and the extracted isoscaling coefficients agree with experiment only when the freeze-out temperatures of the two reactions are confirmed to be nearly equal (5.04 MeV and 5.08 MeV).

## Method

The dynamical stage uses an isospin-dependent BUU transport model (BUU@VECC-McGill) with a Skyrme-type mean field including a symmetry energy term, run with 100 test particles per nucleon in a 200×200×200 fm³ box until 200 fm/c. After removing pre-equilibrium particles (keeping the central 75% of the total mass), the excitation energy of the remaining cluster is obtained by subtracting its Thomas-Fermi ground-state energy, and the freeze-out temperature is extracted by inverting the CTM relation between excitation per nucleon and temperature. The CTM then calculates fragment yields at that temperature, and excited fragments are de-excited via Weisskopf evaporation (including n, p, d, t, ³He, ⁴He and fission).

## Key numbers

- For the ¹¹²Sn+¹¹²Sn reaction at 50 MeV/nucleon, the extracted freeze-out temperature is 5.04 MeV; for the ¹²⁴Sn+¹²⁴Sn reaction at the same beam energy, the temperature is 5.08 MeV.
- The theoretical isoscaling parameter α (neutron exponential coefficient) is 0.41, compared to the experimental value of 0.36.
- The theoretical isoscaling parameter β (proton exponential coefficient) is -0.49, compared to the experimental value of -0.42.
- The BUU simulation box size is 200×200×200 fm³; the projectile and target are initially centered at (100 fm, 100 fm, 90 fm) and (100 fm, 100 fm, 110 fm) respectively.
- The mean-field parameters used are: a = -2230.0 MeV·fm³, b = 2577.85 MeV·fm^(7/6), σ = 7/6, ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³, c = -137.5 MeV·fm⁵, and C_sym = 200 MeV·fm³.

## Context

This work extends the hybrid model previously developed by the same group for Xe+Sn central collisions (Mallik 2011) by incorporating isospin dependence in the BUU transport, enabling the study of isoscaling. It builds on the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) of Bondorf (1995) and the Canonical Thermodynamical Model (CTM) of Das (2005), and it uses the isoscaling technique introduced by Tsang (2001) and Botvina (2002) to relate yield ratios to the symmetry energy coefficient. The direct temperature extraction from the hybrid model allows verification of the equal-temperature assumption in the isoscaling equation, which is often problematic in experimental thermometry due to sequential decay and Fermi motion. The model can be extended to study projectile fragmentation at higher energies and other isospin-dependent observables.
