# Allowed Gamow-Teller Excitations from the Ground State of 14N

> Motivated by the proposed experiment $^{14}N(d,{^2He})^{14}C$, we study the final states which can be reached via the allowed Gamow-Teller mechanism. Much emphasis has been given in the past to the fact that the transition matrix element from the $J^π=1^+ T=0$ ground state of $^{14}N$ to the $J^π=0^+ T=1$ ground state of $^{14}C$ is very close to zero, despite the fact that all the quantum numbers are right for an allowed transition. We discuss this problem, but, in particular, focus on the excitations to final states with angular momenta $1^+$ and $2^+$. We note that the summed strength to the $J^π=2^+ T=1$ states, calculated with a wide variety of interactions, is significantly larger than that to the $J^π=1^+ T=1$ final states.

# Allowed Gamow-Teller Excitations from the Ground State of 14N

## Key claim

The summed Gamow-Teller strength to Jπ=2+ T=1 states in 14C is significantly larger than that to Jπ=1+ T=1 states across a wide variety of interactions, and the near-vanishing of the ground-state-to-ground-state GT transition can be achieved naturally in large model spaces without parameter adjustment, contrary to earlier claims that configuration mixing alone could cause the vanishing.

## Method

The authors performed no-core shell-model (NCSM) calculations using the OXBASH code and the Many-Fermion Dynamics code, computing B(GT) values and summed strengths for allowed Gamow-Teller transitions from the 14N ground state (Jπ=1+ T=0) to final 0+, 1+, and 2+ T=1 states in 14C. They employed three interaction types: the phenomenological Vzz interaction of Zamick and Zheng with variable spin-orbit (x) and tensor (y) strength parameters, the relativistic Bonn A interaction with Dirac effective mass mD/m=0.6, and the realistic Argonne V8' effective interaction in model spaces ranging from 0ℏω up to (0+2+4+6)ℏω.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state-to-ground-state B(GT) with Argonne V8' interaction: 2.518 in 0ℏω space, decreasing to 1.403 in (0+2)ℏω, 0.430 in (0+2+4)ℏω, and 0.164 in (0+2+4+6)ℏω, showing convergence toward zero.
- In the large space with x=1.5 y=0.75, summed strength to 1+ states is 0.193, while summed strength to 2+ states is 3.113.
- The ratio gA/gV = 1.251 was used for converting GT matrix elements to B(GT).
- The ft values for decays of 14C and 14O to the 14N ground state are 1.1×10^9 and 2×10^7, respectively.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the anomalously suppressed Gamow-Teller transition in the A=14 system, first noted by Inglis (1950s) and analyzed by Jancovici and Talmi (1950s) who showed a tensor component could cause the vanishing. The authors contradict the earlier conclusion of Baranger and Meshkov that configuration mixing alone could cause the GT matrix element to vanish, showing instead that in large model spaces with standard spin-orbit strength (x=1) the matrix element cannot vanish for any tensor strength. The results enable interpretation of the proposed 14N(d,2He)14C experiment and demonstrate that many-body correlations in larger model spaces naturally renormalize the effective two-body spin-orbit interaction, achieving agreement with experiment without phenomenological parameter adjustments.
