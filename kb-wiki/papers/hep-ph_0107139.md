# Solitons in nonlocal chiral quark models

> Properties of hedgehog solitons in a chiral quark model with nonlocal regulators are described. We discuss the formation of the hedgehog soliton, the quantization of the baryon number, the energetic stability, the gauging and construction of Noether currents with help of path-ordered P-exponents, and the evaluation of observables. The issue of nonlocality is thoroughly discussed, with a focus on contributions to observables related to the Noether currents. It is shown that with typical model parameters the solitons are not far from the weak nonlocality limit. The methods developed are applicable to solitons in models with separable nonlocal four-fermion interactions.

## Key claim
Stable hedgehog solitons exist in nonlocal chiral quark models without imposing the external chiral-circle constraint, and with typical model parameters the solitons are not far from the weak nonlocality limit. However, the transverse parts of Noether currents are not uniquely fixed by current conservation, introducing an ambiguity that is part of the model building.

## Method
The authors construct hedgehog solitons by minimizing the energy of a bosonized nonlocal chiral quark model with separable four-fermion interactions, using either a Gaussian or monopole regulator. The soliton is formed by a valence quark orbit and a polarized Dirac sea, with the energy-dependent Dirac Hamiltonian diagonalized in the Kahana-Ripka basis for each value of the Euclidean energy variable. Noether currents are constructed using path-ordered P-exponents, and observables are evaluated in the mean-field approximation with cranking for the nucleon.

## Key numbers
* For a Gaussian regulator with a constituent quark mass M = 400 MeV, the fitted cutoff Lambda is 543 MeV, the current quark mass m is 13.2 MeV, and the quark condensate is -185 MeV.
* For a monopole regulator with M = 400 MeV, the fitted cutoff Lambda is 509 MeV, the current quark mass m is 6.44 MeV, and the quark condensate is -235 MeV.
* The pion decay constant f_pi is fixed to 93 MeV and the pion mass m_pi to 139 MeV for all parameter sets.
* The gluon condensate estimate from QCD sum rules is 360 +/- 20 MeV, which favors lower values of M in the model.

## Context
This work extends the study of solitons from local models, such as those using the Schwinger proper-time or Pauli-Villars regularization (Alkofer, 1996; Goeke, 1996), to nonlocal models, which avoid the instability that requires an external chiral-circle constraint in local models (Goeke, 1992; Ripka, 1993). The methods developed are applicable to solitons in models with separable nonlocal four-fermion interactions, such as those derived from the instanton-liquid model (Diakonov, 1986) or Schwinger-Dyson resummations (Roberts, 1994). This work enables future calculations that include center-of-mass projection, rotational 1/N_c corrections, and vector mesons.
