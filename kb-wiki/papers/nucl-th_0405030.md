# Spin structure of spin-1/2 baryon and spinless meson production amplitudes in photo and hadronic reactions

> The most general spin structures of the spin-1/2 baryon and spinless meson production operator for both photon and nucleon induced reactions are derived from the partial-wave expansions of these reaction amplitudes. The present method provides the coefficients multiplying each spin operator in terms of the partial-wave matrix elements. The result should be useful in studies of these reactions based on partial-wave analyses, especially, when spin observables are considered.

## Key claim

The paper derives the most general spin structure of the transition operator for spin-1/2 baryon and spinless meson production in both photon-induced and nucleon-induced reactions, with the hedge that the method provides explicit coefficients for each spin operator in terms of partial-wave matrix elements, which is particularly useful for model-independent analyses when spin observables are considered.

## Method

The authors start from partial-wave expansions of the reaction amplitudes for gamma + N -> M + B and N + N -> B' + B, then invert these expansions to express the partial-wave matrix elements. They re-couple the angular momentum and expand the resulting spin tensors in terms of the available spin operators (Pauli matrices, photon polarization vector, and identity matrix) to extract the most general spin structure for both positive and negative parity baryons. The method is applied to near-threshold kinematics to illustrate the angular and energy dependences of the coefficients.

## Key numbers

- For positive parity baryon B in gamma N -> M B near threshold, the coefficients exhibit dependences: F1 = A0 + (q/Lambda) A1 cos(theta), F2 = (q/Lambda) B1 sin(theta), F3 = (q/Lambda) C1, F4 = 0, where q is the final relative momentum and Lambda is a scale taken as Lambda^2 ~ -t = [m_B - m_N^2/(m_B+m_M)] m_M
- For negative parity baryon B in gamma N -> M B near threshold, the coefficients exhibit: G1 = (q/Lambda) A'_1, G2 = (1/sqrt(2))((sqrt(2)-1)/(sqrt(2)+1)) G1, G3 = B'_0 + (q/Lambda) B'_1 cos(theta), G4 = (q/Lambda) C'_1 sin(theta), G5 = 0
- The positive parity transition operator for gamma N -> M B has six independent spin operators (Equation SSTRUC12_1P), while the negative parity case also has six independent spin operators (Equation SSTRUC_1N)
- For N + N -> B' + B with positive parity B, the transition operator contains eight independent spin structures (Equation SSTRUC1p)

## Context

This work extends the partial-wave expansion method for NN amplitudes from Nakayama and Love (previous work) to meson production reactions, and generalizes the spin structure derivations of Deepak (for NN -> NN pi) to include charged meson production and both photon and nucleon induced reactions. The results enable model-independent partial-wave analyses of spin observables in meson production reactions, and the authors demonstrate that for gamma N -> M B, no observable can distinguish between positive and negative parity baryons unless the baryon polarization is measured, in contrast to the NN -> B' B reaction where parity determination is possible as shown in earlier work by Hanhart, Haidenbauer, Nakayama, and Melnitchouk.
