# Correlated Dirac Eigenvalues and Axial Anomaly in Chiral Symmetric QCD

> We introduce novel relations between the derivatives [$\partial^{n}ρ(λ, m_{l})/\partial m_{l}^n$] of the Dirac eigenvalue spectrum [$ρ(λ, m_{l})$] with respect to the light sea quark mass ($m_{l}$) and the $(n+1)$-point correlations among the eigenvalues ($λ$) of the massless Dirac operator. Using these relations we present lattice QCD results for $\partial^{n}ρ(λ, m_{l})/\partial m_{l}^n$ ($n=1, 2, 3$) for $m_{l}$ corresponding to pion masses $m_π=160-55$ MeV, and at a temperature of about 1.6 times the chiral phase transition temperature. Calculations were carried out using (2+1) flavors of highly improved staggered quarks with the physical value of strange quark mass, three lattice spacings $a=0.12, 0.08, 0.06$ fm, and lattices having aspect ratios $4-9$. We find that $ρ(λ\to0, m_{l})$ develops a peaked structure. This peaked structure arises due to non-Poisson correlations within the infrared part of the Dirac eigenvalue spectrum, becomes sharper as $a\to0$, and its amplitude is proportional to $m_{l}^2$. We demonstrate that this $ρ(λ\to0,m_l)$ is responsible for the manifestations of axial anomaly in two-point correlation functions of light scalar and pseudoscalar mesons. After continuum and chiral extrapolations we find that axial anomaly remains manifested in two-point correlation functions of scalar and pseudoscalar mesons in the chiral limit.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the axial anomaly remains manifested in two-point correlation functions of scalar and pseudoscalar mesons in the chiral limit at a temperature of about 1.6 times the chiral phase transition temperature, but this conclusion depends on the observation that the peaked structure in the Dirac eigenvalue spectrum near zero arises from non-Poisson correlations and has an amplitude proportional to the light quark mass squared.

## Method
The authors used lattice QCD simulations with (2+1) flavors of highly improved staggered quarks at a physical strange quark mass, employing three lattice spacings (a=0.12, 0.08, 0.06 fm) and lattices with aspect ratios 4-9. They computed the Dirac eigenvalue spectrum and its first, second, and third derivatives with respect to the light sea quark mass using Chebyshev polynomial methods, then related these derivatives to n-point correlation functions among Dirac eigenvalues. The calculations were performed at a temperature of approximately 205 MeV (about 1.6 times the chiral phase transition temperature) for pion masses ranging from 55 to 160 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Temperature of simulations: approximately 205 MeV, corresponding to about 1.6 times the chiral phase transition temperature
- Pion mass range: 55-160 MeV
- Three lattice spacings used: a=0.12, 0.08, 0.06 fm
- Lattice sizes: from 32^3×8 to 80^3×16, with aspect ratios 4-9
- For the finest lattice (a=0.06 fm) with m_π=80 MeV: m_s^2 χ_disc/T_c^4 = 3.0(2) and m_s^2 (χ_π - χ_δ)/T_c^4 = 3.7(3) from direct measurements
- For the lightest quark mass (m_π=55 MeV) on N_τ=8 lattices: m_s^2 χ_disc/T_c^4 = 0.059(7) and m_s^2 (χ_π - χ_δ)/T_c^4 = 0.08(1)
- Chebyshev polynomial order p ≥ 100000 was required for saturation of results

## Context
This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of the U(1)_A anomaly in the chiral symmetric phase, building on methods developed for computing Dirac eigenvalue spectra using Chebyshev polynomials by Giusti (2008), Patella (2012), and Cossu (2016). The paper introduces novel relations connecting derivatives of the eigenvalue spectrum to multi-point eigenvalue correlations, which enables the demonstration that non-Poisson correlations in the infrared part of the spectrum are responsible for the manifestation of the axial anomaly. The results enable future investigations of how the U(1)_A anomaly behaves at higher temperatures and in different quark mass regimes, and provide a framework for studying the interplay between chiral symmetry restoration and axial anomaly effects.
