# Opening angle and dineutron correlations in knockout reactions with Borromean two-neutron halo nuclei

> Background. Knockout reactions with proton targets provide an invaluable tool to access the properties of two-neutron halo nuclei. Recently, experimental results for the average opening angle as a function of the intrinsic neutron momentum in 11Li have shown a localization of dineutron correlations on the nucleus surface. Purpose. Study the model dependence and the effect of distortion and absorption on the opening angle distributions to assess the reliability of this observable to extract properties of Borromean two-neutron halo nuclei. Method. A quasifree sudden model is used to describe the knockout process, where absorption effects are modeled by the eikonal S-matrix between the proton target and the core of the Borromean nucleus. Final states in momentum space are built within a three-body model for the projectile, which enables the description of momenta and opening angle distributions. Results. A strong dependence on absorption effects is found for the opening angle at large intrinsic momenta, while the region of lower momenta is mostly insensitive to them. Reasonable agreement with the available data is obtained for 11Li at low momenta with weights for s and p waves different from those previously reported, showing a model dependence in their extraction. For 19B, test calculations show marked sensitivity to small p-wave components. Conclusions. The opening angle for (p,pn) knockout reactions on Borromean nuclei at small intrinsic momenta is a reliable observable mostly sensitive to the structure of the Borromean nucleus. For larger momenta, the reaction mechanism leads to a larger distortion of the distribution. In the case of nuclei with small components of opposite parity to the dominant ones, this observable can be used to explore them. The relation between dineutron in coordinate space and opening angle in momentum space is found to be model-dependent.

## Key claim

The opening angle distribution in (p,pn) knockout reactions on Borromean two-neutron halo nuclei is a reliable observable for extracting nuclear structure information only at low intrinsic momenta, where it is mostly insensitive to absorption and distortion effects; at larger momenta, the reaction mechanism introduces strong model-dependent distortions that limit the extraction of structure information.

## Method

A quasifree sudden model was used to describe the knockout process, with absorption effects modeled by the eikonal S-matrix between the proton target and the core of the Borromean nucleus. Final states in momentum space were built within a three-body model for the projectile using Jacobi coordinates, enabling the description of momentum and opening angle distributions through a distorted Fourier transform of the ground-state wavefunction. The formalism was applied to 11Li and 19B using three-body wavefunctions constructed in the hyperspherical-harmonics framework.

## Key numbers

- For 11Li, the three-body ground-state wavefunction contains 63% s-wave, 32% p-wave, and 2% d-wave components
- The 10Li resonance energies used in the model are 0.37 MeV (1+ state) and 0.61 MeV (2+ state), with a 4.5 MeV d-wave resonance (4- state)
- The average opening angle without absorption is approximately 94 degrees at large momenta, while calculations including absorption show a decreasing trend that overestimates experimental data
- The best agreement with experimental data for the low-momentum maximum of the opening angle was found with approximately 48% s-wave and 48% p-wave weights, different from the three-body calculation values

## Context

This work extends the analysis of knockout reactions on Borromean nuclei beyond the initial study by Kubota (2020), which measured the opening angle in 11Li and extracted s-, p-, and d-wave weights from data fitting. The present study uses a three-body model from Casal (2017) and Gomez-Ramos (2017) that includes core spin and d-wave contributions, unlike the earlier analysis. The results show that the relation between dineutron correlations in coordinate space and opening angle in momentum space is model-dependent, and that the low-momentum region provides the most reliable structure information. This enables future experimental studies of two-neutron halo nuclei such as 19B, where test calculations show marked sensitivity to small p-wave components.
