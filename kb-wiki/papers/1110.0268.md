# Transport Coefficients in Gluodynamics: From Weak Coupling towards the Deconfinement Transition

> We study the ratio of bulk to shear viscosity in gluodynamics within a phenomenological quasiparticle model. We show that at large temperatures this ratio exhibits a quadratic dependence on the conformality measure as known from weak coupling perturbative QCD. In the region of the deconfinement transition, however, this dependence becomes linear as known from specific strongly coupled theories. The onset of the strong coupling behavior is located near the maximum of the scaled interaction measure. This qualitative behavior of the viscosity ratio is rather insensitive to details of the equation of state.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the ratio of bulk to shear viscosity in gluodynamics transitions from a quadratic dependence on the conformality measure at high temperatures (as in weak-coupling perturbative QCD) to a linear dependence near the deconfinement transition (as in strongly coupled theories), with the onset of the strong-coupling behavior located near the maximum of the scaled interaction measure; this qualitative behavior is insensitive to details of the equation of state.

## Method

The authors employ a phenomenological quasiparticle model (QPM) for the gluon plasma, based on an effective kinetic theory approach to QCD, where quasigluon excitations follow a medium-modified dispersion relation with a thermal mass quantified by a running coupling that accommodates non-perturbative effects near T_c via two parameters (lambda and T_s). The bulk and shear viscosity coefficients are derived from the effective kinetic theory, and their ratio is expressed in terms of the conformality measure Delta v_s^2 and temperature-dependent integrals involving the derivative of the coupling, with the model parameters adjusted to reproduce lattice QCD results for the scaled interaction measure from two different data sets (Fit 1 and Fit 2).

## Key numbers

* For Delta v_s^2 less than about 0.07 (i.e., T greater than about 1.5 T_c), the viscosity ratio is entirely determined by the quadratic term 15(Delta v_s^2)^2.
* For Delta v_s^2 greater than about 0.17 (i.e., T less than about 1.15 T_c), a linear dependence develops; a linear fit for Fit 1 yields alpha = 3.78 and beta = -0.305, while for Fit 2 alpha = 3.63 and beta = -0.305.
* The model parameters for Fit 1 are T_s/T_c = 0.73, lambda = 4.3, and B(T_c) = 0.19 T_c^4; for Fit 2 they are T_s/T_c = 0.52, lambda = 2.5, and B(T_c) = 0.48 T_c^4.

## Context

This work extends the quasiparticle model description of transport coefficients in SU_c(3) gluodynamics, previously shown to reproduce lattice QCD results for shear viscosity (Bluhm et al., 2010) and bulk viscosity (Bluhm et al., 2011), by analyzing the ratio zeta/eta and its dependence on the conformality measure. The quadratic dependence at high T matches the weak-coupling result of Arnold et al. (2006), while the linear dependence near T_c is known from holographic strongly coupled theories (hQCD). The results enable a systematic interpolation between the perturbative and non-perturbative regimes, and show that the qualitative behavior is robust against variations in the equation of state.
