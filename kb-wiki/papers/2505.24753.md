# Heavy Ion Double Charge Exchange Reactions as Probes for Two-Body Transition Densities

> Collisional heavy ion double charge exchange (DCE) reactions, induced by second order nucleon-nucleon interactions, are shown to provide access to the two-body transition densities of the complementary DCE transitions in the interacting nuclei. Corresponding two-body operators are introduced, treating the second order distorted wave reaction amplitude in the s-channel interaction form. The theoretical results are applied to the reaction $^{18}O+{}^{76}Se\to {} ^{18}Ne+{}^{76}Ge$ at $T_{lab}=270$~MeV, being $^{76}Ge$ a candidate for neutrino--less double beta decay.

## Key claim

Collisional heavy ion double charge exchange (DCE) reactions, mediated by second-order nucleon-nucleon interactions, can provide access to the two-body transition densities (2BTD) of the complementary DCE transitions in the interacting nuclei, but the s-channel "average approximation" introduced to extract these densities overestimates t-channel calculations by about 35% at forward angles and shows increasing phase mismatch beyond about 5 degrees.

## Method

The authors formulate the double sequential charge exchange (DSCE) reaction amplitude in second-order distorted wave theory, then transform from the standard t-channel representation to an s-channel form by a rotation in angular momentum space. This transformation allows separation of the reaction into probe-independent two-body transition densities and probe-dependent distortion and vertex functionals. The formalism is applied to the reaction 18O + 76Se → 18Ne + 76Ge at 270 MeV laboratory energy, using Hartree-Fock plus BCS and QRPA with three different Skyrme forces (SAMI, SKX, SLy4) to describe nuclear structure, with a momentum cut-off of 2.1 fm^-1 for projectile and 2.2 fm^-1 for target.

## Key numbers

- Laboratory energy: T_lab = 270 MeV (15 MeV/A)
- Momentum cut-off radius: 2.1 fm^-1 for projectile (18O) and 2.2 fm^-1 for target (76Se)
- S-channel results overestimate t-channel calculations by about 35% at forward angles
- Satisfactory agreement between s-channel and t-channel up to theta ~ 5 degrees, corresponding to momentum transfer k_alpha_beta < 200 MeV/c (k_alpha_beta < 1 fm^-1)
- Intermediate states included up to 50 MeV excitation energy
- Saturation of 2BTD contributions reached around J_c = 5 for projectile and J_C = 6 for target
- Propagator normalization factor |L_gamma^(+)(omega_alpha)| ≈ 1/300 MeV^-1 employed to reproduce full DW results
- Proton and neutron pairing gaps Delta_p,n ≈ 1 MeV in 76Ge, neutron gap Delta_n ≈ 1 MeV in 18O

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for heavy ion DCE reactions developed by Lenske (2021, 2024) and Bellone (2020), providing a new s-channel formulation that separates spectroscopic two-body transition densities from reaction dynamics. The approach is directly relevant to the NUMEN collaboration's experimental program at LNS Catania (Cappuzzello 2022) and connects to double beta decay nuclear matrix element calculations (Barea 2015), as 76Ge is a candidate for neutrinoless double beta decay. The formalism enables future studies of the rarely explored higher-order nuclear response functions and can be extended to include the competing Majorana DCE mechanism described by Lenske (2024).
