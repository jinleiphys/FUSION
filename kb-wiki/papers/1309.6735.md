# Relics of Minijets amid Anisotropic Flows in High-energy Heavy-ion Collisions

> Two dimensional low-$p_T$ dihadron correlations in azimuthal angle $ϕ$ and pseudo-rapidity $η$ in high-energy heavy-ion collisions are investigated within both the HIJING Monte Carlo model and an event-by-event (3+1)D ideal hydrodynamic model. Without final-state interaction and collective expansion, dihadron correlations from HIJING simulations have a typical structure from minijets that contains a near-side two-dimensional peak and an away-side ridge along the $η$-direction. In contrast, event-by-event (3+1)D ideal hydrodynamic simulations with fluctuating initial conditions from the HIJING+AMPT model produce a strong dihadron correlation that has an away-side as well as a near-side ridge. Relics of intrinsic dihadron correlation from minijets in the initial conditions still remain as superimposed on the two ridges. By varying initial conditions from HIJING+AMPT, we study effects of minijets, non-vanishing initial flow and longitudinal fluctuation on the final state dihadron correlations. With a large rapidity gap, one can exclude near-side correlations from minijet relics and dihadron correlations can be described by the superposition of harmonic flows up to the 6th order. When long-range correlations with a large rapidity gap are subtracted from short-range correlations with a small rapidity gap, the remaining near-side dihadron correlations result solely from relics of minijets. Low transverse momentum hadron yields per trigger ($p_T^{\rm trig} <4$ GeV/$c$, $p_T^{\rm asso}<2$ GeV/$c$) in central heavy-ion collisions are significantly enhanced over that in p+p collisions while widths in azimuthal angle remain the same, in qualitative agreement with experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that relics of intrinsic dihadron correlations from minijets in the initial conditions of heavy-ion collisions survive the thermalization and hydrodynamic expansion process, appearing as a superimposed near-side peak on top of the long-range ridge structures generated by anisotropic collective flow; however, with a sufficiently large rapidity gap (Δη > 2), these minijet relics can be excluded from the near-side correlation, leaving a signal described purely by harmonic flows up to 6th order.

## Method

The authors used the HIJING Monte Carlo model to simulate intrinsic dihadron correlations from minijets without final-state interactions, and a (3+1)D ideal hydrodynamic model with event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions from the HIJING+AMPT model to study how minijet correlations evolve through collective expansion. Dihadron correlations were computed as associated yields per trigger particle using the standard ratio of same-event to mixed-event pair distributions, with trigger particles in pT trig < 4 GeV/c and associated particles in pT asso < 2 GeV/c. By varying the rapidity gap between trigger and associated particles, the authors isolated contributions from minijet relics (short-range correlations) from those due to anisotropic harmonic flows (long-range correlations).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of dihadron correlations in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where a near-side ridge structure along the η direction was observed (Agakishiev 2011, Putschke 2007, Abelev 2009, Alver 2009) and attributed to higher-order harmonic flows, especially triangular flow from geometric fluctuations (Alver 2010). The paper builds on the consensus that both near-side ridge and away-side double-bump structures arise from anisotropic expansion of fluctuating initial energy densities, as demonstrated by measurements in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC (Aamodt 2011, Chatrchyan 2012, Abelev 2012, ATLAS 2012) and by AMPT Monte Carlo simulations (Ma 2010, Xu 2011). The novel contribution is the systematic study of how minijet relics survive thermalization and hydrodynamic expansion, and the demonstration that subtracting long-range correlations (large rapidity gap) from short-range correlations (small rapidity gap) isolates the minijet relic signal, enabling future experimental extraction of these intrinsic correlations from the dominant flow background.
