# On a scale-invariant Fermi gas in a time-dependent harmonic potential

> We investigate a scale-invariant two-component Fermi gas in a time-dependent isotropic harmonic potential. The exact time evolution of the density distribution in position space in any spatial dimension is obtained. Two experimentally relevant examples, an abrupt change and a periodic modulation of the trapping frequency are solved. Small deviations from scale invariance and isotropy of the confinement are addressed within first order perturbation theory. We discuss the consequences for experiments with ultracold quantum gases such as the excitation of a tower of undamped breathing modes and a new alternative for measuring the Tan contact.

## Key claim

For a scale-invariant two-component Fermi gas in a time-dependent isotropic harmonic potential, the exact time evolution of the density distribution can be obtained from the initial state by a combined gauge and scale transformation, requiring only the solution of a one-dimensional Newton equation for the scaling function λ(t), with no knowledge of the equation of state or spectral functions needed.

## Method

The author considers a two-component Fermi gas with contact interactions tuned to a scale-invariant regime, trapped in a time-dependent isotropic harmonic potential. The time evolution is obtained via an ansatz combining a gauge transformation and a spatial scaling by a factor λ(t), where λ(t) obeys the differential equation λ̈ = ω_in²/λ³ − ω²(t)λ with initial conditions λ(0)=1 and λ̇(0)=0. Two specific time-dependent protocols are solved exactly: an abrupt step change of the trapping frequency and a periodic modulation of the trapping frequency. Small deviations from scale invariance (finite scattering length, finite effective range, long-range interactions) and from isotropy are treated within first-order perturbation theory using recursion relations for the shifts of breathing mode level spacings.

## Key numbers

- For an abrupt frequency change from ω_in to ω_f, the scaling function is λ(t) = √[cos²(ω_f t) + α sin²(ω_f t)] where α = (ω_in/ω_f)².
- In the limit ω_f ≫ ω_in (strong perturbation), the Fourier amplitudes of the breathing mode decomposition are |a₀| = 2/π and |a_n| = 4/[π(4n²−1)] for n ∈ ℕ.
- For the experiment by Vogt (2012) with a weak perturbation α=0.64, the predicted amplitude ratio is |a₂/a₁| ≈ 3%; for a strong perturbation α=0.36, |a₂/a₁| ≈ 6%, both below the experimental resolution limit of ≈20%.
- Parametric resonances occur at modulation frequencies Ω_n = 2ω_in/n for n ∈ ℕ.
- For a small periodic perturbation with f(t)=cos(Ωt), the resonance bands shrink as ∼ Δω^n.
- For the finite-range perturbation, the level spacing shift scales as δΔ_n ∼ n^{−1/2} as n→∞.
- For the inverse-square interaction potential (ρ=2), the perturbation is scale-invariant and does not modify breathing frequencies.

## Context

This work extends the observation by Castin (2004) that the scaling solution for noninteracting particles in a time-dependent harmonic trap also applies to the three-dimensional unitary Fermi gas, generalizing it to any spatial dimension. The paper provides exact solutions for two experimentally relevant protocols and develops perturbation theory for symmetry-breaking effects, connecting to the experiment by Vogt (2012) on two-dimensional Fermi gases. The results enable new approaches to measuring the Bertsch parameter ξ_B and the Tan contact C_trap via breathing mode spectroscopy, and predict that an antitrapped Fermi gas can be stabilized by periodic frequency oscillations (analogous to the Kapitza pendulum).
