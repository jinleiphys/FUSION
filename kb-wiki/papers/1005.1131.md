# Deconfinement and chiral transition with the highly improved staggered quark (HISQ) action

> We report on investigations of the chiral and deconfinement aspects of the finite temperature transition in 2+1 flavor QCD using the Highly Improved Staggered Quark (HISQ) action on lattices with temporal extent $N_τ=6$ and $N_τ=8$. We have performed the calculations for physical values of the strange quark mass $m_s$ and the light quark masses $m_l=0.2m_s$ and $0.05m_s$. Several finite temperature observables, including the renormalized Polyakov loop, the renormalized chiral condensate and the chiral susceptibility have been calculated. We also study the fluctuations and correlations of different conserved charges as well as the trace anomaly at finite temperature. We compare our findings with previous calculations that use different improved staggered fermion formulations: asqtad, p4 and stout.

## Key claim

The HISQ action reduces taste symmetry violations by a factor of about three compared to the asqtad action, but non-negligible pseudo-scalar meson mass splittings remain even for HISQ, meaning that the chiral and deconfinement transition observables still show cutoff effects that must be accounted for when comparing to continuum expectations.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations of 2+1 flavor QCD using the HISQ action with tree-level Symanzik improved gauge action on lattices with temporal extent Nτ=6 and Nτ=8, for two lines of constant physics: light quark masses ml=0.2ms and ml=0.05ms (the latter being near-physical). They calculated the renormalized Polyakov loop, subtracted chiral condensate, chiral susceptibility (disconnected and connected parts), fluctuations and correlations of conserved charges (strangeness, baryon number, light quark number, isospin), and the trace anomaly, comparing results with those from asqtad, p4, and stout actions.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of Sommer scales r0/r1 = 1.459(3) for ml=0.2ms and r0/r1 = 1.481(6) for ml=0.05ms.
- Pion masses on the two lines of constant physics: mπ = 306-312 MeV for ml=0.2ms and mπ = 158-160 MeV for ml=0.05ms.
- The strange quark mass was set using mη_{s\bar s} ≃ sqrt(2B ms) = 686.57 MeV.
- The chiral susceptibility shows a peak-like structure at temperatures around 170 MeV.
- The heaviest pion taste-splitting mass is about 400-600 MeV for stout and asqtad actions at lattice spacings corresponding to Nτ=8, while HISQ quadratic splittings are 2 to 3 times smaller than asqtad.
- The nucleon mass in HISQ calculations is about 10% larger than the experimental value, but the discrepancy is largely reduced compared to asqtad calculations.

## Context

This work extends previous finite-temperature QCD studies using improved staggered fermion formulations, asqtad (MILC Collaboration, 2004), p4 (Karsch et al., 2001), and stout (Fodor et al., 2006, 2009), by applying the HISQ action developed by HPQCD/UKQCD (Follana et al., 2007) to reduce taste symmetry violations. The results enable more reliable determinations of the chiral transition temperature and show that cutoff effects in conserved charge fluctuations can be partially accounted for by using lattice-spacing-dependent hadron masses in Hadron Resonance Gas model calculations, as proposed by Huovinen and Petreczky (2010).
