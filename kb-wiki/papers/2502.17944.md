# Five-body systems with Bethe-Salpeter equations

> We extend the Bethe-Salpeter formalism to systems made of five valence particles. Restricting ourselves to two-body interactions, we derive the subtraction terms necessary to prevent overcounting. We solve the five-body Bethe-Salpeter equation numerically for a system of five scalar particles interacting by a scalar exchange boson. To make the calculations tractable, we implement properties of the permutation group S5 and construct an approximation based on intermediate two- and three-body poles. We extract the five-body ground and excited states along with the spectra obtained from the two-, three-, and four-body equations. In the limit of a massless exchange particle, the two-, three, four- and five-body states coexist within a certain range of the coupling strength, whereas for heavier exchange particles the five-body system becomes Borromean. Our study serves as a building block for the calculation of pentaquark properties using functional methods.

## Key claim

The authors extend the Bethe-Salpeter formalism to five-body systems, but the numerical solutions are obtained under the restriction of two-body interactions only, using a ladder approximation with tree-level propagators and a scalar exchange boson, which limits the applicability of the results to a specific parameter range where bound states exist without becoming tachyonic.

## Method

The authors derive the five-body Bethe-Salpeter equation with two-body kernels, including subtraction terms to prevent overcounting from the ten possible two-body kernel configurations and fifteen double-kernel configurations. They solve the equation numerically for five scalar particles exchanging a scalar boson, using properties of the permutation group S5 to organize the 14 independent Lorentz invariants, and construct a "singlet times pole" approximation that includes explicit two- and three-body poles to reduce the momentum dependence to a single singlet variable. The two- and three-body equations are solved fully without kinematic approximations, while the four- and five-body equations are solved in the singlet approximation or the singlet times pole approximation.

## Key numbers

- For beta = 4 (mass ratio mu/m = 4), the five-body ground state exists only within a certain coupling range; below c approximately 3, the five-body equation admits a ground state while there are no two-, three-, or four-body ground states.
- For beta values less than approximately 0.5, there exist coexistence regions (black rectangles in Fig. 2) where two-, three-, four-, and five-body ground states all exist simultaneously.
- For beta = c = 0.5, the singlet and singlet times pole approximations yield five-body masses that differ by less than or approximately 10 percent.
- For beta = 0.001 and c = 1/4, the inverse eigenvalues of the two-body system at M = 0 become integers, while this is not the case for the three-, four-, and five-body systems.

## Context

This work extends the Bethe-Salpeter formalism, previously applied to two-body systems (Wick-Cutkosky model, studied by Wick (1954) and Cutkosky (1954)), three-body systems (Karmanov (2009), Ydrefors (2017, 2019)), and four-body systems (Eichmann (2015, 2020), Hoffer (2024)), to five-body systems for the first time. The approach is motivated by pentaquark candidates observed by LHCb (2015, 2019, 2022) and aims to provide a building block for calculating pentaquark properties using functional methods, where the five-body amplitude dynamically generates meson-baryon molecular configurations.
