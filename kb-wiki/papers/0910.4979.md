# An improved density matrix expansion for spin-unsaturated nuclei

> A current objective of low-energy nuclear theory is to build non-empirical nuclear energy density functionals (EDFs) from underlying inter-nucleon interactions and many-body perturbation theory (MBPT). The density matrix expansion (DME) of Negele and Vautherin is a convenient method to map highly non-local Hartree-Fock expressions into the form of a quasi-local Skyrme functional with density-dependent couplings. In this work, we assess the accuracy of the DME at reproducing the non-local exchange (Fock) contribution to the energy. In contrast to the scalar part of the density matrix for which the original formulation of Negele and Vautherin is reasonably accurate, we demonstrate the necessity to reformulate the DME for the vector part of the density matrix, which is needed for an accurate description of spin-unsaturated nuclei. Phase-space averaging techniques are shown to yield a significant improvement for the vector part of the density matrix compared to the original formulation of Negele and Vautherin. The key to the improved accuracy is to take into account the anisotropy that characterizes the local-momentum distribution in the surface region of finite Fermi systems. Optimizing separately the DME for the central, tensor and spin-orbit contributions to the Fock energy, one reaches a few-percent accuracy over a representative set of semi-magic nuclei. With such an accuracy at hand, one can envision using the corresponding Skyrme-like energy functional as a microscopically-constrained starting point around which future phenomenological parameterizations can be built and refined.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the original density matrix expansion (DME) of Negele and Vautherin is accurate for the scalar part of the density matrix but requires reformulation for the vector part, which is essential for spin-unsaturated nuclei; the key improvement comes from accounting for the anisotropy of the local-momentum distribution in the surface region of finite Fermi systems, enabling a few-percent accuracy over a representative set of semi-magic nuclei.

## Method

The authors reformulate the density matrix expansion using phase-space averaging (PSA) techniques, applying them consistently to both the scalar and vector parts of the one-body density matrix (OBDM). They test the accuracy of the new PSA-DME by comparing its reproduction of the non-local Fock exchange contributions to the energy against exact results, using two schematic nucleon-nucleon interactions and density matrices obtained from self-consistent Skyrme-EDF calculations of semi-magic nuclei. The central, tensor, and spin-orbit contributions to the Fock energy are analyzed separately, with the DME optimized independently for each contribution.

## Key numbers

* The quadrupole anisotropy \(P^n_2(\vec{R})\) of the local neutron momentum distribution is shown to become negative just inside the nuclear surface and strongly prolate outside, as calculated for a selection of semi-magic nuclei using the SLy4 parametrization of the Skyrme EDF.
* The local Fermi momentum is defined as \(k^q_F \equiv [3 \pi^2 \rho_q(\vec{R})]^{1/3}\), linking the momentum scale to the local density.
* The optimized DME reaches a few-percent accuracy for the central, tensor, and spin-orbit Fock contributions over a representative set of semi-magic nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the original density matrix expansion of Negele and Vautherin (1972), which had focused almost entirely on the scalar part of the density matrix and acknowledged that the vector part expansion was not on the same level. The new PSA-DME addresses this gap, enabling non-empirical construction of Skyrme-like energy density functionals for spin-unsaturated nuclei, which constitute the overwhelming majority of nuclei. The improved accuracy allows the resulting functional to serve as a microscopically-constrained starting point around which future phenomenological parameterizations can be built and refined, complementing efforts by Lesinski (2006), Margueron (2007), and Niksic (2008) to empirically improve energy density functionals.
