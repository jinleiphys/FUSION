# Fluctuation of Gaps in Hadronization at Phase Transition

> Event-by-event fluctuations of hadronic patterns in heavy-ion collisions are studied in search for signatures of quark-hadron phase transition. Attention is focused on a narrow strip in the azimuthal angle with small $Δy$. The fluctuations in the gaps between particles are quantified by simple measures. A scaling exponent $α$ is shown to exist around $T_c$. An index $ξ$ is shown to characterize the critical fluctuation; it is a numerical constant $ξ=0.05\pm0.01$. All the measures considered in this gap analysis are experimentally observable. Whether or not the theoretical predictions, based on simulations using 2-dimensional Ising model, are realistic for heavy-ion collisions, analysis of the experimental data suggested here should be carried out, since the existence of a scaling behavior is of interest in its own right.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a numerical constant ξ = 0.05 ± 0.01, derived from the scaling of gap moments in a 2D Ising model simulation, can serve as an experimentally measurable signature of a second-order quark-hadron phase transition in heavy-ion collisions, but only if the hadronization temperature is near T_c and the transverse momentum window Δp_T is kept small.

## Method

The authors simulated 3 × 10^5 configurations of a 2D Ising lattice of size L = 288 at T = T_c = 2.315 (in units of J/k_B), mapping a row of 72 cells to a narrow strip in azimuthal angle φ with small rapidity width Δy. For each configuration, they placed one particle per cell if the cell density ρ_i exceeded a threshold ρ_0, then computed the gaps x_i between neighboring particles in the cumulative X variable and defined the moments G_q = (1/(N+1)) Σ x_i^q. They constructed the erraticity measure S_q = ⟨G_q ln G_q⟩ / ⟨G_q ln G_q⟩_stat and extracted the scaling exponent α from S_q ∝ q^α, then studied α as a function of the average number of gaps ⟨M⟩ to obtain the slope ξ.

## Key numbers

- Scaling exponent α = 1.67 ± 0.02 at T = T_c for ρ_0 = 20, compared to α = 0.156 for pp collisions at √s = 20 GeV.
- Index ξ = 0.05 ± 0.01 from the grand average over variations in row length (C = 72, 54, 36 cells) and row width (r = 1, 2, 3 adjacent rows).
- For C = 72 cells: ξ = 0.055 ± 0.005 (r = 1), ξ = 0.044 ± 0.003 (r = 2), ξ = 0.045 ± 0.003 (r = 3).
- For r = 1: ξ = 0.055 ± 0.005 (C = 72), ξ = 0.057 ± 0.005 (C = 54), ξ = 0.048 ± 0.007 (C = 36).
- Linear fit parameters at T_c: α_0 = -0.258, ξ = 0.055.
- Simulation used N_e = 3 × 10^5 configurations on a lattice of size L = 288 with cell size ε = 4, giving C = 72 cells per row.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier void analysis in two-dimensional y-φ space (Hwa and Zhang, 2001) by reducing the problem to one-dimensional gaps in φ, simplifying experimental detection. It builds on prior gap analysis applied to hadronic collisions (Hwa and Zhang, 2000) and NA22 data, and uses the 2D Ising model as a proxy for the quark-hadron phase transition, following the universality argument of Gunion, Guralnik, and Peccei (1978). The proposed index ξ enables experimental tests at RHIC and SPS, and the authors suggest future studies of ξ dependence on total transverse energy E_T, centrality, and elliptic flow sectors.
