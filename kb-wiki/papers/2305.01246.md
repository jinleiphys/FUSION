# Properties of quark matter and hybrid stars from a quasiparticle model

> We investigate the properties of hybrid stars with the hadron-quark phase transition by using a quasiparticle model. Results from our study indicate that the coupling constant $g$ can stiffen the EOS of hybrid star matter and thus increase the hybrid star maximum mass and its tidal deformability, whereas it also decreases the mass and radius of the pure quark core. In addition, we find that a step change of the sound velocity occurs in the hadron-quark mixed phase, and it is restored with the decrease of nucleon and lepton degrees of freedom in the high density quark phase. The approximate rule that the polytropic index $γ\leq 1.75$ can also be used as a criterion for separating hadronic from quark matter in our work. The hypothesis of absolutely stable SQM (or "Witten hypothesis") suggests that a hybrid star containing a sufficient amount of SQM in its core will rapidly convert into a strange quark star. The SQM in hybrid stars therefore should break the absolutely stable condition, and the energy per nucleon ($E/A$) of both $ud$QM and SQM must exceed the lowest energy per nucleon 930 MeV. As a result, we provide the maximum mass, minimum radius $R_{1.4}$ and minimum tidal deformation $Λ_{1.4}$ of the hybrid stars as well as the maximum mass and radius of the quark matter core with different $g$ values within the allowable regions ($E/A>930$ MeV) on the $g-B^{1/4}$ plane. Using the constraints from astrophysical observations and heavy-ion experiments for comparison, our results indicate that the recently discovered massive neutron stars be well described as hybrid stars in the quasiparticle model, and confirm that the sizable quark-matter cores ($R_{QC}>6.5$ km) containing the mixed phase can appear in $2M_{\odot}$ massive stars.

# Properties of quark matter and hybrid stars from a quasiparticle model

## Key claim
The quasiparticle model can describe recently discovered massive neutron stars as hybrid stars with sizable quark-matter cores, but only if the strange quark matter (SQM) in hybrid stars breaks the "absolutely stable" condition, meaning the energy per nucleon of both udQM and SQM must exceed 930 MeV.

## Method
The authors construct hybrid star equations of state by combining an improved isospin- and momentum-dependent interaction (ImMDI) model for hadronic matter with a quasiparticle model for strange quark matter, using the Gibbs construction for the hadron-quark mixed phase. The coupling constant g is treated as a free parameter ranging from 1 to 5, and the bag constant B is adjusted to ensure the energy per nucleon exceeds 930 MeV. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are solved to obtain mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities.

## Key numbers
- Maximum mass of hybrid stars increases from 1.51 to 2.06 solar masses as the coupling constant g increases from 1 to 5
- For a 2 solar mass hybrid star, the maximum radius of pure quark cores is about 2.0 km, while mixed quark cores can reach up to 7.2 km
- The maximum mass and radius of pure quark cores are about 0.88 solar masses and 5.5 km respectively
- The polytropic index gamma <= 1.75 can be used as a criterion for separating hadronic from quark matter
- At saturation density, the squared speed of sound is 0.08 and the polytropic index is 2.55 from the ImMDI model

## Context
This work extends previous studies of hybrid stars using the Gibbs construction and modified bag constants (Fischer 2010, Xu 2010, Wei 2011) by excluding absolutely stable SQM inside hybrid stars. The results confirm the finding of Annala (2020) that quark-matter cores with radius greater than 6.5 km can appear in 2 solar mass stars. The study enables future comparisons with kilohertz gravitational wave observations from third-generation detectors, as the step change in sound velocity during the hadron-quark phase transition is relevant to postmerger gravitational wave spectra.
