# Hyperon electromagnetic form factors in the timelike region

> Electromagnetic form factors of hyperons ($Λ$, $Σ$, $Ξ$) in the timelike region, accessible in the reaction $e^+e^- \to \bar YY$, are studied. The focus is on energies close to the reaction thresholds, where the properties of these form factors are significantly influenced by the interaction in the final $\bar YY$ system. This interaction is taken into account in the calculation, utilizing $\bar YY$ potential models that have been constructed by the Jülich group for the analysis of data from the reaction $\bar pp \to \bar YY$ in the past. The enhancement of the effective form factor for energies close to the threshold, seen in experiments of $e^+e^- \to \bar ΛΛ$ and $e^+e^- \to \bar Σ^0Λ$, is reproduced. With regard to the reactions $e^+e^- \to \bar Σ^- Σ^+, \ \barΣ^0Σ^0, \ \barΣ^+Σ^-$ a delicate interplay between the three channels is observed in the results at low energies, caused by the $\barΣΣ$ interaction. Predictions for the electromagnetic form factors $G_M$ and $G_E$ in the timelike region are presented for the $Λ$, $Σ$, and $Ξ$ hyperons.

## Key claim

The paper reproduces the enhancement of the effective form factor near threshold for $e^+e^- \to \bar\Lambda\Lambda$ and $e^+e^- \to \bar\Sigma^0\Lambda$ seen in experiments, but the authors note that the very large cross section extremely close to the $\bar\Lambda\Lambda$ threshold reported by BESIII cannot be reproduced by their $\bar\Lambda\Lambda$ final-state interaction and "a plausible and convincing explanation/interpretation is still missing."

## Method

The authors calculate electromagnetic form factors of hyperons ($\Lambda$, $\Sigma$, $\Xi$) in the timelike region by incorporating the final-state interaction in the $\bar YY$ system using phenomenological $\bar YY$ potential models previously developed by the Jülich group for analyzing $\bar pp \to \bar YY$ data from the PS185 experiment at LEAR. The bare hyperon production vertices are dressed via rescattering in coupled $\bar YY$ channels through a Lippmann-Schwinger equation, with the $\bar YY$ interaction described by meson exchanges (including $K$ and $K^*$ exchange) and phenomenological optical potentials for annihilation.

## Key numbers

- The $\bar\Lambda\Lambda$ cross section near threshold is about a factor of 5 larger than the $\bar\Sigma^0\Lambda$ cross section (based on comparing cross sections at 2.4 GeV)
- The BESIII measurement of the ratio $|G_E/G_M|$ for $\bar\Lambda\Lambda$ at $\sqrt{s}=2.396$ GeV is reproduced roughly by one of the interaction models (model I from Haidenbauer 1991)
- For $\bar\Sigma\Sigma$, the measured cross section ratio $\sigma_{\bar\Sigma^-\Sigma^+}/\sigma_{\bar\Sigma^+\Sigma^-} \approx 10$ requires that the bare form factors $G^{0(\Sigma^+)}_M$ and $G^{0(\Sigma^-)}_M$ are "basically orthogonal"
- The BESIII Collaboration found a ratio $|G_E/G_M| = 1.83 \pm 0.26$ for $\bar\Sigma^-\Sigma^+$ at 2.396 GeV, which the authors note is "extremely large given that this energy is just about 17 MeV away from the $\bar\Sigma^-\Sigma^+$ threshold"

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of $e^+e^- \to \bar pp$ (Haidenbauer 2014) and $e^+e^- \to \bar\Lambda\Lambda$ (Haidenbauer 2016) to other hyperon channels, using the same formalism that successfully reproduced proton form factor data. The $\bar YY$ potential models were originally developed for analyzing $\bar pp \to \bar YY$ data from the PS185 experiment (Haidenbauer 1991, 1992, 1993). The calculations provide predictions for the energy dependence of cross sections and for the magnitude and relative phase of $G_E$ and $G_M$ for $\Lambda$, $\Sigma$, and $\Xi$ hyperons, which can be tested by future BESIII measurements of spin-dependent observables.
