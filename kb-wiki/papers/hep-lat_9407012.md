# An Exploratory Study of Nucleon-Nucleon Scattering Lengths in Lattice QCD

> An exploratory study is made of the nucleon-nucleon $s$-wave scattering lengths in quenched lattice QCD with the Wilson quark action. The $π$-$N$ and $π$-$π$ scattering lengths are also calculated for comparison. The calculations are made with heavy quarks corresponding to $m_π/m_ρ\approx 0.73-0.95$. The results show that the $N$-$N$ system has an attractive force in both spin-singlet and triplet channels, with their scattering lengths significantly larger than those for the $π$-$N$ and $π$-$π$ cases, a trend which is qualitatively consistent with the experiment. Problems toward a more realistic calculation for light quarks are discussed.

## Key claim

This exploratory lattice QCD study finds that nucleon-nucleon (N-N) s-wave scattering lengths are significantly larger than π-N and π-π scattering lengths for heavy quarks (mπ/mρ ≈ 0.73–0.95), a trend qualitatively consistent with experiment, but the absolute values obtained (≈1.0–1.5 fm) are still much smaller than the experimental values (≈10–20 fm) because the simulations use heavy pions (mπ ≈ 0.7–1.5 GeV).

## Method

The authors performed quenched lattice QCD simulations with the Wilson quark action at β = 5.7 (lattice spacing a ≈ 0.14 fm) on a 20⁴ lattice for N-N and a 12³ × 20 lattice for π-N and π-π cases. They extracted s-wave scattering lengths from the energy shift δE of two-hadron states at zero relative momentum in a finite box using Lüscher's formula, computing hadron four-point functions via quark propagators with wall sources and Coulomb gauge fixing.

## Key numbers

- N-N spin-singlet scattering length: a₀(NN) ≈ 1.0–1.5 fm in physical units (converted from lattice units using a ≈ 0.14 fm determined from ρ meson mass extrapolated to chiral limit)
- N-N spin-triplet scattering length: larger than singlet values, indicating stronger attraction consistent with deuteron bound state existence
- Quark masses studied correspond to mπ/mρ ≈ 0.73, 0.85, and 0.95
- Energy shifts δE_NN extracted from fits were quite small (≈ 0.01 in lattice units)
- For π-π and π-N cases, results agreed with current algebra/PCAC predictions within one standard deviation at all quark masses studied

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice studies of π-π scattering (I=2 and I=0 channels) by Kuramashi et al. (1993) and others to the technically more difficult N-N system. It demonstrates the practical feasibility of extracting N-N scattering lengths from lattice QCD using Lüscher's finite-volume method, but identifies three major obstacles to realistic light-quark calculations: the need for much larger lattices (>2–3 fm) to handle large scattering lengths, the problem of constructing the lowest scattering state orthogonal to the deuteron bound state in the triplet channel, and rapidly growing statistical errors for lighter quarks (scaling as exp((2m_N − 3mπ)t) for N-N). The results motivate future calculations with lighter quarks and larger lattices, which must await increased computing power.
