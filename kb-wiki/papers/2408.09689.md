# Gravitational form factor $D$ of charmonium from shear stress

> Based on our recent analysis of the hadronic matrix element of the stress-energy tensor in covariant light front dynamics, we extract the charmonium gravitational form factor $D(Q^2)$ from shear stress $T^{12}$. This is in contrast to our recent work using the (light-front) energy density $T^{+-}$. Indeed, by comparing these two currents, we identify terms that are responsible for the violation of the current conservation. Numerical results based on basis light-front quantization show that the violation effects are small and the $D$-term extracted from the two currents are close to each other, hence validating our previous work using $T^{+-}$.

## Key claim

The gravitational form factor $D(Q^2)$ of charmonium can be extracted from the shear stress component $T^{12}$ of the stress-energy tensor, and for the ground-state $\eta_c$ the results are close to those previously obtained from the light-front energy density $T^{+-}$, validating the earlier approach; however, for the $P$-wave scalar $\chi_{c0}$ some discrepancy appears, indicating that the impulse ansatz used with $T^{+-}$ is a better approximation for more compact systems.

## Method

The authors compute the hadronic matrix element of the stress-energy tensor for charmonium states using covariant light-front dynamics, extracting the gravitational form factor $D(Q^2)$ from the shear stress component $T^{12}$ in the Drell-Yan frame with the transverse Breit frame. The non-perturbative charmonium wave functions are obtained by diagonalizing a low-energy effective light-front Hamiltonian (including confining and one-gluon exchange interactions) within the basis light-front quantization (BLFQ) approach, using harmonic oscillator basis functions in the transverse direction and Jacobi polynomials in the longitudinal direction. The $D$-term from $T^{12}$ is compared with results from $T^{+-}$, and the difference is used to extract the form factor $\bar c(Q^2)$ which quantifies violation of current conservation.

## Key numbers

* $\eta_c$: $D = -4.5(0)$ from $T^{12}$, compared to $-4.7(0)$ from $T^{+-}$ (central values at $N_{\text{max}} = 8$)
* $\chi_{c0}$: $D = -4.5(1)$ from $T^{12}$, compared to $-6.2(1)$ from $T^{+-}$ (central values at $N_{\text{max}} = 8$)
* $\eta_c$ matter radius $r_A = 0.18(1)$ fm from both $T^{12}$ and $T^{+-}$
* $\eta_c$ energy radius $r_E = 0.23(1)$ fm from both currents
* $\eta_c''$ (third radial excitation): $D = -12(2)$ from both $T^{12}$ and $T^{+-}$
* $\chi_{c0}'$: $D = -7.6(8)$ from $T^{12}$, compared to $-9.5(6)$ from $T^{+-}$
* Basis truncation: $N_{\text{max}} = 8$ corresponds to UV resolution $\Lambda_\text{uv} \approx \kappa\sqrt{8}$ with $\kappa \approx 1$ GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of charmonium gravitational form factors using the light-front energy density $T^{+-}$ (Xu et al., 2024) and their systematic covariant light-front study of stress-energy tensor components (Cao et al., 2024). It provides a cross-check of the $D$-term extraction using a different "good current" ($T^{12}$) that does not require the impulse ansatz or assume current conservation. The results enable future studies of the mechanical properties of hadrons, including the pressure distribution, and can be extended to other systems such as nucleons and pions that have been solved in light-front Hamiltonian approaches (Li et al., 2015; Li et al., 2017; various authors, 2010-2024).
