# Analyzing power for the proton elastic scattering from neutron-rich 6He nucleus

> Vector analyzing power for the proton-6He elastic scattering at 71 MeV/nucleon has been measured for the first time, with a newly developed polarized proton solid target working at low magnetic field of 0.09 T. The results are found to be incompatible with a t-matrix folding model prediction. Comparisons of the data with g-matrix folding analyses clearly show that the vector analyzing power is sensitive to the nuclear structure model used in the reaction analysis. The alpha-core distribution in 6He is suggested to be a possible key to understand the nuclear structure sensitivity.

## Key claim

The first measurement of vector analyzing power for proton-6He elastic scattering at 71 MeV/nucleon shows that the data are incompatible with a t-matrix folding model prediction, but comparisons with g-matrix folding analyses reveal that the analyzing power is sensitive to the nuclear structure model used, with the alpha-core distribution in 6He suggested as a possible key to understand this sensitivity.

## Method

A 70.6 ± 1.4 MeV/nucleon radioactive 6He beam produced via projectile fragmentation of a 92 MeV/nucleon 12C beam on a beryllium target bombarded a newly developed polarized proton solid target made of naphthalene doped with pentacene, operating at 0.09 T and 100 K. Scattered 6He particles were detected by a multi-wire drift chamber and plastic scintillators, while recoiled protons were detected by counter telescopes with single-wire drift chambers and CsI(Tl) scintillators covering θ_c.m. = 35°–90°. The absolute proton polarization was calibrated by comparing NMR signal amplitude to asymmetry in p-4He scattering at 80 MeV/nucleon using analyzing power data from Togawa (1987).

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 70.6 ± 1.4 MeV/nucleon for 6He; primary 12C beam at 92 MeV/nucleon with 600 pnA intensity
- Target magnetic field: 0.09 T; target temperature: 100 K
- 6He beam intensity: 3.0 × 10^5 cps; beam purity: 95%
- Proton polarization: 20 ± 4% at maximum, 14 ± 3% on average
- Systematic uncertainty in A_y: 19% (angle-independent); systematic uncertainty in cross section: 9% for θ_c.m. ≤ 87°
- Predicted A_y varies by as much as 0.2 depending on the nuclear structure model used in g-matrix folding analyses

## Context

This work extends earlier spin-asymmetry measurements for p-6He scattering reported by Hatano (2005) which had insufficient accuracy due to an assumed target polarization value, and contradicts the t-matrix folding model prediction by Weppner (2000) which gave large positive analyzing power values inconsistent with the data. The results enable detailed tests of nuclear structure models for halo nuclei, showing that the alpha-core distribution in 6He, rather than direct valence neutron contributions, is the likely origin of the nuclear structure sensitivity in the analyzing power, and demonstrate that the low-magnetic-field polarized target technique can be applied to future radioactive nuclear beam facilities.
