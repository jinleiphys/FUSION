# Superheavy Nuclei and the Changing Face of Nuclear Magicity

> Using a relativistic mean field formalism, we analyzed the magic number sequence for finite nuclei in the superheavy valley. The result for the IOPB-I parameter set is compared with the well-known NL3 force. The magic numbers obtained from IOPB-I and NL3 interactions are found to be similar. Analysing the single-particle levels and the number of nucleons occupied in it, we find the close shell sequence as 2, 8, 18, 34, 50, 58, 80, 82, 92, 114, 120, 120, 138, 164, 172, 184 and 198 for the $^{318}{120}$ mass region. Again, with a careful inspection, we noticed large shell gaps at nucleon numbers 2, 8, 18, 34, 50, 58, 80, 92, 120, 138, 164, 172, 184, and 198, which may be considered as the magic number sequence for the superheavy nuclei. This change may be due to the shape change of the nuclear potential as compared to the stability valley.

## Key claim
Using a relativistic mean field formalism with the IOPB-I and NL3 parameter sets, the authors propose a revised magic number sequence for superheavy nuclei that differs from the traditional sequence in the stability valley, but they hedge that some shell closures (e.g., N=82, 114) are not considered magic because their shell gaps are too small, and the change is attributed to the shape change of the nuclear potential.

## Method
The authors solved the effective field theory motivated relativistic mean field (E-RMF) Lagrangian self-consistently in spherical symmetry for a series of Z=120 isotopes and other superheavy nuclei. They calculated binding energies, charge radii, nuclear densities, nuclear potentials, spin-orbit potentials, and single-particle energy levels for both protons and neutrons using the IOPB-I and NL3 parameter sets, comparing results with experimental binding energies where available.

## Key numbers
- Binding energy of $^{270}$Ds: IOPB-I predicts 1947.7 MeV, NL3 predicts 1952.3 MeV, experimental value 1958.5 MeV (errors of 0.55% and 0.31% respectively)
- Binding energy of $^{318}$120: IOPB-I predicts 2206.38 MeV, NL3 predicts 2212.18 MeV
- Charge radius of $^{318}$120: IOPB-I gives 6.460 fm, NL3 gives 6.447 fm
- Proposed neutron magic numbers for superheavy nuclei: 2, 8, 18, 34, 50, 58, 80, 92, 120, 138, 164, 172, 184, 198
- Proposed proton magic numbers for superheavy nuclei: 2, 8, 18, 28, 34, 50, 58, 80, 92, 120
- The fitted "SOA" potential deviates from the actual nuclear potential by Δ = 1.56 (absolute mean difference)

## Context
This work extends the search for nuclear magic numbers beyond the stability valley, following earlier predictions by Patra and group (1997) and others (Gupta 1997, Sil 2004, Sahoo 2020) that different magic sequences appear in exotic and superheavy regions. It contradicts earlier claims that Z=114 is the next proton magic number (Patyk 1991, Nix 1992, 1994) by finding the shell gap at Z=114 too small to qualify. The results enable experimental searches for predicted double closed shell nuclei such as $^{230}$U, $^{256}$U, $^{278}$Fl, $^{286}$Fl, and $^{304}$120, and suggest that the nuclear potential shape changes significantly with neutron number while being insensitive to proton number in the superheavy region.
