# Chiral medium produced by parallel electric and magnetic fields

> We compute (pseudo)critical temperature, $T_c$, of chiral symmetry restoration for quark matter in the background of parallel electric and magnetic fields. This field configuration leads to the production of a chiral medium on a time scale $τ$, characterized by a nonvanishing value of the chiral density that equilibrates due to microscopic processes in the thermal bath. We estimate the relaxation time $τ$ to be about $\approx 0.1-1$ fm/c around the chiral crossover; then we compute the effect of the fields and of the chiral medium on~$T_c$. We find $T_c$ to be lowered by the external fields in the chiral medium.

# Chiral medium produced by parallel electric and magnetic fields

## Key claim
The critical temperature of chiral symmetry restoration is lowered by parallel electric and magnetic fields in the chiral medium, but the effect of the equilibrated chiral density itself is weak, increasing the constituent quark mass by less than 10% relative to the case without chiral density.

## Method
The authors compute the relaxation time of chiral density using a Boltzmann collision integral within an NJL model, considering chirality-changing processes via pion-exchange and sigma-meson-exchange scattering. They then solve the gap equation and number equation simultaneously to determine the equilibrium chiral density and the temperature-dependent constituent quark mass in the presence of parallel electric and magnetic fields, using the relaxation time to fix the equilibrium value of the chiral density via the chiral anomaly equation.

## Key numbers
- Relaxation time of chiral density around the chiral crossover: approximately 0.1–1 fm/c.
- For the NJL model with pion-exchange, the relaxation time drops to about 0.1 fm/c for temperatures above T_c; for the NJL+quasiparticle model, it stays approximately constant at about 1 fm/c at the crossover.
- For field strengths eE = eB = 8 m_pi^2, the increase of the constituent quark mass due to the equilibrated chiral density is within 10% of the n_5 = 0 result.
- An electric field of eE ≈ 4 m_pi^2 is sufficient to bring the critical temperature down to the zero-field case even for the largest magnetic field considered.

## Context
This work extends studies of the chiral magnetic effect in heavy ion collisions (Kharzeev 2007, Fukushima 2008) and the effect of parallel electric and magnetic fields on chiral symmetry (Babansky 1997, Warringa 2012, Cao 2015). The authors build on their own prior calculations of the relaxation time (Ruggieri 2016) and the critical temperature as a function of chiral chemical potential (Ruggieri 2016). The results enable future studies of neutral pion condensation induced by the chiral anomaly and charged pion condensation from the mismatch of up and down quark chemical potentials in the chiral medium.
