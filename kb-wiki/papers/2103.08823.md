# Density dependence of the quark condensate in isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter

> We compute the density dependence of the quark condensate and how it changes under the influence of isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter. This is achieved by the calculation of the relevant Feynman diagrams using in-medium chiral perturbation theory. We find that the absolute value of the quark condensate decreases in nuclear matter by around 35% at normal nuclear density, in agreement with previous model-independent calculations. We also present an quantitative estimation of the difference of up- and down quark in-medium condensates.

## Key claim
The absolute value of the quark condensate decreases in nuclear matter by around 35% at normal nuclear density, in agreement with previous model-independent calculations, and the authors provide a quantitative estimation of the difference between up- and down-quark in-medium condensates in isospin-asymmetric matter.

## Method
The authors compute the in-medium quark condensate using the chiral Ward identity, relating the pseudoscalar two-point correlation function to the condensate. They evaluate the relevant Feynman diagrams using in-medium chiral perturbation theory with an SU(2) chiral Lagrangian up to second order, expanding the nucleon propagator in a Fermi sea of non-interacting protons and neutrons with different Fermi momenta. The calculation includes one- and two-pion exchange diagrams with nucleon loops, and the authors also estimate the isospin-breaking condensate difference using an SU(3) chiral Lagrangian in the linear density approximation.

## Key numbers
- At normal nuclear density (ρ₀), the quark condensate is reduced by about 35% compared to its vacuum value.
- The low-energy constant c₁ = -0.59 GeV⁻¹, the pion decay constant f_π = 92.4 MeV, and the axial coupling g_A = 1.26 are used.
- The isospin-averaged pion mass m_π = 138 MeV and nucleon mass m_N = 939 MeV are employed.

## Context
This work extends a previous calculation by Goda (2013) for symmetric nuclear matter to the more general case of isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter, where proton and neutron densities can vary independently. The results agree with the model-independent low-density relation of Drukarev (1991) and with in-medium chiral perturbation theory calculations by Kaiser (2008, 2009) for symmetric matter and pure neutron matter. The formalism enables future calculations of in-medium hadron properties, such as in-medium pion properties as studied by Goda (2014).
