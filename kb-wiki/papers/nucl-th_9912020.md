# Local realizations of contact interactions in two- and three-body problems

> Mathematically rigorous theory of the two-body contact interaction in three dimension is reviewed. Local potential realizations of this proper contact interaction are given in terms of Poschl-Teller, exponential and square-well potentials. Three body calculation is carried out for the halo nucleus 11Li using adequately represented contact interaction.

# Local realizations of contact interactions in two- and three-body problems

## Key claim

The mathematically rigorous proper contact interaction (PCI), defined via self-adjoint extension theory, yields finite binding energies without dependence on basis truncation or regularization parameters, and can be realized by a limit of scaled local potentials (Pöschl-Teller, exponential, square-well) whose resolvents converge to that of the PCI in the norm-resolvent sense as the scaling parameter ε → 0.

## Method

The authors review the self-adjoint extension approach to the two-body contact interaction, where the Hamiltonian is defined by a real parameter α characterizing the boundary condition at the origin. They construct families of scaled local potentials V(r/ε)/ε² that possess a zero-energy resonance, and prove that the Green-operator of each family converges to the Green-operator of the PCI in the limit ε → 0. The three-body problem for ¹¹Li is solved using the stochastic variational method with the neutron-neutron interaction represented by the scaled Pöschl-Teller and exponential realizations of the PCI, while the neutron-core interaction is a shallow local potential taken from Esbensen (1997).

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy from PCI: 2.222496 MeV (limit ε → 0)
- For the scaled Pöschl-Teller realization at ε = 0.005, the deuteron binding energy is 2.220789 MeV (numerical) and 2.220783 MeV (exact), with error less than 10 eV
- Effective range of the scaled Pöschl-Teller potential for the dineutron system: r₀ = 0.01 fm at ε = 0.005, extrapolating to r₀ = 0.00 fm at ε = 0
- Two-neutron separation energy of ¹¹Li with PCI (Pöschl-Teller realization, free nn scattering length a_nn = -18.5 fm): extrapolated S₂n = -0.7302 MeV
- Two-neutron separation energy of ¹¹Li with PCI adjusted to reproduce experimental S₂n = -0.295 ± 0.035 MeV: extrapolated S₂n = -0.3130 MeV, with a_nn = -7.32 fm
- Mean square neutron-neutron radius for the adjusted PCI: r_nn² = 53.9 fm²; core-dineutron radius: r_cn² = 74.9 fm²
- Scattering length of the dineutron from scaled Pöschl-Teller potential at ε = 0.005: a_nn = -18.49 fm (target: -18.5 fm)

## Context

This work extends the mathematically rigorous treatment of contact interactions developed by Albeverio, Gesztesy, Hoegh-Krohn, and Holden (1988) and the earlier work of Berezin and Faddeev (1961). It provides explicit local potential realizations of the PCI that can be used in coordinate-space three-body calculations, in contrast to the Faddeev-equation approach of Baz, Zeldovich, and Perelomov (1966). The three-body application to ¹¹Li follows the model of Bertsch and Esbensen (1991) and Esbensen, Bertsch, and Hencken (1997), but replaces their truncated-basis regularization with the mathematically well-defined PCI, achieving effective ranges as small as 0.01 fm compared to the roughly 2 fm effective range in those earlier calculations. This enables future three-body calculations with contact interactions that are free from regularization-dependent parameters.
