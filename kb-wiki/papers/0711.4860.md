# On the nature of the lowest $1/2^-$ baryon nonet and decuplet

> From recent study of properties of the lowest spin-parity $1/2^-$ baryons, $N^*(1535)$ and $Δ^*(1620)$, new pictures for the internal structure of the lowest $1/2^-$ baryon octet and decuplet are proposed. While the lowest $1/2^-$ baryon octet may have large diquark-diquark-antiquark component, the lowest $1/2^-$ baryon decuplet is proposed to have large vector-meson-baryon components. Evidence for the "missing" members of the new pictures is pointed out and suggestions are made for detecting these predicted states from forthcoming experiments.

## Key claim

The lowest spin-parity 1/2- baryon octet (including N*(1535)) may have a large diquark-diquark-antiquark (pentaquark) component, while the lowest 1/2- baryon decuplet (including Δ*(1620)) may have large vector-meson-baryon molecular components; these pictures are proposed as alternatives to the classical three-quark constituent quark model, which fails to explain the mass ordering and decay patterns of these states.

## Method

The author analyzes recent experimental results from BES on J/ψ decays (J/ψ → pK⁻Λ̅, J/ψ → p̅pη) and from COSY-11 on pp → nK⁺Σ⁺, extracting coupling constants for N*(1535) and Δ*(1620) resonances. Using effective Lagrangian approaches and coupled-channel Breit-Wigner formulas, the author examines the decay patterns and mass shifts of these resonances, then proposes SU(3) nonet and decuplet assignments based on diquark cluster and vector-meson-baryon configurations.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of effective coupling constants for N*(1535) to KΛ and pη is g_N*(1535)KΛ / g_N*(1535)pη = 1.3 ± 0.3, deduced from BES branching ratio results on J/ψ → p̅pη and J/ψ → pK⁻Λ̅.
- The Breit-Wigner mass of N*(1535) is found to be around 1400 MeV when including its coupling to KΛ, much smaller than the previous value of about 1535 MeV obtained without that coupling.
- The branching ratio for J/ψ → p̅N*(1535)⁺ is (10 ± 3) × 10⁻⁴, while for J/ψ → Σ̅⁻Σ*(1385)⁺ it is (3.1 ± 0.5) × 10⁻⁴ and for J/ψ → Ξ̅⁺Ξ*(1530)⁻ it is (5.9 ± 1.5) × 10⁻⁴.
- The Δ*(1620) is about 85 MeV below the Nρ threshold; Σ*(1750)1/2⁻ is about 70 MeV below the NK* threshold; Ξ*(1950) is about 60 MeV below the ΛK* threshold.
- A predicted Ω* 1/2⁻ resonance is expected around 2160 MeV.
- Predicted SU(3) nonet partners include: an additional Λ* 1/2⁻ around 1570 MeV, a triplet Σ* 1/2⁻ around 1360 MeV, and a doublet Ξ* 1/2⁻ around 1520 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies (Zou 2007) on intrinsic non-perturbative five-quark components in baryons, building on evidence from deep inelastic scattering showing that in the proton the number of d̅ exceeds u̅ by about 0.12 (Garvey), which cannot be explained by classical quark models but can be explained by meson-cloud models (Thomas) or pentaquark configurations (Zou and Riska). The paper contradicts the classical three-quark constituent quark model's predictions for excited baryons, which fails to explain why N*(1535) is heavier than Λ*(1405) and N*(1440). The proposed pictures enable searches for "missing" baryon resonances in forthcoming experiments at BEPC2, CEBAF, JPARC, and BESIII, and suggest studying baryon octet and vector meson octet interactions following the approach of Sarkar et al. for baryon decuplet and pseudoscalar meson octet interactions.
