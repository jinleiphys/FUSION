# On the nature of the lowest $1/2^-$ baryon nonet and decuplet

> From recent study of properties of the lowest spin-parity $1/2^-$ baryons, $N^*(1535)$ and $őĒ^*(1620)$, new pictures for the internal structure of the lowest $1/2^-$ baryon octet and decuplet are proposed. While the lowest $1/2^-$ baryon octet may have large diquark-diquark-antiquark component, the lowest $1/2^-$ baryon decuplet is proposed to have large vector-meson-baryon components. Evidence for the "missing" members of the new pictures is pointed out and suggestions are made for detecting these predicted states from forthcoming experiments.

## Key claim

The lowest spin-parity 1/2- baryon octet (including N*(1535)) may have a large diquark-diquark-antiquark (pentaquark) component, while the lowest 1/2- baryon decuplet (including őĒ*(1620)) may have large vector-meson-baryon molecular components; these pictures are proposed as alternatives to the classical three-quark constituent quark model, which fails to explain the mass ordering and decay patterns of these states.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies (Zou 2007) on intrinsic non-perturbative five-quark components in baryons, building on evidence from deep inelastic scattering showing that in the proton the number of dŐÖ exceeds uŐÖ by about 0.12 (Garvey), which cannot be explained by classical quark models but can be explained by meson-cloud models (Thomas) or pentaquark configurations (Zou and Riska). The paper contradicts the classical three-quark constituent quark model's predictions for excited baryons, which fails to explain why N*(1535) is heavier than őõ*(1405) and N*(1440). The proposed pictures enable searches for "missing" baryon resonances in forthcoming experiments at BEPC2, CEBAF, JPARC, and BESIII, and suggest studying baryon octet and vector meson octet interactions following the approach of Sarkar et al. for baryon decuplet and pseudoscalar meson octet interactions.
