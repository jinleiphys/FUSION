# Configuration Interactions Constrained by Energy Density Functionals

> A new method for constructing a Hamiltonian for configuration interaction calculations with constraints to energies of spherical configurations obtained with energy-density-functional (EDF) methods is presented. This results in a unified model that reproduced the EDF binding-energy in the limit of single-Slater determinants, but can also be used for obtaining energy spectra and correlation energies with renormalized nucleon-nucleon interactions. The three-body and/or density-dependent terms that are necessary for good nuclear saturation properties are contained in the EDF. Applications to binding energies and spectra of nuclei in the region above 208Pb are given.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new method that constrains the monopole part of configuration interaction (CI) calculations to reproduce the energies of spherical configurations obtained from energy-density-functional (EDF) methods, but only in the limit of single-Slater determinants; the accuracy of the overall method is limited by the accuracy of the EDF results for single-particle energies near doubly-magic nuclei.

## Method

The authors construct a valence CI Hamiltonian for nuclei above ²⁰⁸Pb by taking single-particle energies from the Skxmb Skyrme EDF calculation and using the N³LO nucleon-nucleon interaction renormalized via the Vlowk method with a cutoff of Λ = 2.2 fm, including second-order core-polarization corrections up to 6ℏω. The monopole part of the effective two-body interaction is replaced by values obtained from the EDF using Eq. 2, which computes the energy difference between a closed shell plus two nucleons and the sum of the closed shell plus individual nucleon energies. The CI calculations are performed with the NuShell code for valence protons in orbitals (0h9/2, 1f7/2, 0i13/2) and neutrons in (0i11/2, 1g9/2, 0j15/2).

## Key numbers

- The Skxmb Skyrme interaction reproduces experimental single-particle energies for low-lying states around ²⁰⁸Pb with an rms deviation of about 300 keV.
- The binding energy of ²⁰⁸Pb with Skxmb is 1636.46 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 1636.45 MeV.
- For the 0h9/2 proton orbital, the renormalized N³LO monopole interaction is 0.170 MeV, the EDF monopole from Skxmb is 0.288 MeV, giving a correction of 0.118 MeV.
- For the (0h9/2)¹⁰ configuration, CI gives a binding energy increase of 25.05 MeV relative to ²⁰⁸Pb, while EDF gives 25.24 MeV; without the monopole correction, the CI result would differ by 5.3 MeV.
- For the (1f7/2)⁸ configuration, CI gives 13.27 MeV and EDF gives 13.41 MeV.
- The effective valence three-body monopole strength V̅₃ is on the order of 1–2 keV.
- For ²¹⁸U (Z=92), the M-scheme dimension is about 1.5 million, and the ground state contains only 4.7% of the (0h9/2)¹⁰ component.
- Up to Z=88, the difference between experiment and theory for binding energy relative to ²⁰⁸Pb is on the order of 100 keV, increasing to about 700 keV for ²¹⁸U.
- For the 1g9/2 neutron orbital, the N³LO monopole is −0.076 MeV and the EDF monopole is 0.017 MeV, giving a correction of 0.093 MeV.
- For the 0h9/2 proton and 1g9/2 neutron orbitals, the N³LO monopole is −0.143 MeV and the EDF monopole is −0.205 MeV, giving a correction of −0.062 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the configuration interaction method by incorporating monopole constraints from energy-density-functional theory, addressing the deterioration of CI results for many valence nucleons noted by previous authors (e.g., "bab" and "zuk"). The method is applied to nuclei above ²⁰⁸Pb using the Skxmb Skyrme interaction, which improves upon the Skxm interaction of Brown (2000) by optimizing single-particle energies for ²⁰⁹Bi and ²⁰⁹Pb. The approach enables unified calculations that reproduce EDF binding energies for single-Slater determinants while retaining the ability to compute spectra and correlation energies, and can be extended to other doubly-closed shell systems as EDF functionals improve.
