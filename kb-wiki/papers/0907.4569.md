# $πN$ Scattering in the $Δ(1232)$ Region in an Effective Field Theory

> We develop a generalized version of heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory to describe pion-nucleon scattering in a kinematic domain that extends continuously from threshold to the delta-isobar peak. The $P$-wave phase shifts are used to illustrate this framework. We also compare our approach with those in the literature that concern the delta resonance.

## Key claim

The authors develop a generalized heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory that can describe pion-nucleon scattering continuously from threshold to the delta-isobar peak, but the framework relies on a "kinematic fine-tuning" where the delta-nucleon mass splitting and the pion mass are both treated as comparable low-energy scales, and the expansion is only valid within a narrow window around the resonance where the dressed delta propagator is enhanced.

## Method

The authors construct a heavy-baryon chiral Lagrangian with explicit nucleon and delta fields, organizing interactions by chiral index. They employ a power counting that treats the delta-nucleon mass splitting δ and the pion mass mπ as comparable scales (Q ∼ mπ ∼ δ), and resum the geometric series of one-delta-reducible diagrams near the resonance where the bare delta propagator becomes enhanced. The amplitude is calculated by sewing together two power-counting schemes: standard chiral perturbation theory away from the resonance and a modified counting near the pole, with the dressed delta propagator used only in one-delta-reducible diagrams.

## Key numbers

- The delta resonance mass is mΔ ≡ mN + δ ≃ 1230 MeV, with δ ∼ 290 MeV (the nucleon-delta mass splitting)
- The pion decay constant is fπ ≃ 92 MeV
- The characteristic QCD scale is MQCD ∼ 1 GeV
- The LO delta self-energy ΣΔ^(0) is O(Q³/MQCD²), and the bare delta propagator scales as O(MQCD²/Q³) within a window of size |E − δ| = O(Q³/MQCD²) around the delta peak
- The LO contribution to the pion self-energy Σπ^(0) is O(Q⁴/MQCD²)
- The LO contribution to the nucleon self-energy ΣN^(0) is O(Q³/MQCD²)

## Context

This work extends the approach of Pascalutsa and collaborators (2006-2007) who developed a power counting based on three separate scales mπ ≪ δ ≪ MQCD for electromagnetic reactions in the delta region, but here applied specifically to πN scattering. The authors compare their method with the non-perturbative treatment of the delta in chiral perturbation theory by Ellis and Tang (1998), who resummed the leading delta self-energy, and with unitarization approaches using the N/D method or Bethe-Salpeter equation. This framework enables systematic calculation of πN scattering amplitudes from threshold through the delta resonance in a controlled expansion, which can then inform pion-exchange nuclear forces where convergence was previously slow.
