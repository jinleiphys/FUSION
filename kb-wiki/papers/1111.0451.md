# Symmetry restoration for odd-mass nuclei with a Skyrme energy density functional

> In these proceedings, we report first results for particle-number and angular-momentum projection of self-consistently blocked triaxial one-quasiparticle HFB states for the description of odd-A nuclei in the context of regularized multi-reference energy density functionals, using the entire model space of occupied single-particle states. The SIII parameterization of the Skyrme energy functional and a volume-type pairing interaction are used.

## Key claim

Particle-number and angular-momentum projection of self-consistently blocked triaxial one-quasiparticle HFB states can be performed for odd-mass nuclei using a regularized multi-reference energy density functional, but the choice of which quasiparticle to block is ambiguous at the single-reference level and requires the multi-reference approach to resolve.

## Method

The authors solve self-consistent HFB equations for one-quasiparticle states in odd-A nuclei using the "two-basis method" with the SIII Skyrme energy density functional and a volume-type contact pairing interaction (strength 300 MeV fm³, cutoffs at 5 MeV above and below the Fermi energy). They then apply particle-number projection and angular-momentum projection (with K-mixing via the Hill-Wheeler-Griffin equation) to these states, using a regularized multi-reference EDF to remove spurious contributions. The non-diagonal norm kernels are calculated directly with their sign using a Pfaffian technique.

## Key numbers

- The non-projected minimum for the lowest one-quasiparticle state in ⁴⁹Cr is axial at β = 0.26, γ = 0.0°.
- Particle-number projection gives an almost constant energy gain of about 1 MeV across the entire surface.
- After particle-number and angular-momentum projection to J = 5/2, the minimum shifts to triaxial deformation at β = 0.33, γ = 15.3°, about 5 MeV lower than the non-projected minimum.
- The pairing interaction strength used is 300 MeV fm³.

## Context

This work extends beyond-mean-field methods (symmetry restoration and configuration mixing within the Generator Coordinate Method) from even-even nuclei to odd-A nuclei, where the last nucleon breaks symmetries that make such methods efficient for even-even systems. The authors note that Schunck et al. demonstrated that time-odd components in the EDF make the sextants of the β-γ plane inequivalent, requiring exploration of at least three out of six sextants. The method enables future studies of coupling between single-particle and shape degrees of freedom, separation energies, g factors, and spectroscopic quadrupole moments in odd-A nuclei on the same footing as even-even nuclei.
