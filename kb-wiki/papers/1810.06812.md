# Extrapolation of scattering data to the negative-energy region. Application to the $p-^{16}$O system

> The problem of analytic continuation of the scattering data to the negative-energy region to obtain information on asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) of bound states is discussed. It is shown that a recently suggested $Δ$ method [O.L.Ram\'ırez Suárez and J.-M. Sparenberg, Phys. Rev. C {\bf 96}, 034601 (2017)] is not strictly correct in the mathematical sense since it is not an analytic continuation of a partial-wave scattering amplitude to the region of negative energies. However, it can be used for practical purposes for sufficiently large charges and masses of colliding particles. Both the $Δ$ method and the standard method of continuing of the effective range function are applied to the $p-^{16}$O system which is of interest for nuclear astrophysics. The ANCs for the ground $5/2^+$ and excited $1/2^+$ states of $^{17}$F are determined.

## Key claim

The Delta method of Ramírez Suárez and Sparenberg (2017) is not a mathematically correct analytic continuation of the partial-wave scattering amplitude to negative energies, but it can be used for practical purposes for systems with sufficiently large charges and masses of colliding particles, where the imaginary part of the denominator becomes negligible near zero energy.

## Method

The authors applied both the standard effective-range function (ERF) method and the Delta method to the p-16O system, using experimental elastic scattering phase shifts from Dubov (for the 1/2+ state) and Blue (for the 5/2+ state). For each method, they approximated the relevant function (K_l(E) or Delta_l(E)) by polynomials in energy E, determined the polynomial coefficients by chi-squared fitting to the phase-shift data, and analytically continued the polynomial to the bound-state pole at E = -epsilon to extract the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) via the residue of the renormalized Coulomb-nuclear partial-wave amplitude.

## Key numbers

- For the excited 1/2+ state of 17F (binding energy 0.1052 MeV, l=0): ERF method gives C0 = 101.9 fm^{-1/2}; Delta method gives C0 = 89.1 fm^{-1/2} (converged at polynomial order N=3).
- For the ground 5/2+ state of 17F (binding energy 0.6005 MeV, l=2): ERF method gives C2 = 0.88 fm^{-1/2}; Delta method gives C2 = 2.34 fm^{-1/2} (the latter considered erroneous).
- The Rydberg energy for the p-16O system is 1.50358 MeV, which sets the approximate upper bound for applicability of the Delta method.
- The Delta method failed for the d-alpha system (Rydberg energy 0.13 MeV) but was effective for the alpha-12C system (Rydberg energy 10.7 MeV), as reported in Blokhintsev et al. (2017).

## Context

This work extends the discussion of the Delta method initiated by Ramírez Suárez and Sparenberg (2017) and critically examined by Blokhintsev et al. (2017) and Sparenberg (2017). The p-16O system (Z1Z2=8) is intermediate between the d-alpha system (Z1Z2=2), where the Delta method fails, and the alpha-12C system (Z1Z2=12), where it succeeds. The extracted ANCs for 17F are relevant for evaluating the astrophysical S-factor of the 16O(p,gamma)17F reaction in the CNO cycle. The method enables determination of proton ANCs from elastic scattering data, which can then be used via mirror symmetry to infer neutron ANCs for mirror nuclei, and can also be applied within effective field theory frameworks.
