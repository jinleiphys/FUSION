# Generalized relativistic second-order spin hydrodynamics from Zubarev's non-equilibrium statistical operator

> Inspired by the work in Ref.[1], which considers the additional second-order contributions arising from nonlocal corrections due to two-point correlation functions of tensors of different ranks at distinct spacetime points, we similarly employ the nonequilibrium statistical operator method to extend this framework to include spin degrees of freedom. In addition to obtaining analogous extra second-order terms in the shear stress tensor, bulk viscous pressure, and charge diffusion currents resulting from such contributions, we further derive additional second-order terms originating from the same mechanism in the charge diffusion currents, rotational stress tensor and the boost heat vector. Furthermore, we express all transport coefficients represented by two-point or three-point correlations in terms of retarded Green's functions.

## Key claim

The paper extends the second-order relativistic spin hydrodynamics framework by incorporating additional nonlocal corrections arising from two-point correlation functions of tensors of different ranks at distinct spacetime points, deriving novel second-order terms in the rotational stress tensor and boost heat vector beyond those previously identified for shear stress, bulk viscous pressure, and charge diffusion currents.

## Method

The authors employ Zubarev's non-equilibrium statistical operator method, expanding the statistical operator to second order in the non-equilibrium component to derive constitutive equations for dissipative currents in a relativistic spin fluid. They decompose the energy-momentum tensor, charge currents, and spin tensor into equilibrium and dissipative components using a canonical-like pseudogauge, then compute statistical averages of dissipative fluxes via two-point and three-point correlation functions. All transport coefficients are expressed in terms of retarded Green's functions.

## Key numbers

- The spin chemical potential $\omega_{\mu\nu}$ is treated as a first-order quantity in the gradient expansion, as it reduces to the thermal vorticity tensor $\varpi=-\partial_{[\mu}\beta_{\nu]}$ in global thermal equilibrium
- The expansion scalar $\theta\equiv\partial_{\mu}u^{\mu}$ measures the fluid's expansion ($\theta>0$) or contraction ($\theta<0$)
- The velocity gradient decomposes as $\partial_{\mu}u_{\nu}=u_{\mu}Du_{\nu}+\frac{1}{3}\theta\Delta_{\mu\nu}+\sigma_{\mu\nu}+\nabla_{[\mu}u_{\nu]}$
- The metric signature is $g_{\mu\nu}=\text{diag}\left(+,-,-,-\right)$ with natural units $\hbar=k_{B}=c=1$

## Context

This work extends the framework of Harutyunyan (2025), which identified additional second-order nonlocal corrections from two-point correlation functions in viscous hydrodynamics, to include spin degrees of freedom. It builds upon the authors' previous formulation of relativistic second-order spin hydrodynamics (She 2024) derived via Zubarev's formalism, and generalizes it by incorporating contributions from tensors of different ranks at distinct spacetime points. The results enable future numerical simulations of spin polarization phenomena in heavy-ion collisions that account for spin chemical potential and spin density effects, which modify the polarization vector as shown by Liu (2021).
