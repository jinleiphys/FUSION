# Constraints on charm-anticharm asymmetry in the nucleon from lattice QCD

> We present the first lattice QCD calculation of the charm quark contribution to the nucleon electromagnetic form factors $G^c_{E,M}(Q^2)$ in the momentum transfer range $0\leq Q^2 \leq 1.4$ $\rm GeV^2$. The quark mass dependence, finite lattice spacing and volume corrections are taken into account simultaneously based on the calculation on three gauge ensembles including one at the physical pion mass. The nonzero value of the charm magnetic moment $μ^c_M=-0.00127(38)_{\rm stat}(5)_{\rm sys}$, as well as the Pauli form factor, reflects a nontrivial role of the charm sea in the nucleon spin structure. The nonzero $G^c_{E}(Q^2)$ indicates the existence of a nonvanishing asymmetric charm-anticharm sea in the nucleon. Performing a nonperturbative analysis based on holographic QCD and the generalized Veneziano model, we study the constraints on the $[c(x)-\bar{c}(x)]$ distribution from the lattice QCD results presented here. Our results provide complementary information and motivation for more detailed studies of physical observables that are sensitive to intrinsic charm and for future global analyses of parton distributions including asymmetric charm-anticharm distribution.

## Key claim

The first lattice QCD calculation of the charm quark contribution to nucleon electromagnetic form factors finds nonzero values for both the charm electric form factor and the charm magnetic moment, indicating a nonvanishing asymmetric charm-anticharm sea in the nucleon; however, the quantitative extraction of the [c(x)-c̄(x)] distribution requires additional model-dependent assumptions based on holographic QCD and the generalized Veneziano model.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations of disconnected insertion diagrams for the charm quark electromagnetic form factors G^c_E(Q^2) and G^c_M(Q^2) using valence overlap fermions on three RBC/UKQCD domain-wall fermion gauge ensembles with pion masses of 139, 300, and 330 MeV. They extracted the form factors using a two-state fit of the summed ratio of three-point to two-point correlation functions, then performed a simultaneous chiral, continuum, and infinite volume extrapolation using a z-expansion fit in the momentum transfer range 0 ≤ Q^2 ≤ 1.4 GeV^2. The resulting form factors were used as constraints within the light-front holographic QCD framework combined with the generalized Veneziano model to determine the [c(x)-c̄(x)] asymmetry distribution.

## Key numbers

- Charm magnetic moment μ^c_M = -0.00127(38)_stat(5)_sys, extracted from the z-expansion fit to G^c_M(Q^2)
- Charm electric radius ⟨r^2_E⟩^c = -0.0005(1) fm^2, obtained from the slope of G^c_E(Q^2) at Q^2=0
- Charm magnetic radius ⟨r^2_M⟩^c = -0.0003(1) fm^2, obtained from the slope of G^c_M(Q^2) at Q^2=0
- The J/ψ Regge trajectory used in the analysis: α(t)_J/ψ = t/(4κ_c^2) - 2.066, with κ_c = 0.874 GeV
- The universal function parameter a = 0.480 used in the light-front holographic QCD analysis

## Context

This work extends the methodology previously applied to strange quark electromagnetic form factors by Sufian et al. (2016, 2017) and the subsequent determination of the [s(x)-s̄(x)] asymmetry by Sufian et al. (2018) using light-front holographic QCD and the generalized Veneziano model. The results provide complementary information to global PDF analyses by Dulat et al. (2013), Ball et al. (2016), and Hou et al. (2017), which have assumed [c(x)-c̄(x)]=0, and motivate future experimental searches for intrinsic charm in processes such as J/ψ production near threshold at JLab and charmed hadron production at LHC.
