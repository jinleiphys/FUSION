# In-medium Pions and Partial Restoration of Chiral Symmetry: a model-independent analysis

> Exploiting operator relations in QCD, we derive a novel and model-independent formula relating the in-medium quark condensate <bar-q q>* to the decay constant F*_t and the wave function renormalization constant Z* of the pion in the nuclear medium. Evaluating Z* at low density from the iso-scalar pion-nucleon scattering data, it is concluded that the enhanced repulsion of the s-wave isovector pion-nucleusinteraction observed in the deeply bound pionic atoms implies directly the reduction of the in-medium quark condensate. The knowlege of the in-medium pion mass is not necessary to reach this conclusion.

## Key claim

The paper derives a model-independent relation linking the in-medium quark condensate to the in-medium pion decay constant and the pion wave function renormalization constant, concluding that the enhanced repulsion observed in deeply bound pionic atoms directly implies a reduction of the quark condensate without requiring knowledge of the in-medium pion mass.

## Method

The authors exploit operator relations in QCD, specifically the commutation relation between the axial charge and the pseudoscalar density, to derive a scaling law connecting the in-medium quark condensate, the temporal pion decay constant F_t*, and the wave function renormalization constant Z*. They then evaluate Z* at low density using the linear density approximation, extracting the slope of the isoscalar pion-nucleon scattering amplitude from the Weinberg point (given by the pion-nucleon sigma term) and the threshold scattering length from pionic hydrogen atom data.

## Key numbers

- The pion-nucleon scattering length from pionic hydrogen atom data is a_piN = (0.0016 ± 0.0013) m_pi^{-1}, consistent with zero.
- The conclusion that Z*^{1/2}/Z^{1/2} < 1 holds as long as the scattering length is larger than -0.050 m_pi^{-1} for sigma_piN = 45 MeV, and larger than -0.067 m_pi^{-1} for sigma_piN = 60 MeV.
- The slope beta of the isoscalar piN amplitude is approximately sigma_piN/(F^2 m_pi^2), with F the vacuum pion decay constant.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Weise (2000) and Hatsuda (1999) that attributed the enhanced repulsion in s-wave isovector pion-nucleus interactions to a reduction of the in-medium pion decay constant. It also builds on chiral effective theory calculations by Jido (2000) and the pion optical potential work by Kolomeitsev (2002), which suggested that in-medium wave function renormalization is responsible for the reduction of F_t*. The derived scaling law enables a direct extraction of the in-medium quark condensate from pionic atom data and pion-nucleon scattering without needing the in-medium pion mass.
