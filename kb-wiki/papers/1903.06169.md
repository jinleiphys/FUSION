# Cool baryon and quark matter in holographic QCD

> We establish a holographic bottom-up model which covers both the baryonic and quark matter phases in cold and dense QCD. This is obtained by including the baryons using simple approximation schemes in the V-QCD model, which also includes the backreaction of the quark matter to the dynamics of pure Yang-Mills. We examine two approaches for homogeneous baryon matter: baryons as a thin layer of noninteracting matter in the holographic bulk, and baryons with a homogeneous bulk gauge field. We find that the second approach exhibits phenomenologically reasonable features. At zero temperature, the vacuum, baryon, and quark matter phases are separated by strongly first order transitions as the chemical potential varies. The equation of state in the baryonic phase is found to be stiff, i.e., the speed of sound clearly exceeds the value $c_s^2=1/3$ of conformal plasmas at high baryon densities.

## Key claim
The paper establishes a holographic bottom-up model (V-QCD) that covers both baryonic and quark matter phases in cold and dense QCD, but the authors explicitly note that the first approach (baryons as a thin layer of noninteracting matter) works unsatisfactorily, while the second approach (baryons with a homogeneous bulk gauge field) exhibits phenomenologically reasonable features, with the caveat that the model is restricted to homogeneous approximations and neglects light quark masses and isospin asymmetry.

## Method
The authors extended the V-QCD holographic model by incorporating baryons through two approximation schemes: first, baryons as a nondynamical thin layer localized in the holographic coordinate (point-like sources), and second, a homogeneous ansatz with SU(2) flavor symmetry for the spatial components of the non-Abelian flavor gauge field, sourcing baryon density through Chern-Simons coupling. Both approaches were studied at zero temperature, with the second approach including the coupling of baryons to the tachyon field (dual to the quark bilinear operator) to prevent baryons from falling into the infrared, and the full backreaction of the quark matter to the pure Yang-Mills dynamics was included.

## Key numbers
- The speed of sound in the baryonic phase clearly exceeds the conformal value \(c_s^2 = 1/3\) at high baryon densities, indicating a stiff equation of state.
- The IR asymptotics of the function \(w(\lambda)\) is constrained to \(w(\lambda) \sim \lambda^{-w_p}\) with \(w_p \ge 4/3\) for correct confinement properties, and meson spectra considerations set \(w_p \le 4/3\), pinning down \(w_p = 4/3\).
- The power laws for the potential functions are: \(V_g \sim \lambda^{4/3}\) for glueball trajectories, \(\kappa \sim \lambda^{-4/3}\) for meson trajectories, and \(V_{f0} \sim \lambda^{v_p}\) with \(4/3 \le v_p \le 10/3\) for regularity and phase diagram agreement.
- The latent heat at the baryon to quark-gluon plasma transition is sizable, and the phase transitions between vacuum, baryon, and quark matter phases are strongly first order as the chemical potential varies.

## Context
This work extends the V-QCD model, previously studied for thermodynamics at finite temperature and chemical potential (Alho 2012, Alho 2013, Alho 2015) and fitted to lattice data (Jokela 2018), by including baryon physics for the first time. It builds on holographic approaches to baryons in the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model (Bergman 2007, Rozali 2007) and other probe brane models (Gwak 2012, Evans 2012), while also setting new constraints on the IR asymptotics of the gauge field coupling function \(w(\lambda)\) that complement earlier findings for other potential functions (Gursoy 2007, Jarvinen 2011, Arean 2013). The results enable future studies of neutron star equations of state and the phase transition between nuclear and quark matter at intermediate densities.
