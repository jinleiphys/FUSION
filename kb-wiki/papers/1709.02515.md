# Observation of $^{6}$He+$t$ cluster states in $^{9}$Li

> $^{6}$He+$t$ cluster states of exited $^{9}$Li have been measured by 32.7 MeV/nucleon $^{9}$Li beams bombarding on $^{208}$Pb target. Two resonant states are clearly observed with the excitation energies at 9.8 MeV and 12.6 MeV and spin-parity of 3/2$^{-}$ and 7/2$^{-}$ respectively. These two states are considered to be members of K$^π$=1/2$^{-}$ band. The spin-parity of them are identified by the method of angular correlation analysis and verified by the continuum discretized coupled channels (CDCC) calculation, which agrees with the prediction of the generator coordinate method (GCM). A monopole matrix element about 4 fm$^{2}$ for the 3/2$^{-}$ state is extracted from the distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) calculation. These results strongly support the feature of clustering structure of two neutron-rich clusters in the neutron-rich nucleus $^{9}$Li for the first time.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first experimental observation of two resonant states in $^{9}$Li that decay into $^{6}$He + $t$ fragments, with the claim that these states represent a clustering structure of two neutron-rich clusters. The hedge is that the spin-parity assignments (3/2$^{-}$ for the 9.8 MeV state and 7/2$^{-}$ for the 12.6 MeV state) are identified through angular correlation analysis combined with continuum discretized coupled channels (CDCC) and generator coordinate method (GCM) calculations, but the authors acknowledge that other spin-parity assignments (1/2$^{-}$, 1/2$^{+}$, 3/2$^{+}$) cannot be ruled out by angular correlation alone for the 9.8 MeV state.

## Method

A 32.7 MeV/nucleon $^{9}$Li beam was directed onto a $^{208}$Pb target, and the breakup fragments $^{6}$He and $t$ were detected in coincidence using a zero-degree telescope array of double-sided silicon strip detectors and CsI(Tl) scintillators. The relative energy spectrum of the $^{6}$He+$t$ pairs was reconstructed via the invariant mass method, and the excitation energies of $^{9}$Li were obtained by adding the $^{6}$He+$t$ threshold energy. Angular correlation analysis, CDCC calculations with the FRESCO code, and DWBA calculations were used to identify spin-parities and extract the monopole transition matrix element.

## Key numbers

* Two resonant states observed at excitation energies of 9.8 MeV and 12.6 MeV in $^{9}$Li, decaying into $^{6}$He+$t$.
* Spin-parity assignments: 3/2$^{-}$ for the 9.8 MeV state and 7/2$^{-}$ for the 12.6 MeV state.
* Width of the first resonance peak (9.8 MeV): $\Gamma = 1.1(2)$ MeV (statistical error only).
* Monopole matrix element $M$(IS0) = 4 fm$^{2}$ extracted for the 3/2$^{-}$ state at 9.8 MeV from DWBA calculation, based on an EWSR of 4444.12 MeV$\cdot$fm$^{4}$ and a normalization factor $a_0 = 0.0086(20)$.
* Beam energy: 32.7 MeV/nucleon; beam intensity: $1.1 \times 10^{3}$ particles per second with 99% purity.
* Relative energy resolution: 0.8 MeV at 2.5 MeV, increasing to 1.1 MeV at 5.0 MeV; detection efficiency 37% at 3.3 MeV.

## Context

This work provides the first experimental evidence for $^{6}$He+$t$ cluster states in $^{9}$Li, extending theoretical predictions from GCM calculations (Kanada-En'yo, 2015) and AMD calculations (Kanada-En'yo, 2014) that suggested a K$^{\pi}=1/2^{-}$ band of $^{6}$He+$t$ cluster states near the decay threshold. The extracted monopole matrix element is comparable to those for cluster states in $^{12}$C, $^{16}$O, and $^{12}$Be, and close to the GCM prediction of approximately 6 fm$^{2}$ for $^{9}$Li. The results enable further investigation of cluster formation in neutron-rich nuclei, particularly the role of redundant neutrons in promoting clustering, and call for additional experimental study of states in the 11-14 MeV excitation energy region.
