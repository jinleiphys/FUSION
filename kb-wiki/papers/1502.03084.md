# NJL model approach to diquarks and baryons in quark matter

> We describe baryons as quark-diquark bound states at finite temperature and density within the NJL model for chiral symmetry breaking and restoration in quark matter. Based on a generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck approach to mesons and diquarks we present in a first step the thermodynamics of quark-diquark matter which includes the Mott dissociation of diquarks at finite temperature. In a second step we solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the baryon as a quark-diquark bound state in quark-diquark matter. We obtain a stable, bound baryon even beyond the Mott temperature for diquark dissociation since the phase space occupation effect (Pauli blocking for quarks and Bose enhancement for diquarks) in the Bethe-Salpeter kernel for the nucleon approximately cancel so that the nucleon mass follows the in-medium behaviour of the quark and diquark masses towards chiral restoration. In this situation the baryon is obtained as a "borromean" three-quark state in medium because the two-particle state (diquark) is unbound while the three-particle state (baryon) is bound.

## Key claim

The authors show that within the NJL model, a baryon (nucleon) can remain bound even above the Mott temperature for diquark dissociation, because Pauli blocking for quarks and Bose enhancement for diquarks approximately cancel in the Bethe-Salpeter kernel, making the baryon a "borromean" three-quark state in medium where the two-particle diquark state is unbound but the three-particle baryon state remains bound.

## Method

The authors employ a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with scalar-pseudoscalar, vector, and diquark interaction channels, performing bosonization via Hubbard-Stratonovich transformations to obtain a path integral representation of the partition function. They solve the mean-field gap equation for the quark mass as a function of temperature and chemical potential, then solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation for mesons, diquarks, and baryons (as quark-diquark bound states) using an instantaneous approximation for the quark-diquark interaction vertex. The thermodynamic potential is expressed in a generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck form that includes resonant and continuum contributions from correlations.

## Key numbers

- The baryon mass defect (binding energy) parameter is taken as a constant value of 261.7 MeV in the zero-temperature limit.
- NJL model parameters used: constituent quark mass m = 400 MeV, bare quark mass m0 = 5.588 MeV, cutoff Lambda = 587.9 MeV, scalar coupling GS * Lambda^2 = 2.442, vector coupling GV = 0.5 * GS.
- The NJL-derived nuclear matter saturates at approximately 3.4 times the empirical saturation density n0 = 0.16 fm^-3, with a binding energy of approximately -40 MeV (compared to the empirical -16 MeV).
- For comparison with the Walecka model: GS = 0.33 * GS,Walecka and GV = 0.024 * GV,Walecka, where the Walecka model uses m_sigma = 550 MeV, m_omega = 783 MeV, g_sigma = 10.3, and g_omega = 12.7.

## Context

This work extends the generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck approach previously applied to mesonic correlations by Hufner (1994) and to diquark degrees of freedom by Blaschke (2013, 2014) within the NJL model. It builds on earlier proposals by Cahill (1988) and Reinhardt (1989) for describing baryons as quark-diquark bound states, and on exploratory studies by Wang (2010) of nucleon spectral functions in the QCD phase diagram. The paper aims to prepare the ground for a Beth-Uhlenbeck description of nuclear matter that could unify the hadronic and quark matter phases, extending earlier work by Bentz (2001, 2002) and Lawley (2006) that demonstrated the possibility of deriving a Walecka-type model from quark degrees of freedom but did not achieve a unified description of the nuclear-to-quark matter transition.
