# QCD Wehrl and entanglement entropies in a gluon spectator model at small-$x$

> Recent studies have shown that hadronic multiplicity in deep inelastic scattering can be associated with entanglement entropy. However, such definitions are intrinsically longitudinal and do not capture the full phase-space structure of the proton. In this work, we investigate the proton Wehrl entropy constructed from the gluon Husimi distribution, which provides a positive phase-space description within the present definitions and model calculations. Within this framework, we employ a gluon light-front spectator model based on soft-wall AdS/QCD-inspired wave functions, with free parameters constrained by global NNPDF fits, allowing us to compute both parton distribution functions and Wigner distributions. The Husimi distribution is obtained via Gaussian smearing of the Wigner distribution with width given by the saturation scale in the GBW model. We show that from a normalized Husimi distribution one can decompose the Wehrl entropy into an entanglement entropy term and a residual term associated with transverse degrees of freedom. Numerical results for the proton entanglement entropy are shown and compared with CMS data, while the Wehrl entropy is presented for different values of the virtuality.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the proton Wehrl entropy, constructed from a gluon Husimi distribution obtained via Gaussian smearing of the Wigner distribution with width set by the saturation scale, can be decomposed into an entanglement entropy term and a residual term associated with transverse degrees of freedom, but this decomposition relies on the specific choice of the smearing parameter and the normalization of the Husimi distribution.

## Method

The authors employ a gluon light-front spectator model based on soft-wall AdS/QCD-inspired wave functions, with free parameters constrained by fitting the unpolarized gluon PDF to NNPDF4.0nnlo data at NNLO for virtualities Q = 2, 5, and 10 GeV. The Wigner distribution is constructed from the model's GTMDs via Fourier transform, and the Husimi distribution is obtained by Gaussian smearing of the Wigner distribution with width given by the inverse saturation scale from the Golec-Biernat-Wusthoff (GBW) model. The Wehrl entropy is then computed from the normalized Husimi distribution and compared with the Kharzeev-Levin (KL) entanglement entropy.

## Key numbers

* The saturation scale parameters adopted are x0 = 4.2 x 10^-5, lambda = 0.248, and Q0^2 = 1 GeV^2, from the GBW model.
* The spectator mass is set to M_X = 0.985 GeV and the proton mass to M = 0.938 GeV.
* The AdS/QCD mass scale parameter kappa = 2.62 GeV is used, differing from earlier works where kappa = 0.4 GeV.
* Best-fit parameters for the gluon PDF at Q^2 = 4 GeV^2: a = 5.99 +/- 0.80, b = -0.47 +/- 0.02, N_g = 0.43 +/- 0.05.
* The analysis is performed for virtualities Q = 2, 5, and 10 GeV in the range 0.0001 < x < 1.

## Context

This work extends the Kharzeev-Levin (KL) model (Kharzeev and Levin, 2017), which relates entanglement entropy to gluon PDFs via S_EE = ln[xG(x)], by incorporating transverse phase-space structure through the Wehrl entropy. The approach builds on the light-front spectator model developed by Chakrabarti et al. (2023, 2025) and uses the Husimi distribution formalism introduced by Hagiwara et al. (2015) and Hatta et al. (2016) for QCD. The results enable future comparisons with experimental data from the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) and provide a framework for studying how semiclassical phase-space entropies behave in the small-x regime where gluon saturation becomes relevant.
