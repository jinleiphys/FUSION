# Effective Debye Screening Mass in an Anisotropic Quark Gluon Plasma

> Due to the rapid longitudinal expansion of the quark-gluon plasma created in heavy-ion collisions, large local-rest-frame momentum-space anisotropies are generated during the system's evolution. These momentum-space anisotropies complicate the modeling of heavy-quarkonium dynamics in the quark-gluon plasma due to the fact that the resulting inter-quark potentials are spatially anisotropic, requiring real-time solution of the 3D Schrödinger equation. Herein, we introduce a method for reducing anisotropic heavy-quark potentials to isotropic ones by introducing an effective screening mass that depends on the quantum numbers $l$ and $m$ of a given state. We demonstrate that, using the resulting effective Debye screening masses, one can solve a 1D Schrödinger equation and reproduce the full 3D results for the energies and binding energies of low-lying heavy-quarkonium bound states to relatively high accuracy. The resulting effective isotropic potential models could provide an efficient method for including momentum-anisotropy effects in open quantum system simulations of heavy-quarkonium dynamics in the quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The authors propose that anisotropic heavy-quark potentials in a quark-gluon plasma can be reduced to isotropic ones by introducing an effective Debye screening mass that depends on the quantum numbers \(l\) and \(m\) of a given bound state, allowing one to solve a 1D Schrödinger equation and reproduce full 3D results for low-lying heavy-quarkonium states to relatively high accuracy, provided one uses the "most similar state" concept where the isotropic potential is constructed with the effective screening mass matching the quantum numbers of the target state.

## Method

The authors define an angle-averaged effective screening mass \(\mathcal{M}_{lm}(\lambda,\xi)\) as the expectation value of the angle-dependent anisotropic screening mass \(\tilde{m}_D(\lambda,\xi,\theta)\) with respect to spherical harmonics \(Y_{lm}(\theta,\phi)\). They then expand the anisotropic heavy-quark potential (based on the Karsch-Mehr-Satz model) around an isotropic configuration where \(\tilde{m}_D = \mathcal{M}_{l'm'}\), and solve the resulting 1D radial Schrödinger equation using the leading-order isotropic potential \(V^{(0)}_{l'm'}(r)\). The method is tested by comparing the resulting eigen-energies and binding energies to exact 3D solutions for low-lying bottomonium states at various anisotropies.

## Key numbers

- For the \(\Upsilon\) state at \(\xi=1\) and \(\lambda=1.1T_c\), the 1D result using \(\mathcal{M}_{00}\) gives \(E_{00} = 115.31\ \text{MeV}\), compared to the exact 3D result \(E^{[0]} = 115.358\ \text{MeV}\); the first perturbative correction \(\Delta E_1\) vanishes by construction and \(\Delta E_2 = 0.013\ \text{MeV}\).
- For \(\chi_{b\pm1}\) at \(\xi=1\) and \(\lambda=1.1T_c\), using \(\mathcal{M}_{11}\) gives \(E_{01} = 506.39\ \text{MeV}\) with \(\Delta E_1 = 0\) and \(\Delta E_2 = -0.005\ \text{MeV}\), compared to the exact 3D result \(E^{[1]} = 506.318\ \text{MeV}\).
- For \(\chi_{b0}\) at \(\xi=1\) and \(\lambda=1.1T_c\), using \(\mathcal{M}_{10}\) gives \(E_{01} = 507.97\ \text{MeV}\) with \(\Delta E_1 = 0\) and \(\Delta E_2 = -0.006\ \text{MeV}\), compared to the exact 3D result \(E^{[2]} = 507.878\ \text{MeV}\).
- The ratio \(\mathcal{M}_{lm}/m_D\) at \(\xi=5\) ranges from \(0.5860\) (for \(l=1, m=0\)) to \(0.6642\) (for \(l=10, m=10\)); at \(\xi=10\) it ranges from \(0.5001\) (for \(l=1, m=0\)) to \(0.5527\) (for \(l=10, m=10\)).
- For small anisotropy, the effective screening mass is given by \(\mathcal{M}_{lm}(\lambda,\xi<1) = m_D(\lambda)[1 - (\xi/8)k(l,m)]\) with \(k(l,m) = [6l(l+1) - 2(m^2+2)]/[4l(l+1)-3]\).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of heavy-quarkonium dynamics in anisotropic plasmas by Dumitru, Guo, and Strickland (2009) and Strickland (2011), which established that anisotropic heavy-quark potentials can be parameterized with an angle-dependent screening mass but required solving the 3D Schrödinger equation. The method introduced here enables efficient inclusion of momentum-anisotropy effects in open quantum system simulations of heavy-quarkonium dynamics, such as those based on the Lindblad equation approach by Brambilla and collaborators (2017, 2020, 2021), which previously relied on an assumption of momentum-space isotropy. The effective screening mass approach also predicts polarization of states with non-zero angular momentum, such as \(\chi_{b0}\) and \(\chi_{b\pm1}\), in an anisotropic plasma.
