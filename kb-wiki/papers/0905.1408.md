# Pion-Nucleon Scattering in Kadyshevsky Formalism: II Baryon Exchange Sector

> In this paper, which is the second part in a series of two, we construct tree level baryon exchange and resonance amplitudes for $πN$ / $MB$-scattering in the framework of the Kadyshevsky formalism. We use this formalism to formally implement absolute pair suppression, where we make use of the method of Takahashi and Umezawa. The resulting amplitudes are Lorentz invariant and causal. We continue studying the frame dependence of the Kadyshevsky integral equation using the method of Gross and Jackiw. The invariant amplitudes, including those for meson exchange, are linked to the phase-shifts using the partial wave basis.

## Key claim

The paper constructs tree-level baryon exchange and resonance amplitudes for pion-nucleon scattering within the Kadyshevsky formalism, implementing absolute pair suppression formally for the first time in a covariant and frame-independent way, while maintaining Lorentz invariance and causality. The authors assume that pair suppression holds for SU(3) theories at low energy, but note that this assumption is questionable since N=3 may not be "really large."

## Method

The authors implement absolute pair suppression by splitting fermion fields into positive and negative energy components and imposing that transitions between them vanish in the interaction Lagrangian, using the Takahashi-Umezawa method. They derive interaction Hamiltonians for (pseudo) scalar coupling, (pseudo) vector coupling, and πNΔ₃₃ coupling, then compute S-matrix elements at tree level for baryon exchange and resonance diagrams. The invariant amplitudes are linked to phase-shifts using the partial wave basis in the helicity formalism.

## Key numbers

- The interaction Hamiltonian for (pseudo) scalar coupling contains terms proportional to Δ⁽¹⁾(x-y) which are of order O(g²), essential for obtaining covariant and n-independent S-matrix elements at order O(g²)
- For (pseudo) vector coupling, the coupling constant is f/m_π, where m_π is the pion mass
- The Δ₃₃ resonance is treated as a spin-3/2 field with mass M_Δ, using a gauge invariant interaction Lagrangian with coupling constant g_gi

## Context

This paper is the second part of a two-part series (paper I, Wagenaar and Rijken, 2009) that constructs a pion-nucleon scattering model in the Kadyshevsky formalism. It extends the meson exchange results from paper I into the baryon exchange sector, implementing pair suppression formally using the Takahashi-Umezawa method (Takahashi and Umezawa, 1953; Umezawa, 1956). The work builds on earlier approaches by Henk (year not specified) and VerWest (1976) who used three-dimensional integral equations with approximations, and continues the study of frame dependence using the method of Gross and Jackiw (1969). The formalism enables future development of a fully relativistic model of meson-baryon scattering with absolute pair suppression, which may be applicable to relativistic many-body theories.
