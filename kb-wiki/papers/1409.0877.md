# Halo nuclei 6He and 8He with the Coulomb-Sturmian basis

> The rapid Gaussian falloff of the oscillator functions at large radius makes them poorly suited for the description of the asymptotic properties of the nuclear wave function, a problem which becomes particularly acute for halo nuclei. We consider an alternative basis for ab initio no-core configuration interaction (NCCI) calculations, built from Coulomb-Sturmian radial functions, allowing for realistic exponential falloff. NCCI calculations are carried out for the neutron halo nuclei 6,8He, as well as the baseline case 4He, with the JISP16 nucleon-nucleon interaction. Estimates are made for the root-mean-square radii of the proton and matter distributions.

## Key claim

The Coulomb-Sturmian basis, with its exponential rather than Gaussian asymptotic falloff, provides a more realistic description of halo nuclei in no-core configuration interaction (NCCI) calculations, but the improvement in radius convergence for 6He and 8He is modest compared to the conventional harmonic oscillator basis, and the Coulomb-Sturmian basis actually yields slower energy convergence for the tightly-bound 4He case.

## Method

NCCI calculations were performed for 4He, 6He, and 8He using both the conventional harmonic oscillator basis and the Coulomb-Sturmian basis, with the JISP16 nucleon-nucleon interaction plus Coulomb interaction, without renormalization. The many-body basis was truncated according to the Nmax scheme (up to Nmax=16 for 4He and 6He, Nmax=14 for 8He), and the Coulomb-Sturmian interaction matrix elements were obtained by transforming from oscillator-basis two-body matrix elements using a change-of-basis transformation with cutoff Ncut. Proton-neutron asymmetric bases were also explored, with different length scales for proton and neutron radial basis functions set by a ratio parameter beta.

## Key numbers

- Two-neutron separation energies: 0.97 MeV for 6He and 2.14 MeV for 8He
- Experimental proton radius r_p for 4He: 1.462(6) fm (from electron scattering evaluation by Sick (2008))
- Experimental proton radius r_p for 6He: 1.934(9) fm (from isotope shift data by Wang et al. (2004) and mass measurements by Brodeur et al. (2013))
- Experimental proton radius r_p for 8He: 1.881(17) fm (from isotope shift data by Mueller et al. (2007))
- Experimental matter radius r_m ranges: 1.46-1.66 fm for 4He, 2.23-2.75 fm for 6He, and 2.38-2.61 fm for 8He
- Energy convergence for 4He reached at approximately 10 keV level by Nmax=14 in the harmonic oscillator basis
- The Coulomb-Sturmian basis for 4He lags behind the oscillator basis by about two steps in Nmax for energy convergence

## Context

This work extends the development of general radial basis functions for NCCI calculations by Caprio et al. (2012) to the specific case of halo nuclei, where the Gaussian asymptotics of the conventional harmonic oscillator basis are known to be problematic. The study addresses the challenge of computing long-range observables such as RMS radii, which are highly basis-dependent in NCCI calculations as noted by Bogner et al. (2008) and Cockrell et al. (2012). The results enable future development of more sophisticated extrapolation schemes for radii, such as those being developed by Coon et al. (2013) and Furnstahl et al. (2014), and suggest that proton-neutron asymmetric bases may be worth further exploration for halo systems.
