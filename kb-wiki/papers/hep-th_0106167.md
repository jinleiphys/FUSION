# Does DLCQ S-matrix have a covariant continuum limit?

> We develop a systematic DLCQ perturbation theory and show that DLCQ S-matrix does not have a covariant continuum limit for processes with $p^+=0$ exchange. This implies that the role of the zero mode is more subtle than ever considered in DLCQ and hence must be treated with great care also in non-perturbative approach. We also make a brief comment on DLCQ in string theory.

## Key claim
The DLCQ S-matrix does not have a covariant continuum limit for processes with $p^+=0$ exchange, specifically for forward scattering where there is no $p^+$ momentum exchange. This discrepancy arises because the continuum limit must be taken after all calculations are done with finite $L$, and the zero-mode loop contributions that would restore covariance are absent in DLCQ.

## Method
The authors developed a systematic DLCQ perturbation theory for a $d$-dimensional $\lambda \phi^4$ scalar field theory in a light-like box with periodic boundary conditions. They solved the zero-mode constraint perturbatively to obtain an effective interaction Hamiltonian, used Weyl ordering for equal light-cone time operators, and performed Dyson expansion to calculate scattering amplitudes. The continuum limit ($L \rightarrow \infty$) was examined by first performing momentum integrals with finite $L$, then taking the limit.

## Key numbers
- For $n_2 \neq n_3$ case (non-forward scattering), the DLCQ amplitude in the continuum limit equals the covariant result: $A = \frac{1}{2^d\pi^{\frac{d}{2}}} \int_0^{\infty}d\alpha\,\alpha^{\frac{2-d}{2}}\int_0^{1}d\gamma~ e^{-\alpha\{m^2 -\gamma(1-\gamma)(p_2-p_3)^2\}}$
- For $n_2 = n_3$ case (forward scattering) in $d=2$ dimensions, the DLCQ amplitude $A_F = 0$ in the continuum limit
- The covariant amplitude for the same forward scattering process is $A_F^{cov} = \frac{1}{4\pi m^2}$, which is non-zero and finite
- The continuum light-front amplitude $A_F^{LC} = \frac{1}{4\pi m^2}$, matching the covariant result

## Context
This work extends the long-standing discussion of DLCQ's Lorentz invariance, following earlier work by Maskawa and Yamawaki (1976) who showed that Poincaré invariance is not recovered as operator algebra in the continuum limit, and by Nakanishi and Yamawaki who showed Wightman functions disagree with Lorentz-invariant results. The paper corrects a claim by Harada, Muta, and Vary (1998) that DLCQ S-matrix has a covariant continuum limit in scalar field theory, showing that diagrams with $p^+=0$ exchange were missing in that analysis. The result implies that zero modes must be treated with great care in both perturbative and non-perturbative DLCQ calculations, though the discrepancy only affects a measure-zero region of phase space and does not affect total cross sections via the optical theorem.
