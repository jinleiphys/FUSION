# Old nuclear symmetries and large N(c) as long distance symmetries in the two nucleon system

> Wigner and Serber symmetries for the two-nucleon system provide unique examples of long distance symmetries in Nuclear Physics, i.e. symmetries of the meson exchange forces broken only at arbitrarily small distances. We analyze the large Nc picture as a key ingredient to understand these, so far accidental, symmetries from a more fundamental viewpoint. A set of sum rules for NN phase-shifts, NN potentials and coarse grained V_lowk NN potentials can be derived showing Wigner SU(4) and Serber symmetries not to be fully compatible everywhere. The symmetry breaking pattern found from the partial wave analysis data, high quality potentials in coordinate space at long distances and their V_lowk relatives is analyzed on the light of large N(c) contracted SU(4) symmetry. Our results suggest using large Nc potentials as long distance ones for the two-nucleon system where the meson exchange potential picture is justified and known to be consistent with large Nc counting rules. We also show that potentials based on chiral expansions do not embody the Wigner and Serber symmetries nor do they scale properly with Nc. We implement the One Boson Exchange potential realization saturated with their leading Nc contributions due to pi,sigma,rho and omega mesons. The short distance 1/r^3 singularities stemming from the tensor force can be handled by renormalization of the Schrodinger equation. A good description of deuteron properties and deuteron electromagnetic form factors in the impulse approximation for realistic values of the meson-nucleon couplings is achieved

## Key claim
Wigner SU(4) and Serber symmetries in the two-nucleon system are long-distance symmetries of the meson-exchange potentials that hold only for distances larger than about 1.5–2 fm, and their pattern of breaking is largely compatible with large \(N_c\) contracted SU(4) symmetry, but the symmetries are not fully compatible with each other everywhere.

## Method
The authors analyze NN phase shifts, coordinate-space NN potentials from high-quality partial-wave analyses, and coarse-grained \(V_{\text{low }k}\) potentials to test Wigner and Serber sum rules. They then construct one-boson-exchange (OBE) potentials saturated by the leading large \(N_c\) contributions from \(\pi\), \(\sigma\), \(\rho\), and \(\omega\) mesons, and renormalize the Schrödinger equation to handle the short-distance \(1/r^3\) singularities from the tensor force. Deuteron properties and electromagnetic form factors are computed in the impulse approximation without meson-exchange currents.

## Key numbers
- Scattering lengths: \(\alpha_{^1S_0} = -23.74\ \text{fm}\) and \(\alpha_{^3S_1} = 5.42\ \text{fm}\) (experimental values used to show that a symmetric potential does not imply a symmetric S-matrix).
- Wigner symmetry holds for the \(^1S_0\) and \(^3S_1\) potentials for \(r > 2\ \text{fm}\).
- Serber symmetry holds for spin-triplet P-wave potentials for \(r > 1.2\ \text{fm}\).
- Deuteron binding energy input: \(B_d = 2.224575(9)\ \text{MeV}\), corresponding to \(\gamma = \sqrt{2\mu_{np}B_d} = 0.231605\ \text{fm}^{-1}\).
- For the \(\pi\sigma\rho\omega\) OBE potential: deuteron asymptotic D/S ratio \(\eta = 0.02597\), quadrupole moment \(Q_d = 0.2819\ \text{fm}^2\), D-state probability \(P_D = 7.22\%\), and matter radius \(r_m = 1.9773\ \text{fm}\).
- For the \(\pi\sigma\rho\omega^*\) OBE potential: \(\eta = 0.02625\), \(Q_d = 0.2821\ \text{fm}^2\), \(P_D = 9.09\%\), \(r_m = 1.9659\ \text{fm}\).
- Experimental deuteron values: \(\eta = 0.0256(4)\), \(Q_d = 0.2859(3)\ \text{fm}^2\), \(r_m = 1.9754(9)\ \text{fm}\).
- The \(\sigma\) meson parameters fitted to the \(^1S_0\) phase shift: \(m_\sigma = 501\ \text{MeV}\) and \(g_{\sigma NN} = 9.1\).
- Meson masses used: \(m_\pi = 138.03\ \text{MeV}\), \(m_\rho = 770\ \text{MeV}\), \(m_\omega = 782\ \text{MeV}\); coupling \(g_{\pi NN} = 13.1083\).

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Wigner SU(4) symmetry (Wigner 1936) and Serber symmetry (Serber, circa 1947) as long-distance symmetries in the NN system, building on the authors’ previous sum-rule derivations (Calle Cordon and Ruiz Arriola 2008, 2009). It connects these empirical symmetries to the large \(N_c\) contracted SU(4) symmetry framework of Kaplan and collaborators (1995, 1996), showing that large \(N_c\) OBE potentials can reproduce deuteron properties without strong vertex functions. The results suggest that chiral perturbation theory potentials (Kaiser 1997, 1998) do not embody Wigner or Serber symmetries and do not scale properly with \(N_c\), motivating large \(N_c\) potentials as an alternative long-distance scheme for nuclear physics.
