# Theoretical investigation of two-particle two-hole effect on spin-isospin excitations through charge-exchange reactions

> Coherent one-particle one-hole (1p1h) excitations have given us effective insights into general nuclear excitations. However, the two-particle two-hole (2p2h) excitation beyond 1p1h is now recognized as critical for the proper description of experimental data of various nuclear responses. The spin-flip charge-exchange reactions $^{48}{\rm Ca}(p,n)^{48}{\rm Sc}$ are investigated to clarify the role of the 2p2h effect on their cross sections. The Fermi transition of $^{48}{\rm Ca}$ via the $(p,n)$ reaction is also investigated in order to demonstrate our framework. The transition density is calculated microscopically with the second Tamm-Dancoff approximation, and the distorted-wave Born approximation is employed to describe the reaction process. A phenomenological one-range Gaussian interaction is used to prepare the form factor. For the Fermi transition, our approach describes the experimental behavior of the cross section better than the Lane model, which is the conventional method. For spin-flip excitations including the GT transition, the 2p2h effect decreases the magnitude of the cross section and does not change the shape of the angular distribution. The $Δl=2$ transition of the present reaction is found to play a negligible role. The 2p2h effect will not change the angular-distributed cross section of spin-flip responses. This is because the transition density of the Gamow-Teller response, the leading contribution to the cross section, is not significantly varied by the 2p2h effect.

## Key claim

The two-particle two-hole (2p2h) effect decreases the magnitude of the spin-flip charge-exchange cross section for the Gamow-Teller (GT) transition but does not change the shape of the angular distribution; this is because the transition density of the GT response is not significantly varied by the 2p2h effect, even though the 2p2h configuration mixing broadens the GT strength distribution.

## Method

The authors calculated the transition density for the $^{48}{\rm Ca}(p,n)^{48}{\rm Sc}$ reaction using the second Tamm-Dancoff approximation (STDA) to include 2p2h configurations, with the ground state of $^{48}{\rm Ca}$ obtained from Skyrme-Hartree-Fock using the SGII effective interaction. The reaction process was described using the distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) with a phenomenological one-range Gaussian interaction for the form factor, and the distorting potentials were taken from phenomenological optical models. The model space for the STDA included single-particle orbits up to 100 MeV for the 1p1h configuration and seven specific orbits for the 2p2h configuration.

## Key numbers

* The TDA calculation gives the $0^+$ isobaric analogue state (IAS) of $^{48}{\rm Sc}$ at an excitation energy of 7.0 MeV, while the experimental excitation energy is 6.7 MeV.
* For the low-lying GT resonance, the cross section at $\theta=0^\circ$ calculated with the TDA is higher than that with the STDA by about 20%, and this difference remains almost the same for other angles.
* The $B({\rm GT})$ value for the low-lying resonance is 4.726 for the STDA and 5.681 for the TDA, a difference of approximately 20%.
* The amplitude of the transition density for $l=0$ at the peak around $r_{i_t} \sim 4$ fm is 0.156 for the STDA and 0.173 for the TDA, giving a ratio of about 0.902.
* For the low-lying GT resonance, the 2p2h amplitude $P_{\rm 2p2h}$ is 0.091 in the STDA; for the giant GT resonance, $P_{\rm 2p2h}$ is 0.470 in the STDA.
* The $\Delta l=2$ transition contribution to the cross section at $\theta=0^\circ$ is about five orders of magnitude smaller than that of the GT transition alone.

## Context

This work extends the investigation of 2p2h effects on the GT transition strength $B({\rm GT})$ in $^{48}{\rm Ca}$ performed by Minato (2016) using the fully self-consistent STDA, which showed that the STDA described experimental data better than the 1p1h Tamm-Dancoff approximation (TDA). The present study goes beyond that work by calculating the angular-distributed cross section directly comparable with experimental data, rather than just the strength distribution. The results confirm the assumption made by Yako (2009) that the $\Delta l=2$ transition plays a negligible role in the $^{48}{\rm Ca}(p,n)^{48}{\rm Sc}$ reaction, which was previously confirmed only for the $^{13}{\rm C}(p,n)^{13}{\rm N}$ reaction by Taddeucci (1987). The framework enables future microscopic understanding of how higher-order configurations are involved in charge-exchange cross sections.
