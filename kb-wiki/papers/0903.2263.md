# Shell-Model Monte Carlo Simulations of BCS-BEC Crossover in Few-Fermion Systems

> We study a trapped system of fermions with a zero-range two-body interaction using the shell-model Monte Carlo method, providing {\em ab initio} results for the low particle number limit where mean-field theory is not applicable. We present results for the $N$-body energies as function of interaction strength, particle number, and temperature. The subtle question of renormalization in a finite model space is addressed and the convergence of our method and its applicability across the BCS-BEC crossover is discussed. Our findings indicate that very good quantitative results can be obtained on the BCS side, whereas at unitarity and in the BEC regime the convergence is less clear. Comparison to N=2 analytics at zero and finite temperature, and to other calculations in the literature for $N>2$ show very good agreement.

## Key claim
The Shell-Model Monte Carlo (SMMC) method provides good quantitative results for few-fermion systems on the BCS side of the BCS-BEC crossover, but at unitarity and in the BEC regime the convergence is less clear and the deep BEC regime cannot be accessed.

## Method
The authors model a trapped two-component Fermi gas with a zero-range two-body interaction using the SMMC approach, which uses the Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to linearize the imaginary-time many-body propagator and expresses observables as path integrals of one-body propagators in fluctuating auxiliary fields. The interaction is regularized in a finite model space of harmonic oscillator states using a continuum renormalization scheme that relates the dimensionless coupling \(g\) to the s-wave scattering length \(a\) and the energy cut-off \(E_c = \alpha^2 \hbar\omega\). Energies are calculated as a function of interaction strength, particle number, and temperature for systems with \(N=2\) to \(21\) particles in model spaces up to \(N_{max}=4\) major shells.

## Key numbers
- For \(N=2\) at \(a/b=11\) (BEC side), the SMMC energy is \(1.72(3)\hbar\omega\) at \(k_B T = 1/5\hbar\omega\), compared to the exact pseudopotential result of \(1.92\hbar\omega\).
- For \(N=2\) at \(a/b=-1.0\) (BCS side), the SMMC energy is \(2.49(3)\hbar\omega\) at \(k_B T = 1/5\hbar\omega\), matching the exact pseudopotential result of \(2.49\hbar\omega\).
- For \(N=3\) at unitarity, the exact energy is \(4.27\hbar\omega\); the SMMC results at \(a/b=11\) and \(a/b=-1.0\) lie slightly below and above this value, respectively.
- The coupling \(g=10\) at \(N_{max}=3\) corresponds to \(a=11b\) in the continuum scheme and \(a=7.8b\) in the pseudopotential scheme; the coupling \(g=5.35\) at \(N_{max}=3\) corresponds to \(a=-1.0b\) (continuum) and \(a=-0.9b\) (pseudopotential).
- Energies are converged at inverse temperature \(\beta \geq 5/\hbar\omega\) for all particle numbers and model spaces on the BCS side.

## Context
This work extends the SMMC method, previously used in nuclear physics for finite-temperature properties of nuclei (Johnson 1992, Koonin 1997), to ultracold atomic gas physics for the first time. It provides ab initio results for few-fermion systems where mean-field theory is not applicable, complementing prior Green's function Monte Carlo studies of trapped systems (Bertsch 2007), no-core shell-model calculations (Stetcu 2007), and traditional shell-model approaches (Alhassid 2007). The results enable future studies of deformation properties, higher angular momentum pairing, and finite-temperature quantities such as specific heat and pairing phase transitions in small Fermi systems.
