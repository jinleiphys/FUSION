# FLIC Fermions and Hadron Phenomenology

> A pedagogical overview of the formulation of the Fat Link Irrelevant Clover (FLIC) fermion action and its associated phenomenology is described. The scaling analysis indicates FLIC fermions provide a new form of nonperturbative O(a) improvement where near-continuum results are obtained at finite lattice spacing. Spin-1/2 and spin-3/2, even and odd parity baryon resonances are investigated in quenched QCD, where the nature of the Roper resonance and Lambda(1405) are of particular interest. FLIC fermions allow efficient access to the light quark-mass regime, where evidence of chiral nonanalytic behavior in the Delta-baryon mass is observed.

## Key claim

FLIC fermions provide a new form of nonperturbative O(a) improvement where near-continuum results are obtained at finite lattice spacing, but the scaling analysis is performed in quenched QCD and the claim of nonperturbative improvement is based on the observation that tree-level or mean-field improved coefficients are sufficient to remove O(a) errors to all orders in the strong coupling when smoothed links are used for the irrelevant operators.

## Method

The Fat Link Irrelevant Clover (FLIC) fermion action was constructed by building the irrelevant Wilson and clover terms with APE-smeared ("fat") links while keeping the relevant operators on the original Monte Carlo-generated links. Scaling was analyzed on 12^3 x 24 and 16^3 x 32 lattices at three lattice spacings (0.093, 0.122, and 0.165 fm) using the tree-level O(a^2)-Symanzik-improved gauge action, with 200 configurations per spacing. Baryon resonances were studied on 392 configurations of size 16^3 x 32 at a = 0.122(2) fm using FLIC fermions with 4 sweeps of APE smearing at alpha = 0.7, and light quark simulations were performed on a 20^3 x 40 lattice with a = 0.134 fm using 94 configurations.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacings used in scaling analysis: 0.093, 0.122, and 0.165 fm, determined from a string tension analysis with sqrt(sigma) = 440 MeV
- 200 configurations used at each lattice spacing and volume for scaling analysis; 94 configurations on the 20^3 x 40 lattice for light quark simulations
- Mass splitting of approximately 400 MeV between the nucleon and the lowest N*(1/2-) state, clearly visible for all actions including Wilson
- Excitation energy of the N' (positive parity state from the alternate interpolator) is some 1 GeV above the nucleon
- Mass of the Delta(3/2-) lies some 500 MeV above its parity partner, the Delta(3/2+) ground state
- Light quark simulations reached m_pi / m_rho = 0.35 on a 20^3 x 40 lattice with physical length 2.7 fm
- Lattice spacing for the baryon resonance simulations: a = 0.122(2) fm at beta = 4.60

## Context

This work extends the development of improved lattice fermion actions by introducing the FLIC action, which uses APE-smeared links (Ape 1987) for irrelevant operators to achieve nonperturbative O(a) improvement without the exceptional configuration problems that plague standard clover actions (DeGrand 1999, Bardeen 1998). The baryon resonance results are compared with earlier Wilson fermion studies (NSTAR), domain wall fermion simulations (DWF), and NP-improved clover action results (Richards). The observation of chiral nonanalytic behavior in the Delta mass confirms predictions from quenched chiral perturbation theory by Young et al. (Ross) and enables future unquenched simulations and studies of electromagnetic form factors at light quark masses.
