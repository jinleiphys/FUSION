# Charmonium Production at High $p_t$ at RHIC

> We calculate transverse-momentum ($p_t$) spectra and elliptic flow of $J/ψ$'s in $Au$-$Au$ and $Cu$-$Cu$ collisions at RHIC. We employ an earlier constructed 2-component approach for direct and regenerated charmonia which is fairly consistent with the centrality dependence of inclusive $J/ψ$ yields at SPS and RHIC. Discrepancies with high-$p_t$ data in $Cu$-$Cu$ collisions are addressed by implementing effects of a finite formation time and an estimate of $B$ meson feeddown, which largely resolve the problem. The sensitivity of the spectra to the Cronin effect and different in-medium dissociation rates is studied. The predicted elliptic flow in semicentral $Au$-$Au$ collisions is rather small, $\sim$2-3%.

## Key claim

The paper's two-component model for J/ψ production (direct + regeneration) can describe high-pT spectra in Cu-Cu collisions at RHIC only after including finite formation time effects and B-meson feeddown, which largely resolve a significant discrepancy that existed in the baseline calculation.

## Method

The authors extend a previously constructed two-component approach for charmonium production in heavy-ion collisions, decomposing J/ψ yields into direct production (based on initial N-N spectra with Cronin broadening, followed by Boltzmann transport through QGP and hadron gas with quasifree dissociation) and regeneration (via c-cbar coalescence at hadronization using a thermal blastwave distribution). For high-pT production, they implement finite formation time effects by parameterizing the pre-hadronic dissociation rate as proportional to τ/τ_f^lab, and estimate B-meson feeddown contributions using Tevatron data with ±50% uncertainty. They also compare quasifree dissociation with gluo-dissociation mechanisms.

## Key numbers

- Predicted J/ψ elliptic flow v2 in semicentral Au-Au collisions is small, approximately 2-3% over the entire pT range
- Input charm cross section used: σ_ccbar = 570 μb, consistent with PHENIX data
- Formation times in rest frame: τ_f = 0.89 fm/c for J/ψ, 2.01 fm/c for χ_c, 1.50 fm/c for ψ'
- Cronin broadening parameter a_gN tested at 0.1 GeV^2/fm (baseline) and 0.4 GeV^2/fm (alternative)
- Regeneration contribution becomes significant for pT ≤ 2-3 GeV in semi-/central Au-Au collisions at RHIC
- Discrepancy in Cu-Cu collisions develops above pT ≈ 4 GeV

## Context

This work extends the two-component model of Grandchamp (2001) and Grandchamp (2003) for charmonium production, which had previously described centrality dependence of inclusive J/ψ yields at SPS and RHIC. The paper addresses discrepancies with high-pT data from PHENIX and STAR in Cu-Cu collisions, and provides predictions for J/ψ elliptic flow that could discriminate between suppression and regeneration mechanisms in future measurements. The results enable more precise constraints on the Cronin effect at RHIC energies and on the role of B-meson feeddown in heavy-ion collisions.
