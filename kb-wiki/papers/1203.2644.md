# Deviation from quark-number scaling of the anisotropy parameter v_2 of pions, kaons, and protons in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV

> Measurements of the anisotropy parameter v_2 of identified hadrons (pions, kaons, and protons) as a function of centrality, transverse momentum p_T, and transverse kinetic energy KE_T at midrapidity (|η|<0.35) in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV are presented. Pions and protons are identified up to p_T = 6 GeV/c, and kaons up to p_T = 4 GeV/c, by combining information from time-of-flight and aerogel Cherenkov detectors in the PHENIX Experiment. The scaling of v_2 with the number of valence quarks (n_q) has been studied in different centrality bins as a function of transverse momentum and transverse kinetic energy. A deviation from previously observed quark-number scaling is observed at large values of KE_T/n_q in noncentral Au+Au collisions (20--60%), but this scaling remains valid in central collisions (0--10%).

## Key claim

The previously observed scaling of the elliptic flow parameter v_2 with the number of valence quarks (n_q) holds for central Au+Au collisions (0-10%) but shows a clear deviation at large values of transverse kinetic energy per valence quark (KE_T/n_q) in noncentral collisions (20-60%), contrary to the simpler statement that n_q scaling is universally observed.

## Method

The PHENIX experiment measured v_2 for identified pions, kaons, and protons at midrapidity (|eta|<0.35) in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV using the event-plane method with the reaction-plane detector (RxNP) and muon piston calorimeter (MPC). Particle identification was achieved by combining time-of-flight (TOFw) and aerogel Cherenkov (ACC) detectors, with pions and protons identified up to p_T = 6 GeV/c and kaons up to p_T = 4 GeV/c. The v_2 values were analyzed as functions of centrality, transverse momentum p_T, and transverse kinetic energy KE_T, and the n_q scaling was tested by comparing v_2/n_q versus KE_T/n_q across particle species.

## Key numbers

- Pions and protons identified up to p_T = 6 GeV/c, kaons up to p_T = 4 GeV/c
- Event-plane resolution up to 73% achieved for midcentral events using the RxNP
- Momentum resolution estimated as delta p/p approximately 1.3% plus 1.2% times p (GeV/c)
- BBC timing resolution of sigma_BBC = 37 ps used for time-of-flight start time
- n_q scaling remains valid in central collisions (0-10%) but deviates at large KE_T/n_q in noncentral collisions (20-60%)

## Context

This work extends earlier PHENIX and STAR measurements of identified hadron v_2 (Adler 2003, Adams 2003, Adare 2006, Afanasiev 2007, Abelev 2007, Abelev 2008) to higher p_T and with greater statistical precision, enabling a systematic test of the centrality dependence of n_q scaling. The observed deviation at large KE_T/n_q in noncentral collisions provides constraints on recombination models (Hwa 2002, Fries 2003, Greco 2003, Molnar 2003) and on calculations of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio, and informs the interpretation of the low-energy RHIC scan program where n_q scaling violation has been proposed as a signature of the transition between sQGP formation and hadronic matter.
