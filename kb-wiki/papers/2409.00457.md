# Self-consistent constraints on the collision terms in quantum kinetic theory

> We derive self-consistent constraint conditions for collision terms in quantum kinetic theory using the Wigner function formalism. We present specific solutions for these collision terms that align with the constraints. we develop quantum kinetic theory at relaxation-time approximation. With this framework, we discuss spin polarization and electric charge separation effects in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The collision terms in quantum kinetic theory must satisfy a set of self-consistent constraint conditions derived solely from the consistency of the Wigner equations themselves, beyond the usual requirements of detailed balance and charge or energy-momentum conservation. These constraints are presented for the zeroth-order collision functions in a semiclassical expansion up to first order in \(\hbar\).

## Method

The authors use the Wigner function formalism for quantum electrodynamics and perform a semiclassical \(\hbar\) expansion of the Wigner equations up to first order. They decompose the Wigner function and collision functions into chiral components and introduce a time-like 4-vector \(n_\mu\) to separate time-like and space-like parts. By requiring that the Wigner equations be self-consistent at each order in \(\hbar\) and that the resulting constraints hold for any choice of \(n_\mu\), they derive a set of five independent constraint equations for the zeroth-order collision functions.

## Key numbers

* The semiclassical expansion is performed up to first order in \(\hbar\), with the collision functions expanded as \(\mathscr{C}^\mu_{ab} = \sum_{k=0}^\infty \hbar^k \mathscr{C}^{(k)\mu}_{ab}\) and \(\Delta\mathscr{C}^\mu_{ab} = \sum_{k=0}^\infty \hbar^{k+1}\Delta \mathscr{C}^{(k)\mu}_{ab}\).
* The on-shell condition for the zeroth-order independent Wigner functions is given by \((p^2 - m^2) \mathscr{J}_{s,n}^{(0)} / p_n = 0\) and \((p^2 - m^2) \mathscr{M}_{\mu}^{(0)} / p_n = 0\), leading to the form \(\mathscr{J}_{s,n}^{(0)} = p_n \mathcal{J}_{s,n}^{(0)} \delta(p^2 - m^2)\).
* The constraint \(p^\mu \mathscr{C}_{5,\mu}^{(0)} = 0\) is derived as Eq. (collision-0-f).
* The constraint \(m \sum_s \mathscr{C}^{(0)\mu}_{s,\mu} = p^\mu \mathscr{C}^{(0)}_{\mu}\) is derived as Eq. (collision-1-f).
* The constraint \(2m p_\mu \sum_s s \mathscr{C}^{(0)\nu}_{s,\nu} = [p_\mu p^\lambda \epsilon_{\lambda\nu\alpha\beta} - (p^2 - m^2) \epsilon_{\mu\nu\alpha\beta}] \mathscr{C}^{(0)\nu\alpha\beta}\) is derived as Eq. (collision-2-f).

## Context

This work extends the generalized chiral kinetic theory (GCKT) framework developed in Ma (2022) by incorporating collision terms beyond the mean-field approximation. It builds on the foundational quantum kinetic theory derivations by Heinz (1983, 1985), Elze (1986), and others, as well as the chiral and spin effect research initiated by Liang (2004), Kharzeev (2007), and Gao (2007). The derived self-consistent constraints enable the construction of collision terms in relaxation-time approximation that can be applied to study spin polarization and electric charge separation in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
