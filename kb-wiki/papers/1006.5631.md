# Novel Nuclear Structure Aspects of the Neutrinoless Double Beta Decay

> We explore the influence of the deformation on the nuclear matrix elements of the neutrinoless double beta decay (NME), concluding that the difference in deformation -or more generally in the amount of quadrupole correlations- between parent and grand daughter nuclei quenches strongly the decay. We correlate these differences with the seniority structure of the nuclear wave functions. In this context, we examine the present discrepancies between the NME's obtained in the framework of the Interacting Shell Model and the Quasiparticle RPA. We surmise that part of the discrepancy can be due to the limitations of the spherical QRPA in treating nuclei which have strong quadrupole correlations. We surmise that the NME's in a basis of generalized seniority are approximately model independent, i. e. they are "universal".

## Key claim

The difference in deformation (or more generally, the amount of quadrupole correlations) between parent and grand-daughter nuclei strongly quenches the neutrinoless double beta decay nuclear matrix element (NME), and this seniority mismatch between initial and final wave functions explains most of the discrepancy between Interacting Shell Model (ISM) and Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) calculations.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations in the r3g valence space with the gcn28:50 effective interaction for the mirror transition A=66 (Ge to Se), artificially increasing the deformation of the final nucleus by adding a term λ Q·Q to the effective interaction. They analyzed the seniority structure of the wave functions for both ISM and QRPA approaches, and decomposed the NMEs into a generalized seniority basis to compare the contributions from different seniority components.

## Key numbers

- For the A=66 mirror decay with Δβ=0 (identical wave functions), the NME M'^0ν = 5.16; with Δβ=0.08, the NME drops to 2.12, a reduction by more than a factor of two.
- For the A=66 mirror decay with Δβ=0, the seniority distribution shows 39% s=0, 43% s=4, 7% s=6, 10% s=8, 1% s=10; with Δβ=0.08, the distribution shifts to 6% s=0, 32% s=4, 21% s=6, 31% s=8, 10% s=10.
- For the A=48 Ca decay, the GT NME from the KB3 interaction is 0.75, a factor of five reduction from the seniority-zero limit where M_GT would be approximately 4.
- When QRPA seniority amplitudes are inserted into the generalized seniority formula, the resulting NMEs are approximately 5.73 for A=76 and 4.15 for A=82, matching the QRPA results.

## Context

This work extends earlier findings that the 2νββ decay is hindered by deformation differences between initial and final nuclei (Simkovic 2004, Alvarez-Rodriguez 2004), and that pairing favors the 0νββ decay while truncations in seniority overestimate NMEs (Caurier 2007). The paper directly addresses the long-standing discrepancy between ISM and QRPA calculations of 0νββ NMEs, showing that the QRPA underestimates the seniority mismatch between parent and grand-daughter nuclei, particularly for decays with strong quadrupole correlations such as 76Ge, 82Se, 100Mo, 128Te, and 130Te. The finding that NMEs in a generalized seniority basis are approximately "universal" suggests that future work should focus on the seniority structure of nuclear wave functions produced by different theoretical approaches.
