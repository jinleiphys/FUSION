# Spin dependence of η meson production in proton-proton collisions close to threshold

> Taking advantage of the high acceptance and axial symmetry of the WASA-at-COSY detector, and the high degree of the polarized proton beam of COSY, the reaction pp{\to} ppη has been measured close to threshold to explore the analyzing power Ay. The angular distribution of Ay is determined with the precision improved by more than one order of magnitude with respect to previous results allowing a first accurate comparison with theoretical predictions. The determined analyzing power is consistent with zero for an excess energy of Q = 15 MeV signaling s wave production with no evidence for higher partial waves. At Q = 72 MeV the data reveals strong interference of P s and P p partial waves and cancellation of (P p) and Ss*Sd contributions. These results rule out the presently available theoretical predictions for the production mechanism of the η meson.

## Key claim

The analyzing power for the $\vec{p}p\to pp\eta$ reaction is measured with precision improved by more than one order of magnitude compared to previous results, and the data rule out all presently available theoretical predictions for the production mechanism of the $\eta$ meson. At an excess energy of $Q = 15$ MeV the analyzing power is consistent with zero, signaling pure s-wave production with no evidence for higher partial waves, while at $Q = 72$ MeV the data reveal strong interference of Ps and Pp partial waves and cancellation of (Pp)$^2$ and Ss$^*$Sd contributions.

## Method

The WASA-at-COSY detector at the COSY synchrotron was used to measure the $\vec{p}p\to pp\eta$ reaction with a transversely polarized proton beam incident on an unpolarized hydrogen pellet target. The $\eta$ meson was reconstructed via its $\eta\to\gamma\gamma$ and $\eta\to 3\pi^0\to 6\gamma$ decay channels using missing-mass and invariant-mass techniques, with more than 400000 $\eta$ events identified. The analyzing power $A_y(\theta_\eta)$ was extracted from the azimuthal asymmetry of the $\eta$ production yield as a function of the polar angle $\theta_\eta$ in the center-of-mass system, with the beam polarization determined from elastic proton-proton scattering.

## Key numbers

- Beam momenta of 2026 MeV/c and 2188 MeV/c, corresponding to excess energies $Q = 15$ MeV and $Q = 72$ MeV, respectively.
- Beam polarization values: at 2026 MeV/c, $P = 0.793 \pm 0.010$ (spin-up) and $P = -0.577 \pm 0.007$ (spin-down); at 2188 MeV/c, $P = 0.537 \pm 0.009$ (spin-up) and $P = -0.635 \pm 0.011$ (spin-down).
- At $Q = 15$ MeV, the analyzing power $A_y$ is consistent with zero across all $\theta_\eta$ bins, with values ranging from $-0.016 \pm 0.016 \pm 0.022$ to $0.056 \pm 0.038 \pm 0.011$.
- At $Q = 72$ MeV, $A_y$ shows a clear angular dependence with values up to $0.133 \pm 0.021 \pm 0.017$ in the $36^\circ$--$54^\circ$ bin.
- Fit parameters at $Q = 15$ MeV: $C_1 = (0.001 \pm 0.001)~\mu b/sr$, $C_2 = (-0.002 \pm 0.003)~\mu b/sr$.
- Fit parameters at $Q = 72$ MeV: $C_1 = (0.104 \pm 0.006)~\mu b/sr$, $C_2 = (0.020 \pm 0.012)~\mu b/sr$.

## Context

This work extends previous measurements of $\eta$ meson production in proton-proton collisions by the COSY-11 collaboration (Czyzykiewicz 2006) and the CELSIUS/WASA collaboration (Petren 2010), improving the precision of the analyzing power by more than an order of magnitude. The results contradict predictions from single-meson-exchange models, including the pseudoscalar-exchange model of Nakayama (2003) and the vector-exchange model of Faldt (2001), which predicted $|A_y| \approx 0.18$ at $Q = 15$ MeV. The data provide new constraints for future theoretical models of the $\eta$ production mechanism, particularly regarding the role of isovector meson exchanges and the interference patterns of partial waves.
