# The Pion-Nucleon coupling constant from np charge exchange scattering

> A novel extrapolation method has been used to deduce the charged Pion-Nucleon coupling constant from backward $np$ differential scattering cross sections. We applied it to new measurements performed at 162 MeV at the The Svedberg Laboratory in Uppsala. In the angular range $150^\circ-180^\circ$, the carefully normalized data are steeper than those of most previous measurements. The extracted value, $g^2_{π^\pm} = 14.52 \pm 0.26$, in good agreement with the classical value, is higher than those determined in recent nucleon-nucleon partial-wave analyses.

## Key claim

The charged pion-nucleon coupling constant extracted from new 162 MeV np charge exchange data using the Difference Method is \(g^2_{\pi^\pm} = 14.52 \pm 0.26\), which is higher than values from recent nucleon-nucleon partial-wave analyses (around 13.6–13.7) but consistent with the classical value; the result depends on the steepness of the backward angular distribution and the absolute normalization of the data.

## Method

The authors measured unpolarized np differential cross sections for charge exchange scattering at 162 MeV at The Svedberg Laboratory, covering the center-of-mass angular range \(72^\circ\)–\(180^\circ\). They applied the Difference Method, which extrapolates the Chew function (constructed from the differential cross section as a function of squared momentum transfer \(q^2\)) to the pion pole at \(x = q^2 + m_\pi^2 = 0\), using the difference between a model Chew function and the experimental one to reduce systematic errors. The data were normalized using the total np cross section, and the extrapolation was performed with polynomial fits over two momentum-transfer ranges (\(0 < q^2 < 4 m_\pi^2\) and \(0 < q^2 < 10.1 m_\pi^2\)) using three comparison models: the Nijmegen potential, the Nijmegen energy-dependent PWA NI93, and the Virginia SM95 energy-dependent PWA.

## Key numbers

- Extracted charged pion-nucleon coupling constant: \(g^2_{\pi^\pm} = 14.52 \pm 0.26\) from 162 MeV np charge exchange data.
- Statistical error from extrapolation: \(\sqrt{N} g^2 = 14.52 \pm 0.13\), with systematic error of about \(\pm 0.15\) and normalization uncertainty of \(\pm 0.17\).
- The measurement covered the angular range \(72^\circ\)–\(180^\circ\) in the center-of-mass system, with data in the reduced range \(0 < q^2 < 4 m_\pi^2\) (31 data points) and full range \(0 < q^2 < 10.1 m_\pi^2\) (54 data points).
- The absolute normalization uncertainty was about \(\pm 2\%\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Eri95) using new data that extend the backward measurement to \(72^\circ\), improving normalization. The result (\(g^2 = 14.52 \pm 0.26\)) is higher than values from the Nijmegen group (Stoks 1993) at \(g^2_{\pi^\pm} = 13.58 \pm 0.05\) and the Virginia Tech group (Arndt 1994) around 13.7, but agrees with the classical value from Koch and Pietarinen (1980) of \(14.28 \pm 0.18\). The paper enables a more precise test of the Goldberger-Treiman relation and constrains the quark mass ratio \(m_s/\hat m\) via the Dashen-Weinstein sum rule; a dedicated experiment at 200 MeV with a tagged neutron at IUCF is planned to confirm the steep backward shape.
