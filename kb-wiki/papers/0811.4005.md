# On the mechanisms of heavy-quarkonium hadroproduction

> We discuss the various mechanisms potentially at work in hadroproduction of heavy quarkonia in the light of computations of higher-order QCD corrections both in the Colour-Singlet (CS) and Colour-Octet (CO) channels and the inclusion of the contribution arising from the s-channel cut in the CS channel. We also discuss new observables meant to better discriminate between these different mechanisms.

## Key claim

Higher-order QCD corrections (NLO and partial NNLO) to colour-singlet channels bring predictions for heavy-quarkonium hadroproduction considerably closer to Tevatron and RHIC data, but the agreement is not yet complete for charmonia; the colour-octet mechanism from NRQCD faces a flagrant discrepancy with polarisation measurements, suggesting that the charmonium system may be too light for the quark-velocity expansion to be applicable.

## Method

The author reviews computations of NLO ($\alpha_S^4$) QCD corrections to colour-singlet and colour-octet channels for $J/\psi$, $\psi'$, and $\Upsilon$ hadroproduction, and evaluates partial NNLO ($\alpha_S^5$) contributions from ${\cal Q}+jjj$ production using the automated matrix-element generator MadOnia. The $s$-channel cut contribution to the colour-singlet channel is computed via a gauge-invariant parametrisation of a 4-point $c\bar{c}J/\psi g$ contact term, with an interpolating function $h$ chosen to satisfy crossing symmetry and correct large-$P_T$ scaling.

## Key numbers

- For $J/\psi$ at the Tevatron ($\sqrt{s}=1.96$ TeV), the LO $gg \to J/\psi g$ contribution is already an order of magnitude smaller than the NLO contribution at $P_T\simeq 10$ GeV.
- The ${\cal Q}+Q\bar{Q}$ channel becomes significant for $J/\psi$ at $P_T>20$ GeV; for $\Upsilon$ it remains negligible in the accessible $P_T$ range.
- For colour-octet channels at the Tevatron, NLO $K$ factors are about 1.2 for the $^1S_0^{[8]}$ state and 1.1 for the $^3S_1^{[8]}$ state; at the LHC they are both about 0.8.
- Colour-octet LDMEs fit to Tevatron data at LO are $\langle O^{J/\psi}(^3S_1^{[8]})\rangle \simeq 0.0012$ GeV$^3$ and $\langle O^{J/\psi}(^1S_0^{[8]})\rangle \simeq 0.0045$ GeV$^3$; NLO corrections would reduce these by at most 15%.
- For the $s$-channel cut contribution, parameters $a=4$ and $\kappa=4.5$ GeV reproduce CDF direct $J/\psi$ data up to $P_T\simeq 10$ GeV at $\sqrt{s}=1.8$ TeV.
- The fragmentation approximation for $\psi$ ($\Upsilon$) production in association with a heavy-quark pair requires $P_T \gtrsim 60$ GeV ($P_T \gtrsim 100$ GeV) for 10% accuracy.

## Context

This review extends the discussion of heavy-quarkonium production mechanisms following earlier reviews by Lansberg (2006), Brambilla et al. (2004), and Kramer (2001), and builds on NLO calculations by Campbell et al. (2007) and Artoisenet et al. (2007) for colour-singlet channels, and by Gong et al. (2008) for colour-octet channels. The $s$-channel cut work by Haberzettl et al. (2007) and Lansberg (2005) questions the on-shell assumption common to both the Colour-Singlet Model and NRQCD. The results enable future studies of associated production channels (e.g., $J/\psi + c\bar{c}$) as discriminators between mechanisms, and motivate further work on the real part of the $s$-channel cut amplitude and on photoproduction tests of the 4-point function parametrisation.
