# Characterization of an electrically cooled BEGe detector till E$_γ\sim7$ MeV

> An electrically cooled Broad Energy Germanium (BEGe) detector has been characterized in the energy range E$_γ$ $\sim$ 0.122 - 7 MeV by utilizing the $γ$- rays emitted by a short-lived resonance state in $^{15}$O populated through $^{14}$N(p,$γ$) reaction and standard radioactive source ($^{152}$Eu). The experimental results have been reproduced through simulations with GEANT4 code, including vendor specified detector geometry along with the detailed construction of the target holder flange, to delineate the effects of the holder at various energies and detector position. Later the efficiency with a bare point source has been simulated. It has been found that the electrically cooled BEGe detector is suitable for usage in the $γ$-ray spectroscopy as well as for the study of resonance phenomena in nuclear astrophysics.

# Characterization of an electrically cooled BEGe detector till E$_γ\sim7$ MeV

## Key claim

The electrically cooled BEGe detector is suitable for both γ-ray spectroscopy and nuclear astrophysics resonance studies up to 7 MeV, but only after accounting for the strong attenuation effects of the target holder flange and correcting for source position mismatch between off-beam calibration sources and in-beam resonance data through GEANT4 simulation.

## Method

The detector was characterized using γ-rays from a standard ¹⁵²Eu radioactive source (0.122–1.408 MeV) and from the 7.556 MeV resonance state in ¹⁵O populated via the ¹⁴N(p,γ) reaction at E_p^lab = 278 keV, with the detector placed at 0°, 50°, and 90° relative to the beamline. Experimental spectra were compared to GEANT4.10.00.p04 simulations that included the vendor-specified detector geometry, the detailed target holder flange, beam pipe, and clamp construction. After validating the simulation against experimental data, the absolute efficiency for a bare point source at 5 cm distance was simulated to eliminate the effects of the target holder.

## Key numbers

- Energy resolution (FWHM) at 1.408 keV from ¹⁵²Eu: 2.17 ± 0.02 keV
- Energy resolution (FWHM) at approximately 7 MeV from in-beam spectra: 4.72 ± 0.17 keV
- Absolute detection efficiency at 50° for the experimental setup at 6.7 MeV: 2.72e-04 ± 1.19e-05
- Absolute detection efficiency at 50° for the experimental setup at 7.5 MeV: 2.23e-04 ± 5.08e-05
- Simulated absolute efficiency for a bare point source at 5 cm distance at 7 MeV: 4.8e-04 ± 3.59e-05
- The double escape peak becomes more intense than the full energy peak and single escape peak above 5 MeV in this BEGe detector

## Context

This work extends previous characterizations of BEGe detectors, which had been studied from below 10 keV (bege1, 2010) up to 1.5 MeV (bege1a, 2011), by providing experimental data and validated simulations up to 7 MeV using a proton capture resonance reaction. The study enables the use of electrically cooled BEGe detectors for low-energy nuclear astrophysics experiments requiring high-energy γ-ray detection, particularly for light-element nucleosynthesis reactions with Q-values around 7 MeV or more, where previous work relied on extrapolation from lower-energy radioactive source data or on liquid-nitrogen-cooled HPGe detectors.
