# Stochastic network view on hadron production

> We demonstrate that hadron production viewed as formation of specific stochastic network can explain in natural way the power-law distributions of transverse mass spectra of pions found recently, which seem to substitute the expected Boltzmann statistical factor.

# Stochastic network view on hadron production

## Key claim
The power-law transverse mass spectra of pions observed at high $m_T$ can be explained not by any special equilibrium statistics, but by modeling hadronization as a preferential attachment network process where quarks (vertices) connect via gluons (links), and the observed $m_T$ reflects the number of links in that network. The derivation depends on the conjecture that $m_T \sim k^\alpha$, where $k$ is the number of links and $\alpha$ describes the type of diffusion in momentum space.

## Method
The authors model hadron production as a growing stochastic network: starting with $n_0$ quark-antiquark pairs (vertices), new pairs are added sequentially, each with $k_0$ possible connections to existing vertices. New links attach preferentially to quarks with already large numbers of links (proportional to their "excitation"), following the standard preferential attachment rule. The growth equation yields a power-law distribution of links $P(k) \sim k^{-\gamma}$, which is then transformed into a power-law distribution of transverse mass $P(m_T) \sim m_T^{-\beta}$ via the assumed scaling relation $m_T \sim k^\alpha$.

## Key numbers
- The power index for the link distribution is $\gamma = 5$ for the simplest case (eq. \ref{eq:links}), or $\gamma = 5 - 2/k_0$ for the general case (eq. \ref{eq:growth1}).
- For $\alpha = 1/2$ (random diffusion in momentum space), the resulting transverse mass power index $\beta = 9$ for the simplest case, and $\beta = 9 - 4/k_0$ for the general case, yielding values of $P = 7.66, 8.0, 8.5$ for $k_0 = 3, 4, 8$ respectively.
- The observed power index $P$ from experimental data (MGMG) is quoted as $P \sim 8-10$, depending on energy and particle type.
- The nonextensivity parameter $q$ is related to the power index by $q = 1 + 1/P$, so $\gamma = 5$ corresponds to $q = 1.25$.

## Context
This work extends the phenomenological observation by MGMG (2004) that transverse mass spectra of neutral mesons from $\eta$ to $\Upsilon$ follow a universal power law, and the authors' own prior work (Wilk and Wlodarczyk, 2002) interpreting this via Tsallis nonextensive statistics. The network approach provides a dynamical mechanism for the power law, connecting hadron production to the theory of complex free networks (Albert and Barabasi, 2002) and to self-organized criticality concepts (SOC). It enables future work to classify observed transverse mass spectra into three distinct regimes depending on the type of deterministic diffusion in momentum space (parameter $\alpha$), ranging from exponential to power-law forms.
