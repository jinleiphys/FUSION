# Strangeness neutral equation of state for QCD with a critical point

> We present a strangeness-neutral equation of state for QCD that exhibits critical behavior and matches lattice QCD results for the Taylor-expanded thermodynamic variables up to fourth-order in $μ_B/T$. It is compatible with the SMASH hadronic transport approach and has a range of temperatures and baryonic chemical potentials relevant for phase II of the Beam Energy Scan at RHIC. We provide an updated version of the software BES-EoS, which produces an equation of state for QCD that includes a critical point in the 3D Ising model universality class. This new version also includes isentropic trejectories and the critical contribution to the correlation length. Since heavy-ion collisions have zero global net-strangeness density and a fixed ratio of electric charge to baryon number, the BES-EoS is more suitable to describe this system. Comparison with the previous version of the EoS is thoroughly discussed.

## Key claim
The paper presents a strangeness-neutral equation of state for QCD that incorporates a critical point in the 3D Ising model universality class, matching lattice QCD results up to fourth order in $\mu_B/T$, but the authors emphasize that the chosen critical point location (T_c ~ 143.2 MeV, $\mu_{B,c}$ = 350 MeV) is only illustrative and does not constitute a prediction.

## Method
The authors start from the previously developed BEST collaboration equation of state (Parotto 2018) and impose strangeness neutrality ($\langle n_S \rangle = 0$) and a fixed electric charge to baryon number ratio ($\langle n_Q \rangle = 0.4 \langle n_B \rangle$) to match heavy-ion collision conditions. They use the 3D Ising model parametrization to map critical behavior onto the QCD phase diagram via a linear map, match Taylor expansion coefficients to lattice QCD results order by order, merge with the hadron resonance gas model using the SMASH hadronic list at low temperatures, and compute the critical contribution to the correlation length using Widom's scaling form.

## Key numbers
- Critical point location: T_c ~ 143.2 MeV, $\mu_{B,c}$ = 350 MeV (illustrative, not a prediction)
- Ising model critical exponents: $\beta = 0.326$, $\delta = 4.8$, $\nu = 0.63$
- Mapping parameters: $\alpha_1 = 3.85^\circ$, $\alpha_2 = 93.85^\circ$, $\omega = 1$, $\rho = 2$
- Transition line curvature parameter: $\kappa = -0.0149$ from Bellwied (2015)
- Merging overlap region for pressure: $\Delta T = 17$ MeV
- Merging temperature for lattice-to-HRG: T = 130 MeV
- SMASH hadronic list contains 400 particles, antiparticles and resonances
- Correlation length scaling function merging performed at $x = 5$

## Context
This work extends the equation of state framework developed by Parotto (2018) by imposing strangeness neutrality and electric charge conservation, making it more suitable for describing heavy-ion collisions at the Beam Energy Scan at RHIC. The updated BES-EoS software now includes isentropic trajectories and the critical contribution to the correlation length, enabling studies of critical scaling of transport coefficients as in Dore (2020) and Monnai (2016). The results show that strangeness neutrality pushes isentropic trajectories to larger $\mu_B$ for the same temperature compared to the $\mu_S = \mu_Q = 0$ case, which has implications for initial conditions in hydrodynamic simulations.
