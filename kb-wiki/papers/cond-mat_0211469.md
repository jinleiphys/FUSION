# Slow rotation of a superfluid trapped Fermi gas

> The moment of inertia, Theta, is one of the possible observables for the experimental determination whether a trapped Fermi system has reached the BCS transition or not. In this article we investigate in detail the temperature dependence of Theta below the critical temperature T_c. Special care is taken to account for the small size of the system, i.e., for the fact that the trapping frequency hbar omega is of the same order of magnitude as the gap Delta. It is shown that the usual transport approach, corresponding to the leading order of an expansion in powers of hbar, is not accurate in this case. It turns out that Theta does not change rapidly if T becomes smaller than T_c, but it rather decreases slowly. Qualitatively this behavior can be explained within the two-fluid model, which again corresponds to the leading order in hbar. Quantitatively we find deviations from the two-fluid model due to the small system size.

## Key claim

The moment of inertia of a trapped superfluid Fermi gas does not drop sharply below the critical temperature \(T_c\) but instead decreases slowly, and the usual transport approach (leading order in an expansion in powers of \(\hbar\)) is inaccurate when the trapping frequency \(\hbar\omega\) is of the same order as the gap \(\Delta\). Quantitatively, deviations from the two-fluid model arise due to the small system size.

## Method

The authors calculate the moment of inertia \(\Theta\) of a trapped superfluid Fermi gas by treating the rotation as a perturbation to the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations, linearizing them, and solving for the change in the density matrix \(\rho_1\) and pairing tensor \(\kappa_1\). They use a gauge transformation to eliminate the change in the pairing field, reducing the problem to a linear response calculation, and evaluate the Wigner transforms semiclassically while preserving long-time information from classical trajectories in a harmonic trap. The temperature dependence is included via quasiparticle occupation numbers \(f(E) = 1/[\exp(E/T)+1]\).

## Key numbers

- The trapping frequency \(\hbar\omega\) is of the same order of magnitude as the gap \(\Delta\), making the usual \(\hbar\to 0\) transport approach inaccurate.
- The moment of inertia \(\Theta\) does not change rapidly when \(T\) becomes smaller than \(T_c\), but rather decreases slowly.
- The function \(G(x)\) governing the response is given by \(G(x) = \frac{\arsinh(x)}{x\sqrt{1+x^2}} + \frac{\Delta}{x}\int_0^\infty \frac{d\xi}{\xi} \left( \frac{f(E_+)}{E_+} - \frac{f(E_-)}{E_-} \right)\), with \(E_\pm = \sqrt{(\xi \pm x\Delta)^2 + \Delta^2}\).
- The phase \(\phi(\rv)\) solving the gauge condition is \(\alpha = -\Omega \frac{\omega_+ \omega_- (G_+ + G_-)}{\omega_+^2 G_+ + \omega_-^2 G_-}\), where \(\omega_\pm = \omega_y \pm \omega_x\) and \(G_\pm = G(\hbar\omega_\pm/(2\Delta))\).

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature calculation of the moment of inertia for a trapped superfluid Fermi gas by Farine (2001) to non-zero temperatures below \(T_c\), and generalizes the approach of Migdal (1960) for superfluid nuclei. It shows that the two-fluid model (the \(\hbar\to 0\) limit) gives only a qualitative description, and that finite-size effects (where \(\hbar\omega \sim \Delta\)) cause quantitative deviations. The results enable experimental detection of the BCS transition via moment of inertia measurements, complementing proposals using the scissors mode (Farine, 2001; Zambelli, 1999) and expansion deformation (Menotti, 2002).
