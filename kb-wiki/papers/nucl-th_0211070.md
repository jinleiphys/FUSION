# To bind or not to bind: Lambda-Lambda hypernuclei and Xi hyperons

> Four-body Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations for Lambda-Lambda-p-n do not produce a bound state for Lambda-Lambda hydrogen 4, although suitably defined three-body Faddeev calculations for Lambda-Lambda-d produce a 1+ bound state for Lambda-Lambda interactions fitted to the Lambda-Lambda helium 6 emulsion event from KEK E373. This is opposite to the normal situation where a four-body Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculation yields stronger binding than that due to a suitably defined three-body Faddeev calculation. For stranger systems, Faddeev calculations using Lambda-Xi interactions which simulate the Nijmegen soft-core model NSC97 (which is close to reproducing the KEK event) suggest that Lambda-Xi helium 6 marks the onset of nuclear stability for Xi hyperons.

# To bind or not to bind: Lambda-Lambda hypernuclei and Xi hyperons

## Key claim

Four-body Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations for the $\Lambda\Lambda pn$ system do not produce a bound state for $^{~~4}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$H, even for $\Lambda\Lambda$ interactions considerably stronger than required to reproduce $B_{\Lambda\Lambda}(_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{~~6}$He), in contrast to three-body $\Lambda\Lambda d$ Faddeev calculations which do yield a $1^+$ bound state for the same system.

## Method

The author performed $s$-wave Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations for the four-body $\Lambda\Lambda pn$ system representing $^{~~4}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$H, using Gaussian $YN$ and $YY$ interaction potentials fitted to the Nijmegen soft-core model NSC97, with the $pn$ triplet interaction modeled similarly to the Malfliet-Tjon potential MT-III. For comparison, three-body $\Lambda\Lambda d$ Faddeev calculations were also performed, and for heavier systems, $\alpha\Lambda\Lambda$ and $\alpha\alpha\Lambda\Lambda$ cluster models were used. For $\Xi$ hyperon systems, $\alpha\Lambda\Xi$ three-body Faddeev calculations were carried out using $\Lambda\Xi$ interactions simulating model NSC97.

## Key numbers

- The $\Lambda\Lambda$ scattering length from the KEK E373 $_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{~~6}$He event is $a_{\Lambda\Lambda} \sim -0.8$ fm, compared to $a_{\Lambda N} \sim -2$ fm for the $\Lambda N$ interaction.
- The $\Lambda d$ potential used yields $B_{\Lambda}(_{\Lambda}^3{\rm H}({\frac{1}{2}}^+)) = 0.19$ MeV for model NSC97f; using NSC97e gives $B_{\Lambda}(_{\Lambda}^3{\rm H}) = 0.07$ MeV.
- For $^{~10}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$Be, $\Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda}(^{~10}_{\Lambda\Lambda}{\rm Be})$ is approximately 1.5 MeV in the limit $V_{\Lambda\Lambda} \rightarrow 0$.
- The mass difference $M(\Xi^-) - M(\Xi^0) = 6.5$ MeV determines the stability difference between $_{\Lambda\Xi}^{~~6}$H and $_{\Lambda\Xi}^{~~6}$He.

## Context

This work extends prior Faddeev calculations by Hiyama et al. (1997) for $\Lambda\Lambda$ hypernuclei and addresses the question of whether $^{~~4}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$H is bound, following the KEK E373 identification of $_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{~~6}$He reported by Takahashi (2001). The results contradict the normal situation observed in heavier systems such as $^{~10}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$Be, where four-body Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations yield stronger binding than three-body models. The calculations suggest that $_{\Lambda\Xi}^{~~6}$He may be the lightest particle-stable $S=-3$ hypernucleus, though this prediction depends on assumptions about the $\Xi\alpha$ interaction extrapolated from data on $^{12}$C by Fukuda (1998). More experimental work is needed to decide whether events from the AGS E906 experiment reported by Ahn (2001) correspond to $^{~~4}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$H.
