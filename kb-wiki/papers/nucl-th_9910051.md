# Nuclear Matter with Quark-Meson Coupling II: Modeling a Soliton Liquid

> We study in further detail a nontopological soliton model with coupling between quarks and mesons selected as promising in the previous work. Here we go beyond the Wigner-Seitz approximation by introducing the disorder necessary to reproduce the liquid state. We study nuclear matter, with particular interest in the transition to a quark-gluon plasma. The model studied is a variation of the chiral chromodielectric model of Fai, Perry and Wilets, where explicit coupling to a scalar meson field is introduced.

## Key claim

The chiral chromodielectric model, when modified by adding a scalar meson field and using the significant structure theory of Jhon and Eyring to introduce disorder into the Wigner-Seitz approximation, can produce a qualitatively reasonable equation of state for nuclear matter that interpolates between low-density gas-like and high-density solid-like behavior, but the saturation point occurs at too low a density and the compression modulus remains too high compared to empirical values.

## Method

The authors solve the Euler-Lagrange equations for a nontopological soliton model in the mean field approximation, where a scalar glueball field sigma and a scalar meson field phi couple to quarks, within a spherical Wigner-Seitz cell. They go beyond the simple Wigner-Seitz approximation by applying the significant structure theory of Jhon and Eyring (1969), which introduces holes into the system to model the disorder of a liquid state: the liquid is treated as a combination of solid-like nucleons (those next to filled sites with harmonic motion) and gas-like nucleons (those next to vacant sites with translational degrees of freedom), with the cell volume v treated as a free parameter.

## Key numbers

* For the parameter set with quark-meson coupling g_s = 2 and cell radius R = 0.4 fm: saturation Fermi momentum k_s = 1.06 fm^{-1}, binding energy E_s = 22 MeV, compression modulus K = 1082 MeV.
* For the parameter set with g_s = 3 and R = 0.7 fm: saturation Fermi momentum k_s = 0.95 fm^{-1}, binding energy E_s = 24 MeV, compression modulus K = 671 MeV.
* Empirical comparison values: k_s = 1.36 fm^{-1}, E_s = 16 MeV, K approximately 200 MeV.
* The bag constant B = 46.6 MeV/fm^3, glueball mass m_GB = 1.82 GeV, and vacuum sigma field sigma_v = 0.285 fm^{-1}.
* The transition to the solid state occurs at k_F = 3.8 fm^{-1} for g_s = 2 and at k_F = 2.2 fm^{-1} for g_s = 3, both beyond the transition to the quark-gluon plasma.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Barnea and Walhout, 1999, referred to as Paper I) which used the Wigner-Seitz approximation for dense solitonic matter, corresponding to the cell model of Lennard-Jones and Devonshire (1937). The significant structure theory of Jhon and Eyring (1969), previously applied to dense skyrmion matter by Walhout (1990), is used here to introduce disorder. The model is a variation of the chiral chromodielectric model of Fai, Perry and Wilets (1988). The results enable a consistent treatment of the transition from nuclear matter to a quark-gluon plasma within a single model, though the authors note that adding pions is an essential improvement for quantitative accuracy.
