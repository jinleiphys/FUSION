# Pionic decay of Lambda Hypernuclei in a Continuum Shell Model

> We evaluate pionic decay widths of Lambda hypernuclei using a shell model for, both the nuclear bound and the continuum nucleon wave functions in the final state, and distorted waves for the outgoing pion. An excellent agreement with the recent KEK measurement of $π^-$-decay widths of $^{12}_Λ$C and $^{28}_Λ$Si is found. Besides, results for $^{56}_Λ$Fe are consistent with the existing upper bound.

## Key claim

The authors evaluate pionic decay widths of Lambda hypernuclei using a Continuum Shell Model (CSM) for the nucleon wave functions and distorted waves for the outgoing pion, finding excellent agreement with recent KEK measurements for \(^{12}_{\Lambda}\)C and \(^{28}_{\Lambda}\)Si, though the computed widths are consistently slightly below the experimental data.

## Method

The pionic decay width is split into a discrete contribution (final nucleon in an unoccupied bound shell) and a continuum contribution (final nucleon in the positive-energy continuum), computed using a Woods-Saxon potential with spin-orbit forces fitted to reproduce experimental single-particle energies around the Fermi level. The pion wave functions are obtained from a Klein-Gordon equation with a microscopically developed optical potential that includes s-wave and p-wave terms, second-order absorption corrections, and Coulomb effects. The continuum contribution is treated by replacing the sum over bound orbitals with an integral over nucleon energies and a sum over multipoles, using continuum solutions of the nucleon Schrodinger equation with the same potential as for bound states.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{12}_{\Lambda}\)C \(\pi^{-}\) decay: total width \(\Gamma = 0.106\ \Gamma_{\Lambda}\) (FP potential) and \(0.110\ \Gamma_{\Lambda}\) (NQ potential), compared to experimental \(0.113 \pm 0.013 \pm 0.005\ \Gamma_{\Lambda}\)
- For \(^{28}_{\Lambda}\)Si \(\pi^{-}\) decay: total width \(\Gamma = 0.037\ \Gamma_{\Lambda}\) (FP) and \(0.039\ \Gamma_{\Lambda}\) (NQ), compared to experimental \(0.047 \pm 0.008 \pm 0.002\ \Gamma_{\Lambda}\)
- For \(^{56}_{\Lambda}\)Fe \(\pi^{-}\) decay: total width \(\Gamma = 0.014\ \Gamma_{\Lambda}\) (both FP and NQ), consistent with experimental upper bound \(< 0.015\ \Gamma_{\Lambda}\) (90% C.L.)
- For \(^{12}_{\Lambda}\)C \(\pi^{0}\) decay: total width \(\Gamma = 0.144\ \Gamma_{\Lambda}\) (FP) and \(0.158\ \Gamma_{\Lambda}\) (NQ), compared to older experimental \(0.217 \pm 0.084\ \Gamma_{\Lambda}\)
- The continuum contribution for \(\pi^{-}\) decay of \(^{28}_{\Lambda}\)Si is \(0.019\ \Gamma_{\Lambda}\), approximately 50% of the total measured width
- Free space \(\Lambda\) decay width uses coupling constants \((G m_{\pi}^{2})^{2}/8\pi = 1.945 \times 10^{-15}\), with \(S = 1.06\) and \(P = 0.527\)

## Context

This work extends the earlier Woods-Saxon shell model of Nieves and Oset (1992) by using more realistic nuclear potentials fitted to experimental single-particle energies and a proper Continuum Shell Model treatment of the positive-energy nucleon states, replacing the earlier infinite-barrier discretization method. The results provide a test of the pion-nucleus optical potential developed by Nieves, Oset, and Garcia-Recio (1993) at low energies, and the improved agreement with KEK measurements (Sato et al., 2001) demonstrates the importance of correct nuclear structure and continuum treatment, particularly for medium-mass hypernuclei where the continuum contribution can be as large as the discrete contribution.
