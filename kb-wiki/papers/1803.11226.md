# Semileptonic decay of $B^{-}_c$ into $X(3930)$, $X(3940)$, $X(4160)$

> We study the semileptonic decay of $B^{-}_c$ meson into $\barν l^-$ and the isospin zero $X(3930)~(2^{++})$, $X(3940)~(0^{++})$, $X(4160)~(2^{++})$ resonances. We look at the reaction from the perspective that these resonaces appear as dynamically generated from the vector-vector interaction in the charm sector, and couple strongly to $D^{*}\bar{D^{*}}$ and $D^{*}_{s}\bar{D_{s}^{*}}$. We also look into the $B^{-}_c \rightarrow \barν_{l} l^- D^{*}\bar{D^{*}}$ and $B^{-}_c \rightarrow \barν_{l} l^- D^{*}_{s}\bar{D_{s}^{*}}$ reactions close to threshold and relate the $D^{*}\bar{D^{*}}$ and $D^{*}_{s}\bar{D_{s}^{*}}$ mass distribution to the rate of production of the $X$ resonances.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the semileptonic decay $B^{-}_c \rightarrow \bar{\nu}_l l^- X_i$ (with $X_i$ being $X(3930)$, $X(3940)$, or $X(4160)$) can be used to test the molecular nature of these resonances, but only the absolute decay width for the $X(3940)$ ($0^{++}$) is computed directly; for the two tensor ($2^{++}$) resonances only the ratio of their production rates is given, not absolute values.

## Method

The authors treat the $X$ resonances as dynamically generated from vector-vector ($D^*\bar{D}^*$, $D^*_s\bar{D}^*_s$) interactions using the local hidden gauge approach of Molina (2009). The weak decay $B^-_c \rightarrow \bar{\nu}_l l^- c\bar{c}$ is followed by hadronization of the $c\bar{c}$ pair into $D^*\bar{D}^*$ and $D^*_s\bar{D}^*_s$ pairs, which then undergo final-state interaction to produce the resonances. For the $0^{++}$ $X(3940)$ the coalescence width is computed directly; for the $2^{++}$ states the ratio of widths is obtained by replacing the $P^{\rm cm}_R$ factor with $(P^{\rm cm}_R)^5$ in the phase-space integral.

## Key numbers

* Branching ratio for $B^-_c \rightarrow \bar{\nu}_l l^- X(3940)$: $\Gamma_{\rm coal}/\Gamma_{\rm tot}(B_c) = 2.0 \times 10^{-5}$, using $\Gamma_{\rm coal} = 2.6 \times 10^{-14}$ MeV and $B_c$ mean life $0.507 \times 10^{-12}$ s.
* Ratio of coalescence widths for the two tensor resonances: $\Gamma_{\rm coal}(X(3930)) / \Gamma_{\rm coal}(X(4160)) = 3.6$ (using the $(P^{\rm cm}_R)^5$ replacement, considered more realistic).
* Masses used: $X(3940)$ at $3943$ MeV, $X(3930)$ at $3922$ MeV, $X(4160)$ at $4169$ MeV.
* Cutoff for the two-meson loop function: $q_{\rm max} = 690$ MeV, following Wang (2017).
* Fermi coupling constant $G_{\rm F} = 1.166 \times 10^{-5}$ GeV$^{-2}$; CKM matrix element $V_{bc} = 0.0411$.

## Context

This work extends the molecular interpretation of hidden-charm $X$ states from Molina (2009), where $X(3940)$, $X(3930)$, and $X(4160)$ were dynamically generated from vector-vector interactions. It contrasts with the charmonium-radial-excitation picture of Wang and Zhang, who treat $X(3940)$ and $X(4160)$ as $\eta_c(3S)$ and $\eta_c(4S)$ respectively. The semileptonic decay approach follows earlier work by Navarra (2015) on $B_s$ and $B$ decays to scalar mesons, and by Ikeno (2015) on $\Lambda_c \rightarrow \Lambda(1405) e^+ \nu_e$. The predicted mass distributions in $D^*\bar{D}^*$ and $D^*_s\bar{D}^*_s$ differ significantly from phase space, providing experimental tests for future $B_c$ decay studies.
