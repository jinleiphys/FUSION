# The nimbus of away-side jets

> The conical structure around the away-side jets is discussed. The equations of in-medium gluodynamics are proposed. Their classical lowest order solution is explicitly shown for a color charge moving with constant speed. For nuclear permittivity larger than 1 it describes the shock wave induced by emission of Cherenkov gluons. The values of real and imaginary parts of nuclear permittivity are estimated from fits of RHIC data. Specific effects at LHC energies are described.

# The nimbus of away-side jets

## Key claim

The conical structure observed around away-side jets in high-energy central nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC can be explained as Cherenkov gluon emission by a parton traversing a quark-gluon medium, provided the nuclear permittivity is larger than 1; the classical lowest-order solution of the proposed in-medium gluodynamics equations coincides with Abelian electrodynamical results up to a trivial color factor.

## Method

The author modifies the Yang-Mills equations to include a nuclear permittivity ε, analogous to the dielectric constant in electrodynamics, by replacing the chromoelectric field E_a with ε E_a in the in-vacuum equations. The classical lowest-order solution is then derived for a color charge moving with constant speed, yielding a shock wave whose cone angle is given by the Cherenkov condition cos θ = 1/(v√ε). The real and imaginary parts of the nuclear permittivity are extracted by fitting the Tamm-Frank formula (with dispersion and absorption included) to STAR and PHENIX data on the away-side jet structure.

## Key numbers

- STAR experiment: θ_max = 1.04 rad, ε₁ = 3.95, ε₂ = 0.8
- PHENIX experiment: θ_max = 1.27 rad, ε₁ = 9.5, ε₂ = 1.8
- The ratio (ε₂/ε₁)² ≈ 0.04, indicating the imaginary part is comparatively small
- The density of scatterers ν is estimated at about 10–20 per hadron

## Context

This work extends the Cherenkov-gluon approach previously applied to SPS data (Dremin, 2005) and cosmic-ray observations (Apanasenko et al., 1979) to RHIC results from STAR and PHENIX. It proposes in-medium gluodynamics equations that had not been formulated earlier, filling a gap in the theoretical treatment of collective medium effects in QCD. The results enable specific predictions for LHC energies, including the possibility of observing Cherenkov gluon rings at polar angles of tens of degrees in the center-of-mass frame from forward-moving partons, as seen in some cosmic-ray events.
