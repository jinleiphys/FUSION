# Neutral pion decay into $ν\barν$ in dense skyrmion matter

> We study the weak decay of the neutral pion to a neutrino-antineutrino pair, $π^0\to ν\barν$, in the Skyrme model. In baryon free-space the process is forbidden by helicity while in a dense baryonic medium, the process becomes possible already to leading order in $G_F$ due to the break-down of Lorentz symmetry in the background medium.

# Neutral pion decay into νν̄ in dense skyrmion matter

## Key claim
The weak decay π⁰ → νν̄, which is forbidden in baryon free-space by helicity conservation for massless neutrinos, becomes possible in dense baryonic medium already to leading order in G_F due to the breakdown of Lorentz symmetry in the background medium. The nonvanishing amplitude arises from second-order interactions with the background potentials of dense skyrmion matter, with a strength determined by nonperturbative dynamics to be of order 10⁻³, significantly larger than the (m_ν/m_π)³ behavior for keV neutrinos.

## Method
The authors describe baryonic matter via the Skyrme model, where both pions and dense baryonic matter are described by a single effective chiral Lagrangian. Dense baryonic matter is approximated as a system of skyrmions in a crystal configuration, with pions incorporated as fluctuations on the classical field configuration describing dense matter. The weak vector bosons are incorporated by standard gauging of the Lagrangian, and the process is analyzed by perturbative expansion in the background spatial potentials up to second order, retaining only contributions that can be expressed as a local π⁰νν̄ vertex.

## Key numbers
- The quantity δ, which determines the decay strength, reaches |δ|² ∼ 4.6 × 10⁻⁴ at densities around twice nuclear matter density, representing a significant enhancement over the (m_ν/m_π)³ ∼ 10⁻⁹ suppression for keV neutrinos.
- The in-medium pion velocity v_π obtained in prior work (LPRV04) lies in the range 0.8 < v_π ≤ 1, leading to a larger 1 − v_π² than the |δ| obtained in the second-order calculation.
- For comparison, in the CFL phase studied by JPS02, the pion velocity squared is v_π² = 1/3.
- The branching ratio bound from the pion pole mechanism is R < 2.9 × 10⁻¹³ for π⁰ → νν̄, with an upper bound on neutrino mass of m_ν < 420 keV (LNg91).

## Context
This work extends the study of neutrino emission via the pion pole mechanism (FGRVSHK76, FGRVSHK77) to the hadronic phase using the Skyrme model. It contrasts with JPS02, which studied the same decay in the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase at extreme densities, and with VS86, which studied neutrino-pair radiation from nucleons in π⁰-condensed nucleon matter. The results are qualitative due to limitations of the Skyrme crystal model, but demonstrate that the pion pole mechanism for neutrino emission can already manifest in the lower-density hadronic phase, complementing the medium effects on the pion propagator studied by ANV03. The work enables future studies incorporating finite temperature effects for application to neutrino emissivity in compact stars.
