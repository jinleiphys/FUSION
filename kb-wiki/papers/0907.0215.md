# Nucleon-nucleon charge symmetry breaking and the dd -> alpha pi0 reaction

> We show that using parameters consistent with the charge symmetry violating difference between the strong nn and pp scattering lengths provides significant constraints on the amplitude for the dd -> alpha pi0 reaction.

## Key claim

Using coupling constants that are constrained by the measured charge-symmetry-breaking difference between the strong nn and pp scattering lengths reduces the over-prediction of the dd → απ⁰ cross section from factors of 15–30 to about a factor of 2 (for the AV18 potential), but significant model dependence between different nucleon-nucleon potentials remains.

## Method

The authors compute the dd → απ⁰ transition amplitude by combining chiral effective field theory pion-production operators with four-body wave functions from the CD-Bonn or Argonne V18 two-nucleon potentials plus the Tucson-Melbourne three-nucleon force. They then constrain the meson-nucleon coupling constants (particularly g_ω and g_η) by requiring that the resulting charge-symmetry-breaking contribution to the ¹S₀ NN scattering length difference Δa = a_pp − a_nn matches the experimental value of 1.5 ± 0.5 fm, rather than using the unconstrained values from earlier work.

## Key numbers

- Experimental Δa = a_pp − a_nn = 1.5 ± 0.5 fm (the charge-symmetry-breaking scattering length difference)
- Δa from ρ-ω mixing alone: Δa_ρω = 1.45 fm, using g_ω²(0)/4π = 5.0 and ⟨ρ⁰|H|ω⟩ = −4300 MeV²
- Δa from π-η mixing: Δa_πη = 0.33 fm, using the same coupling constants as in earlier work
- With g_η = 0 and g_ω²/4π = 5.0: σ_CDB = 111.5 pb and σ_AV18 = 34.5 pb at T_d = 228.5 MeV
- Experimental cross section: σ = 12.7 ± 2.2 pb at T_d = 228.5 MeV and 15.1 ± 3.1 pb at 231.8 MeV (Stephenson 2003)

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Nogga et al. (2007) that used four-body theory with realistic interactions but over-predicted the dd → απ⁰ cross section by factors of 15–30. The authors show that using coupling constants consistent with NN charge symmetry breaking (from the meson-exchange model of Machleidt and Muther 2001) substantially reduces the discrepancy, though significant potential dependence between AV18 and CD-Bonn remains. The result enables future work to focus on resolving the remaining factor-of-2 discrepancy and the model dependence, and on justifying the use of Z-graph resonance saturation in a way consistent with chiral power counting.
