# Effect of hadronic interaction on the flow of $K^{*0}$

> We explore the implications of the late stage hadronic rescattering phase on the flow of $K^{*0}$. The model calculations are done using a (3+1)-dimensional hybrid framework, incorporating both hydrodynamic evolution and hadronic transport that is calibrated to agree with bulk observables including the elusive rapidity differential $v_1$ of light-flavor hadrons. We find that the late stage hadronic rescattering phase causes significant qualitative modification of the $K^{*0}$ $v_1$ resulting in $\frac{dv_1}{dy}(K^{*0})-\frac{dv_1}{dy}(K^{+})$ and $\frac{dv_1}{dy}(ϕ)-\frac{dv_1}{dy}(K^{+})$ to have opposite signs with the effect being more pronounced in central collisions as compared to peripheral ones due to the larger multiplicity as well as longer duration of the hadronic phase. Further, this effect is enhanced in low-energy collisions owing to a stronger breaking of boost invariance. On the contrary, the influence of the hadronic phase on the $K^{*0}$ elliptic flow $v_2$ is found to be less significant and quantitative.

## Key claim

The late-stage hadronic rescattering phase causes a significant qualitative modification of the $K^{*0}$ directed flow $v_1$, changing its mid-rapidity slope sign from negative to positive, while the effect on the elliptic flow $v_2$ is less significant and only quantitative; this sign change is more pronounced in central collisions and at lower collision energies.

## Method

The authors used a (3+1)-dimensional hybrid framework combining MUSIC hydrodynamic evolution with the UrQMD hadronic transport afterburner, calibrated to reproduce bulk observables including rapidity differential $v_1$ of light-flavor hadrons. Initial conditions were generated from a Glauber-based tilted energy and baryon density profile, with particlization performed on a constant energy density hypersurface ($\epsilon_f = 0.26$ GeV/fm$^3$) using the iSS code. The directed flow of $K^{*0}$ and $\phi$ was compared between two scenarios: primordial hadrons with instantaneous resonance decay only, and hadrons that subsequently underwent full hadronic transport in UrQMD.

## Key numbers

- The mid-rapidity slope of $v_1(y)$ for $K^{*0}$ changes sign from negative (before hadronic interaction) to positive (after hadronic interaction) in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 27$ GeV for 10-40% centrality.
- The splitting $\frac{dv_1}{dy}(K^{*0})-\frac{dv_1}{dy}(K^{+})$ becomes positive after hadronic transport at all centralities, while $\frac{dv_1}{dy}(\phi)-\frac{dv_1}{dy}(K^{+})$ remains negative at approximately $-0.005$ across collision energies.
- The $v_1$ modification for $K^{*0}$ is most pronounced at low $p_T$ (below $p_T < 1.5$ GeV/c), with reduced effect at higher $p_T$ ($p_T > 1.5$ GeV/c).
- The splitting between $\phi$ and $K^+$ remains constant at approximately $-0.005$ across collision energies for 10-40% centrality.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hadronic rescattering effects on resonance yields (ALICE 2018, STAR 2004, Bleicher 2002) and on elliptic flow of $K^{*0}$ (Oliinychenko 2021) to the directed flow observable. The study uses the same model parameters as Parida (2022) which successfully described rapidity differential directed flow of identified hadrons. The results enable experimental measurement of the predicted sign change in $\frac{dv_1}{dy}(K^{*0})-\frac{dv_1}{dy}(K^{+})$ as a clear signature of hadronic phase effects, and provide a tool to probe the asymmetric distribution of the hadronic fireball in coordinate and momentum space.
