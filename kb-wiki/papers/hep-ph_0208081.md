# Scalar mesons in QCD and tests of the gluon content of the sigma

> We summarize the different features of the scalar mesons from QCD spectral sum rule analyses of the two- and three-point functions. The results do not favour the u-bar u + d-bar d interpretation of the broad and low mass sigma (0.6), and the u-bar s resonance nature of the eventually observed kappa(0.9) meson. We also discuss some OZI-violating and classic semileptonic and radiative decay processes which can reveal in a model-independent way the eventual gluon component sigma_B of the sigma. In a meson-gluonium mixing scenario, one also expects an observation of the K K-bar final states from the sigma_B which may compete (if phase space allowed) with the one from a low mass s-bar s state assumed in the literature to be the SU(3) partner of the $σ(0.6)$ if this latter is a u-bar u + d-bar d state

## Key claim

The QCD spectral sum rule analyses do not favor a pure \(\bar uu+\bar dd\) interpretation of the broad low-mass \(\sigma(0.6)\) nor a \(\bar us\) resonance nature for the \(\kappa(0.9)\); instead, the \(\sigma\) most probably contains a large gluon component, and a quarkonium-gluonium mixing scenario with a maximal mixing angle of about \(40^\circ\) can explain the observed spectrum and widths.

## Method

The paper uses QCD spectral sum rules (QSSR) applied to two-point and three-point correlation functions of scalar quark and gluonic currents, within the standard Operator Product Expansion (OPE) including quark condensate, gluon condensate, and mixed condensate. It also analyzes semileptonic decays of \(D\) and \(D_s\) mesons and radiative decays of \(J/\psi\) and \(\phi\) as model-independent tests of the gluon content of the \(\sigma\) meson.

## Key numbers

- The mass of the unmixed isoscalar scalar \(\bar uu+\bar dd\) meson (\(S_2\)) is predicted to be about 1 GeV, consistent with the observed \(a_0(980)\).
- The width of the \(S_2\) into \(\pi\pi\) is about 100 MeV.
- The lowest mass gluonium \(\sigma_B\) should be below 1 GeV, with a \(\pi^+\pi^-\) width of about 0.2–0.8 GeV for a mass of 0.7–1 GeV.
- The quarkonium-gluonium mixing angle is \(|\theta_S| \approx 40^\circ\).
- The predicted decay rate for \(D \to S_2 l\nu\) is \(\Gamma = (8 \pm 3) \times 10^{-16}\) GeV for \(M_{S_2} \simeq 600\) MeV.
- The branching ratio for \(J/\psi \to \gamma \sigma_B\) times \(\sigma_B \to\) all is expected to be \((4-6) \times 10^{-4}\).
- The branching ratio for \(\phi \to \gamma f_0(980)\) is predicted to be approximately \(1.3 \times 10^{-4}\), compared to the averaged data \((1.08 \pm 0.07 \pm 0.06) \times 10^{-4}\) from CMD.

## Context

This work extends earlier QSSR analyses by Narison (SNB, SNG) and others (VENEZIA, SHORE) on scalar meson masses and couplings, and contradicts the pure \(\bar qq\) interpretation of the \(\sigma\) and \(\kappa\) mesons. It builds on the instanton liquid model of Shuryak and the tachyonic gluon mass approach of Dosch et al. (2002) and Chetyrkin et al. (CNZ). The proposed quarkonium-gluonium mixing scenario (BN) provides an alternative to four-quark state or \(\bar KK\) molecule models (e.g., Black et al.) and enables experimental tests via semileptonic \(D_s\) decays and radiative \(J/\psi\) and \(\phi\) decays to reveal the gluon component of the \(\sigma\).
