# Gluon saturation and energy dependence of hadron multiplicity in pp and AA collisions at the LHC

> The recent results in \sqrt{s}=2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) reported by the ALICE collaboration shows that the power-law energy-dependence of charged hadron multiplicity in Pb+Pb collisions is significantly different from p+p collisions. We show that this different energy-dependence can be explained by inclusion of a strong angular-ordering in the gluon-decay cascade within the Color-Glass-Condensate (or gluon saturation) approach. This effect is more important in nucleus-nucleus collisions where the saturation scale is larger than 1 GeV. Our prescription gives a good description of the LHC data both in p+p and Pb+Pb collisions.

# Gluon saturation and energy dependence of hadron multiplicity in pp and AA collisions at the LHC

## Key claim
The different power-law energy dependence of charged hadron multiplicity between pp and AA collisions at the LHC can be explained within the Color-Glass-Condensate (gluon saturation) approach only when a strong angular-ordering in the gluon-decay cascade is included, an effect that becomes important when the saturation scale exceeds 1 GeV.

## Method
The authors incorporate the gluon-jet decay cascade effect, extracted from e+e- annihilation data on gluon-jet hadron multiplicities, into the kT factorization formalism for hadron production. They use the b-CGC saturation model with impact-parameter dependence for the dipole-nucleon scattering amplitude, extended to nuclear targets via the Wood-Saxon nuclear thickness profile, and fix the two free parameters (gluon jet mass and normalization) to RHIC data at sqrt(s)=200 GeV.

## Key numbers
- For pp collisions: multiplicity scales as s^0.11, with lambda=0.11 in the effective saturation scale
- For AA collisions: multiplicity scales as s^0.145, obtained as s^(lambda/2 + 0.65*lambda/4) with delta=0.65
- The gluon-jet hadron multiplicity N_h^Gluon grows as E_jet^0.65 for E_jet >= 0.85 GeV, and is constant below 0.85 GeV
- The nuclear saturation scale Q_A is approximately Q_p * A^(1/3)
- For pp collisions at sqrt(s)=14 TeV and pT=1 GeV, the proton saturation scale Q_p=0.8 GeV
- The inclusion of the gluon-decay angular-ordering effect increases charged hadron multiplicity by 20-25% at LHC energies in AA collisions
- Prediction for dN_AA/dη at midrapidity for 0-5% Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=5.5 TeV: 1897 ± 133
- The ratio (2/N_par)(dN_AA/dη) for 5.5 TeV AA collisions at midrapidity is predicted to be 1/(0.87 ± 0.06) times the value at 2.76 TeV
- The gluon jet mass parameter m_jet ≈ 0.12-0.14 GeV is consistent with RHIC data
- Experimental errors in Au+Au multiplicity at sqrt(s)=200 GeV induce 7-9% uncertainty in the normalization parameter C

## Context
This work extends the saturation/CGC description of hadron production previously applied to pp collisions at the LHC by Levin and Rezaeian (2010) and to AA collisions at RHIC. It addresses the failure of earlier kT-factorization-based models to describe the ALICE data on Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV with the same accuracy as pp data. The paper enables a unified description of both pp and AA multiplicity data within the CGC framework and predicts scaling properties of multiplicity at different energies and centralities that can be tested at future LHC runs.
