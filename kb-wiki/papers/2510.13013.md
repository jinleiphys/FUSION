# A short review on QCD sum rule studies of P-wave single heavy baryons

> Over the past few decades, the study of singly heavy baryons has entered a golden era, with numerous excited states observed by experimental collaborations. Various theoretical approaches have been developed to investigate their properties, with the QCD sum rule method being one of the most widely applied. This paper provides a review of these QCD sum rule studies. Over the last ten years, we have systematically studied $P$-wave singly heavy baryons using QCD sum rules and light-cone sum rules within the framework of heavy quark effective theory. These $P$-wave singly heavy baryons can explain many excited heavy baryons, including the $Λ_c(2595)^+$, $Λ_c(2625)^+$, $Ξ_c(2790)^{0/+}$, $Ξ_c(2815)^{0/+}$, $Σ_c(2800)^0$, $Ξ_c(2882)^0$, $Ξ_c(2923)^0$, $Ξ_c(2939)^0$, $Ξ_c(2965)^0$, $Ω_c(3000)^0$, $Ω_c(3066)^0$, $Ω_c(3090)^0$, $Ω_c(3050)^0$, $Ω_c(3119)^0$, $Λ_b(5912)^0$, $Λ_b(5920)^0$, $Ξ_b(6087)^0$, $Ξ_b(6095)^0/Ξ_b(6100)^-$, $Σ_b(6097)^\pm$, $Ξ_b(6227)^-$, $Ω_b(6316)^-$, $Ω_b(6330)^-$, $Ω_b(6340)^-$, and $Ω_b(6350)^-$, etc. Furthermore, we predict additional $P$-wave singly heavy baryons, including two $Λ_b$ states, two $Ξ_b$ states, three $Σ_b$ states, three $Ξ_b^\prime$ states, two $Ω_b$ states, two $Λ_c$ states, two $Ξ_c$ states, three $Σ_c$ states, and one $Ω_c$ state, all with relatively narrow decay widths, making them viable candidates for experimental observation. The study of singly heavy baryons is closely related to two meaningful questions:"What is the shortest possible lifetime of an observable particle?" and "How can one generally describe approximate (flavor) symmetries?".

## Key claim

This review argues that the QCD sum rule method, applied within the heavy quark effective theory (HQET) framework, can systematically explain the masses of numerous observed P-wave singly heavy baryons and predict additional narrow states, but the classification relies on the heavy quark limit and the specific assignment of quantum numbers to each observed state remains model-dependent.

## Method

The authors systematically constructed local interpolating currents for P-wave singly heavy baryons by introducing a covariant derivative into S-wave diquark configurations, distinguishing between rho-mode (internal excitation between the two light quarks, l_rho = 1) and lambda-mode (excitation between the heavy quark and the light diquark system, l_lambda = 1). They then applied QCD sum rules and light-cone sum rules within the HQET framework to calculate masses and decay properties, comparing their results with experimental data from LHCb, Belle, BaBar, and other collaborations, as well as with predictions from various quark models.

## Context

This review summarizes a decade-long systematic program by the authors (Chen et al., 2015-2025) applying QCD sum rules and light-cone sum rules within HQET to P-wave singly heavy baryons, extending earlier QCD sum rule studies by Shuryak (1981), Bagan (1991), and others. The work complements quark model calculations by Ebert (2011), Roberts (2007), Yoshida (2015), and Chen (2016), as well as lattice QCD simulations by Padmanath (2013) and Brown (2014). The classification scheme and predictions enable experimental searches for the predicted narrow states at LHCb, Belle II, and other facilities, and the methodology has recently been extended to hypothetical systems containing a single top quark (Zhang, 2025).
