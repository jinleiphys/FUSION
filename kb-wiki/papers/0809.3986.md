# Neutrino scattering with nuclei - Theory of low energy nuclear effects and its applications

> Current long baseline experiments aim at measuring neutrino oscillation parameters with a high precision. A critical quantity is the neutrino energy which can not be measured directly but has to be reconstructed from the observed hadrons. A good knowledge of neutrino-nucleus interactions is thus necessary to minimize the systematic uncertainties in neutrino fluxes, backgrounds and detector responses. In particular final-state interactions inside the target nucleus modify considerably the particle yields through rescattering, charge-exchange and absorption. Nuclear effects can be described with our coupled channel GiBUU transport model where the neutrino first interacts with a bound nucleon producing secondary particles which are then transported out of the nucleus. In this contribution, we give some examples for the application of our model focusing in particular on the MiniBooNE and K2K experiments.

## Key claim

The GiBUU transport model, which treats neutrino-nucleus interactions as a two-step process (initial vertex on a bound nucleon followed by hadronic transport), can describe nuclear effects in neutrino scattering, but the accuracy of neutrino energy reconstruction from detected hadrons depends critically on correctly accounting for final-state interactions (FSI) and in-medium modifications such as RPA correlations, without necessarily requiring a tuned axial mass.

## Method

The authors model neutrino-nucleus scattering using the GiBUU (Giessen Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck) coupled-channel transport model. The initial neutrino interaction with a bound nucleon is treated within a local Thomas-Fermi approximation, including quasielastic scattering, resonance excitation (13 resonances up to 2 GeV), and non-resonant single-pion backgrounds, with medium modifications such as Fermi motion, Pauli blocking, mean-field potentials, in-medium spectral functions, and RPA correlations. The produced hadrons are then propagated through the nucleus using a semi-classical BUU transport equation that accounts for rescattering, charge exchange, absorption, and particle decays, with particles transported off-shell due to their in-medium width.

## Key numbers

* The axial mass for the dipole form factor of the Delta resonance was refitted to \(M_A^\Delta = 0.95\) GeV (set "NEW"), compared to the previous value of 1.28 GeV (set "OLD").
* The ratio of photon to \(\pi^0\) yield from radiative Delta decay in carbon (\({}^{12}\)C) increases by a factor of about 2.4 in the nuclear medium compared to the vacuum estimate of 0.008, reaching 0.019.
* At K2K, the measured CCQE-like cross section underestimates the true CCQE cross section by about 25% due to FSI effects.
* The MiniBooNE flux-averaged \(Q^2\) distribution for CCQE-like events shows a dip at low \(Q^2\) that can be described by including RPA correlations from Nieves (2004) without tuning the axial mass.
* The binding energy correction used in the MiniBooNE neutrino energy reconstruction formula is \(E_B = 34\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the GiBUU model, previously tested on electron- and photon-induced reactions (Buss 2007, Buss 2008), to neutrino-nucleus scattering for the MiniBooNE and K2K experiments. It contrasts with the Rein and Sehgal (1980) model, which underestimates electron scattering data, and with the ANP model of Paschos (2000), which the authors argue does not incorporate well-known \(N\Delta\pi\) dynamics in nuclei. The results enable more reliable neutrino energy reconstruction in long-baseline oscillation experiments and highlight the need for coupled-channel FSI treatments over simpler Glauber approaches.
