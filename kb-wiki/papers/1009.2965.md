# Antiangular Ordering of Gluon Radiation in QCD Media

> We investigate angular and energy distributions of medium-induced gluon emission off a quark-antiquark antenna in the framework of perturbative QCD as an attempt toward understanding, from first principles, jet evolution inside the quark-gluon plasma. In-medium color coherence between emitters, neglected in all previous calculations, leads to a novel mechanism of soft-gluon radiation. The structure of the corresponding spectrum, in contrast with known medium-induced radiation, retains some properties of the vacuum case; in particular, it exhibits a soft divergency. However, as opposed to the vacuum, the collinear singularity is regulated by the pair opening angle, leading to a strict angular separation between vacuum and medium-induced radiation, denoted as antiangular ordering. We comment on the possible consequences of this new contribution for jet observables in heavy-ion collisions.

# Antiangular Ordering of Gluon Radiation in QCD Media

## Key claim

In-medium color coherence between emitters in a quark-antiquark antenna, neglected in all previous calculations, produces a strict geometrical separation between vacuum and medium-induced gluon radiation, with the medium-induced component being suppressed inside the cone defined by the pair opening angle and exhibiting a soft divergence, unlike the infrared-safe single-emitter medium-induced spectrum.

## Method

The authors solved the classical Yang-Mills equations for a quark-antiquark color-singlet antenna traversing a QCD medium, treating the pair field as a perturbation around a strong medium field described by a screened Coulomb potential with Debye mass. They computed the gluon emission spectrum to first order in opacity (two-gluon exchange with the medium) in the light-cone gauge, then squared the amplitude and separated the contributions into the standard Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev (GLV) single-emitter terms and novel interference terms between the quark and antiquark emitters.

## Key numbers

- The opening angle of the quark-antiquark pair used in numerical calculations was $\theta_{q\bar q} = 0.1$ radians.
- The medium thickness was set to $L = 4$ fm, with Debye mass $m_D = 0.5$ GeV, strong coupling $\alpha_s = 1/3$, and one-dimensional scattering center density $n_0 L^+ = 1$.
- Numerical results were shown for gluon energies $\omega = 1$ GeV and $\omega = 5$ GeV.
- The forward dipole scattering amplitude in the adjoint representation is $\Delta_{\text{med}} = \alpha_s C_A n_0 m_D^2 L^{+} r_\perp^2 [\ln(1/r_\perp m_D) + \text{const.}] / 6$, where $r_\perp = \theta_{q\bar q} L$.
- The new coherent contribution leads to a modest growth of about 30% compared to standard GLV estimates for the integrated gluon spectrum.

## Context

This work extends the standard Baier-Dokshitzer-Mueller-Peigné-Schiff-Wiedemann-Zakharov (BDMPS) and Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev (GLV) formalisms for medium-induced gluon radiation, which previously considered only single emitters and thus neglected color coherence between multiple partons in a jet. The authors build on the vacuum antenna framework of Dokshitzer (1991) to show that, contrary to naive expectations of weakened coherence in the medium, color interference effects persist and produce antiangular ordering. This mechanism enables future studies of jet modification in heavy-ion collisions, particularly as a nontrivial distortion of the humpbacked plateau in the soft sector, and differs from the well-known intrajet broadening described by Salgado (2003).
