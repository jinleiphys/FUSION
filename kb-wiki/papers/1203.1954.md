# First observation of the hyper superheavy hydrogen 6ΛH

> Three candidate events of the neutron-rich hypernucleus 6ΛH were uniquely identified in the FINUDA experiment at DAΦNE, Frascati, by observing π+ mesons from the (K-stop,π+) production reaction on 6Li targets, in coincidence with π-mesons from 6ΛH \rightarrow 6He+π- weak decay. Details of the experiment and the analysis of its data are reported, leading to an estimate of (2.9\pm2.0)\cdot10-6/K- stop for the 6ΛH production rate times the two-body π- weak decay branching ratio. The 6ΛH binding energy with respect to 5H + Λ was determined jointly from production and decay to be BΛ = (4.0 \pm 1.1) MeV, assuming that 5H is unbound with respect to 3H + 2n by 1.7 MeV. The binding energy determined from production is higher, in each one of the three events, than that determined from decay, with a difference of (0.98 \pm 0.74) MeV here assigned to the 0+g.s. \rightarrow 1+ excitation. The consequences of this assignment to Λ hypernuclear dynamics are briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The FINUDA collaboration reports the first observation of three candidate events of the neutron-rich hypernucleus 6ΛH, produced via the (K⁻stop,π⁺) reaction on ⁶Li targets, with the caveat that the production rate times the two-body π⁻ weak decay branching ratio is only (2.9±2.0)×10⁻⁶ per stopped K⁻, meaning the statistical significance is limited and the result relies on a small number of events.

## Method

The experiment used the FINUDA spectrometer at the DAΦNE e⁺e⁻ collider in Frascati, detecting π⁺ mesons from the (K⁻stop,π⁺) double charge-exchange production reaction on ⁶Li targets in coincidence with π⁻ mesons from the subsequent weak decay 6ΛH → ⁶He + π⁻. Events were selected by requiring the total kinetic energy T(π⁺)+T(π⁻) to lie in the range 202-204 MeV, with π⁺ momenta between 250-255 MeV/c and π⁻ momenta between 130-137 MeV/c, corresponding to a bound 6ΛH system. Background from the Σ⁺ reaction chain K⁻stop + p → Σ⁺ + π⁻ followed by Σ⁺ → n + π⁺ was evaluated through Monte Carlo simulations and found to contribute only 0.15-0.20 expected events.

## Key numbers

- Three candidate events of 6ΛH were identified from approximately 2.7×10⁷ stopped K⁻ on ⁶Li targets.
- The production rate times the two-body π⁻ weak decay branching ratio is (2.9±2.0)×10⁻⁶ per stopped K⁻.
- The 6ΛH binding energy with respect to ⁵H + Λ is BΛ = (4.0±1.1) MeV, assuming ⁵H is unbound with respect to ³H + 2n by 1.7 MeV.
- The mean mass of 6ΛH from the three events is (5801.4±1.1) MeV.
- The difference between the mass determined from production and from decay is (0.98±0.74) MeV, assigned to the 0⁺ ground state to 1⁺ excitation.
- The π⁺ momentum resolution is σp = (1.1±0.1) MeV/c, and the π⁻ momentum resolution is σp = (1.2±0.1) MeV/c.
- The total energy resolution for a (π⁺,π⁻) pair in coincidence is σT = 1.3 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts to produce neutron-rich hypernuclei at KEK, where only upper limits were obtained for 9ΛHe, 12ΛBe, and 16ΛC (Kubota et al.), and a previous FINUDA analysis of limited statistics gave only upper limits for 6ΛH and 7ΛH production. The observation of 6ΛH confirms predictions by Dalitz and Levi Setti (1963) and Majling that the Λ hyperon can stabilize nuclear cores beyond the neutron drip line, and the measured binding energy falls between the conservative estimate of 4.2 MeV and the stronger binding of 5.8 MeV predicted by Akaishi et al. based on coherent ΛN-ΣN mixing. The results enable future studies of Λ hypernuclear dynamics and the role of ΛN-ΣN coupling in neutron-rich systems, with implications for strange dense stellar matter.
