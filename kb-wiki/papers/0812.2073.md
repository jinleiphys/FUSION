# An effective-field-theory analysis of low-energy parity-violation in nucleon-nucleon scattering

> We analyze parity-violating nucleon-nucleon scattering at energies E<m_π^2/M using the effective field theory appropriate for this regime. The minimal Lagrangian for short-range parity-violating NN interactions is written in an operator basis that encodes the five partial-wave transitions that dominate at these energies. We calculate the leading-order relationships between parity-violating NN asymmetries and the coefficients in the Lagrangian and also discuss the size of sub-leading corrections. We conclude with a discussion of further observables needed to completely determine the leading-order Lagrangian.

## Key claim

The paper provides a model-independent effective-field-theory framework for parity-violating nucleon-nucleon scattering at energies below the pion mass scale, but the leading-order relationships between asymmetries and Lagrangian coefficients are only valid when sub-leading corrections (proportional to the effective range and higher-order momentum dependence) are neglected; the analysis shows that the two existing low-energy data points are consistent with a leading-order analysis only within their combined uncertainties, and the 45 MeV point may be too high for reliable leading-order treatment.

## Method

The authors use pionless effective field theory (EFT(π̸)) to describe parity-violating NN scattering at energies E < m_π²/M, writing the minimal Lagrangian in a partial-wave basis that encodes the five independent S-P wave transitions. They employ a dynamical dibaryon field formalism to resum strong-interaction effective-range corrections, calculate the leading-order relationships between longitudinal asymmetries and the five low-energy constants, and compute Coulomb corrections for pp scattering using an expansion in the small Coulomb parameter η.

## Key numbers

- The pp longitudinal asymmetry at E=13.6 MeV is measured as A_L = (-0.93 ± 0.21) × 10⁻⁷ (Eversheim 1991)
- The pp longitudinal asymmetry at E=45 MeV is measured as A_L = (-1.50 ± 0.22) × 10⁻⁷ (Kistryn 1987)
- From the 13.6 MeV datum, the extracted ratio A_pp/C₀^(¹S₀) = (-1.5 ± 0.3) × 10⁻¹⁰ MeV⁻¹ at leading order
- The leading-order prediction for the 45 MeV asymmetry is A_L = (-1.7 ± 0.4) × 10⁻⁷
- Including effective-range corrections (r^(¹S₀)=2.73 fm) gives A_pp/C₀^(¹S₀) = (-1.1 ± 0.3) × 10⁻¹⁰ MeV⁻¹ and predicts A_L(45 MeV) = (-2.6 ± 0.6) × 10⁻⁷
- The Coulomb parameter η at 13.6 MeV is 0.043, giving only a 3% Coulomb correction

## Context

This work extends the pionless EFT approach to parity-violating NN scattering, building on the operator classification of Girlanda (2008) and the five Danilov amplitudes (Danilov 1965). It provides a model-independent alternative to the Desplanques-Donoghue-Holstein (DDH) framework (Desplanques 1979) that relied on vector-meson exchange assumptions. The results enable a program to determine all five leading-order parity-violating low-energy constants from a combination of pp, np, and (if feasible) nn scattering measurements, though the authors note that the 45 MeV datum may already exceed the EFT's range of validity.
