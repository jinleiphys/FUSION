# Neutron spin structure from polarized deuteron DIS with proton tagging

> Polarized electron-deuteron deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) with detection of the spectator proton ("tagged DIS") enables measurements of neutron spin structure with maximal control of nuclear effects. We calculate the longitudinal spin asymmetries in polarized tagged DIS using methods of light-front nuclear structure and study their dependence on the measured proton momentum. Asymmetries can be formed with all three deuteron spin states ($\pm 1, 0$) or the two maximum-spin states only ($\pm 1$, involving tensor polarization). The proton momentum dependence can be used to select pure S-wave configurations in the deuteron and eliminate D-wave depolarization (transverse momenta $p_{pT} \lesssim$ 100 MeV). Free neutron spin structure can be extracted model-independently through pole extrapolation of the tagged asymmetries. Such measurements could be performed at a future electron-ion collider (EIC) with polarized deuteron beams and forward proton detectors.

## Key claim

Polarized electron-deuteron deep-inelastic scattering with detection of the spectator proton ("tagged DIS") can enable measurements of neutron spin structure with maximal control of nuclear effects, but the extraction depends on using the proton momentum dependence to select pure S-wave configurations and eliminate D-wave depolarization, and the free neutron spin structure can be extracted model-independently through pole extrapolation of the tagged asymmetries.

## Method

The authors calculate longitudinal spin asymmetries in polarized tagged DIS using light-front nuclear structure methods, expressing the cross section in terms of deuteron light-front spectral functions that separate nuclear and nucleonic structure. They compute the "three-state" asymmetry (using all three deuteron spin states ±1, 0) and the "two-state" asymmetry (using only the two maximum-spin states ±1, involving tensor polarization) as functions of the measured proton momentum variables α_p and |p_pT|. The deuteron wave function is constructed from S- and D-wave radial functions using the AV18 potential, with Melosh rotations connecting canonical nucleon spin states to light-front helicity states.

## Key numbers

- Transverse momenta p_pT ≲ 100 MeV can be used to select pure S-wave configurations and eliminate D-wave depolarization
- The two-state asymmetry D_2 equals unity at zero proton transverse momentum for arbitrary α_p (D_2(α_p, |p_pT| = 0) = 1)
- Both D_3 and D_2 decrease significantly at |p_pT| ≳ 150 MeV and pass through zero at |p_pT| ≈ 300 MeV
- The deuteron binding energy ε_D = 2.2 MeV determines the pole position for analytic continuation
- Typical magnitude of the free neutron asymmetry A_∥n is a few × 10^{-2} for x̃ ≳ 10^{-2} and y = O(1)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of unpolarized tagged DIS (Sargsian 2005, Strikman 2017) to the polarized case, and builds on the theoretical framework for deuteron light-front wave functions developed by Frankfurt (1981) and Kondratyuk (1983). The method enables measurements at a future electron-ion collider (EIC) with polarized deuteron beams and forward proton detectors, reducing the theoretical uncertainty from nuclear effects that complicates inclusive DIS measurements on deuterium and ³He targets. The two-state asymmetry is recommended for practical extraction because it requires only preparation of the ±1 deuteron spin states and exhibits much weaker dependence on proton momentum at small momenta.
