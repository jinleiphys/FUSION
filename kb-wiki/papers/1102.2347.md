# QCD sum rule calculation for the charmonium-like structures in the J/psi phi and J/psi omega invariant mass spectra

> Using the QCD sum rules we test if the charmonium-like structure Y(4274), observed in the $J/ψϕ$ invariant mass spectrum, can be described with a $D_s\bar{D}_{s0}(2317)+h.c.$ molecular current with $J^{PC}=0^{-+}$. We consider the contributions of condensates up to dimension ten and we work at leading order in $α_s$. We keep terms which are linear in the strange quark mass $m_s$. The mass obtained for such state is $m_{D_s{D}_{s0}}=(4.80\pm 0.55)$ GeV. We also consider a molecular $0^{-+}$ $D\bar{D}_{0}(2400)+h.c.$ current and we obtain $m_{D{D}_0}=(4.61\pm 0.71)$ GeV. Our study shows that the newly observed Y(4274) in the $J/ψϕ$ invariant mass spectra can be, considering the uncertainties, described using a molecular charmonium current.

# QCD sum rule calculation for the charmonium-like structures in the J/psi phi and J/psi omega invariant mass spectra

## Key claim
The newly observed Y(4274) structure in the J/psi phi invariant mass spectrum can be described as a S-wave D_s D_{s0}(2317) + h.c. molecular state with J^{PC}=0^{-+}, but only when considering the large uncertainties (the central mass obtained is 4.78 GeV, significantly above the experimental 4.274 GeV). The D D_0(2400) molecular state is consistent with the enhancement around 4.2 GeV in J/psi omega, again within large errors.

## Method
The authors used QCD sum rules to evaluate the two-point correlation function for a D_s D_{s0} molecular current with J^{PC}=0^{-+}, working at leading order in alpha_s and including condensate contributions up to dimension ten, keeping terms linear in the strange quark mass m_s. They extracted the mass by taking the derivative of the Borel-transformed sum rule with respect to 1/M^2, and determined the Borel window by requiring OPE convergence (higher dimension condensates below 10% of total), Borel stability, and pole dominance over continuum. The same procedure was applied to the non-strange D D_0 molecular current by setting m_s=0 and replacing the strange quark condensate with the light quark condensate.

## Key numbers
- Mass of D_s D_{s0} molecular state: m = (4.78 ± 0.54) GeV
- Mass of D D_0 molecular state: m = (4.55 ± 0.49) GeV
- Current-state coupling for D_s D_{s0}: lambda = (6.0 ± 3.9) × 10^{-2} GeV^5
- Current-state coupling for D D_0: lambda = (5.4 ± 3.9) × 10^{-2} GeV^5
- Continuum threshold range for D_s D_{s0}: sqrt(s_0) = 5.1 to 5.3 GeV
- Continuum threshold range for D D_0: sqrt(s_0) = 4.9 to 5.1 GeV
- Borel window for D_s D_{s0}: M^2 = 2.8 to 3.66 GeV^2 (for sqrt(s_0)=5.2 GeV)
- Borel window for D D_0: M^2 = 2.7 to 3.40 GeV^2 (for sqrt(s_0)=5.0 GeV)
- Experimental mass of Y(4274) from CDF: M = 4274.4^{+8.4}_{-6.7} (stat) MeV, width Gamma = 32.3^{+21.9}_{-15.3} (stat) MeV

## Context
This work tests the molecular interpretation proposed by Liu (2010) for the Y(4274) observed by CDF (Aaltonen 2011) in B+ → J/psi phi K+ decays, and for a cousin state in J/psi omega. It extends the QCD sum rule approach previously applied to the Y(4140) as a D_s* D_s* scalar molecular state by Albuquerque (2009). The results support the molecular picture but with large uncertainties, and the D D_0 mass agrees with tetraquark calculations by Chen (2010). The work enables future studies of whether these states are better described as molecules, tetraquarks, or hybrid mesons.
