# Nuclear in-medium effects on $η$ dynamics in proton-nucleus collisions

> The dynamics of $η$ meson produced in proton-induced nuclear reactions via the decay of N$^{\ast}(1535)$ has been investigated within the Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics transport model (LQMD). The in-medium modifications of the $η$ production in dense nuclear matter are included in the model, in which an attractive $η$-nucleon potential is implemented. The impact of the $η$ optical potential on the $η$ dynamics is investigated. It is found that the attractive potential leads to the reduction of high-momentum (kinetic energy) production from the spectra of momentum distributions and inclusive cross sections and increasing the reabsorption process by surrounding nucleons.

## Key claim

The attractive eta-nucleon optical potential (strength -94 MeV at saturation density and zero momentum) reduces high-momentum eta production and enhances eta reabsorption in proton-nucleus collisions, with the effect becoming more pronounced for heavier target nuclei.

## Method

The authors used the Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics (LQMD) transport model, which includes hadron-hadron collisions, resonance decays, and mean-field potentials. Eta mesons were produced exclusively via decay of the N*(1535) resonance, and an attractive eta-nucleon optical potential derived from chiral perturbation theory was implemented. The model compared calculations with and without this potential for proton-induced reactions on carbon and calcium targets at 1.5 GeV incident energy.

## Key numbers

- Eta optical potential strength: -94 MeV at saturation density (rho = rho_0 = 0.16 fm^-3) and zero momentum
- Threshold energy for eta production: E_th(eta) = 1.26 GeV
- Incident kinetic energy for proton-nucleus collisions: 1.5 GeV
- N*(1535) decay width: Gamma = 150 MeV, with equal branching ratios to eta and pi (half each)
- Eta/pi0 ratios in heavy-ion collisions at 1.0 A GeV: calculated values range from 1.186% (C+C) to 2.087% (Au+Au), compared to experimental data from TAPS collaboration ranging from 0.57% to 1.4%

## Context

This work extends previous studies of in-medium hadron properties using the LQMD model (Feng 2011, 2012) and addresses the poorly constrained eta-nucleon potential, which various models predict between -20 MeV and -90 MeV at normal nuclear density (Niedermayer 2008). The results are relevant for understanding eta-nucleus bound states and for interpreting future proton-nucleus experiments aimed at extracting the eta-nucleon interaction strength.
