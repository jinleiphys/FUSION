# Structure of the doubly magic nuclei $^{208}$Pb and $^{266}$Pb from ab initio computations

> Theoretical studies indicate that the superheavy neutron-rich nucleus $^{266}_{\ 82}$Pb$_{184}$ is doubly magic and at the neutron drip line. While its density distributions and single-particle energies have been computed, the structure of this nucleus is yet unknown. We perform ab initio computations of $^{266}$Pb using an interaction from an effective field theory of quantum chromodynamics tuned only on properties of nuclei with $A \leq 4$. We validate our theoretical framework by computing the first $2^+$ and $3^-$ excited states of $^{208}$Pb, finding agreement with experimental data. We confirm that $^{266}$Pb is doubly magic and show that its $3^-$ state, located below the $2^+$ state, exhibits an excitation gap of 2.6 MeV with respect to the ground state. Our calculations also suggest that this nucleus is at the neutron drip line.

# Structure of the doubly magic nuclei $^{208}$Pb and $^{266}$Pb from ab initio computations

## Key claim
The authors confirm that $^{266}$Pb is doubly magic and at the neutron drip line, but the excitation energies are predictions based on calculations in a reduced model space adjusted by an estimate of the contribution expected from larger basis sizes, with the $3^-$ state located below the $2^+$ state and exhibiting an excitation gap of 2.6 MeV with respect to the ground state.

## Method
The authors employ the coupled-cluster method in the singles and doubles (CCSD) approximation with an angular-momentum-coupled scheme, using the chiral interaction 1.8/2.0 (EM) derived from an effective field theory of quantum chromodynamics and tuned only on nuclei with mass numbers A = 2, 3, and 4. Excited states are computed using the equation-of-motion coupled-cluster method (EOM-CCSD) with perturbative triples corrections (EOM-CCSD(T)), and neighboring nuclei are studied with the particle-attached EOM-CCSD (PA-EOM-CCSD) technique. Calculations are performed in a spherical harmonic oscillator basis with up to 15 major shells (N_max = 14) at harmonic oscillator frequencies of 12 MeV for lead isotopes near A=208 and 10 MeV for those near A=266.

## Key numbers
- Ground-state energy of $^{208}$Pb: -1630 MeV at the CCSD level, with the experimental value being -1636.336 MeV (Wang 2021)
- Ground-state energy of $^{266}$Pb: -1730 MeV at the CCSD level, corresponding to a binding energy per nucleon of approximately 6.5 MeV
- Excitation energy of the $3^-$ state in $^{266}$Pb: 2.6 MeV with respect to the ground state
- Predicted excitation energy of the $2^+$ state in $^{266}$Pb: 3.4 MeV (estimated converged result)
- Model-space convergence for $^{208}$Pb: difference between N_max = 12 and N_max = 14 is 80 keV for the $2^+$ state and 110 keV for the $3^-$ state
- Triples contributions lower excitation energies by approximately 1.5 MeV in $^{208}$Pb and by 0.9 MeV ($2^+$) and 1.3 MeV ($3^-$) in $^{266}$Pb
- Neutron separation energy S_n for the $11/2^-$ state in $^{267}$Pb: approximately -3 MeV, indicating $^{266}$Pb is at the neutron drip line
- Energy gap of about 2.3 MeV between the $17/2^+$ and $13/2^+$ excited states in $^{267}$Pb, corresponding to a shell gap at N = 258

## Context
This work extends ab initio coupled-cluster calculations from the established doubly magic nucleus $^{208}$Pb to the superheavy neutron-rich nucleus $^{266}$Pb, which had previously only been studied using mean-field-based approaches such as non-relativistic and covariant density functionals (e.g., Nakada 2014, Li 2014, Afanasjev 2015, Agbemava 2015). The authors validate their framework against experimental data for $^{208}$Pb (Heusler 2016) and against prior ab initio results for $^{208}$Pb from Hu (2022) and Hebeler (2023). The results enable first-principles predictions for the structure of superheavy nuclei at the neutron drip line, demonstrating that an interaction tuned only on light nuclei (A = 2, 3, 4) can describe properties of nuclei around $^{266}$Pb.
