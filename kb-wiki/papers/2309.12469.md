# Constraints for the X17 boson from compacts objects observations

> We investigate the hypothetical X17 boson on neutron stars and Quark Stars (QSs) using various hadronic Equation of States (EoSs) with phenomenological or microscopic origin. Our aim is to set realistic constraints on its coupling constant and the mass scaling, with respect to causality and various possible upper mass limits and the dimensionless tidal deformability $Λ_{1.4}$. In particular, we pay special attention on two main phenomenological parameters of the X17, the one is related to the coupling constant $\mathrm{g}$ that it has with hadrons or quarks and the other with the in-medium effects through the regulator $\mathrm{C}$. Both are very crucial concerning the contribution on the total energy density and pressure. In the case of considering the X17 as a carrier of nuclear force in Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) theory, an admixture into vector boson segment was constrained by 20\% and 30\%. In our investigation, we came to the general conclusion that the effect of the hypothetical X17 both on neutron and QSs constrained mainly by the causality limit, which is a specific property of each EoS. Moreover, it depends on the interplay between the main two parameters that is the interaction coupling $\mathrm{g}$ and the in-medium effects regulator $\mathrm{C}$. These effects are more pronounced in the case of QSs concerning all the bulk properties.

## Key claim

The paper constrains the hypothetical X17 boson's coupling constant g and in-medium mass scaling parameter C by requiring that neutron star and quark star equations of state remain causal (speed of sound below light speed) and satisfy observational limits on maximum mass and tidal deformability, rather than claiming a definitive detection or exclusion.

## Method

The authors incorporate the X17 boson as a Yukawa correction to gravity (non-Newtonian gravity model) and as an admixture into the vector boson sector of Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) theory, using 13 RMF equations of state with admixture coefficients of 20% and 30%. They also employ the Momentum Dependent Interaction (MDI) model with three different slope parameters L = 65, 72.5, and 80 MeV, and the Color-Flavor Locked (CFL) model for quark stars with three stiffness levels (CFL2, CFL10, CFL13). The boson contribution to energy density and pressure is added with coupling g in the range 0.01-0.022 and in-medium scaling parameter C varied up to a maximum value C_max determined by causality.

## Key numbers

- For RMF with 30% admixture and +5% rho-coupling, maximum mass reaches 2.33 solar masses
- For RMF with 20% admixture, maximum masses range from 2.1 to 2.27 solar masses with radii between 12.5 km and 13 km
- The coupling constant g is allowed up to approximately 0.022, derived from the constraint that binding energy of symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density n_0 lies between -17 MeV and -15 MeV
- For the X17 boson mass of 17 MeV, the coupling range g = 0.01-0.022 corresponds to (g/m_B c^2)^2 in the interval 0.346 to 1.675 GeV^{-2}
- In quark stars, the boson contribution to energy density and pressure includes an extra factor of 9 compared to neutron stars

## Context

This work extends prior investigations of Weakly Interactive Light Bosons (WILB) in neutron stars, where Sulaksono and collaborators estimated the ratio g^2/mu^2 to be less than 2 GeV^{-2}, and Krivoruchenko (2009) found a range between 25 and 50 GeV^{-2} (with an upper value of 100 GeV^{-2} also considered). For quark stars, Weber and collaborators estimated g^2/mu^2 between 4.58 and 9.32 GeV^{-2} for specific quark masses. Unlike these earlier studies that only constrained the ratio g^2/mu^2, this paper separately constrains the coupling g and the mass scaling parameter C by using the known X17 boson mass of 17 MeV. The results enable future observational constraints from gravitational wave events and pulsar mass measurements to further narrow the allowed parameter space for the X17 boson.
