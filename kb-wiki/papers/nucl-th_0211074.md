# Microscopic description of the scissors mode in odd-mass heavy deformed nuclei

> Pseudo-SU(3) shell-model results are reported for M1 excitation strengths in 157-Gd, 163-Dy and 169-Tm in the energy range between 2 and 4 MeV. Non-zero pseudo-spin couplings between the configurations play a very important role in determining the M1 strength distribution, especially its rapidly changing fragmentation pattern which differs significantly from what has been found in neighboring even-even systems. The results suggest one should examine contributions from intruder levels.

## Key claim

Pseudo-SU(3) shell-model calculations, including for the first time states with pseudo-spin 1 and 3/2, can qualitatively reproduce the highly fragmented M1 strength distributions in odd-mass nuclei, but a fully quantitative description requires including contributions from intruder levels, which were neglected.

## Method

The authors performed pseudo-SU(3) shell-model calculations for the odd-mass deformed nuclei 157Gd, 163Dy, and 169Tm. The Hamiltonian included spherical Nilsson single-particle terms, quadrupole-quadrupole and pairing interactions, and three rotor-like terms diagonal in the SU(3) basis, with three free parameters fixed by reproducing low-energy spectra. The Hilbert space was enlarged beyond the usual pseudo-spin 0 and 1/2 configurations to include pseudo-spin 1 and 3/2 states, allowing the M1 transition operator to connect the ground state with many excited states in the 2-4 MeV energy range.

## Key numbers

- In 157Gd, the summed experimental B(M1) strength in the 2-4 MeV range is 1.596 ± 0.235 μ²; the theoretical summed strength is 0.782 μ².
- In 163Dy, the summed experimental B(M1) strength in the 2-4 MeV range is 1.641 ± 0.338 μ²; the theoretical summed strength is 0.483 μ².
- In 169Tm, the summed experimental B(M1) strength in the 2-4 MeV range is 2.833 ± 0.812 μ²; the theoretical summed strength is 3.769 μ².
- For 163Dy, the spin-only contribution to the summed B(M1) in the 2-4 MeV range is 0.543 μ², the orbital-only contribution is 0.103 μ², and the angle between the spin and orbital channels is 110°.
- For 157Gd, the angle between the spin and orbital channels is 83°; for 169Tm, it is 96°.
- In 157Gd, the ground state has a 13% mixing with pseudo-spin 1 and 3/2 components.

## Context

This work extends pseudo-SU(3) shell-model descriptions previously applied to even-even nuclei (Pop00) and to low-energy bands in odd-mass nuclei (Var01) by including higher pseudo-spin configurations to describe the scissors mode fragmentation. It contrasts with earlier theoretical approaches such as the Interacting Boson Fermion Model (Van89, Fra91), the particle-core-coupling model (Rad89), and the Quasiparticle Phonon Nuclear Model (Sol96), which agreed on the role of the uncoupled nucleon but could not explain the varying fragmentation patterns. The results suggest that future work must include intruder-level contributions and that detailed studies of M1 transitions in other odd-mass nuclei are needed to further test the theory.
