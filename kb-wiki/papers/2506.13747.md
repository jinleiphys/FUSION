# Two-neutrino $0^+ \to 0^+$ double beta decay of $^{48}\mathrm{Ca}$ within the DFT-NCCI framework

> We present a seminal calculation of the nuclear matrix element for the two-neutrino double beta ($2νββ$) decay of ${}^{48}\text{Ca} \rightarrow {}^{48}\text{Ti}$ using a post-Hartree-Fock (HF) Density Functional Theory-based No-Core Configuration-Interaction (DFT-NCCI) framework developed by our group [Phys. Rev. C 94, 024306 (2016)]. In the present calculation, we utilize a variant of the approach that restores rotational symmetry and mixes states projected from self-consistent mean-field configurations obtained by solving the HF equations with the density-independent local Skyrme interaction. Our calculations yield $|\mathcal{M}_{2νββ}| = 0.056(6)$ MeV$^{-1}$ for the nuclear matrix element describing this process. This result is in very good agreement with shell-model studies - for example, with the calculations by Horoi {\it et al.\/} [Phys. Rev. C 75, 034303 (2007)], which yielded 0.054 (0.064) MeV$^{-1}$ for the GXPF1A (GXPF1) interactions, respectively. It is also in a reasonable agreement with the most recent experimental estimate from the review by Barabash, which is 0.068(6) MeV$^{-1}$, assuming quenching $qg_\text{A} \approx 1$. The consistency of our prediction with the shell-model results increases our confidence in the nuclear modeling of this second-order, very rare process which is of paramount importance for further modeling of the neutrinoless double beta ($0νββ$) decay process.

## Key claim

The DFT-NCCI framework yields a nuclear matrix element for the two-neutrino double beta decay of ⁴⁸Ca → ⁴⁸Ti that agrees with shell-model predictions, but the calculation relies on a simplified variant of the model that restores only rotational symmetry (not isospin), justified by the high isospin of the mother nucleus (T = 8).

## Method

The authors used a post-Hartree-Fock Density Functional Theory-based No-Core Configuration-Interaction (DFT-NCCI) framework with the SV_T density-independent Skyrme pseudo-potential augmented with tensor terms. They solved self-consistent HF equations to generate low-lying multiparticle-multihole configurations, applied angular-momentum projection and K-mixing, then solved the Hill-Wheeler-Griffin equation to obtain eigenstates for the 0⁺ ground states of ⁴⁸Ca and ⁴⁸Ti and the 1⁺ intermediate states in ⁴⁸Sc. The Gamow-Teller matrix elements between orthogonal Slater determinants were computed by introducing an isospace rotation to circumvent the limitations of the Generalized Wick Theorem.

## Key numbers

* Nuclear matrix element |M₂νββ| = 0.056(6) MeV⁻¹ for the 2νββ decay of ⁴⁸Ca → ⁴⁸Ti
* Comparison with shell-model results: 0.054 MeV⁻¹ (GXPF1A interaction) and 0.064 MeV⁻¹ (GXPF1 interaction) from Horoi et al. (2007)
* Experimental estimate from Barabash review: 0.068(6) MeV⁻¹, assuming quenching qg_A ≈ 1
* Test calculation for ²¹Na → ²¹Ne β-decay yielded |M_GT| = 1.38182 with 4 mesh points, compared to 1.37547 from the full isospin-projected model
* Test calculation for ⁷⁶Ge → ⁷⁶As yielded |M_GT| = 1.61535 with n ≥ 8 mesh points
* Experimental Q-value Q_ββ = 4273.7 keV and mass difference ΔM = 282 keV used in the matrix element calculation
* Spherical neutron subshell energies in ⁴⁸Ca: −11.1 MeV (νf₇/₂), −4.0 MeV (νp₃/₂), −2.8 MeV (νf₅/₂), −2.1 MeV (νp₁/₂)

## Context

This work extends the DFT-NCCI framework, previously applied to isospin-symmetry breaking and Gamow-Teller β-decay in N∼Z nuclei (Satuła 2009, 2011; Konieczka 2016, 2018), to the rare second-order process of 2νββ decay. The result agrees with shell-model calculations by Horoi et al. (2007) and is consistent with experimental estimates from Barabash. The agreement between the DFT-NCCI and shell-model predictions increases confidence in nuclear modeling for the 0νββ decay process, which is the primary motivation for improving theoretical descriptions of the 2νββ decay nuclear matrix element.
