# Dynamic density and spin responses of a superfluid Fermi gas in the BCS-BEC crossover: Path integral formulation and pair fluctuation theory

> We present a standard field theoretical derivation of the dynamic density and spin linear response functions of a dilute superfluid Fermi gas in the BCS-BEC crossover in both three and two dimensions. The derivation of the response functions is based on the elegant functional path integral approach which allows us to calculate the density-density and spin-spin correlation functions by introducing the external sources for the density and the spin density. Since the generating functional cannot be evaluated exactly, we consider two gapless approximations which ensure a gapless collective mode (Goldstone mode) in the superfluid state: the BCS-Leggett mean-field theory and the Gaussian-pair-fluctuation (GPF) theory. In the mean-field theory, our results of the response functions agree with the known results from the random phase approximation. We further consider the pair fluctuation effects and establish a theoretical framework for the dynamic responses within the GPF theory. We show that the GPF response theory naturally recover three kinds of famous diagrammatic contributions: the Self-Energy contribution, the Aslamazov-Lakin contribution, and the Maki-Thompson contribution. We also show that unlike the equilibrium state, in evaluating the response functions, the linear (first-order) terms in the external sources as well as the induced order parameter perturbations should be treated carefully. In the superfluid state, there is an additional order parameter contribution which ensures that in the static and long wavelength limit, the density response function recovers the result of the compressibility (compressibility sum rule). We expect that the $f$-sum rule is manifested by the full number equation which includes the contribution from the Gaussian pair fluctuations.

## Key claim

The paper presents a field-theoretical derivation of dynamic density and spin response functions for a superfluid Fermi gas across the BCS-BEC crossover, but the results are limited to two specific gapless approximations (BCS-Leggett mean-field theory and Gaussian-pair-fluctuation theory) because the generating functional cannot be evaluated exactly.

## Method

The author uses a functional path integral approach to derive density-density and spin-spin correlation functions by introducing external sources for density and spin density. The response functions are evaluated within two gapless approximations that ensure a gapless Goldstone mode: the BCS-Leggett mean-field theory (which recovers random phase approximation results) and the Gaussian-pair-fluctuation (GPF) theory. The GPF response theory is shown to naturally recover three diagrammatic contributions: the Self-Energy contribution, the Aslamazov-Lakin contribution, and the Maki-Thompson contribution.

## Key numbers

- In 3D, the composite boson scattering length from mean-field theory is a_B = 2a_3D; the GPF theory modifies this to a_B ≈ 0.55a_3D, close to the exact result a_B ≈ 0.6a_3D
- In 2D, the GPF theory predicts a composite boson scattering length very close to the exact result, and the equations of state agree with experimental measurements and quantum Monte Carlo results
- The BCS-BEC crossover is characterized by the dimensionless gas parameter 1/(k_F a_3D), where the BCS limit corresponds to 1/(k_F a_3D) → -∞ and the BEC limit to 1/(k_F a_3D) → +∞
- At the unitary point a_3D → ∞, the gas parameter 1/(k_F a_3D) = 0 and all thermodynamic quantities become universal constants

## Context

This work extends the Gaussian-pair-fluctuation theory, which has achieved quantitative success in describing the equation of state for both 3D and 2D systems (Hu, Liu, and Drummond; Diener, Sensarma, and Randeria), to the calculation of dynamic response functions. The random phase approximation (RPA) approach of Zwerger and Hu, as well as the pseudogap theory of Levin, previously showed only qualitative agreement with experimental Bragg spectroscopy measurements. The GPF response theory enables proper treatment of pair fluctuation contributions to dynamic structure factors, which are measurable via Bragg spectroscopy and are relevant for understanding neutrino emissivity in neutron matter and compact star cooling.
