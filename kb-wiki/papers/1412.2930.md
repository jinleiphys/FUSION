# Productions of $Z^0$ and $W^+/W^-$ in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions at the LHC

> The productions of massive gauge bosons, $Z^0$ and $W^+/W^-$, in heavy-ion reactions at the LHC, provide an excellent tool to study the cold nuclear matter effects in high-energy nuclear collisions. In this paper we investigate $Z^0$ and $W^+/W^-$ productions in p+Pb and Pb+Pb at the LHC, at NLO and NNLO with DYNNLO incorporating the nuclear PDFs (nPDFs) parametrization sets EPS09 and DSSZ, within the framework of perturbative QCD. The numerical simulations of the transverse momentum spectra, rapidity dependence, and related nuclear modification factors for $Z^0$ and $W$ particles, as well as the charge asymmetry for W boson, are provided and tested against the latest experimental data. It is found that the theoretical results with EPS09 and DSSZ nPDFs can give good descriptions of the recent data on $Z^0$ and $W^{\pm}$ particles in p+Pb and Pb+Pb within the experimental error bars, though some differences between results with EPS09 and DSSZ can be observed, especially in the rapidity dependence of the $Z^0$ yield. Theoretical predictions for future measurements on $Z^0$ and $W$ in p+Pb and Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC are also provided.

## Key claim
The authors show that next-to-leading-order (NLO) and next-to-next-to-leading-order (NNLO) perturbative QCD calculations using DYNNLO with nuclear PDF sets EPS09 and DSSZ can describe LHC data on Z0 and W± production in p+Pb and Pb+Pb collisions within experimental error bars, though differences between the two nPDF sets appear in the rapidity dependence of Z0 yields and in the transverse-momentum dependence of nuclear modification factors.

## Method
The authors computed Z0 and W± production cross sections in p+Pb at √sNN = 5.02 TeV and Pb+Pb at √sNN = 2.76 TeV using the DYNNLO program at NLO (O(αs)) and NNLO (O(αs²)), incorporating nuclear parton distribution functions from EPS09 and DSSZ sets with MSTW2008 proton PDFs. They calculated transverse momentum spectra, rapidity distributions, nuclear modification factors RAA and RpA, and W boson charge asymmetry, comparing results to CMS and ATLAS data.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of Drell-Yan gauge boson production in hadronic collisions (Kartvelishvili 1995, Conesa del Valle 2007, 2009) by providing the first complete NNLO theoretical investigation of Z0 and W± production in both p+Pb and Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies. The results enable discrimination between the EPS09 (Eskola 2009) and DSSZ (de Florian 2011) nuclear PDF parametrizations, particularly through Z0 rapidity dependence which constrains quark nuclear modifications, and Z0 transverse momentum dependence which probes gluon nuclear modifications. The work also provides predictions for future LHC measurements.
