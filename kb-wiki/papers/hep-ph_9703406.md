# Nucleon Vector Strangeness Form Factors: Multi-pion Continuum and the OZI Rule

> We estimate the 3 πcontinuum contribution to the nucleon strange quark vector current form factors, including the effect of a 3 π<----> ρπresonance. We find the magnitude of this OZI-violating contribution to be comparable to that of typical OZI-allowed contributions. We also study the isoscalar electromagnetic form factors, and find that the presence of a ρπresonance in the multi-pion continuum may generate an appreciable contribution.

# Nucleon Vector Strangeness Form Factors: Multi-pion Continuum and the OZI Rule

## Key claim

The three-pion continuum contribution to the nucleon strange quark vector current form factors, when including the effects of $3\pi \leftrightarrow \rho\pi$ and $3\pi \leftrightarrow \omega$ resonances, is comparable in magnitude to typical OZI-allowed contributions from two-particle intermediate states containing valence strange quarks. This contradicts the expectation from chiral power counting and the OZI rule that such contributions should be negligible.

## Method

The authors used a dispersion relation framework to compute the $3\pi$ intermediate state contribution to the strange quark vector form factors $F_1^{(s)}$ and $F_2^{(s)}$. The $3\pi$ spectral function was modeled as the sum of a narrow $\omega$ resonance term and a $\rho\pi$ continuum term, where the $N\bar{N} \to \rho\pi$ amplitude was evaluated in the Born approximation using linear sigma model couplings, and the $\langle \rho\pi | \bar{s}\gamma_\mu s | 0 \rangle$ matrix element was parameterized using a vector meson dominance model with $\omega$ and $\phi$ poles. The dispersion integrals were evaluated for the dimensionless mean square Dirac radius $\rho^{(s)}$ and anomalous magnetic moment $\kappa^{(s)}$.

## Key numbers

- The $\rho\pi$ contribution to the strangeness dimensionless mean square Dirac radius: $\rho^{(s)} = 0.86$
- The $\rho\pi$ contribution to the strangeness anomalous magnetic moment: $\kappa^{(s)} = -0.44$
- The $\omega$ resonance contribution to the strangeness dimensionless mean square Dirac radius: $\rho^{(s)} = 1.08$ (or $1.41$ using strong couplings from fits to isoscalar EM form factors)
- The $\omega$ resonance contribution to the strangeness anomalous magnetic moment: $\kappa^{(s)} = 0$ (or $0.04$)
- The $K\bar{K}$ (lightest OZI-allowed) contribution to the strangeness dimensionless mean square Dirac radius: $\rho^{(s)} = 0.53$
- The $K\bar{K}$ contribution to the strangeness anomalous magnetic moment: $\kappa^{(s)} = -0.16$
- The $\rho\pi$ contribution to the isoscalar EM dimensionless mean square Dirac radius: $\rho^{I=0} = 0.60$ (compared to experimental value $-4.56$)
- The $\rho\pi$ contribution to the isoscalar EM anomalous magnetic moment: $\kappa^{I=0} = -0.38$ (compared to experimental value $-0.06$)
- The $\rho\pi$ transition form factor at $t=0$ in the strangeness channel: $g_{\rho\pi}^{(s)}(0) = 0.076$
- The $\rho\pi$ transition form factor at $t=0$ in the isoscalar EM channel: $g_{\rho\pi}^{I=0}(0) = 0.53$

## Context

This work extends the dispersion relation analysis of strange form factors by Musolf and collaborators (1997) and challenges the assumption that OZI-allowed processes dominate the strangeness form factors, as assumed in most hadronic model calculations including those of Geiger and Isgur (1997). The inclusion of the $3\pi \leftrightarrow \rho\pi$ resonance mechanism, motivated by the measured $\phi \to \rho\pi$ decay width and $p\bar{p}$ annihilation data showing $\rho\pi$ structure, has not been considered previously. The results suggest that conventional pole analyses of the isoscalar electromagnetic form factors, such as those by Mergell, Meissner, and Drechsel (1996), may need modification to account explicitly for $\rho\pi$ continuum effects, a conclusion similar to that reached by Meissner (1997).
