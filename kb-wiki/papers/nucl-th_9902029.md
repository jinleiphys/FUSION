# Quasielastic Electron Scattering from Nuclei: Random-Phase vs. Ring Approximations

> We investigate the extent to which the nuclear transverse response to electron scattering in the quasielastic region, evaluated in the random-phase approximation can be described by ring approximation calculations. Different effective interactions based on a standard model of the type g'+V_pi+V_rho are employed. For each momentum transfer, we have obtained the value of g'_0 permitting the ring response to match the position of the peak and/or the non-energy weighted sum rule provided by the random-phase approach has been obtained. It is found that, in general, it is not possible to reproduce both magnitudes simultaneously for a given g'_0 value.

## Key claim

The ring approximation (RA) cannot consistently reproduce the full random-phase approximation (RPA) transverse nuclear response for quasielastic electron scattering when using a standard effective interaction of the type g' + V_pi + V_rho, because it is generally impossible to find a single value of the Landau parameter g'_0 that simultaneously matches both the peak position and the non-energy weighted sum rule of the RPA response.

## Method

The authors performed RPA calculations for the transverse nuclear response of 40Ca in the quasielastic region using a nuclear matter formalism with a zero-range Landau-Migdal force plus finite-range pion and rho meson exchange potentials. They then performed ring approximation (RA) calculations with the same finite-range interaction but varied the g'_0 parameter of the zero-range piece, extracting the values that separately reproduce the RPA peak position and the RPA non-energy weighted sum rule at each momentum transfer. The procedure was repeated for two modified interactions: one omitting the pion exchange potential (V^II) and one omitting both the pion exchange and the tensor part of the rho exchange (V^III).

## Key numbers

* For the full interaction V^I at q=300 MeV/c, the g'_0 value needed to match the RPA peak position was 0.697, while the value needed to match the RPA sum rule was 0.827; the RPA calculation itself used g'_0=0.76.
* At q=400 MeV/c, the peak-matching g'_0 was 0.774 and the sum-rule-matching g'_0 was 0.778, showing near agreement at this momentum transfer.
* At q=550 MeV/c, the peak-matching g'_0 was 0.869 and the sum-rule-matching g'_0 was 0.755.
* For interaction V^II (no pion exchange), a single g'_0 value of 0.505 provided reasonable simultaneous agreement for both magnitudes in the q range 300-500 MeV/c, compared to the RPA value of g'_0=0.64.
* The zero-range parameters for the RPA calculations were fixed by reproducing the energies and B-values of two 1+ states in 208Pb at 5.85 and 7.30 MeV, with C_0=386 MeV fm^3.

## Context

This work extends the comparison of ring approximation and random-phase approximation calculations for nuclear responses, following earlier work by Shigehara et al. (1989) who found that RA could simulate RPA for a G-matrix interaction with weak momentum dependence in the exchange channel. The present study shows that for the standard g' + V_pi + V_rho model, the RA cannot consistently reproduce full RPA results, and that the pion exchange potential plays a crucial role in the discrepancy because it contributes to RPA through exchange terms but gives zero contribution to RA in nuclear matter. The results enable future work to properly account for exchange terms when calculating quasielastic responses, rather than relying on the common but incorrect assumption that RA with adjusted Landau parameters can substitute for full RPA.
