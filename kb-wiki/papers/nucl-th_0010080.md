# Electromagnetic isoscalar rho-pi-gamma exchange current and the anomalous action

> Using modern data, we first refit constants needed to complete an anomalous pi-rho-omega-a_1 Lagrangian obtained within the approach of hidden local symmetries. Then we derive from this Lagrangian electromagnetic isoscalar rho-pi-gamma and rho-a_1-gamma currents needed in calculations of the deuteron electromagnetic form factors at large momentum transfers.

## Key claim

Using modern data, the authors refit the four constants of an anomalous π-ρ-ω-a₁ Lagrangian derived from hidden local symmetries, and then derive from it the electromagnetic isoscalar ρπγ and ρa₁γ exchange currents needed for deuteron form factor calculations at large momentum transfers. The key qualifier is that the sign of the constants remains uncertain, and the extracted constants are known only within a 10–15% accuracy, with the effective couplings g_{ρπγ} and g_{ωπγ} suffering only about 4% uncertainty from inconsistencies in the radiative decay data for charged and neutral ρ mesons and the ω meson.

## Method

The authors first extracted the four constants (c̃₇, c̃₈, c̃₉, c̃₁₀) of the anomalous Lagrangian by fitting to modern data on the radiative decays ρ → πγ, ω → πγ, f₁ → ργ, and the decay f₁ → ρππ, using the constant c̃₇ from KKW (2000). They then used this Lagrangian to derive the electromagnetic form factors K_{ρπγ}(q²) for the ρπγ vertex and K₁(q²), K₂(q²) for the ρa₁γ vertex, and constructed the associated isoscalar exchange currents J_{πμ} and J_{a₁μ} in both relativistic and non-relativistic forms, including relativistic corrections, boost currents, and retardation currents.

## Key numbers

- c̃₇ = 8.64 × 10⁻³ (from g_{ρωπ} = 1.2, using g ≈ 6 from KSFR relation with f_π = 92 MeV and m_ρ = 770 MeV)
- c̃₉ = 9.23 × 10⁻³ (weighted value from charged ρ and ω decays); alternative value c̃₉ = 1.00 × 10⁻² (from neutral ρ⁰ decay)
- c̃₈ = –1.02 × 10⁻¹ (from Γ_{f₁→ρππ} = 2.808 ± 0.360 MeV)
- c̃₁₀ = 1.29 × 10⁻¹ (from Γ_{f₁→ργ} = 1.296 ± 0.288 MeV)
- g_{ρπγ} = 0.585 (from charged decays) or 0.610 (from neutral decays); g_{ωπγ} = 1.78 or 1.86
- Γ_{ρ±→π±γ} = 72.5 keV, Γ_{ω→π⁰γ} = 689.6 keV (using weighted c̃₉); ratio Γ_{ω→π⁰γ}/Γ_{ρ±→π±γ} = 9.5
- Γ_{ω→π⁰γ} = 751 keV, Γ_{ρ⁰→π⁰γ} = 79.4 keV (using c̃₉ = 1.00 × 10⁻²); ratio = 9.5
- g_{f₁ργ} = 1.84; g_{ρa₁γ} = g_{f₁ργ}/3

## Context

This work extends the anomalous Lagrangian approach of Kaiser and Meissner (1997) and Smejkal, Truhlik, and Khanna (1997) by refitting the constants with modern data from the Particle Data Group (2000) and KKW (2000). The resulting ρπγ exchange current differs from the standard approach of Adler (1975), Chemtob, Moniz, and Rho (1974), and Gari and Hyuga (1976) by including an additional momentum-dependent form factor K_{ρπγ}(q²), and introduces a completely new ρa₁γ current. The work enables more complete calculations of deuteron electromagnetic form factors at high momentum transfers, with the new ρa₁γ current and relativistic corrections that were previously absent or treated less rigorously.
