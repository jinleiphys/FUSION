# A benchmark of initial state models for heavy-ion collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}=$ 27 and 62 GeV

> Description of relativistic heavy-ion collisions at the energies of RHIC Beam Energy Scan program with fluid dynamic approach poses several challenges, one of which being a complex geometry and a longer duration of the pre-hydrodynamic stage. Therefore, existing fluid dynamic models for heavy-ion collisions at the RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies rely on rather complex initial states, such as UrQMD cascade or multi-fluid dynamics. In this study, we show that functionally simpler, non-dynamical initial states can be employed for the fluid dynamical simulations of Au-Au collisions at $\mbox{$\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}$}=27$ and 62.4~GeV. We adapt the initial states based on Monte Carlo Glauber model (GLISSANDO 2) and $\sqrt{T_A T_B}$ ansatz based on reduced thickness (T$_{\rm R}$ENTo $p=0$), extended into the longitudinal direction and finite baryon density. We find that both initial states, when coupled to a 3D event-by-event viscous fluid dynamic + cascade model, result in an overall fair reproduction of basic experimental data: pseudorapidity distributions, transverse momentum spectra and elliptic flow, at both collision energies. This is a rather surprising, given that the $\sqrt{T_A T_B}$ ansatz is functionally similar to the EKRT and IP-Glasma models, which are successful at much larger energies and rely on a partonic picture of the initial state.

## Key claim

The authors show that functionally simple, non-dynamical initial state models (Monte Carlo Glauber via GLISSANDO 2 and the reduced thickness T$_{\rm R}$ENTo $p=0$ ansatz) can be employed for fluid dynamic simulations of Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}=27$ and 62.4 GeV, producing an overall fair reproduction of basic experimental data, which is surprising because the $\sqrt{T_A T_B}$ ansatz is functionally similar to the EKRT and IP-Glasma models that rely on a partonic picture and are successful only at much higher energies.

## Method

The authors coupled three different initial state models (UrQMD cascade, Monte Carlo Glauber via GLISSANDO 2, and reduced thickness via T$_{\rm R}$ENTo $p=0$) to a 3D event-by-event viscous fluid dynamic code (vHLLE) followed by a hadronic cascade (UrQMD). The GLISSANDO and T$_{\rm R}$ENTo initial states were extended into the longitudinal direction with finite baryon density profiles, and the total energy and baryon number were fixed to the participant region values. The hydrodynamic evolution used the Israel-Stewart framework with a chiral model equation of state, and particlization occurred at a fixed energy density of 0.5 GeV/fm$^3$.

## Key numbers

- Hydrodynamization time $\tau_0 = 1.0$ fm/c for $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}=27$ GeV and $\tau_0 = 0.7$ fm/c for $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}=62.4$ GeV
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio $\eta/s = 0.12$ for $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}=27$ GeV and $\eta/s = 0.08$ for $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}=62.4$ GeV
- Mixing factor $\alpha = 0.123$ for $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}=27$ GeV and $\alpha = 0.132$ for $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}=62.4$ GeV, regulating participant versus binary collision contributions in the GLISSANDO initial state
- Gaussian smearing width $\sigma = 0.4$ fm for the transverse density deposition in GLISSANDO and T$_{\rm R}$ENTo initial states
- Particlization energy density $\epsilon_\text{sw} = 0.5$ GeV/fm$^3$

## Context

This work extends the 3+1D viscous hydrodynamic framework of Karpenko (2015), which previously used only the UrQMD cascade as an initial state, to include simpler geometrical initial conditions at RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies. The study contradicts the expectation that complex pre-hydrodynamic dynamics (as in UrQMD or multi-fluid models) are mandatory at lower energies, and enables the use of computationally simpler initial states for systematic studies of the RHIC BES program, including future comparisons at GSI FAIR and JINR NICA.
