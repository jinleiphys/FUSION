# The quark condensate at finite temperature

> The temperature evolution of the quark condensate is studied using three different methods. In the spirit of a many-body approach we make an expansion in the scalar density up to second order. Our result is consistent chiral perturbation theory to two-loop order.

## Key claim

The temperature evolution of the quark condensate, expanded to second order in the scalar density of quasi-pions, is independent of the particular choice of pion field representation (the function \(F(X)\) in the nonlinear chiral Lagrangian) and is consistent with chiral perturbation theory to two-loop order, but only when the residue \(\gamma(T)\) is expanded to first order in the coupling constant \(\lambda\).

## Method

The authors use three methods based on the nonlinear sigma model Lagrangian. The first method computes the grand potential (free energy density) from the interacting pion Green's function and applies the Feynman-Hellmann theorem to extract the condensate ratio. The second method directly evaluates the thermal expectation value of the chiral symmetry breaking Hamiltonian. The third method, valid for the PCAC choice of pion field, relates the condensate to the soft-pion self energy via an energy-weighted sum rule and a generalized Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation. All three methods are carried out to second order in the thermal loop integral \(j(T)\), which is proportional to the scalar density.

## Key numbers

* The leading-order contribution to the condensate ratio from a free pion gas is \(1 - 6\lambda j(T)\), where \(\lambda = 1/(6f_\pi^2)\) and \(j(T)\) is the thermal loop integral.
* In the chiral limit (\(m_\pi \to 0\)), where \(j(T) = T^2/8\), the condensate ratio to order \(T^4\) is \(1 - T^2/(8 f_\pi^2) - T^4/(384 f_\pi^4)\), matching the chiral perturbation theory result of Gasser and Leutwyler (1987).
* The effective pion mass squared is \(\tilde m_\pi^2 = m_\pi^2 [1 + (80\alpha - 10/3)\lambda j] / [1 + (80\alpha - 16/3)\lambda j]\), which vanishes in the chiral limit at all temperatures.
* The temperature-dependent residue of the pion propagator is \(\gamma(T) = [1 + (40\alpha - 8/3)\lambda j]^{-1}\).
* From the generalized GOR relation, the in-medium pion decay constant is \(\tilde f_\pi = f_\pi (1 - 4\lambda j)\), in agreement with Gerber and Leutwyler (1989).

## Context

This work extends the many-body approach of Bunatian and Wambach (1994) to explicitly verify the independence of the condensate on the pion field representation, a property previously established for cold baryonic matter by Delorme, Chanfray, and Ericson (1996) and by Kirchbach and Wirzba (1996). The results are consistent with the chiral perturbation theory calculations of Gasser and Leutwyler (1987) and Gerber and Leutwyler (1989) to two-loop order. The next stage enabled by this work is the introduction of rho meson exchange in the \(\pi\pi\) interaction while preserving chiral properties.
