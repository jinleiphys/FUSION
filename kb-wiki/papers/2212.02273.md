# Influence of hyperon-hyperon interaction on the properties of neutron stars

> The properties of neutron stars are studied in a composite model of the strong interaction. In the regime of low to medium baryonic densities a covariant hadronic model is adopted which includes an exclusive channel for the hyperon-hyperon interaction mediated by hidden strangeness mesons, which in turn couple to other mesons through polynomial vertices. The new coupling constants are subject to phenomenological constraints. The presence of free quarks in the core of the star is considered by using the Nambu-Jona Lasinio model supplemented with a vector interaction. The deconfinement process is described by a continuous coexistence of phases. Several structure parameters of neutron stars, such as mass-radius relation, moment of inertia, tidal deformability, and the propagation of nonradial f and g-modes within the relativistic Cowling approximation are studied. The predictions of the model are in good agreement with recent observational data, in particular the maximum inertial mass is greater than the observational lower limit of two solar masses.

# Influence of hyperon-hyperon interaction on the properties of neutron stars

## Key claim
The model predicts a maximum neutron star mass greater than two solar masses, but this result depends on tuning the hyperon-hyperon coupling constant \(G_{\sigma \xi}\) to negative values (specifically \(G_{\sigma \xi} = -100\)), and on choosing a relatively low vector coupling \(G_v = 0.08\,G\) in the Nambu-Jona Lasinio quark model to delay deconfinement and keep the equation of state stiff enough.

## Method
A composite model of strong interaction was constructed: for low to medium baryonic densities, a covariant hadronic mean-field model (Quantum Hadrodynamics) was used, including the full baryon octet, hidden strangeness mesons (\(\xi\), \(\phi\)) for hyperon-hyperon interactions, and polynomial meson-meson vertices (including \(\sigma\)-\(\xi\) mixing). For high densities, the Nambu-Jona Lasinio model with vector interaction described deconfined quark matter. A continuous Gibbs construction was used for the hadron-quark phase transition. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations were solved to obtain mass-radius relations, and the relativistic Cowling approximation was used to compute nonradial f and g-mode oscillation frequencies.

## Key numbers
- Maximum inertial mass \(M_\text{max}/M_\odot = 1.94,\; 1.96,\; 1.98\) for \(G_{\sigma \xi} = 0,\,-20,\,-60\) respectively, with the final model reaching \(M_\text{max} \gtrsim 2\,M_\odot\).
- Normal nuclear density \(n_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) used as reference; binding energy \(E_\text{bind} = -16\) MeV, symmetry energy \(E_\text{sym} = 32\) MeV, nuclear compressibility \(K = 230\) MeV, slope parameter \(L = 50\) MeV adopted at that density.
- Hyperon single-particle potentials in nuclear matter: \(U_\Lambda = -30\) MeV, \(U_\Sigma = 30\) MeV, \(U_\Xi = -18\) MeV.
- Constraint from double-\(\Lambda\) hypernucleus: \(U_\Lambda^{(\Lambda)}(n_0/5) = -0.67\) MeV.
- Tidal deformability upper bound from GW170817: \(\Lambda_{1.4} \leq 970\) (low spin) or \(\Lambda_{1.4} = 190^{+390}_{-120}\).
- Observed mass-radius for PSR J0030+0451: \(M = 1.34^{+0.15}_{-0.16} M_\odot\), \(R = 12.71^{+1.14}_{-1.19}\) km; for PSR J0740+6620: \(M = 2.072^{+0.067}_{-0.066} M_\odot\), \(R = 12.39^{+1.30}_{-0.98}\) km.
- Radius of a \(1.4 M_\odot\) star constrained to \(11.4\) km \(< R < 13.1\) km.
- Deconfinement threshold density set to \(n \geq 4\, n_0\) by choosing \(G_v = 0.08\, G\).

## Context
This work extends the author's previous study (Aguirre, 2020) by fully including hyperon couplings to the \(\xi\) and \(\delta\) mesons, and by adding polynomial \(\sigma\)-\(\xi\) mixing vertices. It addresses the "hyperon puzzle", the difficulty of reaching \(2 M_\odot\) when hyperons soften the equation of state, by introducing a repulsive hyperon-hyperon interaction via hidden strangeness mesons. The model is compared to observational constraints from GW170817 (Abbott et al., 2017, 2019), PSR J0030+0451 (Riley et al., 2019), and PSR J0740+6620 (Riley et al., 2021). The approach enables future studies of how hyperon-hyperon interactions affect neutron star oscillation modes and tidal deformability, and can be contrasted with models using only nucleons (e.g., MS0 by Mueller and Serot, 1996; NL3 by Lalazissis et al., 1997) or first-order phase transitions.
