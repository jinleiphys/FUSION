# Effective Field Theory of Neutron Star Superfluidity

> We apply effective field theory and renormalization group techniques to the problem of Cooper pair formation in neutron stars. Simple analytical expressions for the $^1 S_0$ condensate are derived which are free of nuclear potential model dependencies. The condensate is evaluated using phase shift data from neutron-neutron scattering.

## Key claim

The paper derives a simple analytical expression for the $^1S_0$ neutron superfluid gap in neutron stars that is free of nuclear potential model dependencies, but the result includes an undetermined constant prefactor that must be fixed by matching to the known low-density limit of a neutron gas.

## Method

The authors construct an effective field theory of neutrons near the Fermi surface using Wilsonian renormalization group techniques, integrating out all degrees of freedom except the Cooper pairing four-neutron operator. The running coupling evolves according to a one-loop beta function, and the superfluid gap is identified with the scale of a Landau pole in this running coupling. The initial value of the coupling is obtained by matching to $^1S_0$ neutron-neutron phase shift data from the Nijmegen partial wave analysis via an exact expression for the in-medium scattering amplitude.

## Key numbers

- The superfluid gap expression is $\Delta = \frac{8}{e^2} \frac{p_F^2}{2m_*} e^{-\frac{\pi}{2} \cot\delta}$, where $p_F$ is the Fermi momentum, $m_*$ is the effective neutron mass, and $\delta$ is the $^1S_0$ phase shift.
- The prefactor $\frac{8}{e^2}$ is determined by matching to the low-density limit calculation of Khodel et al., with $\Lambda = \frac{8}{e^2} \epsilon_F$ where $\epsilon_F = p_F^2 / 2m_*$ is the Fermi energy.
- The maximum gap size from calculations with induced potential effects is of order 1 MeV, while calculations using a bare potential produce a maximum gap of approximately 3 MeV.
- The non-relativistic parameter $p_F^2 / m^2$ is at most of order a few percent in the system of interest.

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to Fermi liquids developed by Polchinski (1992) and applied to nuclear systems by others, providing a rigorous justification for BCS-like expressions for the superfluid gap that depend on Fermi liquid parameters. The result is intermediate between calculations with induced potential effects by Wambach et al. (1993), Clark et al. (1995), and Schulze et al. (1996), which produce maximum gaps of order 1 MeV, and those using bare potentials by Schulze et al. (1996) and Khodel et al. (1998), which produce maximum gaps of approximately 3 MeV. The method can be extended to study condensates of higher angular momentum and enables first-principles calculations of the gap using in-medium phase shift data.
