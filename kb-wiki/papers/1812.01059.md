# Towards the P-wave nucleon-pion scattering amplitude in the $Δ(1232)$ channel

> We use lattice QCD and the Lüscher method to study elastic pion-nucleon scattering in the isospin $I = 3/2$ channel, which couples to the $Δ(1232)$ resonance. Our $N_f=2+1$ flavor lattice setup features a pion mass of $m_π\approx 250$ MeV, such that the strong decay channel $Δ\rightarrow πN$ is close to the threshold. We present our method for constructing the required lattice correlation functions from single- and two-hadron interpolating fields and their projection to irreducible representations of the relevant symmetry group of the lattice. We show preliminary results for the energy spectra in selected moving frames and irreducible representations, and extract the scattering phase shifts. Using a Breit-Wigner fit, we also determine the resonance mass $m_Δ$ and the $g_{Δ-πN}$ coupling.

## Key claim

Using lattice QCD with a pion mass of approximately 250 MeV, the authors present preliminary results for the elastic pion-nucleon scattering amplitude in the isospin I=3/2 channel, extracting the Delta(1232) resonance mass and coupling, but note that the analysis uses only one-third of the total available configurations and neglects partial-wave mixing.

## Method

The authors use Nf=2+1 flavor lattice QCD with the Luscher method to study elastic pion-nucleon scattering. They construct correlation matrices from single- and two-hadron interpolating fields (pion, nucleon, and Delta) projected to irreducible representations of the relevant symmetry groups on the lattice, using smeared forward, sequential, and stochastic propagators. The energy spectra are extracted in selected moving frames and irreducible representations, and the scattering phase shifts are obtained, from which a Breit-Wigner fit determines the resonance mass and coupling.

## Key numbers

* Pion mass: m_pi = 258.3(1.1) MeV (lattice ensemble)
* Preliminary Delta resonance mass: m_Delta = 1414(36) MeV
* Preliminary Delta-pi-N coupling: g_Delta-pi-N = 26(7)
* Physical Delta(1232) decay width in Npi: Gamma = 112.4(5) MeV (from PDG)
* Physical Delta mass: 1232(1) MeV (from PDG)
* Physical g_Delta-pi-N: 29.4(3) or 28.6(3) (from PDG)

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of the Delta resonance using the Luscher method by Verduci (2014) and Andersen (2017), as well as studies using the Michael-McNeile method by Alexandrou (2013, 2015). The authors note that none of the previous studies fully accounted for partial-wave mixing, which they also neglect in this preliminary work but plan to include in the full calculation. The results enable future more precise determinations of the Delta resonance parameters with increased statistics, larger lattice volumes, and inclusion of non-resonant contributions from other angular momenta.
