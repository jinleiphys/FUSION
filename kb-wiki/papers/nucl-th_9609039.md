# Confinement, Diquarks and Goldstone's theorem

> Determinations of the gluon propagator in the continuum and in lattice simulations are compared. A systematic truncation procedure for the quark Dyson-Schwinger and bound state Bethe-Salpeter equations is described. The procedure ensures the flavour-octet axial-vector Ward identity is satisfied order-by-order, thereby guaranteeing the preservation of Goldstone's theorem; and identifies a mechanism that simultaneously ensures the absence of diquarks in QCD and their presence in QCD$^{N_c=2}$, where the colour singlet diquark is the ``baryon'' of the theory.

## Key claim
The paper argues that a systematic truncation procedure for the quark Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations, which preserves the flavour-octet axial-vector Ward identity order-by-order, simultaneously explains the absence of diquark bound states in QCD with three colours and their presence as baryons in QCD with two colours, contingent on the cancellation of attractive and repulsive terms at higher orders.

## Method
The author compares continuum Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) studies of the gluon propagator with lattice simulation results, focusing on the infrared behaviour. A skeleton graph expansion of the dressed quark-gluon vertex is used to construct a systematic truncation scheme for the quark DSE and meson/diquark Bethe-Salpeter equations (BSEs), ensuring the axial-vector Ward identity is satisfied order-by-order. The analysis then examines the colour algebra of the repulsive and attractive terms at O(g^4) in the diquark BSE, and checks the mechanism by applying it to QCD with N_c=2.

## Key numbers
- The two-loop perturbative result for the gluon propagator D_T(k^2) is quantitatively reliable for k^2 greater than or approximately 1-2 GeV^2.
- In the IR-enhanced gluon propagator form, the parameter A = 0.44 GeV; in the IR-vanishing form, b = 0.34 GeV; in the massive-boson form, M = 0.13 GeV.
- Lattice data from a 24^3 x 40 lattice with 500 configurations at beta=6.0 finds D_T(k^2=0) is finite and nonzero, with the first 3 data points at k^2 < 0.34 GeV^2 excluded from the fit to achieve chi^2 approximately 1.
- The O(g^4) crossed-box term in the diquark equation is five times stronger than in the meson equation, relative to the vertex correction contributions.

## Context
This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach to QCD, building on prior studies of the gluon propagator by Baker, Ball, and Zachariasen (1989), Alekseev (1995), and lattice simulations by Bernard, Parrinello, and Soni (1994) and Marenzoni, Martinelli, and Stella (1995). The truncation procedure systematizes the rainbow-ladder approximation, which had been phenomenologically successful for ground-state pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector mesons but failed for scalar mesons and diquarks. The mechanism enables future studies of diquark correlations in QCD and predicts an identical spectrum of mesons and baryons (diquarks) in QCD with two colours, including a massless scalar baryon as a consequence of Goldstone's theorem.
