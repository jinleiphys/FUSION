# On differential spectra in the reaction $pp\to K^+\bar K^0 d$ in the nearthreshold region

> The differential spectra in the reaction $pp\to K^+\bar K^0 d$ for different reasonable versions of the production amplitude are discussed at the initial proton energy $T_p=2.645$ GeV.

## Key claim

The paper argues that at the near-threshold proton energy \(T_p = 2.645\) GeV, the \(K^+\bar K^0\)-mass spectrum is highly sensitive to the choice of partial-wave amplitude, but separating a possible \(a^+_0(980)\) resonance signal from nonresonance production is difficult because the two mechanisms produce distributions that are "not drastically different."

## Method

The authors performed a model-independent partial-wave analysis of the reaction \(pp\to K^+\bar K^0 d\) at a fixed incident kinetic energy \(T_p = 2.645\) GeV, considering only the lowest allowed partial waves (\(L_k=1, L_q=0\) and \(L_k=0, L_q=1\)) due to parity, angular momentum, isospin conservation, and the Pauli principle. They constructed general production amplitudes for each case, including a constant-amplitude approximation (which is forbidden by conservation laws) and a resonance amplitude for the \(a^+_0\) meson using a Flatte-type propagator with parameters from prior work. Differential cross sections for the \(K^+\bar K^0\)-mass and \(\bar K^0 d\)-mass spectra were then computed and compared.

## Key numbers

- The incident proton kinetic energy is \(T_p = 2.645\) GeV.
- The \(Q\)-value (excess energy) is \(Q \approx 45.9\) MeV.
- The allowed \(K\bar K\)-mass interval is \(991.4 < M_{K\bar K} < 1037.3\) MeV.
- The expected width of the \(a_0\) meson is \(\Gamma_a \sim 80\) MeV.
- The \(a^+_0\) meson parameters used are \(m_a = 998\) MeV, \(g_1 = 0.243\), and \(g = 0.221\), taken from BNL (1998).

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of scalar meson production in nucleon-nucleon collisions, specifically the reaction \(pp\to a^+_0 d\) studied by Grishina et al. (2000) using a meson exchange model, and addresses the experimental program of the ANKE collaboration at COSY (Jülich). The paper enables future experimental analysis by showing that the \(K^+\bar K^0\)-mass spectrum is the most sensitive observable for distinguishing resonance versus nonresonance production, while the \(\bar K^0 d\)-mass spectrum is more influenced by final-state interactions (as discussed by Oset et al., 1999) and may test the molecular hypothesis of the \(a_0\) meson proposed by Weinstein and Isgur (1990).
