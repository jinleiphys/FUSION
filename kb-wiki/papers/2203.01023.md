# Nuclear Structure with Discrete Non-Orthogonal Shell-Model : new frontiers

> We present developments and applications for the diagonalization of shell-model hamiltonians in a discrete non-orthogonal basis (DNO-SM). The method, and its actual numerical implementation CARINA, based on mean-field and beyond-mean field techniques has already been applied in previous studies and is focused on basis states selection optimization. The method is benchmarked against a full set of $sd$ shell exact diagonalizations, and is applied for the first time to the heavy deformed $^{254}$No nucleus.

## Key claim

The Discrete Non-Orthogonal Shell-Model (DNO-SM) can reproduce full sd-shell exact diagonalizations for ground and low-lying excited states, but the method's truncation of the basis via variational minimization may omit states relevant for nuclear properties not included in the predefined set of angular momenta, leading to underbinding of order 0.1–1.0 MeV in ground-state energies.

## Method

The authors diagonalize valence shell-model Hamiltonians in a discrete non-orthogonal basis generated by Projected Constrained Hartree-Fock (PCHF) states, using quadrupole deformations (β, γ) as generator coordinates and cranking components ⟨Ĵ_z⟩ fixed in advance. The basis is truncated iteratively via a generalized minimization technique that selects CHF states maximizing the energy gain for a chosen set of nuclear states (e.g., J_α ∈ {0₁, 2₁,₂, 4₁, 8₁, 12₁, 16₁}), and the generalized eigenvalue problem is solved after transforming to the natural basis of the norm matrix.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy difference between DNO-SM and exact SM diagonalizations ranges from approximately 0.1 to 1.0 MeV for even Ne, Mg, and Si isotopes (see Figure labeled Egs).
- For ²⁰Ne: DNO-SM gives E_gs = −40.40 MeV vs. exact −40.47 MeV; B(E2; 2⁺₁ → 0⁺₁) = 47.0 e²·fm⁴ vs. 46.9 e²·fm⁴; B(E2; 4⁺₁ → 2⁺₁) = 56.3 e²·fm⁴ vs. 55.3 e²·fm⁴.
- For ²⁴Mg: DNO-SM gives E_gs = −86.82 MeV vs. exact −87.10 MeV; 2⁺₁ energy = 1.52 MeV vs. 1.50 MeV; 4⁺₁ energy = 4.37 MeV vs. 4.37 MeV.
- For ²⁸Si: DNO-SM gives E_gs = −135.54 MeV vs. exact −135.86 MeV; 2⁺₁ energy = 2.12 MeV vs. 1.93 MeV; 4⁺₁ energy = 4.78 MeV vs. 4.61 MeV.
- Number of CHF basis states N_q found by minimization ranges from 12 (²⁸Ne, ³⁶Ar) to 71 (²⁸Si) for the sd-shell nuclei studied.
- For ²⁴Mg, the full mesh of (β, γ, ⟨Ĵ_z⟩) contains 315 points; HF calculations found 277 converged solutions.

## Context

This work extends the line of merging mean-field techniques with shell-model diagonalization, following the pioneering work of Ripka (1968), the VAMPIR implementations, and more recently the Monte Carlo Shell-Model by the Tokyo group and the TAURUS numerical suite. The DNO-SM is benchmarked against exact sd-shell diagonalizations using the USDB effective interaction of Brown (2006), and is applied for the first time to the heavy deformed nucleus ²⁵⁴No, demonstrating the method's potential for extending shell-model calculations to mass regions beyond A ∼ 150 where conventional diagonalization becomes intractable.
