# Diverse, massive-star-associated sources for elements heavier than Fe and the roles of neutrinos

> Massive-star-associated models for production of elements heavier than Fe are reviewed. The important roles of neutrinos in many of these models are discussed along with uncertainties in the relevant neutrino physics. Data on elemental abundances in metal-poor stars are presented and their constraints on diverse sources for elements heavier than Fe are emphasized.

## Key claim

This review argues that multiple distinct massive-star-associated sources, not just the classical rapid neutron-capture (r) process, are required to explain the full inventory of elements heavier than iron, and that neutrinos play a crucial but uncertain role in many of these sources. The paper emphasizes that the neutrino-driven wind from protoneutron stars, long considered a primary r-process site, is now understood to typically produce only elements up to A ~ 110 under standard conditions, with production of heavier r-process nuclei (A ≳ 130) requiring special conditions such as very low electron fraction (Ye ≲ 0.4), high entropy, or rapid expansion.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical models of nucleosynthesis for elements heavier than iron in massive-star-associated environments, including: (1) hot r-process scenarios based on adiabatic expansion from nuclear statistical equilibrium (NSE) in neutrino-driven winds from protoneutron stars, (2) other core-collapse supernova ejecta scenarios including black-hole accretion disk winds and magnetohydrodynamic jets, (3) neutron star merger models, and (4) neutrino-induced nucleosynthesis in helium shells as a cold r-process scenario. The review evaluates each model against constraints from observations of elemental abundances in metal-poor stars and discusses how neutrino physics (luminosities, spectra, flavor oscillations) determines the electron fraction and neutron-to-seed ratio in these environments.

## Key numbers

- Neutron density required for an r process: n_n ≳ 10^18 cm^-3, using nominal values of neutron-capture rate coefficient ⟨vσ_n,γ⟩ ~ 10^-17 cm^3 s^-1 and β-decay rate λ_β ~ 10 s^-1
- Typical neutrino-driven wind parameters: Ye ~ 0.4–0.5, entropy S_0 ~ 10–100 (in units of k per baryon), dynamic timescale τ_dyn ~ 0.01–0.1 s
- For a neutron-rich wind (Ye < 0.5), the condition λ_ν_e n / λ_ν̄_e p < 1 requires ε_ν̄_e − ε_ν_e > 4Δ, where Δ = 1.293 MeV is the neutron-proton mass difference
- The quantity B_0 that determines NSE composition is given by B_0 = T_10 [7.36 + 0.862 ln(S_γ / T_10^3/2)] MeV, where T_10 is temperature in units of 10^10 K and S_γ is photon entropy per baryon
- For T_10 ~ 1 and S_γ ≳ 10, NSE composition predominantly consists of free nucleons; for T_10 ~ 1 and S_γ ~ 1, α particles are favored; for T_10 ~ 1 and S_γ ≲ 0.1, both α particles and heavier nuclei are favored
- The highest binding energy per nucleon among all nuclei is 9.43 MeV for ^4He; the peak for heavier nuclei is 8.95 MeV for ^56Fe, decreasing to ~8 MeV for A ~ 190 and 7.60 MeV for ^238U
- Production of r-process nuclei with A ≳ 130 in winds from a ~2 M_⊙ protoneutron star requires Ye ≲ 0.4
- The charged-current reaction ^4He(ν̄_e, e^+ n)^3H as a neutron source has a threshold of 21.6 MeV
- The outer He shell for neutrino-induced nucleosynthesis is at radii r ~ 10^10 cm with temperature T_9 ~ 0.1

## Context

This review extends earlier reviews of r-process nucleosynthesis (Cowan et al. 1991, Qian 2003, Arnould et al. 2007) and the s-process (Kappeler et al. 2011) by focusing on the diversity of massive-star-associated sources and the critical roles of neutrinos. It discusses how the neutrino-driven wind model, originally inspired by Woosley et al. (1992) and Meyer et al. (1992), has been found insufficient for producing heavy r-process nuclei under typical conditions (Hoffman et al. 1997, Freiburghaus et al. 1999), leading to consideration of alternative scenarios including neutron star mergers (Freiburghaus et al. 1999, Goriely et al. 2011) and the νp process in proton-rich winds (Frohlich et al. 2006). The review enables future work by identifying specific neutrino physics uncertainties (flavor oscillations, medium effects on opacities) and progenitor structure issues that must be resolved to determine which sources dominate the r-process enrichment observed in metal-poor stars.
