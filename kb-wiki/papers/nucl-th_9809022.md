# Antikaon Production and Medium Effects in Heavy Ion Collisions at AGS

> Antikaon production from heavy ion collisions at energies available from the Alternating Gradient Synchrotron (AGS) at the Brookhaven National Laboratory is studied in a relativistic transport (ART) model. We include contributions from the baryon-baryon, meson-baryon, and meson-meson interactions. The final-state interaction of antikaons via both absorption and elastic scattering by nucleons and pions are also considered. To compare with presently available or future experimental data, we have calculated the antikaon rapidity and transverse momentum distributions as well as its collective flow. Medium effects on these observables due to mean field potentials have also been investigated. It is found that the ratio of antikaon transverse momentum spectrum to that of kaon and their transverse flow are most sensitive to the in-medium properties of kaons and antikaons.

## Key claim

The ratio of antikaon transverse momentum spectrum to that of kaon and their transverse flow are the most sensitive observables to the in-medium properties of kaons and antikaons, but the medium effects on antikaons are found to be almost independent of beam energy in the range of 2 to 16 GeV/nucleon, contrary to what might be expected from the energy dependence of the mean field potential.

## Method

The authors extended the relativistic transport (ART) model to include antikaon production from baryon-baryon, meson-baryon, and meson-meson interactions, as well as antikaon absorption and elastic scattering by nucleons and pions. They implemented an attractive mean field potential for antikaons (and a repulsive one for kaons) and simulated Au+Au collisions at AGS energies, calculating rapidity distributions, transverse mass spectra, and collective flow with and without these potentials.

## Key numbers

* In Au+Au collisions at p_beam/A = 11.6 GeV/c and impact parameter b = 4 fm, about 40% of produced K- come from meson-meson collisions, 40% from meson-baryon collisions, and 20% from baryon-baryon collisions.
* Close to half of the K- produced from baryon-baryon collisions are absorbed during propagation; about 40% from meson-baryon and meson-hyperon collisions are absorbed; about one fourth from meson-meson collisions are absorbed.
* The K+/K- ratio around mid-rapidity decreases from about 4.2 to about 3.5 from mid-rapidity to target/projectile rapidities without mean field; with mean field it increases from about 3.0 at mid-rapidity to about 4.0 at target/projectile rapidities.
* The mean field potential reduces the K- inverse slope parameter (apparent temperature) by about 20% across the beam energy range of 2 to 16 GeV/nucleon.
* At normal nuclear density, a kaon at rest has a repulsive potential of about 30 MeV; the K- potential is given by U(rho) = -0.12 (rho_b/rho_0) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous ART model study of K+ production and medium effects (Li and Ko, 1995; Li and Ko, 1996) to antikaons, and compares with results from other transport models such as RQMD (which found meson-baryon interactions dominant) and ARC (which found baryon-baryon interactions dominant). The study enables experimental tests of in-medium kaon and antikaon properties using transverse flow and K+/K- ratio measurements at AGS, and the authors note that preliminary data from the E866 collaboration are consistent with an attractive mean field potential for K- and a repulsive one for K+.
