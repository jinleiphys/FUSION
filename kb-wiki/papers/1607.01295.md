# Shell model studies of competing mechanisms to the neutrinoless double-beta decay in $^{124}$Sn, $^{130}$Te, and $^{136}$Xe

> Neutrinoless double-beta decay is a predicted beyond Standard Model process that could clarify some of the not yet known neutrino properties, such as the mass scale, the mass hierarchy, and its nature as a Dirac or Majorana fermion. Should this transition be observed, there are still challenges in understanding the underlying contributing mechanisms. We perform a detailed shell model investigation of several beyond Standard Model mechanisms that consider the existence of right-handed currents. Our analysis presents different venues that can be used to identify the dominant mechanisms for nuclei of experimental interest in the mass A$\sim$130 region ($^{124}$Sn, $^{130}$Te, and $^{136}$Xe). It requires an accurate knowledge of nine nuclear matrix elements that we calculate, in addition to the associated energy dependent phase-space factors.

## Key claim

The paper presents shell model calculations of nine nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for three isotopes in the A~130 region, showing that if the neutrinoless double-beta decay is observed, the shape of the two-electron angular distribution can distinguish the mass mechanism from the lambda or eta mechanisms, while the energy distribution can discriminate between the lambda and eta mechanisms, but only if tens of events are recorded and the unknown CP-violating phases do not wash out the differences.

## Method

The authors performed shell model calculations in the jj55 model space (0g7/2, 1d5/2, 1d3/2, 2s1/2, 0h11/2 valence orbitals) using the SVD Hamiltonian fine-tuned with experimental data from Sn isotopes. They calculated nine nuclear matrix elements for 124Sn, 130Te, and 136Xe using an optimal closure energy of 3.5 MeV, and combined these with energy-dependent phase-space factors to compute half-lives and two-electron angular and energy distributions for three theoretical scenarios: mass mechanism only, lambda mechanism dominance, and eta mechanism dominance.

## Key numbers

- For 124Sn: M_GT = 1.85, M_F = -0.47, M_T = 0.07 (dimensionless NME values)
- For 130Te: M_GT = 1.66, M_F = -0.44, M_T = 0.05 (dimensionless NME values)
- For 136Xe: M_GT = 1.50, M_F = -0.40, M_T = 0.07 (dimensionless NME values)
- Mass mechanism half-life for 124Sn: 2.73 x 10^26 years
- Mass mechanism half-life for 130Te: 2.12 x 10^26 years
- Mass mechanism half-life for 136Xe: 2.53 x 10^26 years
- Lambda mechanism half-life range for 124Sn: [0.78, 1.07] x 10^26 years (depending on CP phases)
- Eta mechanism half-life range for 130Te: [2.52, 5.05] x 10^25 years (depending on CP phases)
- Phase space factor G_1 for 124Sn: 1.977 x 10^14 yr^-1
- Phase space factor G_1 for 130Te: 3.122 x 10^14 yr^-1
- Phase space factor G_1 for 136Xe: 3.188 x 10^14 yr^-1

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous shell model analysis for 82Se (Horoi and Neacsu, 2016) to three isotopes of immediate experimental interest: 124Sn (under study by TIN.TIN), 130Te (CUORE and SNO+), and 136Xe (NEXT, EXO, and KamLAND-ZEN). The calculations use the same SVD Hamiltonian and formalism as the earlier 82Se study, and the NME results are consistent with previous calculations by the same group (Neacsu and Horoi, 2015). The paper enables experimental collaborations to interpret future neutrinoless double-beta decay observations by comparing measured two-electron angular and energy distributions to the predicted shapes for different beyond-Standard-Model mechanisms, though the authors note that distinguishing the mechanisms requires tens of detected events and may be beyond current experimental capabilities.
