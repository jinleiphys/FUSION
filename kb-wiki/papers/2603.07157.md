# Microscopic investigation of $γ~$ vibrational band structures in odd-mass nuclei

> A systematic investigation of the high-spin band structures observed in $^{103,105,107,109}$Nb and $^{103,105,107,109}$Tc nuclides is performed using the triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) approach. For $^{103,105}$Nb isotopes, four bands have been populated with the lowest three bands corresponding to yrast, $γ$ and 2$γ$ bands. The nature of the fourth observed band has remained unresolved as it has been shown from the transition intensity ratios that this band cannot correspond to the expected 3$γ$ band. It is demonstrated in the present work that this fourth band is the second $γ$ band, resulting from the combination, $K=K_0-2$ with $K_0$ being the "$K$" value of the parent configuration. The excitation energy and other properties of this band structure are predicted for all the studied nuclides.

# Microscopic investigation of γ vibrational band structures in odd-mass nuclei

## Key claim

The fourth excited band observed in ¹⁰³Nb and ¹⁰⁵Nb, previously unresolved and shown not to be the expected 3γ band based on transition intensity ratios, is identified as the second γ band (γ₂) arising from the K = K₀ − 2 combination, where K₀ is the K-value of the parent configuration; this assignment is made for all eight studied nuclides, with the 3γ band predicted to lie higher in excitation energy.

## Method

The triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) approach was employed, using triaxially-deformed Nilsson mean fields with pairing correlations treated in the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer approximation. Multi-quasiparticle configurations (one-proton, one-proton coupled to two-neutrons, three-protons, and three-protons coupled to two-neutrons) were constructed and projected onto good angular momentum using three-dimensional projection operators, followed by diagonalization of a Hamiltonian containing spherical single-particle, quadrupole-quadrupole, and pairing terms via the Hill-Wheeler method. Calculations were performed for ¹⁰³,¹⁰⁵,¹⁰⁷,¹⁰⁹Nb and ¹⁰³,¹⁰⁵,¹⁰⁷,¹⁰⁹Tc using deformation parameters (ε and γ) taken from earlier works.

## Key numbers

- For ¹⁰⁵Nb, the deviation between TPSM and experimental energy for the highest observed spin state I = 25/2⁺ is 18 keV.
- The tentative band head of the third excited band in ¹⁰⁵Nb is I = 9/2⁺ at an excitation energy of 1.687 MeV.
- Axial deformation parameters ε range from 0.208 (¹⁰⁷Nb) to 0.350 (¹⁰⁵Nb); triaxial deformation parameters γ range from 20° (¹⁰⁵Nb) to 28° (¹⁰³Nb).
- Monopole pairing strength parameters: G₁ = 20.25 and G₂ = 16.20, with G_Q ≈ 0.16 G_M.
- B(E2) transition probabilities (in eb²) from γ₁ to yrast band at 9/2 → 5/2 range from 0.062 (¹⁰⁷Tc) to 0.083 (¹⁰⁵Nb); from γ₂ to 2γ band at 9/2 → 13/2 range from 0.081 (¹⁰⁹Tc) to 0.108 (¹⁰⁹Nb).
- Effective charges used: eₙ = 0.5e and eₚ = 1.5e.
- The scaling factor for core subtraction is κ = 32.32 A⁻⁵/³.

## Context

This work extends earlier TPSM studies of γ bands in odd-mass nuclei, particularly the preliminary calculation for ¹⁰³Nb by Sheikh et al. (2010), and addresses the unresolved nature of the fourth band in ¹⁰³,¹⁰⁵Nb reported by Wang et al. (2009) and Li et al. (2005). It contrasts with the multi-phonon method (MPM) approach of PhysRevC.54.189, which predicted γ₂ bands to be lower in excitation energy than γ₁ bands in the A ∼ 162–168 region, whereas in the A ∼ 100 region the TPSM approach correctly predicts γ₁ as the first excited band. The identification of γ₂ bands in these odd-mass systems enables future experimental searches for the predicted low-spin states (I = 7/2, 5/2, 3/2, 1/2) not yet populated, and provides a framework for interpreting γ-band structures built on each intrinsic triaxial configuration across the isotopic chains.
