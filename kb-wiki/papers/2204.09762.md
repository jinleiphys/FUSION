# Effect of various particlization scenarios on anisotropic flow and particle production using UrQMD hybrid model

> We discuss the effect of various particlization scenarios available in the hybrid ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) event generator on different observables in non-central ($b$ $=$ 5--9 $fm$) Au + Au collisions in the beam energy range 1A-158A GeV. Particlization models switch fluid dynamic description to the transport description using various hypersurface criteria. In addition to particlization models, various equations-of-state (EoS) provided by the UrQMD hybrid model were employed. The observables examined in this paper include the excitation function of anisotropic coefficients such as directed ($v_{1}$) and elliptic flow ($v_{2}$), particle ratios of the species, and the shape of net-proton rapidity spectra at mid-rapidity. The results obtained here can help predict and compare the measurements provided by future experiments at the Facility for Antiproton and Ion Research (FAIR) and the Nuclotron-based Ion Collider fAcility (NICA) once the data becomes available. We also study the most suitable combination of the particlization model and EoS, which best describes the experimental measurements.

## Key claim

The iso-energy density (IEF) particlization scenario in the UrQMD hybrid model provides a compelling and qualitative description of experimental measurements of anisotropic flow and particle production across beam energies from 1A to 158A GeV, whereas the gradual (GF) and isochronous (ICF) scenarios often fail to reproduce the data, especially at high beam energies.

## Method

Non-central Au+Au collisions (impact parameter b = 5–9 fm, corresponding to approximately 10–40% centrality) were simulated using the hybrid UrQMD model for beam energies from 1A to 158A GeV. Three equations-of-state (hadron gas, chiral+deconfinement, and bag model) were each combined with three particlization scenarios: gradual slice-by-slice (GF), isochronous (ICF), and iso-energy density (IEF) using the Cornelius routine. The Cooper-Frye formalism was used to convert the fluid description to particles on the chosen hypersurface, after which the transport cascade continued.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy range: 1A–158A GeV for Au+Au collisions at impact parameter b = 5–9 fm
- Directed flow slope of protons shows no sensitivity to underlying degrees of freedom below 20A–30A GeV for all particlization scenarios
- Elliptic flow v₂ using the bag model shows non-monotonous behavior irrespective of particlization mode for both protons and pions
- In the ICF scenario, there is almost no anti-proton production even at higher beam energies, which is described as unrealistic
- Reduced curvature of net-proton rapidity spectra shows large negative values for ICF and large positive values for GF with the bag model EoS at high beam energies

## Context

This work extends previous studies by the same group (Rode 2019, Kundu 2021) that examined anisotropic flow and particle production using the UrQMD hybrid model with different equations-of-state, but without systematic comparison of particlization scenarios. The results are compared with experimental data from E895, NA49, STAR, and E917 collaborations. The findings are intended to guide the choice of simulation parameters for predictions at upcoming FAIR and NICA experiments, and the authors recommend the IEF scenario combined with either hadron gas or chiral EoS as the most realistic combination.
