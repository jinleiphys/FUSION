# Shell-model study of spectroscopies and isospin structures in odd-odd $N=Z$ nuclei employing realistic \emph{NN} potentials

> The structures of odd-odd $N=Z$ nuclei in the lower \emph{fp} shell have been investigated with a new isospin-nonconserving microscopical interaction. The interaction is derived from a high-precision charge-dependent Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential using the folded-diagram renormalization method. Excellent agreements with experimental data have been obtained up to band terminations. Particularly, the relative positions of T=0 and T=1 bands were well reproduced. Calculations with another interaction obtained from the Idaho-A chiral potential showed similar results. Our calculations also give a good description of the existence of high-spin isomeric states in the $0f_{7/2}$ sub-shell. As examples, the spectroscopies and isospin structures of $^{46}$V and $^{50}$Mn have been discussed in detail, with the useful predictions of level structures including electromagnetic properties. Results for $^{50}$Mn were analyzed where experimental observations are still insufficient.

# Shell-model study of spectroscopies and isospin structures in odd-odd $N=Z$ nuclei employing realistic *NN* potentials

## Key claim

The authors derive a new isospin-nonconserving effective interaction for the *fp* shell from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential using the folded-diagram renormalization method, and show that it reproduces the relative positions of $T=0$ and $T=1$ bands in odd-odd $N=Z$ nuclei "up to band terminations", though the largest discrepancy is seen for the $7^+_1$ state in $^{42}$Sc, where core-breaking effects not included in the model may be non-negligible.

## Method

The effective Hamiltonian is constructed by applying the folded-diagram expansion method to the high-precision charge-dependent Bonn (CD-Bonn) nucleon-nucleon potential, with the Brueckner $G$ matrix used to handle the short-range repulsion. The model space consists of the $0f_{7/2}$, $1p_{3/2}$, $0f_{5/2}$, and $1p_{1/2}$ sub-shells above a $^{40}$Ca core, and the $\hat{Q}$-boxes are approximated to third order. Shell-model diagonalizations are performed with the code OXBASH in proton/neutron formalism, and the Coulomb field is included so that isospin is not conserved in the Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- In $^{50}$Mn, the calculated $K^{\pi}=5^+$ ($T=0$) isomeric state lies at 322 keV excitation energy, compared to the experimental value of 229 keV (with a measured half-life of $T_{1/2}=1.75$ m).
- In $^{50}$Mn, the calculated lowest $1^+$ state of the $K^{\pi}=0^+$ ($T=0$) band appears at 661 keV, compared to the experimental value of 651 keV.
- In $^{50}$Mn, the calculated $5^+_2$ state ($T=0$) lies at 1813 keV, compared to the experimental assignment at 1917 keV.
- In $^{46}$V, a calculated $12^+_3$ state at 8309 keV corresponds to a tentatively assigned experimental $12^+$ state at 8267 keV, with isospin $T=1$.
- In $^{42}$Sc, calculations with the Idaho-A chiral potential give the $7^+$ ($T=0$) state lower than the $0^+$ state by about 47 keV.
- For the $K=5^+$ band in $^{50}$Mn, the calculated B(E2; $9^+_1 \rightarrow 7^+_1$) value (126 e$^2$fm$^4$ with SKcsb wave functions and standard effective charges) agrees with the experimental lower limit of $\geq 115^{+23}_{-19}$ e$^2$fm$^4$.
- In $^{46}$V, the calculated B(M1; $1^+_1 \rightarrow 0^+_1$) is 1.49 $\mu_N^2$, compared to the experimental lower limit of $\geq 0.77$ $\mu_N^2$.
- In $^{50}$Mn, the calculated B(M1; $3^+_1 \rightarrow 2^+_1$) is 2.94 $\mu_N^2$, compared to the experimental value of $2.9^{+10}_{-7}$ $\mu_N^2$.

## Context

This work extends the shell-model approach to odd-odd $N=Z$ nuclei in the *fp* shell by constructing a microscopically derived isospin-nonconserving interaction, in contrast to earlier isospin-conserving interactions such as the Kuo-Brown (KB) G matrix (Kuo 1968), KB3 (Caurier 2005), FPD6 (Richter 1991), and GXPF (Honma 2002). The results are compared with experimental data from O'Leary (1999, 2002), Garrett (2001), Pietralla (2002), and others. The study enables future work on isospin-symmetry breaking effects in super-allowed Fermi decays and provides a basis for testing the folded-diagram method in other *fp*-shell nuclei and in lighter shells (e.g., reproducing the $3^+$ ground state of $^{10}$B, a long-standing challenge for realistic interactions).
