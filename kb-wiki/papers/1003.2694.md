# Phase diagram of the D3/D5 system in a magnetic field and a BKT transition

> We study the full temperature and chemical potential dependence of the D3/D5 2+1 dimensional theory in the presence of a magnetic field. The theory displays separate transitions associated with chiral symmetry breaking and melting of the bound states. We display the phase diagram which has areas with first and second order transitions meeting at two critical points similar to that of the D3/D7 system. In addition there is the recently reported BKT transition at zero temperature leading to distinct structure at low temperatures.

## Key claim

The D3/D5 system in a magnetic field displays a phase diagram qualitatively similar to the D3/D7 system, with first and second order transitions meeting at two critical points; however, the chiral symmetry breaking transition at zero temperature is of the Berezinskii-Kosterlitz-Thouless (BKT) type rather than mean-field second order, and even infinitesimal temperature turns it into a mean-field type second order transition.

## Method

The authors use holography via a probe D5 brane in an AdS5 black hole geometry (the D3/D5 system) to model a 2+1 dimensional defect gauge theory with fundamental matter at finite temperature, chemical potential, and magnetic field. They solve the D5 brane equations of motion numerically using Mathematica, classifying embeddings as Minkowski (avoiding the black hole horizon) or black hole (touching the horizon), and compute the condensate and thermodynamic potentials to determine the preferred phase. The analysis is performed on constant temperature slices to map the full phase diagram in the temperature-chemical potential plane.

## Key numbers

- The critical density for the BKT transition at zero temperature is \(\tilde{d}_c = \sqrt{7}\), corresponding to a critical chemical potential \(\tilde{\mu} \sim 2.9\).
- The chiral symmetry restoration transition by temperature alone is first order, occurring at \(\tilde{w}_H = 0.3435\) for massless quarks.
- The condensate near the BKT transition scales as \(-\tilde{c} \sim -\exp\left(-\pi \sqrt{\frac{1+\tilde{d}^2}{\tilde{d}_c^2 - \tilde{d}^2}}\right)\).
- The Breitenlohner-Freedman (BF) bound in AdS2 is violated for \(\tilde{d} < \sqrt{7}\), with the scalar mass squared \(m^2 = -\frac{2}{1+\tilde{d}^2}\) compared to the BF bound of \(-\frac{1}{4}\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the D3/D7 system by Evans et al. (2010) to the D3/D5 system, which describes a 2+1 dimensional defect within a 3+1 dimensional gauge theory and may have relevance for condensed matter systems. The BKT transition at zero temperature was previously reported for this system by Karch et al. (2009), and the present work shows how it distorts the phase diagram at low temperatures compared to the D3/D7 case. The results enable further study of holographic models with tunable scalar masses near the BF bound, as analyzed by Kaplan et al. (2009) and Liu et al. (2009).
