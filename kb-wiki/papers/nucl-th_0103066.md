# A simple functional form for proton-${}^{208}$Pb total reaction cross sections

> A simple functional form has been found that gives a good representation of the total reaction cross sections for the scattering from ${}^{208}$Pb of protons with energies in the range 30 to 300 MeV.

## Key claim

The authors propose a simple three-parameter functional form that accurately reproduces the total reaction cross sections for proton scattering from \({}^{208}\)Pb across 30–300 MeV, but the form is demonstrated only for this single heavy nucleus and the parameters must be fitted to microscopic calculations rather than derived from first principles.

## Method

The authors used \(g\)-folding optical potentials formed by folding realistic two-nucleon (\(NN\)) interactions with nucleon-based structure models of \({}^{208}\)Pb (from a Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculation) to compute \(S\)-matrices and partial reaction cross sections \(\sigma_l^{(R)}(E)\) for proton-\({}^{208}\)Pb scattering at nine energies between 30 and 300 MeV. They then fitted these microscopic "data" with a functional form containing three energy-dependent parameters (\(l_0\), \(a\), and \(\epsilon\)) and derived an integral approximation for the total reaction cross section that can be evaluated analytically.

## Key numbers

- The functional form fits partial reaction cross sections at nine energies: 30, 40, 50, 65, 100, 160, 200, 250, and 300 MeV.
- Parameter \(l_0\) varies from 10.2 at 30 MeV to 34.0 at 300 MeV, with a linear dependence on \(\sqrt{E}\) described by \(l_0 \sim kR - 3\) where \(R \sim 1.6 A^{-1/3}\).
- Parameter \(a\) ranges from 1.123 at 30 MeV to 3.75 at 300 MeV.
- Parameter \(\epsilon\) ranges from -1.19 at 30 MeV to -1.67 at 300 MeV.
- The ratio of the approximate total reaction cross section (using the \(n=1\) truncation of the integral form) to the full microscopic calculation ranges from 0.96 to 1.02 across all nine energies, with most values within 1% of unity.

## Context

This work extends the \(g\)-folding optical potential approach previously applied by Deb, Amos, Karataglidis, Chadwick, and Madland (2001) to \({}^{12}\)C and \({}^{208}\)Pb, and reviewed comprehensively by Amos, Dortmans, von Geramb, Karataglidis, and Raynal (2000) for targets from mass 3 to 238. The simple functional form enables rapid estimation of proton-\({}^{208}\)Pb reaction cross sections for applications such as accelerator-driven system transmutation studies and radiation therapy dosimetry, without requiring the full microscopic optical model calculations that use large-scale computer programs like DWBA98.
