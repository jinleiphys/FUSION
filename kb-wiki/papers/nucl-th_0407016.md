# A possible explanation for AMS doubly charged anomalous event

> By means of the quark mass density-dependent model, a possible explanation for the doubly charged anomalous event with Z/A=0.114 reported by Alpha Magnetic Spectrometer Collaboration is given. It seems a strangelet. The composition, radius and mean lifetime of this strangelet are given.

# A possible explanation for AMS doubly charged anomalous event

## Key claim
Using the quark mass density-dependent (QMDD) model, the doubly charged anomalous event with Z/A = 0.114 reported by the AMS-01 Collaboration can be explained as a metastable strangelet, and this conclusion does not depend on the choice of model parameters within the stability window.

## Method
The authors employed the quark mass density-dependent (QMDD) model at zero temperature to calculate the composition, energy, and stability of strangelets with fixed electric charge Z = 2 and varying baryon number N and strangeness number |S|. They solved self-consistently the equations for quark chemical potentials, particle numbers, and the stability condition δA/δR = 0 for a spherical cavity, using the multi-reflection theory for the density of states. The mean lifetime was computed using the decay formula of Chin and Kerman, incorporating the Cabibbo angle and a relativistic factor.

## Key numbers
- The candidate strangelet Q(14,23,2) has energy 17.53 u, stable radius 1.992 fm, and dynamical mean lifetime 2.25 × 10⁻⁸ s (with B = 170 MeV fm⁻³, m_s0 = 150 MeV).
- The AMS-01 event had Z/A = 0.114 ± 0.01, kinetic energy 2.1 GeV, and a flux of 5 × 10⁻⁵ (m²·sr·s)⁻¹.
- For B = 180 MeV fm⁻³ and m_s0 = 150 MeV, the metastable strangelet Q(14,22,2) has energy 17.50 u, radius 1.962 fm, and lifetime 2.52 × 10⁻⁸ s.
- For B = 190 MeV fm⁻³ and m_s0 = 150 MeV, Q(13,24,2) has energy 17.58 u, radius 1.911 fm, and lifetime 1.41 × 10⁻⁸ s.
- For B = 220 MeV fm⁻³ and m_s0 = 75 MeV, Q(14,24,2) has energy 17.55 u, radius 1.911 fm, and lifetime 1.59 × 10⁻⁷ s.
- For B = 57.54 MeV fm⁻³ and m_s0 = 280 MeV, Q(15,25,2) has energy 17.68 u, radius 2.550 fm, and lifetime 2.13 × 10⁻⁹ s.
- For B = 396.93 MeV fm⁻³ and m_s0 = 150 MeV, Q(11,20,2) has energy 17.51 u, radius 1.517 fm, and lifetime 1.47 × 10⁻⁸ s.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies of strangelets using the QMDD and QMDTD models (Zhang and Su, 2002; Zhang and Su, 2003) to provide a specific explanation for the single anomalous event reported by the AMS-01 Collaboration (Choutko, 2003). The result supports the argument of Greiner, Koch, and Stocker (1987) that strangelets could serve as an unambiguous signature for quark-gluon plasma. The authors note that more events are needed to confirm the existence of the strangelet and express hope that the AMS-02 experiment will provide further information.
