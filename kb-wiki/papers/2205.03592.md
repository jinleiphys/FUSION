# Spectral function for $^4$He using the Chebyshev expansion in coupled-cluster theory

> We compute spectral function for $^4$He by combining coupled-cluster theory with an expansion of integral transforms into Chebyshev polynomials. Our method allows to estimate the uncertainty of spectral reconstruction. The properties of the Chebyshev polynomials make the procedure numerically stable and considerably lower in memory usage than the typically employed Lanczos algorithm. We benchmark our predictions with other calculations in the literature and with electron scattering data in the quasi-elastic peak. The spectral function formalism allows one to extend ab-initio lepton-nucleus cross sections into the relativistic regime. This makes it a promising tool for modeling this process at higher energy transfers. The results we present open the door for studies of heavier nuclei, important for the neutrino oscillation programs.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that combining coupled-cluster theory with a Chebyshev expansion of an integral transform (ChEK) can reconstruct the spectral function of \(^4\)He with controlled uncertainty, avoiding the inversion step required by traditional Lorentz-integral-transform methods. The authors hedge that the approach introduces some model dependence because the relativistic currents and the non-relativistic nuclear ground state are not treated on an equal footing, and that the factorization assumption neglects final-state interactions.

## Method

The authors compute the hole spectral function for \(^4\)He by first constructing the imaginary part of the one-body Green's function via a Chebyshev expansion of a Gaussian integral kernel (ChEK method), using coupled-cluster singles-and-doubles (CCSD) with the NNLO\(_\mathrm{sat}\) interaction. The Chebyshev moments \(\mu_k\) are obtained by iterating the similarity-transformed Hamiltonian on initial \((A-1)\)-body pivot states, without the orthogonalization steps required by the Lanczos algorithm. The spectral function is then reconstructed as a histogram with 3 MeV bin width, and the center-of-mass contamination is removed by deconvolution assuming a Gaussian center-of-mass wavefunction.

## Key numbers

- The dominant peak in the imaginary part of the Green's function for a proton with quantum numbers \(n=0\), \(l=0\) is located at approximately 23.5 MeV, converged for \(N_\mathrm{max}=14\).
- The difference between the energy shift \(E_0 - E_\Psi^*\) (using the \((A-1)\) system) and \(E_0 - E_\Psi\) (using the \(A\)-system Hamiltonian) is around 1.5 MeV.
- The model space used 15 oscillator shells (\(N_\mathrm{max}=14\)) with oscillator spacing \(\hbar\omega = 16\) MeV; the three-nucleon interaction had an energy cut \(E_{3\rm max} = 16\) MeV.
- The integrated strength of smaller excitations above the dominant peak does not exceed 2%.

## Context

This work extends the Chebyshev expansion technique (ChEK) introduced by Roggero (2020) and Sobczyk (2021) from response functions to spectral functions, and applies it within coupled-cluster theory for the first time. It benchmarks against self-consistent Green's function (SCGF) calculations by Rocco (2018) for the momentum distribution of \(^4\)He, finding agreement for the laboratory momentum distribution but differences in the intrinsic momentum distribution attributed to different center-of-mass removal strategies. The method enables ab-initio calculations of spectral functions for heavier nuclei, which is important for modeling lepton-nucleus cross sections in neutrino oscillation experiments.
