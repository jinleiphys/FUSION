# Tau longitudinal and transverse polarizations from visible kinematics in (anti-)neutrino nucleus scattering

> Since the $ν_τ(\barν_τ) A_Z \to τ^\mp X$ reaction is notoriously difficult to be directly measured, the information on the dynamics of this nuclear process should be extracted from the analysis of the energy and angular distributions of the tau decay visible products. These distributions depend on the components of the tau-polarization vector. We give, for the first time, the general expression for the outgoing hadron (pion or rho meson) energy and angular differential cross section for the sequential $ν_τA_Z \to τ^-(π^- ν_τ, ρ^-ν_τ) X$ and $\barν_τA_Z \to τ^+(π^+ \barν_τ, ρ^+ \barν_τ) X$ reactions. Though all possible nuclear reaction mechanisms contribute to the distribution, it may be possible to isolate/enhance one of them by implementing appropriate selection criteria. For the case of the quasi-elastic reaction off oxygen and neutrino energies below 6 GeV, we show that the pion distributions are sensitive to the details of the tau-polarization components. We find significant differences between the full calculation, where the longitudinal and transverse components of the tau polarization vector vary with the energy and the scattering angle of the produced tau, and the simplified scheme in which the polarizations are set to one and zero (respective asymptotic values in the high energy regime). In addition to its potential impact on neutrino oscillation analyses, this result can be used to further test different nuclear models, since these observables provide complementary information to that obtained by means of the inclusive nuclear weak charged-current differential cross section. We also study the effects on the cross section of the $W_4$, $W_5$ nuclear structure functions, which contributions are proportional to the charged lepton mass, and therefore difficult to constrain in muon and electron neutrino experiments.

## Key claim

The paper provides, for the first time, the general expression for the outgoing hadron (pion or rho meson) energy and angular differential cross section for sequential tau-neutrino and tau-antineutrino nucleus reactions, showing that the pion distributions are sensitive to the details of the tau-polarization components for neutrino energies below 6 GeV, with significant differences between the full calculation and the simplified scheme where polarizations are set to their asymptotic high-energy values.

## Method

The authors derived the master formula for the double differential cross section of sequential reactions where a tau lepton produced in (anti-)neutrino-nucleus charged-current interactions subsequently decays into a pion or rho meson plus a (anti-)neutrino. They expressed this distribution in terms of the unpolarized inclusive cross section and the longitudinal and transverse components of the tau-polarization vector integrated over the tau phase space. For illustration, they computed the quasi-elastic contribution in oxygen using the local Fermi gas model of Nieves (2004), without including random phase approximation corrections or energy balance corrections, for neutrino energies between 3 and 10 GeV.

## Key numbers

* The tau lepton mean life is 2.9 x 10^-13 s, which makes its clear identification challenging.
* For the quasi-elastic reaction off oxygen at 3 GeV neutrino energy, the charged-current cross section is about a factor one-hundred larger than the coherent pion production cross section.
* The branching fraction for tau decay to pion plus neutrino (B_pi) and tau decay to rho plus neutrino (B_rho) are used, with the parameter a_rho = (m_tau^2 - 2m_rho^2)/(m_tau^2 + 2m_rho^2) approximately 0.4, indicating smaller polarization effects for rho mesons than for pions.
* The minimum tau neutrino energy considered is 3 GeV, for which the quasi-elastic charged-current cross section in oxygen is about a factor one-hundred larger than the coherent pion production cross section estimated using the model of Amaro (2008).

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of tau longitudinal and transverse polarization in quasi-elastic scattering, which were pioneered by Hagiwara (2003) for free nucleons and improved by Graczyk (2004) with random phase approximation and effective nucleon mass effects, and by Valverde (2006) and Sobczyk (2019) using realistic spectral functions. The paper provides the formalism needed to extract polarization information from visible decay products rather than direct tau measurement, which is relevant for the DUNE oscillation experiment where sizeable tau-neutrino charged-current interactions are expected in the far detector. The results enable further testing of nuclear models by providing complementary observables to the inclusive nuclear weak charged-current differential cross section.
