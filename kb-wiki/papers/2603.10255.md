# Topological structure of the entanglement radius of Yang-Mills flux tubes

> We expand on recent work arXiv:2601.17199 demonstrating the existence of a novel entanglement radius $ξ_0$ characterizing flux tube entanglement entropy (FTE$^2$) in (2+1)D Yang-Mills theory. This physical scale corresponds to the intrinsic thickness of the flux tube that must be fully severed by an entangling region for color degrees of freedom in the flux tube to contribute non-zero FTE$^2$. We consider here geometries of the entanglement region $V$ on the lattice where the length of the region cross-cutting the flux tube is of the same magnitude as $ξ_0$. Our results further the conclusions of arXiv:2601.17199 by adding detailed new information on the topological structure of the entanglement radius of color flux tubes.

## Key claim
The entanglement radius ξ₀ of Yang-Mills flux tubes is not a fixed value but follows a distribution P(ξ) with mean ξ₀ = 0.185(6)σ₀^{-1/2} and support over a broad range of ξ values, as demonstrated by studying entanglement regions with length L_x comparable to ξ₀.

## Method
The authors performed Monte Carlo simulations of SU(2) Yang-Mills theory on a (2+1)D 128×256×64 lattice at T_c/4 with lattice coupling β=24.744 (lattice spacing a√σ₀=0.0557(11)). They computed the flux tube entanglement entropy (FTE²) using the replica trick with q=2, placing quark and antiquark sources as Polyakov loops in region V̅ and measuring the excess entanglement entropy for "small-slab" geometries where the entangling region V has length L_x comparable to the entanglement radius ξ₀.

## Key numbers
- Entanglement radius ξ₀ = 0.185(6)σ₀^{-1/2} (from prior work Amorosso (2026))
- Decay length of FTE² tails λ√σ₀ = 0.223(15), consistent with inverse glueball mass and intrinsic flux tube width (from prior work Amorosso (2026))
- Quark separations studied: 0.67 < L√σ₀ < 0.89
- Lattice spacing: a√σ₀ = 0.0557(11)
- Slab lengths studied: L_x√σ₀ = 0.22 and L_x√σ₀ = 0.33, both less than 2ξ₀√σ₀ = 0.370(12)
- FTE² at x=0 is nonzero for L_x√σ₀ = 0.22 despite L_x < 2ξ₀

## Context
This work extends the authors' prior studies of flux tube entanglement entropy (Amorosso 2023, 2024, 2025, 2026) which established FTE² as a finite, gauge-invariant probe of flux tube structure and identified the entanglement radius ξ₀. The results support the proposal from Amorosso (2026) that the entanglement radius follows a distribution P(ξ) rather than being fixed, and rule out both a zero-width effective string and a fixed-radius model. The findings connect to the intrinsic flux tube width studied by Caselle (2012, 2026) and to the inverse glueball mass from Teper (1998). Future work aims to extend these studies to larger N_c and higher statistical precision to determine the functional form of P(ξ).
