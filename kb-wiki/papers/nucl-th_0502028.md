# Hadron attenuation at HERMES and JLab

> We investigate the attenuation of hadrons in deep inelastic lepton-nucleus scattering in the kinematical regime of the HERMES and Jefferson Lab experiments. The calculation is carried out in the framework of a BUU transport model. Our results indicate a strong influence of (pre)hadronic final state interactions on the observed multiplicity ratios.

## Key claim
The observed attenuation of hadrons in deep inelastic lepton-nucleus scattering at HERMES and Jefferson Lab energies can be largely attributed to prehadronic final-state interactions in the nuclear medium, rather than partonic energy loss prior to hadronization, provided one assumes a formation time of 0.5 fm/c for all hadron species.

## Method
The authors combine a quantum mechanical treatment of nuclear shadowing with a semi-classical Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model. The virtual photon-nucleon interaction is simulated using the Lund Monte Carlo generators Pythia and Fritiof, including nuclear effects such as binding energies, Fermi motion, Pauli-blocking, and shadowing. The resulting (pre)hadrons are then propagated through the nucleus using a coupled-channel transport model, where prehadronic cross sections during the formation time are determined by a constituent quark model.

## Key numbers
- Formation time assumed constant at τ_f = 0.5 fm/c for all hadron species in the rest frame.
- HERMES experiments used 27.6 GeV and 12 GeV positron beams; Jefferson Lab experiment uses a 5.4 GeV electron beam.
- Predictions for Jefferson Lab are made for a 5 GeV electron beam with the CLAS detector.
- Multiplicity ratios are calculated for targets including ¹⁴N, ⁸⁴Kr, ¹²C, ⁵⁶Fe, and ²⁰⁸Pb.
- For antikaons (K⁻ and K̅⁰) at Jefferson Lab energies, the maximum fractional energy z_h does not exceed approximately 0.8 due to kinematic constraints.
- The kinematic cut Q² > 1 GeV² is applied for the virtual photon.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous BUU transport model calculations (Falter et al., 2004) from HERMES and EMC energies down to the lower energies of the Jefferson Lab experiment. It directly contradicts the interpretation of Arleo and Wang (2004) that hadron attenuation is caused by partonic energy loss prior to hadronization, instead supporting the prehadron FSI picture of Kopeliovich (2004) and Accardi (2004). The results enable predictions for the ongoing Jefferson Lab experiment (Brooks, 2004) and provide a framework for future simulations incorporating four-dimensional prehadron production points from the Jetset fragmentation routine.
