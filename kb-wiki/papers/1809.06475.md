# Form Factors and Generalized Parton Distributions of Heavy Quarkonia in Basis Light Front Quantization

> We calculate the electromagnetic (charge, magnetic and quadrupole) form factors and the associated static moments of heavy quarkonia (charmonia and bottomonia) using the Basis Light Front Quantization (BLFQ) approach. For this work, we adopt light front wavefunctions (LFWFs) generated by a holographic QCD confining potential and a one-gluon exchange interaction with fixed coupling. We compare our BLFQ results with the limiting case of a single BLFQ basis state description of heavy quarkonia and with other available results. These comparisons provide insights into relativistic effects. Using the same LFWFs generated in the BLFQ approach, we also present the generalized parton distributions (GPDs) for selected mesons including those for radially excited mesons such as $ψ^\prime$ and $Υ^\prime$. Our GPD results establish the foundation within BLFQ for further investigating hadronic structure such as probing the spin structure of spin-one hadrons in the off-forward limit.

## Key claim

The paper calculates electromagnetic form factors and generalized parton distributions for heavy quarkonia (charmonia and bottomonia) using Basis Light Front Quantization, but the results are limited to the valence Fock sector and use a fixed coupling constant for the one-gluon exchange interaction, with the charge form factors computed for a single quark flavor rather than the full charge-symmetric system.

## Method

The authors solve the light front Hamiltonian eigenvalue problem for heavy quarkonia using Basis Light Front Quantization, employing a transverse confining potential from holographic QCD, a longitudinal confining potential, and a one-gluon exchange interaction with fixed coupling. The resulting light front wavefunctions are used to compute electromagnetic form factors via overlap integrals in the Drell-Yan frame, adopting the Grach-Kondratyuk prescription to extract charge, magnetic, and quadrupole form factors from helicity amplitudes. Generalized parton distributions are computed from the same wavefunctions using non-local matrix elements of the vector current at zero skewness.

- Charge mean squared radius of $J/\psi$: 0.045(3) fm² (BLFQ, $N_{\text{max}} = L_{\text{max}} = 24$)
- Charge mean squared radius of $\Upsilon$: 0.016(1) fm² (BLFQ, $N_{\text{max}} = L_{\text{max}} = 24$)
- Charge mean squared radius of $\eta_b$: 0.016(1) fm² (BLFQ, $N_{\text{max}} = L_{\text{max}} = 24$)
- Charge mean squared radius of $\psi^\prime$: 0.15(1) fm² (BLFQ, $N_{\text{max}} = L_{\text{max}} = 24$)
- Charge mean squared radius of $\Upsilon^\prime$: 0.057(3) fm² (BLFQ, $N_{\text{max}} = L_{\text{max}} = 24$)
- Fixed coupling constant $\alpha_s = 0.3595$ for charmonia, $\alpha_s = 0.2500$ for bottomonia
- Confining strength $\kappa = 0.938$ GeV for charmonia and $\kappa = 1.422$ GeV for bottomonia at $N_{\text{max}} = L_{\text{max}} = 24$

## Context

This work extends the Basis Light Front Quantization approach previously applied to heavy quarkonia spectroscopy (Li et al., 2015) to compute electromagnetic form factors and generalized parton distributions, providing comparisons with the Contact Interaction approach (Bedolla et al., 2015; Raya et al., 2017), Dyson-Schwinger Equation methods (Maris et al., 2006), and Lattice QCD (Dudek et al., 2006). The results for radially excited states such as $\psi^\prime$ and $\Upsilon^\prime$ are previously unavailable in the literature, and the generalized parton distributions establish a foundation within BLFQ for further investigations of hadronic structure, including the spin structure of spin-one hadrons in the off-forward limit.
