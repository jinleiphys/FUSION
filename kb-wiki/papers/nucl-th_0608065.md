# Pion and Kaon Production in Nucleon - Nucleon Collisions

> Inclusive cross section for pion production in proton - proton collisions are calculated based on unintegrated parton distribution functions (uPDFs). In addition to purely gluonic terms the present approach includes also quark degrees of freedom. Phenomenological fragmentation functions from the literature are used. In contrast to standard collinear approach, application of 2 $\to$ 1 $k_t$ - factorization approach can be extended towards much lower transverse momenta, both at mid and forward rapidity region. The results of the calculation are compared with SPS and RHIC data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including quark degrees of freedom alongside purely gluonic terms in a \(k_t\)-factorization approach with unintegrated parton distribution functions (uPDFs) can describe pion and kaon production cross sections down to lower transverse momenta than the standard collinear approach, and that the new quark-initiated mechanisms are responsible for the observed \(\pi^+ - \pi^-\) asymmetry, which a purely gluonic mechanism cannot produce.

## Method

The authors calculate inclusive cross sections for pion and kaon production in proton-proton collisions using a \(2 \to 1\) \(k_t\)-factorization formalism based on unintegrated parton distribution functions (uPDFs) that satisfy Kwiecinski evolution equations. In addition to the purely gluonic \(gg \to g\) mechanism, they include \(q_f g \to q_f\) and \(g q_f \to q_f\) mechanisms (and analogous ones for antiquarks), then convolve the resulting parton distributions with phenomenological fragmentation functions from the literature (BKK95, Kretzer2000, AKK05) to obtain hadron-level distributions. The model includes a Gaussian nonperturbative form factor parameterized by \(b_0\), and results are compared with SPS data at W = 27.4 GeV and RHIC data at W = 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- At W = 27.4 GeV, the nonperturbative form factor parameter is constrained to \(b_0 > 0.5\) GeV\(^{-1}\) to avoid exceeding experimental data in the perturbative regime of \(p_t > 2\) GeV.
- For kaon production at W = 27.4 GeV, data for \(p_t > 0.5\) GeV are well described with \(b_0 = 0.5\) GeV\(^{-1}\), the same value obtained for pions in the same energy range.
- The PHENIX collaboration data for \(\pi^0\) production at W = 200 GeV and \(\eta = 0.0 \pm 0.15\) are compared with the model results using different fragmentation function sets.

## Context

This work extends the earlier purely gluonic approach of Kwiecinski and collaborators (2001) by adding quark and antiquark degrees of freedom, motivated by BRAHMS experiment results showing \(\pi^-/\pi^+\) and \(K^-/K^+\) ratios differing from unity. The new mechanisms enable description of charged hadron asymmetries that the purely gluonic mechanism could not produce, and the \(k_t\)-factorization approach allows extension to lower transverse momenta than the standard collinear factorization method of Owens (1987), Field (1989), and others. The results enable future comparisons with data from PHENIX, BRAHMS, and STAR collaborations at RHIC energies.
