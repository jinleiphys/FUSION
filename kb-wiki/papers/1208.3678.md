# Energy reconstruction in quasielastic scattering in the MiniBooNE and T2K experiments

> Neutrino oscillation probabilities, which are being measured in long-baseline experiments, depend on neutrino energy. The energy in a neutrino beam, however, is broadly smeared so that the neutrino energy in a particular event is not directly known, but must be reconstructed from final state properties. In this paper we investigate the contributions from different reaction mechanisms on the energy-reconstruction method widely used in long-baseline neutrino experiments. Difference between the true-QE and QE-like cross sections in MiniBooNE experiment is investigated in details. It is shown, that fake QE-like events lead to significant distortions in neutrino energy reconstruction. Flux-folded and unfolded cross sections for QE-like scattering are calculated as functions of both true and reconstructed energies. Flux-folded momentum transfer distributions are calculated as functions of both true and reconstructed momentum transfer. Distributions versus reconstructed values are compared with the experimental data. Also presented are the conditional probability densities of finding a true energy for a given reconstructed energy. It is shown, how the energy reconstruction procedure influences the measurement of oscillation parameters in T2K experiment. For the reconstruction procedure based on quasielastic (QE) kinematics, all other reaction channels beside true-QE scattering show a shift of the reconstructed energy towards lower values as compared to the true energy. On average in the MiniBooNE and T2K experiments the shift is 100 - 200 MeV and depends on energy. The oscillation signals are similarly affected. These uncertainties may limit the extraction of a CP violating phase from an oscillation result.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the standard energy reconstruction method based on quasielastic kinematics, used by MiniBooNE and T2K, systematically shifts reconstructed neutrino energies toward lower values for all non-true-QE reaction channels, with the shift averaging 100-200 MeV and depending on energy; this systematic effect may limit the extraction of a CP violating phase from oscillation results.

## Method

The authors used the Giessen Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) transport model to simulate neutrino-nucleus events on carbon targets, including true quasielastic scattering, 2p-2h interactions, resonance excitations (Delta and higher), one-pion background, and deep inelastic scattering. They applied the standard energy reconstruction formula based on QE kinematics on a nucleon at rest to all simulated events, then compared the distributions of true versus reconstructed neutrino energies for each reaction channel, using the MiniBooNE and T2K neutrino fluxes.

## Key numbers

- The shift between true and reconstructed neutrino energy is 100-200 MeV on average in the MiniBooNE and T2K experiments, and depends on energy
- The QE-like cross section is about 30%-40% higher than the true-QE cross section
- The 2p-2h contribution to fake QE-like events is flat at about 20-30% of the true-QE cross section
- The overall event distribution versus reconstructed energy is shifted by about 200 MeV towards lower energies with respect to the true energy
- The stuck-pion cross section from GiBUU peaks at about 300 MeV, while the MiniBooNE-NUANCE background peaks at about 500 MeV
- For true-QE events, the distributions versus true and reconstructed energies nearly coincide, with no visible effect from Fermi-motion broadening

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Leitner (2010) showing entanglement of QE scattering and pion production in Cherenkov detectors, and by Martini (2011) and Nieves (2011) who attributed the large axial mass extracted by MiniBooNE to 2p-2h excitations not included in the NUANCE generator. The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of all reaction channels contributing to QE-like events, including pion production and higher resonances, which were not fully treated in prior 2p-2h-only analyses by Martini (2012) and Nieves (2012). The results enable improved understanding of systematic uncertainties in neutrino energy reconstruction for long-baseline oscillation experiments like T2K, and highlight the need for proper treatment of fake QE events when extracting oscillation parameters.
