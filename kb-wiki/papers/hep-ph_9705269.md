# New QCD Sum Rules for Nucleon Tensor Charge

> Two new QCD sum rules for nucleon tensor charge are derived from a mixed correlator of spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 nucleon interpolating fields. These sum rules are analyzed along with a sum rule obtained from the usual correlator of a general spin-1/2 nucleon interpolating field. The validity and reliability of the sum rules are examined by monitoring the contaminations arising from the transitions and continuum and the convergence of the operator product expansion. Valid sum rules are identified and their predictions are presented. It is found that the vacuum tensor susceptibility induced by the external field plays an important role in determining both the validity and predictions of the sum rules. The uncertainties associated with the sum-rule predictions are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper derives new QCD sum rules for the nucleon tensor charge from a mixed correlator of spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 nucleon interpolating fields, and finds that the vacuum tensor susceptibility induced by the external field plays an important role in determining both the validity and predictions of the sum rules, with the results showing strong dependence on the value of the tensor susceptibility chi.

## Method

The authors used the external-field QCD sum-rule method, expanding the nucleon two-point correlator to first order in a constant external antisymmetric tensor field. They derived sum rules from a mixed correlator of spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 interpolating fields (Eqs. sr-mix-1 and sr-mix-2) and from the usual correlator of a general spin-1/2 interpolating field (Eq. sr-usual), considering both a "usual formalism" where transition contributions are modeled by a single unknown parameter and an "alternative formalism" where the factor (M_N^2 - q^2) is applied before Borel transformation to exponentially suppress transition contributions. The sum rules were analyzed by monitoring contaminations from transitions and continuum and the convergence of the operator product expansion, with valid Borel windows defined where the highest-order term contributes no more than about 10% to the QCD side and transition plus continuum contributions are less than 50% of the total phenomenological side.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum condensate values used: a = 0.52 GeV^3, b = 1.2 GeV^4, m_0^2 = 0.72 GeV^2
- For mixed correlator sum rules: M_N = 0.96 GeV, lambda_1*lambda_2 = 0.41 GeV^6, s_0 = 1.69 GeV^2
- For 1/2 correlator sum rules: M_N = 1.17 GeV, lambda_1^2 = 0.20 GeV^6, s_0 = 2.34 GeV^2
- The vacuum tensor susceptibility chi was considered in the range -0.5 GeV^2 <= chi*a <= 1.5 GeV^2
- The sum rule Eq. sr-mix-2 was found invalid for all chi values considered, with no valid Borel window
- The sum rule Eq. sr-mix-1 works only for a limited range of chi values, with the valid Borel window shrinking as chi decreases
- The sum rule Eq. sr-usual is valid for all chi values considered, with generally larger valid Borel windows than Eq. sr-mix-1

## Context

This work extends previous QCD sum-rule studies of the nucleon tensor charge by He and Ji (1995, 1996), who found that sum rules from Ioffe's nucleon interpolating field were unstable. The mixed correlator approach was motivated by its success in nucleon mass sum rules by Leinweber (1997) and in sum rules for nucleon axial-vector coupling constants by Lee (1997). The paper also addresses issues with the treatment of transitions from ground state to excited states, noting that previous work by He and Ji (1996) incorrectly fixed the continuum threshold to the same value as in the mass sum rule and had incomplete calculations of Wilson coefficients. The results enable future extraction of the nucleon tensor charge from planned experiments measuring quark transversity distributions at RHIC, HERA, and CERN.
