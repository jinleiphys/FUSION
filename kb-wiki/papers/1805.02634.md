# Heavy physics contributions to neutrinoless double beta decay from QCD

> Observation of neutrinoless double beta decay, a lepton number violating process that has been proposed to clarify the nature of neutrino masses, has spawned an enormous world-wide experimental effort. Relating nuclear decay rates to high-energy, beyond the Standard Model (BSM) physics requires detailed knowledge of non-perturbative QCD effects. Using lattice QCD, we compute the necessary matrix elements of short-range operators, which arise due to heavy BSM mediators, that contribute to this decay via the leading order $π^- \to π^+$ exchange diagrams. Utilizing our result and taking advantage of effective field theory methods will allow for model-independent calculations of the relevant two-nucleon decay, which may then be used as input for nuclear many-body calculations of the relevant experimental decays. Contributions from short-range operators may prove to be equally important to, or even more important than, those from long-range Majorana neutrino exchange.

# Heavy physics contributions to neutrinoless double beta decay from QCD

## Key claim

Using lattice QCD, the authors compute the matrix elements of short-range four-quark operators that contribute to neutrinoless double beta decay via leading-order pion-exchange diagrams, finding that these short-range contributions may be equally important to or even more important than those from long-range Majorana neutrino exchange, depending on the particle physics model.

## Method

The authors perform lattice QCD calculations of the on-shell $\pi^- \to \pi^+$ transition matrix elements for five four-quark operators (three color-contracted and two color-mixed) that arise from heavy beyond-the-Standard-Model mediators. They use MILC collaboration HISQ gauge configurations with Möbius domain wall quark propagators, covering multiple lattice spacings (0.09, 0.12, 0.15 fm), pion masses (130, 220, 310 MeV), and volumes, then extrapolate to the continuum, physical pion mass, and infinite volume limits using chiral perturbation theory formulas. The operators are renormalized non-perturbatively in the RI/SMOM scheme and run to a common scale of 3 GeV.

## Key numbers

* $O_1 = -1.91(13) \times 10^{-2} \text{ GeV}^4$ in RI/SMOM at $\mu = 3$ GeV (the matrix element for operator $\mathcal{O}_{1+}^{++}$)
* $O_1' = -7.22(49) \times 10^{-2} \text{ GeV}^4$ in RI/SMOM at $\mu = 3$ GeV (the color-mixed operator $\mathcal{O}_{1+}^{'++}$)
* $O_2 = -3.68(31) \times 10^{-2} \text{ GeV}^4$ in RI/SMOM at $\mu = 3$ GeV
* $O_2' = 1.16(10) \times 10^{-2} \text{ GeV}^4$ in RI/SMOM at $\mu = 3$ GeV
* $O_3 = 1.85(10) \times 10^{-4} \text{ GeV}^4$ in RI/SMOM at $\mu = 3$ GeV
* The bag parameter $B_\pi = 0.420(23)$ in RI/SMOM at $\mu = 3$ GeV, indicating a large deviation from the vacuum saturation approximation
* Uncertainties on the matrix elements are reduced to 5-9% (compared to 20-40% in prior SU(3) flavor symmetry estimates)

## Context

This work provides the first lattice QCD calculation of hadronic matrix elements for short-range operators contributing to neutrinoless double beta decay, extending prior effective field theory analyses by Prezeau (2003) and Graesser (2016) that identified the relevant four-quark operators. The results enable model-independent calculations of the two-nucleon decay amplitude when combined with effective field theory methods, and can be used to place limits on various beyond-the-Standard-Model mechanisms. The matrix elements agree at the one-to-two sigma level with earlier SU(3) flavor symmetry estimates by Cirigliano (2017), but with significantly reduced uncertainties. Future work requires computing the next-to-next-to-leading order contact operator contributions and incorporating these results into nuclear many-body calculations for experimental decays.
