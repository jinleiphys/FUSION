# Plasmons in strong superconductors

> We present a study of the possible plasmon excitations that can occur in systems where strong superconductivity is present. In these systems the plasmon energy is comparable to, or smaller than the pairing gap. As a prototype of these systems we consider the proton component of neutron star matter just below the crust when electron screening is not taken into account. For the realistic case we consider in detail the different aspects of the elementary excitations when the proton, electron components are considered within the Random Phase Approximation generalized to the superfluid case, while the influence of the neutron component is considered only at qualitative level. Electron screening plays a major role in modifying the proton spectrum and spectral function. At the same time the electron plasmon is strongly modified and damped by the indirect coupling with the superfluid proton component, even at moderately low values of the gap. The excitation spectrum shows the interplay of the different components and their relevance for each excitation mode. The results are relevant for neutrino physics and thermodynamical processes in neutron stars. If electron screening is neglected, the spectral properties of the proton component show some resemblance with the physical situation in high T$_c$ superconductors, and we briefly discuss similarities and differences in this connection. In a general prospect, the results of the study emphasize the role of Coulomb interaction in strong superconductors.

## Key claim

In systems where the pairing gap is comparable to or larger than the plasmon frequency, the plasmon excitation spectrum is compressed and can disappear entirely, contrary to the expectation that the spectrum would simply shift upward by the gap energy. This result is demonstrated for the proton component of neutron star matter, but the authors note that in high-Tc superconductors a plasmon mode can exist below 2Δ due to the high dielectric constant along the c-axis, a situation not reproduced in the homogeneous proton system.

## Method

The authors formulate a conserving approximation within the Random Phase Approximation (RPA) generalized to superfluid systems, using a Φ-derivable self-energy scheme that guarantees current conservation and the fulfillment of Generalized Ward Identities. They solve a system of three coupled algebraic RPA equations for the polarization tensor of protons and electrons, including a zero-range pairing interaction and the Coulomb interaction, with the proton Green's functions taken in the BCS form. The electron component is treated in the relativistic Vlasov limit, while the neutron component is considered only qualitatively and is omitted from the full calculation.

## Key numbers

- At a total baryon density of 0.16 fm⁻³, the proton fraction is 3.7% and the proton plasmon energy ωp ≈ 2.105 MeV.
- For Δ = 1 MeV with electron screening included, the pseudo-Goldstone mode below 2Δ has a velocity about three times larger than the weak-coupling Goldstone velocity vF/√3.
- The pseudo-Goldstone mode and the pair-breaking mode near 2Δ appear to touch at an end point of the spectrum at increasing momentum.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of collective excitations in normal neutron star matter by Providencia (2006) and by the same authors in earlier papers (paper1, paper2) to the superfluid case, where the proton component is expected to be superfluid below the neutron star crust. The results are relevant for neutrino physics and thermodynamic processes in neutron stars, and the authors note that the full treatment including the neutron component and entrainment effects (Chamel, 2008; Haensel, 2008) is left for future work.
