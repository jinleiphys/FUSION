# QCD sum rules for rho mesons in nuclear matter

> We investigate QCD sum rules for vector currents in the rho meson channel in the nuclear medium. For increased sensitivity, we subtract out the vacuum contributions. With a saturation scheme often considered in the literature, we find these subtracted sum rules to be unstable. It indicates the importance of other contributions neglected in the saturation scheme. These include more Landau singularities at low energy, additional operators in the medium, and possibly the in-medium width of the rho meson.

## Key claim

The authors find that QCD sum rules for rho mesons in nuclear matter, when evaluated using a saturation scheme commonly employed in the literature, are unstable after subtracting vacuum contributions, indicating that other neglected contributions (more Landau singularities, additional operators, and the in-medium width of the rho meson) are essential for reliable predictions.

## Method

The authors derive QCD sum rules for vector currents in the rho meson channel at finite nuclear density and zero temperature, using the operator product expansion (OPE) up to dimension 6 operators and a spectral function including the rho meson pole (in narrow width approximation) and the N anti-N intermediate state. They subtract the vacuum sum rules from the full in-medium sum rules to retain only terms linear in the nuclear density, then perform a Borel transform and evaluate the resulting sum rules for the longitudinal and transverse invariant amplitudes at zero three-momentum.

## Key numbers

* The vacuum rho meson coupling constant F_rho = 153.5 MeV (experimental value).
* The sigma term (nucleon matrix element of the scalar quark operator) sigma = 45 MeV.
* The quark condensate in vacuum is given by <0|bar{q} q|0>/2 = <0|bar{u} u|0> = -(225 MeV)^3.
* The saturation nucleon number density is bar{n}_s = (110 MeV)^3.
* The quark mass in the SU(2) limit is hat{m} = 7 MeV.
* The coefficients A^q = 0.62 and A^g = 0.35 for the quark and gluon parts of the energy-momentum tensor at Q^2 = 1 GeV^2.

## Context

This work extends earlier QCD sum rule studies of rho mesons in nuclear matter by Hatsuda (1993), Jin and Leinweber (1995), and Cohen (1995), who used similar saturation schemes but evaluated "full" sum rules dominated by vacuum contributions. The authors show that the subtracted sum rules are unstable, contradicting the apparent stability in those earlier works, and point to the need for including additional dimension-6 Lorentz nonscalar operators, more Landau singularities beyond the N anti-N state, and the in-medium width of the rho meson, as also suggested by Klingl (1997) and Leupold (1998).
