# Photoproduction of the Hypertriton

> In the framework of the impulse approximation we study the photoproduction of the hypertriton $^3_Λ$H by using realistic $^3$He wave functions obtained as solutions of Faddeev equations with the Reid soft-core potential for different $^3_Λ$H wave functions. We obtain relatively small cross sections of the order of 1 nb. We also find that the influence of Fermi motion is important, while the effect of different off-shell assumptions on the cross section is not too significant.

## Key claim

The photoproduction cross section for the hypertriton via the reaction \(^3\text{He}(\gamma,K^+)^3_\Lambda\text{H}\) is predicted to be on the order of 1 nb, but this result is obtained within the impulse approximation and depends on the specific choice of hypertriton wave function; the inclusion of higher partial waves reduces the cross section by more than a factor of three compared to a simple S-wave-only calculation.

## Method

The authors used realistic \(^3\)He wave functions from Faddeev equations with the Reid soft-core potential and a simple separable hypertriton wave function (deuteron plus lambda moving in a \(\Lambda\)-\(d\) effective potential) to compute the differential cross section in the laboratory frame. The transition amplitude was evaluated by integrating over internal momenta, including spin-independent and spin-dependent elementary amplitudes, with full treatment of Fermi motion and tests of different off-shell assumptions. A comparison was also made using a correlated Faddeev wave function for the hypertriton from Miyagawa (1995) to probe sensitivity to the wave function model.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of the \(^3\)He cross section to the elementary proton cross section, in an S-wave-only approximation, is approximately \(1.8 \times 10^{-3}\) at a photon energy \(k = 1.8\) GeV.
- The elementary reaction model of Williams (1992) yields a maximum cross section of about 500 nb/sr on the proton at \(k = 1.8\) GeV, implying a corresponding \(^3\)He cross section of about 1 nb/sr at most.
- The inclusion of higher partial waves reduces the cross section by more than a factor of three compared to the S-wave-only calculation.
- The difference between the simple hypertriton wave function and the correlated Faddeev wave function of Miyagawa (1995) is of order 10–20%.
- The largest discrepancy between different off-shell assumptions (initial nucleon on-shell vs. final hyperon on-shell) is 10% at \(k = 2200\) MeV in the forward direction.

## Context

This work extends the study of hypernuclear photoproduction to the lightest hypernucleus, the hypertriton, for which no experimental data existed at the time. It complements the hadronic reaction studies of Komarov (1995) on \(p + d \rightarrow K^+ + {}^3_\Lambda\text{H}\), which estimated maximum differential cross sections below 1 nb/sr. The results enable future experimental searches for hypertriton photoproduction and provide a baseline for testing more sophisticated hypertriton wave functions, such as the correlated Faddeev model of Miyagawa (1995).
