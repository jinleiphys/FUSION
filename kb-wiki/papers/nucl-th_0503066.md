# Effects of particle-number conservation on heat capacity of nuclei

> By applying the particle-number projection to the finite-temperature BCS theory, the $S$-shaped heat capacity, which has recently been claimed to be a fingerprint of the superfluid-to-normal phase transition in nuclei, is reexamined. It is found that the particle-number (or number-parity) projection gives $S$-shapes in the heat capacity of nuclei which look qualitatively similar to the observed ones. These $S$-shapes are accounted for as effects of the particle-number conservation on the quasiparticle excitations, and occur even when we keep the superfluidity at all temperatures by assuming a constant gap in the BCS theory. The present study illustrates significance of the conservation laws in studying phase transitions of finite systems.

## Key claim

The particle-number projection applied to finite-temperature BCS theory produces S-shaped heat capacity curves that look qualitatively similar to observed experimental data, but these S-shapes occur even when superfluidity is artificially maintained at all temperatures by assuming a constant gap, indicating that the S-shape is not exclusive evidence for a superfluid-to-normal phase transition.

## Method

The authors applied particle-number projection and number-parity projection to the finite-temperature BCS (FT-BCS) theory using a model Hamiltonian with Nilsson single-particle levels and pairing interactions, with parameters chosen to reproduce pairing gaps of approximately 12/√A MeV. They calculated heat capacity as C = d⟨H⟩/dT via numerical differentiation for the nuclei ¹⁶¹Dy and ¹⁶²Dy, comparing unprojected, number-parity-projected, and fully number-projected results. Additionally, they performed calculations where the BCS gap and single-particle energies were fixed to their T=0 values to maintain superfluidity at all temperatures.

## Key numbers

- Pairing gaps of Δ_τ ≈ 12/√A MeV were reproduced with coupling constants g_p = 22/A MeV and g_n = 27/A MeV
- Critical temperature T_c ≈ 0.5–0.6 MeV for the superfluid-to-normal transition in the FT-BCS calculations, slightly lower for protons than for neutrons
- The S-shape in the number-parity-projected neutron heat capacity for ¹⁶²Dy appears in the temperature range 0.2 ≲ T ≲ 0.5 MeV, with ΔE_{n,P'} decreasing at 0.2 ≲ T ≲ 0.35 MeV and increasing at 0.35 ≲ T ≲ 0.5 MeV
- For ¹⁶¹Dy, the S-shape is less conspicuous around T_c ≈ 0.6 MeV, and small non-vanishing heat capacity appears at low T (0.05 ≲ T ≲ 0.2 MeV)
- The model space included all single-particle levels satisfying |ε_k − ε_F| < 7 MeV for both protons and neutrons

## Context

This work reexamines the claim by Schiller et al. (2001) that S-shaped heat capacity curves are a fingerprint of the superfluid-to-normal phase transition in nuclei, which was based on continuity between the constant-T model for the superfluid phase and the backshifted Bethe formula for the normal fluid phase. The authors extend earlier finite-temperature BCS work by Goodman (1981) and the number-parity projection formalism of Tanabe, Sugawara-Tanabe, and Matsuo (1981), showing that particle-number conservation effects alone can produce S-shapes. The results suggest that while the S-shapes may still correlate with the phase transition, their relation must be carefully inspected, and conservation laws should not be discarded when studying phase transitions in finite systems.
