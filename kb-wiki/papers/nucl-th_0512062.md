# Theory of the two-proton radioactivity in the continuum shell model

> We develop the microscopic description of the two-nucleon radioactivity in the framework of the Shell Model Embedded in the Continuum. This approach is applied for the description of spontaneous two-proton radioactivity in $^{45}$Fe, $^{48}$Ni and $^{54}$Zn.

## Key claim

The paper develops a microscopic description of two-proton radioactivity within the Shell Model Embedded in the Continuum (SMEC) framework, extending it to include two-particle scattering continua. The authors note that the present formalism allows extraction of nucleon-nucleon correlation features from both the decay width and correlations between emitted protons, but that currently available experimental data only address the first of these theoretical challenges.

## Method

The authors formulate the SMEC with two-particle scattering continuum by decomposing the total Hilbert space into three subspaces: the discrete shell-model states (Q), the one-particle continuum (P), and the two-particle continuum (T). They derive an energy-dependent effective Hamiltonian in the Q subspace that includes couplings to both P and T subspaces, then apply this formalism to describe three limiting cases of two-proton emission: direct diproton cluster emission, sequential emission through intermediate resonances in the A-1 nucleus, and sequential emission through correlated continuum states. The residual interaction between discrete states and the scattering continuum is taken as a Wigner-Bartlett zero-range interaction, and the diproton decay with three-body asymptotics is restricted to the scenario of (2p) cluster emission incorporating final-state interaction between protons via s-wave phase shifts.

## Context

This work extends the SMEC formalism, previously limited to one-particle continuum applications such as (p,p') reactions, radiative capture, Coulomb dissociation, and beta decay (Karim, Benb, and others, early 2000s), to include two-particle scattering continua for describing two-proton radioactivity. The approach builds on the Continuum Shell Model foundation of Feshbach and the Berggren single-particle basis used in the Gamow Shell Model (GSM) of Michel and collaborators (2002), but unlike GSM, the SMEC formalism includes projection onto asymptotic decay channels. The paper enables future studies of proton-proton spatial-momentum correlations in two-proton decay to probe pairing correlations, and compares with earlier R-matrix studies by Barker and theoretical models by Grigorenko, Fedorov, and others.
