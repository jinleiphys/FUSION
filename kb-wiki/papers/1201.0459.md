# Symmetry Energy and Universality classes of holographic QCD

> We study nuclear symmetry energy of dense matter using holographic QCD. We calculate it in a various holographic QCD models and show that the scaling index of the symmetry energy in dense medium is almost invariant under the smooth deformation of the metric as well as the embedding shape of the probe brane. We find that the scaling index depends only on the dimensionality of the branes and space-time. Therefore the scaling index of the symmetry energy characterizes the universality classes of holographic QCD models. We suggest that the scaling index might be also related to the non-fermi liquid behavior of the interacting nucleons.

## Key claim

The scaling index (power-law exponent) of the nuclear symmetry energy with respect to baryon density is nearly invariant under smooth deformations of the holographic metric and of the probe brane embedding shape, depending only on the dimensionality of the color and flavor branes; this index therefore defines universality classes of holographic QCD models, and the authors suggest it may relate to non-Fermi liquid behavior of interacting nucleons.

## Method

The authors use gauge/gravity duality to compute the nuclear symmetry energy in dense matter by introducing two flavor Dq probe branes (for up and down quarks) in the metric background of Nc Dp color branes, in both confining and deconfining geometries. They solve the equations of motion for the probe brane embeddings with a given charge (baryon density) and quark mass, enforce a force balance condition between the baryon vertex and the probe brane, then substitute the numerical embedding solutions into the symmetry energy formula derived from the D-brane action to obtain the density dependence.

## Key numbers

- For D6 probe brane in D4 confining background (3+1 boundary theory): symmetry energy scales as S2 ~ ρ^(1/2), with fitted coefficient S0 between 27 MeV and 36 MeV for quark masses from 5 MeV upward.
- For D7 probe brane in D3 confining background (3+1 boundary theory): symmetry energy scales as S2 ~ Q̃^(1/3), with fitted coefficient S0 between 0.21 and 0.31 (in units of 1/(4π α')).
- For D5 probe brane in D3 confining background (2+1 boundary theory): symmetry energy scales as S2 ~ Q̃^(1/2), with fitted coefficient S0 between 0.34 and 0.5.
- For D4 probe brane in D4 confining background (2+1 boundary theory): symmetry energy scales linearly with density (S2 ~ Q).
- For D2 probe brane in D4 confining background (1+1 boundary theory): symmetry energy is of order constant (O(1)) and nearly independent of quark mass.
- For D3 probe brane in D3 confining background (1+1 boundary theory): symmetry energy grows linearly for small density.
- In deconfined phase at low temperature, D6 probe in D4 black hole background gives S2 = 0.5 Q̃^(1/2); at high temperature it becomes linear in density.
- In deconfined phase at low temperature, D7 probe in D3 black hole background gives S2 = 0.32 Q̃^(1/3); at high temperature it becomes linear.
- The scaling exponent 2ν = n/d, where n = q-d-1 and d is the number of spatial dimensions of the boundary theory, equals 2/3 for D6/D4, 1/2 for D4/D4, 1 for D7/D3, 1 for D5/D3, and 1 for D3/D3.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous holographic calculation of nuclear symmetry energy (Kim et al., 2010; Seo et al., 2008; KSS2010) which found stiff density dependence (~ρ^(1/2)) attributed to Pauli repulsion and anomalous dispersion. It examines the robustness of that result across multiple holographic QCD models (D4 and D3 brane backgrounds with various probe branes), showing the scaling index is universal under metric and embedding deformations. The finding enables classification of holographic QCD models into universality classes based on the symmetry energy scaling exponent, and suggests a possible connection to non-Fermi liquid behavior in strongly interacting dense matter.
