# Determination of $α$-optical potential for reactions with p-nuclei from the study of ($α$,n) reactions in the astrophysically relevant energy region

> Optical potential parameters in nuclear model calculations are determined by fitting elastic scattering angular distribution data. Due to the dominance of Coulomb part, elastic scattering is performed at much higher energies. A different approach using ($α$,n) reaction is suggested to determine the alpha optical potentials suitable for astrophysically relevant low energy regions. Reaction on p-nuclei in the mass region A $\approx$ 92-168 have been chosen and a modified McFadden-Satchler $α$-optical potential is obtained from fitting the ($α$,n) reaction data. The effect of level density and $γ$-ray strength function is also studied using this new potential by analyzing ($α,γ$) cross-sections.

# Determination of α-optical potential for reactions with p-nuclei from the study of (α,n) reactions in the astrophysically relevant energy region

## Key claim

A modified McFadden-Satchler alpha optical potential, derived from fitting (α,n) reaction data rather than elastic scattering angular distributions, can describe (α,n) and (α,γ) cross-sections for p-nuclei in the mass region A ≈ 92-168 at astrophysically relevant low energies, but only for those nuclei where the (α,n) cross-section is predominantly sensitive to the α-width (with non-α-width contributions less than 25%).

## Method

Hauser-Feshbach statistical model calculations were performed using the TALYS code (version 1.95) for (α,n) and (α,γ) reactions on ten even-even p-nuclei (92,94Mo, 96Ru, 106,108Cd, 112Sn, 124Xe, 130Ba, 162Er, 168Yb). The McFadden-Satchler global alpha optical potential was modified by introducing an energy-dependent Fermi function on both the real and imaginary depth parameters, with the energy diffusivity parameter a_c set to 10, and the potential parameters were fitted to experimental (α,n) cross-section data. The resulting potential was then tested on (α,γ) cross-sections, with additional variation of level density models (Back-Shifted Fermi Gas model) and γ-ray strength functions (Brink-Axel Lorentzian).

## Key numbers

- The modified McFadden-Satchler potential uses real depth V0 = 185 / [1 + exp((0.9E_cu - E_cm)/a_c)] and imaginary depth Wv = 25 / [1 + exp((0.9E_cu - E_cm)/a_c)], with radius parameters r_v = 1.40 fm, r_w = 1.40 fm, diffusivities a_v = 0.52 fm, a_w = 0.52 fm, and Coulomb radius r_c = 1.30 fm.
- The energy diffusivity parameter a_c was varied between 2 and 12, with best fit obtained at a_c = 10.
- For the (α,n) reaction on 92Mo, the χ²/N value improved from 188.85 (McFadden-Satchler) to 13.99 (modified McFadden-Satchler); for 96Ru, from 1413.44 to 11.83; for 108Cd, from 137.69 to 8.46; for 124Xe, from 581.28 to 10.39.
- For the (α,γ) reaction on 92Mo, the χ²/N value improved from 71.28 (McFadden-Satchler) to 5.304 (modified McFadden-Satchler with BSFGM and BAL); for 94Mo, from 65.45 to 2.073; for 108Cd, from 25.71 to 1.94.
- The (α,n) reactions suitable for potential extraction (non-α-width contributions less than 25%) involve 92,94Mo, 96Ru, 108Cd, and 130Ba.
- The modified potential parameters F1 and F2 vary with (N-Z): for (N-Z) = 8-16 (excluding 10), F1 = 1 and F2 = 1; for (N-Z) = 18-28, F1 = 0 and F2 = 1; for (N-Z) = 10, F1 = 0 and F2 = 0.

## Context

This work addresses the long-standing discrepancy between Hauser-Feshbach predictions and measured alpha capture cross-sections on p-nuclei, which previous studies (e.g., those cited as references r, s, t, u, v, w, x, y) attributed primarily to inappropriate alpha optical model potentials. The approach of modifying only the imaginary depth of the McFadden-Satchler potential had been attempted earlier (references k1, l1, m1, n1) but gave satisfactory results only for heavier nuclei (A > 130). The present work extends this by modifying both real and imaginary depths with an energy-dependent Fermi function, enabling description of (α,γ) cross-sections across a broader mass range when combined with the Back-Shifted Fermi Gas level density model and Brink-Axel Lorentzian γ-ray strength function. The results enable more reliable reaction rate calculations for γ-process nucleosynthesis networks involving p-nuclei.
