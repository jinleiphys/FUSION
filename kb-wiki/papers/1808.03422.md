# Generalized distribution amplitudes and gravitational form factors for pion

> Generalized parton distributions (GPDs) have been investigated in the deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) to solve the proton spin puzzle. On the other hand, the generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs) can be studied in the two-photon process $γ^* γ\rightarrow h \bar{h}$ which is accessible at KEKB. Namely, the GDAs are the $s$-$t$ crossed quantities of the GPDs. In 2016, the differential cross section of the process $γ^* γ\rightarrow π^0 π^0$ was measured by the Belle collaboration in the $e^+e^-$collision , so that the pion GDAs can be obtained by analyzing the Belle data. Expressing the GDAs with a few parameters, we determined the GDAs by a $χ^2$ analysis . The form factors of the quark energy-momentum tensor are obtained from the determined GDAs. Then, we calculated the mass radius as 0.56-0.69 fm for the pion by using the form factor. This is the first study on gravitational form factors and radius of hadrons from actual experimental measurements . The Belle II will start taking data in 2018 by the upgraded SuperKEKB. Therefore, much accurate data are expected for the pion, and other hadron-pair productions will be measured in the near future. The GDA studies are valuable for understanding not only the 3D structure but also gravitational properties of hadrons.

# Generalized distribution amplitudes and gravitational form factors for pion

## Key claim

The pion's gravitational form factors and mass radius (0.56–0.69 fm) are extracted for the first time from actual experimental measurements, specifically the Belle data on the two-photon process γ*γ → π⁰π⁰, but the result carries a systematic uncertainty from the choice of how additional phase shifts above the KK threshold are assigned between S-wave and D-wave channels.

## Method

The generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs) for the pion were parameterized with a simple functional form including S-wave and D-wave contributions from the f₀(500) and f₂(1270) resonances, and the parameters were determined by a χ² fit to the Belle differential cross section data for γ*γ → π⁰π⁰ at fixed Q² values. From the extracted GDAs, the timelike gravitational form factors Θ₁(s) and Θ₂(s) of the quark energy-momentum tensor were computed, and the spacelike form factors were obtained via a dispersion relation. The three-dimensional root-mean-square radii were then calculated from the slopes of the spacelike form factors at t=0.

## Key numbers

- Mass radius of the pion: √⟨r²⟩_mass = 0.56–0.69 fm, depending on the treatment of the S-wave phase shift above the KK threshold.
- Mechanical radius of the pion: √⟨r²⟩_mech = 1.45–1.56 fm, from the same analysis.
- χ²/d.o.f. = 1.09 for the GDA fit to the Belle data.
- The charge radius of the pion from prior measurements is √⟨r²⟩_charge = 0.672 ± 0.008 fm (Patrignani 2016), provided for comparison.
- The differential cross section data were analyzed at fixed Q² values of 8.92 GeV² and 13.37 GeV², with cosθ fixed at 0.1 and 0.5.

## Context

This work extends the study of generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs), which are the s-t crossed quantities of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) studied in deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) for the proton spin puzzle. The analysis uses the Belle data from 2016 (Masuda 2015) on γ*γ → π⁰π⁰, and the GDA formalism follows the theoretical framework of Diehl (1998, 2000) and Polyakov (1998). This is the first extraction of gravitational form factors and radii of hadrons from experimental data, and it enables future studies with the higher-luminosity Belle II experiment, which will provide more precise data for pion and other hadron-pair production channels.
