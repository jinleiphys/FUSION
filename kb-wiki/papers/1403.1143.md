# Magnetized strange quark matter in a mass-density-dependent model

> We investigate the properties of strange quark matter in a strong magnetic field with quark confinement by the density dependence of quark mass considering the total baryon number conservation, charge neutrality and chemical equilibrium. The strength of the magnetic field considered in this article is $10^{16} \sim 10^{20}$ G. It is found that an additional term should appear in the pressure expression to maintain thermodynamic consistency. At fixed density, the energy density of magnetized strange quark matter varies with the magnetic field strength. The exists a minimum with increasing the field strength, depending on the density. It is about $6\times10^{19}$ Gauss at two times the normal nuclear saturation density.

## Key claim

The energy density of magnetized strange quark matter exhibits a minimum as a function of magnetic field strength, but only when the pure magnetic field contribution is excluded; when the magnetic field energy density \(B_m^2/2\) is included, the total energy density increases monotonically and the minimum disappears.

## Method

The authors use the confinement density-dependent mass (CDDM) model with a cubic-root mass scaling \(m_q = m_{q0} + D / n^{1/3}\) to study strange quark matter in a uniform magnetic field of strength \(10^{16}\) to \(10^{20}\) G. They enforce baryon number conservation, charge neutrality, and chemical equilibrium among \(u\), \(d\), \(s\) quarks and electrons, and derive an additional term in the pressure expression to maintain thermodynamic consistency due to the density dependence of quark masses. The equation of state is then used to compute the mass-radius relation of strange quark stars by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations.

## Key numbers

- The minimum of the energy density of magnetized strange quark matter (excluding the magnetic field contribution) occurs at about \(6\times10^{19}\) G for a baryon number density of \(n = 2n_0\), where \(n_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The energy density begins to decrease when the magnetic field exceeds a critical value of about \(3\times10^{18}\) G for \(n = 2n_0\).
- The confinement parameter \(D^{1/2}\) is treated as a free parameter in the range \((156, 270)\) MeV.
- The strange quark current mass is taken as \(95 \pm 25\) MeV; the light quark current masses are set to zero.
- The electron mass is fixed at \(m_e = 0.511\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the CDDM model with cubic-root mass scaling, previously developed by Peng (1999) and Wen (2005), to include strong magnetic fields. It contrasts with earlier studies using the MIT bag model by Chakrabarty (1996) and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model by Ebert (2003) and Menezes (2009), which treated quark masses as constant or field-dependent in different ways. The results enable future work on a quark mass scaling that includes both confinement and perturbative interactions, as the current model cannot produce quark stars with masses as large as \(2 M_\odot\) as observed by Demorest (2010) and Antoniadis.
