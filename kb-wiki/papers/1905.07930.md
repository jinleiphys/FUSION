# Effects of pairing, continuum, and deformation on particles in the classically forbidden regions for Mg isotopes

> Particles in the classically forbidden regions are studied based on the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum with PC-PK1 for magnesium isotopes. By analyzing the neutron and proton radii, it is found that the largest deviations from the empirical values appear at the predicted neutron halo nuclei $^{42}$Mg and $^{44}$Mg. Consistently, notable increases at $^{42}$Mg and $^{44}$Mg are found in the total number of neutrons in the classically forbidden regions that includes the number of neutrons in continuum. It is shown that the deformation effect, in general, increases the number of particles in the classically forbidden regions below the continuum threshold. The most deeply bound single-particle states play the dominant roles in the increase caused by deformation.

## Key claim
The paper claims that deformation generally increases the number of particles in the classically forbidden regions below the continuum threshold, with the most deeply bound single-particle states playing the dominant role in this increase; however, this conclusion is qualified by the observation that the effect is most pronounced for strongly deformed nuclei such as $^{24}$Mg and that the coupling to continuum (rather than deformation alone) drives the notable increases at the predicted halo nuclei $^{42}$Mg and $^{44}$Mg.

## Method
The authors applied the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) and the spherical relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) theory, both using the PC-PK1 density functional, to even-even magnesium isotopes from the proton drip line to the neutron drip line. They calculated the number of neutrons and protons in the classically forbidden regions by integrating the single-particle densities beyond the classical turning point defined by the mean-field potential, including contributions from continuum states. Constrained DRHBc calculations without pairing were performed for $^{24}$Mg at various quadrupole deformations $\beta_2$ to isolate the deformation effect.

## Key numbers
- The ground state quadrupole deformation of $^{24}$Mg is $\beta_2 \approx 0.60$, consistent with the experimental value $\beta_2 = 0.609(6)$ from 2016ADNDT.
- For $^{24}$Mg in constraint DRHBc calculations without pairing, the neutron number in the classically forbidden regions increases from 1.331 at $\beta_2 = 0.1$ to 1.847 at $\beta_2 = 0.6$.
- In $^{44}$Mg, more than 1.5 neutrons are scattered into continuum in the DRHBc theory, compared to only about 0.5 neutrons in the RCHB theory.
- For the weakly bound states in $^{44}$Mg, the $p$-wave components are 69.6% for the $(1/2)_5^-$ state and 81.5% for the $(3/2)_3^-$ state, while the $(5/2)_1^-$ and $(3/2)_2^-$ states have dominant $f$-wave components of 97.4% and 80.1%, respectively.

## Context
This work extends the earlier study of particles in classically forbidden regions for calcium isotopes using the spherical Skyrme Hartree-Fock theory by Im (2000) to include pairing, continuum, and deformation simultaneously via the DRHBc framework. It builds on prior predictions of halo phenomena in $^{42}$Mg and $^{44}$Mg from Zhou (2010) and Li (2012) using the same DRHBc theory. The results enable future investigations of how deformation and continuum coupling affect halo formation and density distributions in neutron-rich nuclei, particularly for isotopes near the neutron drip line.
