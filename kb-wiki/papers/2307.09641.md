# A new metric improving Bayesian calibration of a multistage approach studying hadron and inclusive jet suppression

> We study parton energy-momentum exchange with the quark gluon plasma (QGP) within a multistage approach composed of in-medium DGLAP evolution at high virtuality, and (linearized) Boltzmann Transport formalism at lower virtuality. This multistage simulation is then calibrated in comparison with high $p_T$ charged hadrons, D-mesons, and the inclusive jet nuclear modification factors, using Bayesian model-to-data comparison, to extract the virtuality-dependent transverse momentum broadening transport coefficient $\hat{q}$. To facilitate this undertaking, we develop a quantitative metric for validating the Bayesian workflow, which is used to analyze the sensitivity of various model parameters to individual observables. The usefulness of this new metric in improving Bayesian model emulation is shown to be highly beneficial for future such analyses.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a new quantitative metric based on Kullback-Leibler divergence within closure tests to validate Gaussian Process emulator performance in Bayesian calibration of a multistage parton energy loss model, though the Bayesian analysis is limited to central (0-10%) Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC and currently yields large statistical uncertainty for heavy flavor observables.

## Method

The authors simulate parton energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma using a multistage approach within the JETSCAPE framework: high-virtuality parton evolution is described by MATTER using in-medium DGLAP evolution based on the higher-twist formalism, while lower-virtuality evolution uses the linearized Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model. The QGP medium is evolved using a 2+1-dimensional boost-invariant model with TRENTo initial conditions, free-streaming, hydrodynamics, and UrQMD hadronic transport. Bayesian model-to-data comparison is performed using Gaussian Process emulators trained on Latin hypercube-sampled design points, with principal component analysis for dimensional reduction, to constrain four model parameters against charged hadron, D-meson, and inclusive jet nuclear modification factors.

## Key numbers

- The switching virtuality between MATTER and LBT stages, t_s = Q_s^2, is treated as a free parameter tuned via Bayesian methods.
- The pre-hydrodynamic free-streaming proper time is tau_FS = 1.2 fm/c.
- 400 TRENTo initial Pb-Pb configurations were generated within the 0-10% centrality class at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV.
- The hydrodynamical simulation stops when fluid cells reach below T_c = 154 MeV.
- The connection time between PYTHIA shower and energy loss models is 0.6 fm/c.
- The effective coupling in LBT has a fixed component alpha_s^(eff) = 0.3.
- The minimum virtuality for heavy quark splitting requires t_min = 2(M^2 + t_0), with t_0 = 1 GeV^2.
- The Debye mass is m^2_D = 6*pi*alpha_s^(eff)*T^2 for N_f = 3.
- The running coupling scale is mu^2_0 = 1 GeV^2.

## Context

This work extends previous JETSCAPE studies (JETSCAPE 2022, JETSCAPE 2023) that explored the effects of model parameters on nuclear modification factors but did not quantify full parameter correlations. It builds on Bayesian calibration approaches developed for heavy-ion collisions by Bernhard (2019), Nijs (2020), and earlier JETSCAPE Bayesian analyses (2021). The new metric for validating Gaussian Process emulators addresses a need identified in large-scale Bayesian analyses of heavy-ion collisions where computational cost necessitates surrogate models, and enables more reliable extraction of the virtuality-dependent transverse momentum broadening transport coefficient q-hat for future studies.
