# Hot spots in a proton

> We explore the consequences of gluonic hot spots inside the proton for the initial eccentricities in a proton-nucleus collision, and the constraints on the parameters describing these hot spots from coherent and incoherent exclusive vector meson production cross sections in deep inelastic scattering. We show that geometric fluctuations of hot spots inside the proton are the dominant source of eccentricity whereas color charge fluctuations only give a negligible correction. We find that the coherent cross section is sensitive to both the size of the target and the structure of the probe. The incoherent cross section is dominated by color fluctuations at small transverse momentum transfer (t), by proton and hot spot sizes as well as the structure of the probe at medium -t and again by color fluctuations at large -t.

# Hot spots in a proton

## Key claim
Geometric fluctuations of hot spots inside the proton are the dominant source of eccentricity in proton-nucleus collisions, while color charge fluctuations give only a negligible correction; however, for realistic charm quark masses, the interpretation of the t-dependence of the vector meson cross section deviates significantly from the infinite quark mass limit.

## Method
The authors construct an analytically tractable hot spot model where color charges are averaged over configurations using a McLerran-Venugopalan-type two-point function, and hot spot positions are averaged over a Gaussian distribution within a proton of radius R. They calculate eccentricities from the energy density two-point correlator at proper time τ=0 in a dilute-dense limit, and compute coherent and incoherent exclusive vector meson production cross sections using a nonrelativistic meson light-cone wavefunction within the Color Glass Condensate framework.

## Key numbers
- Eccentricities ε_n{2} for harmonics n=2,3,4 are calculated as a function of the number of hot spots N_q, with error bars corresponding to varying the IR regulator m by ±50%
- The coherent cross section slope at small |t| is not simply R_c² = (N_q-1)/N_q * R² + r_H² as expected from color charges, but broader by ~1/m
- The incoherent cross section is sensitive to the hot spot radius at intermediate |t| ~ 1/r_H²
- For realistic charm quark masses, the large-|t| tail of the incoherent cross section deviates from the ideal m_Q → ∞ limit

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous analytical hot spot model (Demirci 2021, Demirci 2023) for eccentricities in proton-nucleus collisions and exclusive vector meson production (Demirci 2022). It builds on earlier hot spot approaches including the energy-dependent model of Cepila (2017), the IPglasma model of Mantysaari (2016) for heavy ion collisions, and the hot spot model of Albacete (2016) for proton hollowness. The results enable constraints on hot spot parameters from HERA data, though the need for additional fluctuations (such as fluctuating N_q) is identified to match the normalization of the incoherent cross section.
