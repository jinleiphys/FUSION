# A theoretical overview of isospin and EOS effects in heavy-ion reactions at intermediate energies

> The isospin dependence of in-medium nuclear effective interactions is a fundamental issue in nuclear physics and has broad ramifications in astrophysics. Its uncertainties, especially the difference of neutron-proton interactions in the isosinglet and isotriplet channels, affect significantly the density and momentum dependence of the isovector single-nucleon potential and nucleon-nucleon short-range correlation in neutron-rich matter. Consequently, the neutron-proton effective mass splitting and the density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy are still rather uncertain. Heavy-ion reactions especially those involving rare isotopes is a useful tool for probing the isospin dependence of nuclear effective interactions through (1) the neutron-skin in coordinate and proton-skin in momentum of the initial state of colliding nuclei, (2) the density and momentum dependence of especially the isovector nuclear mean-field as well as (3) the isospin dependence of in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections. Observations of neutron stars especially since GW1710817 have also helped us significantly in understanding the isospin dependence of nuclear effective interactions. {\it We summarize here a review talk on these issues given at the 2021 International Workshop on multi-facets of EOS and Clustering. For details we refer the readers to the original publications and references therein}.

## Key claim
This review talk summarizes the current understanding of isospin and equation-of-state (EOS) effects in heavy-ion reactions at intermediate energies, emphasizing that while significant progress has been made in constraining the nuclear symmetry energy below about twice saturation density, the neutron-proton effective mass splitting and the high-density behavior of the symmetry energy remain poorly constrained due to model dependences in transport simulations and uncertainties in the isospin dependence of nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations.

## Method
The paper reviews the theoretical framework linking the isospin-dependent single-nucleon potential (decomposed into isoscalar and isovector components) to the nuclear symmetry energy and its slope, curvature, and skewness parameters. It surveys results from transport model analyses of heavy-ion reactions (e.g., using the IBUU model with Gogny Hartree-Fock potentials), optical model analyses of nucleon-nucleus scattering data, and Bayesian analyses of giant resonance data, comparing these with constraints from neutron star observations (GW170817, NICER+XMM-Newton measurements of PSR J0740+6620). The review also discusses a differential approach and a Skyrme-Hartree-Fock plus random-phase-approximation (SHF+RPA) Bayesian inference for extracting the incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter from giant monopole resonance data.

## Key numbers
- The slope parameter of the symmetry energy at saturation density from neutron star analyses since GW170817: L ≈ 57.7 ± 19 MeV.
- The curvature parameter of the symmetry energy at saturation density from neutron star analyses: K_sym ≈ -107 ± 88 MeV.
- The symmetry energy at twice saturation density from eleven analyses of heavy-ion reactions and neutron stars: E_sym(2ρ_0) ≈ 51 ± 13 MeV at 68% confidence level.
- The incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter from giant monopole resonance analyses: K_0 in the range 220 to 260 MeV.
- The skewness parameter J_0 of symmetric nuclear matter from a Bayesian calibration using flow data: J_0 = -240^{+110}_{-120} MeV at 68% confidence level.
- The kurtosis parameter Z_0 of symmetric nuclear matter from the same Bayesian calibration: Z_0 = 250^{+300}_{-250} MeV at 68% confidence level.
- The neutron-proton effective mass splitting at saturation density from a survey of analyses: positive values consistent with chiral effective field theory predictions, with one exception from a Bayesian analysis of Sn+Sn reactions giving m*_n-p(ρ_0,δ) = (-0.05 ± 0.09)δ.

## Context
This review extends the long-standing effort to constrain the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy and the isospin dependence of in-medium nuclear effective interactions, building on earlier systematics such as those summarized in Li (2013) and the transport model analyses of Danielewicz (2002). It compares results from terrestrial experiments (nucleon-nucleus scattering, giant resonances, heavy-ion reactions at facilities like NSCL/MSU and GANIL) with multi-messenger astrophysical constraints from neutron star mergers (GW170817) and pulsar mass-radius measurements (NICER+XMM-Newton). The work highlights the need for improved constraints on the symmetry energy above 2ρ_0 and on the neutron-proton effective mass splitting, which remain open questions for future radioactive beam experiments and more precise neutron star observations.
