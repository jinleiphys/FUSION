# Two-detector flavor sensitivity to ultra-high-energy cosmic neutrinos

> Ultra-high-energy (UHE) cosmic neutrinos, with energies above 100 PeV, could be finally discovered in the near future. Measuring their flavor composition would reveal information about their production and propagation, but new techniques are needed for UHE neutrino telescopes to have the capabilities to do it. We address this by proposing a new way to measure the UHE neutrino flavor composition that does not rely on individual telescopes having flavor-identification capabilities. We manufacture flavor sensitivity from the joint detection by one telescope sensitive to all flavors -- the radio array of IceCube-Gen2 -- and one mostly sensitive to $ν_τ$ -- GRAND. From this limited flavor sensitivity, predominantly to $ν_τ$, and even under conservative choices of neutrino flux and detector size, we extract important insight. For astrophysics, we forecast meaningful constraints on the neutrino production mechanism; for fundamental physics, vastly improved constraints on Lorentz-invariance violation. These are the first measurement forecasts of the UHE $ν_τ$ content.

## Key claim
By combining the all-flavor radio array of IceCube-Gen2 with the predominantly tau-neutrino-sensitive GRAND detector, the flavor composition of ultra-high-energy (UHE) cosmic neutrinos can be measured even though neither detector individually has flavor-identification capabilities, yielding the first measurement forecasts of the UHE tau-neutrino content.

## Method
The authors compute expected event rates after 10 years of exposure for IceCube-Gen2 (all flavors) and GRAND (tau neutrinos only) using flavor-specific effective areas, with the diffuse UHE neutrino flux modeled as a log-parabola. They construct a combined Poisson likelihood over energy bins from 10^7 to 10^10 GeV, profiling over flux shape parameters (normalization, width, peak energy) to extract constraints on the flavor composition at Earth and, by undoing oscillations, at the sources.

## Key numbers
- The high benchmark flux yields approximately 100 events in 10 years in IceCube-Gen2 and GRAND50k combined; the low benchmark flux yields approximately 10 events.
- The benchmark flux shape parameters are fixed to phi_0 = 2.2 x 10^-7 GeV cm^-2 s^-1 sr^-1, w = 0.18, and E_bump = 5 x 10^8 GeV for the high flux; the low flux uses a normalization ten times smaller.
- For the high flux, the true flavor composition from pion decay can be distinguished from the muon-damped scenario at nearly 95% confidence level and from neutron decay at more than that; for the low flux, the three benchmarks become indistinguishable at 68% confidence level.

## Context
This work extends the focus of UHE neutrino studies from discovery potential (e.g., Valera 2022, Fiorillo 2022) to flavor composition measurement, which had received little attention for UHE energies. It enables future constraints on neutrino production mechanisms (distinguishing pion decay, muon-damped, and neutron decay scenarios) and on Lorentz-invariance violation, improving limits by many orders of magnitude over existing constraints from IceCube observations of atmospheric neutrinos (IceCube 2017) and TeV-PeV astrophysical neutrinos (IceCube 2021).
