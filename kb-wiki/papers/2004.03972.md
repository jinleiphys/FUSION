# Hybrid Quantum Annealing via Molecular Dynamics

> A novel quantum-classical hybrid scheme is proposed to efficiently solve large-scale combinatorial optimization problems. The key concept is to introduce a Hamiltonian dynamics of the classical flux variables associated with the quantum spins of the transverse-field Ising model. Molecular dynamics of the classical fluxes can be used as a powerful preconditioner to sort out the frozen and ambivalent spins for quantum annealers. The performance and accuracy of our smooth hybridization in comparison to the standard classical algorithms (the tabu search and the simulated annealing) are demonstrated by employing the MAX-CUT and Ising spin-glass problems.

## Key claim

The authors propose a quantum-classical hybrid scheme (HQA-MD) that uses classical molecular dynamics of continuous flux variables as a preconditioner to identify "frozen" and "ambivalent" spins, then solves only the reduced ambivalent-spin subsystem on a quantum annealer or classical solver, achieving improved accuracy over purely classical methods for large-scale combinatorial optimization problems.

## Method

The method introduces a classical Hamiltonian for continuous flux variables and their conjugate momenta, with scheduling functions that mimic the transverse-field Ising model's adiabatic evolution. The Hamilton equations are solved via a leapfrog algorithm on a GPGPU machine, and time-averaged flux variables at the final time are sorted by magnitude: the n variables with smallest magnitude are classified as "ambivalent" and the rest as "frozen." The frozen spins are fixed by the sign of their time-averaged flux, while the ambivalent spins define a reduced Ising subsystem that is solved by embedding into a D-Wave quantum annealer (for n=48) or by classical tabu search (for n up to 4000).

## Key numbers

- For the MAX-CUT problem on a 2000-node complete graph (K2000) with random bimodal edge weights ±1, the theoretical maximum cut is C* = 33,933(4) from finite-size scaling analysis.
- MD alone reaches 0.4% deviation from C* after 500,000 MD steps, compared to 0.6% for simulated annealing (1000 sweeps) and 0.8% for tabu search under comparable computational time.
- HQA(DW48) with the same 500,000 MD steps reaches 0.3% accuracy; HQA(TS1000) reaches 0.2% accuracy.
- The ratio of computational time for the quantum solver (DW48) to MD (500,000 steps) is 0.007, excluding cloud connection; the ratio for TS1000 to MD is 0.24.
- For the Ising spin-glass problem with N=10,000, the precision of simulated annealing with 1000 sweeps can be matched by MD alone with 50,000 MD steps, and simulated annealing with 10,000 sweeps can be matched by MD with 500,000 MD steps.

## Context

This work extends the line of hybrid quantum-classical optimization algorithms that decompose large problems into subproblems for NISQ devices, such as the sample-persistency approaches of Karimi et al. and the QBSolv framework. The classical MD component shares similarities with the Spin-vector Monte Carlo (SVMC) method of Shin et al. (2014), coherent Ising machines (CIM), and simulated bifurcation machines (SBM). The HQA scheme enables the use of current limited-size quantum annealers (48 qubits on the D-Wave 2000Q) to improve solutions for problems with thousands of variables, and the authors suggest future extensions to non-stoquastic interactions and multi-valued variables.
