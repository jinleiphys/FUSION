# Erosion of N = 28 shell closure: Shape coexistence and monopole transition

> Background: In neutron-rich nuclei neighboring 42Si, the quenching of the N = 28 shell gap occurs and is expected to induce the shape coexistence in their excitation spectra. Purpose: We show that different nuclear shapes coexist in N = 28 isotones 40Mg, 42Si, and 44S, and investigate observables to probe it. Method: Antisymmetrized molecular dynamics with Gogny D1S density functional is applied to describe the shape coexistence phenomena without ad hoc assumption of the nuclear shape. Results: We find that rigid shapes with different deformations coexist in the ground and the first excited 0+ states of 40Mg and 42Si, while in 44S the states exhibit large-amplitude collective motion, which does not have any particular shape. These characteristics are reflected well in the monopole transition strengths. Conclusion: The quenching of the N = 28 shell gap leads to the unique shape coexistence in the N = 28 isotones, which can be probed by the monopole transition strengths.

# Erosion of N = 28 shell closure: Shape coexistence and monopole transition

## Key claim
The quenching of the N=28 shell gap leads to shape coexistence in the N=28 isotones 40Mg, 42Si, and 44S, but the specific character of that coexistence differs qualitatively across the three nuclei: rigid prolate-oblate coexistence in 40Mg, oblate-spherical coexistence in 42Si, and large-amplitude collective motion without a definite shape in 44S. The monopole transition strength between the ground and first excited 0+ states is proposed as a probe that can distinguish these different coexistence patterns.

## Method
The authors applied antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) with the Gogny D1S density functional to describe the N=28 isotones 40Mg, 42Si, and 44S without ad hoc assumptions about nuclear shape. Wave functions were parity-projected Slater determinants of deformed Gaussian single-particle wave packets, optimized by energy variation with constraints on quadrupole deformation parameters beta and gamma, then angular-momentum projected and superposed via the generator coordinate method (GCM) to describe shape fluctuations. Neutron single-particle energies and orbits were extracted from the resulting wave functions.

## Key numbers
- 40Mg: energy minimum at (beta, gamma) = (0.36, 14 degrees) in the prolate region; local oblate minimum at (0.31, 44 degrees) lying 2.8 MeV higher
- 42Si: ground state localized around oblate minimum at (beta, gamma) = (0.31, 44 degrees); 0+2 state distributed in region with beta < 0.2
- 44S: energy minimum at (beta, gamma) = (0.31, 16 degrees) but energy changes by only 1 MeV as a function of gamma at fixed beta = 0.3
- B(E2; 2+1 -> 0+1): 97 e2 fm4 (40Mg), 73 e2 fm4 (42Si), 77 e2 fm4 (44S); experimental value for 44S is 63(18) e2 fm4 (Glasmacher 1997) and 44(6) e2 fm4 (Longfellow 2021)
- B(E2; 2+1 -> 0+2): 0 e2 fm4 (40Mg), 7 e2 fm4 (42Si), 4 e2 fm4 (44S); experimental value for 44S is 8.4(26) e2 fm4 (Force 2010)
- B(E0; 0+2 -> 0+1): 0.0 Weisskopf units (Wu) for 40Mg, 0.2 Wu for 42Si, 0.04 Wu for 44S; experimental value for 44S is 0.022(2) Wu (Grevy 2005)
- B(IS0; 0+2 -> 0+1): 0.0 Wu for 40Mg, 2.3 Wu for 42Si, 0.38 Wu for 44S
- Neutron occupation numbers of p-wave orbits: 2.0 (40Mg), 2.1 (42Si), 1.7 (44S) from Suzuki (2021)

## Context
This work extends the authors' prior study of ground-state deformation in N=28 isotones (Suzuki 2021) by focusing on excited 0+ states and shape coexistence. It builds on experimental evidence for shape coexistence in 44S (Santiago-Gonzalez 2011, Utsuno 2015) and earlier theoretical studies (Kimura 2016), but clarifies how the mechanism of N=28 shell quenching depends on nuclear shape. The results enable experimental tests via monopole transition measurements, which are predicted to be strongly suppressed in 40Mg, enhanced in 42Si, and intermediate in 44S, providing a clearer diagnostic than neutron occupation numbers.
