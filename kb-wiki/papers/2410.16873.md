# Coupled-channel analysis of the near-threshold $e^+e^-\to N\bar{N}$ cross sections

> The possible existence of nucleon-antinucleon bound states has been studied for decades. We investigate the $e^+e^-\to p\bar{p}$ and $e^+e^-\to n\bar{n}$ cross sections in the nonrelativistic effective field theory framework. The proton-antiproton and neutron-antineutron coupled-channel final state interactions are considered and found responsible for near-threshold enhancements. Both the proton-neutron mass difference and the Coulomb interaction between $p$ and $\bar{p}$ are considered, and the $N\bar{N}$ strong interactions are taken into account through a short-distance optical potential. By fitting the low energy constants in the amplitudes to the data for the near-threshold $e^+e^-\to N\bar{N}$ cross sections from the BESIII and SND Collaborations, a $N\bar{N}$ quasi-bound state is found just above the $p\bar{p}$ threshold, and another $N\bar{N}$ pole is found on the unphysical Riemann sheet, farther away from the threshold. The constructed coupled-channel amplitude with Coulomb effects also offers a framework that can be used directly in experimental analyses on fine structures near the $N\bar{N}$ thresholds.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a near-threshold $N\bar{N}$ quasi-bound state exists just above the $p\bar{p}$ threshold, and a second $N\bar{N}$ pole exists on an unphysical Riemann sheet farther from threshold; however, the exact pole positions depend on the fit (Fit 1 vs. Fit 2), with one fit placing the physical-sheet pole above the $n\bar{n}$ threshold and the other between the $p\bar{p}$ and $n\bar{n}$ thresholds.

## Method

The authors performed a coupled-channel nonrelativistic effective field theory (NREFT) analysis of the $e^+e^-\to p\bar{p}$ and $e^+e^-\to n\bar{n}$ cross sections near threshold, including $p\bar{p}$--$n\bar{n}$ coupled-channel final state interactions, the Coulomb interaction between $p$ and $\bar{p}$, and the proton-neutron mass difference. The strong $N\bar{N}$ interaction was parametrized by a constant short-distance optical potential with complex low-energy constants, and the amplitudes were fitted simultaneously to BESIII data for $e^+e^-\to p\bar{p}$ (5 data points below 2.0 GeV) and SND data for $e^+e^-\to n\bar{n}$ (13 data points below 1.91 GeV), using a sharp cutoff $\Lambda$ varied between 2.0 and 2.6 GeV.

## Key numbers

* The $p\bar{p}$ threshold is at 1876.27 MeV and the $n\bar{n}$ threshold is at 1879.13 MeV.
* For Fit 1, the pole on the physical Riemann sheet (RS$_{++}$) is at $E_1 = 1882.2_{-4.5}^{+9.4} - i (1.2_{-1.2}^{+6.3})$ MeV, and the pole on the second Riemann sheet (RS$_{-+}$) is at $E_2 = 1882.2_{-46.5}^{+79.4} - i (106.4_{-65.1}^{+83.5})$ MeV.
* For Fit 2, the pole on RS$_{++}$ is at $E_1 = 1878.2_{-1.0}^{+4.5} - i (5.9_{-4.7}^{+1.5})$ MeV, and the pole on RS$_{-+}$ is at $E_2 = 1817.7_{-13.1}^{+53.1} - i (99.4_{-34.2}^{+46.7})$ MeV.
* The Coulomb-modified effective scattering length for the $p\bar{p}$ channel in Fit 1 is $a_{11,\text{eff}} = 0.10_{-0.63}^{+0.63} + i (1.14_{-0.51}^{+1.76})$ fm, and in Fit 2 is $a_{11,\text{eff}} = -0.60_{-0.23}^{+0.68} + i (1.09_{-0.13}^{+0.65})$ fm.
* The $\chi^2$ per degree of freedom is 0.54 for Fit 1 and 0.58 for Fit 2.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of $N\bar{N}$ near-threshold enhancements using optical potentials from the Paris model (El-Bennich 2008, Dmitriev 2007), Nijmegen model (Zhou 2012), and Juelich model (Haidenbauer 2014, Kang 2013), as well as chiral effective field theory (Chen 2011, Kang 2013, Dai 2017, Xiao 2024). Unlike most prior work that ignored Coulomb effects and the proton-neutron mass difference, this analysis includes both and uses the newly released SND data for $e^+e^-\to n\bar{n}$ (Achasov 2024) alongside BESIII data (BESIII 2021). The constructed coupled-channel amplitude with Coulomb effects provides a framework that can be used directly in experimental analyses of fine structures near the $N\bar{N}$ thresholds.
