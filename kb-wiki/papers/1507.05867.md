# The Pentaquark Candidates in the Dynamical Diquark Picture

> Starting with the dynamical picture of the exotic $c\bar c$-containing states $XY \! Z$ as the confinement-induced hadronization of a rapidly separating pair of a compact diquark and antidiquark, we describe the pentaquark candidates $P_c^+(4380)$ and $P_c^+(4450)$ in terms of a confined but rapidly separating color-antitriplet diquark $cu$ and color-triplet "triquark" $\bar c (ud)$. This separation explains the relatively small $P_c^+$ widths, despite these 5-quark systems lying far above both the $J/ψ\, p$ and $Λ_c \bar D^{(*)0}$ thresholds. The $P_c^+$ states are predicted to form isospin doublets with neutral partners $P_c^0$.

## Key claim
The pentaquark candidates \(P_c^+(4380)\) and \(P_c^+(4450)\) can be understood as a confined but rapidly separating color-antitriplet diquark \(cu\) and color-triplet “triquark” \(\bar c (ud)\), rather than as static hadronic molecules; this separation explains the relatively small widths of these states despite their lying far above the \(J/\psi\,p\) and \(\Lambda_c \bar D^{(*)0}\) thresholds.

## Method
The paper extends the dynamical diquark picture previously applied to tetraquark states (Brodsky 2014) to pentaquarks. It proposes that in \(\Lambda_b \to J/\psi\,K^- p\) decay, a pre-existing \((ud)\) diquark from the \(\Lambda_b\) binds with a \(\bar c\) quark to form a color-triplet antitriquark \(\bar c(ud)\), while a \((cu)\) diquark forms separately; the two colored components separate rapidly under the confining linear-plus-Coulomb Cornell potential until hadronization occurs. The separation distances are estimated using the Cornell potential parameters from Barnes (2005), and the resulting quantum numbers are derived from angular-momentum coupling of the diquark spin, antitriquark spin, and orbital excitation.

## Key numbers
- Masses and widths from LHCb: \(m_1 = 4380 \pm 8 \pm 29\) MeV, \(\Gamma_1 = 205 \pm 18 \pm 86\) MeV (9 standard deviations); \(m_2 = 4449.8 \pm 1.7 \pm 2.5\) MeV, \(\Gamma_2 = 39 \pm 5 \pm 19\) MeV (12 standard deviations).
- Mass difference between the two states: \(m_2 - m_1 = 70\) MeV; squared-mass difference: \(m_2^2 - m_1^2 = (790\ \text{MeV})^2\).
- Estimated diquark-antitriquark separation from the Cornell potential: \(R = 0.64\) fm for \(P_c^+(4380)\) and \(R = 0.70\) fm for \(P_c^+(4450)\).
- Estimated root-mean-square matter radii for \(\Lambda_b\) and \(\Lambda_c\) from Albertus (2003): no more than 0.22 fm and 0.31 fm, respectively.
- Estimated size of the \((cu)\) diquark from QCD sum rules: \(m_\delta = 1.860\) GeV (near the \(D^0\) mass 1.865 GeV); antitriquark mass taken as \(m_{\bar\theta} = m_{\Lambda_c} = 2.286\) GeV.
- Cornell potential parameters used: \(\alpha_s = 0.5461\), \(b = 0.1425\) GeV\(^2\), \(m_c = 1.4797\) GeV, \(\sigma = 1.0946\) GeV.
- Estimated \(\langle r_{J/\psi}\rangle = 0.39\) fm from the Cornell potential; proton size \(\langle r_p\rangle \simeq 0.88\) fm.

## Context
This work extends the dynamical diquark picture of Brodsky (2014) for tetraquarks (e.g., \(Z^-(4475)\)) to the pentaquark sector, contrasting with hadronic molecule interpretations (Karliner 2015, Roca 2015) and with the static diquark-triquark model of Karliner (2003) that required a centrifugal barrier. It also differs from the diquark composition of Maiani (2015) by emphasizing the \(\bar c\) belonging to a compact component. The picture predicts isospin doublets with neutral \(P_c^0\) partners and suggests that additional pentaquark states with lower spins may be more difficult to observe experimentally.
