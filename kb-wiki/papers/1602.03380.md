# Chiral nucleon-nucleon forces in nuclear structure calculations

> Realistic nuclear potentials, derived within chiral perturbation theory, are a major breakthrough in modern nuclear structure theory, since they provide a direct link between nuclear physics and its underlying theory, namely the QCD. As a matter of fact, chiral potentials are tailored on the low-energy regime of nuclear structure physics, and chiral perturbation theory provides on the same footing two-nucleon forces as well as many-body ones. This feature fits well with modern advances in ab-initio methods and realistic shell-model. Here, we will review recent nuclear structure calculations, based on realistic chiral potentials, for both finite nuclei and infinite nuclear matter.

## Key claim

Chiral perturbation theory provides a systematic, model-independent framework for deriving nuclear potentials that link QCD to low-energy nuclear structure, but the practical success of these potentials depends on the specific choice of regulator cutoff (here \(\Lambda = 414\) MeV) and the inclusion of three-nucleon forces to reproduce empirical saturation properties of nuclear matter.

## Method

The authors employ the N\(^3\)LOW chiral potential, a next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N\(^3\)LO) nucleon-nucleon potential with a sharp cutoff \(\Lambda = 414\) MeV, derived from the Entem-Machleidt 2003 potential. For finite nuclei, they derive a shell-model effective Hamiltonian using the \(\hat{Q}\)-box plus folded-diagram method, summing diagrams up to third order and using the Lee-Suzuki iteration method. For infinite nuclear matter, they compute the ground-state energy per particle via Goldstone diagrams up to third order, incorporating three-nucleon forces (at N\(^2\)LO) through a density-dependent two-body potential \(\overline{V}_{3N}(k_F)\) obtained by integrating one nucleon up to the Fermi momentum.

## Key numbers

- N\(^3\)LOW potential uses a sharp cutoff \(\Lambda = 414\) MeV (equivalent to 2.1 fm\(^{-1}\) in relative-momentum cutoff).
- The chiral expansion is valid for momenta \(Q < \Lambda_\chi \simeq 1\) GeV, with typical regulator cutoff \(\Lambda \simeq 0.5\) GeV.
- The N\(^3\)LOW potential reproduces empirical phase-shifts of NN scattering up to at least 200 MeV laboratory energy.
- The calculated slope of the symmetry energy at saturation density is \(L = 68\) MeV, compared to the empirical value \(L = 70 \pm 25\) MeV (Tsang 2012).
- The three-nucleon force contribution to the energy per nucleon in symmetric nuclear matter is larger than that in pure neutron matter, and its inclusion is crucial to reproduce empirical saturation properties.

## Context

This work extends the program of using chiral effective field theory potentials for nuclear structure calculations, building on the N\(^3\)LO potential of Entem and Machleidt (2003) and the \(V_{\rm low-k}\) renormalization approach of Bogner (2002). The N\(^3\)LOW potential provides an analytic alternative to \(V_{\rm low-k}\) while maintaining consistency with chiral perturbation theory. The results for carbon and oxygen isotopes, including the prediction that \(^{22}\)C is the last bound isotope and the reproduction of the \(N=16\) subshell closure, demonstrate the potential of this approach for studying exotic nuclei near the neutron dripline. The inclusion of three-nucleon forces via a density-dependent two-body potential (Holt, Kaiser, Weise 2010) enables realistic predictions for infinite nuclear matter and the symmetry energy, which can be connected to neutron star properties and neutron skin thickness measurements.
