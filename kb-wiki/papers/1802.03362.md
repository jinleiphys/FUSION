# Three-body spectrum in a finite volume: the role of cubic symmetry

> The three-particle quantization condition is partially diagonalized in the center-of-mass frame by using cubic symmetry on the lattice. To this end, instead of spherical harmonics, the kernel of the Bethe-Salpeter equation for particle-dimer scattering is expanded in the basis functions of different irreducible representations of the octahedral group. Such a projection is of particular importance for the three-body problem in the finite volume due to the occurrence of three-body singularities above breakup. Additionally, we study the numerical solution and properties of such a projected quantization condition in a simple model. It is shown that, for large volumes, these solutions allow for an instructive interpretation of the energy eigenvalues in terms of bound and scattering states.

## Key claim

The three-particle quantization condition in a finite volume can be partially diagonalized by expanding the kernel of the Bethe-Salpeter equation for particle-dimer scattering in basis functions of the irreducible representations of the octahedral group, which becomes necessary above the breakup threshold where three-body singularities prevent convergence of the conventional partial-wave expansion.

## Method

The authors start from the three-particle quantization condition expressed in terms of the particle-dimer scattering amplitude, which satisfies a Bethe-Salpeter equation with a kernel containing the one-particle exchange diagram and a local three-body force term. They project this equation onto the irreducible representations of the octahedral group \(O_h\) by expanding functions of the discretized lattice momenta in basis functions of the irreps \(A_1^\pm, A_2^\pm, E^\pm, T_1^\pm, T_2^\pm\), using the fact that the kernel and dimer propagator are scalars under the cubic symmetry group. The momentum sums are organized into shells of vectors related by symmetry transformations from a reference vector, and the projected quantization condition is obtained as a determinant equation for each irrep separately.

## Key numbers

* The octahedral group has 48 elements (24 rotations plus inversion), with irreducible representations of dimensions \(s_\Gamma = 1\) for \(A_1^\pm, A_2^\pm\), \(s_\Gamma = 2\) for \(E^\pm\), and \(s_\Gamma = 3\) for \(T_1^\pm, T_2^\pm\).
* The two-body scattering is described by the effective range expansion \(k^*\cot\delta(k^*) = -1/a + (1/2)r(k^*)^2 + O((k^*)^4)\), with the effective range \(r\) set to zero in the numerical model.
* The momentum cutoff is given by \(N = \Lambda L/(2\pi)\), where \(L\) is the spatial box size and \(\Lambda\) is the ultraviolet cutoff.
* The first shell (\(s=1\)) contains the single momentum \((0,0,0)\); the second shell (\(s=2\)) contains 6 vectors derived from \((1,0,0)\) by symmetry transformations.

## Context

This work extends the three-particle quantization condition formalism developed by Pang et al. (2017), Mai et al. (2017), and Briceno et al. (2017) by incorporating the cubic symmetry of lattice QCD simulations. The projection onto irreducible representations of the octahedral group is analogous to the partial-wave expansion in infinite volume, but becomes essential above the three-body breakup threshold where three-body singularities appear. This enables lattice QCD practitioners to extract energy levels in specific irreps independently, matching the way source/sink operators are typically constructed in lattice calculations, and provides a framework for extracting infinite-volume three-body observables from finite-volume spectra.
