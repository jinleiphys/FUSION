# Impact of r-modes on the cooling of neutron stars

> Studying the frequency and temperature evolution of a compact star can give us valuable information about the microscopic properties of the matter inside the star. In this paper we study the effect of dissipative reheating of a neutron star due to r-mode oscillations on its temperature evolution. We find that there is still an impact of an r-mode phase on the temperature long after the star has left the instability region and the r-mode is damped completely. With accurate temperature measurements it may be possible to detect this trace of a previous r-mode phase in observed pulsars.

## Key claim

The paper finds that r-mode oscillations can leave a detectable thermal imprint on a neutron star long after the mode has been fully damped and the star has left the instability region, provided that the saturation amplitude is small enough that the r-mode phase lasts a significant fraction of the star's age.

## Method

The authors solve three coupled evolution equations for the rotation frequency, r-mode amplitude, and temperature of a 1.4 solar mass APR hadronic neutron star, assuming a fixed saturation amplitude for the r-mode and a dissipative saturation mechanism that provides stronger reheating than viscosity alone. The evolution proceeds in three stages: growth of the r-mode until saturation, spin-down at constant amplitude within the instability region, and subsequent decay and cooling outside the instability region.

## Key numbers

- For a 1.4 solar mass APR neutron star with initial conditions Ω₀ = 0.8 Ω_K (Kepler frequency 6020 Hz), α₀ = 10⁻⁶, and T₀ = 30 MeV, the reheating increases the temperature during the r-mode phase by a constant factor up to 5 for realistic saturation amplitudes.
- For saturation amplitudes α_sat < 0.1, the thermal evolution outside the instability region takes a universal form independent of the amplitude, and even after twice the r-mode spin-down time the temperature of a star with an r-mode history is still more than ten percent larger than that of a star without.
- The r-mode spin-down time increases strongly with decreasing saturation amplitude, with calculations performed for α_sat values from 10⁻⁴ to 1.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological r-mode evolution model of Owen (1998) and the thermal evolution approach of Yang (2009), but differs from previous analyses by including a stronger reheating term that accounts for the dissipation required to saturate the r-mode amplitude when viscosity alone cannot stop its growth. The results enable future observational tests: with more accurate temperature measurements of young pulsars, it may be possible to detect the thermal signature of a past r-mode phase, which the authors argue is a consistent mechanism to explain the low spin frequencies of nearly all observed young pulsars for saturation amplitudes between 0.001 and 0.1.
