# Nuclei as Laboratories: Nuclear Tests of Fundamental Symmetries

> The prospect of a rare isosotope accelerator facility opens up possibilities for a new generation of nuclear tests of fundamental symmetries. In this talk, I survey the current landscape of such tests and discuss future opportunities that a new facility might present.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a new rare isotope accelerator facility could enable a new generation of nuclear tests of fundamental symmetries, but the actual utility of such tests depends on pushing experimental sensitivity significantly beyond current levels and on resolving existing theoretical puzzles in hadronic weak interactions.

## Method

The author surveys the current landscape of nuclear tests of fundamental symmetries, focusing on three areas: electric dipole moment (EDM) searches as probes of CP-violation relevant to baryogenesis; precision studies of weak nuclear decays (including superallowed beta-decay for CKM unitarity tests and beta-decay correlation measurements); and parity-violating hadronic weak interaction studies using few-body systems. The discussion connects these experimental programs to specific beyond-Standard Model scenarios, particularly supersymmetric models, and evaluates the sensitivity required to test theoretical predictions.

## Key numbers

- Baryon asymmetry from Big Bang Nucleosynthesis: Y_B = (7.3 ± 2.5) × 10^{-11}
- Baryon asymmetry from WMAP: Y_B = (9.2 ± 1.1) × 10^{-11}
- Current lower bound on Higgs boson mass from LEP II: m_H > 114.4 GeV
- Extracted value of V_ud from superallowed decays: 0.97377(11)(15)(19), where the three errors are combined experimental ft and nuclear structure uncertainty, nuclear Coulomb distortion effects, and theoretical hadronic structure uncertainty respectively
- Current limit on the Fierz interference coefficient b_F in superallowed decays: 0.0026(26)
- The corrected ft values for the twelve best-measured superallowed decays agree at the level of a few parts in 10^4
- For supersymmetric models with heavy sfermions (3-10 TeV), one-loop EDM contributions are suppressed but two-loop contributions involving gauginos and Higgsinos remain

## Context

This talk surveys the landscape of nuclear tests of fundamental symmetries, extending the author's previous reviews (Ramsey-Musolf 2006, Erler 2004) and building on the NSAC 2005 long-range plan for nuclear physics. The paper discusses how EDM searches can test electroweak baryogenesis scenarios, contrasting with higher-scale scenarios like GUT baryogenesis or leptogenesis that are more difficult to test experimentally. The discussion of CKM unitarity tests references the work of Marciano and Sirlin (2005) on reducing hadronic uncertainties, and the analysis of supersymmetric contributions to beta-decay correlations draws on work by Profumo, Ramsey-Musolf, and collaborators (2006). The paper enables future experimental programs at rare isotope facilities and the Spallation Neutron Source, particularly for parity-violating hadronic weak interaction studies using few-body systems that could resolve longstanding puzzles in the Delta S=1 sector.
