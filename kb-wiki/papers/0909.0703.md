# Hadron properties from QCD bound-state equations

> This thesis presents an investigation of meson and baryon properties in the framework of covariant bound-state equations based on the Dyson-Schwinger equations of QCD. Pion and rho-meson, diquark, nucleon and delta-baryon masses are obtained as self-consistent solutions of the respective equations for $q\bar{q}$, $qq$, $qqq$ and $q(qq)$ systems. The common parenthesis is given by a rainbow-ladder truncation in the quark-(anti-)quark channel. It includes an effective quark-gluon coupling as the only phenomenological input and inherent link in the calculation of meson and baryon observables. Results for hadron masses and the pion's and nucleon's static electromagnetic properties as a function of a self-consistently calculated pion mass are presented and compared to lattice results and their chiral extrapolations. The evolution of the nucleon electromagnetic form factors with larger photon momentum is investigated. The impact of further contributions beyond rainbow-ladder, e.g. pionic corrections, and possible future applications are discussed.

## Key claim

This thesis demonstrates that meson and baryon masses, as well as the pion's and nucleon's static electromagnetic properties, can be obtained from self-consistent solutions of covariant bound-state equations based on QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations, but only within a rainbow-ladder truncation that includes an effective quark-gluon coupling as the sole phenomenological input; the results are therefore contingent on this truncation and the specific modeling of the transverse part of the quark-photon vertex.

## Method

The author solves the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation in rainbow-ladder truncation to obtain the quark propagator, then uses homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equations to compute meson (pion, rho) and diquark (scalar, axial-vector) bound-state amplitudes. Baryon masses for the nucleon and delta are obtained from both a three-body Faddeev framework and a quark-diquark approach, with the nucleon's electromagnetic form factors calculated using a modeled quark-photon vertex that includes a Ball-Chiu part and a transverse contribution simulating the rho-meson pole.

## Key numbers

* Quark propagator renormalization point is set to μ = 19 GeV.
* The complex conjugate branch points in the logarithmic tail of the effective coupling lead to a theoretical limitation at -(0.31 GeV)^2 for the squared imaginary part of the external quark momentum.
* The infrared limit of the effective coupling parametrization gives α(k²)/k² → π γ_m / Λ₀² as k² → 0, with Λ₀ = 1 GeV.
* The parameter ρ₁ in the transverse vertex ansatz is chosen as ρ₁ = 0.001 + m_π²/(3.72 GeV²) to reproduce the pion charge radius.
* The parameter ρ₂ in the transverse vertex is set to ρ₂ = 0.001 to maximize agreement with polarization-transfer data for the proton's form factor ratio G_E(Q²)/G_M(Q²).

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter approach to baryons by treating nucleon and delta as bound states of three quarks or as quark-diquark systems, building on earlier rainbow-ladder studies of mesons by Maris and Roberts (1997) and Maris and Tandy (1999). The modeling of the quark-photon vertex with a transverse part simulating the rho-meson pole follows the approach of Maris and Tandy (1999) and Bhagwat and Maris (2006). The results enable future investigations of nucleon electromagnetic form factors at larger photon momentum, though the author notes that the exponential suppression of the transverse vertex ansatz must eventually be replaced by a self-consistent solution from the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation.
