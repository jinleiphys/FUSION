# Fluctuations and the rapidity dependence of charged particles spectra in fixed centrality bins in p$A$ collisions

> We argue that large fluctuations in the saturation momentum are necessary to explain the ATLAS and ALICE data on pA collisions measured at the LHC. Using a form for the distribution of fluctuations motivated by theoretical studies of the non-linear evolution equations for the Color Glass Condensate, we find a remarkably good agreement between theory and the measured distributions. If the saturation momentum fluctuates, we argue that the cross section for a proton probe should also fluctuate, consistent with previous observations. Finally we discuss these results and their possible consistency with what is known about multiplicity fluctuations in such collisions, and attempts to describe the ridge effect from properties of the initial state.

## Key claim

Large fluctuations in the proton saturation momentum, with a rapidity-independent width, are necessary to explain the ATLAS and ALICE data on pPb collisions at the LHC; the authors caution that their results are semi-quantitative because they neglect fluctuations of the nuclear saturation momentum and other sources of fluctuations.

## Method

The authors model charged-particle multiplicity in pA collisions using the Color Glass Condensate framework, where the multiplicity depends on the proton and nuclear saturation scales. They introduce Gaussian fluctuations in the logarithm of the proton saturation momentum, with a width σ treated as a constant at fixed LHC energy, and compute the averaged multiplicity by integrating over the fluctuating saturation scale. The model is compared to ATLAS and ALICE pseudorapidity distributions for fixed centrality bins, with the normalization S⊥ (transverse overlap area) fitted at η=0 and the only free parameter being σ.

## Key numbers

- The best description of both ATLAS and ALICE data is obtained for σ = 1.55 (the width of the Gaussian distribution of ln(Q_s^2 / Q_p^2)).
- The energy-dependence parameter λ for the saturation scales is fixed to 0.32, taken from a previous analysis of HERA DIS data (Praszalowicz, 2012).
- The pseudorapidity-to-rapidity conversion uses η_0^2 = 1.35, corresponding to m^2 + p_T^2 over p_T^2.
- For ATLAS, the mean number of participants ranges from ⟨N_part⟩ = 3.55 (60–90% centrality) to 24.13 (0–1% centrality); for ALICE, from 2.94 (80–100%) to 15.7 (0–5%).
- The fitted transverse area S⊥ for ATLAS follows S⊥^ATLAS = (0.66 + 0.6 ln(N_part))^2; for ALICE, S⊥^ALICE = (0.09 + 0.84 ln(N_part))^2.

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts to describe pA data using the wounded nucleon model (Bialas, 1976; Bialas, 2004) and the simplest Color Glass Condensate model (Kharzeev, 2004), both of which fail to reproduce the measured pseudorapidity distributions. The authors build on theoretical arguments for fluctuations in the saturation momentum from the nonlinear evolution equations (Munier, 2003; Iancu, 2004; Hatta, 2006), and note that their extracted width σ = 1.55 exceeds by a factor of 1.5 the value from a recent analysis by Tribedy (2015). The results suggest that future work must include fluctuations of the nuclear saturation momentum and local spatial structure in the proton, and enable tests of correlations between multiplicity and jet production in the IP-glasma model (Schenke, 2012).
