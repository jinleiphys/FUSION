# Phase-Space Description of Momentum Spectra in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We consider a phase-space model for particle production in nuclear collisions. Once the multiplicities of the individual particle species are known, single-inclusive momentum spectra can be computed after making simplifying assumptions for the matrix element for multiparticle production. Comparison of the calculated spectra with data for pions and kaons from central Pb+Pb collisions at E_{Lab}=158 AGeV reveals a residual longitudinal phase-space dominance in the final state of the reaction. We account for this by modifying the isotropic, relativistic invariant phase space in a way which retains boost invariance in beam direction but suppresses large transverse momenta. Adjusting a single parameter, we obtain a reasonably good description of transverse momentum and rapidity spectra for both pions and kaons.

## Key claim

The paper argues that single-inclusive momentum spectra in relativistic heavy-ion collisions can be described by a phase-space model with minimal dynamical assumptions, and that a residual longitudinal phase-space dominance in the final state requires modifying the isotropic relativistic invariant phase space to suppress large transverse momenta via a single adjustable parameter \(a\), yielding a "reasonably good description" of both transverse momentum and rapidity spectra for pions and kaons, but only after the multiplicities of all particle species are known as input.

## Method

The authors compute the multiparticle phase-space integral \(I_n(P^\mu)\) for central Pb+Pb collisions at \(E_{\rm Lab}=158\) AGeV using the saddle-point method, approximating the matrix element for multiparticle production by a product ansatz \(T({\bf p}_i) = \prod_i f_i({\bf p}_{\perp i})\). Two special cases are studied: the fully Lorentz-invariant case with \(f_i=1\), and a case with \(f_i({\bf p}_{\perp i}) = \exp[-a(m_{\perp i}-m_i)]\) that preserves boost invariance along the beam direction but exponentially suppresses large transverse momenta. Single-inclusive transverse mass and rapidity spectra are then derived from the ratio of phase-space integrals \(I_{n-1}(s')/I_n(s)\) and compared to NA49 data for pions and kaons.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: \(E_{\rm Lab}=158\) AGeV, corresponding to \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 17.3\) AGeV in the center-of-momentum frame.
- For the 5% most central Pb+Pb collisions, the average number of wounded nucleons is \(\langle N_w \rangle = 362\), giving total energy \(\sqrt{s} = 3131.3\) GeV.
- Total number of secondaries used in the calculation: \(n = 2611\) particles with total rest mass \(\sum m_i = 824.65\) GeV and average particle mass \(\langle m_i \rangle \simeq 0.316\) GeV.
- For \(a=0\), the phase-space integral yields \(\ln I_n \simeq 6600\) and the saddle point is at \(\beta = 2.027\) GeV\(^{-1}\).
- For massless particles, the inverse slope parameter \(T^*\) equals \(\beta^{-1}\) and is related to the thermal temperature by \(T^* = \frac{3}{2}T\).
- The half width of the rapidity spectrum for massless particles is \(\Delta y = \ln(1+\sqrt{2}) \simeq 0.8814\), independent of particle number and total energy.

## Context

This work extends the phase-space approach previously applied to hadron-hadron collisions (Hagedorn, Byckling, Hama) to nucleus-nucleus collisions, following earlier studies by Knoll, Rischke, Hsu, Koch, and Becattini. It is presented as complementary to the fluid-dynamical/thermodynamical approach (Huovinen 2001, Kolb 2003) which assumes local thermal equilibrium but faces problems such as the "HBT puzzle" and the difficulty of simultaneously describing particle ratios and collective flow (Gyulassy 1997, Hirano 2002). The phase-space model requires only kinematic constraints and measured multiplicities as input, and deviations from its predictions indicate nontrivial dynamics such as resonance decays or collective flow. The comparison with NA49 data (Afanasiev 2002, Mischke 2002) for central Pb+Pb collisions at 158 AGeV enables investigation of both transverse mass and rapidity spectra.
